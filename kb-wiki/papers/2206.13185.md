# Conserving approximations to dilute equilibrium systems. Pair interaction potential

> We study self-consistent approximations such as the $Φ$-derivable and virial approaches to dilute strongly interacting systems in equilibrium. We consider a system of non-relativistic fermions of one kind interacting via a pair potential Thermodynamical quantities are expressed in terms of various spectral functions. We review the $Φ$ derivable approximation scheme demonstrating the exact conservation of the Noether and the Botermans-Malfliet fermion number densities, and then for $Φ$ described by the tadpole and sandwich diagrams we show the coincidence of these two number densities. As examples of test pair potentials, we consider the Yukawa central nucleon-nucleon potentials within Walecka, CD Bonn, and Reid parameterizations, and the corresponding classical Lennard-Jones potentials. Expressions for the second and third virial coefficients are derived and analyzed for $Φ$ described by the tadpole and sandwich diagrams. Next, we focus on the virial approach to the equation of state. Classical, semiclassical, and purely quantum approaches are studied in detail. Then, different extrapolations of the virial equation of state are considered including the van~der~Waals form and excluded-volume models. We derive the expression for the second virial coefficient using the effective range approximation for the scattering amplitude and compare the result with the purely quantum result using the experimental phase shifts. Attention is focused on the problem of anomalously large value of the nucleon-nucleon scattering length appearing due to the presence of the quasi-bound state in nucleon-nucleon scattering, which can be destroyed in the matter because of the action of the Pauli blocking. We present results for the second virial coefficient subtracting this term. We discuss the validity of such a procedure to describe the equation of state of the nuclear matter in the virial limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for dilute equilibrium systems of non-relativistic fermions interacting via a pair potential, the \(\Phi\)-derivable approximation (retaining only the tadpole and sandwich diagrams in the \(\Phi\) functional) and the virial expansion yield coincident results within the Born approximation for \(n\lambda^3 \ll 1\) (where \(n\) is the fermion number density and \(\lambda\) is the thermal wavelength), but differences appear beyond this common region of validity.

## Method

The authors study a one-component system of non-relativistic fermions (e.g., neutrons) interacting via a spin-independent pair potential, using both the \(\Phi\)-derivable approximation scheme (with the \(\Phi\) functional limited to tadpole and sandwich diagrams) and the virial expansion (classical, semiclassical up to order \(\hbar^3\), and fully quantum Beth-Uhlenbeck). They derive expressions for thermodynamic quantities, including the second and third virial coefficients, and compare results for several test potentials: Yukawa nucleon-nucleon potentials (Walecka, CD Bonn, Reid parameterizations), classical Lennard-Jones potentials, and square-barrier-well potentials. The second virial coefficient is also computed using the effective range approximation for the scattering amplitude and compared with the Beth-Uhlenbeck result using experimental phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The condition for the dilute limit is \(n\lambda^3 \ll 1\), where \(\lambda = \hbar \sqrt{2\pi/(mT)}\) is the thermal wavelength.
- The nucleon-nucleon scattering length is \(a_{nn} \simeq -19\) fm, owing to a quasi-bound state.
- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The quasi-bound state can be destroyed by Pauli blocking for densities \(n \gtrsim 10^{-3} n_0\).
- The Beth-Uhlenbeck result for the second virial coefficient using experimental phase shifts agrees approximately with the effective range approximation for low temperatures \(T \lesssim 20\) MeV.
- For \(T = 10\) MeV, the \(^1\)S\(_0\) phase shifts in medium change their vacuum low-energy behavior for \(n \gtrsim 0.01 n_0\).
- The virial equation of state at \(n\lambda^3 \ll 1\) applies for \(T \gg 100\,(n/n_0)^{2/3}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between \(\Phi\)-derivable approximations (Baym 1962) and virial approaches (Beth and Uhlenbeck 1937) for dilute strongly interacting fermion systems, specifically applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions. It builds on prior studies of the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to dilute nuclear systems (Röpke, Schulz, and others 1982) and the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach including Pauli blocking effects (Zimmermann and Stolz 1985; Schmidt, Röpke, and Schulz 1990). The paper also relates to chiral effective field theory approaches to dilute nuclear matter (Lepage and Kaplan 2002; Drischler, Holt, and Wellenhofer 2021) and discusses the problem of the anomalously large nucleon-nucleon scattering length and its implications for the equation of state in the virial limit.
