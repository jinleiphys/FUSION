# Role of non-collective excitations in heavy-ion fusion reactions and quasi-elastic scattering around the Coulomb barrier

> Despite the supposed simplicity of double-closed shell nuclei, conventional coupled-channels calculations, that include all of the known collective states of the target and projectile, give a poor fit to the fusion cross section for the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb system. The discrepancies are highlighted through the experimental barrier distribution and logarithmic derivative, that are both well defined by the precise experimental fusion data available. In order to broaden our search for possible causes for this anomaly, we revisit this system and include in our calculations a large number of non-collective states of the target, whose spin, parity, excitation energy and deformation paramter are known from high-precision proton inelastic-scattering measurements. Although the new coupled-channels calculations modify the barrier distribution, the disagreemnt with experiment remains both for fusion and for quasi-elastic (QE) scattering. We find that the Q-value distributions for large-angle QE scattering become rapidly more important as the incident energy increases, reflecting the trend of the experimental data. The mass-number dependence of the non-collective excitations is discussed.

# Role of non-collective excitations in heavy-ion fusion reactions and quasi-elastic scattering around the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

Including a large number of non-collective states of 208Pb (up to 7.382 MeV) in coupled-channels calculations for the 16O + 208Pb system modifies the barrier distribution but does **not** resolve the long-standing disagreement with experimental fusion and quasi-elastic scattering data; the main peak in the barrier distribution remains overestimated and the deep sub-barrier hindrance is not explained by non-collective excitations of the colliding nuclei alone.

## Method

The authors solved three-dimensional coupled-channels equations for heavy-ion reactions, including both collective vibrational states (3−, 5−, 2+ in 208Pb and 3− in 16O) and 70 non-collective states of 208Pb with spins, parities, excitation energies, and deformation parameters taken from high-resolution proton inelastic scattering measurements. The calculations used the isocentrifugal approximation and a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential with surface diffuseness a = 0.671 fm, and were performed for fusion cross sections, fusion barrier distributions, quasi-elastic scattering cross sections, quasi-elastic barrier distributions, and Q-value distributions at backward angles. Additional calculations were carried out for 32S + 208Pb and 40Ca + 208Pb to study mass-number dependence.

## Key numbers

- 70 non-collective states in 208Pb below 7.382 MeV were included in the coupled-channels calculations
- Collective deformation parameters used: β3(208Pb) = 0.122, β5(208Pb) = 0.058, β2(208Pb) = 0.058, β3(16O) = 0.733
- Nuclear potential surface diffuseness a = 0.671 fm; potential depth modified from 853 MeV to 550 MeV to compensate adiabatic renormalization
- Double octupole phonon states in 208Pb assumed degenerate at E = 5.23 MeV (twice the single-phonon energy of 2.615 MeV)
- Non-collective excitations smear the barrier distribution at energies around 78 MeV
- Q-value distributions were computed with a Gaussian smearing width Δ = 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier schematic one-dimensional study using random matrix theory (Yusa, Hagino, Rowley, 2010) to a realistic three-dimensional treatment with experimental spectra. It addresses a long-standing problem where conventional coupled-channels calculations for 16O + 208Pb (Mortimer et al., 1999; Evers et al., 2007) overestimate the main peak in the fusion barrier distribution and fail to reproduce the deep sub-barrier hindrance. The results contradict the conjecture that non-collective excitations might explain the smearing observed in 20Ne + 90,92Zr systems (Piasecki et al., 2009). The findings enable future work to focus on other mechanisms such as reduction of the 3− excitation energy in 208Pb (Rowley and Hagino, 2009) or non-collective excitations after the touching of the colliding nuclei, and suggest that for heavy systems relevant to superheavy element synthesis, non-collective excitations must be included explicitly.
