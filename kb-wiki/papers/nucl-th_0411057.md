# Spin-orbit and tensor interactions in homogeneous matter of nucleons: accuracy of modern many-body theories

> We study the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using realistic nucleon--nucleon potentials having non central tensor and spin--orbit components, up to three times the empirical nuclear matter saturation density, $ρ_0=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$. The calculations are carried out within the frameworks of the Brueckner--Bethe--Goldstone (BBG) and Correlated Basis Functions (CBF) formalisms, in order to ascertain the accuracy of the methods. The two hole--line approximation, with the continuous choice for the single particle auxiliary potential, is adopted for the BBG approach, whereas the variational Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain theory, corrected at the second order perturbative expansion level, is used in the CBF one. The energies are then compared with the available Quantum and Variational Monte Carlo results in neutron matter and with the BBG, up to the three hole--line diagrams. For neutron matter and potentials without spin--orbit components all methods, but perturbative CBF, are in reasonable agreement up to $ρ\sim$ 3 $ρ_0$. After the inclusion of the LS interactions, we still find agreement around $ρ_0$, whereas it is spoiled at larger densities. The spin--orbit potential lowers the energy of neutron matter at $ρ_0$ by $\sim$ 3--4 MeV per nucleon. In symmetric nuclear matter, the BBG and the variational results are in agreement up to $\sim$ 1.5 $ρ_0$. Beyond this density, and in contrast with neutron matter, we find good agreement only for the potential having spin--orbit components.

## Key claim

The accuracy of modern many-body theories (Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone and Correlated Basis Functions) for nuclear matter depends strongly on density and on whether spin-orbit interactions are included; agreement between methods is good around saturation density but degrades at higher densities, especially for neutron matter when spin-orbit potentials are present.

## Method

The authors computed the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Argonne v6' and v8' nucleon-nucleon potentials (with and without spin-orbit components) up to three times empirical saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3). They employed the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formalism in the two hole-line approximation with continuous choice for the single-particle auxiliary potential, and the Correlated Basis Functions variational approach using Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain theory with second-order perturbative corrections. Results were compared with available Quantum Monte Carlo (Green's Function Monte Carlo and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo) results for neutron matter and with three hole-line Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone calculations.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit potential lowers the energy of neutron matter at saturation density by approximately 3-4 MeV per nucleon (all methods give Delta E_LS ~ -3 to -4 MeV per nucleon at rho_0)
- For the Argonne v6' potential in pure neutron matter at rho_0: BHF energy = 15.89 MeV, BBG (three hole-line) = 15.09 MeV, GFMC = 14.81(11) MeV, AFDMC = 15.12(4) MeV
- For the Argonne v8' potential in pure neutron matter at rho_0: BHF = 11.99 MeV, BBG = 11.83 MeV, GFMC = 11.90(27) MeV (with box correction -5.1 MeV), AFDMC = 14.98(6) MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter with Argonne v8' at rho_0: variational FHNC/SOC = -15.32 MeV, CBF = -17.86 MeV, BHF = -18.20 MeV
- The three-body cluster corrections (Delta^MI) for Argonne v18 at rho_0 are: -2.6 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter and 0.4 MeV in pure neutron matter
- For Argonne v6' in symmetric nuclear matter at rho_0: BHF tensor contribution <v_t + v_{t tau}> = -25.58 MeV

## Context

This work extends comparisons between Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone and Correlated Basis Functions methods previously performed for simpler interactions, now including tensor and spin-orbit components of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results are compared with Quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003) and Sarsa et al. (2003) for neutron matter, and with three hole-line Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone calculations by Baldo and Maieron (2004). The study enables assessment of which many-body methods remain reliable for neutron star equation of state calculations at high density, and identifies the need for Quantum Monte Carlo results in symmetric nuclear matter and for improved treatment of spin-orbit correlations in the Correlated Basis Functions approach.
