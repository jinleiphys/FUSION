# Electromagnetic currents for dressed hadrons

> We propose an extension of the minimal-substitution prescription for coupling the electromagnetic field to hadronic systems with internal structure. The resulting rules of extended substitution necessarily distinguish between couplings to scalar and Dirac particles. Moreover, they allow for the incorporation of electromagnetic form factors for virtual photons in an effective phenomenological framework. Applied to pions and nucleons, assumed to be fully dressed to all orders, the resulting dressed currents are shown to be locally gauge invariant. Moreover, half-on-shell expressions of (hadron propagator)$\times$(electromagnetic current) needed in all descriptions of physical processes will lose \textit{all} information about hadronic dressing for real photons. The Ball-Chiu ansatz for the spin-1/2 current is seen to suffer from an incomplete coupling procedure where some essential aspects of the Dirac particle are effectively treated as those of a scalar particle. Applied to real Compton scattering on pions and nucleons, we find that \emph{all} dressing information cancels exactly when external hadrons are on shell, leaving only gauge-invariant bare Born-type contributions with physical masses. Hence, nontrivial descriptions necessarily require contact-type two-photon processes obtained by hadrons looping around two photon insertion points.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extension of the minimal-substitution prescription for coupling electromagnetic fields to hadrons, which necessarily distinguishes between scalar and Dirac particles and allows incorporation of electromagnetic form factors for virtual photons. The author claims that for real Compton scattering on pions and nucleons, all dressing information cancels exactly when external hadrons are on shell, leaving only gauge-invariant bare Born-type contributions with physical masses, but this result is limited to processes that can be understood as two sequential one-photon processes and does not include genuine two-photon contact-type processes.

## Method

The author develops "extended substitution" rules using the gauge-derivative formalism, applying them to fully dressed pion (spin-0) and nucleon (spin-1/2) propagators to construct their corresponding dressed electromagnetic currents. For the nucleon, the current is expressed in terms of transverse operators involving Dirac and Pauli form-factor functions, and the Ball-Chiu ansatz is shown to treat some Dirac-particle aspects as scalar-like. The half-on-shell expressions of (hadron propagator) × (electromagnetic current) are then analyzed for real photons, and the cancellation of dressing effects is demonstrated explicitly for Compton scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The pion propagator dressing function Π(q²) is normalized as Π(μ²) = 1, where μ is the physical pion mass.
- For the nucleon propagator, the dressing functions satisfy A(m²) = B(m²) and A(m²) + 2m² d[A(p²)-B(p²)]/dp²|_(p²=m²) = 1, where m is the physical nucleon mass.
- The on-shell pion form factor is given by F_π(k²) = 1 + (k²/μ²) H_π(μ², μ²; k²), with the transverse form-factor function f_π vanishing at k²=0.
- For real Compton scattering on the pion, the full on-shell tensor reduces to e² (2q'+k')^ν (2q+k)^μ/(s-μ²) + e² (2q'-k)^μ (2q-k')^ν/(u-μ²) - 2e² g^{μν}, with all dressing effects canceled.

## Context

This work extends the minimal-substitution framework of Haberzettl (1997) and addresses shortcomings of the Ball-Chiu ansatz (1980) for spin-1/2 currents, which the author shows treats some Dirac-particle aspects as scalar-like. The paper enables a consistent effective-Lagrangian treatment of electromagnetic interactions for fully dressed hadrons, showing that nontrivial Compton-scattering descriptions necessarily require contact-type two-photon processes from hadrons looping around two photon insertion points, resolving a dispute between Kaloshin (1999) and earlier work.
