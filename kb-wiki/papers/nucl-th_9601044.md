# Standard Neutrino Spectrum from B-8 Decay

> We present a systematic evaluation of the shape of the neutrino energy spectrum produced by beta-decay of $^8$B. We place special emphasis on determining the range of uncertainties permitted by existing laboratory data and theoretical ingredients (such as forbidden and radiative corrections). We review and compare the available experimental data on the $^8$B$(β^+){}^8$Be$(2α)$ decay chain. We analyze the theoretical and experimental uncertainties quantitatively. We give a numerical representation of the best-fit (standard-model) neutrino spectrum, as well as two extreme deviations from the standard spectrum that represent the total (experimental and theoretical) effective $\pm3σ$ deviations. Solar neutrino experiments that are currently being developed will be able to measure the shape of the $^8$B neutrino spectrum above about 5 MeV. An observed distortion of the $^8$B solar neutrino spectrum outside the range given in the present work could be considered as evidence, at an effective significance level greater than three standard deviations, for physics beyond the standard electroweak model. We use the most recent available experimental data on the Gamow--Teller strengths in the $A=37$ system to calculate the $^8$B neutrino absorption cross section on chlorine: $σ_{\rm Cl}=(1.14\pm0.11)\times10^{-42}$~cm$^2$ ($\pm3σ$ errors). The chlorine cross section is also given as a function of the neutrino energy. The $^8$B neutrino absorption cross section in gallium is $σ_{\rm Ga}=(2.46^{+2.1}_{-1.1})\times10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ ($\pm3σ$ errors).

## Key claim

The paper provides a best-fit (standard-model) neutrino spectrum from $^8$B decay, along with two extreme deviations representing the total effective $\pm3\sigma$ uncertainties; an observed distortion of the $^8$B solar neutrino spectrum outside this range could be considered evidence for physics beyond the standard electroweak model at an effective significance level greater than three standard deviations.

## Method

The authors compiled and compared four experimental alpha-decay spectra from the $^8$B$(\beta^+){}^8$Be$(2\alpha)$ decay chain (WA1, WA2, FC, and DBW) and used a chi-squared fit to the measured positron spectrum from Napolitano et al. (1987) to determine the optimal alpha-energy bias and its uncertainties. They then calculated the neutrino spectrum including phase space factors, intermediate state smearing, the Fermi function, forbidden corrections, and radiative corrections from Batkin and Sundaresan (1995), and used the resulting spectra to compute absorption cross sections on chlorine and gallium using updated Gamow-Teller strengths.

## Key numbers

- The optimal alpha-energy bias for the WA1 spectrum is $b = 0.025 \pm 0.104$ MeV at $\pm3\sigma$, combining experimental uncertainty ($\pm0.072$ MeV) and theoretical uncertainty ($\pm0.075$ MeV) added in quadrature.
- The maximum amplitude of the $\pm3\sigma$ spectral deviations is approximately $2.5\%$ of the peak neutrino spectrum value.
- The $^8$B neutrino absorption cross section on chlorine is $\sigma_{\rm Cl} = (1.14 \pm 0.11) \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ ($\pm3\sigma$ errors).
- The $^8$B neutrino absorption cross section on gallium is $\sigma_{\rm Ga} = (2.46^{+2.1}_{-1.1}) \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ ($\pm3\sigma$ errors).
- The energy calibration uncertainty of the beta-decay data is $\delta E_\beta = \pm 0.090$ MeV at $3\sigma$, corresponding to a $\pm0.2\%$ error at the endpoint energy of approximately 15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the series of calculations by Bahcall and collaborators (1964, 1978, 1986) and by Napolitano et al. (1987), incorporating new experimental alpha-decay data and the radiative corrections for the neutrino spectrum from Batkin and Sundaresan (1995). The improved spectrum and its quantified uncertainties enable next-generation experiments such as SuperKamiokande, SNO, and ICARUS to search for spectral distortions that would indicate non-standard neutrino physics, and provide updated absorption cross sections for chlorine and gallium detectors that supersede earlier values from Bahcall and Holstein (1986) and Bahcall and Ulrich (1988).
