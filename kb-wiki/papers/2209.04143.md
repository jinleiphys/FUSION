# Interactions between heavy quarks and tilted QGP fireballs in 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions

> Heavy quark observables are applied to probe the initial energy density distribution with violation of longitudinal boost invariance produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using an improved Langevin model coupled to a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model, we study the nuclear modification factor ($R_\text{AA}$), directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) coefficients of heavy mesons and their decayed electrons at the RHIC energy. We find that the counter-clockwise tilt of the nuclear matter in the reaction plane results in a positive (negative) heavy flavor $v_1$ in the backward (forward) rapidity region, whose magnitude increases with the heavy quark transverse momentum. The difference in the heavy flavor $R_\text{AA}$ between different angular regions is also proposed as a complementary tool to characterize the asymmetry of the medium profile. Our model results are consistent with currently available data at RHIC and provide predictions that can be tested by future measurements.

## Key claim

The counter-clockwise tilt of the QGP fireball in the reaction plane produces a positive (negative) heavy flavor directed flow \(v_1\) in the backward (forward) rapidity region, with the magnitude increasing with heavy quark transverse momentum; the authors propose the difference in nuclear modification factor between different angular regions as a complementary tool to characterize the medium asymmetry, but note that the model results are only "consistent with currently available data" and provide predictions that "can be tested by future measurements."

## Method

The authors coupled a modified Langevin transport model (including both elastic scatterings and medium-induced gluon radiation) to the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, with a tilted initial energy density distribution parameterized via wounded nucleon and binary collision distributions. Heavy quarks were initialized using the FONLL perturbative QCD calculation with CT14NLO PDFs and EPPS16 nuclear shadowing, then propagated through the hydrodynamically evolving QGP until hadronization via a hybrid fragmentation and coalescence model, with final heavy flavor hadrons decayed into electrons using Pythia.

## Key numbers

* Spatial diffusion coefficient for charm quarks: \(D_\mathrm{s}(2\pi T)=4\); for bottom quarks: \(D_\mathrm{s}(2\pi T)=3\), extracted from \(R_\mathrm{AA}\) of \(b\)-decay electrons at RHIC.
* Slope parameter \(dv_1/dy\) around \(y=\pm1\) for \(D\) mesons: \(-0.045\pm0.005\); for \(B\) mesons: \(-0.010\pm0.002\) in 10-80% Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV.
* Slope parameter for charm-decayed electrons: \(dv_1/dy = -0.043\pm0.005\); for beauty-decayed electrons: \(dv_1/dy = -0.013\pm0.003\) around \(y=\pm1\).
* For the highest transverse momentum bin (\(4 < p_\mathrm{T} < 10\) GeV), slope parameter for \(D\) mesons: \(dv_1/dy = -0.050\pm0.005\); for \(B\) mesons: \(-0.025\pm0.005\); for charm-decayed electrons: \(-0.065\pm0.005\); for beauty-decayed electrons: \(-0.025\pm0.005\).
* Initial hydrodynamic time: \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\); freeze-out temperature: \(T_\mathrm{frz}=137\) MeV; decoupling temperature for heavy quarks: \(T_\mathrm{d}=165\) MeV.
* Shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio: \(\eta_\mathrm{v}/s = 0.08\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Jiang et al., 2022) which found that heavy flavor \(v_1\) is dominated by the deformed medium profile at RHIC energy but by the electromagnetic field at LHC energy. It builds on the tilted QGP fireball concept from Bozek (2010) and the CLVisc hydrodynamic framework developed by Pang et al. (2012, 2018). The results provide predictions for \(B\) meson and beauty-decay electron observables that can be tested by future STAR measurements with the Heavy Flavor Tracker, and propose the angular-dependent \(R_\mathrm{AA}\) ratio as a new probe of the longitudinal tilt of the initial QGP energy density.
