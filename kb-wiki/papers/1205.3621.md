# Hadron-Quark Crossover and Massive Hybrid Stars with Strangeness

> Using the idea of a smooth crossover from the hadronic matter with hyperons to quark matter with strangeness, we show that the maximum mass of neutron stars with quark matter core can be larger than those without quark matter core. This is in contrast to the conventional softening of equation of state due to exotic components at high density. Essential conditions to reach our conclusion are (i) the crossover takes place at relatively low densities, around 3 times the normal nuclear density, and (ii) the quark matter is strongly interacting in the crossover region. By these, the pressure of the system can be greater than that of purely hadronic matter in the crossover region and leads to the maximum mass greater than 2 solar mass. This conclusion is insensitive to the different choice of the hadronic equation of state with hyperons. Several implications of this result to the nuclear incompressibility, the hyperon mixing, and the neutrino cooling are also remarked.

## Key claim

Using a smooth crossover from hadronic matter with hyperons to quark matter with strangeness, the maximum mass of neutron stars with a quark core can exceed 2 solar masses, contrary to the conventional softening from exotic components. This result requires the crossover to occur at relatively low densities (around 3 times normal nuclear density) and the quark matter to be strongly interacting in the crossover region.

## Method

The authors constructed a hadronic equation of state (EOS) with hyperons using G-matrix effective two-baryon potentials plus a universal three-body repulsion (TNI3u, with incompressibility κ=300 MeV). For quark matter, they used the (2+1)-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a vector coupling g_V/G_S ranging from 0 to 1.5. The hadronic and quark EOSs were interpolated smoothly using a hyperbolic tangent function with central crossover density ρ̄ and width Γ, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation was solved to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with TNI3u hadronic EOS and NJL quark EOS at (ρ̄, Γ) = (3ρ₀, ρ₀) and g_V/G_S = 1.0: M_max = 2.07 M_⊙ (central density 5.9 ρ₀)
- Maximum mass with same parameters but g_V/G_S = 1.5: M_max = 2.18 M_⊙ (central density 5.6 ρ₀)
- Maximum mass for purely hadronic TNI3u EOS (no quark core): M_max = 1.82 M_⊙
- Maximum mass for purely hadronic AV18+TBF+ΛΣ EOS: M_max ≈ 1.22 M_⊙ (central density 7.35 ρ₀)
- Maximum mass for purely hadronic SCL3ΛΣ EOS: M_max ≈ 1.36 M_⊙ (central density 5.89 ρ₀)
- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: M = (1.97 ± 0.04) M_⊙
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Onset density of strange quarks in the NJL model: ρ ≈ 4ρ₀, insensitive to g_V
- For crossover at (ρ̄, Γ) = (4ρ₀, ρ₀) with g_V/G_S = 1.0: M_max = 1.93 M_⊙ (central density 6.7 ρ₀)
- For crossover at (ρ̄, Γ) = (4ρ₀, ρ₀) with g_V/G_S = 1.5: M_max = 2.00 M_⊙ (central density 6.6 ρ₀)
- Radius for hybrid stars with g_V/G_S ≳ 1 and masses 0.5–2 M_⊙: approximately constant at 11–11.5 km

## Context

This work extends the hadron-quark crossover concept from Baym (1979) and Celik (1980) on hadron percolation, and from Baym (2008) and Maeda (2009) on hadron-quark crossover, applying it to neutron star structure. It contradicts the conventional Maxwell or Gibbs construction for first-order hadron-quark transitions (e.g., Burgio 2002), which always softens the EOS and reduces maximum mass. The results enable reconciliation of massive neutron stars (M > 2 M_⊙) with experimental nuclear incompressibility κ = (240 ± 20) MeV from Shlomo (2006), and suggest that hyperon direct Urca cooling can be avoided except in the most massive stars.
