# Identifying neutrinos and antineutrinos in neutral-current scattering reactions

> We study neutrino-induced nucleon knockout from nuclei. Expressions for the induced polarization are derived within the framework of the independent-nucleon model and the non-relativistic plane-wave approximation. Large dissimilarities in the nucleon polarization asymmetries are observed between neutrino- and antineutrino-induced processes. These asymmetries represent a potential way to distinguish between neutrinos and antineutrinos in neutral-current neutrino-scattering on nuclei. We discuss astrophysical applications of these polarization asymmetries. Our findings are illustrated for neutrino scattering on $^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb.

# Identifying neutrinos and antineutrinos in neutral-current scattering reactions

## Key claim

The longitudinal polarization asymmetry of ejected nucleons in neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering provides a potential way to distinguish between neutrinos and antineutrinos, with the effect being largest for forward nucleon knockout where most reaction strength resides. The authors hedge that while correlations and final-state interactions could distort the picture, these secondary effects tend to cancel when addressing ratios like the asymmetry, and the crucial information is revealed by the asymmetry's sign rather than absolute cross sections.

## Method

The authors derived expressions for neutrino- and antineutrino-induced nucleon knockout cross sections and nucleon polarization within the independent-nucleon model using the non-relativistic nuclear shell model with a Woods-Saxon description for bound nucleon states and a plane-wave approximation for ejected nucleons. They computed the longitudinal polarization asymmetry A_l = [sigma(s_N^l=up) - sigma(s_N^l=down)] / [sigma(s_N^l=up) + sigma(s_N^l=down)] for neutral-current scattering on 16O and 208Pb targets over incoming neutrino energies from 10 MeV to 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For neutrino scattering on 208Pb, the cross section is dominated by s_N^l=down nucleons; for antineutrino scattering, the cross section is characterized by a prominence of s_N^l=up nucleons.
- For forward nucleon knockout (theta_N=0) on 16O, observing a forward nucleon with spin antiparallel to its momentum makes it roughly a factor 15 more probable that a neutrino induced the reaction.
- The large asymmetry effect remains over an angular range of approximately 60 degrees.
- The asymmetry is predicted to be very large at incoming neutrino energies as low as 25 MeV.
- Calculations were performed for incoming neutrino energies from 10 MeV up to 500 MeV.
- A neutrino-nucleus based detector would be most sensitive to neutrinos with energies around 40 MeV (cited from Jachowicz and Heyde, 2003).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of neutrino scattering from hadronic matter using Fermi gas, random phase approximation, and shell model calculations (Jachowicz and Heyde, 2003; Kolbe et al., 1992; Kim et al., 1994; Engel et al., 1996; Alberico et al., 1997; Singh et al., 1998; Jachowicz et al., 1999; Fuller et al., 1999; Hayes and Towner, 2000; Volpe et al., 2000; Jachowicz et al., 2002; Volpe et al., 2002; Engel et al., 2003), which paid little or no attention to polarization degrees of freedom for ejected nucleons. The findings enable a new experimental handle for supernova neutrino detection, where distinguishing neutrinos from antineutrinos in neutral-current reactions could provide additional information about the time and energy distribution of arriving neutrinos and their luminosities, particularly for muon and tau neutrinos that do not participate in charged-current reactions at typical supernova energies.
