# Models of Meson-Baryon Reactions in the Nucleon Resonance Region

> It is shown that most of the models for analyzing meson-baryon reactions in the nucleon resonance region can be derived from a Hamiltonian formulation of the problem. An extension of the coupled-channel approach to include $ππN$ channel is briefly described and some preliminary results for the $N^*(1535)$ excitation are presented.

## Key claim

Most models for analyzing meson-baryon reactions in the nucleon resonance region can be derived from a single Hamiltonian formulation, but the extracted resonance parameters (such as the bare and dressed helicity amplitudes for the N*(1535)) depend significantly on how coupled-channel effects are calculated, as shown by the differences between the DMT and LMS results.

## Method

The authors present a Hamiltonian formulation where the interaction is split into a non-resonant (background) term from tree-diagram mechanisms and a resonant term describing N* excitation via a sum over resonance poles. They then show how this formulation reduces to specific models (Unitary Isobar Model, multi-channel K-matrix models, the CMB model, and dynamical models) by making different approximations to the coupled-channel equations, such as using on-shell K-matrix relations, separable interactions, or dispersion relations. An extension to include the ππN channel explicitly is described, using a vertex interaction for ρ→ππ and σ→ππ decays and a non-resonant ππ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The γN→Δ M1 transition strength extracted from amplitude analyses is G_M(0) = 3.18 ± 0.04, about 40% larger than the constituent quark model prediction.
- For the N*(1535) resonance, the DMT model finds M_R = 1528 ± 1 MeV, Γ_R = 95 ± 5 MeV, Γ_π/Γ_R = 40 ± 1%, and a dressed helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = 81 ± 3 (bare A_{1/2} = 108 ± 4).
- For the N*(1535) resonance, the LMS model finds M_R = 1538 MeV, Γ_R = 122 MeV, Γ_π/Γ_R = 36%, and a dressed helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = 61.24 (bare A_{1/2} = 77.64).
- The quark model prediction for the N*(1535)→γN helicity amplitude A_{1/2} is 76.
- The Juelich group describes πN phase shifts up to W = 1.9 GeV by including 5 channels and 5 N* resonances: P33(1232), S11(1535), S11(1530), S11(1650), and D13(1520).
- Chen et al. find that four N* are needed to fit the empirical amplitudes in the S11 channel up to W = 2 GeV.

## Context

This paper provides a unified theoretical framework that connects and contrasts existing models such as MAID (Mainz group), SAID, the Giessen model, the KSU model, the CMB model (Carnegie-Mellon Berkeley collaboration), and dynamical models like Sato-Lee (SL) and Dubna-Mainz-Taiwan (DMT). The work extends previous coupled-channel approaches by explicitly incorporating the ππN channel to satisfy three-body unitarity, which is necessary for reliably extracting N* parameters in the second and third resonance regions. The preliminary results for the N*(1535) show that the meson cloud reduces the bare helicity amplitude to the dressed value, opposite to the enhancement seen in the Δ region, and the authors note that further inclusion of ρN and σN channels is needed to resolve the second S11 resonance near 1.6 GeV.
