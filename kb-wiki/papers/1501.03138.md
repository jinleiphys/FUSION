# Subleading harmonic flows in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions

> We perform a principal component analysis (PCA) of $v_3(p_T)$ in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The PCA procedure identifies two dominant contributions to the two-particle correlation function, which together capture 99.9\% of the squared variance. We find that the subleading flow (which is the largest source of flow factorization breaking in hydrodynamics) is predominantly a response to the radial excitations of a third-order eccentricity. We present a systematic study of the hydrodynamic response to these radial excitations in 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics. Finally, we construct a good geometrical predictor for the orientation angle and magnitude of the leading and subleading flows using two Fourier modes of the initial geometry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the subleading principal component of triangular flow \(v_3(p_T)\) in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations is predominantly a response to radial excitations of the third-order eccentricity, and that this subleading mode is the largest source of flow factorization breaking in hydrodynamics; this claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis is limited to the third harmonic and boost-invariant 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics with specific Glauber initial conditions.

## Method

The authors performed principal component analysis (PCA) on the covariance matrix \(\llangle V_3(p_{T1}) V_3^*(p_{T2}) \rrangle\) from 5000 event-by-event boost-invariant 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, using a Glauber Monte Carlo initial condition with a two-component entropy deposition model. They then correlated the leading and subleading principal components with Fourier modes of the initial triangular geometry \(S_3(k)\) and constructed linear predictors using two or five wave numbers to predict the magnitude and orientation of each flow component. Finally, they tested the linear response assumption by comparing the event-by-event averaged response to single-shot hydrodynamic simulations of the averaged initial geometry in the principal component planes.

## Key numbers

* The first two eigenmodes together capture 99.9% of the squared variance of the triangular flow covariance matrix.
* In central collisions, the scaled magnitudes of the first four principal components are \(\|v_3^{(1)}\| = 1.5 \times 10^{-2}\), \(\|v_3^{(2)}\| = 2.6 \times 10^{-3}\), \(\|v_3^{(3)}\| = 4.8 \times 10^{-4}\), and \(\|v_3^{(4)}\| = 1.1 \times 10^{-4}\).
* The subleading flow magnitude \(\|v_3^{(2)}\|\) is approximately 2.6 × 10^{-3} in central collisions, about a factor of 6 smaller than the leading mode.
* The two-term predictor based on Fourier modes at \(k_b R_o = j_{3,1} \simeq 6.38\) and \(j_{3,2} \simeq 9.76\) with \(R_o \simeq 3 R_{\rm rms}\) achieves high correlation with the subleading flow for 0–40% centrality.
* The single-shot hydrodynamic simulation misses the event-by-event subleading flow curve by approximately 30% at \(p_T \sim 1.2\) GeV in central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the principal component analysis framework introduced by Bhalerao et al. (2014) to the third harmonic \(v_3(p_T)\), and clarifies the origin of flow factorization breaking previously studied by Gardim et al. (2012) and Heinz et al. (2013). The paper demonstrates that the subleading triangular flow arises from radial excitations of the triangular geometry, which are poorly captured by the standard eccentricity \(\varepsilon_3\) alone, and constructs improved geometric predictors using two Fourier modes. This enables future work to systematically study subleading flows for other harmonics and to use these modes as more precise constraints on both the initial state and the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
