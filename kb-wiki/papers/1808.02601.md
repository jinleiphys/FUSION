# Constraints on the hybrid equation of state with a crossover hadron-quark phase transition in the light of GW170817

> In this paper, we use the recent updated source properties of GW170817 to constrain the hybrid equation of state (EOS) constructed by a three-window modeling between the hadronic EOS and quark EOS. Specifically, the hadronic EOS is described by NL3$ωρ$ model whose corresponding pure neutron star (NS) is already excluded by the constraint of tidal deformability (TD) from GW170817, and the quark EOS is calculated with 2+1 flavors Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. We also consider other four constraints on the hybrid EOS. As a result, we find the parameter set ($B^{\frac{1}{4}}, \tildeμ, Γ$) can be well constrained, indicating the possible existence of the hybrid star (HS) with a crossover inside. The type of the two stars in the binary system for nine representative hybrid EOSs is shown in this paper too. Furthermore, the HSs restricted by five constraints do not suggest a pure quark core but a mixed-phase in center.

## Key claim
The hybrid equation of state (EOS) constructed via a three-window crossover between hadronic (NL3ωρ) and quark (NJL) matter can be constrained by GW170817 tidal deformability data, but the resulting hybrid stars do not contain a pure quark core; instead, they feature a mixed-phase center. The parameter set (B^(1/4), μ̃, Γ) is well constrained only when five simultaneous constraints are applied, including the loose condition that the deconfinement chemical potential exceeds the chiral restoration scale (~1 GeV).

## Method
The hadronic EOS was described by the NL3ωρ relativistic mean-field model, and the quark EOS was computed using a 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization. A three-window interpolating approach (sigmoid functions in the pressure-chemical potential plane) connected the two phases, introducing three free parameters: the bag constant B^(1/4), the central chemical potential μ̃, and the half-width Γ of the crossover window. The hybrid EOS was then subjected to five constraints: (1) maximum mass > 1.97 M⊙ from PSR J0348+0432; (2) μ̃ − Γ ≥ 1 GeV; (3) the combined tidal deformability Λ̃ from GW170817 (280^(+490)_(−190) for symmetric 90% credible interval); (4) thermodynamic stability dP/dρ > 0; and (5) stability of the heaviest hybrid star requiring μ_center > μ_binding-energy intersection.

## Key numbers
- The allowed range of B^(1/4) is restricted to (166.16, 171.06) MeV.
- For B^(1/4) = 166.16 MeV, the allowed intervals are Γ ∈ (1.47, 2.51) GeV and μ̃ ∈ (2.47, 3.51) GeV; for B^(1/4) = 171.06 MeV, Γ and μ̃ are each constrained to 3.37 GeV and 4.37 GeV respectively.
- For nine representative hybrid EOSs, maximum gravitational masses range from 2.10 M⊙ to 2.19 M⊙, with radii from 11.99 km to 12.13 km.
- The radius of hybrid stars with mass 1.4 M⊙ is from 11.90 km to 12.18 km.
- The chirp mass from GW170817 is fixed to 1.186 ± 0.0001 M⊙, giving component masses of 1.17 M⊙–1.36 M⊙ and 1.36 M⊙–1.59 M⊙.
- All sound velocities of the hybrid stars are smaller than 0.7 times the speed of light.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of hybrid stars with a crossover hadron-quark transition (Masuda 2013, Kojo 2016) by applying the updated GW170817 tidal deformability constraints from Abbott (2018). It contrasts with first-order phase-transition approaches (e.g., PhysRevD.97.084038) and Gibbs-construction smooth transitions (e.g., 0004-637X-857-1-12). The results enable future multi-messenger constraints on the crossover parameter space and suggest that the two stars in GW170817 could both be hybrid stars with mixed-phase interiors, not pure quark cores.
