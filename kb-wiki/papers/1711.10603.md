# Uncertainties and understanding of experimental and theoretical results regarding reactions forming heavy and superheavy nuclei

> Experimental and theoretical results of the $P_{\mathrm{CN}}$ fusion probability of reactants in the entrance channel and the $W_{\mathrm{sur}}$ survival probability against fission at deexcitation of the compound nucleus formed in heavy-ion collisions are discussed. The theoretical results for a set of nuclear reactions leading to formation of compound nuclei (CNs) with the charge number $Z=102\text{--}122$ reveal a strong sensitivity of $P_{\mathrm{CN}}$ to the characteristics of colliding nuclei in the entrance channel, dynamics of the reaction mechanism, and excitation energy of the system. We discuss the validity of assumptions and procedures for analysis of experimental data, and also the limits of validity of theoretical results obtained by the use of phenomenological models. The comparison of results obtained in many investigated reactions reveals serious limits of validity of the data analysis and calculation procedures.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the systematic discrepancy between experimental and theoretical values of the fusion probability \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}\) for reactions forming superheavy nuclei (Z=102-122) is primarily due to ambiguities in experimental data analysis procedures, particularly the incorrect separation of quasifission events from deep-inelastic collisions and fusion-fission events, rather than solely due to deficiencies in theoretical models.

## Method

The authors compare experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}\) values extracted from measured capture and fusion cross sections with theoretical \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}\) values calculated using the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which explicitly accounts for the competition between complete fusion and quasifission processes. The theoretical calculations include the dependence of capture cross sections on the orientation angles of colliding nuclei, mass asymmetry parameter, center-of-mass energy, and orbital angular momentum. The analysis covers reactions leading to compound nuclei with charge numbers Z=102-122, using data from multiple experimental groups and theoretical calculations from the authors' own work and other published models.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) reaction leading to \(^{256}_{102}\mathrm{No}\), the experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.87\) at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}\approx 30\) MeV (from AIP2006, ItNPA2007) while the theoretical value is \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.027\) at the same excitation energy (from FazioMPL2005)
- For the \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+^{244}\mathrm{Pu}\) reaction leading to \(^{292}_{114}\mathrm{CN}\), the experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.08\) at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}=32\) MeV (from AIP2006, ItNPA2007) while the theoretical value from the present paper is \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.008\) at the same excitation energy
- For the \(^{86}\mathrm{Kr}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) reaction leading to \(^{294}_{118}\mathrm{CN}\), the experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.032\) at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}\approx 30\) MeV (from AIP2006, ItNPA2007) while the theoretical value is \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=2\times 10^{-5}\) at the same excitation energy (from JMPE2010)
- For the \(^{58}\mathrm{Fe}+^{248}\mathrm{Cm}\) reaction leading to \(^{306}_{122}\mathrm{CN}\), the experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=0.07\) at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}=33\) MeV (from AIP2006, ItNPA2007) while the theoretical value is \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}=7\times 10^{-6}\) at the same excitation energy (from Adamian2003)
- For the \(^{26}\mathrm{Mg}+^{248}\mathrm{Cm}\) reaction leading to \(^{274}_{108}\mathrm{CN}\), the theoretical \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}\) decreases from 0.87 at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}=37\) MeV to 0.12 at \(E^{*}_{\mathrm{CN}}=60\) MeV (present paper)
- Varying the radius parameter \(r_0\) from 1.16 fm to 1.18 fm changes the fusion probability by about 2 times at fixed low beam energy

## Context

This work extends the discussion of fusion probability systematics presented in Lovehagen (2015) by covering a wider range of compound nuclei (Z=102-122) and including both very asymmetric and almost symmetric reactions. The paper directly addresses discrepancies between experimental \(P_{\mathrm{CN}}\) values from AIP2006 and ItNPA2007 and theoretical calculations from Adamian (2003), Wang (2011), Zhu (2014), and the authors' own previous work (FazioMPL2005, JMPE2010, PRC84, NasiEPJ49, PRC79). The analysis enables a critical reassessment of experimental data analysis procedures, particularly regarding the separation of quasifission events from deep-inelastic collisions and fusion-fission events, and highlights the need for more reliable experimental determination of capture and fusion cross sections to properly understand entrance channel effects on superheavy element formation.
