# A New Family of Simple Solutions of Perfect Fluid Hydrodynamics

> A new class of accelerating, exact and explicit solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics is found - more than 50 years after the previous similar result, the Landau-Khalatnikov solution. Surprisingly, the new solutions have a simple form, that generalizes the renowned, but accelerationless, Hwa-Bjorken solution. These new solutions take into account the work done by the fluid elements on each other, and work not only in one temporal and one spatial dimensions, but also in arbitrary number of spatial dimensions. They are applied here for an advanced estimation of initial energy density and life-time of the reaction in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
A new class of accelerating, exact, and explicit solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics is found, generalizing the accelerationless Hwa-Bjorken solution. The solutions are valid in arbitrary spatial dimensions and account for work done by fluid elements on each other, but they are limited to smooth initial conditions (not step functions like the Landau-Khalatnikov solution) and to specific combinations of the acceleration parameter λ, spatial dimension d, and speed-of-sound parameter κ listed in Table 1.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic Euler equation and energy conservation equation in Rindler coordinates (τ, η) for a perfect fluid with an equation of state ε − B = κ(p + B), where κ = 1/c_s². They assumed a velocity profile v = tanh(λη) and a pressure ansatz p = p₀(τ₀/τ)^{λ d (κ+1)/κ} [cosh(η/2)]^{-(d-1)φ_λ} − B, then identified the five parameter combinations (λ, d, κ, φ_λ) that satisfy the hydrodynamic equations. The entropy conservation equation was solved to obtain temperature and entropy density fields, introducing a scaling function ν_σ(s) with vanishing comoving derivative.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density estimate for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: ε_c = (2.0 ± 0.1) × ε_Bj = 10.0 ± 0.5 GeV/fm³ at τ₀ = 1 fm/c, using τ_f/τ₀ = 8 ± 2 fm/c and ε_Bj = 5 GeV/fm³ from the Bjorken estimate.
- Life-time correction factor from BRAHMS data: λ = 1.18 ± 0.01, implying τ_c = λ τ_Bj, an 18 ± 1% increase in estimated freeze-out time.
- Gaussian width of rapidity distribution: Δy² = α / (m/T_f ∓ 1/2 + 1/α), with α = (2λ − 1)/(λ − 1), valid for m/T_f ≫ 1 and λ > 0.5.
- Table 1 lists five solution families: (a) λ=1 (Hwa-Bjorken), (b) λ=2, κ=d, (c) λ arbitrary, d=1, κ=1, (d) λ=1/2, κ=1, (e) λ=3/2, κ=(4d−1)/3.

## Context
This work extends the 1+1 dimensional Landau-Khalatnikov solution (Landau 1953, Khalatnikov 1954) and the accelerationless Hwa-Bjorken solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) by providing accelerating exact solutions in 1+1 and 1+d dimensions. The new solutions describe BRAHMS rapidity distribution data (Bearden 2004) better than either the Landau-Khalatnikov or Hwa-Bjorken solutions, which respectively predict Gaussian or flat distributions. The results enable improved estimates of initial energy density and reaction lifetime in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and potentially at SPS and LHC, and can serve as test cases for numerical hydrodynamic codes.
