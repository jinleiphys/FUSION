# AC-driven Quantum Phase Transition in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model

> We establish a set of nonequilibrium quantum phase transitions in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model under monochromatic modulation of the inter-particle interaction. We show that the external driving induces a rich phase diagram that characterizes the multistability in the system. Interestingly, the number of stable configurations can be tuned by increasing the amplitude of the driving field. Furthermore, by studying the quantum evolution, we demonstrate that the system exhibits a set of quantum phases that correspond to dynamically stabilized states.

# AC-driven Quantum Phase Transition in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model

## Key claim

The paper establishes that monochromatic modulation of the inter-particle interaction in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model induces nonequilibrium quantum phase transitions with a rich phase diagram exhibiting multistability, but this result is derived within the rotating wave approximation and the thermodynamic limit, and the novel phases correspond to local minima of the quasienergy landscape rather than true ground states, meaning they are dynamically stabilized metastable states rather than equilibrium phases.

## Method

The authors start from the periodically-driven Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Hamiltonian with a monochromatic modulation of the x-axis interaction strength, perform a Holstein-Primakoff bosonization in the thermodynamic limit to obtain a parametrically-driven harmonic oscillator description of the symmetric phase, and then apply a rotating wave approximation to derive a time-independent effective Hamiltonian in a rotating frame. They analyze the quasienergy landscape obtained from the bosonized effective Hamiltonian in the thermodynamic limit, identifying local minima as metastable phases, and verify the dynamical trapping by numerically integrating the Schrodinger equation for finite systems of N=100 particles initialized in spin coherent states.

## Key numbers

- The resonance condition for m-photon transitions is given by 2*epsilon = m*Omega, where epsilon is the characteristic energy scale of the undriven system and Omega is the driving frequency.
- The detuning from resonance is defined as delta^(m) = -h - m*Omega/2, with the condition delta^(m), gamma_0^x, gamma_y much less than Omega for the rotating wave approximation to hold.
- The eigenvalues of the Jacobian matrix at the origin of the quasienergy landscape are lambda_1 = -2(h + gamma_y) and lambda_2 = -2h - 2*gamma_0^x - (h + gamma_y)*(gamma_1^x/Omega)^2.
- The boundary where the origin transitions from saddle point to local maximum occurs at gamma_1^x = Omega * sqrt((2h + 2*gamma_0^x)/(-h - gamma_y)).
- Numerical simulations are performed for N=100 particles with parameters h/|h| = -1, gamma_0^x/|h| = -1, gamma_1^x/|h| = 210, gamma_y/|h| = 2, and Omega/|h| = 40.
- The undriven second-order quantum phase transition occurs at the critical line gamma_y = -h.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum phase transitions in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model by introducing time-periodic driving, building on earlier investigations of driven quantum systems such as the Dicke model by Bastidas (2012) and the quantum Ising model by Bastidas (2012). The authors contrast their approach with prior work on adiabatic parameter changes across the quantum critical point by Mosseri, fast and slow quenches by Santoro, and periodically-driven uniaxial LMG models by Hangui, Chakrabarti, and Dykman. The results enable experimental exploration of nonequilibrium criticality in systems where the LMG model can be realized, including optical cavity QED as suggested by Parkins and circuit QED as suggested by Larson, and open a route to studying dynamically stabilized phases that have no analogue in equilibrium systems.
