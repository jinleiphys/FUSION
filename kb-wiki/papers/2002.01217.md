# QCD equation of state at vanishing and high baryon density: Chiral Mean Field model

> The thermodynamic properties of high temperature and high density QCD-matter are studied using the Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field model, CMF. The CMF model provides a proper description of lattice QCD data, heavy-ions physics, and static neutron stars. The behavior of lines of constant pressure with increase of baryon density is discussed. The rapid change of pressure behavior at $μ_B/T\approx3$ suggests a strong contribution of baryons to thermodynamic properties at this region. The position of this region is very close to the radius of convergence for a Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure. The role of mesons and unstable hadrons in the hydrodynamic expansion of strongly interacting matter is also discussed.

## Key claim

The Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field (CMF) model predicts that the Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure around zero baryon chemical potential is reliable only up to baryon densities \(n_B \lesssim 2 n_0\) at temperatures \(T < 150\) MeV, because the rapid change in pressure behavior at \(\mu_B/T \approx 3\) (close to the Roberge-Weiss value \(\mu_B/T = \pi\)) marks the limit where baryon contributions become dominant.

## Method

The authors employed the CMF model, a mean-field approach that includes all known hadrons with masses below 2.6 GeV and the three light quark flavors, with interactions mediated by mesonic mean fields (scalar \(\sigma, \zeta\) and vector \(\omega, \rho, \phi\)) and a Polyakov-loop potential tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at \(\mu_B = 0\). The model incorporates parity doubling for the lowest baryon octet, excluded volume corrections for hadron suppression at high densities, and a quark contribution controlled by the Polyakov loop order parameter. Lines of constant pressure (isobars) and isentropic trajectories were computed across the \(n_B\)-\(T\) plane to study the equation of state at finite baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The rapid change in pressure behavior occurs at \(\mu_B/T \approx 3\), which is close to the Roberge-Weiss value \(\mu_B/T = \pi\).
- The CMF model predicts two first-order phase transitions: one associated with nuclear liquid-vapor at densities up to \(n_0\) (normal nuclear saturation density), and another at about four times normal nuclear density due to chiral symmetry restoration; both have critical point temperatures \(T_{CP} \approx 20\) MeV.
- The excluded volume parameter is set to \(v_B = 1\) fm\(^3\) for all baryons and \(v_M = 1/8\) fm\(^3\) for all mesons.
- At temperatures \(T \approx 150\) MeV, the Taylor expansion is reliable up to baryon densities \(n_B \lesssim 2 n_0\), and this range decreases for smaller temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the CMF model previously developed by Papazoglou (1998), Steinheimer (2010), and Motornenko (2019) to systematically study the QCD equation of state at both vanishing and high baryon density. The results directly address limitations of lattice QCD calculations, which are restricted to small \(\mu_B/T\) due to the sign problem and Taylor expansion convergence issues identified by Bazavov (2017) and Vovchenko (2017). The findings enable improved hydrodynamic simulations of low-energy heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers by providing an equation of state that includes the full hadron spectrum, and they highlight that omitting mesons and baryonic resonances in such simulations leads to an artificial increase in temperature.
