# The Glueball in a Chiral Linear Sigma Model with Vector Mesons

> We present a two-flavour linear sigma model with global chiral symmetry and (axial-)vector mesons as well as an additional glueball degree of freedom. We study the structure of the well-established scalar resonances f0(1370) and f0(1500): by a fit to experimentally known decay widths we find that f0(1370) is predominantly a \bar{q}q state and f0(1500) is predominantly a glueball state. The overall phenomenology of these two resonances can be well described. Other assignments for our mixed quarkonium-glueball states are also tested, but turn out to be in worse agreement with the phenomenology. As a by-product of our analysis, the gluon condensate is determined.

# The Glueball in a Chiral Linear Sigma Model with Vector Mesons

## Key claim
The resonance f0(1500) is predominantly a glueball state (76% glueball, 24% quark-antiquark) and f0(1370) is predominantly a quark-antiquark state, but this assignment depends on a fit to experimental decay widths that yields a very small chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.29; alternative assignments (such as f0(1710) as glueball or f0(600) as quarkonium) are tested but produce worse agreement with phenomenology.

## Method
The authors construct a two-flavor linear sigma model with global chiral symmetry, including (axial-)vector mesons and a dilaton/glueball field, where the glueball is introduced via a logarithmic dilaton potential that implements scale symmetry breaking. They perform a chi-squared fit of four free parameters (the gluon condensate C, the bare quarkonium mass M_sigma, the pure glueball mass m_G, and the vector meson mass parameter m_1) to five experimental observables: the masses of f0(1370) and f0(1500), and the decay widths of f0(1500) into pi pi, eta eta, and K anti-K. Decays into mesons containing strange quarks are calculated using SU(3) flavor symmetry despite the N_f=2 framework.

## Key numbers
- Mixing angle between quarkonium and glueball: theta = (29.7 +/- 3.6) degrees, implying f0(1500) is 76% glueball and 24% quark-antiquark
- Gluon condensate from fit: C = (699 +/- 40) MeV, favoring the upper range of the literature interval (300-600 MeV)^4
- Pure glueball mass from fit: m_G = (1369 +/- 26) MeV
- Bare quarkonium mass from fit: M_sigma = (1275 +/- 30) MeV
- f0(1500) decay widths: pi pi = (38 +/- 5) MeV (experiment: 38.04 +/- 4.95 MeV), eta eta = (5.3 +/- 1.3) MeV (experiment: 5.56 +/- 1.34 MeV), K anti-K = (9.3 +/- 1.7) MeV (experiment: 9.37 +/- 1.69 MeV)
- f0(1370) decay width into pi pi: (284 +/- 43) MeV; into eta eta: (72 +/- 6) MeV; into K anti-K: (4.6 +/- 2.1) MeV
- f0(1500) decay into 4 pi via rho rho: 30 MeV (experiment: 54.0 +/- 7.1 MeV)
- f0(1370) mass from fit: M_sigma' = (1191 +/- 26) MeV (experimental range: 1200-1500 MeV)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the preferred scenario: 0.29

## Context
This work extends the linear sigma model with (axial-)vector mesons developed by Paper1 (2010) and earlier studies by including a dilaton/glueball field, building on the first attempt to incorporate a glueball into a linear sigma model by Schechter (1980). It tests mixing scenarios previously discussed by Close (1990s) and others for the scalar resonances f0(1370), f0(1500), and f0(1710), but does so for the first time above 1 GeV within a chirally invariant model that includes vector mesons. The results enable future extensions to N_f=3, inclusion of tetraquark states, and studies of chiral and dilatation symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature and density.
