# Phi meson propagation in a hot hadronic gas

> The Hidden Local Symmetry Lagrangian is used to study the interactions of phi mesons with other pseudoscalar and vector mesons in a hadronic gas at finite temperature. We have found a significantly small phi mean free path (less than 2.4 fm at T > 170 MeV) due to large collision rates with rho mesons, kaons and predominantly K* in spite of their heavy mass. This implies that phi mesons produced after hadronization in relativistic heavy ion collisions will not leave the hadronic system without scattering. The effect of these interactions on the time evolution of the phi density in the expanding hadronic fireball is investigated.

## Key claim

The authors find that the phi meson mean free path in a hot hadronic gas is significantly smaller than previously estimated (less than 2.4 fm at T > 170 MeV), contrary to the common belief that phi mesons interact weakly with the hadronic medium; this conclusion depends on using the Hidden Local Symmetry Lagrangian which includes interaction mechanisms not present in calculations relying only on couplings extracted from observed decays.

## Method

The authors used the Hidden Local Symmetry Lagrangian to compute tree-level amplitudes for all possible binary reactions of phi mesons with pseudoscalar mesons (pion, kaon) and vector mesons (rho, omega, K*, phi) in a hot hadronic gas. They calculated collision rates by integrating the cross sections over Boltzmann-distributed momenta, including form factors with a cutoff parameter of 1.8 GeV for t-channel diagrams and implementing an approximate unitarization for contact amplitudes via a K-matrix inspired ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Phi mean free path is less than 2.4 fm at temperatures above 170 MeV
- At T = 200 MeV, the ratio of K* to kaon number densities is 0.77
- The coupling constant g = 5.89 obtained from the rho mass using a = 2 and f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- The parameter c_V = 0.36 determined from meson masses, c_A = 0.49 from f_K/f_pi = 1.22, and c_WZ = -0.1 from Ref. Bramon (1995)
- The free decay width Gamma_0 = 3.7 MeV for phi decay into kaon pairs (83% of total width)
- The cutoff parameter Lambda = 1.8 GeV used for all form factors

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Ko (1994), Haglin (1995), and Smith (1998) who found a much larger phi mean free path (lambda = 4.4 fm at T = 200 MeV in Haglin's calculation) using phenomenological Lagrangians with couplings extracted from experimental partial decay rates. The present result contradicts the common assumption from Shor (1985) that phi mesons produced after hadronization would leave the hadronic system without scattering, and provides a possible explanation for discrepancies observed at CERN-SPS between phi yields measured via muon pairs versus kaon pairs. The work enables more realistic modeling of phi meson evolution in expanding hadronic fireballs using rate equations with chemical nonequilibrium.
