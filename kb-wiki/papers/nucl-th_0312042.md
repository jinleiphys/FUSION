# Chiral Sigma Model with Pion Mean Field in Finite Nuclei

> The properties of infinite matter and finite nuclei are studied by using the chiral sigma model in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory. We reconstruct an extended chiral sigma model in which the omega meson mass is generated dynamically by the sigma condensation in the vacuum in the same way as the nucleon mass. All the parameters of chiral sigma model are essentially fixed from the hadron properties in the free space. In nuclear matter, the saturation property comes out right, but the incompressibility is too large and the scalar and vector potentials are about a half of the phenomenological ones, respectively. This fact is reflected to the properties of finite nuclei. We calculate N = Z even-even mass nuclei between N = 16 and N = 34. The extended chiral sigma model without the pion mean field leads to the result that the magic number appears at N = 18 instead of N = 20 and the magic number does not appear at N = 28 due to the above mentioned nuclear matter properties. The latter problem, however, could be removed by the introduction of the finite pion mean field with the appearance of the magic number at N = 28. We find that the energy differences between the spin-orbit partners are reproduced by the finite pion mean field which is completely a different mechanism from the standard spin-orbit interaction.

## Key claim

The extended chiral sigma model without pion mean field fails to reproduce the magic numbers at N=20 and N=28 in finite nuclei, but introducing a finite pion mean field restores the magic number at N=28 and reproduces spin-orbit splitting through a mechanism completely different from the standard spin-orbit interaction, though the model still yields too large incompressibility and scalar/vector potentials about half the phenomenological values.

## Method

The authors constructed an extended chiral sigma (ECS) model within relativistic mean field theory, where the omega meson mass is generated dynamically by sigma condensation in the vacuum analogously to the nucleon mass. They solved the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently for N=Z even-even nuclei between N=16 and N=34, first without and then with a finite pion mean field that mixes parity in the single-particle wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ = 0.141 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -16.1 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 650 MeV (compared to 281 MeV for RMF with TM1 parameter set)
- Sigma meson mass: m_σ = 777 MeV; omega-nucleon coupling: g_ω = 7.03 (adjusted to saturation)
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV; nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV; omega meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV; pion mass: m_π = 139 MeV
- Axial coupling constant used in finite nuclei: g_A = 1.15 (reduced from experimental 1.25 to reproduce ⁵⁶Ni binding)
- Ratio of spin-orbit volume integrals between ECS model and TM1 case: 0.48

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Boguta (1983) for dynamical omega meson mass generation and the finite pion mean field formalism developed by Toki et al. (2003) applied to the TM1 parameter set. The ECS model without pion mean field produces a magic number at N=18 instead of N=20 and fails to produce the N=28 shell gap, unlike standard RMF calculations with the TM1 parameter set (Sugahara and Toki, 1994). The inclusion of the pion mean field enables reproduction of the N=28 magic number and spin-orbit splitting through parity-mixed single-particle states, opening the possibility of describing nuclear shell structure from chiral symmetry principles rather than phenomenological spin-orbit potentials.
