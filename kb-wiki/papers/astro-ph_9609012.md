# Applicability Of The Hauser-Feshbach Approach For The Determination of Astrophysical Reaction Rates

> Nuclear Astrophysics requires the knowledge of reaction rates over a wide range of nuclei and temperatures. In recent calculations the nuclear level density - as an important ingredient to the statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) - has shown the highest uncertainties. In a back-shifted Fermi-gas formalism utilizing an energy-dependent level density parameter and employing microscopic corrections from a recent FRDM mass formula, we obtain a highly improved fit to experimental level densities. The resulting level density is used for determining criteria for the applicability of the statistical model on neutron-induced reactions.

## Key claim

The Hauser-Feshbach statistical model is applicable for neutron-induced reactions only when the effective energy window at a given stellar temperature contains at least 5–10 contributing resonances; the authors derive lower temperature limits for applicability using an improved level density description, but note that these limits are insensitive to the exact number of required resonances within a factor of two due to the exponential dependence of level density on excitation energy.

## Method

The authors employed a back-shifted Fermi-gas formalism with an energy-dependent level density parameter \(a(U,Z,N)\) and microscopic corrections from the FRDM mass formula, fitting three free parameters (\(\alpha\), \(\beta\), \(\gamma\)) to experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings at the neutron separation energy. They then computed the number of resonances within the effective energy window of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for neutron-induced reactions at a given stellar temperature, using a lower limit of 5–10 resonances to determine the minimum temperature for statistical model applicability.

## Key numbers

- Level density parameter fit values: \(\alpha = 0.1336\), \(\beta = -0.06712\), \(\gamma = 0.04862\), yielding an averaged global deviation of 1.5 from experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacings.
- The effective energy window for s-wave neutrons coincides with the peak of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at \(E_0 = kT\) (close to the neutron separation energy).
- A lower limit of 5–10 contributing resonances is sufficient for statistical model applicability, based on numerical test calculations.
- Temperature limits are presented in a figure (Fig. 1) for neutron-induced reactions across the nuclear chart, with temperatures given in units of \(T_9\) (10\(^9\) K).

## Context

This work extends prior statistical model calculations by Cowan (1980s) and others by improving the nuclear level density input, which had shown the largest uncertainties among properties entering the Hauser-Feshbach approach. The authors build on the back-shifted Fermi-gas model of Bethe (1937) and the energy-dependent level density parameter formalism of Ignatyuk (1975), using microscopic corrections from the FRDM mass formula of Moller (1995). The resulting applicability map enables users of astrophysical reaction rates to identify which neutron-induced reactions require special attention or experimental investigation, and the method can be extended to other projectiles and incident energy distributions.
