# Self-consistent description of $Λ$ hypernuclei in the quark-meson coupling model

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which has been successfully used to describe the properties of both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, is applied to a study of $Λ$ hypernuclei. With the assumption that the (self-consistent) exchanged scalar, and vector, mesons couple only to the u and d quarks, a very weak spin-orbit force in the $Λ$-nucleus interaction is achieved automatically. This is a direct consequence of the SU(6) quark model wave function of the $Λ$ used in the QMC model. Possible implications and extensions of the present investigation are also discussed.

## Key claim
The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, extended to flavor SU(3), naturally produces a very weak spin-orbit force for Λ hypernuclei without requiring large SU(3) breaking or tensor couplings, but the calculated single-particle energies tend to overestimate experimental binding energies, suggesting the need for a repulsive core from the Pauli principle at the quark level.

## Method
The authors applied the QMC model to Λ hypernuclei by treating both the Λ hyperon and the nucleons in the closed-shell core as three-quark clusters moving in self-consistently generated scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) mean fields, with the mesons coupling only to the u and d quarks. The equations of motion were solved in a relativistic mean-field approach using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, with the effective masses of the nucleon and Λ calculated from the MIT bag model and the spin-orbit force for the Λ arising entirely from Thomas precession.

## Key numbers
- The Λ single-particle energy for the 1s₁/₂ state in ¹⁷_ΛO is -20.5 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -12.5 MeV for ¹⁶_ΛO.
- The Λ single-particle energy for the 1s₁/₂ state in ⁴¹_ΛCa is -27.7 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -20.0 MeV for ⁴⁰_ΛCa.
- Using a scaled coupling constant of 0.93 × g_σ^Λ(σ=0) reproduces the empirical 1s₁/₂ energy of -20.0 MeV in ⁴¹_ΛCa.
- The spin-orbit splitting between the 1p₃/₂ and 1p₁/₂ states is 0.1 MeV for ¹⁷_ΛO and 0.1 MeV for ⁴¹_ΛCa.
- The bag parameters used are (z_N, z_Λ) = (3.295, 3.131), (R_N, R_Λ) = (0.800, 0.806) fm in free space, B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV, (m_u, m_d, m_s) = (5, 5, 250) MeV.
- The parametrization coefficient for the Λ is a_Λ = 9.25 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹.
- The difference between scalar and vector potentials near the center of the hypernuclei is typically 35-40 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the QMC model, previously applied to finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter by Guichon and by Tsushima, Saito, and Thomas (1997), to Λ hypernuclei. It directly addresses the long-standing puzzle of the weak spin-orbit force in Λ hypernuclei, previously explained by Brockman and Weise (1977) in a relativistic Hartree model with large SU(3) breaking, and by Noble (1979) using ω tensor couplings. The results enable future studies of Σ and Ξ hypernuclei and motivate incorporating the Pauli principle at the quark level, as suggested by Hungerford and Biedenharn (1985), to resolve the overbinding problem.
