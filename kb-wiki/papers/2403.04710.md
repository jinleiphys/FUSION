# Isomeric states of fission fragments explored via Penning trap mass spectrometry at IGISOL

> The masses of $^{84}$Br, $^{105}$Mo, $^{115,119,121}$Pd, $^{122}$Ag, $^{127,129}$In, $^{132}$Sb and their respective isomeric states have been measured with the JYFLTRAP Penning trap mass spectrometer using the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance technique. The excitation energies of the isomeric states in $^{132}$Sb and $^{119}$Pd were experimentally determined for the first time, while for $^{84}$Br, $^{115}$Pd and $^{127,129}$In, the precision of the mass values was substantially improved. In $^{105}$Mo and $^{121}$Pd there were no signs of a long-lived isomeric state. The ground-state measurements of $^{119}$Pd and $^{122}$Ag indicated that both are significantly more bound than the literature values. For $^{122}$Ag, there was no indication of a proposed third long-lived state. The results for the $N=49$ nucleus $^{84}$Br and isomers close to doubly magic $^{132}$Sn have been compared to the shell-model and the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reports mass measurements and isomer excitation energies for several neutron-rich fission fragments, claiming that the ground-state masses of $^{119}$Pd and $^{122}$Ag are significantly more bound than literature values, and that no evidence was found for a proposed third long-lived state in $^{122}$Ag or for isomeric states in $^{105}$Mo and $^{121}$Pd, with the caveat that upper limits on possible undetected isomer excitation energies depend on assumptions about production yields and half-lives.

## Method

The masses of $^{84}$Br, $^{105}$Mo, $^{115,119,121}$Pd, $^{122}$Ag, $^{127,129}$In, and $^{132}$Sb and their isomeric states were measured using the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the IGISOL facility, employing the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique. The radioactive ions were produced via 25-MeV proton-induced fission of $^{232}$Th or $^{nat}$U targets, stopped in a helium gas cell, purified by a dipole magnet and radio-frequency quadrupole cooler-buncher, and then injected into the Penning trap where cyclotron frequencies were determined with phase accumulation times ranging from 200 to 782.2 ms. Frequency ratios relative to reference ions ($^{133}$Cs$^+$, $^{85}$Rb$^+$, or isobaric species) were converted to atomic masses and isomer excitation energies, with systematic uncertainties from magnetic field fluctuations, count-rate effects, and mass-dependent corrections included.

## Key numbers

- $^{84}$Br ground-state mass excess: ME = -77767.1(16) keV, 25 times more precise than the literature value of -77783(40) keV (AME20).
- $^{84}$Br$^{m}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 193.6(15) keV, 67 times more precise than the literature value of 310(100) keV (NUBASE20).
- $^{105}$Mo mass excess: ME = -77332.8(21) keV, four times more precise than the literature value of -77331(9) keV (AME20); no isomeric state observed, with an upper limit of 25 keV for a hypothetical isomer if its production rate is at least 10% of the ground state.
- $^{115}$Pd ground-state mass excess: ME = -80420.5(18) keV, eight times more precise than the literature; isomer excitation energy $E_x$ = 86.8(29) keV, agreeing with the literature value of 89.21(16) keV.
- $^{119}$Pd ground-state mass excess: ME = -71609.7(42) keV, 203(9) keV more bound than the literature value of -71407(8) keV (AME20).
- $^{119}$Pd$^{m}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 199.1(30) keV, first experimental determination, compared to the NUBASE20 extrapolation of 300(150)# keV.
- $^{121}$Pd mass excess: ME = -66181.4(37) keV, agreeing with the literature value of -66182(3) keV; no isomeric state observed, with an upper limit of 35 keV for a hypothetical isomer if its fission yield is at least 40% of the ground state.
- $^{122}$Ag ground-state mass excess: ME = -71220.2(52) keV, 110(40) keV more bound than the AME20 value of -71030(60)# keV.
- $^{122}$Ag$^{m2}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 303.7(50) keV, compared to the NUBASE20 extrapolation of 80(50)# keV; no evidence for a third long-lived state.
- $^{127}$In$^{m1}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 407.9(50) keV, compared to the literature value of 394(18) keV (NUBASE20).
- $^{127}$In$^{m2}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 1728.7(12) keV, compared to the literature value of 1770(40) keV (NUBASE20).
- $^{129}$In$^{m1}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 449.1(59) keV, agreeing with the literature value of 450.73(16) keV.
- $^{129}$In$^{m2}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 1646.6(33) keV, agreeing with the literature value of 1650(50) keV.
- $^{132}$Sb ground-state mass excess: ME = -79629.6(27) keV, agreeing with the literature value of -79635.3(25) keV (AME20).
- $^{132}$Sb$^{m}$ isomer excitation energy: $E_x$ = 139.3(20) keV, first experimental determination, compared to the NUBASE20 extrapolation of 150(50)# keV.

## Context

This work extends previous Penning trap mass measurements of fission fragments at IGISOL using the PI-ICR technique, which had already been applied to Rh, Ru, Ag, In, and I isotopic chains (Hukkanen 2023, de Groote 2023, Ge 2024, Nesterenko 2020, Ruotsalainen 2023, Jaries 2023, Beliuskina 2024). The new results resolve ambiguities from earlier TOF-ICR measurements (Hager 2007) that could not separate low-lying isomers, and correct the ground-state mass of $^{119}$Pd, which was previously misidentified as the isomer in Hager (2007). The improved precision for $^{84}$Br and the isomers near doubly magic $^{132}$Sn enables comparisons with shell-model calculations using the jun45 interaction (Honma 2009) and microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model calculations, while the corrected $^{119}$Pd mass removes artificial staggering in two-neutron separation energies around N=73, indicating no significant structural changes in that region.
