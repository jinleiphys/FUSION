# Non-Equilibrium Aspects of Fission Dynamics within the Time Dependent Density Functional Theory

> We will cover briefly the time-dependent density functional theory approach, extended to include pairing correlations, to induced fission of $^{238}$U(n,f), $^{241,243}$Pu, and $^{238}$Np, and also results on scission neutrons and a number of very nontrivial aspects of induced fission.

# Non-Equilibrium Aspects of Fission Dynamics within the Time Dependent Density Functional Theory

## Key claim
The time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) extended to superfluid systems reveals that the Pauli blocking approximation, used for over six decades in nuclear physics to treat odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, is violated during fission dynamics: the single odd quasiparticle state initially occupied with probability 1 does not maintain that occupation probability over time, though exactly one canonical state remains occupied with probability 1 at each time step.

## Method
The authors performed TDDFT simulations extended to superfluid systems for induced fission of odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, using the SeaLL1 energy density functional in a 30²×60 simulation box with up to 60,000 time steps. They evolved 864,000 complex, coupled, nonlinear partial differential equations in 3D+time for 113 total trajectories across four nuclei: ²³⁹U (23 trajectories), ²⁴¹Pu (42 trajectories), ²⁴³Pu (13 trajectories), and the odd-odd nucleus ²³⁸Np (35 trajectories), each with various initial quadrupole and octupole deformations and odd quasiparticle states.

## Key numbers
- 113 total TDDFT trajectories computed for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei: ²³⁹U (23), ²⁴¹Pu (42), ²⁴³Pu (13), and ²³⁸Np (35)
- Simulation box dimensions: 30²×60, evolving 864,000 complex coupled nonlinear PDEs in 3D+time
- Simulations ran for up to 60,000 time steps or longer
- Initial odd quasiparticle states with excitation energy typically smaller than 1 MeV show significantly more complicated trajectories with longer scission times
- Angular momentum threshold for the odd fermion state that cannot persist in either fission fragment: greater than approximately A^(1/3)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous TDDFT studies of even-even fission (Bulgac 2019, 2020) and their recent comprehensive analysis of scission neutrons (Abdurrahman 2024). It directly contradicts the Pauli blocking approximation and equal filling approximation used in prior fission studies of odd-mass nuclei by Schunck (2023) and Pore (2024), and more broadly challenges stochastic and kinetic approaches to saddle-to-scission dynamics by demonstrating the intrinsically non-Markovian character of fission dynamics. The results enable future microscopic treatments of odd-fermion systems in nuclear large amplitude collective dynamics and heavy-ion collisions without relying on the Pauli blocking approximation.
