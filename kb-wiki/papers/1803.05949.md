# Asymmetric regularization of the ground and excited state of the helium-4 nucleus

> We find the threshold structure of the two- and three-nucleon systems, with the deuteron and 3H/3He as the only bound nuclei, sufficient to predict a pair of four-nucleon states: a deeply bound state which is identified with the helium-4 ground state, and a shallow, unstable state at an energy 0.38(25) MeV above the triton-proton threshold which is consistent with data on the first excited state of helium-4. The analysis employs the framework of Pionless EFT at leading order with a generalized regulator prescription which probes renormalization-group invariance of the two states with respect to higher-order perturbations including asymmetrical disturbances of the short-distance structure of the interaction. In addition to this invariance of the bound-state spectrum and the diagonal triton-proton 1S0 phase shifts in the helium-4 channel with respect to the short-distance structure of the nuclear interaction, our multi-channel calculations with a resonating-group method demonstrate the increasing sensitivity of nuclei to the neutron-proton P-wave interaction. We show that two-nucleon phase shifts, the triton channel, and three-nucleon negative-parity channels are less sensitive with respect to enhanced two-nucleon P-wave attraction than the four-nucleon triton-proton 1S0 phase shifts.

## Key claim

The authors find that the threshold structure of the two- and three-nucleon systems (with the deuteron and triton/helium-3 as the only bound nuclei) is sufficient to predict both the deeply bound helium-4 ground state and a shallow, unstable excited state at 0.38(25) MeV above the triton-proton threshold, with the caveat that this prediction holds only under a renormalization-group analysis that probes sensitivity to asymmetrical short-distance distortions of the interaction.

## Method

The analysis employs Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) at leading order with a generalized regulator that includes a spin-orbit term parameterized by a strength epsilon, in addition to the standard momentum cutoff Lambda. Multi-channel calculations using a resonating-group method (RGM) are performed to compute bound-state energies and triton-proton scattering phase shifts in the helium-4 channel, with the two-nucleon low-energy constants calibrated to the deuteron binding energy and singlet np scattering length, and the three-nucleon constant fitted to the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Excited state of helium-4: energy B*_alpha = [0.38 +/- 0.25] MeV above the triton-proton threshold
- Deuteron binding energy: B_D = 2.224 MeV (used as input)
- Triton binding energy: B_t = 8.482 MeV (used as input)
- Singlet np scattering length: a_s = -23.75 fm (used as input)
- Triplet np scattering length: a_t = 5.42 fm (used as input)
- Ground state helium-4 binding energy: extrapolated to approximately 28.9 MeV in the zero-range limit (Lambda to infinity)
- Critical spin-orbit strength for appearance of unphysical two-nucleon bound state in the 3P0 channel: epsilon_c approximately 1.58 at Lambda = 6 fm^-1
- Critical spin-orbit strengths for the helium-4 excited state: epsilon_c approximately 0.58 at Lambda = 4 fm^-1, 0.67 at Lambda = 6 fm^-1, and 0.72 at Lambda = 8 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the Pionless EFT analysis of the helium-4 ground state by Platter (2005) to include the first excited 0+ state, and builds on the suggestion by Konig (2016) of a universal shallow excitation in the four-nucleon system. The authors also reference Deltuva (2010) on four-boson resonant states with infinite scattering length, contrasting the finite two-body scattering length in nuclear systems. The results enable future studies of whether similar deep-ground-state/shallow-excited-state pairs in larger nuclei (such as carbon-12 and oxygen-16) can be predicted from few-body input alone.
