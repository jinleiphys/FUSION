# Structure of the phonon vacuum state

> The action of the long-range residual force on the expectation value of observables in the nuclear ground states is evaluated by finding optimal values for the coefficients of the canonical transformation which connects the phonon vacuum state with the (quasi-)particle ground-state. After estimating the improvements over the predictions of the independent-particle approximation we compare the ground-state wave functions, obtained using the presented approach, with those, obtained using the conventional random phase approximation (RPA) and its extended version. The problem with overbinding of the nuclear ground state calculated using the RPA is shown to be removed if one sticks to the prescriptions of the present approach. The reason being that the latter conforms to the original variational formulation. Calculations are performed within the two-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in which we present results for the ground and first excited state energies as well as for the ground-state particle occupation numbers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that explicitly varying the phonon vacuum state via a variational principle yields a monotonic improvement of the ground-state energy toward the exact solution, removing the overbinding problem of the conventional random phase approximation (RPA) and its extended version (ERPA), but only in the weak-to-moderate interaction regime; beyond the RPA point of collapse, higher-order multi-particle-hole admixtures (beyond 2p-2h) become essential and the phonon vacuum significantly underestimates the degree of correlation.

## Method

The author constructs the nuclear ground-state wave function as a phonon vacuum state using an exponential transformation of the independent-particle state, with correlation coefficients as variational parameters. The variational principle is applied to minimize the ground-state energy within the two-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model, truncating the expansion at 2p-2h admixtures and then including up to 16p-16h correlations. Results are compared with the exact solution, the standard RPA, and the extended RPA (ERPA) of Hara (1964), which incorporates Pauli blocking via explicit quasiparticle occupation numbers.

## Key numbers

* The critical RPA strength is \( G_{crit} = -\varepsilon / [2(N-1)] \), where \(\varepsilon = 1\) MeV is the level spacing; for \(N=8\) this gives \(G_{crit} \approx -0.0714\) MeV, for \(N=16\) it gives \(G_{crit} \approx -0.0333\) MeV.
* Ground-state energies are computed for LMG systems with \(N=8\) and \(N=16\) particles as a function of interaction strength \(G\) (MeV), with the energy gap \(\varepsilon\) set to 1 MeV.
* The occupation number \(\rho\) on the lower LMG level for \(N=16\) is compared across methods; the ERPA yields a critical occupation number \(\rho_{crit} = 1/2 - (1/(2N))(1 - \varepsilon/(2G))\).
* The first excited state energy within the phonon vacuum approach collapses at interaction strengths near \(G_{crit}\), independent of the order of multi-particle-hole correlations included.

## Context

This work extends the phonon vacuum formalism of Sorensen (1961) and Goswami and Pal (1963), who related correlation coefficients to RPA amplitudes, and addresses the overbinding problem identified in the ERPA of Hara (1964) and Jolos and Rybarska (1980). The approach contrasts with the self-consistent RPA of Dukelsky (1990), which avoids explicit ground-state construction but yields first-excited-state wave functions nearly orthogonal to the exact solution near the RPA collapse point (Hirsch, 1999). The results enable future application to real nuclei, particularly for describing transitional regions, cluster configurations, and the influence of long-range forces on mean-field and pairing correlations.
