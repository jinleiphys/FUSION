# Nucleon-nucleus optical potential in the particle-hole approach

> Feshbach's projection formalism in the particle-hole model space leads to a microscopic description of scattering in terms of the many-body self-energy. To investigate the feasibility of this approach, an optical potential for O-16 is constructed starting from two previous calculations of the self-energy for this nucleus. The results reproduce the background phase shifts for positive parity waves and the resonances beyond the mean field. The latter can be computed microscopically for energies of astrophysical interest using Green's function theory.

## Key claim

The many-body self-energy, when constructed from a combination of a sophisticated self-consistent Green's function calculation (which includes long-range correlations but is restricted to a small model space) and a separate mean-field calculation (which includes all momentum components but only second-order correlations), can serve as a microscopic optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering, but only after phenomenological corrections are applied to reproduce experimental single-particle energies.

## Method

The authors combined two previous calculations of the self-energy for 16O: the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) results of Barbieri and Dickhoff (2002), which included coupling to collective excitations via a Faddeev expansion but were limited to a harmonic oscillator model space, and the mean-field results of Dickhoff, Muther, and Polls (DMP), which used a spherical box basis with all relevant momentum components but only second-order perturbation theory. The mean-field components from outside the SCGF model space were extracted from the DMP calculation and added to the SCGF self-energy, with the depth of the resulting mean-field potential adjusted by constants N^I or N^II to reproduce experimental quasiparticle and quasihole energies of 17F and 15N. The Dyson equation was then solved in momentum space to obtain phase shifts and bound-state wave functions for proton scattering from 16O.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator parameter for the SCGF model space was b = 1.76 fm.
- The Coulomb interaction used a uniformly charged sphere radius R_c = 3.1 fm.
- The G-matrix used for the SCGF calculation was derived from the Bonn-C potential with a fixed starting energy of -5 MeV.
- For the s1/2 bound state of 17F, the spectroscopic factor was 0.931 (case I) and 0.921 (case II); the asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) was -82.5 fm^{-1/2} (case I) and -73.9 fm^{-1/2} (case II); the root-mean-square radius was 5.86 fm (case I) and 5.55 fm (case II).
- For the d5/2 bound state of 17F, the spectroscopic factor was 0.913 (case I) and 0.909 (case II); the ANC was 1.07 fm^{-1/2} (case I) and 0.81 fm^{-1/2} (case II); the root-mean-square radius was 4.01 fm (case I) and 3.70 fm (case II).
- The correction factors N^I for the mean-field depth ranged from 1.05 (s1/2) to 1.16 (d5/2); the correction factors N^II ranged from 1.15 (p1/2) to 1.29 (d5/2).
- The experimental quasiparticle energies used for fitting were: s1/2 at -0.1 MeV, d5/2 at -0.6 MeV, d3/2 at 4.4 MeV, p1/2 at -12.1 MeV, and p3/2 at -18.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach projection formalism to the particle-hole Hilbert space, following the theoretical framework of Mahaux and Capuzzi and Jennings and Escher (2002), where the optical potential becomes the many-body self-energy from Green's function theory. It builds on the SCGF calculations of Barbieri and Dickhoff (2001, 2002) and the mean-field calculations of Dickhoff, Muther, and Polls (DMP). The results are comparable to other low-energy methods such as the cluster model, folding potentials, and the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC), and suggest that self-consistent Green's function theory could be applied to study nucleon capture reactions at astrophysical energies once short-range correlations and three-body forces are included.
