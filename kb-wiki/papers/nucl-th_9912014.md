# The directed flow maximum near c_s=0

> We investigate the excitation function of quark-gluon plasma formation and of directed in-plane flow of nucleons in the energy range of the BNL-AGS and for the E(Lab)=40AGeV Pb+Pb collisions performed recently at the CERN-SPS. We employ the three-fluid model with dynamical unification of kinetically equilibrated fluid elements. Within our model with first-order phase transition at high density, droplets of QGP coexisting with hadronic matter are produced already at BNL-AGS energies, E(Lab)=10AGeV. A substantial decrease of the isentropic velocity of sound, however, requires higher energies, E(Lab)=40AGeV. We show the effect on the flow of nucleons in the reaction plane. According to our model calculations, kinematic requirements and EoS effects work hand-in-hand at E(Lab)=40AGeV to allow the observation of the dropping velocity of sound via an increase of the directed flow around midrapidity as compared to top BNL-AGS energy.

# The directed flow maximum near \(c_s=0\)

## Key claim

Within a three-fluid model that includes a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma, the isentropic speed of sound does not drop substantially at BNL-AGS energies (around 10A GeV) even though QGP droplets form; a significant decrease of \(\langle c_s\rangle\) requires higher bombarding energy (40A GeV), and this drop can be observed experimentally via an *increase* of directed in-plane flow around midrapidity compared to top BNL-AGS energy.

## Method

The authors employ a three-fluid dynamical model in 3+1 dimensions, where projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and newly produced particles (the "fireball") are each treated as distinct fluids with their own continuity equations, coupled via a parametrization of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions. Fluids are dynamically unified when they reach local thermal equilibrium, and the equation of state features a first-order phase transition from hadronic matter (nucleons interacting via relativistic mean fields plus thermal pions) to a quark-gluon plasma described by the MIT Bag model (bag parameter \(B^{1/4}=235\) MeV). The directed in-plane flow of nucleons is computed for Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter \(b=3\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Bag parameter: \(B^{1/4}=235\) MeV, yielding a critical temperature \(T_c\simeq 170\) MeV at zero baryon density and a critical baryon-chemical potential \(\mu_c=1.8\) GeV at zero temperature.
- At \(E^{kin}_{Lab}=40A\) GeV, the average isentropic speed of sound \(\langle c_s\rangle\) drops below 0.2 when the phase coexistence region is reached.
- The maximum fraction of mixed phase occurs around a center-of-mass time of \(t\simeq 7\) fm for all energies studied.
- Specific entropy values: \(s/\rho=10\) corresponds to \(E^{kin}_{Lab}=10A\) GeV; \(s/\rho=20\) corresponds to \(E^{kin}_{Lab}=40A\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-fluid dynamical calculations (Scheid et al., 1974; Stöcker and Greiner, 1986) that predicted sharp signals for QGP formation but neglected non-equilibrium effects. It also builds on the three-fluid model developed by Brachmann et al. (1997) and Dumitru et al. (1995). The paper directly addresses the excitation function of collective flow, using the recently available 40A GeV Pb+Pb data from the CERN-SPS (NA49 Collaboration, 1998) to test whether a local maximum of directed flow, related to a substantial decrease of the isentropic speed of sound, can be observed. The results enable experimental verification of the predicted flow signature as a probe of the QCD phase transition.
