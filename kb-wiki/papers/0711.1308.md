# E(5) and X(5) shape phase transitions within a Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS approach

> Self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations are performed to generate potential energy curves (PEC) in various chains of Pd, Xe, Ba, Nd, Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes. The evolution of shapes with the number of nucleons is studied in a search for signatures of E(5) and X(5) critical point symmetries. It is shown that the energy barriers in the PECs are determined to a large extent by the treatment of the pairing correlations.

## Key claim
Self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations support the assumption of flat potentials for E(5) critical point symmetry in Pd, Xe, and Ba isotopes, but do not predict flat potential energy curves for the N=90 isotones (Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy) that have been suggested as X(5) candidates, in agreement with previous relativistic mean-field studies.

## Method
Constrained deformed Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations were performed using the Skyrme parametrization SLy4 (with some results also for Sk3 and SG2) in the particle-hole channel, combined with a zero-range density-dependent pairing force with strengths g=1000 and g=1250 MeV-fm^3. Potential energy curves were generated as functions of the axially symmetric mass quadrupole moment for isotopic chains of Pd (A=96-114), Xe (A=118-136), Ba (A=120-138), Nd (A=142-156), Sm (A=144-158), Gd (A=146-156), and Dy (A=148-158). Two methods for solving the HF+BCS equations were compared: a 3-dimensional cartesian mesh (code ev8) and an expansion in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers
- For Pd isotopes with SLy4 and g=1000 MeV-fm^3, ground state beta_2 values range from 0.10 (^96Pd) to 0.19 (^112Pd) and -0.19 (^114Pd); with g=1250 MeV-fm^3, values range from 0.00 (^96Pd) to 0.16 (^104,106Pd)
- For Xe isotopes with g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.15 (^118Xe) to 0.00 (^136Xe); with g=1250 MeV-fm^3, from 0.27 (^120,122Xe) to 0.00 (^136Xe)
- For Ba isotopes with g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.34 (^120Ba) to 0.00 (^138Ba); with g=1250 MeV-fm^3, from 0.31 (^120Ba) to 0.00 (^138Ba)
- For Nd isotopes with SLy4 and g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.00 (^142Nd) to 0.30 (^156Nd)
- For Sm isotopes with SLy4 and g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.00 (^144Sm) to 0.31 (^158Sm)
- For Gd isotopes with SLy4 and g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.00 (^146Gd) to 0.28 (^156Gd)
- For Dy isotopes with SLy4 and g=1000 MeV-fm^3, beta_2 ranges from 0.00 (^148Dy) to 0.28 (^158Dy)
- Energy barrier in ^148Nd is about 5 MeV; in ^150Nd about 8 MeV
- The zero-range pairing force uses a density cutoff rho_c = 0.16 fm^-3 and a smooth cut-off of 5 MeV around the Fermi level

## Context
This work extends nonrelativistic self-consistent mean-field studies of shape phase transitions to complement previous relativistic mean-field calculations by Meng (2004), Sheng (2005), and Fossion (2005) for the same isotopic chains. The study confirms the E(5) candidates ^108,110Pd, ^128,130Xe, and ^130,132Ba identified from algebraic models by Casten (2000), Zamfir (2002), and Clark (2004), while finding that the X(5) candidates ^150Nd, ^152Sm, ^154Gd, and ^156Dy do not exhibit flat potential energy curves, consistent with the relativistic results of Fossion (2005). The work enables future systematic comparisons between algebraic models and nonrelativistic microscopic approaches, and motivates fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with interactions like Gogny that treat mean-field and pairing channels with the same force.
