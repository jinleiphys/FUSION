# Illustrations of loosely-bound and resonant states in atomic nuclei

> Using the one-dimensional potential well with realistic parameters for atomic nuclei, we illustrate the movement of the poles of the S-matrix, and the transmission coefficient when the well supports an anti-bound state. We calculate the phase shift of the atomic nuclei 5He using the three-dimensional potential well, and compare it with the experimental one. The paper gives a glance of some of the properties found in realistic loosely and resonant nuclear systems, using equations at the level of undergraduate students.

## Key claim

Using one- and three-dimensional square potential wells with parameters realistic for nuclear systems, the paper illustrates that the poles of the S-matrix, the transmission coefficient, and the phase shift can reproduce the qualitative features of loosely-bound states (like the deuteron) and resonances (like in 5He), but only as a pedagogical approximation that captures the essential physics without a full nuclear interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation for a square potential well to obtain bound-state energies, transmission and reflection amplitudes, and the S-matrix poles in the complex k-plane. For the three-dimensional case, they solved the radial Schrödinger equation with a spherical square well (including the centrifugal term) to compute the partial-wave phase shift δ_l via continuity of the wave function and its derivative at the well boundary. The S-matrix poles were found numerically as a function of the well depth V_0, and the phase shift for 5He was compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For the one-dimensional well with R = 2.1 fm and 2μ/ħ² = 0.024 MeV⁻¹ fm⁻², the S-matrix poles were tracked as V_0 varied; bound states appear on the positive imaginary k-axis, anti-bound states on the negative imaginary k-axis, and resonant poles as symmetric pairs in the lower half-plane.
* The phase shift for 5He was calculated using the three-dimensional spherical well and compared with experimental data; the resonant phase shift reaches π/2 at the resonant energy, with the Breit-Wigner width Γ extracted from the rapid variation of δ_l.
* The transmission coefficient T = |t|² for the one-dimensional well reaches maxima T = 1 when 2RK = π, 2π, ... (integer number of half-wavelengths inside the well).

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical approach of Sprung (1996), Maheswari (2010), and Ahmed (2011) by explicitly connecting the one-dimensional S-matrix pole trajectories to the three-dimensional phase-shift calculation for a real nucleus. It enables undergraduate-level instruction on how bound, anti-bound, and resonant states emerge from a simple potential model, and it provides a bridge to more realistic treatments of loosely-bound systems like the deuteron and resonant systems like 5He. The paper contrasts with full nuclear-structure calculations by using only square wells, but it reproduces the qualitative features of the experimental phase shift for 5He.
