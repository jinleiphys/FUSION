# Spurious dipole mode in random-phase approximation and in the RPA-based models

> The problems related to the existence of the spurious dipole mode (SDM) in the self-consistent nuclear-structure models are considered. A method is formulated that allows to eliminate coupling of the SDM with the physical modes in the extended random-phase approximation (ERPA) theories, in particular, in the time blocking approximation (TBA) which is the model of the ERPA type. It is shown that the application of this method in the realistic TBA calculations of the $E1$ excitations gives the results which are very close to the results of the TBA calculations without using this method if the bare external-field $E1$ operators are replaced by the effective ones.

# Spurious dipole mode in random-phase approximation and in the RPA-based models

## Key claim

The spurious dipole mode (SDM) can be fully separated from physical modes in extended random-phase approximation (ERPA) theories using a projection method, but in realistic time blocking approximation (TBA) calculations the results are very close to those obtained without the method if bare external-field E1 operators are replaced by effective ones, meaning the correction is largely redundant when effective operators are already used.

## Method

The author formulates a projection operator method that eliminates coupling of the SDM with physical modes in ERPA theories, specifically in the time blocking approximation (TBA), which includes 1p1h⊗phonon configurations on top of the RPA 1p1h space. The method is derived from symmetry properties of the energy-density functional (EDF) and uses explicit forms of the matrices a^(0,1) and a^(0,2) obtained from the translational invariance of the EDF. Fully self-consistent calculations of electric dipole (E1) excitations are performed using the Skyrme-EDF parametrization SLy4 for three doubly magic nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb) in both discrete and continuum versions of RPA and TBA.

## Key numbers

- SDM energy ω_SDM in ¹⁶O decreases from 2.13 MeV to 0.12 MeV as ε_max increases from 100 MeV to 500 MeV; at ε_max = 2000 MeV, ω_SDM ≈ 3 keV
- SDM energy ω_SDM in ⁴⁸Ca decreases from 1.59 MeV to 0.07 MeV as ε_max increases from 100 MeV to 500 MeV
- SDM energy ω_SDM in ²⁰⁸Pb decreases from 0.88 MeV to 0.03 MeV as ε_max increases from 100 MeV to 500 MeV
- Ratio B(E1)_SDM / B(E1)_max ≤ 1.5×10⁻⁵ for ε_max = 100 MeV and ≤ 3.3×10⁻⁹ for ε_max = 500 MeV, using the effective E1 operator

## Context

This work extends the author's previous developments (Tselyaev 2013, 2014) on eliminating spurious modes in RPA-based models, and addresses problems noted by Nakatsukasa (2007), Repko (2019), and Kvasil (2019) regarding explicit extraction of SDM terms from RPA response functions. The method corrects an incomplete elimination scheme proposed by Mizuyama (2012), where the remainder term from the ghost part of the RPA response function could contribute non-zero effects in beyond-mean-field models. The results enable cleaner calculations of E1 excitations in self-consistent nuclear structure models based on energy-density functionals, particularly for the time blocking approximation and other ERPA-type theories.
