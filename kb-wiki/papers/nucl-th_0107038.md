# The Magnetic Moments of the Octet Baryons in Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We compute the magnetic moments of the octet baryons up to two orders in quenched chiral perturbation theory. In addition to the $\sim\sqrt{m_q}$ contributions that arise in QCD, there are lower-order contributions of the form $M_0^2\log m_q$ from loop diagrams involving hairpin interactions.

# The Magnetic Moments of the Octet Baryons in Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of the octet baryons in quenched QCD receive contributions of the form $M_0^2 \log m_q$ from hairpin interactions, which are lower-order than the $\sim \sqrt{m_q}$ contributions that arise in full QCD; the coefficients $\delta$ and $\beta^{\rm QQCD}$ for each baryon are computed up to two orders in quenched chiral perturbation theory, but the numerical values of the low-energy constants $\mu_D$, $\mu_F$, $D$, $F$, and ${\cal C}$ in QQCD will differ from those in QCD.

## Method

The author constructs the quenched chiral perturbation theory (Q$\chi$PT) Lagrangian for the octet and decuplet baryons using a graded symmetry $SU(3|3)_L \otimes SU(3|3)_R$ with ghost-quark fields, following the formalism of Labrenz and Sharpe (1996). One-loop diagrams involving pseudo-Goldstone bosons, octet baryons, decuplet baryons, and ghost-baryon intermediate states are computed to extract the chiral-logarithm and $\sqrt{m_q}$ contributions to each octet baryon magnetic moment. The tree-level magnetic moments are expressed in terms of two low-energy constants $\mu_D$ and $\mu_F$, and the one-loop coefficients $\beta$ and $\beta'$ for pion and kaon loops are tabulated separately for QCD and QQCD.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level magnetic moments in nuclear magnetons: $p$ and $\Sigma^+$ have $\frac{1}{3}\mu_D + \mu_F$; $n$ and $\Xi^0$ have $-\frac{2}{3}\mu_D$; $\Sigma^0$ has $\frac{1}{3}\mu_D$; $\Sigma^-$ and $\Xi^-$ have $\frac{1}{3}\mu_D - \mu_F$; $\Lambda$ has $-\frac{1}{3}\mu_D$; the $\Sigma^0-\Lambda$ transition moment is $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\mu_D$.
- In QQCD, the $\beta^{(\pi)}$ coefficient for the proton is $-\frac{4}{3}D^2$ (compared to $-(D+F)^2$ in QCD), and for the neutron is $+\frac{4}{3}D^2$ (compared to $(D+F)^2$ in QCD).
- In QQCD, the $\beta^{(K)}$ coefficient for $\Sigma^+$ is $-\frac{4}{3}D^2$ (compared to $-(D+F)^2$ in QCD), and for $\Xi^0$ is $+\frac{4}{3}D^2$ (compared to $(D+F)^2$ in QCD).
- The $\beta^{\prime (\pi)}$ coefficient for the proton in QQCD is $-\frac{1}{6}{\cal C}^2$ (compared to $-\frac{2}{9}{\cal C}^2$ in QCD).
- The $\beta^{\prime (K)}$ coefficient for $\Sigma^+$ in QQCD is $-\frac{1}{6}{\cal C}^2$ (compared to $-\frac{2}{9}{\cal C}^2$ in QCD).
- The pion decay constant is taken as $f \sim 132~{\rm MeV}$ in QCD; the loop function ${\cal F}(m,\Delta,\mu)$ reduces to ${\cal F}(m,0,\mu)=m$ when the decuplet-octet mass splitting $\Delta=0$.

## Context

This work extends the quenched chiral perturbation theory framework established by Labrenz and Sharpe (1996) for baryon masses to the magnetic moments of the octet baryons. It provides the necessary chiral extrapolation formulas for interpreting quenched lattice QCD calculations of magnetic moments, such as those by Leinweber et al. (1991) which used a Pade approximant to extrapolate from pion masses above 600 MeV. The results enable future lattice calculations to be matched onto the correct chiral behavior of QQCD, rather than attempting to match QQCD to the real world, and highlight that the Coleman-Glashow relations (1961) persist at tree level in QQCD despite the different chiral structure.
