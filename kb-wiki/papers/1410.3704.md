# Nuclear Dynamics at the Particle Threshold

> Recently, new low-energy modes of excitation called pygmy resonances have been observed. Their distinct feature is the close connection to nuclear skin oscillations. A successful description of the pygmy resonances could be achieved in a microscopic theoretical approach which incorporates the density functional theory and QRPA formalism extended with multi-phonon degrees of freedom. The latter is found of crucial importance for the understanding of the fine structure of nuclear electric and magnetic excitations at low energies. Corresponding microscopic multi-phonon response functions are implemented in the studies of s-process of nucleosynthesis.

# Nuclear Dynamics at the Particle Threshold

## Key claim
Pygmy resonances (electric dipole and quadrupole) are a generic feature of nuclei with neutron or proton excess, directly correlated with skin thickness, but the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) alone cannot reproduce their fine structure; only by including multi-phonon coupling via the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) can the detailed fragmentation and energy distribution of these low-energy modes be described.

## Method
The authors combine self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM), extending QRPA calculations to include up to three-phonon configurations. Ground-state properties (binding energies, radii, skin thickness) are obtained from HFB with Wood-Saxon potentials adjusted to reproduce experimental data, while excited states are described by QRPA phonons and their multi-phonon couplings. The resulting strength functions are then used as input for statistical reaction calculations of neutron-capture cross sections relevant to the s-process of nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers
- In N=50 nuclei, the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) total strength is found in the excitation energy region below 9 MeV, smoothly decreasing with increasing proton number Z.
- For the reaction 85Kr(n,gamma)86Kr, the QRPA calculation gives a neutron-capture cross section reduced by about 35% compared to experiment, while the three-phonon QPM calculation agrees very well with data; the pure PDR contribution to the QRPA cross section is estimated at about 30%.
- In 90Zr, the M1 giant resonance has a centroid energy of 9 MeV and a summed strength of 4.5(4) mu_N^2 from experiment, fully reproduced by three-phonon QPM calculations.
- The three-phonon QPM predicts a fragmented M1 strength of sum B(M1)upward approximately 2 mu_N^2 in the energy range 11-12.5 MeV in 90Zr, forming a tail around the neutron separation energy Sn=11.97 MeV.
- In Sn isotopes, the most important proton contribution to low-energy 2+ states never exceeds 5% of the state vector, due to the [1g9/2 2d5/2]pi two-quasiparticle component.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of pygmy resonances in N=50 and N=82 nuclei (Tsoneva 2008, 2010, 2011) and provides a unified microscopic framework for low-energy electric and magnetic excitations. The multi-phonon QPM approach goes beyond standard QRPA, which the authors show is insufficient for describing the fine structure of dipole strength. The results enable improved predictions of neutron-capture reaction rates for s-process nucleosynthesis, as demonstrated for the branching-point nucleus 85Kr, and address the long-standing problem of M1 strength quenching and dynamics near the particle threshold (Rusev 2013).
