# The scale dependent nuclear effects in parton distributions for practical applications

> The scale dependence of the ratios of parton distributions in a proton of a nucleus $A$ and in the free proton, $R_i^A(x,Q^2)=f_{i/A}(x,Q^2)/f_i(x,Q^2)$, is studied within the framework of the lowest order leading-twist DGLAP evolution. By evolving the initial nuclear distributions obtained with the GRV-LO and CTEQ4L sets at a scale $Q_0^2$, we show that the ratios $R_i^A(x,Q^2)$ are only moderately sensitive to the choice of a specific modern set of free parton distributions. We propose that to a good first approximation, this parton distribution set-dependence of the nuclear ratios $R_i^A(x,Q^2)$ can be neglected in practical applications. With this result, we offer a numerical parametrization of $R_i^A(x,Q^2)$ for all parton flavours $i$ in any $A>2$, and at any $10^{-6}\le x \le 1$ and any $Q^2\ge 2.25$ GeV$^2$ for computing cross sections of hard processes in nuclear collisions.

# The scale dependent nuclear effects in parton distributions for practical applications

## Key claim

The ratios of nuclear to free-proton parton distributions, \(R_i^A(x,Q^2)=f_{i/A}(x,Q^2)/f_i(x,Q^2)\), are only moderately sensitive to the choice of a specific modern set of free parton distributions (GRV-LO vs. CTEQ4L) when evolved via leading-twist LO DGLAP evolution, so that to a good first approximation this set-dependence can be neglected in practical applications. The authors offer a numerical parametrization of \(R_i^A(x,Q^2)\) for all parton flavours in any \(A>2\) over \(10^{-6}\le x \le 1\) and \(Q^2\ge 2.25\) GeV\(^2\), but note that the initial conditions at \(Q_0^2\) contain assumptions about shadowing saturation at \(x\lesssim 0.01\) and about the gluon and sea-quark EMC effect at large \(x\) that remain unconstrained.

## Method

The authors start from the nuclear structure function ratios \(R_{F_2}^A\) measured in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering and from Drell-Yan cross-section ratios in \(pA\) vs. \(p\)D collisions, together with baryon-number and momentum conservation, to determine initial nuclear parton distributions at \(Q_0^2=2.25\) GeV\(^2\) (GRV-LO) or \(Q_0^2=2.56\) GeV\(^2\) (CTEQ4L). They then evolve these distributions via lowest-order leading-twist DGLAP evolution and compare the resulting ratios \(R_i^A(x,Q^2)\) for individual flavours (valence quarks, sea quarks, gluons, charm) obtained with the two free-proton sets. The set-dependence is quantified for a lead nucleus (\(A=208\)) and for various \(A\) values, and the parametrization (EKS98) is constructed from the GRV-LO results.

## Key numbers

- Initial scale: \(Q_0^2 = 2.25\) GeV\(^2\) for GRV-LO and \(Q_0^2 = 2.56\) GeV\(^2\) for CTEQ4L.
- Validity range of the parametrization: \(10^{-6} \le x \le 1\) and \(Q^2 \ge 2.25\) GeV\(^2\).
- For \(A=208\) (lead) at \(Q^2=10000\) GeV\(^2\), the set-dependence in the strange-quark ratio \(R_s^A\) is at most 5\%.
- The set-dependence in the gluon ratio \(R_G^A\) at small \(x\) is the largest among flavours but remains within \(\sim 5\%\) for large nuclei.
- The parametrization returns unity for \(A \le 2\) (deuterium nuclear effects neglected).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Eskola, Kolhinen, and Ruuskanen (1998) (EKR98) by quantitatively verifying that the nuclear ratios \(R_i^A\) are approximately independent of the choice of free-proton parton distribution set (GRV-LO vs. CTEQ4L). It provides a practical numerical parametrization (EKS98) that can be used with any modern LO set to obtain absolute nuclear parton distributions for hard-scattering cross-section calculations in nuclear collisions. The approach assumes that all nuclear effects are encoded in the initial distributions at \(Q_0^2\) and that higher-twist contributions (e.g., GLR-MQ fusion terms) are neglected; open questions include the need for better constraints on nuclear gluon shadowing at small \(x\) and on the EMC effect in sea quarks and gluons at large \(x\).
