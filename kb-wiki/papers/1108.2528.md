# Roper Properties on the Lattice: An Update

> In this proceeding, we give an overview of the latest progress in lattice-QCD calculations of the Roper resonance, and Roper-nucleon electromagnetic and axial transition form factors using dynamical gauge ensembles.

# Roper Properties on the Lattice: An Update

## Key claim

The paper reports that finite-volume effects on the Roper resonance mass can be as large as 170 MeV even when the rule-of-thumb condition $M_\pi L \approx 4$ is satisfied, and that removing the quenched approximation causes a sign change in the low-$Q^2$ Roper-nucleon electromagnetic transition form factors relative to earlier quenched calculations, potentially resolving a discrepancy with CLAS experimental data.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD calculations of the Roper resonance mass, Roper-nucleon electromagnetic transition form factors $F_1^*$ and $F_2^*$, and the axial transition form factor $G_1$, using $N_f=2+1$ anisotropic clover dynamical gauge ensembles at multiple pion masses (approximately 390, 450, and 875 MeV) and multiple lattice volumes (2, 2.5, 3, and 4 fm). The volume dependence of the Roper mass is studied by comparing results across four volumes at fixed pion mass $M_\pi \approx 390$ MeV, while the transition form factors are computed from three-point correlation functions at various momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

- Roper mass central-value shift of approximately 170 MeV observed when comparing the smallest volume (2 fm) to the largest volume (4 fm) at $M_\pi \approx 390$ MeV
- Nucleon ground-state mass shows approximately 30-MeV discrepancy from its infinite-volume value at $M_\pi L \approx 4$, which is the usual rule-of-thumb threshold for negligible finite-volume effects
- Ratio of Roper overlap factors at the two largest volumes: $A_R(4\ \text{fm})/A_R(3\ \text{fm}) = 0.964(52)$, consistent with one, suggesting the first-excited state is a one-particle state rather than a scattering state at $M_\pi > 390$ MeV
- First lattice calculation of the axial proton-Roper transition form factor $G_1(0)$ gives a value of order 0.1 at $M_\pi \approx 875$ MeV
- Pion form factor results reach $Q^2$ up to nearly 7 GeV$^2$ for the highest-mass ensemble (1350 MeV pion mass)
- Axial decuplet-octet-meson coupling extracted from finite-volume chiral perturbation theory: $|g_{\Delta N \pi}| = 2.80(18)(21)$

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD calculations of the Roper resonance by the same authors (Lin, 2008) and by the CSSM group (Mahbub, 2010) and the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Bulava, 2010; Edwards, 2011), who have produced excited-baryon spectra using $N_f=2+1$ dynamical lattices. The paper reports the first dynamical lattice calculation of the Roper-nucleon electromagnetic transition form factors and the first lattice calculation of the axial proton-Roper transition form factor. The findings on finite-volume effects build on a high-statistics study by Beane (2011) using $N_f=2+1$ anisotropic clover lattices at $M_\pi \approx 390$ MeV. The results enable future studies to incorporate proper finite-volume corrections and to use multiple-volume techniques to extract axial coupling constants such as $g_{A,N^*}$ and $g_{NN^*\pi}$ via chiral perturbation theory, as well as to extend form-factor calculations to higher $Q^2$ using step-scaling methods.
