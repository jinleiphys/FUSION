# QCD and the Hagedorn spectrum

> It is shown that large Nc QCD must have a Hagedorn spectrum (i.e. a spectrum of hadron which grows exponentially with the hadrons mass) provided that certain technical assumptions concerning the applicability of perturbation theory to a certain class of correlation functions apply. The basic argument exploits the interplay of confinement and asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

Large Nc QCD must have a Hagedorn spectrum (exponential growth in the number of hadrons with mass) provided that standard assumptions about the applicability of perturbation theory to certain short-distance correlation functions hold; the argument is not a rigorous theorem but a "physicist's proof" relying on the interplay of confinement and asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The author constructs sequences of sets of linearly independent local gauge-invariant single-color-trace operators with scalar-isoscalar quantum numbers, specifically operators of the form \(\overline{q} \mathcal{O}_{l_1} \mathcal{O}_{l_2} \cdots \mathcal{O}_{l_n} q\) where each \(\mathcal{O}_\pm\) is a gluon bilinear (either \(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}\) or \(F_{\mu\nu}\tilde{F}^{\mu\nu}\)). A lemma is derived bounding the average of the lowest \(k\) meson masses by the derivative of the trace of the logarithm of the correlator matrix. The exponential growth of the number of such operators with \(n\) (specifically \(2^n\)) is then shown, via analysis of short-distance behavior and perturbative corrections at large \(N_c\), to imply exponential growth in the number of hadrons with mass.

## Key numbers

* The number of operators in set \(\mathcal{S}_n\) is \(||\mathcal{S}_n|| = 2^n\), providing exponential growth with the index \(n\).
* The asymptotic free-field value of \(\overline{M}_{\mathcal{S}_n}(r)\) (the derivative quantity bounding average meson masses) approaches \(8n + 6\) as \(r \rightarrow 0\).
* The Hagedorn temperature bound derived is \(T_H \le \frac{2p}{r_0 \log(A)}\) or, with a refined argument, \(T_H \le \frac{p}{r_0 \log(A)}\), where \(p\), \(r_0\), and \(A\) are constants from the sequence properties.
* The operators in \(\mathcal{S}_n\) have engineering dimension \(4n+3\).

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1979) demonstration that large \(N_c\) QCD has an infinite number of mesons with given quantum numbers, using a similar strategy of confronting asymptotic freedom with confinement via correlation functions. It is similar in spirit to Kogan and Zhitnitsky's demonstration that large \(N_c\) QCD with adjoint fermions in 1+1 dimensions has a Hagedorn spectrum. The argument does not assume stringy dynamics or an unbroken center symmetry, and it can be generalized to various QCD-like theories at large \(N_c\), potentially providing insight into the emergence of stringy dynamics from generic properties of correlators.
