# Bose-Fermi Pairs in a Mixture and the Luttinger Theorem within a Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink like Approach

> Bose-Fermion pair correlations in a mixture are considered at zero temperature in the T-matrix approximation. Special attention is paid to the Luttinger theorem. In a strict RPA variant of the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach, it is shown that this theorem is respected also in the homogeneous infinite matter case. We calculate the corresponding occupation numbers of fermions and bosons and the condensate depletion. We also show that in the limit of very small boson density, our results are in good agreement with the results found in the literature for the Fermi polaron in strongly imbalanced Fermi-Fermi mixtures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a strict RPA variant of the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach applied to a homogeneous infinite Bose-Fermi mixture at zero temperature respects the Luttinger theorem, but only when the self-energy in the Dyson equation is treated to first order; if the Dyson equation were resummed to all orders, the Luttinger theorem would most likely be violated.

## Method

The authors construct a boson-fermion T-matrix in ladder approximation (pp-RPA) for a mixture of spinless fermions and bosons interacting via a contact potential, with the boson field shifted by a c-number representing the condensate. The single-particle Green's functions are obtained by treating the self-energy only to first order in the Dyson equation, and occupation numbers for fermions and bosons are calculated from these Green's functions. The fermion chemical potential is fixed at the free Fermi energy, and the correlated ground-state energy density is computed via coupling constant integration.

## Key numbers

- The Luttinger theorem is fulfilled to a relative accuracy of better than 10^{-3} in numerical calculations.
- The number of non-condensed bosons equals the number of fermions above the Fermi surface: n_B^{nc} = ∫_{k>k_F} d^3k/(2π)^3 ρ_F(k).
- For the polaron limit at n_0/n_F = 0.001, the boson chemical potential agrees well with results from Combescot (2007) for negative scattering lengths, but agreement deteriorates for values of (k_F a)^{-1} close to -1.
- The condensate depletion is shown as a function of (k_F a)^{-1} for various mass ratios m_B/m_F and density ratios n_B/n_F, with calculations stopped when n_0 - n_B^{nc} or the Z factor becomes negative.

## Context

This work extends the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach, originally developed for two-component Fermi gases near the superfluid transition temperature (NSR, 1985), to Bose-Fermi mixtures at zero temperature. It explicitly verifies the Luttinger theorem in a continuum system, which had previously been demonstrated only for finite systems such as the Hubbard model by Barillier (2008) and for atomic nuclei by Bouyssy (1974). The results in the polaron limit are compared with the variational approach of Combescot (2007), and the paper identifies the need for future work on the transition to a ground state of composite molecules, as discussed by Dukelsky (2011).
