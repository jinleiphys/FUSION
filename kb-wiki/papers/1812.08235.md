# Chiral crossover in QCD at zero and non-zero chemical potentials

> We present results for pseudo-critical temperatures of QCD chiral crossovers at zero and non-zero values of baryon ($B$), strangeness ($S$), electric charge ($Q$), and isospin ($I$) chemical potentials $μ_{X=B,Q,S,I}$. The results were obtained using lattice QCD calculations carried out with two degenerate up and down dynamical quarks and a dynamical strange quark, with quark masses corresponding to physical values of pion and kaon masses in the continuum limit. By parameterizing pseudo-critical temperatures as $ T_c(μ_X) = T_c(0) \left[ 1 -κ_2^{X}(μ_{X}/T_c(0))^2 -κ_4^{X}(μ_{X}/T_c(0))^4 \right] $, we determined $κ_2^X$ and $κ_4^X$ from Taylor expansions of chiral observables in $μ_X$. We obtained a precise result for $T_c(0)=(156.5\pm1.5)\;\mathrm{MeV}$. For analogous thermal conditions at the chemical freeze-out of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, i.e., $μ_{S}(T,μ_{B})$ and $μ_{Q}(T,μ_{B})$ fixed from strangeness-neutrality and isospin-imbalance, we found $κ_2^B=0.012(4)$ and $κ_4^B=0.000(4)$. For $μ_{B}\lesssim300\;\mathrm{MeV}$, the chemical freeze-out takes place in the vicinity of the QCD phase boundary, which coincides with the lines of constant energy density of $0.42(6)\;\mathrm{GeV/fm}^3$ and constant entropy density of $3.7(5)\;\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The chiral crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is determined to be \(T_c(0) = (156.5 \pm 1.5)\) MeV, but this value is an unweighted average of five different pseudo-critical temperature definitions that only coincide within errors after continuum extrapolation; the authors note that if the true chiral critical temperature in the chiral limit were significantly different, more dispersion among these definitions would be expected.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations with 2+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) at physical pion and kaon masses, using the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12, 16\). They computed the chiral order parameter and disconnected chiral susceptibility and their Taylor expansion coefficients in baryon, strangeness, electric charge, and isospin chemical potentials up to fourth order, then extracted pseudo-critical temperatures from inflection points and peak positions of these observables.

## Key numbers

- \(T_c(0) = (156.5 \pm 1.5)\) MeV: chiral crossover temperature at zero chemical potential, continuum-extrapolated from 5 definitions
- \(\kappa_2^B = 0.012(4)\) and \(\kappa_4^B = 0.000(4)\): curvature coefficients for baryon chemical potential under freeze-out conditions (strangeness-neutral, \(n_Q = 0.4 n_B\))
- \(\kappa_2^B = 0.016(6)\) and \(\kappa_4^B = 0.001(7)\): curvature coefficients for baryon chemical potential at \(\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0\)
- \(\kappa_2^S = 0.017(5)\), \(\kappa_2^Q = 0.029(6)\), \(\kappa_2^I = 0.026(4)\): curvature coefficients for strangeness, electric charge, and isospin chemical potentials at zero other potentials
- Constant energy density \(\epsilon = 0.42(6)\) GeV/fm\(^3\) and constant entropy density \(s = 3.7(5)\) fm\(^{-3}\) at the crossover for \(\mu_B \lesssim 300\) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous HotQCD collaboration results (Bazavov 2011, Bhattacharya 2014) with approximately 6 times improved precision on \(T_c(0)\), achieved using 100-500 times more gauge configurations. The \(\kappa_2^B\) value is about a factor of 2 larger than earlier estimates from Kaczmarek (2011) but consistent with more recent works using analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials (Bonati 2015, Cea 2014). The results enable precise mapping of the QCD phase boundary in the temperature-chemical potential plane for comparison with heavy-ion collision freeze-out parameters from ALICE and STAR experiments.
