# Discovery of High-momentum Nucleon Correlations in Nuclei -- an Early History

> We summarize the reasons for using the light-cone mechanics of nuclei for description of high-energy nuclear processes and describe the steps, which have led to the discovery of high-momentum correlations in nuclei.

# Discovery of High-momentum Nucleon Correlations in Nuclei, an Early History

## Key claim

The paper argues that short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei were discovered through a combination of light-cone quantization methods and experimental observations of backward nucleon production, but the authors note that early claims about studying SRC were rejected by editors because such claims had "ultimately turned out wrong" when based on processes with only a few hundred MeV transfer. The key condition is that resolving short-range nuclear structure requires energy and momentum transfers larger than 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors developed a light-cone (LC) quantization framework for nuclear wave functions, deriving a relation between LC and non-relativistic wave functions for the deuteron and heavier nuclei. They used the impulse approximation to express cross sections for processes such as h + D → N + X in terms of single-nucleon LC densities, and extracted momentum distributions from backward nucleon production data in γ + C → p + X at Eγ ~ 3 GeV. They then extended the analysis to inclusive (e,e') reactions at large x > 1 and Q² ≥ 1.5 GeV², defining a scaling ratio a₂(A) that compares the probability for a nucleon to belong to a pair correlation in a nucleus versus in the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The energy and momentum transfer required to resolve short-range nuclear structure must satisfy q₀ ≥ 1 GeV and |q| ≥ 1 GeV.
- The mass/energy gap for two nucleons in the deuteron channel from ΔΔ components is 2mΔ - 2mN ≈ 600 MeV, corresponding to nucleon momenta k_N = √(mΔ² - mN²) ≈ 800 MeV.
- For NN interaction in the isospin 1 channel with single Δ production, the corresponding momentum is k_N ~ 550 MeV.
- The ratio a₂(C) extracted from backward nucleon production in γ + C → p + X data at momenta ≥ 300 MeV/c was found to be close to the value extracted from inclusive electroproduction data.
- In the EVA experiment at BNL, approximately 30% of events with forward pp trigger showed a backward nucleon with momentum > 250 MeV/c.
- Protons have been observed at α_p up to ~3 in inclusive backward proton production from pA → p + X reactions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of West (1975) on corrections to the impulse approximation for deuteron scattering, and builds on the authors' own earlier work (FS81, FS88) establishing light-cone quantization for nuclear physics. The paper contradicts the prevailing pre-1974 view that short-range correlations could not be observed, and it enabled the series of JLab experiments that directly observed SRC in nuclei and established the dominance of pn correlations. The approach also connects to the work of Ciofi degli Atti (1991) on spectral functions and to the EVA experiment at BNL analyzed by Farrar and Sargsian (2006).
