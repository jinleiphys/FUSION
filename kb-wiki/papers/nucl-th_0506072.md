# Coherent Theta^+ and Lambda(1520) photoproduction off the deuteron

> We analyze an effect of the coherent $Θ^+Λ(1520)$ photoproduction in $γD$ interaction near the threshold. We demonstrate that the coherence effect becomes manifest in a comparison of the $nK^+$ invariant mass distribution when the $pK^-$ invariant equals the $Λ(1520)$ mass. Our model calculations indicate a sizeable contribution of resonant and non-resonant background processes in the $γD\to np K^+K^-$ reaction which generally exceed the contribution of the coherent resonant channel. However, we find that the coherent $Θ^+Λ(1520)$ photoproduction is enhanced relative to the background processes in the forward hemisphere of the $pK^-$ pair photoproduction. Moreover, the coherence effect does not depend on the $Θ^+$ photoproduction amplitude and is defined by the probabilities of the $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction and the $Θ^+\to NK$ transition. Therefore, this coherence effect may be used as an independent method for studying the mechanism of $Θ^+$ production and $Θ^+$ properties.

## Key claim

The coherence effect in $\gamma D \to \Lambda(1520) \Theta^+$ photoproduction in the forward hemisphere of the $pK^-$ pair does not depend on the $\Theta^+$ photoproduction amplitude and is defined only by the probabilities of $\Lambda(1520)$ photoproduction and the $\Theta^+ \to NK$ transition, meaning this effect remains finite even if the $\gamma p \to \Theta^+ \bar K^0$ cross section vanishes.

## Method

The authors model the coherent $\gamma D \to \Lambda(1520) \Theta^+$ reaction using an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams for the elementary $\gamma N \to \Lambda^* K$ and $\gamma N \to \Theta^+ \bar K$ subprocesses, including $K^*$ exchange and Born terms. The deuteron is treated with a non-relativistic wave function (Paris potential), and the loop integrals for the coherent process are evaluated via their imaginary parts by cutting diagrams. Background processes (resonant spectator and non-resonant $K^+K^-$ production) are calculated with the same theoretical framework and compared to the coherent channel.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma_{\Theta} \sim 1$ MeV (assumed $\Theta^+$ decay width)
- $\Gamma_{\Lambda^*} \simeq 15.6$ MeV (total $\Lambda(1520)$ decay width)
- $\Gamma_{\Lambda^* \to N\bar K} \simeq 0.45 \times 15.6$ MeV, giving $|g_{\Lambda^* NK}| = 32.6$
- $M_\Theta = 1.54$ GeV (assumed $\Theta^+$ mass)
- $E_\gamma = 2$ GeV (photon energy used for differential cross section calculations)
- Cut-off parameter $\Lambda_B \simeq 0.5$ GeV for Born terms; $\Lambda_{K^*} = 1.5$ GeV for $K^*$ exchange
- Coupling constants: $e g_{\gamma K^{0} K^{*0}} = -0.35$, $e g_{\gamma K^{\pm} K^{*\pm}} = 0.23$
- $\alpha_{\Theta} = 1.875$ (ratio $g_{\Theta NK^*} / g_{\Theta NK}$)
- $\alpha_{\Lambda^*} = +0.372$ or $-0.657$ (two solutions for $g_{\Lambda^* NK^*} / g_{\Lambda^* NK}$)
- Matching point $E_0 = 2.3$ GeV between effective Lagrangian and Regge regimes for $\Lambda^*$ photoproduction
- Regge trajectory parameters: $\alpha(t) = -0.1 + 0.9t$, $C_R = 29.6$, $s_0 = 1$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Titov et al. (2005) for coherent $\Theta^+ \Lambda(\Sigma^0)$ photoproduction from a deuteron to the specific case of $\Lambda(1520)$ and $\Theta^+$ production. It addresses the controversial experimental situation regarding the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, following the first evidence by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano, 2003) and subsequent conflicting results. The proposed coherence effect provides an independent method to study $\Theta^+$ production mechanisms and properties, potentially clarifying the existence and nature of the pentaquark state.
