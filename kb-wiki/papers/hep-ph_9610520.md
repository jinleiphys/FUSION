# Suppression of PiNN(1535) Coupling in the QCD Sum Rule

> The πNN^* and ηNN^* coupling constants are studied based on the two point functions between the vacuum and a one meson state in the soft meson limit. In terms of a suitably constructed interpolating field for the nucleon and resonances, we have found that the πNN^* couplings vanish, while ηNN^* couplings remain finite. This result explains a relatively suppressed coupling of πNN(1535) as compared with others. We compare the present result with predictions of low energy effective models.

# Suppression of πNN(1535) Coupling in the QCD Sum Rule

## Key claim

The πNN* coupling constant vanishes in the chiral limit (m_q → 0) when computed using QCD sum rules with a suitably chosen interpolating field, while the ηNN* coupling remains finite. This result is a general consequence of chiral symmetry and explains the experimentally observed suppression of the πNN(1535) coupling relative to other meson-baryon couplings.

## Method

The authors compute two-point correlation functions between the vacuum and a one-meson state (π or η) in the soft meson limit (q^μ → 0), using an interpolating field for the nucleon with a mixing parameter t ≈ 0.8 that couples strongly to negative-parity states. The correlation function is evaluated via operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 8, and the soft meson theorem is applied to relate vacuum-to-meson matrix elements to quark condensates. The phenomenological side of the sum rule isolates the term proportional to p-slash γ_5, which exclusively contains transitions from negative-parity resonances to the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Experimental coupling constants: g_{πNN*} ∼ 0.7 and g_{ηNN*} ∼ 2, compared to g_{πNN} ∼ 13 and g_{ηNN} ≤ 5 in the NN sector
- The mixing parameter t = 0.8 is used for the interpolating field to couple strongly to N(1535)
- The OPE coefficients C_4, C_6, and C_8 are computed; C_4 vanishes in the chiral limit (m_q → 0), and the overall πNN* correlation function is of order O(m_q)
- The soft meson theorem yields α_π = 1/√2 for the pion and α_η = 1/√6 for the eta

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) for πNN couplings to the negative-parity resonance sector, building on the authors' previous mass calculations for negative-parity octet baryons (Jido, Kodama, Oka, 1996). The result is consistent with predictions from the nonrelativistic quark model (Isgur-Karl model), large-N_c baryon methods, and the parity-doublet chiral model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989). The vanishing of πNN* coupling in the chiral limit provides a symmetry-based explanation for the experimentally observed suppression, and the method enables future sum rule analyses of ηNN* coupling constants.
