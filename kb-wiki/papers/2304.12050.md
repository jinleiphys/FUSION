# Diquark and chiral condensate in a self-consistent NJL-type model

> In this work, a modified two-flavor NJL-type model is utilized, in which the contact current-current interaction is Fierz-transformed into quark-antiquark interactions and quark-quark interactions, which are directly related to the chiral condensate and diquark condensate, respectively. Under mean-field approximation, the chiral condensate and the diquark condensate are studied on the same footing. We discuss in detail the competition between the chiral condensate and the diquark condensate, which are exclusively paired with u and d quarks, while also investigating the order of the chiral phase transition through an analysis of the resulting chiral susceptibility.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the competition between chiral and diquark condensates in a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type model depends sensitively on the weighting factor α, which controls the relative strength of quark-antiquark and quark-quark interactions after Fierz transformation; the commonly used ratio h₁/g_s^(0) = 3/4 (α = 0.5) is not physically required and varying α changes the order of the chiral phase transition.

## Method

The authors start from a contact current-current interaction and apply a Fierz transformation to decompose it into quark-antiquark (q̄q) and quark-quark (qq) channels, then combine them using a weighting factor α. Under mean-field approximation, they derive self-consistent gap equations for the chiral condensate, scalar diquark condensate Δ₁, vector diquark condensate Δ₂, and color-octet condensates, solving them at zero temperature and finite chemical potential for two-flavor quark matter in the 2SC phase.

## Key numbers

- The scalar diquark gap Δ₁ reaches approximately 100 MeV for α = 0.5, consistent with previous NJL model studies.
- The scalar diquark gap Δ₁ begins to grow smoothly when the quark chemical potential μ ≳ 290 MeV for α = 0.5.
- The bare quark mass is set to m = 5.5 MeV, with g_s^(0) = 5.074 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻² and a three-momentum cutoff Λ = 631 MeV, adopted from Ref. (1989, Nucl. Phys. A 504, 668).
- The coupling ratio h₁/g_s^(0) ranges from 0.5 to 3, corresponding to α varying from 0.6 to 0.2.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL-type model studies of color superconductivity (e.g., Buballa 2005, Phys. Rep. 407, 205; Alford et al. 2008, Rev. Mod. Phys. 80, 1455) by treating chiral and diquark condensates on equal footing through a Fierz-transformed interaction with a variable weighting factor α, rather than fixing the ratio h₁/g_s^(0) = 3/4 as commonly assumed. The inclusion of both scalar and vector color-octet interactions and the vector diquark condensate Δ₂ enables a more complete description of the 2SC phase ground state, with potential applications to the QCD phase diagram and neutron star matter.
