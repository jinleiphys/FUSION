# QCD Sum Rule Study of the Masses of Light Tetraquark Scalar Mesons

> We study the low-lying scalar mesons of light u, d, s flavors in the QCD sum rule. Having all possible combinations of tetraquark currents in the local form, QCD sum rule analysis has been carefully performed. We found that using the appropriate tetraquark currents, the masses of sigma, kappa, f_0 and a_0 mesons appear in the region of 0.6 -- 1 GeV with the expected ordering. The results are compared with that of the conventional \bar{q} q currents, where the masses are considerably larger.

## Key claim

Using carefully chosen linear combinations of tetraquark currents in QCD sum rule analysis, the masses of the low-lying scalar mesons sigma, kappa, f0 and a0 appear in the 0.6–1 GeV region with the expected ordering, whereas conventional quark-antiquark currents yield considerably larger masses.

## Method

The authors constructed all possible independent local tetraquark currents (scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, pseudoscalar) for the scalar meson nonet using diquark-antidiquark combinations in both antisymmetric and symmetric flavor configurations. They computed the two-point correlation functions in the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension eight, keeping finite quark masses, and performed Borel-transformed QCD sum rule analyses to extract masses. The optimal currents were found by taking linear combinations of the axial-vector and vector currents (e.g., eta_sigma = cos(theta) A6_sigma + sin(theta) V3_sigma with cot(theta) = 1/sqrt(2)).

## Key numbers

* Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = (0.6 ± 0.1) GeV, from tetraquark current sum rule with Borel mass ~0.4 GeV and s0 in range 0.5–1.5 GeV^2
* Kappa meson mass: m_kappa = (0.8 ± 0.1) GeV, from tetraquark current sum rule with Borel mass ~0.5 GeV and s0 in range 1–2 GeV^2
* f0 and a0 meson masses: m_f0,a0 = (1.0 ± 0.1) GeV, from tetraquark current sum rule with Borel mass ~0.8 GeV and s0 in range 1.5–2.5 GeV^2
* Quark condensate value: ⟨qq⟩ = -(0.240 GeV)^3; strange quark condensate: ⟨ss⟩ = -(0.8 ± 0.1) × (0.240 GeV)^3
* Gluon condensate: ⟨g_s^2 GG⟩ = (0.48 ± 0.14) GeV^4
* Strange quark mass at 1 GeV: m_s(1 GeV) = 125 ± 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark interpretation of light scalar mesons originally proposed by Jaffe (1976) using diquark correlations, and contrasts with conventional quark model predictions where p-wave qqbar states are expected above 1 GeV. The results support the tetraquark picture over the qqbar interpretation and enable future studies of decay properties and exotic multiquark dynamics, as the authors note that a large part of the mass comes from the gluon condensate rather than the chiral condensate.
