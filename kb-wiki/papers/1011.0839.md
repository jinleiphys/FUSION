# Shear and bulk viscosities for pure glue matter

> Shear $η$ and bulk $ζ$ viscosities are calculated in a quasiparticle model within a relaxation time approximation for pure gluon matter. Below $T_c$ the confined sector is described within a quasiparticle glueball model. Particular attention is paid to behavior of the shear and bulk viscosities near $T_c$. The constructed equation of state reproduces the first-order phase transition for the glue matter. It is shown that with this equation of state it is possible to describe the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio $η/s$ and the bulk viscosity to entropy ratio $ζ/s$ in reasonable agreement with available lattice data but absolute values of the $ζ/s$ ratio underestimate the upper limits of this ratio in the lattice measurements typically by an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle model with relaxation-time approximation can describe the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio and the bulk viscosity to entropy ratio for pure glue matter in reasonable agreement with available lattice data, but the calculated absolute values of the bulk viscosity to entropy ratio underestimate the upper limits of this ratio in the lattice measurements typically by an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors constructed a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model for pure SU(3) gluon matter, using temperature-dependent gluon masses above the critical temperature T_c and temperature-dependent glueball masses for scalar 0++ and tensor 2++ glueball states below T_c. The equation of state was built by matching the gluon and glueball phases via Gibbs conditions at T_c = 265 MeV, and the shear and bulk viscosities were computed within the relaxation-time approximation using two different parametrizations for the gluon relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 265 MeV for the first-order phase transition, taken from lattice SU(3) gluodynamics
- Glueball masses used: scalar 0++ with m_gb^0 = 1470 MeV and tensor 2++ with m_gb^0 = 2150 MeV
- For the shear viscosity to entropy ratio, the minimum value at T slightly above T_c is close to the AdS/CFT bound of 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- The bulk viscosity to entropy ratio at T = 1.02 T_c from lattice data is ζ/s ≈ 0.5 to 2, which the model underestimates by about an order of magnitude
- The ratio ζ/η ≈ 0.3 at T → T_c+0, decreasing to ζ/η ∼ 10^{-2} to 10^{-3} for T > 2 T_c
- Glueball scattering cross section assumed isotropic at σ_gb = 30 mb

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle approach previously applied to gluon matter by Peshier and Cassing (2005) and by Bluhm, Kampfer, and Redlich (2009), using newer lattice data from Panero (2009) for the equation of state and from Meyer (2007) and Sakai and Nakamura (2007) for viscosity coefficients. The model enables comparison of different relaxation-time parametrizations and highlights the need for additional physics, such as soft mode contributions near the critical point, to reproduce the lattice bulk viscosity results.
