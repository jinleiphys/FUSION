# Dilepton Helical Production in a Vortical Quark-Gluon Plasma

> In this paper, we propose an observable counting a weighted difference between right-handed and left-handed lepton pairs, which is coined dilepton helical rate. The weight is the momentum difference of the lepton pairs projected onto an auxiliary vector. We derive the helical rate in a quark-gluon plasma with a vorticity in the limit when the quark and lepton masses are ignored. We find the helical rate is maximized when the auxiliary vector is parallel to the vorticity, in which case it has a nearly spherical oblate ellipsoidal distribution. We propose that it can be used as a vortical-meter for quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new observable called the "dilepton helical rate," which is a weighted difference between right-handed and left-handed lepton pairs, and claim it can serve as a "vortical-meter" for quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The hedge is that this result is derived in the limit where quark and lepton masses are ignored, and the vorticity is treated as a perturbation to first order.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop photon self-energy in a QGP with vorticity, using the Kadanoff-Baym equation in the collisionless limit to obtain the first-order vortical correction to the quark propagator. They then perform a spin sum separating right-handed and left-handed lepton pairs, constructing an antisymmetric tensor \(h^{\mu\nu}\) for their difference. To obtain a nonvanishing result, they introduce a weight \((P'-P)\cdot \hat{n}\) (the momentum difference projected onto an auxiliary vector \(\hat{n}\)) into the phase space integration, and contract the resulting weighted tensor \(H_{\mu\nu}\) with the vortical correction to the photon self-energy.

## Key numbers

* The helical rate in the Boltzmann approximation (\(q_0 \gg T\) and \(q_0 \gg q\)) gives \(R_1 = \frac{e^{-q_0/T}}{3T} q_0 (-q^2 + q_0^2)\) and \(R_2 = 0\), where \(q_0\) is the virtual photon energy and \(q\) is its momentum magnitude.
* For generic \(q_0\) and \(q\), \(R_1\) is about twenty times that of \(R_2\), with \(R_1\) positive and \(R_2\) negative.
* The weighted tensor \(H_{\mu\nu}\) evaluates to \(H_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{iQ^2}{12\pi} \epsilon_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} Q^\rho \hat{n}^\sigma\), where \(Q^2 = q_0^2 - q^2\).

## Context

This work extends studies of polarized dilepton production from vorticity (Wei 2021) and other sources such as magnetic fields (Yee 2013) and chiral imbalance (Mamo 2013, 2015). It contrasts with unpolarized dilepton rates, where the vortical correction vanishes due to cancellation between right-handed and left-handed pairs. The proposed observable enables experimental measurement of QGP vorticity direction via the angular distribution of dileptons, using event-by-event reaction plane determination as employed by the STAR collaboration (2017).
