# Total neutrino and antineutrino nuclear cross sections around 1 GeV

> We investigate neutrino-nucleus interactions at energies around 1 GeV. In this regime, the main contributions to the cross sections come from quasi-elastic and $Δ$ production processes. Our formalism, based on the Impulse Approximation is well suited to describe both types of interactions. We focus on a series of important nuclear effects in the interaction of electron neutrinos with $^{16}O$, also relevant to future $β$-Beams. Our results show that the Fermi gas model, widely used in data analysis of neutrino experiments, overestimates the total cross sections by as much as 20 %.

## Key claim

The Fermi gas model, widely used in neutrino experiment data analysis, overestimates total neutrino-nucleus cross sections by as much as 20% when compared to a more realistic treatment based on nuclear many-body theory and the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for quasi-elastic scattering and Delta resonance production on oxygen-16 using the impulse approximation, where the nuclear target is described by a spectral function obtained from nuclear many-body theory via the local density approximation. The spectral function incorporates nucleon-nucleon correlations and Pauli blocking effects, and the elementary neutrino-nucleon cross sections are folded with the nucleon momentum and removal energy distributions.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi gas model overestimates total cross sections by as much as 20% compared to the spectral function approach.
- Pauli blocking reduces the cross section by 10% at neutrino energy 0.5 GeV and by 6% at 1.5 GeV.
- For oxygen, the average Fermi momentum used in Pauli blocking is 209 MeV, derived from the nuclear density distribution.
- The vector mass used is M_V = 0.84 GeV and the axial mass is M_A = 1.05 GeV.
- The Fermi gas model parameters for oxygen are Fermi momentum p_F = 225 MeV and binding energy E_B = 25 MeV, from electron scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) used in event generators such as NUANCE, NEUGEN, and NEUT, which neglect important nucleon-nucleon correlations. The results are compared to calculations by Nieves (2004) and Maieron (2003), showing satisfactory agreement with the former and excellent agreement with the latter when final state interactions are omitted, consistent with the sum rule argument that total inclusive cross sections are not affected by final state interactions. The formalism is relevant for future beta-beam experiments and for reducing nuclear uncertainties in neutrino oscillation measurements.
