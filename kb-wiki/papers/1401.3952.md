# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance for drip-line and super heavy nuclei in the framework of a relativistic mean field formalism with scaling calculation

> We study the isoscalar giant monopole resonance for drip-lines and super heavy nuclei in the frame work of a relativistic mean field theory with scaling approach. The well known extended Thomas-Fermi approximation in the non-linear $σ$-$ω$ model is used to estimate the giant monopole excitation energy for some selected light spherical nuclei starting from the region of proton to neutron drip-lines. The application is extended to super heavy region for Z=114 and 120, which are predicted by several models as the next proton magic number beyond Z=82. We compared the excitation energy obtained by four successful force parameters NL1, NL3, NL3$^*$ and FSUGold. The monopole energy decreases toward the proton and neutron drip-lines in an isotopic chain for lighter mass nuclei contrary to a monotonous decrease for super heavy isotopes. The maximum and minimum monopole excitation energies are obtained for nuclei with minimum and maximum isospin, respectively in an isotopic chain.

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) excitation energy decreases toward both proton and neutron drip-lines in an isotopic chain for lighter mass nuclei, but shows a monotonous decrease from proton to neutron drip-line for super heavy nuclei (Z=114 and 120), with the maximum and minimum excitation energies occurring for nuclei with minimum and maximum isospin, respectively.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field formalism with a scaling approach, employing the relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximation in the non-linear sigma-omega model to estimate the ISGMR excitation energy. Both scaling and constrained self-consistent calculations were performed for ground state densities, and the monopole excitation energy was obtained from the restoring force and mass parameter derived from the scaled Hamiltonian. The calculations were carried out for selected light spherical nuclei from proton to neutron drip-lines and extended to super heavy nuclei with Z=114 and 120, using four force parameter sets: NL1, NL3, NL3*, and FSUGold.

## Key numbers

* For O isotopes, the scaling excitation energy decreases from 27.83 MeV at N=Z (16O) to 22.51 MeV at the proton drip-line (A=12) and 21.22 MeV at the neutron drip-line (A=26).
* For Ca isotopes, the maximum scaling excitation energy is 24.07 MeV at 40Ca, decreasing to 23.31 MeV at the proton drip-line (A=34) and 16.80 MeV at the neutron drip-line (A=71).
* For Pb isotopes, the scaling excitation energy decreases monotonically from 15.63 MeV at the proton drip-line (180Pb) to 11.45 MeV at the neutron drip-line (280Pb).
* For super heavy nuclei, the scaling excitation energy for 286114 is 13.00 MeV (NL3) and for 304120 is 12.67 MeV (NL3).
* The difference in excitation energy between proton and neutron drip nuclei is 5.32 MeV for Z=50, 4.31 MeV for Z=82, and 2.37 MeV for Z=114.
* The resonance width Sigma for 16O is 5.0 MeV, while for 12O it is 10.92 MeV and for 26O it is 21.62 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of ISGMR to drip-line and super heavy nuclei using a scaling and constrained approach developed by Patra et al. (2001, 2002), which had not been extensively explored for these regions of the periodic chart. The results are compared with experimental data for known nuclei such as 208Pb (14.17 ± 0.28 MeV) and various Sn isotopes, showing that the RETF approximation yields better agreement for heavier masses. The study enables further investigation of the relationship between finite nuclear compressibility and infinite nuclear matter values, which is relevant for understanding the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star properties.
