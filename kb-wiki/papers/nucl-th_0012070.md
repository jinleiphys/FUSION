# Multi-electron SEFs for nuclear reactions involved in advanced stages of stellar evolution

> Multi-electron screening effects encountered in laboratory astrophysical reactions are investigated by considering the reactants Thomas-Fermi atoms. By means of that model, previous studies are extended to derive the corresponding screening enhancement factor (SEF), so that it takes into account ionization, thermal, exchange and relativistic effects. The present study, by imposing a very satisfactory constraint on the possible values of the screening energies and the respective SEFs, corrects the current (and the future) experimental values of the astrophysical factors associated with nuclear reactions involved in advanced stages of stellar evolution.

# Multi-electron SEFs for nuclear reactions involved in advanced stages of stellar evolution

## Key claim
The Thomas-Fermi model provides a tightened constraint on screening enhancement factors (SEFs) for nuclear reactions of the CNO bi-cycle, with the sudden limit (SL) and adiabatic limit (AL) screening energies differing by roughly 100 eV or less across the ionization profile, which the author claims is a very satisfactory constraint for most practical purposes. The non-relativistic SL SEF analytic formula overestimates the CNO SEFs by roughly 2% at Gamow peak energies, with the discrepancy becoming larger at lower energies.

## Method
The author uses the Thomas-Fermi (TF) atomic model to derive screening enhancement factors for nuclear reactions, employing Baker's small-x expansion for the TF equation to obtain the self-consistent potential near the nucleus, rather than the less accurate Tietz approximation used in prior work. The sudden limit (SL) and adiabatic limit (AL) screening energies and SEFs are derived analytically as functions of the ionization degree q, and relativistic corrections are incorporated via the Vallarta-Rosen relativistic TF equation and Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater calculations. Numerical integration of penetration factors is performed for the non-relativistic and relativistic regimes for the CNO bi-cycle reactions.

## Key numbers
- The lower limit for neutral atoms given by the new SL formula is 40% larger than the corresponding one from Liolios (2000), narrowing the gap between sudden and adiabatic limit SEFs.
- For the reaction C13(p,gamma)N14, the DHFS relativistic SL screening energy yields U_DHFS^SL = 406 eV, which is lower than the non-relativistic SL screening energy.
- For a bare Carbon nucleus (Z=6) colliding with an atom of the same nuclear charge with only two electrons in its closed K-shell, the Hartree-Fock SL screening energy is U_HF^SL = 1760 eV, while the TF SL model for q=0 gives U_TF^SL = 3191 eV.
- The SL and AL screening energy limits for CNO bi-cycle reactions differ by roughly 100 eV or less across the ionization profile.
- The solar Gamow peak for N14(p,gamma)O15 is E_GP = 27.2 keV; for C13(p,gamma)N14 it is 24.5 keV; for O16(p,gamma)F17 it is 29.8 keV.
- The non-relativistic SL analytic formula overestimates CNO SEFs by roughly 2% at Gamow peak energies.

## Context
This work extends the Thomas-Fermi screening model previously applied to proton-proton chain reactions by Liolios (2000) and Shoppa et al. (1996) to multi-electron screening in reactions involving heavier nuclei, such as those in the CNO bi-cycle and advanced stellar burning stages. The improved sudden limit using Baker's expansion corrects the earlier Tietz approximation used in Liolios (2000), while the adiabatic limit follows the approach of Assebaum, Langanke, and Rolfs (1987). The study enables more accurate correction of experimental astrophysical factors for reactions relevant to solar center, upper main-sequence stars, and classical novae, particularly as future low-energy experiments (following the LUNA collaboration's work on He3(He3,2p)He4) probe deeper into the Gamow peak region for heavier-ion reactions.
