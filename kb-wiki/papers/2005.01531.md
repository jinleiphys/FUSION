# The X(3872) as a mass distribution

> All existing experimental evidence of the bound state nature of the X(3872) relies on considering its decay products with a finite experimental spectral mass resolution which is typically $Δm \ge 2$ MeV and much larger than its alleged binding energy, $B_X =0.00(18)$MeV. On the other hand, there is a neat cancellation in the $1^{++} $ channel for the invariant $D {\bar D}^*$ mass around the threshold between the continuum and bound state contribution. We discuss the impact of this effect for X(3872) at finite temperature, in prompt production in pp collisions data with a finite $p_T$ or the lineshapes of specific production experiments of exotic states involving triangle singularities

## Key claim

The X(3872) should be regarded as a mass distribution rather than a single particle state because the experimental mass resolution (Δm ≥ 2 MeV) is much larger than its alleged binding energy (BX = 0.00(18) MeV), and there is a neat cancellation in the 1++ channel between the bound state and continuum contributions to the invariant D D* mass around threshold.

## Method

The authors apply the Dashen-Kane formalism for the level density of two-particle states, writing the density of states ρ(m) as a sum over bound state masses plus the derivative of scattering phase shifts with respect to mass. They compute the occupation number at finite temperature using the quantum virial expansion, and extend the analysis to pT distributions using a Tsallis distribution to model production in pp collisions. They also simulate the effect of finite detector resolution by folding the level density with a Gaussian smearing function and performing Monte Carlo sampling for different sample sizes.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of X(3872): BX = 0.00(18) MeV
- Experimental spectral mass resolution: Δm ≥ 2 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.2 MeV, with total mass Md = 2.013 GeV
- Gaussian smearings applied to level density: σ = 1 MeV, with energy window Ew = 3 MeV and ΔM = 20 MeV for lineshape simulations
- Monte Carlo samples: N = 100 and N = 1000 events for smeared lineshape profiles
- Binding energy used in simulations: Eb = 180 keV (both positive and negative values tested)

## Context

This work extends the Dashen-Kane cancellation argument (Dashen and Kane, 1974), originally applied to the deuteron as a weakly bound proton-neutron state, to the X(3872) as a weakly bound D D* molecular candidate. It contradicts interpretations that the large production cross section of X(3872) in pp collisions implies a compact tetraquark state (Esposito et al., 2015), showing instead that deuteron and X(3872) production data are compatible with the same Tsallis distribution parameters. The results enable a critical reassessment of proposed precision mass measurements using triangle singularities (Guo et al., 2019; Braaten et al., 2019), showing that the required statistics to discriminate binding energy signals are much larger than previously assumed.
