# Toward ab initio charge symmetry breaking in nuclear energy density functionals

> We propose a new approach to determine the strength of the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) term in the framework of nuclear density functional theory. It is shown that once ab initio calculations are available including accurate description of isospin symmetry breaking terms in medium and heavy nuclei, the mass difference of mirror nuclei as well as the neutron-skin thickness of doubly-closed-shell nuclei can be used to constrain the strength of the CSB interaction with an uncertainty less than $ 6 \, \% $, separately from other isospin symmetry breaking forces. This method opens a new vista of ab initio nuclear energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The authors propose that once *ab initio* calculations including isospin symmetry breaking become available for medium and heavy nuclei, the mass difference of mirror nuclei and the neutron-skin thickness of doubly-closed-shell nuclei can constrain the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) strength in a nuclear energy density functional with an uncertainty less than 6%, separately from other isospin symmetry breaking forces. The method assumes that the *ab initio* results themselves carry negligible uncertainty.

## Method

The authors add a Skyrme-like zero-range CSB interaction to several existing Skyrme energy density functionals (SLy4, SLy5, SkM*, SAMi, SAMi-noISB, and SAMi-CIB) and compute the total energy difference of the mirror pair ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ni, as well as the neutron-skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, as a function of the CSB strength parameter s₀. They fit the linear dependence of these observables on s₀ and compute the average slope across functionals to determine the uncertainty in extracting s₀ from *ab initio* input.

## Key numbers

- The slope b of ΔE_tot (mass difference of ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ni) versus s₀ has an average value of -0.3079 fm⁻³ with a standard deviation of 0.0167 fm⁻³, giving a relative uncertainty of 5.437%.
- The slope b of ΔR_np (neutron-skin thickness) for ⁴⁸Ca versus s₀ has an average value of -0.9589 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ fm⁴ with a standard deviation of 0.0364 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ fm⁴, giving a relative uncertainty of 3.795%.
- The slope b of ΔR_np for ²⁰⁸Pb versus s₀ has an average value of -1.0605 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ fm⁴ with a standard deviation of 0.0297 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ fm⁴, giving a relative uncertainty of 2.805%.
- The CIB strength u₀ = 25.8 MeV fm³ was previously determined from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of symmetric nuclear matter.
- The original SAMi-ISB CSB strength s₀ = -26.3 MeV fm³ was determined from the isobaric analog energy of ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine isospin symmetry breaking terms in Skyrme EDFs, specifically the determination of the CIB strength by Roca-Maza et al. (2018) using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and the determination of CSB and CIB strengths from isobaric multiplet mass equations by Baczyk et al. (2018, 2019). The proposed method enables a separate constraint on the CSB interaction using *ab initio* results for mirror nuclei mass differences and neutron-skin thickness, which are more accessible to *ab initio* methods than the isobaric analog energy used previously. This opens a path toward constructing *ab initio* nuclear energy density functionals by combining *ab initio* calculations with phenomenological EDFs.
