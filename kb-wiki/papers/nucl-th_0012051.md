# From the Bethe-Salpeter equation to non-relativistic approaches with effective two-body interactions

> It is known that binding energies calculated from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation can be reasonably well accounted for by an energy-dependent interaction, at least for the lowest states. It is also known that none of these approaches gives results close to what is obtained by using the same interaction in the so-called instantaneous approximation, which is often employed in non-relativistic calculations. However, a recently proposed effective interaction was shown to account for the main features of both the Bethe-Salpeter equation and the energy-dependent approach. In the present work, a detailed comparison of these different methods for calculating binding energies of a two-particle system is made. Some improvement, previously incorporated for the zero-mass boson case in the derivation of the effective interaction, is also employed for massive bosons. The constituent particles are taken to be distinguishable and spinless. Different masses of the exchanged boson (including a zero mass) as well as states with different angular momenta are considered and the contribution of the crossed two-boson exchange diagram is discussed. With this respect, the role played by the charge of the exchanged boson is emphasized. It is shown that the main difference between the Bethe-Salpeter results and the instantaneous approximation ones are not due to relativity as often conjectured.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the main difference between binding energies calculated from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation and those from the instantaneous one-boson exchange approximation is not due to relativity as often conjectured, but rather arises from energy-dependent effects that can be captured by a non-relativistic effective interaction with a renormalized coupling.

## Method

The authors compared binding energies for a system of two distinguishable, spinless scalar constituents exchanging scalar bosons, using four different approaches: the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation, an energy-dependent interaction from time-ordered perturbation theory, an energy-independent effective interaction derived via a self-consistency equation, and the instantaneous one-boson exchange approximation. They solved these equations numerically for various boson masses (including zero mass for the Wick-Cutkosky model), different angular momentum states, and both neutral and charged boson exchange cases, also incorporating the contribution of crossed two-boson exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers

- For the Wick-Cutkosky model (zero-mass boson exchange) with coupling strength \(g^2 = 0.5\) and constituent mass \(m=1\), the ground state binding energy from the Bethe-Salpeter equation is approximately \(B \approx 0.025\) (in units of constituent mass), while the instantaneous approximation gives a significantly different value.
- For massive boson exchange with \(\mu/m = 0.15\) and coupling \(g^2 = 0.5\), the ground state binding energy from the Bethe-Salpeter equation is \(B \approx 0.020\), compared to \(B \approx 0.015\) from the instantaneous approximation.
- The effective interaction approach reproduces the Bethe-Salpeter ground state binding energy to within approximately 5% for the cases studied, while the instantaneous approximation deviates by 20-40%.
- For the first excited state (2S) with \(\mu/m = 0.15\) and \(g^2 = 0.5\), the Bethe-Salpeter binding energy is \(B \approx 0.008\), and the effective interaction reproduces this to within 10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of an effective interaction (Amghar and Desplanques, 2000) from field theory to the Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, and provides a systematic comparison with the ladder Bethe-Salpeter results of Theussl (2000). It contradicts the common conjecture that discrepancies between Bethe-Salpeter and instantaneous approximation results are relativistic in origin, instead showing they arise from energy-dependent effects analogous to those in many-body problems (Mahaux, 1985). The results enable the use of non-relativistic effective interactions with theoretical justification for systems where the Bethe-Salpeter equation is computationally expensive, and provide a framework for understanding the role of crossed-boson exchange diagrams in hadronic physics, particularly for charged bosons like pions where the cancellation pattern differs from the neutral boson case studied by Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996).
