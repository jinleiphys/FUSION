# Lepton Asymmetries in FCNC Decays of Λb

> Lepton polarization asymmetries for the flavor-changing neutral current (FCNC) dileptonic decays of Λb baryons are calculated using single-component analytic (SCA) and multicomponent numerical (MCN) form factors. We show that these polarization asymmetries are insensitive to the transition form factors and, thus, the effects of QCD in the nonperturbative regime. Therefore, these observables can provide somewhat model independent ways of extracting the Wilson coefficients.

## Key claim

The longitudinal lepton polarization asymmetries (LLPAs) for the FCNC decays \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda^{(*)} \ell^+ \ell^-\) are largely insensitive to the choice of transition form factors and thus to nonperturbative QCD effects, but the paper qualifies this by showing the results for two specific form-factor models (single-component analytic and multi-component numerical) and for one supersymmetric scenario, with the claim of model independence supported by the near-indistinguishability of the differential LLPAs from the free-quark and leading-order results.

## Method

The authors calculated integrated and differential longitudinal lepton polarization asymmetries for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda^{(*)} \ell^+ \ell^-\) (\(\ell = \mu, \tau\)) using two sets of form factors: single-component analytic (SCA) and multi-component numerical (MCN) form factors from earlier work. They compared results obtained with Standard Model Wilson coefficients (SM1 with SCA, SM2 with MCN) and with Wilson coefficients from a supersymmetric scenario (SUSY, using MCN form factors), and also examined the effect of including or omitting long-distance contributions from charmonium resonances.

## Key numbers

- Integrated LLPA for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \mu^+ \mu^-\): SM1 = \(-58.1 \times 10^{-2}\), SM2 = \(-58.5 \times 10^{-2}\), SUSY = \(-53.2 \times 10^{-2}\) (long-distance contributions omitted).
- Integrated LLPA for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \mu^+ \mu^-\) with long-distance contributions included: SM1 = \(-51.6 \times 10^{-2}\), SM2 = \(-51.9 \times 10^{-2}\), SUSY = \(-49.1 \times 10^{-2}\).
- Integrated LLPA for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \tau^+ \tau^-\): SM1 = \(-10.7 \times 10^{-2}\), SM2 = \(-10.8 \times 10^{-2}\), SUSY = \(-8.2 \times 10^{-2}\) (long-distance contributions omitted).
- Integrated LLPA for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \tau^+ \tau^-\) with long-distance contributions included: SM1 = \(-10.2 \times 10^{-2}\), SM2 = \(-10.3 \times 10^{-2}\), SUSY = \(-8.1 \times 10^{-2}\).
- Integrated LLPA for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1890) \tau^+ \tau^-\): SM1 = \(-0.81 \times 10^{-2}\), SM2 = \(-0.80 \times 10^{-2}\), SUSY = \(-0.58 \times 10^{-2}\) (long-distance contributions omitted).
- The differential LLPA curves for \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \ell^+ \ell^-\) from the SM2 calculation, the free-quark process, and the leading-order result are described as "nearly indistinguishable."

## Context

This work extends calculations of lepton polarization asymmetries in \(\Lambda_b\) FCNC decays beyond the ground-state \(\Lambda(1115)\) to excited \(\Lambda^{(*)}\) states, using form factors from quark-model calculations by the same authors. The results agree with those of Chen et al., who used QCD sum rules and a pole model for the form factors. The insensitivity of LLPAs to form-factor models suggests they can serve as relatively clean probes of the Wilson coefficients in the effective Hamiltonian, and the authors note that transverse and normal polarization components, as well as baryon polarization asymmetries, may also be useful for searching for physics beyond the Standard Model.
