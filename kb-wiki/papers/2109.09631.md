# Green-Kubo formula for Boltzmann and Fermi-Dirac statistics

> Shear viscosity of nuclear matter is extracted via the Green-Kubo formula and the Gaussian thermostated SLLOD algorithm (the shear rate method) in a periodic box by using an improved quantum molecular dynamic (ImQMD) model without mean field, also it is calculated by a Boltzmann-type equation. Here a new form of the Green-Kubo formula is put forward in the present work. For classical limit at nuclear matter densities of $0.4ρ_{0}$ and $1.0ρ_{0}$, shear viscosity by the traditional and new form of the Green-Kubo formula as well as the SLLOD algorithm are coincident with each other. However, for non-classical limit, shear viscosity by the traditional form of the Green-Kubo formula is higher than those obtained by the new form of the Green-Kubo formula as well as the SLLOD algorithm especially in low temperature region. In addition, shear viscosity from the Boltzmann-type equation is found to be less than that by the Green-Kubo method or the SLLOD algorithm for both classical and non-classical limits.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new form of the Green-Kubo formula for shear viscosity that replaces the temperature factor with a momentum-based normalization, and shows that the standard Green-Kubo formula overestimates shear viscosity in fermionic systems at low temperatures, while the new form agrees with the SLLOD algorithm for both classical and non-classical limits.

## Method

Shear viscosity of symmetric nuclear matter was extracted using three approaches within an improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model without mean field in a periodic box: the traditional Green-Kubo formula, a newly derived Green-Kubo formula, and the Gaussian thermostated SLLOD algorithm (shear rate method). Additionally, shear viscosity was calculated from a linear Boltzmann-type equation with Pauli blocking. The system was initialized with Fermi-Dirac momentum distributions, and simulations were run with and without Pauli blocking at densities of 0.4ρ₀ and 1.0ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity was calculated at densities of 0.4ρ₀ and 1.0ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density of nuclear matter.
- The particle number A was fixed at 600 and the total nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_NN was fixed at 40 mb.
- Shear rates used in the SLLOD algorithm: 0.0003 c/fm for T₀ = 4 MeV, 0.0005 c/fm for T₀ = 6 MeV, and 0.002 c/fm for T₀ ≥ 10 MeV.
- The wave-packet width σ in the ImQMD model was taken as 2.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shear viscosity in nuclear matter using transport models and molecular dynamics methods, such as the Green-Kubo formula and SLLOD algorithm used in molecular dynamics simulations (Evans and Morriss, 2006; Daivis and Evans, 1994). The new form of the Green-Kubo formula corrects for the failure of the standard form in fermionic systems at low temperatures, where Pauli blocking effects are strong. The results enable more accurate extraction of shear viscosity from transport simulations of nuclear matter, particularly for comparisons with the KSS bound (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005) and experimental data from relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
