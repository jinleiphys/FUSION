# Meson-Baryon Interactions in Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Meson-Baryon Interactions can be successfully described using both Chiral Symmetry and Unitarity. The $s-$wave meson-baryon scattering amplitude is analyzed in a Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating Chiral Symmetry in the potential. Two body coupled channel unitarity is exactly preserved. The needed two particle irreducible matrix amplitude is taken from lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory in a relativistic formalism. Off-shell behavior is parameterized in terms of low energy constants. The relation to the heavy baryon limit is discussed. The position of the complex poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude determine masses and widths baryonic resonances of the N(1535), N(1670), $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1670)$ resonances which compare well with accepted numbers.

## Key claim

Meson-baryon scattering amplitudes can be successfully described by combining chiral symmetry constraints with exact two-body unitarity via a Bethe-Salpeter equation, provided off-shell effects are parameterized through renormalized low-energy constants; the approach reproduces the masses and widths of the N(1535), N(1670), Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) resonances, but the authors note that the extracted pole positions depend on the model-dependent analytic continuation to the second Riemann sheet.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in a coupled-channel formalism, using the lowest-order chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian as the two-particle irreducible potential. Free baryon and meson propagators are used in the loop integrals, and the resulting divergent quantities (J_{mM}, Δ_m, Δ_M) are treated as 12 renormalized free parameters fitted to experimental data for each isospin-strangeness channel. The on-shell scattering amplitude is obtained by matrix inversion in coupled-channel space, and resonance poles are located by analytically continuing the amplitude to the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

* πN scattering length (I=1/2, S=0): Re a = 0.179(4) fm (BSE result), compared to experimental value 0.252(6) fm
* ηN scattering length (I=1/2, S=0): Re a = 0.772(5) fm, Im a = 0.217(3) fm
* KΛ scattering length (I=1/2, S=0): Re a = 0.0547(5) fm, Im a = 0.032(4) fm
* πΣ scattering length (I=0, S=-1): Re a = 1.10(6) fm
* \bar{K}N scattering length (I=0, S=-1): Re a = -1.20(9) fm, Im a = 1.29(9) fm (BSE), compared to experimental Re a = -1.71 fm, Im a = 0.68 fm
* ηΛ scattering length (I=0, S=-1): Re a = 0.50(5) fm, Im a = 0.27(1) fm
* N(1535) resonance: M_R = 1497(1) MeV, Γ_R = 83(1) MeV (PDG: M=1505(10) MeV, Γ=170(80) MeV)
* N(1670) resonance: M_R = 1684(1) MeV, Γ_R = 194(1) MeV (PDG: M=1660(20) MeV, Γ=160(10) MeV)
* Λ(1405) resonance: M_R = 1443(3) MeV, Γ_R = 50(7) MeV (PDG: M=1407(4) MeV, Γ=50(2) MeV)
* Λ(1670) resonance: M_R = 1678(1) MeV, Γ_R = 29(2) MeV (PDG: M=1670(10) MeV, Γ=35(12) MeV)
* Unobserved resonance (I=0, S=-1): M_R = 1368(12) MeV, Γ_R = 250(23) MeV (compared to Jido et al. (2002): M=1390 MeV, Γ=66 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter coupled-channel approach previously applied to meson-meson scattering by Ecker et al. (1999) to the meson-baryon system, building on earlier studies by the same authors for heavy baryons (J. Nieves and E. Ruiz Arriola, 2001) and in a relativistic formulation (J. Nieves and E. Ruiz Arriola, 2001; C. Garcia-Recio et al., 2002). The approach contrasts with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) calculations by Mojzis (1998), Fettes et al. (1998), and Fettes and Meissner (2000), which fail to describe the \bar{K}N channel near threshold due to the nearby Λ(1405) resonance, as shown by Kaiser (2001). The results enable systematic extraction of resonance pole positions from unitarized chiral amplitudes, though the authors note that the matching to HBChPT reveals parameter redundancy and that the extracted branching ratios require model-dependent extrapolation to the real axis.
