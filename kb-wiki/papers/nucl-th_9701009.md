# Cluster reduction of the four-body Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for bound-state problem and low-energy scattering

> A method using an expansion of the four-body Yakubovsky wave function components onto the basis of the Faddeev-equation solutions for the two-cluster sub-Hamiltonian eigenfunctions is proposed. This expansion reduces the Yakubovsky differential equations to a system of coupled-channel equations for functions depending on the relative coordinates between the subsystems of the two-cluster partitions. On the basis of the resulting equations the four-nucleon bound-state problem and the zero-energy n-t scattering problem are solved on the relatively small computer.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a cluster reduction method that expands four-body Yakubovsky wave function components onto a basis of Faddeev-equation eigenfunctions for two-cluster subsystems, reducing the differential Yakubovsky equations to a system of coupled-channel equations that can be solved on a personal computer rather than requiring supercomputer facilities.

## Method

The authors expand the four-body Yakubovsky wave function components in a series of eigenfunctions of the Faddeev equations for the two-cluster sub-Hamiltonians, imposing boundary conditions on a cylinder of finite radius in the internal coordinates. This expansion reduces the differential Yakubovsky equations to a system of coupled-channel equations for functions depending only on the relative coordinate between the two clusters. The resulting equations are solved numerically using finite-difference approximations and spline expansions for the four-nucleon bound state (⁴He) and zero-energy n-³H scattering, using MT I-III and S3 s-wave nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

* ⁴He binding energy converged within less than 1% uncertainty using about 10 basis functions in the expansion
* Maximal nonorthogonality of the numerically constructed basis functions was ≤ 10⁻⁶ for l ≠ m
* For the spin-singlet n-³H scattering length A₀, the RGM approximation (using only one basis state) gave 3.41 fm, close to the result of Heiss (year not specified in text)
* All calculations were performed on a personal computer with Intel DX-50 processor and 16 Mb of RAM

## Context

This work extends the coupled reaction channel (CRC) decomposition approach previously introduced by Yakovlev and Filikhin (year not specified in text) for four-body systems, and contrasts with the method of Schellingerhout et al. (year not specified in text) which exploits tensor structure of discretized matrices. The cluster reduction enables direct four-body calculations of bound states and low-energy scattering on small computers, and the authors suggest it can be extended to more realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials and to systems with more than four particles.
