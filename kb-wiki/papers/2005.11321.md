# Decay of the pseudoscalar glueball into vector, axial-vector, scalar and pseudoscalar mesons

> We resume the investigation of the ground-state pseudoscalar glueball, $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$, by computing its two- and three-body decays into vector and axial-vector quark-antiquark meson fields additional to scalar and pseudoscalar mesons through the construction of an interaction chiral Lagrangian that produces these decays. We evaluate the branching ratio, via a parameter-free calculation, by setting the mass of the pseudoscalar glueball to $2.6$ GeV as predicted by lattice QCD simulations. We duplicate the computation for the branching ratios for a pseudoscalar glueball mass $2.37$ GeV which matches to the measured mass of the resonance $X(2370)$ in the BESIII experiment. The present channels and states are potentially reached and are interesting for the running BESIII and Belle-II experiments and the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR/GSI which will be able to detect the pseudoscalar glueball within the accessible energy range.

## Key claim

The paper computes branching ratios for two- and three-body decays of the ground-state pseudoscalar glueball into vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar mesons using a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian, but the absolute decay widths cannot be determined because the coupling constant remains unknown, so only relative branching ratios are presented.

## Method

The author constructs a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian within the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) that couples the pseudoscalar glueball field to the chiral multiplets of scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector quark-antiquark mesons. After shifting the scalar-isoscalar fields by their vacuum expectation values and applying wave-function renormalization constants from prior fits to experimental data, tree-level decay amplitudes are computed for two-body channels (PS, PV) and three-body channels (PPP, PPA, PPV, PSV). All branching ratios are normalized to the decay width of the channel \(\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta\) to eliminate the unknown coupling constant.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar glueball mass set to \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV (lattice QCD prediction) and \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV (matching the BESIII candidate \(X(2370)\)).
* For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow a_0\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.1913\); \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow KK\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.9126\); \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta^{\prime}}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.4654\).
* For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow a_0\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.1858\); \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow KK\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.8553\); \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta^{\prime}}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\eta}=0.3986\).
* Chiral condensates: \(\phi_N=0.158\) GeV, \(\phi_S=0.138\) GeV, derived from \(f_\pi=0.0922\) GeV and \(f_K=0.110\) GeV.
* Wave-function renormalization constants: \(Z_\pi=Z_{\eta_N}=1.70927\), \(Z_K=1.60406\), \(Z_{\eta_S}=1.53854\), \(Z_{K^*_0}=1.00105\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pseudoscalar glueball decays within the eLSM framework, which had already analyzed decays into scalar and pseudoscalar mesons (Eshraim 2015) and into nucleons (Eshraim 2016). The new contribution is the inclusion of vector and axial-vector meson final states, providing additional decay channels that could be searched for at the running BESIII and Belle-II experiments and the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR/GSI. The results confirm earlier predictions for the dominant \(KK\pi\) and \(\pi\pi\eta\) channels and show that the branching ratios depend only slightly on the glueball mass between 2.37 and 2.6 GeV.
