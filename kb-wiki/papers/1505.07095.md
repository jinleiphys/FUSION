# Skyrme interaction to second order in nuclear matter

> Based on the phenomenological Skyrme interaction various density-dependent nuclear matter quantities are calculated up to second order in many-body perturbation theory. The spin-orbit term as well as two tensor terms contribute at second order to the energy per particle. The simultaneous calculation of the isotropic Fermi-liquid parameters provides a rigorous check through the validity of the Landau relations. It is found that published results for these second order contributions are incorrect in most cases. In particular, interference terms between $s$-wave and $p$-wave components of the interaction can contribute only to (isospin or spin) asymmetry energies. Even with nine adjustable parameters, one does not obtain a good description of the empirical nuclear matter saturation curve in the low density region $0<ρ<2ρ_0$. The reason for this feature is the too strong density-dependence $ρ^{8/3}$ of several second-order contributions. The inclusion of the density-dependent term ${1\over 6}t_3 ρ^{1/6}$ is therefore indispensable for a realistic description of nuclear matter in the Skyrme framework.

## Key claim

Published second-order contributions to nuclear matter quantities from the Skyrme interaction are incorrect in most cases; in particular, interference terms between s-wave and p-wave components can contribute only to (isospin or spin) asymmetry energies, not to the energy per particle of symmetric matter or pure neutron matter, and even with nine adjustable parameters one does not obtain a good description of the empirical nuclear matter saturation curve in the low-density region \(0<\rho<2\rho_0\) due to the too strong density-dependence \(\rho^{8/3}\) of several second-order contributions.

## Method

The author calculates density-dependent nuclear matter quantities up to second order in many-body perturbation theory using a Galilei-invariant momentum-space formulation of the Skyrme interaction, decomposing the in-medium propagator into vacuum propagator and medium-insertion to handle ultraviolet divergences via dimensional regularization. Finite integrals over three Fermi spheres are solved analytically, yielding results consisting of even powers of the Fermi momentum multiplied by products of Skyrme parameters and numerical coefficients of the form \(\ln 2 + r_j\). The isotropic Fermi-liquid parameters are computed simultaneously to verify the Landau relations, and tensor interactions are included to complete the most general two-nucleon contact-interaction quadratic in momenta.

- Energy per particle of pure neutron matter at second order: \(\bar E_n(k_n)^{(\rm 2nd)} = \frac{M k_n^4}{280\pi^4}\big\{ t_0^2(1-x_0)^2(11-2\ln 2) + \frac{2k_n^2}{27}t_0 t_1(1-x_0)(1-x_1)(167-24\ln 2) + \frac{k_n^4}{1188} t_1^2(1-x_1)^2(4943-564\ln 2) + \frac{k_n^4}{396}t_2^2(1+x_2)^2(1033-156\ln 2) + \frac{8k_n^4}{297}W_0^2 (631-102\ln 2) \big\}\)

- Relativistically improved kinetic energy contributions: \(\bar E(k_f)^{(\rm kin)} = \frac{3k_f^2}{10M} - \frac{3k_f^4}{56 M^3}\) with \(M=939\) MeV

## Context

This work corrects and extends second-order calculations with the phenomenological Skyrme force previously pursued by Moghrabi, Grasso et al. (2014-2015), whose published expressions for second-order contributions are found to be incomplete or incorrect, particularly regarding the omission of the spin-orbit term and the inclusion of spurious s-wave and p-wave interference terms in symmetric matter. The paper provides the correct analytical expressions for nuclear matter quantities and demonstrates that the density-dependent term \(\frac{1}{6}t_3 \rho^{1/6}\) is indispensable for a realistic description, enabling future studies to explore whether improvements beyond first-order mean-field approaches are possible.
