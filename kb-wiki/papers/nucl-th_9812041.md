# Study of the Possibility of Supernarrow Dibaryon Production in the \vecγd-> π^{\pm} +D Reaction

> The possibility of observation of supernarrow dibaryons, decay of which into two nucleons forbidden by the Pauli principle, produced in processes of charged pion photoproduction by polarized photons from the deuteron is analyzed. It is shown that the expectable dibaryon yield may exceed the background by a factor of 10--100.

# Study of the Possibility of Supernarrow Dibaryon Production in the \(\vec\gamma d\to\pi^{\pm}+D\) Reaction

## Key claim
The expected yield of supernarrow dibaryons (whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli principle) produced in charged pion photoproduction from the deuteron may exceed the background by a factor of 10–100, provided that the probability \(\eta\) of a 6-quark state existing in the deuteron is taken as 0.01 and the unknown strong-interaction coupling constants are set to 1.

## Method
The authors calculated the differential cross sections for photoproduction of two specific supernarrow dibaryon states, \(D(T=1,J^P=1^+,S=1)\) and \(D(T=1,J^P=1^-,0)\), in the reaction \(\vec\gamma d\to\pi^{+}D\) using a model from Alekseev et al. They assumed the dibaryon decay proceeds mainly through a singlet virtual \(^{31}S_0\) intermediate state. A Monte-Carlo simulation of the dibaryon photoproduction and the dominant background reactions (radiative photoproduction of a \(\pi^{+}\) meson and two-pion photoproduction) was performed for a 20 cm deuteron target at the CLAS detector with photon energies of 500–1000 MeV, for a 5-hour exposure.

## Key numbers
- The total decay widths \(\Gamma_t\) for the \(D(1,1^+,1)\) dibaryon range from 0.2 eV (at mass \(M=1.90\) GeV) to 35 eV (at \(M=2.013\) GeV); for the \(D(1,1^-,0)\) dibaryon they range from 0.05 eV (at \(M=1.90\) GeV) to 8.75 eV (at \(M=2.013\) GeV).
- The probability \(\eta\) of a 6-quark state in the deuteron is taken as \(\eta=0.01\), based on an upper bound of \(\eta\le 0.03\) from the deuteron magnetic moment.
- For photons polarized parallel to the reaction plane (\(\alpha=90^{\circ}\)), the expected yield of \(D(1,1^-,0)\) dibaryons in 5 hours is 3901 events at \(M=1.90\) GeV, 3552 at \(M=1.95\) GeV, and 3165 at \(M=2.00\) GeV.
- For the same polarization, the expected yield of \(D(1,1^+,1)\) dibaryons is 386 events at \(M=1.90\) GeV, 370 at \(M=1.95\) GeV, and 351 at \(M=2.00\) GeV.
- For photons polarized perpendicular to the reaction plane (\(\alpha=0^{\circ}\)), the \(D(1,1^-,0)\) yield drops to 7 events at \(M=1.90\) GeV, 5 at \(M=1.95\) GeV, and 3 at \(M=2.00\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical search for narrow 6-quark states (dibaryons) proposed by Jaffe (QCD predictions) and follows earlier studies of supernarrow dibaryons by Fil'kov, Akhmedov, and collaborators. The authors propose that the use of polarized photon beams at the CLAS detector (CEBAF accelerator) can enhance the dibaryon signal relative to background compared to unpolarized beams, and can help determine dibaryon quantum numbers. Observation of dibaryons in both \(\vec\gamma d\to\pi^{+}D\) and \(\vec\gamma d\to\pi^{-}D\) reactions would allow measurement of their electromagnetic mass splitting and provide more definitive evidence for these states.
