# Extended NJL model for baryonic matter and quark matter

> By considering baryons as clusters of three quarks, we extend the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to describe baryonic matter, quark matter, and their transitions in a unified manner, where the Dirac sea and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking are considered. In particular, a density-dependent structural function $α_S$ is introduced to modulate the four(six)-point interaction strengths to reproduce the baryon masses in vacuum and medium. Vector interactions are considered with the exchange of $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, where the density-dependent coupling constants are fixed by reproducing nuclear matter properties as well as the $Λ$-hyperon potential depth in nuclear medium. As density increases, quarks will emerge as quasi-free particles and coexist with baryons. This phase is interpreted as quarkyonic matter, where quarks are restricted to the lowest energy states in the presence of baryons, i.e., quarks are still confined. Similar to the treatment of $α$ clustering in nuclear medium, a Pauli blocking term is added to baryon masses so that baryons eventually become unbound in the presence of a quark Fermi Sea. Then at large enough densities baryons vanish and a Mott transition takes place, we consider such a transition as deconfinement phase transition. Depending on the strengths of Pauli blocking term and quark-vector meson couplings, both first-order and continues phase transitions are observed for quarkyonic, chiral, and deconfinement phase transitions. The corresponding compact star structures are then investigated and confronted with various astrophysical constraints.

# Extended NJL model for baryonic matter and quark matter

## Key claim

The paper extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to unify baryonic matter, quark matter, and their transitions by treating baryons as three-quark clusters with a density-dependent structural function α_S, but the specific nature (first-order versus continuous) of the quarkyonic, chiral, and deconfinement phase transitions depends critically on the strengths of the Pauli blocking term and the quark-vector meson couplings, which are not uniquely determined by current constraints.

## Method

The authors construct an extended SU(3) NJL model in the mean-field approximation where baryons (protons, neutrons, Λ-hyperons) are treated as clusters of three valence quarks, with a density-dependent structural function α_S modulating the four-point and six-point interaction strengths to reproduce baryon masses in vacuum and medium. Vector interactions from ω and ρ mesons are included with density-dependent coupling constants fixed by nuclear matter saturation properties and the Λ-hyperon potential depth. A Pauli blocking term is added to baryon masses to model the Mott transition where baryons become unbound in the presence of a quark Fermi sea, and the equations of state for compact star matter are obtained under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with binding energy ε(n₀) = -16 MeV and symmetry energy S(n₀) = 31.5 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 234.5 MeV; symmetry energy slope: L = 42.4 MeV
- Vacuum quark masses: M_u = M_d = 367.6 MeV, M_s = 549.5 MeV (from RKH parameter set with Λ = 602.3 MeV)
- Vacuum baryon masses: M_p = M_n = 938.9 MeV, M_Λ = 1113.7 MeV (with α_S = 0.849 at n_b = 0)
- Λ-hyperon potential depth: U_Λ(n₀) = -30 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter
- Λ-hyperon onset densities in stellar matter: n_onset = 0.51 fm⁻³ (B=0) and 0.64 fm⁻³ (B=1 GeV/fm³)
- Deconfinement transition densities: n_b ≈ 7 fm⁻³ (B=0) and n_b ≈ 1.3 fm⁻³ (B=1 GeV/fm³)
- Maximum compact star masses reach approximately 2.0 M_⊙ for parameter sets (0.7, 1), (1, 0), and (1, 1)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous unified approach combining RMF models and equivparticle models (Xia 2018, Xia 2023) by incorporating spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and Dirac sea contributions that were previously neglected. It builds on the quarkyonic matter concept from McLerran and collaborators (McLerran 2007, 2009) and the treatment of baryons as quark clusters in the NJL framework (Bentz 2001). The model enables future investigations of mixed phase structures in hybrid stars, finite nuclei properties, and the color-flavor locked phase at high densities, while currently the meson masses m_σ, m_ω, and m_ρ remain undetermined pending finite-nuclei studies.
