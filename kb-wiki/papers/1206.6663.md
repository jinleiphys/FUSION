# Quarkyonic percolation in dense nuclear matter

> We examine the phase diagram of hadronic matter when the number of colours $N_c$, as well as temperature and density, are varied. We show that in this regime a new percolation phase transition is possible, and examine the implications of this transition for extrapolations to physical QCD of the large-N_c limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a new "confined but percolating" phase of nuclear matter, distinct from both ordinary confinement and deconfinement, may exist in the temperature-density-colour number phase diagram, and that this regime could potentially extend down to the physical case of three colours, but only at densities several times that of ordinary baryonic matter and at low temperature.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical percolation model on a cubic lattice where baryons (each containing N_c quarks) sit on lattice sites, with quarks distributed as hard spheres of radius Λ_QCD^{-1}. They compute the probability p(N_c) that two baryons exchange quantum numbers via a quark-quark propagator function F(y) that scales as λ/N_c, using three different functional forms for F (step-function in coordinate space, step-function in momentum space, and an exponentially screened version). The percolation threshold N_c^* is extracted via Monte Carlo simulations on cells of size b^3 (with b from 3 to 7) using a renormalisation-group-inspired approach that considers nine relative distances between sites. The deconfinement line in the density-N_c plane is then computed at zero temperature analytically and at finite temperature numerically, using ideal gas formulas with baryon mass m = N_c Λ_QCD and including higher-spin baryons.

## Key numbers

- The reference density for baryons exactly touching is ρ̄_0 = Λ_QCD^3 / 8.
- The density parameter ε ranges from 0.8 to 1.4 in lattice units, giving ρ̄ = ρ̄_0 ε^{-3}.
- The number of colours N_c is varied from 2 to 80.
- The deconfinement temperature at μ=0 is T_c ~ (2/3) Λ_QCD.
- The zero-temperature deconfinement baryon chemical potential scales as μ_0 = N_c^{3/2} N_f^{-1/2} Λ_QCD.
- The baryon mass is m = N_c Λ_QCD.
- The spin-degeneracy for baryons at given N_c = 2Q+1 includes states with spin (η+1)/2 for η = 0,1,...,Q, each with degeneracy 2η+2 and an additional energy η Λ_QCD / N_c.

## Context

This work extends the percolation model introduced in Lottini and Torrieri (2011) from fixed density to variable density, and compares the percolation threshold with deconfinement curves derived from the quarkyonic matter concept proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The model uses large-N_c techniques from 't Hooft (1974) and percolation theory from Stauffer (1994). The results suggest that a confined but percolating phase (potentially identifiable with the quarkyonic phase) may be accessible experimentally at densities several times nuclear saturation density, though the parametric assumptions become questionable at N_c=3 where nuclear matter is a glass rather than a crystal and nucleons are not fully classical objects. The authors note that lattice simulations at high N_c would provide an important cross-check.
