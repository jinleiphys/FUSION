# More on the universal equation for Efimov states

> Efimov states are a sequence of shallow three-body bound states that arise when the two-body scattering length is much larger than the range of the interaction. The binding energies of these states are described as a function of the scattering length and one three-body parameter by a transcendental equation involving a universal function of one angular variable. We provide an accurate and convenient parametrization of this function. Moreover, we discuss the effective treatment of range corrections in the universal equation and compare with a strictly perturbative scheme.

## Key claim

The paper provides a new, more accurate, and continuously differentiable parametrization of the universal function Δ(ξ) that appears in Efimov's transcendental equation for three-body binding energies, improving upon previous piecewise parametrizations that had discontinuities at interval boundaries and an unphysical essential singularity at ξ = −π.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation with a momentum cutoff Λ (which plays the role of the three-body parameter) to generate 5029 new data pairs (1/a, E₃) for the second excited Efimov state, converting these to (ξ, Δ(ξ)) pairs via the parametric form of Efimov's equation. They then performed a Lawson minimax fitting procedure using a single-piece expansion around ξ = −π/4 in powers of (−π/4 − ξ)^(k/2), including the known values at the atom-dimer threshold (ξ = −π/4) and three-particle threshold (ξ = −π) as additional data points, and applied an optimized rounding procedure to the coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The new parametrization uses 8 coefficients c₀ through c₇ (with c₀ = 6.027, c₁ = −9.75, c₂ = 5.56, c₃ = −12.75, c₄ = 27.77, c₅ = −29.29, c₆ = 15.06, c₇ = −3.01) for the expansion Δ(ξ) = Σ cₖ (−π/4 − ξ)^(k/2).
* The maximum absolute deviation of the new parametrization from the data set is about one order of magnitude smaller than the old parametrization, which had a maximum deviation of 1.30 × 10⁻².
* The old parametrization had discontinuities of 0.015 at −3π/8 and 0.013 at −5π/8, and first-derivative discontinuities of 0.38 and 0.10 respectively; the new parametrization is continuous and continuously differentiable by construction.
* For the Gaussian potential, the finite-range parameter Γ^(n) for the lowest three states is Γ^(0) = 0.8472, Γ^(1) = 0.06002, Γ^(2) = 0.0035, with the three-body parameter in units of the Gaussian range r₀ given by r₀κ₊^(0) = 0.4874, r₀κ₊^(1) = 0.02124, r₀κ₊^(2) = 0.000915.

## Context

This work extends the parametrization of the universal function Δ(ξ) first extracted from zero-range STM equations in Braaten and Hammer (2004) and later updated in Naidon and Endo (2016), providing a single-piece fit that eliminates the discontinuities and essential singularity of earlier versions. The new parametrization enables more accurate calculation of Efimov binding energies via the transcendental equation without solving the full STM integral equations, and the authors show that the finite-range extension of Efimov's equation proposed by Kievsky and Gattobigio (2012) agrees quantitatively with the running three-body parameter approach of Ji et al. (2015) for effective range corrections.
