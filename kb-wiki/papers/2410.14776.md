# Regulator-independent equations of state for neutron stars generated from first principles

> We study the equation of state (EoS) of a neutron star (NS) accounting for new advances. In the low energy density, $n\leq 0.1 n_s$, with $n_s$ the saturation density, we use a new pure neutron matter EoS that is regulator independent and expressed directly in terms of experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering data. In the highest-density domain our EoS's are matched with pQCD to $\mathcal{O}(α_s^3)$. First principles of causality, thermodynamic consistency and stability are invoked to transit between these two extreme density regimes. The EoS's are further constrained by the new measurements from PREX-II and CREX on the symmetry energy ($S_0$) and its slope ($L$). In addition, we also take into consideration the recent experimental measurements of masses and radii of different NSs and tidal deformabilities. A band of allowed EoS's is then obtained. Interestingly, the resulting values within the band for $S_0$ and $L$ are restricted with remarkably narrower intervals than the input values, with $32.9\leq S_0 \leq 39.5~\text{MeV}$ and $ 37.3 \leq L\leq 69.0~\text{MeV}$ at the 68\% CL. The band of EoS's constructed also allows possible phase transitions (PTs) for NS masses above 2.1~$M_\odot$ at 68\% CL for $n>2.5n_s$. We find both long and short coexistence regions during the PT, corresponding to first and second order PTs, respectively. We also generate the band of EoS's when excluding the astrophysical observables. This is of interest to test General Relativity and modified theories of gravity. Our band of EoS's for NSs can be also used to study other NS properties and dark matter capture in NS.

## Key claim
The authors construct a band of allowed neutron star equations of state that is regulator-independent at low densities, using a pure neutron matter EoS expressed directly in experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering data rather than fitted potentials, and then interpolate to high-density perturbative QCD results under causality, thermodynamic consistency, and stability constraints, with the additional condition that phase transitions only occur for masses above 2.1 solar masses at 68% confidence level.

## Method
The low-density EoS (up to 0.1 times saturation density) is taken from a renormalized calculation of the energy per neutron that resums ladder diagrams using experimental neutron-neutron phase shifts and mixing angles, with no regulator dependence. A first interpolation from this low-density limit up to saturation density is constrained by experimental values of the symmetry energy and its slope from PREX-II and CREX measurements, plus the Unitary Gas Conjecture as a lower bound. A second interpolation from saturation density up to the perturbative QCD matching point at chemical potential 2.6 GeV uses integral constraints from causality and stability, allowing for phase transitions with both long and short coexistence regions.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(32.9 \leq S_0 \leq 39.5\) MeV at 68% confidence level
- Slope of symmetry energy: \(37.3 \leq L \leq 69.0\) MeV at 68% confidence level
- Phase transitions allowed for neutron star masses above \(2.1\,M_\odot\) at 68% confidence level for densities \(n > 2.5 n_s\)
- Low-density EoS valid up to \(n \leq 0.1 n_s\) (approximately \(0.015\) fm\(^{-3}\))
- Saturation density taken as \(n_s = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Perturbative QCD matching at chemical potential \(\mu_{\text{pQCD}} = 2.6\) GeV
- Upper pressure limit at saturation density: \(P_{\text{PNM}}(n_s) = 7.62\) MeV/fm\(^3\) (from \(L = 143\) MeV)
- Lower pressure limit at saturation density from Unitary Gas Conjecture: \(P_{\text{UG}}(n_s) = 1.33 \pm 0.04\) MeV/fm\(^3\)

## Context
This work extends the regulator-independent neutron matter EoS of Alarcon (2022) by connecting it to high-density perturbative QCD results from Kurkela (2009), Fraga (2013), and Gorda (2021) using the integral constraint method of Komoltsev (2021). The resulting EoS band is narrower than previous approaches that rely on chiral effective field theory with explicit cutoff dependence, such as the N\(^3\)LO calculations of Drischler (2017, 2020). The band enables tests of general relativity versus modified gravity theories when astrophysical observables are excluded, and can be used to study other neutron star properties and dark matter capture.
