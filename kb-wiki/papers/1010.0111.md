# Testing the Lambda(1520) hyperon in-medium width in near-threshold proton-nucleus reactions

> In the framework of the nuclear spectral function approach for incoherent primary proton-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon production processes we study the inclusive Lambda(1520) hyperon production in the interaction of 2.83 GeV protons with nuclei. In particular, the A and momentum dependences of the absolute and relative Lambda(1520) hyperon yields are investigated in two scenarios for its in-medium width. Our model calculations show that the pion-nucleon production channel contributes distinctly to the "low-momentum" Lambda(1520) creation both in light and heavy nuclei in the chosen kinematics and, hence, has to be taken into consideration on close examination of the dependences of the Lambda(1520) hyperon yields on the target mass number with the aim of getting information on its width in the medium. They also demonstrate that both the A dependence of the relative Lambda(1520) hyperon production cross section and momentum dependence of the absolute Lambda(1520) hyperon yield at incident energy of interest are appreciably sensitive to the Lambda(1520) in-medium width, which means that these observables may be an important tool to determine the above width.

## Key claim

The A-dependence of the relative Lambda(1520) production cross section and the momentum dependence of the absolute Lambda(1520) yield in 2.83 GeV proton-nucleus reactions are appreciably sensitive to the Lambda(1520) in-medium width, provided that the secondary pion-nucleon production channel is also taken into account, particularly for low-momentum hyperons.

## Method

The author employed the nuclear spectral function approach to calculate inclusive Lambda(1520) production from both primary proton-nucleon processes (pp -> pK+Lambda(1520), pn -> nK+Lambda(1520), pn -> pK0Lambda(1520)) and secondary pion-nucleon processes (pi+n -> K+Lambda(1520), pi0p -> K+Lambda(1520), pi0n -> K0Lambda(1520), pi-p -> K0Lambda(1520)) in proton-nucleus collisions at 2.83 GeV. The elementary cross sections were estimated using a one-pion-exchange model with a parametrization of the pi-p -> K0Lambda(1520) total cross section fitted to existing data, and the final-state absorption of the Lambda(1520) was treated via an eikonal approximation using two scenarios for its in-medium width: the free width of 15.6 MeV and a density-dependent width including a collisional term of 55(rho_N/rho_0) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The free Lambda(1520) width in vacuum is 15.6 MeV.
- The collisional width adopted for the in-medium scenario is 55(rho_N/rho_0) MeV, yielding a total width of approximately 70 MeV at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 (a factor of about 5 larger than the free width).
- The free total cross section for pn -> pK0Lambda(1520) at 0.8 GeV excess energy is (6.09 +/- 1.74) microbarns.
- At the incident proton energy of 2.83 GeV (corresponding to 20 MeV excess energy), the calculated total cross sections for pp -> pK+Lambda(1520) and pn -> nK+Lambda(1520) are approximately 2 nb and 10 nb, respectively.
- The threshold energy for the pi-p -> K0Lambda(1520) reaction is 2.017 GeV.
- The inelastic pN cross section used is 30 mb; the total piN cross section used is 35 mb.

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to phi meson in-medium width studies (Paryev 2009, Paryev 2010) and to omega meson studies in gamma-induced reactions, now applied to the Lambda(1520) hyperon. It builds upon the hadronic model predictions of Kaskulov and Oset (2006, 2007) which found a fivefold increase in the Lambda(1520) width at saturation density, and upon the earlier calculations of Cabrera et al. (2004) which studied the A-dependence of the total Lambda(1520) production cross section ratio using only one-step mechanisms. The present work enables experimental extraction of the Lambda(1520) in-medium width from data already collected at the ANKE-COSY facility or from future measurements at the CSR facility in Lanzhou, by providing predictions for both absolute and relative yields including the previously neglected two-step pion-induced production channel.
