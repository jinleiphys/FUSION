# Three-body spin-orbit forces from chiral two-pion exchange

> Using chiral perturbation theory, we calculate the density-dependent spin-orbit coupling generated by the two-pion exchange three-nucleon interaction involving virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. From the corresponding three-loop Hartree and Fock diagrams we obtain an isoscalar spin-orbit strength $F_{\rm so}(k_f)$ which amounts at nuclear matter saturation density to about half of the empirical value of $90 $MeVfm$^5$. The associated isovector spin-orbit strength $G_{\rm so}(k_f)$ comes out about a factor of 20 smaller. Interestingly, this three-body spin-orbit coupling is not a relativistic effect but independent of the nucleon mass $M$. Furthermore, we calculate the three-body spin-orbit coupling generated by two-pion exchange on the basis of the most general chiral $ππNN$-contact interaction. We find similar (numerical) results for the isoscalar and isovector spin-orbit strengths $F_{\rm so}(k_f)$ and $G_{\rm so}(k_f)$ with a strong dominance of the p-wave part of the $ππNN$-contact interaction and the Hartree contribution.

# Three-body spin-orbit forces from chiral two-pion exchange

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange three-nucleon interaction, mediated by virtual Δ-isobar excitation or by chiral ππNN-contact vertices, generates a density-dependent isoscalar spin-orbit strength that at nuclear matter saturation density amounts to about half of the empirical value of 90 MeV fm⁵, not a relativistic effect but independent of the nucleon mass M.

## Method

The author uses the density-matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin to compute the nuclear energy density functional from three-loop Hartree and Fock diagrams. Two separate mechanisms are evaluated: (1) the Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon interaction with virtual Δ-isobar excitation, and (2) the general chiral ππNN-contact vertex proportional to the second-order low-energy constants c₁, c₃, and c₄. The spin-orbit coupling term is extracted as the coefficient of ∇ρ·J in the energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter saturation density (k_f ≃ 2 m_π), the Δ-Hartree contribution to the isoscalar spin-orbit strength is F_so(2m_π)^(Δ-Hart) = 48.2 MeV fm⁵
- At the same density, the Δ-Fock contribution is F_so(2m_π)^(Δ-Fock) = 1.2 MeV fm⁵
- The isovector spin-orbit strength G_so(k_f) from Δ-excitation is about a factor of 20 smaller than the isoscalar strength, with G_so^(Δ-Fock) = (7/3) F_so^(Δ-Fock)
- Using the chiral ππNN-contact vertex, the c₃-Hartree contribution at saturation density is F_so(2m_π)^(c₃-Hart) = 65.3 MeV fm⁵
- The c₁-Hartree contribution at the same density is F_so(2m_π)^(c₁-Hart) = 3.6 MeV fm⁵
- The c₃-Fock contribution to the isovector strength at saturation density is G_so(2m_π)^(c₃-Fock) = 3.2 MeV fm⁵
- The empirical isoscalar spin-orbit strength from Skyrme phenomenology is 3W₀/4 ≃ 90 MeV fm⁵
- The ΔN-mass splitting used is Δ = 293 MeV
- Physical parameters used: M = 939 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV, g_A = 1.3
- Low-energy constants used: c₁ = -0.64 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -3.90 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 2.25 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the Fujita-Miyazawa three-nucleon potential (Fujita, 1962) by providing analytical expressions for the spin-orbit coupling it generates in nuclear matter, and generalizes the calculation to the chiral ππNN-contact vertex framework of Friar (1999). The results show that two-pion exchange three-body forces contribute about half the empirical spin-orbit strength, consistent with earlier findings by Pandharipande (1994) and Pieper (2001) on spin-orbit splittings in light nuclei. The author notes that iterated one-pion exchange (from a companion paper, Kaiser 2003) generates an isoscalar spin-orbit strength of the wrong negative sign, so the net pion-exchange spin-orbit coupling may be small; this suggests that Lorentz scalar-vector mean-field models governed by QCD sum rules, as applied by Finelli (2003), may be the appropriate framework for the strong nuclear spin-orbit interaction.
