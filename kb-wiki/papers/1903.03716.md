# Dimensional effects in Efimov physics

> Efimov physics is drastically affected by the change of spatial dimensions. Efimov states occur in a tridimensional (3D) environment, but disappear in two (2D) and one (1D) dimensions. In this paper, dedicated to the memory of Prof. Faddeev, we will review some recent theoretical advances related to the effect of dimensionality in the Efimov phenomenon considering three-boson systems interacting by a zero-range potential. We will start with a very ideal case with no physical scales, passing to a system with finite energies in the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation and finishing with a general system. The physical reason for the appearance of the Efimov effect is given essentially by two reasons which can be revealed by the BO approximation - the form of the effective potential is proportional to $1/R^2$ ($R$ is the relative distance between the heavy particles) and its strength is smaller than the critical value given by $-(D-2)^2/4$, where $D$ is the effective dimension.

## Key claim
Efimov states occur in 3D but disappear in 2D and 1D; however, the disappearance is not abrupt at exactly D=2, but there exists a continuous dimensional interval (an "Efimov band") where the effect persists, which depends on the mass ratio of the particles.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical advances using three approaches: (1) solving generalized Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosyan (STM) integral equations in arbitrary dimension D with zero-range interactions and no physical scales, using Danilov's power-law ansatz for the spectator functions; (2) applying the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation for AAB systems with a heavy-light mass ratio to derive the effective 1/R² potential and its critical strength; (3) using a compactification method in momentum space with periodic boundary conditions along one direction to continuously interpolate between 3D, 2D, and 1D for general mass ratios.

## Key numbers
- For D=3 and equal masses (A=1), the discrete scaling factor s = 1.00623, matching Efimov's original result.
- For three equal-mass bosons, the Efimov effect exists in the dimensional interval 2.3 < D < 3.8 (from Nielsen et al.).
- For a ¹³³Cs-¹³³Cs-⁶Li system (mass ratio A = 6/133), the BO approximation gives the dimensional limits 2.21 < D < 3.90, with less than 2% difference from the exact result.
- For D=3, the effective potential strength g(3) = 0.3216, reproducing the known 3D result from Fonseca, Redish and Shanley.
- The critical strength for the fall-to-center phenomenon is given by -(D-2)²/4.

## Context
This review extends the work of Nielsen et al. (showing the Efimov effect exists in a dimensional interval 2.3<D<3.8 for equal masses) and generalizes the BO approximation results of Fonseca, Redish and Shanley (1979) to arbitrary dimensions. It also builds on the compactification method introduced by Yamashita et al. (2013) to study continuous dimensional transitions. The work enables experimental studies of how effective spatial dimensions in atomic traps (pancake or cigar shapes) influence Efimov observables, such as the number of excited states and energy ratios, which can be measured via three-body recombination loss or direct Coulomb explosion techniques as demonstrated by Dörner's group (2015).
