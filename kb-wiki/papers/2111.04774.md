# Coupled-channel analysis of pion- and eta-electroproduction with the Jülich-Bonn-Washington model

> Pion and eta electroproduction data are jointly analyzed for the first time, up to a center-of-mass energy of 1.6 GeV. The framework is a dynamical coupled-channel model, based on the recent Jülich-Bonn-Washington analysis of pion electroproduction data for the same energy range. Comparisons are made to a number of single-channel eta electroproduction fits. By comparing multipoles of comparable fit quality, we find some of these amplitudes are well determined over the near-threshold region, while others will require fits over an extended energy range.

## Key claim

Pion and eta electroproduction data are jointly analyzed for the first time in a coupled-channel framework up to a center-of-mass energy of 1.6 GeV, but some multipole amplitudes are well determined only over the near-threshold region while others will require fits over an extended energy range to achieve comparable determination.

## Method

The authors extended the dynamical coupled-channel Jülich-Bonn-Washington (JBW) approach, which previously described pion electroproduction, to include eta electroproduction data. The framework constructs electroproduction multipoles on the basis of the JüBo hadronic model at the photon point (Q2=0), introducing Q2 dependence through channel-dependent and channel-independent form factors with polynomial expansions, while enforcing Siegert's theorem at the pseudo-threshold to relate longitudinal and electric multipoles. The fit included 1874 eta electroproduction differential cross section data points from three experiments, combined with the existing pion electroproduction data base, allowing 40 new parameters for the eta channel in addition to the 209 previously available parameters.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers a center-of-mass energy range up to 1.6 GeV and Q2 values from 0 to 4 GeV2.
* The eta electroproduction data set consists of 1874 differential cross section data points from experiments by Armstrong (1998), Thompson (2000), and Denizli (2007).
* The dipole form factor uses b2 = 0.71 GeV2, with Qw2 = 0.5 GeV2 and Qr2 = 4.0 GeV2 for the Woods-Saxon suppression.
* The N(1535) resonance has a 50% branching ratio to the eta N channel, with universal agreement on this branching to the 10% level.
* For the etaMAID model, the N(1650), N(1675), N(1710), and N(1720) resonances have eta N branching ratios at the 3-26% level, while N(1520), N(1680), and N(1700) contribute with branching ratios less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the recent JBW analysis of pion electroproduction data (Mai, 2021) to include eta electroproduction, representing the first unified coupled-channel analysis of these two final states. The framework builds on the JüBo approach (Rönchen, 2015) which fits extensive scattering and photoproduction data in the resonance region, and comparisons are made to single-channel fits from the etaMAID model (Chiang, 2001) and Bonn-Gatchina (Anisovich, 2012). The results enable future expansion to include K Lambda final states and provide a foundation for extracting universal resonance parameters in terms of pole positions and residues from electroproduction data.
