# Quarkonia Theory: From Open Quantum System to Classical Transport

> This is a theoretical overview of quarkonium production in relativistic heavy ion collisions given for the Hard Probes 2024 conference in Nagasaki. The talk focuses on the application of the open quantum system framework and the formulation of the chromoelectric correlator that uniquely encodes properties of the quark-gluon plasma relevant for quarkonium dynamics and thus can be extracted from theory-experiment comparison.

## Key claim

The low-transverse-momentum experimental data on quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions can probe a specific physical object called the chromoelectric correlator, which encodes properties of the quark-gluon plasma relevant for quarkonium dynamics; however, extracting this correlator from data requires combining the open quantum system framework with effective field theory, and the Markovian dynamics of quarkonium becomes trivial in the strong coupling limit.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of the open quantum system framework to quarkonium dynamics, where the heavy quark-antiquark pair and the quark-gluon plasma form a closed quantum system, and tracing out the plasma degrees of freedom yields a non-unitary evolution equation. Depending on the hierarchy of energy scales (heavy quark mass, inverse quarkonium size, binding energy, and medium temperature), different effective field theories are used: nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) or potential NRQCD (pNRQCD), leading to either Lindblad equations in the quantum Brownian motion limit or Boltzmann equations in the quantum optical limit. The dissociation and recombination rates in the Boltzmann equations are factorized into a dipole transition amplitude depending on the quarkonium wavefunction and a chromoelectric correlator that encodes QGP properties.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass scale: M ~ 1.5 GeV; inverse charm quarkonium size: 1/r ~ 0.9 GeV; charm binding energy: E_b ~ 0.5 GeV
- Bottom quark mass scale: M ~ 4.5 GeV; inverse bottom quarkonium size: 1/r ~ 1.5 GeV; bottom binding energy: E_b ~ 0.5 GeV
- The dissociation rate Gamma for a quarkonium state b is given by an integral over relative momentum p_rel of the squared dipole transition amplitude times the chromoelectric correlator [g^{++}_{adj}]^> at energy -Delta E, where Delta E = E_b + p_rel^2/M
- The heavy quark diffusion coefficient kappa_fund is defined by a chromoelectric correlator with fundamental Wilson lines, while the quarkonium transport coefficient kappa_adj is defined by a chromoelectric correlator with adjoint Wilson lines at zero frequency

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Matsui and Satz (1986) and Karsch (1987) on quarkonium melting in a hot medium, and builds upon the open quantum system framework first introduced to heavy ion physics by Akamatsu and Rothkopf (2011, 2014). The paper summarizes the community's efforts as documented in the EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force report by Andronic et al. (2024), and discusses how the chromoelectric correlator approach generalizes perturbative QCD diagrams into a gauge-invariant nonperturbative object valid to all orders in perturbation theory at leading power in rT. The work enables future extraction of the chromoelectric correlator from experimental data at both LHC and RHIC, with RHIC data being particularly important for determining the frequency dependence of the correlator at low temperature.
