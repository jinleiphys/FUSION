# Fluctuations and correlations of baryonic chiral partners

> Fluctuations and correlations of the net-baryon number play an important role in exploring critical phenomena in phase transitions of strongly interacting matter governed by Quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In this work, we use the parity doublet model to investigate the fluctuations of the net-baryon number density in hot and dense hadronic matter. The model accounts for chiral criticality within the mean-field approximation. We focus on the qualitative properties and systematics of the first- and second-order susceptibility of the net-baryon number density, and their ratios for nucleons of positive and negative parity, as well as their correlator. We show that the fluctuations of the positive-parity nucleon do not necessarily reflect the fluctuations of the total net-baryon number density at the phase boundary of the chiral phase transition. We also investigate the non-trivial structure of the correlator. Furthermore, we discuss and quantify the differences between the fluctuations of the net-baryon number density in the vicinity of the chiral and liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter. We indicate a possible relevance of our results with the interpretation of the experimental data on net-proton number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The fluctuations of the positive-parity nucleon (proton) do not necessarily reflect the fluctuations of the total net-baryon number density at the phase boundary of the chiral phase transition, meaning that using net-proton number fluctuations as a proxy for net-baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collision experiments is doubtful, particularly near chiral criticality.

## Method

The authors employed the parity doublet model within the mean-field approximation to compute the first- and second-order susceptibilities of the net-baryon number density for positive-parity nucleons (N+) and negative-parity nucleons (N-), as well as their correlator. The model incorporates chiral symmetry restoration through in-medium masses of baryonic chiral partners coupled to chiral fields (σ, π) and an isosinglet vector field (ωμ), with parameters fixed by vacuum nucleon masses and nuclear ground state properties at saturation density. The susceptibilities were evaluated analytically by differentiating the thermodynamic potential with respect to separate chemical potentials for each parity state, then evaluated numerically at various temperatures and baryon chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral crossover at vanishing baryon chemical potential: Tc = 209 MeV
- Liquid-gas critical point: Tlg = 16 MeV, μB = 909 MeV, nB = 0.053 fm⁻³ (0.33 n₀)
- Chiral critical point: Tch = 7 MeV, μB = 1526 MeV, nB = 1.25 fm⁻³ (7.82 n₀)
- Vacuum masses: m₊ = 0.939 GeV (positive-parity nucleon N(938)), m₋ = 1.500 GeV (negative-parity partner N(1535))
- Chirally invariant mass parameter: m₀ = 0.750 GeV
- Nuclear saturation density: nB = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A - m₊ = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 240 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the parity doublet model (Detar 1988, Jido 1999, Jido 2001) to investigate fluctuations of conserved charges near the QCD phase boundary, building on earlier analyses by Marczenko (2023) that showed non-trivial differences in critical behavior between opposite parity states. The results are relevant for interpreting experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan programs at RHIC (STAR Collaboration) and SPS at CERN, where net-proton number fluctuations are used as a proxy for net-baryon number fluctuations. The authors compare their results with the truncated hadron resonance gas (tHRG) model, which uses vacuum hadron masses and shows no critical behavior, highlighting the importance of in-medium effects from chiral dynamics.
