# Forward Inclusive Jet Productions in pA Collisions

> Motivated by recent experimental LHC measurements on the forward inclusive jet productions and based on our previous calculations on forward hadron productions, we calculate single inclusive jet cross-section in $pA$ collisions at forward rapidity within the color glass condensate framework up to the next-to-leading-order. Moreover, with the application of jet algorithm and proper subtraction of the rapidity and collinear divergences, we further demonstrate that the resulting next-to-leading-order hard coefficients are finite. In addition, in order to deal with the large logarithms that can potentially spoil the convergence of the perturbative expansion and improve the reliability of the numerical predictions, we introduce the collinear jet function and the threshold jet function and resum these large logarithms hidden in the hard coefficients.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the single inclusive jet cross-section in proton-nucleus collisions at forward rapidity within the color glass condensate framework up to next-to-leading order, and demonstrate that the resulting hard coefficients are finite after applying a jet algorithm and proper subtraction of rapidity and collinear divergences, but only within the narrow jet approximation (small cone approximation) which neglects contributions of order \(R^2\) with \(R\) the jet cone size.

## Method

The authors compute the NLO corrections to forward inclusive jet production in \(pA\) collisions by evaluating real and virtual one-loop diagrams for four partonic channels (\(q\to qg\), \(g\to gg\), \(q\to gq\), \(g\to q\bar{q}\)) within the color-dipole/CGC factorization formalism. They employ the narrow jet approximation (NJA) to simplify the calculation of final-state radiation contributions, and use dimensional regularization with the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme to handle divergences. Large logarithms are resummed by introducing collinear jet functions (CJFs) satisfying DGLAP evolution equations and by applying threshold (Sudakov) resummation for logarithms near the kinematic boundary where \(x\to 1\).

## Key numbers

- The kinematic constraint for both final-state quark and gluon to lie inside the same jet cone is \(p_\perp^2 \leq \xi^2(1-\xi)^2 q_\perp^2 R^2\), where \(R\) is the jet cone size, \(\xi\) is the longitudinal momentum fraction carried by the final-state quark, and \(q_\perp\) is the parton transverse momentum.
- The kinematic constraint for false identification of the tagged quark when the emitted gluon is also inside the jet cone is \(p_\perp^2 \leq (1-\xi)^2 q_\perp^2 R^2\).
- The splitting kernel for the \(q\to qg\) channel is given by \(\sum_{\lambda\alpha\beta} \psi^{\lambda\ast}_{\alpha\beta}(\xi, u'_\perp)\psi^\lambda_{\alpha\beta}(\xi, u_\perp) = 2(2\pi)^2\frac{1}{p^+} \left[ \frac{1+\xi^2}{1-\xi} - \epsilon (1-\xi) \right] \frac{u'_\perp \cdot u_\perp}{u'_\perp^2 u_\perp^2}\) in \(D=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions.
- The constant \(c_0 = 2e^{-\gamma_E}\) appears in the calculation, where \(\gamma_E \simeq 0.577\) is the Euler constant.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculations for forward hadron productions in \(pA\) collisions (Chirilli 2012) to the case of forward jet productions, motivated by recent LHC measurements from the CMS experiment (CMS 2018). The results are akin to those in Liu (2022) but employ a different resummation approach. The calculation enables quantitative phenomenological tests of gluon saturation at RHIC and the LHC, and is relevant to the physics program of the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
