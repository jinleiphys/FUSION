# Effect of configuration mixing on quadrupole and octupole collective states of transitional nuclei

> A model is presented that simultaneously describes shape coexistence and quadrupole and octupole collective excitations within a theoretical framework based on the nuclear density functional theory and the interacting boson model. An optimal interacting-boson Hamiltonian that incorporates the configuration mixing between normal and intruder states, as well as the octupole degrees of freedom, is identified by means of self-consistent mean-field calculations using a universal energy density functional and a pairing interaction, with constraints on the triaxial quadrupole and the axially-symmetric quadrupole and octupole shape degrees of freedom. An illustrative application to the transitional nuclei $^{72}$Ge, $^{74}$Se, $^{74}$Kr, and $^{76}$Kr shows that the inclusion of the intruder states and the configuration mixing significantly lower the energy levels of the excited $0^+$ states, and that the predicted low-lying positive-parity states are characterized by the strong admixture of nearly spherical, weakly deformed oblate, and strongly deformed prolate shapes. The low-lying negative-parity states are shown to be dominated by the deformed intruder configurations.

# Effect of configuration mixing on quadrupole and octupole collective states of transitional nuclei

## Key claim

The inclusion of configuration mixing between normal and intruder states in an interacting boson model framework that simultaneously incorporates quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom significantly lowers the calculated energies of excited 0+ states in transitional nuclei near mass A ≈ 70, but the quantitative agreement with experimental data depends on how well the mapped boson energy surface reproduces the softness of the mean-field potential in the triaxial and octupole deformation degrees of freedom.

## Method

Self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov method with a density-dependent point-coupling interaction and separable pairing force were performed with constraints on triaxial quadrupole (β2-γ) and axial quadrupole-octupole (β2-β3) shape degrees of freedom. The resulting potential energy surfaces were mapped onto the expectation value of a configuration-mixing sdf-interacting boson model Hamiltonian in a boson coherent state, with separate unperturbed Hamiltonians for the 0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h intruder configurations and a mixing interaction connecting neighboring boson subspaces. The Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in the boson Hilbert space to produce spectroscopic observables for the four nuclei studied.

## Key numbers

- For 72Ge: the 0+3 state is calculated to be purely of 2p-2h oblate nature (99.99% fraction); the 3-1 state is calculated at excitation energy Ex = 2450 keV, compared to the measured Ex = 2515 keV; the 2-1 state is calculated at Ex = 3924 keV versus the experimental Ex = 3036 keV.
- For 74Se: the spherical local minimum in the β2-γ energy surface is only 72 keV above the oblate global minimum, while in the β2-β3 surface the spherical minimum is 29 keV below the oblate minimum.
- For 74Kr: the 4p-4h prolate configuration constitutes 94% of the 4+1 wave function; the 0+2 state is predicted to be mainly made of the strongly deformed prolate configuration (67% 4p-4h fraction).
- For 76Kr: the global minimum in the SCMF β2-β3 energy surface at (β2, β3) ≈ (0.0, 0.05) is only 17 keV lower in energy than the configuration at (β2, β3) ≈ (0.0, 0.0).
- The Inglis-Belyaev moments of inertia used for the rotational term were increased by 30% to account for the typical 30-40% underestimation of empirical values.
- The maximum number of f bosons was truncated to nfmax = 3 for computational feasibility.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-mixing IBM framework previously developed by Nomura et al. (2012) for quadrupole collective states and by Nomura et al. (2014) for octupole degrees of freedom, combining both effects for the first time in a microscopic mapping from density functional theory. The approach contrasts with purely phenomenological IBM calculations that included both intruder and quadrupole-octupole coupled states in Cd isotopes (Garrett 1999, Lehmann 1999). The results enable future systematic studies of shape coexistence and octupole collectivity across transitional regions, particularly near the neutron N=40 subshell gap and proton Z=34 octupole magic number, though the method shows limitations in reproducing γ-softness and the detailed structure of quasi-γ bands.
