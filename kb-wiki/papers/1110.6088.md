# Relativistic Mean Field in $A\approx$80 nuclei and low energy proton reactions

> Relativistic Mean Field calculations have been performed for a number of nuclei in mass $A\approx$80 region. Ground state binding energy, charge radius and charge density values have been compared with experiment. Optical potential have been generated folding the nuclear density with the microscopic nuclear interaction DDM3Y. S-factors for low energy ($p,γ$) and ($p,n$) reactions have been calculated and compared with experiment.

## Key claim

Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations using the FSU Gold Lagrangian density, combined with a microscopic optical potential from folding the DDM3Y interaction with RMF densities, can reproduce astrophysical S-factors for low-energy (p,γ) and (p,n) reactions in the A≈80 region with reasonable success, though the worst-case calculations are off by a factor less than two while cross sections range over four orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed RMF+BCS calculations in coordinate space using the FSU Gold Lagrangian density with a zero-range pairing force of strength 300 MeV-fm, extracting theoretical density profiles for target nuclei. They constructed microscopic optical potentials by folding the density-dependent DDM3Y effective interaction with these RMF densities, taking the real part as 0.7 times and the imaginary part as 0.1 times the DDM3Y potential. Reaction cross sections and S-factors were then calculated using the TALYS 1.2 code with full Hauser-Feshbach formalism, microscopic level densities from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations, and HFB-based E1 gamma strength functions, without scaling any theoretical results.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy and charge radii from RMF calculations were compared with experimental values for target nuclei in the A≈80 region; the difference between measured and calculated charge radii was less than 1% in most nuclei.
- For the reaction ⁸⁴Sr(p,γ), choosing the imaginary part of the potential as 0.3 times the DDM3Y potential (instead of the standard 0.1) changed the result by 10% and improved agreement with experimental data.
- In the ⁷⁶Ge(p,n) reaction, the calculation systematically overpredicted experimental results by as much as 60%.
- For the ⁸⁵Rb(p,n) reaction, the calculation produced an excellent match with experimental measurements.
- In the worst case among all reactions studied, the calculation was off by a factor less than two, while the cross section values ranged over four orders of magnitude.
- The astrophysical Gamow window for these nuclei lies in the energy region 1.3 to 3.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic approach for (p,γ) reactions in lighter nuclei (Lahiri and Gangopadhyay, 2010) to the A≈80 mass region, and compares results with the NON-SMOKER code calculations of Rauscher et al. The study addresses the need for reliable theoretical reaction rates for p-nuclei nucleosynthesis, particularly for reactions identified as important by Rapp et al. (2006), such as ⁷⁵As(p,n), ⁷⁶Ge(p,n), and ⁸⁵Rb(p,n). The method enables calculation of reaction rates for proton-rich nuclei that cannot be produced via s- or r-processes and are beyond current experimental capabilities, with the authors noting that in astrophysical calculations rates are often varied by a factor of ten or hundred.
