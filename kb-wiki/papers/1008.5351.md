# Core - Corona Model describes the Centrality Dependence of v_2/epsilon

> Event by event EPOS calculations in which the expansion of the system is described by {\it ideal} hydrodynamics reproduce well the measured centrality dependence of $v_2/ε_{part}$, although it has been claimed that only viscous hydrodynamics can reproduce these data. This is due to the core - corona effect which manifests itself in the initial condition of the hydrodynamical expansion. The centrality dependence of $v_2/ε_{part}$ can be understood in the recently advanced core-corona model, a simple parameter free EPOS inspired model to describe the centrality dependence of different observables from SPS to RHIC energies. This model has already been successfully applied to understand the centrality dependence of multiplicities and of the average transverse momentum of identified particles.

## Key claim
The centrality dependence of \(v_2/\epsilon_{part}\) can be reproduced by ideal hydrodynamics when the initial condition includes a core-corona separation, contradicting the claim that only viscous hydrodynamics can describe these data. The core-corona model explains this dependence as arising from the centrality-dependent fraction of nucleons that form a thermally equilibrated core, rather than from incomplete thermalization of all particles.

## Method
Event-by-event EPOS calculations were performed for AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, where string segments in high-density regions (core) are evolved with ideal hydrodynamics while low-density regions (corona) are treated as independent nucleon-nucleon collisions. A simpler parameter-free core-corona model was also applied, defining core particles as those with more than one initial collision in a Glauber model, and using the fraction \(f_{core}\) to compute \(v_2/\epsilon_{part} = f_{core} \cdot v_2^{hydro}/\epsilon\).

## Key numbers
- For AuAu at 200 AGeV, the EPOS calculation with ideal hydrodynamics reproduces the measured centrality dependence of \(v_2/\epsilon_{part}\) with a freeze-out temperature of 166 MeV.
- The 200 AGeV Phobos and Phenix data, when divided by \(f_{core}(N_{part})\), follow a horizontal straight line as predicted by the core-corona model.
- At 62 AGeV, the Phenix data show a 30% difference between CuCu and AuAu in \(v_2^{hydro}/\epsilon_{part}\), which is not understood.
- The NA49 data at \(\sqrt{s}=17.2\) GeV are also compatible with a straight line when \(v_2/\epsilon_{part}\) is divided by \(f_{core}\).

## Context
This work extends the core-corona model previously applied to multiplicities and average transverse momentum (Aichelin 2008, 2010) to elliptic flow, and directly contradicts the analysis of Drescher (2007) which claimed that ideal hydrodynamics fails by at least 25% and that viscous hydrodynamics is required. The results question the use of \(v_2\) centrality dependence to extract viscosity, as done by Luzum (2008) and Song (2008), because the core-corona effect provides an alternative explanation without viscosity. The model enables predictions for lighter systems like CuCu and for SPS energies.
