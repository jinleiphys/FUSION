# Pion form factor in the range -10 GeV^2 < s < 1 GeV^2

> Based on the field-theory-inspired approach, a new expression for the pion form factor F_pi is proposed. It takes into account the pseudoscalar meson loops $π^+π^-$ and $K\bar K$ and the mixing of $ρ(770)$ with heavier $ρ(1450)$ and $ρ(1700)$ resonances. The expression possesses correct analytical properties and describes the data in the wide range of the energy squared $-10{GeV}^2\leq s\leq1$ GeV$^2$ without introducing the phenomenological Blatt -- Weisskopf range parameter $R_π$.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new expression for the pion form factor that correctly describes data from -10 GeV² to 1 GeV² without requiring the phenomenological Blatt-Weisskopf range parameter R_π, but the description is restricted to energies below 1 GeV where only pseudoscalar meson loops (π⁺π⁻ and K anti-K) are considered.

## Method
The authors construct a field-theory-inspired expression for the pion form factor F_π(s) that incorporates the mixing of ρ(770) with ρ(1450) and ρ(1700) resonances, along with π⁺π⁻ and K anti-K loop contributions. The expression is fitted to bare cross section data from e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ measurements by SND, CMD-2, KLOE, and BaBaR detectors in the time-like region up to √s = 1 GeV, then analytically continued to the space-like region for comparison with scattering data.

## Key numbers
- ρ(770) mass from BaBaR fit: 773.92 ± 0.10 MeV
- ρ(770) coupling constant g_{ρ₁ππ} from BaBaR fit: 5.785 ± 0.004
- ρ(770) coupling constant g_{ρ₁} from BaBaR fit: 5.167 ± 0.002
- Pion charge radius r_π from BaBaR fit: 0.668 ± 0.053 fm (compared to PDG average 0.672 ± 0.008 fm)
- χ²/N_d.o.f. for BaBaR fit: 216/260
- ρ(770)-ω(782) mixing parameter Π′_{ρ₁ω} from BaBaR fit: (4.00 ± 0.06) × 10⁻³ GeV²
- ρ(1450) coupling g_{ρ₂ππ} from BaBaR fit: 0.21 ± 0.04
- ρ(1700) coupling g_{ρ₃ππ} from BaBaR fit: 0.011 (+0.479, -0.007)

## Context
This work extends earlier vector dominance model approaches by Gounaris and Sakurai (1968) and the authors' own previous expression (Achasov 1997) which had only 20% normalization accuracy. The new expression provides correct analytical properties across both time-like and space-like regions, enabling continuation to -10 GeV² ≤ s ≤ 0 for comparison with scattering data from Amendolia (1986), Bebek (1978), Horn (1976), and Tadevosyan (2001). The approach avoids the problematic Blatt-Weisskopf centrifugal barrier factor used in standard PDG parametrizations, and future work will include vector-pseudoscalar and axial-vector-pseudoscalar loops for higher energies.
