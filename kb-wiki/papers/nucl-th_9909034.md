# Implications of Weak-Interaction Space Deformation for Neutrino Mass Measurements

> The negative values for the squares of both electron and muon neutrino masses obtained in recent experiments are explained as a possible consequence of a change in metric within the weak-interaction volume in the energy-momentum representation. Using a model inspired by a combination of the general theory of relativity and the theory of deformation for continuous media, it is shown that the negative value of the square of the neutrino mass can be obtained without violating allowed physical limits. The consequence is that the negative value is not necessary unphysical.

## Key claim

The negative values measured for the squares of both electron and muon neutrino masses may not be unphysical, but could instead result from a change in the metric (space deformation) within the weak-interaction volume in the energy-momentum representation, rather than from systematic errors or unphysical dynamics.

## Method

The author models the weak-interaction volume as a deformed four-dimensional Minkowski space, using a formalism inspired by the general theory of relativity combined with the theory of deformation for continuous media. A simple deformation tensor is chosen, analogous to hydrostatic pressure, with a single free parameter epsilon that affects only the spatial (momentum) components while leaving the time (energy) component unchanged. The transformation coefficients are constrained by requiring that the Heisenberg relations hold and that the number of possible states is conserved under deformation.

## Key numbers

* The probability that the square of the electron neutrino mass m_nu_e^2 is positive is only 3% (from Particle Data Group 1994 compilation).
* The square of the muon neutrino mass m_nu_mu^2 is negative by 6.1 or 0.9 standard deviations, depending on the choice of the pion rest mass value (from Particle Data Group 1994 and Assamagan 1994).
* In the limit epsilon approaches 1, the deformation tensor approaches the Minkowski metric tensor and the undeformed space is recovered.

## Context

This work extends the idea from general relativity that the metric of space deforms at distance scales comparable to the range of the weak interaction, motivated by the very short range of the weak force due to the large masses of the W and Z bosons. The paper proposes an alternative to the interpretation that negative squared neutrino masses arise from systematic errors in beta-decay and pion decay experiments (Particle Data Group 1994; Assamagan 1994). The model enables future theoretical work on how space deformation in the interaction volume could affect kinematic reconstructions in weak-interaction processes.
