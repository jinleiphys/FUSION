# Evidence for Three-$α$ Breathing Modes Uncovered by Control Neural Network

> This work introduces a new Control Neural Network (Ctrl.NN) method to uncover evidence of exotic quantum state, \textit{i.e.}, the breathing modes in 3-$α$ resonant states of $^{12}$C nucleus. We provide the most precise microscopic description to date for the $^{12}$C energy spectrum, identify two new exotic breathing states, and uncover strong evidence that directly connects the recent experimental observations to the breathing modes. The Ctrl.NN method significantly simplifies numerical calculations of quantum systems under multiple constraints and offers a new perspective for solving the nuclear many-body problem.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $0_{3}^{+}$, $2_{3}^{+}$, and $4_{3}^{+}$ states of $^{12}$C are breathing modes of the Hoyle band states ($0_{2}^{+}$, $2_{2}^{+}$, $4_{2}^{+}$), supported by the strong agreement between the full theoretical spectrum and experimental data; the claim is hedged by noting that the $4_{3}^{+}$ state is considered a "semi-breathing mode" because the radius difference between it and the $4_{2}^{+}$ state is not significant.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic calculations of $^{12}$C using a Control Neural Network (Ctrl.NN) to evolve 28 sets of basis wave functions under multiple constraints on energy, spatial extension (root-mean-square radius), and rotational symmetry. A total of 840 bases (30 per set) were generated, angular-momentum projected, and diagonalized to obtain the energy spectrum and eigenstates. The Ctrl.NN method was used to impose these constraints simultaneously, guiding the evolution of the 3-$\alpha$ cluster wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of $^{12}$C: $-89.66$ MeV; Hoyle state ($0_{2}^{+}$) energy: $-81.98$ MeV.
- Excitation energies of $0_{3}^{+}$ and $0_{4}^{+}$ states: $9.37$ MeV and $11.08$ MeV, compared to experimental values of $9.04$ MeV and $10.56$ MeV.
- Ratio $B(E2; 4_{2}^{+}\rightarrow2_{2}^{+})/B(E2; 2_{2}^{+}\rightarrow0_{2}^{+})$ = $6.53$, exceeding the rigid rotor value of $10/7$.
- $B(E2; 4_{2}^{+} \rightarrow 2_{3}^{+})$ = $1201.9$ e$^2$fm$^4$.
- $B(E2; 2_{3}^{+} \rightarrow 0_{3}^{+})$ = $809.2$ e$^2$fm$^4$.
- Electric monopole transition strength $M(E0;0_{3}^{+}\rightarrow0_{2}^{+})$ = $42.89$ efm$^2$.
- Electric monopole transition strength $M(E0;2_{3}^{+}\rightarrow 2_{2}^{+})$ = $57.84$ efm$^2$.
- Electric monopole transition strength $M(E0;4_{3}^{+}\rightarrow 4_{2}^{+})$ = $56.45$ efm$^2$.

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic cluster model calculations of $^{12}$C, such as the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke (THSR) approach (Funaki 2015) and the Resonating Group Method (REM) (Zhou 2023), by introducing a multi-objective optimization via a neural network to achieve a more precise reproduction of the experimental spectrum. The identification of the $2_{3}^{+}$ and $4_{3}^{+}$ states as new breathing modes provides a direct theoretical connection to recent experimental observations of excess monopole strength (Li 2022) and supports the interpretation of the $0_{3}^{+}$ band as a "breathing band." This enables future studies of the 3-$\alpha$ reaction rate at stellar temperatures and the structure of $\alpha$-cluster resonances.
