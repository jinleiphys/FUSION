# Finite Size Effects on the Chiral Phase Transition of Quantum Chromodynamics

> We study the effect of periodic boundary conditions on chiral symmetry breaking and its restoration in Quantum Chromodynamics. As an effective model of the effective potential for the quark condensate, we use the quark-meson model, while the theory is quantized in a cubic box of size $L$. After specifying a renormalization prescription for the vacuum quark loop, we study the condensate at finite temperature, $T$, and quark chemical potential, $μ$. We find that lowering $L$ leads to a catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking. The excitation of the zero mode leads to a jump in the condensate at low temperature and high density, that we suggest to interpret as a gas-liquid phase transition that takes place between the chiral symmetry broken phase (hadron gas) and chiral symmetry restored phase (quark matter). We characterize this intermediate phase in terms of the increase of the baryon density, and of the correlation length of the fluctuations of the order parameter: for small enough $L$ the correlation domains occupy a substantial portion of the volume of the system, and the fluctuations are comparable to those in the critical region. For these reasons, we dub this phase as the {\it subcritical liquid}. The qualitative picture that we draw is in agreement with previous studies based on similar effective models. We also clarify the discrepancy on the behavior of the critical temperature versus $L$ found in different models.

## Key claim

Lowering the system size $L$ with periodic boundary conditions catalyzes chiral symmetry breaking, and for $L\lesssim 5$ fm an intermediate "subcritical liquid" phase appears between the hadron gas and quark matter at low temperature and high chemical potential, characterized by a jump in the condensate driven by the zero mode rather than by chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor quark-meson (QM) model quantized in a cubic box of size $L$ with periodic boundary conditions, computing the renormalized thermodynamic potential at one-loop level. They regularize the vacuum quark loop using Pauli-Villars regulators with coefficients $c_0=1$, $c_1=-3$, $c_2=3$, $c_3=-1$, then impose renormalization conditions that the quark loop does not shift the vacuum condensate or sigma meson mass in the infinite volume limit. The condensate is determined by global minimization of the total potential for each temperature $T$ and quark chemical potential $\mu$.

## Key numbers

- At $L=3$ fm and low temperature, the condensate shows a first discontinuity at $\mu_1\approx 340$ MeV dropping to $\sigma_1\approx 93$ MeV, and a second discontinuity at $\mu_2\approx 526$ MeV dropping to $\sigma_2\approx 5$ MeV (chiral restoration).
- At $L=3$ fm and low temperature, baryon density jumps from zero to $n_B^{(1)}\approx 0.95\rho_0$ at $\mu_1$ and to $n_B^{(2)}\approx 6.46\rho_0$ at $\mu_2$, where $\rho_0=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is nuclear saturation density.
- In the subcritical liquid phase at $T=0$, baryon density is $n_B^\mathrm{s.l.}=2N_c N_f/(3L^3)$, giving $n_B\approx \rho_0$ for $L\approx 3$ fm.
- Finite size effects on the condensate are noticeable up to $L\approx 5$ fm; for $L=6$ fm and $L=8$ fm the continuum limit is effectively reached.
- At $L=2$ fm and $T=40$ MeV, the correlation length $\lambda$ satisfies $\lambda/L=O(1)$ near the transitions, indicating proximity to criticality.
- The pseudo-critical temperature $T_c$ at $\mu=0$ increases with $1/L$, consistent with catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of finite-size effects on the QCD phase diagram using effective models, particularly the NJL model calculations of Xu (2019) which also employed Pauli-Villars regularization and found similar qualitative behavior. The authors clarify a discrepancy in the literature regarding the behavior of the critical temperature versus $L$ by showing that $T_c$ increases with $1/L$ when periodic boundary conditions are used, in agreement with Palhares (2009), Wang (2018), and Magdy (2019). The prediction of a subcritical liquid phase with frozen correlation volumes and nonzero baryon density but unchanged chiral symmetry breaking pattern provides a testable scenario for lattice QCD simulations with $N_c=2$, where finite chemical potential is accessible.
