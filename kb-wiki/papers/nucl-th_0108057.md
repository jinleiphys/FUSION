# Accurate Nucleon-Nucleon Potential Based upon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We present an accurate nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potential based upon chiral effective Lagrangians. The model includes one- and two-pion exchange contributions up to chiral order three. We show that a quantitative fit of the $NN$ $D$-wave phase shifts requires contact terms (which represent the short range force) of order four. Within this framework, the $NN$ phase shifts below 300 MeV lab. energy and the properties of the deuteron are reproduced with high-precision. This chiral $NN$ potential represents a reliable starting point for testing the chiral effective field theory approach in exact few-nucleon and microscopic nuclear many-body calculations. An important implication of the present work is that the chiral $2π$ exchange at order four is of crucial interest for future chiral $NN$ potential development.

# Accurate Nucleon-Nucleon Potential Based upon Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The authors present a nucleon-nucleon potential based on chiral effective field theory that reproduces NN phase shifts below 300 MeV lab energy and deuteron properties with high precision, but only when contact terms of chiral order four are included to control the overly attractive two-pion exchange in D-waves. The abstract's claim of "high-precision" is conditioned on the inclusion of these order-four contact terms, which the authors identify as a crucial finding.

## Method
The potential is derived from chiral effective Lagrangians using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one- and two-pion exchange contributions up to chiral order three. The short-range force is parametrized by 24 contact terms up to order Q^4, which are fitted to NN scattering data. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is reduced to a Lippmann-Schwinger equation using the Blankenbecler-Sugar three-dimensional propagator, and the potential is regularized with exponential cutoffs (Lambda approximately 0.4-0.5 GeV) to control high-momentum contributions.

## Key numbers
- NN phase shifts reproduced below 300 MeV lab energy
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.224575 MeV (Idaho-A and Idaho-B potentials)
- np 1S0 scattering length: -23.75 fm (Idaho-A and Idaho-B)
- np 3S1 scattering length: 5.417 fm (Idaho-A and Idaho-B)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S state ratio: 0.0256 (Idaho-A) and 0.0255 (Idaho-B)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.281 fm^2 (Idaho-A) and 0.284 fm^2 (Idaho-B), including meson-exchange current and relativistic corrections of 0.010 fm^2
- Deuteron D-state probability: 4.17% (Idaho-A) and 4.94% (Idaho-B)
- Triton binding energy: 8.14 MeV (Idaho-A) and 8.02 MeV (Idaho-B), compared to empirical 8.48 MeV
- Pion-nucleon low-energy constants used: c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.70 GeV^-1, c4 = 3.40 GeV^-1
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi approximately 1 GeV
- Nucleon mass: 938.919 MeV; pion mass: 138.04 MeV; pion decay constant: 92.4 MeV; axial coupling g_A = 1.29 (equivalent to g_piNN^2/4pi = 13.67)

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez (1992), van Kolck (1994, 1999), and others including Kaiser et al. (1997) and Epelbaum et al. (1998). The authors aim to produce a chiral NN potential with accuracy comparable to high-precision phenomenological potentials from the 1990s such as CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2001), AV18 (Wiringa et al., 1995), and Nijmegen (Stoks et al., 1994). The resulting potential enables reliable tests of chiral effective field theory in exact few-nucleon calculations and microscopic nuclear many-body theory, and the authors identify the inclusion of chiral two-pion exchange at order four (Kaiser, 2001) as a crucial next step for future development.
