# Isospin influences on particle emission and critical phenomenon in nuclear dissociation

> Features of particle emission and critical point behavior are investigated as functions of the isospin of disassembling sources and temperature at a moderate freeze-out density for medium-size Xe isotopes in the framework of isospin dependent lattice gas model. Multiplicities of emitted light particles, isotopic and isobaric ratios of light particles show the strong dependence on the isospin of the dissociation source, but double ratios of light isotope pairs and the critical temperature determined by the extreme values of some critical observables are insensitive to the isospin of the systems. Values of the power law parameter of cluster mass distribution, mean multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments ($IMF$), information entropy ($H$) and Campi's second moment ($S_2$) also show a minor dependence on the isospin of Xe isotopes at the critical point. In addition, the slopes of the average multiplicites of the neutrons ($N_n$), protons ($N_p$), charged particles ($N_{CP}$), and IMFs ($N_{imf}$), slopes of the largest fragment mass number ($A_{max}$), and the excitation energy per nucleon of the disassembling source ($E^*/A$) to temperature are investigated as well as variances of the distributions of $N_n$, $N_p$, $N_{CP}$, $N_{IMF}$, $A_{max}$ and $E^*/A$. It is found that they can be taken as additional judgements to the critical phenomena.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, determined by extreme values of the power-law parameter, Campi's second moment, and information entropy, is insensitive to the isospin of disassembling Xe isotopes within the experimentally accessible range of isospin asymmetry (I = 0.11 to 0.26), although the paper's abstract states this as a finding rather than a conditional result.

## Method

The authors employed an isospin-dependent lattice gas model with Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling on a 7×7×7 cubic lattice to simulate the disassembly of four Xe isotopes (¹²²Xe, ¹²⁹Xe, ¹³⁷Xe, ¹⁴⁶Xe) at a fixed freeze-out density near 0.39ρ₀. The interaction potential distinguished between like-nucleon pairs (εₙₙ = εₚₚ = 0 MeV) and unlike-nucleon pairs (εₚₙ = -5.33 MeV), incorporating Pauli exclusion effects. Clusters were defined using a temperature-dependent bonding probability analogous to the Coniglio-Klein prescription, and 1000 events were accumulated at each temperature from 3 to 7 MeV in 0.5 MeV steps for each isotope.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density: approximately 0.39ρ₀, with actual values of 0.36, 0.38, 0.40, and 0.43 ρ₀ for ¹²²Xe, ¹²⁹Xe, ¹³⁷Xe, and ¹⁴⁶Xe respectively (343 lattice sites)
- Isospin parameters (I = (N-Z)/A): 0.11 for ¹²²Xe, 0.16 for ¹²⁹Xe, 0.21 for ¹³⁷Xe, and 0.26 for ¹⁴⁶Xe
- Critical temperature determined by slopes of average quantities: approximately 5 MeV for all four isotopes
- Critical temperature from power-law parameter τ, Campi's second moment S₂, and information entropy H: approximately 5.5 MeV for all isotopes
- Interaction constants: εₙₙ = εₚₚ = 0 MeV, εₚₙ = -5.33 MeV
- Temperature range investigated: 3 to 7 MeV with 0.5 MeV steps

## Context

This work extends the lattice gas model approach of Pan and Das Gupta (1995-1996) to systematically study isospin effects on nuclear disassembly and critical phenomena, complementing earlier studies of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter by Li, Ko, and Bauer (1998). The finding that critical temperature is insensitive to isospin for medium-mass Xe isotopes suggests that experimental searches for isospin dependence of the liquid-gas phase transition critical temperature will be difficult for medium-size nuclei, while the introduction of information entropy as a critical observable provides a new tool for future experimental and theoretical investigations.
