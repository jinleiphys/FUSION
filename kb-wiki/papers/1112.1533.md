# Theory of time-like baryon form factors near thresholds

> A new mechanism of baryon-antibaryon production via nonperturbative double pair creation in intermediate mesons is proposed and the theory contains no fitting parameters. It is shown, that near-threshold resonances are responsible for enhancements in electroproduction cross sections: $ψ(4S)$ for $Λ^+_c Λ^-_c, Υ(6S)$ for $Λ_b \bar Λ_b$. An admixture of intermediate $D$- wave resonances produces angular dependence in differential cross section and can explain unusual behavior of the ratio $G_E/G_M$ for the proton.

# Theory of time-like baryon form factors near thresholds

## Key claim
The paper proposes that baryon-antibaryon production near threshold is driven by a nonperturbative double string-breaking mechanism in intermediate vector mesons, with no fitting parameters; however, the agreement with experimental data for the proton ratio \(G_E/G_M\) requires artificially increasing the S-D interference term by a factor of approximately 3 to account for possible mixing effects on \(\psi_D(0)\).

## Method
The author calculates electroproduction cross sections for baryon-antibaryon pairs using a double pair creation vertex where the initial \((Q\bar Q)\) meson in a \(^3S_1\) state undergoes string breaking to produce two light quark pairs. The cross section is expressed as a sum over intermediate vector meson resonances, with wave functions approximated by simple harmonic oscillator functions for the meson and a Gaussian hyperspherical mode for the baryon, and the only fundamental parameters are the string tension \(\sigma = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\) and the vacuum correlation length \(\lambda \approx 0.1-0.2\) fm. For angular dependence, the formalism is extended to include \(D\)-wave admixture in the vector meson wave function, producing interference terms that modify the differential cross section and the ratio of electric to magnetic Sachs form factors.

## Key numbers
- String tension: \(\sigma = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\)
- Vacuum correlation length: \(\lambda \approx 0.1-0.2\) fm (best fit for \(\Lambda_c\bar\Lambda_c\) uses \(\lambda = 0.17\) fm)
- Charm quark mass: \(m_c = 1.42\) GeV; bottom quark mass: \(m_b = 4.83\) GeV; strange quark mass: \(m_s \approx 0.17\) GeV
- \(\psi(4S)\) energy: \(E_n = 4.426\) GeV (theory), 4.421 GeV (experiment); width \(\Gamma_n = 62 \pm 20\) MeV
- \(\Upsilon(6S)\) energy: \(E_n = 11.04\) GeV (theory), 11.023 GeV (experiment); width \(\Gamma_n = 79 \pm 16\) MeV
- Light \(3S\) resonance energy: \(E_n = 1.9\) GeV; width \(\Gamma_n = 130-160\) MeV
- Light \(2D\) resonance energy: \(E_n = 1.99\) GeV; width \(\Gamma_n \approx 150\) MeV
- Proton effective form factor at threshold: \(|F_p(0)| = 0.4\) (with \(\lambda = 0.18\) fm); at \(E = 1.95\) GeV, \(|F_p| = 0.384\)
- Baryon mean square radius used: \(\langle r^2_{\Lambda_c}\rangle = (0.8\ \text{fm})^2\)

## Context
This work extends the author's prior formalism for string breaking with single pair creation (Simonov, 2006) to the double pair creation case needed for baryon-antibaryon production, and applies it to the specific calculations reported in Simonov (2011). The mechanism provides an alternative to perturbative approaches using distribution amplitudes (Chernyak and Zhitnitsky, 1984) and to molecular or four-quark explanations (recent work cited as reference 8). The theory predicts threshold enhancements for heavy baryons \(\Lambda_c\bar\Lambda_c\) and \(\Lambda_b\bar\Lambda_b\) via the \(\psi(4S)\) and \(\Upsilon(6S)\) resonances respectively, and explains the angular dependence and \(G_E/G_M\) ratio behavior for the proton and \(\Lambda\) through S-D wave interference in light vector mesons. Future work requires explicit derivation of the energy dependence of the double-pair creation vertex to describe the transition to perturbative behavior at higher energies.
