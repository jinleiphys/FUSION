# Azimuthal anisotropy in particle distribution in A Multi-phase Transport Model

> Anisotropic flow of hadronic matter is considered as a sensitive tool to detect the early stage dynamics of high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Taking the event by event fluctuations of the collision geometry into account, the elliptic flow parameter and the triangular flow parameter derived from the azimuthal distribution of produced hadrons, are investigated within the framework of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, at a collision energy that in near future will typically be available at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research. The dependence of elliptic and triangular flow parameters on initial fluctuations, on parton scattering cross-sections, their mass ordering on different hadron species and on the constituent quark number scaling are examined. The AMPT simulation can not exactly match the elliptic flow results on Pb + Pb collision at 40A GeV of the NA49 experiment. The simulation results presented in this work are expected to provide us with an insight to study flow properties at high baryonic density but moderate temperature, and also with an opportunity to compare similar results available from RHIC and LHC experiments.

## Key claim

The AMPT (string melting) simulation cannot exactly match the elliptic flow results from the NA49 experiment on Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV, even when using a wide range of partonic scattering cross-sections; the disagreement is most prominent at low transverse momentum (below 1.0 GeV/c) for pions and protons in mid-central and peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to generate 10^6 minimum bias fixed-target Au+Au collisions at E_lab = 30A GeV, with event-by-event fluctuations in the positions of participating nucleons taken into account. They calculated the elliptic flow parameter v_2 and triangular flow parameter v_3 from the Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal distribution of charged hadrons, and examined their dependence on parton scattering cross-sections (sigma = 3, 6, and 10 mb), centrality, transverse momentum, and hadron species. The simulation results were compared with NA49 experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum <p_T> for charged hadrons lies within a narrow range between 0.33 and 0.38 GeV/c, with a mean around 0.35 GeV/c, for Au+Au collisions at E_lab = 30A GeV.
- The fit parameter beta from the exponential fit dN_ch/dp_T ~ exp(-beta p_T) in the thermal region (1.5 to 2.5 GeV/c) is 2.344 +/- 0.007 for pions, 2.339 +/- 0.014 for kaons, 2.113 +/- 0.003 for protons, and 2.063 +/- 0.007 for lambda particles.
- The statistical percentage errors on simulated v_2 values for pions in the p_T < 1.0 GeV/c range are less than 4% in peripheral collisions and less than 3% in mid-central collisions; for protons the errors are less than 9% in peripheral and less than 5% in mid-central collisions.
- The v_2/epsilon_2 ratio reaches a maximum at N_part approximately 250 for Au+Au collisions at E_lab = 30A GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT simulations at FAIR energies reported by Sarkar et al. (2018) by incorporating event-by-event initial fluctuations and partonic degrees of freedom, which were not addressed in that earlier analysis. The study is motivated by the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, which will probe high baryon density and moderate temperature conditions complementary to RHIC and LHC experiments. The results are compared with NA49 experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV (NA49 Collaboration, 2002), and the observed discrepancies suggest that either the AMPT model requires fine-tuning or higher-statistics experiments like CBM are needed to resolve the differences.
