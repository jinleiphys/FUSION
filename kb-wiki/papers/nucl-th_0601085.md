# Correlation of transverse momentum and multiplicity in a superposition model of nucleus-nucleus collisions

> In p-p collisions the average transverse momentum is known to be correlated with the multiplicity of produced particles. The correlation is shown to survive in a superposition model of nucleus-nucleus collisions. When properly parameterized, the correlation strength appears to be independent of the collision centrality - it is the same in p-p and central A-A collisions. However, the correlation is strongly suppressed by the centrality fluctuations.

## Key claim

The correlation between average transverse momentum and multiplicity, which is observed in p-p collisions, survives in a superposition model of nucleus-nucleus collisions where particles come from independent sources; however, the correlation is strongly suppressed by fluctuations in the number of particle sources, and when properly parameterized the correlation strength appears independent of collision centrality only when the number of sources is fixed.

## Method

The author develops an analytical superposition model where particles produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions originate from k identical and independent sources, with each source having a linear parameterization of average transverse momentum versus multiplicity. The model first treats the number of sources as fixed, then introduces fluctuations in the source number using a Gamma distribution, and finally applies the results to the Wounded Nucleon Model and a "mixing" version of that model to make predictions for the NA49 experiment at SPS energy (158 AGeV) in the forward rapidity window [4.0,5.5].

## Key numbers

- Average multiplicity of charged particles in p-p interaction within the NA49 acceptance: ⟨N⟩ = 1.4
- Calorimeter resolution formula for dispersion of projectile participant number: σ_proj(⟨N_proj⟩) = 0.77√(A - ⟨N_proj⟩) - 0.28 - 0.032(A - ⟨N_proj⟩), where A=208 for lead
- For lead projectile, σ varies between 3.5 and 4.0 for 1 < ⟨N_proj⟩ < 150, dropping to 1.6 at ⟨N_proj⟩ = 200
- In the mixing model, the variance of particle sources is parameterized as σ²(N_proj) = 1.32 N_proj - 0.004 N_proj², with maximum 10.4 at N_proj = 165
- The target participant variance parameterization: σ²_targ(N_proj) = 3.28 N_proj - 0.0158 N_proj², reaching maximum 13.1 at N_proj = 104

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transverse momentum fluctuations quantified by the measure Φ(p_T) introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (2004), where the non-monotonic behavior of Φ(p_T) as a function of collision centrality was explained by multiplicity fluctuations combined with the p_T vs. N correlation. The paper provides analytical predictions for the NA49 Collaboration's ongoing measurement of the p_T vs. N correlation in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, and tests two models: the transparency-class Wounded Nucleon Model of Bialas (1976) and the mixing models of Gazdzicki (2005) and Konchakovski (2005), which successfully described multiplicity fluctuations at fixed number of projectile participants. The results will enable a coherent picture of event-by-event fluctuations when combined with existing data on multiplicity fluctuations and transverse momentum fluctuations.
