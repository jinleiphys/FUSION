# Shapes of the Proton

> A model proton wave function, constructed using Poincare invariance, and constrained by recent electromagnetic form factor data, is used to study the shape of the proton. Spin-dependent quark densities are defined as matrix elements of density operators in proton states of definite spin-polarization, and shown to have an infinite variety of non-spherical shapes. For high momentum quarks with spin parallel to that of the proton, the shape resembles that of a peanut, but for quarks with anti-parallel spin the shape is that of a bagel.

# Shapes of the Proton

## Key claim

A model proton wave function built with Poincaré invariance and constrained by recent electromagnetic form factor data reveals that the proton exhibits an infinite variety of non-spherical shapes when spin-dependent quark densities are examined, but these shapes are only visible in matrix elements taken for protons in a fixed polarization state, the Wigner-Eckart theorem prevents any static quadrupole moment for a spin-1/2 particle.

## Method

The author constructs a three-quark wave function using Poincaré invariance, expressed in terms of relative momentum variables and antisymmetrized, with a spatial wave function of the Schlumpf form. Spin-dependent charge-density operators are defined in both momentum space and coordinate space, and their matrix elements are evaluated in proton states of definite spin polarization using only the proton's rest-frame wave function and ordinary coordinates, avoiding complications from Lorentz boosts or light-cone coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The spatial wave function parameters are: β = 0.607 GeV, γ = 3.5, quark mass m = 0.267 GeV.
- The ratio γ(K) = (E(K) − m)/(E(K) + m) reaches a value of 0.6 for K = 1 GeV/c.
- The most likely value of K corresponds to γ(K) = 0.16.
- The most likely value of the coordinate-space ratio ρ_L/ρ_U is about 0.25.
- The integral of the spin-dependent density operator for real nucleons gives 0.5 ± 0.34 using experimental values of Δq, and the model gives 0.5 ± 0.37.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of recent data from Jones (1999) and Gayou (2001) showing that the ratio G_E/G_M falls with increasing Q^2, which indicates nonzero orbital angular momentum in the proton as argued by Ralston, Braun (2001), Miller (2002), and Ji. The model builds on the wave function approach of Frank (1995) and Schlumpf, and the spin-dependent density technique is illustrated with non-relativistic and relativistic (Hydrogen atom) examples. The results enable future calculations of spin-dependent structure functions g_1 and g_2, the NΔ electromagnetic transition form factor, and spin-dependent cross sections in high-momentum-transfer polarized proton scattering.
