# Canonical interpretations of the newly observed $őě_c(2923)^0$, $őě_c(2939)^0$ and $őě_c(2965)^0$ resonances

> Three neutral resonances $őě_c(2923)^0$, $őě_c(2939)^0$ and $őě_c(2965)^0$ have been observed in the $őõ_c^+ K^-$ mass spectrum by the LHCb Collaboration. Given the $őě_c$ and $őě_c^\prime$ mass spectra predicted by the constituent quark models, these three resonances are tentatively treated as the $őĽ-$mode $őě_c(2S)$, $őě_c^\prime(2S)$, $őě_c^{\prime *}(2S)$, and $őě_c^\prime(1P)$ states, and their strong decay behaviors are calculated within the $^3P_0$ model. Our results indicate that the $őě_c(2923)^0$ and $őě_c(2939)^0$ can be clarified into the $J^P=3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ $őě_c^\prime(1P)$ states respectively, and the $őě_c(2965)^0$ can be regarded as the $J^P=1/2^+$ $őě_c^\prime(2S)$ state. Also, we suggest that the previous observed $őě_c(2930)$ may be the overlap of two structures $őě_c(2923)$ and $őě_c(2939)$, and the $őě_c(2970)$ should be the same state as $őě_c(2965)$. Other theoretical information on the missing $őě_c(2S)$, $őě_c^{\prime *}(2S)$, and $őě_c^\prime(1P)$ states may be helpful for future experimental searches.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the three newly observed resonances can be assigned to specific conventional quark-model states: the őě_c(2923)^0 and őě_c(2939)^0 are identified as J^P=3/2^- and 5/2^- őě_c'(1P) states respectively, while the őě_c(2965)^0 is identified as a J^P=1/2^+ őě_c'(2S) state; this assignment is conditional on the calculated strong decay widths within the ^3P_0 model agreeing with the experimental data, and the paper notes that the őě_c(2S) assignment is excluded because it cannot decay to őõ_c KŐÖ.

## Method

The strong decay widths of the three resonances were calculated under various assignments (őě_c(2S), őě_c'(2S), őě_c'*(2S), and five őě_c'(1P) states) using the ^3P_0 quark-pair-creation model. The model parameters (harmonic oscillator parameter őĪ_ŌĀ, quark masses, and overall pair-creation strength ő≥) were taken from previous successful studies of singly heavy baryons. The calculated total widths and partial decay modes were compared with the experimental masses and widths from LHCb to determine the most likely assignments.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the low-lying őě_c and őě_c' spectra using the ^3P_0 model, following previous quark-model studies by Ebert (2011), Chen (2014, 2016), and others. The paper contradicts the QCD sum rule analysis by Yang (2020), which suggested all three resonances are P-wave őě_c' baryons. The results enable future experimental searches for the missing őě_c(2S), őě_c'*(2S), and other őě_c'(1P) states, and suggest that the previously observed őě_c(2930) (from BaBar) is an overlap of the two new narrow structures, while őě_c(2970) (from Belle) is the same state as őě_c(2965).
