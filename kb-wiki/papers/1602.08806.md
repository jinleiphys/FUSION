# Can $X(5568)$ be a tetraquark state?

> Very recently, the D0 collaboration has reported the observation of a narrow structure, $X(5568)$, in the decay process $X(5568)\to B^0_sπ^\pm$ using the 10.4${\rm fb}^{-1}$ data of $p\bar p$ collision at $\sqrt s= 1.96$ TeV. This structure is of great interest since it is the first hadronic state with four different valence quark flavors, $b,s,u,d$. In this work, we investigate tetraquarks with four different quark flavors. Based on the diquark-antidiquark scheme, we study the spectroscopy of the tetraquarks with one heavy bottom/charm quark and three light quarks. We find that the lowest-lying S-wave state, a tetraquark with the flavor $[su][\bar b\bar d ]$ and the spin-parity $J^P=0^+$, is about 150 MeV higher than the $X(5568)$. Further detailed experimental and theoretical studies of the spectrum, production and decays of tetraquark states with four different flavors in the future are severely needed towards a better understanding its nature and the classification of hadron exotic states.

# Can X(5568) be a tetraquark state?

## Key claim
The lowest-lying S-wave tetraquark state with flavor [su][\bar b\bar d] and spin-parity J^P=0^+ is predicted at approximately 5.7 GeV, which is about 150 MeV higher than the X(5568) mass reported by the D0 collaboration (5567.8 ± 2.9 MeV). The authors conclude that identifying X(5568) as a tetraquark with J^P=0^+ is a challenge to the tetraquark model, and that the observed state is too light to be the bottom-sector partner of the D_{s0}*(2317).

## Method
The authors employ a diquark-antidiquark scheme with an effective Hamiltonian containing spin-spin interactions inside and between the diquark and antidiquark, plus spin-orbit and orbit-orbit terms. They construct mass matrices for tetraquark states with quark content [qq'][\bar q\bar Q] (where q,q' are light quarks and Q is a bottom or charm quark) for J^P = 0^+, 1^+, and 2^+, using spin-spin coupling constants and diquark masses previously determined from analyses of X(3872) and Y_b(10890) by Maiani et al. (2004), Ali et al. (2009, 2011, 2014), and Zhu (2015). The spectra are computed for S-wave (L=0) configurations.

## Key numbers
- X(5568) mass: M_X = (5567.8 ± 2.9) MeV, width: Γ_X = (21.9 ± 6.4) MeV, from D0 collaboration using 10.4 fb^{-1} of p\bar p collision data at √s = 1.96 TeV.
- The lowest-lying S-wave 0^+ tetraquark with flavor [su][\bar b\bar d] is predicted at approximately 5.7 GeV, about 150 MeV above the X(5568) mass.
- In the charm sector, the lowest tetraquark state is about 60 MeV higher than the D_{s0}*(2317).
- Diquark masses used: m_{[cq]} = 1.932 GeV, m_{[bq]} = 5.249 GeV, quoted from Ali et al. (2009, 2011) and Zhu (2015).
- Spin-spin couplings for quark-antiquark pairs (in MeV): q\bar q = 315, s\bar s = 121, s\bar q = 195, c\bar q = 70, c\bar s = 72, c\bar c = 59, b\bar q = 23, b\bar s = 23.
- Spin-spin couplings for diquarks in color antitriplet (in MeV): qq = 103, ss = 72, sq = 64, cq = 22, cs = 25, bq = 6.6.

## Context
This work extends the diquark-antidiquark tetraquark model previously applied to hidden-charm and hidden-bottom XYZ states by Maiani et al. (2004), Ali et al. (2009, 2011, 2014), and others, to the case of tetraquarks with four different quark flavors. The X(5568) was the first reported hadron with four distinct valence flavors (b,s,u,d). The authors note that the X(5568) is too far from the B_d^0 K^± threshold (5774 MeV) for a hadronic molecule interpretation, and that B_s^0 π^± interactions are too weak to form a bound state per Guo (2009). The LHCb Collaboration did not observe a signal for X(5568) in the B_s^0 π^± invariant mass scan between 5.5 and 5.7 GeV, and the authors suggest searching for tetraquark states with four different flavors above 5.7 GeV.
