# A Mesonic Analog of the Deuteron

> Using the LAMP model for nuclear quark structure, we calculate the binding energy and quark structure of a $B$ meson merging with a $D$ meson. The larger-than-nucleon masses of the two heavy quarks allow for a more reliable application of the Born-Oppenheimer-like approximation of the LAMP. With the absence of quark-level Pauli Exclusion Principle repulsive effects, the appearance of a bound state is unsurprising. Our variational calculation shows that the molecular, deuteron-like state structure changes rather abruptly, as the separation between the two mesons decreases, at a separation of about 0.45 fm, into a four-quark bound state, although one maintaining an internal structure rather than that of a four-quark bag. Unlike the deuteron, pion exchange does not provide any contribution to the $\approx 150$ MeV binding.

## Key claim

Using the LAMP model, a variational calculation shows that a $B$ meson and a $D$ meson can form a bound four-quark state with a binding energy of approximately 150 MeV, but the state's structure changes abruptly at a heavy-quark separation of about 0.45 fm from a molecular, deuteron-like configuration into a four-quark bound state that still retains internal structure rather than becoming a four-quark bag.

## Method

The authors apply the relativistic Los Alamos Model Potential (LAMP), a Born-Oppenheimer-like framework where heavy $b$ and $c$ quarks are treated as fixed centers providing linear Lorentz scalar and vector confining potentials for the light $\bar{u}$ and $\bar{d}$ antiquarks. They construct a two-well wave function for each light antiquark, allowing for delocalization (tunneling) between the two wells parameterized by a mixing parameter $\epsilon$, and minimize the expectation value of the squared Dirac Hamiltonian $\langle H_D^2 \rangle$ variationally with respect to $\epsilon$ and the half-separation $\delta$ between the heavy quarks.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $b\,c\,\bar{u}\,\bar{d}$ four-quark state: approximately 150 MeV (from a dimensionless squared-energy valley depth $\Delta H_D^{\ 2} = 0.097$ at $\epsilon = 1.0$ and $\delta = 0.18$).
- Heavy-quark separation at which the molecular-to-four-quark transition occurs: about 0.45 fm (corresponding to $\delta \approx 0.2$ with $\kappa = 2.520$ fm$^{-1}$).
- The confining potential strength used: $\kappa^2 = 1.253$ GeV/fm, with $\kappa = 2.520$ fm$^{-1}$.
- Ground state eigenenergy for a single well: 0.375 GeV.
- The energy difference between $\epsilon = 0$ and $\epsilon = 1$ at the valley minimum: about 24 MeV (0.0023 dimensionless squared-energy units).
- Predicted mass range for the bound state: approximately 7 GeV/$c^2$.

## Context

This work extends the LAMP model, previously applied to $^3$He and $^4$He (Goldman, Silbar, and collaborators, 1990s), to a mesonic system that avoids the complications of pion exchange and quark Pauli repulsion present in the deuteron. The calculation contrasts with conventional nuclear physics approaches (e.g., potential models fit to scattering data as reviewed by Carlson, or effective field theory expansions as in Bira's work) by explicitly incorporating internal quark structure. The result enables future studies of collective excitations (center-of-mass motion and breathing modes) in heavy-quark systems and suggests that the small energy difference between molecular and four-quark configurations may explain the difficulty in identifying unambiguous four-quark states experimentally.
