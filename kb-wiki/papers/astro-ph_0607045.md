# Thermal evolution of rotating strange stars in color superconductivity phase

> Under the combination effect of the recommencement heating due to spin-down of strange stars and the heat perseveration due to weak conduct heat of the crust, the Cooper pair breaking and formation(PBF) in color superconduction quark matter arises. We investigated the cooling of the strange stars with a crust in color superconductivity phase including both decomfinement heating and PBF process. We find that deconfinement heating can delay the thermal evolution of strange stars and the PBF process suppresses the early temperature rise of the stars. The cooling strange stars behave within the brightness constraint of young compact objects when the color superconductivity gap is small enough.

# Thermal evolution of rotating strange stars in color superconductivity phase

## Key claim

The combination of deconfinement heating (from spin-down of strange stars with a crust) and the Cooper pair breaking and formation (PBF) process in color superconducting quark matter can produce cooling curves that are consistent with observational brightness constraints, but only when the color superconductivity gap is small enough (around 1 MeV or less); larger gaps (tens to 100 MeV) may produce cooling curves that conflict with the absence of observed very hot compact objects.

## Method

The authors solved the thermal evolution (cooling) equation for a canonical 1.4 solar mass strange star with a nuclear crust, including neutrino emissivities from the quark direct Urca, quark modified Urca, and quark bremsstrahlung processes (each suppressed by exponential factors in the color-flavor locked phase), plus the PBF neutrino emissivity. They also included deconfinement heating from neutrons falling into the quark core as the star spins down via magnetic dipole radiation, using a crust mass that depends quadratically on rotation frequency. The surface temperature was related to internal temperature via a standard envelope relation, and gravitational redshift was applied to obtain the effective surface temperature seen by a distant observer.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino emissivity from quark direct Urca process: \(\epsilon^{(D)} \simeq 8.8 \times 10^{26} \alpha_c (\rho_b/\rho_0) Y_e^{1/3} T_9^6\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density and \(T_9\) is temperature in units of \(10^9\) K
- Neutrino emissivity from quark modified Urca process: \(\epsilon^{(M)} \simeq 2.83 \times 10^{19} \alpha_c^2 (\rho_b/\rho_0) T_9^8\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Neutrino emissivity from quark bremsstrahlung: \(\epsilon^{(QB)} \simeq 2.98 \times 10^{19} (\rho_b/\rho_0) T_9^8\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- PBF neutrino emissivity base value: \(\epsilon^{(PBF)}_0 \simeq 1.4 \times 10^{20} N_\nu F a_q (\rho_b/\rho_0)^{2/3} T_9^7\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Electron specific heat: \(c_e \simeq 2.5 \times 10^{20} (\rho_b/\rho_0)^{2/3} T_9\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) K\(^{-1}\)
- Quark specific heat in color superconductivity phase: \(c_{sq} = 3.2 c_q (T_c/T) \times [2.5 - 1.7(T/T_c) + 3.6(T/T_c)^2] \exp(-\Delta/k_B T)\), with \(\Delta = 1.76 T_c\)
- Crust mass: \(M_c \leq 10^{-5} M_\odot\) in the static case, with a quadratic dependence on rotation frequency: \(M_c = M_c^0 (1 + 0.24 \nu_3^2 + 0.16 \nu_3^8)\) where \(\nu_3 = \nu/10^3\) Hz
- Heat release per absorbed neutron: \(q_n \sim 10-40\) MeV, with a chosen value of 20 MeV for the calculations
- Initial temperature: \(T_0 = 10^9\) K; initial period: \(P_0 = 0.78\) ms; magnetic tilt angle: \(\theta = 45^\circ\)
- Surface temperature relation: \(T_s = 3.08 \times 10^6 g_{s,14}^{1/4} T_9^{0.5495}\) K, where \(g_{s,14}\) is surface gravity in units of \(10^{14}\) cm s\(^{-2}\)
- Gap parameters explored: 0.1 MeV to 100 MeV
- Deconfinement heating can delay cooling so that CSSs could be observed until \(10^5 \sim 10^7\) years old

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strange star cooling by Blaschke, Klähn and Voskresensky (2000), who found that bare strange stars in the color-flavor locked phase cool too rapidly to match observational data. The authors incorporate two effects neglected in that prior work: deconfinement heating from crust accretion during spin-down (previously studied by Yuan and Zhang (1999) and Yu and Zheng (2006)), and the PBF neutrino process in quark matter (following Prashanth and Madappa (2001)). The key advance is showing that for small enough gaps (around 1 MeV), the PBF process suppresses the early temperature rise that would otherwise produce very hot objects inconsistent with observations, while deconfinement heating keeps the stars warm enough at later ages to be consistent with X-ray data from young compact objects. The results constrain which color superconductivity phases (gap sizes) can exist in strange stars with crusts.
