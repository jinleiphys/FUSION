# Leading order pQCD hadron production and nuclear modification factors at RHIC and the LHC

> Hadron production in leading order pQCD is reviewed. The shape of the single inclusive particle spectra is well described for $p_T \geq 2-3$ GeV at center of mass energies from 20 GeV to 2 TeV. The phenomenological K-factor is found to decrease systematically with $\sqrt{s}$. For ultra-relativistic heavy ion reactions the calculation is augmented with the effects of initial multiple parton scattering and final state radiative energy loss. Baseline CERN-LHC predictions for hadron production in $p+p$ and suppression in central $Pb+Pb$ reactions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV are given in comparison to the corresponding results at BNL-RHIC and CERN-SPS energies.

## Key claim

Leading-order pQCD with a phenomenological K-factor and intrinsic kT-smearing can describe the shape of single inclusive hadron spectra for pT ≥ 2-3 GeV across a wide range of center-of-mass energies, but the extracted K-factor decreases systematically with √s and carries a projected 50% uncertainty when extrapolated to LHC energies of √s = 5.5-8.8 TeV.

## Method

The author computes single inclusive hadron production in p+p and p+anti-p collisions using the standard factorized pQCD formalism at leading order, convolving GRV98 LO parton distribution functions, BKK LO fragmentation functions, and lowest-order partonic cross sections with a fixed Gaussian intrinsic kT-smearing of ⟨kT²⟩ = 1.8 GeV². For heavy ion reactions, the calculation is augmented with initial-state multiple parton scattering (Cronin effect) using a Glauber-type formalism, nuclear shadowing via the EKS'98 parameterization, and final-state radiative energy loss computed to third order in opacity using the GLV reaction operator approach, with the energy loss probability distribution applied as a modification to the fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological K_NLO decreases from approximately 6 at √s ≈ 50 GeV to approximately 1.5 at √s ≈ 2 TeV when no intrinsic kT-smearing is used and scales are fixed to Q_PDF = Q_FF = pT
- With fixed ⟨kT²⟩ = 1.8 GeV², the extracted K_NLO follows K_NLO = 2.7924 - 0.0999 ln s (linear fit) or K_NLO = 3.8444 - 0.3234 ln s + 0.0107 ln² s (quadratic fit) in ln s
- At √s = 5.5 TeV, the fractional difference between p+anti-p and p+p cross sections varies from 2.5% at pT = 5 GeV to 4.8% at pT = 150 GeV
- The π⁰/0.5(h⁺ + h⁻) ratio becomes approximately 0.5 at pT ≈ 15 GeV at RHIC and pT ≈ 75 GeV at the LHC
- The transverse momentum transfer per unit length in cold nuclear matter is μ²/λ ≈ 0.05 GeV²/fm
- At LHC energies, the nuclear modification factor R_AA varies by a factor of 5: from 10-20 fold suppression at pT = 10 GeV to a factor 2-3 suppression at pT = 100 GeV
- The effective initial gluon densities used are ρ_g(RHIC) = 30-50/fm³ and ρ_g(LHC) = 130-275/fm³
- At CERN-SPS energies (√s = 17 GeV), Cronin enhancement reaches 250% in d+Au and 400% in central Au+Au at pT ≈ 4 GeV

## Context

This work extends leading-order pQCD calculations of hadron production from lower energies (CERN-SPS at √s = 17 GeV, RHIC at √s = 200 GeV) to LHC energies (√s = 5.5 TeV for Pb+Pb, 8.8 TeV for p+Pb), complementing next-to-leading order calculations by Accardi (2002) and others. The paper provides baseline predictions for the CERN Yellow Report on Hard Probes, enabling future experimental measurements at the LHC to distinguish between conventional nuclear effects (Cronin enhancement, shadowing) and the non-Abelian energy loss signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, with the key prediction that the nuclear modification factor R_AA at the LHC will show a much stronger pT dependence than the approximately constant suppression observed at RHIC.
