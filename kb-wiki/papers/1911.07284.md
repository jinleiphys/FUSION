# The d*(2380) and its family of pion assisted dibaryons

> The status of $NО”$ and $О”О”$ dibaryons introduced by Dyson and Xuong in 1964 is briefly reviewed with focus on the d*(2380), tentatively assigned as a $О”О”$ dibaryon resonance. It is argued that the apparently small value of width, $О“_{d^\ast}$$\approx$70 MeV, favors hadronic structure for the d*(2380) dibaryon rather than a six-quark structure.

# The d*(2380) and its family of pion assisted dibaryons

## Key claim
The d*(2380) dibaryon resonance, tentatively assigned as a О”О” dibaryon, is better described by a hadronic structure with a diffuse ПҖNО” component than by a compact six-quark or hexaquark configuration, because the observed width of approximately 70 MeV is too large for a compact О”О” object but consistent with a ПҖD12 decay mode.

## Method
The author reviews the Dyson-Xuong (1964) SU(6) to SU(4) mass formula for nonstrange s-wave dibaryons and then presents a hadronic three-body model using Faddeev equations for the ПҖNN and ПҖNО” systems with separable pairwise interactions. In this model, the О” resonance is treated as a stable isobar that decays into ПҖN, and the coupled-channel BО” вҶ” ПҖD problem is reduced to a single integral equation for the BО” T-matrix, where D represents NN or NО” dibaryon isobars. The width of the d*(2380) is then evaluated by averaging the momentum-dependent О” вҶ’ NПҖ decay width over the О”О” bound-state momentum distribution, using the r.m.s. radius from the wavefunction.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the Dyson-Xuong (1964) SU(6) prediction of nonstrange dibaryons, focusing on the NО” (D12, D21) and О”О” (D03, D30) states. The author's hadronic three-body Faddeev calculations (Gal and Garcilazo, 2013, 2014) generate pole positions for these dibaryons that agree well with experimental data from the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2011, 2013, 2014, 2015) and the ELPH experiment (2017, 2019). The paper argues against compact hexaquark interpretations (Wang et al., 2014; Dong et al., 2016) and a recent diquark model (Shi et al., 2019), showing that the observed width of the d*(2380) requires a diffuse structure with a significant ПҖD12 component rather than a compact О”О” or six-quark configuration. The analysis enables future searches for the D30 dibaryon, for which only upper limits exist (WASA-at-COSY, 2016), and clarifies the role of pion-assisted dynamics in forming dibaryon resonances beyond the NN system.
