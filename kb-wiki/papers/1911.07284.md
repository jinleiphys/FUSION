# The d*(2380) and its family of pion assisted dibaryons

> The status of $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ dibaryons introduced by Dyson and Xuong in 1964 is briefly reviewed with focus on the d*(2380), tentatively assigned as a $ΔΔ$ dibaryon resonance. It is argued that the apparently small value of width, $Γ_{d^\ast}$$\approx$70 MeV, favors hadronic structure for the d*(2380) dibaryon rather than a six-quark structure.

# The d*(2380) and its family of pion assisted dibaryons

## Key claim
The d*(2380) dibaryon resonance, tentatively assigned as a ΔΔ dibaryon, is better described by a hadronic structure with a diffuse πNΔ component than by a compact six-quark or hexaquark configuration, because the observed width of approximately 70 MeV is too large for a compact ΔΔ object but consistent with a πD12 decay mode.

## Method
The author reviews the Dyson-Xuong (1964) SU(6) to SU(4) mass formula for nonstrange s-wave dibaryons and then presents a hadronic three-body model using Faddeev equations for the πNN and πNΔ systems with separable pairwise interactions. In this model, the Δ resonance is treated as a stable isobar that decays into πN, and the coupled-channel BΔ ↔ πD problem is reduced to a single integral equation for the BΔ T-matrix, where D represents NN or NΔ dibaryon isobars. The width of the d*(2380) is then evaluated by averaging the momentum-dependent Δ → Nπ decay width over the ΔΔ bound-state momentum distribution, using the r.m.s. radius from the wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- The d*(2380) resonance has a total width Γ ≈ 70 MeV, with Γ_{d* → NNππ} ≈ 65 MeV dominating the decay.
- The Dyson-Xuong mass formula predicts M = A + 10B for the ΔΔ dibaryon D03, where A = 1878 MeV (NN threshold) and B ≈ 47 MeV, giving M ≈ 2350 MeV.
- The D12 (NΔ) dibaryon S-matrix pole position from NN phase shift analyses is 2148 − i63 MeV, and from dedicated pp ↔ npπ+ coupled-channels analyses is 2144 − i55 MeV.
- In the hadronic Faddeev model with rΔ = 1.3 fm, the D12 pole is at 2147 − i60 MeV, the D21 at 2165 − i64 MeV, the D03 at 2383 − i41 MeV, and the D30 at 2411 − i41 MeV.
- With rΔ = 0.9 fm, the D03 pole is at 2343 − i24 MeV and the D30 at 2370 − i22 MeV.
- The ΔΔ binding energy in the d*(2380) is B_{ΔΔ} = 2 × 1232 − 2380 = 84 MeV.
- The free-space Δ width is Γ_Δ ≈ 115 MeV; the in-medium single-Δ width reduced by phase space is 81 MeV.
- For a compact ΔΔ object with R_{ΔΔ} = 0.76 fm (from quark-based RGM), the upper limit for Γ_{d* → NNππ} is about 47 MeV.
- The ELPH γd → dπ0π0 experiment reports a Breit-Wigner resonance centered at 2370 MeV with width 68 MeV.
- The dπ invariant-mass peak from WASA-at-COSY is shifted about 20 MeV below the D12(2150) mass and has a width smaller by about 40 MeV than the D12 width.
- Branching ratios (theory vs. experiment): dπ0π0 11.2% vs. 14±1%, dπ+π− 20.4% vs. 23±2%, pnπ0π0 11.6% vs. 12±2%, pnπ+π− 25.8% vs. 30±5%, ppπ−π0 4.7% vs. 6±1%, nnπ+π0 4.7% vs. 6±1%, NNπ 8.3% vs. ≤9%, NN 13.3% vs. 12±3%, total 100% vs. 103%.
- The hexaquark cross section calculation for γd → d*(2380) → dπ0π0 underestimates the measured value of about 18 nb by a factor of approximately 20.
- 6q admixture probability in the d*(2380) is estimated to be smaller than 1%, based on a 20 MeV potential matrix element and 300 MeV energy separation from the 7S3 6q dibaryon.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the Dyson-Xuong (1964) SU(6) prediction of nonstrange dibaryons, focusing on the NΔ (D12, D21) and ΔΔ (D03, D30) states. The author's hadronic three-body Faddeev calculations (Gal and Garcilazo, 2013, 2014) generate pole positions for these dibaryons that agree well with experimental data from the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2011, 2013, 2014, 2015) and the ELPH experiment (2017, 2019). The paper argues against compact hexaquark interpretations (Wang et al., 2014; Dong et al., 2016) and a recent diquark model (Shi et al., 2019), showing that the observed width of the d*(2380) requires a diffuse structure with a significant πD12 component rather than a compact ΔΔ or six-quark configuration. The analysis enables future searches for the D30 dibaryon, for which only upper limits exist (WASA-at-COSY, 2016), and clarifies the role of pion-assisted dynamics in forming dibaryon resonances beyond the NN system.
