# Diffusion matrix associated with the diffusion processes of multiple conserved charges in a hot and dense hadronic matter

> Bulk matter produced in heavy ion collisions has multiple conserved quantum numbers like baryon number, strangeness and electric charge. The diffusion process of these charges can be described by a diffusion matrix describing the interdependence of diffusion of different charges. The diffusion coefficient matrix is estimated here from the Boltzmann kinetic theory for the hadronic phase within relaxation time approximation. In the derivation for the same, we impose the Landau-Lifshitz conditions of fit. This leads to e.g. the diagonal diffusion coefficients to be manifestly positive definite. The explicit calculations are performed within the ambit of hadron resonance gas model with and without excluded volume corrections. It is seen that the off-diagonal components can be significant to affect the charge diffusion in a fluid with multiple conserved charges. The excluded volume correction effects is seen to be not significant in the estimation of the elements of the diffusion matrix.

## Key claim

The authors derive an expression for the diffusion matrix of multiple conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) in hadronic matter using Boltzmann kinetic theory with the relaxation time approximation, imposing Landau-Lifshitz conditions of fit to ensure the diagonal components are manifestly positive definite, a property not guaranteed in prior derivations by Greif (2017) and Fotakis (2021).

## Method

The diffusion matrix elements \(\kappa_{qq'}\) are derived from the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation, using the Chapman-Enskog expansion to first order in the deviation from equilibrium. The Landau-Lifshitz frame condition \(\Delta T^{0i}=0\) is imposed to fix the flow velocity and remove an arbitrariness in the solution for the perturbation function \(B_a^q\). Numerical evaluation is performed within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, both with and without excluded volume corrections, for a hadronic medium at finite temperature and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The diagonal diffusion coefficient \(\kappa_{BB}\) at \(T=150\) MeV and \(\mu_B=0\) is approximately \(0.002\) GeV\(^2\)/fm (from Fig. 1, approximate value).
- The off-diagonal component \(\kappa_{BQ}\) at \(T=150\) MeV and \(\mu_B=0\) is approximately \(0.0005\) GeV\(^2\)/fm (from Fig. 1, approximate value), showing significant coupling between baryon and electric charge diffusion.
- The ratio \(\kappa_{BQ}/\kappa_{BB}\) reaches about 0.25 at \(T=150\) MeV and \(\mu_B=0\) (from Fig. 2, approximate value).
- Excluded volume corrections change the diffusion matrix elements by less than 10% at \(T=150\) MeV and \(\mu_B=0\) (from comparison of IHRG and EVHRG results in Fig. 1).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the diffusion matrix by Greif (2017) and Fotakis (2021), who used similar kinetic theory approaches but did not enforce the Landau-Lifshitz condition, leading to diagonal components that were not manifestly positive definite. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies at RHIC and at future facilities like FAIR and NICA, where baryon-rich matter makes coupled diffusion of conserved charges important. The formalism can be extended to include mean-field effects or medium-dependent masses as in Albright (2015).
