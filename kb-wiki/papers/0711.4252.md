# Decoupling in the Similarity Renormalization Group for Nucleon-Nucleon Forces

> Decoupling via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) of low-energy nuclear physics from high-energy details of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is examined for two-body observables and few-body binding energies. The universal nature of this decoupling is illustrated and errors from suppressing high-momentum modes above the decoupling scale are shown to be perturbatively small.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of nucleon-nucleon potentials achieves a universal decoupling of low-energy observables from high-momentum degrees of freedom, such that errors from suppressing high-momentum modes above the decoupling scale are perturbatively small and follow a power-law dependence predicted by first-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors evolve initial nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne V18 and chiral EFT N3LO) using SRG flow equations with the generator \(G_s = T_{\text{rel}}\), producing evolved potentials \(V_\lambda\) characterized by the flow parameter \(\lambda\) (in fm\(^{-1}\)). They then apply a smooth exponential regulator \(\exp[-(k^2/\Lambda^2)^n]\) to cut off high-momentum matrix elements above a cutoff \(\Lambda\), and compute relative errors in phase shifts, deuteron observables, and few-body ground-state energies (using the No-Core Shell Model for \(^3\)H, \(^4\)He, and \(^6\)Li) between cut and uncut potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel using the N3LO (500 MeV) potential evolved to \(\lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), phase shifts from cut potentials with \(\Lambda > \lambda\) show good agreement with uncut results up to laboratory energies \(E_{\text{lab}} \approx 2\Lambda^2/m\).
- The relative error in phase shifts, deuteron binding energy, and radius follows a power-law dependence \(1/\Lambda^{2n}\) in the decoupling region \(\Lambda > \lambda\), where \(n\) is the regulator exponent (tested with \(n = 8\) and other integer values).
- For \(^4\)He ground-state energies computed with NCSM at basis size \(N_{\text{max}} = 12\), the relative error shows the same power-law decrease for \(\Lambda\) slightly above \(\lambda\), with precision reaching \(10^{-6}\) to \(10^{-5}\).
- For \(^6\)Li at basis size \(N_{\text{max}} = 8\), the uncut energies vary with \(\lambda\) (e.g., \(\lambda = 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) vs. \(3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\)) due to the two-body-only SRG evolution, but the decoupling pattern persists.

## Context

This work extends the decoupling demonstration of Bogner (2007) from the Argonne V18 potential to chiral EFT potentials (N3LO) and to few-body nuclei up to \(A = 6\), verifying the universal nature of SRG decoupling. The results explain the improved convergence with basis size observed in NCSM calculations by Bogner (2007) and enable future controlled truncations of three-body forces in SRG evolution, as anticipated by Bogner (2007).
