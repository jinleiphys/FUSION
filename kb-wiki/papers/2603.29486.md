# Optimizing the description of the Delta region in the Ghent Hybrid model for single-pion production

> Single-pion production is an important contribution to the total neutrino-nucleus interaction cross section in accelerator-based neutrino oscillation experiments. The goal of this paper is to improve the Ghent model in the Delta resonance region by incorporating as many physical constraints as possible while keeping the number of fitted parameters as low as possible. A multipole decomposition of the model is performed allowing the use of $K$-matrix theory to unitarize the background contributions. Watson's theorem is enforced by consistently modifying both the Delta and background contributions across all multipoles. Furthermore, the decay width and form factors of the Delta contribution are modified to ensure compliance with Watson's theorem, while the model is extended to include $ρ$- and $ω$-exchange diagrams. These adjustments are compared with other pion production models, as well as with CLAS pion electroproduction data on the nucleon. The results show considerable improvement in the description of the Delta peak region.

## Key claim

The Ghent Hybrid model for single-pion production is improved in the Delta resonance region by enforcing Watson's theorem across all multipoles for both background and resonance contributions, using a multipole decomposition and K-matrix unitarization, while keeping the number of fitted parameters low; the improvements are validated against CLAS electroproduction data and show considerable improvement in describing the Delta peak region, but the paper only presents results for the vector current, with axial current results deferred to future work.

## Method

A multipole decomposition of the Ghent model's hadronic current was performed, allowing the use of K-matrix theory to unitarize the background contributions and enforce Watson's theorem across all multipoles for both Delta and background terms. The Delta decay width and form factors were modified using results from the MAID analysis, and the model was extended by including rho- and omega-exchange diagrams in the vector current. The resulting amplitudes were compared with CLAS pion electroproduction data on the nucleon and with other pion production models (MAID and DCC).

## Key numbers

*   The Delta resonance region accounts for around 20% of the total interaction rate in T2K, Hyper-Kamiokande, and SBN program detectors.
*   The model uses a dipole form factor with \(M_V^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\) for the rho- and omega-exchange diagrams, following the MAID description.
*   All multipoles up to \(l=4\) are used, as higher multipoles do not contribute significantly to the responses in the Delta peak region.
*   The transition from the background to a Reggeized tree-level background occurs around \(W = 1.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Ghent Hybrid model for single-pion production (Raul_Hybrid_model) by imposing unitarity constraints more completely than the earlier method using Olsson phases (Alvarez-Ruso_Watson_Delta). It builds on the approach of Hernandez_HNVoffnucleon and the ANL-Osaka Dynamical Coupled Channel (DCC) framework (Kamano:2016bgm, Nakamura:2015rta), and uses the MAID analysis (Drechsel_MAID2007) to constrain form factors. The improved model is intended for use in neutrino event generators like NuWro and for interpreting data from experiments such as T2K, Hyper-Kamiokande, and ESSnuSB.
