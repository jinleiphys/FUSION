# Amplitude Analysis

> Even if a `complete set' of experimental observables were measured for the elastic scattering or photo/electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons, ambiguities would remain in the extracted partial-wave and isospin decomposed amplitudes. As these problems are not widely understood, the present work outlines the way model-dependence enters into analyses of data from both hadronic and electromagnetic facilities.

## Key claim

Even if a "complete set" of experimental observables were measured for elastic scattering or photo/electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons, ambiguities would remain in the extracted partial-wave and isospin decomposed amplitudes because the unknown overall phase of the scattering amplitude prevents unique inversion of the partial-wave series and, in cases like pion photoproduction, the isospin decomposition cannot be fixed from charge-state amplitudes alone.

## Method

The paper reviews formal results on amplitude analysis for spinless and spin-0–spin-1/2 scattering, then examines the problem of the unmeasurable overall phase in partial-wave and isospin decompositions. It discusses how the overall phase prevents unique extraction of partial-wave amplitudes from a known angular distribution, and shows that for pion photoproduction the four charge-state amplitudes cannot be uniquely decomposed into three isospin amplitudes even if overall phases are fixed for two reactions. The work then surveys practical approaches used in N* physics: restricted multipole analyses (e.g., Grushin fit), dispersion relations, and direct model fits including Breit-Wigner plus background contributions.

## Key numbers

- For spinless scattering, the cross section gives only \(|f|^2\), leaving the phase \(\phi(E,\theta)\) undetermined.
- In pion photoproduction, there are 4 charge-state amplitudes (\(\pi^0 p\), \(\pi^+ n\), \(\pi^- p\), \(\pi^0 n\)) but only 3 isospin amplitudes; the triangle relation \(\sqrt{2} A^{\pi^0 n} + A^{\pi^- p} + A^{\pi^+ n} = \sqrt{2} A^{\pi^0 p}\) does not fix relative angles.
- For CLAS data with type-\(S\) (cross section and single-polarization) and \(BT\) (beam-target double-polarization) measurements, only one relative phase remains undetermined apart from the overall phase.

## Context

This work extends formal results on amplitude ambiguities from spinless scattering (Hohler, 1983; Bowcock and Burkhardt, 1975) and from studies of \(\pi N\) scattering (Dean and Lee, 1970s), pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Chiang and Tabakin, 1970s), and electroproduction (Dmitrasinovic, Donnelly, and Gross, 1970s). It highlights that the overall phase problem is more severe for photoproduction than for \(\pi N\) scattering, where the triangle relation among charge-state amplitudes can resolve isospin ambiguities. The paper enables future work to determine the minimal theoretical input (e.g., analyticity constraints) needed to fix the overall phase in photo/electroproduction, particularly for CLAS data analysis.
