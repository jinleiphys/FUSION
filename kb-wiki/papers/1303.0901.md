# Measurement of Charge Multiplicity Asymmetry Correlations in High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at 200 GeV

> A study is reported of the same- and opposite-sign charge-dependent azimuthal correlations with respect to the event plane in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. The charge multiplicity asymmetries between the up/down and left/right hemispheres relative to the event plane are utilized. The contributions from statistical fluctuations and detector effects were subtracted from the (co-)variance of the observed charge multiplicity asymmetries. In the mid- to most-central collisions, the same- (opposite-) sign pairs are preferentially emitted in back-to-back (aligned on the same-side) directions. The charge separation across the event plane, measured by the difference, $Δ$, between the like- and unlike-sign up/down $-$ left/right correlations, is largest near the event plane. The difference is found to be proportional to the event-by-event final-state particle ellipticity (via the observed second-order harmonic $v^{\rm obs}_{2}$), where $Δ=(1.3\pm1.4({\rm stat})^{+4.0}_{-1.0}({\rm syst}))\times10^{-5}+(3.2\pm0.2({\rm stat})^{+0.4}_{-0.3}({\rm syst}))\times10^{-3}v^{\rm obs}_{2}$ for 20-40% Au+Au collisions. The implications for the proposed chiral magnetic effect are discussed.

## Key claim

The charge separation across the event plane, quantified by the difference Δ between like- and unlike-sign up/down minus left/right correlations, is found to be proportional to the event-by-event final-state particle ellipticity (via the observed second-order harmonic v₂ᵒᵇˢ) in mid- to most-central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, with the caveat that the intercept is consistent with zero within large statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured charge-dependent azimuthal correlations relative to the event plane in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using the STAR detector at RHIC. They constructed charge multiplicity asymmetries between the up/down and left/right hemispheres defined relative to the event plane, and subtracted contributions from statistical fluctuations and detector effects from the (co-)variance of these asymmetries. The resulting observable Δ, defined as the difference between like- and unlike-sign up/down minus left/right correlations, was studied as a function of collision centrality and event-by-event ellipticity.

## Key numbers

- For 20-40% central Au+Au collisions, the charge separation Δ is parameterized as Δ = (1.3 ± 1.4(stat)⁺⁴·⁰₋₁·₀(syst)) × 10⁻⁵ + (3.2 ± 0.2(stat)⁺⁰·⁴₋₀·₃(syst)) × 10⁻³ v₂ᵒᵇˢ.
- The same-sign pairs are preferentially emitted in back-to-back directions relative to the event plane, while opposite-sign pairs are preferentially emitted on the same side.
- The charge separation Δ is largest near the event plane.

## Context

This work extends previous searches for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions by using a new observable based on charge multiplicity asymmetries rather than the traditional γ correlator. The results provide constraints on the magnitude of CME signals in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, and enable future comparisons with theoretical calculations of CME-induced charge separation. The measurement is consistent with expectations from background sources such as local charge conservation coupled with elliptic flow, as discussed in prior work by the STAR Collaboration and others.
