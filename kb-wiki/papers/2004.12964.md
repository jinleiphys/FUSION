# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering with Coupled Nucleon-Delta Channels in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> In this work the elastic scattering of two nucleons is calculated in chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order taking into account the coupled N$Δ$-, $Δ$N- and $ΔΔ$-channels. To solve the coupled channel scattering equation one needs as input the potentials for all combinations of these initial and final states. Up to next-to leading order these (transition) potentials arise from one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange and contact interactions. For the two-pion exchange we give analytic expressions for the spectral functions derived from all contributing one-loop diagrams. The forms of the contact potentials at leading and next-to-leading order are determined. We perform a fit of the low energy constants, that belong to the $NN \rightarrow NN$ contact potential and contribute up to next-to-leading order to $S$- and $P$-waves of NN scattering only. The influence of the $Δ$-isobar dynamics entering through the coupled channels is studied in detail.

## Key claim

The paper calculates elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering in chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order by explicitly including coupled NΔ, ΔN, and ΔΔ channels, but the fit of low-energy constants is restricted to those belonging to the NN→NN contact potential that contribute only to S- and P-waves, meaning the delta-isobar dynamics in peripheral partial waves are predictions without adjustable parameters.

## Method

The authors derived all transition potentials (one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange from one-loop diagrams, and contact interactions) for the coupled NN, NΔ, ΔN, and ΔΔ channels up to next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory using the heavy baryon approach. They solved the Kadyshevsky coupled-channel scattering equation with these potentials, using spectral functions from Cutkosky cutting rules to evaluate the two-pion exchange contributions, and identified irreducible parts of planar box diagrams by analyzing the l0-integral over heavy baryon and pion propagators. The nine low-energy constants from the NN→NN contact potential were fitted to S- and P-wave phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis for cutoff values Λ = 400–700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting: Δ = 293 MeV
- Average pion mass: mπ = 138.03 MeV
- Weak pion decay constant: fπ = 92.4 MeV
- Nucleon axial vector coupling: gA = 1.29, chosen to give gπNN²/(4π) = 13.6 (consistent with gπN²/(4π) = 13.69 ± 0.20 from Baru (2011))
- Cutoff range for fitting low-energy constants: Λ = 400–700 MeV
- Threshold for real delta production: Tlab = 632 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral effective field theory calculations of the NN potential (Kaiser 1997, 1998; Epelbaum 1998, 2000) by treating the Δ-isobar as an explicit degree of freedom in coupled channels rather than only in intermediate states of two-pion exchange. It builds on phenomenological coupled-channel approaches (Bulla 1992; Poepping 1987; Sauer 2014) and the CD-Bonn extension (Deltuva 2003), and enables systematic study of Δ-isobar dynamics in NN scattering within a chiral effective field theory framework, with the potential for extension to higher orders and three-nucleon forces.
