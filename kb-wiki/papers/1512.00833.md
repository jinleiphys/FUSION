# Hyperon elastic electromagnetic form factors in the space-like momentum region

> We present a calculation of the electric and magnetic form factors of ground-state octet and decuplet baryons including strange quarks. We work with a combination of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator and covariant Bethe-Salpeter equations describing baryons as bound states of three (non-perturbative) quarks. Our form factors for the octet baryons are in good agreement with corresponding lattice data at finite $Q^2$; deviations in some isospin channels for the magnetic moments can be explained by missing meson cloud effects. At larger $Q^2$ our quark core calculation has predictive power for both, the octet and decuplet baryons.

## Key claim
The authors present a calculation of hyperon electromagnetic form factors using a rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, which reproduces lattice QCD data at finite momentum transfer but systematically underestimates magnetic moments at zero momentum transfer due to the omission of meson cloud effects; the calculation therefore has predictive power primarily at larger momentum transfers where such cloud contributions are negligible.

## Method
The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow-ladder truncation using the Maris-Tandy effective interaction model, then use the resulting quark propagators as input to the covariant three-body Bethe-Salpeter (Faddeev) equation for baryon bound states. Electromagnetic form factors are obtained by "gauging" the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which couples an external photon to the quark lines while preserving gauge invariance, and the quark-photon vertex itself is solved from an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the same truncation.

## Key numbers
- Octet hyperon masses from the Faddeev equation: Sigma/Lambda 1.073(1) GeV, Xi 1.235(5) GeV, Sigma* 1.33(2) GeV, Xi* 1.47(3) GeV; relative differences to experiment range from 4% to 10%.
- Magnetic moments (in nuclear magnetons mu_N): Sigma+ 1.82(2) vs. experimental 2.46(1); Sigma- -0.78(2) vs. -1.16(3); Xi0 -1.05(1) vs. -1.250(14); Xi- -0.57(4) vs. -0.651(3); Lambda -0.435(5) vs. -0.613(4).
- Electric mean-square radii (in fm^2): Sigma+ 0.56(3), Sigma0 0.057(8), Sigma- 0.45(3), Xi0 0.10(1), Xi- 0.37(4), Lambda 0.04(1).
- Magnetic mean-square radii (in fm^2): Sigma+ 0.43(2), Sigma0 0.39(3), Sigma- 0.50(1), Xi0 0.35(3), Xi- 0.20(2), Lambda 0.21(1).
- The effective interaction scale Lambda = 0.72 GeV; current quark masses m_u/d = 3.7 MeV and m_s = 85 MeV at renormalization scale mu^2 = (19 GeV)^2.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter calculations of nucleon, Delta, and Omega form factors (Eichmann 2011, Sanchis-Alepuz 2013) to all ground-state octet and decuplet hyperons. It provides a quark-core baseline that can be compared to lattice QCD results from Shanahan (2014) and to experimental magnetic moments; the systematic deviations in some isospin channels (e.g., Sigma+ vs. Sigma-) are attributed to missing pion and kaon cloud effects, which the authors plan to include in future work following the approach of Fischer (2008) and Sanchis-Alepuz (2014). The results enable predictions for hyperon form factors at momentum transfers beyond the reach of current lattice QCD.
