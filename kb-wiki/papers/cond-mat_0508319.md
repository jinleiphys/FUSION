# Loop updates for quantum Monte Carlo simulations in the canonical ensemble

> We present a new non-local updating scheme for quantum Monte Carlo simulations, which conserves particle number and other symmetries. It allows exact symmetry projection and direct evaluation of the equal-time Green's function and other observables in the canonical ensemble. The method is applied to bosonic atoms in optical lattices, neutron pairs in atomic nuclei and electron pairs in ultrasmall superconducting grains.

# Loop updates for quantum Monte Carlo simulations in the canonical ensemble

## Key claim

The paper presents a new class of non-local loop updates for quantum Monte Carlo simulations that explicitly conserve particle number and other symmetries, enabling direct sampling of the canonical ensemble with all moves accepted and without bounces or critical slowing down, provided the worm operator commutes with the residual interaction.

## Method

The method starts from a decomposition of the imaginary time propagator and performs a Markovian random walk in an extended configuration space related to the operator U'(β,τ) = e^{-τH} A e^{-(β-τ)H}, where A is a worm operator that commutes with the residual interaction V. The algorithm constructs loops by moving the worm operator through the world-line representation, inserting and removing interaction vertices according to locally optimal transition probabilities, until the configuration returns to a diagonal state where the states to the left and right of the worm operator are equal.

## Key numbers

- One-dimensional Bose-Hubbard model on 128 sites at inverse temperature β = 128t^{-1} and density of exactly one particle per site, showing superfluid and condensed fractions
- Pairing model for 10 neutrons in the pf+sdg shell with constant pairing interaction G = 0.286, showing Jz-projected internal energies as a function of temperature
- Picket fence model at half filling with pairing interaction strength G = 0.224d for system sizes of 44, 86, 174 and 300 levels, showing canonical pairing gap Δ_can as a function of temperature in units of the bulk gap Δ

## Context

This work extends the continuous-time loop algorithm of Beard and Wiese (1996) and the worm algorithm of Prokof'ev, Svistunov, and Tupitsyn (1998) by introducing particle-number-conserving updates, overcoming the limitation that previous loop algorithms sampled the grand canonical ensemble. The method enables exact finite-temperature results for systems where particle-number conservation is crucial, such as the nuclear shell model (Koonin, Dean, and Langanke, 1997; Rombouts, Heyde, and Jachowicz, 1998) and ultrasmall superconducting grains (Dukelsky and Sierra, 1999; Delfino, 2001), and addresses the open problem of extending exact ground-state calculations for the picket fence model to finite temperatures.
