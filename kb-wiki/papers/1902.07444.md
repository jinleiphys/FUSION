# Nuclear states and spectra in holographic QCD

> A new method to study nuclear physics via holographic QCD is proposed. Multiple baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto background are described by a matrix model which is a low energy effective theory of D-branes of the baryon vertices. We study the quantum mechanics of the matrix model and calculate the eigenstates of the Hamiltonian. The obtained states are found to coincide with known nuclear and baryonic states, and have appropriate statistics and charges. Calculated spectra of the baryon/nucleus for small baryon numbers show good agreement with experimental data. For hyperons, the Gell-Mann--Okubo formula is approximately derived. Baryon resonances up to spin $5/2$ and isospin $5/2$ and dibaryon spectra are obtained and compared with experimental data. The model partially explains even the magic numbers of light nuclei, $N=2,8$ and $20$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a matrix model derived from holographic QCD (the Sakai-Sugimoto model) can reproduce nuclear spectra, baryon resonances, and even magic numbers of light nuclei, but only under the working hypothesis that the commutator-squared potential can be approximated by a harmonic potential and treated perturbatively, with the effective mass of the adjoint scalar fields treated as a free parameter determined by experimental data.

## Method

The authors start from the nuclear matrix model, a U(A) matrix quantum mechanics derived from D4-branes (baryon vertices) in the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD background. They separate the Hamiltonian into an unperturbed part H0 with harmonic oscillator potentials for the adjoint scalar fields X^I and the fundamental scalar fields w (representing quarks), and a perturbation V containing the original commutator-squared and quartic interactions. Physical states are required to satisfy a U(A) charge constraint Q_{U(A)} - N_c I_A = 0, which forces the states to be SU(A) singlets with exactly N_c A excitations of the w fields. For baryon number A ≤ 2N_f with N_f=2, the physical ground states are constructed from antisymmetric combinations of w creation operators only, and the perturbation V is diagonalized in the space of these degenerate zero-th order states.

## Key numbers

* The mass parameter M (for the adjoint scalar X^I) is related to the Kaluza-Klein mass M_KK as M = (1/√6) M_KK, and the coupling λ = λ_QCD M_KK^3 / (54π N_c)
* The zero-th order ground state energy for A baryons is E_0 = N_c A m_w, where m_w is the mass of the w fields
* The model reproduces baryon resonances up to spin 5/2 and isospin 5/2
* The model partially explains the magic numbers N = 2, 8, and 20 for light nuclei

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matrix model approach of Hashimoto (2010) by performing a new quantization scheme that treats the potential as a perturbation rather than solving the ADHM equation at the classical level, allowing access to states beyond the soliton limit. It builds on the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model (2004, 2005) and previous studies of baryons as instantons in that framework, including work on baryon static properties by Hong (2007) and Hata (2007, 2008), nucleon-nucleon potentials by Hashimoto (2009) and Kim (2009), and the holographic deuteron by Kim (2009) and Pahlavani (2010, 2014). The new approach enables calculation of baryon resonances that cannot be obtained from the Skyrme model and provides a connection to the nuclear shell model and quark model pictures.
