# Role of mesonic fluctuations in the Polyakov loop extended quark-meson model at imaginary chemical potential

> We explore the thermodynamics and phase structure of the Polyakov loop-extended two flavor chiral quark--meson (PQM) model beyond the mean-field approximation at imaginary chemical potential. Our approach is based on the functional renormalization group (FRG) method. At finite temperature and imaginary chemical potential, we solve the renormalization group flow equation for a scale-dependent thermodynamic potential in the presence of the gluonic background field. We determine behavior of order parameters of the PQM model in the FRG approach and compute the phase diagram. We compare our FRG results with that obtained in the mean-field approximation at imaginary chemical potential.

## Key claim

Mesonic fluctuations, included via the functional renormalization group (FRG) method, substantially modify the thermodynamics and phase structure of the Polyakov loop-extended quark-meson (PQM) model at imaginary chemical potential compared to the mean-field approximation, specifically by smoothing the chiral transition and altering the curvature of the chiral critical line, though the order of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint remains unchanged.

## Method

The authors solved the FRG flow equation for a scale-dependent thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and imaginary chemical potential (μ = iθT) in the presence of a gluonic background field, treating the Polyakov loop as a mean-field background while accounting for fluctuations of the meson and quark fields. They compared these FRG results with those obtained in the mean-field approximation, where mesonic fluctuations are neglected and meson fields are replaced by their classical expectation values. The model parameters were fixed to reproduce vacuum properties: pion mass mπ = 138 MeV, pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV, sigma mass mσ = 600 MeV, and constituent quark mass mq = 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 138 MeV, pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV, sigma mass mσ = 600 MeV, constituent quark mass mq = 300 MeV (vacuum input parameters)
- Initial cutoff for FRG flow: Λ = 1.2 GeV
- Polyakov loop potential parameters: a0 = 6.75, a1 = -1.95, a2 = 2.625, a3 = -7.44, b3 = 0.75, b4 = 7.5, with T0 = 270 MeV
- At θ = π/3, the RW endpoint is located at T_RW/T_pc = 1.39 in the FRG approach and at T_RW/T_pc = 1.22 in the mean-field approximation
- The chiral critical line meets the first-order RW transition at T_ce/T_pc = 1.47 with fluctuations (FRG) and at T_ce/T_pc = 1.30 in the mean-field approximation
- The peak value of -d(σ/fπ)/dT at vanishing chemical potential in FRG calculations is almost half of the mean-field counterpart

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the PQM model at real chemical potential (Skokov 2010) and the PNJL model at imaginary chemical potential (Morita 2011) by incorporating mesonic fluctuations via the FRG method, going beyond the mean-field approximation used in those earlier works. The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice QCD calculations at imaginary chemical potential (de Forcrand 2007, D'Elia and Lombardo) and provide a framework for extrapolating the phase boundary from imaginary to real chemical potential, while also demonstrating that fluctuations must be included when using effective chiral models to quantify lattice results.
