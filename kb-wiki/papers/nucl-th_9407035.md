# An updated analysis of NN elastic scattering data to 1.6 GeV

> An energy-dependent and set of single-energy partial-wave analyses of $NN$ elastic scattering data have been completed. The fit to 1.6~GeV has been supplemented with a low-energy analysis to 400 MeV. Using the low-energy fit, we study the sensitivity of our analysis to the choice of $πNN$ coupling constant. We also comment on the possibility of fitting $np$ data alone. These results are compared with those found in the recent Nijmegen analyses. (Figures may be obtained from the authors upon request.)

## Key claim

The authors present updated energy-dependent and single-energy partial-wave analyses of nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering data up to 1.6 GeV, but the np component is truncated at 1.3 GeV due to a lack of data between 1.3 and 1.6 GeV; the low-energy fit to 400 MeV shows sensitivity to the piNN coupling constant but the authors caution that this is a consistency check rather than a reliable determination.

## Method

Energy-dependent partial-wave analyses were performed using 77 isovector and 44 isoscalar parameters for the full fit to 1.6 GeV (SM94), and a separate low-energy fit to 400 MeV (VZ40) used 26 I=1 and 27 I=0 parameters. Single-energy fits of pp and np data were obtained up to 1.25 GeV, with additional pp-only analyses at 1.3 and 1.6 GeV. The sensitivity to the piNN coupling constant was probed by mapping chi-squared versus g^2/4pi for the VZ40 solution, and separate fits to pp data alone and np data alone were performed to test charge independence.

## Key numbers

- Total database: 12,838 pp data and 10,918 np data in the SM94 solution
- SM94 chi-squared/datum: 22371/12838 for pp data, 17516/10918 for np data
- VZ40 (0-400 MeV) chi-squared/datum: 3098/2170 for pp data, 4595/3367 for np data
- The np database increased by a factor of 1.3 since 1991
- Pruning high-chi-squared data (removing 74 points with chi-squared > 9) decreased chi-squared by about 1000, with only a 45 reduction after re-searching
- Separate fit to np data alone (removing all pp data) changed chi-squared from 4595 to 4422 for the np data
- Separate fit to pp data alone (removing np data) changed chi-squared from 3098 to 3083 for the pp data
- The chosen value of g^2/4pi was 13.7

## Context

This work updates the previous partial-wave analyses of Arndt, Roper, Workman, and McNaughton (1992) by incorporating a substantial amount of new np data from LAMPF and Saclay produced since 1991. The results are compared with the recent Nijmegen analyses of Stoks, Klomp, Rentmeester, and de Swart (1993), showing good agreement at low energies except in the epsilon_1 mixing parameter. The analysis enables future comparisons with upcoming TUNL measurements of the np P parameter at 8 and 12 MeV, new PSI measurements of RT and DT between 260 and 550 MeV, and new Indiana data of P and AYY at 180 MeV.
