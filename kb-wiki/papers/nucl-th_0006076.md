# A simple model illustrating the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects

> We consider a simple model and make use of field transformations in Lagrangian field theories to illustrate the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and related processes.

# A simple model illustrating the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects

## Key claim

Off-shell effects in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and related processes cannot be unambiguously extracted from S-matrix elements because they are both model-dependent and representation-dependent; any apparent off-shell contribution in one Lagrangian can be exactly cancelled by a contact term arising from a field transformation to an equivalent Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors construct a toy model Lagrangian for proton-neutron bremsstrahlung with a simplified strong interaction (scalar coupling) and standard electromagnetic couplings including the anomalous magnetic moment. They then perform a field transformation on the proton field, parameterized by real constants, generating a class of equivalent Lagrangians that produce different off-shell behavior in both the nucleon-nucleon amplitude and the photon-nucleon vertex, and demonstrate that the total S-matrix amplitude remains unchanged due to cancellations between off-shell terms and newly generated contact terms.

## Key numbers

- The transformation parameter \(\tilde{a}\) (real constant) generates off-shell dependence in the proton legs of the \(pn\) amplitude proportional to \(i \tilde{a}g [(\slsh{p}_3 -m) + (\slsh{p}_1 -m)] \overline{u}_n(p_4)u_n(p_2)\), which is exactly cancelled by a \(pn\gamma\) contact term \(-2 i \tilde{a}eg \overline{u}_p(p_3) \left( \slsh{\epsilon} -\frac{i \kappa}{2m} \sigma_{\mu\nu} \epsilon^\mu k^\nu \right) u_p(p_1) \overline{u}_n(p_4)u_n(p_2)\).
- The cancellation holds for both the charge part (enforced by gauge invariance) and the magnetic part (not fixed by gauge invariance).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous discussions of field transformations in chiral perturbation theory for pions, specifically Compton scattering (Scherer, 1995) and pion-pion bremsstrahlung (Fearing, 1998), to a spin-one-half system, removing any uncertainty that earlier results depended on the simplicity of spin-zero processes. The result is a concrete illustration of the equivalence theorem of Lagrangian field theory, and a similar observation for the charge part was made by Kaloshin (1999) for real Compton scattering off a charged pion. The work implies that attempts to extract off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitudes from bremsstrahlung data, as pursued in earlier theoretical work on nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, cannot yield unique, representation-independent information.
