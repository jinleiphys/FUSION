# Nonrelativistic inverse square potential, scale anomaly, and complex extension

> The old problem of a singular, inverse square potential in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics is treated employing a field-theoretic, functional renormalization method. An emergent contact coupling flows to a fixed point or develops a limit cycle depending on the discriminant of its quadratic beta function. We analyze the fixed points in both conformal and non-conformal phases and perform a natural extension of the renormalization group analysis to complex values of the contact coupling. Physical interpretation and motivation for this extension is the presence of an inelastic scattering channel in two-body collisions. We present a geometric description of the complex generalization by considering renormalization group flows on the Riemann sphere. Finally, using bosonization, we find an analytical solution of the extended renormalization group flow equations, constituting the main result of our work.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the renormalization group flow of the contact coupling in the inverse square potential problem can be extended to complex values, where the real axis acts as a separatrix between two complex fixed points; this complex extension is physically motivated by the presence of an inelastic scattering channel in two-body collisions.

## Method

The authors use a functional renormalization group (Wetterich equation) with a sharp cutoff regulator to derive the flow equation for a momentum-independent (pointlike) contact coupling \(\lambda_{\psi}\) in a nonrelativistic field theory of bosons interacting via the long-range inverse square potential \(V(r)=-\kappa/r^2\) in \(d\) spatial dimensions. They analyze the resulting quadratic beta function for the dimensionless rescaled coupling \(\lambda_{\psi R}\), classify the fixed point structure by the discriminant \(D\) of the beta function, and then extend the flow equations to complex values of the coupling. Using bosonization, they obtain an analytical solution of the complexified flow equations.

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling strength separating conformal and non-conformal phases: \(\kappa_{cr} = (d-2)^2/4\) (Eq. 5), derived from the condition \(D=0\) for the discriminant of the quadratic beta function.
* For the sharp cutoff in general dimension \(d\), the discriminant of the beta function is \(D = -4\kappa + (d-2)^2\).
* The period of the periodic solution for the real coupling in the \(D<0\) regime is \(T = 2\pi/\sqrt{-D}\).
* The Efimov parameter \(s_0 \approx 1.0062\) appears in the one-dimensional radial Schrödinger equation with the inverse square potential that describes the three-body Efimov effect (Eq. 3).
* Near the critical coupling \(\kappa_{cr}\) in the non-conformal regime, the energy \(B\) of the lowest bound state vanishes as \(B \sim \exp\left(-\pi/\sqrt{\kappa-\kappa_{cr}}\right)\) (Eq. 5), analogous to the BKT transition.

## Context

This work treats the long-standing problem of the singular inverse square potential using a modern functional renormalization group approach, extending prior RG studies by Kolomeisky, Gupta, Beane (2000), Barford (2002), Bawin (2003), Ho, Braaten, Hammer (2005, 2008), and Kaplan. The complex extension of the RG flow is a new methodological development that regularizes the periodic divergences of the real coupling in the overcritical regime, replacing them with a tower of resonances in the imaginary part; this technique may be applicable to more technical few-body problems such as the Efimov effect.
