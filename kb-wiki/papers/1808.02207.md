# Strangeness and $Δ$ resonance in compact stars with relativistic-mean-field models

> We explore the effects of strangeness and $Δ$ resonance in baryonic matter and compact stars within the relativistic-mean-field (RMF) models. The covariant density functional PKDD is adopted for $N$-$N$ interaction, parameters fixed based on finite hypernuclei and neutron stars are taken for the hyperon-meson couplings, and the universal baryon-meson coupling scheme is adopted for the $Δ$-meson couplings. In light of the recent observations of GW170817 with the dimensionless combined tidal deformability $197 \leq \barΛ\leq 720$, we find it is essential to include the $Δ$ resonances in compact stars, and small $Δ$-$ρ$ coupling $g_{ρΔ}$ is favored if the mass $2.27{}_{-0.15}^{+0.17}\ M_\odot$ of PSR J2215+5135 is confirmed.

## Key claim
The inclusion of both hyperons and Delta resonances is essential for compact-star models to simultaneously satisfy the GW170817 tidal-deformability constraint (197 ≤ Λ̄ ≤ 720) and the high mass of PSR J2215+5135 (2.27^{+0.17}_{-0.15} M⊙), but only if the Delta-rho coupling g_{ρΔ} is small (e.g., zero) and the Delta mass is low enough (e.g., 1112 MeV).

## Method
The authors use the relativistic-mean-field (RMF) model with the covariant density functional PKDD for nucleon-nucleon interactions. Hyperon-meson couplings are fixed from previous studies of hypernuclei and neutron stars, while Delta-meson couplings follow the universal baryon-meson coupling scheme, with g_{ρΔ} set to either g_{ρN} or zero. The equation of state (EoS) for neutron-star matter is computed under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation is solved to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability for various Delta masses (1112, 1232, and 1352 MeV).

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless combined tidal deformability from GW170817 is constrained to 197 ≤ Λ̄ ≤ 720.
- The mass of PSR J2215+5135 is 2.27^{+0.17}_{-0.15} M⊙.
- For the PKDD functional, the saturation density n₀ = 0.149552 fm⁻³, saturation energy E₀ = -16.267 MeV, incompressibility K = 262.181 MeV, and symmetry energy E_sym = 36.790 MeV.
- With m_Δ = 1112 MeV and g_{ρΔ} = 0, a first-order phase transition occurs in the density range n = 0.083–0.17 fm⁻³, yielding a smallest radius of R = 11.3 km for a 1.4 M⊙ compact star.
- The onset density of Σ⁻ is n = 0.27 fm⁻³; the onset density of Λ⁰ is n = 0.39 fm⁻³ without Σ⁻ and n = 0.46 fm⁻³ with Σ⁻ included.
- For m_Δ = 1112 MeV and g_{ρΔ} = 0, the onset density of Λ⁰ increases to n = 0.74 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends previous RMF studies of hyperonic matter (e.g., Sun 2016, Sun 2018, Liu 2018) and Delta-resonance effects (e.g., Drago 2014, Cai 2015, Zhu 2016) by simultaneously confronting the GW170817 tidal-deformability constraint and the high mass of PSR J2215+5135. It shows that including only hyperons or only Delta resonances is insufficient; both are needed, with a small g_{ρΔ} to satisfy the mass constraint. The results enable future work to refine Delta-meson couplings using microscopic many-body calculations and to test the predicted first-order phase transition with future gravitational-wave observations.
