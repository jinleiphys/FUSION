## Method
The authors use the relativistic-mean-field (RMF) model with the covariant density functional PKDD for nucleon-nucleon interactions. Hyperon-meson couplings are fixed from previous studies of hypernuclei and neutron stars, while Delta-meson couplings follow the universal baryon-meson coupling scheme, with g_{ПЃО”} set to either g_{ПЃN} or zero. The equation of state (EoS) for neutron-star matter is computed under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation is solved to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability for various Delta masses (1112, 1232, and 1352 MeV).

## Context
This work extends previous RMF studies of hyperonic matter (e.g., Sun 2016, Sun 2018, Liu 2018) and Delta-resonance effects (e.g., Drago 2014, Cai 2015, Zhu 2016) by simultaneously confronting the GW170817 tidal-deformability constraint and the high mass of PSR J2215+5135. It shows that including only hyperons or only Delta resonances is insufficient; both are needed, with a small g_{ПЃО”} to satisfy the mass constraint. The results enable future work to refine Delta-meson couplings using microscopic many-body calculations and to test the predicted first-order phase transition with future gravitational-wave observations.
