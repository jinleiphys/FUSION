# Baryons from the lattice: past, present and future

> I review recent lattice results on the spectrum and structure of baryons. Limitations due to the quenched approximation and un-physically heavy up and down quarks are discussed, and interfaces between first principles studies of QCD (or approximations to it) and ``model building'' are highlighted.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD results for baryon properties are still dominated by two major systematic uncertainties: the quenched approximation (neglecting sea-quark polarisation effects) and unphysically heavy up and down quark masses, which require long chiral extrapolations from \(m_{\pi}^2 > 0.25\,m_{\rho}^2\) to the physical limit \(m_{\pi}^2 \approx 0.03\,m_{\rho}^2\). The abstract highlights "limitations" but does not quantify that quenched predictions for light hadron masses differ from experiment by typically less than 10 %, while wave-function-sensitive quantities like decay constants can change by as much as 30 %.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations of baryon spectra and structure, focusing on results obtained with the quenched approximation and with two flavours of dynamical sea quarks. Key techniques include the use of improved actions (Wilson-Clover, \(D_{234}\), FLIC), domain-wall and overlap (Ginsparg-Wilson) fermions for better chiral properties, and chiral extrapolation models (e.g. the Adelaide cloudy bag model) to connect lattice data at heavy pion masses to the physical limit. The review covers spectrum calculations for nucleon resonances, electromagnetic \(N \to \Delta\) transition form factors, charge and matter distributions, three-body forces, and hyperon spin content.

## Key numbers

- Quenched light hadron masses differ from experiment by typically less than 10 % (Section 2).
- The quenched approximation reduces computational effort by a factor of approximately \(10^3\) (Section 2).
- The Roper \(N'(1440)\) mass data from the Kentucky-Washington group (Ginsparg-Wilson fermions) show a downward bend as the pion mass drops below about 550 MeV, with the lightest pion mass reaching \(m_{\pi} > 180\) MeV (Section 4).
- For the \(N \to \Delta\) transition at \(q^2 \approx 0.53\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio \(R_{EM} = -0.03(1)\) and \(R_{SM} = -0.03(2)\) from two-flavour dynamical simulations, compared with experimental values \(R_{EM} \approx -0.02\) and \(R_{SM} \approx -0.07\); the quenched reference gives \(R_{EM} = -0.009(8)\) (Section 5).
- The quenched proton charge radius from direct lattice calculation is \(r_p = 0.59(4)\) fm after polynomial extrapolation, versus the experimental value \(r_p \approx 0.81\) fm from dipole fits (Section 5).
- With two flavours of sea quarks, QCDSF finds \(r_p \approx 0.7\) fm (Section 5).
- The axial charge \(g_A = \Delta u_p - \Delta d_p\) from lattice data falls short of the experimental value \(g_A \approx 1.26\) by 10–20 % (Section 5).
- Lattice results for \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin fractions give \(\Delta u_{\Lambda} = \Delta d_{\Lambda} = -0.02(4)\) in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV (Section 5).
- Combining experimental proton helicity data with lattice \(SU(3)_F\) symmetry yields predictions \(\Delta u_{\Lambda} = \Delta d_{\Lambda} = -0.17(3)\) and \(\Delta s_{\Lambda} = 0.63(3)\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV (Section 5).

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice work on baryon spectroscopy (e.g. CP-PACS, UKQCD, LHPC/SESAM, QCDSF) and structure (e.g. QCDSF form factors) by emphasising the impact of the quenched approximation and heavy quark masses on results. It highlights the recent breakthrough by the Kentucky-Washington group (Lee et al., 2002) using Ginsparg-Wilson fermions to reach pion masses as low as 180 MeV, which reveals the chiral behaviour of the Roper resonance. The paper enables future unquenched simulations (expected by early 2004) to test model predictions (e.g. cloudy bag model, NJL, Bethe-Salpeter approaches) and to provide first-principles constraints on baryon structure, including deformation and three-body forces.
