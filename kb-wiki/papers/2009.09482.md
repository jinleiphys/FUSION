# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Deconfinement in the Contact Interaction Model of Quarks with a Parallel Electric and Magnetic Fields

> We study the impact of steady, homogeneous, and external parallel electric and magnetic field strength ($eE\parallel eB$), on the chiral symmetry breaking-restoration and confinement-deconfinement phase transitions. We also sketch the phase diagram of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature $T$ and in the presence of background fields. Our unified formalism for this study is based on the Schwinger-Dyson equations, symmetry preserving vector-vector contact interaction model of quarks, and the proper time regularization scheme. At $T=0$, in the purely magnetic case ($eE\rightarrow 0$), we observe the well known magnetic catalysis effect. On the other hand, in the pure electric field background ($eB\rightarrow 0$), the electric field tends to restore the chiral symmetry and deconfinement above the pseudo-critical electric field $eE^{χ, C}_c$. In the presence of both $eE$ and $eB$: we find the magnetic catalysis effect in the particular region where $eB$ dominates over $eE$, whereas, we observe the chiral inhibition (or electric chiral rotation) effect, when $eE$ stand over $eB$. At finite $T$, in the pure electric field case, the phenomenon of inverse electric catalysis appears to exist in our model. On the other hand for pure magnetic field background, we notice the magnetic catalysis effect in the mean-field approximation and inverse magnetic catalysis with $eB$-dependent coupling. The combined effect of both $eE$ and $eB$ on the pseudo-critical $T^{χ, C}_c$ yields the inverse electromagnetic catalysis, with and without $eB-$dependent effective coupling of the model. Our findings are satisfactory in agreement with already predicted results by lattice simulations and other reliable effective models of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the contact interaction model of quarks, the combined effect of parallel electric and magnetic fields yields inverse electromagnetic catalysis (reduction of the pseudo-critical temperature) at finite temperature, both with and without a magnetic-field-dependent effective coupling, and that the pseudo-critical electric field for chiral restoration decreases with increasing magnetic field until a critical end point at (eB_p = 0.3 GeV², eE_p = 0.33 GeV²) where the transition changes from crossover to first order.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equations in rainbow-ladder truncation using a symmetry-preserving vector-vector contact interaction model, with the Schwinger proper time regularization scheme that implements confinement via an infrared cutoff. The gap equation is solved numerically at zero and finite temperature in the presence of uniform, parallel electric and magnetic fields (eE ∥ eB) using an average common quark charge, and the quark-antiquark condensate and confinement length scale are computed as order parameters for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass at zero field: M_f = 367 MeV (with bare quark mass m_f = 7 MeV)
- Quark-antiquark condensate at zero field: ⟨ūu⟩^{1/3} = ⟨dd̄⟩^{1/3} = -243 MeV
- Pseudo-critical electric field for chiral restoration in pure electric case at T=0: eE^{χ,C}_c ≈ 0.348 GeV²
- Pseudo-critical magnetic field for first-order transition: eB_c = 0.3 GeV²
- Pseudo-critical electric field for first-order transition: eE_c = 0.33 GeV²
- Critical end point coordinates: (eB_p = 0.3 GeV², eE_p = 0.33 GeV²)
- Pseudo-critical temperature at zero field: T^{χ,C}_c ≈ 0.22 GeV (from inflection points of thermal gradients)
- Infrared cutoff parameter: τ_ir = (240 MeV)^{-1}
- Ultraviolet cutoff parameter: τ_uv = (905 MeV)^{-1}
- Effective coupling: α_eff(0) = 5.739 × 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement under electromagnetic fields using effective QCD models, particularly the contact interaction model previously applied to pure magnetic fields by Ahmad (2016). The results for magnetic catalysis at zero temperature and inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperature with eB-dependent coupling agree with lattice QCD simulations by Bali (2012, 2013) and with other effective model calculations by Ruggieri (2016), Wang (2017), and Tavares (2018, 2019). The paper enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram under realistic electromagnetic field configurations relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as to astrophysical objects such as neutron stars and magnetars.
