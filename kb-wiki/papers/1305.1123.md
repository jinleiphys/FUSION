# Phonon coupling effects in magnetic moments of magic and semi-magic nuclei

> Phonon coupling (PC) corrections to magnetic moments of odd neighbors of magic and semi-magic nuclei are analyzed within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. The perturbation theory in g_L^2 is used where g_L is the phonon-particle coupling vertex. A model is developed with separating non-regular PC contributions, the rest is supposed to be regular and included into the standard TFFS parameters. An ansatz is proposed to take into account the so-called tadpole term which ensures the total angular momentum conservation with g_L^2 accuracy. An approximate method is suggested to take into account higher order terms in g_L^2. Calculations are carried out for four odd-proton chains, the odd Tl, Bi, In and Sb ones. Different PC corrections strongly cancel each other. In the result, the total PC correction to the magnetic moment in magic nuclei is, as a rule, negligible. In non-magic nuclei considered it is noticeable and, with only one exception, negative. On average it is of the order of -(0.1 - 0.5) μ_N and improves the agreement of the theory with the data. Simultaneously we calculated the gyromagnetic ratio g_L^{ph} of all low-lying phonons in 208Pb. For the 3^-_1 state it is rather close to the Bohr-Mottelson model prediction whereas for other L-phonons, two 5^- and six positive parity states, the difference from the Bohr-Mottelson values is significant.

## Key claim

Phonon coupling (PC) corrections to magnetic moments of odd neighbors of magic and semi-magic nuclei are analyzed within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al., using perturbation theory in \(g_L^2\) where \(g_L\) is the phonon-particle coupling vertex. The authors develop a model that separates only the non-regular PC contributions, assuming the rest are regular and included in the standard TFFS parameters, and find that the total PC correction in magic nuclei is negligible while in non-magic nuclei it is noticeable and, with only one exception, negative.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent TFFS based on the Fayans Energy Density Functional (DF3-a version) to calculate magnetic moments of odd neighbors of magic and semi-magic nuclei. They employed perturbation theory in \(g_L^2\) and developed a model that separates non-regular PC contributions (end corrections, triangle diagrams, and phonon magnetic moment contributions) from regular ones, with an ansatz to account for the tadpole term ensuring total angular momentum conservation. Calculations were carried out for four odd-proton chains: odd Tl, Bi, In, and Sb nuclei, and the gyromagnetic ratios \(g_L^{\rm ph}\) of low-lying phonons in \(^{208}\)Pb were also computed.

## Key numbers

- The total PC correction to magnetic moments in non-magic nuclei is on average of the order of \(-(0.1 \div 0.5)\;\mu_N\).
- For the \(3^-_1\) state in \(^{208}\)Pb, the gyromagnetic ratio \(g_L^{\rm ph}\) is rather close to the Bohr-Mottelson model prediction (\(g^{\rm ph}_{L,\rm BM}=Z/A\)).
- For other \(L\)-phonons in \(^{208}\)Pb (two \(5^-\) and six positive parity states), the difference from the Bohr-Mottelson values is significant.
- The accuracy of the single-quasiparticle approximation for magnetic moments is \(\delta \mu \simeq 0.1\div0.2\;\)n.m. on average, with "bad" cases showing \(\delta \mu \simeq 0.5\;\)n.m.
- The normalization factor \(C_0 = 300\;\)MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) and the parameter \(r_0 = 0.4\;\)fm determine the momentum dependence of the spin-dependent LM amplitude.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent TFFS calculations of magnetic moments (Saperstein et al., 2009; 2010) and quadrupole moments (Saperstein et al., 2010; 2011) that were limited to the single-quasiparticle approximation, where PC effects were only estimated. It builds on the Nuclear Field Theory approach of Hamamoto (1974) and the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model of Soloviev, and enables more accurate predictions for magnetic moments of odd spherical nuclei, particularly for semi-magic nuclei where PC corrections are non-negligible. The method also provides a framework for calculating phonon gyromagnetic ratios in magic nuclei, which can be compared with the Bohr-Mottelson model.
