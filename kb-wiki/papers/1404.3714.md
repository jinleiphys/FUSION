# Probing the early-time dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions with electromagnetic radiation

> Using 3+1D viscous relativistic fluid dynamics, we show that electromagnetic probes are sensitive to the initial conditions and to the out-of-equilibrium features of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Within the same approach, we find that hadronic observables show a much lesser sensitivity to these aspects. We conclude that electromagnetic observables allow access to dynamical regions that are beyond the reach of soft hadronic probes.

## Key claim
Electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) are sensitive to the initial conditions and out-of-equilibrium features of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, while hadronic observables show much less sensitivity to these aspects. This sensitivity depends on the specific electromagnetic probe and the parameter being varied, with photons showing greater sensitivity to the shear relaxation time than dileptons.

## Method
The authors used the 3+1D viscous relativistic fluid dynamics code MUSIC with optical Glauber initial conditions tuned to RHIC hadronic observables. They varied the shear relaxation time tau_pi (with coefficient b_pi = 3, 5, and 10) and the initial shear-stress tensor pi^{mu nu}_0 (with values c = 0 and 2 times the Navier-Stokes value) while keeping eta/s fixed at 1/(4 pi). Thermal electromagnetic production rates were extended to include viscous corrections through asymmetric modifications to the distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio fixed at eta/s = 1/(4 pi)
- Shear relaxation time parameter b_pi varied as 3, 5, and 10, where tau_pi = b_pi * [eta/(epsilon + P)]
- Initial shear-stress tensor set to pi^{mu nu}_0 = c * 2 * eta * sigma^{mu nu} with c = 0 and c = 2
- Varying the shear relaxation time leads to an increase of thermal photon elliptic flow of approximately 30% at p_T = 3 GeV for Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV in the 20-40% centrality class

## Context
This work extends the study of viscous hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, specifically investigating how electromagnetic probes can access early-time dynamics that are inaccessible to hadronic probes. The authors build on prior work using Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1976, Israel 1979) and the MUSIC hydrodynamic simulation (Schenke 2010). The results suggest that electromagnetic observables could lead to a recalibration of the parameters used in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that an upcoming study will re-examine these effects with event-by-event fluid dynamics and IP-Glasma initial states (Gale 2012).
