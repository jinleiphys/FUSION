# Photon-tagged heavy meson production in high energy nuclear collisions

> We study the photon-triggered light and heavy meson production in both p+p and A+A collisions. We find that a parton energy loss approach that successfully describes inclusive hadron attenuation in nucleus-nucleus reactions at RHIC can simultaneously describe well the experimentally determined photon-triggered light hadron fragmentation functions. Using the same framework, we generalize our formalism to study photon-triggered heavy meson production. We find that the nuclear modification of photon-tagged heavy meson fragmentation functions in A+A collision is very different from that of the photon-tagged light hadron case. While photon-triggered light hadron fragmentation functions in A+A collisions are suppressed relative to p+p, photon-triggered heavy meson fragmentation functions can be either enhanced or suppressed, depending on the specific kinematic region. The anticipated smaller energy loss for $b$-quarks manifests itself as a flatter photon-triggered $B$-meson fragmentation function compared to that for the $D$-meson case. We make detailed predictions for both RHIC and LHC energies. We conclude that a comprehensive comparative study of both photon-tagged light and heavy meson production can provide new insights in the details of the jet quenching mechanism.

# Photon-tagged heavy meson production in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The nuclear modification of photon-tagged heavy meson fragmentation functions in A+A collisions can be either enhanced or suppressed depending on the kinematic region, in contrast to the universal suppression seen for light hadrons. This behavior arises from the interplay between the shape of heavy quark fragmentation functions and the magnitude of parton energy loss, which is smaller for heavier quarks.

## Method
The authors employed the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) parton energy loss formalism, using medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung distributions and energy loss probabilities for light partons and heavy quarks obtained in prior work. For heavy meson production they used a fixed-flavor-number scheme (FFNS) with explicit heavy quark masses (1.3 GeV for charm, 4.5 GeV for bottom), computing the 2-to-3 partonic processes qqbar -> Q Qbar gamma and gg -> Q Qbar gamma. The energy loss was implemented as an effective replacement of fragmentation functions via a convolution with the probability distribution P(epsilon) for fractional energy loss, averaged over jet production points in an optical Glauber model with Bjorken expanding medium.

- A 25% uncertainty in the magnitude of energy loss was included in LHC predictions

## Context
This work extends the GLV parton energy loss formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2007) from inclusive hadron suppression to photon-tagged observables, building on earlier theoretical studies of gamma-hadron correlations by Wang (1996), Zhang (2009), Qin (2009), and Renk (2009). The authors address the long-standing puzzle of non-photonic electron suppression at RHIC (Dokshitzer 2001, Wicks 2005), where heavy quark energy loss alone appeared insufficient to explain the data. The predictions enable direct experimental tests at both RHIC and LHC of how the quark mass dependence of medium-induced energy loss manifests in gamma-tagged D and B meson production, providing a more differential probe of jet quenching than inclusive hadron measurements.
