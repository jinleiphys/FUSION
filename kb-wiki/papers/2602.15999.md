# Tensor Polarizability of the Nucleus and Angular Mixing in Muonic Deuterium

> We investigate the effects of the tensor polarizability of a nucleus on the bound-state energy levels, and obtain a general formula for the contribution of the tensor polarizability to the energy levels in two-body bound systems. In particular, it is demonstrated that the tensor polarizability leads to mixing between states with different orbital angular momenta. The effect of tensor polarizability is evaluated for the hyperfine-structure components of P states and for the mixing of S and D states in muonic deuterium.

# Tensor Polarizability of the Nucleus and Angular Mixing in Muonic Deuterium

## Key claim

The tensor polarizability of a nucleus with spin greater than 1/2 leads to mixing between bound states with different orbital angular momenta in two-body systems, including S-D mixing in muonic deuterium; however, the resulting energy shifts are numerically suppressed by roughly a factor of 1/100 relative to scalar polarizability effects and are not yet discernible in experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors derive a general formula for the energy shift due to nuclear tensor polarizability in two-body bound systems using angular momentum reduction with the coupling scheme L + S_muon = J, then J + S_nucleus = F. They evaluate the angular factors G involving 6j and 3j symbols and compute radial matrix elements of 1/r^4 using associated Laguerre polynomials, then apply the formalism to the 2P and 3S/3D manifolds of muonic deuterium using the deuteron tensor polarizability constant from Friar and Payne (2005).

## Key numbers

- Scalar polarizability of the deuteron: alpha_E = 0.6330(13) fm^3
- Tensor polarizability of the deuteron: tau_N = 0.0317(3) fm^3
- Dimensionless scalar polarizability constant: tilde_alpha_E = 543.6(1.1)
- Dimensionless tensor polarizability constant: tilde_tau_N = 27.22(26)
- Scalar polarizability contribution to the 2P energy level: E_alpha(2P) = -3.555(8) micro-eV
- Tensor polarizability correction for 2P_{3/2}^{F=5/2} state: E_tau = 0.0356(4) micro-eV
- Tensor polarizability off-diagonal matrix element for 3S_{1/2}^{F=1/2} and 3D_{3/2}^{F=1/2}: 0.0105(1) micro-eV
- Tensor polarizability correction for 3D_{5/2}^{F=7/2} state: E_tau = 0.000670(7) micro-eV

## Context

This work extends the treatment of nuclear polarizability in muonic atoms beyond the scalar (electric dipole) polarizability considered by Friar and Payne (2005) and Pachucki (2011), who showed that for S states the effect is proportional to the probability density at the origin while for non-S states it enters via 1/r^4 matrix elements. The tensor polarizability introduces angular mixing that is absent in the scalar-only treatment, including S-D mixing first appearing at n=3. The results are relevant for interpreting 2S-2P interval measurements in muonic deuterium by Pohl et al. (2016), where current experimental uncertainties are roughly 4 micro-eV. The authors suggest a possible experimental detection scheme analogous to parity-violation measurements in cesium (Bouchiat and Bouchiat 1974; Wood et al. 1997), using interference between tensor-polarizability-induced and externally-induced D-state admixtures in 3S to 4F transitions.
