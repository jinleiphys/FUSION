# $Λ-$hyperon interaction with nucleons

> We study the interaction of $Λ-$hyperon with proton and neutron inside a nucleus within the framework of relativistic mean field formalism. The single particle energy levels for some of the specific proton and neutron orbits are analyzed with the addition of $Λ-$successively. We found that the interaction of $Λ$ with neutron is more stronger than proton.

## Key claim

The authors find that the interaction of a Λ-hyperon with a neutron is more attractive than with a proton, confirming a charge symmetry breaking effect expected from the ΛN-ΣN coupling mechanism, but the analysis is limited to specific single-particle orbitals in medium and superheavy hypernuclei within a relativistic mean field model.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* parameter set, including additional strange scalar (σ*) and vector (φ) mesons to simulate ΛΛ interactions. They calculate single-particle energy levels for selected proton and neutron orbitals in ⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb, and ²⁹⁸114 nuclei, adding Λ-hyperons successively (up to 14 in ⁴⁸Ca, 60 in ²⁰⁸Pb, and 80 in ²⁹⁸114) while replacing neutrons to maintain charge neutrality. The relative coupling constants for Λ-meson interactions are fixed at R_σ = 0.621, R_ω = 2/3, R_σ* = 0.69, and R_φ = -√2/3.

## Key numbers

- Single-Λ binding energy for the s-state in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: 26.9 MeV (experimental value 26.3 ± 0.8 MeV)
- Single-Λ binding energy for the p-state in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: 23.0 MeV (experimental value 21.3 ± 0.7 MeV)
- Total binding energy of ⁴⁸Ca: 414.2 MeV; of ⁴⁸_ΛCa: 428.2 MeV
- Total binding energy of ²⁰⁸Pb: 1639.3 MeV; of ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: 1660.1 MeV
- Total binding energy of ²⁹⁸114: 2119.3 MeV; of ²⁹⁸_Λ114: 2168.6 MeV
- Root-mean-square radius of ⁴⁸Ca: 3.496 fm; of ⁴⁸_ΛCa: 3.454 fm
- Neutron mean potential depth for ⁴⁸Ca with one Λ: approximately -85 MeV
- The potential depth for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb increases with addition of up to 40 Λ's, then reverts with 60 Λ's
- The potential depth for ²⁹⁸_Λ114 increases with addition of up to 60 Λ's, then reduces with more Λ's

## Context

This work extends the study of Λ-nucleon interactions in hypernuclei using RMF theory, building on prior calculations by Schaffner (1994), Mares (1994), and others who established the formalism for multi-strange systems. The results confirm the charge symmetry breaking observed in mirror hypernuclei such as ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe, as discussed by Gibson (1988, 1995) and Dalitz (1964). The finding that Λn interaction is stronger than Λp is consistent with scattering data, and the study enables future work on high-density systems such as neutron stars or pure hyperonic matter, where clusters of baryon octet may exist.
