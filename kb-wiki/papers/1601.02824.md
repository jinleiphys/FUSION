# Holographic Nambu Jona-Lasinio Interactions

> NJL interactions are introduced into the D3/ probe D7 system using Witten's double trace operator prescription which includes the operator as a classical term in the effective potential. In the supersymmetric system they do not induce chiral symmetry breaking which we attribute to the flat effective potential with quark mass in the supersymmetric theory. If additional supersymmetry breaking is introduced then standard NJL behaviour is realized. In examples where chiral symmetry breaking is not preferred such as with a B field plus an IR cut off chiral condensation is triggered by the NJL interaction at a second order transition after a finite critical coupling. If the model already contains chiral symmetry breaking, for example in the B field case with no IR cut off, then the NJL interaction enhances the quark mass at all values of the NJL coupling. We also consider the system at finite temperature: the temperature discourages condensation but when combined with a magnetic field we find regions of parameter space where the NJL interaction triggers a first order chiral transition above a critical coupling.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) four-fermion interactions can be introduced into the D3/probe D7 holographic system using Witten's double trace operator prescription, but the standard NJL behavior of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking only occurs when an additional source of supersymmetry breaking is present; in the supersymmetric system the effective potential is flat with quark mass and the NJL interaction does not induce chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors introduce NJL interactions into the D3/probe D7 holographic system by adding a UV boundary term to the D7 brane action that implements Witten's double trace operator prescription, relating the quark condensate (parameter c) to the quark mass (parameter m) via the NJL coupling. They then solve the D7 brane equations of motion subject to the new UV boundary condition and an IR boundary condition (L'=0 or black hole horizon), computing the effective potential from the bulk action plus the UV boundary term to find the minimum that determines the dynamically generated quark mass. The analysis is performed for various deformations: a magnetic field B, an IR cut off, finite temperature, and combinations thereof.

## Key numbers

- The critical NJL coupling for the standard NJL model is g_c^2 = 4 pi^2, above which chiral symmetry breaking occurs.
- In the magnetic field plus IR cut off case (B=1, Lambda_IR=1, Lambda_UV=100), a second order chiral transition occurs at a finite critical value of g/Lambda_UV.
- In the magnetic field case with no IR cut off (B=1, Lambda_UV=100), the NJL interaction generates a non-zero quark mass for any non-zero NJL coupling, with the mass growing smoothly as g increases.
- At finite temperature with w_H=1 and B=1 (Lambda_UV=100), a first order chiral transition occurs at g/Lambda_UV approximately equal to 8.
- The meson-melting phase transition in the finite temperature case occurs at m=1.22 (in units of w_H).

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to chiral symmetry breaking by introducing NJL-type four-fermion interactions into the D3/probe D7 system, which was originally developed by Karch and Katz (2002) to model N=2 quark multiplets in N=4 super Yang-Mills theory. The double trace operator prescription follows Witten (2001). The paper builds on prior studies of magnetic field effects by Filev et al. (2007) and finite temperature effects by Babington et al. (2003) and Mateos et al. (2006). The results enable future investigations of extended technicolor-type interactions in holographic models of technicolor dynamics, such as those studied by Alho et al. (2013), and connect to similar four-fermion operator studies in holographic superconductors by Faulkner et al. (2010).
