# Electron Scattering Intensities and Patterson Functions of Skyrmions

> The scattering of electrons off nuclei is one of the best methods of probing nuclear structure. In this paper we focus on electron scattering off nuclei with spin and isospin zero within the Skyrme model. We consider two distinct methods and simplify our calculations by use of the Born approximation. The first method is to calculate the form factor of the spherically averaged Skyrmion charge density; the second uses the Patterson function to calculate the scattering intensity off randomly oriented Skyrmions, and spherically averages at the end. We compare our findings with experimental scattering data. We also find approximate analytical formulae for the first zero and first stationary point of a form factor.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for spin- and isospin-zero nuclei modeled as Skyrmions, the classically averaged scattering intensity (using the Patterson function) and the quantum averaged form factor yield different predictions, particularly regarding the presence of zeros in the intensity; however, these differences are difficult to distinguish experimentally because discrete momentum sampling cannot separate a sharp minimum from a true zero.

## Method

The authors computed electron scattering intensities for Skyrmions with baryon numbers B = 4, 8, 12, 16, 32, and 108 using the Born approximation. They employed two averaging procedures: quantum averaging, where the spherically averaged charge density yields a form factor F(q^2), and classical averaging, where the Patterson function (autocorrelation of the charge density) is spherically averaged to give the intensity I(q^2). The Skyrmion length scale was calibrated to the experimental root-mean-square charge radius of each corresponding nucleus, with the rescaled pion mass fixed to m = 1.

## Key numbers

- For the B=4 Skyrmion (representing He-4), the first minimum in the quantum averaged form factor occurs at q^2 ≈ 4.5 fm^{-2}, and the experimental data for He-4 extend up to q^2 = 80 fm^{-2}.
- The B=4 Skyrmion calibration uses F_π = 87.3 MeV and e = 3.65, giving a length unit of 1.24 fm and an energy unit of 5.98 MeV.
- The B=8 Skyrmion has its first minimum at q^2 ≈ 3.4 fm^{-2}, lower than the B=4 value due to its larger charge radius.
- The experimental charge radii used for calibration are: B=4 (He-4) 1.68 fm, B=8 (Be-9 estimate) 2.52 fm, B=12 2.47 fm, B=16 2.70 fm, B=32 3.26 fm, and B=108 (Ag-107) 4.50 fm.
- For the B=4 Skyrmion rotating with angular momentum L_3 = 1, the angular velocity gives a surface speed of about 1/20 the speed of light, and the electron crossing time is about 1/125 of the nuclear rotation period.

## Context

This work extends prior Skyrme model calculations of electron scattering by Battye and collaborators (2010) and Manton and Wood (2006), who used only the quantum averaging method. The paper introduces the Patterson function approach from X-ray crystallography (Guinier, 1963) as an alternative classical averaging method for randomly oriented nuclei. The results enable comparison of Skyrme model predictions with experimental data for He-4 (from McCarthy and collaborators, 1977) and provide a framework for testing whether the Skyrme model can fit scattering data with only one adjustable parameter (the length scale), unlike phenomenological Fermi distributions that require multiple parameters.
