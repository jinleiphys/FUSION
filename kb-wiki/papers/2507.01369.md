# Effects of beyond-mean-field correlations on nuclear Schiff moments

> We compute the nuclear Schiff moments of the diamagnetic atoms $^{129}$Xe, $^{199}$Hg, and $^{225}$Ra in multireference covariant density functional theory. Beyond-mean-field correlations, arising from symmetry restoration and shape mixing, are incorporated via the generator coordinate method with projection onto states with well-defined parity, particle number, and angular momentum. Our results reveal a correlation between the contributions of nuclear intermediate states to Schiff moments and the electric dipole transition strengths from these states to the ground state. The new beyond-mean-field effects can either enhance or suppress the Schiff moments. In $^{225}$Ra, they do the latter, reducing the enhancement from octupole deformation somewhat.

## Key claim

Beyond-mean-field correlations, incorporated via the generator coordinate method with symmetry restoration, significantly modify nuclear Schiff moments in diamagnetic atoms; in ²²⁵Ra they reduce the enhancement from octupole deformation, contrary to the expectation that such correlations would only enhance the moments.

## Method

The authors used multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) with the generator coordinate method to mix mean-field configurations with different quadrupole and octupole deformations, projecting onto states with well-defined parity, particle number, and angular momentum. They evaluated the matrix elements of the P,T-odd pion-exchange two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction between ground and excited states relativistically, and computed the Schiff moment structure factors a_α for ¹²⁹Xe, ¹⁹⁹Hg, and ²²⁵Ra.

## Key numbers

* ¹²⁹Xe structure factors (extrapolated to infinite oscillator shells): a_0 = -0.0052 e·fm³, a_1 = +0.0134 e·fm³, a_2 = -0.0097 e·fm³
* ¹⁹⁹Hg structure factors (extrapolated to infinite oscillator shells): a_0 = -0.0163 e·fm³, a_1 = +0.0327 e·fm³, a_2 = -0.0375 e·fm³
* ²²⁵Ra structure factors (extrapolated to infinite oscillator shells): a_0 = +0.15 e·fm³, a_1 = -1.9 e·fm³, a_2 = +1.3 e·fm³
* Calculated magnetic dipole moments: ¹²⁹Xe ground state -0.785 μ_N (experiment -0.778 μ_N); ¹⁹⁹Hg ground state +0.541 μ_N (experiment +0.506 μ_N); ²²⁵Ra ground state -0.783 μ_N (experiment -0.734 μ_N)
* Calculated B(E2) strengths: ¹²⁹Xe 5/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁ = 24 W.u. (experiment 21(4) W.u.); ²²⁵Ra 5/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁ = 101 W.u. (experiment 102(6) W.u.); ¹⁹⁹Hg 5/2⁻₁ → 1/2⁻₁ = 19 W.u. (experiment 17.6(3) W.u.)
* In ²²⁵Ra, shape mixing reduces the structure factors by up to a factor of three compared to mean-field results
* The parity-doublet partner in ²²⁵Ra is predicted at 0.069 MeV excitation energy (experimental value 0.055 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous Schiff moment calculations that used mean-field approaches such as Skyrme Hartree-Fock (Dobaczewski 2005, Dobaczewski 2018), random-phase approximation (Dmitriev 2003, Dmitriev 2004), and shell-model methods (Yoshinaga 2018, Yanase 2020) by incorporating beyond-mean-field correlations through symmetry restoration and shape mixing. The results enable more reliable interpretation of ongoing and next-generation electric dipole moment experiments searching for CP violation beyond the Standard Model, particularly for ²²⁵Ra where the calculation is considered most reliable. The correlation found between Schiff moment contributions and electric dipole transition strengths suggests that experimental B(E1) measurements could help constrain nuclear models used for Schiff moment predictions.
