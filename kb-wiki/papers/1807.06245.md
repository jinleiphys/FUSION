# Global Dyson-Schwinger-Bethe-Salpeter Approach to Mesons with Open Flavour

> Exploiting an interplay of the Bethe-Salpeter equation enabling us to regard mesons as bound states of quark and antiquark and the Dyson-Schwinger equation controlling the dressed quark propagator, we amend existing studies of quarkonia by a comprehensive description of open-flavour mesons composed of all conceivable combinations of quark flavour. Employing throughout a fixed set of model parameters, we predict some basic characteristics of these mesons, i.e., their masses, leptonic decay constants and corresponding in-hadron condensates entering in a generalized formulation of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.

## Key claim

Using a fixed set of model parameters within a combined Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter framework, the authors predict masses, leptonic decay constants, and in-hadron condensates for all conceivable open-flavour meson combinations, extending prior work that focused on quarkonia. The hedge is that these predictions are made within a truncated (rainbow-ladder) approximation and that systematic uncertainties are estimated only by comparing results from two different effective-interaction models.

## Method

The dressed quark propagator is obtained from the Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation, using a Landau-gauge free gluon propagator and an effective coupling that subsumes the full gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex. The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder truncation is then solved for the meson bound-state amplitude, expanded in Lorentz covariants (four for spin-zero, eight for nonzero spin). From the solution, the meson mass, leptonic decay constant, and in-hadron condensate (the matrix element of the chiral density between vacuum and bound state) are extracted.

## Context

This work extends prior covariant Dyson-Schwinger–Bethe-Salpeter studies that were limited to quarkonia (flavourless mesons) by applying the same formalism to all open-flavour combinations. It builds on the rainbow-ladder truncation approach used in earlier studies by the same group (GRHK, GRHKL) and adopts two popular effective-interaction models (EIa, EIb) to estimate systematic uncertainties. The predictions enable comparison with experimental meson spectra and decay constants, and provide input for generalized formulations of the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation via the in-hadron condensates.
