# The propagation of a massive spin-$\frac{3}{2}$ field, with application to $Ï€N$ scattering

> In the present paper, we investigate the propagation of a massive spin-$\frac{3}{2}$ field, aiming at a direct application in hadronic models of the pion-nucleon ($Ï€N$) interaction. Suitable expressions for the contributions to the standard invariant amplitudes $A$ and $B$ are derived, applicable in the general case of isospin decomposition. We first deal with the details of the lengthy calculation involving the Rarita-Schwinger propagator and confirm the validity of the expressions which had appeared in the literature in the early 1970s. We subsequently derive the corresponding contributions when following two other approaches, one featuring the Williams propagator, the other being known as Pascalutsa's method.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the validity of expressions for the Rarita-Schwinger propagator contributions to pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes that had appeared in the early 1970s, and derives analogous expressions using the Williams propagator and Pascalutsa's method, with the caveat that the pole contributions are independent of the off-shell parameter Z while the non-pole contributions depend on it.

## Method

The authors calculate the contributions of a massive spin-3/2 intermediate state (such as the Delta(1232) resonance) to the standard invariant amplitudes A and B in pion-nucleon scattering, using the Rarita-Schwinger propagator, the Williams propagator, and Pascalutsa's method. They separate the contributions into pole terms (inversely proportional to s - m_R^2 or u - m_R^2) and non-pole terms, and provide explicit expressions for the s-channel and u-channel contributions with general isospin decomposition parameters alpha and beta.

## Key numbers

- The Rarita-Schwinger propagator spin-projection operator contains the parameter A, with the choice A = -1 used in the paper (eliminating part of the spin-1/2 contributions)
- The vertex factor Theta^{mu nu} = g^{mu nu} - (Z + 1/2) gamma^mu gamma^nu, where Z is a free parameter; the authors' partial-wave analyses favor Z = -1/2
- For the Delta(1232) intermediate state, the isospin decomposition uses alpha = 2/3 and beta = 1/3 for the s-channel, and alpha = 2/3 and beta = -1/3 for the u-channel
- For the N(1720) intermediate state, the isospin decomposition uses alpha = 1 and beta = -1 for the s-channel, and alpha = 1 and beta = 1 for the u-channel
- The pole contribution to A_{s,p}^pm for the Delta(1232) is given by (2/1) * (g_{pi N Delta}^2 / (36 m_p^2)) * (alpha_1 + alpha_2 t) / (m_Delta^2 - s), where alpha_1 = 3 (m_R + m_p) |q_R|^2 + (m_R - m_p) (p_{0R} + m_p)^2 and alpha_2 = 3/2 (m_R + m_p)

## Context

This work extends the treatment of massive spin-3/2 fields in hadronic models of pion-nucleon scattering, building on the Rarita-Schwinger formalism from the late 1930s and early 1940s, and the work of Hohler (1983) and Nath, Etemadi, and Kimel (1971) who provided the concise formulae for the Delta(1232) contributions. The paper also addresses the Williams propagator from the mid 1980s, which Benmerrouche, Davidson, and Mukhopadhyay (1989) argued has no inverse and therefore cannot be correct, and Pascalutsa's method from the late 1990s. The results enable future partial-wave analyses of pion-nucleon scattering data with fewer free parameters if Z can be fixed to -1/2, and may be relevant for treating higher-spin fields (spin-5/2 and spin-7/2).
