# Statistical Theory of Breakup Reactions

> We propose alternatives to coupled-channels calculations with loosely-bound exotic nuclei (CDCC), based on the the random matrix (RMT) and the optical background (OPM) models for the statistical theory of nuclear reactions. The coupled channels equations are divided into two sets. The first set, described by the CDCC, and the other set treated with RMT. The resulting theory is a Statistical CDCC (CDCC$_S$), able in principle to take into account many pseudo channels.

## Key claim
The authors propose that the computational burden of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations for reactions with loosely-bound exotic nuclei can be reduced by treating a large number of weakly-coupled continuum channels statistically using random matrix theory (RMT) or the optical background formalism, rather than solving all coupled equations exactly. The resulting Statistical CDCC (CDCC_S) approach is presented as a formal framework, but the paper does not demonstrate a complete numerical implementation or validation against experimental data.

## Method
The coupled-channels equations are divided into two sets: a small set of strongly-coupled "P-space" channels treated by standard CDCC, and a large set of weakly-coupled "Q-space" channels treated statistically. The statistical channels are characterized by random matrix properties (zero mean, specified second moments for the coupling matrix elements) and are eliminated in favor of an average polarization potential added to the P-space equations. Three formal variants are developed: (1) an averaged CDCC_S with a polarization potential derived from random-matrix averaging; (2) time-dependent balance equations for occupation probabilities with gain and loss terms; and (3) an optical background formalism using Feshbach projection operators and energy averaging over an interval Delta, leading to a split of the S-matrix into a direct part and a fluctuating part.

## Key numbers
- A numerical test used 400 equidistant q-levels, 40 channels, and 20 equidistant coordinate points where the coupling HPQ was set to a Gaussian-distributed random interaction.
- The energy of the single P-space state was taken as E = 20 MeV, with 100 energy points for Lorentzian averaging between 18 and 22 MeV.
- The adopted energy averaging interval was Delta = 0.5 MeV.
- The condition Gamma/D >> 1 was assumed (width much larger than level spacing) for the test case.
- s-wave scattering only was considered in the numerical test.

## Context
This work extends the standard CDCC method (Bertulani 1992, Descouvemont 2013) by incorporating statistical treatments originally developed for compound-nucleus reactions and neutron-induced reactions. The optical background formalism builds on the Feshbach projection method and on prior work by Arbanas (2012) and by Weidenmuller and collaborators. The approach is intended to enable CDCC calculations for reactions with many weakly-coupled continuum channels that would otherwise be computationally prohibitive, and the authors suggest applications in quantum optics, atomic physics, and open quantum or mesoscopic systems.
