# The model-discriminating power of lepton flavor violating tau decays

> Within an effective field theory framework, we discuss the possibility to discriminate among different operators that contribute to lepton flavor violating (LFV) tau decays. Correlations among decay rates in different channels are shown to provide a basic handle to unravel the origin of LFV in these processes. More information about the underlying dynamics responsible for LFV can be gathered from differential distributions in three-body decays like tau -> mu pi pi or tau -> 3 mu: these are considered in some detail. We incorporate in our analysis recent developments in the determination of the hadronic form factors for tau -> mu pi pi. Future prospects for the observation of LFV tau decays and its interpretation are also discussed.

## Key claim

Within an effective field theory framework, the authors show that correlations among decay rates in different lepton flavor violating tau decay channels provide a basic handle to discriminate among contributing operators, but the full discriminating power requires analyzing differential distributions in three-body decays like tau to mu pi pi or tau to 3 mu, contingent on proper determination of hadronic form factors in the resonance region.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian for tau-mu lepton flavor violating transitions, encoding new physics effects in dimension-five dipole operators, dimension-six four-fermion operators involving quark bilinears, dimension-seven gluonic operators, and four-lepton operators. They compute decay rates for radiative (tau to mu gamma), leptonic (tau to 3 mu), and semileptonic (tau to mu pi, tau to mu eta, tau to mu pi pi) decay modes, incorporating recent dispersive determinations of hadronic form factors for the tau to mu pi pi channel that cover the full accessible phase space rather than relying on leading-order Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu gamma): 4.4 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle/BaBar)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to 3 mu): 2.1 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle/BaBar/LHCb)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu pi pi): 2.1 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu rho): 1.2 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu f0): 3.4 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu pi0): 1.1 x 10^{-7} at 90% confidence level (Belle/BaBar)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu eta): 6.5 x 10^{-8} at 90% confidence level (Belle)
- Upper limit on BR(tau to mu eta'): 1.3 x 10^{-7} at 90% confidence level (Belle)
- Expected sensitivity gain of one order of magnitude in many LFV tau decay modes with 50 ab^{-1} of data at Belle II

## Context

This work extends previous effective field theory analyses of lepton flavor violating tau decays by Black (2002), Brignole (2004), Dassinger (2007), Matsuzaki (2007), Giffels (2008), Petrov (2013), and Crivellin (2013), which relied on leading-order Chiral Perturbation Theory for hadronic matrix elements. The key improvement is the incorporation of proper hadronic form factors for tau to mu pi pi decays from Daub (2012) and Celis (2013), which cover the resonance region up to the kinematic limit. This enables more reliable discrimination among scalar, vector, dipole, and gluonic operators through the pion invariant mass spectrum in three-body decays, complementing the Dalitz plot analysis approach for leptonic decays. Future Belle II data and potential Super Tau-Charm Factory measurements could test the predicted correlation patterns.
