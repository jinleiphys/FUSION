# Testing the phase transition parameters inside neutron stars with the production of protons and lambdas in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We demonstrate the consistency of the quark deconfinement phase transition parameters in the beta-stable neutron star matter and in the nearly symmetric nuclear matter formed in heavy-ion collisions (HICs). We investigate the proton and $Λ$ flow in Au+Au collisions at 3 and 4.5 GeV/nucleon incident beam energies with the pure hadron cascade version of a multi-phase transport model. The phase transition in HICs and neutron stars is described based on a class of hybrid equations of state from the quark mean-field model for the hadronic phase and a constant-speed-of-sound parametrization for the high-density quark phase. The measurements of the anisotropic proton flow at 3 GeV/nucleon by the STAR collaboration favor a relatively low phase transition density lower than $\sim 2.5$ times saturation density indicated by the gravitational wave and electromagnetic observations of neutron stars. And the proton flow data at the higher energy of 4.5 GeV/nucleon can be used to effectively constrain the softness of high-density quark matter equations of state. Finally, compared to the proton flow, the $Λ$ flow is found to be less sensitive and not constraining to the equations of state.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the phase transition parameters (transition density, strength, and sound speed) for quark deconfinement in neutron star matter are consistent with those inferred from heavy-ion collision data, but only if the transition density is relatively low (below about 2.5 times nuclear saturation density) and the high-density quark matter is not too stiff.

## Method
The authors used the pure hadron cascade version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport Model) to simulate Au+Au collisions at 3 and 4.5 GeV/nucleon beam energies. They employed a class of hybrid equations of state from the quark mean-field (QMF) model for the hadronic phase and a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parametrization for the high-density quark phase, with a first-order deconfinement phase transition described by Maxwell construction. The simulated proton and Λ directed flow (v₁) and elliptic flow (v₂) were compared to STAR experimental data for various combinations of transition density, transition strength, and quark sound speed.

## Key numbers
- Transition density favored by STAR proton flow data at 3 GeV/nucleon: n_trans/n_0 ≲ 2.5 (where n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density)
- Saturation density of the QMF hadronic EoS: n_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy at saturation: E/A = -16 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: K = 240 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 31 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope at saturation: L = 40 MeV
- Critical transition pressure from neutron star observations: about 16.8 MeV fm⁻³
- Critical baryon chemical potential from neutron star observations: around 1050 MeV
- Sound speed squared in quark matter favored by data: c_QM² close to 1/3 (the conformal limit)
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Λ(1.4 M⊙) = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ at 90% credible level
- Maximum mass constraint from PSR J0740+6620: M ∼ 2.07 M⊙

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis of neutron star observational data (Li et al., 2022, MNRAS) that constrained phase transition parameters from multimessenger astrophysics (LIGO/Virgo gravitational wave data and NICER mass-radius measurements). It directly tests whether the same phase transition parameters apply to the nearly symmetric nuclear matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, following the approach of Li et al. (2021, Phys. Lett. B) who used a bag-model description. The results enable future Bayesian joint analyses of heavy-ion and astrophysical data to constrain the dense matter equation of state, and highlight that Λ flow is less constraining than proton flow for determining phase transition properties.
