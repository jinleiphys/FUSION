# Relativistic corrections to energy spectrum of hydrogen due to the full one-photon-exchange interaction

> In this work, we present expressions for the full effective potential corresponding to the one-photon exchange interaction within the framework of an effective Schrödinger-like equation, which is derived exactly from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in quantum electrodynamics. The final effective potential is expressed in terms of eight scalar functions. When these eight scalar functions are expanded in terms of velocities order by order, we can return to the non-relativistic effective potential systematically organized by velocities. By retaining the exact momentum dependence in the effective potential, we estimate its corrections to the energy spectrum of hydrogen using a highly precise numerical method. The comparison of the numerical results with those obtained using conventional bound-state perturbative theory is discussed. Our calculations suggest that it is possible to accurately account for all relativistic contributions using this method. It would be interesting to extend these calculations to positronium, muonic hydrogen, and scenarios involving nuclear structure and radiative corrections.

## Key claim

By retaining the exact momentum dependence of the one-photon-exchange interaction kernel (without expanding in velocities) and solving the effective Schrödinger-like equation numerically, the authors claim it is possible to accurately account for all relativistic contributions to the hydrogen energy spectrum at order α_e, including contributions beyond the Breit potential, without encountering the additional ultraviolet divergences that appear in conventional bound-state perturbative theory.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in QED and derive an effective Schrödinger-like equation with a full one-photon-exchange interaction kernel expressed in terms of eight scalar functions. They do not expand this kernel in powers of velocities or mass ratios, but instead solve the equation numerically by expanding the wave function in a basis of Gegenbauer polynomials (up to n_max=100) and diagonalizing the resulting matrix. The angular integrations are performed analytically, while the radial momentum integrations are done numerically with a relative precision of 10^(-20).

## Key numbers

- The physical constants used are m_e = 0.510998950 MeV, m_p = 938.27208816 MeV, and 1/α_e = 137.035999084.
- For the 1S state, the correction beyond the Breit potential for the spin-independent part (ΔE_{1,0}(S)) is -2349361 peV (10^(-12) eV).
- For the 2S state, ΔE_{2,0}(S) = -289552 peV; for the 2P state, ΔE_{2,0}(P) = 813 peV.
- For the 3S state, ΔE_{3,0}(S) = -85154 peV; for the 3P state, ΔE_{3,0}(P) = 424 peV; for the 3D state, ΔE_{3,0}(D) = 13 peV.
- The correction to the hyperfine structure beyond the Breit potential for the 1S state (ΔE_{1}^{hyf}(S)) is -7999 peV; for the 2S state it is -1003 peV.
- The correction to the fine structure beyond the Breit potential for the 2P state (ΔE_{2}^{fin}(P)) is -857 peV.
- The uncertainty from finite basis size is smaller than 0.5 peV when n_max > 60.

## Context

This work extends the bound-state perturbative theory approach used in standard QED treatments of hydrogen (as reviewed by Eides (2007), Jentschura (2022), and Pachucki (2024)) by avoiding the velocity expansion that introduces additional ultraviolet divergences. The method builds on the effective Schrödinger-like equation framework of Lepage (1978) and provides a numerical benchmark for comparing with results from NRQED (Lepage, 1986) and quasi-potential approaches (Faustov, 1985; Martynenko, 2005). The authors suggest that the method can be extended to positronium, muonic hydrogen, and systems involving nuclear structure and radiative corrections.
