# Electronic bridge process in 229Th^+

> We have studied the effect of atomic electrons on the nuclear transition from the isomeric {229m}Th state to the ground {229g}Th state in {229}Th+ due to the electronic bridge process. The exact value of the nuclear transition frequency is unknown so far; therefore, we have developed a formalism that can be used for any nuclear transition frequency. We have calculated positions of several high-lying even-parity states which are not presented in experimental atomic spectra databases. We have found their energy levels and g factors.

# Electronic bridge process in 229Th^+

## Key claim

The electronic bridge process can enhance the nuclear transition probability from the isomeric 229mTh state to the ground 229gTh state in 229Th+ by factors of 225 to 720 relative to the bare nuclear M1 transition, but only if the unknown nuclear transition frequency (between 2 and 8 eV) happens to lie close to an atomic resonance for which the intermediate state has a sufficiently large product of hyperfine and electric-dipole matrix elements.

## Method

The authors used a combined configuration-interaction and many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) approach with a Dirac-Hartree-Fock starting potential to calculate energy levels, g factors, and transition matrix elements for even-parity states of Th+ with total angular momenta J=3/2 and J=5/2 in the energy range 2–8 eV above the ground state. They then computed the coefficient R_n (the squared product of the magnetic-dipole hyperfine matrix element from the initial 6d^2 7s (J=3/2) state to each intermediate state and the electric-dipole matrix element from that intermediate state to the final 5f 7s^2 (J=5/2) state), and used these to evaluate the electronic bridge enhancement factor β_M1 for candidate nuclear transition frequencies.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear transition frequency ω_N is experimentally uncertain, with reported values ranging from 3.5 ± 1.0 eV (ReiHel90) to 7.6 ± 0.5 eV (BecBecBei07).
- For ω_N = 3.5 eV (28231 cm⁻¹), the resonant intermediate state is the 5f7s7p (J=5/2) level at 28824 cm⁻¹, with R_n = 0.2 a.u., yielding β_M1 = 225.
- For ω_N = 5.5 eV (44363 cm⁻¹), the resonant intermediate state is the 5f²6d + 5f6d7p (J=5/2) level at 44389 cm⁻¹, with R_n = 0.001 a.u., yielding β_M1 = 720.
- The calculated energy levels for states up to 56391 cm⁻¹ agree with experimental data to within several percent, with the largest deviation (15%) for states of the 6d³ configuration.
- The coefficients R_n for all even-parity states between 2 and 8 eV range from 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻¹ a.u.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the electronic bridge process in 229Th³⁺ (PorFla10) to the more complex three-valence-electron ion 229Th⁺, which has a much denser atomic spectrum and therefore offers more opportunities for resonant enhancement. The nuclear transition is of interest for building a nuclear clock (PeiTam03) and for studying temporal variation of fundamental constants (Fla06). The results provide the necessary atomic data (energy levels, g factors, and R_n coefficients) so that once the nuclear transition frequency is precisely measured, the electronic bridge enhancement factor can be immediately determined; however, for the highest candidate frequency of 7.6 eV the atomic levels in that region are not experimentally identified, so reliable prediction is not yet possible.
