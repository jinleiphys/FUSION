# The Mu and Tau Number of Supernovae

> The neutrino-nucleon cross section is slightly larger than that for $\barν-N$. Therefore, $\barν$ will escape more quickly from core collapse supernovae leaving the star $ν$ rich. A diffusion formalism is used to calculate the time evolution of the mu and tau lepton number densities. These quickly reach steady state equilibria. We estimate that a protoneutron star with a maximum temperature near 50 MeV will contain over 50 % more $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ than $\barν_μ$ and $\barν_τ$. Supernovae may be the only known systems with large mu and or tau lepton numbers.

# The Mu and Tau Number of Supernovae

## Key claim

Core collapse supernovae may contain over 50% more muon and tau neutrinos than their corresponding antineutrinos, but this asymmetry depends on the protoneutron star reaching a steady-state equilibrium via fast diffusion of lepton number, and the effect arises specifically from recoil and weak magnetism corrections to neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections that are of order E/M (neutrino energy over nucleon mass).

## Method

The authors derived a diffusion equation for the muon (and tau) lepton number density by subtracting the transport equations for neutrinos and antineutrinos, using the difference in their mean free paths arising from weak magnetism corrections to the neutral-current elastic scattering cross section. They solved this diffusion equation numerically for a uniform-density protoneutron star model with a simple temperature profile, starting from zero chemical potential and evolving until steady state was reached. The steady-state chemical potential was then used to compute the total lepton number asymmetry integrated over the star.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient δ in the fractional cross section difference D = δ(E/M) is 3.32 for neutrons and 2.71 for protons.
- At a central temperature of 35 MeV, the chemical potential reaches half its equilibrium value by t ≈ 0.25 seconds.
- The density asymmetry (ρ − ρ̄)/(ρ + ρ̄) = 4.98 T/M, giving 0.186 at T = 35 MeV.
- For a maximum temperature T₀ = 50 MeV, the total muon lepton number N − N̄ = 9.40 × 10⁵³, with total neutrino number N + N̄ = 4.43 × 10⁵⁴, corresponding to 54% more ν_μ than ν̄_μ.
- The protoneutron star radius is 11.25 km for a uniform density of 5 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³ and baryon mass 1.5 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends previous supernova neutrino transport calculations (Burrows and Lattimer, 1986; Keil and Janka, 1995; Mayle and Wilson, 1991; Bowers and Wilson, 1982; Bruenn and Haxton, 1991; Bruenn, 1985) which assumed equal neutrino and antineutrino cross sections, by including recoil and weak magnetism corrections of order E/M. The authors note that Burrows and Sawyer (1998) argued RPA correlations would reduce cross sections, but the authors contend these corrections affect neutrinos and antineutrinos similarly. The predicted large muon and tau lepton numbers could enable new tests of weak long-range forces coupling to muon or tau number, affect matter-enhanced neutrino oscillations (Kostelecky and Samuel, 1995), and influence nucleosynthesis by altering the proton fraction through electron neutrino asymmetries.
