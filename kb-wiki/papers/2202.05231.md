# Landau parameters and entrainment matrix of cold stellar matter: effect of the symmetry energy and strong magnetic fields

> Nuclear matter properties based on a relativistic approach suitable for the description of multi-component systems are calculated. We use a set of nuclear relativistic mean-field models that satisfy acceptable nuclear matter properties and neutron star observations. The effects of the density dependence of the symmetry energy and of the Landau quantization due to the presence of a strong external magnetic field are discussed. Properties such as the proton fraction, the Landau mass, Landau parameters and entrainment matrix, the adiabatic index and speed of sound are calculated for cold $β$-equilibrium matter. A large dispersion on the calculated properties is obtained at two to three times saturation density $ρ_0 $. The proton Landau mass can be as low as one third of the vacuum nucleon mass at 2-3$~ρ_0 $. Similar effects are obtained for the Landau parameters, in particular, the ones involving protons, where the relative dispersion of $F^0_{pp}$ and $F^1_{pp}$ is as high as 30\% to 50\% at 2-3$~ρ_0 $. These parameters are particularly sensitive to the symmetry energy. The effect of the magnetic field on the nuclear properties is small for fields as high as 10$^{18}$G except for a small range of densities just above the crust-core transition. Tables with the EoS, and the parameters, are provided in the Supplementary Material section.

# Landau parameters and entrainment matrix of cold stellar matter: effect of the symmetry energy and strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy produces a large dispersion in Landau parameters and entrainment matrix coefficients at two to three times saturation density, with the proton Landau parameters $F^0_{pp}$ and $F^1_{pp}$ showing relative dispersions of 30% to 50% across different relativistic mean-field models; however, the effect of magnetic fields as strong as $10^{18}$ G on these nuclear properties remains small except for a narrow density range just above the crust-core transition.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework with non-linear meson couplings (scalar $\sigma$, vector $\omega$, isovector $\rho$, and $\omega$-$\rho$ mixing) to describe cold $\beta$-equilibrium nuclear matter, including nucleons and electrons. They considered a family of models (NL3$\omega\rho$55, NL3$\omega\rho$88, FSU2, FSU2R, FSU2H, BigApple, TM1, TM1e) that differ primarily in the isovector channel and thus in the density dependence of the symmetry energy. For magnetized matter, the Landau quantization of charged particles was implemented with the magnetic field aligned along the $z$-axis, and the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of nucleons was included in some calculations.

## Key numbers

- The proton Landau effective mass can be as low as one third of the vacuum nucleon mass (approximately 313 MeV) at 2–3 times saturation density $\rho_0$.
- At $2\rho_0$, the proton effective mass ranges from $0.37\,m_N$ to $0.47\,m_N$ across models; at $3\rho_0$, the range is $0.3\,m_N$ to $0.4\,m_N$.
- At $2\rho_0$, the neutron effective mass ranges from $0.53\,m_N$ to $0.6\,m_N$; at $3\rho_0$, from $0.5\,m_N$ to $0.57\,m_N$.
- The relative dispersion of the Landau parameters $F^0_{pp}$ and $F^1_{pp}$ is 30% to 50% at 2–3$\rho_0$.
- The symmetry energy slope $L$ for the models ranges from 40 MeV (BigApple, TM1e) to 118 MeV (NL3), with the reference model NL3$\omega\rho$55 having $L=55$ MeV.
- The symmetry energy $J$ at saturation ranges from 30.5 MeV (FSU2H) to 37.6 MeV (FSU2).
- Magnetic fields up to $2\times 10^{18}$ G ($B^*=4\times 10^4$) were considered; visible effects on the speed of sound and adiabatic index appear only for fields $\gtrsim 10^{18}$ G.
- The proton fraction shows noticeable magnetic field effects for $B \gtrsim 5\times 10^{17}$ G in the outer core.
- The NL3$\omega\rho$55 model yields a proton fraction slightly above 0.1 at three times saturation density, while models with stiff symmetry energy (NL3, TM1, FSU2) give proton fractions of order 0.2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Landau parameters and entrainment in neutron star matter (Caillon 2001, Gusakov 2009, Gusakov 2014) by systematically comparing models with different symmetry energy slopes, including softer symmetry energies consistent with modern constraints from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2013) and astrophysical observations. The NL3$\omega\rho$ family was introduced in earlier work (Pais 2016) to vary the isovector channel while keeping isoscalar properties fixed. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star hydrodynamics, including superfluid and superconducting components relevant for glitch phenomena (Andersson 2012, Chamel 2012, Chamel 2013) and neutrino scattering in neutron star interiors.
