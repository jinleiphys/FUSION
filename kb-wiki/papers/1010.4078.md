# The Electric Dipole Form Factor of the Nucleon in Chiral Perturbation Theory to Sub-leading Order

> The electric dipole form factor (EDFF) of the nucleon stemming from the QCD theta term and from the quark color-electric dipole moments is calculated in chiral perturbation theory to sub-leading order. This is the lowest order in which the isoscalar EDFF receives a calculable, non-analytic contribution from the pion cloud. In the case of the theta term, the expected lower bound on the deuteron electric dipole moment is |d_d| > 1.4 10^(-4) θe fm. The momentum dependence of the isovector EDFF is proportional to a non-derivative time-reversal-violating pion-nucleon coupling, and the scale for momentum variation ---appearing, in particular, in the radius of the form factor--- is the pion mass.

## Key claim

The electric dipole form factor (EDFF) of the nucleon from the QCD theta term and quark color-electric dipole moment is calculated to sub-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, where the isoscalar momentum dependence receives a calculable non-analytic contribution from the pion cloud for the first time; however, the isoscalar EDFF at this order is entirely due to isospin-breaking terms related to the nucleon mass splitting, and the momentum dependence of the isovector EDFF remains proportional to a non-derivative time-reversal-violating pion-nucleon coupling with the pion mass as the scale for momentum variation.

## Method

The authors used heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with SU(2)L x SU(2)R symmetry to calculate the nucleon electric dipole form factor to next-to-leading order (NLO), including one-loop diagrams with insertions from the leading-order strong and T-violating Lagrangians plus one insertion from the sub-leading strong Lagrangian or sub-leading T-violating pion-nucleon couplings. They evaluated diagrams in d-dimensional regularization, considering both the QCD theta term and the quark color-electric dipole moment as sources of T violation, and incorporated isospin-breaking effects from the nucleon mass difference and pion mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- Lower bound on the deuteron electric dipole moment from the theta term: |d_d| > 1.4 x 10^(-4) theta e fm
- Neutron EDM from theta term: |d_n| approx (1.99 + 0.12 - 0.04 + 0.03) x 10^(-3) theta e fm
- Proton EDM from theta term: |d_p| approx (1.99 + 0.46 - 0.04 - 0.03) x 10^(-3) theta e fm
- Deuteron EDM lower bound from theta term: |d_d| approx (1.7 - 0.3) x 10^(-4) theta e fm
- Isoscalar Schiff moment from theta term: S'_0 approx -5.0 x 10^(-6) theta e fm^3
- Isovector Schiff moment from theta term: S'_1 approx 6.8 x 10^(-5) theta e fm^3
- NLO correction to the isovector EDM is about 15% of the leading non-analytic term; NLO correction to the isoscalar EDM is about 10%
- NLO correction to the isovector Schiff moment is about 60% due to the numerical factor 5pi/4

## Context

This work extends the LO calculation of the nucleon EDFF from the theta term by WHvK and from the qCEDM by jordyetal, and reproduces the NLO EDM results of Ottnad obtained from a relativistic large-Nc U(3) chiral perturbation theory, except for isospin-violating terms neglected there. The calculation enables improved predictions for the deuteron EDM and Schiff moment, providing a lower bound estimate for the deuteron EDM from the theta term that is about 10% of the neutron EDM signal, which is relevant for proposed experiments aiming to improve bounds on the neutron EDM by nearly two orders of magnitude and to constrain proton and deuteron EDMs at similar levels.
