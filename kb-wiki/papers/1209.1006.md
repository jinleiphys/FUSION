# Hydrodynamic fluctuations and the minimum shear viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity

> We study hydrodynamic fluctuations in a non-relativistic fluid. We show that in three dimensions fluctuations lead to a minimum in the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$ as a function of the temperature. The minimum provides a bound on $η/s$ which is independent of the conjectured bound in string theory, $η/s \geq \hbar/(4πk_B)$, where $s$ is the entropy density. For the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity we find $η/s\gsim 0.2\hbar$. This bound is not universal -- it depends on thermodynamic properties of the unitary Fermi gas, and on empirical information about the range of validity of hydrodynamics. We also find that the viscous relaxation time of a hydrodynamic mode with frequency $ω$ diverges as $1/\sqrtω$, and that the shear viscosity in two dimensions diverges as $\log(1/ ω)$.

# Hydrodynamic fluctuations and the minimum shear viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations in three dimensions produce a minimum in the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s as a function of temperature, yielding a bound η/s ≳ 0.2 ħ for the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity that is independent of the string theory bound η/s ≥ ħ/(4πk_B). This bound is not universal; it depends on thermodynamic properties of the unitary Fermi gas and on empirical information about the range of validity of hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors compute the retarded correlation function of the stress tensor at one-loop order using classical fluid dynamics, expanding the stress tensor in derivatives of thermodynamic variables and including thermal fluctuations of the velocity field. They evaluate the loop integral for shear modes, sound modes, and mixed shear-sound contributions in three dimensions, regulating ultraviolet divergences with a momentum cutoff Λ set by the breakdown scale of hydrodynamics K_hyd. The physical shear viscosity is obtained by combining the bare viscosity with the fluctuation-induced correction, and the bound on η/s is derived by minimizing the resulting expression with respect to the bare viscosity.

## Key numbers

- For the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity in three dimensions: η/s ≳ 0.2 ħ, with a minimum in the temperature range T/T_F ∼ 0.3–0.9.
- The bound on η/s scales as (Λ/K_hyd)^{1/3}, where Λ is the momentum cutoff and K_hyd is the hydrodynamic breakdown scale; the authors use Λ/K_hyd = 0.5 ± 0.25.
- The fluctuation correction to the shear viscosity in three dimensions is (17/120π²)(ρ T Λ/η), leading to a minimum η/n ≃ 0.5 at T ≃ 0.2 T_F.
- In two dimensions, the shear viscosity diverges as log(1/ω), with the physical viscosity given by η_phys = η + (mT/16π)(n/η) log(√2 Λ² η/(mω n)).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations in non-relativistic fluids, building on the foundational work of Landau and the review by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977). It closely follows recent studies of fluctuations in relativistic fluids by Kovtun (2011) and Peralta-Ramos (2011). The results provide a lower bound on η/s for the unitary Fermi gas that is compatible with experimental measurements by Cao (2010) and T-matrix calculations by Enss (2010), but larger than Path Integral Monte Carlo results by Wlazlowski (2012); the discrepancy may arise from the lattice spacing used in that work being unable to resolve the non-analytic ω^{3/2} behavior of the spectral function. The two-dimensional results connect to molecular dynamics simulations by Alder (1967) and lattice gas simulations by Kadanoff (1989).
