# High-accuracy calculation of the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors in chiral effective field theory

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors based on the latest two-nucleon potentials and charge density operators derived in chiral effective field theory. The single- and two-nucleon contributions to the charge density are expressed in terms of the proton and neutron form factors, for which the most up-to-date empirical parametrizations are employed. By adjusting the fifth-order short-range terms in the two-nucleon charge density operator to reproduce the world data on the momentum-transfer dependence of the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors, we predict the values of the structure radius and the quadrupole moment of the deuteron: $r_{\rm str}=1.9729\substack{+0.0015\\ -0.0012}\ \text{fm},\ Q_d=0.2854\substack{+0.0038\\ -0.0017}\ \text{fm}^2. $ A comprehensive and systematic analysis of various sources of uncertainty in our predictions is performed. Following the strategy advocated in our recent publication Phys. Rev. Lett. 124, 082501 (2020), we employ the extracted structure radius together with the accurate atomic data for the deuteron-proton mean-square charge radii difference to update the determination of the neutron charge radius, for which we find: $r_n^2=-0.105\substack{+0.005\\ -0.006} \, \text{fm}^2$. Given the observed rapid convergence of the deuteron form factors in the momentum-transfer range of $Q \simeq 1-2.5$ fm$^{-1}$, we argue that this intermediate-energy domain is particularly sensitive to the details of the nucleon form factors and can be used to test different parametrizations.

## Key claim

By adjusting fifth-order short-range terms in the two-nucleon charge density operator to reproduce world data on the momentum-transfer dependence of the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors, the authors predict the structure radius and quadrupole moment of the deuteron, and update the neutron charge radius determination. The hedge is that the predictions rely on the assumption that the rapid convergence of the chiral expansion in the momentum-transfer range Q approximately 1-2.5 fm^{-1} makes this domain particularly sensitive to nucleon form factors, allowing empirical parametrizations to be used instead of the strict chiral expansion for nucleon form factors.

## Method

The authors calculated the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors using chiral effective field theory, including all contributions to the charge-density operator at fourth order (N^3LO) relative to the leading single-nucleon operator and short-range operators at fifth order (N^4LO). They expressed both single- and two-nucleon charge density operators in terms of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors using the most up-to-date empirical parametrizations, and adjusted the strength of the N^4LO short-range operators to fit experimental data for the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors. The deuteron wave functions were obtained from the latest chiral NN potentials of Reinert et al. (2020) that include isospin-breaking corrections.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron structure radius: r_str = 1.9729 (+0.0015 / -0.0012) fm
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: Q_d = 0.2854 (+0.0038 / -0.0017) fm^2
- Neutron charge radius squared: r_n^2 = -0.105 (+0.005 / -0.006) fm^2
- The momentum-transfer range used for fitting the short-range operators extended to Q approximately 6 fm^{-1}, compared to Q approximately 4 fm^{-1} used in Filin et al. (2020)
- The rapid convergence of deuteron form factors was observed in the momentum-transfer range Q approximately 1-2.5 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Filin et al. (2020) by employing the latest version of the NN potential from Reinert et al. (2020) that includes isospin-breaking corrections, carrying out a combined analysis of both charge and quadrupole deuteron form factors, and extending the momentum range for fits to Q approximately 6 fm^{-1}. It builds on chiral EFT NN potentials of Reinert et al. (2017) and the charge density operators derived by Kolling et al. (2009, 2011) and Krebs et al. (2019). The results enable improved determination of the neutron charge radius when combined with atomic data on the deuteron-proton mean-square charge radii difference, and provide a test of nucleon form factor parametrizations in the intermediate-energy domain.
