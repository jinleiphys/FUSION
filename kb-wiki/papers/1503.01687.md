# Charge symmetry breaking in $Λ$ hypernuclei revisited

> The large charge symmetry breaking (CSB) implied by the $Λ$ binding energy difference $ΔB^{4}_Λ(0^+_{\rm g.s.})\equiv B_Λ(_Λ^4$He)$-$$B_Λ(_Λ^4$H) = 0.35$\pm$0.06 MeV of the $A=4$ mirror hypernuclei ground states, determined from emulsion studies, has defied theoretical attempts to reproduce it in terms of CSB in hyperon masses and in hyperon-nucleon interactions, including one pion exchange arising from $Λ-Σ^0$ mixing. Using a schematic strong-interaction $ΛN\leftrightarrowΣN$ coupling model developed by Akaishi and collaborators for $s$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei, we revisit the evaluation of CSB in the $A=4$ $Λ$ hypernuclei and extend it to $p$-shell mirror $Λ$ hypernuclei. The model yields values of $ΔB^{4}_Λ (0^+_{\rm g.s.})\sim 0.25$ MeV. Smaller size and mostly negative $p$-shell binding energy differences are calculated for the $A=7-10$ mirror hypernuclei, in rough agreement with the few available data. CSB is found to reduce by almost 30 keV the 110 keV $_{~Λ}^{10}$B g.s. doublet splitting anticipated from the hyperon-nucleon strong-interaction spin dependence, thereby explaining the persistent experimental failure to observe the $2^-_{\rm exc}\to 1^-_{\rm g.s.}$ $γ$-ray transition.

## Key claim

The large charge symmetry breaking (CSB) binding energy difference in A=4 mirror hypernuclei, which has resisted explanation by previous theoretical approaches, can be reproduced at approximately 0.25 MeV (versus the experimental 0.35±0.06 MeV) using a schematic ΛN↔ΣN coupling model with central interactions, but only if the strong-interaction ΛΣ coupling is dominated by central rather than tensor components.

## Method

The author applies a schematic strong-interaction ΛN↔ΣN coupling model developed by Akaishi and collaborators for s-shell Λ hypernuclei, using G-matrix YN effective interactions derived from NSC97 models. For the A=4 hypernuclei, the CSB contributions are calculated from Eq. (13) which relates the CSB potential matrix element to the strong-interaction matrix element ⟨NΣ|V|NΛ⟩ multiplied by a factor of -0.0297. For p-shell hypernuclei (A=7-10), the model is extended using 0p_N0s_Y central-interaction matrix elements that are roughly half the size of the s-shell matrix elements, and CSB contributions are evaluated by summing over p-shell nucleons using weak-coupling Λ-hypernuclear shell-model wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- ΔB⁴_Λ(0⁺_g.s.) = 0.35±0.06 MeV from emulsion studies for the A=4 mirror hypernuclei ground states
- The present (ΛΣ)ₑ model yields ΔB⁴_Λ(0⁺_g.s.) = 226 keV, and the (ΛΣ)ₓ model yields 266 keV
- The CSB OPE contribution alone gives ΔB_Λ^OPE(0⁺_g.s.) ≈ 95 keV for the A=4 ground state
- For p-shell hypernuclei: ΔB_Λ^calc = -17 keV for A=7, +49 keV for A=8, -54 keV for A=9, -136 keV for A=10
- The 110 keV ¹⁰B g.s. doublet splitting anticipated from strong-interaction spin dependence is reduced by almost 30 keV due to CSB, yielding approximately 85 keV
- Σ admixture probabilities P_Σ in the (ΛΣ)ₑ model: 0.72% for A=4, 0.12% for A=7, 0.20% for A=8, 0.23% for A=9, 0.053% for A=10

## Context

This work extends the schematic ΛN↔ΣN coupling model of Akaishi (2000, 2002) for s-shell hypernuclei to p-shell mirror hypernuclei, addressing the long-standing puzzle that four-body calculations by Carlson (1991), Coon (1999), Nogga (2002, 2007, 2013) and others could not reproduce the large CSB binding energy difference in A=4 hypernuclei, producing at most 100 keV. The model also explains the persistent experimental failure to observe the 2⁻_exc → 1⁻_g.s. γ-ray transition in ¹⁰B, which had been noted by Chrien (1990) and Tamura (2005). The results enable future work on heavier hypernuclei such as ¹²C–¹²B mirror pairs, though the author notes the need for more accurate measurements beyond existing emulsion data.
