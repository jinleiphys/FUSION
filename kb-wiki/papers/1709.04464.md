# Jet Substructure at the Large Hadron Collider: A Review of Recent Advances in Theory and Machine Learning

> Jet substructure has emerged to play a central role at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), where it has provided numerous innovative new ways to search for new physics and to probe the Standard Model in extreme regions of phase space. In this article we provide a comprehensive review of state of the art theoretical and machine learning developments in jet substructure. This article is meant both as a pedagogical introduction, covering the key physical principles underlying the calculation of jet substructure observables, the development of new observables, and cutting edge machine learning techniques for jet substructure, as well as a comprehensive reference for experts. We hope that it will prove a useful introduction to the exciting and rapidly developing field of jet substructure at the LHC.

## Key claim

This review provides a comprehensive pedagogical introduction and reference for both theoretical and machine learning developments in jet substructure at the LHC, covering key physical principles, observable engineering, and cutting-edge machine learning techniques, but the authors note that due to the wide range of topics they must be selective, emphasizing representative examples and underlying physical principles rather than technical details of calculations.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical aspects of jet substructure calculations, including resummation of logarithmically enhanced contributions, fixed-order perturbative corrections, and non-perturbative corrections from hadronization, using the jet mass as a concrete example. They then review a wide range of jet substructure observables and calculations, discuss prospects for improving precision, and examine new frontiers connecting jet substructure to other areas of physics. The machine learning section introduces technical aspects of ML and highlights applications to current problems in jet substructure.

## Key numbers

* Jet mass scale at the LHC is typically of order 10 GeV, while jet transverse momentum is of order 500 GeV, motivating the scaling log(p_TJ/m_J) ~ 1/alpha_s
* The QCD confinement scale Lambda_QCD is approximately 1 GeV, relevant for non-perturbative effects from hadronization
* Leading logarithm (LL) resummation includes terms of order O(alpha_s^{-1}), next-to-leading logarithm (NLL) includes terms of order O(1), next-to-next-to-leading logarithm (NNLL) includes terms of order O(alpha_s), and NNNLL includes terms of order O(alpha_s^2)
* The highest-order resummation for e+e- event shapes has been achieved at NNNLL, while the highest precision jet substructure calculations have been performed at NNLL

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on jet substructure calculations initiated by Sterman and Weinberg (1977) on IRC safe jet algorithms, Clavelli (1979) on jet mass predictions, and Catani, Trentadue, Turnock, and Webber (CTTW) on the first resummed jet substructure observable for heavy jet mass in e+e- collisions. It builds upon earlier specialized reviews by Salam (2009), Shelton (2013), Plehn (2011), Cacciari (2015), Adams (2015), Altheimer (2012, 2013), and Abdesselam (2010). The review enables future work by providing a wish list of goals to drive theory progress, and by outlining challenges for jet substructure at future colliders.
