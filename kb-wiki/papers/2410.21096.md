# Angular momentum dependence of $α$-decay spectroscopic factors

> The probabilities to form an $α$-particle on the surface of heavy nuclei (spectroscopic factors) in the states of different angular momenta are calculated within the dinuclear system (DNS) approach. It is shown that this dependence is determined not only by the energies of the excited states of the daughter nucleus, as is suggested by the Boltzmann distribution, but also by its quadrupole and octupole deformation parameters. The calculations were performed for actinide nuclei $^{222-228}$Ra, $^{222-232}$Th, $^{226-236}$U, and $^{234-242}$Pu. The results obtained will be useful in the future studies on the fine structure of alpha-decay.

## Key claim

The angular momentum dependence of alpha-decay spectroscopic factors is determined not only by the excitation energies of the daughter nucleus (as assumed by the Boltzmann distribution) but also by its quadrupole and octupole deformation parameters, with the deformation effects producing a staggering pattern where odd-angular-momentum states are systematically hindered relative to even-angular-momentum states.

## Method

The authors calculated spectroscopic factors for alpha-particle formation in states of different angular momenta using the dinuclear system (DNS) approach, where the parent nucleus wave function is a superposition of a mononuclear configuration and an alpha-cluster DNS component. The Hamiltonian for angular vibrations in the DNS was diagonalized on a basis of bipolar spherical harmonics, with the potential energy calculated from a double-folding procedure using Migdal nucleon-nucleon forces and Fermi density distributions. The calculations used experimental binding energies and ground-state deformations from Moller (2016) for the daughter nuclei.

- The Boltzmann distribution constant $c_I$ varies between approximately 5 MeV$^{-1}$ and 20 MeV$^{-1}$ across different nuclei and angular momenta, with $c_{even-I} \approx 15$ MeV$^{-1}$ for strongly quadrupole-deformed nuclei and $c_{odd-I} \approx 16$ MeV$^{-1}$ for strongly octupole-deformed nuclei

## Context

This work extends the dinuclear system model approach of Shneidman (2003, 2015) to explicitly calculate the angular momentum dependence of alpha-decay spectroscopic factors, which previous semiclassical approaches (Ren 2006, Santosh 2011, Denisov, Seif 2023) and microscopic coupled-channel calculations (Ren 2010, Ren 2011) had either assumed equal population of all angular momenta or treated empirically using the Boltzmann distribution. The results enable future studies of the fine structure of alpha-decay by providing a microscopic basis for the angular momentum dependence that can be incorporated into calculations of alpha-decay half-lives to excited states.
