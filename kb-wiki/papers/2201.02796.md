# Extracting the jet transport coefficient from hadron suppressions by confronting current NLO parton fragmentation functions

> Nuclear modification factors of single hadrons and dihadrons at large transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are studied in a next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model. Parton fragmentation functions (FFs) in $A+A$ collisions are modified due to jet energy loss which is proportional to the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ characterizing the interaction between the parton jet and the produced medium. By confronting 6 current sets of NLO parton FFs for large $p_{\rm T}$ hadron productions, we extract $\hat{q}$ quantitatively via a global fit to data for both single hadron and dihadron suppressions, and obtain $\hat{q}/T^3 = 4.74 - 6.72$ at $T = 370$ MeV in central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV, and $\hat{q}/T^3 = 3.07 - 3.98$ at $T = 480$ MeV in central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV. The numerical results show that the uncertainties for $\hat{q}$ extraction are brought by the different contributions of gluon-to-hadron in the 6 sets of FFs due to gluon energy loss being $9/4$ times of quark energy loss.

## Key claim

The extracted jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) depends significantly on which set of next-to-leading-order (NLO) parton fragmentation functions (FFs) is used, because the different gluon-to-hadron contributions in the six FF sets lead to different required energy loss, given that gluon energy loss is 9/4 times that of quarks.

## Method

The authors used a NLO perturbative QCD parton model with medium-modified fragmentation functions, where the energy loss is calculated via the high-twist approach and is proportional to the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\). They performed a global \(\chi^2\) fit to experimental data for both single hadron nuclear modification factors \(R_{AA}(p_{\rm T})\) and dihadron modification factors \(I_{AA}(z_{\rm T})\) in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV and central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV, using six different sets of NLO parton fragmentation functions (KRE, KKP, BFGW, HKNS, AKK08, DSS) with the same scale choices.

## Key numbers

*   \(\hat{q}/T^3 = 4.74 - 6.72\) at \(T = 370\) MeV in central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV.
*   \(\hat{q}/T^3 = 3.07 - 3.98\) at \(T = 480\) MeV in central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV.
*   Best-fitting \(\hat{q}_0\) values from global fits at RHIC range from \(\hat{q}_0=1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm (with DSS FFs) to \(\hat{q}_0=1.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm (with KRE or HKNS FFs).
*   Gluon energy loss is \(9/4\) times that of quark energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the quantitative extraction of the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) performed by the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013) by systematically evaluating the uncertainty arising from the choice of parton fragmentation functions. It builds on prior studies of single hadron and dihadron suppressions (Zhang 2007, Chen 2011, Xie 2019) and uses the same (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model for medium evolution (Hirano 2001). The results enable more precise constraints on \(\hat{q}\) by identifying the gluon fragmentation function as a major source of systematic uncertainty, guiding future extractions within frameworks like JETSCAPE.
