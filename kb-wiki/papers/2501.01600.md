# Heavy quark suppression and anisotropic flow at intermediate momentum

> In an intermediate-momentum regime where mass effects are significant, heavy quark suppression and anisotropic flow are computed to investigate the transition between the collisional and radiative energy loss. Based on the collision kernel for diffusion, elastic scattering and semi-collinear gluon-bremsstrahlung can be consistently incorporated into a Boltzmann equation that involves the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. Using the running coupling constant and the diffusion coefficient constrained by lattice QCD data, the collisional and radiative energy-loss contributions to the $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ are studied in hydrodynamically expanding thermal media. The evolution of the observables, the bulk flow effect, and the dependence on mass and centrality are discussed in noncentral heavy-ion collisions.

# Heavy quark suppression and anisotropic flow at intermediate momentum

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the intermediate-momentum regime where heavy quark mass effects are significant, the transition between collisional and radiative energy loss can be consistently described through a single transport parameter (the heavy quark diffusion coefficient), but the qualitative features of the two mechanisms are distinguishable in their momentum dependence of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, with the radiative effect making the observables more independent of $p_T$ compared to those determined entirely by collisional energy loss.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann equation that incorporates both a Fokker-Planck operator for elastic scattering and a semi-collinear gluon-bremsstrahlung term, with the radiative emission rate proportional to the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient. The heavy quark diffusion coefficient at zero momentum is constrained by lattice QCD data, while its momentum and temperature dependence are determined by leading-log results and running coupling constant, respectively. The Boltzmann equation is coupled to a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic background (MUSIC code) for noncentral PbPb collisions with smooth initial conditions from an optical Glauber model.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: $m = 1.5$ GeV; bottom quark mass: $m = 4.5$ GeV
- Spatial diffusion coefficient range: $(2\pi T_c)D_s(T_c) = 2-3$, constrained by lattice QCD data
- Critical temperature: $T_c \sim 157$ MeV (HotQCD Collaboration, 2018)
- Initial hydrodynamic time: $t_0 = 0.6$ fm; maximum energy density: $e_0 = 100$ GeV/fm$^3$; shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta/s = 0.08$
- Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters: $R_0 = 6.62$ fm, $a = 0.546$ fm for PbPb collisions
- Impact parameters studied: $b = 3.5, 6, 10, 12.5$ fm
- Charm quark $v_2$ peaks at $\sim 3$ GeV in transverse momentum
- Ratio of bottom to charm $R_{AA}$ near $p_T \sim 20$ GeV is comparable to the measured nonprompt to prompt $D^0$-meson $R_{AA}$ ratio of $1.70 \pm 0.18$ (ALICE, 2022)
- $p_T$-integrated range for $v_2^{\rm int}$ and $R_{AA}^{\rm int}$: $4 < p_T < 20$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Hong (2023) for heavy quark suppression in Bjorken expansion to noncentral heavy-ion collisions, computing both $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ simultaneously. The approach addresses the longstanding challenge noted by Andronic (2015), Dong (2019), Apolinario (2022), and He (2022) that while suppression factors are well-reproduced by transport models, elliptic flow is often underestimated. The paper enables future quantitative comparisons with experimental data from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE at LHC, particularly for $D$ and $B$ meson measurements, though the authors note that hadronization effects (especially coalescence) and realistic initial conditions with fluctuations must be incorporated for direct comparison.
