# Shooting String Holography of Jet Quenching at RHIC and LHC

> We derive a new formula for jet energy loss using finite endpoint momentum shooting strings initial conditions in SYM plasmas to overcome the difficulties of previous falling string holographic scenarios. We apply the new formula to compute the nuclear modification factor RAA and the elliptic flow parameter v2 of light hadrons at RHIC and LHC. We show furthermore that Gauss-Bonnet quadratic curvature corrections to the AdS5 geometry improve the agreement with the recent data.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new formula for jet energy loss using finite endpoint momentum (F.E.M.) "shooting strings" in strongly coupled SYM plasmas, which overcomes the difficulties of previous falling string holographic scenarios by providing a clearer definition of instantaneous energy loss and yielding greater stopping distances; however, the simultaneous fit of RHIC and LHC central RAA data remains challenging and may require temperature uncertainties or adjustments to formation time and shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors model an energetic light quark as the endpoint of an open string in AdS5-Schwarzschild (and later with Gauss-Bonnet corrections) that starts near the black hole horizon and moves upward toward the boundary, carrying finite momentum that is gradually transferred to the rest of the string. They derive an instantaneous energy loss formula dE/dx from the null geodesic motion of the endpoint, then compute the nuclear modification factor RAA and elliptic flow v2 for pions by integrating this energy loss over a Glauber-model background with longitudinal and transverse expansion, using LO pQCD production spectra and fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss formula is dE/dx = -(π/2)√λ T² (1/ẑ₀ + πTx)², where ẑ₀ ∈ [0,1] and λ is the 't Hooft coupling.
- For conformal RAA at RHIC with λ=3, ẑ₀=1, ti=1 fm/c, Tfreeze=170 MeV, and b=3 fm, the calculation matches PHENIX data for 0-5% centrality.
- For conformal RAA at LHC with λ=0.25, ẑ₀=1, ti=1 fm/c, Tfreeze=170 MeV, and b=3 fm, the calculation matches CMS data for 0-5% centrality.
- With Gauss-Bonnet corrections at λGB=-0.2 (giving η/s=1.8/(4π)) and λ=1, the LHC central RAA is well matched; at RHIC the same parameters overpredict the data.
- The Gauss-Bonnet correction with λGB=-0.2 reduces the energy loss by up to a factor of two compared to pure AdS.
- For RHIC, using λ=4, λGB=-0.2, and ti=0.6 fm/c brings the calculation close to the data; for LHC with λ=4, λGB=-0.2, and ti=1 fm/c, a 10% temperature reduction (T→κT with κ≈0.9) is needed to match the data.
- The shear viscosity with Gauss-Bonnet corrections is η/s = (1-4λGB)/(4π), with λGB constrained to -7/36 < λGB ≤ 9/100.

## Context

This work extends the falling string holographic approach of Gubser, Gulotta, Pufu, and Rocha (2008) and Chesler, Jensen, and Karch (2008), which found Δxmax ∝ E^(1/3)T^(-4/3) but predicted too strong jet quenching at LHC. The finite endpoint momentum shooting strings introduced by Ficnar and Gubser (2013) provide a clearer definition of energy loss and greater stopping distances. The authors apply this to compute RAA and v2, finding that Gauss-Bonnet corrections improve agreement with data, and note that the temperature sensitivity of their formula, combined with uncertainties in formation time and shear viscosity, may enable simultaneous RHIC and LHC fits. This enables more realistic holographic modeling of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
