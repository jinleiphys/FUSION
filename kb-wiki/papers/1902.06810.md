# Absence of Landau-Peierls Instability in the Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave Phase of Dense QCD

> We investigate the stability of the Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave (MDCDW) phase of cold and dense QCD against collective low-energy fluctuations of the order parameter. The appearance of additional structures in the system free-energy due to the explicit breaking of the rotational and isospin symmetries by the external magnetic field and the field-induced asymmetry of the lowest Landau level modes play a crucial role in the analysis. The new structures not only affect the condensate minimum equations, but also the spectrum of the thermal fluctuations, which lacks the transverse soft modes that typically affect single-modulated inhomogeneous phases in the absence of a magnetic field. Consequently, the long-range order of the MDCDW phase is preserved at finite temperature. The lack of Landau-Peierls instabilities in the MDCDW phase makes this inhomogeneous phase of dense quark matter particularly relevant for the physics of neutron stars.

## Key claim
The Magnetic Dual Chiral Density Wave (MDCDW) phase of dense QCD in an external magnetic field does not exhibit the Landau-Peierls instability that plagues single-modulated inhomogeneous phases at finite temperature, because the magnetic field introduces new structures in the free energy that stiffen the transverse fluctuation spectrum, making it linear in all directions.

## Method
The authors derived a generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the MDCDW thermodynamic potential in powers of the order parameter and its derivatives, including new structural terms induced by the magnetic field that break rotational and isospin symmetries. They then constructed the low-energy theory of thermal fluctuations about the condensate solution by expanding the order parameter to quadratic order in a phonon fluctuation field, and computed the mean-square fluctuation amplitude to determine whether long-range order survives at finite temperature.

## Key numbers
- The phonon spectrum is linear in all directions: \(E \simeq \sqrt{v^2_z k^2_z + v_\bot^2 k_\bot^2}\), where \(v_\bot\) is nonzero only due to the magnetic-field-induced coefficients \(a^{(1)}_{i,j}\).
- The mean-square fluctuation \(\langle q^2 u^2 \rangle \simeq \pi T / (m \sqrt{v^2_z v_\bot^2})\) is finite at nonzero temperature, in contrast to the zero-field case where \(\langle u^2 \rangle \simeq (T / 4\pi v_z \zeta) \ln(v_z l_\bot / \zeta)\) diverges as \(l_\bot \to \infty\).
- The threshold temperature for fluctuation-driven loss of long-range order is \(T_h = m |v_z| |v_\bot| / \pi\); if \(T_h < T_c\) (the chiral restoration temperature), quasi-long-range order appears in the region \(T_h < T < T_c\).
- For neutron star applications, magnetic fields of order \(10^{16}\) G and higher are sufficient to ignore charged pion effects, based on quasiparticle masses of order 60 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the DCDW phase (Tatsumi, 2009) and the quarkyonic phase (Pisarski, 2015), which found Landau-Peierls instability in single-modulated phases at zero magnetic field. It also builds on the authors' prior work on the MDCDW phase (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2018) and on general arguments by Hidaka (2015) about single-modulated phases with an external vector field. The result makes the MDCDW phase a robust candidate for neutron star cores, as it avoids the instability that would otherwise destroy long-range order at any finite temperature.
