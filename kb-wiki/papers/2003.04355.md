# The effect of stout smearing on the phase diagram from multiparameter reweigthing in lattice QCD

> The phase diagram and the location of the critical endpoint (CEP) of lattice QCD with unimproved staggered fermions on a $N_t=4$ lattice was determined fifteen years ago with the multiparameter reweighting method by studying Fisher zeros. We first reproduce the old result with an exact algorithm (not known at the time) and with statistics larger by an order of magnitude. As an extension of the old analysis we introduce stout smearing in the fermion action in order to reduce the finite lattice spacing effects. First we show that increasing the smearing parameter $ρ$ the crossover at $μ= 0$ gets weaker, i.e., the leading Fisher zero gets farther away from the real axis. Furthermore as the chemical potential is increased the overlap problem gets severe sooner than in the unimproved case, therefore shrinking the range of applicability of the method. Nevertheless certain qualitative features remain, even after introducing the smearing. Namely, at small chemical potentials the Fisher zeros first get farther away from the real axis and later at around $aμ_q = 0.1 - 0.15$ they start to get closer, i.e., the crossover first gets weaker and later stronger as a function of $μ$. However, because of the more severe overlap problem the CEP is out of reach with the smeared action.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the location of the critical endpoint (CEP) for lattice QCD with unimproved staggered fermions on an Nt=4 lattice, but finds that introducing stout smearing to reduce finite lattice spacing effects makes the overlap problem worse, preventing a reliable determination of the CEP with the smeared action.

## Method

The authors used multiparameter reweighting to study Fisher zeros of the partition function in the complex β plane at finite baryochemical potential, simulating Nf=2+1 rooted staggered fermions with physical quark masses on lattices of size 6³×4, 8³×4, 10³×4, and 12³×4. They employed the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm (exact, unlike the R algorithm used in prior work) and performed infinite volume extrapolations linear in 1/V to determine whether the imaginary part of the leading Fisher zero extrapolates to zero, indicating a first-order phase transition. The analysis was repeated for four values of the stout smearing parameter ρ = {0, 0.01, 0.02, 0.03}, using both standard rooting and the geometric matching method for the fermion determinant.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint for unimproved action (ρ=0): μB^c/T = 2.28 ± 0.07 (statistical error only)
- Bare quark masses: mud = 0.0092, ms = 0.25 (same as Fodor and Katz (2004))
- Simulation β0 values for ρ = {0, 0.01, 0.02, 0.03}: {5.188, 5.171, 5.154, 5.137} respectively
- Number of configurations for the largest volume (Ns=12): 49020 (ρ=0), 26653 (ρ=0.01), 26861 (ρ=0.02), 56107 (ρ=0.03)
- On the 12³×4 lattice at ρ=0, the slope of the imaginary part of the leading Fisher zero at μ=0 is positive within 3σ
- At small chemical potentials, Fisher zeros first move away from the real axis until around aμq = 0.1–0.15, then start moving closer

## Context

This work reproduces and extends the seminal determination of the CEP for Nt=4 lattice QCD by Fodor and Katz (2004), using an exact RHMC algorithm (Clark 2006) not available at that time and with an order of magnitude larger statistics. The study also implements the geometric matching method introduced by Giordano et al. (2019) to address analyticity issues of the rooted staggered determinant. The results show that stout smearing, while reducing UV effects, weakens the crossover at μ=0 (consistent with the critical line of the Columbia plot moving away from physical masses as shown by de Forcrand (2006) and Varnhorst (2015)), and exacerbates the overlap problem, making the CEP unreachable with this method. The paper concludes that the overlap problem, not the sign problem, is the primary bottleneck for extending reweighting studies closer to the continuum limit.
