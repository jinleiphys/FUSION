# Dynamical instabilities of warm $npe^-$ matter: the $δ$ meson effects

> The effects of $δ$ mesons on the dynamical instabilities of cold and warm nuclear and stellar matter at subsaturation densities are studied in the framework of relativistic mean-field hadron models (NL3, NL$ρ$ and NL$ρδ$) with the inclusion of the electromagnetic field. The distillation effect and the spinodals for all the models considered are discussed. The crust-core transition density and pressure are obtained as a function of temperature for $β$-equilibrium matter with and without neutrino trapping. An estimation of the size of the clusters formed in the non-homogeneous phase is made. It is shown that cluster sizes increase with temperature. The effects of the $δ$-meson on the instability region are larger for low temperatures, very asymmetric matter and densities close to the spinodal surface. It increases the distillation effect above $\sim 0.4ρ_0$ and has the opposite effect below that density.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the scalar isovector δ meson in relativistic mean-field models increases the distillation effect (proton enrichment of clusters) at densities above approximately 0.4ρ₀ (∼0.06 fm⁻³) and reduces it below that density, with the effect being larger for low temperatures, very asymmetric matter, and densities close to the spinodal surface.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic non-linear Walecka model in the Mean-Field Approximation within the Vlasov formalism to study nuclear collective modes of npe⁻ matter at finite temperature. They employed three parametrizations (NL3, NLρ, and NLρδ) to compare the effects of the δ meson on the dynamical spinodal surfaces, the ratio of proton to neutron density fluctuations (distillation effect), and the most unstable modes that determine cluster sizes in the liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀: 0.160 fm⁻³ for NLρ and NLρδ; 0.148 fm⁻³ for NL3
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 30.335 MeV (NLρ), 30.711 MeV (NLρδ), 37.344 MeV (NL3)
- Symmetry energy slope L: 84.510 MeV (NLρ), 102.664 MeV (NLρδ), 118.320 MeV (NL3)
- For β-equilibrium matter with trapped neutrinos (Y_L=0.4) at T=0 MeV, crust-core transition density: 0.092 fm⁻³ (NLρ), 0.091 fm⁻³ (NLρδ), 0.081 fm⁻³ (NL3)
- Corresponding transition pressure at T=0 MeV: 1.242 MeV·fm⁻³ (NLρ), 1.254 MeV·fm⁻³ (NLρδ), 0.995 MeV·fm⁻³ (NL3)
- For neutrino-free matter, the non-homogeneous phase disappears above T ∼ 3 MeV; for matter with trapped neutrinos, the EoS crosses the spinodal for T < 13.2 MeV (NLρδ and NLρ) and T < 11.6 MeV (NL3)
- The spinodal envelope for NLρδ and NLρ is characterized by k ∼ 100 MeV; for NL3, k ∼ 75 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the δ meson effect on asymmetric nuclear matter by Liu (2002) and Greco (2003) and on pasta phases by Avancini (2009), applying the relativistic Vlasov formalism previously used by Providencia (2006) and Lucilia (2006) to warm matter. The results are compared with non-relativistic Skyrme model predictions from Ducoin (2008) and with the NL3 parametrization from Lalazissis (1997). The findings enable better constraints on the equation of state at finite temperature for neutron star crust physics, particularly for understanding cluster formation in proto-neutron stars and its potential impact on neutrino interactions during supernova dynamics.
