# Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics

> We discuss preliminary work on a formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics that uses reflection-positive Euclidean Green functions or their generating functionals as phenomenological input. We discuss the construction of a Poincare invariant S-matrix from matrix element of exp(- βH).

# Euclidean formulation of relativistic quantum mechanics

## Key claim
The paper presents a preliminary framework for constructing a fully relativistic quantum theory using reflection-positive Euclidean Green functions or generating functionals as input, without requiring locality or explicit analytic continuation. The approach is tested only on a solvable two-nucleon model, and the authors state it "warrants further investigation" rather than claiming general applicability.

## Method
The authors construct Hilbert space vectors as wave functionals of Euclidean fields, with an inner product defined via the Euclidean generating functional and the time-reflection operator. Mass eigenstates are obtained from a mass operator expressed as derivatives of a translation operator acting on these functionals, and single-particle states are built by projecting onto irreducible representations of the Poincaré group. Scattering states are constructed using Haag-Ruelle injection operators and the Kato-Birman invariance principle, replacing the Hamiltonian with a compact operator \( e^{-\beta H} \) to enable polynomial approximation.

## Key numbers
- Test calculations used a solvable quantum mechanical model of the two-nucleon system.
- Convergence to exact transition matrix elements was exhibited for relative momenta between about 100 MeV up to 2 GeV.
- The test used narrow wave packets, a large finite \( n \), and a Chebyshev polynomial expansion of \( e^{inx} \).

## Context
This work extends the Euclidean axioms of quantum field theory, as formulated by Osterwalder and Schrader (1970s) and Fröhlich (1970s), by removing the requirement of locality for reconstruction of the quantum theory. It builds on the Haag-Ruelle scattering formalism (Jost, 1965; Simon, 1979) and the two-Hilbert-space formulation of scattering theory (Simon, 1979). The framework enables calculations of Poincaré-invariant S-matrix elements directly in Euclidean space without analytic continuation, and the authors note that cluster properties are relatively easy to satisfy in this approach.
