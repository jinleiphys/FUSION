# Spectral function for overoccupied gluodynamics from classical lattice simulations

> We study the spectral properties of an overoccupied gluonic system far from equilibrium. Using classical Yang-Mills simulations and linear response theory, we determine the statistical and spectral functions. We measure dispersion relations and damping rates of transversally and longitudinally polarized excitations in the gluonic plasma, and also study further structures in the spectral function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the spectral function of an overoccupied gluonic system far from equilibrium can be extracted using classical lattice simulations and linear response theory, and that the resulting dispersion relations and damping rates agree reasonably well with hard thermal loop (HTL) expectations when a scale separation between hard and soft modes exists, though some observables related to the effective temperature of soft modes show less agreement.

## Method

The authors perform classical Yang-Mills (CYM) simulations on a lattice in temporal gauge, using the standard Hamiltonian lattice formulation with link variables. They extract the spectral function by applying a linearized perturbation (a delta-function current) to the system, evolving the resulting fluctuation via linearized equations of motion, and measuring the correlation of the perturbed field with the current to obtain the retarded propagator and thus the spectral function. The statistical function is obtained from the symmetric two-point correlator of the classical fields. The test system is an isotropic self-similar UV cascade, initialized with an overoccupied gluon distribution up to a characteristic hard momentum scale.

## Key numbers

- The scaling behavior of the momentum distribution follows \( f(t,p) = t^{-4/7} f_\mathrm{s}(p/t^{1/7}) \), with the hard scale growing as \( p_\mathrm{max} \sim t^{1/7} \) and the Debye mass scaling as \( m \sim t^{-1/7} \).
- The ratio of the plasmon mass to the asymptotic mass is measured as \( \omega_\mathrm{pl} / m_\infty = 0.96 \), compared to the HTL prediction of \( \sqrt{2/3} \approx 0.82 \).
- The effective temperature for soft fields scales as \( T_* \sim t^{-3/7} \), and the measured damping rate is consistent with this time scaling.
- Simulations are performed at \( Qt = 1500 \) for the primary results, with lattice spacings \( Qa = 0.47 \) and \( Qa = 0.7 \) used for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the classical lattice approach to spectral functions in overoccupied gluodynamics, building on the linear response algorithm developed by Kurkela et al. (2016). It tests the HTL approximation (Pisarski, 1993; Schulz, 1993) in a regime where scale separation can be varied, finding good agreement for spectral functions but discrepancies in infrared equal-time correlators. The method enables future calculations of transport coefficients (heavy quark diffusion, shear viscosity, jet quenching) in nonequilibrium plasmas and can be applied to anisotropic systems such as the 2-dimensional glasma relevant to the initial stage of heavy ion collisions.
