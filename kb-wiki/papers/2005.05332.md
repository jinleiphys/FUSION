# Resonant Neutrino Self-Interactions

> If neutrinos have self-interactions, these will induce scatterings between astrophysical and cosmic neutrinos. Prior work proposed to look for possible resulting resonance features in astrophysical neutrino spectra in order to seek a neutrino self-interaction which can be either diagonal in the neutrino flavor space or couple different neutrino flavors. The calculation of the astrophysical spectra involves either a Monte Carlo simulation or a computationally intensive numerical integration of an integro-partial-differential equation. As a result only limited regions of the neutrino self-interaction parameter space have been explored, and only flavor-diagonal self-interactions have been considered. Here, we present a fully analytic form for the astrophysical neutrino spectra for arbitrary neutrino number and arbitrary self-coupling matrix that accurately obtains the resonance features in the observable neutrino spectra. The results can be applied to calculations of the diffuse supernova neutrino background and of the spectrum from high-energy astrophysical neutrino sources. We illustrate with a few examples.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully analytic form for astrophysical neutrino spectra with arbitrary neutrino self-interactions, accurately capturing resonance features without requiring Monte Carlo simulations or computationally intensive numerical integration. The authors hedge that this analytic solution only holds when each cosmic neutrino species is cold (non-relativistic), and that the delta-function approximation for the resonance cross section requires the coupling to satisfy \(g \lesssim 0.5 [\Delta E/(1\ \mathrm{MeV}) / (E_R/(25\ \mathrm{MeV}))]^{1/2}\) for detector resolution \(\Delta E\).

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation for astrophysical neutrinos propagating through a cosmic neutrino background, treating resonant self-interactions via a scalar mediator. They approximate the Breit-Wigner scattering cross section as a Dirac delta function at the resonant energy \(E_R = m_\phi^2/(2m_\nu)\), then derive closed-form expressions for the specific flux by integrating around each resonance and applying boundary conditions. The general solution for multiple neutrino species with an arbitrary self-coupling matrix \(g_{ij}\) is obtained by solving sequentially from the highest resonant energy downward, accounting for cascading through multiple resonances.

## Key numbers
- Resonant energy: \(E_R = [m_\phi^2/(2 m_\nu)]c^2\), with example values \(m_\phi c^2 = 1\ \mathrm{keV}\), \(m_\nu c^2 = 2\times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{eV}\) giving \(E_R \approx 25\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Coupling threshold for delta-function approximation: \(g \lesssim 0.5 [\Delta E/(1\ \mathrm{MeV}) / (E_R/(25\ \mathrm{MeV}))]^{1/2}\) for detector resolution \(\Delta E \approx 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Super-K)
- Resonant cross section: \(\sigma_R = (\hbar c)^2 \pi g^2/(m_\phi c^2)^2\)
- Neutrino mass ranges: normal hierarchy \(m_1c^2 \in [0, 0.030]\ \mathrm{eV}\), \(m_2c^2 \in [0.0087, 0.031]\ \mathrm{eV}\), \(m_3c^2 \in [0.050, 0.059]\ \mathrm{eV}\); inverted hierarchy \(m_1c^2 \in [0.050, 0.052]\ \mathrm{eV}\), \(m_2c^2 \in [0.051, 0.053]\ \mathrm{eV}\), \(m_3c^2 \in [0, 0.015]\ \mathrm{eV}\)
- Planck upper bound on sum of neutrino masses: \(\sum_j m_j c^2 < 0.12\ \mathrm{eV}\)
- Supernova total energy in neutrinos: \(E_{\mathrm{tot}} = 3\times 10^{46}\ \mathrm{J}\)
- Supernova temperature range: \(4\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq k_B T_{\mathrm{SN}} \leq 8\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Minimum neutrino energy for inverse beta decay: \(E_\nu^{\mathrm{min}} = 1.806\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- IceCube observation time: \(T = 988\ \mathrm{days}\)
- HEAN power law index: \(\gamma = 2.53\) with \(E_0 = 100\ \mathrm{TeV}\)
- HEAN flux normalization: \(E_0^2 \Phi_e(E_0)/(4\pi) = C_0 \Phi_0\) with \(C_0 = 3\times 10^{-18}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\mathrm{cm}^{-2}\mathrm{s}^{-1}\mathrm{sr}^{-1}\) and \(\Phi_0 = 1.66\)

## Context
This work extends prior numerical approaches that explored only limited regions of neutrino self-interaction parameter space, such as those assuming a universal self-coupling matrix (Ng and Beacom 2014; Ioka and Murase 2014), a diagonal self-coupling matrix (Cherry et al. 2014), no flavor dynamics (Blinnikov et al. 2014; Das and Sen 2020), or particular mediator masses and couplings (Blennow et al. 2014; Cherry et al. 2014; Kelly and Machado 2018). The analytic solution enables systematic exploration of arbitrary self-coupling matrices for both the diffuse supernova neutrino background (DSNB) and high-energy astrophysical neutrino (HEAN) sources, with forecasted constraints for Super-Kamiokande with gadolinium and IceCube. The results can be applied to test models that propose neutrino self-interactions to resolve the Hubble tension (Kreisch et al. 2019) or to reconcile eV-scale sterile neutrinos with Planck constraints (Archidiacono et al. 2013; Hannestad et al. 2013).
