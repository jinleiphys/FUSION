# Thermal Charm and Charmonium Production in Quark Gluon Plasma

> We study the effect of thermal charm production on charmonium regeneration in high energy nuclear collisions. By solving the kinetic equations for charm quark and charmonium distributions in Pb+Pb collisions, we calculate the global and differential nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}(N_{part})$ and $R{AA}(p_t)$ for $J/Ψ$s. Due to the thermal charm production in hot medium, the charmonium production source changes from the initially created charm quarks at SPS, RHIC and LHC to the thermally produced charm quarks at Future Circular Collider (FCC), and the $J/Ψ$ suppression ($R_{AA}<1$) observed so far will be replaced by a strong enhancement ($R_{AA}>1$) at FCC at low transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at Future Circular Collider (FCC) energies (√sNN = 39 TeV), thermal charm quark production inside the quark-gluon plasma becomes so significant that it reverses the J/ψ suppression (RAA < 1) observed at SPS, RHIC, and LHC into a strong enhancement (RAA > 1) at low transverse momentum, a transition that depends on the medium temperature and lifetime being sufficient for thermal charm production to dominate over initial charm production.

## Method

The authors solve coupled rate equations for charm quark density and a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium distributions in Pb+Pb collisions, using ideal hydrodynamics to provide the local temperature and fluid velocity of the medium. The charm quark production and annihilation rates are calculated from perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order (MNR-NLO), with thermal masses for gluons and light quarks and a temperature-dependent coupling fitted to lattice QCD. The charmonium dissociation cross section is taken from the operator product expansion in vacuum and scaled by the temperature-dependent squared radius from a potential model with lattice heavy-quark potential, while regeneration is obtained via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- Initial charm quark cross sections at mid-rapidity: dσc̄c/dη|η=0 = 0.7 mb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, 1.0 mb at 5.5 TeV, and 2.5 mb at 39 TeV (extracted from FONLL simulation).
- Initial temperature of the medium in central collisions at FCC: T0 = 840 MeV at √sNN = 39 TeV.
- At √sNN = 5.5 TeV, thermal charm production leads to a 15% increase in total charm quark number at the end of the QGP phase.
- At √sNN = 39 TeV, thermal charm production enhances the total charm quark number by 50% at the end of the QGP phase.
- In very central collisions at √sNN = 5.5 TeV, the J/ψ RAA increases from 0.26 to 0.37 (a ~40% enhancement) when thermal charm production is included.
- In very central collisions at √sNN = 39 TeV, the J/ψ RAA increases from 0.2 to 0.75 (a factor of 3 enhancement) due to thermal charm production.
- At FCC energy with shadowing switched off, the J/ψ RAA at low pt can reach 1.6.
- Charged multiplicity parameterization: dNch/dη = 1600 at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, 2000 at 5.5 TeV, and 3700 at 39 TeV.
- Initial time for hydrodynamics: τ0 = 0.6 fm/c at √sNN = 2.76 and 5.5 TeV, and 0.3 fm/c at 39 TeV.
- Freeze-out energy density: 60 MeV/fm3.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions, where the mechanism shifts from initial production at SPS energies (Hufner, Vogt, Zhu) to initial production plus regeneration at RHIC and LHC energies (Thews, Rapp, Yan, Strikland, Blaizot, Katz). The authors specifically build on earlier calculations of in-medium charm quark production at leading order (LO1, LO2, LO3) and next-to-leading order (Zhang), and use the EKS98 model for nuclear shadowing. The key advance is predicting that at FCC energies, thermal charm production in the QGP becomes the dominant source for charmonium regeneration, enabling J/ψ to serve as a more sensitive probe of medium properties. The results also show that the deep valley in RAA(Npart) at peripheral collisions and the transition from suppression to enhancement at low pt are distinctive signatures of this mechanism.
