# Neutron channelling in a magnetic tube trap

## Key claim
Neutron motion in a magnetic tube trap is governed by an effective spin-independent potential proportional to the square of the magnetic field strength, which arises only in second-order perturbation theory because the first-order contribution (the average magnetic field) vanishes. The resulting transverse motion is quantized and described by a cylindrically symmetric harmonic oscillator, but this treatment applies only for energy levels below the height of the effective potential barrier.

## Method
The author solves the Schrödinger equation for an ultracold neutron in a cylindrical magnetic tube trap, starting from the spin-dependent interaction with the magnetic field. Using the fact that the magnetic field inside the tube is independent of the axial coordinate and that the average field over the tube cross-section is zero, the problem is transformed via the Green function method into an effective Schrödinger equation with a spin-independent potential proportional to B²(ρ). For a magnetic field that decreases linearly toward the trap center (B ∝ ρ), the resulting equation reduces to that of a two-dimensional cylindrically symmetric harmonic oscillator, whose eigenvalues and eigenfunctions are obtained analytically.

## Key numbers
- Typical radius of the magnetic trap: ρ ∼ 10⁻⁴ cm (from prior work cited as [1])
- Ultracold neutron wavelength: λ ∼ 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁶ cm
- For magnetic field √⟨B²_max⟩ = 10⁴ gauss, the transverse oscillation frequency Ω_trans ≈ 10⁴ to 10⁵ s⁻¹
- For a neutron speed of 10² cm/s in a 1 cm tube, flight time τ ∼ 10⁻² s, giving Ω_trans τ ≫ 1 (many oscillations occur)
- Critical angle for capture: for neutron speed 1 m/s and B ≈ 10⁴ gauss, ϑ_cr ≈ 1 rad; for neutron speed 10 m/s, ϑ_cr ≈ 10⁻² rad

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum effects in neutron traps from the one-dimensional gravitational trap studied by prior authors (referenced as [3]) to the two-dimensional transverse motion in a magnetic tube trap. The analysis shows that neutron channelling in a magnetic tube trap is analogous to axial channelling of charged particles in crystals, and that bent magnetic tube traps could be used for focusing wide beams of ultracold neutrons. The periodic arrangement of such traps, proposed in earlier work (referenced as [1]), would strengthen this analogy and enable experimental studies of neutron level spectra through angular distribution measurements.
