# Eta Production in the pp Scattering

> Total cross section for pp->pp eta process has been calculated in meson exchange model. Eta production T-matrices are from our PWA. Initial and final state interactions are disregarded for the moment, due to large uncertainties. Preliminary results do not show rho meson exchange dominance.

## Key claim
The paper presents a meson-exchange model calculation of the total cross section for pp → ppη, using eta-nucleon elastic T-matrices from the authors' own partial-wave analysis (PWA), but explicitly disregards initial and final state interactions (FSI) due to large theoretical uncertainties in their treatment.

## Method
The authors applied two-body T-matrices from the Zagreb-UCLA-ANL meson-nucleon PWA to the three-body pp → ppη process, which is an isospin 1/2 reaction. The production mechanism was modeled via meson exchange and decay of nucleon resonances, with contributions from π, η, and ρ meson exchanges considered. Initial and final state interactions were omitted, and the model was tested for energies from 100 to 500 MeV, though experimental data in this range are scarce.

## Key numbers
- The model was applied to the pp → ppη process for energies from 100 to 500 MeV (the range where FSI is significant near threshold and where the model is most conveniently testable).
- The paper reports "enormous discrepancy below few MeV" due to the omission of final state interaction, but no specific numerical cross-section values are given in the text.

## Context
This work extends the Zagreb-UCLA-ANL meson-nucleon PWA (Bat95) to three-body processes, aiming to reduce uncertainties in two-body T-matrices. It contradicts models that predict rho meson exchange dominance (RhoM), as the preliminary results do not show such dominance. The authors plan to calculate differential cross sections and total cross sections for pn → ηpn to improve the picture, with completed results to be reported elsewhere.
