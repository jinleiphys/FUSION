# A Study of $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ Nature : Compact v.s. Molecule

> A central question in exotic-hadron physics is their internal structure whether these states are loosely bound hadronic molecules or compact multiquark configurations. To shed light on this issue, we develop a model that incorporates mixing between hadronic-molecular and compact multiquark components. We then apply this framework to the specific case of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ and analyze the peak structure in the $D^0D^0π^+$ invariant-mass spectrum reported by LHCb. We find that a scenario based on a predominantly compact tetraquark provides the best fitted solution which can explain the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$. We also find that the model admits two more solutions of comparable quality, both of which imply that the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ is a molecular state: (1) the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ is a $D^{*+}D^0$ molecule and there is a $D^{*0}D^+$ molecular state in addition; (2) the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ is a $D^{*0}D^+$ molecule and an aditional $D^{*+}D^0$ molecular state is found below $D^0D^0π^+$ threshold. These molecular states are not simple $I = 0$ states, but mixtures of $I = 0$ and $I = 1$ states. We show that all three scenarios are also consistent with the experimentally observed near-threshold $D^0D^0$ and $D^0D^+$ invariant-mass distributions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ can be explained by three different scenarios of comparable statistical quality: a predominantly compact tetraquark, a $D^{*+}D^0$ molecule, or a $D^{*0}D^+$ molecule. The authors hedge that current experimental data cannot decisively distinguish between these interpretations, as the $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}$ values differ by less than 0.1.

## Method

The authors develop a low-energy effective model incorporating mixing between hadronic-molecular ($DD^*$) and compact tetraquark components via a contact-interaction Lagrangian with a compact $T_{cc}^+$ field. They fit the model parameters to the 200 keV-binned $D^0D^0\pi^+$ invariant-mass spectrum from LHCb in the energy region $3.873 < m_{D^0 D^0 \pi^+} < 3.877$ GeV using a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation, then compute the $D^0D^0$ and $D^0D^+$ invariant-mass distributions from the fitted parameters to test consistency.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit Mol.+Compact scenario has $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 15.0/(19-6) = 1.15$, with the compact state mass $m_{4q} = 3.8757$ GeV and coupling $g_0 = \pm 0.14$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
* The Mol.1 scenario (pure molecular) has $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 16.0/(19-6) = 1.23$, with contact couplings $C_0 = -22.9$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $C_1 = -20.2$ GeV$^{-2}$.
* The Mol.2 scenario (pure molecular) has $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 15.9/(19-6) = 1.22$, with contact couplings $C_0 = -29.5$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $C_1 = -23.6$ GeV$^{-2}$.
* In the M+C scenario, the bound state lies 311 keV below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold, and the compact state shifts downward by 954 keV due to coupling to the $DD^*$ channel.
* In Mol.1, State 1 is bound by 81 keV below the $D^{*0}D^+$ threshold and State 2 by 338 keV below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold.
* In Mol.2, State 1' is bound by 372 keV below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold (with a binding energy of 1.78 MeV relative to the $D^{*0}D^+$ channel) and State 2' by 6.16 MeV below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ beyond purely molecular models (e.g., Albaladejo (2021), Du (2021)) by incorporating mixing with a compact tetraquark component, following the approach used for the $X(3872)$ where a compact core coexists with a molecular component (Yamaguchi (2019), Esposito (2025)). The results enable future higher-statistics data from LHCb to distinguish between compact, molecular, and mixed interpretations of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$, with the Mol.2 scenario predicting a $D^{*0}D^+$ molecular state potentially observable in the $D^0D^+\gamma$ radiative decay mode.
