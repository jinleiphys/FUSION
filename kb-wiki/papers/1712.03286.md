# Effects of energy conservation on equilibrium properties of hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> Based on the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, which includes relativistic scalar and vector potentials on baryons, we consider a $N-Δ-π$ system in a box with periodic boundary conditions to study the effects of energy conservation in particle production and absorption processes on the equilibrium properties of the system. The density and temperature of the matter in the box are taken to be similar to the hot dense matter formed in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. We find that to maintain the equilibrium numbers of $N$, $Δ$ and $π$, which depend on the mean-field potentials of $N$ and $Δ$, requires the inclusion of these potentials in the energy conservation condition that determines the momenta of outgoing particles after a scattering or decay process. We further find that the baryon scalar potentials mainly affect the $Δ$ and pion equilibrium numbers, while the baryon vector potentials have considerable effects on the effective charged pion ratio at equilibrium. Our results thus indicate that it is essential to include in the transport model the effect of potentials in the energy conservation of a scattering or decay process, which is ignored in most transport models, for studying pion production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

To correctly reproduce the equilibrium numbers of nucleons, Δ resonances, and pions in a hot asymmetric nuclear matter box, the mean-field potentials of baryons must be included in the energy conservation condition that determines the momenta of outgoing particles after scattering or decay processes; most transport models ignore this effect.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model to simulate a N-Δ-π system confined in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. The system was initialized with densities and temperature resembling hot dense matter formed in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions, and the baryon mean-field potentials were taken from the nonlinear relativistic mean-field NLρ model. The box evolution was run for three cases of energy conservation: including both scalar and vector potentials (S+V), including only scalar potential (S), and including no potentials (free), and the resulting equilibrium particle numbers and momentum distributions were compared to thermal model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density ρ = 0.24 fm⁻³ (1.5ρ₀) and isospin asymmetry δ_like = 0.2 were used for the box system.
- The resulting mean-field potentials were: scalar potential g_σσ = 292.2 MeV, isoscalar-vector potential g_ωω⁰ = 256.6 MeV, and isovector-vector potential g_ρρ₃⁰ = -7.0 MeV.
- Initial temperature was T = 60 MeV; in the "free" case (no potentials), the equilibrium temperature dropped to 51.6 MeV.
- Omitting the vector potential reduced the effective charged pion ratio (π⁻/π⁺)_like by 26.7% (from 3.0 to 2.2).
- Omitting both scalar and vector potentials reduced the effective pion number π_like by a factor of about 2.2 (from 15.42 to 6.85).
- Increasing the ΔΔρ coupling constant from g_ρ to 3g_ρ reduced π_like by 3.4% and (π⁻/π⁺)_like by 17.3%.

## Context

This work extends the study of energy conservation effects in transport models first highlighted by Song (2014) and Cozma (2016) using non-relativistic approaches, by employing a relativistic framework (RVUU) that includes both scalar and vector mean-field potentials. The results demonstrate that neglecting potentials in the energy conservation condition for N+N↔N+Δ and Δ↔N+π processes leads to incorrect equilibrium particle numbers and charged pion ratios, which is critical for interpreting pion production data from heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. This study complements the transport code comparison project by Xu (2016) and provides a foundation for more reliable modeling of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio.
