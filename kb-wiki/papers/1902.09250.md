# A cranked self-consistent mean-field description of the triaxially deformed rotational bands in 138Nd

> The survey of different configurations near Fermi surface of 138Nd results in 12 lowest configurations, at both positive- and negative-deformations. These are calculated to be the energetically lowest configurations. The results show that, for both EDFs, the rotational states based on positive-minimum, which is at gamma~35, are lower than the respective configurations with negative-deformation. The general trends of the spin-versus-omega curve, and the energy-versus-spin curve reproduce well those of the experimental data. Further, for the observed bands `T1-T8', the calculated results using SLy4L allows the configurations of the observed bands to be assigned. The calculations predict transitional quadrupole moments, which can be used to compare with future experimental data. The current cranked self-consistent mean-field calculations of the near-yrast high-spin rotational bands in 138Nd reproduce well the experimental data. The results suggest that the experimentally observed bands can be assigned to the calculated bands with various configurations at the positive-deformation. The predictions of the current calculations are complementary to that of the well-know macroscopic-microscopic calculations, both of which await future experiment to verify.

# A cranked self-consistent mean-field description of the triaxially deformed rotational bands in 138Nd

## Key claim

The cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations reproduce the experimental rotational bands in 138Nd and allow configuration assignments for bands T1-T8, but the calculated excitation energies systematically underestimate the observed values by approximately 1.5 MeV for the SLy4L energy density functional, and the interpretations of structural changes along the bands differ from those of previous macroscopic-microscopic calculations.

## Method

Cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations were performed using the symmetry-unrestricted solver HFODD (version 2.49t) with Skyrme energy density functionals SLy4L (including time-odd terms via Landau parameters) and SkM* (time-odd terms determined by local gauge invariance). The single-particle Hamiltonian was expanded in 969 deformed harmonic oscillator basis states, and calculations with constraints on quadrupole moments Q20 and Q22 were first performed, then released to obtain unconstrained solutions at local minima. Parity and signature symmetries were enforced throughout, and the ground state was calculated using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The positive-gamma minimum is at gamma approximately 35 degrees for both energy density functionals
- For SLy4L, the positive-gamma minimum is deeper than the negative-gamma minimum by approximately 3.0 MeV in energy; for SkM*, the energy difference is approximately 1.5 MeV
- The calculated ground state total energy is -1145.207 MeV for SLy4L and -1141.870 MeV for SkM*
- The calculated ground state quadrupole moments are Q20 = 2.62 eb, Q22 = 0.00 eb for SLy4L, and Q20 = 2.90 eb, Q22 = 1.15 eb for SkM*
- The calculated spin values for the rotational bands range from approximately 20 to 45 hbar for rotational frequencies above 0.5 MeV
- The alignment (backbending) for configuration pg01 occurs at hbar-omega approximately 0.7 MeV for SLy4L and approximately 0.6 MeV for SkM*
- The experimental T7 band upbends at hbar-omega approximately 0.75 MeV; the calculations underestimate this frequency by about 0.15 MeV for SLy4L and 0.25 MeV for SkM*
- Transitional quadrupole moments for configuration pg01 at hbar-omega = 0.6 MeV are Qt = 2.7 eb (positive-gamma) and 5.4 eb (negative-gamma)
- The calculated excitation energies systematically underestimate the observed values by approximately 1.5 MeV for SLy4L

## Context

This work extends the cranked self-consistent mean-field approach to the triaxially deformed nucleus 138Nd, where previous macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Bengtsson and collaborators (1985) and Petr and collaborators (2013, 2015) had predicted pronounced static triaxial deformation and suggested possible re-orientation of the rotational axis. The present calculations provide alternative interpretations of the structural changes in the rotational bands, differing from the macroscopic-microscopic model by attributing slope changes in energy curves to neutron alignment rather than a transition from positive- to negative-gamma deformation. The predicted transitional quadrupole moments enable future lifetime measurements to distinguish between these interpretations, and the results are complementary to the macroscopic-microscopic calculations, both awaiting experimental verification.
