# Quartets of superdeformed bands and supersymmetry breaking

> We examine the prediction of supersymmetric quantum mechanics that bands with identical gamma-ray energies occur in quartets. The experimental data suggest that this scenario is actually realized in nature. In the $A=150$ mass region, four known pairs of isospectral bands can be grouped in two quartets, while there are indications of such patterns around $A=190$. We introduce a small supersymmetry breaking, necessary to describe the details of the data. # Quartets of superdeformed bands and supersymmetry breaking

## Key claim
The paper argues that supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) predicts that identical gamma-ray bands in superdeformed nuclei should occur in quartets of four isospectral sequences across three neighboring nuclei (A, A+1, A+2), and that two such quartets are actually realized in the A=150 mass region, with indications of similar patterns around A=190. However, the quartets are not perfectly isospectral, requiring a small supersymmetry breaking to describe the data.

## Method
The authors group four known isospectral superdeformed bands in the A=150 region into two quartets: one comprising bands in 152Dy, 151Tb (two bands), and 150Gd; another comprising bands in 148Gd, 147Gd (two bands), and 146Gd. They introduce a Hamiltonian with a spin-orbit (or pseudo-spin-orbit) term and an L-dependent moment-of-inertia correction to model the supersymmetry breaking, and derive relations for transition strengths and quadrupole moments among the four bands using angular momentum algebra with a pseudo-spin-1/2 coupling scheme.

## Key numbers
- For the 152Dy/151Tb/150Gd quartet: epsilon = -b/2 = -6.3 +/- 1.3 keV, with a0 = 47 +/- 1 keV
- For the 148Gd/147Gd/146Gd quartet: epsilon = -b/2 = 8.3 +/- 0.6 keV, with a0 = 55 +/- 3 keV
- For the suggested Z=80 quartet (192Hg/193Hg/194Hg): epsilon = -0.05 +/- 0.48 keV and b = 8.85 +/- 2.35 keV (with one spin assignment); alternatively epsilon = 9.3 +/- 0.42 keV and b = -9.85 +/- 2.19 keV (with spins shifted by two units)
- Gamma-ray energies in the 152Dy/151Tb/150Gd quartet range from approximately 602 keV (L=24 in 152Dy) to 1449 keV (L=60 in 152Dy), with energy differences between corresponding bands (Delta E_gamma_01 and Delta E_gamma_12) typically within a few keV
- Gamma-ray energies in the 148Gd/147Gd/146Gd quartet range from approximately 700 keV (L=26 in 148Gd) to 1580 keV (L=58 in 148Gd), with deviations growing larger above L=46 due to a level crossing

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Amado et al., 1991) that identical gamma-ray bands in superdeformed nuclei can be described by supersymmetric quantum mechanics, now showing that all four bands of a predicted SSQM quartet are observable rather than two being at unobservably high excitation. The paper contrasts with the approach of Ragnarsson (1991), who noted similar energy relations among Gadolinium isotopes but without the SSQM framework and with different spin assignments. The results enable experimental tests of spin assignments and transition rate predictions using upcoming facilities like EUROBALL and GAMMASPHERE, and suggest a connection between SSQM and more microscopic descriptions of superdeformation.
