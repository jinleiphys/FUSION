# Recent developments in chiral dynamics of hadrons and hadrons in nuclei

> In this talk I present recent developments in the field of hadronic physics and hadrons in the nuclear medium. I review the unitary chiral approach to meson baryon interaction and address the topics of the two dynamically generated $Λ(1405)$ resonances, with experiments testing it, the $Λ(1520)$ and $Δ(1700)$ resonances, plus the $Λ(1520)$, $Σ(1385)$ and $ω$ in the nuclear medium.

# Recent developments in chiral dynamics of hadrons and hadrons in nuclei

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach to meson-baryon interaction dynamically generates several known resonances (including two distinct Λ(1405) states, the Λ(1520), and the Δ(1700)) from the interaction of the octet of pseudoscalar mesons with the octet and decuplet of baryons, but the detailed properties of these resonances, such as the Λ(1520) radiative decay to Σγ, suggest that additional three-quark components beyond the purely dynamical generation may be needed to fully reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors use the N/D method adapted for chiral dynamics: they take the lowest-order chiral Lagrangians as the dynamical interaction, implement unitarity in coupled channels to obtain the imaginary part of the inverse T-matrix from the phase space of intermediate states, and apply a dispersion relation to obtain the real part, with a subtraction constant of natural size. For the octet-of-mesons with decuplet-of-baryons interaction, the S-wave transition amplitudes take the form V_ij = -(1/4f²) C_ij (k⁰ + k'⁰), with coefficients C_ij tabulated for all strangeness and charge combinations.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1520) width at nuclear matter density ρ₀ is about five times bigger than its free width (the free width is not given numerically in the text, but the factor of ~5 at ρ=ρ₀ is stated).
- For the Δ(1700) dynamically generated from the octet-decuplet interaction, the couplings to Δπ, Σ*K, and Δη in I=3/2 are proportional to 1, 11.56, and 4.84 respectively, compared to 1, 2/5, and 1/5 if the Δ(1700) belonged to an SU(3) decuplet, differences of a factor 400–900 depending on the channel.
- The ω meson in the nuclear medium requires a width of about 90 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, with no need for a mass shift, based on reanalysis of the γA → ω(π⁰γ)A' reaction.
- The K⁻p → π⁺π⁻Λ cross section at the peak is a factor two larger than the K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Λ cross section.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously developed for meson-meson interactions (NSD) and meson-baryon interactions (Joseulf) to the interaction of the pseudoscalar meson octet with the baryon decuplet, following earlier studies by Kolo and Sarkar. The two-Λ(1405) prediction from Jido (2003) is tested against the recent K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Σ⁰ experiment by Prakhov (2004), showing different resonance shapes that support the two-pole interpretation. The results enable further experimental tests at facilities such as JPARC and ELSA, and provide a framework for studying hadron properties in nuclear matter, including the large in-medium broadening of the Λ(1520) and the reinterpretation of ω-nucleus data from Trnka (2005).
