# Quasicrystals in QCD

> We study the ground state of the low energy dense QCD with the assumption of chiral condensates of quarks. Under an external magnetic field, mesons could form soliton lattices via the chiral anomaly. For such scenarios, we present a unified description of pions and $η$ meson with a $U(2)$ field in the framework of the chiral perturbation theory. Our result shows the ground state is a mixture of the magnetized domain walls formed by neutral pion $π^0$ and $η$ meson when they coexist. The winding number of the ground state would alter according to the strength of the magnetic field. When the magnetic field is strong or the chemical potential is large, the proportion of the mixture is determined by the decay constants and the contributions to the anomalous action of $π^0$ and $η$ meson. The resulting configuration is either a mixed soliton lattice or a quasicrystal which could be dubbed a ``chiral soliton quasicrystal''.

## Key claim

The ground state of low-energy dense QCD under an external magnetic field, described by a $U(2)$ chiral perturbation theory including both pions and the $\eta$ meson, can be a mixed soliton lattice or even a one-dimensional quasicrystal rather than a pure neutral pion or $\eta$ chiral soliton lattice, but this depends on the ratio of decay constants and the magnetic field strength being in specific ranges.

## Method

The authors construct a $U(2)$ chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian with kinetic, mass, and anomalous terms for the neutral pion $\pi^0$ and $\eta$ meson under a homogeneous external magnetic field along the $z$-direction. They reduce the problem to one dimension by assuming transverse derivatives vanish in the ground state, then solve the coupled sine-Gordon equations of motion numerically for various winding numbers $(p,q)$ that specify the boundary conditions for $\phi_3 = \pi_3/f_\pi$ and $\phi_0 = \eta/f_\eta$ over a lattice period $d$. The ground state is determined by minimizing the energy density $\mathcal{E} = H/d$ as a function of the dimensionless magnetic field parameter $\gamma$.

## Key numbers

- The critical $\gamma$ for the $\eta$ soliton lattice is approximately $\gamma_\eta \sim \mathcal{O}(3\gamma_\pi)$, reflecting the factor $1/3$ in the anomalous coupling for $\eta$ compared to $\pi^0$.
- For $\beta=0$ and $\alpha > \alpha_c = 0.4$, the mixed soliton lattice with $(p,q)=(1,1)$ has a lower critical $\gamma$ than the pure $\pi^0$ CSL ($\gamma_\text{m} < \gamma_\pi$).
- For the exemplary case $\beta=\pi/16$ and $\alpha=0.7$, the critical values are $\gamma_\text{m}=6.5$, $\gamma_\pi=6.7$, $\gamma_\eta=26.7$, and the transition between mixed and $\pi^0$ ground states occurs at $\gamma_{\pi\text{m}}=7.2$.
- In the strong field limit $\gamma \to \infty$, the ground state satisfies the ratio $p/q \simeq 3/\alpha$, derived from the analytic minimization of the approximate Hamiltonian $H_\infty$.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral soliton lattices (CSL) in QCD, previously established for neutral pions under magnetic fields by Son (2007) and Brauner (2016, 2019, 2021), and for $\eta$ mesons under rotation by Huang (2017), Nishimura (2020), and Eto (2021). It builds on the $U(2)$ chiral perturbation theory framework introduced by Aoki (2014) and Eto (2021), but replaces rotation with a magnetic field, which couples anomalously to both $\pi^0$ and $\eta$ simultaneously. The result enables the study of quasicrystalline phases in a realistic QCD setting, a phenomenon previously known only in condensed matter systems.
