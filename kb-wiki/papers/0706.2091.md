# Pion freeze-out as seen through HBT correlations in heavy ion collisions from FAIR/AGS to RHIC energies

> We perform a systematic analysis of several HBT parameters in heavy ion collisions from $E_{\rm beam}=2$ AGeV to $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV within the UrQMD transport approach and compare the results to experimental data where available. We find that the 'lifetime' of the emission source as calculated from $τ\sim \sqrt{R_O^{2}-R_S^{2}}$, is larger than the experimentally observed values at all investigated energies. The calculated volume of the pion source ($V_f$) is found to increase monotonously with increasing beam energy and the experimentally observed decrease of the measured $V_f$ at AGS is not seen. Finally, we calculate the mean free path $λ_f=0.5-1$ fm of pions at freeze-out and find a good description of the experimental data above the AGS energy region, supporting the suggestion of a universal kinetic decoupling criterion up to the highest RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The UrQMD cascade model systematically overpredicts the 'lifetime' of the pion emission source (quantified by √(R_O² - R_S²)) at all beam energies from 2 AGeV to √s_NN = 200 GeV, while the calculated mean free path of pions at freeze-out (λ_f = 0.5–1 fm) agrees with experimental data above the AGS energy region, supporting a universal kinetic decoupling criterion.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v2.2 transport model in cascade mode to simulate central heavy-ion collisions (Au+Au, Pb+Pb, Pb+Au) at beam energies from 2 AGeV to √s_NN = 200 GeV. They computed two-pion correlation functions using the CRAB program (v3.0β) and extracted the HBT radii R_L, R_O, R_S, and the cross term R_OL via Gaussian fits in the LCMS system. From these radii they derived the freeze-out volume V_f = (2π)^(3/2) R_L R_S² and the pion mean free path λ_f = V_f / (N_N σ_πN + N_π σ_ππ) using fixed cross sections σ_πN = 72 mb and σ_ππ = 13 mb.

## Key numbers

- The calculated quantity √(R_O² - R_S²) is about 3.5–5 fm across all energies, while the measured values are 1.5–4 fm.
- The calculated mean free path λ_f of pions at freeze-out increases gradually from ~0.5 fm to ~1 fm from AGS to RHIC energies, with experimental values also between 0.5–1 fm.
- At E_beam = 2 AGeV, the measured freeze-out volume V_f is about 2–3 times larger than the calculated value.
- The calculated sideward radii R_S are systematically about 15% smaller than experimental data at almost all energies.
- The thermal homogeneity length in rapidity y_th is 0.98 at k_T = 100 MeV and 0.81 at k_T = 200 MeV, assuming T_f = 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD-based HBT studies at AGS (Li 2006), SPS (Li 2006), and RHIC (Li 2006) energies by providing a systematic excitation function of HBT parameters, freeze-out volume, and mean free path from FAIR/AGS to RHIC. It contradicts the experimentally observed decrease of V_f at AGS energies (seen by E895 and E802) and confirms the persistent HBT 'puzzle' of overpredicted source lifetime seen in other cascade transport approaches. The finding of a nearly constant λ_f ≈ 0.7 fm supports the interpretation of a universal kinetic decoupling criterion proposed by Heiselberg (1997) and Tomasik (1998), and enables future studies incorporating potential interactions and in-medium effects to resolve discrepancies at low AGS energies.
