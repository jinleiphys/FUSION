# A possibility of quark spin polarized phase in high density quark matter

> It is shown that the quark spin polarization may occur for each quark flavor by the use of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks, while the two-flavor color superconducting phase in two-flavor case may be realized at high density quark matter.

## Key claim

Using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks, the authors show that a quark spin polarized phase may occur for each quark flavor at high density, potentially coexisting with or replacing the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase depending on the chemical potential.

## Method

The authors start from an NJL Lagrangian that includes a tensor-type four-point interaction term and a diquark coupling term for color superconductivity. They apply the mean-field approximation, assume chiral symmetry restoration (so the chiral condensate vanishes), expand the quark field in helicity states, and introduce a BCS-type state. From this, they derive the thermodynamic potential as a function of the spin polarized condensate \(F\) and the color superconducting gap \(\Delta\), and map its minima for different values of the chemical potential \(\mu\).

## Key numbers

- The tensor coupling constant is set to \(G = 20.0\ \text{GeV}\), the diquark coupling constant to \(G_c = 6.6\ \text{GeV}\), and the three-momentum cutoff to \(\Lambda = 0.631\ \text{GeV}\).
- At a chemical potential \(\mu = 0.40\ \text{GeV}\), the thermodynamic potential minimum corresponds to the 2SC phase (\(F = 0\), \(\Delta \neq 0\)).
- At \(\mu = 0.50\ \text{GeV}\), the minimum corresponds to the spin polarized phase (\(F \neq 0\), \(\Delta = 0\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the NJL model with tensor interactions (Tsue et al., prior papers) by retaining the full momentum-dependent factor \(f_p(\eta)\) in the thermodynamic potential, which smooths the quantitative behavior without changing the qualitative phase structure. The results suggest that in high-density quark matter relevant to compact stars, a spin polarized phase may compete with or succeed the 2SC phase as density increases, opening the possibility of a new phase in the QCD phase diagram.
