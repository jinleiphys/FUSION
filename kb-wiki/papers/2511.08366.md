# Finite-range pairing in nuclear density functional theory

> Pairing correlations are ubiquitous in low-energy states of atomic nuclei. To incorporate them within nuclear density functional theory, often used for global computations of nuclear properties, pairing functionals that generate nucleonic pair densities and pairing fields are introduced. Many pairing functionals currently used can be traced back to zero-range nucleon-nucleon interactions. Unfortunately, such functionals are plagued by deficiencies that become apparent in large model spaces that contain unbound single-particle (continuum) states. In particular, the underlying computational schemes diverge as the single-particle space increases, and the results depend on how marginally occupied states are incorporated. These problems become more pronounced for pairing functionals that contain gradient-density dependence, such as in the Fayans functional. To remedy this, finite-range pairing functionals are introduced. In this study, this is done by folding the pair density with Gaussians. We show that a folding radius of about 1\,fm offers the best compromise between quality and stability, and substantially reduces the pathological behavior in different numerical applications.

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a finite-range pairing functional via Gaussian folding of the pair density, with a folding radius of about 1 fm, offers the best compromise between quality and stability while substantially reducing the pathological behavior (divergences, box-size dependence, and pairing isomerism) that plagues zero-range pairing functionals in large model spaces containing continuum states.

## Method

The authors replace the standard zero-range pairing functional with a separable finite-range functional where the pair density is folded with a Gaussian operator. They implement this in both Skyrme and Fayans energy density functionals, performing HFB calculations for spherical and deformed nuclei (primarily 120Sn and 170Yb) with varying folding radii, cutoff energies, box sizes, and numerical representations (1D spherical and 2D axial grids). The functionals are recalibrated via chi-squared fits to nuclear ground-state data for each folding radius.

## Key numbers

- A folding radius of about 1 fm offers the best compromise between quality and stability; chi-squared fits show preference for folding radii between 0.7 and 0.9 fm (0.7 fm for Skyrme, 0.9 fm for Fayans)
- For 120Sn with SV-bas, the neutron pairing strength changes dramatically with folding radius: 317 MeV fm^2 at R_F=0 fm, 4340 MeV fm^2 at R_F=1.5 fm, and 26700 MeV fm^2 at R_F=3 fm
- For 120Sn with Fy(Delta r) EDF at cutoff 45 MeV, fluctuations in charge radius and pairing energy with pairing space size are reduced by an order of magnitude at R_F=1 fm compared to zero folding
- For 170Yb with SV-bas and R_F=1 fm, the error on charge radius remains below 0.0005 fm and total energy below 0.1 MeV for N_st^(1/3) above 9
- No HFB pairing isomers were found for folding radii R_F >= 0.5 fm
- For 120Sn with SV-bas at cutoff 25 MeV, HFB results with R_F=1 fm show box-size dependence comparable to zero folding, while R_F=3 fm shows large box dependence for box radius less than about 33 fm

## Context

This work extends the line of research on pairing functionals in nuclear DFT, addressing known deficiencies of zero-range pairing functionals that diverge in large model spaces (Bruun 1999, Bulgac 2002) and require regularization schemes (Dobaczewski 1996, Bulgac 2002). It builds on earlier finite-range approaches including the D1S Gogny functional (Berger 1984) and separable pairing functionals (Tian 2009), but introduces a fourfold separable ansatz using Gaussian folding that maintains momentum conservation while allowing a simple energy-density functional form. The approach enables more portable HFB implementations between different numerical representations (1D spherical vs. 2D axial grids) and avoids the pairing isomerism problem that plagues zero-range HFB calculations.
