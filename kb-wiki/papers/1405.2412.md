# Photon asymmetries in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ using EFT($π/!/!/!/$) approach

> The parity-violating Lagrangian of the weak nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction in the pionless effective field theory (EFT($//!/!/!/pi$)) approach contains five independent unknown low-energy coupling constants (LECs). The photon asymmetry with respect to neutron polarization in $np\rightarrow$ $dγ$ $A^{np}_γ$, the circular polarization of outgoing photon in $np\rightarrow$ $dγ$ $P^{np}_γ$, the neutron spin rotation in hydrogen $\frac{1}ρ\frac{dϕ^{np}}{dl}$, the neutron spin rotation in deuterium $\frac{1}ρ\frac{dϕ^{nd}}{dl}$ and the circular polarization of $γ$-emission in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ $P^{nd}_γ$ are the parity-violating observables which have been recently calculated in terms of parity-violating LECs in the EFT($//!/!/!/pi$) framework. We obtain the LECs by matching the parity-violating observables to the Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) best value estimates. Then, we evaluate photon asymmetry with respect to the neutron polarization $a^{nd}_γ$ and the photon asymmetry in relation to deuteron polarization $A^{nd}_γ$ in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ process. We finally compare our EFT($//!/!/!/pi$) photon asymmetries results with the experimental values and the previous calculations based on the DDH model.

## Key claim

The authors determine the five unknown parity-violating low-energy coupling constants (LECs) of pionless effective field theory (EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$)) by matching EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) predictions for five parity-violating observables to the Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) "best" value estimates, rather than to experimental data, and then use these LECs to predict the photon asymmetries $a^{nd}_\gamma$ and $A^{nd}_\gamma$ in the $nd\rightarrow$ $^3H\gamma$ process.

## Method

The parity-violating Lagrangian of the weak nucleon-nucleon interaction in EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) is constructed with five independent LECs corresponding to S-P partial wave transitions. The authors compute the leading-order parity-conserving (M1) and parity-violating (E1) amplitudes for $nd\rightarrow$ $^3H\gamma$ using dibaryon fields and Faddeev equations in cluster-configuration space, then express the observables $P^{nd}_\gamma$, $a^{nd}_\gamma$, and $A^{nd}_\gamma$ in terms of the LECs. They match the EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) relations for $A^{np}_\gamma$, $P^{np}_\gamma$, $\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{d\phi^{np}}{dl}$, $\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{d\phi^{nd}}{dl}$, and $P^{nd}_\gamma$ to DDH model estimates to extract the five LECs, and then evaluate $a^{nd}_\gamma$ and $A^{nd}_\gamma$ using those extracted values.

## Key numbers

- The EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) result for $P^{nd}_\gamma$ in terms of LECs is: $P^{nd}_\gamma = [0.26\,g^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)} - 0.23\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} + 0.15\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=1)} - 1.08\,g^{(^3S_1-^3P_1)}] \times 10^{3}$.
- The EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) result for $a^{nd}_\gamma$ in terms of LECs is: $a^{nd}_\gamma = [-0.51\,g^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)} + 0.83\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} - 0.47\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=1)} + 1.36\,g^{(^3S_1-^3P_1)}] \times 10^{3}$.
- The EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) result for $A^{nd}_\gamma$ in terms of LECs is: $A^{nd}_\gamma = [1.36\,g^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)} - 1.50\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} + 0.94\,g^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=1)} - 4.47\,g^{(^3S_1-^3P_1)}] \times 10^{3}$.
- The DDH model values used for matching are: $P^{np}_\gamma = 1.76 \times 10^{-8}$ (AV18+DDH-II), $A^{np}_\gamma = 5.29 \times 10^{-8}$ (AV18+DDH-II), $\frac{d\phi^{np}}{dl} = 3.2 \times 10^{-17}$ rad.fm$^{-1}$ (DDH-best), $\frac{d\phi^{nd}}{dl} = 9.32 \times 10^{-17}$ rad.fm$^{-1}$ (AV18+UIX/DDH-best), $P^{nd}_\gamma = -7.30 \times 10^{-7}$ (AV18+UIX/DDH-II).
- The coefficient matrix $a_X(\bar{X}-\bar{Y})$ for the PV amplitude is computed at cutoff $\Lambda = 700$ MeV, with values in units of $10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-1}$ for $^2S_{1/2}$ and $10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ for $^4S_{3/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the EFT($\pi\!\!\!/$) framework for parity violation in few-nucleon systems, previously applied to $np\rightarrow d\gamma$ by Schindler et al. (2013) and to neutron spin rotations by Griesshammer et al. (2012), to the $nd\rightarrow$ $^3H\gamma$ process. The authors use the DDH model (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980) as a proxy for experimental data, which are currently unavailable or imprecise for many parity-violating observables. The results enable future comparison with planned experiments measuring photon asymmetries in neutron-deuteron capture, and provide a pathway to determine the weak LECs once experimental data become available.
