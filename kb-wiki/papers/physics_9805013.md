# Exact $Z^2$ scaling of pair production in the high-energy limit of heavy-ion collisions

> The two-center Dirac equation for an electron in the external electromagnetic field of two colliding heavy ions in the limit in which the ions are moving at the speed of light is exactly solved and nonperturbative amplitudes for free electron-positron pair production are obtained. We find the condition for the applicability of this solution for large but finite collision energy, and use it to explain recent experimental results. The observed scaling of positron yields as the square of the projectile and target charges is a result of an exact cancellation of a nonperturbative charge dependence and holds as well for large coupling. Other observables would be sensitive to nonperturbative phases.

## Key claim

The exact nonperturbative solution of the two-center Dirac equation for electron-positron pair production in the high-energy limit of heavy-ion collisions yields transition amplitudes that differ from second-order perturbation theory only by nonperturbative phases; these phases cancel exactly when integrated over impact parameter, so the observed \(Z^2\) scaling of total positron yields holds for arbitrarily large coupling \(Z\alpha\) and is not a perturbative effect.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent two-center Dirac equation for an electron in the external electromagnetic field of two heavy ions moving on straight-line trajectories at the speed of light, using a short-range representation that reduces the interaction to delta-function singularities on the light fronts \(z = \pm t\). They obtained exact closed-form integral representations for the scattering amplitudes by matching plane-wave solutions across the light fronts and integrating the conserved transition four-current. The resulting amplitudes were expressed in terms of a transverse-momentum transfer distribution \(Q^{\vec{b}}_Z(\vec{\kappa})\) derived from the Fourier transform of the phase shift at each light front, regularized to exclude unphysical contributions from large transverse distances.

## Key numbers

* For 33-TeV Pb\(^{82+}\) projectiles on a Au target at \(\gamma_T = 168\), the observed target-charge dependence of positron yields is \(Z_T^{2.03 \pm 0.03}\); for 6.4-TeV S\(^{16+}\) projectiles (\(\gamma_T = 212\)), it is \(Z_T^{1.99 \pm 0.02}\).
* The projectile-charge dependence was observed to be \(Z_P^{2.0 \pm 0.1}\).
* The condition for applicability of the infinite-\(\gamma\) solution at finite energy is \(|\vec{\kappa}| \gg 2\alpha Z / \gamma\); for the CERN SPS experiments with \(\gamma \approx 10\), this restricts perturbative validity to positron momenta \(j > 0.4\) MeV/c for sulfur data and \(j > 2\) MeV/c for lead data.
* For future experiments, \(\gamma \approx 100\) at RHIC and \(\gamma \approx 3000\) at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative two-photon exchange calculations of Bertulani and Baur (1988) and provides a nonperturbative explanation for the \(Z^2\) scaling observed in the CERN SPS experiments by Vane et al. (1992). The exact solution shows that the leading-order perturbative result for impact-parameter-integrated pair production rates is exact in the high-energy limit, not merely at small \(\alpha Z\). This enables future searches for nonperturbative effects in observables that are sensitive to the phases, such as correlations and multiple-pair production, rather than in the inclusive positron yield.
