# Charmed Tetraquarks Tcc and Tcs from Dynamical Lattice QCD Simulations

> Charmed tetraquarks $T_{cc}=(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})$ and $T_{cs}=(cs\bar{u}\bar{d})$ are studied through the S-wave meson-meson interactions, $D$-$D$, $\bar{K}$-$D$, $D$-$D^{*}$ and $\bar{K}$-$D^{*}$, on the basis of the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with the pion mass $m_π \simeq $410, 570 and 700 MeV. For the charm quark, the relativistic heavy quark action is employed to treat its dynamics on the lattice. Using the HAL QCD method, we extract the S-wave potentials in lattice QCD simulations, from which the meson-meson scattering phase shifts are calculated. The phase shifts in the isospin triplet ($I$=1) channels indicate repulsive interactions, while those in the $I=0$ channels suggest attraction, growing as $m_π$ decreases. This is particularly prominent in the $T_{cc} (J^P=1^+,I=0)$ channel, though neither bound state nor resonance are found in the range $m_π =410-700$ MeV. We make a qualitative comparison of our results with the phenomenological diquark picture.

## Key claim
Using (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at pion masses of 410, 570, and 700 MeV, the authors find attractive S-wave interactions in the isospin-zero channels relevant to the charmed tetraquarks Tcc and Tcs, but no bound state or resonance is observed in the pion mass range studied; the existence of such states at the physical pion mass remains an open question.

## Method
The authors employ (2+1)-flavor full QCD gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS collaboration on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with a lattice cutoff of 2176 MeV. They use the HAL QCD method to extract S-wave meson-meson potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, and then solve the Schrodinger equation with these potentials to obtain scattering phase shifts and scattering lengths. The charm quark dynamics are treated using a relativistic heavy quark action.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses studied: 411(2) MeV, 572(2) MeV, and 699(1) MeV
- Lattice cutoff: a^{-1} = 2176 MeV (lattice spacing a = 0.0907(13) fm)
- Spatial lattice volume: L^3 ~ (2.9 fm)^3
- Scattering length in the I=0 D-D* channel at m_pi = 411 MeV: 0.427(223)(104) fm
- Scattering length in the I=0 Kbar-D channel at m_pi = 411 MeV: 0.266(70)(56) fm
- Scattering length in the I=0 Kbar-D* channel at m_pi = 411 MeV: 0.265(166)(161) fm
- D meson mass at m_pi = 411 MeV: 1902(3) MeV
- D* meson mass at m_pi = 411 MeV: 2048(12) MeV

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of charmed tetraquarks beyond the heavy-quark limit by using a relativistic heavy quark action for the charm quark. The results are compared qualitatively with the phenomenological diquark picture of Selem and Wilczek (2006). The authors note that previous predictions for charmed tetraquark binding energies range widely from resonances to 100 MeV bound, depending on the model (diquark model, constituent quark model, meson-meson molecular model, lattice QCD in the heavy-quark limit). The study enables future simulations at or near the physical pion mass, which the authors plan to carry out with PACS-CS configurations and coupled-channel schemes.
