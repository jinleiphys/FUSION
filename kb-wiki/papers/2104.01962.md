# Two-body wave functions and compositeness from scattering amplitudes: II. Application to the physical $N ^{\ast}$ and $Δ^{\ast}$ resonances

> The meson-baryon molecular components for the $N^{\ast}$ and $Δ^{\ast}$ resonances are investigated in terms of the compositeness, which is defined as the norm of the two-body wave function from the meson-baryon scattering amplitudes. The scattering amplitudes are constructed in a $πN$-$ηN$-$σN$-$ρN$-$πΔ$ coupled-channels problem in a meson exchange model together with several bare $N^{\ast}$ and $Δ^{\ast}$ states, and parameters are fitted so as to reproduce the on-shell $πN$ partial wave amplitudes up to the center-of-mass energy 1.9 GeV with the orbital angular momentum $L \le 2$. As a result, the Roper resonance $N (1440)$ is found to be dominated by the $πN$ and $σN$ molecular components while the bare-state contribution is small. The squared wave functions in coordinate space imply that both in the $πN$ and $σN$ channels the separation between the meson and baryon is about more than 1 fm for the $N (1440)$ resonance. On the other hand, dominant meson-baryon molecular components are not observed in any other $N^{\ast}$ and $Δ^{\ast}$ resonances in the present model, although they have some fractions of the meson-baryon clouds.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance N(1440) is found to be dominated by πN and σN molecular components with a small bare-state contribution, while no other N* or Δ* resonances in the model show dominant meson-baryon molecular components, although they have some fractions of meson-baryon clouds. This conclusion is model-dependent because both the compositeness Xj and the missing-channel contribution Z are not physical observables.

## Method

The author constructed πN-ηN-σN-ρN-πΔ coupled-channels scattering amplitudes using a meson exchange model with effective Lagrangians, including bare N* and Δ* states. Parameters were fitted to reproduce the on-shell πN partial wave amplitudes from the SAID database up to center-of-mass energy 1.9 GeV with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 2, covering S11, S31, P11, P31, P13, P33, D13, D33, D15, and D35 partial waves. The two-body wave functions and compositeness were extracted from the residues of the off-shell scattering amplitudes at the resonance poles using the complex scaling method.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance N(1440) is dominated by πN and σN molecular components with a small bare-state contribution; the squared wave functions in coordinate space imply that in both the πN and σN channels the separation between the meson and baryon is about more than 1 fm.
- The scattering amplitudes were fitted to reproduce πN partial wave amplitudes up to center-of-mass energy 1.9 GeV.
- The πN N and ηN N couplings used D + F = 1.26 and D - F = 0.33, with meson decay constants fπ = 92.1 MeV and fη = 1.3 fπ.
- The real-valued physical Δ(1232) mass used was mΔ = 1210 MeV; the ρ meson mass used was mρ = 775.3 MeV; the σ meson bare mass used was mσ0 = 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Sekihara (2016) for extracting two-body wave functions and compositeness from scattering amplitudes, applying it to physical N* and Δ* resonances. It builds on dynamical coupled-channels models by Krehl (1999), Chen (2007), Ronchen (2012), and Kamano (2013), as well as the K-matrix approaches by Anisovich (2011) and Workman (2012). The results enable future studies of the internal structure of nucleon resonances and can be compared with lattice QCD simulations by Lang (2016), Liu (2015, 2016), and Wu (2016, 2017).
