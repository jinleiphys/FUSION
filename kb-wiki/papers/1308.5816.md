# Suppression of Excited-State Contributions to Stellar Reaction Rates

> It has been shown in previous work [Phys. Rev. Lett. 101, 191101 (2008); Phys. Rev. C 80, 035801 (2009)] that a suppression of the stellar enhancement factor (SEF) occurs in some endothermic reactions at and far from stability. This effect is re-evaluated using the ground-state contributions to the stellar reaction rates, which were shown to be better suited to judge the importance of excited state contributions than the previously applied SEFs. An update of the tables shown in Phys. Rev. C 80, 035801 (2009) is given. The new evalution finds 2350 cases (out of a full set of 57513 reactions) for which the ground-state contribution is larger in the reaction direction with negative reaction Q-value than in the exothermic direction, thus providing exceptions to the commonly applied Q-value rule. The results confirm the Coulomb suppression effect but lead to a larger number of exceptions than previously found. This is due to the fact that often a large variation in the g.s. contribution does not lead to a sizeable change in the SEF. On the other hand, several previously identified cases do not appear anymore because it is found that their g.s. contribution is smaller than inferred from the SEF.

## Key claim

The paper re-evaluates the "Coulomb suppression" of excited-state contributions to stellar reaction rates using ground-state contributions \(X_0\) instead of the previously used stellar enhancement factors (SEFs), finding 2350 exceptions to the Q-value rule (out of 57513 reactions), more than previously reported, because a large variation in \(X_0\) does not always produce a sizeable change in the SEF, and some previously identified cases disappear because their \(X_0\) is smaller than inferred from the SEF.

## Method

The author used the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model code SMARAGD with updated excited-state information, masses, and an improved barrier penetration routine to compute ground-state contributions \(X_0\) for forward and reverse reaction directions across the nuclear chart (targets from Ne to Bi, between proton and neutron driplines). Reactions with light projectiles (nucleons, \(\alpha\)) were studied, and cases were selected where \(X_0^\mathrm{rev}/X_0^\mathrm{forw} > 1.3\) and \(X_0^\mathrm{rev} > 0.8\) at plasma temperatures \(T \leq 4.5\) GK, ensuring the reverse (endothermic) direction is dominated by ground-state contributions and the difference between directions is at least 30%.

## Key numbers

- 2350 reactions (out of 57513 total) show \(X_0^\mathrm{rev} > X_0^\mathrm{forw}\), providing exceptions to the Q-value rule.
- Selection criteria: \(X_0^\mathrm{rev}/X_0^\mathrm{forw} > 1.3\) and \(X_0^\mathrm{rev} > 0.8\).
- Temperature cutoff: \(T \leq 4.5\) GK.
- Example reaction: \(^{85}\mathrm{Rb(p,n)}^{85}\mathrm{Sr}\) has \(Q = -1.847\) MeV; its SEF is close to unity for all temperatures, but \(X_0\) decreases with increasing temperature and does not meet the selection criteria.
- Tables list target nuclides for endothermic reactions including (\(\alpha,\gamma\)), (p,\(\gamma\)), (\(\gamma\),n), (\(\gamma\),p), (n,\(\alpha\)), (n,\(\gamma\)), (p,n), (n,p), (\(\alpha\),n), (\(\alpha\),p), and (p,\(\alpha\)) reactions.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of Coulomb suppression of excited-state contributions first reported in Rauscher (2008) and Rauscher (2009), which used stellar enhancement factors (SEFs) and found fewer exceptions to the Q-value rule. The present study uses the ground-state contribution \(X_0\), shown by Rauscher (2012) to be a better measure of excited-state impact than the SEF. The results enable improved identification of reactions where measuring the endothermic direction is preferable for constraining stellar rates, particularly for (\(\alpha,\gamma\)) reactions on proton-rich targets relevant to p-nucleus synthesis and (p,n) reactions important in \(\gamma\)-process and \(\nu\)p-process nucleosynthesis.
