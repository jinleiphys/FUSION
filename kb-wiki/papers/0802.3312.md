# Closed-Orbit Theory of Spatial Density Oscillations in Finite Fermion Systems

> We investigate the particle and kinetic-energy densities for $N$ non-interacting fermions confined in a local potential. Using Gutzwiller's semi-classical Green function, we describe the oscillating parts of the densities in terms of closed non-periodic classical orbits. We derive universal relations between the oscillating parts of the densities for potentials with spherical symmetry in arbitrary dimensions, and a ``local virial theorem'' valid also for arbitrary non-integrable potentials. We give simple analytical formulae for the density oscillations in a one-dimensional potential.

# Closed-Orbit Theory of Spatial Density Oscillations in Finite Fermion Systems

## Key claim

The oscillating parts of particle and kinetic-energy densities in finite fermion systems can be described semiclassically using closed non-periodic classical orbits (not periodic orbits), and a "local virial theorem" relating kinetic and particle density oscillations holds for arbitrary local potentials in any dimension, provided the semiclassical limit of large particle numbers N is valid and the Fermi energy oscillations are small compared to the smooth Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors start from the exact quantum-mechanical expressions for particle density ρ(r) and two forms of kinetic-energy density τ(r) and τ₁(r) in terms of the Green function. They replace the exact Green function with Gutzwiller's semiclassical approximation, which sums over all classical trajectories connecting two points. For the diagonal case r=r', only closed trajectories contribute; the authors separate these into periodic orbits (which give the smooth Thomas-Fermi densities) and non-periodic orbits (which yield the density oscillations). The energy integration is performed by parts to leading order in ħ, keeping only the upper integration limit at the smooth Fermi energy λ̄.

## Key numbers

- For N=40 fermions in the potential V(x)=x⁴/4, the semiclassical result for δρ(x) from Eq. (delrhogen) agrees very well with the quantum-mechanical result, except near the classical turning point where the Thomas-Fermi approximation breaks down.
- The universal wavelength of central oscillations in one dimension is ħπ/p_λ, independent of the particular form of the potential V(x).
- For D-dimensional spherically symmetric potentials, the rapid oscillations near r=0 are given by a Bessel function J_ν(2rp_λ/ħ) with index ν=D/2-1, generalizing the result of Thouless and Thorpe (1976) for D=3.
- The relation δτ(r) = [λ̄ - V(r)] δρ(r) (the local virial theorem) holds for arbitrary local potentials in arbitrary dimensions.
- In one dimension, the additional relation δτ₁(x) = -δτ(x) holds due to the opposite initial and final momenta of the contributing non-periodic orbits.

## Context

This work extends the periodic orbit theory (POT) of Gutzwiller (1971), which relates level densities to periodic orbits, to the spatial density oscillations first analyzed by Kohn and Sham (1965) using one-dimensional WKB methods and by Thouless and Thorpe (1976) for three-dimensional radial systems. The authors show that periodic orbits do not contribute to density oscillations (they give only the smooth Thomas-Fermi part), while non-periodic orbits produce the oscillating parts. The results enable a classical-orbit interpretation of shell effects in spatial densities for finite fermion systems such as atoms, nuclei, quantum dots, and trapped fermionic gases, and provide a challenge for experimental verification of the local virial theorem.
