# Instabilities in Anisotropic Chiral Plasmas

> Using the Berry-curvature modified kinetic equation we study instabilities in anisotropic chiral plasmas. It is demonstrated that even for a very small value of anisotropic parameter the chiral-imbalance instability is strongly modified. The instability is enhanced when the modes propagates in the direction parallel to the anisotropy vector and it is strongly suppressed when the modes propagate in the perpendicular direction. Further the instabilities in the jet-plasma system is also investigated. For the case when the modes are propagating in direction parallel to the stream velocity we find that there exist a new branch of the dispersion relation arising due to the parity odd effects. We also show that the parity-odd interaction can enhance the streaming instability.

# Instabilities in Anisotropic Chiral Plasmas

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that even a very small momentum-space anisotropy parameter can strongly modify the chiral-imbalance instability in a chiral plasma: the instability is enhanced for modes propagating parallel to the anisotropy direction and strongly suppressed for modes propagating perpendicular to it. The parity-odd (chiral) interaction can also enhance the streaming instability in a jet-plasma system, producing a new branch of the dispersion relation.

## Method

The authors use the Berry-curvature modified kinetic (Vlasov) equation, following the power-counting scheme of Son and collaborators (2012), to compute the linear response polarization tensor for an anisotropic chiral plasma. They introduce anisotropy by rescaling the equilibrium distribution function along a preferred direction using an anisotropy parameter ξ, then decompose the self-energy into six independent tensor projectors (including parity-odd ones) and solve for the dispersion relation in the quasi-static limit (|ω| << k) to linear order in ξ.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass squared is given by m²_D = e²(μ²/(2π²) + T²/6), where μ is the chiral chemical potential and T is the temperature.
- The axial current coefficient is C_E = e²μ/(4π²).
- The maximum growth rate for the pure chiral instability is Γ_ch = (4/27)(4α_e³μ³/(π⁴m²_D)), where α_e = e²/(4π) is the electromagnetic coupling.
- The maximum growth rate for the pure Weibel instability at θ_n = 0 is Γ_w = 2(4α_e³μ³/(π⁴m²_D))((ξπ²m²_D)/(9μ²α_e²))^(3/2).
- For μ ~ T, the ratio m²_D/μ² = (2/3)(α_e/π)(3 + π²), and the critical anisotropy parameter where both instabilities have comparable growth rates is ξ_c ~ 2^(2/3)[3α_e/(2π(3+π²))].
- For propagation parallel to the anisotropy (θ_n = 0), the instability range extends to k_N < 1 + (4/3)ξ(π²m²_D/(4μ²α_e²)), where k_N = πk/(μα_e) is the normalized wavenumber.
- For propagation perpendicular to the anisotropy (θ_n = π/2), the instability range reduces to k_N < 1 - (2/3)ξ(π²m²_D/(4μ²α_e²)).
- The upper bound on ξ for θ_n = 0 and k_N = 1 is ξ = 3π/4.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of collective modes in chiral plasmas by Akamatsu and collaborators (2013), who studied the chiral-imbalance instability in isotropic systems, to include momentum-space anisotropy. It also generalizes the treatment of Weibel instability in anisotropic plasmas by Romatschke and collaborators (2003) to incorporate parity-odd (chiral) effects. The results are relevant for understanding magnetic field generation and thermalization in quark-gluon plasmas created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as well as for studying Weyl semimetals in condensed matter physics. The authors note that for sufficiently small anisotropy (ξ < ξ_c), the chiral instability dominates, while for larger anisotropy the Weibel instability dominates.
