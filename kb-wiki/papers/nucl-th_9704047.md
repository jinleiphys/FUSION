# Naturalness in the quark-meson coupling model

> The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model is examined using Georgi's ``naive dimensional analysis''. We argue that the QMC model is quite natural as an effective field theory for nuclei.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model yields dimensionless coupling constants of order unity under Georgi's naive dimensional analysis, making it a "natural" effective field theory for nuclei, provided the ad-hoc parametrization of the in-medium sigma-meson mass is not taken to extremes.

## Method

The authors applied naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to the QMC lagrangian in the mean-field approximation, scaling each interaction term by the pion-decay constant f_pi (93 MeV) and a cutoff Lambda (taken as the nucleon mass, 939 MeV). They computed the dimensionless coefficients c_{l m n p} for two versions of the model: QMC-I, where only the nucleon structure responds to the scalar field, and QMC-II, where the vector meson masses also become density-dependent via a quark-model treatment, while the sigma-meson mass is parametrized by a quadratic function of the scalar field with three parameter sets (A, B, C).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation: -15.7 MeV
- Bulk symmetry energy: 35 MeV
- QMC-I dimensionless coefficients all lie in the range 0.5 to 1.0 (e.g., c_{1100} = 0.82, c_{1010} = -0.81, c_{1001} = -0.92)
- In QMC-II, 14 or 15 of the 16 coefficients are natural; the unnatural ones are c_{0500} = 8.3 (set C) and c_{0600} = -22 (sets A and C), -5.7 (set B)
- Free hadron masses used: m_N = 939 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV
- Bag radius of free nucleon: 0.8 fm; bare quark mass: 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of NDA, previously used by Furnstahl et al. (1996) to show that the relativistic Hartree approximation in QHD yields unnatural coefficients, and by the Nikolaus-Hoch-Madland (NHM) group (1992) who found 6 of 9 couplings natural. The QMC model is contrasted with the FSTW model of Furnstahl, Serot, Tang and Walecka (1996), which has 16 mostly natural couplings but requires phenomenological fitting to nuclear data, whereas QMC determines its three basic couplings from saturation properties and derives the rest from hadron structure. The analysis enables future work to refine the sigma-meson in-medium behavior within a natural effective field theory framework.
