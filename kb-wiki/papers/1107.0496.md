# Short-range tensor interaction and high-density nuclear symmetry energy

> Effects of the short-range tensor interaction on the density-dependence of nuclear symmetry energy are examined by applying an approximate expression for the second-order tensor contribution to the symmetry energy derived earlier by G.E. Brown and R. Machleidt. It is found that the uncertainty in the short-range tensor force leads directly to a divergent high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The uncertainty in the short-range tensor force leads directly to a divergent high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, but the short-range tensor force is probably not the only reason for the poorly known high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors applied an approximate expression for the second-order tensor contribution to the symmetry energy derived by Brown and Machleidt (1994), using free single-particle wave functions to evaluate the expectation value of the tensor interaction. They adopted several tensor forces with different short-range behaviors (standard pi+rho exchange, G-Matrix, M3Y, Av18, and a case where the tensor force vanishes for r less than or equal to 0.7 fm) that all merge to the same Av18 tensor force at longer range (r greater than 0.7 fm). The resulting symmetry energies were compared with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock predictions using the Av18 interaction.

## Key numbers

- The effective energy parameter e_eff used in the second-order tensor contribution formula is approximately 200 MeV.
- The Brueckner-Hartree-Fock prediction using the Av18 interaction is about 7 MeV higher than the prediction from the approximate expression using the same Av18 interaction over the whole density range considered.
- The short-range cutoff for the pi+rho exchange interaction was set at r = 0.4 fm, with V(r less than 0.4 fm) set equal to V(r = 0.4 fm).

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation by Brown and Machleidt (1994) of the second-order tensor contribution to the symmetry energy, and builds on studies by Otsuka et al. (2005) who showed that short-range tensor behavior has no effect on nuclear structures. The results indicate that the uncertain short-range tensor force contributes significantly to the divergence of symmetry energy predictions at supra-saturation densities, which is relevant for ongoing experimental efforts at facilities such as CSR/China, FRIB/USA, GSI/Germany, RIKEN/Japan, and KoRIA/Korea to constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.
