# QCD at Finite Temperature: A Variational Approach:

> We develop here a nonperturbative framework to study quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperatures using the thermofield dynamics (TFD) method of Umezawa. The methodology considered here is self-consistent and variational. There is a dynamical generation of a magnetic gluon mass. This eliminates the infrared problems associated with perturbative QCD calculations at finite temperatures. We obtain here the thermodynamical quantities like free energy density, pressure and entropy density. We also calculate the temperature dependence of SVZ parameter ${α_{s} \over π}<{G^a}_{μν} {G^{a μν}}>$. The condensate vanishes at the critical temperature in accordance with recent hot sum rule calculations. The present method gives an insight to the vacuum structure in QCD at zero temperature as well as at finite temperatures in a co-ordinated manner. (to appear in Z. Physik C )

## Key claim

The paper claims that a nonperturbative variational approach combined with thermofield dynamics can describe QCD at finite temperatures, yielding a dynamically generated magnetic gluon mass that eliminates infrared divergences; however, the method relies on a Gaussian ansatz for the gluon condensate function and omits three-gluon condensates for simplicity, limiting the scope to low-energy physics.

## Method

The authors quantize QCD in Coulomb gauge and define a nonperturbative vacuum through a Bogoliubov transformation with a Gaussian ansatz for the condensate function sinh f(k) = A exp(-B k²/2). They extend this to finite temperature using thermofield dynamics, introducing a thermal vacuum with a temperature-dependent distribution function parametrized by a self-consistently determined effective gluon mass. The free energy density is extremized with respect to the condensate parameter A at each temperature, while the gluon mass is determined iteratively from a self-consistency equation derived from the quartic gluon interaction term.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature T_c ≈ 275 MeV for coupling α_s = g²/4π = 0.8, above which the condensate parameter A_min becomes zero and the SVZ parameter changes sign.
* Self-consistent gluon mass around 300-400 MeV at zero temperature for the same coupling.
* SVZ parameter at zero temperature: (g²/4π²)⟨G²⟩ = 0.012 GeV⁴, with magnetic sector contributing 0.00585 GeV⁴ and electric sector contributing 0.00615 GeV⁴ for α_s = 0.8.
* High-temperature limit of Δε/T⁴ ≈ 4.6 for α_s = 0.8, compared to the free gluon limit of 5.26 for N=3.
* Critical coupling g_c with g_c²/4π ≈ 0.39, above which the perturbative vacuum becomes unstable (A_min ≠ 0).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier zero-temperature variational approach to QCD vacuum structure (Mishra et al., 1991) by incorporating finite temperature via thermofield dynamics (Umezawa). It addresses the infrared divergence problem in finite-temperature perturbative QCD identified by Linde (1980) and provides a nonperturbative alternative to semiclassical expansions around chromomagnetic monopoles (Polyakov, 1977). The temperature dependence of the SVZ parameter agrees with hot sum rule calculations (Domínguez, 1991) and contrasts with low-temperature expansion results (Gerber, 1989); the critical temperature of 275 MeV is compared to the J/ψ dissolution temperature of about 210 MeV from heavy ion collision phenomenology (Karsch, 1989). The method enables calculation of thermodynamic quantities (free energy, pressure, entropy density) and can be compared with lattice QCD results (Engels, 1990; Celik, 1983).
