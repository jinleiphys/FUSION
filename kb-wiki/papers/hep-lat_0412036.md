# The speed of sound and specific heat in the QCD plasma: hydrodynamics, fluctuations and conformal symmetry

> We report the continuum limits of the speed of sound, c_s, and the specific heat at constant volume, c_v, in quenched QCD at temperatures of 2Tc and 3Tc. At these temperatures, c_s is within 2-sigma of the ideal gas value, whereas c_v differs significantly from the ideal gas. However, both are compatible with results expected in a theory with conformal symmetry. We investigate effective measures of conformal symmetry in the high temperature phase of QCD.

## Key claim

The speed of sound in quenched QCD at 2Tc and 3Tc is within 2-sigma of the ideal gas value of 1/3, and the specific heat is compatible with the conformal symmetry relation cv/T^3 = 4 epsilon/T^4, even though the energy density and pressure individually deviate from ideal gas values by about 24-40%.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD simulations of pure SU(3) gauge theory with the Wilson action, employing the "differential" operator method to compute second derivatives of the free energy directly as operator expectation values. They performed simulations at multiple lattice spacings (N_tau = 8, 10, 12, 14 for 2Tc; N_tau = 10, 12 for 3Tc) and extrapolated to the continuum limit linearly in 1/N_tau^2. The specific heat and speed of sound were extracted from the conformal measure C = Delta/epsilon and its temperature derivative Gamma, using thermodynamic identities.

## Key numbers

- At T = 2Tc: epsilon/T^4 = 3.3(3), P/T^4 = 1.0(1), s/T^3 = 4.2(3), cv/T^3 = 15(1), cs^2 = 0.30(1)
- At T = 3Tc: epsilon/T^4 = 4.2(5), P/T^4 = 1.3(2), s/T^3 = 5.6(6), cv/T^3 = 18(2), cs^2 = 0.30(2)
- The ideal gas values are epsilon/T^4 = 8 pi^2/15 and P/T^4 = 8 pi^2/45; at 3Tc the lattice values differ from ideal gas by about 24%
- At 2Tc the deviation from ideal gas is about 40%, significant at 7-sigma
- The 't Hooft coupling N_c g^2 at scale 2 pi T is 8.1(1) at 2Tc and 7.0(1) at 3Tc

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD equation of state measurements of Boyd et al. (1996) by computing second-derivative thermodynamic quantities (specific heat and speed of sound) that go beyond the equation of state. The results test the relevance of conformal symmetry to finite-temperature QCD, following the suggestion by Gubser (2000) that conformal field theory approaches lattice results. The measured entropy density at 3Tc shows quantitative agreement with the strong-coupling expansion of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory from Gubser (2000), but at 2Tc the ratio s/s_0 falls below the strong-coupling limit of 3/4, indicating that this particular strong-coupling theory fails where weak-coupling theories also break down. The results enable hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC with a measured rather than assumed speed of sound, and provide a baseline for event-by-event fluctuation analyses.
