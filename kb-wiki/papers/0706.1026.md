# Charge separation induced by P-odd bubbles in QCD matter

> We examine the recent suggestion that P- and CP-odd effects in QCD matter can induce electric charge asymmetry with respect to reaction plane in relativistic heavy ion collisions. General arguments are given which confirm that the angular momentum of QCD matter in the presence of non-zero topological charge should induce an electric field aligned along the axis of the angular momentum. A simple formula relating the magnitude of charge asymmetry to the angular momentum and topological charge is derived. The expected asymmetry is amenable to experimental observation at RHIC and LHC; we discuss the recent preliminary STAR result in light of our findings.

# Charge separation induced by P-odd bubbles in QCD matter

## Key claim

The authors argue that P- and CP-odd domains in QCD matter, when combined with the angular momentum present in off-central heavy ion collisions, generate an electric field aligned along the angular momentum axis, leading to a separation of electric charge across the reaction plane. The effect is derived from the effective Lagrangian approach and is presented as a topological phenomenon that should persist at finite temperature, though the numerical estimates are performed at zero temperature and may change in more realistic calculations.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach incorporating the axial anomaly to derive the relationship between the topological charge (parameterized by a fluctuating theta angle), angular momentum, and induced electric field. They apply the Larmor theorem to map the effect of angular momentum onto an equivalent magnetic field, then minimize the action density with respect to the electric field to obtain the induced field. The charge separation is computed by integrating the resulting charge density over the transverse area of the system, assuming the system has sufficient time to equilibrate.

## Key numbers

- For a semi-central Au-Au event at RHIC with angular momentum quantum number l = 4, the estimated excess of quarks over antiquarks in the upper hemisphere is approximately 5 quarks, with an equal excess of antiquarks over quarks in the lower hemisphere (derived from Eq. est2: N_{q-qbar} = 3 e (theta/pi) l).
- The resulting asymmetry between quarks and antiquarks is estimated as A = N_{q-qbar} / N_{q+qbar} ~ 3-5%, assuming a total multiplicity of approximately 500 hadrons for semi-central collisions at RHIC.
- The charge density accumulated at opposite sides of the system is given by sigma_{xy} = -N_c sum_f (e_f mu_f / 2 pi^2) Omega_z (theta / N_f), which is independent of the system size along the z direction.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Kharzeev (2004) that P- and CP-violation in heavy ion collisions should manifest as electric charge separation relative to the reaction plane, and provides a more detailed theoretical derivation using the effective Lagrangian approach. The authors discuss the preliminary STAR data from Selyuzhenkov (2005) and find qualitative agreement with their predictions, particularly the vanishing of the asymmetry in central collisions and its increase toward more peripheral events. The paper also connects this phenomenon to cosmological implications, suggesting that similar charge separation during the QCD phase transition in the early Universe could lead to the formation of stable quark nuggets that may serve as dark matter candidates, extending ideas from Zhitnitsky (2002) and Oaknin (2003).
