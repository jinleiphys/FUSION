# Neutrino Capture Cross Sections for Ar-40 and beta-decay of Ti-40

> Shell-model calculations of solar neutrino absorption cross sections for $^{40}$Ar, the proposed component of the ICARUS detector, are presented. It is found that low-lying Gamow-Teller transitions lead to a significant enhancement of the absorption rate over that expected from the Fermi transition between the isobaric analog states, leading to an overall absorption rate of 6.7 SNU. We also note that the pertinent Gamow-Teller transitions in $^{\sss 40}$Ar are experimentally accessible from the $β$-decay of the mirror nucleus $^{\sss 40}$Ti. Predictions for the branching ratios to states in $^{\sss 40}$Sc are presented, and the theoretical halflife of 53~ms is found to be in good agreement with the experimental value of $56^{+18}_{-12}$~ms.

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations show that low-lying Gamow-Teller transitions enhance the solar neutrino absorption cross section on Ar-40 by nearly a factor of three over the Fermi transition alone, yielding a total capture rate of 6.67 SNU. However, the authors note that the magnitude of the uncertainty in the theoretical results is difficult to predict, and the shell-model underpredicts the branching ratio to the first J^pi=1+ state.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations in a mixed (0+2)hbar-omega model space including the sd and fp oscillator shells, diagonalizing the 0hbar-omega and 2hbar-omega spaces separately to avoid the "n-hbar-omega truncation catastrophe." They used the Wildenthal matrix elements for the sd-shell, McGory's Hamiltonian for the fp-shell, and a modified Millener-Kurath potential for the cross-shell interaction, with the free-nucleon Gamow-Teller operators multiplied by 0.775 to account for quenching. The neutrino capture cross section was computed for all states in K-40 up to the particle-decay threshold at 7.58 MeV, folded with the B-8 solar neutrino spectrum, and the beta-decay of Ti-40 was calculated using the same transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Total solar neutrino absorption cross section on Ar-40: Sigma_tot = 11.5 x 10^-43 cm^2, corresponding to a capture rate of 6.67 SNU (compared to 2.2 SNU from the Fermi transition alone)
- Theoretical half-life of Ti-40: 53 ms, in agreement with the experimental value of 56 (+18, -12) ms
- The pure Fermi transition limit would give a Ti-40 half-life of 157 ms
- 20 +/- 4% of Ti-40 decays proceed via beta-decay to the second J^pi=1+ state in Sc-40 at 2.7 MeV, followed by proton emission
- The minimum electron kinetic energy observable in ICARUS: E_cut = 4.489 MeV
- Total integrated B-8 neutrino flux used: F = 5.8 x 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1
- The Hamiltonian reproduced ground-state binding energies for 25 nuclei with Z=13-20 with an rms deviation of 305 keV

## Context
This work extends earlier ICARUS detector feasibility assessments (ICARUS collaboration, 1991) that assumed neutrino absorption was dominated by Fermi transitions to the isobaric analog state, giving a capture rate of only 2.2 SNU. The calculations support the interpretation of existing experimental data from Moltz et al. (1994) on Ti-40 beta-decay, which showed that Gamow-Teller transitions play an important role. The authors strongly suggest that the Ti-40 beta-decay experiment be repeated with full detection of beta+ transitions and their corresponding proton decays to Sc-40, which would allow experimental determination of the B(GT) values and provide an accurate calibration for the ICARUS detector.
