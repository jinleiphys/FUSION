# Photoproduction of Pseudoscalar Mesons from Nuclei

> The subject of this doctoral study is the pseudoscalar meson photoproduction from nuclei. Two types of processes are investigated in this regard: coherent and quasifree processes. In the case of the coherent process, we study it for the photoproduction of pion and eta mesons. We place special emphasis on the various sources that put into question earlier nonrelativistic-impulse-approximation calculations. These include: final-state interactions, relativistic effects, off-shell ambiguities, and violations to the impulse approximation. By far the largest uncertainty emerges from the ambiguity in extending the many on-shell-equivalent representations of the elementary amplitude off the mass shell. In the case of the quasifree process, we study it for the photoproduction of the kaon meson. We compute the recoil polarization of the lambda-hyperon and the photon asymmetry as well as the differential cross section. By introducing the notion of a ``bound-nucleon propagator'' we exploit Feynman's trace techniques to develop closed-form, analytic expressions for all photoproduction observables. Our results indicate that polarization observables are sensitive only to the fundamental physics, making them ideal tools for the study of modifications to the elementary process in the nuclear medium.

# Photoproduction of Pseudoscalar Mesons from Nuclei

## Key claim

The largest uncertainty in coherent pseudoscalar meson photoproduction calculations arises from the ambiguity in extending the many on-shell-equivalent representations of the elementary amplitude off the mass shell, with two equivalent parameterizations yielding results that can differ by up to an order of magnitude when evaluated with bound (off-shell) nucleons.

## Method

The study employed a relativistic impulse approximation framework for coherent photoproduction of pions and eta mesons from nuclei, using the Walecka model for nuclear structure and maintaining full relativistic structure in both the elementary amplitude and the nuclear wavefunctions. For quasifree kaon photoproduction, the author introduced a "bound-nucleon propagator" and used Feynman trace techniques to develop closed-form analytic expressions for all photoproduction observables. Final-state interactions were incorporated through an optical potential formalism, distinguishing between the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) and the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA).

## Key numbers

- For coherent photoproduction, pionic distortions produced a factor-of-two enhancement at low energies and up to a factor-of-five reduction at high energies relative to plane-wave values
- Ambiguities from different choices of optical-model parameters were relatively small, at most 30%
- The tensor and vector parameterizations of the elementary amplitude, although equivalent on-shell, predicted coherent cross sections that differed by up to an order of magnitude when evaluated off-shell with bound nucleons
- For closed-shell nuclei with free-space relation between Dirac spinor components, the tensor density becomes proportional to the vector density as ρ_T(q) = -(q/2M_N) ρ_V(q) plus a negligible second term

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic impulse-approximation calculations by Boffi et al. (1986), Cenni et al. (1987), Bennhold and Tiator (1990), and others, which used nonrelativistic reductions of the elementary amplitude and nonrelativistic nuclear structure models. The author argues that maintaining full relativistic structure is necessary because nonrelativistic formalisms cannot produce more than one nuclear density and therefore miss the off-shell ambiguity entirely. The findings enable future work using effective microscopic models calibrated to reproduce two- and many-body scattering amplitudes, which could largely remove the off-shell ambiguity that currently forces reliance on the impulse approximation.
