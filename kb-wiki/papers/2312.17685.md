# Spinodal enhancement of fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Subensemble Acceptance Method (SAM) [1,2] is an essential link between measured event-by-event fluctuations and their grand canonical theoretical predictions such as lattice QCD. The method allows quantifying the global conservation law effects in fluctuations. In its basic formulation, SAM requires a sufficiently large system such as created in central nucleus-nucleus collisions and sufficient space-momentum correlations. Directly in the spinodal region of the First Order Phase Transition (FOPT) different approximations should be used that account for finite size effects. Thus, we present the generalization of SAM applicable in both the pure phases, metastable and unstable regions of the phase diagram [3]. Obtained analytic formulas indicate the enhancement of fluctuations due to crossing the spinodal region of FOPT and are tested using molecular dynamics simulations. A rather good agreement is observed. Using transport model calculations with interaction potential we show that the spinodal enhancement of fluctuations survives till the later stages of collision via the memory effect [4]. However, at low collision energies the space-momentum correlation is not strong enough for this signal to be transferred to second and third order cumulants measured in momentum subspace. This result agrees well with recent HADES data on proton number fluctuations at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV which are found to be consistent with the binomial momentum space acceptance [5].

# Spinodal enhancement of fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The generalized Subensemble Acceptance Method (SAM) predicts an enhancement of fluctuations when a system crosses the spinodal region of a first-order phase transition, but this enhancement is only transferred to higher-order cumulants (kurtosis) measured in momentum subspace at low collision energies, not to second- and third-order cumulants, due to insufficient space-momentum correlations.

## Method

The authors generalized the Subensemble Acceptance Method to apply in pure phases, metastable regions, and unstable (spinodal) regions of the phase diagram. They tested the analytic formulas using molecular dynamics simulations of a Lennard-Jones system in a box and transport model (UrQMD with a chiral mean-field potential) calculations with an artificially introduced first-order phase transition, comparing fluctuations in coordinate and momentum subspaces.

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance of particle number distribution in the central cell was computed for acceptance parameter α = 0.2 at reduced temperature T/T_c = 0.76 in the mixed phase.
- The cluster model described the nucleation region for reduced density 0.16 ≤ n/n_c ≤ 0.35, while the Minecraft model described the spinodal region for 0.35 ≤ n/n_c ≤ 1.75.
- Transport model calculations used a central cell volume of 27 fm³.
- HADES data at center-of-mass energy √s_NN = 2.4 GeV on proton number fluctuations were compared to binomial acceptance formulas for scaled variance ω[p], skewness Sσ[p], and kurtosis κσ²[p] as functions of rapidity interval Δy.
- The largest rapidity window used for input to binomial formulas was ΔY = 1.

## Context

This work extends the Subensemble Acceptance Method developed by Vovchenko (2020) and Poberezhnyuk (2020) from pure phase and supercritical regions into the mixed phase of a first-order phase transition. It builds on molecular dynamics studies of Lennard-Jones systems by Kuznietsov (2022) and transport model investigations by Savchuk (2022). The results explain why recent HADES data at √s_NN = 2.4 GeV show proton number fluctuations consistent with binomial acceptance, indicating that large fluctuations at the largest rapidity window are not local in rapidity space and require alternative explanations such as nucleon participant fluctuations or nuclear fragment contributions.
