# The scissors mode from a different perspective

> The scissors mode, a magnetic dipole excitation-mainly orbital is usually discussed in terms of a transition from a J=0^{+} ground state to a J=1^{+} excited state. This is understandable because it follows from the way the experiment is performed-e.g. inelastic electron scattering. Here however, we start with the excited 1^{+} state and consider all possible transitions to J=0,1 and 2 states.

# The scissors mode from a different perspective

## Key claim

The authors reverse the usual experimental perspective on the scissors mode by starting from the excited J=1+ state and considering all possible transitions to J=0+, 1+, and 2+ states, rather than only the conventional ground-state to excited-state transition. They find that the transition from the 1+ state to the 0₂+ state is often larger than the transition to the ground state, and that the "sum of sums" of all B(M1) values is independent of the interaction used.

## Method

The authors perform shell-model calculations for ⁴⁴Ti and ⁴⁶Ti using four different interactions: J=0 T=1 pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q), MBZE, and J_max T=0 pairing. They compute B(M1) values for transitions from J=1+ states to J=0+, 1+, and 2+ final states, tabulating results for each interaction and isotope. They also examine selection rules for vanishing B(M1) values and study the non-monotonic behavior of the 1₁⁺ to 0₁⁺ transition strength as a function of mixing between J=0 and J_max pairing interactions.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁶Ti with the Q.Q interaction, the B(M1) from 1₁ to 0₁ is 1.3901 (μ_N)², while the B(M1) from 1₁ to 0₂ is 2.6505 (μ_N)², nearly twice as large
- For ⁴⁶Ti with the MBZE interaction, the B(M1) from 1₁ to 0₁ is 0.55962 (μ_N)², significantly smaller than the Q.Q value of 1.3901 (μ_N)²
- For ⁴⁴Ti with J=0 pairing, the sum of all B(M1) values from all 1+ states to all 0+ states is 26.2296 (μ_N)², and to all 2+ states is 131.1483 (μ_N)²
- For ⁴⁴Ti with Q.Q, the sum of all B(M1) values from all 1+ states to all 0+ states is 26.2306 (μ_N)², confirming interaction independence of the sum of sums
- For a mixture of J=0 pairing (a=-1.15) and J_max pairing (b=-1), the B(M1) for 1₁⁺ to 0₁⁺ in ⁴⁶Ti reaches a minimum of 0.210 (μ_N)², compared to 1.082 for pure J=0 pairing and 1.641 for pure J_max pairing
- The diagonal "B(M1)" values for 1+ to 1+ transitions in ⁴⁴Ti are all 0.1466 (μ_N)², corresponding to a g-factor of 0.55

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Harper and Zamick, 2014) on selection rules for M1 transitions with J=0 T=1 pairing interactions, now making quantitative comparisons across multiple interactions. It is motivated by recent experimental work by Beller et al. (2014) on M1 excitations starting from I=1+ initial states. The paper builds on the extensive review of scissors modes by Heyde et al. (2010) and earlier foundational work by Bohle and Richter on the collective orbital magnetic dipole excitation picture. The results enable future studies of the double scissors mode interpretation for the 0₂⁺ state and demonstrate that the sum of all B(M1) strengths is a robust, interaction-independent quantity.
