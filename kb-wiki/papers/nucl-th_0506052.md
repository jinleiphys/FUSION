# Effective mass and decay of $Θ^+$ in nuclear matter in quark-meson coupling model

> The in-medium mass of a $\thetaplus$, $\mtheta^*$, in cold symmetric nuclear matter is calculated by using the quark-meson coupling model. The $Θ^+$ is treated as an MIT bag with the quark content $uudd\bar s$. Bag parameters for a free $\thetaplus$ are fixed to reproduce the observed mass of the $\thetaplus$. In doing so, we use three different values of the $s$-quark mass since the mass of the $s$-quark is not well known. As usual, the strengths of the $u$ and $d$ quark couplings to $σ$- and $ω$-meson fields are determined to fit the nuclear saturation properties. However, the coupling constant $g_σ^s$ between the $s$-quark and the $σ$-meson cannot be fixed from the saturation properties, and thus we treat $g_σ^s$ as a free parameter and investigate how $\mtheta^*$ depends on $g_σ^s$. %$\mtheta^*$ is calculated up to 2.5 times the nuclear saturation density, %and we find that We find that $\mtheta^*$ depends significantly on the value of $g_σ^s$ but not on the mass of the $s$-quark. Chemical potentials of the $Θ^+$ and the $K+N$ system are calculated to discuss the decay of a $Θ^+$ in nuclear matter. We calculate the effective mass of a kaon in nuclear matter in two ways; using the optical potential of $K^-$ in matter and using quark model. By comparing the effective masses calculated from these two methods, we find the magnitude of the real part of the optical potential that is consistent with the usual quark model is about 100 MeV.

# Effective mass and decay of $\Theta^+$ in nuclear matter in quark-meson coupling model

## Key claim

The in-medium mass of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark depends significantly on the coupling constant $g_\sigma^s$ between the $s$-quark and the $\sigma$-meson, but is insensitive to the strange quark mass $m_s$ or the bag radius $R_0$; the mass shift at saturation density is about 10% or more, but the amount varies widely depending on whether the standard QMC or modified QMC (MQMC) model is used.

## Method

The $\Theta^+$ is treated as an MIT bag with quark content $uudd\bar s$ in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, with bag parameters fixed to reproduce the free $\Theta^+$ mass of 1540 MeV. The $u$ and $d$ quark couplings to $\sigma$- and $\omega$-meson fields are determined from nuclear saturation properties, while $g_\sigma^s$ is treated as a free parameter (values -2, 0, 2). The effective mass of a kaon in nuclear matter is calculated both by treating the kaon as a meson bag and by using an optical potential approach, and chemical potentials of the $\Theta^+$ and $K+N$ system are compared to discuss in-medium decay.

## Key numbers

- Free $\Theta^+$ mass taken as $m_{\Theta^+} = 1540$ MeV, based on LEPS collaboration claim.
- Nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ and binding energy per nucleon $E_b = 16.0$ MeV used to fix $u,d$ quark couplings.
- In the QMC model at saturation density, $m_{\Theta^+}^*/m_{\Theta^+} = 0.90$ to 0.92 for $g_\sigma^s = 0$, independent of $m_s$ (50, 150, 300 MeV) and $R_0$ (0.6, 0.8, 1.0 fm).
- In the MQMC model at saturation density, $m_{\Theta^+}^*/m_{\Theta^+} = 0.82$ to 0.84 for $g_\sigma^s = 0$.
- The real part of the $K^-$ optical potential consistent with the quark model is about $U_{\bar K}(\rho_0) = -100$ MeV, yielding $g_{\sigma K} = 1.997$.
- For $U_{\bar K}(\rho_0) = -150$ MeV, the corresponding $g_\sigma^s$ value is 2.6.
- Critical densities for $\Theta^+$ decay: $\rho_1 \approx 0.75 \rho_0$ (below which $\Theta^+ \rightarrow KN$ is allowed) and $\rho_2 \approx 1.95 \rho_0$ (above which all $KN$ decay channels are closed).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the $\Theta^+$ in-medium mass by K. L. Lee (2004) and S. T. (2004), which gave conflicting results (nearly no change versus considerable change). The authors use the QMC model framework previously applied to nucleons and hypernuclei by Shen (2002) and Y. Fuji, and to kaon-nucleon systems by Gal and Oset. The results enable predictions for $\Theta^+$ production in heavy ion collisions and the possibility of stable $\Theta^+$ states in dense nuclear matter, with implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars.
