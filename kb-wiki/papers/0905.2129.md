# The Smoothness of Physical Observables

> The Garvey-Kelson (GK) relations are powerful algebraic expressions connecting the masses of neighboring atomic nuclei and derived under reasonable physical assumptions. In this contribution we show that these relations are even more general than originally assumed as their validity is conditioned by the smoothness of the underlying nuclear mass function and nothing else. Based on the assumed smoothness of the underlying physical function, the main conclusions from this study are: (1) the GK relations are model independent; (2) any slowly-varying physical observable satisfies the GK relations; and (3) the accuracy of the GK relations may be systematically improved. Examples from nuclear physics (charge radii) and from particle physics (baryon masses) are used to illustrate the flexibility of the approach.

## Key claim

The Garvey-Kelson (GK) relations are model independent and hold for any slowly-varying physical observable, not just nuclear masses; their validity depends only on the smoothness of the underlying function, not on the specific nuclear dynamics originally assumed.

## Method

The authors treat neutron number N and proton number Z as continuous variables and perform a Taylor series expansion of the nuclear mass function M(N,Z) around a reference point. They show that the two original GK relations, which connect masses of six neighboring nuclei, are proportional to third-order mixed derivatives of M(N,Z) plus higher-order terms. They also derive two additional relations connecting four nuclei along isotopic and isotonic chains, and demonstrate that extended relations involving 21 nuclei cancel all derivatives up to fifth order.

## Key numbers

- For a smooth liquid-drop mass formula, the GK mass relations are satisfied to better than 150 keV for A ≥ 50 and to better than 50 keV for A ≥ 100 for nuclei near the line of stability.
- Using the experimental database of nuclear masses, the GK relations are satisfied on average to about 180–200 keV throughout the periodic table.
- For nuclear charge radii, the GK relations yield an rms deviation of about 0.01 fm for approximately 130 nuclei that satisfy at least one of the two original GK relations.
- The charge radius of 195Pb is predicted to be R_ch = 5.437(5) fm from averaging four independent GK estimates.
- For the baryon octet, the Coleman-Glashow relation Δ_CG = (p-n) - (Σ⁺-Σ⁻) + (Ξ⁰-Ξ⁻) vanishes within experimental error.
- For charmed baryons, the linear combination 3Ξ_cc⁺⁺ - Ω_ccc⁺⁺ is predicted to be approximately 6328 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original Garvey-Kelson relations (Garvey and Kelson, 1966) by showing they follow solely from the smoothness of the mass function, a fact noted earlier by deShalit (1974). It generalizes the approach beyond nuclear masses to charge radii and baryon masses, reproducing the Coleman-Glashow relation (Coleman and Glashow, 1961) originally derived from flavor SU(3) symmetry. The results enable model-independent predictions for unknown observables such as the charge radius of 195Pb and masses of charmed baryons, and suggest the method can be systematically improved by using extended relations that cancel higher-order derivatives, as previously explored by Barea et al. (2005, 2008).
