# Deciphering the influence of neutron transfer in Si-based fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier

> Purpose: We aim to investigate the role of a few neutron transfer channels on the dynamics of fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier by judicially selecting 11 different $^{28,30}$Si-induced systems. These reactions are chosen in such a way that they possess positive and negative Q-values for neutron transfer channels to make the comparison more apparent. Furthermore, a comparative study on fusion barrier parameters using different proximity potentials and parametrizations is also a prime goal. Method: A channel coupling approach within the framework of a semiclassical model is being used to investigate the role of multi-neutron transfer with positive Q-values on fusion phenomena near and below the Coulomb barrier. The fusion barrier parameters have been extracted and analyzed within the framework of seven different potential models. Results: The sub-barrier fusion enhancement compared to the one-dimensional barrier penetration model (uncoupled) is investigated by considering collective excitations in colliding nuclei and multi-neutron transfer channels with Q $>$ 0 within the channel coupling model. Furthermore, GRAZING calculations are performed to predict the cross-section of target-like fragments after 2n pickup transfer. Conclusion: All the fusion excitation functions (EFs) have been successfully explained by the coupled channel calculations using the channel coupling model. Only the significant effect of up to 2n pickup transfer with Q $>$ 0 was found on sub-barrier fusion. Despite having positive Q values for transfer channels, no noticeable impact of more than 2n transfer was observed. GRAZING predictions are grossly in the same order as the quantitative contribution of 2n transfer channels observed by channel coupling model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that only up to two-neutron pickup transfer channels with positive Q-values significantly affect sub-barrier fusion enhancement in the selected Si-induced systems, while multi-neutron transfers beyond 2n show no noticeable impact even when they have positive Q-values. This conclusion is conditional on the specific systems studied and the empirical channel coupling model used, and the effect depends on the magnitude of the Q-value and the deformation changes after transfer.

## Method

The authors used an empirical channel coupling (ECC) approach within a semiclassical model to calculate fusion excitation functions for 11 different 28,30Si-induced reactions on Ni, Zr, and Nd targets. They incorporated collective excitations (rotational and vibrational) of the colliding nuclei and multi-neutron transfer channels with positive Q-values, considering up to four-neutron transfer. Fusion barrier parameters were extracted from experimental data using Wong's formula and compared with predictions from seven different proximity potential models. Additionally, GRAZING calculations were performed to predict cross-sections of target-like fragments after 2n pickup transfer.

## Key numbers

- For 28Si+64Ni, the Q-value for 2n pickup transfer is +2.6 MeV, and the sub-barrier fusion enhancement is significant; for 28Si+62Ni, Q+2n = +0.7 MeV shows no significant enhancement compared to the system without transfer
- For 28Si+92Zr, Q+2n = +3.3 MeV; for 28Si+94Zr, Q+2n = +4.1 MeV; for 28Si+96Zr, Q+2n = +4.8 MeV, all show significant 2n transfer effects on sub-barrier fusion
- For 28Si+144Nd, Q+2n = +5.1 MeV, and the main effect comes from 1n+2n channels on sub-barrier fusion
- All potential models except Prox 77 explain experimental barrier height and radius within 5% and 20% differences, respectively
- The Woods-Saxon ion-ion nuclear potential parameters for 28Si+64Ni: well depth V0 = -63.3 MeV, radius parameter r0 = 1.174 fm, diffuseness a = 0.654 fm, Bass barrier curvature hbar-omega-b = 3.56 MeV

## Context

This work extends the empirical channel coupling approach of Zagrebaev (2001) to systematically study the role of positive Q-value neutron transfer in fusion reactions, following experimental measurements by Stefanini (1984) for Ni isotopes, Kalkal (2010) and Khushboo (2017) for Zr isotopes, and Sinha (1997) for 144Nd. The study contradicts earlier expectations that positive Q-value neutron transfer always enhances sub-barrier fusion, showing that only up to 2n transfer matters and that the effect depends on the Q-value magnitude and deformation changes. The results enable better predictions for fusion cross-sections in heavy-ion reactions and provide benchmarks for future radioactive ion beam experiments at facilities like GSI/FAIR and RIKEN.
