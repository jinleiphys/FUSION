# A new leading contribution to neutrinoless double-beta decay

> Within the framework of chiral effective field theory we discuss the leading contributions to the neutrinoless double-beta decay transition operator induced by light Majorana neutrinos. Based on renormalization arguments in both dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction and a coordinate-space cutoff scheme, we show the need to introduce a leading-order short-range operator, missing in all current calculations. We discuss strategies to determine the finite part of the short-range coupling by matching to lattice QCD or by relating it via chiral symmetry to isospin-breaking observables in the two-nucleon sector. Finally, we speculate on the impact of this new contribution on nuclear matrix elements of relevance to experiment.

## Key claim

Within chiral effective field theory, the leading-order contribution to neutrinoless double-beta decay induced by light Majorana neutrinos requires a short-range operator that is missing in all current calculations; this operator must be promoted to leading order to achieve a finite, regulator-independent amplitude, contrary to the naive dimensional analysis assumed in prior work (Cirigliano 2017).

## Method

The authors analyzed the renormalization of the two-nucleon amplitude for $nn \to ppee$ in the $^1S_0$ partial wave using two regularization schemes: dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction ($\overline{\rm MS}$) and a coordinate-space cutoff scheme with a smeared delta-function regulator. They demonstrated that the two-loop diagram in the neutrino-exchange amplitude contains a logarithmic divergence that cannot be absorbed by the existing strong-interaction counterterms, requiring a new leading-order short-range $\Delta L=2$ contact operator with coupling $g_\nu^{N\!N}$. The finite part of this coupling was estimated by relating it via chiral symmetry to electromagnetic isospin-breaking observables in the two-nucleon sector, specifically the charge-independence-breaking combination of scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental half-life limits: $T_{1/2} > 5.3\times10^{25}$ y for $^{76}$Ge (Agostini 2017) and $T_{1/2} > 1.07\times10^{26}$ y for $^{136}$Xe (KamLAND-Zen 2016)
- The short-range coupling scales as $g_\nu^{N\!N} \sim \mathcal O(F_\pi^{-2})$ where $F_\pi = 92.2$ MeV, rather than $\mathcal O((4\pi F_\pi)^{-2})$ as in Weinberg's counting
- From fitting to charge-independence-breaking scattering lengths ($a_{np} = -23.7$ fm, $a_{nn} = -18.9$ fm, $a_{pp} = -7.8$ fm), the dimensionless combination $(\tilde C_1+\tilde C_2)/2 \approx 2.5$ at $\mu = m_\pi$ in $\overline{\rm MS}$ and $\approx 2.0$ at $R_S = 0.5$ fm in the cutoff scheme
- For $nn \to ppee$, the short-range contribution relative to the long-range neutrino-exchange amplitude is $\mathcal A_{\Delta L=2}^{(N\!N)}/\mathcal A_{\Delta L=2}^{(\nu)}\sim 30\%$ at $R_S \sim 0.1$ fm, decreasing to $\sim 10\%$ at $R_S \sim 0.6$ fm
- For $\Delta I=2$ nuclear transitions (e.g., $^{12}$Be $\rightarrow$ $^{12}$C), the short-range contribution is enhanced to $\mathcal A_{\Delta L=2}^{(N\!N)}/\mathcal A_{\Delta L=2}^{(\nu)}\sim 25\%$ (from the authors' fit), $\sim 55\%$ (fit from Piarulli 2014, 2016), and $\sim 60\%$ (using AV18 representation of short-range CIB potential)

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT analysis of neutrinoless double-beta decay initiated by Cirigliano (2017), which derived transition operators up to next-to-next-to-leading order in Weinberg's power counting. The authors demonstrate that Weinberg's scheme breaks down due to renormalization requirements, similar to known issues in nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kaplan 1996, Beane 2001, Nogga 2005) and nuclear processes with external currents (Valderrama 2014). The short-range coupling can be determined by matching to future lattice QCD calculations (Nicholson 2016, Shanahan 2017) or estimated from charge-independence-breaking observables using chiral symmetry relations. The results suggest that existing nuclear matrix element calculations, which omit this leading-order short-range operator, may need significant revision, particularly for $\Delta I=2$ transitions relevant to experimental searches.
