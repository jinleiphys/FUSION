# QCD resummation of dijet azimuthal decorrelations in pp and pA collisions

> We study the azimuthal angular decorrelations of dijet production in both proton-proton (pp) and proton-nucleus (pA) collisions. By utilizing soft-collinear effective theory, we establish the factorization and resummation formalism at the next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy for the azimuthal angular decorrelations in the back-to-back limit in pp collisions. We propose an approach where the nuclear modifications to dijet production in pA collisions are accounted for in the nuclear modified transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (nTMDPDFs), which contain both collinear and transverse dynamics. This approach naturally generalizes the well-established formalism related to the nuclear modified collinear parton distribution functions (nPDFs). We demonstrate strong consistency between our methodology and the CMS measurements in both pp and pA collisions, and make predictions for dijet production in the forward rapidity region in pA collisions at LHC kinematics and for mid-rapidity kinematics at sPHENIX. Throughout this paper, we focus on the application of this formalism to a simultaneous fit to both collinear and transverse momentum dependent contributions to the transverse momentum dependent distributions.

## Key claim

The authors establish a factorization and resummation formalism at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy for dijet azimuthal angular decorrelations in the back-to-back limit, and propose that nuclear modifications in proton-nucleus collisions can be accounted for through nuclear-modified transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (nTMDPDFs), though the applicability of nTMDPDFs to dijet production had not been previously determined.

## Method

Using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), the authors derived a factorization formula for the azimuthal angular distribution of dijet production in the joint back-to-back and narrow jet radius limit, employing the "direct" method to avoid divergences associated with the "indirect" method. They performed QCD resummation at NLL accuracy by solving renormalization group equations for the hard, jet, soft, and collinear-soft functions, and incorporated nuclear modifications by replacing standard TMDPDFs with nuclear-modified TMDPDFs (nTMDPDFs) within the TMD-like factorization formalism. Non-global logarithms were included using a fitting function at leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The factorization formula is derived at NLL accuracy for the azimuthal decorrelation in the back-to-back limit where $\delta\phi = \pi - \Delta\phi \to 0$, with the jet radius satisfying $R \ll 1$.
- The hard anomalous dimension matrix includes terms with $\gamma_{\rm cusp}(\alpha_s)$ up to two-loop order and single logarithms up to one-loop order, with the matrix $\bm{M}_{ab\to cd}$ containing terms proportional to $\ln r$ and $\ln(r/(1-r))$ where $r = -\hat t/\hat s$.
- The jet function anomalous dimension is given by $\Gamma^{J_i}(\alpha_s) = - C_i \gamma_{\rm cusp}(\alpha_s) \ln(p_T^2 R^2/\mu^2) + \gamma^{J_i}(\alpha_s)$, where $C_i = C_F$ or $C_A$ depending on the parton flavor.

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization approach for dijet azimuthal decorrelations previously developed for proton-proton collisions (Banfi 2008, Sun 2014, Chien 2019) to proton-nucleus collisions by introducing nTMDPDFs, generalizing the well-established nuclear-modified collinear PDF (nPDF) formalism (Eskola 2013, Hirai 2007, de Florian 2003). The formalism enables simultaneous extraction of both collinear and transverse momentum dependent contributions from bound nucleons inside heavy nuclei, which had not been performed previously, and provides a baseline for distinguishing nTMDPDF effects from saturation/color glass condensate effects (Mueller 2013, Taels 2022, Caucal 2023) in forward rapidity dijet production at the LHC and sPHENIX.
