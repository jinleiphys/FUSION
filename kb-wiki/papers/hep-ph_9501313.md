# Nuclear Shadowing in the Structure Function $F_3(x)$

> Nuclear modification of the structure function $F_3$ is investigated. Although it could be estimated in the medium and large $x$ regions from the nuclear structure function $F_2^A$, it is essentially unknown at small $x$. The nuclear structure function $F_3^A$ at small $x$ is investigated in two different theoretical models: a parton-recombination model with $Q^2$ rescaling and an aligned-jet model. We find that these models predict completely different behavior at small $x$: {\it antishadowing} in the first parton model and {\it shadowing} in the aligned-jet model. Therefore, studies of the ratio $F_3^A/F_3^D$ at small $x$ could be useful in discriminating among different models, which produce similar shadowing behavior in the structure function $F_2$. We also estimate currently acceptable nuclear modification of $F_3$ at small $x$ by using $F_2^A/F_2^D$ experimental data and baryon-number conservation.

## Key claim
The nuclear structure function ratio \(R_3 = F_3^A/F_3^D\) at small \(x\) is essentially unknown, and two different theoretical models predict completely opposite behavior there: antishadowing in a parton-recombination model with \(Q^2\) rescaling versus shadowing in an aligned-jet model. Both predictions are currently allowed by existing \(F_2\) data and baryon-number conservation, so measuring \(F_3^A/F_3^D\) at small \(x\) could discriminate between models that give similar results for \(F_2\).

## Method
The authors calculated the nuclear structure function \(F_3^A\) at small \(x\) using two distinct theoretical frameworks: a parton-recombination model with \(Q^2\) rescaling (applied to valence-quark distributions) and an aligned-jet model based on vector-meson-dominance extended to \(q\bar q\) continuum. They then estimated an experimentally allowed region for the ratio \(R_3\) by extrapolating SLAC \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) data for calcium at \(x>0.3\) into the small-\(x\) region, imposing baryon-number conservation and allowing up to 6% antishadowing at \(x=0.1-0.2\) based on NMC data and sea-quark distributions.

## Key numbers
- The parton-recombination model with \(Q^2\) rescaling predicts antishadowing (ratio above unity) at small \(x\) for calcium at \(Q^2=0.8\) GeV\(^2\).
- The aligned-jet model predicts shadowing (ratio below unity) at small \(x\), with \(F_3^A/F_3^N \approx F_2^A/F_2^N\).
- The upper bound for valence-quark antishadowing at \(x=0.1-0.2\) is estimated as roughly 6%, based on experimental antishadowing in \(F_2\) and nuclear sea-quark distributions.
- The baryon-number conservation integral \(\int dx V(x) = 3\) constrains the allowed region for \(R_3\) at small \(x\).

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear shadowing from the well-measured structure function \(F_2\) (where the EMC effect was first observed by the European Muon Collaboration) to the valence-dominated structure function \(F_3\). Prior models such as the parton-recombination model of Kumano and Miyama (1994) and the aligned-jet model of Frankfurt, Liuti, and Strikman (1994) produce similar shadowing in \(F_2\) but diverge for \(F_3\), making the ratio \(F_3^A/F_3^D\) a potential discriminator. The results enable future neutrino experiments to test these competing explanations of nuclear modification.
