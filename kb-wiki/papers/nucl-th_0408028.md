# Properties of Excited States in the ^{160}Dy Nucleus

> Positive-parity levels and 16 rotational bands are theoretically analyzed on the basis of phenomenological models of the atomic nucleus with the use of new experimental data on excited states in the ^{160}Dy nucleus recently gained in the investigation of the decay ^{160}Er &#8594; ^{160m,g}Ho &#8594; ^{160}Dy.

# Properties of Excited States in the \(^{160}\)Dy Nucleus

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM-1) in the SU(3) limit, extended with a pairing term, provides a satisfactory description of only the lowest excited positive-parity states in \(^{160}\)Dy, with an average deviation of 209.6 keV between calculated and experimental energies; the paper's central claim is that four phenomenological rotational formulae (Bohr-Mottelson, Q-phonon, variable moment of inertia, and a formula with Coriolis term) can reproduce the energies of 16 rotational bands with typical average deviations of a few keV to a few tens of keV, but only when the analysis is restricted to states below the \(\beta\)-decay energy of 3300 keV.

## Method

The authors analyzed positive-parity levels using the IBM-1 Hamiltonian with a pairing term in the SU(3) limit, fitting parameters \(k = 5.43\) keV, \(k' = 10.39\) keV, and \(k'' = 14.6\) keV to the experimental \(2^+_1\) (86.8 keV) and \(2^+_2\) (966.2 keV) energies. For 16 rotational bands (including ground-state, \(\gamma\)-vibrational, octupole-vibrational, \(S\)-band, and two-quasiparticle bands), they fitted level energies using four separate formulae: the Bohr-Mottelson expansion, the Q-phonon model, a variable-moment-of-inertia formula with a sign-changing term, and a formula including a Coriolis term. The calculations were confined to states with excitation energies not exceeding the \(^{160m,g}\)Ho \(\rightarrow\) \(^{160}\)Dy \(\beta\)-decay energy of 3300 keV.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state band: inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/2\Theta\) decreases smoothly from 14.46 keV (at \(2^+\) 86.8 keV) to 9.30 keV (at \(16^+\) 3091.7 keV); average deviation \(<|Ee-Ec|>\) does not exceed 7 keV for all four formulae.
- \(K^\pi = 2^+\) \(\gamma\)-vibrational band: best fit by Bohr-Mottelson formula (6) with average deviation 2.67 keV; calculations by (7), (8), and (9) give deviations no larger than 7 keV.
- \(K^\pi = 2^-\) octupole band: average deviations are a few tens of keV (e.g., 18.1 keV for formula (6), 22.6 keV for formula (7)).
- \(K^\pi = 0^+\) \(S\)-band: average deviation between 15 and 20 keV for all formulae; the \(0^+\) head state at 1456.7 keV has an estimated \(X = B(E0)/B(E2) < 2.2 \times 10^{-3}\).
- First \(K^\pi = 1^+\) band: average deviation approximately 4 keV for formulae (6), (8), and (9), but 21.33 keV for the three-parameter Q-phonon formula (7).
- Second \(K^\pi = 1^+\) band: average deviation does not exceed 4 keV in the worst case; inertia parameters range from 10.5 to 13.5 keV.
- \(K^\pi = 1^-\) octupole band: formulae (8) and (9) give average deviations about 5 keV; formula (6) gives 18.1 keV, formula (7) gives 22.6 keV.
- Second \(K^\pi = 1^-\) band: average deviation ranges from 1.22 to 4.86 keV across all four formulae.
- \(K^\pi = 0^+\) band on \(0^+_2\) state (1280.0 keV): \(X = 0.31\) for the \(0^+\) state at 1280.0 keV.
- \(K^\pi = 0^+\) band on \(0^+_4\) state (1708.2 keV): estimated \(X = B(E0)/B(E2) < 3.5 \times 10^{-2}\); inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/2\Theta = 8.11\) keV.
- \(K^\pi = 0^+\) band on \(0^+_5\) state: inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/2\Theta = 10.07\) keV.
- \(K^\pi = 0^-\) (octupole?) band: inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/2\Theta = 15.38\) keV.
- \(K^\pi = 4^-\) band: average deviation approximately 2 keV for all formulae.
- \(K^\pi = 4^+\) band: average deviation no larger than 0.2 keV for all formulae.
- Second \(I^\pi = 4^+\) band: inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/2\Theta = 9.76\) keV.
- Highest experimentally observed state in the ground-state band: \(I^\pi = 28^+\) at 7231 keV (from reaction with \(^7\)Li ions, reference [8]).
- Highest experimentally observed state in the \(K^\pi = 2^-\) octupole band: \(I^\pi = 26^-\) at 6967 keV (from reference [8]).

## Context

This work extends the experimental level scheme of \(^{160}\)Dy from the decay chain \(^{160}\)Er \(\rightarrow\) \(^{160m,g}\)Ho \(\rightarrow\) \(^{160}\)Dy reported in the authors' companion paper (Adam et al., 2004, reference [2]), adding over a hundred new levels to those previously known from nuclear reactions, Coulomb excitation, and \(\beta\) decays of \(^{160}\)Tb and \(^{160}\)Ho (reference [1]). The theoretical analysis compares four phenomenological rotational models (Bohr-Mottelson, Q-phonon from reference [5], variable moment of inertia from reference [6], and a formula with Coriolis term from reference [7]) against the expanded experimental data, and tests the IBM-1 in the SU(3) limit against positive-parity states. The results enable future searches for predicted but unobserved band members (e.g., \(6^+\) and \(8^+\) states in several \(K^\pi = 0^+\) bands) and clarify the assignment of two-quasiparticle configurations, particularly resolving which of two \(K^\pi = 1^+\) bands corresponds to the \((n5/2[523]-n3/2[521])\) configuration.
