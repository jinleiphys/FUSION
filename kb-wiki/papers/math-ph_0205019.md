# Determining Static Stresses of Deformed Solitons

> An equation for the quasi-static soliton ansatz depending on an arbitrary set of collective variables is covariantly derived on the basis of the variational approach to the method of collective variables. The field configuration and the static stresses of the deformed $Φ^4$-kink that are produced by the excitation of the internal soliton mode are exactly determined. The kink interaction potential at large distances is considered for the example of the nonlinear Klein-Gordon system. A general approach to the problem of exactly determining the intersoliton potential for the entire set of physically admissible two-soliton configurations is discussed.

# Determining Static Stresses of Deformed Solitons

## Key claim
The paper derives a covariant equation for quasi-static soliton configurations that is invariant under reparameterization of collective variables, and uses it to exactly determine the field configuration and static stresses of a deformed Φ⁴-kink under excitation of its internal mode. The approach is limited to quasi-static configurations where the collective variables are non-Goldstone coordinates, and the exact solution for the deformed kink is valid only within a finite range of the internal mode amplitude.

## Method
The author uses a variational approach on a Riemannian manifold interpretation of field configuration space, projecting the Hamiltonian gradient onto directions orthogonal to the submanifold defined by collective coordinates. For the Φ⁴-kink with an internal mode, the resulting equation reduces to a nonlinear diffusion equation after fixing a gauge condition, yielding exact solutions. For the kink-antikink and kink-kink interaction problem in nonlinear Klein-Gordon systems, the equation is linearized at large intersoliton distances to derive asymptotic expressions for the interaction potential.

## Key numbers
- The internal mode frequency of the Φ⁴-kink is ω² = 3.
- The energy of the deformed Φ⁴-kink is E(q) = 4/3 + (3/2)q² + O(q³) for small deformations, where the first term (4/3) is the kink mass.
- The physically admissible range of the internal mode amplitude q is confined to the interval -√(2/3) < q < +√(2/3).
- The asymptotic intersoliton potential at large distances is E(R) = 2M ∓ 2ma²e^(-2mr), where the minus sign gives attraction for kink-antikink and the plus sign gives repulsion for kink-kink configurations.

## Context
This work extends the variational approach to collective variables previously developed by Tsukanov (2002) and addresses difficulties in defining intersoliton distance for deformed solitons, as distinctly stated by M2. The asymptotic interaction formula for Φ⁴ and sine-Gordon systems was previously found by R2 using an approximate equation with a phenomenological delta-shaped source. The approach enables numerical determination of the entire set of physically admissible two-soliton configurations via a nonlinear diffusion equation, and is particularly relevant for the Skyrme model where exact treatment of soliton deformation at small distances is needed for nucleon-nucleon potential calculations.
