# Nuclear level densities away from line of $β$-stability

> The variation of total nuclear level densities (NLDs) and level density parameters with proton number $(Z)$ are studied around the $β$-stable isotope, $Z_{0}$, for a given mass number. We perform our analysis for a mass range $A=40$ to 180 using the NLDs from popularly used databases obtained with the single-particle energies from two different microsopic mass-models. These NLDs which include microscopic structural effects such as collective enhancement, pairing and shell corrections, do not exhibit inverted parabolic trend with a strong peak at $Z_{0}$ as predicted earlier. We also compute the NLDs using the single-particle energies from macroscopic-microscopic mass-model. Once the collective and pairing effects are ignored, the inverted parabolic trends of NLDs and the corresponding level density parameters become somewhat visible. Nevertheless, the factor that governs the $(Z-Z_{0})$ dependence of the level density parameter, leading to the inverted parabolic trend, is found to be smaller by an order of magnitude. We further find that the $(Z-Z_{0})$ dependence of NLDs is quite sensitive to the shell effects.

# Nuclear level densities away from line of β-stability

## Key claim

The inverted parabolic trend of nuclear level densities (NLDs) with a strong peak at the β-stable isotope Z₀, predicted by earlier parameterizations, is not observed when microscopic structural effects (collective enhancement, pairing, and shell corrections) are included. When collective and pairing effects are ignored, the inverted parabolic trend becomes somewhat visible, but the factor governing the (Z−Z₀) dependence of the level density parameter is smaller by an order of magnitude than previously reported.

## Method

The authors analyzed total NLDs from the HF (Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing) and HFB (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) databases for masses A = 40 to 180 at excitation energies E* = 10 and 30 MeV, which include collective enhancement, pairing, and shell corrections. They also computed NLDs using the statistical model with single-particle energies from the macroscopic-microscopic Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), systematically removing collective enhancement, pairing, and shell effects to isolate their contributions. The level density parameters were extracted from the FRDM-based NLDs and fitted to the AGMR form (AlQuraishi et al., 2003) to obtain the parameters α and γ.

## Key numbers

- Mass range analyzed: A = 40 to 180, with Z₀ values from the semi-empirical mass formula ranging from Z₀ = 19 (A=40) to Z₀ = 74 (A=180).
- The fitted parameter α from FRDM-based NLDs at E* = 30 MeV: α = 0.1048 ± 0.0024 MeV⁻¹, close to the AGMR value of 0.1068 MeV⁻¹.
- The fitted parameter γ from FRDM-based NLDs at E* = 30 MeV: γ = 0.0036 ± 0.0018, which is smaller by an order of magnitude than the AGMR value of 0.0389.
- The AGMR form predicts a reduction in level density parameter by a factor of 2 for (Z−Z₀) = 4, corresponding to a reduction in level density by approximately 10⁴ for A = 200 at E* = 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of (Z−Z₀) dependence of NLDs beyond the earlier parameterization by AlQuraishi et al. (2003), which was based on fits to data mostly for |Z−Z₀| ≤ 1 and predicted strong suppression for larger deviations. Recent measurements for ⁷⁰Ni (Z−Z₀ = 3) by Liddick et al. (2016) and ¹¹⁵Te (Z−Z₀ = 2) by Roy et al. (2020) showed that the strong reduction predicted by AlQuraishi et al. (2003) was overestimated. The present results, showing a weaker (Z−Z₀) dependence and the dominant role of shell effects, enable improved extrapolations of NLDs for nuclei far from stability, which are critical inputs for astrophysical reaction rate calculations. The weaker γ value for heavier masses is consistent with suggestions by Grimes et al. (2008).
