# Quasielastic charged-current neutrino scattering in the scaling model with relativistic effective mass

> We use a recent scaling analysis of the quasielastic electron scattering data from $^{12}$C to predict the quasielastic charge-changing neutrino scattering cross sections within an uncertainty band. We use a scaling function extracted from a selection of the $(e,e')$ cross section data, and an effective nucleon mass inspired by the relativistic mean-field model of nuclear matter. The corresponding super-scaling analysis with relativistic effective mass (SuSAM*) describes a large amount of the electron data lying inside a phenomenological quasielastic band. The effective mass incorporates the enhancement of the transverse current produced by the relativistic mean field. The scaling function incorporates nuclear effects beyond the impulse approximation, in particular meson-exchange currents and short range correlations producing tails in the scaling function. Besides its simplicity, this model describes the neutrino data as reasonably well as other more sophisticated nuclear models.

## Key claim

The SuSAM* model predicts quasielastic charged-current neutrino scattering cross sections on $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O within an uncertainty band inherited directly from electron scattering data, under the assumption that the same phenomenological scaling function extracted from $(e,e')$ data applies to neutrino scattering by replacing electromagnetic currents with vector and axial weak currents. The authors acknowledge that the model does not include 2p-2h meson-exchange currents or pion emission explicitly, and that the uncertainty band should be considered an upper limit on systematic error from nuclear modeling of QE processes.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean-field (Walecka) model of nuclear matter with a relativistic effective mass $M^*=0.8$ and Fermi momenta $k_F=225$ MeV/c for $^{12}$C and $k_F=230$ MeV/c for $^{16}$O, taken from a previous superscaling analysis of $(e,e')$ data. The nuclear response functions are factorized into single-nucleon responses times a phenomenological scaling function $f^*(\psi^*)$ extracted from a subset of about 20000 existing $(e,e')$ data points on $^{12}$C, parameterized as a sum of two Gaussians with central, minimum, and maximum coefficients. The scaling function incorporates nuclear effects beyond the impulse approximation, including meson-exchange currents and short-range correlations that produce tails in the scaling function. The neutrino cross sections are computed by folding the model predictions with the MiniBooNE and T2K neutrino fluxes.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass $M^* = 0.8$ (dimensionless, relative to free nucleon mass) used for both $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O
- Fermi momentum $k_F = 225$ MeV/c for $^{12}$C and $k_F = 230$ MeV/c for $^{16}$O
- Scaling function central parameters: $a_1 = -0.0465$, $a_2 = 0.469$, $a_3 = 0.633$, $b_1 = 0.707$, $b_2 = 1.073$, $b_3 = 0.202$
- Scaling function minimum band parameters: $a_1 = -0.0270$, $a_2 = 0.442$, $a_3 = 0.598$, $b_1 = 0.967$, $b_2 = 0.705$, $b_3 = 0.149$
- Scaling function maximum band parameters: $a_1 = -0.0779$, $a_2 = 0.561$, $a_3 = 0.760$, $b_1 = 0.965$, $b_2 = 1.279$, $b_3 = 0.200$
- Fermi constant $G = 1.166 \times 10^{-11}$ MeV$^{-2}$ and Cabibbo angle $\cos\theta_c = 0.975$ used in cross section normalization
- About one third of the approximately 20000 existing $(e,e')$ data points approximately scale to a universal superscaling function

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass (SuSAM*) previously applied to electron scattering by Amaro et al. (2015, 2017) to neutrino and antineutrino quasielastic scattering. It differs from the traditional SuSA approach of Megias et al. (2016, 2018) by using different assumptions and definitions for the scaling functions and variables, and from the RMF model of Udias et al. (2011, 2013) by using constant potentials with a fixed effective mass rather than local potentials in finite nuclei. The model enables direct translation of the uncertainty band from $(e,e')$ data to neutrino cross sections, providing a simple benchmark that any more sophisticated nuclear model should reproduce; the authors note that the MiniBooNE and T2K data generally fall within the predicted band, with systematic differences attributed to 2p-2h effects and other scaling-violating processes not included in the model.
