# Chiral symmetry, the angular content of the vector current in QED and QCD, and the holographic description of hadrons

> We perform a general chiral symmetry and unitarity based analysis of a local process of the fermion-antifermion creation from the vacuum by a high-energy photon as well as an explicit partial wave analysis of the vector current in QED and QCD. It turns out that such a local process proceeds necessarily via a certain superposition of the $S$- and $D$-wave contributions. These constraints from chiral symmetry and unitarity are confronted then with the well-known theoretical and experimental results on $e^+e^-\toγ\to e^+e^-$, $e^+e^-\toγ\to μ^+μ^-$, and $e^+e^-\toγ\to q\bar{q}$ in the ultrarelativistic limit. It is shown that these well-known results are consistent with the $S+D$-wave structure of the vertex and are inconsistent with the pure $S$-wave interpretation of the vertex. Then a free quark loop in the $1^{--}$ channel, representing the leading term in the Operator Product Expansion, contains both $S$-wave and $D$-wave contributions. This fact rules out the possibility that there is only one radial trajectory for the $ρ$-mesons with the fixed $S$-wave content. It also implies that all holographic models that assume a pure $S$-wave content of the $ρ$-meson have to fail to satisfy the matching conditions at the ultraviolet border $z=0$.

# Chiral symmetry, the angular content of the vector current in QED and QCD, and the holographic description of hadrons

## Key claim
The local vertex for creation of a fermion–antifermion pair from the vacuum by a high-energy photon necessarily proceeds via a fixed superposition of S-wave and D-wave contributions, not a pure S-wave, as required by chiral symmetry and unitarity. This conclusion applies in the ultrarelativistic limit where fermion masses can be neglected and chiral symmetry is unbroken.

## Method
The authors perform a general chiral symmetry and unitarity analysis of the fermion–antifermion creation vertex, relating the chiral basis of SU(2)L × SU(2)R representations to the {2S+1LJ} basis via a unitary transformation. They then carry out an explicit partial wave analysis of the vector current in QED and QCD, using vector spherical harmonics to decompose the squared amplitude for e+e− → γ → f f̄ and compare the resulting angular distribution with the chiral symmetry prediction.

## Key numbers
- The squared amplitude for e+e− → γ → f f̄ in the ultrarelativistic limit is ∑|M|² = 1 + cos²θ, which contains a cos²θ term indicating D-wave contribution.
- The total cross-section for e+e− → γ → f f̄ is σ = πα²Q²/(3E²) (for Q=1).
- The vector current decomposition is |q̄γⁱq⟩ = √(2/3)|³S₁⟩ + √(1/3)|³D₁⟩, with coefficients 2/3 and 1/3 for S-wave and D-wave respectively.
- The pseudotensor current decomposition is |q̄σ⁰ⁱq⟩ = √(1/3)|³S₁⟩ − √(2/3)|³D₁⟩.
- If the D-wave contribution were removed, the total cross-section would become only 2/3 of the correct result.

## Context
This work extends the chiral symmetry classification of qq̄ states developed by Glozman (2004, 2007) and the unitary transformation between chiral and LS bases by Glozman and Nefediev (2008). It contradicts the common assumption, cited as Ref. [Swave1], that the point-like photon–fermion vertex has a pure S-wave structure. The results rule out holographic models such as those by Brodsky and de Teramond (2008) that assume fixed L=0 for ρ-mesons at the ultraviolet boundary, and also rule out the possibility of a single radial ρ-meson trajectory with fixed S-wave content as assumed in semiclassical Regge models by Shifman and Vainshtein. The analysis enables proper matching conditions for AdS/QCD holographic models at z=0 and constrains the Operator Product Expansion in the vector-isovector channel.
