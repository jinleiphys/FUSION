# Superfluidity in neutron stars and cold atoms

> We discuss superfluidity in neutron matter, with particular attention to induced interactions and to universal properties accessible with cold atoms.

# Superfluidity in neutron stars and cold atoms

## Key claim
Superfluidity in neutron matter is strongly influenced by induced interactions (polarization effects), which reduce the S-wave pairing gap by a factor of 3–4 compared to BCS predictions, and the universal properties of dilute Fermi gases with large scattering lengths allow cold atom experiments to constrain low-density neutron matter.

## Method
The author solves the BCS gap equation with a free spectrum using low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions (Vlowk) with cutoff Λ > 1.6 fm⁻¹, and applies a one-loop renormalization group (phRG) equation in the particle-hole channels starting from Vlowk to include induced interactions beyond the perturbative limit. For the ³P₂–³F₂ channel, non-central spin-orbit and tensor interactions are included, with a first perturbative calculation of non-central induced interactions.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.5 ± 0.3 fm
- Maximum ¹S₀ superfluid pairing gap including polarization effects: Δ ≈ 0.8 MeV (a factor 3–4 reduction from BCS)
- For k_F ≈ 0.4 fm⁻¹, neutron matter has k_F r_e ≈ 1 and Δ/ε_F ≈ 0.1
- The dilute limit reduction factor for the S-wave gap: (4e)^(-1/3) ≈ 0.45
- ³P₂–³F₂ gaps below 10 keV are possible from non-central induced interactions, with ⟨V_ind⟩/⟨Vlowk⟩ < 0.5

## Context
This work extends the BCS treatment of neutron superfluidity by incorporating induced interactions through the renormalization group approach, building on the low-momentum interactions Vlowk developed by the author's group. The results are compared to the GFMC calculations of Carlson et al. for cold atoms in the unitary regime and to the CBF results of the AFDMC group, which do not include long-range polarization effects. The findings impact neutron star cooling models (Yakovlev) and suggest core neutrons become superfluid only at late times (t ~ 10⁵ years).
