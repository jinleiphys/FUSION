# Radiative proton capture on $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ within effective field theory

> The astrophysical $S$ factor for the radiative proton capture process on the $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ nucleus, i.e., $^{15}\mathrm{N}(p, γ)^{16}\mathrm{O}$, at stellar energies are studied within the framework of the cluster effective field theory. The thermonuclear $^{15}\mathrm{N}(p, γ)^{16}\mathrm{O}$ reaction links the type-I to type-II cycles of the carbon-nitrogen-oxygen cycle and affects the abundances of elements in the univere. For investigating this reaction in the effective field theory formalism, we first construct an effective Lagrangian that is appropriate for this reaction at low-energies. Since the intermediate excited states of the $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ nucleus have a crucial role in this reaction, we include these resonances in the formalism. The corresponding radiative capture amplitudes and cross section are calculated, which lead to the astrophysical $S$ factor. The low energy constants introduced in the effective Lagrangian are determined by fitting the theoretical results to the observed $S$ factors in the range of $130~\mathrm{keV} < E_p < 2500~\mathrm{keV}$ using three different experimental data sets. Considering the recent data sets, we obtain $S(0) = 29.8\mbox{-}34.1~\mathrm{keV\ b}$, which is in a good agreement with the estimates from $R$-matrix approaches in the literature. The values of $S$ at the Gamow energy are found to be larger than $S(0)$ values by about $10\%$.

# Radiative proton capture on $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ within effective field theory

## Key claim

The astrophysical $S$ factor at zero energy for $^{15}\mathrm{N}(p, \gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}$ is determined to be $S(0) = 29.8\text{-}34.1~\mathrm{keV~b}$ when fitting to recent experimental data sets, but this result depends on which experimental data sets are used and on the truncation order of the effective range expansion for the two resonant states.

## Method

The authors construct a cluster effective field theory Lagrangian treating the proton, $^{15}\mathrm{N}$, and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ as point-like particles, with two $s$-wave resonant states of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ represented as auxiliary di-fields with isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$. The radiative capture amplitudes are calculated from Feynman diagrams including non-resonant direct capture, resonant capture through the two intermediate states, and counterterm diagrams, with Coulomb interactions between the proton and $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ incorporated via the Coulomb Green's function. The low-energy constants and effective range parameters are determined by fitting the theoretical astrophysical $S$ factor to three different experimental data sets in the proton energy range $130~\mathrm{keV} < E_p < 2500~\mathrm{keV}$.

## Key numbers

- $S(0) = 29.8\text{-}34.1~\mathrm{keV~b}$ for the astrophysical $S$ factor at zero energy, obtained from fitting to recent experimental data sets
- $S$ values at the Gamow energy are larger than $S(0)$ values by about $10\%$
- The first resonance energy is $E_R = 312~\mathrm{keV}$ and the second resonance energy is $E_R = 964~\mathrm{keV}$ in the center-of-mass frame
- The binding momentum of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ relative to the $p$-$^{15}\mathrm{N}$ threshold is $\gamma \approx 146~\mathrm{MeV}$
- The large energy scale is set by the breakup energy $\Delta E = 3.54~\mathrm{MeV}$, giving $\Lambda_H = 80~\mathrm{MeV}$
- The low momentum scales are $Q_1 = 24~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $Q_2 = 41~\mathrm{MeV}$, yielding expansion parameters $Q_1/\Lambda_H \simeq 0.3$ and $Q_2/\Lambda_H \simeq 0.5$
- Theoretical uncertainties are estimated as $(Q_1/\Lambda_H)^2 \simeq 0.09$ for the first resonance and $(Q_2/\Lambda_H)^6 \simeq 0.016$ for the second resonance

## Context

This work extends the cluster effective field theory approach previously applied to reactions such as $\alpha$ capture on $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ (Ando, 2018) and radiative proton capture on $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ (KKS, 2017) to the $^{15}\mathrm{N}(p, \gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}$ reaction, which links the type-I and type-II cycles of the CNO cycle. The results are consistent with $R$-matrix analyses in the literature, including the post-NACRE value of $33.1~\mathrm{keV~b}$ (MLK, 2011), and improve upon the authors' preliminary work (Son, Ando, Oh, 2022) that considered only the first resonance. The single-channel calculation presented here provides a foundation for future coupled-channel calculations that would include the $\alpha$-$^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and $\alpha$-$^{12}\mathrm{C}^*$ channels.
