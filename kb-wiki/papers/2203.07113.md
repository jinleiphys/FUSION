# Energy-Energy Correlators for Precision QCD

> In this contribution to the Proceedings of the US Community Study on the Future of Particle Physics (Snowmass 2021) we review recent progress in the evaluation and application of the Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC) event shape observable in $e^+e^-$ annihilation, hadronic collisions, and deep inelastic scattering. The importance of EEC as a precision probe of the perturbative and non perturbative aspects of QCD dynamics is emphasized. It can be used to extract the strong coupling constant and to constrain TMD distribution functions. Closely related energy-correlation shape variables have also been used to tag boosted objects produced in high energy collisions. The opportunities to study EEC at the future Electron-Ion Collider are also highlighted.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress on Energy-Energy Correlators (EEC) as precision probes of QCD, claiming that the back-to-back limit of EEC in electron-positron annihilation has been resummed analytically at N3LL' accuracy, making it the first event shape to achieve this level of analytic precision; however, the authors note that for a full phenomenological analysis including extraction of the strong coupling constant, several additional effects such as electroweak corrections, finite quark mass effects, and hadronization corrections must still be incorporated.

## Method

The authors derive factorization theorems for EEC in the back-to-back limit using Soft and Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), relating the observable to transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) and the transverse momentum distribution for di-hadron production. They compute the EEC jet function at N3LO and combine it with known hard and soft functions and anomalous dimensions to achieve N3LL' resummation, solving renormalization group equations in both the virtuality scale mu and the rapidity scale nu. For hadronic collisions, they present a factorization formula for the transverse-energy-energy correlator (TEEC) involving matrix-valued hard and soft functions in color space, validated against fixed-order predictions from NLOJET++.

## Key numbers

- The EEC quark and gluon jet functions were calculated at N3LO, providing the last missing ingredient for the complete analytic result at O(alpha_s^3) in QCD for the back-to-back limit
- The cusp anomalous dimension is known to 4 loops, and the non-cusp hard anomalous dimension has been obtained at 4 loops in QCD
- The rapidity anomalous dimension has been calculated to N3LO
- ATLAS measurement of TEEC at 13 TeV yields alpha_S(m_Z) = 0.1196 +/- 0.0004 (exp.) +0.0072/-0.0105 (theo.), using only data for |cos phi| < 0.92

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of EEC from earlier fixed-order calculations by Dixon (2018), Luo (2019), and Belitsky (2013), and from resummation studies by Moult (2018), Dixon (2019), Kologlu (2019), Korchemsky (2019), and Chen (2020). The N3LL' resummation of EEC in the back-to-back limit by Ebert (2020) constitutes the first event shape resummed analytically at this order, surpassing the status of thrust and C-parameter where the O(alpha_s^3) soft function remains unknown analytically. The TEEC factorization formula by Gao (2019) provides a framework for precision QCD studies at hadron colliders, enabling future extractions of the strong coupling constant using data from the back-to-back region that was previously excluded due to poor theoretical control, and opens the possibility to probe factorization breaking effects and gluon spin correlations at higher orders.
