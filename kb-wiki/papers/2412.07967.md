# Melting of $c \bar c$ and $b \bar b$ pairs in the pre-equilibrium stage of proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We study the melting of $c\bar c$ and $b\bar b$ pairs in the early stage of high-energy proton-nucleus collisions. We describe the early stage in terms of an evolving $SU(3)$ glasma stage, that is dominated by intense, out-of-equilibrium gluon fields. On top of these fields, we liberate heavy quark-antiquark pairs, whose constituents are let evolve according to relativistic kinetic theory coupled to the gluon fields. We define a pair-by-pair probability that the pair melts during the evolution, which we relate to the fluctuations of the color charges induced by the interaction of the quarks with the gluon fields. We find that color decorrelation is the main melting mechanism within the pre-equilibrium stage. Moreover, we estimate that within $Δτ\approx0.4-0.5$ fm/c after the formation time of the pairs, about $50\%$ of $c\bar c$ and $b\bar b$ pairs are melted.

## Key claim

The authors find that color decorrelation of the heavy quark-antiquark pair, rather than spatial separation, is the dominant melting mechanism within the pre-equilibrium glasma stage of proton-nucleus collisions, with approximately 50% of both charm and bottom pairs melting within 0.4-0.5 fm/c after formation, though this estimate depends on the specific definition of pair survival based on color charge fluctuations.

## Method

The early stage of proton-nucleus collisions is modeled as an evolving SU(3) glasma using classical Yang-Mills theory on a lattice, with color charges generated via the McLerran-Venugopalan model including event-by-event fluctuations for the proton. Heavy quark-antiquark pairs are initialized at their formation time and evolved using the Wong equations for relativistic kinetic theory coupled to the background gluon fields, including a dynamically evolving color-dependent potential between the quark and antiquark that accounts for transitions between color-singlet and color-octet states.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 50% of both c-cbar and b-bbar pairs melt within Delta tau = 0.4-0.5 fm/c after the formation time of the pairs
- Charm quark mass M = 1.3 GeV, bottom quark mass M = 4.2 GeV, giving formation times tau_form ~ 0.08 fm/c for charm and tau_form ~ 0.02 fm/c for bottom
- The initial relative distance distribution for c-cbar pairs uses r_0 = 0.4 fm, for b-bbar pairs r_0 = 0.2 fm
- The coupling constant g = 2, corresponding to alpha_s = 0.3
- The MV parameter for the nucleus is mu = 0.5 GeV, with N_s = 50 color sheets
- The screening mass m = 0.2 GeV = 1 fm^{-1}
- The transverse lattice size L = 3 fm with 64x64 lattice points
- Simulations run up to 1 fm/c with 1000 time steps and 1000 HQ pairs per event

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in the glasma by Ruggieri (2018), Sun (2019), and Liu (2019-2020) to the specific case of heavy quarkonium dissociation in proton-nucleus collisions. It differs from the earlier work by Pooja (2024) by using SU(3) instead of SU(2), including transverse expansion and event-by-event fluctuations, and implementing a survival probability based on color charge decorrelation rather than spatial separation criteria. The results enable improved modeling of heavy quarkonium suppression in small collision systems where the thermalized QGP stage is short-lived, potentially providing cleaner signatures of the glasma stage.
