# Relativistic hydrodynamics with phase transition

> Assessing the applicability of hydrodynamic expansions close to phase transition points is crucial from either theoretical or phenomenological points of view. We explore this within the gauge/gravity duality, using the Einstein-Klein-Gordon model, a bottom-up string theory construction. This model incorporates a parameter, $B_4$, that simulates different types of phase transitions in the strongly coupled field theory existing at the boundary. We thoroughly examine the thermodynamics and dynamics of time-dependent, linearized perturbations in the spin-2, spin-1, and spin-0 sectors. Our findings suggest that "hydrodynamic series breakdown near transition points" is valid exclusively for second-order phase transitions, not for crossovers or first-order phase transitions. Additionally, we observe that the high-temperature and low-temperature limits of the radius of convergence for the hydrodynamic series ($q^2_c$) are equal. We also discover that the relationship $(\text{Max}\vert q^2_c \vert)_{\text{spin-2}} < (\text{Max}\vert q^2_c\vert)_{\text{spin-0}} < (\text{Max}\vert q^2_c \vert)_{\text{spin-1}}$ is consistent for different spin sectors, regardless of the phase transition type. At the chaos point, we observe the emergence of pole-skipping behavior for both gravity and scalar perturbations at $ω_n = - 2πT n i$. Lastly, comparing the chaos momentum with $q^2_c$, we find that $q^2_{ps} < q^2_c$, except for extremely high temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the paradigm "hydrodynamic series breakdown near transition points" is valid exclusively for second-order phase transitions, not for crossovers or first-order phase transitions, based on a holographic Einstein-Klein-Gordon model with parameter $B_4$ controlling the transition type.

## Method

The author uses the gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) with a bottom-up Einstein-Klein-Gordon model in five dimensions, parameterized by a superpotential with quartic coefficient $B_4$. Linearized time-dependent perturbations in the spin-2, spin-1, and spin-0 sectors are analyzed using spectral methods with Chebyshev polynomials to compute quasinormal mode spectra, from which the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic series is determined via analytical continuation of momenta into the complex plane.

## Key numbers

- For $B_4 = 0$ (crossover), the critical temperature is $T_c \approx 0.08888$ and critical scalar field $\phi_c \approx 3.779$ (in units where $L_{AdS}=1$ and $8\pi G_5=1$).
- For $B_4 = -0.00983$ (second-order), the critical temperature is $T_c \approx 0.11463$ and $\phi_c \approx 5.371$.
- For $B_4 = -0.02$ (first-order), the critical temperature is $T_c \approx 0.1447$ with three critical scalar field values $\phi_c \approx (2.446, 4.202, 12.715)$.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is universally $\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)$ across all transition types.
- At very high temperatures in the spin-2 sector, the radius of convergence is $q^2_c = 1.486\, e^{i \theta_c}$ with $\theta_c = 0.98 \pi$, independent of phase transition type.
- The critical exponent for entropy density near the second-order transition is estimated as $\tilde{\gamma}=2/3$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of hydrodynamic series convergence initiated by Withers (2018) and Grozdanov et al. (2019) to systems with phase transitions, using the Einstein-Klein-Gordon model developed by Gubser (2008). The results enable systematic investigation of how different phase transition types affect the validity of relativistic hydrodynamics near critical points, with implications for the Beam Energy Scan theory program at RHIC and the study of the QCD phase diagram. The finding that hydrodynamic series breakdown is specific to second-order transitions contradicts the common assumption that hydrodynamics universally fails near any phase transition point.
