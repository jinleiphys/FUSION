# Predictions for $p+$Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ TeV: Comparison with Data

> Predictions made in Albacete {\it et al} prior to the LHC $p+$Pb run at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ TeV are compared to currently available data. Some predictions shown here have been updated by including the same experimental cuts as the data. Some additional predictions are also presented, especially for quarkonia, that were provided to the experiments before the data were made public but were too late for the original publication are also shown here.

## Key claim

The paper compares pre-LHC-run predictions for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5 TeV to the data that became available after the run, noting that some predictions were updated by including the same experimental cuts as the data and that additional quarkonia predictions provided to experiments before data release but too late for the original publication are also shown.

## Method

The authors collected predictions from members of the JET Collaboration for charged hadrons, identified particles, photons, jets, J/psi, and gauge bosons in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, using event generators (HIJING, HIJINGBbarB, AMPT), perturbative QCD approaches with collinear factorization, and color glass condensate (CGC) saturation models including rcBK and b-CGC. They then compared these predictions to data from the ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS Collaborations, updating calculations where necessary to match experimental cuts and conventions.

## Key numbers

* Charged particle pseudorapidity density dN_ch/deta in minimum-bias p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: b-CGC calculations with mini-jet mass m_jet approximately 5 MeV give the best description of ALICE data with uncertainty less than 4 percent.
* Average transverse momentum <p_T> of charged particles in the range 0.15 < p_T < 10 GeV as a function of charged particle multiplicity N_ch: shown for p+p at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV, p+Pb at 5.02 TeV, and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, with |eta| < 0.3.
* HIJINGBbarB2.0 calculations used strong color fields with kappa = 2.1 GeV/fm and hard scattering scale p_0 = 3.1 GeV.
* b-CGC calculations for centrality dependence of dN_ch/deta compared to ATLAS data in centrality bins from (1-5)% to (60-90)% with m_jet = 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the predictions compiled in Albacete et al. (2013) prior to the LHC p+Pb run, confronting them with data from ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS that became available after the run. The paper tests multiple theoretical approaches including CGC saturation models (rcBK, b-CGC, IP-Sat), event generators (HIJING, HIJINGBbarB, AMPT), and perturbative QCD calculations against the same data sets, enabling discrimination among models and identification of which approaches best describe different observables and kinematic regions.
