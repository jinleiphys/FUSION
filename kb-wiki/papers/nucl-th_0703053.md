# Flavor content of nucleon form factors

> The flavor content of nucleon form factors is analyzed using two different theoretical approaches. The first is based on a phenomenological two-component model in which the external photon couples to both an intrinsic three-quark structure and a meson cloud via vector-meson dominance. The flavor content of the nucleon form factors is extracted without introducing any additional parameter. A comparison with recent data from parity-violating electron scattering experiments shows a good overall agreement for the strange form factors. A more microscopic approach is that of an unquenched quark model proposed by Geiger and Isgur which is based on valence quark plus glue dominance to which quark-antiquark pairs are added in perturbation. In the original version the importance of $s \bar{s}$ loops in the proton was studied. Here we present the formalism for a new generation of unquenched quark models which, among other extensions, includes the contributions of $u \bar{u}$ and $d \bar{d}$ loops. Finally, we discuss some preliminary results in the closure limit.

## Key claim

The strange quark contribution to nucleon form factors is small but nonvanishing, and the two-component model reproduces the experimental data for both strange electric and magnetic form factors in the range \(0 < Q^2 < 1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) without introducing any additional parameters beyond those already fixed by the electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors analyze the flavor content of nucleon form factors using two approaches. The first is a phenomenological two-component model in which the external photon couples to an intrinsic three-quark structure (described by a dipole form factor) and to a meson cloud via vector-meson dominance (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\)); the strange form factors are extracted from the isoscalar couplings using the \(\omega\)-\(\phi\) mixing angle. The second is an unquenched quark model based on the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur, where quark-antiquark pairs are added perturbatively via a \(^3P_0\) pair-creation operator, and the formalism is extended to include \(u\bar{u}\) and \(d\bar{d}\) loops in addition to \(s\bar{s}\) loops, with the sum over intermediate states performed up to saturation using group-theoretical techniques.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) for nucleon form factors and the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur (1997) for baryons. The two-component model results are compared with parity-violating electron scattering data from the HAPPEX, SAMPLE, PVA4, and G0 collaborations, as well as with lattice QCD calculations by Leinweber et al. (2005). The unquenched quark model generalizes the earlier work of Geiger and Isgur (1997) on \(s\bar{s}\) loops in the proton to include \(u\bar{u}\) and \(d\bar{d}\) loops, enabling future studies of the proton spin crisis, electromagnetic couplings, and sea quark content of baryon resonances.
