# Absorption of 1$P$-wave heavy charmonium $χ_{c1}(1P)$ in nuclei

> We study the inclusive heavy charmonium $χ_{c1}(1P)$ photoproduction from nuclei near the kinematic threshold within the collision model, based on the nuclear spectral function, for incoherent direct photon--nucleon charmonium creation processes. The model accounts for the final $χ_{c1}(1P)$ absorption in nuclear medium, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion. We calculate the absolute and relative excitation functions on $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei at near-threshold photon beam energies of 8.25--16.0 GeV, the absolute momentum differential cross sections and ratios of them for its production off these target nuclei at laboratory polar angles of 0$^{\circ}$--10$^{\circ}$ and for photon energy of 13 GeV as well as the A-dependences of the transparency ratios for the $χ_{c1}(1P)$ at photon energy of 13 GeV within the different scenarios for its absorption cross section in nuclei. We demonstrate that the absolute and relative observables considered reveal distinct sensitivity to these scenarios. Therefore, they might be useful for the determination of this cross section from the comparison of them with the experimental data from the future experiments at the upgraded up to 22 GeV CEBAF facility, which is of crucial importance in understanding of charmonium production and suppression in high-energy heavy--ion collisions in a search for the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that absolute and relative observables (excitation functions, momentum differential cross sections, and transparency ratios) for near-threshold photoproduction of χc1(1P) mesons from nuclei are distinctly sensitive to the assumed χc1–nucleon absorption cross section (3.5, 7, 14, or 20 mb), and that future measurements at the upgraded CEBAF facility (up to 22 GeV) could discriminate between these scenarios, thereby constraining the poorly known χc1–nucleon interaction.

## Method

The author employs a nuclear spectral function approach within a first-collision model for incoherent direct photon–nucleon charmonium creation, accounting for target nucleon binding, Fermi motion, and final-state absorption of the χc1(1P) meson in nuclear matter. The free total cross section for the elementary γp → χc1p reaction is taken from a vector-meson-exchange parametrization (Eq. 7). The in-medium cross section is obtained by replacing the free center-of-mass energy with an expression that includes the struck nucleon's binding energy and momentum, and the effective number of participating nucleons is computed using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution. Calculations are performed for 12C and 184W targets at photon energies of 8.25–16.0 GeV, and for a set of nine nuclei (12C, 27Al, 40Ca, 63Cu, 93Nb, 112Sn, 184W, 208Pb, 238U) for the A-dependence of transparency ratios at 13 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The free-space χc1(1P) meson mass is mχc1 = (3510.67 ± 0.05) MeV, with full width Γχc1 = (0.84 ± 0.04) MeV.
* The threshold photon energy for χc1(1P) photoproduction on a free proton at rest is 10.08 GeV; the corresponding center-of-mass threshold energy is Wth = 4.44895 GeV.
* The free total cross section for γp → χc1p at 13–15 GeV is of order 0.1–0.2 nb, about one order of magnitude smaller than the J/ψ production cross section at these energies.
* The J/ψ feed-down contributions from χc1,c2(1P) and ψ(2S) decays in γp collisions do not exceed 6% and 3.5%, respectively, at the considered photon energies.
* The coherence length for χc1 production at Eγ = 13 GeV is lc ≈ 0.4 fm; the formation length for a χc1 with momentum ≈10 GeV/c is lf ≈ 1 fm.
* The four adopted values for the in-medium χc1–nucleon absorption cross section are: 3.5, 7, 14, and 20 mb.
* The r.m.s. c-cbar separations from quark potential models are approximately 0.4 fm for J/ψ, 0.6 fm for χc states, and 0.8 fm for ψ(2S).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to X(3872) photoproduction (Paryev, 2023) to the case of the χc1(1P) charmonium state, which has not been studied experimentally in low-energy photon-induced reactions on nuclei. The calculations are motivated by the planned GlueX measurements at the upgraded CEBAF facility (up to 22 GeV) and by the need to constrain the poorly known χc1–nucleon absorption cross section, which is relevant for understanding charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions and the search for the quark-gluon plasma. The paper builds on prior theoretical predictions for χc1–nucleon cross sections from Refs. [71] and [72] (about 13 mb and 6.5 mb at 10 GeV c.m. energy, respectively) and on the J/ψ–nucleon cross section of (3.5 ± 0.8) mb extracted from the SLAC photoproduction experiment (1966).
