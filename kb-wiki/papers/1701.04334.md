# Gluonic Lorentz violation and chiral perturbation theory

> By applying chiral-perturbation-theory methods to the QCD sector of the Lorentz-violating Standard-Model Extension, we investigate Lorentz violation in the strong interactions. In particular, we consider the CPT-even pure-gluon operator of the minimal Standard-Model Extension. We construct the lowest-order chiral effective Lagrangian for three as well as two light quark flavors. We develop the power-counting rules and construct the heavy-baryon chiral-perturbation-theory Lagrangian, which we use to calculate Lorentz-violating contributions to the nucleon self energy. Using the constructed effective operators, we derive the first stringent limits on many of the components of the relevant Lorentz-violating parameter. We also obtain the Lorentz-violating nucleon-nucleon potential. We suggest that this potential may be used to obtain new limits from atomic-clock or deuteron storage-ring experiments.

# Gluonic Lorentz violation and chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The paper constructs a chiral perturbation theory framework for the CPT-even pure-gluon Lorentz-violating operator in the minimal Standard-Model Extension, but the derived limits on the 19 independent components of the Lorentz-violating parameter \(k_G^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\) are order-of-magnitude estimates only, with the authors explicitly allowing for two orders of magnitude uncertainty from unknown low-energy constants and possible cancellations between different Lorentz-violating coefficients.

## Method

The author applies chiral perturbation theory methods to the QCD sector of the Standard-Model Extension, constructing the lowest-order chiral effective Lagrangian for three and two light quark flavors that incorporates the CPT-even pure-gluon operator. Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory is developed with modified momentum separation to maintain consistent power counting, and the Lorentz-violating contributions to the nucleon self energy are calculated. The effective operators are then used to derive limits on the Lorentz-violating parameter components from existing bounds on nucleon and photon observables.

## Key numbers

- The only previously reported bound on \(k_G^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\) is \(|\tilde{k}_{tr}| = \frac{2}{3}|k^{00}| < 4 \times 10^{-15}\), obtained through quantum mixing with the electron Lorentz-violating coefficient
- For the semi-traceless part \(k^{\mu\nu}\): \(k^{TT} < 10^{-21}\), \(k^{TJ} < 10^{-19}\), \(k^{JK} < 10^{-27}\), \(k^{XX}, k^{YY} < 10^{-27}\), \(k^{ZZ} < 10^{-20}\) (in the Sun-centered inertial reference frame)
- For the fully traceless part \(W^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\): components \(\frac{1}{2}W^{TYXZ}, \frac{1}{2}W^{TXYZ}, W^{TXXY}, W^{TXXZ}, W^{TYXY} < 10^{-34}\); components \(W^{TYTY}, W^{TZTZ}, W^{TXTY}, W^{TXTZ}, W^{TYTZ} < 10^{-35}\)
- The pion decay constant used is \(F_\pi = 92.4\ {\rm MeV}\); the chiral-breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 1\ {\rm GeV}\)
- Low-energy constants \(D = 0.80\) and \(F = 0.50\) (at tree level) are used for the baryon Lagrangian

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to Lorentz violation previously applied to quark-sector operators by the same research community (LVchiPT1, LVchiPT2) into the pure-gluon sector of the minimal Standard-Model Extension. The method is similar in spirit to studies of parity breaking by Kaplan (1993) and time-reversal symmetry breaking by Mereghetti (2010). The derived nucleon-nucleon potential from gluonic Lorentz violation is suggested for use in obtaining new limits from atomic-clock experiments or deuteron storage-ring experiments, building on the experimental framework of the Standard-Model Extension (Kostelecky and collaborators, 1995 onward). The 10 components of the fully traceless part \(W^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\) that do not induce kinetic nucleon terms are constrained through their mixing with photon operators, using bounds from gamma-ray burst observations of vacuum birefringence.
