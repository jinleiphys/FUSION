# Preponderant Orbital Polarization in Relativistic Magnetovortical Matter

> We establish thermodynamic stability and gauge invariance in the magnetovortical matter of Dirac fermions under the coexistent rotation and strong magnetic field. The corresponding partition function reveals that the orbital contribution to bulk thermodynamics preponderates over the conventional contribution from anomaly-related spin effects. This orbital preponderance macroscopically manifests itself in the sign inversion of the induced charge and current in the magnetovortical matter, and can be tested experimentally as the flip of the angular momentum polarization of magnetovortical matter when the magnetic field strength is increased.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in magnetovortical matter of Dirac fermions under coexistent rotation and strong magnetic field, the orbital contribution to bulk thermodynamics preponderates over the conventional spin contribution from anomaly-related effects, leading to a sign inversion of the induced charge and current density. This result depends on using the gauge-invariant kinetic angular momentum rather than the canonical angular momentum in the partition function.

## Method

The authors construct a gauge-invariant partition function using the kinetic angular momentum operator \( \mathcal{J}_\mathrm{kin} \) instead of the canonical angular momentum, which satisfies thermodynamic stability through a balance condition between the radial drift force from the magnetic field and an induced electric field. They evaluate this partition function in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation using the Ritus basis for spin-degenerate Landau levels, obtaining an effective chemical potential \( \nu = \Omega \langle L_\mathrm{kin} + S \rangle_\mathrm{LLL} = -s\Omega/2 \) that includes both orbital and spin contributions. The pressure and charge density are then computed from the resulting thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- The charge density in the massless limit is \( \rho = -eB\Omega/(4\pi^2) \), with spin contribution \( \rho_\mathrm{spin} = eB\Omega/(4\pi^2) \) and orbital contribution \( \rho_\mathrm{orb} = -eB\Omega/(2\pi^2) \)
- The pressure in the massless limit is \( P = \frac{|eB|}{4\pi^2} \left[ (\mu - s\Omega/2)^2 + \pi^2 T^2/3 \right] \)
- The induced angular momentum from the anti-Einstein-de Haas effect is estimated as \( \Omega_\mathrm{ind} = 7.76 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{rad/s} \) for parameters \( \mu = 10^{-1} \, \text{eV} \), \( B = 10^2 \, \text{T} \), \( \rho_\mathrm{mass} = 1 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \), and \( R = 10^{-2} \, \text{cm} \)
- The zero-temperature vacuum pressure is \( P = \frac{|eB|\Omega^2}{16\pi^2} \left[ \sqrt{1-\alpha} - \alpha \ln \frac{1+\sqrt{1-\alpha}}{\sqrt{\alpha}} \right] \theta(1-\alpha) \) with \( \alpha = (2M/\Omega)^2 \)

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetovortical matter beyond the conventional spin-dominant picture used in the chiral magnetic and vortical effects (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008, Son 2009, Landsteiner 2011). It corrects previous thermodynamic formulations (Chen 2015) that used the canonical angular momentum and suffered from unphysical divergences (Ebihara 2016, Fukushima 2020), and resolves a sign discrepancy with earlier linear response theory (Hattori 2016) and chiral kinetic theory (Yang 2020, Mameda 2023, Yang 2024) that only captured the spin contribution. The results enable experimental tests in Weyl or Dirac semimetals via the predicted anti-Einstein-de Haas effect, and provide a benchmark for lattice QCD simulations of magnetovortical matter.
