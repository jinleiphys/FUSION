# Correlation between initial spatial anisotropy and final momentum anisotropies in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The particle momentum anisotropy ($v_n$) produced in relativistic nuclear collisions is considered to be a response of the initial geometry or the spatial anisotropy $ε_n$ of the system formed in these collisions. The linear correlation between $ε_n$ and $v_n$ quantifies the efficiency at which the initial spatial eccentricity is converted to final momentum anisotropy in heavy ion collisions. We study the transverse momentum, collision centrality, and beam energy dependence of this correlation for different charged particles using a hydrodynamical model framework. The ($ε_n -v_n$) correlation is found to be stronger for central collisions and also for n=2 compared to that for n=3 as expected. However, the transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependent correlation coefficient shows interesting features which strongly depends on the mass as well as $p_T$ of the emitted particle. The correlation strength is found to be larger for lighter particles in the lower $p_T$ region. We see that the relative fluctuation in anisotropic flow depends strongly in the value of $η/s$ specially in the region $p_T <1$ GeV unlike the correlation coefficient which does not show significant dependence on $η/s$.

# Correlation between initial spatial anisotropy and final momentum anisotropies in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The linear correlation between initial spatial anisotropy ($\epsilon_n$) and final momentum anisotropy ($v_n$) is stronger for central collisions and for n=2 compared to n=3, but the transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependent correlation coefficient shows strong dependence on particle mass and $p_T$ region, with the correlation strength being larger for lighter particles in the lower $p_T$ region. The correlation coefficient does not show significant dependence on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($\eta/s$), unlike the relative fluctuation in anisotropic flow which depends strongly on $\eta/s$ especially for $p_T < 1$ GeV.

## Method
The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional longitudinally boost invariant hydrodynamical model (MUSIC) with Monte Carlo Glauber fluctuating initial conditions to calculate initial spatial anisotropies ($\epsilon_2$, $\epsilon_3$) and corresponding anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_2$, $v_3$) for positively charged pions, kaons, and protons. They computed the correlation coefficient C($\epsilon_n$, $v_n$) as the covariance of the two variables divided by the product of their standard deviations, studying its dependence on collision centrality, transverse momentum, beam energy, system size, and $\eta/s$ for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV (LHC) and Cu+Cu collisions at 200A GeV (RHIC).

## Key numbers
- Average $\epsilon_2$ values for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV: 0.139 (0-20% centrality), 0.280 (20-40%), 0.405 (40-60%), and 0.527 (60-80%)
- Average $\epsilon_3$ values for Pb+Pb collisions: 0.099 (0-20%), 0.167 (20-40%), 0.245 (40-60%), and 0.395 (60-80%)
- $\langle \epsilon_3 \rangle$ is about 40% smaller than $\langle \epsilon_2 \rangle$ for all four centrality bins
- C($\epsilon_2$, $v_2$) for pions in Pb+Pb: 0.95 (0-20%), 0.94 (20-40%), 0.91 (40-60%), 0.60 (60-80%)
- C($\epsilon_3$, $v_3$) for pions in Pb+Pb: 0.74 (0-20%), 0.71 (20-40%), 0.51 (40-60%), 0.07 (60-80%)
- C($\epsilon_2$, $v_2$) for pions in Cu+Cu at 200A GeV: 0.91 (0-20%), 0.84 (20-40%), 0.68 (40-60%)
- C($\epsilon_3$, $v_3$) for pions in Cu+Cu: 0.72 (0-20%), 0.51 (20-40%), 0.11 (40-60%)
- Probable errors in correlation coefficients: less than 1% for ($\epsilon_2$, $v_2$) and less than 2% for ($\epsilon_3$, $v_3$)
- Initial formation time of plasma: 0.4 fm/$c$ for both collision systems
- Fixed $\eta/s$ value of 0.08 used for primary calculations

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Niemi et al. (2013) on the correlation between initial spatial anisotropy and final momentum anisotropy, and by Alvioli et al. on initial state anisotropies from Monte Carlo Glauber models. The study provides a systematic investigation of the $p_T$-dependent correlation coefficient across different particle species, which had not been explored in detail previously. The results enable better understanding of how initial geometry information is encoded in final state flow observables, and suggest that the experimentally measurable quantity $\langle v_n \rangle / \sigma_{v_n}$ may serve as a proxy for the correlation coefficient to infer initial state properties. The finding that the correlation coefficient is insensitive to $\eta/s$ while relative fluctuations are sensitive provides a way to disentangle initial state and transport property effects in future experimental analyses.
