# Normal forms and uniform approximations for bridge orbit bifurcations

> We discuss various bifurcation problems in which two isolated periodic orbits exchange periodic ``bridge'' orbit(s) between two successive bifurcations. We propose normal forms which locally describe the corresponding fixed point scenarios on the Poincaré surface of section. Uniform approximations for the density of states for an integrable Hamiltonian system with two degrees of freedom are derived and successfully reproduce the numerical quantum-mechanical results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes normal forms that locally describe bridge orbit bifurcations, scenarios where two isolated periodic orbits exchange a periodic "bridge orbit" that exists only in a finite interval of a system parameter, and derives uniform approximations for the density of states that successfully reproduce numerical quantum-mechanical results, but the authors note that for asymmetric bridges with unequal repetition numbers no suitable normal form has yet been found.

## Method

The authors construct normal forms using canonical variables on a Poincaré surface of section, with a bifurcation parameter, to describe the fixed-point scenarios of bridge orbit bifurcations in both integrable and non-integrable Hamiltonian systems with two degrees of freedom. For the integrable case, they map action-angle variables to Poincaré variables using a transformation based on the isotropic harmonic oscillator; for non-integrable cases, they introduce an angle-dependent normal form. They then derive local and global uniform approximations for the semiclassical density of states from these normal forms and compare the results with fully quantum-mechanical calculations for the corresponding quantum Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- For the integrable model (Hamiltonian H_κ with α=2.4 and κ=0), the bridge orbit B exists only for parameter values between the bifurcation points κ₋ and κ₊, where the stability trace TrM of the circular orbits C± equals +2.
- For the non-integrable model with shape function f_{β,β₄} (α=2.2, β₄=0.002), fitting the normal form parameters yields aρ = -0.12 and bρ = -0.145, giving bifurcation points at ε = ±0.24 for the B1 bridge and ε = ±0.05 for the B2 bridge.
- For the coupled quartic oscillator (Hamiltonian H_α), the bridge orbit Q has a maximum value of |TrM - 2| smaller than 10⁻⁷, and the two isolated orbits F and P are created at α=0.6315 in a period-tripling bifurcation.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of uniform approximations for generic codimension-one bifurcations (Sieber 1996, Schomerus 1997, Sieber 1998) and codimension-two bifurcations (Schomerus 1997, Kaidel 2004) to the previously unstudied case of bridge orbit bifurcations, which involve global phase-space changes that cannot be treated with standard perturbative normal forms from Birkhoff-Gustavson expansions. The bridge orbit scenario has been observed in both integrable and non-integrable systems (Arita 2004, Arita 2006, Dahl 2004) and the results enable semiclassical trace formulae for systems where periodic orbits exchange stability via bridge orbits, with potential applications to understanding quantum shell effects in finite fermion systems.
