# Schematic model of generations

> We propose a model of generations that has exactly three generations. This model has several attractive features: There is a simple mechanism to produce the CKM quark mixings and their neutrino analogs. There are definite predictions for particles of much higher mass than the quarks and leptons of the standard model, including the prediction of two-body decays without missing mass for the higher mass particles. There is a natural dark matter particle. A discussion of masses of the quarks suggests that the large mass differences between the generations could have a qualitative explanation, and there could be a simple way to make the $u$-$d$ mass difference negative, but the $c$-$s$ and $t$-$b$ mass differences positive. The model also suggests a completely new scenario for the production, mass and decays of the Higgs meson that will be analysed in a separate paper.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a composite model of quark and lepton generations that yields exactly three generations, but the author explicitly states that the discussion of quark and lepton masses is "highly speculative" and that the validity of the model should not be based on those mass comments.

## Method

The model introduces two constituents: a spin-1/2 fermion called the "hexon" (carrying all standard model quantum numbers of one generation) and a spin-0 boson called the "generon" (carrying no standard model quantum numbers), confined by a product gauge group [SU(3) × SU(3)]_b with gauge bosons called "bindons." The three generations are identified as the only single-centered [SU(3)×SU(3)]_b singlet bound states containing one hexon: h\bar{g}\bar{g} (generation I), h(gg)\bar{g} (generation II), and h(gg)(gg) (generation III). A mixing term L_mix = λ ε^{α1β1γ1} ε^{α2β2γ2} g_{α1,α2} g_{β1,β2} g_{γ1,γ2} produces first-order transitions between neighboring generations to generate the CKM matrix.

## Key numbers

- The compositeness scale is required to be greater than 4 to 9 TeV based on experimental evidence
- The ratio a/L extracted from quark mass fits is approximately 1.6 × 10^{-2}, corresponding to L/a ∼ 63
- The generon mass m_g from the quark fit is 1.72 × 10^2 MeV/c^2
- For leptons, two possible fits give a/L = 2.48 × 10^{-3} and a/L = 1.16 × 10^{-1}
- The confining group [SU(3)×SU(3)]_b leads to asymptotic freedom for n_f ≤ 33

## Context

This work extends the composite model approach to quarks and leptons, following earlier suggestions by Ramond (1970s), Greenberg (1970s), and others, but differs by focusing specifically on explaining the existence of three generations rather than internal structure within a generation. The model contrasts with approaches introducing new symmetries relating generations (e.g., Appelquist) or radical extensions like string theory or extra dimensions (e.g., Dvali). The paper enables future calculations of the CKM matrix, predictions for excited quark and lepton states in the TeV range with distinctive two-body decays, and a new scenario for Higgs phenomenology to be analyzed in a subsequent paper.
