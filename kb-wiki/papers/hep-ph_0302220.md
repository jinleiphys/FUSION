# $ππ$ scattering S wave from the data on the reaction $π^-p\toπ^0π^0n$

> The results of the recent experiments on the reaction $π^-p\toπ^0π^0n$ performed at KEK, BNL, IHEP, and CERN are analyzed in detail. For the I=0 $ππ$ S wave phase shift $δ^0_0$ and inelasticity $η^0_0$ a new set of data is obtained. Difficulties emerging when using the physical solutions for the $π^0π^0$ S and D wave amplitudes extracted with the partial wave analyses are discussed. Attention is drawn to the fact that, for the $π^0π^0$ invariant mass, m, above 1 GeV, the other solutions, in principle, are found to be more preferred. For clarifying the situation and further studying the $f_0(980)$ resonance thorough experimental investigations of the reaction $π^-p\toπ^0π^0n$ in the m region near the $K\bar K$ threshold are required.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the physical solutions selected in four recent experiments on the reaction π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n lead to values of the I=0 ππ S-wave phase shift δ⁰₀ and inelasticity η⁰₀ that are incompatible with known results from π⁺π⁻ data for invariant masses above 1 GeV, and that alternative solutions are in principle more preferred in that region.

## Method

The authors analyzed data from KEK (8.9 GeV), BNL (18.3 GeV), IHEP (38 GeV), and CERN (100 GeV) experiments on π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n. For the KEK data, they extracted δ⁰₀ and η⁰₀ simultaneously using the relative phase δ and S-wave intensity |A_S|² in the mass region 0.68–1.64 GeV. For the BNL data, they performed Chew-Low extrapolation of the S and D₀ wave intensities and relative phase φ_{S-D₀} from five t-intervals in the range 0 < -t < 0.2 GeV² to the pion pole, then extracted δ⁰₀ and η⁰₀ using the same formalism. The D-wave phase φ_D was modeled with a Breit-Wigner amplitude for the f₂(1270) resonance.

## Key numbers

- f₂(1270) resonance parameters from KEK data: m_{f₂} = 1.283 ± 0.008 GeV, Γ_{f₂} = 0.170 ± 0.014 GeV, B_{f₂ππ} = 0.760 ± 0.034
- f₂(1270) resonance parameters from BNL data (physical solution): m_{f₂} = 1.279 ± 0.002 GeV, Γ_{f₂} = 0.205 ± 0.005 GeV, B_{f₂ππ} = 0.697 ± 0.008
- f₂(1270) resonance parameters from BNL data (other solution): m_{f₂} = 1.281 ± 0.002 GeV, Γ_{f₂} = 0.211 ± 0.005 GeV, B_{f₂ππ} = 0.712 ± 0.007
- GAMS measured σ_{D₀}(π⁻p → f₂(1270)n → π⁰π⁰n) = 2.3 ± 0.2 μb at 38 GeV, which is 1.57 times smaller than the OPE model estimate of approximately 3.6 μb
- The BNL data indicate B_{f₂ππ} amounts to approximately 84% of the PDG value; the KEK data give approximately 90% of the PDG value

## Context

This work extends the analysis of ππ scattering from the reaction πN → ππN, which has been the major source of information on ππ scattering (reviews available by early 1999 in Refs. [1,2]). The authors compare their results with the "canonical" data extracted from 17.2 GeV experiments on π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n (Refs. [7-12]). The paper highlights difficulties with the physical solutions selected in the four experiments and suggests that the data indicate a smaller f₂(1270) → ππ branching ratio than the PDG value. The authors call for new precise measurements of π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n in the mass region 0.9–1.1 GeV near the K̄K threshold to better determine the f₀(980) coupling constant to K̄K and resolve the long-standing ambiguity in δ⁰₀ above the K̄K threshold raised by Ref. [16].
