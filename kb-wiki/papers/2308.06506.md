# New extended method for $ψ^{\prime}$ scaling function of inclusive electron scattering

> Scaling analyses have been successfully applied to study the inclusive electron scattering $(\textit{e}, \textit{e}^{\prime})$ over the past few decades. In this paper, we utilize the $ψ^{\prime}$ scaling function in momentum space to analyze the $(\textit{e}, \textit{e}^{\prime})$ cross sections, where the nucleon momentum distributions are derived from self-consistent mean-field calculations. By further introducing the energy and momentum conservation in the scaling analysis, an improved $ψ^{\prime}$ scaling function is proposed to investigate the high-momentum part of the momentum distributions. Using the proposed scaling function, we systematically explore the effects of the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation ($\textit{NN}$-SRC) on the $(\textit{e}, \textit{e}^{\prime})$ cross sections. From the experimental $(\textit{e}, \textit{e}^{\prime})$ data, the \textit{NN}-SRC strength is further extracted within the framework of the improved $ψ^{\prime}$ scaling function. The studies in this paper offer a new method to investigate the nucleon momentum distributions and the $\textit{NN}$-SRC effects in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an improved \(\psi^{\prime}\) scaling function in momentum space that explicitly incorporates energy and momentum conservation, making it sensitive to high-momentum nucleons and thereby enabling the extraction of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation (NN-SRC) strength from inclusive electron scattering data, whereas the standard scaling function without these conservation constraints is less sensitive to such high-momentum components.

## Method

The authors first compute nucleon momentum distributions (NMDs) using an axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model for the mean-field part and a light-front dynamics (LFD) method for the correlation part. They then construct the standard \(\psi^{\prime}\) scaling function in momentum space via the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) and introduce an improved version by replacing the lower integration limit \(k_F|\psi^{\prime}|\) with kinematic limits \(k_{\min}\) and \(k_{\max}\) derived from energy and momentum conservation in the \((e,e^{\prime})\) process. The inclusive cross sections are computed as a sum of quasielastic (QE) and \(\Delta\)-region contributions using the improved scaling function.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) is \(\beta_2 = -0.33\) and of \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\) is \(\beta_2 = 0.17\), obtained from the deformed HFB model with SLY4 parameters.
- The mean-field NMDs provide a good description for \(k < 1.5~\mathrm{fm^{-1}}\).
- The correlation part of NMDs is constructed using LFD components \(n_2(k)\) and \(n_5(k)\), where \(n_2(k)\) dominates in the region \(1.5 < k < 3~\mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) and \(n_5(k)\) dominates for \(k > 2.5~\mathrm{fm^{-1}}\).
- The inclusive cross sections for \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) are calculated at incident energy \(\varepsilon = 1108\) MeV and scattering angle \(\theta = 37.5^{\circ}\); for \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\) at \(\varepsilon = 961\) MeV and \(\theta = 37.5^{\circ}\).
- The parameter 0.75 in the QE scaling function is replaced by 0.72, and in the \(\Delta\)-region scaling function by 0.54, to match experimental data.
- Pauli blocking is neglected because the momentum transfer satisfies \(q > 500\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the \(\psi^{\prime}\) scaling approach, originally developed for the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model by Alberico (1988) and later refined with the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) by Antonov (2004, 2005, 2006), by moving from coordinate-space density distributions to momentum-space distributions. The improved scaling function enables a more direct investigation of NN-SRC effects, which are known from exclusive experiments (Subedi, 2008) to be dominated by neutron-proton pairs. The method can be applied to future experiments at facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC) and provides a new way to extract SRC strengths from inclusive data.
