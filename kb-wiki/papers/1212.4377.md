# Giant Quadrupole Resonances in 208Pb, the nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness

> Recent improvements in the experimental determination of properties of the Isovector Giant Quadrupole Resonance (IVGQR), as demonstrated in the A=208 mass region, may be instrumental for characterizing the isovector channel of the effective nuclear interaction. We analyze properties of the IVGQR in 208Pb, using both macroscopic and microscopic approaches. The microscopic method is based on families of non-relativistic and covariant Energy Density Functionals (EDF), characterized by a systematic variation of isoscalar and isovector properties of the corresponding nuclear matter equations of state. The macroscopic approach yields an explicit dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at some subsaturation density, for instance S(ρ=0.1 fm^{-3}), or the neutron skin thickness Δr_{np} of a heavy nucleus, on the excitation energies of isoscalar and isovector GQRs. Using available data it is found that S(ρ=0.1 fm{}^{-3})=23.3 +/- 0.6 MeV. Results obtained with the microscopic framework confirm the correlation of the Δr_{np} to the isoscalar and isovector GQR energies, as predicted by the macroscopic model. By exploiting this correlation together with the experimental values for the isoscalar and isovector GQR energies, we estimate Δr_{np} = 0.14 +/- 0.03 fm for 208Pb, and the slope parameter of the symmetry energy: L = 37 +/- 18 MeV.

## Key claim

The isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR) excitation energy, combined with the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) energy, can be used to extract the nuclear symmetry energy at subsaturation density and the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, but the extracted values depend on the specific macroscopic model assumptions and the choice of the symmetry energy at saturation J.

## Method

The authors employed both a macroscopic liquid-drop-model-based approach and microscopic self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using non-relativistic Skyrme (SAMi, KDE, SkI3) and covariant relativistic (NL3, DD-ME2) energy density functionals. Families of functionals with systematically varied isoscalar (effective mass) and isovector (symmetry energy) properties were constructed to study correlations. Particle-vibration coupling (PVC) calculations were also performed for the IVGQR width using the SAMi functional.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at subsaturation density: S(ρ=0.1 fm^{-3}) = 23.3 ± 0.6 MeV, extracted from experimental GQR energies via the macroscopic model
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Δr_np = 0.14 ± 0.03 fm, estimated from the correlation between GQR energies and the skin thickness
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: L = 37 ± 18 MeV
- Weighted average experimental ISGQR energy in 208Pb: 10.9 ± 0.1 MeV
- Weighted average experimental IVGQR energy in 208Pb (from data on 209Bi): 22.7 ± 0.2 MeV
- IVGQR spreading width from PVC calculation with SAMi: 3.8 MeV, with centroid shifted from 22 MeV (RPA) to 21 MeV (PVC)
- Alternative macroscopic estimate of Δr_np using a different J value (32 ± 1 MeV): 0.22 ± 0.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of giant resonances as probes of nuclear matter properties, building on the established correlation between the ISGQR energy and the nucleon effective mass (Blairot 1980) and on the droplet model relation between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy (Brown 2000, Centelles 2009). The recent experimental improvement in IVGQR determination (Hensley 2011) enabled this combined analysis. The results provide a new constraint on the symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness that can be compared with other approaches such as those of Tsang (2012) and Lattimer (2012), and the extracted values are consistent with the range Δr_np = 0.18 ± 0.03 fm from Tsang (2012). The work enables future measurements of IVGQR in other mass regions to further constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
