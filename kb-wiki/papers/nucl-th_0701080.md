# Transition from Baryon- to Meson-Dominated Freeze Out -- Early Decoupling around 30 A GeV?

> The recently discovered sharp peak in the excitation function of the K+/pi+ ratio around 30 A GeV in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is discussed in the framework of the Statistical Model. In this model, the freeze-out of an ideal hadron gas changes from a situation where baryons dominate to one with mainly mesons. This transition occurs at a temperature T = 140 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu(B) = 410 MeV corresponding to an energy of sqrt(s) = 8.2 GeV. The calculated maximum in the K+/pi+ ratio is, however, much less pronounced than the one observed by the NA49 Collaboration. The smooth increase of the K-/pi- ratio with incident energy and the shape of the excitation functions of the Lambda/pi+, Xi-/pi+ and Omega/pi ratios all exhibiting maxima at different incident energies, is consistent with the presently available experimental data. The measured K+/pi+ ratio exceeds the calculated one just at the incident energy when the freeze-out condition is changing. We speculate that at this point freeze-out might occur in a modified way. We discuss a scenario of an early freeze-out which indeed increases K+/pi+ ratio while most other particle ratios remain essentially unchanged. Such an early freeze-out is supported by results from HBT studies.

# Transition from Baryon- to Meson-Dominated Freeze Out -- Early Decoupling around 30 A GeV?

## Key claim
The Statistical Model of an ideal hadron gas predicts a maximum in the K+/pi+ ratio around 30 A GeV due to a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated freeze-out, but the calculated maximum is much less pronounced than the sharp peak observed by the NA49 Collaboration; the authors speculate that an early freeze-out at higher temperature could increase the K+/pi+ ratio while leaving most other particle ratios essentially unchanged.

## Method
The authors applied the Statistical Model of an ideal hadron gas in chemical equilibrium, characterized by temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B along the freeze-out curve defined by fixed average energy per particle of about 1 GeV. They calculated particle ratios using a canonical ensemble for strangeness with a canonical correlation volume for Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions, and analyzed the Wroblewski factor lambda_s (relative strangeness content) and individual strange-to-non-strange particle ratios as functions of sqrt(s_NN). They also examined entropy density contributions from baryons versus mesons along the freeze-out curve using the Thermus code.

## Key numbers
- The transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated freeze-out occurs at temperature T = 140 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 410 MeV, corresponding to sqrt(s_NN) = 8.2 GeV (30 A GeV laboratory energy).
- The Wroblewski factor lambda_s reaches a maximum at mu_B approximately 500 MeV and T approximately 130 MeV, corresponding to 30 A GeV.
- The Wroblewski factor decreases towards higher energies and reaches a limiting value of about 0.43.
- The K+/pi+ ratio can never exceed a value of 0.25 in an equilibrium condition.
- The entropy density divided by T^3 is constant for all incident energies except the low-energy SIS region.

## Context
This work extends the Statistical Model approach of Cleymans et al. (2006) and the freeze-out curve condition of fixed average energy per particle of about 1 GeV. It addresses the sharp peak in the K+/pi+ ratio observed by the NA49 Collaboration (Gazdzicki, 2004; NA49 Collaboration, 2002) and the strong variation in Lambda/pi ratios predicted by Gorenstein et al. (2004). The results contradict the interpretation that the peak signals the QCD critical point as suggested by Stock (2005), instead attributing the behavior to hadron gas properties. The paper enables future experimental verification of predicted maxima in multi-strange baryon ratios at different energies, which would weaken the case for a phase transition. The early freeze-out scenario is supported by HBT volume studies from the CERES Collaboration (2006) showing a minimum in extracted volumes between top AGS and lowest SPS energies.
