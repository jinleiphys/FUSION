# Coulomb force effects in low-energy $α$-deuteron scattering

> The $α$-proton Coulomb interaction is included in the description of $α$-deuteron scattering using the screening and renormalization approach in the framework of momentum-space three-particle equations. The technical reliability of the method is demonstrated. Large Coulomb-force effects are found.

## Key claim

The screening and renormalization approach, previously demonstrated for proton-deuteron scattering, can be extended reliably to alpha-deuteron scattering, where large Coulomb-force effects are found; the method's technical reliability is demonstrated through convergence with screening radius, but the results depend strongly on the choice of the alpha-nucleon potential model.

## Method

The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-particle scattering equations were solved in momentum space using a screened Coulomb potential with a sharpness parameter n=4 and screening radii up to 20 fm, with renormalization applied to recover the unscreened limit. Three different alpha-nucleon potential parametrizations (αN-I, αN-II, αN-III) were used, with the charge-dependent Bonn potential for the neutron-proton interaction, including partial waves up to total angular momentum I ≤ 2 plus ³D₃ for nuclear forces and up to L ≤ 11 for the screened Coulomb potential.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius R = 10 fm is practically sufficient for elastic αd scattering at 4.81 MeV deuteron lab energy
- αd breakup observables at 15 MeV α lab energy require at least R = 15 fm for convergence
- For the αN-I potential, the Pauli-forbidden state was moved to Γ ≥ 1000 MeV, with results practically independent of Γ above that value
- The αp Coulomb potential at short distances uses a uniformly charged sphere with radius R_I = 1.5 fm
- Results are shown at deuteron lab energies of 4.81 MeV and 17 MeV for elastic scattering, and at 15 MeV α lab energy and 12 MeV deuteron lab energy for breakup

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach previously developed for proton-deuteron scattering (Deltuva, 2005a, 2005d, 2006a) to alpha-deuteron scattering, which had previously been treated only with approximate methods such as the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method (Austern, 1987). The results enable more stringent tests of alpha-nucleon force models and provide a foundation for applying exact three-body equations to reactions involving halo nuclei and to structure calculations of ⁶Li and ⁶He.
