# Superscaling in lepton-nucleus scattering

> We suggest that superscaling analyses of few-GeV inclusive electron scattering from nuclei, both in the quasielastic peak and in the region where the $Δ$-excitation dominates, allow one to make reliable predictions for charge-changing neutrino reactions at energies of a few GeV, relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments.

# Superscaling in lepton-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The authors suggest that superscaling analyses of few-GeV inclusive electron scattering data, covering both the quasielastic peak and the Delta-excitation region, allow one to make reliable predictions for charge-changing neutrino reactions at energies of a few GeV. The hedge is that the method yields predictions at the 15-20% level, with the uncertainty related to violations of superscaling arising from two-body contributions (meson-exchange currents and associated correlations) not included in the phenomenological representation.

## Method

The authors extract phenomenological superscaling functions from world inclusive electron scattering data by constructing reduced cross sections (experimental cross section divided by appropriate single-nucleon factors) as functions of scaling variables psi (for quasielastic) and psi_Delta (for Delta-excitation). They then apply these universal, q- and A-independent scaling functions to predict charged-current and neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections by multiplying the phenomenological scaling function by the appropriate elementary neutrino-nucleon factors, assuming the same scaling function applies to the vector and axial response channels.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological superscaling function includes an energy shift of about 20 MeV (the "prime" in psi-prime) to reproduce the correct experimental position of the quasielastic peak.
- The net effect of meson exchange currents and associated correlations in the quasielastic region is assessed to be roughly 10-15%.
- For neutrino energy of 1 GeV and muon scattering angle of 45 degrees, the antineutrino cross section is about 5 times smaller than the neutrino cross section.
- The superscaling analysis yields predictions for neutrino scattering at the 15-20% level uncertainty.
- For neutral current reactions, the axial strange form factor g_A^s = G_A^(s)(0) is taken to be -0.2 for the strangeness sensitivity study.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis (SuSA) approach previously developed by the same authors (PRC, NC) for quasielastic electron scattering to also cover the Delta-resonance region, and applies it to neutrino and antineutrino reactions relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments. The approach contrasts with the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, which reproduces gross features of inclusive electron scattering but does not account for detailed structure, and with more sophisticated relativistic calculations (semirel, PRL, Antonov_nu, Juan, Giusti, Co', Omar). The method enables predictions for charged-current and neutral-current neutrino cross sections that can be used in neutrino oscillation analyses and for extracting nucleon strange form factors, complementing parity-violating electron scattering studies (Musolf, Bill0).
