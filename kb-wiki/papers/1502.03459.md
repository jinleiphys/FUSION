# Flavor dependence of baryon melting temperature in effective models of QCD

> We apply the three-flavor (Polyakov-)Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to generate baryons as quark-diquark bound states using many-body techniques at finite temperature. All the baryonic states belonging to the octet and decuplet flavor representations are generated in the isospin-symmetric case. For each state we extract the melting temperature at which the baryon may decay into a quark-diquark pair. We seek for an evidence of the strangeness dependence of the baryon melting temperature as suggested by the statistical thermal models and supported by lattice-QCD results. A clear and robust signal for this claim is found, pointing to a flavor dependence of the hadronic deconfinement temperature.

## Key claim

The paper finds a clear and robust signal that the melting (Mott) temperature of baryons depends on their strangeness content, supporting the idea that the hadronic deconfinement temperature is flavor-dependent, as suggested by statistical thermal models and lattice-QCD results.

## Method

The authors apply the three-flavor (Polyakov-)Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL/PNJL) model to generate baryons as quark-diquark bound states using the Fadeev equation in the static approximation at finite temperature. Diquarks are first obtained as bound states of two quarks via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the random-phase approximation, and then baryons belonging to the octet and decuplet flavor representations are constructed as quark-diquark bound states in the isospin-symmetric case at vanishing chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of flavor-dependent hadronization temperatures suggested by statistical thermal model fits of ALICE data (Preghenella 2011; Andronic 2005) and lattice-QCD calculations of the crossover temperature (Bellwied 2013). The results support the finding of Chatterjee (2013) that thermal fits with two independent freeze-out temperatures for non-strange and strange hadrons (differing by about 15 MeV) provide a better description of hadronic yields. The approach enables future studies of baryon melting in heavy-ion collisions and provides a microscopic explanation for the experimentally observed larger phi/pi ratio than expected from a common hadronization temperature.
