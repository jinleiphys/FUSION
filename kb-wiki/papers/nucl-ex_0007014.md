# A consistent analysis of (e,e'p) and (d,3He) experiments

> The apparent discrepancy between spectroscopic factors obtained in (e,e'p) and (d,3He) experiments is investigated. This is performed first for 48Ca(e,e'p) and 48Ca(d,3He) experiments and then for other nuclei. It is shown that the discrepancy disappears if the (d,3He) experiments are re-analyzed with a non-local finite range DWBA analysis with a bound-state wave function that is obtained from (e,e'p) experiments.

# A consistent analysis of (e,e'p) and (d,3He) experiments

## Key claim

The apparent discrepancy between spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p) and (d,3He) experiments disappears when the (d,3He) data are re-analyzed using a non-local finite-range DWBA analysis combined with bound-state wave functions obtained from (e,e'p) experiments. The authors demonstrate this specifically for 48Ca and then extend the analysis to other nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Coulomb Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (CDWIA) analysis of 48Ca(e,e'p) data at 440 MeV beam energy to extract spectroscopic factors and bound-state wave functions. They then re-analyzed two existing 48Ca(d,3He) datasets (at 79.2 MeV deuteron energy) using non-local finite-range DWBA calculations with the (e,e'p)-derived bound-state wave functions, applying Perey non-locality corrections and the local energy approximation for finite-range effects. The radial sensitivity of both reactions was investigated by varying the lower radial integration bound to determine which region of the bound-state wave function each reaction probes.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca(e,e'p): spectroscopic factor for the 1/2+ ground state transition is 1.07(7), for the 3/2+ state at 0.36 MeV is 2.26(16), and for the 5/2+ state at 3.42 MeV is 0.683(49)
- The (e,e'p) experiment used an electron beam energy of 440 MeV and outgoing proton kinetic energy of 100 MeV, with an experimental systematic error of 4%
- The (d,3He) experiment from Banks (1985) used an incoming deuteron energy of 79.2 MeV
- After re-analysis with non-local finite-range corrections and (e,e'p)-derived bound-state wave functions, the average ratio of (d,3He) to (e,e'p) spectroscopic factors becomes 1.0, compared to the original local zero-range analysis which gave values on average 50% higher
- The non-locality correction used a range parameter beta of 0.85 fm for both the distorted waves and the bound-state wave function
- The bound-state wave function Woods-Saxon well used a diffuseness a0 of 0.65 fm and Thomas spin-orbit parameter lambda of 25
- The uncertainty in the (d,3He) spectroscopic factors from the rms radius uncertainty (about 3%) yields 25% for the 1/2+ transition, 28% for the 3/2+ transition, and 29% for the 5/2+ transition

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p) knockout reactions, which gave values substantially below the independent-particle shell model sum-rule limit (as reported by Blok (1987), Her (1988), and Lapikas (1993)), and those from (d,3He) transfer reactions, which gave values close to the IPSM limit. The authors show that the (d,3He) reaction is only sensitive to the exponential tail of the bound-state wave function (between r=5 and 10 fm for 48Ca), while (e,e'p) probes the entire radial region, making the (d,3He) spectroscopic factors highly dependent on the assumed bound-state wave function shape. This enables future nuclear-structure calculations based on realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (such as those by Pandharipande (1997), Geurts (1996), and Rijsdijk (1992, 1996)) to be tested against accurate absolute spectroscopic factors from both reactions.
