# Baryon stopping as a signal of the mixed phase onset

> It is argued that the experimentally observed baryon stopping indicates a non-monotonous behaviour as a function of the incident energy of colliding nuclei. This can be quantified by a midrapidity reduced curvature of the net-proton rapidity spectrum and reveals itself as a zigzag irregularity in the excitation function of this curvature. The three-fluid dynamic calculations with a hadronic equation of state (EoS) fail to reproduce this irregularity. At the same time, the same calculations with an EoS involving a first-order phase transition and a crossover one into the quark-gluon phase do reproduce this zigzag behaviour, however only qualitatively.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed baryon stopping, quantified by a midrapidity reduced curvature of the net-proton rapidity spectrum, shows a non-monotonous zigzag irregularity as a function of incident energy. Three-fluid dynamic calculations with a hadronic equation of state fail to reproduce this irregularity, while calculations with equations of state involving a first-order phase transition or a crossover into the quark-gluon phase reproduce the zigzag behavior, but only qualitatively.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental net-proton rapidity distributions from central Au+Au (AGS) and Pb+Pb (SPS) collisions across energies from 2A GeV to 158A GeV. The data were fitted with a two-source thermal model (Equation 2-sources-fit) using a least-squares method in the rapidity range |y-y_cm|/y_cm < 0.7, from which a midrapidity reduced curvature C_y was extracted. These results were compared to three-fluid dynamics (3FD) calculations using a hadronic equation of state, a first-order phase transition equation of state (Gibbs construction), and a crossover equation of state into the quark-gluon phase.

## Key numbers

- The zigzag irregularity in the reduced curvature C_y is observed at center-of-mass energies around s^{1/2} = 4.7 GeV, where a maximum in y_cm (dN/dy)_cm occurs due to light fragment production becoming negligible above this energy.
- The 3FD calculation without coalescence (fragment production) shows a monotonous decrease of y_cm (dN/dy)_cm beginning from s^{1/2} = 2.7 GeV, the lowest energy considered.
- At 20A GeV, the hadronic equation of state predicts a pronounced dip at midrapidity instead of the bump observed in data, while at 40A GeV the description is still satisfactory.
- The biased fit for data at 80A GeV and 158A GeV constrained the total normalization of the distribution to be less than the total proton number in colliding nuclei (=164).
- The lower point at s^{1/2} = 17.3 GeV corresponds to the new data at 158A GeV, with its upper error resulting from normalization uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of baryon stopping within the three-fluid dynamics model (3FD, Ivanov et al. 2005-2007) and comparisons with hadron-string dynamics models (Bratkovskaya et al. 2004, 2009) and the hybrid hydro-kinetic model (Bleicher 2009). The energy location of the observed anomaly coincides with previously observed anomalies for other hadron-production properties at low SPS energies (Alt 2007, Gazdzicki 1998). The author notes that the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions may be inappropriate for the fast dynamics of heavy-ion collisions (Randrup 2009, Voskresensky 2009) and that upgraded data from FAIR at GSI, NICA at Dubna, and the low-energy-scan program at RHIC are needed to confirm or discard the existence of the zigzag irregularity.
