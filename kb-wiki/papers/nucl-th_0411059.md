# Analysis and Applications of the Generalised Dyson Mapping

> Generalised Dyson boson-fermion mappings are considered. These are techniques used in the analysis of the quantum many-body problem, and are instances of so-called boson expansion methods. A generalised Dyson boson-fermion mapping is a one-to-one linear but non-unitary operator that can be applied to vectors representing the states of a many-fermion system. A vector representing a fermion system maps onto a vector that represents a state of a many-body system that contains both bosons and fermions. The motivation for doing such a mapping is the hope that it will reveal some property of the system that simplifies its analysis and that was hidden in the original form. The aims of this text are to review the theory of generalized Dyson boson-fermion mappings and to find a useful application for a generalized Dyson boson-fermion mapping, by considering a non-trivial model, namely the Richardson model for superconductivity. It is the first time that a boson expansion technique is implemented for a system where the roles of both collective and non-collective fermion pairs are important. The Dyson mapping uncovers non-trivial properties of the system that aid the construction of time-independent as well as time-dependent perturbation expansions. The time-independent expansions agree with results that other authors have obtained through methods other than boson expansions. The time-dependent expansions might in future prove useful in understanding aspects of the dynamics of ultra-cold fermi gases, when time-dependent magnetic fields are used to vary the atom-atom interaction strenght.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a generalized Dyson boson-fermion mapping can be applied to the Richardson model for superconductivity, and that this mapping uncovers non-trivial properties enabling both time-independent and time-dependent perturbation expansions. The hedge is that the time-dependent expansions "might in future prove useful" for understanding ultra-cold Fermi gas dynamics, rather than being immediately applicable.

## Method

The author constructs a non-unitary, one-to-one linear operator T that maps vectors from the many-fermion Hilbert space (or a subspace thereof) onto vectors in a boson-fermion Hilbert space, using a superalgebra framework to guarantee invertibility. A representation of the superalgebra is built using fermion pair creation and annihilation operators in the fermion space, and a corresponding representation is constructed in the boson-fermion space using ideal boson and fermion operators. The mapping is then refined by applying an additional linear invertible operator to remove undesirable properties, yielding the final Dyson mapping.

## Key numbers

* The number of linearly independent fermion pair operators in the fermion Hilbert space is at most (1/2)Ω(Ω-1), where Ω is the dimension of the one-particle fermion Hilbert space, placing an upper bound M ≤ (1/2)Ω(Ω-1) on the number of collective pair operators.
* The orthogonality condition for the structure coefficients is (1/2) χ_i^{μν} χ^j_{μν} = g δ_i^j, where g is a positive real constant independent of i and j.
* The foundational vector eigenvalue is λ_i^j = g δ_i^j for the representation in the fermion space.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Dyson mapping formalism developed by Dobaczewski, Scholtz and Geyer (1993) and Navratil, Geyer and Dobaczewski (1994-1996), and reviewed by Cejnar and Geyer (2002). It is the first implementation of a boson expansion technique for a system where both collective and non-collective fermion pairs are important, and the time-dependent expansions may enable future studies of ultra-cold Fermi gas dynamics with time-dependent magnetic fields, as studied experimentally by Greiner, Regal and Jin (2003).
