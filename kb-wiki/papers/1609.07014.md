# Chiral-scale effective theory including a dilatonic meson

> A scale-invariant chiral effective Lagrangian is constructed for octet pions and a dilaton figuring as Nambu-Goldstone bosons with vector mesons incorporated as hidden gauge fields. The Lagrangian is built to the next-to-leading order in chiral-scale counting without baryon fields and then to leading order including baryons. The resulting theory is hidden scale-symmetric and local symmetric. We also discuss some possible applications of the present Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a scale-invariant chiral effective Lagrangian that includes a dilatonic meson (identified with the $f_0(500)$ scalar) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson from spontaneously broken scale symmetry, combined with hidden local symmetry for vector mesons, but the authors explicitly note that the existence of an infrared fixed point in QCD for three flavors remains "not yet established and remains highly controversial" and that the construction assumes the system is "near but not on the IR fixed point."

## Method

The authors build a chiral-scale perturbation theory ($\chi$PT$_\sigma$) using a conformal compensator field $\chi$ that transforms linearly under scale transformations, with octet pions and a dilaton $\sigma$ as Nambu-Goldstone bosons. The Lagrangian is constructed to next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral-scale counting for mesons without baryons, then to leading order including baryons via heavy-baryon expansion, with vector mesons incorporated through hidden local symmetry (HLS) by decomposing the chiral field $U(x)=\xi_L^\dagger(x)\xi_R(x)$ and introducing gauge fields $V_\mu(x)$ for the $[SU(3)]_{\rm HLS}$ gauge symmetry.

## Key numbers

* The dilaton decay constant $f_\sigma$ is roughly estimated to be $100$ MeV (from Ref. [Crewther:2013vea]).
* The dilaton mass $m_\sigma$ satisfies $m_\sigma^2 f_\sigma^2 = 4\beta^\prime(4 + \beta^\prime)c \simeq 16 \beta^\prime c$ for small $\beta^\prime$, analogous to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for pions.
* The density $n_{1/2} \sim (2-3)n_0$ (where $n_0$ is normal nuclear matter density) is cited as the density above which skyrmions fractionize to half-skyrmions in dense matter.
* The parameter $\beta^\prime$ (slope of the beta function at the IR fixed point) is noted to be possibly $\sim (2-3)$ from skyrmion crystal simulations in dense baryonic matter.

## Context

This work extends the Crewther-Tunstall (C-T) proposal (Crewther, 2013) that the $f_0(500)$ scalar meson is a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson from spontaneously broken scale symmetry, combining it with hidden local symmetry for vector mesons (Harada, 2003) to build a systematic chiral-scale perturbation theory. The paper enables future systematic calculations of nuclear phenomena involving scalar mesons, particularly for dense baryonic matter in compact stars, and provides a framework to treat strangeness consistently in such systems, which has been a long-standing problem. The authors compare their C-T approach to an alternative approach by Golterman and Shamir (2016) that uses a conformal window in the Veneziano limit.
