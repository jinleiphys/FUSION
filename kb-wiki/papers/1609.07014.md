# Chiral-scale effective theory including a dilatonic meson

> A scale-invariant chiral effective Lagrangian is constructed for octet pions and a dilaton figuring as Nambu-Goldstone bosons with vector mesons incorporated as hidden gauge fields. The Lagrangian is built to the next-to-leading order in chiral-scale counting without baryon fields and then to leading order including baryons. The resulting theory is hidden scale-symmetric and local symmetric. We also discuss some possible applications of the present Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a scale-invariant chiral effective Lagrangian that includes a dilatonic meson (identified with the $f_0(500)$ scalar) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson from spontaneously broken scale symmetry, combined with hidden local symmetry for vector mesons. The construction assumes the existence of an infrared fixed point in QCD's running coupling, but the authors note this remains controversial and unestablished for three-flavor QCD in vacuum, and suggest the framework may be more relevant as an emergent symmetry in dense matter.

## Method

The authors build a chiral-scale perturbation theory ($\chi$PT$_\sigma$) using a conformal compensator field $\chi$ that transforms linearly under scale transformations, with octet pions and a dilaton $\sigma$ as Nambu-Goldstone bosons. The Lagrangian is constructed to next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral-scale counting for mesons without baryons, then extended to leading order including baryons using heavy-baryon expansion. Vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$) are incorporated via hidden local symmetry (HLS), with the dilaton compensator inserted into the HLS Lagrangian to make it gauge equivalent to $\chi$PT$_\sigma$ after integrating out vector mesons.

## Key numbers

* The dilaton decay constant $f_\sigma$ is roughly estimated to be $100$ MeV (from Ref. [Crewther:2013vea]).
* The dilaton mass squared satisfies $m_\sigma^2 f_\sigma^2 = 4\beta^\prime(4 + \beta^\prime)c \simeq 16 \beta^\prime c$ for small $\beta^\prime$, analogous to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for pions.
* The $\sigma$ meson mass scale is comparable to that of kaons, motivating three-flavor extension of chiral perturbation theory.
* In dense skyrmion matter, a transition occurs at density $n_{1/2} \sim (2-3)n_0$ (where $n_0$ is normal nuclear matter density), above which half-skyrmions retain a sizable residual mass.

## Context

This work extends the Crewther-Tunstall (C-T) proposal (Crewther, 2013) that the $f_0(500)$ scalar is a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson from spontaneously broken scale symmetry near an infrared fixed point, combining it with hidden local symmetry for vector mesons (Harada, 2003). The authors compare their approach to an alternative by Golterman and Shamir (2016) that uses a conformal window in the Veneziano limit. The Lagrangian is intended for systematic application to dense baryonic matter, including compact-star matter, building on earlier mean-field treatments by Li, Ma, and Rho (2015) and Paeng, Kuo, Lee, and Rho (2016).
