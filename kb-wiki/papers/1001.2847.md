# Energy Dependence of High Moments for Net-proton Distributions

> High moments of multiplicity distributions of conserved quantities are predicted to be sensitive to critical fluctuations. To understand the effect of the complicated non-critical physics backgrounds on the proposed observable, we have studied various moments of net-proton distributions with AMPT, Hijing, Therminator and UrQMD models, in which no QCD critical point physics is implemented. It is found that the centrality evolution of various moments of net-proton distributions can be uniformly described by a superposition of emission sources. In addition, in the absence of critical phenomena, some moment products of net-proton distribution, related to the baryon number susceptibilities ratio in Lattice QCD calculation, are predicted to be constant as a function of the collision centrality. We argue that a non-monotonic dependence of the moment products as a function collision centrality and the beam energy may be used to locate the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, in the absence of QCD critical point physics, the moment product $\kappa \sigma^2$ of net-proton distributions is constant as a function of both collision centrality and beam energy across several models; a non-monotonic dependence of such moment products on beam energy may therefore serve as a signature to locate the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors calculated the mean, variance, skewness, and kurtosis of event-by-event net-proton distributions from four models (AMPT, Hijing, Therminator, UrQMD) that contain no QCD critical point physics. They applied an Identical Independent Emission Source (IIES) assumption to derive scaling relations for the centrality evolution of these moments, and tested whether the moment products $S\sigma$, $\kappa\sigma/S$, and $\kappa\sigma^2$ remain constant with centrality and beam energy. The analysis used protons and anti-protons with $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV/c and $|y| < 0.5$ for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the mean ($M$) and variance ($\sigma$) of net-proton distributions increase monotonically with $<N_{part}>$, while skewness ($S$) and kurtosis ($\kappa$) decrease monotonically across all models.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV, $\kappa\sigma^2$ of net-proton distributions is constant with respect to $<N_{part}>$ within errors for all models studied.
- The $\kappa\sigma^2$ values from all models show no dependence on beam energy and are close to unity; the Therminator thermal model result is much closer to unity than the transport models.
- The baryon chemical potential range covered by the RHIC beam energy scan is from $\mu_B \sim 0$ to $\mu_B \sim 500$ MeV, with the scan spanning $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ down to 5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework linking high moments of conserved quantities to critical fluctuations, as proposed by Stephanov (2009) and Asakawa (2009), and connects to Lattice QCD susceptibility calculations by Cheng (2008). It provides a baseline for non-critical physics backgrounds using AMPT (Lin), Hijing (Wang), UrQMD (Petersen), and Therminator (Kisiel) models, enabling the RHIC beam energy scan to search for the QCD critical point by looking for deviations from the constant moment-product behavior predicted here.
