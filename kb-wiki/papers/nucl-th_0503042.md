# Double pion photoproduction on nucleon and deuteron

> Photoproduction of two pions on nucleon and deuteron is studied for photon energies from threshold up to $E_γ= 1.5$ GeV. For the elementary operator an effective Lagrangean approach is used with resonance and Born contributions. The model parameters are fixed by resonance decay widths and multipole analyses of single pion photoproduction. A satisfactory description of total cross sections of two-pion production on the proton for various charge channels is achieved, except for $π^0π^0$ production for which a significant underestimation is found near threshold. The operator then serves for the evaluation of this reaction on the deuteron in the impulse approximation. In addition, $NN$ rescattering in the final state is taken into account, but $πN$ and $ππ$ rescatterings are neglected. Results are presented for total cross sections and target asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for double pion photoproduction on the nucleon and deuteron that achieves a satisfactory description of total cross sections for various charge channels on the proton, except for π⁰π⁰ production where a significant underestimation is found near threshold; the model then evaluates the reaction on the deuteron using impulse approximation with NN rescattering included, but πN and ππ rescatterings are neglected.

## Method

The elementary γN → ππN operator was constructed using an effective Lagrangian approach with resonance and Born contributions, with model parameters fixed by resonance decay widths and multipole analyses of single pion photoproduction. The operator was then applied to the deuteron target in the impulse approximation, with additional NN final-state rescattering included while πN and ππ rescatterings were neglected. The isotopic spin structure of the amplitude was decomposed into six independent charge channels using symmetric and antisymmetric isospin operators.

- πNN coupling constant: fπNN = 1.0
- M1 N→Δ transition coupling: gM = -1.845

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to double pion photoproduction by Oset, Laget, Ochi, and Mokeev, which showed strong model dependence, particularly regarding the role of final-state interactions. The paper addresses the need for neutron data by studying nuclear effects in deuteron targets, following earlier work by Zabrod (showing similar yields for ¹H and ²H targets) and Kleber (extracting γn→π⁰π⁰n cross sections under the spectator model assumption). The results enable future systematic analysis of the isotopic spin structure of the elementary amplitude and study of "missing" resonances weakly coupled to the πN channel.
