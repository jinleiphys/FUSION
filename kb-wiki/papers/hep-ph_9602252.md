# Shadowing Aspects of Nuclear Parton Distributions

> Various shadowing aspects of nuclear parton distributions are discussed in a parton-model framework. We explain existing $x$ dependent data of $F_2^A/F_2^D$, then the model is applied to a flavor asymmetry $\bar u-\bar d$ in a nucleus, to shadowing in valence-quark distributions, and to nuclear dependence of $Q^2$ evolution. First, we find that a finite nuclear $\bar u-\bar d$ distribution could be possible due to nuclear interactions even in the flavor symmetric case in the nucleon ($\bar u -\bar d$=0). Second, it is indicated that valence-quark shadowing could be used for discriminating among various shadowing models. Third, we find that nuclear dependence of $Q^2$ evolution $\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C]/ \partial [\ln Q^2]$, which was found by NMC, is essentially understood by modification of parton distributions in nuclei. However, higher-twist effects in nuclear interactions could be tested by studying the details of the nuclear $Q^2$ evolution.

## Key claim

Nuclear parton distributions can exhibit flavor asymmetry (nonzero $\bar u - \bar d$) purely from nuclear interactions even when the nucleon itself has a symmetric sea ($\bar u - \bar d = 0$), and valence-quark shadowing predictions differ dramatically between the parton-recombination model and the aligned-jet model, though both remain consistent with existing experimental data.

## Method

The author applies a hybrid parton model combining $Q^2$-rescaling at medium $x$ (to account for the EMC effect via confinement-radius changes or nuclear binding) with parton-recombination mechanisms at small $x$ (where longitudinal localization of partons exceeds the average nucleon separation). Nuclear parton distributions are calculated at an initial $Q_0^2 = 0.8$ GeV$^2$ and then evolved to higher $Q^2$ using leading-order DGLAP, next-to-leading-order DGLAP, and parton-recombination evolution equations. The model is applied to compute the flavor asymmetry $\bar u - \bar d$ in tungsten, valence-quark shadowing ratios $V_A/V_D$, and the nuclear dependence of $Q^2$ evolution $\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2]$.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-excess parameter $\varepsilon = (N-Z)/(N+Z)$ for tungsten $^{184}_{74}W_{110}$ is 0.196.
- The initial $Q^2$ for parton evolution is taken as $Q_0^2 = 0.8$ GeV$^2$ when using KMRS-B type input distributions.
- Nuclear modification of $\bar u - \bar d$ in tungsten is estimated to be of order 2% to 10% compared with the asymmetry suggested by the MRS-D0 distribution at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$.
- The NMC found significant deviations $\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2] \ne 0$, which the author analyzes at $Q^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the parton-recombination and $Q^2$-rescaling model previously developed by Kumano (1993, 1994) to new observables beyond the standard $F_2^A/F_2^D$ ratio. It addresses the NMC finding of nuclear $Q^2$ evolution dependence (Mucklich and Sandacz, private communication) and the Gottfried sum rule violation discovered by NMC in 1991. The paper enables future discrimination among shadowing models through measurements of valence-quark shadowing (potentially at HERA via charged pion production) and through detailed study of nuclear $Q^2$ evolution to test higher-twist recombination effects.
