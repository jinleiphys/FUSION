# Fission of heavy $Λ$ hypernuclei with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

> Fission-related phenomena of heavy $Λ$ hypernuclei are discussed with the constraint Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS (SHF+BCS) method, in which a similar Skyrme-type interaction is employed also for the interaction between a $Λ$ particle and a nucleon. Assuming that the $Λ$ particle adiabatically follows the fission motion, we discuss the fission barrier height of $^{239}_Λ$U. We find that the fission barrier height increases slightly when the $Λ$ particle occupies the lowest level. In this case, the $Λ$ particle is always attached to the heavier fission fragment. This indicates that one may produce heavy neutron-rich $Λ$ hypernuclei through fission, whose weak decay is helpful for the nuclear transmutation of long-lived fission products. We also discuss cases where the $Λ$ particle occupies a higher single-particle level.

# Fission of heavy Λ hypernuclei with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

## Key claim

The fission barrier height of 239ΛU increases slightly (by about 0.27 MeV for the inner barrier and 0.82 MeV for the outer barrier) when the Λ particle occupies the lowest single-particle level and adiabatically follows the fission motion; however, when the Λ particle occupies certain higher single-particle levels, the fission barrier height can decrease relative to that of 238U.

## Method

The authors used the constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS (SHF+BCS) method with axial symmetry, employing the SkM* parameter set for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and a Skyrme-type interaction (parameter set No. 4 from Yamamoto (1988), optimized to 209ΛPb) for the Λ-nucleon interaction. The potential energy surface for fission of 239ΛU was obtained by constraining the total quadrupole moment Q2, with the octupole moment optimized by adding a small octupole moment to the initial wave functions, assuming the Λ particle adiabatically follows the fission motion.

## Key numbers

- Inner fission barrier height for 238U: 8.20 MeV; for 239ΛU with Λ in the lowest level: 8.47 MeV (increase of 0.27 MeV)
- Outer fission barrier height for 238U: 6.60 MeV; for 239ΛU with Λ in the lowest level: 7.42 MeV (increase of 0.82 MeV)
- Energy difference of the Λ particle between the inner saddle point and ground state: ΔEΛ = 0.25 MeV, which nearly accounts for the increase in the inner fission barrier height
- At Q2 = 200 b, the Λ particle attracts approximately 0.54 protons and 0.86 neutrons into the heavier fission fragment due to the attractive ΛN interaction
- For Λ in higher single-particle levels (labeled A, B, C, D), inner barrier heights range from 6.87 MeV to 8.16 MeV, and outer barrier heights range from 3.23 MeV to 5.61 MeV, all lower than the corresponding barriers for 238U

## Context

This work extends the study of hyperon impurity effects from light hypernuclei (such as the shrinkage of 6Li core in 7ΛLi predicted by cluster models of Bando (1983) and Hiyama (1996, 1999), and confirmed experimentally by Tanida (2001)) to heavy fissile nuclei. It also builds on earlier theoretical studies of Λ attachment probability in fission by Krappe and Pashkevich (1993, 1996) and Karpeshin (1995) using phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials and statistical methods, and on the experimental work of Armstrong (1993) on hypernuclei formed in antiproton annihilation on 209Bi and 238U. The results suggest that fission of heavy Λ hypernuclei could provide a novel method to produce heavy neutron-rich Λ hypernuclei, complementing direct production methods such as the (π−,K+) reaction used by Saha (2005) and Tamura (2009), and may have applications in nuclear transmutation of long-lived fission products.
