# $α$-Particle Spectrum in the Reaction p+$^{11}$B$\to α+ ^8Be^*\to 3α$

> Using a simple phenomenological parametrization of the reaction amplitude we calculated $α$-particle spectrum in the reaction p+$^{11}$B$\to α+ ^8Be^*\to 3α$ at the resonance proton energy 675 KeV. The parametrization includes Breit-Wigner factor with an energy dependent width for intermediate $^8Be^*$ state and the Coulomb and the centrifugal factors in $α$-particle emission vertexes. The shape of the spectrum consists of a well defined peak corresponding to emission of the primary $α$ and a flat shoulder going down to very low energy. We found that below 1.5 MeV there are 17.5% of $α$'s and below 1 MeV there are 11% of them.

## Key claim

Using a simple phenomenological parametrization of the reaction amplitude, the calculated alpha-particle spectrum in p+11B -> alpha + 8Be* -> 3alpha at the resonance proton energy 675 keV shows that 17.5% of alphas have energy below 1.5 MeV and 11% below 1 MeV, but this result depends on the inclusion of Coulomb and centrifugal factors in the emission vertices and an energy-dependent width for the intermediate 8Be* state.

## Method

The reaction amplitude was parametrized with Breit-Wigner factors including an energy-dependent width for the intermediate 8Be* state, plus Coulomb and centrifugal barrier factors in the alpha-particle emission vertices. The amplitude was symmetrized for three identical Bose particles in the final state, and the spectrum was obtained by integrating the squared amplitude over phase space, with normalization to one. The calculation used the resonance at 675 keV proton energy corresponding to the 16.57 MeV excited state of 12C with quantum numbers 2- and isospin T=1, and considered only the L=1 partial wave for the primary alpha emission.

## Key numbers

- Resonance proton energy: 675 keV
- Excited 12C state energy: 16.57 MeV with total width Gamma = 0.2 MeV
- Excited 8Be* state energy: E* = 3.06 MeV with width 1.513 MeV and lifetime 4.35 x 10^-22 s
- Q-value for primary transition Q1 = 6.14 MeV; total Q-value Q = 9.295 MeV
- Cross section at resonance peak: approximately 1.2 b
- 17.5% of alpha particles have energy below 1.5 MeV
- 11% of alpha particles have energy below 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Beck and Lin (no year given) that produced spectra for fixed angles of alpha particles, by providing a theoretical model for the integrated spectrum. The low-energy tail of the alpha spectrum is crucial for aneutronic fusion proposals by Rost (no year given) and Volos (no year given) using the p+11B reaction for energy production. The calculation enables estimation of the fraction of low-energy alphas that must be handled in reactor designs, and could be refined by including the L=3 partial wave or alpha-8Be interactions omitted in this model.
