# Non-perturbative constraints on perturbation theory at finite temperature

> It has long been understood that the inclusion of temperature in the perturbative treatment of quantum field theories leads to complications that are not present at zero temperature. In these proceedings we report on the non-perturbative obstructions that arise, and how these lead to deviations in the predictions of lattice scalar correlation functions in massive $ϕ^{4}$ theory. Using the known non-perturbative spectral constraints satisfied by finite-temperature correlation functions we outline why the presence of distinct particle-like excitations could provide a resolution to these issues.

# Non-perturbative constraints on perturbation theory at finite temperature

## Key claim
Standard finite-temperature perturbation theory in massive φ⁴ theory breaks down at two-loop order when using vacuum-like free-field propagators with purely real poles, and this breakdown is a symptom of the fundamental Narnhofer-Requardt-Thirring (NRT) theorem which prohibits the existence of scattering states with real dispersion relations at T>0. The presence of distinct particle-like excitations called thermoparticles could provide a consistent basis for a finite-temperature perturbative expansion.

## Method
The authors compared spatial correlator predictions from lattice perturbation theory (computed up to two-loop order using the discretised free-field propagator) with lattice simulation results for real φ⁴ theory in the symmetric phase, using identical bare parameters (am₀, g₀) on finite Nₛ³ × Nτ lattices. They then extracted thermoparticle spectral components from the spatial correlator data and used these to predict temporal correlators, comparing those predictions with lattice data to test consistency.

## Key numbers
- Lattice simulations used Nₛ=16 with bare parameters am₀=0.15 and g₀=1.5
- At the highest temperature (Nτ=2), two-loop perturbative predictions were larger than one-loop predictions, indicating qualitative departure from asymptotic-like behaviour
- The zero-temperature-like case used large Nτ=16, while finite temperature used small Nτ=2
- The free-field propagator zero mode increasingly diverges for smaller values of am₀

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Bros, Buchholz, and collaborators (1992-1996) who established non-perturbative spectral constraints for finite-temperature scalar QFTs, including the generalised Källén-Lehmann representation and the concept of thermoparticles. It directly tests the conclusion of Weldon (2001) that finite-temperature perturbation theory breaks down at two-loop order in φ⁴ theory. The analysis follows the same approach used by Lowdon (2022) and Bala (2023) for light pseudo-scalar mesons in lattice QCD around the chiral crossover region. The results suggest that a consistent perturbative expansion should be parametrised in terms of thermoparticle degrees of freedom rather than vacuum-like propagators.
