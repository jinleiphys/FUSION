# Generic multi-particle transverse momentum correlations as a new tool for studying nuclear structure at the energy frontier

> The mean transverse momentum of produced particles, [pt], and its event-by-event fluctuations give direct access to the initial conditions of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and help probe the colliding nuclei's structure. The [pt] fluctuations can be studied via multi-particle pt correlations; so far, only the lowest four orders have been studied. Higher-order fluctuations can provide stronger constraints on the initial conditions and improved sensitivity to the detailed nuclear structure; however, their direct implementation can be challenging and is still lacking. In this paper, we apply a generic recursive algorithm for the genuine multi-particle pt correlations, which enables the accurate study of higher-order [pt] fluctuations without computationally heavy processing for the first time. With this algorithm, we will examine the power of multi-particle pt correlations through Monte Carlo model studies with different nuclear structures. The impact on the nuclear structure studies, including the nuclear deformation and triaxial structure, will be discussed. These results will demonstrate the usefulness of multi-particle pt correlations for studying nuclear structure in high-energy nuclei collisions at RHIC and the LHC, which could serve as complementary to existing low-energy nuclear structure studies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that multi-particle transverse momentum cumulants (genuine correlations) of arbitrary order can be computed efficiently using a generic recursive algorithm, and that higher-order cumulants (beyond second order) are sensitive to the triaxial nuclear structure parameter γ, which cannot be resolved by existing anisotropic flow measurements.

## Method

The authors developed a recursive algorithm for computing genuine multi-particle transverse momentum correlations (cumulants) of any order, based on constructing sums of powers of transverse momentum and particle weights (P_k and W_k). They implemented this algorithm in the AMPT transport model with String Melting for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.44 TeV, using four different configurations of ¹²⁹Xe nuclear structure (spherical, prolate with β₂=0.18 and γ=0°, triaxial with β₂=0.18 and γ=27°, and oblate with β₂=0.18 and γ=60°). They also provided baseline predictions using the HIJING event generator for cumulants up to eighth order.

## Key numbers

- The second-order cumulant κ₂ increases by about 10-15% in central collisions for β₂ ≠ 0 compared to spherical nuclei, but shows no distinction between prolate, triaxial, and oblate configurations
- The third-order cumulant κ₃ shows approximately 30% larger magnitude for oblate nuclei (γ=60°) and approximately 30% smaller magnitude for prolate nuclei (γ=0°) compared to spherical nuclei in central collisions
- The fourth-order cumulant κ₄ for prolate nuclei shows almost a 100% difference with respect to the spherical case in central collisions
- The HIJING model predicts κ₁-κ₃ are strictly positive across all centralities, κ₄ changes sign around semi-central collisions, and κ₆ is strictly negative

## Context

This work extends the study of mean transverse momentum fluctuations from the lowest four orders (Jia 2021, Bhatta 2021) to arbitrary orders via a recursive algorithm similar to the Generic Framework for azimuthal angle correlations (Bilandzic 2013, Moravcova 2020). The results show that κ₃ can distinguish triaxial structures that anisotropic flow measurements (Lu 2023) cannot resolve, and that κ₄ shows unexpected γ-sensitivity not predicted by the simple liquid-drop model (Jia 2021). This enables complementary nuclear structure studies at high-energy colliders (RHIC and LHC) to existing low-energy nuclear structure measurements.
