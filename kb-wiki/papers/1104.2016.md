# Application of complex-scaling method for few-body scattering

> Formalism based on complex-scaling method is developed for solving the few particle scattering problem by employing only trivial boundary conditions. Several applications are presented proving efficiency of the method in describing elastic and three-body break-up reactions for Hamiltonians which may include both short and long-range interaction.

## Key claim

The complex-scaling method can solve few-particle scattering problems (elastic and three-body break-up) using only trivial boundary conditions, but for three-body break-up above threshold the complex scaling angle must be restricted to small values (e.g., below 14.2 degrees at 14.1 MeV lab energy) to ensure convergence, and the method works for Hamiltonians containing both short-range and long-range (Coulomb) interactions.

## Method

The authors formulate the scattering problem using driven Schrodinger equations (two-body) and Faddeev equations (three-body) in configuration space, then apply a uniform complex scaling to all particle coordinates by a factor exp(i theta). This transformation causes the outgoing scattered waves to vanish exponentially at large distances, allowing the equations to be solved on a finite grid with vanishing boundary conditions. For three-body break-up, the Faddeev-Merkuriev equations are used to handle Coulomb interactions, and scattering observables are extracted either from asymptotic normalization coefficients or from integral relations derived via Green's theorem.

## Key numbers

- NN 1S0 phase shift at E_cm = 1 MeV with MT I-III potential: exact value 63.512 degrees; integral method with r_max = 100 fm and theta = 30 degrees reproduces 63.512 degrees to five digits
- NN 1S0 phase shift at E_cm = 1 MeV with MT I-III plus Coulomb: exact value 50.491 degrees; integral method with r_max = 100 fm and theta = 30 degrees reproduces 50.491 degrees
- NN 1S0 phase shift at E_cm = 50 MeV with MT I-III: exact value 20.027 degrees; integral method with r_max = 50 fm and theta = 10 degrees reproduces 20.027 degrees
- NN 1S0 phase shift at E_cm = 50 MeV with MT I-III plus Coulomb: exact value 20.755 degrees; integral method with r_max = 50 fm and theta = 10 degrees reproduces 20.755 degrees
- n-d doublet scattering at E_lab = 14.1 MeV: Re(delta) = 105.50 degrees, inelasticity eta = 0.4653 (benchmark: 105.49, 0.4649) using theta in [4,12.5] degrees
- n-d doublet at E_lab = 42 MeV: Re(delta) = 41.55 degrees, eta = 0.5014 (benchmark: 41.35, 0.5022) using theta in [4,7.5] degrees
- n-d quartet at E_lab = 14.1 MeV: Re(delta) = 68.97 degrees, eta = 0.9784 (benchmark: 68.95, 0.9782)
- Maximum allowed scaling angle for n-d at E_lab = 14.1 MeV: theta_max = 14.2 degrees; at E_lab = 42 MeV: theta_max = 8.9 degrees

## Context

This work extends the complex-scaling method, previously applied to resonance scattering (Moiseyev) and two-body scattering (Curdy 2004, Elander), to full three-body scattering including break-up channels. It provides an alternative to methods that require explicit treatment of asymptotic wave functions, such as the Faddeev calculations of Friar (2005) and Deltuva (2009) for three-body systems, and the Green Function Monte Carlo and No-Core Shell Model approaches that are limited to binary scattering. The method enables accurate calculation of elastic and break-up observables for few-nucleon systems with both short-range and Coulomb interactions, and could be extended to four-body scattering.
