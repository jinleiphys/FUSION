# The nuclear energy density functionals with modified radial dependence of the isoscalar effective mass

> Calculations for infinite nuclear matter with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions suggest that the isoscalar effective mass (IEM) of a nucleon at the saturation density equals $m^*/m\sim 0.8\pm 0.1$, at variance with empirical data on the nuclear level density in finite nuclei which are consistent with $m^*/m\approx 1$. This contradicting results might be reconciled by enriching the radial dependence of IEM. In this work four new terms are introduced into the Skyrme-force inspired local energy-density functional: $τ(\nablaρ)^2$, $τ\frac{dρ}{dr}$, $τ^2$ and $τΔρ$. The aim is to investigate how they influence the radial dependence of IEM and, in turn, the single-particle spectra.

# The nuclear energy density functionals with modified radial dependence of the isoscalar effective mass

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the contradiction between infinite nuclear matter calculations (isoscalar effective mass m*/m ~ 0.8 ± 0.1 at saturation density) and empirical finite-nucleus level density data (m*/m ≈ 1) can be reconciled by enriching the radial dependence of the isoscalar effective mass, specifically by introducing four new terms into the Skyrme energy-density functional. However, the study is exploratory and limited to three spherical nuclei (⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Ni, ¹⁰⁰Sn), with only one new term active at a time and only the coupling constant Cᵗ⁰ readjusted to enforce a volume-averaged IEM of unity.

## Method

Four new isoscalar terms were added to the standard Skyrme energy-density functional: τ(∇ρ)² (variant A), τ dρ/dr (variant B), τ² (variant C), and τΔρ (variant D). For each variant, the new coupling constant was varied manually over its stable range while the coupling constant Cᵗ⁰ was readjusted to satisfy the constraint that the density-weighted average of m*/m equals 1; all other coupling constants were kept at the SkXc parameterization values. The resulting radial profiles of the isoscalar effective mass and the single-particle spectra (spin-orbit splittings and centroids) were computed for ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Ni, and ¹⁰⁰Sn.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar effective mass from infinite nuclear matter calculations is m*/m ~ 0.8 ± 0.1 at saturation density, while empirical nuclear level density data are consistent with m*/m ≈ 1.
- For variant B (τ dρ/dr), the onset of a surface peak quenches the 1f spin-orbit splitting in ⁴⁰Ca, driving it closer to the experimental value; for variant A, the peak enhances this splitting, which is undesired because existing Skyrme parameterizations already overestimate it.
- Inclusion of terms A and B changes nuclear masses by no more than 10%; inclusion of term C shifts binding energies by more than 50% when going from zero to the limiting coupling constant values.
- Variant D yields only small fluctuations of the IEM value and does not produce a clear trend in single-particle spectra.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Ma and Wambach (1983), who explored a surface-peaked IEM in a non-self-consistent model, and by Farine et al. (2001), who worked within a self-consistent framework. The standard Skyrme functional cannot produce a surface-peaked IEM because the mass field depends only on ρ₀, which is monotonic. The paper builds on the SkXc parameterization of Brown (1998), chosen for its IEM close to unity and its attention to single-particle spectra. The results enable future refits of all coupling constants to masses and radii, and generalization of the promising τ dρ/dr term to non-spherical shapes.
