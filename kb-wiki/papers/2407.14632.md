# Restricted Boltzmann Machines Propagators for Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo

> The auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method uses imaginary-time projection techniques to accurately solve the ground-state wave function of atomic nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. In this work, we present a novel representation of the imaginary-time propagator based on restricted Boltzmann machines. We test its accuracy against the routinely employed Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations by evaluating ground-state energies and single-particle densities of selected light nuclei. This analysis paves the way for incorporating more realistic nuclear potentials in the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method, including isospin-dependent spin-orbit terms and cubic spin-isospin operators, which characterize accurate phenomenological and chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that restricted Boltzmann machine (RBM) representations of the imaginary-time propagator can replace the standard Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation in auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations, achieving ground-state energies within 2% of the highly accurate hyperspherical harmonics method for light nuclei, but the authors note that both the RBM and conventional HS results are obtained within the constrained-path approximation to control the fermion-sign problem, and unconstrained propagations would likely bring the AFDMC energies into better agreement with the HH benchmark.

## Method

The authors implemented an RBM-based imaginary-time propagator into the existing FORTRAN 2003 AFDMC production code, using the Argonne v6' (AV6P) nucleon-nucleon interaction which contains strong tensor components. They derived analytic expressions for the RBM parameters (network weights and biases) in terms of the two-body interaction strength, avoiding numerical optimization during the simulation. The RBM propagator treats each pair of nucleons with a single discrete auxiliary variable h in {0,1}, requiring 9 x A(A-1)/2 auxiliary fields for the spin-spin terms, compared to 3A Gaussian auxiliary fields in the conventional diagonalized HS approach. Ground-state energies and point-nucleon densities were computed for 2H, 3H, 3He, and 4He and benchmarked against both the conventional HS propagator and the hyperspherical harmonics method.

- 3H ground-state energy: RBM = -7.85(2) MeV, HS = -7.97(2) MeV, HH = -7.91(1) MeV (AV6P potential with Coulomb)
- 3He ground-state energy: RBM = -7.12(1) MeV, HS = -7.28(1) MeV, HH = -7.26(1) MeV
- 4He ground-state energy: RBM = -26.05(5) MeV, HS = -26.37(6) MeV, HH = -26.13(1) MeV
- 2H ground-state energy: RBM = -2.28(1) MeV, HS = -2.27(1) MeV, HH = -2.24(1) MeV

- The RBM propagator requires 9 x A(A-1)/2 discrete auxiliary fields for the spin-spin interaction terms, compared to 3A Gaussian auxiliary fields in the conventional HS approach

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Rrapaj (2020) which showed that RBM representations can generalize discrete auxiliary field transformations for simple spin/isospin operators, now applied to realistic nuclear Hamiltonians with strong tensor components. The current main limitation of AFDMC is its inability to encompass isospin-dependent spin-orbit terms (present in the Argonne v18 potential and chiral effective field theory potentials at N3LO) and cubic spin-isospin operators (present in three-nucleon forces), which the RBM propagator is designed to handle. This enables future incorporation of more realistic phenomenological and chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians into AFDMC calculations, potentially allowing high-precision studies of medium-mass nuclei and isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.
