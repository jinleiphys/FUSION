# The $K^-p\to Σ^0π^0$ reaction at low energies in a chiral quark model

> A chiral quark-model approach is extended to the study of the $\bar{K}N$ scattering at low energies. The process of $K^-p\to Σ^0π^0$ at $P_K\lesssim 800$ MeV/c (i.e. the center mass energy $W\lesssim 1.7$ GeV) is investigated. This approach is successful in describing the differential cross sections and total cross section with the roles of the low-lying $Λ$ resonances in $n=1$ shells clarified. The $Λ(1405)S_{01}$ dominates the reactions over the energy region considered here. Around $P_K\simeq 400$ MeV/c, the $Λ(1520)D_{03}$ is responsible for a strong resonant peak in the cross section. The $Λ(1670)S_{01}$ has obvious contributions around $P_K=750$ MeV/c, while the contribution of $Λ(1690)D_{03}$ is less important in this energy region. The non-resonant background contributions, i.e. $u$-channel and $t$-channel, also play important roles in the explanation of the angular distributions due to amplitude interferences.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model approach successfully describes the differential and total cross sections for $K^-p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0$ at low energies, but the analysis suggests that configuration mixings exist within the $\Lambda(1405)S_{01}$ and $\Lambda(1670)S_{01}$ states as admixtures of specific $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ configurations, with the mixing angle determined as a leading-order result rather than assuming pure quark-model assignments.

## Method

The authors extended a chiral quark-model approach, previously applied to meson photoproduction and $\pi N$ scattering, to $\bar{K}N$ scattering. They calculated $s$-channel, $u$-channel, and $t$-channel (including $K^*$ and $\kappa$ exchange) transition amplitudes at the quark level using an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling, then separated individual resonance contributions for $n=0$ and $n=1$ shell $\Lambda$ resonances. The differential cross section was computed by summing coherent amplitudes with resonance propagators including width effects, and the $g$-factors for spin-flavor transition strengths were extracted in the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry limit.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range studied: $P_K\lesssim 800$ MeV/c (center-of-mass energy $W\lesssim 1.7$ GeV)
- The $\Lambda(1520)D_{03}$ produces a strong resonant peak in the total cross section around $P_K\simeq 400$ MeV/c
- The $\Lambda(1670)S_{01}$ has obvious contributions around $P_K=750$ MeV/c
- In the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ limit without configuration mixing, the ratio $g_{S_{01}(1405)}/g_{S_{01}(1670)} = -3$ and $g_{D_{03}(1520)}/g_{D_{03}(1690)} = -3$
- The $g$-factor values extracted in the symmetric quark model include: $g_{s2}=2/3$, $g_{v2}=1$, $g_{S_{01}(1405)}=3/2$, $g_{S_{01}(1670)}=-1/2$, $g_{D_{03}(1520)}=3/2$, $g_{D_{03}(1690)}=-1/2$
- The $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange is more dominant than the $\kappa$-exchange; the $\kappa$ contributions are negligibly small

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to meson photoproduction (Li 1997, Zhao 2001) and $\pi N$ scattering (Zhong 2007) to the $\bar{K}N$ scattering channel $K^-p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0$, using recent higher-precision data from Manweiler (2008) at eight beam momenta between 514 and 750 MeV/c. The study contrasts with unitary chiral perturbation theory approaches (Kaiser 1996, Oset 1997, Oller 2000, Jido 2003) that treat $\Lambda(1405)$ as a dynamically generated resonance, instead providing a quark-model framework where $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Lambda(1670)$ are $L=1$ orbital excited $qqq$ states with configuration mixing. The results enable future tests of the predicted mixing angle and resonance transition strengths against new experimental data, and help clarify where the non-relativistic constituent quark model breaks down relative to multiquark or dynamically generated resonance scenarios.
