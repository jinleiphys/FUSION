# The $σK$ coupling in the chiral unitary approach and the isoscalar $\bar{K}N$, $\bar{K}A$ interaction

> We evaluate the "$σ$" exchange contribution to the $\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N$ scattering within a chiral unitary approach. We show that the chiral transition potentials for $ππ\to K \bar{K}$ in the $t$-channel lead to a "$σ$" contribution that vanishes in the $\bar{K}$ forward direction and, hence, would produce a null "$σ$" exchange contribution to the $K^-$ optical potential in nuclear matter in a simple impulse approximation. This is a consequence of the fact that the leading order chiral Lagrangian gives an I=0 $ππ\to K\bar{K}$ amplitude proportional to the squared momentum transfer, $q^2$. This finding poses questions on the meaning or the origin of "$σ$" exchange potentials used in relativistic mean field approaches to the $K^-$ nuclear selfenergy. This elementary "$σ$" exchange potential in $\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N$ is compared to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and is found to be smaller than present theoretical uncertainties but will be relevant in the future when aiming at fitting increasingly more accurate data.

# The σK coupling in the chiral unitary approach and the isoscalar \(\bar{K}N\), \(\bar{K}A\) interaction

## Key claim

The "σ" exchange contribution to \(\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N\) scattering, evaluated within a chiral unitary approach, vanishes exactly in the forward direction at the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold and remains small compared to the dominant Weinberg-Tomozawa term. This null result for the forward amplitude means that a simple impulse approximation would give no "σ" exchange contribution to the \(K^-\) optical potential in nuclear matter, raising questions about the microscopic meaning of σ-exchange potentials used in relativistic mean field models of the \(K^-\)-nucleus interaction.

## Method

The authors evaluate the correlated isoscalar two-pion exchange contribution to \(\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N\) scattering by computing triangle diagrams with intermediate nucleon or Δ states, where the \(\pi\pi\to K\bar{K}\) transition amplitude is taken from the chiral unitary approach. The on-shell \(\pi\pi\to K\bar{K}\) amplitude at leading chiral order is proportional to the squared momentum transfer \(q^2\), which vanishes for forward scattering. The resulting "σ" exchange amplitude is compared to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, and higher-order contributions from the \(\eta\eta\) channel and from next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangians are also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the "σ" exchange potential to the Weinberg-Tomozawa potential is zero at threshold and reaches about 8% at \(|\vec{k}| \sim 200\) MeV/c, and less than 12% at 400 MeV/c.
- The \(\eta\eta\) channel contribution to the forward \(K^-p\to K^-p\) amplitude is \(1.4 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-1}\), compared to the isoscalar \(t\)-matrix at threshold of about \(0.025\) MeV\(^{-1}\) and an effective \(t\)-matrix in medium of about \(-0.038\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- The next-to-leading order chiral contribution to the forward \(K^-p\to K^-p\) amplitude at \(q^2=0\) is approximately \(0.0014\) MeV\(^{-1}\), about a factor 30 smaller than the effective \(t\)-matrix in medium.
- The coupling ratio \(\alpha_\sigma = g_{\sigma K}/g_{\sigma N}\) extracted from the chiral unitary approach at the σ pole is in the range 0.260–0.307, using \(g_{\sigma N}=8.45\) from Holinde (standard value).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the NN interaction by Oset et al. (2000) and to hyperon-nucleon interactions by Sasaki et al., now applied to the \(\bar{K}N\) system. It contradicts the assumption in relativistic mean field models by Gal et al. (2006) and others that a σ-exchange potential contributes to the \(K^-\)-nucleus optical potential; the authors find that at the elementary level such a contribution vanishes for forward scattering. The result enables a clearer separation of the dominant Weinberg-Tomozawa term from subleading contributions, and suggests that future precision fits to \(\bar{K}N\) data may need to account for the small but non-zero correlated two-pion exchange at non-forward angles.
