# Thermal radiation and entanglement in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> The origin of the apparent thermalization in high-energy collisions is investigated using the data of the ATLAS and CMS Collaborations at the LHC. For this purpose, we analyze the transverse momentum distributions in the following proton-proton collision processes, all at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV: i) inclusive inelastic $pp$ collisions; ii) single- and double-diffractive Drell-Yan production $pp \to μ^+ μ^- X$; and iii) Higgs boson production. We confirm the relation between the effective temperature and the hard scattering scale observed at lower energies, and find that it extends even to the Higgs boson production process. In addition we find that the thermal component disappears in diffractive events (even though many charged hadrons are still produced). We discuss the implications of our study for the mechanism of multi-particle production -- in particular, we test the hypothesis about the link between quantum entanglement and thermalization in high-energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the relation between effective temperature and hard scattering scale observed at lower energies extends to LHC energies and even to Higgs boson production, but finds that the thermal component disappears in diffractive events, supporting the hypothesis that quantum entanglement is the origin of apparent thermalization in high-energy collisions rather than conventional final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum distributions from ATLAS and CMS data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV for three classes of proton-proton collisions: inclusive inelastic events, single- and double-diffractive Drell-Yan production pp to mu+ mu- X, and Higgs boson production. They fit the distributions with a superposition of an exponential (thermal) component and a power-law (hard scattering) component, extracting the effective temperature T_th, the hard scale parameter T, and the ratio R of the hard component integral to the total integral.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature for charged hadrons in inclusive pp collisions: T_th = 0.17 GeV, with hard scale parameter T = 0.72 GeV and n = 3.1
- Ratio R for inclusive pp collisions: R = 0.16 plus or minus 0.05, consistent with ISR measurements at sqrt(s) = 23, 31, 45, and 53 GeV
- Ratio R for diffractive Drell-Yan production: R approximately 1, indicating no thermal component
- Effective temperature for Higgs boson from H to gamma gamma decay: T_th approximately 3.5 GeV, with hard scale parameter T approximately 14.4 GeV
- Ratio R for Higgs boson from H to gamma gamma: R = 0.15 plus or minus 0.05
- Ratio R for Higgs boson from H to 4 leptons: R = 0.23 plus or minus 0.05

## Context

This work extends the relation between effective temperature and saturation momentum proposed by Kharzeev (2005, 2007, 2014) from RHIC energies to LHC energies, and tests the entanglement-thermalization link proposed by Kharzeev (2017). The disappearance of the thermal component in diffractive events confirms earlier observations by Bylinkin and Rostovtsev (2014) at HERA and OPAL at sqrt(s) = 15 and 35 GeV. The results enable further investigation of how entanglement entropy transforms into Boltzmann entropy following the rapid quench in high-energy collisions, a problem relevant to understanding thermalization in strongly coupled gauge theories and quantum decoherence in quantum computers.
