# Nuclear interactions and the space-like structure of the pion

> Three instances are discussed in which results produced by chiral perturbation theory can be reliably pushed to high space-like values of transferred momenta: 1. nuclear interactions, 2. nucleon sigma-term and 3. space-like structure of the pion

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory results for nuclear interactions, the nucleon sigma-term, and the pion scalar form factor can be reliably pushed to high space-like momenta (|t| up to ~40 M_pi^2) despite the breakdown of the expected chiral hierarchy, provided a phase-transition cutoff is introduced at a critical radius R ~ 0.6 fm where the quark condensate is fully depleted.

## Method

The author analyzes the two-pion exchange NN potential using chiral perturbation theory diagrams (families I, II, III) and compares the isospin-even central component V_C^+ (O(q^3)) with the isospin-odd V_C^- (O(q^2)). For the nucleon sigma-term and pion scalar form factor, perturbative results in momentum space are Fourier-transformed to configuration space, where a cutoff radius R is imposed at the point where the scalar density reaches the condensate value (cos theta = 0), representing a phase transition. The pion case is extended by including a scalar resonance of mass 980 MeV with known coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- |V_C^+| ~ 10 x |V_C^-| in regions of physical interest, despite V_C^+ being formally O(q^3) and V_C^- being O(q^2)
- Nucleon sigma-term: 43 MeV < sigma_N < 49 MeV, compared to empirical value 45 +/- 8 MeV
- Pion scalar mean square radius (pion loops only): <r^2>_S^pi = 0.50 fm^2, with cutoff R = 0.500 fm (using M_pi = 139.57 MeV, F_pi = 92.4 MeV)
- Pion scalar mean square radius (with scalar resonance): <r^2>_S^pi = 0.59 fm^2, compared to precise estimate 0.61 +/- 0.04 fm^2 from Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (2001)
- LEC bar_l_4 (pion loops only): 3.9; with scalar resonance: 4.3, compared to precise estimate 4.4 +/- 0.2 from Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (2001)
- Critical radius for phase transition: R ~ 0.6 fm (|t| ~ 40 M_pi^2) for both nucleon and pion cases

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework for nuclear forces developed by Epelbaum and others, and the nucleon sigma-term calculations by Gasser, Leutwyler, and collaborators. The author's earlier work (Robilotta, 2001) established the connection between V_C^+ and the scalar form factor. The pion structure results are compared directly with the precise two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations of Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler (2001). The approach enables a unified description of nuclear forces, the nucleon sigma-term, and pion structure using a phase-transition cutoff that extends the range of applicability of chiral perturbation theory to higher momentum transfers.
