# Pair Fluctuations in Ultra-small Fermi Systems within Self-Consistent RPA at Finite Temperature

> A self-consistent version of the Thermal Random Phase Approximation (TSCRPA) is developed within the Matsubara Green's Function (GF) formalism. The TSCRPA is applied to the many level pairing model. The normal phase of the system is considered. The TSCRPA results are compared with the exact ones calculated for the Grand Canonical Ensemble. Advantages of the TSCRPA over the Thermal Mean Field Approximation (TMFA) and the standard Thermal Random Phase Approximation (TRPA) are demonstrated. Results for correlation functions, excitation energies, single particle level densities, etc., as a function of temperature are presented.

## Key claim

The Self-Consistent Thermal Random Phase Approximation (TSCRPA) accurately describes pair fluctuations in ultra-small Fermi systems above the critical temperature, and can be continued into the superfluid regime without encountering the collapse of excitation energies that plagues standard Thermal RPA, though the formalism is formally limited to the normal phase.

## Method

The authors develop a self-consistent version of the Thermal Random Phase Approximation (TSCRPA) within the Matsubara Green's Function formalism, applying it to the many-level pairing model (Picket Fence Model) with equidistant, twofold degenerate levels. The method solves coupled equations for the two-body Green's function and the single-particle occupation numbers, using a mass operator derived from the SCRPA T-matrix, and compares results with exact grand canonical ensemble calculations, Thermal Mean Field Approximation (TMFA), and standard Thermal RPA (TRPA).

## Key numbers

- For a system with \(\Omega = N = 10\) levels and particles, the TSCRPA intrinsic energy practically coincides with exact results.
- The critical interaction constant for standard RPA collapse at zero temperature is \(G_{cr} \simeq 0.33\); at temperature \(T = 0.5\), this collapse occurs at \(G_{cr} \simeq 0.43\).
- For interaction constant \(G = 0.4\), the BCS critical temperature is \(T_{cr}^{BCS} = 0.38\), where the TMFA and TRPA show discontinuities in heat capacity, while TSCRPA shows only a kink.
- The effective gap \(\Delta\) calculated with SCRPA shows no phase transition at the point where the BCS gap vanishes, with results deteriorating for values of \(G\) well beyond the critical value.
- For a system with 50 fermions and interaction constants \(G = 0.127\) and \(G = 0.255\), corresponding to \(\delta / \Delta = 50\) and \(\delta / \Delta = 1\) respectively, the specific heat approaches linear dependence with temperature when \(\delta / \Delta \to \infty\) and shows a bump structure when \(\delta \sim \Delta\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) developed by Dukelsky and Schuck (2001) to finite temperature using the Matsubara Green's function formalism, and is applied to the Picket Fence Model whose exact solution was found by Richardson and Sherman. The approach improves upon the Static Path Approximation (SPA) used by Muhlschlegel, Scalapino, and Denton (1972) and later by Falci and collaborators, and upon standard TRPA by avoiding the collapse of excitation energies at the critical point. The results enable accurate treatment of strong pairing fluctuations in ultrasmall metallic grains where \(\delta / \Delta \geq 1\), a regime where BCS mean-field theory breaks down, and show a pseudo-gap in the density of states above \(T_c\) similar to that observed in high-\(T_c\) superconductors.
