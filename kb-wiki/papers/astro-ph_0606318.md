# Systematic quantum corrections to screening in thermonuclear fusion

> We develop a series expansion of the plasma screening length away from the classical limit in powers of $\hbar^{2}$. It is shown that the leading order quantum correction increases the screening length in solar conditions by approximately 2% while it decreases the fusion rate by approximately $ 0.34%$. We also calculate the next higher order quantum correction which turns out to be approximately 0.05%.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic series expansion of the plasma screening length in powers of \(\hbar^{2}\) and finds that the leading quantum correction increases the screening length by approximately 2% under solar conditions, which in turn decreases the thermonuclear fusion rate by approximately 0.34%; the next higher order correction to the rate is approximately 0.05%.

## Method

The authors start from the classical Poisson-Boltzmann equation for a single species of ions and electrons and linearize it to obtain the classical screening length. They then introduce a quantum treatment for electrons using second-quantized Grassmann variables, integrate out the electronic degrees of freedom in the partition function, and expand the resulting functional determinant in powers of a dimensionless quantum parameter \(\Delta_Q = \hbar^2 \Lambda^{-2} / (2 m k_B T)\). The effective energy density is expanded to quadratic order in the electrostatic potential around a chosen background value \(\phi_0 = \sqrt{Z}\), and the corrected screening length is extracted from the resulting equation of motion.

## Key numbers

- Under solar conditions (density \(100\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\), temperature \(T = 15 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{K}\), \(Z = 2\)), the classical screening length is \(\Lambda_0 \approx 0.281\ \mathrm{\AA}\).
- The dimensionless quantum parameter \(\Delta_Q\) is approximately \(0.032\) for these conditions.
- The leading quantum correction increases the screening length by approximately \(2\%\).
- The classical Salpeter rate enhancement factor of \(1.17\) is reduced to \(1.16\) by quantum corrections, corresponding to a decrease in the fusion rate of approximately \(0.34\%\).
- The next higher order quantum correction to the fusion rate is approximately \(0.05\%\).

## Context

This work extends the seminal treatment of screening in thermonuclear fusion by Salpeter (1954) and the more recent quantum calculations of Gruzinov and Bahcall (1998), who evaluated the electronic density matrix using Feynman’s imaginary-time Schroedinger equation but could not systematically estimate higher-order quantum corrections. The paper provides a systematic expansion in \(\hbar^2\) that yields the leading and next-to-leading quantum corrections, and it complements the results of Brown et al. (2006) showing that classical non-linear effects on screening are small. The improved precision may be useful for interpreting solar neutrino flux measurements, where experimental uncertainties are approximately \(1\%\).
