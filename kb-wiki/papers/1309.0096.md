# Quark deconfinement transition in neutron stars with the field correlator method

> A phase of strong interacting matter with deconfined quarks is expected in the core of massive neutron stars. In this article, we perform a study of the hadron-quark phase transition in cold (T = 0) neutron star matter and we calculate various structural properties of hybrid stars. For the quark phase, we make use of an equation of state (EOS) derived with the field correlator method (FCM) recently extended to the case of nonzero baryon density. For the hadronic phase, we consider both pure nucleonic and hyperonic matter, and we derive the corresponding EOS within a relativistic mean field approach. We make use of measured neutron star masses, and particularly the mass $M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 \, M_\odot$ of PSR J1614 -2230 to constrain the values of the gluon condensate $G_2$, which is one of the EOS parameters within the FCM. We find that the values of $G_2$ extracted from the mass measurement of PSR J1614 -2230 are consistent with the values of the same quantity derived within the FCM from recent lattice QCD calculations of the deconfinement transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential. The FCM thus provides a powerful tool to link numerical calculations of QCD on a space-time lattice with measured neutron star masses.

## Key claim

The values of the gluon condensate \(G_2\) extracted from the mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 (\(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 \, M_\odot\)) are consistent with the values derived within the field correlator method from lattice QCD calculations of the deconfinement transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential, but only for a specific range of \(G_2\) values that allow the pulsar to be a hybrid star.

## Method

The authors used the field correlator method (FCM) to derive the equation of state (EOS) for the quark phase, with the gluon condensate \(G_2\) and the large-distance static \(Q\bar{Q}\) potential \(V_1\) as parameters. For the hadronic phase, they employed a relativistic mean field model (GM1 parametrization) for both pure nucleonic matter and hyperonic matter (with hyperon-nucleon coupling \(x_\sigma\) in the range 0.6–0.8). The hadron-quark phase transition was modeled using both the Gibbs construction (global charge neutrality) and the Maxwell construction (separate charge neutrality), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to compute hybrid star structures.

## Key numbers

- The mass of PSR J1614-2230 is \(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 \, M_\odot\), used to constrain the gluon condensate.
- For pure nucleonic matter, hybrid star maximum masses range from \(M_{max} = 1.44 \, M_\odot\) (for \(G_2 = 0.006 \, \text{GeV}^4\)) to \(M_{max} = 2.05 \, M_\odot\) (for \(G_2 = 0.016 \, \text{GeV}^4\)).
- Using the lower bound of the PSR J1614-2230 mass, the constraint is \(G_2 \gtrsim 0.0129 \, \text{GeV}^4\); the upper bound defines \(G_2^* \simeq 0.018 \, \text{GeV}^4\).
- The gluon condensate from QCD sum rules is \(G_2 = (0.012 \pm 0.006) \, \text{GeV}^4\).
- The vacuum correlation length used is \(\lambda = 0.34 \, \text{fm}\), from lattice QCD.
- Current-quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 5 \, \text{MeV}\), \(m_s = 150 \, \text{MeV}\).
- For hyperonic matter with \(x_\sigma = 0.6\), the phase transition point shifts to higher pressures compared to pure nucleonic matter.

## Context

This work extends the study of Baldo (2013) by using a relativistic mean field model (GM1) for the hadronic phase instead of a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, and it finds a wider stability window for hybrid stars (including cases with hyperons) than that earlier work. The FCM approach enables a direct link between lattice QCD calculations of the deconfinement temperature (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2012) and neutron star mass measurements, allowing the gluon condensate to be constrained astrophysically. This enables future tests of QCD at high baryon density using neutron star observations.
