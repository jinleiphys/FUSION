# Conformal Symmetry and Duality between Free Particle, H-atom and Harmonic Oscillator

> We establish a duality between the free massless relativistic particle in d dimensions, the non-relativistic hydrogen atom (1/r potential) in (d-1) space dimensions, and the harmonic oscillator in (d-2) space dimensions with its mass given as the lightcone momentum of an additional dimension. The duality is in the sense that the classical action of these systems are gauge fixed forms of the same worldline gauge theory action at the classical level, and they are all described by the same unitary representation of the conformal group SO(d,2) at the quantum level. The worldline action has a gauge symmetry Sp(2) which treats canonical variables (x,p) as doublets and exists only with a target spacetime that has d spacelike dimensions and two timelike dimensions. This spacetime is constrained due to the gauge symmetry, and the various dual solutions correspond to solutions of the constraints with different topologies. For example, for the H-atom the two timelike dimensions X^{0'},X^{0} live on a circle. The model provides an example of how realistic physics can be viewed as existing in a larger covariant space that includes two timelike coordinates, and how the covariance in the larger space unifies different looking physics into a single system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the free massless relativistic particle, the non-relativistic hydrogen atom, and the harmonic oscillator are dual to each other, meaning they are gauge-fixed forms of the same worldline gauge theory action at the classical level and described by the same unitary representation of the conformal group SO(d,2) at the quantum level. The hedge is that this duality holds only when the target spacetime has d spacelike dimensions and two timelike dimensions, and the different physical systems correspond to different solutions of the constraints with different topologies.

## Method

The author constructs a worldline action with Sp(2) gauge symmetry that treats position and momentum as a doublet in a target spacetime with signature (d,2), including two timelike dimensions. By choosing different gauge fixings for the "time" coordinate (which is not unique due to the two timelike dimensions), the action reduces to the first-order action for the free massless relativistic particle, the hydrogen atom, or the harmonic oscillator. The quantum theory is analyzed by constructing the SO(d,2) generators in each gauge and requiring that physical states are Sp(2) singlets, which fixes the quadratic Casimir of SO(d,2) to C2 = 1 - d²/4 and determines operator ordering ambiguities.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic Casimir of SO(d,2) for physical states is C2 = 1 - d²/4.
- For the hydrogen atom in (d-1) space dimensions, the energy spectrum is En = -Mα²/2 × (½(d-4) + n)⁻², where n = 1,2,3,... and l = 0,1,...,(n-1).
- The degeneracy at fixed energy level n in (d-1) space dimensions is Dn(d-1) = (n+d-3)!/((d-2)! n!) × (2n+d-2).
- The ordering parameters a and b in the hydrogen atom gauge are fixed to a = (5-2d)/8 and b = -1/8.
- For the SO(d-1) rotation subgroup, the quadratic Casimir eigenvalue is l(l+d-3) for l = 0,1,2,...

## Context

This work extends the duality construction of Bars (1998) that showed the free relativistic particle is described by a specific unitary representation of SO(d,2), now showing the hydrogen atom and harmonic oscillator are also described by the same representation. It generalizes the well-known SO(4) symmetry of the three-dimensional hydrogen atom (Barut) to any dimension, where the hydrogen atom in (d-1) dimensions has an SO(d) symmetry. The paper enables a unified treatment of these seemingly different systems through a common underlying gauge theory with two timelike dimensions, and suggests that realistic physics can be viewed as existing in a larger covariant space.
