# Nuclear effects on longitudinal-transverse structure function ratio in the deuteron

> Nuclear modifications of the nucleon's structure function $F_2^N$ have been investigated mainly since the discovery of the EMC nuclear effect in 1983, and there were many experimental measurements from the deuteron to a heavy nucleus. Now, the details of the modifications of $F_2^N$ are known from small $x$ to large $x$. On the other hand, it is taken as granted that a nuclear modification does not exist for the longitudinal-transverse structure function ratio $R_N=F_L^N/(2xF_1^N)$. However, such a nuclear modification does exist theoretically. A nucleon in a nucleus moves in any space direction, which is not necessary the longitudinal direction along the virtual-photon momentum in charged-lepton scattering. Because of this transverse Fermi motion, the longitudinal and transverse structure functions mix with the mixture probability proportional to the nucleon's transverse-momentum squared $\vec p_T^{\,\, 2}/Q^2$. In this paper, the nuclear modifications are shown numerically for the deuteron by using a standard convolution description. The magnitude of the modifications is of the order of a few percent in the deuteron; however, they should be large in large nuclei. In handling high-energy nuclear data, such nuclear modifications need to be taken into account for a precise determination of physical quantities. Now, the longitudinal-transverse structure-function ratio and tensor-polarized experiments are under preparation for the deuteron at JLab. We hope that such effects will be confirmed experimentally for not only for the deuteron but also for larger nuclei.

## Key claim
Nuclear modifications of the longitudinal-transverse structure function ratio \(R_N = F_L^N/(2xF_1^N)\) do exist theoretically in the deuteron, contrary to the common assumption that they are negligible; the modifications arise from transverse Fermi motion of nucleons in the nucleus and from differences in the x-dependent functional forms of the longitudinal and transverse structure functions after convolution.

## Method
The author used a standard convolution formalism with a spectral function based on the deuteron momentum-space wave function (Bonn potential) and the MSTW08 unpolarized parton distribution functions at leading order in QCD, together with the SLAC 1990 parametrization for \(R_N\). The nuclear structure functions \(F_1^A\), \(F_2^A\), and \(F_L^A\) were computed from the convolution integrals in Eq. (12L-convolution), which include mixing terms between longitudinal and transverse components proportional to \(\vec p_{N\perp}^2 / Q^2\). The nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) were then evaluated at \(Q^2 = 1\), 5, 10, and 100 GeV\(^2\) for the deuteron.

## Key numbers
- At \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the nuclear modifications of \(F_1^N\), \(F_2^N\), and \(F_L^N\) for the deuteron show negative modifications at medium \(x \sim 0.4\) (from nuclear binding) and steep positive rises at large \(x \sim 0.7\) (from Fermi motion).
- The nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) for the deuteron are of the order of a few percent, with the magnitude depending strongly on \(x\) and decreasing as \(Q^2\) increases from 1 to 100 GeV\(^2\).
- At \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\), the modifications are not small even without the longitudinal-transverse admixture (dashed curves), because the x-dependent functional forms of the longitudinal and transverse structure functions differ.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical proposal of Ericson (2003) that nuclear modifications of \(R_N\) should exist, despite negative experimental results from HERMES (2003), CCFR/NuTeV (2001), and JLab (2007). The numerical demonstration for the deuteron uses the same convolution approach as Hirai (2010) and Cosyn (2017) for nuclear structure functions. The results enable precise handling of nuclear data in ongoing and planned JLab experiments on the longitudinal-transverse ratio and tensor-polarized deuteron measurements, and motivate experimental confirmation for heavier nuclei.
