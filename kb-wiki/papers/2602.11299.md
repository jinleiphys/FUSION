# Generalized gauge-space rotations in atomic nuclei: A critical insight

> We critically reexamine the concepts of pairing rotations and moments of inertia in gauge space extracted from experimental binding energies. Our analysis focuses on pairing correlations among like nucleons, neutron-proton pairing, and $α$-type correlations. By investigating $α$ separation energies and binding-energy differences along chains of fixed isospin projection and subtracting macroscopic contributions, we reveal a remarkably smooth and nearly universal behavior in the residual $α$ correlation energy. These results exhibit the parabolic trends characteristic of collective rotations in gauge space. We demonstrate that the standard definition of the gauge-space moment of inertia for like-nucleon pairing is dominated by macroscopic contributions from Coulomb and symmetry energies. Once these are removed, the remaining moment of inertia becomes negative. This suggests that the observed behavior reflects the loss of correlation energy due to Pauli-blocking effect. Our results indicate that $α$ correlations constitute a genuine collective mode associated with quartetting dynamics arising from the coherent coupling of two superfluid components.

## Key claim

The standard definition of the gauge-space moment of inertia for like-nucleon pairing, extracted from experimental binding energies, is dominated by macroscopic contributions from Coulomb and symmetry energies; once these are removed, the remaining moment of inertia becomes negative, suggesting that the observed behavior reflects the loss of correlation energy due to Pauli-blocking effects rather than genuine pairing collectivity.

## Method

The authors extracted α-correlation energies from experimental binding energies using the definition Eα(N,Z) = B(N,Z) − B(N−2,Z−2) − Bα, then subtracted macroscopic liquid-drop contributions including Coulomb and symmetry energies to isolate residual nuclear effects. They analyzed the quadratic dependence of binding energies along isotopic and isotonic chains (Sn, Pb, N=82) and along α-decay chains of fixed isospin projection, fitting the residual energies to parabolic forms to extract moments of inertia in gauge space. The analysis was complemented by comparing with exact and BCS solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian in single-j and multi-j shell models.

## Key numbers

- Eα values extracted from experimental binding energies for nuclei above 100Sn lie in the range of approximately −4 to −10 MeV (the gray-shaded region where α decay is energetically favored).
- For the Sn isotopic chain between N=50 and 82, a nearly symmetric quadratic behavior is observed when A0=116 (N0=66), corresponding to the exact mid-shell.
- For a single-j shell with degeneracy 2Ω, the BCS ground-state energy is E(N) = εN − (G/4)N(2Ω − N), yielding a constant pairing moment of inertia J_single = 2/G.
- The neutron-proton moment of inertia extracted from double binding-energy differences is approximately 1/J_np ≈ 2a_sym/A, dominated by the symmetry energy contribution.

## Context

This work critically reexamines the concepts of pairing rotations and moments of inertia in gauge space originally developed by Bes (1966), Brink (2005), and Broglia (2000), and recently revisited by Papenbrock (effective field theory), Hinohara (2016, moment-of-inertia tensor), and Potel (2011, 2013). The authors extend their previous work on α decay as a collective phenomenon (Qi, 2021) and demonstrate that α correlations constitute a genuine collective mode associated with quartetting dynamics, distinct from conventional like-nucleon or neutron-proton pairing. The analysis shows that the smooth quadratic behavior of α-correlation energies along fixed-isospin chains provides a unified framework free from macroscopic symmetry-energy contributions, enabling simultaneous study of like-particle pairing, neutron-proton correlations, and quartetting.
