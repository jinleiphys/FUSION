# Relativistic effects on the back-to-back correlation functions of boson-antiboson pairs in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the back-to-back correlation (BBC) functions of relativistic boson-antiboson pairs in high energy heavy ion collisions using the Monte Carlo method. The relativistic effects on the BBC functions of $Ï•\,Ï•$ and $K^+K^-$ pairs are investigated. The investigations indicate that the relativistic effects on the BBC functions of $K^+K^-$ pairs with large momenta are significant, and the effect is sensitive to the particle freeze-out temperature.

## Key claim

The relativistic effect on the back-to-back correlation (BBC) functions of boson-antiboson pairs is significant for \(K^+K^-\) pairs with large momenta and is sensitive to the particle freeze-out temperature; the effect can decrease the BBC peak by up to 30% for \(\phi\phi\) pairs at \(k=0.8\) GeV/\(c\) and by up to 45% for \(K^+K^-\) pairs at the same momentum, depending on source velocity and temperature.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo method to compute the BBC functions for \(\phi\phi\) and \(K^+K^-\) pairs in a local-equilibrium expanding source, employing a Bogoliubov transformation to relate freeze-out bosons to quasiparticles with a shifted mass \(m_*\) in the medium. They modeled the source with a Gaussian spatial distribution of radius \(R = 7\) fm, an exponential emission time distribution with \(\Delta t = 2\) fm/\(c\), and a velocity field \(\mathbf{v}(x) = \langle u \rangle \mathbf{r}/(2R)\). The BBC function \(C(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k})\) was evaluated by numerically integrating the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes over the source, comparing relativistic and non-relativistic (low-velocity expansion) treatments.

## Key numbers

- For \(\phi\phi\) pairs at freeze-out temperature \(T = 140\) MeV and zero source velocity (\(\langle u \rangle = 0\)), the relativistic effect decreases the BBC peak by 30% at pair momentum \(k = 0.8\) GeV/\(c\).
- For \(K^+K^-\) pairs at \(T = 170\) MeV and \(\langle u \rangle = 0\), the relative difference \(D_{\rm rel}\) between relativistic and non-relativistic BBC functions reaches 45% at \(k = 0.8\) GeV/\(c\).
- The source radius \(R\) was taken as 7 fm and the emission time parameter \(\Delta t\) as 2 fm/\(c\).
- The BBC functions were computed for \(\phi\phi\) at \(k = 500\) and 1000 MeV/\(c\) and for \(K^+K^-\) at \(k = 300\) and 800 MeV/\(c\), with freeze-out temperatures of 140, 160, and 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic BBC studies of Padula et al. (2006, 2010) for \(\phi\phi\) and \(K^+K^-\) pairs in local-equilibrium expanding sources by incorporating relativistic kinematics. The results show that relativistic corrections are non-negligible for high-momentum pairs and lower freeze-out temperatures, and they enable more accurate modeling of BBC signals in heavy ion collisions. The authors suggest further investigations using hydrodynamical sources, referencing Efa and Zhang (2005, 2012).
