# Effective theory for the Goldstone field in the BCS-BEC crossover at T=0

> We perform a detailed study of the effective Lagrangian for the Goldstone mode of a superfluid Fermi gas at zero temperature in the whole BCS-BEC crossover. By using a derivative expansion of the response functions, we derive the most general form of this Lagrangian at the next to leading order in the momentum expansion in terms of four coefficient functions. This involves the elimination of all the higher order time derivatives by careful use of the leading order field equations. In the infinite scattering length limit where conformal invariance is realized, we show that the effective Lagrangian must contain an unnoticed invariant combination of higher spatial gradients of the Goldstone mode, while explicit couplings to spatial gradients of the trapping potential are absent. Across the whole crossover, we determine all the coefficient functions at the one-loop level, taking into account the dependence of the gap parameter on the chemical potential in the mean-field approximation. These results are analytically expressed in terms of elliptic integrals of the first and second kind. We discuss the form of these coefficients in the extreme BCS and BEC regimes and around the unitary limit, and compare with recent work by other authors.

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian for the Goldstone mode in the BCS-BEC crossover at zero temperature, at next-to-leading order in the momentum expansion, necessarily contains a term proportional to \((\partial_i \partial_j \theta)^2\) that had not been noticed before, while explicit couplings to spatial gradients of the trapping potential are absent; these features are a necessary consequence of conformal invariance at unitarity.

## Method

The authors perform a derivative expansion of the retarded response functions (density-density, current-current, and pairing-field response) at zero temperature, deriving a linearized equation of motion for the Goldstone field \(\theta\). They construct the most general next-to-leading-order effective Lagrangian from Galilean-invariant scalars of order \(p^2\), then eliminate higher-order time derivatives by using the leading-order field equations and integration by parts, preserving the perturbative content. The coefficient functions are computed at one-loop level in the mean-field approximation, with the gap parameter depending on the chemical potential, and expressed analytically in terms of elliptic integrals of the first and second kind.

## Key numbers

* The static transverse current response \(\chi_\text{T}(0)\) vanishes, showing that the entire system is superfluid at \(T=0\): \(\langle n \rangle = n_s\).
* The ratio of the two spatial-gradient coefficients is \(g_3(X)/g_2(X) = 2\), giving \(g_2(X) = b_5/6\) and \(g_3(X) = b_5/3\).
* The leading-order coefficient \(b_2 = -\langle n \rangle/m\), where \(\langle n \rangle\) is the total particle density.
* The static density-density response at zero momentum is \(\chi_{nn}(0) = -\int d^3k/(2\pi)^3 \, \Delta_0^2 / E_k^3\).
* The static density-pairing response is \(\chi_{n1}(0) = -\int d^3k/(2\pi)^3 \, \Delta_0 \xi_k / E_k^3\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic effective-field-theory approach of Son and Wingate (2006) for the unitary limit to the entire BCS-BEC crossover at zero temperature, providing the first complete one-loop determination of all next-to-leading-order coefficient functions in terms of elliptic integrals. It builds on the leading-order effective Lagrangian derived by Greiter et al. (1989) and Aitchison et al. (1995), and on the mean-field theory of Leggett (1980) and its extensions by Nozières and Schmitt-Rink (1985) and Sá de Melo, Randeria, and Engelbrecht (1993). The results enable systematic low-energy studies of superfluid Fermi gases across the crossover, including the unitary limit where the authors compare their energy density functional with the epsilon-expansion results of Rupak and Schäfer (2007), finding discrepancies of about 30% but good agreement for the quantum-pressure coefficient.
