# Contact interaction study of proton parton distributions

> Using a symmetry-preserving formulation of a vector$\,\times\,$vector contact interaction (SCI) and treating the proton as a quark + interacting-diquark bound state, whose structure is obtained by solving a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation, we provide a comprehensive, coherent set of predictions for unpolarised and polarised proton parton distribution functions (DFs): valence, glue, and four-flavour separated sea. The results enable many themes to be addressed, including: the asymmetry of antimatter in the proton; the neutron:proton structure function ratio; helicity retention in hard scattering processes; the charm quark momentum fraction; the sign and size of the polarised gluon DF; and the origin of the proton spin. In all cases where sound analyses of data are available, SCI predictions are semiquantitatively in agreement with the results. Those mismatches which exist are typically attributable to the momentum-independence of the underlying interaction. Judiciously interpreted, the SCI delivers a sound and insightful explanation of proton structure as expressed in DFs.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving formulation of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) and treating the proton as a quark + interacting-diquark bound state, the authors provide predictions for unpolarised and polarised proton parton distribution functions that are semiquantitatively in agreement with data, with mismatches typically attributable to the momentum-independence of the underlying interaction.

## Method

The proton is treated as a quark + interacting-diquark bound state whose structure is obtained by solving a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation. Hadron-scale valence parton distribution functions are computed algebraically using the SCI framework, then evolved to experimental scales using the all-orders (AO) evolution scheme. The calculations include contributions from scalar and axialvector diquark correlations, with helicity-dependent distributions additionally incorporating scalar-axialvector transition terms.

## Key numbers

- Proton axial charge: g_A = 0.92 (SCI) compared to g_A = 1.25 from QCD-kindred predictions (Cheng 2023)
- Hadron-scale u-quark momentum fraction: ⟨x⟩_{u_p}^{ζ_H} = 0.686 (SCI) compared to 0.687 from QCD-kindred predictions (Chang 2022)
- Hadron-scale d-quark momentum fraction: ⟨x⟩_{d_p}^{ζ_H} = 0.314 (SCI) compared to 0.313 from QCD-kindred predictions (Chang 2022)
- x=1 ratio d/u = 0.36 (SCI) compared to 0.23(6) from realistic predictions (Chang 2022, Cheng 2023)
- x=1 ratio F_2^n/F_2^p = 0.56 (SCI) compared to 0.45(5) from realistic predictions (Chang 2022, Cheng 2023)
- x=1 ratio Δd/Δu = -0.12 (SCI) compared to -0.14(3) from realistic predictions (Chang 2022, Cheng 2023)
- Hadron-scale polarised:unpolarised ratio at x≈0: Δu/u = 0.34, Δd/d = -0.31

## Context

This work extends the SCI approach previously applied to meson distribution amplitudes and nucleon elastic form factors (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2010, Roberts 2011, Wilson 2011) to the full set of proton parton distribution functions. It complements QCD-kindred predictions from continuum Schwinger function methods (Lu 2022, Cheng 2023) by providing algebraic transparency that enables robust insights and algorithm validation. The results enable comparisons with data from MARATHON (Abrams 2021), HERMES (2004), COMPASS (2010), and CLAS experiments, and address open questions about the proton spin structure and antimatter asymmetry.
