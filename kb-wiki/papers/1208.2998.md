# Higher Twist in Electroproduction: Flavor Non-Singlet QCD Evolution

> We present results for the one loop anomalous dimension matrix of flavor non-singlet twist-4 operators of lowest spin that contribute to the leading moment of the $F_2$ structure function in deep inelastic electron-nucleon scattering. We analyze the flavor structure of the anomalous dimension matrix and decompose the leading moment of $F_2$ into separate flavor channels. In addition to building on previous work with higher-twist operators, these results can provide a benchmark for future work that generalizes to include the higher moments as well. We include non-perturbative input from the lattice and phenomenological estimates of the twist-4 matrix elements and estimate the twist-4 contributions to the leading moment of $F_2$. The results suggest that the overall twist-4 contribution may be suppressed due to either cancellations among the twist-4 terms or inherently small twist-4 matrix elements.

## Key claim

The one-loop anomalous dimension matrix for flavor non-singlet twist-4 operators of lowest spin is computed, and the results suggest that the overall twist-4 contribution to the leading moment of the $F_2$ structure function may be suppressed due to either cancellations among the twist-4 terms or inherently small twist-4 matrix elements, consistent with the CLAS analysis.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop anomalous dimension matrix for flavor non-singlet twist-4 operators of lowest spin (n=2) that contribute to the leading moment of the $F_2$ structure function in deep inelastic electron-nucleon scattering, including all mixing effects among four-quark (4Q) and two-quark (2Q) operators. They classified the operator basis into 4Q, 2Q, and gluonic types, computed the relevant Feynman diagrams (self-renormalization and mixing graphs), and combined the perturbative results with non-perturbative input from lattice calculations and phenomenological estimates of twist-4 matrix elements to estimate the twist-4 contributions to the leading moment of $F_2$.

## Key numbers

- The higher twist contributions to the lowest moment of $F_2^p$ were less than about 5% of the leading twist moment for $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$ (from the CLAS analysis cited in the paper).
- The analysis used an input scale $\mu$ with $\mu^2 \sim Q^2$ and a typical hadronic scale $\Lambda \sim 1$ GeV for scaling the non-perturbative matrix elements.
- The CLAS analysis found that the moments of the $F_2^p(x,Q^2)$ structure function were dominated by the leading twist terms down to $Q^2 \sim 1$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the one-loop anomalous dimension matrix for twist-4 operators by Gottlieb (1978), Okawa (1980, 1981), where parts of the matrix were computed but the annihilation graphs (Fig. 4qgraphs B) were not included and results for other graphs differed. The results provide a benchmark for future work generalizing to higher moments and can be used to interpret data from the CLAS collaboration (Osipenko 2003) and future parity-violating deep inelastic scattering experiments at JLAB (Souder 2008, Subedi 2009). The calculations also enable more accurate extractions of higher twist matrix elements from DIS data and can serve as input for lattice QCD computations.
