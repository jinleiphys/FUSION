# A Mixed Phase Model and the 'Softest Point' Effect

> Particularities of the statistical mixed phase model for describing the deconfinement phase transition and physics constraints on the model parameters, are discussed. The modifications proposed concern an improved treatment of hadron-hadron interactions within the nonlinear mean-field model of nuclear matter and an inclusion of the one-gluon exchange correction into the quark-gluon sector of the mixed phase thermodynamic potential. It is shown that the mixed phase model successfully reproduces both the available lattice data on the QCD deconfinement transition and thermodynamic characteristics of hadron systems evaluated within the modern models. The 'softest point' of the equation of state resulting in a possible formation of a long-lived fireball in nuclear collisions (the 'softest point' effect) is analyzed. The obtained results are confronted with the data on the two-phase model. Some manifestation of the mixed phase formation and deconfinement transition is estimated in fireball expansion dynamics. We discuss experimental possibilities of observing the softest point effect as a signal of the deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a statistical mixed phase model, where unbound quarks and gluons coexist homogeneously with hadrons without spatial separation, can reproduce the continuous crossover transition seen in lattice QCD at zero baryon density and the variety of deconfinement scenarios, provided the mean field acting on color charges depends on the total density of quarks and gluons (both bound and unbound) rather than only on the plasma density.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian for a mixed phase where hadrons and unbound quarks/gluons coexist, using a semi-relativistic mean-field approach with a confinement-motivated term \(A/\rho^{\gamma}\) in the single-particle spectra. The mean fields are constrained by thermodynamic consistency conditions derived from the requirement that the Hamiltonian does not depend explicitly on temperature or densities. The hadron-hadron interactions are treated within a nonlinear mean-field model of nuclear matter, and the one-gluon exchange correction is included in the quark-gluon sector.

## Key numbers

* The (pseudo)critical deconfinement temperature for the \(SU(3)\) system with two light flavors at zero baryon density is obtained as \(T_{dec} = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with the lattice prediction interval \(T_{dec}=143\div154\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The 'softest point' energy density \(\varepsilon_{SP}\) from earlier two-phase model estimates varies between \(0.7\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\) and \(1.5\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\) depending on the smoothing procedure.
* For the pure gluonic case, the latent heat of the phase transition relative to the Stefan-Boltzmann energy density is given by \(\triangle\varepsilon / \varepsilon_{SB} = (1 + 1/(3\gamma)) \exp((\gamma-1)/\gamma)\), which can be zero only in the limit \(\gamma \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the statistical mixed phase model developed by Shanenko et al. (1997) to improve the treatment of hadron-hadron interactions and include one-gluon exchange corrections, aiming to resolve discrepancies between the two-phase model and lattice QCD results. The two-phase model (Suh, 1990; Dix, 1991) predicts a first-order phase transition with a sharp jump in energy and entropy at zero baryon density, contradicting the continuous crossover found in lattice calculations (Redlich, 1996; Brown, 1996; Blum, 1997; Bernard, 1997). The mixed phase model enables a consistent description of deconfinement across different quark-gluon systems and provides a framework for estimating the softest point effect as a possible experimental signal of the QCD phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.
