# Three-body force effect on the properties of nuclear matter under the gap and continuous choices within the BHF approach

> We have calculated and compared the three-body force effects on the properties of nuclear matter under the gap and continuous choices for the self-consistent auxiliary potential within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach by adopting the Argonne $V18$ and the Bonn B two-body potentials plus a microscopic three-body force (TBF). The TBF provides a strong repulsive effect on the equation of state of nuclear matter at high densities for both the gap and continuous choices. The saturation point turns out to be much closer to the empirical value when the continuous choice is adopted. In addition, the dependence of the calculated symmetry energy upon the choice of the self-consistent auxiliary potential is discussed.

# Three-body force effect on the properties of nuclear matter under the gap and continuous choices within the BHF approach

## Key claim

The three-body force (TBF) provides a strong repulsive effect on the equation of state of nuclear matter at high densities under both the gap and continuous choices for the self-consistent auxiliary potential, but the saturation point becomes much closer to the empirical value only when the continuous choice is adopted, not under the gap choice.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Argonne V18 and Bonn B two-body potentials, each supplemented with a microscopic three-body force constructed consistently via the meson exchange current approach. The TBF was reduced to an effective two-body force by averaging over the third nucleon degree of freedom in nuclear medium, and the self-consistent single-particle potentials were computed separately under the gap choice (zero above the Fermi surface) and the continuous choice (non-zero for all momenta).

## Key numbers

- For the AV18 potential without TBF under the continuous choice at density 0.17 fm^-3, the total binding energy per nucleon is -16.5 MeV; with TBF it becomes -15.03 MeV.
- For the Bonn B potential without TBF under the continuous choice at density 0.17 fm^-3, the total binding energy per nucleon is -18.49 MeV; with TBF it becomes -14.62 MeV.
- For the AV18 potential without TBF under the gap choice at density 0.17 fm^-3, the total binding energy per nucleon is -10.94 MeV; with TBF it becomes -9.54 MeV.
- For the Bonn B potential without TBF under the gap choice at density 0.17 fm^-3, the total binding energy per nucleon is -14.92 MeV; with TBF it becomes -10.1 MeV.
- The continuous choice gives binding energies about 4-6 MeV larger than the gap choice for symmetric nuclear matter, but only about 2 MeV larger for pure neutron matter.
- The discrepancy between the single-particle potentials obtained with AV18 and Bonn B is about 3-4 MeV at k=0 fm^-1, regardless of the choice of auxiliary potential.

## Context

This work extends previous BHF calculations by Baldo and Song (2000s) and Day and Wiringa (1980s), who showed that the three-hole line contribution is non-negligible under the gap choice and that the continuous choice yields faster convergence. The authors build on earlier TBF studies by the same group (Zuo et al., 2008) that demonstrated saturation point improvement under the continuous choice, and they systematically compare the TBF effect under both choices for the first time. The results enable future work where TBF should be included at the three-hole line level by solving Bethe-Fadeev equations to check convergence again.
