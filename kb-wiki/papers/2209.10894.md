# Kaonic Hanbury-Brown-Twiss radii at 200 GeV and 5.02 TeV

> We use 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) to make predictions for kaon Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii in 200 GeV and 5.02 TeV heavy-ion collisions. Using previously determined aHydroQP parameters, we compute kaonic HBT radii and their ratios as a function of the mean transverse momentum of the pair $k_T$. We first consider Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, finding good agreement between aHydroQP predictions and experimental data up to $k_T \sim 0.8$ GeV. We then present predictions for kaonic HBT radii and their ratios in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Our aHydroQP predictions do not exhibit a clear $k_T$ scaling of the pion and kaon source radii, however, an approximate transverse mass $m_T$ scaling is observed, particularly at 200 GeV.

## Key claim

Using 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) with previously tuned parameters, the authors predict kaon HBT radii at 200 GeV and 5.02 TeV, finding good agreement with PHENIX data only up to \(k_T \sim 0.8\) GeV; at higher \(k_T\) the predictions exceed the data, and the claimed approximate \(m_T\) scaling is observed primarily at 200 GeV and only at high \(m_T\).

## Method

The authors used 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) with a lattice-based equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, evolving the system from \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c with smooth Glauber initial conditions. The hydrodynamic output was converted to hadrons via an anisotropic Cooper-Frye freezeout at \(T_{\rm FO}=130\) MeV using a customized version of THERMINATOR 2, which samples from anisotropic hadron distributions and includes hadronic production and decays. Kaonic HBT radii were extracted by fitting two-particle correlation functions in the Bertsch-Pratt parameterization to a Gaussian form.

## Key numbers

- For 200 GeV Au-Au collisions: initial temperature \(T_0 = 455\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c, specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 0.179\).
- For 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions: initial temperature \(T_0 = 630\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c, specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 0.159\).
- Good agreement between aHydroQP predictions and PHENIX data for kaonic HBT radii up to \(k_T \sim 0.8\) GeV in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions; at larger \(k_T\) the predictions lie above the data.
- For 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, the \(k_T\) bins used for charged kaons were 0.3–0.36, 0.36–0.42, 0.42–0.48, 0.48–0.54, 0.54–0.60, 0.60–0.75, and 0.75–1.0 GeV.
- The ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) is consistently close to unity in all centrality classes at 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends prior aHydroQP studies of pionic HBT radii at RHIC and LHC energies (Alqahtani 2017, Alqahtani 2020) to kaonic femtoscopy, using the same model parameters tuned to identified-particle spectra in earlier papers (Almaalol 2018, Alqahtani 2020). The predictions for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions await experimental comparison, as no kaonic femtoscopy data at that energy are yet available. The observed violation of \(m_T\) scaling at low \(m_T\) for 5.02 TeV collisions contrasts with earlier ideal hydrodynamic models (Beker 1994, Chapman 1994, Akkelin 1995, Csorgo 1995) and with viscous hydrodynamics results from Kisiel (2014) and Chakraborty (2020) that found approximate \(m_T\) scaling at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.
