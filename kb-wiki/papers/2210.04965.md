# State Preparation in the Heisenberg Model through Adiabatic Spiraling

> An adiabatic state preparation technique, called the adiabatic spiral, is proposed for the Heisenberg model. This technique is suitable for implementation on a number of quantum simulation platforms such as Rydberg atoms, trapped ions, or superconducting qubits. Classical simulations of small systems suggest that it can be successfully implemented in the near future. A comparison to Trotterized time evolution is performed and it is shown that the adiabatic spiral is able to outperform Trotterized adiabatics.

## Key claim

The authors propose that eigenstates of the Heisenberg model are approximate eigenstates of the Ising model with a strong external field pointed in an appropriate direction, up to corrections of order O(1/Omega), enabling a new adiabatic state preparation technique called the adiabatic spiral that does not require the switching time to be an integer multiple of 2pi/Omega.

## Method

The method works by beginning in a Neel state (eigenstate of the Ising model) and adiabatically varying both the direction of an external drive field and a penalty field that splits initial degeneracies, following a spiral path on the Bloch sphere in the interaction picture. The time-dependent Hamiltonian implemented is a combination of Ising interactions with time-varying single-qubit terms, where the drive field strength Omega is taken large to ensure the eigenstates of the Ising and Heisenberg models are close. Classical numerical simulations were performed by computing time-ordered products of exponentials for small systems, and the path through parameter space was optimized by varying coefficients in a Fourier expansion of the switching function.

## Key numbers

- For a Heisenberg comb of length 4 with J=J_P=1 and switching time T=25, the adiabatic spiral with Omega=8 and h_P(0)=0.18 prepares a state below the first excited state energy
- The Rydberg atom experimental parameters considered used a max drive field amplitude of Omega_max = 2pi x 4.3 MHz and a max coherence time of 3 microseconds
- For the D-Wave Advantage 6.1 hardware, calibration errors are on the order of delta J approximately 4 x 10^(-3)
- The optimal path parameter beta_1 was found to be 1/pi, the largest value allowed by the constraint that maximum driving field is obtained at the end of the spiral

## Context

This work extends previous results showing that the Ising model with large transverse fields approximates the XY model dynamics (Richerme 2014, Jurcevic 2014) and generalizes the authors' own recent work showing that Ising models with large external fields can approximate Heisenberg model time evolution at discrete time intervals (Ciavarella 2022). The technique is related to pre-thermalization studies (Abanin 2017) and is specifically motivated by the Heisenberg comb model that reproduces the O(3) nonlinear sigma model in the continuum limit (PhysRevD.100.054505, PhysRevLett.126.172001). The adiabatic spiral enables near-term quantum simulations of the anti-ferromagnetic Heisenberg model on Rydberg atom platforms, trapped ions, or superconducting qubits, and could inform strategies for simulating quantum chromodynamics through universality arguments.
