# Curvature of the chiral pseudo-critical line in QCD

> We determine the curvature of the pseudo-critical line of strong interactions by means of numerical simulations at imaginary chemical potentials. We consider $N_f=2+1$ stout improved staggered fermions with physical quark masses and the tree level Symanzik gauge action, and explore two different sets of lattice spacings, corresponding to temporal extensions $N_t = 6$ and $N_t = 8$. Both the renormalized chiral condensate and the renormalized chiral susceptibility are used to locate the transition. The determinations obtained from the two quantities are in good agreement, a preliminary continuum extrapolation yields $κ= 0.013(2)(1)$. We also investigate the impact of a non-zero strange quark chemical potential and compare our results to previous determinations in the literature, discussing the possible sources of systematic errors affecting the various procedures.

## Key claim

The curvature of the chiral pseudo-critical line in QCD is determined to be κ = 0.013(2)(1) from a preliminary continuum extrapolation, but this value is obtained using the method of analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials and depends on the specific lattice discretization and renormalization prescriptions adopted.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with Nf=2+1 stout improved staggered fermions at physical quark masses and tree level Symanzik gauge action, using temporal extensions Nt=6 and Nt=8 to estimate ultraviolet cutoff effects. They simulated at imaginary chemical potentials and located the pseudo-critical temperature using both the renormalized chiral condensate (inflection point) and the renormalized chiral susceptibility (peak position), then extracted the curvature by fitting Tc as a function of the imaginary chemical potential. Two different chemical potential setups were considered: μs=0 and μs=μl, with the μs=0 case providing a larger region of imaginary chemical potentials available for analytic continuation.

## Key numbers

- Curvature κ = 0.013(2)(1) from preliminary continuum extrapolation, with the first error statistical and the second systematic
- Pseudo-critical temperature at zero chemical potential: Tc(0) = 154.2(4) MeV from chiral condensate and 155.6(7) MeV from chiral susceptibility on the 32³×8 lattice
- For the 32³×8 lattice with μs=0 and μl,I/(πT)=0.275, Tc = 169.5(9) MeV from condensate and 169.8(7) MeV from susceptibility
- For the 32³×8 lattice with μs=μl and μl,I/(πT)=0.275, Tc = 175.4(6) MeV from condensate and 177.0(8) MeV from susceptibility
- Pion mass mπ ≈ 135 MeV and strange-to-light quark mass ratio ms/ml = 28.15 used for the line of constant physics

## Context

This work extends previous lattice determinations of the chiral pseudo-critical line curvature by using the analytic continuation method with stout staggered fermions, complementing earlier studies using Taylor expansion methods (Kaczmarek 2011, Endrodi 2011, Borsanyi 2012) and a recent HISQ/tree action determination (ccp). The study enables comparison between different lattice discretizations and methods, and provides a first systematic investigation of the effects of including a non-zero strange quark chemical potential on the critical line, which is relevant for connecting lattice QCD predictions to heavy ion collision freeze-out curves.
