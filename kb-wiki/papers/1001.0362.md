# Photodisintegration of $^3H$ in a three dimensional Faddeev approach

> An interaction of a photon with $^3H$ is invstigated based on a three dimensional Faddeev approach. In this approach the three-nucleon Faddeev equations with two-nucleon interactions are formulated with consideration of the magnitude of the vector Jacobi momenta and the angle between them with the inclusion of the spin-isospin quantum numbers, without employing a partial wave decomposition. In this formulation the two body t-matrices and triton wave function are calculated in the three dimensional approach using AV18 potential. In the first step we use the standard single nucleon current in this article.

## Key claim
The paper formulates the Faddeev integral equations for photodisintegration of tritium (³H) in a three-dimensional (3D) approach without partial wave decomposition, using the AV18 potential and a standard single-nucleon current as a first step; the actual calculation of observables is stated as "underway" and not yet completed.

## Method
The authors construct a 3D Faddeev formalism for the photodisintegration of ³H using vector Jacobi momenta and the angle between them, with explicit spin-isospin quantum numbers, avoiding partial wave expansion. The two-body t-matrices and the triton wave function are computed in this 3D representation using the AV18 potential, and the single-nucleon electromagnetic current (convection plus spin current) is employed. The integral equations are derived for the nuclear matrix elements and the auxiliary state |U⟩, and a method to handle the moving singularity in the propagator is described.

## Key numbers
- The two-body t-matrix singularity is removed by writing it as a regular part divided by (z − E_d), where z = E − 3q²/(4m) is the subsystem energy and E_d is the deuteron energy.
- The free propagator denominator in the integral equations is E − (q² + q′² + q·q′)/m, where m is the nucleon mass.
- The convection current component is J_{±1}^{convec} = G_E(Q) q_{±1} / m_N, and the spin current component is J_{±1}^{Spin} = −√2 Q / (2 m_N) G_M(Q) S_±, where Q is the photon momentum.
- The triton wave function is expressed in a basis with quantum numbers (s½)Sm_S and (t½)Tm_T, related to individual nucleon spin/isospin states via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and rotation matrices D_{m_i m'_i}(θ_q, φ_q).

## Context
This work extends the 3D Faddeev approach previously developed by the Ohio-Bochum collaboration (Elster, Glöckle et al.) and Bayegan et al. for few-body bound and scattering calculations (Fachruddin et al., 2000; Bayegan et al., 2008) to the photodisintegration of ³H. It contrasts with traditional partial-wave decomposition methods used in earlier Faddeev calculations of photon interactions with ³He (Golak, 2005) and with variational, Green-function-Monte-Carlo (Carlson, 1987), hyperspherical harmonic (Viviani, 2000), and Lorentz integral transform (Efros, 1994) approaches. The formalism enables future calculations including two- and three-body currents and three-body forces, as well as radiative capture processes.
