# Polarization probes of vorticity in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the information that can be deduced from a measurement of particle (hyperon or vector meson) polarization in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. We describe the sensitivity of polarization to initial conditions, hydrodynamic evolution and mean free path, and find that the polarization observable is sensitive to all details and stages of the system's evolution. We suggest that an experimental investigation covering production plane and reaction plane polarizations, as well as the polarization of jet-associated particles in the plane defined by the jet and particle direction, can help in disentangling the factors contributing to this observable. Scans of polarization in energy and rapidity might also point to a change in the system's properties.

## Key claim

Hyperon polarization in heavy ion collisions is sensitive to all stages of the system's evolution (initial conditions, hydrodynamic evolution, and mean free path), but the observable can be significantly altered by each stage, making quantitative description problematic; measuring polarization in multiple directions (reaction plane, production plane, and jet plane) could help disentangle these factors.

## Method

The authors analytically model the initial generation of reaction-plane polarization from the QCD spin-orbit coupling, comparing two initial-condition scenarios: the "firestreak" model (where projectile and target nucleons stick locally) and the Brodsky-Gunion-Kuhn (BGK) picture (where partons are produced across longitudinal rapidity). They then discuss how relativistic hydrodynamic evolution can create or destroy vorticity, using a (3+1)D hydrodynamical simulation of a jet traversing a uniform fluid. Finally, they estimate the residual polarization that survives hydrodynamic evolution via a dimensional-analysis relation connecting polarization to the mean free path, temperature, and local momentum gradient.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of BGK to firestreak predictions for reaction-plane polarization at top RHIC energy, even with a spacetime-flow rapidity correlation length σ_η of one unit, is suppressed by about two orders of magnitude relative to the firestreak expectation.
- For LHC energies, the suppression of BGK relative to firestreak grows to several orders of magnitude.
- The energy-dependent parameter c(s) in the firestreak model is estimated as c(s) ~ (1/1.5) ln(√s / 1.5 GeV) ln(2√s / GeV), giving c(s) ~ 18 at top RHIC energies.
- The net polarization from the BGK initial condition at mid-rapidity scales as ⟨p_z x_⊥⟩ ∝ B exp(σ_η²/2) σ_η².
- The ratio of BGK to firestreak polarization is given by c(s) (m_N exp(σ_η²/2) σ_η²) / √s.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for hyperon polarization as a probe of heavy ion collisions, building on earlier proposals by Hoyer (1980s), Stock (1980s), and Panagiotou (1986) that production-plane polarization disappearance could signal deconfinement, and by Liang (2005) and Becattini (2006) for reaction-plane polarization from angular momentum. The authors contrast the firestreak initial condition used by Liang (2005) with the BGK picture from Brodsky, Gunion, and Kuhn (1977) and Gyulassy and collaborators, and note that the STAR collaboration has reported reaction-plane polarization results consistent with zero. The paper suggests that measuring polarization in the jet plane could provide a new probe of jet-induced vorticity, and that scanning polarization in energy and rapidity might reveal the transition from a viscous hadron gas to a strongly interacting quark-gluon liquid.
