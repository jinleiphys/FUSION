# Hydrodynamic flow amplitude correlations in event-by-event fluctuating heavy-ion collisions

> The effects of event-by-event fluctuations in the initial geometry of the colliding nuclei are important in the analysis of final flow observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We use hydrodynamic simulations to study the amplitude correlations between different orders of event-by-event fluctuating anisotropic flow harmonics. While the general trends seen in the experimental data are qualitatively reproduced by the model, deviations in detail, in particular for peripheral collisions, point to the need for more elaborate future calculations with a hybrid approach that describes the late hadronic stage of the evolution microscopically. It is demonstrated explicitly that the observed anti-correlation between $v_2$ and $v_3$ is the consequence of approximately linear hydrodynamic response to a similar anti-correlation of the corresponding initial eccentricities $ε_2$ and $ε_3$. For $n{\,>\,}3$, the hydrodynamic correlations between $v_{2,3}$ and $v_n$ deviate from the rescaled correlations among the corresponding initial eccentricities, demonstrating nonlinear mode coupling effect in higher order flows.

# Hydrodynamic flow amplitude correlations in event-by-event fluctuating heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed anti-correlation between elliptic flow $v_2$ and triangular flow $v_3$ arises from approximately linear hydrodynamic response to a similar anti-correlation of the corresponding initial eccentricities $\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$, but for higher-order harmonics ($n>3$) the hydrodynamic correlations deviate from rescaled eccentricity correlations due to nonlinear mode coupling effects. The pure hydrodynamic simulations qualitatively reproduce experimental trends but show quantitative deviations in peripheral collisions, indicating the need for hybrid approaches that include a microscopic description of the late hadronic stage.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the VISH2+1 code with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions and a fixed specific shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$, starting evolution at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$ and ending on an isothermal decoupling surface at $T_\mathrm{dec} = 120$ MeV. They generated 3000 hydrodynamically evolved events in each of 14 centrality bins (0%-5% through 65%-70%) based on final charged multiplicity density at midrapidity, and applied event-shape engineering by binning events according to their $q_2$ or $q_3$ values to study flow amplitude correlations.

## Key numbers

- 14 centrality bins defined from 0%-5% to 65%-70% based on final charged multiplicity density at midrapidity
- 3000 hydrodynamically evolved events generated in each centrality bin
- Specific shear viscosity fixed to $\eta/s = 0.08$ for the main analysis
- Hydrodynamic evolution started at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/$c$ and ended at $T_\mathrm{dec} = 120$ MeV
- Events subdivided into 6 event-shape selected classes with $q_2$ or $q_3$ bin fractions: 0-0.1, 0.1-0.2, 0.2-0.5, 0.5-0.8, 0.8-0.9, and 0.9-1.0
- For $v_4$ and $v_5$, the nonlinear contribution $v_n^\mathrm{NL}$ is expressed as $v_4^\mathrm{NL} = |\mathrm{Re} \langle V_4 V_2^{*2} \rangle| / \sqrt{\langle v_2^4 \rangle}$ and $v_5^\mathrm{NL} = |\mathrm{Re} \langle V_5 V_2^* V_3^* \rangle| / \sqrt{\langle v_2^2 v_3^2 \rangle}$

## Context

This work extends the experimental analysis by the ATLAS Collaboration (2015) of flow amplitude correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, which found linear anti-correlation between $v_3$ and $v_2$ but nonlinear correlations for $v_4$ and $v_5$ with $v_2$ and $v_3$. The study builds on previous hydrodynamic calculations of event-by-event fluctuations by Heinz and collaborators and on the eccentricity correlation framework developed by Yan and collaborators (2015). The results enable future use of flow amplitude correlations as constraints for both initial state fluctuation models and QGP transport coefficients, though this will require full hybrid model simulations that correctly implement early and late non-equilibrium dynamics.
