# Baryons, multi-hadron systems, and composite dark matter in non-relativistic QCD

> We provide a formulation of potential non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics (pNRQCD) suitable for calculating binding energies and matrix elements of generic hadron and multi-hadron states made of heavy quarks in $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory using quantum Monte Carlo techniques. We compute masses of quarkonium and triply-heavy baryons in order to study the perturbative convergence of pNRQCD and validate our numerical methods. Further, we study $SU(N_c)$ models of composite dark matter and provide simple power series fits to our pNRQCD results that can be used to relate dark meson and baryon masses to the fundamental parameters of these models. For many systems comprised entirely of heavy quarks, the quantum Monte Carlo methods employed here are less computationally demanding than lattice field theory methods, although they introduce additional perturbative approximations. The formalism presented here may therefore be particularly useful for predicting composite dark matter properties for a wide range of $N_c$ and heavy fermion masses.

## Key claim

The paper provides a formulation of potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) that can be applied to arbitrary hadronic and multi-hadron systems composed entirely of heavy quarks, including composite dark matter candidates in SU(Nc) gauge theories, but only in the weak-coupling regime where mQv >> ΛQCD, which is valid for top and bottom quarks but becomes less reliable for charm-like masses.

## Method

The authors generalize pNRQCD to treat arbitrary bound systems of heavy fermions charged under SU(Nc) by constructing operators and matching coefficients describing two- and three-quark potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for general Nc. They apply variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods to compute binding energies and mass spectra, using ensembles of 5,000 configurations with 3NQ degrees of freedom representing spatial coordinates of NQ heavy quarks. The heavy quark mass is tuned to reproduce the spin-averaged mass of the measured 1S state of the associated quarkonia before predicting triply-heavy baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- The pNRQCD calculations use ensembles of 5,000 configurations with 3NQ degrees of freedom, in contrast to lattice QCD calculations that commonly use ensembles with 10^8 or more degrees of freedom
- The color-singlet potential is known to N3LO in QCD and NLO in the Standard Model, with the NNLO correction factor for three-quark potentials given by δaA = Nc(Nc-2)/2 × π²(π²-12) for antisymmetric and δaS = Nc(Nc+2)/2 × π²(π²-12) for symmetric representations
- The study covers SU(Nc) gauge theories for Nc ∈ {3,...,6} and extrapolates to large Nc
- The beta function coefficients used are β0 = (11/3)CA - (4/3)TFnf and β1 = (34/3)CA² - 4CFTFnf - (20/3)CATFnf

## Context

This work extends previous pNRQCD studies that focused primarily on heavy quarkonia in QCD (Brambilla 1999, Kniehl 2002, Pineda 2011) and earlier extensions to doubly- and triply-heavy baryons (Brambilla 2005). It enables efficient scanning over a wide range of mass scales for composite dark matter models, where the fundamental parameters of the underlying theory are not yet known, and provides power series fits that can be used in phenomenological studies of composite DM.
