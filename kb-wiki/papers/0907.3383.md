# Extended Thermodynamic Relation and Fluctuation Theorem in Stochastic Dynamics with Time Reversed Process

> We consider a stochastic model described by two stochastic differential equations of motion; one is for the stochastic evolution forward in time and the other for backward in time. We further introduce averaged quantities for the two processes and construct the extended thermodynamic relation following the strategy of Sekimoto. By using this relation, we derive the fluctuation theorems such as the Seifert relation, the Jarzynski relation and the Komatsu-Nakagawa non-equilibrium steady state with respect to the introduced averaged quantities.

# Extended Thermodynamic Relation and Fluctuation Theorem in Stochastic Dynamics with Time Reversed Process

## Key claim
The authors derive fluctuation theorems (Seifert relation, Jarzynski relation, Komatsu-Nakagawa non-equilibrium steady state) for a stochastic model that explicitly includes both forward and backward time evolution, but these theorems are expressed in terms of *averaged* thermodynamic quantities of the two processes and contain an additional heat term δQ_J that vanishes only when the probability density has no explicit time dependence (i.e., no macroscopic flow).

## Method
The authors introduce two coupled stochastic differential equations: one for forward time evolution (dt > 0) and one for backward time evolution (dt < 0). They enforce consistency between the corresponding Fokker-Planck equations, which yields a relation between the forward force u and the backward force u* involving the gradient of log ρ. Following Sekimoto's thermodynamic interpretation, they define averaged thermodynamic quantities (averaged energy Ū, averaged heat δQ̄, averaged work δW̄) and perform stochastic differentiation using the Ito formula to obtain an extended thermodynamic relation that includes a new term δQ_J. They then compute probability ratios along trajectories to derive fluctuation theorems for these averaged quantities.

## Key numbers
- The noise term satisfies ⟨dW_i(t) dW_j(t)⟩ = δ_{ij}|dt|, with β constant and γ set to 1 in special units.
- For free Brownian motion, the probability density is ρ(r,t) = exp(-r²/(4β⁻¹t)) / √(4πβ⁻¹t).
- For free Brownian motion, the averaged energy is Ū(X(t),t) = X²(t)/(4t) + (β⁻¹/2) ln(4πβ⁻¹t).
- For free Brownian motion, δQ_J = (β⁻¹/(2t) - X²(t)/(4t²)) dt, and its expectation vanishes because ⟨X²(t)⟩ = 2β⁻¹t.
- The Seifert-type relation derived is ⟨exp(-R(Γ))⟩ = 1, where R is defined via the ratio of forward and backward path probabilities.

## Context
This work extends Sekimoto's (1998) thermodynamic interpretation of stochastic dynamics to systems where the time-reversed process is nontrivial, unlike in deterministic dynamics. It generalizes the fluctuation theorems of Seifert (2005), Jarzynski (1997), and Komatsu and Nakagawa (2008) by expressing them in terms of averaged quantities of forward and backward processes, introducing a new heat term δQ_J that connects to extended irreversible thermodynamics. The formalism enables future investigation of thermodynamic relations in relativistic Brownian motion and pre-equilibrium dynamics, and the authors note a connection to Nelson's formulation of quantum mechanics.
