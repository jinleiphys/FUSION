# Phenomenological constraints on the Jaffe-Wilczek model of pentaquarks

> A model recently introduced by Jaffe and Wilczek based on the quarks being dynamically bound into diquarks has been used to predict that the recently observed exotic baryons (pentaquarks) fall into a nearly ideally mixed combination of an octer and anti-decuplet representations of SU(3) flavor. The model predicts two states with nucleon quantum numbers which have tentatively been identified with the N*(1440) and the N*(1710). This paper examines the viability of this model by focusing on the decay width of the nucleon members of the multiplet. An inequality relating the partial widths of these nucleon states in the N+nucleon channel to the width of the theta+ is derived for this model under the assuming ideal mixing and that the only significant exact SU(3) symmetry violations are the result of ideal mixing, threshold effects and the masses of pseudo-Goldstone bosons. This inequality is badly violated if the states in the multiplet are the N*(1440) and the N*(1710) and if the recent bounds extracted for the theta+ width are reliable. Thus, the model appears to require a scenario with the existence of at least one presently unknown resonance with nucleon quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The Jaffe-Wilczek pentaquark model, which predicts nearly ideal mixing of an SU(3) octet and anti-decuplet, is inconsistent with identifying the two predicted nucleon states as the N*(1440) (Roper resonance) and the N*(1710) if the theta+ width is as narrow as the indirect bound of 1-2 MeV; the model requires at least one presently unknown resonance with nucleon quantum numbers.

## Method

The author derives an inequality relating the partial widths for the decays of the two ideally mixed pentaquark nucleon states into pion plus nucleon to the width of the theta+ decaying into kaon plus nucleon, using SU(3) symmetry with the assumptions that the only significant violations are ideal mixing, threshold effects, and pseudo-Goldstone boson masses. The inequality is then tested by inserting the experimentally extracted masses and partial widths for the N*(1440) and N*(1710) from the Particle Data Group, along with bounds on the theta+ width from analyses of kaon-deuteron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1440) (Roper resonance) has a mass range 1430-1470 MeV, total width 250-450 MeV, and branching fraction to pi+N of 60%-70%; best estimate partial width to pi+N is 227.5 MeV.
- The N*(1710) has a mass range 1680-1740 MeV, total width 50-250 MeV, and branching fraction to pi+N of 10%-20%; best estimate partial width to pi+N is 15 MeV.
- Using best estimate values and a theta+ width bound of 20 MeV (direct experimental resolution), the inequality is modestly violated: left-hand side 71.8 vs. right-hand side 61.8.
- Using best estimate values and a theta+ width bound of 2 MeV (from indirect analyses), the inequality is grossly violated: left-hand side 77.1 vs. right-hand side 19.7, a violation by nearly a factor of 4.
- Under extremely optimistic assumptions for the N* masses and widths, with a theta+ width of 2 MeV, the inequality is still violated by more than a factor of 2: left-hand side 33.5 vs. right-hand side 15.8.
- The indirect bound on the theta+ width from kaon-deuteron scattering is 1.5-2 MeV, with a similar bound of 1 MeV from xenon experiments.

## Context

This work tests the pentaquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003), which predicted that the recently observed exotic baryons form a nearly ideally mixed octet and anti-decuplet of SU(3) flavor. The analysis extends the phenomenological constraints by focusing on decay widths rather than masses alone, and contradicts the tentative identification of the model's nucleon states with the N*(1440) and N*(1710). The results enable future searches for previously undiscovered narrow or broad nucleon resonances in pion-nucleon scattering data, as suggested by the work of Gothe and Nussinov (2003) and the re-analysis by the GW group (2003).
