# Spin-Polarization Response Functions in High-Energy (e,e'p) Reactions

> Spin-polarization response functions are examined for high-energy $(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ reaction by computing the full 18 response functions for the proton kinetic energy $T_{p'}=$ 0.515 GeV and 3.179 GeV with an 16O target. The Dirac eikonal formalism is applied to account for the final-state interactions. The formalism is found to yield the response functions in good agreement with those calculated by the partial-wave expansion method at 0.515 GeV. We identify the response functions that depend on the spin-orbital potential in the final-state interactions, but not on the central potential. Dependence on the Dirac- or Pauli-type current of the nucleon is investigated in the helicity-dependent response functions, and the normal-component polarization of the knocked-out proton, $P_n$, is computed.

## Key claim

The Dirac eikonal formalism yields spin-polarization response functions for high-energy (e,e'p) reactions that agree within 10% at the peak with partial-wave expansion calculations at 0.515 GeV, but the authors note that important physical effects such as off-shell effects and current conservation are neglected in this exploratory work.

## Method

The authors computed the full 18 spin-polarization response functions for the (e,e'p) reaction on an 16O target using the Dirac eikonal formalism to account for final-state interactions, with the bound-state proton described by Hartree mean-field wave functions from the Walecka model. The calculations were performed at two proton kinetic energies (0.515 GeV and 3.179 GeV) using the fρ-form optical potential with pN-scattering amplitudes from phase-shift analyses, and the response functions were examined with and without the spin-orbit force in the final-state interaction.

## Key numbers

- Proton kinetic energies examined: Tp' = 0.515 GeV and 3.179 GeV, corresponding to momenta |p| = 1.113 GeV/c and 4.024 GeV/c respectively
- Momentum transfer Q² = 1 (GeV/c)² at Tp' = 0.515 GeV and Q² = 6 (GeV/c)² at Tp' = 3.179 GeV
- Dirac eikonal and partial-wave methods agree within 10% at the peak for most response functions at 0.515 GeV, with R_TT^n showing larger discrepancy of about 20%
- At 135 MeV, the discrepancy between methods is 30-40% and reaches 80-100% for transverse responses (R^t_TT, R^n_TT, R^l_TT)
- Response function magnitudes at 3.179 GeV are smaller by two orders of magnitude than those at 0.515 GeV
- P_n magnitude at 3.179 GeV is more than 40% smaller than at 0.515 GeV, becoming comparable to expected experimental accuracy ΔP_n ≈ 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of polarization response functions that focused on low proton energies of several hundred MeV or less (Picklesimer and Van Orden, 1990s) and earlier calculations of (e,e'p) response functions in the GeV region (Green et al., Alder et al., 1990s). The authors present the first calculation of the full 18 spin response functions for (e,e'p) in the GeV region, enabling comparison with planned experiments at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (TJNAF) and providing benchmark results for future refined calculations that include current conservation and off-shell effects.
