# $ϕ$ and $J/ψ$ Production in Proton-Proton Scattering through Hadronic Molecules

> This study estimates the contributions of hidden-strangeness hadronic molecular states ($N(2080)3/2^-$, $N(2270)3/2^-$) to $ϕ$ production and hidden-charm $P_c$ states to $J/ψ$ production in $pp$ collisions. The calculated cross-sections reach $\sim 10~μb$ for $pp\to ppϕ$ and $\sim 0.1~nb$ for $pp\to pp J/ψ$ at energies $\sim 2$~GeV above threshold. We also compute the spin density matrix element $ρ_{00}$ and the decay angular distributions for $ϕ\to K^+K^-$ and $J/ψ\toμ^+μ^-$. To support future experiments, we conduct Monte Carlo simulations for the four-body final states of $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ and $pp\to ppμ^+μ^-$. The resulting lab-frame distributions are expected to inform detector design at facilities like the High-Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) and the proposed Chinese Advanced Nuclear Physics Research Facility (CNUF), enabling more precise measurements and tighter theoretical constraints. Furthermore, to explain the STAR collaboration's result on $ϕ$ meson spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions, we propose a novel mechanism involving hidden-strangeness molecular states, testable with future high-statistics $pp\to ppϕ$ data.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that hidden-strangeness hadronic molecular states \(N(2080)3/2^-\) and \(N(2270)3/2^-\) can produce \(\phi\) mesons in proton-proton scattering with cross sections on the order of \(10~\mu\)b, and hidden-charm \(P_c\) states can produce \(J/\psi\) mesons with cross sections on the order of \(0.1\) nb, but only at center-of-mass energies approximately \(2\) GeV above the respective production thresholds; the calculations rely on specific coupling constant solutions (Solution A and Solution B) that are constrained by photoproduction data and near-threshold \(pp\to pp\phi\) data.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with \(\rho\)-meson exchange to describe the proton-proton interaction, introducing \(s\)-channel \(N(2080)3/2^-\) and \(N(2270)3/2^-\) hadronic molecular states for \(\phi\) production and six \(P_c\) states (\(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4380)\), \(P_c(4440)\), \(P_c(4457)\), \(P_c(4500)\), \(P_c(4511)\)) for \(J/\psi\) production. They determine coupling constants from branching ratios in the hadronic molecular picture and from fits to photoproduction data, then compute total and differential cross sections, spin density matrix elements \(\rho_{00}\), and decay angular distributions, followed by Monte Carlo simulations of the four-body final states.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for \(pp\to pp\phi\) reaches \(\sim 10~\mu\)b at center-of-mass energies \(\sim 2\) GeV above threshold; Solution A gives cross section stabilizing around \(5~\mu\)b, Solution B reaches \(20~\mu\)b at \(W=5\) GeV.
- Total cross section for \(pp\to pp J/\psi\) reaches \(\sim 0.1\) nb at energies \(\sim 2\) GeV above threshold.
- At proton beam momentum \(p_{\text{lab}}=3.67\) GeV/\(c\), the spin density matrix element \(\rho_{00}\) for \(\phi\) meson is \(0.36\) for \(J^P=3/2^-\) states, \(0.35\) for \(J^P=1/2^-\) states, and \(0.20\) for the \(g_1\) term of a \(J^P=5/2^-\) state, compared to the experimental value \(0.23\pm0.04\) from the DISTO collaboration.
- The proton intrinsic strangeness contribution to \(pp\to pp\phi\) yields cross sections of \(0.5~\mu\)b (\(\kappa_\phi=-0.5\)) and \(3~\mu\)b (\(\kappa_\phi=-4.0\)) at \(W=5\) GeV.
- For the \(J/\psi\) production process, coupling constants are taken as \(g_c(V=J/\psi)=0.05\) and \(g_c(V=\rho)=0.01\) for the \(P_c\) state vertices.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular picture previously applied to photoproduction reactions \(\gamma p\to \phi p\) (Wu 2023) and \(\gamma p\to J/\psi p\) (Duan 2024) to proton-proton scattering, using the same \(N(2080)3/2^-\) and \(N(2270)3/2^-\) states from the hidden-strangeness sector and six \(P_c\) states from the hidden-charm sector. The calculations build on branching ratio predictions from Lin (2018) and Ben (2024) for molecular states, and aim to inform detector design at the High-Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) and the proposed Chinese Advanced Nuclear Physics Research Facility (CNUF). The proposed mechanism for \(\phi\) meson spin alignment involving hidden-strangeness molecular states offers a testable explanation for the unexpectedly large \(\rho_{00}\) observed by the STAR collaboration in heavy-ion collisions.
