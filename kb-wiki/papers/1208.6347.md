# Few-Body Systems Composed of Heavy Quarks

> Within the past ten years many new hadrons states were observed experimentally, some of which do not fit into the conventional quark model. I will talk about the few-body systems composed of heavy quarks, including the charmonium-like states and some loosely bound states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several newly discovered heavy-quark hadrons, such as the charged Zb states and X(3872), are best interpreted as loosely bound hadronic molecules rather than conventional quark-model states, but this interpretation relies on the condition that the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model is reliable only when there is no annihilation or for very shallow bound states.

## Method

The authors apply the one-boson-exchange (OBE) formalism, previously used to describe the deuteron, to systems of heavy mesons and heavy baryons. They derive effective potentials from pion, eta, rho, and omega exchange, include S-wave and D-wave mixing, and solve coupled-channel Schrodinger equations with a form factor cutoff constrained by deuteron data. For X(3872), they progressively add neutral and charged D meson components, coupled-channel effects, and explicit isospin-breaking mass splittings.

## Key numbers

- Zb(10610) mass: 10608.4 ± 2.0 MeV/c², width: 15.6 ± 2.5 MeV/c²
- Zb(10650) mass: 10653.2 ± 1.5 MeV/c², width: 14.4 ± 3.2 MeV/c²
- Both Zb states favor quantum numbers I^G(J^P) = 1^+(1^+)
- B B* branching ratio for lower Zb: (86.0 ± 3.6)%; for heavier Zb: (73.4 ± 7.0)%
- X(3872) width is less than 1.2 MeV
- In OPE model for X(3872) with charged mode included (Case II): binding energy 0.32 MeV, root-mean-square radius 4.97 fm, S-wave probability 98.81%, D-wave 1.19% at cutoff 1.55 GeV
- With coupled-channel effects (Case III): binding energy 0.76 MeV, radius 3.79 fm at cutoff 1.10 GeV
- With physical mass splitting (Case IV): isoscalar component 74%, isovector component 26% at binding energy 0.3 MeV; at ~11 MeV binding, isoscalar 98.5%, isovector 1.5%
- Predicted branching fraction ratio R = B(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻π⁰J/ψ)/B(X(3872)→π⁺π⁻J/ψ) = 0.42 at binding energy 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the OBE molecular framework from nuclear physics (deuteron) to heavy-quark systems, following earlier theoretical predictions of B*B* molecular states by Liu (2008) and others. It directly addresses the charmonium-like X, Y, Z states discovered since 2003 by Belle and BaBar, particularly the Zb states observed by Belle (2011) and X(3872) discovered by Belle. The results enable future searches for heavy dibaryons (ΛcΛc and ΛbΛb) at facilities like LHCb, J-PARC, and sBelle, and suggest that the Upsilon(5S) resonance is an ideal factory for producing heavy molecular states.
