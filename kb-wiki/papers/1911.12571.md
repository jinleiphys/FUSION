# Impact of uncertainties of unbound $^{10}$Li on the ground state of two-neutron halo $^{11}$Li

> Recently, the energy spectrum of $^{10}$Li was measured upto 4.6 MeV, via one-neutron transfer reaction d($^{9}$Li, p)$^{10}$Li. Considering the ambiguities on the $^{10}$Li continuum spectrum with reference to new data, we report the configuration mixing in the ground state of the two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Li for two different choices of the $^{9}$Li$+$n potential. For the present study, we employ a three-body (core$+$n$+$n) structure model developed for describing the two-neutron halo system by explicit coupling of unbound continuum states of the subsystem (core$+$n), and discuss the two-neutron correlations in the ground state of $^{11}$Li.

## Key claim

The configuration mixing in the ground state of the two-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Li is sensitive to the choice of the $^{9}$Li$+n$ potential, specifically the depth of the $s$-wave potential, which determines whether the $(s_{1/2})^2$ or $(p_{1/2})^2$ configuration dominates and whether the halo is $s$-wave or $p$-wave in character.

## Method

The authors employed a three-body (core$+n+n$) structure model that explicitly couples unbound continuum states of the $^{10}$Li subsystem. Single-particle continuum wave functions for $s_{1/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $d_{5/2}$, and $d_{3/2}$ states were calculated using Woods-Saxon core-neutron potentials with two different parameter sets (Set A and Set B) that differ only in the $s$-wave depth. A density-dependent contact-delta pairing interaction was used, with its strength adjusted to reproduce the two-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Li.

## Key numbers

* The $p_{1/2}$ resonance in $^{10}$Li was reproduced at $0.45$ MeV with a decay width of $0.36$ MeV, consistent with the experimental value of $0.45\pm0.03$ MeV from Cavallaro et al. (2017).
* The $d_{5/2}$ resonance in $^{10}$Li was placed at $2.98$ MeV with a decay width of $1.39$ MeV.
* For Set A (deeper $s$-wave potential), the ground state of $^{11}$Li consists of $55.5\%$ $(s_{1/2})^2$, $33.1\%$ $(p_{1/2})^2$, and $7.1\%$ $(d_{5/2})^2$ configurations.
* For Set B (shallower $s$-wave potential), the ground state consists of $24.5\%$ $(s_{1/2})^2$, $59.6\%$ $(p_{1/2})^2$, and $9.1\%$ $(d_{5/2})^2$ configurations.
* The calculated matter radii are $3.53$ fm for Set A and $3.24$ fm for Set B.
* The two-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Li was taken as $S_{2n} = -0.369$ MeV from Smith et al. (2008).

## Context

This work extends three-body structure models previously applied to $^{6}$He, $^{22}$C, and $^{26}$O by Singh and collaborators (2016, 2019) to the specific case of $^{11}$Li, using new experimental data on the $^{10}$Li continuum spectrum from Cavallaro et al. (2017). The results are consistent with configuration mixing calculations by Hagino and Sagawa (2005) and Gomez-Ramos et al. (2017), but show that the ambiguity in the $^{10}$Li $s$-wave virtual state prevents a unique determination of the $^{11}$Li ground state composition, motivating further studies of reaction observables sensitive to the partial wave content.
