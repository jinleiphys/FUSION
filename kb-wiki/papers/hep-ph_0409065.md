# Exotica

> The first evidence for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), the theory of the strong interactions, came from the systematics of baryon and meson spectroscopy. An important early observation was the apparent absence of exotics, baryons requiring more than three quarks or mesons requiring more than quark-antiquark. Years later, QCD is well established, hadron spectroscopy has been relatively inactive, but the absence of exotics remains poorly understood. The recent observation of narrow, prominent exotic baryons has stirred up new interest in hadron spectroscopy. At present the experimental situation is confused; so is theory. The recent discoveries are striking. So too is the complete absence of exotic mesons, and, except for the recent discoveries, of exotic baryons as well. Whether or not the new states are confirmed, the way we look at complicated states of confined quarks and gluons has changed. Perhaps the most lasting result, and the one emphasized in these notes, is a new appreciation for the role of diquark correlations in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that whether or not the recently reported exotic baryons like the Θ⁺ are confirmed, the most lasting result is a new appreciation for the role of diquark correlations in QCD, which can qualitatively explain both the general absence of exotics and the possible existence of a few narrow exotic states.

## Method

The author reviews the history of exotic hadron searches, summarizes the experimental sightings of the Θ⁺ and other exotic candidates reported since January 2003, and examines theoretical perspectives including scattering theory, large N_c expansions, chiral soliton models, quark models, and lattice results. The paper then develops a diquark correlation framework to explain the rarity of exotics, the existence of a supernumerary scalar meson nonet, and the possible structure of pentaquark states.

## Key numbers

- Θ⁺ mass reported between 1520 and 1540 MeV across multiple experiments, with width limits ranging from <1 MeV (from KN scattering analysis) to <25 MeV (from direct detection)
- Φ⁻⁻ mass reported at 1860 MeV with width <18 MeV
- Charm exotic (uudd\bar c) mass reported at 3099 MeV with width <12 MeV
- Λ(1520) has partial width into \overline KN of approximately 7 MeV
- Center of mass momentum for KN scattering at Θ⁺ mass is approximately 270 MeV
- For ℓ=0, the ratio g_{KNΘ}/g_{\overline KNΛ(1520)} would need to be approximately 0.02 to obtain Γ(Θ) ≈ 1 MeV; for ℓ=1, approximately 0.06

## Context

This work extends the long-standing puzzle of why exotics are absent in QCD spectroscopy, a question dating to Gell-Mann's 1964 quark paper, and responds to the 2003 sightings of the Θ⁺ by the LEPS collaboration and others. The paper builds on earlier diquark work by the author and collaborators (Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003) and contrasts with chiral soliton model predictions by Manohar, Chemtob, and others. It enables future lattice QCD studies to test diquark correlation ideas and provides a framework for interpreting ongoing experimental searches for exotic hadrons.
