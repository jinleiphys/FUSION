# The deconvolution problem of deeply virtual Compton scattering

> Generalised parton distributions are instrumental to study both the three-dimensional structure and the energy-momentum tensor of the nucleon, and motivate numerous experimental programmes involving hard exclusive measurements. Based on a next-to-leading order analysis and a careful study of evolution effects, we exhibit non-trivial generalised parton distributions with arbitrarily small imprints on deeply virtual Compton scattering observables. This means that in practice the reconstruction of generalised parton distributions from measurements, known as the deconvolution problem, does not possess a unique solution for this channel. In this Letter we discuss the consequences on the extraction of generalised parton distributions from data and advocate for a multi-channel analysis.

## Key claim

The authors prove that, even at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD and including evolution effects, the reconstruction of generalised parton distributions (GPDs) from deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) observables is fundamentally non-unique: there exist nontrivial "ghost" GPDs that produce arbitrarily small imprints on DVCS observables and thus cannot be distinguished by data.

## Method

The authors construct polynomial double distributions (DDs) of total degree N and enforce vanishing of the forward limit, the LO CFF, the NLO coefficient function contributions, and continuity conditions through linear systems of equations. They demonstrate that the number of free parameters (scaling as N^2) exceeds the number of constraints (scaling as N), yielding a vector space of ghost GPDs. They then verify numerically that such ghost GPDs produce CFF contributions smaller than 10^{-5} at typical collider kinematics (xi = 0.1, Q^2 = 100 GeV^2) after NLO evolution using APFEL++ and PARTONS.

## Key numbers

- The number of free coefficients for a polynomial DD of total degree N is (N+1)(N+3)/8, while the constraints from forward limit and LO CFF cancellation scale as N+1 and N+2 respectively, making the problem underconstrained for sufficiently large N.
- For a ghost GPD of degree 25 added to the Goloskokov-Kroll model at mu_0^2 = 1 GeV^2 and evolved to Q^2 = 100 GeV^2 with xi = 0.1, the imaginary part of the NLO CFF is less than 10^{-5}, about 5 orders of magnitude smaller than the ghost GPD itself.
- The NLO CFF contribution from the ghost GPD scales as O(alpha_s^2(mu^2)), confirmed by a quadratic fit to computations at different values of alpha_s(mu^2 = 100 GeV^2).

## Context

This work extends the early discussion of the deconvolution problem by Freund (1999) and the detailed status report by Diehl (2003), which had noted the absence of a generic deconvolution procedure. The authors provide the first quantitative proof beyond leading order that evolution alone cannot resolve the ambiguity, contradicting long-held expectations. The result calls for multi-channel analyses (e.g., double DVCS) and motivates further studies of positivity constraints and lattice QCD inputs to bound ghost GPDs, as well as careful treatment of the D-term for extracting proton mechanical properties.
