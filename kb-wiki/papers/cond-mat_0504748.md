# q-deformed structures and generalized thermodynamics

> On the basis of the recently proposed formalism [A. Lavagno and P.N. Swamy, Phys. Rev. E 65, 036101 (2002)], we show that the realization of the thermostatistics of q-deformed algebra can be built on the formalism of q-calculus. It is found that the entire structure of thermodynamics is preserved if we use an appropriate Jackson derivative instead of the ordinary thermodynamic derivative. Furthermore, in analogy with the quantum q-oscillator algebra, we also investigate a possible q-deformation of the classical Poisson bracket in order to extend a generalized q-deformed dynamics in the classical regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the thermostatistics of q-deformed algebra can be consistently formulated using q-calculus, and that the entire structure of thermodynamics is preserved if the Jackson derivative replaces the ordinary thermodynamic derivative; this claim is conditional on the prescription that any thermodynamic derivative of functions depending on exponential variables must first use the ordinary chain rule and then apply the Jackson derivative with respect to the exponential variable.

## Method

The authors first review the q-boson algebra defined by deformed commutation relations for creation and annihilation operators, then derive the mean occupation number for a q-boson gas in the grand canonical ensemble. They show that the ordinary thermodynamic derivative with respect to fugacity must be replaced by the Jackson derivative to recover the correct particle number and internal energy. Finally, they construct a q-deformed Poisson bracket on a non-commutative phase-space by requiring invariance under the q-deformed symplectic group Sp_q(1), using the q-differential calculus on the q-plane generated by non-commuting coordinates satisfying \(\hat p \hat x = q \hat x \hat p\).

## Key numbers

- The q-basic number is defined as \([x] = (q^{2x} - 1)/(q^2 - 1)\), reducing to the ordinary number \(x\) in the limit \(q \rightarrow 1\).
- The mean occupation number for q-bosons is \(n_i = \frac{1}{2 \log q} \log\left( \frac{z^{-1}e^{\beta\epsilon_i} - 1}{z^{-1}e^{\beta\epsilon_i} - q^2} \right)\), where \(z = e^{\beta\mu}\) is the fugacity.
- The q-Poisson bracket yields the fundamental relations \(\{\hat x, \hat p\}_q = 1\) and \(\{\hat p, \hat x\}_q = -q^2\), showing it is not antisymmetric.
- The q-deformed plane satisfies \(\hat p \hat x = q \hat x \hat p\), with the q-derivatives acting on monomials as \(\hat\partial_x(\hat x^n \hat p^m) = [n] \hat x^{n-1} \hat p^m\) and \(\hat\partial_p(\hat x^n \hat p^m) = [m] q^n \hat x^n \hat p^{m-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Lavagno and Swamy (2002) by showing that q-calculus provides the natural mathematical framework for q-deformed thermostatistics, and that the Jackson derivative preserves the Legendre transform structure. The paper also addresses the open question of whether a classical counterpart to quantum q-deformations exists, building on the q-Poisson bracket approach of Klimek (reference given in text) and the q-plane formalism of Reshetikhin and Wess. The results enable further investigation into q-deformed classical dynamics, including harmonic oscillators, the equipartition theorem, Euler's theorem, and the Liouville theorem, in analogy with the role of Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics (Tsallis, 1988) in classical physics.
