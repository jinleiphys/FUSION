# Uncertainty Quantification of the $^{76}$Ge Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay Nuclear Matrix Element

> The experimental pursuit of neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0νββ$) constitutes one of the most compelling avenues for probing lepton-number violation and exploring physics beyond the Standard Model. Within this landscape, $^{76}$Ge has consistently ranked among the most promising isotopes for current and next-generation bolometric and liquid-scintillator experiments, notably GERDA and LEGEND. In the present work, we adapt a rigorous statistical protocol previously established for $^{48}$Ca~\cite{Horoi-prc22} and $^{136}$Xe~\cite{Horoi-Xe-2023} to the $^{76}$Ge system, utilizing a valence configuration that aligns with our recent investigation of $^{82}$Se~\cite{Neacsu-Symmetry-2024}. Our methodology introduces systematic, bounded fluctuations to the two-body matrix elements of established effective interactions, subsequently monitoring how these perturbations propagate through a suite of low-energy nuclear observables. Special emphasis is placed on the $0νββ$ nuclear matrix element (NME), whose theoretical uncertainty currently dominates the interpretation of experimental half-life limits. By integrating these simulated variations into a Bayesian Model Averaging framework and benchmarking against empirical spectroscopic data, we derive a constrained probability distribution for the NME. The resulting analysis yields a central value of 2.46 with an associated standard deviation of 0.25, thereby quantifying the intrinsic theoretical spread within the interacting shell model approach. Furthermore, we perform a comprehensive correlation analysis across all computed observables to evaluate internal consistency, identify non-trivial structural dependencies, and establish benchmarks that may guide the refinement of future effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reports a central value of 2.46 for the $^{76}$Ge neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element with a standard deviation of 0.25, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of three baseline effective Hamiltonians in the $jj44$ valence space and a Bayesian Model Averaging weighting scheme that assigns 4/6 weight to GCN2850 and 1/6 each to JUN45 and JJ44b, rather than using the pure posterior weights of (1,0,0).

## Method

The authors perturbed the two-body matrix elements of three established effective Hamiltonians (GCN2850, JUN45, JJ44b) within a ±10% window, generating 200 perturbed Hamiltonians per interaction in the $jj44$ model space ($f_{5}pg_{9}$ orbitals above a $^{56}$Ni core). For each perturbed Hamiltonian, they computed twelve low-energy observables including the $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME, $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME, excitation energies, B(E2) strengths, and Gamow-Teller transition probabilities, then applied Bayesian Model Averaging to synthesize the three ensembles into a unified probability distribution for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME.

## Key numbers

- Central $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME value: 2.46 (dimensionless) with standard deviation 0.25, from Bayesian Model Averaging of three Hamiltonian ensembles
- 90% confidence interval for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME: [1.89, 3.07]
- Correlation coefficient between $M_{0\nu}$ and $M_{2\nu}$ exceeds 0.8 across all three Hamiltonian ensembles
- Perturbation amplitude for two-body matrix elements: ±10% window around original values
- Gamow-Teller quenching factor: $q=0.65$, standard in the $fp$ space
- Effective charges for B(E2) calculations: $e_p=1.5$ and $e_n=1.1$, as recommended for the $jj44$ valence space
- Experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ NME for $^{76}$Ge: 0.129 ± 0.001

## Context

This work extends a rigorous statistical uncertainty quantification protocol previously applied to $^{48}$Ca (Horoi 2022) and $^{136}$Xe (Horoi 2023) to the $^{76}$Ge system, using the same $jj44$ valence configuration as the authors' recent investigation of $^{82}$Se (Neacsu 2024). The resulting constrained NME distribution provides a theoretical prior for extracting neutrino mass limits from experimental half-life bounds from GERDA and LEGEND, and enables more accurate isotope-mass scaling for next-generation detectors. The paper contrasts the stability of shell-model predictions across different baseline interactions with the parameter sensitivity typical of pn-QRPA approaches (Engel and Menendez 2017).
