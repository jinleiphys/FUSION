# Event-by-event generation of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions

> In a noncentral heavy-ion collision, the two colliding nuclei have finite angular momentum in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane. After the collision, a fraction of the total angular momentum is retained in the produced hot quark-gluon matter and is manifested in the form of fluid shear. Such fluid shear creates finite flow vorticity. We study some features of such generated vorticity, including its strength, beam energy dependence, centrality dependence, and spatial distribution.

## Key claim
In noncentral heavy-ion collisions, the vorticity generated from the angular momentum of the colliding nuclei decreases with increasing collision energy, even though the total angular momentum increases, because the moment of inertia grows more rapidly and more angular momentum is carried by particles at finite rapidity.

## Method
The authors used the HIJING event generator to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions event-by-event, defining two types of velocity fields (particle flow velocity and energy flow velocity) from the generated particle momenta and positions. The vorticity was then computed using both a nonrelativistic definition and a relativistic definition, with spatial smearing via Gaussian wave packets. Results were averaged over 10^5 events for each collision configuration.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with impact parameter b=10 fm, the magnitude of the space-averaged y-component of vorticity at mid-rapidity decreases as sqrt(s) increases.
- The vorticity magnitude at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is such that T^2 omega ~ mu eB for temperature T~350 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu=10 MeV.
- The proper time was fixed at tau=0.4 fm and impact parameter b=10 fm for the energy dependence study.
- For impact parameter b less than approximately 2 R_A (where R_A is the nuclear radius), the vorticity increases with centrality.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of vorticity generation in heavy-ion collisions beyond earlier analytical estimates by providing event-by-event numerical results using the HIJING generator. It builds on prior work by Csernai (2013), Becattini (2015), and others who recognized that noncentral collisions produce finite flow vorticity. The results enable quantitative studies of related phenomena such as the chiral vortical effect, chiral vortical waves, and Lambda baryon spin polarization, and can be used as input for hydrodynamic simulations of these effects.
