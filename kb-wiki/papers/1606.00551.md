# JIMWLK evolution of the odderon

> We study the effects of a parity-odd "odderon" correlation in JIMWLK renormalization group evolution at high energy. Firstly we show that in the eikonal picture where the scattering is described by Wilson lines, one obtains a strict mathematical upper limit for the magnitude of the odderon amplitude compared to the parity even pomeron one. This limit increases with N_c, approaching infinity in the infinite N_c limit. We use a systematic extension of the Gaussian approximation including both 2- and 3-point correlations which enables us to close the system of equations even at finite N_c. In the large-N_c limit we recover an evolution equation derived earlier. By solving this equation numerically we confirm that the odderon amplitude decreases faster in the nonlinear case than in the linear BFKL limit. We also point out that, in the 3-point truncation at finite N_c, the presence of an odderon component introduces azimuthal angular correlations ~ cos(n phi) at all n.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the JIMWLK eikonal framework, the odderon amplitude has a strict mathematical upper limit relative to the pomeron amplitude arising from the SU(Nc) group structure of Wilson lines, and that this limit increases with Nc, approaching infinity in the infinite-Nc limit.

## Method

The authors systematically extend the Gaussian approximation to include both 2- and 3-point correlations, enabling closure of the evolution equations at finite Nc. They derive evolution equations for the odderon component from an exponential n-point truncation and solve these numerically with a further approximation to the lowest nontrivial cos(nθ) azimuthal harmonic. They complement this with full untruncated JIMWLK evolution using the Langevin framework for parity-even correlations.

## Key numbers

* For Nc=3, the odderon amplitude is constrained by κ ≤ 1/3 when requiring the average amplitude to stay within the group manifold, where κ parametrizes the ratio of odderon to pomeron characteristic scales.
* For the series parametrization e^{-r²Q₀²}(1+iκr³Q₀³/8), the amplitude stays within the triangular region allowed for linear combinations of SU(3) Wilson lines for κ ≤ 0.9867.
* The perturbative short-distance behavior gives P ∝ r² for the pomeron and O ∝ κ r³ for the odderon, with the r³ dependence being the mildest r-dependence allowed by the group theory constraint.
* For the exponential parametrization e^{-r²Q₀²/4+iκr³Q₀³/8}, the odderon peak size is severely limited by the bounds on κ.

## Context

This work extends earlier large-Nc results by Kovchegov (2003) and Hatta (2005) to finite Nc, providing evolution equations for the odderon amplitude in the exponential 3-point approximation. The paper builds on the McLerran-Venugopalan model and earlier work by Jeon (2005) and Dumitru (2011) on odderon correlations, while also connecting to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation framework. The results enable future phenomenological studies of observables sensitive to parity-odd correlations, such as the Single Transverse Spin Asymmetry mechanism proposed by Kovchegov and Sievert (2013).
