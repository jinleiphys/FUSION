# Algebraic models of hadron structure: I. Nonstrange baryons

> We introduce an algebraic framework for the description of baryons. Within this framework we study a collective string-like model and show that this model gives a good overall description of the presently available data. We discuss in particular masses and electromagnetic couplings, including the transition form factors that can be measured at new electron facilities.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an algebraic framework based on U(7) for describing baryon structure, and claims that a collective string-like model within this framework gives a good overall description of the presently available data on nonstrange baryon masses and electromagnetic couplings, though the authors note that the experimental mass spectrum is not known accurately enough to detect rotation-vibration couplings.

## Method

The authors construct a spectrum-generating algebra U(7) using bosonic quantization of the two Jacobi coordinates (ρ and λ) that describe the relative positions of three constituent parts in a string-like configuration, plus an auxiliary scalar boson. They expand the mass-squared operator into elements of U(7) that are invariant under the permutation group S₃, and diagonalize it in either a harmonic oscillator basis (U(3)⊗U(4) chain) or a Morse oscillator basis (U(3)⊗SO(4) chain). The spatial wave functions are combined with spin-flavor wave functions classified by SU(6)⊗SU(3)⊗SU(2) to enforce total antisymmetry of the baryon wave function.

## Key numbers

- The nonperturbative regime is defined as E <~ 3 GeV.
- Early quark model calculations used n_ρ+n_λ ≤ 2, corresponding to N=2 in U(7); more recent calculations used n_ρ+n_λ ≤ 6, corresponding to N=6.
- The nucleon wave function in harmonic oscillator quark models is still dominated by the n_ρ+n_λ=0 component, typically of order 80%.
- The vibrational eigenvalues to leading order in N are M²_vib = N[κ₁ n_u + κ₂(n_v+n_w)], where n_u,n_v,n_w are the eigenvalues of number operators for the three fundamental vibrations.
- The rotational eigenvalues are M²_rot = ξ₃ L(L+1) + ξ₄ K²_y, or alternatively M²_rot = α(L+1/2) + β|K_y| for linear Regge trajectories.
- The Casimir operator eigenvalues for SU_sf(6) are 45 for the 56 representation (symmetric), 33 for the 70 (mixed symmetry), and 21 for the 20 (antisymmetric).

## Context

This work extends algebraic methods previously successful in nuclear physics (Interacting Boson Model) and molecular physics (vibron model) to baryon structure, providing an alternative to nonrelativistic valence quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1978) and relativized quark models (Capstick and Isgur, 1986). The framework enables systematic calculation of masses and electromagnetic transition form factors that can be measured at new electron facilities, and allows contrasting single-particle harmonic oscillator quark models with collective string-like models. The authors note that lattice calculations of the excitation spectrum of hadrons remain a daunting problem years away from solution.
