# Heavy quarkonium properties from Cornell potential using variational method and supersymmetric quantum mechanic

> Using the variational method and supersymmetric quantum mechanic we calculate in a approximate way eigenvalues, eigenfunctions and wave functions at origin of Cornell potential. We compare results with numerical solutions for heavy quarkonia $c\bar{c}$, $b\bar{b}$ y $b\bar{c}$.

## Key claim

Using a combination of the variational method and supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY QM), the authors obtain approximate analytical eigenvalues, eigenfunctions, and wave functions at the origin for the Cornell potential, with the hedge that the approximation works well for the ground state and first excited state but begins to disagree with exact numerical solutions for higher radial excitations.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear term) for S-wave states of heavy quarkonia by first using a trial wave function of the form \(U(r) = N r^{\gamma} e^{-a r^{b}}\) in a variational calculation for the ground state. They then construct supersymmetric partner potentials via SUSY QM, where the ground state of each partner potential corresponds to an excited state of the original Cornell potential, and apply the variational method iteratively to obtain energies and wave functions for the 1S, 2S, and 3S states. Wave functions at the origin are computed using two methods: Method 1 uses the expectation value of the derivative of the potential, and Method 2 evaluates the wave function directly at \(r=0\).

## Key numbers

*   For \(c\bar{c}\): ground state (1S) energy = 0.2578 GeV (exact numerical: 0.2575 GeV); 2S energy = 0.8096 GeV (exact: 0.8482 GeV); 3S energy = 1.1427 GeV (exact: 1.2720 GeV).
*   For \(b\bar{b}\): ground state (1S) energy = -0.1702 GeV (exact: -0.1704 GeV); 2S energy = 0.3579 GeV (exact: 0.4214 GeV); 3S energy = 0.5612 GeV (exact: 0.7665 GeV).
*   For \(b\bar{c}\): ground state (1S) energy = 0.1113 GeV (exact: 0.1110 GeV); 2S energy = 0.6324 GeV (exact: 0.6813 GeV); 3S energy = 0.9065 GeV (exact: 1.0686 GeV).
*   Wave function at origin for \(c\bar{c}\) 1S: Method 1 = 1.4384 GeV\(^{3/2}\) (exact: 1.4591 GeV\(^{3/2}\)), Method 2 = 1.2897 GeV\(^{3/2}\).
*   Wave function at origin for \(b\bar{b}\) 1S: Method 1 = 13.9824 GeV\(^{3/2}\) (exact: 14.1294 GeV\(^{3/2}\)), Method 2 = 13.0031 GeV\(^{3/2}\).
*   Wave function at origin for \(b\bar{c}\) 1S: Method 1 = 3.1486 GeV\(^{3/2}\) (exact: 3.1950 GeV\(^{3/2}\)), Method 2 = 2.8380 GeV\(^{3/2}\).
*   Cornell potential parameters used: \(\kappa = 0.52\), \(\beta = 1/(2.34)^2\) GeV\(^2\); quark masses \(\mu_c = 1.84\) GeV/\(c^2\), \(\mu_b = 5.18\) GeV/\(c^2\).

## Context

This work extends the approach suggested by Gozzi (1993) and Cooper (1995) that combines variational methods with SUSY QM to solve the Schrödinger equation for potentials without analytical solutions, here specifically applied to the Cornell potential for heavy quarkonia. The results are compared with exact numerical solutions obtained using the MATHEMATICA program mathschroe.nb by Lucha (1998). The analytical wave functions obtained are orthogonal and can be used to compute other heavy quarkonium properties, with the method being complementary to WKB for higher radial excitations.
