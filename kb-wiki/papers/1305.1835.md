# Parity violating quantum kinetic theory in (2+1)-dimensions

> We study the kinetic theory for a (2+1)-dimensional fermionic system with special emphasis on the parity violating properties associated with the fermion mass. The Wigner function approach is used to derive hydrodynamical transport coefficients to the first spatial derivative order. As a first attempt, the collisions between fermions are neglected. The resulting system is dissipationless. The parity violating Hall electric conductivity has the same temperature and chemical potential dependence as the quantum field theory result at one-loop. Vorticity dependent transport properties, which were not considered before, also emerge naturally in this approach.

## Key claim

The paper derives parity-violating transport coefficients in (2+1)-dimensional fermionic systems using a quantum kinetic theory approach based on the Wigner function, but only in the collisionless, dissipationless limit where interactions between fermions are neglected and the electromagnetic background field is treated as constant.

## Method

The authors expand the Wigner function in powers of the Knudsen number (gradient expansion) for a (2+1)-dimensional fermionic system with mass m and charge Q, solving the Dirac equation for the Wigner function order by order in space-time derivatives. They decompose the Wigner function into scalar and vector components using the Clifford algebra in (2+1)D, then integrate over momentum to obtain the fermion number current, energy-momentum tensor, and entropy current. The vacuum contribution to the current is matched to the known Chern-Simons theory result from quantum field theory at one-loop.

## Key numbers

- The Hall electric conductivity coefficient is given by ξ_B = Q sign(m)[c_+(m) - 1/(4π)], where c_±(m) = (1/(4π))[1/(e^{β(|m|-μ)}+1) ± 1/(e^{β(|m|+μ)}+1)]
- At zero temperature, the Hall conductivity coefficient reduces to ξ_B = -(Q/(4π)) sign(m) θ(m² - μ²)
- The vorticity transport coefficient at zero temperature is ξ = (1/(4π)) sign(μ) m θ(μ² - m²)
- The fermion number density is n = (1/(2π)) ∫_{|m|}^{∞} dE_p E_p [1/(e^{β(E_p-μ)}+1) - 1/(e^{β(E_p+μ)}+1)]
- The energy density is ε = (1/(2π)) ∫_{|m|}^{∞} dE_p E_p² [1/(e^{β(E_p-μ)}+1) + 1/(e^{β(E_p+μ)}+1)]
- The pressure is P = (1/(4π)) ∫_{|m|}^{∞} dE_p (E_p² - m²) [1/(e^{β(E_p-μ)}+1) + 1/(e^{β(E_p+μ)}+1)]

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum kinetic theory for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in (3+1) dimensions (Gao et al., 2012; Chen et al., 2012) to (2+1) dimensions, where the fermion mass explicitly breaks parity. The results reproduce the parity-violating Hall conductivity from Chern-Simons theory (Niemi, 1983; Redlich, 1984) and are consistent with the entropy principle analysis of parity-violating hydrodynamics in (2+1)D by Jensen et al. (2011). The approach enables future studies of dissipative effects by including collisions and provides a kinetic theory foundation for transport phenomena in (2+1)-dimensional systems such as graphene.
