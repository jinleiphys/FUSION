# Breakdown of the tensor component in the Skyrme energy density functional

> The tensor force, as an important component of strong nuclear force, generates a variety of intriguing effects ranging from few-body systems to neutron stars. It is responsible for the nucleon-nucleon correlation beyond mean-field approximation, and is accordingly proved to play no role in the standard Skyrme energy density functionals in the present work. Therefore, the Skyrme's original tensor interaction that is extensively-employed presently is invalid. As an alternative strategy, we introduced a central interaction, i.e., the $\bm{σ}_{1}\cdot \bm{σ}_{2}$ term, to improve the description of experimental single-particle structure, and to address its effect, we established two Skyrme interactions IMP1 and IMP2 complemented by the calibrated charge-violating interactions. The central $\bm{σ}_{1}\cdot \bm{σ}_{2}$ interaction turns out to substantially improve the description of shell evolution in Sn isotopes and $N=82$ isotones.

## Key claim
The tensor force, as a well-defined component of the strong nuclear force responsible for deuteron binding and nucleon-nucleon correlation, plays no role in standard Skyrme energy density functionals because its contributions either vanish exactly, are of too high order in momentum, or can be reabsorbed into existing Skyrme terms; therefore the widely-used zero-range tensor interaction in Skyrme functionals is invalid.

## Method
The authors derived the tensor force in momentum space via partial-wave expansion of the Fourier transform of a realistic tensor potential, and evaluated each partial-wave channel's contribution to the Skyrme-type interaction. They then introduced a central spin-spin (σ₁·σ₂) interaction as a replacement, and calibrated two new Skyrme parameter sets (IMP1 and IMP2) using chi-square minimization against empirical nuclear matter properties, ground-state observables of closed-shell nuclei, the equation of state of pure neutron matter from Akmal et al., and charge-symmetry breaking interactions from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key numbers
- The diagonal matrix element V_T(k',k)(³S₁-³S₁) is exactly zero; the ³D₁-³D₁ diagonal element is of order k⁴, beyond standard Skyrme interactions.
- IMP1 and IMP2 both yield saturation density ρ₀ = 0.160 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon B/A = -16.0 MeV, and incompressibility K∞ = 236 MeV (IMP1) and 239 MeV (IMP2).
- Symmetry energy J = 32.1 MeV (IMP1) and 31.8 MeV (IMP2); slope L = 44 MeV (IMP1) and 43 MeV (IMP2).
- Maximum neutron star mass M_max = 2.1 M⊙ for both forces; radius for a 1.4 M⊙ canonical neutron star R = 11.7 km (IMP1) and 11.8 km (IMP2).
- Neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb ΔR_np = 0.15 fm (IMP1) and 0.14 fm (IMP2); symmetry energy coefficient a_sym(²⁰⁸Pb) = 23.9 MeV for both.
- rms deviation of binding energy: 1.2 MeV (IMP1) and 1.3 MeV (IMP2); charge rms radius deviation: 0.035 fm (IMP1) and 0.037 fm (IMP2).
- For the mirror pair ⁴⁸Ni-⁴⁸Ca, the Coulomb displacement energy is 67.00 MeV (IMP1) and 67.30 MeV (IMP2), compared to the AME2016 value of 67.28(48) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Stancu et al. and the revived interest in tensor forces within Skyrme functionals (e.g., Otsuka et al., SO1-SO4) by demonstrating that the tensor component cannot be consistently included in standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations. The authors build on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with the AV18 interaction and the pure neutron matter equation of state of Akmal et al. (1998). The new IMP1 and IMP2 interactions enable improved description of shell evolution in Sn isotopes and N=82 isotones, but the authors caution that the parameter sets may not be valid for excited states such as Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole states, and that including tensor-force-induced correlations beyond mean-field remains an open problem.
