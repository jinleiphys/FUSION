# Polyakov SU(3) extended linear $σ$-model: Sixteen mesonic states in chiral phase-structure

> The derivative of the grand potential in mean field approximation, non-strange and strange condensates and deconfinement phase-transition in thermal and dense hadronic medium are verified in extended SU(3) linear sigma-model (eLSM). In determining the chiral phase-transition, the chiral condensates sigma_x and sigma_y are analysed. The chiral mesonic phase-structures in temperature- and density-dependence are taken as free parameters to be fitted. These parameters are classified corresponding to scalar meson nonets; (pseudo)-scalar and (axial)-vector. For deconfinement phase-transition, effective Polyakov loop-potentials phi and phi^* are utilized. We investigated the in-medium effects on the masses of sixteen mesonic states states. The results are presented for two different forms for the effective Polyakov loop-potential and compared with other models with and without anomalous terms. The Polyakov loop potential in LSM has considerable effects on the chiral phase-transition in meson masses so that the restoration of the chiral symmetry becomes sharper and faster than LSM. We normalize all mesonic states with respect to the lowest Matsubara frequency. It has been found that the various mesonic states have different dissolving temperatures and chemical potentials, i.e. they survive the typically-averaged QCD phase boundary, defined by the QCD critical temperatures with varying chemical potentials. The thermal behavior of all meson masses has been investigated in the large-$N_c$ limit and found that the scalar meson masses are T-independent at large $N_c$ and high $T$ (except pi and sigma). For the pseudoscalar meson masses, the large N_c limit unifies the $T$-dependence of all states in a universal bundle. The same is also observed for axial and axialvector meson masses in the large-N_c limit.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop potential in the linear sigma model (PLSM) sharpens and accelerates the restoration of chiral symmetry compared to the standard LSM without the Polyakov loop, and the sixteen mesonic states each have distinct dissolving temperatures and chemical potentials, meaning they survive the typically-averaged QCD phase boundary defined by QCD critical temperatures at varying chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors extended the SU(3) linear sigma model (LSM) by coupling it to the Polyakov-loop potential, which encodes the deconfinement dynamics from pure Yang-Mills lattice simulations. They minimized the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation with respect to the non-strange and strange chiral condensates (σ_x and σ_y) and the Polyakov-loop fields (φ and φ*), solving the resulting equations of motion at finite temperature and baryon-chemical potential. The masses of sixteen mesonic states (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector) were then computed from the curvature of the effective potential in the thermal and dense medium.

## Key numbers

* The chiral restoration temperature for the non-strange condensate is T_c^q ~ 181 MeV at vanishing baryon-chemical potential.
* The chiral restoration temperature for the strange condensate is T_c^s ~ 270 MeV at vanishing baryon-chemical potential.
* At zero temperature, the chiral condensates are σ_x0 = 92.4 MeV and σ_y0 = 94.5 MeV.
* The light constituent quark mass is m_l = 300 MeV (with coupling g = 6.5), and the strange constituent quark mass is m_s ~ 433 MeV at zero temperature and vanishing chemical potential.
* At a fixed temperature T = 10 MeV, the critical baryon-chemical potential for the chiral transition is μ = 360 MeV.
* The deconfinement temperature in the pure gauge sector is T_0 = 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the SU(3) linear sigma model at finite temperature (Lenaghan 2000, Petropoulos 1998) and the Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model (Costa 2009) by including vector and axial-vector meson fields alongside the scalar and pseudoscalar nonets. It also builds on lattice QCD results for pseudoscalar and vector meson states from the HotQCD and PACS-CS collaborations. The results enable systematic determination of the critical temperature and density at which each individual meson state dissolves into free quarks, refining the QCD phase diagram beyond a single averaged phase boundary.
