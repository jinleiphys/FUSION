# Non-Perturbative Gluon pair production from a Constant Chromo-Electric Field via the Schwinger Mechanism in Arbitrary Gauge

> We study the non-perturbative production of gluon pairs from a constant SU(3) chromo-electric background field via the Schwinger mechanism. We fix the covariant background gauge with an arbitrary gauge parameter α. We determine the transverse momentum distribution of the gluons, as well as the total probability of creating pairs per unit space time volume. We find that the result is independent of the covariant gauge parameter αused to define arbitrary covariant background gauges. We find that our non-perturbative result is both gauge invariant and gauge parameter αindependent.

# Non-Perturbative Gluon Pair Production from a Constant Chromo-Electric Field via the Schwinger Mechanism in Arbitrary Gauge

## Key claim

The non-perturbative gluon pair production rate and transverse momentum distribution from a constant SU(3) chromo-electric background field, computed via the Schwinger mechanism in covariant background gauge, are independent of the arbitrary gauge parameter α and therefore both gauge invariant and gauge parameter independent. This result holds specifically for the one-loop effective action with a constant chromo-electric field along the z-axis in the gauge A₀ᵃ = 0.

## Method

The authors use the background field method of QCD, splitting the gauge field into a classical background field Aμᵃ and a quantum gluon field Qμᵃ, with covariant background gauge fixing Dμ[A]Qμᵃ = 0 containing an arbitrary gauge parameter α. They compute the one-loop effective action via the vacuum-to-vacuum transition amplitude expressed as a functional determinant of the operator Mμνᵃᵇ[A] for gluons, plus a ghost contribution. The gauge parameter α dependence is isolated by writing Mμνᵃᵇ[A] = Mμνᵃᵇ|α=1[A] + α′[Dμ(A)Dν(A)]ᵃᵇ with α′ = (1/α − 1), and the trace of the α-dependent term is evaluated using operator identities and cyclic properties of the trace, showing cancellation between the interacting and free parts.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum distribution for gluon pair production per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum is given by: dN_{g,g}/(dt d³x d²p_T) = (1/4π³) Σⱼ₌₁³ |gλⱼ| Ln[1 + exp(−π p_T²/|gλⱼ|)]
- The eigenvalues λⱼ² depend on two Casimir/gauge invariants of SU(3): C₁ = EᵃEᵃ and C₂ = [d_abc EᵃEᵇEᶜ]²
- λ₁² = (C₁/2)[1 − cos θ], λ₂² = (C₁/2)[1 + cos(π/3 − θ)], λ₃² = (C₁/2)[1 + cos(π/3 + θ)]
- cos³θ = −1 + 6C₂/C₁³
- The α-dependent trace contribution evaluates to 8 Ln[1 + α′] ∫ d⁴x = −8 Ln[α] ∫ d⁴x, which cancels exactly between interacting and free parts

## Context

This work extends the calculation of Nayak and van Nieuwenhuizen (2005) for gluon pair production in the background Feynman-t'Hooft gauge (α = 1) to arbitrary covariant gauge parameter α, proving that the α = 1 result is the correct gauge invariant and α-independent result. It parallels earlier work by Nayak (2005) on quark-antiquark production, which was trivially gauge parameter independent. The result enables future studies of the back reaction problem with time- and space-dependent background fields obeying generalized Yang-Mills equations, analogous to the approach of Cooper and Mottola for the electric field problem in QED.
