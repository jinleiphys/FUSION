# $ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$ and $ŽĆ^0 ŽĆ^0$ pair production in photon-photon and in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We discuss $╬│╬│\to ŽĆŽĆ$ reactions, starting from the two-pion threshold up to about $\sqrt{s_{╬│╬│}}$ = 6 GeV. We include the dipion continuum due to pion exchange (for $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$) and $Žü^\pm$ exchange (for $ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$) as well as the dipion $Žā(600)$, $f_0(980)$ and $f_2(1270)$ s-channel resonances. We discuss also a possible contribution of less pronounced scalar resonances $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ as well as the tensor resonances $f'_2(1525)$, $f_2(1565)$ and $f_2(1950)$. We find that the inclusion of the spin-four $f_4(2050)$ resonant state improves the situation at $\sqrt{s_{╬│╬│}} \approx$ 2 GeV. We estimate the relevant diphoton partial decay width to be $╬ō_{f_4 \to ╬│╬│} \approx$ 0.7 keV. At higher energies, Brodsky-Lepage and hand-bag mechanisms are included in addition. We nicely describe the world data for $╬│╬│\to ŽĆŽĆ$ for the first time both for the total cross section and angular distributions. The cross section for the production of two-pions in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is calculated in the impact parameter space Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA). We obtain the nuclear cross section of 46.7 mb for $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ and 8.7 mb for $ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$ at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 3.5 TeV and minimal cut on pion transverse momentum $p_t>$ 0.2 GeV. Differential distributions in impact parameter, dipion invariant mass, single pion and dipion rapidity, pion transverse momentum as well as pion pseudorapidity are shown. The $╬│╬│\to ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$ subprocess constitutes a background to $A A \to A Žü^0 (\to ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-) A$ process.

## Key claim

The authors construct a hybrid multi-component model for $\gamma\gamma\to\pi\pi$ reactions that simultaneously describes both charged and neutral pion pair production data from threshold to $\sqrt{s_{\gamma\gamma}}\approx 6$ GeV, but the description relies on several free parameters (resonance widths, form-factor cutoffs, and a smooth switching function for perturbative QCD contributions) rather than being a fully predictive first-principles calculation.

## Method

The elementary $\gamma\gamma\to\pi\pi$ cross section is built from: (i) a Born-term continuum for $\pi^+\pi^-$ with finite-size pion form factors; (ii) a coupled-channel $\rho^\pm$-exchange mechanism for $\pi^0\pi^0$; (iii) s-channel scalar ($\sigma(600)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_0(1500)$, $f_0(1710)$), tensor ($f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, $f_2(1565)$, $f_2(1950)$), and spin-4 ($f_4(2050)$) resonances with energy-dependent widths and Blatt-Weisskopf form factors; and (iv) Brodsky-Lepage and hand-bag perturbative QCD mechanisms at high energies, suppressed at low energies by an empirical switching function. The nuclear cross section for ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions is then obtained by folding the elementary cross section with equivalent photon fluxes in impact-parameter space, imposing an absorptive cut at $b > 2R_A$.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear cross section for $PbPb\to Pb(\pi^+\pi^-)Pb$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.5$ TeV with $p_t>0.2$ GeV: 46.7 mb
* Nuclear cross section for $PbPb\to Pb(\pi^0\pi^0)Pb$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.5$ TeV with $p_t>0.2$ GeV: 8.7 mb
* Estimated diphoton partial decay width for $f_4(2050)$: $\Gamma_{f_4\to\gamma\gamma}\approx 0.7$ keV
* Best-fit parameters for $\sigma(600)$: $\Gamma_R=400$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}=0.5$ keV (compared to PDG ranges $\Gamma_R=600-1000$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}=1.2-10$ keV)
* Best-fit product $\frac{\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}\Gamma_{\pi\pi}}{\Gamma_R}$ for $f_4(2050)$: 0.12 keV (compared to Belle value 0.0231 keV and Oest upper limit $<1.1$ keV)
* Energy range covered: from two-pion threshold up to $\sqrt{s_{\gamma\gamma}}\approx 6$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of high-mass dipion production (K┼éusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2013) by including low-energy resonances and continuum mechanisms, and updates the earlier model of Szczurek (2003) with many more resonances and new Belle data (Belle Collaboration, 2009). The nuclear cross-section predictions are the first realistic estimates for dipion production in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and the $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ subprocess is identified as a background to the exclusive $AA\to A\rho^0(\to\pi^+\pi^-)A$ process initiated by photon-pomeron exchange. The results enable quantitative separation of resonant signals (including glueball candidates $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$) from the continuum in both elementary and nuclear reactions.
