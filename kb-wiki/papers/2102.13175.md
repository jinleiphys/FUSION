# Test of the nonrelativistic $c\bar{c}$ potential

> We analyze the charmonium states by testing a phenomenological nonrelativistic potential and propose a new set of parameters. This new set of parameters are fixed using only the lowest lying S-wave states of charmonia where the spin-orbit and tensor interactions will not contribute. After fitting the parameters we analyze the whole fine structure of charmonium states taking into account the spin-orbit and tensor interactions too. Calculations showed that the nonrelativistic potential model with the phenomenologically defined parameters is indeed well approximation for describing the charmonium states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a nonrelativistic potential model with only four parameters, fitted exclusively to the four lowest S-wave charmonium states (where spin-orbit and tensor interactions do not contribute), can reproduce the entire fine structure of charmonium states as well as or better than models fitted to eleven states across multiple orbital angular momentum channels.

## Method

The author used a nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with a "Coulomb+linear" central potential plus spin-dependent terms derived from nonrelativistic expansion of vector one-gluon-exchange and scalar confinement. The four potential parameters (charm quark mass m_c, strong coupling alpha_s, string tension kappa, and smearing parameter sigma) were fitted using only the 1^1S_0, 2^1S_0, 1^3S_1, and 2^3S_1 charmonium states as input. The Schrodinger equation was solved numerically using the Gaussian expansion method with 30-50 basis functions and the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.4796 GeV (NR4 model)
- Strong coupling alpha_s = 0.5426 (NR4 model)
- String tension kappa = 0.1444 GeV^2 (NR4 model)
- Smearing parameter sigma = 1.1510 GeV (NR4 model)
- J/psi(1^3S_1) reproduced at 3098 MeV vs experimental 3096.900 +/- 0.006 MeV (2 MeV difference)
- eta_c(1^1S_0) reproduced at 2984 MeV vs experimental 2983.9 +/- 0.5 MeV (0.1 MeV difference)
- psi(2^3S_1) reproduced at 3682 MeV vs experimental 3686.097 +/- 0.025 MeV (4 MeV difference)
- eta_c(2^1S_0) reproduced at 3638 MeV vs experimental 3637.6 +/- 1.2 MeV (0.4 MeV difference)
- chi_c0(1^3P_0) reproduced at 3415 MeV vs experimental 3414.71 +/- 0.30 MeV (0.3 MeV difference)
- chi_c2(2^3P_2) reproduced at 3978 MeV vs experimental 3927.2 +/- 2.6 MeV (51 MeV difference)
- psi(3^3S_1) reproduced at 4084 MeV vs experimental 4039 +/- 1 MeV (45 MeV difference)

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic potential model approach of Barnes (2005), who fitted parameters to eleven charmonium states, by testing whether a minimal fit to only four S-wave states can adequately describe the full spectrum. The author argues that the nonrelativistic expansion relating central and spin-dependent potentials (Eichten, 1980) is validated by the success of this minimal fitting approach. The results enable future work to use the same parameterization for predicting unobserved charmonium states and for testing the applicability of nonrelativistic potential models to other heavy quarkonium systems.
