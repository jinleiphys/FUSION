# Gaussian characterization of the unitary window for $N=3$: bound, scattering and virtual states

> The three-body system inside the unitary window is studied for three equal bosons and three equal fermions having $1/2$ spin-isospin symmetry. We perform a gaussian characterization of the window using a gaussian potential to define trajectories for low-energy quantities as binding energies and phase shifts. On top of this trajectories experimental values are placed or, when not available, quantities calculated using realistic potentials that are known to reproduce experimental values. The intention is to show that the gaussian characterization of the window, thought as a contact interaction plus range corrections, captures the main low-energy properties of real systems as for example three helium atoms or three nucleons. The mapping of real systems on the gaussian trajectories is taken as indication of universal behavior. The trajectories continuously link the physical points to the unitary limit allowing for the explanation of strong correlations between observables appearing in real systems and which are known to exist in that limit. In the present study we focus on low-energy bound, scattering and virtual states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple Gaussian potential, characterized by a single range parameter \(r_0\), can quantitatively capture the low-energy universal properties of three-body systems (bosons and fermions) inside the unitary window, including bound states, scattering, and virtual states, with finite-range corrections absorbed into the Gaussian parametrization. The hedge is that this mapping works for the specific low-energy observables studied and that the Gaussian characterization is intended as a low-energy representation, not a replacement for realistic interactions at higher energies.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for three equal bosons and three equal fermions (with 1/2 spin-isospin symmetry) interacting via a Gaussian potential, varying the potential strength to scan the unitary window. They computed binding energies, phase shifts, atom-dimer scattering lengths, and virtual-state pole positions using two complementary methods: the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas version of Faddeev equations in momentum space, and hyperspherical harmonic expansion with Rayleigh-Ritz (bound states) and Kohn (scattering) variational principles. Real systems (helium trimers and three-nucleon systems) were placed on the resulting universal trajectories by matching their experimental or realistic-potential values of scattering length, binding energy, and effective range.

## Key numbers

*   For three equal bosons at the unitary limit, the dimensionless binding momenta are \(\kappa_*^{(0)} r_0 = 0.4883\) (ground state) and \(\kappa_*^{(1)} r_0 = 0.02125\) (first excited state), with ratio \(\kappa_*^{(0)}/\kappa_*^{(1)} = 22.98\).
*   For the helium trimer (LM2M2 potential), the Gaussian ranges extracted are \(r_0^{(0)} = 11.15\;a_0\) (ground state) and \(r_0^{(1)} = 11.70\;a_0\) (first excited state), predicting unitary-limit energies \(E_*^{(0)} = 83.0\;\text{mK}\) and \(E_*^{(1)} = 0.157\;\text{mK}\).
*   The Gaussian potential predicts the threshold scattering lengths for the helium trimer as \(a_-^{(0)} \approx -48.7\;a_0\) and \(a_-^{(1)} \approx -842\;a_0\), compared to \(-48.2\;a_0\) and \(-832\;a_0\) from Hiyama (2014).
*   The product \(a_-^{(0)}\kappa_*^{(0)} = -2.14\) for the ground state and \(a_-^{(1)}\kappa_*^{(1)} = -1.56\) for the first excited state; the latter is close to the zero-range limit value of 1.507.
*   For the helium trimer, the Gaussian potential predicts the atom-dimer scattering length \(a_{AD} = 217\;a_0\), compared to the LM2M2 value of \(218.4\;a_0\) (Carbonell, 2005), a 1% error.
*   For three nucleons, the physical triton point has \(\kappa_3 a_B = \tan\xi = 1.95\) and \(r_0/a_B = 0.457\), yielding the doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length \({}^2a_{nd} = 0.4\;\text{fm}\) (experimental value is approximately 0.65 fm).
*   The Gaussian potential predicts the three-nucleon system at unitarity has energy \(E_* \approx 2.5\;\text{MeV}\), in agreement with previous estimates by Epelbaum (2009) and Gattobigio (2019).

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian characterization of the unitary window previously applied to discrete energy spectra (Kievsky 2016, Gattobigio 2019) to include continuum states, scattering, and virtual states. It builds on the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970, 1971) and the zero-range universal function parametrization (Gattobigio 2019), showing that a single Gaussian range parameter can simultaneously describe two-body and three-body observables for both helium trimers and three-nucleon systems. The results enable a unified understanding of the Phillips line (Phillips 1968) and the near-zero value of the doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length, and they provide a framework for predicting virtual-state positions that can be tested against experimental neutron-deuteron scattering data.
