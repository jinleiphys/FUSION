# Coupled-channel effective field theory and proton-$^7$Li scattering

> We apply the renormalisation group (RG) to analyse scattering by short-range forces in systems with coupled channels. For two S-wave channels, we find three fixed points, corresponding to systems with zero, one or two bound or virtual states at threshold. We use the RG to determine the power countings for the resulting effective field theories. In the case of a single low-energy state, the resulting theory takes the form of an effective-range expansion in the strongly interacting channel. We also extend the analysis to include the effects of the Coulomb interaction between charged particles. The approach is then applied to the coupled $p+{^7}$Li and $n+{^7}$Be channels which couple to a $J^P=2^-$ state of $^8$Be very close to the $n+{^7}$Be threshold. At next-to-leading order, we are able to get a good description of the $p+{^7}$Li phase shift and the ${^7}$Be(n,p)${^7}$Li cross section using four parameters. Fits at one order higher are similarly good but the available data are not sufficient to determine all five parameters uniquely.

## Key claim

The paper develops a coupled-channel effective field theory (EFT) for systems with short-range forces and Coulomb interactions, finding that a fixed point with a single virtual state at threshold is the most physically relevant because it requires fine-tuning of only one quantity, unlike previous approaches that required fine-tuning two parameters.

## Method

The authors used renormalisation group (RG) analysis to find fixed points for two S-wave coupled channels with short-range forces, then expanded the potential around the fixed point with a single low-energy state to determine power counting. They extended the analysis to include Coulomb interactions and applied the resulting EFT to the coupled \(p+{}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) and \(n+{}^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) channels, fitting the scattering parameters to experimental phase shift and cross section data at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Key numbers

* The \(n+{}^{7}\mathrm{Be}\) threshold lies at \(\Delta = 1.6442\) MeV, with a \(J^P = 2^-\) state of \({}^{8}\mathrm{Be}\) within a few keV of this threshold.
* The thermal neutron cross section for \({}^{7}\mathrm{Be}(n,p){}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) is \(\sigma \simeq 38.4 \times 10^3\) b.
* The momentum scale corresponding to the threshold difference is \(\delta = 51.95\) MeV, and the inverse Bohr radius for the \(p+{}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) channel is \(\kappa_1 = 17.96\) MeV.
* At LO, the fitted scattering length is \(a_1 = -17.76\) fm and the mixing angle is \(\phi = 46.63^\circ\).
* The low-energy parameters \(a_2\), \(r_0\), and \(r_1\) are of natural size for an effective theory with an underlying length scale of approximately 2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the single-channel RG analysis of Birse (1998) to coupled-channel systems and provides an alternative to the \(R\)-matrix approach widely used for reactions with resonant or virtual states. The EFT framework developed here can describe near-threshold states in nuclear and hadronic physics, such as the \(X(3872)\) studied by Hanhart et al. (2011), and the application to \(p+{}^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) scattering demonstrates that the available data are insufficient to uniquely determine parameters beyond NLO, leaving open the need for higher-precision measurements.
