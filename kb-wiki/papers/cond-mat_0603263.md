# Mismatch Induced Type Transition of a Superconductor

> We use the Ginzburg-Landau theory near the transition temperature in order to examine the behavior of an inhomogeneous superconductor in the presence of a magnetic field. We find that a transition from type I to type II superconductivity occurs. Furthermore we calculate the critical value of the mismatch and estimate the contributions of the fluctuations of the condensate and the higher order terms in the Ginzburg-Landau framework.

## Key claim

Near the transition temperature, a type I superconductor with zero Fermi-momentum mismatch can evolve into a type II superconductor beyond a critical value of the mismatch parameter δ/Δ₀, contradicting the conventional belief that type II superconductivity is associated with stronger pairing forces. The crossover is predicted within Ginzburg-Landau theory but may be marginally described by that framework, and does not extend to zero temperature.

## Method

The authors derive the Ginzburg-Landau free energy density for a nonrelativistic electronic superconductor with a mismatch δ between the Fermi surfaces of spin-up and spin-down electrons, starting from a mean-field Hamiltonian with an inhomogeneous condensate. They expand the thermodynamic potential to powers of the order parameter φ and its gradient, obtaining the GL coefficients α, b, and c as functions of δ, and then compute the GL parameter κ = 1/(m_A ξ) from the Meissner mass and coherence length. The robustness of the predicted type transition is assessed via the Ginzburg criterion for condensate fluctuations and by estimating higher-order corrections (Δ⁶ term) to the GL expansion.

## Key numbers

- The LOFF threshold occurs at δ_LOFF ≃ 0.608 Δ₀ at the transition temperature and δ'_LOFF ≃ Δ₀/√2 at T=0.
- At the LOFF threshold, g(δ) ≃ 0.140 and T_δ ≃ 0.562 T₀.
- The GL parameter κ diverges at δ_LOFF because f(δ) vanishes there; the critical value separating type I (κ < 1/√2) from type II (κ > 1/√2) is κ_c = 1/√2.
- For aluminum (Al): T₀ = 1.18 K, μ = 1.35×10⁵ K, v_F = 2.02×10⁸ cm/s; the left-hand side of the validity condition is ~4.9×10⁻⁵ and the right-hand side is ~2.0×10⁻⁴.
- For lead (Pb): T₀ = 7.19 K, μ = 1.09×10⁵ K, v_F = 1.82×10⁸ cm/s; the left-hand side is ~4.4×10⁻⁵ and the right-hand side is ~7.9×10⁻⁴.
- The magnetic field needed for Zeeman splitting with δ ≃ δ_LOFF is approximately (1.76×10⁴ / g) G, where g is the gyromagnetic ratio.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau treatment of superconductors with Fermi-surface mismatch, building on prior studies of the LOFF state (Takada, 1970) and the Sarma/breached-pairing states for cold atoms and quark matter (Wilczek, 2003; Huang, 2004; Bedaque, 2003). The authors find that the GL parameter κ increases with mismatch and diverges at the LOFF threshold, enabling a type-I to type-II crossover near T_c that does not persist to zero temperature. This prediction opens the possibility of observing the transition in superconductors with ferromagnetic impurities, where the exchange field can produce a significant mismatch without destroying superconductivity, and suggests that the conventional association of type II behavior with strong coupling may need revision.
