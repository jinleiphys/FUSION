# Comment on Neutron-Proton Spin-Correlation Parameter A_{ZZ} at 68 Mev

> We present two arguments indicating that the large value for the $ε_1$ mixing parameter at 50 MeV, which the Basel group extracted from their recent $A_{zz}$ measurement, may be incorrect. First, there are nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials which predict the $ε_1$ at 50 MeV substantially below the Basel value and reproduce the Basel $A_{zz}$ data accurately. Second, the large value for $ε_1$ at 50 MeV proposed by the Basel group can only be explained by a model for the NN interaction which is very unrealistic (no $ρ$-meson and essentially a point-like $πNN$ vertex) and overpredicts the $ε_1$ in the energy range where it is well determined (150--500 MeV) by a factor of two.

# Comment on Neutron-Proton Spin-Correlation Parameter A_{ZZ} at 68 MeV

## Key claim

The large value of 2.9° ± 0.3° for the ε₁ mixing parameter at 50 MeV extracted by the Basel group from their A_zz measurement is likely incorrect, because realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials that predict ε₁ substantially lower (≈ 1.5°) reproduce the Basel A_zz data accurately, and the only model that can explain the large Basel value is unrealistic (lacking ρ-meson exchange and using a point-like πNN vertex).

## Method

The authors compare the Basel A_zz data at 67.5 MeV with predictions from four established nucleon-nucleon potentials (Reid, Nijmegen, Paris, Bonn A), each with a different ε₁ value at 50 MeV, and evaluate the χ² per datum for each fit. They also examine the consistency of these potentials with world data on the A_yy observable at 50 MeV, the np differential cross section at backward angles at 50 MeV from the Karlsruhe group, and the well-determined ε₁ values from phase shift analyses in the 150–500 MeV energy range.

## Key numbers

- The Basel group extracted ε₁ = 2.9° ± 0.3° at 50 MeV from their A_zz measurement at 67.5 MeV.
- The Bonn A potential predicts ε₁ = 1.55° at 50 MeV and fits the Basel A_zz data with χ²/datum = 1.72.
- The Paris potential predicts ε₁ = 1.89° at 50 MeV and fits the Basel A_zz data with χ²/datum = 1.55.
- The Nijmegen potential predicts ε₁ = 2.27° at 50 MeV and fits the Basel A_zz data with χ²/datum = 47.7.
- The Reid potential predicts ε₁ = 2.36° at 50 MeV and fits the Basel A_zz data with χ²/datum = 116.7.
- Paris and Bonn A predict the ¹P₁ phase shift at 50 MeV as -10.95° and -11.05°, respectively; the Basel group uses -9.4°.
- Paris and Bonn A fit the Karlsruhe np backward-angle differential cross section data at 50 MeV with χ²/datum = 0.2 and 0.8, respectively.
- Paris and Bonn A fit the world A_yy data at 50 MeV with χ²/datum = 1.37 and 1.27, respectively.
- Paris and Bonn A fit all world np observables at 50 MeV with χ²/datum = 1.6 and 1.4, respectively.
- A new phase-shift analysis including the Basel data reports ε₁ = 2.2° ± 0.5° at 50 MeV.
- The unrealistic model (no ρ-meson, point-like πNN vertex with cutoff mass 10 GeV) overpredicts ε₁ in the 150–500 MeV range by a factor of two.

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the result of the Basel group (Ham91), who reported a large ε₁ from their A_zz measurement at 67.5 MeV. The authors show that established potentials such as Paris (Lac80) and Bonn A (Mac89) fit the Basel data better than the potentials with larger ε₁, contradicting the Basel claim. The work also extends the long-standing effort to determine ε₁ at low energies, noting that recent phase shift analyses (Bug90, Arn93) agree well on ε₁ in the 150–500 MeV range, and that a new analysis (KSS92) including the Basel data yields a lower ε₁ value consistent with the authors' argument. This enables a more reliable determination of the ³S₁–³D₁ mixing parameter at low energies, which is critical for understanding the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
