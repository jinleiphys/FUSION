# Cutoff regulators in chiral nuclear effective field theory

> Three-dimensional cutoff regulators are frequently employed in multi-nucleon calculations, but they violate chiral symmetry and Lorentz invariance. A cutoff regularization scheme is proposed to compensate systematically at subleading orders for these symmetry violations caused by regulator artifacts. This is especially helpful when a soft momentum cutoff has to be used for technical reasons. It is also shown that dimensional regularization can still be used for some Feynman (sub)diagrams while cutoff regulators are used for the rest.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a cutoff regularization scheme for chiral nuclear effective field theory that systematically compensates for chiral symmetry and Lorentz invariance violations at subleading orders, enabling the use of soft momentum cutoffs while maintaining control over symmetry-breaking artifacts.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with a chiral and Lorentz-invariant kinetic term in D dimensions that includes a Gaussian momentum cutoff, then expand this term to generate a string of symmetry-breaking operators with fixed, Λ-dependent coefficients that cancel the symmetry violations order by order in 1/Λ and 1/m_N. They demonstrate the scheme for one-nucleon self-energy calculations and for two-nucleon scattering via a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation, showing that dimensional regularization can still be used for some Feynman diagrams while cutoff regulators are used for others.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 92 MeV
- Chiral-symmetry-breaking scale: Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV
- The chiral index ν = 1 for the lowest-order operator to cancel chiral violations by the cutoff regulator, which can be relegated to ν = 2 by invoking the equation of motion for nucleons
- The momentum cutoff Λ and energy cutoff λ are related by λ ≡ Λ²/(2m_N)
- The scale 4πf_π ≈ 1.2 GeV is given as the threshold below which pion loops are generally suppressed

## Context

This work extends discussions by Bernard (2003) and Epelbaum (2002) on symmetry violations from cutoff regulators in nucleon structure and nuclear forces, but differs by building symmetries and regularization simultaneously at the Lagrangian level rather than checking observables against symmetry constraints after calculation. The scheme enables reuse of previous dimensional regularization calculations for one-nucleon processes and irreducible pion-exchange diagrams while using cutoff regulators for resummation in multi-nucleon processes, and raises open questions about why analyticity and unitarity may impose more constraints on regularization schemes than symmetries and power counting do, as discussed in the context of local versus nonlocal regulators by Epelbaum (2014) and Gezerlis (2013, 2014).
