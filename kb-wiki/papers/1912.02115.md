# Heavy quark flow as better probes of QGP properties

> In earlier studies we have proposed that most parton $v_2$ comes from the anisotropic escape of partons, not from the hydrodynamic flow, even for semi-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. Here we study the flavor dependence of this escape mechanism with a multi-phase transport model. In contrast to naive expectations, we find that the charm $v_2$ is much more sensitive to the hydrodynamic flow than the lighter quark $v_2$, and the fraction of $v_2$ from the escape mechanism decreases strongly with the quark mass for large collision systems. We also find that the light quark collective flow is essential for the charm quark $v_2$. Our finding thus suggests that heavy quark flows are better probes of the quark-gluon-plasma properties than light quark flows.

# Heavy quark flow as better probes of QGP properties

## Key claim

The charm quark elliptic flow ($v_2$) is much more sensitive to hydrodynamic collective flow than lighter quark $v_2$, and the fraction of $v_2$ arising from the anisotropic escape mechanism decreases strongly with quark mass for large collision systems; this suggests heavy quark flows are better probes of quark-gluon plasma properties than light quark flows, but this conclusion is drawn from a specific transport model (AMPT) and applies most strongly to large systems at high energies.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model to simulate p+Pb collisions at 5A TeV, Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV, and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV. They tracked the full parton collision history for light (u/d), strange, and charm quarks, and performed azimuth-randomized simulations where the azimuthal angle of each final-state parton was randomized after every scattering to isolate the escape mechanism contribution from the collective flow contribution to $v_2$.

## Key numbers

- In Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV and $b=8$ fm, the fraction of $v_2$ from the escape mechanism is 42.5% for u/d quarks, 26.5% for strange quarks, and 8.5% for charm quarks
- In Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV and $b=6.6-8.1$ fm, the escape fraction is 65.6% for u/d, 47.4% for strange, and 21.8% for charm
- In p+Pb collisions at 5A TeV and $b=0$ fm, the escape fraction is 72.9% for u/d, 59.1% for strange, and 56.8% for charm
- In d+Au collisions at 200A GeV and $b=0$ fm, the escape fraction for all flavors combined is 93%
- The quark masses used in AMPT are: $m_u=5.6$ MeV/$c^2$, $m_d=9.9$ MeV/$c^2$, $m_s=199$ MeV/$c^2$, and $m_c=1.2$ GeV/$c^2$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by He et al. (2015) and Lin et al. (2015) that proposed the anisotropic escape mechanism as the dominant source of parton $v_2$ even in semi-central Au+Au collisions, by now examining the flavor dependence of this mechanism. The finding that charm quark $v_2$ is dominated by hydrodynamic flow in large systems, and that light quark collective flow is essential for generating charm $v_2$, supports similar claims from other approaches by Esha et al. (2016) and Greco et al. (2017). This enables the interpretation that heavy quark flow measurements can more directly probe QGP transport properties than light quark flow measurements.
