# Nonadditive thermostatistics and thermodynamics

> Nonadditive composition rules for several physical quantities are treated in thermodynamics. It is argued that the zeroth law defines the existence of their additive forms, the formal logarithms. A further principle, the universal thermostat independence leads to a particular formal logarithm, equivalent to Tsallis entropy S_q. We connect q with generalized susceptibilities of the thermostat.

# Nonadditive thermostatistics and thermodynamics

## Key claim

The paper argues that the zeroth law of thermodynamics, when applied to nonadditive composition rules for extensive quantities, forces the existence of formal logarithms that map those rules to additive ones; a further principle called "universal thermostat independence" then selects the Tsallis entropy \(S_q\) as the unique form that cancels first-order corrections from a finite reservoir, with the parameter \(q\) connected to the generalized susceptibility of the thermostat.

## Method

The authors derive the consequences of the zeroth law for nonadditive composition rules by requiring both separability (the equation of state of each body is independent of its partner) and transitivity (if body 1 is in equilibrium with body 2, and body 2 with body 3, then body 1 is in equilibrium with body 3). They show that these conditions force the existence of formal logarithms \(L(X)\) that convert nonadditive composition into addition. They then impose a "universal thermostat independence" principle: in a canonical treatment of a small subsystem coupled to a finite reservoir, the leading first-order correction in the Taylor expansion of the maximum entropy condition must vanish. Solving the resulting differential equation for the function \(K(S)\) that maps the Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy to a generalized entropy yields the Tsallis entropy formula.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis composition rule is \(S_{12} = S_1 + S_2 + a S_1 S_2\), where \(a = 1 - q\).
- The formal logarithm for the Tsallis rule is \(\hat{L}(S) = (1/a) \ln(1 + a S)\).
- The Tsallis entropy is \(S_T = (1/a) \left( \sum_i p_i^{1-\hat{a}} - p_i \right)\) with \(\hat{a} = 1 - q\) and normalization \(\sum_i p_i = 1\).
- The Renyi entropy is \(S_R = \hat{L}(S_T) = (1/(1-q)) \ln \sum_i p_i^q\).
- The universal thermostat independence condition gives \(K''(S)/K'(S) = a\), with solution \(K(S) = (e^{aS} - 1)/a\).
- The parameter \(a\) is related to the generalized susceptibility of the reservoir by \(a = 1/\chi(X_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic framework for nonadditive composition rules previously developed by Abe (2002), Biro (2008), and Biro and Van (2011). It provides a physical interpretation of the Tsallis \(q\) parameter in terms of reservoir properties, rather than treating it as a pure fitting parameter. The derivation connects the Tsallis entropy density to the Renyi total entropy via the formal logarithm, and it explains the power-law tails observed in transverse momentum spectra from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, proton-proton collisions at LHC, RHIC, Fermilab and SPS, and electron-positron collisions (Biro et al. 2010, Barnafoldi et al. 2011, Urmossy et al. 2011). The result enables a canonical maximum-entropy treatment of systems with finite reservoirs, where the equilibrium distribution follows from maximizing the Renyi entropy subject to constraints.
