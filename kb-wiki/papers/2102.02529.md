# Selfinteracting Particle-Antiparticle System of Bosons

> Thermodynamic properties of a system of interacting boson particles and antiparticles at finite temperatures are studied within the framework of the thermodynamically consistent Skyrme-like mean-field model. The mean field contains both attractive and repulsive terms. Self-consistency relations between the mean field and thermodynamic functions are derived. We assume conservation of the isospin density for all temperatures. It is shown that, independently of the strength of the attractive mean field, at the critical temperature $T_{\rm c}$ the system undergoes the phase transition of second-order to the Bose-Einstein condensate, which exists in the temperature interval $0 \le T \le T_{\rm c}$. We obtained that the condensation represents a discontinuity of the derivative of the heat capacity at $T = T_{\rm c}$, and condensate occurs only for the component with a higher particle-number density in the particle-antiparticle system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a system of interacting boson particles and antiparticles with conserved isospin density undergoes a second-order phase transition to Bose-Einstein condensation at a critical temperature, and that this critical temperature is practically independent of the strength of the attractive mean field for "weak" attraction (parameter κ ≤ 1). The condensate forms only for the component with higher particle-number density.

## Method

The authors develop a thermodynamically consistent Skyrme-like mean-field model for a boson particle-antiparticle system (conventionally called "pions") with a mean field U(n) = -A n + B n² containing both attractive and repulsive terms. They work in the Canonical Ensemble with fixed isospin density n_I and solve self-consistent equations for the total density n and chemical potential μ as functions of temperature. For temperatures below the critical point, they generalize the equations to include a condensate component for the high-density particle species (π⁻ mesons) while the low-density species (π⁺ mesons) remains in the thermal phase.

## Key numbers

- Boson mass m = 139 MeV (conventional "pion" mass)
- Repulsive coefficient B/m = 2.025 fm⁶, kept constant throughout calculations
- Particle volume v₀ = 0.45 fm³ (corresponding to particle radius r₀ ≈ 0.3 fm)
- For isospin density n_I = 0.1 fm⁻³ and attraction parameter κ = 0.5, the critical temperature T_c^(−) = 129 MeV
- For isospin density n_I = 0.1 fm⁻³ and attraction parameter κ = κ_c = 1.0, the critical temperature T_c1^(−) = 251 MeV
- The average critical temperature for n_I = 0.1 fm⁻³ is T_c ≈ 129 MeV
- The critical temperature difference when the attraction parameter κ changes does not exceed 4 MeV for n_I = 0.1 fm⁻³
- For 0 ≤ κ ≤ 1, the chemical potential is in the range 103 ≤ μ ≤ 142 MeV (from 0.74 m_π to 1.02 m_π)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Anchishkin and Mishustin (2019), which considered boson systems at zero chemical potential in the Grand Canonical Ensemble, to the Canonical Ensemble with fixed isospin density. The study is relevant to understanding meson systems in heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, and cosmology, and complements lattice calculations by Brandt and collaborators (2016-2018) and first-principles studies of dense pion systems. The model enables future investigation of the "strong" attraction regime (κ > 1), where the authors note that first-order phase transitions may occur as shown in Anchishkin and Mishustin (2019).
