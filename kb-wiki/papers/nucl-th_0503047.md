# Covariant description of kinetic freeze out through a finite space-like layer

> The problem of Freeze Out (FO) in relativistic heavy ion reactions is addressed. We develop and analyze an idealized one-dimensional model of FO in a finite layer, based on the covariant FO probability. The resulting post FO phase-space distributions are discussed for different FO probabilities and layer thicknesses.

## Key claim

The paper develops a covariant, one-dimensional kinetic model of freeze-out (FO) through a finite space-like layer, showing that the FO process can be completed within a finite thickness \(L\) only if the escape rate is parameterized with a specific functional form that forces the escape probability to unity at the outer boundary; the model generalizes earlier infinite-layer treatments by introducing a finite layer thickness \(L\) as a parameter.

## Method

The authors split the phase-space distribution into interacting (\(f_i\)) and free (\(f_f\)) components and evolve them along the direction of the FO normal vector \(d\sigma_\mu\) using a set of differential equations with an escape rate \(W_{esc}\). The escape rate is constructed as a Lorentz-invariant scalar proportional to \((p^\mu d\sigma_\mu)/(p^\mu u_\mu)\) and includes a factor \((L/(L-s))\) to ensure FO completes at \(s = L\). The model is solved semianalytically for a space-like normal vector in the Rest Frame of the Front (RFF), with the interacting component approximated by an equilibrium distribution at each step to account for rethermalization via a relaxation length \(\lambda_0\).

## Key numbers

* The FO layer thickness \(L\) is treated as a parameter; the model requires \(L\) to be a few initial mean free paths for nucleons in heavy ion collisions, citing Csernai and Bravina.
* For the power-law FO profile (\(a=1\)), the escape probability is \(\mathcal{P}_{esc} = 1 - \left( \frac{L-s}{L} \right)^{\frac{L}{\lambda} \cos \theta_{\vec{p}} \Theta(p^s)}\).
* For \(a>1\) the FO is exponential-like; for \(a<1\) FO is not complete within the finite layer.
* The initial characteristic FO length \(\lambda\) is defined at \(s=0\) and \(\cos\theta_{\vec{p}}=1\).
* The rethermalization length \(\lambda_0\) is taken to satisfy \(\lambda_0 < \lambda\) or \(\lambda_0 \ll \lambda\) for the approximate solution to hold.

## Context

This work extends the infinite-layer kinetic FO models of Csernai et al. (2003–2004) and the sharp-hypersurface Cooper-Frye prescription (1974) by introducing a finite FO layer thickness \(L\), addressing the unrealistic assumption of instantaneous FO. It also generalizes the earlier escape-rate formulation by making it Lorentz-invariant via the factor \((p^\mu d\sigma_\mu)/(p^\mu u_\mu)\), enabling future application in expanding 3D systems. The model enables qualitative study of FO in a finite space-time domain, with the next step being full 3D simulations of expanding and freezing-out systems.
