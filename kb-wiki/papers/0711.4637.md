# A nonperturbative parametrization and scenario for EFT renormalization

> We present a universal form of the $T$-matrices renormalized in nonperturbative regime and the ensuing notions and properties that fail conventional wisdoms. A universal scale is identified and shown to be renormalization group invariant. The effective range parameters are derived in a nonperturbative scenario with some new predictions within the realm of contact potentials. Some controversies are shown to be due to the failure of conventional wisdoms.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in nonperturbative effective field theory (EFT) renormalization of nucleon-nucleon scattering with contact potentials, the conventional perturbative renormalization wisdom fails: a universal scale \(J_0\) (the real part of \(\mathcal{I}_0\)) is renormalization-group invariant and physically meaningful, and nonperturbative counterterms do not need to follow EFT power counting, resolving the apparent "inconsistency" of Weinberg's power counting.

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) algebraically for all partial-wave channels using contact potentials constructed according to chiral EFT, employing a factorization ansatz that reduces the LSE to a matrix equation. The on-shell \(T\)-matrices are then expressed in a universal form where the complex parameter \(\mathcal{I}_0 = J_0 + iMp/(4\pi)\) appears isolated from the couplings and other prescription-dependent constants \([J_{2m+1}, m>0]\). The effective range expansion (ERE) parameters are derived from this form, and a "natural" scenario for the prescription parameters is proposed to yield unnaturally large \(S\)-wave scattering lengths while keeping other ERE parameters natural.

## Key numbers

- The universal scale \(J_0\) is estimated as \(J_0 \sim M\Lambda/(4\pi) \sim |1/C_0|\), with \(\Lambda \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV}\) as the upper limit for low-energy \(NN\) scattering.
- The other prescription parameters are taken as \(J_{2m+1} \sim M\mu^{2m+1}/(4\pi)\) for \(m>0\), with \(\mu\) of order \(m_\pi\) or \(\sim 100\ \text{MeV}\).
- In the \(^1S_0\) channel at chiral order \(\Delta=4\), the coefficients of the highest power term in \(N_0, D_0\) are \(N_{0;2}=C_4^2 J_3^2\) and \(D_{0,3}=-C_4^2 J_3\), which cannot simultaneously make the ratios \(N_{0;2}/N_{0;0}\) and \(D_{0;3}/N_{0;0}\) RG invariant.
- The paper predicts that in uncoupled channels, the ERE parameter at order \(p^{2L}\) (the coefficient of \(p^{2L}\) in the expansion) might be unnaturally sized, while all other ERE parameters are natural; for example, in \(^1S_0\) only the scattering length \(a\) is unnatural, while in \(^1P_1, ^3P_0, ^3P_1\) only the effective range \(r_e\) might be unnatural.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative renormalization analysis of the \(^1S_0\) channel by Yang (2007) to all partial-wave channels, providing universal forms for both uncoupled and coupled channels. It directly addresses controversies in EFT treatments of nucleon-nucleon scattering, such as those debated by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck; Phillips, van Kolck, and Rupak; and Epelbaum and Meissner, by showing that the apparent inconsistency of Weinberg's power counting arises from applying perturbative renormalization wisdom to a nonperturbative regime. The results enable a systematic understanding of naturalness in effective range parameters across all channels and provide a field-theoretical solution to the universality of large scattering lengths in atomic and molecular systems as discussed by Braaten and Hammer.
