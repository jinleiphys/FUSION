# A systematic study of the fusion barriers using different proximity type potentials for $N = Z$ colliding nuclei : New extensions

> By using 14 different versions and parametrizations of a proximity potential and two new versions of the potential proposed in this paper, we perform a comparative study of fusion barriers by studying 26 symmetric reactions. The mass asymmetry $η_{A}$ = $(\frac{A_{2}-A_{1}}{A_{2}+A_{1}}),$ however, is very large. Our detailed investigation reveals that most of the proximity potentials reproduce experimental data within $\pm$8% on the average. A comparison of fusion cross sections indicates that Bass 80, AW 95, and Denisov DP potentials have a better edge than other potentials. We also propose new versions of the proximity potential as well as Denisov parametrized potential. These new versions improve the agreement with the data.

## Key claim

Most proximity-type potentials reproduce experimental fusion barrier heights for symmetric N=Z colliding nuclei within ±8% on average, but the agreement depends sensitively on technical parameters such as the surface energy coefficient and the radius formula rather than on the fundamental form of the potential.

## Method

The authors calculated fusion barrier heights and positions for 26 symmetric (N=Z) reactions using 14 existing proximity-type potentials plus two new modified versions (Prox 00DP and Denisov DP) that incorporate a more precise radius formula from Royer and Rousseau (2009). They compared the theoretical barrier heights and fusion cross sections (calculated using the Wong model) against experimental data taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Most proximity potentials reproduce experimental barrier heights within ±8% on average across the 26 symmetric reactions studied.
- Bass 77 and Bass 80 reproduce experimental barrier heights within 1.5%.
- Prox 77 deviates from experimental fusion barrier heights by 6.73%; the newer Prox 00 version reduces this deviation to 5.34%.
- Ngô 80 deviates by more than 7% from experimental barrier heights, whereas its earlier version (Ngô 75) reproduces barrier heights within 2% (but only two systems fall within its parametrization limits).
- Different versions of the proximity potential differ by as much as 17 MeV in the nuclear potential depth for the reaction 40Ca+40Ca.

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of proximity-type potentials for heavy-ion fusion, following earlier studies by Myers and Swiatecki (2000), Bass (1973, 1977, 1980), and Denisov (2002). The authors show that the choice of radius formula and surface energy coefficient matters more than the specific potential form, and they propose two new parametrizations (Prox 00DP and Denisov DP) that improve agreement with experimental data. The results enable more reliable predictions of fusion barriers for symmetric systems and highlight the need for consistent radius parametrizations across different proximity models.
