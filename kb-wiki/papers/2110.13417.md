# QCD viscosity by combining the gradient flow and sparse modeling methods

> We give a new description to obtain the shear viscosity in QCD at finite temperature. Firstly, we obtain the correlation function of the renormalized energy-momentum tensor using the gradient flow method. Secondly, we estimate the spectral function from the smeared correlation functions using the sparse modeling method. The combination of these two methods looks promising to determine the shear viscosity precisely.

# QCD viscosity by combining the gradient flow and sparse modeling methods

## Key claim
The combination of the gradient flow method for defining the renormalized energy-momentum tensor and the sparse modeling method for estimating the spectral function from smeared correlation data is promising for determining the shear viscosity in QCD at finite temperature, but the results have not yet taken the continuum limit or the \(t \rightarrow 0\) limit, so the reported value at \(T/T_c = 1.65\) is preliminary.

## Method
The authors used quenched QCD lattice simulations with the Wilson plaquette gauge action at \(\beta = 6.93\) on a \(64^3 \times 16\) lattice, corresponding to \(T = 1.65 T_c\). They applied the gradient flow method to define the renormalized energy-momentum tensor and measured the two-point correlation function of the \(T^R_{12}\) component. The spectral function was then estimated from the smeared correlation function using the sparse modeling method with an intermediate-representation (IR) basis, an \(L_1\) regularization term, and the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm, under the non-negativity condition for the spectral function.

## Key numbers
- The lattice setup uses \(\beta = 6.93\) on \(N_s^3 \times N_\tau = 64^3 \times 16\) lattices, realizing the system at \(T = 1.65 T_c\).
- The thermal entropy from the gradient flow method is \(s/T^3 = 4.98(24)\) after \(a \rightarrow 0\) and \(t \rightarrow 0\) extrapolations.
- The number of gauge configurations for measurements is only 2,000, compared to 800,000 in Nakamura (2004) and 6 million in Pasztor (2018).
- The bootstrap analysis uses \(N_{boot} = 1,000\) resampled sets of the 2,000 configurations.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) at \(T/T_c = 1.65\) is plotted as a green symbol in the comparison figure, but the continuum limit and \(t \rightarrow 0\) limit have not yet been taken.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Itou (2020) by applying the sparse modeling method to the gradient-flow-smeared correlation functions, addressing the ill-posed inverse problem that becomes harder when the gradient flow reduces the number of usable temporal data points. The results are compared with prior lattice QCD calculations of \(\eta/s\) in quenched QCD by Meyer (2007, 2009), Mages (2015), Astrakhantsev (2017), and Pasztor (2018). The method enables a precise determination of the shear viscosity using far fewer gauge configurations than previous works, and the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) appears to approach the Kovtun-Son-Starinet (KSS) bound of \(1/(4\pi)\) near the critical temperature, consistent with AdS/CFT predictions.
