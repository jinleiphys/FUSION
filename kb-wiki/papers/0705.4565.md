# Renormalization of NN Interaction with Relativistic Chiral Two Pion Exchange

> The renormalization of the NN interaction with the Chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential computed using relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory is considered. The short distance singularity reduces the number of counter-terms to about a half as those in the heavy-baryon expansion. Phase shifts and deuteron properties are evaluated and a general overall agreement is observed.

## Key claim

The Lorentz-invariant (relativistic baryon) chiral two-pion exchange potential has a short-distance singularity of the form \(1/r^7\), which reduces the number of required counter-terms to about half of those needed in the heavy-baryon expansion, but the overall agreement with phase shifts and deuteron properties depends on the chosen set of chiral low-energy constants and on the specific partial wave.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in configuration space for the NN system, using a Lorentz-invariant chiral two-pion exchange potential derived from the Becher-Leutwyler formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. They impose short-distance boundary conditions determined by the attractive or repulsive nature of the eigenpotentials at the origin, and fix the required counter-terms (scattering lengths) from high-quality phenomenological potentials (NijmII and Reid93). Phase shifts and deuteron properties are computed as a function of a short-distance cutoff \(r_c\) and extrapolated to the renormalized limit \(r_c \to 0\).

## Key numbers

- The short-distance behavior of the Lorentz-invariant TPE potential is \(\U_{2\pi}(r) \to M \mathbf{C}_7 / r^7\), a relativistic Van der Waals-type force, in contrast to the \(1/r^5\) and \(1/r^6\) behavior in the heavy-baryon expansion.
- For the deuteron, using the RB-TPE potential with Set \(\eta\) (\(c_1 = -0.81\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.80\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 4.50\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\)), the asymptotic D/S ratio is predicted as \(\eta = 0.02566(1)\), compared to the experimental value \(0.0256(4)\).
- The deuteron binding energy is fixed to \(B = 2.224575(9)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (corresponding to \(\gamma = 0.231605\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)), and the S-wave normalization is \(A_S = 0.88426(2)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}\) for Set \(\eta\).
- The deuteron D-state probability for RB-TPE Set \(\eta\) is \(P_D = 5.59(1)\%\), compared to \(5.67(4)\%\) experimental and \(5.635\%\) for NijmII.
- The quadrupole moment for RB-TPE Set \(\eta\) is \(Q_d = 0.2749(1)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), compared to the experimental \(0.2859(3)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The number of independent counter-terms required for the RB-TPE potential is reduced compared to HB-TPE: for example, in the \(^3P_0\) channel, HB-NNLO requires an input counter-term for Sets I, II, and III, while RB-TPE requires none (marked as "---(*)"), meaning the behavior is very dependent on the chiral coupling set.
- The convergence of the asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta\) toward the renormalized value follows \(\eta(r_c) - \eta(0) \sim \exp[-4/5 (R_+/r_c)^{5/2}]\) for the RB-TPE potential, compared to \(\exp[-2 (R_+/r_c)^{1/2}]\) for OPE.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization program for NN potentials previously developed by Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005-2006) using heavy-baryon chiral potentials, now applying it to the Lorentz-invariant chiral two-pion exchange potential derived by Higa, Becher, and Leutwyler (2003-2005). The key difference is that the relativistic potential has a more singular \(1/r^7\) short-distance behavior, which reduces the number of required counter-terms compared to the heavy-baryon expansion, and provides a faster convergence of observables with respect to the cutoff. This enables a more predictive description of NN scattering and deuteron properties, though the results are sensitive to the choice of chiral low-energy constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\).
