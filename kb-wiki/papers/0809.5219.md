# Theoretical update on low energy neutrino-nucleus reactions

> We review Quasielastic (QE) inclusive and semi--inclusive neutrino/antineutrino Charged Current (CC) and Neutral Current (NC) induced nuclear reactions at intermediate energies. We pay special attention to nuclear corrections besides Pauli blocking: Long and Short range nuclear correlations (RPA and SRC) and particle and hole Spectral Functions (SF). We also critically review the use of the Plane and Distorted Wave Impulse approximations (PWIA and DWIA) to describe inclusive one nucleon knockout reactions off nuclei. In this context, we present results from a Monte Carlo cascade method to account for the rescattering of the outgoing nucleon. Finally, we examine the effects of chiral non-resonant terms in neutrino pion production off the nucleon, and present some preliminary results on nuclear coherent pion production induced by neutrinos.

# Theoretical update on low energy neutrino-nucleus reactions

## Key claim
The authors argue that a simple Fermi Gas model, widely used in neutrino oscillation analyses, fails to provide a satisfactory description of measured cross sections, and that inclusion of nuclear corrections beyond Pauli blocking, specifically long- and short-range correlations (RPA and SRC) and realistic spectral functions, is necessary. The abstract glosses over the fact that these corrections produce sizeable effects that partially compensate in integrated cross sections but significantly alter differential distributions.

## Method
The authors compute the neutrino-nucleus cross section using the Local Density Approximation, starting from a Fermi Gas model, and evaluate the W-boson selfenergy in infinite nuclear matter. They incorporate Random Phase Approximation (RPA) correlations using a Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction with explicit pion and rho meson exchanges, and include short-range correlations and Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. For final-state interactions, they use nucleon propagators dressed with realistic self-energies to obtain particle and hole spectral functions, and employ a Monte Carlo cascade method to account for rescattering of the outgoing nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Relative uncertainties of the QE neutrino/antineutrino-nucleus cross section model are about 10-15%, as estimated by Monte Carlo propagation of input uncertainties.
- Uncertainties affecting the ratios σ(μ)/σ(e) and σ(μ̄)/σ(ē) are not larger than about 5%, mostly from deficiencies of the local Fermi Gas picture.
- For neutrino pion production off the nucleon, the best fit to ANL νμ p → μ⁻ p π⁺ data yields C₅ᴬ(0) = 0.867 and M_AΔ = 0.985 GeV, compared to the Goldberger-Treiman relation value C₅ᴬ(0) = 1.2, indicating corrections of about 30% to the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Nieves et al. (1997) for neutrino-nucleus reactions and provides a systematic comparison of nuclear corrections. The results agree qualitatively and quantitatively with the spectral function approach of Benhar et al. and with the transport model of the Giessen group (Buss et al., 2007). The findings are relevant for interpreting data from neutrino oscillation experiments, where accurate nuclear cross sections are essential. The model enables improved predictions for both inclusive quasielastic reactions and semi-inclusive nucleon emission processes, and provides a framework for nuclear coherent pion production calculations.
