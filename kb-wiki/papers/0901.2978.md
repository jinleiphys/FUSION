# Tensor-optimized shell model with bare nucleon-nucleon interaction for 4He

> The pion exchange between nucleons generates a strong tensor interaction, which provides a large attractive contribution for the binding energy of nucleus. This non central tensor interaction is difficult to handle in the shell model framework, which hinders full understanding of nuclear structure. We develop the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) for the strong tensor interaction and now we are able to use bare nucleon-nucleon interaction with the help of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) for the short-range hard core. We adopt the nucleon-nucleon interaction, AV8', and calculate explicitly the ground state of $^4$He and make a detailed comparison with rigorous few-body model calculations. We show a large amount of success of the tensor-optimized shell model with bare nucleon-nucleon interaction for 4He.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) combined with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) can treat the bare AV8' nucleon-nucleon interaction for 4He, but the total binding energy of approximately -22 MeV falls short of the rigorous few-body result of -26 MeV, indicating that the separate treatment of short-range and tensor correlations is not fully complete.

## Method

The authors combine the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM), which uses a shell-model wave function expanded in 0p0h and 2p2h configurations with optimized radial Gaussian basis functions to handle the tensor interaction, with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) to treat the short-range repulsive core. They apply a modified S-wave UCOM (S-UCOM) that restricts the unitary correlation to only the relative s-wave component in even channels, while using full UCOM for odd partial waves, and compute the ground state of 4He using the AV8' potential without the Coulomb term.

## Key numbers

* Total energy of 4He in TOSCOM: approximately -22 MeV, compared to the rigorous benchmark value of -26 MeV (Kamada et al., 2001).
* Tensor interaction matrix element in TOSCOM: -55 MeV, compared to the rigorous value of -68 MeV.
* Kinetic energy in TOSCOM: approximately 90 MeV, compared to the rigorous value of 102 MeV.
* Central interaction matrix element in TOSCOM: approximately -55 MeV, matching the rigorous value.
* Contribution of 4p4h configurations within the p-shell to the binding energy: approximately 50 keV.
* Optimized UCOM parameters for the triplet even channel: alpha = 1.33 fm, beta = 0.93 fm, gamma = 0.41 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOSM approach (Myo et al., 2006, 2007) by incorporating UCOM (Feldmeier et al., 1998; Neff and Feldmeier, 2003) to handle the short-range repulsive core, enabling the use of the bare AV8' interaction rather than a G-matrix. The results are compared with rigorous few-body calculations for 4He from the benchmark study by Kamada et al. (2001), which includes methods such as the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, the stochastic variational method, and the no-core shell model. The approach aims to provide a framework applicable to medium and heavy nuclei, in contrast to methods using relative Jacobi coordinates that are limited to light systems.
