# Hybrid framework of Fermi-Dirac spin hydrodynamics

> We outline the hybrid framework of spin hydrodynamics, combining classical kinetic theory with the Israel-Stewart method of introducing dissipation. We obtain the local equilibrium expressions for the baryon current, the energy-momentum tensor, and the spin tensor of particles with spin 1/2 following the Fermi-Dirac statistics and compare them with the previously derived versions where the Boltzmann approximation was used. The expressions in the two cases have the same form, but the coefficients are governed by different functions. The relative differences between the tensor coefficients in the Fermi-Dirac and Boltzmann cases are found to grow exponentially with the baryon chemical potential. In the proposed formalism, nonequilibrium processes are studied including mathematically possible dissipative corrections. Standard conservation laws are applied, and the condition of positive entropy production allows for transfer between the spin and orbital parts of angular momentum.

## Key claim

The hybrid framework of spin hydrodynamics yields tensor coefficients for the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor that have the same mathematical form whether one uses Fermi-Dirac statistics or the Boltzmann approximation, but the coefficients are governed by different functions; the relative differences between the two cases grow exponentially with the baryon chemical potential, becoming significant at high chemical potential where the Boltzmann approximation fails.

## Method

The author derives local equilibrium expressions for the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor by integrating the Fermi-Dirac distribution function for spin-1/2 particles over momentum and spin degrees of freedom, expanding the distribution to second order in the spin polarization tensor ω^{μν} for the current and energy-momentum tensor, and to first order for the spin tensor. The resulting tensor coefficients are expressed in terms of the functions J_{mn} and their derivatives, then compared with the Boltzmann case where the integrals reduce to Bessel functions K_n(z). Dissipative corrections are introduced following the Israel-Stewart method, with general nonequilibrium tensor decompositions constrained by conservation laws and the condition of positive entropy production.

## Key numbers

- Particle mass m = 1000 MeV, temperature T = 100 MeV, and baryon chemical potential μ ranging from 0 to 1000 MeV are used for the comparison plots
- Relative differences between Fermi-Dirac and Boltzmann coefficients reach order 1 for ξ = μ/T ≈ 10 (with m = 1000 MeV, T = 100 MeV)
- The baryon current coefficient n_0 in the Fermi-Dirac case is given by (m³/π²) J_{21}^∓, compared to (2 sinh ξ / π²) z² T³ K₂(z) in the Boltzmann case
- The energy density coefficient ε_0 in the Fermi-Dirac case is (m⁴/π²) J_{22}^±, compared to (2 cosh ξ / π²) z² T⁴ [z K₃(z) - K₂(z)] in the Boltzmann case
- The spin tensor coefficient A₁ in the Fermi-Dirac case is (spin² m³ / 9π²) (J̇_{21}^± + 2 J̇_{23}^±), compared to (2 spin² cosh ξ / 3π²) z T³ [z K₂(z) + 2 K₃(z)] in the Boltzmann case

## Context

This work extends the hybrid framework of spin hydrodynamics previously developed by Drogosz, Florkowski, and Hontarenko (2024-2025), which combines classical kinetic theory with the Israel-Stewart method for dissipation. The paper complements the outline given by Florkowski (2025) by providing the full Fermi-Dirac expressions, whereas earlier work had only considered the Boltzmann approximation. The results enable more realistic numerical simulations of spin hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at high baryon chemical potential where the Boltzmann approximation is inadequate; the first numerical test of the hybrid framework in a boost-invariant perfect-fluid case was performed by Drogosz (2025).
