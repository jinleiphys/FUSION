# The Generalized Counting Rule and Oscillatory Scaling

> We have studied the energy dependence of the $pp$ elastic scattering data and the pion-photoproduction data at 90$^\circ$ c.m. angle in light of the new generalized counting rule derived for exclusive processes. We show that by including the helicity flipping amplitudes (with energy dependence given by the generalized counting rule) and their interference with the Landshoff amplitude, we are able to reproduce the energy dependence of all cross-section and spin-correlation (A$_{NN}$) data available above the resonance region. The pion-photoproduction data can also be described by this approach, but in this case data with much finer energy spacing is needed to confirm the oscillations about the scaling behavior.

## Key claim

By including helicity-flipping amplitudes (with energy dependence given by the generalized counting rule of Ji et al.) and their interference with the Landshoff amplitude, the authors reproduce the energy dependence of all available pp elastic cross-section and spin-correlation (A\(_{NN}\)) data above the resonance region; for pion-photoproduction, the approach can describe the data, but finer energy spacing is needed to confirm oscillations about the scaling behavior.

## Method

The authors fit world data on pp elastic scattering at 90\(^\circ\) center-of-mass angle using a model that combines three helicity-conserving short-distance amplitudes (scaling as \(s^{-4}\)), two helicity-flipping amplitudes (scaling as \(s^{-4.5}\) and \(s^{-5}\) according to the generalized counting rule), and a Landshoff amplitude with an energy-dependent phase. Two parametrizations of the Landshoff phase were tested: one with a fixed logarithmic form \(\phi(s) = \frac{\pi}{0.06}\ln\ln(s/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)\) and a more general parametrization with additional free parameters. The same approach was applied to A\(_{NN}\) spin-correlation data and to charged pion-photoproduction data (\(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n\) and \(\gamma n \rightarrow \pi^- p\)) at 90\(^\circ\) c.m. angle, where the helicity-flip amplitude scales as \(s^{-3}\) and the Landshoff contribution enters at sub-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The three helicity-conserving amplitudes for pp scattering have an energy dependence of \(\sim 1/s^4\).
- The two helicity-flipping amplitudes for pp scattering have energy dependences of \(\sim 1/s^{4.5}\) and \(\sim 1/s^{5}\) respectively, as predicted by the generalized counting rule.
- In the fit of Ralston and Pire, the ratio of the Landshoff amplitude to the short-distance amplitude is 1:0.04, using \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 100\) MeV.
- The helicity-flip amplitude for pion-photoproduction has an energy dependence of \(s^{-3}\) according to the generalized counting rule, while the helicity-nonflip amplitude scales as \(s^{-2.5}\).
- The Landshoff amplitude for pp scattering scales as \(\sim s^{-3.5}\).
- The helicity-flip contributions are significant at low energies (\(s < 10\) GeV\(^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the earlier interference model of Ralston and Pire (1988) and Carlson et al. (1992), which used only helicity-conserving amplitudes and the Landshoff term to describe oscillations in pp scattering, but failed at low energies (\(s < 10\) GeV\(^2\)). By incorporating the generalized counting rule of Ji et al. (2003), which includes helicity-flipping amplitudes related to quark orbital angular momentum, the authors achieve a better description of both the cross-section and spin-correlation data at lower energies. The approach also provides a framework for interpreting oscillatory scaling in exclusive photoreactions, and the authors note that the upcoming Jefferson Lab experiment E02010 will provide finer-energy-spacing data on pion-photoproduction needed to confirm the predicted oscillations.
