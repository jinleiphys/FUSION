# Quantum Kinetic Equation for spin polarization of massive quarks from pQCD

> We review the recent progress in formulating a quantum kinetic theory for the polarization of spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ massive quarks from the leading-log order of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading-log order collision terms for the quantum kinetic equation governing spin polarization of massive quarks in perturbative QCD, but the result is limited to the zeroth-order relaxation operator \(\Gamma_0\) which describes relaxation to an unpolarized state in the absence of vorticity and magnetic field; the first-order terms \(\Gamma_1\) incorporating vorticity and magnetic field effects are stated as work in progress.

## Method

The author formulates the time evolution of the spin density matrix for massive spin-1/2 quarks using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism, expanding the interaction Hamiltonian to quadratic order in the QCD coupling. The thermal average of gluon two-point functions includes one-loop Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) gluon self-energy, and the resulting "cross" and "self-energy" diagrams are evaluated to obtain separate evolution equations for the particle number distribution \(f(\bm p)\) and the spin polarization density \(\bm S(\bm p)\) at leading-log order \(g^4 \log(1/g)\).

## Key numbers

* The characteristic relaxation rate of spin polarization for massive quarks is of order \(\alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s)\), with the logarithmic contribution arising from t-channel soft gluon exchange between the Debye screening mass scale \(m_D \sim gT\) and the hard scale \(T\).
* The time evolution equations for \(f(\bm p,t)\) and \(\bm S(\bm p,t)\) are given with a prefactor \(C_2(F) \frac{m_D^2 g^2 \log(1/g)}{(4\pi)} \frac{1}{2p E_p}\), where \(C_2(F)\) is the quadratic Casimir for the fundamental representation.
* The detailed balance condition for the particle number distribution is satisfied when \(f^{\rm eq}(\bm p) = z e^{-E_p/T}\) (Boltzmann distribution with arbitrary fugacity \(z\)).
* In the massless limit, the detailed balance condition for the spin polarization density is satisfied when \(\bm S(\bm p) = z e^{-p/T} \hat{\bm p}\).

## Context

This work extends the free-streaming Boltzmann equation for spin-1/2 massive quarks studied by Mueller (2019), Weickgenannt (2019), Gao (2019), and Hattori (2019) by including the previously missing collision terms at leading-log order of pQCD. It complements the relaxation time study for strange quark spin polarization by Kapusta (2019) and provides a microscopic framework for dynamical spin evolution that is a prerequisite for understanding hadron spin polarization (such as \(\Lambda\) hyperon polarization observed by the STAR collaboration) in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future extensions beyond the spatial homogeneity limit and inclusion of vorticity and magnetic field effects.
