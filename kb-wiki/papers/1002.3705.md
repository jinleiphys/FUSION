# Unified description of neutron superfluidity in the neutron-star crust with analogy to anisotropic multi-band BCS superconductors

> The neutron superfluidity in the inner crust of a neutron star has been traditionally studied considering either homogeneous neutron matter or only a small number of nucleons confined inside the spherical Wigner-Seitz cell. Drawing analogies with the recently discovered multi-band superconductors, we have solved the anisotropic multi-band BCS gap equations with Bloch boundary conditions, thus providing a unified description taking consistently into account both the free neutrons and the nuclear clusters. Calculations have been carried out using the effective interaction underlying our recent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nuclear mass model HFB-16. We have found that even though the presence of inhomogeneities lowers the neutron pairing gaps, the reduction is much less than that predicted by previous calculations using the Wigner-Seitz approximation. We have studied the disappearance of superfluidity with increasing temperature. As an application we have calculated the neutron specific heat, which is an important ingredient for modeling the thermal evolution of newly-born neutron stars. This work provides a new scheme for realistic calculations of superfluidity in neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The authors solve the anisotropic multi-band BCS gap equations with Bloch boundary conditions to provide a unified description of neutron superfluidity in the neutron-star crust that consistently accounts for both free neutrons and nuclear clusters. They find that while inhomogeneities lower the neutron pairing gaps, the reduction is much less than that predicted by previous calculations using the Wigner-Seitz approximation.

## Method

The authors used the fourth-order Extended Thomas-Fermi method with Strutinsky-Integral theorem (ETFSI) to determine the equilibrium structure and composition of the inner crust, employing the Skyrme force BSk16 underlying the HFB-16 nuclear mass model. They solved the three-dimensional Schroedinger equation with Bloch boundary conditions for neutron single-particle states, then solved the anisotropic multi-band BCS gap equations iteratively for five crustal layers with average nucleon densities between 0.05 fm^-3 and 0.07 fm^-3. The pairing matrix elements were computed using the special-point method for k-space integration and 110 special r-points for real-space integration.

## Key numbers

- For average nucleon density 0.070 fm^-3: average pairing gap Δ_F = 1.44 MeV, compared to uniform matter gap Δ_u = 1.79 MeV (reduction of approximately 20%)
- For average nucleon density 0.050 fm^-3: average pairing gap Δ_F = 2.29 MeV, compared to uniform matter gap Δ_u = 2.59 MeV (reduction of approximately 12%)
- The pairing gaps at the Fermi level vary by about 0.2-0.4 MeV across different bands and wave vectors
- The critical temperature follows the BCS relation T_c/Δ_F ≈ exp(ζ)/π where ζ ≈ 0.577 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- In the shallowest layer at 0.05 fm^-3, the neutron pairing field is increased by a factor of approximately 4 inside clusters due to proximity effects

## Context

This work extends beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation used in previous calculations by Baldo et al. (2007), which found much stronger suppression of pairing gaps (40-60% reduction at similar densities). The authors draw analogies with multi-band superconductors discovered in condensed matter physics, such as magnesium diboride and iron pnictide superconductors. This unified scheme enables realistic calculations of neutron specific heat for modeling the thermal evolution of newly-born neutron stars and provides a framework for studying the dynamics of the neutron superfluid believed to be responsible for pulsar glitches.
