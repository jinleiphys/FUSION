# Low-energy structure and anti-bubble effect of dynamical correlations in 46Ar

> The low-energy structure of 46Ar is of particular interest due to the possible coexistence of different shapes and the possible existence of proton bubble structure. In this work, we apply a beyond relativistic mean-field approach to study the low-energy structure of 46Ar. Correlations beyond the mean field are introduced by configuration mixing of both particle-number and angular-momentum projected axially deformed mean-field states. The low-lying spectroscopy and charge density in laboratory frame are calculated and an excellent agreement with available data is achieved. Even though an evident proton bubble structure is shown in the spherical state of 46Ar, it eventually disappears after taking into account the dynamical correlation effects. Moreover, our results indicate that the existence of proton bubble structure in argon isotopes is unlikely.

## Key claim

Even though a semi-bubble structure (central depletion) appears in the proton and charge densities of the spherical state of 46Ar, this bubble structure disappears after including dynamical correlation effects from angular-momentum projection and quadrupole shape mixing, making the observation of a proton bubble in any argon isotope unlikely.

## Method

The authors performed a beyond relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculation using the generator coordinate method combined with particle-number and angular-momentum projection (GCM+PNAMP). Axially deformed mean-field states were generated from RMF+BCS calculations with constraint on the quadrupole moment, using two relativistic point-coupling energy density functionals (PC-PK1 and PC-F1). The low-lying spectroscopy, transition strengths, and laboratory-frame charge densities were computed from the configuration mixing of these projected states.

## Key numbers

- The neutron N=28 spherical energy gap in 46Ar is 4.09 MeV (PC-PK1 force) and 3.73 MeV (PC-F1 force), compared to 4.80 MeV in 49Ca and 4.47 MeV in 47Ar from neutron stripping reactions.
- The single-particle energy difference between proton 2s1/2 and 1d3/2 orbitals in the spherical state of 46Ar is +1.3 MeV (PC-PK1 force), with inversion found in 44,46,48Ar.
- The spin-orbit splitting of proton 2p3/2-2p1/2 orbitals in the spherical state changes from +0.581 MeV (without pairing) to -0.387 MeV (with pairing) using PC-PK1.
- The depletion factor Fmax (central density depletion) becomes zero for all argon isotopes when dynamical deformation effects are included in the full GCM calculation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bubble structure in 46Ar that were limited to spherical mean-field approaches, such as those by Khan (2008) and Chu (2010), and addresses the controversy between tensor-force effects (Wang, 2011) and pairing anti-bubble effects (Nakada, 2013). The authors had previously shown that dynamical correlations quench but do not eliminate the bubble in 34Si (Yao, 2012; Yao, 2013), but here they find complete disappearance in argon isotopes. The results are intended to be tested by future electron scattering measurements at the SCRIT facility at RIKEN.
