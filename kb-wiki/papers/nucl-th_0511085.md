# Pair production and optical lasers

> Electron-positron pair creation in a standing wave is explored using a parameter-free quantum kinetic equation. Field strengths and frequencies corresponding to modern optical lasers induce a material polarisation of the QED vacuum, which may be characterised as a plasma of e^+ e^- quasiparticle pairs with a density of ~10^{20}cm^{-3}. The plasma vanishes almost completely when the laser field is zero, leaving a very small residual pair density, n_r, which is the true manifestation of vacuum decay. The average pair density per period is proportional to the laser intensity but independent of the frequency, ν. The density of residual pairs also grows with laser intensity but n_r \propto ν^2. With optical lasers at the forefront of the current generation, these dynamical QED vacuum effects may generate 5-10 observable two-photon annihilation events per laser pulse.

## Key claim

Electron-positron pair creation in a standing wave formed by counter-propagating optical laser beams may produce 5-10 observable two-photon annihilation events per laser pulse, even though the field strength is far below the Schwinger critical field, because the large spot volume of optical lasers compensates for the low pair density.

## Method

The authors solved a parameter-free quantum Vlasov equation (kinetic equation) for the single-particle momentum distribution function in a spatially uniform, time-dependent electric field, using a harmonic field model for the standing wave formed by two counter-propagating laser beams. For the weak-field optical laser regime, they simplified the integro-differential equation by neglecting the internal field and using the approximation [1-2f] ≈ 1, then computed the quasiparticle pair density and residual pair density via numerical integration. The two-photon annihilation rate was estimated using the computed distribution functions and the QED cross-section for e⁺e⁻ → γγ.

## Key numbers

- Optical laser field strength: \(E_m^O = 3 \times 10^{-5} E_{cr}\), where \(E_{cr} = 1.3 \times 10^{16}\) V/cm
- Optical laser wavelength: \(\lambda_O = 795\) nm
- Average quasiparticle pair density: \(\sim 10^{20}\) cm\(^{-3}\)
- Residual pair density for optical laser: \(n_r \sim 10^{-4} \lambda_O^{-3}\) (approximately \(10^{-4}\) pairs per cubic wavelength)
- Mean pair density per period for optical laser: \(\langle n \rangle \sim 10^7 \lambda_O^{-3}\)
- Ratio \(n_r/\langle n \rangle \simeq 10^{-11}\) for optical laser parameters
- For XFEL comparison: \(E_m^X = 0.24 E_{cr}\), \(\lambda_X = 0.15\) nm, with \(n_r \sim \lambda_X^{-3}\), \(\langle n \rangle \sim 10^4 \lambda_X^{-3}\), and \(n_r/\langle n \rangle \sim 10^{-4}\)
- Estimated annihilation events per laser pulse: 5-10, based on pulse intensity \(I = 10^{20}\) W/cm\(^2\), pulse duration \(\tau_L = 85\) fs, \(\lambda = 795\) nm, and spot diameter 2.5 μm
- Mean energy of annihilation photons: \(\approx 1\) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pair production in time-dependent electric fields (Grib, Smolyansky, and collaborators) and the quantum kinetic approach developed in Refs. [Roba, Robb] for near-critical fields and X-ray frequencies. It directly contradicts the prevailing conclusion from earlier work (Bunkin, Avetissian, Piazza, Bulanov) that vacuum decay is not observable with current laser parameters, by showing that the much larger spot volume of optical lasers (\(\sim \lambda^3\)) compared to XFELs can yield observable signals despite far lower field strengths. The results enable experimental searches for nonperturbative QED vacuum effects at existing laser facilities (Jena, SLAC) rather than requiring future XFELs.
