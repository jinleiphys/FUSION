# Quantum Phase Transition in Finite-Size Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Model

> Lipkin model of arbitrary particle-number N is studied in terms of exact differential-operator representation of spin-operators from which we obtain the low-lying energy spectrum with the instanton method of quantum tunneling. Our new observation is that the well known quantum phase transition can also occur in the finite-N model only if N is an odd-number. We furthermore demonstrate a new type of quantum phase transition characterized by level-crossing which is induced by the geometric phase interference and is marvelously periodic with respect to the coupling parameter. Finally the conventional quantum phase transition is understood intuitively from the tunneling formulation in the thermodynamic limit.

## Key claim
The conventional quantum phase transition (CQPT) from deformed to normal phases, which is usually thought to be absent in finite-size systems because quantum tunneling removes degeneracy, can occur in the finite-N Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model only when the particle number N is odd, due to quenching of tunneling by quantum-phase interference.

## Method
The authors map the N-particle LMG model onto a giant-spin Hamiltonian and use an exact differential-operator representation of spin operators to obtain a potential-barrier tunneling Hamiltonian. They then compute the low-lying energy spectrum via the instanton method, evaluating the tunnel splitting from path-integral propagators with stationary-phase approximation, accounting for quantum-phase interference between clockwise and anti-clockwise tunneling paths.

## Key numbers
- For the "-" sign case, the critical transition point is given by \( h_c = \lambda(1-\gamma)(s+1/2) + \lambda\sqrt{(1-\gamma)^2(s+1/2)^2 + 4\gamma s(s+1)} \), which reduces to \( h_c^{th} = \lambda' \) in the thermodynamic limit.
- For the "+" sign case, level-crossing QPT critical points occur at \( h_c(n) = 2\lambda n\sqrt{\gamma} \) for odd-N and \( (2n+1)\lambda\sqrt{\gamma} \) for even-N, where n is an integer.
- The finite-size scaling of the energy gap is \( \Delta E \sim \exp[-N] \), vanishing in the thermodynamic limit for both even and odd N.
- At the critical point for the "+" sign case, the energy gap behaves as \( \Delta E(h \rightarrow h_c) = (\pi/\lambda\sqrt{\gamma}) P(h_c) e^{-W(h_c)} |h - h_c| \).

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, originally invented for nuclear giant monopole studies, by explicitly calculating tunneling effects that previous mean-field treatments (e.g., Dusuel and Vidal (2004), Ribeiro et al. (2005)) could not capture. The discovery of a periodic level-crossing QPT induced by geometric phase interference provides a potential mechanism for realizing qubits in practical spin systems, and the authors note a forthcoming paper on this application.
