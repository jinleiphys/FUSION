# Same-sign WW production in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC as a signal for double parton scattering

> The production of same-sign W-boson pairs from double parton scatterings (DPS) in proton-lead (p-Pb) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider is studied. The signal and background cross sections are estimated with next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations using nuclear parton distribution functions for the Pb ion. At sqrt(sNN) = 8.8 TeV the cross section for the DPS process is about 150 pb, i.e. 600 times larger than that in proton-proton collisions at the same centre-of-mass energy and 1.5 times higher than the pPb --> WW+2-jets single-parton background. The measurement of such a process, where 10 events with fully leptonic W's decays are expected after cuts in 2 pb^{-1}, would constitute an unambiguous DPS signal and would help determine the effective sigma_eff parameter characterising the transverse distribution of partons in the proton.

## Key claim

The production of same-sign W-boson pairs from double parton scatterings (DPS) in proton-lead collisions at the LHC provides an unambiguous DPS signal, with a cross section about 600 times larger than in proton-proton collisions at the same centre-of-mass energy, but this enhancement relies on the simplifying assumption that double parton distribution functions factorize into products of single-parton distributions, which actually provides a conservative estimate of the DPS cross section.

## Method

The authors computed the DPS same-sign WW signal cross section using the factorized formula σDPS_pA→ab = (m/2) × σSPS_pN→a × σSPS_pN→b / σeff_pA, where σeff_pA = σeff_pp / (A + σeff_pp × F_pA) accounts for both same-nucleon and different-nucleon contributions in the lead nucleus. Single-parton W production and the SPS same-sign WW+2-jet background were calculated at NLO accuracy using the MCFM code with CT10 proton PDFs and EPS09 nuclear PDFs for the lead ion, while the vector-boson-fusion WWjj background was computed with VBFNLO.

## Key numbers

- DPS same-sign WW cross section in p-Pb at √s_NN = 8.8 TeV: 152 ± 27 pb
- DPS same-sign WW cross section in p-Pb at √s_NN = 5.0 TeV: 44 ± 8 pb
- Single-W⁺ production cross section in p-Pb at 8.8 TeV: 12.6 ± 1.3 μb
- Single-W⁻ production cross section in p-Pb at 8.8 TeV: 11.1 ± 1.1 μb
- SPS W⁺W⁺jj (QCD) background at 8.8 TeV: 40.4 ± 4.0 pb
- SPS W⁺W⁺jj (VBF) background at 8.8 TeV: 51.8 ± 2.0 pb
- Effective cross section for p-Pb DPS: σeff_pA = 21.5 ± 1.1 μb
- Expected number of DPS same-sign WW events with fully leptonic W decays after cuts in 2 pb⁻¹ integrated luminosity at 8.8 TeV: 10 events
- DPS enhancement factor in p-Pb compared to A-scaled p-N collisions: approximately 3
- Significance enhancement factor for DPS measurements in p-Pb compared to pp: approximately 40

## Context

This work extends the proposal of same-sign WW production as a "smoking gun" DPS signal in pp collisions (Kulesza 1999, Cattaruzza 2005) to proton-nucleus collisions, exploiting the larger transverse parton density in nuclei to enhance the DPS cross section. The calculations use the factorized DPS formalism developed by Paver (1982), Strikman (2001), and others, and employ modern NLO pQCD tools (MCFM, VBFNLO) with nuclear PDFs (EPS09) that properly account for isospin differences in the lead nucleus. The measurement would help determine the effective σeff parameter characterizing the transverse distribution of partons in the proton, independently of pp DPS measurements, since the nuclear transverse density profile is comparatively better known.
