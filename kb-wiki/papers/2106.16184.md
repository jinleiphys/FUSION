# Investigation of neutron transfer in $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn reaction

> The relative importance of neutron transfer and breakup process in reaction around Coulomb barrier energies have been studied for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. Coupled channel calculations have been performed to understand the one neutron stripping and pickup cross sections along with the breakup in the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. The systematics of one and two neutron striping and pickup cross sections with $^7$Li projectile on several targets show an approximate universal behaviour that have been explained by a simple model. Complete reaction mechanism have been studied by comparing the reaction cross sections with cumulative cross sections of total fusion and one neutron transfer.

## Key claim

The paper claims that one-neutron stripping and pickup cross sections for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system, along with systematics across multiple targets, can be described by coupled-channel calculations, but the pickup channel is underpredicted due to incomplete spectroscopic information for the residual nucleus $^{123}$Sn.

## Method

Coupled Reaction Channel (CRC) calculations were performed using both global phenomenological optical model potentials (CRC1) and microscopic Sao Paulo potentials (CRC2) for the $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn system. Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations were used to model the breakup process, and combined CDCC+CRC calculations were performed to simultaneously account for breakup and neutron transfer channels. The calculations used the FRESCO code and included one-neutron stripping to $^{125}$Sn and one-neutron pickup to $^{123}$Sn, with spectroscopic factors taken from Cohen and Kurath (1967) and from Bingh (1973) and Flem (1982) for the residual nuclei.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron stripping cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn were measured in Ref. [VVP18] and compared with CRC1, CRC2, and CDCC+CRC calculations; good agreement was found for states in $^{125}$Sn up to 3 MeV excitation energy.
- One-neutron pickup cross sections for $^{7}$Li+$^{124}$Sn were measured in Ref. [VVP18]; calculations underpredicted the data due to partially available spectroscopic information for $^{123}$Sn.
- The systematic fits to one-neutron stripping data across targets used a modified Wong formula with parameters a = -3.58 MeV and c = 0.44 MeV$^{-1}$ for a separation energy S$_n$ = 7.25 MeV.
- For two-neutron stripping systematics, the fit parameters were a = -2.86 MeV and c = 0.37 MeV$^{-1}$ for S$_{2n}$ = 12.92 MeV.
- For one-neutron pickup systematics, the fit parameters were a = -1.86 MeV and c = 2.16 MeV$^{-1}$ for S$_n$ = 2.03 MeV.
- The CDCC calculations used continuum bins up to k$_{max}$ = 0.8 fm$^{-1}$ with relative orbital angular momenta L = 0, 1, 2, and 3.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of neutron transfer with weakly bound projectiles, following measurements for $^{7}$Li on targets including $^{65}$Cu (Shri06), $^{93}$Nb (Pandit17), $^{124}$Sn (VVP18), $^{197}$Au (Pals14), $^{198}$Pt (Ara13), and $^{209}$Bi (Dasgupta04). The universal behaviour observed for neutron transfer cross sections is similar to that previously reported for $^{9}$Be projectiles by Fang (2016) and for inclusive alpha, triton capture, fusion, and reaction cross sections by Santra (2012), Pandit (2017), Jha (2020), and Kola (2016). The results enable a more complete understanding of the reaction mechanism by comparing total fusion and neutron transfer cross sections with the total reaction cross section, showing that other channels such as proton transfer, non-capture breakup, and target inelastic states may be needed for a full description.
