# The Upper Energy Limit of HBChPT in Pion Photoproduction

> We assess the energy limit up to which Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) can be applied to the process of neutral pion photoproduction from the proton by analyzing the latest data collected by the MAMI/A2 collaboration at Mainz. The high-quality differential cross section and the photon asymmetry measured data allow to test the theory at a level not previously achieved. We find that, within the current experimental status, the agreement between theory and experiment is excellent up to $\sim$170 MeV. Above this energy HBChPT fails to provide high-quality fits while an empirical parametrization of the multipoles still provides an excellent description of the data.

## Key claim

Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) provides an excellent description of neutral pion photoproduction data up to approximately 170 MeV in photon energy, but above this energy the theory fails to produce high-quality fits, while an empirical multipole parametrization remains successful up to about 185 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed single-energy fits to differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data from the MAMI/A2 collaboration, extracting the Legendre coefficients T0, T1, T2, and S0, and from these the real parts of the E0+, E1+, M1+, and M1- multipoles. They then compared three theoretical approaches: HBChPT to O(q^4), relativistic ChPT to O(q^4), and an empirical multipole parametrization without chiral symmetry constraints, fitting each to data up to different maximum photon energies between 158.72 and 191.94 MeV and evaluating the chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The upper energy limit for valid HBChPT description is approximately 170 MeV photon energy in the laboratory frame.
- The empirical fit provides good agreement with data up to approximately 185 MeV.
- The lowest-energy fit used 100 experimental data points (at E_gamma_max = 158.72 MeV) and the highest-energy fit used 514 data points (at E_gamma_max = 191.94 MeV).
- Systematic uncertainties are not included in the chi-squared and can amount to up to 4% in the differential cross section and 5% in the photon asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends previous tests of chiral perturbation theory in neutral pion photoproduction by using the high-precision data from the A2 and CB-TAPS collaborations at MAMI (Hornidge et al.). The analysis establishes a clear energy limit for HBChPT that was not previously quantified, and shows that relativistic ChPT calculations (Hilt et al., 2012) do not improve the agreement. The results suggest that including the Delta(1232) resonance explicitly in the chiral calculation may be necessary to extend the theory above 170 MeV, as proposed by DeltaBChPT approaches.
