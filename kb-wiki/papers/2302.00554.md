# Anomaly-Induced Quenching of ${g_A}$ in Nuclear Matter and Impact on Search for Neutrinoless $ββ$ Decay

> How to disentangle the possible {\it genuine} quenching of $g_A$ caused by scale anomaly of QCD parameterized by the scale-symmetry-breaking quenching factor $q_{ssb}$ from nuclear correlation effects is described. This is done by matching the Fermi-liquid fixed point (FLFP) theory to the ``Extreme Single Particle (shell) Model" (acronym ESPM) in superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions in heavy doubly-magic shell nuclei. The recently experimentally observed indication for $(1-q_{ssb})\neq 0$ -- that one might identify as ``fundamental quenching ({\it FQ})" -- in certain experiments seems to be alarmingly significant. I present arguments how symmetries hidden in the matter-free vacuum can emerge and suppress such {\it FQ} in strong nuclear correlations. How to confirm or refute this observation is discussed in terms of the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in the doubly-magic nucleus $^{100}$Sn and in the spectral shape in the multifold forbidden $β$ decay of $^{115}$In.

# Anomaly-Induced Quenching of \(g_A\) in Nuclear Matter and Impact on Search for Neutrinoless \(\beta\beta\) Decay

## Key claim

The paper argues that a genuine, fundamental quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in nuclear medium can arise from the scale (trace) anomaly of QCD, parameterized by a factor \(q_{ssb}\), which is distinct from the quenching due to ordinary nuclear correlations. The author presents arguments that symmetries hidden in the matter-free vacuum may emerge in strong nuclear correlations to suppress such fundamental quenching, but notes that recent experimental data from RIKEN on the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in \(^{100}\)Sn, if correct, would indicate a large fundamental quenching factor \(q_{ssb} \approx 0.58-0.69\), which would drastically impact searches for neutrinoless double-beta decay.

## Method

The author matches the Fermi-liquid fixed point (FLFP) theory, formulated as a density-functional theory (G\(n\)EFT) incorporating hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry, to the "Extreme Single Particle (shell) Model" (ESPM) for superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions in heavy doubly-magic shell nuclei. The Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation is used to calculate the effective axial coupling \(g_A^L\) of a quasiparticle on the Fermi surface, yielding \(g_A^L \approx 1\) from nuclear correlations alone, while the scale-symmetry-breaking factor \(q_{ssb}\) is introduced via the trace anomaly of QCD, parameterized by the dilaton condensate and the derivative of the QCD beta function at the infrared fixed point.

## Key numbers

- The free-space axial coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.276\).
- The Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point calculation gives \(g_A^L / g_A = q^{\rm Landau}_{snc} \simeq 1/g_A \simeq 0.78\), yielding \(g_A^L \simeq 1\).
- The GSI measurement of the Gamow-Teller strength in \(^{100}\)Sn gives \(\mathcal{B}_{\rm GT}^{\rm GSI} \approx 10\), implying no appreciable fundamental quenching.
- The RIKEN measurement of the Gamow-Teller strength in \(^{100}\)Sn gives \(\mathcal{B}_{\rm GT}^{\rm RIKEN} = 4.4^{+0.9}_{-0.7}\), implying a fundamental quenching factor \(q_{\rm ssb}^{\rm RIKEN} \approx 0.58 - 0.69\).
- The spectral shape of the multifold forbidden \(\beta\) decay of \(^{115}\)In requires a quenching factor \(q^{^{115}{\rm In}} \sim (0.65 - 0.75)\).
- The theoretical prediction for the Gamow-Teller strength in \(^{100}\)Sn with no fundamental quenching is \(\mathcal{B}_{\rm GT}^{\rm theory} \approx 10.8\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous G\(n\)EFT framework (Rho, 2021) for compact-star physics and addresses the long-standing "quenched \(g_A\)" puzzle in nuclear physics, which has been reviewed by many authors (e.g., Wilkinson, 1977). The paper directly confronts the conflicting experimental results from GSI (1990s) and RIKEN (2023) on the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in \(^{100}\)Sn, and discusses implications for neutrinoless double-beta decay searches. The approach contrasts with standard nuclear chiral effective field theory calculations (e.g., "first-principles resolution" by other authors) which attribute quenching to many-body currents without invoking fundamental renormalization. The paper proposes that measurements in two doubly-magic nuclei at different densities could extract the QCD parameters \(\beta^\prime\) and \(c_A\) that govern the anomaly-induced quenching.
