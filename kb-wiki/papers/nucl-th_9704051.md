# Space-time Characteristics of the Fireball from HBT Interferometry

> We present the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrisation of the correlation function. Compared to the conventionally used Cartesian parametrisation, this one provides more straightforward measurement of the duration of the emission process in the fireball and a clearer signal of the longitudinal expansion, which is expected in ulrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrisation of the two-particle correlation function provides a more direct measurement of the emission duration and a clearer signal of longitudinal expansion than the conventional Cartesian parametrisation, but this advantage relies on the assumption of complete chaoticity of the source and on the validity of the Gaussian approximation, with the interpretation of the YK velocity as a signal of longitudinal expansion being based on model studies within a class of thermal models rather than on model-independent results.

## Method

The authors derive model-independent expressions for the YKP radius parameters and the Yano-Koonin velocity in terms of space-time variances of the emission function, assuming azimuthally symmetric sources. They then perform a numerical model study using a thermal emission function with Bjorken-type longitudinal expansion and a linear transverse expansion profile, calculating the YKP parameters for pions and kaons at a temperature of 140 MeV. The results are compared to the Cartesian parametrisation and to the actual effective emission duration and longitudinal velocity of the source.

## Key numbers

- Temperature set to 140 MeV in the model calculation.
- Transverse geometric radius R = 3 fm, space-time rapidity width Δη = 1.2, average freeze-out proper time τ₀ = 3 fm/c, mean proper emission duration Δτ = 1 fm/c.
- Pion mass m_π = 139 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 494 MeV.
- For the case with transverse flow parameter η_f = 0.6, R₀ is about 50% bigger than the real lifetime of the source at high M_⊥.
- For realistic cases, the transverse flow parameter η_f is expected to be about 0.2–0.3.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical development of the YKP parametrisation, previously introduced by Yano and Koonin (1978) and Podgoretskii (1983), and further developed by Chapman, Nix, and Heinz (1995). It contrasts with the conventional Cartesian parametrisation of the correlation function, which mixes spatial and temporal information and requires subtracting two large numbers (R_o² – R_s²) to extract the emission duration, leading to large statistical errors. The paper enables experimental studies of the longitudinal expansion dynamics and emission duration in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, with the caveat that the interpretation of the YK velocity as a signal of longitudinal expansion is based on model studies within a class of thermal models, not on model-independent results.
