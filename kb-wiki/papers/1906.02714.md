# The CKM unitarity problem: A trace of new physics at the TeV scale?

> After the recent high precision determinations of $V_{us}$ and $V_{ud}$, the first row of the CKM matrix shows more than $4σ$ deviation from unitarity. Two possible scenarios beyond the Standard Model can be investigated in order to fill the gap. If a 4th quark $b'$ participates in the mixing, with $\vert V_{ub'} \vert \sim0.04$, then its mass should be no more than 6 TeV or so. A different solution can come from the introduction of the gauge horizontal family symmetry acting between the lepton families and spontaneously broken at the scale of about 6 TeV. Since the gauge bosons of this symmetry contribute to muon decay in positive interference with Standard Model, the Fermi constant is slightly smaller than the muon decay constant so that unitarity is recovered. Also the neutron lifetime problem, that is about $4σ$ discrepancy between the neutron lifetimes measured in beam and trap experiments, is discussed in the light of the these determinations of the CKM matrix elements.

## Key claim

The first row of the CKM matrix shows a more than 4σ deviation from unitarity when using the most recent determinations of |V_ud| and |V_us|, which could indicate new physics at the TeV scale rather than a statistical fluctuation or systematic error.

## Method

The authors performed a two-parameter fit of the three independent measurements of |V_ud|, |V_us|, and |V_us/V_ud| without imposing unitarity, using updated values from new lattice QCD calculations (4-flavor simulations for kaon form factors and decay constants) and a new calculation of short-distance radiative corrections in neutron decay. They then compared the resulting probability distribution to the three-family unitarity condition and explored two beyond-Standard-Model scenarios that could restore unitarity: introducing a fourth down-type quark b' with mixing, or introducing a horizontal gauge symmetry between lepton families.

## Key numbers

- The deviation from CKM unitarity is at the 4.3σ level (Δχ² = 21.6), corresponding to 99.998% confidence level disfavoring unitarity
- For the fourth quark scenario, the required mixing is |V_ub'| = 0.04 ± 0.01 (at 95% C.L.), and the b' mass must be less than about 6 TeV assuming perturbative Yukawa couplings
- The new determination of |V_ud| = 0.97370(14) uses the updated inner radiative correction Δ_R^V = 0.02467(22)
- The new determination of |V_us| = 0.22333(60) uses the 4-flavor lattice QCD result f_+(0) = 0.9696(18)
- The ratio |V_us/V_ud| = 0.23130(50) uses the FLAG 2019 four-flavor average f_K±/f_π± = 1.1932(19)
- The LHC limit on extra b' mass is M > 880 GeV

## Context

This work extends the precision tests of CKM unitarity previously performed by the Particle Data Group (PDG 2018), which showed consistency with unitarity using older data. The new results contradict the earlier PDG findings and are enabled by improved lattice QCD calculations from Bazavov (2018) and FLAG (2019), as well as updated radiative corrections from Seng (2018). The paper discusses two possible new physics explanations: a fourth vector-like down-type quark, or a horizontal gauge symmetry SU(2)_ℓ between lepton families broken at about 6 TeV, which would modify the relationship between the Fermi constant and the muon decay constant. The neutron lifetime problem (a 4σ discrepancy between beam and trap measurements) is also discussed in this context.
