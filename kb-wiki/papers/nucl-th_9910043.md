# The Role of $Δ(1232)$ in Two-pion Exchange Three-nucleon Potential

> In this paper we have studied the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential $(2πE-3NP)$ using an approximate $SU(2) \times SU(2)$ chiral symmetry of the strong interaction. The off-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes obtained from the Weinberg Lagangian are supplemented with contributions from the well-known $σ$-term and the $Δ(1232)$ exchange. It is the role of the $Δ$-resonance in $2πE-3NP$, which we have investigated in detail in the framework of the Lagrangian field theory. The $Δ$-contribution is quite appreciable and, more significantly, it is dependent on a parameter Z which is arbitrary but has the empirical bounds $|Z| \leq 1/2$. We find that the $Δ$-contribution to the important parameters of the $2πE-3NP$ depends on the choice of a value for Z, although the correction to the binding energy of triton is not expected to be very sensitive to the variation of Z within its bounds.

## Key claim

The Δ(1232) resonance makes a large contribution to the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential (2πE-3NP), but the net effect on the triton binding energy correction is not very sensitive to the arbitrary parameter Z (within its empirical bounds |Z| ≤ 1/2) because the Z-dependence of the Δ-contribution to the parameter b is compensated by a Z-dependence in the σ-term contribution.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for off-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the Weinberg Lagrangian (nonlinear chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry) supplemented by a σ-term amplitude and the most general πNΔ interaction Lagrangian containing the parameter Z. They performed a nonrelativistic reduction of these amplitudes to derive the 2πE-3NP in coordinate-space form with parameters a, b, c, d, then estimated the triton binding energy correction by scaling first-order perturbation results from Ishikawa et al. (1990s) using the Reid soft-core two-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The πNΔ coupling constant is taken as f*²/4π = 0.3359
- The σ-term parameter σ_NN(t=2μ²) = 82 MeV, independent of Z
- The slope parameter σ′ varies with Z: 0.66 (Z=1/2), 0.50 (Z=1/4), 0.40 (Z=0), 0.36 (Z=-1/4), 0.37 (Z=-1/2)
- The parameter b ranges from -2.808 μ⁻³ (Z=1/2) to -2.779 μ⁻³ (Z=0 and Z=-1/2), showing little variation
- The parameter d ranges from -0.877 μ⁻³ (Z=1/2) to -0.486 μ⁻³ (Z=-1/4), varying substantially
- The ratio b_Δ/d_Δ ranges from 1.26 (Z=1/2) to 4.0 (Z=-1/2)
- The triton binding energy correction B₃ ranges from 0.92 MeV (Z=1/2) to 0.80 MeV (Z=-1/2), with the experimental binding energy of ³H being 8.48 MeV
- The two-nucleon Reid soft-core potential alone gives B₂ = 7.24 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) potential (Coon et al., 1970s) and the Brazil potential (Coelho, Das, Robilotta, 1980s) by systematically investigating the Z-dependence of the Δ(1232) contribution. The authors show that the often-quoted result b_Δ/d_Δ = 4 holds only for Z = -1/2, not for other allowed values. This enables more reliable three-nucleon force calculations by clarifying that the triton binding energy correction is robust against the uncertainty in Z, though the individual Δ and σ contributions vary. The results are consistent with the finding of Stadler et al. (1990s) and Ishikawa et al. (1990s) that the d-parameter contribution to the binding energy is much smaller than the b-parameter contribution.
