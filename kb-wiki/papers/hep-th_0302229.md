# Fermion production in time-dependent fields

> The exact fermion propagator in a classical time-dependent gauge field is derived by solving the equation of motion for the Dirac Green's functions. From the retarded propagator obtained in this way the momentum spectrum for the produced fermion pairs is calculated. Different approximations and the exact solution for the propagator and the momentum spectrum are presented.

## Key claim

The exact fermion propagator in a classical time-dependent gauge field can be derived by solving the Dirac Green's function equation of motion directly, and from the retarded propagator the momentum spectrum for produced fermion pairs can be calculated; however, the Feynman propagator cannot be expressed as a path-ordered exponential because it is defined with mixed boundary conditions and is always non-invertible in the mixed representation.

## Method

The equation of motion for the Dirac Green's function was solved exactly for classical gauge fields depending on one rectilinear coordinate by Fourier transforming into an ordinary differential equation and factorizing the homogeneous solution into a path-ordered exponential of an integrated argument function. The retarded propagator was obtained by multiplying the homogeneous solution with a Heaviside function, and the momentum spectrum for fermion pair production was calculated from this propagator. Three approximation schemes were developed and compared: a weak-field expansion in powers of the gauge field A (valid for A << ω), a strong-field expansion in powers of the on-shell energy ω (valid for ω << A), and an Abelian approximation that neglects commutators of the Clifford and charge algebra elements.

## Key numbers

- For gluons at RHIC with √s = 130 GeV, the initial occupation number for gluons of average transverse momentum |k_T| ≈ 1 GeV in the center of collisions is roughly equal to 1.5 (Mueller, no year given in text).
- The free homogeneous solution for time-like coordinates yields ω = √(|k|² + m²) as the on-shell energy.
- The error in the Abelian approximation for an interval of width 2Δ scales as O(Δ⁴) for the first-order splitting, proportional to the commutator [g_H(0,Δ,k), dg_H(0,Δ,k)/dΔ]Δ.
- The error in the standard linear-factor product representation for the same interval scales as O(Δ²) and does not vanish even for constant gauge fields.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger constant-field method (Schwinger, 1951) for particle production, which assumes slowly varying fields, to fields with arbitrary time dependence, and provides a non-perturbative alternative to the standard perturbative series and the static approximation (Gromes, no year given in text). The exact solution and its approximations enable self-consistent calculations of decay time scales for classical fields in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where perturbative methods tend to predict times that are too short and the Schwinger method tends to predict development that is too slow. The results are applicable to both QED electron-positron pair production and QCD quark-antiquark pair production in chromoelectric fields formed in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.
