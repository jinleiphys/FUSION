# Nuclear structure in Parity Doublet Model

> Using an extended parity doublet model with the hidden local symmetry, we study some properties of nuclei in the mean field approximation to see if the parity doublet model could reproduce nuclear properties and also to estimate the value of the chiral invariant nucleon mass $m_0$ preferred by nuclear structure. We first determine our model parameters using the inputs from free space and from nuclear matter properties. Then, we study some basic nuclear properties such as the nuclear binding energy with several different choices of the chiral invariant mass. We observe that our results approach the experimental values as $m_0$ is increased until $m_0=700$ MeV and start to deviate more from the experiments afterwards with $m_0$ larger than $m_0=700$ MeV. From this observation, we conclude that $m_0=700$ MeV is preferred by nuclear properties. We then calculate some properties of several selected nuclei with $m_0=700$ MeV and compare them with experiments. Finally, we study the neutron-proton mass difference in some nuclei.

## Key claim
The parity doublet model, when applied to finite nuclei in the mean field approximation, reproduces experimental binding energies and charge radii best when the chiral invariant nucleon mass \(m_0\) is set to 700 MeV; values above or below this yield larger root-mean-square deviations from experiment.

## Method
The authors extended a parity doublet model with hidden local symmetry to include vector mesons (\(\omega\), \(\rho\)) and solved the coupled mean-field equations of motion for the \(\sigma\), \(\omega_0\), \(\rho_0^3\), and Coulomb fields together with the Dirac equation for nucleons in spherical nuclei. Model parameters were first fixed using free-space inputs (nucleon masses, meson masses, pion decay constant) and nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy) for several choices of \(m_0\) between 600 and 900 MeV. Binding energies and charge radii were then computed for 15 stable nuclei and compared with experimental values.

## Key numbers
- The chiral invariant mass \(m_0 = 700\) MeV yields the smallest RMS deviation for binding energy per nucleon: 0.737 MeV (parameter set 1, \(K=240\) MeV) and 0.438 MeV (parameter set 2, \(K=215\) MeV).
- For charge radii, the RMS deviation is smallest at \(m_0=700\) MeV: 0.052 fm (set 1) and 0.046 fm (set 2).
- With a refined parameter set 3 (\(m_0=700\) MeV, \(K=215\) MeV, \(E/A - m_N = -16.3\) MeV, \(E_{\rm sym}=30\) MeV), the RMS deviation for binding energy is 0.204 MeV and for charge radius is 0.045 fm across 15 nuclei.
- The calculated binding energy per nucleon for \(^{208}\)Pb with set 3 is 7.860 MeV (experiment: 7.867 MeV); the charge radius is 5.529 fm (experiment: 5.501 fm).
- The central density in \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca increases with \(m_0\); for \(m_0=700\) MeV (set 2) the density profile is shown.

## Context
This work extends prior parity doublet model studies of dense matter (Motohiro 2015) to finite nuclei for the first time, and it refines the estimate of \(m_0\) from nuclear matter properties (Zschiesche 2006, Gallas 2011) to a specific value of 700 MeV preferred by nuclear structure. The results are compared with the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory using the PC-PK1 functional (Zhao 2010, Xia 2017), showing that the parity doublet model achieves quantitative agreement with experiment. The study enables future inclusion of pairing correlations and deformation to improve the description of open-shell and deformed nuclei.
