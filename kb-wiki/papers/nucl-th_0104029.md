# Properties of $ρ$ and $ω$ Mesons at Finite Temperature and Density as Inferred from Experiment

> The mass shift, width broadening, and spectral density for the $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons in a heat bath of nucleons and pions are calculated using a general formula which relates the self-energy to the real and imaginary parts of the forward scattering amplitude. We use experimental data to saturate the scattering amplitude at low energies with resonances and include a background Pomeron term, while at high energies a Regge parameterization is used. The real part obtained directly is compared with the result of a dispersion integral over the imaginary part. The peaks of the spectral densities are little shifted from their vacuum positions, but the widths are considerably increased due to collisional broadening. Where possible we compare with the UrQMD model and find quite good agreement. At normal nuclear matter density and a temperature of 150 MeV the spectral density of the $ρ$ meson has a width of 345 MeV, while that for the $ω$ is in the range 90--150 MeV.

# Properties of ρ and ω Mesons at Finite Temperature and Density as Inferred from Experiment

## Key claim

The mass shifts of ρ and ω mesons in a heat bath of nucleons and pions are small (at most a few tens of MeV), but the widths are considerably increased due to collisional broadening; however, the authors note that the data are imperfect, particularly for the ω meson, and they adopt two extreme models with reality expected to lie somewhere between them.

## Method

The authors construct forward scattering amplitudes for ρ and ω mesons scattering from pions and nucleons using experimental data: low-energy amplitudes are saturated with Breit-Wigner resonances plus a background Pomeron term, while high-energy amplitudes use a Regge parameterization. The self-energy is then calculated using a leading-order expansion that assumes only single scatterings are important, evaluated in the rest frame of the thermal system. The real part obtained directly is compared with the result of a dispersion integral over the imaginary part to check consistency.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density (n₀ = 0.16 nucleons/fm³) and temperature T = 150 MeV, the spectral density of the ρ meson has a width of 345 MeV (full width at half maximum).
- At the same density and temperature, the ω meson spectral density width is in the range 90–150 MeV, depending on the model.
- The vacuum width of the ρ meson is 150 MeV; the vacuum width of the ω meson is 8.4 MeV.
- At T = 150 MeV with no nucleons (n_N = 0), the collisional width from pions alone is about 50 MeV for the ρ meson and about 50 MeV for the ω meson.
- At n_N = 1 and T = 150 MeV, the ω meson width is enhanced by a factor of 12–18 over its vacuum value.
- The mass shifts for both mesons are at most a few tens of MeV; at zero momentum and n_N = 1, Δm_ω is in the range −15 to +15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Eletsky and Kapusta (EIK) with an improved treatment of the scattering amplitude, including a background Pomeron term and updated resonance parameters. It compares favorably with the UrQMD model and with results from Rapp and coworkers (Rapp, 1999; Rapp, 2000) and Schneider and Weise (2000). The results are intended for use in space-time models of high-energy heavy ion collisions to reproduce observed e⁺e⁻ mass spectra.
