# Equation of state sensitivities when inferring neutron star and dense matter properties

> Understanding the dense matter equation of state at extreme conditions is an important open problem. Astrophysical observations of neutron stars promise to solve this, with NICER poised to make precision measurements of mass and radius for several stars using the waveform modelling technique. What has been less clear, however, is how these mass-radius measurements might translate into equation of state constraints and what are the associated equation of state sensitivities. We use Bayesian inference to explore and contrast the constraints that would result from different choices for the equation of state parametrization; comparing the well-established piecewise polytropic parametrization to one based on physically motivated assumptions for the speed of sound in dense matter. We also compare the constraints resulting from Bayesian inference to those from simple compatibility cuts. We find that the choice of equation of state parametrization and particularly its prior assumptions can have a significant effect on the inferred global mass-radius relation and the equation of state constraints. Our results point to important sensitivities when inferring neutron star and dense matter properties. This applies also to inferences from gravitational wave observations.

## Key claim

The choice of equation of state parametrization and its prior assumptions significantly affect the inferred global mass-radius relation and equation of state constraints from neutron star observations, even when using the same observational data; this sensitivity applies to both NICER X-ray waveform modeling and gravitational wave observations.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using Nested Sampling with the MultiNest tool to compare two equation of state parametrizations: a piecewise polytropic (PP) model with three segments and a speed-of-sound (CS) parametrization based on a logistic function matched to chiral effective field theory calculations up to 1.1 times nuclear saturation density. They generated synthetic mass-radius posterior distributions modeled as bivariate Gaussians for up to four neutron stars, including one with a known mass (PSR J0437-4715 at 1.44 solar masses), and compared the Bayesian posterior constraints to simple compatibility cuts.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Transition density for chiral EFT matching: n_cEFT = 1.1 n0
- Lower 1-sigma mass constraint for neutron star support: 1.97 solar masses (from PSR J0348+0432)
- Speed of sound asymptotic pQCD limit: c_s/c = 1/sqrt(3)
- Fermi liquid theory upper bound at n = 1.5 n0: c_s^2 ≤ 0.163 c^2
- For typical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars, the 68% credible region from the CS prior encloses less than 1 km in radius
- Maximum central density reached: n_max ≈ 8.3 n0

## Context

This work extends the comparison of equation of state parametrizations initiated by Read (2009) for piecewise polytropes and Tews (2018) for sound-speed based models, applying Bayesian inference following the framework of Riley (2018) and Raaijmakers (2018). The results demonstrate that prior choices in parametrization can dominate over likelihood information, which has direct implications for interpreting NICER observations and gravitational wave constraints from events like GW170817 (Abbott 2017). The findings enable more careful design of inference pipelines for upcoming missions including eXTP and STROBE-X.
