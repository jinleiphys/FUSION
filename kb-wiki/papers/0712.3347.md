# Structure of the Lambda(1405) baryon resonance from its large Nc behavior

> We study the behavior with the number of colors (Nc) of the two poles associated to the Lambda(1405) resonance obtained dynamically within the chiral unitary approach. The leading order chiral meson-baryon interaction manifests a nontrivial Nc dependence for SU(3) baryons, which gives a finite attractive interaction in some channels in the large Nc limit. As a consequence, the SU(3) singlet (Kbar N) component of the Lambda(1405) survives in the large Nc limit as a bound state, while the other components dissolve into the continuum. The Nc dependence of the decay widths shows different behavior from the general counting rule for a qqq state, indicating the dynamical origin of the two poles for the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Key claim

The two poles associated with the Lambda(1405) resonance, dynamically generated within the chiral unitary approach, show Nc scaling of their decay widths that contradicts the general counting rule for a qqq state, indicating that the resonance is not predominantly a three-quark state but rather has a dynamical (molecular) origin. The SU(3) singlet (Kbar N) component survives in the large Nc limit as a bound state, while the other components dissolve into the continuum.

## Method

The authors study the Nc dependence of the two poles of the Lambda(1405) resonance using the chiral unitary approach, where the scattering amplitude T = [1 - VG]^{-1} V is solved with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel as the driving force. The Nc dependence is introduced by scaling the baryon masses as M_i proportional to Nc, meson masses as m_i proportional to 1, and the meson decay constant f as proportional to sqrt(Nc), with the coupling strengths C_{ij} in the SU(3) and isospin bases carrying explicit Nc dependence from group theory. The pole trajectories are tracked from Nc=3 to Nc=12 in the complex energy plane, and the regularization scheme dependence is checked by comparing the dispersion integral method with a cutoff scheme using two different Nc scalings for the cutoff.

## Key numbers

- At Nc=3, the two poles associated with the Lambda(1405) are located at z1 = -20 - 15i MeV and z2 = -61 - 66i MeV, where the real part is the excitation energy measured from the Kbar N threshold and the imaginary part is the half width.
- The coupling strengths in the SU(3) basis are given by the diagonal matrix C_{ij}^{SU(3)}(Nc) = diag((9+Nc)/2, 3, 3, (-1-Nc)/2) for the channels 1, 8, 8', and 27.
- The critical coupling for bound state formation is C_crit(Nc) = 2[f(Nc)]^2 / (m_i [-G(M_i(Nc)+m_i)]), which increases more slowly than the Nc/2 growth of the singlet channel coupling strength.
- The diagonal Kbar N coupling strength in the isospin basis is (Nc+3)/2, and the diagonal K Xi channel coupling is (-Nc+9)/2, changing sign from attractive to repulsive for Nc greater than 9.

## Context

This work extends the large Nc methodology previously applied to mesonic resonances (largeNCmesons, newaxialsNc) into the baryon sector for the first time, focusing on the Lambda(1405) resonance that is well reproduced in the chiral unitary approach as a dynamically generated state (Oller, 2000; Jido, 2003). The study builds on the nontrivial Nc dependence of the leading order chiral meson-baryon interaction for SU(3) baryons (Hyodo, 2007) and contrasts with the Nc behavior of baryon resonances studied in the SU(6) symmetric limit (Garcia-Recio, 2006). The results enable future discrimination between molecular and qqq components in other baryonic resonances and connect to studies of kaonic nuclei and kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan, 1986).
