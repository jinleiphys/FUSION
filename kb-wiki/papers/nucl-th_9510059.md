# Optimized $δ$ expansion for the Walecka model

> The optimized $δ$-expansion is used to study vacuum polarization effects in the Walecka model. The optimized $δ$-expansion is a nonperturbative approach for field theoretic models which combines the techniques of perturbation theory and the variational principle. Vacuum effects on self-energies and the energy density of nuclear matter are studied up to ${\cal O}(δ^2)$. When exchange diagrams are neglected, the traditional Relativistic Hartree Approximation (RHA) results are exactly reproduced and, using the same set of parameters that saturate nuclear matter in the RHA, a new stable, tightly bound state at high density is found.

## Key claim
The optimized δ-expansion, when applied to the Walecka model up to O(δ²) and neglecting exchange diagrams, exactly reproduces the Relativistic Hartree Approximation (RHA) results; using the same RHA parameter set, the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) applied to the renormalized energy density reveals a second, new stable and tightly bound state of nuclear matter at high density, though the authors note that exchange terms (of the same order in δ) must be included for consistency.

## Method
The authors applied the optimized linear δ-expansion to the Walecka model Lagrangian, interpolating between a solvable free theory (with a variational mass parameter μ) and the full interacting theory. They calculated the nucleon self-energy and the energy density of nuclear matter up to O(δ²) in the δ-expansion, using dimensional regularization and on-mass-shell renormalization. The unknown parameter μ was fixed by the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS), requiring the energy density to be stationary with respect to μ, which yields a self-consistent equation for the effective nucleon mass Ω*.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation in the RHA (reproduced by the δ-expansion) occurs at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm⁻¹, with a binding energy of 16 MeV per nucleon, using parameters g_s² = 62.89, g_v² = 79.78, m_s = 550 MeV, m_v = 783 MeV, and M = 939 MeV.
- The new high-density saturation point from the PMS solution occurs at k_F ≈ 5.3 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a density approximately 50 times normal nuclear matter density, with a binding energy of about 570 MeV per nucleon.

## Context
This work extends the application of the optimized δ-expansion, previously used to reproduce Dirac-Hartree-Fock results without vacuum effects (Krein, Menezes, Pinto, 1995), to include vacuum polarization in the Walecka model. It directly reproduces the standard Relativistic Hartree Approximation (RHA) results of Serot and Walecka (1986) and the renormalization framework of Bielajew and Serot (1984). The new high-density state found via the PMS condition suggests a possible phase transition in nuclear matter, but the authors caution that exchange diagrams (Fock terms) must be included for a consistent calculation, which is left for future work.
