# Nonperturbative parton distributions and the proton spin problem

> The Lorentz contracted form of the static wave functions is used to calculate the valence parton distributions for mesons and baryons, boosting the rest frame solutions of the path integral Hamiltonian. It is argued that nonperturbative parton densities are due to excited multigluon baryon states. A simple model is proposed for these states ensuring realistic behavior of valence and sea quarks and gluon parton densities at $Q^2= 10 (GeV/c)^2$. Applying the same model to the proton spin problem one obtains $Σ_3 = 0.18 $ for the same $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parton distributions measured in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) do not reflect the ground state proton wave function but instead arise from highly excited multigluon baryon states, and that applying this multihybrid model to the proton spin problem yields a value of Σ₃ = 0.18 at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)², which is significantly smaller than the naive quark model expectation.

## Method

The author uses Lorentz-contracted static wave functions from the path integral Hamiltonian formalism to compute valence parton distributions for mesons and baryons. A multihybrid baryon model is introduced where excited states consist of three quarks plus N gluons "sitting" on the QCD confining string, with the total energy given by M_N = 3ω + Nm, where ω and m are the center-of-mass energies of the quark and gluon respectively. The parton distribution functions are constructed by summing over the multihybrid sequence with coefficients |C_N|² ~ N^{-3/2} for large N, and the resulting expressions are evaluated at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)².

## Key numbers

- The gluon energy parameter is taken as m = 0.7 GeV, with κ = 0.36 GeV and ω = κ = m/2 = 0.313 GeV, leaving the problem without free parameters.
- The proton spin projection Σ₃ = 0.18 is obtained at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)².
- The normalization condition gives |c₀|² + |c̄|² ζ(3/2) = 1, where ζ(3/2) = 2.62.
- At x = 2/3, the gluon distribution satisfies xg(x) = 0.11 |c̄|².
- At small x ≪ 0.1, the gluon distribution behaves as g(x) = |c̄|² (1.5 x^{-1/2} + x^{-3/2}).
- At small x ≪ 0.1, the valence u-quark distribution behaves as u(x) = 1.5 |c₀|² + |c̄|² (2.82 x^{-1/2} + 2.70).
- At x = 1/3, the valence u-quark distribution is u(x) = 4.05 |c₀|² + 1.74 |c̄|².

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz contraction formalism developed in Simonov (2014) for computing parton distributions from nonperturbative wave functions, and addresses the proton spin problem discussed in the review by Bass (2005). The approach contrasts with standard light-cone quark models reviewed by Brodsky et al. (1998) and with the DGLAP evolution framework, by arguing that DIS data probe highly excited baryon states rather than the ground state proton. The multihybrid model provides a nonperturbative input at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)² that can serve as an initial condition for DGLAP evolution, and may help resolve the discrepancy between the naive quark model expectation and the measured small contribution of quark spins to the proton spin.
