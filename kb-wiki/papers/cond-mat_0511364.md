# Ginzburg-Landau theory of superconducting surfaces under electric fields

> A boundary condition for the Ginzburg-Landau wave function at surfaces biased by a strong electric field is derived within the de Gennes approach. This condition provides a simple theory of the field effect on the critical temperature of superconducting layers.

# Ginzburg-Landau theory of superconducting surfaces under electric fields

## Key claim
The Ginzburg-Landau boundary condition for a superconducting surface under a strong electric field is derived, showing that the field-induced correction to the critical temperature depends on the ratio of the gap at the surface to the bulk value, a factor that previous simple estimates omitted.

## Method
The authors start from the de Gennes boundary condition for the Ginzburg-Landau wave function, which expresses the logarithmic derivative at the surface in terms of an integral over the local density of states and the gap function. They incorporate the electric field via the Anderson theorem, assuming the field modifies the density of states through a local linear expansion in the electron density deviation, and then evaluate the resulting integral using the Thomas-Fermi screening approximation. The effective potential entering the boundary condition is expressed in terms of standard Ginzburg-Landau parameters and the logarithmic derivative of the critical temperature with respect to electron density.

## Key numbers
- For niobium: effective potential \( U_{\rm s} = -1.3 \times 10^6 \) V, with GL parameter \( \kappa = 0.78 \), logarithmic derivative \( \partial \ln T_{\rm c} / \partial \ln n = 0.75 \), and effective mass \( m \approx 1.2 m_{\rm e} \).
- For the overdoped cuprate sample Sm\(_{0.7}\)Ca\(_{0.3}\)Cu\(_3\)O\(_y\) (T\(_{\rm c}\) = 50 K): effective potential \( U_{\rm s} = -56.6 \) V, GL parameter \( \kappa = 100 \), coherence length \( \xi(0) = 1.3 \) nm, carrier density \( n = 5.75 \times 10^{21} \) /cm\(^3\), effective mass \( m = 3.46 m_{\rm e} \), and logarithmic derivative \( \partial \ln T_{\rm c} / \partial \ln n = -3.12 \).
- For the cuprate sample with applied effective field \( E = 7.8 \times 10^7 \) V/cm and layer width \( L = 50 \) nm: \( |EL/U_{\rm s}| = 6.9 \), giving a predicted suppression \( T^* - T_{\rm c} = -0.064 \) K and a predicted enhancement \( T^* - T_{\rm c} = 1.6 \) K (compared to the reported shift of 1 K).
- The maximal depression of the critical temperature in the thick layer limit is bounded by \( T_{\rm c} - T^* < T_{\rm c} \pi^2 \xi^2(0) / (4L^2) \), yielding for the cuprate sample a lower estimate of 0.08 K.

## Context
This work extends the de Gennes (1966) approach to boundary conditions for the Ginzburg-Landau wave function by including electric field effects, building on earlier experimental studies of field effects on superconducting layers (Glover and Sherrill, 1960; Frey and Mannhart, 1995; Ahn et al., 2003). The derived boundary condition provides a simple theoretical framework for interpreting field-effect experiments on thin superconducting layers, and recovers the surface superconductivity predicted by Shapiro (1985) in the thick-layer limit. The theory enables quantitative comparison with experiments such as those by Matijasevic et al. (1994) on cuprate superconductors, and clarifies that the surface gap ratio modifies the linear coefficient compared to earlier simple estimates.
