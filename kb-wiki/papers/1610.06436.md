# Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation for the fermion propagator in QED in arbitrary dimensions

> We explore the dependence of fermion propagators on the covariant gauge fixing parameter in quantum electrodynamics (QED) with the number of spacetime dimensions kept explicit. Gauge covariance is controlled by the the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation (LKFT). Utilizing its group nature, the LKFT for a fermion propagator in Minkowski space is solved exactly. The special scenario of 3D is used to test claims made for general cases. When renormalized correctly, a simplification of the LKFT in 4D has been achieved with the help of fractional calculus.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have solved the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation (LKFT) for the fermion propagator in Minkowski space exactly by utilizing its group nature, but this solution is achieved under the assumption that the fermion propagator admits a spectral representation with specific analytic structures (branch cuts and poles) and that the spectral representation is bijective.

## Method

The authors derive the LKFT in differential form for the momentum-space fermion propagator by taking a first-order derivative with respect to the gauge parameter xi and then Fourier transforming. They introduce a spectral representation for the fermion propagator in terms of two spectral functions rho_1 and rho_2, corresponding to the Dirac vector and scalar components. Using the group properties of the LKFT, they express the gauge dependence of the spectral functions as an exponential of a distribution, with the generator Phi_j determined by evaluating an effective one-loop integral using Feynman parameterization and dimensional regularization in d = 4 - 2 epsilon dimensions.

## Key numbers

* The number of spacetime dimensions is parameterized as d = 4 - 2 epsilon, where epsilon is related to dimensional regularization.
* The LKFT differential equation for the momentum-space fermion propagator is given by Eq. (12): partial/partial xi S_F(p; xi) = i e^2 integral d underline l (1/(l^4 + i epsilon)) [S_F(p; xi) - S_F(p - l; xi)].
* The functions Xi_j(p^2, s) characterizing the LKFT in spectral form are given by Eqs. (36) and (37), which involve integrals over Feynman parameter x from 0 to 1 and the ratio z = p^2/s, with a factor Gamma(epsilon)(4 pi mu^2/s)^epsilon.
* The spectral representation for the fermion propagator is S_j(p^2; xi) = integral from m^2 to +infinity ds rho_j(s; xi) / (p^2 - s + i epsilon) for j = 1, 2.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation formalism, originally formulated for coordinate-space Green's functions by Landau (1955), Fradkin (1955), and Zumino (1959), to directly solve for the momentum-space fermion propagator using spectral representations. The authors note that previous work by Bashir (2002) required lengthy Fourier transforms to check gauge covariance of Schwinger-Dyson equation solutions. The paper enables future consistency checks between LKFT solutions and Schwinger-Dyson equation truncation schemes, and provides a framework applicable to QED in 3D and 4D simultaneously by keeping the number of spacetime dimensions explicit.
