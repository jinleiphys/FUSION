# Fission fragment distributions of neutron-rich nuclei based on Langevin calculations: toward r-process simulations

> The nuclear fission of very neuron-rich nuclei related to the r-process is essential for the termination of nucleosynthesis flows on the nuclear chart and the final abundances. Nevertheless, most of the available fission data for the r-process calculations are based on theory predictions, including phenomenological treatments. In this study, we calculated a series of nuclear fission distribution for neutron-rich nuclei away from the beta-stability line. As most of these nuclei are experimentally unknown, we are based on theoretical calculations based on the dynamical fission model with the Langevin method. We performed fission distribution calculations for neutron-rich actinoid nuclei, applicable to the r-process nucleosynthesis simulations. In the present paper, we compared the obtained mass and charge distributions with experimental data. We also show the results of the systematic behaviour of mass distribution for neutron-rich U and Fm isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Langevin-based dynamical fission calculations can reproduce experimental fission fragment distributions for less neutron-rich uranium isotopes, and that the transition from asymmetric to symmetric fission occurs between specific mass numbers (e.g., between 252U and 253U, and between 254Fm and 255Fm), but they note that the exact boundary disagrees with previous work (which suggested A = 257) due to uncertainties in model parameters and initial conditions.

## Method

The authors solved multi-dimensional Langevin equations of motion on a three-parameter deformation space (distance between fragments, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) using the two-centre-shell model for the nuclear shape. They calculated fission fragment mass distributions for neutron-rich uranium (Z=92) and fermium (Z=100) isotopes at low excitation energies (E* = 7 MeV for the neutron-rich cases, 10 MeV for 236U). Charge distributions were obtained by applying the unchanged charge distribution (UCD) assumption with a Gaussian width of sigma = 0.01.

## Key numbers

- For 236U at excitation energy E* = 10 MeV, the calculated mass and charge distributions showed good agreement with experimental data from JENDL-4.0.
- For neutron-rich uranium isotopes (250U to 256U) at E* = 7 MeV, the fission fragment distribution changed from two peaks (asymmetric) to one peak (symmetric) between 252U and 253U.
- For fermium isotopes (250Fm to 256Fm) at E* = 7 MeV, the transition from asymmetric to symmetric fission occurred between 254Fm and 255Fm.
- The authors applied a Gaussian width of sigma = 0.01 for the charge distribution along the N/Z line based on the UCD assumption.

## Context

This work extends dynamical fission calculations using the Langevin method, previously applied by Tanaka (2019) and Miyamoto (2019), to very neutron-rich actinoid nuclei relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations. The results contradict the earlier suggested boundary of A = 257 for the symmetric fission transition in the trans-uranium region, as reported in Miyamoto (2019). The systematic behaviour of fission fragment distributions for U through Db isotopes (Tanaka et al., in preparation) can be used to constrain r-process abundance calculations and, conversely, r-process observations can help restrict fission mechanisms.
