# Density fluctuations and chiral phase transition

> Based on an effective QCD Lagrangian we discuss the properties of charge density fluctuations in the vicinity of chiral phase transition. We explore thermodynamics in the presence of spinodal phase separation. We show that appearance of spinodal decomposition in a non-equilibrium first order phase transition results in divergence of the charge density fluctuations related with the electric charge and baryon number conservation. Consequently, divergent fluctuations at the chiral phase transition are not only attributed to the critical end point but are also there along the first order phase transition if the spinodal phase separation take place. Based on the mean field dynamics, the critical exponents for these singular behavior of charge susceptibilities are also discussed.

# Density fluctuations and chiral phase transition

## Key claim
The authors show that divergent charge density fluctuations at the chiral phase transition are not exclusively a signature of the critical end point (CEP); such divergences also appear along the first-order phase transition line when spinodal phase separation occurs in a non-equilibrium system. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the first-order transition proceeds out of equilibrium, as in heavy-ion collisions, rather than in the idealized equilibrium case.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, an effective chiral Lagrangian for two quark flavors, in the mean-field approximation to study thermodynamics of the chiral phase transition. They compute the thermodynamic potential, dynamical quark mass, pressure, and net quark number density self-consistently, then derive the net quark number susceptibility and identify spinodal lines from the condition where the isothermal pressure derivative with respect to volume vanishes. The critical exponents for the susceptibility are extracted numerically and compared to analytic results from a Ginzburg-Landau potential.

## Key numbers
- At the isothermal spinodal line, the critical exponent for the net quark number susceptibility is γ = 1/2.
- At the critical end point (CEP), the critical exponent is γ = 2/3, in agreement with mean-field results.
- The dynamical quark mass is shown at fixed temperature T = 30 MeV as a function of quark chemical potential, with the equilibrium first-order transition indicated.
- The net quark number susceptibility is shown at T = 50 MeV as a function of quark number density across the first-order phase transition.
- The susceptibility scaling is studied as a function of reduced chemical potential t = (μ − μ_c)/μ_c at fixed T = 30 MeV and at T = T_CEP.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of charge fluctuations as signals of the QCD critical end point, particularly the predictions by Stephanov (1998) and Hatta and Ikeda (2003) that fluctuations diverge at the CEP. The authors show that spinodal decomposition, previously studied in the context of nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions (experimental evidence reported by the ALADIN collaboration), produces similar divergent behavior along the first-order transition line. This implies that non-monotonic fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions could arise over a broader range of beam energies than previously expected, not only at the CEP. The results are based on the NJL model but the authors argue the conclusion is model-independent.
