# Isospin dependence in single-nucleon removal cross sections explained through valence-core destruction effects

> The discrepancy between experimental data and theoretical calculations in one-nucleon removal reactions at intermediate energies (quantified by the so-called "quenching factors") and its dependence on the isospin asymmetry of the nuclei has been an open problem in nuclear physics for the last fifteen years. In this work, we propose an explanation for this long-standing problem, which relies on the inclusion of the process of core destruction due to its interaction with the removed nucleon. To include this effect, we extend the commonly used eikonal formalism via an effective nucleon density, and apply it to a series of nucleon knockout reactions. The effect of core destruction is found to depend strongly on the binding energy of the removed nucleon, leading to a significant reduction of the cross section for deeply bound nucleons, which reduces the isospin dependence of the "quenching factors", making them more consistent with the trends found in transfer and (p,pN) reactions.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between experimental and theoretical cross sections in one-nucleon knockout reactions, quantified by "quenching factors" that depend on isospin asymmetry, can be largely explained by including the destruction of the residual core through its interaction with the removed nucleon, an effect previously omitted from standard eikonal calculations.

## Method

The authors extended the standard eikonal formalism by replacing the closure approximation with an explicit calculation of continuum wavefunctions using a complex valence-core optical potential, producing an effective two-dimensional local density that accounts for core destruction. They applied this formalism to stripping reactions for neutron and proton removal from ⁴⁰Si, ²⁴Si, and ¹²C, using the imaginary part of the Morillon global dispersive potential (2007) for the nucleon-core interaction, with compound-nucleus calculations from PACE (Model I) and GEMINI (Model II) to rescale the reaction cross sections by removing the elastic compound-nucleus contribution.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Si, the valence neutron in the 1f₇/₂ orbital is bound by 4.72 MeV, while the valence proton in the 1d₅/₂ orbital is bound by 23.1 MeV.
- The ratio of stripping cross sections with core destruction to standard eikonal calculations is approximately 0.5 for the more deeply bound nucleon and approximately 0.9 for the weakly bound nucleon.
- The slope of the quenching factor R_s as a function of ΔS (difference in proton-neutron separation energy) is reduced from the original -0.013 MeV⁻¹ to -0.004 MeV⁻¹ for Model I and -0.005 MeV⁻¹ for Model II.
- The effective density calculation without core destruction agrees with standard eikonal calculations to within at most 1.5% difference in stripping cross section.

## Context

This work addresses the fifteen-year-old puzzle of isospin-dependent quenching factors in knockout reactions first systematically reported by Gade et al. (2008), which had been interpreted as evidence for short-range correlations but contradicted by transfer reactions (Kay 2013, Flavigny 2013, 2018) and (p,2p) reactions (Atar 2018, Gomez-Ramos 2018, Holl 2019). The proposed core destruction mechanism makes knockout reaction results consistent with these other reaction types and with coupled-cluster calculations for oxygen isotopes (Jensen 2011), suggesting that future work should focus on better determining the nucleon-core interaction from first-principles calculations or direct measurements, particularly for exotic nuclei with large isospin asymmetry.
