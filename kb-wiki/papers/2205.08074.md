# Discrete ambiguities in a partial-wave analysis of pseudoscalar photoproduction with truncation in total angular momentum

> The discrete ambiguities appearing in the complete experiment problem for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction within truncated partial-wave analysis are discussed. It is shown that, in addition to the double ambiguity known from previous works, it is always necessary to take into account another ambiguity arising when truncation in total angular momentum is employed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in truncated partial-wave analysis of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, there is always a second discrete ambiguity (beyond the well-known double ambiguity from complex conjugation of polynomial coefficients) that arises specifically when the partial-wave expansion is truncated in total angular momentum \(j\), and this second ambiguity is approximate when truncation is done in orbital angular momentum \(l\).

## Method

The authors analyze the mathematical structure of transversity amplitudes \(b_i(\theta)\) and helicity amplitudes \(H_{\mu\lambda}(\theta)\) for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, using both the Gersten roots method (polynomials \(A_{2L}, B_{2L}\) in \(x=\tan(\theta/2)\)) and the expansion in Wigner rotation matrices \(d^j_{\lambda\mu}\). They derive the transformation properties of the 16 polarization observables under complex conjugation of the polynomial coefficients (the known double ambiguity) and under a new transformation \(H_1\to H^*_1, H_2\to -H^*_2, H_3\to -H^*_3, H_4\to H^*_4\) (the new ambiguity), and demonstrate numerically at \(W=1300\) MeV for \(\gamma p\to\pi^0 p\) using MAID2007 multipoles truncated at \(L=2\) how the two ambiguities differ in their effect on observables.

## Key numbers

- \(W = 1300\) MeV: the energy at which numerical calculations are performed for \(\gamma p\to\pi^0 p\) using MAID2007 multipole amplitudes.
- \(L = 2\): the maximum orbital angular momentum used in the truncation (multipoles from \(E_{0^+}\) to \(E_{2^+}, M_{2^+}\) are included).
- \(2L\): the order of the polynomials \(A_{2L}\) and \(B_{2L}\) in \(x=\tan(\theta/2)\), giving \(4L\) independent coefficients mapped to \(4L\) multipole amplitudes.
- \(2J\): the order of the polynomials \(C_{2J}\) and \(D_{2J}\) when truncation is in total angular momentum \(j_\mathrm{max}=J\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Gersten (1970) and Omelaenko (1981) on discrete ambiguities in partial-wave analysis of pseudoscalar photoproduction, and builds on the truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA) approach developed by Grushin (1982), Omelaenko (1981), and Wunderlich (2014-2017).
