# Collectivity in small and large amplitude microscopic mean-field dynamic

> The time-dependent energy density functional with pairing allows to describe a large variety of phenomena from small to large amplitude collective motion. Here, we briefly summarize the recent progresses made in the field using the TD-BCS approach. A focus is made on the mapping of the microscopic mean-field dynamic to the macroscopic dynamic in collective space. A method is developed to extract the collective mass parameter from TD-EDF. Illustration is made on the fission of $^{258}$Fm. The collective mass and collective momentum associated to quadrupole deformation including non-adiabatic effects is estimated along the TD-EDF path. With these information, the onset of dissipation during fission is discussed.

# Collectivity in small and large amplitude microscopic mean-field dynamic

## Key claim
The time-dependent energy density functional with pairing (TD-BCS) can describe both small and large amplitude collective motion, but the collective mass extracted from the microscopic evolution deviates from the adiabatic limit near scission, and approximately 10% of the final total kinetic energy is dissipated into internal degrees of freedom during fission.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent BCS (TD-BCS) calculations for the fission of ²⁵⁸Fm, solving the evolution of quasi-particle states in their self-consistent mean-field and pairing field without pre-selecting collective degrees of freedom. They developed a method to extract the collective mass and collective momentum associated with quadrupole deformation directly from the TD-EDF evolution, using the time-dependent density to compute the mass parameter M(t) = m / Tr[ρ(t) ∇Q · ∇Q]. The collective momentum was then used to analyze the energy balance and dissipation along the fission path.

## Key numbers
- The total kinetic energy (TKE) obtained in the symmetric compact fission of ²⁵⁸Fm is close to 200 MeV.
- Approximately 10% of the final TKE is dissipated during fission, corresponding to around 20 MeV of initial energy transferred to internal degrees of freedom.
- The dissipated energy can approach 10% of the final TKE of the daughter nuclei after fission.

## Context
This work extends recent efforts to give a unified description of small and large amplitude collective motion within microscopic TD-EDF theory, including pairing effects that have opened new perspectives for giant resonances (Scamps 2013, 2014) and direct reactions like nucleon transfer (Scamps 2013). The fission analysis builds on prior TD-BCS studies (Scamps 2015, Tanimura 2015) and earlier work without pairing (Simenel 2014, Goddard 2014). The method for extracting collective mass and momentum from TD-EDF, proposed in Tanimura (2015), enables direct connection between microscopic transport models and macroscopic collective models, and allows quantification of dissipative effects during fission that can lead to particle evaporation.
