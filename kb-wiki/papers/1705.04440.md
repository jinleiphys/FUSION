# Conserved charge fluctuations using the $D$-measure in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the net-charge fluctuations, $D$-measure variable, in high energy heavy-ion collisions in heavy-ion jet interaction generator (HIJING), ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) and hadron resonance gas (HRG) models for various center-of-mass energies (\sqsn). The effect of kinematic acceptance and resonance decay, in the pseudorapidity acceptance interval ($Δη$) and lower transverse momentum ($p_{T}^{min}$) threshold, on fluctuation measures are discussed. A strong dependence of $D$ with the $Δη$ in HIJING and UrQMD models is observed as opposed to results obtained from the HRG model. The dissipation of fluctuation signal is estimated by fitting the $D$-measure as a function of the $Δη$. An extrapolated function for higher $Δη$ values at lower \sqsn is different from the results obtained from models. Particle species dependence of $D$ and the effect of the $p_T^{min}$ selection threshold are discussed in HIJING and HRG models. The comparison of $D$, at midrapidity, of net-charge fluctuations at various \sqsn obtained from the models with the data from the ALICE experiment is discussed. The results from the present work as a function of $Δη$ and \sqsn will provide a baseline for comparison to experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $D$-measure of net-charge fluctuations in HIJING and UrQMD models shows a strong dependence on the pseudorapidity acceptance interval ($\Delta\eta$) with a decreasing trend continuing up to large $\Delta\eta$, in contrast to the flattening behavior predicted by extrapolation of ALICE experimental data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV; the HRG model calculations do not show $\Delta\eta$ dependence of $D$.

## Method

The authors calculated the $D$-measure for net-charge fluctuations using three models: HIJING (a perturbative QCD-based event generator with minijet partons and string fragmentation), UrQMD (a microscopic transport model with constituent quarks and hadronic degrees of freedom), and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model (a thermal model including all hadrons up to 2.5 GeV mass with resonance decay contributions). They studied the $D$ as a function of pseudorapidity interval ($\Delta\eta$), collision energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6, 200, and 2760 GeV), lower transverse momentum threshold ($p_T^{min}$), and particle species (net-pion, net-kaon, net-proton) for central (0%-5%) Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. The diffusion parameter $\sigma$ was extracted by fitting $\langle N_{ch}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}^{corr}$ with an error function $Erf(\Delta\eta/\sqrt{8}\sigma)$ up to $\Delta\eta = 2.0$.

## Key numbers

- For HIJING and UrQMD models at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the $D$ values from both models agree for all $\Delta\eta$ intervals within statistical uncertainties, with $D$ decreasing as a function of $\Delta\eta$
- The ALICE experiment reported $D = 2.3 \pm 0.22$ for $\Delta\eta = 1.6$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (Pb+Pb collisions)
- The HRG model calculation with proper kinematic acceptance and resonance decay contributions yields $D \simeq 4.4$, serving as a baseline for hadronic phase comparison
- The diffusion parameter $\sigma$ was extracted by fitting $\langle N_{ch}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}^{corr}$ values up to $\Delta\eta = 2.0$ using the error function
- For HIJING model at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, a saturation trend in $D$ is observed after $p_T^{min} \gtrsim 0.5$ GeV/$c$ for net-charge and net-pion fluctuations

## Context

This work extends studies of net-charge fluctuations using the $D$-measure from earlier experimental measurements at SPS (Sako 2004), RHIC (Abelev 2008), and LHC (Abelev 2012) energies, where the ALICE experiment at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV found $D$ values lower than at lower energies and approaching QGP expectations. The study provides a baseline for comparison with experimental data by calculating $D$ in HIJING, UrQMD, and HRG models with realistic kinematic acceptance effects, including resonance decay contributions and diffusion in rapidity space as discussed by Aziz (2004) and Shuryak (2000). The results enable future experimental measurements to distinguish between hadronic and QGP phase contributions to charge fluctuations by comparing data with the model calculations across different $\Delta\eta$ intervals and collision energies.
