# What can we learn from hydrodynamic analysis at RHIC?

> We can establish a new picture, the perfect fluid sQGP core and the dissipative hadronic corona, of the space-time evolution of produced matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It is also shown that the picture works well also in the forward rapidity region through an analysis based on a new class of the hydro-kinetic model and that this is a manifestation of rapid increase of entropy density in the vicinity of QCD critical temperature, namely deconfinement.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the success of ideal hydrodynamic descriptions of RHIC data does not imply perfect fluidity throughout the entire evolution; instead, the data are consistent with a picture where a perfect fluid strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (sQGP) core is surrounded by a dissipative hadronic corona, and this picture works also in forward rapidity regions.

## Method
The author systematically compares three different assumptions for the hadron phase in hydrodynamic simulations: chemical equilibrium (CE), partial chemical equilibrium (PCE), and hadronic cascade (HC) models. A hybrid dynamical model is developed that combines fully three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the QGP phase with a kinetic hadronic cascade model (JAM) for the hadron phase, using color glass condensate (CGC) initial conditions.

## Key numbers
- Impact parameter b = 5 fm and collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV used in hydrodynamic simulations for mean transverse momentum calculations
- Critical temperature T_c = T^ch = 170 MeV used in the hydrodynamic model
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamic to kinetic description T_sw = 169 MeV in the hybrid model
- Shear viscosity around T_c estimated as eta ~ 0.1 GeV/fm^2 for both QGP and hadron phases
- Minimum conjectured eta/s = 1/4pi from supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory, cited as a possible value for the QGP phase

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic analyses of elliptic flow at RHIC (Huovinen 2003, Heinz 2003) by showing that the common assumption of chemical equilibrium in the hadron phase artificially reproduces data through a "reheating" effect that mimics dissipation. The hybrid model with CGC initial conditions (Nonaka 2005) enables extraction of transport properties of the sQGP, while also revealing that initial condition uncertainties (instability 2005) must be resolved before drawing conclusions about QGP viscosity in peripheral collisions. The picture supports lattice QCD predictions (Karsch 2002) of a rapid increase in entropy density near the QCD critical temperature as the signature of deconfinement.
