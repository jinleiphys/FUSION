# Astrophysical relevance of $γ$ transition energies

> The relevant gamma energy range is explicitly identified where additional gamma$ strength has to be located for having an impact on astrophysically relevant reactions. It is shown that folding the energy dependences of the transmission coefficients and the level density leads to maximal contributions for gamma energies of 2<=E_gamma<=4 MeV unless quantum selection rules allow isolated states to contribute. Under this condition, electric dipole transitions dominate. These findings allow to more accurately judge the relevance of modifications of the γstrength for astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper identifies that additional gamma strength (such as from a pygmy resonance) must be located in the energy range of about 2 to 4 MeV to affect astrophysically relevant capture reactions, unless quantum selection rules allow isolated states to contribute; this condition limits the impact of pygmy resonances on neutron capture because they are typically predicted to lie above the neutron separation energy.

## Method

The author computed Hauser-Feshbach transmission coefficients for gamma emission by folding the energy dependence of the E1 transmission coefficient (based on a Lorentzian Giant Dipole Resonance description) with the nuclear level density, using standard parameterizations from Rauscher et al. (1997) and the Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL-2). The integrand of the gamma transmission coefficient was evaluated for neutron capture at 60 keV on sequences of Sn, Pb, and Ru isotopes, and for proton capture at 5 MeV and alpha capture at 10 MeV on Sn and Sm isotopes, including the weighting by the initial channel transmission coefficient.

## Key numbers

- The maximally contributing gamma energies for astrophysical capture reactions lie in the range 2 ≤ E_gamma ≤ 4 MeV for most cases studied.
- The distributions of contributing gamma energies have a full width at half maximum of about 2 MeV.
- For neutron capture, the relevant projectile energy is below 100 keV, which is almost negligible compared to neutron separation energies of several MeV.
- For charged-particle capture, the relevant projectile energies are 5-7 MeV for protons and 10-12 MeV for alpha particles, depending on the target charge.
- Changes in the location of the maxima when using different descriptions of low-energy GDR strength are on the level of 10% or smaller.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of photon strength functions and their astrophysical impact, particularly regarding the pygmy dipole resonance observed experimentally in nuclei such as 132Sn by GSI (2004) and others (Alarcon et al. 1987, Zilges et al. 1990, Kneissl et al. 1996). The paper contradicts the common motivation that pygmy resonances significantly affect neutron capture in the r process, showing instead that the relevant gamma energies are 2-4 MeV while pygmy resonances are predicted to lie above the neutron separation energy (Vretenar et al. 2006, Liang et al. 2006). The findings enable more accurate assessment of whether modifications to gamma strength functions matter for nucleosynthesis calculations in the r process, p process, rp process, and nu p process.
