# Pion and Strangeness Production as Signals of QCD Phase Transition

> It is shown that data on pion and strangeness production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are consistent with the hypothesis of a Quark Gluon Plasma formation between 15 A GeV/c (BNL AGS) and 160 A GeV/c (CERN SPS) collision energies. The experimental results interpreted in the framework of a statistical approach indicate that the effective number of degrees of freedom increases by a factor of about 3 in the course of the phase transition and that the plasma created at CERN SPS energy may have a temperature of about 280 MeV (energy density $\approx$ 10 GeV/fm^3). Experimental studies of central Pb+Pb collisions in the energy range 20-160 A GeV/c are urgently needed in order to localize the threshold energy, and study the properties of the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

Data on pion and strangeness production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are consistent with the hypothesis of Quark Gluon Plasma formation between 15 A GeV/c (BNL AGS) and 160 A GeV/c (CERN SPS) collision energies, but the interpretation relies on a statistical approach and the generalized Landau model with no free parameters, and the claimed temperature of about 280 MeV and energy density of about 10 GeV/fm^3 are presented as possibilities rather than firm measurements.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental data from approximately 100 different experiments on pion multiplicity and strangeness yields (kaons, hyperons) in central nucleus-nucleus collisions and nucleon-nucleon interactions. The normalized entropy (S, calculated from pion multiplicity with corrections for kaon production and pion absorption) and the strangeness ratio E_S (defined as the sum of Lambda and kaon multiplicities divided by pion multiplicity) were studied as functions of the number of participant nucleons and collision energy, using the Fermi energy variable F. A generalized Landau model assuming baryon-free QGP formation in a Lorentz-contracted nuclear volume was used for quantitative comparison, with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The effective number of degrees of freedom increases by a factor of about 3 in the course of the phase transition, estimated as 1.33^4 ≈ 3 from the ratio of slopes of straight lines describing low and high energy A+A data.
- The plasma created at CERN SPS energy may have a temperature of about 280 MeV and an energy density of about 10 GeV/fm^3.
- The normalized entropy for A+A collisions at SPS energy is about 30% higher than the corresponding value for N+N interactions.
- The strange quark mass in QGP was taken as 175 ± 25 MeV/c^2, determined at an energy scale of 1 GeV.
- The effective radius of the nucleus was taken as r_0 A^{1/3} with r_0 = 1.30 ± 0.03 fm from fits to inelastic cross section data.
- The inelastic energy at SPS was measured to be (67 ± 7)% of the available energy, the same for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions.
- The conversion factor between calculated entropy and experimental entropy was taken as 4 (entropy per pion at T ≈ 150 MeV).
- The conversion factor between total strangeness and strangeness measured by Lambda and kaon yields was taken as 1.36.

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach to entropy and strangeness production in nuclear collisions, building on the Landau model (Landau 1953) and earlier suggestions to use entropy (Van Hove 1982) and strangeness (Koch 1986) as QGP signals. The analysis uses the Fermi energy variable (Fermi 1950, Landau 1953) and references compilation papers by Gazdzicki for the experimental data. The paper identifies the energy range between AGS (15 A GeV/c) and SPS (160 A GeV/c) as the likely transition region and calls urgently for experimental studies of central Pb+Pb collisions in the 20-160 A GeV/c range to localize the threshold energy and study QCD phase transition properties.
