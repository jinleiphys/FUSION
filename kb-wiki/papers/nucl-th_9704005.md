# Helicity Asymmetry for Proton Emission from Polarized Electrons in the Eikonal Regime

> The nuclear response to longitudinally polarized electrons, detected in coincidence with out-of-plane high-energy protons, is discussed in a simple model where the ejectile wave function is approximated as a plane wave with a complex wave vector. This choice is equivalent to solve the problem of Final-State Interactions (FSI) in homogeneous nuclear matter, as the residual nucleus can be described to a first approximation when dealing with very fast emitted protons. The main advantage of the present method is that in the framework of the Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) it allows for an analytical derivation of all the components of the nuclear response. It emerges that cancellations among the leading contributions determine the very small absolute size of the socalled fifth structure function and produce a nontrivial asymptotic scaling of the related helicity asymmetry for large values of the momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the eikonal regime for quasielastic proton knockout from a $s_{1/2}$ shell, the fifth structure function and the associated helicity asymmetry arise purely from geometrical final-state interactions (FSI) modeled by a plane wave with a complex wave vector, but the absolute size of these observables is very small due to cancellations among leading contributions, with the caveat that the model assumes homogeneous nuclear matter and a constant damping factor.

## Method

The authors model the outgoing proton wave function as a plane wave with a complex wave vector ${\bold P}' = {\bold p}' + i {\bold p}''$, where the imaginary part produces a constant damping representing FSI in homogeneous nuclear matter. Within the Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) at the one-photon exchange level, they analytically derive all components of the nuclear response for proton knockout from the $s_{1/2}$ shell, including the fifth structure function $f'_{01}$ and the helicity asymmetry $A$, using a nonrelativistic expansion of the current operator up to fourth order in the inverse nucleon mass. The calculations are performed for the $^{12}{\rm C}(\vec e,e'p)^{11}{\rm B}_{s_{1/2}}$ reaction with kinematics above the inelastic threshold ($p' \gtrsim 1$ GeV/$c$).

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum of $^{12}{\rm C}$: $p_{\rm Fermi} = 221$ MeV/$c$; typical missing momentum used: $p_m = 200$ MeV/$c$
- Damping factor: $p'' = 50$ MeV/$c$, derived from the Glauber choice $W = 0.036 \ p'$ MeV with $V=0$
- The analytical formula for the helicity asymmetry agrees with the numerical code PV5FF for $q \lesssim 0.8$ GeV/$c$; above this threshold the numerical code loses accuracy
- The fifth structure function $f'_{01}$ is amplified by a factor $10^2$ relative to each individual contribution (spin-flip and non-spin-flip terms), indicating dramatic cancellations
- The helicity asymmetry $A$ is plotted for $\theta = 40^\circ$ and shows a change of sign with a long asymptotic tail as a function of $q$

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approach previously developed by the same authors (Bianconi and Radici, 1996) for angular distributions of emitted protons, now applied to polarization observables in $(\vec e,e'p)$ reactions. The model provides an analytical alternative to the Glauber method (Glauber, 1959) for describing FSI at high momentum transfer, relevant for experiments at CEBAF and ELFE facilities investigating Color Transparency predictions (Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman, 1994; Nikolaev, 1994; Benhar, Bhattacharya, and Khadkikar, 1994). The results enable analytical study of the fifth structure function's sensitivity to FSI, though the small predicted asymmetry suggests experimental detection will be challenging.
