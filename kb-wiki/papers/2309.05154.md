# The functional generalization of the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation and its Gauge-like symmetry

> We argue that one can model deviations from the ensemble average in non-equilibrium statistical mechanics by promoting the Boltzmann equation to an equation in terms of {\em functionals} , representing possible candidates for phase space distributions inferred from a finite observed number of degrees of freedom. We find that, provided the collision term and the Vlasov drift term are both included, a gauge-like redundancy arises which does not go away even if the functional is narrow. We argue that this effect is linked to the gauge-like symmetry found in relativistic hydrodynamics \cite{bdnk} and that it could be part of the explanation for the apparent fluid-like behavior in small systems in hadronic collisions and other strongly-coupled small systems\cite{zajc}. When causality is omitted this problem can be look at via random matrix theory show, and we show that in such a case thermalization happens much more quickly than the Boltzmann equation would infer. We also sketch an algorithm to study this problem numerically

## Key claim

The paper argues that when the number of degrees of freedom is finite (rather than infinite as required for the ensemble average limit), promoting the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation to an equation in terms of functionals representing possible phase space distributions introduces a gauge-like redundancy that persists even when the functional distribution is narrow, potentially explaining apparent fluid-like behavior in small systems where conventional transport theory would not predict it.

## Method

The author generalizes the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation by replacing the single phase space distribution function f(x,p) with a functional W[f₁,f₂] representing the probability of a given distribution, then averaging the collision and Vlasov operators over this functional. A Gaussian ansatz is adopted for the functional, with width σ_f scaling as N_DoF^{-1/2}, and the analysis examines how the right-hand side of the generalized equation behaves when both the Boltzmann collision term and the Vlasov drift term are present simultaneously. The author also considers a random matrix theory approach when causality and Lorentz invariance are omitted.

## Key numbers

- The functional width σ_f is argued to scale as N_DoF^{-1/2}, where N_DoF is the number of degrees of freedom
- The paper references systems with as few as 20 final state particles in proton-proton and γ-nucleus collisions where collective behavior is observed
- The Vlasov term is estimated to scale as g²⟨A²⟩ while the Boltzmann term scales as g²⟨A⁴⟩, where g is the quantum field coupling constant
- The soft scale for Vlasov terms is given as k ∼ gT, with ultra-soft modes at k ∼ g²T

## Context

This work extends the Gibbsian approach to hydrodynamics proposed in BDNK (2020) and the author's earlier work on ergodicity, which argued that fluctuations in small systems create a redundancy of hydrodynamic descriptions. It directly addresses the puzzle of apparent hydrodynamic behavior in small systems observed in heavy ion collisions (CMS Collaboration, 2019; Zajc, 2020) and cold atom systems (Giacalone, 2019), where conventional Boltzmann transport would not predict collectivity. The paper contrasts this approach with hydrodynamic attractor theories (attractor, attractor2) and argues that the functional generalization provides a mechanism for fast thermalization that the Boltzmann equation alone cannot capture. The work enables numerical studies of small-system thermalization using the sketched algorithm and random matrix theory methods.
