# Effects of the Coulomb interaction on parameters of resonance states in mirror three-cluster nuclei

> We investigate how the Coulomb interaction affects the energy $E$ and width $Γ$ of resonance states in mirror nuclei. We employ a three-cluster microscopic model to determine position of resonance states in two- and three-body continua. Two parameters are introduced to quantify effects of the Coulomb interactions. As the energy and width of the corresponding resonance states of mirror nuclei are displayed on an $E$-$Γ$ plane, these parameters determine a rotation and a dilatation. With the help of these parameters we found resonance states with strong, small and medium effects of the Coulomb interaction. We also found two different scenarios of the motion of resonance states due to the Coulomb interaction. The first standard (major) scenario represent resonance states with the larger energy and larger width than their counterparts have. The second rear scenario includes resonance states with the larger energy but smaller width.

## Key claim

The paper introduces two quantitative parameters, a Coulomb rotational angle and a Coulomb shift, to characterize how the Coulomb interaction alters the energy and width of resonance states in mirror nuclei, and identifies two distinct scenarios: a standard (major) scenario where the resonance in the more proton-rich nucleus has both larger energy and larger width, and a rare scenario where it has larger energy but smaller width.

## Method

The authors employ two microscopic three-cluster models (AMGOB and AMHHB), which are modifications of the resonating group method, to study resonance states in the mirror pairs \(^{7}\)Li-\(^{7}\)Be, \(^{8}\)Li-\(^{8}\)B, \(^{9}\)Be-\(^{9}\)B, and \(^{11}\)B-\(^{11}\)C. The models use square-integrable bases (Gaussian or oscillator bases) and hyperspherical harmonics to describe inter-cluster motion, with the nucleon-nucleon interaction kept identical between mirror partners so that all differences arise solely from the Coulomb interaction of protons. Resonance parameters are extracted by solving the system of linear algebraic equations obtained from expanding the total wave function in cluster oscillator functions and applying the Breit-Wigner formula to the S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* For the mirror pair \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{9}\)B, the Coulomb rotational angle \(\theta_C\) and shift \(R_C\) were introduced in Ref. (2017) to quantify the Coulomb effect on resonance states in the \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) and \(\alpha+\alpha+p\) continua.
* The charge difference \(\Delta Z = Z_R - Z_L\) is 1 for the pairs \(^{7}\)Li-\(^{7}\)Be, \(^{9}\)Be-\(^{9}\)B, and \(^{11}\)B-\(^{11}\)C, and 2 for the pair \(^{8}\)Li-\(^{8}\)B.
* The Coulomb shift parameter is defined as \(R_C = \sqrt{[E(R)-E(L)]^2 + [\Gamma(R)-\Gamma(L)]^2}\), and the Coulomb rotational angle as \(\theta_C = \arctan[(\Gamma(R)-\Gamma(L))/(E(R)-E(L))]\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Coulomb effects on resonance states previously applied to \(^{9}\)Be and \(^{9}\)B in Vasilevsky et al. (2017) to additional mirror pairs (\(^{7}\)Li-\(^{7}\)Be, \(^{8}\)Li-\(^{8}\)B, \(^{11}\)B-\(^{11}\)C) using three-cluster microscopic models. It contrasts with earlier studies that focused primarily on bound states and the Thomas-Erhman effect (Thomas, 1952; Erhman, 1951), and with more recent work on mirror nuclei \(^{11}\)Li-\(^{11}\)O and \(^{12}\)Be-\(^{12}\)O using the Gamow coupled-channel approach (Wang et al., 2019). The introduced parameters enable systematic classification of resonance states by the strength of Coulomb effects (strong, medium, small) and identification of the two motion scenarios.
