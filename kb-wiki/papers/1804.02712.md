# Momentum Sum Rule Violation in QCD at High Energy Colliders and Confinement

> Momentum sum rule in QCD is widely used at high energy colliders. Although the exact form of the confinement potential energy is not known but the confinement potential energy at large distance $r$ can not rise slower than ${\rm ln}(r)$. In this paper we find that if the confinement potential energy at large distance $r$ rises linearly with $r$ (or faster) then the momentum sum rule in QCD is violated at the high energy colliders if we consider the hadron size as infinite. For finite size hadron we find that the momentum sum rule in QCD is violated at the high energy colliders for any form of the confinement potential energy.

## Key claim

The momentum sum rule in QCD, which states that the sum of parton momenta equals the hadron momentum, is violated at high energy colliders when confinement is taken into account. The violation occurs for any form of the confinement potential if the hadron has finite size, and for a linearly rising (or faster) potential if the hadron size is taken as infinite.

## Method

The author derives the momentum conservation equation in QCD from the gauge-invariant Noether's theorem applied to the Yang-Mills theory, extending the analogy from QED where the boundary surface term vanishes at infinite distance. By analyzing the behavior of the chromo-electric field and the Yang-Mills potential at large distances under confinement conditions, the author shows that the boundary surface term (momentum flux) does not vanish in QCD, leading to an additional term in the momentum sum rule. The analysis considers both the infinite hadron size limit and the physically realistic finite hadron size case.

## Key numbers

- The confinement potential energy at large distance \(r\) cannot rise slower than \(\ln(r)\).
- If the confinement potential rises linearly with \(r\) (or faster), the momentum sum rule is violated for infinite hadron size.
- For finite size hadron, the momentum sum rule is violated for any form of the confinement potential energy.
- The chromo-electric field \(\vec{E}^c(t,r)\) cannot decay faster than \(r^{-3/2}\) due to confinement, and the chromo-electric field energy density \(\frac{\vec{E}^c(t,r) \cdot \vec{E}^c(t,r)}{2}\) cannot decay faster than \(r^{-3}\).
- If the potential energy rises linearly (or faster) with distance \(r\), the chromo-electric field \(\vec{E}^c(t,r)\) cannot decay faster than \(r^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard QCD factorization framework used in PDF sets such as CTEQ, GRV, and MRST, which rely on the momentum sum rule to constrain parton distributions from limited experimental data. The paper argues that the momentum sum rule, as expressed in the standard form by Collins and others, is incomplete because it neglects the non-zero momentum flux arising from confinement. The result suggests that a new term \(<\hat{p}_{\text{flux}}>\) must be added to the sum of quark, antiquark, and gluon momenta to reproduce the hadron momentum, which would require revision of how parton distribution functions are extracted and constrained at high energy colliders.
