# Exploration of resonance properties in chiral perturbation theory with explicit U_A(1) anomaly

> We study the resonance properties within chiral perturbation theory by explicitly taking into account the U_A(1) anomaly effect. This assures we have the appropriate degrees of freedom of low energy QCD in the large Nc limit. We calculate the various resonance properties, such as mass, width and residues, for the physical case, i.e. Nc=3. Then we extrapolate the values of Nc to study the trajectories of resonance poles.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that explicitly including the singlet eta_1 meson (the ninth pseudo-Goldstone boson) in chiral perturbation theory via U(3) formalism is necessary to correctly study resonance pole trajectories in the large Nc limit, and that this inclusion reveals which resonances behave as standard q-bar-q states and which do not.

## Method
The authors performed a complete one-loop calculation of meson-meson scattering within U(3) chiral perturbation theory, incorporating tree-level exchanges of scalar and vector resonances, wave function renormalizations, mass renormalizations, pseudo-Goldstone weak decay constants, and eta-eta' mixing. They unitarized the perturbative amplitudes using a simplified N/D method and fitted the resulting phase shifts, S-matrix moduli, and invariant mass distributions to experimental data to determine unknown parameters. Resonance poles were then located on unphysical Riemann sheets, and the number of colors Nc was extrapolated from 3 to larger values to study the Nc trajectories of pole positions.

## Key numbers
- For Nc=3, resonance pole positions were obtained for vector resonances: rho(770) with IJ=1 1, K*(892) with IJ=1/2 1, and phi(1020) with IJ=0 1.
- For Nc=3, scalar resonances found include: sigma, f0(980), and f0(1370) for IJ=0 0; a0(980) and a0(1450) for IJ=1 0; kappa and K*(1430) for IJ=1/2 0.
- For rho(770), K*(892), f0(980), f0(1370), a0(1450), and K*(1430), the product Gamma*Nc was plotted as a function of Nc, showing decay widths decreasing as 1/Nc for large Nc.
- For sigma and kappa, the pole positions in the complex s-plane approached the real and negative axis when increasing Nc, making it inappropriate to interpret the imaginary part of sqrt(s) as the decay width.
- For a0(980), both its width and mass increased when increasing Nc.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Nc trajectories of resonance poles (e.g., Uchpt2, 2009) by incorporating the singlet eta_1 meson, which had been commonly ignored in prior approaches. The inclusion of the U_A(1) anomaly effect via U(3) chiral perturbation theory fills a gap in understanding resonance properties at large Nc. The results enable future studies to distinguish standard q-bar-q resonances from non-standard ones (such as sigma, kappa, and a0(980)) based on their Nc scaling behavior, and provide a more complete framework for analyzing the low-energy QCD spectrum in the large Nc limit.
