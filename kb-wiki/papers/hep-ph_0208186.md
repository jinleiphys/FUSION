# Relativistic Calculation of the Meson Spectrum: a Fully Covariant Treatment Versus Standard Treatments

> A large number of treatments of the meson spectrum have been tried that consider mesons as quark - anti quark bound states. Recently, we used relativistic quantum "constraint" mechanics to introduce a fully covariant treatment defined by two coupled Dirac equations. For field-theoretic interactions, this procedure functions as a "quantum mechanical transform of Bethe-Salpeter equation". Here, we test its spectral fits against those provided by an assortment of models: Wisconsin model, Iowa State model, Brayshaw model, and the popular semi-relativistic treatment of Godfrey and Isgur. We find that the fit provided by the two-body Dirac model for the entire meson spectrum competes with the best fits to partial spectra provided by the others and does so with the smallest number of interaction functions without additional cutoff parameters necessary to make other approaches numerically tractable. We discuss the distinguishing features of our model that may account for the relative overall success of its fits. Note especially that in our approach for QCD, the resulting pion mass and associated Goldstone behavior depend sensitively on the preservation of relativistic couplings that are crucial for its success when solved nonperturbatively for the analogous two-body bound-states of QED.

## Key claim

The two-body Dirac model, based on relativistic constraint mechanics, provides a fit to the entire meson spectrum that competes with the best fits to partial spectra from other models (Wisconsin, Iowa State, Brayshaw, and Godfrey-Isgur) while using the smallest number of interaction functions and requiring no additional cutoff parameters for numerical tractability.

## Method

The authors solve two coupled Dirac equations (the Two-Body Dirac Equations) for a 16-component wave function describing a quark-antiquark pair, derived from relativistic constraint dynamics and functioning as a quantum-mechanical transform of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. They incorporate QCD interactions through a relativistic version of the Adler-Piran static quark potential, using two invariant functions (a vector potential A(r) and a scalar potential S(r)) that enter the equations via minimal substitution and scalar coupling forms. The equations are reduced to a Schrodinger-like Pauli form for the upper-upper spinor component, which is solved numerically to obtain meson masses.

## Key numbers

* The two-body Dirac model uses only three potential parameters in two different invariant interaction functions, compared to ten potential parameters in six interaction functions for the Godfrey-Isgur model.
* The invariant b^2(w) = (w^4 - 2w^2(m1^2 + m2^2) + (m1^2 - m2^2)^2) / 4w^2 plays the role of the energy eigenvalue in the Schrodinger-like equation.
* Todorov's collective energy variable is defined as epsilon_w = (w^2 - m1^2 - m2^2) / 2w, and the relativistic reduced mass is m_w = m1 m2 / w.
* The vector interaction replaces epsilon_w by epsilon_w - A, and the scalar interaction replaces m_w by m_w + S in the effective one-body Einstein condition.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of relativistic constraint dynamics for two spin-one-half particles (Crater and Van Alstine, 1982-1994) and tests it against several established approaches: the Wisconsin model of Gara, Durand, Durand, and Nickisch; the Iowa State model of Sommerer, Spence, and Vary; the Breit equation model of Brayshaw; and the semirelativistic model of Godfrey and Isgur. The paper enables future comparisons between fully covariant two-body Dirac treatments and other relativistic quark models, while highlighting that the preservation of full relativistic coupling structure is crucial for obtaining the correct pion mass and Goldstone behavior in QCD.
