# Additional strange resonances from Lattice QCD

> Recent Lattice QCD (LQCD) studies suggest that there are missing resonances in the strange sector of the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. By adopting the continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum, we present how different medium compositions influence the HRG predictions of conserved charge fluctuations. It is shown that missing strange resonances may be partially accounted for by applying the Hagedorn mass spectrum extracted from experimentally established hadrons. On the other hand, the strange-baryonic spectra, extracted from LQCD results for fluctuations, are found to be consistent with the unconfirmed states in the Particle Data Group (PDG) database, whilst the strange-mesonic spectrum points towards yet undiscovered states in the intermediate mass region.

## Key claim

Missing strange resonances in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model may be partially accounted for by applying the Hagedorn mass spectrum extracted from experimentally established hadrons, but the strange-mesonic spectrum extracted from Lattice QCD results points towards yet-undiscovered states in the intermediate mass region, while the strange-baryonic spectrum is consistent with unconfirmed states in the Particle Data Group (PDG) database.

## Method

The author compared three schemes for the hadronic mass spectrum in the HRG model: (I) the discrete PDG spectrum of established hadrons only; (II) the continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum fitted to the PDG data; and (III) the continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum fitted to Lattice QCD results for baryon-strangeness correlations and strangeness fluctuations. The second-order fluctuations of conserved charges (net-baryon number and net-strangeness) were computed from the grand canonical pressure and compared across schemes.

## Key numbers

- The experimental PDG spectrum saturates at approximately 2.5 GeV, motivating the use of the asymptotic Hagedorn spectrum for heavier resonances.
- The Hagedorn mass spectrum is parameterized as \(\rho^{H}(m) = A e^{m/T_H} / (m^2 + m_0^2)^{5/4}\), where \(T_H\) is the Hagedorn limiting temperature and \(A\) and \(m_0\) are free parameters.
- The strange-mesonic spectrum extracted from Lattice QCD exceeds the PDG spectrum even after including unconfirmed states, suggesting undiscovered resonances below 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas model (Braun-Munzinger, 2003) by incorporating the continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum (Hagedorn, 1965; Hagedorn, 1971) to address discrepancies with Lattice QCD results from the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov, 2012) and the Budapest-Wuppertal Collaboration (Borsanyi, 2011) for baryon-strangeness correlations and strangeness fluctuations. The analysis follows the approach of Lo (2015) for extracting the Hagedorn spectrum. The results enable future investigations into whether broad resonances, which may be overestimated in the point-like approximation (Friman, 2015; Broniowski, 2015), or contributions from the non-strange sector could further resolve the remaining discrepancies.
