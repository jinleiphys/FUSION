# 3D Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with a separable pairing interaction

> A recently introduced separable pairing force for relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations, adjusted in nuclear matter to the pairing gap of the Gogny force, is employed in the 3D RHB model for triaxial shapes. The pairing force is separable in momentum space but, when transformed to coordinate space in calculations of finite nuclei, it is no longer separable because of translational invariance. The corresponding pairing matrix elements are represented as a sum of a finite number of separable terms in the basis of a 3D harmonic oscillator. The 3D RHB model is applied to the calculation of binding energy surfaces and pairing energy maps for a sequence of even-A Sm isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a separable pairing force, originally adjusted to reproduce the Gogny force pairing gap in nuclear matter, can be extended to triaxial (3D) nuclear shapes within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework, with the caveat that the force is no longer exactly separable in coordinate space due to translational invariance but can be represented as a finite sum of separable terms in a harmonic oscillator basis.

## Method

The authors solve the 3D relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis in Cartesian coordinates, allowing for both axial and triaxial nuclear shapes. The pairing interaction is a separable force in momentum space with a Gaussian form factor, parameterized by two constants (G = -728 MeV fm^3 and a = 0.644 fm) adjusted to reproduce the Gogny D1S pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter. The pairing matrix elements are expressed as a sum of separable terms using Talmi-Moshinsky transformations of the harmonic oscillator wave functions to center-of-mass and relative coordinates, with the expansion truncated at N_z^0 = 14 and N_x + N_y ≤ 5 for the Sm isotope calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pairing force parameters: G = -728 MeV fm^3 and a = 0.644 fm, adjusted to reproduce the Gogny D1S force pairing gap in nuclear matter
- Basis includes N_f^max = 14 major oscillator shells for the 3D harmonic oscillator expansion
- Cut-off conditions for the separable expansion: N_z = 0,2,...,14 and N_x + N_y = 0,2,...,5 (corresponding to N_p^0 = 5)
- The 3D RHB model is applied to even-A Sm isotopes from ^134Sm to ^156Sm (Z=62, neutron numbers 72 to 94)
- For the magic neutron number N=82 (^144Sm), a stable spherical minimum is found in the β-γ plane
- Heavy Sm isotopes show prolate minima at β > 0.3

## Context

This work extends the separable pairing force approach, previously developed for spherical nuclei (Niksic et al., 2009a) and axially deformed nuclei (Niksic et al., 2009c), to triaxial nuclear shapes. The separable force reproduces pairing properties of the Gogny D1S force (Berger et al., 1991) with greatly reduced computational cost, while retaining the natural momentum-space cut-off that finite-range forces provide. The model enables systematic calculations of RHB binding energy surfaces in the β-γ plane that can serve as input for generator coordinate method configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected triaxial wave functions, or to determine parameters of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom.
