# Revisiting the symmetric reactions for synthesis of super heavy nuclei of $Z\geq $120

> Extensive efforts have been made experimentally to reach nuclei in the super heavy mass region of Z = 110 and above with suitable choices of projectile and target nuclei. The cross sections for production of these nuclei are seen to be in the range of a few picobarn or less, and pose great experimental challenges. Theoretically, there have been extensive calculations for highly asymmetric (hot-fusion) and moderately asymmetric (cold-fusion) collisions and only a few theoretical studies are available for near symmetric collisions to estimate the cross sections for production of super-heavy nuclei. In the present article, we revisit the symmetric heavy ion reactions with suitable combinations of projectile and target nuclei in the rare-earth region, that will lead to compound systems with very low excitation energy and with better neutron-to-proton ratio for higher stability.

# Revisiting the symmetric reactions for synthesis of super heavy nuclei of Z ≥ 120

## Key claim
Near-symmetric collisions using rare-earth projectile and target nuclei (e.g., 154Sm + 150Nd, 154Sm + 154Sm, 160Gd + 154Sm) may produce compound nuclei with Z ≥ 120 at very low excitation energy (~8 MeV) and with better neutron-to-proton ratio than hot- or cold-fusion routes, but the estimated cross sections are extremely small (on the order of 10⁻¹¹ barn), requiring experimental verification.

## Method
The authors apply the Fusion by Diffusion (FBD) model, originally developed by Swiatecki (2005) and refined by Cap et al. (2011), to estimate evaporation residue cross sections for three proposed symmetric reactions using rare-earth nuclei. The sticking cross section is computed from a diffused barrier formula, the diffusion probability is calculated from a parabolic barrier model using an empirically derived injection parameter (s_inj) from fits to GSI cold-fusion data, and the survival probability is taken as a lower limit of 10⁻⁴ based on systematics from cold-fusion reactions for Z = 104–119.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction 160Gd + 154Sm producing compound nucleus (Z=126, A=314): the Coulomb barrier (V_Coul) is 16 MeV lower than the |Q|-value (412.2 MeV), enabling compound nucleus formation at low excitation energy.
- At injection parameter s_inj = -4 fm, the barrier height H opposing fusion is approximately 5.5 ± 1.5 MeV for all three reactions considered.
- The diffusion probability (P_Diffus) at H = 5 ± 1.5 MeV lies between 10⁻⁶ and 10⁻³ for temperatures between 0.5 MeV and 1.0 MeV.
- The lower limit of the product σ_stick × P_Diffus is approximately 10⁻⁷ barn for the present reactions.
- The lower limit of final cross sections for synthesis of super-heavy nuclei with Z ≥ 120 is estimated to be in the range of 1.7 × 10⁻¹¹ barn to 3.0 × 10⁻¹¹ barn.
- The survival probability (P_surv) for cold-fusion reactions with Z = 104–119 lies in a narrow range of 0.2 × 10⁻⁴ to 6 × 10⁻⁴, with fission barriers varying from 5.5 to 9 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of near-symmetric collisions for super-heavy element synthesis, such as 136Xe + 136Xe (Oganessian, 2009) which gave an upper limit of 4 picobarn for Hs, and 149La + 149La (Aritomo, 1996) with an estimated upper limit of 10 picobarn for Z=114. The authors propose that rare-earth target-projectile combinations offer a gain factor of about 10⁴ in σ_stick × P_Diffus over cold-fusion reactions (Swiatecki, 2005), while also providing better neutron-to-proton ratio and lower excitation energy. The work enables experimental investigations using stable rare-earth beams and calls for full microscopic calculations to understand the fusion mechanism for these heavy systems.
