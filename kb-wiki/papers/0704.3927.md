# Alpha decay chains from element 113

> Theoretical estimates of $α$-decay half lives of several nuclei in the decay from element 113 are presented. Calculations in a WKB framework using DDM3Y interaction and experimental Q-values are in good agreement with the experimental data. Half life calculations are found to be quite sensitive to the Q-values and angular momenta transfers. Calculated decay lifetime decreases, owing to more penetrability as well as thinner barrier, as Q-value increases. Deviations to this predominant behaviour observed in some recent experimental data may be attributed to non zero spin-parities in some cases.

# Alpha decay chains from element 113

## Key claim

Theoretical α-decay half-lives calculated in a WKB framework using DDM3Y interaction and experimental Q-values show good agreement with most experimental data, but discrepancies observed for four nuclei (²⁷⁸113, ²⁷⁴111, ²⁷⁰109, ²⁶⁶107) may be explained by non-zero angular momentum transfers due to spin-parity conservation in odd-odd nuclei, though the experimental data are based on only one or two observed events.

## Method

The authors calculated α-decay half-lives for parent nuclei with Z=113–107 using a WKB barrier penetration framework with microscopic double-folding potentials obtained from α and daughter nucleus density distributions folded with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Experimental Q-values were derived from measured α-particle kinetic energies using standard recoil correction and electron shielding correction following Perlman and Rasmussen (1957). Theoretical half-lives were computed for zero and non-zero angular momentum transfers and compared with experimental data from Morita et al. (2004) and Oganessian et al. (2004, 2006).

## Key numbers

- ²⁸⁴113: experimental Q=10.15(6) MeV, experimental half-life 0.48⁺⁰·⁵⁸₋₀·₁₇ s, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 1.55⁺⁰·⁷²₋₀·₄₈ s
- ²⁸³113: experimental Q=10.26(9) MeV, experimental half-life 100⁺⁴⁹⁰₋₄₅ ms, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 201.6⁺¹⁶⁴·⁹₋₈₄·₇ ms
- ²⁷⁸113: experimental Q=11.90(4) MeV, experimental decay time 344 μs, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 101⁺²⁷₋₁₈ μs (l=0); with l=3 angular momentum transfer, theoretical half-life becomes 307⁺⁶⁷₋₅₅ μs
- ²⁷⁴111: experimental Q=11.36(7) MeV, experimental decay time 9.26 ms, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 0.39⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀·₁₂ ms (l=0); with l=5 angular momentum transfer, theoretical half-life becomes 6.45⁺²·⁸³₋₁·⁹₈ ms
- ²⁷⁰109: experimental Q=10.23(7) MeV, experimental decay time 7.16 ms, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 52.05⁺²⁷·⁰²₋₁₇·⁶⁸ ms (l=0)
- ²⁶⁶107: experimental Q=9.26(4) MeV, experimental decay time 2.47 s, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 5.73⁺¹·⁸²₋₁·³⁸ s (l=0)
- In a repeat experiment (Morita 2007): ²⁷⁸113 with Q=11.737(40) MeV, experimental half-life 1.8⁺³·³₋₁·₃ ms, DDM3Y half-life with Q_ex 0.23⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·⁰⁴ ms (l=0) and 3.71⁺⁰·⁶⁸₋₀·⁸⁴ ms (l=5)

## Context

This work extends the WKB framework with DDM3Y interaction previously applied to superheavy nuclei by Chowdhury et al. (2006) and complements calculations using the Generalized Liquid Drop Model including proximity effects by Zhang et al. (2006). The calculations provide theoretical estimates for α-decay half-lives of element 113 decay chains measured by Oganessian et al. (2004, 2006) and Morita et al. (2004, 2007), and suggest that discrepancies in the Morita et al. (2004) data may be resolved by considering non-zero angular momentum transfers, though higher-statistics measurements are needed for definitive conclusions.
