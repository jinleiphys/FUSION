# Constraining the neutron star equation of state by including the isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling using the Bayesian analysis

> We constrain the nuclear matter equation of state within the relativistic mean field model by including the isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling term at a fundamental level using the Bayesian analysis. We used the nuclear saturation properties and recent astrophysical observations to constrain the dense matter equation of state. We obtained about 20000 sets of equations of states out of sampling about 60 millions sets of equations of states. All 20000 equations of states satisfy nuclear matter saturation properties at saturation densities and produces high mass neutron stars. In our findings, we find that the non-zero value of isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling parameter and negative value of sigma meson self-coupling stiffen the equation of state. Our sets of equations of state produces neutron stars of mass larger than 2.5 M$_{\odot}$ to include the recent gravitational waves observation GW190419.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including non-zero isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling terms (specifically the \(\Lambda_{\omega\rho}\) parameter) in the relativistic mean field model, together with a negative sigma meson self-coupling (\(\lambda\)), stiffens the equation of state enough to produce neutron stars with masses larger than \(2.5 M_\odot\), consistent with the gravitational wave event GW190419, but this result is conditional on the specific Bayesian sampling that yielded about 20,000 acceptable EOS sets from roughly 60 million sampled sets.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis within the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework using the FSUGold interaction Lagrangian, which includes isoscalar-vector (\(\omega_\mu\)) and isovector-vector (\(\rho_\mu\)) mesons along with their cross-coupling term \(\Lambda_{\omega\rho}\). They sampled roughly 60 million sets of coupling parameters and retained about 20,000 that simultaneously satisfy nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its slope) at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16 \pm 0.02\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and astrophysical constraints from NICER mass-radius measurements and the gravitational wave event GW170817, while also requiring subluminal sound speed (\(c_s^2 \le 1\)). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were then solved for each accepted EOS to obtain neutron star mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

*   About 20,000 EOS sets were retained from roughly 60 million sampled sets.
*   The mean maximum neutron star mass across the accepted EOS sets is \(2.420 \pm 0.055\ M_\odot\) (1\(\sigma\) range: \(2.245\) to \(2.599\ M_\odot\)).
*   The mean radius of a \(1.4\ M_\odot\) neutron star is \(12.718 \pm 0.178\ \mathrm{km}\) (1\(\sigma\) range: \(12.265\) to \(13.557\ \mathrm{km}\)).
*   The mean tidal deformability for a \(1.4\ M_\odot\) neutron star is \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 372.931 \pm 24.914\) (1\(\sigma\) range: \(308.004\) to \(464.866\)).
*   The mean incompressibility \(K_0\) is \(236.692 \pm 18.789\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (1\(\sigma\) range: \(170.224\) to \(304.694\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
*   The mean symmetry energy slope \(L_0\) is \(42.854 \pm 6.014\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (1\(\sigma\) range: \(29.941\) to \(77.850\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
*   Selected parameter sets produce neutron stars with maximum masses ranging from \(2.556\ M_\odot\) (set 8) to \(2.558\ M_\odot\) (set 28), with corresponding radii at maximum mass of \(12.25\ \mathrm{km}\) and \(12.28\ \mathrm{km}\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the FSUGold RMF model (Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz, 2005; Horowitz and Piekarewicz, 2001) by using Bayesian analysis to systematically constrain the isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector coupling strength \(\Lambda_{\omega\rho}\) against both nuclear saturation properties and recent multi-messenger astrophysical data from NICER (Miller et al., 2021; Riley et al., 2019) and gravitational wave observations (LIGO/Virgo, 2017, 2020). The resulting EOS sets that produce neutron stars above \(2.5\ M_\odot\) are relevant for interpreting the GW190814 event, which may involve either a high-mass neutron star or a low-mass black hole, and provide a foundation for future studies of neutron star tidal deformability, non-radial oscillations, and the role of the symmetry energy slope at high density.
