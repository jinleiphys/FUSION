# The $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ pentaquark and its partners in the molecular picture

> The LHCb collaboration has detected a new hidden-charm pentaquark with the quantum numbers of a $Λ$ baryon: the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$. This pentaquark will be interpreted as a $\bar{D}_s Λ_c$-$\bar{D} Ξ_c$ resonance within a contact-range theory. Here we briefly comment on the relation of the new $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ with the $P^Λ_{ψs}(4459)$. We find that the $P_{ψs}^Λ(4338)$ and $P_{ψs}^Λ(4459)$ both accept a common description in terms of the same parameters, which predicts the existence of a few additional $P_ψ^N$, $P_{ψs}^Λ$, $P_{ψs}^Σ$ and $P_{ψs s}^Ξ$ molecular pentaquarks composed of a charmed antimeson and an antitriplet charmed baryon. The most robust of these predicted pentaquarks is a $P_{ψs}^Λ$ with a mass in the $(4235-4255)\,{\rm MeV}$ range, while other two interesting ones are a $P_ψ^{N}(4150)$ and a $P_ψ^Σ(4335)$, the latter basically at the same mass as the $P_ψ^Λ(4338)$, with which it might mix owing to isospin symmetry breaking effects.

# The $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ pentaquark and its partners in the molecular picture

## Key claim

The newly discovered $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ pentaquark and the previously known $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ can both be described within the same contact-range effective field theory as $\bar{D}_s \Lambda_c$-$\bar{D} \Xi_c$ coupled-channel resonances, but the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ is not necessarily a molecular state in the strict sense because its center-of-mass energy relative to the $\bar{D} \Xi_c$ threshold is $(1.9 - 3.5\,i)\,{\rm MeV}$, where the imaginary part exceeds the real part, meaning the pair dissociates before orbiting even once.

## Method

The authors model the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ and $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ as poles in the $\bar{D}^{(*)} \Xi_c$ and $\bar{D}_s^{(*)} \Lambda_c$ scattering amplitudes using a contact-range potential with Gaussian regularization at cutoff $\Lambda = 0.75\,{\rm GeV}$ (and checked at $1.5\,{\rm GeV}$). They determine two independent coupling constants $d_a$ and $\tilde{d}_a$ from the mass and width of either the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ or the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ (using single-peak, double-peak, and averaged solutions), then solve the bound-state equation to predict masses of additional $\bar{H}_c T_c$ pentaquarks in the $N$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ sectors.

## Key numbers

- $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ mass: $M = 4338.2 \pm 0.7\,{\rm MeV}$, width: $\Gamma = 7.0 \pm 1.2\,{\rm MeV}$ (LHCb, Breit-Wigner determination)
- $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ single-peak mass: $M = 4458.8 \pm 2.9 {}^{+4.7}_{-1.1}\,{\rm MeV}$, width: $\Gamma = 17.3 \pm 6.5 {}^{+8.0}_{-5.7}\,{\rm MeV}$
- $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ double-peak masses: $M_1 = 4454.9 \pm 2.7\,{\rm MeV}$, $M_2 = 4467.8 \pm 3.7\,{\rm MeV}$
- Predicted $\bar{D}_s \Lambda_c$ partner ($P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}$) mass range: $(4235-4255)\,{\rm MeV}$ across all input sets
- Predicted $\bar{D} \Lambda_c$ bound state ($P_{\psi}^N(4150)$) mass: $4150.9-4152.0\,{\rm MeV}$ (set $B_3$, $\Lambda = 0.75-1.5\,{\rm GeV}$)
- Predicted $\bar{D} \Xi_c$ isovector state ($P_{\psi s}^{\Sigma}(4335)$) mass: $4332.8-4336.3\,{\rm MeV}$ (sets $B_2$-$B_5$)
- Isospin-breaking branching ratio for $P_{\psi s}^{\Sigma^0} \to J/\psi \Lambda$: $(0.2-6.4) \times 10^{-2}$ depending on input set and cutoff
- Couplings from $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ input: $d_a = -(0.98-0.42)\,{\rm fm}^2$, $\tilde{d}_a = -(0.26-0.25)\,{\rm fm}^2$ for $\Lambda = (0.75-1.5)\,{\rm GeV}$

## Context

This work extends the molecular pentaquark program that previously interpreted the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$ as a $\bar{D}^* \Xi_c$ molecule (Peng 2020, Yan 2021) and predicted a $\bar{D} \Xi_c$ bound state near $(4319-4337)\,{\rm MeV}$ (Wang 2019, Dong 2021, Xiao 2021, Yan 2021). The new $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ from LHCb (2022) fits this pattern. The analysis contrasts with Burns and Swanson (2022), who argue the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ is a triangle singularity rather than a resonance, and aligns with Nakamura and Wu (2022), who find a narrower pole at $4338.0 \pm 1.1\,{\rm MeV}$ with $\Gamma = 1.7 \pm 0.4\,{\rm MeV}$. The predictions enable searches for additional pentaquarks: a $\bar{D}_s \Lambda_c$ bound state near $4250\,{\rm MeV}$, an isovector $P_{\psi s}^{\Sigma}(4335)$ that could mix with the $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ via isospin breaking, and a $P_{\psi}^N(4150)$ possibly corresponding to a structure hinted in the $J/\psi p$ mass distribution from LHCb.
