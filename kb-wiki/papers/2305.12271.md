# Electric current from Schwinger's time-ordered propagator

> The quasistatic electric current density of fermions in the presence of an external electric field is determined through the utilization of a time-ordered Schwinger propagator. The study encompasses the necessary conditions for establishing a well-defined time-ordered propagator within the Schwinger formalism, specifically concentrating on constant and uniform electromagnetic fields. Within this theoretical framework, a chemical potential is introduced, resulting in non-zero values for the electric current and charge number density. Consequently, the dependence of electric conductivity on the strength of the electric field and the charge number density is investigated.

## Key claim

The quasistatic electric current density of fermions in a constant, uniform external electric field is derived using a time-ordered Schwinger propagator, but the result is reliable only in the regime of weak electric fields because the quasistatic motion assumption neglects the induced magnetic field, and the apparent oscillations in conductivity at large fields may not have physical significance.

## Method

The author constructs a time-ordered (Feynman) Schwinger propagator for fermions under a constant, uniform electric field by imposing the condition that the vector potential is time-independent, which makes the Green's function local in time coordinates and allows a Fourier transform. A chemical potential is introduced, and the propagator is regularized by shifting the Hamiltonian as \(H \to H - i\eta\) with a regulator \(\eta = \epsilon p_0(p_0 + \tilde\mu)\), where \(\tilde\mu = \mu - qEz\) is an effective, position-dependent chemical potential. The electric current density \(j\) and charge number density \(n\) are then computed from the trace of the propagator, and the electric conductivity \(\sigma = j/E\) is studied as a function of \(E\) and \(n\).

## Key numbers

* The electric conductivity at zero electric field, \(\sigma_0 = q^2 m / \pi^2\), is the value from which all conductivity curves start at \(E=0\).
* The charge number density in vacuum at finite chemical potential is \(n_0(\mu) = \frac{\epsilon(\mu)}{3\pi^2} \theta(|\mu|-m) (\mu^2 - m^2)^{3/2}\).
* For \(n=0\) and \(E=0\), the conductivity is zero; it jumps from 0 to \(\sigma_0\) as soon as a charge distribution appears in the system.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger proper-time method (Schwinger, 1951) for fermions in external electromagnetic fields, specifically addressing the regularization subtleties noted by Tsamis and Woodard (2000) that the Schwinger Green's function is not automatically a time-ordered propagator. It contrasts with approaches using in- and out-state expectation values (Copinger, 2018; Cao, 2019) and with Kubo-formula-based linear response calculations (Kapusta and Gale, 2006). The results enable future comparisons with those formalisms and can be extended to include finite temperature and combined electric and magnetic fields.
