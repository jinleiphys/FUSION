# The energies and residues of the nucleon resonances N(1535) and N(1650)

> We extract pole positions for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances using two different models. The positions are determined from fits to different subsets of the existing $πN\toπN$, $πN\toηN$ and $γp\toηp$ data and found to be 1515(10)--i85(15)MeV and 1660(10)--i65(10)MeV, when the data is described in terms of two poles. Sensitivity to the choice of fitted data is explored. The corresponding $ππ$ and $ηη$ residues of these poles are also extracted.

## Key claim

The pole positions for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances are extracted from fits to different subsets of data using two different models, and the results are found to be 1515(10)–i85(15) MeV and 1660(10)–i65(10) MeV when the data is described in terms of two poles, but the positions shift by about 50 MeV depending on whether partial-wave amplitudes or the original experimental data are fitted.

## Method

The authors used a two-channel K-matrix model for S-wave πN and ηN scattering (Green and Wycech model) and a separate Chew-Mandlestam K-matrix model (VPI approach) coupling πN to ηN and πΔ channels. They fitted the models to subsets of πN→πN, πN→ηN, and γp→ηp data over the center-of-mass energy range 1350 to 1700 MeV, then analytically continued the T-matrix into the complex energy plane to extract pole positions and residues.

## Key numbers

- N(1535) pole position: 1515(10)–i85(15) MeV (from two-pole fit to combined data)
- N(1650) pole position: 1660(10)–i65(10) MeV (from two-pole fit to combined data)
- Best single estimate for N(1535) pole: 1510(10)–i85(15) MeV
- Best single estimate for N(1650) pole: 1660(10)–i70(10) MeV
- N(1535) pole position shifts about 50 MeV higher when fitting partial-wave amplitudes instead of experimental πN data
- Tππ residue modulus for N(1535): [50(10), 0(10)] (modulus in unspecified units, phase in degrees)
- Tππ residue modulus for N(1650): [45(10), –40(10)] (modulus in unspecified units, phase in degrees)
- Error estimates from Minuit: δE_P(1535) ≈ 10 MeV, δΓ_P/2(1535) ≈ 10 MeV, δE_P(1650) ≈ 5 MeV, δΓ_P/2(1650) ≈ 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the two-channel K-matrix analysis of Green and Wycech (1997) by extracting T-matrix pole positions and residues for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances, rather than just the eta-nucleon scattering length and effective range. The results are compared to earlier analyses by Cutkosky (1980), Hoehler (1983), and the Particle Data Group listings, with the authors finding improved precision due to better data. The study enables future simultaneous fits to all available πN→πN, πN→ηN, and ηN→ηN data to resolve ambiguities in whether these resonances are primarily K-matrix poles or dynamically generated through channel coupling.
