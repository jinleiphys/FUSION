# Light nuclei production in relativistic heavy ion collisions from the AMPT model

> Based on an improved multiphase transport (AMPT) model, which gives a good description of proton production with a smooth quark matter to hadronic matter transition in relativistic heavy ion collisions, we study deuteron and triton production from the coalescence of nucleons at the kinetic freezeout of these collisions. For Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$ from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV available at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), we find that the yield ratio $N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/N_\text{d}^2$ of proton, deuteron\com{,} and triton is essentially a constant as a function of collision energy. Our result confirms the expectation that without a first-order quark to hadronic matter phase transition in the produced matter or its approach to the critical point of the QCD matter, this yield ratio does not show any non-monotonic behavior in its collision energy dependence.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model, which implements a smooth crossover from partonic to hadronic matter, the yield ratio \(N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/N_\text{d}^2\) is essentially constant as a function of collision energy from 7.7 to 200 GeV, confirming that a non-monotonic dependence of this ratio would require a first-order phase transition or approach to the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors used the improved AMPT model to generate kinetically freeze-out nucleons in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}\) from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They then applied a phase-space coalescence model using Gaussian Wigner functions for the deuteron (with \(\sigma \approx 2.26\) fm) and triton (with \(\sigma_\text{t} = 1.59\) fm) internal wave functions, free-streaming nucleons with earlier freeze-out times to the latest freeze-out time before computing coalescence probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The yield ratio \(N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/N_\text{d}^2\) is approximately constant at a value of about 0.4 across \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV in central Au+Au collisions.
- The AMPT model overestimates the measured \(N_\text{d}/N_\text{p}\) ratio by about a factor of 3 and the \(N_\text{t}/N_\text{p}\) ratio by about a factor of 9 for all collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV.
- The constant value of 0.4 is slightly larger than the analytical value of about 0.29 obtained assuming a uniform nucleon distribution (Sun 2017, Sun 2018).
- The deuteron Wigner function parameter \(\sigma\) is related to the deuteron root-mean-square radius by \(\sigma = \sqrt{4/3}~r_\text{d} \approx 2.26\) fm.
- The triton Wigner function parameter \(\sigma_\text{t} = r_\text{t} = 1.59\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Sun (2017) and Sun (2018) that the yield ratio \(N_\text{t}N_\text{p}/N_\text{d}^2\) could show non-monotonic behavior due to density fluctuations from a first-order phase transition or critical point. The result is similar to a recent study by Liu (2019) using a pure hadronic JAM transport model with a coalescence model based on coalescence radii, which also found a constant ratio. The AMPT baseline enables the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC to distinguish background contributions from signals of a non-smooth phase transition or the QCD critical point.
