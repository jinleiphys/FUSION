# The astrophysical $S-$factor and reaction rate for $^{15}$N($p,γ$)$^{16}$O within the modified potential cluster model

> We study a radiative $p^{15}$N capture on the ground state of $^{16}$O at stellar energies within the framework of a modified potential cluster model (MPCM) with forbidden states, including low-lying resonances. The investigation of the $^{15}$N($p,γ_{0}$)$^{16}$O reaction includes the consideration of $^{3}S_{1}$ resonances due to $E1$ transitions and the contribution of $^{3}P_{1}$ scattering wave in $p$ + $^{15}$N channel due to $^{3}P_{1}\longrightarrow $ $^{3}P_{0}$ $M1$ transition. We calculated the astrophysical low-energy $S-$factor, and extrapolated $S(0)$ turned out to be within $34.7-40.4$ keV$\cdot $b. The important role of the asymptotic constant (AC) for the $^{15}$N($p,γ_{0}$)$^{16}$O process with interfering $^{3}S_{1}$(312) and $^{3}S_{1}$(962) resonances is elucidated. A comparison of our calculation for $S-$factor with existing experimental and theoretical data is addressed, and a reasonable agreement is found. The reaction rate is calculated and compared with the existing rates. It has negligible dependence on the variation of AC, but shows a strong impact of the interference of $^{3}S_{1}$(312) and $^{3}S_{1}$(962) resonances, especially at temperatures, referring to the CNO Gamow windows. We estimate the contribution of cascade transitions to the reaction rate based on the exclusive experimental data by \textit{Imbriani, et al. 2012}. The reaction rate enhancement due to the cascade transitions is observed from $T_{9} > 0.3 $ and reaches the maximum factor $\sim $\ 1.3 at $T_{9}=1.3$. We present the Gamow energy window and a comparison of rates for radiative proton capture reactions $^{12}$N($p,γ$)$^{13}$O, $^{13}$N($p,γ$) $^{14}$O, $^{14}$N($p,γ$)$^{15}$O, and $^{15}$N($p,γ$)$^{16}$O obtained in the framework of the MPCM and give temperature windows, prevalence, and significance of each process.

## Key claim

The extrapolated zero-energy astrophysical S-factor S(0) for the 15N(p,γ0)16O reaction is found to lie within 34.7–40.4 keV·b, but this range depends on the interference between the two 3S1 resonances at 312 keV and 962 keV and on the choice of asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) within the experimental interval 12.88–14.76 fm−1/2.

## Method

The authors applied the modified potential cluster model (MPCM) with forbidden states, constructing Gaussian-form intercluster potentials for the p+15N channel that reproduce elastic scattering phase shifts, resonance energies and widths, binding energy, and the experimental ANC for the 16O ground state. They calculated E1 transition amplitudes from the two interfering 3S1 resonances and included a non-resonant M1 transition from the 3P1 scattering wave to the 3P0 ground state, then derived the astrophysical S-factor and thermonuclear reaction rate for the 15N(p,γ0)16O reaction.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated S(0) = 34.7–40.4 keV·b for the 15N(p,γ0)16O reaction.
- First 3S1 resonance: center-of-mass energy 312(2) keV, width 91(6) keV (Ajzenberg 1993) or 101.5(10) keV (Sukhoruchkin 2016); theoretical width obtained in this work ranges 125–141 keV.
- Second 3S1 resonance: center-of-mass energy 962(2) keV, width 139(2) keV (Sukhoruchkin 2016); theoretical width obtained in this work is 131 keV.
- Experimental total cross section at the 312 keV resonance: 6.0 ± 0.6 μb (Caciolli 2011) and 6.5 ± 0.6 μb (LeBlanc 2010); theoretical cross section in this work is 5.8–5.9 μb.
- Reaction rate enhancement from cascade transitions reaches a maximum factor of approximately 1.3 at T9 = 1.3, with enhancement appearing above T9 = 0.3.
- ANC range used: 12.88–14.76 fm−1/2, corresponding to dimensionless CW = 1.82–2.09.
- Binding energy of 16O in the p+15N channel: 12.1276 MeV.
- Root mean square charge radius of 16O: 2.54 fm; matter radius: 2.58 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous MPCM studies of radiative proton capture on light nuclei (Dubovichenko 2015, 2019) and specifically addresses the 15N(p,γ)16O reaction, which is the branching point between the CN and NO cycles of CNO burning. The results are compared with experimental data from Hebbard (1960), Rolfs (1974), LeBlanc (2010), Imbriani (2012), and Caciolli (2011), and with theoretical approaches including the R-matrix analysis by deBoer (2013) and cluster effective field theory by Son (2022). The calculated reaction rates enable improved predictions for 16O, 17O, and 18O nucleosynthesis yields during CNO cycle burning in massive stars.
