# The Single-Particle Spectral Function of $^{16}{\rm O}$

> The influence of short-range correlations on the $p$-wave single-particle spectral function in $^{16}{\rm O}$ is studied as a function of energy. This influence, which is represented by the admixture of high-momentum components, is found to be small in the $p$-shell quasihole wave functions. It is therefore unlikely that studies of quasihole momentum distributions using the $(e,e'p)$ reaction will reveal a significant contribution of high momentum components. Instead, high-momentum components become increasingly more dominant at higher excitation energy. The above observations are consistent with the energy distribution of high-momentum components in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations do not significantly alter the quasihole wave functions of the p-shell in 16O at low excitation energy, making it unlikely that (e,e'p) studies of quasihole momentum distributions will reveal high-momentum components; instead, high-momentum components become dominant only at higher excitation energy in the A-1 system.

## Method

The authors calculated the p-wave nucleon hole spectral function for 16O by solving the Dyson equation for the single-particle propagator, using a G-matrix interaction derived from the realistic OBEPC nucleon-nucleon potential. The irreducible self-energy was computed from second-order diagrams containing two-particle-one-hole and two-hole-one-particle terms, with the real part obtained via dispersion relations. The calculation employed a Hartree-Fock single-particle basis with bound states and plane waves for positive-energy states, properly orthogonalized and discretized in a box.

## Key numbers

- Quasihole energies: -17.9 MeV for the p3/2 state and -14.1 MeV for the p1/2 state in 16O
- Spectroscopic factors: 0.89 for the p1/2 state and 0.91 for the p3/2 state
- The theoretical spectroscopic factors overestimate experimental results from NIKHEF by about 0.2
- The largest eigenvalue of the one-body density matrix for the p3/2 orbital is 0.93, nearly identical to the quasihole strength of 0.91
- The continuum p1/2-strength integrated over momentum yields 4% of single-particle strength

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculation of the nucleon hole spectral function from nuclear matter (Ramos 1989, Benhar 1989) and 3He (Tadokoro 1987) to a heavier nucleus, 16O, using the method developed by Borromeo (1992). The results contradict the phenomenological prescription of Ma and Wambach (1991) and Mahaux and Sartor (1991), which predicted a strong suppression of the mean-field wave function in the nuclear interior due to short-range correlations. The findings enable future studies of higher partial waves (d, f, s) and the Koltun sum rule, as well as guiding experimental (e,e'p) searches for high-momentum components at higher excitation energies.
