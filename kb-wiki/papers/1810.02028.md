# The chiral vortical effect in Wigner function approach

> It is more subtle to obtain the chiral vortical effect (CVE) than chiral magnetic effect (CME) in quantum transport approach. To investigate the subtlty of the CVE we present two different derivation in the Wigner function approach. The first one is based on the method in our previous work \cite{Gao:2012ix} in which the CVE was derived under static-equilibrium conditions without details. We provide a detailed derivation using a more transparent and powerful method, which can be easily generalized to higher order calculation. In this derivation of the CVE current, there is an explicit Lorentz covariance. The second derivation is based on a more general chiral kinetic theory in a semi-classical expansion of the Wigner function without assuming static-equilibrium conditions \cite{Gao:2018wmr}. In this derivation, there is a freedom to choose a reference frame for the CVE current, so the explicit Lorentz covariance seems to be lost. Howerver, under static-equilibrium conditions, we show that the CVE current in this derivation can be decomposed into two parts, identified as the normal and magnetization current. Each part depends on the reference frame, but the sum of two parts does give the total CVE current which is independent of the reference frame. In the comoving frame of the fluid, it can be shown that the normal and magnetization current give one-third and two-thirds of the total CVE current respectively. This gives a natural solution to the 'one-third' puzzle in the CVE current in three-dimensional version of the chiral kinetic theory in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the chiral vortical effect (CVE) current derived from a semiclassical expansion of the Wigner function without assuming static-equilibrium conditions can be decomposed into normal and magnetization parts, each depending on the choice of reference frame, but their sum yields a frame-independent total CVE current that resolves the "one-third puzzle" in three-dimensional chiral kinetic theory.

## Method

The authors present two derivations of the CVE using the Wigner function approach for massless chiral fermions. The first derivation solves the Wigner function equations under static-equilibrium conditions with constant electromagnetic fields, using a self-iterative method to obtain solutions to first order in the Planck constant. The second derivation employs a semiclassical expansion of the Wigner function without assuming static-equilibrium conditions, introducing a time-like reference vector to define the independent component of the Wigner function, and then imposing thermal distribution and Killing conditions to extract the CVE current.

## Key numbers

- The normal current contributes one-third and the magnetization current contributes two-thirds of the total CVE current in the comoving frame of the fluid
- The CVE coefficient for the vector current is given by \(\xi = \mu\mu_5/\pi^2\)
- The CVE coefficient for the axial vector current is given by \(\xi_5 = T^2/6 + (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2)/(2\pi^2)\)
- The CME conductivity for the vector current is \(\xi_B = Q\mu_5/(2\pi^2)\)
- The CME conductivity for the axial vector current is \(\xi_{B5} = Q\mu/(2\pi^2)\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivations of the CVE in Gao (2012) and the semiclassical chiral kinetic theory in Gao (2018) by providing detailed derivations and resolving the "one-third puzzle" in the literature regarding the CVE current in three-dimensional chiral kinetic theory. The results enable consistent quantum transport descriptions of both the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect, which are relevant for understanding spin-vorticity couplings and hadron polarization in heavy ion collisions as measured by the STAR collaboration (2017).
