# Chiral Symmetry and s-wave Low-Lying Meson-Baryon Resonances

> The $s-$wave meson-baryon scattering is analyzed for the isospin-strangeness $I=1/2, S=0$ and $I=0,S=-1$ sectors, in a Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating Chiral Symmetry. For both sectors, four channels have been considered: $πN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$, $K Σ$ and $πΣ$, $\bar K N$, $ηΛ$, $K Ξ$, respectively. The needed two particle irreducible matrix amplitudes are taken from lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory in a relativistic formalism. There appear undetermined low energy constants, as a consequence of the renormalization of the amplitudes, which are obtained from fits to the available data: elastic $πN $ phase-shifts, $π^- p \to ηn$ and $π^- p \to K^0 Λ$ cross sections and to $πΣ\toπΣ$ mass-spectrum, the elastic $\bar K N \to \bar K N$ and $ \bar K N\to πΣ$ $t$--matrices and to the $ K^- p \to ηΛ$ cross section data. The position and residues of the complex poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude determine masses, widths and branching ratios of the $S_{11}-$ $N$(1535) and $-N$(1650) and $S_{01}-$ $Λ$(1405) and $-Λ$(1670) resonances, in reasonable agreement with experiment. A good overall description of data, from threshold up to around 2 GeV is achieved despite the fact that three-body channels have not been explicitly included.

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter coupled-channel formalism with a kernel from lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory can describe s-wave meson-baryon scattering data from threshold up to around 2 GeV, but only after fitting 12 undetermined low-energy constants per sector to data, and despite the explicit omission of three-body channels.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for the isospin-strangeness sectors (I=1/2, S=0) and (I=0, S=-1), using the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian as the two-particle irreducible kernel. The four coupled channels for each sector are πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ and πΣ, K̅N, ηΛ, KΞ respectively. The 12 unknown low-energy constants per sector arising from renormalization are fitted to elastic πN phase shifts, π⁻p → ηn and π⁻p → K⁰Λ cross sections, πΣ → πΣ mass spectrum, elastic and inelastic K̅N t-matrices, and K⁻p → ηΛ cross section data. Resonance properties are extracted from the positions and residues of complex poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The analysis covers data from threshold up to around 2 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- 12 undetermined low-energy constants are fitted per strangeness-isospin sector.
- Four resonances are identified: S₁₁ N(1535), S₁₁ N(1650), S₀₁ Λ(1405), and S₀₁ Λ(1670).
- The πN S₁₁ phase shifts and inelasticities are shown as functions of √s (no numerical values given for the phase shifts themselves in the truncated text).
- The πΣ → πΣ mass spectrum and K⁻p → ηΛ cross section are shown as functions of center-of-mass energy (no explicit numerical values given in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter coupled-channel approach to meson-baryon scattering in the S=0 and S=-1 sectors, building on earlier unitarization methods such as the Inverse Amplitude Method (Pelaez and Oset, 2000; Oller and Oset, 2000), dispersion relations (Jido and Oset, 2001; Meissner and Oller, 1999), and Lippmann-Schwinger equation approaches (Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise, 1995; Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2002). The authors note that Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to one loop fails completely already at threshold for the S=-1 channel due to the nearby Λ(1405) resonance (Kaiser, 2001). The results enable future work to incorporate three-body channels and to restore crossing symmetry at subleading order, which would reduce the number of undetermined low-energy constants.
