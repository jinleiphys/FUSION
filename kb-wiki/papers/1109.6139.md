# Role of asymmetry of the reaction and momentum dependent interactions on the balance energy for neutron rich nuclei

> We present a systematic study of disappearance of flow i.e. balance energy $E_{bal}$ for an isotopic series of Ca with N/Z varying from 1 to 2 for different density dependences of symmetry energies. We also extend this study for asymmetric reactions having radioactive projectile and stable target.

## Key claim

The balance energy \(E_{bal}\) for an isotopic series of Ca (N/Z from 1 to 2) depends linearly on N/Z (or N/A) and is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but the N/Z dependence of \(E_{bal}\) is independent of the asymmetry of the reaction (i.e., whether the reaction is symmetric or asymmetric) and is preserved when momentum dependent interactions (MDI) are included, even though MDI changes the absolute value of \(E_{bal}\) by about 30%.

## Method

The study used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate thousands of events for symmetric Ca+Ca reactions (N/Z from 1.0 to 2.0) and asymmetric reactions with a stable \(^{40}\)Ca target and projectiles from \(^{40}\)Ca to \(^{60}\)Ca (N/A from 0.5 to 0.6). Balance energies were extracted by running simulations in small steps of 10 MeV/nucleon around the disappearance of flow for three forms of symmetry energy: \(F_1(u) \propto u\) (stiff), \(F_2(u) \propto u^{0.4}\) (soft), and \(F_3(u) \propto u^{2}\) (super stiff), where \(u = \rho/\rho_0\). The effect of momentum dependent interactions (MDI) was also tested for the stiff symmetry energy case.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric Ca+Ca reactions, \(E_{bal}\) was calculated for N/Z varying from 1.0 to 2.0 (N/A from 0.5 to 0.67) using three forms of symmetry energy; the stiff form \(F_1(u)\) gave lower \(E_{bal}\) than the soft form \(F_2(u)\), while the super stiff form \(F_3(u)\) gave higher \(E_{bal}\) than \(F_2(u)\).
- For asymmetric reactions with a fixed \(^{40}\)Ca target and projectiles from \(^{40}\)Ca to \(^{60}\)Ca, the N/A of the reaction varied from 0.5 to 0.6 and the reaction asymmetry \(\delta = (A_1 - A_2)/(A_1 + A_2)\) varied from 0 to 0.2; the \(E_{bal}\) for \(^{60}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca matched that of the symmetric reaction with the same N/A (0.6).
- Inclusion of MDI changed the absolute value of \(E_{bal}\) by about 30% for the symmetric reactions with the stiff symmetry energy \(F_1(u)\), but the N/Z (N/A) dependence of \(E_{bal}\) remained unchanged.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Gautam et al. (2010) which showed that in-plane flow is sensitive to the symmetry energy and its density dependence, with sensitivity increasing for neutron-rich systems. It also builds on earlier work by Sood (2010) on the role of MDI in collective flow disappearance. The results enable the use of balance energy measurements in both symmetric and asymmetric reactions (including radioactive beams on stable targets) as a probe of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, independent of reaction asymmetry and MDI effects.
