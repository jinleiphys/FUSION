# The Quenched ${g_A}$ in Nuclei and Emergent Scale Symmetry in Baryonic Matter

> A recent RIKEN experiment on the quenched $g_A$ in the superallowed Gamow-Telller transition from $^{100}$Sn indicates a role of scale anomaly encoded in the anomalous dimension $β^\prime$ of the gluonic stress tensor ${\rm Tr \ G}_{μν}^2$. This observation provides a support to the notion of hidden scale symmetry emerging by strong nuclear correlations with an IR fixed point realized -- in the chiral limit -- in the Nambu-Goldstone mode. We suggest there is an analogy in the way scale symmetry manifests in nuclear medium to the continuity from the unitarity limit at low density (in light nuclei) to the dilaton limit at high density (in compact stars). In between the limits, say, at normal nuclear matter density, the symmetry is not visible, hence hidden.

# The Quenched \(g_A\) in Nuclei and Emergent Scale Symmetry in Baryonic Matter

## Key claim

The RIKEN experiment on the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition from \(^{100}\)Sn, if confirmed, indicates that the anomalous dimension \(\beta'\) of the gluonic stress tensor \(\text{Tr } G_{\mu\nu}^2\) plays a non-negligible role in the quenching of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\), contradicting the leading-order scale symmetry (LOSS) approximation which predicts \(g_A^{\text{eff}} \approx 1\) for all nuclei. The authors suggest that scale symmetry emerges at both low density (unitarity limit in light nuclei) and high density (dilaton limit in compact stars) but is hidden at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors employ a generalized scale-chiral effective field theory (G\(\sigma\)EFT) that includes nucleons, pions, the lowest-lying vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\)) with hidden local symmetry, and a scalar dilaton \(\chi\) (identified with \(f_0(500)\)) as the pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of broken scale symmetry. The quenching factor is decomposed into two parts: \(q_{\text{ssb}}\) from explicit scale symmetry breaking (dependent on \(\beta'\)) and \(q_{\text{snc}}\) from strong nuclear many-body correlations, the latter computed using a Fermi-liquid fixed-point (FLFP) approach with the Landau-Migdal formalism. The LOSS approximation sets \(c_A = 1\) in the axial current, eliminating the \(\beta'\) dependence from the operator.

## Key numbers

- The free axial coupling constant is \(g_A^{\text{free}} = 1.276\) from neutron decay in vacuum.
- For light nuclei (\(A \lesssim 60\)), shell-model calculations yield \(g_A^{\text{eff}} = 0.98 - 1.18\), corresponding to a quenching factor \(q_{\text{light}} \approx 0.78\) for the case \(g_A^{\text{eff}} \approx 1\).
- The RIKEN experiment on \(^{100}\)Sn gives a Gamow-Teller strength \(\mathcal{B}_{\text{GT}}^{\text{RIKEN}} = 4.4^{+0.9}_{-0.7}\), yielding a quenching factor \(q^{\text{ESPM}}_{\text{RIKEN}} = 0.46 - 0.55\).
- The ESPM prediction for the \(^{100}\)Sn GT strength is \(\mathcal{B}_{\text{GT}}^{\text{ESPM}} = 17.78\).
- In the LOSS approximation, the strong nuclear correlation factor is \(q^{\text{Landau}}_{\text{snc}} \simeq 0.79\) at nuclear matter density \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), giving \(g_A^{\text{Landau}} \simeq 1.0\).
- With \(c_A \approx 0.15\) and \(\beta' \approx 2.0\), the scale-symmetry-breaking factor becomes \(q_{\text{ssb}} \approx 0.63\), leading to a total quenching factor \(q^{\text{ESPM}}_{\text{G}\sigma\text{EFT}} \approx 0.50\), consistent with the RIKEN result.

## Context

This work extends the G\(\sigma\)EFT framework previously applied to compact-star equations of state (Ma and Rho, 2019; MR-PCM) to the long-standing problem of \(g_A\) quenching in nuclei, first noted by Wilkinson (1970s). The authors argue that the LOSS approximation, which successfully describes nuclear matter and compact-star properties, predicts \(g_A^{\text{eff}} \approx 1\) for all nuclei, consistent with older data but contradicted by the recent RIKEN measurement on \(^{100}\)Sn. They contrast their approach with standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT), which they claim cannot reliably compute the many-body corrections at the required order. The paper suggests a continuity of scale symmetry from the unitarity limit at low density (pionless EFT) to the dilaton-limit fixed point at high density, with the \(g_A\) quenching providing a test of the Crewther-Tunstall (2015) scenario for an infrared fixed point in QCD with \(N_f \sim 2-3\). Future precision experiments on superallowed GT transitions in doubly magic nuclei are needed to confirm the role of \(\beta'\).
