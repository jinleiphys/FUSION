# Antinucleon-nucleus interaction near threshold from the Paris $\bar NN$ potential

> A general algorithm for handling the energy dependence of hadron-nucleon amplitudes in the nuclear medium, consistently with their density dependence, has been recently applied to antikaons, eta mesons and pions interacting with nuclei. Here we apply this approach to antiprotons below threshold, analyzing experimental results for antiprotonic atoms across the periodic table. It is also applied to antiproton and antineutron interaction with nuclei up to 400 MeV/c, comparing with elastic scattering and annihilation cross sections. The underlying $\bar pN$ scattering amplitudes are derived from the Paris $\bar NN$ potential, including modifications in the medium. Emphasis is placed on the role of the $P$-wave amplitudes with respect to the repulsive $S$-wave amplitudes.

## Key claim

The Paris $\bar NN$ potential's free-space $S$-wave amplitudes are repulsive near threshold, contradicting the empirically required attractive $\bar p$-nucleus optical potential; the authors show that in-medium modifications (WRW prescription and subthreshold energy shifts) can reverse the $S$-wave sign to attraction, but the resulting microscopic potential still fails to reproduce antiprotonic atom data and 48 MeV elastic scattering without adding an empirical $P$-wave term, indicating that the Paris potential's $P$-wave amplitudes are inconsistent with the $S$-wave amplitudes within the present in-medium model.

## Method

The authors apply an algorithm that maps free-space $\bar pN$ scattering amplitudes (from the 2009 Paris $\bar NN$ potential) to in-medium amplitudes using density-dependent subthreshold energy shifts (Eq.~(\ref{eq:SCmodifMS}) for atoms, Eq.~(\ref{eq:SCmodifMSrev}) for scattering) and the Waas-Rho-Weise (WRW) multiple-scattering correction for $S$-wave Pauli correlations (Eq.~(\ref{eq:WRW})). They solve the Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential containing both $S$-wave and $P$-wave terms (Eqs.~(\ref{eq:EEs}) and (\ref{eq:EEp})), performing global fits to 90 data points from antiprotonic atoms (PS209 collaboration) and 83 data points from 48 MeV elastic scattering on C, Ca, and Pb, comparing with empirical potentials and testing scaling of the $P$-wave amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For antiprotonic atoms: the best-fit empirical potential yields $\chi^2 = 199$ (90 data points); the unmodified Paris 2009 in-medium potential (no adjustable parameters) gives $\chi^2 = 2304$; replacing the $P$-wave term with an empirical one (real $2.9\pm0.1$ fm$^3$, imaginary $1.8\pm0.1$ fm$^3$) while keeping the medium-modified $S$-wave gives $\chi^2 = 203$.
- For 48 MeV elastic scattering: the best-fit empirical potential yields $\chi^2 = 151$ (83 data points); the unmodified Paris 2009 in-medium potential gives $\chi^2 = 1823$; replacing the $P$-wave with an empirical term (real $0.31\pm0.03$ fm$^3$, imaginary $0.50\pm0.03$ fm$^3$) with a folding range of $1.5\pm0.1$ fm gives $\chi^2 = 176$.
- The subthreshold energy shift $\delta\sqrt{s}$ for $\bar p$ atoms reaches about $-60$ MeV at nuclear-matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The Paris 2009 potential generates a quasi-bound $^{33}P_1$ state at $E = -4.5 - i9.0$ MeV and a broad $^{11}S_0$ state at $E = -4.8 - i26$ MeV.
- The $\bar pp$ scattering length (Coulomb-corrected) from the Paris potential is $-0.81 + i0.72$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous algorithm for handling energy- and density-dependent hadron-nucleon amplitudes (applied to antikaons, eta mesons, and pions in Friedman et al. (2013) and Friedman et al. (2014)) to antiprotons. It directly confronts the long-standing puzzle, recognized since Green and Wycech (1982) and Suzuki and Narumi (1983), that free-space $\bar pN$ amplitudes are repulsive while empirical $\bar p$-nucleus potentials are attractive; earlier attempts to add a $P$-wave term (Batty et al. (1995)) failed to respect neutron density constraints. The present results enable future tests of other microscopic $\bar NN$ potentials (e.g., Zhou and Timmermans (2012), the Bonn-Juelich chiral EFT model of Kang et al. (2014)) in $\bar p$-nucleus calculations, and highlight the need for improved in-medium $P$-wave treatment or inclusion of $D$-wave contributions above threshold.
