# Finite-temperature extension for cold neutron star equations of state

> Observations of isolated neutron stars place constraints on the equation of state (EOS) of cold, neutron-rich matter, while nuclear physics experiments probe the EOS of hot, symmetric matter. Many dynamical phenomena, such as core-collapse supernovae, the formation and cooling of proto-neutron stars, and neutron star mergers, lie between these two regimes and depend on the EOS at finite temperatures for matter with varying proton fractions. In this paper, we introduce a new framework to accurately calculate the thermal pressure of neutron-proton-electron matter at arbitrary density, temperature, and proton fraction. This framework can be expressed using a set of five physically-motivated parameters that span a narrow range of values for realistic EOS and are able to capture the leading-order effects of degenerate matter on the thermal pressure. We base two of these parameters on a new approximation of the Dirac effective mass, with which we reproduce the thermal pressure to within <~30% for a variety of realistic EOS at densities of interest. Three additional parameters, based on the behavior of the symmetry energy near the nuclear saturation density, allow for the extrapolation of any cold EOS in beta-equilibrium to arbitrary proton fractions. Our model thus allows a user to extend any cold nucleonic EOS, including piecewise-polytropes, to arbitrary temperature and proton fraction, for use in calculations and numerical simulations of astrophysical phenomena. We find that our formalism is able to reproduce realistic finite-temperature EOS with errors of <~20% and offers a 1-3 orders-of-magnitude improvement over existing ideal-fluid models.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new analytic framework to extend any cold nucleonic equation of state (EOS) in beta-equilibrium to finite temperatures and arbitrary proton fractions, with the hedge that the model is currently specific to neutron-proton-electron matter and reproduces realistic finite-temperature EOS with errors of less than about 20% for densities above nuclear saturation density, offering a 1–3 orders-of-magnitude improvement over existing ideal-fluid hybrid models.

## Method

The authors separate the total energy per baryon into cold and thermal components, then further decompose the cold part using a parabolic expansion in the neutron excess parameter (1-2Y_p) around symmetric matter, with the symmetry energy parameterized by three quantities: the symmetry energy at saturation density S_0, its slope L, and a density-dependence exponent gamma. The thermal pressure is approximated using a new two-parameter description based on the Dirac effective mass, which captures the leading-order effects of degeneracy. The complete model is analytic and closed-form, allowing any cold nucleonic EOS (including piecewise-polytropes) to be extended to arbitrary temperature and proton fraction by adding the symmetry-energy-dependent correction and the effective-mass-based thermal component.

## Key numbers

- The thermal pressure is reproduced to within less than or equal to 30% for a variety of realistic EOS at densities of interest using the new Dirac effective mass approximation.
- The complete formalism reproduces realistic finite-temperature EOS with errors of less than or equal to 20% for densities above the nuclear saturation density (n_sat = 0.16 fm^-3).
- The model offers a 1–3 orders-of-magnitude improvement over existing ideal-fluid hybrid EOS models.
- The symmetry energy parameter gamma spans a range from approximately 0.15 to 1.0 across the EOS sample, with specific fitted values including: TM1 (gamma=0.75), NL3 (gamma=0.62), FSUGold (gamma=1.11), DD2 (gamma=0.91), STOS (gamma=0.77), SFHo (gamma=0.41), LS (gamma=1.05), and SLY4-RG (gamma=0.35).
- The nuclear saturation density is fixed to n_sat = 0.16 fm^-3 for all EOS comparisons.
- For the hybrid EOS comparison, the thermal adiabatic index Gamma_th is constrained to be between 1 and 2.

## Context

This work extends the parametric EOS approach pioneered by Read (2009) and Ozel (2009) for cold neutron star matter into the finite-temperature regime, directly addressing the need identified by Bauswein (2010) that hybrid EOS with constant Gamma_th produce post-merger frequencies 50–250 Hz smaller and remnant lifetimes differing by a factor of two compared to realistic finite-temperature EOS. The framework builds on the Sommerfeld expansion results of Constantinou (2015) showing that thermal properties depend strongly on the nucleon effective mass, but removes the requirement of knowing the microphysical potentials of the cold EOS, making it applicable to piecewise-polytropes. The model enables numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron star cooling, and neutron star mergers to incorporate finite-temperature effects with arbitrary proton fractions without needing pre-computed EOS tables for every possible composition.
