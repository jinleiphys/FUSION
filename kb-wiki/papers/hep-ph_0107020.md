# Pion Generalized Dipole Polarizabilities by Virtual Compton Scattering $πe \to πeγ$

> We present a calculation of the cross section and the event generator of the reaction $πe\to πe γ$. This reaction is sensitive to the pion generalized dipole polarizabilities, namely, the longitudinal electric $α_L(q^2)$, the transverse electric $α_T(q^2)$, and the magnetic $β(q^2)$ which, in the real-photon limit, reduce to the ordinary electric and magnetic polarizabilities $\barα$ and $\barβ$, respectively. The calculation of the cross section is done in the framework of chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$. A pion VCS event generator has been written which is ready for implementation in GEANT simulation codes or for independent use.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of the cross section and an event generator for the reaction πe → πeγ, claiming that this reaction provides direct access to the pion generalized dipole polarizabilities α_L(q²), α_T(q²), and β(q²) without the need for extrapolation to the pion pole, but only in the low-energy limit where the non-Born contribution is approximated using chiral perturbation theory at O(p⁴), which introduces a degeneracy β(q²) = −α_L(q²) = −α_T(q²) that will only be lifted at higher orders.

## Method

The authors computed the fourfold differential cross section for πe → πeγ by decomposing the invariant amplitude into Bethe-Heitler (BH) and virtual Compton scattering (VCS) contributions, where the VCS part is further separated into generalized Born terms (containing the pion electromagnetic form factor) and a non-Born residual term encoding the generalized dipole polarizabilities. The non-Born tensor was evaluated using chiral perturbation theory at O(p⁴), and the squared matrix elements for the pure BH, pure VCS, and BH-VCS interference terms were derived analytically. A Monte Carlo event generator using the acceptance-rejection method was then constructed to simulate events in the physical phase space, with cuts applied to avoid the pole regions of the BH and VCS amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The charged pion RCS polarizabilities from current-algebra low-energy theory are \(\bar{\alpha}_{\pi^\pm} = -\bar{\beta}_{\pi^\pm} = (2.68 \pm 0.42) \times 10^{-43}\) cm³
- The pion-decay constant used is \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV
- Corrections at O(p⁶) to the O(p⁴) values are 12% for \(\bar{\alpha}\) and 24% for \(\bar{\beta}\)
- The ratio \(\bar{\alpha} / (\frac{4}{3}\pi r_\pi^3) \sim 10^{-3}\), where \(r_\pi\) is the pion electromagnetic radius
- The minimal final electron energy cut is set at \(E_{k'}^{\rm min} = 10\) GeV
- The minimal final photon energy cut is set at \(E_{q'}^{\rm min} = 5\) GeV
- The invariant \(s_1\) is generated in the range \(1000\,m_e^2 \leq s_1 \leq m_\rho^2\)
- The invariant \(s_2\) is generated in the range \(2\,m_\pi^2 \leq s_2 \leq m_\rho^2\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of nucleon VCS experiments at MAMI (Roche, 2000) to the pion, where generalized polarizabilities were originally introduced for nuclei (Arenhoevel, 1974) and later applied to the nucleon (Guichon, 1995). The calculation uses the O(p⁴) chiral perturbation theory framework of Unkmeir (2000) and Lvov (2001), and the event generator is designed for implementation in GEANT simulation codes to support future experiments at facilities such as CERN COMPASS, Jefferson Lab, and MAMI. The paper enables experimental extraction of the pion generalized dipole polarizabilities in the space-like region, which would provide access to the local polarization response of the pion rather than just the integrated global response measured in real Compton scattering.
