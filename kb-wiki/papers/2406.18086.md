# Quantum corrections to tunnelling amplitudes of neutral scalar fields

> Though theoretical treatments of quantum tunnelling within single-particle quantum mechanics are well-established, at present, there is no quantum field-theoretic description (QFT) of tunnelling. Due to the single-particle nature of quantum mechanics, many-particle effects arising from quantum field theory are not accounted for. Such many-particle effects, including pair-production, have proved to be essential in resolving the Klein-paradox. This work seeks to determine how quantum corrections affect the tunnelling probability through an external field. We investigate a massive neutral scalar field, which interacts with an external field in accordance with relativistic quantum mechanics. To consider QFT corrections, we include another massive quantised neutral scalar field coupling to the original via a cubic interaction. This study formulates an all-order recursive expression for the loop-corrected scalar propagator, which contains only the class of vertex-corrected Feynman diagrams. This equation applies for general external potentials. Though there is no closed-form analytic solution, we also demonstrate how to approximate the QFT corrections if a perturbative coupling to the quantised field is assumed.

## Key claim
This work develops a formalism for incorporating quantum field theory corrections into quantum tunnelling amplitudes, but only for a restricted class of vertex-corrected Feynman diagrams; the extraction of actual tunnelling amplitudes from the dressed propagator remains unsolved and is left for future work.

## Method
The authors consider a massive neutral scalar field interacting with an external scalar potential and a second quantised neutral scalar field via a cubic interaction. They derive an all-order recursive integral equation for the dressed scalar propagator that includes only vertex-corrected diagrams, analogous to the self-consistent Hartree-Fock expansion. They then make a perturbative coupling approximation (treating the quantised field coupling as small while keeping the external potential non-perturbative) to obtain a one-loop expression that is no longer self-referential.

## Key numbers
- The cubic interaction coupling constant is denoted $\mu$, with vertex factor $-i\mu$; the external field coupling is $e$, with vertex factor $-ie\tilde{u}(p-k)$ where $\tilde{u}$ is the Fourier transform of the external potential.
- The number of diagrams in the $(n+1)$th generation of the recursive expansion grows as $m_{n+1} = (m_n+1)^2$.
- The Schwinger limit for the Klein paradox is given as $eV > 2m$, where $m$ is the scalar field mass.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Zielinski 2024) which recovered relativistic quantum mechanical tunnelling amplitudes for delta-function potentials via analytic continuation of the S-matrix, and also extends the QFT-based scattering formalisms of de Leo (2009) and Xu (2016) which were limited to above-barrier scattering and neglected quantised fields. The formalism is conceptually similar to the self-consistent Hartree-Fock diagrammatic expansion (Fetter 2003) and the Furry expansion in strong-field QED (Fedotov 2023). The work provides the necessary groundwork for future numerical determination of how loop corrections affect tunnelling probabilities, potentially enabling studies of more physically interesting Lagrangians such as QED.
