# Ferromagnetic Neutron Stars in Scalar-Tensor Theories of Gravity

> Ferromagnetic spin ordering can take place in neutron stars. This phase transition alters the neutron star equation of state. Here, applying the scalar-tensor theories of gravity, we investigate the structure of neutron stars which are in the ferromagnetic phase. Considering the equation of state of ferromagnetic neutron matter with Skyrme-type interactions at zero temperature and using the scalar-tensor theories of gravity with sufficiently negative coupling constant, we explore the spontaneous scalarization in ferromagnetic neutron stars. In this regard, the mass versus the central density, the profiles of scalar field and mass and density, the central scalar field for different central densities, and the mass-radius relation of ferromagnetic neutron stars are presented. Moreover, we investigate the influences of the coupling constant on the scalarization of ferromagnetic neutron stars. The effects of the coupling constant on the critical densities of scalarization and the scalar charge of ferromagnetic neutron stars are also calculated. In addition, we study the maximum value of the coupling constant at which the spontaneous scalarization takes place presenting the influence of the neutron matter equation of state on this critical value.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how ferromagnetic spin ordering in neutron matter alters the structure and spontaneous scalarization of neutron stars within scalar-tensor theories of gravity, finding that the softer equation of state of ferromagnetic neutron matter allows scalarization to persist at high central densities where non-ferromagnetic neutron stars would return to general relativity solutions.

## Method

The authors use Skyrme-type interactions (SLy230a model) to compute the equation of state for ferromagnetic neutron matter at zero temperature, determining the equilibrium spin polarization parameter at each density. They then solve the generalized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations in scalar-tensor theories of gravity for two coupling function models (M1 and M2) with various negative coupling constants, integrating the equations with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm and iterating on the central scalar field to satisfy the boundary condition at infinity.

## Key numbers

- Ferromagnetic transition begins at density about 5.40 times the nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 1.66 x 10^17 kg/m^3) and reaches full polarization at about 10.41 rho_0
- Maximum mass of non-ferromagnetic neutron stars in general relativity: 2.15 solar masses; maximum mass of ferromagnetic neutron stars in general relativity: 1.36 solar masses
- Maximum mass of ferromagnetic neutron stars in scalar-tensor theory ranges from 1.16 to 1.36 solar masses depending on model and coupling constant
- Maximum mass of non-ferromagnetic neutron stars in scalar-tensor theory ranges from 1.93 to 2.48 solar masses depending on model and coupling constant
- Maximum compactness of ferromagnetic neutron stars in scalar-tensor theory ranges from 0.13 to 0.17; in general relativity it is 0.17
- Maximum compactness of non-ferromagnetic neutron stars in scalar-tensor theory ranges from 0.25 to 0.30; in general relativity it is 0.33
- Critical coupling constant for scalarization: -4.35 for non-ferromagnetic neutron stars and -4.29 for ferromagnetic neutron stars

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spontaneous scalarization in neutron stars within scalar-tensor theories of gravity, particularly the foundational work by Damour and Esposito-Farese (1993) who showed strong-field deviations from general relativity occur for coupling constants beta less than about -4. The authors apply the ferromagnetic neutron matter equation of state from Rios (2007) and others who studied spin ordering with Skyrme forces, and compare with prior scalarization results using polytropic equations of state (beta-4.35, 2013) and realistic nuclear matter equations of state (Motahar, 2017; Sotani, 2014). The results enable future studies of massive scalar-tensor theories where the coupling constant can be much smaller than the binary pulsar bound of beta greater than -4.5 from Freire (2012), and suggest that ferromagnetic neutron stars may exhibit scalarization signatures distinct from non-ferromagnetic ones.
