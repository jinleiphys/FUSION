# Linear scaling of lepton charge asymmetry in $W^\pm$ production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> The lepton charge asymmetry in $W^\pm$ production in the nuclear collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV is investigated with a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. Recently published ALICE and the ATLAS data of lepton charge asymmetry are well reproduced. An interesting linear scaling behavior is observed in the lepton charge asymmetry as a function of the collision system valence quark number asymmetry among the different size of nuclear collision systems at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. This linear scaling behavior may serve as an additional constraint on the PDF (nPDF) extractions.

## Key claim

The paper claims a universal linear scaling between the lepton charge asymmetry from W± decays and the valence quark number asymmetry of the collision system, observed across both nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. This scaling behavior is presented as a potential additional constraint for parton distribution function (PDF) and nuclear PDF (nPDF) extractions, though the authors note that nuclear modifications to the vacuum nucleon PDF should break this scaling when examined differentially in lepton pT and rapidity.

## Method

The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE (based on Pythia 6.4.28) to simulate W± production in elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions (pp, pn, np, nn) and symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions (Cu+Cu, Xe+Xe, Au+Au, Pb+Pb, U+U) at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. The simulations were stopped at the partonic initialization stage after initial hard scattering and parton shower processes, with W± production channels activated and parton shower switched off to prevent double-counting, achieving precision equivalent to next-to-leading-order pQCD. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, nucleon positions were sampled according to the Woods-Saxon distribution and collisions were executed sequentially based on minimum approaching distance.

## Key numbers

- Muon charge asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV for 0-80% centrality class: PACIAE results agree with ATLAS data across |ηl| bins from (0,0.21) to (2.18,2.5), with kinematic cuts pTl > 25 GeV/c, pTν > 25 GeV/c, |ηl| < 2.5, mT > 40 GeV/c²
- Muon charge asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV for 0-90% centrality class: PACIAE result agrees with ALICE datum with kinematic cuts pTμ > 10 GeV/c and 2.5 < yμ < 4.0
- In elementary NN collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV with pTμ > 10 GeV/c: Aμ is positive in mid-rapidity (|yμ| < 1) for all four collision types (pp, pn, np, nn), negative in forward rapidity (2.5 < |yμ| < 4.0), and for full rapidity phase-space Aμ in pn and np collisions is consistent with zero while Aμ in pp and nn are almost equal in magnitude but opposite in sign
- In symmetric AA collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV with pTμ > 10 GeV/c: Aμ is positive in mid-rapidity (|yμ| < 1) for all five systems (Cu+Cu, Xe+Xe, Au+Au, Pb+Pb, U+U) but negative in forward (2.5 < |yμ| < 4.0) and full rapidity regions

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of lepton charge asymmetry in pp collisions at √s = 7 and 8 TeV by ATLAS (2011-2019), CMS (2011-2016), and LHCb (2012-2016), as well as in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV by ALICE (2016) and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV by ATLAS (2014) and CMS (2012). The linear scaling behavior observed between Aμ and Av across both NN and AA collision systems may provide a new tool to constrain nPDF in a wide Bjorken-x region, particularly by measuring deviations from scaling when examining pT and y differential distributions between NN and AA collisions.
