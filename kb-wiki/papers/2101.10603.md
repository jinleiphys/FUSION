# System size dependence of baryon-strangeness correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions from a multiphase transport model

> The system size dependence of baryon-strangeness (BS) correlations ($C_{BS}$) are investigated with a multiphase transport (AMPT) model for various collision systems from $\mathrm{^{10}B+^{10}B}$, $\mathrm{^{12}C+^{12}C}$, $\mathrm{^{16}O+^{16}O}$, $\mathrm{^{20}Ne+^{20}Ne}$, $\mathrm{^{40}Ca+^{40}Ca}$, $\mathrm{^{96}Zr+^{96}Zr}$, and $\mathrm{^{197}Au+^{197}Au}$ at RHIC energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ of 200, 39, 27, 20, and 7.7 GeV. Both effects of hadron rescattering and a combination of different hadrons play a leading role for baryon-strangeness correlations. When the kinetic window is limited to absolute rapidity $|y|>3$, these correlations tend to be constant after the final-state interaction whatever kind of hadrons subset we chose based on the AMPT framework. The correlation is found to smoothly increase with baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, corresponding to the collision system or energy from the quark-gluon-plasma-like phase to the hadron-gas-like phase. Besides, the influence of initial nuclear geometrical structures of $α$-clustered nuclear collision systems of $\mathrm{^{12}C+^{12}C}$ as well as $\mathrm{^{16}O+^{16}O}$ collisions is discussed but the effect is found negligible. The current model studies provide baselines for searching for the signals of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase transition and critical point in heavy-ion collisions through the BS correlation.

## Key claim

The baryon-strangeness correlation coefficient \(C_{BS}\) smoothly increases with baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) across different collision systems and energies, transitioning from a quark-gluon-plasma-like phase to a hadron-gas-like phase, but the effect of initial nuclear \(\alpha\)-clustering geometry on \(C_{BS}\) is found to be negligible.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model to simulate central (0-5%) collisions of seven systems (\({}^{10}\)B+\({}^{10}\)B, \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{16}\)O+\({}^{16}\)O, \({}^{20}\)Ne+\({}^{20}\)Ne, \({}^{40}\)Ca+\({}^{40}\)Ca, \({}^{96}\)Zr+\({}^{96}\)Zr, and \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au) at RHIC energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200, 39, 27, 20, and 7.7 GeV. They calculated \(C_{BS}\) from the net-baryon number and net-strangeness in each event, comparing results with and without hadron rescattering, and for different subsets of hadrons (e.g., all hadrons vs. only p, n, and K). They also examined the dependence on the maximum rapidity acceptance \(y_{\text{max}}\) and on the initial nucleon distribution (Woods-Saxon vs. \(\alpha\)-clustering for \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{16}\)O).

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV, \(C_{BS}\) goes down to 0.8 at the top RHIC energy, below the ideal QGP value of 1.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV, the correlation coefficient \(C_{BS}\) with hadron rescattering slightly increases with baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) as system size increases; \(\mu_B\) values were extracted from a thermal model.
- For \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions, the correlation coefficient \(C_{BS}\) shows strong energy dependence at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 11.5, 20, 27, 39, and 200 GeV.
- When the kinetic window is limited to absolute rapidity \(|y| > 3\), \(C_{BS}\) tends to a constant value independent of the hadron subset chosen.
- For \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{16}\)O+\({}^{16}\)O collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 10, 200, and 6370 GeV, the \(\alpha\)-clustering structure shows nearly no visible difference from the Woods-Saxon distribution in \(C_{BS}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon-strangeness correlations using the AMPT model (Jin 2008) and the UrQMD model (Haussler 2007, Luo 2009) by performing a systematic scan over collision system size and energy. It provides baselines for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (BES-I, BES-II) to search for the QCD phase transition and critical point. The finding that \(C_{BS}\) is insensitive to initial nuclear geometry (e.g., \(\alpha\)-clustering in light nuclei) helps isolate the effects of hadron rescattering and the hadron combination on the correlation.
