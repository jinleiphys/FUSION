# The Schwinger mechanism and graphene

> The Schwinger mechanism, the production of charged particle-antiparticle pairs in a macroscopic external electric field, is derived for 2+1 dimensional theories. The rate of pair production per unit area for four species of massless fermions, with charge $q$, in a constant electric field $E$ is given by $ π^{-2} \hbar^{-3/2} \tilde{c}^{-1/2} (q E)^{3/2} $ where $\tilde{c}$ is the speed of light for the two-dimensional system. To the extent undoped graphene behaves like the quantum field-theoretic vacuum for massless fermions in 2+1 dimensions, the Schwinger mechanism should be testable experimentally. A possible experimental configuration for this is proposed. Effects due to deviations from this idealized picture of graphene are briefly considered. It is argued that with present day samples of graphene, tests of the Schwinger formula may be possible.

## Key claim

The Schwinger mechanism (pair production in a constant electric field) can be tested experimentally in undoped graphene, which behaves like the quantum field-theoretic vacuum for massless fermions in 2+1 dimensions; the authors argue that with present-day graphene samples, tests of the Schwinger formula may be possible at least at a semi-quantitative level, though precision tests will likely require cleaner samples.

## Method

The authors derive the Schwinger pair production rate per unit area for massless fermions in 2+1 dimensions by adapting Schwinger's original calculation and by using a tunneling derivation in the time-independent gauge, where the electric field alters single-particle energy levels and filled levels in the elevated Dirac sea tunnel through an effective potential. They then propose a specific experimental configuration: a graphene sheet placed between two conducting plates held at constant voltage, with the transient current density from Schwinger pairs measured in the time window between the transient time scale and the filling time scale of the reservoirs.

## Key numbers

- Pair production rate per unit area for four species of massless fermions in a constant electric field: $\Gamma^{2+1} = f (q E)^{3/2} / (4 \pi^2 \hbar^{3/2} \tilde{c}^{1/2})$ with $f=4$, giving $\Gamma^{2+1} = (q E)^{3/2} / (\pi^2 \hbar^{3/2} \tilde{c}^{1/2})$
- The vacuum decay rate per unit area for massless fermions: $w^{2+1} = f \zeta(3/2) (q E)^{3/2} / (4 \pi^2 \hbar^{3/2} \tilde{c}^{1/2})$ where $\zeta(3/2) \approx 2.612$
- For the proposed experiment with $V_0 = 1$ V, $L = 1$ $\mu$m: $\sqrt{\hbar \tilde{c} / q E_0} = 25$ nm
- Transient time scale: $\tau_{\rm trans} = \sqrt{\hbar / (\tilde{c} q E)} \approx 2.6 \times 10^{-14}$ s for the proposed parameters
- Filling time scale: $\tau_{\rm fill} = \sqrt{q V_0 L / (\hbar \tilde{c}^3)} \, L_{\rm res} \approx 1.9 \times 10^{-9}$ s for $L_{\rm res} = 49$ $\mu$m
- Temperature condition: $T \ll 1800^\circ$K for the proposed parameters
- Voltage condition: $V_0 \ll \hbar \tilde{c} / (q a) \approx 2.5$ V
- Impurity density in reported samples: $n_{imp} \sim 2 \times 10^{11}$ cm$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical derivation of the Schwinger mechanism from 3+1 dimensions (Schwinger, 1951) to 2+1 dimensions and proposes graphene as a condensed matter analog computer to test the effect, which has never been observed experimentally due to the enormous fields required ($\sim 10^{16}$ V/cm) in vacuum. The paper builds on the recognition that charged quasiparticles in graphene have a linear dispersion relation (Semenov, 1984) and on prior work distinguishing the pair production rate $\Gamma$ from the vacuum decay rate $w$ (Cohen and McGady, 2007; Nikishov, 1970). The proposed experiment differs from standard conductivity measurements in graphene p-n junctions (Katsnelson et al., 2006) by measuring a transient current rather than steady-state conductance, and the authors argue the Schwinger current should dominate over ordinary conductivity by a factor of $\sqrt{L^2 q E_0 / (\hbar \tilde{c})}$.
