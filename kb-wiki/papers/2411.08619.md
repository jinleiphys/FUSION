# Correlation of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities and neutron-skin thickness in low-energy antiproton induced reactions

> Within the framework of Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics transport model, the neutron-skin thickness and its impact on the nuclear dynamics induced by low-energy antiprotons are investigated thoroughly. The correlation of the neutron-skin thickness and stiffness of symmetry energy is implemented into the transport model via the Fermi distributions of the proton and neutron density profiles. It is found that antiprotons are predominantly annihilated in the subsaturation density region (0.4$ρ_{0}$-0.8$ρ_{0}$). The isospin ratios of free neutrons to protons (n/p) and charged pion yields ($π^{-}$/$π^{+}$) in collisions of antiprotons on $^{48}\rm{Ca}$ and $^{208}\rm{Pb}$ are analyzed systematically for extracting the symmetry energy in the domain of subsaturation densities. The n/p ratio is sensitive to the stiffness of symmetry energy in the low-density region and a soft symmetry energy leads to the larger n/p ratio, in particular with decreasing the beam momentum. The $π^-/π^+$ ratio is also enhanced with the soft symmetry energy at kinetic energies below 150 MeV.

# Correlation of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities and neutron-skin thickness in low-energy antiproton induced reactions

## Key claim
Antiprotons annihilate predominantly in the subsaturation density region (0.4ρ₀–0.8ρ₀), and the isospin ratios of free neutrons to protons (n/p) and charged pion yields (π⁻/π⁺) in antiproton collisions on ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb are sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy, specifically, a soft symmetry energy (slope parameter L = 53 MeV) leads to larger n/p and π⁻/π⁺ ratios, with the effect being more pronounced at lower beam momenta and for ⁴⁸Ca due to weaker pion reabsorption.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate low-energy antiproton-induced reactions on ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb. The neutron-skin thickness was correlated with the symmetry energy slope parameter L by varying the diffusion coefficient of the neutron Fermi distribution while keeping the proton distribution fixed, with initial density profiles generated from Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SKM* parameter) calculations. The model included antiproton-nucleon annihilation channels, charge-exchange reactions, elastic and inelastic scattering, and pion-nucleon resonance formation via the Breit-Wigner formula.

## Key numbers
- Antiprotons annihilate predominantly in the subsaturation density region of 0.4ρ₀ to 0.8ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The symmetry energy slope parameters used are L = 53 MeV (soft), 82 MeV (linear), and 139 MeV (hard), corresponding to stiffness parameters γₛ = 0.5, 1, and 2, respectively.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, neutron-skin thickness values of 0.18 fm and 0.38 fm were selected; for ⁴⁸Ca, values of 0.08 fm and 0.16 fm were selected.
- The linear relationships between slope parameter L and neutron-skin thickness are L = 1075 × r_skin⁴⁸ − 33 MeV for Ca and L = 430 × r_skin²⁰⁸ − 24.4 MeV for Pb.
- The n/p ratio is sensitive to symmetry energy stiffness for kinetic energies above 50 MeV.
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio shows a peak at the Δ(1232) resonance energy of E_π = 0.19 GeV, and the soft symmetry energy enhances the ratio at kinetic energies below 150 MeV.
- Pion multiplicity reaches equilibrium at 150 fm/c; 3–5 pions are produced per antiproton-nucleon annihilation.

## Context
This work extends the LQMD transport model (Feng, 2014, 2016) to correlate neutron-skin thickness with symmetry energy in antiproton-induced reactions for the first time. It builds on prior measurements of neutron-skin thickness from antiprotonic atom X-ray cascades (Kl07), which reported Δr_np = 0.16 ± 0.02(stat) ± 0.04(syst) fm for ²⁰⁸Pb, and from the PREX-II (Ad21) and CREX (Ad22) experiments. The study contrasts with heavy-ion collision approaches (Zhang17, Yong21, Fe06, Gao13) that probe high-density symmetry energy via pion production, instead targeting the subsaturation density region accessible through low-energy antiproton annihilation. The results enable future experimental extraction of the low-density symmetry energy and neutron-skin thickness using antiproton beams.
