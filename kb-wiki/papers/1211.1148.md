# Evaluation of the Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term from CHAOS Data

> We have reanalyzed the $π^{\pm} p$ scattering data at low energy in the Coulomb-nuclear interference region as measured by the CHAOS group at TRIUMF with the aim to determine the pion-nucleon $σ$ term. The resulting value $σ=(44\pm 12)$ MeV, while in agreement with lattice QCD calculations and compatible with other recent analyses, is significantly lower than that from the GWU-TRIUMF analysis of 2002.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon sigma term derived from CHAOS data is σ = (44 ± 12) MeV, which is significantly lower than the GWU-TRIUMF analysis of 2002 but agrees with lattice QCD calculations and other recent analyses, though the authors note that systematic uncertainties of the CHAOS experiment prevent a precision determination.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed π⁺p and π⁻p differential cross sections measured by the CHAOS detector at TRIUMF at five energies between 19.9 and 43.3 MeV, focusing on the Coulomb-nuclear interference region at small angles. They extracted the real part of the forward C⁺ amplitude from the scattering data, then used forward dispersion relations to connect the threshold behavior of the amplitude to the sub-threshold coefficients needed for the sigma term. The analysis employed polynomial fits with a convergence test function method and incorporated constraints from analyticity and unitarity through dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- σπN = (44 ± 12) MeV, the final value of the pion-nucleon sigma term derived from CHAOS data
- Σ = (59 ± 12) MeV, the experimental sigma term at the Cheng-Dashen point before chiral corrections
- c₀ = (−0.140 ± 0.013) mπ⁻¹, the first coefficient in the expansion of Re C⁺(q²,0) at threshold
- c₁ = (2.146 ± 0.187) mπ⁻³, the second coefficient in the expansion of Re C⁺(q²,0)
- a₀₊⁺ = (−9.7 ± 0.9) × 10⁻³ mπ⁻¹, the isoscalar s-wave scattering length obtained from the analysis
- 2a₁₊⁺ + a₁₋⁺ = (199 ± 14) × 10⁻³ mπ⁻³, the combination of p-wave scattering lengths
- C⁺(mπ) = (−0.139 ± 0.019) mπ⁻¹, the average subtraction constant from forward dispersion relations
- Δ₂ = −1.381 mπ⁻¹, the dispersion integral connecting the amplitude at threshold to the center of the Mandelstam triangle
- Δ₁ = −0.364 mπ⁻³, the dispersion integral for the forward slope correction

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Denz et al. (2006), who measured π⁺p and π⁻p cross sections with the CHAOS detector but only qualitatively concluded that the sigma term was smaller than previously claimed. The result contradicts the larger sigma term of about 64 MeV from the GWU-TRIUMF analysis by Pavan et al. (2002), which used the full SAID database, but agrees with the canonical value of 49 MeV from Koch et al. (1982) based on pre-meson-factory data. The analysis enables improved constraints on the strange quark content of the nucleon and, as noted in the introduction, has implications for dark matter scattering cross sections that depend on the sigma term value.
