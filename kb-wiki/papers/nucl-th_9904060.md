# The c-term of the TM 3-body Force: to be or not to be

> In Faddeev calculations of ^3Hwe study the dependence of the binding on the three nucleon force. We adopt the 2 pi -exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force and investigate phenomenologically the dependence on the strength of the individual three-body force operators (a -, b -, c - and d -terms). While the a -term provides a tiny contribution the b - and d -terms provides a tiny contribution the b - and d -terms are important to gain the experimental binding energy. We find two solutions for the c -term, one around the value used in the Tucson-Melbourne model and a new one close to zero, which supports the recentrecommendation of chiral perturbation theory that the short-range c -term should be dropped.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the strength of the c-term in the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) three-nucleon force can be set to nearly zero (15% of its original value) while still reproducing the experimental triton binding energy, which supports the chiral perturbation theory recommendation to drop the short-range part of the c-term; however, this is a phenomenological finding based on fitting the triton binding energy alone, not a first-principles derivation.

## Method

The authors performed rigorous Faddeev calculations for the triton (³H) using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. They systematically varied the strength parameters (a, b, c, d) of the three-body force operators individually and in combination, multiplying each by a factor X between 0 and 1.5, and computed the resulting triton binding energy to find which parameter sets reproduce the experimental value of 8.48 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The experimental triton binding energy is 8.48 MeV; theoretical predictions with NN forces only lack about 500-800 keV.
- The original TM parameters are: a = 1.13 m_π⁻¹, b = -2.58 m_π⁻³, c = 1.00 m_π⁻³, d = -0.753 m_π⁻³, with cut-off Λ = 4.856 m_π (adjusted for CD-Bonn).
- The new solution for the c-term is 0.15 m_π⁻³, which is 15% of the original value of 1.00 m_π⁻³.
- The ratio b/d for the TM model is 3.43, while the Fujita-Miyazawa Δ-excitation mechanism gives b/d = 4.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon forces (Fujita and Miyazawa, 1957; Coon et al., 1981) by systematically testing the individual operator terms of the TM force, motivated by the chiral perturbation theory recommendation of Friar (1999) to drop the short-range c-term. The results enable testing of the parameter sets (b, c, d) that fit the triton binding energy against high-energy three-nucleon continuum data (100-300 MeV) from experiments at IUCF, RIKEN, and KVI, which may discriminate between different parameter choices or reveal the need for additional three-body force mechanisms beyond 2π-exchange.
