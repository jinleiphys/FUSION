# Spin-aligned neutron-proton pairs in $N=Z$ nuclei

> A study is carried out of the role of the aligned neutron-proton pair with angular momentum J=9 and isospin T=0 in the low-energy spectroscopy of the $N=Z$ nuclei $^{96}$Cd, $^{94}$Ag, and $^{92}$Pd. Shell-model wave functions resulting from realistic interactions are analyzed in terms of a variety of two-nucleon pairs corresponding to different choices of their coupled angular momentum $J$ and isospin $T$. The analysis is performed exactly for four holes ($^{96}$Cd) and carried further for six and eight holes ($^{94}$Ag and $^{92}$Pd) by means of a mapping to an appropriate version of the interacting boson model. The study allows the identification of the strengths and deficiencies of the aligned-pair approximation.

## Key claim

The paper examines Blomqvist's proposal that yrast states in \(N=Z\) nuclei near \(^{100}\text{Sn}\) can be interpreted in terms of aligned neutron-proton pairs with angular momentum \(J=9\) and isospin \(T=0\), but finds that this approximation works well for most yrast states except those with total angular momentum \(J\approx 8\), where the overlap with the aligned-pair configuration is conspicuously small.

## Method

Shell-model wave functions for four holes in the \(1g_{9/2}\) orbit (\({}^{96}\text{Cd}\)) were computed using three realistic two-body interactions (SLGT0, GF, and an interaction derived from the experimental spectrum of \({}^{90}\text{Nb}\)) and analyzed in terms of two-nucleon pairs with various coupled angular momenta and isospins. For six and eight holes (\({}^{94}\text{Ag}\) and \({}^{92}\text{Pd}\)), a direct shell-model analysis in the pair basis was avoided; instead, the fermion system was mapped onto an interacting boson model using both the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello (OAI) and democratic mapping methods, with the boson Hamiltonian determined from the two- and four-particle systems.

## Key numbers

* The two-body matrix element for the \((\lambda,T)=(9,0)\) pair is \(-1.504\) MeV for SLGT0, \(-1.752\) MeV for GF, and \(-1.376\) MeV for the Nb90 interaction (Table I).
* The fraction of the \(2p_{1/2}+1g_{9/2}\) shell-model eigenstate lying within the \(1g_{9/2}\) subspace for the \(0^+_1\) state is 96% for \({}^{96}\text{Cd}\) and 90% for \({}^{92}\text{Pd}\) (Table II).
* The particle-hole matrix element for the ground state of \({}^{90}\text{Nb}\) (\(\lambda=8\)) was determined from binding energies as \(\nu_8^{\text{ph}} = 0.078\) MeV (derived from \(E(^{90}\text{Zr}) = -783.794\) MeV, \(E(^{90}\text{Nb}) = -776.895\) MeV, \(E(^{91}\text{Nb}) = -788.942\) MeV, \(E(^{89}\text{Zr}) = -771.825\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends Blomqvist's proposal (Blomqvist, unpublished) for aligned neutron-proton pairs in \(N=Z\) nuclei and relates it to the older stretch scheme of Danos and Gillet (1967). The analysis uses realistic interactions from Serduke et al. (1976) and Gross and Frenkel (1976), and the mapping methods follow Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello (1978) and Skouras et al. (1990). The results enable a quantitative test of the aligned-pair approximation and identify its limitations, particularly for states near \(J=8\), guiding future shell-model studies of neutron-deficient nuclei in the \(A=86\) to 100 region.
