# Testing of various parametrizations of effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in elastic and inelastic scattering of polarized protons from $^{12}$C

> The parametrization schemes of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction proposed by Geramb and by Nakayama and Love are compared in their ability to reproduce the inelastic scattering observables of polarized protons from $^{12}$C with the excitation of the $1^+$ level with T=1. The off-shell behavior of the effective $t$ matrix is shown to be important. Density dependence of the effective interaction is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The Geramb (Paris-Hamburg) parametrization of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction reproduces inelastic scattering observables for the excitation of the \(1^+, T=1\) level in \(^{12}\)C better than the Nakayama-Love (NL) parametrization, because the Geramb scheme approximately accounts for the off-shell behavior of the \(g\) matrix while the NL scheme ignores it entirely.

## Method

The authors compared the Geramb and Nakayama-Love (NL) parametrizations of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction by calculating elastic and inelastic scattering observables for polarized protons on \(^{12}\)C at approximately 200 MeV. For inelastic scattering to the \(1^+, T=1\) level at \(E^*=15.11\) MeV, they used the Cohen-Kurath wave function and performed distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with the program DWBA-91, both with and without density dependence of the effective interaction. The elastic scattering calculations used a first-order microscopic optical potential derived from each \(g\) matrix.

## Key numbers

* Differential cross section, analyzing power, polarization, and polarization transfer coefficient \(D_{NN'}\) were calculated for the \(1^+, T=1\) level in \(^{12}\)C at a proton energy of approximately 200 MeV (data from Comfort at 200 MeV and Opper at 198 MeV).
* The excitation energy of the \(1^+, T=1\) level in \(^{12}\)C is \(E^*=15.11\) MeV.
* The Geramb interaction better reproduces the experimental analyzing power and polarization data than the NL interaction; the NL analyzing power shows a shift towards larger angles.
* For density-dependent calculations, the NL interaction describes the cross section almost as well as the Geramb interaction; the deterioration with increasing scattering angle is also seen in inelastic electron scattering where the Cohen-Kurath wave function does not properly describe the second maximum of the transverse form factor.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of effective nucleon-nucleon interactions for inelastic proton scattering, specifically testing the Geramb (Paris-Hamburg) parametrization from Geramb (year not given) against the Nakayama-Love parametrization from Nakayama (year not given). The authors hold the converse opinion to Kelly (year not given), who claimed the NL construction better reproduces the low-density behavior of the tensor component; instead, they argue that the similarity between the NL interaction and the free NN interaction (Franey and Love parametrization) is a disadvantage. The results enable further refinement of effective interactions for describing polarization observables in inelastic scattering, particularly for isovector spin-dependent transitions.
