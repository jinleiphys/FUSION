# Shell structure of superheavy nuclei in self-consistent mean-field models

> We study the extrapolation of nuclear shell structure to the region of superheavy nuclei in self-consistent mean-field models -- the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach and the relativistic mean-field model -- using a large number of parameterizations. Results obtained with the Folded-Yukawa potential are shown for comparison. We focus on differences in the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction and the effective mass between the models and their influence on single-particle spectra. While all relativistic models give a reasonable description of spin-orbit splittings, all non-relativistic models show a wrong trend with mass number. The spin-orbit splitting of heavy nuclei might be overestimated by 40%-80%. Spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei are found at (Z=114,N=184), (Z=120,N=172) or (Z=126,N=184) depending on the parameterization. The Z=114 proton shell closure, which is related to a large spin-orbit splitting of proton 2f states, is predicted only by forces which by far overestimate the proton spin-orbit splitting in Pb208. The Z=120 and N=172 shell closures predicted by the relativistic models and some Skyrme interactions are found to be related to a central depression of the nuclear density distribution. This effect cannot appear in macroscopic-microscopic models which have a limited freedom for the density distribution only. In summary, our findings give a strong argument for (Z=120,N=172) to be the next spherical doubly-magic superheavy nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the next spherical doubly-magic superheavy nucleus is most likely (Z=120, N=172), but this prediction depends on the specific mean-field parameterization used; the Z=114 shell closure is only predicted by forces that overestimate the proton spin-orbit splitting in Pb-208 by 40%-80%.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using multiple parameterizations of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, comparing results with the Folded-Yukawa potential used in macroscopic-microscopic models. They analyzed the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction and effective mass across models, and tested predictions against experimental spin-orbit splittings in O-16, Sn-132, and Pb-208.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit splitting of heavy nuclei might be overestimated by 40%-80% in non-relativistic models
- Spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei are found at (Z=114, N=184), (Z=120, N=172), or (Z=126, N=184) depending on the parameterization
- Effective masses for Skyrme forces range from 0.577 (SkI3) to 1.000 (SkP), while RMF forces have effective masses between 0.583 and 0.600 at k=0
- The incompressibility K for Skyrme forces ranges from 202 MeV (SkP) to 258 MeV (SkI3), and for RMF forces from 172 MeV (NL-Z2) to 272 MeV (NL3)
- Equilibrium density for Skyrme forces is approximately 0.16 fm^-3, for RMF forces approximately 0.15 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spherical shell closures in superheavy nuclei by Rutz et al. and Buervenich et al., and contradicts the older macroscopic-microscopic predictions by Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and subsequent work by Patyk and Sobiczewski (1989) that favored (Z=114, N=184). The finding that (Z=120, N=172) is related to a central depression of the nuclear density distribution cannot be captured by macroscopic-microscopic models or semi-classical approaches like the extended Thomas-Fermi-Strutinski integral method. The results enable future experimental searches for spherical superheavy nuclei to focus on the N=172 neutron shell closure rather than N=184.
