# Measuring light-by-light scattering at the LHC and FCC

> Elastic light-by-light scattering, $γγ\toγγ$, can be measured in electromagnetic interactions of lead (Pb) ions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and Future Circular Collider (FCC), using the large (quasi)real photon fluxes available in ultraperipheral collisions. The $γγ\toγγ$ cross sections for diphoton masses m$_{γγ}>$ 5 GeV in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at LHC ($\sqrt{\rm s_{_{NN}}}$ = 5.5, 8.8, 14 TeV) and FCC ($\sqrt{\rm s_{_{NN}}}$ = 39, 63, 100 TeV) center-of-mass energies are presented. The measurement has controllable backgrounds in PbPb collisions, and one expects about 70 and 2500 signal events per year at the LHC and FCC respectively, after typical detector acceptance and reconstruction efficiency selections.

## Key claim

Elastic light-by-light scattering can be observed in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at the LHC and FCC, with about 70 signal events per year at the LHC and 2500 at the FCC after detector acceptance and reconstruction efficiency selections, provided that the dominant backgrounds from central exclusive production and QED dilepton events are controlled through kinematic cuts.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for elastic photon-photon scattering in ultraperipheral proton-proton, proton-lead, and lead-lead collisions using the equivalent photon approximation, convolving the photon fluxes of the colliding hadrons with the leading-order expression for the elementary gamma-gamma to gamma-gamma cross section including all quark and lepton loops. The MadGraph v.5 Monte Carlo framework is used for the convolution, with proton photon spectra derived from the elastic form factor and impact-parameter dependent ion photon spectra including a correction to ensure exclusive collisions without hadronic overlap. The expected event yields are obtained by multiplying the cross sections by nominal integrated luminosities per year and applying geometric detector acceptance and photon reconstruction efficiency factors.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV): exclusive gamma-gamma cross section for diphoton masses above 5 GeV is 370 +/- 70 nb, yielding about 70 signal events per year after cuts
- For PbPb collisions at FCC (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV): exclusive gamma-gamma cross section for diphoton masses above 5 GeV is 1.85 +/- 0.37 microbarn, yielding about 2500 signal events per year after cuts
- For pp collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 14 TeV): exclusive gamma-gamma cross section for diphoton masses above 5 GeV is 105 +/- 10 fb, yielding about 12 signal events per year after cuts
- For pp collisions at FCC (sqrt(s_NN) = 100 TeV): exclusive gamma-gamma cross section for diphoton masses above 5 GeV is 240 +/- 24 fb, yielding about 50 signal events per year after cuts
- The overall significance of the measurement in PbPb is about 6 sigma at the LHC and about 35 sigma at FCC, considering 20% theoretical uncertainty on LbyL and 50% on CEP yields
- The QED background from gamma-gamma to e+e- in PbPb at LHC has a cross section of 5.4 mb for dielectron masses above 5 GeV, reduced to about 20% of the visible LbyL cross section after requiring both electrons to be misidentified as photons with probability 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by d'Enterria (2013) for measuring light-by-light scattering at the LHC to the Future Circular Collider energies, showing that the cross sections increase by a factor of 2-5 from LHC to FCC and the event yields by about a factor of 35. The measurement would be the first direct observation of elastic photon-photon scattering in the laboratory, a process that has only been indirectly tested through the anomalous magnetic moments of the electron and muon. The results enable searches for physics beyond the Standard Model through new heavy charged particles contributing to the virtual loops, such as supersymmetric particles, monopoles, axions, unparticles, low-scale gravity effects, and non-commutative interactions, particularly at the higher diphoton masses accessible at the FCC.
