# Global trends of the electric dipole polarizability from shell-model calculations

> Shell-model calculations of the electric dipole (E1) polarizability have been performed for the ground state of selected p- and sd-shell nuclei, substantially advancing previous knowledge. Our results are slightly larger compared with the somewhat more scattered photo-absorption cross-section data, albeit agreeing with ab initio calculations at shell closures and presenting a smooth trend that follows the leptodermus approximation provided by the finite-range droplet model (FRDM). The total E1 strengths also show an increasing trend proportional to the mass number which follows from the classical oscillator strength (TRK) sum rule for the E1 operator. The enhancement of the energy-weighted sum over E1 excitations with respect to the TRK sum rule arises from the use of experimental single-particle energies and the residual particle-hole interaction.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations of the electric dipole polarizability for selected p- and sd-shell nuclei produce values that are slightly larger than those derived from photo-absorption cross-section data, but agree with ab initio calculations at shell closures and follow a smooth trend described by the leptodermus approximation of the finite-range droplet model (FRDM).

## Method

The authors performed 1ℏω shell-model calculations using the OXBASH code with the Warburton and Brown (WBP) and Florida State University (FSU) Hamiltonians within the spsdpf model space, calculating all E1 matrix elements connecting ground states to intermediate states (up to 1000 for even-even nuclei) following the inverse energy-weighted sum rule. For N=Z self-conjugate nuclei, only ΔT=1 isovector transitions were considered, while both ΔT=0 and ΔT=1 transitions were included for other nuclei; spurious center-of-mass states were removed using the Gloeckner-Lawson method.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of κ values for ⁹Be and ¹²C by Orce (2012) and Raju (2018) using no-core shell-model (NCSM) with CD-Bonn and chiral effective field theory forces, and agrees with coupled-cluster calculations by Miorelli (2016) using NNLO_sat for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca. The results enable systematic comparison of the electric dipole polarizability across the p and sd shells, showing that the smooth trend follows the leptodermus approximation of the FRDM symmetry energy coefficients, while deviations in light nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be) are attributed to cluster configurations and virtual breakup into the continuum.
