# Neutron stars within relativistic central variational method

> The properties of neutron stars are investigated within the relativistic central variational method by using a realistic nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction. The strong repulsion of realistic $NN$ interactions at short distances is treated by a Jastrow central correlation function, whose form is completely determined through minimization of the total energy of the nuclear many-body system. The relativistic Hartree-Fock wave functions are chosen as the trial wave function. In this framework, the equation of state of the neutron star matter in $β$ equilibrium is obtained self-consistently. We further determine the properties of neutron stars via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using Bonn A, B, and C potentials. The maximum masses of neutron stars with these realistic potentials are around $2.18 M_\odot$ and their corresponding radii are around $11$ km. These results are in accordance with the calculations of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with the same potentials. Furthermore, we also find that the splitting of proton-neutron effective masses will be reversed at high density in the neutron star matter, which are caused by the contribution of short-range correlation on kinetic energy.

## Key claim
The relativistic central variational (RCV) method, using only a central Jastrow correlation function without tensor correlations, reproduces the equation of state of neutron star matter and the resulting neutron star properties (maximum masses around 2.18 solar masses, radii around 11 km) obtained by the more computationally expensive relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory, but only for neutron-rich matter where tensor force effects are suppressed.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) trial wave function and multiply it by a Jastrow central correlation function whose form is determined variationally by minimizing the total energy of the nuclear many-body system. They use the Bonn A, B, and C one-boson-exchange potentials, which are realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions fitted to scattering data. The equation of state of neutron star matter in beta equilibrium (including neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) is obtained self-consistently, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation is then solved to determine neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.184, 2.181, and 2.179 solar masses for Bonn A, Bonn B, and Bonn C potentials, respectively
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: 10.99, 11.08, and 11.22 km for Bonn A, Bonn B, and Bonn C, respectively
- Central density at maximum mass: 1.078 fm^-3 for all three Bonn potentials
- The central correlation on kinetic energy contributes approximately half of the total binding energy per nucleon for neutron star matter
- Direct URCA processes occur at densities between 0.25 and 0.35 fm^-3 for all Bonn potentials
- The splitting of proton-neutron effective masses reverses at a density above 0.4 fm^-3

## Context
This work extends the relativistic central variational method previously developed by Hu et al. (2011) for pure neutron matter to the full beta-equilibrated neutron star matter including leptons. The results are compared with the non-relativistic variational method of Akmal et al. (1998) using the AV18 potential and with the RBHF calculations of Krastev and Sammarruca (2006) using the same Bonn potentials. The method enables a more economical calculation of neutron star properties compared to these other ab initio approaches, while also allowing self-consistent treatment of beta equilibrium conditions that were previously handled with approximations. The authors note that the reversal of proton-neutron effective mass splitting at high density may explain conflicting predictions between different treatments in RBHF theory by Brockmann and Machleidt versus Dalen et al.
