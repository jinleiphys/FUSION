# Double parton scattering in pA collisions at the LHC revisited

> We consider the production of $W$-boson plus dijet, $W$-boson plus b-jets and same sign $WW$ via double parton scattering (DPS) in $pA$ collisions at the LHC and evaluate the corresponding cross sections. The impact of a novel DPS contribution pertinent to $pA$ collisions is quantified. Exploiting the experimental capability of performing measurements differential in the impact parameter in $pA$ collisions, we discuss a method to single out this novel DPS contribution. The method allows the subtraction of the leading twist (LT) single parton scattering background and it gives access in a very clean way to double parton distribution functions in the proton. We calculate leading twist and DPS cross sections and study the dependence of the observables on the cuts in the jets phase space. In the $Wjj$ channel the observation of DPS is possible with data already accumulated in $pA$ runs and the situation will greatly improve for the next high luminosity runs. For the $Wb\bar{b}$ final state, the statistics within the current data is too low, but there is possibility to observe DPS in this channel in future runs, albeit with much reduced sensitivity than in $Wjj$ final state. Finally the DPS observation in the same sign $WW$ channel will require either significant increase of integrated luminosity beyond that foreseen in next $pA$ runs or improved methods for $W$ reconstruction, along with its charge, in hadronic decay channels.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the novel DPS2 contribution in pA collisions, which scales quadratically with the nuclear thickness function T(B), can be isolated from the leading twist SPS background and DPS1 contribution by exploiting the experimental capability to perform measurements differential in impact parameter B, but only for certain final states: the Wjj channel has sufficient statistics with already accumulated data, while the Wbb and same-sign WW channels require higher luminosity or improved reconstruction methods.

## Method
The authors calculate DPS cross sections for W+jets, W+bb, and same-sign WW production in pA collisions at sqrt(s_pN)=8.16 TeV using a factorized mean-field approximation for double parton distribution functions, with the DPS2 contribution evaluated from the product of single parton densities and nuclear thickness functions. They compute leading twist SPS backgrounds using ALPGEN at leading order, and propose a method based on the ratio R_W of W+jet events to inclusive W events as a function of nuclear thickness T_A(B) to isolate the DPS2 contribution, which produces a linear increase in this ratio while SPS and DPS1 contributions would give a constant.

## Key numbers
- DPS2 cross section for Wjj in pA collisions: 49 nb for pT_j>20 GeV, 22 nb for pT_j>25 GeV, 11 nb for pT_j>30 GeV (charged-summed W cross sections including muon and electron decay channels)
- DPS1 cross section for Wjj: 19 +/- 6 nb for pT_j>20 GeV, 8 +/- 3 nb for pT_j>25 GeV, 4 +/- 2 nb for pT_j>30 GeV
- SPS background cross section for Wjj: 81 nb for pT_j>20 GeV, 57 nb for pT_j>25 GeV, 41 nb for pT_j>30 GeV
- DPS2 cross section for Wbb: 196 pb for pT_b>20 GeV, 92 pb for pT_b>25 GeV, 48 pb for pT_b>30 GeV
- DPS1 cross section for Wbb: 74 +/- 25 pb for pT_b>20 GeV, 35 +/- 12 pb for pT_b>25 GeV, 18 +/- 6 pb for pT_b>30 GeV
- SPS background cross section for Wbb: 234 pb for pT_b>20 GeV, 158 pb for pT_b>25 GeV, 114 pb for pT_b>30 GeV
- Average sigma_eff used for Wjj and Wbb calculations: 18 +/- 6 mb, combining ATLAS (15 +/- 3 stat +5-3 syst mb at 7 TeV) and CMS (20.7 +/- 0.8 stat +/- 6.6 syst mb at 7 TeV) measurements
- Sigma_eff for ssWW: 12.7 +5.0 -2.9 mb from CMS at 13 TeV
- Integrated luminosity assumed for current pA runs: 0.1 pb^-1
- Centre-of-mass energy per nucleon: sqrt(s_pN)=8.16 TeV
- Boost between proton-nucleon CM and lab frame: Delta y=0.465

## Context
This work extends the method proposed by Alvioli (2019) for studying DPS2 in two-dijet processes in pA collisions to electroweak boson final states, specifically Wjj, Wbb, and same-sign WW. The DPS2 mechanism was first predicted theoretically by Strikman (2001) and involves two partons from the incoming proton interacting with partons from two different nucleons in the target nucleus. The paper builds on experimental DPS measurements in pp collisions by ATLAS and CMS for W+dijet and by CMS for same-sign WW production, and on the theoretical framework for DPS in pA collisions developed by BSW. The results enable future experimental searches for DPS2 in pA runs at the LHC, particularly in the Wjj channel where existing data may already be sufficient for observation.
