# Proton-Neutron Pairing in N=Z Nuclei within the Quark-Meson-Coupling Energy Density Functional

> We investigate the impact of isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations on the ground-state properties of even-even N=Z nuclei with mass numbers between A=16 and A=120. Nuclear mean fields are generated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) energy density functional, while pairing correlations are treated within the quartet condensation model (QCM). Ground-state energies are obtained from axially deformed, self-consistent QMC+QCM calculations employing a zero-range pairing interaction with a density-dependent term derived consistently within the QMC framework. We show that proton-neutron pairing provides a significant contribution to the binding energies of N=Z nuclei, leading to improved agreement with experimental data.

# Proton-Neutron Pairing in N=Z Nuclei within the Quark-Meson-Coupling Energy Density Functional

## Key claim

Proton-neutron pairing correlations, treated via the quartet condensation model (QCM), provide a significant contribution to the binding energies of even-even N=Z nuclei from A=16 to A=120 when combined with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) energy density functional, leading to improved agreement with experimental data; however, the isoscalar (T=0) pairing channel does not significantly change the binding energies for most nuclei in the sd-shell, and the pairing strength parameters must be adjusted separately for different mass regions due to the model-space dependence of the zero-range pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent axially deformed calculations combining the QMC energy density functional (QMCpi-III version) with the quartet condensation model (QCM), which treats both isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing correlations while exactly conserving particle number and isospin. The mean field was generated using the QMC functional with parameters fitted to doubly magic and semi-magic nuclei within a QMC+BCS framework, while pairing correlations were treated using a zero-range pairing interaction with a density-dependent term derived consistently within the QMC approach. The QCM calculations were performed with n_q=3 quartets (six protons and six neutrons outside a core) scattering among ten single-particle states, with the isovector pairing strength fixed by fitting to odd-even mass differences of N>Z nuclei with Z=8, 20, 50, and the isoscalar pairing strength set to w=1.6 times the isovector strength based on HFB+QRPA calculations of Gamow-Teller states.

## Key numbers

- The latest QMC model (QMCpi-III) predicts nuclear binding energies with a root-mean-square deviation of 1.74 MeV, compared to 3.11 MeV for the Skyrme SV-min functional, despite using only five adjustable parameters versus fourteen for SV-min.
- For sd-shell nuclei (16 < A < 40), QMC+QCM calculations yield binding-energy residuals below 100 keV for the first three nuclei and about 700 keV for the last two, compared to approximately 2 MeV underestimation in QMC calculations without proton-neutron pairing.
- In 20Ne, the total BCS pairing energy for nn and pp channels is about 3.8 MeV; including isovector pn pairing increases the total pairing energy to about 5.5 MeV, with all T=1 channels contributing equally.
- The isoscalar pn pairing energy in 20Ne is approximately 1.8 MeV, but the total pairing energy increases by only about 600 keV when isoscalar pairing is included, due to a reduction of the isovector pairing energy by about 1.2 MeV.
- Scaling factors for the isovector pairing interaction strength (relative to the QMC-derived value s=1) are s=1.3 for oxygen isotopes, s=1.5 for calcium isotopes, and s=1.8 for tin isotopes.
- The QMC model parameters used are: G_sigma = 9.62, G_omega = 5.21, G_rho = 4.71, M_sigma = 503 MeV, and lambda_3 = 0.05.
- The scalar polarizability d = 0.18 fm for a bag radius of 1 fm.
- The nuclear-matter saturation density in the QMC model is rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme+QCM calculations (Skyrme_qcm, 2024) that demonstrated proton-neutron pairing significantly improves agreement with experimental binding energies for N=Z nuclei, by applying the same QCM treatment to the QMC energy density functional framework. The QMC model differs from conventional Skyrme (SV-min, UNEDF1) and Gogny functionals by treating nucleons as clusters of confined quarks interacting through meson fields, which generates density-dependent modifications to nucleon properties and automatically incorporates spin-orbit and tensor interactions without additional parameters. The results contradict earlier HFB calculations (Goodman, 2001) that predicted isoscalar pairing does not coexist with isovector pairing in ground states of light and medium-mass N=Z nuclei, though the present work finds that isoscalar pairing does not significantly alter binding energies for most sd-shell nuclei. The study enables future investigations of heavy N=Z nuclei above A=100 where isoscalar pairing may become dominant, and motivates refitting the QMC parameters within the QCM framework for more consistent treatment of doubly magic nuclei.
