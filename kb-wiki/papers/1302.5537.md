# Simulating fermion production in 1+1 dimensional QED

> We investigate fermion--anti-fermion production in 1+1 dimensional QED using real-time lattice techniques. In this non-perturbative approach the full quantum dynamics of fermions is included while the gauge field dynamics can be accurately represented by classical-statistical simulations for relevant field strengths. We compute the non-equilibrium time evolution of gauge invariant correlation functions implementing 'low-cost' Wilson fermions. Introducing a lattice generalization of the Dirac-Heisenberg-Wigner function, we recover the Schwinger formula in 1+1 dimensions in the limit of a static background field. We discuss the decay of the field due to the backreaction of the created fermion--anti-fermion pairs and apply the approach to strongly inhomogeneous gauge fields. The latter allows us to discuss the striking phenomenon of a linear rising potential building up between produced fermion bunches after the initial electric pulse ceased.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that real-time lattice techniques with low-cost Wilson fermions can accurately simulate non-perturbative fermion pair production in 1+1 dimensional QED, including the full backreaction of created pairs on the gauge field, but the classical-statistical approximation for gauge fields is only valid for sufficiently high occupation numbers or field strengths and breaks down once typical gauge field occupancies become of order unity.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dirac and Maxwell equations on a 1+1 dimensional space-time lattice using a compact U(1) gauge formulation with temporal axial gauge. They employ a stochastic ensemble of classical spinors (male and female) to represent the fermion dynamics, which avoids the computational cost of mode function expansions that scale with phase space volume. A spatial Wilson term suppresses fermion doublers while temporal doublers are avoided by choosing the temporal lattice spacing much smaller than the spatial lattice spacing and initializing only the physical mode. The authors also construct a lattice generalization of the Dirac-Heisenberg-Wigner function to extract gauge-invariant correlation functions and particle number pseudo-distributions.

## Key numbers

- The lattice Wigner function is defined on Wigner lattices with twice as many grid points as the original spatial lattice: $\Lambda_\mathcal{W}$ has $2N_s$ points and $\tilde{\Lambda}_\mathcal{W}$ has $2N_s$ momentum points
- The temporal lattice spacing $a_t$ must satisfy $a_t \ll a_s$ to avoid temporal doublers
- The low-cost fermion algorithm scales as $N_s^d N_\mathrm{sto}$ in $d$ dimensions, compared to $N_s^{2d}$ for mode function approaches
- The lattice mass is modified by the spatial Wilson term: $\tilde{m} = m + \frac{2}{a_s}\sin^2\left(\frac{\pi q}{N_s}\right)$
- The lattice momentum is defined as $\tilde{q} = \frac{1}{a_s}\sin\left(\frac{2\pi q}{N_s}\right)$ with $\tilde{\omega} = \sqrt{\tilde{m}^2 + \tilde{q}^2}$

## Context

This work extends real-time lattice gauge theory techniques (Aarts 1998) to fermion pair production in QED, building on low-cost fermion algorithms (Borsanyi 2008) previously applied to scalar inflaton decay and baryogenesis (Berges 2010, Saffin 2011). The approach goes beyond external field approximations (Brezin 1970, Popov 1972) and Vlasov-type kinetic descriptions (Kluger 1992, Schmidt 1998) by including the full backreaction of created pairs on the gauge field. The method enables first-time computation of the self-consistent electric field that builds up between produced fermion and anti-fermion bunches after the initial pulse, analogous to a capacitor with a linear rising potential.
