# Pion screening mass at finite chemical potential

> We present a method to compute the responses of meson screening masses to the chemical potential by Taylor expanding the correlator using lattice QCD simulation. We start by comparing the free theory lattice results with the analytical expression. Then, using symmetry arguments, we obtain an expression for the correlator in a series of the chemical potential at finite temperature. Using this, we obtain the lowest order correction to the screening mass at a finite chemical potential for temperatures around 2.5 GeV. Our lattice analysis is limited to isoscalar chemical potential for the pseudoscalar channel. The calculations were performed using (2+1)-flavors of the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ/tree) action, with the ratio of the strange quark mass to the light quark mass $m_s/m_\ell=20$ corresponding to pion masses of 160 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method to compute the second-order Taylor coefficient of the pseudoscalar meson screening mass with respect to isoscalar chemical potential, but the results are limited to high temperatures (around 2.5 GeV) and the extracted correction is small and consistent with zero within error at the higher temperature studied.

## Method

The authors Taylor-expand the screening correlator in powers of the isoscalar chemical potential over temperature, using lattice QCD with (2+1)-flavors of Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ/tree) action on \(64^3 \times 8\) lattices. They compute the second and fourth Taylor coefficients of the correlator, form ratios \(\Gamma(z) = C^{(2)}/C^{(0)}\) and \(\Sigma(z) = C^{(4)}/C^{(0)}\), and fit these to quadratic and quartic polynomials in \(zT\) respectively, from which the screening mass corrections \(M_I'\) and \(M_R''\) are extracted. The method is validated against free theory on an \(80^3 \times 8\) lattice before application at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

* Free theory lattice test: on an \(80^3 \times 8\) lattice with \(am_\ell = 0.00014\), the fit to \(\Gamma_\text{free}\) over \(2.0 \leq zT \leq 4.0\) yields \(-\alpha_2 = 3.995(4)\), compared to the exact value of 4.
* Free theory fit to \(\Sigma_\text{free}\) over \(2.0 \leq zT \leq 4.0\) yields \(\beta_4 = 15.99(7)\), compared to the exact value of 16.
* Finite temperature analysis uses \(N_\tau = 8\) lattices at \(T = 2.24\) GeV and \(T = 2.90\) GeV, with light quark mass \(m_\ell = m_s/20\) corresponding to a pion mass of 160 MeV.
* The second-order correction \(M''(0)\) is found to be small but non-zero within error at \(T = 2.24\) GeV, while it is consistent with zero within error at \(T = 2.90\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Taylor expansion method previously applied to screening masses by Pushkina (2004) and the QCD-TARO collaboration (2001), and to temporal correlators by Nikolaev (2020). It builds on the exact free theory result for the screening correlator at finite chemical potential from Vepsalainen (2007). The method enables future extraction of screening mass corrections at finite baryon density using larger aspect ratio lattices, which could inform the search for the conjectured QCD critical point in the \(T\)-\(\mu_B\) phase diagram.
