# Nuclear Potential of Final State Nucleons and Nucleons Plus Pions in Lepton Nucleus Scattering

> Within the impulse approximation, the modeling of the energy of final state leptons in electron and neutrino quasielastic and pion production processes on nuclear targets in the region of the $Δ$ resonance depends on several parameters. These parameters include the removal energy of the initial state nucleon from the nucleus $ε^{P,N}$, the potentials of electrons, nucleons and pions in the Coulomb field of the nucleus $|V_{eff}|$, and the kinetic energy dependent nuclear potential for final state nucleons ($U^{QE}_{opt}$) and "nucleon plus pion" final states in the region of the $Δ$ resonance which we refer to as $U^Δ_{opt}$. We extract these parameters from electron scattering data. Previous studies have shown that real part of the optical potential for a nucleon bound in $_{6}^{12}C$ at zero kinetic energy $U^{P,N}_{opt}(T=0)\approx$ 44 MeV is larger than that for the $Δ$(1232) resonance $U^Δ_{opt}(T=0)\approx$ 30 MeV. We find the reverse at higher kinetic energies. For example at T=100 MeV we find a nucleon potential $U^{P,N}_{opt}(T=100 MeV)$=20$\pm$5 MeV and $U^Δ_{opt}(T=100 MeV)$= 30$\pm$5 MeV. The two results are consistent for two reasons. First, theoretically the kinetic energy dependence of the $Δ$ potential is flatter than that of the nucleon. Secondly, in our analysis the extracted $U^Δ_{opt}$ values are the nuclear potential for {\it"nucleon plus pion"} final states in the region of the $Δ$ resonances and therefore includes contributions from both resonance and non resonance pion production processes. For Monte Carlo generators that only include the effects of Fermi motion and nuclear potentials, the relevant parameter is the effective nuclear potential for the "nucleon plus pion" final state.

## Key claim

The authors extract the kinetic-energy-dependent nuclear potential for final-state nucleons ($U^{QE}_{opt}$) and for "nucleon plus pion" final states in the $\Delta$ resonance region ($U^{\Delta}_{opt}$) from electron scattering data on carbon and oxygen, and find that while the nucleon potential is larger at zero kinetic energy (44 MeV vs. 30 MeV), the reverse holds at higher kinetic energies (e.g., at T=100 MeV the nucleon potential is 20$\pm$5 MeV and the $\Delta$ potential is 30$\pm$5 MeV). The authors argue that this is consistent because the $\Delta$ potential has a flatter kinetic energy dependence and because $U^{\Delta}_{opt}$ includes contributions from both resonance and non-resonance pion production.

## Method

The authors fit relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) models to 33 inclusive electron scattering quasielastic (QE) differential cross sections on $^{12}_{6}C$ and four on $^{16}_{8}O$ to extract $U^{QE}_{opt}$ for final-state nucleons, and to 15 spectra extending into the $\Delta$(1232) resonance region to extract $U^{\Delta}_{opt}$ for "nucleon plus pion" final states. The removal energies $\epsilon^{P,N}$ are taken from spectral functions measured in $ee'P$ experiments, and the effective Coulomb potential $|V_{eff}|$ is extracted from comparisons of electron and positron scattering. The extracted potentials are compared with theoretical calculations by Cooper (1993, 2009), Udias, Ankowski, and Oset and Salcedo (1987).

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}_{6}C$, the effective Coulomb potential $|V_{eff}| = 3.1 \pm 0.25$ MeV.
- For $^{12}_{6}C$, the average removal energy for protons $\epsilon^{P} = 27.5 \pm 3$ MeV and for neutrons $\epsilon^{N} = 30.1 \pm 3$ MeV.
- At zero kinetic energy, the nucleon optical potential $U^{P,N}_{opt}(T=0) \approx 44 \pm 5$ MeV and the $\Delta$ potential $U^{\Delta}_{opt}(T=0) \approx 30 \pm 5$ MeV.
- At T=100 MeV, the nucleon potential $U^{P,N}_{opt}(T=100\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 20 \pm 5$ MeV and the $\Delta$ potential $U^{\Delta}_{opt}(T=100\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 30 \pm 5$ MeV.
- For carbon, the extracted potentials are described by linear functions: $U^{QE}_{opt}(T) = -0.030 + 0.092T$ and $U^{\Delta}_{opt}(T) = -0.050 + 0.161T$, where U and T are in GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses in Bodek and Cai (Eur. Phys. J. C79, 2019 and Eur. Phys. J. C80, 2020) and addresses a comment by Mosel (arXiv:2007.10260, 2020) regarding consistency with zero-energy optical potentials. The extracted potentials are directly relevant for modeling final-state interactions in Monte Carlo generators for neutrino oscillation experiments, where the effective nuclear potential for the "nucleon plus pion" final state is the appropriate parameter when only Fermi motion and nuclear potentials are included.
