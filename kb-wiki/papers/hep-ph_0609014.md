# Exotic hadrons in s-wave chiral dynamics

> We study s-wave scattering of a hadron and a Nambu-Goldstone boson induced by the model-independent low energy interaction in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit. Establishing the general structure of the low energy interaction based on group theoretical arguments, we find that the interaction in the exotic channels are in most cases repulsive, and that for possible attractive channels the interaction strengths are weak and uniquely given independent of channel. Solving the scattering problem with this interaction, we show that the attraction in the exotic channels is not strong enough to generate a bound state from the physically known target hadrons. We also find that there are no attractive interaction in the exotic channels in large Nc limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, the model-independent Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction for s-wave scattering of a Nambu-Goldstone boson from a target hadron is repulsive in most exotic channels; where attraction does occur, it is universally weak (C_exotic = 1) and insufficient to bind any experimentally known target hadron, though bound states could appear for target masses above approximately 14 GeV.

## Method

The authors use group theory to decompose the direct product of the target hadron's SU(3) representation [p,q] with the adjoint representation of the NG boson, then compute the coupling strength C_alpha,T of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction for each resulting channel using quadratic Casimir operators. They define exoticness E and identify channels with Delta E = 1 as exotic. For the attractive exotic channel (target in symmetric representation [p,0] and channel [p-1,2]), they solve the s-wave scattering problem using the N/D method with elastic unitarity and a subtraction condition G(M_T)=0, then determine the critical coupling C_crit needed to produce a bound state as a function of target mass.

## Key numbers

* The universal attractive coupling strength in exotic channels is C_exotic = 1 (dimensionless).
* The critical coupling strength C_crit to bind a NG boson of average mass m = 368 MeV (average of pi, K, eta masses) with decay constant f = 93 MeV is larger than 1 for target masses M_T below 6 GeV.
* To produce a bound state with C_exotic = 1, the target hadron mass must exceed 14 GeV for m = 368 MeV and f = 93 MeV.
* For a heavier NG boson mass m = 500 MeV, an exotic bound state appears at target mass M_T approximately 2500 MeV, as cited from Lutz (2003).
* In the large N_c limit, the attractive exotic channel for N_c = 3 becomes repulsive, eliminating any possibility of bound states.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach used to dynamically generate known baryon resonances such as Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1670) (studied by the Octet collaboration and others) to the question of exotic hadrons. It contradicts the possibility that exotic states like the pentaquark Theta+ could arise as simple s-wave NG-boson-hadron bound states in chiral dynamics. The analysis enables future work to focus on other formation mechanisms for exotic states, such as genuine quark states, and provides a systematic group-theoretic classification of which channels can be attractive.
