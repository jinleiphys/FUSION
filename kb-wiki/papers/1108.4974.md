# Azimuthal asymmetries in single polarized proton-proton Drell-Yan processes

> We study the azimuthal asymmetries in proton-proton Drell-Yan processes with one incident proton being transversely or longitudinally polarized. We consider particularly the asymmetries contributed by the leading-twist chiral-odd quark distributions. We analyze the asymmetries with $\sin(2ϕ+ϕ_S)$ and $\sin(2ϕ-ϕ_S)$ modulations in transverse single polarized $p^\uparrow p$ Drell-Yan and $\sin2ϕ$ asymmetries in longitudinal single polarized $p^\rightarrow p$ Drell-Yan at RHIC, J-PARC, E906 (Fermi Lab) and NICA (JINR). We show that the measurements of the asymmetries in those facilities can provide valuable information of the chiral-odd structure of the nucleon both in the valence and sea regions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that azimuthal asymmetries with \(\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)\), \(\sin(2\phi+\phi_S)\), and \(\sin2\phi\) modulations in single polarized proton-proton Drell-Yan processes can be measured at RHIC, J-PARC, E906, and NICA, and that these measurements will provide valuable information on the chiral-odd structure of the nucleon, but the predictions depend on model assumptions for the less-known chiral-odd transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions, particularly for sea quarks, where only positivity bounds are used to estimate the range of possible asymmetries.

## Method

The authors calculate the structure functions for the azimuthal asymmetries \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)}\), \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi+\phi_S)}\), and \(A_{LU}^{\sin2\phi}\) in the TMD factorization framework, using the Boer-Mulders function \(h_1^\perp\) extracted from unpolarized Drell-Yan data (Lu, 2009) and the \(T\)-even chiral-odd distributions \(h_1\), \(h_{1T}^\perp\), and \(h_{1L}^\perp\) from the light-cone quark-diquark model. For sea quarks, the contributions of \(h_1\), \(h_{1T}^\perp\), and \(h_{1L}^\perp\) are constrained by positivity bounds, and the unpolarized distributions \(f_1^q\) are taken from MSTW2008 LO with a Gaussian transverse momentum dependence.

## Key numbers

*   At RHIC collider (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(4<Q<9\) GeV, \(0<q_T<1\) GeV, \(-1<y<2\)), the asymmetry \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)}\) is positive in the forward rapidity region, with magnitudes that increase with rapidity; the asymmetry \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi+\phi_S)}\) and \(A_{LU}^{\sin2\phi}\) tend to be negative.
*   At RHIC fixed-target (\(\sqrt{s}=22\) GeV, \(4.5<Q<8\) GeV, \(0<q_T<1\) GeV, \(0.2<x_1<0.6\)), the magnitudes of \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)}\) and \(A_{LU}^{\sin2\phi}\) are larger than at the collider mode.
*   At J-PARC (\(E_p=50\) GeV, \(\sqrt{s}\simeq 10\) GeV, \(4<Q<5\) GeV, \(0<q_T<1\) GeV, \(0.5<x_1<0.9\)), the asymmetry \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)}\) is positive and \(A_{LU}^{\sin2\phi}\) is negative in all allowed rapidity regions, with larger magnitudes than at RHIC.
*   At E906 (\(E_p=120\) GeV, \(\sqrt{s}=15\) GeV, \(4<Q<5\) GeV, \(0<q_T<1\) GeV, \(0.1<x_1<0.9\)), the asymmetry \(A_{TU}^{\sin(2\phi-\phi_S)}\) is positive and \(A_{LU}^{\sin2\phi}\) is negative in the forward rapidity region.
*   The parameter \(\omega\) for the Boer-Mulders function, with a range \(0.48 < \omega < 2.1\) from Lu (2009), affects the magnitude of the asymmetries; larger \(\omega\) decreases the asymmetries in the forward rapidity region.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological studies of single spin asymmetries in Drell-Yan processes, focusing on chiral-odd TMD distributions beyond the Sivers effect, which has been studied in SIDIS by the HERMES and COMPASS collaborations. The predictions are relevant for planned experiments at RHIC, J-PARC, E906, and NICA, and the results can be compared with future extractions of transversity (Anselmino, 2008) and the Boer-Mulders function (Lu, 2009). The analysis enables tests of the chiral-odd structure of the nucleon in both valence and sea regions, and the sign change of the Sivers function between SIDIS and Drell-Yan is noted as a separate crucial prediction of QCD dynamics.
