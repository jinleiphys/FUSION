# Transverse response functions in the $Δ$-resonance region

> We calculate transverse response functions for quasi-elastic electron scattering at high momentum transfers in a relativistic Hartree approximation in configuration space. We treat the excitation of the $Δ$ resonance using its free mass and width. Good agreement with experiment is found in the dip region.

## Key claim

The authors find "surprisingly good" agreement with experimental transverse response data in the dip region using a simplified treatment of the gamma-N-Delta vertex (reducing the spin-3/2 Delta to an effective spin-1/2 particle) and the free Delta mass and width, while treating nuclear structure carefully via a relativistic Hartree approximation in configuration space; they caution that the agreement "may be fortuitous" given the severity of the vertex approximation.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse response function S_T for quasi-elastic electron scattering by evaluating the polarization tensor in a relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) using a non-spectral Green function in configuration space. Nucleon excitation is treated with the full RHA self-consistent field, while Delta excitation is included by adding an imaginary term i*Gamma/2 to the energy in the Dirac propagator (using the free Delta mass M_Delta=1232 MeV and width Gamma=110 MeV) and employing a simplified gamma-N-Delta vertex that reduces the spin-3/2 Delta to an effective spin-1/2 particle with a constant factor alpha_0 set to 1.0. The nucleon and Delta contributions are summed incoherently.

## Key numbers

- Delta resonance mass: M_Delta = 1232 MeV/c^2, decay width: Gamma = 110 MeV (free values used throughout)
- For C-12 at |q| = 550 MeV/c, the calculated transverse response S_T in the dip region falls by about a factor of 4 from 300 MeV to 200 MeV energy loss
- The effective coupling alpha(|q|) for the simplified vertex varies slowly, from approximately 0.60 to 0.75 for the C-12 dip region at |q| = 550 MeV/c
- Calculations are performed at momentum transfers |q| = 400 MeV/c (for C-12) and |q| = 550 MeV/c (for C-12, Ca-40, and Fe-56 treated as Ni-56)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approach of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (1990) for nucleon-only response to include Delta excitation, contrasting with the momentum-space Delta-hole calculations of Koch and Ohtsuka (1985) and the relativistic local-density-approximation calculation of Wehrberger, Bedau, and Beck (1989). The authors' configuration-space treatment produces a slower falloff of the Delta line shape below threshold compared to these prior works, which they argue is the key to reproducing experimental data in the dip region; they note that multinucleon effects such as meson exchange currents calculated by Dekker, Brussaard, and Tjon (1991) are not precluded but should be built on realistic single-nucleon and single-Delta treatments.
