# New Physics in Neutron Beta Decay

> Limits on parameters describing physics beyond the Standard Model are presented. The most general Lorentz invariant effective Hamiltonian at the quark-lepton level involving vector, scalar and tensor operators has been used. The fits have been done using the most precise and up to date experimental data for correlation coefficients measured in free neutron beta decay as well as the Fierz term measured in superallowed Fermi decays.

## Key claim

Limits on parameters describing physics beyond the Standard Model are presented, but the authors note that free neutron decay data alone cannot provide the strongest constraints on exotic couplings; the results depend strongly on the still-unknown value of the Fierz term \(b\) and on the specific selection of experimental data for the correlation coefficient \(A\).

## Method

The authors performed a least-squares fit of the most general Lorentz-invariant effective Hamiltonian at the quark–lepton level (including vector, scalar, and tensor operators) to the most precise available experimental data for the correlation coefficients \(a\), \(A\), and \(B\) from free neutron beta decay, supplemented by the Fierz term \(b_F\) from superallowed Fermi decays. The analysis treated the nucleon form factors \(g_V\), \(g_A\), \(g_S\), and \(g_T\) as free real parameters, allowing separation of new-physics contributions from nucleon structure effects in the vector part of the interaction. Fits were performed for one, two, and three parameters simultaneously, and the \(\chi^2\) function accounted for the energy-dependent corrections arising from the Fierz interference terms.

## Key numbers

*   One-parameter fit for \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\) (assuming only Standard Model couplings): \(\lambda = 1.2755 \pm 0.0011\) at 68.27% C.L., \(\pm 0.0018\) at 90% C.L., \(\pm 0.0022\) at 95.45% C.L.
*   Fierz term from superallowed Fermi decays used as additional constraint: \(b_F = -0.0022 \pm 0.0026\).
*   For the fit \(\chi^2(\lambda, s_L, T_{LL})\) without \(b_F\), the Fierz term \(b\) at the \(\chi^2\) minimum is \(0.0821\), with a 95.45% C.L. interval \(-0.28 < b < 0.58\); with \(b_F\) included, the minimum shifts to \(b = 0.1019\) with interval \(-0.045 < b < 0.20\).
*   For the fit \(\chi^2(\lambda, s_L)\) without \(b_F\), the Fierz term \(b\) at the minimum is \(-0.0276\) with interval \(-0.052 < b < 0.0096\); with \(b_F\) included, the minimum shifts to \(b = 0.000352\) with interval \(-0.00075 < b < 0.0015\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Holeczek et al., 2011) and the approach of Herczeg (2001) by performing a self-consistent least-squares fit at the quark–lepton level rather than the nucleon–lepton level, using updated experimental data for the correlation coefficients \(a\), \(A\), and \(B\) from free neutron decay. The results are compared with the PDG average for \(\lambda\) and with the joint analyses of Severijns et al. and Konrad et al., which included pion and nuclear beta decays and gave stronger constraints on exotic couplings. The analysis enables future application of the derived bounds to the description of neutrino states in beta-beam oscillation experiments.
