# Magnetic form factors of the octet baryons from lattice QCD and chiral extrapolation

> We present a 2+1--flavor lattice QCD calculation of the electromagnetic Dirac and Pauli form factors of the octet baryons. The magnetic Sachs form factor is extrapolated at six fixed values of $Q^2$ to the physical pseudoscalar masses and infinite volume using a formulation based on heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with finite-range regularization. We properly account for omitted disconnected quark contractions using a partially-quenched effective field theory formalism. The results compare well with the experimental form factors of the nucleon and the magnetic moments of the octet baryons.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the magnetic Sachs form factors for all octet baryons, extrapolated to physical quark masses and infinite volume using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with finite-range regularization; the extrapolation explicitly accounts for the omission of disconnected quark contractions in the lattice simulations by employing a "connected chiral perturbation theory" formalism, and the results agree well with experimental nucleon form factors and octet baryon magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1-flavor lattice QCD simulations with non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson fermions on a single volume of 32^3 × 64 at a lattice spacing of 0.074(2) fm, using six ensembles with pion masses ranging from 310 to 465 MeV. They extracted the Dirac and Pauli form factors from three-point correlation functions at six fixed values of momentum transfer Q^2 up to approximately 1.3 GeV^2, then performed independent chiral extrapolations at each Q^2 bin using a connected chiral perturbation theory framework that treats valence and sea quarks separately and includes finite-volume corrections from leading one-loop chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.074(2) fm
- Lightest pion mass in simulations: m_π ≈ 310 MeV
- Finite-volume corrections to the nucleon form factor: approximately 2–4% at the lowest Q^2 value (≈ 0.26 GeV^2) and 0.03–0.06% at the largest Q^2 value (≈ 1.35 GeV^2)
- The highest Q^2 bin shows a variation of 1.29–1.37 GeV^2 across different baryon masses
- Regulator mass used in finite-range regularization: Λ = 0.8 ± 0.1 GeV
- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: f = 0.0871 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Alexandrou 2007, Gockeler 2003, Hagler 2007, Lin 2008, Collins 2011, Bratt 2010) to include all octet baryons, which had received limited attention (Boinepalli 2006, Lin 2008, Wang 2008, Leinweber 1990). The connected chiral perturbation theory formalism follows the partially-quenched approach developed by Bernard (1993), Sharpe (2001), and Leinweber (2002), and the finite-range regularization scheme builds on work by Leinweber (2003) and Young (2002). The results enable precise comparison with experimental form factors and magnetic moments, and provide insight into the environmental sensitivity of quark distributions within baryons, such as how the u-quark distribution differs between the proton and the Σ^+ due to spectator quark mass differences.
