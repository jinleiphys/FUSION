# Constraining freeze-out with yields and fluctuations

> We show that the simultaneous measurement of yields and fluctuations in heavy ion collisions is capable of falsifying and constraining the statistical hadronization model. We show how such a measurement can test for chemical non-equilibrium, and distinguish between a high temperature chemically equilibrated freeze-out from a supercooled freeze-out with an over-saturated phase space. We further explain how this measurement can be used to obtain a model-independent estimate of the effect of post-freezeout hadronic interactions.

# Constraining freeze-out with yields and fluctuations

## Key claim
Simultaneous measurement of particle yields and event-by-event fluctuations can distinguish between a high-temperature chemically equilibrated freeze-out (T ~ 170 MeV) and a supercooled non-equilibrium freeze-out (T ~ 140 MeV with oversaturated phase space occupancies γ_q ~ 1.5, γ_s ~ 2), but only if the same temperature and γ_q extracted from yields and fluctuations agree within a narrow correlated band; otherwise the statistical hadronization model itself can be falsified.

## Method
The authors used the Grand Canonical ensemble implemented in the open-source SHARE code to compute both first cumulants (average yields) and second cumulants (event-by-event fluctuations) of hadron multiplicities as functions of temperature T, chemical potentials, and phase-space occupancy parameters γ_q and γ_s. They constructed correlation diagrams plotting yield ratios (such as Ξ/φ) against fluctuation observables (such as σ_{π⁺/π⁻} or the charge fluctuation measure v(Q) = ⟨ΔQ²⟩/⟨N_ch⟩) to show that different freeze-out scenarios occupy distinct narrow bands in these observable spaces.

## Key numbers
- Supercooled non-equilibrium freeze-out temperature: ~140 MeV, with γ_q ~ 1.6 and γ_s ~ 2.5 (from entropy matching between QGP and hadron gas)
- Equilibrium freeze-out temperature range from fits: 155 MeV (Gammaq_energy) to 177 MeV (Bdm)
- Statistical significance difference between equilibrium and non-equilibrium fits at SPS and RHIC: ~20%
- RHIC experiments observe mid-rapidity slice comprising roughly 1/8 of total multiplicity, justifying Grand Canonical treatment
- The π⁺/π⁻ fluctuation observable σ_{π⁺/π⁻} is correlated with Ξ/φ yield ratio such that the combination depends primarily on freeze-out temperature alone, constraining the SHM parameter space to a narrow band

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) tradition originating from Fermi (1950), Pomeranchuk (1951), and Landau (1953), and the full resonance spectrum approach of Hagedorn (1965). It directly addresses the ambiguity between equilibrium fits (Barannikova, Bdm) and non-equilibrium fits (Gammaq_energy) that both describe SPS and RHIC yield data within ~20% statistical significance. The method enables model-independent testing of the difference between chemical and thermal freeze-out, and can probe the extent of hadronic reinteraction after freeze-out, a question relevant to the HBT puzzle. The authors build on their earlier work (Prlfluct, 2005) and a parallel technical paper (Qm2005nukleonika).
