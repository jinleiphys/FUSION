# Critical phenomena from the two-particle irreducible 1/N expansion

> The 1/N expansion of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action is employed to compute universal properties at the second-order phase transition of an O(N)-symmetric N-vector model directly in three dimensions. At next-to-leading order the approach cures the spurious small-N divergence of the standard (1PI) 1/N expansion for a computation of the critical anomalous dimension eta(N), and leads to improved estimates already for moderate values of N.

## Key claim

The 2PI 1/N expansion at next-to-leading order cures the spurious small-N divergence of the standard 1PI 1/N expansion for the critical anomalous dimension eta(N), but the NLO approximation still cannot compete with more elaborate estimates from alternative methods for small N (e.g., the result for N=4 is about 35-40% too high compared to alternative estimates).

## Method

The authors employ the 1/N expansion of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action for an O(N)-symmetric scalar N-vector model directly in three dimensions, computing the critical anomalous dimension eta(N) from self-consistent equations for the full propagator at the critical point. They derive a self-consistency equation for eta by inserting the critical propagator form into the gap equation at zero external source, then solve analytically using hypergeometric functions and verify numerically for selective values of N. The calculation is performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the 1/N expansion, which includes chain-sum resummations of bubble diagrams.

## Key numbers

- For N=4, the 2PI NLO result is eta = 0.061, which is about 35-40% higher than alternative estimates
- The anomalous dimension approaches eta -> 0.5^- as N -> 0^+ in the 2PI expansion, curing the spurious divergence of the 1PI expansion
- The result from the 2PI 1/N expansion at NLO agrees with Bray's Self-Consistent Screening Approximation (SCSA) from 1974
- The limit N -> 0 describes the universality class for critical swelling of long polymer chains

## Context

This work extends the 2PI 1/N expansion approach previously developed for nonequilibrium dynamics (Berges 2001, Aarts 2002) to equilibrium critical phenomena, showing it can reliably describe thermal equilibrium properties at second-order phase transitions. The method improves upon the standard 1PI 1/N expansion (Ma 1973), which exhibits a spurious divergence as N approaches zero, and agrees with Bray's Self-Consistent Screening Approximation (1974) for the zero-source case. The approach enables systematic improvement through inclusion of NNLO corrections and provides a method that can handle both equilibrium and nonequilibrium critical phenomena, with applications to QCD critical point physics and Bose-Einstein condensation.
