# Low-Mass Dileptons from in-Medium Hadronic Interactions

> Medium Effects in low-mass dilepton production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are investigated using hadronic models. The rescattering of pions and rho mesons within a hot and dense hadron gas leads to substantial modifications in the $π^+π^- \to e^+ e^-$ annihilation process, which are shown to be important for understanding the recently observed dilepton enhancement at the CERN-SpS. Possible implications for the nature of the chiral phase transition are outlined.

## Key claim

Medium modifications of the rho meson propagator due to hadronic rescattering in a hot and dense fireball can explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed at the CERN-SpS, but the question of whether this "melting" of the rho resonance signals partial restoration of chiral symmetry remains open.

## Method

The electromagnetic current correlator is calculated using the vector dominance model (VDM), where the rho meson propagator is dressed with medium-modified selfenergies. Medium effects include renormalization of the pion cloud through p-wave nucleon-nucleonhole and delta-nucleonhole excitations with short-range correlations, and direct rho-nucleon scattering via s-channel baryon resonance formation using eight resonances (N(939), Delta(1232), N(1440), N(1520), Delta(1620), Delta(1700), N(1720), Delta(1905)). The model is constrained by photoabsorption data on protons and nuclei, and then applied to dilepton production in Pb+Au collisions at 158 GeV/u using an expanding fireball model with temperature and density evolution.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Free rho meson lifetime: tau_rho = 1.3 fm/c
- Typical hadronic fireball lifetime: tau_FB = 10-20 fm/c
- Hadronic fireball temperature range: T = 120-200 MeV
- The low-mass dilepton enhancement is most pronounced for invariant masses M = 0.2-0.6 GeV and pair transverse momenta q_t <= 0.7 GeV
- Photoabsorption calculations for finite nuclei use an average density of rho_N = 0.8 rho_0

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic model calculations of the electromagnetic current correlator by Rapp, Chanfray, and Wambach (1996) and by Friman and Pirner (1997), and is motivated by the unexplained low-mass dilepton excess observed by the CERES/NA45 collaboration at the CERN-SpS. The approach contrasts with the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (1991), which directly links vector meson masses to the reduction of the chiral condensate, and with chiral reduction formalism approaches by Steele, Yamagishi, and Zahed (1997). The results enable future investigation of a_1(1260) meson in-medium properties as a test of chiral symmetry restoration, though the author notes this will be experimentally difficult.
