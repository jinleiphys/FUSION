# Neutron-neutron correlation in the halo dissociation of light exotic nuclei

> We present model results for the two-halo-neutron correlation functions, $C_{nn}$, for the dissociation process of light exotic nuclei modelled as two neutrons and a core. A minimum is predicted for $C_{nn}$ as a function of the relative momentum of the two neutrons, $p_{nn}$, due to the coherence of the neutrons in the halo and final state interaction. Studying the systems $^{14}$Be, $^{11}$Li and $^{6}$He within this model, we show that the numerical asymptotic limit, $C_{nn}\to 1$, occurs only for $p_{nn}\gtrsim$ 400 MeV$/c$, while such limit is reached for much lower values of $p_{nn}$ in an independent particle model as the one used in the analysis of recent experimental data. Our model is consistent with data once the experimental correlation function is appropriately normalized.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the two-neutron correlation function \(C_{nn}\) for halo dissociation of Borromean nuclei exhibits a minimum as a function of the relative neutron momentum \(p_{nn}\), and that the asymptotic limit \(C_{nn}\to 1\) is only reached for \(p_{nn}\gtrsim 400\) MeV/\(c\) in their coherent-source model, contrary to the much lower momenta assumed in the independent-particle model used to normalize experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the halo nuclei \(^{14}\)Be, \(^{11}\)Li, and \(^{6}\)He as three-body systems (two neutrons plus a core) using a renormalized zero-range interaction in a Faddeev framework. The correlation function \(C_{nn}\) is computed from the breakup amplitude including the neutron-neutron final-state interaction (FSI) via a distorted-wave approach, with the halo treated as a coherent source of neutrons. The model is parameterized by the two-neutron separation energy \(S(2n)\), the neutron-core virtual state energy \(E_{nA}\), and the neutron-neutron virtual state energy (0.143 MeV).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{14}\)Be, the asymptotic limit \(C_{nn}\to 1\) occurs for \(p_{nn}\gtrsim 400\) MeV/\(c\); the model reproduces data when multiplied by a factor of 1.425 (i.e., \(C_{nn}\approx 0.70\;C_{nn}^{exp}\)).
* For \(^{11}\)Li, the asymptotic limit occurs for \(p_{nn}\gtrsim 250\) MeV/\(c\); the model requires a normalization factor of 2.5 to fit data (i.e., \(C_{nn}\approx 0.40\;C_{nn}^{exp}\)).
* For \(^{6}\)He, the asymptotic limit occurs for \(p_{nn}\gtrsim 500\) MeV/\(c\); the model requires a normalization factor of 1.48 (dashed curve) or 1.12 (solid curve) to fit data.
* The minimum value of \(C_{nn}\) is approximately 0.35, arising from interference effects due to the inclusion of FSI.
* The root-mean-square neutron-neutron radius for the halo is \(r^{rms}_{nn}=5.9\pm1.2\) fm (from Marqués et al., 2000) and \(r^{rms}_{nn}=8.5\) fm (from the authors' model for \(^{11}\)Li).

## Context

This work extends the three-body continuum calculation of \(C_{nn}\) for \(^{6}\)He by Danilin et al. (2004) to the systems \(^{14}\)Be and \(^{11}\)Li, and directly challenges the independent-particle model of Lednicky and Lyuboshits (1982) used in the experimental analyses of Marqués et al. (2000, 2001) and Petrascu et al. (2004). The authors argue that the halo neutrons must be treated as a coherent source, and their results call for a renormalization of the experimental correlation functions. The prediction of a minimum in \(C_{nn}\) at intermediate \(p_{nn}\) motivates future experiments at higher relative momenta to confirm this feature.
