# Recent Developments in the Study of Deconfinement in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Deconfinement refers to the creation of a state of quasi-free quarks and gluons in strongly interacting matter. Model predictions and experimental evidence for the onset of deconfinement in nucleus-nucleus collisions were discussed in our first review on this subject. These results motivated further experimental and theoretical studies. This review addresses two subjects. First, a summary of the past, present and future experimental programmes related to discovery and study of properties of the onset of deconfinement are %briefly presented. Second, recent progress is reviewed on analysis methods and preliminary experimental results for new strongly intensive fluctuation measures are discussed, which are relevant for current and future studies of the onset of deconfinement and searches for the critical point of strongly interacting matter

## Key claim

This review argues that the onset of deconfinement in nucleus-nucleus collisions is indicated by rapid changes in the energy dependence of hadron production properties (such as strangeness-to-pion ratio and inverse slope parameters) occurring at low SPS energies, but the authors acknowledge that unambiguous evidence for the quark-gluon plasma state remains "nearly impossible to obtain" because quantitative predictions of proposed QGP signals from the theory of strong interactions are difficult.

## Method

The authors review experimental programmes at the CERN SPS (NA49, NA61/SHINE), BNL RHIC (BES programme), and LHC, comparing hadron production properties in central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions across collision energies from AGS to LHC. They examine energy dependence of relative strangeness production (E_S ratio), inverse slope parameters of transverse mass spectra, elliptic flow scaling (v_2), nuclear modification factors (R_CP), and higher moments of net-proton multiplicity distributions, comparing results to models with and without deconfinement phase transitions (SMES, HGM, RQMD, UrQMD, HSD).

## Key numbers

* The onset of deconfinement is predicted to occur at beam energies between 30A GeV and 60A GeV, with confined phase below 30A GeV, mixed phase from 30A GeV to 60A GeV, and deconfined phase above 60A GeV.
* NA49 recorded central Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV at the CERN SPS.
* The LHC data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (approximately 360 times higher than the onset energy) show smooth evolution from top SPS (17.2 GeV) to LHC energies.
* RHIC BES phase I recorded Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19, 27, and 39 GeV, complemented by data at 62, 130, and 200 GeV.
* The NA61/SHINE programme plans a Pb+Pb energy scan at 13A, 19A, 30A, 40A, 75A, and 150A GeV with event statistics about 10 times that of NA49 data.
* The CBM experiment at FAIR SIS-100 will deliver heavy ion beams with energies up to 11A GeV, with planned SIS300 extension to 35A GeV after 2025.
* The MPD at NICA will operate at maximum energy of about sqrt(s_NN) approximately 11 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the authors' first review on the onset of deconfinement (Gazdzicki, Gorenstein, Seyboth, 2007) by summarizing subsequent experimental programmes and new analysis methods for event-by-event fluctuations. The work builds on the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) developed by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein, which predicted first-order phase transition signatures, and contrasts with models that do not assume deconfinement (HGM, RQMD, UrQMD, HSD) which fail to describe the data. The review enables future searches for the critical point of strongly interacting matter and precision studies of deconfinement properties at upcoming facilities (NICA, FAIR) and ongoing programmes (NA61/SHINE, BES phase II).
