# Message-Passing Neural Quantum States for the Homogeneous Electron Gas

> We introduce a message-passing-neural-network-based wave function Ansatz to simulate extended, strongly interacting fermions in continuous space. Symmetry constraints, such as continuous translation symmetries, can be readily embedded in the model. We demonstrate its accuracy by simulating the ground state of the homogeneous electron gas in three spatial dimensions at different densities and system sizes. With orders of magnitude fewer parameters than state-of-the-art neural-network wave functions, we demonstrate better or comparable ground-state energies. Reducing the parameter complexity allows scaling to $N=128$ electrons, previously inaccessible to neural-network wave functions in continuous space, enabling future work on finite-size extrapolations to the thermodynamic limit. We also show the Ansatz's capability of quantitatively representing different phases of matter.

# Message-Passing Neural Quantum States for the Homogeneous Electron Gas

## Key claim

The message-passing neural quantum state (MP-NQS) architecture achieves ground-state energies for the homogeneous electron gas that are better than or comparable to state-of-the-art neural-network wave functions while using one to two orders of magnitude fewer variational parameters, but this performance is demonstrated only for specific system sizes and densities up to N=128 electrons, and the method does not universally outperform all competing approaches at every density.

## Method

The authors construct a variational wave function as a single Slater determinant of single-particle orbitals evaluated at many-body backflow coordinates, where the backflow transformation is parameterized by a permutation-equivariant message-passing neural network operating on an all-to-all connected graph of particle positions. The network uses a particle attention mechanism to compute messages between nodes, with initial node features set to a learnable embedding vector (independent of particle index) and edge features encoding translation-invariant particle distances, their norms, and spin products. The Ansatz is optimized using Variational Monte Carlo with Stochastic Reconfiguration, and the homogeneous electron gas Hamiltonian with periodic boundary conditions is treated using Ewald summation for the Coulomb interactions.

## Key numbers

- The MP-NQS uses approximately 19,000 variational parameters, independent of system size, compared to orders of magnitude more parameters in state-of-the-art neural-network wave functions.
- For N=14 electrons, the MP-NQS energy difference from full configuration interaction (FCI) results is less than 1.5 mHa per particle across all densities studied.
- For N=54 electrons at high densities, the MP-NQS obtains significantly better ground-state energies than fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo (FN-DMC) and backflow diffusion Monte Carlo (BF-DMC), unlike the FermiNet-based architecture of Li et al. (2022) which does not improve upon BF-DMC energies.
- The MP-NQS scales to N=128 electrons, previously inaccessible to neural-network wave functions in continuous space, with previous limits at N=27 and N=54 electrons.
- The liquid-crystal phase transition of the homogeneous electron gas is reproduced around r_s = 100, matching previous predictions by Ceperley (1980), Drummond et al. (2004), and Azadi et al. (2022).

## Context

This work extends neural-network quantum state methods from small molecular and few-particle systems to larger periodic electronic systems, specifically the homogeneous electron gas, which is a prototypical model for electronic structure in solids. It improves upon prior neural-network wave function approaches such as FermiNet (Cassella et al., 2022) and WAPNet (Wilson et al., 2022), which were limited to system sizes of a few tens of electrons, and upon the LiNet architecture (Li et al., 2022) which did not improve over backflow diffusion Monte Carlo results. The ability to simulate N=128 electrons enables finite-size extrapolations to the thermodynamic limit, and the reproduction of the Wigner crystallization transition around r_s=100 demonstrates the Ansatz's capability to represent different phases of matter quantitatively better than previous neural quantum state studies of the homogeneous electron gas.
