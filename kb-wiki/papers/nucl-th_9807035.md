# Bethe-Salpeter Approach for Meson-Meson Scattering in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation restores exact elastic unitarity in the s- channel by summing up an infinite set of chiral loops. We use this equation to show how a chiral expansion can be undertaken by successive approximations to the potential which should be iterated. Renormalizability of the amplitudes in a broad sense can be achieved by allowing for an infinite set of counter-terms as it is the case in ordinary Chiral Perturbation Theory. Within this framework we calculate the $ππ$ scattering amplitudes both for s- and p-waves at lowest order in the proposed expansion where a successful description of the low-lying resonances ($σ$ and $ρ$) and threshold parameters is obtained. We also extract the SU(2) low energy parameters ${\bar l}_{1,2,3,4}$ from our amplitudes.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation, when solved at lowest order in a chiral expansion using only the O(p²) potential and free pion propagator, produces s-channel unitarized ππ scattering amplitudes that describe the σ and ρ resonances and threshold parameters, but only after introducing three (s-wave) and two (p-wave) renormalized subtraction constants that are fitted to data; crossing symmetry is violated at this order and must be restored perturbatively via a constraint relating the five parameters.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two identical pseudoscalar mesons is solved in the ladder approximation, using the O(p²) chiral potential as the two-particle irreducible kernel and the free meson propagator. For the isoscalar s-wave and isovector p-wave channels, polynomial ansätze for the off-shell amplitude reduce the integral equation to a linear algebraic system, yielding closed-form expressions for the on-shell inverse amplitudes that contain divergent loop integrals. Renormalization is performed by absorbing the quartic (s-wave) and quadratic (p-wave) divergences into finite subtraction constants I^R_n(4m²), which are fitted to experimental phase shifts; crossing symmetry at O(1/f⁴) is then imposed as a constraint linking the five parameters, reducing them to four independent ones that map onto the standard ChPT low-energy constants l̅₁,₂,₃,₄.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m = 134.98 MeV and pion decay constant f = 92.4 MeV used throughout.
- For the fit to data of Froggatt-Petersen (1977) and Estabrooks-Martin (1979): I^R_0(4m²) = -0.0289 ± 0.0005 (s-wave), I^R_0(4m²) = -0.0980 ± 0.0017 (p-wave), I^R_2(4m²) = 0.072 ± 0.005 (p-wave), I^R_4(4m²) = -0.003 ± 0.003 (s-wave), in units of (2m)^n.
- Threshold parameters from that fit: m a₀₀ = 0.204 ± 0.008, m³ b₀₀ = 0.267 ± 0.006; m³ a₁₁ = 0.0356 ± 0.0006, m⁵ b₁₁ = 0.0062 ± 0.0002.
- Extracted low-energy constants: l̅₁ = -0.90 ± 0.09, l̅₂ = 6.03 ± 0.08, l̅₃ = 2 ± 7, l̅₄ = 3.9 ± 0.4.
- For the I=2, J=0 channel (predicted from crossing): m a₂₀ = -0.0390 ± 0.0018, m³ b₂₀ = -0.0701 ± 0.0010, with χ²/(num. data) = 0.9 up to 1450 MeV.
- s-wave scattering length ratio: a₀₀ / a₀₀^tree = 1 + 0.27 (one loop) + 0.07 (two loops).

## Context

This work extends the program of unitarizing chiral perturbation theory for meson-meson scattering, following earlier approaches such as the Inverse Amplitude Method (Dobado-Pelaez, 1993), Pade resummation (Dobado-Herrero-Truong, 1990), and the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger approach (Oller-Oset, 1997). Unlike the latter, which introduces a three-momentum cutoff and breaks Lorentz invariance, the Bethe-Salpeter approach maintains manifest covariance and avoids cutoffs in the physical amplitudes. The method enables a systematic chiral expansion of the potential to be iterated, with crossing symmetry restored perturbatively; the authors note that including O(p⁴) corrections to the potential and propagator (currently underway) would recover t- and u-channel unitarity logs and allow extension to higher partial waves and two-loop comparisons with Bijnens et al. (1995).
