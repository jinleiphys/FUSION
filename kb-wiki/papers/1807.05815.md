# Astrophysical S-factor for the deep sub-barrier fusion reactions of light nuclei

> The cross sections for the deep sub-barrier fusion reaction of light nuclei are calculated within the theoretical framework of the selective resonant tunneling model. In this model, assumption of a complex square-well nuclear potential is invoked to describe the absorption inside the nuclear well. The theoretical estimates for these cross sections agree well with the experimentally measured values. The features of the astrophysical S-factor are derived in terms of this model. Present formalism appears to be particularly useful for the low energy resonant reactions between two charged nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the selective resonant tunneling model, using a complex square-well nuclear potential, accurately reproduces experimental cross sections for deep sub-barrier fusion of light nuclei, but the model's applicability is explicitly limited to light nuclei; the authors note that reactions involving medium and heavy nucleus-nucleus systems require a completely different approach.

## Method
The authors apply the selective resonant tunneling model, which treats fusion as a single-step process where tunneling probability depends on decay lifetime. They use a complex square-well nuclear potential with two adjustable parameters (real part \(V_r\) and imaginary part \(V_i\)) to describe absorption inside the nuclear well, and the radius parameter \(r_0\) is slightly varied between systems. The cross sections and dimensionless astrophysical S-functions are calculated using the derived expressions for s-wave phase shifts, with \(V_r\) and \(V_i\) adjusted to match the resonance peak position and magnitude.

## Key numbers
- For p+D fusion: \(V_r = -55.0\) MeV, \(V_i = -0.0235\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.177\) fm.
- For p+\(^6\)Li fusion: \(V_r = -44.25\) MeV, \(V_i = -7.5\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.180\) fm.
- For p+\(^7\)Li fusion: \(V_r = -85.0\) MeV, \(V_i = -395.0\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.330\) fm.
- For D+D fusion (from prior work): \(V_r = -48.52\) MeV, \(V_i = -263.27\) keV, \(r_0 = 2.778\) fm.
- For D+T fusion (from prior work): \(V_r = -40.69\) MeV, \(V_i = -109.18\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.887\) fm.
- For D+\(^3\)He fusion (from prior work): \(V_r = -11.859\) MeV, \(V_i = -259.02\) keV, \(r_0 = 3.331\) fm.
- The magnitude of \(V_i\) for p+\(^6\)Li is about twenty times larger than that for p+\(^7\)Li.

## Context
This work extends the selective resonant tunneling model previously applied to D+D, D+T, and D+\(^3\)He fusion (Li et al., 2004, 2008) to the astrophysically important reactions p+D, p+\(^6\)Li, and p+\(^7\)Li. The model contrasts with the compound nucleus model (Feshbach, 1992), which assumes independent formation and decay steps, and with the one-dimensional Gamow tunneling model (Gamow, 1938) that neglects reflections inside the nuclear well. The results enable calculation of astrophysical S-factors for these light-nuclei fusion reactions at deep sub-barrier energies relevant to stellar nucleosynthesis.
