# Competition between allowed and first-forbidden $β$ decay in $r$-process waiting-point nuclei within a relativistic beyond-mean-field approach

> We compute $β$-decay half-lives of isotonic nuclear chains located at neutron shell closures $N=50$, $82$, $126$ and $184$, which are of particular importance for the $r$-process nucleosynthesis, and study the role of first-forbidden transitions in a framework that includes complex nucleonic correlations beyond the quasiparticle random phase approximation. Such correlations are accounted for by coupling single nucleons to collective degrees of freedom (nuclear vibrations), and are found essential to reproduce available experimental $β$-decay rates and more precise many-body methods. We find that the nucleon-vibration correlations tend to decrease the probability of decay via first-forbidden transition near stability, as they enhance Gamow-Teller transitions at low energy. While in the lighter systems allowed transitions dominate, the decay of $N=126$ and $N=184$ nuclei is found to occur to a large extent via first-forbidden transitions, and in particular those induced by $1^-$ and $0^-$ operators. Overall the many-body method based on nucleon-vibration coupling provides an ideal framework for future large-scale calculations. Upcoming experimental measurements of $β$-decay rates in the $N=126$ region by radioactive-beam facilities will be crucial in order to validate the approach.

## Key claim

The authors find that nucleon-vibration coupling (QVC) systematically decreases the probability of decay via first-forbidden transitions near stability by enhancing Gamow-Teller transitions at low energy, but for the heaviest waiting-point nuclei (N=126 and N=184) first-forbidden transitions, particularly those induced by 1⁻ and 0⁻ operators, still dominate the decay.

## Method

The authors apply the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA), which extends the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) by coupling single nucleons to collective nuclear vibrations (phonons). They compute β⁻-decay half-lives for even-even isotonic chains at neutron shell closures N=50, 82, 126, and 184, including both Gamow-Teller (1⁺) and first-forbidden (0⁻, 1⁻, 2⁻) transition operators within a fully relativistic framework using the NL3 parametrization of the meson-nucleon Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The weak axial coupling constant used is g_A = -1.2764(13), taken from Workman (2022).
- The decay rate constant K is taken as 6144 ± 2 s.
- Phonons with multipolarities J^π = 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻, 6⁺ are included up to excitation energy Ω_μ^max = 20 MeV.
- For N=50 and N=82 nuclei, QVC is included in an energy window of 30 MeV; for N=126 and N=184 nuclei, a 20 MeV window is used.
- The zero-range Landau-Migdal parameter for short-range correlations is set to g' = 0.6.
- The pairing strength parameter G is adjusted to the empirical gap formula 12/√A.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic QRPA calculations of β-decay rates (Marketin 2016) and earlier non-relativistic QRPA studies (Ney 2020, Moeller 2003) by incorporating complex nucleonic correlations beyond the QRPA through quasiparticle-vibration coupling. It also extends earlier RQTBA studies of Gamow-Teller transitions (Robin 2016, Robin 2018) to include first-forbidden transitions systematically for the first time. The results enable future large-scale calculations of β-decay rates for r-process nuclei and highlight the need for experimental measurements of β-decay rates in the N=126 region from radioactive-beam facilities to validate the approach.
