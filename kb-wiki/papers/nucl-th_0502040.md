# Nuclear pasta structures in neutron stars and the charge screening

> Non-uniform structures of the nucleon matter are expected at subnuclear densities and above the nuclear density: they are called nuclear pastas and kaon pastas, respectively. We numerically study these phases by means of the density functional theory with relativistic mean-fields and the electric field; the electric field is properly taken into account. Our results demonstrate a particular role of the charge screening effects on these non-uniform structures.

# Nuclear pasta structures in neutron stars and the charge screening

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that charge screening effects, particularly the rearrangement of proton density within nuclear lumps, play a significant role in determining the structure and stability of nuclear pasta phases in neutron star crusts, contrary to earlier bulk calculations that treated the Coulomb interaction more simply and discarded such rearrangement.

## Method

The authors use density functional theory with a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, solving coupled equations of motion for nucleons, electrons, meson mean-fields, and the Coulomb potential self-consistently via relaxation methods in the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation. They consider spherical (3D), cylindrical (2D), and slab (1D) geometries to represent droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble pasta configurations, imposing global charge neutrality and beta equilibrium where applicable.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter used in the RMF parameter set: $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$, with minimum energy per baryon $-16.3$ MeV, incompressibility $K(\rho_0) = 240$ MeV, effective nucleon mass $m_N^*(\rho_0) = 0.78 m_N$, and isospin-asymmetry coefficient $a_{\rm sym} = 32.5$ MeV.
- For symmetric matter ($Y_p = 0.5$) in the droplet phase, the atomic number of droplets ranges from approximately 25 at the lower density limit to approximately 65 at the maximum droplet density $\rho_{B,d}^{(\rm max)} \simeq 0.025$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The appearance of nuclear pastas softens the equation of state, lowering the energy per baryon by up to about 15 MeV per baryon compared to the uniform case.
- The surface tension parameter used in the bulk comparison calculation is $\tau = 1.03$ MeV/fm$^2$, fitted to liquid-drop binding energies of finite nuclei.
- The sigma meson mass used is 400 MeV (smaller than the popular value of approximately 500 MeV) to avoid overbinding finite nuclei by about 3 MeV per baryon.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear pastas by Ravenhall (1983), Hashimoto (1984), and others that relied on bulk calculations with sharp boundaries and surface tension, where the Coulomb interaction was treated simply and proton rearrangement was discarded. The authors note that Gendenshtein (2003) discussed electron screening effects but did not address proton density rearrangement. The framework enables future studies of mixed phases at other phase transitions in neutron stars, such as kaon condensation and quark-hadron deconfinement, by including the same self-consistent Coulomb treatment.
