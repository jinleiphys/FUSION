# Deuteron Breakup pd\to pnp in Specific Kinematics and Short-Range NN-Interaction

> Deuteron breakup processes $p+d\to (NN)_{s,t}+N$ are studied in the kinematics of backward elastic pd-scattering at law relative momenta of the NN-pair $k\sim 0-50 MeV/c$ for initial energies $T_0=0.5-2.5 GeV$ in the framework of the known mechanisms of the $pd\to dp$ process including rescatterings in the initial and final states. A considerable suppression of the $Δ-$ and $N^*-$ isobar excitation mechanisms is found for the production of the singlet $(NN)_s$-pair. As a result, one nucleon exchange mechanism can be identified in the cross section and polarization observables by the node of the half-off-shell NN$(~^1S_0)$-scattering amplitude, $t(q,k)$, at $q\sim 0.4 $ GeV/c.

# Deuteron Breakup \(pd\to pnp\) in Specific Kinematics and Short-Range NN-Interaction

## Key claim

The reaction \(p+d\to (pp)(0^\circ)+n(180^\circ)\) with a singlet \((NN)_s\)-pair at low relative energy (\(E_{pp}=0-3\) MeV) can reveal the node of the half-off-shell NN(\(^1S_0\))-scattering amplitude \(t(q,k)\) at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, because the \(\Delta\)- and \(N^*\)-isobar excitation mechanisms are suppressed by an isotopic spin factor of \(-1/3\) for the singlet channel, allowing the one-nucleon exchange mechanism to dominate.

## Method

The author extends the ONE+SS+\(\Delta\) model previously developed for backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering (Lak et al., Kondratyuk et al.) to the breakup reaction \(p+d\to (pp)_s+n\) in quasi-backward kinematics. Isotopic spin factors are derived for the meson-nucleon rescattering diagram (Fig. 1f) including isovector mesons (\(\pi,\rho,\dots\)) and both \(T=1/2\) and \(T=3/2\) intermediate states, showing the ratio of singlet to triplet amplitudes is \(-1/3\) independent of the unknown \(\pi N\to\pi N\) amplitudes. Spatial matrix elements for the ONE, \(\Delta\), and \(N^*\) mechanisms are computed using the RSC NN-potential, and rescatterings in initial and final states are treated in the eikonal distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) following Blu et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- Relative momentum of the NN-pair: \(k\sim 0-50\) MeV/c; initial energies \(T_0=0.5-2.5\) GeV.
- Node of the half-off-shell NN(\(^1S_0\))-amplitude \(t(q,k)\) at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c; node of the deuteron S-wave function \(u(q)\) at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c.
- Node of the half-off-shell t-matrix at \(q'\sim 0.38\) GeV/c.
- Isotopic spin ratio for singlet/triplet pair production via isovector meson exchange: \(R_I^{iv} = -1/3\).
- Spatial enhancement factor for singlet relative to triplet pair: \(R_X(ONE)\sim R_X(\Delta)\sim R_X(SS)\sim 1.2-1.5\).
- Cross section minimum from the t-matrix node appears at \(T_0=0.75\) GeV.
- Maximal cross section expected at \(E_{pp}=0.3-0.7\) MeV; Coulomb repulsion reduces cross section below \(E_{pp}<0.3\) MeV.
- The node of the half-off-shell t-matrix at \(q'\sim 0.38\) GeV/c was not yet observed experimentally.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of backward elastic \(pd\to dp\) scattering (Lak et al., Kondratyuk et al., 1989; Uzikov, 1998) to the breakup channel with a singlet NN-pair, where the \(\Delta\)-isobar mechanism is suppressed by isotopic spin factors previously noted for the time-reversed process \(p+(nn)_s\to d+n\) (Imambekov et al., 1987; Albrecht et al., 1980). The proposed reaction enables direct observation of the node in the half-off-shell NN-scattering amplitude at high internal momenta \(q\sim 0.3-0.65\) GeV/c, which is masked in elastic \(pd\to dp\) by D-wave contributions and in electron-deuteron scattering by the need for complicated \(T_{20}\) extractions (Nikhef collaboration). The reaction has not yet been investigated experimentally with sufficient energy resolution; the author suggests it could be studied at COSY.
