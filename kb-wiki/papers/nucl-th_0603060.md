# Giant neutron halos in the non-relativistic mean field approach

> Giant neutron halos in medium-heavy nuclei are studied in the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with Skyrme interactions. The appearance of such structures depends sensitively on the effective interaction adopted. This is illustrated by comparing the predictions of SLy4 and SkI4 in the Ca and Zr isotopic chains. The former force gives no halo effect, the latter predicts a neutron halo in the Zr chain with A>122 due to the weakly bound orbitals 3p1/2 and 3p3/2. The structure of the halo is analyzed in terms of the occupation probabilities of these orbitals and their partial contributions to the neutron density. The anti-halo effect in Ni and Zr isotopes is also discussed by comparing the occupation probabilities of Hartree-Fock neutron single-particle states near the Fermi energy with the corresponding HFB quasiparticle states.

# Giant neutron halos in the non-relativistic mean field approach

## Key claim

Giant neutron halos in medium-heavy nuclei can appear in the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with Skyrme interactions, but only if the effective interaction predicts a neutron drip line far enough to allow weakly bound low-angular-momentum orbitals to form; the effect is strongly model dependent, with SkI4 predicting a halo in Zr isotopes with A>122 while SLy4 gives no halo effect.

## Method

The authors solved the spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space using Skyrme-type effective interactions for the mean field and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction. They compared two Skyrme parametrizations, SLy4 and SkI4, for the Ca and Zr isotopic chains, using box boundary conditions for the continuum (checked against full continuum calculations) and adjusting the pairing strength to reproduce odd-even mass differences. The structure of the halo was analyzed through occupation probabilities of quasiparticle resonances and partial contributions of different (l,j) channels to the neutron density.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip line for Ca isotopes: located at 62Ca for both SLy4 and SkI4, compared to 72Ca in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations of Meng et al. (2002)
- Neutron drip line for Zr isotopes: located at 122Zr for SLy4 and at 138Zr for SkI4, the latter similar to the RHB result of 140Zr
- Pairing strengths used: V0 = -365 MeV·fm3 (SLy4) and -290 MeV·fm3 (SkI4) for Ca isotopes; V0 = -350 MeV·fm3 (SLy4) and -300 MeV·fm3 (SkI4) for Zr isotopes
- In 132Zr, the quasiparticle continuum starts at energy E = -λ = 0.251 MeV
- Box radius of 20 fm used for discretization, checked against full continuum calculations

## Context

This work extends the study of giant neutron halos, previously predicted in relativistic mean field approaches by Meng et al. (2002) for Ca isotopes with A>60 and by Meri et al. for Zr isotopes with A>122, into the non-relativistic Skyrme-HFB framework. The authors show that the SkI4 parametrization, which was designed by Reinhard and Flocard to reproduce the charge isotope shift in Pb isotopes through a modified spin-orbit potential, yields results similar to relativistic calculations for Zr, while the more standard SLy4 force does not. The anti-halo effect, where pairing correlations reduce neutron radii, was previously discussed by Bennaceur et al. for light nuclei; this work shows that pairing can also increase neutron radii depending on the relative occupancy of weakly bound orbitals. The results enable systematic comparisons between relativistic and non-relativistic predictions for drip-line nuclei and highlight the need for further study of which parts of the mean field govern drip line positions.
