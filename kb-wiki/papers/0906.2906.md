# Influence of the symmetry energy on the giant monopole resonance of neutron-rich nuclei

> We analyze the influence of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the average excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in stable and exotic neutron-rich nuclei by applying the relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi method in scaling and constrained calculations. For the effective nuclear interaction, we employ the relativistic mean field model supplemented by an isoscalar-isovector meson coupling that allows one to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy without compromising the success of the model for binding energies and charge radii. The semiclassical estimates of the average energy of the GMR are known to be in good agreement with the results obtained in full RPA calculations. The present analysis is performed along the Pb and Zr isotopic chains. In the scaling calculations, the excitation energy is larger when the symmetry energy is softer. The same happens in the constrained calculations for nuclei with small and moderate neutron excess. However, for nuclei of large isospin the constrained excitation energy becomes smaller in models having a soft symmetry energy. This effect is mainly due to the presence of loosely-bound outer neutrons in these isotopes. A sharp increase of the estimated width of the resonance is found in largely neutron-rich isotopes, even for heavy nuclei, which is enhanced when the symmetry energy of the model is soft. The results indicate that at large neutron numbers the structure of the low-energy region of the GMR strength distribution changes considerably with the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which may be worthy of further characterization in RPA calculations of the response function.

## Key claim

The influence of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the average excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) is moderate for isotopes not far from stability, but becomes more notable for nuclei with large neutron numbers, where the structure of the low-energy region of the GMR strength distribution changes considerably with the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The effect is mainly due to the presence of loosely-bound outer neutrons in these highly neutron-rich isotopes.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) method in scaling and constrained calculations to estimate the average excitation energy of the GMR along the Pb and Zr isotopic chains. They employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) model supplemented by an isoscalar-isovector meson coupling (Λ_V) between the ω and ρ meson fields, which allows modification of the density dependence of the symmetry energy without altering the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter or the agreement with binding energies and charge radii. The semiclassical estimates were compared with full relativistic Hartree-RPA calculations for validation.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb with the NL3.00 model (Λ_V = 0.00): RETF constrained excitation energy = 13.92 MeV, RETF scaling excitation energy = 14.58 MeV; quantal Hartree-RPA centroid energy (m1/m0) = 14.32 MeV
- For 208Pb with the NL3.03 model (Λ_V = 0.03): RETF constrained excitation energy = 14.20 MeV, RETF scaling excitation energy = 14.83 MeV; quantal Hartree-RPA centroid energy (m1/m0) = 14.74 MeV
- For 90Zr with the NL3.00 model: RETF constrained excitation energy = 19.04 MeV, RETF scaling excitation energy = 19.53 MeV; quantal Hartree-RPA centroid energy (m1/m0) = 18.62 MeV
- For 90Zr with the NL3.03 model: RETF constrained excitation energy = 19.12 MeV, RETF scaling excitation energy = 19.62 MeV; quantal Hartree-RPA centroid energy (m1/m0) = 18.75 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density S(ρ0) varies from 37.4 MeV (Λ_V = 0.00) to 31.7 MeV (Λ_V = 0.03)
- The measured excitation energy of the GMR in 208Pb is 14.17 ± 0.28 MeV (Young 1999)
- The nuclear incompressibility K0 of symmetric matter is constrained to the range 200-280 MeV from comparison of RPA results with the 208Pb measurement

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the GMR in neutron-rich nuclei beyond the light isotopic chains of Ca and Ni studied by Capelli et al. (2009) using Skyrme forces, to heavy (Pb) and medium-mass (Zr) isotopic chains using relativistic mean field theory. The study is motivated by the finding of Khan (2009) that measuring the GMR in a single isotope such as 208Pb may not suffice to extract K0 of nuclear matter, and that whole isotopic chains should be measured. The results indicate that the impact of the symmetry energy on the GMR excitation energy is moderate for isotopes near stability but amplified at large neutron numbers, suggesting that further characterization through full RPA calculations of the response function would be valuable, though such calculations become computationally demanding for highly neutron-rich nuclei.
