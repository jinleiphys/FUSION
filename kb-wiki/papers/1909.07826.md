# Can extreme electromagnetic fields accelerate the $α$ decay of nuclei?

> The possibility to control the $α$ decay channel of atomic nuclei with electromagnetic fields of extreme intensities envisaged for the near future at multi-petawatt and exawatt laser facilities is investigated theoretically. Using both analytic arguments based on the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin approximation and numerical calculations for the imaginary time method applied in the framework of the $α$ decay precluster model, we show that no experimentally detectable modification of the $α$ decay rate can be observed with super-intense lasers at any so-far available wavelength. Comparing our predictions with those reported in several recent publications, where a considerable or even giant laser-induced enhancement of the decay rate has been claimed, we identify there the misuse of a standard approximation.

## Key claim

No experimentally detectable modification of the α decay rate can be observed with super-intense lasers at any so-far available wavelength, contradicting several recent publications that claimed considerable or giant laser-induced enhancement due to misuse of the Kramers-Henneberger transformation approximation.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical approaches: an analytical Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation treating the laser field as a quasi-static perturbation, and a numerical imaginary time method (ITM) applied within the α decay precluster model. Both methods calculated the laser-induced correction to the tunneling rate for several α emitters, treating the sub-barrier motion as one-dimensional and the laser field as monochromatic with intensity 10²⁶ W/cm² and 800 nm wavelength.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic electric field needed to modify α decay rates is E_eff ≈ 8·10¹⁶ V/cm for ²³²Pu (Q_α ≈ 5 MeV), about 10⁻² of the naive estimate E* ≈ 10¹⁹ V/cm
- For ¹⁴⁴Nd (Q_α = 1.907 MeV), E_eff = 2.12·10¹⁶ V/cm, corresponding to intensity I ≈ 5·10²⁹ W/cm²
- At laser intensity I = 10²⁶ W/cm² (E_0 = 2.74·10¹⁴ V/cm), the linear correction to the decay rate R_L - 1 ranges from 5.57·10⁻⁴ (¹⁰⁶Te) to 2.57·10⁻² (¹⁴⁴Nd)
- The time- and angle-averaged correction \bar{R}_L - 1 ranges from 2.59·10⁻⁸ (¹⁰⁶Te) to 5.54·10⁻⁵ (¹⁴⁴Nd)
- For ¹⁴⁴Nd, the field-free half-life is 7.2·10²² s (≈ 2.3·10¹⁵ years), so the number of field-free decays per laser shot N_0 ≈ 10⁻²⁵ is negligible
- The maximum number of laser-induced additional decays ΔN for any isotope is 4·10⁻³ (²¹²Po), far below detection limits

## Context

This work extends the Gamow model (Gamow 1928) and the precluster model (Buck) for α decay to include external electromagnetic fields, using methods from laser-atom physics such as the imaginary time method (Popov 2004). It directly contradicts predictions by Delion and Ghinescu (2017), Kis and Szilvasi (2018), and Bai et al., who claimed large enhancements at intensities 10²⁰–10²² W/cm² based on misuse of the Kramers-Henneberger transformation. The results agree quantitatively with Qi et al. (2019) but disagree with their conclusion that the effect is experimentally detectable. The work enables a correct assessment of whether future multi-petawatt and exawatt laser facilities could influence nuclear decay processes, concluding that laser-assisted α decay is practically out of reach for nuclear waste recycling applications.
