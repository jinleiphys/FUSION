# Quantum computer specification for nuclear structure calculations

> Recent studies to solve nuclear structure problems using quantum computers rely on a quantum algorithm known as Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE). In this study, we calculate the correlation energy in Helium-6 using VQE, with a \textit{full-term} unitary-paired-coupled-cluster-doubles (UpCCD) ansatz on a quantum computer simulator and implement a set of custom termination criteria to shorten the optimization time. Using this setup, we test out noisy quantum computer simulators of various coherence times and quantum errors to find the required specification for such calculations. We also look into the contribution of errors from the quantum computers and optimization process. We find that the minimal specification of 5~ms coherence times and $10^{-4}$ quantum errors is required to reliably reproduce state-vector results within 8\% discrepancy. Our study indicates the possibility of performing VQE calculations using a full-term UpCCD ansatz on a slightly noisy quantum computer, without implementing quantum error correction.

## Key claim

The authors find that a quantum computer with coherence times of at least 5 ms and quantum errors no larger than 10^{-4} is necessary to reproduce state-vector correlation energy results for Helium-6 within 8% discrepancy using a full-term UpCCD ansatz, but they note that current transmon qubits have coherence times in the range of 0.3 ms to 0.6 ms, meaning such specifications are not yet available on current hardware.

## Method

The authors used the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) algorithm with a first-order trotterized full-term unitary-paired-coupled-cluster-doubles (UpCCD) ansatz to compute the correlation energy of Helium-6, encoding the Hamiltonian with Jordan-Wigner mapping onto 12 qubits. They employed the SPSA optimizer with custom termination criteria based on logarithmic fitting of the convergence curve, and tested the calculation on a series of modified FakeGuadalupe quantum computer simulators (called FakeJohors) where they independently varied coherence times (T1 = T2) from 0.005 ms to 500 ms and quantum errors (gate, readout, and SPAM errors) from 10^{-8} to 10^{-2}.

## Key numbers

*   Minimum required coherence time of 5 ms and maximum quantum error of 10^{-4} to reproduce state-vector results within 8% discrepancy for the Helium-6 correlation energy calculation.
*   The state-vector correlation energy (E_corr^SV) obtained was -0.117 MeV, with the termination criteria at iteration k=100 reproducing this value exactly.
*   Current transmon qubit coherence times are in the range of 0.3 ms to 0.6 ms (Wang 2022, Bal 2024), and quantum gate errors of approximately 10^{-5} for one-qubit gates (Li 2023) and approximately 10^{-4} for two-qubit gates (Kubo 2024) have been reported.
*   The HF+BCS calculation using a pairing intensity of 1 MeV yielded a binding energy of -29.28 MeV compared to the experimental value of -29.27 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior VQE studies on nuclear structure that used simplified ansatzes to reduce circuit depth on noisy quantum computers, such as those by Kiss (2022) and Dumitrescu (2018), by instead using a full-term UpCCD ansatz. The study provides a quantitative benchmark for the hardware specifications needed to perform meaningful nuclear structure calculations without quantum error correction, and it enables future work to assess whether near-term quantum devices can reach the required 5 ms coherence time and 10^{-4} error thresholds for practical nuclear physics applications.
