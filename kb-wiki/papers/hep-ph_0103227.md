# Color superconductivity and nondecoupling phenomena in 2+1 dimensional QCD

> The possibility of generating a color superconducting state in 2+1 dimensional QCD is analyzed. The gap equation in the leading, hard-dense loop improved, one-gluon exchange approximation is derived and solved. The magnitude of the order parameter is proportional to a power of the coupling constant. For an asymptotically large chemical potential, a qualitatevely new (with respect to the 3+1 dimensional case) phenomenon of nondecoupling of the fermion pairing dynamics from the infrared one is revealed and discussed.

# Color superconductivity and nondecoupling phenomena in 2+1 dimensional QCD

## Key claim

Color superconductivity can occur in (2+1)-dimensional QCD at finite density, but unlike the (3+1)-dimensional case, the fermion pairing dynamics does not decouple from the nonperturbative infrared dynamics even at asymptotically large chemical potential, making the hard-dense-loop improved rainbow approximation unreliable except possibly in the large-\(N_f\) limit.

## Method

The authors derive and solve the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) gap equation in the hard-dense-loop (HDL) improved one-gluon exchange approximation for (2+1)-dimensional QCD at zero temperature and finite chemical potential. They compute the HDL polarization tensor for gluons, obtain the gluon propagator in the static limit, and then derive an approximate analytical solution for the color superconducting gap in the Landau gauge, assuming the gap depends only on the temporal momentum component.

## Key numbers

- The color superconducting gap in the HDL improved rainbow approximation (Landau gauge) is \(|\Delta^{-}_{0}| \simeq b \frac{\alpha^{3}}{M^{2}} = b \frac{\alpha^{2}}{2\mu}\), where \(b \approx 0.331\), \(\alpha\) is the (dimensionful) coupling constant of (2+1)-dimensional QCD, and \(M^{2} = 2 \alpha \mu N_f\) is the Debye mass.
- For large \(N_f\), the gap scales as \(|\Delta^{-}_{0}| \simeq \bar{b} \frac{\bar{\alpha}^{2}}{2 N_f^{2} \mu}\), where \(\bar{\alpha} = N_f \alpha\) and \(\bar{b}\) is of order one.
- The Debye mass is \(M^{2} = 2 \alpha \mu N_f\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity, previously analyzed extensively in (3+1)-dimensional QCD by Son (1999), Pisarski and Rischke (1999), and others, to the (2+1)-dimensional case. The authors find that, in contrast to (3+1)-dimensional QCD where the gap is exponentially small in the coupling (\(\Delta \sim \mu \exp(-C/\sqrt{\alpha_s})\)), the (2+1)-dimensional gap follows a power law in the coupling. The nondecoupling phenomenon identified here suggests that the \(1/N_f\) expansion, previously applied to (2+1)-dimensional QED and QCD by Appelquist et al. (1984) and others, may be necessary to control the infrared dynamics. This work opens questions about the interplay between color and chiral condensates at finite density in lower-dimensional theories and about the role of the Meissner effect in modifying the gluon interaction in the diquark channel.
