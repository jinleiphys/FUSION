# Pairing strength on the nuclear size in relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> The influence of pairing correlations on the nuclear size and in particular on the formation of nuclear halos is studied in the framework of relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory. It turns out that the contributions from the weakly-bound orbits with low orbital angular momenta $l$ play an important role. As an example, we investigate the neutron-rich Mg isotopes as a function of the pairing strength in situations, where the neuron Fermi surface are below, between and above two weakly-bound $2p$ levels. We find that the size of the pairing correlations has a two-fold influence on the density distribution of the neutrons and therefore on the total nuclear size. First it can change the root mean square (rms) radius of the individual weakly-bound orbits and second it can enhance the occupation probabilities of these orbits in the nuclear system. On one side increasing pairing correlations reduce the rms radii of the orbits with small orbital angular momenta close to the continuum limit (pairing anti-halo effect), on the other side they also can lead to an enhanced occupation of low-$l$ orbits above the Fermi surface producing in this way a strong increase of the total radius of the nuclear system. As a consequence, a nuclear halo can form even in cases, where non of the individual low-$l$ orbits is very close to the continuum. This leads to the fact that compared with well-bound nuclei, the impact of the pairing strength on the nuclear size is more pronounced in weakly-bound nuclei than in well-bound systems.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations have a two-fold influence on nuclear size in weakly-bound nuclei: they reduce the root-mean-square (rms) radii of individual low-orbital-angular-momentum orbits (the "pairing anti-halo effect"), but they also enhance the occupation probabilities of those orbits, which can increase the total nuclear radius and even produce a halo when no single low-\(l\) orbit is very close to the continuum.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) equations in coordinate space within a spherical box of radius 20 fm and step size 0.1 fm, using the PK1 relativistic energy density functional and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force. They first employed fixed Woods-Saxon potentials fitted to the self-consistent neutron potential of \(^{42}\)Mg, varying the pairing strength \(v_0\) as an external parameter, and then performed fully self-consistent calculations for the neutron-rich Mg isotopes \(^{40,42,44,46}\)Mg. The neutron Fermi surface was placed below, between, and above two weakly-bound \(2p\) levels to study the effect on the total neutron rms radius.

## Key numbers

* For \(N=28\) with fixed Woods-Saxon potentials, as the average pairing gap \(\Delta\) increases from 0 to 1.5 MeV, the total neutron rms radius \(R_N\) increases monotonically, driven by scattering from the \(1f_{7/2}\) orbit to the \(2p\) orbits.
* For \(N=30\) with fixed potentials, \(R_N\) increases up to \(\Delta = 0.9\) MeV, then decreases for stronger pairing.
* For \(N=32\) with fixed potentials, \(R_N\) increases up to \(\Delta = 0.9\) MeV, then decreases for stronger pairing.
* For \(N=34\) with fixed potentials, as \(\Delta\) increases from 0 to 1.5 MeV, the occupation probabilities of the \(2p_{1/2}\) and \(2p_{3/2}\) states decrease by 15.2% and 9.10%, respectively, while their individual rms radii decrease by about 3.76% and 2.38%; the total neutron rms radius decreases monotonically.
* For a well-bound nucleus with \(\varepsilon_{2p_{1/2}} = -5.0\) MeV, increasing \(\Delta\) from 0.0 to 1.5 MeV changes \(R_N\) by only \(\sim 0.004\) fm.
* For weakly-bound cases with \(\varepsilon_{2p_{1/2}} = -0.5, 0.0, +0.5\) MeV, the same increase in \(\Delta\) produces increases in \(R_N\) of more than 0.1 fm.
* The shell gap between the \(1d_{3/2}\) and \(1f_{7/2}\) orbits is 6.09 MeV, and between the \(1f_{7/2}\) and \(2p_{3/2}\) orbits is 0.67 MeV, in the Woods-Saxon potential fitted to \(^{42}\)Mg.
* The saturation density used in the pairing force is \(\rho_{\text{sat}} = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) framework previously used to describe the neutron halo in \(^{11}\)Li (Meng 1996) and giant halos in Ca and Zr isotopes (Meng 1998, 2002). It directly addresses the "pairing anti-halo effect" proposed by Bennaceur (2000), which argued that pairing correlations prevent the formation of an infinite-radius halo by reducing the asymptotic mean-square radius of individual orbits. The present study shows that in realistic nuclei, the competing effect of increased occupation of low-\(l\) orbits can dominate, enabling halo formation even when no single orbit is extremely weakly bound. This work enables future studies of how level density near the Fermi surface, including high-\(l\) orbits, indirectly influences nuclear size through pairing.
