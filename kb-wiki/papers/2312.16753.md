# $e^+ e^- \to Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ cross sections and the $Λ_c^+$ electromagnetic form factors within the extended vector meson dominance model

> Within the extended vector meson dominance model, we investigate the $e^+ e^- \to Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ reaction and the electromagnetic form factors of the charmed baryon $Λ_c^+$. The model parameters are determined by fitting them to the cross sections of the process $e^+e^-\rightarrow Λ_c^+ \barΛ_c^-$ and the magnetic form factor $|G_M|$ of $Λ^+_c$. By considering four charmoniumlike states, called $ψ(4500)$, $ψ(4660)$, $ψ(4790)$, and $ψ(4900)$, we can well describe the current data on the $e^+ e^- \to Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ reaction from the reaction threshold up to $4.96 \ \mathrm{GeV}$. In addition to the total cross sections and $|G_M|$, the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ and effective form factor $|G_{\mathrm{eff}}|$ for $Λ^+_c$ are also calculated, and found that these calculations are consistent with the experimental data. Within the fitted model parameters, we have also estimated the charge radius of the charmed $Λ_c^+$ baryon.

## Key claim
The extended vector meson dominance model, including four charmoniumlike states ($\psi(4500)$, $\psi(4660)$, $\psi(4790)$, and $\psi(4900)$), can describe the BESIII measurements of $e^+ e^- \to Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ total cross sections and the $Λ_c^+$ magnetic form factor $|G_M|$ from threshold up to $4.96$ GeV, but the fit relies on the assumption that these specific resonances dominate the reaction mechanism and that the Belle-reported $Y(4630)$ structure is not required.

## Method
The authors employed an extended vector meson dominance (VMD) model where the virtual photon couples to $Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ through four intermediate charmoniumlike vector mesons: $\psi(4500)$, $\psi(4660)$, $\psi(4790)$, and $\psi(4900)$. The Dirac and Pauli form factors $F_1$ and $F_2$ were parameterized with Breit-Wigner propagators for each resonance plus a dipole intrinsic form factor $g(s) = 1/(1-\gamma s)^2$, and a Flatte-type width was used for $\psi(4500)$ to account for its coupling to the $Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$ channel near threshold. A nine-parameter $\chi^2$ fit was performed to 28 data points from BESIII (2018 and 2023) covering the total cross sections and $|G_M|$ from threshold to $4.96$ GeV.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section at threshold: $\sigma_{\rm thre.} = 193.1$ pb, corresponding to $|G_{\rm eff}(4 M^2_{\Lambda_c^+})| = 1.16$.
- Fitted dipole parameter: $\gamma = 0.147 \pm 0.017$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Fitted coupling constants: $g_{\Lambda_c} = 1.173 \pm 0.259$ (for $\psi(4500)$ to $Λ^+_c \barΛ^-_c$); $\beta_1 = 1.883 \pm 0.484$, $\beta_2 = -1.101 \pm 0.302$, $\beta_3 = -0.439 \pm 0.194$, $\beta_4 = -0.141 \pm 0.097$; $\alpha_2 = 1.089 \pm 0.297$, $\alpha_3 = 0.438 \pm 0.192$, $\alpha_4 = 0.133 \pm 0.096$.
- $\chi^2 / {\rm d.o.f} = 0.6$ for the fit to 28 data points.
- Mean squared charge radius of $Λ_c^+$: $\langle r_E^2 \rangle_{\Lambda_c^+} = 0.07$ fm$^2$.
- Predicted $\sin \Delta \Phi$ exceeds $0.8$ in the energy range $4.68$ to $4.78$ GeV, with a zero crossing near $\sqrt{s} = 4.84$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the VMD approach previously applied to nucleons (e.g., by Bijker and Iachello) and to hyperons such as $\Lambda$ (by Yan et al.), $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ (by Yang et al.) to the charmed baryon sector. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the Belle (2008) observation of a $Y(4630)$ peak and the BESIII (2023) measurement showing a long plateau from threshold to $4.66$ GeV, confirming that the new BESIII data can be explained without a distinct $Y(4630)$ resonance. The study enables future experimental searches for the $\psi(4900)$ state around $4.9$ GeV and provides predictions for the relative phase $\sin \Delta \Phi$ that can guide polarization measurements of $Λ_c^+$ in $e^+ e^-$ collisions, particularly near $\sqrt{s} = 4.69$ GeV.
