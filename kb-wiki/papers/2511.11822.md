# Coexisting phases in the chiral transition within the Linear sigma model with quarks

> It is believed at present that the chiral transition changes from a smooth crossover to a first-order transition at low temperatures and high densities. Such regime is commonly analyzed using effective models since first principle calculations, as in lattice arrangements, are not feasible. This transition is assumed to be discontinuous, with unstable or metastable intermediate states. However, if multiple charges are simultaneously conserved the system could undergo a continuous change through a coexistence of equilibrium states. This type of transition has multiple manifestations, as in the nuclear liquid-gas transition causing the spinodal fragmentation. The coexistence of phases in the chiral transition is studied here for quark matter assuming the conservation of the isospin composition. Using the Linear sigma model with quarks several remarkable effects are found and discussed.

# Coexisting phases in the chiral transition within the Linear sigma model with quarks

## Key claim
The chiral transition in quark matter at finite baryon density and with isospin asymmetry can proceed as a continuous change through a coexistence of equilibrium phases (rather than a discontinuous first-order transition) if multiple charges are simultaneously conserved and a low surface tension is assumed, but this Gibbs construction does not apply for flavor-symmetric matter (x=0) where only one charge is conserved.

## Method
The author employs the Linear Sigma Model with quarks (LSMq) in the mean-field approximation, including scalar isoscalar meson σ, pseudoscalar isovector mesons π, and an additional scalar isovector meson ζ. The grand potential is computed at one-loop level for quarks, while meson contributions are disregarded due to imaginary pion masses at finite density. The Gibbs construction for phase coexistence is applied under conditions of fixed temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential, with a parameter λ indicating the relative abundance of coexisting phases.

## Key numbers
- The σ meson mass is set to M_σ0 = 500 MeV, the pion mass to M_π0 = 138 MeV, and the ζ meson mass (identified with the a0(980)) to M_ζ0 = 984 MeV.
- The quark constituent mass is adopted as m_q0 = 250 MeV, yielding a pseudo-critical temperature T_0 ≃ 120 MeV (below the LQCD range of 160-180 MeV).
- The equilibrium coexistence region (ECR) for pressure P = 43.5 MeV fm⁻³ extends up to a maximum temperature T = 9.2 MeV, where only states with x_a ≃ x_b ≃ 0 are involved.
- For x=1/3 and T=20 MeV (above the critical endpoint), the isothermal speed of sound shows a non-monotonic trend with its minimum at the transition point.

## Context
This work extends studies of the chiral transition in quark matter with isospin imbalance (Toublan, Frank, Barducci, Loewe, He, Zhang, Mukherjee, Kovacs, Mu, Abuki, Xia, Stiele, Liu, Avancini, Lopes, Adhikari, Costa, Chu, Ayala, 2000-2020) to finite baryon density, where lattice QCD calculations are not feasible due to the sign problem. The Gibbs construction for phase coexistence follows the approach of Glendenning and Heiselberg for multiple conserved charges, analogous to the nuclear liquid-gas transition causing spinodal fragmentation. The results on susceptibilities are compared with prior work using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Sasaki, 2007-2010) and the LSMq (Stokic, Skokov, Almasi, 2009-2019), showing that within the coexistence region the baryon number susceptibility remains bounded for low asymmetry parameters, in contrast to calculations using the unstable sector of the equation of state.
