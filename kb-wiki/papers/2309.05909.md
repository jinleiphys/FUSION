# Exploring Short-Range Correlations in Symmetric Nuclei: Insights into Contacts and Entanglement Entropy

> The Short-Range Correlations between nucleons in nuclei is regarded as a complex system. We investigate the relationship between the orbital entanglement entropy of SRCs $S_{ij}$ in nuclear structures and Tan contact $c_{ij}$, and find that the orbital entanglement entropies and Tan contacts corresponding to proton-proton SRC pairs and neutron-proton SRC pairs in nuclei demonstrate a scaling relation. More specifically, the proportionality of entanglement entropy between proton-proton pairs and neutron-proton pairs is directly related to the ratio of nuclear contacts within the atomic nucleus, demonstrating an approximate ratio of 2.0. Our research suggests that this scaling relationship should hold true for all symmetric nuclei, furthermore, we offer a possible explanation for this phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the ratio of the single-orbital entanglement entropy for proton-proton SRC pairs to that for neutron-proton SRC pairs, divided by the corresponding ratio of reduced nuclear contacts, converges to a constant value of approximately 2.0 for all symmetric nuclei considered, but this scaling relation is derived from the Generalized Contact Formalism and is therefore model-dependent.

## Method

The authors used the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) to extract reduced nuclear contacts for proton-proton (spin-0) and neutron-proton (spin-0 and spin-1) SRC channels from two-body density distributions in both coordinate space and momentum space, as well as from experimental data. They then computed the single-orbital SRC entanglement entropy from the reduced nuclear contacts using the von Neumann entropy formula for a 2x2 density matrix, where the occupancy probability of the SRC orbital is given by the reduced contact. The ratio defined in Eq. (6) was evaluated for symmetric nuclei from 4He to 40Ca using contacts previously extracted by Weiss et al. (2016).

## Key numbers

* For 4He, the ratio R = (S_pp/S_np)/(c_pp/c_np) = 2.029 ± 0.167 from k-space contacts (c_np = 0.1299 ± 0.0010, c_pp = 0.0065 ± 0.0003) and 2.028 ± 0.003 from r-space contacts (c_np = 0.1218 ± 0.0003, c_pp = 0.00567 ± 0.00004).
* For 12C, the ratio R = 2.047 ± 0.071 from k-space contacts (c_np = 0.182 ± 0.008, c_pp = 0.013 ± 0.002) and 2.092 ± 0.007 from r-space contacts (c_np = 0.1573 ± 0.0010, c_pp = 0.0083 ± 0.0001).
* For 40Ca, the ratio R = 1.953 ± 0.025 from r-space contacts (c_np = 0.1233 ± 0.0030, c_pp = 0.0073 ± 0.0004).
* The ratio c_pp/c_np from the charge-density parameterization (Weiss 2018) gives approximately 0.06, using constants k1 ≈ 0.023 and k2 ≈ 0.085.

## Context

This work extends the single-orbital entanglement entropy approach of Robin et al. (2020) and Pazy et al. (2022) by connecting it explicitly to nuclear contacts from the GCF framework of Weiss et al. (2015, 2016). It provides a constraint on the values of nuclear contacts for different SRC channels, suggesting that the ratio of entanglement entropies to contacts is approximately 2 for all symmetric nuclei. The results could enable predictions of the pp/np SRC pair ratio for nuclei that have not yet been measured experimentally, but the authors note that high-precision testing requires future SRC measurements.
