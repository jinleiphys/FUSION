# Isomeric excitation energy for $^{99}$In$^{m}$ from mass spectrometry reveals constant trend next to doubly magic $^{100}$Sn

> The excitation energy of the 1/2$^-$ isomer in $^{99}$In at ${N=50}$ is measured to be 671(37) keV and the mass uncertainty of the 9/2$^+$ ground state is significantly reduced using the ISOLTRAP mass spectrometer at ISOLDE/CERN. The measurements exploit a major improvement in the resolution of the multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer. The results reveal an intriguing constancy of the $1/2^-$ isomer excitation energies in neutron-deficient indium that persists down to the $N = 50$ shell closure, even when all neutrons are removed from the valence shell. This trend is used to test large-scale shell model, \textit{ab initio}, and density functional theory calculations. The models have difficulties describing both the isomer excitation energies and ground-state electromagnetic moments along the indium chain.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of the 1/2⁻ isomer in ⁹⁹In at the N=50 shell closure is measured to be 671(37) keV, revealing that the constancy of isomer excitation energies in neutron-deficient indium persists even when all neutrons are removed from the valence shell, though all tested theoretical models have difficulties describing both the isomer excitation energies and ground-state electromagnetic moments along the indium chain.

## Method

The ISOLTRAP multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-ToF MS) at ISOLDE/CERN was used to measure the time-of-flight difference between the 9/2⁺ ground state and 1/2⁻ isomer in ⁹⁹In, exploiting a major improvement in mass resolving power achieved through an extended active and passive multi-mirror voltage stabilization system and optimization of the initial ion-bunch emittance. The neutron-deficient indium isotopes were produced by impinging a 1.4 GeV proton beam onto a thick lanthanum carbide target, surface-ionized with element-selective two-step laser ionization, and the time-of-flight spectrum was analyzed using a multi-component exponentially modified Gaussian (hyperEMG) fit to extract ground-state and isomeric state parameters.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the 1/2⁻ isomer in ⁹⁹In: 671(33)stat(16)syst keV (total 671(37) keV)
- Ground-state mass excess of ⁹⁹In: -61431(12)stat(8)syst keV, improving precision by a factor of five compared to previous measurements
- Excitation energy of the 1/2⁻ isomer in ¹⁰¹In: 658(14)stat(7)syst keV
- Mass resolving power achieved: 5 × 10⁵, an improvement factor of 2.5 compared to the previous experiment
- Time-of-flight widths: Δt_FWHM ≈ 50 ns for a time-of-flight of 50 ms
- Ground-state-to-isomer ratio in ⁹⁹In: 13:1, with less than 0.3 isomers per second delivered to the spectrometer
- Average proton current: 2.0 μA, with about 3 × 10¹³ protons per pulse, yielding roughly four ⁹⁹In⁺ ions per second

## Context

This work extends mass measurements of neutron-deficient indium isotopes to the N=50 shell closure, following previous measurements by Mougeot et al. (2021) and Xu et al. (2019), and provides the first experimental determination of the ⁹⁹In isomer excitation energy. The results test large-scale shell model calculations, ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations, coupled-cluster shell model (CCSM) calculations from Sun et al. (2021), and density functional theory (DFT) calculations, finding that the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction reproduces the flat trend of the splitting while the ΔNNLO_GO interaction predicts a linear decrease at odds with experiment. The measurements enable future readjustments of nuclear density functionals and motivate further measurements of nuclear moments of the 1/2⁻ and 9/2⁺ states down to N=50.
