# Structure of nuclei in dileptons production in proton-nucleus scattering

> We investigate production of electron-positron pairs (dileptons) in the scattering of protons off nuclei. Focus is directed on clarifying role of nuclear interactions which make basis in mechanisms of scattering process and structure of nuclei. For that, we constructed a new model of production of dileptons, where scattering of nuclei and their structure are described on the basis of quantum mechanics. Cross sections of dilepton production are calculated in the scattering of protons on \isotope[9]{B} at energy of proton beam of 2.1~GeV. Tendency of the calculated full spectrum is in good agreement with experimental data of DLS Collaboration. Contribution of incoherent processes has a leading role in production of dileptons in comparison with coherent one. In our model calculated cross section of dileptons is sensitive to nuclear part of potential of interactions between proton and nucleus in scattering. Influence of structure of nucleus on calculations of cross sections of production of dileptons is essential. This result provides save basis for new opportunity to extract new information about structure of nuclei, nuclear part of potential from experimental data of dileptons at studied energies of proton beam.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the calculated cross section of dilepton production in proton-nucleus scattering is sensitive to the nuclear part of the proton-nucleus interaction potential and to the structure of the nucleus, but this sensitivity is established only for the specific case of protons on ⁹B at 2.1 GeV and relies on the model's quantum-mechanical treatment of the many-nucleon system.

## Method

The authors constructed a quantum-mechanical model for electron-positron pair production in proton-nucleus scattering, starting from a second-order S-matrix in QED and extending it to a non-relativistic many-nucleon formalism. They calculated the full nuclear matrix element for photon emission by including coherent electric and magnetic monopole terms plus incoherent magnetic and background terms, then integrated over dilepton phase space to obtain cross sections. The model was applied to proton scattering on ⁹B at a beam energy of 2.1 GeV, and the resulting dilepton spectrum was compared with experimental data from the DLS Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam energy: 2.1 GeV for scattering on ⁹B.
- The calculated full spectrum shows good agreement with experimental data from the DLS Collaboration (no numerical goodness-of-fit metric provided).
- Magnetic moments used: proton magnetic moment μ_p^(an) = 2.79284734462 and neutron magnetic moment μ_n^(an) = -1.91304273, both in units of nuclear magneton μ_N.
- The radial integrals J_1(0,1,0) and J_1(0,1,2) appear in the coherent electric monopole matrix element M_p^(E, mon, 0), with a coefficient of 47/40 √(1/2) for the J_1(0,1,2) term.

## Context

This work extends the unified model for bremsstrahlung photon emission in nuclear reactions developed by Maydanyuk (2023) to the production of dileptons, building on the many-nucleon scattering formalism established by Maydanyuk and Vasilevsky (2023) and Shaulskyi, Maydanyuk, and Vasilevsky (2024). The study focuses on the simplest nuclear reaction with incoherent processes, proton-nucleus scattering, and does not include channels related to resonances, meson decays, or Dalitz decay as studied by Zetenyi and Wolf (2012) and Wolf (1993). The results open the possibility to extract information about nuclear structure and the nuclear part of the interaction potential from experimental dilepton data at beam energies around 1-5 GeV, where the DLS Collaboration (Wilson 1998, Porter 1997) and HADES Collaboration (Agakishiev 2012) have provided data.
