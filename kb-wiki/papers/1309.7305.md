# Charming mesons with baryons and nuclei

> The properties of charmed mesons in nuclear matter and nuclei are reviewed. Different frameworks are discussed paying a special attention to unitarized coupled-channel approaches which incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry. Several charmed baryon states with negative parity are generated dynamically by the s-wave interaction between pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ baryons. These states are compared to experimental data. Moreover, the properties of open-charm mesons in matter are analyzed. The in-medium solution accounts for Pauli blocking effects, and for the meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. The behavior in the nuclear medium of the rich spectrum of dynamically-generated baryon states is studied as well as their influence in the self-energy and, hence, the spectral function of open charm. The possible experimental signatures of the in-medium properties of open charm are finally addressed, such as the formation of charmed nuclei, in connection with the future FAIR facility.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how charmed meson properties in nuclear matter and nuclei can be described using unitarized coupled-channel approaches that incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS), but the identification of dynamically-generated baryon states with experimental resonances depends on soft symmetry-breaking mechanisms and the specific renormalization scheme used.

## Method

The author extends the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction to spin-flavor SU(6) with HQSS constraints, constructing a meson-baryon potential that includes both pseudoscalar and vector mesons with 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons. The scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation with a subtracted loop function, and dynamically-generated baryon states are identified as poles of these amplitudes on the complex energy plane. For in-medium calculations, Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies are incorporated self-consistently into the coupled-channel framework.

## Key numbers

* The dynamically-generated Λc(2595) resonance is predicted at 2618.8 MeV with a width of 1.2 MeV, compared to the experimental mass of 2592.25 ± 0.28 MeV and width 2.6 ± 0.6 MeV
* The Λc(2625) resonance is predicted at 2666.6 MeV with width 53.7 MeV, compared to the experimental mass of 2628.11 ± 0.19 MeV and width less than 0.97 MeV
* The Ξc(2790) is assigned to a predicted state at 2804.8 MeV with width 20.7 MeV, compared to experimental mass 2789.1 ± 3.2 MeV and width less than 15 MeV
* The Ξc(2815) is assigned to a predicted state at 2845.2 MeV with width 44.0 MeV, compared to experimental mass 2816.6 ± 0.9 MeV and width less than 3.5 MeV
* A C = -1, S = 0, I = 0, J = 1/2 state is generated bound by only 1 MeV from the coupled D̅N and D̅*N channels
* Hidden-charm N and Δ states with c c̅ pairs are predicted with masses between 3918 and 4027 MeV for N states, and around 4306-4307 MeV for Δ states
* The QMC model predicts an attractive D+-nucleus potential of about -140 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ0
* QCD sum-rule approaches suggest a D-meson mass shift of -50 MeV at ρ0, or alternatively a D+/D- mass splitting of 60 MeV at ρ0

## Context

This review extends the successful unitarized coupled-channel approaches previously applied in the light-quark sector (such as for the Λ(1405) resonance) to the charm sector, incorporating HQSS constraints that were absent in earlier models like the t-channel vector-exchange model of Hofmann (2006) and the hidden-gauge formalism of Wu (2010-2012). The work enables future experimental searches at the FAIR facility (PANDA and CBM experiments) for charmed mesic nuclei and provides predictions for in-medium modifications of open-charm mesons that could affect J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier studies by Tsushima (1998) and Sibirtsev (1999) on D-meson bound states in nuclei.
