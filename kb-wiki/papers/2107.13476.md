# Estimating Microscopic Nuclear Data by Compact Star Observations

> We studied recent observation data of pulsar masses and radii of PSR J0740$+$6620, PSR J0348$+$0432, and PSR J1614$-$2230 from different measurements, based on the extended version of $σ$-$ ω$ model. Throughout our analysis, we assumed that these pulsars are maximal-mass compact stars, thus we applied the core approximation. Based on the linear relation between the microscopic and macroscopic parameters of compact stars evaluated by our model, we estimated the average Landau mass $m_L = 752.46^{+49.1}_{-42.5}$ MeV and compressibility $K = 261.7^{+57.2}_{-28.0}$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors estimate microscopic nuclear matter parameters (Landau mass and compressibility) from pulsar observations by assuming that the observed pulsars are maximal-mass compact stars and applying a core approximation, which may not be valid for softer equations of state where crust contributions become relevant.

## Method

The authors used an extended sigma-omega model in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature and finite chemical potential to describe nuclear matter, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations for maximal-mass stars. They fitted linear relations between macroscopic observables (mass and radius of maximal-mass stars) and microscopic parameters (Landau mass and compressibility), then inverted these relations using observational data from three pulsars (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J1614-2230) to extract the nuclear parameters.

## Key numbers

- Average Landau mass: m_L = 752.46^{+49.1}_{-42.5} MeV
- Average compressibility: K = 261.7^{+57.2}_{-28.0} MeV
- For PSR J0740+6620 (from J0740 reference): mass 2.17^{+0.11}_{-0.10} solar masses, corresponding Landau mass 748.39^{+63.3}_{-57.2} MeV, compressibility 351.8^{+115}_{-84.5} MeV
- For PSR J0348+0432: mass 2.01^{+0.04}_{-0.04} solar masses, Landau mass 785.25^{+20.0}_{-20.3} MeV, compressibility 206.4^{+42.7}_{-20.5} MeV
- For PSR J1614-2230: mass 1.97^{+0.04}_{-0.04} solar masses, Landau mass 794.47^{+20.1}_{-20.4} MeV, compressibility 170.0^{+15.5}_{-20.9} MeV
- Linear relation between maximal mass and Landau mass: M_maxM [solar masses] = 5.418 - 0.0043 * m_L [MeV] with 0.8% theoretical uncertainty
- Linear relation between maximal mass and compressibility: M_maxM [solar masses] = 1.940 + 0.000880 * K [MeV] with less than 2% theoretical uncertainty

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Posfay (2020) and Barnafoldi (2020) that established linear relations between microscopic and macroscopic parameters of compact stars, and it applies these relations to recent observational data from NICER and XMM-Newton missions. The results agree with Bayesian analyses carried out on the same extended sigma-omega model by Alvarez (2020) and Barnafoldi (2020). The approach enables estimation of nuclear matter properties from astrophysical observations, though the authors note that the core approximation may break down for softer equations of state, leading to radius uncertainties larger than 1 km.
