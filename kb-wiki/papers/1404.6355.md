# Sigma-terms and axial charges for hyperons and charmed baryons

> We present results for the $σ$-terms and axial charges for various hyperons and charmed baryons using $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions. For the computation of the three-point function we use the fixed current method. For one of the $N_f=2+1+1$ ensembles with pion mass of 373 MeV we compare the results of the fixed current method with those obtained with a stochastic method for computing the all-to-all propagator involved in the evaluation of the three point functions.

# Sigma-terms and axial charges for hyperons and charmed baryons

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD results for sigma-terms and axial charges of hyperons and charmed baryons using Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass fermions, but the results are obtained at a single ensemble with pion mass of 373 MeV and include only connected contributions, meaning the reported values are not yet extrapolated to the physical pion mass and omit disconnected diagrams.

## Method
The authors use Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass fermion configurations at beta=1.95 with Osterwalder-Seiler valence quarks, tuning the strange and charm quark masses by matching kaon and D-meson masses in unitary and mixed action setups. Three-point functions are computed using the fixed current method for all baryons, and for one ensemble at pion mass 373 MeV, a stochastic method for all-to-all propagators is tested and compared against the fixed sink method. Axial charges are extracted from matrix elements of the axial-vector current at zero momentum transfer, while sigma-terms are computed directly from the scalar matrix element sigma_q = m_q <h|bar-psi psi|h>.

## Key numbers
- Tuned strange quark mass: a mu_s = 0.01671(18)(28), where the first error is statistical and the second systematic from the difference between polynomial and linear fits
- Tuned charm quark mass: a mu_c = 0.2195(15)(10), with the same error definitions
- The SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking parameter delta_SU(3) at the physical strange quark mass is found to be around 10% from a chiral extrapolation using delta_SU(3) = a x^2
- For the stochastic method comparison at pion mass 373 MeV using 500 gauge configurations, N_r=6 noise vectors are needed to match the fixed sink method for the electric form factor, while N_r=4 suffices for the axial form factor
- The stochastic method with N_r=2 and summed spin projectors yields about 3 times the error of the fixed sink method, but using unsummed projectors and averaging over proton and neutron reduces the error ratio to approximately 1

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of axial charges and sigma-terms beyond the nucleon to the full SU(4) 20-plet of baryons, including hyperons and charmed baryons where experimental values are poorly known or absent. The authors test SU(3) flavor symmetry relations from Tiburzi (2008) and Alexandrou (2009) by computing the breaking parameter delta_SU(3), and compare their stochastic method results against the fixed sink approach. The sigma-term calculations provide input for dark matter direct detection experiments, complementing phenomenological determinations from pi-N scattering and earlier lattice calculations using the Feynman-Hellman theorem by Young (2013) and direct matrix element calculations by Alexandrou (2012, 2013). The stochastic method demonstrated here enables computation of matrix elements for all 40 SU(4) baryon states without additional inversions per hadron or operator, opening the possibility for systematic studies of baryon structure across the full flavor multiplets.
