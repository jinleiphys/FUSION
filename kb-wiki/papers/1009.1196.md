# Equation of State of Structured Matter at Finite Temperature

> We investigate the properties of nuclear matter at the first-order phase transitions such as liquid-gas phase transition and hadron-quark phase transition. As a general feature of the first-order phase transitions of matter consisting of many species of charged particles, there appears a mixed phases with geometrical structures called ``pasta'' due to the balance of the Coulomb repulsion and the surface tension between two phases. The equation of state (EOS) of mixed phase is different from the one obtained by a bulk application of the Gibbs conditions or by the Maxwell construction due to the effects of the non-uniform structure. We show that the charge screening and strong surface tension make the EOS close to that of the Maxwell construction. The thermal effects are elucidated as well as the above finite-size effects.

# Equation of State of Structured Matter at Finite Temperature

## Key claim
The paper shows that at finite temperature, the equation of state of mixed-phase nuclear matter (both liquid-gas and hadron-quark) approaches that of the Maxwell construction due to thermal effects, but the temperature dependence of the geometrical structure size is opposite for the two types of mixed phases.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model for nuclear matter and the MIT bag model for quark matter, solving the coupled field equations within Wigner-Seitz cells of spherical, cylindrical, or slab symmetry. The Coulomb interaction is included self-consistently, and finite-temperature effects are incorporated by using Fermi-Dirac distributions for fermions while neglecting temperature dependence of meson fields and antiparticle contributions.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction Y_p=0.5), the Maxwell construction gives baryon partial pressure P_B=0 for the mixed phase.
- At T=14 MeV, no pasta structure is obtained for the liquid-gas mixed phase because the baryon partial pressure of uniform matter becomes a monotonic function of density.
- For the hadron-quark mixed phase, the geometrical structure becomes mechanically unstable above T ~ 60 MeV.
- The hadron-quark mixed phase pressure is shown at T=0 and T=30 MeV over a range of baryon densities up to several times nuclear saturation density.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature studies of pasta structures in liquid-gas mixed phase (Maruyama et al., various years), kaon condensed mixed phase (Maruyama et al.), and hadron-quark mixed phase (Endo et al., Yasutake et al.). It also builds on earlier work by Ravenhall and Hashimoto on pasta structures. The finite-temperature results are relevant for proto-neutron stars, supernova collapse, and neutron star mergers, where temperatures reach several tens of MeV. The paper enables future comparisons with other quark matter models such as the NJL model (Burgio et al., 2008; Yasutake et al., 2009) or PNJL model (Fukushima et al., 2008).
