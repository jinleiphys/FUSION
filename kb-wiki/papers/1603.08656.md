# Deuteron Stripping on Nuclei at Intermediate Energies

> A general analytical expression for the double differential cross section of inclusive deuteron stripping reaction on nuclei at intermediate energies of incident particles was obtained in the diffraction approximation. Nucleon-nucleus phases were calculated in the framework of Glauber formalism and making use of the double-folding potential. The exact wave function of deuteron with correct asymptotics at short and long distances between nucleons was used. The calculated angular dependencies of cross sections are in good agreement with corresponding experimental data.

# Deuteron Stripping on Nuclei at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
The double differential cross section for inclusive deuteron stripping can be expressed in a fully analytical closed form by expanding both the deuteron wave function and the nucleon-nucleus profile functions in Gaussian basis sets, provided that the exact deuteron wave function with correct short- and long-distance asymptotics is used rather than a simple model function.

## Method
The author derived the cross section from the diffraction approximation using Glauber formalism for nucleon-nucleus phases, with the double-folding potential providing the eikonal phases. The deuteron S-wave function was expanded in a basis of 10 Gaussians fitted to a triplet nucleon-nucleon potential, and the target density distribution was similarly expanded in a Gaussian series. The resulting fivefold integral in the general formula was reduced to a sum of analytical terms involving the expansion coefficients and the neutron momentum components.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron wave function expansion used N=10 Gaussian terms, reproducing experimental binding energy and root-mean-square radius.
- Target density expansion used K=10 Gaussian terms, with basis widths set as a_Tj^2 = R_rms^2 / j.
- Nucleon density width parameter a_N^2 = r_0^2 / ln2, with r_0^2 = 0.65 fm^2.
- Normalization factors N_W for the imaginary part of the double-folding potential were 0.68 at T=787 MeV, 0.49 at 858 MeV, and 0.15 at 1242 MeV for the reaction 2H(d,n)3He.
- Model wave function parameter xi = 0.049 fm^{-2} was chosen to reproduce the experimental deuteron root-mean-square radius.

## Context
This work extends the diffraction model of deuteron stripping originally formulated by Akhiezer and Sitenko (1960s), which contained a fivefold integral impractical for direct calculation. The analytical result improves on earlier approximations that assumed opaque, non-diffuse nuclei or neglected the deuteron size relative to the target. The approach enables analysis of stripping, pickup, and breakup reactions for light and heavy ions, and can be generalized to include spin-orbit interactions for polarization calculations.
