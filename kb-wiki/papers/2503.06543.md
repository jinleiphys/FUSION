# Revisiting interpolating flows in $(1+1)$ hydrodynamics

> We revisit the general analytic solution space for relativistic $(1+1)$-dimensional hydrodynamics for a perfect fluid flowing along the longitudinal direction. We work out the explicit one-parameter family of interpolating flows between boost-invariant and boost-non-invariant regimes, where a direct and simple dialing of the parameter at the level of solutions is possible. We also discuss the construction of generalised rapidity distribution of entropy for such interpolating flows at the level of potentials.

# Revisiting interpolating flows in (1+1) hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The authors present a refined one-parameter family of analytic solutions for relativistic (1+1)-dimensional perfect fluid hydrodynamics that interpolates between boost-invariant (Bjorken) and boost-non-invariant (Landau) regimes, where the parameter A can be dialed directly at the level of solutions, unlike prior work where the integration constant prevented a smooth limit to Bjorken flow as the parameter approached zero.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic hydrodynamic equations in lightcone coordinates with a linear equation of state (ε = g p, where 1/√g is the speed of sound). They introduce a generalized ansatz for the rapidity y = (1/2) ln(f₊(z₊)/f₋(z₋)) with arbitrary functions f± of the lightcone coordinates z± = t ± z. Substituting into the consistency condition yields the differential equation f f'' = A²/2, which they solve with the specific choice of integration constant C = 1 (unlike prior work which set C = A² ln H). The resulting implicit solution is integrated to obtain explicit expressions for pressure and rapidity, and the Khalatnikov potential formalism is used to derive the entropy distribution per unit rapidity on constant-temperature hypersurfaces.

## Key numbers
- The speed of sound is given by c_s² = 1/g, where g is the parameter in the equation of state ε = g p; the paper treats g as arbitrary throughout.
- In the A → 0 limit, the pressure reduces to p(z₊, z₋) = p₀ (z₊ z₋)^(-(g+1)/(2g)) = p₀ (τ/τ₀)^(-(g+1)/g), which is the standard Bjorken solution.
- The entropy per unit rapidity in the Bjorken limit (A → 0) becomes dS/dy = s₀ τ₀, a constant independent of rapidity.
- For the general harmonic flow solutions with parameters A and C, setting A = 1 and C = 0 reproduces the entropy distribution dS/dy = (s₀/√(2g)) exp[((g-1)/2)(-θ + (θ² - y²/g)^(1/2))] (1 + θ (θ² - y²/g)^(-1/2)), matching prior results from PhysRevC.78.064909.

## Context
This work refines the interpolating flow solutions originally proposed in PhysRevC.76.054901, where the integration constant C = A² ln H prevented a smooth A → 0 limit to Bjorken flow. By setting C = 1 instead, the authors achieve a genuine one-parameter interpolation. The work extends the Khalatnikov potential approach from Khalatnikov (1954) and its application to entropy distributions in PhysRevC.78.064909 and Peschanski (2010). The results enable systematic study of deviations from boost invariance in heavy-ion collisions, with the parameter A potentially related to rapidity width of the beam. The authors note connections to recent Carrollian symmetry approaches to Bjorken physics by Bagchi (2023), suggesting A may encode an effective velocity parameter.
