# Long-range pseudorapidity dihadron correlations in $d$+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV

> Dihadron angular correlations in $d$+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV are reported as a function of the measured zero-degree calorimeter neutral energy and the forward charged hadron multiplicity in the Au-beam direction. A finite correlated yield is observed at large relative pseudorapidity ($Δη$) on the near side (i.e. relative azimuth $Δϕ\sim0$). This correlated yield as a function of $Δη$ appears to scale with the dominant, primarily jet-related, away-side ($Δϕ\simπ$) yield. The Fourier coefficients of the $Δϕ$ correlation, $V_{n}=\langle\cos nΔϕ\rangle$, have a strong $Δη$ dependence. In addition, it is found that $V_{1}$ is approximately inversely proportional to the mid-rapidity event multiplicity, while $V_{2}$ is independent of it with similar magnitude in the forward ($d$-going) and backward (Au-going) directions.

## Key claim

A finite correlated yield is observed at large relative pseudorapidity on the near side in d+Au collisions, and this yield appears to scale with the away-side (jet-related) yield, but the data do not naturally fit into a hydrodynamic description, suggesting instead a pair-wise correlation correlated with dijet production.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured dihadron angular correlations in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using the Time Projection Chamber and Forward Time Projection Chamber detectors. Correlations were analyzed as a function of the measured zero-degree calorimeter neutral energy and forward charged hadron multiplicity in the Au-beam direction, with Fourier coefficients V_n = <cos n Delta-phi> extracted from the Delta-phi correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV for d+Au collisions at RHIC
- V_1 is approximately inversely proportional to the mid-rapidity event multiplicity
- V_2 is independent of mid-rapidity event multiplicity with similar magnitude in the forward (d-going) and backward (Au-going) directions
- Fourier coefficients V_n show a strong dependence on Delta-eta (relative pseudorapidity)

## Context

This work extends previous ridge observations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Adams 2005, Abelev 2009) and LHC (Chatrchyan 2013, ALICE 2011) to small-system d+Au collisions, and follows similar ridge observations in high-multiplicity pp (Khachatryan 2010) and p+Pb collisions (CMS 2012, Abelev 2012, Aad 2012) at the LHC. The results challenge the expectation that hydrodynamics does not describe small-system collisions, as the observed ridge is suggestive of a hydrodynamic origin, but the data do not naturally fit into a hydrodynamic description, leaving open questions about the mechanism for long-range correlations in small collision systems.
