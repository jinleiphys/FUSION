# Dilepton Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The present status of our understanding of low mass dilepton production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed. The focus of the discussion will be the sensitivity of dilepton measurements to in medium changes of hadrons and the restoration of chiral symmetry. We will finally discuss how the presence of strong long wavelength pion modes, i.e. disoriented chiral condensates can be seen in the dilepton spectrum.

# Dilepton Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Low-mass dilepton production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is sensitive to in-medium changes of hadrons and chiral symmetry restoration, but the CERES data for S+Au at 200 GeV per nucleon can be understood within systematic and statistical errors by including pion annihilation alone without any further in-medium modifications; only if one seeks to reproduce the central values of the data must in-medium modifications be invoked.

## Method
The paper reviews calculations of dilepton invariant mass spectra for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies (200 GeV per nucleon), comparing them to CERES collaboration data for S+Au. The calculations consider hadronic decay channels (Dalitz decays of η, Δ, ω, a1; direct decays of ρ, ω, Φ) and heavy-ion-specific channels (pion annihilation, bremsstrahlung from secondary collisions). In-medium effects are evaluated via three mechanisms: screening of the ργ coupling from pion loops, modification of the ρ as a π-π resonance due to changed pion dispersion relations and coupling to delta-hole and nucleon-hole states, and coupling of the ρ to N*(1720)-hole states. For disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs), the dilepton signal from annihilation of a thermal pion with a DCC pion is computed for quench initial conditions.

## Key numbers
- CERES data for S+Au at 200 GeV per nucleon show a considerable deviation from hadronic decay calculations at invariant masses of about 400 MeV.
- For DCC states, the dilepton momentum spectrum at an invariant mass of M = 300 MeV shows a strong enhancement of about a factor of 100 close to twice the pion mass.
- The DCC enhancement is confined to momenta below 300 MeV; if the CERES acceptance cut of p_t ≥ 200 MeV per dilepton could be relaxed to p_t ≥ 100 MeV, a factor of 10 enhancement in the invariant mass spectrum should be visible.

## Context
This review extends the discussion of dilepton measurements from the DLS collaboration at the BEVALAC and the CERES collaboration at the CERN-SPS, focusing on the sensitivity to in-medium hadron changes and chiral symmetry restoration. It builds on the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) that the ρ meson mass scales with the quark condensate, which Li et al. (1996) used to reproduce CERES central points, but notes that current algebra arguments (DEI90) rule out this scaling at low temperatures to order T². The work also references calculations by Rapp, Weise, and Steele on in-medium ρ modifications via delta-hole and nucleon-hole states, and by Friman and Pirner (1997) on N*(1720)-hole coupling. The DCC analysis follows the work of Bjorken and Wilczek on disoriented chiral condensates, and the specific calculations are detailed in Koch, Kluger, Randrup, and Wang (1997). The results enable future experimental searches for DCC signals by relaxing low-momentum cuts in dilepton measurements.
