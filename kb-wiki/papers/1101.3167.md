# Hadron Production in Ultra-relativistic Nuclear Collisions and the QCD Phase Diagram: an Update

> We summarize our current understanding of the connection between the QCD phase line and the chemical freeze-out curve as deduced from thermal analyses of yields of particles produced in central collisions between relativistic nuclei.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out curve extracted from thermal fits to hadron yields in central nucleus-nucleus collisions closely coincides with the QCD phase boundary for baryon chemical potentials below 250 MeV, but for larger values the connection remains speculative and may involve a transition to quarkyonic matter rather than a deconfined quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors compile published results from statistical hadron resonance gas model fits to measured hadron multiplicities in central heavy-ion collisions across a range of center-of-mass energies. They extract the chemical freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB for each energy, then compare the resulting freeze-out curve to lattice QCD predictions for the phase boundary, to lines of constant energy per particle, and to percolation model calculations. They also examine the energy dependence of hadron yield ratios (e.g., K+/π+, Λ/π) and the chemical freeze-out volume.

## Key numbers

* The temperature saturates at an asymptotic value of about 160 MeV for center-of-mass energies above √sNN = 10 GeV.
* At μB = 0, lattice QCD simulations indicate a rapid rise in energy density at a temperature Tc of 155–175 MeV, in close agreement with the chemical freeze-out temperatures at small μB.
* The highest collision energies studied at RHIC give μB ≈ 25 MeV; the LHC point will be at μB ≈ 1 MeV.
* At LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV), the chemical freeze-out volume for central Pb–Pb collisions reaches about 4 times the volume of a Pb nucleus, and the thermal freeze-out volume about 7 times that of a Pb nucleus.
* The curvature of the chemical freeze-out curve in the region μB < 250 MeV is consistent with both the parabolic fit function from Karsch (2010) and a function with twice that slope parameter, within systematic uncertainties.

## Context

This paper updates the authors' earlier work from 1995 (Braun-Munzinger and Stachel, 1995) and extends the comparison of chemical freeze-out data to lattice QCD results from Karsch (2010) and others. The analysis strengthens the argument that chemical freeze-out occurs very close to the QCD phase boundary for small μB, contradicting the suggestion by Karsch (2010) of a dense hadronic phase between freeze-out and the phase boundary. For large μB, the authors speculate that the transition may involve quarkyonic matter, as proposed in their earlier work (quarkyonic, 2010), or a splitting of chiral and deconfinement transitions. The results motivate further experimental exploration at high baryon density by the RHIC beam energy scan, FAIR, and DUBNA programs.
