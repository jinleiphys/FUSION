# Characterization of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider through temperature fluctuations

> We propose to characterize heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies by using fluctuations of energy density and temperature. Temperature fluctuations on an event-by-event basis have been studied both in terms of global temperature of the event, and locally by constructing fluctuation maps in small phase space bins in each event. Global temperature fluctuations provide an estimation of the specific heat of the system. Local temperature fluctuations of the event may be ascribed to the remnants of initial energy density fluctuations. Together these two observables give an insight into the system created in heavy-ion collisions and its evolution. Event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations indeed provide adequate theoretical basis for understanding the origin of the fluctuations. We demonstrate the feasibility of studying global and local temperature fluctuations at the Large Hadron Collider energy by the use of AMPT event generator.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that event-by-event temperature fluctuations, both global and local, can characterize the hot and dense system created in heavy-ion collisions, but the feasibility demonstration relies on the AMPT event generator rather than experimental data, and the extraction of the specific heat depends on the assumption that transverse flow velocity fluctuations are negligible for central collisions.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic calculations with a wounded nucleon profile and lattice-based equation of state for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV to study the time evolution of energy density and temperature fluctuations. They then used the string melting mode of the AMPT event generator to simulate events, extracting effective temperatures from the mean transverse mass of pions within central rapidity and full azimuth, and calculated global temperature fluctuations to obtain the heat capacity and specific heat. Local temperature fluctuations were studied by dividing the y-phi phase space into bins and constructing fluctuation maps for each event.

## Key numbers

- Initial average energy density at tau = 0.14 fm is approximately 168 GeV/fm^3, falling to approximately 20 GeV/fm^3 at tau = 1 fm for central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Initial average temperature at tau = 0.14 fm is approximately 530 MeV, falling to approximately 300 MeV at tau = 1 fm, with freeze-out temperature near 160 MeV
- Bin-to-bin fluctuations at early times are approximately 90% for energy density and approximately 35% for temperature
- Dynamical component of global temperature fluctuation is Delta T_eff = 3.12 MeV, yielding heat capacity C = 927.1
- Specific heat per pion c_v = 0.633, with a range of 0.594 to 0.687 considering beta_T variation between 0.63 and 0.67
- Mean local temperature fluctuation for 6x6 bins in central rapidity is 12.98%, with dynamical component of 7.2% after subtracting statistical component of 10.79%

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations from mean transverse momentum and particle ratio fluctuations (Shuryak, Stephanov, Wilk) to direct temperature fluctuations, and connects to lattice QCD predictions for heat capacity near the phase transition (Gavai and Gupta). The approach enables estimation of specific heat at LHC energies for the first time from model calculations, and the local fluctuation maps provide a basis for power spectrum analysis similar to cosmic microwave background radiation studies (Ajit, Paul). The results are consistent with specific heat values obtained at RHIC energies (Chinese collaboration) and may help locate the QCD critical point when studied as a function of beam energy (STAR collaboration, Gavai).
