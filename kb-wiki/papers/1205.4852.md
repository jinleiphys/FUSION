# Holographic QCD: Past, Present, and Future

> At the dawn of a new theoretical tool based on the AdS/CFT correspondence for nonperturbative aspects of quantum chromodynamics, we give an interim review on the new tool, holographic QCD, with some of its accomplishment. We try to give an A-to-Z picture of the holographic QCD, from string theory to a few selected top-down holographic QCD models with one or two physical applications in each model. We may not attempt to collect diverse results from various holographic QCD model studies.

## Key claim

This paper is an interim review of holographic QCD, presenting an "A-to-Z picture" from string theory to selected top-down models with a few physical applications, but explicitly does not attempt to collect diverse results from various holographic QCD model studies.

## Method

The authors first provide a pedagogical introduction to string theory essentials, including Einstein gravity, point particle dynamics and quantization, and p-brane actions. They then review three specific top-down holographic QCD models: the D3/D7 model (applied to quark-gluon plasma probes), the D4/D8 model (with discussion of holographic baryons and nuclear forces), and the D4/D6 model (applied to nuclear symmetry energy and nuclear-to-strange matter transition). The paper concludes with a brief survey of recent results from various holographic studies.

## Key numbers

- The Schwarzschild radius is defined as \(r_{\rm S}^{D-3} = \frac{16\pi G^{(D)}_{\rm N} M}{(D-2)V_{S^{(D-2)}}}\) for a D-dimensional spacetime.
- The low energy regime for perturbative gravity is given by \(\omega \ll \frac{1}{\kappa^{\frac{2}{D-2}}}\), where \(\kappa = \sqrt{8\pi G_{\rm N}^{(D)}}\).
- The Newton potential for a point particle of mass \(M\) in D dimensions is \(V(r) = -\frac{8\pi G_{\rm N}^{(D)}M}{(D-2)V_{S^{(D-2)}}} \frac{1}{r^{D-3}}\).
- The D-dimensional Planck length is defined as \(l_{\rm P}^{(D)} = (8\pi G_{\rm N}^{(D)})^{1/(D-2)}\).

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical tradition of introducing holographic QCD to non-experts, building on the foundational AdS/CFT correspondence established by Maldacena (1997), Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998), and Witten (1998). It specifically covers prototypical top-down models including the D3/D7 system, the D4/D8 model of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004), and the D4/D6 model, while referring readers to Erdmenger et al. (2007), McGreevy (2009), Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2011), and de Teramond and Brodsky (2012) for broader coverage, and to Kim and Shin (2011) for a summary focused on bottom-up models. The paper enables newcomers to understand the string theory foundations necessary to engage with holographic QCD literature, though it does not provide an exhaustive survey of results.
