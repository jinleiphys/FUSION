# A model for A=3 antinuclei production in proton-nucleus collisions

> A simple coalescence model based on the same diagrammatic approach of antimatter production in hadronic collisions as used previously for antideuterons is used here for the hadroproduction of mass 3 antinuclei. It is shown that the model is able to reproduce the existing experimental data on Tbar and 3hebar production without any additional parameter.

# A model for A=3 antinuclei production in proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The diagrammatic coalescence model previously applied to antideuteron production can reproduce the existing experimental data on triton and helium-3 antinuclei production without any additional free parameter, provided the inclusive antiproton production cross sections are well known. However, the agreement with the Serpukhov data required using a renormalized antiproton parameterization that is not consistent with the full body of antiproton data, introducing a puzzle about the model's consistency.

## Method
The authors generalized a diagrammatic coalescence model from antideuteron to A=3 antinuclei production in proton-nucleus collisions. The model expresses the coalescence momentum in terms of the slope parameter of the inclusive nucleon production spectrum and the three-nucleon wave function, using the wave function of Muether (1984). Threshold effects and anisotropy of angular distributions were incorporated via a phase-space correction factor R(x) computed using the CERN library program GENBOD. The antiproton production cross sections needed as input were taken from fits to existing data, and the pion production cross sections needed to extract experimental values from the CERN data were parameterized using a functional form fitted to data from Baker (1974).

## Key numbers
- Serpukhov experiment: 70 GeV/c proton beam on aluminium target; one data point for $\overline{t}$ at 25 GeV/c and 0°, one for $\overline{^3He}$ at 20 GeV/c and 27 mrad.
- CERN experiment (WA33): four experimental points for $\overline{t}$ and eight for $\overline{^3He}$ in proton-beryllium collisions at 200, 210, and 240 GeV/c at 0°.
- The factor of about 2 between antiproton cross sections predicted by different parameterizations translates into a factor of about 10 for the $\overline{t}$ cross section due to the approximately cubic dependence on the antiproton cross section.
- Pion production fit parameters: C1=0.94, C2=1.88, C3=7.05, C4=1.69 (dimensionless, from fitting data at 200 and 300 GeV/c).

## Context
This work extends the diagrammatic coalescence approach of Kondratyuk (1992) and Duperray (2003) from antideuteron to A=3 antinuclei production, providing the first microscopic calculation of this cross section. It contrasts with the standard coalescence model used by Chardonnet (1997) for $\overline{^3He}$ production, which treated the coalescence momentum p0 as a free parameter fitted to antideuteron data. The model enables predictions for antinuclei fluxes in cosmic rays that could be tested by the AMS and PAMELA experiments. The limited experimental data (only two experiments from Serpukhov and CERN) and the need for extrapolated antiproton cross sections highlight the need for new measurements, particularly in proton-proton collisions where no data exist.
