# Global $α$-decay study based on the mass table of the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> The $α$-decay energies ($Q_α$) are systematically investigated with the nuclear masses for $10 \leq Z \leq 120$ isotopes obtained by the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the covariant density functional PC-PK1, and compared with available experimental values. It is found that the $α$-decay energies deduced from the RCHB results present similar pattern as those from available experiments. Owing to the large predicted $Q_α$ values ($\geq$ 4 MeV), many undiscovered heavy nuclei in the proton-rich side and super-heavy nuclei may have large possibilities for $α$-decay. The influence of nuclear shell structure on $α$-decay energies is also analysed.

## Key claim

The RCHB mass table with the PC-PK1 functional reproduces the global pattern of experimental α-decay energies, but the agreement is schematic rather than detailed; the authors emphasize that the aim is to investigate the evolution of Qα rather than to perform a precise comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the covariant density functional PC-PK1 to compute binding energies for 9035 bound nuclei with \(10 \leq Z \leq 120\), assuming spherical symmetry. From these binding energies, they derived α-decay energies \(Q_\alpha\) via \(Q_\alpha = E_B(Z-2,N-2) + E_B(2,2) - E_B(Z,N)\) and compared the results with experimental values from the AME2012 mass table. The RCHB equations were solved in coordinate space in a box of radius 20 fm with a step of 0.1 fm, using a density-dependent delta pairing force with strength \(V_0 = 685.0\ \mathrm{MeV\cdot fm^3}\).

## Key numbers

- Among 9035 predicted bound nuclei, 3703 have positive \(Q_\alpha\) values: 1629 with \(Q_\alpha < 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), 1299 with \(Q_\alpha\) between 4 and 8 MeV, 734 with \(Q_\alpha\) between 8 and 12 MeV, and 41 with \(Q_\alpha > 12\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The lightest nucleus predicted to have positive \(Q_\alpha\) is \(^{20}\mathrm{Ne}\) with \(Q_\alpha = 0.14\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Experimentally, 1067 nuclei have positive \(Q_\alpha\) values from AME2012; 719 nuclei are observed with α-decay radioactivity, and \(^{187}\mathrm{Re}\) has the smallest observed \(Q_\alpha\) at 1.66 MeV.
- About 70% of the 719 observed α-decay nuclei have \(Q_\alpha > 4.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\); 68 nuclei with \(Q_\alpha > 4.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) are not observed to decay by α in the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of the RCHB mass table (Xia 2015) to α-decay energies, following earlier mass-table calculations using the same framework that predicted extended neutron drip-lines due to continuum coupling (Qu 2013). The results are compared with experimental data from the AME2012 mass table (Audi 2012) and with prior theoretical mass models such as FRDM (Moller 1995), WS3 (Wang 2011), HFB-21 (Goriely 2010), and TMA (Geng 2005). The study enables future investigation of cluster decay energies and the role of deformation via the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc).
