# Survival of Back-to-Back Correlations for Finite Expanding Fireballs

> Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs are predicted to appear if hot and dense hadronic matter is formed in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The BBC correlations are related to in-medium mass-modification and squeezing of the quanta involved. The formalisms are analogous in the bosonic (bBBC) and in the fermionic (fBBC) cases. Both the bBBC and the fBBC are positive and unlimited correlations of similar strength. For testing the survival and the intensity of the effect in a more realistic situation, we study the back-to-back correlations for mass-modification occurring in a finite sized, thermalized medium. For illustration, we consider a non-relativistically expanding fireball with finite emission time. We show that the BBC signal indeed survives the expansion and flow effects, with sufficient magnitude to be observed at RHIC.

## Key claim

Back-to-back correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs survive in finite, expanding fireballs with sufficient magnitude to be observed at RHIC, provided the mass-modification region is large enough and the emission duration is short; the signal is significantly suppressed by finite emission time but less sensitive to finite system size.

## Method

The authors model a non-relativistically expanding, spherically symmetric fireball with a smeared freeze-out distribution of finite duration, using a hydrodynamical parametrization from Csorgo (2000). They compute the squeezed correlation function for boson-antiboson pairs (specifically φφ pairs) via Bogoliubov-Valatin transformations, assuming a Gaussian profile for the mass-shifted region and a Boltzmann limit for the in-medium distribution, in the weak-flow, small-mass-shift approximation.

## Key numbers

- BBC signal is significant for transverse momentum \(p_T < 2\) GeV/c (from prior work, cited as context).
- A smearing of the freeze-out time by \(\Delta \tau \sim 2\) fm/c severely reduces the BBC signal magnitude compared to sudden freeze-out.
- The BBC correlation function shows pronounced maxima when the in-medium mass \(m_\star\) is approximately equal to the vacuum mass \(m\) (i.e., \(m \approx m_\star\)).
- The BBC magnitude increases proportionally to the size of the mass-shifting region (radius \(R_s\)) relative to the total fireball radius \(R\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of back-to-back correlations for boson-antiboson pairs (Ann. Phys. 1999) and fermion-antifermion pairs (Padula et al., 2001) by incorporating finite system size and collective expansion, which had not been studied previously. It uses the non-relativistic hydrodynamical model of Csorgo (2000) and the freeze-out formalism of Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988). The results enable experimental searches for squeezed BBC signals at RHIC, though the authors note that future work must include model-based mass shifts, more realistic flow calculations, and experimental acceptance constraints.
