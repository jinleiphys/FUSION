# Neck configuration of Cm and Cf nuclei in the fission state within relativistic mean field formalism

> A correlation is established between the neutron multiplicity and the neutrons number in the fission state of Curium and Californium isotopes within a microscopic study using relativistic mean field formalism. The study includes the isotopes of Cm and Cf nuclei near the valley of stability, and hence is likely to play an important role in the artificial synthesis of superheavy nuclei. The static fission path, the neutron$-$proton asymmetry, the evolution of the neck and their composition in terms of nucleon numbers are also estimated. We find a maximum ratio for average neutron to proton density, which is about $1.6$ in the breakdown of the liquid$-$drop picture for $^{248}$Cm and $^{252}$Cf. A strong dependence of the neutron$-$proton asymmetry on the neutron multiplicity in an isotopic chain is also observed.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a correlation between neutron multiplicity and the number of neutrons in the fission state of Curium and Californium isotopes, but this correlation is demonstrated only for nuclei near the valley of stability and under the assumption of reflection symmetry, which limits the model to symmetric fission.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* force parameter set and constant gap BCS pairing to calculate potential energy surfaces, density distributions, and neck properties for even-even isotopes of Cm and Cf. Constrained solutions were obtained by minimizing H' = H0 - λQ2 over a large harmonic oscillator basis (up to 24 major shells for fission states) to map the static fission path from ground state to the scission configuration.

## Key numbers

- Maximum ratio of average neutron to proton density in the neck region is about 1.6 for 248Cm and 252Cf
- First barrier height for 242Cm: 7.92 MeV (empirical value: 6.65 MeV)
- Second barrier height for 242Cm: 5.76 MeV (empirical value: 5.10 MeV)
- First barrier height for 250Cf: 8.06 MeV (empirical value: 5.60 MeV)
- Second barrier height for 250Cf: 2.83 MeV (empirical value: 3.80 MeV)
- Neck length for all Cm isotopes: 4.56 fm; for all Cf isotopes: 4.52 fm
- Average neutron-to-proton number ratio in the neck region: 1.02 for all isotopes studied
- Ground state quadrupole deformation β2 for 242Cm: 0.287; fission state β2: 5.036
- Ground state quadrupole deformation β2 for 248Cf: 0.288; fission state β2: 4.973
- Binding energy of neck configuration lies approximately 15 MeV below the respective ground state

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of fission using the RMF formalism (Bhuyan 2011, 2015; Lu 2014) and compares with macroscopic-microscopic models such as the Finite-Range Droplet Model (Moller 1995, 1997) and Hartree-Fock + BCS (Goriely 2001). The neck configurations found are similar to those from constrained calculations with the Gogny interaction (Dubreuil 2008) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (Bonaccorso 2006). The results enable further investigation of the role of neck composition in r-process nucleosynthesis and the synthesis of superheavy nuclei.
