# Nonstandard neutrino interactions and transition magnetic moments

> We constrain generic nonstandard neutrino interactions with existing experimental data on neutrino transition magnetic moments and derive strong bounds on tensorial couplings of neutrinos to charged fermions. We also discuss how some of these tensorial couplings can be constrained by other experiments, e.g., on neutrino-electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives constraints on nonstandard neutrino interactions (NSIs) by using existing experimental bounds on neutrino transition magnetic moments, showing that tensor-type NSI couplings can be bounded at the loop level even when direct scattering constraints are weak.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop contribution of tensor-type dimension-6 four-fermion operators (of the form \((\bar\nu_\beta \sigma_{\mu\nu} \nu_\alpha)(\bar f \sigma^{\mu\nu} f)\)) to neutrino transition magnetic moments. They then equate the resulting expressions to the strongest astrophysical bound on neutrino magnetic moments (\(\mu_\nu < 3\times10^{-12}~\mu_B\) from globular cluster red giant cooling) to extract upper limits on the NSI couplings \(\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^f\) for each charged fermion species \(f\). They also compare these bounds with constraints from neutrino-electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical bound on all diagonal and transition neutrino magnetic moments: \(\mu_\nu < 3\times10^{-12}~\mu_B\) (from globular cluster red giant energy loss).
- Best terrestrial laboratory bound on diagonal \(\mu_{ee}\): \(\mu_{\bar\nu_e} < 2.9\times10^{-11}~\mu_B\) at 90% C.L. (GEMMA experiment).
- Global fit limit on transition moments: \(\mu_{12}, \mu_{23}, \mu_{31} < 1.8\times10^{-10}~\mu_B\) at 90% C.L.
- For \(M=1\) TeV, the derived upper bounds on tensor NSI couplings include: \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^e| < 3.9\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^\mu| < 3.0\times10^{-2}\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^\tau| < 2.6\times10^{-3}\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^u| < 0.49\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^d| < 0.49\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^s| < 3.3\times10^{-2}\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^c| < 1.7\times10^{-3}\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^b| < 1.2\times10^{-3}\), \(|\epsilon_{\alpha\beta}^t| < 4.8\times10^{-5}\).
- From TEXONO \(\bar\nu_e\)-\(e\) scattering data: \(|\epsilon^{e}_{e\beta}| < 6.6\) at 90% C.L. (rescaled to GEMMA: \(|\epsilon^{e}_{e\beta}| < 2.7\) at 90% C.L.).
- Planned coherent scattering experiments could reach sensitivity \(|\epsilon^{u,d}_{e\beta}| < 0.2~(M/1\, \text{TeV})^2\) at 90% C.L.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonstandard neutrino interactions beyond the usual vector and axial-vector operators (commonly considered in analyses by Kopp (2007), Ohlsson (2012), and others) to include tensor-type interactions, which are often neglected. The derived bounds from magnetic moments are complementary to constraints from neutrino-electron scattering (TEXONO, GEMMA) and neutrino-nucleus scattering (Barranco (2011)), and are particularly relevant for models with scalar leptoquarks (Povarov (2007)) and R-parity-violating supersymmetry (Gozdz (2012)). The results enable future experiments, such as planned coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering measurements, to further tighten limits on tensor NSI couplings.
