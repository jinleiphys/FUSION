# Anisotropic distribution of nucleon participating in elliptical flow

> Using the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, we study the effect of charge asymmetry and isospin dependent cross-section on $\frac{dN}{d(<Cos2ϕ>)}$ and $\frac{dN}{p_tdp_t}$. Simulations have been carried out for the reactions of $^{124}X_{m}+^{124}X_{m}$, where m = (47, 50 and 59) and $^{40}S_{16}+^{40}S_{16}$. Our study shows that these parameters depend strongly on the isospin of cross-section and charge asymmetry. The distribution of nucleons and fragments is not symmetric around the beam axis.

## Key claim

Using the IQMD model, the authors claim that the distribution of nucleons and fragments contributing to elliptical flow is not symmetric around the beam axis, with the number of particles emitted in-plane being larger than those emitted out-of-plane; however, this asymmetry depends on the isospin of the cross-section and the charge asymmetry of the system, and the effect is more pronounced for light-mass fragments (LMFs) than for free nucleons.

## Method

Simulations were performed with the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for reactions of \(^{124}X_{m}+^{124}X_{m}\) (with m = 47, 50, and 59) and \(^{40}S_{16}+^{40}S_{16}\). The phase space was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method to identify free nucleons (A=1), light-mass fragments (LMFs, 2 ≤ A ≤ 4), and intermediate-mass fragments (IMFs, 5 ≤ A ≤ A_tot/6). The azimuthal angle dependence of \(\frac{dN}{d(\langle Cos2\phi \rangle)}\) and the transverse momentum dependence of \(\frac{dN}{p_t dp_t}\) were computed to study the anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(^{124}Ag_{47}+^{124}Ag_{47}\) at E = 100 MeV/nucleon, the peak in \(\frac{dN}{d(\langle Cos2\phi \rangle)}\) is more pronounced at \(2\phi = 0\) than at \(2\phi = \pi\), indicating more particles emitted in-plane than out-of-plane.
- The transition energy from negative to positive elliptic flow is cited as \(E_{trans} = 4A\) GeV from Pinkenburg et al., and the authors show that at E = 236 MeV/nucleon the phase-space distribution becomes spherical (isotropic) for the reaction \(^{124}Ag_{47}+^{124}Ag_{47}\).
- For the reaction \(^{124}Sn_{50}+^{124}Sn_{50}\) (N/Z = 1.48) and \(^{40}S_{16}+^{40}S_{16}\) (N/Z = 1.5), the energy dependence of \(\frac{dN}{d(\langle Cos2\phi \rangle)}\) shows that out-of-plane emission is more for the Sn system, while in-plane emission is more for the S system.
- The value of \(\frac{dN}{p_t dp_t}\) is larger for the neutron-rich system \(^{124}Ag_{47}+^{124}Ag_{47}\) than for the neutron-deficient system \(^{124}Pr_{59}+^{124}Pr_{59}\) at E = 100 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends studies of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\), which was previously measured by Pinkenburg et al. (2000) for Au+Au at 2–8 GeV/nucleon and by Lukasik et al. (2004) for incident energies 40–150 MeV/nucleon. The authors use the IQMD model (Hartnack et al., 1998) to investigate the asymmetry of nucleon and fragment distributions in momentum space, which had not been explicitly examined before. The results enable further studies of how charge asymmetry and isospin-dependent cross-sections affect the shape of the elliptic flow distribution, and may help refine models of the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections.
