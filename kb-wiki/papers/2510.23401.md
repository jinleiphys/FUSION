# Determination of the Muon Lifetime in $^{76}$Se with the MONUMENT experiment

> Ordinary muon capture provides a benchmark for the nuclear physics models of neutrinoless double beta decay under comparable momentum transfer conditions. The total capture strength defines the lifetime of the muonic atom. The muon lifetime in $^{76}$Se, the daughter nucleus of $^{76}$Ge, was determined with improved accuracy by the MONUMENT collaboration, using an array of high-purity germanium detectors and a set of scintillator counters at the $π$E1 muon beam line of the Paul Scherrer Institute. The new value of (135.1 $\pm$ 0.5) ns agrees with phenomenological calculations based on the quasiparticle random phase approximation with unquenched axial-vector coupling.

## Key claim

The MONUMENT collaboration measured the muon lifetime in ⁷⁶Se with improved accuracy, obtaining (135.1 ± 0.5) ns, which agrees with phenomenological calculations based on the quasiparticle random phase approximation with unquenched axial-vector coupling. The abstract glosses over that this result corrects a previous measurement by Zinatulina (2018) that suffered from experimental shortcomings with the timing instrumentation.

## Method

Negative muons with momentum of about 38 MeV/c from the πE1 beam line at the Paul Scherrer Institute were stopped in a 2 g selenium powder target enriched to 99.68% of ⁷⁶Se. The muon lifetime was determined from time differences between muon signals in plastic scintillator counters and subsequent gamma-ray emissions detected by an array of eight high-purity germanium detectors, using two independent data acquisition systems (ALPACA and MIDAS) for cross-validation. Time profiles of gamma-ray lines from ordinary muon capture on ⁷⁶Se were extracted and fitted with an exponential decay convolved with a prompt timing response obtained from muonic X-ray lines.

## Key numbers

- Muon lifetime in ⁷⁶Se: (135.1 ± 0.5) ns, measured with the MONUMENT experiment at PSI
- Target enrichment: 99.68% of ⁷⁶Se, with chemical purity of 99.96%
- Total acquisition time: approximately 125 h in MIDAS and 126 h in ALPACA
- Stopped muon rate: on the order of 10⁴ per second
- Timing resolutions of the HPGe detectors: ranging between 20 ns and 100 ns
- 70 statistically independent lifetime values obtained: 25 in ALPACA and 45 in MIDAS

## Context

This measurement provides a benchmark for nuclear physics models used to compute nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay of ⁷⁶Ge, which is searched for by experiments like LEGEND. The work corrects a previous measurement by Zinatulina (2018) that suffered from instrumentation problems with timing information. The result enables improved comparisons between theoretical calculations of muon capture rates and experimental data, testing the quasiparticle random phase approximation approach under momentum transfer conditions comparable to neutrinoless double beta decay.
