# Ab initio coupled-cluster approach to nuclear structure with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We perform coupled-cluster calculations for the doubly magic nuclei 4He, 16O, 40Ca and 48Ca, for neutron-rich isotopes of oxygen and fluorine, and employ "bare" and secondary renormalized nucleon-nucleon interactions. For the nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory at order next-to-next-to-next-to leading order, we find that the coupled-cluster approximation including triples corrections binds nuclei within 0.4 MeV per nucleon compared to data. We employ interactions from a resolution-scale dependent similarity renormalization group transformations and assess the validity of power counting estimates in medium-mass nuclei. We find that the missing contributions due to three-nucleon forces are consistent with these estimates. For the unitary correlator model potential, we find a slow convergence with respect to increasing the size of the model space. For the G-matrix approach, we find a weak dependence of ground-state energies on the starting energy combined with a rather slow convergence with respect to increasing model spaces. We also analyze the center-of-mass problem and present a practical and efficient solution.

## Key claim

The authors find that the coupled-cluster approximation including triples corrections binds nuclei within 0.4 MeV per nucleon compared to data when using the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO, but this agreement depends on the assumption that missing three-nucleon forces account for the remaining scale dependence observed when varying the momentum cutoff of the interaction.

## Method

The authors performed spherical coupled-cluster calculations at the CCSD and Lambda-CCSD(T) levels for doubly magic nuclei (4He, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca) and neutron-rich oxygen and fluorine isotopes, using both "bare" chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interactions and interactions renormalized via similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformations, low-momentum interactions (Vlowk), G-matrix methods, and the unitary correlator method (UCOM). They employed model spaces of up to 20 major oscillator shells and used a Hartree-Fock basis, with the center-of-mass problem addressed by demonstrating approximate factorization of the wave function and determining the effective center-of-mass frequency.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of doubly magic nuclei computed with the chiral N3LO interaction and Lambda-CCSD(T) agree with experimental data within 0.4 MeV per nucleon
- For 16O with a low-momentum interaction (smooth cutoff lambda = 1.8 fm^-1 derived from chiral N3LO), the ground-state energy varies by only about 15 keV when the center-of-mass Hamiltonian parameter beta is varied from 0 to 1
- The center-of-mass expectation value E_cm(tilde-omega) for 16O with the bare N3LO interaction is approximately -10 keV, indicating good factorization
- The effective center-of-mass frequency for 16O stays approximately constant at hbar-tilde-omega approximately 20 MeV across a range of underlying oscillator frequencies

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen et al. (2008) on medium-mass nuclei such as 40Ca, 48Ca, and 48Ni, and by Hagen et al. (2009) on oxygen isotopes, providing a systematic comparison of multiple renormalization approaches (SRG, Vlowk, G-matrix, UCOM) within a single framework. The results enable future studies of neutron-rich isotopes and continuum effects using Gamow basis expansions, as demonstrated in subsequent work by Hagen et al. (2010), and provide benchmarks for assessing the role of three-nucleon forces in medium-mass nuclei.
