# Configurational entropy and the $N^*(1440)$ Roper resonance in QCD

> The electroexcitation of the ${\cal N}^*(1440)$ Roper resonance, which defines the first radially excited state of the nucleon, is examined within the soft-wall AdS/QCD model. Such excited Fock states are characterized by the leading three-quark component, which determines the main properties of Roper resonance. The differential configurational entropy (DCE) was used in the nuclear interaction with a gauge vector field for ${\cal N}^*(1440)$ transition. Comparing the main results with the recent data of the CLAS Collaboration at JLab shows a good agreement on the accuracy of the computed data.

## Key claim

The differential configurational entropy (DCE) method, applied within the soft-wall AdS/QCD model, can determine the adjustable parameters describing the nucleon and the N*(1440) Roper resonance by finding the global minimum of the DCE, which corresponds to the most configurationally stable state; the resulting parameters agree with those from prior AdS/QCD fits to helicity amplitude data to within 1.1% to 6.8%, depending on the parameter.

## Method

The author computed the differential configurational entropy (DCE) from the energy-momentum tensor of the bulk fermion fields in the soft-wall AdS/QCD model, using the Fourier transform of the energy density along the holographic coordinate z. The DCE was minimized as a function of the adjustable coefficients c_tau^N and c_tau^R that weight the contributions of Fock states with twist dimensions tau = 3, 4, and 5 for the nucleon and the Roper resonance, respectively, while fixing the scale parameter kappa = 383 MeV and the experimental Roper mass of 1440 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The global minimum of the DCE for the nucleon occurs at DCE = 767.503 nat, at parameter values c_3^N = 1.264 and c_4^N = 0.169, agreeing with the prior AdS/QCD soft-wall model values (c_3^N = 1.25, c_4^N = 0.16) within 1.1% and 5.6%, respectively.
* The global minimum of the DCE for the Roper resonance occurs at DCE = 17.639 nat, at parameter values c_3^R = 0.803 and c_4^R = -0.171, agreeing with the prior AdS/QCD soft-wall model values (c_3^R = 0.78, c_4^R = -0.16) within 2.9% and 6.8%, respectively.
* The scale parameter kappa is fixed at 383 MeV, and the experimental Roper mass is M_R^exp = 1440 MeV.
* The nucleon mass is M_N^exp = 938.27 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DCE approach previously applied to the N*(1535) resonance by da Rocha (2022) to the N*(1440) Roper resonance, using the same soft-wall AdS/QCD formalism developed by Gutsche (2012) for baryon structure. The results demonstrate that DCE minimization can independently reproduce the adjustable parameters obtained from fitting to CLAS Collaboration helicity amplitude data, suggesting that configurational entropy provides a criterion for selecting the most stable Fock state composition without direct reliance on experimental cross-section fits. This enables future applications of DCE to other nucleon resonances and electromagnetic transitions within the AdS/QCD framework.
