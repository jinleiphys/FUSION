# Vacuum stabilized by anomalous magnetic moment

> An analytical result for Euler-Heisenberg effective action, valid for electron spin $g-$factor $|g|<2$, was extended to the domain $|g|>2$ via discovered periodicity of the effective action. This allows for a simplified computation of vacuum instability modified by the electrons measured $g=2.002319$. We find a strong suppression of vacuum decay into electron positron pairs when magnetic fields are dominant. The result is reminiscent of mass catalysis by magnetic fields.

# Vacuum stabilized by anomalous magnetic moment

## Key claim

The electron's anomalous magnetic moment (g = 2.002319) strongly suppresses vacuum decay into electron-positron pairs when magnetic fields are dominant over electric fields, an effect that can overwhelm the conventional enhancement of pair production by magnetic fields seen at g = 2. This suppression is only valid for magnetic field strengths below |eB| < m²/|g/2 - 1| ≈ 862 m², beyond which the Landau energy spectrum becomes imaginary and self-adjointness is lost.

## Method

The authors extended the Euler-Heisenberg effective action, previously valid only for |g| < 2, to the domain |g| > 2 by exploiting a discovered periodicity in g: a shift g → g + 4k leaves the summed Landau orbit spectrum unchanged, allowing any g value to be periodically reset into the principal domain -2 < g < 2. Using this periodic reset, they computed the imaginary part of the effective action for combined electric and magnetic fields via a meromorphic expansion and residue summation, then evaluated the ratio of pair production rates between g = 2.002319 and g = 2.

## Key numbers

- The electron's measured g-factor is g = 2.002319, with the principal domain for the effective action being -2 < g < 2 after periodic reset.
- The condition for valid magnetic field strengths is |eB| < m²/|g/2 - 1| ≈ 862 m², where m is the electron mass.
- For a pure electric field, the difference between g = 2.002319 and g = 2 is less than 0.1% for electric field strength a < 60 m²/e (in units of the Euler-Heisenberg critical field), and less than 1% for 60 < a < 100 m²/e.
- At field ratios b/a ∼ 10³, the vacuum decay is suppressed by a factor of approximately 10⁻³ relative to the g = 2 result.
- Pair production is maximized for b/a ∼ 10², where b is the magnetic field parameter and a is the electric field parameter in units of the Euler-Heisenberg critical field.

## Context

This work extends prior Euler-Heisenberg effective action studies that assumed g = 2 (Schwinger 1951) and earlier extensions to |g| < 2 (O'Connell 1968, Dittrich 1977, Kruglov 2001, Labun 2012). The periodicity in g was previously identified by Rafelski (2012). The suppression effect is reminiscent of magnetic mass catalysis (Gusynin 1995, Ferrer 2000, Shovkovy 2012) in that it can be described by a modification of particle mass induced by the magnetic field. The results enable future computation of two-loop effective action incorporating field-dependent g and mass corrections, relevant for understanding QED vacuum structure in magnetars and heavy ion collisions.
