# Including off-diagonal anisotropies in anisotropic hydrodynamics

> In this paper we present a method for efficiently including the effects of off-diagonal local rest frame momentum anisotropies in leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics. The method relies on diagonalization of the space-like block of the anisotropy tensor and allows one to reduce the necessary moments of the distribution function in the off-diagonal case to a linear combination of diagonal-anisotropy integrals. Once reduced to diagonal-anisotropy integrals, the results can be computed efficiently using techniques described previously in the literature. We present a general framework for how to accomplish this and provide examples for off-diagonal anisotropy moments entering into the energy-momentum tensor and viscous update equations which emerge when performing anisotropic pressure matching.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for efficiently including off-diagonal local rest frame momentum anisotropies in leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics by diagonalizing the space-like block of the anisotropy tensor, thereby reducing off-diagonal moment integrals to linear combinations of diagonal-anisotropy integrals that can be computed using previously developed techniques.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the real symmetric 3x3 matrix κ (constructed from the anisotropy tensor ξ^{ij} and the parameter Φ) using a unitary matrix A whose columns are the eigenvectors of κ, transforming the momentum integration variables to a rotated frame where the anisotropy tensor becomes diagonal. In this rotated frame, the distribution function takes the same form as the diagonal-anisotropy case, allowing all necessary moment integrals (for the energy-momentum tensor components and the viscous update equations) to be expressed as linear combinations of previously tabulated Q-functions that depend only on diagonal anisotropies. The method is demonstrated for the energy-momentum tensor components and for the shear and bulk viscous equations derived from anisotropic pressure matching in the relaxation-time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The Q-functions used for diagonal anisotropies can be approximated by Taylor expansions to 12th order in δ̃ = α̃ − α̃_iso around multiple expansion points (Appendix B of Alqahtani (2017))
- The method reduces the problem from evaluating complicated three-dimensional integrals on demand to computing linear combinations of diagonal-anisotropy integrals
- For the energy-momentum tensor, out of 9 possible T^{ij} values there are only 6 unique terms that must be calculated
- For the four-index function F^{ijkl}, out of 81 possible values there are only 15 unique terms that must be calculated

## Context

This work extends the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) formalism, which previously only included diagonal (elliptical) anisotropies in phenomenological applications (reviewed by Alqahtani (2017)), to include off-diagonal components ξ^{ij} with i ≠ j. The method enables complete treatment of the shear viscous tensor π^{ij} in the local rest frame, which is necessary for realistic simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The diagonalization technique builds on the efficient evaluation of diagonal-anisotropy integrals developed by Alqahtani (2017) and the anisotropic pressure matching framework of Tinti (2015) and Molnar (2016), and allows implementation of aHydro codes that can use advanced numerical techniques for solving the partial differential equations.
