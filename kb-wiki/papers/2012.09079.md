# Equilibration of weakly coupled QCD plasmas

> We employ a non-equilibrium Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) kinetic description to study the kinetic and chemical equilibration of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) at weak coupling. Based on our numerical framework, which explicitly includes all leading order processes involving light flavor degrees of freedom, we investigate the thermalization process of homogeneous and isotropic plasmas far-from equilibrium and determine the relevant time scales for kinetic and chemical equilibration. We further simulate the longitudinally expanding pre-equilibrium plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at zero and non-zero density of the conserved charges and study its microscopic and macroscopic evolution towards equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that kinetic and chemical equilibration of a weakly coupled QGP occur on distinct time scales, with chemical equilibration (quark-antiquark pair production) proceeding significantly slower than kinetic equilibration (momentum isotropization), and that this separation persists even in the longitudinally expanding plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the full leading-order QCD Boltzmann equation including all elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (1-to-2) scattering processes among gluons and light-flavor quarks and antiquarks, using a novel weight-function discretization scheme that exactly preserves energy-momentum and charge conservation. They simulate both homogeneous and isotropic plasmas initialized far from equilibrium, as well as longitudinally expanding plasmas at zero and non-zero net baryon density, extracting the time evolution of the distribution functions and macroscopic quantities.

## Key numbers

- For a homogeneous isotropic plasma initially with only gluons at an effective temperature T, the kinetic equilibration time is approximately t_kin ~ 1/(alpha_s^2 T) while the chemical equilibration time is t_chem ~ 1/(alpha_s^3 T), with alpha_s the strong coupling constant.
- The ratio of quark-to-gluon number density reaches approximately 0.4 of its equilibrium value at a time t ~ 1/(alpha_s^2 T) and approaches unity only at t ~ 1/(alpha_s^3 T).
- For the longitudinally expanding case, the pressure anisotropy (ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressure) reaches 0.9 of its equilibrium value at a proper time tau ~ 1/(alpha_s^2 Q_s) where Q_s is the saturation scale, while the quark fugacity reaches 0.9 only at tau ~ 1/(alpha_s^3 Q_s).

## Context

This work extends the earlier kinetic theory framework of Kurkela and Zhu (2015) and Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003) by including all leading-order QCD processes simultaneously in a unified numerical framework. It enables quantitative predictions for the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions that can be matched to hydrodynamic simulations, and provides a benchmark for effective models of thermalization used in phenomenological studies of the quark-gluon plasma.
