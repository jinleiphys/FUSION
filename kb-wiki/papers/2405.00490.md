# A first-order deconfinement phase transition in the early universe and gravitational waves

> We clarify the conditions of the cosmic quantum chromodynamics (QCD) first-order phase transition in the early universe by carefully distinguishing the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions. While the chiral one with light quarks at zero chemical potential is unlikely to be first order based on the recent lattice QCD calculations, the latter one can be naturally extended with one extra rolling scalar to be first order. The argument is also valid for the dark QCD theory with arbitrary $N_c$ with a wide range of phase transition temperatures, which can be from hundreds of MeV up to beyond TeV. Notably, here we derive the general formula for the deconfinement phase transition potential of SU($N_c$) gauge theory characterized by the Polyakov loop. With the effective potential in hand, the gravitational wave spectrum is then determined via the sound shell model, which then enables us to give for the first time the quantitative analysis of the gravitational wave signals coming from the QCD deconfinement phase transition and awaits the check from future space interferometers.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a first-order deconfinement phase transition in the early universe, characterized by the Polyakov loop, can be naturally induced through a scalar field coupled to the gluon sector, in contrast to the chiral phase transition which requires large chemical potential and is unlikely to be first order at zero chemical potential based on recent lattice QCD calculations. The claim holds for SU(\(N_c\)) gauge theories with \(N_c \geq 3\) and phase transition temperatures ranging from hundreds of MeV to beyond TeV.

## Method

The authors derive a general formula for the effective potential of the deconfinement phase transition for SU(\(N_c\)) gauge theory using the Polyakov loop as the order parameter, incorporating a gluon effective mass generated either by a scalar field coupling or by the gauge coupling itself. They compute the gravitational wave spectrum via the sound shell model, solving the bounce equation for the Euclidean action to determine the percolation temperature and the parameters \(\alpha\) (trace anomaly normalized to radiation energy density) and \(\beta\) (inverse time scale of the transition). The analysis covers \(N_c = 3, 5, 10, 25, 50\) and assumes a relativistic bubble wall velocity in the detonation regime.

## Key numbers

- The first-order phase transition for SU(3) occurs at a critical ratio \(m_g/T_c = 2.75\) (for \(d=4\) dimensions); for SU(2) the transition is second order at \(m_g/T_c = 2.97\).
- The condition for a first-order phase transition in the new Yang-Mills coupling is \(\alpha_{\mathrm{new}}(T_c) N_c \sim 5.45\).
- For \(N_c = 3\), the product \(g_* \alpha = 0.65\) and \(\beta/H = 1.36 \times 10^{-5}\); for \(N_c = 50\), \(g_* \alpha = 197.74\) and \(\beta/H = 1.66 \times 10^{-5}\).
- The peak frequency of gravitational waves is set to \(10^{-4}\) Hz as a benchmark, with the strength \(\Omega_\text{GW}\) remaining unchanged when the temperature is shifted.
- The latent heat scales as \(\sim T^4\); for a benchmark temperature of 1 GeV, \(\Delta L = 4.5 \times 10^8\) GeV\(^4\); for 10 TeV, \(\Delta L = 4.5 \times 10^{23}\) GeV\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the study of first-order phase transitions in the early universe beyond the electroweak sector, focusing on the QCD deconfinement transition characterized by the Polyakov loop, which has been studied in lattice QCD simulations (Lucini 2005, Datta 2010) and effective potential approaches (Reinosa 2014). The authors distinguish their scenario from chiral phase transition models that require large lepton asymmetry (Gao 2021, Gao 2023) or scalar-induced chiral symmetry breaking (Ipek 2018, von Harling 2017). The results enable quantitative predictions for gravitational wave signals detectable by future space interferometers such as LISA, eLISA, DECIGO/BBO, ALIA, Taiji, and Tianqin, and provide a framework for studying large-\(N_c\) gauge theories in a cosmological context.
