# Comment on ``Large-space shell-model calculations for light nuclei''

> In a recent publication Zheng, Vary, and Barrett reproduced the negative quadrupole moment of Li-6 and the low-lying positive-parity states of He-5 by using a no-core shell model. In this Comment we question the meaning of these results by pointing out that the model used is inadequate for the reproduction of these properties.

## Key claim

The no-core shell-model results of Zheng, Vary, and Barrett (1994) for the negative quadrupole moment of Li-6 and the low-lying positive-parity states of He-5 cannot be considered physically meaningful because the model treats the few-body dynamics underlying these effects improperly, specifically failing to reproduce correct separation energies and using an incorrect asymptotic boundary condition for unbound states.

## Method

The authors critique the no-core shell-model calculations by analyzing the Li-6 quadrupole moment in the context of the model's failure to reproduce the correct alpha+d separation energy, and by performing their own cluster-model calculations for He-5 using an alpha+n and d+t basis with correct asymptotic boundary conditions. They search for resonances in He-5 by locating complex-energy poles of the S-matrix using both analytic continuation and complex scaling methods, and also demonstrate the sensitivity of the Li-6 quadrupole moment by showing analogous behavior in a Li-7 alpha+t cluster model.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Li-6 quadrupole moment is -0.083 e*fm^2; the no-core shell model of Zheng et al. produces Q = -0.116 e*fm^2
- The no-core shell model predicts Li-6 to be unstable against alpha+d breakup by 0.21 MeV, whereas experimentally the ground state of Li-6 lies 1.475 MeV below the alpha+d threshold
- In the authors' cluster model for He-5, the 3/2+ state appears at 16 MeV excitation energy, with no sign of any low-lying 1/2+, 3/2+, or 5/2+ states
- When artificially adjusting the interaction to produce a 1/2+ state in He-5, the pole was found at 19 MeV excitation energy with a width of 34 MeV, even with highly non-physical interaction parameters
- The experimental Li-7 quadrupole moment is -4.06 e*fm^2 with an alpha+t separation energy of 2.47 MeV

## Context

This Comment extends the debate on the Li-6 quadrupole moment, where previous calculations including variational three-body calculations (Kukulin et al.), the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (Danilin et al.), the Faddeev approach (Schellingerhout et al., 1993), and a six-body alpha+p+n three-cluster model (Csoto and Lovas, 1992) all gave positive values in the 0.2-0.6 e*fm^2 range. The authors argue that the no-core shell model's success in reproducing the negative sign is fortuitous due to incorrect separation energies, and that the claimed low-lying positive-parity states of He-5 are likely artifacts of the incorrect boundary condition, analogous to a spurious 2+ state in Li-8 that disappeared when proper boundary conditions were applied. The work suggests that future calculations should supplement shell-model wave functions with cluster terms having correct asymptotics, or examine stability of results against changes in basis size.
