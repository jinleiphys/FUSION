## Key claim

The Interacting Quark Diquark Model (IQDM) can describe baryon spectroscopy using quark-diquark degrees of freedom, and its relativistic version predicts a zero in the ratio of the proton's electric to magnetic form factor at \(Q^2 = 8\ \text{GeV}^2\), a feature that is impossible to reproduce with three-quark models and which the planned JLab 12 GeV experiment can test.

## Method

The IQDM treats baryons as two-body bound states of a valence quark and a valence diquark (either scalar or axial-vector), with a Hamiltonian containing a direct Coulomb-plus-linear confining interaction and an exchange interaction depending on spin, isospin, and flavor (using SU\(_f\)(3) Gell-Mann matrices for strange baryons). The model is solved analytically for the Coulomb part and perturbatively for the other terms, or numerically via variational diagonalization with harmonic oscillator trial wave functions. The relativistic point-form version uses a mass operator with relativistic kinetic energies and a smeared Coulomb term, and includes a contact interaction to reproduce the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) mass splitting.

## Key numbers

*   The model predicts a zero in the ratio \(\mu_p G^p_E(Q^2)/G^p_M(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 8\ \text{GeV}^2\) (proton form factor ratio).
*   Calculated masses for \(N\)-type resonances include: \(N(939)P_{11}\) at 940 MeV (experiment: 939 MeV), \(N(1440)P_{11}\) at 1538 MeV (experiment: 1410–1450 MeV), \(N(1520)D_{13}\) at 1543 MeV (experiment: 1510–1520 MeV), and \(N(1535)S_{11}\) at 1538 MeV (experiment: 1525–1545 MeV).
*   Calculated masses for \(\Delta\)-type resonances include: \(\Delta(1232)P_{33}\) at 1235 MeV (experiment: 1230–1234 MeV), \(\Delta(1620)S_{31}\) at 1673 MeV (experiment: 1600–1660 MeV), and \(\Delta(1950)F_{37}\) at 1930 MeV (experiment: 1915–1950 MeV).
*   For \(\Lambda\)-type resonances, the model gives: \(\Lambda(1116)P_{01}\) at 1116 MeV (experiment: 1116 MeV), \(\Lambda(1670)S_{01}\) at 1650 MeV (experiment: 1660–1680 MeV), and \(\Lambda^*(1405)S_{01}\) at 1431 MeV (experiment: 1402–1410 MeV).
*   The relativistic IQDM predicts seven missing \(\Lambda\) octet states and six missing \(\Lambda\) singlet states below 2.0 GeV.
*   In the Unquenched Quark Model (UQM), the proton spin composition is: 67.6% from quark and antiquark spins, and 32.4% from orbital angular momentum (31.7% from relative motion of baryon and meson, 0.7% from orbitally excited intermediate states).
*   Self-energy corrections to charmonium masses are of order 2–6%, and for bottomonium masses of order 1%.

## Context

This paper reviews the IQDM, originally formulated by Santopinto (2004), and extends it to strange baryons and a relativistic point-form version (Ferretti and Santopinto, 2011, 2015). The model addresses the "missing resonances" problem in baryon spectroscopy, where three-quark models (e.g., Isgur-Karl, Capstick-Isgur) predict many more \(\Lambda\) states than observed. The IQDM predicts fewer \(\Lambda\) states than three-quark models, which could affect the systematic error in pentaquark analyses (e.g., LHCb 2015). The model also enables predictions for heavy baryon spectroscopy (charmed and bottomed baryons) and, via the UQM formalism, provides a framework for including continuum (sea quark) effects in hadron observables such as the proton spin structure and meson self-energy corrections.
