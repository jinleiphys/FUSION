# Relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model for axially deformed nuclei

> Staring from the Lagrangian density that foots on the meson-propagated picture of nuclear force, the full Hamiltonian, that contains both mean field and pairing contributions, is derived by quantizing the Dirac spinor field in the Bogoliubov quasi-particle space, and the expectation with respect to the Bogoliubov ground state gives the full energy functional. As an extension of the D-RHF model, the degree of freedom associated with the $ρ$-tensor ($ρ$-T) coupling is implemented, and incorporating with the Bogoliubov scheme the finite-range Gogny force D1S is utilized as the pairing force. Moreover, qualitative analysis on the nature of the $π$-PV and $ρ$-T couplings are presented for better understanding their enhancements on the deformation effects. Space convergence related to the spherical DWS base is confirmed for the D-RHFB model by taking light nucleus $^{24}$Mg and mid-heavy one $^{156}$Sm as candidates. Compared to light nuclei, extraordinary more negative energy states are necessitated to keep the expansion completeness on the spherical DWS base for mid-heavy and heavy nuclei, due to the enhanced correlations between the expansion components with large $κ$-quantity as indicated by the nature of the $π$-PV and $ρ$-T couplings. Furthermore, because of the enhanced deformation effects by the $π$-PV and $ρ$-T couplings, the RHF Lagrangian PKA1 presents deeper bound ground state for $^{24}$Mg than the other selected Lagrangians, in addition to predicting a fairly deep bound local minimum with large oblate deformation.

## Key claim

The paper develops the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model, extending the D-RHF model by implementing the ρ-tensor (ρ-T) coupling degree of freedom and incorporating the Bogoliubov scheme with the finite-range Gogny force D1S as the pairing force, with the aim of reliably describing wide-range unstable nuclei.

## Method

Starting from the Lagrangian density based on the meson-propagated picture of nuclear force, the full Hamiltonian containing both mean field and pairing contributions was derived by quantizing the Dirac spinor field in the Bogoliubov quasi-particle space, and the expectation with respect to the Bogoliubov ground state gave the full energy functional. The Bogoliubov quasi-particle spinors were expanded on the spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) base, and the finite-range Gogny force D1S was utilized as the pairing force. Space convergence related to the spherical DWS base was confirmed by taking light nucleus ²⁴Mg and mid-heavy one ¹⁵⁶Sm as candidates.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Mg, the RHF Lagrangian PKA1 presented deeper bound ground state than other selected Lagrangians due to enhanced deformation effects from the π-PV and ρ-T couplings.
- For ¹⁵⁶Sm, extraordinary more negative energy states were necessitated to keep the expansion completeness on the spherical DWS base compared to light nuclei.
- The one- and/or two-nucleon separation energies of unstable nuclei can be as small as 1–2 MeV or even less due to extreme imbalanced neutron-proton ratio.

## Context

This work extends the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock (D-RHF) model (Geng, 2020) by incorporating the Bogoliubov scheme for pairing correlations, replacing the BCS method used previously. It also extends the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (D-RHB) model (Lalazissis, 1999; Zhou, 2010; Li, 2012; Chen, 2012) by including Fock terms that naturally account for tensor force components via π-pseudovector and ρ-tensor couplings. The model enables reliable description of unstable nuclei far from the β-stability line, where pairing correlations and continuum effects are essential.
