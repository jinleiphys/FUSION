# Effects of finite coverage on global polarization observables in heavy ion collisions

> In non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions, the created matter possesses a large initial orbital angular momentum. Particles produced in the collisions could be polarized globally in the direction of the orbital angular momentum due to spin-orbit coupling. Recently, the STAR experiment has presented polarization signals for $Λ$ hyperons and possible spin alignment signals for $ϕ$ mesons. Here we discuss the effects of finite coverage on these observables. The results from a multi-phase transport and a toy model both indicate that a pseudorapidity coverage narrower than $|η|< \sim 1$ will generate a larger value for the extracted $ϕ$-meson $ρ_{00}$ parameter; thus a finite coverage can lead to an artificial deviation of $ρ_{00}$ from 1/3. We also show that a finite $η$ and $p_T$ coverage affect the extracted $p_H$ parameter for $Λ$ hyperons when the real $p_H$ value is non-zero. Therefore proper corrections are necessary to reliably quantify the global polarization with experimental observables.

## Key claim

Finite pseudorapidity coverage narrower than approximately \(|\eta| < 1\) artificially increases the extracted \(\phi\)-meson spin alignment parameter \(\rho_{00}\) above its true value, and finite \(\eta\) and \(p_T\) coverage also affect the extracted \(\Lambda\) hyperon global polarization parameter \(p_H\) when the true \(p_H\) is non-zero; these coverage effects may be a dominant contribution to the experimentally observed deviation of \(\rho_{00}\) from 1/3.

## Method

The authors modified the string-melting version of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to include polarized decays of \(\phi\) mesons (via \(\phi \to K\bar{K}\)) and \(\Lambda\) hyperons (via \(\Lambda \to p\pi^{-}\) and \(n\pi^{0}\)), assuming uniform polarization according to the standard angular distribution formulas. They also constructed a toy model that samples \(\phi\) and \(\Lambda\) kinematics from AMPT fits and decays particles with PYTHIA. Both models were used to extract \(\rho_{00}\) and \(p_H\) after applying various \(\eta\) and \(p_T\) cuts, and analytical derivations were performed for the case of parent hadrons at rest.

## Key numbers

- For \(\phi\) mesons with input \(\rho_{00}=1/3\) (no spin alignment) in minimum bias Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, applying STAR \(\eta\) and \(p_T\) cuts yields extracted \(\rho_{00}\) values systematically higher than 1/3, with a weak energy dependence similar to STAR preliminary data.
- For \(\Lambda\) hyperons at rest with input \(p_H=0.01\), the extracted \(p_H\) using Eq. (2) is \(4/\pi\) times the real value in the limit of zero \(\eta\) coverage; using Eq. (1) the extracted value is 50% higher than the real value in the same limit.
- For \(\phi\) mesons at rest with input \(\rho_{00}=1/3\), the extracted \(\rho_{00}\) under finite \(\eta\) coverage is given by \(\rho^{\rm extracted}_{00} = 1/3 + (5\sin^2\epsilon)/12\), where \(\epsilon = 2\arctan(e^{-\eta_{\rm max}})\).
- The STAR \(p_T\) cuts applied are: \(p_T^K > 0.1\) GeV/\(c\) and \(0.4 < p_T^{\phi} < 3\) GeV/\(c\) for \(\phi\) mesons; \(p_T^{\rm daughter} > 0.15\) GeV/\(c\) and \(0.4 < p_T^{\Lambda} < 3\) GeV/\(c\) for \(\Lambda\) hyperons.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of global polarization in heavy ion collisions, following the STAR collaboration's measurements of \(\Lambda\) polarization (STAR, 2017) and \(\phi\)-meson spin alignment (STAR, 2015, 2016). The study builds on earlier predictions of spin-orbit coupling effects by Liang and Wang (2005) and the vorticity evolution calculations by several theory groups (2016-2017). The results demonstrate that finite detector acceptance corrections are essential before quantitative conclusions can be drawn from experimental polarization observables, and they suggest that the current STAR \(\rho_{00}\) data may be dominated by phase-space cut effects rather than genuine spin alignment.
