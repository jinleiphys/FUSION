# Strange Stars within Bosonic and Fermionic Admixed Dark Matter

> In this work, we study dark matter (DM) admixed strange quark stars exploring the different possibilities about the nature of the DM and their effects on the macroscopic properties of strange stars, such as maximum masses, radii, as well the dimensionless tidal parameter. We observe that the DM significantly affects the macroscopic properties that depend on its mass, type, and fraction inside the star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that dark matter (DM) significantly affects the macroscopic properties of strange quark stars (maximum masses, radii, and dimensionless tidal deformability), but the magnitude and direction of these effects depend critically on whether the DM is bosonic or fermionic, on the DM particle mass, and on the DM fraction inside the star.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for strange quark stars using two quark matter models: the vector MIT (vMIT) bag model and a constant-sound-speed (CSS) analytical approximation for color-flavor locked (CFL) superconducting quark matter. They admix two types of dark matter: bosonic DM described by a Gross-Pitaevskii mean-field equation of state, and fermionic DM described by a Higgs-mediated self-interaction in mean-field approximation. For each DM type and quark model, they vary the DM mass and fraction and compute the mass-radius relation and dimensionless tidal deformability parameter Lambda.

## Key numbers

- For bosonic DM (mass 100 MeV) within the vMIT bag model, maximum mass increases from 2.41 solar masses (pure strange star) to 2.51 solar masses (DM fraction 0.10); radius of the 1.4 solar mass canonical star remains between 11.37 km and 11.40 km.
- For bosonic DM (mass 400 MeV) within the vMIT bag model, maximum mass decreases from 2.41 solar masses to 2.29 solar masses (DM fraction 0.10); radius of the canonical star drops to 11.08 km.
- For bosonic DM (mass 100 MeV) within CFL quark matter, maximum mass increases from 2.81 solar masses (pure) to 2.88 solar masses (DM fraction 0.10); canonical star radius is about 11.57 km.
- For bosonic DM (mass 400 MeV) within CFL quark matter, maximum mass decreases from 2.81 solar masses to 2.61 solar masses (DM fraction 0.10); canonical star radius drops to 11.29 km.
- For fermionic DM (mass 200 GeV) within the vMIT bag model, maximum mass drops from 2.41 solar masses (no DM) to 1.80 solar masses (Fermi momentum 0.06 GeV); canonical star radius drops to 8.95 km.
- For fermionic DM (mass 60 GeV) within the vMIT bag model, maximum mass drops to 2.16 solar masses (Fermi momentum 0.06 GeV); canonical star radius drops to 10.42 km.
- The dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda for the canonical star ranges from 644 (pure vMIT) down to 108 (fermionic DM 200 GeV, Fermi momentum 0.06 GeV), and from 721 (pure CFL) down to 531 (bosonic DM 400 MeV, fraction 0.10).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM-admixed strange stars by Panotopoulos (2017) and Lopes (2018), who used the original massless MIT bag model, by employing the more realistic vector MIT bag model and CFL superconducting quark matter. It also builds on the fermionic DM formalism of Das (2021) and Odilon (2021). The results are compared against observational constraints from the pulsars PSR J0740+6620 (mass 2.08 solar masses, Fonseca 2021), PSR J0952-0607 (mass 2.35 solar masses, Romani 2022), NICER radius measurements (Miller 2019, Riley 2019), and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (Abbott 2018). The work enables future studies to distinguish between bosonic and fermionic DM scenarios in compact stars using multi-messenger observations.
