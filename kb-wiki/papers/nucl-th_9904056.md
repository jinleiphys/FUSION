# Isospin Effects of the Critical Behavior in the Lattice Gas Model

> Isospin effects of the critical phenomena were studied via Xe isotopes in the frame of lattice gas model. All the critical temperatures for four Xe isotopes are close to 5.5 MeV at the same freeze-out density of about 0.39 $ρ_0$. The critical values of power law parameter of mass distribution, mean multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMF), information entropy and Campi's second moment show minor dependence on the isospin at the critical point.

# Isospin Effects of the Critical Behavior in the Lattice Gas Model

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in Xe isotopes is approximately 5.5 MeV regardless of isospin, but this universality holds only in a narrow region around the critical point; outside that region, observables such as the power-law parameter tau, mean IMF multiplicity, information entropy, and Campi's second moment do show isospin dependence.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using the lattice gas model on a 7x7x7 cubic lattice (343 sites) for four Xe isotopes (122, 129, 137, 146) with isospin parameters ranging from 0.11 to 0.26. Nucleons were placed by Metropolis sampling at freeze-out densities between 0.36 and 0.43 rho_0, and fragments were constructed using the Coniglio-Klein prescription where neighboring nucleons belong to the same fragment if their relative kinetic energy is insufficient to overcome the attractive bond. For each isotope, 1000 events were accumulated at temperatures from 4 to 7 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for all four Xe isotopes: approximately 5.5 MeV
- Freeze-out density: approximately 0.39 rho_0 (individual values: 0.36, 0.38, 0.40, and 0.43 rho_0 for 122Xe, 129Xe, 137Xe, and 146Xe respectively)
- Isospin parameters (N-Z)/A: 0.11 for 122Xe, 0.16 for 129Xe, 0.21 for 137Xe, and 0.26 for 146Xe
- Interaction energy epsilon_pn,np = -5.33 MeV; epsilon_nn,pp = 0 MeV
- Lattice size: 343 sites (7 x 7 x 7)

## Context

This work extends the lattice gas model, originally developed by Yang and Lee (1952) for atomic systems and applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Biro (1986) and Pan and Das Gupta (1995), to investigate isospin-asymmetric systems. The study introduces information entropy as a new observable for identifying critical behavior in nuclear disassembly, complementing established observables such as the power-law parameter tau, mean IMF multiplicity, and Campi's second moment. The results suggest that experiments with radioactive beams could test the predicted isospin independence of critical behavior near the critical point.
