# Summary of Spin Physics Parallel Sessions

> We summarize the activities in the spin physics parallel sessions of the $8^{\rm th}$ conference on intersections between particle and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the spin physics parallel sessions of the 8th conference on intersections between particle and nuclear physics, reporting on experimental and theoretical progress in understanding the spin structure of the nucleon. The abstract glosses over the fact that the gluon helicity distribution remains poorly constrained by existing data, with global fits unable to determine even its sign.

## Method

The authors synthesized 24 scheduled talks (14 experimental, 2 on machine/instrumentation, 8 theoretical) presented over five parallel sessions totaling more than 500 minutes. They organized the summary by major topics: polarized parton distributions, single spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS), generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and the RHIC spin program.

## Key numbers

- Quark helicity contribution to the proton spin is about ΔΣ ∼ 0.2, much smaller than unity expected from the naive quark model
- The COMPASS collaboration recorded 1.2 fb⁻¹ DIS data with a longitudinally polarized target and 0.3 fb⁻¹ DIS data with a transversely polarized target in the 2002 run
- Asymptotically, quarks (including helicity and orbital angular momentum) carry about 52% of total proton spin, while gluons carry the rest
- RHIC beam polarization was 35-40% in 2002-2003, with plans to raise it to 70%
- RHIC polarized beam luminosity was about a factor 30 below the design value

## Context

This conference summary extends the work of Filippone (2001) on the nucleon spin sum rule and the global fits by the Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (Hirai, 2001) and Wendtland (2001) on polarized parton distributions. It reports on the first polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC in 2002 and 2003, which enabled measurements of transverse spin asymmetries and the first double spin asymmetries in a hadron collider. The results enable future extraction of the gluon helicity distribution from RHIC data and improved constraints on transversity distributions through SIDIS measurements at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab.
