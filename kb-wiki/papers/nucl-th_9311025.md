# Nuclear Polarization Corrections for the S--Levels of Electronic and Muonic Deuterium

> We calculate the second-order corrections to the atomic energy level shifts in ordinary and muonic deuterium due to virtual excitations of the deuteron which are important for ongoing and planned precise experiments in these systems. For light atoms a method can be used in which the shift is expressed as integrals over the longitudinal and transverse inelastic structure functions of the nucleus. We employ the structure functions arising from separable NN potentials of the Yamaguchi and Tabakin form which can be evaluated analytically. Special emphasis is put on gauge invariance which requires a consistent inclusion of interaction currents and seagull terms. The effect of the D-wave component of the deuteron is investigated for the leading longitudinal contribution. We also estimate the shift for pionic deuterium.

# Nuclear Polarization Corrections for the S-Levels of Electronic and Muonic Deuterium

## Key claim
The second-order nuclear polarization corrections to atomic energy levels in ordinary and muonic deuterium are calculable with percent-level accuracy using realistic NN potentials, yielding shifts of approximately -20.5/n³ kHz for electronic deuterium and -11.6/n³ meV for muonic deuterium, but these results depend on the specific separable potential model used and the consistent inclusion of gauge-invariant interaction currents and seagull terms.

## Method
The authors calculate the nuclear polarization shift for atomic S-states by expressing it as integrals over the longitudinal and transverse inelastic structure functions of the deuteron, using the approximation that the lepton wavefunction is constant over the nuclear volume. They employ separable NN potentials of the Yamaguchi and Tabakin forms, which allow analytic evaluation of the structure functions, and enforce gauge invariance by consistently including interaction currents and seagull terms derived from minimal coupling in the nonlocal potential. The D-wave component of the deuteron is investigated for the leading longitudinal contribution using a Yamaguchi separable potential with tensor force.

## Key numbers
- Electronic deuterium 1S nuclear polarization shift: -20.5 ± 1.3 kHz (scaling as 1/n³)
- Muonic deuterium 1S nuclear polarization shift: -11.6 ± 0.5 meV (scaling as 1/n³)
- Pionic deuterium estimated shift: approximately -28 meV (scaling as 1/n³)
- Electric dipole polarizability from Yamaguchi potential: 0.613 fm³ (experimental range: 0.61–0.70 fm³)
- Point rms radius from Yamaguchi model: 1.92 fm (experimental value: 1.96 fm)
- Dipole enhancement factor κ = 0.176 for the Yamaguchi potential
- Finite-size correction factor for muonic deuterium: R̄^(μ) = 0.9793; for electronic deuterium: R̄^(e) = 0.99989

## Context
This work extends earlier estimates by using a square-well model with closure approximation (which gave approximately -19 kHz for the 1S shift in electronic deuterium) to a more realistic treatment that avoids the closure approximation and includes transverse excitations previously neglected. The method follows the approach of Rosenfelder (Ro2) for calculating nuclear polarization shifts in light nuclei via inelastic structure functions, but applies it to the deuteron where the structure functions can be calculated from first principles rather than using phenomenological models. The results are relevant for ongoing experiments at PSI measuring muonic hydrogen and deuterium transitions, and for planned high-precision isotope shift measurements in electronic hydrogen and deuterium that aim to reduce experimental uncertainty to about 1 kHz.
