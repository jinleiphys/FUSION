# PDF in PDFs from Hadronic Tensor and LaMET

> We point out a problem of the phenomenological definition of the valence partons as the difference between the partons and antipartons in the context of the NNLO evolution equations. After demonstrating that the classification of the parton degrees of freedom (PDF) of the parton distribution functions (PDFs) are the same in the QCD path-intergral formulations of the hadronic tensor and the quasi-PDF with LaMET, we resolve the problem by showing that the proper definition of the valence should be in terms of those in the connected insertions only. We also prove that the strange partons appear as the disconnected sea in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The conventional phenomenological definition of valence partons as the difference between partons and antipartons ($q^- \equiv q - \bar{q}$) is incorrect; the proper definition of valence should be based on connected insertions only ($q^v \equiv q^{v+cs} - \bar{q}^{cs}$), which resolves apparent contradictions in NNLO evolution equations and the classification of strange quarks.

## Method

The author uses the Euclidean path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor to classify parton degrees of freedom into three topologically distinct and gauge-invariant insertions: valence plus connected sea (CI), connected sea antipartons, and disconnected sea (DI) partons and antipartons. This classification is shown to be identical to that obtained from quasi-PDF calculations using large momentum effective theory (LaMET) on the lattice, where the same connected and disconnected insertion diagrams appear. The author then demonstrates that the NNLO evolution equations can be separated into independent equations for the valence partons (from CI only) and the disconnected sea differences.

## Key numbers

- The NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule gave $0.235 \pm 0.026$, a 4$\sigma$ difference from the Gottfried sum rule value of $1/3$, implying $\bar{u} \neq \bar{d}$
- For DIS qualification, the energy transfer must satisfy $\nu > 4.86$ GeV $+ 0.533$ (GeV$^{-1}$) $Q^2$; for $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$, this implies $\nu > 7$ GeV
- The connected insertion partons have small-$x$ behavior $q^{v+cs}, \bar{q}^{cs} \sim x^{-1/2}$ for $q = u,d$, while disconnected insertion partons have $q^{ds}, \bar{q}^{ds} \sim x^{-1}$ for $q = u,d,s,c$
- The renormalization matrix for the second moment $\langle x \rangle$ involves a $6 \times 6$ matrix with mixing between CI and DI components, where zeros reflect that CI cannot mix into glue and DI but not vice versa

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor (Liu 1993) which identified the origin of Gottfried sum rule violation, and the classification of parton degrees of freedom (Liu 1999, 1998). It resolves puzzles about the valence definition that arise in NNLO evolution equations (Moch 2004, Cafarella 2008) and the NuTeV anomaly discussions (Davidson 2001, Kretzer 2003). The separation of connected and disconnected sea partons enables direct one-to-one comparison between lattice calculations and global PDF analyses, and the extended evolution equations (Liu 2017) allow the CS and DS to be separated at different $Q^2$ values in global fits.
