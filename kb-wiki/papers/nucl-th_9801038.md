# Relativistic Mean Field Theory in Rotating Frame: Single-particle Properties at Superdeformation

> Single-particle properties at superdeformation are investigated within the Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory on the example of superdeformed rotational bands observed in the A~140-150 mass region. Applying the effective alignment approach it is shown that CRMF theory provides a reasonable description of the alignment properties of the single-particle orbitals. The agreement with experiment is good in most of the cases. This suggests that many features of the observed superdeformed bands can be well understood in terms of an almost undisturbed single-particle motion. The stability of the results with respect to the parameterizations used in Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory is also investigated, employing some frequently used non-linear effective forces. It turns out, with the exception of the single-particle ordering in superdeformed minimum, that the dependence of the calculated observables on the parameterization is rather small.

## Key claim

The Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory provides a reasonable description of the alignment properties of single-particle orbitals in superdeformed rotational bands, with good agreement with experiment in most cases; however, the dependence of calculated observables on the chosen Lagrangian parameterization is rather small, except for the single-particle ordering in the superdeformed minimum.

## Method

The authors applied the Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory in a one-dimensional cranking approximation along the x-axis, solving the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields in the rotating frame self-consistently, including the spatial components of vector mesons (nuclear magnetism). They used the effective alignment approach to analyze single-particle orbital contributions by comparing the relative spins of different superdeformed bands at constant rotational frequency, employing the NL1 parameterization primarily, with additional calculations using NL3 and NLSH parameter sets for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For the lowest SD configuration of $^{152}$Dy at $\Omega_x=0.8$ MeV, the maximal variations in total energy were 0.02%, total spin 0.5%, kinematic moment of inertia $J^{(1)}$ 0.5%, charge quadrupole moments $Q_0$ 0.17%, and mass hexadecupole moment $Q_{40}$ 0.11% when testing basis truncation schemes.
- The numerical uncertainty in the calculation of effective alignments $i_{eff}$ is typically less than $0.1\hbar$, with the $\nu[402]5/2(r=\pm i)$ orbitals showing uncertainty less than $0.05\hbar$.
- The accuracy in relative charge quadrupole moments $\Delta Q_0$ is about 0.02 $e$b.
- For the $\nu[770]1/2(r=-i)$ orbital, CRMF overestimates the observed effective alignments by approximately $0.8\hbar$ for $^{151}$Dy(1)/$^{152}$Dy(1), $0.4\hbar$ for $^{150}$Tb(1)/$^{151}$Tb(1), and $0.5\hbar$ for $^{149}$Gd(1)/$^{150}$Gd(1) at the highest observed rotational frequencies.

## Context

This work extends previous CRMF studies of superdeformed bands in the A~140-150 mass region (Afanasjev 1996) by applying the effective alignment approach, which had been widely used within the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CN) model by Ragnarsson (1991, 1993) for orbital identification. The results enable a more detailed test of single-particle properties in rotating relativistic mean field theory, and suggest that deviations from additivity of effective alignments can serve as fingerprints of residual interactions not captured in the pure single-particle picture, while also highlighting the need for future studies including pairing correlations.
