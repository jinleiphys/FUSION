# Recent developments in chiral and spin polarization effects in heavy-ion collisions

> We give a brief overview of recent theoretical and experimental results on the chiral magnetic effect and spin polarization effect in heavy-ion collisions. We present updated experimental results for the chiral magnetic effect and related phenomena. The time evolution of the magnetic fields in different models is discussed. The newly developed quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions is reviewed. We present theoretical and experimental results for the polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and the $ρ_{00}$ value of vector mesons.

## Key claim

This review argues that while the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and spin polarization effects are well-motivated by quantum anomalies and vorticity, the experimental signals remain ambiguous because background contributions (especially flow-related correlations and local charge conservation) dominate the measured observables, and the CME fraction is constrained to be small (8 ± 4 ± 8% for Au+Au at 200 GeV and U+U at 193 GeV combined).

## Method

The paper surveys experimental measurements of charge-dependent azimuthal correlators (γ112, γ123, γ132) and signed balance functions in Au+Au, U+U, Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, p+Au, and d+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, comparing them to anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (AVFD) and AMPT model simulations. It also reviews theoretical developments in magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) for time-dependent magnetic fields, quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions using covariant Wigner functions, and the decomposition of orbital angular momentum and spin from total angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions are on the order of 10^17–18 Gs (peak values a few times mπ^2, where mπ is the pion mass)
- The average angular velocity or vorticity from Λ hyperon polarization was estimated as ω ∼ 10^22 s^−1
- Magnetic field lifetime constrained to tB ≃ 0.5–1 fm/c or tB = A/√sNN with A = 115 ± 16 GeV·fm/c
- Combined CME fraction for Au+Au at 200 GeV and U+U at 193 GeV: fCME = 8 ± 4 ± 8%
- CMS upper limits on CME fraction: 13% for p+Pb and 7% for Pb+Pb collisions at 95% confidence level
- AVFD predicts for isobaric collisions: ζisobar^EP ≃ −(0.41 ± 0.27) with ⟨cos(2ΨB − 2ΨEP)⟩ ≃ −0.46; ζisobar^RP ≃ −(0.90 ± 0.45) with ⟨cos(2ΨB − 2ΨEP)⟩ ≃ −0.95
- Mass-corrected CME current: j^z = (e^2 EB / 2π^2) coth(Bπ/E) exp(−π m^2 / eE)
- Axial Ward identity with mass: ∂μ j5^μ = (e^2 EB / 2π^2) exp(−π m^2 / eE)
- Freeze-out temperature Tfo = 155 MeV used in polarization estimates

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral transport phenomena initiated by Kharzeev (2004), Fukushima (2008), and the STAR global polarization measurement (2017). It updates the experimental status of CME searches using the γ correlator method developed by Voloshin (2004) and the signed balance function approach proposed by Tang (2019). The paper enables future work by identifying that the dominant component of Δγ is likely background rather than CME signal, and by highlighting the need for improved quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions beyond the chiral limit used in earlier chiral kinetic theory formulations by Stephanov (2012) and Son (2012).
