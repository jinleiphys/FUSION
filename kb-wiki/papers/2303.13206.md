# The isospin-3 three-particle $K$-matrix at NLO in ChPT

> The three-particle $K$-matrix, $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$, is a scheme-dependent quantity that parametrizes short-range three-particle interactions in the relativistic-field-theory three-particle finite-volume formalism. In this work, we compute its value for systems of three pions at maximal isospin through next-to-leading order (NLO) in Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT). We compare the values to existing lattice QCD results and find that the agreement between lattice QCD data and ChPT in the first two coefficients of the threshold expansion of $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ is significantly improved with respect to leading order once NLO effects are incorporated.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) Chiral Perturbation Theory prediction for the three-particle K-matrix Kdf,3 significantly improves agreement with lattice QCD results for the first two coefficients of the threshold expansion compared to leading order, although NLO corrections are found to be large, particularly for the coefficient K1.

## Method

The authors compute the three-particle K-matrix Kdf,3 for maximal-isospin three-pion systems through NLO in Chiral Perturbation Theory by combining the NLO three-meson scattering amplitude M3 from Bijnens et al. (2021, 2022) with the relativistic-field-theory finite-volume formalism of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015). They establish an algebraic and linear relation between Kdf,3 and M3 valid at NLO, evaluate the cutoff-dependent subtraction terms using a tailored numerical approach, and isolate the five independent coefficients of the threshold expansion (K0, K1, K2, KA, KB) through analytical calculations checked numerically.

## Key numbers

- The threshold expansion of m^2 Kdf = K0 + K1 Delta + K2 Delta^2 + KA DeltaA + KB DeltaB + O(Delta^3), where Delta = (P^2 - 9m^2)/(9m^2) and the other kinematic variables are defined in Eqs. (1.2)-(1.4) of the paper.
- At LO in ChPT, all terms except K0 and K1 vanish; at NLO, all five coefficients become nonzero.
- The quantization condition det[F3^{-1}(E,P,L) + Kdf(E*)] = 0 is valid in the energy range m < E* < 5m, where m is the renormalized pion mass.
- The two-particle phase-space factor is defined as rho(p) = -i q*/(16 pi E*) for pair energies E* >= 2m.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order ChPT calculation of Kdf for 3pi+ scattering by Blanton et al. (2019), which showed significant disagreement with lattice QCD results from Blanton et al. (2019), Fischer et al. (2020), and Blanton et al. (2021). The NLO results enable quantitative comparison between ChPT and lattice QCD for three-pion systems, analogous to the successful synergy between ChPT and lattice QCD for two-pion scattering described in the FLAG report (2021). The calculation also provides a nontrivial check of the RFT formalism properties beyond leading order.
