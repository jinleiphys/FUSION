# Graviton and Radion Production at LHC: From pp and PbPb Collisions

> If the Planck scale is around a TeV in theories with large extra dimension, then gravitons should be produced in high energy hadronic collisions at the LHC. In this paper we compute the direct graviton production cross section in a pp and PbPb collision at LHC at \sqrt s^{NN}= 14 TeV and 5.5 TeV respectively. The graviton production cross section in a lead-lead collision is enhanced in comparison to a pp collision at LHC depending upon the minimum transverse energy of the jet E_{T,jet}^{min} above which the graviton production cross section is computed. For two extra dimensions and above E_{T,jet}^{min} = 100, 500, 1000, 1500 GeV the ratio of graviton production cross sections in a PbPb collision to that in a pp collision is found to be 2220, 400, 43, 2. For four extra dimensions this ratio is found to be 470, 100, 10, 0.5. In the Randall-Sundrum model the radion production cross section is also found to be enhanced in a lead-lead collision over that in a pp collision at LHC. The ratio of the radion production cross sections in a PbPb collision to that in a pp collision at LHC is found to be 1650, 460, 180, 67, 23, 1 for radion of masses 100, 500, 1000, 1500, 2000, 3000 GeV respectively.

## Key claim

If the Planck scale is around a TeV in theories with large extra dimensions, graviton and radion production cross sections are significantly enhanced in lead-lead (PbPb) collisions compared to proton-proton (pp) collisions at the LHC, but only for certain ranges of minimum jet transverse energy or radion mass; the enhancement factor depends on the number of extra dimensions and the specific kinematic cut.

## Method

The author computed the direct graviton plus jet production cross section (2-to-2 partonic processes ij -> kG) and the radion production cross section via gluon fusion (gg -> phi) for both pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV. The calculation used CTEQ6 parton distribution functions, assumed unshadowed nuclear parton distributions (R_A = 1) for TeV-scale physics, and integrated over graviton Kaluza-Klein masses as a continuous variable. For graviton production, the cross section was computed above a minimum jet transverse energy E_T,jet^min, with rapidity cuts |y_jet,G| < 3.

## Key numbers

* For two extra dimensions (d=2), the ratio of graviton production cross section in PbPb to pp collisions at LHC is 2220, 400, 43, and 2 for E_T,jet^min = 100, 500, 1000, and 1500 GeV respectively.
* For four extra dimensions (d=4), the ratio of graviton production cross section in PbPb to pp collisions is 470, 100, 10, and 0.5 for E_T,jet^min = 100, 500, 1000, and 1500 GeV respectively.
* The ratio of radion production cross section in PbPb to pp collisions is 1650, 460, 180, 67, 23, and 1 for radion masses of 100, 500, 1000, 1500, 2000, and 3000 GeV respectively.
* The pp and PbPb center-of-mass energies used were sqrt(s) = 14 TeV and sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of direct graviton production in pp collisions at the LHC by Wells (1999) and Vacavant and Hinchliffe (2001), and radion production in pp collisions by Cheung (2001) and others, to the case of PbPb collisions. It is motivated by the observation by Chamblin and Nayak (2002) that black hole production cross sections are much larger in PbPb than pp collisions at the LHC. The results suggest that PbPb collisions can serve as a "graviton production factory" for jet transverse energies up to about 1.5 TeV and a "radion production factory" for radion masses up to about 3 TeV, while pp collisions are better suited above those thresholds, enabling searches for extra dimensions at the LHC using both collision systems.
