# The Role of Strangeness in Astrophysics - an Odyssey through Strange Phases

> The equation of state for compact stars is reviewed with special emphasis on the role of strange hadrons, strange dibaryons and strange quark matter. Implications for the properties of compact stars are presented. The importance of neutron star data to constrain the properties of hypothetic particles and the possible existence of exotic phases in dense matter is outlined. We also discuss the growing interplay between astrophysics and heavy-ion physics.

## Key claim

This review argues that strangeness must be included as a degree of freedom in astrophysical systems, and that neutron star data can constrain the properties of hypothetical strange particles and exotic phases in dense matter, but the conclusions depend strongly on unknown parameters such as the kaon optical potential, the bag constant, and the renormalization scale in perturbative QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models for the equation of state of compact stars incorporating strange hadrons (hyperons, kaons), strange dibaryons (H-dibaryon), and strange quark matter (MIT bag model, perturbative QCD). It compares predictions for mass-radius relations, third family solutions (twin stars), and neutrino signals from proto-neutron stars across these different strange phases, and connects these to constraints from observed pulsar masses and heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear in neutron star matter at a critical density of \(n_c \sim 2 n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density)
- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar mass is \(M = (1.4411 \pm 0.00035) M_\odot\)
- The lower limit on the H-dibaryon mass from double \(\Lambda\) hypernuclear data is \(m_H > 2m_\Lambda - B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \sim 2220\) MeV
- Twin star solutions within the MIT bag model give radii \(R = 10\)–12 km for a narrow range of bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 178\)–182 MeV
- The mass range for delayed collapse of proto-neutron stars with a strange phase is \(M = (1.6\)–\(2.2) M_\odot\)
- For perturbative QCD, the maximum mass for a pure quark star is \(M = 1.05 M_\odot\) (case 2) and \(M = 2.14 M_\odot\) (case 3) with radii \(R = 5.8\) km and \(R = 12\) km respectively
- The radio-quiet neutron star RX J185635-3754 has an effective temperature of \(T \sim 49\) eV and a black-body radius of \(R_\infty = 6\) km from first parallax measurements
- For quark inflation at the cosmological phase transition, a large surface tension of \(\sigma = 50\)–120 MeV fm\(^{-2}\) is required

## Context

This review extends the line of work on compact star equations of state that began with Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and Glendenning (1985) on hyperon inclusion, and connects to recent developments in color superconductivity (Alford, Bowers, Rajagopal 2001) and perturbative QCD approaches (Fraga, Pisarski, Schaffner-Bielich 2001). It enables future simultaneous mass-radius measurements from isolated neutron stars like RX J185635-3754 to distinguish between different strange phases and determine the order of the QCD phase transition at high density, while also linking to heavy-ion collision experiments searching for strangelets and MEMOs.
