# Cumulants of Net-Proton, Net-Kaon and Net-Charge Multiplicity Distributions in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC BES Energies from UrQMD Model

> Fluctuations of conserved quantities are sensitive observables to probe the signature of QCD phase transition and critical point in heavy-ion collisions. With the UrQMD model, we have studied the centrality and energy dependence of various order cumulants and cumulant ratios (up to fourth order) of net-proton,net-charge and net-kaon multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200 GeV. The model results show that the production mechanism of the particles and anti-particles have significant impacts on the cumulants of net-particles multiplicity distributions and show strong energy dependence. We also made comparisons between model calculations and experimental data measured in the first phase of the beam energy scan (BES) program by the STAR experiment at RHIC. The comparisons indicate that the baryon conservation effect strongly suppress the cumulants of net-proton distributions at low energies and the non-monotonic energy dependence for the net-proton {\KV} at the most central Au+Au collisions measured by the STAR experiment can not be described by the UrQMD model. Since there has no physics of QCD phase transition and QCD critical point implemented in the UrQMD, the model results provide us baselines and qualitative estimates about the non-critical background contributions to the fluctuations observables in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model, which contains no QCD phase transition or critical point physics, cannot reproduce the non-monotonic energy dependence of the net-proton kappa*sigma^2 observable measured by STAR in the most central Au+Au collisions, particularly for the wider transverse momentum coverage of 0.4-2 GeV/c; the model instead shows a monotonic decrease with decreasing energy, consistent with baryon number conservation effects.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 2.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at seven beam energy scan energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV). They calculated cumulants up to fourth order (C1 through C4) and cumulant ratios (S*sigma = C3/C2 and kappa*sigma^2 = C4/C2) for net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge multiplicity distributions as functions of centrality and collision energy, applying the same kinematic cuts as the STAR experiment: protons and anti-protons at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) with 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c, charged particles at |eta| < 0.5 with 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c, and kaons at |y| < 0.5 with 0.2 < pT < 1.6 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Statistics used: 72.5 million events at 7.7 GeV, 105 million at 11.5 GeV, 106 million at 19.6 GeV, 81 million at 27 GeV, 133 million at 39 GeV, 38 million at 62.4 GeV, and 56 million at 200 GeV
- The net-proton kappa*sigma^2 from STAR shows a minimum around 19.6 GeV for the wider pT coverage (0.4-2 GeV/c), then becomes above unity at energies below 19.6 GeV; UrQMD fails to describe this non-monotonic behavior
- At low energies, net-proton cumulants follow closely with proton cumulants; anti-proton cumulants (C1 through C4) are well described by Poisson expectations
- Net-kaon C2 deviates below Poisson baselines, indicating a strong positive correlation (covariance) between K+ and K-
- The dN/dy of net-proton at y=0 monotonically increases with decreasing collision energy, while anti-proton dN/dy at y=0 monotonically decreases

## Context

This work extends the UrQMD-based baseline calculations for fluctuation observables used in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, complementing experimental measurements from the STAR Collaboration (STAR, 2014; STAR, 2015). The results provide non-critical background estimates for the search for the QCD critical point, as the UrQMD model contains no critical point physics. The findings enable comparisons with lattice QCD susceptibility calculations (Karsch and Redlich, 2011) and with theoretical predictions relating higher-order moments to the correlation length (Stephanov, 2009). The inability of UrQMD to describe the non-monotonic net-proton kappa*sigma^2 observed by STAR suggests that baryon number conservation effects dominate at low energies, while the non-monotonic feature may be a signature of critical point physics.
