# Covariant Spectator Theory of heavy-light and heavy mesons and the predictive power of covariant interaction kernels

> The Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) is used to calculate the mass spectrum and vertex functions of heavy-light and heavy mesons in Minkowski space. The covariant kernel contains Lorentz scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector contributions. The numerical calculations are performed in momentum space, where special care is taken to treat the strong singularities present in the confining kernel. The observed meson spectrum is very well reproduced after fitting a small number of model parameters. Remarkably, a fit to a few pseudoscalar meson states only, which are insensitive to spin-orbit and tensor forces and do not allow to separate the spin-spin from the central interaction, leads to essentially the same model parameters as a more general fit. This demonstrates that the covariance of the chosen interaction kernel is responsible for the very accurate prediction of the spin-dependent quark-antiquark interactions.

# Covariant Spectator Theory of heavy-light and heavy mesons and the predictive power of covariant interaction kernels

## Key claim
A covariant interaction kernel fitted exclusively to pseudoscalar meson states (which are insensitive to spin-orbit and tensor forces and do not allow separation of spin-spin from central interactions) yields essentially the same model parameters as a fit to a broader set of meson states, demonstrating that the covariance of the kernel correctly predicts spin-dependent quark-antiquark interactions without being fitted to them.

## Method
The Covariant Spectator Equation (CSE), derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation by keeping only quark-propagator pole contributions, was solved in its one-channel form (1CSE) for heavy-light and heavy mesons in Minkowski momentum space. The interaction kernel contained Lorentz scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector contributions as a relativistic generalization of the Cornell potential, with a linear confining potential, one-gluon-exchange, and a constant term. Relativistic wave functions with definite orbital angular momentum were expanded in B-spline bases, and strong singularities in the confining kernel were treated numerically with Pauli-Villars regularization.

## Key numbers
- Model P1 (fitted to 9 pseudoscalar states only) gave rms deviation of 0.036 GeV from measured meson masses.
- Model PSV1 (fitted to 25 pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector mesons) gave rms deviation of 0.030 GeV from measured meson masses.
- Quark masses used: m_b = 4.892 GeV, m_c = 1.600 GeV, m_s = 0.448 GeV, m_u = m_d = 0.346 GeV.
- Model P1 parameters: sigma = 0.2493 GeV^2, alpha_s = 0.3643, C = 0.3491 GeV.
- Model PSV1 parameters: sigma = 0.2247 GeV^2, alpha_s = 0.3614, C = 0.3377 GeV.
- Axial-vector charmonium and bottomonium C-parity pairs are separated by only 5-6 MeV in bottomonium and 14 MeV in charmonium.

## Context
This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) approach of Gross (1982) and Stadler (2011) from nucleon systems to heavy-light and heavy mesons, using a manifestly covariant generalization of the nonrelativistic Cornell potential of Eichten (1975). It contrasts with the Dyson-Schwinger-Bethe-Salpeter approach of Maris (1999) and others, which implements confinement through dressed quark propagators in Euclidean space rather than through a confining kernel. The results enable future calculations of form factors in the timelike region without analytic continuation, and demonstrate that covariant kernels can predict spin-dependent interactions from fits to spin-independent data alone.
