# Peculiarity of the stellar CNO cycle with energetic particles

> A peculiarity of the stellar CNO cycle caused by MeV alpha-particles and protons generated in exoergic nuclear processes is analyzed. The main parameters of these particles and suprathermal reactions induced by them in a stellar core are calculated. It is shown that these reactions can trigger an abnormal nuclear flow in the second branch of the stellar CNO cycle. A conjecture is made that the phenomenon is of a general nature and can manifest in various stars at non-exploding stages of their evolution. The influence of the abnormal flow on some CNO characteristics is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that MeV alpha-particles and protons generated by exoergic nuclear reactions in stellar cores can trigger an abnormal nuclear flow in the second branch of the CNO cycle (CNO-II), and that this phenomenon is of a general nature that can manifest in various stars at non-exploding stages of evolution, not just in the Sun.

## Method

The author calculated the emission rates of fast protons and alpha-particles from five key processes (Table 1) in a model star with mass M = 5 M_sun and metallicity Z = 0.02, using radial temperature and density profiles from the MESA code. The formalism of in-flight reaction probability was applied to compute suprathermal reaction rates for the reverse 14N(alpha,p)17O and forward 17O(p,alpha)14N processes, accounting for particle energy loss rates via Coulomb scattering in the plasma. The resulting rates were compared with thermal reaction rates to determine the direction of nuclear flow between 14N and 17O across different stellar radii.

## Key numbers

- The maximum alpha-particle emission rate R_alpha^max reaches 8 × 10^9 cm^{-3}s^{-1}, provided by the CNO reaction 15N(p,alpha)12C; in the Sun this rate is lower by two orders of magnitude
- The thermalization range of fast particles is at most 0.1 cm and thermalization time is at most 60 ps
- In the outer core region (R/R_sun > 0.7), the reverse (alpha,p) rate is twice as high as the forward (p,alpha) rate, causing flow redirection
- The abnormal flow occupies the outer core region constituting approximately 70% of the total core volume
- At R/R_sun >= 0.5, the suprathermal component increases the total power density for the 17O+p <-> alpha+14N processes up to 15 times
- In the outer core, suprathermal reactions increase the abundance of 17O and 18F by about 10%–30%
- The nuclear flow redirection point occurs at R_r/R_sun = 0.7 (compared to 0.08 in the Sun)
- The enhancement factor for 17O mass fraction is 1–1.3, for 18F mass fraction is 1–1.27, and for 18F neutrino emission rate is 1–1.27

## Context

This work extends the author's previous findings on suprathermal effects in the solar CNO cycle (Voronchev 2017a, 2017b, 2019) to a more massive star where the CNO cycle, rather than the pp chain, is the predominant burning mechanism. The results contradict the standard assumption that thermal reaction networks alone suffice for modeling CNO burning in stellar interiors, and suggest that suprathermal reactions must be included in nucleosynthesis models for stars with keV temperatures and densities of a few tens to a few hundreds g/cm^3. The paper enables future case-by-case studies of suprathermal effects in individual stars, as the author notes that no universal rule can accurately predict the strength of these effects across different objects.
