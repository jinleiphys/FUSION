# Electromagnetic structure of axial-vector mesons and implications for the muon $g-2$

> The electromagnetic structure of axial-vector mesons is investigated via elastic and two-photon transition form factors (TFFs). To this end, we employ a framework based on the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations within a contact interaction model. This largely algebraic approach transparently exposes the role of symmetries and their breaking, and has proven successful in describing anomaly-sensitive processes, including pseudoscalar to two-photon TFFs, $γ\to 3 π$, and vector-to-pseudoscalar radiative decays. Restricting our analysis to the lowest-lying states, $\text{A}=\{ a_1, f_1,f_1'\}$, we also evaluate the corresponding light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and obtain $a_μ^{\text{A}}=11.30(4.71)\times10^{-11}$, consistent with contemporary estimates.

## Key claim

The authors compute the light-by-light contribution of axial-vector mesons to the muon anomalous magnetic moment using a contact interaction model, obtaining a value consistent with contemporary estimates, but they note that their transition form factors at zero virtuality are systematically underestimated compared to data-driven constraints, primarily due to the simplified Bethe-Salpeter amplitude used for axial-vector mesons and the model's inability to simultaneously satisfy chiral anomaly constraints and reproduce the full tensor structure of these systems.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving contact interaction model based on the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using a modified rainbow-ladder truncation that includes an anomalous magnetic moment term in the quark-photon vertex to restore chiral anomalies. They compute the elastic electromagnetic form factors and two-photon transition form factors for the lowest-lying axial-vector mesons (a1, f1, f1') within the impulse approximation, and incorporate spin-orbit repulsion effects through a coupling parameter g_SO to correct the underestimated axial-vector meson masses that arise from the rainbow-ladder truncation.

## Key numbers

- Muon anomalous magnetic moment contribution from axial-vector mesons: a_μ^A = 11.30(4.71)×10^{-11}
- Axial-vector meson masses: m_a1 = 1.333(41) GeV (Case-I, reproducing experimental m_a1-m_ρ splitting), m_a1 = 1.230(41) GeV (Case-II, reproducing experimental m_a1), m_a1 = 1.080 GeV (Case-O, no spin-orbit repulsion)
- f1' meson masses: 1.553(35) GeV (Case-I), 1.469(31) GeV (Case-II), 1.352 GeV (Case-O)
- Transition form factors at zero virtuality (averaged over mass range): F_1^A(0,0) = 0.545(27), F_2^A(0,0) = 0.259(30), F_3^A(0,0) = -0.005(2)
- Transition radii: r_1^Aγ = 0.76(1) fm, r_2^Aγ = 0.86(1) fm, r_3^Aγ = 0.95(1) fm
- Magnetic moments: μ_a1 = 2.12(5), μ_f1' = 2.13(6)
- Quadrupole moments: Q_a1 = -0.69(6), Q_f1' = -0.50(12)
- Zero-crossing of electric form factor: x_c = Q_c^2/m_H^2 = 6.55(1.61) for a1, 5.61(1.43) for f1'

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction framework previously applied to pseudoscalar meson transition form factors (Dang 2023, Xing 2024) and vector meson electromagnetic structure (Xing 2021) to the axial-vector meson sector. The results are compared with data-driven TFF parametrizations from Hoferichter (2023) and Zanke (2021), and with the axial-vector meson contributions computed in Eichmann (2024). The study enables improved theoretical predictions for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, though the authors note that a more sophisticated truncation capturing both chiral anomalies and the full tensor structure of axial-vector meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes is needed to match low-energy constraints.
