# Binding Energy in Two and Three-Body Relativistic Dynamics

> Two-body and three-body systems of scalar bosons are considered in the framework of covariant constraint dynamics. The reduced equation obtained after eliminating redundant degrees of freedom can be viewed as an eigenvalue equation for an observable which is intimately related with the relative motion. We display the connection of this observable with binding energy.

# Binding Energy in Two and Three-Body Relativistic Dynamics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in covariant constraint dynamics, the binding energy (mass defect) of two-body and three-body scalar boson systems can be related to the eigenvalue of a translation-invariant observable N that depends only on relative variables; however, this relationship is straightforward only for two-body systems, while for three-body systems it requires a nontrivial canonical transformation and is tractable primarily in the limit where constituent masses are small compared to the total mass.

## Method

The authors start from coupled mass-shell constraint equations for scalar bosons, where each particle's squared-mass operator includes an identical interaction term 2V. For two-body systems, they eliminate redundant degrees of freedom by fixing the total momentum and solving the difference of constraints, yielding a reduced wave equation (N + λ)φ = 0 where N depends on relative transverse momenta and the interaction. For three-body systems, they apply a point transformation in momentum space to map the difference equations into a form that allows factorization of relative times, then sum the three mass-shell constraints to obtain a reduced equation (9m² - M²)ψ = 6Nψ, where N includes an additional term P²Ξ that has no two-body counterpart and must be expanded in powers of 1/M².

## Key numbers

- For two-body systems, the eigenvalue λ is related to total mass M and constituent masses m₁, m₂ by λ = M²/4 + ν²/M² - μ, where μ = (m₁² + m₂²)/2 and ν = (m₁² - m₂²)/2.
- For weak binding (|λ| ≪ m_a²), the mass defect for two bodies is m₁ + m₂ - M = -λ/(2m₀) + O(λ²), where m₀ is the reduced mass.
- For three-body equal-mass systems, the reduced equation gives 6λ = M² - 9m², and the mass defect is 3m - M = √(9m² + 6λ).
- For three-body weak binding (|λ| ≪ m²), the mass defect expands as 3m - M = -λ/m + (1/6)(λ²/m³) + ...
- The term P²Ξ in the three-body case expands as M²Ξ = (1/M²)Γ, where Γ₍₀₎ = (ŷ₂²)² + (ŷ₃²)² + (ŷ₂·ŷ₃)² + (ŷ₂² + ŷ₃²)(ŷ₂·ŷ₃) - ŷ₂²ŷ₃², a positive operator that survives even without interaction.

## Context

This work extends the covariant constraint dynamics approach previously developed by Droz-Vincent (2000) for three-body systems, building on earlier two-body formulations by Droz-Vincent (1985) and Bel and Stern (1980). The method relates to the Bethe-Salpeter equation and quasi-potential approaches as discussed by Sazdjian (1985) and Todorov (1971). The paper shows that while two-body systems can be directly mapped to a nonrelativistic form, three-body systems require a light-constituent approximation (m² ≪ M²) to achieve a similar reduction, and weak binding in three-body systems does not correspond to a nonrelativistic wave equation. Future work plans to incorporate spin and improve contact with other approaches.
