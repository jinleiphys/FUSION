# Probing exotic phenomena at the interface of nuclear and particle physics with the electric dipole moments of diamagnetic atoms: A unique window to hadronic and semi-leptonic CP violation

> The current status of electric dipole moments of diamagnetic atoms which involves the synergy between atomic experiments and three different theoretical areas -- particle, nuclear and atomic is reviewed. Various models of particle physics that predict CP violation, which is necessary for the existence of such electric dipole moments, are presented. These include the standard model of particle physics and various extensions of it. Effective hadron level combined charge conjugation (C) and parity (P) symmetry violating interactions are derived taking into consideration different ways in which a nucleon interacts with other nucleons as well as with electrons. Nuclear structure calculations of the CP-odd nuclear Schiff moment are discussed using the shell model and other theoretical approaches. Results of the calculations of atomic electric dipole moments due to the interaction of the nuclear Schiff moment with the electrons and the P and time-reversal (T) symmetry violating tensor-pseudotensor electron-nucleus are elucidated using different relativistic many-body theories. The principles of the measurement of the electric dipole moments of diamagnetic atoms are outlined. Upper limits for the nuclear Schiff moment and tensor-pseudotensor coupling constant are obtained combining the results of atomic experiments and relativistic many-body theories. The coefficients for the different sources of CP violation have been estimated at the elementary particle level for all the diamagnetic atoms of current experimental interest and their implications for physics beyond the standard model is discussed. Possible improvements of the current results of the measurements as well as quantum chromodynamics, nuclear and atomic calculations are suggested.

## Key claim

This review argues that electric dipole moments (EDMs) of diamagnetic atoms provide a uniquely powerful probe of hadronic and semi-leptonic CP violation beyond the Standard Model, but that extracting fundamental CP-violating couplings requires combining experimental limits with calculations from three distinct theoretical domains (particle physics, nuclear structure, and atomic many-body theory), each carrying its own uncertainties that must be reduced simultaneously.

## Method

The authors review the chain connecting elementary CP-violating operators (quark EDMs, quark chromo-EDMs, the QCD theta term, the Weinberg operator, CP-odd four-quark interactions, and CP-odd electron-quark interactions) to hadronic CP-odd interactions, then to the nuclear Schiff moment via shell-model and other nuclear-structure calculations, and finally to atomic EDMs via relativistic many-body theories. They compile experimental upper limits for diamagnetic atoms (notably Hg, Xe, and Ra) and combine these with theoretical calculations to extract constraints on the underlying CP-violating couplings at the elementary-particle level.

## Key numbers

* The current experimental limit on the mercury-199 EDM is \(d_{\rm Hg} < 7.4 \times 10^{-30} e\) cm (Graner, 2016), the lowest EDM limit reported for any system.
* The Standard Model contribution to the nucleon EDM from the CKM phase is around \(d_N \sim 10^{-35} e\) cm, far below the neutron EDM experimental limit of \(d_n < 10^{-26} e\) cm (Baker; Pendlebury).
* In the MSSM with supersymmetric masses \(M_{\rm SUSY} = O({\rm TeV})\) and CP phases of order unity, the electron EDM is \(d_e = O(10^{-27}) e\) cm, the quark EDM is \(d_q = O(10^{-25}) e\) cm, and the quark chromo-EDM is \(d^c_f = O(10^{-25})\) cm at the 1 TeV scale.
* In the two-Higgs-doublet model, the quark chromo-EDM from Barr-Zee diagrams scales as \(d^c_q \sim Y_t^{(+)} \times 10^{-25}\) cm for Yukawa couplings \(Y_q^{(\pm)} \sim 10^{-6}\), and the Weinberg operator is typically \(w \sim 10^{-10}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) for a Higgs mass of 125 GeV.
* The Standard Model electron EDM from the CKM phase is \(d_e \sim 10^{-44} e\) cm (four-loop level), and the QCD theta term generated by the CKM phase is \(\bar \theta \sim 10^{-17}\), yielding a nucleon EDM of \(|d_N| \sim 10^{-33} e\) cm.

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the framework connecting particle-physics CP violation to atomic EDM experiments, building on earlier reviews by Khriplovich, Pospelov and Ritz, and Engel. It covers all diamagnetic atoms of current experimental interest (Hg, Xe, Ra) and provides the first comprehensive compilation of coefficients linking elementary CP-odd operators to atomic EDMs across the full chain of QCD, nuclear, and atomic calculations. The work enables future experimental improvements and theoretical refinements to jointly constrain or discover new CP-violating physics beyond the Standard Model, particularly in models such as the two-Higgs-doublet model, supersymmetric extensions, left-right symmetric models, vectorlike fermion models, and leptoquark models.
