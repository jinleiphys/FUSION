# Renormalization persistency of tensor force in nuclei

> In this work we analyze the tensor-force component of effective interactions appropriate for nuclear shell-model studies, with particular emphasis on the monopole term of the interactions. Standard nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions such as AV8' and $χ$N$^3$LO are tailored to shell-model studies by employing $V_{low k}$ techniques to handle the short-range repulsion of the $NN$ interactions and by applying many-body perturbation theory to incorporate in-medium effects. We show, via numerical studies of effective interactions for the $sd$ and $pf$ shells, that the tensor-force contribution to the monopole term of the effective interaction is barely changed by these renormalization procedures, resulting in almost the same monopole term as the one of the bare $NN$ interactions. We propose to call this feature {\it Renormalization Persistency} of the tensor force, as it is a remarkable property of the renormalization and should have many interesting consequences in nuclear systems. For higher multipole terms, this feature is maintained to a somewhat smaller extent. We present general intuitive explanations for the renormalization persistency of the tensor force, as well as analyses of core-polarization terms in perturbation theory. The central force does not exhibit a similar renormalization persistency.

## Key claim

The tensor-force contribution to the monopole term of the effective shell-model interaction is barely changed by renormalization procedures (Vlowk and many-body perturbation theory), resulting in almost the same monopole term as that of the bare NN interactions; this "Renormalization Persistency" holds for the monopole term and, to a somewhat smaller extent, for higher multipole terms, while the central force does not exhibit a similar persistency.

## Method

The authors start from bare realistic NN interactions (AV8' and chiral χN³LO), apply Vlowk techniques with a sharp cutoff Λ to integrate out high-momentum components, and then use many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) with a Q-box expansion up to second and third order, including folded diagrams, to derive effective interactions for the sd-shell and pf-shell model spaces. The tensor-force component is extracted from the resulting effective interactions via spin-tensor decomposition (setting rank p=2 in the decomposition formula), and the monopole matrix elements are computed using harmonic-oscillator bases with ℏω = 14 MeV (sd-shell) and 11 MeV (pf-shell).

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Λ of Vlowk varied from 1.0 fm⁻¹ to 5.0 fm⁻¹; typical cutoff value around 2.0 fm⁻¹ (determined by best reproduction of binding energies of ³H and ⁴He)
- For the Vlowk interaction derived from AV8', the monopole part of the tensor force shows almost no cutoff dependence for Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹ and 5.0 fm⁻¹, both in sd-shell and pf-shell
- Harmonic oscillator energies: ℏω = 14 MeV for sd-shell, ℏω = 11 MeV for pf-shell
- Full convergence of the monopole part of Veff is obtained with approximately 8–10 ℏω excitations in each Q-box diagram (using Vlowk with Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹)
- The effective central force ΔVeff from tensor-force second-order effects in the deuteron ³S₁ channel is comparable to VC in strength

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Otsuka (2005) that the tensor force plays a key role in shell evolution toward the drip lines, where previously the tensor-force component in effective interactions was modelled via π and ρ meson exchange only. The paper provides a microscopic justification for that approach by demonstrating that the tensor-force monopole term survives renormalization procedures essentially unchanged. This enables future shell-model studies to use the tensor force from bare NN interactions directly, without needing to renormalize it, and supports the interpretation of shell evolution phenomena in neutron-rich nuclei as driven by the tensor force.
