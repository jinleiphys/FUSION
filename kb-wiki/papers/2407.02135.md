# The role of the effective range in strongly-interacting few-body systems

> Strongly interacting systems appear in several areas of physics and are characterized by attractive interactions that can almost, or just barely, loosely bind two particles. Although this definition is made at the two-body level, this gives rise to fascinating effects in larger systems, including the so-called Efimov physics. In this context, the zero-range theory aims to describe low-energy properties based only on the scattering length. However, for a broad range of physical applications, the finite range of the interactions plays an important role. In this work, I discuss some aspects of finite-range effects in strongly interacting systems. I present the zero-range and shapeless universalities in two-body systems with applications in atomic and nuclear physics. I derived an analytical expression for the $s$-wave bound-state spectrum of the modified Pöschl-Teller potential for two particles in three dimensions, which is compared with the approximations to illustrate their usefulness. Concerning three identical bosons, I presented a trimer energy scaling function that explicitly includes the effective range. The implications for larger systems are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
Finite-range corrections, parameterized by the effective range \(r_0\), are necessary to accurately describe low-energy properties of strongly-interacting few-body systems beyond the zero-range approximation based solely on the scattering length; the paper derives an analytical bound-state spectrum for the modified Poschl-Teller potential and presents a trimer energy scaling function that explicitly includes the effective range, but notes that universal behavior is only observed when the trimer size is much larger than the range of the microscopic force.

## Method
The author derived an analytical expression for the \(s\)-wave bound-state energies of the modified Poschl-Teller potential for two particles in three dimensions by solving the radial Schrodinger equation with appropriate boundary conditions and using hypergeometric functions. For three identical bosons, the Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation was solved with first-order effective range corrections, and the resulting trimer energies were fit to a proposed universal scaling function \(F(x,y)\) expressed as a series expansion in dimensionless parameters \(x\) (inverse scattering length) and \(y\) (effective range). Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations with microscopic two- and three-body interactions were then compared to the scaling function to test universality.

## Key numbers
- For the \(^4\)He dimer: scattering length \(a = 9.04\) nm, binding length \(a_B = 8.65\) nm.
- For the deuteron (p-n, \(1^+\)): scattering length \(a = 5.42\) fm, binding length \(a_B = 4.32\) fm.
- For neutron-neutron (\(0^+\)): scattering length \(a = -18.90\) fm, binding length \(a_B = -20.19\) fm.
- The analytical bound-state spectrum for the modified Poschl-Teller potential is \(E = -\frac{\hbar^2 \mu^2}{2 m_r} (\lambda - 2 - 2n)^2\) for \(n = 0,1,2,\dots\) with \(n \leq \lambda/2 - 1\).
- The shapeless approximation (including effective range) yields results differing by only a few percent for \(r_0/a \lesssim 0.1\) when compared to the analytical dimer energy.

## Context
This work extends the zero-range theory of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970, 1981) and the effective range expansion introduced by Bethe (1949) to include finite-range corrections in both two-body and three-body systems. It builds on prior studies of range corrections for the Phillips line (Efimov and Tkachenko 1985) and the Gaussian parametrization approach of Gattobigio and Kievsky (2014-2020). The derived scaling function for trimers enables systematic investigation of finite-range effects in Efimov trimers and opens the possibility of extending such universal descriptions to larger bosonic clusters, as recently studied by Carlson (2017) for up to \(N=60\) particles.
