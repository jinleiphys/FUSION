# Hidden-Bottom Pentaquarks

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently reported strong evidences of the existence of pentaquark states in the hidden-charm baryon sector, the so-called $P_c(4380)^+$ and $P_c(4450)^+$ signals. Five-quark bound states in the hidden-charm sector were explored by us using, for the quark-quark interaction, a chiral quark model which successfully explains meson and baryon phenomenology, from the light to the heavy quark sector. We extend herein such study but to the hidden-bottom pentaquark sector, analyzing possible bound-states with spin-parity quantum numbers $J^P=\frac12^\pm$, $\frac32^\pm$ and $\frac52^\pm$, and in the $\frac12$ and $\frac32$ isospin sectors. We do not find positive parity hidden-bottom pentaquark states; however, several candidates with negative parity are found with dominant baryon-meson structures $Σ_b^{(\ast)}\bar{B}^{(\ast)}$. The calculated distances among any pair of quarks within the bound-state reflect that molecular-type bound-states are favored when only color-singlet configurations are considered in the coupled-channels calculation whereas compact pentaquarks, which are also deeply bound, can be found when hidden-color configurations are added. Finally, our findings resemble the ones found in the hidden-charm sector but, as expected, we find in the hidden-bottom sector larger binding energies and bigger contributions of the hidden-color configurations.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous chiral quark model study of hidden-charm pentaquarks to the hidden-bottom sector, finding that no positive-parity hidden-bottom pentaquark states exist, but several negative-parity candidates are bound; the binding energies are larger and hidden-color configurations contribute more significantly than in the hidden-charm case, though the results depend on whether only color-singlet or also hidden-color configurations are included in the coupled-channels calculation.

## Method

The authors use a chiral quark model (ChQM) with a Hamiltonian containing confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions, with parameters previously fitted to hadron, hadron-hadron, and multiquark phenomenology. They solve the five-body bound-state problem via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle with a Gaussian expansion method (GEM), considering both color-singlet and hidden-color configurations for the \(uudb\bar{b}\) system with isospin \(I=1/2\) and \(3/2\), and spin-parity \(J^P = \frac12^\pm, \frac32^\pm, \frac52^\pm\). The spatial wave function uses infinitesimally-shifted Gaussians on Jacobi coordinates, and the Pauli principle is enforced via antisymmetrization operators for the two quark arrangements \((udb)(\bar{b}u)+(uub)(\bar{b}d)\) and \((uud)(\bar{b}b)\).

## Key numbers

- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac12^-)\): the \(\Sigma_b\bar{B}\) channel yields a bound state at mass \(11080\) MeV (color-singlet only) with binding energy \(-15\) MeV; the coupled-channels (singlet+hidden-color) calculation gives mass \(11078\) MeV with binding energy \(-17\) MeV and 98.5% color-singlet component.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac12^-)\): the \(\Sigma_b\bar{B}^*\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(11043\) MeV with binding energy \(-93\) MeV and 57.9% color-singlet, 42.1% hidden-color composition.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac12^-)\): the \(\Sigma^*_b\bar{B}^*\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(10861\) MeV with binding energy \(-292\) MeV and 15.8% color-singlet, 84.2% hidden-color composition.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac32^-)\): the \(\Sigma_b\bar{B}^*\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(11122\) MeV with binding energy \(-14\) MeV and 99.6% color-singlet component.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac32^-)\): the \(\Sigma^*_b\bar{B}\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(11045\) MeV with binding energy \(-67\) MeV and 55.5% color-singlet, 44.5% hidden-color composition.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac32^-)\): the \(\Sigma^*_b\bar{B}^*\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(10958\) MeV with binding energy \(-195\) MeV and 22.2% color-singlet, 77.8% hidden-color composition.
- For \(I(J^P)=\frac12(\frac52^-)\): the \(\Sigma^*_b\bar{B}^*\) channel in coupled-channels gives mass \(11140\) MeV with binding energy \(-13\) MeV and 99.6% color-singlet component.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of hidden-charm pentaquarks (Yang et al., 2015) to the hidden-bottom sector, motivated by the LHCb observation of the \(P_c(4380)^+\) and \(P_c(4450)^+\) states (LHCb Collaboration, 2015). The results resemble those found in the hidden-charm sector but with larger binding energies and bigger hidden-color contributions, as expected from the heavier bottom quark. The findings provide predictions for future experimental searches for hidden-bottom pentaquarks, complementing theoretical studies such as those by Shimizu et al. (2016) who predicted hidden-bottom pentaquarks around \(11.08-11.11\) GeV with \(J^P=3/2^-\), and the coupled-channel unitary approach of Wu et al. (2010).
