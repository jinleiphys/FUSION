# The shear viscosity of two-flavor crystalline color superconducting quark matter

> We present the first calculation of the shear viscosity for two-flavor plane wave (FF) color superconducting quark matter. This is a member of the family of crystalline color superconducting phases of dense quark matter that may be present in the cores of neutron stars. The paired quarks in the FF phase feature gapless excitations on surfaces of crescent shaped blocking regions in momentum space and participate in transport. We calculate their contribution to the shear viscosity. We note that they also lead to dynamic screening of transverse $t^1$, $t^2$, $t^3$ gluons which are undamped in the 2SC phase. The exchange of these gluons is the most important mechanism of the scattering of the paired quarks. We find that the shear viscosity of the paired quarks is roughly a factor of $100$ smaller compared to the shear viscosity of unpaired quark matter. Our results may have implications for the damping of $r-$modes in rapidly rotating, cold neutron stars.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of paired quarks in the two-flavor plane wave (FF) crystalline color superconducting phase is roughly a factor of 100 smaller than the shear viscosity of unpaired quark matter, with the reduction depending on the properties of the gluon mediators rather than simply on the reduced phase space of gapless excitations.

## Method

The authors solved the multi-component Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for Bogoliubov quasi-particles in the FF phase, accounting for the anisotropic crescent-shaped gapless regions in momentum space. They calculated the collision integral numerically for scattering of paired quarks mediated by exchange of transverse t^1, t^2, and t^3 gluons, which are dynamically screened (Landau damped) in the FF phase unlike in the 2SC phase where they remain undamped. The calculation separated the two Bogoliubov branches into particle-like and hole-like modes relative to the chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity of paired quarks in the FF phase is roughly a factor of 100 smaller compared to the shear viscosity of unpaired quark matter (no explicit numerical value for the viscosity itself is given in the truncated text).
* The splitting between Fermi surfaces is given by 2δp_F = m_s^2/(4μ) from the strange quark mass stress.
* The condition for disruption of symmetric CFL pairing occurs when m_s^2/μ > 2Δ_{0CFL}.
* Gapless excitations in the FF phase appear on surfaces defined by |ξ| = ±√(δμ^2 - Δ^2) when Δ < δμ.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transport properties in crystalline color superconducting phases, which were motivated by the discovery of chromomagnetic instabilities in gapless 2SC and gapless CFL phases by Huang (2004) and Casalbuoni (2004). The calculation is the first shear viscosity result for the FF phase, a member of the LOFF family originally proposed by Fulde and Ferrell (1964) and Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964). The results enable modeling of r-mode damping in rapidly rotating cold neutron stars, where previous work by Alford (2013) showed that unpaired quark matter provides sufficient damping while CFL matter does not.
