# Collective modes of gluons in an anisotropic thermo-magnetic medium

> We study the collective modes of gluons in an anisotropic thermal medium in the presence of a constant background magnetic field using the hard-thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory. The momentum space anisotropy of the medium has been incorporated through the generalized $`$Romatschke-Strickland' form of the distribution function, whereas, the magnetic modification arising from the quark loop contribution has been taken into account in the lowest Landau level approximation. We consider two special cases: (i) a spheroidal anisotropy with the anisotropy vector orthogonal to the external magnetic field and (ii) an ellipsoidal anisotropy with two mutually orthogonal vectors describing aniostropies along and orthogonal to the field direction. The general structure of the polarization tensor in both cases are equivalent and consists of six independent basis tensors. We find that the introduction of momentum anisotropy ingrains azimuthal angular dependence in the thermo-magnetic collective modes. Our study suggests that the presence of a strong background magnetic field can significantly reduce the growth rate of the unstable modes which may have important implications in the equilibration of magnetized quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors study gluon collective modes in an anisotropic thermal medium with a constant background magnetic field, finding that a strong magnetic field can significantly reduce the growth rate of unstable modes, which may have implications for the equilibration of magnetized quark-gluon plasma. The key condition is that the analysis is performed in the lowest Landau level approximation, valid when the hierarchy \(\sqrt{|eB|}\gg T\gg g_sT\) holds.

## Method

The authors use hard-thermal-loop (HTL) perturbation theory within the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to compute the one-loop gluon self-energy. Momentum-space anisotropy is incorporated via the generalized Romatschke-Strickland form of the distribution function, while magnetic modifications from the quark loop are treated in the lowest Landau level approximation. Two special cases are considered: spheroidal anisotropy with the anisotropy vector orthogonal to the magnetic field, and ellipsoidal anisotropy with two mutually orthogonal vectors describing anisotropies along and orthogonal to the field direction. The gluon collective modes are obtained from the poles of the effective propagator derived from the Dyson-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass from the quark loop contribution is \(\bar{m}_D^2 = \sum_f g_s^2 \frac{|e_f B|}{4\pi^2}\), and the total Debye mass is \(\hat{m}_D = \sqrt{\tilde{m}_D^2 + \bar{m}_D^2}\), where \(\tilde{m}_D^2 = \frac{g_s^2 \Lambda_T^2}{3} N_c\) with \(N_c=3\).
- The ratio of the squared Debye masses from quark and gluon contributions is \(\frac{\bar{m}_D^2}{\tilde{m}_D^2} = \mathcal{R}^2 \sum_f |q_f|\), where \(2\pi\mathcal{R} = \sqrt{|eB|}/\Lambda_T\).
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, a magnetic field of strength \(\sim 100 m_\pi^2\) rapidly decays to a very low value within around 0.1 fm/c after the initial impact, following a \(1/t^3\) behavior.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermo-magnetic collective modes by Hattori et al. (2017) and Karmakar et al. (2018) by incorporating momentum-space anisotropy alongside the magnetic field. It also builds on the Romatschke-Strickland (2003, 2004) formalism for anisotropic distribution functions and the ellipsoidal generalization by Ghosh et al. (2020). The results enable further investigation of plasma instabilities and thermalization dynamics in magnetized quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the regime where the magnetic field is strong enough to cause dimensional reduction to 1+1 dimensions.
