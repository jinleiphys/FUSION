# Probes of the quark-gluon plasma and plasma instabilities

> Penetrating probes in heavy-ion collisions, like jets and photons, are sensitive to the transport coefficients of the produced quark-gluon plasma, such as shear and bulk viscosity. Quantifying this sensitivity requires a detailed understanding of photon emission and jet-medium interaction in a non-equilibrium plasma. Up to now, such an understanding has been hindered by plasma instabilities which arise out of equilibrium and lead to spurious divergences when evaluating the rate of interaction of hard probes with the plasma. In this paper, we show that taking into account the time evolution of an unstable plasma cures these divergences. We calculate the time evolution of gluon two-point correlators in a setup with small initial momentum anisotropy and show that the gluon occupation density grows exponentially at early times. Based on this calculation, we argue for a phenomenological prescription where instability poles are subtracted. Finally, we show that in the Abelian case instability fields do not affect medium-induced photon emission to our order of approximation.

## Key claim

Taking into account the time evolution of an unstable plasma cures the spurious divergences that arise when evaluating hard probe interactions in a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma, provided one considers a setup with small initial momentum anisotropy and early times where the hard-thermal-loop approximation remains valid.

## Method

The authors calculate the time evolution of gluon two-point correlators in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma with a slightly anisotropic initial quasiparticle distribution, where the anisotropy parameter xi is much smaller than the coupling constant g. They solve the Dyson equation for the retarded propagator using a contour integration that goes above all instability poles in the upper half complex plane, and derive the rr correlator by separating the soft scale g Lambda from the instability growth rate scale xi g Lambda. For the Abelian case, they evaluate whether instability fields affect medium-induced photon emission at their order of approximation.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy parameter xi is defined as xi ~ |<p_z> - <p_perp>| / <p_z> and is assumed to satisfy xi << g, where g << 1 is the coupling constant.
- The instability growth rate gamma is of order xi g Lambda, where Lambda is the energy scale of hard quasiparticles.
- Soft gluons have energy scale g Lambda, while hard quasiparticles have energy scale Lambda.
- The retarded correlator for instability modes gives exponential growth as e^{gamma (t_x - t_y)} for t_x > t_y.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hard probes in non-equilibrium plasmas that encountered divergent rates due to Weibel instabilities, as seen in calculations of jet-medium interaction by Baier (2008) and Romatschke (2006), heavy-quark potential by Nopoush (2017), and quasiparticle interaction rates by Arnold (2002). The authors' prescription of subtracting instability poles enables finite calculations of photon emission and jet splitting rates, which can be combined with hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions. The approach is supported by classical-statistical simulations by Berges (2013) suggesting that plasma instabilities only play a role in the very early stages of heavy-ion collisions.
