# Probing topological order of QCD at Electron Ion Collider

> The idea that the nuclear matter may posses long range topological order is supported by the theory and the lattice calculations. At high temperature this order is instrumental in producing anomalous phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect. In the cold nuclear matter it affects the gluon distribution in the nuclear wave function at low $x$. The effect of the topological order is encapsulated in the unintegrated gluon distribution functions which are proportional, at the leading order, to the square of the gluon propagator at finite topological charge density. It is argued that the Electron Ion Collider is well suited to study the topological order of the cold nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that cold nuclear matter may possess long-range topological order, and that this order can be probed at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC) through measurements of unintegrated gluon distribution functions at small Bjorken x, specifically in the range between 0.1 and 0.01, where the coherence length exceeds the typical scale of topological charge variation but gluon saturation effects are not yet dominant.

## Method

The author derives the gluon propagator in a topologically non-trivial background characterized by a constant vector βμ = cA∂μθ, where θ is the pseudo-scalar field conjugate to topological charge density. Using this propagator, the scattering amplitude for a gluon dipole on a nucleon is calculated at order O(αs²) in the eikonal approximation. The resulting amplitude is then used to compute the unintegrated gluon distribution functions φ(1) and φ(2) for a heavy nucleus, which are proportional to the square of the gluon propagator at finite topological charge density.

## Key numbers

- The coherence length for small-x interactions is ℓc ∼ fm/x, which can exceed the nuclear size R = A^(1/3) fm for sufficiently small x.
- The kinematic region proposed for study at EIC is k⊥ ≪ Qs, with x in the range 10^(-2) to 10^(-1).
- The saturation momentum is given by Qs² = 4π αs² ρ T(b), characterizing the gluon density of the nucleus.
- In the topologically trivial background (β = 0), the average scattering amplitude is A⟨Γ^N⟩ = r² Qs² ln(1/r)/4.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of topological effects in QCD, previously studied in the context of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in hot nuclear matter (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008), to cold nuclear matter at small x. It builds on the argument by Zhitnitsky (2013) that topological correlation lengths can significantly exceed 1 fm, and on lattice calculations (Horvath 2003, Alexandru 2005, Ilgenfritz 2007) supporting the existence of topological order. The paper proposes that the EIC (Accardi 2012) can study these effects through event-by-event fluctuations of azimuthal angle correlations in particle spectra, which are expected to grow as x decreases toward 0.1 and then decrease at even smaller x due to gluon saturation (Gribov 1984).
