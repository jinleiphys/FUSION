# Binding of Hypernuclei in the Latest Quark-Meson Coupling Model

> The most recent development of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which the effect of the mean scalar field in-medium on the hyperfine interaction is also included self-consistently, is used to compute the properties of finite hypernuclei. The calculations for $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hypernuclei are of comparable quality to earlier QMC results without the additional parameter needed there. Even more significantly, the additional repulsion associated with the increased hyperfine interaction in-medium completely changes the predictions for $Σ$ hypernuclei. Whereas in the earlier work they were bound by an amount similar to $Λ$ hypernuclei, here they are unbound, in qualitative agreement with the experimental absence of such states. The equivalent non-relativistic potential felt by the $Σ$ is repulsive inside the nuclear interior and weakly attractive in the nuclear surface, as suggested by the analysis of $Σ$-atoms.

## Key claim

Including the self-consistent effect of the mean scalar field on the hyperfine interaction within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model changes the predictions for Sigma hypernuclei from bound to unbound, in qualitative agreement with the experimental absence of such states beyond A=4, while maintaining comparable quality for Lambda and Xi hypernuclei without additional parameters.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic shell model in the Hartree approximation for the nucleon core and Hartree-Fock couplings for the hyperons, with the baryon masses parametrized as functions of the scalar field based on an improved MIT bag model. The hyperfine interaction was multiplied by a factor F_s^n (with n the number of strange quarks) to account for short-distance enhancement of the quark wave function, with F_s = 0.726 and strange quark mass m_s = 0.297 GeV fitted to the Lambda, Sigma, and Xi masses. For the Sigma hyperon, an additional vector repulsion term lambda_Sigma = 50.3 MeV-fm^3 was included to model the effect of the coupled Sigma N - Lambda N channel.

## Key numbers

- Lambda binding energy in the 1s_{1/2} level of Pb-208: 26.9 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental value of 26.3 +/- 0.8 MeV
- Sigma^0 binding energy in the 1s_{1/2} level of Pb-208: 3.3 MeV (calculated), representing a reduction of more than 23 MeV relative to the Lambda
- Xi^0 binding energy in the 1s_{1/2} level of Pb-209: 15.0 MeV (calculated)
- Lambda binding energy in the 1s_{1/2} level of O-17: 16.2 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental value of 12.42 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.36 MeV
- Effective non-relativistic potential for Sigma^0 in Calcium reaches approximately 30 MeV repulsive at the center
- Effective non-relativistic potential for Sigma^0 in Lead reaches approximately 12 MeV repulsive at the center
- Hyperfine splitting enhancement parameter epsilon approximately 7% at nuclear matter density, leading to approximately 20 MeV less binding for Sigma relative to Lambda
- Bag model parameters: B = 0.5541 fm^{-1}, Z_0 = 2.6422, alpha_s = 0.4477, with nucleon radius 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the QMC model previously applied to nuclear matter and neutron stars by Rikovska Stone (2006) to finite hypernuclei, addressing the major problem that the original QMC model predicted Sigma hypernuclei bound similarly to Lambda hypernuclei. The improved treatment of the hyperfine interaction, motivated by Barnes (1984), produces results consistent with the phenomenological analysis of Sigma-atoms by Batty (1994) and provides parameter-free predictions for Xi hypernuclei that can be tested at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR.
