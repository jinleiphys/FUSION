# Vector Manifestation and Fate of Vector Mesons in Dense Matter

> We describe in-medium properties of hadrons in dense matter near chiral restoration using a Wilsonian matching to QCD of an effective field theory with hidden local symmetry at the chiral cutoff $Λ$. We find that chiral symmetry is restored in vector manifestation à la Harada and Yamawaki at a critical matter density $n_c$. We express the critical density in terms of QCD correlators in dense matter at the matching scale. In a manner completely analogous to what happens at the critical $N_f^c$ and at the critical temperature $T^c$, the vector meson mass is found to vanish (in the chiral limit) at chiral restoration. This result provides a support for Brown-Rho scaling predicted a decade ago.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, in the chiral limit, the vector meson mass vanishes at a critical matter density \(n_c\) where chiral symmetry is restored, in a manner completely analogous to what happens at a critical number of flavors \(N_f^c\) and at the critical temperature \(T_c\). This result is conditional on the assumption that the chiral transition is not strongly first order, and that the quark condensate approaches zero continuously as \(n \rightarrow n_c\).

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory with hidden local symmetry (HLS) and perform a Wilsonian matching of the bare parameters of the HLS Lagrangian to QCD correlators at the chiral cutoff \(\Lambda \approx \Lambda_\chi\). They extend this matching, originally formulated for vacuum and finite temperature, to non-zero baryon density by introducing an "intrinsic" density dependence into the bare parameters. Near the critical density, quasiquark degrees of freedom are included explicitly, and the renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the parameters \(F_\pi\), \(a\), \(g\), and the quasiquark mass \(m_q\) are solved to show that the fixed point \((g, a, m_q) = (0, 1, 0)\) is reached, leading to a vanishing vector meson pole mass.

## Key numbers

* The critical number of flavors for chiral restoration at zero temperature and density is \(N_f^c \sim 5\) (from Harada and Yamawaki).
* The critical temperature for chiral restoration for \(N_f = 3\) is \(T_c \sim 250\) MeV (from Harada and Sasaki).
* The critical density \(n_c\) is determined by the condition that the bare pion decay constant at the matching scale satisfies \(F_\pi^2(\Lambda; n_c) = \frac{N_f}{2(4\pi)^2} \Lambda^2\), which requires \(F_\pi^2(\Lambda; n_c) / F_\pi^2(\Lambda; 0) \sim 3/5\).
* At the critical density, the on-shell pion decay constant \(f_\pi(\mu_c) = 0\) and the \(\rho\) meson pole mass \(m_\rho(\mu_c) = 0\) (in the chiral limit).

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) scenario of Harada and Yamawaki, previously shown to hold for large \(N_f\) and at finite temperature, to the case of high baryon density. The result provides a theoretical foundation for the Brown-Rho scaling predicted a decade earlier, which proposed that vector meson masses drop in dense nuclear matter. The authors note that their scenario differs from the standard sigma-model scenario for chiral restoration and that the dropping-mass vector meson will become sharper with vanishing width near the critical point, in contrast to strong-coupling models that predict an expanding width (Rapp, 1999). The paper also discusses how the "intrinsic" density dependence, governed by Wilsonian matching, is missing in many model descriptions, such as the Rapp-Wambach approach (Brown et al., 1998; Klingl et al., 1999).
