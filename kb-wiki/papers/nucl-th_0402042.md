# Charmonium chemistry in A+A collisions at relativistic energies

> Charmonium production and suppression in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies is investigated within different models, i.e. the comover absorption model, the threshold suppression model, the statistical coalescence model and the HSD transport approach. In HSD the charmonium dissociation cross sections with mesons are described by a simple phase-space parametrization including an effective coupling strength $|M_i|^2$ for the charmonium states $i=χ_c, J/ψ, ψ^\prime$. This allows to include the backward channels for charmonium reproduction by $D \bar{D}$ channels -- which are missed in the comover absorption and threshold suppression model -- employing detailed balance without introducing any new parameters. It is found that all approaches yield a reasonable description of $J/ψ$ suppression in S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies. However, they differ significantly in the $ψ^\prime/J/ψ$ ratio versus centrality at SPS and especially at RHIC energies. These pronounced differences can be exploited in future measurements at RHIC to distinguish the hadronic rescattering scenarios from quark coalescence close to the QGP phase boundary.

## Key claim

All four models (comover absorption, threshold suppression, statistical coalescence, and HSD transport) give a reasonable description of J/ψ suppression in S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but they differ significantly in the ψ′/J/ψ ratio versus centrality at SPS and especially at RHIC energies, which can be exploited in future measurements to distinguish hadronic rescattering scenarios from quark coalescence near the QGP phase boundary.

## Method

The authors compare four approaches: the comover absorption model, the threshold suppression model, the statistical coalescence model (SCM), and the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach. In HSD, charmonium dissociation cross sections with mesons are described by a phase-space parametrization with an effective coupling strength |M_i|^2 for each charmonium state i = χ_c, J/ψ, ψ′, which allows inclusion of backward channels for charmonium reproduction by D D̅ channels via detailed balance without new parameters. The SCM assumes charmonia form at hadronization near the QGP phase boundary, with the distribution of c and c̅ among hadron states controlled by statistical laws using temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, and volume V.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear absorption cross section: σ_abs = 4.4 mb, taken from SPS data analysis
- Effective comover dissociation cross section for J/ψ: σ_co^{J/ψ} = 1.0 mb, obtained from fit to NA50 Pb+Pb data
- Effective comover dissociation cross section for ψ′: σ_co^{ψ′} = 5 mb, from fit of ψ′/J/ψ ratio in S+U collisions at SPS
- Threshold participant densities for suppression: n_χ = 2.0 fm^{-2} (χ_c), n_{J/ψ} = 3.8 fm^{-2} (J/ψ), n_{ψ′} = 1.7 fm^{-2} (ψ′)
- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), scaled threshold densities: n_χ* ≈ 1.3 fm^{-2}, n_{J/ψ}* ≈ 2.5 fm^{-2}, n_{ψ′}* ≈ 1.1 fm^{-2}
- HSD matrix element squared: |M_0|^2 = 0.17 fm/GeV^2, fixed by J/ψ suppression data from NA38 and NA50
- Charm production cross section at RHIC: σ^{NN}_{c\bar{c}} ≈ 650 μb, giving N̅_{c\bar{c}} ≈ 16–17 for central Au+Au collisions
- SCM freeze-out parameters at RHIC: T = 177 MeV, μ_B = 29 MeV
- HSD predicts ~16 D D̅ pairs in central Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV, a factor of ~160 relative to primordial J/ψ multiplicity

## Context

This work extends previous HSD studies (Bratkovskaya 2003) by focusing on the ψ′/J/ψ ratio as a discriminating observable. It contrasts the suppression-only models (comover model of Capella and threshold model of Blaizot) with the statistical coalescence model (Braun-Munzinger, Stachel) and the HSD transport approach that includes charmonium regeneration via D D̅ annihilation. The results enable future RHIC measurements to distinguish between hadronic rescattering scenarios and quark coalescence near the QGP phase boundary, addressing open questions about whether charm quarks achieve thermal equilibrium and whether charmonium formation from open charm mesons becomes essential at higher energies.
