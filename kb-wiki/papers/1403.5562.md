# Sivers Effect in Two-Hadron Electroproduction

> The Sivers effect in single hadron semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a transversely polarized nucleon describes the modulation of the cross section with the sine of the azimuthal angle between the produced hadron's transverse momentum and the nucleon spin ($\boldsymbol{P}_h$ and $φ_S$ respectively). This effect is attributed to the so-called Sivers parton distribution function of the nucleon. We employ a simple phenomenological parton model to derive the relevant cross section for two-hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering including the Sivers effect. We show that the Sivers effect can be observed in such process as sine modulations involving the azimuthal angles $φ_T$ and $φ_R$ of both the total and the relative transverse momenta of the hadron pair. The existence of the modulation with respect to $φ_R$ is new. Finally, we employ a modified version of the $\tt{LEPTO}$ Monte Carlo event generator that includes the Sivers effect to estimate the size of single spin asymmetries corresponding to these modulations. We show that $\sin(φ_R-φ_S)$ modulations can be significant, especially if we impose asymmetric cuts on the momenta of the hadrons in the pairs.

## Key claim

The Sivers effect in two-hadron semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering produces a new, previously unrecognized azimuthal modulation proportional to \(\sin(\varphi_R - \varphi_S)\) in addition to the known \(\sin(\varphi_T - \varphi_S)\) modulation, and these modulations can be significant especially when asymmetric kinematic cuts are imposed on the momenta of the two hadrons.

## Method

The authors employed a simple phenomenological parton model with Gaussian transverse momentum distributions for both the unpolarized and Sivers parton distribution functions (PDFs) and for the unpolarized dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF), including a correlation term between the transverse momenta of the two hadrons. They derived the leading-twist cross section for two-hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on a transversely polarized nucleon, expressing it in terms of the total transverse momentum \(\mathbf{P}_T\) and the relative transverse momentum \(\mathbf{R}\) of the hadron pair. They then used a modified version of the LEPTO Monte Carlo event generator (mLEPTO) that includes the Sivers effect at leading order to simulate single-spin asymmetries (SSAs) for COMPASS kinematics, first validating the generator against single-hadron Sivers asymmetry data and then predicting two-hadron SSAs.

## Key numbers

- Generated \(10^{11}\) DIS events in mLEPTO using COMPASS kinematics: muon beam energy \(E_\mu = 160\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(Q^2 > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(0.1 < y < 0.9\), \(0.03 < x < 0.7\), \(W > 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- For single-hadron SSA validation, kinematic cuts were \(P_T > 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(z > 0.2\).
- For two-hadron predictions, baseline cuts were \(P_{1(2)T} > 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(z_{1(2)} > 0.1\); asymmetric cuts additionally required \(z_1 > 0.3\) and \(P_{T1} > 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the positively charged hadron.
- The \(\sin(\varphi_R - \varphi_S)\) modulation is nonzero and can be significantly enhanced by asymmetric cuts on the hadron momenta.

## Context

This work extends the study of the Sivers effect from single-hadron SIDIS, where SSAs have been measured by HERMES (Airapetian 2009), COMPASS (Adolph 2012), and JLab Hall A (Qian 2011), to two-hadron production. It contradicts the leading-twist cross section of Bianconi (1999), which contained only the \(\sin(\varphi_T - \varphi_S)\) modulation, and the subleading-twist expression of Bacchetta (2003), which also lacked the \(\sin(\varphi_R - \varphi_S)\) term when integrated over total transverse momentum. The results enable a new experimental observable for extracting Sivers PDFs, complementary to single-hadron SIDIS and Drell-Yan measurements, and the authors note that simulations for the SoLID experiment at JLab12 produce SSAs comparable to those for COMPASS.
