# Pairing reentrance in hot rotating nuclei

> The pairing gaps, heat capacities and level densities are calculated within the BCS-based quasiparticle approach including the effect of thermal fluctuations on the pairing field within the pairing model plus noncollective rotation along the z axis for $^{60}$Ni and $^{72}$Ge nuclei. The analysis of the numerical results obtained shows that, in addition to the pairing gap, the heat capacity can also serve as a good observable to detect the appearance of the pairing reentrance in hot rotating nuclei, whereas such signature in the level density is rather weak.

## Key claim

The pairing reentrance effect (where the pairing gap reappears at finite temperature after being quenched at high angular momentum) can be detected experimentally via the heat capacity or its ratio to temperature, but the signature in the level density is weak and may be an artifact of using identical single-particle spectra for neutrons and protons as in prior Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations by Dean (2001).

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature BCS (FTBCS) and FTBCS1 equations, where FTBCS1 includes quasiparticle-number fluctuations in the pairing field, for a spherical system undergoing noncollective rotation about the symmetry z-axis. The pairing Hamiltonian with monopole pairing force is treated via Bogoliubov transformation, and the level-weighted pairing gap, heat capacity, and level density are computed as functions of temperature at fixed total angular momentum M for ⁶⁰Ni and ⁷²Ge using realistic Woods-Saxon single-particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁰Ni: neutron pairing gap at T=0 is Δ_N = 1.7 MeV, obtained with pairing strength G_N = 0.347 MeV
- For ⁷²Ge: neutron pairing gap at T=0 is Δ_N = 1.7 MeV (G_N = 0.291 MeV); proton pairing gap at T=0 is Δ_Z = 1.5 MeV (G_Z = 0.34 MeV)
- For ⁶⁰Ni at M = 14 ħ, the FTBCS1 gap increases from zero at T=0 to approximately 0.3 MeV at T ≈ 0.8 MeV
- For ⁶⁰Ni at M = 4 and 6 ħ, the pairing gap increases with T up to T ≈ 0.3 MeV in both FTBCS and FTBCS1
- The quadrupole vibrational enhancement coefficient K_vib is approximately 1.06 for ⁶⁰Ni at T = 0.3 MeV and 1.94 for ⁷²Ge at T = 0.4 MeV
- The rotational enhancement coefficient K_coll for ⁷²Ge (β₂ = -0.224) is approximately 1.96 at T = 0.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the FTBCS1 approach developed by the authors (2010) that includes quasiparticle-number fluctuations, which was previously shown to reproduce the smoothing of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition and pairing reentrance. The paper directly addresses claims by Dean (2001) from Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations that found pairing reentrance signatures in the heat capacity and level density of ⁷²Ge; the present results show that using realistic single-particle spectra (including the Coulomb barrier for protons) shifts the pairing reentrance from neutrons to protons in ⁷²Ge, and that the claimed level density irregularity likely arises from unphysically large SMMC heat capacities at low temperature. The results enable experimental searches for pairing reentrance using heat capacity measurements rather than the elusive pairing gap extraction from odd-even mass differences.
