# Comparison between the Lipkin-Nogami and Richardson solutions with complex single particle energy in the Lipkin's model

> The pairing interaction is one of the most important contribution of the residual interaction and then, it is of major importance for the study of many-body systems. One can get solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian throught the Bardeen-Cooper-Schieffer (BCS) or the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) approximations but, the pairing Hamiltonian admit exact solution worked out by Richardson. Nuclei far away from the stability line have important correlations with the continuum part of the energy spectrum, due that the Fermi level is very close to the contiuum thershold. The correlations with the continuum can be included in the many-body description through the complex energy states, called Gamow states. In this work we compare the approximates and exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian in real and complex-energy representations. In the application of this formulation to the symmetric Lipkin model, we found that the LN solution is in a good agreement with the exact one; besides, the extension of the BCS solution to the complex energy plane gives solution even for strength below the critical one, which is purely imaginary.

## Key claim

The Lipkin-Nogami (LN) approximation agrees very well with the exact Richardson solution for both real and complex single-particle energies in the symmetric Lipkin model, while the BCS approximation extended to the complex energy plane yields a purely imaginary gap for strengths below the critical value, which is nonphysical.

## Method

The author solved the constant pairing Hamiltonian in the symmetric Lipkin model (two equally degenerate levels at half filling, with N=10 particles and degeneracy 2Ω) using three approaches: the BCS approximation (first-order Taylor expansion in the particle number operator), the Lipkin-Nogami approximation (second-order Taylor expansion), and the exact Richardson solution (system of coupled equations for pair energies). The single-particle energies were taken as complex, with the upper level having an imaginary part γ, to incorporate continuum correlations via Gamow states. The ground-state energies and gap parameters were compared as functions of the pairing strength g.

## Key numbers

- N = 10 particles, with degeneracy 2Ω per level; level separation ε = ε_u - ε_d = 1.0 MeV (ε_d = -0.5 MeV, ε_u = 0.5 - iγ MeV).
- BCS critical strength: g_c = ε/(2Ω - 1); for Ω=5, g_c ≈ 0.111 MeV.
- LN critical strength: g_c = (ε - α)/(2Ω), where α > 0 solves a cubic equation; LN has no trivial solution for any g.
- For γ = 0, the BCS gap Δ becomes purely imaginary for g < g_c; for γ = 0.25 MeV, the BCS energy diverges when the gap is purely imaginary.
- The LN ground-state energy agrees closely with the exact Richardson energy for all g in both real (γ=0) and complex (γ=0.25 MeV) cases.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of BCS, Lipkin-Nogami, and Richardson solutions (Nogami, 1965) to the case of complex single-particle energies, which are relevant for nuclei far from stability where continuum correlations are important (Okolowicz, 2003; Michel, 2009). The results show that the LN approximation is a reliable alternative to the exact Richardson solution for constant pairing, and may be applicable to more general interactions where the Richardson method cannot be used.
