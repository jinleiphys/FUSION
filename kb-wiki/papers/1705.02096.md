# Warm unstable asymmetric nuclear matter: critical properties and the density dependence of the symmetry energy

> The spinodal instabilities in hot asymmetric nuclear matter and some important critical parameters derived thereof are studied using six different families of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. The slopes of the symmetry energy coefficient vary over a wide range within each family. The critical densities and proton fractions are more sensitive to the symmetry energy slope parameter at temperatures much below its critical value ($T_c\sim$14-16 MeV). The spread in the critical proton fraction at a given symmetry energy slope parameter is noticeably larger near $T_c$, indicating that the warm equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities is not sufficiently constrained. The distillation effects are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy at low temperatures which tend to wash out with increasing temperature.

## Key claim

The critical densities and proton fractions in hot asymmetric nuclear matter are more sensitive to the symmetry energy slope parameter at temperatures much below the critical temperature (T_c ~ 14-16 MeV), while the spread in critical proton fraction at a given symmetry energy slope is noticeably larger near T_c, indicating that the warm equation of state at sub-saturation densities is not sufficiently constrained.

## Method

The authors used six families of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (NL3ωρ, NL3σρ, TM1ωρ, TM1σρ, Fρ, and F2ρ) built from three base models (NL3, TM1, BKA22) with additional cross-coupling terms between the ρ-meson and σ or ω mesons to vary the symmetry energy slope L over a wide range. They calculated the thermodynamical spinodal surfaces and critical points in the (ρp, ρn, T) space by requiring the curvature matrix of the free energy density to be positive, identifying the instability region where the smaller eigenvalue λ- equals zero.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures for symmetric matter: 14.55 MeV (NL3), 15.62 MeV (TM1), 14.01 MeV (Fρ/F2ρ base model BKA22), 13.73 MeV (DD2), 13.12 MeV (DD-ME2)
- Critical proton fractions for F2ρ7 (small L) vs Fρ1 (large L): 0.0039 vs 0.0604 at T=0 MeV; 0.0112 vs 0.1307 at T=6 MeV; 0.0788 vs 0.2587 at T=12 MeV
- At L ~ 56 MeV, critical proton fraction Ypc varies between 0.018 and 0.023 at T=0 MeV, and between 0.225 and 0.478 at T=14 MeV
- Critical densities at L ~ 56 MeV range from 0.080-0.087 fm^-3 at T=0 to 0.044-0.054 fm^-3 at T=14 MeV
- Incompressibilities: 281 MeV (TM1), 272 MeV (NL3), 220 MeV (BKA22)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of liquid-gas phase transitions in warm asymmetric nuclear matter by Avancini et al. (2006) and Pais et al. (2016), who used the thermodynamical spinodal method with RMF models but did not systematically connect results to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The study uses six families of RMF models with varying symmetry energy slopes, comparing with experimental critical temperatures from Natowitz et al. (2002), Karnaukhov et al. (2003), and Elliott et al. (2013), as well as theoretical constraints from Lourenco et al. (2016). The results enable better constraints on the equation of state for core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for matter at sub-saturation densities and temperatures of 5-12 MeV.
