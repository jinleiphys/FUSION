# Chiral asymmetry in QED matter in a magnetic field

> We calculate the electron self-energy in a magnetized QED plasma to the leading perturbative order in the coupling constant and to the linear order in an external magnetic field. We find that the chiral asymmetry of the normal ground state of the system is characterized by two new Dirac structures. One of them is the familiar chiral shift previously discussed in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The other structure is new. It formally looks like that of the chiral chemical potential but is an odd function of the longitudinal component of the momentum, directed along the magnetic field. The origin of this new parity-even chiral structure is directly connected with the long-range character of the QED interaction. The form of the Fermi surface in the weak magnetic field is determined.

## Key claim

In magnetized QED plasma, the chiral asymmetry of the normal ground state is characterized by two Dirac structures at leading perturbative order: the familiar chiral shift previously found in NJL models, and a new parity-even structure that formally resembles a chiral chemical potential but is an odd function of the longitudinal momentum component, arising specifically from the long-range nature of the QED interaction.

## Method

The authors calculated the electron self-energy in dense QED with an external magnetic field to leading order in the coupling constant α and linear order in the magnetic field B, using the Schwinger-Dyson approach. They projected the self-energy onto Landau levels and derived explicit expressions for the chiral shift Δ_n and the new chiral chemical potential-like function μ_{5,n} for each level, then evaluated these in the weak magnetic field limit (|eB|/μ² ≪ 1) by expanding the fermion propagator to linear order in B.

## Key numbers

- The ratio |eB|/μ² ≈ 6 × 10⁻⁴ (B/10¹⁵ G)(100 MeV/μ)², justifying the weak field approximation for magnetar applications
- The vacuum part of the chiral shift Δ_vac(p) contains a term proportional to α eB (p₀+μ)/[2π((p₀+μ)²−p²)²] times a logarithmic function of mass and momentum
- The matter part Δ_mat(p) includes terms with α eB/(4π|p|) multiplying logarithms of ratios involving μ, m, and momentum components
- The new function μ₅(p) is proportional to p³ and contains terms with α eB p³/(2π p²) multiplying combinations of μ, m, and momentum-dependent logarithms

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral asymmetry in NJL models (chiral-shift-1, chiral-shift-2, 2010) to full QED, where the long-range interaction produces qualitatively new effects absent in local models. The chiral shift Δ was previously found in NJL models by chiral-shift-1 and chiral-shift-2 using gap equations, while the new parity-even μ₅ structure has no analog in those models. The results enable calculation of interaction-induced modifications to the Fermi surface and dispersion relations for left- and right-handed fermions in magnetized QED matter, relevant for neutron star and magnetar applications.
