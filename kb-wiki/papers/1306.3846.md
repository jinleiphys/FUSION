# Renormalization group flow and symmetry restoration in de Sitter space

> We compute the renormalization group flow of O(N) scalar field theories in de Sitter space using nonperturbative renormalization group techniques in the local potential approximation. We obtain the flow of the effective potential on superhorizon scales for arbitrary space-time dimension D=d+1. We show that, due to strong infrared fluctuations, the latter is qualitatively similar to the corresponding one in Euclidean space with D=0. It follows that spontaneously broken symmetries are radiatively restored in any space-time dimension and for any value of N.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that spontaneously broken O(N) symmetries are radiatively restored in de Sitter space for any space-time dimension and any value of N, using nonperturbative renormalization group techniques. The key hedge is that this result assumes a smooth and small enough anomalous dimension, and that the analysis is performed in the local potential approximation (LPA) with a specific regulator choice.

## Method
The author derives the Wetterich flow equation for the effective potential of O(N) scalar field theories in de Sitter space using the physical momentum (p-)representation of de Sitter correlators, within the local potential approximation (LPA'). The flow equation is obtained for superhorizon scales by employing a Litim regulator and solving for the mode functions in the Bunch-Davies vacuum, with the infrared regime analyzed by expanding the Hankel functions for small momentum. The resulting flow equation is then rescaled to remove explicit scale dependence, revealing a structure analogous to the Euclidean flow in zero dimensions.

## Key numbers
- The flow equation in the infrared limit (kappa << 1) contains the factor A_d = d*Gamma(d/2)/(8*pi^(d/2+1)), which for d=3 gives A_3 = 3/(16*pi^2).
- The flow of the potential minimum (bar_rho_kappa) satisfies dot(bar_rho_kappa) = (2-eta_kappa)*{bar_rho_kappa + A_d*[N-1 + (3+2*bar_rho_kappa*g_kappa)/(1+2*bar_rho_kappa*lambda_kappa)^2]}, where lambda_kappa and g_kappa are the second and third derivatives of the rescaled potential at the minimum.
- The infrared finiteness of the flow equation requires bar_nu_kappa < d/2, i.e., kappa^2 + V_kappa''(phi)/Z_kappa > 0.

## Context
This work extends the nonperturbative renormalization group approach to de Sitter space, building on the formulation by Kaya (2013) for the N=1 symmetric phase. The results support earlier findings by Ratra (1984) and Serreau (2011) that spontaneously broken symmetries are radiatively restored in de Sitter space, contradicting studies using the Hartree approximation by Prokopec (2011) and Boyanovsky (2012) which predicted first-order transitions. The dimensional reduction phenomenon observed here (dS flow analogous to Euclidean D=0) is similar to that seen for fermions in spaces of constant negative curvature by Gorbar (1999) and Gies (2013). The work enables further investigation of possible Kosterlitz-Thouless-type transitions and the role of de Sitter symmetry breaking terms along the flow.
