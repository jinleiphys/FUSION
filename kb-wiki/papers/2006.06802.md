# QCD2019 Workshop Summary

> The topical workshop {\it Strong QCD from Hadron Structure Experiments} took place at Jefferson Lab from Nov. 6-9, 2019. Impressive progress in relating hadron structure observables to the strong QCD mechanisms has been achieved from the {\it ab initio} QCD description of hadron structure in a diverse array of methods in order to expose emergent phenomena via quasi-particle formation. The wealth of experimental data and the advances in hadron structure theory make it possible to gain insight into strong interaction dynamics in the regime of large quark-gluon coupling (the strong QCD regime), which will address the most challenging problems of the Standard Model on the nature of the dominant part of hadron mass, quark-gluon confinement, and the emergence of the ground and excited state hadrons, as well as atomic nuclei, from QCD. This workshop aimed to develop plans and to facilitate the future synergistic efforts between experimentalists, phenomenologists, and theorists working on studies of hadron spectroscopy and structure with the goal to connect the properties of hadrons and atomic nuclei available from data to the strong QCD dynamics underlying their emergence from QCD. These results pave the way for a future breakthrough extension in the studies of QCD with an Electron-Ion Collider in the U.S.

## Key claim

The workshop demonstrated that synergistic efforts between experimentalists, phenomenologists, and theorists studying hadron spectroscopy and structure can connect observable properties of hadrons and atomic nuclei to the strong QCD dynamics underlying their emergence from the QCD Lagrangian, but the abstract's claim of "impressive progress" is qualified by the need for expanded development of reaction models and amplitude analysis approaches to fully capitalize on the wealth of new data.

## Method

The workshop gathered world experts and early career researchers in hadron physics at Jefferson Lab from November 6-9, 2019, consisting of 42 invited talks and a concluding discussion session. Conveners of topical sessions contributed to this summary document covering meson and baryon spectra, nucleon structure in one and three dimensions, resonance electroexcitation amplitudes, and theoretical frameworks including lattice QCD, continuum QCD approaches, light-front holography, and quark models.

## Key numbers

- Nine new N* and Delta* resonances reported in the recent PDG edition with three- and four-star status, discovered in global multi-channel analysis of exclusive photoproduction data
- Eight light-flavored baryon states in the mass range from 1.85 to 2.15 GeV have been discovered or had evidence strengthened significantly over the past six years
- Electroexcitation amplitudes for most excited nucleon states in the mass range up to 1.8 GeV have become available at photon virtualities Q^2 <= 5.0 GeV^2
- First results on pion and kaon elastic electromagnetic form factors and PDFs expected in the still almost unexplored range of high photon virtualities Q^2 > 5.0 GeV^2
- The COMPASS experiment using a 190 GeV pi- beam provided 4.6M events for 3pi final state analysis
- The GlueX experiment features a linearly polarized tagged photon beam covering photon energies from around 8 to 9 GeV

## Context

This workshop summary extends the series of "Strong QCD from Hadron Structure Experiments" meetings following previous gatherings in France and South Korea, building on results from CLAS, COMPASS, GlueX, and other facilities. The work enables future synergistic efforts between experimental programs at Jefferson Lab's 12-GeV era, European facilities (ELSA, MAMI, GSI), Asian facilities (BES, SPring-8, JPARC), and the planned U.S. Electron-Ion Collider. The document provides scientific background for joint projects between experts in diverse areas of hadron physics, with the next workshop planned to be hosted by the Institute for Nonperturbative Physics at Nanjing University in 2021.
