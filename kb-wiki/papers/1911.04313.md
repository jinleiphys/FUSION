# Mean-field calculations of charge radii in ground and isomeric states of Cd isotopes

> Quadrupole moments and charge radii in cadmium isotopes are studied from a microscopic perspective. The results obtained from self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations with Skyrme forces are compared with isomer and isotope shifts measured from high resolution laser spectroscopy experiments. The microscopic calculations reproduce fairly well the main features observed in those isotopes that include the linear increase with the neutron number of the quadrupole moments of the 11/2- isomers, as well as the parabolic behavior of the mean-square charge radii difference between isomers and ground states.

## Key claim

Self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations with standard Skyrme forces (SLy4 and SkM*) reproduce the main experimental features of charge radii and quadrupole moments in Cd isotopes, including the linear increase of quadrupole moments in the 11/2⁻ isomers and the parabolic behavior of mean-square charge radii differences between isomers and ground states, although the theoretical accuracy is not sufficient to predict the high-precision experimental values unambiguously.

## Method

The author performed axially symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme interactions SLy4 and SkM*, including BCS pairing correlations with either fixed gap parameters (determined from experimental odd-even mass differences) or constant pairing strength. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis using twelve major shells, with the basis parameters optimized to minimize energy for each isotope. For odd-A nuclei, the equal filling approximation was used to treat the unpaired nucleon, and the blocked state was chosen according to experimentally known spin and parity assignments.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole moments of 11/2⁻ isomers in Cd isotopes show a linear increase with neutron number; the experimental data were fitted with Q = ((120-A)/9)Q_sp + Q_core, where Q_sp = -667 mb and Q_core = -85 mb
- Root-mean-square charge radii of ground states in ¹⁰⁰-¹³²Cd isotopes are compared with experiment, using r_c(¹¹⁴Cd) = 4.612 fm as the reference radius
- Mean-square charge radii differences between 11/2⁻ isomers and ground states in ¹¹¹-¹²⁹Cd odd-A isotopes are of the order of 10 μb (microbarns) experimental precision
- Typical mean deviations of theoretical charge radii calculations are around ε̄ = 0.001 fm and root-mean-square deviations σ = 0.026 fm for modern Skyrme interactions; Gogny D1S and D2 interactions give ε̄ = 0.03-0.04 fm
- Macroscopic-microscopic models yield σ = 0.04-0.08 fm; Garvey-Kelson relations give σ = 0.01 fm; effective formulas give σ = 0.022 fm

## Context

This work extends microscopic mean-field studies of Cd isotopes using nonrelativistic Skyrme forces, complementing earlier relativistic mean-field calculations by Zhao (2014) that demonstrated the role of pairing correlations in quadrupole moments, and Fayans functional calculations by Reinhard (2017) that reproduced charge radii features. The calculations are compared with high-resolution laser spectroscopy experiments at ISOLDE-CERN by Yordanov (2013, 2016, 2018) and Hammen (2018). The results enable further investigation of shape coexistence phenomena in the Z=50 region, though the shallow deformation-energy curves in mid-shell Cd isotopes make precise theoretical predictions of charge radii challenging.
