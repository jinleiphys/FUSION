# Thermodynamics of strong-interaction matter from Lattice QCD

> We review results from lattice QCD calculations on the thermodynamics of strong-interaction matter with emphasis on input these calculations can provide to the exploration of the phase diagram and properties of hot and dense matter created in heavy ion experiments. This review is organized as follows: 1) Introduction, 2) QCD thermodynamics on the lattice, 3) QCD phase diagram at high temperature, 4) Bulk thermodynamics, 5) Fluctuations of conserved charges, 6) Transport properties, 7) Open heavy flavors and heavy quarkonia, 8) QCD in external magnetic fields, 9) Summary.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of bulk thermodynamic observables have reached sufficient precision to provide continuum-extrapolated results for physical quark masses, but the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model description of the equation of state is reliable only at the 10% level for temperatures below about 140-145 MeV, and the crossover energy density is a rather small value of (0.34 ± 0.16) GeV/fm³.

## Method

The authors review lattice QCD calculations of the thermodynamics of strong-interaction matter using the Euclidean path integral formalism, with (2+1)-flavor QCD (degenerate up and down quarks plus a heavier strange quark). Bulk thermodynamic observables such as pressure, energy density, and entropy density are obtained from the trace anomaly (ε - 3P)/T⁴, which is computed from expectation values of the gauge action and chiral condensates, with zero-temperature subtractions to remove divergent vacuum contributions. The pressure is reconstructed by integrating the trace anomaly over temperature, and results are extrapolated to the continuum limit using staggered fermion discretization schemes (HISQ and stout) with physical quark masses fixed by the pion and kaon masses.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature: T_c = (154 ± 9) MeV
- Critical energy density: ε_c = (0.34 ± 0.16) GeV/fm³
- Energy density of ordinary nuclear matter: ε ≈ 0.15 GeV/fm³
- Energy density inside a nucleon: ε ≈ 0.45 GeV/fm³ (assuming nucleon radius R_N ≈ 0.8 fm)
- Light-to-strange quark mass ratio used in HISQ calculations: m_s/m_l = 27 (Borsanyi 2013) and 20 (Bazavov 2014)
- Physical light-to-strange quark mass ratio: m_s/m_l = 27.5
- Taylor expansion of pressure at non-zero baryon chemical potential is well controlled for μ_B/T ≤ 2, covering beam energies √s ≥ 20 GeV at RHIC

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice QCD thermodynamics calculations by providing continuum-extrapolated results for the equation of state with physical quark masses, comparing with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model of Hagedorn (1965) and perturbative calculations using hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory and dimensional reduction (EQCD). The results enable hydrodynamic modeling of matter created in heavy ion collisions, particularly for the beam energy scan at RHIC, and provide input for determining the QCD phase diagram at non-vanishing baryon chemical potential through Taylor expansion methods. The work also highlights that conserved charge fluctuations (discussed in later sections) reveal the breakdown of hadronic descriptions above about 160 MeV, which is not apparent from bulk thermodynamics alone.
