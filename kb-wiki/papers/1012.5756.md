# Dark Atoms of Dark Matter from New Stable Quarks and Leptons

> The nonbaryonic dark matter of the Universe can consist of new stable charged leptons and quarks, if they are hidden in elusive "dark atoms" of composite dark matter. Such possibility can be compatible with the severe constraints on anomalous isotopes, if there exist stable particles with charge -2 and there are no stable particles with charges +1 and -1. These conditions cannot be realized in supersymmetric models, but can be satisfied in several recently developed alternative scenarios. The excessive -2 charged particles are bound with primordial helium in O-helium "atoms", maintaining specific nuclear-interacting form of the Warmer than Cold Dark Matter. The puzzles of direct dark matter searches appear in this case as a reflection of nontrivial nuclear physics of O-helium.

## Key claim

Dark matter may consist of composite "dark atoms" formed from stable doubly-charged particles (charge -2) bound to primordial helium, provided that no stable particles with charges +1 or -1 exist; this scenario can explain the results of DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA experiments without contradicting other dark matter searches, but only within a narrow range of nuclear physics parameters.

## Method

The paper develops the O-helium (OHe) scenario, where an excess of stable -2 charged particles (X−−) binds with primordial 4He after Big Bang Nucleosynthesis to form neutral (4He++X−−) "atoms." The author calculates the formation, cosmological evolution, and nuclear interaction of OHe, then models its radiative capture by detector nuclei using a rectangular potential approximation to the OHe-nucleus system, solving the Schrödinger equation to find bound-state energies. The rate of radiative capture is estimated using an analogy with neutron-proton capture, accounting for isospin nonconservation and suppression of E1 transitions.

## Key numbers

- O-helium ionization potential: I_o ≈ 1.6 MeV (with correction to ≈ 1.3 MeV for non-point-like charge distribution in He)
- O-helium size: R_o ≈ 2 × 10⁻¹³ cm
- O-helium formation temperature: T_o ≈ 60 keV (at time 100 s ≤ t ≤ 300 s)
- Decoupling temperature: T_od ≈ 200 S₃²/³ eV, where S₃ = m_o/(1 TeV)
- O-helium-nucleus elastic cross section: σ ≈ σ_o ≈ π R_o² ≈ 10⁻²⁵ cm²
- Mass within cosmological horizon at OHe formation: M_o ≈ 10³⁷ g
- Mass within cosmological horizon at decoupling: M_od ≈ 2 × 10⁴⁴ S₃⁻² g ≈ 10¹¹ S₃⁻² M_⊙
- Drift velocity in terrestrial matter: V ≈ 80 S₃ A¹/² cm/s (with A ≈ 30)
- Local OHe dark matter density: n₀ = 3 × 10⁻⁴ S₃⁻¹ cm⁻³
- Relaxation time at depth L ≈ 10⁵ cm: t_dr ≈ 2.5 × 10² S₃⁻¹ s
- Radiative capture rate formula: σv = (f π α)/(m_p²) × (3/√2) × (Z/A)² × T/√(A m_p E), with f = (m_n - m_p)/m_N ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³
- Binding energy of Na-OHe system: E_Na = 3 keV (to reproduce DAMA signal)
- OHe mass assumed: m_o = 1 TeV (results weakly depend on m_o > 1 TeV)

## Context

This work extends the composite dark matter idea first proposed by Glashow (2005) with stable tera-particles, which faced the problem of anomalous isotope overproduction. The author's earlier work (Khlopov 2006) showed that only stable particles with charge -2 can avoid this problem. The O-helium scenario is developed from models including AC-leptons from almost-commutative geometry (Fargion et al. 2006), technileptons from walking technicolor (Khlopov and Kouvaris 2008), and heavy quark clusters from fourth or fifth generation quarks. The paper enables a specific explanation for the DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA annual modulation signal as radiative capture of OHe to a few keV bound state with sodium, while predicting no such signal in detectors with heavy (Xe) or very light (³He) nuclei, potentially explaining the null results from CDMS and XENON100.
