# Two-body Pion Absorption on $^3He$ at Threshold

> It is shown that a satisfactory explanation of the ratio of the rates of the reactions $^3He(π^-,nn)$ and $^3He(π^-,np)$ for stopped pions is obtained once the effect of the short range two-nucleon components of the axial charge operator for the nuclear system is taken into account. By employing realistic models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the construction of these components of the axial charge operator, the predicted ratios agree with the empirical value to within 10-20\%.

# Two-body Pion Absorption on $^3He$ at Threshold

## Key claim

The ratio of stopped-pion absorption rates $^3He(\pi^-,nn)/^3He(\pi^-,np)$ can be explained to within 10-20% of the empirical value only when short-range two-nucleon components of the axial charge operator are included, in addition to the single-nucleon and long-range pion-exchange terms; without these short-range contributions, the predicted ratio exceeds the experimental value by more than an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors constructed the S-wave pion absorption operator from the axial charge density operator of the nuclear system, including one-body, long-range pion-exchange (rescattering), and short-range contributions derived from the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The short-range components were obtained from scalar ($\sigma$, $\delta$) and vector ($\omega$, $\rho$) meson exchange mechanisms, with parameters taken from the Bonn potential model (Machleidt 1989) and from the Paris potential model (Lacombe 1980) via a Fermi invariant decomposition. The transition rates for stopped pions on $^3He$ were calculated using a harmonic oscillator wavefunction for $^3He$ with oscillator parameter $b = 1.36$ fm, and the ratio of the two absorption channels was evaluated in a partial-wave decomposed formalism.

## Key numbers

- The empirical ratio $R = \,^3He(\pi^-,nn)/^3He(\pi^-,np)$ is $6.3 \pm 1.1$ (Gotta et al. 1994).
- With only the single-nucleon term $A^{(1)}$ and the pion rescattering term $A^{(\pi)}$, the predicted ratio is 533.42 (both Bonn and Paris models).
- Including $\sigma$ and $\omega$ exchange reduces the ratio to 12.40 (Bonn) and 16.81 (Paris).
- Including all short-range mechanisms ($\sigma$, $\omega$, $\delta$, $\rho$) yields ratios of 9.786 (Bonn) and 8.0349 (Paris), exceeding the empirical value by 10-20%.
- The $^3He$ wavefunction oscillator parameter is $b = 1.36$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lee and Riska (1993), who showed that short-range axial exchange currents explain the $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ cross section near threshold, to the more complex isospin structure of $^3He$ pion absorption. The authors demonstrate that the long-standing overprediction of the $^3He(\pi^-,nn)/^3He(\pi^-,np)$ ratio by the pion rescattering mechanism alone (Koltun and Reitan 1968) is resolved by including short-range components derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. This enables future investigations of polarization observables in $\pi d \rightarrow pp$ and $NN \rightarrow \pi NN$ reactions, as well as three-body absorption mechanisms in $^3He$ at higher energies.
