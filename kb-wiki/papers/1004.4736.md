# Some indication for a missing chiral partner eta_4 around 2 GeV

> The high-lying mesons in the light quark sector previously obtained from the partial wave analysis of the proton-antiproton annihilation in flight at 1.9 - 2.4 GeV region at CERN reveal a very high degree of degeneracy. This degeneracy can be explained as due to an effective restoration of both SU(2)_L * SU(2)_R and U(1)_A symmetries combined with a principal quantum number n + J. In this case there must be chiral partners for the highest spin states in the 2 and 2.3 GeV bands presently missing in the data. Here we reanalyze the Crystal Barrel data and show an indication for existence of the missing $4^{-+}$ state around 2 GeV. This result calls for further experimental search of the missing states both in the proton-antiproton annihilation and in the production reactions.

# Some indication for a missing chiral partner eta_4 around 2 GeV

## Key claim
The authors reanalyze Crystal Barrel data from proton-antiproton annihilation and find an indication for a missing $4^{-+}$ state (the chiral partner of the known $f_4(2050)$) around 2 GeV, but caution that the evidence is not definitive: the likelihood for the solution including this state is worse by 135 units than the best solution, and the minimum in the mass scan at 1950–1980 MeV is less pronounced than the one at 2330 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed a partial wave analysis of the proton-antiproton annihilation in flight into the $\pi^0\pi^0\eta$ final channel, using data from the Crystal Barrel experiment at LEAR (CERN) in the 1.9–2.4 GeV energy region. They substituted the previously reported $\eta_4(2328)$ $4^{-+}$ resonance with a state at 1980 MeV, allowing decays into $f_2(1275)\eta$, $a_2(1320)\pi$, $a_0(980)\pi$, $\sigma\eta$, and $f_0(1500)\eta$ channels, and optimized the fit with Blatt-Weiskopf form factors for the centrifugal barrier.

## Key numbers
- The mass scan shows a minimum at 1950–1980 MeV for the $4^{-+}$ state, with width $\Gamma = 380$ MeV (constant width parameterization, $r=1.2$ fm) or $\Gamma = 360$ MeV (dynamical width via $a_0(980)\pi$, $r=1.2$ fm).
- The ratio of centrifugal factors $cf_4/cf_3$ at 2 GeV is $\sim 0.06$ for resonance radius 0.8 fm and $\sim 0.13$ for radius 1.2 fm; at 2.3 GeV this ratio increases to 0.25 (0.8 fm) and 0.60 (1.2 fm).
- The total likelihood for the solution with $\eta_4(1950)$ was -89406, which is worse by 135 than the best solution.
- The known $f_4(2050)$ has mass between 1950–2020 MeV and a two-pion branching of $17 \pm 1.5\%$.
- The previously reported $\eta_4(2328)$ state has mass $2328 \pm 38$ MeV and width $240 \pm 90$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the conjecture of effective chiral restoration in highly excited hadrons, previously applied to baryons by Glozman (2003) and to mesons by Glozman (2004, 2005), which predicts that high-lying mesons should appear in degenerate chiral multiplets. The analysis directly tests the prediction that missing chiral partners for the highest spin states in the 2 GeV and 2.3 GeV bands should exist, contradicting the alternative scenario based on a principal quantum number $n+L$ in the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (Anisovich et al., 2000; Klempt and Zaitsev, 2007). The result calls for further experimental searches in proton-antiproton annihilation and production reactions to confirm or disprove the missing $\eta_4$ and other predicted high-spin states.
