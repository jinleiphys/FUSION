# The quark gluon plasma equation of state and the expansion of the early Universe

> Our knowledge of the equation of state of the quark gluon plasma has been continuously growing due to the experimental results from heavy ion collisions, due to recent astrophysical measurements and also due to the advances in lattice QCD calculations. The new findings about this state may have consequences on the time evolution of the early Universe, which can estimated by solving the Friedmann equations. The solutions of these equations give the time evolution of the energy density and also of the temperature in the beginning of the Universe. In this work we compute the time evolution of the QGP in the early Universe, comparing several equations of state, some of them based on the MIT bag model (and on its variants) and some of them based on lattice QCD calculations. Among other things, we investigate the effects of a finite baryon chemical potential in the evolution of the early Universe.

# The quark gluon plasma equation of state and the expansion of the early Universe

## Key claim
The time evolution of energy density and temperature in the early Universe QGP phase is sensitive to the chosen equation of state, but only at late times approaching the quark-hadron phase transition (around 10^-5 s to 10^-4 s); at earlier times, different equations of state (MIT bag model, lattice-based models, and mean-field QCD models) produce nearly identical results. A finite baryon chemical potential does not significantly affect the time evolution in the range considered.

## Method
The authors solved the Friedmann equation numerically for the time evolution of energy density and temperature, using six different equations of state for the QGP: the MIT bag model, two modified bag models fitted to lattice QCD data (Models 1 and 2), a mean-field QCD model derived from an effective Lagrangian (Model 3), a quasi-particle model for pure gauge SU(3) (Model 4), and a lattice QCD parametrization with three quark flavors (Model 5). They evolved the system from an initial time of 10^-9 s (electroweak phase transition) to 10^-4 s (QCD phase transition) with a fixed initial energy density of 10^7 MeV/fm^3, and also investigated the effect of adding an electroweak matter component.

## Key numbers
- Initial condition: energy density ε_i = 10^7 MeV/fm^3 at time t_i = 10^-9 s; evolution run to t_f = 10^-4 s
- Model 1 parameters (from Begun): σ_1 = 4.73, A = 3.94 T_c^3, B_1 = -2.37 T_c^4, where T_c is the critical temperature
- Model 2 parameters (from Begun): σ_2 = 13.01, C = 6.06 T_c^2, B_2 = -2.34 T_c^4
- Model 4 quasi-particle mass parameters: a = 0.47 GeV, b = 0.125 GeV, c = 0.385, with critical temperature T_0 ≈ 280 MeV
- Model 5 critical temperature: 200 MeV; dimensionless parameters include h_0 = 0.1396, f_0 = 1.05, f_1 = 6.39, f_2 = -4.72, g_1 = -0.92, g_2 = 0.57
- Model 5 chemical susceptibility parameters: h_3 = -0.5022, h_4 = 0.5950, f_3 = 0.1359, f_4 = 6.3290, f_5 = -4.8303

- For model 3 with m_g = 10 MeV, the speed of sound is approximately 50% larger than the MIT bag model value

## Context
This work extends previous cosmological studies using the Friedmann equations with QGP equations of state, such as those by Guardo, Florko, and others, which focused primarily on the nature of the QGP-to-hadron gas phase transition. The authors compare their results to the MIT bag model (MIT, 1974) as a baseline, and to lattice QCD parametrizations from Ratti (2014) and Fodor (2014). The study enables more precise predictions for gravitational wave spectra from the early Universe, as discussed by Lugo, Domi, and Tigran, where different equations of state yield different spectra at frequencies above 10^-5 Hz, potentially detectable by future observatories like eLISA/NGO. The inclusion of a finite baryon chemical potential addresses the possibility of fluctuations in charged particle asymmetry in the QGP phase, as pointed out by Sany.
