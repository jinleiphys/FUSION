# A monopole-optimized effective interaction for tin isotopes

> We present a systematic configuration-interaction shell model calculation on the structure of light tin isotopes with a new global optimized effective interaction. The starting point of the calculation is the realistic CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The unknown single-particle energies of the $1d_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$ and $0h_{11/2}$ orbitals and the T=1 monopole interactions are determined by fitting to the binding energies of 157 low-lying yrast states in $^{102-132}$Sn. We apply the Hamiltonian to analyze the origin of the spin inversion between $^{101}$Sn and $^{103}$Sn that was observed recently and to explore the possible contribution from interaction terms beyond the normal pairing.

# A monopole-optimized effective interaction for tin isotopes

## Key claim

The authors present a systematic configuration-interaction shell model calculation for light tin isotopes using a new effective interaction optimized by fitting to 157 low-lying yrast states in 102-132Sn, but the monopole Hamiltonians derived from Monte Carlo and singular value decomposition approaches are similar only under the condition that step lengths are restricted to ≤1 MeV; otherwise the SVD approach diverges in most cases.

## Method

The calculation starts from the realistic CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the perturbative G-matrix approach, assuming doubly-magic 100Sn as the inert core with a model space comprising the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals. The unknown single-particle energies of the 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals and the T=1 monopole interactions are determined by minimizing χ² against experimental binding energies of 157 yrast states using both a Monte Carlo global optimization method and a singular value decomposition approach. The fitting is carried out in three steps, progressively including nuclei from 102-112Sn and 120-132Sn, then 113-114Sn and 118-119Sn, and finally 115-117Sn.

## Key numbers

- The initial root-mean-square deviation from the realistic CD-Bonn potential is approximately 750 keV, reduced to about 110 keV after 10 iterations.
- The final mean-square deviation χ² is approximately 2.35 MeV², corresponding to an average deviation of about 123 keV for the 157 fitted states.
- The single-particle energies from the MC approach are: ε(1d3/2) = 5.013 ± 3.10 MeV, ε(2s1/2) = 0.369 ± 2.63 MeV, and ε(0h11/2) = 3.249 ± 0.83 MeV.
- The single-particle energy of 1d5/2 is fixed at ε(1d5/2) = 0.172 MeV.
- The 1d5/2-1d3/2 monopole interaction is -0.607 MeV (MC) or -0.726 MeV (SVD), compared to -0.231 MeV from the original CD-Bonn potential.
- The 1d3/2-2s1/2 monopole interaction is -0.768 MeV (MC) or -0.783 MeV (SVD), compared to -0.134 MeV from CD-Bonn.
- The 1d5/2-2s1/2 monopole interaction is +0.203 MeV (MC) or +0.228 MeV (SVD), compared to -0.201 MeV from CD-Bonn.
- The largest deviation from experiment is at N=15 (115Sn), where the ground state energy is under-estimated by approximately 420 keV.
- The 126 excited states are reproduced within an average deviation of about 150 keV.
- The B(E2) values for 10+1 to 8+1 transitions in 122,124Sn are calculated with neutron effective charges eeff_n = 1.0e and 0.88e, with better agreement using 0.88e.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations on tin isotopes (Grawe 2001, Banu 2005, Dar 2010) by fine-tuning the monopole part of the realistic CD-Bonn interaction, motivated by the fact that realistic interactions give satisfactory multipole but not monopole channels, possibly due to missing three-body forces (Zuker 2003). The optimized interaction is used to analyze the spin inversion between 101Sn and 103Sn observed by Dar (2010), where the ground state of 101Sn has 0g7/2 configuration while 103Sn has 5/2+ ground state, and to explore contributions from interaction terms beyond normal pairing. The new interaction has already been applied in studies of 109Te (Pro 2012) and E2 transition properties in Sn isotopes (Back 2012).
