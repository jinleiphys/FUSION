# Theoretical predictions of experimental observables sensitive to the symmetry energy: Results of the SMF transport model

> In the framework of mean-field based transport approaches, we discuss recent results concerning heavy ion reactions between charge asymmetric systems, from low up to intermediate energies. We focus on isospin sensitive observables, aiming at extracting information on the density dependence of the isovector part of the nuclear effective interaction and of the nuclear symmetry energy. For reactions close to the Coulomb barrier, we explore the structure of collective dipole oscillations, rather sensitive to the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy. In the Fermi energy regime, we investigate the interplay between dissipation mechanisms, fragmentation and isospin effects. At intermediate energies, where regions with higher density and momentum are reached, we discuss collective flows and their sensitivity to the momentum dependence of the isovector interaction channel, which determines the splitting of neutron and proton effective masses. Finally, we also discuss the isospin effect on the possible phase transition from nucleonic matter to quark matter. Results are critically reviewed, also trying to establish a link, when possible, with the outcome of other transport models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the density dependence of the symmetry energy (Asy-EOS) can be constrained by comparing transport model simulations to experimental observables from heavy-ion reactions, but the results are model-dependent and the extracted constraints are qualitative rather than definitive, as the authors state they "critically review" results and "try to establish a link" with other transport models.

## Method

The authors employ the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model, an approximate treatment of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation, to simulate heavy-ion reactions from Coulomb barrier energies up to the AGeV range. They test three different parameterizations of the symmetry energy density dependence (asysoft, asystiff, and asysuperstiff) by implementing them into the mean-field potential of the transport code and comparing the simulated observables. The simulations use test-particle methods to solve the transport equations, with the isoscalar part of the interaction fixed to the Skyrme SKM* parametrization (compressibility modulus K=200 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation: 29.9 MeV for asysoft, 28.3 MeV for asystiff and asysuperstiff
- Slope parameter L: 14.4 MeV for asysoft, 72.6 MeV for asystiff, 96.6 MeV for asysuperstiff
- Neutron skin thickness of 132Sn: 0.25 fm for asysoft, 0.30 fm for asystiff, 0.31 fm for asysuperstiff
- PDR energy centroid at approximately 8.5 MeV, insensitive to the Asy-EOS choice
- Integrated cross section over the PDR region: 2.7% for asysoft, 4.4% for asystiff, 4.5% for asysuperstiff of the total cross section
- GDR centroid for medium-heavy nuclei scales as 80 A^{-1/3} MeV

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of the symmetry energy by Colonna, Baran, and Di Toro (2006) and reviews results in the context of the broader effort to constrain the Asy-EOS, which is largely unknown away from normal density. The paper connects to experimental work on the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in Sn isotopes (Ref2, Ref3, Ref4, Colo) and to the prompt dipole gamma-ray emission observed in dissipative collisions (Chomaz 1993, Pierrou 2005, Medea collaboration). The results enable future comparisons with radioactive beam experiments and other transport model predictions, particularly for constraining the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy through collective flow observables and the momentum dependence of the isovector interaction.
