# Corrections to the Nuclear Axial Vector Coupling in a Nuclear Medium

> We examine further corrections to the time component of the axial vector coupling constant in a nuclear medium. The dominant correction is that of exchange currents. The corrections we examine make the remaining discrepancy worse.

## Key claim

The authors examine whether Pauli-exclusion-principle corrections to the one-nucleon pion loop, together with sigma mean-field effects, can account for the missing ~10% enhancement of the time component of the axial vector coupling in nuclei; they find that these corrections are of the required magnitude but have the wrong sign, making the existing discrepancy between theory and experiment worse.

## Method

The authors use a chiral Lagrangian with nucleons, pions, sigma mesons, and chiral vector mesons, treating the sigma field in mean-field theory. They compute the in-medium correction to the time component of the axial vector coupling from three classes of diagrams: the nucleon self-energy with a pion loop (Fig. 2a), the exchange-current diagram with a pion and sigma (Fig. 2b), and the sigma-mean-field vertex correction (Fig. 2c). The calculations incorporate a dipole form factor with cutoff Lambda = 0.9 or 1.1 GeV and account for the reduced nucleon effective mass M* ~ 0.8 M at nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

* The total correction delta to the axial coupling g_A* = g_A (1 + delta) at nuclear matter density is delta = -0.13 for cutoff Lambda = 1.1 GeV and delta = -0.11 for cutoff Lambda = 0.9 GeV.
* The experimental enhancement delta_exp is approximately 0.64 for A ~ 16, 0.75 for A ~ 96, and 1 for A ~ 200; theoretical exchange-current calculations give delta_th approximately 0.5-0.6, 0.6, and 0.8 for the same mass numbers.
* The free-space axial coupling constant is g_A = 1.26; the sigma vacuum expectation value is sigma_0 = 102 MeV.
* The contribution from Fig. 2a (nucleon self-energy) at cutoff 1.1 GeV gives +0.21 from the integral up to k_F and -0.26 from the mass-difference integral; at cutoff 0.9 GeV the values are +0.19 and -0.21.
* The contribution from Fig. 2b (exchange current) at cutoff 1.1 GeV gives -0.18 from the integral up to k_F and -0.03 from the mass-difference integral; at cutoff 0.9 GeV the values are -0.17 and -0.02.
* The contribution from Fig. 2c (sigma vertex) at cutoff 1.1 GeV gives +0.01 from the integral up to k_F and +0.08 from the mass-difference integral; at cutoff 0.9 GeV the values are +0.004 and +0.054.

## Context

This work extends the chiral-Lagrangian approach of Carter and Prakash (2002) and Carter, Ellis, and Rudaz (1998) by examining additional Pauli-blocking corrections to the one-nucleon pion loop that were not included in earlier exchange-current calculations by Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1970s) and by Warburton (1990s) and Towner (1990s). The results show that these corrections, rather than resolving the missing ~10% enhancement of the time component, worsen the disagreement with experimental data from first-forbidden beta decays and polarized-mirror-nuclei measurements; this suggests that other mechanisms, such as two-pion exchange or heavier meson exchanges, may need to be considered.
