# Quantized meson fields in and out of equilibrium. II: Chiral condensate and collective meson excitations

> We develop a quantum kinetic theory of the chiral condensate and meson quasi-particle excitations using the O(N) linear sigma model which describe the chiral phase transition both in and out of equilibrium in a unified way. A mean field approximation is formulated in the presence of mesonic quasi-particle excitations which are described by generalized Wigner functions. It is shown that in equilibrium our kinetic equations reduce to the gap equations which determine the equilibrium condensate amplitude and the effective masses of the quasi-particle excitations, while linearization of transport equations, near such equilibrium, determine the dispersion relations of the collective mesonic excitations at finite temperatures. Although all mass parameters for the meson excitations become at finite temperature, apparently violating the Goldstone theorem, the missing Nambu-Goldstone modes are retrieved in the collective excitations of the system as three degenerate phonon-like modes in the symmetry-broken phase. We show that the temperature dependence of the pole masses of the collective pion excitations has non-analytic kink behavior at the threshold of the quasi-particle excitations in the presence of explicit symmetry breaking interaction.

## Key claim

The paper develops a quantum kinetic theory for the O(N) linear sigma model that describes the chiral condensate and meson quasi-particle excitations both in and out of equilibrium, but the mean field approximation used leads to a first-order phase transition (a known generic problem) and gives massive pion quasi-particles that apparently violate the Goldstone theorem; the missing Nambu-Goldstone modes are retrieved instead as three degenerate phonon-like collective modes in the symmetry-broken phase.

## Method

The authors formulate a mean field approximation for the O(N) linear sigma model in the presence of mesonic quasi-particle excitations described by generalized Wigner functions (2x2 matrices of one-body density matrices including anomalous off-diagonal components). They derive coupled equations of motion: nonlinear Klein-Gordon equations for the classical condensate fields and kinetic equations for the Wigner functions. In the long-wavelength limit, the diagonal components of the Wigner functions obey Landau-Vlasov-type kinetic equations. Near equilibrium, the kinetic equations are linearized to obtain self-consistency conditions that determine the dispersion relations of collective mesonic excitations.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian contains a quartic self-coupling constant lambda/4! and a mass parameter m^2; the explicit symmetry breaking term h_i = epsilon * delta_{i,1} is chosen to reproduce the observed pion mass in vacuum.
* The quasi-particle energy for species i is defined as epsilon_i(p,r,t) = omega_{i,p} + U_i(r,t), where omega_{i,p} = sqrt(p^2 + mu_i^2) and mu_i^2 is given by a self-consistent gap equation involving the condensate and fluctuations.
* In equilibrium, the gap equations determine the condensate amplitude and effective masses of quasi-particle excitations; the solution exhibits a first-order phase transition.
* The temperature dependence of the pole masses of collective pion excitations shows non-analytic kink behavior at the threshold of quasi-particle excitations in the presence of explicit symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-component scalar field kinetic theory (Matsuo and Matsui, 2008, referred to as I) to the O(N) symmetric linear sigma model, which for N=4 serves as an effective field theory of QCD with light quark flavors. It builds upon equilibrium mean field (Hartree) treatments of the chiral phase transition at finite temperature by authors such as Baym and Grinstein (1977), Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), and others, and addresses the known problem that the Hartree approximation gives massive pions violating the Goldstone theorem, as previously discussed by Okopinska (1996), Dmitrasinovic et al. (1996), and others. The formalism enables the study of non-equilibrium dynamics of the chiral condensate and collective meson excitations, relevant for describing the freezeout stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
