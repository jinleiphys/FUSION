# Time-delay in a multi-channel formalism

> We reexamine the time-delay formalism of Wigner, Eisenbud and Smith, which was developed to analyze both elastic and inelastic resonances. An error in the paper of Smith has propagated through the literature. We correct this error and show how the results of Eisenbud and Smith are related. We also comment on some recent time-delay studies, based on Smith's erroneous interpretation of the Eisenbud result.

## Key claim

The paper corrects an error in Smith's time-delay formalism that has propagated through the literature: Smith's claimed connection between his time-delay matrix and Eisenbud's result is based on a formula that is not equivalent to Eisenbud's original multi-channel time-delay expression, and the correct relationship involves eigenphases rather than channel phase shifts.

## Method

The authors re-derive both Eisenbud's and Smith's time-delay formalisms using eigenphase decomposition of the S-matrix, showing that the trace of Smith's Q-matrix equals 2ℏ times the sum of eigenphase derivatives. They then construct a simple two-channel Breit-Wigner resonance model to explicitly compare the correct Eisenbud result with Smith's misattributed formula, and also relate the results to speed-plot methods used in resonance identification.

## Key numbers

- For a single resonant eigenphase, the trace of Smith's Q-matrix gives \( \text{tr}~Q = 2\hbar \sum_\alpha d\phi_\alpha/dE \), which agrees with Eisenbud's result apart from a factor of two.
- In the two-channel example, Eisenbud's relation gives \( \Delta t_{ij} = \hbar d\phi_2/dE \) for both channels, independent of branching ratios.
- Smith's misattributed formula applied to the two-channel case gives \( \Delta t_{11} = 2\hbar d\delta_1/dE \), while the correct Smith Q-matrix element is \( Q_{11} = 2\hbar d\delta_1/dE + \hbar(1 - \eta^2) d(\delta_2 - \delta_1)/dE \), where η is the inelasticity.
- For the Breit-Wigner resonance at E = E_R, the speed-plot relation gives \( Q_{ii} = 2\hbar \,\text{Speed}(E_R) \) with \( \text{Speed}(E) = |dT_{ii}(E)/dE| \).

## Context

This work corrects an error in Smith (1960) that misrepresented Eisenbud's (1948) multi-channel time-delay result, an error that propagated through the Dalitz and Moorhouse review article and into recent resonance identification studies by Kelkar and collaborators. The corrected formalism shows that Eisenbud's time-delay is always positive for a single resonance and proportional to the eigenphase derivative, resolving apparent inconsistencies between speed-plot and time-delay methods. The results enable proper application of time-delay analysis to multi-channel scattering problems and clarify the relationship between different resonance identification techniques in nuclear and atomic physics.
