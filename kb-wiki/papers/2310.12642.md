# Semi-inclusive two-nucleon emission in (anti) neutrino CC scattering within the relativistic mean field framework

> This paper delves into the distribution of semi-inclusive events involving the emission of two nucleons in (anti) neutrino charged-current scattering. The analysis is conducted within the framework of relativistic mean field theory applied to nuclear matter. To quantify the likelihood of such semi-inclusive events occurring, we employ a relativistic model of meson-exchange currents that aligns with the 2p2h inclusive cross-section. The outcomes are presented in terms of one-fold and two-fold integrated semi-inclusive cross sections. To highlight disparities among the various emission channels, including proton-proton, neutron-proton, and neutron-neutron, we compare them against a purely phase-space isotropic distribution within the center of mass of the two nucleons. These comparisons reveal significant differences in the event distributions, shedding light on the distinctive characteristics of each channel.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive distribution of two-nucleon events in (anti)neutrino charged-current scattering, computed within a relativistic mean field (RMF) framework with meson-exchange currents (MEC), deviates significantly from the isotropic center-of-mass distribution assumed in most Monte Carlo event generators, with the specific differences depending on the emission channel (proton-proton, neutron-proton, or neutron-neutron).

## Method

The authors compute the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor for two-nucleon emission using a relativistic model of meson-exchange currents (including pion-exchange diagrams and the full Delta-isobar propagator with both real and imaginary parts) within the relativistic mean field (RMF) description of nuclear matter, which introduces an effective nucleon mass and vector energy. They discretize the eight-dimensional phase space of hole momenta and relative angles of the two outgoing nucleons in the center-of-mass frame, then integrate over subsets of final-state variables to obtain one-fold and two-fold semi-inclusive cross sections for the proton-proton, neutron-proton, and neutron-neutron channels.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inclusive 2p2h MEC calculations within the RMF framework (Martinez-Consentino 2021, 2021b, 2023) to the semi-inclusive domain, providing a microscopic alternative to the isotropic two-nucleon distribution assumption used in event generators such as NuWro (Sobczyk 2012), NEUT (Sobczyk 2020), and GENIE (Dolton 2020). The results enable improved modeling of final-state nucleon kinematics in neutrino event generators, which is needed for better neutrino energy reconstruction in experiments by T2K, MINERvA, and MicroBooNE (Abe 2018, Lu 2018, Cai 2020, Abratenko 2020, 2020b).
