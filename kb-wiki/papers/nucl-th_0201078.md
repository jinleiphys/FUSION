# Reaction Operator Approach to Multiple Elastic Scatterings

> We apply the GLV Reaction Operator formalism to compute the effects of multiple elastic scatterings of jets propagating through dense matter. We derive the elastic Reaction Operator and demonstrate that the recursion relations have a closed form solution that reduces to the familiar Glauber form. We also investigate the accuracy of the Gaussian dipole approximation for jet transverse momentum broadening.

# Reaction Operator Approach to Multiple Elastic Scatterings

## Key claim

The GLV Reaction Operator formalism, previously developed for radiative energy loss, can be applied to multiple elastic scatterings of jets in dense matter, yielding a closed-form solution that reduces to the familiar Glauber form; however, for moderate opacities χ < 10 the Gaussian dipole approximation fails to reproduce the power-law tails of the jet transverse momentum distribution and the logarithmic enhancement of the mean squared transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors derive the elastic Reaction Operator R = D†D + V† + V, where D is the single Born (direct) operator and V is the double Born (virtual) operator, and show that the recursion relations for the inclusive jet distribution at each order in opacity can be summed to all orders via impact parameter space resummation. The calculation uses a Yukawa color-screened scattering potential with range 1/μ much smaller than the mean free path λ, and assumes the high-energy eikonal approximation where path ordering of successive scatterings is enforced and correlations between scattering centers are negligible. The summation recovers the classical Glauber multiple collision series, expressed as a Poisson random walk in transverse momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The opacity parameter χ = σ_el T(b_0) = L/λ, where L is the path length and λ the mean free path, is the expansion parameter; the paper considers χ = 3 and χ = 10 as representative values for nuclear physics applications.
- The Yukawa screening mass is taken as μ² = 0.25 GeV² for the numerical illustrations.
- In the Gaussian dipole approximation, the mean squared transverse momentum is ⟨p²⟩ = χ μ² ξ, where ξ is a constant absorbing logarithmic variation.
- The exact result gives ⟨p²⟩ ≈ χ μ² log(1 + Q²/χ μ²), showing a logarithmic enhancement with the kinematic cutoff Q_max.
- The condition for the validity of the path-ordering approximation is λμ ≫ 1, ensuring that the range of the potential is much smaller than the mean free path.

## Context

This work extends the GLV Reaction Operator formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000) from radiative energy loss to elastic multiple scattering, recovering the classical Glauber multiple collision series (Glauber, 1970). The paper demonstrates that the Gaussian dipole approximation, often used in impact parameter space resummation (Blaizot, 1986; Gupta, 1994), fails for moderate opacities χ < 10 relevant to nuclear physics applications at RHIC. The results enable future combination of elastic and LPM-suppressed inelastic processes in a unified formalism, applicable to jet acoplanarity observables and jet tomography in heavy ion collisions.
