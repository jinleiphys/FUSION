# Deconstructing triplet nucleon-nucleon scattering

> Nucleon-nucleon scattering in spin-triplet channels is analysed within an effective field theory where one-pion exchange is treated nonperturbatively. Justifying this requires the identification of an additional low-energy scale in the strength of that potential. Short-range interactions are organised according to the resulting power counting, in which the leading term is promoted to significantly lower order than in the usual perturbative counting. In each channel there is a critical momentum above which the waves probe the singular core of the tensor potential and the new counting is necessary. When the effects of one- and two-pion exchange have been removed using a distorted-wave Born approximation, the residual scattering in waves with L<=2 is well described by the first three terms in the new counting. In contrast, the scattering in waves with L>=3 is consistent with the perturbative counting, at least for energies up to 300 MeV. This pattern is in agreement with estimates of the critical momenta in these channels.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon scattering, the momentum scale \(\lambda_\pi \simeq 290\) MeV associated with one-pion exchange should be treated as a low-energy scale (of order \(Q\)) rather than a high-energy scale, which justifies treating one-pion exchange nonperturbatively and leads to a new power counting where the leading short-range potential is promoted to order \(Q^{-1/2}\) for all partial waves, but only in channels where the critical momentum for probing the singular tensor core is low enough (waves with \(L \leq 2\)).

## Method

The author solves the radial Schrödinger equation for uncoupled spin-triplet channels (\(^3P_0\), \(^3P_1\), \(^3D_2\), \(^3F_3\), \(^3G_4\)) with the one-pion-exchange potential, then uses distorted-wave Born approximation to remove the effects of one-pion exchange and subsequently two-pion exchange (at orders \(Q^2\) and \(Q^3\)) plus relativistic corrections. The residual short-range interactions are extracted from empirical Nijmegen partial-wave analyses (PWA93, NijmegenI, NijmegenII, Reid93) using a delta-shell potential at radius \(R_0 = 0.1\) fm, and their energy dependence is compared to the predictions of the new power counting.

## Key numbers

- The momentum scale \(\lambda_\pi = m_\pi^2 / (f_{\pi NN}^2 M_N) \simeq 290\) MeV is identified as the low-energy scale justifying nonperturbative treatment of one-pion exchange.
- Critical momentum for breakdown of perturbative one-pion exchange is of order \(m_\pi\) or \(\lambda_\pi\) in \(S\), \(P\) and \(D\) waves, but \(\sim 2\) GeV in waves with \(L \geq 3\).
- The leading short-range potential in the new counting is of order \(Q^{-1/2}\) for any orbital angular momentum \(L\), with subleading terms at orders \(Q^{3/2}\), \(Q^{7/2}\), etc.
- In the \(^3P_0\) channel, the short-distance interaction \(\widetilde V^{(2)}(p)\) extracted with \(\alpha=0\) is larger than in the \(^3P_1\) channel by a factor of 30 or more.
- The \(^3P_0\) residual interaction after subtracting order-\(Q^{2,3}\) long-range forces is well described by a quadratic fit in energy up to 300 MeV lab kinetic energy.
- The \(^3G_4\) residual interactions \(\widetilde V^{(2)}(p)\) and \(\widetilde V^{(4)}(p)\) are both consistent with zero given the spread of partial-wave analyses.

## Context

This work extends the renormalisation-group analysis of Birse (2007) from formal power-counting results to practical phenomenological tests, applying the distorted-wave methods previously used for singlet channels by Birse and McGovern (2004). It confirms the numerical observations of Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (2005) that leading contact interactions are substantially promoted in channels with attractive tensor one-pion exchange, and provides quantitative support for the new power counting proposed there. The results enable systematic effective-field-theory descriptions of few-nucleon systems where one-pion exchange must be treated nonperturbatively, and clarify the boundary between channels requiring the new counting (\(L \leq 2\)) and those where standard Weinberg counting remains valid (\(L \geq 3\)).
