# Uncertainties in the analysis of neutron resonance data

> Recent analyses of the distribution of reduced neutron widths in the Nuclear Data Ensemble (NDE) and in the Pt isotopes find strong disagreement with predictions of random-matrix theory. These analyses combine the maximum-likelihood method with a cutoff on the reduced neutron widths. We show that the method introduces a systematic error (the "cutoff error"). That error (seemingly taken into account for the Pt data) increases with increasing cutoff and decreasing number of data points. We reanalyse the NDE taking the cutoff error into account. While differing in detail, our results confirm the earlier conclusion that the NDE disagrees significantly from RMT predictions.

## Key claim

The cutoff procedure used in maximum-likelihood analyses of neutron resonance data introduces a systematic "cutoff error" that, when properly accounted for, increases the uncertainty on the extracted distribution parameter ν but does not remove the strong discrepancy between the Nuclear Data Ensemble (NDE) and random-matrix theory (RMT) predictions.

## Method

The authors applied a maximum-likelihood analysis with an energy-independent cutoff to simulated sets of reduced neutron widths drawn from a Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD, ν=1). They derived two coupled equations from the likelihood function and studied how their intersection point (giving the most likely ν and mean width ⟨x⟩) shifts with cutoff value x_min and finite sample size N. For the NDE, they estimated the cutoff error σ_c by simulating 2500 sets of widths for each nuclide using the same N and cutoff parameters as Koehler (2011), then combined σ_c in quadrature with the original maximum-likelihood uncertainty σ_ML.

## Key numbers

- For N=100 simulated widths with cutoff x_min=0.2, the distribution of extracted ν values had mean 1.10 and standard deviation 0.59; for x_min=0.1, mean 1.06 and standard deviation 0.42.
- The weighted average of ν for the NDE including cutoff error is 1.25 ± 0.13, compared to 1.217 ± 0.092 from Koehler (2011).
- Excluding ²³²Th, the weighted average becomes 1.20 ± 0.13.
- For individual nuclides, σ_c values range from 0.22 (⁶⁴Zn, N=103, T_max=0.05) to 1.3 (¹¹⁴Cd, N=17, T_max=0.45).
- The Pt isotope analysis rejected RMT with 99.997% probability; the NDE analysis rejected RMT with 99.17% probability (both from Koehler 2010, 2011).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Koehler (2010, 2011) who used a cutoff method to separate s-wave and p-wave resonances and found strong disagreement with RMT predictions for both the NDE and Pt isotopes. The authors show that the cutoff procedure introduces a systematic error comparable in magnitude to the statistical uncertainty, but their reanalysis confirms the earlier conclusion that the NDE disagrees significantly from RMT, primarily driven by the ²³²Th data. The results enable more reliable uncertainty quantification in future analyses of neutron resonance width distributions.
