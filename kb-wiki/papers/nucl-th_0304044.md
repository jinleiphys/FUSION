# Generalized q-Deformed Symplectic sp(4) Algebra for Multi-shell Applications

> A multi-shell generalization of a fermion representation of the q-deformed compact symplectic sp_q(4) algebra is introduced. An analytic form for the action of two or more generators of the Sp_q(4) symmetry on the basis states is determined and the result used to derive formulae for the overlap between number preserving states as well as for matrix elements of a model Hamiltonian. A second-order operator in the generators of Sp_q(4) is identified that is diagonal in the basis set and that reduces to the Casimir invariant of the sp(4) algebra in the non-deformed limit of the theory. The results can be used in nuclear structure applications to calculate beta-decay transition probabilities and to provide for a description of pairing and higher-order interactions in systems with nucleons occupying more than a single-j orbital.

# Generalized q-Deformed Symplectic sp(4) Algebra for Multi-shell Applications

## Key claim

The paper introduces a multi-shell generalization of the q-deformed sp_q(4) algebra that depends on the dimensionality of the underlying fermion space (2Ω), making the deformation non-trivial because the single-j q-deformed algebras do not close within themselves but only within the generalized multi-shell algebra; the direct sum structure sp_q(4) = ⊕_j sp_q^{(j)}(4) holds only in the boson approximation limit of large Ω.

## Method

The authors construct a fermion realization of the q-deformed sp_q(4) algebra using q-deformed creation and annihilation operators α^†_{j,m,σ} and α_{j,m,σ} defined through anticommutation relations that depend on the total number of particles of each type averaged over the multi-shell space (N_σ/(2Ω)). They derive analytical forms for the action of products of two or more generators (T_±, F_0, F_±1, G_0, G_±1) on the completely paired basis states |n_1, n_0, n_{-1})_q, and use these to compute overlaps between number-preserving states, matrix elements of a model Hamiltonian, and a second-order diagonal operator that reduces to the sp(4) Casimir invariant in the non-deformed limit.

## Key numbers

- The total fermion space dimension is 2Ω = Σ_j 2Ω_j = Σ_j (2j+1), where the sum runs over all active orbits.
- The q-deformed anticommutation relation is {α_{j,σ,m}, α^†_{j,σ,m'}}_{±1} = q^{± N_σ/(2Ω)} δ_{m,m'}.
- The single-j q-deformed number operator satisfies Σ_m α^†_{j,σ,m} α_{j,σ,m} = 2Ω_j [N_σ/(2Ω)].
- The eigenvalue of the second-order diagonal operator O_2(sp_q(4)) in the basis states reduces to Ω+3 in the non-deformed (q→1) limit.
- The model Hamiltonian H_q includes strength parameters G_q, F_q, E_q, C_q, and D_q for pairing and symmetry interactions.
- In the large Ω limit (Ω ≫ {N/2, N_±1}), the anticommutation relation reduces to {α_{j,σ,m}, α^†_{j,σ,m'}}_{q^{±1}} = δ_{m,m'} and all q-brackets [X] or [X]_{1/(2Ω)} go to X when X is not a function of Ω.

## Context

This work extends the single-j fermion representation of the q-deformed sp_q(4) algebra introduced by Sviratcheva et al. (2003) to multi-shell applications, building on the classical sp(4) ≅ so(5) algebra used for pairing correlations with two types of particles (Hecht; Ginocchio; Goto, Li, and Spector). The generalization enables studies of nuclei with mass numbers 56 < A < 100, where nucleons occupy multiple j-orbitals such as 1f_{5/2}2p_{1/2}2p_{3/2}1g_{9/2}. The results provide the algebraic framework for calculating β-decay transition probabilities and for exactly solvable descriptions of isovector pairing and higher-order interactions in deformed nuclear systems, with the q-deformation introducing non-linear effects that account for the shell structure dependence.
