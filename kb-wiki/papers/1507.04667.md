# Heavy baryons in the large Nc limit

> It is shown that in the large Nc limit heavy baryon masses can be estimated quantitatively in a 1/Nc expansion using the Hartree approximation. The results are compared with available lattice calculations for different values of the ratio between the square root of the string tension and the heavy quark mass tension independent of Nc. Using a potential adjusted to agree with the one obtained in lattice QCD, a variational analysis of the ground state spin averaged baryon mass is performed using Gaussian Hartree wave functions. Relativistic corrections through the quark kinetic energy are included. The results provide good estimates for the first sub-leading in 1/Nc corrections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that heavy baryon masses in the large \(N_c\) limit can be estimated quantitatively using a Hartree mean-field approximation with a \(1/N_c\) expansion, but only under the assumption that the string tension \(\sigma\) is independent of \(N_c\) and that the quark-antiquark potential follows Casimir scaling.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with two-body interactions derived from the quark-antiquark potential, which is taken to be the bosonic string model \(V_{Q\bar Q}(r) = -\pi/(12r) + \sigma r\) for all \(N_c\). A variational Gaussian Hartree wave function is used to compute the ground-state spin-averaged baryon mass, including center-of-mass corrections via Peierls-Yoccoz projection and relativistic corrections by replacing the kinetic energy with \(\sqrt{p^2 + m_Q^2}\). The results are compared with quenched lattice QCD data for \(N_c = 3, 5, 7\) from DeGrand (2012, 2013) and Cordon (2014).

## Key numbers

- For \(N_c = 3\), the Sommer parameter \(r_1\) gives \(r_1 \sqrt{\sigma} = 0.859\), consistent with lattice values of 0.856(5) for \(N_c=3\), 0.850(4) for \(N_c=5\), and 0.845(2) for \(N_c=7\).
- The lattice spacing in units of string tension is \(\sqrt{\sigma} \, a = 0.22\) for all \(N_c\), derived from \(r_1/a\) values in DeGrand (2012).
- The relativistic calculation reproduces the lattice results for the dimensionless ratio \((\mathring M_B - N_c m_Q)/(N_c \sqrt{\sigma})\) within 20%.
- In the heavy quark limit, the variational Gaussian ansatz gives \(\mathring M_B - 3 m_Q = -0.00031 \alpha_s^2 m_Q\) for \(N_c=3\) and \(\sigma=0\), compared to the exact value of \(-0.00034 \alpha_s^2 m_Q\).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field approach for heavy baryons originally proposed by Witten (1979) and builds on earlier studies by Cohen (2011) and Adhikari (2013) by including \(1/N_c\) corrections such as center-of-mass motion and relativistic effects. It provides a framework for interpreting lattice QCD results for baryon masses at varying \(N_c\) from DeGrand (2012, 2013) and Cordon (2014), and suggests that extending lattice calculations to heavier quark masses would allow more precise tests of the \(1/N_c\) expansion.
