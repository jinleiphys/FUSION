# Timelike Compton scattering: exclusive photoproduction of lepton pairs

> We investigate the exclusive photoproduction of a heavy timelike photon which decays into a lepton pair, gamma p -> l+ l- p. This can be seen as the analog of deeply virtual Compton scattering, and we argue that the two processes are complementary for studying generalized parton distributions in the nucleon. In an unpolarized experiment the angular distribution of the leptons readily provides access to the real part of the Compton amplitude. We estimate the possible size of this effect in kinematics where the Compton process should be dominated by quark exchange.

## Key claim

Timelike Compton scattering (TCS) is the "inverse" of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and the two processes are complementary for studying generalized parton distributions in the nucleon, but the Bethe-Heitler (BH) contribution always dominates over TCS in the kinematic regime of interest, so information on the Compton amplitude must be extracted from the interference term rather than from the cross section directly.

## Method

The authors compute the Born-level amplitudes for the exclusive photoproduction of a lepton pair, $\gamma p \to \ell^+\ell^- p$, separating the process into the timelike Compton scattering (TCS) contribution, the Bethe-Heitler (BH) contribution, and their interference. They evaluate the helicity amplitudes for the Compton process using the handbag diagrams with generalized parton distributions, and parameterize the BH amplitude using the Dirac and Pauli form factors $F_1(t)$ and $F_2(t)$. The angular distribution of the lepton pair is analyzed to isolate the interference term, which is odd under exchange of $\ell^+$ and $\ell^-$ momenta.

## Key numbers

- The BH cross section is parametrically enhanced relative to TCS by a factor $Q'^2 /(-t)$ and has an extra factor of $1/\sin^2\theta$ in the angular dependence.
- The minimum value of the lepton propagator product $L$ is approximately $L_{\mathrm{min}} \approx Q'^2 m_\ell^2 + Q'^2 \Delta_T^2 \sin^2\varphi / (1-\tau)^2$, where $m_\ell$ is the lepton mass.
- The variable $\tau = Q'^2 / (s - M^2)$ for TCS relates to the scaling variable $\eta$ as $\eta = \tau / (2 - \tau)$ to leading-twist accuracy.
- The authors estimate that ranges of $Q'$ where the leading-twist description may work are between about 1.5 to 2 GeV and the $J/\Psi$ mass, and above the charmonium resonances.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), developed by Muller (1994), Ji (1997), and Radyushkin (1997), to the timelike case where the final-state photon has large timelike virtuality. The authors show that TCS and DVCS carry the same information on generalized parton distributions at Born level and leading twist, but that the one-loop corrections differ, making the combination of both processes a more powerful constraint than DVCS alone. The analysis enables experimental extraction of the real part of the Compton amplitude through the lepton angular distribution in unpolarized photoproduction, analogous to the lepton charge asymmetry in DVCS but without requiring beams of both charges.
