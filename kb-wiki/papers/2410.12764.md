# Polarization options in inclusive DIS off tensor polarized deuteron

> In the near future, the Jefferson Lab $b_1$ experiment will provide the second measurement of tensor polarized asymmetries in inclusive DIS on the deuteron. In this asymmetry, 4 independent tensor polarized structure functions contribute. This necessitates systematic approximations in the extraction of the leading twist structure function $b_1$ from a single tensor asymmetry measurement. Contamination from higher twist structure functions and kinematic effects is discussed here. Using a deuteron convolution model, we quantify the systematic errors from these approximations for two different choices for the target polarization direction (momentum transfer, electron beam direction). For Jefferson Lab 12 GeV kinematics, the systematic error turns out to be comparable between the two polarization options, while at higher $Q^2$ values the momentum transfer direction is preferred.

## Key claim

The systematic error in extracting the leading-twist structure function \(b_1\) from a single tensor asymmetry measurement is comparable for target polarization along the momentum transfer direction and along the electron beam direction at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV kinematics, but at higher \(Q^2\) values the momentum transfer direction is preferred.

## Method

The authors used a deuteron convolution model that computes unpolarized and tensor polarized deuteron structure functions through a convolution of unpolarized inclusive nucleon structure functions with deuteron light-front densities, matched to non-relativistic wave functions from Argonne V18, CDBonn, and Paris parametrizations. They compared data-driven parametrizations of nucleon \(F_{2N}\) (including resonance contributions) with leading-order partonic distribution function parametrizations from MSTW2008 and CTEQ. The model was used to compute the true \(b_1\) and the tensor asymmetry, then \(b_1\) was extracted using the approximate relations from Table 1 and compared to the true value to quantify systematic errors.

* For \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) at \(x=0.5\), \(\gamma = 0.66\); for \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) at \(x=0.5\), \(\gamma = 0.30\).
* At \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\), the Callan-Gross relation \(b_2 = x b_1\) holds to within approximately 20% relative difference on average in the JLab experiment region of interest.
* The HERMES measurement reported a 44% relative uncertainty on \(A^T\); the JLab \(b_1\) experiment proposal aims for 9.2% relative uncertainty on \(A^T\).
* The JLab experiment will cover kinematics \(0.8~\text{GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 5.0~\text{GeV}^2\) and \(0.16 < x < 0.49\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the HERMES measurement (2005) of the tensor asymmetry \(A_{zz}\) and the extraction of \(b_1\), which used the Bjorken-limit approximation \(A^T = -(2/3)(b_1/F_1)\). The upcoming Jefferson Lab \(b_1\) experiment will provide a second measurement with significantly improved precision. The study quantifies systematic errors from approximations needed to extract \(b_1\) from a single asymmetry measurement, comparing two polarization direction options, and enables better planning for the experimental configuration.
