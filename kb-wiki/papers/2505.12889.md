# Basis light-front quantization approach to deuteron

> We obtain the deuteron's wave functions as eigenstates of the light-front quantum chromodynamics (QCD) Hamiltonian using a fully relativistic and nonperturbative approach based on light-front quantization, without an explicit confining potential. These eigenstates include six-quark and six-quark--one-gluon components. The deuteron wave function consists of both a singlet-singlet color state and additional hidden color states arising from non-trivial color rearrangements. Our results reveal that while the singlet-singlet state is present, the hidden color states collectively dominate, contributing a larger probability to the deuteron wave function. This highlights the significant role of hidden color components in the QCD description of nuclear structure. Using these wave functions, we investigate the deuteron's electromagnetic properties.

## Key claim

The deuteron's light-front wave function, obtained from a first-principles QCD Hamiltonian without an explicit confining potential, is dominated by hidden-color states (55.5% total probability) rather than the conventional singlet-singlet nucleon-nucleon configuration (44.5%), at the model scale defined by basis truncations \(N_{\text{max}} = 8\) and \(K = 9\).

## Method

The authors solve the light-front QCD Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), expanding the deuteron state in a Fock space truncated to six-quark (\(qqqqqq\)) and six-quark–one-gluon (\(qqqqqqg\)) components. The longitudinal dynamics are discretized with antiperiodic/periodic boundary conditions in a box of length \(2L\) with total longitudinal momentum quantum number \(K = 9\), while transverse degrees of freedom are represented by two-dimensional harmonic oscillator wave functions with truncation \(N_{\text{max}} = 8\) and scale parameter \(b = 0.30\) GeV. The model parameters \(\{m_u, m_d, m_f, g_s\} = \{1.00, 0.95, 42.56, 1.90\}\) (masses in GeV, \(g_s\) dimensionless) are fitted to the deuteron mass and its electromagnetic properties.

## Key numbers

- Hidden-color states collectively contribute 55.5% of the total probability in the deuteron wave function, while the singlet-singlet state contributes 44.5% (for both \(m_J = 0\) and \(m_J = \pm 1\) spin projections).
- The deuteron charge radius is calculated as \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_{C} \rangle} = 1.66\) fm, compared to the experimental value of \(2.130 \pm 0.003 \pm 0.009\) fm (JLABt20, 2000).
- The magnetic radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_{M} \rangle} = 1.64\) fm, compared to the experimental measurement of \(1.90 \pm 0.14\) fm (Afanasev, 1998).
- Basis truncation parameters: \(N_{\text{max}} = 8\), \(K = 9\), harmonic oscillator scale \(b = 0.30\) GeV.
- Fitted quark masses: \(m_u = 1.00\) GeV, \(m_d = 0.95\) GeV; coupling constant \(g_s = 1.90\).

## Context

This work extends the basis light-front quantization approach, previously applied to mesons and baryons (Vary, 2009; Lan, 2019; Mondal, 2019; Kaur, 2023), to the deuteron as the lightest nuclear bound state. It provides the first fully relativistic, nonperturbative QCD calculation of the deuteron wave function including explicit gluonic degrees of freedom, going beyond traditional nucleon-nucleon models and earlier hidden-color discussions (Matveev, 1977; Brodsky, 1983; Bakker, 2014). The results enable future studies of deuteron parton distribution functions, tensor-polarized structure functions \(b_1\) (where discrepancies with HERMES data exist), and comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (Chen, 2024), as well as predictions for experiments at Jefferson Lab, Fermilab, and the Electron-Ion Collider.
