# Scaling of hadronic transverse momenta in a hydrodynamic treatment of relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The transverse momenta of hadrons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are evaluated in a boost invariant hydrodynamics with transverse expansion. Quark gluon plasma is assumed to be formed in the initial state which expands and cools via a first order phase transition to a rich hadronic matter and ultimately undergoes a freeze-out. The average transverse momentum of pions, kaons, and protons is estimated for a wide range of multiplicity densities and transverse sizes of the system. For a given system it is found to scale with the square-root of the particle rapidity density per unit transverse area, and consistent with the corresponding values seen in $p\overline{p}$ experiments at 1800 GeV, suggesting a universal behaviour. The average transverse momentum shows only an approximate scaling with multiplicity density per nucleon which is at variance with the $p\overline{p}$ data.

# Scaling of hadronic transverse momenta in a hydrodynamic treatment of relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The average transverse momentum of hadrons produced in central nucleus-nucleus collisions, calculated using boost-invariant hydrodynamics with transverse expansion, scales with the square-root of the particle rapidity density per unit transverse area and is consistent with corresponding values seen in p-pbar experiments at 1800 GeV, suggesting a universal behaviour. However, the scaling with multiplicity density per nucleon is only approximate and at variance with the p-pbar data.

## Method

The author assumes a thermally and chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma formed at an initial time tau_0 and temperature T_0, which undergoes longitudinally boost-invariant and azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion. The plasma cools via a first-order phase transition at T_C = 180 MeV to a hadronic matter consisting of all hadrons and resonances with mass below 2.5 GeV, and freezes out at 120 MeV. The hydrodynamic equations are solved using the Cooper-Fry formulation to obtain hadronic spectra, with the initial energy density profile following a wounded-nucleon distribution and initial time taken as 1 fm/c.

## Key numbers

* Initial time tau_0 = 1 fm/c used in all calculations
* Phase transition temperature T_C = 180 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_F = 120 MeV
* For nu = 5, decreasing T_F to 100 MeV increases pion <p_T> from 0.44 GeV/c to 0.46 GeV/c, and proton <p_T> from 0.83 GeV/c to 1.02 GeV/c
* For nu = 50, decreasing T_F to 100 MeV increases pion <p_T> from 0.70 GeV/c to 0.72 GeV/c, and proton <p_T> from 1.5 GeV/c to 1.7 GeV/c
* Fitted scaling relations: pion <p_T> = 0.326 + 0.052 sqrt(nu); kaon <p_T> = 0.423 + 0.087 sqrt(nu); proton <p_T> = 0.530 + 0.137 sqrt(nu) (units: GeV/c)
* Average transverse momenta for systems with identical nu decrease by about 4% for pions and about 7% for protons with increasing transverse size
* Changing T_C by +/- 20 MeV changes proton <p_T> by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends earlier dimensional arguments by Kapusta, McLerran, and Srivastava (1992) that transverse momenta scale with particle rapidity density per unit transverse area, and builds on prior hydrodynamic treatments by von Gersdorff et al. (1986) and Cleymans, Redlich, and Srivastava (1997). The results are compared to p-pbar data from the E735 experiment at 1800 GeV (Alexopoulos et al., 1993) and to colour gluon condensate model predictions by McLerran and Schaffner-Bielich (2001). The scaling behaviour enables predictions for RHIC and LHC energies and suggests that the initial conditions seed the transverse momentum scaling that is broadly retained through hydrodynamic expansion, though the author notes that precise data at higher energies and large p_T are needed to distinguish hydrodynamic from partonic treatments.
