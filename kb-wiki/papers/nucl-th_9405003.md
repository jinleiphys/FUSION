# Probing Short Range Nucleon Correlations in High Energy Hard Quasielastic pd Reactions

> We show that the strong dependence of the amplitude for $NN$ hard scattering on the collision energy can be used to magnify the effects of short range nucleon correlations in quasielastic $pd$ scattering. Under specific kinematical conditions the effect of initial and final state interactions can be accounted for by rescaling the cross section calculated within the plane wave impulse approximation. The feasibility to investigate the role of relativistic effects in the deuteron wave function is demonstrated by comparing the predictions of different formalisms. Binding effects due to short range correlations in deuteron are discussed as well.

## Key claim

The strong energy dependence of the hard NN scattering amplitude can be used to magnify the effects of short range nucleon correlations in quasielastic pd reactions, but only under specific kinematical conditions where initial and final state interactions can be accounted for by a simple rescaling of the plane wave impulse approximation cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for the quasielastic d(p,2p)n reaction within the light-cone impulse approximation, using a phenomenological parameterization of the elastic pp differential cross section that reproduces the strong s-dependence of hard scattering. They evaluate the effects of initial and final state interactions using the eikonal approximation, summing the PWIA diagram plus three rescattering diagrams (one for ISI and two for FSI) to derive a scale factor kappa_p. They compare the light-cone formalism with the virtual nucleon formalism to assess relativistic effects, and estimate the suppression of point-like configurations due to nucleon binding using a phenomenological factor.

## Key numbers

- The probability to find a nucleon with momentum above 0.3 GeV/c in the deuteron wave function is at the level of 3-5%, with the D wave dominating for high momentum components.
- The pp cross section parameterization behaves as approximately s^{-(10-4gamma)} for fixed -t >= 2 GeV^2, where gamma = 1.6.
- Off-shell effects cause at most approximately 10% change in cross section values for spectator momenta up to about 0.3 GeV/c.
- Initial and final state interactions are significant for |alpha-1| >= about 0.3 for any transverse spectator momentum, and at large transverse spectator momentum (> about 0.1 GeV/c) for a large range of alpha.
- For spectator momenta |p_s| <= 0.25 GeV/c in parallel geometry and |p_s| <= 0.4 GeV/c in antiparallel geometry, the rescaling factor kappa differs from 1 by typically <= 20-25%.
- There is a considerable difference (about 20-25%) between light-cone and virtual nucleon approaches even at relatively low spectator momenta of about 0.25 GeV/c.
- The asymmetry parameter A(delta) in the virtual nucleon approximation reaches about 0.5 at spectator momenta p_sz ~ -0.25 GeV/c for parallel and p_sz ~ 0.34 GeV/c for antiparallel geometries.
- The parameter Delta E characterizing bound nucleon excitation in the deuteron is approximately 0.6 GeV, and the threshold t_0 for point-like configuration dominance is taken as -2 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the plane wave impulse approximation approach of Frankfurt, Piasetsky, Sargsyan, and Strikman (1994) for probing short range correlations in the deuteron using hard quasielastic pd scattering. It builds on prior studies of deuteron structure using elastic form factors (Arnold 1975, 1987), near-threshold ed scattering (Rock), elastic pd scattering, and backward particle production, which established the presence of high-momentum components but could not provide detailed knowledge of the wave function. The analysis enables future experiments at high-intensity proton and electron facilities (such as CEBAF) to directly investigate short range correlations, relativistic effects in the deuteron wave function, and binding effects related to the EMC effect, by measuring the complete kinematics of the two outgoing protons and the spectator neutron.
