# Probing Unquenching Effects in the Gluon Polarisation in Light Mesons

> We introduce an extension to the ladder truncated Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons and the rainbow truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equations which includes quark-loop corrections to the gluon propagator. This truncation scheme obeys the axialvector Ward-Takahashi identity relating the quark self-energy and the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. Two different approximations to the Yang-Mills sector are used as input: the first is a sophisticated truncation of the full Yang-Mills Dyson-Schwinger equations, the second is a phenomenologically motivated form. We find that the spectra and decay constants of pseudoscalar and vector mesons are overall described well for either approach. Meson mass results for charge eigenstate vector and pseudoscalar meson masses are compared to lattice data. The effects of unquenching the system are small but not negligible.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including quark-loop corrections to the gluon propagator (unquenching) in the Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger framework produces small but non-negligible effects on light meson masses and decay constants, provided the truncation scheme obeys the axialvector Ward-Takahashi identity. The results hold for two different input approximations to the Yang-Mills sector, but the authors caution that the study only addresses unquenching via quark loops in the gluon self-energy, not other effects like meson decays.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for ghost, gluon, and quark propagators in Landau gauge, using two approaches: (1) a full SDE truncation with ansätze for the ghost-gluon, three-gluon, and quark-gluon vertices, and (2) a phenomenological model where the effective quark-gluon interaction is a Gaussian form plus an explicit quark-loop term. The resulting propagators are then used as input into the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector mesons, with the kernel constructed to satisfy the axialvector Ward-Takahashi identity. Meson masses and decay constants are computed for both quenched and unquenched cases and compared to lattice data.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar and vector meson masses and decay constants are computed for the full SDE scheme and the phenomenological model; the unquenching effects are "small but not negligible" (no explicit numerical shifts given in the truncated text).
- For the phenomenological model, parameters ω = 0.5 GeV and d = 16 GeV⁻² give realistic results for pseudoscalar and vector channels (Alkofer 2002).
- The infrared fixed point of the running coupling is α(0) ≈ 2.972 for SU(3) with a bare ghost-gluon vertex, derived from the power-law exponent κ = (93 − √1201)/98 ≈ 0.595.
- Lattice simulations indicate κ ≈ 0.5 (Leinweber 1998, Bowman 2004, Gattnar 2004, Oliveira 2004, Sternbeck 2005).

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation by including quark-loop corrections to the gluon propagator, building on prior studies of unquenching in the gluon polarisation (Fischer 2003) and meson decay mechanisms (Watson 2004). It contrasts with purely phenomenological models (Maris 1999, Alkofer 2002) that replace the Yang-Mills sector with an effective interaction, by explicitly resolving the dressed gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex from SDEs. The results enable future investigations of unquenching effects in other meson channels and provide a controlled comparison with lattice simulations for light meson observables.
