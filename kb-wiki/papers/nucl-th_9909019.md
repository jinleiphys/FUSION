# Strange nuclear matter within Brueckner-Hartree-Fock Theory

> We have developed a formalism for microscopic Brueckner-type calculations of dense nuclear matter that includes all types of baryon-baryon interactions and allows to treat any asymmetry on the fractions of the different species (n, p, $Λ$, $Σ^0$, $Σ^+$, $Σ^-$, $Ξ^-$ and $Ξ^0$). We present results for the different single-particle potentials focussing on situations that can be relevant in future microscopic studies of beta-stable neutron star matter with strangeness. We find the both the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions play a non-negligible role in determining the chemical potentials of the different species.

## Key claim

The authors develop a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism that incorporates all baryon-baryon interactions (including hyperon-hyperon) and allows arbitrary asymmetries in the fractions of eight baryon species, finding that both hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions significantly affect the single-particle potentials and chemical potentials, but the actual composition of beta-stable neutron star matter requires a separate self-consistent calculation beyond the scope of this work.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in coupled channels across strangeness sectors S=0 (NN), S=-1 (YN), and S=-2 (YY), using the Nijmegen model (e) baryon-baryon potential. They compute single-particle potentials for each baryon species (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, allowing different Fermi momenta for particles within the same isospin-strangeness multiplet. The self-consistent calculation determines the single-particle energies and binding energy per baryon for various baryonic densities and compositions.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ in symmetric nuclear matter, the nucleon single-particle potential at zero momentum is approximately -79 MeV
- The Λ single-particle potential at zero momentum in symmetric nuclear matter is approximately -38 MeV, compared to -30 MeV from hypernuclear extrapolation and -24 MeV from the 1989 Nijmegen YN potential
- The Σ⁻ single-particle potential at zero momentum changes from about -20 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter to -37 MeV in pure neutron matter
- At ρ = 0.6 fm⁻³ with hyperon fraction x_Y = 0.1, the Σ⁻-Σ⁻ contribution to the Σ⁻ single-particle potential is about -25 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter
- The binding energy per baryon is calculated for densities up to 0.6 fm⁻³ for various proton and hyperon fractions

## Context

This work extends the microscopic Brueckner-type calculations of Schulze et al. (1995, 1997, 1998) by incorporating the recently available hyperon-hyperon interaction from Stoks and Rijken (1999) and allowing asymmetric compositions of baryon species, which is essential for beta-stable neutron star matter. The authors find that the YY interaction significantly increases attraction for hyperons compared to calculations using only YN interactions, which would lower the density at which hyperons appear in neutron stars. The formalism enables future microscopic studies of beta-stable neutron star matter with strangeness, which the authors state will be presented in a separate publication (Isaac et al., 1999).
