# Analysis of flow effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the CBUU approach

> We study flow phenomena in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, both in transverse and radial direction, in comparison to experimental data. The collective dynamics of the nucleus-nucleus collision is described within a transport model of the coupled channel BUU type (CBUU). This recently developed version includes all nucleonic resonances up to 1.95 GeV in mass and mean-field potentials both of the Skyrme and momentum dependent MDYI type. We find that heavy resonances play an important role in the description of transverse flow above 1 AGeV incident energy. For radial flow we analyse reaction times and equilibration and extract the parameters $T$ and $β$ for temperature and collective flow velocity within different prescriptions. Furthermore, we apply a coalescence model for fragment production and check the mass dependence of the flow signals.

## Key claim

The CBUU transport model, which includes all nucleonic resonances up to 1.95 GeV in mass and momentum-dependent mean-field potentials, can describe both transverse and radial flow phenomena in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the extracted temperature and flow velocity parameters depend significantly on the prescription used for their evaluation and on whether fragment formation via coalescence is taken into account.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (CBUU) transport equation using the test-particle method, including 14 nucleon resonances up to 1.95 GeV/c² mass and mesons (π, η, ρ, σ) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom. The mean-field potential uses a momentum-dependent MDYI-type parametrization fitted to nuclear matter properties from Wiringa et al. (1988), with the non-relativistic potential transformed to a scalar potential in the local rest frame to ensure Lorentz properties. A coalescence model with coalescence radius r₀ and momentum cutoff p₀ = 250 MeV is applied to the final state test-particle distribution to form fragments and study mass dependence of flow signals.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.168 fm⁻³, with binding energy E/A = -16 MeV at ρ₀
- Three compressibilities fitted: K = 210, 260, and 380 MeV for testing different equations of state
- Time step of 0.5 fm/c used for numerical integration of equations of motion
- Total energy loss in full calculation (mean-field plus collisions) is about 1.8% for a central 1 AGeV Au+Au collision
- Coalescence momentum cutoff p₀ fixed to 250 MeV, corresponding roughly to the average Fermi momentum of light nuclei
- Energy range studied: 150 AMeV to 2 AGeV for various systems

## Context

This work extends the CBUU transport model previously applied to pion production (Teis et al., 1997) and photoproduction/absorption (Effenberger et al., 1997) to the systematic study of collective flow phenomena. It builds on earlier BUU approaches by Bertsch and Das Gupta (1988) and Cassing et al. (1988, 1990), and on momentum-dependent potential formulations by Welke et al. (1988) and Gale et al. (1990). The results enable comparison with experimental flow data from the EOS, Plastic Ball, and FOPI collaborations as compiled by Herrmann (1996), and with fragment flow measurements by Lisa et al. (1995) and Reisdorf and Ritter (1997).
