# Quark-model relations among TMDs in the parton model

> The covariant parton model (CPM) is a consequent application of the parton model concept to the nucleon structure. In this model, there is a choice to put quarks either in a pure-spin state or in a mixed-spin state. We show that the mixed-spin version of the CPM does not support the quark-model relations among transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) which were shown to hold in a large class of quark models. One can enforce the quark-model relations to be valid in the CPM by imposing a condition which is equivalent to putting the quarks in a pure-spin state. This gives a complementary perspective on the connection of the pure- and mixed-spin state CPM versions, and provides a fresh view on the question whether the quark-model relations could be realized in QCD as "approximate relations" with some useful numerical accuracy.

## Key claim

The mixed-spin state version of the covariant parton model (CPM) does not support the quark-model relations (QMRs) among transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) that hold in a wide class of other quark models; imposing the validity of these QMRs in the CPM forces the condition that quarks be in a pure-spin state, which is equivalent to the pure-spin state version of the model.

## Method

The authors derived the explicit expressions for all T-even TMDs in the mixed-spin state CPM in terms of three independent covariant amplitudes (A3, A8, A11). They then inserted these model expressions into each of the five linear QMRs and two nonlinear QMRs, and evaluated whether the relations held identically or required a condition on the amplitudes. For the nonlinear QMRs, they used the on-shell identity to eliminate the variable P·k under the k- integral and averaged over dummy integration variables to simplify the expressions.

## Key numbers

- The violation of the nonlinear QMR between h1, h1Lperp, and h1Tperp (Eq. 7) is proportional to mq^2/M^2, which is numerically of order 10^{-6} for light u- and d-flavors.
- The pure-spin state condition A8 = -A11 reduces the CPM from 3 independent amplitudes (mixed-spin) to 2 independent amplitudes (pure-spin).
- The quark mass mq appears in the expressions for TMDs; for massless quarks in the mixed-spin state CPM, chiral-even polarized TMDs depend only on A8 and chiral-odd polarized TMDs depend only on A11.

## Context

This work extends the resolution of a puzzle between two groups studying TMDs in the CPM (Efremov et al. 2009-2020 and D'Alesio et al. 2009), where Aslan (2022) showed the groups' results are equivalent except for the treatment of quark polarization state. The QMRs studied here were previously observed to hold in a wide class of quark models including spectator models, bag models, and light-front constituent quark models (Jakob 1997, Avakian 2007-2010, Pasquini 2008, Lorce 2011). The results enable future investigation of whether QMRs could hold in QCD as approximate relations with useful numerical accuracy in some range of x and kT, and raise the open question of whether other quark models that support QMRs necessarily have quarks in a pure-spin state.
