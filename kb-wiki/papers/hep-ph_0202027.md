# Instanton Contribution to the Pion Electro-Magnetic Formfactor at Q^2 > 1 GeV^2

> We study the effects of instantons on the charged pion electro-magnetic formfactor at intermediate momenta. In the Single Instanton Approximation (SIA), we predict the pion formfactor in the kinematic region Q^2=2-15 GeV^2. By developing the calculation in a mixed time-momentum representation, it is possible to maximally reduce the model dependence and to calculate the formfactor directly. We find the intriguing result that the SIA calculation coincides with the vector dominance monopole form, up to surprisingly high momentum transfer Q^2~10 GeV^2. This suggests that vector dominance for the pion holds beyond low energy nuclear physics.

# Instanton Contribution to the Pion Electro-Magnetic Formfactor at Q^2 > 1 GeV^2

## Key claim
The Single Instanton Approximation (SIA) calculation of the charged pion electromagnetic formfactor coincides with the vector dominance monopole form up to surprisingly high momentum transfer Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2, suggesting that vector dominance for the pion holds beyond low energy nuclear physics. The authors hedge that the SIA breaks down for Q^2 > 20 GeV^2, where multi-instanton effects become significant and the method can no longer be used.

## Method
The authors evaluate the ratio of the three-point to two-point Euclidean correlation functions in a mixed time-momentum representation, isolating the pion pole contribution at large Euclidean time t ~ 0.7 fm. They compute the quark propagators in the background of a single instanton using the zero-mode approximation, averaging over instanton collective coordinates with a size distribution fitted to lattice QCD results (mean instanton size bar-rho = 0.37 fm, width sigma = 0.15 fm). The formfactor is obtained directly from the ratio of these correlation functions without requiring a model for the pion wave function or continuum contributions.

## Key numbers
- The SIA calculation predicts the pion formfactor in the kinematic region Q^2 = 2-15 GeV^2.
- The SIA result coincides with the monopole form up to Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2.
- The monopole form uses M_rho = 770 MeV.
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV appears in the asymptotic pQCD prediction.
- The mean instanton size from lattice QCD is bar-rho = 0.37 fm, with a simpler model using bar-rho = 1/3 fm.
- The free contribution to correlation functions is at most a 30% correction at |q| = 5 GeV.
- The pion pole dominates for t > 0.5 fm, with the calculation using t = 0.7 fm.
- The SIA approach is not applicable for Q^2 > 20 GeV^2.
- The asymptotic pQCD regime for the neutral pion transition formfactor is reached at Q^2 ~ 2 GeV^2 (CLEO measurement).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Blotz and Shuryak, who found agreement between Instanton Liquid Model simulations and the monopole fit at small momentum transfer, and complements the earlier SIA calculation by Forkel and Nielsen, which used QCD sum rules with a model-dependent continuum description. The paper contrasts the charged pion formfactor with the gamma gamma* pi^0 neutral pion transition formfactor measured by CLEO, where the asymptotic pQCD regime is reached much earlier (Q^2 ~ 2 GeV^2) due to different chiral structure. The results enable a microscopic explanation of vector dominance and predict that upcoming JLAB measurements at Q^2 up to 6 GeV^2 will test the role of instanton-induced 't Hooft interactions in hadronic physics.
