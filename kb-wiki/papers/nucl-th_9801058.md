# Anisotropy as a Consequence of Pre-equilibrium Rescattering

> It is shown that azimuthal asymmetry in transverse momentum distribution of particles observed in non-central heavy ion collisions can originate from pre-equilibrium rescattering. The phenomenon is studied using computer simulation of expanding pion gas created in Pb+Pb 160 GeV/n collisions. Results obtained are discussed in comparison with experimental results of NA49 collaboration. Theoretical understanding of mechanism generating the asymmetry is presented. Conclusion about non-equilibrium properties of the phenomenon studied is drawn.

# Anisotropy as a Consequence of Pre-equilibrium Rescattering

## Key claim
The paper claims that elliptic flow (second-order azimuthal asymmetry) in non-central heavy ion collisions can originate from rescattering among produced pions during the pre-equilibrium stage, without requiring full thermalization of nuclear matter. The authors show that the asymmetry is generated even with as few as 0.5 collisions per pion, and that the strength of the asymmetry decreases when the number of collisions per pion exceeds approximately 4, which they interpret as a non-equilibrium feature.

## Method
The authors performed a computer simulation (CASCUS 1.0 model) of an expanding pion gas created in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon. The initial state was generated by a cascade program with azimuthally isotropic momentum distributions and azimuthally asymmetric spatial distributions in the transverse plane. Pions propagated classically and underwent elastic collisions when their distance was less than the critical distance derived from the isospin-averaged elastic pion-pion cross section, with formation time and interaction delay parameters incorporated. The resulting azimuthal distributions were fitted to extract the second-order asymmetry parameter S₂.

## Key numbers
- The simulation was performed for Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon beam energy.
- Impact parameters studied: b = 3, 5, 7, 9, 10, 11, 12 fm.
- Time step in the simulation: ΔT = 0.1 fm/c.
- Non-zero asymmetry was generated for as few as 0.5 collisions per pion (at b = 11 fm).
- The asymmetry increased with impact parameter up to b = 9–10 fm, then decreased.
- For b = 7 fm events, the asymmetry strength exhibited a maximum near N_coll/π ≈ 4 collisions per pion.
- At N_coll/π ≈ 16, the asymmetry appeared to saturate.
- The rapidity dependence showed a maximum at ΔY = 0.75 from central rapidity, with a decrease at midrapidity.
- The asymmetry increased with transverse momentum p_t for N_coll/π = 4, 8, and 14 collisions per pion.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of elliptic flow beyond the hydrodynamical model of Ollitrault (1992), which assumed thermalization of nuclear matter in the asymmetrical overlapping region. The results contradict the assumption of Sorge (1997) that elliptic flow at AGS energies has weak sensitivity to non-equilibrium effects in the central rapidity region. The simulation results are compared with experimental data from the NA49 collaboration (presented at Quark Matter 97 and elsewhere). The paper enables future determination of the formation time of pions from experimental data through careful analysis of the p_t dependence of the asymmetry, and provides a two-point source decomposition formalism for analytical study of the asymmetry in the one-collision-per-pion approximation.
