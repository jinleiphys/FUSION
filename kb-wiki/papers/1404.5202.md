# Mirror energy difference and the structure of loosely bound proton-rich nuclei around A = 20

> The properties of loosely bound proton-rich nuclei around A = 20 are investigated within the framework of nuclear shell model. In these nuclei, the strength of the effective interactions involving the loosely bound proton s1=2 orbit are significantly reduced in comparison with those in their mirror nuclei. We evaluate the reduction of the effective interaction by calculating the monopole-baseduniversal interaction (VMU) in the Woods-Saxon basis. The shell-model Hamiltonian in the sd shell, such as USD, can thus be modified to reproduce the binding energies and energy levels of the weakly bound proton-rich nuclei around A = 20. The effect of the reduction of the effective interaction on the structure and decay properties of these nuclei is also discussed.

# Mirror energy difference and the structure of loosely bound proton-rich nuclei around A = 20

## Key claim

The large mirror energy differences (MED) observed in proton-rich nuclei around A=20 can be reproduced by modifying the USD-family shell-model Hamiltonians to include both a shift of the single-particle energy of the proton 1s1/2 orbit (lowered by 0.375 MeV relative to the neutron) and a reduction of the two-body matrix elements involving that orbit, with reduction factors as small as 0.68 for the (1s1/2)2 proton-proton interaction. The reduction is evaluated using the monopole-based universal interaction (VMU) in a Woods-Saxon basis, and the effect is dominated by the loosely bound nature of the proton s1/2 orbit rather than by charge symmetry breaking of the nuclear force.

## Method

The authors start from the USD, USDA, and USDB shell-model Hamiltonians for the sd shell and modify them for proton-rich nuclei. They calculate reduction factors for proton-proton and proton-neutron two-body matrix elements involving the 1s1/2 orbit by expanding Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis (up to N=9 shells) and evaluating matrix elements of the VMU interaction plus a spin-orbit force from M3Y. The single-particle energy of the proton 1s1/2 orbit is lowered by 0.375 MeV relative to the neutron based on the experimental excitation energy difference between 17F and 17O. Shell-model calculations are performed with the code OXBASH for nuclei 18Ne, 19Na, 20Mg, 21-24Al, and 22-24Si.

- The reduction factor for ⟨1s1/2 0d5/2|V|(0d5/2)2⟩pp21 is 0.87.

## Context

This work extends the USD-family shell-model Hamiltonians (Wildenthal 1984, Brown and Richter 2006) to the proton-rich side of the sd shell, where the loosely bound nature of the 1s1/2 orbit produces large mirror energy differences not captured by the original interactions. The reduction of effective interactions involving the s1/2 orbit was previously noted by Ogawa et al. (1999) for nuclei around 16O, and the present work provides systematic reduction factors using the VMU interaction of Otsuka et al. (2010). The results enable improved predictions for binding energies, excitation spectra, and Gamow-Teller decay strengths of proton drip-line nuclei such as 18Ne, 19Na, 21-24Al, and 24Si, which are relevant for nuclear astrophysics processes including the 17F(p,γ)18Ne reaction in stellar explosions.
