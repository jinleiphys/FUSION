# Pre-equilibrium mechanisms in the93Nb(p,alpha) inclusive reaction at incident energies from 65 to 160 MeV

> The reaction mechanism of pre-equilibrium proton-induced $α$-particle emission from 93Nb at an incident energy of 100 MeV was investigated with polarized projectiles. A formalism based on the statistical multistep direct emission model of Feshbach, Kerman and Koonin was found to give a reasonably good reproduction of cross section and analyzing power angular distributions at various emission energies. Existing experimental distributions for the same reaction at an incident energy of 65 MeV were also analyzed with the same model. The incident-energy variation from 65 MeV up to 160 MeV was found to be consistent with the predictions of the basic model. However, whereas knockout of an $α$ cluster is the dominant reaction mechanism in the final stage at the lowest- and highest incident energies, at 100 MeV a pickup process competes with comparable intensity in yield.

## Key claim

The Feshbach-Kerman-Koonin statistical multistep direct emission model reproduces cross section and analyzing power angular distributions for the 93Nb(p,alpha) reaction at 65, 100, and 160 MeV, but the dominant reaction mechanism changes with incident energy: knockout of an alpha cluster dominates at 65 MeV and 160 MeV, while at 100 MeV a pickup process competes with comparable intensity.

## Method

The authors measured double-differential cross sections and analyzing powers for the 93Nb(p,alpha) reaction at 100 MeV incident energy using polarized protons at iThemba LABS, with detector telescopes at symmetric angles and polarization switching to minimize systematic errors. They analyzed these new data together with existing published data at 65 MeV using the FKK multistep direct reaction formalism, performing DWBA calculations for both alpha-cluster knockout and triton pickup mechanisms with microscopic optical potentials based on double-folding and high-energy approximation methods.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 100 ± 0.5 MeV for the new measurements at iThemba LABS
- Main systematic uncertainty in cross section: about 8%, originating from target thickness and uniformity
- Beam polarization: nominal 80% perpendicular to the reaction plane, with less than 10% variation between polarization directions
- Energy bins: 4 MeV wide intervals for cross sections and analyzing powers
- Overall accuracy of emission-energy scale: better than 4%
- Momentum mismatch between entrance and exit channels: 400 to 600 MeV/c across the incident energies studied
- Q-value of the reaction: 6.4 MeV (positive), giving a kinematic limit of 106 MeV for outgoing alpha particles
- Emission energies analyzed at 100 MeV: from 98 MeV down to 34 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of proton-induced composite-particle emission by the same group, including studies of (p,3He) reactions (Cowley et al., 1997, 2000, 2007, 2012) and the 93Nb(p,alpha) reaction at 160 MeV (Dimitrova et al., 2014). The finding that pickup and knockout compete at 100 MeV contrasts with the dominance of knockout at both lower (65 MeV, data from Sakai et al., 1980) and higher (160 MeV) incident energies, and contradicts earlier assumptions that a single mechanism dominates across the energy range. The results enable further theoretical work to understand the energy-dependent transition between reaction mechanisms and motivate complementary experimental studies for proper evaluation of the optical potential parameters.
