# Rho-omega mixing, vector meson dominance and the pion form-factor

> We review the current status of rho-omega mixing and discuss its implication for our understanding of charge-symmetry breaking. In order to place this work in context we also review the photon-hadron coupling within the framework of vector meson dominance. This leads naturally to a discussion of the electromagnetic form-factor of the pion and of nuclear shadowing.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of rho-omega mixing and vector meson dominance (VMD) as they relate to the pion form-factor, arguing that the theoretical consistency of the traditional approach to charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) in nuclear physics has been challenged by recent work suggesting the rho-omega mixing amplitude changes sign between the rho pole and the space-like region relevant to the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors present a historical and theoretical review of VMD, deriving two Lagrangian formulations (VMD1 and VMD2) and showing their equivalence under universality. They then discuss the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando et al. and the bosonised Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model as more modern theoretical foundations. The paper examines rho-omega mixing in the context of the pion electromagnetic form-factor and its implications for charge-symmetry violation in nuclear systems, particularly the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is given as 93 MeV.
- The rho meson mass is denoted as m_rho, with the relation m_rho^2 = a g^2 f_pi^2 derived from the hidden local symmetry model.
- The pion form-factor at zero momentum transfer is constrained to F_pi(0) = 1.
- The coupling constant renormalisation from VMD dressing gives e^2 → e^2 (1 - e^2/g_rho^2) as q^2 → 0.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on vector meson dominance initiated by Sakurai and formalised by Kroll, Lee and Zumino (1967), and connects to the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando et al. and the bosonised Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model. The paper addresses the challenge posed by recent claims that the rho-omega mixing amplitude changes sign between the rho pole and the space-like region, which would affect the conventional explanation of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei. It enables further theoretical work on the consistency of charge-symmetry breaking in nuclear physics and on the interpretation of the pion form-factor data.
