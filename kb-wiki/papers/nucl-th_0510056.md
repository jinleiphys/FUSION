# Forward-angle K+ Lambda photoproduction in a Regge-plus-resonance approach

> We present an effective-Lagrangian description for forward-angle K+ Lambda photoproduction from the proton, valid for photon lab energies from threshold up to 16 GeV. The high-energy part of the amplitude is modeled in terms of t-channel Regge-trajectory exchange. The sensitivity of the calculated observables to the Regge-trajectory phase is investigated in detail. The model is extended towards the resonance region by adding a number of s-channel resonances to the t-channel background. The proposed hybrid ``Regge-plus-resonance'' (RPR) approach allows one to exploit the p(gamma,K+)Lambda data in their entirety, resulting in stronger constraints on both the background and resonance couplings. The high-energy data can be used to fix the background contributions, leaving the resonance couplings as the sole free parameters in the resonance region. We compare various implementations of the RPR model, and explore to what extent the description of the data can be improved by introducing the ``new'' resonances D13(1900) and P11(1900). Despite its limited number of free parameters, the proposed RPR approach provides an efficient description of the p(gamma,K+)Lambda dynamics in and beyond the resonance region.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a hybrid "Regge-plus-resonance" (RPR) approach for forward-angle K+Λ photoproduction that uses high-energy data to fix the t-channel background contributions, leaving only resonance couplings as free parameters in the resonance region; the authors qualify that the Regge parameterization is assumed to remain physical in the resonance region despite being designed for high energies, and that the double-counting issue from superimposing a small number of resonances onto the Regge background may not be severe.

## Method
The authors model the high-energy amplitude (ω_lab ≳ 4 GeV) using t-channel Regge-trajectory exchange for K(494) and K*(892) mesons, with an s-channel electric Born term added to restore gauge invariance. They extend this to the resonance region by superimposing s-channel nucleon resonances (including S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720), and exploring D13(1900) and P11(1900)) using Feynman propagators with Gaussian form factors that quench resonance contributions above 4 GeV. The background parameters (g_KΛp, G_K*^v, G_K*^t) are fitted against high-energy data at 5, 8, 11, and 16 GeV, while resonance couplings are determined from lower-energy data.

## Key numbers
- Photon lab energy range: from threshold up to 16 GeV
- K trajectory slope: 0.70 GeV^(-2); K* trajectory slope: 0.85 GeV^(-2)
- Four background model variants yield χ² values of 2.73, 3.17, 3.37, and 4.37 against high-energy data
- g_KΛp/√(4π) values range from -3.00 to -3.31 across the four viable models
- The K-K* interference oscillation period for constant K* phase: 2.9 GeV²; for two rotating phases: 13.3 GeV²
- High-energy data comprise 56 differential cross sections at ω_lab = 5, 8, 11, and 16 GeV, plus 7 recoil polarization and 9 photon beam asymmetry points

## Context
This work extends the Regge formalism previously applied to pion and kaon photoproduction (Guidal, 1997) and to η and η' production (Chiang, 2003) by developing a hybrid model for the K+Λ channel. It addresses the uncertainty in background modeling that plagued earlier isobar approaches (Mart and Bennhold, 2000; Janssen et al., 2001), where extracted resonance couplings were sensitive to the background model choice. The RPR approach enables using the full p(γ,K+)Λ data set to constrain both background and resonance couplings, and allows testing the evidence for "missing" resonances such as D13(1900) and P11(1900) that were previously debated in constituent-quark model predictions (Capstick and Roberts, 1994) and other analyses (Mart and Bennhold, 2000; Saghai, 2001).
