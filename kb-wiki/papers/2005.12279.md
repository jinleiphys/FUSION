# Recoil-free azimuthal angle for precision boson-jet correlation

> The azimuthal decorrelation between a vector boson and a jet is an essential hard probe in high energy proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions. We overcome intrinsic limitations of previous studies by using a recoil-free axis, achieving unprecedented next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy with small nonperturbative corrections. This choice of axis also makes the observable robust in the presence of a large background. Furthermore, the azimuthal angle distribution is minimally changed when determined using only charged particle tracks, which offer superior angular resolution for precise measurements. Our effective field theory includes full jet dynamics, and we find contributions from linearly-polarized gluon transverse momentum distributions in the initial and final state.

## Key claim

By using a recoil-free jet axis (Winner-Takes-All recombination scheme) instead of standard jet axes, the authors achieve next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy for the azimuthal decorrelation between a vector boson and a jet, overcoming the intrinsic limitation of non-global logarithms that hampered previous studies at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization formula in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that separates the cross section into transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) beam functions, a TMD jet function, a soft function, and a hard function, all evaluated in the back-to-back limit where the azimuthal angle deviation delta-phi approaches zero. They resum large logarithms by evaluating each ingredient at its natural scale and using renormalization group evolution, including for the first time contributions from linearly-polarized gluon TMD distributions in both the initial and final state. The factorization is validated by comparing to fixed-order NLO calculations from MCFM, and the track-based measurement is analyzed using track functions.

## Key numbers

- The jet function at one-loop order for quarks: C_J_q^(1) = C_F [ L_b (3 + 4 ln(nu/omega)) + 7 - 2pi^2/3 - 6 ln 2 ]
- The jet function at one-loop order for gluons: C_J_g^(1) = C_A [ L_b (11/3 + 4 ln(nu/omega)) + 131/18 - 2pi^2/3 - 22/3 ln 2 ] + T_F n_f [ -4/3 L_b - 17/9 + 8/3 ln 2 ]
- The linearly-polarized gluon jet function at one-loop order: C_J_g^(L,1) = -1/3 C_A + 2/3 T_F n_f
- The soft function at one-loop order: S^(1)(b_perp, mu, nu) = -2 L_b^2 + 8 L_b ln(mu/nu) - pi^2/3, where L_b = ln(b_perp^2 mu^2 e^(2 gamma_E)/4)
- The analysis uses jets with anti-k_T algorithm R=0.5, leading jet p_T,J > 60 GeV and |eta_J| < 2, at LHC sqrt(s)=13 TeV
- The natural scales are: mu_H ~ nu_B ~ nu_J ~ p_T,V ~ m_V, and mu_B ~ mu_J ~ mu_S ~ nu_S ~ 1/|b_x|

## Context

This work extends the NLL resummation results of Banfi (2008) for boson-jet azimuthal decorrelation to NNLL accuracy, overcoming the non-global logarithm problem that limited previous approaches using p_T^n-weighted recombination schemes. The use of a recoil-free axis was proposed for lepton-ion collisions by Arratia (2019), and the authors adapt this to hadron collisions with more complex color and spin structure. The results enable precision comparisons to LHC data and provide a baseline for heavy-ion collision studies where the underlying event background is large, as well as access to three-dimensional gluon dynamics inside the proton through linearly-polarized TMD distributions.
