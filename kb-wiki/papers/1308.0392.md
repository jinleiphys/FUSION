# Cluster states and monopole transitions in $^{16}$O

> Cluster structures and monopole transitions in positive parity states of $^{16}$O were investigated based on the generator coordinate method calculation of an extended $^{12}$C+$α$ cluster model. The ground and excited states of a $^{12}$C cluster are taken into account by using $^{12}$C wave functions obtained with the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The $0^+_2$ state of $^{16}$O and its rotational members, the $2^+_1$ and $4^+_1$ states are described well by the cluster states dominated by the $^{12}$C($0^+_1$)+$α$ structure. Above the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$α$ threshold energy, we obtained a $0^+$ state having the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$α$ cluster structure, which is considered to be a candidate for the $4α$ cluster gas state. The band structures were discussed based on the calculated $E2$ transition strength. Isoscalar Monopole excitations from the ground state were also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fifth $0^+$ state ($0^+_V$) obtained at 17.1 MeV excitation energy in a microscopic $^{12}$C(AMD)+$\alpha$GCM calculation has a developed $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$\alpha$ cluster structure and is a candidate for the $4\alpha$ cluster gas state, but the authors note that this state is obtained within a bound-state approximation and its stability should be checked carefully by considering mixing of continuum states.

## Method

The authors performed a generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation of an extended $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster model for $^{16}$O, where the $^{12}$C cluster wave functions were obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after parity and angular-momentum projection (VAP). They superposed $^{16}$O wave functions constructed from three AMD configurations of $^{12}$C (for the $0^+_1$, $0^+_2$, and $1^-_1$ states) and an $\alpha$ cluster, using six inter-cluster distances from 1.2 to 7.2 fm, and solved the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the final states.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+_V$ state (candidate for $4\alpha$ cluster gas) is obtained at 17.1 MeV excitation energy, with a charge radius of 3.8 fm and an $M(E0)$ monopole transition matrix element of 1.4 fm$^2$ (1.4% of the isoscalar monopole energy-weighted sum rule).
- The $0^+_{II}$ state (assigned to experimental $0^+_2$ at 6.05 MeV) is obtained at 8.5 MeV excitation energy, with charge radius 3.5 fm and $M(E0)=4.0$ fm$^2$ (5.4% of EWSR).
- The $0^+_{III}$ state at 12.5 MeV has charge radius 3.9 fm and $M(E0)=3.5$ fm$^2$ (6.4% of EWSR).
- The $0^+_{IV}$ state at 14.0 MeV has charge radius 3.5 fm and $M(E0)=6.0$ fm$^2$ (20% of EWSR).
- The $B(E2)$ value for the $2^+_I \rightarrow 0^+_{II}$ transition is 177 e$^2$fm$^4$, and for the $4^+_I \rightarrow 2^+_I$ transition is 290 e$^2$fm$^4$.
- The $0^+_V$ state has more than 60% overlap with the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$\alpha$ wave function at an inter-cluster distance of 4-5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic $4\alpha$-OCM calculations by Funaki et al. (2008, 2010) that suggested the $^{16}$O($0^+_6$) state near the $4\alpha$ threshold as a candidate for the $4\alpha$ cluster gas state, and the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ OCM calculations by Suzuki (1976) that described lower excited states. The present microscopic calculation improves upon earlier microscopic RGM and GCM calculations by Libert et al. (1980) and others that overestimated the $0^+_2$ excitation energy (giving $E_x(0^+_2)\sim 16$ MeV) by using AMD wave functions for $^{12}$C that better reproduce the $^{12}$C binding energy and excited state properties. The results enable further experimental searches for the $4\alpha$ cluster gas state and its rotational band members, though the authors find that the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$\alpha$ structure is fragile under rotation, with the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)+$\alpha$ component decreasing and fragmenting among several $2^+$ and $4^+$ states as spin increases.
