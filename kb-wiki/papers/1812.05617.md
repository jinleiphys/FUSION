# Nuclear structure factors for general spin-independent WIMP-nucleus scattering

> We present nuclear structure factors that describe the generalized spin-independent coupling of weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) to nuclei. Our results are based on state-of-the-art nuclear structure calculations using the large-scale nuclear shell model. Starting from quark- and gluon-level operators, we consider all possible coherently enhanced couplings of spin-1/2 and spin-0 WIMPs to one and two nucleons up to third order in chiral effective field theory. This includes a comprehensive discussion of the structure factors corresponding to the leading two-nucleon currents covering, for the first time, the contribution of spin-2 operators. We provide results for the most relevant nuclear targets considered in present and planned dark matter direct detection experiments: fluorine, silicon, argon, and germanium, complementing our previous work on xenon. All results are also publicly available in a Python notebook.

## Key claim
The paper presents nuclear structure factors for generalized spin-independent WIMP-nucleus scattering based on large-scale nuclear shell model calculations, covering fluorine, silicon, argon, and germanium targets. The authors note that their structure factors are not fully consistent because the many-body calculations use phenomenological interactions rather than chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) interactions, which would be required for a fully consistent treatment.

## Method
The authors combine chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) with large-scale nuclear shell model calculations using the ANTOINE code, considering WIMP couplings to one and two nucleons up to third order in the chiral expansion. They calculate nuclear structure factors for all stable isotopes of fluorine, silicon, argon, and germanium, including for the first time the contribution of spin-2 operators from the traceless components of the energy-momentum tensor. The shell model calculations use phenomenological effective interactions (USDB for sd-shell nuclei, SDPF.SM for argon, and RG for germanium) in truncated configuration spaces appropriate for each mass region.

## Key numbers
- Pion scalar couplings: f_u^π = 0.32(2) and f_d^π = 0.68(2), based on the quark mass ratio m_u/m_d = 0.46(3) from Aoki (2016)
- Pion spin-2 couplings from parton distribution functions: f_{u,π}^{(2)} = f_{d,π}^{(2)} = 0.26(5), f_{s,π}^{(2)} = 0.01(5), with gluon contribution f_{g,π}^{(2)} = 0.47(15) from Badier (1983)
- Updated pion spin-2 couplings from global analysis at μ=2 GeV: f_{u,π}^{(2)} = f_{d,π}^{(2)} = 0.298(8), f_{s,π}^{(2)} = 0.055(4), f_{g,π}^{(2)} = 0.341(19) from Barry (2018)
- Valence quark momentum fraction in pion: ⟨x⟩_v^π = 0.217(11) from Wijesooriya (2005), updated to 0.256(13) at μ=2 GeV from Abdel-Rehim (2015)
- Calculated charge radius of ¹⁹F: 2.83 fm (experimental: 2.898(2) fm)
- Calculated charge radius of ²⁸Si: 3.19 fm (experimental: 3.122(2) fm)
- Calculated magnetic moment of ¹⁹F: 2.9 n.m. (experimental: 2.628868(8) n.m.)
- Calculated B(E2) for ¹⁹F 5/2⁺₁→1/2⁺_gs: 19 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 20.9(2) e²fm⁴)
- Calculated B(E2) for ¹⁹F 9/2⁺₁→5/2⁺₁: 19 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 25(3) e²fm⁴)
- Calculated B(M1) for ¹⁹F 3/2⁺₁→5/2⁺₁: 3.4 n.m.² (experimental: 4.1(2.5) n.m.²)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study on xenon targets (Hoferichter 2016) to fluorine, silicon, argon, and germanium, which are the nuclear targets used in present and planned dark matter direct detection experiments. The ChEFT approach builds on the non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework of Fitzpatrick (2012) and Anand (2013), but additionally predicts consistent two-nucleon currents that cannot be captured by single-nucleon NREFT operators alone. The results enable experimental collaborations to include two-nucleon contributions and spin-2 operators in their dark matter searches, as already demonstrated for the first constraint on two-nucleon currents by Aprile (2018) for xenon.
