# Pion emission source shape in UrQMD Au+Au collisions at STAR energies

> Femtoscopic measurements of two-pion Bose--Einstein correlations have established that particle-emitting sources in heavy-ion collisions are well described by Lévy $α$-stable distributions, motivating systematic studies across a wide range of collision energies. In this work, we present a three-dimensional femtoscopic analysis of pion pairs in Au+Au collisions simulated with the UrQMD model for collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$--$27\,\mathrm{GeV}$, taking the RHIC BES-II range of collider and fixed target experiment energies for reference. Using Lévy-type source parameterisations, we extract the pair multiplicity parameter $λ^{*}$ (related to the correlation strength $λ$), Lévy index $α$, and three-dimensional radii $R_\mathrm{out}$, $R_\mathrm{side}$, and $R_\mathrm{long}$. We investigate their dependence on the transverse mass ($m_T$) and collision energy, along with derived quantities such as the radius difference $R_{\mathrm{diff}}^2=R_{\mathrm{out}}^2-R_\mathrm{side}^2$ and the ratio $R_\mathrm{out}/R_\mathrm{side}$. We find that $R_\mathrm{out,side,long}$ all decrease with increasing $m_T$ and increase with collision energy, consistent with collective expansion, $R_\mathrm{long}$ showing the strongest and $R_\mathrm{side}$ the weakest energy dependence. The Lévy index $α$ decreases with collision energy, with a larger $m_T$-dependence towards higher energies. The $λ^{*}$ parameter is consistent with a constant close to unity in the absence of pions from long-lived resonances. These results provide a baseline for future comparisons with experimental measurements from the STAR Collaboration, contributing to constraints on the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
In UrQMD Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$--$27\,\mathrm{GeV}$, the Lévy index $\alpha$ decreases monotonically with collision energy and shows increasing $m_T$-dependence at higher energies, establishing a smooth hadronic-transport baseline free of critical-point effects against which future experimental data from the STAR Collaboration can be tested for non-monotonic signatures of the QCD critical point.

## Method
The authors simulated Au+Au collisions using the UrQMD event generator version 3.4 in default hadronic cascade mode (no hydrodynamic phase) for 11 collision energies between 3.0 and 27.0 GeV, selecting 0--10% central events. They directly computed three-dimensional pair distance distributions $D(\vec\rho)$ from freeze-out coordinates of identical charged pions in the Longitudinal Co-Moving System (LCMS), then simultaneously fitted one-dimensional projections along the Bertsch-Pratt coordinates (out, side, long) with Lévy-stable source parameterizations to extract $\alpha$, $\lambda^{*}$, $R_\mathrm{out}$, $R_\mathrm{side}$, and $R_\mathrm{long}$ as functions of transverse mass $m_T$ and collision energy.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy range: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.0$ to $27.0\,\mathrm{GeV}$, covering the RHIC BES-II range
- Simulated $100\,000$ events for lower energies (3.0, 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, 4.5 GeV) and $10\,000$ events for higher energies (7.7, 9.2, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27.0 GeV)
- Impact parameter range: 0 to 4.73 fm, corresponding approximately to 0--10% centrality
- Track cuts: transverse momentum $0.15 < p_T < 1.0\,\mathrm{GeV}/c$, pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 1.0$
- Default $Q_\mathrm{LCMS}^\mathrm{max} = \sqrt{0.15 \cdot m_T}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ with systematic variations at $0.05$ and $0.25\,\mathrm{GeV}$
- Default fit range upper limit $\rho_{\mathrm{fit,default}}^{\mathrm{max}} = 80.0\,\mathrm{fm}$, scaled by $\sqrt{\sqrt{s_{NN}}/11.5\,\mathrm{GeV}}$ and varied by $\pm25\%$
- Number of merged events per fit ranged from 100 (at 27.0 GeV) to 20,000 (at 3.0 GeV) for default configuration
- Total systematic uncertainties on $\alpha$ at 3.0 GeV: +0.75% / -1.98%; at 27.0 GeV: +0.30% / -1.25%
- Total systematic uncertainties on $R_\mathrm{long}$ at 3.0 GeV: +3.43% / -4.37%; at 27.0 GeV: +4.20% / -5.14%

## Context
This work extends previous femtoscopic studies using Lévy-stable source parameterizations at SPS (Adhikary 2023, Porfy 2024), RHIC (Adare 2018, Kovacs 2023, Mukherjee 2023, Abdulameer 2024, Kincses 2024), and LHC (Tumasyan 2024, Korodi 2023) energies by providing a theoretical baseline from a hadronic transport model without critical-point dynamics. It directly complements a similar analysis using the EPOS4 model by Huang et al. (2025) in the overlapping 7.7--27 GeV range, finding excellent quantitative agreement for $\alpha$ but differences in $R_\mathrm{out}$ energy dependence attributed to the presence or absence of a hydrodynamic phase. The results enable future comparisons with STAR BES-II experimental measurements to identify non-monotonic energy dependence that could signal the QCD critical point, as proposed by Csanad et al. (2025).
