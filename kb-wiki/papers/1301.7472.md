# Effects of the two-body and three-body hyperon-nucleon interactions in Λ-hypernuclei

> Background: The calculation of the hyperon binding energy in hypernuclei is crucial to understanding the interaction between hyperons and nucleons. Purpose: We assess the relative importance of two- and three-body hyperon-nucleon force by studying the effect of the hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interaction in closed shell Λ-hypernuclei from A=5 to 91. Methods: The Λ-binding energy has been calculated using the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method for the first time, to study light and heavy hypernuclei within the same model. Results: Our results show that including a three-body component in the hyperon-nucleon interaction leads to a saturation of the Λ-binding energy remarkably close to the experimental data. In contrast, the two-body force alone gives an unphysical limit for the binding energy. Conclusions: The repulsive contribution of the three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon force is essential to reproduce, even qualitatively, the binding energy of the hypernuclei in the mass range considered.

## Key claim

The repulsive three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon (ΛNN) interaction is essential to reproduce the saturation of the Λ binding energy in hypernuclei from A=5 to 91; the two-body ΛN force alone produces an unphysical, ever-increasing binding energy with mass number.

## Method

The authors used the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to compute the ground-state energy of closed-shell Λ-hypernuclei, treating nucleons and the Λ hyperon as nonrelativistic particles. They employed a coordinate-space Hamiltonian with two-body NN potentials (Argonne AV4', AV6', or Minnesota), a two-body ΛN interaction, and a three-body ΛNN interaction from the Usmani family of potentials. The Λ-separation energy BΛ was extracted as the difference between the total binding energy of the nucleus and that of the corresponding hypernucleus.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵_ΛHe with the AV4' NN potential: BΛ = 7.1(1) MeV using only the two-body ΛN force; BΛ = 5.1(1) MeV when the three-body ΛNN force is included (experimental value: 3.12(2) MeV).
- For ¹⁷_ΛO with the AV4' NN potential: BΛ = 43(1) MeV using only the two-body ΛN force; BΛ = 19(1) MeV when the three-body ΛNN force is included (semiempirical reference: 13.0(4) MeV).
- For ⁹¹_ΛZr, the two-body ΛN force alone gives a BΛ of approximately 100 MeV, while inclusion of the three-body ΛNN force brings the value to about 20 MeV, qualitatively close to the experimental trend.
- The kaon-exchange correction (ε=0.1) to the total energy is -0.33(6) MeV in ⁵_ΛHe and +0.2(4) MeV in ¹⁷_ΛO.

## Context

This work extends earlier quantum Monte Carlo studies of hypernuclei by Bodmer, Usmani, and Carlson (1984-2008) and provides the first consistent AFDMC calculation spanning light to medium-heavy hypernuclei up to A=91. The results contradict the expectation from Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Nogga et al. (2002), who found that an attractive ΛNN interaction was needed for ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe; here the three-body force is found to be repulsive and essential for saturation. The findings enable future studies of the equation of state of Λ-neutron matter in neutron star cores, potentially reconciling hyperon onset with observed neutron star masses near 2 M⊙.
