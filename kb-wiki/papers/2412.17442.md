# Spectral analysis for nucleon-pion and nucleon-pion-pion states in both parity sectors using distillation with domain-wall fermions

> We present a study using the distillation method to analyze the spectra of nucleon, nucleon-pion, and nucleon-pion-pion states in the positive-parity sector, as well as nucleon and nucleon-pion states in the negative-parity sector. The study uses seven domain-wall fermion ensembles with varying pion masses ($m_π= 139 - 279~\text{MeV}$), lattice spacings ($a^{-1} = 1.730~\text{GeV}$ and $a^{-1}=2.359~\text{GeV}$) and volumes ($m_πL = 3.8 - 7.5$). To address the large number of contractions in this project, we implemented an algorithm to automate the contraction of nucleon-pion correlation functions that contain an arbitrary number of pions. In the positive parity sector, we extrapolate the nucleon mass to the physical point. This study demonstrates the effectiveness of the distillation method for baryonic quantities with a focus on multi-hadronic states and establishes a foundation for future work.

## Key claim

The distillation method is effective for computing baryonic and multi-hadronic correlation functions, including nucleon-pion-pion states for the first time, but the truncation of high modes of the Laplace operator may show signs of systematic effects in the negative parity sector.

## Method

The authors used seven domain-wall fermion ensembles from the RBC/UKQCD collaboration with varying pion masses, lattice spacings, and volumes to compute correlation functions for nucleon, nucleon-pion, and nucleon-pion-pion states in both parity sectors. They applied the distillation method with eigenvectors of the three-dimensional spatial Laplace operator to construct smeared quark fields, and implemented an automated contraction algorithm to handle the large number of Wick contractions (up to 882 non-vanishing diagrams for some correlation functions). A generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) analysis was performed on the matrix of correlation functions to extract the eigenenergies of the finite-volume states.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses range from 139 MeV to 279 MeV across the seven ensembles
- Two lattice spacings used: a^{-1} = 1.730 GeV (from 48I ensemble) and a^{-1} = 2.359 GeV (from 64I ensemble)
- Spatial volumes span m_pi L = 3.8 to 7.5
- Number of Laplace eigenmodes used: N_c = 60 for six ensembles, N_c = 120 for the physical pion mass ensemble (C)
- Ensemble C has m_pi = 139.32(30) MeV and m_K = 499.44(88) MeV on a 64^3 x 128 x 24 lattice with 25 configurations
- Ensemble 4 has m_pi = 274.8(2.5) MeV on a 24^3 x 48 x 24 lattice with 94 configurations
- Ensemble L has m_pi = 278.9(4.9) MeV on a 64^3 x 128 x 24 lattice with 20 configurations

## Context

This work extends previous distillation studies of baryonic quantities by Lang (2013, 2017) and Egerer (2019), which used either large pion masses, small physical volumes, or fermion discretizations without good chiral properties. It also builds on recent efforts by Barca (2023), Alexandrou (2023, 2024), and Grebe (2023) to include multi-hadronic operators with five and seven quarks for improved control over excited state systematics in nucleon structure calculations. The study establishes a foundation for future computations of nucleon form factors and scattering phase shifts using the GEVP eigenbasis, and the automated contraction algorithm enables systematic treatment of multi-pion states in baryonic correlation functions.
