# On momentum dependence of the reaction $π^- p \to ωn$ near threshold

> We discuss the near-threshold behavior of the $ω$ production amplitude in the reaction $π^-p \to ωn$. In contrast to the results of earlier analyses we find that the averaged squared matrix element of the production amplitude must be a decreasing function of energy in order to describe the existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The near-threshold behavior of the reaction π⁻p → ωn is actually normal, contrary to earlier claims of an anomalous P*² dependence; the averaged squared matrix element must be a decreasing function of energy near threshold, not an increasing one, once the experimental integration over beam energy is treated with correct variable dependence.

## Method

The authors re-derive the differential cross section for production of an unstable particle (the ω meson) by convolving the stable-particle cross section with a Breit-Wigner spectral density, then integrating over a small momentum bin ΔP around a fixed outgoing neutron momentum P*. They compare this to the procedure used in earlier experiments, where the beam energy was integrated while keeping P* fixed, and show that the earlier analyses incorrectly treated the energy-conserving delta function, leading to an erroneous P* dependence. The correct expression yields a cross section proportional to P*² + (ΔP)²/12 for small P*, not simply P*.

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum where the cross section behavior transitions from P*² to linear is P*_cr = μΓ / 2ΔP ≈ 90 MeV/c, for ΔP ≈ 20 MeV/c as specified in Key (year).
- For the η meson, P*_cr = 0.01 MeV; for the η′ meson, P*_cr = 2.4 MeV, explaining why those mesons showed normal behavior in Bin (year).
- The data points for P* ≤ 110 MeV/c show a matrix element that is practically constant; above 110 MeV/c the matrix element decreases smoothly with P*.
- The point at P* = 30 MeV/c from Key (year) was averaged over ΔP = 20 MeV/c.

## Context

This work corrects the interpretation of experimental data from Bin (year), Key (year), and Kar (year), who claimed an anomalous P*² dependence of the π⁻p → ωn cross section near threshold and interpreted it as possible evidence for a resonance in the ωN system. The authors show that the same data are consistent with a constant or smoothly decreasing matrix element when the integration limits are treated properly. The corrected πN → ωN transition amplitude is an important input for theoretical analyses of reactions such as pp → ppω (Wilkin, year), pn → dω (Kondratyuk, year), and dp → ³Heω, as well as ω production in proton–nucleus collisions (Sibirtsev, year).
