# Neutron-proton charge-exchange amplitudes at 585 MeV

> The differential cross section and deuteron analysing powers of the dp -> {pp}n charge-exchange reaction have been measured with the ANKE spectrometer at the COSY storage ring. Using a deuteron beam of energy 1170 MeV, data were obtained for small momentum transfers to a {pp} system with low excitation energy. A good quantitative understanding of all the measured observables is provided by the impulse approximation using known neutron-proton amplitudes. The proof of principle achieved here for the method suggests that measurements at higher energies will provide useful information in regions where the existing np database is far less reliable.

## Key claim

The differential cross section and deuteron analysing powers of the \(dp \to \{pp\}n\) charge-exchange reaction at a deuteron beam energy of 1170 MeV (585 MeV per nucleon) are well described by the impulse approximation using known neutron-proton amplitudes, but only for small momentum transfers to a diproton system with low excitation energy. This serves as a proof of principle that the method can be extended to higher energies where the existing neutron-proton database is less reliable.

## Method

The experiment used the ANKE magnetic spectrometer at the COSY storage ring with a polarised deuteron beam of 1170 MeV incident on a hydrogen cluster-jet target. The two fast protons from the charge-exchange reaction were detected in coincidence using the Forward Detector system, and the luminosity was determined by normalising to the quasi-free \(dp \to p_{\rm sp} d\pi^0\) reaction, which has similar acceptance. The differential cross section and deuteron tensor analysing powers \(t_{20}\) and \(t_{22}\) were extracted as functions of momentum transfer \(q\) and diproton excitation energy \(E_{pp}\), and compared to impulse approximation calculations using the Paris deuteron wave function and known neutron-proton amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity for the unpolarised mode: \(\mathcal{L} = (12.5 \pm 0.5)~\text{nb}^{-1}\), determined from quasi-free \(np \to d\pi^0\) production with an estimated accuracy better than 4%.
- Ratio of luminosities from elastic \(dp \to dp\) and quasi-free \(np \to d\pi^0\) methods: \(\mathcal{L}(dp \to dp) / \mathcal{L}(np \to d\pi^0) = 0.80 \pm 0.01\) (statistical only), with about 5% of the 20% discrepancy attributable to shadowing corrections.
- Missing mass of the neutron from \(dp \to \{pp\}n\): \(M_X = (940.4 \pm 0.2)~\text{MeV}/c^2\) with width \(\sigma = 13~\text{MeV}/c^2\), sitting on a 2% background.
- Unpolarised differential cross section for \(dp \to \{pp\}n\) integrated over \(q < 100~\text{MeV}/c\) as a function of \(E_{pp}\) shows good agreement with impulse approximation predictions; for \(E_{pp} < 3~\text{MeV}\) the cross section variation with \(q\) is described out to \(q = 140~\text{MeV}/c\) with a global systematic uncertainty of about 6%.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Bugg and Wilkin (1987) and the refined calculations of Carbonell (2004) for the \(dp \to \{pp\}n\) reaction within the impulse approximation, providing the first combined measurement of the differential cross section and deuteron analysing powers at 585 MeV per nucleon. The results confirm that the method can reliably extract neutron-proton charge-exchange amplitudes in a region where the neutron-proton database is well known, enabling future measurements at higher COSY energies (up to 1.15 GeV per nucleon) where the neutron-proton database is far less reliable, and where spin-correlation studies with polarised beam and target are planned.
