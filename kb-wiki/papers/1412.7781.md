# Effects of quark chemical equilibration on thermal photon elliptic flow

> Large hadronic elliptic flow $v_2$ is considered as an evidence for the existence of a strongly-coupled QGP fluid in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. On the other hand, direct photon $v_2$ has recently been found to be much larger than hydrodynamic estimations, which is recognized as "photon $v_2$ puzzle". In this study, I discuss the implication of late production of quarks in an initially gluon-rich medium because photons are coupled to quarks. Numerical analyses imply that thermal photon $v_2$ can be visibly enhanced. This indicates that interplay of equilibration processes and collective expansion would be important.

# Effects of quark chemical equilibration on thermal photon elliptic flow

## Key claim
Slow quark chemical equilibration in an initially gluon-rich QGP medium can visibly enhance thermal photon elliptic flow $v_2$, because photons couple to quarks but not to gluons, so early-time photon emission is suppressed and the contribution of late-time photons with larger anisotropy is effectively enhanced. This mechanism may partially explain the "photon $v_2$ puzzle" where direct photon $v_2$ exceeds hydrodynamic predictions, but the author notes this is one of several possible contributing factors and that overestimation could arise from the assumption of vanishing initial quark number.

## Method
A (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic model is coupled to rate equations for quark and gluon number densities in the QGP phase, considering the processes $g \rightleftharpoons g+g$, $g \rightleftharpoons q+\bar{q}$, and $q(\bar{q}) \rightleftharpoons q(\bar{q})+g$. The QGP photon emission rate is modified by scaling the phase-space distributions by $n_q/n_q^\mathrm{eq}$ or $n_g/n_g^\mathrm{eq}$, while hadronic photon emission is assumed chemically equilibrated; the two are smoothly connected at $T_c = 0.17$ GeV. Initial conditions use a Glauber model for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with impact parameter $b = 7$ fm, assuming zero initial quark energy density and number density.

## Key numbers
- Thermal photon $v_2(p_T)$ becomes visibly larger for slower quark production, with the quark chemical reaction rate parameter varied as $c_b = 0.2$, $0.5$, and $2.0$ (with $c_a = c_c = 1.5$ fixed).
- Estimated chemical relaxation times for the chosen parameters are $\tau_\mathrm{ch} \sim 1/c_b T \sim 2.0$, $0.5$, and $0.2$ fm/$c$ for the average temperature $T \sim 0.2$ GeV.
- The crossover temperature between QGP and hadronic phases is set to $T_c = 0.17$ GeV with width $\Delta T_c = 0.017$ GeV.
- Initial hydrodynamic time is $\tau_0 = 0.4$ fm/$c$.
- The gluon splitting/merging and gluon emission rates are varied as $c_a = c_c = 0.0$, $1.5$, and $3.0$ for fixed $c_b = 0.5$, showing weaker dependence on these rates than on $c_b$.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions by incorporating chemical non-equilibrium, motivated by color glass condensate theory (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) which indicates the medium can be initially gluon-rich. It addresses the "photon $v_2$ puzzle" first observed by the PHENIX collaboration (Adare, 2011) and ALICE collaboration (Lohner, 2012), where direct photon elliptic flow exceeds predictions from standard hydrodynamic models that assume simultaneous thermal and chemical equilibration. The results imply that chemical composition and equilibration processes are important for understanding heavy-ion collision observables, and future work should constrain chemical equilibration rates from microscopic theory and include prompt photon contributions.
