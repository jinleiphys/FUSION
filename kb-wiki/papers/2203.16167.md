# Quantum Monte Carlo in Configuration Space with Three-Nucleon Forces

> Neutron matter, through its connection to neutron stars as well as systems like cold atom gases, is one of the most interesting yet computationally accessible systems in nuclear physics. The Configuration-Interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) method is a stochastic many-body technique allowing to tackle strongly coupled systems. In contrast to other Quantum Monte Carlo methods employed in nuclear physics, the CIMC method can be formulated directly in momentum space allowing for an efficient use of non-local interactions. In this work we extend CIMC method to include three-nucleon interactions through the normal-ordered two-body approximation. We present results for the equation of state of neutron matter in line with other many-body calculations that employ low resolution chiral interactions, and provide predictions for the momentum distribution and the static structure factor.

## Key claim

The Configuration-Interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) method is extended to include three-nucleon forces through the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation, enabling stochastic ground-state calculations of neutron matter directly in momentum space. The results are presented as an upper bound to the ground-state energy, with the sign problem found to be very mild for this system, allowing converged results at densities up to twice nuclear saturation with at most a 50 keV/N correction from extrapolation.

## Method

The CIMC method projects the ground state by iteratively applying the imaginary-time propagator exp(-tau(H - E_T)) to an initial state, using a stochastic walk in the space of Slater determinants built from momentum eigenstates. Three-nucleon forces are included via the NO2B approximation, where the three-body Hamiltonian is normal-ordered with respect to the Hartree-Fock reference and the residual three-body term is discarded. The guiding wave function is obtained from second-order Møller-Plesset perturbation theory (MP2) amplitudes, and calculations are performed in a periodic cubic box with 66 neutrons using the NNLO_opt chiral interaction plus a next-to-next-to-leading-order three-nucleon force with local regulators at Lambda = 500 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Energy per particle at rho = 0.32 fm^{-3}: the linear extrapolation over the auxiliary Hamiltonian parameter gamma yields at most a correction of 50 keV/N.
- Depletion of the momentum distribution at zero momentum: between 1% and 2%, independent of density up to saturation density.
- Static structure factor S(q) at nuclear saturation density (rho = 0.16 fm^{-3}): interaction effects are of order approximately 1% relative to the free Fermi gas result.
- The momentum-space cutoff for convergence: k^2_max = 24 in squared units of the momentum-space lattice.

## Context

This work extends the CIMC method, previously limited to two-body forces (Roggero 2014, Roggero 2015), to include three-nucleon interactions via the NO2B approximation, a strategy validated for neutron matter by Hagen (2014) and Drischler (2017). The results for the equation of state agree with coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) and CCD with perturbative triples (CCD(T)) calculations using the same NNLO_opt interaction (Hagen 2014), and fall within the uncertainty bands of fourth-order many-body perturbation theory calculations with various chiral forces (Drischler 2017). The momentum-space formulation enables direct computation of the momentum distribution and static structure factor, quantities relevant for neutrino scattering in neutron stars, and sets the stage for future comparisons between local and non-local regulators in chiral effective field theory.
