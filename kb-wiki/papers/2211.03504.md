# Proton structure functions at NLO in the dipole picture with massive quarks

> We predict heavy quark production cross sections in Deep Inelastic Scattering at high energy by applying the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. We demonstrate that when the calculation is performed consistently at next-to-leading order accuracy with massive quarks it becomes possible, for the first time in the dipole picture with perturbatively calculated center-of-mass energy evolution, to simultaneously describe both light and heavy quark production data at small $x_\mathrm{Bj}$. We furthermore show how the heavy quark cross section data provides additional strong constraints on the extracted non-perturbative initial condition for the small-$x_\mathrm{Bj}$ evolution equations.

## Key claim
For the first time in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with perturbatively calculated center-of-mass energy evolution, a simultaneous description of both light and heavy quark production data at small Bjorken-x is achieved, but only when the calculation is performed consistently at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy with massive quarks and only for a small subset of the previously fitted initial conditions.

## Method
The authors compute heavy quark production cross sections in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at NLO in the dipole picture, using massive quark impact factors from light-cone perturbation theory. The energy dependence is obtained by solving the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) small-x evolution equation with initial conditions previously fitted to HERA total cross section data in Beuf (2020), using three different resummation schemes (ResumBK, KCBK, TBK). The predicted charm and bottom reduced cross sections are compared to HERA data to identify which of the 12 initial-condition fits are compatible with heavy quark production.

- Chi-squared per data point for bottom: 1.12, 1.37, and 1.58 for the three compatible fits (Table 1).
- The analysis covers the kinematic region x_Bj < 0.01 and 2.5 GeV^2 ≤ Q^2 < 50 GeV^2 for charm production.
- For the total longitudinal structure function at EIC kinematics, predictions from different fits differ by up to 20% at all Q^2.

## Context
This work extends the NLO CGC framework by Beuf (2020), who fitted the BK evolution initial condition to total DIS cross section data using only massless quarks. Previous LO analyses by Albacete (2010) could not simultaneously describe inclusive and heavy quark data without introducing additional parameters, and the approximative NLO evolution used by Ducloue (2019) with LO impact factors also failed to achieve simultaneous description. The results enable future global analyses of all small-x structure function data at NLO, including at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and identify the three specific fits from Beuf (2020) that should be used in all NLO CGC phenomenology.
