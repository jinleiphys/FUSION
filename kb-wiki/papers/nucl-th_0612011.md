# Wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei

> We discuss some characteristic features of the wobbling motion excited on the triaxial superdeformed Lu nucleus. We show how these features are connected to the moments of inertia microscopically calculated by means of the quasiparticle RPA in the rotating frame.

## Key claim
The wobbling motion observed in triaxial superdeformed (TSD) nuclei like \(^{163}\)Lu exists because the alignment contribution from the last odd quasiparticle to the moment of inertia, superimposed on the collective irrotational-like contribution, makes \(\mathcal{J}_x > \mathcal{J}_y^\mathrm{(eff)}\); this condition is necessary for a real wobbling frequency, and the paper shows that the sign of the triaxial parameter \(\gamma\) alone does not determine the ordering of moments of inertia.

## Method
The authors used the cranking model plus random phase approximation (RPA) in the rotating frame, with a Nilsson plus BCS Hamiltonian and residual pairing plus quadrupole interaction. They solved the nonlinear dispersion relation for the wobbling frequency \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{wob}\) that involves dynamical moments of inertia \(\mathcal{J}_{y,z}^\mathrm{(eff)}(\omega_\mathrm{wob})\) determined simultaneously with the frequency. Calculations were performed for \(^{163}\)Lu with \(\epsilon_2 = 0.43\), \(\gamma = +20^\circ\), and pairing gaps \(\Delta_n = \Delta_p = 0.3\) MeV.

## Key numbers
- The calculation adopted \(\epsilon_2 = 0.43\), \(\gamma = +20^\circ\), and \(\Delta_n = \Delta_p = 0.3\) MeV for \(^{163}\)Lu.
- The proton \(BC\) crossing occurs at around \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} \geq 0.55\) MeV in the calculation.
- The calculated interband \(E2\) transition ratios \(B(E2)_\mathrm{out}/B(E2)_\mathrm{in}\) are a factor 2–3 smaller than experimental values when using \(\gamma = +20^\circ\) of the Nilsson potential.
- The two-phonon wobbling excitation (TSD3) and one-phonon wobbling (TSD2) excitation energies relative to the yrast TSD band (TSD1) in \(^{163}\)Lu were compared with data from Ref. [2phonon], showing strong anharmonicity.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei, first predicted by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Landau and Lifshitz (classical mechanics). It addresses the puzzle that the irrotational model suggests \(\gamma \simeq -20^\circ\) for the correct ordering of moments of inertia, while potential energy surface calculations by Ragnarsson (year not given) favor \(\gamma \simeq +20^\circ\). The paper shows that the odd quasiparticle in \(^{163}\)Lu resolves this discrepancy and enables the description of the one-phonon wobbling band first reported in \(^{163}\)Lu by the experimental group (2001). The results also enable future studies of anharmonicity in two-phonon wobbling excitations and possible phase transitions to tilted axis rotation.
