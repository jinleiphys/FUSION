# Shell model method for Gamow-Teller Transitions in heavy, deformed nuclei

> A method for calculation of Gamow-Teller transition rates is developed by using the concept of the Projected Shell Model (PSM). The shell model basis is constructed by superimposing angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle configurations, and nuclear wave functions are obtained by digonalizing the two-body interactions in these projected states. Calculation of transition matrix elements in the PSM framework is discussed in detail, and the effects caused by the Gamow-Teller residual forces and by configuration-mixing are studied. With this method, it may become possible to perform a state-by-state calculation for beta-decay and electron-capture rates in heavy, deformed nuclei at finite temperatures. Our first example indicates that, while experimentally known Gamow-Teller transition rates from the ground state of the parent nucleus are reproduced, stronger transitions from some low-lying excited states are predicted to occur, which may considerably enhance the total decay rates once these nuclei are exposed to hot stellar environments.

## Key claim

The Projected Shell Model (PSM) can reproduce experimentally known Gamow-Teller transition rates from the ground state of heavy, deformed parent nuclei, while also predicting stronger transitions from some low-lying excited states that may considerably enhance total decay rates in hot stellar environments.

## Method

The authors construct a shell model basis by superimposing angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle configurations from a deformed Nilsson-BCS calculation, then diagonalize a two-body Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, quadrupole-pairing, and Gamow-Teller separable forces in this projected space. Transition matrix elements for the Gamow-Teller operator are evaluated between different quasiparticle sets representing parent and daughter nuclei, using the Thouless theorem and HFB transformation techniques to compute the required rotated matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For the Ikeda sum-rule test in 164Dy (N=98, Z=66): S(GT-) = 97.4957 and S(GT+) = 4.1717, exhausting 97.2% of the sum-rule value 3(N-Z)
- With equal neutron and proton harmonic-oscillator frequencies: S(GT-) = 97.7118 and S(GT+) = 1.7797, approaching 99.9% of the sum-rule value
- Gamow-Teller interaction strengths adopted: chi_GT = 23/A = 0.14 MeV and kappa_GT = 7.5/A = 0.046 MeV for A=164
- Monopole-pairing coupling constants: G1 = 20.12 MeV and G2 = 13.13 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation parameter: epsilon_2 = 0.28
- The model space includes N = 3, 4, 5, 6 harmonic-oscillator shells for both neutrons and protons, yielding 1718 total 1+ states

## Context

This work extends the Projected Shell Model (PSM) framework, previously developed for structure calculations, to Gamow-Teller transitions in heavy deformed nuclei beyond the mass-60 region where full spherical shell model diagonalization becomes intractable. The method builds on earlier PSM developments (Sheikh 1999, Chen 2001, Sun 2002, Sun 2003, Gao 2006) and uses the Gamow-Teller separable interaction form from Kuzmin and Soloviev (1988). This enables state-by-state beta-decay and electron-capture rate calculations for astrophysical applications, particularly for nuclei in hot stellar environments where thermal population of excited states becomes important.
