# Nuclear Reaction Network for Primordial Nucleosynthesis: a detailed analysis of rates, uncertainties and light nuclei yields

> We analyze in details the standard Primordial Nucleosynthesis scenario. In particular we discuss the key theoretical issues which are involved in a detailed prediction of light nuclide abundances, as the weak reaction rates, neutrino decoupling and nuclear rate modeling. We also perform a new analysis of available data on the main nuclear processes entering the nucleosynthesis reaction network, with particular stress on their uncertainties as well as on their role in determining the corresponding uncertainties on light nuclide theoretical estimates. The current status of theoretical versus experimental results for 2H, 3He, 4He and 7Li is then discussed using the determination of the baryon density as obtained from Cosmic Microwave Background anisotropies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a detailed reanalysis of the standard Primordial Nucleosynthesis (BBN) reaction network, quantifying how uncertainties in nuclear reaction rates propagate into theoretical predictions for light nuclide abundances, under the condition that the baryon density is fixed by CMB measurements (WMAP + CBI + ACBAR + 2dFGRS) at ω_b = 0.023 ± 0.001.

## Method

The authors developed a FORTRAN BBN code modifying the public code of Kawano (1992) to consistently include neutrino decoupling via numerical solution of the Boltzmann equations (with QED radiative corrections), weak reaction rates for n ↔ p evaluated up to order α QED with finite nucleon mass and thermal effects, and a new data reduction for all leading nuclear reaction rates in the BBN network. The nuclear rate analysis combined available experimental data using a defined protocol to obtain best estimates and uncertainties, which were then propagated through the reaction network to compute final abundance predictions and their error budgets.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density parameter used: ω_b ≡ Ω_b h² = 0.023 ± 0.001, from WMAP combined with CBI, ACBAR, and 2dFGRS
- Asymptotic photon-to-neutrino temperature ratio after full numerical treatment: T/T_ν = 1.3984, compared to the instantaneous decoupling value of 1.4010
- Electron neutrino distortion coefficients (asymptotic values, in units of 10⁻⁴): c₀^e = -2.507, c₁^e = -2.731, c₂^e = 6.010, c₃^e = -0.1419
- Muon/tau neutrino distortion coefficients (asymptotic values, in units of 10⁻⁴): c₀^x = -2.003, c₁^x = -2.196, c₂^x = 3.061, c₃^x = -0.1091
- Theoretical uncertainty on ⁴He mass fraction from weak rates: of order 0.1%, dominated by experimental uncertainty on neutron lifetime
- QED finite-temperature corrections to ⁴He mass fraction: affect Y_p at the level of 0.0001

## Context

This work extends the earlier nuclear rate compilations of Fowler and Hoyle (1964), Caughlan and Fowler (1988), Krauss and Romanelli (1990), and Smith, Kawano, and Malaney (1993), and builds upon the NACRE compilation (1999) by focusing specifically on BBN-relevant reactions with updated experimental data (e.g., LUNA results on ²H + p → γ + ³He from Casella et al., 2002). The analysis enables parameter-free consistency tests of standard BBN using the independently measured baryon density from CMB, and provides a framework for constraining exotic physics by comparing theoretical predictions with observed light element abundances. Similar contemporaneous analyses were performed by Cyburt et al. (2004), Cuoco et al. (2004), and Descouvemont et al. (2004).
