# Electron recombination with multicharged ions via chaotic many-electron states

> We show that a dense spectrum of chaotic multiply-excited eigenstates can play a major role in collision processes involving many-electron multicharged ions. A statistical theory based on chaotic properties of the eigenstates enables one to obtain relevant energy-averaged cross sections in terms of sums over single-electron orbitals. Our calculation of the low-energy electron recombination of Au$^{25+}$ shows that the resonant process is 200 times more intense than direct radiative recombination, which explains the recent experimental results of Hoffknecht {\em et al.} [J. Phys. B {\bf 31}, 2415 (1998)].

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed 200-fold enhancement of low-energy electron recombination with Au$^{25+}$ over direct radiative recombination is caused by capture into dense, chaotic multiply-excited states of Au$^{24+}$, rather than by conventional dielectronic resonances. The authors qualify that this mechanism applies specifically to open-shell many-electron ions where the single-particle spectrum lacks large gaps, enabling strong configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors developed a statistical theory based on many-body quantum chaos, where multiply-excited eigenstates of the compound ion Au$^{24+}$ are treated as random superpositions of basis states with a Breit-Wigner spreading width. They computed the energy-averaged capture cross section by summing over two-electron doorway states using reduced Coulomb matrix elements between Dirac-Fock orbitals, and estimated the radiative width from single-particle dipole transitions. The calculation was performed for Au$^{25+}$ at an electron energy of 0.5 eV, summing over orbitals and electron partial waves up to $h_{11/2}$.

## Key numbers

- The resonant recombination cross section for Au$^{25+}$ at $\varepsilon = 0.5$ eV is $\sigma_r \approx 23 \times 10^{-16}$ cm$^2$, compared to the direct radiative recombination cross section $\sigma_d = 0.12 \times 10^{-16}$ cm$^2$, giving a factor of 200 enhancement.
- The experimental value from Hoffknecht (1998) is $\sigma^{\rm (exp)} = 27 \times 10^{-16}$ cm$^2$.
- The level density of multiply-excited states near the ionization threshold ($I \approx 27.5$ a.u.) is $\rho_{J^\pi} \approx 3.6 \times 10^4$ a.u., corresponding to a spacing $D = 1/\rho_{J^\pi} \sim 1$ meV.
- The spreading width is $\Gamma_{\rm spr} \approx 0.5$ a.u., giving $N \sim 2 \times 10^4$ principal components per eigenstate.
- The average autoionisation width is $\Gamma^{(a)} \sim 5 \times 10^{-7}$ a.u., while the radiative width is $\Gamma^{(r)} = 3 \times 10^{-5}$ a.u., so $\Gamma^{(r)} \gg \Gamma^{(a)}$ and the fluorescence yield $\omega_f \approx 1$.

## Context

This work extends the statistical treatment of chaotic many-body states previously applied to nuclei (Zel) and complex atoms (Ce) to the problem of electron-ion recombination. It directly addresses the puzzle of enhanced low-energy recombination observed in Au$^{25+}$ by Hoffknecht (1998) and similar anomalies in U$^{28+}$ (Uwira, 1996) and Au$^{51+}$ (Uwira, 1997), where conventional dielectronic recombination theory (Burgess, 1964) failed to reproduce the rates. The theory enables calculation of energy-averaged cross sections for processes mediated by chaotic multiply-excited states, and the authors suggest it could be applied to charge transfer in collisions of multiply-charged ions with neutral atoms.
