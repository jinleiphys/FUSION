# Configuration Mixing in Relativistic Quark Models

> Starting from the standard and commonly used relativistic nucleon wave function, we show that residual interactions based on gluon or Goldstone boson exchanges generate additional components leading to configuration mixing. This result suggests that realistic nucleon wave functions can be expected to be far more complex than seemingly successful form factor fits in relativistic quark models would have one believe.

## Key claim

The paper shows that residual interactions based on gluon or Goldstone boson exchanges, when applied to the standard relativistic nucleon wave function, generate additional components that lead to configuration mixing, suggesting that realistic nucleon wave functions are far more complex than the seemingly successful form factor fits in relativistic quark models would indicate.

## Method

The authors start from the standard relativistic nucleon wave function in the light front formalism, which contains only positive-energy Fock components (the Pauli-Melosh basis). They then evaluate the spin-isospin matrix elements that appear in the three-body equation of motion, incorporating Goldstone boson exchange (pion exchange) and gluon exchange as residual interactions between quarks. Using light front perturbation theory and Fierz transformations, they calculate the resulting spin invariants and perform angular integrations over the transverse momentum to determine which new nucleon spin components are generated.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration scale is given as \(\Lambda_{\chi}=4\pi f_{\pi}\sim 1.17\) GeV, with the pion decay constant \(f_{\pi}=0.093\) GeV.
- The axial vector-quark coupling constant \(g_A\) is taken to be one in the effective interaction Lagrangian.
- The neutral pion exchange interaction \(v_5(12)\) acting on the nucleon spin invariants \(G_2\) and \(G_6\) generates transitions to the nucleon spin invariants \(G_3\), \(G_5\), and \(G_8\).
- The charged pion exchange interaction generates additional spin invariants including \(G_1\), \(G_3\), \(G_6\), \(G_7\), and \(G_8\), with radial integrals \(r_{\pm}\) and \(R_{\pm}\) defined as \(\pi \int dk^{2}_{\perp} k^2 (M_0 R_0)_0 [\dots]\) and \(\pi \int dk^{2}_{\perp} k^2 (R_0)_0 [\dots]\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model (NQM) studies of configuration mixing from gluon and Goldstone boson exchange interactions (as used by K.I. and Plessas and collaborators) into the relativistic light front framework, where previously only ground state wave functions without configuration mixing had been used. The results enable future coupled-channel calculations of the nucleon wave function that include N* spin invariants (such as the negative parity N*(1535) \(S_{11}\) configuration) arising from the entanglement of orbital angular momentum with the three-quark spin structure, and suggest that the Pauli-Melosh basis is too limited for realistic nucleon descriptions.
