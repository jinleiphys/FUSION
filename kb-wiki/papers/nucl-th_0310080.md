# The mass of charmonium in nuclear matter

> The masses of charmonium states immersed in nuclear matter are calculated in LO QCD and in QCD sum rules. While the mass shift for $J/ψ$ are found to be less than -10 MeV, those for the $χ_{0,1,2}$ and $ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$ are found to be more than -40 MeV. We investigate the feasibility of observing such mass shifts in the future accelerator project at GSI.

## Key claim

The mass shifts of charmonium states in nuclear matter are calculated using leading-order QCD and QCD sum rules, with the condition that the shifts for excited states (χ₀,₁,₂, ψ(3686), ψ(3770)) exceed -40 MeV while the J/ψ shift is less than -10 MeV, and these shifts are predicted to be observable at the future GSI antiproton facility.

## Method

The mass shifts are computed via the QCD second-order Stark effect, where the forward scattering matrix element of a charmonium state with a nucleon is expanded in an operator product expansion (OPE) at leading order, with the dominant contribution coming from the color electric field condensate ⟨(αₛ/π)E²⟩. The wave functions of the charmonium states are taken as Gaussian with oscillator constants β determined by their squared radii from potential models (0.47², 0.74², 0.96², and 1 fm² for J/ψ, χ₀,₁,₂, ψ(3686), and ψ(3770 respectively), and the QCD parameters αₛ=0.84 and m_c=1.95 GeV are fixed by the energy splitting between J/ψ and ψ(3686) in free space. The mass shift formula is evaluated at normal nuclear matter density using the linear density approximation and nucleon expectation values of the gluon condensate.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass shift: -8 MeV from LO QCD (second-order Stark effect), -7 MeV from QCD sum rules
- χ₀,₁,₂ mass shift: -40 MeV from LO QCD, -60 MeV from QCD sum rules
- ψ(3686) mass shift: -100 MeV from LO QCD
- ψ(3770) mass shift: -140 MeV from LO QCD
- η_c mass shift: -8 MeV from LO QCD, -5 MeV from QCD sum rules
- Gluon condensate in vacuum: ⟨(αₛ/π)F²⟩ ∼ 1.5 GeV/fm³
- Quark condensate in vacuum: ⟨q̄q⟩ ∼ 2 fm⁻³
- Gluon condensate decreases by 6% at normal nuclear matter density; quark condensate decreases by 30%
- Nucleon expectation value of color electric field: ⟨(αₛ/π)E²⟩_N ∼ 0.5 GeV²
- Required antiproton momentum to create J/ψ: 4.1 GeV/c; for ψ(3770): 6.5 GeV/c
- Expected event rates at GSI (luminosity 2×10³² cm⁻² s⁻¹): J/ψ → e⁺e⁻: 100 events/day; χ_c2 → J/ψ+γ: 5950 events/day

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules analysis for J/ψ in nuclear matter by Klingl et al. (1999) and Kim and Lee (2001), and the leading-order perturbative QCD calculation by Peskin (1979) and Luke et al. (1992), to excited charmonium states (χ₀,₁,₂, ψ(3686), ψ(3770)) using realistic wave functions from potential models. The results are consistent with other non-perturbative QCD studies such as the effective potential model by Brodsky et al. (1990), Wasson (1991), and Navarra et al. (1995). The paper enables experimental searches for in-medium charmonium mass shifts at the future GSI antiproton facility, where the predicted cross sections (0.6–350 pb) and event rates (10–5950 per day) suggest observability.
