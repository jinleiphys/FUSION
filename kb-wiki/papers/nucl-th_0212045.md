# Breakup of the weakly bound 17F nucleus

> The breakup of the radioactive 17F nucleus into a proton and 16O is studied for the reaction 17F + 208Pb --> p + 16O + 208Pb at 65 MeV/nucleon. The possibility of using this reaction as a test case for studying dynamical Coulomb reacceleration effects is assessed. It is shown that the reaction is dominated by elastic nuclear breakup (diffraction dissociation).

## Key claim

The exclusive breakup of 17F into p + 16O on a 208Pb target at 65 MeV/nucleon is dominated by elastic nuclear breakup (diffraction dissociation), not by Coulomb breakup, making the reaction unsuitable as a clean test case for studying dynamical Coulomb reacceleration effects despite earlier suggestions in the literature.

## Method

The authors performed first-order perturbation theory calculations for both Coulomb and elastic nuclear breakup of 17F, treating the projectile as a proton bound to an inert 16O core. The Coulomb breakup was computed using the virtual photon method with eikonal phases from an M3Y-derived optical potential, while the nuclear breakup was calculated in the DWBA framework with eikonal approximation for the center-of-mass scattering. The bound and continuum wavefunctions were obtained from a single-particle potential model fitted to reproduce the known separation energies (0.6 MeV for the ground state, 0.1 MeV for the first excited state) and the astrophysical S-factor data for 16O(p,γ)17F.

## Key numbers

- Total Coulomb breakup cross section for ground-state to continuum transitions: σ_E1 = 0.88 mb, σ_E2 = 0.054 mb, σ_total = 0.94 mb
- Coulomb excitation cross section to the 1/2+ excited state: 0.33 mb
- Nuclear breakup cross sections are at least two orders of magnitude larger than the Coulomb cross sections
- Photo-absorption cross section integrated over the line for the 5/2+ → 1/2+ bound-state transition: ∫σ_γ dE_x = 7×10⁻⁶ mb MeV
- B(E2) value for the 1/2+ → 5/2+ down transition: 61.5 e² fm⁴ (compared to experimental value 66.4 ± 1.4 e² fm⁴)
- E2 photo-absorption cross sections are at least a factor of 10³ smaller than E1 cross sections

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of 17F breakup initiated by Esbensen and Bertulani (2002) and is motivated by the need to test dynamical Coulomb reacceleration effects that complicate the Coulomb dissociation method used for astrophysical capture reactions such as 7Be(p,γ)8B (studied by Motobayashi (1994), Kikuchi (1998), Iwasa (1999), and others). The authors find that unlike the 8B case, where Coulomb breakup is substantial, the 17F breakup is dominated by nuclear dissociation, making it impractical for isolating Coulomb reacceleration effects; this suggests that alternative systems or experimental strategies are needed for such tests.
