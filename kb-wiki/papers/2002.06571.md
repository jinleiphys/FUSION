# Neutron star matter equation of state including $d^*$-hexaquark degrees of freedom

> We present an extension of a previous work where, assuming a simple free bosonic gas supplemented with a relativistic meand field model to describe the pure nucleonic part of the EoS, we studied the consequences that the first non-trivial hexaquark $d^*$(2380) could have on the properties of neutron stars. Compared to that exploratory work we employ a standard non-linear Walecka model including additional terms that describe the interaction of the $d^*(2380)$ di-baryon with the other particles of the system through the exchange of $σ$- and $ω$-meson fields. Our results have show that the presence of the $d^*(2380)$ leads to maximum masses compatible with the recent observations of $\sim 2$M$_\odot$ millisecond pulsars if the interaction of the $d^*(2380)$ is slightly repulsive or the $d^*(2380)$ does not interacts at all. An attractive interaction makes the equation of state too soft to be able to support a $2$M$_\odot$ neutron star whereas an extremely repulsive one induces the collapse of the neutron star into a black hole as soon as the $d^*(2380)$ appears.

## Key claim

The presence of the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark in neutron star matter can produce maximum masses compatible with the observed $\sim 2 M_\odot$ millisecond pulsars, but only if the $d^*(2380)$ interaction with other particles is slightly repulsive or zero; an attractive interaction makes the equation of state too soft to support a $2 M_\odot$ neutron star, while an extremely repulsive one induces collapse into a black hole as soon as the $d^*(2380)$ appears.

## Method

The authors extend a previous free-bosonic-gas model by employing a standard non-linear Walecka model (relativistic mean-field theory) that includes explicit interaction terms for the $d^*(2380)$ di-baryon with the surrounding medium via $\sigma$- and $\omega$-meson exchange. The nucleonic part is described using the GM1 and GM3 parametrizations of the Glendenning–Moszkowski model, and the $\Delta$-isobar couplings are taken in two variants: universal coupling (UC) and stronger coupling (SC). The Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass-radius relation of non-rotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The $d^*(2380)$ onset density ranges from $\sim 2\rho_0$ up to $\sim 7\rho_0$ depending on the choice of dimensionless couplings $x_{\sigma d^*}$ and $x_{\omega d^*}$ (where $\rho_0 \sim 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is the nuclear saturation density).
- The GM1 model with either UC or SC $\Delta$ couplings predicts a maximum mass compatible with the pulsar PSR J0740+6620 mass of $2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot$ (68.3% credibility) only if the $d^*(2380)$ does not interact or feels slight repulsion.
- For the GM1 model, attractive couplings $(x_{\sigma d^*}, x_{\omega d^*}) = (0.1, 0.1), (0.2, 0.2), (0.3, 0.3)$ lead to maximum masses below the $2 M_\odot$ threshold; repulsive couplings $(-0.2, -0.2)$ and $(-0.3, -0.3)$ produce a negative pressure gradient, signaling collapse into a black hole.
- For the GM3 model, the coupling $(-0.3, -0.3)$ also leads to a negative pressure gradient and collapse.

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study of Mantziris et al. (2018) (referred to as Vid18 in the text) which treated the $d^*(2380)$ as a free bosonic gas, by now including its interaction with the nuclear medium via meson exchange. It addresses the same class of puzzles as the "hyperon puzzle" (Vidana, 2018) and the "$\Delta$ puzzle" (Drago et al., 2014; Kolomeitsev et al., 2017), where exotic degrees of freedom soften the equation of state and threaten compatibility with the $\sim 2 M_\odot$ pulsar observations (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013; Cromartie et al., 2019). The results provide first constraints on the sign and strength of the $d^*(2380)$ interaction in dense matter and open the possibility of new cooling mechanisms for neutron stars.
