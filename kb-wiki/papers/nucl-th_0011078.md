# Strange Dibaryons in Neutron Stars and in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The formation of dibaryons with strangeness are discussed for the interior of neutron stars and for central relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We derive limits for the properties of H-dibaryons from pulsar data. Signals for the formation of possible bound states with hyperons at BNL's Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are investigated by studying their weak decay patterns and production rates.

## Key claim

The paper derives constraints on the properties of H-dibaryons from pulsar data, showing that a deeply bound H-dibaryon with an attractive optical potential at normal nuclear density is incompatible with the measured mass of the Hulse-Taylor pulsar, but the existence of such dibaryons is not ruled out for all parameter ranges.

## Method

The author uses an extended Relativistic Mean-Field Model incorporating the H-dibaryon as a quasiparticle with minimal coupling to scalar and vector meson fields, fixing the vector coupling via quark counting rules and varying the scalar coupling to produce optical potentials at normal nuclear density in the range -30 to +30 MeV. For heavy-ion collisions, production rates at RHIC are estimated using a coalescence model, and weak decay patterns of possible dibaryon states are calculated using an SU(3) symmetric effective Lagrangian parameterized to reproduce experimental hyperon decay amplitudes, combined with a Hulthen-type wavefunction for varying binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons are predicted to appear at twice normal nuclear density (2ρ₀) in neutron star matter, a result consistent across multiple models.
- The optical potential of the H-dibaryon at normal nuclear density is varied in the range U_H(ρ₀) = -30 to +30 MeV.
- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar has a measured mass of M = 1.44 M⊙, used as the constraint for maximum neutron star mass.
- Production rates for dibaryons with hyperons at RHIC are estimated in the range of 10⁻² to 10⁻⁴ per single event in the coalescence model.
- The weak decay amplitudes are parameterized with constants D=4.72, F=-1.62, G=40.0, H=47.8, and J=-7.1 (in units of 10⁻¹⁰).
- Decay lengths for all dibaryons studied are in the range cτ = 1–5 cm.
- For a bound ΛΛ state, the nonmesonic decay mode ΛΛ→Σ⁻+p dominates for binding energies of 4 MeV or more.
- For a bound Ξ⁰p state, the decay mode Ξ⁰p→Λp has the largest branching ratio for binding energies of 1.5 MeV or more.

## Context

This work extends earlier speculations about exotic particles in neutron stars, following Cameron (1959) on hyperon appearance, Jaffe (1977) on the H-dibaryon, and the Nijmegen potential predictions of deeply bound two-hyperon states by Stoks (1999). The paper enables experimental searches for strange dibaryons at RHIC through three detection methods: tracking charged track splitting in a TPC, invariant mass reconstruction near threshold, and correlation function analysis of hyperon pairs. The results also connect to studies of hyperonic matter phase transitions and the possibility of neutron star twins with similar masses but different radii, as discussed in the author's related work.
