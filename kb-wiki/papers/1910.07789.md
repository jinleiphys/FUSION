# A variety of clustering in $^{18}$O

> We investigate excited states of $^{18}$O by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. It is found that five different types of cluster states exist which we call ${}^{14}{\rm C}+α$, higher-nodal ${}^{14}{\rm C}+α$, two molecular states, and $4α$ linear-chain. The calculated $α$-decay widths are compared with the observed data. The higher-nodal ${}^{14}{\rm C}+α$ cluster states reasonably agree with resonances reported by the recent experiments. We predict that the $α$-particle emission is dominant for the ${}^{14}{\rm C}+α$ cluster states while the molecular states prefer the $^{6}$He emission.

## Key claim

Using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S interaction, the authors identify five distinct types of cluster configurations in excited states of $^{18}$O, but the calculated excitation energies for the molecular states are overestimated compared to experimental suggestions, and the assignment of the negative-parity ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ inversion doublet bandhead remains controversial.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ using AMD wave functions, followed by angular momentum projection and the generator coordinate method (GCM) with $\beta$ as the generator coordinate. They calculated $\alpha$-decay widths from the reduced width amplitude using the Laplace expansion method with a channel radius of 5.2 fm, and analyzed single-particle orbits of valence neutrons to characterize the configurations.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: calculated $-139.97$ MeV, observed $-139.81$ MeV
- ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ bandhead $0^+_2$ at $E_x = 5.44$ MeV (observed $0^+_2$ at 3.63 MeV)
- Higher-nodal ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ $0^+_4$ state at $E_x = 12.15$ MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 184$ keV (observed $0^+$ at 9.9(1) MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 3200(800)$ keV)
- ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ $6^+_1$ state at $E_x = 16.00$ MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 830$ keV and $\theta^2_\alpha = 0.18$ (observed $6^+_1$ at 11.69 MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 12(1)$ keV and $\theta^2_\alpha = 0.23$)
- Negative-parity ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ bandhead $1^-_4$ at $E_x = 11.41$ MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 575$ keV and $\theta^2_\alpha = 0.23$ (observed $1^-$ at 9.76(2) MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 630(60)$ keV and $\theta^2_\alpha = 0.46$)
- ${}^{12}{\rm C}+\alpha+2n$ molecular $0^+_3$ state at $E_x = 11.34$ MeV with $\Gamma_\alpha = 11.0$ keV
- $4\alpha$ linear-chain $0^+_9$ bandhead at $E_x = 26.50$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations on $^{18}$O by Furutachi et al. (2008) with improved effective interaction and wave functions, and provides the first theoretical investigation of the ${}^{12}{\rm C}+\alpha+2n$ molecular states suggested by von Oertzen et al. (2010). The results are compared with experimental data from Cunsolo et al. (1981), Gai et al. (1983, 1991), Curtis et al. (2002), Avila et al. (2014), and Yang et al. (2019). The calculation enables quantitative comparison of $\alpha$-decay widths and predicts distinct decay patterns: ${}^{14}{\rm C}+\alpha$ states decay by $\alpha$ emission while molecular states prefer $^{6}$He emission, which can guide future experiments to distinguish these configurations.
