# Off-shell effects in the energy dependence of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 astrophysical S factor

> I show that off-shell effects, like antisymmetrization and Be-7 distortions, can significantly influence the energy dependence of the nonresonant Be7(p,gamma)B8 astrophysical S factor at higher energies. The proper treatment of these effects results in a vitrually flat E1 component of the S factor at E_cm = 0.3-1.5 MeV energies in the present eight-body model. The energy dependence of the nonresonant S factor, predicted by the present model, is in agreement with the low-energy direct capture data and the existing high-energy Coulomb dissociation data. Irrespective of whether or not the present energy dependence is correct, off-shell effects can cause 15--20% changes in the value of S(0) extrapolated from high-energy (E_cm > 0.7 MeV) data.

## Key claim

Off-shell effects, primarily antisymmetrization and continuum excited distortions of Be-7, can significantly alter the energy dependence of the nonresonant E1 component of the Be-7(p,gamma)B-8 astrophysical S factor at higher energies, contrary to the common assumption that this energy dependence is well known. The author notes that this agreement with existing data might be fortuitous and could prove incorrect with a larger model space.

## Method

The author used an eight-body resonating-group method (RGM) with a He-4 + He-3 + p three-cluster configuration to describe both the B-8 ground state and the Be-7 + p scattering states. The relative motion functions for the scattering states were determined using the Kohn-Hulthén variational method, while the bound ground state was solved with a variational Siegert method, all using the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction. To isolate the effect of antisymmetrization, the author also computed the S factor by taking the relative motion wave functions from behind the antisymmetrizer and using them as if they came from a potential model.

## Key numbers

- The present eight-body model predicts a virtually flat E1 component of the S factor in the energy range E_cm = 0.3-1.5 MeV.
- Off-shell effects can cause 15-20% changes in the value of S(0) extrapolated from high-energy data (E_cm > 0.7 MeV).
- The zero-energy S factor from the present model is S_17(0) = 25 eVb.
- The six direct capture measurements give S_17(0) between 15 eVb and 40 eVb, with a weighted average of 22.2 +/- 2.3 eVb.
- The Coulomb dissociation experiment by Motobayashi (1994) gives extrapolated S_17(0) = 16.7 +/- 3.2 eVb (s-wave extrapolation) or 15.5 +/- 2.8 eVb (s+d-wave extrapolation).
- The 1+ resonance in Be-7(p,gamma)B-8 is at E_cm = 632 keV.
- The photon energy is given by E_gamma = E_cm + 0.137 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous eight-body model (Csoto 1995) and addresses the long-standing confusion in S_17(0) values, where direct capture measurements by Kavanagh (1969) and Filippone (1983) disagree by 25%. The paper challenges the common belief, established by Christy and Duck (1961) and Tombrello (1965) and later refined by Robertson (1973) and Barker (1980), that the energy dependence of the nonresonant S factor is known. The importance of off-shell effects was recently emphasized for the mirror reaction Li-7(n,gamma)Li-8 by Brown (1996). This work enables a re-evaluation of extrapolation procedures for S_17(0) from high-energy data and suggests that future fully dynamical ab initio eight-body calculations will be needed to resolve the remaining uncertainties.
