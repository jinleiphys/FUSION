# On the Relativistic Separable Functions for the Breakup Reactions

> In the paper the so-called modified Yamaguchi function for the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a separable kernel is discussed. The type of the functions is defined by the analytic stucture of the hadron current with breakup - the reactions with interacting nucleon-nucleon pair in the final state (electro-, photo-, and nucleon-disintegration of the deuteron).

## Key claim

The standard covariant generalization of the Yamaguchi separable function for the Bethe-Salpeter equation becomes non-integrable for certain kinematic regions, specifically when the laboratory kinetic energy of the nucleon-nucleon pair exceeds approximately 0.8 GeV; the authors propose a modified Yamaguchi-type function that avoids these singularities and remains integrable for all energies.

## Method

The authors start from the partial-wave decomposed Bethe-Salpeter equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering with a rank-I separable kernel. They analyze the analytic structure of the loop integral \(h(s)\) that appears in the solution, identifying the pole structure of the standard Yamaguchi-type form factor \(g(p_0,p) = 1/(p_0^2 - p^2 - \beta^2 + i\epsilon)\). To remove the non-integrable singularities that arise at high energies, they replace this form factor with a modified version \(g_{\rm MY}(p_0,p) = 1/((p_0^2 - p^2 - \beta^2)^2 + \alpha^4)\), and specify a contour integration procedure that yields a real contribution from the form factor poles and ensures unitarity.

## Key numbers

* The critical laboratory kinetic energy for the \(np\)-pair above which the standard Yamaguchi function becomes non-integrable is \(T_{lab}^c \simeq 4\beta\), which for the minimal parameter \(\beta_{\min} = 0.2\) GeV gives \(T_{lab}^{\min} = 0.8\) GeV.
* The bound state deuteron mass is given by \(\sqrt{s} = M_d = (2m - \epsilon_D)\), and the minimal \(\beta_{\min} = 0.2\) GeV always satisfies \(\beta_{\min} > \epsilon_D/2\), ensuring integrability for the bound state.
* The modified Yamaguchi-type functions have been used to describe neutron-proton scattering observables for total angular momentum \(J = 0, 3\) in a wide energy range.

## Context

This work extends the separable kernel approach to the Bethe-Salpeter equation previously developed by Bondarenko (2002) and others, which was limited by singularities in the relativistic generalization of the nonrelativistic Yamaguchi-type functions used by Mathelitsch (1981) and Haidenbauer (1984). The modified form factors enable calculations for breakup reactions of the deuteron (electro-, photo-, and nucleon-disintegration) at high energies where the standard functions fail, and allow description of on-shell nucleon-nucleon data up to high energies without meson-production thresholds.
