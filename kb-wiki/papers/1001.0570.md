# Background magnetic field stabilizes QCD string against breaking

> The confinement of quarks in hadrons occurs due to formation of QCD string. At large separation between the quarks the QCD string breaks into pieces due to light quark-antiquark pair creation. We argue that there exist a critical background magnetic field e B ~ 16 m_π^2, above which the string breaking is impossible in the transverse directions with respect to the axis of the magnetic field. Thus, at strong enough magnetic field a new, asymmetrically confining phase may form. The effect - which can potentially be tested at LHC/ALICE experiment - leads to abundance of u-quark rich hadrons and to excess of radially excited mesons in the noncentral heavy-ion collisions compared to the central ones.

## Key claim

There exists a critical background magnetic field above which the QCD string cannot break in the directions transverse to the magnetic field axis, because the magnetic length restricts the separation of created light quark-antiquark pairs and the energy gain from breaking becomes insufficient. The critical fields are estimated at zero temperature assuming the string tension remains at its zero-field value and the dynamical quark masses are unchanged by the magnetic field.

## Method

The author compares the energy of an unbroken QCD string between a static quark-antiquark pair with the energy of a broken string state where a light quark-antiquark pair removes a segment of length equal to the magnetic length. The condition for no string breaking is derived as \(2 m_q(B) \geq \sigma(B) l_q(B)\), using the dynamical quark masses at zero field and the phenomenological string tension. The critical magnetic fields are then computed separately for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks using their respective electric charges and magnetic lengths.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for suppressing string breaking via \(u\bar{u}\) pair creation: \(e B^{(u)}_{\mathrm{cr}} \simeq 8 m_\pi^2\)
- Critical magnetic field for suppressing string breaking via \(d\bar{d}\) pair creation: \(e B^{(d)}_{\mathrm{cr}} = 16 m_\pi^2\)
- Maximum magnetic field at LHC: \(e B^{\mathrm{max}}_{\mathrm{LHC}} \sim 15 m_\pi^2\) (from prior estimates)
- Maximum magnetic field at RHIC: \(e B^{\mathrm{max}}_{\mathrm{RHIC}} \sim m_\pi^2\) (from prior estimates)
- String breaking distance at zero field from lattice QCD: \(R_{\mathrm{br}} \approx 1.13\ \mathrm{fm}\) (with statistical and systematic errors of order \(0.1\ \mathrm{fm}\) each)
- String tension at zero field: \(\sigma(B=0) = (440\ \mathrm{MeV})^2\)
- Dynamical quark mass in QCD vacuum: \(m^{\mathrm{dyn}}_q \simeq 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Zero-temperature crossover scale between confining and deconfining regions: \(e B_{\mathrm{cross}}[T=0] \sim (700\ \mathrm{MeV})^2 \sim 25 m_\pi^2\)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of magnetic field effects on QCD confinement initiated by Miransky and Shovkovy (2002), who first pointed out anisotropic effects of magnetic fields on confining scales. It also relates to thermodynamic arguments by Agasian (2008) suggesting that magnetic fields shift and weaken the confinement-deconfinement phase transition. The prediction can be tested in lattice QCD simulations with dynamical fermions, where magnetic fields of the required strength have already been achieved in quenched simulations by Buividovich et al. (2009). Experimentally, the effect could be observed at LHC/ALICE in noncentral heavy-ion collisions as an abundance of \(u\)-quark rich hadrons and radially excited mesons compared to central collisions.
