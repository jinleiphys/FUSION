# Nuclear structure aspects of spin-independent WIMP scattering off xenon

> We study the structure factors for spin-independent WIMP scattering off xenon based on state-of-the-art large-scale shell-model calculations, which are shown to yield a good spectroscopic description of all experimentally relevant isotopes. Our results are based on the leading scalar one-body currents only. At this level and for the momentum transfers relevant to direct dark matter detection, the structure factors are in very good agreement with the phenomenological Helm form factors used to give experimental limits for WIMP-nucleon cross sections. In contrast to spin-dependent WIMP scattering, the spin-independent channel, at the one-body level, is less sensitive to nuclear structure details. In addition, we explicitly show that the structure factors for inelastic scattering are suppressed by ~ 10^{-4} compared to the coherent elastic scattering response. This implies that the detection of inelastic scattering will be able to discriminate clearly between spin-independent and spin-dependent scattering. Finally, we provide fits for all calculated structure factors.

# Nuclear structure aspects of spin-independent WIMP scattering off xenon

## Key claim
Based on large-scale shell-model calculations using only the leading scalar one-body currents, the spin-independent WIMP scattering structure factors for xenon isotopes agree very well with the phenomenological Helm form factors at momentum transfers relevant to direct dark matter detection, but this agreement holds only at the one-body current level and additional contributions from two-body currents are expected.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for all stable xenon isotopes (128, 129, 130, 131, 132, 134, 136) using an isospin symmetric 100Sn core with a valence space consisting of the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals and the GCN5082 effective nuclear interaction. The structure factors for spin-independent WIMP scattering were computed from the scalar one-body current operator, and fits were provided for all calculated structure factors. For 128Xe and 130Xe, proton and neutron excitations were restricted to three and six, respectively, to keep matrix dimensions tractable.

## Key numbers
- Inelastic spin-independent structure factors are suppressed by approximately 10^{-4} compared to the coherent elastic scattering response for 129Xe and 5 x 10^{-5} for 131Xe at the global maxima
- At the kinematically allowed region for inelastic scattering around u ~ 1, the suppression is about a factor 2 x 10^{-3} for 129Xe and 10^{-4} for 131Xe
- The spin-dependent neutron spin expectation value for 129Xe is 0.329 (Klos et al.) compared to 0.248 (Fitzpatrick et al.)
- The spin-dependent neutron spin expectation value for 131Xe is -0.272 (Klos et al.) compared to -0.199 (Fitzpatrick et al.)
- The first excited state in 129Xe is at 39.6 keV and in 131Xe at 80.2 keV
- Matrix dimensions range from 1500 for 136Xe to 410 x 10^6 for 130Xe

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies on spin-dependent WIMP scattering off xenon (Menendez et al., Klos et al.) and inelastic spin-dependent interactions to the spin-independent channel. The results validate the phenomenological Helm form factors (Lewin and Smith) currently used in experimental analyses by experiments such as XENON and LUX, showing that the extracted limits are consistent in the underlying nuclear structure. The calculations are compared to those of Fitzpatrick et al., which used an older nuclear interaction and more severe truncations, finding good agreement for spin-independent scattering but larger differences for spin-dependent scattering. The work enables improved interpretation of direct dark matter detection experiments and demonstrates that detection of inelastic scattering would clearly discriminate between spin-independent and spin-dependent interactions.
