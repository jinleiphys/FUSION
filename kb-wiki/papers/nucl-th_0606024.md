# Structure properties of even-even actinides

> Structure properties of fifty five even-even actinides have been calculated using the Gogny D1S force and the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach as well as the configuration mixing method. Theoretical results are compared with experimental data.

# Structure properties of even-even actinides

## Key claim

The Gogny D1S force combined with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and configuration mixing methods reproduces a broad range of actinide structure properties, but the authors note that superdeformed states predicted to lie below normal deformed states in certain Fm and No isotopes may not survive as bound states because they are only a few hundred keV below the octupole-unstable outer barrier.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny D1S force were performed for 55 even-even actinides, with the triaxial degree of freedom left free at low elongation and parity broken at large elongation. Positive parity collective states were determined using the Generator Coordinate Method with the Gaussian Overlap Approximation, solving a Bohr Hamiltonian-like equation for the five collective quadrupole coordinates. Partial half-lives for gamma-back decay and fission of shape isomers were computed using the WKB approximation with collective masses from the Adiabatic Time Dependent Hartree-Fock formalism.

## Key numbers

- Triaxial inner barriers are systematically lowered by up to 4 MeV compared to axial barriers across all seven studied isotopic chains.
- The theoretical uncertainty in inner barrier heights, measured as the difference between two calculation methods, is of the order of 500 keV.
- Shape isomers in Th and U decay by gamma-emission, while fission and gamma-back decay compete for Pu and Cm isotopes; shape isomers in Cf, Cm and No decay through fission.
- Longer half-lives are predicted for N = 146 Th, U, Pu and Cm isotopes, suggesting N = 146 is a magic number at super deformation.
- A shallow potential minimum on top of the triaxial inner barrier is predicted in 246Pu, 248,250Cm and 252,254Cf isotopes, with collective levels located between 5 and 6 MeV in 250Cm.

## Context

This work extends systematic microscopic calculations of actinide structure using the Gogny D1S force, building on earlier mean-field studies of heavy nuclei. The results provide a benchmark for understanding superheavy element stability, as the actinide region serves as the gateway to superheavy nuclei. The predictions for shape isomer half-lives and third well spectroscopy in nuclei such as 250Cm can be compared with fission probability measurements by Britt (1978) and experimental data compiled in Nuclear Data Sheets and by Bjornholm (1980).
