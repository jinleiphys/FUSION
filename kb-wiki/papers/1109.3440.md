# Propagation of photons and massive vector mesons between a parity breaking medium and vacuum

> The problem of propagation of photons and massive vector mesons in the presence of Lorenz and CPT invariance violating medium is studied when the parity-odd medium is bounded by a hyperplane separating it from the vacuum. The solutions in both half-spaces are carefully discussed and in the case of space-like boundary stitched on the boundary with help of the Bogolubov transformations provided by the space-like Chern-Simons vector. The presence of two different Fock vacua is shown and the probability amplitude for transmission of particles from vacuum to parity breaking medium is calculated. We have also found classical solutions and showed that the results are consistent with ones obtained by canonical quantization formalism. In the cases, both of entrance to and of escaping from parity-odd medium, the probabilities for reflecting and passing through were found for each polarization using the classical solutions. Finally, the propagator for each polarization is obtained in the momentum space. Boundary effects under consideration are of certain importance for registration of local parity violation in the finite volume of heavy ion fireball and/or of a star with cold axion condensate.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a parity-odd medium separated from vacuum by a space-like hyperplane, the vector field solutions in the two half-spaces are related by Bogolyubov transformations, leading to two distinct Fock vacua and nontrivial transmission and reflection probabilities for photons and massive vector mesons at the boundary, provided the spatial momentum remains below an ultraviolet cutoff.

## Method

The authors consider a Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) Lagrangian with a Proca mass term and a Stückelberg auxiliary field, where a constant Chern-Simons vector \(\zeta^\mu\) is introduced via a linear pseudoscalar background confined to one half-space. They solve the field equations separately in the parity-odd medium and in vacuum, construct the canonical quantization for both regions, and impose boundary conditions at the hyperplane \(\zeta\cdot x=0\) to relate the creation and destruction operators via Bogolyubov transformations. Classical solutions are also obtained and shown to be consistent with the quantized formalism, and the propagator for each polarization is derived in momentum space.

## Key numbers

* For a temporal Chern-Simons vector \(\zeta_\mu=(\zeta_0,0,0,0)\), the dispersion relations for chiral polarizations are \(\omega_{\mathbf{k},\pm}=\sqrt{\mathbf{k}^2+m^2\pm\zeta_0|\mathbf{k}|}\), with the negative chirality mode requiring \(|\mathbf{k}|<m^2/|\zeta_0|\) to stay within the causal cone.
* For a spatial Chern-Simons vector \(\zeta_\mu=(0,-\zeta_x,0,0)\), the dispersion relations are \(\omega_{\mathbf{k},\pm}=\sqrt{\mathbf{k}^2+m^2+\frac12\zeta_x^2\pm\zeta_x\sqrt{k_1^2+m^2+\frac14\zeta_x^2}}\), with the negative chirality mode requiring \(|\mathbf{k}|<m^2/|\zeta_x|\).
* The momentum cutoff for well-defined negative chirality states is \(\Lambda=m^2/\zeta\), where \(\zeta\) stands for either \(|\zeta_0|\) or \(|\zeta_x|\).

## Context

This work extends the Carroll-Field-Jackiw electrodynamics (Carroll, Field, and Jackiw, 1989) to the case of a bounded parity-odd medium, building on prior analyses of Maxwell-Chern-Simons quantization (Andrianov, Giacconi, and Soldati, 2002; Andrianov, Espriu, and Soldati, 2009; Andrianov, Cannata, Giacconi, and Soldati, 2010). It enables the calculation of transmission and reflection probabilities for photons and massive vector mesons at the boundary of a parity-breaking region, which is relevant for detecting local parity violation in heavy ion fireballs or in astrophysical objects such as stars with cold axion condensates.
