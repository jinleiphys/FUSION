# Heaven and Earth: Connecting Jefferson Lab to the Cosmos

> The nuclear equation of state (EOS) serves as the fundamental bridge between atomic nuclei and neutron stars--objects that differ in size by almost 20 orders of magnitude. Central to this connection is the nuclear symmetry energy, which controls both the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei and the radii of neutron stars. Recent electroweak experiments at Jefferson Lab, specifically PREX, have provided the cleanest terrestrial constraints on the EOS near saturation density. Complementary to neutron skins is the isovector giant dipole resonance, particularly the electric dipole polarizability. This contribution discusses the implications of these measurements on the nuclear EOS and the role of upcoming electroweak capabilities at Jefferson Lab in addressing their impact on the structure and composition of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state (EOS) and its symmetry energy provide the fundamental bridge between atomic nuclei and neutron stars, with parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab (specifically PREX) offering the cleanest terrestrial constraints on the EOS near saturation density, though these constraints are limited to the slope parameter \(L\) and require combination with electric dipole polarizability measurements to simultaneously determine both the magnitude \(J\) and density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The paper reviews how the nuclear EOS is parameterized in terms of bulk parameters at saturation density \(n_0 \approx 0.15\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), including the symmetry energy slope \(L\) which determines the pressure of neutron-rich matter. It describes how parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) measures the weak form factor of \({}^{208}\)Pb via the asymmetry \(A_{PV}(Q^2)\) arising from interference between photon and \(Z^0\) exchange, providing a model-independent probe of neutron densities. Complementary constraints come from the electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_D\) extracted from the inverse energy-weighted sum rule \(m_{-1}\) of the isovector giant dipole resonance, measured via Coulomb excitation in inelastic proton scattering at RCNP.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_0 \approx 0.15\,\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Neutron-star radii extend over roughly 13 km, compared to neutron-skin thicknesses of a few tenths of a femtometer in \({}^{208}\)Pb
- The product \(\alpha_D J\) (electric dipole polarizability times symmetry energy at saturation) shows a nearly universal linear correlation with the neutron-skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb across a broad set of relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals
- Under idealized conditions (no muons, neglecting masses), beta equilibrium and charge neutrality yield a proton fraction \(Y_p = 1/9\) and neutron-proton asymmetry \(\alpha = 7/9\) in neutron-star matter

## Context

This work extends the program of connecting laboratory measurements to neutron-star structure pioneered by Horowitz (2000) and Donnelly (1989), who proposed PVES as a clean probe of neutron densities. It builds on the PREX measurements at Jefferson Lab (Abrahamyan 2012, Adhikari 2021, 2022) and the high-resolution dipole response measurements of \({}^{208}\)Pb at RCNP (Tamii 2011, 2013; Poltoratska 2012). The paper enables future combined analyses of neutron-skin thickness and electric dipole polarizability to simultaneously constrain both \(J\) and \(L\), with the upcoming MREX experiment at Mainz and FRIB's rare-isotope program providing complementary probes.
