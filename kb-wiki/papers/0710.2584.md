# Single Pion production from Nuclei

> We have studied charged current one pion production induced by $ν_μ(\barν_μ)$ from some nuclei. The calculations have been done for the incoherent pion production processes from these nuclear targets in the $Δ$ dominance model and take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and renormalization of $Δ$ properties in the nuclear medium. The effect of final state interactions of pions has also been taken into account. The numerical results have been compared with the recent results from the MiniBooNE experiment for the charged current 1$π$ production, and also with some of the older experiments in Freon and Freon-Propane from CERN.

# Single Pion production from Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, renormalization of Δ properties, and final-state interactions of pions) reduce the charged-current single-pion production cross section on nuclei by 30–40% relative to the free-nucleon prediction, but the shape of the Q² distribution is largely unaffected except at very low Q². This reduction is a combined effect of medium modifications and pion absorption, not just the medium effects alone.

## Method

The authors calculate incoherent charged-current single-pion production from nuclear targets (¹²C, ¹⁶O, Freon-Propane) using the Δ dominance model, where neutrinos or antineutrinos excite a Δ resonance in the nucleus. The Δ propagation is treated via a relativistic Rarita-Schwinger equation with a self-energy computed in nuclear many-body theory using the local density approximation, modifying the Δ mass and width as functions of nuclear density. Final-state interactions of the pions (elastic scattering and absorption) are simulated via Monte Carlo methods, propagating pions along straight-line trajectories through the nucleus until they exit.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear medium effects alone reduce the total cross section by 12–15% for neutrino energies E_ν = 0.7–2 GeV on ¹²C.
- Including pion absorption effects along with nuclear medium effects gives a total reduction of 30–40% in the cross section for the same energy range.
- For the Q² distribution averaged over the MiniBooNE flux, the reduction is about 15% in the peak region with nuclear medium effects alone, and about 35% when pion absorption is also included.
- The Δ mass shift in the medium is parameterized as Re Σ_Δ = 40 (ρ/ρ₀) MeV, where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The ratio σ(CC1π⁺)/σ(CCQE) on ¹²C is presented and compared with preliminary MiniBooNE results.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino-induced pion production on free nucleons (Lalakulich et al.) by incorporating nuclear medium effects using the many-body approach of Oset et al. and the pion final-state interaction treatment of Vicente Vacas. It provides a direct comparison with the MiniBooNE experiment’s charged-current single-pion production data and with older CERN experiments on Freon and Freon-Propane targets (Bolognese et al.). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions at intermediate energies relevant to oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE and K2K, and highlight the need to include both medium modifications and pion absorption to reproduce experimental cross sections.
