# Progress in the calculation of nucleon transition form factors

> We give a brief account of the Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev-equation approach and its application to nucleon resonances and their transition form factors. We compare the three-body with the quark-diquark approach and present a quark-diquark calculation for the low-lying nucleon resonances including scalar, axialvector, pseudoscalar and vector diquarks. We also discuss the timelike structure of transition form factors and highlight the advantages of form factors over helicity amplitudes.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev-equation approach, applied in a quark-diquark variant including scalar, axialvector, pseudoscalar and vector diquarks, can calculate nucleon resonance masses and transition form factors, but the masses of states dominated by pseudoscalar or vector diquarks are severely underestimated compared to experiment, likely requiring repulsive shifts beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation.

## Method

The author solves the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) and then calculates diquark amplitudes and propagators from their Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) and quark loop integrals in rainbow-ladder truncation. These ingredients enter the quark-diquark BSE, from which baryon masses are determined; the electromagnetic current matrix element is constructed using the dressed quark-photon vertex, diquark-photon vertices, and seagull amplitudes. The approach is applied to the low-lying nucleon resonances, comparing the relative importance of scalar, axialvector, pseudoscalar, and vector diquark channels.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass calculated as 0.95 GeV in rainbow-ladder truncation (experimental value ~0.94 GeV).
- N(1535) mass calculated as 1.21 GeV, compared to the experimental value near 1.535 GeV.
- Delta(1232) mass calculated as 1.28 GeV (experimental value ~1.232 GeV).
- Delta(1620) mass calculated as 1.42 GeV; Delta(1700) mass calculated as 1.46 GeV; Delta(1910) mass calculated as 1.60 GeV.
- Scalar diquark mass is approximately 0.80 GeV; pseudoscalar diquark mass is 1.01 GeV; vector diquark mass is 1.12 GeV; axialvector diquark mass is 1.00 GeV.
- The magnetic dipole form factor for the N-gamma-Delta transition agrees with experiment only above Q^2 greater than about 1 GeV^2 and deviates by about 30% at Q^2 = 0.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev-equation approach, previously applied to nucleon and Delta ground states (Eichmann 2009), to negative-parity nucleon resonances such as the N(1535) and N(1520). It compares the three-body Faddeev approach with the quark-diquark approximation and builds on earlier quark-diquark models (Oettel 1998, Roberts 2011). The results enable future calculations of transition form factors for excited baryons, particularly in the timelike region, and highlight the advantage of analyzing form factors rather than helicity amplitudes to avoid kinematic constraints.
