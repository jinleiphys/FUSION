# Transport Theory beyond Binary Collisions

> Using the Schwinger-Keldysh technique, we derive the transport equations for a system of quantum scalar fields. We first discuss the general structure of the equations and then their collision terms. Taking into account up to three-loop diagrams in ϕ^3 model and up to four-loop diagrams in ϕ^4 model, we obtain the transport equations which include the contributions of multi-particle collisions and particle production processes, in addition to mean-field effects and binary interactions.

## Key claim

Using the Schwinger-Keldysh technique, the authors derive transport equations for quantum scalar fields that include multi-particle collisions and particle production processes beyond the binary collision approximation, but only to lowest order in the gradient expansion (the Markovian limit where memory terms are neglected) and under the assumptions of weak inhomogeneity and weak coupling.

## Method

The authors start from the Dyson-Schwinger equations for contour Green's functions in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, perform a Wigner transformation, and apply a gradient expansion assuming macroscopic quasi-homogeneity. They then extract the transport and mass-shell equations for the Wigner function, and perform a perturbative analysis of the self-energies: in the phi^3 model they include up to three-loop diagrams, and in the phi^4 model up to four-loop diagrams, to obtain leading-order contributions from 2-to-3 processes (phi^3) and 2-to-4 and 3-to-3 processes (phi^4).

## Key numbers

- The transport equation is derived to lowest order in the gradient expansion, where the small parameter epsilon characterizes the gradient expansion and the coupling expansion parameter lambda equals g for phi^3 and sqrt(g) for phi^4 theory.
- Leading order contributions to Pi_delta + Re Pi^+ are of order lambda^2, and to Pi^< and Pi^> are of order lambda^4, in both phi^3 and phi^4 theory.
- The free spectral function is A_0(X,p) = 2 pi delta(p^2 - m^2)(Theta(p_0) - Theta(-p_0)).
- The quasi-particle approximation requires m^2 - Pi_delta - Re Pi^+ >> |Im Pi^+| for 1/g >> 1 with finite bare mass.

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym approach (Kad62) and earlier relativistic formulations by Bez72, Li (1982), Danielewicz, Calzetta (1986), and Mrowczynski (1989, 1992, 1997) to include multi-particle collisions beyond binary interactions. It provides a systematic first-principles derivation that had been lacking even in scalar field theory, enabling future studies of dense relativistic systems such as the quark-gluon plasma where gluon multiplication (discussed by Xiong (1992), Geiger (1991), Baier (2000), Arnold (2002), and Heinz (2004)) and particle-number-changing processes (shown by Jeon (1994, 1995) to affect bulk viscosity) are expected to be important.
