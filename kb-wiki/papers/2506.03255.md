# Reducing Hadronic Uncertainty in Low-Energy Neutral-Current Processes

> We analyze the hadronic uncertainty from light-quark loops coupled to (anti)neutrino in low-energy neutral-current (anti)neutrino scattering, estimated at the $3$-$4$ permille level. This uncertainty arises from limited knowledge of the charge-isospin correlation function of quark currents. We study the charge-charge and charge-isospin correlators within $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ and $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). In $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ ChPT, the two correlators are identical to all orders in the chiral and electromagnetic expansions. We further perform a leading-order $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ ChPT calculation and discuss the relevant counterterms. Our findings reduce the hadronic uncertainty in neutral-current processes such as (anti)neutrino-electron and coherent elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering by a factor $\sim 35$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, the charge-charge and charge-isospin correlators are identical to all orders in the chiral and electromagnetic expansions, reducing the hadronic uncertainty in low-energy neutral-current (anti)neutrino scattering by a factor of approximately 35 compared to earlier estimates that relied on the SU(3) flavor symmetry approximation with 20% uncertainty.

## Method

The author analyzes the charge-charge and charge-isospin correlation functions of quark currents using SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), evaluating the correlators at zero momentum transfer where ChPT provides a controlled effective field theory framework. In SU(2) ChPT, the meson charge matrix is expressed in terms of the isospin matrix, making the two correlators identical order-by-order; in SU(3) ChPT, a leading-order calculation is performed with explicit counterterms, and the SU(3) counterterms are matched to SU(2) results by integrating out kaon fields at the scale around the kaon mass. The uncertainty is estimated from isospin-violating mass differences and higher-order chiral corrections, combined in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic uncertainty in neutral-current processes is reduced by a factor of approximately 35 compared to earlier estimates (Tomalak 2019, Tomalak 2020).
- In elastic (anti)neutrino-electron scattering, the hadronic contribution to the relative error is 0.011% for νμ e → νμ e(γ), 0.009% for ν̄μ e → ν̄μ e(γ), 0.008% for νe e → νe e(γ), and 0.011% for ν̄e e → ν̄e e(γ), for incident neutrino energy much larger than the electron mass me.
- In coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on 40Ar, the hadronic contribution to the relative error is 0.016% for incident νμ energies of 50 MeV, 30 MeV, and 10 MeV.
- The charge-isospin correlator at the scale μ = 2 GeV for nf = 3 quark flavors was previously estimated as Π̂(3)3γ(0)|μ=2 GeV = (1 ± 0.2) Π̂(3)γγ(0)|μ=2 GeV, with Π̂(3)γγ(0)|μ=2 GeV = 3.597 ± 0.021 from dispersive analysis.
- The meson decay constant F0 is approximately 93 MeV.
- Lattice-QCD calculations validate the equality Π3γ(q2) ≈ Πγγ(q2) within 5-10% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the formulation of light-quark contributions to neutral-current (anti)neutrino scattering in terms of non-perturbative hadronic objects developed by Jegerlehner (1985, 2011) and applied to (anti)neutrino-electron scattering by Tomalak (2019) and Hill (2019), and to coherent elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering by Tomalak (2020). The paper reduces the dominant hadronic uncertainty that previously limited the interpretation of low-energy neutral-current processes, and the results provide a benchmark for future lattice-QCD calculations of current-current correlation functions, with implications for parity-violating electron scattering and atomic parity violation.
