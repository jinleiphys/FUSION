# Hadrons in Nuclei -- from High (200 GeV) to Low (1 GeV) energies

> The study of the interaction of hadrons, produced by elementary probes in a nucleus, with the surrounding nuclear medium can give insight into two important questions. First, at high energies, the production process, the time-scales connected with it and the prehadronic interactions can be studied by using the nuclear radius as a length-scale. We do this here by analyzing data from the EMC and HERMES experiements on nuclear attenuation. Second, at low energies the spectral function, and thus the selfenergy of the produced hadron, can be studied. Specifically, we analyze the CBELSA/TAPS data on $ω$ production in nuclei and discuss the importance of understanding in-medium effects both on the primary production cross section and the final state branching ratio. In both of these studies an excellent control of the final state interactions is essential.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a consistent description of nuclear attenuation data from high (200 GeV) to low (1 GeV) energies requires the prehadronic interaction strength to grow linearly with time after production, and that the observed low-mass enhancement in the omega meson spectral function from the CBELSA/TAPS experiment is primarily a threshold effect rather than a direct signature of in-medium mass modification.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA event generator for the primary lepton-nucleon interaction, incorporating Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and nuclear shadowing, then fed the output into the semiclassical GiBUU transport code to model prehadronic and hadronic final state interactions. For the low-energy omega production analysis, they extracted the s-dependence of the elementary cross section from free experimental data and scaled the matrix element for in-medium use, integrating over Fermi motion and inserting an in-medium spectral function with a downward mass shift of 14-16% at saturation density.

## Key numbers
- EMC experiment beam energies: 100 GeV and 280 GeV, with average Q^2 values of 4-12 GeV^2
- HERMES experiment energy: approximately 20 GeV, with Q^2 values of 1-2 GeV^2
- Formation times for EMC at z_h approximately 0.8 approach 30 fm, well beyond nuclear radii
- Omega mass shift of 14-16% at saturation density was put in by hand to describe the CBELSA/TAPS data
- Photon energy range for CBELSA/TAPS experiment: 900-2200 MeV, covering the free omega production threshold at 1100 MeV
- Mass resolution folding of 25 MeV applied to the invariant mass spectra for calcium

## Context
This work extends the analysis of nuclear attenuation data from the EMC collaboration (approximately 200 GeV) and HERMES collaboration (approximately 20 GeV) using the GiBUU transport framework, building on earlier work by Falter and collaborators and the PYTHIA-based formation time studies by Gallmeister and Mosel (2007). The low-energy analysis of omega meson production in nuclei addresses the CBELSA/TAPS data and contrasts with heavy-ion experiments (HADES, CERES, NA60) where thermal emission removes production-phase complications; it also notes that recent calculations by Voskresensky and collaborators and by Leupold, Wolf, and collaborators give significantly smaller mass shifts than the 14-16% used here. The results enable more reliable extraction of in-medium hadron properties by demonstrating that threshold effects and final state interactions must be carefully controlled before attributing spectral enhancements to genuine medium modifications.
