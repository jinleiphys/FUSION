# Pi0 photo- and electroproduction at threshold within a dynamical model

> We show that, within a meson-exchange dynamical model describing most of the existing pion electromagnetic production data up to the second resonance region, one is also able to obtain a good agreement with the pi0 photo- and electroproduction data near threshold. The potentials used in the model are derived from an effective chiral Lagrangian. The only sizable discrepancy between our results and the data is in the P-wave amplitude P3 = 2M1+ + M1-, where our prediction underestimate the data by about 20%. In the case of pi0 production, the effects of final state interaction in the threshold region are nearly saturated by single charge exchange rescattering. This indicates that in ChPT it might be sufficient to carry out the calculation just up to one-loop diagrams for threshold neutral pion production.

## Key claim

Within a meson-exchange dynamical model that successfully describes pion electromagnetic production data up to the second resonance region, the authors achieve good agreement with pi0 photo- and electroproduction data near threshold, but their prediction for the P-wave amplitude P3 = 2M1+ + M1- underestimates the data by about 20%.

## Method

The authors use a dynamical model where the t-matrix for pion photo- and electroproduction is given by t = v + v g0 t_piN, with v derived from an effective chiral Lagrangian containing Born terms and rho- and omega-exchange. The piN t-matrix is obtained from a meson-exchange piN model solved in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with Cooper-Jennings reduction, and the coupled channels equation is solved for pi0 production including elastic and charge exchange rescattering. For electroproduction, gauge invariance is restored by a substitution on the electromagnetic current, and the K-matrix approximation is used to account for pion mass difference effects below pi+ threshold.

## Key numbers

- E0+ multipole at threshold: -1.16 x 10^-3/m_pi+ (total model), compared to experimental value -1.33 +/- 0.11 x 10^-3/m_pi+
- P3 amplitude at threshold: 9.35 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2 (total model), compared to experimental value 11.48 +/- 0.41 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2 and ChPT prediction 10.36 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2
- P1 amplitude at threshold: 9.30 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2 (total model), compared to experimental value 9.47 +/- 0.37 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2
- P2 amplitude at threshold: -10.15 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2 (total model), compared to experimental value -9.46 +/- 0.39 x 10^-3 q/m_pi+^2
- Cutoff parameter Lambda = 440 MeV, obtained from analysis of Delta(1232) resonance multipole M1+^(3/2)
- At Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 and Delta W = 2.5 MeV, E0+ = 2.98 +/- 0.20 x 10^-3/m_pi+ and L0+ = -1.38 +/- 0.04 x 10^-3/m_pi+ from local fit using DM P-waves
- Single rescattering approximation differs from full coupled channels calculation by only 5% for Re E0+

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Kamalov and Yang (1999) from the first resonance region down to threshold, and contrasts with earlier failed attempts by Yang (1989) and Lee and Pearce (1991) which used different off-shell prescriptions. The finding that final state interaction effects are nearly saturated by single charge exchange rescattering indicates that one-loop ChPT calculations by Bernard et al. (1991, 1996) should be a reliable approximation for threshold neutral pion production. The model enables improved analysis of electroproduction data from NIKHEF and Mainz, and leads to a new version of the MAID unitary isobar model that includes a phenomenological correction term for off-shell pion rescattering.
