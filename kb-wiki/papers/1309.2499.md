# Limits on Tensor Coupling from Neutron $β$-Decay

> Limits on the tensor couplings generating a Fierz interference term, b, in mixed Gamow-Teller Fermi decays can be derived by combining data from measurements of angular correlation parameters in neutron decay, the neutron lifetime, and $G_{\text{V}}=G_{\text{F}} V_{ud}$ as extracted from measurements of the $\mathcal{F}t$ values from the $0^{+} \to 0^{+}$ superallowed decays dataset. These limits are derived by comparing the neutron $β$-decay rate as predicted in the standard model with the measured decay rate while allowing for the existence of beyond the standard model couplings. We analyze limits derived from the electron-neutrino asymmetry, $a$, or the beta-asymmetry, $A$, finding that the most stringent limits for $C_\text{T}/C_\text{A}$ under the assumption of no right-handed currents is $-0.0026 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0024$ (95% C.L.) for the two most recent values of $A$.

## Key claim

The paper derives limits on tensor couplings in neutron beta-decay by comparing the measured neutron lifetime with a prediction based on superallowed Fermi decays and angular correlation measurements, finding that the most stringent limit under the assumption of no right-handed currents is \(-0.0026 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0024\) (95% C.L.) for the two most recent values of the beta-asymmetry parameter \(A\). This limit depends critically on the assumption of maximal parity violation (no right-handed currents) and on the specific choice of angular correlation data used to extract the axial-vector coupling constant \(\lambda\).

## Method

The analysis uses a "lifetime-consistency test" that compares the measured neutron decay rate to a rate predicted from the weak coupling strength \(G_\text{V}\) extracted from \(0^+ \to 0^+\) superallowed Fermi decays and the axial-vector coupling constant \(\lambda = g_\text{A}/g_\text{V}\) from neutron angular correlation measurements. The Fierz interference term \(b\), which depends on scalar and tensor couplings, modifies both the neutron decay rate and the extracted value of \(G_\text{V}\) from superallowed decays, and also dilutes the measured angular correlation coefficients \(a\) (electron-neutrino asymmetry) and \(A\) (beta-asymmetry) via an energy-dependent factor \(1/(1 + b\, m_e/E_e)\). The authors derive closed-form expressions for limits on \(C_\text{T}/C_\text{A}\) by solving the coupled equations for the decay rate difference and the modified correlation coefficients, using the PDG values for \(\lambda\), \(\tau_n\), and \(V_{ud}\) along with limits on scalar couplings from the superallowed dataset.

## Key numbers

- Neutron lifetime (PDG value): \(\tau_n = 880.1 \pm 1.1\) s
- Axial-vector coupling constant (PDG value): \(\lambda = -1.2701 \pm 0.0025\)
- CKM matrix element: \(V_{ud} = 0.97425 \pm 0.00040\)
- Scalar coupling limit from superallowed decays: \(C_\text{S}/C_\text{V} = 0.0011 \pm 0.0013\)
- Combined constant \(K = 1.934(2) \times 10^{-4}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Most stringent limit on tensor coupling (using Perkeo II and UCNA values of \(A\)): \(-0.0026 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0024\) (95% C.L.)
- Limit using only Perkeo II result (\(\lambda = -1.2739 \pm 0.0019\)): \(-0.0012 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0065\) (95% C.L.)
- Limit from electron-neutrino correlation \(a\) (PDG value \(a_0 = -0.1030 \pm 0.0040\)): \(-0.0134 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0324\) (95% C.L.)
- Limit from the beta-asymmetry \(A\) using PDG \(\lambda\): \(-0.0015 < C_\text{T}/C_\text{A} < 0.0079\) (95% C.L.)
- Proposed sensitivity for Nab experiment: \(|C_\text{T}/C_\text{A}| < 0.0015\) with current lifetime uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bhattacharya et al. (2012) and provides a self-consistent derivation of tensor coupling limits that is comparable to global fits to all beta-decay data, such as those by Severijns et al. (2006) and Wauters et al. (2013), when similar assumptions are made (no right-handed neutrinos). The analysis uses the superallowed decay dataset compiled by Hardy and Towner (2009) and the neutron lifetime and angular correlation measurements from Perkeo II (Mund et al., 2012), UCNA (Mendenhall et al., 2013), and the PDG compilation (Beringer et al., 2012). The results enable next-generation experiments such as Nab, aCORN, aSPECT, and PERC to improve limits on tensor couplings, with the neutron lifetime uncertainty becoming the dominant contribution once angular correlation precision reaches the 0.1% level.
