# Evolution of higher moments of multiplicity distribution

> Evolution of a multiplicity distribution can be described with the help of master equation. We first look at 3rd and 4th factorial moments of multiplicity distributions and derive their equilibrium values. From them central moments and other ratios can be calculated. We study the master equation for a fixed temperature, because we want to know how fast different moments of the multiplicity distribution approach their equilibrium value. Then we investigate the situation in which the temperature of the system decreases. We find out that in the non-equilibrium state, higher factorial moments differ more from their equilibrium values than the lower moments and that the behaviour of a combination of the central moments depends on the combination we choose.

# Evolution of higher moments of multiplicity distribution

## Key claim

Higher factorial moments of the multiplicity distribution depart further from their equilibrium values than lower moments during non-equilibrium evolution, and the apparent freeze-out temperature extracted from different moments can differ depending on which moment or combination of moments is used. This effect is demonstrated for a cooling fireball using the specific reaction π⁺ + n ↔ K⁺ + Λ⁰, but the authors note that qualitatively similar results are obtained with other parameter sets.

## Method

The authors model the evolution of the multiplicity distribution using a master equation for a binary reversible process a₁a₂ ↔ b₁b₂, where b particles carry a conserved abelian charge. They first study relaxation of scaled factorial moments (F₂, F₃, F₄) at fixed temperature, then simulate a cooling fireball using a 1D longitudinally boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion scenario with temperature dropping from 165 MeV to 100 MeV. The inelastic cross-section for π⁺ + n ↔ K⁺ + Λ⁰ is parametrized, and a density-dependent mass of the Λ hyperon is assumed.

## Key numbers

- Initial hadronisation temperature: T₀ = 165 MeV; final temperature: 100 MeV
- Initial effective volume: V₀ = 125 fm³; initial particle density: ρ₀ = 0.08 fm⁻³
- Initial time: t₀ = 2.2 fm/c; final time: 10 fm/c
- Parameter ε set to 0.1 for the fixed-temperature relaxation study
- Initial mean multiplicity N₀ = 0.005 for binomial initial conditions
- Λ hyperon mass parametrization: m(ρ) = -2.2 GeV·fm³ · ρ + m_Λ₀

## Context

This work extends studies of multiplicity fluctuations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, motivated by the observation that statistical model fits to particle yields give temperatures above 160 MeV (Andronic, 2017), while higher-order susceptibilities from lattice QCD compared to fluctuation data yield lower temperatures (Alba, 2014). The paper builds on prior master equation treatments of multiplicity distributions (references 1 and 2 in the text) and demonstrates that non-equilibrium effects during cooling can produce different apparent freeze-out temperatures from different moments, cautioning against naive interpretation of fluctuation measurements. The results enable more careful analysis of freeze-out temperature extraction from experimental data on higher moments.
