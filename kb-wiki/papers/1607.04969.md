# Applicability of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method to the deuteron breakup at low energies

> We re-examine the deuteron elastic breakup cross sections on 12C and 10Be at low incident energies, for which a serious discrepancy between the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) and the Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas theory (FAGS) was pointed out. We show the closed-channels neglected in the preceding study affect significantly the breakup cross section calculated with CDCC, resulting in good agreement with the result of FAGS.

## Key claim
The previously reported serious discrepancy between CDCC and FAGS for deuteron elastic breakup cross sections at low incident energies (below about 20 MeV) is mainly due to the neglect of closed channels in the earlier CDCC calculation; when closed channels are included, the converged CDCC results agree well with FAGS results.

## Method
The authors performed CDCC calculations for deuteron elastic breakup on 12C and 10Be using a p+n+A three-body model, discretizing the p-n continua with the momentum-bin average method. They systematically increased the maximum p-n breakup momentum kmax to check convergence, and compared results obtained with only open channels against results including closed channels (channels with Ei ≤ 0). The calculations used the one-range Gaussian p-n interaction of Ohmura (1970) and the CH89 global nucleon-nucleus optical potential of Varner (1991), matching the model settings of the prior study by Upadhyay (2012).

## Key numbers
- For 12C(d,pn)12C at Ed=12 MeV: converged CDCC with closed channels agrees well with FAGS, while CDCC with open channels only overshoots FAGS by about a factor of three at most.
- For 12C(d,pn)12C at Ed=12 MeV: used Δk=0.05 fm⁻¹, ℓmax=8, kmax=0.9 fm⁻¹, with convergence confirmed to 98% accuracy.
- For 12C(d,pn)12C at Ed=56 MeV: the adopted kmax (0.9 fm⁻¹) is very close to the open-channel threshold of 1.05 fm⁻¹, making closed-channel effects negligible.
- For 10Be(d,pn)10Be at Ed=21 MeV: used kmax=1.1 fm⁻¹, with closed channels again crucial for agreement with FAGS.

## Context
This work extends the systematic comparison between CDCC and FAGS performed by Upadhyay (2012), which found serious discrepancies at low deuteron energies. The authors show that the earlier study omitted closed channels, whose importance for low-energy reactions was previously noted by Austern (1987, 1996) and numerically confirmed in one-dimensional scattering by A. M. Moro and collaborators (2010) and for 11Be scattering by D. D. Di Pietro and collaborators (2012). The results validate CDCC as applicable to low-energy elastic breakup, enabling future systematic studies of closed-channel effects in transfer reactions.
