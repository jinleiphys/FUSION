# Shape phase transitions in odd-A Zr isotopes

> Spectroscopic properties that characterize shape phase transitions in neutron-rich odd-A Zr isotopes are investigated using the framework of nuclear density functional theory and particle-core coupling. The interacting-boson Hamiltonian of the even-even core nuclei, and the single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of the unpaired neutron are completely determined by deformation constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with a choice of a universal energy density functional and pairing interaction. The triaxial $(β,γ)$ deformation energy surfaces for even-even $^{94-102}$Zr indicates transition from triaxial or $γ$-soft ($^{94,96}$Zr) to prolate ($^{98}$Zr), and triaxial ($^{100,102}$Zr) shapes. The corresponding low-energy excitation spectra of the odd-A Zr isotopes are in very good agreement with recent experimental results. Consistent with the structural evolution of the neighboring even-even Zr nuclei, the state-dependent effective deformations and their fluctuations in the odd-A isotopes indicate a pronounced discontinuity around the transitional nucleus $^{99}$Zr.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shape phase transitions in neutron-rich odd-A Zr isotopes can be described microscopically using nuclear density functional theory combined with particle-core coupling, but the method requires adjusting several boson-fermion interaction strength parameters separately for each odd-A nucleus to reproduce experimental low-energy spectra, and it does not include intruder configurations or configuration mixing that would be needed to describe very low-lying excited 0+ states in the even-even cores.

## Method

The authors performed deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to generate potential energy surfaces for even-even 94-102Zr. They mapped these surfaces onto the expectation value of an interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian to determine the boson-core parameters, then coupled an unpaired neutron to the boson core using the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2), with spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities taken from the same mean-field calculations and the boson-fermion interaction strengths adjusted to reproduce experimental low-lying excitation spectra for each odd-A nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The SCMF energy surfaces for 94-102Zr show a triaxial minimum at gamma approximately 40 degrees for 94Zr, a prolate minimum at beta approximately 0.5 for 98Zr, and a triaxial global minimum at gamma approximately 15 degrees for 102Zr
- The IBM-2 Hamiltonian parameters include epsilon values ranging from 0.036 MeV (100Zr) to 0.501 MeV (94Zr), and kappa values from -0.040 MeV (102Zr) to -0.090 MeV (96Zr)
- Single-particle energies for the 1h11/2 orbital range from -0.894 MeV (95Zr) to -1.929 MeV (103Zr), with occupation probabilities v^2 from 0.014 (95Zr) to 0.036 (103Zr)
- The exchange interaction strength Lambda for the h11/2 configuration takes values up to 47.0 MeV (97Zr), with the resulting effective constant Lambda_jj approximately -2.2 MeV for 97Zr
- The energy ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) for 94,96Zr is predicted to be greater than 2, while experimental results show a ratio less than 2

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of shape phase transitions in even-even Zr isotopes (Togashi 2016, Nomura 2016, Sazonov 2019, Garcia-Ramos 2019, Gavrielov 2019) to the odd-A systems 95-103Zr, where much less theoretical research has been performed despite recent experimental measurements (Rząca-Urban 2018 for 97Zr, Spagnoletti 2019 and Boulay 2020 for 99Zr). The method builds on the authors' earlier framework (Nomura 2016) for computing spectroscopic properties of odd-A nuclei using density functional theory and particle-core coupling, and enables systematic description of quantum phase transitions in odd-mass systems, though future work would need to incorporate intruder configurations and configuration mixing to fully describe shape coexistence effects seen in the even-even cores.
