# On a three-body confinement force in hadron spectroscopy

> Recently it has been argued that a three-body colour confinement interaction can affect the stability condition of a three-quark system and the spectrum of a tetraquark described by any constituent quark model. Here we discuss the role of a three-body colour confinement interaction in a simple quark model and present some of its implications for the spectra of baryons, tetraquarks and six-quark systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a three-body colour confinement force, with a strength parameter \(c\) satisfying \(-\frac{3}{2} < c < \frac{2}{5}\), can substantially enlarge the gap between the physical colour-singlet baryon state and unphysical coloured states, while simultaneously raising the expectation value of octet-octet \(q\overline{q}\) pairs relative to singlet-singlet pairs in tetraquarks, thereby reducing tetraquark binding, a result that depends on the sign and magnitude of \(c\) and on the choice of the arbitrary constant \(c_1\) in the two-body confinement term.

## Method

The authors adopt a simple harmonic-oscillator quark model with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, a two-body confinement potential \(V_{2b}\) (with a colour operator \(F_i^a F_j^a\)), and a three-body confinement potential \(V_{3b}\) proportional to the cubic SU(3) invariant operator \(d^{abc} F_i^a F_j^b F_k^c\). They compute expectation values of the full Hamiltonian for \(q^3\) systems in colour-singlet, octet, and decuplet states using variational wavefunctions with a single oscillator parameter \(b\), and for \(q^2\overline{q}^2\) tetraquarks by evaluating the three-body operator in the two possible colour-singlet intermediate couplings (\(|\overline{3}_{12}3_{34}\rangle\) and \(|6_{12}\overline{6}_{34}\rangle\)) and then transforming to the physical singlet-singlet and octet-octet bases. For the \(q^6\) system relevant to the NN interaction, they calculate the three-body matrix elements in the five colour components of the totally symmetric orbital state \([6]_O\) and in the mixed-symmetry states \([42]_O\), using fractional parentage coefficients to obtain the expectation values for physical NN, \(\Delta\Delta\), and hidden-colour CC states.

## Key numbers

- The allowed range for the three-body strength parameter is \(-\frac{3}{2} < c < \frac{2}{5}\) (from the stability condition for the \(q^3\) colour-singlet state).
- For \(c = -1.5\) (the lower limit), the octet-singlet gap \(\Delta E \simeq 10.7 \hbar \omega\); for \(c = 0\) (no three-body force), \(\Delta E \simeq 3.5 \hbar \omega\).
- Using typical quark-model parameters \(b = 0.437\) fm and \(m = 0.340\) GeV gives \(\hbar \omega = 0.6\) GeV; then for \(c = -1.43\) the octet-singlet gap \(\Delta E \approx 5.5\) GeV, compared to \(\Delta E \approx 2.1\) GeV for \(c = 0\).
- For the \(q^6\) system in the \([6]_O\) symmetry state, the total three-body matrix element equals \(20/9\), which is twice the value for a single baryon.
- The expectation values of \(V_{3b}\) for the physical NN and \(\Delta\Delta\) states are both \(c/9\), while for the hidden-colour CC state it is \(20c/9\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic treatment of a three-body confinement force introduced by Dmitrasinovic (1999) (referred to as DMI in the text), who first derived the operator in terms of SU(3) invariants and applied it to \(q^3\) and \(q^2\overline{q}^2\) systems. The present paper rederives those results, clarifies the role of the arbitrary constant \(c_1\) in the two-body potential, and provides entirely new calculations for the \(q^6\) system relevant to the NN interaction. The three-body confinement force is distinct from the short-range instanton-induced three-body force of Dorokhov and Oganesian (1995) and from the phenomenological scalar three-body force of Descouvemont (1992) used for the Roper resonance. The results are valid for any constituent quark model irrespective of the hyperfine interaction, and the authors note that further study is needed to examine how the confinement contribution changes with separation distance between clusters.
