# Gamow-Teller strength distributions in 76Ge and 76Se from deformed QRPA

> We study Gamow-Teller strength distributions of 76Ge and 76Se within a deformed QRPA formalism, which includes residual spin-isospin forces in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. We consider two different methods to construct the quasiparticle basis, a selfconsistent approach based on a deformed Hartree-Fock calculation with density-dependent Skyrme forces and a more phenomenological approach based on a deformed Woods-Saxon potential. Both methods contain pairing correlations in the BCS approach. We discuss the sensitivity of Gamow-Teller strength distributions to the deformed mean field and residual interactions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that Gamow-Teller strength distributions in ⁷⁶Ge and ⁷⁶Se are sensitive to the choice of deformed mean field (Hartree-Fock with Skyrme forces versus Woods-Saxon potential), and that different mean fields require different residual interaction strengths to reproduce experimental data; the authors caution that the coupling strengths used are not necessarily optimal for these nuclei because they were fitted to a different set of nuclei and a different single-particle model.

## Method

The authors performed deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations including residual spin-isospin forces in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. They constructed the quasiparticle basis using two approaches: a selfconsistent deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculation with Skyrme forces (Sk3 and SG2) and a phenomenological deformed Woods-Saxon (WS) potential, both with BCS pairing correlations. The residual interaction strengths were taken from Homma et al. (1996), with the particle-hole strength reduced for the HF calculations to better match the experimental Gamow-Teller resonance position.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps for ⁷⁶Ge: Δ_ν = 1.54 MeV, Δ_π = 1.56 MeV; for ⁷⁶Se: Δ_ν = 1.71 MeV, Δ_π = 1.75 MeV (from experimental binding energies).
- Residual interaction strengths used for WS calculations: χ^{ph}_{GT} = 0.25 MeV, κ^{pp}_{GT} = 0.027 MeV (for A=76, from the parametrization χ^{ph}_{GT}=5.2/A^{0.7} MeV and κ^{pp}_{GT}=0.58/A^{0.7} MeV).
- For HF calculations, the particle-hole strength was reduced to χ^{ph}_{GT} = 0.16 MeV, with the same κ^{pp}_{GT} = 0.027 MeV.
- Ikeda sum rule values: 3(N-Z) = 36 for ⁷⁶Ge and 3(N-Z) = 24 for ⁷⁶Se, fulfilled within 0.3% accuracy in the calculations.
- Experimental charge radii: r_c(⁷⁶Ge) = 4.057 fm and r_c(⁷⁶Se) = 4.119 fm from relativistic mean field calculations, in good agreement with the authors' results.
- Experimental intrinsic quadrupole moments: Q_p(exp)(⁷⁶Ge) = 66(21) fm² and Q_p(exp)(⁷⁶Se) = 119(25) fm² from laboratory moments; absolute values from B(E2) strengths: |Q_p(exp)|(⁷⁶Ge) = 164(24) fm² and |Q_p(exp)|(⁷⁶Se) = 205(24) fm².

## Context

This work extends deformed QRPA calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions to the double beta-decay candidates ⁷⁶Ge and ⁷⁶Se, building on prior studies by Sarriguren et al. (2001) for exotic nuclei and by Homma et al. (1996) for residual interaction parametrizations. The results show that deformation increases both β⁺ and β⁻ total strengths by a few percent compared to spherical calculations, contradicting earlier SU(3) and shell model results by Auerbach et al. (1973) for lighter nuclei, which the authors attribute to the larger single-particle basis (up to N=10 oscillator shells) used here. The work enables more reliable estimates of double beta-decay half-lives by clarifying the dependence on the deformed mean field and residual interactions.
