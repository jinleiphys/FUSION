# Stability of the Heaviest Nuclei on Spontaneous-Fission and Alpha-Decay

> Spontaneous-fission half-lives of the heaviest even-even nuclei are evaluated and compared with their alpha-decay mode. Calculations of spontaneous-fission half-lives are performed in the dynamical way with potential energy obtained by the macroscopic-microscopic method and the inertia tensor obtained by the cranking approximation. The alpha-decay half-lives are calculated for the same region of nuclei by use of the Viola and Seaborg formula. The ground-state properties such as mean square radii and electric quadrupole moments are also studied. From the analysis of spontaneous-fission half-life values it is found that a peninsula of deformed metastable superheavy nuclei near Z=106 and N=162 is separated from an island of the spherical superheavy, around the doubly magic nucleus with Z=114 and N=184, by a trench in the vicinity of neutron number N=170.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a peninsula of deformed metastable superheavy nuclei near Z=106 and N=162 is separated from an island of spherical superheavy nuclei around the doubly magic nucleus Z=114 and N=184 by a trench in spontaneous-fission half-lives in the vicinity of neutron number N=170, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of a five-dimensional deformation space (beta2, beta4, beta6, Delta_p, Delta_n) and the use of a pairing correction to approximate calculations in that full space due to computational limits.

## Method

The authors calculated spontaneous-fission half-lives for even-even nuclei with Z=100-114 and N=142-180 using a dynamical multi-dimensional WKB approach with the dynamic-programming method to find the least-action trajectory. The potential energy was obtained from the macroscopic-microscopic method (Yukawa-plus-exponential model with Strutinsky shell and pairing corrections from a Woods-Saxon single-particle Hamiltonian), and the inertia tensor was computed using the cranking approximation. Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using the Viola-Seaborg phenomenological formula with Q_alpha values from mass differences.

## Key numbers

- The local maximum of spontaneous-fission half-life is centered at the nucleus 268_106Sg_162 with a value of 2.5 hours.
- The local minimum in the trench region is obtained for the nucleus 272_102No_170 with a value of 10 microseconds.
- The spontaneous-fission half-lives of the two heaviest nuclei considered, 292_114_178 and 294_114_180, are comparable to those of the most stable Fm isotopes with neutron numbers N=152 and 154 (approximately 100 years).
- The total half-lives for 292_114_178 and 294_114_180 are found larger than 1 year.
- The pairing degrees of freedom reduce spontaneous-fission half-lives for Fm isotopes with N>152 by about 3 orders of magnitude.
- The number of assaults on the fission barrier per unit time is taken as n approximately 10^20.38 s^-1, corresponding to a vibrational frequency of hbar*omega_0 = 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic-microscopic calculations of spontaneous-fission half-lives by Smolanczuk et al. (1995) and others, using a more comprehensive multi-dimensional deformation space that includes beta_6 and pairing degrees of freedom as dynamical variables. The results are generally similar to those published by other groups employing the macroscopic-microscopic method, such as Smolanczuk and Sobiczewski (1995), and are also in agreement with fully selfconsistent microscopic nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Berger et al. (1995). The study enables identification of specific nuclei where alpha-decay or spontaneous-fission dominates, and maps the transition region between deformed and spherical superheavy nuclei.
