# Probing the chiral magnetic effect via transverse spherocity event classification in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present the first study of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) using transverse spherocity as an event-shape classifier in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, simulated with the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with a realistic CME implementation. Transverse spherocity separates events into jetty and isotropic topologies based on the geometric distribution of transverse momentum. Unlike traditional event shape engineering methods, which use the flow vector as an event classifier that is itself contaminated by the very backgrounds it is intended to suppress, spherocity provides a cleaner, geometry-driven classification that avoids this circular limitation. CME inclusion shifts the spherocity distribution toward more isotropic events, confirming its sensitivity to CME-induced charge separation. The charge-dependent azimuthal correlator $Δγ$ and correlated background coupled with elliptic flow are consistently higher in jetty events. The scaled ratio $Δγ/v_2$ shows enhanced values for isotropic events, confirming effective background suppression after elliptic flow scaling. Our results demonstrate that isotropic event selection via transverse spherocity provides a cleaner and more reliable environment for CME searches by simultaneously suppressing flow-driven and resonance-decay backgrounds, making it a powerful complementary method to existing flow-vector-based methods.

## Key claim
The paper claims that transverse spherocity event classification provides a cleaner, geometry-driven alternative to traditional flow-vector-based event shape engineering for isolating the Chiral Magnetic Effect signal, but this claim is conditional on the AMPT model implementation and the specific assumption that the CME-induced charge separation systematically biases events toward isotropic topologies.

## Method
The authors simulated 5 × 10^5 minimum-bias Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV using the string-melting version of the AMPT model, incorporating a CME implementation that exchanges p_y momentum components between downward-moving quarks and upward-moving antiquarks of the same flavor with a charge separation fraction f. Events were classified by transverse spherocity S_0, calculated within |η| < 0.8 and p_T > 0.15 GeV/c with at least 10 charged particles, into jetty (S_0 → 0) and isotropic (S_0 → 1) classes using three selection criteria: (70%-30%), (80%-20%), and (90%-10%). The charge-dependent azimuthal correlator Δγ and its scaling with elliptic flow v_2 were then compared across these spherocity-selected event classes.

## Key numbers
- Spherocity distributions for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV show a systematic shift toward higher S_0 values when CME is implemented, for both (30-50)% and (50-70)% centrality classes.
- The (90%-10%) spherocity cut, which isolates the top 10% and bottom 10% of events in the spherocity distribution, yields the largest separation in v_2 between jetty and isotropic event classes across all p_T bins.
- Elliptic flow v_2 is largest in jetty events, intermediate in integrated events, and smallest in isotropic events for all applied spherocity cuts in (30-50)% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the event shape engineering approach of the ALICE collaboration (ALICE 2017, ALICE 2023) by replacing the flow-vector-based classifier with transverse spherocity, which avoids the circular limitation of using v_2 itself as a classifier when v_2 is entangled with the backgrounds it aims to suppress. The study builds on prior AMPT-based CME implementations by Ma (2011) and the authors' own previous work (Dey 2025, Dey 2026), and responds to the inconclusive isobar run results from STAR (2021) that highlighted the difficulty of isolating CME from backgrounds. The results enable future experimental CME searches at LHC and RHIC to use spherocity-based event selection as a complementary method to existing flow-vector-based techniques.
