# Experiments with $Ξ^-$ atoms

> Experiments with $Ξ^-$ atoms are proposed in order to study the nuclear interaction of $Ξ$ hyperons. The production of $Ξ^-$ in the ($K^{-}$, $K^{+}$) reaction, the $Ξ^-$ stopping in matter, and its atomic cascade are incorporated within a realistic evaluation of the results expected for $Ξ^-$ X-ray spectra across the periodic table, using an assumed $Ξ$-nucleus optical potential $V_{opt}$. Several optimal targets for measuring the strong-interaction shift and width of the X-ray transition to the `last' atomic level observed are singled out: F, Cl, I, Pb. The sensitivity of these observables to the parameters of $V_{opt}$ is considered. The relevance of such experiments is discussed in the context of strangeness -2 nuclear physics and multistrange nuclear matter. Finally, with particular reference to searches for the $H$ dibaryon, the properties of $Ξ^-d$ atoms are also discussed. The role of Stark mixing, its effect on $S$ and $P$ state capture of $Ξ^-$ by the deuteron, together with estimates of the resulting probability for producing the $H$ dibaryon are considered in detail.

# Experiments with $\Xi^-$ atoms

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring X-ray spectra from $\Xi^-$ atoms across the periodic table can provide meaningful information about the $\Xi$-nucleus strong interaction, provided that targets are carefully selected (F, Cl, I, Pb) and that the experimental yields are sufficient; the feasibility relies on the assumption that $\Xi^-$ atoms behave similarly to $\Sigma^-$ atoms, which have already been successfully observed.

## Method

The authors performed atomic cascade calculations for $\Xi^-$ atoms using a $t\rho$ optical potential $V_{opt}$ with complex parameter $b_0$, and also explored density-dependent (DD) potentials derived from the Nijmegen model D $G$-matrix interaction. They calculated strong-interaction shifts and widths for X-ray transitions to the "last" observed atomic level across a range of target nuclei, and evaluated the sensitivity of these observables to the potential parameters. For $\Xi^-$ deuterium atoms, they used the Borie and Leon model for Stark mixing to compute $S$ and $P$ state capture fractions and the resulting probability for $H$ dibaryon production.

## Key numbers

- The $\Xi$-nucleus potential well depth from analysis of old emulsion data is $V_{0}^{(\Xi)} = 21 - 24$ MeV (Dover and Gal, 1983)
- A minimum well depth of about 15 MeV is required for the possibility of strange hadronic matter
- The KEK E224 experiment on $^{12}$C$(K^-, K^+)X$ gave an estimate of $V_{0}^{(\Xi)}$ between 15 to 20 MeV with energy resolution of about 10 MeV
- Binding energy from emulsion events: $B_{\Xi^{-}}(^{12}{\rm C}) = 0.58 \pm 0.14$ MeV; $B_{\Xi^{-}}(^{14}{\rm N}) = 0.35 \pm 0.20$ MeV; $B_{\Xi^{-}}(^{16}{\rm O}) = 0.31 \pm 0.23$ MeV
- The sensitivity of binding energies and strong interaction widths to the $\Xi$-nucleus strong interaction for light nuclei is of the order of 100 eV, substantially smaller than a typical emulsion error of 100 keV
- $\Xi^-$ and $\Sigma^-$ masses: 1321.32 MeV and 1197.34 MeV respectively; lifetimes: 0.1642 nsec and 0.1482 nsec respectively
- The $K^-p \rightarrow \Xi^-K^+$ reaction has total cross section larger than 100 $\mu$b only in the $K^-$ kinetic energy range of 1.0 to 1.8 GeV
- Up to 50% of produced $\Xi^-$ may survive the slowing down process
- For the cascade population parameter: $\alpha = -0.056 \pm 0.020$ from fits to $\Sigma^-$ Pb data
- Selection criteria for X-ray transitions: energies greater than 100 keV, strong interaction shift at least 0.5 keV, width less than about 10 keV, relative yield at least 10%
- For the $t\rho$ potential with $b_0=0.25+i0.04$ fm, the calculated shifts are generally equal to or up to 30% larger than the widths when both are smaller than 10 keV
- For the density-dependent potential G (from Nijmegen model D with hard-core radius $r_c = 0.46$ fm), the depth is about 18 MeV in medium-weight and heavy nuclei and about half that in light nuclei
- The r.m.s. radius difference between potential G and the nuclear density distribution amounts to about 1.3 fm
- For the inversion test using potential G as pseudo-data: best-fit $t\rho$ gave ${\rm Re} b_0 = 0.402 \pm 0.022$ fm, ${\rm Im} b_0 = 0.053 \pm 0.021$ fm, $\chi^2/F = 8.8$
- For the DD fit with $\alpha = 1/3$: ${\rm Re} b_0 = 1.27 \pm 0.21$ fm, ${\rm Im} b_0 = 0.019 \pm 0.009$ fm, ${\rm Re} B_0 = -1.05 \pm 0.26$ fm, $\chi^2/F = 4.65$
- For the DD fit with $\alpha$ free ($\alpha = -0.7$): ${\rm Re} b_0 = 0.268 \pm 0.033$ fm, ${\rm Im} b_0 = 0.040 \pm 0.009$ fm, ${\rm Re} B_0 = 0.056 \pm 0.011$ fm, $\chi^2/F = 1.77$
- For $\Xi^-d$ atoms: total strong interaction widths range $\Gamma_{1S} = 1$ to 50 keV for $S$ states and $\Gamma_{2P} = 0.2$ to 0.8 eV for $P$ states (Aerts and Dover, 1984)
- $H$ particle production is more likely from $S$ states than $P$ states by a factor varying from 1.4 at $m_H = 2.23$ GeV ($B(H) = 0$ MeV) to 14 at $m_H = 2.18$ GeV ($B(H) = 50$ MeV)
- The fraction of $S$ state capture increases from 0.46 to 0.57 and $P$ state fraction decreases from 0.38 to 0.27 as $B(H)$ increases from 0 to 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to $\Sigma^-$ atoms (Batty, Friedman, and Gal, 1994) to the case of $\Xi^-$ atoms, proposing experiments to study the $\Xi$-nucleus interaction where emulsion data have proven insufficient. The paper builds on theoretical predictions by Dover and Gal (1983, 1984) using Nijmegen model D, and on $G$-matrix evaluations by Yamamoto et al. (1994, 1995). The results enable future experiments to extract the $\Xi$-nucleus optical potential parameters, which are essential for understanding strangeness -2 nuclear physics, multistrange nuclear matter, and searches for the $H$ dibaryon via $\Xi^-d$ atoms as pursued in AGS experiment E813 (reviewed by Chrien, 1998).
