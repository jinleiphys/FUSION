# 4He decay of excited states in 14C

> A study of the 7Li(9Be,4He 10Be)2H reaction at E{beam}=70 MeV has been performed using resonant particle spectroscopy techniques and provides the first measurements of alpha-decaying states in 14C. Excited states are observed at 14.7, 15.5, 16.4, 18.5, 19.8, 20.6, 21.4, 22.4 and 24.0 MeV. The experimental technique was able to resolve decays to the various particle bound states in 10Be, and provides evidence for the preferential decay of the high energy excited states into states in 10Be at ~6 MeV. The decay processes are used to indicate the possible cluster structure of the 14C excited states.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurements of alpha-decaying states in 14C, but the authors hedge that the observed states may correspond to multiple unresolved states and that spin measurements could not be determined from the angular distributions due to the complexity of the reaction.

## Method

The 7Li(9Be,4He 10Be)2H reaction was studied at a beam energy of 70 MeV using resonant particle spectroscopy with an array of four charged particle telescopes, each consisting of thin silicon quadrant detectors, position-sensitive strip detectors, and CsI stopping detectors. The energies and angles of the detected 4He and 10Be particles were used to reconstruct the 14C excitation energy spectrum for decays to the 10Be ground state, first 2+ state at 3.4 MeV, and the group of states at approximately 6 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Excited states in 14C observed at 14.7, 15.5, 16.4, 18.5, 19.8, 20.6, 21.4, 22.4, and 24.0 MeV
- Beam energy of 70 MeV for 9Be, with intensity of 3 enA and integrated beam exposure of 0.45 mC
- Energy resolution of the telescopes was 300 keV; total energy spectrum resolution was approximately 2.0 MeV
- The Q-value for the 7Li(9Be,10Be alpha)2H reaction is -1.91 MeV
- The total energy spectrum showed peaks at 68.1 MeV (10Be ground state), 64.7 MeV (10Be 2+ at 3.4 MeV), and approximately 62 MeV (10Be states at 5.958, 5.960, 6.179, and 6.263 MeV)

## Context

This work extends studies of molecular cluster structures in neutron-rich Be isotopes, particularly the molecular orbit model calculations for 10Be by Itagaki (2000) and antisymmetrised molecular dynamics calculations by Kanada-En'yo (1999, 2002). The results suggest that 14C excited states may contain molecular characteristics similar to those proposed for 10Be, where the ground state and first 2+ state correspond to neutrons in pi-type orbits while the 6 MeV states involve sigma-type orbits. The measurements enable future experiments capable of determining the spins of these states to provide more definitive evidence for molecular chain configurations in 14C.
