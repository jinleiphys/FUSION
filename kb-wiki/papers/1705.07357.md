# Dispersion and decay of collective modes in neutron star cores

> We calculate the frequencies of collective modes of neutrons, protons and electrons in the outer core of neutron stars. The neutrons and protons are treated in a hydrodynamic approximation and the electrons are regarded as collisionless. The coupling of the nucleons to the electrons leads to Landau damping of the collective modes and to significant dispersion of the low-lying modes. We investigate the sensitivity of the mode frequencies to the strength of entrainment between neutrons and protons, which is not well characterized. The contribution of collective modes to the thermal conductivity is evaluated.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that collective modes in neutron star outer cores exhibit significant dispersion and Landau damping due to coupling between nucleons and electrons, with mode frequencies sensitive to the poorly characterized strength of entrainment between neutrons and protons.

## Method

The authors treat neutrons and protons in a hydrodynamic approximation and electrons as collisionless, using linear response theory with the random phase approximation for the electron response. They solve the coupled equations of motion for small longitudinal perturbations about a uniform state, deriving the dispersion relation from a 4x4 matrix eigenvalue problem. The nucleon contributions to thermodynamic derivatives are based on chiral effective field theory from Hebeler et al. (2013), while the entrainment parameter is estimated using two approaches: one from the SLy4 Skyrme interaction and another from considerations of nucleon effective masses.

## Key numbers

* Entrainment parameter from SLy4 Skyrme interaction: n_np^(a) = -1.567 fm^3 n_n n_p = -0.04012 (n_n n_p / n_0^2) fm^{-3}
* Entrainment parameter from effective mass considerations: n_np^(b) = -0.0800 (n_n n_p / n_0^2) fm^{-3}, roughly twice the SLy4 value
* At nuclear matter density and T = 10^8 K, mean free path of predominantly neutron mode: 3.022 x 10^{-9} cm (no entrainment), 3.248 x 10^{-9} cm (n_np^(a)), 3.321 x 10^{-9} cm (n_np^(b))
* At nuclear matter density and T = 10^8 K, mean free path of predominantly charged particle mode: 7.003 x 10^{-10} cm (no entrainment), 6.454 x 10^{-10} cm (n_np^(a)), 6.492 x 10^{-10} cm (n_np^(b))
* Electron mean free path for viscosity at nuclear matter density and T = 10^8 K: approximately 10^{10} fm or 10^{-3} cm
* Electron plasma frequency as k approaches 0: approximately (4 pi n_e e^2 c^2 / mu_e)^{1/2}

## Context

This work extends the long-wavelength treatment of Kobyakov and Pethick (2015) to shorter wavelengths where the plasma is no longer locally neutral electrically, and where electrons must be treated as collisionless rather than hydrodynamic. It builds on earlier studies of collective modes by Epstein (1988), Baldo and Ducoin (2003), Bedaque and Reddy (2014), and Kobyakov et al. (2015). The results enable calculation of the contribution of collective modes to thermal conductivity in neutron star cores, with mean free paths much shorter than those from phonon-phonon scattering calculated by Manuel and Tolos (2011), suggesting that collective modes could play a significant role in heat transport.
