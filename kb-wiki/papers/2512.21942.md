# Multi-reference Trial State for Lattice Quantum Monte Carlo Simulations

> Nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) is an efficient \textit{ab initio} tool for solving nuclear many-body systems using the imaginary-time projection technique, where the preparation of trial states is essential for substantially reducing the computational cost required to achieve the desired numerical precision. It has been challenging in forming optimal multi-reference trial states using multiple Slater determinants within auxiliary-field based quantum Monte Carlo frameworks like NLEFT. In this work, we develop a novel sampling method for efficiently incorporating such multi-reference trial states into NLEFT calculations. We applied the optimized trial state to $^7$Li and $^8$Li, finding overall improvements in calculated energies, electromagnetic properties, and transitions compared to results obtained without these optimizations. Our approach provides a reliable foundation for accurately simulating nuclear ground and low-lying excited states within the NLEFT framework.

## Key claim

The authors develop a novel sampling method that efficiently incorporates multi-reference trial states (linear combinations of multiple Slater determinants) into Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) calculations, demonstrating overall improvements in calculated energies, electromagnetic properties, and transitions for \(^7\)Li and \(^8\)Li compared to results obtained without such optimizations, but the improvement is contingent on the sign problem being under good control for the specific nuclei studied.

## Method

The trial state is constructed from shell-model single-particle wave functions based on a deformed harmonic oscillator potential, with oscillator frequency \(\omega\) and deformation parameter \(\delta\) optimized first. Then, correlation functions between Slater determinants representing different valence nucleon configurations are calculated using a new algorithm (MultSlat-Sampler II) that propagates individual orbits rather than full Slater determinants, reducing computational cost from \(\mathcal{O}(N_S A)\) to \(\mathcal{O}(N_O)\) for propagation and from \(\mathcal{O}(N_S^2 A^2)\) to \(\mathcal{O}(N_O^2)\) for measurement. The coefficients of the multi-reference trial state are determined variationally by solving a generalized eigenvalue equation from the correlation functions at a chosen projection time \(\tau\).

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing \(a = 1.32\) fm used for all calculations.
- Cubic lattice size \(L = 14.52\) fm used without quantifying finite-volume uncertainties.
- For \(^7\)Li(3/2\(^-\)), the total number of Slater determinants \(N_S = 5\).
- The computational time required by the new method (MultSlat-Sampler II) is only 1.2 to 1.5 times more than the traditional method (MultSlat-Sampler I) for the same statistics.
- The comparison trial state uses \(\omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV and \(\delta_0 = 0\), with \(\omega_0\) phenomenologically determined from the mean square radius of spherical nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the NLEFT framework, which previously limited trial wave functions to single Slater determinants (except for projections onto total momentum and angular momentum as in Epelbaum (2009) and Shen (2023)), by incorporating multi-reference techniques common in beyond-mean-field calculations such as the generator coordinate method (Reinhard, 1987) and shell-model based ab initio methods. The approach enables more accurate simulations of nuclear ground and low-lying excited states, particularly for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei where dense low-lying states cause significant excited-state contamination, and builds on recent sign-problem mitigation developments including the leading-order interactions of Lu (2019) and Niu (2025).
