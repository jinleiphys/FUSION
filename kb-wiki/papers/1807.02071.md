# General thermodynamic equilibrium with axial chemical potential for the free Dirac field

> We calculate the constitutive equations of the stress-energy tensor and the currents of the free massless Dirac field at thermodynamic equilibrium with acceleration and rotation and a conserved axial charge by using the density operator approach. We carry out an expansion in thermal vorticity to the second order with finite axial chemical potential $μ_A$. The obtained coefficients of the expansion are expressed as correlators of angular momenta and boost operators with the currents. We confirm previous observations that the axial chemical potential induces non-vanishing components of the stress-energy tensor at first order in thermal vorticity due to breaking of parity invariance of the density operator with $μ_A \ne 0$. The appearance of these components might play an important role in chiral hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-zero axial chemical potential μ_A introduces new parity-breaking terms in the constitutive equations of the stress-energy tensor and currents for a free massless Dirac field at thermodynamic equilibrium with acceleration and rotation, specifically at first order in thermal vorticity, which would be absent for μ_A = 0.

## Method

The authors use the covariant density operator approach to describe global thermodynamic equilibrium, expanding the mean values of local operators in thermal vorticity to second order. They express the expansion coefficients as connected correlators of angular momentum and boost operators with the stress-energy tensor and currents, calculated using the homogeneous statistical operator in the imaginary time formalism. The calculation is performed for the free massless Dirac field with a conserved axial charge, treating the axial chemical potential as a parameter in the density operator.

## Key numbers

- The expansion in thermal vorticity is carried out to second order, with thermal vorticity being dimensionless and typically ≪ 1 under most circumstances such as relativistic heavy ion collisions.
- Three new chiral coefficients appear at first order in thermal vorticity: A, W1, and W2, defined as A = ⟨⟨K^3 (T^{12} - T^{21})/2⟩⟩_T, W1 = ⟨⟨J^3 T^{30}⟩⟩_T, and W2 = ⟨⟨J^3 T^{03}⟩⟩_T.
- The chiral vortical effect coefficient for the vector current is W^V = ⟨⟨J^3 j^3_V⟩⟩_T, and for the axial current W^A = ⟨⟨J^3 j^3_A⟩⟩_T.
- The conservation equation imposes the constraint -2A = |β| dW1/d|β| + 3W1 + W2 for the first-order chiral coefficients.
- For a massless spinor field, W^V must be proportional to |β|^{-3} to satisfy the conservation relation |β| dW^V/d|β| + 3W^V = 0.

## Context

This work extends the density operator approach previously developed by Becattini (2015) and Buzzegoli (2017) for deriving constitutive relations of the stress-energy tensor in the presence of acceleration and vorticity, now including a finite axial chemical potential. The results confirm earlier observations that axial chemical potential induces non-vanishing stress-energy tensor components at first order in thermal vorticity due to parity breaking, which may play an important role in chiral hydrodynamics and the implementation of chiral magnetohydrodynamics, particularly relevant for the Chiral Magnetic Effect studied by Kharzeev (2015) and the global polarization of Λ hyperons observed at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration (2017).
