# Nuclear Transition in the Strong Coupling Limit

> Lattice QCD at finite baryon chemical potential has the infamous sign problem which hinders Monte Carlo simulations. This can be remedied by a dual representation that makes the sign problem mild. In the strong coupling limit, the dual formulation with staggered quarks is well established. We have used this formulation to study the quark mass dependence of the baryon mass and the nuclear transition. This allows us to quantify the nuclear interaction. We have also compared our Monte Carlo results with mean field predictions.

## Key claim

The paper uses a dual formulation of lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit (infinite bare gauge coupling, beta = 0) to study the quark mass dependence of the baryon mass and the nuclear transition, quantifying the nuclear interaction and comparing Monte Carlo results with mean field predictions, but the results are limited to a fixed temperature of aT = 0.125 and the strong coupling limit far from the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors simulate the dual formulation of lattice QCD with staggered quarks at infinite bare gauge coupling (beta = 0), where the gauge integration is performed first followed by Grassmann integration to obtain a partition function in terms of color-singlet mesons (dimers and monomers) and baryons (self-avoiding loops). They use the worm algorithm to simulate the dual partition function, which has a mild sign problem that can be handled exactly via sign reweighting. The baryon mass is extracted from the free energy difference between configurations with and without a static baryon world line on an 8^3 x 8 lattice at aT = 0.125, and the nuclear transition is studied by measuring the baryon density and susceptibility as functions of the baryon chemical potential for different quark masses.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass increases with quark mass; the first order transition has a smaller gap beyond quark mass am_q = 1.5 and vanishes at am_q = 1.8.
- The best guess for the nuclear critical end point at aT = 0.125 is am_q^c = 1.7(1).
- The critical baryon chemical potential is taken as the value where baryon density n_B = 0.5 for crossover transitions.
- The nuclear interaction, quantified as (mu_Bc - m_B)/m_B, decreases as quark mass increases and tends to zero for large quark masses.
- The baryon mass from the dual formulation lies between the lower bound from 1-dimensional QCD and the upper bound from mean field theory.

## Context

This work extends previous strong coupling lattice QCD results for the nuclear transition at low quark masses and intermediate temperatures (Fromm 2010, arXiv:1611.09120) to higher quark masses and lower temperatures where the nuclear critical end point moves to large mu_B/T. The results are compared with mean field theory predictions based on a 1/d expansion (PhysRevD.69.094501) and with the baryon mass from hopping parameter expansion with Wilson fermions (HOEK 1982). The dual formulation results at large quark masses agree with the 3-dimensional effective theory based on hopping parameter expansion (Langelage 2014). Future work will include gauge corrections and extend simulations to two-flavor QCD using the continuous time limit.
