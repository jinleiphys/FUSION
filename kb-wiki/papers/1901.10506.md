# Initial state description of azimuthally collimated long range correlations in ultrarelativistic light-heavy ion collisions

> It was argued in arXiv:1805.09342 and arXiv:1807.00825 that the systematics of the azimuthal anisotropy coefficients $v_{2,3}$ measured in ultrarelativistic light-heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC can be described in an initial state dilute-dense Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. We elaborate here on the discussion in these papers and provide further novel results that strengthen their conclusions. The underlying mathematical framework and numerical techniques employed are very similar to those in the CGC based IP-Glasma model used previously as initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions. The uncertainties in theory/data comparisons for small systems are discussed, with unknowns that are specific to the model distinguished from those that are generic to all models. We present analytical arguments that demonstrate that quantum interference effects such as Bose enhancement and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations of gluons, as well as coherent multiple scattering of gluons in the projectile off color domains in the target, are enhanced in rare events. The quantum origins of the large anisotropies in small systems are corroborated by numerical results for deuteron-gold collisions that show that large anisotropies in rare configurations can occur when the nucleons in the projectile overlap significantly. This is at variance with the classical intuition of hydrodynamical models. We also comment on the consequences of ignoring the many-body color charge correlations of gluons in models that only consider geometrical fluctuations in the energy density.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the systematics of azimuthal anisotropy coefficients $v_{2,3}$ measured in ultrarelativistic light-heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC can be described within an initial-state dilute-dense Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, but the authors emphasize that this description depends on quantum interference effects (Bose enhancement, Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations, and coherent multiple scattering) that are enhanced in rare events, and that the uncertainties in theory/data comparisons include both model-specific and generic unknowns.

## Method

The authors employ the dilute-dense CGC framework, expanding the Yang-Mills equations to lowest nontrivial order in the projectile color charge density while retaining all orders in the target color charge density. They compute the parity-even and parity-odd contributions to gluon production using analytical expressions involving Wilson lines and color charge densities, then evaluate the two-particle anisotropy coefficients $v_n\{2\}$ by averaging over event-by-event color charge configurations sampled from Gaussian (McLerran-Venugopalan) weight functionals. The numerical implementation uses lattice gauge theory techniques identical to those in the IP-Glasma model, but without requiring real-time evolution of gauge fields.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energies considered: $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon for RHIC (p+Au, d+Au, $^3$He+Au) and $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV/nucleon for LHC (p+Pb)
- The $v_2$ and $v_3$ results are presented for the $0-5\%$ centrality class in p+Au, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon
- The $v_n$ coefficients are computed in $p_\perp$ bins of width $\Delta=0.5$ GeV, with results shown out to $p_\perp=2$ GeV
- Event-plane resolutions for experimental $v_n\{EP\}$ measurements in these systems are quoted as less than $10\%$
- The leading parity-even contribution to single inclusive gluon production scales as $(g^2 \rho_{\rm pr}/k_\perp^2)^2$, while the leading parity-odd contribution (giving odd $v_n$) scales as $(g^2 \rho_{\rm pr}/k_\perp^2)^3$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous results in Mace et al. (2018) (arXiv:1805.09342 and arXiv:1807.00825) that showed semi-quantitative agreement with PHENIX data on $v_{2,3}$ in p+Au, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions. The numerical framework is nearly identical to the IP-Glasma model of Schenke et al. (2012), but the dilute-dense approximation provides analytical expressions that avoid the need for real-time Yang-Mills evolution. The results contradict the classical intuition of hydrodynamic models (which would predict larger $v_2$ from larger spatial anisotropies at larger nucleon separations) and instead show that the largest $v_2$ contributions come from overlapping nucleon configurations. The paper also contrasts its approach with the Monte Carlo Glauber model of Miller et al. (2007) and with models that ignore many-body color charge correlations of gluons, such as that of Nagle et al. (2018).
