# Generalized Partial Dynamical Symmetry in Nuclei

> We introduce the notion of a generalized partial dynamical symmetry for which part of the eigenstates have part of the dynamical symmetry. This general concept is illustrated with the example of Hamiltonians with a partial dynamical O(6) symmetry in the framework of the interacting boson model. The resulting spectrum and electromagnetic transitions are compared with empirical data in $^{162}$Dy.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a "generalized partial dynamical symmetry" (GPDS) in which only a subset of eigenstates preserve only a subset of the dynamical symmetry chain, combining two previously separate types of partial dynamical symmetry. For the specific case of the O(6) chain in the interacting boson model, the authors construct a two-body Hamiltonian where the ground band is solvable with good O(6) symmetry but broken O(5) symmetry, while excited bands exhibit O(6) mixing.

## Method

The authors construct an IBM-1 Hamiltonian (H2) containing an O(6) pairing term and a boson-pair term, and analyze its algebraic structure using Casimir operators and intrinsic-state formalism. They compare the resulting spectrum and E2 transition rates for the nucleus 162Dy against those from a previously known partial dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian (H1) and against experimental data. Band-mixing calculations are performed using large-N intrinsic states to decompose the structure of excited bands into beta and gamma phonon components.

## Key numbers

- For 162Dy with boson number N=15, the H2 Hamiltonian parameters are h0=28.5 keV, h2=6.3 keV, and λ2=13.45 keV (rotational term)
- The K=2_1 band in H2 is 99.85% pure gamma phonon and 0.15% beta-gamma phonon; the K=0_2 band is 36.29% beta, 63.68% gamma^2(K=0), and 0.03% beta^2
- O(6) decomposition of the K=0_2 bandhead in H2: 85.50% σ=N, 14.45% σ=N-2, 0.05% σ=N-4
- O(6) decomposition of the K=2_3 band L=3+ member in H2: 50.36% σ=N, 49.25% σ=N-2, 0.38% σ=N-4, 0.01% σ=N-6
- Measured B(E2) ratio R1 = B(E2; 0+_K=0_2 → 2+_K=2_1)/B(E2; 0+_K=0_2 → 2+_K=0_1) = 10(5); H1 predicts 0.90, H2 predicts 75.09
- Measured B(E2) ratio R2 = B(E2; 2+_K=0_2 → 4+_K=0_1)/B(E2; 2+_K=0_2 → 0+_K=0_1) = 65(28); H1 predicts 3.76, H2 predicts 3.77
- B(E2; 2+_K=0_1 → 0+_K=0_1) = 1.07 e^2b^2 for both H1 and H2, matching the experimental value 1.07(2) e^2b^2

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) previously developed for the interacting boson model, where earlier work by Leviatan (1996) and Leviatan and Sinai (1999) demonstrated PDS of the first type (subset of states with full symmetry) for the SU(3) chain, and Van Isacker (1999) demonstrated PDS of the second type (all states with partial symmetry) for the O(6) chain. The generalized PDS introduced here combines both features and is shown to be relevant for axially deformed nuclei like 162Dy, where the pattern of interband E2 transitions from the K=0_2 band differs qualitatively from predictions of the earlier PDS Hamiltonian. The results enable future experimental measurements of absolute B(E2) values from the K=0_2 band to clarify which symmetry-breaking pattern is realized in nature.
