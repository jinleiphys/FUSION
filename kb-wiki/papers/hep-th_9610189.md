# Eliminating Infrared Divergences in the Pressure

> The pressure of a system in thermal equilibrium is expressed as a mass integral over a sum of thermal propagators. This allows a Dyson resummation and is used to demonstrate that potential infrared divergences are rendered harmless.

## Key claim

The pressure of a thermal system can be expressed as a mass integral over a sum of Dyson-resummed thermal propagators, which demonstrates that potential infrared divergences are rendered harmless even in nonabelian gauge theories where no thermal screening mass is generated for static chromomagnetic fields.

## Method

The authors derive an exact formula for the partition function by differentiating with respect to auxiliary masses introduced for all fields, expressing the pressure as an integral over the mass parameter of the imaginary part of the resummed propagator. They then separate the zero-mode contribution (identified with the imaginary-time formalism) from the non-zero mode part, and analyze the infrared behavior of each term using the spectral density and its properties at zero frequency.

## Key numbers

- Infrared divergences in the pressure set in at three-loop order in four-dimensional theories.
- The zero-mode contribution is infrared harmless as long as the number of spatial dimensions is greater than two.
- The singular piece of \(N(q^0,T)\) behaves as \(\epsilon(q^0)T/q^0\) near \(q^0=0\).
- The integral over the singular piece of \(N(q^0,T)\) times the spectral density yields \(-1/(q^2-m^2-\Pi_r(q,T))\) evaluated at \(q^0=0\).
- For large \(m^2\), \(I_r\) behaves as \(1/m^4\), assuming \({\rm Im}\,\Pi_r\) does not blow up.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of infrared divergences in finite-temperature field theory, which were first identified by Linde (1980) and Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) as an insurmountable barrier for perturbation theory in nonabelian gauge theories beyond three-loop order. The authors provide a new formula that circumvents the need for a thermal screening mass to remove infrared divergences, building on a conjecture by de Almeida and Frenkel (1993) in the ladder approximation. This enables a potentially improved perturbative evaluation of the pressure that goes beyond ordinary perturbation theory and avoids the no-go argument against a perturbative approach.
