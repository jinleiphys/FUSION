# Hadrons in Medium

> We review the current status of theories and experiments aiming at an understanding and a determination of the properties of light vector and scalar mesons inside strongly interacting hadronic matter, with an emphasis on linking calculated properties of hadrons in medium to actual observables.

## Key claim
The paper argues that even if theoretical calculations of in-medium hadron spectral functions were undisputed, extracting these spectral functions from actual experimental observables (invariant-mass distributions of decay products) is highly nontrivial due to entanglement with branching ratios, density-dependent suppression of high-density contributions, and final-state interactions.

## Method
The authors review theoretical approaches to calculate in-medium self-energies of vector and scalar mesons (ρ, ω, φ) and analyze how these relate to experimental observables. They derive expressions for the cross section of vector-meson production on nuclear targets using a Glauber approximation, showing how the imaginary part of the in-medium self-energy determines nuclear transparency. They then examine how the mass-dependent branching ratio for hadronic and dilepton decays, combined with the density profile of the nucleus, distorts the observed invariant-mass distribution relative to the true spectral function.

## Key numbers
- KEK-E325 pA experiment at 12 GeV: reports Δm/m = -9% for ρ and ω, Δm/m = -3.4% for φ, and Γ_φ(ρ₀)/Γ_φ = 3.6
- CLAS γA experiment (0.6-3.8 GeV): reports Δm ≈ 0 for ρ, ΔΓ ≈ 70 MeV at ρ ≈ ρ₀/2
- CBELSA/TAPS γA experiment (0.9-2.2 GeV): reports Δm ≈ 0 for ω, ΔΓ(ρ₀) ≈ 130 MeV for ω with ⟨p_ω⟩ = 1.1 GeV/c
- SPring8 γA experiment (1.5-2.4 GeV): reports ΔΓ(ρ₀) ≈ 70 MeV for φ with ⟨p_φ⟩ = 1.8 GeV/c
- For ω meson: vacuum width 8.4 MeV compared to in-medium width up to 80 MeV; the summed spectrum of these two Breit-Wigner distributions yields a fitted width of about 12 MeV
- In transport simulations for γ + ⁴⁰Ca at E_γ = 1.5 GeV, the fitted Breit-Wigner width of the π⁰γ spectrum stays nearly constant when the in-medium width is increased by a factor of 3

## Context
This review extends the authors' earlier comprehensive review (Leupold, Metag, Mosel, 2010) by focusing specifically on the problem of connecting theoretical in-medium self-energies to experimental observables. It addresses inconsistencies between experiments such as KEK-E325 (which reports mass shifts for ρ, ω, and φ), CLAS (which finds broadening but no mass shift for ρ), and CBELSA/TAPS (which initially claimed a dropping ω mass but could not reproduce this in re-analysis). The work enables future experiments at CLAS, HADES, PHENIX, and JPARC to properly account for the density-dependent suppression of high-density contributions and the mass-dependent branching ratio effects when extracting spectral functions from data.
