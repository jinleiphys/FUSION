# Cumulant dynamics in finite-memory diffusion

> Fluctuations of conserved charges are among the main proposed signatures of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) critical point, but their interpretation requires a dynamical description of how fluctuation correlators evolve during the finite lifetime of the quark--gluon plasma (QGP) fireball. The standard baseline for this evolution is Fickian diffusion, in which the diffusive current follows the local density gradient instantaneously. This instantaneous-current limit can miss delayed-response effects when the current-relaxation time becomes comparable to the relaxation time of the relevant fluctuation modes. In this work we extend this baseline to Maxwell--Cattaneo diffusion, where the current relaxes on a finite time scale and therefore retains memory. We derive closed evolution equations for multi-point Wigner functions and convert the freezeout correlators into acceptance-dependent cumulants along representative trajectories in the QCD phase diagram. While Fickian diffusion already causes the correlators to lag behind their instantaneous equilibrium values, finite current relaxation introduces an additional memory effect beyond this diffusive lag. As a result, current memory can suppress, shift, and reshape the non-monotonic behavior of the cumulants relative to both instantaneous equilibrium and Fickian diffusion, with the most visible effects appearing in higher-order cumulants and their ratios.

## Key claim

While Fickian diffusion already causes fluctuation correlators to lag behind their instantaneous equilibrium values, introducing a finite current-relaxation time in Maxwell-Cattaneo diffusion adds an additional memory effect that can suppress, shift, and reshape the non-monotonic behavior of cumulants, with the most visible effects appearing in higher-order cumulants and their ratios.

## Method

The authors formulate stochastic Maxwell-Cattaneo dynamics in an enlarged variable space containing both the charge density \(n\) and the diffusive current \(J\), then derive closed evolution equations for equal-time two-, three-, and four-point Wigner functions \(W_2, W_3, W_4\) from the nonlinear one-point equations using Itô causality. These Wigner functions are evolved along representative trajectories in the QCD phase diagram and converted into acceptance-dependent cumulants via Fourier-space integration over a finite longitudinal acceptance window \(\Delta\) at freezeout.

## Key numbers

* The acceptance window width is set to \(\Delta \simeq \tau \Delta\eta\) with \(\Delta\eta \approx \Delta y = 0.5\), where \(\tau\) is the proper time at freezeout.
* The characteristic wavenumber separating Fickian from memory-dominated regimes is \(q_\star \sim 1/(2\sqrt{\tau\gamma})\), where \(\tau\) is the current-relaxation time and \(\gamma\) is the diffusion coefficient.
* The Bjorken expansion time scale is \(\tau_h \sim 1\)–\(10\ \text{fm}\), over which the QGP expands and hadronizes.
* Near the critical point, the transport coefficient scales as \(\gamma \sim \xi^{-a}\) with \(a=2\) in Model B and \(a=1\) in Model H, where \(\xi\) is the correlation length.

## Context

This work extends the Fickian diffusion baseline for conserved-charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously developed by An et al. (2020, 2022), to Maxwell-Cattaneo diffusion where the current retains memory over a finite relaxation time. It builds on the concept of critical slowing down and delayed current response from Hydro+/++ frameworks by Stephanov (2017) and An et al. (2019), and on the treatment of the diffusive current as a dynamical variable in Ref. Du et al. (2021). The results enable a systematic comparison between Fickian and memory-modified cumulant evolution, providing a minimal laboratory for isolating how memory in the constitutive relation affects fluctuation observables before incorporating full critical dynamics.
