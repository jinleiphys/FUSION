# Non-Abelian Anyons and Non-Abelian Vortices in Topological Superconductors

> Anyons are particles obeying statistics of neither bosons nor fermions. Non-Abelian anyons, whose exchanges are described by a non-Abelian group acting on a set of wave functions, are attracting a great attention because of possible applications to topological quantum computations. Braiding of non-Abelian anyons corresponds to quantum computations. The simplest non-Abelian anyons are Ising anyons which can be realized by Majorana fermions hosted by vortices or edges of topological superconductors, $ν=5/2$ quantum Hall states, spin liquids, and dense quark matter. While Ising anyons are insufficient for universal quantum computations, Fibonacci anyons present in $ν=12/5$ quantum Hall states can be used for universal quantum computations. Yang-Lee anyons are non-unitary counterparts of Fibonacci anyons. Another possibility of non-Abelian anyons (of bosonic origin) is given by vortex anyons, which are constructed from non-Abelian vortices supported by a non-Abelian first homotopy group, relevant for certain nematic liquid crystals, superfluid $^3$He, spinor Bose-Einstein condensates, and high density quark matter. Finally, there is a unique system admitting two types of non-Abelian anyons, Majorana fermions (Ising anyons) and non-Abelian vortex anyons. That is $^3P_2$ superfluids (spin-triplet, $p$-wave paring of neutrons), expected to exist in neutron star interiors as the largest topological quantum matter in our universe.

## Key claim

Non-Abelian anyons can be classified into two distinct types, Ising anyons (from Majorana fermions) and vortex anyons (from non-Abelian first homotopy groups), and the $^3P_2$ superfluid in neutron star interiors is the only known system that simultaneously hosts both types, though Ising anyons alone are insufficient for universal quantum computation.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of non-Abelian anyons by examining their representation through braid group generators, fusion rules, and the $F$-matrix and $R$-matrix formalisms from conformal field theory. It then surveys physical realizations: Ising anyons as Majorana zero modes in topological superconductors and Kitaev spin liquids, Fibonacci anyons in $\nu=12/5$ quantum Hall states, Yang-Lee anyons from nonunitary conformal field theories, and vortex anyons from non-Abelian first homotopy groups in ordered states such as nematic liquid crystals, superfluid $^3$He, spinor Bose-Einstein condensates, and $^3P_2$ superfluids.

## Key numbers

- Ising anyons: the $R$-matrix for two $\sigma$ anyons fusing to ${\bf 1}$ or $\psi$ has diagonal entries $R^{\sigma\sigma}_{\bf 1}=1$ and $R^{\sigma\sigma}_{\psi}=i$ (multiplied by a common phase $e^{-i\pi/8}$), and the $F$-matrix is $F^{\sigma}_{\sigma\sigma\sigma} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}$.
- Fibonacci anyons: the $R$-matrix entries are $R^{\tau\tau}_{\bf 1}=e^{i4\pi/5}$ and $R^{\tau\tau}_{\tau}=-e^{i2\pi/5}$; the $F$-matrix uses the golden ratio $\phi=(1+\sqrt{5})/2$ with entries $\phi^{-1}$ and $\phi^{-1/2}$.
- Yang-Lee anyons: the central charge is $c=-22/5$ and the scaling dimension of the nontrivial primary field is $\Delta=-2/5$; the $R$-matrix entries are $R^{\tau\tau}_{\bf 1}=e^{i2\pi/5}$ and $R^{\tau\tau}_{\tau}=e^{i\pi/5}$.
- In a chiral superconductor vortex, the level spacing between the Majorana zero mode and the lowest excitation is $\Delta E \sim \Delta_0^2/\varepsilon_{\rm F}$; the hybridization band width between neighboring Majorana modes is $\Delta E_{\rm M} \sim e^{-D/\xi}$, where $D$ is the mean vortex distance and $\xi$ is the superconducting coherence length.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of non-Abelian anyons from the foundational work on braid groups by Wu (1984) and the classification of anyon models by Kitaev (2006) and Nayak et al. (2008). It contrasts Ising anyons (realized in Moore-Read states at $\nu=5/2$ quantum Hall filling, Kitaev's honeycomb model, and topological superconductors) with Fibonacci anyons (predicted in $\nu=12/5$ Read-Rezayi states) and the nonunitary Yang-Lee anyons. The paper uniquely highlights $^3P_2$ superfluids, expected in neutron star interiors, as the only system that simultaneously admits both Majorana-based Ising anyons and non-Abelian vortex anyons, enabling future studies of coexisting anyon types in the largest topological quantum matter in the universe.
