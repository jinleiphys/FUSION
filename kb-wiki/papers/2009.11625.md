# Mathematical aspects of phase rotation ambiguities in partial wave analyses

> The observables in a single-channel $2$-body scattering problem remain invariant once the amplitude is multiplied by an overall energy- and angle-dependent phase. This invariance is known as the continuum ambiguity. Also, mostly in truncated partial wave analyses (TPWAs), discrete ambiguities originating from complex conjugation of roots are known to occur. In this note, it is shown that the general continuum ambiguity mixes partial waves and that for scalar particles, discrete ambiguities are just a subset of continuum ambiguities with a specific phase. A numerical method is outlined briefly, which can determine the relevant connecting phases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the general continuum ambiguity (an energy- and angle-dependent phase rotation of the full amplitude) mixes partial waves via a derived mixing formula, and that for scalar particles the discrete ambiguities known from truncated partial wave analyses are merely a subset of continuum ambiguities with a specific phase, not a separate class.

## Method

The author derives a mixing formula for partial waves under a general angle-dependent phase rotation by expanding the rotation function in Legendre polynomials and using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. For truncated polynomial amplitudes, the Gersten formalism of root complex conjugation is shown to produce phase rotation functions that are rational functions of cos(theta). A numerical functional minimization method is then outlined that scans for all unimodular rotation functions that preserve the truncation order of the original amplitude, demonstrating that only the Gersten rotations satisfy these constraints.

## Key numbers

* For a toy model truncated at L=2 with partial waves A0=5, A1=0.4+0.3i, A2=0.02+0.01i (arbitrary units), there are 2^2 = 4 Gersten ambiguity rotations (including the identity).
* The number of discrete Gersten ambiguities for a truncated amplitude at cutoff L is 2^L, rising exponentially with L.
* The functional minimization procedure converged to the correct Gersten rotations after up to 500 iterations of the minimizer, independent of the choice of initial function.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of continuum ambiguities by Bowcock and Burkhardt and discrete ambiguities by Gersten, and the mixing formula is noted as not previously appearing in the literature in this form. The paper connects to earlier work on mixing phenomena in pi-N scattering by Dean and Lee and in photoproduction by Omelaenko. The results enable a unified understanding of ambiguities in partial wave analyses and provide a numerical tool to verify that Gersten's root-conjugation scheme exhausts all discrete ambiguities for truncated models, with potential generalization to reactions with spin such as pseudoscalar meson photoproduction.
