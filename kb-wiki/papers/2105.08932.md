# Reentrant pion superfluidity and cosmic trajectories within PNJL model

> In this work, we self-consistently explore the possibility of charged pion superfluidity and cosmic trajectories in early Universe under the framework of Polyakov-Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. By taking the badly constrained lepton flavor asymmetries $l_{\rm e}$ and $l_μ$ as free parameters, the upper boundaries of pion superfluidity phase are consistently found to be around the pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potentials. So the results greatly support the choice of $T=0.16~{\rm GeV}$ as the upper boundary of pion superfluidity in the previous lattice QCD study. Take $l_{\rm e}+l_μ=-0.2$ as an example, we demonstrate the features of pion condensation and the associated cosmic trajectories with the evolution of early Universe. While the trajectory of electric chemical potential reacts strongly at both the lower and upper boundaries of reentrant pion superfluidity, the trajectories of other chemical potentials only respond strongly at the upper boundary.

## Key claim

The upper boundary of charged pion superfluidity in the early Universe is consistently found to be around the pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potentials (approximately 0.21 GeV in the PNJL model), which supports the previous lattice QCD choice of T = 0.16 GeV as the upper boundary, but the exact value depends on the model's treatment of the deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors self-consistently solve the coupled gap equations of the two-flavor and three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, including the Polyakov loop potential fitted to lattice QCD data, to compute the charged pion condensate, constituent quark masses, and Polyakov loop as functions of temperature. The QCD sector is coupled to a free gas description of the quantum electroweak dynamics (QEWD) sector containing electrons, muons, neutrinos, and photons, with lepton flavor asymmetries l_e and l_mu treated as free parameters under the constraints of baryon number density and electric charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- The threshold lepton flavor asymmetry for pion superfluidity is |l_e + l_mu| ~ 0.09 at T = 0.16 GeV, compared to ~0.1 in extrapolated lattice QCD calculations.
- The upper boundaries of pion superfluidity are consistently found at T ~ 0.21 GeV in both two-flavor and three-flavor PNJL models.
- The threshold lepton flavor asymmetries are 0.08 (two-flavor) and 0.075 (three-flavor) at the top temperature.
- The muon mass is taken as m_mu = 113 MeV; the electron mass is set to zero.
- Model parameters for two-flavor PNJL: m_0l = 5 MeV, Lambda = 653 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 2.10.
- Model parameters for three-flavor PNJL: m_0l = 5.5 MeV, m_0s = 140.7 MeV, Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 1.835, K Lambda^5 = 12.36.

## Context

This work extends the study of charged pion superfluidity in the early Universe previously explored using hadron resonance gas models, lattice QCD, and free quark gas models by Wygas (2018) and Vovchenko (2020), who could not predict the upper phase boundary due to insufficient treatment of temperature effects on the pion mass. The PNJL model allows self-consistent calculation of the true order parameter (charged pion condensate) rather than relying on the criterion |mu_Q| > m_pi, and the results support the lattice QCD choice of T = 0.16 GeV as the upper boundary made by Vovchenko (2020). The findings enable more realistic modeling of cosmic trajectories and the impact of pion superfluidity on primordial gravitational waves and black hole generation in the QCD epoch.
