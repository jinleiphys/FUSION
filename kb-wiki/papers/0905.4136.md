# The exact three-dimensional half-shell t-matrix for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential in the screening limit

> The three-dimensional half-shell t-matrix for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential is analytically derived together with its asymptotic form without reference to partial wave expansion. The numerical solutions of the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation for increasing cut-off radii provide half-shell t-matrices which are in quite a good agreement with the asymptotic values.

## Key claim

The half-shell t-matrix for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential converges in the screening limit to the pure Coulomb half-shell t-matrix multiplied by a renormalisation factor, plus an additional oscillatory term that does not vanish pointwise as the cut-off radius goes to infinity but is expected to disappear only after integration over angular intervals in the sense of a distribution.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the three-dimensional half-shell t-matrix for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential using the exact three-dimensional wave function inside the potential range, without partial wave expansion. They evaluated the resulting integrals in the screening limit (cut-off radius R → ∞) using contour integration and the method of steepest descent, treating separately the cases p' > p and p' < p. They then solved the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation numerically for finite cut-off radii by generating the Neumann series and summing it with Padé approximants, and compared the numerical half-shell t-matrices to the asymptotic analytical forms.

## Key numbers

- The system studied is two protons scattering solely by the Coulomb force at laboratory energy E_p^lab = 13 MeV, corresponding to q_0 ≈ 0.396 fm⁻¹.
- Cut-off radii of R = 80 fm and R = 200 fm were used for numerical comparisons.
- For R = 80 fm, the real part of the half-shell t-matrix is typically one order of magnitude larger than the imaginary part, except at x = 0.62 where both parts are comparable.
- The analytical asymptotic form agrees very well with numerical results for the real part in the region q' < q_0, and less satisfactorily for q' > q_0.
- For R = 200 fm, the real part is clearly dominant for all considered values of x (x = -0.91, -0.50, 0.03, 0.62, 0.90, corresponding to angles θ = 155.5°, 120.0°, 88.3°, 51.7°, and 25.8°).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical derivation of the three-dimensional wave function and on-shell t-matrix for a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential (Glockle et al., 2009) to the half-shell case. It addresses the discontinuous property of the pure Coulomb half-shell t-matrix at the on-shell point, previously discussed by Ford (1964, 1966) using partial wave decomposition, which the authors argue may have been incomplete due to difficulties in summing infinite partial wave components. The results enable future studies of Coulomb effects in few-nucleon scattering without relying on partial wave expansions, and provide a rigorous foundation for understanding the screening limit in momentum-space calculations.
