# On pionic hydrogen. Quantum field theoretic, relativistic covariant and model-independent approach

> We consider pionic hydrogen A_(pi p), the bound pi^- p state. Within the quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant approach we calculate the shift and width of the energy level of the ground state of pionic hydrogen caused by strong low-energy interactions treated perturbatively. The generalization of the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBT) formulas (Phys. Rev. 96, 774 (1954)) is given. The generalized DGBT formulas for the energy level displacement of the ground state of pionic hydrogen contain the non-perturbative and model-independent correction about 1%, caused by the relativistic covariant smearing of the wave function of the ground state around origin. This non-perturbative correction is very important for the precise extraction of the S-wave scattering lengths of pion-nucleon scattering from the experimental data on the energy level displacements in pionic hydrogen by the PSI Collaboration. In addition the shift of the energy level of the ground state of pionic hydrogen is improved by the second order correction of strong low-energy interactions which is about 0.1%. This testifies the applicability of the perturbative treatment of strong low-energy interactions to the analysis of pionic hydrogen. We show that the width of the energy level of the ground state of pionic hydrogen is valid to all order of perturbation theory in strong low-energy interactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant generalization of the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBT) formulas for the energy level shift and width of pionic hydrogen, finding a non-perturbative and model-independent correction of about 1% due to relativistic smearing of the ground-state wave function, which is important for precise extraction of S-wave pion-nucleon scattering lengths from experimental data.

## Method

The authors treat strong low-energy interactions perturbatively within a quantum field theoretic framework, using an effective Lagrangian for strong interactions without specifying its form. They calculate the energy level shift to first and second order in perturbation theory and the width of the ground state of the bound pi- p system, expressing results in terms of the S-wave scattering lengths a0^{1/2} and a0^{3/2} for pion-nucleon scattering with isotopic spin I=1/2 and I=3/2, respectively. The wave function of pionic hydrogen is defined in a relativistic covariant momentum representation, and the relativistic smearing correction arises from the factor sqrt(m_pi- m_p / E_pi-(k) E_p(k)) in the momentum integrals.

## Key numbers

* Bohr radius of pionic hydrogen: a_B = 222.664 fm, computed from reduced mass mu = 121.497 MeV (using m_pi- = 139.570 MeV, m_p = 938.272 MeV, and fine-structure constant alpha = 1/137.036)
* Relative momentum for the decay A_{pi p} -> pi^0 + n: p* = 28.040 MeV
* Non-perturbative relativistic smearing correction to the DGBT formulas: delta^{(s)}_{1s} = -9.69 x 10^{-3} (about 0.969%)
* Second-order correction to the energy level shift relative to the first-order shift: delta^{(2)}_{1s} = (0.111 +/- 0.006)% (from Section 8 using PSI experimental data)
* Ground-state binding energy scale: E_{1s} = -alpha / (2 a_B)

## Context

This work extends the original DGBT formulas (Deser, Goldberger, Baumann, Thirring, 1954) derived within a non-relativistic potential approach, providing a fully quantum field theoretic and relativistic covariant derivation. It complements the quantum field theoretical analysis of QCD isospin-breaking and electromagnetic corrections by Lyubovitskij and Rusetsky (2000) and Lyubovitskij et al. (2001). The results enable more precise extraction of S-wave pion-nucleon scattering lengths from experimental data by the PSI Collaboration, whose current precision is about 0.2% for the shift and 1% for the width of the ground-state energy level.
