# Form factors in RQM approaches: constraints from space-time translations

> Different relativistic quantum mechanics approaches have recently been used to calculate properties of various systems, form factors in particular. It is known that predictions, which most often rely on a single-particle current approximation, can lead to predictions with a very large range. It was shown that accounting for constraints related to space-time translations could considerably reduce this range. It is shown here that predictions can be made identical for a large range of cases. These ones include the following approaches: instant form, front form, and "point-form" in arbitrary momentum configurations and a dispersion-relation approach which can be considered as the approach which the other ones should converge to. This important result supposes both an implementation of the above constraints and an appropriate single-particle-like current. The change of variables that allows one to establish the equivalence of the approaches is given. Some points are illustrated with numerical results for the ground state of a system consisting of scalar particles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that form factor predictions from different relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) approaches (instant form, front form, point form) can be made identical to each other and to a dispersion-relation approach, but only if constraints from space-time translation covariance are implemented and an appropriate single-particle-like current is used; the equivalence requires a specific change of variables that the authors derive.

## Method

The authors derived a factor α that multiplies the momentum transfer q in each RQM approach (instant, front, and point form) to enforce the equality (p_i − p_f)² = q², which is required by Poincaré space-time translation covariance but is violated in standard RQM calculations using a one-body current. They then expressed the form factors F₁(Q²) and F₀(Q²) for a J=0 system of two scalar constituents in the Breit frame, using the modified momentum transfer, and showed that after a change of variables the RQM expressions become identical to those from the dispersion-relation approach of Krutov and Troitsky (2002).

## Key numbers

- The violation of the equality (p_i − p_f)² = q² in the point-form approach reaches a factor of 35000 at Q² = 100 (GeV/c)² for a pion-like system, as reported from prior work (Desplanques, 2004).
- The smallest violation was observed for the standard instant form (Breit frame) where it amounts to a few percent, and for the standard front form (q⁺ = 0) where it is absent.
- The normalization N for the form factors is given by N = ∫ ds √[(s − 4m²)/s] φ²(s), with the condition F₁(0) = 1.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Amghar (2002) and Desplanques (2004) that showed large discrepancies between RQM form factor predictions and that implementing space-time translation constraints could reduce the range. It directly addresses the failure of different RQM forms to converge, which was demonstrated for the pion charge form factor by Junhe (2004) and for nucleon form factors by Julia-Diaz (2004) and Plessas (2004). The dispersion-relation approach of Krutov and Troitsky (2002) is identified as the convergence point that the other RQM approaches should approach. The results enable consistent form factor calculations across all RQM forms without requiring explicit many-body currents, provided the translation constraints are enforced.
