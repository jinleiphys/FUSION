# Threshold meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> A brief overview on recent model calculations for near threshold pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is given. Results from our own investigations of the reactions $pp \rightarrow ppπ^0$ and $pn \rightarrow dπ^0$ are presented. Direct production, heavy meson exchange and pion rescattering are taken into account. For the latter a T-matrix obtained from a microscopic model of the $πN$ interaction is employed. We obtain a significant contribution from rescattering, but not enough to describe the data for $pp \to ppπ^0$. The missing production rate can be provided by heavy meson exchanges. For the first time the effect of off--shell rescattering is investigated for the reaction $pn \rightarrow dπ^0$. Isoscalar rescattering in combination with isovector rescattering is able to describe the s-wave production data. We confirm that heavy meson exchanges are negligible in this reaction.

# Threshold meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

## Key claim

The authors show that both off-shell pion rescattering and heavy meson exchange contributions are necessary to reproduce the experimental cross section for $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ near threshold, contradicting earlier claims that either mechanism alone could account for the data. For $pn \rightarrow d\pi^0$, isoscalar rescattering (negligible in on-shell approximations) provides a significant 50% enhancement and is essential for agreement with experiment, while heavy meson exchanges play only a minor role.

## Method

The authors perform momentum-space calculations of the reactions $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ and $pn \rightarrow d\pi^0$ near threshold using time-ordered perturbation theory, with the Bonn OBEPT potential for the NN interaction and a microscopic meson-exchange model of the $\pi N$ interaction for the rescattering contributions. Direct pion production, s-wave pion rescattering (using a half-off-shell T-matrix from the $\pi N$ model), and heavy meson exchange (sigma and omega mesons) are included, with distortions in initial and final NN states taken into account.

## Key numbers

- For $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$, direct production alone underestimates the experimental cross section by a factor of about 20; adding rescattering increases the cross section by a factor of around 10 but still underestimates data by a factor of 2.
- The sigma meson coupling constant required to reproduce the $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ data is $g^2_{\sigma}/4\pi = 5.7$ with cutoff $\Lambda_\sigma = 1700$ MeV, nearly identical to the value in the full Bonn NN model.
- For $pn \rightarrow d\pi^0$, the predicted cross section coefficient $\alpha$ (from $\sigma = \frac{1}{2}(\alpha \eta + \beta \eta^3)$) is 204 $\mu$b, compared to the experimental value of $184 \pm 5 \pm 13$ $\mu$b from Hutcheon et al.
- The isoscalar rescattering contribution enhances the $pn \rightarrow d\pi^0$ cross section by about 50% relative to isovector rescattering alone.
- The $\pi N$ s-wave scattering lengths from the model are $a_1 = 0.173 m_\pi^{-1}$ and $a_3 = -0.084 m_\pi^{-1}$, yielding $\lambda_1 = -0.001$.
- The typical momentum transfer at pion production threshold is 370 MeV/c, corresponding to NN separations of roughly 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the model of Koltun and Reitan (1966) by incorporating a realistic microscopic $\pi N$ interaction model (from the Julich group) to treat off-shell rescattering, and by including heavy meson exchange currents as proposed by Lee and Riska (1993). It addresses the controversy between Hernandez and Oset (1995), who argued that off-shell rescattering alone could explain the $pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0$ data, and chiral perturbation theory studies (1995-1996) that found heavy meson exchanges insufficient. The results enable future studies of the energy dependence of the cross section and of other $NN \rightarrow NN\pi$ channels to disentangle the short-range NN interaction from the off-shell $\pi N$ properties.
