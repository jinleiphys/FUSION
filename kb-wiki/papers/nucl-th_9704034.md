# Yang-Mills Radiation in Ultra-relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The classical Yang-Mills radiation computed in the McLerran-Venugopalan model is shown to be equivalent to the gluon bremsstrahlung distribution to lowest order in pQCD. The classical distribution is also shown to match smoothly onto the conventional pQCD mini-jet distribution at a scale characteristic of the initial parton transverse density of the system. The atomic number and energy dependence of that scale is computed from available structure function information. The limits of applicability of the classical Yang-Mills description of nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies are discussed.

## Key claim

The classical Yang-Mills radiation distribution from the McLerran-Venugopalan model is shown to be equivalent to the lowest-order (g^6) pQCD gluon bremsstrahlung distribution, and both match smoothly onto the conventional pQCD mini-jet distribution at a scale k_perp^2 ~ chi, where chi is the initial parton transverse density of the system; however, the classical derivation neglects nonlinearities that strongly distort the perturbative solution below k_perp^2 < alpha^2 chi, limiting its applicability.

## Method

The authors derived the classical Yang-Mills radiation distribution for two colliding nuclei by solving the classical equations of motion in the forward light cone with boundary conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, using a Gaussian ensemble for the color source density with a rapidity-dependent charge squared per unit area mu^2(y). They compared this classical result to the pQCD bremsstrahlung formula of Bertsch and Gunion (1976) and to the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin (GLR) formula, and computed the energy and atomic number dependence of the density parameter chi from available structure function information using DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

- The classical radiation distribution scales as dN/dy d^2k_perp ~ 1/k_perp^4 times a logarithmic factor L(k_perp, lambda) ~ log(k_perp^2/lambda^2), where lambda ~ alpha sqrt(chi) is the color screening scale.
- The parameter sqrt(chi) approaches on the order of 1 GeV by LHC energies (sqrt(s)=6500 AGeV) for A~200.
- The matching between bremsstrahlung and mini-jet distributions occurs at k_perp ~ sqrt(chi), where both results agree at the level of 20-40%.
- The GLR normalization factor K_N required to match the classical result is K_N = (2pi)^2/(N_c^2-1) ≈ 5.
- For RHIC energies (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV), the relevant energy scale is specified.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and corrects the classical derivation of Kovchegov, McLerran, and Weigert (1996) by properly accounting for contact terms and omitted factors of 2 and 2pi. The paper shows that the classical Yang-Mills bremsstrahlung, the pQCD bremsstrahlung of Bertsch and Gunion (1976), and the GLR formulation (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin, 1983) are equivalent up to form factor effects, resolving apparent discrepancies between these approaches. This enables improved estimates of initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC, and suggests that beam jet bremsstrahlung could dominate the few GeV transverse momentum region, modifying proposed signatures of the quark-gluon plasma.
