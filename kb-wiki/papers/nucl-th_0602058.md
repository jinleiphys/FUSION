# Two models with rescattering for high energy heavy ion collisions

> The effects of hadronic rescattering in RHIC-energy Au+Au collisions are studied using two very different models to describe the early stages of the collision. One model is based on a hadronic thermal picture and the other on a superposition of parton-parton collisions. Operationally, the output hadrons from each of these models are used as input to a hadronic rescattering calculation. The results of the rescattering calculations from each model are then compared with rapidity and transverse momentum distributions from the RHIC BRAHMS experiment. It is found that in spite of the different points of view of the two models of the initial stage, after rescattering the observed differences between the models are mostly "washed out" and both models give observables that agree reasonably well with each other and experiment.

## Key claim

After hadronic rescattering, the observable differences between a thermal-like model and a parton-parton collision model for the initial stage of RHIC-energy Au+Au collisions are mostly "washed out," and both models give observables that agree reasonably well with each other and with BRAHMS experimental data, though the paper does not claim that either model uniquely describes the initial state.

## Method

The authors generated initial hadron distributions from two distinct models: a three-source thermal model with Boltzmann energy distributions and a partonic jet model based on binary parton-parton collisions with jet fragmentation. The output hadrons from each model were then used as input to a semi-classical Monte Carlo hadronic rescattering calculation that simulated strong binary collisions, resonance decays, and freeze-out over a time evolution of 100 fm/c. The final rapidity and transverse momentum distributions from both model chains were compared with each other and with data from the RHIC BRAHMS experiment.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the thermal model set to T = 200 MeV and later increased to T = 270 MeV.
- Hadronization proper time parameter used in the rescattering calculation: τ_had = 1 fm/c.
- Inverse slope parameters from the thermal model at T = 270 MeV: pions at y=0 after rescattering have slope 195 ± 1 MeV; kaons at y=0 after rescattering have slope 267 ± 5 MeV; nucleons at y=0 after rescattering have slope 449 ± 13 MeV.
- Inverse slope parameters from the thermal model at T = 270 MeV: pions at y=3 after rescattering have slope 195 ± 2 MeV; kaons at y=3 after rescattering have slope 259 ± 7 MeV; nucleons at y=3 after rescattering have slope 313 ± 9 MeV.
- Partonic jet model used a jet p_t cutoff of p_t,cut = 0.3 GeV/c.
- Pion coalescence cutoff in the partonic model: -Q²_inv = 0.04² GeV².
- Rescattering calculation for central collisions (b=0 fm) produced at freezeout about 5000 pions, 500 kaons, and 650 nucleons per event.

## Context

This work extends earlier rescattering calculations by Humanic (2002, 2006) that used a simpler thermal model for RHIC collisions, by introducing a more elaborate thermal model and a second, parton-based initial-stage model. The study addresses the question of how much hadronic rescattering obscures information about the early collision stage, and it enables future work to test whether other initial-state models (such as HIJING, which the partonic model resembles) also converge after rescattering. The results are compared with BRAHMS data from Peter (2003) and Djamel (2004) and with PHOBOS multiplicity measurements from Back (2000).
