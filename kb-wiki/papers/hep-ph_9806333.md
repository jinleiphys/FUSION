# Studies of structure functions at a low-energy facility

> We discuss the studies on structure functions at the possible future RCNP facility. At this stage, an electron-proton or proton-proton collider with sqrt{s}=5 - 10 GeV is considered. We explain large-x physics, nuclear modification of sea-quark and gluon distributions, and tensor spin structure function as the interesting topics at the facility. The large-x parton distributions are important for finding new physics beyond QCD in anomalous events such as the CDF jet data. The nuclear parton distributions are valuable in detecting a quark-gluon signature in heavy-ion reactions. The tensor structure function b_1 is a new field of high-energy spin physics. Considering these physics possibilities, we believe that the possible RCNP facility is important for the hadron-structure community.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a proposed low-energy (sqrt(s)=5-10 GeV) electron-proton or proton-proton collider at RCNP could make unique contributions to hadron-structure physics, despite being unable to compete with higher-energy facilities on small-x measurements. The authors hedge that the energy range is not yet fixed and that the facility's value depends critically on achieving sufficiently high intensity to measure small quantities.

## Method

The authors review three physics topics that could be investigated at the proposed RCNP facility: large-x parton distributions (x>0.01), nuclear modifications of sea-quark and gluon distributions, and the tensor spin structure function b_1 for spin-1 hadrons. For each topic, they discuss the relevant kinematic reach of the proposed collider, compare with existing experimental data from SLAC, NMC, E665, E772, and CDF, and identify specific measurements that would be feasible at RCNP but not at higher-energy facilities.

## Key numbers

- The proposed collider has center-of-mass energy sqrt(s)=5-10 GeV, giving a minimum Bjorken x of x_min ~ 0.01 for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2
- The CDF anomalous jet events at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV showed ~30% deviation from NLO QCD predictions in the jet transverse energy region E_T > 300 GeV
- The contributing partons in the CDF events have momentum fraction x_{1,2} ~ 2 E_T/sqrt(s) ~ 0.4 for E_T ~ 350 GeV
- The CTEQ4HJ gluon distribution at x>0.4 and Q^2=4 GeV^2 is significantly larger than the CTEQ3M distribution, explaining the CDF data
- The b_1 structure function is expected to be very small, with b_1/F_1 ~ 0.01 in a naive quark model for the deuteron
- The E772 Drell-Yan data for nuclear sea-quark distributions show carbon data above unity and calcium data below unity at x ~ 0.1

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of structure function measurements from high-energy facilities (HERA, RHIC, polarized HERA) to a proposed lower-energy facility at RCNP. It builds on the EMC results (1983) showing nuclear modification of parton distributions, the CDF anomalous jet events that motivated large-x gluon measurements, and the theoretical work on the b_1 structure function by the authors and collaborators (cited as "ck" and "elfeb1" in the text). The paper enables future experimental proposals for measuring nuclear sea-quark and gluon distributions in the x>0.1 region, and for measuring the tensor structure function b_1, which the HERMES collaboration is expected to report on but with insufficient accuracy.
