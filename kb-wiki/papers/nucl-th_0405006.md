# Improved analysis of neutral pion electroproduction off deuterium in chiral perturbation theory

> Near threshold neutral pion electroproduction on the deuteron is studied in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We include the next-to-leading order corrections to the three-body contributions. We find an improved description of the total and differential cross section data measured at MAMI. We also obtain more precise values for the threshold S-wave multipoles. We discuss in detail the theoretical uncertainties of the calculation.

## Key claim

Including next-to-leading (fourth) order corrections to the three-body contributions in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory yields an improved but only moderately better description of total and differential cross section data for neutral pion electroproduction off deuterium near threshold, with the theoretical uncertainty reduced compared to the earlier third-order calculation of KBM2.

## Method

The authors calculated the 59 non-vanishing fourth-order three-body diagrams (tree graphs with one insertion from the dimension-two chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian and diagrams with four-nucleon contact interactions) and sandwiched the resulting transition operators between deuteron wave functions generated from chiral nuclear effective field theory at NNLO. They performed two types of fits to the MAMI data: fit type 1 with one free parameter (a fifth-order low-energy constant \(a_5^n\)) and a constraint from a leading-order low-energy theorem, and fit type 2 with two free parameters (the low-energy constants \(a_3^n\) and \(a_4^n\)) for the elementary neutron amplitude. The theoretical uncertainty was estimated by varying the cutoff in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation between 450 and 650 MeV and by comparing the two fit procedures.

## Key numbers

- Photon virtuality: \(k^2 = -0.1\) GeV\(^2\) (the value at which MAMI data were taken)
- Pion excess energy range: \(\Delta W = 0.5\) to \(3.5\) MeV
- Fitted low-energy constant \(a_3^n\) from fit type 2: 4.148 GeV\(^{-4}\) (for cutoff \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV) and 4.140 GeV\(^{-4}\) (for \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV)
- Fitted low-energy constant \(a_4^n\) from fit type 2: \(-7.085\) GeV\(^{-4}\) (for \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV) and \(-6.730\) GeV\(^{-4}\) (for \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV)
- Fitted low-energy constant \(a_5^n\) from fit type 1: \(-38.71\) GeV\(^{-5}\) (for \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV) and \(-37.05\) GeV\(^{-5}\) (for \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV)
- Four-nucleon LEC \(C_{1S0}\) from NN scattering: \(-0.149 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (for \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV) and \(-0.151 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (for \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV)
- Four-nucleon LEC \(C_{3S1}\) from NN scattering: \(-0.130 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (for \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV) and \(-0.168 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (for \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV)
- Resonance saturation estimate for \(a_3^n\): \(-4.11\) GeV\(^{-4}\)
- Value of \(c_4\) used: \(3.4\) GeV\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier third-order calculation of neutral pion electroproduction off deuterium by KBM2 (Krebs, Bernard, Meissner, 2003) by including fourth-order three-body corrections, achieving the same theoretical precision as the successful analysis of coherent neutral pion photoproduction off the deuteron by Beane, Bedaque, Lee, and van Kolck (2000). The improved framework enables more precise extraction of the elementary neutron amplitude and threshold S-wave multipoles, and the authors note that further improvement awaits the availability of fourth-order pion-nucleon P-wave multipoles for the electroproduction case, which are under development by Bernard, Krebs, and Meissner.
