# The dd -> 4He eta Reaction Near Threshold

> The cross section for the reaction $dd\rightarrow \,^4\!H\!e\,η$ close to threshold is estimated in a two-step model, similar to that which successfully describes near-threshold $pd\rightarrow \,^3\!H\!e\,η$ production. A $π$-meson, created in a $dp\rightarrow \,^3\!H\!e\,π^o$ process on one nucleon in the target deuteron, is converted into an $η$-meson by a secondary $π^o n \to n\,η$ reaction on the other nucleon. The kinematics are such that both processes are nearly physical so that only small Fermi momenta are required. The predictions of the model are in good agreement with recent Saclay experimental data. An analogous model, when applied to the near-threshold $n\,^3\!H\!e\rightarrow \,^4\!H\!e\,η$ reaction, predicts a cross section of a rather similar size, but experimental difficulties render this a doubtful means of studying the $η\, ^4\!H\!e$ interaction.

# The dd → ⁴He η Reaction Near Threshold

## Key claim

The cross section for dd → ⁴He η near threshold is estimated using a two-step model where a pion created in a dp → ³He π⁰ process on one nucleon is converted into an η via π⁰ n → n η on the other nucleon; the model predicts a threshold amplitude squared of (44 ± 8) nb/sr, which is slightly higher than the experimental value of about 30 nb/sr, in contrast to the pd → ³He η case where the model underpredicted the data by a factor of 2.8.

## Method

The two-step model treats the reaction as a virtual pion beam: a dp → ³He π⁰ reaction on one nucleon in the target deuteron produces a π⁰, which then undergoes π⁰ n → n η on the other nucleon. The nuclear matrix element is written as an integral over the ⁴He and deuteron Fermi momenta; the propagator is linearized to first order in Fermi momenta, reducing the six-dimensional integral to a single integral over a configuration-space form factor. Final-state interactions are treated using an effective wave technique with four scattering centers placed at tetrahedron vertices, with the η-nucleon scattering length taken as a = (0.476 + 0.279i) fm.

## Key numbers

- Threshold laboratory deuteron kinetic energy: 1.12 GeV
- Helicity m = 1 differential cross section for pd → ³He π⁰ at θ_cm = 180°: (1.0 ± 0.1) μb/sr
- π⁻ p → η n threshold amplitude squared: (680 ± 60) μb/sr
- Transition form factor magnitude |S| = 11.2 fm⁻² at threshold
- Predicted threshold amplitude squared for dd → ⁴He η: (44 ± 8) nb/sr
- Experimental threshold value: about 30 nb/sr
- Final-state enhancement factor |Ω|² = 1.9 at threshold
- Zero-Fermi-momentum energy difference ΔE₀ = −135 MeV for dd → ⁴He η
- For n ³He → ⁴He η: predicted amplitude squared (21 ± 4) nb/sr; neutron laboratory kinetic energy at threshold 0.75 GeV; form factor |S_S − √2 S_D| = 17.3 fm²

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to pd → ³He η (Faeldt and Wilkin, 1995, referred to as I) to the heavier ⁴He system, following measurements at Saturne by the SPESIV (Roudot) and SPESIII (Amina) groups. The model builds on ideas by Germond and by Kilian and Nann regarding the "kinematic miracle" where both sub-processes proceed near the physical energy shell. The near-threshold energy variation is dominated by final-state interactions reflecting η-nucleus dynamics, which should be largely independent of the entrance channel. The work also estimates the n ³He → ⁴He η reaction as an alternative means to study the η-⁴He interaction, but concludes experimental difficulties (broad-band neutron beam or radioactive tritium target) make it less competitive than the dd reaction.
