# Quark stars in $D_3$-$D_7$ holographic model

> This work investigates static and dynamical quark star properties within a $D_3-D_7$ holographic model. We solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the quark matter equation of state obtained from the brane configuration. We determine the mass-radius diagram for a range of model parameters and compare with recent NICER observational data for the pulsars PSR J$0030+0451$ and PSR J$0740+6620$. Motivated by the GW170817 event detected by the LIGO-Virgo collaboration, we also calculate the tidal deformability parameter obtained for each component of the binary star system. We show that quark stars composed of flavor-independent quark matter derived from the $D_3-D_7$ holographic model are not able to satisfy simultaneously the LIGO-Virgo and NICER astrophysical bounds.

## Key claim
Quark stars composed of flavor-independent quark matter derived from the D3-D7 holographic model are not able to satisfy simultaneously the LIGO-Virgo and NICER astrophysical bounds, despite the model not violating causality. The constituent quark mass is the only adjusted parameter of the equation of state, and no parametrization in the range studied can reproduce both the tidal deformability of GW170817 and a stellar mass of 2 solar masses.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium using the equation of state obtained from the D3-D7 holographic model at zero temperature, where the free energy density of the flavor fields is given by F_flavor = - (3/(4 pi^2)) (mu_q^2 - m^2)^2. They computed mass-radius sequences and tidal deformability parameters for constituent quark masses m ranging from 300 MeV to 360 MeV, then compared the results with NICER observational data for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 and with LIGO-Virgo data from the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass m range explored: 300 MeV to 360 MeV
- For PSR J0030+0451: inferred mass 1.34 (+0.15/-0.16) solar masses (Riley 2019) and 1.44 (+0.15/-0.14) solar masses (Miller 2019); radius estimates 12.71 (+1.14/-1.19) km (Riley 2019) and 13.02 (+1.24/-1.06) km (Miller 2019)
- For PSR J0740+6620: mass 2.072 (+0.0067/-0.066) solar masses (Riley 2021); radius estimates 13.7 (+2.6/-1.5) km (Miller 2021) and 12.39 (+1.30/-0.98) km (Riley 2021)
- Chirp mass of GW170817 event: 1.188 (+0.004/-0.002) solar masses
- Maximum central pressure increases with m, reaching highest at m = 360 MeV; radius of maximum mass star decreases monotonically with increasing m
- For m = 360 MeV, tidal deformability is consistent with GW170817 but maximum mass does not reach 2 solar masses

## Context
This work extends prior holographic approaches to compact stars, particularly following the analysis of Annala (2017) which compared different compact star solutions with LIGO-Virgo data. The D3-D7 holographic model, originally developed by Karch (2002) and Karch (2007), provides an equation of state for quark matter where the constituent quark mass is the only free parameter. The results show that flavor-independent quark matter from this model cannot simultaneously satisfy both NICER mass-radius constraints and GW170817 tidal deformability constraints, suggesting that future improvements should include SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking with different masses for up, down, and strange quarks, along with beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality.
