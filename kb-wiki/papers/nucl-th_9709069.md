# Isospin Breaking Effects in the Extraction of Isoscalar and Isovector Spectral Functions From $e^+e^-\to hadrons$

> We investigate the problem of the extraction of the isovector and isoscalar spectral functions from data on $e^+e^-\to hadrons$, in the presence of non-zero isospin breaking. It is shown that the conventional approach to extracting the isovector spectral function in the $ρ$ resonance region, in which only the isoscalar contribution associated with $ω\to ππ$ is subtracted, fails to fully remove the effects of the isoscalar component of the electromagnetic current. The additional subtractions required to extract the pure isovector and isoscalar spectral functions are estimated using results from QCD sum rules. It is shown that the corrections are small ($\sim 2%$) in the isovector case (though relevant to precision tests of CVC), but very large ($\sim 20%$) in the case of the $ω$ contribution to the isoscalar spectral function. The reason such a large effect is natural in the isoscalar channel is explained, and implications for other applications, such as the extraction of the sixth order chiral low-energy constant, $Q$, are discussed.

## Key claim

The conventional method for extracting the isovector spectral function from \(e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}\) data, which only subtracts the isoscalar contribution from \(\omega\to\pi\pi\), fails to fully remove the isoscalar component of the electromagnetic current; additional subtractions are required, and while the correction is small (\(\sim 2\%\)) for the isovector case, it is very large (\(\sim 20\%\)) for the \(\omega\) contribution to the isoscalar spectral function.

## Method

The author used QCD sum rule analyses of the mixed-isospin (\(ab=38\)) vector current correlator to extract the isospin-breaking vector meson decay constants \(F^{(8)}_\rho\), \(F^{(3)}_\omega\), and \(F^{(3)}_\phi\). The hadronic spectral function was parametrized with Breit-Wigner forms for the \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\phi\) mesons, employing the results of the IJL analysis (1995) with the \(\rho\) treated using a finite width and the \(\omega\) and \(\phi\) in the narrow-width approximation. The OPE side of the Borel-transformed dispersion relation was truncated at operators of dimension 6, and the input light quark mass ratio was taken as \(r_m = 0.54 \pm 0.04\) to obtain the decay constant values.

## Key numbers

*   \(f_\rho = 4\sqrt{3}\, F^{(3)}_\rho F^{(8)}_\rho = 0.0030 \pm 0.0012\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the QCD sum rule analysis with a stable Borel regime \(1.15\ \text{GeV} < M < 2.45\ \text{GeV}\).
*   \(f_\omega = -4\sqrt{3}\, F^{(3)}_\omega F^{(8)}_\omega = 0.0025 \pm 0.0009\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the same analysis.
*   \(f_\phi = 4\sqrt{3}\, F^{(3)}_\phi F^{(8)}_\phi = -0.0002 \pm 0.0002\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the same analysis.
*   The correction to the isovector spectral function is \(\sim 2\%\), relevant for precision tests of CVC.
*   The correction to the \(\omega\) contribution to the isoscalar spectral function is \(\sim 20\%\).
*   In the illustrative axial-vector example, the isospin-breaking parameters are \(\epsilon_1 = 1.37 \times 10^{-2}\) and \(\epsilon_2 = 1.11 \times 10^{-2}\), leading to a correction factor of \(0.984\) for the isovector and \(1.040\) for the isoscalar spectral function.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of isospin breaking in \(e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}\), such as the conventional approach of Korner and Schilcher (1990) which only subtracted the \(\omega\to\pi\pi\) contribution, and the QCD sum rule studies of the mixed-isospin correlator by IJL (1995) and earlier authors. The results enable more precise tests of the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis by providing the corrections needed to extract the pure isovector spectral function, and also impact the extraction of the sixth-order chiral low-energy constant \(Q\) via the inverse chiral sum rules method of Gasser and Kaiser (1995).
