# Some Applications of Hard Thermal Loop Perturbation Theory in Quark Gluon Plasma

> This thesis is mainly devoted to the study of thermodynamics for quantum Chromodynamics. In this thesis I apply hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory, which is a gauge-invariant reorganization of the conventional perturbative expansion for quantum gauge theories to study the thermodynamics of QCD in leading-order, next-to-leading-order and next-to-next-to-leading order at finite temperature and finite chemical potential. I also discuss about various order diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities in leading order as well as beyond leading order. For all the observables, I compare our results with available lattice QCD data and we find good agreement. Along with the computation of thermodynamic quantities of hot and dense matter, I also discuss about low mass dilepton rate from hot and dense medium using both perturbative and non-perturbative models and compare them with those from lattice gauge theory and in-medium hadron gas.

# Some Applications of Hard Thermal Loop Perturbation Theory in Quark Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

Hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) provides a gauge-invariant reorganization of the conventional perturbative expansion for QCD thermodynamics that yields leading-order quark number susceptibilities and temporal correlation functions in good agreement with lattice QCD data, though the leading-order results still show some deviation from lattice data within their wide variation and require extension to next-to-leading order for greater reliability in heavy-ion collision phenomenology.

## Method

The author applies hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory to compute thermodynamic functions including pressure, entropy density, number density, and quark number susceptibilities at leading order (one-loop) for QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential. The calculation separates contributions into quasiparticle parts from poles of the HTL-resummed quark propagator and Landau damping parts from the branch cut, using the thermodynamic sum rule to relate the temporal Euclidean correlation function to the quark number susceptibility. Results are compared with perturbative QCD calculations and lattice QCD data from multiple collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order HTLpt quark number susceptibility (QNS) for two-flavor QCD is plotted as a function of $T/T_C$ for renormalization scales $Q = 2\pi T$ and $Q = 4\pi T$, showing reduced scale dependence compared to pQCD results of order $g^2$
- The ratio $R \equiv (\chi_{htl} - \chi_f)/(\chi_{p(g^2)} - \chi_f)$ approaches unity in the weak coupling limit, confirming correct inclusion of order $g^2$ contributions
- Temporal correlation function $G_{00}(\tau T) = -T\chi_q(T)$ is computed at $T = 1.45 T_C$ for $N_f = 1$ and $N_f = 3$ and compared with lattice data on $128^3 \times 48$ lattices with quark mass $0.1T$ in quenched QCD
- Temporal correlation function is also presented at $T = 1.2 T_C$ (preliminary lattice data on $148^3 \times 40$ lattices) and at $T = 2.98 T_C$ (preliminary lattice data on $148^3 \times 16$ lattices)

## Context

This work extends the HTL resummation approach of Blaizot, Iancu, and collaborators (2001-2002) for QCD thermodynamics, correcting an over-counting issue in earlier HTLpt treatments by Chakraborty, Mustafa, and Thoma (2001-2003) that used HTL N-point functions uniformly for all momentum scales. The leading-order results are compared with lattice QCD data from the p4 and asqtad collaborations for (2+1)-flavor systems (Allton 2005, Petreczky 2009, Bazavov 2009, Bernard 2004) and 2-flavor systems (Gavai 2001-2002), as well as with improved quenched lattice calculations of the temporal correlation function by Ding (2010). The work enables next-to-leading-order and next-to-next-to-leading-order calculations of QCD thermodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential, which are presented in subsequent chapters of the thesis.
