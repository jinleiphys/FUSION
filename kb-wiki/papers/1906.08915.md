# Measuring Momentum-Dependent Flow Fluctuations in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In heavy-ion collisions, momentum-dependent pair correlations can be characterized by a principal component analysis (PCA), in which subleading modes are expected to reveal new information on flow fluctuations. However, we find that, as currently measured, these modes can be dominated by multiplicity fluctuations, which serve as an unwanted background. Here, we propose new PCA observables that are robust against multiplicity fluctuations and isolate novel sources of flow fluctuations, thus being suited to provide fresh insight into the initial stages of the system at small length scales.

## Key claim

The subleading principal component modes of anisotropic flow, as currently measured via PCA, can be dominated by multiplicity fluctuations rather than revealing new momentum-dependent flow fluctuations; the authors propose new PCA observables that remove this unwanted background and isolate genuine anisotropic flow fluctuations.

## Method

The authors perform a principal component analysis (PCA) of the flow covariance matrix \(V_{n\Delta}(\bm p_a,\bm p_b)\) and compare it to the PCA of the multiplicity-weighted matrix \(V_{n\Delta}^N(\bm p_a,\bm p_b)\) used in prior work (Bhalerao 2014, CMS 2017). They derive analytic expressions showing that multiplicity fluctuations contaminate the subleading PCA modes, then propose a new covariance matrix \(V_{n\Delta}^R\) that normalizes by the average number of particle pairs. The new observables are tested using a hybrid hydrodynamic model (TRENTO+MUSIC+UrQMD) for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the leading PCA mode from multiplicity fluctuations is at most \(\sim 3\%\) for \(V_0^{N(1)} \lesssim 0.25\)
- In noncentral collisions, the subleading mode \(V_2^{N(2)}\) from CMS data is closely approximated by the product \(V_n^{N(1)}\,V_0^{N(2)}\), indicating dominance by multiplicity fluctuations
- The new subleading modes \(V_n^{R(2)}\) match the per-particle flow modes \(V_n^{(2)}\) to good approximation in hydrodynamic simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV
- The new subleading modes show relative stability against changes in centrality across the centrality range studied

## Context

This work extends the PCA framework for flow fluctuations introduced by Bhalerao (2014) and measured by the CMS Collaboration (2017), and the factorization breaking ratio \(r_n(p_T^a,p_T^b)\) introduced by Gardim (2012). The authors identify a critical redundancy in the standard PCA observables, where multiplicity fluctuations (studied via \(V_{0\Delta}^N\) by Gardim 2019) contaminate the subleading anisotropic flow modes. The proposed new observables enable cleaner extraction of genuine momentum-dependent flow fluctuations, which can provide new constraints on initial-state fluctuations at sub-nucleonic length scales and on the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
