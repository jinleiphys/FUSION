# 'Running' under tight constraints in pionless effective field theory

> The contents of renormalization group invariance and equations under tight constraints are explored and demonstrated with closed-form on-shell $T$ matrices of pionless effective field theory for nuclear forces right within the effective field theory philosophy. The 'running' couplings under such tight constraints are presented in $^1S_0$ and uncoupled $P$ channels up to truncation order $\mathcal{O}(Q^4)$. Some linear relations are exposed and in turn employed in the pursuit of nonperturbative 'running' solutions which serves as an alternative choice without resorting to special prescription and additional operations or treatments. The utility of such 'running' behaviors inherent in the closed-form $T$-matrices of pionless effective field theory is remarked and a number of important issues related to effective field theory constructed with various truncations are interpreted or discussed from the underlying theory perspective.

# Running under tight constraints in pionless effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the tight constraints imposed by closed-form on-shell T matrices in pionless effective field theory, which are usually discarded or circumvented, can instead be exploited to derive nonperturbative running couplings that differ fundamentally from conventional perturbative renormalization group behavior. The key hedge is that this alternative scenario only works within a limited energy window below a singularity scale, beyond which the effective field theory description breaks down.

## Method

The authors solve Lippmann-Schwinger equations for contact potentials in pionless effective field theory to obtain closed-form on-shell T matrices for NN scattering in the ¹S₀ and uncoupled P channels up to truncation order O(Q⁴). They employ a general parametrization of loop integrals (the J parameters) that avoids special prescriptions like power divergence subtraction (PDS), and exploit linear relations among the coefficients of the T matrix to identify which parameters are renormalization group invariant versus running. The running couplings are then solved by requiring that the physical ratios defining the functional shape of the T matrix remain invariant under changes of the running scale.

## Key numbers

- The closed-form T matrix for ¹S₀ channel at O(Q⁴) is given by 1/T(p) = J₀ + iM_Np/4π + (N_{S;0} + N_{S;1}p² + N_{S;2}p⁴)/(D_{S;0} + D_{S;1}p² + D_{S;2}p⁴ + D_{S;3}p⁶), with p ≡ √(M_N E)
- At O(Q²) in ¹S₀: 1/T(p) = J₀ + iM_Np/4π + N_{S;0}/(D_{S;0} + D_{S;1}p²), with N_{S;0} = (1 - C₂J₃)², D_{S;0} = C₀ + C₂²J₅, D_{S;1} = 2C₂ - C₂²J₃
- The running coupling C₀(μ) at O(Q⁰) in ¹S₀: C₀(μ) = 1/((M_N/4π)a⁻¹ - J₀(μ))
- The singularity scale μ_sing at O(Q²) is given by μ_sing = (-α̃₁j̃₃)⁻¹/³ Λ_π̸ ∼ 2⁻¹/³ Λ_π̸, where α̃₁ ∼ j̃₃ ∼ O(1)
- Infrared fixed points at O(Q⁴): C₀^(IR) = finite, C₂^(IR) = finite, C₄^(IR) = finite
- Ultraviolet fixed points at O(Q⁴): C₀^(UV) = ∞, C₂^(UV) = ∞, C₄^(UV) = 0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of closed-form T matrices in pionless effective field theory (Lü and Yang, 2017; 2018) and provides an alternative to the conventional renormalization program established in perturbation theory by Stueckelberg, Gell-Mann, Callan, Symanzik, 't Hooft, and Weinberg. It contrasts with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) running (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998; 1999) derived using power divergence subtraction (PDS), which the authors argue overkills the nontrivial J parameters that are the true sources of running in closed-form T matrices. The work also differs from Wilsonian renormalization group approaches (Wilson, Wegner, Nicoll, Polchinski, Wetterich, Ellwanger) because those cannot separate physical from running components in the presence of tight constraints. The results enable a novel nonperturbative renormalization scenario where lower-order running couplings depend on higher-order interaction parameters, consistent with the expectation that short-distance renormalization should be independent of long-distance physics while the reverse is not true.
