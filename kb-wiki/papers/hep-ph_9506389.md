# Nucleon to Delta Weak Excitation Amplitudes in the Non-relativistic Quark Model

> We investigate the nucleon to Delta(1232) vector and axial vector amplitudes in the non-relativistic quark model of the Isgur-Karl variety. A particular interest is to investigate the SU(6) symmetry breaking, due to color hyperfine interaction. We compare the theoretical estimates to recent experimental investigation of the Adler amplitudes by neutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic quark model of the Isgur-Karl variety can reproduce the measured vector and axial vector transition form factors for the weak excitation of the Delta(1232) resonance, but only with the caveat that the model systematically underestimates the vector form factor \(C_3^V\) at \(Q^2=0\) and fails to satisfy the partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) relation for \(C_6^A\), while accidentally satisfying the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Method

The authors compute the transverse helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}^V\), \(A_{3/2}^V\), \(A_{1/2}^A\), and \(A_{3/2}^A\) for the reaction \(p + \nu_\mu \rightarrow \Delta^{++} + \mu^{-}\) using non-relativistic quark model wave functions of the nucleon and Delta(1232) that include SU(6) symmetry breaking via the color hyperfine interaction (Isgur-Karl model). They relate these helicity amplitudes to the Adler form factors \(C_i^V\) and \(C_i^A\) (\(i=3,4,5,6\)) through the Rarita-Schwinger formalism, and compare their theoretical estimates to experimental data from the Brookhaven National Laboratory neutrino experiment.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator parameter of the Isgur-Karl model is \(\alpha_{HO} = 320\ \mathrm{MeV}\), obtained from fitting the nucleon spectra.
- The energy transfer at the "real photon" point is \(K_0 = 255.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\), defined as \((M_\Delta^2 - M^2)/(2M_\Delta)\).
- The wave function coefficients for the Delta are: \(0.97|^4S_s\rangle + 0.20|^4S_s'\rangle - 0.097|^4D_s\rangle + 0.065|^2D_M\rangle\); for the nucleon: \(0.95|^2S_s\rangle - 0.24|^2S_s'\rangle - 0.20|^2S_M\rangle - 0.042|^4D_M\rangle\), corresponding to energies \(E_\Delta = 1230\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(E_N = 940\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation gives \(C_5^A(0) \approx 1.2\); the SU(6) limit yields \(C_5^A(0) = 1.17\), and the Isgur-Karl model yields \(C_5^A(0) = 1.16\).
- The \(\Delta^{++} \rightarrow p\pi^{+}\) coupling constant is \(g_\Delta = 28.6 \pm 0.3\).
- From the Brookhaven experiment, the fitted vector mass parameter is \(M_V = 0.89_{-0.07}^{+0.04}\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and the axial mass parameter is \(M_A = 0.97_{-0.11}^{+0.14}\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the long history of theoretical investigations of the nucleon-to-Delta transition, including the SU(6) symmetry approach of Gursey, Radicati (1964) and Sakita (1964), and the non-relativistic quark model calculations of Isgur and Karl (1978-1979). It provides the first calculation of weak transition amplitudes using the general Isgur-Karl wave functions that include color hyperfine mixing, complementing earlier work by Schreiner and von Hippel (1973) and Bourdeau and Mukhopadhyay (1992). The results enable direct comparison with the Adler form factors extracted from the Brookhaven neutrino experiment by Kitagaki et al. (1990), and motivate further experimental study of the axial-vector analogue of the \(E2/M1\) ratio as a probe of SU(6) breaking in the weak sector.
