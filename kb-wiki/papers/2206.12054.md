# Quark anomalous magnetic moment leads to the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomena of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions in $μ_B-T$ plane

> The effect of quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) to chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions in baryon chemical potential-temperature $(μ_B-T)$ plane under magnetic fields is investigated in frame of a Pauli-Villars regularized PNJL model. It's found that the quark AMM plays the role of inverse catalysis to the phase transitions, and large quark AMM will change the magnetic catalysis phenomena of phase transitions to inverse magnetic catalysis in the whole $μ_B-T$ plane. For a fixed magnetic field, the critical temperature $T_c$ and critical baryon chemical potential $μ_B^c$ decreases with quark AMM. The stronger the magnetic field is, the inverse catalysis effect of AMM becomes more important. For a small AMM $κ=κ_1$, it shows the magnetic catalysis effect for critical temperature $T_c$ at vanishing $μ_B$ with increasing magnetic field, and (inverse) magnetic catalysis effect for critical baryon chemical potential $μ_B^c$ at vanishing $T$ under (weak) strong magnetic field. At finite $T$ and $μ_B$, there exist some crossings of the phase transition lines with different magnetic field. For a large AMM $κ=κ_2$, we obtain the inverse magnetic catalysis effect in the whole $μ_B-T$ plane, and no crossings of phase transition lines happen.

# Quark anomalous magnetic moment leads to the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomena of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions in μ_B-T plane

## Key claim

The quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) acts as an inverse catalyst for both chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions; with a sufficiently large quark AMM, the usual magnetic catalysis effect is reversed to inverse magnetic catalysis across the entire baryon chemical potential-temperature (μ_B-T) plane, but for a small AMM the behavior depends on the magnetic field strength and the region of the phase diagram.

## Method

The author employs a two-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the chiral limit, regularized with the gauge-covariant Pauli-Villars scheme to avoid nonphysical oscillations and causality violations. The quark AMM is introduced via a term (1/2) a σ_{μν} F^{μν} in the Lagrangian, and the thermodynamic potential is minimized at finite temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B to obtain coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass m, the Polyakov loop Φ, and its conjugate Φ̄. All Landau levels and longitudinal momenta are included in the numerical calculations, with two representative AMM parameter sets (κ₁ and κ₂) fitted to nucleon magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Two AMM parameter sets used: κ_u^(1) = 0.00995 GeV⁻¹, κ_d^(1) = 0.07975 GeV⁻¹ (small AMM, denoted κ₁); κ_u^(2) = 0.29016 GeV⁻¹, κ_d^(2) = 0.35986 GeV⁻¹ (large AMM, denoted κ₂).
- Model parameters fixed by fitting f_π = 93 MeV and chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = (-250 MeV)³ in vacuum, yielding G = 7.04 GeV⁻² and Λ = 1127 MeV with N = 3 Pauli-Villars masses.
- For vanishing AMM (κ₀ = 0) at zero temperature, the turning point between inverse magnetic catalysis (weak field) and magnetic catalysis (strong field) for critical baryon chemical potential μ_B^c occurs at eB/m_π² = 10.
- For small AMM κ₁ at zero temperature, the turning point for μ_B^c is retarded to eB/m_π² = 11.
- Magnetic fields studied include eB/m_π² = 10 and eB/m_π² = 20, with m_π = 134 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Mao, 2022) at finite temperature and vanishing baryon chemical potential to the full μ_B-T plane, using the same Pauli-Villars regularized PNJL framework. It addresses the open question of explaining inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD simulations (Lattice QCD collaborations, 2010s), where the critical temperature for chiral restoration decreases with increasing magnetic field, contrary to most mean-field model calculations. The results show that including quark AMM can reconcile model calculations with lattice findings, and the competition between magnetic catalysis (from the magnetic field) and inverse catalysis (from the AMM) determines the phase structure. The work enables further studies of QCD phase diagrams under strong magnetic fields relevant to compact stars and heavy-ion collisions.
