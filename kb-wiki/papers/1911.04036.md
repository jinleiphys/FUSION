# Long-range electroweak amplitudes of single hadrons from Euclidean finite-volume correlation functions

> A relation is presented between single-hadron long-range matrix elements defined in a finite Euclidean spacetime, and the corresponding infinite-volume Minkowski amplitudes. This relation is valid in the kinematic region where any number of two-hadron states can simultaneously go on shell, so that the effects of strongly-coupled intermediate channels are included. These channels can consist of non-identical particles with arbitrary intrinsic spins. The result accommodates general Lorentz structures as well as non-zero momentum transfer for the two external currents inserted between the single-hadron states. The formalism, therefore, generalizes the work by Christ et al.~[Phys.Rev. D91 114510 (2015)], and extends the reach of lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) to a wide class of new observables beyond meson mixing and rare decays. Applications include Compton scattering of the pion ($πγ^\star \to [ππ, K \overline K] \to πγ^\star$), kaon ($K γ^\star \to [πK, ηK] \to K γ^\star$) and nucleon ($N γ^\star \to N π\to N γ^\star$), as well as double-$β$ decays, and radiative corrections to the single-$β$ decay, of QCD-stable hadrons. The framework presented will further facilitate generalization of the result to studies of nuclear amplitudes involving two currents from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general relation between single-hadron long-range matrix elements computed in a finite Euclidean spacetime (as in lattice QCD) and the corresponding infinite-volume Minkowski amplitudes, valid when any number of two-hadron intermediate states can go on shell, including channels with non-identical particles and arbitrary intrinsic spins, and accommodating non-zero momentum transfer between the two external currents.

## Method

The authors derive a master formula that relates the finite-volume Euclidean correlation function \(G_L(\tau, \boldsymbol{q})\) to the infinite-volume Minkowski amplitude \(\mathcal{T}(\omega, \boldsymbol{q})\) by: (1) subtracting the contributions of low-lying finite-volume states from the Euclidean correlator using a spectral decomposition, (2) performing a convergent integral over Euclidean time to convert the exponential time dependence to poles, and (3) adding back a correction term \(\Delta T_L(\omega, \boldsymbol{q})\) constructed from the Lellouch-Luscher formalism, which uses the finite-volume quantization condition and the generalized Lellouch-Luscher matrices to remove finite-volume effects. The correction term requires independent lattice QCD determinations of the relevant \(2 \to 2\) scattering amplitudes and \(1 \to 2\) transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The finite-volume energies \(E_n(L)\) and \(E_{\bar n}(L)\) are defined with three-momenta quantized as \(\boldsymbol{P} = 2\pi \boldsymbol{n}/L\) with \(\boldsymbol{n} \in \mathbb{Z}^3\).
- The finite-volume correction \(\Delta T_L\) is of order \(\mathcal{O}(e^{-m L})\) when the energy \(\omega + M\) is below all single- and multi-hadron thresholds, where \(m\) is the mass of the lightest degree of freedom (the pion in QCD).
- The finite-volume states are normalized to unity: \(\langle P, L | P', L \rangle = \delta_{\boldsymbol{P}, \boldsymbol{P}'}\).
- The infinite-volume states satisfy relativistic normalization: \(\langle P | P' \rangle = 2E (2\pi)^3 \delta^3(\boldsymbol{P} - \boldsymbol{P}')\).

## Context

This work generalizes the formalism of Christ et al. (2015) for extracting long-range matrix elements from finite-volume Euclidean correlation functions, which was originally developed for the \(K_L\)-\(K_S\) mass splitting and the CP-violating parameter \(\epsilon_K\). The new result extends the reach of lattice QCD to a wider class of observables including Compton scattering of the pion, kaon, and nucleon, as well as double-beta decays and radiative corrections to single-beta decays of QCD-stable hadrons. The framework enables future generalizations to multi-hadron bi-local matrix elements and nuclear amplitudes involving two currents from lattice QCD.
