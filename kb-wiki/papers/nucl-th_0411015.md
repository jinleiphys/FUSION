# Phase structure of the two-fluid proton-neutron system

> The phase structure of a two-fluid bosonic system is investigated. # Phase structure of the two-fluid proton-neutron system

## Key claim
The proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) possesses a phase diagram with three control parameters (ξ', χ_π, χ_ν) and multiple order parameters, featuring surfaces of quantum phase transition between spherical, axially-symmetric deformed, and SU*(3) triaxial phases. The analysis is limited to the F-spin invariant Hamiltonian of Eq. (1) and does not include Majorana contributions or different strengths for the quadrupole-quadrupole terms that would be needed for realistic phenomenological studies.

## Method
The classical limit of the IBM-2 Hamiltonian is obtained by evaluating its expectation value in a coherent state, yielding an energy surface E(β_π, γ_π, β_ν, γ_ν, ϑ_1, ϑ_2, ϑ_3) as a function of deformation parameters for proton and neutron fluids and their relative Euler angles. For the Hamiltonian considered, the global minimum occurs only for aligned proton and neutron intrinsic frames, reducing the order parameters to β_π, γ_π, β_ν, and γ_ν. Analytic stability analysis is performed for the ξ'=1 plane and for the spherical-to-deformed transition, while the remainder of the phase diagram is obtained by numerical minimization of the energy surface on a fine mesh (Δβ_ρ=0.03, Δγ_ρ=2°) with iterative refinement of local minima.

## Key numbers
- The second-order transition between spherical and deformed structure occurs at ξ'=1/5 and χ_S=0 for N_π/N_ν=1, where the minimum at β=0 becomes unstable.
- Along the SU(3)-SU*(3) line (χ_π=-√7/2) for N_π/N_ν=1, the axial-to-triaxial transition occurs at χ_ν≈0.4035.
- The boundary curve for the axial-to-triaxial transition in the ξ'=1 plane is given by Eq. (7), expressed in terms of equilibrium β_π and β_ν values from Eq. (6): β_ρ = [(χ_ρ/√14)²+1]^{1/2} - χ_ρ/√14.
- For N_π/N_ν=1, the second derivative at zero deformation is ∂²E/∂β²|_β=0 = (1-3ξ')(u_π²+u_ν²) - 4ξ'u_πu_νcos(γ_π-γ_ν), which first becomes unstable at ξ'=1/5.
- The line of second-order phase transition for arbitrary N_π/N_ν obeys χ_π/χ_ν = -N_ν/N_π.

## Context
This work extends the phase structure analysis of the one-fluid interacting boson model (IBM), which exhibits three dynamical symmetries separated by first and second order phase transitions (Dieperink 1980, Feng 1981), to the two-fluid IBM-2 which supports four dynamical symmetries (Van Isacker 1986, Dieperink 1982). The IBM-2 phase diagram provides a framework for studying transitions between axial and triaxial structure in nuclei, where triaxial deformation may arise from distinct deformations of proton and neutron fluids. The analysis enables future phenomenological studies using more realistic Hamiltonians with Majorana contributions and different quadrupole-quadrupole strengths (Lipas 1990), and may serve as a model for other multi-fluid bosonic systems including core-skin modes in neutron-rich nuclei (Warner 1997), coupled vibronic bending modes in acetylene, and two-species atomic Bose-Einstein condensates.
