# CHIRAL BACKGROUND FOR THE TWO PION EXCHANGE NUCLEAR POTENTIAL: A PARAMETRIZED VERSION

> We argue that the minimal chiral background for the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction has nowadays a rather firm conceptual basis, which entitles it to become a standard ingredient of any modern potential. In order to facilitate applications, we present a parametrized version of a configuration space potential derived previously. We then use it to assess the phenomenological contents of some existing NN potentials.

## Key claim

The minimal chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, involving only pions and nucleons, has a sufficiently firm conceptual basis to become a standard ingredient of any modern NN potential, but it accounts for less than 25% of the values needed to reproduce observations, meaning it is necessary but not sufficient to describe reality.

## Method

The authors present a parametrized version in configuration space of their previously derived non-relativistic two-pion exchange potential (da Rocha and Robilotta 1994), based on the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation using non-linear chiral Lagrangians with either PS+S or PV+V couplings. They then subtract the one-pion exchange potential from three realistic potentials (dTRS, Paris, and Argonne v14) and compare the remainder, divided by the fourth power of the pion-nucleon coupling constant, with the minimal chiral background to assess the phenomenological content of each potential.

## Key numbers

- The parametrized expressions reproduce the numerical results of da Rocha and Robilotta (1994) to within 0.25% except in a few cases and regions.
- The parametrization is valid up to 0.5 fm and is completely free of cutoff parameters.
- The minimal chiral background accounts for less than 25% of the values needed to reproduce observations, based on comparisons with the dTRS, Paris, and Argonne v14 potentials.
- For the (T=1, S=1) tensor potential, realistic potentials are roughly coherent below 2.5 fm and about 3 times greater than the background; for the (T=0, S=1) component, this ratio is about 5.
- The asymptotic behavior of the bubble diagram yields numerical values of 275.299 for the central potential and 5.88607 for the spin-orbit potential in the square brackets of Eqs. (A) and (B).
- The pion mass is taken as mu = 137.29 MeV and the nucleon mass as m = 938.92 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry approach to two-pion exchange NN potentials developed by Ordonez and van Kolck (1992), Celenza, Pantziris and Shakin (1992), Birse (1994), Friar and Coon (1994), and the authors' own previous calculation (da Rocha and Robilotta 1994). The parametrized form enables straightforward comparison with existing potentials such as dTRS (de Tourreil, Rouben and Sprung 1975), Paris (Lacombe et al. 1980), and Argonne v14 (Wiringa, Smith and Ainsworth 1984), and the authors note that an extension including other fields and processes has been recently considered by Ordonez, Ray and van Kolck (1994) with promising results.
