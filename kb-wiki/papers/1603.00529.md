# Open Heavy Flavor in QCD Matter and in Nuclear Collisions

> We review the experimental and theoretical status of open heavy-flavor (HF) production in high-energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC. We first overview the theoretical concepts and pertinent calculations of HF transport in QCD matter, including perturbative and non-perturbative approaches in the quark-gluon plasma, effective models in hadronic matter, as well as implementations of heavy-quark (HQ) hadronization. This is followed by a brief discussion of bulk evolution models for heavy-ion collisions and initial conditions for the HQ distributions which are needed to calculate HF spectra in comparison to observables. We then turn to a discussion of experimental data that have been collected to date at RHIC and LHC, specifically for the nuclear suppression factor and elliptic flow of semileptonic HF decays, D mesons, non-prompt $J/ψ$ from B-meson decays, and b-jets. Model comparisons to HF data are conducted with regards to extracting the magnitude, temperature and momentum-dependence of HF transport coefficients from experiment.

# Open Heavy Flavor in QCD Matter and in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The paper reviews the experimental and theoretical status of open heavy-flavor production in high-energy nuclear collisions, concluding that while heavy-quark transport coefficients can be extracted from comparisons of model calculations to data, the temperature and momentum dependence of these coefficients remain uncertain and require further constraints from more differential observables and higher-statistics measurements.

## Method

The authors compile and compare experimental data from RHIC and LHC on the nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v_2) of heavy-flavor decay leptons, D mesons, non-prompt J/psi from B-meson decays, and b-jets. They review theoretical calculations of heavy-quark transport in the quark-gluon plasma using perturbative QCD, T-matrix approaches, lattice QCD, and AdS/CFT correspondence, as well as hadronic matter models and heavy-quark hadronization via fragmentation and coalescence. Model-to-data comparisons are conducted to extract the magnitude, temperature, and momentum dependence of heavy-flavor transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Energy density in central Au–Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=19 GeV: about 2 GeV/fm^3; at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: about 5 GeV/fm^3; in central Pb–Pb collisions at LHC: about 14 GeV/fm^3
- Charm production cross section increases by about a factor of 10 from RHIC to LHC energies; beauty production increases by about a factor of 50
- D^0, D^+, and D_s^+ meson mean proper decay lengths (c*tau): approximately 120, 310, and 150 micrometers respectively
- B^0, B^+, and B_s^0 meson mean proper decay lengths: approximately 500 micrometers
- Branching ratio of inclusive beauty-hadron decays into J/psi measured at LEP: 1.16%
- D^0 -> K^- pi^+ branching ratio: 3.88%; D^+ -> K^- pi^+ pi^+: 9.13%; D_s^+ -> phi pi^+ -> K^- K^+ pi^+: 2.24%
- AdS/CFT friction coefficient estimate yields gamma_Q approximately 0.3-0.5/fm at T=250 MeV
- Charm- and bottom-quark friction coefficients at zero momentum differ by approximately the mass ratio m_b/m_c, within 20% for most approaches

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier heavy-flavor measurements at the SPS by NA50 and NA60 (Abreu 2000, Arnaldi 2008) and at RHIC by PHENIX and STAR, and provides a comprehensive comparison with LHC data from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb. The paper synthesizes theoretical approaches including pQCD calculations, T-matrix calculations (Riek 2010, Huggins 2012), lattice QCD results (Banerjee 2011, Ding 2012, Francis 2015), and AdS/CFT correspondence (Herzog 2006, Gubser 2006, Casalderrey-Solana 2006). It enables future work by identifying the need for higher-statistics measurements of angular correlations, higher-order flow coefficients, and more differential observables to constrain the temperature and momentum dependence of heavy-quark transport coefficients.
