# Renormalization group, dimer-dimer scattering, and three-body forces

> We study the ratio between the fermion-fermion scattering length and the dimer-dimer scattering length for systems of nonrelativistic fermions, using the same functional renormalisation technique as previously applied to fermionic matter. We find a strong dependence on the cutoff function used in the renormalisation flow for a two-body truncation of the action. Adding a simple three-body term substantially reduces this dependence.

## Key claim

The ratio of dimer-dimer to fermion-fermion scattering lengths computed using a functional renormalisation group approach with only two-body interactions shows a strong dependence on the cutoff function parameter \(c_B\); adding a simple local three-body term substantially reduces this cutoff dependence and brings the ratio closer to the exact value of 0.6, but does not eliminate the dependence entirely.

## Method

The authors use the Exact Renormalisation Group (ERG) approach with a scale-dependent average effective action \(\Gamma_k\) for nonrelativistic fermions, introducing an auxiliary boson field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They compute the evolution of the boson self-energy, boson wave-function renormalisation, and the boson-boson coupling \(u_2\) from the ERG flow equations, first with only two-body interactions and then adding a local three-body term \(\lambda\). The bosonic cutoff function includes a parameter \(c_B\) that sets the relative scale of fermionic and bosonic regulators, and the ratio \(a_B/a_F\) is studied as a function of \(c_B\).

## Key numbers

- Without three-body term, \(c_B = 1\) gives \(a_B/a_F = 1.13\); with three-body term, \(c_B = 1\) gives \(a_B/a_F = 0.74\).
- Without three-body term, \(c_B = \sqrt{2}\) gives \(a_B/a_F = 0.75\); with three-body term, \(c_B = \sqrt{2}\) gives \(a_B/a_F = 0.69\).
- The exact result from solving the Schrödinger equation for two composite bosons is \(a_B = 0.6 a_F\) (Shl).
- The mean-field result (boson loops neglected) gives \(a_B = 2 a_F\) (Ha).
- The fermionic cutoff function used is \(R_F(\vec{q},k) = (k^2 - q^2)/(2M)\,\theta(k-q)\) (Litim).

## Context

This work extends previous ERG studies of nonrelativistic fermions (Kri, Diehl, Bir) and of two-fermion scattering (Bir) by examining the dimer-dimer scattering length and its cutoff dependence. It contrasts with the approach of DKS, who derived the Skorniakov-Ter-Matirosian equation from the RG flow with energy- and momentum-dependent three-body couplings, which is difficult to extend to many-body systems. The results show that including the simplest local three-body term improves the stability of the ratio \(a_B/a_F\) with respect to the cutoff parameter, suggesting that further extensions (four-body interactions, energy- or momentum-dependent three-body forces) may be needed for full cutoff independence, and that this formalism can be extended to many-body systems.
