# An improved nuclear mass formula: WS3

> We introduce a global nuclear mass formula which is based on the macroscopic-microscopic method, the Skyrme energy-density functional and the isospin symmetry in nuclear physics. The rms deviation with respect to 2149 known nuclear masses falls to 336 keV, and the rms deviations from 1988 neutron separation energies and α-decay energies of 46 super-heavy nuclei are significantly reduced to 286 and 248 keV, respectively. The predictive power of the mass formula for describing new measured masses in GSI and those in AME2011 is excellent. In addition, we introduce an efficient and powerful systematic method, radial basis function approach, for further improving the accuracy and predictive power of global nuclear mass models.

# An improved nuclear mass formula: WS3

## Key claim

The WS3 mass formula achieves a root-mean-square (rms) deviation of 336 keV for 2149 known nuclear masses, but this accuracy depends on incorporating isospin symmetry via mirror-nuclei shell corrections and a new isospin-dependent form for the symmetry energy coefficient, which together improve the model by approximately 10% and 5% respectively over the earlier WS and WS* versions.

## Method

The authors construct a global nuclear mass formula using the macroscopic-microscopic method, combining a modified Bethe-Weizsacker liquid-drop energy with Strutinsky shell corrections from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential. The liquid-drop energy includes a new mass- and isospin-dependent symmetry energy coefficient, a pairing term with isospin dependence, and a parabolic deformation energy based on the Skyrme energy-density functional. The microscopic shell correction incorporates the shell energy of both the nucleus and its mirror nucleus to enforce isospin symmetry, and a Wigner term accounts for enhanced binding along the line N = 1.37Z + 13.5.

## Key numbers

- rms deviation from 2149 measured nuclear masses: 336 keV (WS3), compared to 656 keV (FRDM), 581 keV (HFB-17), and 360 keV (DZ28)
- rms deviation from 1988 neutron separation energies: 286 keV (WS3), compared to 399 keV (FRDM) and 306 keV (DZ28)
- rms deviation from alpha-decay energies of 46 super-heavy nuclei (Z >= 106): 248 keV (WS3), compared to 566 keV (FRDM) and 936 keV (DZ28)
- rms deviation for 154 new masses in AME2011: 433 keV (WS3), compared to 723 keV (FRDM) and 622 keV (DZ28)
- rms deviation for 53 new masses measured at GSI: 360 keV (WS3), compared to 600 keV (FRDM) and 504 keV (DZ28)
- With radial basis function (RBF) correction applied to WS3: rms deviation for 154 new AME2011 masses reduces to 397 keV; for 53 new GSI masses reduces to 229 keV
- The symmetry energy coefficient at normal density c_sym is approximately 30 MeV
- Alpha-decay energies of super-heavy nuclei are reproduced within +/- 0.5 MeV with WS3, compared to +/- 1.0 MeV with FRDM

## Context

This work extends the earlier Weizsacker-Skyrme (WS and WS*) mass formulas by Wang and collaborators (2010, 2011), improving upon the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (1995), the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model HFB-17, and the Duflo-Zuker models DZ10, DZ28, and DZ31. The WS3 formula enables more reliable predictions for super-heavy nuclei, particularly for locating the island of stability, and the radial basis function correction provides a systematic method to further improve global mass models by 10% to 40% without introducing new parameters. The predicted shell corrections suggest a shell gap at N = 178 in addition to the known gaps at N = 162 and N = 184, influencing the central position of the island of stability for super-heavy nuclei.
