# Density Dependence of Two-Body Interactions for Beyond Mean-Field Calculations

> This paper deals with the theoretical foundation of effective two-body forces for the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and the projected mean-field method. The first aim of this paper is to reduce into various local-densities the in-medium content of a generalized $G$ matrix removing the hard core problem in this extended context. Then, we consider the possible renormalization of multi-body forces through a density-dependent two-body interaction in the context of configuration mixing calculations. A density dependence of the form $ρ^σ$, as used in Skyrme and Gogny forces, is successfully interpreted as doing so when the mixed density is used. Finally, we propose a simple extension of the Skyrme force dedicated to the calculation of matrix elements between non-orthogonal product states, which are needed to evaluate the correlated energy.

## Key claim

The paper provides a theoretical foundation for using the mixed density (rather than the mean-field or correlated density) in density-dependent two-body interactions when performing configuration mixing calculations such as the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) and projected mean-field methods, but only under the condition that the density dependence originates from the renormalization of three-body (or higher multi-body) forces.

## Method

The authors extend Goldstone-Brueckner perturbation theory to configuration mixing calculations involving non-orthogonal product states, deriving a generalized Brueckner matrix \(G^{(\beta,\alpha)}\) from two standard Brueckner matrices \(G^\beta\) and \(G^\alpha\) referring to different vacua. They then analyze the operator \(Q^\alpha/(W_\alpha - h^\alpha_0)\) responsible for in-medium effects in the Brueckner ladder summation, and examine how the density dependence of effective forces can be reduced to local densities through a Local Density Approximation (LDA) or Density Matrix Expansion (DME). Finally, they derive the expression for three-body force matrix elements between non-orthogonal product states using the generalized Wick theorem and show that these can be expressed entirely in terms of the mixed density.

## Key numbers

* The standard Brueckner matrix \(G^\alpha\) satisfies a self-consistent equation of the form \(G^\alpha(W_\alpha) = V + V \frac{Q^\alpha}{W_\alpha - h^\alpha_0} G^\alpha(W_\alpha)\), where \(Q^\alpha\) is the Pauli operator excluding occupied states in \(|\Phi^\alpha_0\rangle\).
* The generalized Brueckner matrix for non-orthogonal vacua is given by \(G^{(\beta,\alpha)}(W_\beta,W_\alpha) = G^\beta(W_\beta) V^{-1} G^\alpha(W_\alpha)\).
* The three-body force matrix element between non-orthogonal product states is expressed as \(\langle \Phi^\beta_0 | V^{(3)} | \Phi^\alpha_0 \rangle = \frac{1}{6} \sum_{i,j,k} \sum_{i',j',k'} \bar{v}^{(3)}_{ijk,i'j'k'} \rho^{\beta\alpha}_{ii'} \rho^{\beta\alpha}_{jj'} \rho^{\beta\alpha}_{kk'}\), where \(\rho^{\beta\alpha}\) is the mixed density matrix.
* For a zero-range three-body force of the form \(V^{(3)} = t_3 \delta(\vec{r}_1 - \vec{r}_2) \delta(\vec{r}_2 - \vec{r}_3)\), the matrix element reduces to \(\frac{t_3}{6} \int d\vec{R} [\rho^{\beta\alpha}_0(\vec{R})]^3\), where \(\rho^{\beta\alpha}_0\) is the local scalar-isoscalar part of the mixed density.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of effective interactions beyond the mean-field level, where previous prescriptions for the density dependence in GCM and projected mean-field calculations (such as those by Bonche et al. (1990), Valatin (1961), and Heenen et al. (1993)) lacked rigorous justification. The paper builds on the generalized perturbation theory developed by Duguet (2002) and the standard Brueckner theory of Negele (1970) and Day (1967), while also referencing the work of Vautherin and Brink (1972) on Skyrme forces and Decharge and Gogny (1980) on Gogny forces. The results enable the proper definition of density-dependent effective forces for beyond mean-field calculations, particularly for symmetry restoration methods and the Generator Coordinate Method, and the authors propose a specific extension of the Skyrme force for use with non-orthogonal product states.
