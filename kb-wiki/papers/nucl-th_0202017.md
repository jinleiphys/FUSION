# The extent of strangeness in equilibration in quark-gluon plasma

> The evolution and production of strangeness from chemically equilibrating and transversely expanding quark gluon plasma which may be formed in the wake of relativistic heavy ion collisions is studied with initial conditions obtained from the Self Screened Parton Cascade (SSPC) model. The extent of partonic equilibration increases almost linearly with the square of the initial energy density, which can then be scaled with number of participants.

# The extent of strangeness equilibration in quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The extent of strangeness equilibration in a chemically equilibrating and transversely expanding quark-gluon plasma rises approximately linearly with the square of the initial energy density, but the plasma is not fully chemically equilibrated at either RHIC or LHC energies; most strange quarks are produced when the temperature is still above about 300 MeV and then freeze out.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations for an ideal fluid with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, coupled to master equations for chemical equilibration via gluon multiplication ($gg \rightarrow ggg$) and quark production ($gg \rightarrow q\bar{q}$). Initial conditions are taken from the Self Screened Parton Cascade (SSPC) model. Parton distribution functions are scaled by nonequilibrium fugacities $\lambda_j$ for gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks, and the rate equations include temperature-dependent coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC and LHC energies, the ratio of strange to non-strange quark fugacity ($\lambda_s/\lambda_{u,d}$) rises rapidly and freezes once the temperature falls below about 300 MeV ($\sim 2m_s$).
- For initial energy densities beyond about 20–40 GeV, the final fugacities for all partons increase almost linearly with the square of the initial energy density.
- The temperature of the partonic plasma is reduced considerably faster than ideal Bjorken scaling ($T = T_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{1/3}$) due to additional parton production consuming energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Levai (1995) that used HIJING initial conditions with only longitudinal expansion, and by the authors' own prior work (Pal, 2001) on transverse expansion. It also builds on findings by Chen (1997) and Duncan (1997) that radial expansion slows chemical equilibration of light flavours and gluons. The result that final fugacities scale linearly with the square of initial energy density, similar to observations at CERN SPS energies (Jeannet, 1999), may enable scaling of initial energy density with number of participants in future heavy-ion collision experiments.
