# Strong plasma screening in thermonuclear reactions: Electron drop model

> We analyze enhancement of thermonuclear fusion reactions due to strong plasma screening in dense matter using a simple electron drop model. The model assumes fusion in a potential that is screened by an effective electron cloud around colliding nuclei (extended Salpeter ion-sphere model). We calculate the mean field screened Coulomb potentials for atomic nuclei with equal and nonequal charges, appropriate astrophysical S factors, and enhancement factors of reaction rates. As a byproduct, we study analytic behavior of the screening potential at small separations between the reactants. In this model, astrophysical S factors depend not only on nuclear physics but on plasma screening as well. The enhancement factors are in good agreement with calculations by other methods. This allows us to formulate the combined, pure analytic model of strong plasma screening in thermonuclear reactions. The results can be useful for simulating nuclear burning in white dwarfs and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The electron drop model provides a simple analytic description of strong plasma screening in thermonuclear reactions that agrees well with more computationally intensive Monte Carlo simulations, but the model's accuracy is limited to the regime where the quantum parameter ζ ≲ 1 (thermonuclear burning with strong screening) and the screening potential at small separations differs from Monte Carlo results by up to 0.2%.

## Method

The authors model the electron cloud around two colliding nuclei as an incompressible, uniformly charged liquid drop of constant volume whose shape minimizes the Coulomb energy of the system (the two point-like ions plus the electron drop). They calculate the mean-field screened Coulomb potential numerically by optimizing the drop shape at each inter-ion separation, then fit the numerical results to an analytic expression that reproduces the correct small-separation expansion and matches the 1/r behavior at large separations. Using this screened potential, they compute the astrophysical S factor via WKB barrier penetration probabilities and the thermonuclear reaction rate assuming a Maxwellian velocity distribution, from which they derive the enhancement factor f = R/R₀.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹²C+¹²C reaction at ρ = 10¹⁰ g cm⁻³, the plasma screening reduces the Coulomb potential by H₀ = 0.702 MeV (compared to 0.151 MeV at ρ = 10⁸ g cm⁻³ and 0.326 MeV at ρ = 10⁹ g cm⁻³).
- At ρ = 10¹⁰ g cm⁻³ and T ≈ 1.5 × 10⁸ K, the screening enhances the reaction rate by more than 20 orders of magnitude.
- The outer turning point for ¹²C+¹²C at T = 8 × 10⁸ K is r₂ = 24.88 fm, at T = 4 × 10⁸ K is 39.49 fm, and at T = 2 × 10⁸ K is 62.69 fm.
- The Gamow peak energy for ¹²C+¹²C at T = 8 × 10⁸ K is E_pS = 2.08 MeV, at T = 4 × 10⁸ K is 1.31 MeV, and at T = 2 × 10⁸ K is 0.827 MeV.
- For equal charges (z=1), the expansion coefficients of the screening potential are b₀ ≈ 1.0573, b₂ = -0.25, and b₄ ≈ 0.0394.
- The analytic fit to the screening potential has formal errors less than 0.2% compared to the numerical electron drop calculations.

## Context

This work extends the original Salpeter ion-sphere model (Salpeter, 1954) by including the full radial dependence of the screening potential rather than just the constant H₀ term, and provides analytic expressions for the expansion coefficients b₀, b₂, and b₄ that were previously derived by Jancovici (1977) and Ogata et al. (1991). The results agree well with Monte Carlo simulations by Chugunov, DeWitt, and Yakovlev (2009) and Itoh et al. (2003), enabling a combined analytic model for strong plasma screening that can be used for simulating nuclear burning in white dwarfs and neutron stars.
