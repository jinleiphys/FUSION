# Azimuthal correlations in diffractive scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We calculate azimuthal correlations between the exclusively produced vector meson and the scattered electron in Deep Inelastic Scattering processes at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). We identify "kinematical" and "intrinsic" contributions to these correlations, and show that the correlations are sensitive to the non-trivial correlations in the gluon distribution of the target. Realistic predictions at the EIC kinematics are provided using two different approaches to describe the dipole-proton interaction at relatively small $x$.

## Key claim
Azimuthal correlations between the exclusively produced vector meson and the scattered electron in Deep Inelastic Scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider are sensitive to non-trivial correlations in the gluon distribution of the target, but the sign and magnitude of the modulation coefficient v2 depend strongly on whether the dipole-proton interaction is described by a Color Glass Condensate approach (impact parameter dependent McLerran-Venugopalan model) or by a light cone perturbation theory calculation, with the two approaches even giving opposite signs.

## Method
The authors calculated the cross section for exclusive J/psi production in electron-proton collisions at EIC kinematics, including differential dependence on squared momentum transfer |t| and azimuthal angle between the outgoing electron and produced vector meson. They used two approaches to describe the dipole-proton interaction at small x: a Color Glass Condensate setup with impact parameter dependent McLerran-Venugopalan model initial conditions evolved via JIMWLK evolution equations, and a light cone perturbation theory dipole amplitude derived from a non-perturbative wave function constrained by low-energy data. The modulation coefficients v1 and v2 were extracted from the azimuthal dependence of the cross section.

## Key numbers
- v2 modulation for exclusive J/psi production at Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 is significant (approximately 5%) in the impact parameter dependent MV model with JIMWLK evolution at EIC kinematics
- v1 modulation for heavy meson production is extremely small, dominated by the off-forward phase factor whose contribution is small when dipole size is approximately 1/(Q^2 + M_J/psi^2)
- At xpom = 0.01, the LCPT dipole calculation gives negative v2 with smaller magnitude compared to the positive v2 from the MV model
- When the LCPT dipole has its dipole orientation dependence artificially removed, a small and positive v2 remains due to the non-zero off-forward phase factor

## Context
This work extends the study of azimuthal correlations in diffractive scattering previously calculated in the same Color Glass Condensate framework by Hatta (2017), and connects to the gluon transversity generalized parton distribution and gluon Wigner distribution as discussed by Hatta (2016) and Mantysaari (2019). The results enable experimental tests at the future Electron-Ion Collider that can distinguish between different models of the proton's small-x gluon structure, particularly the transition from the dilute regime described by light cone perturbation theory to the dense regime described by the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The authors note that much larger modulations are predicted for deeply virtual Compton scattering, but the Bethe-Heitler contribution may complicate interpretation of those measurements.
