# Evolution of structure functions in momentum space

> We formulate the momentum-space Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) evolution equations for structure functions measurable in deeply inelastic scattering. We construct a six-dimensional basis of structure functions that allows for a full three flavor structure and thereby provides a way to calculate perturbative predictions for physical cross sections directly without unobservable parton distribution functions (PDFs) and without the associated scheme dependence. We derive the DGLAP equations to first non-zero order in strong coupling $α_s$, but the approach can be pursued to arbitrary order in perturbation theory. We also numerically check our equations against the conventional PDF formulation.

## Key claim

The paper formulates DGLAP evolution equations directly for physical structure functions measurable in deeply inelastic scattering, constructing a six-dimensional basis that avoids unobservable parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their associated scheme dependence; the derivation is performed to first non-zero order in the strong coupling αs, but the authors state the approach can be extended to arbitrary order in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors invert the leading-order relations between structure functions and PDFs to express quark singlet, gluon, and individual flavor PDFs in terms of the structure functions F2, FL, F3, F2^W-, F3^W-, and F2c^W- (for three active flavors). They then take the logarithmic Q^2 derivative of the structure function expressions and substitute the inverted PDF relations, yielding DGLAP-type evolution equations for the structure functions themselves, with kernels that are independent of factorization scheme and scale. The resulting equations are solved numerically using an ODE solver from the GNU Scientific Library, with initial conditions at Q^2 = 2.0 GeV^2 computed from several leading-order PDF sets (CT14lo_NF3, MSTW2008lo68cl_nf3, NNPDF30_lo_as_0118_nf_3) via the standard coefficient function convolutions.

## Key numbers

- Initial condition for numerical evolution set at Q^2 = 2.0 GeV^2
- Three PDF sets used for comparison: CT14lo_NF3 (Dulat 2015), MSTW2008lo68cl_nf3 (Martin 2010), NNPDF30_lo_as_0118_nf_3 (NNPDF 2014)
- For CT14 and MSTW2008, excellent agreement found between physical-basis evolution and standard PDF-based evolution across all x values
- For NNPDF30_lo_as_0118_nf_3, deviations appear in the region x ≲ 10^{-6}, attributed to a numerical problem with that PDF set failing to satisfy the Q^2 independence of the momentum sum rule
- The six-dimensional basis includes neutral-current structure functions FL, F2, F3 plus charged-current structure functions F2^W-, F3^W-, and F2c^W- (with tagged charm)

## Context

This work extends the concept of a "physical basis" for DGLAP evolution first discussed in the 1980s by Furmanski and others (Furmanski 1981), and later revisited in various contexts by Catani (1996), Blumlein (2000, 2021), Hentschinski (2013), Harland-Lang (2018), van Neerven (1999), and Ruiz Arriola (1998). While previous literature was restricted to special cases or case studies, this paper presents a full dimension-six physical basis corresponding to three active parton flavors, which in principle allows for a global analysis of world DIS and collider data. The approach enables direct calculation of perturbative predictions for physical cross sections without PDFs, evading issues such as PDF positivity (Altarelli 1998, Candido 2020, Collins 2021) and scheme dependence, and could put evidence for small-x resummation (Ball 2017, xFitter Developers Team 2018) on a stronger footing by removing the possibility of mimicking genuine small-x effects through x-dependent factorization scales as done by Hou (2019).
