# A new scheme of causal viscous hydrodynamics for relativistic heavy-ion collisions: A Riemann solver for quark-gluon plasma

> In this article, we present a state-of-the-art algorithm for solving the relativistic viscous hydrodynamics equation with the QCD equation of state. The numerical method is based on the second-order Godunov method and has less numerical dissipation, which is crucial in describing of quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We apply the algorithm to several numerical test problems such as sound wave propagation, shock tube and blast wave problems. In sound wave propagation, the intrinsic numerical viscosity is measured and its explicit expression is shown, which is the second-order of spatial resolution both in the presence and absence of physical viscosity. The expression of the numerical viscosity can be used to determine the maximum cell size in order to accurately measure the effect of physical viscosity in the numerical simulation.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Riemann solver for relativistic ideal hydrodynamics specifically adapted to the QCD equation of state at high temperature and low baryon density, which avoids the singularity that arises in standard approximation schemes when baryon density approaches zero. The scheme is claimed to have numerical viscosity that scales as second-order in spatial resolution, enabling accurate measurement of physical viscosity in simulations of quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors construct an approximate Riemann solver for the QCD equation of state by modifying the Mignone et al. (2005) approximation scheme, which originally relied on specific enthalpy that diverges at vanishing baryon density. They derive analytic expressions for the Taub adiabat and baryon flux across shocks using the approximation that energy density depends only on pressure at zero baryon chemical potential, and solve the contact discontinuity condition via Newton-Raphson iteration. The Riemann solver is then incorporated into a second-order Godunov method for causal viscous hydrodynamics following the scheme of Takamoto et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- Sound wave test: initial pressure amplitude δp = 10⁻¹ fm⁻⁴, background pressure p₀ = 10³ fm⁻⁴, giving δp/p₀ = 10⁻⁴; system length λ = 2 fm
- L1 norm for pressure after one cycle scales as λ δp / N_cell² for the second-order scheme, tested with N_cell = 48, 144, 240, 400, 720, 1200, and 3600 cells
- At RHIC, baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 24 MeV; at LHC, μ_B ≈ 1 MeV (from Andronic, 2011)
- Correction from next-to-leading order in pressure is approximately 0.3(μ_B/T)², yielding 0.4% at RHIC and 0.00075% at LHC
- Lattice QCD parameterization: pressure p ≈ 1-4 T⁴ at T ≈ 200-1000 MeV; baryon susceptibility χ ≈ 0.2-0.3 T² at T ≈ 200-400 MeV (from Borsanyi et al., 2010, 2011)

## Context

This work extends the approximation scheme of Mignone et al. (2005) for relativistic Riemann solvers to the QCD equation of state at low baryon density, which is relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where μ_B is small. The authors compare their scheme against existing numerical methods such as Kurganov-Tadmor (KT), Nessyahu-Tadmor (NT), and SHASTA, claiming advantages in numerical dissipation for viscous hydrodynamic simulations. The scheme enables more precise extraction of transport coefficients like shear viscosity from experimental data by reducing numerical viscosity below the physical viscosity signal.
