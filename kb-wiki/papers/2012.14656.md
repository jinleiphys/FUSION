# New fit of time-like proton electromagnetic form factors from $e^+e^-$ colliders

> The data on the proton form factors in the time-like region from the BaBar, BESIII and CMD-3 Collaborations are examined to have coherent pieces of information on the proton structure. Oscillations in the annihilation cross section, previously observed, are determined with better precision. The moduli of the individual form factors, determined for the first time, their ratio and the angular asymmetry of the annihilation reaction $e^+e^-\to\bar p p$ are discussed. Fiits of the available data on the cross section, the effective form factor, and the form factor ratio, allow to propose a description of the electric and magnetic time-like form factors from the threshold up to the highest momenta.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that regular oscillations previously observed in the BaBar data on the proton effective form factor in the time-like region are also present in the newer BESIII and CMD-3 data, ruling out the possibility that the oscillations are an artefact of the initial state radiation technique or of photon detection.

## Method
The authors performed a global fit of the total cross section data for the reaction e+e- → p pbar from the BaBar, BESIII, and CMD-3 Collaborations, using a function that combines a smooth three-pole background and a damped oscillatory component expressed in terms of the relative momentum p of the final hadrons. They also separately fitted the ratio R = |GE|/|GM| using a monopole function with an oscillatory term, and extracted the individual moduli |GE| and |GM| from the fits of the effective form factor and the ratio.

## Key numbers
- The fit of the effective form factor using the three-pole function (Eq. 3) for the combined BaBar, BESIII-ISR, and BESIII-SC data gave F0 = 8.9 ± 0.2 and ma² = 8.8 ± 0.6 GeV², compared to F0 = 7.7 ± 0.3 and ma² = 15 ± 1 GeV² for BaBar data alone.
- The oscillatory component parameters for the combined data set (BaBar, BESIII-ISR, BESIII-SC) were A = 0.07 ± 0.01, B = 0.93 ± 0.09 GeV⁻¹, C = 5.9 ± 0.1 GeV⁻¹, D = 0.1 ± 0.2, with χ²/n.d.f. = 2.2.
- The six-parameter fit of the annihilation cross section including CMD-3 data gave F0 = 9.7 ± 0.3, ma² = 7.1 ± 0.5 GeV², A = 0.073 ± 0.007, B = 1.05 ± 0.07 GeV⁻¹, C = 5.51 ± 0.09 GeV⁻¹, D = 0.04 ± 0.1, with χ²/(118-6) = 2.5.
- The angular asymmetry A(s) crosses zero at s = (4.62 ± 0.07) GeV², where |GE| = |GM|.
- The common value of the form factors at the production threshold from the fit is F_th ≈ 0.48.

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis of Bianconi (2015) that first identified oscillations in the BaBar data, by incorporating new data from BESIII (Ablikim 2019) and CMD-3 (2018). The results confirm the oscillatory behavior and provide a more precise determination of the oscillation parameters, which have been related to sub-hadron scale processes. The extracted individual form factors |GE| and |GM| are compared with predictions from a QCD model (Tomasi-Gustafsson 2005) and a vector meson dominance model (Bijker 2004), and the work enables future studies of the unphysical region below the production threshold, accessible at the PANDA@FAIR facility.
