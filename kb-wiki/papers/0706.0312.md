# Chiral Perturbation Theory and Baryon Properties

> Theoretical as well as experimental progress has been made in the last decade in describing the properties of baryons. In this review I will mostly report on the theoretical issues. Two non-perturbative methods are privileged frameworks for studying these properties in the low energy domain: chiral perturbation theory, the effective field theory of the Standard Model at energies below 1 GeV and lattice QCD. I will mainly concentrate here on the first one but I will also discuss the complementarity of the two methods. Chiral extrapolations for lattice simulations of some nucleon properties will be investigated. I will then concentrate on processes involving at most two nucleons, describing for example pion-nucleon and pion-deuteron scattering, pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon and the deuteron and doubly virtual Compton scattering. Three flavor calculations will also be reviewed.

## Key claim

Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) provides a model-independent effective field theory framework for describing baryon properties at low energies (below ~1 GeV), but its applicability relies on the assumption of a "large" quark condensate (standard scenario) versus a potentially small condensate (Generalized CHPT), with the standard picture currently favored at least within SU(2) though not definitively settled.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of baryon CHPT, starting from the QCD Lagrangian with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, writing the most general effective Lagrangian in terms of Goldstone boson fields (pions, kaons, eta) and nucleon fields, and expanding in external momenta and quark masses according to a power counting scheme. The review covers both the relativistic formulation and the heavy-baryon approach, discusses the role of explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, and examines the determination of low-energy constants (LECs) through resonance saturation, large-Nc arguments, and lattice QCD matching.

## Key numbers

- The scale of chiral symmetry breaking is Λ_χ ~ 1 GeV
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is F, with the physical value F_π = F(1 + O(M)) where M is the pion mass
- The quark condensate is estimated as -⟨0|q̄q|0⟩^{1/3} ~ 200-370 MeV from lattice simulations
- The nucleon mass in the chiral limit is m_0, with physical mass m differing by terms proportional to quark masses
- The axial vector coupling constant in the chiral limit is g_0, with physical g_A ≡ G_A(0)
- The LECs c_i in the πN Lagrangian at order p^2 are expected to be of order g_A/Λ_χ ~ 1 GeV^{-1}
- The meson LECs l_i are expected to be of order 4/(4π)^2 ~ 0.025
- The Gell-Mann-Okubo relation relating π, K, and η masses is well verified experimentally

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on CHPT by Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) to include baryons, complementing lattice QCD calculations that currently use pion masses far from their physical value. The paper discusses chiral extrapolations for lattice simulations of nucleon properties, enabling connections between lattice results at unphysical quark masses and physical observables, and reviews applications to πN and πd scattering, pion photo- and electroproduction off nucleons and deuterons, and doubly virtual Compton scattering.
