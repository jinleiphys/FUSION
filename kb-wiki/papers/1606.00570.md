# Particle-number conserving analysis of the high-spin structure of $^{159}$Ho

> The high-spin rotational bands in odd-$Z$ nuclei $^{159}$Ho ($Z=67$) are investigated using the cranked shell model with the pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The experimental moments of inertia and alignments and their variations with the rotational frequency $\hbarω$ are reproduced very well by the calculations. The splitting between the signature partners of the yrast band $7/2^-[523]$ is discussed and the splitting of the excited band $7/2^+[404]$ above $\hbarω\sim 0.30$~MeV is predicted due to the level crossing with $1/2^+[411]$. The calculated $B(E2)$ transition probabilities are also suggested for future experiments.

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with a particle-number conserving method, which treats blocking effects exactly, reproduces the experimental moments of inertia and alignments of high-spin rotational bands in $^{159}$Ho very well, but the calculated backbending frequency for the $1/2^-[541]$ band ($\hbar\omega \sim 0.28$ MeV) is much smaller than the data ($\hbar\omega \sim 0.35$ MeV), and the sharp backbending in the experimental moments of inertia is not very well reproduced by the calculation.

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian, including Nilsson, Coriolis, and monopole plus quadrupole pairing terms, was solved directly in a truncated Fock space of cranked many-particle configurations (dimension ~1500 for both protons and neutrons). The particle number was exactly conserved, and Pauli blocking effects were taken into account exactly. The deformation parameters $\varepsilon_2 = 0.227$ and $\varepsilon_4 = -0.0205$ were taken from an average of neighboring even-even Dy and Er isotopes, with slightly modified Nilsson parameters to reproduce experimental bandhead energies.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: $\varepsilon_2 = 0.227$, $\varepsilon_4 = -0.0205$.
- Effective monopole and quadrupole pairing strengths: $G_{\rm 0p}=0.47$ MeV and $G_{\rm 2p}=0.010$ MeV for protons; $G_{\rm 0n}=0.32$ MeV and $G_{\rm 2n}=0.008$ MeV for neutrons.
- Calculated backbending frequency for the $1/2^-[541]$ band: $\hbar\omega \sim 0.28$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of $\hbar\omega \sim 0.35$ MeV.
- Increasing the deformation of the $1/2^-[541]$ band to $\varepsilon_2 = 0.25$ (about 10% larger) only reproduced a delay of about 20 keV in the backbending frequency.
- The splitting between signature partners in the excited band $7/2^+[404]$ is predicted above $\hbar\omega \sim 0.30$ MeV due to a level crossing with $1/2^+[411]$.
- The calculated $B(E2)$ values for the $7/2^+[404]$ band with signature $\alpha=-1/2$ show a jump at $\hbar\omega \sim 0.30$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the particle-number conserving cranked shell model method, previously applied to normally deformed and superdeformed bands in nuclei with $A \approx 160$, 190, and 250 (Zeng 1994, Liu 2002, He 2005, Zhang 2009, He 2009, Zhang 2011, Zhang 2012, Zhang 2013), to the odd-Z nucleus $^{159}$Ho. It addresses the limitations of BCS and HFB approaches, which suffer from particle-number non-conservation and pairing collapse at high spin, and provides a framework for analyzing signature splitting and predicting $B(E2)$ transition probabilities for future experiments. The delayed backbending in the $1/2^-[541]$ band is discussed in the context of the deformation driving effect (Nazarewicz 1990) and possible residual neutron-proton interaction (Yang 1994).
