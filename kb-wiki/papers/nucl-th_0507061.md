# Variational Monte Carlo study of pentaquark states

> Accurate numerical solution of the five-body Schrodinger equation is effected via variational Monte Carlo. The spectrum is assumed to exhibit a narrow resonance with strangeness S=+1. A fully antisymmetrized and pair-correlated five-quark wave function is obtained for the assumed non-relativistic Hamiltonian which has spin, isospin, and color dependent pair interactions and many-body confining terms which are fixed by the non-exotic spectra. Gauge field dynamics are modeled via flux tube exchange factors. The energy determined for the ground states with J=1/2 and negative (positive) parity is 2.22 GeV (2.50 GeV). A lower energy negative parity state is consistent with recent lattice results. The short-range structure of the state is analyzed via its diquark content.

## Key claim

The variational Monte Carlo calculation finds that the lowest-energy pentaquark state has negative parity (2.22 GeV) and lies below the positive parity state (2.50 GeV) by about 280 MeV, consistent with lattice results at large pion mass, but only under the assumption that the theta+ is a narrow resonance treated as a stable state with respect to strong interactions.

## Method

The five-body Schrodinger equation is solved using variational Monte Carlo with a fully antisymmetrized, pair-correlated wave function for a non-relativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian. The Hamiltonian includes spin-, isospin-, and color-dependent pair interactions from one-gluon exchange and one-pion exchange, plus many-body confining terms with flux tube exchange factors modeling gauge field dynamics. The variational wave function is constructed from uncorrelated flavor-spin-color-orbital states of four light quarks (uudd) coupled to a strange antiquark, then acted upon by two-body correlation operators whose parameters are tuned to minimize the variational energy.

## Key numbers

- Ground state mass for negative parity (J=1/2-): 2.22 GeV
- Ground state mass for positive parity (J=1/2+): 2.50 GeV
- Positive parity state masses for other symmetry configurations: 2.57 GeV (n=2;1+), 2.75 GeV (n=3;0+), 2.81 GeV (n=3;1+), 2.83 GeV (n=4;0+), 2.88 GeV (n=4;1+)
- Statistical errors on all masses are less than 5 MeV
- The constant V0(4q-qbar) extrapolated from the single baryon spectrum gives -385.5(5) MeV for the combination 4mq+ms-V0(4q-qbar), using light quark mass mq=313 MeV and strange quark mass ms=550 MeV
- The negative parity state has potential energy 180 MeV below the lowest positive parity state, while the positive parity state has kinetic energy about 450 MeV higher due to orbital angular momentum excitation

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic constituent quark model approach previously applied to single hadrons (Paris 2000) and the six-quark deuteron (Paris 2000) to the five-quark flavor-exotic sector. It provides a fully antisymmetrized variational solution that goes beyond earlier model calculations by Strottman (1979), Jaffe (2003), Karliner (2003), and others, and is consistent with lattice results from Mathur (2004) and Lasscock (2005) in finding the negative parity state lower than positive parity. The calculation enables future work to address chiral symmetry effects that are neglected in the present model and to study the admixture of positive parity states.
