# Study of Proton Induced Reactions in a Radioactive 129-I Target at Ep=660 MeV

> Two NaI (85% 129-I and 15% 127-I) targets were exposed to a beam of 660-MeV protons. Cross sections for formation of 76 residual nuclei were obtained by the induced activity method. The results are compared with other experimental data on 127-I and theoretical calculations by eleven models contained in the codes LAHET3 (using the Bertini+Dresner, ISABEL+Dresner, INCL+Dresner, and INCL+ABLA options), CASCADE, CEM95, CEM2K, LAQGSM+GEM2, CEM2k+GEM2, LAQGSM+GEMINI, and CEM2k+GEMINI. Most of the models describe spallation products with masses close to the target reasonably well while the reliability of the codes differs greatly in the deep spallation and fission/fragmentation regions. The difficulties in describing products with A=40-80 by all of the codes tested here except for CEM2k+GEMINI and LAQGSM+GEMINI is related to the neglect of fission (and fragmentation) processes for targets as light as 129-I.

## Key claim

Cross sections for 76 residual nuclei from 660-MeV proton bombardment of a mixed 85% 129-I and 15% 127-I target were measured, but the experimental cross sections for pure 129-I had to be deduced by subtracting interpolated 127-I data from the mixed-target results. No single model among the eleven tested reproduced all data well, and the best agreement for products in the mass range A=40-80 came only from codes that included fission or fragmentation processes for light targets like iodine.

## Method

Two NaI targets (85% 129-I and 15% 127-I) were irradiated with 660-MeV protons from the JINR Phasotron. Cross sections for 76 residual nuclei were determined using the induced activity method, measuring gamma-ray spectra from the activated targets. The experimental results were compared with calculations from eleven different model codes: LAHET3 (with Bertini+Dresner, ISABEL+Dresner, INCL+Dresner, and INCL+ABLA options), CASCADE, CEM95, CEM2k, LAQGSM+GEM2, CEM2k+GEM2, LAQGSM+GEMINI, and CEM2k+GEMINI. For the pure 129-I cross sections, the authors used previous 127-I measurements from Modolo and Holzbach, reduced to 660 MeV by linear interpolation between 600 and 800 MeV, and subtracted the 127-I contribution from the mixed-target data.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections for 76 residual nuclei were measured from the mixed 85% 129-I and 15% 127-I target at 660 MeV
- For spallation products (A >= 95), the best mean simulated-to-experimental ratio was <F> = 1.55 from LAQGSM+GEMINI, with 16 of 26 comparisons within 30% and 23 within a factor of two
- For all 48 selected isotopes (spallation and fission/fragmentation), the best <F> = 2.78 came from CEM2k+GEMINI, with 12 of 48 within 30% and 29 within a factor of two
- The worst overall performance was from LAQGSM+GEM2 with <F> = 14.37 and S(<F>) = 12.78 for all 48 isotopes
- For spallation products, CEM2k gave <F> = 1.72 with 12 of 26 within 30% and 22 within a factor of two
- The CASCADE code had the poorest spallation performance with <F> = 3.79 and only 9 of 26 within 30%

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by Adam et al. on 237-Np and 241-Am targets at 660 MeV, and earlier studies by Krivopustov et al. on 129-I with 3.7-GeV protons. The paper provides the first comprehensive experimental cross-section dataset for 129-I at 660 MeV, enabling systematic testing of spallation and fission models. The finding that only CEM2k+GEMINI and LAQGSM+GEMINI could describe products in the A=40-80 region highlights the need for improved fission/fragmentation models for light targets, and the authors note that newer calculations with extended GEM2 models (fitting the level-density parameter ratio a_f/a_n) or with the Statistical Multifragmentation Model by Botvina et al. may achieve similar results without invoking fission.
