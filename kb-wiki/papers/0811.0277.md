# Structure of meson-baryon interaction vertices

> We present a microscopic derivation of the form factors of strong-interaction piNN and piNDelta vertices within a relativistic constituent quark model. The results are compared with form factors from phenomenological meson-baryon models and recent lattice QCD calculations. We give an analytical representation of the vertex form factors suitable for applications in further studies of hadron reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free microscopic derivation of the strong-interaction πNN and πNΔ vertex form factors using a relativistic constituent quark model (RCQM), but the results are only qualitatively similar to the bare (undressed) form factors from the Sato-Lee dynamical meson-baryon model, while lattice QCD results show a much slower fall-off with momentum transfer that may or may not be attributable to hadron dressing effects.

## Method

The authors calculate the πNN and πNΔ transition amplitudes in the point form of Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics using a spectator model for the transition operator, with baryon eigenstates from a relativistic constituent quark model that had previously been successful in reproducing baryon mass spectra and electroweak structure. The quark-meson coupling constant is fixed to g²_qqm/4π = 0.67, the same value used in the Goldstone-boson-exchange RCQM, and the off-shell extrapolation is made by keeping all hadrons and quarks on their respective mass shells. The resulting form factors are parametrized using an intermediate monopole-dipole form G(q²) = 1/[1 + (q/Λ₁)² + (q/Λ₂)⁴] and compared with phenomenological models and lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- RCQM πNN coupling constant: f²_N/4π = 0.0691, compared to phenomenological value ≈ 0.075
- RCQM πNΔ coupling constant: f²_Δ/4π = 0.188
- RCQM πNN cut-off parameters: Λ₁ = 0.451 GeV, Λ₂ = 0.931 GeV
- RCQM πNΔ cut-off parameters: Λ₁ = 0.594 GeV, Λ₂ = 0.998 GeV
- Sato-Lee πNN coupling constant: f²_N/4π = 0.08, with Λ₁ = 0.453 GeV, Λ₂ = 0.641 GeV
- Polinder-Rijken πNN coupling constant: f²_N/4π = 0.075, with Λ₁ = 0.940 GeV, Λ₂ = 1.102 GeV
- Lattice QCD (Alexandrou et al., set A) πNN monopole cut-off: Λ = 1.65 GeV, with coupling constant f²_N/4π = 0.0412
- Lattice QCD (Erkol et al.) πNN monopole cut-off: Λ = 0.614 GeV, with coupling constant f²_N/4π = 0.0481
- Lattice QCD (Liu et al.) πNN monopole cut-off: Λ = 0.747 GeV, with coupling constant f²_N/4π = 0.0649
- RCQM πNΔ cut-off parameters are up to 25% larger than the πNN ones

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic constituent quark model studies of baryon resonance decays (Melde 2005, 2006, 2007) to the calculation of strong meson-baryon vertex form factors, which are crucial inputs for meson-exchange NN potentials (such as the Nijmegen, Bonn, and Argonne potentials), three-nucleon forces, and πN dynamical models. The results are compared with the phenomenological form factors from the Sato-Lee (1996) and Polinder-Rijken (2005) dynamical meson-baryon models, as well as with lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou (2007), Erkol (2008), and Liu (1994, 1998). The paper enables future studies that could include explicit meson-cloud dressing effects in relativistic constituent quark models to resolve discrepancies with lattice QCD results.
