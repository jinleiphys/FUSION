# Decorrelation of anisotropic flows along the longitudinal direction

> The initial energy density distribution and fluctuation in the transverse direction lead to anisotropic flows of final hadrons through collective expansion in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Fluctuations along the longitudinal direction, on the other hand, can result in decorrelation of anisotropic flows in different regions of pseudo rapidity ($η$). Decorrelation of the $2$nd and $3$rd order anisotropic flows with different $η$ gaps for final charged hadrons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is studied in an event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic model with fully fluctuating initial conditions from A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model. The decorrelation of anisotropic flows of final hadrons with large $η$ gaps are found to originate from the spatial decorrelation along the longitudinal direction in the AMPT initial conditions through hydrodynamic evolution. The decorrelation is found to consist of both a linear twist and random fluctuation of the event-plane angles. The agreement between our results and recent CMS data in most centralities suggests that the string-like mechanism of initial parton production in AMPT model captures the initial longitudinal fluctuation that is responsible for the measured decorrelation of anisotropic flows in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. Our predictions for Au+Au collisions at the highest RHIC energy show stronger longitudinal decorrelation, indicating larger longitudinal fluctuations at lower beam energies. Our study also calls into question some of the current experimental methods for measuring anisotropic flows and extraction of transport coefficients through comparisons to hydrodynamic simulations that do not include longitudinal fluctuations.

## Key claim

The decorrelation of anisotropic flows along pseudorapidity in high-energy heavy-ion collisions originates from both a linear twist and random fluctuations of event-plane angles in the initial state, and the agreement with CMS data suggests that the string-like mechanism of initial parton production in the AMPT model captures the initial longitudinal fluctuations responsible for the measured decorrelation.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations with fully fluctuating initial conditions from the AMPT model, using the s95p-PCE-v0 equation of state from lattice QCD. They computed the factorization ratio r_n(η_a, η_b) = ⟨Q_n(-η_a) Q_n*(η_b)⟩ / ⟨Q_n(η_a) Q_n*(η_b)⟩ for charged hadrons with transverse momentum cut [0.3, 3.0) GeV/c, comparing results for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with CMS data and making predictions for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The factorization ratio r_2 for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV shows agreement with CMS data across centralities from 0-5% to 40-50%, except for the 0-5% most central collisions where the simulated decorrelation is more linear than the data.
- The decorrelation for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV is predicted to be stronger than at LHC energy, consistent with larger longitudinal fluctuations at lower beam energies.
- The initial thermalization time τ_0 is 0.2 fm for LHC energy and 0.4 fm for RHIC energy.
- The normalization factor K in the initial energy-momentum tensor is 1.5 for LHC energy and 1.4 for RHIC energy, using the s95p-PCE-v0 equation of state.
- Gaussian smearing widths σ_r = 0.6 fm and σ_η_s = 0.6 are used in the initial condition construction, with σ_η_s varied between 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8 to test sensitivity.
- The factorization ratio r_n shows no dependence on the Gaussian smearing width σ_η_s in spatial rapidity.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of longitudinal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using (3+1)D hydrodynamics with AMPT initial conditions (Pang 2012) and the torqued fireball model (Bozek 2010), and directly compares with CMS measurements (Khachatryan 2015). The results call into question current experimental methods for measuring anisotropic flows and extracting transport coefficients through comparisons to hydrodynamic simulations that do not include longitudinal fluctuations, and provide predictions for RHIC that could test the string picture of initial parton production.
