# The Quark Distributions of Baryons

> The recent progress in the investigations on the quark structure of the $Λ$ and $Σ$ baryons are reviewed. It is shown that the quark structure of $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperons can provide a new domain to test various theories concerning the spin and flavor structure of the nucleon. The $Λ$ and $Σ$ Physics might be new directions to explore the quark distributions of baryons both theoretically and experimentally.

# The Quark Distributions of Baryons

## Key claim
The quark structure of Λ and Σ hyperons can provide a new domain to test theories about the spin and flavor structure of the nucleon, but the available Λ-polarization data from e+e- annihilation at the Z-pole cannot yet make a clear distinction between the SU(6) quark-diquark model and perturbative QCD predictions for the flavor and spin structure of the Λ.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical predictions for valence quark distributions in Λ and Σ baryons from the light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model, where the mass difference between scalar and vector diquarks breaks exact SU(6) symmetry. The quark distributions inside the Λ are connected to quark-to-Λ fragmentation functions via a reciprocity relation valid near x=1 and z=1, allowing comparison with Λ-polarization data from e+e- annihilation at the Z-pole. The analysis also covers predictions for Drell-Yan processes with Σ± beams on isoscalar targets.

## Key numbers
- The SU(6) quark-diquark model predicts F₂ⁿ(x)/F₂ᵖ(x) → 1/4 as x → 1, while pQCD predicts F₂ⁿ(x)/F₂ᵖ(x) → 3/7 as x → 1.
- The quark-diquark model predicts Δd(x)/d(x) = -1/3 for the non-dominant valence d quark in the nucleon, while pQCD predicts Δq(x)/q(x) = 1 for both u and d.
- For the Λ at x → 1, the quark-diquark model predicts u(x)/s(x) → 0 for unpolarized distributions, Δs(x)/s(x) → 1 for the dominant s quark, and Δu(x)/u(x) → 1 for the non-dominant u and d quarks.
- The quark-diquark model fit to Λ-polarization data from e+e- annihilation at the Z-pole agrees well within present precision.

## Context
This review extends the author's prior work with Schmidt and Yang (1999, 2000) on using Λ and Σ hyperons to test nucleon structure theories, and builds on the SU(6) quark-diquark model developed by Ma (1996). It contrasts the quark-diquark model predictions with perturbative QCD predictions from Farrar and Jackson (1975) and Brodsky et al. (1995) for the ratio d(x)/u(x) at x → 1. The work enables future measurements of Λ production in neutrino and antineutrino deep inelastic scattering processes, as suggested by Ma and Soffer (1999), which are more sensitive to different quark flavors than the existing e+e- annihilation data.
