# Magnetic Field Induced Polarization Difference between Hyperons and Anti-hyperons

> Recent STAR measurements suggest a difference in the global spin polarization between hyperons and anti-hyperons, especially at relatively low collision beam energy. One possible cause of this difference is the potential presence of in-medium magnetic field. In this study, we investigate the phenomenological viability of this interpretation. Using the AMPT model framework, we quantify the influence of different magnetic field evolution scenarios on the size of the polarization difference in a wide span of collision beam energies. We find that such difference is very sensitive to the lifetime of the magnetic field. For the same lifetime, the computed polarization difference only mildly depends on the detailed form of its evolution. Assuming magnetic polarization as the mechanism to enhance anti-hyperon signal while suppress hyperon signal, we phenomenologically extract an upper limit on the needed magnetic field lifetime in order to account for the experimental data. The so-obtained lifetime values are in a quite plausible ballpark and follow approximately the scaling relation of being inversely proportional to the beam energy. Possible implications on other magnetic field related effects are also discussed.

## Key claim

The observed difference in global spin polarization between hyperons and anti-hyperons, especially at low collision beam energies, can be plausibly explained by the presence of an in-medium magnetic field, provided that the magnetic field lifetime is sufficiently long; the required lifetimes follow an approximate scaling of being inversely proportional to beam energy and are in a plausible ballpark, but the study does not address how such a long-lived field would arise dynamically.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV, extracting the four-velocity and energy density distributions. They computed the spin polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons from a formula that combines the thermal vorticity (including both kinetic vorticity and temperature gradient terms) with a magnetic field term proportional to the hyperon magnetic moment and the local magnetic field. Three different parameterized forms for the time evolution of the magnetic field (type-1: Lorentzian squared, type-2: Lorentzian cubed, type-3: exponential) were tested, each controlled by a single lifetime parameter t_B, and the resulting polarization difference was compared to STAR data to extract the optimal lifetime at each energy.

## Key numbers

* The initial magnetic field strength at the center point eB_0/m_pi^2 equals 0.222, 0.282, 0.383, 0.528, 0.764, 1.235, and 3.922 for beam energies sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 63, and 200 GeV respectively.
* The optimal magnetic field lifetime t_B needed to explain the polarization splitting is about 5 fm/c at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV and about 0.5 fm/c at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
* The extracted lifetimes follow the scaling t_B = A / sqrt(s_NN) with A = 92 GeV*fm/c for type-1, A = 125 GeV*fm/c for type-2, and A = 128 GeV*fm/c for type-3; an average over types gives A = 115 +/- 16 GeV*fm/c.
* The vacuum magnetic field lifetime estimate is t_vac ~ 26 GeV*fm/c / sqrt(s_NN), considerably shorter than the extracted values.

## Context

This work extends the puzzle of the hyperon/anti-hyperon polarization difference observed by the STAR Collaboration (STAR 2017, Adam 2018), which could not be explained by vorticity-induced polarization alone. Prior studies by Becattini (2016), Muller (2018), and Guo (2019) had proposed magnetic fields as a possible cause. The present study quantifies this scenario across a wide energy range and extracts empirical constraints on the magnetic field lifetime, enabling future dynamical simulations of magnetic field evolution and providing input for other magnetic-field-related effects such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect.
