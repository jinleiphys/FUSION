# Structure of neutron stars with unified equations of state

> We present a set of three unified equations of states (EoSs) based on the nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) theory.These EoSs are based on generalized Skyrme forces fitted to essentially all experimental atomic mass data and constrained to reproduce various properties of infinite nuclear matter as obtained from many-body calculations using realistic two- and three-body interactions. The structure of cold isolated neutron stars is discussed in connection with some astrophysical observations.

## Key claim
The paper presents three unified equations of state (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) for cold isolated neutron stars, but the softest EoS (BSk19) is ruled out by the measured mass of PSR J1614−2230, suggesting the dense core may contain non-nucleonic matter.

## Method
The authors constructed three unified EoSs using generalized Skyrme energy-density functionals (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) fitted to 2149 experimental atomic masses with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV, and constrained to reproduce neutron-matter EoSs of varying stiffness from many-body calculations. The outer crust was treated with the BPS model using experimental and theoretical HFB masses, the inner crust with a 4th-order Thomas-Fermi method plus perturbative proton shell corrections, and the core as uniform nucleon-lepton matter. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved with the LORENE library to compute neutron-star structure.

## Key numbers
- The EDFs were fitted to 2149 measured masses of nuclei with N and Z ≥ 8 from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation, with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV.
- The outer crust spans densities from a few times 10⁴ g cm⁻³ to ∼4 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³.
- The liquid core begins above ∼10¹⁴ g cm⁻³.
- The maximum mass predicted by BSk19 is too low to reach the 2 M⊙ mass of PSR J1614−2230 (including error bars), even accounting for rotation effects (Chamel 2011).

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme-EDF approach of Goriely (2010) by constructing fully unified EoSs covering the outer crust, inner crust, and core consistently. The results contradict the softest EoS (BSk19), which is favored by heavy-ion collision data (Xiao 2009), but is ruled out by the pulsar mass measurement of Demorest (2010). The findings enable further study of non-nucleonic matter in neutron-star cores, as discussed by Chamel (2012).
