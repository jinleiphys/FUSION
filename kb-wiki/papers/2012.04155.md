# Study of angular momentum effects in fission

> Background: The role of angular momentum in fission has long been discussed but the observable effects are difficult to quantify. Purpose: We discuss a variety of effects associated with angular momentum in fission and present quantitative illustrations. Methods: We employ the fission simulation model $\mathtt{FREYA}$ which is well suited for this purpose because it obeys all conservation laws, including linear and angular momentum conservation at each step of the process. We first discuss the implementation of angular momentum in $\mathtt{FREYA}$ and then assess particular observables, including various correlated observables. We also study potential effects of neutron-induced fission of the low-lying isomeric state of $^{235}$U relative to the ground state. Results: The fluctuations inherent in the fission process ensure that the spin of the initial compound nucleus has only a small influence on the fragment spins which are therefore nearly uncorrelated. There is a marked correlation between the spin magnitude of the fission fragments and the photon multiplicity. We also consider the dynamical anisotropy caused by the rotation of an evaporating fragment and study especially the distribution of the projected neutron-neutron opening angles, showing that while it is dominated by the effect of the evaporation recoils, it is possible to extract the signal of the dynamical anisotropy by means of a Fourier decomposition. Finally, we note that the use of an isomeric target, $^{235 {\rm m}}$U($n_{\rm th}$,f), may enhance the symmetric yields and can thus result in higher neutron multiplicities for low total fragment kinetic energy.

## Key claim

The fragment spins in fission are dominated by statistical fluctuations acquired at scission rather than by the angular momentum of the initial compound nucleus, making the two fragment spins nearly uncorrelated; the observable effects of fragment rotation on neutron angular distributions are small and difficult to isolate from the dominant evaporation recoil effects.

## Method

The authors used the FREYA Monte Carlo fission event generator, which conserves linear and angular momentum at each step, to simulate complete fission events for neutron-induced fission of 235U and spontaneous fission of 252Cf. They sampled fragment spins from the statistical distribution of the four perpendicular rotational modes (wriggling and bending) at scission, then simulated the full evaporation cascades including the centrifugal boost on evaporated neutrons from rotating fragments and the subsequent photon emission.

## Key numbers

- For thermal neutron-induced fission of 235U, the angular momentum brought in by the neutron is negligible: S0 ≈ 0.34 ħ on average
- At En = 20 MeV, the angular momentum of the fissioning compound nucleus is S0 ≈ 5 ħ, but the fragments acquire only ≈ 0.26 ħ on average from this overall rotation
- The mean spin fluctuations are 4.8 ħ for the light fragment and 6.4 ħ for the heavy fragment in thermal fission of 235U; at En = 20 MeV they are 5.8 ħ and 7.1 ħ respectively
- The distribution of the opening angle between the two fragment spins can be approximated as P(ϕLH) ≈ 1 + f2 cos ϕLH with Fourier amplitude f2 ≈ −0.082, giving P(180°)/P(0°) ≈ 1.18
- The dynamical anisotropy A for 235U(nth,f) is ≈ 0.093
- The Legendre coefficients for the neutron angular distribution relative to the fragment spin direction in 235U(nth,f) are α2 = −0.061 and α4 = 0.0056
- The slope of the mean photon multiplicity versus total fragment spin for large S is dNγ/dS ≈ 0.84 for the standard moment of inertia reduction factor cI = 0.5
- The projected neutron-neutron opening angle undulation amplitude c2 is ≈ 0.042% in the ideal scenario and ≈ 0.058% with a 0.9 MeV energy threshold for 252Cf(sf) with enhanced spin temperature

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the FREYA model (Vogt and Randrup, 2014, 2018) and their earlier study of angular momentum effects in fission (Vogt and Randrup, 2017). It provides quantitative support for the long-standing qualitative understanding that fragment spins are primarily determined at scission rather than inherited from the compound nucleus, as discussed in earlier experimental work by Wilhelmy (1972) and theoretical work by Nix and Swiatecki (1965) and Moretto (1975). The results enable experimentalists to interpret measurements of photon multiplicities as a proxy for fragment angular momenta, and provide a framework for analyzing proposed dynamical anisotropy signals such as those introduced by Chietera (2018).
