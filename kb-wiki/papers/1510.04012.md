# Crystalline Ground States in Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Models

> Nambu--Jona-Lasinio-type models have been used extensively to study the dynamics of the theory of the strong interaction at finite temperature and quark chemical potential on a phenomenological level. In addition to these studies, which are often performed under the assumption that the ground state of the theory is homogeneous, searches for the existence of crystalline phases associated with inhomogeneous ground states have attracted a lot of interest in recent years. In this work, we study the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and find that the existence of a crystalline phase is stable against a variation of the parametrization of the underlying Polyakov loop potential. To this end, we adopt two prominent parametrizations. Moreover, we observe that the existence of a quarkyonic phase depends crucially on the parametrization, in particular in the regime of the phase diagram where inhomogeneous chiral condensation is favored.

## Key claim
The existence of a crystalline (inhomogeneous) phase in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model is stable against variation of the parametrization of the Polyakov loop potential, but the existence of a quarkyonic phase depends crucially on whether the parameter T0 is taken as constant or as dependent on the quark chemical potential.

## Method
The authors compute the mean-field effective potential of the two-flavor PNJL model in the chiral limit using the fermion doubling trick, which allows detection of second-order chiral phase transitions. They search numerically for the saddle point in the space of the Polyakov loop variables Φ and Φ̄, the chiral condensate amplitude σ̄, and the wave-vector Q of a single-cosine ansatz for the inhomogeneous order parameter. Two parametrizations of the Polyakov loop potential are compared: one with constant T0 = 208 MeV and one with a μ-dependent T0(μ) following Schaefer (2007).

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass m_q ≈ σ̄_0 = 325 MeV and pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV are used to fix the coupling λ̄Λ² ≈ 4.34 and UV cutoff Λ = 651 MeV at T = μ = 0.
- Chiral phase transition temperature at μ = 0 is T_χ = 227 MeV for the μ-dependent T0 parametrization.
- For the μ-dependent T0 parametrization, the Lifshitz point is shifted to lower temperatures and larger μ compared to the constant T0 case, and the inhomogeneous phase shrinks.
- For constant T0 at μ/m_q = 1.02, the deconfinement transition (defined by ⟨Φ⟩ = 0.5) occurs at T/T_χ,0 ≈ 0.8, well above the chiral transition from the inhomogeneous phase; for μ-dependent T0 at the same μ, ⟨Φ⟩ ≈ 0.5 coincides with the chiral transition temperature T_χ(μ).

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous phases in NJL-type models (Nickel, 2009; Kojo, 2009) by coupling them to Polyakov loop dynamics, following the PNJL framework of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005). It directly addresses the ambiguity in the Polyakov loop potential parametrization highlighted by Schaefer (2007), who found that a μ-dependent T0 eliminates the quarkyonic phase in homogeneous studies. The present results show that the inhomogeneous phase persists under both parametrizations, but the quarkyonic phase (confined but chirally symmetric) appears only with constant T0, even when inhomogeneous condensation is allowed. The work enables future studies using functional renormalization group methods to compute the Polyakov loop potential with quark backreaction, which could resolve the remaining uncertainties.
