# $pp\to ppπ^0$ near threshold in pionless effective field theory

> In this talk, we review our recent calculation for the $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction near threshold in pionless effective field theory with a di-baryon and external pions.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory (EFT) with a di-baryon and external pions can reproduce the measured near-threshold total cross section for $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reasonably well, but only for one specific sign choice of the unknown low-energy constant $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$; the opposite sign leads to almost vanishing cross sections due to cancellation between the one-body and two-body amplitudes.

## Method

The author constructed a pionless EFT Lagrangian containing a $^1S_0$ di-baryon field, an external-pion–di-baryon–two-nucleon contact interaction with unknown low-energy constant $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$, and a $^3P_0$ two-nucleon contact interaction. The $S$-wave $pp$ scattering amplitude was matched to effective-range parameters including Coulomb effects, and the $^3P_0$ LEC was fixed to the phase shift at threshold ($\delta_p(p_{th})\simeq -7.5^\circ$ at $p_{th}\simeq \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$). The total cross section was then computed from the sum of one-body and two-body amplitudes, including strong initial-state and strong-plus-Coulomb final-state interactions, with $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$ either estimated from a one-pion-exchange loop diagram in chiral perturbation theory or fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The $^3P_0$ phase shift at threshold is $\delta_p(p_{th})\simeq -7.5^\circ$ at $p_{th}\simeq \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$.
* The LEC $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$ estimated from the one-pion-exchange diagram is $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}\simeq \pm 0.140\ \text{fm}^{5/2}$.
* The fitted value of $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$ that reproduces the experimental data over the whole near-threshold energy range is $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)\text{fitted}} = -0.12\ \text{fm}^{5/2}$ (with a second solution $+0.55\ \text{fm}^{5/2}$).
* The chiral LECs used in the one-pion-exchange estimate are $c_1 = -0.64$, $c_2 = 1.79$, $c_3 = -3.90$ (all in GeV$^{-1}$).

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT with di-baryon and external pions, previously applied to $np\to d\gamma$ and neutron-neutron fusion by Ando and collaborators, to the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction. It contrasts with heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT) calculations by Park et al. (1996), Cohen et al. (1996), and Sato et al. (1997), which suffered from large cancellations at NLO and poor convergence of the chiral series. The approach enables a systematic treatment where the only unknown parameter is the contact LEC $\tilde{d}_\pi^{(2)}$, and it confirms that the energy dependence of the cross section is dominated by phase space and final-state interactions, as noted by Meyer et al. (1992).
