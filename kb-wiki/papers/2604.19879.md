# A First Account of the Impact of Ion Electromagnetic Dissociation on Event Exclusivity in Ultraperipheral LHC Collisions

> In this Letter we explore the modelling of hadron production in electromagnetic ion dissociation (EMD) processes in high-energy ultraperipheral collisions at LHC energies. Since EMD can accompany exclusive particle production in these interactions, we demonstrate that the resulting hadrons can break the exclusivity vetos typically imposed by experiments. As two representative examples, we calculate the impact on existing LHC measurements of exclusive muon pair production ($γγ\toμμ$) and exclusive coherent $J/ψ$ production. We demonstrate that accounting for this effect resolves long-standing tensions between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hadrons produced by high-energy electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) of ions can break the exclusivity vetos in ultraperipheral LHC collisions, and that accounting for this effect resolves long-standing tensions between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements for exclusive muon pair and coherent J/psi production, but only if the photon energy in the EMD process is sufficiently high to produce particles at mid-rapidity.

## Method

The authors model hadron production in high-energy gamma-Pb EMD using the Pythia 8.316 Monte Carlo generator with the Angantyr model in a gamma-Pb beam configuration. They calculate veto-breaking probabilities as a function of incident photon energy, parameterized from simulated charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions, and apply these to correct the lowest-order EMD probability in the SuperChic and STARlight MC generators. The corrected probabilities are then used to recalculate cross sections and EMD event fractions for exclusive dimuon production and coherent J/psi production, comparing with ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- For exclusive dimuon production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, the predicted event fraction failing the ATLAS exclusivity veto is 5-10% in the lower mass, central rapidity region, approaching 10-20% at high mass.
- For coherent J/psi production, the values of the 0nXn photon flux are reduced by about 20-25%, and the XnXn photon flux by about 45-50% due to the EMD veto-breaking effect, relative to default STARlight predictions.
- The ALICE measurement reports correction factors epsilon^{0nXn}_{EMD}=0.88 and epsilon^{XnXn}_{EMD}=0.84 for forward J/psi production, which the authors find to be significantly underestimated.
- For high-W_{gamma N} data, the values of sigma(gamma Pb -> J/psi Pb) increase by about a factor of two after applying EMD veto corrections.
- The nuclear shadowing factor S_g=0.4 is used as baseline, with variations of +/-50% for model uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the modelling of EMD processes in UPCs beyond the standard treatment that only considers forward neutron emission, building on prior calculations by Baltz (2009) and Harland-Lang (2023) for EMD probabilities. It directly addresses tensions between SuperChic MC predictions and ATLAS dimuon data noted by Harland-Lang (2021), and between theoretical models and CMS/ALICE coherent J/psi data. The methodology enables future LHC measurements to properly account for exclusivity veto effects, and the authors note that a dedicated LHC measurement of gamma-Pb cross sections at high photon energies could help constrain the dominant model uncertainty from nuclear shadowing.
