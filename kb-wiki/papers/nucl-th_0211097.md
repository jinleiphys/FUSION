# In medium sigma meson effects in two pion photoproduction in nuclei

> We show theoretical results for $(γ, π^0 π^0)$ production on nucleons and nuclei in the kinematical region where the scalar isoscalar $ππ$ amplitude is influenced by the $σ$ pole. The final state interaction of the pions modified by the nuclear medium produces a spectacular shift of strength of the two pion invariant mass distribution induced by the moving of the $σ$ pole to lower masses and widths as the nuclear density increases.

# In medium sigma meson effects in two pion photoproduction in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper shows that the shift of strength to lower invariant masses in the two pion invariant mass distribution for the (γ, π⁰π⁰) reaction on nuclei provides a clear signature of the modified ππ interaction in the nuclear medium, which can be interpreted as the σ meson pole moving to lower masses and widths as nuclear density increases. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on comparing their theoretical results to preliminary experimental data from Messchendorp (2002).

## Method

The authors model the elementary (γ, ππ) reaction using tree level diagrams that couple photons to mesons, nucleons, and the resonances Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), and Δ(1700), following the approach of Nacher (2000). They implement final state interaction of the two pions by factorizing the on-shell tree level γN → ππN and ππ → ππ amplitudes in loop functions, using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest order chiral amplitude for ππ → ππ in isospin I=0. For nuclei, they replace the free loop function G and ππ scattering matrix t with their in-medium counterparts from Chiang (1998), evaluated at local density, and calculate the cross section using many-body techniques from the imaginary part of the photon selfenergy with a particle-hole excitation and two pion lines.

## Key numbers

- The two pion invariant mass distribution for (γ, π⁰π⁰) on ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb shows an appreciable shift of strength to the low invariant mass region when using the in-medium ππ interaction compared to the free one.
- The shift is not seen in the (γ, π±π⁰) channel because π±π⁰ are not allowed to be in isospin I=0.
- The results are compared to preliminary measurements from Messchendorp (2002), which show a remarkably similar shift.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of unitary extensions of chiral perturbation theory (UχPT) to finite nuclear density, building on the model of Chiang (1998) for in-medium ππ scattering and the elementary (γ, ππ) reaction model of Nacher (2000). The (γ, π⁰π⁰) reaction is argued to be better suited than the (π, ππ) reaction for investigating ππ modification in nuclear matter because photons are not distorted by the nucleus and can test higher densities. The results provide evidence that the σ meson is dynamically generated by multiple scattering of pions through underlying chiral dynamics, and enable future experimental confirmation with higher-statistics data.
