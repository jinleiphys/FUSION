# The impact of domain walls on the chiral magnetic effect in hot QCD matter

> The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) -- the separation of positive and negative electric charges along the direction of the external magnetic field in quark-gluon plasma and other topologically non-trivial media -- is a consequence of the coupling of electrodynamics to the topological gluon field fluctuations that form metastable $CP$-odd domains. In phenomenological models it is usually assumed that the domains are uniform and the influence of the domain walls on the electric current flow is not essential. This paper challenges the latter assumption. A simple model consisting of a uniform spherical domain in a uniform time-dependent magnetic field is introduced and analytically solved. It is shown that (i) no electric current flows into or out of the domain, (ii) the charge separation current, viz. the total electric current flowing inside the domain in the external field direction, is a dissipative Ohm current, (iii) the CME effect can be produced either by the anomalous current or by the boundary conditions on the domain wall and (iv) the charge separation current oscillates in plasma long after the external field decays. These properties are qualitatively different from the CME in an infinite medium.

## Key claim

The paper challenges the standard assumption that domain walls in hot QCD matter are irrelevant for the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), showing through an analytically solvable model that the charge separation current inside a finite spherical domain is a dissipative Ohm current rather than an anomalous current, and that no electric current flows into or out of the domain.

## Method

The author solves the Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) equations for a uniform spherical domain of radius \(R\) with a constant pseudoscalar field \(\Theta\) inside, immersed in a topologically trivial medium, under a uniform time-dependent external magnetic field. The solutions inside and outside the domain are expanded in eigenfunctions of the curl operator (vector spherical harmonics) and matched using boundary conditions derived from the MCS theory, including the condition that the normal component of the magnetic field vanishes on the domain wall. The total electric current in the direction of the external field is then computed analytically.

## Key numbers

* The chiral anomaly coefficient is \(c_A = N_c \sum_f q_f^2 e^2 / 2\pi^2\); for QCD with \(N_c=3\) and three light quarks, \(c_A = 1/129\).
* The axial chemical potential is estimated as \(\mu_5 = 1.3\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for an optimistic chiral conductivity \(\sigma_\chi = 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
* The first three zeros of the spherical Bessel function \(j_1(x)\) are \(x_1 = 4.49\), \(x_2 = 7.73\), and \(x_3 = 10.90\); these determine the resonance frequencies \(\omega_n = x_n / R\) of the spherical domain.
* For a domain radius \(R < 2.6\ \mathrm{fm}\), the magnetic field inside the domain is suppressed by a factor \(e^{-t_0 x_1 / R}\) where \(t_0 = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}\) is the characteristic decay time of the external magnetic field.
* The electrical conductivity of QGP is taken as \(\sigma = 1/(36\ \mathrm{fm})\) and the chiral conductivity as \(\sigma_\chi = 1/(100\ \mathrm{fm})\) in the numerical examples.

## Context

This work extends the standard CME phenomenology (Fukushima, Kharzeev, Warringa 2008; Kharzeev 2009) by incorporating finite-size effects of \(CP\)-odd topological domains, which are typically assumed to be uniform and infinite. The model is an exactly solvable case of the Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory (Wilczek 1987; Carroll, Field, Jackiw 1990) applied to heavy-ion collisions. The results enable more realistic modeling of charge separation in quark-gluon plasma, showing that the CME signal depends critically on domain size and that the current oscillates long after the external magnetic field decays, which may affect experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions.
