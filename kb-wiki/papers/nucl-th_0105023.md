# Comprehensive calculations of three--body breakup cross sections

> We present in detail a theoretical model for fragmentation reactions of three--body halo nuclei. The different reaction mechanisms corresponding to the different processes are described and discussed. Coulomb and nuclear interactions are simultaneously included and the method is therefore applicable for any target, light, intermediate and heavy. Absolute values of many differential cross sections are then available as function of beam energy and target. We apply the method to fragmentation of $^6$He and $^{11}$Li on C, Cu and Pb. A large variety of observables, cross sections and momentum distributions, are computed. In almost all cases we obtain good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a comprehensive theoretical model for fragmentation reactions of three-body halo nuclei that simultaneously includes Coulomb and nuclear interactions, making it applicable to any target (light, intermediate, or heavy). The model claims good agreement with available experimental data "in almost all cases," leaving room for discrepancies that are not detailed in the abstract.

## Method

The model describes the fragmentation reaction as the sum of all possible processes where one, two, or all three halo constituents interact simultaneously with the target, using a participant-spectator separation. For large momentum transfer (violent collisions), the final state is treated as two independent subsystems (target-plus-participant and two spectators) using distorted wave Born approximation; for small momentum transfer (gentle Coulomb excitation), the final state is treated as a three-body continuum wave function expanded in adiabatic hyperspherical basis functions. The method divides momentum transfer into large and small values at a grazing impact parameter, with large momentum transfer contributions described by elastic and absorption cross sections from optical model participant-target scattering, and small momentum transfer contributions described by Coulomb dissociation using the adiabatic approximation.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy threshold: the model is applicable for beam energies above 50 MeV/nucleon
- The method is applied to fragmentation of $^6$He and $^{11}$Li on three targets: C, Cu, and Pb
- The grazing impact parameter separating low and large momentum transfer is given by $b_g = R_0 + R_c + \pi a /2$, where $a = \hbar c Z_0 Z_c e^2 / 2 E_{kin}$ is half the distance of closest core-target approach
- The adiabatic cutoff momentum transfer is $q_a = (Z_0 Z_i e^2)/(\hbar c)^2 \cdot \hbar \omega_a / (\gamma - 1)$, where $\hbar \omega_a$ is the adiabatic cutoff energy estimated as approximately the three-body binding energy $B$
- The minimum momentum transfer for dissociation from energy conservation is $q_L c \approx B \sqrt{1 + m_0^2 c^2 / p_0^2}$, reducing to $B/v$ in the non-relativistic limit

## Context

This work extends earlier models that treated nuclear and Coulomb breakup separately, such as the sudden approximation for two-neutron halos (Garrido 1996, 1997) and the participant-spectator model with optical model interactions (Garrido 1998). Previous approaches were limited to either light targets (nuclear breakup only) or heavy targets (Coulomb dissociation only), with intermediate targets requiring separate calculations that were then simply added (Bertsch 1993, Barranco 1993). The model enables computation of absolute values for all differential breakup cross sections (two-neutron removal, core breakup, interaction cross sections, and momentum distributions) on any target, and addresses the controversial reaction mechanisms that were previously unresolved (Garrido 2000a, 2000b, Ershov 2000).
