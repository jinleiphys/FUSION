# Entanglement in Reggeized Scattering using AdS/CFT

> The eikonalized parton-parton scattering amplitude at large $\sqrt{s}$ and large impact parameter, is dominated by the exchange of a hyperbolic surface in walled AdS. Its analytical continuation yields a worldsheet instanton that is at the origin of the Reggeization of the amplitude and a thermal-like quantum entropy ${\cal S}_T$. We explicitly construct the entangled density matrix following from the exchanged surface, and show that its von-Neumann entanglement entropy ${\cal S}_E$ coincides with the thermal-like entropy, i.e. ${\cal S}_T={\cal S}_E$. The ratio of the entanglement entropy to the transverse growth of the exchanged surface is similar to the Bekenstein entropy ratio for a black-hole, with a natural definition of saturation and the on-set of chaos in high energy collisions. The largest eigenvalues of the entangled density matrix obey a cascade equation in rapidity, reminiscent of non-linear QCD evolution of wee-dipoles at low-x and weak coupling. We suggest that the largest eigenvalues describe the probability distributions of wee-quanta at low-x and strong coupling that maybe measurable at present and future pp and ep colliders.

## Key claim
The eikonalized parton-parton scattering amplitude at large sqrt(s) and large impact parameter is dominated by the exchange of a hyperbolic surface in walled AdS, and the von-Neumann entanglement entropy of the exchanged surface coincides exactly with the thermal-like entropy previously identified, i.e. S_T = S_E, with the caveat that this equality holds for the specific holographic string description and the identification of rapidity with the logarithm of the number of string bits.

## Method
The authors used the AdS/CFT correspondence to evaluate the eikonalized parton-parton scattering amplitude via Wilson loop correlators dominated by minimal surface exchange in a slice of AdS. They analytically continued the hyperbolic worldsheet solution to obtain a worldsheet instanton, then discretized the transverse string fluctuations as coupled harmonic oscillators and computed the von-Neumann entanglement entropy by splitting the density matrix into subsystems following the method of Srednicki (1993). The entanglement entropy was compared to the thermal entropy derived from the Casimir energy (Luscher term) of the worldsheet instanton.

## Key numbers
- The entanglement entropy per transverse dimension for a string with fixed ends is S_E(n,N) = (D_perp/6) ln(n) for 1 << n << N, with D_perp = 3, matching the thermal entropy S_T = (D_perp/6) chi where chi is the rapidity.
- The Pomeron intercept is alpha_P(0) = 1 + D_perp/12 = 1.25 for D_perp = 3.
- The saturation momentum from the Bekenstein-like relation is Q_S^2 = 1/(6 pi alpha_prime), giving Q_S approximately 0.5 GeV for a string length sqrt(alpha_prime) = 0.1 fm.
- The saturation rapidity for pp scattering is estimated as chi_S approximately 10.6, corresponding to collision energy sqrt(s)/m_N approximately 200.
- The entanglement entropy growth rate in Gribov time is dS_E/dt_chi = D_perp/(3 alpha_prime) = kappa_L.

## Context
This work extends the holographic approach to Reggeized scattering developed by Stoffers and Zahed (2012) and Basar et al. (2012), who first identified the thermal-like entropy in the exchanged worldsheet instanton. The authors explicitly construct the entangled density matrix and prove the equality of thermal and entanglement entropies, connecting to the broader program of using AdS/CFT to understand entropy deposition and thermalization in high-energy collisions. The result enables a holographic interpretation of wee-dipole distributions at low-x and strong coupling, with the largest eigenvalues of the entangled density matrix obeying a cascade equation reminiscent of non-linear QCD evolution of wee-dipoles at weak coupling as described by Mueller. The black-hole analogy suggests a natural saturation scale and chaos bound for the entropy production rate.
