# Quadrupole Collective Dynamics from Energy Density Functionals: Collective Hamiltonian and the Interacting Boson Model

> Microscopic energy density functionals (EDF) have become a standard tool for nuclear structure calculations, providing an accurate global description of nuclear ground states and collective excitations. For spectroscopic applications this framework has to be extended to account for collective correlations related to restoration of symmetries broken by the static mean field, and for fluctuations of collective variables. In this work we compare two approaches to five-dimensional quadrupole dynamics: the collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrations and rotations, and the Interacting Boson Model. The two models are compared in a study of the evolution of non-axial shapes in Pt isotopes. Starting from the binding energy surfaces of $^{192,194,196}$Pt, calculated with a microscopic energy density functional, we analyze the resulting low-energy collective spectra obtained from the collective Hamiltonian, and the corresponding IBM-2 Hamiltonian. The calculated excitation spectra and transition probabilities for the ground-state bands and the $γ$-vibration bands are compared to the corresponding sequences of experimental states.

## Key claim

The paper compares two beyond-mean-field approaches to quadrupole collective dynamics, the collective Hamiltonian and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2), starting from the same microscopic relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) binding energy surfaces for \(^{192,194,196}\)Pt, but the mapping to the IBM cannot reproduce the shallow triaxial minima of the RHB surfaces because the IBM Hamiltonian is too restricted to produce a triaxial minimum, leading to differences in the predicted \(\gamma\)-band structure.

## Method

The authors performed constrained RHB calculations using the relativistic functional DD-PC1 with a separable pairing force to generate binding energy surfaces in the \(\beta-\gamma\) plane for \(^{192,194,196}\)Pt. From these surfaces, they constructed a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrations and rotations, with deformation-dependent mass parameters and moments of inertia computed in the cranking and Inglis-Belyaev approximations. Separately, they mapped the RHB energy surfaces onto the IBM-2 Hamiltonian by equating the boson energy surface to the microscopic one up to \(\approx 2\) MeV above the minimum, determining the IBM coupling constants \(\epsilon\), \(\kappa\), \(\chi_\pi\), and \(\chi_\nu\) through the mapping procedure.

## Key numbers

- The energy ratio \(R_{4/2} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) for \(^{192}\)Pt is 2.59 with the collective Hamiltonian and 2.69 with the IBM, compared to the experimental value of 2.48.
- The IBM energy surfaces are mapped to reproduce the RHB surfaces up to \(\approx 2\) MeV above the mean-field minimum.
- The RHB energy surfaces for \(^{192,194,196}\)Pt show minima at \(\gamma \approx 30^\circ\) (triaxial), with the softest nucleus being \(^{196}\)Pt.
- The IBM parameters satisfy \(\chi_\pi + \chi_\nu \sim 0\), characteristic of a \(\gamma\)-soft energy surface.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that derived IBM Hamiltonians from microscopic energy density functionals (Nomura et al., 2010; Nomura et al., 2011) and earlier collective Hamiltonian calculations based on relativistic functionals (Nikšić et al., 2009; Li et al., 2009, 2010). It directly compares two approximations, the collective Hamiltonian and the IBM, starting from the same RHB energy surfaces, which had not been done systematically before. The results enable future improvements to the IBM by including three-body (cubic) terms to better reproduce \(\gamma\)-band staggering, and by incorporating \(\hat L \cdot \hat L\) terms for rotational nuclei where the simple mapping fails.
