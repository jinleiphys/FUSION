# Photon radiation from heavy-ion collisions in the $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19-200$ GeV regime

> We present calculations of prompt and thermal photon production in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19-200$~GeV. We discuss features of the spacetime profile of the plasma relevant for electromagnetic emission. We highlight how the suppression of prompt photon production at low $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ can provide a window to measure thermal photons in low collision energies.

## Key claim

The suppression of prompt photon production at low center-of-mass energies (large $x_T^\gamma = 2 p_T^\gamma / \sqrt{s_{NN}}$) can provide a window to measure thermal photons in low collision energies, but the extrapolation procedure used for low-$p_T^\gamma$ prompt photons introduces significant uncertainty, especially for low $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$.

## Method

The spacetime evolution of the quark-gluon plasma in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19-200$ GeV was modeled using 3+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with dynamical initialization for energy deposition over an extended period. Thermal photon production was calculated by convoluting the spacetime profile from hydrodynamics with a photon emission rate: a hadronic rate for temperatures below 180 MeV and a perturbative QCD rate above this temperature. Prompt photons were calculated with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD, using an extrapolation procedure to obtain low-$p_T^\gamma$ photons.

## Key numbers

- Average temperature at $\eta_s \approx 0$ is not significantly higher at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV than at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV, because the low-temperature spacetime volume dominates the average.
- The baryon chemical potential at $\eta_s \approx 0$ in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV collisions corresponds approximately to the baryon chemical potential at $\eta_s \approx \pm 2.5$ in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV collisions.
- The central plateau in spatial rapidity extends two to three units at high collision energies and essentially vanishes at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV.
- With $p_T^\gamma \sim 2$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV, $x_T^\gamma \approx 0.2$, where prompt photon production decreases more rapidly with $x_T^\gamma$.
- The multiplicity of higher $p_T^\gamma$ thermal photons ($p_T^\gamma > 2.5$ GeV) increases rapidly with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, while the multiplicity of lower $p_T^\gamma$ thermal photons ($p_T^\gamma > 0.6$ GeV) increases much more slowly.

## Context

This work extends calculations of photon production in heavy-ion collisions to the lower collision energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (Phase 1), where baryon chemical potential becomes important and dynamical initialization is required for hydrodynamics. The results build on prior work by Shen (2017) on dynamical initialization, Paquet (2015) on photon emission rates, and Aurenche (2006) on prompt photon calculations. The findings support investing more resources into theoretical and experimental investigations of photon production at low collision energies, particularly to exploit the suppression of prompt photons as a window for thermal photon measurements.
