# OZI violation in low energy omega and phi production in the pp system in a quark-gluon model

> We investigate OZI violation in near-threshold omega and phi production in the pp system. Assuming ideal omega/phi mixing (corrections are estimated), the energy dependence of the ratio R(omega/phi) is analyzed in a perturbative quark-gluon exchange model up to the third other in the strong coupling constant alpha(s) with the proton represented as a quark - scalar diquark system. We give a very natural explanation of the violation of the OZI rule in omega/phi production and its energy dependence near the production thresholds.

# OZI violation in low energy omega and phi production in the pp system in a quark-gluon model

## Key claim

The OZI rule violation in near-threshold omega and phi production in pp collisions can be explained naturally by a perturbative quark-gluon exchange model up to third order in the strong coupling constant, assuming ideal omega/phi mixing (with corrections estimated to be less than 10%). The model reproduces the experimentally observed decrease of the phi/omega ratio with decreasing excess energy Q, though significant parameter sensitivity remains, particularly to final-state pp interactions and the quark-diquark structure of the proton.

## Method

The transition amplitude is constructed from effective quark and gluon degrees of freedom using a relativistic one-gluon exchange operator, with the proton represented as a quark-scalar diquark system to simplify the six-quark many-body problem. The total omega and phi cross sections and their ratio are calculated as a function of excess energy Q by integrating the squared transition amplitude over three-body phase space, with the final-state pp interaction treated via a scattering length-effective range parametrization and the initial-state pp interaction approximated from elastic-to-total cross section ratios.

## Key numbers

- The OZI prediction for the phi/omega ratio from ideal mixing deviations (mixing angle deviation of 3.7 degrees) yields R_OZI = 4.2 x 10^{-3}
- A qualitative color matrix element estimate gives R^1_colour = (7/(7+8+32))^2 = 2.2 x 10^{-2} for the suppression of phi relative to omega production
- The calculation uses a constituent quark mass m_q = 330 MeV, strong coupling constant alpha_s = 1.7, scalar diquark mass m_s = 600 MeV, and constituent gluon mass m_g = 800 MeV
- The phi cross section is normalized at Q_0 = 83 MeV for comparison of the Q-dependence
- Deviations from ideal omega/phi mixing enhance R_phi/omega by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends meson-exchange model studies of near-threshold omega and phi production (Nakayama (1998), Sibirtsev (1996), Tsushima (2000), Fuchs (2000)) by providing a quark-gluon based alternative that more explicitly follows OZI arguments. The approach contrasts with meson-exchange models that rely on the poorly known g_phi pp coupling (which has uncertainties up to one order of magnitude in the literature, as noted by Tsushima (2000)). The results enable future tests against existing and upcoming data from ANKE (Hartmann) and DISTO (Disto), and motivate refined modelling of the quark-diquark structure of the proton, incorporation of baryon resonances, and exploration of the s anti-s content of the proton and its influence on spin structure (Oh (2000), Wiedner (2000)).
