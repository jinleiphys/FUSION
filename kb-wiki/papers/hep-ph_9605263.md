# Parton Distributions in Nuclei: Overview and Prospect

> We give a brief overview of nuclear parton distributions. First, the EMC effect is discussed together with possible interpretations such as nuclear binding and $Q^2$ rescaling. Next, we explain shadowing descriptions: vector-meson-dominance-type and parton-recombination models. Nuclear dependence of $Q^2$ evolution should be interesting in testing whether or not DGLAP equations could be applied to nuclear structure functions. Status of nuclear sea-quark and gluon distributions is discussed. The structure function $b_1$, which will be measured at HERMES and possibly at ELFE, could shed light on a new aspect of high-energy spin physics.

## Key claim

Nuclear parton distributions are modified relative to free-nucleon distributions across all $x$, but the interpretation of the EMC effect at medium $x$ is ambiguous: the binding model (which keeps the bound-nucleon $F_2$ unchanged) and the $Q^2$ rescaling model (which modifies the nucleon itself) can be related via factorization-scale independence, so neither is uniquely correct.

## Method

The paper reviews the convolution formalism for nuclear structure functions, where a nuclear parton distribution is a convolution of a hadron momentum distribution with a parton distribution in that hadron. It compares the nuclear binding model, which uses spectral functions from shell-model wave functions and binding energies, with the $Q^2$ rescaling model, which scales the momentum-transfer variable by a nuclear-dependent factor $\xi_A$. For shadowing at small $x$, it contrasts vector-meson-dominance (VMD) and Pomeron-exchange descriptions in the target rest frame with parton-recombination models in the infinite-momentum frame, and it examines the nuclear dependence of DGLAP evolution by including higher-twist recombination terms.

## Key numbers

* The calcium-deuteron $F_2$ ratio at medium $x$ ($x\sim 0.5$) is approximately 10% smaller than unity.
* In the binding model, the light-cone momentum distribution $f(z)$ is peaked at $z \approx 0.98$ (typical binding energy $|\epsilon_i|/m_N \approx 0.02$), giving $F_2^A(x) \approx F_2^N(x/0.98)$; at $x=0.60$, $F_2^N(0.61)/F_2^N(0.60)=0.88$, explaining the 10% EMC effect.
* The parton-recombination shadowing has a $1/Q^2$ dependence and an $A^{1/3}$ dependence; the initial scale $Q_0^2$ is fixed at 0.8 GeV$^2$ by fitting NMC calcium shadowing data.
* The NMC data for $\partial(F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C)/\partial(\ln Q^2)$ are compared with LO DGLAP, NLO DGLAP, and parton-recombination (PR) evolution at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$.
* The propagation length of vector mesons is $\lambda \approx 0.2/x$ fm, exceeding 2 fm at $x<0.1$.
* The average nucleon separation in a nucleus is about 2 fm, comparable to the nucleon diameter.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on nuclear parton distributions that began with the EMC discovery (1983) and the subsequent binding model of Akulinichev, Kulagin, and Vagradov (1985) and the $Q^2$ rescaling model. It contrasts these with VMD and parton-recombination models for shadowing, and it discusses the NMC measurement of nuclear-dependent $Q^2$ evolution (NMC, 1995) as a test of DGLAP applicability to nuclei. The paper enables future measurements of the spin-one structure function $b_1$ at HERMES and possibly ELFE, which could reveal non-nucleonic components or new tensor structure at the parton level.
