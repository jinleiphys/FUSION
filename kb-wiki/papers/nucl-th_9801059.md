# Chemical potential dependence of pi and rho properties

> Using a confining, Dyson-Schwinger equation model of QCD at finite temperature (T) and chemical potential (mu) we study the behaviour of <q-bar q>, m_pi, f_pi and the masses of the rho- and phi-mesons. For each of these quantities there is a necessary anticorrelation between its response to T and its response to mu. (-<q-bar q>) and f_pi decrease with T and increase with mu; m_pi is almost insensitive to T and mu until very near the border of the confinement domain; and the mass of the longitudinal component of the vector mesons increases with T and decreases with mu. At T=0, the rho-meson mass is reduced by approximately 15% at nuclear matter density. These results are a consequence of the necessary momentum-dependence of the dressed-quark self-energy.

## Key claim

Using a simple, confining Dyson-Schwinger equation model of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, the authors demonstrate a necessary anticorrelation between the response of several quantities (quark condensate, pion decay constant, vector meson masses) to increasing temperature versus increasing chemical potential, with the longitudinal component of the rho-meson mass decreasing with chemical potential while the transverse component remains insensitive.

## Method

The authors employed a Dyson-Schwinger equation model with a simplified dressed-gluon propagator (a delta-function in momentum space) and the rainbow truncation for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex, which reduces the integral equations to algebraic equations. They solved the quark gap equation at finite temperature and chemical potential, then used the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder truncation to compute meson masses, with the pion properties constrained by the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- At T=0 and nuclear matter density (mu_0 = 260 MeV), the longitudinal rho-meson mass is reduced to approximately 0.75 of its vacuum value; a more realistic estimate accounting for ultraviolet corrections gives 0.85 of the vacuum value.
- At T=0 and nuclear matter density, the longitudinal phi-meson mass is reduced to approximately 0.85 of its vacuum value; a more realistic estimate gives 0.90 of the vacuum value.
- At T=0 and the critical chemical potential, the longitudinal rho-meson mass drops to approximately 0.65 of its vacuum value, and the phi-meson mass to 0.80 of its vacuum value.
- With model parameters eta = 1.37 GeV and current-quark mass m = 30 MeV, the model yields f_pi = 92 MeV and m_pi = 140 MeV at T=0, mu=0.
- With eta = 1.06 GeV and strange quark mass m_s = 180 MeV, the model yields M_phi = 1.02 GeV at T=0, mu=0.
- The chiral-limit transverse rho-meson mass is M_rho-^2 = (1/2) eta^2, independent of temperature and chemical potential.
- The chiral-limit longitudinal rho-meson mass follows M_rho+^2 = (1/2) eta^2 - 4(mu^2 - pi^2 T^2).

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of in-medium hadron properties, contrasting with effective Lagrangian approaches such as Rapp, Chanfray and Wambach (1997) and Klingl, Kaiser and Weise (1997) that find no mass shift but increased width, and agreeing qualitatively with the QCD sum rules analysis of Jin and Leinweber (1995) that finds a decreasing rho-meson mass. The results contradict the scaling law conjectured by Brown and Rho (1991). The study enables future calculations of meson-photon observables at finite temperature and chemical potential once the dressed-quark-photon vertex is generalized to nonzero T and mu.
