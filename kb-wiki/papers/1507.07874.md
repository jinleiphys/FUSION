# Degenerate limit thermodynamics beyond leading order for models of dense matter

> Analytical formulas for next-to-leading order temperature corrections to the thermal state variables of interacting nucleons in bulk matter are derived in the degenerate limit. The formalism developed is applicable to a wide class of non-relativistic and relativistic models of hot and dense matter currently used in nuclear physics and astrophysics (supernovae, proto-neutron stars and neutron star mergers) as well as in condensed matter physics. We consider the general case of arbitrary dimensionality of momentum space and an arbitrary degree of relativity (for relativistic mean-field theoretical models). For non-relativistic zero-range interactions, knowledge of the Landau effective mass suffices to compute next-to-leading order effects, but in the case of finite-range interactions, momentum derivatives of the Landau effective mass function up to second order are required. Numerical computations are performed to compare results from our analytical formulas with the exact results for zero- and finite-range potential and relativistic mean-field theoretical models. In all cases, inclusion of next-to-leading order temperature effects substantially extends the ranges of partial degeneracy for which the analytical treatment remains valid.

## Key claim

The paper derives next-to-leading order (NLO) temperature corrections to the thermal state variables of interacting nucleons in the degenerate limit, showing that these corrections substantially extend the ranges of partial degeneracy for which the analytical treatment remains valid compared to standard Landau Fermi-Liquid Theory (FLT), but the formalism is limited to cases where the degeneracy parameter η = (μ - ε(p=0))/T is large and does not include effects from single particle-hole excitations, collective modes, or pairing correlations near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors perform a Sommerfeld expansion of Fermi integrals to order T⁴, deriving analytical expressions for the entropy density, thermal energy, thermal pressure, thermal chemical potential, and specific heats in D-dimensional momentum space. For non-relativistic models with zero-range interactions, only the Landau effective mass is needed, while finite-range interactions require momentum derivatives of the Landau effective mass function up to second order. The analytical results are compared to exact numerical calculations for three models: a finite-range Yukawa-type model (MDI(A)), a zero-range Skyrme model (SkO′), and a relativistic mean-field theoretical model (MFT), for both pure neutron matter (PNM) and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) at T=20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The leading order FLT entropy density is s = (p_F m* T)/(3 ħ³) for D=3, with the NLO correction term - (2π²/15 ħ³)(m*³ T³/p_F³)(1 - L_F) where L_F involves derivatives of the effective mass function
- The NLO corrections extend agreement with exact results down to sub-nuclear densities of 0.5 to 1 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) compared to 2-3 n₀ for FLT alone
- For the MFT model, the Landau effective mass m* exhibits a minimum at n_min = 0.57 fm⁻³ for SNM and 0.52 fm⁻³ for PNM
- The density at which p_F = M* (onset of relativistic effects) occurs at n_R = 0.643 γ (M*/m)³ fm⁻³, about (2/3) n_min = 0.38 fm⁻³ for SNM and 0.34 fm⁻³ for PNM
- For the MDI(A) model, the Yukawa range parameter Λ = 420.9 MeV and coefficients C₂ = -23.06 MeV, C₄ = -128.9 MeV are used

## Context

This work extends Landau Fermi-Liquid Theory (FLT) as developed by Landau (1957) and Baym and Chin (1976) by adding next-to-leading order temperature corrections to the standard quadratic-in-temperature results. The formalism is applicable to models used in supernova, proto-neutron star, and neutron star merger simulations, as well as condensed matter physics. The results enable more accurate modeling of thermal effects in hyper-massive neutron stars formed in mergers, where temperatures may exceed the Fermi temperature, and improve upon the leading-order FLT results that were previously the standard analytical treatment for degenerate matter.
