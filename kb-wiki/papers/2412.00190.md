# Complete dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to muon $g-2$

> Hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering defines one of the critical contributions in the Standard-Model prediction of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. In this Letter, we present a complete evaluation using a dispersive formalism, in which the HLbL tensor is reconstructed from its discontinuities, expressed in terms of simpler hadronic matrix elements that can be extracted from experiment. Profiting from recent developments in the determination of axial-vector transition form factors, short-distance constraints for the HLbL tensor, and the vector-vector-axial-vector correlator, we obtain $a_μ^\text{HLbL}=101.9(7.9)\times 10^{-11}$, which meets the precision requirements set by the final result of the Fermilab experiment.

# Complete dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution to muon $g-2$

## Key claim

The authors present a complete data-driven dispersive evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, obtaining $a_\mu^\text{HLbL}=101.9(7.9)\times 10^{-11}$, which meets the precision requirements set by the final Fermilab experiment. The result is conditional on the matching between exclusive hadronic states and short-distance constraints (SDCs) at a scale $Q_0$ varied between 1.2 and 2.0 GeV, with central values quoted for $Q_0=1.5$ GeV, and relies on $U(3)$ symmetry assumptions for axial-vector transition form factors (TFFs) with a global 30% uncertainty assigned.

## Method

The HLbL tensor is reconstructed from its discontinuities via dispersion relations in four-point kinematics, expressing the discontinuities in terms of simpler hadronic matrix elements extracted from experiment. Exclusive hadronic intermediate states (pseudoscalar poles, two-meson boxes, S-wave rescattering, axial-vector and tensor resonances) are summed at low energies, while at high energies the representation is matched to perturbative QCD (quark loop with $\alpha_s$ corrections) and to an operator product expansion (OPE) relating the HLbL tensor to the vector-vector-axial-vector ($VVA$) correlator. The matching is performed by introducing effective poles to account for missing higher intermediate states and by scanning over the matching scale $Q_0$ and the OPE parameter $r$.

## Key numbers

- $a_\mu^\text{HLbL} = 101.9(7.9) \times 10^{-11}$: final result for the full HLbL contribution.
- $a_\mu^\text{exp} = 116\,592\,059(22) \times 10^{-11}$: experimental world average for the muon anomalous magnetic moment, dominated by the Fermilab experiment.
- Pseudoscalar poles ($\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$): $91.2^{+2.9}_{-2.4} \times 10^{-11}$.
- $\pi^\pm$ box: $-15.9(2) \times 10^{-11}$.
- $K^\pm$ box: $-0.5(0) \times 10^{-11}$.
- $S$-wave rescattering: $-9.1(1.0) \times 10^{-11}$.
- Sum of exclusive hadronic states (pseudoscalar poles, boxes, S-wave rescattering): $65.7^{+3.1}_{-2.6} \times 10^{-11}$.
- Subleading contributions total: $33.2(7.2) \times 10^{-11}$, split into $a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{1,2}] = 26.9(5.4) \times 10^{-11}$ and $a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{3\text{--}12}] = 6.3(3.0) \times 10^{-11}$.
- Charm loop: $3(1) \times 10^{-11}$.
- Matching scale $Q_0$ varied in $[1.2, 2.0]$ GeV; OPE parameter $r$ varied in $[1/8, 1/2]$.
- Effective pole masses: $M_P^\text{eff} = 2.2$ GeV (pseudoscalar), $M_A^\text{eff} = 1.7$ GeV (axial-vector).

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to HLbL scattering developed by Colangelo, Hoferichter, Procura, Stoffer, and others (Colangelo 2015, 2017), providing the first complete evaluation that includes axial-vector contributions via global fits to $f_1$ TFF data (Zanke 2021, Hoferichter 2023), subleading SDC corrections (Bijnens 2020-2024), and a dispersive reconstruction of the $VVA$ correlator (Ludtke 2024). The result reduces the uncertainty of the previous phenomenological evaluation (Aoyama 2020, White Paper 2020) by more than a factor of two, and is consistent with lattice QCD results from RBC/UKQCD (Blum 2019, QED$_L$) and Mainz (Chao 2021, 2022, QED$_\infty$), while showing small tension with RBC/UKQCD (Blum 2023, QED$_\infty$) and BMWc (Fodor 2024). Future improvements could come from new data on axial-vector TFFs at BESIII and Belle II, and from a dispersive treatment of tensor contributions in triangle kinematics.
