# Anomalous diffusion in non-equilibrium relativistic heavy-ion rapidity spectra

> There is experimental and theoretical evidence that the broad rapidity distribution of net proton yield in central heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies could be a signal of non-equilibrium properties of the system. We show that the broad rapidity shape can be well reproduced in the framework of a non-linear relativistic Fokker-Planck dynamics which incorporates non-extensive statistics and anomalous diffusion.

## Key claim

The broad rapidity distribution of net protons in central Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies can be reproduced by a non-linear relativistic Fokker-Planck equation with non-extensive statistics, but only if the non-extensive parameter \(q\) deviates from 1 (specifically \(q=1.25\)), implying anomalous superdiffusion and a finite interaction time \(\tau=0.82\) rather than equilibrium.

## Method

The author models the time evolution of the rapidity distribution using a non-linear relativistic Fokker-Planck equation (NLFPE) with diffusion and drift coefficients chosen to satisfy the Einstein relation in the relativistic case. The stationary solution of the NLFPE is a Tsallis-like distribution, and the time-dependent solution is obtained by numerical integration over transverse mass, with the rapidity distribution depending on two parameters: the interaction time \(\tau = \gamma t\) and the non-extensive parameter \(q\). The calculated spectra are compared to net proton data from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c from the NA49 experiment.

## Key numbers

*   The beam rapidity in the center-of-mass frame is fixed to \(y_{\rm cm}=2.9\) for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c.
*   The best fit to the NA49 net proton data uses a non-extensive parameter \(q=1.25\) and an interaction time \(\tau=0.82\).
*   The linear case (\(q=1\)) requires a different interaction time \(\tau=1.2\) but does not reproduce the broad experimental shape.
*   The calculated spectra are normalized to 164 protons.

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to describe baryon stopping in heavy-ion collisions, which used isotropic thermal models or linear Fokker-Planck equations with a linear drift coefficient (Wolschin, 1999) that violated the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. The author shows that a non-linear Fokker-Planck equation satisfying the Einstein relation naturally yields Tsallis non-extensive statistics, reproducing the broad rapidity shape without introducing more free parameters than previous studies (NA44, Braun, Capella). The result suggests a connection between non-equilibrium properties and anomalous superdiffusion in the rapidity space, and the author notes that a microscopic justification for these non-extensive effects is still lacking, though a possible link to diquark breaking in the dual parton model (Capella, 1999) is under investigation.
