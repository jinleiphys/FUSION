# Gaussian fluctuating Generally covariant diffusion

> We extend the previously developed [1] generally covariant formalism to include diffusion of conserved charges, and comment on the seming difference between the chemica potential term and the diffusion term

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously developed generally covariant formalism (Montenegro & Torrieri, 2025) to include diffusion of conserved charges, showing that the dynamics of the diffusion matrix can be determined from the Ward identity enforcing charge conservation and linear response, but note that this is an "academic exercise" because no known physical system (such as those in heavy ion collisions or neutron stars) has conserved charge fluctuation and dissipation scales that are much longer than energy-momentum fluctuation scales.

## Method

The authors construct a Gaussian partition function for the conserved current operator \(J_\mu\) and derive equations of motion for its average \(\langle J_\mu(x,t)\rangle\) and its two-point correlation function \(D_{\mu\nu}(x,t)\) by imposing three requirements: general covariance under space-time refoliations, a fluctuation-dissipation relation representing thermal equilibrium, and exact charge conservation enforced by a Ward identity for every element of the ensemble. The linear response equation is written in covariant form and combined with the charge conservation constraint to close the system without needing volume-preserving diffeomorphisms, unlike the full hydrodynamic case in Montenegro & Torrieri (2025).

## Key numbers

* The diffusion constant \(D\) is given by the Kubo formula \(D = \lim_{k \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{k} \mathrm{Im} \int e^{ikx} \langle [J_x(x,t),J_x(0,0)]\rangle d^4 x\).
* The relaxation time \(\tau_Q\) appears in the Maxwell-Cattaneo-type equation \(\tau_Q \frac{d \vec{J}}{dt}+\vec{J} = -D\vec{\nabla}\mu\), which restores causality for \(\tau_Q\) long enough relative to \(\sqrt{D}\).
* In the full local equilibration limit, \(D_{\mu \nu} \rightarrow 0\) and \(\langle J_\mu \rangle\) is conserved exactly.

## Context

This work extends the generally covariant Gaussian fluctuation formalism developed by Montenegro & Torrieri (2025) for hydrodynamics to the simpler case of diffusion of conserved charges. It contrasts with existing approaches such as the gradient expansion and Schwinger-Keldysh effective theories (Kovtun, 2012; Delacretaz, 2020; Xin, 2020) by treating fluctuations non-perturbatively rather than as perturbations around a thermostatic background. The formalism enables future study of systems with phase transitions and critical points, though going beyond the Gaussian approximation would require higher-order Ward identities and is left for future work.
