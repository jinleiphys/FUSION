# Relativistic three-nucleon calculations within the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The relativistic properties of the three-nucleon system are investigated using the Faddeev equations within the Bethe-Salpeter approach. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is chosen in a separable form. The Gauss quadrature method is used to calculate the integrals. The system of the integral equations are solving by iterations method. The binding energy and the partial-wave amplitudes (1S 0 and 3S 1) of the triton are found.

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations with a rank-one separable Yamaguchi-type nucleon-nucleon interaction yield a triton binding energy of 10.99 MeV, which overestimates the experimental value of 8.48 MeV; the authors attribute this discrepancy to the simplicity of the rank-one separable kernel and note that including higher partial waves and spinor propagators would be needed for improvement.

## Method
The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev integral equations for three equal-mass nucleons using a rank-one separable potential with relativistic Yamaguchi-type form factors. The system was reduced to a set of coupled integral equations for the partial-wave amplitudes in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels, with the total orbital angular momentum \(L=0\) and internal angular momenta \(l=\lambda=0\). The equations were solved by iteration after applying a Wick rotation to Euclidean variables and mapping the integration intervals to \([-1,1]\).

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy: \(E_B = 10.99\) MeV (calculated), compared to the experimental value 8.48 MeV.
- Potential parameters for the \(^1S_0\) state: \(\lambda = -1.12087\) GeV\(^4\), \(\beta = 0.287614\) GeV.
- Potential parameters for the \(^3S_1\) state: \(\lambda = -3.15480\) GeV\(^4\), \(\beta = 0.279731\) GeV.
- The nucleon-nucleon potential parameters reproduce phase shifts up to laboratory energy 100–120 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach to three-nucleon systems, following the formalism of Bondarenko (2002) for the two-body separable kernel and the recoupling-coefficient method of Ref1. The calculated binding energy (10.99 MeV) is significantly larger than the experimental triton value (8.48 MeV), indicating that the rank-one separable potential is insufficient. The authors suggest that increasing the rank of the separable kernel and including \(P\)- and \(D\)-wave states, as well as spinor propagators, would improve agreement with experiment.
