# Theory and Phenomenology of Vector Mesons in Medium

> Electromagnetic probes promise to be direct messengers of (spectral properties of) hot and dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, even at soft momentum transfers essential for characterizing possible phase transitions. We examine how far we have progressed toward this goal by highlighting recent developments, and trying to establish connections between lattice QCD, effective hadronic models and phenomenology of dilepton production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that electromagnetic probes (dileptons) can directly reveal the spectral properties of hot and dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, but the actual progress toward this goal is conditional on establishing connections between lattice QCD, effective hadronic models, and phenomenology, with the caveat that the interpretation of data depends on the specific theoretical framework used (e.g., hadronic many-body theory vs. chiral reduction formalism).

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for vector meson modifications in medium by combining three elements: (1) lattice QCD computations of susceptibilities and sum rules relating chiral order parameters to spectral functions; (2) hadronic many-body theory calculations of vector-meson selfenergies from effective interactions integrated over thermal meson and baryon momentum distributions; (3) phenomenological application to dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions via convolution over space-time fireball or hydrodynamic models, compared to experimental data from NA60 and CERES/NA45 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \( f_\pi = 92 \) MeV (chiral scale reference)
- Baryon density conditions for \(\rho\)-meson spectral function calculations: \((\varrho_B/\varrho_0, T/\text{MeV}) \simeq (0.1, 120), (0.7, 150), (2.6, 180)\) at fixed \(\mu_B = 330\) MeV (reflecting CERN-SPS conditions)
- Fireball lifetime for NA60 central In-In collisions: about 7 fm/c, with initial/final temperatures \(T_{\text{i,fo}} \simeq 195, 120\) MeV
- Dimuon mass bins for \(q_T\) spectra: \(0.6\) GeV \(< M < 0.9\) GeV and \(1.0\) GeV \(< M < 1.4\) GeV in semicentral 158 AGeV In-In collisions

## Context

This review extends earlier work on electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions (Rapp 1999, Alam 1999, Cassing 1999, Gale 2005) by synthesizing recent developments in lattice QCD, effective hadronic models, and dilepton phenomenology. It connects to specific prior approaches: hadronic many-body theory calculations (Rapp 1999), empirical scattering amplitude methods (Eletsky 2001), Hidden Local Symmetry approach (Harada 2003), and chiral reduction formalism (Steele 1997). The paper enables future work on determining the in-medium axialvector spectral function and establishing direct connections between chiral order parameters from lattice QCD and experimental data via chiral and QCD sum rules.
