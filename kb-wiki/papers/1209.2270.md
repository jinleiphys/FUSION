# Correlations in Nuclear Matter

> We analyze the nuclear matter correlation properties in terms of the pair correlation function. To this aim we systematically compare the results for the variational method in the Lowest Order Constrained Variational (LOCV) approximation and for the Bruekner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) scheme. A formal link between the Jastrow correlation factor of LOCV and the Defect Function (DF) of BHF is established and it is shown under which conditions and approximations the two approaches are equivalent. From the numerical comparison it turns out that the two correlation functions are quite close, which indicates in particular that the DF is approximately local and momentum independent. The Equations of State (EOS) of Nuclear Matter in the two approaches are also compared. It is found that once the three-body forces (TBF) are introduced the two EOS are fairly close, while the agreement between the correlation functions holds with or without TBF.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a formal link between the Jastrow correlation factor used in the Lowest Order Constrained Variational (LOCV) method and the Defect Function (DF) of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) scheme, showing that the two correlation functions are quantitatively quite close (mean absolute deviation below 2% for r > 0.25 fm) and that the Defect Function is approximately local and momentum independent, but only under specific conditions and approximations that the abstract does not fully detail.

## Method

The authors compared the pair correlation functions from the LOCV variational method and the BHF (Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone) expansion for symmetric nuclear matter, using the Argonne v18 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction. They introduced a mixed representation of the correlation functions, expanding in partial waves for each two-body channel (defined by quantum numbers lSJT), and performed numerical calculations at and around saturation density, both with and without the inclusion of three-body forces (TBF) using the Urbana IX model. The correlation functions were computed for different relative initial momenta, total momenta, and partial wave channels, and the equations of state (EOS) were also compared.

## Key numbers

- At Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1} (density 0.17 fm^{-3}), the mean absolute deviation between LOCV and BHF correlation functions for r > 0.25 fm is below 2% for all channels considered.
- The correlation energy per particle at saturation density (0.17 fm^{-3}) with three-body forces included is -39.32 MeV for LOCV (kinetic part 16.77 MeV, potential part -56.09 MeV) and -37.97 MeV for BHF.
- At density 0.10 fm^{-3} with TBF, LOCV total correlation energy is -29.51 MeV (kinetic 11.24 MeV, potential -40.75 MeV) and BHF is -29.24 MeV.
- At density 0.40 fm^{-3} with TBF, LOCV total correlation energy is -39.71 MeV (kinetic 32.12 MeV, potential -71.83 MeV) and BHF is -40.46 MeV.
- The contribution of three-body forces around saturation is about 1-3 MeV in absolute value, compared to total correlation energy of about -40 MeV at that density.
- A small hard core radius R_c = 0.1 fm was introduced in the variational method for numerical reasons.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing comparison between variational methods (LOCV) and diagrammatic expansion methods (BBG/BHF) for nuclear matter, which have been applied systematically with different two-body interactions by authors such as Modarres (1979) and Bordbar (various). The paper builds on prior studies of the connection between BBG and the coupled cluster (e^S) method by Kummel (1978) and Day (1981), and on the analysis of spectral functions in BHF by Benhar (various) and in LOCV by Modarres (2012). The findings enable future work on transport coefficients, neutrino and electron scattering cross sections, and other quantities more directly related to correlation and spectral functions, as noted by Lombardo (various).
