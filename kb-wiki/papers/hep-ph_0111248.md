# Hadron-Hadron Scattering in the Nonrelativistic Quark Model

> In this HADRON2001 contribution we summarize the status of our quark-model calculations of hadron-hadron scattering amplitudes in annihilation-free channels. The predictions are in reasonably good agreement with experimentally known S-wave meson-meson and meson-baryon phase shifts, and there are very recent indications that S-wave pi-omega scattering (extracted from FSIs in b_1 decay) may also be similar to our predictions. Finally, novel applications of this formalism to the dissociation cross sections of charmonia on light hadrons (relevant for QGP studies at RHIC) are discussed.

## Key claim

Quark-model Born-order calculations of hadron-hadron scattering amplitudes, using a standard Coulomb + linear + smeared hyperfine Hamiltonian, produce predictions that are in reasonably good agreement with experimentally known S-wave meson-meson and meson-baryon phase shifts, though the approach is limited to annihilation-free channels and the agreement is qualified as "reasonably good" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors evaluate scattering amplitudes using the resonating-group method at Born order, employing quark Born diagrams where a single gluon-exchange interaction between quarks in different hadrons is combined with quark interchange to produce color-singlet final states. For each hadron-hadron channel, the complete set of capture and transfer diagrams is enumerated, and the spatial overlap integrals are weighted by color, spin, and flavor matrix elements. With Gaussian wavefunctions and standard quark model parameters (alpha_s = 0.6, m_q = 0.33 GeV, b = 0.18 GeV^2, beta = 0.4 GeV), the T-matrices and phase shifts are evaluated in closed form for meson-meson scattering, while numerical techniques are used for charmonium dissociation calculations.

## Key numbers

- I=2 pi-pi S-wave phase shifts are given by a sum of three contributions (OGE spin-spin, color Coulomb, and linear confinement) expressed in terms of the pion momentum |A|, with beta = 0.4 GeV as the standard quark model q-qbar wavefunction width parameter
- For P-wave I=2 rho-pi elastic scattering, the quark-model spin-orbit splitting delta(3P2) - delta(3P0) peaks at about 40 degrees
- The predicted S-wave minus D-wave phase shift difference for omega-pi scattering at the b1 mass is delta_S(omega-pi) - delta_D(omega-pi) = -14 degrees
- The E852 Collaboration extracted a consistent result of delta_S(omega-pi) - delta_D(omega-pi) approximately -19 degrees with statistical error 4 degrees and systematic error 8 degrees (delta_D - delta_S = 0.327 +/- 0.061 +/- 0.143 radians, tentative unpublished result)
- Charmonium + light meson dissociation cross sections at low energy are typically about 1 mb in scale
- The rho + J/psi dissociation cross section is much larger than pi + J/psi, in part because rho + J/psi to D Dbar is exothermic and diverges as threshold is approached

## Context

This work extends the quark-model scattering approach previously applied to NN repulsive cores, I=3/2 K-pi, I=0,1 KN, and I=0,1 BB scattering (compared to lattice gauge theory data), and is similar to the variational results of Weinstein and Isgur (1990) who used essentially the same interactions but included contributions beyond Born order. The formalism enables novel applications to charmonium dissociation on light hadrons relevant for quark-gluon plasma studies at RHIC, where earlier work by Martins, Blaschke and Quack (1995) using the same scattering formalism found somewhat larger cross sections due to their assumption of a color-independent confining interaction.
