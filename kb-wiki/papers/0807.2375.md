# alpha particle momentum distributions from 12C decaying resonances

> The computed $α$ particle momentum distributions from the decay of low-lying $^{12}$C resonances are shown. The wave function of the decaying fragments is computed by means of the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The large-distance part of the wave functions is crucial and has to be accurately calculated. We discuss energy distributions, angular distributions and Dalitz plots for the $4^+$, $1^+$ and $4^-$ states of $^{12}$C.

## Key claim
The computed α-particle momentum distributions from the decay of low-lying 12C resonances are shown, but the results depend on the assumption that the decay mechanism is independent of how the initial state was formed and that the small-distance many-body dynamics is unimportant for the process.

## Method
The authors describe 12C as a pure three-α cluster system and solve Faddeev equations in coordinate space using the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The large-distance part of the wave functions is computed up to hyperradii of about 60–100 fm, with 80 fm chosen as the value where energy distributions are computed. A Monte Carlo integration over phase space is performed to obtain probability distributions, and the momentum distributions are determined by Fourier transform of the coordinate-space wave function.

## Key numbers
- The 4+ state at an excitation energy of 14.1 MeV (6.83 MeV above the 3α threshold at 7.275 MeV) has an estimated 20% decay via the 8Be ground state.
- The 1+ state at an excitation energy of 12.7 MeV (5.42 MeV above the 3α threshold) shows almost perfect agreement with experiment for the single α energy distribution.
- The 4- state is suggested to correspond to the state at 13.35 MeV excitation energy (6.08 MeV above the 3α threshold).
- The asymptotic behavior of the wave functions is reached for hyperradii larger than about 60 fm, with small variation from 70 to 100 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 12C resonances below the proton separation threshold, which have been motivated partly by astrophysical importance and where open questions remain on the 0+ and 2+ resonances (Morinaga, 1956). The authors compare their theoretical predictions with preliminary experimental data from the reaction 11B(3He,dααα) studied at CMAM (Madrid) by M. Alcorta and collaborators (2008), finding very good agreement. The results enable direct comparison with oncoming experimental data and can be used to assign spin and parity of measured states, as suggested by Freer et al. (2007) for the 2- and 4- states.
