# Kelvin-Helmholz instability in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The dynamical development of collective flow is studied in a (3+1)D fluid dynamical model, with globally symmetric, peripheral initial conditions, which take into account the shear flow caused by the forward motion on the projectile side and the backward motion on the target side. While at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76A$\,TeV semi-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions the earlier predicted rotation effect is visible, at more peripheral collisions, with high resolution and low numerical viscosity the initial development of a Kelvin-Helmholtz instability is observed, which alters the flow pattern considerably. This effect provides a precision tool for studying the low viscosity of Quark-gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Kelvin-Helmholtz (KH) instability can develop in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only under specific conditions: sufficiently high impact parameter ($b \geq 0.6 b_{\rm max}$), low viscosity (kinematic viscosity $\nu < 5$ fm $c$), and high numerical resolution that keeps numerical viscosity small enough to avoid suppressing the instability.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)D relativistic fluid dynamical simulations using the Particle-in-Cell (PIC) method on an Eulerian grid with Lagrangian marker particles, for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76A$ TeV. The initial state was based on longitudinally expanding flux tubes (streaks) that produce a shear flow configuration, with projectile-side matter moving forward and target-side matter moving backward. The simulations varied impact parameters ($b = 0.5$ and $0.7 b_{\rm max}$), grid resolutions (cell sizes from 0.585 fm down to 0.35 fm), and marker particle densities to study the onset and growth of KH instability.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76A$ TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Impact parameters studied: $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$ and $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$, where $b_{\rm max} = 14$ fm (for Pb, $R = 7$ fm)
- Transverse size of initial shear flow: $L = 7.0$ fm for $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$, $L = 4.2$ fm for $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$
- Longitudinal length of initial state: $l_z = 10.5$ fm for $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$, $l_z = 13.1$ fm for $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$
- Minimal wave number: $k = 0.598$ fm$^{-1}$ for $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$, $k = 0.479$ fm$^{-1}$ for $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$
- Velocity of top/bottom layer for peripheral collisions: $V = |u| \approx 0.4c$
- Kolmogorov length scale: $\lambda_{\rm Kol} = 2.1$ to $5.4$ fm for $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$, $\lambda_{\rm Kol} = 1.4$ to $3.6$ fm for $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$
- Critical kinematic viscosity: $\nu_c \simeq 5$ fm $c$
- Reynolds number: $Re = 0.3$ to $1$ for $\eta/s = 1$, $Re = 3$ to $10$ for $\eta/s = 0.1$
- At $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$ with highest resolution ($dx = 0.35$ fm), KH instability wave amplitude reached 1.1 fm, which is 6% of final profile height
- Directed flow $v_1$ peak at $y = 0.45$ increased by 13% for $b = 0.5 b_{\rm max}$ and by 21% for $b = 0.7 b_{\rm max}$ when cell size decreased from 0.585 fm to 0.35 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of collective rotation in peripheral heavy ion collisions by Csernai and collaborators (hydro2) and provides a more detailed mechanism for flow pattern modification. The study builds on the initial state model of Magas (M2001-2) using longitudinally expanding flux tubes, and applies Kolmogorov's theory of turbulence to estimate the minimal stable vortex size in QGP. The results suggest that the KH instability can serve as a precision tool for measuring QGP viscosity, complementing other approaches such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio estimates by Son (Son) and Csernai, Kapusta, and McLerran (CKM). The paper enables experimental searches for KH instability signatures in LHC data, particularly through directed flow measurements at forward rapidities.
