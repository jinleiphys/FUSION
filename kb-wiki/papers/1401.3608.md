# Effects of isovector scalar $δ$-meson on hypernuclei

> We analyze the effects of $δ-$ meson on hypernuclei within the frame-work of relativistic mean field theory. The $δ-$ meson is included into the Lagrangian for hypernuclei. The extra nucleon-meson coupling ($g_δ$) affects the every piece of physical observables, like binding energy, radii and single particle energy of hypernuclei. The lambda mean field potential is investigated which is consistent with other predictions. Flipping of single particle energy levels are observed with the strength of $g_δ$ in the considered hypernuclei as well as normal nuclei. The spin-orbit potentials are observed for considered hypernuclei and the effect of $g_δ$ on spin-orbit potentials is also analyzed. The calculated single-$Λ$ binding energies ($B_Λ$) are quite agreeable with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The isovector scalar δ-meson affects binding energies, radii, and single-particle energies of hypernuclei, but the magnitude of these effects is smaller in hypernuclei than in their normal nuclear counterparts; the λ hyperon potential is completely unaffected by the δ-meson coupling due to the hyperon's zero isospin.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian for hypernuclei to include the δ-meson, using the NL3* parameter set. To avoid double counting with the ρ-meson, they adjusted the g_ρ coupling constant for each chosen value of g_δ so that the combined (g_ρ, g_δ) set reproduced the same binding energies as the pure NL3* set for the hypernuclei ⁴⁸_ΛCa, ⁹⁰_ΛZr, and ²⁰⁸_ΛPb and their normal nuclei. The Dirac equations for nucleons and the Λ hyperon were solved self-consistently, and the constant-gap BCS approximation was used for pairing.

## Key numbers

- The calculated single-Λ binding energy B_Λ(s) for ¹⁶_ΛN is 13.80 MeV, compared to the experimental value of (13.76 ± 0.16) MeV.
- The calculated B_Λ(s) for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb is 26.58 MeV, compared to the experimental value of (26.3 ± 0.8) MeV.
- The lambda central potential depth is 32.87 MeV for ⁴⁸_ΛCa, 30.41 MeV for ⁹⁰_ΛZr, and 31.95 MeV for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb.
- No convergent solutions exist for g_δ values beyond approximately 8.0.
- The lambda potential depth is 38–40% of the nucleon potential depth.

## Context

This work extends RMF studies of hypernuclei (e.g., Mares (1993), Vretenar (1998)) by adding the δ-meson, which is known to be important for asymmetric normal nuclei (Kubis (1997), Singh (2013)). The results show that the δ-meson has a smaller effect on hypernuclei than on normal nuclei, and the authors suggest this degree of freedom may help resolve the charge radius anomaly between ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca, and potentially in hyper-calcium isotopes. The work enables future studies of δ-meson effects in heavy and superheavy hypernuclei with large isospin asymmetry.
