# Hydrodynamical analysis of centrality dependence of charged particle's multiplicity in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> In Israel-Stewart's theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, we have analyzed the recent ALICE data for the centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity, centrality dependence of integrated elliptic flow and 0-5% charged particles $p_T$ spectra in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions and determined the initial or the thermalisation time. Analysis indicate that the ALICE data disfavor very early thermalsiation $τ_i$=0.2 fm, or very late thermalsation $τ_i$= 4fm. Data are best explained for thermalisation time $τ_i=0.88^{-0.14}_{+0.68}$ fm.

## Key claim

The ALICE data for charged particle multiplicity, elliptic flow, and $p_T$ spectra in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions are best explained by a thermalisation time of $\tau_i=0.88^{+0.68}_{-0.14}$ fm in Israel-Stewart dissipative hydrodynamics, with very early (0.2 fm) or very late (4 fm) thermalisation disfavored by the combined data sets.

## Method

The authors solved Israel-Stewart's second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations with boost invariance using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code, assuming a baryon-free QGP fluid with viscosity-to-entropy ratio $\eta/s=1/4\pi$ and an equation of state joining Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD results to a hadronic resonance gas at $T_c=174$ MeV. Initial energy density profiles were taken from a Glauber model with hard scattering fraction $f=0.9$, and the central energy density was tuned to reproduce the charged particle multiplicity in 0-5% central collisions for each assumed initial time $\tau_i=0.2, 0.6, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0$ fm. The fluid was frozen out at fixed temperature $T_F=130$ MeV, and $\chi^2/N$ values were computed for the three data sets (centrality-dependent multiplicity, integrated elliptic flow, and 0-5% $p_T$ spectra) individually and combined.

## Key numbers

- Thermalisation time from combined data fit: $\tau_i = 0.88^{+0.68}_{-0.14}$ fm (parabolic fit to $\chi^2/N$ values)
- Central energy density required for $\tau_i=0.2$ fm: $\varepsilon_i = 370 \pm 30$ GeV/fm$^3$ (initial temperature $T_i = 690 \pm 10$ MeV)
- Central energy density required for $\tau_i=1.0$ fm: $\varepsilon_i = 72 \pm 5$ GeV/fm$^3$ (initial temperature $T_i = 464 \pm 7$ MeV)
- Central energy density required for $\tau_i=4.0$ fm: $\varepsilon_i = 12 \pm 1$ GeV/fm$^3$ (initial temperature $T_i = 301 \pm 3$ MeV)
- Combined $\chi^2/N$ minimum: 2.9 for $\tau_i=1.0$ fm; for $\tau_i=0.2$ fm, $\chi^2/N=14.5$; for $\tau_i=4.0$ fm, $\chi^2/N=13.1$
- Experimental charged particle multiplicity in 0-5% collisions: $dN_{ch}/dy = 1601 \pm 60$
- For $\tau_i=0.6$ fm at LHC, $\varepsilon_i \approx 126$ GeV/fm$^3$ compared to $\varepsilon_i \approx 30$ GeV/fm$^3$ at RHIC for similar thermalisation time

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic analyses previously applied to $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC (where near-ideal QGP fluid with $\tau_i\approx 0.6$ fm explained a large variety of data) to the new LHC energy regime using ALICE data from 2010. The analysis determines the thermalisation time at LHC energies for the first time using three observables simultaneously, showing that single-observable fits (e.g., elliptic flow alone favoring $\tau_i=0.2$ fm) can be misleading. The result enables future studies to incorporate additional initial condition uncertainties (e.g., varying $\eta/s$, initial velocity profiles, or including bulk viscosity) and to test whether the extracted thermalisation time remains robust when more complete dynamical freeze-out prescriptions are used.
