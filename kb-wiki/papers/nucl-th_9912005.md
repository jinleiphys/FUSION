# Tensor correlations in nuclei and exlusive electron scattering

> The effect of tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations upon exclusive and semi-exclusive electronuclear reactions is studied. Differential cross sections for the semi-exclusive ^{16}O(e,e'p) and exclusive ^{16}O(e,e'pn) processes are computed by explicitly evaluating the dynamical electromagnetic coupling to a tensor correlated nucleon pair. In both reaction channels the tensor correlations contribute in a very substantial way. Tensor correlations are found to generate more electronuclear strength than central Jastrow correlations do.

# Tensor correlations in nuclei and exclusive electron scattering

## Key claim

Tensor nucleon-nucleon correlations produce substantially larger contributions to both exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e'pn) and semi-exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e'p) cross sections than central Jastrow correlations do, but this enhanced sensitivity is conditional on the reaction being dominated by absorption on proton-neutron pairs in a $^3S_1$ configuration; for transitions where the $^3S_1$ configuration does not contribute (e.g., $0^+$ final states), central and tensor correlations are of roughly equal importance.

## Method

The authors computed differential cross sections for $^{16}$O(e,e'pn) and semi-exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e'p) by evaluating transition matrix elements in the lowest-order approximation (LOA) of a cluster expansion, using correlated ground-state wave functions that include both a central (Jastrow) correlation function $g_c$ and a tensor correlation function $f_{t\tau}$ acting on $S=1$ components. The calculations employed a distorted-wave description of the ejectiles, included one-body currents in the impulse approximation and two-body currents from pion-exchange and intermediate $\Delta_{33}$-resonance terms, and used the Argonne $v_{14}$ NN potential to obtain correlation functions from variational calculations for the $^{16}$O ground state. Kinematics were chosen in a "super-parallel" configuration to suppress rescattering contributions.

## Key numbers

- At $\theta_{pq}=40^\circ$ in semi-exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e'p), where missing momentum ranges over $275 \leq p_m \leq 450$ MeV/c, inclusion of tensor correlations increases the strength by a factor of five relative to central correlations alone.
- For the $^{16}$O(e,e'pn) ground-state transition, neglecting tensor correlations results in a cross section about a factor of two too small.
- The semi-exclusive calculations used electron kinematics of $\epsilon=1.2$ GeV, $\epsilon'=0.9$ GeV, and $\theta_e=16^\circ$, with $\omega=300$ MeV and $|\vec{q}|=416$ MeV/c.
- The exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e'pn) calculations used $\epsilon=855$ MeV, $\epsilon'=640$ MeV, and $\theta_{e'}=18^\circ$.
- The tensor force is operative at intermediate internucleon distances of approximately 1–2.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier inclusive $A(e,e')$ response studies (Orlandini, 1983; Fabrocini, 1997) where tensor correlation signals were difficult to isolate due to competing effects, and provides a framework for interpreting upcoming exclusive experiments at MAMI (scheduled $^{16}$O(e,e'pn) measurements at $Q^2 \approx 0.05$ (GeV/c)$^2$) and TJNAF ($^{12}$C(e,e'pN) at $Q^2 \geq 2$ (GeV/c)$^2$). The results enable separation of tensor from central correlation effects in two-nucleon knockout reactions and suggest that the recoil polarization observable $P_t'$ in $A(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}n)$ measurements can serve as a relatively unambiguous probe of tensor correlations in the nuclear medium.
