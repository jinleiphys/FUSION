# Quark Degrees of Freedom in Finite Nuclei

> Properties of finite nuclei are investigated based on relativistic Hartree equations which have been derived from a relativistic quark model of the structure of bound nucleons. Nucleons are assumed to interact through the (self-consistent) exchange of scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$ and $ρ$) mesons at the quark level. The coupling constants and the mass of the $σ$-meson are determined from the properties of symmetric nuclear matter and the rms charge radius in $^{40}$Ca. Calculated properties of static, closed-shell nuclei, as well as symmetric nuclear matter are compared with experimental data and with the results of Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic quark model (the quark meson coupling model, QMC) can successfully describe the properties of finite nuclei at the typical energy scale of a few tens of MeV, with the key hedge being that the quantitative investigation of whether quark degrees of freedom are necessary for finite nuclei has "just now started."

## Method

The authors solved relativistic Hartree equations derived from the QMC model, where nucleons are described by the MIT bag model and interact through the exchange of scalar (sigma) and vector (omega and rho) mesons at the quark level. The coupling constants and sigma meson mass were determined by fitting the binding energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter (-15.7 MeV) and the rms charge radius of 40Ca (3.48 fm). The resulting equations were then solved for static, closed-shell nuclei and compared with experimental data and results from Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear compressibility K = 280 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 (QMC), compared to K = 565 MeV for QHD and the experimentally required range of 200-300 MeV.
- Effective nucleon mass M_N* = 754 MeV at normal nuclear density (QMC), compared to M_N* = 504 MeV for QHD.
- Sigma meson mass m_sigma = 418 MeV for finite nuclei (QMC), compared to m_sigma = 520 MeV for QHD.
- Coupling constants for finite nuclei in QMC: g_sigma^2/4pi = 3.12, g_omega^2/4pi = 5.31, g_rho^2/4pi = 6.93.
- Bag model parameters used: bag radius R_B = 0.8 fm in free space, bag pressure B = (170 MeV)^4, current quark mass m_q = 5 MeV, z_0 = 3.295.

## Context

This work extends the quark meson coupling model, originally suggested by Guichon (1988) for nuclear matter, to finite nuclei, building on earlier studies of nuclear matter by the same authors. The QMC model successfully reproduces the nuclear compressibility in the experimentally required range (200-300 MeV), whereas QHD (Serot and Walecka, 1986) significantly overestimates it. The approach enables further investigations of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, the nuclear EMC effect, and super-allowed Fermi beta-decay by incorporating explicit quark degrees of freedom.
