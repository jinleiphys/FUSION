# Baryon Properties from Continuum-QCD

> We provide an inkling of recent progress in hadron physics made using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, reviewing: the notion of in-hadron condensates and a putative solution of a gross problem with the cosmological constant; a symmetry-preserving computation that simultaneously correlates the masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states, and contributes to a resolution of the conundrum of the Roper resonance; and a prediction for the Q^2-dependence of u- and d-quark Dirac and Pauli form factors in the proton, which exposes the critical role played by diquark correlations within the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing problem of the cosmological constant can be alleviated by recognizing that quark and gluon condensates are not spacetime-independent vacuum properties but rather are confined within hadrons, which reduces the mismatch between theoretical vacuum energy estimates and the observed cosmological constant by a factor of 10^46.

## Method

The authors review recent progress using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), a continuum framework that enables nonperturbative computation of hadron properties. They employ a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction to compute the dressed-quark-core masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states within a single framework, and use Schwinger functions constrained by meson phenomena and perturbative/nonperturbative QCD to predict nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors and their quark flavor decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The mismatch between theoretical vacuum energy contributions and the observed cosmological constant is reduced by a factor of 10^46 when condensates are understood as confined within hadrons rather than permeating spacetime.
- The DSE-computed Roper resonance mass is 1.82 ± 0.07 GeV, identified as the proton's first radial excitation.
- The root-mean-square-relative-error per degree-of-freedom for the computed light-hadron spectrum compared with relevant quantities is 13%.
- The zero in the d-quark Dirac form factor within the proton occurs at Q^2 = 4.9 M_N^2.
- The ρ-meson electric form factor exhibits a zero at z_Q^ρ ≈ √6 m_ρ.
- DCSB is identified as responsible for 98% of a proton's mass.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework developed by Roberts (1994) and Chang (2010) to unify hadron spectroscopy, form factors, and parton distribution functions. It directly addresses the Roper resonance conundrum that has persisted since its discovery in 1963, demonstrating agreement with the coupled-channels analysis of Suzuki (2009). The paper also builds on the nucleon form factor predictions of Cloet (2008) and the in-hadron condensate concept of Brodsky (2010), enabling future work to chart the long-range quark-quark interaction through comparison of form factor predictions from different interaction kernels.
