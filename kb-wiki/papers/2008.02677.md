# Investigation of Quark Distributions in a Family of Pentaquarks using the Thomas-Fermi Quark Model

> Using the Thomas-Fermi quark model, a collective, spherically symmetric density of states is created to represent a gas of interacting fermions with various degeneracies at zero temperature. Over a family of multi-pentaquarks, color interaction probabilities are obtained after averaging over all the possible configurations. It is found that three different Thomas-Fermi functions are necessary for light, charm, and anti-charm quarks. These are assumed to be linearly related by proportionality constants resulting in consistency conditions. We analyze these conditions and find that they lead to an interesting pattern of spherical quark distributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Thomas-Fermi quark model can describe a family of hidden-charm multi-pentaquarks (systems of 5η particles with flavor content uudc\bar{c}) using three distinct Thomas-Fermi functions for light, charm, and anti-charm quarks, but only under the condition that these functions are linearly related by proportionality constants that yield specific consistency conditions.

## Method

The authors first calculated color interaction probabilities for a system of 5η particles (η pockets, each containing four quarks and one antiquark) by averaging over all 3^η possible color-singlet configurations. They then combined these color probabilities with flavor statistics to construct the total energy expression, converted the discrete particle system into a continuous density of states, and minimized the total energy using Lagrange multipliers to obtain Thomas-Fermi differential equations for the quark densities. The resulting equations were solved under the assumption that the Thomas-Fermi functions for light, charm, and anti-charm quarks are linearly related by proportionality constants, leading to consistency conditions that determine the allowed quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- The system contains 5η particles total, with 4η quarks and η antiquarks per system
- The probability for color-color repulsion (CCR) interactions is P_ii = 2(8η-5)/[45(5η-1)]
- The probability for color-color attraction (CCA) interactions is P_ij = 2(16η-1)/[45(5η-1)]
- The probability for color-anti-color repulsion (CAR) interactions is \overline{P}_{ii} = 4(4η-1)/[45(5η-1)]
- The probability for color-anti-color attraction (CAA) interactions is \overline{P}_{ij} = 4(2η+1)/[45(5η-1)]
- The probability for anti-color-anti-color repulsion (AAR) interactions is \overline{\overline{P}}_{ii} = (η-1)/[45(5η-1)]
- The probability for anti-color-anti-color attraction (AAA) interactions is \overline{\overline{P}}_{ij} = 2(η-1)/[45(5η-1)]
- The combined color coupling strength yields a net attractive interaction of -4g^2/3(5η-1)

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi quark model previously applied to baryons (Liu 2012, Liu 2019) and multi-quark mesons (Baral 2019) to the family of hidden-charm pentaquarks, motivated by the experimental discovery of pentaquark states P_C(4312)^+, P_C(4440)^+, and P_C(4457)^+ by LHCb (Aaij 2019). The approach offers an inexpensive alternative to Lattice QCD for investigating families of bound states rather than individual cases, and the authors suggest it could be key to identifying families of bound states rather than individual cases (Wilcox 2018). The paper establishes the theoretical framework for calculating quark distributions but does not present numerical solutions to the differential equations, leaving the actual radial distributions and binding energies for future work.
