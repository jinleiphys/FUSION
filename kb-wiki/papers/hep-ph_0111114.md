# Direct Photons from Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Direct photons have been proposed as a promising signature for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Recently WA98 presented the first data on direct photons in Pb+Pb-collisions at SPS. At the same time RHIC started with its experimental program. The discovery of the QGP in these experiments relies on a comparison of data with theoretical predictions for QGP signals. In the case of direct photons new results for the production rates of thermal photons from the QGP and a hot hadron gas as well as for prompt photons from initial hard parton scatterings have been proposed recently. Based on these rates a variety of different hydrodynamic models, describing the space-time evolution of the fireball, have been adopted for calculating the direct photon spectra. The results have been compared to the WA98 data and predictions for RHIC and LHC have been made. So far the conclusions of the various models are controversial. The aim of the present review is to provide a comprehensive and up-to-date survey and status report on the experimental and theoretical aspects of direct photons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Direct photons are a promising signature for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the conclusions from various hydrodynamic models comparing theoretical predictions to the first WA98 data remain controversial, and the review aims to provide a comprehensive survey of the experimental and theoretical status.

## Method

The authors review the perturbative calculation of the photon production rate from the QGP using quark annihilation and Compton scattering diagrams, assuming Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions for incoming partons (valid for energetic photons with E >> T). They then describe the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation technique, which cures infrared problems in finite-temperature gauge theories by extracting and resumming 1-loop diagrams with hard loop momenta and soft external momenta, and apply it to calculate the photon production rate to leading logarithm order. The review also covers thermal photon rates from a hot hadron gas, prompt photons from initial hard parton scatterings, and various hydrodynamic models describing the space-time evolution of the fireball.

## Key numbers

- The photon production rate to leading logarithm order from the HTL approach is given by dN/(d^4x d^3p) = (5/(18π^2)) α α_s e^(-E/T) (T^2/E) ln(0.2317 E/(α_s T))
- The effective quark mass in the HTL approximation is m_q^2 = g^2 T^2 / 6
- The Boltzmann approximation for the hard part introduces an error of less than a few percent compared to using exact Fermi and Bose distributions (citing Neubert (1989))
- The high-energy limit of the effective quark mass is 2m_q^2 = g^2 T^2 / 3
- Euler's constant γ = 0.57722 and ζ'(2)/ζ(2) = -0.569996 appear in the analytic expressions for the production rate

## Context

This review extends the work of Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991) and Baier, Nakkagawa, Niégawa, and Redlich (1992) on perturbative photon production rates, and applies the HTL resummation technique developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to improve infrared behavior. The review enables comparison of theoretical predictions with the first direct photon data from WA98 in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, and provides predictions for upcoming RHIC and LHC experiments, while noting that the various hydrodynamic model conclusions remain controversial.
