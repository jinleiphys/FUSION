# Interactions of $B_{c}$ Meson in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We calculate the absorbtion cross-sections of $B_{c}$ mesons by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons including anomalous processes using an effective hadronic Lagrangian. The enhancement of Bc production is expected due to QGP formation in heavy-ion experiments. However it is also expected that the production rate of Bc meson can be affected due to the interaction with comovers. These processes are relevant for experiments at RHIC. Thermal average cross-sections of $B_{c}$ are evaluated with form factor when a cut off parameter in it is 1 and 2 GeV. Using these thermal average cross-sections in the kinetic equation we investigate the time evolution of $B_{c}$ mesons due to dissociation in the hadronic matter formed at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that including anomalous couplings (PVV, PPPV, PVVV) in the effective hadronic Lagrangian significantly affects the dissociation cross-sections of \(B_c\) mesons by \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) mesons, with the resulting thermal average cross-sections and time evolution in hadronic matter at RHIC depending on the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) in the monopole form factor, taken as 1 and 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct an effective hadronic Lagrangian with both normal and anomalous interaction terms to compute the dissociation cross-sections of \(B_c\) mesons by \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) mesons for eight reaction channels (e.g., \(B_c^+ + \pi \rightarrow D + B\), \(B_c^+ + \rho \rightarrow D^* + B^*\)). They evaluate the scattering amplitudes for each process, including Feynman diagrams with t-channel and u-channel exchanges, and apply monopole form factors with cutoff parameters \(\Lambda = 1\) and 2 GeV to account for the finite size of hadrons. Thermal average cross-sections are then computed using a standard formula, and these are used in a kinetic equation within a schematic expanding fireball model to study the time evolution of \(B_c\) meson abundance at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- For the process \(B_c^+ + \pi \rightarrow D^* + B^*\) (including anomalous diagrams), the cross section ranges between 2 to 8 mb away from threshold with form factors included.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \pi \rightarrow D + B\), the cross section decreases as c.m. energy increases and becomes very small beyond 12 GeV with form factors included; threshold energy is 7.15 GeV.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \pi \rightarrow D^* + B\), the cross section ranges between 0.005 to 0.1 mb away from threshold with form factor included; threshold energy is 7.29 GeV.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \pi \rightarrow D + B^*\), the cross section varies between 0.0007 to 0.1 mb; threshold energy is 7.20 GeV.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \rho \rightarrow D^* + B\), the cross section with form factor ranges between 2 to 7 mb; threshold energy is 7.20 GeV.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \rho \rightarrow D + B^*\), the cross section ranges between 0.1 to 0.7 mb; threshold energy is 7.34 GeV.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \rho \rightarrow D + B\), the cross section ranges from 0.0001 to 0.03 mb when form factor is included.
- For the process \(B_c^+ + \rho \rightarrow D^* + B^*\), the cross section with form factor approximately ranges between 0.01 to 0.08 mb for large \(\sqrt{s}\).
- The coupling constant \(g_{\pi B_c D^* B}\) and \(g_{\pi B_c D B^*}\) are both 21.56 GeV\(^{-3}\), derived from SU(5) symmetry.
- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho B_c D^* B^*}\) is 67 GeV\(^{-1}\), derived from SU(5) symmetry.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(B_c\) meson dissociation by \(\pi\) mesons (Lodhi et al., 2011) and by \(\rho\) mesons (Faisal et al., 2011), which used only normal couplings, by including anomalous interaction terms (PVV, PPPV, PVVV) that were found significant for charmonium dissociation by Oh et al. (2001) and for \(B_c\) dissociation by nucleons (Faisal et al., 2012). The calculated thermal average cross-sections and time evolution of \(B_c\) abundance in hadronic matter at RHIC provide input needed to distinguish suppression due to comover absorption from enhancement due to QGP formation, as suggested by Schroeder et al. (2000) and Rafelski et al. (2002).
