# $Z_c(4430)$, $Z_c(4200)$, $Z_1(4050)$, and $Z_2(4250)$ as triangle singularities

> $Z_c(4430)$ discovered in $\bar{B}^0\toψ(2S)K^-π^+$, $Z_c(4200)$ found in $\bar{B}^0\to J/ψK^-π^+$, and $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ observed in $\bar{B}^0\toχ_{c1}K^-π^+$ are candidates of charged charmonium-like states. All surviving theoretical models interpreted these candidates as four-quark states, until we recently identified a compelling alternative. We discuss that kinematical singularities in triangle loop diagrams induce a resonance-like behavior that can consistently explain the properties (such as spin-parity, mass, width, and Argand plot) of $Z_c(4430)$, $Z_c(4200)$, $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ from experiments. In terms of the triangle singularities, we can also naturally understand interesting experimental findings such as the appearance (absence) of $Z_c(4200)$($Z_c(4430)$)-like contribution in $Λ_b^0\to J/ψpπ^-$, and the highly asymmetric shape of the spectrum bump for $Z_1(4050)$; the other theoretical models have not successfully addressed these points. Although Pakhlov et al. proposed another triangle diagram to generate a $Z_c(4430)$-like bump, we argue that this scenario is very unlikely.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the charged charmonium-like state candidates \(Z_c(4430)\), \(Z_c(4200)\), \(Z_1(4050)\), and \(Z_2(4250)\) are not genuine four-quark resonances but rather resonance-like enhancements produced by kinematical triangle singularities (TS) in specific triangle loop diagrams; the abstract's claim of a "compelling alternative" is hedged by the fact that the TS explanation depends on the specific masses and widths of the intermediate particles involved and is not a universal alternative for all exotic candidates.

## Method

The author constructs triangle-loop amplitudes for the decays \(\bar{B}^0 \to \psi_f K^- \pi^+\) (with \(\psi_f = J/\psi, \psi(2S)\)), \(\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi p \pi^-\), and \(\bar{B}^0 \to \chi_{c1} K^- \pi^+\), using an \(s\)-wave rescattering interaction for most diagrams to match the experimentally determined spin-parity \(J^P = 1^+\) of \(Z_c(4430)\) and \(Z_c(4200)\). The amplitude is integrated over the intermediate particle momenta, with unstable particles given complex energies including their widths, and the resulting invariant-mass distributions are fitted with Breit-Wigner forms to extract effective mass and width parameters for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- \(Z_c(4430)\): Breit-Wigner mass \(4463 \pm 13\) MeV and width \(195 \pm 16\) MeV from the triangle diagram of Fig. (a), compared with Belle's \(4485 \pm 22^{+28}_{-11}\) MeV and \(200^{+41}_{-46}{}^{+26}_{-35}\) MeV, and LHCb's \(4475 \pm 7^{+15}_{-25}\) MeV and \(172 \pm 13^{+37}_{-34}\) MeV.
- \(Z_c(4200)\): Breit-Wigner mass \(4233 \pm 48\) MeV and width \(292 \pm 56\) MeV from the triangle diagram of Fig. (b), compared with Belle's \(4196^{+31}_{-29}{}^{+17}_{-13}\) MeV and \(370 \pm 70^{+70}_{-132}\) MeV.
- \(Z_1(4050)\): Breit-Wigner mass \(4041 \pm 1\) MeV and width \(115 \pm 17\) MeV from the triangle diagram of Fig. (d), compared with Belle's \(4051 \pm 14^{+20}_{-41}\) MeV and \(82^{+21}_{-17}{}^{+47}_{-22}\) MeV; the model predicts \(J^P = 1^-\).
- \(Z_2(4250)\): For the \(J^P = 1^+\) case from Fig. (e), Breit-Wigner mass \(4247 \pm 53\) MeV and width \(345 \pm 67\) MeV; for the \(J^P = 1^-\) case, mass \(4309 \pm 116\) MeV and width \(468 \pm 90\) MeV; compared with Belle's \(4248^{+44}_{-29}{}^{+180}_{-35}\) MeV and \(177^{+54}_{-39}{}^{+316}_{-61}\) MeV.
- The triangle diagram for \(Z_1(4050)\) produces a peak at \(m_{\chi_{c1}\pi} \sim 4.025\) GeV with a highly asymmetric shape, where the \(X(3872)\pi^+\) threshold opens at \(\sim 4.01\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the triangle-singularity interpretation of exotic candidates, previously applied by the author to other systems, and directly contradicts the prevailing four-quark (including hadron-molecule) interpretations of \(Z_c(4430)\), \(Z_c(4200)\), \(Z_1(4050)\), and \(Z_2(4250)\) that were considered the only surviving models. The paper also argues against an alternative triangle-diagram scenario proposed by Pakhlov et al. (2011, 2015), which the author shows is kinematically forbidden by the Coleman-Norton theorem and produces a clockwise Argand plot ruled out by LHCb data. The results enable future experimental tests: higher-statistics data for the \(Z_1(4050)\) region could confirm the asymmetric bump shape, and searches for similar TS effects in other decay channels could distinguish this mechanism from genuine resonances.
