# On the Two $R$-Factors in the Small-$x$ Shockwave Formalism

> There are two $R$-factors frequently used in the phenomenology of exclusive processes at small values of the Bjorken $x$ variable. One $R$-factor takes into account the effects of non-zero longitudinal momentum transfer, which is assumed to be zero in the dipole scattering amplitude. Another $R$-factor accounts for the real part of the elastic scattering amplitude which is often neglected, with the standard dipole scattering amplitude giving only the imaginary part of the elastic amplitude. In this work we present two new theoretical developments aimed at eliminating the need for the two $R$-factors. We argue that the $R$-factors can be replaced by (i) modifying the argument of the dipole scattering amplitude and by (ii) augmenting the initial conditions for its non-linear small-$x$ evolution. Specifically, we show that to account for the effects of non-zero skewness $ξ$, one has to replace the rapidity argument $Y = \ln (1/x)$ of the eikonal dipole amplitude $N$ and the odderon dipole amplitude $\cal O$ by $Y = \ln \min \left\{ 1/|x|, 1/|ξ|\right\}$. The prescription applies to the elastic scattering cross sections, as well as for calculations of the Generalized Parton Distributions and Generalized Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distributions at small $x$ and at small but non-zero skewness $ξ$. We also show that the real part of the scattering amplitude, proportional to Im~$N$, which is intimately connected to the signature factor of the amplitude, can be accounted for by a more careful evaluation of the initial condition for the evolution and by writing the non-linear evolution equation in an integral form. One can similarly construct Im~$\cal O$ for the odd-signature odderon amplitude. We hope that future implementation of our prescriptions presented here will eliminate the need for both phenomenological $R$-factors.

## Key claim

The two phenomenological $R$-factors used in exclusive processes at small Bjorken $x$, one accounting for non-zero longitudinal momentum transfer (skewness) and one for the real part of the elastic scattering amplitude, can be eliminated by (i) replacing the rapidity argument of the dipole amplitude with $Y = \ln \min\{1/|x|, 1/|\xi|\}$ and (ii) modifying the initial condition for the small-$x$ evolution to include an imaginary part proportional to $\ln(|s-\Lambda^2|/|s+\Lambda^2|)$.

## Method

The authors start from the operator definitions of unpolarized gluon and quark Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) and Generalized Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) and convert them into expressions in the shock wave formalism using Wilson line correlators. They then analyze the effect of non-zero skewness $\xi$ on the small-$x$ evolution of the dipole scattering amplitude by considering general diagrams including non-linear terms where gluon emission splits a dipole into two independent gluon cascades. For the real part of the scattering amplitude, they derive a modified Glauber-Gribov-Mueller/McLerran-Venugopalan (GGM/MV) initial condition for the integral form of the small-$x$ evolution equation, incorporating a logarithmic imaginary term.

## Key numbers

- The rapidity argument of the eikonal dipole amplitude $N$ and the odderon dipole amplitude $\mathcal{O}$ is replaced by $Y = \ln \min \left\{ 1/|x|, 1/|\xi|\right\}$, where $x$ is the average parton longitudinal momentum fraction and $\xi$ is the skewness variable.
- The modified initial condition for the dipole scattering amplitude is given by $N_{10}^{(0)} (s) = 1 - \exp \left\{ - \frac{1}{4} \, x_{10}^2 \, Q_{s0}^2 \, \ln \left( \frac{1}{x_{10} \, \Lambda} \right) \, \left[ \theta \left(s - \Lambda^2 \right) + \frac{i}{\pi} \, \ln \left( \frac{|s - \Lambda^2|}{|s + \Lambda^2 |} \right) \right] \right\}$, where $x_{10}$ is the transverse dipole size, $Q_{s0}$ is the initial saturation scale, $\Lambda$ is an infrared cutoff, and $s$ is the center-of-mass energy squared.
- The unpolarized gluon GPD at small $x$ and small $\xi$ is expressed as $H^g (|x| \ll 1, |\xi| \ll 1, t, Q^2) = - \frac{N_c}{2 \pi^2 \, \alpha_s} \int d^2 b_\perp e^{i \vec{\Delta} \cdot \vec{b}} \left[ \left( \nabla_{\vec{x}_1} \cdot \nabla_{\vec{x}_0} \right) N_{10} (Y) \right]_{x_{10}^2 = 1/Q^2}$.

## Context

This work extends the shock wave formalism for exclusive processes at small $x$ beyond the usual approximations that ignore non-zero longitudinal momentum transfer and the real part of the scattering amplitude, which were previously restored only through phenomenological $R$-factors as in Kowalski (2006), Toll (2012), and Mantysaari (2016). The authors build on their own prior work (Kovchegov 2025) on GTMD/GPD operators at small $x$ and the inclusion of skewness in leading logarithmic approximation, as well as on the foundational shock wave formalism of Gribov (1984), Iancu (2003), and others. The results enable first-principles calculations of GPDs and GTMDs at small $x$ with non-zero skewness, and provide a framework for computing spin-dependent parton distributions that are energy-suppressed and thus absent in standard shock wave calculations.
