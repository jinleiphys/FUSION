# Phenomenological Aspects of Isospin Violation in the Nuclear Force

> Phenomenological Lagrangians and dimensional power counting are used to assess isospin violation in the nucleon-nucleon force. The $πNN$ coupling constants (including the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy), charge-symmetry breaking, and meson-mixing models are examined. A one-loop analysis of the isospin-violating $πNN$ coupling constants is performed using chiral perturbation theory. Meson-mixing models and the $^3$He - $^3$H mass difference are also discussed in the context of naturalness.

# Phenomenological Aspects of Isospin Violation in the Nuclear Force

## Key claim

Isospin violation in the nucleon-nucleon force can be systematically described using chiral perturbation theory with natural-sized parameters, and the observed charge-symmetry breaking is consistent with QCD symmetries without requiring large isospin-violating pion-nucleon coupling constants. The analysis shows that the isospin-violating parameters β₁ and β̅₁₀ extracted from Nijmegen phase-shift analysis are consistent with zero within uncertainties, and the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy is smaller than many previous values at 2.1(5)%.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological chiral Lagrangian with isospin-violating terms arising from quark-mass differences (proportional to ε = (m_d − m_u)/(m_d + m_u) ∼ 0.3) and from hard electromagnetic processes, organized by dimensional power counting with Δ = d + n_F/2 − 2. They performed a one-loop calculation of isospin violation in the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP), keeping only nonanalytic terms, and extracted the isospin-conserving Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy d−1 and the isospin-violating πNN coupling constants β₁ and β̅₁₀ from the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis results. They also evaluated meson-mixing models (ρ−ω, π−η, and axial-vector meson mixing) in the context of naturalness.

## Key numbers

- ε = (m_d − m_u)/(m_d + m_u) ∼ 0.3 (quark mass difference parameter)
- a_np = −23.75 fm and a_pp = −17.3 fm (¹S₀ scattering lengths from Nijmegen PSA, after removing long-range electromagnetic effects)
- a_nn = −18.8(4) fm (nn ¹S₀ scattering length from π⁻ + d → n + n + γ experiment)
- Difference a_nn − a_pp = −1.5(5) fm (best experimental information on charge-symmetry breaking in NN force)
- ³He − ³H binding energy difference = 764 keV, with ∼693 keV from Coulomb and other electromagnetic effects, leaving ∼70(25) keV consistent with short-range CSB
- f²_π⁰pp = 0.0745(6), f_π⁰pp f_π⁰nn = 0.0745(9), f²_πᶜnp = 0.0748(3) (pion-nucleon coupling constants from Nijmegen PSA)
- f²(d=1) = 0.0718(5) (chiral limit value)
- d−1 = 2.1(5)% (Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy)
- β₁ = 0(9)·10⁻³ (isospin-violating πNN coupling from np and pp data)
- β̅₁₀ = 5(18)·10⁻³ (isospin-violating πNN coupling from np and pp data)
- Δβ̅₁₀ = 6·10⁻³ (one-loop nonanalytic contribution to β̅₁₀)
- γ_s^{ρω} ∼ −0.54 GeV⁻² (ρ−ω mixing contribution to short-range CSB), with c_{ρω} ≅ −0.5
- β₁^η ∼ −3.5·10⁻³ (π−η mixing contribution to β₁), with c_η ≅ −0.6
- g_A = 1.2573(28) (axial vector coupling constant)
- f_π = 92.4(3) MeV (pion decay constant)
- Exchanged π⁰ mass = 135.6(13) MeV, π± mass = 139.4(10) MeV (from Nijmegen PSA)

## Context

This work extends the systematic chiral perturbation theory approach to isospin violation in the nuclear force developed by van Kolck (1994), applying it to the analysis of existing Nijmegen phase-shift analysis results (de Swart, 1995) and experimental data on scattering lengths and the ³He−³H mass difference. The paper reconciles meson-mixing models (including ρ−ω mixing from Henley and Miller, and π−η mixing from Coon and collaborators) with the chiral Lagrangian framework, showing that the assumption of saturation by on-shell meson mixing is consistent with naturalness. This enables a model-independent understanding of charge-symmetry breaking in nuclear forces, where specific mechanisms need not be uniquely identified as long as the effective parameters are of natural size.
