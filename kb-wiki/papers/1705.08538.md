# Effect of finite particle number sampling on baryon number fluctuations

> The effects of finite particle number sampling on the net baryon number cumulants, extracted from fluid dynamical simulations, are studied. The commonly used finite particle number sampling procedure introduces an additional Poissonian (or multinomial if global baryon number conservation is enforced) contribution which increases the extracted moments of the baryon number distribution. If this procedure is applied to a fluctuating fluid dynamics framework one severely overestimates the actual cumulants. We show that the sampling of so called test-particles suppresses the additional contribution to the moments by at least one power of the number of test-particles. We demonstrate this method in a numerical fluid dynamics simulation that includes the effects of spinodal decomposition due to a first order phase transition. Furthermore, in the limit where anti-baryons can be ignored, we derive analytic formulas which capture exactly the effect of particle sampling on the baryon number cumulants. These formulas may be used to test the various numerical particle sampling algorithms.

## Key claim

The commonly used Cooper-Frye finite particle sampling procedure introduces an additional Poissonian (or multinomial, if global baryon number conservation is enforced) contribution to net baryon number cumulants that severely overestimates the actual fluctuations when applied to a fluctuating fluid dynamics framework; using test-particles suppresses this extra contribution by at least one power of the number of test-particles.

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas for the cumulants of net baryon number after Cooper-Frye freeze-out, assuming Poisson sampling or multinomial sampling with global baryon number conservation, in the limit where anti-baryons can be ignored. They then tested these formulas by performing a 3+1D fluid dynamical simulation of central Pb+Pb collisions at a fixed target beam energy of 3.5 A GeV, which included spinodal decomposition due to a first-order phase transition, and compared the cumulants extracted directly from the fluid to those obtained after sampling particles with varying numbers of test-particles.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of the scaled variance due to spinodal clumping is strongest (approximately 25%) at a time t = 3.0 fm/c for central Pb+Pb collisions at E_lab = 3.5 A GeV.
- The total baryon number in the selected events is B_tot = 397, corresponding to N_part = 397 participating nucleons.
- For a single test-particle per baryon (N_T = 1), the scaled variance after Cooper-Frye freeze-out is twice as large as that of the hydro ensemble if the baryon number in a cell is Poisson-distributed.
- Using 20 test-particles per real particle gives results reasonably close to the original second and third order susceptibilities from the fluid dynamical simulation.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of how freeze-out procedures affect fluctuation observables in heavy-ion collisions, following prior studies on the effects of finite particle sampling and global charge conservation (Bzdak 2012, Gorenstein 2008, Begun 2004). The results are directly relevant to hybrid fluid-dynamical models that use Cooper-Frye freeze-out followed by hadronic transport (Petersen 2008, Huovinen 2012), and to fluctuating hydrodynamics approaches that aim to include critical phenomena (Nahrgang 2011, Plumberg 2017, Young 2014). The paper enables more reliable extraction of baryon number cumulants from dynamical simulations of the QCD phase transition and critical point, and provides analytic formulas that can be used to test and validate freeze-out algorithms.
