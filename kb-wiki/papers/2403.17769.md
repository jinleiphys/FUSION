# Spectra of correlators in the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory

> The relaxation time approximation (RTA) of the kinetic Boltzmann equation is likely the simplest window into the microscopic properties of collective real-time transport. Within this framework, we analytically compute all retarded two-point Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor and a conserved $U(1)$ current in thermal states with classical massless particles (a `CFT') at non-zero density, and in the absence and presence of broken translational symmetry. This is done in $2+1$ and $3+1$ dimensions. RTA allows a full explicit analysis of the analytic structure of different correlators (poles versus branch cuts) and the transport properties that they imply (the thermoelectric conductivities, and the hydrodynamic, quasihydrodynamic and gapped mode dispersion relations). Our inherently weakly coupled analysis thereby also enables a direct comparison with previously known strongly coupled results in holographic CFTs dual to the Einstein-Maxwell-axion theories.

## Key claim

The paper analytically computes all retarded two-point Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor and a conserved U(1) current within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) of kinetic theory for thermal states with classical massless particles, including at non-zero density and with broken translational symmetry, but only within the inherently weakly coupled regime of a dilute gas of particles.

## Method

The authors solve the RTA Boltzmann equation for a system of classical massless particles in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions, incorporating a finite chemical potential and a momentum relaxation parameter Gamma that breaks translational symmetry either parallel or perpendicular to the wavevector. They analytically compute all retarded two-point correlators of T^{mu nu} and J^{mu} and extract the dispersion relations of hydrodynamic, quasihydrodynamic, and gapped modes, as well as transport coefficients such as thermoelectric conductivities via linear response theory and Kubo formulae.

## Key numbers

- The branch cut in all spectra runs between branch points at omega = +/- k - i/tau_R, where tau_R is the RTA relaxation time.
- In 3+1 dimensions, correlators decompose into decoupled spin 0, 1, and 2 channels with respect to SO(2) symmetry about the momentum vector.
- In 2+1 dimensions, correlators decompose into even and odd channels with respect to Z_2 parity transformations about the momentum vector.
- At mu = 0, the <T^{mu nu} J^{rho}> correlators vanish.
- The 2+1 dimensional even current-current (density-density) correlator exhibits a gapped mode that collides with the diffusive mode, after which both modes become propagating, analogous to the "hydrodynamic-to-relativistic" crossover described in holography by Witczak-Krempa (2013).

## Context

This work extends the RTA analysis of Romatschke (2015), who first computed the full analytic structure of retarded two-point functions in RTA in 3+1 dimensions, to include finite density and broken translational symmetry. It enables direct comparison with strongly coupled holographic results from Einstein-Maxwell-axion theories studied by Davison and Gouteraux (2014) and others, bridging the gap between weakly coupled kinetic theory and strongly coupled holographic descriptions of thermal states with finite density and broken translations.
