# Consequences of omitting spin-orbit partner configurations on B(E2)'s and quadrupole moments in nuclei

> Calculations of B(E2)'s and quadruplole moments in the g_{9/2 region below ^{100} Sn are hampered by the fact that the inclusion of the g_{7/2}configuration leads to model spaces that are too large to handle. We therefore examine lighter nuclei if the fp region where one can easily include all the orbitals, f,{}_{7/2}, p_{3/2}, p_{1/2}and f_{5/2} . We perform such calculations but then take a step back and exclude the f_{5/2} orbital. By comparing the 2 calculations we can hope to get insight into the importance of the missing spin-orbit partner in other regions.

## Key claim

By comparing shell-model calculations in the full $fp$ space (including $f_{7/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, and $f_{5/2}$) with calculations that omit the $f_{5/2}$ orbital, the authors find that excluding the spin-orbit partner systematically reduces both B(E2) values and quadrupole moments, but that for many nuclei the effect on B(E2) values can be approximately compensated by a simple rescaling, while quadrupole moments are more sensitive to the truncation.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed using the Antoine code for the even-even nuclei $^{44}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti, $^{48}$Ti, $^{48}$Cr, $^{50}$Cr, and $^{52}$Fe in the full $fp$ model space ($f_{7/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $f_{5/2}$) and in a truncated space with the $f_{5/2}$ orbital removed. Two effective interactions were used: FPD6 (with effective charges $e_p=1.33$, $e_n=0.64$) and GXPF1 (with $e_p=1.5$, $e_n=0.5$). Quadrupole moments Q($2_1^+$) and Q($4_1^+$) and B(E2) values for $2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+$ and $4_1^+\rightarrow2_1^+$ transitions were computed in both spaces and compared via ratios.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Cr with FPD6, all four ratios (Q($2_1^+$) squared ratio, Q($4_1^+$) squared ratio, B(E2;$2\rightarrow0$) ratio, B(E2;$4\rightarrow2$) ratio) are approximately 1.40, showing "simple behavior" where the effect of omitting $f_{5/2}$ can be represented by a uniform rescaling.
- B(E2;$2\rightarrow0$) ratios (full/truncated) for FPD6 range from 1.16 ($^{44}$Ti) to 1.77 ($^{52}$Fe); for GXPF1 they range from 1.13 ($^{48}$Ti) to 1.54 ($^{52}$Fe).
- B(E2;$4\rightarrow2$) ratios for FPD6 range from 1.21 ($^{44}$Ti, $^{48}$Ti) to 1.87 ($^{52}$Fe); for GXPF1 they range from 1.16 ($^{48}$Ti) to 1.58 ($^{52}$Fe).
- Q($2_1^+$) values are negative for all nuclei in both full and truncated spaces, except Q($2_1^+$) truncated for $^{44}$Ti with GXPF1 which is +5.147 $e(fm)^2$.
- For $^{44}$Ti with GXPF1, the squared ratio (Q($4_1^+$) full/Q($4_1^+$) truncated)$^2$ = 28.516, indicating a dramatic failure of simple rescaling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations in Ge isotopes (Robinson 2011) where the omission of spin-orbit partner orbitals ($g_{7/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$) led to quadrupole moments that sometimes had opposite signs to experiment. It is motivated by the practical impossibility of including the $g_{7/2}$ orbital in $g_{9/2}$-region calculations near $^{100}$Sn. The results connect to the well-known work of Federman and Pittel (1977, 1979) on the role of spin-orbit partner occupation in driving nuclear deformation. The finding that B(E2) ratios are more robust than quadrupole moment ratios suggests that for heavy nuclei where full-space calculations are prohibitive, B(E2) values may be approximately corrected by rescaling, but quadrupole moments require more careful treatment.
