# Crystallization in single and multicomponent Neutron Star crusts

> We use Molecular Dynamics simulations to study the formation and stability of single and multicomponent lattices in the outer crust of Neutron Stars. Including an improved treatment for Gaussian charge distributions of ions we obtain the expressions for the potential and forces arising from electron screened Coulomb interactions using the efficient Ewald sum procedure. %in the plasma. Our findings show that for baryon densities in the outer crust a point-like ion treatment can not fully describe the crystallization behaviour thus the Coulomb parameter, $Γ_C$, along with the usual screening parameter, $κ$, due to charge neutralizing electron polarizable background, must be complemented with an additional parameter, $η$, providing information on the finite size of ions. In our approach we find that including beyond point-like approaches in screened plasmas has a robust impact on calculated lattice energetic stability decreasing crystallization energies per baryon up to $\sim 40\%$ with respect to point-like interaction and, as a consequence, melting point resulting displaced to lower temperatures.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for baryon densities in the outer crust of neutron stars, a point-like ion treatment cannot fully describe crystallization behavior, and that including finite-size Gaussian charge distributions for ions decreases crystallization energies per baryon by up to approximately 40% compared to point-like interactions, consequently shifting melting points to lower temperatures.

## Method
The authors performed Molecular Dynamics simulations in the NVT ensemble using an original code (USALMD_GI) with Ewald summation to compute potentials and forces for screened Coulomb interactions under the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Ions were modeled as finite-size Gaussian charge distributions with width dependent on mass number A, and the system included a polarizable degenerate electron background characterized by the Thomas-Fermi screening length. Simulations were run for one-component plasmas (OCP) with Z=38, A=124 and for two multicomponent mixtures (M1, M2) with five ion species each, at ion densities ranging from 10^-6 to 10^-4 fm^-3.

## Key numbers
- Crystallization energies per baryon decreased up to approximately 40% when including finite-size ion effects compared to point-like interactions.
- For the OCP with Z=38, A=124 at n_I = 2.06 x 10^-6 fm^-3, the melting Coulomb parameter Gamma_m was found in the interval [181, 200] for the screened case, compared to [171, 177] for the unscreened point-like case.
- For the OCP at n_I = (1, 2, 4) x 10^-4 fm^-3 with fixed Gamma = 190, the energy change relative to point-like description was Delta U/U approximately 16%, 24%, and 36% respectively.
- For mixture M1 at n_I = 1 x 10^-4 fm^-3, the energy change between point-like and finite-size ions was Delta U/U approximately 11% for Gamma_MCP in [190, 214].
- For mixture M2 at n_I = 1 x 10^-4 fm^-3 with Gamma_MCP = 195, lattice formation occurred with a decrease of approximately 14% in energy per ion compared to the fluid state.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Coulomb crystals in neutron star crusts, particularly the one-component plasma (OCP) models by Pollock and Hansen (1973) and the multicomponent plasma simulations by Caplan et al. It builds on the Thomas-Fermi screening approach used in earlier crust calculations, while incorporating finite nuclear size effects that were only partially addressed by Debye-Waller approximations in works by Potekhin and Chabrier (2000). The results enable more accurate modeling of crust properties such as thermal conductivity and shear modulus, which affect neutron star cooling, magnetic field evolution, and seismic activity.
