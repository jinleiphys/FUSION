# Asymmetric behavior of the B$(E2 \uparrow; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ values in $^{104-130}$Sn and Generalized Seniority

> We present freshly evaluated B$(E2\uparrow;0^+\rightarrow2^+)$ values across the even-even Sn-isotopes which confirm the presence of an asymmetric behavior as well as a dip in the middle of the full valence space. We explain these features by using the concept of generalized seniority. The dip in the B$(E2)$ values near $^{116}$Sn is understood in terms of a change in the dominant orbits before and after the mid shell, which also explains the presence of asymmetric peaks in the B$(E2)$ values. This approach helps in deciding the most active valence spaces for a given set of isotopes, and single out the most useful truncation scheme for Large Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations. The LSSM calculations so guided by generalized seniority are also able to reproduce the experimental data on B$(E2)\uparrow$ values quite well.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the B(E2) values in even-even Sn isotopes exhibit an asymmetric parabolic shape with a dip near \(^{116}\)Sn, and explains this dip not as reduced collectivity but as a consequence of a change in the dominant valence orbits (the \(g_{7/2}\) orbit freezes out and the \(h_{11/2}\) orbit takes over) across the mid-shell, using the framework of generalized seniority.

## Method

The authors first re-evaluated the experimental B(E2) values for \(^{104-130}\)Sn by weighted averaging of available measurements, excluding data from the \((e,e')\) method and other model-dependent techniques. They then applied a generalized seniority formula for multi-j degenerate orbits, dividing the full valence space (\(g_{7/2}, d_{5/2}, h_{11/2}, d_{3/2}, s_{1/2}\)) into two subspaces: one with \(\Omega=10\) (using \(^{100}\)Sn core, orbits \(g_{7/2} \otimes d_{5/2} \otimes d_{3/2} \otimes s_{1/2}\)) and one with \(\Omega=12\) (using \(^{108}\)Sn core, orbits \(d_{5/2} \otimes d_{3/2} \otimes s_{1/2} \otimes h_{11/2}\)). They also performed large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations using the Nushell code with the SN100PN effective interaction, guided by the same valence-space truncations, using neutron effective charges of \(e_\nu = 1.2\) for the first space and \(e_\nu = 1.0\) for the second.

## Key numbers

*   Evaluated B(E2) values for \(^{104}\)Sn: 0.176(26) \(e^2b^2\).
*   Evaluated B(E2) values for \(^{116}\)Sn: 0.2030(45) \(e^2b^2\) (the dip in the middle).
*   Evaluated B(E2) values for \(^{130}\)Sn: 0.023(5) \(e^2b^2\).
*   The two valence spaces used in generalized seniority calculations had total pair degeneracies \(\Omega = 10\) and \(\Omega = 12\).
*   Neutron effective charges used in LSSM calculations were \(e_\nu = 1.2\) (first valence space) and \(e_\nu = 1.0\) (second valence space).

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority approach previously applied by Maheshwari et al. to E1 and E3 transitions in Sn isotopes, and directly addresses the dip in B(E2) values near \(^{116}\)Sn reported by Jungclaus et al. (2011). The authors' interpretation supports the earlier explanation by Morales et al. (2011) that the dip arises from different rates of filling of the \(g_{7/2}\) and \(h_{11/2}\) orbits, rather than from reduced collectivity. The results provide a simple guide for truncating the valence space in large-scale shell model calculations, enabling more efficient calculations for semi-magic nuclei.
