# Structure of low-lying quadrupole states in nuclei near 132Sn

> The properties of the low-lying 2^+ states in the even-even nuclei around 132Sn are studied within the quasiparticle random phase approximation. Starting from a Skyrme interaction in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent zero-range interaction in the particle-particle channel, we use the finite rank separable approach in our investigation. It is found that the fourth 2^+ state in 132Te could be a good candidate for a mixed-symmetry state.

## Key claim
The fourth 2⁺ state (2⁺₄) in ¹³²Te is identified as a candidate for a mixed-symmetry state based on QRPA calculations, but the authors caution that this is only a rough estimate because the effects of phonon-phonon coupling are not included.

## Method
The authors performed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using a finite rank separable approximation, starting from a Skyrme interaction (SLy4) in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent zero-range force in the particle-particle channel. The single-particle spectrum was obtained from HF-BCS calculations with spherical symmetry, and the continuous part was discretized on a harmonic oscillator basis. The residual interaction was approximated by its Landau-Migdal form keeping only l=0 terms, and the QRPA equations were reduced to a secular equation with matrix dimensions never exceeding 6N × 6N.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strength V₀ = -940 MeV·fm³, used with a soft cutoff at 10 MeV above the Fermi energies
- Calculated energy of the 2⁺₄ state in ¹³²Te: 3.1 MeV
- Calculated excitation strength B(E2; 0⁺_gs → 2⁺₄) = 330 e²fm⁴ for ¹³²Te
- Calculated B(M1; 2⁺₄ → 2⁺₁) = 0.25 μ_N² for ¹³²Te
- In ¹³⁰Cd, the proton configuration {1g₉/₂, 1g₉/₂} contributes about 97% of the 2⁺₁ wave function normalization
- In ¹²⁸Pd, the proton configuration {1g₉/₂, 1g₉/₂} contributes 96% of the 2⁺₁ wave function normalization

## Context
This work extends the finite rank separable QRPA approach developed by Severyukhin et al. (2008) to include the particle-particle residual interaction, building on earlier work by Giai et al. (1998) and Severyukhin et al. (2002). The calculations describe the evolution of 2⁺₁ state energies and B(E2) values across isotopic chains of Pd, Cd, Sn, Te, and Xe near the N=82 shell closure, which is relevant for understanding the A≈130 peak in the solar r-process abundance distribution. The identification of the 2⁺₄ state in ¹³²Te as a mixed-symmetry state candidate provides a target for future experiments, though the authors note that phonon-phonon coupling effects must be included to finalize the study.
