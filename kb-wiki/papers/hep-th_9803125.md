# Skyrmions, Spectral Flow and Parity Doubles

> It is well-known that the winding number of the Skyrmion can be identified as the baryon number. We show in this paper that this result can also be established using the Atiyah-Singer index theorem and spectral flow arguments. We argue that this proof suggests that there are light quarks moving in the field of the Skyrmion. We then show that if these light degrees of freedom are averaged out, the low energy excitations of the Skyrmion are in fact spinorial. A natural consequence of our approach is the prediction of a $(1/2)^{-}$ state and its excitations in addition to the nucleon and delta. Using the recent numerical evidence for the existence of Skyrmions with discrete spatial symmetries, we further suggest that the the low energy spectrum of many light nuclei may possess a parity doublet structure arising from a subtle topological interaction between the slow Skyrmion and the fast quarks. We also present tentative experimental evidence supporting our arguments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the winding number of a Skyrmion can be identified as baryon number using the Atiyah-Singer index theorem and spectral flow arguments, which suggests that light quarks are created and bound to the Skyrmion during its formation; the authors then argue that averaging out these fast quark degrees of freedom via the Born-Oppenheimer approximation makes the low-energy excitations of the Skyrmion spinorial, leading to a prediction of a (1/2)⁻ state alongside the nucleon and delta, and further suggest that parity doublets may appear in light nuclei due to topological interactions between slow Skyrmions and fast quarks.

## Method

The authors consider a two-flavor chiral model with a Skyrme Lagrangian and a parity-invariant Yukawa coupling of Dirac fermions (quarks) to the chiral field U. They generalize the Lagrangian to include a gauge field, allowing continuous paths between flat connections of different winding numbers, and use instanton-like configurations to compute the spectral flow of the Dirac Hamiltonian via the Atiyah-Singer index theorem, showing that creating a winding-number-1 Skyrmion creates one fermionic state. They then apply the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to separate fast quark degrees of freedom from slow Skyrmion collective coordinates, quantizing the zero modes of the hedgehog configuration and analyzing the transformation properties of the fast and slow wave functions under spatial and isospin rotations.

## Context

This work extends the standard identification of Skyrmion winding number as baryon number (Witten 1983, Balachandran et al. 1982-1983) by providing an alternative proof using the Atiyah-Singer index theorem and spectral flow, which explicitly introduces light quarks into the Skyrmion picture. The Born-Oppenheimer treatment of Skyrmions with quarks builds on prior work by Balachandran and Vaidya (1997) on the bundle-theoretical formulation of the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for systems with discrete symmetries, and the prediction of parity doublets in light nuclei extends earlier suggestions by Iachello and Kirchbach about parity doubles among light baryons. The numerical evidence for Skyrmions with discrete spatial symmetries comes from work by Braaten et al. (1990), Carson (1991), and Battye and Sutcliffe (1997).
