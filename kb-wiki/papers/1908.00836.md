# Jet overlap in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies and its consequences on jet shape

> Central lead-lead collisions at the LHC energies may pose a particular challenge for jet identification as multiple jets are produced per each collision event. We simulate the jet evolution in central Pb-Pb events at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ GeV collision energy with EPOS3 initial state, which typically contains multiple hard scatterings in each event. Therefore the partons from different jets have a significant chance to overlap in momentum space. We find that 30% of the jets with $p_\perp > 50$ GeV, identified by the standard anti-$k_\perp$ jet finding algorithm with jet cone size R=0.3, contain `intruder' particles from overlapping generator-level jets. This fraction increases with increasing beam energy and increasing R. The reconstructed momentum of the jet differs from that of the modelled jet by the loss due to jet partons which are outside of the jet cone and by the gain due to intruder partons. The sum of both may be positive or negative. These intruder partons particularly affect the radial jet momentum distribution because they contribute mostly at large angles $Δr$ with respect to the jet centre. The study stresses the importance of the jet overlap effect emerging in central lead-lead collisions at the LHC energies, while being negligible in peripheral PbPb or $p$Pb/$pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

In central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, approximately 30% of jets with \(p_\perp > 50\) GeV identified by the standard anti-\(k_\perp\) algorithm with cone size R=0.3 contain "intruder" particles from overlapping generator-level jets, an effect that is negligible in peripheral PbPb or \(p\)Pb/\(pp\) collisions.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS3 model to generate initial-state hard partons for central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, then evolved each parton via a Monte Carlo implementation of DGLAP equations down to a virtuality scale \(Q_0 = 0.3\) GeV without hadronization or medium effects. The final-state partons were fed into the FASTJET package using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm to reconstruct jets, and the authors tracked which modelled jet each parton originated from via an auxiliary variable to quantify the fraction of momentum contributed by "intruder" partons from neighbouring jets.

## Key numbers

- 30% of jets with \(p_\perp > 50\) GeV, reconstructed with anti-\(k_\perp\) algorithm and cone size R=0.3 in 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions, contain intruder particles from overlapping generator-level jets.
- In 45-55% central Pb-Pb collisions at the same energy, only about 3% of jets show momentum contamination from neighbouring jets.
- Increasing the jet cone size from R=0.3 to R=0.5 reduces the fraction of pure (uncontaminated) jets to 50% in 0-5% central collisions.
- Intruder jets contribute almost 20% of the reconstructed jet momentum at angular distance \(r=0.25\) from the jet axis for R=0.3, and above 40% at larger \(r\) for R=0.5.
- Each central Pb-Pb collision at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV contains of the order of ten hard partons with \(p_\perp > 10\) GeV within 4 units of rapidity.

## Context

This study extends the understanding of jet reconstruction in heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard paradigm that treats jets as isolated objects, showing that in central Pb-Pb events at LHC energies, multiple hard scatterings cause significant jet overlap that affects jet momentum and radial momentum distribution. The work builds on the EPOS3 framework (Werner, 2013) and uses the anti-\(k_\perp\) algorithm from the FASTJET package (Cacciari, 2011). The results are relevant for interpreting experimental measurements by CMS (Sirunyan, 2018) and ALICE (Acharya, 2019) of jet shape modifications in PbPb relative to \(pp\), and suggest that theoretical calculations must either model solitary jets through QGP or simulate full AA events with background subtraction matching experimental procedures.
