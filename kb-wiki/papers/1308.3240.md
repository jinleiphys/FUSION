# Precision measures of the primordial abundance of deuterium

> We report the discovery of deuterium absorption in the very metal-poor ([Fe/H] = -2.88) damped Lyman-alpha system at z_abs = 3.06726 toward the QSO SDSS J1358+6522. On the basis of 13 resolved D I absorption lines and the damping wings of the H I Lyman alpha transition, we have obtained a new, precise measure of the primordial abundance of deuterium. Furthermore, to bolster the present statistics of precision D/H measures, we have reanalyzed all of the known deuterium absorption-line systems that satisfy a set of strict criteria. We have adopted a blind analysis strategy (to remove human bias), and developed a software package that is specifically designed for precision D/H abundance measurements. For this reanalyzed sample of systems, we obtain a weighted mean of (D/H)_p = (2.53 +/- 0.04) x 10^-5, corresponding to a Universal baryon density100 Omega_b h^2 = 2.202 +/- 0.046 for the standard model of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis. By combining our measure of (D/H)_p with observations of the cosmic microwave background, we derive the effective number of light fermion species, N_eff = 3.28 +/- 0.28. We therefore rule out the existence of an additional (sterile) neutrino (i.e. N_eff = 4.046) at 99.3 percent confidence (2.7 sigma), provided that N_eff and the baryon-to-photon ratio (eta_10) did not change between BBN and recombination. We also place a strong bound on the neutrino degeneracy parameter, xi_D = +0.05 +/- 0.13 based only on the CMB+(D/H)_p observations. Combining xi_D with the current best literature measure of Y_p, we find |xi| <= +0.062. In future, improved measurements of several key reaction rates, in particular d(p,gamma)3He, and further measures of (D/H)_p with a precision comparable to those considered here, should allow even more stringent limits to be placed on new physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper reports a weighted mean primordial deuterium abundance of (D/H)_p = (2.53 ± 0.04) × 10^-5 from a reanalyzed sample of absorption-line systems, but this result depends on the assumption that the effective number of neutrino species (N_eff) and the baryon-to-photon ratio (η_10) did not change between Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and recombination.

## Method

The authors discovered a new deuterium absorption system in the damped Lyman-alpha system at z_abs = 3.06726 toward QSO SDSS J1358+6522, using Keck HIRES spectroscopy at resolution R ~ 48,000. They reanalyzed all known deuterium absorption-line systems meeting strict criteria (including HI column density log N(HI)/cm^-2 ≥ 19, at least two resolved optically thin DI transitions, and data with R ≥ 30,000) using a blind analysis strategy with purpose-built software (ALIS) that simultaneously fits the quasar continuum, zero level, instrumental broadening, and all HI and DI Lyman series transitions.

## Key numbers

- Weighted mean primordial deuterium abundance: (D/H)_p = (2.53 ± 0.04) × 10^-5
- Corresponding universal baryon density: 100 Ω_b h^2 = 2.202 ± 0.046
- Effective number of light fermion species derived from combining D/H with CMB: N_eff = 3.28 ± 0.28
- Exclusion of an additional sterile neutrino (N_eff = 4.046) at 99.3 percent confidence (2.7 sigma)
- Neutrino degeneracy parameter from CMB + (D/H)_p: ξ_D = +0.05 ± 0.13
- Combined 2-sigma upper bound on neutrino degeneracy parameter: |ξ| ≤ +0.062
- Metallicity of the new DLA toward J1358+6522: [Fe/H] = -2.88
- Redshift of the new DLA: z_abs = 3.06726
- HI column density of the new DLA: log N(HI)/cm^-2 = 20.21 ± 0.06 (component 1a) and 20.16 ± 0.07 (component 1b)

## Context

This work extends the precision D/H measurements previously reported by Pettini and Cooke (2012) for the system J1419+0829, and addresses the long-standing concern first noted by Steigman (2001) that the dispersion in reported D/H measures exceeds quoted errors. The reanalysis of all known systems using a consistent blind methodology enables tighter constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model, including limits on sterile neutrinos and neutrino degeneracy, and the authors note that improved measurements of the d(p,γ)^3He reaction rate and additional D/H measurements will further strengthen these constraints.
