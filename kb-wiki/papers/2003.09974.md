# Naturalness in Nuclear Effective Field Theories

> Nuclear effective field theories (EFTs) have been developed over the last quarter-century with considerable impact on the description of light and even medium-mass nuclei. At the core of any EFT is a systematic expansion of observables, which is usually obtained from a rule based on an assumption of naturalness. I discuss naturalness in the context of the relatively weak binding of nuclei, where discrete scale invariance plays a role in the emergence of complexity.

## Key claim

Nuclear effective field theories (EFTs) rely on an assumption of naturalness to organize interactions into a systematic expansion, but nuclear physics exhibits apparent unnaturalness in the form of shallow bound states; this unnaturalness can be explained away for light nuclei by discrete scale invariance emerging in the unitarity limit, suggesting nuclear physics might be technically natural rather than finely tuned.

## Method

The paper examines naturalness in nuclear EFTs by contrasting naive dimensional analysis (NDA) with a modified NDA appropriate for nonrelativistic systems containing heavy, stable nucleons. It analyzes the renormalization of loop diagrams in Pionless EFT (where pions are integrated out and all loops are reducible) and in Chiral EFT, showing how regulator cutoff dependence reveals the natural size of low-energy constants. The author then demonstrates that discrete scale invariance, emerging in the unitarity limit where two-body binding energies vanish, can account for the unnaturally shallow binding of light nuclei within Pionless EFT.

## Key numbers

* Two-nucleon binding momenta: triplet bound state at κ₂₀ ≈ 45 MeV and singlet virtual state at κ₂₁ ≈ -8 MeV, both unnatural compared to the modified NDA expectation of |a₂⁻¹| = O(m_π) where m_π ≈ 137.3 MeV
* Alpha particle binding momentum: κ₄ ≈ 100 MeV, compared to the QCD scale M_QCD ≈ 1 GeV and nucleon mass m_N ≈ 940 MeV
* Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 92 MeV, with NDA estimate f_π = O(M_QCD/4π) ≈ 80 MeV
* Pion mass: m_π ≈ 137.3 MeV, with NDA estimate m_π² = O(hat{m} M_QCD) ≈ (70 MeV)²
* Charge-neutral pion squared-mass splitting: δm_π² ≈ (35.5 MeV)², with NDA estimate δm_π² = O(α_e M_QCD²/4π) ≈ (30 MeV)²
* Neutron-proton mass splitting: δm_N ≈ 1.3 MeV, with NDA estimate δm_N = O(1 MeV)
* Nucleon axial coupling: g_A ≈ 1.27, with NDA estimate |g_A| = O(1)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of naturalness from particle physics (where it frames debates about the Standard Model gauge hierarchy, strong CP, and cosmological constant problems) to nuclear EFTs, building on foundational work by Weinberg (1978, 1990, 1991), Manohar and Georgi (1983), and Georgi (1986, 1992). It connects to recent developments by Konig and collaborators (2015-2019) on discrete scale invariance in Pionless EFT, and to the comprehensive review of nuclear EFTs by Hammer and collaborators (2019). The paper enables a framework for understanding whether nuclear binding represents fine tuning or can be explained by an emergent symmetry, with implications for extending these ideas from light to heavier nuclei.
