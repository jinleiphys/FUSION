# Possible doublet mechanism for a regular component of parity violation in neutron scattering

> A nucleus with octupole deformation of the mean field reveals rotational doublets with the same angular momentum and opposite parity. Mediated by the Coriolis-type interaction, the doublet structure leads to a strong regular component in the parity violation caused by weak interaction. This can explain sign correlations observed in polarized neutron scattering by $^{232}$Th.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in nuclei with octupole deformation, rotational doublets of opposite parity can produce a regular (non-random) component of parity violation in neutron scattering, which could explain the same-sign asymmetries observed in polarized neutron capture on \(^{232}\)Th, contrary to the statistical randomness expected from the standard compound-nucleus enhancement mechanism.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework for parity mixing in octupole-deformed nuclei, where rotational doublets with the same angular momentum and opposite parity arise from the pear-shaped mean field. They show that direct mixing of doublet states by the parity-violating weak interaction is forbidden for a time-reversal-even interaction, so the mixing must proceed via a two-step process involving a parity-conserving interaction (such as the Coriolis force) that couples the doublet states to other configurations, combined with the weak interaction. The resulting parity admixture amplitude is estimated using a closure approximation, summing over distant intermediate states with typical excitation energies of order 100 keV.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle matrix element of the weak interaction is \((H_W)_{s-p} \simeq 5\) eV.
- The mixing amplitude between compound states of opposite parity is estimated as \(\beta_{comp} \simeq 10^{-4}\), using \(\langle H_W \rangle_{comp} \approx 1.3 \times 10^{-3}\) eV and level spacing \(D = 15\) eV.
- The regular component from the doublet mechanism gives an estimated mixing amplitude \(\beta \simeq 5 \times 10^{-5}\), leading to a predicted longitudinal asymmetry \(P \simeq 10^{-2}\).
- The statistical enhancement factor is \(\sim \sqrt{N} \sim 10^3\) where \(N \sim 10^5 - 10^6\) is the number of shell model configurations.
- The kinematical enhancement factor from s-wave and p-wave interference is \((\Gamma_s^{(n)} / \Gamma_p^{(n)})^{1/2} \sim 1/kR \sim 10^2 - 10^3\).
- The octupole deformation parameter is \(\beta_3 \simeq 0.35\) at excitation energies near neutron threshold.

## Context

This work extends the statistical compound-nucleus model of parity violation developed by Haas (1960), Shapiro (1968), and others, which predicts random signs for parity-violating asymmetries. It directly addresses the unexpected sign correlations observed in the LAMPF experiments by Bowman (1990) for neutron capture on \(^{232}\)Th, which contradicted the random-sign expectation. The mechanism relies on the octupole deformation and rotational doublet structure previously studied in Th isotopes by Leander (1982), Nazarewicz (1984), and Jolos (1994), and enables a framework for calculating regular parity-violating effects in deformed nuclei using thermal quasiparticle models as in Vorov (1994).
