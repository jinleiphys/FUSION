# Effective interactions in medium heavy nuclei

> We present a brief overview of microscopic nuclear structure approaches to nuclei with mass number from 100 to 132. The emphasis is on the shell model and theories for deriving effective interactions starting from the free interactions between nucleons. New results for (105,106,107)Sb are presented.

# Effective interactions in medium heavy nuclei

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, including folded-diagram renormalization through third order in the G-matrix, can reproduce the low-lying yrast states of the proton drip line nuclei 105,106,107Sb, provided the model space uses 100Sn as a closed shell core and includes the 2s1/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 0g7/2, and 0h11/2 orbits for both protons and neutrons.

## Method
The authors start with the charge-dependent CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and compute the G-matrix using a double-partitioning scheme to handle the repulsive core. They then renormalize the G-matrix elements using the Q-box method, including all non-folded diagrams to third order in G, and apply the folded-diagram expansion to obtain the effective shell-model interaction. Large-scale shell-model calculations are performed using the Lanczos algorithm for the even tin isotopes from 116Sn to 130Sn and for the odd antimony isotopes 105,106,107Sb, with model spaces consisting of the 2s1/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 0g7/2, and 0h11/2 orbits.

## Key numbers
- For 116Sn, the experimental 2+1-0+1 excitation energy is 1.29 MeV; the optimal effective interaction Veff gives 1.17 MeV, the bare G-matrix gives 1.14 MeV, the 1S0-only G-matrix gives 1.38 MeV, and omitting 1S0 and 3P2 partial waves gives no meaningful 0+ ground state.
- For 130Sn, the experimental 2+1-0+1 excitation energy is 1.23 MeV; Veff gives 1.46 MeV, the bare G-matrix gives 0.97 MeV, and the 1S0-only G-matrix gives 1.18 MeV.
- For 105Sb, the calculated ground state is 5/2+ at 0 MeV (theory and experiment), the 9/2+ state is at 1.22 MeV (both), the 13/2+ state is at 1.94 MeV (theory) versus 1.84 MeV (experiment), and the 19/2+ state is at 2.94 MeV (theory) versus 2.99 MeV (experiment).
- For 106Sb, the calculated 2+ ground state is at 0 MeV (both), the 4+ state is at 0.25 MeV (theory) versus 0.10 MeV (experiment), and the 10+ state is at 2.57 MeV (theory) versus 2.26 MeV (experiment).
- For 107Sb, the calculated 5/2+ ground state is at 0 MeV (both), the 9/2+ state is at 1.08 MeV (theory) versus 1.06 MeV (experiment), and the 17/2+ state is at 2.83 MeV (theory) versus 2.75 MeV (experiment).
- The dimensionality of the Hamiltonian matrix for 116Sn is on the order of n ~ 10^8.
- The proton separation energy for 106Sb is approximately 400 keV.

## Context
This work extends shell-model studies of medium-heavy nuclei near A=100-132, building on prior effective interaction derivations by Hjorth-Jensen et al. (1995) and Engeland et al. (1998). The authors demonstrate that the 1S0 and 3P2 partial waves, previously shown by Hjorth-Jensen and Heiselberg (2000) to be crucial for superfluidity in neutron star matter, are equally essential for reproducing the near-constant 2+1-0+1 excitation energy in even tin isotopes. The results for 105,106,107Sb connect to recent experimental work on proton emitters near the drip line, including studies of 105Sb and 109I as ground-state proton emitters and the level scheme for 106Sb proposed in Ref. sb106. Further analysis of nuclei such as Ag, Cd, In near A~100 and Sn isotopes near A~132 is in progress.
