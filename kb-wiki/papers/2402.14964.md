# Pairing enhancement through the photography of the intermediate nucleus spectrum in a two-nucleon transfer process

> While forming an ($A$+2) nucleus from a nucleus $A$ via a two-neutron transfer reaction, the constructive interference of the many possible reaction channels favors significant pairing enhancement through the continuum of the intermediate ($A$+1) nucleus [Phys. Lett. B \textbf{834} 137413 (2022)]. I analyse this situation in more generality, from the point of view of a varying pairing field and different continua leading to the formation of ($A$+2) nucleus. I consider $^6$He and $^{22}$C, described as housing two-neutrons in orbitals of $^5$He and $^{21}$C, respectively. The different possible situations manifest that the continuum correlations are extremely crucial to the extension of the pairing enhancement observed in the system.

## Key claim

The pairing enhancement in two-neutron transfer reactions, previously demonstrated for a fixed final state of the (A+2) nucleus, is shown to be a more general phenomenon that persists across varying pairing field strengths and different continuum structures of the intermediate (A+1) nucleus, but the degree of enhancement depends critically on whether the intermediate nucleus is unbound with a resonance, just bound, or artificially bound.

## Method

The author performs sequential two-neutron transfer calculations using a modified version of the Transfer Form Factors (TFF) code, working in the prior-prior representation for the reactions 18O(4He,6He)16O and 18O(20C,22C)16O at a lab beam energy of 100 MeV. The spectra of the intermediate nuclei 5He and 21C are discretized using the transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) pseudo-state method, with 8 states used to describe each spectrum; for 5He, three scenarios are considered (bound with Sn=1 MeV, bound with Sn=0.1 MeV, and naturally unbound with a resonance at 0.69 MeV), while for 21C a virtual state is mimicked at 0.0288 MeV. A contact delta pairing interaction is used to generate different final states in 6He and 22C by varying the coupling strength.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron transfer cross-section for the bound state in the Sn=1 MeV case of 5He: 9.94 mb, contributing 90% of the total one-neutron transfer cross-section.
* One-neutron transfer cross-section for the bound state in the Sn=0.1 MeV case of 5He: 6.12 mb, contributing 74% of the total one-neutron transfer cross-section.
* One-neutron transfer cross-section for the resonant state (third state at 0.69 MeV above zero) in the unbound 5He case: 1.66 mb, contributing 28% of the total one-neutron transfer cross-section.
* The resonance in unbound 5He is generated at 0.69 MeV above zero, close to the natural p3/2 resonance at 0.735 MeV (Wang 2021).
* The virtual state in 21C is mimicked as the first discretised state at 0.0288 MeV above the breakup threshold.
* The THO parameters for 5He cases: gamma=1.8, 2.0, 2.0 and b=1.0, 1.0, 1.2 fm for the Sn=1 MeV, Sn=0.1 MeV, and unbound cases respectively; for 21C: gamma=2.0 and b=1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing enhancement in two-neutron transfer reactions from Singh, Fortunato, and Vitturi (2022, Phys. Lett. B 834, 137413), which demonstrated significant enhancement through the continuum of an unbound intermediate nucleus for a fixed final state of 6He. The present analysis generalizes this by varying the pairing field to produce different final states in 6He and by examining the 22C system where only a virtual state exists in the intermediate 21C nucleus. The results enable quantification of how continuum correlations affect pairing enhancement across different nuclear systems, with implications for understanding the stability of Borromean nuclei and nucleosynthesis in astrophysical environments.
