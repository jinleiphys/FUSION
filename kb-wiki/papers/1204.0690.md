# Parity nonconserving cold neutron-parahydrogen interactions

> Three pion dominated observables of the parity nonconserving interactions between the cold neutrons and parahydrogen are calculated. The transversely polarized neutron spin rotation, unpolarized neutron longitudinal polarization, and photon-asymmetry of the radiative polarized neutron capture are considered. For the numerical evaluation of the observables, the strong interactions are taken into account by the Reid93 potential and the parity nonconserving interactions by the DDH model along with the two-pion exchange.

## Key claim

The paper calculates three parity-nonconserving (PNC) observables for cold neutron-parahydrogen interactions using the DDH model with two-pion exchange contributions, finding that the two-pion exchange reduces the one-pion exchange effect by over 30% in the spin rotation and polarization, but the results are sensitive to the choice of form factors and cutoff masses used to regularize the two-pion exchange potentials.

## Method

The author computed the transversely polarized neutron spin rotation, unpolarized neutron longitudinal polarization, and photon-asymmetry of radiative polarized neutron capture using the Reid93 potential for strong interactions and the DDH meson-exchange model for PNC interactions, including both one-meson exchange (π, ρ, ω) and two-pion exchange contributions. The scattering observables were calculated in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) using exact solutions of the coupled Schrödinger equation, while the capture asymmetry required parity-admixed wavefunctions and regularization of the two-pion exchange potentials via monopole and dipole form factors with cutoff masses of 0.8, 1.0, and 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- DDH "best" value for neutron spin rotation in liquid parahydrogen: \(3.31 \times 10^{-7}\) rad/m, with one-pion exchange contributing about 85% of the total
- Unregularized two-pion exchange contribution to spin rotation: \(-0.101 h^{(1)}_\pi\) rad/m from NN intermediate states only, and \(-0.209 h^{(1)}_\pi\) rad/m including Δ(1232) isobar effects, reducing the OPE effect by over 30%
- DDH OME contribution to neutron polarization at \(T_{\rm Lab}^n = 10\) meV: \(7.43 \times 10^{-12}\) m\(^{-1}\)
- Calculated thermal neutron capture cross-section \(\sigma(np \rightarrow \gamma d) = 334.42\) mb at \(T_{\rm Lab}^n = 25\) meV, compared to experimental value \(334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb
- Reduced matrix elements for spin rotation components (e.g., \(\mathcal{I}^{{}^3P_0\pi}_{{}^1S_0[\times]_-} = -30.1992\) mb) and polarization components (e.g., \(\mathcal{G}^{{}^3P_0\pi}_{{}^1S_0[\times]_-} = -8.6220 \times 10^{-10}\) fm/meV) are constants within neutron kinetic energy range 0-1 keV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of PNC neutron spin rotation in parahydrogen by Liu et al. (2007) and Schiavilla et al. (2008) that used the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential, and by Avishai (1979) using the Paris potential, with the present results consistent in magnitude but half the size of Liu et al. due to using molecular rather than atomic number density. The inclusion of two-pion exchange contributions, previously neglected in PNC analyses, follows recent calculations by Carlson et al. (2002) and Driscoll et al. (2002) showing their importance in \(\vec{p}p\) elastic scattering. The results enable comparison with the ongoing NPDGamma experiment measuring \(\gamma\)-asymmetry in \(\vec{n}p \rightarrow \gamma d\) to determine the weak \(NN\pi\) coupling \(h^{(1)}_\pi\), and provide predictions for the planned neutron spin rotation measurement at the Spallation Neutron Source.
