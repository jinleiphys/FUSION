# Hadronic Spectral Functions above the QCD Phase Transition

> We extract the spectral functions in the scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial vector channels above the deconfinement phase transition temperature (Tc) using the maximum entropy method (MEM). We use anisotropic lattices, 32^3 * 32, 40, 54, 72, 80, and 96 (corresponding to T = 2.3 Tc --> 0.8 Tc), with the renormalized anisotropy xi = 4.0 to have enough temporal data points to carry out the MEM analysis. Our result suggests that the spectral functions continue to possess non-trivial structures even above Tc and in addition that there is a qualitative change in the state of the deconfined matter between 1.5 Tc and 2 Tc.

## Key claim

The spectral functions in the scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels continue to possess non-trivial structures even above the deconfinement phase transition temperature (Tc), and there is a qualitative change in the state of the deconfined matter between 1.5 Tc and 2 Tc, as suggested by the MEM analysis of lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors used anisotropic lattices with renormalized anisotropy ξ = 4.0 and the quenched approximation (standard Wilson action for quarks, naive plaquette action for gluons) on 32^3 × Nτ lattices with Nτ = 32, 40, 54, 72, 80, and 96, corresponding to temperatures from 2.3 Tc down to 0.8 Tc. They extracted spectral functions in the scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels from Euclidean 2-point correlation functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM), leaving out data points near the temporal edges (τ = 1–4 and Nτ−2 to Nτ) to avoid artifacts from unphysical modes.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 32^3 × Nτ with Nτ = 32, 40, 54, 72, 80, and 96, corresponding to T = 2.3 Tc, 1.9 Tc, 1.4 Tc, 1.04 Tc, 0.93 Tc, and 0.78 Tc, respectively.
- Renormalized anisotropy ξ = 4.0; temporal lattice spacing aτ = 9.75 × 10^(-3) fm.
- Tc determined from Polyakov loop susceptibility: Tc = 271 MeV (comparable to the real value 271 ± 2 MeV).
- At T ≈ 1.4 Tc (Nτ = 54): a sharp peak at about ω = 2 GeV in all four channels, with two bumps above the peak; spectral functions nearly degenerate across channels.
- At T ≈ 1.9 Tc (Nτ = 40): peaks around ω ≈ 4.5 GeV are significantly broader than those around 2 GeV at T ≈ 1.4 Tc; an apparent peak around ω = 0 (not statistically significant).
- Hopping parameter corresponds to mπ/mρ ≈ 0.7 at T = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of MEM to lattice QCD at zero temperature (Nakahara, Asakawa, and Hatsuda, 1999; Asakawa, Hatsuda, and Nakahara, 2001) and addresses the need for sufficient temporal data points (at least about 30) for reliable MEM analysis, as demonstrated on a 40^3 × 30 isotropic lattice at β = 6.47. The results contradict the expectation that the quark-gluon plasma behaves as a free gas of massive quarks and gluons, instead showing nontrivial spectral structures above Tc. The use of Nτ twice as large as that employed in earlier work (Bielefeld group, 2001) enables more reliable MEM analysis. The findings enable further investigation of the nature of the deconfined phase and the possible qualitative change between 1.5 Tc and 2 Tc.
