# Kaon Electromagnetic Production on Nuclei

> The formation and excitation of hypernuclei through kaon photoproduction is reviewed. Basic features of the production process are emphasized. The possibility of extracting new information on hypernuclear structure and on the wave function of the bound $Λ$ is discussed. New results are presented for the quasifree production process $A(γ, K Λ)B$. Observables of this reaction are shown to be sensitive to the $Λ$-nucleus final state interaction.

# Kaon Electromagnetic Production on Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper reviews hypernuclear formation via kaon photoproduction and presents new predictions for quasifree production, showing that observables are sensitive to the Λ-nucleus final state interaction; however, the authors caution that the elementary production amplitude must be measured more precisely before reliable predictions can be made, as different elementary operators that fit the same nucleon data can produce hypernuclear cross sections differing by more than an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors compute hypernuclear formation cross sections using a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework with single-particle matrix elements that separate nuclear structure (via reduced density matrix elements) from the elementary photoproduction operator. For quasifree production on nuclei such as ¹²C, they employ harmonic oscillator bound-state wave functions, a tρ optical potential for kaon distortion derived from K⁺N phase shifts, and a global Dirac optical potential for the hyperon-nucleus final state interaction based on the constituent quark model.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O(γ,K⁺)¹⁶_ΛN at Θ_K=0°, the differential cross section increases from approximately 15 nb/sr at E_γ=0.84 GeV to 330 nb/sr at E_γ=2 GeV for the transition shown.
- The K⁺ mean free path in the nuclear medium is 5-7 fm.
- The formation probability of bound hypernuclear states is estimated at 5-10% of the total (γ,K⁺) strength on nuclear targets.
- For the reaction ¹²C(γ,KY)¹¹B_g.s. at E_γ=1.4 GeV and Q=120 MeV/c under quasifree kinematics, kaon and hyperon energies reach approximately 500-700 MeV, and the hyperon angle reaches up to 45 degrees depending on the channel.
- Kaon distortion reduces cross sections by about 10-20% for p-shell nuclei; including hyperon final state interaction reduces angular distributions by up to 30% at forward angles.
- The s_Λ doublet in ⁹_ΛLi (3/2⁺ and 5/2⁺) is split by 0.51 MeV according to the "standard" ΛN interaction.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hypernuclear excitation via kaon photoproduction from about 8-10 years prior (Bennhold 1989, Cotanch 1986, Rosenthal 1988, Cohen 1989) and applies a DWIA framework previously used for quasifree pion and eta photoproduction (Lee 1993, Lee 1996). The paper presents the first theoretical predictions for quasifree kaon photoproduction from nuclei, enabling planned experiments at Jefferson Lab (Hall B) to study the Λ-nucleus final state interaction and to extract the bound Λ wave function via stretched transitions dominated by the Kroll-Ruderman term.
