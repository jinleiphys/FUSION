# Symmetry Breaking and Bifurcations in the Periodic Orbit Theory: I: Elliptic Billiard

> We derive an analytical trace formula for the level density of the two-dimensional elliptic billiard using an improved stationary phase method. The result is a continuous function of the deformation parameter (eccentricity) through all bifurcation points of the short diameter orbit and its repetitions, and possesses the correct limit of the circular billiard at zero eccentricity. Away from the circular limit and the bifurcations, it reduces to the usual (extended) Gutzwiller trace formula which for the leading-order families of periodic orbits is identical to the result of Berry and Tabor. We show that the circular disk limit of the diameter-orbit contribution is also reached through contributions from closed (periodic and non-periodic) orbits of hyperbolic type with an even number of reflections from the boundary. We obtain the Maslov indices depending on deformation and energy in terms of the phases of the complex error and Airy functions. We find enhancement of the amplitudes near the common bifurcation points of both short-diameter and hyperbolic orbits. The calculated semiclassical level densities and shell energies are in good agreement with the quantum mechanical ones.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical trace formula for the level density of the two-dimensional elliptic billiard that remains continuous and finite through all bifurcation points of the short diameter orbit and its repetitions, as well as through the circular limit, whereas the standard Gutzwiller trace formula diverges at these critical points.

## Method

Starting from a phase-space trace formula in action-angle variables, the authors perform the integrals over both action and angle variables using an improved stationary phase method that keeps the integration limits finite rather than extending them to infinity. They expand the phase and amplitude around stationary points up to second order, and include contributions from stationary points that lie outside the physical integration region (including complex "ghost" orbits). The Maslov indices are obtained in terms of the phases of complex error and Airy functions, depending on deformation and energy.

## Key numbers

* The bifurcation deformations for the short diameter orbit M(1,2) are given by η_bif = 1/sin(π n_u/n_v) = 1/cos(nπ/2M), where n = 1,2,3,...,M-1; for example, the triply repeated short diameter 3(1,2) has bifurcation points at η = 2/√3 and η = 2.
* The curvature K_s for the primitive short diameter orbit is K_s = -1/(π p R η^(3/2)), which is finite and negative for all deformations.
* The separatrix value of the action I_u at σ_s = 1 is I_u^(s) = 2pζ/π.
* The maximum action I_u^(cr) for the creeping orbit at σ_cr = η²/(η²-1) is I_u^(cr) = (2pR√η/π) E(π/2, √(η²-1)/η).

## Context

This work extends the periodic orbit theory of Gutzwiller (1971) and the Berry-Tabor trace formula (1976, 1977) for integrable systems to handle symmetry breaking and bifurcations simultaneously. It addresses the singularities that arise in the standard stationary phase method when a continuous symmetry is broken (as in the circular-to-elliptic transition) or when periodic orbits bifurcate, problems previously treated separately by uniform approximations such as those of Sieber and Schomerus (1996, 1997) and Tomsovic, Grinberg and Ullmo (1995). The approach enables a unified treatment of multiple nearby critical points and provides a foundation for extending the method to the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity in a succeeding paper.
