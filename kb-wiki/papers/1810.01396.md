# Effect of the Quark-Gluon Vertex on Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> In this work, we investigate how the details of the quark-gluon interaction vertex affect the quantitative description of chiral symmetry breaking through the gap equation for quarks. We start from two gluon propagator models widely used in literature and constructed in direct connection with our gradually improved understanding of infrared quantum chromodynamics coupled with its exact one-loop limit. The gap equation is then solved by employing a variety of vertex \emph{Ansätze}, which have been constructed in order to implement some of the key aspects of quantum chromodynamics, namely, multiplicative renormalizability of the quark propagator, gauge invariance, matching with perturbation theory in the weak coupling regime, independence from unphysical kinematic singularities as well as manifestly correct transformation properties under charge conjugation and parity operations. On general grounds, all truncation schemes exhibit the same qualitative and quantitative pattern of chiral symmetry breaking, ensuring the overall robustness of this approach and its potentially reliable description of the hadron spectrum and properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, despite employing a variety of increasingly sophisticated quark-gluon vertex *Ansätze* (Ball-Chiu, Curtis-Pennington, Kizilersu-Pennington, and Bashir et al.) combined with two different gluon propagator models (Maris-Tandy and Qin-Chang), all truncation schemes yield the same qualitative and quantitative pattern of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, confirming the robustness of the approach for describing hadron properties.

## Method

The authors solved the quark Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation in the Landau gauge using two infrared-enhanced gluon propagator models: the Maris-Tandy (MT) model and the Qin-Chang (QC) model. For each gluon model, they employed five different vertex *Ansätze*: the bare vertex, the Ball-Chiu (BC) longitudinal vertex, the Curtis-Pennington (CP) vertex, the Kizilersu-Pennington (KP) vertex, and the Bashir et al. (BB) vertex, each incorporating progressively more constraints from multiplicative renormalizability, gauge invariance, and perturbation theory matching. The resulting coupled integral equations for the quark dressing functions A(p²) and B(p²) were solved numerically to extract the quark mass function M(p²) and wavefunction renormalization Z(p²).

## Key numbers

- The MT model parameters are ω = 0.5 GeV and D such that the product m_G³ = ωD is held constant, with typical m_G values in the range 0.4–0.8 GeV.
- The QC model uses the same infrared scale ω ∈ [0.4, 0.6] GeV, with the product ωD kept constant to ensure insensitivity of hadron properties to small variations.
- The BB vertex coefficients are fixed to momentum-independent constants: a₁ = 0, a₂ = 3.4, a₃ = 1, a₄ = 1, a₅ = -4/3, a₆ = -1/2, a₇ = 2.167, a₈ = -3.7.
- The ultraviolet term in both gluon models uses N_f = 4, Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV, m_t = 0.5 GeV, and γ_m = 12/(33 - 2N_f).
- The renormalization-point-invariant quark mass is defined as \hat{m} = m(μ) [½ ln(μ²/Λ_QCD²)]^{γ_m}.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of Schwinger-Dyson equation truncations for QCD, building on the Maris-Tandy model (Maris, 1999) and the Qin-Chang model (Qin, 2011) for the gluon propagator, which themselves incorporate lattice QCD results for the massive gluon solution. The vertex *Ansätze* tested include the Ball-Chiu construction (Ball, 1980), the Curtis-Pennington vertex (Curtis, 1990), the Kizilersu-Pennington vertex (Kizilersu, 2009), and the Bashir et al. family of vertices (Bashir, 2011). The results enable future applications to hadron spectroscopy and properties via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, particularly for channels where the rainbow-ladder truncation is insufficient, such as axial vector mesons.
