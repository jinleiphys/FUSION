# Friction terms in multi-fluid description of heavy-ion collisions

> In multi-fluid description of heavy-ion collisions, the primary scatterings and particle production are described in terms of interaction between fluids, so called friction. These friction terms can be derived from kinetic theory, but they are not unique. We compare different approaches to derive the friction terms, introduce a new ``charge transfer" friction, which allows to move charge to the midrapidity fireball, and implement them in the MUFFIN model. The charge transfer friction is more consistent with the assumption of three fluids clearly separated in momentum space, and allows better comparisons of the experimental data and underlying equation of state. It also leaves room for entropy generation due to dissipation in individual fluids, and we present the first results obtained using viscous multi-fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new "charge transfer" friction model for multi-fluid descriptions of heavy-ion collisions, which allows baryon charge to be transferred to the midrapidity fireball fluid, but the authors find that this model alone underestimates charged hadron multiplicity at midrapidity; this apparent failure is actually a feature because it leaves room for entropy generation from shear viscosity within individual fluids, and including viscous effects resolves the tension between reproducing charged hadron and net-proton distributions.

## Method

The authors implement three different friction models (Csernai-type, Ivanov-Mishustin-Satarov-type, and the new charge transfer friction) in the MUFFIN multi-fluid solver, which evolves three interacting fluids (projectile, target, and fireball) using hydrodynamic equations with source terms. The charge transfer friction assigns outgoing nucleons to fluids based on a rapidity threshold parameter beta and an energy transfer parameter alpha, interpolating between the two existing models. The authors then compare the resulting pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons and rapidity distributions of net protons against experimental data from PHOBOS and NA49 at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, and subsequently include shear viscosity using a baryon chemical potential dependent parametrization from Jahan (2024).

## Key numbers

- Collision energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV used for benchmarking friction models, with data from PHOBOS at 19.6 GeV and NA49 at 17.3 GeV
- Charge transfer friction parameters: alpha = 0.7, beta = 0.1, xi_pt = 1.0, xi_f = 0.1 used as optimal combination
- Shear viscosity parametrization: eta_0 = 0.045, eta_2 = 0.28, eta_4 = 0.287 for the ratio eta*T/(epsilon+p) at different baryon chemical potential ranges (0 to 0.2 GeV, 0.2 to 0.4 GeV, and above 0.4 GeV)
- Best fit at 62.4 GeV required different parameters: alpha = 0.75, xi_pt = 0.8
- Electric charge density fixed to n_Q = 0.4 n_B in the equation of state

## Context

This work extends the multi-fluid model approach originally developed by Csernai (1982) and later refined by Satarov, Mishustin, Ivanov, and Toneev (1990-2005), which describes heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies (sqrt(s_NN) ~ 10-100 GeV) where baryon transparency produces a double peak structure in net baryon distributions. The new charge transfer friction model enables exploration of the equation of state at finite baryon density by allowing the fireball to acquire net baryon charge, and the inclusion of shear viscosity represents the first dissipative multi-fluid calculations. This enables more consistent comparisons with experimental data across multiple collision energies (7.7, 19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV) and observables including transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow.
