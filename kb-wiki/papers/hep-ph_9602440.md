# Lorentz Boosts as Squeeze Transformations and the Parton Picture

## Key claim
The Lorentz boost of a hadron is a squeeze transformation in light-cone coordinates, and Feynman's parton picture emerges as the high-momentum limit of Lorentz-squeezed hadronic wave functions in the covariant harmonic oscillator formalism, resolving the apparent paradox that quarks appear as bound states in one frame and as incoherent free partons in another.

## Method
The paper uses the covariant harmonic oscillator formalism to construct Lorentz-covariant bound-state wave functions for hadrons composed of two quarks. It expresses these wave functions in light-cone coordinates (u, v) and shows that a Lorentz boost along the longitudinal direction becomes a diagonal squeeze transformation that expands the u-component and contracts the v-component by factors exp(η) and exp(-η) respectively. The formalism is then used to derive both the space-time and momentum-energy wave functions, and to compute the ratio of the quark's interaction time with an external signal to the internal oscillation period.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the quark's interaction time with an external signal (T_ext) to the internal oscillation period (T_osc) is exp(-2η), where η is the rapidity parameter.
- For a proton with energy 900 GeV from the Fermilab accelerator, this ratio becomes 10^{-6}.
- The uncertainty product in light-cone variables satisfies <u²><q_u²> = 1/4 and <v²><q_v²> = 1/4, demonstrating Lorentz invariance of Planck's constant.
- The product of longitudinal position and momentum widths is <z²><q_z²> = (1/4)[cosh(2η)]², which increases with velocity.

## Context
This work extends the space-time picture of Lorentz deformation of hadronic interaction regions developed by Gribov, Ioffe, and Pomeranchuk (1966) and by Ioffe (1969), who showed that combinations of space and time variables (light-cone variables) are needed to measure the interaction region. The paper connects this squeeze picture to Feynman's parton model (1969) and to Wigner's little groups (1939), showing that the covariant harmonic oscillator formalism (Kim and Noz, 1986) provides a unified framework where the quark model and parton model are two manifestations of the same covariant entity. This enables a quantitative understanding of why partons appear as incoherent free particles at high energies and why the number of partons appears infinite while only a finite number of quarks exist inside the hadron.
