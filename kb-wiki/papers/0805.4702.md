# Pairing within the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation at finite temperature

> An approach to pairing in finite nuclei at nonzero temperature is proposed, which incorporates the effects due to the quasiparticle-number fluctuation (QNF) around Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) mean field and dynamic coupling to quasiparticle-pair vibrations within the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA). The numerical calculations of pairing gap, total energy, and heat capacity were carried out within a doubly folded multilevel model as well as realistic nuclei $^{56}$Fe and $^{120}$Sn. The results obtained show that, under the effect of QNF, in the region of moderate and strong couplings, the sharp transition between the superconducting and normal phases is smoothed out, resulting in a thermal pairing gap, which does not collapse at the BCS critical temperature, but has a tail, which extends to high temperature. The dynamic coupling of quasiparticles to SCQRPA vibrations significantly improves the agreement with the results of exact calculations and those obtained within the finite-temperature quantal Monte Carlo method for the total energy and heat capacity. It also causes a deviation of the quasiparticle occupation numbers from the Fermi-Dirac distributions for free fermions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that including quasiparticle-number fluctuation (QNF) and dynamic coupling to self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (SCQRPA) vibrations smooths out the sharp superconducting-to-normal phase transition in finite nuclei, producing a thermal pairing gap that does not collapse at the BCS critical temperature but instead extends as a tail to higher temperatures, with the caveat that this behavior is most pronounced in the region of moderate and strong pairing couplings.

## Method

The authors extended the SCQRPA formalism to nonzero temperature by deriving renormalized BCS equations that incorporate QNF through the variance of the quasiparticle-number operator and screening factors from the expectation values of quasiparticle-pair creation and annihilation operators. They solved the gap equation, particle-number equation, and SCQRPA equations self-consistently, using the method of double-time Green's functions with a Bogoliubov-Tyablikov decoupling to obtain quasiparticle occupation numbers that include dynamic coupling to SCQRPA phonons. Two approximation schemes were implemented: FTBCS1 (including QNF but no dynamic coupling) and FTBCS1+SCQRPA (including both QNF and dynamic coupling), with a Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection variant (FTLN1 and FTLN1+SCQRPA).

## Key numbers

- The BCS critical temperature is given by \( T_{\rm c} = 0.567\Delta(0) \), where \(\Delta(0)\) is the pairing gap at zero temperature.
- Numerical calculations were performed for the doubly folded multilevel model and realistic nuclei \(^{56}\)Fe and \(^{120}\)Sn.
- The quasiparticle occupation number \( n_j \) is defined as \( n_j = \langle {\cal N}_j \rangle / (2\Omega_j) = \frac{1}{2}(1 - \langle {\cal D}_j \rangle) \), where \(\Omega_j = j + 1/2\) is the shell degeneracy.
- The QNF for the \(j\)-th orbital is given by \(\delta {\cal N}_j^2 \equiv n_j(1 - n_j)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature SCQRPA for the Richardson model (Dang and Hung, 2007) to finite temperature, incorporating QNF effects previously studied in the modified BCS (MBCS) theory (Dang and Arima, 1997-2004) and modified HFB (MHFB) theory. It goes beyond the static-path approximation (SPA) and Landau theory of phase transitions (Moretto, 1972; Goodman, 1981) by including dynamic coupling to pair vibrations, which improves agreement with exact shell-model calculations and finite-temperature quantal Monte Carlo results. The approach enables microscopic studies of thermal pairing in finite nuclei without the artificial collapse of the pairing gap at the BCS critical temperature, and it predicts deviations of quasiparticle occupation numbers from Fermi-Dirac distributions for free fermions.
