# Energy loss at NLO in a high-temperature Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We present an extension of the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe kinetic equations for jet energy loss to NLO in the strong coupling constant. A novel aspect of the NLO analysis is a consistent description of wider-angle bremsstrahlung (semi-collinear emissions), which smoothly interpolates between 2<->2 scattering and collinear bremsstrahlung. We describe how many of the ingredients of the NLO transport equations (such as the drag coefficient) can be expressed in terms of Wilson line operators and can be computed using a Euclidean formalism or sum rules, both motivated by the analytic properties of amplitudes at light-like separations. We conclude with an outlook on the computation of the shear viscosity at NLO.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) kinetic equations for jet energy loss to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling constant, with the caveat that this extension is valid only in the weak-coupling regime where \(g\ll 1\) and requires extrapolation to temperatures where \(\alpha_s\sim 0.3\) to test the stability of perturbation theory.

## Method

The author reorganizes the LO AMY collision operator into three components: large-angle processes (hard \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) scattering with momentum transfer \(Q\gtrsim T\)), diffusion processes (soft momentum exchanges described by a Fokker-Planck equation with drag and momentum broadening coefficients), and collinear processes (\(1\leftrightarrow 2\) splittings). At NLO, soft gluon loops suppressed by \(g\) rather than \(g^2\) are included, and a new semi-collinear process (wider-angle bremsstrahlung) is introduced, which smoothly interpolates between \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) scattering and collinear bremsstrahlung. The soft contributions are expressed in terms of Wilson line operators and evaluated using a Euclidean formalism or sum rules based on analytic properties of amplitudes at light-like separations.

## Key numbers

* The transverse momentum broadening coefficient at LO is \(\hat{q} = \frac{g^2 C_A T m_D^2}{2\pi} \ln\frac{\mu_\perp}{m_D}\), where \(m_D^2 = N_c g^2 T^2/3\) is the Debye mass.
* The longitudinal momentum broadening coefficient at LO is \(\hat{q}_L = \frac{g^2 C_A T m_\infty^2}{2\pi} \ln\frac{\mu_\perp}{m_\infty}\), where \(m_\infty^2 = m_D^2/2\) is the asymptotic gluon mass.
* The NLO correction to the longitudinal coefficient involves replacing \(m_\infty^2\) with \(m_\infty^2 + \delta m_\infty^2\), where \(\delta m_\infty^2\) is the \(\mathcal{O}(g)\) correction to the asymptotic mass.
* Soft gluon loops are suppressed by \(g\) rather than \(g^2\) due to Bose enhancement \(n_B(gT) \sim 1/g\).

## Context

This work extends the AMY effective kinetic theory (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002) for jet energy loss and transport coefficients (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2003) to NLO. The Euclidean mapping technique was first applied to the collinear sector by Caron-Huot (2008), and the new sum rule for the longitudinal diffusion coefficient was developed in Ghiglieri (2015). The NLO results enable a numerical implementation in Monte Carlo generators such as MARTINI (Schenke, 2009) and provide a framework for computing the shear viscosity at NLO, though this requires handling two off-equilibrium distributions in the same process.
