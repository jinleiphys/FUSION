# Tetraneutron resonance and its isospin analogues

> We discuss isospin analogues of the tetraneutron resonance and their possible manifestations in nuclear structure and reactions.

## Key claim

The tetraneutron resonance likely exists, but its theoretical prediction depends on the specific internucleon interaction used; modern chiral effective field theory (χEFT) NN forces without softening fail to produce it, while interactions like JISP16 and Daejeon16 that mimic three-nucleon forces do support it, and the resonance's isospin analogues in A=4 nuclei could provide an independent test.

## Method

The authors review existing experimental and theoretical studies of the tetraneutron (⁴n) resonance, including their own prior no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations extended to the four-body continuum via the SS-HORSE method with democratic decay approximation. They then use charge independence of nuclear forces to predict the properties of the isospin T=2 analogues: excited 0⁺ states in ⁴H, ⁴He, ⁴Li, and the tetraproton (⁴Be). They also analyze existing data on narrow T=2 states in ⁸Be, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O to argue that these states contain a ⁴He*(0⁺,T=2) cluster component.

## Key numbers

- Tetraneutron resonance from 2022 experiment (Duer): energy E_r = 2.37 ± 0.38(stat) ± 0.44(syst) MeV, width Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22(stat) ± 0.30(syst) MeV.
- Tetraneutron resonance from 2016 experiment (Kisamori): E_r = 0.83 ± 0.65(stat) ± 1.25(syst) MeV, width Γ < 2.6 MeV.
- Tetraneutron resonance predicted with JISP16 interaction (Shirokov 2016): E_r = 0.844 MeV, Γ = 1.378 MeV.
- Tetraneutron resonance predicted with Idaho N3LO softened by V_low-k (Michel): E_r = 2.64 MeV, Γ = 2.38 MeV.
- Predicted 0⁺,T=2 resonance in ⁴H: excitation energy E_x ≈ 8 MeV, width Γ ≈ 1.8 MeV (based on ⁴H binding energy 5.6 ± 0.1 MeV).
- Predicted 0⁺,T=2 resonance in ⁴Li: excitation energy E_x ≈ 8 MeV, width Γ ≈ 2 MeV (based on ⁴Li binding energy 4.6 ± 0.2 MeV).
- Predicted tetraproton (⁴Be) 0⁺,T=2 resonance: energy ≈ 3.5 MeV, width ≈ 2.5 MeV.
- Predicted 0⁺,T=2 state in ⁴He: excitation energy ≈ 31 MeV.
- Observed ⁸Be*(0⁺,T=2) state: excitation energy 27.494 MeV, width Γ = 5.5 keV.
- Observed ¹²C*(0⁺,T=2) state: excitation energy 27.595 MeV, width Γ < 30 keV.
- Observed ¹⁶O*(0⁺,T=2) state: excitation energy 22.721 MeV, width Γ = 12.5 keV.
- Binding energy contribution of tetraneutron in ⁸He ground state: approximately 3.1 MeV.
- Binding energy contribution of ⁴He*(0⁺,T=2) in ⁸Be*(0⁺,T=2): approximately 0.8 MeV relative to ⁴He+p+p+n+n threshold.

## Context

This paper extends the long debate over the tetraneutron resonance, which began with the 2002 GANIL claim of a bound tetraneutron (Marqués 2002) and continued through the 2016 experimental detection (Kisamori 2016) and the 2022 confirmation (Duer 2022). The authors address skepticism from groups such as Sobotka and Piarulli (2023) and Lazauskas et al. (2023), who propose alternative reaction mechanisms. By predicting isospin analogues in ⁴H, ⁴He, ⁴Li, and ⁴Be, the work enables experimental searches that could confirm or refute the tetraneutron resonance independently of the ⁸He reaction channel. The interpretation of narrow T=2 states in ⁸Be, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O as containing a ⁴He*(0⁺,T=2) cluster provides indirect evidence and suggests that the tetraneutron's isospin partner may already have been observed in heavier nuclei.
