# Observation of near-scission "polar" and "equatorial" proton emission in heavy-ion induced fission

> Proton and $α$-particle energy spectra were measured in coincidence with fission fragments at different relative angles in $^{16}$O (96 MeV) + $^{232}$Th reaction. The multiplicity spectra were analyzed within the framework of a Moving Source Disentangling Analysis (MSDA) to determine contributions from different emission stages. The MSDA conclusively shows ``Near Scission Emission (NSE)" as an essential component in the multiplicity spectra. In contrast to NSE $α$ particles which emit mainly perpendicular (``equatorial emission"), the NSE protons are observed to be emitted perpendicular as well as parallel (``polar emission") to the fission axis with similar intensities ($\sim$20\% for each). Thus, around 40\% of total pre-scission protons are emitted near the scission stage, whereas the same fraction for $α$ particles is only around 10\%. The inevitable presence of ``polar" and ``equatorial" NSE protons in a heavy-ion induced fission has been observed for the first time. Present results open up a new avenue to study the heavy-ion induced fission dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for the first time in heavy-ion induced fission, near-scission proton emission is observed with both "polar" (parallel to the fission axis) and "equatorial" (perpendicular to the fission axis) components having similar intensities, around 20% each. This contrasts with near-scission alpha particles, which emit mainly perpendicular to the fission axis, and the result is conditional on the Moving Source Disentangling Analysis (MSDA) showing that including both polar and equatorial near-scission emission components improves the fit quality significantly.

## Method

Proton and alpha-particle energy spectra were measured in coincidence with fission fragments at seven different laboratory angles in the 16O (96 MeV) + 232Th reaction using CsI(Tl)-Si(PIN) detectors and two large-area Multi-Wire Proportional Counters (MWPCs) for fission fragment detection. The multiplicity spectra were analyzed using a Moving Source Disentangling Analysis (MSDA) that initially included three sources (compound nucleus pre-scission, and two fission fragment post-scission sources) and was then extended to include two near-scission emission (NSE) components: equatorial emission (EE) and polar emission (PE), each modeled with Gaussian energy and angular distributions in the rest frame.

## Context

This work extends studies of near-scission particle emission from low-energy fission (where alpha particles dominate as Long Range Alphas) to heavy-ion induced fission, where previous work by the same group (Y.K. Gupta et al., 2022) established the presence of near-scission alpha emission. The observation of both polar and equatorial proton emission with similar intensities contradicts the pattern seen in low-energy fission (Piasecki, 1982) where polar proton emission was about twenty times stronger than equatorial emission, and the ratio of alpha to proton equatorial yield drops from about 100 in low-energy fission to about 5.5 here. The results enable future studies of nuclear viscosity at the scission stage and provide a new probe for heavy-ion induced fission dynamics, with the angular distributions qualitatively agreeing with theoretical predictions by Wada (2015) for scission particle emission.
