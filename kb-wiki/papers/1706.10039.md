# Triviality of quantum electrodynamics revisited

> Quantum electrodynamics is considered to be a trivial theory. This is based on a number of evidences, both numerical and analytical. One of the strong indications for triviality of QED is the existence of the Landau pole for the running coupling. We show that by treating QED as the leading order approximation of an effective field theory and including the next-to-leading order corrections, the Landau pole is removed. Therefore, we conclude that the conjecture, that for reasons of self-consistency, QED needs to be trivial is a mere artefact of the leading order approximation to the corresponding effective field theory.

## Key claim

By treating quantum electrodynamics (QED) as the leading-order approximation of an effective field theory and including the next-to-leading-order Pauli term (a dimension-five operator), the Landau pole in the running coupling is removed, suggesting that the conjecture of QED triviality is an artefact of the leading-order approximation rather than a fundamental requirement.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general U(1) locally gauge-invariant effective Lagrangian for QED, including the standard QED terms plus the Pauli term (dimension-five operator) and higher-order operators. They calculated the photon self-energy at one-loop order for the running renormalized coupling and at two-loop order for the bare coupling as a function of a cutoff, using higher-derivative regularization to preserve gauge invariance and handle power-law divergences. The coefficients of the renormalization group equation for the bare coupling were computed using FeynCalc and Form, with the method of dimensional counting.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop coefficient \(A_1 = 2/(3\pi) \simeq 0.212\) for the renormalization group equation of the bare coupling.
- The two-loop coefficient \(A_3 = -7/(40\pi^3) \simeq -0.0056\) from the Pauli term contributions.
- The two-loop coefficient \(A_2 \simeq 0.0040\) from the Pauli term contributions.
- For standard QED (Pauli term absent), the Landau pole in the running renormalized coupling appears at \(q^2_L = -m^2 \exp[12\pi^2/e_r^2]\).
- For standard QED, the bare coupling has a pole at cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm P} = m \exp[1/(A_1 \alpha_0)]\).
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the electron in standard QED agrees with experiment, suggesting the Pauli term is suppressed by a scale larger than \(4\times 10^7\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the original analysis by Landau and collaborators (1950s) who found the Landau pole in QED and argued for triviality, as reviewed by Callaway (1988). It also builds on the modern effective field theory framework of Weinberg (1979), where the Standard Model is treated as a leading-order approximation. The result enables a resolution of the triviality problem without requiring QED to be non-interacting, and suggests that similar triviality issues in other renormalizable theories (such as \(\phi^4\) theory) may also be artefacts of the leading-order approximation in an effective field theory context.
