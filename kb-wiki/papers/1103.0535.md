# Brownian shape motion on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces: Nuclear fission-fragment mass distributions

> Although nuclear fission can be understood qualitatively as an evolution of the nuclear shape, a quantitative description has proven to be very elusive. In particular, until now, there exists no model with demonstrated predictive power for the fission fragment mass yields. Exploiting the expected strongly damped character of nuclear dynamics, we treat the nuclear shape evolution in analogy with Brownian motion and perform random walks on five-dimensional fission potential-energy surfaces which were calculated previously and are the most comprehensive available. Test applications give good reproduction of highly variable experimental mass yields. This novel general approach requires only a single new global parameter, namely the critical neck size at which the mass split is frozen in, and the results are remarkably insensitive to its specific value.

## Key claim

The paper claims that fission-fragment mass distributions can be calculated by treating nuclear shape evolution as Brownian motion, performing random walks on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces, with only a single new global parameter (the critical neck size at scission). The authors hedge that the results are "remarkably insensitive" to the specific value of this parameter, and that the approach is a "preliminary" treatment that may need future refinement.

## Method

The authors treat the nuclear shape evolution as a random walk on a five-dimensional lattice of shape parameters, using potential-energy surfaces calculated previously with the macroscopic-microscopic finite-range liquid-drop model. The random walk is simulated via the Metropolis procedure, where transition probabilities between lattice points are determined by the Boltzmann factor exp(-ΔV/T) with the nuclear temperature T obtained from the Fermi-gas formula. Scission is defined as the point when the neck radius c_neck decreases to a specified value c_0 = 2.5 fm, at which point the mass split is frozen in.

## Key numbers

- Critical neck radius for scission: c_0 = 2.5 fm
- Level-density parameter: a_A = A/(8 MeV)
- Bias potential strength used: V_0 = 15 MeV
- Excitation energies studied: 5-20 MeV
- For 234U(γ,f) data: photon energies 8-14 MeV; calculation made for E* = 11 MeV
- For (n_th,f) reactions: E* ≈ 6.5 MeV
- For 222Th: the calculation yields a symmetric mass distribution even though the saddle point shape has a mass ratio of 129:93

## Context

This work extends the compound nucleus concept from Bohr (1939) and the dissipative one-body friction mechanism from Blocki (1978) by implementing the stochastic component of one-body dissipation for mononuclear dynamics in the Smoluchowski limit. It contrasts with static scission models (Fong 1956, Wilkins 1976) that cannot account for dynamical effects, and with Langevin treatments (Karpov 2001, Nadtochy 2002, Ryabov 2008) that require higher excitations. The approach enables predictive calculations of fission-fragment mass distributions for nuclei across the chart, including those of astrophysical interest for r-process fission recycling (Seeger, Breun 2007).
