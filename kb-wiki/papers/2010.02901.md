# Rapidly rotating $Δ$-resonance-admixed hypernuclear compact stars

> We use a set of hadronic equations of state derived from covariant density functional theory to study the impact of their high-density behavior on the properties of rapidly rotating $Δ$-resonance-admixed hyperonic compact stars. In particular, we explore systematically the effects of variations of the bulk energy isoscalar skewness, $Q_{\mathrm{sat}}$, and the symmetry energy slope, $L_{\mathrm{sym}}$, on the masses of rapidly rotating compact stars. With models for equation of state satisfying all the modern astrophysical constraints, excessively large gravitational masses of around $2.5 \, M_{\odot}$ are only obtained under three conditions: (a) strongly attractive $Δ$-resonance potential in nuclear matter, (b) maximally fast (Keplerian) rotation, and (c) parameter ranges $Q_{\mathrm{sat}}\gtrsim500$ MeV and $L_{\mathrm{sym}}\lesssim50$ MeV. These values of $Q_{\mathrm{sat}}$ and $L_{\mathrm{sym}}$ have a rather small overlap with a large sample (total of about 260) parametrizations of covariant nucleonic density functionals. The extreme nature of requirements (a)-(c) reinforces the theoretical expectation that the secondary object involved in the GW190814 event is likely to be a low-mass black hole rather than a supramassive neutron star.

# Rapidly rotating Δ-resonance-admixed hypernuclear compact stars

## Key claim
Excessively large gravitational masses of around 2.5 M⊙ in Δ-resonance-admixed hyperonic compact stars are only obtained under three simultaneous conditions: (a) strongly attractive Δ-resonance potential in nuclear matter, (b) maximally fast (Keplerian) rotation, and (c) parameter ranges Qsat ≳ 500 MeV and Lsym ≲ 50 MeV. The extreme nature of these requirements reinforces the theoretical expectation that the secondary object in the GW190814 event is likely a low-mass black hole rather than a supramassive neutron star.

## Method
The authors use covariant density functional theory with density-dependent meson-baryon couplings to generate hadronic equations of state for nucleonic matter and for matter containing the full baryonic octet (nucleons and hyperons) plus Δ-resonances. They systematically vary the isoscalar skewness Qsat and the symmetry energy slope Lsym via Taylor expansions of the bulk and symmetry energies around saturation density, while keeping lower-order parameters fixed to the DD-ME2 functional. Stellar configurations are computed for both static (Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff) and maximally rotating (Keplerian) sequences using the RNS code, and the Δ-resonance potential in symmetric nuclear matter is varied over the range VΔ/VN = 1, 4/3, and 5/3.

## Key numbers
- Maximum gravitational masses of Keplerian Δ-admixed hyperonic sequences reach 2.4 M⊙ ≲ Mmax ≲ 2.7 M⊙, depending on Qsat, Lsym, and Δ-potential depth.
- For static Δ-admixed hyperonic stars, the maximum possible mass is Mmax ≈ 2.2 M⊙, requiring Qsat ≳ 300 MeV.
- Purely nucleonic static stars reach M ∼ 2.5 M⊙ only for Qsat ≳ 600 MeV, but these models give tidal deformabilities Λ1.4 ≳ 700, in tension with the GW170817 upper bound of Λ1.4 = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ (90% credibility).
- For Δ-admixed models with VΔ/VN = 5/3, all choices of Qsat and Lsym are consistent with the Λ1.4 constraint; for VΔ/VN = 1, only Qsat = 300 and 600 MeV with Lsym = 30 MeV are allowed.
- The smallest Qsat reaching 2.5 M⊙ in Keplerian Δ-admixed models is Qsat ≈ 300 MeV for Lsym = 30 MeV, and Qsat ≈ 900 MeV for Lsym = 70 MeV.
- The analysis uses about 260 parametrizations of covariant nucleonic density functionals for comparison.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Sedrakian 2020) on hyperonization in dense matter using the DD-ME2 functional, now adding Δ-resonance admixture and systematic variation of high-density nuclear parameters. It builds on earlier covariant density functional calculations of hypernuclear and Δ-admixed matter (Li 2018b, Li 2019a, Ribes 2019) and addresses the nature of the secondary object in the GW190814 event, which was also studied for rapidly rotating nucleonic stars (Most 2020, Zhang 2020, Tsokaros 2020) and with constant-speed-of-sound or piecewise polytropic equations of state (Tews 2020). The results enable a direct comparison between the parameter ranges required for a neutron-star interpretation of GW190814 and the values of Qsat and Lsym realized in large samples of non-relativistic and relativistic density functionals (Dutra 2012, Dutra 2014).
