# Mott-hadron resonance gas and lattice QCD thermodynamics

> We present an effective model for the generic behaviour of hadron masses and phase shifts at finite temperature which shares basic features with recent developments within the PNJL model for correlations in quark matter. On this basis we obtain the transition between a hadron resonance gas phase and the quark gluon plasma in the spirit of the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach where the Mott dissociation of hadrons is encoded in the hadronic phase shifts. Here we restrict ourselves to low-lying hadronic channels and perform a discussion of recent lattice QCD thermodynamics results from this perspective. We find agreement in the asymptotic regions while for the description of the transition itself the inclusion of further hadronic channels as well as a selfconsistent determination of the continuum thresholds is required.

## Key claim
The authors present an effective model for hadron masses and phase shifts at finite temperature that reproduces the asymptotic low-temperature hadron gas and high-temperature quark-gluon plasma limits of lattice QCD thermodynamics, but they explicitly state that the description of the transition region itself requires the inclusion of further hadronic channels and a self-consistent determination of continuum thresholds.

## Method
The model combines a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) mean-field treatment of quark and gluon thermodynamics with a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for hadronic correlations. Temperature-dependent phase shifts are constructed for each hadronic channel, encoding the Mott dissociation of hadrons as bound states transform into resonances in the scattering continuum when their masses reach the two- or three-quark threshold. The total pressure is the sum of partial pressures from mesons and baryons, the PNJL quark-gluon pressure, and a perturbative correction from running coupling interactions.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum light quark mass: m(0) = 394.7 MeV; strange quark mass: m_s(0) = 604.745 MeV (PNJL parameters: Lambda = 600 MeV, G_s Lambda^2 = 2.403, m_0 = 5.4285 MeV, m_s = 136.459 MeV).
- Mott dissociation temperatures: T_Mott = 303 MeV (pion), 338 MeV (kaon), 196 MeV (rho), 252 MeV (nucleon).
- The constant slope for the linear rise of resonance mass above Mott temperature is a = 2.5.
- The running coupling uses alpha(T) = 12 pi / (11 N_c - 2 N_f) * (1/ln(r^2/c^2) - c^2/(r^2 - c^2)) with r = 3.2 T and c = 350 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for pion and sigma meson dissociation in the NJL model (Hufner 1994, Blaschke 2013) to the PNJL model case (Wergieluk 2012, Dubinin 2013, Blaschke 2014). It builds on earlier attempts to describe hadron dissociation via temperature-dependent spectral widths (Blaschke 2003, 2005; Turko 2011, 2014) and aims to provide a microscopic foundation for the Mott-hadron resonance gas. The model enables future inclusion of the full hadron spectrum and self-consistent back-reaction of the hadron gas on the chiral condensate, which should lower the onset of chiral restoration toward the lattice QCD crossover temperature.
