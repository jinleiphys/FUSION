# Convective stability of global thermodynamic equilibrium

> We apply the convection stability criterion to a fluid in global thermodynamic equilibrium with a rigid rotation or with a constant acceleration along the streamlines. Different equations of state describing strongly interacting matter are considered and for each of them the analysed system is found to be stable with respect to convection. This finding brings new evidence for physical relevance of non-static global equilibrium states. Our results can be directly used for other similar media to check their convective stability.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fluids in global thermodynamic equilibrium with rigid rotation or constant acceleration are convectively stable for several equations of state of strongly interacting matter, supporting the physical relevance of non-static equilibrium states. The stability is determined by a single thermodynamic coefficient \(\kappa_{\rm conv}\) that is positive for all tested cases, though the authors note that a general proof of this positivity is difficult to deliver.

## Method

The authors apply the convective stability criterion \(\Omega^2 = -\frac{1}{w} \left( \frac{\partial w}{\partial \sigma} \right)_P a^\mu \partial_\mu \sigma > 0\) (where \(w\) is enthalpy density, \(\sigma\) entropy per baryon, and \(a^\mu\) the four-acceleration) to two classes of global thermodynamic equilibrium: rigid rotation around the \(z\)-axis and constant acceleration along streamlines. For each case, they reduce the stability condition to a single thermodynamic coefficient \(\kappa_{\rm conv}(T,\mu)\) that depends only on the equation of state, and evaluate it for five different models of strongly interacting matter: a massive quark model with \(m=150\) MeV, a running-coupling QGP model with \(\Lambda=150\) MeV, an ideal hadron gas from the SHARE code, and a three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory QCD equation of state.

## Key numbers

* For the massive quark model (Eq. P_m with \(m=150\) MeV) and the running-coupling QGP model (Eq. P_Lambda with \(\Lambda=150\) MeV), \(\kappa_{\rm conv}\) values are in the range \(10^{-7}\) to \(10^{-6}\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for temperatures 0.6 GeV \(\leq T \leq\) 1 GeV and chemical potentials 0 \(\leq \mu \leq\) 0.5 GeV.
* For the classical particle equation of state (Eq. P_cl), \(\kappa_{\rm conv} = f(x)/T\) where \(x=m/T\), with \(f(x) \approx 2x/5\) for \(x\rightarrow\infty\) and \(f(x) \approx x^4/16\) for \(x\rightarrow 0\).
* The hadron gas equation of state is evaluated in the range 0 \(\leq T \leq\) 0.15 GeV and 0 \(\leq \mu \leq\) 0.45 GeV, with \(\kappa_{\rm conv}\) positive throughout.
* The hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory QCD equation of state yields \(\kappa_{\rm conv}\) values several orders of magnitude larger than the simplified models (Eqs. P_m and P_Lambda).

## Context

This work extends the convective stability analysis of Florkowski (1992) to global thermodynamic equilibrium states with rotation and acceleration, which have become relevant due to the spin polarization of \(\Lambda\)-hyperons observed in heavy-ion collisions (STAR collaboration, 2017; Becattini, 2016, 2017) and connections to the Unruh effect (Unruh, 1976). The results confirm that non-static global equilibria, which are exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation (Cercignani) and arise from the Zubarev statistical operator formalism (Becattini, 2012), are physically viable. The analysis enables future checks of convective stability for other media and supports the use of rotating and accelerating equilibrium states in heavy-ion phenomenology.
