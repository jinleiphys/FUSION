# Scaling behavior of anisotropic flow harmonics in the far from equilibrium regime

> We consider the development of anisotropic flow in an expanding system of particles undergoing very few rescatterings, using a kinetic-theoretical description with a nonlinear collision term. We derive the scaling behaviors of the harmonic coefficients $v_n$ with the initial-state eccentricities and the mean number of rescatterings, and argue that hexagonal flow $v_6$ should follow a nontrivial behavior, different from that of the lower harmonics. Our findings should be observable in experimental data for small systems.

# Scaling behavior of anisotropic flow harmonics in the far from equilibrium regime

## Key claim

The authors derive that in a system of particles undergoing very few rescatterings, the hexagonal flow coefficient $v_6$ receives contributions that scale with different powers of the mean number of rescatterings per particle: the term proportional to $\epsilon_3^2$ scales linearly with $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$, while the term proportional to $\epsilon_2^3$ scales quadratically with $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$. This nontrivial scaling behavior, which has not been noted before, means that the development of the $\epsilon_2^3$ contribution to $v_6$ requires more rescatterings than the $\epsilon_3^2$ contribution, and the authors argue this should be observable in experimental data for small systems.

## Method

The authors model a system of particles that undergo very few rescatterings using a kinetic theory framework with the Boltzmann equation for elastic two-to-two scatterings. They start from a factorized initial phase-space distribution with spatial eccentricities $\tilde{\epsilon}_n$ and no initial momentum anisotropy, then write the solution as a free-streaming term plus a small correction. The collision term is approximated using only the free-streaming distribution, and the anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ are obtained by integrating the collision term over time. The authors derive scaling relations for $v_2$ through $v_6$ by examining which products of eccentricity terms in the initial distribution contribute to each harmonic when multiplied through the collision integral.

## Key numbers

- $v_2 \sim \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{2,2}\epsilon_2$ and $v_3 \sim \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{3,3}\epsilon_3$: linear scaling with mean number of rescatterings and corresponding eccentricity
- $v_4 \sim \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{4,4}\epsilon_4 + \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{4,22}\epsilon_2^2$: linear in $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$ for both terms
- $v_5 \sim \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{5,5}\epsilon_5 + \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}\kappa_{5,23}\epsilon_2\epsilon_3$: linear in $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$ for both terms
- $v_6 \sim \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}(\kappa_{6,6}\epsilon_6 + \kappa_{6,33}\epsilon_3^2 + \kappa_{6,24}\epsilon_2\epsilon_4) + \bar{N}_{\rm resc.}^2\kappa_{6,222}\epsilon_2^3$: the $\epsilon_2^3$ term scales quadratically with $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$, while the other terms scale linearly
- $v_8 \sim {\cal O}(\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}^2)\epsilon_2\epsilon_3^2 + {\cal O}(\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}^3)\epsilon_2^4$: higher harmonics require higher powers of $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$ for contributions involving only $\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$

## Context

This work extends the "far from equilibrium" or "one-hit dynamics" approach to anisotropic flow generation, following earlier studies by Heiselberg (1998), Borghini (2010), and more recent works by Romatschke (2018) and Kurkela (2018). The scaling relations for $v_2$ through $v_5$ with eccentricities match those found in fluid-dynamical studies by Teaney (2012), Gardim (2011), and Niemi (2012), but the authors show these same relations emerge even far from equilibrium. The key new result is the different scaling of $v_6$ contributions with $\bar{N}_{\rm resc.}$, which could be tested in small collision systems like proton-lead or proton-proton where fluid dynamics is questionable. The findings also have implications for hybrid models that use a transport cascade before hydrodynamical evolution, as studied by Liu (2015), since the pre-hydrodynamization stage may imprint a deficit in the $\epsilon_2^3$ contribution to $v_6$.
