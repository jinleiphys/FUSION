# Interference between f0(980) and rho(770) resonances in B --> pion-pion-kaon decays

> We study the contribution of the strong interaction between the two pions in S- and P-waves to the weak B --> pion pion kaon decay amplitudes. The interference between these two waves is analyzed in the pion-pion effective mass range of the rho(770) and f0(980) resonances. We use a unitary pion-pion and antikaon-kaon coupled-channel model to describe the S-wave interactions and a Breit-Wigner function for the P-wave amplitude. The weak B-decay amplitudes, obtained from QCD factorization, are supplemented with charming penguin contributions in both waves. The four complex parameters of these long distance terms are determined by fitting the model to the BaBar and Belle data on B^{+,-,0} --> pi+ pi- K^{+,-,0} branching fractions, CP asymmetries, pion-pion effective mass and helicity-angle distributions. This set of data, and in particular the large direct CP-asymmetry for B^{+,-} --> rho0(770) K^{+,-} decays, is well reproduced. The interplay of charming penguin amplitudes and the interference of S- and P-waves describes rather successfully the experimental S and A values of the CP-violating asymmetry for both B0 --> f0(980) K0S and B0 --> rho0(770) K0S decays.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the interference between S-wave (f0(980)) and P-wave (rho(770)) resonances, combined with charming penguin contributions, can successfully reproduce the experimental branching fractions, CP asymmetries, and mass/angular distributions for B → ππK decays, including the large direct CP asymmetry in B± → ρ(770)0 K± decays of about 0.3. The success depends on introducing four complex charming penguin parameters (two for each wave) whose values are determined by fitting to BaBar and Belle data.

## Method

The authors construct weak B-decay amplitudes using QCD factorization, supplemented by charming penguin long-distance contributions parameterized by four complex parameters (S_u, S_t for S-wave; P_u, P_t for P-wave). The S-wave ππ and K̅K interactions are described by a unitary coupled-channel model incorporating the f0(980) and f0(600) resonances, while the P-wave ρ(770) amplitude uses a Breit-Wigner function. The model is fitted to 204 data points from Belle and BaBar, including ππ effective-mass distributions, helicity-angle distributions, branching fractions, and CP asymmetry parameters for both charged and neutral B decays.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction for B± → ρ(770)0 K±: (2.91 ± 0.10) × 10⁻⁶ (model) vs. (2.76 ± 0.45) × 10⁻⁶ (Belle) and (3.60 ± 0.71) × 10⁻⁶ (BaBar), integrated over m_ππ range (0.66, 0.90) GeV
- Direct CP asymmetry A_CP for B± → ρ(770)0 K±: 0.32 ± 0.03 (model) vs. 0.30 ± 0.14 (Belle) and 0.32 ± 0.16 (BaBar)
- Branching fraction for B± → f0(980) K±: (6.93 ± 0.16) × 10⁻⁶ (model) vs. (6.06 ± 1.08) × 10⁻⁶ (Belle) and (6.53 ± 0.85) × 10⁻⁶ (BaBar), integrated over m_ππ range (0.90, 1.06) GeV
- Mixing-induced CP asymmetry S for B0 → π⁺π⁻ K⁰_S in f0(980) region (0.86-1.10 GeV): -0.63 ± 0.05 (model) vs. -0.95 ± 0.29 (BaBar)
- Direct CP asymmetry A for B0 → π⁺π⁻ K⁰_S in f0(980) region (0.86-1.10 GeV): -0.11 ± 0.07 (model) vs. 0.24 ± 0.34 (BaBar)
- ρ(770) mass: m_ρ = 775.8 MeV; decay width: Γ_ρ = 150.3 MeV
- Charming penguin parameter magnitudes: |P_u| = 1.09 ± 0.21, |P_t| = 0.065 ± 0.002, |S_u| = 0.15 ± 0.10, |S_t| = 0.020 ± 0.002
- χ² values from fit: χ²_I/W_I = 9.8 (for 18 observables), χ²_II = 336.3 (for 204 differential distribution data points)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of El-Bennich et al. (2004) (Ref. [fkll]) which studied only B → f0(980)K decays, by adding the ρ(770) resonance contribution and analyzing S- and P-wave interference in B → ππK decays. The approach contrasts with that of Cheng, Chua and Yang (2005), who studied scalar mesons in B decays using QCD factorization without hadronic long-distance contributions or final-state interactions. The model enables improved interpretation of CP-violating parameters S and A in neutral B decays, which is relevant for searches for new physics in b → s transitions as discussed by Buchalla (2005) and Beneke (2005). The results also address the known discrepancy that QCD factorization alone underestimates the B → ρK branching ratio by a factor of two or more, as noted by Leitner, Guo and Thomas (2004) and Beneke and Neubert (2003).
