# Correlations in hypernuclear matter

> We investigate short range correlations in nuclear and hypernuclear matter. Self-energies due to short range correlations and their influence on the nucleon and $Λ$-hyperon spectral functions are described in an approach accounting for a realistic treatment of mean-field dynamics and a self-consistently derived quasi-particle interaction. Landau-Migdal theory is used to derived the short range interaction from a phenomenological Skyrme energy density functional, subtracting the long range pionic contributions to the nucleonic spectral functions. We discuss our results for different hyperon-baryon ratios to show the influence of strangeness on the correlations in hypernuclear matter.

# Correlations in hypernuclear matter

## Key claim

Short-range correlations in hypernuclear matter are dominated by nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon interactions, while hyperon-hyperon correlations are suppressed; the nucleon and Λ-hyperon subsystems are found to be almost decoupled, with strangeness influencing the correlations only at a very low level.

## Method

The authors extend a transport-theoretical approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Lehr 2001, Froemel 2003, Konrad 2005) to hypernuclear matter containing protons, neutrons, and Λ-hyperons. They compute dynamical self-energies from two-particle-one-hole and one-particle-two-hole polarization diagrams, using a constant short-range matrix element derived from Landau-Migdal theory applied to a Skyrme energy density functional (SLy230a for nucleons, Ly-IV and SLL1 for hyperon interactions), with the long-range pionic contributions subtracted from the nucleonic spectral functions. The calculation is iterated until the spectral functions converge, corresponding to a summation of the sunset diagram to all orders.

## Key numbers

- Total baryon density fixed to nuclear matter density ρ_B = 0.16 fm⁻³ for all calculations.
- On-shell widths for Λ-hyperons are much smaller than for nucleons, increasing by only a few MeV when the strangeness fraction is raised from Y_Λ = 0.1 to Y_Λ = 0.5.
- Spectral functions are shown at momentum k = 312.5 MeV/c, slightly above the Fermi momentum.
- Strangeness fractions compared: Y_Λ = 0.1 (low) and Y_Λ = 0.5 (high), where Y_Λ = ρ_Λ / ρ_B.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of short-range correlations in pure nuclear matter (Konrad 2005, Lehr 2001, Froemel 2003) into the strangeness sector, representing the first systematic investigation of dynamical correlations in hypernuclear matter. The results agree with the sharp spectral structures observed in hypernuclear spectra (Hotchi 2001) and support the conclusion that hypernuclear matter can be treated as nearly decoupled nucleon and Λ-hyperon subsystems. The approach enables future studies of correlation effects in neutron star matter and other astrophysical environments containing strangeness.
