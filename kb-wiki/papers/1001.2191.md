# Quark-hadron mixed phases in protoneutron stars

> We consider the possible formation of the quark hadron mixed phase in protoneutron stars. We discuss two cases: the first one, corresponding to a vanishingly small value of the surface tension of quark matter, is the well known mixed phase in which the global electric charge neutrality condition is imposed. In turn, this produces a non-constant pressure mixed phase. In the second case, corresponding to very large values of the surface tension, the charge neutrality condition holds only locally. However, the existence in protoneutron star matter of an additional globally conserved charge, the lepton number, allows for a new type of non-constant pressure mixed phase. We discuss the properties of the new mixed phase and the possible effects of its formation during the evolution of protoneutron stars.

# Quark-hadron mixed phases in protoneutron stars

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in protoneutron stars, where lepton number is conserved due to neutrino trapping, a new type of quark-hadron mixed phase (MP2) can exist even when the surface tension is very large and local charge neutrality is enforced, contrary to the standard Maxwell construction which would forbid a mixed phase under those conditions. This mixed phase is only present during the neutrino-trapped stage and disappears as the star deleptonizes.

## Method

The authors model protoneutron star matter at fixed lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4 and entropy per baryon S/N_B = 1, using the relativistic mean field model with TM1 parameterization for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model (bag constant B^{1/4} = 165 MeV, strange quark mass 100 MeV) for quark matter. They solve two sets of Gibbs conditions: one for vanishing surface tension (MP1) where electric charge is conserved globally, and one for large surface tension (MP2) where electric charge is conserved locally but lepton number is conserved globally, with the lepton chemical potential continuous across the phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- Lepton fraction fixed at Y_L = (n_e + n_nu)/n_B = 0.4
- Entropy per baryon fixed at S/N_B = 1
- Bag constant set to B^{1/4} = 165 MeV
- Strange quark mass set to 100 MeV
- For a star with baryonic mass M_B = 1.7 M_sun in the MP2 case, a 2 km layer of mixed phase is present in the protoneutron star stage, which disappears in the cold configuration
- Interface charge separation layer size is of order the Debye screening length, approximately 10 fm

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs construction for mixed phases originally developed by Glendenning (1992) for cold neutron star matter, and addresses the finding by Voskresensky (2002) and Endo (2005) that large surface tension values effectively reduce the mixed phase to a constant-pressure Maxwell construction in cold matter. The key new ingredient is the additional globally conserved lepton number in protoneutron stars, which allows a non-constant pressure mixed phase even with local charge neutrality. The paper enables future studies of neutrino transport in this new mixed phase, which could affect the neutrino signal from supernovae, and suggests that motion of mixed-phase structures might produce gravitational waves.
