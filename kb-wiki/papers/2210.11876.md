# Energy dependence of light hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions from a coalescence and statistical-thermal model perspective

> A comparison of light hypernuclei production, from UrQMD+coalescence and the thermal model, in heavy ion collisions over a wide range of beam energies and system sizes is presented. We find that both approaches provide generally similar results, with differences in specific details. Especially the ratios of hypertriton to $Λ$ are affected by both the source radius $Δr$ of the coalescence procedure as well as canonical effects. On the other hand, the double ratio $S_3$ is almost independent of canonical effects, which is in contrast to coalescence. Thus, both the beam energy dependence and centrality dependence of $S_3$ can be used to constrain the hypertriton source radius. To do so the currently available data is not yet sufficient. Elliptic flow is shown to be unaffected by the source size of the nuclei and an almost perfect mass scaling of the elliptic flow is observed. Our predictions further suggest that the existence of the H-dibaryon ($ΛΛ$) seems ruled out by ALICE data.

## Key claim

The UrQMD+coalescence and thermal model approaches give generally similar predictions for light hypernuclei production across a wide range of beam energies and system sizes, but the double ratio S3 shows a qualitative difference between the two approaches: it is almost independent of canonical effects in the thermal model while strongly dependent on the coalescence source radius in the coalescence model, meaning that the beam energy and centrality dependence of S3 can be used to constrain the hypertriton source radius, though currently available data is not yet sufficient for this purpose.

## Method

The authors compared two theoretical approaches for light hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions: (1) a coalescence model (box-coalescence) applied to freeze-out distributions from the UrQMD transport model in both cascade and hybrid (hydrodynamic) mode, and (2) a canonical statistical-thermal model implemented in the Thermal-FIST package. The coalescence model uses step-function probability densities with parameters for maximum relative distance Δr and relative momentum Δp between constituents, with two parameter sets for hypertriton: Set I with Δr = 9.5 fm (large wave function) and Set II with Δr = 4.3 fm (triton-like size). The thermal model incorporates exact local strangeness conservation through a canonical ensemble with correlation radius Rc = 2.4 fm, and uses a chemical freeze-out curve parameterized as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton Λ separation energy: BΛ = 0.162 ± 0.044 MeV
- Coalescence parameters for NN pairs: Δrmax = 3.575 fm, Δpmax = 0.285 GeV
- Coalescence parameters for ΛΛ and ΞN pairs: Δrmax = 9.5 fm, Δpmax = 0.135 GeV
- Coalescence parameters for NNN (triton): Δrmax = 4.3 fm, Δpmax = 0.33 GeV
- Canonical correlation radius for strangeness: Rc = 2.4 fm (Vc = 4π/3 Rc^3)
- Thermal model temperature for LHC centrality studies: T = 155 MeV
- Canonical correlation volume for centrality studies: Vc = 1.6 dV/dy
- Upper limit on H-dibaryon from ALICE: 2 × 10^{-4} at 99% CL
- Iso-energy-density hypersurface for hybrid model: ε = 3ε0, where ε0 ≈ 145 MeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light nuclei production using coalescence (Sombun 2018, Bleicher 2019, Hillmann 2021) and thermal models (Andronic 2010, Vovchenko 2018) to hypernuclei across a broad energy range from √sNN = 2 GeV to 5 TeV. The paper addresses the hypertriton puzzle (Donigus 2020) and the suggestion by Sun (2018) that the weakly bound structure of hypertriton would lead to observable consequences in system size dependence. The results enable future experimental measurements at STAR and ALICE to constrain the hypertriton source radius through the S3 double ratio, and suggest that the H-dibaryon (ΛΛ) existence is ruled out by ALICE data.
