# Multiple Collisions and Induced Gluon Bremsstrahlung in QCD

> Induced soft gluon bremsstrahlung associated with multiple collisions is calculated via perturbative QCD. We derive the non-abelian analog of the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect that suppresses induced soft radiation with formation times exceeding the mean free path, $λ$. The dependence of the suppression effect on the $SU(N)$ representation of the jet parton as well as the kinematic variables is expressed through a radiation formation factor. Unlike in QED, the finite contribution from the small $x$ regime in QCD leads to an approximately constant radiative energy loss per unit length, $dE/dz\propto μ^2$, in the high energy limit that is sensitive to the infrared screening scale, $μ$, of the medium. As a function of the dimensionless parameter $ζ=λμ^2/E$, we show furthermore how the energy dependence of $dE/dz$ evolves from the above constant for $ζ\ll 1$ to the more familiar (Bethe-Heitler) linear dependence for $ζ\gg 1$.

## Key claim

The authors derive the non-abelian analog of the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect in QCD, showing that induced soft gluon bremsstrahlung from multiple collisions is suppressed when formation times exceed the mean free path, but unlike in QED, the finite contribution from the small x regime leads to an approximately constant radiative energy loss per unit length in the high energy limit, rather than a linearly energy-dependent loss.

## Method

The authors calculate induced soft gluon bremsstrahlung via perturbative QCD for a high energy parton passing through a random color field produced by a color neutral ensemble of static partons with Debye screened potentials. They compute the multiple elastic scattering amplitudes in the eikonal (straight line) limit for small momentum transfers, then derive the soft gluon radiation amplitudes from internal jet lines and external lines, summing them to obtain an effective color current. The radiation formation factor controlling the suppression of induced radiation is introduced, and the radiative energy loss per unit length is estimated in the kinematic region where x is much less than 1 and transverse momentum k_perp is much less than the infrared screening scale mu.

## Key numbers

- The radiative energy loss per unit length in the high energy limit is approximately constant: dE/dz proportional to mu^2, where mu is the infrared screening scale of the medium.
- The dimensionless parameter controlling the energy dependence is zeta = lambda * mu^2 / E, where lambda is the mean free path and E is the jet energy.
- For zeta much less than 1, dE/dz is approximately constant; for zeta much greater than 1, dE/dz exhibits the Bethe-Heitler linear dependence on energy.
- The condition for the approximate factorization of the radiation amplitude breaks down for large numbers of collisions m when x is much less than 1/sqrt(m) * k_perp/mu, which is much less than 1/sqrt(m).
- For SU(3), the color factors C_i for qq, qg, and gg scattering are 4/9, 1, and 9/4 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect from QED to non-abelian QCD, addressing the need for a detailed study of destructive interference in multiple collision theory that takes into account essential non-abelian features. Prior estimates for dE/dz varied widely, ranging from energy independent (Brodsky and collaborators), logarithmic energy dependent (Highpt and collaborators), to linearly energy dependent (Gavin, Ryskin, and collaborators). The results enable comparison with phenomenological string models (Plumer, Lund, Hijing) for high energy eA, pA, and AA reactions, and are relevant for developing QCD transport theory and parton cascade models.
