# Effect of energy conservation on in-medium $\mbox{NN}\to \mbox{N}Δ$ cross section in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> In this paper, the in-medium $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ cross section is calculated in the framework of the one-boson exchange model by including the isovector mesons, i.e. $δ$ and $ρ$ mesons. Due to the isospin exchange in the $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ process, the vector self-energies of the outgoing particles are modified relative to the incoming particles in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, and it leads to the effective energies of the incoming $NN$ pair being different from the outgoing $NΔ$ pair. This effect is investigated in the calculation of the in-medium $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ cross section. With the corrected energy conservation, the cross sections of the $Δ^{++}$ and $Δ^+$ channels are suppressed, and the cross sections of the $Δ^0$ and $Δ^-$ channels are enhanced relative to the results obtained without properly considering the potential energy changes. Our results further confirm the dependence of medium correction factor, $R=σ_{ NN\rightarrow NΔ}^*/σ_{NN\rightarrow NΔ}^{\text{free}}$, on the charge state of $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ especially around the threshold energy, but the isospin splitting of medium correction factor $R$ becomes weak at high beam energies.

## Key claim

The in-medium $NN\rightarrow N\Delta$ cross section in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter depends on the charge state of the reaction, with the medium correction factor $R$ showing isospin splitting near threshold energy that weakens at high beam energies, but only when the correct energy conservation using canonical momenta (rather than kinetic momenta) is applied to account for the different vector self-energies of incoming and outgoing particles.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium $NN\rightarrow N\Delta$ cross section using the one-boson exchange model with a relativistic Lagrangian including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\delta$, and $\pi$ mesons, employing the NL$\rho\delta$ parameter set. They imposed energy-momentum conservation using canonical momenta ($\delta^4(p_1+p_2-p_3-p_4)$) rather than kinetic momenta, which accounts for the difference in vector self-energies between incoming and outgoing particles in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The cross section was computed by integrating over the $\Delta$ mass distribution with a spectral function that includes the proper decay width in asymmetric matter.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density $\rho_0$ with isospin asymmetry $I=0.2$, the difference in vector self-energies $\Delta\Sigma^0$ between initial and final states is $-39.8$ MeV for $pp\rightarrow n\Delta^{++}$ and $+39.8$ MeV for $nn\rightarrow p\Delta^{-}$
- The Dirac effective mass ratio $m_i^*/m_i$ is 0.75 for nucleons and 0.81 for $\Delta$ baryons at saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter
- The coupling constants used include $g_{\rho N\Delta}=4.9183$ (derived from the static quark model) and $g_{\pi N\Delta}=2.202$
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{\Delta}=0.41$ GeV for both $\rho N\Delta$ and $\pi N\Delta$ form factors, determined by fitting free-space $NN\rightarrow N\Delta$ cross section data from $\sqrt{s}=2.0$ to 5.0 GeV
- The $\Delta$ decay width parameters are $\Gamma_0=0.118$ GeV and $\eta=0.2$ GeV/$c$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Larionov (2003) by including isovector $\delta$ and $\rho$ mesons to describe isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, which was not considered in the symmetric case. It follows the approach of Ferini (2005) for threshold effects and Song (2015) for energy conservation in transport models, but applies the corrected energy conservation directly to the in-medium cross section calculation. The results enable more accurate treatment of $\Delta$ production in transport models for neutron-rich heavy ion collisions, particularly for interpreting $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio data from the FOPI Collaboration, where different models (IBUU04, ImIQMD, RVUU, TuQMD, pBUU) have reached conflicting conclusions about the symmetry energy at suprasaturation density.
