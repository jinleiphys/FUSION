# Pairing in two-dimensional Fermi gases with a coordinate-space potential

> In this work we theoretically study pairing in two-dimensional Fermi gases, a system which is experimentally accessible using cold atoms. We start by deriving the mean-field pairing gap equation for a coordinate-space potential with a finite interaction range, and proceed to solve this numerically. We find that for sufficiently short effective ranges the answer is identical to the zero-range one. We then use Diffusion Monte Carlo to evaluate the total energy for many distinct particle numbers; we employ several variational parameters to produce a good ground-state energy and then use these results to extract the pairing gap across a number of interaction strengths in the strongly interacting two-dimensional crossover. Extracting the gap via the odd-even energy staggering, our microscopic results can be used as benchmarks for other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The authors find that for sufficiently short effective ranges (specifically \(k_F r_e \approx 0.006\)), the mean-field pairing gap in two-dimensional Fermi gases is identical to the zero-range analytical result, and that their Diffusion Monte Carlo results for the pairing gap show a consistent suppression relative to mean-field BCS theory throughout the strongly interacting crossover, unlike earlier finite-size-uncorrected calculations which had shown enhancement on the BCS side.

## Method

The authors first derive the two-dimensional BCS gap equation for a finite-range coordinate-space potential (modified Poschl-Teller form) and solve it numerically via fixed-point iteration alongside the constant-density equation. They then perform Variational Monte Carlo using a Jastrow-BCS trial wave function with 10 variational parameters, followed by fixed-node Diffusion Monte Carlo to compute ground-state energies for systems of \(N=10, 18, 26, 42, 50, 58\) particles and their \(N+1\) and \(N+2\) neighbors. The pairing gap is extracted via the odd-even energy staggering formula \(\Delta(N) = E(N+1) - \frac{1}{2}[E(N+2) + E(N)]\), with the thermodynamic limit approximated by averaging results from the two largest particle numbers (\(N=50\) and \(N=58\)).

## Key numbers

- Two-body binding energy for \(\eta=0.5\) is essentially unchanged for effective ranges up to \(k_F r_e \approx 0.1\); for \(\eta=-0.5\) the binding energy changes by a few percent over the same range.
- The effective range used in DMC calculations is \(k_F r_e \approx 0.006\), which is short enough to neglect finite-range effects.
- For \(\eta=0.5\) the fluctuation in energy per particle from largest to smallest system size is roughly 5%, compared to approximately 15% in the non-interacting gas.
- The largest particle number employed is \(N=58\); a calculation for \(N=58\) is roughly 11 times more demanding than one for \(N=26\).
- The pairing gap sharply increases as the BEC regime is approached and is reduced in the BCS regime; the results show suppression relative to mean-field BCS, qualitatively similar to the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov theory which gives suppression by a factor of \(e\).

## Context

This work extends earlier DMC studies of two-dimensional Fermi gases by Galea et al. (2016, 2017), which used only \(N=26\) particles and produced finite-size-uncorrected pairing gaps that became larger than mean-field BCS on the BCS side. The present results correct those finite-size effects by using larger particle numbers and systematically investigating the dependence on system size. The findings enable benchmarking for other theoretical approaches such as the imaginary-time Green's function method of Vitali et al. (2017) and the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov theory (Gorkov 1961, Petrov 2003), and are relevant to understanding superfluidity in neutron-star crusts and nuclear pasta phases.
