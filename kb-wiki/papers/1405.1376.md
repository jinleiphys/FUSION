# Polarization of Direct Photons from Gluon Anisotropy in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show how anisotropy in momentum of the gluon distribution in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions gives rise to polarization of direct photons produced via gluon-quark Compton scattering, as well as by quark-antiquark annihilation into a gluon-photon pair. We estimate the polarization asymmetry from the Compton process within a toy model where the polarized photons are produced from thermal gluons scattered by heavy-quark scattering centers moving with Bjorken boost-invariant flow, and find that it could be as large as 10%. We conclude that polarization measurements of directly produced photons can shed light on gluon pressure anisotropy in the early stages of collisions.

# Polarization of Direct Photons from Gluon Anisotropy in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The polarization of direct photons produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can serve as a probe of gluon momentum anisotropy in the pre-equilibrium stage of the quark-gluon plasma, with the polarization asymmetry potentially reaching 10% in a toy model where quarks are treated as heavy scattering centers co-moving with Bjorken boost-invariant flow. The actual magnitude depends on the fraction of photons produced by Compton scattering versus pair annihilation, the dynamical quark mass, and infrared screening effects that were not fully calculated.

## Method

The authors derived the invariant matrix elements for photon polarization from gluon-quark Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation into a gluon-photon pair, showing that the polarization-dependent terms have opposite signs between the two processes. They then constructed a toy model treating quarks as infinitely heavy scattering centers in Bjorken flow, using the Romatschke-Strickland phenomenological anisotropic gluon distribution parameterized by anisotropy parameters ξ (longitudinal) and ζ (transverse). The polarization asymmetry was computed by integrating the emission rate over space-time evolution from initial proper time τ₀ to final proper time τ_f, with temperature scaling as T = T₀(τ₀/τ)^(1/3).

## Key numbers

- Polarization asymmetry r from Compton scattering could be as large as 10% (stated in abstract, based on toy model estimate)
- For direct photons with q⊥ ~ 1-3 GeV, the total polarization asymmetry r_tot ~ 0.5 f_C ξ₀ (q⊥/2 GeV)(0.2 GeV/T₀), where f_C is the Compton fraction
- With ξ₀ ~ 1, r_tot ~ 10-40% (reduced by a factor ~2 when finite quark masses are considered)
- The ratio of unpolarized emission rates from Compton to pair annihilation ρ_C/P ranges from ~2.5 at Q₀ = 5 (f_C = 0.71) to ~1.4 at Q₀ = 10 (f_C = 0.58), for g² = 4
- The polarization anisotropy parameter λ is related to the Romatschke-Strickland parameter ξ by λ ≃ 2kξ/15T to leading order
- The QCD critical temperature T_c ~ 160 MeV is used as the final temperature cutoff
- The degeneracy factors are D_C = 320/3 for Compton scattering and D_A = 20 for pair annihilation in 2-flavor QCD

## Context

This work extends the study of direct photons as probes of the quark-gluon plasma, previously focused on unpolarized spectra and flow harmonics (Schenke, 2006), by introducing polarization as a new observable sensitive to gluon anisotropy. It contrasts with earlier mechanisms for photon polarization in heavy ion collisions: synchrotron radiation in color magnetic fields (Goloviznin, 1997), circular polarization from spin-orbit coupling in non-central collisions (Ipp, 2011), and the chiral magnetic effect (Yee, 2013), which gave polarization asymmetries at the 0.1-0.2% level. The present mechanism is argued to be simpler and more robust, operating even in central collisions. The results enable future experimental searches for direct photon polarization via conversion to e⁺e⁻ pairs, with optimal sensitivity at transverse momenta around 1 GeV where thermal photons dominate over perturbative QCD background.
