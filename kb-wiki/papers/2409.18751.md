# Updates on transversity extractions

> We give a brief overview on some of the latest results on the transversity function. We focus on some recent extractions from azimuthal asymmetries data measured in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering processes, and on the impact of transverse single-spin asymmetry data measured in polarised proton-proton collisions on these extractions.

# Updates on transversity extractions

## Key claim
The Soffer bound, when applied automatically in the parametrization of transversity functions, introduces an extra bias that can be removed by applying it a posteriori on Monte Carlo sets; this new approach reveals that the extracted $d_v$ transversity function may appear to violate the Soffer bound, but the violation has a statistical significance smaller than $1\sigma$ where data are available.

## Method
The transversity function $h_1^q(x)$ was extracted from azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data using a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) framework, with a parametrization that initially did not enforce the Soffer bound automatically. Instead, the Soffer bound was applied a posteriori on Monte Carlo sets generated for uncertainty estimation, allowing a test of compatibility with the bound. A Bayesian simultaneous reweighting was then applied to incorporate transverse single-spin asymmetry ($A_N$) data from polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC (BRAHMS and STAR Collaborations), using both the Generalised Parton Model (GPM) and its Colour Gauge Invariant extension (CGI-GPM).

## Key numbers
- The fit using the Soffer bound a posteriori and the fit without applying the bound at all both yield $\chi^2_{\rm dof} \approx 0.93$.
- For the extraction of Ref. Boglione (2024), the uncertainty reduction from $A_N$ data reweighting is up to 80-90% for $h_1^q$ at large $x$.
- Tensor charges at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ from the "using SB" extraction of Ref. DAlesio (2020): $\delta u = 0.42 \pm 0.09$, $\delta d = -0.15 \pm 0.11$, $g_T = 0.57 \pm 0.13$ (symmetric uncertainties at $2\sigma$).
- Tensor charges at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ from the unweighted extraction of Ref. Boglione (2024): $\delta u = 0.46^{+0.10}_{-0.09}$, $\delta d = -0.15^{+0.10}_{-0.07}$, $g_T = 0.60^{+0.13}_{-0.11}$ (asymmetric uncertainties at $2\sigma$).
- Tensor charges at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ from the CGI-GPM reweighted extraction of Ref. Boglione (2024): $\delta u = 0.47^{+0.08}_{-0.05}$, $\delta d = -0.19^{+0.07}_{-0.05}$, $g_T = 0.65^{+0.10}_{-0.07}$.

## Context
This work extends the transversity extractions of Anselmino (2015) and DAlesio (2020) by investigating the role of the Soffer bound as a bias in the parametrization, and by incorporating new HERMES data (2020) and $A_N$ data from RHIC. The results are compared with lattice QCD estimates of tensor charges from Alexandrou (2024) and with other phenomenological analyses. The finding that the Collins mechanism dominates $A_N$ in the (CGI-)GPM, after applying the Soffer bound a posteriori, establishes consistency with observations in the collinear twist-3 formalism of Gamberg (2022). Future COMPASS measurements (2023) are expected to further reduce uncertainties on the extracted transversity functions.
