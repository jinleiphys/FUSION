# Dilepton Enhancement by Thermal Pion Annihilation in the CERES Experiment

> We compare the recent CERES data on dielectron production in 200 GeV/u S+Au collisions with the theoretical predictions due to pion annihilation in a thermal hadronization and a string fragmentation scenario. Both models yield similar results for the dilepton mass spectrum. A satisfactory description of the experimental spectrum requires an in-medium reduction of the rho-mass and a freeze-out temperature of about 150 MeV in the thermal model. We emphasize and discuss the influence of experimental acceptance and resolution corrections.

## Key claim

A satisfactory description of the CERES dielectron spectrum requires an in-medium reduction of the rho-meson mass and a freeze-out temperature of about 150 MeV in the thermal model, but the authors note that the justification of such an assumption remains a theoretical challenge and that the simple model of a first order phase transition is an oversimplification.

## Method

The authors compared two models for dielectron production from pion annihilation in 200 GeV/u S+Au collisions: a thermal hadronization model based on the Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion scenario with quark, mixed, and hadronic phases, and a string fragmentation scenario using VENUS event generator results. They applied experimental acceptance cuts (pseudorapidity 2.1-2.65, transverse momentum above 0.2 GeV, opening angle above 35 mrad) and detector mass resolution corrections to the theoretical spectra before comparison with CERES data. The thermal model was used to test two simple parametrizations of medium-modified rho-meson properties: a temperature-dependent width and a dropping rho-mass in the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- CERES collaboration reports a five-fold enhancement of the dilepton spectrum in the mass range from two-pion threshold up to 1.5 GeV compared to expectations from independent nucleon-nucleon collisions
- Thermal model with temperatures (T_i, T_c, T_f) = (250, 160, 150) MeV and dropping rho-mass to m_rho(T_c) = m_rho/2 approximately 380 MeV at critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV gives a good fit to the spectrum shape
- With T_f = 120 MeV and free pion form factor, theoretical predictions exceed CERES data by about a factor four in the rho-mass region; with T_f = 150 MeV, the excess is about a factor three
- The acceptance function reduces the observed spectrum by nearly an order of magnitude (factor 0.136 for zero transverse momentum)
- Detector mass resolution is delta M/M = 0.08 for M less than 0.5 GeV, and delta M/M = 0.03 + 0.1 M/GeV for M greater than 0.5 GeV
- Mean charged particle multiplicity is approximately 125 with RMS value approximately 135
- For Pb+Pb collisions with dN_pi/dy approximately 700, the predicted scaling factor is about 3.5 relative to S+Au results

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model calculations of dilepton production (Feinberg 1976, McLerran and Toimela 1985, Kajantie and Ruuskanen 1989) and string fragmentation approaches (Schulze 1990) to the first quantitative comparison with CERES data (Agakichiev et al. 1995). The results are consistent with more sophisticated transport model analyses by Cassing, Ehehalt, and Ko (1995), Li, Ko, and Brown (1995), and Chanfray, Rapp, and Wambach (1996), and support the larger freeze-out temperatures of about 150-170 MeV deduced from hadron abundance fits by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, Wessels, and Xu (1996). The work enables future studies of medium modifications of hadron properties and predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS.
