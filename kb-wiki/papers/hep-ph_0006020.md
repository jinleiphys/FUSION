# Two Hagedorn temperatures: a larger one for mesons, a lower one for baryons

> We show that the Hagedorn temperature obtained from experimental spectra is larger for mesons than for baryons. Dual string models explain this behavior asymptotically.

# Two Hagedorn temperatures: a larger one for mesons, a lower one for baryons

## Key claim
The Hagedorn temperature extracted from experimental particle spectra is not universal for all hadrons: mesons yield a higher Hagedorn temperature than baryons. This difference is substantial and persists across flavors, contrary to the long-held assumption of a single universal Hagedorn temperature.

## Method
Cumulants of the experimental hadron spectrum (number of states with mass below a given value) were constructed from Particle Data Tables, using spin-isospin degeneracy factors. A Hagedorn-like fit of the form ρ_theor(m) = f(m) exp(m/T) with f(m) = A/(m² + (500 MeV)²)^(5/4) was applied separately to meson and baryon data via least-square fitting to log N_theor, extracting distinct Hagedorn temperatures for each class.

## Key numbers
- Meson Hagedorn temperature: T_meson = 195 MeV
- Baryon Hagedorn temperature: T_baryon = 141 MeV
- The ratio T_meson / T_baryon from the dual string model is √3 ≈ 1.732
- The linear behavior of log N extends from very low mass up to m ~ 1.8 GeV
- To make the meson line parallel to the baryon line would require aggregating approximately 500 additional meson states up to m = 1.8 GeV, compared to the present number of about 400

## Context
This work contradicts the long-standing assumption of a single universal Hagedorn temperature for all hadrons, dating back to Hagedorn's original hypothesis (Hagedorn, 1965; Hagedorn, 1994). The statistical bootstrap models (Hagedorn; SBM2) predict equal Hagedorn temperatures for mesons and baryons and cannot explain the observed difference. Dual string models (Jacob, 1974) naturally produce the effect, with the baryon's three independent vibrating strings yielding T_baryon = T_meson / √3 asymptotically. The result enables future work on multi-string exotic hadron configurations, where the Hagedorn temperature is predicted to scale inversely with the square root of the number of strings.
