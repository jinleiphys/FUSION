# Heavy flavor quenching and flow: the roles of initial condition, pre-equilibrium evolution and in-medium interaction

> Within an advanced Langevin-hydrodynamics framework coupled to a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence hadronization model, we study heavy flavor quenching and flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We investigate how the initial heavy quark spectrum, the energy loss and hadronization mechanisms of heavy quarks in medium, the evolution profile of pre-equilibrium stage, the flow of medium and the temperature dependence of heavy quark diffusion coefficient influence the suppression and elliptic flow of heavy mesons at RHIC and the LHC. Our result shows that different modeling of initial conditions, pre-equilibrium evolution and in-medium interaction can individually yield about 10-40% uncertainties in D meson suppression and flow at low transverse momentum. We also find that a proper combination of collisional versus radiative energy loss, coalescence versus fragmentation in hadronization, and the inclusion of medium flow are the most important factors for describing the suppression and elliptic flow of heavy mesons.

## Key claim

Different modeling choices for initial heavy quark spectra, pre-equilibrium evolution, and in-medium interactions can individually yield about 10-40% uncertainties in D meson suppression and flow at low transverse momentum, but a proper combination of collisional versus radiative energy loss, coalescence versus fragmentation in hadronization, and the inclusion of medium flow are the most important factors for describing the data.

## Method

The authors used a modified Langevin equation that simultaneously incorporates both collisional (drag and thermal random force) and radiative (medium-induced gluon emission via higher-twist formalism) energy loss for heavy quarks propagating through a hydrodynamic QGP medium. The heavy quark evolution was coupled to (2+1)-dimensional Vishnew and (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamic simulations, and hadronization was treated with a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model using Wigner functions for meson and baryon formation. The study systematically varied the initial heavy quark spectrum (FONLL vs. leading-order pQCD), the starting time and temperature profile of the pre-equilibrium stage, the relative contributions of collisional and radiative energy loss, the hadronization mechanism, the inclusion of medium flow, and the temperature dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers

- Different modeling of initial conditions, pre-equilibrium evolution and in-medium interaction can individually yield about 10-40% uncertainties in D meson suppression and flow at low transverse momentum.
- Using the FONLL spectrum leads to 13% smaller R_AA at RHIC and 25% smaller R_AA at the LHC at low p_T compared to using the LO spectrum (without nuclear shadowing).
- Nuclear shadowing suppresses the initial charm quark spectrum in AA collisions by 15% at RHIC and 27% at the LHC at low p_T (EPPS16 within FONLL).
- Using a later starting time (tau_0=1.2 fm) requires about 35% smaller diffusion coefficient D_s to reproduce the same R_AA as tau_0=0.6 fm, and yields 8% larger v_2 at RHIC and 24% larger v_2 at the LHC at low p_T.
- The free-streaming assumption for the pre-equilibrium stage yields about 39% larger v_2 at RHIC and 19% larger v_2 at the LHC than constant temperature and Bjorken evolution profiles at low p_T.
- The crossing point where collisional and radiative energy loss contribute equally to D meson R_AA is around 5 GeV at RHIC and around 7 GeV at the LHC.
- Coalescence dominates D meson yield up to p_T ~ 5 GeV, while fragmentation dominates above that.
- The effect of different temperature dependences of D_s on D meson v_2 is within 12% at the LHC at low p_T and negligible at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on systematic uncertainties in heavy flavor observables from various model components, such as those discussed by Rapp (2018), Cao (2018), Xu (2018), Andres (2019), and Katz (2019). The authors use a state-of-the-art Langevin-hydrodynamics framework with both collisional and radiative energy loss, coupled to a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence hadronization model that includes both s and p-wave charmed hadron states. The study enables a more precise understanding of which model ingredients are most critical for describing heavy meson suppression and flow at RHIC and the LHC, and provides guidance for future model-to-data comparisons to extract heavy quark transport coefficients.
