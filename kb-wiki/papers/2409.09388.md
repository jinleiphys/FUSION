# Pion-nucleon scattering with decuplet contribution in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the complete $T$ matrices with decuplet contributions for pion-nucleon scattering to order $\mathcal{O}(ε^3)$ in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. The baryon mass in the chiral limit $M_0$ and the low-energy constants are determined by fitting to phase shifts of $πN$, the experimental octet-baryon masses, and the value of $σ_{πN}$ simultaneously. By using these constants, we obtain the $KN$ $σ$ terms, $σ_{KN}^{(1)}=(375.07\pm33.02)$ MeV and $σ_{KN}^{(2)}=(275.32\pm32.24)$ MeV, with the errors being only statistical. An excellent description of the phase shifts is obtained for all partial waves. We also present results for scattering lengths and scattering volumes. In addition, the convergence of the approach is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate complete T-matrices for pion-nucleon scattering including decuplet-baryon contributions to order O(ε³) in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, claiming an excellent description of phase shifts for all partial waves. The hedge is that the convergence of the approach is still not fully established at this order, and the results for the P33 partial wave show the most prominent improvement, while the overall description is still slightly better in the fourth-order results without decuplet contributions from prior work.

## Method

The authors compute the complete T-matrices for elastic pion-nucleon scattering in heavy baryon SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, including explicit decuplet-baryon fields (Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω) to order O(ε³) in the small scale expansion. They determine the baryon mass in the chiral limit M₀ and the low-energy constants by simultaneously fitting to πN phase shifts from Roy-Steiner equations (1125-1220 MeV CMS total energy) and the WI08 Solution, the experimental octet-baryon masses (N, Σ, Ξ, Λ), and the σπN value. The amplitudes include tree diagrams and one-loop diagrams with decuplet contributions, using dimensional regularization for divergent loop integrals.

## Key numbers

- σKN(1) = (375.07 ± 33.02) MeV and σKN(2) = (275.32 ± 32.24) MeV, predicted from the Roy-Steiner fit with only statistical errors
- M₀ = (559.91 ± 32.56) MeV for the Roy-Steiner fit and M₀ = (714.11 ± 34.60) MeV for the WI08 fit
- χ²/d.o.f. = 0.98 for the Roy-Steiner fit and χ²/d.o.f. = 3.45 for the WI08 fit
- σπN = (52.08 ± 2.61) MeV from the Roy-Steiner fit, compared to the input value of (59.1 ± 3.5) MeV from Roy-Steiner equations
- S-wave scattering length a0+^(3/2) = (-0.119 ± 0.004) fm and a0+^(1/2) = (0.214 ± 0.003) fm from the Roy-Steiner fit
- P-wave scattering volume a1+^(3/2) = (0.853 ± 0.008) fm³ from the Roy-Steiner fit

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(3) HBChPT calculations of pion-nucleon scattering without decuplet contributions at third order (Huang 2020) and fourth order (Huang 2020) by explicitly including the Δ(1232) resonance and other decuplet baryons as dynamical degrees of freedom. It follows the small scale expansion framework established by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) for including the Δ resonance in baryon ChPT, and parallels the SU(2) analysis of Fettes and Meissner (2001) which showed improved convergence from including the Δ. The predicted KN sigma terms may be useful for dark matter direct detection experiments, and the σsN value of (37.99 ± 31.11) MeV from the Roy-Steiner fit is consistent with lattice QCD results from Lin (2011) of 43(8) MeV.
