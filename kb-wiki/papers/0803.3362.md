# Correlation functions for a di-neutron condensate in asymmetric nuclear matter

> Recent calculations with an effective isospin dependent contact interaction show the possibility of the crossover from superfluidity of neutron Cooper pairs in $^1S_0$ pairing channel to Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of di-neutron bound states in dilute nuclear matter. The density and spin correlation functions are calculated for a di-neutron condensate in asymmetric nuclear matter with the aim to find the possible features of the BCS-BEC crossover. It is shown that the zero-momentum transfer spin correlation function satisfies the sum rule at zero temperature. In symmetric nuclear matter, the density correlation function changes sign at low momentum transfer across the BCS-BEC transition and this feature can be considered as a signature of the crossover. At finite isospin asymmetry, this criterion gives too large value for the critical asymmetry $α_c^d\sim0.9$, at which the BEC state is quenched. Therefore, it can be trusted for the description of the density-driven BCS-BEC crossover of neutron pairs only at small isospin asymmetry. This result generalizes the conclusion of the study in Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 95}, 090402 (2005), where the change of sign of the density correlation function at low momentum transfer in two-component quantum fermionic atomic gas with the balanced populations of fermions of different species was considered as an unambiguous signature of the BCS-BEC transition.

## Key claim

In symmetric nuclear matter, the density correlation function changes sign at low momentum transfer across the BCS-BEC transition and can be considered a signature of the crossover, but this criterion gives too large a value for the critical isospin asymmetry (α_c^d ~ 0.9) at which the BEC state is quenched, so it can be trusted only at small isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The author solved the zero-temperature BCS gap equation for neutron \(^1S_0\) pairing self-consistently with the neutron number density equation, using an effective density-dependent contact interaction (screened-II interaction) with parameters fitted to reproduce G-matrix results from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential including medium polarization. The single-particle energies were taken from the SLy4 Skyrme interaction. The density correlation function \(D(q)\) and spin correlation function \(S(q)\) were then computed from the normal and anomalous distribution functions via Fourier transforms of the two-neutron correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The BEC state in symmetric nuclear matter exists in the neutron Fermi momentum range \(0.09\,\text{fm}^{-1} < k_{Fn} < 0.25\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) based on the sign of the effective neutron chemical potential.
- The BEC state is quenched at a critical isospin asymmetry \(\alpha_c \approx 0.26\) based on the chemical potential criterion.
- The density-correlation-function criterion gives a much larger critical asymmetry \(\alpha_c^d \sim 0.9\) for quenching of the BEC state.
- The zero-momentum transfer spin correlation function satisfies the sum rule \(S(q=0) = -1/4\) at zero temperature.
- The BEC state from the density correlation function criterion spans a wider range \(0.03\,\text{fm}^{-1} < k_{Fn} < 0.5\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) in symmetric nuclear matter.
- The di-neutron BEC is best formed at neutron densities \(\varrho_n/\varrho_0 \sim 10^{-3}\) (corresponding to \(k_{Fn} \sim 0.2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\)).
- The bare neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a_{nn} \approx -18.5\,\text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter from earlier analyses of di-neutron correlations using the regularized gap equation and effective interactions (Matsuo, 2006; Matsuo et al., 2007) and from studies of density correlation functions in ultracold atomic gases (Minguzzi et al., 2005). The paper generalizes the conclusion of Minguzzi et al. (2005), who considered the sign change of the density correlation function an unambiguous signature of the BCS-BEC transition in balanced fermionic atomic gases, by showing that in asymmetric nuclear matter this criterion fails at large isospin asymmetry. The results enable future work to identify which observables can reliably distinguish BCS and BEC regimes in low-density nuclear systems such as neutron star crusts or dilute nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions.
