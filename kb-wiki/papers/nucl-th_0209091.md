# Hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars

> Possibility of structured mixed phase at first order phase transitions is examined by taking into account of charge screening and surface effects. Hadron-quark phase transition in dense neutron star interiors is considered as a concrete example.

# Hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars

## Key claim
The structured mixed phase (droplets, rods, slabs) at the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars is prohibited for realistic values of surface tension when charge screening effects are properly included, contrary to earlier claims that a wide mixed-phase region is inevitable. The critical surface tension parameter above which the mixed phase becomes unstable is β₁c ≈ 0.6 when screening and correlation effects are both considered.

## Method
The authors use a density functional method within a Wigner-Seitz cell approximation, treating a quark droplet immersed in nuclear (n,p,e) matter with a sharp boundary. They solve the Poisson equation for the electric potential including Debye screening, expand the charge density to second order in the potential, and compute the effective energy per droplet volume as a function of scaled droplet radius ξ = R/λᵈᵉᵇʸᵉ_I for various values of the surface tension parameter β₁ and droplet concentration f.

## Key numbers
- Debye screening length for electrons: λₑ,ᴅ ≥ 13 fm; for quarks: λₙ,ᴅ ≈ 5 fm
- For typical parameters (μₑ ≈ 170 MeV, μₙ ≈ 1020 MeV, αₑ ≈ 0.4, mₛ ≈ 120–150 MeV), the energy scale parameter β₀ ≈ 1.6 mₚ⁴
- With surface tension σ ≈ 1.3 mₚ³, the surface tension parameter β₁ ≈ 0.7; with σ ≈ 10 MeV/fm² ≈ 0.14 mₚ³, β₁ ≈ 0.08
- Critical surface tension parameter for mixed phase stability: β₁c ≈ 0.6 (including screening and correlation effects); would be β₁c ≈ 0.03 if only electromagnetic and surface energies are considered

## Context
This work extends the earlier treatment by Glendenning (2000), who advocated Gibbs conditions for a structured mixed phase in neutron stars but disregarded finite-size effects, and by Heiselberg et al. (1993), who noted that large surface tension could make the mixed phase unfavorable. The authors show that charge screening, previously neglected, further suppresses the mixed phase for realistic surface tensions, and that non-spherical droplet geometries (rods and slabs) do not change this conclusion. The result enables a return to the Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star interiors, with the mixed phase replaced by a boundary layer between two separated phases.
