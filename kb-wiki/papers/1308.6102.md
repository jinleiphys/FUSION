# Possible Existence of Charmonium-Nucleus Bound States

> Possible existence of $(c\bar{c})-$nucleus bound states are examined. We adopt Gaussian potentials for the $η_{c}-N$ and $J/ψ-N$ interactions. The relations between the scattering lengths $a$ of $(c\bar{c})-N$ interactions and the binding energies of $η_{c}-NN$, $J/ψ-NN$ and $J/ψ-^{4}$He are given. The results show that scattering lengths $a \le -0.95$ fm are needed to make $η_{c}-NN$ and $J/ψ-NN$ bound states, while for $a \le -0.24$ fm there may exist a $J/ψ-^{4}$He bound state.

## Key claim

The authors find that charmonium-nucleus bound states could exist if the charmonium-nucleon scattering length is sufficiently attractive, but the required attraction is stronger than that indicated by recent lattice QCD calculations, making the existence of such bound states uncertain for light nuclei like the deuteron.

## Method

The authors model the $\eta_c-N$ and $J/\psi-N$ interactions using single-Gaussian potentials with a fixed range parameter $\mu = (1.0\ \text{fm})^{-2}$. They compute the scattering lengths for these two-body systems and then apply the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) to solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for $\eta_c-NN$ and $J/\psi-NN$ systems, using the Minnesota potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the $J/\psi-^4$He system, they treat it as a two-body problem using a folding potential constructed from the $J/\psi-N$ interaction and a Gaussian nucleon density distribution for $^4$He.

## Key numbers

* The critical scattering length to form a $J/\psi$-deuteron or $\eta_c$-deuteron bound state is $a \le -0.95$ fm.
* The critical scattering length to form a $J/\psi-^4$He bound state is $a \le -0.24$ fm.
* The lattice QCD spin-averaged scattering length for $J/\psi-N$ is $a^{J/\psi-N}_{\text{SAV}} \simeq -0.35$ fm with an error of around 0.1 fm (Kawanai 2010).
* The lattice QCD scattering length for $\eta_c-N$ is $a^{\eta_c-N} \simeq -0.25$ fm with an error of around 0.1 fm (Kawanai 2010).
* The critical potential depth for a $J/\psi-N$ bound state is $v_{\text{eff}} = -72.6$ MeV, while for a $J/\psi$-deuteron bound state the critical effective potential depth is $V_{\text{eff}} \le -33$ MeV.
* The reduced mass used is 713.7 MeV for $\eta_c-p$ and 720.1 MeV for $J/\psi-p$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Brodsky (1990), Wasson (1991), Belyaev (2006), and de Teramond (1998) who assumed specific Yukawa or Gaussian potential forms for the charmonium-nucleon interaction. The authors instead derive general relations between the charmonium-nucleon scattering length and the binding energy of charmonium-nucleus systems, making the results less dependent on the detailed potential shape. The recent lattice QCD results of Kawanai and Sasaki (2010) provide scattering lengths that are too small to bind charmonium to a deuteron, but the critical scattering length for $^4$He ($-0.24$ fm) is closer to the lattice values, suggesting that $J/\psi-^4$He bound states may be possible. This work enables future experimental searches for charmonium-nucleus bound states and provides a framework for extracting the spin-dependent $J/\psi-N$ interaction from nuclear binding data.
