# Heavy quark production at RHIC and LHC within a partonic transport model

> The production and space-time evolution of charm and bottom quarks in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC are investigated with the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings). Heavy quarks, produced in primary hard parton scatterings during nucleon-nucleon collisions, are sampled using the Monte Carlo event generator PYTHIA or the leading order mini-jet model in conjunction with the Glauber model, revealing a strong sensitivity on the parton distribution functions, scales, and heavy quark mass. In a comprehensive study exploring different charm masses, K factors, and possible initial gluon conditions, secondary production and the evolution of heavy quarks are examined within a fully dynamic BAMPS simulation for central heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Although charm production in the quark-gluon plasma can be neglected at RHIC, it is significant at LHC but very sensitive to the initial conditions and the charm mass. Bottom production in the quark-gluon plasma, however, is negligible both at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

Charm production in the quark-gluon plasma is significant at LHC but very sensitive to the initial conditions and the charm mass, while bottom production in the quark-gluon plasma is negligible at both RHIC and LHC; the abstract's claim that charm production can be neglected at RHIC is conditioned on the large chemical equilibration time scale of about 700 fm/c compared to the QGP lifetime.

## Method

The authors used the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate the 3+1 dimensional space-time evolution of gluons, charm, and bottom quarks in central heavy ion collisions. Initial heavy quark yields from primary hard parton scatterings were sampled using PYTHIA 6.4 or a leading order mini-jet model combined with the Glauber model, with parton distributions scaled from nucleon-nucleon to nucleus-nucleus collisions using binary collision scaling for hard processes and energy conservation for soft processes. The BAMPS simulation included pQCD processes: gluon-gluon elastic scattering, gluon splitting and merging (2↔3), gluon fusion to charm pairs, and elastic scattering of gluons with heavy quarks, using a constant strong coupling α_s = 0.3.

## Key numbers

- Chemical equilibration time for charm in a static medium at T₀ = 400 MeV (RHIC temperature): τ_eq ≈ 700 fm/c
- Chemical equilibration time for charm in a static medium at T₀ = 800 MeV (LHC temperature): τ_eq ≈ 30 fm/c
- Charm mass used: M_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom mass used: M_b = 4.8 GeV
- For central Au+Au at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): number of binary collisions N_bin ≈ 1000 (with shadowing), total produced particles ≈ 4600, of which ≈ 4400 are partons
- For central Pb+Pb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.5 TeV): number of binary collisions N_bin ≈ 1500 (with shadowing)
- In nucleon-nucleon collisions at RHIC: average 7.5 particles per collision, of which 5.4 are partons; partons carry only 30 GeV of the total 200 GeV energy
- In the box simulation at T₀ = 400 MeV, the gluon temperature drops to about 370 MeV due to charm mass creation; at T₀ = 800 MeV, it drops to about 720 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark production using rate equations and static medium approximations (Matsui 1985, Biro 1993, Levai 1994) by implementing a fully dynamic 3+1 dimensional parton cascade. The results contrast with earlier expectations that heavy quarks thermalize slowly due to the dead cone effect (Dokshitzer 2001, Zhang 2003), and provide a framework for interpreting experimental observations of heavy flavor energy loss and elliptic flow at RHIC (Abelev 2006, Adare 2006). The study enables future investigations of hidden charm and bottom production (J/ψ and Υ) within the same transport framework.
