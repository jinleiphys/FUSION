# Chiral Quark Dynamics in Dense Nuclear Matter

> We consider a new approach to the description of dense nuclear matter in the framework of chirally symmetric, quark-based hadron models. As previously in the Skyrme model, the dense environment is described in terms of hyperspherical cells of unit baryon number. The intrinsic curvature of these cells generates a new gauge interaction for the quark fields which mediates interactions with the ambient matter. We apply this approach to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, construct its curved-space quark propagator and solve the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion. We find a high-density phase transition to chiral restoration, discuss the density dependence of the chiral order parameter and of the pion properties, and compare with results of the conventional chemical-potential approach. The new approach can additionally describe baryon-density-free cavities in the dense medium.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that dense nuclear matter can be described using hyperspherical unit cells of unit baryon number, where the intrinsic curvature generates a new gauge interaction for quark fields that mediates interactions with the ambient matter; this approach predicts a high-density phase transition to chiral restoration, though the authors acknowledge that any unit-cell description of nuclear matter is "bound to be qualitative at best" except at very high densities such as in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors implement quark fields into a curved unit cell with the geometry of a three-dimensional hypersphere S³(L) × R, replacing the flat Minkowski metric and replacing gradients with gauge-covariant derivatives containing the spin connection. They apply this framework to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, construct the curved-space quark propagator in Hartree-Fock approximation using the Maurer-Cartan gauge, and solve the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion. The propagator is expressed as sums over geodesic paths on the compact hypersphere, with the Fermi sea of three valence quarks implemented by modifying the boundary conditions on the propagator integrals.

## Key numbers

- The quark energy spectrum on S³ × R is given by ω_n = √(k_n² + m²) with k_n = (2n+1)/(2L) for n ∈ {1,2,3,...}, and degeneracies D_n = 2n(n+1) per flavor and color
- The integration measure on the hypersphere is dμ(x) = L³ sin²μ sinθ dμ dθ dφ dt, with metric determinant g(x) = -L⁶ sin⁴μ sin²θ
- The spin connection in Maurer-Cartan gauge takes the form ω_a = (i/4L) ε_{abc} σ^{bc} for a,b,c ∈ {1,2,3} and ω₀ = 0
- The propagator amplitudes are expressed with prefactor α = 1/(4πL²) and involve sums over n from -∞ to +∞ of terms containing the two-dimensional propagator I(μ,t) and its derivatives

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical unit-cell approach, previously applied successfully in the Skyrme model by Manton and Ruback (1987) and Forkel (1992), to quark-based hadron models for the first time. The approach contrasts with the conventional chemical-potential method for dense matter in the NJL model studied by Bernard et al. (1987) and others, and the authors compare their results to those of that approach. The method enables consistent description of baryon structure modifications at high densities and can additionally describe baryon-density-free cavities in the dense medium, though the authors note that lattice simulations with chemical potential remain difficult due to the complex Euclidean action of QCD (Barbour, 1990).
