# Structure of exotic nuclei around double shell closures

> In this paper, we first give a brief review of the theoretical framework for microscopic shell-model calculations starting from the free nucleon-nucleon potential. In this context, we discuss the use of the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V-low-k in the derivation of the shell-model effective interaction and emphasize its practical value as an alternative to the Brueckner G-matrix method. Then, we present some results of our current study of exotic nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn, which have been obtained starting from the CD-Bonn potential renormalized by use of the V-low-k approach. Attention is focused on the nuclei 134Te, 134Sn, and 136Te, in particular on the latter which is a direct source of information on the proton-neutron effective interaction in the 132Sn region. Comparison shows that our results are in very good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that shell-model calculations using the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V-low-k, derived from the CD-Bonn potential, reproduce experimental spectra and transition rates for exotic nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn with very good agreement, though the calculated B(E2; 0+ → 2+) for 136Te shows a significant discrepancy (44 W.u. vs. 25 ± 4 W.u. experimentally) that may be resolved by a newer measurement yielding a 50% higher value.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for 134Te, 134Sn, and 136Te using 132Sn as a closed core, with valence protons occupying the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals and valence neutrons occupying the 0h9/2, 1f7/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, and 0i13/2 orbitals. The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the V-low-k approach with a cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹, using a Q-box folded-diagram method including diagrams up to second order in the two-body interaction, with calculations performed using the OXBASH shell-model code.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹ used for the V-low-k renormalization of the CD-Bonn potential
- Differences between level energies calculated with CD-Bonn, Nijmegen II, and Argonne V18 potentials for 134Te do not exceed 80 keV
- Oscillator parameter ħω = 7.88 MeV used in the harmonic-oscillator basis
- Effective proton charge 1.55e and effective neutron charge 0.70e used for E2 transition calculations
- Experimental B(E2; 0+ → 2+) for 136Te: 25 ± 4 W.u.; calculated: 44 W.u.
- Experimental B(E2; 0+ → 2+) for 134Sn: 7 ± 1 W.u.; calculated: 8 W.u.
- Experimental B(E2; 0+ → 2+) for 134Te: 24 ± 3 W.u.; calculated: 20 W.u.
- Experimental B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 134Sn: 0.89 ± 0.17 W.u.; calculated: 0.81 W.u.
- Experimental B(E2; 6+ → 4+) for 134Te: 2.05 ± 0.04 W.u.; calculated: 1.9 W.u.
- Experimental B(E2; 4+ → 2+) for 134Te: 4.3 ± 0.40 W.u.; calculated: 4.3 W.u.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of nuclei around 100Sn and 132Sn (Coraggio 2002, 2004, 2005, 2006) using realistic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn potential via the V-low-k approach, which was introduced by Bogner (2002) as an alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method. The results for 136Te are compared with experimental data from Radford (2002) and with theoretical calculations by Shimizu (2004) using a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, where the two studies disagree on the structure of the 2₂⁺ state. The paper enables future experimental studies of neutron-rich nuclei around 132Sn by providing predictions for unobserved states, such as five predicted states in 134Sn between the 6⁺ and 8⁺ levels.
