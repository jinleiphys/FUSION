# Meson-Baryon-Baryon Vertex Function and the Ward-Takahashi Identity

> Ohta proposed a solution for the well-known difficulty of satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity for a photo-meson-baryon-baryon amplitude ($γ$MBB) when a dressed meson-baryon-baryon (MBB) vertex function is present. He obtained a form for the $γ$MBB amplitude which contained, in addition to the usual pole terms, longitudinal seagull terms which were determined entirely by the MBB vertex function. He arrived at his result by using a Lagrangian which yields the MBB vertex function at tree level. We show that such a Lagrangian can be neither hermitian nor charge conjugation invariant. We have been able to reproduce Ohta's result for the $γ$MBB amplitude using the Ward-Takahashi identity and no other assumption, dynamical or otherwise, and the most general form for the MBB and $γ$MBB vertices. However, contrary to Ohta's finding, we find that the seagull terms are not robust. The seagull terms extracted from the $γ$MBB vertex occur unchanged in tree graphs, such as in an exchange current amplitude. But the seagull terms which appear in a loop graph, as in the calculation of an electromagnetic form factor, are, in general, different. The whole procedure says nothing about the transverse part of the ($γ$MBB) vertex and its contributions to the amplitudes in question.

# Meson-Baryon-Baryon Vertex Function and the Ward-Takahashi Identity

## Key claim

The authors show that Ohta's prescription for satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity for the photo-meson-baryon-baryon amplitude can be reproduced using only the Ward-Takahashi identity itself and the most general form for the vertices, without any dynamical assumptions; however, contrary to Ohta's finding, the resulting seagull terms are not robust and differ between tree graphs and loop graphs.

## Method

The authors analyze the general three-point and four-point functions for meson-baryon-baryon (MBB) and gamma-MBB vertices using Lorentz structure and the Ward-Takahashi identity alone, without reference to any particular Lagrangian or dynamical model. They then test the robustness of the seagull terms by examining two contrasting subsets of Feynman diagrams for the KP-Lambda and gamma-KP-Lambda vertices: one where the vertex arises from kaon self-energy alone, and another where the vertex is dressed by ladder exchanges of a neutral scalar meson coupling only to strangeness.

## Key numbers

- The kaon form factor normalization is set to f(m^2) = 1, where m is the renormalized kaon mass.
- The renormalized coupling constant is given by g_{KNLambda} = g^0_{KNLambda} * tilde{f}(m^2), where tilde{f}(m^2) is the wave function renormalization factor at the kaon mass shell.
- The kaon self-energy satisfies Sigma(m^2) = 0 at the renormalized kaon mass m.
- The phenomenological form factor mass parameters are typically assigned values in the range 500 MeV to 1400 MeV.

## Context

This work addresses a difficulty in hadronic Lagrangian approaches to baryon properties, specifically the problem of satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity when dressed meson-baryon-baryon vertex functions are used. The authors extend and critique the approach of Ohta (1991), which had become popular for one-loop calculations of baryon electromagnetic and strangeness properties by authors such as Musolf (1994), Cohen (1995), Forkel (1994), and others. The paper shows that while Ohta's result for the gamma-MBB amplitude can be reproduced generally, the seagull terms are not universally applicable, which has implications for calculations of electromagnetic form factors and exchange current amplitudes.
