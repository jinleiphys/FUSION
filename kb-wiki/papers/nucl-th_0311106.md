# Angular Momentum Projection and Quadrupole Correlations Effects in Atomic Nuclei

> The degrees of freedom associated with shape fluctuations and space orientation of atomic nuclei are analyzed with effective forces and large configuration spaces. A pedagogical theoretical introduction to the topic of symmetries restoration broken in the mean field approach as well as to the concept of generator coordinate is presented. We discuss global properties, like binding energies, two neutron separation energies and quadrupole moments, as well as spectroscopic ones like excitation energies or transition probabilities. The analysis covers selected examples of light and heavy nuclei, as well as some examples from the stability line to the drip lines.

## Key claim

The paper argues that beyond-mean-field methods (angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method) with effective Skyrme or Gogny forces provide a universal description of nuclear structure across the entire nuclide chart without needing effective charges, but the computational cost restricts practical calculations to axially symmetric configurations and at most two-dimensional generator coordinate spaces.

## Method

The authors perform constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with Skyrme or Gogny effective interactions to generate axially symmetric, parity-conserving intrinsic wave functions as functions of the quadrupole deformation parameter q20. They then apply angular momentum projection (AMP) using the projector operator formalism with a three-dimensional Euler angle integration, which reduces to a one-dimensional integral over the angle beta for axially symmetric even-even nuclei. For shape mixing, they combine AMP with the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) using the quadrupole moment as the generator coordinate, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain configuration-mixed wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The HFB energy curves are computed as a function of q = q20/A^(5/3) for five representative nuclei: 32Mg, 32S, 164Er, 186Pb, and 208Pb
- For 32Mg (N=20 magic nucleus), a spherical minimum at q=0 is obtained with a shoulder at approximately 1 MeV excitation energy
- For 186Pb, three minima are found: one spherical (absolute minimum) and two excited minima (one prolate, one oblate) with energies very close to the ground state
- For 208Pb, the energy has a minimum at q=0 with a very deep and stiff well, indicating no spontaneous symmetry breaking
- The projected norms N^I(q) for I=0,2,4,6,8 are plotted as functions of q for 32Mg and 164Er, showing that spherical solutions (q20=0) only have I=0 component

## Context

This work extends the mean-field approach with effective Skyrme and Gogny interactions (reviewed in Bender (2003)) by incorporating symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, building on the angular momentum projection method of Peierls (1957) and the generator coordinate method of Hill (1953). The approach contrasts with the traditional shell model (reviewed in Brown (2001) and implemented by Caurier (1998)), the variational method of the Tubingen group (Schmid, 2001), the Projected Shell Model of Hara and Sun (1995), and the Monte Carlo Shell Model of Otsuka (2001), all of which require limited configuration spaces and effective charges. The AMPGCM method enables calculations for heavy nuclei and exotic regions near the drip lines where shape coexistence and soft potential energy surfaces are common.
