# Photonic Probes of the Deuteron in Effective Field Theory

> I give a taste of recent progress in using chiral perturbation theory to understand nucleon and deuteron structure.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (χPT) provides a systematic effective field theory framework for nucleon and deuteron structure, but a full O(q⁴) calculation is necessary before neutron polarizabilities can be reliably extracted from Compton scattering data, as an incomplete O(q⁴) fit yields polarizability values in "startling disagreement" with O(q³) χPT expectations.

## Method

The author computes neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron to O(q⁴) in chiral perturbation theory, and electroproduction and Compton scattering on the deuteron to O(q³). For Compton scattering, an incomplete O(q⁴) calculation is used to fit experimental data at 95 MeV by adjusting the neutron polarizabilities as free parameters, while the O(q³) result is compared to 69 MeV data.

## Key numbers

- Threshold amplitude for γ d → π⁰ d predicted by χPT at O(q⁴): E_d = (-1.8 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺
- Experimental threshold amplitude from Saskatoon: E_d = (-1.45 ± 0.09) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺
- When the elementary neutron amplitude is set to zero, the predicted threshold amplitude becomes E_d = -2.6 × 10⁻³/m_π⁺, yielding a cross-section twice as large as the χPT prediction
- Fitting incomplete O(q⁴) Compton scattering calculations to 95 MeV data gives neutron polarizabilities α_n = 4.4 and β_n = 10 (units not specified in text)

## Context

This work extends the program of applying chiral perturbation theory to low-energy nuclear physics, as reviewed in Beane (2000). The neutral pion photoproduction prediction differs significantly from conventional nuclear models and shows agreement with experimental data from Saskatoon (Jack, 2000). The Compton scattering analysis at 69 MeV agrees with data from Lucas (1999), but the incomplete O(q⁴) fit at 95 MeV from Dave (2000) produces neutron polarizability values that conflict with O(q³) χPT expectations, motivating a full O(q⁴) calculation currently underway. Electroproduction measurements from Mainz (Aaron, 2000) show discrepancies with O(q³) theory that also await an O(q⁴) computation.
