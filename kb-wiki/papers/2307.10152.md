# Gauge invariant spectral analysis of quark hadronization dynamics

> We study the Dirac decomposition of the gauge invariant quark propagator, whose imaginary part describes the hadronization of a quark as this interacts with the vacuum, and relate each of its coefficients to a specific sum rule for the chiral-odd and chiral-even quark spectral functions. Working at first in light-like axial gauge, we obtain a new sum rule for the spectral function associated to the gauge fixing vector, and show that its second moment is in fact equal to zero. Then, we demonstrate that the first moment of the chiral-odd quark spectral function is equal in any gauge to the so-called inclusive jet mass, which is related to the mass of the particles produced in the hadronization of a quark. Finally, we present a gauge-dependent formula that connects the second moment of the chiral-even quark spectral function to invariant mass generation and final state rescattering in the hadronization of a quark.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first moment of the chiral-odd quark spectral function equals the inclusive jet mass in any gauge, not only in the light-cone gauge as previously established, and derives a new sum rule showing that the second moment of the spectral function associated to the gauge-fixing vector vanishes identically.

## Method

The authors perform a Dirac decomposition of the gauge-invariant quark propagator (the inclusive jet correlator) and relate its coefficients to sum rules for chiral-odd and chiral-even quark spectral functions. They work first in light-like axial gauge to derive a new sum rule for the spectral function associated to the gauge-fixing vector, then extend the analysis to a generic gauge using a convolution representation that separates the gauge-dependent quark bilinear operator from the gauge-invariant Wilson line, evaluating the relevant integrals via half-plane Fourier transforms.

## Context

This work extends the spectral analysis of the gauge-invariant quark propagator previously developed by Accardi, Signori, and collaborators (Accardi 2019, Accardi 2020) from the light-cone gauge to a generic gauge, proving the gauge invariance of the chiral-odd jet mass sum rule. It connects the study of quark propagator analytic properties (as investigated in continuum methods by Alkofer (2000) and Horak (2022), and lattice calculations by Skullerud (2002) and Oliveira (2012)) to the phenomenology of hadronization in hard scattering processes, enabling the calculation of mass corrections to semi-inclusive processes at sub-leading twist and providing motivation for nonperturbative calculations of quark spectral functions in arbitrary gauges.
