# The effect of hidden color channels on nucleon-nucleon interaction in quark model

> In the framework of constituent quark model, the effect of hidden color channels on the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction is studied. By adjusting the color confinement strength between the hidden color channels and color singlet channels and/or between the hidden color channels and hidden color channels, the experimental data of $S$ to $I$ partial-wave phase shifts of $NN$ scattering can be fitted well. The results show that the hidden color channel coupling might be important in producing the intermediate-range attraction of $NN$ interaction. The deuteron properties and dibaryon candidates have also been studied with this model.

## Key claim

By including hidden color channels and adjusting the color confinement strength between hidden color channels and color singlet channels (and/or between hidden color channels themselves), the model can fit experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts well, suggesting that hidden color channel coupling might be important for producing the intermediate-range attraction of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, though the authors note this is an alternative approach and the mechanism remains an open question.

## Method

The authors used the resonating-group method (RGM) within a constituent quark model framework to calculate nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts for partial waves from S through I. They introduced a multiplying factor k to the quadratic confinement potential for interactions involving hidden color channels (color octet baryon clusters), with recipe 1 (QDCCM1) adjusting k only for color singlet-hidden color couplings and recipe 2 (QDCCM2) adjusting k also for hidden color-hidden color couplings, while keeping all other model parameters identical to the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM1). The results were compared with the chiral quark model (ChQM) and QDCSM calculations.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m_u = m_d = 313 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, oscillator parameter b = 0.518 fm (for ChQM, QDCSM1, and QDCCM)
- Color confinement strength a_c = 56.755 MeV fm^{-2} (for QDCSM1 and QDCCM), strong coupling alpha_s = 0.485, chiral coupling alpha_ch = 0.027
- For the ^3S_1 partial wave (I=0, J=1), the multiplying factor k_1 = 1.40 (recipe 1) and k_2 = 1.38 (recipe 2)
- For the ^1S_0 partial wave (I=1, J=0), the multiplying factor k_1 = 1.42 and k_2 = 1.39
- Deuteron binding energy: 1.0 MeV (QDCCM1), 2.2 MeV (QDCCM2); root mean square radius: 1.2 fm (QDCCM1), 1.1 fm (QDCCM2); D-wave probability: 4.0% (both QDCCM1 and QDCCM2)
- Dibaryon candidate resonance mass for I=0, J=3 (Delta-Delta state): 2443 MeV (QDCCM0), 2298 MeV (QDCCM1), 2156 MeV (QDCCM2)
- Dibaryon candidate resonance mass for I=1, J=2: 2144 MeV (QDCCM1), 2130 MeV (QDCCM2)

## Context

This work extends the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) developed by the same group and the chiral quark model (ChQM, Salamanca version) by explicitly including hidden color channels that were ignored in most previous quark model calculations of nucleon-nucleon interaction. The approach is inspired by lattice QCD calculations showing multi-body confinement interactions in multi-quark systems, and by earlier work from Maltman (1980s) and Oka (1980s) on hidden color effects and string-flip models. The results suggest that the phenomenological color screening confinement in QDCSM and the sigma meson exchange in ChQM may be effective descriptions of hidden color channel coupling, supporting Anderson's conjecture about the similarity between nuclear and molecular forces. The model enables further study of dibaryon candidates, particularly the isoscalar ABC structure observed by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration at 2.36 GeV.
