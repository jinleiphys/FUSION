# Ordinary Muon Capture in Hydrogen Reexamined

> The rate of muon capture in a muonic hydrogen atom is calculated in heavy-nucleon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to leading order. To this order, we present the systematic evaluation of all the corrections due to the QED and electroweak radiative corrections and the proton-size effect. Since the low-energy constants involved can be determined from other independent sources of information, the theory has predictive power. For the hyperfine-singlet $μp$ capture rate $Γ_0$, our calculation gives $Γ_0=710 \,\pm 5\,s^{-1}$, which is in excellent agreement with the experimental value obtained in a recent high-precision measurement by the MuCap Collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the ordinary muon capture rate in hydrogen using heavy-nucleon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), including all QED and electroweak radiative corrections and the proton-size effect, and obtain a hyperfine-singlet capture rate of Γ₀ = 710 ± 5 s⁻¹, which agrees with the MuCap Collaboration's experimental value. The hedge is that the low-energy constants (LECs) involved can be determined from other independent sources, giving the theory predictive power, but the result depends on the specific values chosen for these LECs (e.g., g_A, g_πNN, and the axial radius).

## Method

The authors used heavy-nucleon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to evaluate the muon capture amplitude in the proton rest frame, expanding the nucleon weak form factors (vector, weak-magnetism, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar) up to NNLO. They included QED radiative corrections to the muonic hydrogen atomic wave function (vacuum polarization and finite proton size) and electroweak loop corrections identical to those in neutron beta-decay, with the LEC ẽ_V^R(m_N) fixed from earlier work. The capture rate was then computed from the helicity amplitudes for the hyperfine-singlet and triplet states.

## Key numbers

- Γ₀ (hyperfine-singlet μp capture rate) = 710 ± 5 s⁻¹ (theoretical prediction).
- Experimental Γ₀ from MuCap Collaboration = 714.9 ± 5.4 (stat) ± 5.1 (syst) s⁻¹.
- Neutrino energy E_ν = 99.149 MeV (kinematic value for muon capture at rest).
- Vacuum polarization correction to the capture rate: 2δψ_VP_1s(0) = 1.654 α/π (total), with individual contributions: zero-photon exchange = 1.045 α/π, Coulomb pole subtraction = 0.358 α/π, multi-photon exchange = 0.250 α/π.
- Finite-proton-size correction to the capture rate: 2δψ_FS_1s(0) = -0.0055(1 ± 0.06).
- Pseudoscalar form factor g_P from HBχPT = 8.26 ± 0.23 (from Bernard et al., 1994).
- Empirical g_P extracted from MuCap data = 8.06 ± 0.55.
- LEC ẽ_V^R(m_N) = 19.5 (determined from comparison with Sirlin-Marciano approach).

## Context

This work extends the HBχPT treatment of radiative corrections previously applied to neutron beta-decay (Ando et al., 2004) and inverse beta-decay (Raha et al., 2011) to the muon capture process, using the same set of low-energy constants. It provides a model-independent effective field theory alternative to the Sirlin-Marciano approach used by Czarnecki et al. (2007) for the same corrections, and confirms the experimental result of the MuCap Collaboration (2007). The calculation enables future extraction of the pseudoscalar form factor G_P(q²) with controlled theoretical uncertainty, and can be extended to other low-energy weak processes involving the same LECs.
