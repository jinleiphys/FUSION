# Non-relativistic bound states in a finite volume

> We derive general results for the mass shift of bound states with angular momentum l >= 1 in a periodic cubic box in two and three spatial dimensions. Our results have applications to lattice simulations of hadronic molecules, halo nuclei, and Feshbach molecules. The sign of the mass shift can be related to the symmetry properties of the state under consideration. We verify our analytical results with explicit numerical calculations. Moreover, we comment on the relations connecting the effective range parameter, the binding momentum of a given state and the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the corresponding wave function. We give explicit expressions for this relation in the shallow binding limit.

## Key claim

The paper derives general formulas for the finite-volume energy shift of bound states with angular momentum ℓ ≥ 1 in a periodic cubic box, showing that the sign and magnitude of the shift depend on the transformation properties of the state under the cubic symmetry group rather than solely on parity. The authors verify these analytical results with explicit numerical calculations, but note that the formulas apply only for stable bound states and assume no partial wave mixing.

## Method

The authors start from the Schrödinger equation for a two-body system with finite-range potential in infinite volume, then construct a periodic trial wave function by summing copies of the infinite-volume bound state wave function over lattice vectors. They compute the finite-volume mass shift by evaluating the matrix element of the finite-volume Hamiltonian between the exact finite-volume state and this trial state, using the asymptotic form of the wave function and the Green's function for the Helmholtz operator to perform the integrals. The results are organized by irreducible representations of the cubic group O, with explicit coefficients given for angular momenta ℓ = 0 through ℓ = 3.

## Context

This work extends Lüscher's (1985) original formula for S-wave finite-volume mass shifts to higher partial waves, providing the first systematic treatment for ℓ ≥ 1 bound states in a cubic periodic box. The results enable lattice simulations of hadronic molecules with angular momentum, P-wave Feshbach dimers in atomic physics (as studied experimentally by Regal (2003) and Schunck (2005) for ⁶Li and ⁴⁰K), and halo nuclei such as the Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ excited state in ¹¹Be. The derived relation between the asymptotic normalization coefficient γ and low-energy scattering parameters (effective range rℓ and binding momentum κ) also allows extraction of zero-energy capture reaction rates from finite-volume lattice data, relevant for nuclear astrophysics.
