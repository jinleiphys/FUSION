# Neutron stars in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model

> We utilize the top-down holographic QCD model, the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, in a hybrid setting with the SLy4, soft chiral EFT and stiff chiral EFT equations of state to describe neutron stars with high precision. In particular, we employ a calibration that bootstraps the nuclear matter by fitting the Kaluza-Klein scale and the 't Hooft coupling such that the physical saturation density and physical symmetry energy are achieved. We obtain static stable neutron star mass-radius data via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations that yield sufficiently large maximal masses of neutron stars to be compatible with the recently observed PSR-J0952-0607 data as well as all other known radius and tidal deformation constraints.

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, when calibrated to reproduce the physical saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³) and symmetry energy (30.6–32.8 MeV) rather than the usual meson-sector fit, can produce neutron star masses compatible with the PSR-J0952-0607 observation, but only when hybridized with low-density equations of state from chiral effective field theory or SLy4.

## Method

The authors employed the homogeneous Ansatz for nuclear matter in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, including time-dependent moduli to incorporate isospin asymmetry, and solved the resulting equations of motion for the holographic fields. They calibrated the two free parameters (Kaluza-Klein scale M_KK and 't Hooft coupling λ) by fitting the symmetric matter saturation density to 0.16 fm⁻³ and the symmetry energy at saturation to either 30.6 MeV or 32.8 MeV. The holographic equation of state was then patched at densities near saturation (1.032–1.059 d₀) with three different low-density equations of state (SLy4, soft chiral EFT, stiff chiral EFT), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations were solved to obtain static neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density fitted to d₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (phenomenological value)
- Two fits used: (λ = 67.55, M_KK = 368.6 MeV) giving symmetry energy S_N(d₀) = 30.6 MeV; (λ = 57.76, M_KK = 394.01 MeV) giving S_N(d₀) = 32.8 MeV
- Patching density between holographic and low-density equations of state lies in the range d_P ∈ (1.032 d₀, 1.059 d₀)
- Muon mass taken as m_μ^phys = 105.66 MeV
- The standard meson-sector fit (M_KK = 949 MeV, λ = 16.6) yields d₀ = 0.43 fm⁻³ and S_N(d₀) = 97 MeV, producing neutron stars that never reach 1.5 M_⊙ with radii at most slightly above 7 km

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to neutron stars in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, building on the authors' previous demonstration (Bartolini 2022) that realistic symmetry energies can be obtained with a baryon-sector calibration rather than the standard meson fit. It addresses the problem of unrealistically large symmetry energies (factor of 30 too large) found in earlier WSS neutron star studies by Kovensky (2021), which also used the homogeneous Ansatz but with the meson-sector fit. The hybrid patching method follows the approach introduced by Hoyos (2016) in a D3/D7 model, and the results enable future work incorporating strange quarks or more sophisticated crust descriptions.
