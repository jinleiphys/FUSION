# Feasibility of the observation of $Î·^{\prime}$ mesic nuclei in the semi-exclusive $^{12}$C($p, dp$) reaction

> We study theoretically the feasibility of the semi-exclusive $^{12}$C($p,dp$)$X$ reaction for the observation of $Î·^\prime$ mesic nuclei using the microscopic transport model JAM. The semi-exclusive measurements of the ($p,d$) reaction with protons from $Î·^\prime$ absorption are found to be significant for the observation of the $Î·^\prime$ bound states. Especially, the measurements of the energetic protons from $Î·^\prime$ non-mesic two-body absorption ($Î·^\prime NN \to NN$) are considered to be critically important. The Green's function method is used to calculate the expected spectrum of forward going deuterons corresponding to the excitation energy spectrum of the $Î·^\prime \otimes {}^{11}$C system in the semi-exclusive measurement. The semi-exclusive measurements are shown to be important in general for the $Î·^\prime$ mesic nucleus observation.

## Key claim

The semi-exclusive measurement of the $^{12}$C($p,dp$) reaction, where protons from $\eta^\prime$ absorption are detected in coincidence with forward deuterons, can substantially improve the signal-to-background ratio for observing $\eta^\prime$ mesic nuclei, provided that the detected protons have high momenta (above 0.8 GeV/$c$) and are emitted at backward angles (above 90 degrees).

## Method

The authors used the microscopic transport model JAM to simulate both the background $^{12}$C($p,d$)$X$ reaction and the signal process of $\eta^\prime$ mesic nucleus formation followed by decay via $\eta^\prime$ absorption. The Green's function method was employed to calculate the expected forward deuteron spectrum for the $\eta^\prime \otimes {}^{11}$C system, and intra-nuclear cascade simulations were performed for the $\eta^\prime$ absorption processes including $\eta^\prime N \to \eta N$, $\eta^\prime N \to \pi N$, and $\eta^\prime NN \to NN$. The improvement factor $f_{S/B}$ of the signal-to-background ratio was evaluated by imposing various cut conditions on the momentum and emission angle of coincident protons.

## Key numbers

- For the non-mesic two-body absorption $\eta^\prime NN \to NN$, the improvement factor $f_{S/B}$ reaches $2.1 \times 10^2$ for cut conditions $p_p > 0.8$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_p > 90^\circ$, and $4.1 \times 10^3$ for $p_p > 1.0$ GeV/$c$ and $\cos\theta_p < -0.75$.
- For branching ratios $(B_\eta, B_\pi, B_{NN}) = (0.2, 0.2, 0.6)$, the $f_{S/B}$ factor with $p_p > 0.8$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_p > 90^\circ$ is $1.3 \times 10^2$.
- For branching ratios $(B_\eta, B_\pi, B_{NN}) = (0.4, 0.4, 0.2)$, the $f_{S/B}$ factor with $p_p > 0.8$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_p > 90^\circ$ is $5.6 \times 10^1$.
- The momentum of protons from $\eta^\prime$ two-body absorption $\eta^\prime NN \to NN$ is approximately 1.04 GeV/$c$ as the central value inside the nucleus.
- The momentum of protons from one-body absorption is 0.58 GeV/$c$ for $\eta^\prime N \to \eta N$ and 0.71 GeV/$c$ for $\eta^\prime N \to \pi N$.
- The background simulation used $1.44 \times 10^{11}$ incident protons with kinetic energy $T_p = 2.5$ GeV and deuteron angle $\theta_d = 0^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of $\eta^\prime$ mesic nucleus formation by Nagahiro (2004, 2006, 2012) and Itahashi (2012), and responds to experimental results from the n-PRiME/Super-FRS collaboration (2016, 2017) and Sekiya (2025) where indications of $\eta^\prime$ bound states were found but with insufficient statistical significance due to large background. The semi-exclusive approach was proposed earlier by Fujioka (2014) and the Super-FRS collaboration (2015). The method enables future experiments to identify $\eta^\prime$ bound states more clearly by selecting events with high-momentum protons at backward angles, which are characteristic of $\eta^\prime$ absorption and distinct from the forward-peaked multi-pion background.
