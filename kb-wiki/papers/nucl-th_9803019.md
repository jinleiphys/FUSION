# Kinetic equation consistent with the equation of state of nuclear matter

> A kinetic equation which combines the quasiparticle drift of Landau's equation with a dissipation governed by a nonlocal and noninstant scattering integral in the spirit of Snider's equation for gases is derived. Consequent balance equations for the density, momentum and energy include quasiparticle contributions and the second order quantum virial corrections. The medium effects on binary collisions are shown to mediate the latent heat, i.e., an energy conversion between correlation and thermal energy. An implementation to heavy ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a kinetic equation for dense Fermi systems that is thermodynamically consistent with the equation of state up to second-order virial corrections, but only under the condition that the "extended quasiparticle approximation" (which includes off-shell contributions) is used instead of the plain quasiparticle approximation.

## Method

Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym transport equation for quasiparticles, the authors construct the self-energy from the Bethe-Goldstone two-particle T-matrix and apply the extended quasiparticle approximation to convert the functional of Green's functions into a functional of the quasiparticle distribution. They perform a gradient expansion of the self-energy, keeping all terms linear in gradients, and recollect the resulting nineteen gradient terms into a nonlocal and noninstant scattering integral with Enskog-type shifts parameterized by derivatives of the scattering phase shift.

## Key numbers

- The gradient expansion of the self-energy yields one nongradient term and **nineteen** gradient terms that are recollected into the nonlocal scattering integral.
- For hard spheres of diameter \(d\), the scattering phase shift \(\phi \to \pi - |q|d\) gives \(\Delta_4 = \Delta_2 = \frac{q}{|q|}d\) with all other \(\Delta\)'s zero.
- For two isolated particles interacting via a single-channel separable potential, the displacements satisfy \(\Delta_{3,4} = \frac{k+p}{2m}\Delta_t\), where \(\frac{k+p}{2m}\) is the center-of-mass velocity.
- The energy-conserving delta function in the dilute quantum gas limit reduces to \(\delta\left(\frac{k^2}{2m} + \frac{p^2}{2m} - \frac{(k-q)^2}{2m} - \frac{(p+q)^2}{2m}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal and noninstant corrections to the Boltzmann equation, previously developed for classical gases by Bogoliubov (1946) and Green (1952) and for quantum gases by Snider (1960) and Nacher, Tastevin, and Laloë (1991), to dense Fermi systems with medium effects on binary collisions. It also generalizes the results of Bornath, Kremp, Kraeft, and Schlanges (1996) from non-degenerated to degenerated systems. The derived kinetic equation is numerically tractable by Monte Carlo codes such as that used by Kortemayer, Daffin, and Bauer (1996), and it enables improved extraction of the nuclear matter equation of state from heavy ion collision data by providing a consistent link between kinetic theory and thermodynamics.
