# Nucleon resonance contributions to unpolarised inclusive electron scattering

> The first CLAS12 experiments will provide high-precision data on inclusive electron scattering observables at a photon virtuality $Q^2$ ranging from 0.05 GeV$^2$ to 12 GeV$^2$ and center-of-mass energies $W$ up to 4 GeV. In view of this endeavour, we present the modeling of the resonant contributions to the inclusive electron scattering observables. As input, we use the existing CLAS electrocoupling results obtained from exclusive meson electroproduction data off protons, and evaluate for the first time the resonant contributions based on the experimental results on the nucleon resonance electroexcitation. The uncertainties are given by the data and duly propagated through a Monte Carlo approach. In this way, we obtain estimates for the resonant contributions, important for insight into the nucleon parton distributions in the resonance region and for the studies of quark-hadron duality.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first evaluation of nucleon resonance contributions to inclusive electron scattering observables based on experimental results on nucleon resonance electroexcitation amplitudes (electrocouplings) from exclusive meson electroproduction data, rather than from inclusive measurements themselves. The authors note that the results outside the data range in Q^2 (above 5.0 GeV^2 for most resonances) should be taken with care, as they rely on extrapolation estimates.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic Breit-Wigner ansatz to compute resonant contributions to inclusive virtual photon-proton cross sections (transverse, longitudinal, and unpolarized) and the structure function F_2. As input, they employed existing CLAS results on gamma_v p N* electrocouplings (A_{1/2}, A_{3/2}, S_{1/2}) for nucleon resonances with masses below 1.8 GeV, together with resonance masses, total widths, and branching fractions from the Review of Particle Physics. Uncertainties from the electrocoupling data were propagated through a Monte Carlo bootstrap approach with 10^4 samples per Q^2 and resonance.

## Key numbers
- The photon virtuality Q^2 range covered is from 0.05 GeV^2 to 12 GeV^2, with center-of-mass energies W up to 4 GeV (from abstract; CLAS12 coverage).
- Resonance electrocouplings were used for Q^2 up to 5.0 GeV^2 for most resonances, and up to 1.5 GeV^2 for Delta(1620)1/2^-, N(1720)3/2^+, N'(1720)3/2^+, and Delta(1700)3/2^-.
- At Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, the resonant contribution accounts for about 70% of the unpolarized cross section in the first resonance region; at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 it accounts for less than 40%.
- In the second resonance region, resonant contributions remain almost unchanged with Q^2 at the level of 80%.
- In the third resonance region, the resonant contribution decreases from 90% to 50% over the Q^2 range from 1 to 4 GeV^2.
- The expected statistical precision of CLAS12 data is in the range from 0.2% to 2.0% for integrated luminosity 12.8 microbarn^{-1} with bin sizes Delta W = 0.01 GeV and Delta Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2.
- The longitudinal part of the resonant cross section does not exceed 30% of the total cross section in the kinematic region shown.

## Context
This work extends previous CLAS analyses of exclusive meson electroproduction (Aznauryan 2009, Mokeev 2012, Mokeev 2015) that determined individual nucleon resonance electrocouplings, and provides a phenomenological tool for analyzing upcoming CLAS12 inclusive electron scattering data (Burkert 2018). The results enable evaluation of non-resonant contributions to inclusive observables by subtracting the computed resonant parts from measured inclusive data, which is relevant for studies of quark-hadron duality (Bloom 1970, Melnitchouk 2005) and for accessing parton distribution functions in the resonance region. The online tool based on this work (Golubenko 2019) allows interactive evaluation of inclusive observables with user-defined kinematics.
