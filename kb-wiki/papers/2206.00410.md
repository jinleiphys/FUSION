# Kinetic mass shifts of $ρ$(770) and $K^*$(892) in Au+Au reaction at $E_\mathrm{beam}=1.23~A$GeV

> We explore the properties of the last generation of $ρ$(770) and $K^*$(892) resonances created in Au+Au reactions at E$_\mathrm{lab}=1.23$~AGeV via the reconstruction in the hadronic channel using the UrQMD model. Such resonance studies allow to obtain detailed information on the emission source in such reactions in a complementary way to di-lepton or HBT studies. We observe a freeze-out hierarchy and predict mass shifts for the $ρ$(770) resonance ($Δm_ρ\approx -330$ MeV) and the $K^*$(892) resonance ($Δm_{K^*} \approx -30$ MeV). These mass shifts are related to the regeneration cycles of each resonance species and are of purely kinetic origin. Experimentally our predictions can be tested using the GSI-HADES experiment or the lowest RHIC-BES energy.

## Key claim

The paper predicts observable mass shifts for the ρ(770) and K*(892) resonances reconstructed in their hadronic decay channels in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV, but these shifts are of purely kinetic origin from regeneration cycles and freeze-out dynamics, not from in-medium modifications of the spectral function.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model in cascade mode (no potential interactions) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at E_beam = 1.23 AGeV. They identified every decaying resonance in the simulation and tracked its daughter particles through the calculation to determine if the resonance could be reconstructed experimentally, considering only cases where daughter particles either escape without further interaction or undergo only elastic rescattering. The invariant mass distributions of reconstructable ρ^0 and K^(0*) resonances were then analyzed as a function of transverse momentum and fitted with a Breit-Wigner spectral function multiplied by a thermal phase-space factor to extract effective decoupling temperatures.

## Key numbers

- ρ^0 mass shift at vanishing transverse momentum: Δm_ρ ≈ -330 MeV
- K^(0*) mass shift at vanishing transverse momentum: Δm_(K*) ≈ -30 MeV
- ρ^0 kinetic decoupling temperature from BW×PS fit: 40-60 MeV
- K^(0*) kinetic decoupling temperature from BW×PS fit: 50-120 MeV (increasing with transverse momentum)
- At zero transverse momentum, the yield of reconstructable ρ mesons is suppressed by up to a factor of ≈100 relative to all decaying ρ resonances
- At zero transverse momentum, the yield of reconstructable K* mesons is suppressed by up to a factor of ≈10 relative to all decaying K* resonances
- The K* has a vacuum width of 47 MeV, corresponding to a lifetime of ≈4 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the connection between kinetic decoupling and invariant mass of hadronically reconstructed resonances, following the methodology developed for Δ resonances by Reichert (2019) and confirmed by the HADES experiment (Adamczewski-Musch, 2020). The FOPI collaboration (Hong, 1997; Eskef, 2001) had previously suggested mass shifts of Δ resonances in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1-2 AGeV. The predictions can be tested using the GSI-HADES experiment or the lowest RHIC-BES energy data, and complement di-lepton studies of the ρ meson (NA60 experiment at 160 AGeV, Damjanovic 2006; Arnaldi 2006) which showed broadening rather than mass shifts at higher energies.
