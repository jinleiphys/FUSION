# Coupled channels approach to $Î·N$ and $Î·' N$ interactions

> We present a coupled channels separable potential approach to $Î·N$ and $Î·'N$ interactions using a chiral-symmetric interaction kernel. The s-wave $Ď€N$ amplitudes and $Ď€^{-}p$ induced total cross sections are reproduced satisfactorily in a broad interval of energies despite limiting the channel space to two-body interactions of pseudoscalar mesons with the baryon ground-state octet. It is demonstrated that an explicit inclusion of the $Î·_0$ meson singlet field leads to a more attractive $Î·N$ interaction, with the real part of the scattering length exceeding 1 fm. The $Î·'N$ diagonal coupling appears sufficient to generate an $Î·'N$ bound state but the inter-channel dynamics moves the respective pole far from physical region making the $Î·'N$ interaction repulsive at energies around the channel threshold. The $N^{*}(1535)$ and $N^{*}(1650)$ resonances are generated dynamically and the origin and properties of the $S$-matrix poles assigned to them are studied in detail. We also hint at a chance that the $N^{*}(1895)$ state might also be formed provided a suitably varied model setting is found.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the explicit $\eta_0$ singlet field in a coupled channels chiral approach leads to a more attractive $\eta N$ interaction with a real part of the scattering length exceeding 1 fm, but the inter-channel dynamics moves the $\eta'N$ bound state pole far from the physical region, making the $\eta'N$ interaction repulsive at energies around the channel threshold despite the diagonal $\eta'N$ coupling being sufficient to generate a bound state.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled channels separable potential model for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in the zero-strangeness sector, using a chiral U(3) Lagrangian that includes the $\eta_0$ singlet field and a one-mixing-angle scheme for $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing. The tree-level potentials derived from the effective Lagrangian are unitarized via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with Yamaguchi form factors as regulators, and the model parameters are fitted to $\pi N$ SAID amplitudes, $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta n$, $\pi^- p \rightarrow K^0 \Lambda$, and $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta' n$ cross section data.

## Key numbers

- Real part of the $\eta N$ scattering length exceeds 1 fm when the $\eta_0$ singlet field is explicitly included
- $\eta'N$ channel threshold energy: $E_{th}(\eta'N) = 1897$ MeV
- $\pi N$ threshold energy: $E_{th}(\pi N) = 1077$ MeV
- $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle: $\vartheta = -15.5^\circ \pm 1.3^\circ$ from Bramon (1997), with lattice QCD prediction $\vartheta = -14.1^\circ \pm 2.8^\circ$ from Christ (2010)
- Meson decay constants used: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $f_K = 110.0$ MeV, $f_\eta = 118.8$ MeV
- Born term couplings: $F = 0.46$, $D = 0.80$ from hyperon decay analyses
- $b_D = 0.1$ GeV$^{-1}$ fixed from baryon mass analyses
- Inverse range parameters $\alpha_{jb}$ constrained to the interval $\langle 400, 1200 \rangle$ MeV
- $w_s$ parameter constrained to $\langle -0.3, 0.3 \rangle$ (though fits favored $w_s \approx -1$)
- $D_s$ set from interval $\langle -0.6, -0.2 \rangle$ based on photoproduction fits from Borasoy (2002)
- SAID $\pi N$ amplitude data fitted from 1095 MeV to 1600 MeV for $S_{11}$ (30 data points) and up to 1450 MeV for $S_{31}$ (21 data points)
- $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta n$ cross section data from 1500 to 1600 MeV (10 data points)
- $\pi^- p \rightarrow K^0 \Lambda$ cross section data up to 1750 MeV (50 data points)
- $\pi^- p \rightarrow \eta' n$ cross section data below 2 GeV (4 data points)

## Context

This work extends the chirally motivated coupled channels approach of Cieply (2013) for meson-baryon scattering in the zero-strangeness sector by explicitly including the $\eta'$ degree of freedom, building on the U(3) chiral Lagrangian framework developed by Borasoy (2002) for $\eta$ and $\eta'$ photoproduction. The results are relevant for assessing the possibility of $\eta$-nuclear and $\eta'$-nucleonic quasi-bound states, as discussed by Inoue (2002), Cieply (2013), Barnea (2017), and in the review by Machner (2014), and for understanding the $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ resonances as dynamically generated states. The paper also hints that the $N^*(1895)$ state might be formed in a suitably varied model setting.
