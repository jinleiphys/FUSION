# Fluid dynamics of out of equilibrium boost invariant plasmas

> We establish a set of equations for moments of the distribution function. In the relaxation time approximations, these moments obey a coupled set of equations that can be truncated order-by-order. Solving the equations of moments, we are able to identify an attractor solution that controls a transition from a free streaming fixed point to a hydrodynamic fixed point. In particular, this attractor solution provides a renormalization of the effective value of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, taking into account off-equilibrium effects.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that a set of moment equations for the distribution function, truncated order-by-order, reveals an attractor solution that controls the transition from a free-streaming fixed point to a hydrodynamic fixed point; this attractor provides a renormalization of the effective shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, taking into account off-equilibrium effects, but the analysis is limited to the relaxation time approximation and boost-invariant systems.

## Method

The authors introduce a set of L-moments defined as integrals of the distribution function weighted by Legendre polynomials, which encode the anisotropic structure of the phase space distribution. They derive coupled equations of motion for these moments from a kinetic equation with relaxation time approximation for a boost-invariant, conformal system. The moment equations are truncated at various orders and solved to identify fixed points in the limits of free streaming (tau/tau_R approaching 0) and hydrodynamics (tau/tau_R approaching infinity), and the attractor solutions connecting these fixed points are extracted numerically.

## Key numbers

- The stable free-streaming fixed point for all L-moments is at g_n approximately -1, while the unstable fixed point is at g_n approximately -2
- When truncating the moment equations at n=1, the stable free-streaming fixed points shift to g_0 = g_1 = -0.92937 instead of -1
- In the hydrodynamic limit, the fixed points depend on order n as g_n = -(4+2n)/3
- The renormalization factor Z_{eta/s}^{-1} is presented as a function of tau/tau_R, showing reduction of effective eta/s when the system is far from equilibrium (tau/tau_R approaching 0)

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors previously investigated by Heller (2015) for hydrodynamics and Romatschke (2017) for kinetic theory, as well as systems beyond Bjorken symmetry studied by Denicol (2018). The L-moment framework provides a systematic way to incorporate higher-order viscous corrections beyond second-order Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics, enabling the application of fluid dynamics to out-of-equilibrium systems such as the early stages of heavy-ion collisions where thermalization timescales are short (O(1) fm/c).
