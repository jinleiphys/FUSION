# Spectroscopic factor calculations in the \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model

> The convergence properties of spectroscopic factors in the \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model are hereby investigated. For this, we consider nuclear energies and spectroscopic factors in $A = 6$ and 7 isotopes, using the chiral forces NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ and N$^3$LO. While low-lying spectrum energy demonstrates remarkable convergence with the increase of model space within the no-core shell model, the spectroscopic factor exhibits no definitive convergence trend and seems independent of the employed nuclear interaction. The use of spectroscopic factors issued from the no-core shell model to calculate cross-sections of knockout reaction might then be questionable. The results are compared with that of the standard shell model and \textit{ab initio} Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that while low-lying nuclear energies converge reliably with increasing model space in the no-core shell model (NCSM), spectroscopic factors (SFs) show no definitive convergence trend and appear nearly independent of the chosen chiral interaction, making their use in knockout reaction cross-section calculations questionable from a theoretical standpoint.

## Method
The authors performed *ab initio* NCSM calculations for A = 6 and 7 isotopes (⁶He, ⁶Li, ⁷Li) using two chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions: NNLOₒₚₜ (bare) and N³LO (softened via similarity renormalization group with λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹). They computed energies and spectroscopic factors for p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ partial waves across model spaces Nₘₐₓ = 0 to 12 with harmonic-oscillator frequencies ħω ranging from 10 to 32 MeV, then used the calculated SFs to predict knockout reaction cross-sections via the eikonal model.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of ⁶He extrapolated to infinite model space: −27.378(042) MeV (NNLOₒₚₜ) and −29.246(040) MeV (N³LO); experimental value is −29.271(054) MeV
- Ground state energy of ⁷Li extrapolated: −37.777(196) MeV (NNLOₒₚₜ) and −40.195(034) MeV (N³LO); experimental value is −39.245(042) MeV
- For the (⁷Li,⁶He) proton knockout reaction, NCSM SFs give an inclusive cross-section of 27.91 mb (NNLOₒₚₜ) and 28.33 mb (N³LO), compared to 49.00 mb from the standard shell model and 13.4(7) mb experimentally
- For the (⁷Li,⁶Li) neutron knockout inclusive cross-section, NCSM gives 50.73 mb (NNLOₒₚₜ) and 52.15 mb (N³LO), versus 63.81 mb from the standard shell model and 30.7(18) mb experimentally
- The SF for ⁶He(0₁⁺) ⊗ p₃/₂ → ⁷Li(3/2₁⁻) is approximately 0.459 (NNLOₒₚₜ) and 0.466 (N³LO), compared to 0.806 from the standard shell model

## Context
This work extends prior NCSM studies (Barrett 2013) that established energy convergence but lacked systematic SF convergence analysis. The results contradict the standard shell model approach (which yields systematically larger SFs) and align with *ab initio* variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations from PhysRevC.86.024315 and PhysRevC.84.024319. The findings question the reliability of using unconverged NCSM SFs for knockout reaction predictions, motivating development of new extrapolation methods or renormalization schemes for SFs analogous to those used for energies (PhysRevC.86.054002, PhysRevC.91.061301).
