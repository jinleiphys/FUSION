# Impact of pairing correlations on the orientation of the nuclear

> For the first time, the tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory with pairing correlations has been formulated and implemented in a fully self-consistent and microscopic way to investigate the evolution of the spin axis and the pairing effects in rotating triaxial nuclei. The measured energy spectrum and transition probabilities for the Nd-135 yrast band are reproduced well without any ad hoc renormalization factors when pairing effects are taken into account. A transition from collective to chiral rotation has been demonstrated. It is found that pairing correlations introduce additional admixtures in the single-particle orbitals, and, thus, influence the structure of tilted axis rotating nuclei by reducing the magnitude of the proton and neutron angular momenta while merging their direction.

## Key claim

For the first time, tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory with pairing correlations has been formulated and implemented self-consistently; the measured energy spectrum and transition probabilities for the Nd-135 yrast band are reproduced well without any ad hoc renormalization factors only when pairing effects are taken into account.

## Method

The authors solved the fully relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) problem in a rotating intrinsic frame with a constant angular velocity vector not parallel to any principal axis, using the point-coupling energy density functional PC-PK1 for the particle-hole channel and a monopole pairing force with constant strength (G_n = 0.12 MeV fm^3 for neutrons, G_p = 0.13 MeV fm^3 for protons) for the particle-particle channel. The calculations were performed in a three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with N=10 major shells, allowing only rotations in the (x,z) plane, and the tilt angle was determined self-consistently by minimizing the total Routhians. The 1-qp (νh_{11/2}) and 3-qp (νh_{11/2}πh_{11/2}^2) configurations in the yrast band of Nd-135 were investigated.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing strength G_n = 0.12 MeV fm^3; proton pairing strength G_p = 0.13 MeV fm^3, determined from odd-even mass differences.
- Triaxial deformation γ ~ 22° for the bandhead of the 1-qp configuration in Nd-135.
- Neutron pairing gaps for the 3-qp band decrease from 1.25 MeV (ħω = 0.2 MeV) to 1.14 MeV (ħω = 0.6 MeV).
- Pairing reduces the angle between proton and neutron angular momenta by up to 20%.
- Backbending occurs in the angular momentum region I = 10–14 ħ.

## Context

This work extends previous tilted axis cranking calculations based on single-particle potentials with pairing-plus-quadrupole models (Frauendorf, 2000) and earlier relativistic density functional studies without pairing (Madokoro, 2000; Zhao, 2011) that required ad hoc renormalization factors (0.3 for B(M1) values) to match data. The inclusion of pairing correlations eliminates the need for such renormalization and enables self-consistent investigation of the transition from collective to chiral rotation in triaxial nuclei, specifically addressing the long-standing question of how pairing influences tilted axis rotors.
