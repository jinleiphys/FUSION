# Chiral electric separation effect in the quark-gluon plasma

> In this paper we introduce and compute a new transport coefficient for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at very high temperature. This new coefficient $σ_{χe}$, the CESE (Chiral Electric Separation Effect) conductivity, quantifies the amount of axial current $\vec J_A$ that is generated in response to an externally applied electric field $e\vec E$: $\vec J_A = σ_{χe} (e\vec E)$. Starting with rather general argument in the kinetic theory framework, we show how a characteristic structure $σ_{χe}\propto μμ_5$ emerges, which also indicates the CESE as an anomalous transport effect occurring only in a parity-odd environment with nonzero axial charge density $μ_5\neq 0$. Using the Hard-Thermal-Loop framework the CESE conductivity for the QGP is found to be $σ_{χe} = (\#) T\frac{{\rm Tr}_{\rm f}Q_eQ_A}{g^4\ln(1/g)} \frac{μμ_5}{T^2}$ to the leading-log accuracy with the numerical constant (#) depending on favor content, e.g. (#)$=14.5163$ for $u,d$ light flavors.

## Key claim

The paper introduces and computes a new transport coefficient for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at very high temperature, the Chiral Electric Separation Effect (CESE) conductivity σχe, which quantifies the axial current generated in response to an externally applied electric field. The effect occurs only in a parity-odd environment with nonzero axial charge density (μ5 ≠ 0) and the conductivity is proportional to the product of vector and axial chemical potentials (μ μ5), a structure confirmed by both kinetic theory arguments and explicit Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) calculation.

## Method

The authors first use kinetic theory with the relaxation time approximation to derive the general structure σχe ∝ μ μ5 from a Taylor expansion of the distribution functions. They then compute the CESE conductivity for high-temperature QGP using Kubo formulae within the Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) framework, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the effective vertex via a variational procedure to obtain the leading-log accuracy result.

## Key numbers

* For two light flavors (u,d): σχe^(2f) = T (Tr_f Q_e Q_A) / (g^4 ln(1/g)) × 14.5163 × (μ μ5 / T^2)
* For three light flavors (u,d,s): σχe^(3f) = T (Tr_f Q_e Q_A) / (g^4 ln(1/g)) × 13.0859 × (μ μ5 / T^2)
* The ordinary electric conductivity for two flavors: σ^(2f) = T (Tr_f Q_e Q_V) / (g^4 ln(1/g)) × (12.9989 + 6.50554 × (μ^2 + μ5^2)/T^2)
* The ordinary electric conductivity for three flavors: σ^(3f) = T (Tr_f Q_e Q_V) / (g^4 ln(1/g)) × (11.8687 + 5.60215 × (μ^2 + μ5^2)/T^2)

## Context

This work extends the previous computation of the CESE conductivity for a QED plasma by Huang and Liao (2013) and for strongly coupled holographic gauge plasma by Pu and Yang (2014) to the case of weakly coupled QGP. The result provides a perturbative benchmark for the CESE transport coefficient that can be used in phenomenological simulations of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where the effect may be observable through charge-dependent azimuthal correlations, complementing ongoing searches for the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Magnetic Wave.
