# Gauge invariance of the color-superconducting gap on the mass shell

> The gap parameter for color superconductivity is expected to be a gauge invariant quantity, at least on the appropriate mass shell. Computing the gap to subleading order in the QCD coupling constant, g, we show that the prefactor of the exponential in 1/g is gauge dependent off the mass shell, and independent of gauge on the mass shell.

# Gauge invariance of the color-superconducting gap on the mass shell

## Key claim
The prefactor of the exponential in 1/g for the color-superconducting gap parameter is gauge dependent when computed off the quasiparticle mass shell, but becomes independent of gauge when computed on the correct quasiparticle mass shell, contrary to earlier claims that gauge dependence enters at subleading order.

## Method
The authors compute the gauge-dependent terms in the QCD gap equation for two-flavor color superconductivity in a general Coulomb gauge with gauge parameter ξ_C. They first evaluate the gauge-dependent contribution X^+(K) while neglecting terms proportional to p_0^2 (working off the mass shell), finding an apparent subleading-order gauge dependence. They then restore the p_0^2 terms and evaluate the gap function on the correct quasiparticle mass shell k_0 = ε_k^+, performing the Matsubara sum and momentum integration to determine the true order of the gauge-dependent contribution.

## Key numbers
- The gap solution at the Fermi surface has the form φ_0 = 2 b μ exp(-c/g) [1 + O(g)], where c/g = π/(2ḡ) with ḡ = g/(3√2)
- The prefactor b = b_0/g^5, where b_0 = 256 π^4 (2/N_f)^(5/2) b_0' and b_0' = exp(-(π^2 + 4)/8) ≃ 0.177
- Off the mass shell, the gauge-dependent term gives a BCS logarithm ∼ g^2 φ_0 ln(μ/φ_0), which is of subleading order and multiplies the prefactor by exp(3 ξ_C/2)
- On the mass shell, the gauge-dependent term evaluates to X_μ^+ ∼ g^2 φ_0, which is of sub-subleading order O(g^2 φ_0) rather than subleading order O(g φ_0)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the color-superconducting gap in QCD at high density, following the leading-order calculation by Son (1999) and the subleading calculations by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999) and Pisarski and Rischke (2000). It corrects an earlier argument by Pisarski and Rischke (2000) that had incorrectly neglected additional powers of gluon momentum, and confirms by analytical means the numerical results of Shuster and Rajagopal (2000). The result enables reliable calculation of the gap prefactor to subleading order without gauge ambiguity, and clarifies that gauge-dependent terms only affect the gap at order O(g) beyond the leading exponential.
