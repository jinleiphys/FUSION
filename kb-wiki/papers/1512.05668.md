# Axion electrodynamics and nonrelativistic photons in nuclear and quark matter

> We argue that the effective theory for electromagnetic fields in spatially varying meson condensations in dense nuclear and quark matter is given by the axion electrodynamics. We show that one of the helicity states of photons there has the nonrelativistic gapless dispersion relation $ω\sim k^2$ at small momentum, while the other is gapped. This "nonrelativistic photon" may also be realized at the interface between topological and trivial insulators in condensed matter systems.

# Axion electrodynamics and nonrelativistic photons in nuclear and quark matter

## Key claim

The low-energy effective theory for electromagnetic fields in spatially varying meson condensations (such as pion domain walls or meson supercurrent phases) is given by axion electrodynamics, leading to one photon helicity state having a nonrelativistic gapless dispersion relation ω ∼ k² at small momentum while the other is gapped; this result is model-independent and based only on symmetries and topology, and the same physics should also appear at the interface between topological and ordinary insulators.

## Method

The author starts from the chiral perturbation theory effective Lagrangian for neutral pions, including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term that couples the pion field to electromagnetic fields via the anomaly. For a spatially varying pion condensate ⟨∇π⁰⟩ ≠ 0, the equations of motion for dynamical electromagnetic fields are derived from the full Lagrangian, yielding modified Gauss's law and Ampère's law with additional terms proportional to ⟨∇π⁰⟩. The plane-wave solution for electromagnetic waves propagating along the direction of the gradient is then analyzed to obtain the dispersion relation for each photon helicity state.

## Key numbers

- The domain wall solution has thickness ∼ 1/mπ, where mπ is the pion mass.
- The energy density per unit area of the π⁰ domain wall is E/S = 8 fπ² mπ.
- The baryon number per unit surface area on the domain wall is NB/S = Bex/(2π), where Bex is the external magnetic field strength.
- The condition for the π⁰ domain wall to be energetically favorable over nuclear matter is Bex > 16π fπ² mπ / mN.
- In the chiral limit (mπ = 0), the ground state gradient is ⟨∇π⁰⟩ = C μB Bex, where C = 1/(4π² fπ).
- The effective mass for the gapped photon helicity state is m* = C ⟨∂zπ⁰⟩/ε, where ε is the permittivity of the medium.
- For k ≪ C⟨∂zπ⁰⟩, the gapless photon has dispersion ω₊ = v²k²/m* and the gapped photon has ω₋ = m*.

## Context

This work extends the concept of axion electrodynamics, previously applied to topological insulators by Qi (2008) and to the π⁰ domain wall in a magnetic field by Son (2007), to a broader class of spatially varying meson condensations in dense nuclear and quark matter, including the meson supercurrent phase studied by Gerhold (2006). The nonrelativistic gapless photon dispersion is a new result not discussed in the closely related work by Ferrer (2015) on the dual chiral density wave. The findings suggest that electromagnetic properties of neutron stars could be modified by the quantum Hall effect, and the same nonrelativistic photons may be testable in table-top experiments with topological insulators.
