# On the Mass Difference between pion and rho meson using a Relativistic Two-Body Model

> The big mass difference between the pion and rho meson possibly originates from the spin-dependent nature of the interactions in the two states since these two states are similar except for spin. Both pi and rho are quark-antiquark systems which can be treated using the two-body Dirac equations (TBDE) of constraint dynamics. This relativistic approach for two-body system has the advantage over the non-relativistic treatment in the sense that the spin-dependent nature is automatically coming out from the formalism. We employed Dirac's relativistic constraint dynamics to describe quark-antiquark systems. Within this formalism, the 16-component Dirac equation is reduced to the 4-component 2nd-order differential equation and the radial part of this equation is simply a Schroedinger-type equation with various terms calculated from the basic radial potential. We used a modified Richardson potential for quark-antiquark systems which satisfies the conditions of confinement and asymptotic freedom. We obtained the wave functions for these two mesons which are not singular at short distances. We also found that the cancellation between the Darwin and spin-spin interaction terms occurs in the pi mass but not in the rho mass and this is the main source of the big difference in the two meson masses.

## Key claim

The large mass difference between the pion and rho meson originates from a cancellation between the Darwin and spin-spin interaction terms that occurs in the pion mass but not in the rho mass, rather than solely from chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors employed the two-body Dirac equations (TBDE) of constraint dynamics to describe the quark-antiquark systems, reducing the 16-component Dirac equation to a 4-component second-order differential equation with a Schroedinger-type radial form. They used a modified Richardson potential with three parameters (Lambda, K, B) that satisfies both confinement and asymptotic freedom, fitting the invariant masses of 32 mesons to determine the potential parameters and then solving the resulting differential equations for the pion and rho meson wave functions and masses.

## Key numbers

- Fitted potential parameters: Lambda = 0.4218 GeV, B = 0.05081, K = 4.198
- Quark masses used: m_u = 0.0557 GeV, m_d = 0.0553 GeV
- Calculated pion invariant mass: 0.159 GeV (experimental: 0.140 GeV)
- Calculated rho meson invariant mass: 0.792 GeV (experimental: 0.775 GeV)
- Kinetic energy contribution for pion: 0.8508 GeV^2
- Kinetic energy contribution for rho: 0.3085 GeV^2 (S-state) and 0.2812 GeV^2 (D-state)
- Darwin term contribution for pion: -3.804 GeV^2
- Spin-spin term contribution for pion: 3.340 GeV^2
- Darwin term contribution for rho (S-state): -0.2790 GeV^2
- Spin-spin term contribution for rho (S-state): -0.0364 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic two-body Dirac constraint dynamics approach developed by Van Alstine and Crater (1982) and Yoon et al. (2009) to light meson systems, building on the Richardson potential (1979) for quark-antiquark interactions. The results demonstrate that relativistic treatment is essential for light quarks (kinetic energies near 1 GeV compared to quark masses of about 0.0557 GeV) and provide a mechanism for the pion's small mass that is consistent with chiral symmetry breaking. The approach enables systematic calculation of meson spectra without introducing cutoff parameters and can be applied to other light meson systems.
