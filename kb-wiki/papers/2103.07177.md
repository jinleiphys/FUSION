# Supremacy of optimal beam energy for synthesis of superheavy elements

> Besides right choice of entrance channel, selection of optimal beam energies for synthesis of superheavy elements plays a crucial role. A thorough investigation with the advanced statistical and dinuclear system models on all the experiments performed for the synthesis of the successful superheavy elements Z=104-118 and failed superheavy elements Z=119-120 leads us to infer that improper choice of the beam energies may be responsible for too low production cross sections to measure and thus the cause for the debacle. We have predicted the optimal beam energies to obtain the maximum production cross sections for all the reactions used for the superheavy elements Z=104-120. Hope exploitation of these predictions may be on the cards soon to extend the periodic table for the eighth period

# Supremacy of optimal beam energy for synthesis of superheavy elements

## Key claim
Improper choice of beam energy, specifically, using energies much higher than the predicted optimal values, is the primary cause for the failure of all experiments attempting to synthesize superheavy elements Z=119 and 120, rather than an intrinsic limitation of the chosen entrance channel reactions.

## Method
The authors employed the advanced statistical model (ASM) and the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate excitation functions and evaporation residue cross sections for all cold and hot fusion reactions used in successful syntheses of superheavy elements Z=104-118 and failed attempts for Z=119-120. They compared the beam energies actually used in experiments with the model-predicted optimal energies that would yield maximum production cross sections, and evaluated the corresponding cross sections at both energy sets.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction ⁵⁰Ti + ²⁴⁹Cf, the DNS model predicts a 3n evaporation residue cross section of 582 fb at E_cm = 227.5 MeV, compared to 760 fb in prior work by Nasirov et al. (2011)
- For the reaction ⁵⁴Cr + ²⁴⁸Cm, the DNS model predicts a 3n cross section of 118 fb at E_cm = 241.5 MeV, compared to 76 fb in prior work by Nasirov et al. (2011)
- The optimal beam energies for synthesizing Z=119 and 120 are predicted to be lower than those used experimentally; the best reaction for Z=119 is ⁵⁴Cr + ²⁴³Am → ²⁹⁷119 and for Z=120 is ⁵⁹Co + ²³⁷Np → ²⁹⁶120
- According to both ASM and DNS models, several reactions for Z=119 and 120 could yield optimal evaporation residue cross sections greater than 100 pb if conducted at the predicted optimal energies

## Context
This work extends prior excitation function studies using the ASM and DNS models (D'Arrigo 1994, Sagaidak 1998, Adamian 1996, Fazio 2004) to all superheavy element synthesis experiments from Z=104 to 120. It builds on the finding by Tanaka (2020) that optimum incident energy is important for hot fusion reactions and can be determined from barrier distributions. The study contradicts the assumption that higher beam energy is beneficial, noting that while heavy-ion fusion reactions show saturation or slowly decreasing cross sections above the barrier (Reisdorf 1985, Brinkmann 1994), superheavy ion reactions exhibit a bell-shaped excitation function. The results enable future experiments to use the same entrance channel reactions but at the predicted optimal beam energies, potentially achieving production cross sections sufficient to synthesize elements Z=119 and 120 and extend the periodic table into the eighth period.
