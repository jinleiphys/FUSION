# A Phenomenological Lagrangian Approach to Two Kaon Photoproduction and Pentaquark Searches

> We examine cross sections for the processes $γN\to NK\bar{K}$ in the framework of a phenomenological Lagrangian. We include contributions from $Λ$ and $Σ$ resonances up to spin 3/2, as well as those from an exotic $Θ^+$. We allow the $Θ^+$ to have spin 1/2 or 3/2, with either positive or negative parity in each case. We also allow the state to be either isovector or isoscalar. We find that the scenario that most closely matches observations at Jefferson Laboratory requires a moderately large coupling of the $Θ^+$ to $NK^*$.

## Key claim

The scenario that most closely matches Jefferson Laboratory observations requires a moderately large coupling of the Θ⁺ to the NK* vector meson, but the paper does not claim definitive evidence for the pentaquark state; rather it examines multiple spin, parity, and isospin assignments to find which best reproduces the measured cross sections.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological Lagrangian model for the reaction γN → NK̄K, including contributions from Λ and Σ hyperon resonances up to spin 3/2, vector mesons (K* and φ), and an exotic Θ⁺ pentaquark. They allowed the Θ⁺ to have spin 1/2 or 3/2, positive or negative parity, and either isoscalar or isovector character, and computed cross sections for all six possible charge channels using coupling constants derived from known decay widths and Goldberger-Treiman relations.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant gΘNK for a spin-1/2, positive-parity Θ⁺ with width 1 MeV is 0.27; for width 10 MeV it is 0.87.
- The coupling constant gΘNK for a spin-3/2, negative-parity Θ⁺ with width 1 MeV is 4.35; for width 10 MeV it is 13.76.
- The φKK coupling constant is 4.3; the K*Kπ coupling constant is 5.6.
- The gNΛK coupling constant obtained from Goldberger-Treiman relations is -6.51; the gNΣK coupling constant is 3.2.
- The Λ(1520) has mass 1520 MeV, spin 3/2, negative parity, total width 16 MeV, NK branching fraction 0.45, and gYNK = 15.2.
- The form factor cutoff parameter X is chosen as 1.8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Lagrangian approach of Nam and Oh (2004) for γn → nK⁺K⁻ by including a much larger set of hyperon resonances (14 states) and vector meson contributions, and by systematically exploring the spin, parity, and isospin dependence of the Θ⁺ signal. The results are directly relevant to pentaquark searches at Jefferson Laboratory (CLAS Collaboration, 2003-2004), the Spring-8 Collaboration (2003), and the Saphir Collaboration, and enable comparison of exotic Θ⁺ production cross sections with those from non-exotic hyperons as emphasized by Close and Zhao (2004).
