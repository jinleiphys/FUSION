# Nuclear Excitation by Electron Capture in Excited Ions

> A nuclear excitation following the capture of an electron in an empty orbital has been recently observed for the first time. So far, the evaluation of the cross section of the process has been carried out widely using the assumption that the ion is in its electronic ground state prior to the capture. We show that by lifting this restriction new capture channels emerge resulting in a boost of more than three orders of magnitude to the electron capture resonance strength.

## Key claim

By lifting the restriction that the ion is in its electronic ground state prior to electron capture, new capture channels emerge that boost the nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) resonance strength by more than three orders of magnitude, specifically for the example of $^{73}$Ge.

## Method

The authors computed NEEC resonance strengths for $^{73}$Ge by considering all possible initial and final electronic configurations for charge states $q = 29+$ to $32+$, including configurations with electrons distributed across K, L, and M shells. They used two approaches: a scaling procedure based on internal conversion coefficients (ICCs) for neutral atoms, and a more advanced wavefunction formalism using the Flexible Atomic Code (FAC) with Feshbach projection operator theory, incorporating a recoupling coefficient $\Lambda$ for cases where electron capture breaks orbital angular momentum coupling.

## Key numbers

- The maximum NEEC resonance strength under the ground state assumption (GSA) for $^{73}$Ge is $S_{\mathrm{NEEC}} = \SI{1.25e-4}{\barn\electronvolt}$, occurring for an L3 subshell capture at $q = 32+$
- Lifting the GSA yields a maximum resonance strength of $\SI{5.18e-1}{\barn\electronvolt}$ for a K-capture channel in $^{73}$Ge at $q = 29+$ with initial configuration $1\mathrm{s}^1\ 2\mathrm{s}^1\ 3\mathrm{p}_{3/2}^1$ and resonance energy $E_\mathrm{r} = \SI{0.18}{\electronvolt}$
- A total of 32723 capture channels were found for L- and M-shells when lifting the GSA, compared to 47 channels under the GSA, plus 100 newly opened K-capture channels at $q = 29+$
- The direct-photoexcitation resonance strength for the $E2$ transition in $^{73}$Ge is $S_\gamma = \SI{1.93e-6}{\barn\electronvolt}$, significantly smaller than the highest NEEC values

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of NEEC beyond the ground state assumption used in prior studies by Palffy (2008), Wu (2018), Gunst (2018), and Rzadkiewicz (2019). The authors note that the first experimental observation of NEEC in $^{93}$Mo by Chiara (2018) showed a discrepancy of about nine orders of magnitude with state-of-the-art theory (Wu, 2019), and recent work by Rzadkiewicz (2021) partially addressed this by considering Compton profiles. The inclusion of excited electronic configurations could potentially reduce this discrepancy and enable reevaluation of isotopes previously disregarded for NEEC applications, particularly in out-of-equilibrium scenarios such as laser-generated plasmas.
