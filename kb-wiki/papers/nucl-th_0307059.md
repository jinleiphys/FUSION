# Characterization of Jets in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Jet quenching is considered to be one of the signatures of the formation of quark gluon plasma. In order to investigate the jet quenching, it is necessary to detect jets produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, determine their properties and compare those with the jets one obtains in hadron-hadron or $e^+-e^-$ collisions. In this work, we propose that calculation of flow parameters may be used to detect and characterize jets in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the calculation of flow parameters (specifically higher-order flow coefficients $v_m$) from the azimuthal distribution of hadrons can be used to detect and characterize jets in relativistic heavy ion collisions, provided the number of jet particles constitutes more than about 5% of the total number of particles after a transverse momentum cut.

## Method

The method computes flow coefficients $v_m^2$ from the pairwise azimuthal correlations of all particles with transverse momentum above a chosen cutoff $p_0$. For a single jet, the authors derive an analytic expression showing that $v_m^2$ as a function of $m^2$ follows a linear form whose intercept gives the number of jet particles $N_J$ and whose slope gives the jet opening angle $\Delta\phi$ (or variance $\sigma$). They test the method on synthetic events where jet particles are added by hand to background events generated by a hadron string cascade model for 130 GeV gold-gold collisions, using transverse momentum cuts of 1.5 and 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The probability of jet production in central gold-gold collisions is estimated at about $10^{-3}$, based on a hard jet production cross section of 100 nb in nucleon-nucleon collisions.
- For events with a single jet of 20 particles and opening angle $\pi/6$, the extracted values of the number of jet particles and opening angle are within 25% of the input values.
- The method requires that the number of jet particles constitute more than 5% of the total number of particles after the transverse momentum cut for meaningful extraction.
- For central collisions with a 1.5 GeV cutoff, the total number of particles (background plus jet) was 74; for a 2 GeV cutoff it was 28. For peripheral collisions, the corresponding numbers were 48 and 28.
- For two-jet events with jets separated by $\pi$, the flow coefficients for odd $m$ are generally smaller than those for even $m$; for example, with 20 particles in each jet and opening angle $\pi/6$, $v_1^2 = 0.0008$ and $v_2^2 = 0.78$ at a 2 GeV cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the standard use of flow coefficients (directed flow $v_1$, elliptic flow $v_2$) in heavy ion collisions, which are normally used to study collective motion and the equation of state of hot dense matter (flow1). The authors propose using higher-order flow coefficients ($v_m$ for $m>2$) to extract jet properties, complementing indirect jet-tagging methods using photons (tag-phot) or di-leptons (tag-phot, tag-dielept). The method enables direct measurement of jet quenching effects in relativistic heavy ion collisions, such as changes in the number of jet particles and opening angle as a function of impact parameter.
