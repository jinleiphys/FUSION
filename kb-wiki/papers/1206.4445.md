# Tensor polarization of deuterons passing through matter

> It is shown that the magnitude of tensor polarization of the deuteron beam, which arises owing to the spin dichroism effect, depends appreciably on the angular width of the detector that registers the deuterons transmitted through the target. Even when the angular width of the detector is much smaller than the mean square angle of multiple Coulomb scattering, the beam's tensor polarization depends noticeably on rescattering. When the angular width of the detector is much larger than the mean square angle of multiple Coulomb scattering (as well as than the characteristic angle of elastic nuclear scattering), tensor polarization is determined only by the total reaction cross sections for deuteron-nucleus interaction, and elastic scattering processes make no contribution to tensor polarization.

## Key claim

The magnitude of tensor polarization of a deuteron beam arising from spin dichroism depends appreciably on the angular width of the detector that registers transmitted deuterons; when the detector angular width is much larger than both the mean square angle of multiple Coulomb scattering and the characteristic angle of elastic nuclear scattering, tensor polarization is determined only by total reaction cross sections, with elastic scattering processes making no contribution.

## Method

The authors solve the kinetic equation for the spin density matrix of deuterons (spin-1 particles) passing through matter, treating the scattering amplitude as a sum of Coulomb and nuclear contributions. For thin targets (thickness less than the deuteron free path), they use first-order perturbation theory; for thicker targets where multiple Coulomb scattering becomes important, they treat nuclear scattering as a perturbation on the Coulomb-dominated solution. The tensor polarization component p_zz is then computed by integrating the density matrix over the solid angle defined by the detector collimator.

## Key numbers

* For detector angular width approaching zero, tensor polarization is given by p_zz ≈ -(2/3)N(σ_tot^(±1) - σ_tot^0)z, depending on the difference in total cross sections for deuteron spin states with magnetic quantum numbers M=±1 and M=0.
* For detector angular width approaching π (full acceptance), tensor polarization becomes p_zz ≈ -(2/3)N(σ_r^(±1) - σ_r^0)z, depending only on the difference in inelastic (reaction) cross sections.
* For deuterons with energies in the range of hundreds of megaelectron-volts moving in carbon, depolarization from Coulomb scattering is approximately 10^(-3) for path lengths of order the nuclear collision length or smaller.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin dichroism and birefringence for particles with spin S ≥ 1 passing through matter, originally described by Baryshevsky (references 1,2) and experimentally observed for 5-20 MeV deuterons on carbon targets in 2005 (references 4,5,6) and for 5 GeV/c deuterons at the nuclotron in 2007 (references 7,8). The paper generalizes earlier treatments by explicitly accounting for the detector angular acceptance and multiple Coulomb scattering, showing that previous results (reference 11) on Coulomb-nuclear interference sign reversal can be understood within this framework. The results enable more accurate interpretation of planned experiments (reference 9) to measure the electric dipole moment of deuterons (references 3,10) and provide guidance for designing tensor-polarized deuteron beam sources.
