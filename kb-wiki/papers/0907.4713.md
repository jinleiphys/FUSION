# The QCD Phase Diagram from Chiral Approaches

> I show an updated QCD phase diagram with recent developments from chiral effective theories and phenomenological models. Expected signals of a QCD critical point accessible in heavy-ion collisions are also discussed. In particular, non-monotonic behavior of fluctuations associated with conserved charges is focused on.

## Key claim

The existence and location of a QCD critical point remain uncertain, as model studies show that the critical point's position depends strongly on parameters such as current quark masses and coupling strengths of hadronic interactions, and mean-field approximations may omit quantum fluctuations that could qualitatively change results.

## Method

The paper reviews the QCD phase diagram using chiral effective theories and phenomenological models, specifically the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with Polyakov loops (PNJL model) and chiral effective field theories (EFTs) constrained by nuclear equation of state data. It analyzes order parameters (chiral condensate and Polyakov loop), fluctuations of conserved charges (net quark number susceptibility), and the behavior of phase transitions at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, including the large \(N_c\) limit and spinodal instabilities.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent at the isothermal spinodal line is \(\gamma = 1/2\), with \(\chi_q \sim (\mu - \mu_c)^{-\gamma}\), while at the critical point \(\gamma = 2/3\).
- The net quark number susceptibility \(\chi_q\) is normalized to the three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 587.9\) MeV used in the \(N_f = 2\) NJL model.
- In the chiral limit (\(m_\pi = 0\) MeV), the in-medium chiral condensate vanishes at approximately \(1.5\,\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear matter density).
- For physical pion mass (\(m_\pi = 135\) MeV), the in-medium chiral condensate is stabilized and chiral symmetry restoration is delayed to much higher density than \(2\,\rho_0\).
- The spectroscopic factor for various nuclei indicates that single-particle-ness of excitations reaches approximately 70%.

## Context

This work extends prior model studies of the QCD phase diagram using chiral models (e.g., NJL and PNJL models) and Schwinger-Dyson equations, and it contrasts with lattice QCD results that are reliable only at small chemical potentials. The paper discusses the possibility of multiple critical points (including those from diquark couplings in the NJL model) and the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran (2007) based on large \(N_c\) counting. It enables future work on more realistic modeling of dense baryonic matter using chiral EFTs with mesons and baryons, and it highlights open questions about the role of quantum fluctuations and the nature of chiral symmetry restoration for baryons.
