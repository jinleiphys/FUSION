# Relativistic mean-field corrections for interactions of dark matter particles with nucleons

> We investigate the interactions of weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPS) with nucleons in nuclear medium by taking into account the effect of nuclear dynamics. We derive the nonrelativistic effective operators starting from the relativistic mean-field approximation. Certain interactions receive non-negligible corrections, which may significantly change the sensitivities of WIMP-nucleus scattering cross section to these effective operators.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the strong Lorentz scalar and vector mean fields present in atomic nuclei can modify the nonrelativistic effective operators for WIMP-nucleon interactions, leading to corrections of 21–66% for certain operators, depending on the nuclear model and location within the nucleus.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Dirac equation for a nucleon bound by Lorentz scalar and vector mean fields, then perform a nonrelativistic reduction to obtain effective WIMP-nucleon operators in the nuclear medium. They derive the modified operators for 20 relativistic interaction channels (Table 1) by replacing the free nucleon mass \(m_N\) with an effective mass \(\widetilde{m}_N\) that incorporates the scalar potential \(V_s\), vector potential \(V_v\), and binding energy \(B\). The corrections are compared to the free-nucleon results of Anand et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- The scalar and vector potentials in quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) are \(V_s \approx -400\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(V_v \approx 350\ \mathrm{MeV}\), while the quark meson coupling (QMC) model gives \(V_s \approx -190\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(V_v \approx 130\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The correction factor for certain operators is \(1/[1+(V_s - V_v)/2m_N]\), which yields corrections as large as 21–66% depending on the nuclear model.
- The effective nucleon mass in medium is defined as \(m_N^* = m_N + V_s\), and the modified operator uses \(\widetilde{m}_N = m_N^* + (B - (V_s + V_v))/2\), with binding energy \(B < 0\).

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic effective field theory framework for WIMP-nucleon interactions developed by Anand et al. (2013) and Fitzpatrick et al. (2013) by incorporating the relativistic mean-field effects from nuclear dynamics, building on the Walecka model (1974) and subsequent nuclear structure work. The corrections are analogous to those previously observed in pion production on nuclei by Noble (1975, 1979) and Bolsterli et al. (1974). The results enable more accurate predictions for direct detection experiments by accounting for nuclear medium effects, and may help reconcile results from experiments using different nuclear targets once a dark matter signal is found.
