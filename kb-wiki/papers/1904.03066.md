# Isospin effect in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies

> Within the framework of the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, the isospin effect in peripheral heavy-ion collisions has been investigated thoroughly. A coalescence approach is used for recognizing the primary fragments formed in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The secondary decay process of the fragments is described by the statistical code, GEMINI. Production mechanism and isospin effect of the projectile-like and target-like fragments are analyzed with the combined approach. It is found that the isospin migration from the high-isospin density to the low-density matter takes place in the neutron-rich nuclear reactions, i.e., $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{86}$Kr+$^{48}$Ca/$^{208}$Pb/$^{124}$Sn, $^{136}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb, $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn and $^{136}$Xe+$^{136}$Xe. A hard symmetry energy is available for creating the neutron-rich fragments, in particular in the medium-mass region. The isospin effect of the neutron to proton (n/p) ratio of the complex fragments is reduced once including the secondary decay process. However, a soft symmetry energy enhances the n/p ratio of the light particles, in particular at the kinetic energies above 15 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The isospin effect (neutron-to-proton ratio) of complex fragments is reduced after including secondary decay via the GEMINI code, but a soft symmetry energy enhances the n/p ratio of light particles at kinetic energies above 15 MeV/nucleon, while a hard symmetry energy is favorable for creating neutron-rich fragments in the medium-mass region.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies. Primary fragments were identified via a coalescence approach (relative momentum < 200 MeV/c and relative distance < 3 fm at freeze-out), and their secondary decay was treated with the statistical code GEMINI. The symmetry energy was varied using three stiffness parameters (γs = 0.5 for soft, 1 for linear, 2 for hard) to study its effect on fragment isospin composition.

- Incident energies studied: 15, 20, and 30 MeV/nucleon for reactions such as ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb, ⁸⁶Kr+⁴⁸Ca/²⁰⁸Pb/¹²⁴Sn, ¹³⁶Xe+²⁰⁸Pb, ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn, and ¹³⁶Xe+¹³⁶Xe.

- Symmetry energy at normal nuclear density: 31.5 MeV (with C_sym = 38 MeV).
- Compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter: K = 230 MeV.
- The soft symmetry energy enhances the n/p ratio of light particles at kinetic energies above 15 MeV/nucleon; the hard symmetry energy gives larger n/p ratios for primary fragments in the medium-mass region.
- For the ¹³⁶Xe+¹³⁶Xe reaction at 15 MeV/nucleon, a large ⟨N/Z⟩ ratio is obtained with the hard symmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin dynamics in Fermi-energy heavy-ion collisions, particularly the investigation of intermediate mass fragment (IMF) N/Z ratios from neck fragmentation by Fíšt et al. (2012, 2014) and Feng et al. (2016). It also builds on the LQMD model development by Feng et al. (2011, 2014, 2015, 2018) and the GEMINI statistical decay code by Charity (1988). The results enable future experiments at INFN-LNS Superconducting Cyclotron (Catania, Italy) and the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (Michigan State University) to constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities through combined measurements of heavy fragment N/Z ratios and gas-phase particle n/p yield ratios.
