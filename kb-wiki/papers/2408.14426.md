# Collisional and radiative energy loss in small systems

> We present an energy loss model which includes small system size corrections to both the radiative and elastic energy loss. Our model is used to compute the nuclear modification factor $R_{AB}$ of light and heavy flavor hadrons, averaged over realistic collision geometries for central and peripheral $A+A$ and central $p / d / {}^3\text{He} + A$ collisions at LHC and RHIC. We find that the predicted suppression in small systems is almost entirely due to elastic energy loss. Our results are keenly sensitive to the crossover between elastic energy loss calculated with hard thermal loop propagators and vacuum propagators, respectively, which leads to a large theoretical uncertainty. We find that the $R_{AB}$ is largely insensitive to the form of the elastic energy loss distribution - Gaussian or Poisson - surprisingly so in small systems where the central limit theorem is inapplicable. We present an expansion of the $R_{AB}$ in terms of the moments of the energy loss probability distribution, which allows for a rigorous understanding of the dependence of the $R_{AB}$ on the underlying energy loss distribution.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in small collision systems (p/d/³He+A), the predicted suppression of high-pT hadrons is almost entirely due to elastic energy loss, but this result is acutely sensitive to the theoretical uncertainty in the transition between hard thermal loop (HTL) and vacuum propagators used to calculate the elastic energy loss, leading to significantly different suppression predictions depending on which kernel is employed.

## Method

The authors extend the Wicks-Horowitz-Djordjevic-Gyulassy (WHDG) energy loss model by incorporating small system size corrections to both radiative and elastic energy loss. For radiative energy loss, they use the DGLV opacity expansion with a short pathlength correction that relaxes the large pathlength assumption L >> μ⁻¹. For elastic energy loss, they compare three approaches: the Braaten-Thoma (BT) kernel using HTL propagators for soft and vacuum propagators for hard momentum transfers, a pure HTL kernel using HTL propagators for all momentum transfers, and a Gaussian approximation to the HTL kernel. The nuclear modification factor R_AB is computed by averaging over realistic collision geometries for central and peripheral A+A and central p/d/³He+A collisions at LHC and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- The short pathlength corrected DGLV radiative energy loss grows linearly in L (pathlength) as opposed to the original DGLV result which grows as L²
- The BT elastic energy loss for E >> M²/T is given by dE/dz = 2 C_R π α_s² T² (1 + n_f/6) × log(2^{n_f/(2(6+n_f))} × 0.92 × √(ET)/m_g)
- The BT elastic energy loss for E << M²/T is given by dE/dz = 2 C_R π α_s² T² [(1/v) - (1-v²)/(2v²) log((1+v)/(1-v))] × log(2^{n_f/(6+n_f)} B(v) × ET/(m_g M)) × (1 + n_f/6)
- The number of active quark flavors is taken as n_f = 2 throughout the calculations
- The magnetic mass μ_M is set equal to μ (the Debye mass) in all HTL results

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Faraday 2023) which found that elastic energy loss dominates in small systems, attributed to breakdown of the central limit theorem assumption for the elastic energy loss distribution. The model builds on the WHDG energy loss model (Wicks 2005) and the DGLV opacity expansion (Djordjevic 2003, Gyulassy 2000). The short pathlength correction to radiative energy loss was derived by Kolbe (2015). The pure HTL elastic energy loss kernel follows the "HTL-X1" prescription from Wicks (2008). The paper does not compare with experimental data, stating that a future work (Faraday 2024) will perform a global fit of the strong coupling to large system data from RHIC and LHC, enabling quantitative conclusions about suppression measurements in small versus large systems.
