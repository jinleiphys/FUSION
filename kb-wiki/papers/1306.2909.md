# Interaction of eta mesons with nuclei

> Back in the mid eighties, a new branch of investigation which was related to the interaction of eta mesons with nuclei came into existence. It started with the theoretical prediction of possible exotic states of eta mesons and nuclei bound by the strong interaction and later developed into an extensive experimental program to search for such unstable states as well as understand the underlying interaction via eta meson producing reactions. The vast literature of experimental as well as theoretical works which studied various aspects of eta producing reactions such as the $π^+$ $n$ $\to ηp$, $p d \to ^3$He $η$, $p \,^6$Li $\to ^7$Be $η$ and $γ^3$He $\to η$ X, to name a few, had but one objective in mind: to understand the eta - nucleon ($ηN$) and hence the $η$-nucleus interaction which could explain the production data and confirm the existence of some $η$-mesic nuclei. In spite of these efforts, there remain uncertainties in the knowledge of the $ηN$ and hence the $η$-nucleus interaction. The present review is hence an attempt to bind together the findings in these works and draw some global and specific conclusions which can be useful for future explorations.

## Key claim

Despite decades of theoretical and experimental work, the strength of the eta-nucleon (ηN) interaction remains uncertain, with the ηN scattering length extracted from different models and data analyses spread over a wide range (0.18 ≤ Re aηN ≤ 1.03 fm and 0.16 ≤ Im aηN ≤ 0.49 fm), and no definitive confirmation of η-mesic nuclei exists.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive review of theoretical coupled-channel analyses (K-matrix, dynamical models, and chiral perturbation theory) of πN → ηN and γN → ηN reactions to extract the ηN scattering amplitude, and of few-body and many-body calculations (Faddeev equations, optical potentials, quark-meson-coupling models) to predict η-nucleus quasibound states. They also surveyed experimental searches for η-mesic nuclei using reactions such as pd → ³Heη, γ³He → π⁰pX, and p²⁷Al → ³HeX, analyzing production cross sections and final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

- ηN scattering length spread: 0.18 ≤ Re aηN ≤ 1.03 fm and 0.16 ≤ Im aηN ≤ 0.49 fm, from various theoretical models and data analyses.
- Lower limit on Im(aηN) ≥ 0.172 ± 0.009 fm, derived from the π⁻p → ηn threshold cross section σ/qη = 15.2 ± 0.8 μb/MeV using the optical theorem and detailed balance.
- Best-fit effective range parameters from one K-matrix analysis: a = 0.91 + i 0.27 fm, r₀ = -1.33 - i 0.30 fm, s = -0.15 - i 0.04 fm³.
- Scattering length from a dynamical coupled-channel model: aηN = 0.30 + i 0.18 fm (agreeing closely with an earlier 1985 result of 0.27 + i 0.22 fm).
- Chiral perturbation theory results: aηN = 0.20 + i 0.26 fm, 0.26 + i 0.25 fm, 0.18 + i 0.42 fm, and one larger value 0.54 + i 0.49 fm.
- The S₁₁(1535) resonance lies 49 MeV above the ηN threshold with a width Γ = 150 MeV; its branching ratio to ηN for D₁₃(1520) is 0.0023 ± 0.0004.
- π⁰-η mixing angle from pd → ³Hπ⁺/³Heπ⁰ analysis: 0.006 ± 0.005 radians (literature range 0.01 to 0.034 radians).
- A predicted quasibound state in the ηNN-πNN system at mass 2430 MeV with width 10-20 MeV.

## Context

This review consolidates findings from the theoretical prediction of η-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and subsequent experimental programs at BNL, LAMPF, SATURNE, COSY, and MAMI. It extends earlier reviews by covering both the elementary ηN interaction (from coupled-channel analyses by Bhalerao and Liu (1985), the GWU group, and chiral perturbation theory) and the status of η-nucleus bound states in light systems (deuteron, ³He, ⁴He) and heavier nuclei (¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb). The work enables future experiments at J-PARC, MAMI, and COSY by identifying the remaining uncertainties in the ηN scattering length and the need for more precise data on η production reactions and final-state interactions.
