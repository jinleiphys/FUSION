# Examination of the possibility of $π^0$ condensation and magnetization in freely interpenetrating nuclei

> Conditions are found, at which in nuclear matter there may appear a spatially nonuniform $p$ wave $π^0$ condensate supplemented by a spatially varying spontaneous magnetization. The pion-nucleon interaction and the anomaly contributions to magnetization are taken into account. Response of the system on external magnetic field is also considered. Then the model of nonoverlapped nucleon Fermi spheres is employed. Arguments are given in favor of possibility of the occurrence of the $π^0$-condensation and a spatially varying magnetization as well as effects of pronounced anisotropic pion fluctuations at finite pion momentum in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. Relevant effects such as response on the rotation, charged pion condensation and other are discussed.

# Examination of the possibility of π⁰ condensation and magnetization in freely interpenetrating nuclei

## Key claim

Spatially nonuniform p-wave π⁰ condensation supplemented by spatially varying spontaneous magnetization may occur in nuclear matter under specific conditions, particularly in peripheral heavy-ion collisions where nucleon Fermi spheres of colliding nuclei do not overlap. The claim is hedged by the condition that this occurs only for densities below the opaque density (n < n_op ≈ 1.2 n₀) and for collision energies in the range 0.2 ≤ E/m_N ≤ several GeV per nucleon, with the effect weakening for smaller collision energies where Fermi spheres partially overlap.

## Method

The author constructs three simplified models (Model I, II, III) for the pion self-energy in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter (N ≈ Z), incorporating the nucleon particle-hole term, Δ isobar-nucleon hole term, and a residual regular part, with nucleon-nucleon and Δ-nucleon hole correlations included via Landau-Migdal parameters. For the non-equilibrium case of nonoverlapped Fermi spheres, the pole part of the equilibrium pion self-energy is modified by replacing the Lindhard function with a sum of two shifted Lindhard functions, one for each Fermi sphere, and the squared effective pion gap is minimized to find critical densities. The Gibbs free energy density of the π⁰ condensate is then constructed and minimized in the presence of spontaneous magnetization and external magnetic fields, including contributions from the pion-nucleon interaction and the Wess-Zumino-Witten axial anomaly term.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ ≈ 0.48 m³_π, with m_π ≈ 140 MeV
- Opaque density for peripheral collisions: n_op ≈ 1.2 n₀ (for d = 4 fm, impact parameter b ≈ (1–1.7)R)
- Critical density for liquid-like pion condensation in nonoverlapped Fermi sphere model: n_c1 ≈ 0.034 n₀ from simplified expression, or n_c1 ≈ (0.04–0.05) n₀ with correction terms
- Landé factors: g_p ≈ 5.58, g_n ≈ -3.85, with amplification factor ζ = g_p - g_n ≈ 9.43
- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 92 MeV
- Nucleon Σ term: Σ ≈ 45 MeV (value used in numerical evaluations)
- Rotational frequency in peripheral Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV: Ω ≈ (9 ± 1) × 10²¹ Hz ≈ 0.05 m_π
- Maximum magnetic field estimate: h_max ~ h_VA Z|e|, with H_π = m_π²/|e| ≈ 3.5 × 10¹⁸ G
- London moment estimate for charged pion condensate: h_L ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁵ Ω, giving h ~ 3 × 10¹⁷ G for Ω ≈ 10²² Hz
- Landau-Migdal parameter range: g'(n₀) ≈ 0.6–1.1, vacuum value g'_0 ≈ 1/3
- Δ isobar energy: ω_Δ(k=0) ≈ 2.1 m_π
- πNΔ coupling: f_πNΔ ≈ 2.1 m_π⁻¹
- Coefficient b̃ for pion dispersion at n ≈ n₀: b̃ ≈ 0.3 (Model I), b̃ ≈ 0.2 (Model II), b̃ ≈ 0.15–0.2 (phenomenological Kisslinger parametrization)
- s-wave pion condensation critical density: n_c,s ≈ (1.4–2.5) n₀ for on-shell parametrization, shifting to ~4 n₀ with modified parametrization

## Context

This work extends the long-standing line of research on p-wave pion condensation in nuclear matter initiated by Migdal (1971), Scalapino (1972), Sawyer (1972), and Baym (1973), and later developed within chiral σ models by Campbell (1974), Dautry, and Voskresensky (1978). It builds specifically on the nonoverlapped Fermi sphere model of Pirner (1994), who studied pion instabilities in peripheral heavy-ion collisions and predicted peaks in pion production at large rapidity. The paper also connects to studies of the axial anomaly contribution to magnetization by Son and Stephanov, and to the chiral-wave condensate analysis of Hashimoto (2015) in neutron-rich matter. The results enable experimental checks of pion condensation effects in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at GSI and in rainbow scattering experiments, as previously discussed by the Ogloblin group (2003), and may explain features observed in pion cross sections at impact parameter b ≈ 7.9 fm reported by Bottcher.
