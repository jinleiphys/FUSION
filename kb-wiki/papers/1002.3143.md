# Quark pair creation in color electric fields and effects of magnetic fields

> The time evolution of a system where a uniform and classical SU(3) color electric field and quantum fields of quarks are dynamically coupled with each other is studied focusing on non-perturbative pair creation and its back reaction. We characterize the color direction of the electric field in a gauge invariant way, and investigate its dependence. Momentum distributions of created quarks show plasma oscillation as well as quantum effects such as the Pauli blocking and interference. Pressure of the system is also calculated, and we show that pair creation moderates degree of anisotropy of pressure. Furthermore, enhancement of pair creation and induction of chiral charge under a color magnetic field which is parallel to the electric field are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the time evolution of quark pair creation from a uniform SU(3) color electric field, including back reaction, exhibits plasma oscillation, Pauli blocking, and interference effects, with the color direction of the field (characterized by a gauge-invariant parameter) affecting the dynamics. The claim is hedged by the restriction to spatially homogeneous, Abelianized color fields and the use of an instantaneous particle picture as a working definition rather than a fundamental particle concept.

## Method

The author solves the coupled Dirac and Maxwell equations for a spatially uniform, Abelianized SU(3) color electric field interacting with quantum quark fields (N_f = 3 flavors, equal mass m), using canonical quantization with a time-dependent Bogoliubov transformation to define instantaneous particle states. The color direction of the electric field is parametrized by an angle θ (0 ≤ θ ≤ π/6) related to the second Casimir invariant, and the system is evolved numerically from an initial vacuum state with the electric field switched on at t = 0. The momentum distributions, color current density, electric field strength, and pressure are computed, with the polarization current separated from the conduction current.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter a = m²/(2gE₀) is set to 0.01 or 0, with g = 1 and θ = 0 for the primary numerical results.
- The occupation number of created quarks is approximately exp(-π m_T² / |w_i| g E₀), where m_T = √(m² + p_x² + p_y²) is the transverse mass and w_i are the color weights (e.g., w₁ = 1/2 for "blue" quarks at θ = 0).
- For zero-mode particles (m = 0, p_T = 0), the occupation number saturates exactly to 1 due to Pauli blocking.
- The plasma oscillation frequency is approximated as Ω = √[ (4N_f/(2π)³) E₀ Σ_i |w_i g|³ exp(-π m²/|w_i| g E₀) ].
- The characteristic time t_c when the electric field first reaches zero is t_c = (π/2) √[ (2π)³ / (4N_f E₀ Σ_i |w_i g|³ exp(-π m²/|w_i| g E₀)) ].
- The polarization current contains a factor m_T in its integrand, so zero transverse mass modes do not contribute to it.

## Context

This work extends field-theoretical treatments of the Schwinger mechanism with back reaction from the Abelian case (Tanji, 2009) to non-Abelian SU(3) color fields, incorporating the color direction dependence characterized by the Casimir invariant (Nayak, 2005a; Nayak, 2005b). It builds on earlier studies of back reaction using kinetic theory (Gatoff, 1987; Asakawa, 1995; Dawson, 1999) and quantum field theory (Kluger, 1991-1993; Kluger, 1998; Bloch, 1999; Vinnik, 2001), and addresses the lack of field-theoretical treatment for color electric fields. The results enable further study of isotropization of pressure in heavy-ion collisions and the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev, 2007b), and are extended to include parallel color magnetic fields, which enhance pair creation and induce chiral charge.
