# The $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ K ~ Ξ$ decay and the higher order chiral terms of the meson baryon interaction

> We study the weak decay of the $Λ_b$ into $ J/ψ~ K ~ Ξ$ and $J/ψ~ η~ Λ$ states, and relate these processes to the $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ \bar K ~N$ decay mode. The elementary weak transition at the quark level proceeds via the creation of a $J/ψ$ meson and an excited $sud$ system with $I=0$, which upon hadronization leads to $\bar K N$ or $ηΛ$ pairs. These states undergo final state interaction in coupled channels and produce a final meson-baryon pair. The $K Ξ$ state only occurs via rescattering, hence making the $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ K ~ Ξ$ process very sensitive to the details of the meson-baryon interaction in strangeness $S=-1$ and isospin $I=0$. We show that the corresponding invariant mass distribution is dominated by the next-to-leading order terms of the chiral interaction. The $I=0$ selectivity of this decay, and its large sensitivity to the higher order terms, makes its measurement very useful and complementary to the $K^- p \to K Ξ$ cross section data. The rates of the $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ K ~ Ξ$ and $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ η~ Λ$ invariant mass distributions are sizable compared to those of the $Λ_b \to J/ψ~ \bar K ~N$ decay, which is measured experimentally, thus, we provide arguments for an experimental determination of these decay modes that will help us understand better the chiral dynamics at higher energies.

## Key claim

The $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~K~\Xi$ decay filters isospin $I=0$ in the final state and is dominated by the next-to-leading order (NLO) terms of the chiral Lagrangian, making it highly sensitive to the higher-order chiral dynamics, a sensitivity that the abstract states but does not emphasize is conditional on the $K\Xi$ pair being produced only via rescattering, not directly.

## Method

The authors study the weak decay $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~K~\Xi$ and $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~\eta~\Lambda$ by modeling the elementary weak transition at the quark level, where a $J/\psi$ meson and an excited $sud$ system with $I=0$ are created. This system hadronizes into primary $\bar{K}N$ and $\eta\Lambda$ pairs, which then undergo final-state interaction via coupled-channel scattering using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a kernel from the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian up to NLO. Two models from Feijoo et al. (2015) are used: Model 1 uses only the NLO chiral dynamics, while Model 2 adds explicit $\Sigma(2030)$ and $\Sigma(2250)$ resonances; both are fitted to $K^-p$ scattering data including $K\Xi$ production.

## Key numbers

- The $\chi^2_{\rm d.o.f.}$ for Model 1 is 1.48 and for Model 2 is 1.05, compared to 2.26 for a fit without NLO terms (WT+RES).
- The ratio of $\pi\Sigma$ to $\bar{K}N$ invariant mass distributions at their maxima is 4.9 for Model 1 and 3.5 for Model 2.
- The $I=0$ contribution to $K^-p \to K\Xi$ cross sections peaks around 2300 MeV for Model 1 and around 2400 MeV for Model 2.
- The $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~\eta~\Lambda$ mass distribution is about twenty times larger in magnitude than the $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~K~\Xi$ one.
- The $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~K^+\Xi^-$ distribution from Model 1 shows a peak between 2250 MeV and 2300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to meson-baryon scattering in the $S=-1$, $I=0$ sector, building on the models of Feijoo et al. (2015) that incorporated $K\Xi$ production data to constrain NLO parameters. It complements studies of $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~\bar{K}N$ decays by Roca et al. (2015) and the experimental measurements by CDF (2010) and LHCb (2013, 2014, 2015). The $I=0$ selectivity of these decays provides information orthogonal to $K^-p$ scattering data (which mixes $I=0$ and $I=1$), and the sizable predicted rates relative to the measured $\Lambda_b \to J/\psi~\bar{K}N$ mode motivate experimental determination to better constrain chiral dynamics at higher energies.
