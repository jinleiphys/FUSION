# Jet quenching in strongly coupled plasma

> We present calculations in which an energetic light quark shoots through a finite slab of strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma, with thickness $L$, focussing on what comes out on the other side. We find that even when the "jets" that emerge from the plasma have lost a substantial fraction of their energy they look in almost all respects like "jets" in vacuum with the same reduced energy. The one possible exception is that the opening angle of the "jet" is larger after passage through the slab of plasma than before. Along the way, we obtain a fully geometric characterization of energy loss in the strongly coupled plasma and show that $dE_{\rm out}/dL \propto L^2/\sqrt{x^2_{\rm stop}-L^2}$, where $E_{\rm out}$ is the energy of the "jet" that emerges from the slab of plasma and $x_{\rm stop}$ is the (previously known) stopping distance for the light quark in an infinite volume of plasma.

## Key claim

Even when an energetic light quark traversing a finite slab of strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma loses a substantial fraction of its energy, the "jet" that emerges looks in almost all respects like a "jet" in vacuum with the same reduced energy, with the one possible exception being that its opening angle is larger after passage through the slab than before.

## Method

The authors model an energetic light quark as a falling string in an asymptotically AdS$_5$ geometry dual to a finite slab of strongly coupled ${\cal N}=4$ SYM plasma at constant temperature $T$, with the slab occupying the region $x \in (0,L)$. They construct null string solutions from a congruence of null geodesics, solve the string equations of motion perturbatively about the null configuration, and compute the energy transported along geodesics on the string worldsheet to determine how much energy emerges from the slab and how the jet's opening angle changes.

## Key numbers

- The stopping distance $x_{\rm stop}$ for a light quark in an infinite volume of plasma is given by $x_{\rm stop} = -u_h \, _2 F_1(\frac{1}{4},\frac{1}{2};\frac{5}{4};\frac{1}{\zeta(\sigma_*)}) + \frac{u_h^2}{u_{\rm in}(\sigma_*)} \, _2 F_1(\frac{1}{4},\frac{1}{2};\frac{5}{4};\frac{u_h^4}{\zeta(\sigma_*)\, u_{\rm in}(\sigma_*)^4})$, where $u_h = 1/(\pi T)$.
- For a specific numerical example with slab thickness $L = 10/(\pi T)$, initial endpoint angle $\sigma_* = 0.01$, and $x_0 = -5$, the ratio of outgoing to incoming energy is $E_{\rm out}/E_{\rm in} = 0.643$ from the exact formula and 0.57 from the $x_0 \to -\infty$ approximation.
- For a more realistic case with the quark produced next to the slab at $x_0 = -10^{-3}$, $\sigma_* = 0.025$, and slab thickness $L = 8/(\pi T)$, the ratio is $E_{\rm out}/E_{\rm in} = 0.757$ (exact) and 0.780 (approximation), corresponding to an incident jet energy $E_{\rm in} = 87.0 \sqrt{\lambda} \,\pi T$ and outgoing jet energy $E_{\rm out} = 65.9 \sqrt{\lambda} \,\pi T$.
- The rate of energy loss satisfies $dE_{\rm out}/dL \propto L^2/\sqrt{x^2_{\rm stop}-L^2}$, showing a Bragg peak where energy loss is greatest for quarks that are fully stopped.

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of jet quenching in strongly coupled plasma, building on prior calculations of energy loss for a beam of gluons in ${\cal N}=4$ SYM plasma by Chesler (2011) and on the stopping distance calculations by Gubser (2008) and Chesler (2008). The results are consistent with qualitative observations from LHC experiments (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011, 2012) that quenched jets emerging from heavy ion collisions look remarkably similar to vacuum jets with the same reduced energy. The calculation enables future systematic exploration of how jet modification depends on the initial conditions of the string (parameters $u_0$ and $\sigma_*$) and provides a fully geometric characterization of energy loss that could be compared with weak-coupling analyses such as those by Casalderrey-Solana (2010) and the review by Mehtar-Tani (2013).
