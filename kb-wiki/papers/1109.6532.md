# Computing Early-time Dynamics in Heavy Ion Collisions: Status, Problems and Prospects

> We discuss some recent developments towards a quantitative understanding of the production and early-time evolution of bulk quark-gluon matter in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory provides a unified, systematic non-perturbative (albeit weak coupling) approach to compute key features of e+A, p+A, and A+A collisions, but comparisons to data are not yet fully quantitative.

## Method

The paper reviews the CGC framework for describing high-energy nuclear wavefunctions as classical color fields, then discusses how these fields collide and decohere to produce the Glasma. It presents the JIMWLK renormalization group equations for the evolution of multi-point correlators, and outlines a formalism combining classical Yang-Mills solutions with Gaussian quantum fluctuations to compute inclusive quantities. The final section develops a kinetic theory approach using the Boltzmann equation in the small-angle approximation to model the transition from the overpopulated Glasma to a thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(Q_S(x)\) grows with decreasing longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\), with \(Q_S \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) allowing weak coupling descriptions.
- The classical Yang-Mills energy-momentum tensor in the Glasma is \(T^{\mu\nu}={\rm diag}\,(\epsilon,\epsilon,\epsilon,-\epsilon)\), giving negative longitudinal pressure at very early times.
- For a fixed box, the thermalization time is \(t_{\rm th} \sim {1 \over Q_{\rm s}}\left( {1\over \alpha_{\rm s}} \right)^{\frac{7}{4}}\).
- For an expanding system with anisotropy parameter \(\delta\), the thermalization time is \(\left( \frac{t_{\rm th}}{t_0} \right)\sim \left(\frac{1}{\alpha_{\rm s}}\right)^{ \frac{7}{3-\delta}}\).
- The gluon number density in the expanding case scales as \(n_{\rm g}\sim \frac{Q_{\rm s}^3}{\alpha_{\rm s}} \left( \frac{t_0}{t}\right)^{(6+5\delta)/7}\).
- The Debye mass in the expanding case scales as \(m^2\sim Q_{\rm s}^2 \left( \frac{t_0}{t}\right)^{(5+3\delta)/7}\).
- The condensate energy density relative to gluon energy density scales as \(\frac{\epsilon_{\rm c}}{\epsilon_{\rm g}}\sim \left( \frac{t_0}{t}\right)^{(5-11\delta)/14}\).

## Context

This review extends the CGC framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the JIMWLK hierarchy by Jalilian-Marian, Iancu, McLerran, Weigert, Leonidov, and Kovner (1997-2002). It discusses the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2000) and the role of plasma instabilities studied by Romatschke and Strickland (2005-2006). The kinetic theory analysis follows Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2011) and connects to the 2PI formalism of Berges (2008). The work enables future non-perturbative non-equilibrium simulations of the Glasma-to-QGP transition and provides a framework for understanding long-range rapidity correlations (the "ridge") observed at RHIC and LHC.
