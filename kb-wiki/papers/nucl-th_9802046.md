# Canonical Strangeness Enhancement

> According to recent experimental data and theoretical developments we discuss three distinct topics related to strangeness enhancement in nuclear reactions. We investigate the compatibility of multi-strange particle ratios measured in a restricted phase space with thermal model parameters extracted recently in 4pi. We study the canonical suppression as a possible reason for the observed strangeness enhancement and argue that a connection between QGP formation and the undersaturation of strangeness is not excluded.

## Key claim

The observed strangeness enhancement from p+p to A+A collisions cannot be fully explained by canonical suppression (volume effects) alone when using the standard volume per participating baryon of 5 fm³; however, if a smaller volume of 2.5 fm³ per baryon is assumed, canonical suppression alone could account for the enhancement, but this scenario conflicts with measured φ meson yields.

## Method

The authors compared thermal model predictions for multi-strange baryon ratios (Λ, Ξ, Ω) with experimental data from WA94 (S+S), WA85 (S+W), and WA97 (Pb+Pb) at SPS energies, applying acceptance correction factors derived from hydrodynamic momentum distributions. They then calculated canonical suppression effects on strange-to-non-strange ratios (Kₛ⁰/h⁻ and Eₛ) as a function of participating baryons, using thermal parameters T = 180 MeV and γₛ = 0.7 with two different volume assumptions. Finally, they solved an implicit equation equating the strange quark-to-entropy ratio in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to that in a hadron gas to determine the γₛ value that would result from QGP hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Thermal model parameters from Becattini (1997): T = 180 ± 10 MeV, γₛ = 0.7 ± 0.05, μ_B/T = 1.25 ± 0.1
- For p+p collisions, γₛ = 0.5 ± 0.05 (Becattini, 1997) versus γₛ = 0.7 ± 0.05 for A+A collisions (Becattini, 1997)
- With volume V = 5 fm³ × B_part, canonical suppression accounts for only half of the observed strangeness enhancement
- With volume V = 2.5 fm³ × B_part, canonical suppression alone can account for the observed enhancement
- For Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV, the measured Ω/Ξ ratio is 0.192 ± 0.024, while the thermal model predicts 0.126 ± 0.011
- The φ meson yield in p+p collisions deviates from a thermal fit by 12σ for γₛ ≈ 0.7, compared to 4σ for γₛ = 0.5
- The strange quark mass used in QGP calculations is mₛ = 160 ± 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis of Becattini (1997) by testing its predictions against multi-strange baryon data from WA94, WA85, and WA97 that were previously omitted due to limited phase space measurements. The paper addresses the long-standing question of whether canonical suppression (volume effects) or QGP formation is responsible for the strangeness enhancement observed by Gazdzicki (1996) and others. The argument connecting γₛ undersaturation to QGP hadronization, following Gazdzicki (1997) and Redlich (1998), suggests that the observed γₛ ≈ 0.7 in A+A collisions could be a signature of partonic matter, but the authors note that non-perturbative QCD effects prevent a definitive conclusion.
