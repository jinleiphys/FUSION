# Nuclear Modification Factor in d+Au Collisions: Onset of Suppression in the Color Glass Condensate

> We perform a quantitative analysis of the nuclear modification factor in deuteron--gold collisions R(dAu) within the Color Glass Condensate approach, and compare our results with the recent data from RHIC experiments. Our model leads to Cronin enhancement at mid-rapidity, while at forward rapidities it predicts strong suppression of R(dAu) at all pT due to low-x evolution. We demonstrate that our results are consistent with the data for dAu charged hadron spectra, R(dAu) and R(CP) recently reported for rapidities in the interval eta=0--3.2 by the BRAHMS experiment at RHIC. We also make a prediction for R(pA) at mid-rapidity in pA collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach predicts a Cronin enhancement of the nuclear modification factor R(dAu) at mid-rapidity in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, but a strong suppression at forward rapidities due to low-x quantum evolution; the model is consistent with BRAHMS data for rapidities eta = 0 to 3.2, provided that nonperturbative momentum broadening (kappa = 0 to 1 GeV) and isospin effects are accounted for.

## Method

The authors model inclusive hadron production in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV using a dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude that interpolates between the McLerran-Venugopalan model at low rapidity and the geometric scaling regime at high rapidity, with an anomalous dimension gamma inspired by BFKL evolution. Gluon and valence quark production cross sections are expressed in terms of the adjoint and quark dipole amplitudes, respectively, and are convolved with leading-order fragmentation functions and parton distribution functions. The saturation scale Q_s^2(y) = 0.13 GeV^2 exp(lambda y) N_coll is taken from fits to DIS data, with lambda=0.3, and a nonperturbative shift kappa^2 A^{1/3} is added to the saturation scale in the Glauber exponents to account for low-energy hadronic rescatterings.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at RHIC: Q_s^2(y) = 0.13 GeV^2 exp(lambda y) N_coll, with lambda = 0.3 and Lambda = 0.6 GeV, fixed by DIS data.
- The evolution onset rapidity is fitted to y_0 = 0.6, with parameter c = 4 in the anomalous dimension formula.
- Nonperturbative momentum broadening scale kappa is taken as either 0 or 1 GeV, with mu = 1 GeV as the infrared cutoff.
- At mid-rapidity (eta < y_0), the Cronin maximum appears at p_T ~ 2 to 3 GeV; at forward rapidities (eta > y_0), R(dAu) is suppressed at all p_T.
- For LHC pA collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV, the model predicts R(pA) at mid-rapidity similar to R(dAu) at eta=3.2 at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative CGC predictions of Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2003) and the authors' own earlier analysis (Kharzeev, Kovchegov, Tuchin, 2003) to a quantitative comparison with BRAHMS data at RHIC. It contrasts with the traditional multiple-scattering picture (Accardi et al., 2004; Barnafoldi et al., 2004), which would predict increasing Cronin enhancement at forward rapidities. The model enables predictions for pA collisions at the LHC, where mid-rapidity suppression would indicate that initial-state CGC dynamics contribute to the high-p_T suppression in AA collisions alongside jet quenching.
