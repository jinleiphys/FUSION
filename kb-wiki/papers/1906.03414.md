# Overlap of electron shells in $β$ and double-$β$ decays

> The $β$ and double-$β$ decay channels, which are not accompanied by excitation of the electron shells, are suppressed due to the nonorthogonality of the electron wave functions of the parent and daughter atoms. The effect is sensitive to the contribution of the outer electron shells. Since valence electrons participate in chemical bonding and collectivize in metals, the decay rates of the unstable nuclides are modified when they are embedded in a host material. Core electrons are less affected by the environment, and their overlap amplitudes are more stable. The suppression effect is estimated for $ β^- $ decay of $^{87}$Kr, electron capture in $^{163}$Ho, and $2β^-$ decays of $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe. The overlap amplitude of the electron shells enters the relationship between the half-life of neutrinoless $2β$ decay and the effective electron neutrino Majorana mass.

## Key claim

The suppression of beta and double-beta decay channels without electron-shell excitation due to nonorthogonality of parent and daughter electron wave functions is sensitive to the contribution of outer electron shells, and the effect modifies decay rates when unstable nuclides are embedded in a host material; the overlap amplitude enters the relationship between neutrinoless double-beta decay half-life and the effective electron neutrino Majorana mass, but for decays with very low total overlap amplitude (such as Ge and Te) the dominant channels involve multiple excited electron shells, taking the process beyond the simple relationship.

## Method

The authors computed overlap amplitudes of individual electron levels (OAELs) using a relativistic Dirac equation approach with effective nuclear charges determined from semi-empirical electron binding energies from Larkins (1977). They also developed analytic nonrelativistic Coulomb models with and without nuclear charge shielding, and compared results to Dirac-Fock calculations from Amand and Faessler (2015). The total overlap amplitude of the electron shells was obtained by multiplying OAELs over all occupied electron levels for each parent-daughter atom pair.

## Key numbers

- For EC in $^{163}$Ho, the survival probability of the electron shells without excitation is $K_Z^2 \approx 0.95$
- For $2\beta^-$ decay of $^{76}$Ge, the survival probability equals $K_Z^2 \approx 0.75$
- Total overlap amplitude $K_Z$ for $^{76}$Ge: $6.2 \times 10^{-3}$; for $^{130}$Te: $1.4 \times 10^{-4}$
- Total overlap amplitude $K_Z$ for $^{100}$Mo: 0.56; for $^{136}$Xe: 0.22; for $^{87}$Kr: 0.89; for $^{163}$Ho: 0.53
- Core-shell overlap amplitude $K_Z^{\text{core shells}}$ for $^{76}$Ge: 0.26; for $^{130}$Te: 0.069; for $^{100}$Mo: 0.58; for $^{136}$Xe: 0.36
- For neutrinoless $2\beta^-$ decays of $^{100}$Mo and $^{136}$Xe, neutrino mass limits increase by factors of $1/K_Z = 1.8$ and 4.6, respectively
- Environment-independent scaling factors from core shells: $1/K_Z^{\text{core shells}} = 1.7$ for $^{100}$Mo and 2.8 for $^{136}$Xe
- OAEL for the 1s level in $^{163}$Ho EC: 0.999916 (nonrelativistic) and 0.99987 (relativistic with shielding)
- OAEL for the 4s level in $^{163}$Ho EC: 0.997287 (with shielding)
- GERDA and KamLAND-Zen give limits $|m_{\beta\beta}| < 120-260$ meV and $61-165$ meV, respectively
- NEMO-3 and CUORE give limits $|m_{\beta\beta}| < 330-620$ meV and $270-760$ meV, respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electron wave function overlap effects in beta decay, including calculations by Amand and Faessler (2015) for EC in $^{163}$Ho and by Faessler (2016) and Brass (2018) on excited states. The paper provides systematic estimates across multiple isotopes relevant to current neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments: GERDA (Ge), CUORE (Te), KamLAND-Zen (Xe), NEMO-3, CUPID-Mo, and AMoRE (Mo), and the ECHo experiment (Ho). The results enable improved extraction of the effective Majorana neutrino mass from half-life measurements by accounting for electron-shell overlap suppression, and identify cases (Ge, Te) where the simple relationship between half-life and neutrino mass breaks down due to dominance of excited-shell decay channels.
