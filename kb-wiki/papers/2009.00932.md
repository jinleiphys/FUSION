# Bridging the quartet and pair pictures of isovector proton-neutron pairing

> The formal implications of a quartet coherent state ansatz for proton-neutron pairing are analyzed. Its nonlinear annihilation operators, which generalize the BCS linear quasiparticle operators, are computed in the quartetting case. Their structure is found to generate nontrivial relationships between the many body correlation functions. The intrinsic structure of the quartet coherent state is detailed, as it hints to the precise correspondence between the quartetting picture and the symmetry restored pair condensate picture for the proton-neutron pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the quartet condensate (QCM) state for isovector proton-neutron pairing in even-even N=Z nuclei is analytically equivalent to the isospin- and particle-number-projected BCS state, while for N>Z systems the equivalence is only numerical, with overlaps exceeding 0.999 and relative errors in correlation energies below 0.5%.

## Method

The authors analyze a quartet coherent state ansatz (QBCS) for the isovector pairing Hamiltonian, computing its nonlinear annihilation operators that generalize BCS linear quasiparticle operators. They use a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to express the QBCS state as a superposition of BCS states, then perform analytic isospin projection on the BCS state to recover the QCM state for T=0. For N>Z systems, they perform variation-after-projection calculations using a picket-fence model of eight equidistant doubly degenerate levels with a state-independent interaction, comparing projected BCS states to QCM states.

## Key numbers

- Overlaps between projected BCS and QCM states for N>Z systems do not decrease lower than 0.999
- Relative errors in correlation energies between projected BCS and QCM states do not exceed 0.5%
- For the case of equal pair and quartet mixing amplitudes (x=y), overlaps with the QCM state (having x≠y) are always greater than 0.98, and errors in correlation energies are always smaller than 8%
- The model uses eight doubly degenerate levels with single particle energies ε_k = k-1

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation models (QCM) developed by Sandulescu et al. (2012) and the exact quartet solutions by Sambataro et al. (2020), building on the earlier recognition by Beliaev et al. (1960) that alpha-like quartets are needed beyond BCS. The paper bridges the quartet picture with the symmetry-restored BCS approach of Kyotoku (1979, 1987) and Raduta et al. (2000-2012), showing their equivalence for N=Z systems. The results enable future applications of the quantum many-body bootstrap approach to quartet condensation and extensions to isovector-isoscalar pairing Hamiltonians.
