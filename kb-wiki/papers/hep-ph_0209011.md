# Light-flavor sea-quark distributions in the nucleon in the SU(3) chiral quark soliton model (I) -- phenomenological predictions --

> Theoretical predictions are given for the light-flavor sea-quark distributions including the strange quark ones on the basis of the flavor SU(3) version of the chiral quark soliton model. Careful account is taken here of the SU(3) symmetry breaking effects due to the mass difference between the strange and nonstrange quarks. This effective mass difference $Δm_s$ between the strange and nonstrange quarks is the only one parameter necessary for the flavor SU(3) generalization of the model. A particular emphasis of study is put on the {\it light-flavor sea-quark asymmetry} as exemplified by the observables $\bar{d} (x) - \bar{u} (x), \bar{d} (x) / \bar{u} (x), Δ\bar{u} (x) - Δ\bar{d} (x)$ as well as on the {\it particle-antiparticle asymmetry} of the strange quark distributions represented by $s (x) - \bar{s} (x), s (x) / \bar{s} (x), Δs (x) - Δ\bar{s} (x)$ etc. As for the unpolarized sea-quark distributions, the predictions of the model seem qualitatively consistent with the available phenomenological information provided by the NMC data for $\bar{d} (x) - \bar{u} (x)$, the E866 data for $\bar{d} (x) / \bar{u} (x)$, the CCFR data and Barone et al.'s fit for $s (x) / \bar{s} (x)$ etc. The model is shown to give several unique predictions also for the spin-dependent sea-quark distribution, such that $Δs (x) \ll Δ\bar{s}(x) \lesssim 0$ and $Δ\bar{d}(x) < 0 < Δ\bar{u}(x)$, although the verification of these predictions must await more elaborate experimental investigations in the near future.

## Key claim

The SU(3) chiral quark soliton model predicts that particle-antiparticle asymmetry of strange-quark excitations in the nucleon most likely exists, with the magnitude of asymmetry more profound for longitudinally polarized distributions than for unpolarized ones, though the verification of spin-dependent predictions must await more elaborate experimental investigations.

## Method

The author generalizes the flavor SU(2) chiral quark soliton model to SU(3) by embedding the SU(2) hedgehog mean-field into an SU(3) matrix and quantizing the collective rotational motion semiclassically. SU(3) symmetry breaking due to the strange-nonstrange quark mass difference Δm_s is treated via first-order perturbation theory, and the resulting parton distribution functions at the model scale (Q² = 0.30 GeV²) are evolved to higher Q² using next-to-leading order DGLAP equations from the Saga group.

## Key numbers

- The dynamically generated quark mass M is fixed at 375 MeV, with the Pauli-Villars cutoff mass M_PV ≈ 562 MeV determined from the relation (N_c M² / 4π²) ln(M_PV² / M²) = f_π² using f_π = 93 MeV.
- The effective strange-nonstrange quark mass difference Δm_s is varied within 60 MeV < Δm_s < 170 MeV, with the best overall agreement found for Δm_s = 100 MeV.
- The initial energy scale for Q²-evolution is set to Q² = 0.30 GeV².
- The scalar charge of the nucleon σ̄ is taken as approximately 7.5, obtained from pion-nucleon sigma term analysis.
- Theoretical unpolarized s- and s̄-quark distributions are compared with CCFR data at Q² = 4 GeV² and Q² = 20 GeV².
- The difference s(x) - s̄(x) at Q² = 20 GeV² is compared with Barone et al.'s global fit.

## Context

This work extends the flavor SU(2) chiral quark soliton model predictions for sea-quark distributions (Wakamatsu and Kubota, 1998; Wakamatsu and Yoshida, 1991) to include strange quarks via SU(3) generalization, building on the soliton model framework of Diakonov, Petrov, and Pobylitsa (1988). The predictions for unpolarized sea-quark asymmetry are qualitatively consistent with NMC data for d̄(x) - ū(x), E866 data for d̄(x)/ū(x), and CCFR data for s(x)/s̄(x). The model's predictions for spin-dependent sea-quark distributions, such as Δs(x) ≪ Δs̄(x) ≲ 0 and Δd̄(x) < 0 < Δū(x), await experimental verification and are qualitatively supported by the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model argument of Brodsky and Ma (1996).
