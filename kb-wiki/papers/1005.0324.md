# On Fluctuations of Conserved Charges : Lattice Results Versus Hadron Resonance Gas

> We compare recent lattice results on fluctuations and correlations of strangeness, baryon number and electric charge obtained with p4 improved staggered action with the prediction of hadron resonance gas model. We show that hadron resonance gas can describe these fluctuations reasonably well if the hadron properties are as calculated on the lattice.

# On Fluctuations of Conserved Charges: Lattice Results Versus Hadron Resonance Gas

## Key claim
The hadron resonance gas model can describe lattice QCD results for fluctuations of conserved charges reasonably well, but only if the hadron masses in the model are modified to account for discretization effects (lattice artifacts) present in the lattice calculations. The agreement is not uniform across all observables; baryon number–strangeness correlations are not well described by this approach.

## Method
The authors calculated quadratic and fourth-order fluctuations of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, as well as baryon number–strangeness correlations, using the hadron resonance gas model with hadron masses adjusted to incorporate lattice discretization effects. The mass modifications were based on formulas derived from asqtad action calculations, with doubled quadratic splittings in the pseudo-scalar meson sector to account for the p4 action. All resonances with mass up to 2.5 GeV were included, and a cutoff mass of 1.9 GeV was used for excited baryon states. The results were compared to lattice data obtained with the p4 improved staggered action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4 and Nτ = 6.

## Key numbers
- Quadratic fluctuations of strangeness and electric charge calculated on the lattice with p4 action fall well below the hadron resonance gas curve obtained with physical hadron masses.
- A cutoff mass of m_cut^B = 1.9 GeV was used for modifying excited baryon state masses in all calculations.
- Lattice calculations were performed on Nτ = 4 and Nτ = 6 lattices using the p4 action.
- All resonances with mass up to 2.5 GeV were included in the hadron resonance gas calculations.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Huovinen and Petreczky, 2009) of baryon number and strangeness fluctuations using asqtad action data to now include electric charge fluctuations and correlations, and to compare with p4 action lattice results from the HotQCD collaboration (2009). The study addresses the discrepancy noted in earlier lattice calculations where fluctuations differed significantly from standard hadron resonance gas predictions, which was attributed to lattice discretization effects on the hadron spectrum (Huovinen and Petreczky, 2009). The results are important for constructing realistic equations of state at finite baryon density for hydrodynamic models, similar to what was done for zero baryon density in the authors' previous work. The approach does not give a good description of baryon number–strangeness fluctuations, indicating that a more refined treatment of cutoff effects in the baryon sector is needed.
