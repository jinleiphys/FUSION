# Form factors and photoproduction amplitudes

> We examine the use of phenomenological form factors in tree level amplitudes for meson photoproduction. Two common recipes are shown to be fundamentally incorrect. An alternate form consistent with gauge invariance and crossing symmetry is proposed.

# Form factors and photoproduction amplitudes

## Key claim
Two common recipes for inserting phenomenological form factors into tree-level meson photoproduction amplitudes are fundamentally incorrect. The authors propose an alternate form that satisfies both gauge invariance and crossing symmetry, but note that the resulting amplitude is not unique and that the constraints only ensure the gauge-violating term can be cancelled by a contact term.

## Method
The authors examine the insertion of strong form factors at the πNN vertex in Born diagrams for meson photoproduction, specifically analyzing the invariant amplitude A₂ which is unmodified in Ohta's minimal-substitution prescription. They identify a flaw in Haberzettl's proposed form factor for A₂, showing it introduces uncancelled pole contributions, and derive the constraints that a crossing-symmetric form factor must satisfy to allow cancellation by a contact term. The method is applied to η′ photoproduction and compared with earlier approaches.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff mass Λ = 1 GeV used in the monopole form factor F₁(s) = [1 + (s−m²)²/Λ⁴]⁻¹ for kaon photoproduction calculations
- KΛN coupling constant of 7.5 used in the kaon photoproduction comparison
- η′ coupling constant g_{η′} in the range 1 ≤ g_{η′} ≤ 6 as estimated in prior work (Nimai, Mukhopadhyay)
- For η′ photoproduction with g_{η′} = 3.5 and Λ = 2 GeV, the earlier non-crossing-symmetric model could qualitatively reproduce the total cross section with Born terms alone
- Using the proposed crossing-symmetric form factor with g_{η′} at the upper limit and Λ = 700 MeV, the Born term total cross section levels off at about 0.2 μb at a photon energy of 2 GeV
- The proposed form factor cannot reproduce the total cross section shape with only Born terms for Λ values in the range 500 to 2000 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Ohta (1989) on gauge-invariant form factor prescriptions and corrects a flaw in the method of Haberzettl (1997), which had been widely adopted in single- and multi-channel fits to meson photoproduction data (Feuster, 1998). The authors show that the earlier treatments of η′ photoproduction by Nimai and Mukhopadhyay violate crossing symmetry and therefore produce theoretically unacceptable amplitudes. The proposed prescription narrows the possible interpretations of experimental data by showing that the η′ photoproduction total cross section cannot arise solely from Born terms, in contrast to the claims of Mukhopadhyay. This enables more reliable extraction of resonance contributions in phenomenological analyses.
