# Magnetic dipole probes of the sd and pf shell crossing in the A=36,38 argon isotopes

> We have calculated the M1 strength distributions in the A=36,38 argon isotopes within large-scale shell model studies which consider valence nucleons in the sd and pf shells. While the M1 strength in 36Ar is well reproduced within the sd shell, the experimentally observed strong fragmentation of the M1 strength in 38Ar requires configuration mixing between the sd and the pf shells adding to our understanding of correlations across the N=20 shell gap.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed strong fragmentation of the M1 strength in 38Ar requires configuration mixing between the sd and pf shells, while the M1 strength in 36Ar is well reproduced within the sd shell alone; however, the agreement with data for 38Ar is only marginal even with cross-shell excitations, and higher-order configurations may be needed for full convergence.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed for 36Ar and 38Ar using the ANTOINE code, first within the complete sd shell using the USD interaction, then within the full sdpf shell with 2p2h and 4p4h cross-shell excitations, and finally within a truncated sdpf_1 model space that includes the s1/2, d3/2, f7/2, p3/2 orbitals plus 1p1h spin-flip excitations from the d5/2 orbital and from the f7/2p3/2 subspace to the f5/2p1/2 subspace. The spin part of the M1 operator was quenched by a factor of 0.75 throughout.

## Key numbers

- Total calculated M1 strength for 36Ar: 6.76 mu_N^2; experimentally observed summed M1 strength up to 13.8 MeV: 2.65(12) mu_N^2, in agreement with the calculated value of 2.8 mu_N^2 in that energy window
- For 36Ar, the calculation predicts 1.4 mu_N^2 of M1 strength in the energy interval 13.8-15 MeV where experimentally no strength has been observed
- Total calculated M1 strength for 38Ar: 6.01 mu_N^2; experimental value in the measured energy window: 2.86(18) mu_N^2, compared to the calculated value of 2.88 mu_N^2
- In the 38Ar ground state, the pure sd-shell configuration contributes only 33% of the wave function; 0p0h and 2p2h excitations together amount to about 75%
- For 38Ar, the summed B(M1) strength increases from 2.95 mu_N^2 (n_t=3 case) to 4.02 mu_N^2 (n_t=4) and 4.20 mu_N^2 (n_t=5) as more pf-shell components are mixed in
- The dimension of the 4p4h model space for 38Ar is 227,625,436 for M=0+ states; the sdpf_1 space dimension for 38Ar is nearly 75 million

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model studies of M1 strength in 36Ar and 38Ar by Fol94, which showed that pure sd-shell calculations could not reproduce the fragmentation observed in 38Ar. The calculations build on the effective interaction of Num01, which combines the USD interaction for the sd shell, KB' matrix elements for the pf shell, and a G-matrix for cross-shell matrix elements, with monopole terms adjusted to experimental data. The results demonstrate the onset of erosion of the N=20 shell closure near A=40, consistent with earlier findings of large sd configuration admixtures in Ca isotopes by Cau01 and Schi03, and enable future comparisons with very recent photon scattering data on 40Ar by Chu06.
