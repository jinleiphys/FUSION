# Large-scale exact diagonalizations reveal low-momentum scales of nuclei

> Ab initio methods aim to solve the nuclear many-body problem with controlled approximations. Virtually exact numerical solutions for realistic interactions can only be obtained for certain special cases such as few-nucleon systems. Here we extend the reach of exact diagonalization methods to handle model spaces with dimension exceeding $10^{10}$ on a single compute node. This allows us to perform no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 6Li in model spaces up to $N_\mathrm{max} = 22$ and to reveal the 4He+d halo structure of this nucleus. Still, the use of a finite harmonic-oscillator basis implies truncations in both infrared (IR) and ultraviolet (UV) length scales. These truncations impose finite-size corrections on observables computed in this basis. We perform IR extrapolations of energies and radii computed in the NCSM and with the coupled-cluster method at several fixed UV cutoffs. It is shown that this strategy enables information gain also from data that is not fully UV converged. IR extrapolations improve the accuracy of relevant bound-state observables for a range of UV cutoffs, thus making them profitable tools. We relate the momentum scale that governs the exponential IR convergence to the threshold energy for the first open decay channel. Using large-scale NCSM calculations we numerically verify this small-momentum scale of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the momentum scale governing the exponential infrared (IR) convergence of no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations in a harmonic-oscillator basis is the separation momentum corresponding to the lowest-energy open decay channel, and that IR extrapolation formulas can yield accurate bound-state observables even when ultraviolet (UV) convergence is not fully achieved.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale NCSM exact diagonalizations for 6Li in model spaces up to Nmax = 22, with dimensions exceeding 10^10, using the pANTOINE code with implicit matrix construction. They applied IR extrapolation formulas for energies and radii at fixed UV cutoffs to NCSM and coupled-cluster results for several s-shell nuclei (3H, 3He, 4He) and p-shell nuclei (6Li, 6He, 8He, 16O), using the NNLOopt nucleon-nucleon interaction with a regulator cutoff of 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- NCSM model space dimension for 6Li at Nmax = 22: 2.5 × 10^10 (an order of magnitude increase from 2.7 × 10^9 at Nmax = 18)
- Converged ground-state energies: 3He at -7.52 MeV and 3H at -8.25 MeV (NNLOopt interaction, Nmax = 40)
- Separation momenta from binding energies: k_sep ≈ 0.50 fm^-1 for 3He and 0.54 fm^-1 for 3H
- Extracted k_infinity from fits for 3H: 0.54(1) fm^-1 (average from energy and radius extrapolations at largest Nmax and Λ)
- Extracted ANC parameter a0 for 3H: approximately 280 MeV (at largest Nmax and Λ), corresponding to ANC γ_sep ≈ 3.5 fm^-1/2 in the orthogonal Jacobi coordinate

## Context

This work extends the IR extrapolation framework previously developed for the harmonic-oscillator basis by Furnstahl et al. (2012, 2014, 2015), More et al. (2013), and Wendt et al. (2015), which was based on the analogy with Lüscher's formula for lattice calculations. The paper numerically verifies that the relevant low-momentum scale for finite nuclei is the separation momentum of the lowest decay channel, consistent with recent lattice results by Koenig et al. (2018). The improved IR extrapolation scheme enables reliable extraction of bound-state energies and radii from NCSM calculations that are not fully UV converged, and the extension of exact diagonalization to model spaces with dimensions exceeding 10^10 opens the possibility to study halo structures such as the 4He+d configuration in 6Li.
