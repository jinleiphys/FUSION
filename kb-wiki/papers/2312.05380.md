# Exact Polarization of Particles of Any Spin at Global Equilibrium

> The polarization of the $Λ$ particle offers the unique opportunity to study the hydrodynamic gradients in the Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions. However, the theoretical formula commonly used to calculate polarization is only a linear order expansion in thermal vorticity and neglects higher-order corrections. Here, I present an exact calculation to all orders in (constant) thermal vorticity at global equilibrium, obtaining the analytic form of the spin density matrix and the polarization vector for massive particles of any spin. Finally, I extend these results to local equilibrium and assess their phenomenological impact by numerically calculating the polarization vector in a 3+1 hydrodynamic simulation.

## Key claim
The paper derives an exact analytic formula for the spin density matrix and polarization vector of massive particles of any spin, valid to all orders in constant thermal vorticity at global equilibrium, correcting the commonly used linear-order expansion. The author finds that for Au-Au collisions at 30 GeV, the exact formula and the linear approximation coincide, but small discrepancies may arise at lower collision energies where thermal vorticity is larger.

## Method
The author starts from the statistical density operator for a system at global equilibrium with constant thermal vorticity, then uses an analytic continuation technique (mapping thermal vorticity to an imaginary rotation parameter) to compute exact expectation values of creation and annihilation operators. The resulting spin density matrix is expressed as a geometric series of matrices, which is summed analytically to yield a closed-form expression for the polarization vector for any spin. The author then generalizes this result to local equilibrium by promoting thermal vorticity to a local variable and integrating over the decoupling hypersurface, implementing the formula in a 3+1 hydrodynamic simulation using the vHLLE code with GLISSANDO initial conditions.

## Context
This work extends the linear-response calculation of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, originally developed by Becattini (2013) and others, which only included first-order terms in thermal vorticity. The exact formula provides a foundation for computing all spin observables including vector meson spin alignment, and enables assessment of higher-order corrections that may become important at lower collision energies where thermal vorticity is larger. The author notes that additional contributions from thermal shear (Becattini, 2021) and the spin Hall effect (Fu, 2022) are not addressed in this work.
