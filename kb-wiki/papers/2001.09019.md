# Deuteron-induced reactions on manganese at low energies

> The scarce data systematics and complexity of deuteron interactions demand the update of both the experimental database and theoretical frame of deuteron activation cross sections. Various reactions induced by neutrons and protons following the deuteron breakup (BU) should be also taken into account. On the other hand, deuteron reaction cross sections recommended recently for high-priority elements are still based on data fit without predictive power. Purpose: Accurate new measurements of low-energy deuteron-induced reaction cross sections for monoisotopic (55Mn) natural manganese target enhance the related database as well as the opportunity of an unitary and consistent account of the related reaction mechanisms. Method: Activation cross sections of 54,56Mn, and 51Cr nuclei by deuterons on $^{55}$Mn were measured at energies <20 MeV by the stacked-foil technique and high resolution gamma spectrometry at the U-120M cyclotron of CANAM, NPI CAS. Then all available data for deuterons on 55Mn up to 50 MeV are analyzed paying particular attention to BU and direct reaction (DR) mechanisms. Results: Newly measured activation cross sections strengthen the deuteron database at low energies, at once with a consistent account for the first time of all available data. Conclusions: Due account of deuteron-induced reactions on 55Mn, including particularly the new experimental data at low energies, is provided by a suitable BU and DR assessment.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a consistent account of all available deuteron-induced reaction data on $^{55}$Mn up to 50 MeV can be achieved by properly including breakup (BU) and direct reaction (DR) mechanisms, but this success depends on using the Daehnick et al. optical model potential and accounting for the reduction of the compound-nucleus flux by direct interactions, rather than relying on data-fitting approaches without predictive power.

## Method

Activation cross sections for $^{54,56}$Mn and $^{51}$Cr produced by deuterons on monoisotopic $^{55}$Mn were measured at energies up to 20 MeV using the stacked-foil technique and high-resolution gamma spectrometry at the U-120M cyclotron of CANAM, NPI CAS. The authors then analyzed all available data for deuterons on $^{55}$Mn up to 50 MeV, combining distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with FRESCO for stripping and pick-up reactions, empirical parametrizations for elastic and inelastic breakup, and TALYS-1.9 for pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus contributions, with a reduction factor applied to the total-reaction cross section to account for flux lost to direct interactions.

## Key numbers

- $^{55}$Mn$(d,p)^{56}$Mn cross section at 7.17 MeV: 231.2 mb (uncertainty 173 mb, last digit in parentheses)
- $^{55}$Mn$(d,x)^{54}$Mn cross section at 19.22 MeV: 133.1 mb (uncertainty 77 mb)
- $^{55}$Mn$(d,x)^{51}$Cr cross section at 19.22 MeV: 0.353 mb (uncertainty 44 mb)
- $^{55}$Mn$(d,p)^{56}$Mn cross section at 4.80 MeV: 229.5 mb (uncertainty 137 mb)
- $^{55}$Mn$(d,p)^{56}$Mn cross section at 3.13 MeV: 134.4 mb (uncertainty 132 mb)
- The pre-equilibrium plus compound-nucleus fraction of the total-reaction cross section reaches a maximum of approximately 64% at deuteron energies of 10-12 MeV
- The direct-interaction fraction (breakup plus stripping plus pick-up) and the pre-equilibrium plus compound-nucleus fraction each account for close to half of the total-reaction cross section at energies around 42 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of deuteron-induced reactions on structural fusion materials previously applied to V, Cr, Fe, Co, Ni, and Cu by the same group (Avrigeanu et al., various years), and addresses deficiencies in the TENDL-2017 evaluated data library and the Padé-fit approach recommended by Hermanne et al. (2018) and Engle et al. (2019). The new low-energy data for $^{51}$Cr production are the first measurements below 20 MeV, and the consistent treatment of breakup and direct reactions enables improved predictions for deuteron activation of manganese, which is relevant to the SS-316L steel alloys used in the DONES project beam guides.
