# $^{12}$C and $α$-clusters, $0^+$ spectrum, and Hoyle-state candidates in $^{24}$Mg

> Background: A recent inelastic alpha-scattering experiment [Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 102701 (2022)] found $0^+$ resonances in $^{24}$Mg on and above the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C break-up threshold. It has been conjectured that the states have a $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster structure, and play a similar role in accelerating $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion to the manner in which the Hoyle state accelerates production of $^{12}$C in massive stars. Purpose: We wish to build up a quantitative theoretical basis for the considerations of the Hoyle-state paradigm, by calculating the distribution of the $0^+$ states in the shell, as well as in the relevant cluster models. Methods: We determine the spectrum of excited $0^+$ states in $^{24}$Mg nucleus using multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry calculations leading to a unified description of the quartet (or shell), $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{20}$Ne+$^{4}$He cluster configurations. Results: The density of $0^+$ states in the quartet spectrum is comparable to that found in experiment; however, the density of cluster states is considerably less. Conclusions: The recently observed alpha-scattering resonances do not seem to be simple $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster states, but are more plausibly interpreted as fragmented cluster states due to coupling to quartet excitations, as background states.

## Key claim

The recently observed $0^+$ resonances in $^{24}$Mg near the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C breakup threshold are not simple $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster states, but are more plausibly interpreted as fragmented cluster states arising from coupling to quartet (shell-model) excitations acting as background states.

## Method

The authors applied multiconfigurational dynamical symmetry (MUSY) calculations to obtain a unified description of the quartet (shell), $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, and $^{20}$Ne+$^{4}$He cluster configurations in $^{24}$Mg. A dynamically symmetric Hamiltonian with 3+2 free parameters (for the energy functional and moment of inertia) was fitted to the low-energy experimental spectrum, including the two well-established rotational bands and four theoretically predicted shape isomers. The cluster spectra were then obtained as parameter-free predictions from the same Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator energy was taken from systematics as $\hbar\omega = 12.595$ MeV for $^{24}$Mg.
- The fitted Hamiltonian parameters were: $a = -0.148228$ MeV, $b = 0.000392$ MeV, $d = 0.666544$ MeV, $a_0 = 0.141965\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}$, $a_1 = 0.015811\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}$, with a goodness-of-fit value $F = 0.11378$.
- The B(E2;$2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1$) transition strength used was 21.5 W.u., giving $\alpha^2 = 0.817$ W.u.
- The number of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C resonance states in the experimental energy windows: for $L=0$, 3 experimental vs. 2 theoretical; for $L=2$, 10 vs. 4; for $L=4$, 10 vs. 9; for $L=6$, 7 vs. 10; for $L=8$, 10 vs. 8; for $L=10$, 4 vs. 4; for $L=12$, 5 vs. 4; for $L=14$, 6 vs. 6; for $L=16$, 2 vs. 2; for $L=18$, 1 vs. 1; for $L=20$, 1 vs. 0.

## Context

This work provides a theoretical response to the experimental finding of Jenkins et al. (2022) in Physical Review Letters 129, 102701, which identified four $0^+$ resonances in $^{24}$Mg and conjectured they might be $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster states analogous to the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C. The present calculation extends earlier work by Cseh et al. (1993) on the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model, which only considered the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C configuration, by now including quartet states and $^{20}$Ne+$^{4}$He clusterization in a unified framework. The results suggest that the observed resonances are fragmented cluster states rather than pure $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster states, which has implications for understanding $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion in stellar carbon burning and the role of background states in nuclear reactions.
