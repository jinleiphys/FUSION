# Pion-nucleon charge-exchange amplitudes above 2 GeV

> The amplitudes for the pion-nucleon charge exchange reaction of the Karlsruhe-Helsinki and the George-Washington-University partial wave analyses are compared with those of a Regge-cut model with the aim to explore the possibility to provide high energy constraints for theoretical baryon resonance analyses in the energy region above 2 GeV.

# Pion-nucleon charge-exchange amplitudes above 2 GeV

## Key claim
The Regge-cut model can reproduce differential cross sections for the pion-nucleon charge-exchange reaction down to energies around √s ≈ 3 GeV, suggesting that the appropriate energy region for matching meson-nucleon dynamics to diffractive scattering is approximately 3 GeV for the helicity flip amplitude, but the helicity non-flip amplitudes remain poorly constrained, with significant model dependence in the t-dependence.

## Method
The authors constructed a Regge-cut model for the reaction π⁻ p → π⁰ n consisting of a rho-pole trajectory and a rho-Pomeron cut, with vertex functions parameterized by a single exponential. They fitted the model parameters to differential cross section and polarization data for pion beam momenta above 4 GeV/c (√s ≥ 3 GeV) and four-momentum transfer squared |t| ≤ 2 GeV², then compared the resulting amplitudes with those from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH80) partial wave analysis (up to 3.49 GeV) and the George Washington University (GWU) partial wave analysis (up to 2.5 GeV), as well as with the Barger-Phillips Regge parameterization.

## Key numbers
- The Regge-cut model reproduces data down to √s ≈ 3 GeV for |t| < 2 GeV²; the KH80 analysis covers energies up to 3.49 GeV using partial waves up to angular momentum j = 37/2 (l = 18 at the highest energy)
- The GWU analysis (SP06) covers energies up to 2.5 GeV using partial waves up to l = 8
- The rho trajectory slope parameter from the fit is α′_ρ = 0.81 ± 0.01 GeV⁻²
- The Pomeron slope is fixed at α′_P = 0.1 GeV⁻²
- The helicity non-flip residue parameter β₀^{++} for the rho-pole is -23.8 ± 0.3; the helicity flip residue β₀^{+-} is 151.3 ± 1.1
- The differential cross section shows a dip near -t = 0.6 GeV²; the polarization maximum occurs near -t = 0.5 GeV²
- The Regge-cut model reproduces the total cross-section difference for π⁻p and π⁺p scattering in the energy interval from 3 GeV to 24 GeV

## Context
This work extends the comparison of Regge phenomenology to partial wave analyses of pion-nucleon scattering, specifically the KH80 analysis by Koch and Hohler (1980) and the GWU analyses by Arndt and collaborators (2004, 2006). It is a preparatory step for extending coupled-channel meson-nucleon approaches (such as the Julich model by Gasparyan et al., 2003) to energies above 2 GeV, analogous to how Regge constraints have been used in Roy equation studies for meson-meson scattering by Basdevant (1973), Ananthanarayan (2000), and Pelaez (2004). The paper identifies that the helicity flip amplitude from the Regge-cut model joins smoothly with the KH80 analysis near √s = 3 GeV, while the helicity non-flip amplitude remains poorly constrained and would benefit from new measurements of polarization and spin rotation parameters at forward angles, such as those planned at J-PARC or with the HADES detector at FAIR/GSI.
