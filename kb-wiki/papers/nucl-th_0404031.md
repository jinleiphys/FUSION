# Study of $Λ(1405)$ in photoproduction of $K^*$

> We investigate the photoproduction of $K^*$ vector meson for the study of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. The invariant mass distribution of $πΣ$ shows a different shape from the nominal one, peaking at 1420 MeV. This is considered as a consequence of the double pole structure of $Λ(1405)$, predicted in the chiral unitary model. Combined with other reactions, such as $π^- p \to K^0 πΣ$, experimental confirmation of this fact will reveal a novel structure of the $Λ(1405)$ state.

# Study of Λ(1405) in photoproduction of K*

## Key claim

The photoproduction reaction γ p → π⁺ K⁰ MB (where MB are meson-baryon final states) can isolate the second pole of the Λ(1405) resonance at around 1420 MeV, because the initial K⁻ p channel couples dominantly to the z₂ pole of Λ(1405) rather than the z₁ pole, provided the Σ(1385) background is small enough to allow the structure to be seen.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section and invariant mass distributions for the reaction γ p → K* Λ(1405) → π⁺ K⁰ MB using a model that combines a vector-meson photoproduction amplitude (derived from effective Lagrangians) with an s-wave meson-baryon scattering amplitude from the chiral unitary model. The K⁻ p → MB amplitude includes both the chiral unitary coupled-channel amplitude (from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and an explicit p-wave Σ(1385) pole term with couplings derived from SU(6) symmetry. The calculation is performed via Monte-Carlo integration over the final-state phase space.

## Key numbers

- The two poles of Λ(1405) in the chiral unitary model are located at z₁ = 1390 − 66i MeV and z₂ = 1426 − 16i MeV, with coupling strengths to πΣ of 2.9 and 1.5 respectively, and to K̅N of 2.1 and 2.7 respectively.
- The invariant mass distribution of π⁰Σ⁰ (pure I=0) from the proposed reaction peaks at approximately 1420 MeV, corresponding to the z₂ pole position.
- The threshold photon energy for K* Λ(1405) production is 2350 MeV; calculations are shown at E_γ = 2500 MeV (√s ≈ 2350 MeV).
- The universal vector meson coupling used is g_{VPP} = −6.05.
- The Σ(1385) pole term uses g_A = 1.26, f = 93 × 1.123 MeV, and a form factor cutoff Λ = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model studies of the Λ(1405) double pole structure (Oller 2000, Jido 2003) by proposing a specific photoproduction reaction to isolate the higher pole z₂, complementing earlier work on the π⁻ p → K⁰ π Σ reaction (Hyodo 2003) which favored the lower pole z₁. The double pole structure was first found in the cloudy bag model (Fink 1990) and has been confirmed in multiple chiral unitary analyses. The reaction enables experimental confirmation of the two-pole nature of Λ(1405), which would distinguish the meson-baryon molecular picture from a simple three-quark state description, and could be combined with existing data from π⁻ p → K⁰ π Σ (Thomas 1973) and kaon photoproduction (Ahn 2003).
