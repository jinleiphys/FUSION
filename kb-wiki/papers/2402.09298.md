# Minijet quenching in non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> We study the energy deposition and thermalisation of high-momentum on-shell partons (minijets) travelling through a non-equilibrium Quark-Gluon Plasma using QCD kinetic theory. For thermal backgrounds, we show that the parton energy first flows to the soft sector by collinear cascade and then isotropises via elastic scatterings. In contrast, the momentum deposition from a minijet reaches the equilibrium distribution directly. For expanding non-equilibrium QGP, we study the time for a minijet perturbation to lose memory of its initial conditions, namely, the hydrodynamisation time. We show that the minijet evolution scales well with the relaxation time $τ_R \propto η/s/T (τ )$, where $T (τ )$ is the effective temperature and $η/s$ is the viscosity over entropy ratio.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for thermal backgrounds, minijet energy thermalises in two stages, first via a collinear cascade to the soft sector, then via elastic scatterings that isotropise the distribution, while for expanding non-equilibrium QGP, the hydrodynamisation time of a minijet perturbation scales with the relaxation time \(\tau_R \propto \eta/s/T(\tau)\), where \(T(\tau)\) is the effective temperature and \(\eta/s\) is the viscosity over entropy ratio.

## Method

The authors solve the linearised QCD kinetic theory (AMY) Boltzmann equations for a high-momentum on-shell parton (minijet) perturbation \(\delta f\) on top of a background distribution \(\bar f\), including both elastic \(2\leftrightarrow2\) and inelastic \(1\leftrightarrow2\) collision kernels. They study non-expanding thermal and anisotropic backgrounds, as well as longitudinally expanding backgrounds relevant to heavy-ion collisions, using a boost-invariant formulation and initial conditions from a CGC-inspired overoccupied gluonic plasma.

## Key numbers

- For a thermal background with coupling \(\lambda = 10\) and minijet energy \(E = 30\overline{T}\), the particle number \(\delta n\) reaches equilibrium at time \(t_{1\leftrightarrow2}\overline{T} \approx 11\), while isotropisation (transverse pressure \(\delta P_T\) reaching equilibrium) occurs at \(t_{2\leftrightarrow2}\overline{T} \approx 19\).
- The isotropisation time for a minijet with \(E = E_0 = 30\overline{T}\) and \(\lambda = 10\) is \(t \approx 18.5\, t_R\), where \(t_R = (\eta/s)/\overline{T} \sqrt{E/E_0}\).
- The specific shear viscosity values used for pure gluon plasma at couplings \(\lambda = 2, 5, 10, 15, 20\) are \(\eta/s \approx 7.84, 1.81, 0.624, 0.361, 0.235\), respectively.
- The initial minijet perturbation is centred at energy \(E = 30\overline{T}\) (for constant-temperature backgrounds) or \(E = 30 Q_s\) (for non-equilibrium backgrounds), with a Gaussian width \(\sigma = 0.1 E\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of minijet thermalisation in thermal backgrounds (Iancu 2015, Mehtar-Tani 2018, Schlichting 2020, Mehtar-Tani 2022, Sirimanna 2022) to anisotropic and longitudinally expanding backgrounds relevant to heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the QCD kinetic theory framework used to describe QGP equilibration and hydrodynamisation (Kurkela 2014, Kurkela 2015, Kurkela 2018, Du 2020) and provides a unified description of minijet energy loss and medium response that can inform phenomenological models such as the hybrid model (Casalderrey-Solana 2020), CoLBT-hydro (Chen 2017), and JETSCAPE (JETSCAPE 2022).
