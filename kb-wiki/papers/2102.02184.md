# Large-$N_c$ analysis of two-nucleon neutrinoless double beta decay and charge-independence-breaking contact terms

> The interpretation of experiments that search for neutrinoless double beta decay relies on input from nuclear theory. Cirigliano et al. recently showed that, for the light Majorana exchange formalism, effective field theory calculations require a $nn\to pp e^- e^-$ contact term at leading order. They estimated the size of this contribution by relating it to measured charge-independence-breaking (CIB) nucleon-nucleon interactions and making an assumption about the relative sizes of CIB operators. We show that the assumptions underlying this approximation are justified in the limit of the number of colors $N_c$ being large. We also obtain a large-$N_c$ hierarchy among CIB nucleon-nucleon interactions that is in agreement with phenomenological results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the assumption made by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019) that the two charge-independence-breaking (CIB) low-energy coefficients C1 and C2 are approximately equal in size and sign is justified in the limit of a large number of colors Nc, supporting the approximation that the neutrinoless double beta decay contact term LEC g_nu^NN can be estimated as half the sum of the two CIB LECs.

## Method

The authors perform a large-Nc analysis of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) Lagrangian containing two spurion fields, using the spin-flavor symmetry that emerges in the large-Nc limit of QCD. They construct a minimal basis of CIB operators with manifest large-Nc scaling, derive the large-Nc hierarchy among these operators, and map this basis onto the CIB Lagrangian of Cirigliano et al. (2019) to determine the relative large-Nc scalings of the LECs C1 and C2.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass scales as O(Nc) in the large-Nc limit, with momenta in the t-channel scaling as p_plus ~ 1/Nc and p_minus ~ 1
- The pion decay constant F scales as O(sqrt(Nc)) in the large-Nc limit, so each additional pion field in the expansion yields a suppression by 1/sqrt(Nc)
- The nucleon and Delta mass splitting is O(1/Nc), making them degenerate in the large-Nc limit
- Single-nucleon matrix elements of spin operator S^i and isospin operator I^a scale as O(1), while the spin-isospin operator G^{ia} and identity operator 1 scale as O(Nc)

## Context

This work extends the large-Nc analysis of NN interactions previously applied to parity-conserving scattering (Kaplan 1996, 1997; Schindler 2018), parity-violating couplings (Schindler 2016), and time-reversal-violating couplings (Vanasse 2019). It provides a complementary theoretical constraint to the Cottingham-formula-based estimates of Cirigliano et al. (2020, 2021) for the LEC g_nu^NN, which is currently inaccessible to lattice QCD calculations and insufficiently constrained by data. The results enable a more reliable estimation of the short-range contact term contribution to neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements.
