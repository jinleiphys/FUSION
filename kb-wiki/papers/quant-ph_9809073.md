# Towards new understanding of nuclear rotation

> Properties of time evolution of wave packets built up from rotator eigenstates are discussed. The mechanism of perfect cloning of the initial wave packet for "circular states" at fractional revival times is explained. The smooth transition from "circular" to "linear" through intermediate "elliptic" is described. Example of time evolution of a nuclear wave packet created in Coulomb excitation mechanism is presented.

# Towards new understanding of nuclear rotation

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that wave packets built from rotational eigenstates of a quantum rotator exhibit perfect cloning at fractional revival times for "circular states" (η=1), while for "elliptic" or "linear" states the fractional revivals produce mutants with different shapes; however, the authors note that the absolute time scales for nuclear rotation (T_rev ~ 10^-19 s, T_cl ~ 10^-20 s) are still beyond the time resolution of present experimental techniques, so the predictions remain theoretical.

## Method

The authors constructed coherent states of angular momentum depending on two real parameters N and η, with η=1 corresponding to circular states (maximal M=I), η=0 to linear states (M=0), and intermediate η to elliptic states. They expanded the initial wave packet in spherical harmonics and analyzed its time evolution using the rotator Hamiltonian H = (ħ²/2J) I(I+1), rewriting the evolution in terms of classical period T_cl and revival time T_rev. For the nuclear application, they calculated amplitudes of excited angular momentum eigenstates from Coulomb excitation of ²³⁸U bombarded by ⁴⁰Ar at 170 MeV using semiclassical theory, and compared the "quantum carpet" time evolution for ideal rotor energies versus experimental energies.

## Key numbers

- For the quantum rotator, the revival time T_rev and classical period T_cl satisfy T_cl << T_rev, with T_rev ~ 10⁻¹⁹ s and T_cl ~ 10⁻²⁰ s for nuclear rotation.
- The coherent state wave function Ψ_{N,η}(θ,φ) depends on parameters N and η, with ⟨L_z⟩ → η(N - 1/2) in the limit N→∞.
- The parameter η = ⟨L_z⟩/(2ΔL_y²) = ±√(ΔL_x²/ΔL_y²), with η=1 for circular states and η=0 for linear states.
- For η=1, the number of clones at fractional revival times t = (m/n)T_rev equals q = n/2 for even n or q = n for odd n.
- The Coulomb excitation example used ²³⁸U bombarded by ⁴⁰Ar at E = 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the universal scenario of wave packet revival discovered by Averbukh and Perelman (1990) to the specific case of the quantum rotator, which has an exactly quadratic energy dependence on quantum number I(I+1). The authors apply this framework to nuclear rotation excited via Coulomb excitation, building on the semiclassical theory of Broglia and Fonda. The paper enables future experimental tests of rotational wave packet dynamics once time resolution improves beyond the current ~10⁻¹⁹ s limit, and provides a theoretical classification of clone versus mutant fractional revivals depending on the topology of the initial coherent state.
