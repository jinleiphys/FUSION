# $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in hot and dense matter

> The $D$ and $\bar {D}$ mesons are studied in hot dense matter within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach taking, as bare interaction, a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term. The in-medium solution at finite temperature incorporates Pauli blocking effects, baryon mean-field bindings, and $π$ and open-charm meson self-energies. In the $DN$ sector, the $Λ_c$ and $Σ_c$ resonances remain close to their free-space position while acquiring a remarkable width. As a result, the $D$ meson spectral density shows a single pronounced peak close to the free mass that broadens with increasing density specially towards lower energies. The low-density theorem is not a good approximation for the repulsive $\bar D$ self-energy close to saturation density. We discuss the implications for the $J/Ψ$ suppression at CBM (FAIR).

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of D and anti-D mesons in hot dense nuclear matter is studied, finding that the low-density theorem is not a good approximation for the repulsive anti-D self-energy close to saturation density, and that the J/Psi suppression at FAIR is unlikely to occur via direct D anti-D absorption but may proceed through hadronic collisions of excited charmonia.

## Method

The authors perform a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation using a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction as the bare interaction, supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term. The in-medium solution at finite temperature incorporates Pauli blocking effects, baryon mean-field bindings via a temperature-dependent sigma-omega model, and pion and open-charm meson self-energies in the intermediate propagators. The multi-channel transition matrix T is solved using cutoff regularization, fixed by reproducing the position and width of the I=0 Lambda_c(2593) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda_c and Sigma_c resonances remain close to their free-space position while acquiring a remarkable width; at T=0 the lower peak built up by the tilde Lambda_c N^{-1} mode goes up by about 50 MeV relative to the calculation without mean-field binding
- For the anti-D N sector, scattering lengths for model A (with scalar term) are a^{I=0}=0.61 fm and a^{I=1}=-0.26 fm; for model B (without scalar term) they are a^{I=0}=0 fm and a^{I=1}=-0.29 fm
- At T=0, the D meson optical potential is attractive at -12 MeV (model A) or -18 MeV (model B), while the anti-D optical potential is repulsive at 11 MeV (model A) or 20 MeV (model B)
- The in-medium anti-D mass increases by about 10-20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel study of Tolos (2004, 2006) and Mizutani (2006) to finite temperature, examining implications for J/Psi suppression at the CBM experiment of FAIR. The results contrast with earlier work by Tsushima (1999) that suggested D meson mass reduction in nuclear medium, showing instead that the low-density approximation breaks down for the repulsive anti-D self-energy. The study enables predictions for charmonium suppression mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions, finding that direct J/Psi absorption via D anti-D is unlikely but suppression through excited charmonia (chi_c(1P) or Psi') hadronic collisions appears kinematically feasible.
