# SVZ sum rules : 30 + 1 years later

> For this exceptional 25th anniversary of the QCD-Montpellier series of conferences initiated in 85 with the name "Non-perturbative methods", we take the opportunuity to celebrate the 30 + 1 years of the discovery of the SVZ (also called ITEP, QCD or QCD spectral) sum rules by M.A. Shifman, A.I. Vainshtein and V.I. Zakahrov in 79 [1]. In this talk, I have the duty to present the status of the method. I shall (can) not enumerate the vast area of successful applications of sum rules in hadron physics but I shall focus on the historical evolution of field and its new developments. More detailed related discussions and more complete references can be found in the textbooks [2,3].

## Key claim

The SVZ (Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov) sum rules, discovered in 1979, remain one of the most important developments in non-perturbative QCD phenomenology, but the method's typical accuracy is limited to about 10-20% and its reliability depends on the validity of the naive duality ansatz parametrization of the spectral function, which can be violated when high precision is required.

## Method

The method improves the Kallen-Lehmann dispersion relation for a hadronic two-point correlator by incorporating non-perturbative vacuum condensate contributions via an operator product expansion (OPE) a la Wilson. The spectral function is parametrized using a minimal duality ansatz of one resonance plus a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c, where the continuum comes from the discontinuity of QCD diagrams in the OPE to ensure matching at high energies. Various forms of the sum rules are used including exponential (Borel/Laplace) sum rules, moment sum rules, finite energy sum rules (FESR), Gaussian sum rules, tau-like sum rules, and double ratios of sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The original SVZ gluon condensate value from charmonium sum rules was about 0.04 GeV^4
- More recent refined analyses indicate a slightly higher gluon condensate value of (0.06 ± 0.01) GeV^4
- The naive duality ansatz parametrization works within the expected 10-20% accuracy of the approach
- The OPE for the Adler function in e+e- → hadrons includes perturbative series terms: C_0 = 1 + a_s + 1.640 a_s^2 + 6.371 a_s^3 + 49.076 a_s^4 + ... (where a_s ≡ α_s/π)

## Context

This talk reviews the status of SVZ sum rules 31 years after their discovery by Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979), extending the historical overview of the method's development and its new developments. The work complements contributions from the two inventors (Shifman and Zakharov), the historical talk of Dosch on baryon sum rules, and various modern applications presented at the QCD 10 conference. The method enables predictions of hadron masses and properties from QCD first principles, with current open questions including the precise determination of the gluon condensate, the role of quadratic 1/q^2 terms, and the quantification of duality violation effects in high-precision applications such as tau-decay analyses.
