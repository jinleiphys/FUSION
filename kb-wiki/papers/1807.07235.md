# Radiative pion capture in 2H, 3He and 3H

> The $ПҖ^- + {^2{\rm H}} \rightarrow Оі+ n + n$, $ПҖ^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow Оі+ {^3{\rm H}}$, $ПҖ^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow Оі+ n + d$, $ПҖ^- + {^3{\rm He}} \rightarrow Оі+ n + n + p$ and $ПҖ^- + {^3{\rm H}} \rightarrow Оі+ n + n + n$ capture reactions are studied with the AV18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana~IX three-nucleon potential. We provide for the first time realistic predictions for the differential and total capture rates for all these processes, treating consistently the initial and final nuclear states. Our results are based on the single nucleon Kroll-Ruderman-type transition operator and concentrate on the full treatment of the nuclear final state interactions. They are compared with older theoretical predictions and experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors provide for the first time realistic predictions for differential and total capture rates of radiative pion capture on 2H, 3He, and 3H using consistent treatment of initial and final nuclear states from realistic two-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials, but only with a single-nucleon Kroll-Ruderman-type transition operator, leaving room for improvement from two-nucleon contributions and chiral perturbation theory corrections.

## Method

The authors use the AV18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential to calculate nuclear initial and final states in momentum space with partial wave decomposition. They employ a single-nucleon axial current operator derived from the Kroll-Ruderman form, compute nuclear matrix elements for the transverse components of the current, and integrate over phase space to obtain differential and total capture rates for five reactions: ПҖвҒ» + ВІH вҶ’ Оі + n + n, ПҖвҒ» + ВіHe вҶ’ Оі + ВіH, ПҖвҒ» + ВіHe вҶ’ Оі + n + d, ПҖвҒ» + ВіHe вҶ’ Оі + n + n + p, and ПҖвҒ» + ВіH вҶ’ Оі + n + n + n.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework for muon capture on A вүӨ 3 systems (Golak et al., 2014, 2016) to radiative pion capture, providing the first calculations with modern realistic nuclear forces for three-nucleon systems. It builds on earlier theoretical work by Phillips and Roig (1974, 1975) who used separable s-wave potentials and the Amado model, and by GГҘrdestig and Phillips (2006) who applied heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to the deuteron capture reaction. The results enable future studies incorporating two-nucleon transition operators and chiral perturbation theory corrections, and allow extraction of the neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn with improved theoretical control.
