# Ground State Properties of Charmed Hypernuclei with Mean Field Approach

> Closed shell charmed hypernuclei $^5_{Λ_c}$Li, $^{17}_{Λ_c}$F, $^{41}_{Λ_c}$Sc, $^{57}_{Λ_c}$Cu, $^{133}_{Λ_c}$Sb and $^{209}_{Λ_c}$Bi are calculated within Hartree-Fock approach by using three different force sets derived from microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of $Λ$ hypernuclei. Ground state properties (binding energies, $Λ_c$ separation energies, $Λ_c$ single particle energies and $Λ_c$ densities) of charmed nuclei are examined. Due to the Coulomb repulsion between protons and the $Λ_c$ baryon, charmed hypernuclei are most bound for $16\leq$A$\leq 41$, where $^{17}_{Λ_c}$F can be considered as an excellent candidate to measure charmed hypernuclei. The competition between the attractive nucleon-$Λ_c$ interaction and the Coulomb repulsion is discussed, and we compare $Λ$ and $Λ_c$ hypernuclei properties.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charmed hypernuclei are most bound for mass numbers between 16 and 41, with \(^{17}_{\Lambda_c}\)F being an excellent candidate for experimental measurement, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the N-\(\Lambda_c\) interaction derived from scaling the N-\(\Lambda\) interaction by a factor K=0.8, and the results depend significantly on which of the three force sets (DF-NSC89-C, DF-NSC97a-C, DF-NSC97f-C) is used.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for six closed-shell charmed hypernuclei (\(^{5}_{\Lambda_c}\)Li, \(^{17}_{\Lambda_c}\)F, \(^{41}_{\Lambda_c}\)Sc, \(^{57}_{\Lambda_c}\)Cu, \(^{133}_{\Lambda_c}\)Sb, \(^{209}_{\Lambda_c}\)Bi) using the SLy5 Skyrme interaction for the nucleonic part. The N-\(\Lambda_c\) interaction was obtained by scaling three existing N-\(\Lambda\) density functionals (DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, DF-NSC97f) by a factor K=0.8, following the approach of Starkov et al. (1986), with Coulomb interaction included between protons and the positively charged \(\Lambda_c\) baryon.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda_c\) separation energy for \(^{17}_{\Lambda_c}\)F is predicted to be -7 MeV for DF-NSC89-C, -12 MeV for DF-NSC97a-C, and -8 MeV for DF-NSC97f-C.
* The maximum difference in \(\Lambda_c\) separation energies between the DF-NSC89-C and DF-NSC97a-C force sets is 6 MeV across the studied mass range.
* The difference in the 1s channel binding between DF-NSC97a-C and DF-NSC89-C is 3 MeV for \(^{5}_{\Lambda_c}\)Li and 5 MeV for \(^{133}_{\Lambda_c}\)Sb.
* The average difference in \(\Lambda_c\) Fermi energies is 5 MeV between DF-NSC89-C and DF-NSC97a-C, and 2 MeV between DF-NSC89-C and DF-NSC97f-C.

## Context

This work extends previous density functional theory studies of charmed hypernuclei, including relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations by Tan et al. (2004), quark meson coupling (QMC) model calculations by Tsushima et al. (2003, 2004) and Wu et al. (2020), and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana et al. (2019). The results are compatible with the QMC predictions of Wu et al. (2020) but contrast with the HAL QCD results of Miyamoto et al. (2018), which predicted bound \(\Lambda_c\) hypernuclei only up to A=58, whereas the present work finds bound states up to \(^{209}_{\Lambda_c}\)Bi. The study enables future experimental searches at GSI-FAIR and JPARC facilities by identifying \(^{17}_{\Lambda_c}\)F as the most promising candidate for detection.
