# Light (anti)nuclei production in Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=200$GeV

> The production of light (anti)nuclei have been investigated using the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence model based on the final-state hadrons generated by the PACIAE model in Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=200$GeV with $|η|<0.5$ and $0<p_T<8$GeV/c. The results show that there is a strong centrality dependence of yields of $\rm d$, $\rm\overline d$, $\rm ^3He$, $\rm^3\overline {He}$, $\rm ^4He$, and $\rm^4\overline {He}$, i.e., their yields decrease rapidly with the increase of centrality, and the greater the mass is, the greater the dependence shows; whereas their ratio of antinucleus to nucleus and coalescence parameter $B_A$ remain constant as the centrality increases. In addition, the yields of (anti)nuclei are strongly dependent on the mass of the (anti)nuclei, indicating that the (anti)nuclei produced have mass scaling properties in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Our results are consistent with the STAR experimental data.

# Light (anti)nuclei production in Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Key claim

The dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model, applied to final-state hadrons from the PACIAE model, reproduces the centrality and mass-number dependence of light (anti)nuclei yields in Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, with the caveat that the model parameters (K factor and PYTHIA parj parameters) were fitted to STAR data for p, anti-p, Lambda, and anti-Lambda yields before predicting the (anti)nuclei yields.

## Method

The PACIAE model (based on PYTHIA 6.4) simulates Cu+Cu collisions through four stages: parton initiation, parton rescattering via 2-to-2 LO-pQCD cross sections, hadronization by Lund string fragmentation, and hadron rescattering until freeze-out. The final-state nucleons and hyperons are then fed into the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model, which forms light (anti)nuclei by requiring that candidate nucleons satisfy an invariant mass constraint (m_0 <= m_inv <= m_0 + Delta m) and a spatial coalescence condition (q_ij <= D_0, where D_0 = 2 r_0 A^{1/3} with values 1.50, 2.02, and 2.23 fm for d, He-3, and He-4 respectively). The yields are computed for centrality bins 0-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-60% with |eta| < 0.5 and 0 < p_T < 8 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Integrated yield dN/dy of deuterons in 0-10% centrality: (26.77 +/- 0.15)E-03; in 40-60% centrality: (3.84 +/- 0.15)E-04
- Integrated yield dN/dy of anti-deuterons in 0-10% centrality: (17.90 +/- 0.08)E-03; in 40-60% centrality: (2.46 +/- 0.02)E-04
- Integrated yield dN/dy of He-3 in 0-10% centrality: (6.76 +/- 0.66)E-05; in 40-60% centrality: (2.26 +/- 0.45)E-07
- Integrated yield dN/dy of anti-He-3 in 0-10% centrality: (3.46 +/- 0.40)E-05; in 40-60% centrality: (1.12 +/- 0.25)E-07
- Integrated yield dN/dy of He-4 in 0-10% centrality: (9.16 +/- 0.44)E-08; in 40-60% centrality: (8.13 +/- 0.98)E-11
- Integrated yield dN/dy of anti-He-4 in 0-10% centrality: (3.60 +/- 0.18)E-08; in 40-60% centrality: (3.12 +/- 0.38)E-11
- Ratio anti-d/d remains approximately 0.66-0.69 across all centrality bins
- Ratio anti-He-3/He-3 remains approximately 0.50-0.52 across all centrality bins
- Ratio anti-He-4/He-4 remains approximately 0.37-0.41 across all centrality bins
- STAR minimum bias data for anti-d: (2.69 +/- 0.43)E-03; for anti-He-3: (0.59 +/- 0.09)E-05
- Coalescence parameter B_A for d, He-3, and He-4 remains roughly constant with centrality, with B_2 > B_3 > B_4

## Context

This work extends the PACIAE+DCPC approach previously applied to Au+Au collisions (Chen et al., several years) and Pb+Pb collisions (She et al., several years) to the Cu+Cu system at the same center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair. The results are consistent with STAR experimental data (Aggarwal et al., 2011; Agakishiev et al., 2011; Abelev et al., 2009; Zhou et al., several years) and confirm the exponential mass-number scaling of (anti)nuclei yields observed by STAR in Au+Au collisions (Agakishiev et al., 2011). The study enables predictions for heavier (anti)nuclei production rates in smaller collision systems and provides a test of the coalescence mechanism across different system sizes.
