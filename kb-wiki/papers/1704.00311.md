# Proton distributions in $dp$ dielectron production within Regge theory

> The processes of dielectron production in deuteron-proton collisions at intermediate incident deuteron beam energies are analyzed in the spectator model within the one-pion exchange reggeized approach. We focus mainly on the momentum and angle distributions of the proton-spectator and the proton emitted in quasi-free $NN$ processes at small angles in the laboratory frame. It is shown that the inclusion of many channels in quasi-free $NN$ interaction allows us to describe the HADES data quite satisfactorily at incident deuteron kinetic energies of about 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The spectator model within the one-pion exchange reggeized (OPER) approach, including many channels in quasi-free NN interactions, describes the HADES data on proton momentum and angle distributions in dp dielectron production quite satisfactorily at incident deuteron kinetic energies of about 2.5 GeV, though the description is less satisfactory in the region of 4.0° < Θ_FW < 6.0° at M_ee > 140 MeV/c² and p_FW > 2000 MeV/c where error bars are large.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo simulation that inputs the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function and differential cross sections for NN → NN X e⁺e⁻ reactions calculated within the one-pion exchange reggeized (OPER) model. They included multiple channels for dielectron production in quasi-free np and pp interactions, including π⁰, η⁰, ρ⁰ meson production and Δ-isobar decays, with decay amplitudes calculated using the PLUTO model. The calculated momentum and angular distributions of protons registered by the Forward Wall (FW) detector were compared to HADES experimental data for two dielectron mass ranges (M_ee < 140 MeV/c² and M_ee > 140 MeV/c²) and three angular intervals (0.5°–2.0°, 2.0°–4.0°, and 4.0°–6.0°).

## Key numbers

- Incident deuteron kinetic energy: about 2.5 GeV
- The FW detector covered polar angles between 0.3° and 7.0°, located 7 m downstream from the proton target
- The slope of the pion Regge trajectory α'_π = 0.7 GeV⁻²
- The Regge radius parameter R²₁ = 3.3 GeV⁻² was chosen from best description of observables
- At M_ee < 0.140 GeV/c², the np → np π⁰ channel contributes 0.826 (82.6%) of the total yield at 0.5° < Θ < 2.0°, 0.759 at 2.0° < Θ < 4.0°, and 0.533 at 4.0° < Θ < 6.0°
- At M_ee > 0.140 GeV/c², the np → np e⁺e⁻ channel contributes 0.463 (46.3%) at 0.5° < Θ < 2.0°, 0.292 at 2.0° < Θ < 4.0°, and 0.259 at 4.0° < Θ < 6.0°
- The effective mass spectrum is described satisfactorily except at M_ee > 0.6 GeV/c²
- The transverse momentum spectrum is described satisfactorily at p_T ≤ 0.4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the OPER model, previously applied successfully to two-pion production in np collisions at kinetic energies of 1–5 GeV (Jerusalimov et al., 2015; HADES Collaboration, 2015), to dielectron production in dp collisions. The analysis uses the spectator mechanism where dielectrons are produced in quasi-free np or pp interactions, with the spectator proton detected by the HADES Forward Wall. The results enable interpretation of dielectron spectra measured by HADES in elementary reactions, which is important for understanding vector meson properties at finite baryonic densities and for studying baryonic resonances in heavy-ion collisions at 1–2 AGeV.
