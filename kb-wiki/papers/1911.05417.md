# $g$-matrix folding-model approach to reaction cross sections for scattering of Ca isotopes on a C target

> We first predict the ground-state properties of Ca isotopes, using the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) with and without the angular momentum projection (AMP). We find that $^{64}$Ca is an even-dripline nucleus and $^{59}$Ca is an odd-dripline nucleus, using $A$ dependence of the one-neutron separation energy $S_{1}$ and the two-neutron separation energy, $S_{2}$. As for $S_{1}$, $S_{2}$ and the binding energies $E_{\rm B}$, our results agree with the experimental data in $^{40-58}$Ca. As other ground-state properties of $^{40-60,62,64}$Ca, we predict charge, proton, neutron, matter radii, neutron skin and deformation. As for charge radii, our results are consistent with the experimental data in $^{40-52}$Ca. For $^{48}$Ca, our results on proton, neutron, matter radii agree with the experimental data. Very lately, Tanaka et. al. measured interaction cross sections for $^{42-51}$Ca scattering on a $^{12}$C target at an incident energy per nucleon of $E_{\rm lab}=280$MeV. Secondly, we predict reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}$ for $^{40-60,62,64}$Ca, using a chiral $g$-matrix double-folding model (DFM). To show the reliability of the present DFM for $σ_{\rm R}$, we apply the DFM for the data on $^{12}$C scattering on $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al targets in $30 < E_{\rm lab} < 400 $MeV, and show that the present DFM is good in $30 < E_{\rm lab} < 100 $MeV and $250 < E_{\rm lab} < 400 $MeV. For $110 < E_{\rm lab} < 240 $MeV, our results have small errors. To improve the present DFM for $σ_{\rm R}$, we propose two prescriptions.

## Key claim

The authors predict that $^{64}$Ca is an even-dripline nucleus and $^{59}$Ca is an odd-dripline nucleus, based on the mass-number dependence of one- and two-neutron separation energies calculated with the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) method; the abstract does not specify that the angular momentum projection (AMP) was not applied for odd nuclei due to computational complexity.

## Method

The authors used the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) method, with and without angular momentum projection (AMP) for even Ca isotopes, to calculate ground-state properties including binding energies, radii, and deformation parameters. They then predicted reaction cross sections $\sigma_{\rm R}$ for $^{40-60,62,64}$Ca scattering on a $^{12}$C target at $E_{\rm lab}=280$ MeV using the Kyushu chiral $g$-matrix double-folding model (DFM), with the projectile and target densities taken from the GHFB calculations.

## Key numbers

- $^{64}$Ca is identified as the even-dripline nucleus and $^{59}$Ca as the odd-dripline nucleus, based on $S_1$ and $S_2$ from GHFB calculations.
- For $^{40-52}$Ca, GHFB charge radii $r_{\rm ch}$ deviate from experimental data by at most 0.9%.
- For $^{48}$Ca, the GHFB+AMP matter radius $r_{\rm m}=3.539$ fm deviates from experimental data by 1.1%.
- For $^{48}$Ca, the experimental neutron skin $r_{\rm skin}=0.14$--0.20 fm from Birkhan (2016) yields $r_{\rm n}=3.54$--3.60 fm and $r_{\rm m}=3.48$--3.52 fm.
- The GHFB+AMP binding energies for even Ca isotopes deviate from GHFB results by at most 0.73%.
- The GHFB+AMP charge radii for even Ca isotopes deviate from GHFB results by at most 0.66%.
- The GHFB+AMP matter radii for even Ca isotopes deviate from GHFB results by at most 0.8%.
- The Kyushu $g$-matrix DFM is reliable for $\sigma_{\rm R}$ in $30 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 100$ MeV and $250 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 400$ MeV, with small errors in $110 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 240$ MeV.
- At $E_{\rm lab}=380$ MeV, the $t$-matrix DFM overestimates $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C $\sigma_{\rm R}$ data by only 4%.
- The fitted DFM for $^{12}$C scattering on $^{9}$Be overestimates data by at most 13% for $E_{\rm lab}$ below 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Kyushu $g$-matrix double-folding model previously applied to proton and $^4$He scattering (Toyokawa 2014, 2015, 2017) to reaction cross sections for Ca isotopes, motivated by upcoming measurements by Tanaka (2019) for $^{42-51}$Ca. The structure calculations using GHFB and GHFB+AMP (Tagami) are compared with coupled-cluster results using NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ (Hagen 2015) and charge radii data from Angeli (2013). The predicted reaction cross sections will enable extraction of matter radii for Ca isotopes, similar to prior work for halo nuclei such as $^6$He, $^{11}$Li (Tanihata 1986), $^{19}$C (Kanungo 2016), and $^{37}$Mg (Takechi 2014).
