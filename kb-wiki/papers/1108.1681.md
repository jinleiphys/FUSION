# Chirally symmetric and confining dense matter with a diffused quark Fermi surface

> It is possible that at low temperatures and large density there exists a confining matter with restored chiral symmetry, just after the dense nuclear matter with broken chiral symmetry. Such a phase has sofar been studied within a confining and chirally symmetric model assuming a rigid quark Fermi surface. In the confining quarkyonic matter, however, near the Fermi surface quarks group into color-singlet baryons. Interaction between quarks leads to a diffusion of the quark Fermi surface. Here we study effects of such diffusion and verify that it does not destroy a possible existence of a confining but chirally symmetric matter at low temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper verifies that a confining, chirally symmetric phase of dense matter can exist even when the quark Fermi surface is diffused (smeared) rather than rigid, as long as the diffusion width is not unrealistically large. The authors conclude that for any reasonable diffusion width there always exists a critical "Fermi momentum" at which the chiral restoration phase transition persists.

## Method

The authors use a 3+1 dimensional model with an instantaneous linear confining potential (Coulomb-type) between quarks, which is manifestly confining and chirally symmetric. They parameterize a smooth valence quark distribution function with a diffusion width Δ, then solve the gap equation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, incorporating Pauli blocking via a weight function that removes occupied quark levels from the integration.

## Key numbers

- Critical Fermi momentum for a rigid Fermi surface: p_f^cr(Δ=0) = 0.109 √σ (in units of the string tension σ)
- For a small diffusion width Δ = 0.02 √σ (much smaller than the critical Fermi momentum), the phase transition occurs at "Fermi momenta" rather close to the rigid-surface critical value
- Quark condensate is shown in units of σ^(3/2) as a function of Fermi momentum (in units of √σ) for various diffusion widths Δ (also in units of √σ)
- Meson masses (pseudoscalar and scalar) are shown in units of √σ as functions of p_f and Δ, demonstrating degeneracy above the critical Fermi momentum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Glozman, Wagenbrunn 2008) which assumed a rigid quark Fermi surface and found a chiral restoration phase transition in confining dense matter. The present paper addresses the more realistic case where quarks near the Fermi surface cluster into color-singlet baryons, causing diffusion of the Fermi surface. The model builds on the 't Hooft model (1974) and its 3+1 dimensional generalization by Adler and Davis (1984) and by 't Hooft's group. The results support the possibility of a quarkyonic matter phase (Pisarski 2007) with restored chiral symmetry but persistent confinement, which could exist at densities much higher than normal nuclear matter density.
