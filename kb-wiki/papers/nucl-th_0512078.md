# Universal scaling of the elliptic flow data at RHIC

> Recent PHOBOS measurements of the excitation function for the pseudo-rapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, have posed a significant theoretical challenge. Here we show that these differential measurements, as well as the RHIC measurements on transverse momentum satisfy a universal scaling relation predicted by the Buda-Lund model, based on exact solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics. We also show that recently found transverse kinetic energy scaling of the elliptic flow is a special case of this universal scaling.

## Key claim

The paper shows that PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR measurements of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC satisfy a universal scaling relation predicted by the Buda-Lund model, with the caveat that small scaling violations appear for transverse momentum data above 2 GeV and for pseudorapidity data near the beam rapidity.

## Method

The authors fit the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model to PHOBOS pseudorapidity-dependent elliptic flow data at multiple collision energies (19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) and centralities, and to PHENIX and STAR transverse momentum-dependent elliptic flow data for pions, kaons, and protons at 200 GeV. They extracted the scaling variable w from these fits using approximative formulas, then plotted all v2 data against w to test the predicted universal scaling curve v2 = I1(w)/I0(w).

## Key numbers

- PHOBOS veta fits at 200 GeV: A = 0.048 ± 0.001, B = 0.33 ± 0.01, chi2/NDF = 18/15 (CL = 28%)
- PHOBOS veta fits at 19.6 GeV: A = 0.035 ± 0.004, B = 0.53 ± 0.1, chi2/NDF = 1.7/11 (CL = 91%)
- STAR vpt fits for pions: A' = 5.4 ± 0.1 × 10^-4 /MeV, B' = 16 ± 1 × 10^-4 /MeV, C' = -1.5 ± 0.1 × 10^-6 /MeV^2, chi2/NDF = 96/27
- PHENIX vpt fits for pions: A' = 7.8 ± 0.2 × 10^-4 /MeV, B' = 1.4 ± 0.6 × 10^-4 /MeV, C' = -1.6 ± 0.3 × 10^-7 /MeV^2, chi2/NDF = 21/10
- Relative error of reconstructed w variable ranges from 5-50%, above 20% only for veta points with large eta and pion vpt points with large pt
- Scaling violations observed for pt > 2 GeV and for pseudorapidity beyond etabeam - 0.5

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model predictions from Csorgo (2001) and Csanad (2003) to experimental data from PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations. The universal scaling found here is more general than the transverse kinetic energy scaling of v2 discovered by PHENIX (Adare 2006, Afanasiev 2007), which the authors show is a special case of their result at mid-rapidity. The scaling differs from azimuthally sensitive Blast-wave models (Retiere 2003) where v2 depends on coordinate space anisotropy, whereas the Buda-Lund model predicts v2 depends only on momentum space anisotropy. This work enables future tests of higher-order flow predictions v2n = In(w)/I0(w) and suggests these should be applied at the quark level.
