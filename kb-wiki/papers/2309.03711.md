# Search for nonextensivity in electron-proton interactions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 300 GeV

> Study of canonical entropy in electron-proton interactions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 300 GeV is presented. The precision data collected by the H1 experiment at the HERA in different ranges of invariant hadronic mass $W$ and the squared four-momentum exchange $Q^{2}$ in $ep$ interactions have been analyzed in the ensemble theory approach. The canonical partition function relates to the multiplicity distribution which is often studied in collider experiments. We use the canonical ensemble partition function to explore the dynamics of hadron production in $ep$ interactions by devising different methods to find the entropic parameter and the collision temperature. The inverse slope of the transverse momentum spectrum of produced hadrons also relates to the temperature. In the recent past, the CMS, ATLAS and ALICE experiments at the LHC have studied the charged hadron transverse momentum and particle distributions in proton-proton and proton-nucleus interactions by using the Tsallis function within this approach. A detailed investigation into the role of the system volume and relation amongst different dynamical parameters reveals interesting results.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics (with entropic index \(q>1\)) can describe charged-particle multiplicity distributions in electron-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=300\) GeV, but only when a Van der Waals excluded volume is introduced to regularize the otherwise divergent partition function of the ideal Tsallis gas.

## Method
The authors fit negative binomial distributions (NBD) to charged-particle multiplicity data from the H1 experiment at HERA (four bins of invariant hadronic mass \(W\) and four pseudorapidity intervals each) and from ISR proton-proton collisions (four energies). Using the NBD parameters \(\langle N \rangle\) and \(k\), they solve two coupled equations derived from the Tsallis–Van der Waals partition function to extract the entropic index \(q\) and the inverse temperature \(\beta\). Two independent solution methods (graphical intersection and numerical root-finding) are cross-validated on the ISR 44.5 GeV data against Aguiar and Kodama (2003).

## Key numbers
- For \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=44.5\) GeV: \(\beta^{-1} \approx 162.60\) MeV, \(q \approx 1.0089\) (validation against Aguiar and Kodama (2003)).
- For \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=30.4\) GeV: \(q = 1.0076 \pm 0.0004\), \(\beta^{-1} = 160.81 \pm 1.11\) MeV.
- For \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=62.2\) GeV: \(q = 1.0097 \pm 0.0004\), \(\beta^{-1} = 164.46 \pm 0.92\) MeV.
- The entropic index \(q\) increases linearly with \(\sqrt{s}\) in \(pp\) data as \(q = 7.4849\times10^{-5}\,\sqrt{s} + 1.0055\).
- The excluded volume is fixed at \(v_0 = 0.368\) fm\(^3\) and system volume at \(V = 40.1\) fm\(^3\), taken from Aguiar and Kodama (2003).
- For \(ep\) data at HERA (\(\sqrt{s}=300\) GeV), 16 datasets (4 \(W\) ranges \(\times\) 4 pseudorapidity sectors) are analyzed; NBD fit results are tabulated but the extracted \(q\) and \(\beta^{-1}\) values for \(ep\) are not given as explicit numbers in the truncated text.

## Context
This work extends the Tsallis-statistics analysis of multiplicity distributions, previously applied to \(pp\) and heavy-ion collisions by CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE at the LHC, to electron-proton collisions at HERA for the first time. It builds on the theoretical framework of Aguiar and Kodama (2003), who derived the Tsallis–Van der Waals partition function for hadron gas, and on earlier NBD-based studies of multiplicity distributions at ISR and LEP. The results enable a comparison of nonextensive parameters across different collision systems and energies, and may inform future studies of thermalization and entropy in deep inelastic scattering.
