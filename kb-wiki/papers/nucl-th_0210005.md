# Transverse spectra of hadrons at RHIC

> We present results on spectra of pions, kaons and (anti)protons from a study of heavy ion collisions using the perturbative QCD + saturation model to calculate the production of initial (transverse) energy and baryon number followed by a hydrodynamic description of the expansion of produced matter. In particular, we study how the hadron spectra and multiplicities depend on the decoupling temperature $\Tdec$ when the low temperature phase contains all hadrons and and hadron resonances with mass below 2 GeV. We show that the spectra and multiplicities of pions, kaons and (anti)protons measured at RHIC in central Au+Au collisions with $\sqrt s=130$ GeV can be obtained with a single decoupling temperature 150$...$160 MeV, common for both the chemical and the kinetic freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the measured transverse momentum spectra and multiplicities of pions, kaons, and (anti)protons in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 GeV can be simultaneously reproduced using a single decoupling temperature of 150–160 MeV, common for both chemical and kinetic freeze-out, within a model that combines perturbative QCD with saturation and hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors first calculate the initial transverse energy and net baryon number production using a perturbative QCD + saturation model (EKRT model), where the minijet cut-off momentum is fixed by a saturation condition. These initial conditions are then fed into a hydrodynamic description of the expanding matter, using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition from an ideal QGP at T_c = 165 MeV to a hadron resonance gas containing all hadrons and resonances with mass below 2 GeV. Final hadron spectra are obtained via the Cooper–Frye freeze-out procedure, including two- and three-body decays of all resonances.

## Key numbers

- For p_T > 1.5 GeV, decreasing T_dec from 150 MeV to 120 MeV changes the inverse slope T_eff from 265 MeV to 305 MeV for pions, from 275 MeV to 330 MeV for kaons, and from 295 MeV to 375 MeV for protons.
- The average transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ changes from 0.46, 0.65, and 0.86 GeV to 0.53, 0.78, and 1.08 GeV for pions, kaons, and protons, respectively, when T_dec is lowered from 150 MeV to 120 MeV.
- Decreasing T_dec from 150 MeV to 120 MeV reduces the multiplicity of kaons by 21% and of protons by 52%, while pion multiplicity increases slightly.
- For T_dec = 150 MeV, the calculated yields of protons and antiprotons (corrected for hyperon feed-down) are dN/dy = 20.2 and 13.1, respectively, in agreement with PHENIX measurements of 19.3 ± 0.6 and 13.7 ± 0.7.
- The calculated ratio anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.70 is consistent with the PHENIX result of 0.75 ± 0.09.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD + saturation + hydrodynamics model (EKRT, 2000; ENRR, 2001) to predict detailed hadron spectra at RHIC, building on earlier studies that successfully described multiplicities and transverse energy at mid-rapidity (ERRT, 2001). The finding that a single decoupling temperature of 150–160 MeV simultaneously reproduces multiplicities, spectral slopes, and baryon-to-antibaryon ratios supports the conclusion of Redlich (2001) from thermal models that chemical freeze-out occurs around this temperature, and is consistent with the analysis of Florkowski (2002). The results enable further tests of the model at higher RHIC energies and with more differential observables, and raise questions about the thermalization time scale, as the initial time τ_0 ≈ 0.2 fm/c is argued to be too short for full thermalization.
