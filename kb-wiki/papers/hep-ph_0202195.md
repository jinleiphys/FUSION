# The Elusiveness of Infrared Critical Exponents in Landau Gauge Yang--Mills Theories

> We solve a truncated system of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in SU($N_c$) Yang-Mills theories in Faddeev-Popov quantization on a four-torus. This compact space-time manifold provides an efficient mean to solve the gluon and ghost Dyson-Schwinger equations without any angular approximations. We verify that analytically two power-like solutions in the very far infrared seem possible. However, only one of these solutions can be matched to a numerical solution for non-vanishing momenta. For a bare ghost-gluon vertex this implies that the gluon propagator is only weakly infrared vanishing, $D_{gl}(k^2) \propto (k^2)^{2κ-1}$, $κ\approx 0.595$, and the ghost propagator is infrared singular, $D_{gh}(k^2) \propto (k^2)^{-κ-1}$. For non-vanishing momenta our solutions are in agreement with the results of recent SU(2) Monte-Carlo lattice calculations. The running coupling possesses an infrared fixed point. We obtain $α(0) = 8.92/N_c$ for all gauge groups SU($N_c$). Above one GeV the running coupling rapidly approaches its perturbative form.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while two power-like infrared solutions for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theories are analytically possible, only one of these solutions (with κ ≈ 0.595) can be matched to a numerical solution for non-vanishing momenta; the other solution (κ = 1) cannot be realized numerically.

## Method

The authors solve a truncated system of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in SU(N_c) Yang-Mills theories formulated on a four-torus, which allows them to avoid angular approximations in the momentum integrals. They employ a bare ghost-gluon vertex truncation including the gluon loop, using a one-parameter family of projectors to handle ultraviolet divergences, and enforce correct one-loop scaling in the ultraviolet through a two-parameter ansatz for the gluon vertex renormalization constant.

## Key numbers

- Infrared exponent κ ≈ 0.595 for the gluon propagator scaling D_gl(k²) ∝ (k²)^(2κ-1) and ghost propagator scaling D_gh(k²) ∝ (k²)^(-κ-1) with the transverse projector (ζ=1)
- Infrared fixed point of the running coupling α(0) = 8.92/N_c for all gauge groups SU(N_c), giving α(0) = 2.972 for N_c=3
- For the Brown-Pennington projector (ζ=4), the infrared analysis yields κ = 1, but no numerical solution for non-vanishing momenta could be found matching this behavior
- The one-loop anomalous dimensions are γ = -13/22 and δ = -9/44 for the gluon and ghost dressing functions respectively

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger studies of von Smekal (1997) and Atkinson (1998) by solving the equations on a compact manifold without angular approximations, resolving a long-standing ambiguity about which infrared exponent is physically realized. The results agree with recent SU(2) lattice calculations for non-vanishing momenta and confirm the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion through the infrared singular ghost propagator. The infrared fixed point of the running coupling enables future studies of non-perturbative QCD phenomena and hadronic physics.
