# Lattice-based equation of state with a critical point from constant entropy contours and its comparison to effective QCD approaches

> In this work, we systematically assess the performance of a new method from [H. Shah et al., Phys. Rev. C 113, L012201] for locating the QCD critical point using constant-entropy contours by testing it against various effective QCD approaches. We demonstrate that, while the method yields spurious critical points in purely hadronic models (HRG) due to non-parabolic contour behavior at low temperatures ($T \lesssim 120$ MeV), it accurately reproduces the CP location in frameworks that feature a genuine phase transition and benchmarked against lattice QCD, such as Holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton, and Functional QCD approaches. Building on our previous determination of constant entropy contours using lattice data, we extend that analysis to construct a complete Lattice-based Equation of State (EoS) at finite density, which features a critical point at $(T, μ_B) \approx (114, 602)$ MeV. By integrating the extrapolated entropy density with respect to temperature, we reconstruct the pressure, baryon density, susceptibility, and speed of sound in the critical region, and analyze the focusing behavior of isentropic trajectories in the vicinity of the critical point.

# Lattice-based equation of state with a critical point from constant entropy contours and its comparison to effective QCD approaches

## Key claim

The constant-entropy contour expansion truncated at second order in baryon chemical potential successfully reproduces the critical point location in holographic and functional QCD approaches that feature a genuine phase transition and are benchmarked against lattice QCD, but it yields spurious critical points in hadronic resonance gas models at low temperatures (T0 below 120 MeV) due to non-parabolic contour behavior. The lattice-based estimate of the critical point at (T, μB) ≈ (114, 602) MeV is compatible with predictions from holographic and functional QCD frameworks, though the possibility remains that this estimate does not correspond to a true critical point of QCD.

## Method

The method expands constant entropy density contours from zero baryon chemical potential to second order in μB, using lattice QCD input for the entropy density and baryon susceptibility at μB = 0 to compute the expansion coefficient α2(T0). The critical point is located by solving α2''(T0,c) = 0 to determine T0,c, then computing μB,c from the spinodal condition, and finally obtaining Tc from the expansion. The method was tested on ideal gas, hadronic resonance gas models (ideal HRG, excluded volume HRG, van der Waals HRG, cluster expansion model), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, a holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton black hole model, and a functional QCD (fRG-DSE) approach, and was also applied to construct a complete lattice-based equation of state by integrating the extrapolated entropy density over temperature.

## Key numbers

- Lattice-based critical point estimate: (T, μB) = (114, 602) MeV
- Holographic black hole model: quadratic expansion yields CP at (T, μB) = (104, 637) MeV; actual CP in model is at (103, 599) MeV (1.4% error in T, 6.4% error in μB)
- Holographic model energy density contours yield CP at (104, 611) MeV; enthalpy density contours yield (104, 638) MeV
- Functional QCD (fRG-DSE) approach: quadratic expansion reproduces CP within 10% of the actual value
- Ideal HRG model: spurious CP from entropy contours at (T, μB) = (86, 817) MeV; from enthalpy at (88, 791) MeV; from energy at (86, 777) MeV
- Excluded volume HRG model: spurious CP at (T, μB) = (85, 831) MeV
- van der Waals HRG model: spurious CP from entropy contours at (T, μB) ≈ (85, 830) MeV; from energy at (86, 789) MeV; from enthalpy at (87, 805) MeV
- Cluster expansion model: spurious CP at (T, μB) = (95, 815) MeV
- Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (standard parameterization): actual CP at (T, μB) = (82, 966) MeV; method produces null result (no CP)
- Spurious critical points in HRG-type models consistently appear at T0,c ≤ 120 MeV
- van der Waals HRG model contains a nuclear liquid-gas CP at (T, μB) ≈ (20, 908) MeV, distinct from the spurious signal

## Context

This work systematically tests the constant-entropy contour method introduced by Shah et al. (2024) against multiple QCD-inspired models, extending the analysis of Marczenko et al. (2025) who explored limitations in NJL model parametrizations. The method was previously applied by the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2024) to exclude a critical point for μB below 450 MeV under strangeness-neutral conditions. The resulting lattice-based equation of state with a critical point can be used to test critical point effects in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and to compare baryon number fluctuation predictions with proton cumulant measurements from RHIC and future FAIR experiments, though caveats regarding theory-to-experiment comparisons must be addressed.
