# Symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter in the Thomas-Fermi model at finite temperatures

> The properties of warm symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated in the frame of the Thomas-Fermi approximation using a recent modern parametrization of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction of Myers and Swiatecki. Special attention is paid to the liquid-gas phase transition, which is of special interest in modern nuclear physics. We have determined the critical temperature, critical density and the so-called flash temperature. Furthermore the equation of state for cold neutron star matter was calculated.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Fermi model with the Myers and Swiatecki interaction predicts a liquid-gas phase transition in warm nuclear matter, with a critical temperature of 20.8 MeV for symmetric matter, but this transition disappears for asymmetries larger than 0.853.

## Method

The authors extended the zero-temperature Thomas-Fermi model of Myers and Swiatecki to finite temperatures by incorporating Fermi-Dirac distribution functions into the single-particle potential and density expressions. They calculated the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and intermediate asymmetries at various temperatures, using a Yukawa-type finite-range effective interaction with six free parameters fitted to nuclear matter properties. The liquid-gas phase transition was analyzed via Maxwell construction, and the flash temperature was determined from the condition where the isothermal incompressibility vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: T_c = 20.8 MeV
- Flash temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: T_fl = 15.61 MeV, occurring at density ρ = 0.0973 fm^-3
- Saturation density of cold nuclear matter: ρ_0 = 0.16545 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: u = 16.527 MeV
- Incompressibility of cold nuclear matter: K = 301.27 MeV
- Asymmetry parameter: J = 31.375 MeV
- Maximum asymmetry for liquid-gas coexistence: δ = 0.853 (above which the phase transition disappears)
- Range parameter of the Yukawa interaction: a = 0.59542 fm

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach of Myers and Swiatecki (1990-1998) to finite temperatures, complementing earlier non-relativistic calculations by the Brueckner approach and relativistic Hartree treatments. The results for critical temperature (14-22 MeV range) and critical density (approximately 1/3 of saturation density) agree with other non-relativistic calculations cited in the paper. The model provides a simple theoretical tool for studying the nuclear equation of state relevant to heavy-ion collisions, supernova explosions, and neutron star structure, including protoneutron stars as studied by Prakash (1997) and Strobel (1998).
