# The Kaon-Photoproduction Of Nucleons In The Quark Model

> In this paper, we develop a general framework to study the meson-photoproductions of nucleons in the chiral quark model. The S and U channel resonance contributions are expressed in terms of the Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu (CGLN) amplitudes. The kaon-photoproduction processes, $γp\to K^+ Λ$, $γp\to K^+ Σ^0$, and $γp\to K^0Σ^+$, are calculated. The initial results show that the quark model provides a much improved description of the reaction mechanism for the kaon-photoproductions of the nucleon with less parameters than the traditional phenomenological approaches.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model provides a much improved description of kaon-photoproduction reaction mechanisms with fewer parameters than traditional phenomenological approaches, though the author notes that many effects have not been included and no serious effort was made to fit experimental data, making the success "very encouraging" rather than definitive.

## Method

The author developed a general framework for meson-photoproduction of nucleons in the chiral quark model, expressing S- and U-channel resonance contributions in terms of Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu (CGLN) amplitudes. The transition amplitudes were derived from a low-energy QCD Lagrangian assuming kaons as Goldstone bosons, with nonrelativistic expansion of the quark-photon-kaon interaction. The calculations included leading-order Born amplitudes (seagull term, nucleon exchange in s-channel, Lambda and Sigma exchange in u-channel, kaon and K* exchange in t-channel), u-channel contributions from excited strange baryons (particularly Sigma*(1385)), and s-channel contributions from nonstrange baryon resonances up to n=2 in the harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The axial coupling constant g_A for kaon-nucleon-strange baryon couplings in the SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry limit is sqrt(3/2) for KPΛ and -1/(3*sqrt(2)) for KPΣ^0
- The magnetic moment for the Λ-Σ^0 transition is μ_{ΛΣ}=1.61
- The magnetic moments used for Σ*(1385) contributions are μ_Λ=-0.61, μ_{Σ^0}≈1.0, and μ_{Σ^+}=2.38
- The quark mass effects lead to about 30 percent deviation from the measured value of the coupling constant α_K via the generalized Goldberg-Treiman relation
- The form factor in the harmonic oscillator basis is F(k,q)=exp(-(k-q)^2/(6α^2)) where α is the oscillator strength
- The resonance S_{11}(1535) receives a coefficient of 0.6 and S_{11}(1650) receives 0.4 from mixing in the potential quark model calculation

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier investigation on threshold pion photoproduction (Li, 1994), which clarified the long-standing problem of the low energy theorem in that process. The approach contrasts with traditional hadronic models (Mart, year not specified) which showed a factor 10 to 100 larger cross section for γp→K^0Σ^+ than for γp→K^+Σ^0, while the quark model provides improved description. The framework is intended to be general, with η and π photoproductions related to kaon photoproduction by SU(3) coefficients, and enables future studies of effects not included in the present calculation.
