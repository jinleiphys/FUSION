# Jet quenching and the gravity dual

> We compute the momentum broadening of an energetic parton as it moves through strongly interacting matter, using the gravity dual of a non conformal gauge theory with matter in the fundamental representation. Our approach defines a family of theories, all of which have logarithmic running of the couplings in the far IR but becomes almost conformal in the far UV. For a given set of degrees of freedom, we obtain average transverse momentum square of the parton as a function of temperature and compare with results obtained from the AdS/CFT correspondence.

# Jet quenching and the gravity dual

## Key claim
The authors compute the transverse momentum broadening of an energetic parton moving through strongly interacting matter using a gravity dual of a non-conformal gauge theory with fundamental matter, obtaining a correction to the AdS/CFT result that depends on the number of effective degrees of freedom and is valid only for large N where the duality holds.

## Method
The authors construct a ten-dimensional dual geometry with a black hole horizon by placing N D3 branes at the tip of a conifold, wrapping M D5 branes around the S² of the base, and embedding Nf D7 branes, yielding an SU(N+M)×SU(N) gauge theory with SU(Nf)×SU(Nf) flavor symmetry and logarithmically running couplings. They compute the Wilson loop at strong coupling via holography using the Nambu-Goto action, derive the diffusion coefficient κ_T from the resulting Green's functions, and match the solution across a matching radius r_c where the warp factor transitions from logarithmic to power-law behavior.

## Key numbers
- The diffusion coefficient is given by κ_T = √(γ g_s N_eff) π T³ (1 + B), where B is of order O(g_s Ñ) and γ = 1/√(1-v²)
- The effective number of degrees of freedom N_eff = N (1 + 27g_s²M²N_f/(32π²N) - 3g_sM²/(4πN) + [3g_sM²/(4πN) - 9g_s²M²N_f/(16π²N)] log(r_c/r_0) + 9g_s²M²N_f/(8π²N) log²(r_c/r_0))
- The gauge coupling running is given by ∂/∂ ln Λ [4π²/ğ₁² + 4π²/ğ₂²] = -3N_f/4 and ∂/∂ ln Λ [4π²/ğ₁² - 4π²/ğ₂²] = 3M[1 + (3g_s N_f/(2π)) ln Λ]
- The temperature is T = ζ_h/(π√h(ζ_h)) where ζ_h = 1/r_h
- The warp factor for r < r_c is h = L⁴/r⁴ (1 + A ln(r/r_0) + B ln²(r/r_0)) with A, B of order O(g_s M²/N, g_s² N_f)

## Context
This work extends the AdS/CFT calculation of jet quenching parameter κ_T from the conformal case (DTCS) to a non-conformal gauge theory with fundamental matter, making it more relevant for QCD. The authors note that for N_f = M = 0, their result reduces to the value computed in DTCS, while for non-zero M and N_f the analysis generates a correction. This approach enables the study of various phases of a strongly interacting medium by using different brane configurations that yield different effective degrees of freedom for the boundary gauge theory.
