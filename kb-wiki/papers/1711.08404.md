# Fission of SHN & its hindrance: odd nuclei & isomers

> After shortly analyzing data relevant to fission hindrance of odd-A nuclei and high-$K$ isomers in superheavy (SH) region we point out the inconsistency of current fission theory and propose an approach based on the instanton formalism. A few results of this method, simplified by replacing selfconsistency by elements of the macro-micro model, are given to illustrate its features.

## Key claim

Current fission theory, which relies on the adiabatic approximation and cranking inertia, is fundamentally unsuitable for odd-A nuclei and high-K isomers because the cranking formula becomes nearly singular at avoided level crossings and can produce negative contributions to the inertia parameter; the authors propose an instanton-based approach as a more appropriate alternative.

## Method

The authors analyze experimental data on spontaneous fission hindrance in odd-A nuclei and high-K isomers in the superheavy region, then demonstrate the failure of the standard cranking formula for the inertia parameter when blocked orbitals are present. They adapt the instanton formalism from mean-field theory to a simplified macro-micro model using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, solving the imaginary-time dependent equations for single-particle states along a fission path and computing the resulting action.

## Key numbers

- Odd-A nuclei show fission half-lives 3-5 orders of magnitude longer than their even-even neighbours
- For $^{250}$No: ground state $T_{sf} = 3.7\ \mu$s, isomeric $T_{sf} > 45\ \mu$s, hindrance factor $> 10$
- For $^{254}$No: ground state $T_{sf} = 3\times 10^4$ s, isomeric $T_{sf} = 1400$ s, hindrance factor $\approx 1/20$
- For $^{254}$Rf $(8^-)$ isomer: ground state $T_{sf} = 23\ \mu$s, isomeric $T_{sf} > 50\ \mu$s, hindrance factor $> 2$
- For $^{254}$Rf $(16^+)$ isomer: isomeric $T_{sf} > 600\ \mu$s, hindrance factor $> 25$
- For multiple fission isomers in even-even actinides: hindrance factors of 1-10 for Pu isotopes and $10^3$-$10^4$ for Cm isotopes
- In a 4-level test system for $^{272}$Mt, the adiabatic action overestimates the instanton action by more than an order of magnitude (e.g., $S_{adiab}/\hbar = 55.048$ vs $S_{inst}/\hbar = 2.6818$ at $\hbar\dot{q}_{max} = 0.14$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fission hindrance in odd-A nuclei and high-K isomers previously reviewed by FisHess and Kon, specifically addressing the inconsistency between experimental data and theoretical predictions from specialization energy models. The authors build on the instanton formalism developed for mean-field settings by LNP, N2, and JS, and contrast their approach with the earlier idea of MorBa and calculations by SBPB regarding variable pairing gaps. The paper enables a new framework for calculating spontaneous fission half-lives in odd and odd-odd superheavy nuclei, with work on self-consistency and paired systems under way.
