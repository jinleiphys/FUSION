# Eta-photoproduction in a gauge-invariant chiral unitary framework

> We analyse photoproduction of eta mesons off the proton in a gauge-invariant chiral unitary framework. The interaction kernel for meson-baryon scattering is derived from the leading order chiral effective Lagrangian and iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation. The recent precise threshold data from the Crystal Ball at MAMI can be described rather well and the complex pole corresponding to the S11(1535) is extracted. An extension of the kernel is also discussed.

## Key claim
The authors show that a gauge-invariant chiral unitary framework, using only the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the driving term in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, can describe the recent precise threshold data for eta photoproduction off the proton rather well, but the description is restricted to center-of-mass energies below 1609.9 MeV because p- and d-waves become important at higher energies.

## Method
The meson-baryon scattering kernel is derived from the leading-order chiral effective Lagrangian, retaining only the Weinberg-Tomozawa contact interaction, and iterated to infinite order via a Bethe-Salpeter equation in d dimensions. The photoproduction amplitude is then constructed by coupling the photon to every possible line and vertex of the hadronic skeleton, following the gauge-invariant construction of Borasoy (2007), which automatically satisfies two-body unitarity in the meson-baryon channel subspace. The model parameters are four renormalization scales (one per isospin channel), fitted to differential cross section data from the Crystal Ball at MAMI.

## Key numbers
- The fit to Crystal Ball data yields a reduced chi-squared of χ²_dof = 0.9997 for the energy range 1487.8 MeV < E_cms < 1609.9 MeV.
- The complex pole of the S11(1535) resonance is extracted at E_cms = (1525.9 +4.4 -3.6 - i 111.4 +1.9 -2.0) MeV.
- The s-wave multipole E0+ at threshold is (-12.39 +1.51 -1.05 + i 16.15 +2.23 -1.85) × 10⁻³ / M_π⁺, with modulus |E0+| = 20.35 +2.41 -2.38 × 10⁻³ / M_π⁺.
- The ratio of imaginary to real part of E0+ at threshold is between 1.06 and 1.68.

## Context
This work extends the gauge-invariant chiral unitary framework developed by Borasoy (2007) for associated strangeness photoproduction to the process γp → ηp. It builds on earlier unitarized chiral perturbation theory studies of the S11(1535) resonance by Kaiser (1995, 1996) and others, but now incorporates gauge invariance explicitly. The results enable future extension to next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions, which the authors note are needed to reach higher energies and to study eta photoproduction off the neutron, with such work reported as underway by Bruns (2010) and Mai (2011).
