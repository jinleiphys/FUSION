# Relaxation of particle-hole type excitation in a Fermi system within the diffusion approximation of kinetic theory for the case of constant diffusion and drift coefficients

> The time evolution of the distribution function for a particle-hole excitation in a Fermi system was calculated using the direct numerical solution of a nonlinear diffusion equation in momentum space. A phenomenological expression for calculating the relaxation time of such an excitation to its equilibrium value has been proposed. It is shown that the relaxation time is dependent on both the excitation energy and the mass number.

## Key claim

The relaxation time of a particle-hole excitation in a Fermi system depends on both the excitation energy and the mass number, but the calculated relaxation times are approximately an order of magnitude smaller than expected, suggesting the method or the momentum-integrated quantity may not fully capture the relaxation at the Fermi surface itself.

## Method

The author derived a nonlinear diffusion equation in momentum space from the kinetic equation with a collision integral, using the diffusion approximation with constant diffusion and drift coefficients. The time evolution of the distribution function for an initial step distribution and for a particle-hole type excitation was obtained through direct numerical solution of this diffusion equation. A phenomenological method was proposed to calculate the relaxation time by integrating the normalized root-mean-square deviation of the distribution function from equilibrium over time, equating the area under the nonexponential decay curve to that under an equivalent exponential decay.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum: \(p_F \approx 8.4 \times 10^{-22}\) MeV·fm\(^{-1}\)·s, corresponding to Fermi energy \(E_F \approx 33.7\) MeV
- Diffusion coefficient: \(D_{p,0} \approx 3.4 \times 10^{-22}\) MeV\(^{2}\)·fm\(^{-2}\)·s
- Drift coefficient: \(K_{p,0} \approx -8.3 \times 10^{-23}\) MeV·fm\(^{-2}\)·s
- Equilibrium temperature: \(T_{eq} \approx 4\) MeV, with equilibrium Fermi energy \(E_{F,eq} \approx 33.3\) MeV
- Relaxation time for step-like initial distribution: \(\tau_{eq} \approx 7.3 \times 10^{-24}\) s
- Excitation energy range considered for particle-hole pairs: \(2 \leq E_{ex} \leq 10\) MeV, with hole energy fixed at \(E'_1 = E_F - 2\) MeV
- Nuclear radius parameter: \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm

## Context

This work extends the diffusion approximation approach previously applied by Wong (1982) and by Ko and collaborators (2014, 2015) to the relaxation of excitations in finite Fermi systems. The author builds on the analytical solution for constant transport coefficients from Wong (1982) and the numerical methods from Ko and Lukyanov (2014, 2015), while also using kinetic coefficient values from Lukyanov (2023). The study enables future investigation of momentum-dependent kinetic coefficients and raises open questions about the discrepancy between calculated relaxation times and expected values, as well as the independence of the equilibrium temperature from the excitation energy.
