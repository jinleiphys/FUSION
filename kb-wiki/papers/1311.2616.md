# Description of Induced Nuclear Fission with Skyrme Energy Functionals: I. Static Potential Energy Surfaces and Fission Fragment Properties

> Eighty years after its experimental discovery, a microscopic description of induced nuclear fission based solely on the interactions between neutrons and protons and quantum many-body methods still poses formidable challenges. The goal of this paper is to contribute to the development of a predictive microscopic framework for the accurate calculation of static properties of fission fragments for hot fission and thermal or slow neutrons. To this end, we focus on the 239Pu(n,f) reaction and employ nuclear density functional theory with Skyrme energy densities. Potential energy surfaces are computed at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation with up to five collective variables. We find that the triaxial degree of freedom plays an important role, both near the fission barrier and at scission. The impact of the parameterization of the Skyrme energy density on deformation properties from the ground-state up to scission is also quantified. We introduce a general template for the detailed description of fission fragment properties. It is based on the careful analysis of the scission point, using both advanced topological methods and recently proposed quantum many-body techniques. We conclude that an accurate prediction of fission fragment properties at low incident neutron energies, although technologically demanding, should be within the reach of current nuclear density functional theory.

# Description of Induced Nuclear Fission with Skyrme Energy Functionals: I. Static Potential Energy Surfaces and Fission Fragment Properties

## Key claim
An accurate prediction of static fission fragment properties at low incident neutron energies, although technologically demanding, should be within the reach of current nuclear density functional theory, provided that the triaxial degree of freedom is included and that the scission point is carefully identified using both topological methods and quantum localization techniques. The authors qualify that the work is an intermediate step toward a predictive theory, and that the potential energy surfaces computed here are only an auxiliary basis for the later time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) calculation of fission fragment yields.

## Method
The authors employed nuclear density functional theory (DFT) with Skyrme energy densities at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation, using up to five collective variables (elongation Q20, triaxiality Q22, mass asymmetry Q30, hexadecapole moment Q40, and Gaussian neck operator QN) to compute static potential energy surfaces for the compound nucleus 240Pu formed in the 239Pu(n,f) reaction. They used three Skyrme parametrizations (SkM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) and solved the HFB equations with the DFT solvers HFODD and HFBTHO in a one-center harmonic oscillator basis with Nmax = 31 and Nstates = 1100. Scission configurations were identified using both topological methods and the concept of quantum localization.

## Key numbers
- The ground-state of 240Pu is located at (q20, q22) approximately (35 b, 0 b); the fission isomer is at (q20, q22) approximately (80 b, 0 b).
- Including triaxiality lowers the first fission barrier by approximately 2 MeV; the degree of triaxiality at the first barrier is gamma approximately 32 degrees, and at the second barrier gamma approximately 15 degrees.
- The first fission barrier height with SkM* is EA approximately 7.64 MeV, about 1.6 MeV higher than the empirical barrier.
- The one-neutron separation energy of 240Pu computed with SkM* is Sn = 7.04 MeV.
- For SkM*, the scission point for asymmetric fission is located at (q20, q30, q40) approximately (345 b, 43 b^(3/2), 136 b^2).
- For UNEDF0, the scission point is at (q20, q30, q40) approximately (354 b, 44 b^(3/2), 144 b^2); for UNEDF1, at (354 b, 45 b^(3/2), 146 b^2).
- The prescission energy (difference between potential energy at the top of the second barrier and at scission) is approximately 12.5 MeV for UNEDF1 and only 3.4 MeV for UNEDF0.
- The surface-symmetry energy is assym = -44 MeV for UNEDF0 and assym = -29 MeV for UNEDF1.
- The basis truncation error on the total energy near the scission point is empirically estimated to be of the order of 2-3 MeV.
- For the Gogny D1S functional, the hot scission point for asymmetric fission is located around (q30, q40) approximately (64 b^(3/2), 187 b^2), while for SkM* it is (40 b^(3/2), 136 b^2).

## Context
This work extends the general description of induced fission developed over the years at the Commissariat a l'Energie Atomique in France and Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, as described in references such as Berger et al. (1984, 1986, 1989), Goutte et al. (2004, 2005), Dubray et al. (2008), and Younes et al. (2009, 2011, 2012, 2013). It contrasts with the macroscopic-microscopic approach used by Moller et al. (1995, 2009, 2012) and the Langevin equation methods of Nadtochy et al. (2007, 2012). The paper enables the next step: using these static potential energy surfaces as input for the time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) to compute fission fragment yields, mass and charge distributions, and kinetic energies in a forthcoming paper.
