# Spectral flow of trimer states of two heavy impurities and one light condensed boson

> The spectral flow of three-body (trimer) states consisting of two heavy (impurity) particles sitting in a condensate of light bosons is considered. Assuming that the condensate is weakly interaction and that an impurity and a boson have a zero-range two-body interaction, we use the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to determine the effective three-body potential. We solve the resulting Schrödinger equation numerically and determine the trimer binding energies as a function of the coherence length of the light bosonic condensate particles. The binding energy is found to be suppressed by the presence of the condensate when the energy scale corresponding to the coherence length becomes of order the trimer binding energy in the absence of the condensate. We find that the Efimov scaling property is reflected in the critical values of the condensate coherence length at which the trimers are pushed into the continuum.

# Spectral flow of trimer states of two heavy impurities and one light condensed boson

## Key claim

The condensate coherence length acts as an effective large-distance cutoff on the attractive inverse-square potential that produces Efimov trimers, suppressing binding when the energy scale corresponding to the coherence length becomes comparable to the trimer binding energy in the absence of the condensate. The Efimov scaling property is reflected in the critical values of the condensate coherence length at which the trimers are pushed into the continuum, but the quantitative predictions rely on a Born-Oppenheimer approximation that is not expected to hold when the coherence length becomes of order the impurity-boson scattering length.

## Method

The author models two heavy impurity particles embedded in a weakly interacting Bose-Einstein condensate of light bosons, assuming a zero-range impurity-boson interaction near resonance and a large mass ratio (M/m = 80). Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, the light boson's motion is solved for fixed impurity separation R, with the boson's dispersion modified by the condensate via Bogoliubov theory to include a linear low-momentum regime. The resulting effective impurity-impurity potential is obtained by solving the regularized momentum-space Schrödinger equation, and the three-body bound state energies are computed numerically by solving the radial Schrödinger equation for the heavy particles as a function of the condensate coherence length ξ.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov scaling parameter for mass ratio M/m = 80 is s₀ = 3.55, giving e^(π/s₀) = 2.42 and e^(2π/s₀) = 5.87
- For the non-condensed limit (ξ → ∞) with resonant impurity-boson interaction (|a| = ∞), the solution to the eigenvalue equation gives αR = x₀ ≈ 0.567
- The number of three-body Efimov states found numerically for large ξ is N_B = 8
- Ratios of binding energies between adjacent states range from 5.85 to 6.23, compared to the analytical value of 5.87
- Ratios of critical coherence lengths ξ_c between adjacent states range from 2.41 to 2.54, compared to the analytical value of 2.42
- The potential strength used in the numerical calculations corresponds to Ω = 40x₀² for mass ratio M/m = 80

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study (Zinner 2013) that suggested a dramatic effect of the condensate environment on the Efimov three-body spectrum, providing quantitative numerical confirmation and expansion of those ideas. The spectral flow of trimer binding energies with coherence length shows behavior similar to that seen for Efimov states in the presence of a degenerate Fermi sea (MacNeill and Nygaard 2011, Niemann 2012), with the Fermi energy scale replaced by the coherence length energy scale E_ξ = ħ²/Mξ². The results enable experimental guidance for observing Efimov states in mass-imbalanced bosonic mixtures such as ⁴He with ⁸⁷Rb, ⁷Li with ¹³³Cs, or Ytterbium isotopes with light atoms, where the condensate coherence length can be tuned via density or Feshbach resonances.
