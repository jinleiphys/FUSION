# Molecular vibrational states in the binary cold fission of $^{252}$Cf

> We predict a molecular vibrational state in the cold binary fission of $^{252}$Cf using a simple decay cluster model. The Hamiltonian of two even-even fragments in the pole-pole configuration is built in the same fashion as that for the dinuclear molecule formed in heavy-ions collisions. The interaction between the two fragments is described by the double-folding M3Y potential. The spectrum of the butterfly vibrations is derived and its dependence on fragments deformation and mass-assymetry is discussed. Some experimental implications are commented.

# Molecular vibrational states in the binary cold fission of $^{252}$Cf

## Key claim
The authors predict that butterfly-type molecular vibrational states can exist in the cold binary fission of $^{252}$Cf, but only for fragment pairs where both fragments are well-deformed; if one fragment is nearly spherical, the first excited state energy shifts upward by a factor of 10 or more, making such states unlikely to be observed.

## Method
The authors construct a Hamiltonian for two even-even fragments in a pole-pole (nose-to-nose) configuration, analogous to a dinuclear molecule formed in heavy-ion collisions. The interaction between fragments is described by the double-folding M3Y potential, expanded to monopole and quadrupole terms. The butterfly vibration stiffness is obtained from a second-order Taylor expansion of the potential in the angular variable, and the energy spectrum is derived by solving the zeroth-order Hamiltonian for an asymmetric giant molecule.

## Key numbers
- Butterfly zero-point energies $\hbar\omega_{\varepsilon}$ for even-even splittings of $^{252}$Cf range from 517.88 keV (for $^{94}$Kr + $^{158}$Sm) to 14497.94 keV (for $^{120}$Cd + $^{132}$Sn)
- For the $^{94}$Kr + $^{158}$Sm channel: $\hbar\omega_{\varepsilon} = 517.88$ keV, with light fragment deformation $\varepsilon_L = 0.270$ and heavy fragment deformation $\varepsilon_H = 0.300$
- For the $^{120}$Cd + $^{132}$Sn channel: $\hbar\omega_{\varepsilon} = 14497.94$ keV, with light fragment deformation $\varepsilon_L = 0.030$ and heavy fragment deformation $\varepsilon_H = -0.010$
- For the $^{112}$Ru + $^{140}$Xe channel: $\hbar\omega_{\varepsilon} = 2051.47$ keV, with $\varepsilon_L = 0.220$ and $\varepsilon_H = 0.120$
- For the $^{106}$Mo + $^{146}$Ba channel: $\hbar\omega_{\varepsilon} = 1074.16$ keV, with $\varepsilon_L = 0.245$ and $\varepsilon_H = 0.200$

## Context
This work extends the concept of nuclear molecules from heavy-ion collisions (Greiner and collaborators, 1984) to the cold fission of $^{252}$Cf, which was recently observed using multiple Ge-detector arrays and gamma-gamma-gamma coincidence techniques (Takamiya and collaborators, 1994). The prediction of molecular vibrational states in fission builds on earlier work showing that the potential between fragments can have pockets (Gherghescu and collaborators, 1995), and on the cluster radioactivity framework of Sandulescu, Poenaru and Greiner (1980). The results enable experimental searches for gamma transitions from molecular states using existing data from the Gamma sphere facility at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and the authors plan to extend calculations to odd-odd splittings and higher multipolarity deformations.
