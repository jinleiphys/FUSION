# Collision-induced flavor instability in dense neutrino gases with energy-dependent scattering

> We investigate the collision-induced flavor instability in homogeneous, isotropic, dense neutrino gases in the two-flavor mixing scenario with energy-dependent scattering. We uncover a simple expression of the growth rate of this instability in terms of the flavor-decohering collision rates and the electron lepton number distribution of the neutrino. This growth rate is common to the neutrinos and antineutrinos of different energies, and is independent of the mass-splitting and vacuum mixing angle of the neutrino, the matter density, and the neutrino density, although the initial amplitude of the unstable oscillation mode can be suppressed by a large matter density. Our results suggest that neutrinos are likely to experience collision-induced flavor conversions deep inside a core-collapse supernova even when both the fast and slow collective flavor oscillations are suppressed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple expression for the exponential growth rate of collision-induced flavor instability in dense neutrino gases, showing that this growth rate depends only on the flavor-decohering collision rates and the electron lepton number (νELN) distribution, and is independent of the mass-splitting, vacuum mixing angle, matter density, and neutrino density when both the matter and neutrino densities are large.

## Method

The authors perform linear stability analysis of the flavor evolution equations for homogeneous, isotropic neutrino gases in the two-flavor mixing scenario, first for a mono-energetic gas and then for a gas with a continuous energy spectrum. They solve the linearized equations analytically to obtain the growth rate of the unstable collective mode, and verify their results with numerical solutions of the full nonlinear equations using discretized energy spectra with 400 bins between 0 and 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study by Johns (2021) on collision-induced flavor conversions in mono-energetic neutrino gases to the more realistic case of energy-dependent scattering. The derived growth rate formula generalizes the results of Padilla-Gay (2022) and shows that collision-induced flavor conversions require a crossing in the νELN distribution, similar to the condition for fast flavor conversions identified by Dasgupta (2009) and Dasgupta (2021). The results suggest that such conversions can occur deep inside core-collapse supernovae where both fast and slow collective flavor oscillations are suppressed by large matter and neutrino densities, but may not occur in neutron star mergers where electron antineutrinos have larger average energy and abundance than electron neutrinos.
