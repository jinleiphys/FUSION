# Heavy quark quenching from RHIC to LHC and the consequences of gluon damping

> In this contribution to the Quark Matter 2012 conference, we study whether energy loss models established for RHIC energies to describe the quenching of heavy quarks can be applied at LHC with the same success. We also benefit from the larger $p_T$-range accessible at this accelerator to test the impact of gluon damping on observables such as the nuclear modification factor.

## Key claim
The authors argue that their effective energy loss models, which successfully describe heavy quark quenching at RHIC, can be applied to LHC with only a 10% rescaling of the coupling constant for intermediate transverse momenta, but at higher pT (above 10 GeV/c) new effects such as gluon damping may be required to match the data.

## Method
The authors use a microscopic approach for heavy quark quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, assuming interactions with light partons through both elastic and radiative processes evaluated with a parameterized running coupling constant. The partons are spatially distributed along a hydrodynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. For the LHC case, they modify the initial pT distribution according to the FONLL scheme and adjust the initial entropy density to reproduce the final charged particle density dNch/dy = 1600 at mid-rapidity. They introduce gluon damping by quenching the LPM radiation spectrum with an acceptance probability based on the ratio of damping time to formation length.

## Key numbers
- For RHIC Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the optimal K rescaling factor for the collisional + radiative (LPM) model is K ≈ 0.7 for NPSE RAA, with best agreement at K = 0.7 (three stars) for NPSE and K = 0.6-0.7 for D0 mesons in 0-10% centrality.
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, a 10% decrease of the coupling leads to reasonable agreement for D meson RAA at pT less than 10 GeV/c in 0-20% centrality.
- For the 0-7.5% centrality class at LHC, data has been measured up to pT = 30 GeV/c, where the pure collisional energy loss component better reproduces the rising RAA.
- Gluon damping is evaluated for an arbitrary width Gamma = 0.75 T, which softens the radiation spectra and reduces average energy loss, especially at larger energies.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic approach for heavy quark quenching (Gossiaux 2008, Gossiaux 2010) from RHIC to LHC energies, where they had successfully described nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of non-photonic single electrons at RHIC. The study confronts model predictions with D and B meson production data from ALICE and CMS collaborations at LHC. The gluon damping effect, previously studied in electrodynamics (Bluhm 2011) and QCD (Bluhm 2012), is introduced to address the discrepancy at high pT where radiative energy loss is expected to dominate but pure collisional loss better matches the data.
