# Thermalization & hydrodynamics in Bjorken & Gubser flows

> The dynamical scaling behavior of hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic moments of the distribution function is studied using third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics and anisotropic hydrodynamics for systems undergoing Bjorken and Gubser expansions, where exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation in Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) are available for comparison. While dimensionless quantities like normalized shear, pressure anisotropy and normalized entropy show at late times universal scaling relations with small (large) Knudsen number for Bjorken (Gubser) flows, dimensionful quantities like the entropy per unit rapidity do not. Although the two hydrodynamic approximation schemes describe the exact attractors for normalized shear with high accuracy, their description for the normalized entropy is less precise. We attribute this to non-negligible couplings to non-hydrodynamic modes in the entropy evolution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics and anisotropic hydrodynamics accurately describe the exact attractors for normalized shear stress and pressure anisotropy in both Bjorken and Gubser flows, their description of the normalized entropy density is less precise; the authors attribute this discrepancy to non-negligible couplings to non-hydrodynamic modes in the entropy evolution.

## Method

The authors compared the time evolution of shear stress and entropy from third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics and second-order anisotropic hydrodynamics (with \(P_L\)-matching) against exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) for systems with Bjorken and Gubser symmetries. For Bjorken flow, the Knudsen number decreases at late times (approach to local thermal equilibrium), while for Gubser flow the Knudsen number increases exponentially at late times (approach to free-streaming), allowing tests of hydrodynamic approximations in opposite limits.

## Key numbers

- For Bjorken flow, the normalized shear stress approaches the Navier-Stokes limit \(\pi/(\epsilon+P) \to 1/(3\pi \tilde w)\) at late times, where \(\tilde w \equiv \tau T(\tau)/(4\pi\eta/s) = \mathrm{Kn}^{-1}\)
- For Gubser flow, the Navier-Stokes solution diverges as \(\bigl(\pi/(\epsilon+P)\bigr)_\mathrm{NS} = \tilde{w}/(6\pi)\) at large de Sitter times, while the exact solution and hydrodynamic approximations approach the free-streaming value \(\hat\pi/(\hat\epsilon+\hat{P}) = 0.5\)
- The specific shear viscosity values studied were \(4\pi\eta/s = 1, 3,\) and 10
- Bjorken flow was initialized with temperature \(T_0 = 300\) MeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/\(c\); Gubser flow was initialized with rescaled temperature \(\hat T_0 = 0.002\) at \(\rho_0 = -10\)
- The third-order Chapman-Enskog transport coefficients for Bjorken flow are \(\lambda = 38/21\), \(\chi = 72/245\), and \(\beta_\pi = 4P/5\); for Gubser flow \(\hat\lambda = 46/21\), \(\hat\chi = 72/245\), and \(\hat\beta_\pi = 4\hat P/5\)

## Context

This work extends comparisons between hydrodynamic approximations and exact kinetic theory solutions for Bjorken flow (Florkowski 2013, Denicol 2014) and Gubser flow (Denicol 2014) to include entropy evolution and third-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics. The results enable testing the limits of hydrodynamic applicability in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for small systems where gradients are large, and show that anisotropic hydrodynamics performs well in both the hydrodynamic and free-streaming limits while third-order Chapman-Enskog fails for Gubser flow at late times.
