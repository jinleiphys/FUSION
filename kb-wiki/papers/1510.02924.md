# No influence of a N=126 Neutron Shell Closure in Fission Fragment Mass Distributions

> Mass distributions of the fragments in the fission of $^{206}$Po and the N=126 neutron shell closed nucleus $^{210}$Po have been measured. No significant deviation of mass distributions has been found between $^{206}$Po and $^{210}$Po, indicating the absence of shell correction at the saddle point in both the nuclei, contrary to the reported angular anisotropy and pre-scission neutron multiplicity results. This new result provides benchmark data to test the new fission dynamical models to study the effect of shell correction on the potential energy surface at saddle point.

## Key claim

The paper reports that fission fragment mass distributions for the N=126 neutron shell closed nucleus $^{210}$Po and the non-shell-closed $^{206}$Po show no significant deviation from each other, indicating the absence of shell correction at the saddle point in both nuclei, contrary to earlier indirect evidence from angular anisotropy and pre-scission neutron multiplicity measurements.

## Method

Fission fragment mass distributions were measured for $^{206}$Po and $^{210}$Po compound nuclei formed via $^{12}$C + $^{194}$Pt and $^{12}$C + $^{198}$Pt fusion reactions at near-Coulomb barrier energies (58-78 MeV). Correlated fission fragments were detected using two large-area position-sensitive multi-wire proportional counters, and masses were extracted from time-of-flight, angular coordinates, and recoil velocities. The experimental mass distributions were compared to theoretical calculations using the Finite Range Liquid Drop Model (FRLDM) potential energy surface without microscopic shell corrections, with dynamical effects accounted for by a scaling factor.

## Key numbers

- Mass resolution achieved was approximately 5 u.
- The standard deviation ($\sigma$) of the fitted symmetric mass distributions was found to be constant when normalized by the square root of saddle temperature ($\sqrt{T}$), indicating purely statistical compound nuclear fission.
- The stiffness parameter $k$ for the mass asymmetry degree of freedom was found to be consistent with the comprehensive compilation of data presented in Itkis (1991).
- Theoretical mass distributions were reproduced using constant scaling factors $B$ of 1.93 for $^{206}$Po and 1.82 for $^{210}$Po.
- A shell correction of 7 MeV at symmetry, which was required to fit neutron multiplicity data for $^{210}$Po in Golda (2007), produced a clearly asymmetric theoretical mass distribution, in contrast to the experimental observation.

## Context

This work directly tests the role of the N=126 neutron shell closure on fission fragment mass distributions, extending earlier studies that reported anomalous angular anisotropy (Aradhana 2008, Mahata 2006) and pre-scission neutron multiplicity (Golda 2007) for the same systems. The results contradict those indirect probes and support the dynamical calculations of Schmitt (2010), which showed that angular anisotropy and neutron data could be explained with purely macroscopic potential without shell correction at the saddle point. The findings provide benchmark data for testing fission dynamical models relevant to the production of spherical superheavy nuclei around the next closed neutron shell at N=184.
