# Quantum fluctuation effects on hyperfragment formation from Xi^- absorption at rest on 12C

> Formation mechanisms of single, twin, and double hypernuclei from Xi^- absorption at rest on 12C are investigated with an refined microscopic transport model, that incorporates the recently developed Quantal Langevin treatment into Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics. The quantum fluctuations suppress the formation probability of double hyperfragments to around 10%, which is comparable to the experimental data, and the dynamical formation of twin hyperfragment can be described qualitatively.

## Key claim

The refined transport model (AMD-QL) predicts that quantum fluctuations suppress the formation probability of double hyperfragments to around 10%, which is comparable to experimental data, but the authors note that the experimental statistics are poor and the constraints on theory are "very loose at present."

## Method

The authors extended the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) model by incorporating the Quantal Langevin treatment, which adds a random force and drift term to the equations of motion for the Gaussian wave packet parameters to ensure that the system approaches quantum statistical equilibrium. They applied this AMD-QL model to simulate the absorption of a stopped Xi- particle on a 12C target, using a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets for nucleons (width parameter nu_N = 0.16 fm^-2) and hyperons (width scaled by mass ratio), with the effective interaction including an attractive Lambda-Lambda interaction.

## Key numbers

- The double hyperfragment formation probability is calculated to be around 10% when the Lambda-Lambda interaction is attractive.
- The experimental lower limit (90% confidence level) for double hyperfragment formation is 4.8%, and the upper limit is 77.9%.
- The experimental lower limit for twin hyperfragment formation is 0.66%, and the upper limit is 81.5%.
- Rough experimental estimates for double hyperfragment formation probability range from 3% to 9%, and for twin hyperfragment formation from 6% to 18%, based on 31.1 +/- 4.8 observed Xi- absorption events at rest on light targets in the KEK E176 experiment.
- The nucleon Gaussian width parameter is nu_N = 0.16 fm^-2.

## Context

This work extends prior statistical decay models of the double hyperon compound nucleus, which gave double hyperfragment formation probabilities that were too large compared to twin hyperfragment formation when the Lambda-Lambda interaction was attractive. It also extends the direct fragmentation picture of Yamada and Ikeda (1997), which underestimated twin hypernuclei formation probability. The AMD-QL model enables a consistent microscopic description of both dynamical and statistical processes in hyperfragment formation, and the authors note that further experimental searches for double hypernuclear formation are being carried out at BNL and KEK.
