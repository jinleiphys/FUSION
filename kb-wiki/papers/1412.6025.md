# Delayed Magnetic Catalysis

> We study the effect of an external magnetic field on the chiral phase transition in the theory of the strong interaction by means of a renormalization-group (RG) fixed-point analysis, relying on only one physical input parameter, the strong coupling at a given large momentum scale. To be specific, we consider the interplay of the RG flow of four-quark interactions and the running gauge coupling. Depending on the temperature and the strength of the magnetic field, the gauge coupling can drive the quark sector to criticality, resulting in chiral symmetry breaking. In accordance with lattice Monte-Carlo simulations, we find that the chiral phase transition temperature decreases for small values of the external magnetic field. For large magnetic field strengths, however, our fixed-point study predicts that the phase transition temperature increases monotonically.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral phase transition temperature in QCD first decreases with increasing magnetic field (inverse catalysis) and then, for sufficiently strong fields, increases monotonically (delayed magnetic catalysis), based on a fixed-point analysis of the renormalization-group flow that treats the gauge coupling as the only physical input parameter.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization-group (RG) flow of the four-quark coupling in the scalar-pseudoscalar channel, coupled to the running strong gauge coupling, in the presence of an external magnetic field at finite temperature. They compute the magnetic-field dependence of the loop integrals (box, triangle, and quark-loop diagrams) in the lowest Landau-level approximation, and compare the running gauge coupling to a scale-dependent critical coupling to determine the onset of chiral symmetry breaking. The only input parameter is the strong coupling \(g^2/(4\pi) \approx 0.322\) at the \(\tau\)-mass scale \(m_\tau \approx 1.78\,\text{GeV}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(B=0\), the chiral critical temperature is \(T_c \approx 220\,\text{MeV}\).
* The critical temperature \(T_c\) decreases with increasing magnetic field, reaching a minimum at \(eB \approx 0.2\,\text{GeV}^2\).
* For large magnetic fields, the critical temperature increases monotonically with \(eB\).
* The critical gauge coupling scales as \(g^2_{\text{cr}} \sim b^{-1/4}\) for large dimensionless magnetic field \(b = eB/k^2\) in the deep infrared limit.
* The quark-loop contribution to the four-quark beta function scales as \(l_{\lambda_\sigma^2} \sim b\) in the large-\(b\) limit.
* The box-diagram contribution scales as \(l_{g^4} \sim b^{-1/2}\) in the large-\(b\) limit.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-point analysis of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD developed by Gies (2005) and Braun (2005, 2006) to include external magnetic fields. It directly addresses the unexpected inverse-catalysis effect observed in lattice Monte-Carlo simulations by Bali (2011, 2012), where the chiral critical temperature decreases with increasing magnetic field, in contrast to purely fermionic models that exhibit magnetic catalysis. The study provides a mechanism for inverse catalysis at weak fields and predicts a delayed onset of magnetic catalysis at strong fields, which can be tested by future lattice simulations and may guide improvements to low-energy QCD models.
