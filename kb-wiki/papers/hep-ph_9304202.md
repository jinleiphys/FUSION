# Isospin splitting in heavy baryons and mesons

> A recent general analysis of light-baryon isospin splittings is updated and extended to charmed baryons. The measured $Σ_c$ and $Ξ_c$ splittings stand out as being difficult to understand in terms of two-body forces alone. We also discuss heavy-light mesons; though the framework here is necessarily less general, we nevertheless obtain some predictions that are not strongly model-dependent.

# Isospin splitting in heavy baryons and mesons

## Key claim

The measured $\Sigma_c$ and $\Xi_c$ isospin splittings are difficult to understand using only two-body forces, suggesting that three-body effects are important for isospin splittings in charmed baryons even though the isospin-averaged central masses can be fitted with a sum of two-body energies. For heavy-light mesons, the authors obtain predictions for the differential vector-pseudoscalar isospin splittings that are not strongly model-dependent, including a negative value for $\Delta(B)$ that disagrees with most other authors.

## Method

The authors extend a previous analysis of light-baryon isospin splittings (Cutkosky, 1993) to charmed baryons by expressing central masses and isospin splittings in terms of spin-dependent and spin-independent pair energies for quark pairs, assuming $D_{sc}/D_{nc} \sim 2/3$ as suggested by the non-charm sector. For mesons, they use an interpolation technique to extract the quark-mass contribution to the differential vector-pseudoscalar isospin splitting $\Delta(M)$ from measured hyperfine splittings, and calculate the electromagnetic contribution using a Coulomb-plus-linear potential model with scaling laws that yield predictions nearly independent of the potential parameters.

## Key numbers

- The fit to octet and decuplet masses has a $\chi^2$ of 3.2 for 4 degrees of freedom, with almost all $\chi^2$ arising from the $\Delta$ masses
- The fit to charmed baryon central masses gives an overall $\chi^2$ of 0.37 with one degree of freedom, using a model error of 8.9 MeV
- The fit to charmed baryon isospin splittings gives a total $\chi^2$ of 6.23; $\Sigma_c^2$ contributes $\chi^2 = 3.95$ and $\Xi_c^1$ contributes $\chi^2 = 2.17$
- $\Delta(D) = 1.48 \pm 0.1$ MeV (measured by CLEO, 1992)
- $\Delta(K) = -0.49 \pm 0.37$ MeV (Particle Data Group, 1992)
- Predicted $\Delta(B) = -0.29 \pm 0.07$ MeV
- Predicted $\Delta(K) = -0.15 \pm 0.49$ MeV
- $D^*-D = 141.4 \pm 0.1$ MeV; $D_s^*-D_s = 141.5 \pm 1.9$ MeV
- $B^*-B = 46.0 \pm 0.6$ MeV; $B_s^*-B_s = 47.0 \pm 2.6$ MeV
- $K^*-K = 398$ MeV
- Predicted $\Sigma_c^*$ mass: $2494 \pm 16$ MeV
- Predicted $\Xi_c^{\prime}$ mass: $2587 \pm 12$ MeV
- Predicted $\Xi_c^*$ mass: $2621 \pm 15$ MeV
- Predicted $\Omega_c^*$ mass: $2743 \pm 17$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the general analysis of light-baryon isospin splittings by Cutkosky (1993) to charmed baryons and heavy-light mesons. The authors find that while the central masses of charmed baryons can be fitted with two-body pair energies, the $\Sigma_c$ and $\Xi_c$ isospin splittings indicate substantial three-body effects, contradicting the assumption that three-body forces are negligible. For mesons, the authors obtain a prediction for $\Delta(B)$ that is negative ($-0.29 \pm 0.07$ MeV), disagreeing with most other calculations (Chan, 1977; Sinha et al., 1986; Godfrey and Isgur, 1986; Amundson et al., 1992) which predict positive values. Their result for $\Delta(K)$ agrees with the measured value, suggesting that the longstanding discrepancy between theory and experiment for $\Delta(K)$ can be explained without invoking large relativistic or unitarity effects. Improved measurements of the $\Delta$, $\Sigma_c$, $\Xi_c$, and $B^*$ splittings would provide significant tests of model calculations.
