# Modern nuclear force predictions for the alpha particle

> We present new calculations of the $α$-particle which are based on the most modern nucleon-nucleon interactions alone and combined with the Tucson-Melbourne or the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. Results for the binding energies and some properties of the wavefunction are given. On that phenomenological level little room is left for the action of a possible four-nucleon force.

# Modern nuclear force predictions for the alpha particle

## Key claim

After adjusting three-nucleon forces (3NFs) to reproduce the triton and helium-3 binding energies, the resulting alpha-particle binding energies from modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces plus 3NFs are rather close to the experimental value, indicating that four-nucleon forces (at least for T=0) will be unimportant. However, on this phenomenological level it is not possible to definitively decide about the need for four-nucleon forces, since one could always add another piece of a 3NF (such as a repulsive component) to reach the alpha-particle binding energy more accurately.

## Method

The authors solved the four-nucleon bound state using Yakubovsky equations in momentum space with a partial wave decomposition, reducing the 18 Yakubovsky components to two coupled integral equations for identical nucleons. They used four modern NN potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijmegen I, and Nijmegen II) alone and combined with the Tucson-Melbourne (TM), modified Tucson-Melbourne (TM'), or Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, adjusting the TM cut-off parameter Lambda to reproduce the triton and helium-3 binding energies for each NN force separately.

## Key numbers

- CD-Bonn NN force alone gives helium-4 binding energy -26.26 MeV (experiment: -28.30 MeV)
- AV18 NN force alone gives helium-4 binding energy -24.28 MeV (experiment: -28.30 MeV)
- CD-Bonn + TM 3NF (adjusted to helium-3) gives helium-4 binding energy -29.06 MeV, overbinding by about 800 keV
- AV18 + TM 3NF (adjusted to helium-3) gives helium-4 binding energy -28.56 MeV, overbinding by about 300 keV
- AV18 + Urbana IX 3NF gives helium-4 binding energy -28.50 MeV, close to the experimental -28.30 MeV
- AV18 + TM' 3NF (adjusted to helium-3) gives helium-4 binding energy -28.36 MeV
- Numerical accuracy estimated at about +/- 3 keV for the 3N system and +/- 50 keV for the 4N system
- S-state probability for helium-4 with AV18 alone is 85.45%; with AV18+Urbana IX it drops to 82.93%
- D-state probability for helium-4 with AV18 alone is 13.74%; with AV18+Urbana IX it rises to 16.04%

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of few-nucleon systems using Faddeev and Yakubovsky methods (Kamada 1992, Gloeckle 1993) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (Pudliner 1997) and CHH (Viviani 1998) approaches. The authors confirm the Tjon line correlation between triton and alpha-particle binding energies (Tjon 1975) for modern NN forces. The results enable future studies of upcoming force structures from chiral perturbation theory (van Kolck 1994, Epelbaum 2000) and demonstrate that the Yakubovsky equations are a well-controlled tool for realistic NN and 3NF calculations.
