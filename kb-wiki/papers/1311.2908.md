# Positronium in Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We present a calculation of the mass spectrum of positronium within the framework of the recently developed Basis Light-Front Quantization approach to non-perturbative quantum field theory. In this calculation, we employ a two-body effective interaction for the photon exchange, neglecting self-energy effects. We demonstrate the cancellation of Light-Front small-{\it x} divergences within our non-perturbative approach. The resulting spectrum is compared to both non-relativistic quantum mechanics and previous work in Discretized Light-Cone Quantization.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first application of Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) to a bound state problem, using positronium as a test case, but the calculation employs a two-body effective interaction that neglects fermion self-energy effects and uses an artificially large coupling constant (alpha = 0.3) to exaggerate binding energies and hyperfine splitting for numerical testing purposes.

## Method

The authors truncate the Fock space to include only electron-positron and electron-positron-photon sectors, then construct a Bloch-form effective Hamiltonian that acts only on the two-particle sector. They introduce a counterterm to cancel unphysical Light-Front small-x divergences that arise in the non-perturbative calculation, and regulate the remaining physical infrared divergence with a fictitious photon mass mu. The basis uses a 2D Harmonic Oscillator for transverse modes with an N_max truncation and discretized longitudinal momentum with a K cutoff, and the resulting matrix eigenvalue problem is solved numerically using ScaLAPACK on the Hopper Cray XE6 at NERSC.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy converges rapidly with increasing K; fitting function used is E = a + b*exp(-c*sqrt(K))
- Ground state energy as a function of N_max is fit to E = a + b*exp(-c*sqrt(N_max)) for each value of mu
- The coupling constant used is alpha = 0.3 (unphysical, to exaggerate binding and hyperfine splitting)
- The basis energy parameter is b = 0.5m_f, where m_f is the fermion mass
- The infrared regulator mu is taken as mu = 0.1m_f for the convergence studies
- The ground state energy below 2 fermion mass units indicates a bound state
- BLFQ results show significant overbinding compared to non-relativistic quantum mechanics predictions, but the hyperfine splitting is well reproduced

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach, previously applied to free electron problems and Schwinger's anomalous magnetic moment (Honkanen, Zhao), to bound state calculations for the first time. The results are compared to non-relativistic quantum mechanics and to previous Discretized Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ) results from Ref. [Ref7], which also found overbinding. The authors note that previous work by Karmanov and others on ladder truncation of positronium on the Light Front showed ultraviolet cutoff dependence that may require inclusion of crossed ladder graphs, and suggest future applications to heavy quarkonia by adding a confining potential from AdS/QCD.
