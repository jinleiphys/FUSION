# Calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment with two dynamical flavors of domain wall fermions

> We present a study of the neutron electric dipole moment ($\vec d_N$) within the framework of lattice QCD with two flavors of dynamical lig ht quarks. The dipole moment is sensitive to the topological structure of the gaug e fields, and accuracy can only be achieved by using dynamical, or sea quark, calc ulations. However, the topological charge evolves slowly in these calculations, le ading to a relatively large uncertainty in $\vec d_N$. It is shown, using quenched configurations, that a better sampling of the charge d istribution reduces this problem, but because the CP even part of the fermion determinant is absent, both the topological charge dis tribution and $\vec d_N$ are pathological in the chiral limit. We discuss the statistical and systematic uncertainties arising from the topological charge distr ibution and unphysical size of the quark mass in our calculations and prospects fo r eliminating them. Our calculations employ the RBC collaboration two flavor domain wall fermion and DBW2 gauge action lattices with inverse lattice spacing $a^{-1}\approx$ 1.7 GeV, physical volume $V\approx (2$ fm)$^3$, and light quark mass roughly equal to the strange quark mass ($m_{sea}=0.03 $ and 0.04). We determine a value of the electric dipole moment that is zero withi n (statistical) errors, $|\vec d_N| = -0.04(20)$ e-$θ$-fm at the smaller sea quark mass. Satisfactory results for the magnetic and electric form factors of the proton and neutron are also obtained and presented.

## Key claim

The neutron electric dipole moment is consistent with zero within statistical errors in this lattice QCD calculation with two dynamical quark flavors, but the result carries a relatively large uncertainty because the topological charge evolves slowly in dynamical simulations, and the quark masses used are unphysically large (roughly equal to the strange quark mass).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the RBC collaboration's two-flavor domain wall fermion action and DBW2 gauge action on lattices with inverse lattice spacing a⁻¹ ≈ 1.7 GeV and physical volume V ≈ (2 fm)³. They computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions to extract the CP-even and CP-odd form factors, using ratios of three-point functions to isolate the magnetic and electric dipole moments. The mixing angle α between CP-even and CP-odd nucleon states in the θ-vacuum was determined from the ratio of CP-odd to CP-even parts of the two-point correlation function, and this mixing was subtracted to obtain the electric dipole moment form factor F₃.

## Key numbers

- Inverse lattice spacing: a⁻¹ ≈ 1.7 GeV
- Physical volume: V ≈ (2 fm)³
- Sea quark masses: m_sea = 0.03 and 0.04 (roughly equal to the strange quark mass)
- Neutron electric dipole moment at m_sea = 0.03: |d_N| = -0.04(20) e-θ-fm (consistent with zero within statistical errors)
- Nucleon mass at m_sea = 0.03, zero momentum: E(p) = 0.8646(53) in lattice units
- Nucleon mass at m_sea = 0.04, zero momentum: E(p) = 0.9264(54) in lattice units
- Neutron magnetic form factor ratio at q² = 0.399 GeV², m_sea = 0.03: -0.957(37)
- Neutron magnetic form factor ratio at q² = 0.401 GeV², m_sea = 0.04: -0.913(23)

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment from the quenched approximation to dynamical simulations with two flavors of light quarks, following earlier work by Aoki et al. (2004) on topological charge distributions. The authors show that quenched calculations produce pathological results in the chiral limit because the CP-even part of the fermion determinant is absent, as previously discussed by Aoki (1990) and Witten (1979). The results enable future calculations with lighter quark masses and better sampling of topological charge, which are needed to reduce the statistical and systematic uncertainties and provide a meaningful comparison to the experimental bound on θ.
