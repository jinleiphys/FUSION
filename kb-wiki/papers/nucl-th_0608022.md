# Dileptons and Medium Effects in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss the status of calculating in-medium modifications of vector-meson spectral functions in hot and dense matter, their application to dilepton spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and possible relations to chiral symmetry restoration. We emphasize the importance of constraining in-medium spectral functions by empirical information from scattering data, QCD sum rules, and lattice QCD. This is a mandatory prerequisite to arrive at reliable predictions for low-mass dileptons in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-medium modifications of vector-meson spectral functions, particularly the broadening of the ρ meson, are essential for describing dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, but emphasizes that these calculations must be constrained by empirical data from scattering experiments, QCD sum rules, and lattice QCD to be reliable.

## Method

The author reviews hadronic many-body calculations that evaluate in-medium self-energy insertions for the ρ meson, including modifications of the pion cloud in ππ decay, direct ρ-baryon couplings (e.g., ρ-N(1520)N⁻¹ excitation), and direct ρ-meson couplings (e.g., ρ+π→a₁ excitation). These calculations are constrained by independent empirical information from πN→ρN scattering data, nuclear photoabsorption, and QCD sum rules, then applied to compute thermal dilepton emission rates which are convoluted over the space-time evolution of heavy-ion collisions using fireball or hydrodynamic models.

## Key numbers

- The scalar quark condensate in vacuum is ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ (−250 MeV)³, the pion-decay constant is f_π = 93 MeV, and the constituent quark mass is m*_{u,d} ≈ 350 MeV.
- The chiral partner mass splitting in the low-energy hadronic spectrum is ΔM ≈ 0.5 GeV.
- At nuclear saturation density ρ_N = 0.16 fm⁻³, the predicted ρ-meson width and mass are (m_ρ, Γ_ρ) ≈ (0.8, 0.45) GeV.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is T_c ≈ 175 MeV.
- The fireball lifetime in central In-In collisions at SPS is τ_FB ≈ 7 fm/c, with an uncertainty of about 25% in absolute yield.
- The chiral mixing parameter is ε = T²/6f_π², and at T_c the mixing formula assumes ε(T_c) ≡ 1/2.
- The vector dominance model prefactors satisfy C_ρ : C_ω : C_ϕ = 9:1:2.

## Context

This work extends the many-body approach of Rapp (1999) and Urban (1999) for in-medium ρ spectral functions, which were constrained by nuclear photoabsorption data from Rapp (1997) and πN→ρN scattering from Lutz (2001). The calculations are compared to new high-precision NA60 dilepton data from Arnaldi (2006) for central In-In collisions at SPS, and are consistent with the chiral virial expansion approach of Dusling (2006) and the hydrodynamic simulations of Renk (2006). The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC and the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at GSI to probe the QCD phase diagram across different net baryon densities.
