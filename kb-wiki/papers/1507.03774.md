# Dirac Coupled Channel Analyses of Proton Inelastic Scattering from $^{40}$Ar Nucleus

> 0.8 GeV proton inelastic scatterings from $^{40}$Ar nucleus are analyzed using an optical potential model in the Dirac coupled channel formalism. A rotational collective model is used to obtain the transition optical potentials for the low lying excited states of the rotational band. The optical potential parameters of a Woods-Saxon shape and the deformation parameters of the excited states are searched phenomenologically to reproduce the experimental differential cross-section data by using the sequential iteration method. The effect of a multistep process is investigated by including the channel coupling between two excited states in addition to the couplings between the ground state and the excited states. The calculated deformation parameters of the excited states are compared with those obtained by using the nonrelativistic calculations. The results of the Dirac coupled channel calculations are found to show pretty good agreements with the experimental data of the ground state and the low-lying excited states. The multistep excitation process via the channel coupling with the second $2^+$ state is found to be important for the excitation of the $4^+$ state at the inelastic scatterings from the deformed nucleus, $^{40}$Ar.

## Key claim

The Dirac coupled channel formalism, using a rotational collective model with Woods-Saxon optical potentials, reproduces experimental differential cross-section data for 0.8 GeV proton inelastic scattering from \(^{40}\)Ar with "pretty good agreements," but the agreement for the \(4^+\) state is only significantly improved when multistep excitation via channel coupling with the second \(2^+\) state is included, not when coupling only the ground state and the \(4^+\) state or the ground, first \(2^+\), and \(4^+\) states.

## Method

The authors performed phenomenological Dirac coupled channel calculations using the computer code ECIS, fitting 12 parameters of Woods-Saxon scalar and vector optical potentials to elastic scattering data for 0.8 GeV unpolarized protons on \(^{40}\)Ar. Transition potentials for the low-lying excited states (first \(2^+\) at 1.461 MeV, second \(2^+\) at 2.524 MeV, and \(4^+\) at 2.89 MeV) were obtained by deforming the diagonal potentials in a first-order rotational collective model, with deformation parameters \(\beta_S\) and \(\beta_V\) searched alongside potential strengths via sequential iteration. The effect of multistep processes was investigated by including channel coupling between the ground state and one excited state, and then by adding coupling between two excited states (e.g., \(2^+\) and \(4^+\)).

## Key numbers

- The scalar real potential strength is -472.8 MeV, with radius 3.154 fm and diffusiveness 0.5967 fm; the scalar imaginary strength is 120.3 MeV, radius 3.013 fm, diffusiveness 0.6896 fm.
- The vector real potential strength is 264.0 MeV, radius 3.153 fm, diffusiveness 0.5954 fm; the vector imaginary strength is -127.3 MeV, radius 3.129 fm, diffusiveness 0.6254 fm.
- \(\chi^2/N\) for the elastic scattering fit is about 13.4.
- The real effective central potential from the Dirac approach is about -130 MeV at the center of the nucleus, compared to about 4.0 MeV from the nonrelativistic Schrodinger approach.
- Deformation parameters from the Dirac calculation: for the first \(2^+\) state (1.461 MeV), \(\beta_S = 0.28\) and \(\beta_V = 0.27\); for the second \(2^+\) state (2.524 MeV), \(\beta_S = 0.085\) and \(\beta_V = 0.058\); for the \(4^+\) state (2.89 MeV), \(\beta_S = 0.092\) and \(\beta_V = 0.057\).
- The nonrelativistic deformation parameter \(\beta_{NR}\) from prior work is 0.28 for the first \(2^+\), 0.082 for the second \(2^+\), and 0.0935 for the \(4^+\) state.

## Context

This work extends Dirac coupled channel analyses, previously applied to spherically symmetric and some deformed nuclei, to the deformed nucleus \(^{40}\)Ar, where only nonrelativistic distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations (neglecting channel coupling) or low-energy nonrelativistic coupled channel calculations had been performed (Refs. 14, 15 in the paper). The results show that multistep excitation via the second \(2^+\) state is critical for describing the \(4^+\) state, consistent with findings for other axially symmetric deformed nuclei (e.g., Refs. 10-13 in the paper). The work enables future inclusion of the excited \(0^+\) state at 2.12 MeV, which was omitted due to known difficulties with \(\lambda=0\) transitions, to potentially resolve slight discrepancies in the diffraction pattern for the second \(2^+\) state.
