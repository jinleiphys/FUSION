# Relativistic $O(q^4)$ two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential: configuration space

> We have recently performed a relativistic $O(q^4)$ chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange $NN$ potential, and here we explore its configuration space content. Interactions are determined by three families of diagrams, two of which involve just $g_A$ and $f_π$, whereas the third one depends on empirical coefficients fixed by subthreshold $πN$ data. In this sense, the calculation has no adjusted parameters and gives rise to predictions, which are tested against phenomenological potentials. The dynamical structure of the eight leading non-relativistic components of the interaction is investigated and, in most cases, found to be clearly dominated by a well defined class of diagrams. In particular, the central isovector and spin-orbit, spin-spin, and tensor isoscalar terms are almost completely fixed by just $g_A$ and $f_π$. The convergence of the chiral series in powers of the ratio (pion mass/nucleon mass) is studied as a function of the internucleon distance and, for $r>$ 1 fm, found to be adequate for most components of the potential. An important exception is the dominant central isoscalar term, where the convergence is evident only for $r>$ 2.5 fm. Finally, we compare the spatial behavior of the functions that enter the relativistic and heavy baryon formulations of the interaction and find that, in the region of physical interest, they differ by about 5%.

## Key claim

The relativistic O(q^4) chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, when expressed in configuration space, is determined by three families of diagrams with no adjusted parameters, and its convergence in powers of the ratio (pion mass/nucleon mass) is adequate for most components at internucleon distances r > 1 fm, except for the dominant central isoscalar term where convergence is evident only for r > 2.5 fm.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic O(q^4) chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange NN potential using covariant Feynman rules derived from an effective Lagrangian containing pion-pion and pion-nucleon interactions up to O(q^3). The potential was obtained by going to the center-of-mass frame, subtracting the iterated one-pion exchange potential to avoid double counting in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and then Fourier transforming to configuration space while keeping only local and spin-orbit contributions. The dynamical content was organized into three families of diagrams: family I involves only the constants g_A and f_pi, family II contains two-pion correlations in the t-channel, and family III depends on empirical low-energy constants fixed by subthreshold pi-N data.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic and heavy baryon formulations of the interaction differ by about 5% in the region of physical interest
- The chiral series convergence is adequate for most components of the potential for r > 1 fm
- The dominant central isoscalar term shows evident convergence only for r > 2.5 fm
- The influence of the two-pion exchange potential in the tensor isovector component becomes significant for r < 2 fm, and in the spin-spin isovector component for r < 3 fm
- Dimensionless subthreshold coefficients used: delta_00^+ = -4.72, delta_10^+ = 3.34, delta_01^+ = 4.15, beta_00^+ = -10.57, delta_00^- = 7.02, delta_10^- = -3.35, delta_01^- = -2.05, beta_00^- = 5.04

## Context

This work extends the relativistic O(q^4) chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange NN potential previously performed by the same authors (Higa and Robilotta, 2003) into configuration space, providing a complementary view to the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997) and Kaiser (2001). The results are tested against the Argonne Av14 and Av18 phenomenological potentials (Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla, 1995), and the approach builds upon the seminal framework of Taketani, Nakamura, and Sasaki (1951) as well as the Paris potential work of the 1960s. This enables future phase shift analyses using the chiral potential as a constraint for distances beyond a certain radius, similar to the established procedure for the one-pion exchange potential.
