# Finite-Size Scaling of Net-Proton Cumulants in Heavy-Ion Collisions: Remarks on the Interpretation of a Recent Analysis

> Finite-size scaling (FSS) provides a framework for investigating the possible presence of a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram using fluctuation observables measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A recent analysis reported a finite-size scaling representation of a susceptibility constructed from net-proton cumulants and interpreted the resulting scaling behavior as evidence for a CEP near $μ_B \approx 625$ MeV \cite{FSSpaper}. This note examines several aspects of that scaling construction. These include identifying the pseudorapidity acceptance window with the physical system size used in the finite-size scaling relations, assessing the influence of acceptance-driven multiplicity scaling on the susceptibility used for the scaling analysis, and treating thermodynamic scaling fields in the scaling variable. These considerations clarify several issues relevant for the consistent implementation and interpretation of finite-size scaling analyses in heavy-ion collision experiments. Taken together, they indicate that the reported scaling behavior does not uniquely establish critical dynamics nor the presence of a CEP near $μ_B \approx 625$ MeV.

# Finite-Size Scaling of Net-Proton Cumulants in Heavy-Ion Collisions: Remarks on the Interpretation of a Recent Analysis

## Key claim

The reported finite-size scaling collapse of a susceptibility constructed from net-proton cumulants does not uniquely establish critical dynamics nor the presence of a critical end point (CEP) near μ_B ≈ 625 MeV, because the scaling behavior can arise from acceptance-driven multiplicity scaling and methodological issues in the scaling construction rather than from genuine critical correlations.

## Method

The author examines three specific aspects of a recent finite-size scaling analysis: (1) the identification of the pseudorapidity acceptance window W with the physical system size L in the finite-size scaling relations, (2) the influence of acceptance-driven multiplicity scaling on the susceptibility χ₂ = C₂/(T_fo³ W dV_fo/dy) used for the scaling analysis, and (3) the treatment of thermodynamic scaling fields in the scaling variable, where the baryon chemical potential is associated with the exponent 1/ν despite being generally related to the ordering-field direction in the mapping to the three-dimensional Ising universality class.

## Key numbers

- The recent analysis claimed a CEP near μ_B ≈ 625 MeV, which the author argues is not uniquely established by the reported scaling behavior.
- For approximately uncorrelated particle production, the second cumulant scales as C₂ ∝ N, and since multiplicity increases approximately linearly with the pseudorapidity interval, C₂ ∝ W, leading to cancellation in the constructed susceptibility χ₂ ∼ W/W ≈ const to leading order.
- Near a critical point, cumulants scale with the correlation length ξ as C₂ ∼ ξ², C₃ ∼ ξ⁴·⁵, and C₄ ∼ ξ⁷, indicating progressively stronger powers of the correlation length for higher-order cumulants.

## Context

This note critically examines the finite-size scaling analysis reported in a recent paper (FSSpaper, 2025-2026) that interpreted scaling behavior of net-proton cumulants as evidence for a CEP. It extends earlier finite-size scaling studies by Lacey (2014) and Lacey (2024) that explored applications of finite-size scaling methods to fluctuation-derived observables in heavy-ion collisions. The work clarifies that the physical system size in heavy-ion collisions is determined by the geometric overlap region (L ∼ R_⊥, with R_⊥ ∝ N_part^(1/3)) rather than the pseudorapidity acceptance window, and that consistent finite-size scaling analyses require examining multiple cumulants and cumulant ratios (C₂/C₁, C₃/C₂, C₄/C₂, C₄/C₁) simultaneously, as demonstrated in Lacey (2024).
