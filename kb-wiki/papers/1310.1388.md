# Single particle potentials of asymmetric nuclear matter in different spin-isospin channels

> We investigate the neutron and proton single particle (s.p.) potentials of asymmetric nuclear matter and their isospin dependence in various spin-isospin $ST$ channels within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. It is shown that in symmetric nuclear matter, the s.p. potentials in both the isospin-singlet T=0 channel and isospin-triplet T=1 channel are essentially attractive, and the magnitudes in the two different channels are roughly the same. In neutron-rich nuclear matter, the isospin-splitting of the proton and neutron s.p. potentials turns out to be mainly determined by the isospin-singlet T=0 channel contribution which becomes more attractive for proton and more repulsive for neutron at higher asymmetries.

## Key claim

In symmetric nuclear matter, the single-particle potentials in the isospin-singlet T=0 and isospin-triplet T=1 channels are both attractive and roughly equal in magnitude; however, in neutron-rich matter, the isospin-splitting between neutron and proton potentials is dominated by the T=0 channel contribution, which becomes more attractive for protons and more repulsive for neutrons at higher asymmetries.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix with the Argonne V18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force (TBF) reduced to an effective two-body interaction. The continuous choice for the auxiliary potential was adopted, and the G-matrix was decoupled into partial-wave channels labeled by total angular momentum J, total spin S, and total isospin T. The neutron and proton single-particle potentials were then decomposed into contributions from different spin-isospin ST channels (00, 10, 01, 11) and from the summed T=0 and T=1 channels.

## Key numbers

- At density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and isospin asymmetry β = 0.6, the neutron and proton s.p. potentials in symmetric nuclear matter are attractive and comparable in magnitude in both the T=0 and T=1 channels.
- At ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and β = 0.6, the contributions in the even channels (ST=10 and ST=01) are considerably larger in magnitude than those in the odd channels (ST=00 and ST=11).
- At ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³, the attraction in the T=1 channel becomes slightly smaller for both protons and neutrons at higher asymmetry, in contrast to the behavior near saturation density.
- The symmetry potential U_sym = (U_n - U_p)/(2β) in the T=0 channel is positive (repulsive on neutrons, attractive on protons) and much larger in magnitude than the negative contribution from the T=1 channel, especially at low momenta.
- The symmetry potential in the T=0 channel is almost independent of density, while the T=1 channel contribution becomes slightly larger at ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³ compared to ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BHF study of the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter (Zuo 2009) by decomposing the single-particle potentials into spin-isospin channels, providing microscopic constraints for effective interactions such as Skyrme forces. The results show that the isovector part of the s.p. potential is governed by the T=0 channel, consistent with earlier findings for the symmetry energy, and that the T=0 tensor SD coupled channel dominates the symmetry potential. This enables improved parametrizations of Skyrme interactions (e.g., the LNS set from Cao 2006) and provides a microscopic basis for transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions that aim to extract the symmetry energy from isospin observables.
