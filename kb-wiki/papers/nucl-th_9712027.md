# Medium dependence of the bag constant in the quark-meson coupling model

> Possible variations of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model are examined in which the bag constant decreases in the nuclear medium. The reduction is supposed to depend on either the mean scalar field or the effective mass of the nucleon. It is shown that the electric and magnetic radii of the bound nucleon are almost linearly correlated with the bag constant. Using the fact that the size of the bound nucleon inside a nucleus is strongly constrained by $y$-scaling data in quasielastic, electron-nucleus scattering, we set a limit for the reduction allowed in the bag constant for these two models. The present study implies that the bag constant can decrease up to 10--17 % at average nuclear density, depending on the details of the model.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether the bag constant in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can decrease in nuclear medium, and finds that the reduction is limited to 10–17% at average nuclear density, depending on whether the bag constant is coupled to the mean scalar field or to the effective nucleon mass. This is a more restrictive bound than the 35% reduction previously suggested by Jin and Jennings (1996).

## Method

The authors solve self-consistently the equations of motion for quarks and mesons in two modified QMC models where the bag constant decreases either with the mean scalar field (direct coupling model) or with the effective nucleon mass (scaling model). They then calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the bound nucleon using the Peierls-Thouless projection method with Lorentz contraction corrections, and extract the root-mean-square electric and magnetic radii. These radii are compared against experimental constraints from y-scaling data in quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering to set limits on the allowed bag constant reduction.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant reduction limit: 10–17% at average nuclear density, depending on the model (direct coupling or scaling)
- In the standard QMC model (constant bag constant), the quark r.m.s. radius increases slightly while the bag radius typically decreases by a few percent at normal nuclear density
- The term g_sigma^q * sigma-bar is of order 100 MeV at normal nuclear density
- The free nucleon mass is set to 939 MeV as input
- The pi-NN coupling constant used is f^NN ≈ 3.03, corresponding to f^2_piNN ≈ 0.081

## Context

This work extends the QMC model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and later developed by several groups, and directly addresses the modification suggested by Jin and Jennings (1996) who proposed a 35% reduction of the bag constant at normal nuclear density. The paper uses experimental constraints from y-scaling analyses by Sick (1985) and Day (1990) to limit the nucleon size change. The results enable more realistic calculations of in-medium nucleon properties and nuclear matter saturation, while maintaining consistency with quasielastic electron scattering data.
