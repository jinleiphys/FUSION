# An algebraic model for the pion's valence-quark GPD

> We briefly report on a recent computation, with the help of a fruitful algebraic model, sketching the pion valence dressed-quark generalized parton distribution. Then, preliminary, we introduce on a sensible procedure to get reliable results in both Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) and Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) kinematical regions, grounded on the GPD overlap representation and its parametrization of a Radon transform of the so-called double distribution (DD).

## Key claim

The authors present an algebraic model for the pion's valence-quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) that, while initially only reliable near the forward limit (ξ=0) within the DGLAP region, can be extended to the ERBL kinematical region through a Radon-transform inversion procedure applied to the GPD overlap representation.

## Method

The authors compute the pion's valence-quark GPD using a symmetry-preserving algebraic model based on Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), employing a dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from Chang (2013). They obtain the GPD in the DGLAP region via the overlap representation of light-cone wave functions, then invert the Radon transform of the double distribution (DD) representation to extract the DD, which fully determines the GPD in both DGLAP and ERBL regions. The inversion is performed numerically by discretizing the DD support domain and solving a linear system, constrained by parity in α and the condition that the DD vanishes for β<0.

## Key numbers

* The algebraic model uses a dressed-quark mass scale M = 0.40 GeV, with results shown for M = 0.40 ± 0.05 GeV (producing the band in the pion electromagnetic form factor comparison).
* The pion valence dressed-quark PDF from the overlap representation is q_π(x) = N_ν x^(2ν) (1-x)^(2ν), with ν=1 and normalization factor k_1=30.
* The model scale is ζ_H = 0.51 GeV.
* The analytical Pobylitsa-gauge double distribution for the overlap GPD at t=0 is f_P(β,α) = (30/4) (1 - 3α^2 - 2β + 3β^2).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based computation of the pion's valence-quark GPD from Mezrag (2014) and Mezrag (2016), which was limited to the forward limit and DGLAP region. The Radon-transform inversion procedure builds on the double distribution representation of Mueller (1998) and Radyushkin (1998), and the Pobylitsa gauge from Pobylitsa (2002). The approach enables future computation of GPDs from first principles and provides a framework for generic GPD modeling, including extension to non-zero skewness and the ERBL region.
