# B_{c} absorption cross sections by nucleons

> The cross sections of $B_{c}$\ absorption by nucleons are calculated in meson-baryon exchange model using hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(4)/SU(5) flavor symmetries. The values of different coupling constants used in the model are obtained from vector meson dominance model, QCD sum rule or SU(4)/SU(5) flavor symmetries. Calculated values of cross sections are found to be significantly different from the previous study in which b-flavored hadron exchange is neglected. These results could be useful in calculating production rate of $B_{c}$ meson in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The cross sections for \(B_c\) absorption by nucleons are calculated in a meson-baryon exchange model and are found to be significantly different from a previous study (Lodhi 2007) that neglected b-flavored hadron exchange; the authors note that the SU(5) flavor symmetry relations used for several coupling constants are less reliable due to large variation in quark masses.

## Method
The authors use a hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(4)/SU(5) flavor symmetries to derive effective interaction vertices for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector (PPV), baryon-baryon-pseudoscalar (BBP), and baryon-baryon-vector (BBV) couplings. Scattering amplitudes are computed at tree level for four processes: \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B\), \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B^*\), \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D} \Lambda_b\), and \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D}^* \Lambda_b\), including both c-flavor and b-flavor exchange diagrams. Cross sections are evaluated with two sets of coupling constants (Set 1 from SU(4)/SU(5) symmetry and empirical \(K N \Lambda\) couplings; Set 2 from QCD sum rules) and a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) varied between 1 and 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For process \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B\) with Set 1 couplings and cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 19 mb; without b-exchange it is 7 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B^*\) with Set 1 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 0.8 mb; without b-exchange it is 0.6 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D} \Lambda_b\) with Set 1 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 14 mb; without b-exchange it is 10.5 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D}^* \Lambda_b\) with Set 1 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 2 mb; without b-exchange it is 2 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B\) with Set 2 couplings and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 28 mb; without b-exchange it is 23 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^+ \rightarrow \Lambda_c B^*\) with Set 2 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 0.75 mb; without b-exchange it is 0.22 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D} \Lambda_b\) with Set 2 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 4.2 mb; without b-exchange it is 1.5 mb.
- For process \(N B_c^- \rightarrow \overline{D}^* \Lambda_b\) with Set 2 and \(\Lambda = 2\) GeV, the peak cross section with b-exchange is 2.1 mb; without b-exchange it is 2.3 mb.
- The coupling constants used are: \(g_{B_c B D^*} = g_{B_c B^* D} = 11.9\) (from VMD and SU(5)); \(g_{D N \Lambda_c} = 13.1\) (Set 1) or 7.9 (Set 2); \(g_{D^* N \Lambda_c} = 4.3\) (Set 1) or 7.5 (Set 2); \(g_{B N \Lambda_b} = 13.1\) (Set 1) or 7.9 (Set 2); \(g_{B^* N \Lambda_b} = 4.3\) (Set 1) or 7.5 (Set 2); \(g_{B_c \Lambda_c \Lambda_b} = -10.7\) (Set 1) or -6.5 (Set 2).

## Context
This work extends the meson-baryon exchange approach previously applied to \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) absorption by pions, \(\rho\) mesons, and nucleons (Haglin 2000, Lin 2000, Liu 2001, Lin 2001). It directly improves upon the earlier calculation of \(B_c\) absorption by nucleons (Lodhi 2007) by including b-flavored hadron exchange and anomalous parity interaction. The results are intended for use in calculating \(B_c\) meson production rates in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the suppression or enhancement of heavy hadrons may serve as a probe of quark-gluon plasma properties.
