# D mesons in non-central heavy-ion collisions: fluctuating vs. averaged initial conditions

> The suppression of D mesons in non-central heavy-ion collisions is investigated. The anisotropy in collisions at finite impact parameter leads to an ordering of all-angle, in- and out-of-plane nuclear modification factors due to the different in-medium path lengths. Within our MC@sHQ+EPOS model of heavy-quark propagation in the QGP we demonstrate that fluctuating initial conditions lead to an effective reduction of the energy loss of heavy quarks, which is seen in a larger nuclear modification factor at intermediate and high transverse momenta. The elliptic flow at small transverse momenta is reduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, within the MC@sHQ+EPOS model, fluctuating initial conditions lead to an effective reduction of heavy-quark energy loss compared to averaged initial conditions, resulting in a larger nuclear modification factor at intermediate and high transverse momenta and a reduced elliptic flow at small transverse momenta. This result is conditional on the specific implementation of energy loss and the background medium, and is qualitatively opposite to findings by Cao (2014).

## Method

The authors couple the Monte-Carlo heavy-quark propagation code MC@sHQ to the EPOS fluid dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, initializing charm quarks at nucleon-nucleon collision points with FONLL momentum distributions. Heavy quarks are propagated through the medium by Monte-Carlo sampling of the Boltzmann equation with either purely elastic scatterings or collisional plus radiative corrections, with cross sections rescaled by a K-factor of 0.8 tuned to reproduce ALICE data for D mesons in central collisions. Hadronization occurs at T=155 MeV via coalescence and fragmentation, and the study compares results from event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions versus initial conditions averaged over 1000 EPOS events for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV in the 30-50% centrality class.

## Key numbers

- The K-factor for the collisional+radiative interaction mechanism is K=0.8, close to the generic case of K=1.0
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA is studied for transverse momenta from 1 GeV to 20 GeV in the 30-50% centrality class
- The in-plane R_AA is defined for D mesons with Delta-phi in [-pi/4, pi/4] and [3pi/4, 5pi/4]; out-of-plane R_AA for Delta-phi in [pi/4, 3pi/4] and [5pi/4, 7pi/4]
- The exponent relating energy loss to energy density is delta = beta/n < 1, where beta ranges from approximately 1 (collisional with running alpha_s) to approximately 2 (radiative LPM), and n is approximately 4
- For the elliptic flow study, N_HQ = 10^4 heavy-quark events are run per EPOS fluid dynamical event
- Hadronization temperature is set to T=155 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MC@sHQ+EPOS model previously presented by Nahrgang (2013) to non-central heavy-ion collisions, studying the influence of fluctuating versus averaged initial conditions on heavy-quark observables. The results contradict findings by Cao (2014), who reported enhanced energy loss of heavy quarks in a medium with local fluctuations, highlighting that different implementations of energy loss mechanisms and background medium evolution can emphasize different aspects of the physics. The study enables future investigations of path-length dependence of energy loss mechanisms and the role of viscous fluid dynamics, and motivates improved models with consistent charm quark initialization within the EPOS multiple scattering approach.
