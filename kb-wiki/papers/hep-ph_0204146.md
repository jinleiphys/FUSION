# A simple sum rule for the thermal gluon spectral function and applications

> In this paper, we derive a simple sum rule satisfied by the gluon spectral function at finite temperature. This sum rule is useful in order to calculate exactly some integrals that appear frequently in the photon or dilepton production rate by a quark gluon plasma. Using this sum rule, we rederive simply some known results and obtain some new results that would be extremely difficult to justify otherwise. In particular, we derive an exact expression for the collision integral that appears in the calculation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect.

## Key claim

The paper derives a simple sum rule for the gluon spectral function at finite temperature that allows exact analytic evaluation of certain integrals appearing in photon and dilepton production rates, under the condition that the gluon self-energy depends only on the ratio \(x = k_0/k\) and satisfies specific analyticity properties (vanishing imaginary part for \(x \ge 1\) and non-negative real part for \(x \ge 1\)).

## Method

The authors start from the integral representation of the functions \(J_{T,L}\) and \(K_{T,L}\) that appear in the two-loop photon polarization tensor, where the integrand contains the HTL-resummed gluon self-energies \(\Pi_{T,L}(x)\). By rewriting the integral in terms of the spectral function \(\overline{\rho}(k_0,k)\) of the resummed gluon propagator and using its spectral representation, they derive a closed-form sum rule that reduces the \(x\)-integral to a simple difference of two terms involving \(\operatorname{Re}\Pi(0)\) and \(\operatorname{Re}\Pi(\infty)\). This sum rule is then applied to obtain analytic expressions for \(J_{T,L}\) and \(K_{T,L}\) in terms of a single function \(F(x)\), which can be expressed via dilogarithms or studied asymptotically.

## Key numbers

* For \(N_c=3\), \(N_F=2\) flavors and real photons (\(Q^2=0\)): \(J_L - J_T = \pi \ln(2)\) and \(K_L - K_T = \frac{\pi}{4}(1 - 2\ln(2))\).
* For \(N_c=3\), \(N_F=3\) flavors and real photons: \(J_L - J_T = \pi\left[\frac{\pi^2}{8} - \frac{33}{8}\ln^2(2) + 3\ln(2)\ln(3) - \frac{3}{2}\operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) - \frac{3}{2}\operatorname{Li}_2\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)\right]\).
* In the limit \(M_{\rm eff} \ll m_g\): \(J_T \approx -\frac{\pi}{8}\ln^2\left(\frac{m_g^2}{M_{\rm eff}^2}\right)\) and \(K_T \approx \frac{\pi}{8}\left[\ln\left(\frac{m_g^2}{M_{\rm eff}^2}\right) - 2\right]\).
* In the limit \(M_{\rm eff} \gg m_g\): \(J_T \approx -\frac{\pi}{8}\frac{m_g^2}{M_{\rm eff}^2}\ln\left(\frac{M_{\rm eff}^2}{m_g^2}\right)\) and \(K_T \approx \frac{\pi}{48}\frac{m_g^2}{M_{\rm eff}^2}\ln\left(\frac{M_{\rm eff}^2}{m_g^2}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-loop calculations of photon and dilepton production in a quark-gluon plasma by Aurenche et al. (2000, 2002), where the functions \(J_{T,L}\) and \(K_{T,L}\) were only evaluated numerically. The sum rule provides analytic control over these integrals, enabling exact asymptotic expansions that were previously inaccessible. The results also generalize beyond the HTL approximation to any quasi-particle model with well-defined screening masses, and the same technique is applied to obtain an exact expression for the collision integral appearing in the resummation of ladder diagrams for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect, as formulated by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001, 2002).
