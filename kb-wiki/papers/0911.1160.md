# On the energy dependence of $K/π$ fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In this note we will discuss the energy dependence of particle ratio fluctuations in heavy ion collisions. We study how the inherent multiplicity dependence of ratio fluctuations is reflected in the excitation function of the dynamical fluctuations. Specifically, we will show that the observed excitation function of dynamical $K/π$ fluctuations is consistent with the expected dependence on the number of accepted pions and kaons in both the STAR and NA49 experiments.

## Key claim

The observed steep increase of dynamical \(K/\pi\) fluctuations towards lower beam energies is consistent with a "trivial" dependence on the number of accepted (identified) kaons and pions, rather than requiring an interpretation in terms of the QCD critical point. The authors show that the excitation function is reproduced by scaling the dynamical fluctuations from top RHIC energy using the inverse of the accepted multiplicities, provided one uses the actual number of identified particles within the experimental acceptance rather than extrapolated mid-rapidity yields.

## Method

The authors derive the formal expression for dynamical fluctuations of a particle ratio \(A/B\) in terms of scaled correlation coefficients \(C_{AB}\), showing that \(\sigma_{\text{dynamical}}^2 = (1/\langle A \rangle)C_{AA} + (1/\langle B \rangle)C_{BB} - (2/\sqrt{\langle A \rangle\langle B \rangle})C_{AB}\). They then apply several multiplicity-scaling prescriptions (Poisson scaling, particle-number scaling, kaon-number scaling, pion-number scaling, and geometric scaling) to the STAR and NA49 data on \(K/\pi\) fluctuations, using the measured numbers of identified kaons and pions within each experiment's acceptance. The scaled fluctuations at lower energies are predicted from the value at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV by multiplying by the appropriate ratio of mean multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical fluctuations \(\sigma_{\text{dynamical}}^{K/\pi}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=6.27\) GeV (NA49): \(7.89\%\), with \(\langle \pi \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 30.9\) and \(\langle K \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 5.7\)
- Dynamical fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s}=17.3\) GeV (NA49): \(3.17\%\), with \(\langle \pi \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 416.6\) and \(\langle K \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 54.2\)
- Dynamical fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (STAR): \(3.77\%\), with \(\langle \pi \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 432.6\) and \(\langle K \rangle_{\text{ident.}} = 20.6\)
- Poisson-scaled quantity \(f_{\text{Poisson}}\) for \(K/\pi\) fluctuations ranges from \(3.0\%\) at \(\sqrt{s}=6.27\) GeV to \(2.8\%\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, showing weak energy dependence
- Scaled correlation coefficients in a simple resonance gas model at \(T=170\) MeV: \(C_{KK}=0.1\), \(C_{\pi\pi}=0.36\), \(C_{K\pi}=0.13\)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of particle ratio fluctuations initiated by the NA49 collaboration (Afanasev 2000, Alt 2008) and the STAR collaboration (Abelev 2009), who observed a steep rise of dynamical \(K/\pi\) fluctuations towards lower collision energies that could not be reproduced by thermal model calculations (Torrieri 2007) or the UrQMD transport model (Alt 2008). The authors argue that this rise is a consequence of the decreasing number of accepted kaons and pions in fixed-target experiments like NA49, rather than a signature of the QCD critical point as speculated by Koch (2005). The paper enables future studies to correct for "trivial" multiplicity dependencies using the proposed Poisson scaling \(f_{\text{Poisson}}\) before drawing conclusions about phase transitions, and suggests that the disagreement with UrQMD may still point to undiscovered sources of correlations such as quantum statistics.
