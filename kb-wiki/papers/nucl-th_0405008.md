# Evidence of Pentaquark States from K+ N Scattering Data?

> Motivated by the recent experimental evidence of the exotic B = S = +1 baryonic state Theta(1540), we examine the older existing data on K+ N elastic scattering through the time delay method. We find positive peaks in time delay around 1.545 and 1.6 GeV in the D03 and P01 partial waves of K+ N scattering respectively, in agreement with experiments. We also find an indication of the J=3/2 Theta* spin-orbit partner to the Theta, in the P03 partial wave at 1.6 GeV. We discuss the pros and contras of these findings in support of the interpretation of these peaks as possible exotics.

## Key claim

The authors find positive peaks in time delay around 1.545 and 1.6 GeV in the D03 and P01 partial waves of K+ N scattering respectively, which they interpret as possible evidence for the exotic Theta(1540) pentaquark state, but they caution that the phase shifts in these partial waves do not show the full 180-degree jump expected for a proper resonance, leaving open the possibility that these peaks arise from non-resonant background rather than genuine resonant states.

## Method

The authors applied the time delay method of Eisenbud, Wigner, and Smith to single-energy values of K+ N elastic scattering phase shifts from older data, calculating the time delay as 2 hbar times the derivative of the phase shift with respect to energy. They used polynomial fits to the single-energy phase shift values rather than model-dependent T-matrix solutions, and examined the D03, P01, and P03 partial waves for peaks that could correspond to the Theta(1540) and its proposed spin-orbit partner Theta*.

## Key numbers

- Time delay peak at 1.545 GeV in the D03 partial wave of K+ N elastic scattering
- Time delay peak at 1.6 GeV in the P01 partial wave of K+ N elastic scattering
- Time delay peak around 1.6 GeV in the P03 partial wave, proposed as the J=3/2 spin-orbit partner of the P01 resonance
- The experimental Theta(1540) mass and width reported by Nakano et al. (2003) are 1.54 +/- 0.01 GeV and 0.025 GeV respectively
- In their earlier work (we2), the authors found peaks at 1.49 GeV (D03), 1.57 GeV (P01), and 1.48 GeV (P13) using model-dependent T-matrix solutions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the time delay method to K+ N scattering (we2, 2003) by using single-energy phase shifts instead of model-dependent T-matrix solutions, reducing model dependence. The analysis was motivated by the experimental evidence for the Theta(1540) pentaquark reported by Nakano et al. (2003) and the CLAS collaboration, and by the prediction from Close (2003) and Glozman (2003) of a J=3/2 spin-orbit partner Theta*. The results contradict partial wave analyses that found no pole values in the 1.5 GeV region, and the authors note that the absence of a full pi-jump in the phase shifts casts doubt on interpreting these peaks as proper resonances, similar to the conclusion of Krein (2004). The work enables future experimental searches for higher-lying exotic states around 1.8 GeV.
