# Fast prediction of the hydrodynamic QGP evolution in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions using Fourier Neural Operators

> Recent research in machine learning has employed neural networks to learn mappings between function spaces on bounded domains termed ``neural operators''. As such, these operators can provide alternatives to standard numerical methods for partial differential equation (PDE) solutions. In particular, the Fourier Neural Operator (FNO) has been shown to map solutions for classical fluid flow problems with accuracy competitive with traditional PDE solvers and with much greater computing speed. This paper explores the first application of FNOs to model ultra-relativistic hydrodynamic flow of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The application in ultra-relativistic flow is novel relative to classical flow, due to the hydrodynamic evolution of the QGP occurring in femtometer-scaled explosions characterized by rapid expansion cooling. In this study we investigate the applicability of FNOs as computationally fast alternatives to standard numerical PDE solvers. The FNO predictions are evaluated by comparing to standard PDE solutions, using \MUSIC in the \JETSCAPE Monte Carlo event generator framework. The performance of calculating established experimental observables for flow and jet quenching using FNOs in the MC framework are also reported.

## Key claim

Fourier Neural Operators (FNOs) can serve as computationally fast alternatives to standard numerical PDE solvers for modeling the hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though the application is novel because the QGP evolution involves femtometer-scaled explosions with rapid expansion cooling, unlike classical fluid flow problems where FNOs have previously been validated.

## Method

The authors trained a Fourier Neural Operator to map initial conditions to the full spacetime evolution of the QGP hydrodynamic fields, using the MUSIC hydrodynamic solver within the JETSCAPE Monte Carlo event generator framework to generate training data and reference solutions. The FNO predictions were evaluated by comparing them to the standard PDE solutions from MUSIC, and the performance was further assessed by computing established experimental observables for flow and jet quenching within the Monte Carlo framework.

## Key numbers

- The paper reports that FNOs achieve accuracy "competitive with traditional PDE solvers" for classical fluid flow problems, but no specific numerical accuracy metrics (e.g., relative error percentages) are provided for the QGP application in the available text.
- No explicit speedup factor or wall-clock time comparison between FNO and MUSIC is given in the truncated text.
- No numerical results for flow or jet quenching observables (e.g., elliptic flow v2, nuclear modification factor R_AA) are presented in the available text.

## Context

This work extends the application of neural operators, specifically Fourier Neural Operators previously demonstrated for classical fluid dynamics, to the relativistic hydrodynamic regime relevant for heavy-ion collisions. The approach contrasts with traditional PDE solvers like MUSIC (used in the JETSCAPE framework) by offering a potentially much faster surrogate model. If successful, this would enable rapid Bayesian parameter estimation and large-scale Monte Carlo simulations that are currently computationally prohibitive with standard numerical solvers.
