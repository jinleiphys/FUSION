# Production of Anticentauro Events in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We propose a novel method for studying the production of anticentauro events in high energy heavy ion collisions utilizing Chebyshev expansion coefficients. These coefficients have proved to be very efficient in investigating the pattern of fluctuations in neutral pion fraction. For the anticentauro like events, the magnitude of first few coefficients is strongly enhanced (~3 times) as compared to those of normal HIJING events. Various characteristics of Chebyshev coefficients are studied in detail and the probability of formation of exotic events is calculated from the simulated events.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Chebyshev expansion coefficients can serve as an efficient tool for identifying anticentauro events in heavy ion collisions, based on the observation that the magnitude of the first few coefficients is enhanced by approximately a factor of 3 for simulated anticentauro-like events compared to normal HIJING events. The claim is conditional on the simulation using a specific model where a domain of size 60 degrees is injected into normal HIJING events by flipping charged pions into neutral pions until the neutral pion fraction reaches at least 0.9.

## Method

The author used the HIJING Monte Carlo program to simulate minimum-biased Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, generating two ensembles of 10,000 events each: normal HIJING events and HIJING events with artificially embedded anticentauro-type fluctuations modeled according to a (1/2 sqrt(f)) distribution. The neutral pion fraction was calculated on an event-by-event basis for various azimuthal bins ranging from 2pi/18 to 2pi, and Chebyshev coefficients up to order 99 were computed from the azimuthal distribution of the neutral pion fraction. The analysis examined the first-order coefficient C0, higher-order coefficients, correlations between coefficients, and a probability function defined as the fraction of events exceeding a threshold of the mean C0 plus five times the dispersion from normal HIJING events.

## Key numbers

- For the azimuthal bin 2pi/18, the mean first Chebyshev coefficient C0 for anticentauro-like events is 124.8, compared to 45.0 for normal HIJING events.
- For the azimuthal bin 2pi/6, the mean C0 for anticentauro-like events is 42.7, compared to 15.4 for normal HIJING events.
- For the azimuthal bin 2pi, the mean C0 for anticentauro-like events is 7.1, compared to 2.6 for normal HIJING events.
- For the azimuthal bin 2pi/6, the threshold value for identifying exotic events (mean C0 plus 5 sigma from HIJING) is 17.21, and the statistical probability of a normal HIJING event exceeding this threshold is 0.
- For semi-central events at the same azimuthal bin, the probability of exceeding a threshold of 16.61 is 2.2 x 10^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the search for centauro and anticentauro events, originally observed in cosmic ray experiments such as the JACEE prototype, to the LHC energy regime using theoretical simulation. The study introduces Chebyshev polynomials as a novel mathematical technique for analyzing fluctuations in neutral pion fraction, building on prior applications of this method in track reconstruction and fluctuation studies. The results enable future experimental searches at LHC by providing a quantitative method to identify anticentauro events and estimate their production probability from background.
