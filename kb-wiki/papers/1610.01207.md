# Manifestation of the $P$-wave diproton resonance in single-pion production in $pp$ collisions

> It is demonstrated that many important features of single-pion production in $pp$ collisions at intermediate energies ($T_p \simeq 400$-$800$ MeV) can naturally be explained by supposing excitation of intermediate diproton resonances in $pp$ channels ${}^1D_2$, ${}^3F_3$ and ${}^3P_2$, in addition to conventional mechanisms involving an intermediate $Δ$-isobar. We predict for the first time the crucial role of the ${}^3P_2$ diproton resonance, found in recent experiments on the single-pion production reaction $pp \to pp({}^1S_0) π^0$, in reproducing the proper behavior of spin-correlation parameters in the reaction $pp \to d π^+$ which were poorly described by conventional meson-exchange models to date. The possible quark structure of the $P$-wave diproton resonances is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the ${}^3P_2$ diproton resonance, though contributing less than 10% to the total $pp \to d\pi^+$ cross section, is crucial for reproducing spin-correlation parameters that conventional meson-exchange models and earlier dibaryon-inclusive models have poorly described. The claim is conditional on using "soft" cut-off parameters in meson-baryon vertices (below 1 GeV) consistent with $\pi N$ elastic scattering data, which forces the need for additional short-range mechanisms beyond conventional $\Delta$-isobar excitation.

## Method

The authors model the reaction $pp \to d\pi^+$ at proton kinetic energies $T_p \simeq 400$–$800$ MeV using three mechanisms: one-nucleon exchange, intermediate $\Delta$-isobar excitation via $t$-channel pion exchange, and intermediate dibaryon resonance excitation in the ${}^1D_2P$, ${}^3F_3D$, and ${}^3P_2D$ partial waves. They use a monopole parameterization for the $\pi N\Delta$ and $\pi NN$ vertex functions with cut-off parameters $\tilde{\Lambda}_* = 0.3$ GeV (fitted to $\pi N$ $P_{33}$ partial cross section data) and $\tilde{\Lambda} = 0.7$ GeV, and fit the dibaryon resonance parameters to the partial-wave amplitudes from the SAID coupled-channel PWA solution C500.

## Key numbers

- The ${}^3P_2$ diproton resonance mass is $M_D = 2211$ MeV and total width $\Gamma_D = 195$ MeV, with product of partial widths $\Gamma_i \Gamma_f = 450$ MeV$^2$.
- The ${}^1D_2$ diproton resonance mass is $M_D = 2155$ MeV and total width $\Gamma_D = 101$ MeV.
- The ${}^3F_3$ diproton resonance mass is $M_D = 2197$ MeV and total width $\Gamma_D = 152$ MeV.
- Conventional ONE + $N\Delta$ mechanisms give approximately 40–50% of the partial cross sections in the ${}^1D_2P$ and ${}^3F_3D$ waves, and only about 2.5% of the ${}^3P_2D$ partial cross section near the resonance peak.
- The cut-off parameter in the $\pi N\Delta$ vertex fitted to $\pi N$ elastic scattering $P_{33}$ data is $\tilde{\Lambda}_* = 0.3$ GeV.
- The ${}^3P_2$ resonance parameters agree with those from Komarov (2016): $M_D = 2207 \pm 12$ MeV and $\Gamma_D = 170 \pm 32$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Platonova and Kukulin, 2016, in *Nuclear Physics A*) that combined conventional mechanisms with dibaryon resonances in the ${}^1D_2$ and ${}^3F_3$ channels, now adding the ${}^3P_2$ diproton resonance. It contradicts the view that all resonance-like behavior in $pp$ scattering near $N\Delta$ thresholds arises solely from pseudoresonances (Simonov, 1979) rather than genuine dibaryon states. The results support the recent experimental evidence for the ${}^3P_2$ and ${}^3P_0$ diproton resonances from Komarov (2016) at COSY-ANKE, and enable future studies of the quark structure of $P$-wave diproton resonances as six-quark states with hidden-color configurations, as discussed for the $d^*$ dibaryon by Bashkanov (2013), Huang (2014), and Dong (2015).
