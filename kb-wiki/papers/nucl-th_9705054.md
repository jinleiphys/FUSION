# An HFB scheme in natural orbitals

> We present a formulation of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations which solves the problem directly in the basis of natural orbitals. This provides a very efficient scheme which is particularly suited for large scale calculations on coordinate-space grids.

# An HFB scheme in natural orbitals

## Key claim

The paper presents a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) formulation that solves the problem directly in the basis of natural orbitals, claiming that this scheme is particularly efficient for large-scale calculations on coordinate-space grids. The key qualifier is that the derivation assumes a time-even ground state (even-even nuclei) and a separable energy functional with a mean-field part and a pairing part, using a volume pairing with zero-range force as a specific model.

## Method

The authors start from the BCS ansatz for the pairing wave function and derive variational equations with respect to both the occupation amplitudes and the single-particle wavefunctions, using the natural orbital basis where the one-body density matrix is diagonal. The variation yields a state-dependent generalized mean-field Hamiltonian and a symmetric matrix of Lagrange multipliers for orthonormality constraints. The resulting nonlinear system is solved iteratively using a damped gradient step method adapted for coordinate-space techniques, with a state-dependent damping operator that accounts for the kinetic energy and the pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- The scheme costs only 20% more iterations than the much simpler BCS approximation.
- The damping operator uses a numerical parameter x0 ≈ 0.2.
- The damping operator includes a term with 50 MeV plus the kinetic energy operator.
- The scheme was tested for nuclei from 16O to the isotopes of Pb, including both proton-rich and neutron-rich exotic nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the standard HFB solution scheme described in RinSch and Doba, which typically requires a four-times-larger super-density-matrix or a double set of single-particle wavefunctions, both of which become cumbersome for deformed nuclei. The present approach offers a promising alternative that simplifies large-scale calculations of deformed nuclei by handling only a single set of single-particle wavefunctions, with each iteration being as fast as in the BCS case. The paper builds on earlier work on damped gradient techniques (dampgrad) and on the physical content of the pair density (newdoba).
