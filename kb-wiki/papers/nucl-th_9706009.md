# The pd <--> pi+ t reaction around the Delta resonance

> The pd <--> pi+ t process has been calculated in the energy region around the Delta-resonance with elementary production/absorption mechanisms involving one and two nucleons. The isobar degrees of freedom have been explicitly included in the two-nucleon mechanism via pi-- and rho-exchange diagrams. No free parameters have been employed in the analysis since all the parameters have been fixed in previous studies on the simpler pp <--> pi+ d process. The treatment of the few-nucleon dynamics entailed a Faddeev-based calculation of the reaction, with continuum calculations for the initial p-d state and accurate solutions of the three-nucleon bound-state equation. The integral cross-section was found to be quite sensitive to the NN interaction employed while the angular dependence showed less sensitivity. Approximately a 4% effect was found for the one-body mechanism, for the three-nucleon dynamics in the p-d channel, and for the inclusion of a large, possibly converged, number of three-body partial states, indicating that these different aspects are of comparable importance in the calculation of the spin-averaged observables.

## Key claim

The integral cross-section for the $pd \leftrightarrow \pi^+ t$ reaction around the Delta resonance is quite sensitive to the NN interaction employed, while the angular dependence shows less sensitivity; approximately a 4% effect was found for the one-body mechanism, for the three-nucleon dynamics in the p-d channel, and for the inclusion of a large number of three-body partial states, indicating these aspects are of comparable importance for spin-averaged observables.

## Method

The authors performed a Faddeev-based calculation of the $pd \leftrightarrow \pi^+ t$ reaction, including elementary production/absorption mechanisms involving one nucleon (via the $\pi$NN vertex) and two nucleons (via $\pi$- and $\rho$-exchange diagrams with explicit isobar degrees of freedom). The three-nucleon dynamics were treated with continuum calculations for the initial p-d state and accurate solutions of the three-nucleon bound-state equation, using a separable expansion method (EST) to represent the NN interaction. No free parameters were employed, as all parameters were fixed in previous studies of the simpler $pp \leftrightarrow \pi^+ d$ process.

## Key numbers

- The integral cross-section showed approximately a 4% effect from the one-body mechanism, from the three-nucleon dynamics in the p-d channel, and from the inclusion of a large number of three-body partial states.
- The $\Delta$ isobar mass was set to $M_\Delta = 1232$ MeV.
- The calculation included three-body partial waves with angular momenta up to $l=2$ for the $\Delta$N subsystem, with convergence checked up to $l=5$ in preliminary test calculations.
- The bound-state wavefunction from Ref. Sauer consisted of 48 three-baryon partial waves, of which 22 refer to purely nucleonic states.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic models for the $pd \rightarrow \pi^+ t$ reaction, such as those by Gremaq, Gresai, and Sainio, which used simpler s-wave deuteron and triton wavefunctions and limited numbers of intermediate states. It also goes beyond the "deuteron model" approach originally employed by Ruderman and later refined by Ingram, Fearing, and LochWeb, which linked the cross section to $pp \rightarrow \pi^+ d$ experimental data. The calculation achieves several improvements over previous microscopic analyses by including a Faddeev-based treatment of the initial-state three-body dynamics, full antisymmetrization of the three nucleons, and a larger number of partial waves, enabling more reliable comparisons with experimental data in the Delta resonance region.
