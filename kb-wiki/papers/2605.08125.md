# Probing Jet-Medium Interactions in Heavy-Ion Collisions Using Energy-Energy Correlators

> Energy-energy correlators (EECs) provide a sensitive probe of both perturbative and nonperturbative dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Jet-medium interactions enhance particle multiplicity within the jet cone, which must be properly accounted for when extracting the EEC of jet shower hadrons in experiments. To address this issue, we develop an augmentation method that exploits momentum conservation between the near-side and away-side regions, using $γ$-jet events with 0-10\% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV simulated with the CoLBT-hydro model. This approach yields an experimentally reconstructed EEC that shows improved agreement with the EEC of hadrons originating primarily from jet parton splittings. Comparing EECs of jets from Pb+Pb and p+p collisions with different matching conditions can be sensitive to jet medium interaction dynamics, and provide a novel means to test the scenario of jet energy loss in the QGP, followed by fragmentations outside the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that jet-induced medium excitation (JIME) biases traditional energy-energy correlator (EEC) extraction in heavy-ion collisions, and proposes a data-driven augmentation method that exploits momentum conservation between near-side and away-side regions to recover the true EEC of jet shower hadrons, with the caveat that this method relies on three specific assumptions about the signal and minimum-bias samples.

## Method

The authors simulate γ-jet events in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV using the CoLBT-hydro model, which couples the linear Boltzmann transport model for jet propagation with (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics for the bulk medium. They develop a data-augmented background subtraction method that estimates the JIME-induced enhancement of soft hadrons inside the jet cone by measuring the corresponding depletion in an away-side cone, using the vector sum of transverse momentum enhancement to balance the jet energy loss. The method is validated by comparing the reconstructed EEC with the true EEC of jet shower hadrons from the simulation.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: Pb+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, 0-10% centrality, with γ-jet events
- Jet radius parameter: Rj = 0.5
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 137 MeV
- Specific shear viscosity: η/s = 0.15
- Initial time for hydrodynamics: τ0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Soft hadron threshold: pT < 4 GeV/c for the data-augmented method
- Soft hadron enhancement threshold: pT ≲ 2 GeV/c for wake front hadrons
- The vector sum of pT enhancement along the γ axis is restricted to less than 10 GeV/c
- Multiplicity matching between signal and minimum-bias events: within 0.1% in region P
- pT matching for event selection: within 5% for photon pT, quenched jet pT, or leading-hadron pT

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions (CMS 2025, ALICE 2024, Liang 2025) by addressing the contamination from jet-induced medium excitation, which was previously studied in the context of hydrodynamic wakes (Yang 2022, Tachibana 2020, Park 2021). The data-augmented method enables experimental isolation of the jet shower EEC, allowing tests of the scenario where parton energy loss occurs in the QGP followed by fragmentation outside the medium, complementing traditional RAA measurements (Marshall 2025). Future applications to inclusive jet samples, different centrality classes, and varying collision energies at RHIC and LHC are suggested.
