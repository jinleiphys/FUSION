# Nucleon Helicity Generalized Parton Distribution at Physical Pion Mass from Lattice QCD

> The generalized parton distributions (GPDs) offer a window on three-dimensional imaging of the nucleon, providing understanding of how the fundamental properties of the nucleon, such as its mass and spin, arise from the underlying quark and gluon degrees of freedom. In this work, we present the first lattice calculation of the nucleon isovector helicity GPD at physical pion mass, using an $a \approx 0.09$ fm lattice ensemble with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks generated by MILC Collaboration. We perform the GPD calculation in Breit frame using averaged nucleon boost momentum $P_z \approx 2.2$ GeV with nonzero momentum transfers in $[0.2,1.0]\text{ GeV}^2$. Nonperturbative renormalization in RI/MOM scheme is used to obtain the quasi-distribution before matching to the lightcone GPDs. The three-dimensional distribution $\tilde{H}(x,Q^2)$ is presented, along with the three-dimensional nucleon tomography and impact-parameter--dependent distribution for selected Bjorken $x$ at $μ=3$ GeV in $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme.

# Nucleon Helicity Generalized Parton Distribution at Physical Pion Mass from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon isovector helicity GPD \(\tilde{H}(x, Q^2)\) at physical pion mass, but the results are limited to the zero-skewness limit and the \(x > 0.05\) region, with systematic uncertainties that grow significantly in the small-\(x\) and antiquark regions due to the finite boost momentum of \(P_z \approx 2.2\) GeV and the need for extrapolation of large-\(z\) matrix elements.

## Method

The calculation uses a \(64^3 \times 96\) MILC ensemble with \(a \approx 0.09\) fm, 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks, and clover valence fermions with Gaussian momentum smearing. Matrix elements are computed in the Breit frame with nucleon boost momentum \(P_z \approx 2.2\) GeV and momentum transfers \(Q^2 \in [0.2, 1.0]~\text{GeV}^2\), using 501,760 measurements over 1960 configurations. Nonperturbative renormalization in the RI/MOM scheme at \(\mu_R = 3.8\) GeV is applied, followed by Fourier transform to quasi-GPDs using both a derivative method and a Regge-inspired extrapolation, then matching to lightcone GPDs at \(\mu = 3\) GeV in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: \(a \approx 0.09\) fm, volume \(64^3 \times 96\)
- Nucleon boost momentum: \(P_z \approx 2.2\) GeV
- Momentum transfer squared values: \(Q^2 \in \{0, 0.19, 0.39, 0.77, 0.97\}~\text{GeV}^2\)
- Total measurements: 501,760 over 1960 configurations
- Renormalization scale: \(\mu_R = 3.8\) GeV (RI/MOM scheme)
- Final scale: \(\mu = 3\) GeV (\(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme)
- Source-sink separations: \(t_\text{sep} \in [8, 12]\) lattice units
- Lattice spatial extent: \(L = 5.63\) fm, giving \(M_\pi L \approx 4\)

## Context

This work extends the LaMET (quasi-PDF) approach to the helicity GPD \(\tilde{H}\), following prior lattice calculations of unpolarized GPDs by MSULat (Lin, 2020) at physical pion mass and polarized GPDs by ETM Collaboration (Alexandrou, 2020) at \(M_\pi \approx 260\) MeV with boost momentum 1.67 GeV. The results for the axial form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) and generalized form factor \(\tilde{A}_{20}(Q^2)\) are compared with traditional OPE-based lattice calculations from ETMC (Alexandrou, 2019), RQCD (Bali, 2019), and PNDME (Jang, 2018), showing consistency within two sigma. This enables the first lattice-QCD determination of the impact-parameter-dependent polarized quark distribution \(\Delta q(x, b)\), previously only accessible from QCD models.
