# Relativistic transport equations with generalized mass shell constraints

> We reexamine the derivation of relativistic transport equations for fermions when conserving the most general spinor structure of the interaction and Green function. Such an extension of the formalism is needed when dealing with {\it e.g.} spin-polarized nuclear matter or non-parity conserving interactions. It is shown that some earlier derivations can lead to an incomplete description of the evolution of the system even in the case of parity-conserving, spin-saturated systems. The concepts of kinetic equation and mass shell condition have to be extended, in particular both of them acquire a non trivial spinor structure which describe a rich polarization dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard derivation of relativistic transport equations for fermions, which often imposes a simple mass shell condition \(p^2=m^2\) by hand, is incomplete; the mass shell constraint generally retains a nontrivial spinor structure and dynamical content, and neglecting this can lead to incorrect dynamics even for parity-conserving, spin-saturated systems.

## Method

The authors reexamine the derivation of relativistic transport equations for fermions using the Keldysh contour Green function formalism, keeping the full spinor decomposition of the Wigner function into 16 components (scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, pseudoscalar). They compare two derivation methods: the "quadratic" method of Elze et al. (1987), which squares the Dirac-like equations to obtain kinetic and mass shell equations, and the "Zubarev" method of Smolyansky et al., which uses a Hamiltonian form along a proper time. They then solve the stationary, homogeneous case for mean fields with pseudoscalar or pseudovector couplings to illustrate the resulting mass shell structure.

## Key numbers

* For the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model (scalar and vector mean fields only), the mass shell condition for the scalar distribution \(f\) is \((\Pi^2 - M^2)f + \Pi_\mu \mathcal{D}_\nu f^{\mu\nu} = 0\), showing that the deviation from the simple mass shell \(\Pi^2 = M^2\) is given by derivatives of the tensor component \(f^{\mu\nu}\).
* For a system with pseudoscalar mean field \(\Sigma_5\), the mass shell condition becomes \(p_*^2 - m_*^2 - \Sigma_5^2 = 0\), and the solution yields four independent distribution functions: the scalar \(f\) and three components of \(f_5^\mu\) orthogonal to \(p_*^\mu\).
* For a system with pseudovector mean field \(\Sigma_{5\mu}\), the mass shell condition is given by the determinant \(D = 0\) (equation 39), which involves terms like \((1 - p_*^2/m_*^2 - \Sigma_{5\alpha}\Sigma_5^\alpha/m_*^2)^2 - 4(p^*_\alpha\Sigma_5^\alpha/m_*^2)^2 + 4\Sigma_{5\alpha}\Sigma_5^\alpha/m_*^2\), and the solution contains only one independent distribution function (the scalar \(f\)).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of relativistic transport theory beyond the standard Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) approach, which assumes a simplified Wigner function \(F(x,p) = (\gamma^\mu p_\mu + m)\varphi(x,p)\) and a trivial mass shell \(p^2 = m^2\). It directly addresses a controversy with Smolyansky et al. (1990s), who claimed that a "non-quadratic" derivation yields a kinetic equation without the mass term \(M \partial_\alpha(\Sigma_s) \nabla^\alpha\); the authors show this discrepancy stems from an incorrect mass shell condition in the Zubarev method. The results enable future work on spin-polarized nuclear matter, non-parity-conserving interactions, and the chiral transition modeled by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Florkowski and collaborators, 1996-1998), where pseudoscalar condensates require coupled transport equations for scalar and axial-vector distributions.
