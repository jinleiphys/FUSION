# Quark anomalous magnetic moment and its effects on the $ρ$ meson properties

> A symmetry-preserving treatment of mesons, within a Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations approach, demands an interconnection between the kernels of the quark gap equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equation. Appealing to those symmetries expressed by the vector and axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identitiges (WGTI), we construct a two-body Bethe-Salpeter kernel and study its implications in the vector channel; particularly, we analyze the structure of the quark-photon vertex, which explicitly develops a vector meson pole in the timelike axis and the quark anomlaous magnetic moment term, as well as a variety of $ρ$ meson properties: mass and decay constants, electromagnetic form factors, and valence-quark distribution amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a modified rainbow-ladder (MRL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, which includes a non-ladder term in the two-body kernel, automatically generates a quark anomalous magnetic moment term in the quark-photon vertex, whereas the standard rainbow-ladder truncation requires this term to be added by hand; the authors show this within a contact interaction model, with the caveat that the model's momentum-independent interaction limits the generality of the results.

## Method

The authors construct a symmetry-preserving two-body Bethe-Salpeter kernel that satisfies vector and axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identities, adding a non-ladder term proportional to a parameter ξ with Dirac structures {𝕀, γ₅, iσ_μν/√6}. They implement this modified rainbow-ladder truncation within a contact interaction model of QCD, where the effective gluon propagator is replaced by a constant 1/m_G² with m_G = 0.132 GeV, and solve the quark gap equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equations for the ρ meson. The quark-photon vertex is obtained from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the ρ meson electromagnetic form factors and valence-quark distribution amplitudes are computed in the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- ρ meson mass: m_ρ = 0.770 GeV in CI-MRL (tuned via ξ = 0.6), compared to m_ρ = 0.929 GeV in CI-RL
- ρ meson leptonic decay constant: f_ρ = 0.125 GeV in CI-MRL, f_ρ = 0.129 GeV in CI-RL
- ρ meson tensor decay constant: f_ρ^⊥ = 0.134 GeV in CI-MRL, f_ρ^⊥ = 0.133 GeV in CI-RL
- Canonically normalized ρ meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes: E_ρ = 1.230, F_ρ = 0.503 in CI-MRL; E_ρ = 1.531, F_ρ = 0 (by construction) in CI-RL
- Quark anomalous magnetic moment strength at Q² = 0: ζ_MRL ≈ 0.19
- ρ meson magnetic moment: μ_ρ = 2.500 in CI-MRL, μ_ρ = 2.110 in CI-RL
- ρ meson quadrupole moment: Q_ρ = -0.327 in CI-MRL, Q_ρ = -0.850 in CI-RL
- Electric charge radius: r_E = 0.692 fm in CI-MRL, r_E = 0.561 fm in CI-RL
- Zero crossing of electric form factor G_E: Q² ≈ 1.96 GeV² in CI-MRL, Q² ≈ 5 GeV² in CI-RL
- Pion mass: m_π = 0.140 GeV (same in both truncations)
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 0.101 GeV (same in both truncations)
- Model parameters: m_G = 0.132 GeV, Λ_UV = 0.905 GeV, Λ_IR = 0.240 GeV, current quark mass m = 7 MeV, dressed quark mass M = 0.368 GeV

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction model approach of Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010) and Roberts (2010, 2011) by incorporating a modified Bethe-Salpeter kernel that goes beyond the standard rainbow-ladder truncation, following the symmetry-preserving construction of Chang (2020). The automatic generation of the quark anomalous magnetic moment term addresses a known limitation of the rainbow-ladder truncation, where such a term must be added by hand as in Wilson (2011) and Raya (2018, 2021). The results for ρ meson static properties are compared with more sophisticated Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations from Xu (2019) and lattice QCD from Owen (2015), showing improved agreement for the magnetic and quadrupole moments. This work enables systematic studies of how the anomalous magnetic moment affects electromagnetic form factors of spin-1 mesons and baryons within a symmetry-preserving framework.
