# Viscous Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions from RHIC to LHC

> We present a systematic hydrodynamic study of the evolution of hadron spectra and their azimuthal anisotropy from the lowest collision energy studied at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), sqrt(s) = 7.7 A GeV, to the highest energy reachable at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), sqrt(s) = 5500 A GeV. The energy dependence of the flow observables are quantitatively studied for both the Monte-Carlo Glauber and Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) models. For MC-Glauber model initial conditions with η/s = 0.08, the differential charged hadron elliptic flow v_2^{ch}(p_T, sqrt(s)) is found to exhibit a very broad maximum in the region 39 < sqrt(s) < 2760 A GeV. For MC-KLN initial conditions with η/s = 0.2, a similar "saturation" is not observed up to LHC energies. We emphasize that this "saturation" of elliptic flow arises from the interplay between radial flow and elliptic flow which shifts with sqrt(s) depending on the fluid's viscosity. By generalizing the definition of spatial eccentricity to isothermal hyper-surface, we also calculate ε_x on the kinetic freeze-out surface at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the "saturation" of differential charged hadron elliptic flow observed for MC-Glauber initial conditions with η/s = 0.08 in the region 39 < sqrt(s) < 2760 A GeV is not a universal feature, as it is not observed for MC-KLN initial conditions with η/s = 0.2 up to LHC energies; this saturation arises from the interplay between radial flow and elliptic flow which shifts with sqrt(s) depending on the fluid's viscosity.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with constant specific shear viscosity, using a lattice QCD based equation of state for matter with zero net baryon density and assuming longitudinal boost invariance. They systematically varied the collision energy from sqrt(s) = 7.7 A GeV to 5500 A GeV, using both Monte-Carlo Glauber and Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) initial condition models, and computed charged hadron multiplicities, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For MC-Glauber model initial conditions with η/s = 0.08, the differential charged hadron elliptic flow v_2^ch(p_T, sqrt(s)) exhibits a very broad maximum in the region 39 < sqrt(s) < 2760 A GeV.
- For MC-KLN initial conditions with η/s = 0.2, a similar "saturation" of elliptic flow is not observed up to LHC energies (sqrt(s) = 5500 A GeV).
- At sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV, MC-Glauber runs with η/s = 0.08 quantitatively reproduce the STAR azimuthal HBT data for final spatial eccentricity, while MC-KLN initial conditions with η/s = 0.2 result in 15-20% larger final eccentricities.
- The final spatial eccentricity ε_f is predicted to approach zero around sqrt(s) = 2.76-5.5 A TeV.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of flow observables by systematically covering the collision energy range from the lowest RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energy (7.7 A GeV) to the highest LHC energy (5500 A GeV), using two different initial condition models (MC-Glauber and MC-KLN). The study builds on earlier work by Shen (2011) on multiplicity scaling and by Song (2010) on hybrid approaches, and enables future experimental discrimination between initial condition models through the predicted violation of sqrt(s)-scaling of centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity at lower energies. The calculations also provide predictions for the RHIC BES program and for the final fireball eccentricity that can be tested by azimuthal HBT analyses from the STAR Collaboration.
