# Local spin polarization by color-field correlators and momentum anisotropy

> We study the local spin polarization of quarks induced by color-field correlators stemming from the correlation of chromo-Lorentz force and chromo-magnetic polarization or chromo-spin Hall effect in the presence of momentum anisotropy. Such effects can trigger longitudinal polarization from fluctuating color fields in glasma or quark gluon plasma phases with transverse expansion for relativistic heavy ion collisions. Especially, from the glasma effect, the resulting longitudinal polarization spectrum of $Λ/\barΛ$ hyperons has a sinusoidal structure with twice the azimuthal angle relative to the anisotropic direction. An order-of-magnitude estimate of the effect aligns with experimental observations. Our findings highlight the significant role of coherent gluon fields as a novel source for spin polarization phenomena in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that parity-even correlators of chromo-magnetic and chromo-electric fields, combined with momentum anisotropy from collective flow, can generate longitudinal spin polarization of quarks, producing a sinusoidal angular structure with twice the azimuthal angle relative to the anisotropic direction. The effect is estimated to be of order 0.1–1%, comparable to experimental observations, but the overall sign depends on whether the dominant contribution comes from glasma fields (corona) or thermal color fields in QGP (core).

## Method

The authors employ quantum kinetic theory for massive quarks in background color fields to derive the spin polarization spectrum induced by color-field correlators. They solve the collisionless color-octet Vlasov equation perturbatively to obtain the color-octet distribution function, then compute the color-singlet axial-vector Wigner function from correlators of the field strength tensor. The resulting polarization is evaluated by integrating over a freeze-out hypersurface, using parameterized flow profiles and either Schwinger-type or quasi-equilibrium quark distributions for the glasma phase, and a thermal model with Fermi-Dirac distributions for the QGP phase.

## Key numbers

- For glasma with Schwinger-type quark distributions: $\mathcal{P}^z \sim 4 \times 10^{-3}$ at $p_T = 2$ GeV and $\phi = \pi/4$, using $Q_s = 1.5$ or $2$ GeV, $\Delta t = 0.2$ fm, $r_m = 0.5$ fm, $u_T = 0.01$, $\delta = 0.3$, $m = 0.5$ GeV, $M_\Lambda = 1.12$ GeV
- For glasma with quasi-equilibrium quark distributions: $\mathcal{P}^z$ of similar magnitude at $p_T = 2$ GeV, using $u_T = 0.2$, with sign flipping at sufficiently large $p_T$
- For QGP with thermal color fields: $\mathcal{P}^z \sim -1.5 \times 10^{-3}$ at $p_T = 2$ GeV and $\phi = \pi/4$ for 30–60% centrality, using $T_f = 165$ MeV, $\tilde{\tau}_f = 4.3$ fm, $r_m = 3.8$ fm, $\epsilon = 0.137$, $\delta = 0.37$
- For QGP at 0–15% centrality: $\tilde{\tau}_f = 7.7$ fm, $r_m = 6.5$ fm, $\epsilon = 0.055$, $\delta = 0.12$
- The overall order-of-magnitude estimate for $\mathcal{P}^z$ is 0.1–1%, comparable to experimental measurements

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization from color-field correlators by Muller, Yang, and collaborators (2021), which required parity-odd correlators between chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields. The present paper demonstrates that parity-even correlators (pairs of chromo-magnetic or chromo-electric fields) also induce polarization in the presence of collective flow, naturally producing the $\sin 2\phi$ angular structure observed in longitudinal spin polarization measurements by STAR (2019) and CMS (2025). The mechanism complements thermal vorticity and thermal-shear approaches (Becattini 2013, Fu 2021) and may help explain the sign and multiplicity dependence of polarization in proton-nucleus collisions. The results also connect to studies of vector meson spin alignment from strong-force fields (Sheng 2022, Kumar 2022, Yang 2024) and could provide initial conditions for spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2018, Huang 2024).
