# QCD Analysis of the H dibaryon

> The status of theoretical studies of the $H$ dibaryon is reviewed. Some recent developments including the effect of the instanton induced interaction and the QCD sum rule results are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical studies of the H dibaryon and concludes that the H dibaryon is either barely bound or unbound, depending on the strength of the instanton induced interaction; even if unbound, a Lambda-Lambda resonance state below the N-Xi threshold remains possible.

## Method

The author reviews the quark cluster model (resonating group method) applied to the six-quark system, incorporating full antisymmetrization, color-magnetic gluon exchange, effective meson exchange interactions, and the instanton induced interaction derived from the axial U(1) anomaly. The QCD sum rule approach is also applied, constructing an interpolating current for the H dibaryon as a product of two flavor-octet baryon currents and comparing the theoretical correlator with a phenomenological spectral density parameterized by discrete poles and continuum parts.

## Key numbers

- The color-magnetic gluon interaction energy for the H dibaryon is estimated as E_cm(H) approximately -450 MeV, derived from E_0 approximately 18 MeV (from the N-Delta mass splitting of 16 x E_0).
- The two-body threshold for Lambda-Lambda is 2231 MeV, for N-Xi is 2254-2260 MeV, and for Sigma-Sigma is 2385-2387 MeV.
- The net effect of the instanton induced interaction pushes the H mass up by about 40-50 MeV.
- Recent (K-,K+) reaction experiments on emulsion targets identified a few candidates of double-hypernuclei with binding energy of less than 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original prediction of the H dibaryon by Jaffe (1977) in the MIT bag model, and the quark cluster model studies by Oka-Shimizu-Yazaki (1983) which found that quark exchange interactions alone cannot make H bound. The review also discusses the Skyrme model approach by Balachandran and collaborators, and lattice QCD calculations by Iwasaki and collaborators, noting that lattice sizes may not be large enough to contain the whole H when the binding energy is small. The inclusion of the instanton induced interaction, determined from the eta-eta' mass difference, provides a new constraint that shifts the prediction toward a weakly bound or unbound H, enabling future experimental searches to focus on a possible Lambda-Lambda resonance rather than a deeply bound state.
