# Analisys of $0^-$ excitations in $^{16}$O from inelastic scattering of polarized protons of intermediate energy

> Comparison of the calculation of inelastic proton scattering from $^{16}$O with excitation of $0^-$ levels with $T=0,1$ with accessible experimental data at different energies of incident protons is presented. The role of antisymmetrization in reaction formalism and the manifestation of the pion condensation in nuclear are discussed. To obtain more solid conclusions on these points more experimental data are needed.

## Key claim

The paper compares DWIA calculations with full versus approximate antisymmetrization for 0⁻ excitations in ¹⁶O, finding no clear advantage of either approach based on available experimental data; the author states that more experimental data are needed to draw solid conclusions about the role of antisymmetrization and the possible manifestation of pion condensation.

## Method

The author performed DWIA calculations for inelastic proton scattering from ¹⁶O exciting the 0⁻ levels at 10.96 MeV (T=0) and 12.8 MeV (T=1), using the DWBA-91 code (full antisymmetrization) and the LEA code (local exchange approximation). Wave functions were obtained from NuShellX@MSU with the Millener-Kurath interaction, and the Paris-Gamburg density dependent (PGDD) interaction was used as the effective nucleon-nucleon force, with the Nakayama-Love (NL) interaction tested at 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The 0⁻ excitations in ¹⁶O have energies 10.96 MeV (T=0) and 12.8 MeV (T=1).
- Calculated cross-section for the T=0 level at 65 MeV incident proton energy exceeds experimental data by a factor of about 2.5 at forward angles.
- For the T=0 level at 135 MeV, the calculated cross-section exceeds experimental data by a factor of 1.4 at forward angles.
- For the T=1 level at 295 MeV, a normalization coefficient of 0.4 applied to the calculated cross-section is needed to fit experimental data.
- The ground state density was calculated using oscillator model wave functions with oscillator length 1.73 fm.
- The momentum transfer corresponding to the angle range 25-35 degrees for the T=1 excitation at 65 MeV is about 0.85 fm⁻¹; the expected pionic enhancement momentum transfer is about 1.7 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 0⁻ excitations in ¹⁶O using polarized proton scattering, with experimental data from PhysRevC.30.746, PhysRevC.39.1222, PhysRevC.47.1615, and PhysRevC.44.1077 for the T=0 level at energies 65, 135, 200, 317, and 400 MeV, and from PhysRevC.30.746 and Wakasa (2006) for the T=1 level. The investigation of pion condensation precursors follows the approach of Wakasa (2006). The author concludes that no evidence for pion condensation is seen in the available data and that additional experimental data at smaller momentum transfer are needed to resolve the question.
