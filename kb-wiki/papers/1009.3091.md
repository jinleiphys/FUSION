# Dilepton flow and deconfinement phase transition in heavy ion collisions

> The dilepton radial flow in Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV is investigated. The space-time evolution of the fireball is described by a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with a variety of equations of state. The slope parameters of the transverse momentum spectra from the partonic and hadronic phases show distinct features and are sensitive to equation of state parameters. The elliptic flow and breaking of M_T scaling are also studied and have distinct features for the two phases. These features can serve as clean signals for the formation of a quark-gluon plasma in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The slope parameter of dilepton transverse momentum spectra shows distinct trends in the low-mass region (hadronic phase, \(M\lesssim 1\) GeV) versus the intermediate-mass region (partonic phase, \(1\lesssim M\lesssim 3\) GeV) when using a lattice-based equation of state or a mixed-phase equation of state, but only if the fireball evolution includes both phases; the effect is absent for pure hadron-gas or pure QGP equations of state.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model to simulate the space-time evolution of the fireball created in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, with initial time \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm/c and peak temperature about 520 MeV in central collisions. They employed four different equations of state: ideal 3-flavor QGP gas, resonance hadron gas, a first-order phase-transition mixed equation of state, and a lattice-QCD-based crossover equation of state. Thermal dilepton production rates were computed from the quark-loop (QGP) and rho-meson-mediated (hadron gas) channels, and the resulting transverse momentum spectra were fitted to extract the inverse slope parameter \(T_{eff}\) as a function of invariant mass \(M\).

## Key numbers

- Initial peak temperature in central Au+Au collisions: about 520 MeV (for \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm/c).
- Phase transition temperature \(T_c\) set to 180 MeV as a tuning parameter (crossover from resonance hadron gas to QGP in the lattice EOS occurs in the range 180–200 MeV).
- Freezeout temperature set to \(T_f=120\) MeV.
- For the QGP-EOS, the ideal gas relation used: \(\epsilon = 3p = (19/12)\pi^2 T^4\) for 3-flavor QGP.
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) calculated at impact parameter \(b=7\) fm for non-central collisions.
- The ratio \(R\) for \(M_T\) scaling evaluated at \(M_T=2.6\) GeV and \(p_T=2\) GeV versus \(p_T=0\) GeV, scanning impact parameters \(b=[0,15]\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the NA60 measurement of dilepton radial flow at SPS energies (Arnaldi, 2007) to RHIC energies, where the longer-lived QGP phase allows partonic collectivity to develop. It contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic studies that used later initial times (0.6 fm/c) and simpler equations of state, and it builds on the approach of Chatterjee (2007) and Dusling (2008) for electromagnetic probes. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC and LHC to distinguish hadronic from partonic emission sources via the mass dependence of the inverse slope parameter, elliptic flow, and \(M_T\) scaling breaking.
