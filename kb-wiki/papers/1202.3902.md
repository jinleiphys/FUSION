# Skyrme Interaction and Nuclear Matter Constraints

> This paper presents a detailed assessment of the ability of the 240 Skyrme interaction parameter sets in the literature to satisfy a series of criteria derived from macroscopic properties of nuclear matter in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density at zero temperature and their density dependence, derived by the liquid drop model, experiments with giant resonances and heavy-ion collisions. The objective is to identify those parameterizations which best satisfy the current understanding of the physics of nuclear matter over a wide range of applications. Out of the 240 models, only 16 are shown to satisfy all these constraints. Additional, more microscopic, constraints on density dependence of the neutron and proton effective mass beta-equilibrium matter, Landau parameters of symmetric and pure neutron nuclear matter, and observational data on high- and low-mass cold neutron stars further reduce this number to 5, a very small group of recommended Skyrme parameterizations to be used in future applications of the Skyrme interaction of nuclear matter related observables. Full information on partial fulfillment of individual constraints by all Skyrme models considered is given. The results are discussed in terms of the physical interpretation of the Skyrme interaction and the validity of its use in mean-field models. Future work on application of the Skyrme forces, selected on the basis of variables of nuclear matter, in Hartree-Fock calculation of properties of finite nuclei, is outlined.

## Key claim

Out of 240 Skyrme interaction parameter sets in the literature, only 16 satisfy a set of macroscopic constraints derived from nuclear matter properties near saturation density, and only 5 survive additional microscopic constraints including effective masses, Landau parameters, and neutron star observations; the authors adopt a validity range of 0.01ρ₀ ≤ ρ ≤ 3ρ₀ for the Skyrme interaction, noting that using nucleon-only Skyrme forces beyond 2–3ρ₀ may yield misleading results due to the expected appearance of hyperons.

## Method

The authors tested 240 Skyrme parameter sets against eleven macroscopic constraints on symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) properties at and near saturation density, derived from the liquid-drop model, giant resonance experiments, heavy-ion collision flow and kaon production data, and low-density Fermi gas studies. Parameterizations that passed all macroscopic constraints were then subjected to additional microscopic constraints: density dependence of neutron and proton effective masses in beta-equilibrium matter, Landau parameters for SNM and PNM, and observational data on high-mass and low-mass cold neutron stars. All nuclear matter quantities were computed analytically from the Skyrme energy density functional using standard expressions for energy per particle, pressure, incompressibility, skewness, and symmetry energy and its derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 ± 0.03 fm⁻³ (consensus empirical value)
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E₀ ≈ 16 MeV (from semi-empirical mass formula)
- Incompressibility constraint (SM1): K₀ = 230 ± 30 MeV
- Skewness constraint (SM2): K′ = 700 ± 500 MeV (where K′ = −Q₀)
- Out of 240 Skyrme parameter sets, only 16 satisfy all macroscopic constraints
- After applying microscopic constraints (effective masses, Landau parameters, neutron star data), the number reduces to 5 recommended parameterizations
- Adopted validity density range for Skyrme interactions: 0.01ρ₀ ≤ ρ ≤ 3ρ₀
- Critical density for hyperon appearance: 2–3ρ₀

## Context

This work provides a comprehensive, systematic filter of the large number of existing Skyrme parameterizations against modern nuclear matter constraints, extending earlier assessments that focused on minimal saturation properties. It is the first part of a planned series that will apply the same constraints to Gogny forces, Separable Monopole model (SMO) interactions, and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, as described by the authors. The study builds on prior work by Blaizot (1980, 1995) on incompressibility from giant monopole resonance, Danielewicz (2002) on pressure from heavy-ion collision flow, and Farine (1997) on the skewness coefficient, and it enables future Hartree-Fock calculations of finite nuclei using only the 5 recommended Skyrme forces that are consistent with both macroscopic and microscopic nuclear matter constraints.
