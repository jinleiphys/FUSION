# Tests of collectivity in $^{98}$Zr by absolute transition rates

> Lifetimes of low-spin excited states in $^{98}$Zr were measured using the recoil-distance Doppler-shift technique and the Doppler-shift attenuation method. The nucleus of interest was populated in a $^{96}$Zr($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{98}$Zr two-neutron transfer reaction at the Cologne FN Tandem accelerator. Lifetimes of six low-spin excited states, of which four are unknown, were measured. The deduced $B(E2)$ values were compared with Monte Carlo shell model and interacting boson model with configuration mixing calculations. Both approaches reproduce well most of the data but leave challenging questions regarding the structure of some low lying states.

## Key claim
The paper reports new lifetime measurements for six low-spin states in $^{98}$Zr, four of which were previously unknown, but notes that while both Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) and interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) calculations reproduce most of the data, "challenging questions regarding the structure of some low lying states" remain unresolved, particularly for the $2^+_2$ state where the branching ratio to the $0^+_3$ state leads to an unrealistically large $B(E2)$ value.

## Method
Lifetimes were measured using the recoil-distance Doppler-shift (RDDS) technique and the Doppler-shift attenuation (DSA) method following population of $^{98}$Zr via the $^{96}$Zr($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{98}$Zr two-neutron transfer reaction at 50 MeV beam energy from the Cologne FN Tandem accelerator. The RDDS data were analyzed using both Bateman equations and the differential decay curve method (DDCM), while the $2^+_3$ state lifetime was extracted from DSA lineshape fits using the APCAD code with Geant4 simulations and stopping powers from SRIM2013.

## Key numbers
- Lifetime of $2^+_1$ state: 10(2) ps (adopted value), compared to 3.8(8) ps from Singh (2018)
- Lifetime of $2^+_2$ state: 9(4) ps (previously unknown)
- Lifetime of $3^-_1$ state: 14(3) ps (previously unknown)
- Lifetime of $4^+_1$ state: 13(5) ps (adopted value), compared to 7.5(15) ps from Singh (2018)
- Lifetime of $4^+_2$ state: 24(5) ps (previously unknown)
- Lifetime of $2^+_3$ state: $6^{+2}_{-3}$ ps (previously unknown)
- Average velocity of ejected $^{98}$Zr nuclei: 1.89(6)% c
- $B(E2;2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ = $1.1^{+0.3}_{-0.2}$ W.u.
- $B(E2;2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ = $46^{+35}_{-14}$ W.u. (assuming pure E2 transition)
- $B(E2;4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ = $25^{+15}_{-7}$ W.u.
- $B(E2;4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_2)$ = $38^{+26}_{-13}$ W.u.

## Context
This work extends the study of shape coexistence and the rapid onset of collectivity in the A $\approx$ 100 region, specifically for $^{98}$Zr which lies at the interface between spherical and deformed configurations near neutron number N=60. The results are compared to large-scale Monte Carlo shell model calculations by Togashi (2016) and to IBM-CM calculations by Gavrielov (2019, 2020) and Garcia-Ramos (2019), which describe the shape transition through configuration mixing of normal and intruder boson spaces. The new lifetime data for the $2^+_2$, $2^+_3$, $4^+_2$, and $3^-_1$ states provide crucial tests for theoretical models, particularly regarding the mixing of $2^+$ states and the nature of the $0^+$ states, enabling future refinements of shell-model Hamiltonians and IBM-CM parameters for this transitional region.
