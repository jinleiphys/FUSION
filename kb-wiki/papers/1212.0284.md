# Recent progress on the determination of the symmetry Energy

> We summarize the current status on constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy from terrestrial laboratory measurements and astrophysical observations. While the value $E_{sym}({ρ_{0}})$ and density slope $L$ of the symmetry energy at saturation density $ρ_{0}$ can vary largely depending on the data or methods, all the existing constraints are essentially consistent with $E_{sym}({ρ_{0}}) = 31 \pm 2$ MeV and $L = 50 \pm 20$ MeV. The determination of the supra-saturation density behavior of the symmetry energy remains a big challenge.

## Key claim

All existing constraints from nuclear reactions, nuclear structures, and neutron star properties are essentially consistent with a symmetry energy at saturation density of \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31 \pm 2\) MeV and a density slope of \(L = 50 \pm 20\) MeV, but these values can vary largely depending on the data or methods used, and the supra-saturation density behavior remains a big challenge.

## Method

The paper reviews and compiles 18 different constraints on the symmetry energy parameters \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\) and \(L\) from three categories: terrestrial laboratory measurements (heavy ion collisions analyzed with transport models such as IBUU04, AMD, ImQMD, and UrQMD; nucleon optical potentials from elastic scattering and charge-exchange reactions), nuclear structure properties (nuclear masses fitted with Thomas-Fermi, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, and finite-range droplet models; neutron skin thickness; isobaric analog state energies; pygmy dipole resonance strengths), and astrophysical observations (neutron star mass and radius measurements with Bayesian analysis). The author compares these constraints in the \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\)-\(L\) plane and highlights the correlations between the two parameters from different methods.

## Key numbers

*   \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31 \pm 2\) MeV and \(L = 50 \pm 20\) MeV: the central values that all 18 reviewed constraints are essentially consistent with.
*   \(L = 86 \pm 25\) MeV with \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 30.5\) MeV: constraint from isospin diffusion data using the IBUU04 transport model with Gogny momentum dependence (Che05a, Che05b).
*   \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31.3 \pm 4.5\) MeV and \(L = 52.7 \pm 22.5\) MeV: constraint from averaging world data on nucleon optical potentials (XuC10).
*   \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 32.5 \pm 0.5\) MeV and \(L = 70 \pm 15\) MeV: constraint from the FRDM-2011a nuclear mass model (Mol12).
*   \(L = 65 \pm 25\) MeV with \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31.6\) MeV: constraint from isoscaling of fragment yields in heavy ion collisions (She07).
*   \(L = 51.5\) MeV with \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 30.1\) MeV: constraint from isospin diffusion at E/A = 35 MeV (Sun10).
*   \(\gamma = 0.9 \pm 0.4\): exponent for the density dependence of the potential term of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, from elliptic-flow ratio analysis of Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon within the UrQMD model (Rus11).

## Context

This review extends the work of Bar05, LCK08, and others who summarized earlier constraints on the symmetry energy. It contrasts the relatively well-determined saturation density parameters with the still controversial supra-saturation density behavior, where FOPI data on the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio (Xia09) favor a soft symmetry energy while an opposite conclusion was obtained from the ImIQMD model (Fen10). The paper enables future work by highlighting that combining the SHF+N-Skin (2010) constraint (which shows a negative correlation between \(L\) and \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)\)) with other constraints (which show positive correlations) can significantly improve the determination of both parameters.
