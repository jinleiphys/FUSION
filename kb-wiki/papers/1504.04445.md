# Influence of statistics on the measured moments of conserved quantities in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study statistics dependence of the probability distributions and the means of measured moments of conserved quantities, respectively. The required statistics of all interested moments and their products are estimated based on a simple simulation. We also explain why the measured moments are underestimated when the statistics are insufficient.With the statistics at RHIC/BES, the second and third order moments can be reliably obtained based on the method of Centrality bin width correction (CBWC), which can not be applied for the fourth order moments at low energy. With planning statistics at RHIC/BES II, and improved CBWC method, $κσ^2$ in a finer centrality bin scale should be measurable. This will help us to understand the current observation of energy and centrality dependence of high-order moments.

## Key claim

The reliability of measured high-order moments (especially the fourth order and the product kappa*sigma^2) of conserved quantities in relativistic heavy ion collisions depends critically on having sufficient event statistics; when statistics are insufficient, the measured moments are systematically underestimated because the tail of the net-particle multiplicity distribution is not well sampled, and the Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method cannot reliably extract fourth-order moments at low RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies.

## Method

The authors performed a simple simulation where particle and anti-particle are produced independently by Poisson distributions, so the net-particle follows a Skellam distribution with parameters set to m1 = 14.5 and m2 = 0.6 (reference to proton and anti-proton means at RHIC/STAR low energies). They generated 5 million independent samples and studied how the probability distributions of the measured mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis, and the product kappa*sigma^2 change with increasing event statistics from 100 to 1 million events. The fit goodness (chi^2/ndf) of a normal distribution to each measured moment's probability distribution was used as a criterion for sufficient statistics.

## Key numbers

- For the second order moment (variance sigma), statistics of 1000 events yield a normal fit with chi^2/ndf = 4.96; with 10,000 events chi^2/ndf improves to 1.55.
- For the third order moment (skewness S), statistics of 10,000 events give chi^2/ndf = 7.97; with 1000 events chi^2/ndf = 56.9.
- For the fourth order moment (kurtosis kappa), statistics of 1 million events yield chi^2/ndf = 4.00.
- For the product kappa*sigma^2, statistics of 1 million events are required for a normal distribution fit; with 10,000 events the mean of kappa*sigma^2 saturates to within d less than 1% of its expectation (d = 0.97%).
- At RHIC/BES I, the statistics for net-proton analysis range from 3 million events (at 7.7 GeV) to 238 million events (at 200 GeV); for net-charge, from 1.4 million to 75 million events.
- At planned RHIC/BES II, proposed statistics range from 100 million events (at 7.7 GeV) to 400 million events (at 19.6 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Chen, Li, and Wu, 2014) on statistics dependence of measured moments and provides a systematic framework for determining required statistics for each moment order. It directly addresses the reliability of the CBWC method used by the STAR Collaboration (STAR, 2014) for net-proton and net-charge cumulant analysis, where the observed suppression of kappa*sigma^2 in central collisions may be partially caused by insufficient statistics rather than purely physical critical fluctuations. The results enable planning for RHIC/BES II, where with 100-400 million events and an improved CBWC method using delta-1% centrality bins, it should become possible to measure kappa*sigma^2 in finer centrality bins and clarify the centrality dependence of high-order moments.
