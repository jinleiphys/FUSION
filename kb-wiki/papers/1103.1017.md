# Sensitivity of the transverse flow towards symmetry energy

> We study the sensitivity of transverse flow towards symmetry energy in the Fermi energy region as well as at high energies. We find that transverse flow is sensitive to symmetry energy as well as its density dependence in the Fermi energy region. We also show that the transverse flow can address the symmetry energy at densities about twice the saturation density, however it shows the insensitivity towards the symmetry energy at densities $ρ/ρ_{0}$ $>$ 2. The mechanism for the sensitivity of transverse flow towards symmetry energy as well as its density dependence is also discussed.

## Key claim

Transverse collective flow in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the symmetry energy and its density dependence in the Fermi energy region (around 100 MeV/nucleon), but only up to densities of about twice saturation density; at higher densities (ρ/ρ₀ > 2) the transverse flow becomes insensitive to the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for neutron-rich systems of ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca and ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca at incident energies of 100, 400, and 800 MeV/nucleon at impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2–0.4. They employed a soft equation of state and isospin- and energy-dependent cross sections reduced by 20% (σ = 0.8 σ_nn^free), and compared results for three different density dependences of the symmetry energy: proportional to ρ, ρ^0.4, and ρ², as well as calculations without symmetry energy. The transverse in-plane flow was analyzed using both the (p_x/A) versus rapidity method and the directed transverse flow ⟨p_x^dir⟩.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is E_sym(ρ₀) = 32 MeV.
- At 100 MeV/nucleon for the reaction ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, the maximum density reached during the collision is about 2.0 times the saturation density (ρ_max/ρ₀ ≈ 2.0).
- For the symmetry energy form E_sym ∝ ρ^0.4 at 100 MeV/nucleon, the directed transverse flow ⟨p_x^dir⟩ = 0 (zero flow).
- The study used incident energies of 100, 400, and 800 MeV/nucleon for both ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca and ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca systems.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the density dependence of symmetry energy, which have used observables such as the pre-equilibrium n/p ratio (Zhang 2005, Li 2004), isospin fractionation (Baran 1998), and the π⁺/π⁻ ratio (Reisdorf 2007, Xiao 2009). The results show that transverse flow can probe symmetry energy at densities around twice saturation density, complementing low-density studies by Shetty (2004) and Famiano (2006) that extracted symmetry energy forms with γ = 0.69 and γ = 0.5, respectively. The finding that transverse flow becomes insensitive above ρ/ρ₀ > 2 highlights the need for complementary observables (such as π⁺/π⁻ ratios) to constrain the high-density behavior of symmetry energy.
