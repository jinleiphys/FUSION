# Effective field theory for proton halo nuclei

> We use halo effective field theory to analyze the universal features of proton halo nuclei bound due to a large S-wave scattering length. With a Lagrangian built from effective core and valence-proton fields, we derive a leading-order expression for the charge form factor. Within the same framework we also calculate the radiative proton capture cross section. Our general results at leading order are applied to study the excited 1/2+ state of Fluorine-17, and we give results for the charge radius and the astrophysical S-factor.

## Key claim

Halo effective field theory (EFT) can be applied for the first time to one-proton halo nuclei, but the leading-order results for the charge radius and radiative capture cross section of the excited 1/2+ state of Fluorine-17 carry an expected error of approximately 50% due to the proximity of a bound state at 495.33 keV below the halo state.

## Method

The authors constructed a Lagrangian with effective core and valence-proton fields, including Coulomb interactions through the full Coulomb Green's function expressed via its spectral representation. They derived leading-order expressions for the charge form factor and radiative proton capture cross section by calculating the irreducible three-point function and the derivative of the self-energy with respect to total energy. The coupling constant was determined by matching to the Coulomb-corrected proton-core scattering length, though an explicit expression was not required for the electromagnetic observables at leading order.

## Key numbers

* Charge radius squared for the relative motion of the proton-core system: ⟨r_C²⟩_rel = (0.59 fm)² for the excited 1/2+ state of Fluorine-17
* Proton separation energy of the excited 1/2+ state of Fluorine-17: B = 104.94 (35) keV
* Astrophysical S-factor at threshold: S(0) ≈ 7 keV·b for capture into the 1/2+ excited state of Fluorine-17
* Expected leading-order error: γ₀/√(2m_R E₀) ≈ 1/2, where E₀ = 495.33 (10) keV is the energy of the bound state below the 1/2+ state

## Context

This work extends halo EFT, previously applied to neutron halos (Canham 2008, Hammer 2011, Rupak 2011, Rupak 2012, Rotureau 2012, Hagen 2013, Acharya 2013) and uncharged systems such as αα (Higa 2008) and αp (Higa 2010), to charged proton halo nuclei for the first time. The results enable predictions for continuum observables of proton halo systems using only the one-proton separation energy as input, and provide a benchmark for ab initio calculations using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions. The approach can be extended to heavier systems like Boron-8, which requires two low-energy constants due to P-wave dominance.
