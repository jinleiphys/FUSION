# Charmonium absorption cross section by nucleon

> The $J/ψ$ absorption cross section by nucleon is studied using a gauged SU(4) hadronic Lagrangian but with empirical particle masses, which has been used previously to study the $J/ψ$ absorption cross section by pion and rho meson. Including both two-body and three-body final states, we find that with a cutoff parameter of 1 GeV at interaction vertices involving charm hadrons, the $J/ψ-N$ absorption is at most 5 mb and is consistent with that extracted from $J/ψ$ production from both photo-nuclear and proton-nucleus reactions.

## Key claim

Using a gauged SU(4) hadronic Lagrangian with empirical particle masses and a cutoff parameter of 1 GeV at vertices involving charm hadrons, the total J/ψ absorption cross section by nucleon is at most 5 mb, consistent with values extracted from photo-nuclear and proton-nucleus reactions.

## Method

The authors extended a meson-exchange model based on gauged SU(4) flavor symmetry (previously used for J/ψ absorption by pion and rho meson) to study J/ψ absorption by nucleon. They evaluated cross sections for two-body final states (J/ψ N → D̄Λc and J/ψ N → D̄*Λc via charm exchange) and three-body final states (J/ψ N → D*D̄N, D̄*DN, DD̄N, and D*D̄*N via virtual pion and rho meson exchange), including contributions from anomalous parity interactions, using coupling constants from empirical data, vector dominance model, and SU(4) relations with monopole form factors.

## Key numbers

- Total J/ψ-N absorption cross section is at most **5 mb** with a cutoff parameter of **1 GeV** at vertices involving charm hadrons.
- Cross sections for J/ψ absorption via virtual pion and rho meson cloud (three-body final states) are all less than **2 mb**.
- Cross sections for J/ψ absorption via charm exchange (two-body final states) are less than **1 mb**.
- Cross sections from anomalous parity interactions are less than **0.15 mb**.
- Empirical coupling constants used: gπNN = **13.5**, gρNN = **3.25**, κρ = **6.1**, gπDD* = **4.4**.
- From vector dominance model: gρDD = gρD*D* = **2.52**, gψDD = gψD*D* = **7.64**.
- From SU(4) relations: gDNΛc = **13.5**, gD*NΛc = **-5.6**, gψΛcΛc = **-1.4**.
- Anomalous parity coupling constant gψDD* = **8.61 GeV⁻¹**.
- Cutoff parameters at πNN and ρNN vertices: ΛπNN = **1.3 GeV**, ΛρNN = **1.4 GeV**.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model of Lin and Ko (2000) and Oh, Ko, and Lee (2001) for J/ψ absorption by pion and rho meson to the nucleon case, providing a constraint on those cross sections via comparison with empirical J/ψ-N absorption values of about 4 mb from photo-nucleus reactions and about 7 mb from proton-nucleus collisions. The results contradict the much larger cross sections (up to 20 mb) obtained by Sibirtsev (1999) due to different cutoff parameters and form factor treatments, and enable more reliable modeling of J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions by showing that the meson-exchange model with a 1 GeV cutoff gives J/ψ-π absorption cross sections of about 3 mb, consistent with comover model values.
