# Phases of SU(3) Gauge Theories with Fundamental Quarks via Dirac Spectral Density

> We propose that, in SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental quarks, confinement can be inferred from spectral density of the Dirac operator. This stems from the proposition that its possible behaviors are exhausted by three distinct types (Fig.1). The monotonic cases are standard and entail confinement with valence chiral symmetry breaking (A) or the lack of both (C,C'). The bimodal (anomalous) option (B) was frequently regarded as an artifact (lattice or other) in previous studies, but we show for the first time that it persists in the continuum limit, and conclude that it informs of a non-confining phase with broken valence chiral symmetry. This generalization rests on the following. $(α)$ We show that bimodality in $N_f$=0 theory past deconfinement temperature $T_c$ is stable with respect to removal of both infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs, indicating that anomalous phase is not an artifact. $(β)$ We demonstrate that transition to bimodality in $N_f$=0 is simultaneous with the loss of confinement: anomalous phase occurs for $T_c < T < T_{ch}$, where $T_{ch}$ is the valence chiral restoration temperature. $(γ)$ Evidence is presented for thermal anomalous phase in $N_f$=2+1 QCD at physical quark masses, whose onset too coincides with the conventional "crossover $T_c$''. We conclude that the anomalous regime $T_c < T < T_{ch}$ is very likely a feature of nature's strong interactions. $(δ)$ Our past studies of zero-temperature $N_f$=12 theories revealed that bimodality also arises via purely light-quark effects. As a result, we expect to encounter anomalous phase on generic paths to valence chiral restoration. We predict its existence also for $N_f$ massless flavors ($T=0$) in the range $N_f^c < N_f < N_f^{ch}$, where $N_f^c$ could be quite low. Conventional arguments would associate $N_f^{ch}$ with the onset of conformal window.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the infrared behavior of the Dirac spectral density can be used to infer the confinement and valence chiral symmetry breaking structure of SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental quarks, and specifically argues that a bimodal (anomalous) spectral density indicates a non-confining phase with broken valence chiral symmetry, a regime that the authors claim persists in the continuum limit and is not a lattice artifact.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of SU(3) gauge theories using the overlap Dirac operator to define valence quark dynamics, computing the spectral density of the Dirac operator at various temperatures and volumes. For N_f=0 theory, they simulated systems at T=1.12 T_c with varying lattice sizes (N=20,24,32,48) and lattice spacings (a=0.0496 to 0.0850 fm) to test stability against infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs, and also studied systems just below and above T_c (T=0.98 T_c and 1.02 T_c). For N_f=2+1 QCD at physical quark masses, they used N_t=8 lattice ensembles from the Wuppertal-Budapest group at temperatures T=150, 175, and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- N_f=0 theory at T=1.12 T_c: lattice spacing a=0.085 fm (beta=6.054, r_0=0.5 fm), with largest system linear size L=4.1 fm (N=48)
- N_f=0 theory at T=0.98 T_c: achieved by changing N_t from 7 to 8 at fixed beta=6.054, a=0.085 fm
- N_f=0 theory at T=1.02 T_c: achieved with beta=6.0783, a=0.0817 fm on 36^3 x 8 lattice
- N_f=2+1 QCD ensembles: temperatures T=150, 175, and 200 MeV, with smallest linear size L=3.9 fm at T=200 MeV
- The anomalous peak in N_f=0 theory at T=1.12 T_c has a finite width of a few MeV
- The anomalous phase in N_f=0 theory occurs for T_c < T < T_ch, where T_ch is the valence chiral restoration temperature

## Context

This work extends previous observations of bimodal Dirac spectral density in N_f=0 theories above deconfinement temperature (Edwards 1999) and in zero-temperature N_f=12 theories (Alexandru 2014), which had been suspected to be lattice artifacts (Bui 2008). The authors provide the first systematic demonstration that the bimodal behavior persists in the continuum limit, and generalize the finding to propose that anomalous (bimodal) spectral density occurs generically on paths to valence chiral symmetry restoration, including thermal effects in N_f=2+1 QCD at physical quark masses and for increasing numbers of massless flavors at zero temperature. This enables a new classification of phases in SU(3) gauge theories where deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are separate transitions, with an intermediate anomalous phase of broken valence chiral symmetry without confinement.
