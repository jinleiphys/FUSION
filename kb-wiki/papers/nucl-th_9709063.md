# Compton Scattering and the Spin Structure of the Nucleon at Low Energies

> We analyze polarized Compton scattering which provides information on the spin-structure of the nucleon. For scattering processes with photon energies up to 100 MeV the spin-structure dependence can be encoded into four independent parameters-the so-called spin-polarizabilities $γ_i, i=1...4$ of the nucleon, which we calculate within the framework of the "small scale expansion" in SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory. Specific application is made to "forward" and "backward" spin- polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the four nucleon spin-polarizabilities \(\gamma_i\) (i=1...4) to order \(\epsilon^3\) in the "small scale expansion" of SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the authors caution that an order \(\epsilon^4\) calculation is needed to determine the convergence of the perturbative series and to resolve sign discrepancies with existing multipole analyses for the forward spin-polarizability.

## Method

The authors computed all contributions to the spin-dependent Compton scattering amplitude up to \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\) in the "small scale expansion" framework, which treats both the nucleon and the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance as explicit degrees of freedom. They evaluated diagrams from \(\pi N\) continuum states, \(\Delta(1232)\) pole graphs, \(\pi\Delta\) continuum states, and \(\pi^0\gamma\gamma\) anomaly effects, using coupling constants \(g_A\), \(g_{\pi N\Delta}\), and \(b_1\) fixed from neutron beta decay and the experimental \(\Delta(1232)\) decay width within the same expansion scheme.

## Key numbers

* Forward spin-polarizability of the proton from multipole analysis: \(\gamma_0^{(p)} \approx -1.34 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (from dispersive analysis of single-pion photoproduction data, ref. SWK94)
* Forward spin-polarizability of the neutron from multipole analysis: \(\gamma_0^{(n)} \approx -0.38 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (same dispersive analysis)
* Experimental backward spin-polarizability of the proton (LEGS group): \(\delta_p \equiv -\gamma_\pi^{\rm exp} = (27.7 \pm 2.3 +2.8/-2.4) \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (from dispersion-based global fit to low-energy Compton data)
* Theoretical isoscalar forward spin-polarizability at \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\): \(\gamma_0^{(s)\,{\rm th.}} = +2.0 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (sum of \(N\pi\)-loop +4.6, \(\Delta\)-pole -2.4, \(\Delta\pi\)-loop -0.2, all in units of \(10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\))
* Theoretical isoscalar backward spin-polarizability at \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\): \(\gamma_\pi^{(s)\,{\rm th.}} = +6.8 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (sum of \(N\pi\)-loop +4.6, \(\Delta\)-pole +2.4, \(\Delta\pi\)-loop -0.2, all in units of \(10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\))
* Anomalous contribution to backward spin-polarizability for the proton: \(\gamma_\pi^{(p)\,{\rm anom.}} = -43.5 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\) (from \(\pi^0\gamma\gamma\) anomaly)
* Anomalous contribution to backward spin-polarizability for the neutron: \(\gamma_\pi^{(n)\,{\rm anom.}} = +43.5 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\)
* Theoretical backward spin-polarizability including anomaly for the proton: \(\delta_p^{\rm th.} = +36.7 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\)
* Theoretical backward spin-polarizability including anomaly for the neutron: \(\delta_n^{\rm th.} = -50.3 \times 10^{-4} \,{\rm fm}^4\)
* Coupling constants used: \(g_A = 1.26\), \(g_{\pi N\Delta}^{(1)} = 1.05 \pm 0.02\), \(b_1^{(2)} = 3.85 \pm 0.15\), with \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(M_N = 938\) MeV, \(\Delta = 294\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous \({\cal O}(\epsilon^3)\) calculation of unpolarized Compton scattering (Hemmert, Holstein, Kambor 1997) to the polarized case, and builds upon the SU(2) heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the spin-independent polarizabilities by Bernard, Kaiser, Schmidt and Meissner (1994). The results enable future comparisons with planned polarized Compton scattering experiments at several laboratories, and provide a systematic framework for extracting the individual spin-polarizabilities \(\gamma_i\) from measurements at angles other than 0 and 180 degrees, which would complement the existing multipole analysis of the forward spin-polarizability by Sandorfi, Whisnant, Khandaker and Lee (1994) and the LEGS backward spin-polarizability measurement.
