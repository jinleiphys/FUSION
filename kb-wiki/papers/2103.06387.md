# Weinberg's proposal of 1990: A very personal view

> My personal encounter with Weinberg's proposal of 1990 was a really entertaining one: My collaborator David Entem and I had embarked to show that Weinberg's idea, though smart and beautiful, was essentially useless in practice (like so many of those genius ideas of the 1980s where people claimed to have "derived the nuclear force from QCD"). However, in trying to do so, we showed the opposite; namely, we showed that Weinberg's idea worked better than allowed by any reasonable means.

# Weinberg's proposal of 1990: A very personal view

## Key claim

The authors initially set out to prove that Weinberg's 1990 proposal to use chiral effective Lagrangians for nuclear forces was practically useless, but instead found that a chiral N$^3$LO potential could be as quantitative as any high-accuracy potential of the 1990s, provided that contact terms up to fourth order (24 total parameters) were included rather than the nine contacts available at NNLO.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential in momentum space by combining two-pion exchange (2PE) contributions up to N$^3$LO (fourth order) derived by Kaiser with contact terms up to fourth order. They derived the 15 additional contact operators at fourth order beyond the nine at second order, yielding 24 total free parameters, and fitted these to NN scattering data. The 2PE contributions at N$^3$LO were added but did not substantially change the NNLO 2PE results.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO chiral potential had only nine contact parameters, which the authors found insufficient for quantitative fits, particularly in D waves.
- At N$^3$LO, 15 additional contact terms were included, leading to a total of 24 free parameters.
- The Nijmegen phase shift analysis used 35 parameters to fit np data; high-accuracy potentials typically use about 40 parameters.
- The resulting chiral N$^3$LO potential (Entem and Machleidt, 2003) achieved quantitative quality comparable to the high-accuracy potentials of the 1990s.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by van Kolck and coworkers (1994, 1996) at NNLO in configuration space, Kaiser et al. (1997) who derived 2PE contributions up to NNLO in momentum space, and Epelbaum et al. (1998, 2000) who created the first momentum-space potential with NNLO contacts. The authors explicitly contrast their approach with earlier QCD-inspired models of the 1980s (bag models, Skyrme model) and with meson-theoretic potentials such as the Paris potential (Vinh Mau, 1979; Lacombe, 1980) and the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1987). The resulting N$^3$LO chiral potential enabled reliable ab initio nuclear structure calculations.
