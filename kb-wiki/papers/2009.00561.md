# Energy Loss Versus Energy Gain of Heavy Quarks in a Hot Medium

> We study the energy loss and the energy gain of heavy quarks in a hot thermal medium. These include the study of the energy change due to the polarization and to the interaction with the thermal fluctuations of the medium. The dynamics of the heavy quarks with the medium is described by the Wong equations, that allow for the inclusion of both the backreaction on the heavy quarks due to the polarization of the medium, and of the interaction with the thermal fluctuations of the gluon field. Both the momentum as well as the temperature dependence of the energy loss and gain of charm and bottom quark are studied. We find that heavy quark energy gain dominate the energy loss at high-temperature domain achievable at the early stage of the high energy collisions. This finding supports the recently observed heavy quarks results in Glasma and will have a significant impact on heavy quark observables at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim
The authors find that heavy quark energy gain from thermal fluctuations dominates energy loss from medium polarization in the high-temperature regime (p/T less than or approximately 1), but this conclusion depends on the specific ultraviolet cutoff KMAX and the initial quark momentum; for charm quarks with p=10 GeV and KMAX=m_D, energy loss dominates over the entire temperature range studied.

## Method
The authors model heavy quarks as classical color sources obeying Wong equations, solving for the energy change per unit length from two contributions: the induced electric field from medium polarization (energy loss) and the fluctuating gluon field from thermal fluctuations (energy gain). They compute the longitudinal and transverse dielectric permittivities using semi-classical transport theory, then evaluate the energy change integrals numerically for charm and bottom quarks at various temperatures and momenta, using two values of the ultraviolet cutoff KMAX (m_D and 2m_D).

## Key numbers
- For a charm quark with initial momentum p=10 GeV at temperature T=1 GeV with KMAX=m_D: polarization energy loss -(dE/dx)_ind ≈ 0.1 GeV/fm
- For a charm quark with initial momentum p=10 GeV at temperature T=1 GeV with KMAX=2m_D: polarization energy loss -(dE/dx)_ind ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm
- For a charm quark with initial momentum p=10 GeV at temperature T=1 GeV with KMAX=m_D: fluctuation energy change -(dE/dx)_fluct ≈ -0.02 GeV/fm (energy gain)
- For a charm quark with initial momentum p=10 GeV at temperature T=1 GeV with KMAX=2m_D: fluctuation energy change -(dE/dx)_fluct ≈ -0.4 GeV/fm (energy gain)
- The crossover between energy loss and energy gain dominance occurs near p/T ≈ 1 for both charm and beauty quarks
- For charm with p=1 GeV, energy gain dominates over the full temperature range studied (up to approximately 2 GeV)
- For charm with p=5 GeV and p=10 GeV, energy loss dominates up to T ≈ 1 GeV, then energy gain dominates at higher temperatures (except p=10 GeV with KMAX=m_D)

## Context
This work extends the study of heavy quark energy loss in hot QCD media by Chakraborty (2006) and Jamal (2019) by consistently including both polarization-induced energy loss and thermal fluctuation-induced energy gain. The results support the diffusion-dominated propagation scenario for heavy quarks in the evolving Glasma proposed by Ruggieri (2018) and Liu (2019), where energy gain from field fluctuations is crucial at early times. The findings have implications for heavy quark observables at RHIC and LHC, including the nuclear suppression factor and elliptic flow, and suggest that for momenta p less than about 2 GeV diffusion dominates while for p greater than about 10 GeV energy loss is substantial.
