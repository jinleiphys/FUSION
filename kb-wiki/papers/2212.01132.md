# Hybrid stars with large strange quark cores

> The possible existence of hybrid stars is studied using several multi-quark interaction channels. The hadronic phase consists of an equation of state (EoS) with presently accepted nuclear matter properties and the quark model is constrained by the vacuum properties of several light mesons. The dependence of several NS properties on the different quark interactions is analyzed. We show that the present constraints from neutron star observations allow for the existence of hybrid stars with large strangeness content and large quark cores.

# Hybrid stars with large strange quark cores

## Key claim
The authors show that current neutron star observational constraints allow for the existence of hybrid stars containing large strange quark cores, provided the quark model includes sufficiently strong repulsive vector interactions; the existence of such cores depends on the specific choice of the vector and isovector coupling ratios in the NJL-type quark model.

## Method
The hadronic phase is described by the DDME2 relativistic mean-field model with standard saturation properties, while the quark phase uses a three-flavor NJL-type model extended with vector (G_ωω) and isovector (G_ρ) multi-quark interaction channels. A first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark matter is implemented via Maxwell construction, and the resulting hybrid equations of state are used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, which are compared to constraints from massive pulsars and the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers
- Hadronic model saturation properties: K_sat = 250 MeV, E_sym = 32.3 MeV, L_sym = 51.2 MeV, K_sym = -87 MeV
- Quark model parameters: cutoff Λ = 623.58 MeV, light quark current masses m_u,d = 5.70 MeV, strange quark current mass m_s = 136.60 MeV, G_S Λ² = 1.67, G_D Λ⁵ = 13.67, vacuum constituent quark masses M_u,d = 332.2 MeV, M_s = 510.7 MeV
- Maximum hybrid star masses reach approximately 2.2 M_⊙ for models with strong repulsive vector interactions
- For a 1.9 M_⊙ hybrid star, quark core mass fractions can range from 71% to 82% of the maximum mass, depending on the coupling parameters
- No quark core exists for χ_ωω > 21 in a 1.9 M_⊙ neutron star
- The radius of PSR J0740+6620 (2.072 M_⊙) is R = 12.39 km with uncertainties -0.98/+1.30 km, used as a constraint

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using NJL-type models (Ferreira 2020, 2021) by systematically analyzing the role of vector and isovector multi-quark interactions. It addresses the tension between the stiff equations of state required by massive pulsars such as PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) and MSP J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019, Fonseca 2021) and the softer equations of state favored by the tidal deformability upper bound from GW170817 (LIGO/Virgo 2017, 2018). The results enable future searches for observational signatures of large strange quark cores in neutron stars, particularly through combined mass-radius and tidal deformability measurements.
