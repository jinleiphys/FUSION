# Systematic study of Coulomb distortion effects in exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

> A technique to deal with Coulomb electron distortions in the analysis of (e,e'p) reactions is presented. Thereby, no approximations are made. The suggested technique relies on a partial-wave expansion of the electron wave functions and a multipole decomposition of the electron and nuclear current in momentum space. In that way, we succeed in keeping the computational times within reasonable limits. This theoretical framework is used to calculate the quasielastic (e,e'p) reduced cross sections for proton knockout from the valence shells in $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb. The final-state interaction of the ejected proton with the residual nucleus is treated within an optical potential model. The role of electron distortion on the extracted spectroscopic factors is discussed.

# Systematic study of Coulomb distortion effects in exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

## Key claim

The paper presents a technique for treating Coulomb electron distortions in (e,e'p) reactions to all orders with no approximations, while keeping computational times within reasonable limits; the authors demonstrate that an exact treatment of electron distortion is highly needed to reproduce the shape of measured cross sections and obtain reliable spectroscopic factors, particularly for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors perform a partial-wave expansion of the electron wave functions and a multipole decomposition of both the electron and nuclear current in momentum space, solving the Dirac equation for electrons in a central Coulomb potential via phase-shift analysis. The nuclear final state is treated as a linear combination of one particle-one hole excitations, with bound hole states from a Hartree-Fock calculation using a Skyrme-type effective interaction (SkE2) and continuum particle states evaluated within an optical potential model using the Schwandt parameterization. The calculated cross sections are divided by the off-shell electron-proton cross section using the cc1 prescription to obtain reduced cross sections, which are then compared with experimental data from NIKHEF and with predictions from three other model calculations.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the 3s1/2 hole transition to the ground state in 207Tl varies from 0.40 (Qui88) to 0.71 (Jin92) depending on the model used in analysis
- Occupation number for the proton 3s1/2 orbit in 208Pb calculated theoretically varies from 1.42 (Pandharipande 1984) to 1.66 (Mahaux 1991)
- The CERES method gives the 3s1/2 occupation number in 208Pb as 1.57(10)
- Multipolarities up to L_max approximately equal to 2 q R are required for convergence, where R is the radius of the target nucleus
- The effective momentum approximation replaces the plane wave with k_eff = k + (3Z alpha)/(2R) times the unit vector in the k direction

## Context

This work extends earlier approximate treatments of electron distortion by Boffi et al. (1993), who used a high-energy expansion in powers of Z alpha implemented in the DWEEPY code, and provides a full comparison with the completely relativistic calculations of Jin et al. (1992) and Udias et al. (1993). The technique enables systematic calculation of (e,e'p) reduced cross sections for proton knockout from valence shells in 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb, and allows quantitative assessment of how electron distortion affects extracted spectroscopic factors, which is critical for interpreting experimental data from NIKHEF, Saclay, Mainz, and MIT-Bates.
