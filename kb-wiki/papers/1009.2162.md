# Orbital dependent nucleonic pairing in the lightest known isotopes of tin

> By studying the 109Xe-->105Te-->101Sn superallowed alpha-decay chain, we observe low-lying states in 101Sn, the one-neutron system outside doubly magic 100Sn. We find that the spins of the ground state (J = 7=2) and first excited state (J = 5=2) in 101Sn are reversed with respect to the traditional level ordering postulated for 103Sn and the heavier tin isotopes. Through simple arguments and state-of-the-art shell model calculations we explain this unexpected switch in terms of a transition from the single-particle regime to the collective mode in which orbital-dependent pairing correlations, dominate.

## Key claim
The ground state of 101Sn has spin J=7/2 and the first excited state J=5/2, which is reversed compared to the level ordering in 103Sn and heavier tin isotopes. This inversion is explained by a transition from single-particle behavior to a collective regime where orbital-dependent pairing correlations, particularly the unusually strong pairing in the g7/2 orbital, dominate.

## Method
The 109Xe to 105Te to 101Sn superallowed alpha-decay chain was studied at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility using heavy-ion fusion evaporation reactions of 54Fe and 58Ni. Reaction products were separated by the Recoil Mass Spectrometer and implanted into a double-sided silicon strip detector, with digital electronics capturing double-pulse waveforms from sequential alpha decays. Two campaigns were performed: a low-rate high-resolution run and a high-rate run with the CARDS gamma-ray detector array for alpha-gamma coincidence measurements.

## Key numbers
- Alpha-decay energies from 105Te to 101Sn: 4711(3) keV and 4880(20) keV, with branching ratios of 89(4)% and 11(4)% respectively
- First excited state energy in 101Sn: 170(20) keV from alpha-decay energy difference, with a gamma-ray transition at 172(2) keV
- First excited state energy in 105Te: 153(11) keV from the 3910(10) keV and 4063(4) keV alpha-decays of 109Xe
- Pairing matrix elements from N3LO potential: Vpair(g7/2)=1.40 MeV, Vpair(d5/2)=0.84 MeV, with difference Delta Vpair=0.56 MeV
- Single-particle energy splitting between g7/2 and d5/2 levels: approximately 172 keV

## Context
This work extends studies of the 111Xe to 107Te to 103Sn alpha-decay chain by Seweryniak (2002) and Fahlander (2001), where the traditional 5/2+ ground state ordering was observed. The results contradict earlier tentative assignments of a 5/2+ ground state in 101Sn from beta-delayed proton emission measurements by Kavalov (2007) and gamma-ray studies by Seweryniak (2007). The findings enable new tests of shell model calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (N3LO, AV18, CD-Bonn) in the region above doubly magic 100Sn, and connect to predictions by Otsuka (2010) regarding the role of the tensor force in producing the level inversion.
