# Binding energy of the $T_{bb}$ tetraquark from lattice QCD with relativistic and nonrelativistic heavy-quark actions

> We present a new determination of the $\bar b \bar b u d$ ($J^P=1^+$, $I=0$) tetraquark binding energy using lattice QCD with domain-wall light quarks and a nonperturbatively tuned three-parameter anisotropic-clover ``relativistic'' action for the $b$ quarks. We also perform a direct comparison with a reanalysis of data generated in prior work using a lattice-NRQCD action for the $b$ quarks and otherwise identical parameters. Using the new data with relativistic $b$ quarks from seven different ensembles with multiple lattice spacings and pion masses, we perform combined chiral and continuum extrapolations and obtain $(m_{T_{bb}}-m_B-m_{B^*})_{\rm RHQ}=(-76 \pm 23)$ MeV. For the NRQCD data from five ensembles, we perform chiral-only extrapolations and obtain $(m_{T_{bb}}-m_B-m_{B^*})_{\rm NRQCD}=(-74 \pm 17 \pm 10)$ MeV. The lower magnitude of the results obtained here, compared to the original analysis in Phys. Rev. D 100, 014503 (2019), is due to the use of the symmetric parts of the correlation matrices with local four-quark operators only.

## Key claim

The $\bar b \bar b u d$ tetraquark is bound with a binding energy of $(-76 \pm 23)$ MeV when using a relativistic heavy-quark (RHQ) action, and $(-74 \pm 17 \pm 10)$ MeV when using a lattice-NRQCD action, but these values have lower magnitude than the original 2019 analysis because the present work uses only the symmetric parts of the correlation matrices with local four-quark operators, omitting the scattering operators at the sink that had previously led to lower extracted ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on seven RBC/UKQCD ensembles with domain-wall light quarks and a nonperturbatively tuned three-parameter anisotropic-clover RHQ action for the $b$ quarks, extracting the $T_{bb}$ ground-state energy from $3\times 3$ correlation matrices of local four-quark operators via the generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) with single-exponential fits and Bayesian model averaging over fit ranges. They also reanalyzed data from prior work (Leskovec 2019) using a lattice-NRQCD action on five shared ensembles with identical smearing parameters and the same GEVP methodology, extracting only the $3\times 3$ sub-blocks of the original $5\times 3$ correlation matrices. Combined chiral and continuum extrapolations were performed for the RHQ data, while chiral-only extrapolations were performed for the NRQCD data.

## Key numbers

- RHQ result: $(m_{T_{bb}}-m_B-m_{B^*})_{\rm RHQ}=(-76 \pm 23)$ MeV from combined chiral and continuum extrapolations of seven ensembles with lattice spacings $0.114$ to $0.073$ fm and pion masses $431$ to $139$ MeV
- NRQCD result: $(m_{T_{bb}}-m_B-m_{B^*})_{\rm NRQCD}=(-74 \pm 17 \pm 10)$ MeV from chiral-only extrapolations of five ensembles
- RHQ $B^*-B$ hyperfine splitting extrapolated to physical pion mass and continuum limit: $45.4(1.9)$ MeV, in agreement with the experimental value $45.18(20)$ MeV (Particle Data Group 2024)
- Individual ensemble binding energies for RHQ range from $-40(8)$ MeV (C01 ensemble, $m_\pi=431$ MeV) to $-142(28)$ MeV (C00078 ensemble, $m_\pi=139$ MeV)
- Individual ensemble binding energies for NRQCD range from $-38(11)$ MeV (F004 ensemble) to $-84(22)$ MeV (C00078 ensemble)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD study of the $T_{bb}$ tetraquark by Leskovec (2019) using the same gauge ensembles and smearing parameters but with a relativistic heavy-quark action instead of NRQCD, enabling a controlled comparison of heavy-quark discretization effects. The lower binding energy magnitude compared to Leskovec (2019) is attributed to the use of only local four-quark operators in symmetric correlation matrices, consistent with findings by Alexandrou (2024) that scattering operators are not needed for the ground-state extraction. The results enable future calculations of $T_{bb}$ properties such as electromagnetic form factors and decay widths with controlled systematic uncertainties from both heavy-quark actions.
