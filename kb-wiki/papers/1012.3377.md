# QCD Thermodynamics at Intermediate Coupling

> The weak-coupling expansion of the QCD free energy is known to order g_s^6log{g_s}, however, the resulting series is poorly convergent at phenomenologically relevant temperatures. In this proceedings, I discuss hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) which is a gauge-invariant reorganization of the perturbative expansion for gauge theories. I review a recent NNLO HTLpt calculation of QCD thermodynamic functions. I show that the NNLO HTLpt results are consistent with lattice data down to temperatures T~2T_c.

## Key claim
Hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) results at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for QCD thermodynamic functions are consistent with lattice data down to temperatures of approximately 2T_c, but only when the renormalization scale is varied by a factor of 2 around the central value of 2πT; the NNLO result still represents a significant correction to the LO and NLO results, indicating the series is not fully converged.

## Method
The author applies hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), a gauge-invariant reorganization of finite-temperature QCD where an HTL improvement term is added to and subtracted from the bare Lagrangian, dressing propagators and vertices self-consistently. The renormalized NNLO thermodynamic potential for QCD with N_f flavors and N_c colors is computed analytically, with the Debye mass m_D set to the NLO mass parameter of three-dimensional electric QCD (EQCD) and the quark mass set to zero. The strong coupling constant α_s is taken from three-loop running with Λ_MSbar = 344 MeV, and the renormalization scale μ is varied by a factor of 2 around μ = 2πT to produce uncertainty bands.

## Key numbers
- The weak-coupling expansion of the QCD free energy is known to order g_s^6 log(g_s), but the series shows no sign of convergence at phenomenologically relevant temperatures.
- The NNLO HTLpt result for the scaled pressure agrees with lattice data down to temperatures of order 2T_c ~ 340 MeV for both N_f = 3 and N_f = 4.
- The strong coupling constant at RHIC initial temperatures (T ~ 2T_c ~ 340 MeV) is approximately g_s ~ 2 or α_s = g_s^2/(4π) ~ 0.3.
- The QCD critical temperature is T_c ~ 170 MeV.
- LHC initial temperatures are expected up to 4-6 T_c.
- The Debye mass squared at NLO is given by an expression involving c_A, s_F, s_2F, α_s, T, and the renormalization scale μ.
- The renormalization scale is varied by a factor of 2 around μ = 2πT for the central lines in the pressure plots.
- The hotQCD collaboration estimates the systematic error on their pressure results to be between 5-10%.

## Context
This work extends the HTLpt program previously applied to QED (Ref. qed), pure-glue Yang-Mills theory (Ref. ym), and full QCD (Ref. qcd1), where the NNLO result for pure glue was a significant correction to LO and NLO. The approach contrasts with the standard weak-coupling expansion of the QCD free energy (known to order g_s^6 log g_s from work by Braaten and Nieto, Ref. bn) which is poorly convergent at phenomenologically relevant temperatures. The NNLO HTLpt results are compared to lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi et al., using stout action with continuum estimate from N_τ = 8 and N_τ = 10) and the hotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., using asqtad and p4 actions at N_τ = 8). The agreement down to T ~ 2T_c enables further study of whether the quark-gluon plasma at LHC temperatures (4-6 T_c) can be described by weakly interacting quasiparticles.
