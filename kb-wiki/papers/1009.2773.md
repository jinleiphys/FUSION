# sigma and f_0(980) substructures from gamma-gamma to pi-pi, J/psi, phi radiative and D_s semi-leptonic decays

> Using an improved "analytic K-matrix model", we reconsider the extraction of the sigma/f_0(600) and f_0(980) gamma-gamma widths from gamma-gamma to pi-pi scatterings data of Crystal Ball and Belle. Our main results are summarized in Tables 3 and 4. The averaged sigma "direct width" to gamma-gamma is 0.16(3) keV which confirms a previous result of [1] and which does neither favour a large four-quark (diquark-antidiquark) nor a molecule nor a pure \bar qq component. The "direct width" of the f_0(980) of 0.28(2) keV is much larger than the four-quark expectation but can be compatible with a \bar ss or gluonium component. We also found that the rescattering part of the amplitude is relatively large indicating an important contribution of the meson loops in the determination of the gamma-gamma total widths. This is mainly due to the large couplings of the sigma and f_0(980) to pi-pi and/or \bar KK, which can also be due to a light scalar gluonium with large OZI violating couplings but not necessary to a four-quark or molecule state. Our average results for the total (direct+rescattering) gamma-gamma widths: Gamma_sigma^{tot}= 3.08(82) keV, Gamma_{f_0}^{tot}= 0.16(1) keV} are comparable with the ones from dispersion relations and PDG values. Using the parameters from QCD spectral sum rules, we complete our analysis by showing that the production rates of unmixed scalar gluonia sigma_B(1) and G (1.5-1.6) agree with the data from J/ψ, phi radiative and D_s semi-leptonic decays.

## Key claim

Using an improved "analytic K-matrix model," the authors extract the direct and total two-photon widths of the sigma/f0(600) and f0(980) mesons from gamma-gamma to pi-pi scattering data, finding that the direct widths disfavor a pure four-quark, molecule, or pure quark-antiquark assignment for the sigma, while the f0(980) direct width is compatible with an s-quark-antiquark or gluonium component, but the rescattering (meson loop) contribution is large and important for the total widths.

## Method

The authors employ an analytic K-matrix model that separates direct and rescattering (meson loop) contributions to the gamma-gamma couplings of scalar mesons. They fit the model to gamma-gamma to pi-pi scattering data from the Crystal Ball and Belle collaborations, using a shape function with an Adler zero and a left-hand-cut pole to parametrize the strong interaction amplitudes. The analysis is extended by using parameters from QCD spectral sum rules to predict production rates of unmixed scalar gluonia in J/psi, phi radiative, and D_s semi-leptonic decays.

## Key numbers

*   Averaged sigma "direct width" to gamma-gamma: 0.16(3) keV.
*   "Direct width" of the f0(980) to gamma-gamma: 0.28(2) keV.
*   Average total (direct + rescattering) gamma-gamma width for sigma: Gamma_sigma^{tot} = 3.08(82) keV.
*   Average total (direct + rescattering) gamma-gamma width for f0(980): Gamma_{f0}^{tot} = 0.16(1) keV.
*   Ratio of sigma couplings to K+ K- versus pi+ pi-: |g_{sigma K+ K-}| / |g_{sigma pi+ pi-}| = 0.37(6).
*   Ratio of f0(980) couplings to K+ K- versus pi+ pi-: |g_{f K+ K-}| / |g_{f pi+ pi-}| = 2.59(1.34).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous K-matrix analyses of the sigma and f0(980) hadronic couplings (Mennessier, Narison, Ochs 2008; Kaminski, Mennessier, Narison 2009; Mennessier, Narison, Wang 2010) by including new Belle data on gamma-gamma to pi-pi. The results confirm the earlier finding that the sigma direct width disfavors a four-quark or molecule interpretation, and the f0(980) direct width is too large for a four-quark state. The analysis enables further tests of the scalar meson substructure by comparing the production rates of unmixed scalar gluonia with data from J/psi, phi radiative, and D_s semi-leptonic decays, using QCD spectral sum rule parameters.
