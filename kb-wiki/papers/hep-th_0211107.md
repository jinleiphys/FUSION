# Hadron Masses and Screening from AdS Wilson Loops

## Key claim

In strongly coupled N=4 SYM, the binding energy of a heavy and a light quark becomes independent of the coupling constant at large 't Hooft coupling, being an order-one number times the light quark mass; this allows the "Coulomb string" to snap and color charges to be screened, provided the binding energy exceeds the dynamical quark mass.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute Wilson loop expectation values in the presence of dynamical flavors, represented by probe D7 branes (for hypermultiplets) or probe D3 branes (for W-bosons) in the supergravity dual. They evaluate the Legendre-transformed Nambu-Goto action for string configurations stretching between a heavy quark brane at infinity and the flavor brane, and separately compute the flux contribution to the meson-meson potential from gauge fields on the D-brane worldvolume.

## Key numbers

- The heavy-light binding energy is given by \(E_{bind}=2m(\cos^2(\phi_1/2)+\cos^2(\phi_2/2))\), where \(m\) is the light quark mass and \(\phi_i\) are angular parameters on the \(S^5\).
- The screening length beyond which the meson configuration dominates is \(L_{sc} \sim 2\sqrt{g^2_{YM} N}/m\).
- For the D3 brane case, the binding energy is \(E=2m(\cos^2(\alpha_1/2)+\cos^2(\alpha_2/2))\), with \(\alpha_i\) the angles between heavy quark and dynamical W-boson on the \(S^5\).
- The masses of the pions (light-light mesons) are order-one numbers times the quark mass \(m\), determined by the zeros of Bessel functions \(J_1(m_n/U)=0\) or \(J_1'(m_n/U)=0\) at the IR cutoff \(U=2\pi m\).

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT prescription for including dynamical flavors via spacetime-filling D-branes (Karch and Katz 2002) to study string breaking and color screening, phenomena analogous to QCD string snapping. The authors show that even in a conformal theory without confinement, color charges can be screened when the binding energy exceeds the dynamical quark mass, in contrast to weakly coupled Coulomb systems like QED where the binding energy (13.6 eV for hydrogen) is much smaller than the dynamical quark mass (0.5 MeV for the electron). The results enable quantitative study of meson-meson interactions via pion exchange in the gravity dual, with the D7 brane case yielding exponentially suppressed interactions (massive pions) while the D3 brane case gives a residual Coulomb force from massless U(M) gauge bosons.
