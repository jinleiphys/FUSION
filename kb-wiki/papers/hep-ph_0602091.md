# J/psi, psi' and Upsilon Production at Hadron Colliders: a review

> We give an overview of the present status of knowledge of the production of J/psi, psi' and Upsilon in high-energy hadron collisions. We first present two early models, namely the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) and the Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM). The first is the natural application of pQCD to quarkonium production and has been shown to fail dramatically to describe data, the second is its phenomenological counterpart and was introduced in the spirit of the quark-hadron duality in the late seventies. Then, we expose the most recent experimental measurements of J/psi, psi' and Upsilon prompt and direct production at nonzero p_T from two high-energy hadron colliders, the Tevatron and RHIC. In a third part, we review six contemporary models describing J/psi, psi' and Upsilon production at nonzero p_T.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the status of quarkonium production models and data as of 2006, arguing that the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) fails dramatically to describe direct J/ψ and ψ' production at hadron colliders, with discrepancies reaching two orders of magnitude at high transverse momentum, while the Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM) and several contemporary models offer alternative descriptions but none fully resolves all anomalies.

## Method

The paper first presents the historical development of the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) and the Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM), then reviews experimental measurements of prompt and direct J/ψ, ψ', and Υ production at nonzero transverse momentum from the Tevatron (p\(\bar p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=1.8\) TeV for Run I and 1.96 TeV for Run II) and RHIC (pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV). Finally, it reviews six contemporary models for quarkonium production at nonzero pT, comparing their predictions to the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass: 3.097 GeV; ψ' mass: 3.686 GeV; Υ(1S) mass: 9.460 GeV (from PDG tables in the paper)
- J/ψ leptonic width Γμμ: 5.40 keV; ψ' leptonic width Γμμ: 2.12 keV; Υ(1S) leptonic width Γμμ: 1.26 keV
- Branching ratio Γ(μ+μ-)/Γ(total) for J/ψ: 0.0588 ± 0.0010; for ψ(2S): 0.0073 ± 0.0008; for Υ(1S): 0.0248 ± 0.0006
- CDF Run I integrated luminosity: 17.8 ± 0.6 pb-1 at √s = 1.8 TeV for ψ' analysis; 15.4 ± 0.6 pb-1 for J/ψ analysis
- Direct J/ψ cross section at Tevatron found to be 30 times above expectations from LO CSM plus fragmentation
- At large pT, the factor of discrepancy between data and LO CSM for J/ψ is approximately two orders of magnitude

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on the ψ' anomaly identified by CDF in the 1990s (CDF Collaboration, 1997), where prompt ψ' production was orders of magnitude above LO CSM predictions by Braaten et al. (1994). It also discusses the fragmentation approach within CSM developed by Braaten and Yuan (1993) and Cacciari and Greco (1994), which reduced but did not eliminate the discrepancy. The paper enables future work by cataloging six contemporary models that attempt to describe quarkonium production at nonzero pT, setting the stage for tests with higher-statistics data from the Tevatron Run II and the LHC.
