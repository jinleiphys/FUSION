# Quasi-equilibrium configurations of binary systems of dark matter admixed neutron stars

> Using an adapted version of the SGRID code, we construct for the first time consistent quasi-equilibrium configurations for a binary system consisting of two neutron stars in which each is admixed with dark matter. The stars are modelled as a system of two non-interacting fluids minimally coupled to gravity. For the fluid representing baryonic matter the SLy equation of state is used, whereas the second fluid, which corresponds to dark matter, is described using the equation of state of a degenerate Fermi gas. We consider two different scenarios for the distribution of the dark matter. In the first scenario the dark matter is confined to the core of the star, whereas in the second scenario the dark matter extends beyond the surface of the baryonic matter, forming a halo around the baryonic star. The presence of dark matter alters the star's reaction to the companion's tidal forces, which we investigate in terms of the coordinate deformation and mass shedding parameters. The constructed quasi-equilibrium configurations mark the first step towards consistent numerical-relativity simulations of dark matter admixed neutron star binaries.

## Key claim

The paper constructs, for the first time, consistent quasi-equilibrium configurations of binary neutron star systems where each star contains both baryonic matter and dark matter, modeled as two non-interacting fluids. The authors note that these configurations are only the first step toward full numerical-relativity simulations, and that the quasi-circular approximation (neglecting radial velocity) is used, which is justified because the change in orbital separation per orbit is much smaller than the orbital period.

## Method

The authors adapted the pseudo-spectral SGRID code to solve the extended conformal thin sandwich (XCTS) equations coupled with a continuity equation for velocity potentials, treating baryonic matter (SLy equation of state) and dark matter (degenerate Fermi gas) as two non-interacting perfect fluids. They constructed initial guesses by superimposing boosted Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff-like two-fluid solutions, then iteratively solved the seven coupled partial differential equations while adjusting constants to match target rest masses. The grid boundaries were fitted to the surface of the outer fluid component, and the stars were kept centered by driving the point of maximum total proper energy density back to the desired position.

## Key numbers

- Total rest mass of each neutron star: \(m_0^{(BM)} + m_0^{(DM)} = 1.4 M_\odot\) for all configurations
- Dark matter particle masses considered: 170, 250, 350, 500, 750, and 1000 MeV
- Dark matter rest mass fractions: 0%, 0.5%, and 5%
- For the dark halo configuration (ID 2, 170 MeV, 0.5% DM): baryonic radius \(R^{(BM)} = 6.4 M_\odot\) (9.5 km), dark matter radius \(R^{(DM)} = 11.1 M_\odot\) (16.4 km)
- For the dark core configuration (ID 11, 1000 MeV, 5% DM): baryonic radius \(R^{(BM)} = 6.1 M_\odot\) (9.0 km), dark matter radius \(R^{(DM)} = 2.7 M_\odot\) (4.0 km)
- For the single-fluid configuration (ID 1, no DM): gravitational mass \(m^{(BM)} = 1.27300 M_\odot\), radius \(R^{(BM)} = 6.4 M_\odot\) (9.5 km)
- Separation between neutron star centers for the shown dark halo configuration: 32 \(M_\odot\) (47.3 km)
- Estimated mean free path for dark matter in the most dilute configuration: \(3.7 \times 10^{-17}\) m
- Estimated scattering time for dark matter: \(1.5 \times 10^{-25}\) s

## Context

This work extends the study of dark matter effects on neutron stars from isolated stars (as in PhysRevD.102.063028, Sagun 2021) to binary systems, and provides the first consistent initial data for two-fluid neutron star binaries that satisfy the Hamiltonian and momentum constraints. Previous numerical-relativity simulations of dark matter admixed neutron star binaries by EmmSchPan22 used approximate initial data from superimposed isolated solutions rather than consistent quasi-equilibrium configurations. The constructed configurations enable future numerical-relativity simulations of dark matter admixed neutron star binary mergers, which could reveal how dark matter alters tidal deformations, orbital dynamics, and gravitational wave signals compared to standard neutron star binaries.
