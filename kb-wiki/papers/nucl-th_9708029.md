# pi- and K-meson Bethe-Salpeter Amplitudes

> Independent of assumptions about the form of the quark-quark scattering kernel, K, we derive the explicit relation between the flavour-nonsinglet pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, Gamma_H, and the dressed-quark propagator in the chiral limit. In addition to a term proportional to gamma_5, Gamma_H necessarily contains qualitatively and quantitatively important terms proportional to gamma_5 gamma.P and gamma_5 gamma.k k.P, where P is the total momentum of the bound state. The axial-vector vertex contains a bound state pole described by Gamma_H, whose residue is the leptonic decay constant for the bound state. The pseudoscalar vertex also contains such a bound state pole and, in the chiral limit, the residue of this pole is related to the vacuum quark condensate. The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity relates these pole residues; with the Gell-Mann--Oakes-Renner relation a corollary of this identity. The dominant ultraviolet asymptotic behaviour of the scalar functions in the meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is fully determined by the behaviour of the chiral limit quark mass function, and is characteristic of the QCD renormalisation group. The rainbow-ladder Ansatz for K, with a simple model for the dressed-quark-quark interaction, is used to illustrate and elucidate these general results. The model preserves the one-loop renormalisation group structure of QCD. The numerical studies also provide a means of exploring procedures for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation without a three-dimensional reduction.

## Key claim

The paper derives the explicit relation between the flavour-nonsinglet pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the dressed-quark propagator in the chiral limit, independent of assumptions about the form of the quark-quark scattering kernel. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude necessarily contains terms proportional to γ₅γ·P and γ₅γ·k k·P in addition to the γ₅ term, and these subleading Dirac components are qualitatively and quantitatively important.

## Method

The authors use the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) together with the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity (AV-WTI) to derive model-independent relations between the pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the dressed-quark propagator. They employ a renormalisable Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) model of QCD that preserves the one-loop renormalisation group structure, using the rainbow-ladder truncation with a specific Ansatz for the effective quark-quark interaction that combines an integrable infrared singularity with a perturbative ultraviolet tail. Numerical solutions are obtained for the quark DSE and meson BSE without a three-dimensional reduction, focusing on π- and K-mesons.

## Context

This work extends the general results of Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997) on preserving the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity in Bethe-Salpeter equation studies, providing a concrete numerical illustration using a renormalisable model. The paper demonstrates that the rainbow-ladder truncation, which preserves the AV-WTI, can give a qualitatively and quantitatively good description of flavour-nonsinglet pseudoscalar mesons without fine-tuning, in contrast to earlier studies by Tandy (1994) and Vary (1994) that required fine-tuning. The results enable systematic studies of meson properties within a framework that respects chiral symmetry and its breaking, and provide a foundation for extending calculations beyond the ladder truncation to address channels where the ladder approximation fails, such as scalar and flavour-singlet pseudoscalar mesons.
