# Gamow-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Method: Representation of quasiparticles with Berggren sets of wave functions

> Single-particle resonant states, also called Gamow states, as well as bound and scattering states of complex energy form a complete set, the Berggren completeness relation. It is the building block of the recently introduced Gamow Shell Model, where weakly bound and resonant nuclear wave functions are expanded with a many-body basis of Slater Determinants generated by this set of single-particle states. However, Gamow states have never been studied in the context of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, except in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schriefer (BCS) approximation, where both the upper and lower components of a quasiparticle wave function are assumed to possess the same radial dependence with that of a Gamow state associated with the Hartree-Fock potential. Hence, an extension of the notion of Gamow state has to be effected in the domain of quasiparticles. It is shown theoretically and numerically that bound, resonant and scattering quasiparticles are well defined and form a complete set, by which bound Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground states can be constructed. It is also shown that the Gamow-Hartree-Fock single-particle basis can be used to solve the Gamow-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov problem. As an illustration, the proposed method is applied to neutron-rich Nickel isotopes close to the neutron drip-line.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates theoretically and numerically that bound, resonant, and scattering quasiparticle states are well defined and form a complete set (the Berggren completeness relation) in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, enabling construction of bound HFB ground states for drip-line nuclei. This extends the Gamow state concept beyond the BCS approximation, where previously both upper and lower components of a quasiparticle wave function were assumed to share the same radial dependence as a Gamow state from the Hartree-Fock potential.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations with Skyrme energy density functionals and density-dependent contact pairing interactions under spherical symmetry, using quasiparticle Jost functions to determine S-matrix poles and scattering states of complex energy. They define boundary conditions for the upper and lower components of quasiparticle wave functions, normalize resonant states using complex scaling, and construct the Berggren completeness relation by deforming the real energy contour into the complex plane. The method is applied to neutron-rich Nickel isotopes near the neutron drip-line using direct integration of radial differential equations with a multi-dimensional Newton method.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential λ for neutrons is determined from the average particle number condition ⟨N̂⟩ = Σᵢ Nᵢ = N, where Nᵢ = ∫₀^∞ vᵢ²(r) dr
- The auxiliary BCS equation Σᵢ (1 - (ēᵢ - λ)/√((ēᵢ - λ)² + Δ̄ᵢ²)) = 2N is used to find the approximate chemical potential
- Bound quasiparticle states are normalized by ∫₀^∞ [u(kₙ,r)² + v(kₙ,r)²] dr = 1
- Scattering quasiparticle states satisfy Dirac delta normalization ⟨k|k'⟩ = δ(k - k') with the condition Cᵤ⁺ Cᵤ⁻ = k/(2π kᵤ)
- The matching radius R₀ is chosen around the nuclear surface for the Jost function calculations

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) approach of Michel et al. (2002-2004) to the HFB framework, which is necessary for medium and heavy nuclei where GSM becomes computationally prohibitive due to the large number of Berggren Slater determinants. It improves upon the PTG basis method of Michel et al. (2008) and the THO basis of Stoitsov et al. (2004), which cannot describe particle-decaying excited states. The method enables future QRPA calculations of excited states with particle-decay widths in weakly bound unstable nuclei, and provides a foundation for unbound HFB theory.
