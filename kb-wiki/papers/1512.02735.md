# Composite nature of Lambda(1405) from the spacial structure of KbarN system

> We discuss the spacial structure of the resonance state, Lambda(1405), solving the Schrodinger equation with the KbarN local potential. The potential is constructed by paying attention to the two points, the constraint from the recent experimental data and the reliability in the complex energy region. Using the new local potential, we investigate the KbarN spacial structure and obtain the result indicating that Lambda(1405) is the meson-baryon molecular state, that is, the composite state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is a meson-baryon molecular state (a composite state of $\bar{K}N$) based on its spatial structure, but this conclusion depends on the interpretation of the root-mean-square distance; the complex-valued radius from the full calculation (1.04-0.61i fm) is difficult to interpret directly, so the authors rely on the magnitude of the wave-function-based distance (1.44 fm) to support the molecular picture.

## Method

The authors construct a $\bar{K}N$ local potential by extracting a single-channel effective interaction from the chiral unitary approach via Feshbach projection, then fitting a range parameter and a correction term to reproduce the original scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane. They solve the Schrödinger equation with this potential to obtain the $\bar{K}N$ wave function and compute the root-mean-square distance of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance using two different expectation value formulas.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square distance from the energy-dependent Gamow expectation value: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_G} = 1.04-0.61i\ {\rm fm}$.
- Estimated mean distance from the weakly-binding formula $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} \sim 1/(2\sqrt{-\mu E})$: $0.85-0.58i\ {\rm fm}$.
- Root-mean-square distance from the wave-function magnitude (Eq. 4): $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 1.44\ {\rm fm}$.
- Charge radius of the proton for comparison: $\sim 0.85\ {\rm fm}$.
- Charge radius of the kaon for comparison: $\sim 0.55\ {\rm fm}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Lambda(1405)$ as a $\bar{K}N$ molecular state, previously discussed in terms of compositeness from observables by Kamiya (2015) and from lattice QCD strangeness magnetic form factors by Hall (2014). The new local potential is designed to be reliable in the complex energy plane, enabling future few-body calculations such as the $\bar{K}NN$ system, and provides spatial-structure evidence that supports the molecular picture over the elementary three-quark state picture.
