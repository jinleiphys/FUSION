# Variational calculation of the ppK^- system based on chiral SU(3) dynamics

> The ppK^- system, as a prototype for possible quasibound Kbar nuclei,is investigated using a variational approach. Several versions of energy dependent effective KbarN interactions derived from chiral SU(3) dynamics are employed as input, together with a realistic NN potential (Av18). Taking into account theoretical uncertainties in the extrapolations below the KbarN threshold, we find that the antikaonic dibaryon ppK^- is NOT deeply bound. With the driving s-wave KbarN interaction the resulting total binding energy is B(ppK^-) = 20 $\pm$ 3 MeV and the mesonic decay width involving KbarN -> πY is expected to be in the range 40 - 70 MeV. Properties of this quasibound ppK^- system (such as density distributions of nucleons and antikaon) are discussed. The Λ(1405), as an I=0 quasi-bound state of Kbar and a nucleon, appears to survive in the ppK^- cluster. Estimates are given for the influence of p-wave KbarN interactions and for the width from two-nucleon absorption (KbarNN -> YN) processes. With inclusionof these effects and dispersive corrections from absorption, the ppK^- binding energy is expected to be in the range 20 - 40 MeV, while the total decay width can reach 100 MeV but with large theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The ppK^- system is NOT deeply bound, contrary to earlier claims; with the driving s-wave KbarN interaction the total binding energy is only 20 ± 3 MeV, and even after including p-wave effects and dispersive corrections the binding energy is expected to be in the range 20-40 MeV, with large theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

A variational calculation was performed for the ppK^- system (J^π=0^-, T=1/2) using a trial wave function with Gaussian single-particle orbitals and Gaussian correlation functions for both NN and KbarN pairs. The Hamiltonian employed the Argonne v18 NN potential and energy-dependent effective KbarN interactions derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics, with the real part used variationally and the imaginary part treated perturbatively to estimate the mesonic decay width.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy with s-wave KbarN interaction: B(ppK^-) = 20 ± 3 MeV
- Mesonic decay width (KbarN → πY) range: 40 - 70 MeV
- Total decay width including all effects: can reach 100 MeV, with large theoretical uncertainties
- Binding energy range including p-wave and dispersive corrections: 20 - 40 MeV
- KbarN two-body binding energy from chiral potentials: B ~ 12 MeV (compared to 27 MeV in phenomenological models)
- Root mean distance between antikaon and nucleon in Λ(1405): ~1.9 fm (compared to 1.36 fm in phenomenological models)
- Dispersive shift from imaginary part of potential: ΔB ~ 3-7 MeV for the two-body system

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Doté, Hyodo, Weise 2008) and is complementary to Faddeev calculations with chiral SU(3) constraints (Ikeda et al.). It contradicts earlier claims of deeply bound Kbar-nuclear states from Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and subsequent variational calculations (Akaishi et al.) that used a phenomenological potential interpreting the Λ(1405) directly as a KbarN bound state with 27 MeV binding. The chiral SU(3) dynamics approach, which properly treats coupled-channel effects between KbarN and πΣ channels, shows that the Λ(1405) resonance structure appears at about 1420 MeV in the KbarN amplitude rather than 1405 MeV, implying substantially weaker attraction. This work enables more realistic estimates of Kbar-nuclear quasibound states and provides input for reaction studies of ppK^- formation (Koike et al. 2007).
