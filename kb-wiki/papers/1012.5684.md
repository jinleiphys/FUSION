# Coupled channel approach to (S,I)=(-2,0) baryon-baryon interactions from lattice QCD

> We investigate baryon-baryon interactions with strangeness $S=-2$ and isospin I=0 system from Lattice QCD. In order to solve this system, we prepare three types of baryon-baryon operators ($ΛΛ$, $NΞ$ and $ΣΣ$) for the sink and construct three source operators diagonalizing the $3\times3$ correlation matrix. Combining of the prepared sink operators with the diagonalized source operators, we obtain nine effective Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. The $3\times3$ potential matrix is calculated by solving the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation. The flavor SU(3) breaking effects of the potential matrix are also discussed by comparing with the results of the SU(3) limit calculation. Our numerical results are obtained from three sets of 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations provided by the CP-PACS/JLQCD Collaborations.

# Coupled channel approach to (S,I)=(-2,0) baryon-baryon interactions from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The authors extract a 3x3 potential matrix for the coupled (S,I)=(-2,0) baryon-baryon system (Lambda-Lambda, N-Xi, Sigma-Sigma) from lattice QCD, finding that the transition potential between the SU(3) singlet and octet states, which is forbidden in the SU(3) symmetric limit, becomes nonzero and strengthens as flavor SU(3) breaking increases. The results are obtained at unphysical pion masses (661-875 MeV) on a small spatial volume of about (2.0 fm)^3.

## Method
The authors compute Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions from 2+1 flavor lattice QCD gauge configurations using three types of baryon-baryon sink operators (Lambda-Lambda, N-Xi, Sigma-Sigma) and three diagonalized source operators obtained from a 3x3 correlation matrix. The 3x3 coupled-channel potential matrix is then extracted by solving the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in both the particle basis and the SU(3) irreducible representation basis. Calculations are performed on three ensembles with different light quark masses at a fixed lattice spacing of a = 0.1209 fm.

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD method for extracting nucleon-nucleon potentials from NBS wave functions, originally developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009), to the coupled-channel baryon-baryon system with strangeness S=-2. The approach builds on prior applications by Nemura (2008, 2009), Murano (2010), Ikeda (2010), and Inoue (2010) for other baryon-baryon channels. The results enable future studies of hyperon-hyperon interactions relevant to neutron star structure and supernova explosions, and provide a framework for systematically investigating SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in baryon-baryon interactions.
