# Strange dibaryon and KNN-pi Sigma N coupled channel equation

> KNN three body resonance has been studied by KNN-pi Sigma N coupled channel Faddeev equation. The S-matrix pole has been investigated using the analytically continued scattering amplitude on the unphysical Riemann sheet. As a result we found a three-body resonance of strange dibaryon system with the binding energy and width B=76MeV and Γ=54MeV.

# Strange dibaryon and \(\bar{K}NN\)-\(\pi\Sigma N\) coupled channel equation

## Key claim
Using a coupled-channel Faddeev equation that includes the \(\bar{K}NN\) and \(\pi\Sigma N\) Fock spaces, the authors find a three-body resonance pole of the strange dibaryon system with binding energy \(B \sim 76\) MeV and width \(\Gamma \sim 54\) MeV, but note that the \(\bar{K}N\) interaction is not well determined experimentally and further investigation of the possible range of resonance energy is necessary.

## Method
The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equation for the \(\bar{K}NN\)-\(\pi\Sigma N\) coupled system, restricting to s-wave and total quantum numbers \(J^\pi = 0^-\), isospin \(I=1/2\). The two-body \(\bar{K}N\)-\(\pi\Sigma\) potentials are derived from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian with a single cutoff parameter fitted to the \(\bar{K}N\) scattering length of Martin; the \(NN\) (\({}^1S_0\)) and \(\pi N\) interactions are also included. The three-body resonance pole is located by analytically continuing the integral equation onto the unphysical Riemann sheet (below \(\bar{K}NN\) threshold, above \(\pi\Sigma N\) threshold) and searching for the energy where an eigenvalue of the kernel equals unity.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy and width of the \(\bar{K}NN\) resonance (relativistic model): \(B = 76.1\) MeV, \(\Gamma/2 = 27.1\) MeV (i.e., \(\Gamma = 54.2\) MeV), corresponding to pole position \(W = 2m_N + m_K - 76.1 - 27.1i\) MeV.
- Binding energy and width of the \(\bar{K}NN\) resonance (non-relativistic model): \(B = 69.7\) MeV, \(\Gamma/2 = 34.2\) MeV (i.e., \(\Gamma = 68.4\) MeV), corresponding to pole position \(W = 2m_N + m_K - 69.7 - 34.2i\) MeV.
- When only \(\bar{K}N\)-\(\bar{K}N\) interaction is included (no coupling to \(\pi\Sigma\)), the bound state binding energy below \(\bar{K}NN\) threshold is increased by including \(NN\) interaction to 29.1 MeV (relativistic model) and 25.2 MeV (non-relativistic model).
- The \(\bar{K}N\) scattering lengths from the relativistic model: \(I=0\): \(-1.71 + i0.56\) fm; \(I=1\): \(0.66 + i0.64\) fm.
- The \(\bar{K}N\) scattering lengths from the non-relativistic model: \(I=0\): \(-1.78 + i0.59\) fm; \(I=1\): \(0.78 + i0.66\) fm.
- The two-body \(\bar{K}N\)-\(\pi\Sigma\) resonance (corresponding to \(\Lambda(1405)\)) pole positions: relativistic model \(1413.6 - i29.0\) MeV; non-relativistic model \(1414.3 - i26.4\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of the \(K^-pp\) (strange dibaryon) system by including the full three-body dynamics of the \(\bar{K}NN\)-\(\pi\Sigma N\) coupled channels via the Faddeev equation, in contrast to earlier approaches by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) that used a \(\bar{K}N\) \(g\)-matrix without the \(\pi\Sigma\) channel and predicted \((B,\Gamma) = (48,61)\) MeV. The results are compared with a similar coupled-channel Faddeev calculation by Shevchenko, Gal and Mares (2006), who reported \(B \sim 55-70\) MeV and \(\Gamma \sim 95-110\) MeV; the present relativistic model gives a smaller width. The work enables more precise predictions for the \(K^-pp\) resonance that can be tested against experimental signals such as those reported by the FINUDA collaboration (2005), which gave \((B,\Gamma) = (115,67)\) MeV.
