# Light nuclei with semilocal momentum-space regularized chiral interactions up to third order

> We present a systematic investigation of few-nucleon systems and light nuclei using the current LENPIC interactions comprising semilocal momentum-space regularized two- and three-nucleon forces up to third chiral order (N$^2$LO). Following our earlier study utilizing the coordinate-space regularized interactions, the two low-energy constants entering the three-body force are determined from the triton binding energy and the differential cross section minimum in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering. Predictions are made for selected observables in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and in the deuteron breakup reactions, for properties of the $A=3$ and $A=4$ nuclei, and for spectra of $p$-shell nuclei up to $A = 16$. A comprehensive error analysis is performed including an estimation of correlated truncation uncertainties for nuclear spectra. The obtained predictions are generally found to agree with experimental data within errors. Similar to the coordinate-space regularized chiral interactions at the same order, a systematic overbinding of heavier nuclei is observed, which sets in for $A \sim 10$ and increases with $A$.

## Key claim
The semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) chiral interactions up to third order (N²LO) produce predictions for few-nucleon systems and light nuclei up to A=16 that generally agree with experimental data within errors, but a systematic overbinding of heavier nuclei sets in for A ~ 10 and increases with A, similar to what was observed with coordinate-space regularized interactions at the same order.

## Method
The two low-energy constants c_D and c_E entering the three-nucleon force were determined by fitting the triton binding energy and the differential cross section minimum in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering at 70 MeV, using the SMS two-nucleon potentials of Reinert (2017) with cutoff values Λ = 450 MeV and 500 MeV. The Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations were solved in momentum space for A=3 and A=4 bound states, while the no-core shell model was employed for p-shell nuclei up to A=16, with truncation uncertainties estimated using a Bayesian model.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy reproduced exactly for Λ = 450 MeV with c_D = 2.485, c_E = -0.528; for Λ = 500 MeV with c_D = -1.626, c_E = -0.063.
- For ³H at N²LO+3NF with Λ = 450 MeV: total energy E = -8.483 MeV, point-proton radius r(p) = 1.576 fm, point-neutron radius r(n) = 1.725 fm.
- For ⁴He at N²LO+3NF with Λ = 450 MeV: total energy E = -28.62 MeV, D-state probability 12.81%, point-proton radius 1.423 fm.
- For ³He at N²LO+3NF with Λ = 450 MeV: total energy E = -7.734 MeV, point-proton radius r(p) = 1.766 fm, point-neutron radius r(n) = 1.598 fm.
- The LECs c_1 = -0.74 GeV⁻¹, c_3 = -3.61 GeV⁻¹, c_4 = 2.44 GeV⁻¹ were used, taken from Roy-Steiner equation analysis of Hoferichter (2015).

## Context
This work extends the LENPIC collaboration's earlier studies using coordinate-space regularized chiral interactions (Epelbaum 2018) by employing the SMS NN potentials of Reinert (2017) with consistent momentum-space regularization for the three-nucleon force. The results enable systematic uncertainty quantification for nuclear spectra using a correlated error Bayesian model, and provide a foundation for future calculations at higher chiral orders (N³LO and beyond) where relativistic corrections and additional three-nucleon force contributions become important.
