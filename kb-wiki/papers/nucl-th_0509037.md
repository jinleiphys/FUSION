# $Žā$ meson exchange effect on nonmesonic hypernuclear weak decay observables

> We analyze the influence of $Žā$ meson exchange on the main nonmesonic hypernuclear weak decay observables: the total rate, $╬ō_{NM}$, the neutron-to-proton branching ratio, $╬ō_{n/p}$, and the proton asymmetry parameter, $a_╬ø$. The $Žā$ meson exchange is added to the standard strangeness-changing weak $╬øN\to NN$ transition potential, which includes the exchange of the complete pseudoscalar and vector mesons octet ($ŽĆ$, $╬Ę$, $K$, $Žü$, $Žē$, $K^*$). Using a shell model formalism, the $Žā$ meson weak coupling constants are adjusted to reproduce the recent $╬ō_{NM}$ and $╬ō_{n/p}$ experimental data for $^5_╬øHe$. Numerical results for the remaining observables of $^5_╬øHe$ and all the observables of $^{12}_╬øC$ decays are presented. They clearly show that the addition of the $Žā$ meson, in spite of improving some observables values, is not enough to reproduce simultaneously all the measurements, and the puzzle posed by the experimental data remains unexplained.

## Key claim

Adding the scalar-isoscalar sigma meson to the standard octet of pseudoscalar and vector mesons in the weak Lambda N to NN transition potential improves the total nonmesonic decay rate and the neutron-to-proton branching ratio for hypernuclei, but it is not sufficient to simultaneously reproduce the positive proton asymmetry parameter measured for Helium-5, leaving the longstanding puzzle unresolved.

## Method

The authors extended their shell-model formalism (previously developed for the pseudoscalar and vector meson octet: pi, eta, K, rho, omega, K*) by adding a one-sigma-exchange potential. The sigma meson weak coupling constants (parity-conserving A_sigma and parity-violating B_sigma) were treated as adjustable parameters, fixed by requiring that the total nonmesonic rate and the neutron-to-proton branching ratio for Helium-5 match the experimental central values from Outa (2005). With these constants determined, the proton asymmetry parameter for Helium-5 and all three observables for Carbon-12 were then predicted.

## Key numbers

*   For Helium-5, the OCT+S model (sigma mass 550 MeV, A_sigma=0.67, B_sigma=13.39) yields Gamma_NM = 0.424 (in units of Gamma_0 = 2.50 x 10^-6 eV), Gamma_n/p = 0.450, and a_Lambda = -0.326.
*   For Carbon-12, the same parameter set predicts Gamma_NM = 0.776, Gamma_n/p = 0.427, and a_Lambda = -0.322.
*   The inclusion of the sigma meson reduces the total nonmesonic decay rate for Helium-5 by approximately 40% and increases the neutron-to-proton branching ratio by approximately 36% compared to the octet-only model.
*   The predicted a_Lambda for Helium-5 is approximately -0.34 across all sigma masses tested (550, 650, 750 MeV), which is negative, whereas the experimental value is positive (e.g., 0.07 +/- 0.08 from Bhang (2005)).

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations of Barbero and Mariano (2003, 2005) by adding the sigma meson, motivated by the effective field theory approach of Park (2004) which suggested the need for isospin and spin-independent central interactions. It directly contrasts with the nuclear-matter calculation of Sasaki (2005) that included the sigma meson and found better agreement for Helium-5, but the present finite-nucleus shell-model result does not reproduce the positive asymmetry. The paper concludes that additional degrees of freedom, such as Delta I=3/2 contributions as in Sasaki (2005) but within a finite nucleus scheme, or a different decay mechanism beyond the Born approximation, are needed to resolve the puzzle.
