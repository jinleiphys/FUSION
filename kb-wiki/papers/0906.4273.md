# Quark Gluon Plasma Bags as Reggeons

> Within an exactly solvable model I discuss the influence of the medium dependent finite width of quark gluon plasma (QGP) bags on their equation of state. It is shown that the large width of the QGP bags not only explains the observed deficit in the number of hadronic resonances, but also clarifies the reason why the heavy QGP bags cannot be directly observed as metastable states in a hadronic phase. I show how the model allows one to estimate the minimal value of the width of QGP bags being heavier than 2.5 GeV from a variety of the lattice QCD data and to get the minimal resonance width at zero temperature of about 600 MeV. The Regge trajectories of large and heavy QGP bags are established both in a vacuum and in a strongly interacting medium. It is shown that at high temperatures the average mass and width of the QGP bags behave in accordance with the upper bound of the Regge trajectory asymptotics (the linear asymptotics), whereas at low temperatures (below a half of the Hagedorn temperature T_H [1] they obey the lower bound of the Regge trajectory asymptotics (the square root one). Thus, for temperatures below T_H/2 the spin of the QGP bags is restricted from above, whereas for temperatures above T_H/2 these bags demonstrate the typical Regge behavior consistent with the string models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the medium-dependent finite width of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) bags, modeled with a volume-dependent width scaling as \(\Gamma(v) \propto v^{1/2}\), simultaneously explains the observed deficit of heavy hadronic resonances above 2.5 GeV and prevents heavy QGP bags from appearing as metastable states in the hadronic phase, with the caveat that this subthreshold suppression only occurs for temperatures below half the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H/2\).

## Method

The author constructs an exactly solvable finite width model (FWM) of QGP bags, extending the gas of bags model (GBM) and the quark gluon bag with surface tension model (QGBSTM). The mass-volume spectrum of bags is parameterized with a Hagedorn-like exponential term and a Gaussian attenuation around the bag mass \(Bv\), with a volume-dependent Gaussian width \(\Gamma(v) = \gamma v^{1/2}\). The isobaric partition function is analyzed via its rightmost singularities, and the pressure of infinite bags is derived for two regimes: when the most probable mass \(\langle m \rangle > 0\) (high temperatures) and when \(\langle m \rangle \le 0\) (low temperatures), the latter producing subthreshold suppression.

## Key numbers

- The minimal resonance width at zero temperature is about 600 MeV, derived from the true width \(\Gamma_R(V_0, T=0) \approx 1.22\, V_0^{1/2} \, T_c^{5/2} \alpha \approx 600\) MeV.
- The resonance width at the Hagedorn temperature is \(\Gamma_R(V_0, T=T_H) = \sqrt{12}\,\Gamma_R(V_0, T=0) \approx 2000\) MeV.
- The Hagedorn temperature is taken as \(T_H \approx 190\) MeV for numerical estimates.
- The coefficient \(a_r^2(T = T_H) \approx 22.18 \, T_H\) gives an invariant mass scale \(|S|^{1/2} \approx 4.21\) GeV where the asymptotic Regge behavior breaks down.
- The coefficient \(a_r^2(T \gg T_H) \approx 11.09 \, T_H\) gives a scale \(|S|^{1/2} \approx 2.1\) GeV for the asymptotic regime.

## Context

This work extends the gas of bags model (GBM) of Bugaev (Ref7) and the quark gluon bag with surface tension models (QGBSTM1 and QGBSTM2) by incorporating a finite width for QGP bags, resolving two long-standing conceptual problems: the deficit of observed hadronic resonances above 2.5 GeV compared to the Hagedorn spectrum, and the absence of heavy QGP bags in finite nuclear systems. The results connect to lattice QCD data and establish Regge trajectories for QGP bags that are consistent with string models and AdS/CFT, enabling future experimental searches at NICA and FAIR for resonance widening effects near \(T_H/2\).
