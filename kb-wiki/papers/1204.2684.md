# Next to leading order non Fermi liquid corrections to the neutrino emissivity and cooling of the neutron star

> In this work we derive the expressions of the neutrino mean free path(MFP) and emissivity with non Fermi liquid corrections up to next to leading order(NLO) in degenerate quark matter. The calculation has been performed both for the absorption and scattering processes. Subsequently the role of these NLO corrections on the cooling of the neutron star has been demonstrated. The cooling curve shows moderate enhancement compared to the leading order(LO) non-Fermi liquid result. Although the overall correction to the MFP and emissivity are larger compared to the free Fermi gas, the cooling behavior does not alter significantly.

## Key claim

The paper derives neutrino mean free path and emissivity in degenerate quark matter including non-Fermi liquid corrections beyond the leading logarithmic order, but finds that although the next-to-leading order corrections to the mean free path and emissivity are larger than the free Fermi gas result, the cooling behavior of the neutron star does not alter significantly compared to the leading-order non-Fermi liquid result.

## Method

The authors evaluate the quark self-energy using a hard-dense-loop resummed gluon propagator, then extract the modified quark dispersion relation near the Fermi surface. Using this dispersion relation, they compute the phase space integrals for neutrino mean free path (both absorption and scattering processes) and emissivity for degenerate and non-degenerate neutrinos, including terms up to next-to-leading order in the non-Fermi liquid expansion. The cooling curve is obtained by solving the energy balance equation using the calculated emissivity and the specific heat of degenerate quark matter from Ipp et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- Quark chemical potential assumed to be 500 MeV, electron chemical potential 15 MeV, and strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.1\)
- The Fermi liquid emissivity is \(\varepsilon_0 \simeq \frac{457}{630} G_F^2 \cos^2\theta_c \alpha_s \mu_e T^6 \mu^2\), agreeing with Iwamoto (1982)
- The leading-order non-Fermi liquid emissivity is \(\varepsilon_{LO} \simeq \frac{457}{3780} G_F^2 \cos^2\theta_c C_F \alpha_s \mu_e T^6 \frac{(g\mu)^2}{\pi^2} \ln\left(\frac{4g\mu}{\pi^2 T}\right)\), agreeing with Schafer (2004)
- The next-to-leading order emissivity contains terms proportional to \(T^{2/3}(g\mu)^{4/3}\), \(T^{4/3}(g\mu)^{2/3}\), and \(T^2 \ln(0.656 g\mu / \pi T)\) with numerical coefficients \(c_2 = 2^{2/3}/(9\sqrt{3}\pi^{5/3})\), \(c_3 = 40\times 2^{1/3}/(27\sqrt{3}\pi^{7/3})\), and \(c_4 = (6144-256\pi^2+36\pi^4-9\pi^6)/(144\pi^4)\)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order non-Fermi liquid calculations of neutrino emissivity by Schafer (2004) and mean free path by Pal et al. (2011) to next-to-leading order in degenerate quark matter, using the quark self-energy expansion beyond leading logarithmic order from Rebhan et al. (2005). The results enable more precise modeling of neutron star cooling with quark matter cores, though the modest enhancement over leading order suggests that the leading-order non-Fermi liquid treatment may be sufficient for cooling studies. The specific heat used is from Ipp et al. (2004).
