# Thermodynamic properties of a relativistic Bose gas under rigid rotation

> We study the thermodynamic properties of a rigidly rotating relativistic Bose gas. First, we derive the solution of the equation of motion corresponding to a rotating complex Klein-Gordon field and determine the free propagator of this model utilizing the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method. Using this propagator, we then obtain the thermodynamic potential of this model in the zeroth and first perturbative level. In addition, we compute the nonperturbative ring contribution to this potential. Our focus is on the dependence of these expressions on the angular velocity, which effectively acts as a chemical potential. Using this thermodynamic potential, we calculate several quantities, including the pressure, angular momentum and entropy densities, heat capacity, speed of sound, and moment of inertia of this rigidly rotating Bose gas as functions of temperature ($T$), angular velocity ($Ω$), and the coupling constant ($α$). We show that certain thermodynamic instabilities appear at high temperatures and large couplings. They are manifested as zero and negative values of the above quantities, particularly the moment of inertia and heat capacity. Zero moment of inertia leads to the phenomenon of supervorticity at certain $T$ or $α$. Supervortical temperatures (couplings) decrease with increasing coupling (temperature). We also observe superluminal sound velocities at high $T$ and for large $α$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a rigidly rotating relativistic Bose gas exhibits thermodynamic instabilities at high temperatures and large couplings, including negative moment of inertia and heat capacity, as well as superluminal sound velocities; these instabilities are conditional on the coupling constant and temperature exceeding certain thresholds, with the angular velocity effectively acting as a chemical potential.

## Method

The authors start from a complex Klein-Gordon field in a rotating metric and solve the equation of motion using cylindrical coordinates, obtaining Bessel-function solutions. They derive the free propagator using the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method and compute the thermodynamic potential at zeroth and first perturbative order, plus a nonperturbative ring contribution, using the imaginary time formalism. A high-temperature expansion is performed, and the summation over the azimuthal quantum number \(\ell\) is carried out numerically to obtain thermodynamic quantities including pressure, angular momentum density, entropy density, heat capacity, speed of sound, and moment of inertia as functions of temperature \(T\), angular velocity \(\Omega\), and coupling \(\alpha \equiv \lambda/\pi^2\).

## Key numbers

* The zeroth-order thermodynamic potential for a free massless nonrotating Bose gas is \(V_{\text{eff}}^{(0)T} = -\pi^2 T^4 / 45\) (Equation A16).
* For \(\ell=1\), the first nonvanishing \(\Omega\)-dependent correction to the free thermodynamic potential is \(V_{\text{eff}}^{(0)T} \approx -\frac{\pi^2 T^4}{45}\left(1 + \frac{15}{\pi^2}(\Omega\beta)^2\right)\) (Equation A17).
* The supervortical temperature from lattice QCD for gluodynamics is cited as \(T_s \approx 1.5 T_c\), where \(T_c\) is the confinement/deconfinement phase transition temperature.

## Context

This work extends the study of rigid rotation in relativistic systems, previously applied to gluodynamics by Chernodub (2023) where a negative moment of inertia and supervorticity were found in a spin-one bosonic system. The authors aim to determine whether similar phenomena occur in a spin-zero relativistic Bose gas, building on prior work by Zamora (2023) who used the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method for propagators in rotating Yukawa models, and by Ambrus (2023) who studied massless and massive scalar fields under rotation with a focus on imaginary rotation. The results enable further investigation of thermodynamic instabilities in rotating bosonic systems and their potential relevance to quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
