# Energy loss of heavy quarkonia in hot QCD plasmas

> We compute the energy loss of heavy quarkonia in high temperature QCD plasmas and investigate the energy loss effects on quarkonium suppression. Based on the effective vertex derived from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for quarkonium, the collisional and radiative energy loss are determined by quarkonium-gluon elastic scattering and the associated gluon-bremsstrahlung, respectively. In the energy regime $E<m_Υ^2/T$ the collisional energy loss is dominant over the radiative one, and the total energy loss increases with the plasma temperature and the initial energy of quarkonium. Our numerical analysis indicates that the medium-induced energy loss of the $Υ$(1S) results in stronger suppression at higher momentum, although the energy loss effects are found to be small compared with the previous estimates of quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions.

# Energy loss of heavy quarkonia in hot QCD plasmas

## Key claim
The collisional energy loss of heavy quarkonia is dominant over radiative energy loss in the regime where the quarkonium energy E is less than m_Υ²/T, and the total energy loss increases with plasma temperature and initial quarkonium energy; however, the medium-induced energy loss of the Υ(1S) produces only a small effect on suppression compared with previous estimates of quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors compute collisional energy loss from quarkonium-gluon elastic scattering using an effective vertex derived from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for quarkonium, and radiative energy loss from gluon-bremsstrahlung associated with that elastic scattering. The momentum diffusion coefficient is obtained from the mean-squared momentum transfer per unit time using a Coulombic bound state wavefunction. The total energy loss during a Bjorken expansion of the QGP is then used to shift the transverse momentum spectrum of the Υ(1S) measured in pp collisions to estimate the nuclear modification factor R_AA.

## Key numbers
- The momentum diffusion coefficient for quarkonium is κ = 128π g⁴ T⁵ / (1215 m²), which is suppressed by T²/m² compared with heavy quark diffusion κ_HQ ∼ g⁴ T³.
- The collisional energy loss of the Υ(1S) is less than about half of the bottom quark energy loss (Braaten and Thoma, 1991; Mustafa and Thoma, 2005; Djordjevic, 2006).
- The radiative energy loss of the Υ(1S) is approximately an order of magnitude smaller than the radiative energy loss of a bottom quark (Djordjevic, 2009).
- The Υ(1S) energy loss affects the high momentum spectra up to about 15% at q_T ∼ 30 GeV.
- The energy loss of the Υ(2S) is more than six times as large as the Υ(1S) energy loss.

## Context
This work extends the formalism developed by Hong (2019) for quarkonium dissociation through color-dipole interactions to compute energy loss, and provides the first estimate of quarkonium energy loss in hot QCD plasmas. The results are compared with heavy quark energy loss calculations by Braaten and Thoma (1991), Mustafa and Thoma (2005), and Djordjevic (2006, 2009), and with quarkonium diffusion results by Yao and Muller (2019) and Teaney and Moore (2005). The energy loss effects on R_AA are evaluated using the same initial conditions as Hong (2019) for PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, and the authors note that together with dissociation and regeneration studies by Yao and collaborators (2018-2020), the energy loss can weaken the enhancement influence at high momentum to be more consistent with CMS data.
