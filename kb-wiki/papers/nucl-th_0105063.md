# Relativistic Generalization of the Post-Prior Equivalence for Reaction of Composite Particles

> In the non-relativistic description of the reaction of composite particles, the reaction matrix is independent of the choice of post or prior forms for the interaction. We generalize this post-prior equivalence to the relativistic reaction of composite particles by using Dirac's constraint dynamics to describe the bound states and the reaction process.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the non-relativistic post-prior equivalence theorem to relativistic reactions of composite particles, showing that the reaction matrix element is independent of the choice of post or prior forms for the interaction, provided the interaction inducing the reaction is the same as that generating the composite bound states and the matrix element is evaluated using those bound-state wave functions.

## Method

The authors use Dirac's constraint dynamics to formulate relativistic two-body bound states, where generalized mass shell constraints for each particle lead to a total Hamiltonian that separates center-of-mass and relative motion. They then extend this to a four-constituent system (reaction A(12)+B(34)→C(14)+D(32)), partitioning the total Hamiltonian into unperturbed and residual interaction parts in two different ways (post and prior forms) and demonstrating their equality algebraically using the bound-state equations.

## Key numbers

- The bound state mass \(M\) is given by \(M=\sqrt{2\mu E+m_{1}^{2}}+\sqrt{2\mu E+m_{2}^{2}}\), where \(\mu=m_1m_2/(m_1+m_2)\) and \(E\) is the eigenvalue of the Schrödinger-like relative-motion equation.
- The relative-motion equation is cast as \(\left\{ {\frac{{\bf q}^{2}}{2\mu }}+{\frac{\Phi ({\bf r})}{2\mu }} \right \} |\psi\rangle= E |\psi\rangle\), matching the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation term by term.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic post-prior equivalence theorem (Sch68) to relativistic energies, using Dirac's constraint dynamics as an alternative to the Bethe-Salpeter equation. It builds on prior work by Barnes and Swanson (1992) on non-relativistic constituent-interchange processes and enables unique perturbative calculations of rearrangement reactions at relativistic energies in nuclear and particle physics, such as those studied in Refs. [2,3] cited in the paper.
