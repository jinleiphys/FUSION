# New Enhanced Tunneling in Nuclear Processes

> The small sub-barrier tunneling probability of nuclear processes can be dramatically enhanced by collision with incident charged particles. Semiclassical methods of theory of complex trajectories have been applied to nuclear tunneling, and conditions for the effects have been obtained. We demonstrate the enhancement of alpha particle decay by incident proton with energy of about 0.25 MeV. We show that the general features of this process are common for other sub-barrier nuclear processes and can be applied to nuclear fission.

## Key claim

The sub-barrier tunneling probability in nuclear processes, such as alpha decay, can be dramatically enhanced by collision with incident charged particles (protons) through a mechanism called Euclidean resonance, where the tunneling probability becomes not exponentially small even for a barrier that is normally hardly transparent. This enhancement occurs specifically when the incident proton energy is about 0.25 MeV and the energy transfer between the proton and the alpha particle is negative (the alpha particle loses energy to the proton), contrary to the expectation that lower tunneling energy would reduce the probability.

## Method

The authors applied semiclassical methods based on the theory of complex trajectories (trajectories in imaginary time) to describe the cooperative tunneling motion of an alpha particle and an incident proton. They derived a joint action for the alpha-proton system, accounting for the energy exchange between the two particles during the under-barrier motion, and found the condition for optimum energy transfer that minimizes the total action. The method was applied to the specific reaction of uranium-235 alpha decay induced by a proton beam, with the classical Newtonian equations for the two particles solved in imaginary time to determine the energy transfer and the resulting tunneling probability.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 235U + p → 231Th + α + p, the alpha particle energy is E = 4.678 MeV.
- The energy transfer for the case of parallel motion (angle φ = 0°) is |ΔE| ≃ 1.89 MeV, which leads to a non-physical (negative) value of the incident proton energy.
- The proton energy at which the energy transfer ΔE = 0 is ε₀ = 1.85 MeV (with angle φ ≃ 30°), where the tunneling probability matches conventional alpha decay: W ∼ exp[-Aα(4.678 MeV)] ≃ e⁻⁸⁰·⁷⁵ ≃ 10⁻³⁵.
- The Euclidean resonance occurs at proton energy εR = 0.25 MeV, with energy transfer ΔE = -1.15 MeV and angle between particles φ ≃ 11°.
- At the resonance, the emitted alpha particle energy becomes E - |ΔE| = 3.53 MeV, and the outgoing proton energy becomes ε + |ΔE| = 1.40 MeV.
- The exit point of the alpha particle at resonance is 7.3 R₀, and of the outgoing proton is 10 R₀; the incident proton is stopped at about 54 R₀.
- The width of the resonance peak is Δε ≃ 10⁻⁴ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of tunneling enhancement in nonstationary fields, previously discovered in condensed matter physics by Ivlev and co-workers (Ivlev 2000, 2001), to nuclear processes. It applies the same semiclassical formalism of complex trajectories used for photon-assisted tunneling to the case of a pulsed electromagnetic field created by incident protons. The paper enables experimental searches for sharp resonances in proton-induced alpha decay and suggests the mechanism could be applied to nuclear fission, while noting that applicability to fusion reactions requires further study.
