# Regularization issues for a cold and dense quark matter model in $β-$equilibrium

> A regularization scheme that explicitly separates vacuum contributions from medium effects is applied to a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with diquark interactions and in $β-$equilibrium. We perform a comparison of this proposed scheme with the more traditional one, where no separation of vacuum and medium effects is done. Our results point to both qualitative and quantitative important differences between these two methods, in special regarding the phase structure of the model in the cold and dense nuclear matter case.

# Regularization issues for a cold and dense quark matter model in β-equilibrium

## Key claim

The choice of regularization scheme in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with diquark interactions qualitatively changes the predicted phase structure of cold, dense quark matter under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. Specifically, the medium separation scheme (MSS), which properly separates vacuum contributions from medium effects, predicts a transition from a gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase to a conventional 2SC phase at higher chemical potentials, whereas the traditional regularization scheme (TRS) predicts the system remains in the g2SC phase throughout.

## Method

The authors study a two-flavor NJL model with scalar, pseudoscalar, and diquark interaction channels in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature, working in the chiral limit. They impose β-equilibrium and both electric and color charge neutrality conditions, solving the gap equation for the diquark condensate Δ alongside the neutrality conditions for the electron chemical potential μₑ and the color chemical potential μ₈. The regularization of ultraviolet-divergent momentum integrals is performed using two distinct procedures: the traditional regularization scheme (TRS), where a sharp cutoff Λ is applied to all momentum integrals including medium-dependent terms, and the medium separation scheme (MSS), where only vacuum-dependent terms are regularized while medium contributions are treated as finite integrals.

## Key numbers

- The diquark condensate Δ in the TRS case drops to zero for chemical potentials μ ≳ 0.53 GeV, while in the MSS case Δ continues to grow with μ.
- The cutoff scale is Λ = 653.3 MeV, the scalar coupling constant is Gₛ = 5.0163 GeV⁻², and the diquark coupling is set to G_d = 0.75 Gₛ (ratio η = 0.75).
- The vacuum values used for parameter fitting are the pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and the chiral condensate ⟨ψ̅_f ψ_f⟩^{1/3} = -250 MeV.
- The saturation density is ρ₀ ∼ 0.15 fm⁻³, with compact star cores expected to reach densities of order 10 ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of regularization scheme dependence previously noted by Farias (2006) in the context of color superconductivity in NJL models, and by Menezes (2008), Allen (2015), Duarte (2015), and Coppola (2017) for magnetized quark matter. The results contradict the behavior predicted by the traditional regularization scheme, where the diquark condensate eventually vanishes at high chemical potential, a result at odds with lattice QCD simulations for N_c = 2 by Kogut (2001) and Braguta (2016), and with chiral perturbation theory studies by Kogut (2000), all of which show an increasing diquark condensate with chemical potential. The MSS procedure enables a physically more consistent description of color superconducting phases relevant for compact star interiors, where densities of order 10 ρ₀ are expected.
