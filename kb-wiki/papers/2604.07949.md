# Wave-Function Femtometry: Hypertriton - The Ultimate Halo Nucleus

> The interaction between nucleons and hyperons - baryons containing a strange quark - is key to understanding the properties of dense nuclear matter, such as that expected in the interior of neutron stars. Direct scattering experiments are hindered by the short lifetime of hyperons, prompting the study of hypernuclei - bound states of nucleons and hyperons - as an alternative approach. The lightest known hypernucleus, the hypertriton ($^3_Λ$H), is a weakly bound state composed of a proton, a neutron and a $Λ$ hyperon, and is believed to exhibit a halo-like structure with the $Λ$ being loosely bound to a deuteron core. Based on the first measurement of hypertriton production in proton-proton collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC), its halo structure is confirmed. A successful description of the hypertriton production yield within the nuclear coalescence framework enables an estimation of the $Λ$ separation from the deuteron core as $9.54^{+2.67}_{-1.11}$ fm.

## Key claim

The hypertriton (³_ΛH) is confirmed to have a halo structure, with the Λ hyperon loosely bound to a deuteron core, based on the first measurement of its production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. The claim is conditional on the successful description of the production yield within the nuclear coalescence framework, which enables an estimation of the Λ separation distance.

## Method

The ALICE Collaboration measured hypertriton production in proton-proton collisions at the CERN LHC. The production yield was analyzed within the nuclear coalescence framework, which models the formation of composite particles from final-state nucleons and hyperons produced in the collision. By fitting the coalescence model to the measured yield, the spatial separation between the Λ hyperon and the deuteron core was extracted.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton Λ separation from the deuteron core: 9.54^{+2.67}_{-1.11} fm, extracted from the coalescence model fit to the production yield in proton-proton collisions at the LHC.

## Context

This work provides the first experimental confirmation of the halo structure of the hypertriton, which had been predicted theoretically but never directly measured. The result extends previous studies of hypernuclei and halo nuclei, and enables future investigations of the nucleon-hyperon interaction in a regime relevant for understanding dense nuclear matter in neutron stars. The use of the coalescence framework follows the approach applied to other light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions, but here it is applied to the smallest hypernucleus in proton-proton collisions for the first time.
