# The Off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman Relation and Its Discrepancy

> We study the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation (ODGTR) and its discrepancy (ODGTD) in the N, Delta, pi sector through O(p^2) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. To this order, the ODGTD and axial vector N to Delta transition radius are determined solely by low energy constants. Loop corrections appear at O(p^4). For low-energy constants of natural size, the ODGTD would represent a ~ 2% correction to the ODGTR. We discuss the implications of the ODGTR and ODGTD for lattice and quark model calculations of the transition form factors and for parity-violating electroexcitation of the Delta.

# The Off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman Relation and Its Discrepancy

## Key claim

The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation (ODGTR) for the N, Delta, pi sector holds to within a few percent accuracy as a consequence of chiral symmetry, with the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy (ODGTD) representing a ~2% correction when low-energy constants are of natural size. However, the current experimental uncertainty in the axial transition form factor C5^A(0) is an order of magnitude larger than this theoretical expectation, preventing a precise test.

## Method

The authors analyze the ODGTR and ODGTD through O(p^2) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) with the small scale expansion, treating the Delta-nucleon mass difference δ as O(ε) in chiral power counting. They compute the matrix elements of the axial current between Delta+ and proton states, relate them to the strong πNΔ form factor via the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis, and extract the ODGTD from the q^2-dependence of the relevant form factors. Loop corrections are shown to appear only at O(p^4), so the ODGTD and axial vector N to Delta transition radius r_A^2 are determined solely by low-energy counterterms at this order.

## Key numbers

- The ODGTD scale is (m_π/Λ_χ)^2 ~ 0.01, where Λ_χ = 4πF_π ~ 1 GeV and F_π = 92.4 MeV
- The strong πNΔ coupling constant from πN scattering analysis: g_{πNΔ} = 0.98 ± 0.05
- The combined low-energy constant from πN scattering: (b̃_3 + b̃_8) = 0.59 ± 0.10
- The strong πNΔ form factor from πN scattering: G_{πNΔ}(m_π^2) = 11.6 ± 1.3
- The leading-order ODGTR prediction for C_5^A(0): C_5^A(0)_{l.o.} = 0.93 ± 0.10
- Experimental C_5^A(0) from neutrino scattering: C_5^A(0) = (1/√3)(2.0 ± 0.4)
- Experimental ODGTD from πN scattering analysis: Δ_π^{exp} = -0.24 ± 0.3
- Experimental ODGTD from older K-matrix analysis: Δ_π^{exp} = 0.01 ± 0.2
- Alternative G_{πNΔ}(m_π^2) from K-matrix analysis: 14.3 ± 0.2
- Axial vector transition radius from neutrino data: r_A^2/6 = (1.82 to 2.14) GeV^{-2}
- Corresponding low-energy constant c_2 = -(3.1 to 3.5)
- Quark model prediction: C_5^A(0)_{Q.M.} = 0.87

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the diagonal Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy (GTD) by Goity et al. (2002) within HBχPT to the off-diagonal N-Δ sector, following the same theoretical framework. The ODGTR provides a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of axial vector N→Δ transition form factors, which remain to be computed, and for parity-violating electroexcitation of the Δ at Jefferson Lab by the G0 Collaboration. The results enable future determinations of electroweak radiative corrections R_A^Δ from combined measurements of PV electroexcitation and the ODGTR prediction, which has taken on added interest following the SAMPLE experiment results suggesting larger-than-expected corrections in elastic scattering.
