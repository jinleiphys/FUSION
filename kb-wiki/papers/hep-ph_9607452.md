# Dispersion Analysis of the Strange Vector Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We analyze the nucleon matrix element of the strange quark vector current in a nucleon--model independent dispersive approach with input from the current world data set for the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors. The update of Jaffe's minimal 3-pole ansatz for the spectral functions yields a 40% larger (Sachs) strangeness radius, $(r_s^2)_{Sachs} = 0.20 {\rm fm}^2$, and a by 20% reduced magnitude of the strangeness magnetic moment, $μ_s = -0.26$. In the pole approximation these values are shown to be upper bounds. After extending the ansatz in order to implement the asymptotic QCD momentum dependence (which the 3-pole form factors cannot reproduce), we find the magnitude of the 3-pole results reduced by up to a factor of 2.5. The signs of the leading moments originate primarily from the large $ϕ$-meson couplings and are generic in the pole approximation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the minimal 3-pole dispersive analysis of the strange vector form factors, updated with current electromagnetic form factor data, yields values for the strangeness radius and magnetic moment that are likely upper bounds; when the ansatz is extended to match the asymptotic QCD momentum dependence, the magnitude of these 3-pole results is reduced by up to a factor of 2.5.

## Method

The authors use a nucleon-model independent dispersive approach, constructing N-pole approximations for the spectral functions of the strange Dirac and Pauli form factors. The pole masses and couplings for the omega and phi mesons are extracted from fits to the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors (specifically the MMD fit), exploiting the shared quantum numbers of the isoscalar and strange currents. The remaining pole couplings are fixed by imposing superconvergence relations that enforce the required asymptotic behavior, and the ansatz is extended from three to four poles to implement the faster asymptotic decay predicted by QCD counting rules.

## Key numbers

- Updated 3-pole Sachs strangeness radius: \((r_s^2)_{\text{Sachs}} = 0.20 \, \text{fm}^2\), a 40% increase over Jaffe's earlier estimate.
- Updated 3-pole strangeness magnetic moment: \(\mu_s = -0.26\), a 20% reduction in magnitude from Jaffe's earlier value.
- Third pole mass in the MMD fit: \(m_3 = 1600 \, \text{MeV}\), identified with the \(\omega(1600)\) resonance.
- Dipole mass parameters from the 3-pole fit: \(M_1 = 1.31 \, \text{GeV}\) and \(M_2 = 1.26 \, \text{GeV}\).
- Ratio of MMD to average Hohler phi couplings: \((A_1^{(\phi)})_{\text{MMD}} / (\bar{A}_1^{(\phi)})_H = 1.28\) and \((A_2^{(\phi)})_{\text{MMD}} / (\bar{A}_2^{(\phi)})_H = 0.84\).

## Context

This work extends Jaffe's (1989) minimal 3-pole dispersive analysis of the strange vector form factors by updating the input with the current world data set for the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors, specifically using the MMD fit (Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel, 1995). The paper goes beyond Jaffe's analysis by implementing the asymptotic QCD momentum dependence predicted by counting rules (Brodsky and Lepage, 1980), which the 3-pole ansatz cannot reproduce, and by considering both extrinsic and intrinsic asymptotic behaviors. The results provide theoretical guidance for upcoming parity-violating lepton scattering experiments at CEBAF, MAMI, and Bates that aim to measure the strange form factors for the first time.
