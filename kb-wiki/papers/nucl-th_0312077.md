# Counting pairs in $^{44}$Ti with various interactions

> We count the number of pairs in the single $j-$shell model of $^{44}$Ti for various interactions. For a state of total angular momentum $I$, the wave function can be written as $Ψ=\sum_{J_P J_N} D(J_P J_N) [(j^2)_{J_P}(j^2)_{J_N}]_I$, where $D(J_P J_N)$ is the probability amplitude that the protons couple to $J_P$ and the neutrons to $J_N$. For I=0 there are three states with ($I=0, T=0$) and one with ($I=0, T=2$). The latter is the double analog of $^{44}$Ca. In that case (T=2), the magnitude of $D(JJ)$ is the same as that of a corresponding two particle fractional parentage coefficient. In counting the number of pairs with an even angular momentum $J_A$ we find a new relationship obtained by diagonalizing a unitary nine-j symbol. We are also able to get results for the 'no-interaction' case for T=0 states, for which it is found that there are less ($J=1, T=0$) pairs than on the average. Relative to this 'no-interaction case' we find for the most realistic interaction used that there is an enhancement of pairs with angular momentum $J_A=0,2,1$ and 7, and a depletion for the others. Also considered are interactions in which only the ($J=0, T=1$) pair state is at lower energy, only the ($J=1, T=0$) pair state is lowered and where both are equally lowered.

# Counting pairs in $^{44}$Ti with various interactions

## Key claim

The number of pairs with a given angular momentum in the ground state of $^{44}$Ti depends on the interaction, but for even angular momenta a new relationship simplifies the counting: the number of even-$J$ pairs equals $3|D(J,J)|^2$ for $T=0$ states, independent of which isospin-conserving interaction is used. This relationship holds because the unitary nine-j symbol matrix has eigenvalues $-1$ (for the $T=2$ state) and $0.5$ (triply degenerate for the three $T=0$ states).

## Method

The authors performed a single-$j$-shell calculation for $^{44}$Ti with two valence protons and two valence neutrons in the $f_{7/2}$ shell, writing the $I=0$ wave function as a sum over proton-neutron pair couplings $D(JJ)$. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian for five different interactions: pure $(J=0,T=1)$ pairing, pure $(J=1,T=0)$ pairing, equal $J=0$ and $J=1$ pairing, the MBZ interaction of McCullen, Bayman and Zamick (1964), and the experimental spectrum of $^{42}$Sc. They derived expressions for the number of pairs of angular momentum $J_{12}$ using unitary nine-j symbols and applied a new eigenvalue relation for the even-$J$ case.

## Key numbers

- For the $(I=0,T=2)$ state (double analog of $^{44}$Ca): number of pairs is 1.5 for $J_{12}=0$, 0.8333 for $J_{12}=2$, 1.5 for $J_{12}=4$, and 2.16667 for $J_{12}=6$.
- For the "no-interaction" case (average over three $T=0$ states): number of $J_{12}=1$ pairs is 0.432, compared to the equal-distribution value of 0.75.
- For the realistic interaction (spectrum of $^{42}$Sc): number of $J_{12}=0$ pairs increases from 0.75 to 1.8617; $J_{12}=1$ pairs from 0.432 to 0.6752; $J_{12}=7$ pairs from 0.667 to 1.8945; $J_{12}=6$ pairs decreases from 0.639 to 0.0457; $J_{12}=5$ pairs decreases from 1.00 to 0.1587.
- For pure $(J=0,T=1)$ pairing: $J_{12}=0$ pairs = 2.25, $J_{12}=1$ pairs = 0.250, $J_{12}=7$ pairs = 1.250.
- For pure $(J=1,T=0)$ pairing: $J_{12}=1$ pairs = 1.297, $J_{12}=0$ pairs = 0.433, $J_{12}=7$ pairs = 1.311.
- For equal $J=0$ and $J=1$ pairing: $J_{12}=0$ pairs = 2.043, $J_{12}=1$ pairs = 0.618, $J_{12}=7$ pairs = 1.654.
- The $^{42}$Sc experimental excitation energies used (in MeV): 0.0 ($J=0$), 0.6111 ($J=1$), 1.5863 ($J=2$), 1.4904 ($J=3$), 2.8153 ($J=4$), 1.5101 ($J=5$), 3.2420 ($J=6$), 0.6163 ($J=7$), with a constant pairing energy of -3.182 MeV added.

## Context

This work extends a previous note by Moya de Guerra et al. (2002) on charge operator sum rules and proton-neutron pairing, providing a detailed counting of pairs in $^{44}$Ti. The authors compare their results to the earlier MBZ calculation by McCullen, Bayman and Zamick (1964) and find qualitative agreement. The new relationship for even-$J$ pairs using the unitary nine-j symbol eigenvalues simplifies calculations and is independent of the interaction. The results are relevant to two-nucleon transfer experiments, though $^{44}$Ti is not stable; the authors plan to address other cases in future work.
