# Bound state formation in the $DDK$ system

> We study the $DDK$ system in a coupled channel approach, by including $DD_sÎ·$ and $DD_sĎ€$, and find that the dynamics involved in the system forms a bound state with isospin $1/2$ and mass $4140$ MeV when one of the $DK$ pair is resonating in isospin 0, forming the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$. The state can be interpreted as a $DD^*_{s0}(2317)$ molecule like state with exotic quantum numbers: doubly charged, doubly charmed, and with single strangeness.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $DDK$ system forms a bound state with mass 4140 MeV and isospin $1/2$, but only when one of the $DK$ pairs resonates in isospin 0 to form the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ state; the bound state disappears if the interaction strength generating the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ is reduced by about 10%.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the coupled three-body channels $DDK$, $DD_s\pi$, and $DD_s\eta$, using two-body $t$-matrices obtained from effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy quark symmetry, unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The two-body interactions for the $DK$ system were taken from the model of Guo (2006) and Guo (2017), while the $DD$ and $DD_s$ interactions were described through vector meson exchange using hidden local symmetry. The three-body scattering matrix was studied as a function of total energy and the invariant mass of a $DK$ subsystem to identify bound states.

## Key numbers

- Bound state mass: 4140 MeV, approximately 90 MeV below the $DDK$ three-body threshold.
- The $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ state generated in the $DK$ subsystem appears at 2318 MeV in the isospin 0 configuration.
- Mean square distance of the bound state: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} \sim 1.0$ to $1.4$ fm, which is 1.4 to 2 times larger than the mean square distance of the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ itself (approximately 0.7 fm).
- The bound state has spin-parity $J^P = 0^-$, total isospin $1/2$, charm $+2$, and strangeness $+1$.

## Context

This work extends the study of Sanchez-Sanchez (2017), who treated the $D-D^*_{s0}(2317)$ system as an effective two-body problem and predicted a bound state with binding energy of 15-50 MeV using a kaon exchange potential. The present paper confirms that result by explicitly solving the three-body Faddeev equations, finding a bound state with similar properties. The state can be interpreted as a $DD^*_{s0}(2317)$ molecule with exotic quantum numbers (doubly charged, doubly charmed, single strangeness), and its experimental search is motivated for facilities studying doubly charmed hadrons.
