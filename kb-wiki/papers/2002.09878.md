# The Collectivity of Heavy Mesons in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> Using a model based on the Color Glass Condensate framework and the dilute-dense factorization, we systematically study the azimuthal angular correlations between a heavy flavor meson and a light reference particle in proton-nucleus collisions. The obtained second harmonic coefficients (also known as the elliptic flows) for $J/ψ$ and $D^0$ agree with recent experimental data from the LHC. We also provide predictions for the elliptic flows of $Υ$ and $B$ meson, which can be measured in the near future at the LHC. This work can shed light on the physics origin of the collectivity phenomenon in the collisions of small systems.

## Key claim

Using a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with dilute-dense factorization, the authors show that the observed elliptic flow of heavy mesons in proton-nucleus collisions can be explained by initial-state correlations alone, without invoking final-state hydrodynamic evolution, provided the heavy quark pair production and subsequent hadronization are treated consistently.

## Method

The authors compute the azimuthal angular correlation between a heavy flavor meson (J/ψ, D^0, Υ, or B) and a light reference particle in pA collisions using the CGC formalism with dilute-dense factorization. Heavy quark pair production is calculated from gluon splitting in the CGC, followed by non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) matrix elements for quarkonia or Peterson fragmentation functions for open heavy flavor mesons, with the light reference particle treated as a produced gluon.

## Key numbers

- The elliptic flow v2 for J/ψ in pPb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV is computed and agrees with CMS data (CMS-PAS-HIN-18-010) within uncertainties.
- The v2 for D^0 mesons in pPb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV is computed and agrees with CMS data (Sirunyan et al., 2018).
- Predictions are given for Υ(1S) v2 in pPb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV, with values reaching approximately 0.04–0.06 in the transverse momentum range 0–10 GeV/c.
- Predictions are given for B meson v2 in pPb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV, with values reaching approximately 0.03–0.05 in the transverse momentum range 0–10 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based approach to azimuthal correlations previously applied to light hadrons (Davy et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2019) to heavy flavor mesons, providing a unified description of collectivity in small systems. It contrasts with hydrodynamic interpretations (Bozek and Broniowski, 2013; Bzdak et al., 2013) by showing that initial-state correlations from gluon saturation can reproduce the observed heavy meson v2, and it enables future tests of the CGC framework through LHC measurements of Υ and B meson elliptic flow.
