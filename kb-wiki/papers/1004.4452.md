# Third-order dissipative hydrodynamics from the entropy principle

> We review the entropy based derivation of third-order hydrodynamic equations and compare their solutions in one-dimensional boost-invariant geometry with calculations by the partonic cascade BAMPS. We demonstrate that Grad's approximation, which underlies the derivation of both Israel-Stewart and third-order equations, describes the transverse spectra from BAMPS with high accuracy. At the same time solutions of third-order equations are much closer to BAMPS results than solutions of Israel-Stewart equations. Introducing a resummation scheme for all higher-oder corrections to one-dimensional hydrodynamic equation we demonstrate the importance of higher-order terms if the Knudsen number is large.

# Third-order dissipative hydrodynamics from the entropy principle

## Key claim
Extending the entropy current expansion to third order in dissipative fluxes yields hydrodynamic equations whose solutions for pressure isotropy agree much better with the partonic cascade BAMPS than Israel-Stewart (second-order) equations, but only when the Knudsen number is not too large; at early times where the expansion scalar is large, even third-order equations deviate and an approximate resummation of all higher-order terms is needed to match BAMPS.

## Method
The authors derived a third-order evolution equation for the shear stress tensor by expanding the entropy current in kinetic theory up to third order in the deviation from equilibrium, imposing the second law of thermodynamics, and using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the off-equilibrium distribution function. They solved the resulting one-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic equations for a massless Boltzmann gas of gluons and compared the pressure isotropy to solutions of the Boltzmann equation obtained from the partonic cascade BAMPS with elastic isotropic cross sections adjusted to give constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio values used: η/s = 0.4 and η/s = 3.
- Initial temperature T₀ = 500 MeV at initial proper time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c.
- Deviations of Grad's approximation from the actual BAMPS distribution do not exceed 10% for η/s = 0.4 below p_T ∼ 3 GeV.
- The resummation coefficient for all higher-order terms was determined to be χ = -5/3 from the free-streaming limit (τ_π → ∞).
- The third-order equation corresponds to χ = -3 in the resummation ansatz; Israel-Stewart corresponds to χ = 0.

## Context
This work extends the entropy-based derivation of Israel-Stewart (Israel and Stewart, 1976) to third order in dissipative fluxes, following the same approach as Muronga (2004) and the authors' own earlier work (El et al., 2009, 2010). It directly compares with BAMPS calculations that have been used to study elliptic flow (Xu and Greiner, 2009), second-order viscosity (El et al., 2009), and shock waves (Bouras et al., 2009). The results enable future work to understand differences between various formulations of second-order dissipative hydrodynamics, such as those by Romatschke (2009, 2010) and Denicol et al. (2010), and to investigate whether extending Grad's approximation can further improve agreement between hydrodynamics and kinetic transport.
