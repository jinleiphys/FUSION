# Probing QCD critical fluctuations from intermittency analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> It is shown that intermittency, a self-similar correlation with respect to the size of the phase space volume, is sensitive to critical density fluctuations of baryon numbers in a system belonging to the three-dimensional (3D) Ising universality class. The relation between intermittency index and relative baryon density fluctuation is obtained. We thus suggest that measuring the intermittency in relativistic heavy-ion collisions could be used as a good probe of density fluctuations associated with the QCD critical phenomena. From recent preliminary results on neutron density fluctuations in central Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV at RHIC/STAR, the collision energy dependence of intermittency index is extracted and shows a non-monotonic behavior with a peak at around 20 - 27 GeV, indicating that the strength of intermittency becomes the largest in this energy region. The transport UrQMD model without implementing critical physics cannot describe the observed behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the intermittency index (a measure of self-similar correlations in momentum space) is monotonically related to the relative baryon density fluctuation in a system belonging to the 3D Ising universality class, and that extracting the intermittency index from experimental data can serve as a probe of critical density fluctuations associated with the QCD critical endpoint. The authors do not claim direct experimental observation of the critical point; rather, they extract the intermittency index indirectly by mapping preliminary STAR neutron density fluctuation measurements onto their model-derived relation, finding a non-monotonic energy dependence with a peak around 20–27 GeV.

## Method

The authors implemented a critical Monte Carlo (CMC) model based on the effective action of a 3D Ising universality class system, using a Levy random walk method to generate baryon momenta correlated according to a power-law two-particle correlator in transverse momentum space. They performed event-by-event calculations of the second-order scaled factorial moments (SFM) in a two-dimensional momentum space and extracted the intermittency index \(\phi_2\) by fitting the power-law dependence of SFM on the number of partitioned bins. The relative baryon density fluctuation \(\Delta n\) was computed from the same simulated events, and the relation between \(\phi_2\) and \(\Delta n\) was established. The authors then mapped preliminary experimental neutron density fluctuations from central Au+Au collisions at seven RHIC/STAR BES I energies onto this relation to obtain the energy dependence of \(\phi_2\), and compared the results with the UrQMD transport model (which lacks critical physics).

## Key numbers

- The second-order intermittency index from the CMC model (600 events, mean multiplicity \(\langle n_B\rangle = 20\)) is \(\phi_2 = 0.834 \pm 0.001\), consistent with the theoretical expectation \(\phi_2 = 5/6\) for a 3D Ising universality class system with fractal dimension \(\tilde{d}_F \simeq 1/3\); the systematic uncertainty is less than 1\% when varying model parameters \(p_{\rm min}\) or \(p_{\rm max}\).
- The UrQMD model with the same mean multiplicity (\(\sim 20\)) yields an intermittency index near 0 (flat SFM with increasing number of bins).
- The extracted intermittency index from STAR neutron density fluctuations in central Au+Au collisions (0\%–10\%) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200\) GeV shows a non-monotonic energy dependence with a peak at around 20–27 GeV; the value approaches zero at 200 GeV.
- The UrQMD model calculations at the same collision energies show a flat trend with \(\phi_2\) around 0 at all energies.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework linking critical opalescence and intermittency in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies by Antoniou et al. (2006, 2010, 2016, 2018) who showed that critical fluctuations in a 3D Ising system manifest as power-law behavior of scaled factorial moments. The paper provides a direct quantitative relation between the intermittency index and relative baryon density fluctuation, complementing the coalescence-model approach used by the NA49 Collaboration (which found a peak in neutron density fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 8.8\) GeV) and the STAR Collaboration’s preliminary neutron density fluctuation measurements. The results enable future experimental searches for the QCD critical endpoint via direct measurement of intermittency in the RHIC BES II program, alongside other observables such as higher cumulant ratios and directed flow.
