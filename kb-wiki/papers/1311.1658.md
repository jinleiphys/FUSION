# Antinucleon-nucleon interaction in chiral effective field theory

> Results of an exploratory study of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction within chiral effective field theory are reported. The antinucleon-nucleon potential is derived up to next-to-next-to-leading order, based on a modified Weinberg power counting, in close analogy to pertinent studies of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The low-energy constants associated with the arising contact interactions are fixed by a fit to phase shifts and inelasticities provided by a recently published phase-shift analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data. The overall quality of the achieved description of the antinucleon-nucleon amplitudes is comparable to the one found in case of the nucleon-nucleon interaction at the same order. For most S-waves and several P-waves good agreement with the antinucleon-nucleon phase shifts and inelasticities is obtained up to laboratory energies of around 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The antinucleon-nucleon (bar N N) interaction can be described within chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order, with an overall quality comparable to that achieved for the nucleon-nucleon interaction at the same order, but only for laboratory energies up to around 200 MeV and with the caveat that the description is exploratory and relies on a modified Weinberg power counting.

## Method

The authors derived the antinucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using chiral effective field theory, following the same approach used by Epelbaum et al. for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The low-energy constants for contact interactions were fixed by fitting to phase shifts and inelasticities from a recently published partial-wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data by Zhou and Timmermans (2012). Annihilation processes were incorporated by introducing complex low-energy constants with a specific unitary form, and the scattering equation was solved using a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation with cutoff values between 450 and 650 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion exchange potential uses the axial coupling constant g_A = 1.29, pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV, isospin-averaged nucleon mass m = 938.918 MeV, and isospin-averaged pion mass M_pi = 138.039 MeV.
- The low-energy constants c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.40 GeV^{-1}, and c_4 = 3.40 GeV^{-1} were adopted from a Q^3-analysis of the pion-nucleon system by Paul (2000).
- Cutoff values for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation were chosen in the range Lambda = 450-600 MeV at NLO and Lambda = 450-650 MeV at NNLO, with spectral function regularization cutoff tilde-Lambda = 500-700 MeV.
- The fit to phase shifts and inelasticities was performed for laboratory energies below 125 MeV (corresponding to p_lab less than or equal to 500 MeV/c).
- For the ^1S_0 channel with isospin I=0, the phase shift is described well up to fairly high energies even at NLO, while for I=1 the phase shift shows a resonance-like behavior around 150 MeV that is difficult to reproduce quantitatively.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach, previously successful for the nucleon-nucleon interaction (Epelbaum et al., 2005; Entem and Machleidt, 2003), to the antinucleon-nucleon system for the first time at next-to-next-to-leading order. It builds on a recent partial-wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data by Zhou and Timmermans (2012) and contrasts with earlier studies by Chen et al. (2010, 2011) who used the Kaplan-Savage-Wise resummation scheme for the bar N N interaction up to NLO. The results enable future applications to near-threshold enhancements observed in J/psi and B meson decays and to the analysis of data from the PANDA experiment at FAIR.
