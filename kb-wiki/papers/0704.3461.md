# The vector-scalar mixing in nuclear medium and the two quark component of scalar meson from QCD sum rules

> We derive the QCD sum rules for the vector and scalar meson mixing in nuclear medium, using a two quark interpolating field for both mesons. Modeling the mixing via a nucleon hole contribution with known coupling constant, the sum rule can be used to determine the overlap of the interpolating field and the scalar meson. In the I=0 channel, we find a stable Borel curve and an overlap that is about 10% of the corresponding value in the pseudo scalar or vector channel. The sum rule in the I=1 channel is less reliable but also consistent with a small value for the overlap. These results suggest that both the $σ$ and $a_0$ have a small two quark and thus probably a large tetraquark components. We discuss the possibility of observing these scalar mesons from vector mesons emanating from the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules for the vector-scalar correlation function in nuclear matter, the overlap of a two-quark interpolating field with the scalar meson is found to be about 10% of the corresponding value in the pseudoscalar or vector channel for the I=0 channel, and about 20% for the I=1 channel, suggesting both the sigma and a0 have small two-quark components and likely large tetraquark components; however, the I=1 sum rule is less reliable and the quoted value is an asymptotic estimate at higher Borel masses.

## Method

The authors derive the operator product expansion (OPE) for the correlation function between scalar and vector currents in nuclear matter, using two-quark interpolating fields for both mesons. They model the phenomenological side through a nucleon hole contribution with known coupling constants from the Bonn potential, and perform a Borel transformation to suppress excited state contributions. The sum rule is then used to extract the overlap of the scalar meson with the two-quark current, normalized to the pion overlap constant.

## Key numbers

- For the I=0 (omega-sigma) channel: the extracted overlap f_sigma normalized to the pion overlap constant is 0.10 (10%), obtained from a stable Borel plateau at Borel mass around 1.0-2.0 GeV^2.
- For the I=1 (rho-a0) channel: the asymptotic value of the normalized overlap f_a0 is about 0.2 (20%) at higher Borel masses, though no stable Borel region exists.
- Input parameters used: g_omega = 11.5, g_sigma = 9.4, m_sigma = 0.55 GeV, f_omega = 0.12 GeV^2; for I=1: kappa = 14.2, g_a0 = 2.8, f_rho = 0.12 GeV^2.
- The vacuum quark condensate is taken as <q_bar q> = -(0.23 GeV)^3 at mu = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, previously applied to constrain the momentum-dependent part of the light vector meson self-energy (FLK99). It addresses the long-standing controversy over the quark content of scalar mesons, where lattice calculations using two-quark interpolating fields predict masses above 1.3 GeV for the isospin 1 channel (Bardeen 2004, Prelovsek 2004, Suganuma 2005, Mathur 2006, Burch 2006), while four-quark interpolating fields yield masses around 1 GeV for f0 and a0 (Suganuma 2005, Ishii 2006) and around 600 MeV for sigma (Mathur 2006). The present analysis avoids the single instanton contamination that plagued earlier scalar QCD sum rules (Shuryak 1993) by using a non-diagonal correlation function. The results enable quantitative inclusion of scalar-vector mixing effects in dilepton spectra from heavy ion collisions, as previously modeled phenomenologically by Gale and collaborators (2000, 2001).
