# Alpha Chain Structures of ^{12}C

> N-αstructures of light nuclei with axial symmetry are studied using relativistic Hartree approximation. Metastable excited states are searched in a configuration space which allows linear alpha chain structures. As a result, it is shown that ^{12}C has ^8Be + αresonance state at about 1 MeV above ^8Be-αthreshold as an asymmetric 3-αlinear-chain structure, which plays an important role in stellar nucleosynthesis.

# Alpha Chain Structures of ^{12}C

## Key claim
Using a relativistic Hartree approximation with the L1 parameter set, the authors find that ^{12}C has a ^{8}Be + α resonance state about 1.3 MeV above the ^{8}Be–α threshold, which they interpret as an asymmetric 3-α linear-chain structure. However, this energy is much higher than the empirical value for the second 0⁺ state (0.28 MeV above the ^{8}Be + α threshold), and the authors caution that the L1 parameter set gives too little binding for light nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed axially symmetric relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the L1 parameter set, constructing a functional basis space from single particle levels of ^{4}He to accommodate highly deformed alpha-chain configurations. Starting with two or three α particles linearly arranged on the z-axis at various initial separations (4 fm to 13 fm for 2-α; 4 fm to 10 fm for 3-α), they solved the RMF equations self-consistently and examined the converged solutions for binding energies and spatial configurations.

## Key numbers
- Ground state of ^{8}Be: binding energy 43.1 MeV (empirical 56.5 MeV), with 2.8 fm separation between the two α clusters.
- Ground state of ^{12}C: binding energy 94.0 MeV using L1 parameter set (empirical 92.2 MeV).
- ^{8}Be–^{4}He continuum state at ~10 fm separation: energy −57.2 MeV, which is 1.3 MeV above the ^{8}Be + α threshold (−58.5 MeV for L1).
- Empirical second 0⁺ state of ^{12}C: resonance energy 0.3796 MeV above the 3-α threshold (0.278 MeV above ^{8}Be + α threshold), with full width Γ = 8.5 × 10⁻⁶ MeV.
- Binding energy of ^{4}He with NL1 parameter set: 31.9 MeV (empirical 28.27 MeV); with L1 parameter set: 15.4 MeV to 3.6 MeV (too little binding).

## Context
This work extends earlier nonrelativistic macroscopic and microscopic studies of α-cluster structures in ^{12}C and ^{16}O (Fedorov, Visschers, Csoto, Hayes, Itagaki, Pichler, Descouvemont, Dunbar, Pauli) by applying a relativistic mean field approach that treats nucleons explicitly rather than inert α clusters. The results support the Hoyle mechanism (Hoyle, 1954) for stellar nucleosynthesis, in which ^{12}C is produced via a ^{8}Be + α resonance rather than a direct 3-α resonance. The authors note that no 3-α resonance state with equally spaced α particles was found, and they suggest future work should find a better parameter set for the mass range 4–30 and extend the method to ^{16}O, ^{20}Ne, and ^{24}Mg.
