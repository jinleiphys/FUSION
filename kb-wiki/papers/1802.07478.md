# Neutrino astrophysics and its connections to nuclear physics

## Key claim
Neutrino flavor evolution in dense astrophysical environments can be formally connected to many-body problems in nuclear physics through the BBGKY hierarchy, with the linearized neutrino evolution equations shown to be analogous to the Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) used for nuclear collective excitations. The paper presents these connections as a framework for understanding novel flavor conversion phenomena, but notes that pinning down the underlying mechanisms requires demanding simulations much more complex than in the solar case.

## Method
The author reviews the application of the relativistic BBGKY hierarchy to a gas of self-interacting neutrinos and antineutrinos in astrophysical environments, showing that the first equation yields the Liouville-Von Neumann equation for single-particle densities commonly used for neutrino flavor evolution. The linearized version of the neutrino evolution equations is shown to be formally equivalent to RPA for atomic nuclei, with the off-diagonal terms of the neutrino and antineutrino density matrices replacing the forward X and backward Y amplitudes of nuclear RPA. The paper also reviews likelihood analyses for reconstructing supernova neutrino spectra and gravitational binding energy from future observations.

## Key numbers
- The proton-proton reaction chain is responsible for 99% of energy production in the Sun, confirmed by precise solar neutrino measurements (Bellini 2014)
- Approximately 10^58 neutrinos are emitted during a core-collapse supernova explosion, taking away about 3 × 10^53 erg of gravitational binding energy from the newly formed neutron star
- The combination of inverse-beta decay and electron scattering events in Super-Kamiokande allows measurement of the total gravitational energy with 11% precision (GalloRosso 2017)
- Flux-averaged neutrino-nucleus cross sections for typical supernova neutrino fluxes can vary by several tens of percent, e.g. for the case of lead (Vaananen 2011)
- Solar neutrinos with less than 2 MeV energy undergo averaged vacuum oscillations, while those above 2 MeV convert via the MSW effect (Bellini 2014)

## Context
This proceedings paper extends the formal connection between neutrino flavor evolution in astrophysical environments and nuclear many-body physics, building on the application of BBGKY hierarchy to neutrino gases by Volpe (2013) and the linearized RPA-like analysis by Vaananen (2013). The work reviews how the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1977, Mikheev 1986) explains solar neutrino flavor conversion, and discusses novel phenomena from neutrino self-interactions including bipolar oscillations, spectral splits, matter-neutrino resonance (Malkus 2012), and fast modes (Sawyer 2015). The paper enables future work on understanding flavor conversion in core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, with implications for explosion dynamics, r-process nucleosynthesis, and determination of the neutron star equation of state from future supernova neutrino observations.
