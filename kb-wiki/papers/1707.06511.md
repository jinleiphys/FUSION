# Effect of Finite Binding on the Apparent Spin-Orbit Splitting in Nuclei

> The apparent splitting between orbitals that are spin-orbit partners can be substantially influenced by the effects of finite binding. In particular, such effects can account for the observed decrease in separation of the neutron $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ orbitals between the $^{41}$Ca and $^{35}$Si isotopes. This behavior has been the subject of recent experimental and theoretical works and cited as evidence for a proton "bubble" in $^{34}$Si causing an explicit weakening of the spin-orbit interaction. The results reported here suggest that the change in the separation between the $1p_{3/2}$ and $1p_{1/2}$ partners occurs dominantly because of the behavior of the energies of these $1p$ neutron states near zero binding.

# Effect of Finite Binding on the Apparent Spin-Orbit Splitting in Nuclei

## Key claim
The observed decrease in the neutron 1p spin-orbit splitting between 41Ca and 35Si can be explained by finite-binding effects as the 1p1/2 orbital approaches the neutron separation threshold, without requiring a weakening of the spin-orbit interaction or invoking a proton "bubble" in 34Si.

## Method
Woods-Saxon potential calculations were performed using the code of Volya with a fixed spin-orbit strength (Vso = 6 MeV) and standard geometric parameters (r0 = 1.28 fm, a = 0.63 fm, rso0 = 1.1, aso = 0.65 fm). The potential depth was adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the 1p3/2 orbital for each nucleus, with depths of 51.8 MeV for 40Ca+n and 47.0 MeV for 34Si+n. The resulting 1p1/2 binding energies were compared to experimental centroids from neutron-adding (d,p) reactions on 40Ca, 38Ar, 36S, and 34Si.

## Key numbers
- The spin-orbit splitting between neutron 1p3/2 and 1p1/2 states decreases by almost 1 MeV from 41Ca to 35Si, with most of the decrease occurring between 37S and 35Si.
- The change in separation between the 1p3/2 and 1p1/2 orbitals from 41Ca to 35Si is relatively insensitive to Woods-Saxon parameter choice, varying from 0.7 to 0.9 MeV across reasonable parameter sets.
- At 35Si, the 1p1/2 orbital is bound by only a few hundred keV.
- The centrifugal barrier for l=1 neutrons is approximately 500 keV.
- Two-body matrix element calculations show the proton-neutron interaction changes the 1p splitting by about 100 keV from Si to Ca, approximately 10-20% of the weak-binding effect.

## Context
This work extends the earlier findings of Hoffman, Kay, and Schiffer (2014, 2016) on weak-binding effects for low-angular-momentum states, and the calculations of Hamamoto and Sagawa (2004) who observed a decrease in 1p splitting as the 1p1/2 approached threshold in a generic A=44 system. The paper directly challenges the interpretation of Burgunder et al. (2014) and Mutschler et al. (2017), who attributed the reduced splitting to a proton "bubble" in 34Si causing explicit weakening of the spin-orbit interaction. The results suggest that calculations using harmonic oscillator basis expansions, such as those of Karakatsanis et al. (2017) and Duguet et al. (2017), cannot capture these finite-binding geometric effects. This work enables a simpler interpretation of near-threshold phenomena including s- and p-wave halos and the ordering of sd states in light nuclei.
