# Nucleon Magnetic Moments and Electric Polarizabilities

> Electromagnetic properties of the nucleon are explored with lattice QCD using a novel technique. Focusing on background electric fields, we show how the electric polarizability can be extracted from nucleon correlation functions. A crucial step concerns addressing contributions from the magnetic moment, which affects the relativistic propagation of nucleons in electric fields. By properly handing these contributions, we can determine both magnetic moments and electric polarizabilities. Lattice results from anisotropic clover lattices are presented. Our method is not limited to the neutron; we show results for the proton as well.

# Nucleon Magnetic Moments and Electric Polarizabilities

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with background electric fields, the electric polarizability of the nucleon can be extracted from nucleon correlation functions only after performing a Born subtraction to remove the magnetic moment contribution; the method works for both the neutron and the proton, but the results presented are only "connected" contributions at a single pion mass and lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors implement a compact U(1) background electric field on anisotropic clover lattices generated by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration, using a quantization condition qE = 2πn/LT to ensure uniform fields. For the neutron, they measure both unpolarized and boost-projected correlation functions (using projection matrices P± = 1/2 (1 ± K3)) to separate the magnetic moment and electric polarizability contributions. For the proton, they account for additional Born couplings to the total charge and anomalous magnetic moment, performing simultaneous two-state fits to boost-projected correlators across multiple field strengths.

## Key numbers

- Neutron magnetic moment: μn = -1.63(10)(4)(5) [μN] (connected contribution)
- Neutron electric polarizability: αnE = 3.3(1.5)(2)(3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (connected contribution)
- Proton magnetic moment: μp = 2.63(13)(1)(4) [μN] (connected contribution)
- Proton electric polarizability: αpE = 2.4(1.9)(3)(2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (connected contribution)
- Unpolarized neutron combination An = 1/2 (αE - μn²/Mn) = 1.3(9)(1)(1) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from unpolarized data, and 1.3(7)(2)(1) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from boost-projected data
- Lattice parameters: mπ = 390 MeV, mK = 546 MeV, lattice size 20³ × 128, with electric field strengths |e at as E| ranging from 0.00000 to 0.02945 for n = 0 through n = ±4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous background field method for pseudoscalar mesons (Detmold 2009) to the nucleon, and is the first lattice QCD treatment of the proton in background electric fields. The method enables first-principles determination of nucleon electromagnetic polarizabilities, which are experimentally accessible through Compton scattering and provide constraints on chiral perturbation theory descriptions of the pion cloud deformation. Future refinements needed include calculations at multiple pion masses and lattice spacings, inclusion of the background field in gauge generation, and treatment of finite volume effects from the non-periodic gauge action and non-trivial holonomy (Hu 2007, Tiburzi 2008, Detmold 2009).
