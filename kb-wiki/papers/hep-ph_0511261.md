# The nucleon and Delta-resonance masses in relativistic chiral effective-field theory

> We study the chiral behavior of the nucleon and $Δ$-isobar masses within a manifestly covariant chiral effective-field theory, consistent with the analyticity principle. We compute the $πN$ and $πΔ$ one-loop contributions to the mass and field-renormalization constant, and find that they can be described in terms of universal relativistic loop functions, multiplied by appropriate spin, isospin and coupling constants. We show that these relativistic one-loop corrections, when properly renormalized, obey the chiral power-counting and vanish in the chiral limit. The results including only the $πN$-loop corrections compare favorably with the lattice QCD data for the pion-mass dependence of the nucleon and $Δ$ masses, while inclusion of the $πΔ$ loops tends to spoil this agreement.

## Key claim

The relativistic one-loop chiral effective-field theory calculation, when including only πN-loop corrections, can describe lattice QCD data for the pion-mass dependence of both the nucleon and Δ masses up to mπ² ≈ 0.5 GeV² with only one free parameter; however, inclusion of the πΔ loops tends to spoil this agreement, requiring an additional mπ⁴ term to restore the fit.

## Method

The authors computed the πN and πΔ one-loop contributions to the nucleon and Δ self-energies within a manifestly covariant chiral effective-field theory, using a specific chiral Lagrangian where the πNN coupling differs from the standard pseudovector form and the spin-3/2 Δ field couplings are gauge-invariant. The loop integrals were evaluated via dimensional regularization, and the results were renormalized by subtracting mπ⁰ and mπ² terms to absorb into available low-energy constants, yielding a universal expression for the mass and field-renormalization corrections. The resulting mass formula was then fitted to lattice QCD data from the MILC Collaboration for the nucleon and Δ masses as functions of mπ².

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: πN-loop fit with M_N^(0) = 0.883 GeV and c_1N = -0.87 GeV⁻¹ describes lattice data up to mπ² ≈ 0.5 GeV².
- Nucleon sigma-term from πN-loop only: σ_N = 67 (from c_1N) - 17 (loop) = 50 MeV.
- Nucleon sigma-term from πN + πΔ loops: σ_N = 85 - 17 - 11 = 57 MeV.
- Δ mass: πN-loop fit with M_Δ^(0) = 1.20 GeV and c_1Δ = -0.40 GeV⁻¹ describes lattice data up to mπ² ≈ 0.4 GeV².
- Δ width formula yields h_A ≈ 2.85 from the experimental width Γ_Δ ≈ 115 MeV.
- Leading non-analytic mπ³ coefficient for nucleon mass from πN loop: -3/(32π fπ²) g_A².
- Leading non-analytic mπ³ coefficient for Δ mass from πΔ loop: -3/(32π fπ²) (25/81) H_A².

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral effective-field theory approach of Gegelia (1999) and the manifestly covariant formulation of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), but avoids the infrared regularization scheme which spoils analyticity. The calculation improves upon earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory results (e.g., Banerjee and Milana, 1994; Jenkins and Manohar, 1991) by resumming relativistic corrections that improve convergence without introducing additional free parameters. The results enable more reliable extrapolations of lattice QCD data (from the MILC Collaboration, 2001) to the physical pion mass, and provide a framework for future complete fourth-order calculations of both πN and πΔ loops.
