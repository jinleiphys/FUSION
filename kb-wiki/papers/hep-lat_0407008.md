# Nucleon Properties at Finite Volume: the Epsilon Prime Regime

> We study the properties of the nucleon in highly asymmetric volumes where the spatial dimensions are small but the time dimension is large in comparison to the inverse pion mass. To facilitate power-counting at the level of Feynman diagrams, we introduce $ε^\prime$-power-counting which is a special case of Leutwyler's $δ$-power-counting. Pion zero-modes enter the $ε^\prime$-counting perturbatively, in contrast to both the $ε$- and $δ$-power-countings, since $m_q < q\bar{q}> V$ remains large. However, these modes are enhanced over those with non-zero momenta and enter at lower orders in the $ε^\prime$-expansion than they would in large volume chiral perturbation theory. We discuss an application of $ε^\prime$-counting by determining the nucleon mass, magnetic moment and axial matrix element at the first nontrivial order in the $ε^\prime$-expansion.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new power-counting scheme, \(\epsilon^\prime\)-power-counting, for calculating nucleon properties in highly asymmetric lattice volumes where spatial dimensions are small (\(m_\pi L \ll 1\)) but the time dimension is large (\(m_\pi L_4 \gg 1\)), and claims that in this regime pion zero-modes remain perturbative (unlike in the \(\epsilon\)- or \(\delta\)-regimes) because \(m_q \langle q\bar{q}\rangle V\) remains large, yet these modes are enhanced over non-zero modes and enter at lower orders than in large-volume chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors define \(\epsilon^\prime\)-power-counting as a special case of Leutwyler's \(\delta\)-power-counting, with scaling relations \(\Lambda_\chi L \sim 1/\epsilon^\prime\), \(m_\pi^2/\Lambda_\chi^2 \sim \epsilon^{\prime 4}\), and \(\Lambda_\chi L_4 \sim 1/\epsilon^{\prime \alpha}\) for \(\alpha \gg 2\). They then compute the nucleon mass, magnetic moment, and axial matrix element at first nontrivial order in the \(\epsilon^\prime\)-expansion using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with nucleon and \(\Delta\)-resonance degrees of freedom, treating spatial momentum zero-modes and non-zero modes separately in loop integrals.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the \(\epsilon^\prime\)-regime: \(M_N(L) = M_0 + \frac{1}{f^2 L^3} \left[ \frac{3}{4} g_A^2 + \frac{2}{3} g_{\Delta N}^2 \right] + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{\prime 4})\), where \(f \sim 132\) MeV.
- Nucleon magnetic moment in the \(\epsilon^\prime\)-regime: \(\hat\mu(L) = \mu_0 + \mu_1 \tau^3 \left[1 - \frac{1}{m_\pi f^2 L^3}\right] + \frac{M_N c_1}{6\pi f^2 L} \left[ g_A^2 + \frac{2}{9} g_{\Delta N}^2 \right] \tau^3 + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{\prime 2})\), where \(c_1 = -2.8372974\).
- Axial matrix element in the \(\epsilon^\prime\)-regime: \(\Gamma_{NN}(L) = g_A \left[ 1 - \frac{1}{m_\pi f^2 L^3} \right] + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{\prime 2})\).
- For \(m_\pi = 300\) MeV and \(L = 2.5\) fm, the leading \(\epsilon^\prime\) correction to \(g_A\) is 10% (lower than infinite volume), and the modification of the nucleon mass is \(\sim 2\%\).
- The \(\epsilon^\prime\)-power-counting applies when \(m_\pi L \ll 2\pi\) and \(\Lambda_\chi L \gg 2\pi\); for physical \(m_\pi\) and \(\Lambda_\chi\), a lattice dimension of \(L = 2.5\)–4 fm is in the \(\epsilon^\prime\)-regime (\(\epsilon^\prime < 1/2\)).

## Context

This work extends Leutwyler's \(\delta\)-power-counting (Leutwyler, 1987) to a new regime where the time dimension is effectively infinite, and contrasts with both the \(\epsilon\)-regime (Gasser, 1987; Hansen, 1990; Hasenfratz, 1989; Leutwyler, 1992) where pion zero-modes must be resummed non-perturbatively, and the \(p\)-expansion valid for \(m_\pi L \gg 1\) where finite-volume effects are exponentially suppressed (Luscher, 1985; Beane, 2004). The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to compute and correct for finite-volume effects in highly asymmetric volumes, potentially explaining observed discrepancies in lattice calculations of \(g_A\) (Sasaki, 2003; Dolgov, 2002; Capitani, 1999), and provide a method to determine the transition axial coupling \(g_{N\Delta}\) from the volume dependence of the nucleon mass.
