# Second Backbend in the Mass A ~ 180 Region

> Within the framework of selfconsistent cranked Hartree-Fock- Bogoliubov theory(one-dimensional) we predict second backbend in the yrast line of Os-182 at $I \approx 40 $, which is even sharper than the first one observed experimentally at $I \approx 14 $. Around such a high spin the structure becomes multi-quasiparticle type, but the main source of this strong discontinuity is a sudden large alignment of i_13/2 proton orbitals along the rotation axis followed soon by the alignment of j_15/2 neutron orbitals. This leads to drastic structural changes at such high spins. When experimentally confirmed, this will be observed for the first time in this mass region, and will be at the highest spin so far.

# Second Backbend in the Mass A ~ 180 Region

## Key claim
Within selfconsistent cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) theory, a second backbend is predicted in the yrast line of 182Os at I ≈ 40 ħ, which is even sharper than the first backbend observed experimentally at I ≈ 14 ħ; however, the first backbend itself is not well reproduced by the calculation, suggesting that an extra mechanism such as tilting may be needed to explain the first backbend.

## Method
Selfconsistent one-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations were performed for 182Os using a pairing-plus-quadrupole model Hamiltonian, with and without an additional hexadecapole (Q4) term. The CHFB equations were solved selfconsistently in terms of seven collective variables (pairing gaps Δp and Δn, and deformation parameters qλμ for λ=2,4) with particle number and angular momentum constraints, for spins up to I = 50 ħ. Rotational g-factors were also computed using the standard cranking expression with gs values attenuated by a factor of 0.6.

## Key numbers
- First backbend observed experimentally at I ≈ 14 ħ; second backbend predicted at I ≈ 40 ħ
- At I = 14, the contribution of ni13/2 orbitals is about 9 ħ, close to the experimental estimate of 10 ħ (Kuts et al.)
- At I = 40, the contribution of ni13/2 orbitals suddenly drops by about 6 ħ in one step
- γ changes sign from positive to negative by a sizeable amount around I = 40, with β increasing by about 20% (stretching)
- Δn goes to zero at I = 14; Δp vanishes at I = 30 with Q4 term, and at I = 26 without it
- With only quadrupole force, proton pairing recovers at I = 34 (Δp = 0.214 MeV) and I = 40 (Δp = 0.239 MeV); with Q4 term, recovery only at I = 34 (Δp = 0.174 MeV)
- g(I=2) = 0.245 for Q4 case and 0.230 for Q2 case, compared to gR = 0.27 used by Kuts et al.
- Experimental γ-ray energy differences: Eγ(32) - Eγ(30) = 78 keV, Eγ(34) - Eγ(32) = 65 keV
- Yrast line experimentally known up to I = 34 ħ (Kuts et al., Firestone et al.)

## Context
This work extends the traditional CHFB approach, which was most successful in explaining backbending in the rare-earth region during the 1970s and 1980s (Ring and Schuck, Diebel et al.), to the mass A ~ 180 region where recent experiments on W-Os isotopes (Kuts et al., Firestone et al.) have revealed high-K bands near the yrast line. The prediction of a second backbend at I ≈ 40 ħ in 182Os would be the first observation of such a feature in this mass region and at the highest spin so far. The calculation contrasts with recent band mixing spin projected shell model results (Sheikh et al.) which found that backbends in 178,180,182W and 184Os originate from normal s-band crossing, with tilted bands appearing slightly above the yrast line. The author notes that Onishi and collaborators are attempting generator coordinate method calculations for 182Os treating tilting angles as generator coordinates, which could elucidate the distribution of K components.
