# Interaction of K^- mesons with light nuclei

> We calculate the scattering lenths A(K-,3He) and A(K-,4He) using the multiple scattering approach and different parameters sets for the elementary a(Kbar,N). Within the zero-range approximation, we find for both systems loosely bound states with binding energies in the range 2-7 MeV and widths 11-18 MeV. It is demonstrated that the existence of deeply bound K-,4He states, which have been predicted in literature, can be tested by measuring the reaction dd -> 4He K-K+ at COSY.

## Key claim

Using a multiple-scattering approach with zero-range approximation, the authors find loosely bound states in the \(K^{-}\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\) and \(K^{-}\alpha\) systems with binding energies of 2–7 MeV and widths of 11–18 MeV, but they note that this model is valid only near threshold and cannot describe the deeply bound states predicted by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2003).

## Method

The authors calculated the \(K^{-}\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\) and \(K^{-}\alpha\) scattering lengths using a multiple-scattering approach with five different parameter sets for the elementary \(\bar{K}N\) scattering amplitudes (Sets 1–5 from prior fits). They then unitarized the constant scattering length within the zero-range approximation to reconstruct the scattering amplitude and locate its pole, which gives the binding energy and width of possible \(K^{-}X\) resonances.

## Key numbers

*   \(K^{-}\alpha\) scattering length from Set 1: \((-1.80 + i0.90)\) fm.
*   \(K^{-}\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\) scattering length from Set 1: \((-1.50 + i0.83)\) fm.
*   Pole position for \(K^{-}\alpha\) with Set 1: binding energy \(E_R = 6.7\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_R = 18\) MeV.
*   Pole position for \(K^{-}\alpha\) with Set 4: binding energy \(E_R = 2.0\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_R = 11.3\) MeV.
*   Pole position for \(K^{-}\alpha\) with Set 5: binding energy \(E_R = 4.8\) MeV, width \(\Gamma_R = 14.9\) MeV.
*   Akaishi and Yamazaki (2003) predicted a deeply bound \(\bar{K}\alpha\) state at \(E^* = (-86 - i34/2)\) MeV, which is far from the authors' solutions.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of \(K^{-}d\) scattering lengths using multiple-scattering and Faddeev approaches (Grishina 2004, Deloff 2000) to the \(K^{-}\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\) and \(K^{-}\alpha\) systems. The results contradict the deeply bound \(\bar{K}\alpha\) state predicted by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2003), but the authors suggest both loosely and deeply bound states could be different eigenvalues of the effective Hamiltonian. The paper proposes that the reaction \(dd \to \alpha K^{-}K^{+}\) at COSY can test the existence of bound \(\bar{K}\alpha\) states through final-state interaction effects in the \(K^{-}\alpha\) invariant mass spectrum.
