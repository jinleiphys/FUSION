# A Generalized False Vacuum Skyrme model

> We propose a generalization of the False Vacuum Skyrme model for any simple compact Lie groups $G$ that leads to Hermitian symmetric spaces. The Skyrme field that in the original theory takes its values in $SU(2)$ Lie group, now takes its values in $G$, while the remaining fields correspond to the entries of a symmetric, positive, and invertible $\dim G \times \dim G$-dimensional matrix $h$. This model is also an extension of the generalized BPS Skyrme model. We prove that the global minima correspond to the $h$ fields being self-dual solutions of the generalized BPS Skyrme model, together with a particular field configuration for the Skyrme field that leads to a spherically symmetric topological charge density. As in the case of the original model, the minimization of the energy leads to the so-called reduced problem, defined in the context of false vacuum decay. This imposes a condition on the Skyrme field, which, if satisfied, makes the total energy of the global minima, as well as the main properties of the model, equivalent to those obtained for the $G=SU(2)$ case. We study this condition and its consequences within the generalized rational map ansatz and show how it can be satisfied for $G=SU(p+q)$, where $p$ and $q$ are positive integers, with the Hermitian symmetric spaces being $SU(p+q)/SU(p) \otimes SU(q) \otimes U(1)$. In such a case, the model is completely equivalent to the $G=SU(2)$ False Vacuum Skyrme model, independent of the values of $p$ and $q$. We also provide a numerical study of the baryon density, RMS radius, and binding energy per nucleon, which deepens the previous analysis conducted for the $SU(2)$ False Vacuum Skyrme model. Additionaly, in the case of $G = SU(3)$, we have studied the application of our model to the description of the binding energies and masses of the $Λ$-hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalization of the False Vacuum Skyrme model to any simple compact Lie group G that leads to Hermitian symmetric spaces, and prove that for G = SU(p+q) the model becomes completely equivalent to the original SU(2) False Vacuum Skyrme model, independent of the values of p and q, provided the Skyrme field satisfies a specific condition derived from the reduced problem of false vacuum decay.

## Method

The model is constructed as the sum of two energy functionals E = E1 + E2, where E1 is the static energy of the generalized BPS Skyrme model (defined for any simple compact Lie group G leading to Hermitian symmetric spaces G/H ⊗ U(1)) and E2 contains kinetic and potential terms involving a power of the topological charge density plus a topological term approximating Coulomb interaction. The authors prove that global minima correspond to self-dual solutions for the h fields from the generalized BPS Skyrme model together with a particular Skyrme field configuration, and study the condition for spherical symmetry of the topological charge density using the generalized rational map ansatz. For G = SU(3), the model is applied to describe binding energies and masses of Λ-hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The BPS energy bound for the static energy E1 is given by E1(Q) ≥ 48π² |m0|/|e0| |Q|, saturated by solutions of the self-duality equations.
- The topological charge Q = [(f(r) - sin f(r))/(2π)]_{r=0}^{r=∞} Q_top, where Q_top = (iη/4π) ∫ dz d\bar{z} tr(P^{(+)}_χ P^{(-)}_{\bar{χ}}).
- The topological charge density within the rational map ansatz is Q = -η f' sin²(f/2)/(4π² r²) F, with F = -(1/2)(1+|z|²)² tr(P^{(+)}_χ P^{(-)}_{\bar{χ}}).
- The binding energy per nucleon EB = E_{|Q|=1} - E_Q/|Q| receives contributions only from the E2 term, as E1 gives vanishing contribution.
- Numerical results are presented for baryon density, RMS radius, and binding energy per nucleon from Q = 1 to Q = 240 for each value of N ≥ 2.

## Context

This work extends the False Vacuum Skyrme model previously developed for SU(2) by Ferreira and Livramento (2025) to arbitrary simple compact Lie groups, and also extends the generalized BPS Skyrme model introduced by Ferreira and Livramento (2025). The model builds on the self-dual Skyrme model proposed by Ferreira and Livramento (2017) and the quasi-self-dual extensions by Ferreira and Livramento (2024). The reduction to Coleman's false vacuum problem follows the framework of Coleman (1977, 1980). For G = SU(3), the application to Λ-hypernuclei extends studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron star physics as discussed by Glendenning (1984, 1991), Ozel (2016), Tolos (2020), and Burgio (2021).
