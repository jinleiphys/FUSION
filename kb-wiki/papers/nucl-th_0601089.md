# Continuous phase transition and negative specific heat in finite nuclei

> The liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei is studied in a heated liquid-drop model where the nuclear drop is assumed to be in thermodynamic equilibrium with its own evaporated nucleonic vapor conserving the total baryon number and isospin of the system. It is found that in the liquid-vapor coexistence region the pressure is not a constant on an isotherm indicating that the transition is continuous. At constant pressure, the caloric curve shows some anomalies, namely, the systems studied exhibit negative heat capacity in a small temperature domain. The dependence of this specific feature on the mass and isospin of the nucleus, Coulomb interaction and the chosen pressure is studied. The effects of the presence of clusters in the vapor phase on specific heat have also been explored.

## Key claim

In a heated liquid-drop model with exact baryon number and isospin conservation, the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei is continuous (not first order) because pressure is not constant on an isotherm in the coexistence region; negative specific heat at constant pressure can occur in a small temperature domain even for a continuous transition, contrary to claims that negative heat capacity is a signature of a first-order transition.

## Method

The authors model a finite nucleus as a charged liquid drop in thermodynamic equilibrium with its own evaporated nucleonic vapor (and later, a clusterized vapor) within a mean-field finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi framework using the SkM* interaction. Phase coexistence is governed by equality of pressure and neutron and proton chemical potentials between the liquid and gas phases, with total baryon number and isospin conserved. The free energy includes volume, Coulomb, and surface terms, and the system is studied at constant pressure to obtain caloric curves and specific heats for symmetric and asymmetric nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the asymmetric system $^{150}$Nd, the critical temperature is 13.1 MeV, critical pressure is 0.174 MeV fm$^{-3}$, and critical density is 0.050 fm$^{-3}$.
- The surface energy coefficient parameters for the SkM* interaction are $\sigma(0,0) = 17.51$ MeV, surface asymmetry coefficient $a_s = 38.6$ MeV, and critical temperature $T_c = 14.61$ MeV.
- Negative specific heat at constant pressure occurs in a temperature interval of typically 1.0 to 1.5 MeV for the systems studied.
- With nucleonic vapor, negative specific heat persists up to excitation energies as high as 14 MeV/A; with clusterized vapor, this upper bound is reduced to approximately 10 MeV/A.
- The reduction in Coulomb free energy as the liquid-drop center moves from the center to the boundary of the freeze-out volume is less than 4% of $F_c$.

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in finite nuclei (Sil et al., Lee and Mekjian) by including exact conservation laws and Coulomb interaction, and contrasts with the Clausius-Clapeyron analysis of Moretto et al. (2002, 2003) which found negative specific heat only for drops with $A \leq 60$ and assumed a first-order transition. The results contradict experimental findings from the TAMU group and Chomaz et al. that negative heat capacity occurs only in a narrow excitation energy range around 3-7 MeV/A; the authors attribute this discrepancy to the mean-field model's neglect of fluctuations and to the fact that experimental fragmentation conditions may be neither isobaric nor isochoric. The inclusion of clusters in the vapor phase reduces the excitation energy range of negative heat capacity but does not fully resolve the disagreement.
