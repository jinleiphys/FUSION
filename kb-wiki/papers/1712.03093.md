# Elliptic Flow and the Nuclear Equation of State

> New constraints for the nuclear equation of state at suprasaturation densities have been obtained by measuring collective particle flows in heavy-ion reactions at relativistic energies. Ratios and differences of neutron and hydrogen flows in 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon were used in studies of the asymmetric-matter equation of state. The comparison with predictions of transport models favors a moderately soft to linear density dependence, consistent with ab-initio nuclear matter theories. Model predictions suggest that comprehensive data sets collected at higher bombarding energies will provide information on the asymmetric-matter equation of state in the density range up to two or three times the saturation value.

# Elliptic Flow and the Nuclear Equation of State

## Key claim

The comparison of neutron and hydrogen elliptic-flow data from 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon with transport-model predictions favors a moderately soft to linear density dependence of the symmetry energy, consistent with ab-initio nuclear matter theories, but the authors note that the existing data set had large statistical uncertainty and that a dedicated measurement (the ASY-EOS experiment) was needed to improve accuracy.

## Method

The authors analyzed elliptic flow (the second Fourier component of azimuthal anisotropy) of neutrons and hydrogen isotopes measured with the FOPI-LAND detector system for 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. They compared the experimental neutron-proton elliptic-flow differences and ratios to predictions from the UrQMD and TuQMD transport models using different parametrizations of the symmetry energy, characterized by a power-law coefficient γ in the potential term of the form 22 MeV · (ρ/ρ0)^γ plus a kinetic term 12 MeV · (ρ/ρ0)^(2/3).

## Key numbers

- The FOPI-LAND data were collected for 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, with the system having N/Z = 1.49.
- The power-law coefficients γ = 0.5 and γ = 1.5 were used in UrQMD calculations, producing relative differences in neutron and proton elliptic flows at the level of 15%.
- The slope parameter L = 3ρ0 · dEsym/dρ at saturation density, with most results falling in the interval 20 MeV ≤ L ≤ 100 MeV and a most probable value L ≈ 60 MeV, corresponding roughly to γ = 0.6.
- The saturation density is ρ0 ≈ 0.16 nucleons/fm^3.
- The symmetry energy at saturation is approximately 30 MeV, with the kinetic contribution being about 12 MeV · (ρ/ρ0)^(2/3) and the potential contribution about 22 MeV · (ρ/ρ0)^γ.

## Context

This work extends the earlier FOPI-LAND analysis of neutron squeeze-out (Leifels et al., 1993; Lambrecht et al., 1994) and the FOPI Collaboration's extensive flow studies (Reisdorf et al., 2012; Le Fevre et al., 2016) by focusing on isotopic flow differences to isolate the symmetry-energy contribution. The results contradict the super-soft symmetry-energy conclusion derived from π−/π+ production ratios analyzed by Xiao et al. (2009) and support constraints from neutron-star observations (Steiner et al., 2010; Lattimer and Lim, 2013). The findings motivated the ASY-EOS experiment at GSI, which collected new data for 197Au + 197Au, 96Zr + 96Zr, and 96Ru + 96Ru collisions to improve statistical accuracy and probe the asymmetric-matter equation of state up to two or three times saturation density.
