# Sign ambiguity in the K Sigma channel

> Ambiguities of the signs of $N\to ΣK$ coupling constants are studied in a multichannel partial wave analysis of a large body of pion and photo-induced reactions. It is shown that the signs are not free from some ambiguities, and further experimental data are needed. Data on the reactions $π^+p\to Σ^+K^+$ and $γp\to K^+Σ^0$ define rather well the isospin 3/2 contributions to these channels. However the lack of information on polarization observables for the reactions $π^-p\to Σ^0K^0$, $π^-p\to Σ^-K^+$ and $γp\to K^0Σ^+$ does not allow us to fix uniquely the signs of $N\to ΣK$ coupling constants. As a consequence, also the contributions of nucleon resonances to these channels remain uncertain.

## Key claim

The signs of the N to Sigma K coupling constants for nucleon resonances are not uniquely determined by the current data; two distinct solutions (BnGa2011-02M and BnGa2013-02) both provide acceptable descriptions of the global data set, and the ambiguity can only be resolved with new polarization measurements.

## Method

The Bonn-Gatchina group performed a multichannel partial wave analysis of 31,180 data points from pion and photo-induced reactions (including pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, pi pi N, and pi eta N final states) plus approximately 500,000 events from three-body final states. They systematically varied the signs of all N to K Sigma K-matrix coupling constants (resonances and background terms) in all possible combinations, finding that the optimum solution required sign changes for the S11, D13, and F15 partial waves. The new solution (BnGa2013-02) was compared to the previous solution (BnGa2011-02M) and to results from the Bonn-Juelich group.

## Key numbers

* Total data set: 31,180 data points from two-body reactions described with chi-squared of 48,710, giving chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.6.
* Approximately 500,000 events from three-body final states included in an event-based likelihood fit.
* For the reaction pi- p to K0 Sigma0, the differential cross section chi-squared improved from 1.02 (BnGa2011-02M) to 0.69 (BnGa2013-02) for 220 data points from Hart (1979).
* For the reaction pi- p to K0 Sigma0, the polarization chi-squared improved from 1.53 (BnGa2011-02M) to 1.21 (BnGa2013-02) for 85 data points from Hart (1979).
* The overall likelihood value for the new solution BnGa2013-02 was about 740 better than for solution BnGa2011-02M.
* For gamma p to K+ Sigma0, the CLAS differential cross section chi-squared changed from 1.30 (BnGa2011-02M) to 1.49 (BnGa2013-02) for 1,590 data points from Dey (2010).

## Context

This work extends the Bonn-Gatchina group's previous comprehensive analyses (Anisovich 2011, 2012) by specifically investigating the sign ambiguity in the K Sigma channel. It contrasts with the Bonn-Juelich group's coupled-channel analyses (Doring 2010, Ronchen 2012), which used different model assumptions and found different partial wave contributions in the isospin 3/2 sector. The results enable future experimental programs: measurements of the recoil polarization in pi- p to K+ Sigma- (potentially at GSI by HADES) or polarization observables in gamma p to K0 Sigma0 (by CBELSA/TAPS and CLAS) can resolve the ambiguity and better define nucleon resonances in the 2 GeV region.
