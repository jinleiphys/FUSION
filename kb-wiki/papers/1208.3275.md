# alpha-cluster correlations and symmetry breaking in light nuclei

> $α$-cluster correlations in the ground states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are studied. Because of the $α$ correlations, the intrinsic states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O have triangle and tetrahedral shapes, respectively. The deformations are regarded as spontaneous symmetry breaking of rotational invariance, and the resultant oscillating surface density is associated with a density wave (DW) state caused by the instability of Fermi surface with respect to a kind of $1p$-$1h$ correlations. To discuss the symmetry breaking between uniform density states and the oscillating density state, a schematic model of a few clusters on a Fermi gas core in a one-dimensional finite box was introduced. The model analysis suggests structure transitions from a Fermi gas state to a DW-like state via a BCS-like state, and to a Bose Einstein condensation (BEC)-like state depending on the cluster size relative to the box size. It was found that the oscillating density in the DW-like state originates in Pauli blocking effects.

## Key claim

The intrinsic ground states of \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O exhibit triangle and tetrahedral shapes, respectively, due to \(\alpha\)-cluster correlations; these deformations are interpreted as spontaneous symmetry breaking of rotational invariance, and the oscillating surface density is associated with a density wave (DW) state caused by the instability of the Fermi surface with respect to a kind of \(1p\)-\(1h\) correlation, with the caveat that the \(\alpha\) clusters are not ideal \((0s)^4\) clusters but somewhat dissociated and the geometric configurations are distorted rather than regular.

## Method

The authors applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to the ground states of \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O, performing microscopic many-body calculations without assuming any clusters or geometric configurations a priori. They analyzed the intrinsic density distributions via multipole decomposition to extract the \((Y_3^{-3}-Y_3^{+3})/\sqrt{2}\) component characterizing the surface density oscillation. Additionally, they introduced a schematic one-dimensional model of two clusters on a Fermi gas core in a finite box to study Pauli blocking effects and the transition between Fermi gas, BCS-like, DW-like, and BEC-like states.

## Key numbers

* Quadrupole deformation parameters for \(^{12}\)C ground state: \((\beta,\gamma) = (0.31, 0.13)\).
* Quadrupole deformation parameters for \(^{16}\)O ground state: \((\beta,\gamma) = (0.25, 0.09)\).
* Width parameter \(\nu = 0.19\) fm\(^{-2}\) used in the AMD calculation, corresponding to a size parameter \(b\) via \(\nu = 1/2b^2\).
* Radius \(R_0 = 2.53\) fm used for the multipole decomposition of the \(^{12}\)C intrinsic density.
* Radius \(R_0 = 2.81\) fm used for the multipole decomposition of the \(^{16}\)O intrinsic density.

## Context

This work extends previous cluster-model studies of \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O, such as those by Wheeler (1937) and Dennison (1954) using non-microscopic \(\alpha\)-cluster models, and by Brink (1970), Ikeda (1972), and Fujiwara (1980) using microscopic and semi-microscopic cluster models. The AMD+VAP results confirm the triangle and tetrahedral shapes suggested by earlier cluster models and provide a microscopic interpretation in terms of density wave correlations and Pauli blocking effects. The schematic one-dimensional model offers a framework to understand the transition from angular-correlated (DW-like) states to uncorrelated (BEC-like) cluster gas states, as seen in the \(0^+_1\) and \(0^+_2\) states of \(^{12}\)C, and may guide future studies of symmetry breaking and restoration in finite nuclear systems.
