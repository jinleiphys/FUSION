# Relativistic mean field study of the newly discovered $α$-decay chain of $^{287}$115

> The single particle structure of the low lying levels in the nuclei belonging to the newly observed $α$-decay chain starting from $^{287}$115 has been studied. Relativistic mean field calculation with two forces NL1 and NL3 has been performed in the blocked BCS approximation. Q-values for possible decay have been calculated and compared with experiment. Lifetime estimates obtained from phenomenological relations have also been compared.

# Relativistic mean field study of the newly discovered α-decay chain of ²⁸⁷115

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field calculation with NL1 and NL3 forces in the blocked BCS approximation reproduces the experimental Q-values for α-decay in the chain starting from ²⁸⁷115, particularly for transitions involving negative parity states calculated with the NL3 force, but the ground state spin-parity predictions are only correct or within approximately 0.5 MeV for the heaviest known odd-proton nuclei.

## Method

Relativistic mean field calculations were performed in the blocked BCS approximation using two parameter sets, NL1 and NL3, with 22 oscillator shells in both fermion and boson sectors and constant gap pairing given by Δₙ,ₚ = 11.2/√A. The space-like components of the meson fields were neglected as their contributions to binding energy and deformation are small. The one-quasiparticle levels near the ground state were calculated for each nucleus in the α-decay chain, and Q-values were computed assuming decays between states of the same parity and nearly equal spin.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation varies smoothly from approximately 0.28 to 0.17 with increasing mass number across the chain from ²⁶⁷Db to ²⁸⁷115, in contrast to a previous calculation by Geng et al. that found a sudden jump to 0.50 at ²⁸⁷115
- The Viola-Seaborg formula parameters used for lifetime estimates: a = 1.66175, b = -8.5166, c = -0.20228, d = -33.9069, with T_α in seconds and Q_α in MeV
- In ²⁸⁷115, the 11/2⁺ state is predicted to be an isomeric state in both NL1 and NL3 calculations
- The pairing gap is given by Δₙ,ₚ = 11.2/√A (no specific numerical values for individual nuclei provided in the text)

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean field studies of superheavy odd-proton nuclei, following earlier calculations by Ren et al. using TMA and NLZ2 forces, Rashdan using NLRA1 for ²⁷¹111, and Geng et al. using TMA force with blocking for the same decay chain. The present results differ from Geng et al. in finding a smooth deformation decrease rather than an abrupt jump at ²⁸⁷115, which is consistent with experimental lifetimes that do not show a large increase for decay from that nucleus. The calculations enable identification of possible isomeric states and decay paths in the chain, and suggest neutron shell closure at N = 172 and proton shell closure at Z = 120 in agreement with other relativistic calculations.
