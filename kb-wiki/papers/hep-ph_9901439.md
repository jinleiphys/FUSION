# Is there diquark clustering in the nucleon?

> It is shown that the instanton-induced interaction in qq pairs, iterated in t-channel, leads to a meson-exchange interactions between quarks. In this way one can achieve a simultaneous understanding of low-lying mesons, baryons and the nuclear force. The discussion is general and does not necessarily rely on the instanton-induced interaction. Any nonperturbative gluonic interaction between quarks, which is a source of the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and explains the $π$ - $ρ$ mass splitting, will imply an effective meson exchange picture in baryons. Due to the (anti)screening there is a big difference between the initial 't Hooft interaction and the effective meson-exchange interaction. It is demonstrated that the effective meson-exchange interaction, adjusted to the baryon spectrum, does not bind the scalar diquark and does not induce any significant quark-diquark clustering in the nucleon because of the nontrivial role played by the Pauli principle.

## Key claim

The effective meson-exchange interaction, derived from iterating the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction in the t-channel rather than the s-channel, does not bind a scalar diquark nor induce significant quark-diquark clustering in the nucleon, contrary to claims based on s-channel iteration. The Pauli principle plays a nontrivial role in preventing clustering even though the interaction is most attractive in the scalar diquark channel.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate analytically that iterating the 't Hooft four-fermion interaction in the quark-quark t-channel produces effective meson-exchange potentials (pion, sigma, eta, eta-prime) between constituent quarks, with the pion-exchange term becoming dominant due to antiscreening near the Goldstone boson pole. They then solve the semirelativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for the nucleon using a Hamiltonian that includes linear confinement plus the meson-exchange interaction, with parameters previously adjusted to reproduce the baryon spectrum in Glozman et al. (1998). The scalar diquark mass is computed from the same interaction in a color-antitriplet quark-quark system.

## Key numbers

- Scalar diquark mass: 744 MeV for the T=0, J^P=0+ state, which lies above the two-constituent-quark threshold (2 × 340 MeV = 680 MeV)
- Constituent quark mass: 340 MeV, fixed from the nucleon magnetic moment and consistent with lattice QCD results
- Instanton size r_0: between 0.3 and 0.35 fm, used in the regularization of the delta-function interaction
- The pion-exchange interaction matrix element in the most attractive channel (S_ij=0, T_ij=0) is -9 times the spin-isospin operator expectation value, compared to -1 in the S_ij=1, T_ij=1 channel

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange picture of baryon structure developed by Glozman and Riska (1996) and Glozman et al. (1998), which successfully explained light and strange baryon spectroscopy. It directly contradicts the s-channel diquark clustering picture advocated by Rapp et al. (1998) and Shuryak (1998) based on the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction, and also differs from the diquark-quark Faddeev approach in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model used by Vogl and Weise (1991) and Bentz et al. (1997). The result enables a unified understanding of mesons, baryons, and the nuclear force through effective meson exchange, and suggests that the transition to color-superconducting quark matter at high density may be shifted relative to predictions based on s-channel diquark binding.
