# Inferring the neutron star equation of state with nuclear-physics informed semiparametric models

> Over the past decade, an abundance of information from neutron-star observations, nuclear experiments and theory has transformed our efforts to elucidate the properties of dense matter. However, at high densities relevant to the cores of neutron stars, substantial uncertainty about the dense matter equation of state (EoS) remains. In this work, we present a semiparametric EoS framework aimed at better integrating knowledge across these domains in astrophysical inference. We use a Meta-model at low densities, and Gaussian Process extensions at high densities. Comparisons between our semiparametric framework to fully nonparametric EoS representations show that imposing nuclear theoretical and experimental constraints through the Meta-model up to nuclear saturation density results in constraints on the pressure up to twice nuclear saturation density. We show that our Gaussian Process trained on EoS models with nucleonic, hyperonic, and quark compositions extends the range of EoS explored at high density compared to a piecewise polytropic extension schema, under the requirements of causality of matter and of supporting the existence of heavy pulsars. We find that maximum TOV masses above $3.2 M_{\odot}$ can be supported by causal EoS compatible with nuclear constraints at low densities. We then combine information from existing observations of heavy pulsar masses, gravitational waves from binary neutron star mergers, and X-ray pulse profile modeling of millisecond pulsars within a Bayesian inference scheme using our semiparametric EoS prior. With current astrophysical observations, we find a favored pressure at two times nuclear saturation density of $P(2ρ_{\rm nuc}) = 1.98^{+2.13}_{-1.08}\times10^{34}$ dyn/cm$^{2}$, a radius of a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ neutron star value of $R_{1.4} = 11.4^{+0.98}_{-0.60}$\;km, and $M_{\rm max} = 2.31_{-0.23}^{+0.35} M_{\odot}$ at the 90\% credible level.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semiparametric framework that combines a nuclear-physics-informed Meta-model at low densities with Gaussian Process extensions at high densities, claiming that this approach better integrates nuclear experimental and theoretical constraints with astrophysical observations than fully nonparametric or piecewise polytropic methods, while still allowing for a broad range of high-density behaviors including phase transitions to exotic matter.

## Method

The authors construct a low-density equation of state (EoS) using the Meta-model approach of Margueron (2018), which employs a fourth-order Taylor expansion in isospin asymmetry parameterized by nuclear empirical parameters sampled from experimentally informed uniform priors and constrained by chiral effective field theory calculations up to nuclear saturation density. At densities above the transition density (0.16 fm^-3), they generate high-density EoS extensions using Gaussian Process regression trained on tabulated nuclear theory EoSs with nucleonic, hyperonic, and quark compositions, conditioning the GP on the Meta-model EoS at the stitching density. They then perform hierarchical Bayesian inference combining radio pulsar mass measurements, gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and GW190425, and X-ray pulse profile modeling from four NICER pulsars using the inference code LWP.

## Key numbers

- Pressure at two times nuclear saturation density: P(2ρ_nuc) = 1.98^{+2.13}_{-1.08} × 10^34 dyn/cm^2 at 90% credible level
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_1.4 = 11.4^{+0.98}_{-0.60} km at 90% credible level
- Maximum TOV mass: M_max = 2.31^{+0.35}_{-0.23} solar masses at 90% credible level
- Maximum TOV masses above 3.2 solar masses can be supported by causal EoS compatible with nuclear constraints at low densities
- Transition density ρ_nuc = 2.65 × 10^14 g/cm^3 corresponding to n_nuc = 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the approach of Davis (2024), which used piecewise polytropic extensions above the Meta-model, by replacing those with Gaussian Process extensions that allow for greater variability and can emulate EoSs with phase transitions to hyperonic and quark matter. The semiparametric framework bridges the gap between fully nonparametric approaches like those of Legred (2021) and parametric nuclear-physics-informed models, enabling future work to incorporate additional nuclear theory constraints and new astrophysical observations as they become available.
