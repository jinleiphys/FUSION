# Cross correlation of low-lying levels of even-even nuclei

> We consider the cross correlations of low-lying energy levels of nuclei belonging to small intervals of the excitation-energy ratio R_{4/2} of the first 4^+ to 2^+ states. The mean value of the cross-correlation coefficients, plotted as a function of R_{4/2} has deep minima at values of R_{4/2}=2.2 and 2.9, which correspond to the critical dynamical symmetries of the interacting Boson model. The distribution of the calculated coefficients for nuclei belonging to different R_{4/2} classes has different pattern.

## Key claim
The mean cross-correlation coefficient (CCC) of low-lying energy levels among even-even nuclei grouped by narrow intervals of the excitation-energy ratio \(R_{4/2}=E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)\) shows deep minima at \(R_{4/2}=2.2\) and \(2.9\), which correspond to the critical dynamical symmetries \(E(5)\) and \(X(5)\) of the interacting boson model (IBM), rather than to the standard IBM symmetry limits.

## Method
The authors compiled 4177 low-lying levels (spins 0 to 6, both parities) from 478 even-even nuclei using published data compilations. They grouped nuclei into 17 classes defined by narrow intervals of \(R_{4/2}\) (width 0.1 when at least 20 nuclei were available, wider otherwise). For each class, they constructed an \(18\times18\) cross-correlation matrix between all pairs of level types (e.g., \(2_1^+\), \(4_1^+\), etc.) and computed the mean value \(\langle C \rangle\) and standard deviation \(\sigma\) of the off-diagonal matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- The mean CCC \(\langle C \rangle\) has deep minima at \(R_{4/2}=2.10\pm0.05\) and \(R_{4/2}=2.85\pm1.0\), corresponding to the critical symmetries \(E(5)\) and \(X(5)\) respectively.
- The mean CCC has maxima at \(R_{4/2}=2.0\), \(2.5\), and \(3.3\), corresponding to the IBM dynamical symmetries \(U(5)\), \(SO(6)\), and \(SU(3)\).
- About 80% of the CCC values for classes at the maxima are greater than 0.95, and practically none are less than 0.75.
- The data set comprises 4177 levels from 478 nuclei.

## Context
This work extends prior statistical analyses of level spacings for \(2^+\) states in even-even nuclei (Simbel and Abul-Magd, 2004), which found minima in the chaoticity parameter at \(R_{4/2}=2.0\), \(2.5\), and \(3.3\). It also builds on the empirical linear relation between \(E(4_1^+)\) and \(E(2_1^+)\) observed by Casten et al. (1985) and Zamfir et al. (1993). The results provide a new, model-independent criterion for identifying candidate nuclei at the critical points of shape phase transitions, complementing comparisons with the IBM predictions for \(E(5)\) and \(X(5)\) symmetries introduced by Iachello (2000, 2001). The authors note that analysis of transition strengths, such as E0 transitions (von Brentano et al., 2004), is needed for a fuller test.
