# Massive photons: an infrared regularization scheme for lattice QCD+QED

> Standard methods for including electromagnetic interactions in lattice quantum chromodynamics calculations result in power-law finite-volume corrections to physical quantities. Removing these by extrapolation requires costly computations at multiple volumes. We introduce a photon mass to alternatively regulate the infrared, and rely on effective field theory to remove its unphysical effects. Electromagnetic modifications to the hadron spectrum are reliably estimated with a precision and cost comparable to conventional approaches that utilize multiple larger volumes. A significant overall cost advantage emerges when accounting for ensemble generation. The proposed method may benefit lattice calculations involving multiple charged hadrons, as well as quantum many-body computations with long-range Coulomb interactions.

# Massive Photons: An Infrared Regularization Scheme for Lattice QCD+QED

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that introducing a photon mass as an infrared regulator for lattice QCD+QED calculations yields hadron mass splittings consistent with conventional massless-photon methods, but can be performed at a single lattice volume rather than requiring multiple volumes for extrapolation. The method is validated for photon masses up to $m_\gamma/\bar m_\pi \lesssim 1$, provided $m_\gamma L \gtrsim 1$ and $m_\pi L \gtrsim 4$, with the caveat that dynamical pions must be accounted for when $m_\gamma \gtrsim 2 m_\pi$.

## Method

The authors perform electroquenched lattice QCD+QED calculations using dynamical SU(3) flavor-symmetric QCD configurations at a single lattice spacing $a = 0.1453(16)$ fm, with three spatial extents ($L\sim 3.48$, $4.64$, and $6.96$ fm). Photon fields are generated using two actions: a conventional massless Coulomb gauge-fixed action (QED$_{TL}$) and a massive photon action (QED$_{M}$) based on a Landau-gauge-fixed $R_\xi$ Lagrangian with a photon mass term. Hadron mass differences are extracted from correlation functions using effective mass formulas that nonperturbatively account for the zero mode of the temporal photon field, then extrapolated to infinite volume or zero photon mass using analytic expressions from a nonrelativistic effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a = 0.1453(16)$ fm
- Pion/kaon mass: $\bar m_\pi = \bar m_K = 807.0(9.1)$ MeV
- Nucleon mass: $\bar m_n = 1.634(18)$ GeV
- Photon mass values used: $m_\gamma/\bar m_\pi \in [1/14, 1/7, 1/4, 1/3, 5/12, 1/2, 7/12, 1]$
- Proton-neutron mass splitting from QED$_{TL}$ infinite-volume extrapolation: $\Delta M/M = 0.73(05) \times 10^{-3}$ ($K_L=1$)
- Proton-neutron mass splitting from QED$_{M}$ zero-photon-mass extrapolation at $L/a=24$: $\Delta M/M = 0.79(06) \times 10^{-3}$ ($m_\gamma/\bar m_\pi$ range 1/14-1, $K_\gamma=2$)
- Kaon mass splitting from QED$_{TL}$ infinite-volume extrapolation: $\Delta M/M = 3.71(06) \times 10^{-3}$ ($K_L=1$)
- Kaon mass splitting from QED$_{M}$ zero-photon-mass extrapolation at $L/a=24$: $\Delta M/M = 3.77(06) \times 10^{-3}$ ($m_\gamma/\bar m_\pi$ range 1/14-1, $K_\gamma=2$)
- Computational cost ratio: $L/a=32$ inversion cost is approximately 1.3 times that of $L/a=24$; $L/a=48$ cost is approximately 3.8 times that of $L/a=24$

## Context

This work extends the conventional approach to including QED in lattice QCD, which uses massless photons and requires extrapolation over multiple volumes to remove power-law finite-volume corrections as developed by Davoudi (2014), Borsanyi (2014), and Fodor (2015). The massive photon method provides an alternative infrared regulator that maintains locality and is perturbatively renormalizable, similar in spirit to the QED$_L$ formulation of Patella (2015) and Lucini (2015). The results enable more efficient calculations of electromagnetic corrections to hadron masses, and potentially to charged-particle scattering where the photon mass gap extends the range of applicability of the Luscher method for extracting scattering phase shifts.
