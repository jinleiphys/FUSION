# Medium effect in high-density nuclear matter probed by systematic analyses of nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering

> We investigate the property of the high-density nuclear matter probed by the nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering in the framework of the double-folding (DF) model with the complex $G$-matrix interaction. The medium effect including three-body-force (TBF) effect is investigated with present two methods based on the frozen density approximation (FDA). The medium effect is clearly seen on the potential and the elastic cross section for the $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O system at $E/A =$ 70 MeV. The crucial role of the medium effect is also confirmed with other effective nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions. In addition, the present methods are applied to other heavy-ion elastic scattering systems. Again, the medium effect is clearly seen in the heavy-ion elastic cross section. The medium effect on the elastic cross section becomes invisible with the increase of the target mass and the incident energy (up to $E/A =$ 200 MeV). However, the medium effect is again important to fix the heavy-ion scattering over $E/A =$ 200 MeV. Finally, we make clear the crucial role of the TBF effect up to $k_F =$ 1.6 fm$^{-1}$ in the nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The medium effect in high-density nuclear matter, including the three-body-force (TBF) effect, is clearly observable in the elastic cross section of nucleus-nucleus scattering up to Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.6\) fm\(^{-1}\), but only for specific systems and energies; the effect becomes invisible with increasing target mass and incident energy up to \(E/A = 200\) MeV, yet reappears as important for fixing the scattering above \(E/A = 200\) MeV.

## Method

The authors used the double-folding (DF) model with a complex \(G\)-matrix interaction based on the ESC08 two-body interaction supplemented by the multi-Pomeron exchange potential (MPP) for the three-body-force effect, within the frozen density approximation (FDA). Two methods were introduced: a "cutting method" that artificially limits the local density to a maximum value \(\rho_{\text{cut}}\) (parameterized by \(k_{\text{cut}}\)), and a "replacement method" that switches from the interaction with TBF to the interaction without TBF when the local density exceeds a threshold \(\rho_{\text{rep.}}\) (parameterized by \(k_{\text{rep.}}\)). The DF potentials and elastic cross sections were calculated for the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{16}\text{O}\) system at \(E/A = 70\) MeV and for other heavy-ion systems at various energies, and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{16}\text{O}\) system at \(E/A = 70\) MeV, the medium effect is clearly seen in the elastic cross section up to \(k_F = 1.60\) fm\(^{-1}\) (cutting method) and the TBF effect is clearly seen up to \(k_F = 1.65\) fm\(^{-1}\) (replacement method).
- The total reaction cross section for the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{16}\text{O}\) system at \(E/A = 70\) MeV shows almost no sensitivity to the medium effect, varying only from 1428 mb (FDA) to 1434 mb (cutting at \(k_{\text{cut}} = 1.40\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- The incompressibility \(K\) values for the \(G\)-matrix interactions used are: MPa = 283 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.154\) fm\(^{-3}\), MPb = 254 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.154\) fm\(^{-3}\), MPc = 243 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.156\) fm\(^{-3}\), CEG07b = 197 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.160\) fm\(^{-3}\), and CDM3Y6 = 252 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy \(E_{sym}\) and slope parameter \(L\) for the MPa (MPb) interaction are 31.7 (31.7) MeV and 67.1 (66.7) MeV, respectively.
- The TBF effect is crucial to reproduce the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{16}\text{O}\) elastic scattering data at backward angles at \(E/A = 70\) MeV; without TBF, the calculated cross section fails to match the data.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Furumoto et al. (2014) that introduced the cutting and replacement methods but only applied them to the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{16}\text{O}\) system at \(E/A = 70\) MeV. The present paper systematically applies these methods to multiple target nuclei (\(^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{28}\text{Si}\), \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), \(^{90}\text{Zr}\), \(^{208}\text{Pb}\)) and a range of incident energies (up to \(E/A = 400\) MeV), confirming the crucial role of the TBF effect up to \(k_F = 1.6\) fm\(^{-1}\). The results enable future studies to link heavy-ion elastic scattering observables to the equation of state of high-density nuclear matter, particularly the stiffness relevant for neutron-star properties, building on the MPP model of Yamamoto et al. (2013, 2014) and the complex \(G\)-matrix approach of Furumoto et al. (2009).
