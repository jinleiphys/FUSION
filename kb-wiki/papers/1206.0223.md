# Calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states based on chiral meson-baryon amplitudes

> In-medium ${\bar K}N$ scattering amplitudes developed within a new chirally motivated coupled-channel model due to Cieply and Smejkal that fits the recent SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift and width are used to construct $K^-$ nuclear potentials for calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states. The strong energy and density dependence of scattering amplitudes at and near threshold leads to $K^-$ potential depths $-Re V_K \approx 80 -120$ MeV. Self-consistent calculations of all $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states, including excited states, are reported. Model dependence, polarization effects, the role of p-wave interactions, and two-nucleon $K^-NN\rightarrow YN$ absorption modes are discussed. The $K^-$ absorption widths $Γ_K$ are comparable or even larger than the corresponding binding energies $B_K$ for all $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states, exceeding considerably the level spacing. This discourages search for $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states in any but lightest nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong energy and density dependence of in-medium $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes, when treated self-consistently, produces $K^-$ nuclear potentials with depths of 80–120 MeV, but that the resulting absorption widths are comparable to or larger than the binding energies for all quasi-bound states, which discourages experimental searches except in the lightest nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors constructed $K^-$ nuclear potentials from in-medium $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes using a chirally motivated coupled-channel separable interaction model (versions NLO30, TW1, and CS30) that reproduces the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift and width. They solved the Klein–Gordon equation self-consistently for $K^-$ quasi-bound states in selected nuclei across the periodic table, incorporating subthreshold energy extrapolations, Pauli blocking, hadron self-energies, p-wave interactions, and phenomenological two-nucleon absorption terms.

## Key numbers

- $K^-$ potential depths: $-{\rm Re}V_K \approx 80$–$120$ MeV, depending on the chiral model and in-medium treatment.
- Binding energies $B_K$ of $1s$ states in Ca: 70.5 MeV (static) and 72.3 MeV (dynamical) using NLO30 `+SE' amplitudes.
- Widths $\Gamma_K$ of $1s$ states in Ca: 14.9 MeV (static) and 14.8 MeV (dynamical) using NLO30 `+SE' amplitudes, without two-nucleon absorption.
- After including two-nucleon absorption, total widths in Ca exceed 50 MeV for all states (e.g., $1s$: 58.9 MeV, $1p$: 53.6 MeV, $1d$: 59.7 MeV, $2s$: 67.1 MeV).
- Model dependence: differences between chiral models (TW1, CS30, NLO30) yield up to 20 MeV variation in binding energies and up to 10 MeV in widths.
- Polarization effects increase $B_K$ by $\sim 6$ MeV in Li but less than 2 MeV in Ca and less than 0.5 MeV in Pb.
- p-wave interaction increases $B_K$ by only a few MeV, with at most $\sim 2$ MeV in Pb.

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent calculations by Gazda, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2006–2009) that used relativistic mean-field models with phenomenological imaginary potentials fitted to kaonic atom data, and by Weise and Härtle (2008) who used chiral-model amplitudes in a local density approximation for $^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb. The present study applies the new NLO30 chiral model fitted to SIDDHARTA data and systematically evaluates model dependence, polarization, p-wave effects, and two-nucleon absorption across all quasi-bound states. The results discourage experimental searches for $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states in any but the lightest nuclear systems, guiding future experimental efforts toward very light nuclei such as Li.
