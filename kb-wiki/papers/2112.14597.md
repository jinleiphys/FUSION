# Resummed relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics

> In this review, we present the motivation for using relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics to study the physics of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We then highlight the main ingredients of the 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics model and present phenomenological comparisons with experimental data at different collision energies. These comparisons show that anisotropic hydrodynamics can describe many bulk observables of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

This review argues that anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) provides a resummed relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic framework that automatically includes an infinite series of terms in the inverse Reynolds number, thereby avoiding the breakdown of fixed-order viscous hydrodynamics at early times in heavy-ion collisions when the system is far from equilibrium.

## Method

The authors present the 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model, which assumes a system of massive relativistic quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses determined from lattice QCD. The evolution equations are obtained from the first and second moments of the quasiparticle Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, using an ansatz for the one-particle distribution function parameterized by three independent momentum-anisotropy parameters and a non-equilibrium momentum scale. The model is solved numerically and coupled to a modified version of THERMINATOR 2 for hadronization via an extended Cooper-Frye prescription.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature for the QCD deconfinement transition: \(T_{\rm pc} \simeq 155\) MeV from continuum extrapolated lattice QCD
- Initial temperature \(T_0 = 455\) MeV for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV
- Initial temperature \(T_0 = 600\) MeV for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV
- Initial temperature \(T_0 = 630\) MeV for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV
- Specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 0.179\) for 200 GeV collisions
- Specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 0.159\) for both 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV collisions
- At high temperatures (\(T \sim 0.6\) GeV), the scaled quasiparticle mass is \(\sim T\)
- The aHydro model reproduces experimental spectra up to \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV for pions, kaons, and protons
- The \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) ratio from HBT radii is described well for low \(k_T\) up to \(\sim 0.4\) GeV

## Context

This review extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and subsequent authors, which addresses the failure of second-order viscous hydrodynamics at early times when shear corrections become large. The aHydro approach resums viscous contributions to all orders in the inverse Reynolds number, in contrast to fixed-order truncations by Denicol (2012) and Jaiswal (2013). The work enables phenomenological comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC collaborations including PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR, and ALICE, demonstrating that aHydroQP can describe bulk observables such as particle spectra, elliptic flow, charged particle multiplicity, and HBT radii across multiple collision energies.
