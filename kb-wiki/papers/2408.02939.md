# A new method to clarify contribution of chiral magnetic effect in small collision system $p^{\uparrow} + A$ involving a transversely polarized proton

> In this paper, we propose a new experiment method to check contribution of chiral magnetic effect (CME). With experimental data of DIS involving transversely polarized proton, we have calculated the 3-D charge density inside the polarized proton, which is found to have a significant violation to spherical symmetry. Then we have calculated the property of electromagnetic field (E-M field) generated by a single transversely polarized proton ($p^{\uparrow}$). Based on them, the E-M field generated in small collision system $p^{\uparrow}+A$ are studied. We find that the orientation of this E-M field has a significant dependence on the polarization direction of the proton, and the correlator ($Δγ$ ) has also significant dependence on the angle between reaction plane and polarization direction. As background contribution are canceled comparing two collision geometry schemes, only contribution of CME is remained.

# A new method to clarify contribution of chiral magnetic effect in small collision system \(p^{\uparrow} + A\) involving a transversely polarized proton

## Key claim

The authors propose that in \(p^{\uparrow}+A\) collisions with a transversely polarized proton, the difference in the CME correlator \(\Delta\gamma\) between two specific collision geometry schemes (scheme II and scheme IV) isolates the chiral magnetic effect contribution, because background flow effects cancel completely when taking this difference, even if the background is not negligible.

## Method

The authors first compute the 3-dimensional charge density of a transversely polarized proton using experimental data from deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on polarized nucleons, finding a significant violation of spherical symmetry. They then calculate the electromagnetic field generated by a single polarized proton flying at 100 GeV using the Lienard-Wiechert potential, and extend this to \(p^{\uparrow}+\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV simulated with the HIJING model event-by-event. The CME correlator \(\Delta\gamma \propto B^2 \cdot \cos[2(\Phi_B - \Phi_2)]\) is evaluated for four collision geometry schemes defined by the angle between the reaction plane and the polarization direction.

## Key numbers

- The polarized proton is set to fly at 100 GeV (RHIC energy scale) along the \(+z\) direction.
- The \(p^{\uparrow}+\)Au collision system is studied at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
- The typical proton radius used for reference is \(r = 0.85\) fm.
- The difference in CME correlators between scheme II and scheme IV increases with impact parameter and reaches the same order as in Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zhang et al., 2021) of electromagnetic fields in small p+A systems using a Charge-Profile model, now incorporating transversely polarized protons. The method addresses the long-standing challenge of separating CME signals from QCD background effects (particularly flow \(v_2\)) in heavy-ion collisions, as discussed by Xu (2017), Adamczyk (2013), and others. The proposed technique enables future experimental tests at RHIC using Zero Degree Calorimeters to distinguish the two geometry schemes, potentially clarifying the CME contribution in small collision systems where QGP may be produced.
