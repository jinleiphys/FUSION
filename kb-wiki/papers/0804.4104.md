# Hydro-kinetic approach to relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We develop a combined hydro-kinetic approach which incorporates a hydrodynamical expansion of the systems formed in \textit{A}+\textit{A} collisions and their dynamical decoupling described by escape probabilities. The method corresponds to a generalized relaxation time ($τ_{\text{rel}}$) approximation for the Boltzmann equation applied to inhomogeneous expanding systems; at small $τ_{\text{rel}}$ it also allows one to catch the viscous effects in hadronic component - hadron-resonance gas. We demonstrate how the approximation of sudden freeze-out can be obtained within this dynamical picture of continuous emission and find that hypersurfaces, corresponding to a sharp freeze-out limit, are momentum dependent. The pion $m_{T}$ spectra are computed in the developed hydro-kinetic model, and compared with those obtained from ideal hydrodynamics with the Cooper-Frye isothermal prescription. Our results indicate that there does not exist a universal freeze-out temperature for pions with different momenta, and support an earlier decoupling of higher $p_{T}$ particles. By performing numerical simulations for various initial conditions and equations of state we identify several characteristic features of the bulk QCD matter evolution preferred in view of the current analysis of heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper develops a hydro-kinetic approach that replaces the sudden freeze-out approximation with a continuous emission picture, showing that the Cooper-Frye prescription can be recovered as a limiting case only when the emission function has a sharp temporal maximum and the relaxation time is much smaller than the temporal homogeneity length of the distribution function. The authors find that there is no universal freeze-out temperature for pions with different momenta, and that higher transverse momentum particles decouple earlier.

## Method

The authors formulate a generalized relaxation time approximation for the Boltzmann equation applied to inhomogeneous expanding systems formed in heavy ion collisions, where the relaxation time tends to infinity at late times to describe the transition to free streaming. They decompose the distribution function into a local equilibrium part and a deviation term, then derive hydrodynamic equations with source terms that account for the back reaction of particle emission on the fluid evolution. The momentum spectra are computed by integrating the emission density over space-time, with the escape probability expressed as an exponential of the integrated collision rate along the particle's world line.

## Key numbers

- The temporal width of the emission zone at freeze-out is approximately equal to the relaxation time: \( D(\mathbf{r}, p) \simeq \tau_{\text{rel}}(t_\sigma, \mathbf{r}, p) \)
- The freeze-out hypersurface is defined by the condition \( d\tau_{\text{rel}}/dt' = 1 \) at the point of maximum emission
- The heuristic freeze-out criterion \( \tau_{\text{scat}} \approx \tau_{\text{exp}} \) is recovered from the saddle-point condition when momentum dependence and deviations from local equilibrium are neglected

## Context

This work extends the continuous decoupling approach introduced by Hama et al. (2005) and the Boltzmann-equation-based method of Akkelin and Sinyukov (2005) by providing a systematic formalism that accounts for the back reaction of emission on fluid dynamics. The paper addresses inconsistencies in the Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper and Frye, 1974) for freeze-out hypersurfaces containing non-space-like sectors, as previously noted by Bugaev (1996) and Sinyukov et al. (1994). The results enable more realistic modeling of hadronic emission from relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies, particularly for understanding how different momentum particles decouple at different times and temperatures.
