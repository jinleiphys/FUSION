# Possible Color Octet Quark-Anti-Quark Condensate in the Instanton Model

> Inspired by a recent proposal for a Higgs description of QCD we study the possible formation of a color-octet/flavor-octet quark-anti-quark condensate in the instanton liquid model. For this purpose we calculate two-point correlation functions of color-singlet and octet quark-anti-quark operators. We find long range order in the standard $<\barψψ>$ channel, but not in the color-octet channel. We emphasize that similar calculations in lattice QCD can check whether or not a color-flavor locked Higgs phase is realized in QCD at zero temperature and baryon density.

# Possible Color Octet Quark-Anti-Quark Condensate in the Instanton Model

## Key claim

The instanton liquid model shows no evidence for long-range order in the color-octet/flavor-octet quark-anti-quark correlation function at zero temperature and baryon density, contradicting the proposal that a color-octet condensate could serve as a Higgs field for QCD confinement. However, the author emphasizes that lattice QCD calculations of the same correlation functions could provide a definitive test, as the instanton model calculation is not a full solution of QCD.

## Method

The author calculated two-point correlation functions of color-singlet and color-octet quark-anti-quark operators in the instanton liquid model, using both quenched and unquenched simulations. The correlation functions were computed from quark propagators in the instanton background, with the color-octet/flavor-octet correlator defined to avoid gauge-dependent issues by studying its asymptotic behavior rather than the condensate itself. Additional calculations were performed with explicit flavor symmetry breaking by varying the quark mass differences among up, down, and strange quarks.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate in the quenched instanton liquid: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ ≈ -(250 MeV)³
- Chiral condensate in the unquenched instanton liquid: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ ≈ -(220 MeV)³
- Screening mass of the color-octet/flavor-octet correlator in the unquenched calculation: 700 MeV
- Screening mass of the color-singlet/flavor-octet correlator in the unquenched calculation: consistent with 1 GeV
- Flavor symmetry breaking parameter values tested: δm = 0.0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3 fm⁻¹, with fixed m₀ = 0.1 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work tests the Higgs description of QCD proposed by Wetterich (1999, 2000) and Berges (2000), in which a color-octet quark-anti-quark condensate spontaneously breaks SU(3) gauge symmetry. The calculation extends the instanton liquid model previously used by Schafer (1998) and Diakonov (1995) for chiral symmetry breaking, and by Rapp (1998) and Alford (1998) for diquark condensation at high density. The results contradict the Higgs scenario at zero density but suggest that lattice QCD calculations of the same correlation functions could resolve the question definitively.
