# Schrödinger cat state of trapped ions in harmonic and anharmonic oscillator traps

> We examine the time evolution of a two level ion interacting with a light field in harmonic oscillator trap and in a trap with anharmonicities. The anharmonicities of the trap are quantified in terms of the deformation parameter $τ$ characterizing the q-analog of the harmonic oscillator trap. Initially the ion is prepared in a Schrödinger cat state. The entanglement of the center of mass motional states and the internal degrees of freedom of the ion results in characteristic collapse and revival pattern. We calculate numerically the population inversion I(t), quasi-probabilities $Q(t),$ and partial mutual quantum entropy S(P), for the system as a function of time. Interestingly, small deformations of the trap enhance the contrast between population inversion collapse and revival peaks as compared to the zero deformation case. For β=3 and $4,(% β$ determines the average number of trap quanta linked to center of mass motion) the best collapse and revival sequence is obtained for τ=0.0047 and τ=0.004 respectively. For large values of τdecoherence sets in accompanied by loss of amplitude of population inversion and for τ\sim 0.1 the collapse and revival phenomenon disappear. Each collapse or revival of population inversion is characterized by a peak in S(P) versus t plot. During the transition from collapse to revival and vice-versa we have minimum mutual entropy value that is S(P)=0. Successive revival peaks show a lowering of the local maximum point indicating a dissipative irreversible change in the ionic state. Improved definition of collapse and revival pattern as the anharminicity of the trapping potential increases is also reflected in the Quasi- probability versus t plots.

# Schrödinger cat state of trapped ions in harmonic and anharmonic oscillator traps

## Key claim

Small deformations of the trapping potential, quantified by the deformation parameter τ (where q = e^τ), can enhance the contrast between collapse and revival peaks in population inversion for a two-level ion initially prepared in a Schrödinger cat state, but only up to a specific optimal τ value; for τ ~ 0.1 the collapse and revival phenomenon disappears entirely.

## Method

The authors model the anharmonic trap using a q-deformed harmonic oscillator, where the deformation parameter τ characterizes the anharmonicity (with x^6 anharmonicities for small τ). They numerically solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a two-level ion interacting with a classical light field, starting from an initial Schrödinger cat state where the ion has equal probability of being in ground or excited state coupled to coherent states of opposite phase. They compute population inversion I(t), quasi-probabilities Q(t), and partial mutual quantum entropy S(P) as functions of time for various τ values with β = 3 and 4 (where β determines the average number of trap quanta in the center-of-mass motion).

## Key numbers

- For β = 3, the best collapse and revival sequence is obtained at τ = 0.0047
- For β = 4, the best collapse and revival sequence is obtained at τ = 0.004
- For τ ~ 0.1, the collapse and revival phenomenon disappears
- The numerical calculation used parameters: ω/Ω = 50, ε/Ω = 0.05, Δ/Ω = -50, with maximum n restricted to n = 32
- For β = 3 and τ = 0.0047, successive revival peaks in S(P) occur at t = 58.6 (S(P) = 0.628), t = 175.6 (S(P) = 0.285), t = 295.6 (S(P) = 0.181), and t = 415.4 (S(P) = 0.175)
- For β = 4 and τ = 0.004, successive revival peaks in S(P) occur at t = 67.8 (S(P) = 0.731), t = 201.2 (S(P) = 0.512), t = 336.8 (S(P) = 0.397), and t = 472.6 (S(P) = 0.324)
- For β = 4 and τ = 0.0 (harmonic trap), revival peaks in S(P) occur at t = 85.8 (S(P) = 0.333) and t = 266.8 (S(P) = 0.106)
- The initial state S(P) value is 0.693 for all cases
- During transitions from collapse to revival and vice versa, S(P) = 0

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Sharma 1997) on ions in harmonic and anharmonic traps, where they examined collapses and revivals for an initial state with the ion in its ground state and center-of-mass motion described by a coherent state. The present work instead uses an initial Schrödinger cat state and introduces partial mutual quantum entropy as a measure of entanglement. The q-deformed oscillator approach follows the formalism of Macfarlane (1989) and Biedenharn (1989), with the deformation parameter τ modeling anharmonicities that Bonatsos et al. (1994, 1999) showed correspond to x^6 anharmonicities. The study enables understanding of how controlled trap anharmonicity could improve coherence properties for potential applications in quantum computation and quantum communication, where entanglement of ionic internal states with center-of-mass motion may be used to encode information, as discussed by Bennett et al. (1992, 1993) and Ekert (1991, 1992).
