# Electromagnetic and transport properties of QGP within PLSM approach

> In order to study the response of the quantum chromodynamic matter to finite electromagnetic fields, we utilize the Polyakov linear - sigma model (PLSM) in mean-field approximation. Due to participants' momentum imbalance and off-center relativistic motion of the spectators' electric charges, localized, short-lived, huge electromagnetic fields are to be generated in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We report on various electromagnetic and transport properties of the new-state-of matter; the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) within the QCD-like approach, PLSM. We find an excellent agreement between our PLSM calculations and various recent lattice QCD simulations and notice that the magnetization and the magnetic susceptibility and the relative permeability obviously increase in the QGP phase. We predict that increasing the magnetic field remarkable decreases the viscosity, especially in hadron phase. while in QGP phase, the viscous properties seem not being affected with.

## Key claim
The Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) in mean-field approximation shows excellent agreement with recent lattice QCD simulations for electromagnetic and transport properties of QGP, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the model predicts that increasing the magnetic field remarkably decreases viscosity only in the hadron phase, while in the QGP phase the viscous properties seem not to be affected.

## Method
The authors utilized the Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) in mean-field approximation, implementing magnetic catalysis and Landau quantization with the magnetic field aligned along the z-direction. They computed the free energy density from the PLSM potential, then derived magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and relative magnetic permeability from its derivatives. Transport coefficients (bulk and shear viscosity) were obtained from the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equations using the relaxation time approximation and Chapman-Enskog expansion.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC, the magnetic field strength is approximately |q|B ≈ mπ², while at LHC energies, |q|B ≈ 10-15 m²π, where mπ is the pion mass.
- The magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and relative permeability were calculated at μ=0 and eB=0.2 GeV².
- Shear viscosity (η/s) and bulk viscosity (ζ/s) were calculated at μ=0 and at eB=0.0 GeV², eB=0.2 GeV², and eB=0.4 GeV².
- Lattice QCD simulations show various thermodynamic quantities including viscosity are 15-20% below the Boltzmann limit even at temperatures greater than the critical temperature.

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD matter under extreme electromagnetic fields, building on the discovery at RHIC of strongly correlated QGP matter (Gyulassy 2004) and lattice QCD simulations showing viscosity below the Boltzmann limit (Philipsen 2012). The paper confronts PLSM calculations to recent lattice QCD simulations (lattice 2014) and enables predictions for how huge magnetic fields (~10¹⁹ Gauss) generated in heavy-ion collisions affect the transport properties of hadron and parton matter, with implications for early Universe physics, magnetars, and cosmic ray acceleration.
