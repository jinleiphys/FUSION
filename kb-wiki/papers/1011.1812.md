# Generalized parton distributions and the parton structure of light nuclei

> The measurement of nuclear Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) represents a valuable tool to understand the structure of bound nucleons and the phenomenology of hard scattering off nuclei. By using a realistic, non-relativistic microscopic approach for the evaluation of GPDs of 3He, it will be shown that conventional nuclear effects, such as isospin and binding ones, or the uncertainty related to the use of a given nucleon-nucleon potential, are bigger than in the forward case so that, if great attention is not paid, conventional nuclear effects can be easily mistaken for exotic ones. It is stressed that 3He, for which the best realistic calculations are possible, represents a unique target to discriminate between conventional and exotic effects. The complementary information which could be obtained by using a 3H target, the possible extraction of the neutron information, as well as the relevance of a relativistic treatment, will be also addressed.

# Generalized parton distributions and the parton structure of light nuclei

## Key claim

Conventional nuclear effects (isospin, binding, and nucleon-nucleon potential dependence) on the generalized parton distributions of 3He are larger than in the forward (parton distribution) case, and can reach 10% differences between flavors; if not carefully accounted for, these conventional effects can be mistaken for exotic medium modifications of nucleons.

## Method

The author evaluates the unpolarized quark GPD H_q^3 of 3He using a realistic non-relativistic impulse approximation (IA) with a non-diagonal spectral function built from AV18 interaction wave functions. The nucleon GPDs are modeled via the Double Distribution representation, and the calculation is performed in the nuclear Breit frame for kinematics where Δ^2 ≪ Q^2, M^2 and ξ is small. The full result is compared to a factorized approximation where the Δ^2 dependence is given by the nuclear form factor, and the ratio R_q(x,ξ,Δ^2) is used to isolate nuclear effects.

## Key numbers

- The removal energy E_min = |E_3He| - |E_2H| = 5.5 MeV, used in the spectral function definition.
- At Δ^2 = -0.25 GeV^2 and ξ_3 = 3ξ = 0.2, the difference between the ratio R_q for d and u flavors is of order 10%.
- Binding effects (difference between full spectral function and momentum distribution with closure approximation) grow from a few percent in the forward limit to 5-10% between x = 0.4 and 0.7 at Δ^2 = -0.25 GeV^2 and ξ_3 = 0.2.
- The IA calculation reproduces nuclear form factor data up to -Δ^2 = 0.25 GeV^2, which is the relevant range for coherent processes.
- The super-ratio S_du for 3He and 3H targets shows deviations from unity of only a few percent at low Δ^2 and ξ (Δ^2 ≤ 0.25 GeV^2), with the difference increasing with Δ^2 and ξ.

## Context

This work extends the IA calculation of GPDs for 3He from Scopetta (2009) to include flavor dependence, binding effects, and dependence on the nucleon-nucleon potential (AV18 vs AV14). It builds on earlier studies of nuclear GPDs by Cano and Pire (2004) and by Liuti and Taneja (2005), and on the use of trinucleon targets for extracting neutron information proposed by Thomas (1988). The results show that nuclear GPDs cannot be trivially inferred from nuclear parton distributions, and that 3He is a unique target for discriminating conventional from exotic effects because its flavor dependence cannot be studied with isoscalar targets like 2H or 4He. The work also extends the super-ratio method of Thomas (1988) from unpolarized PDFs to GPDs for simultaneous 3He and 3H measurements, which could be pursued after the 12 GeV upgrade at JLab.
