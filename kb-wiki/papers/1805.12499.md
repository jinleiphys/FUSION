# Experimental and theoretical study of deuteron-proton elastic scattering for proton kinetic energies between $T_p = 882.2\;\textrm{MeV}$ and $T_p = 918.3\;\textrm{MeV}$

> New precise unpolarised differential cross sections of deuteron-proton elastic scattering have been measured at 16 different deuteron beam momenta between $p_d = 3120.17\;\textrm{MeV}/c$ and $p_d =3204.16\;\textrm{MeV}/c$ at the COoler SYnchrotron COSY of the Forschungszentrum Jülich. The data, which were taken using the magnetic spectrometer ANKE, cover the equivalent range in proton kinetic energies from $T_p = 882.2\;\textrm{MeV}$ to $T_p = 918.3\;\textrm{MeV}$. The experimental results are analysed theoretically using the Glauber diffraction model with accurate nucleon-nucleon input. The theoretical cross section at $T_p = 900\;\textrm{MeV}$ agrees very well with the experimental one at low momentum transfers $|t| <0.2\;(\textrm{GeV}/c)^2$.

## Key claim

The refined Glauber model, using accurate nucleon-nucleon (NN) amplitudes from the SAID partial-wave analysis, describes the new precise differential cross sections very well only at low momentum transfers \(|t| < 0.2\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\); at higher momentum transfers the model begins to deviate from experiment, likely due to the failure of small-momentum-transfer approximations or missing three-nucleon force contributions.

## Method

Unpolarised deuteron-proton elastic scattering differential cross sections were measured at 16 fixed deuteron beam momenta between 3120.17 MeV/\(c\) and 3204.16 MeV/\(c\) using the ANKE magnetic spectrometer at the COSY synchrotron, with a hydrogen cluster-jet target. Forward-going deuterons were detected in the Forward detection system, and events were selected in the momentum transfer range \(0.08 < |t| < 0.26\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\) with a bin width of \(\Delta t = 0.01\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\). The experimental results were compared to calculations from the refined Glauber model, which fully includes spin degrees of freedom, the \(D\)-wave component of the deuteron wave function, and double-charge-exchange processes, using NN amplitudes from the SAID partial-wave analysis solutions SM16 and WF16.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections were measured at 16 deuteron beam momenta from \(p_d = 3120.17\;\text{MeV}/c\) to \(p_d = 3204.16\;\text{MeV}/c\), corresponding to proton kinetic energies \(T_p = 882.2\;\text{MeV}\) to \(T_p = 918.3\;\text{MeV}\).
- The averaged differential cross section at \(|t| = 0.085\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\) is \(29898 \pm 193\;\mu\text{b}/(\text{GeV}/c)^2\); at \(|t| = 0.255\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\) it is \(1379 \pm 20\;\mu\text{b}/(\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
- The theoretical cross section at \(T_p = 900\;\text{MeV}\) agrees with experiment for \(|t| < 0.2\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\); the difference between the WF16 and SM16 NN inputs changes the predicted cross section by 1% to 3% in the range \(0.08 < |t| < 0.26\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
- The calculated difference between cross sections at \(T_p = 800\;\text{MeV}\) and \(T_p = 1000\;\text{MeV}\) reaches 13% at \(|t| = 0.2\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\); the integrated cross section over \(0 < |t| < 0.2\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\) increases by 4% from 800 to 1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the sparse experimental database for deuteron-proton elastic scattering at momentum transfers \(|t| > 0.1\;(\text{GeV}/c)^2\), where previous data from Dalkhazhav (1969), Winkelmann (1980), Irom (1984), Velichko (1988), and Guelmez (1991) were limited. The refined Glauber model, developed by Platonova and Kukulin (2010, 2016), is tested against the new high-precision data, confirming its validity at low momentum transfers but revealing deviations at higher \(|t|\) that may indicate the need for three-nucleon force contributions, such as those from two-pion exchange with a \(\Delta(1232)\) isobar or from meson exchange with a deuteron dibaryon core. The results enable improved normalisation for meson production experiments, such as the COSY-WASA \(\eta\)-meson production data (Adlarson, 2018), and motivate future measurements of polarisation observables.
