# Nuclear recoil measurements in Superheated Superconducting Granule detectors

> The response of Superheated Superconducting Granule (SSG) devices to nuclear recoils has been explored by irradiating SSG detectors with a 70Me$\!$V neutron beam. In the past we have tested Al SSG and more recently, measurements have been performed with Sn and Zn detectors. The aim of the experiments was to test the sensitivity of SSG detectors to recoil energies down to a few ke$\!$V. In this paper, the preliminary results of the neutron irradiation of a SSG detector made of Sn granules 15-20$μ$m in diameter will be discussed. For the first time, recoil energy thresholds of $\sim$1ke$\!$V have been measured.

## Key claim

For the first time, Superheated Superconducting Granule (SSG) detectors made of 15–20 µm tin granules have been shown to be sensitive to nuclear recoil energies down to approximately 1 keV, although the limited angular resolution of the neutron hodoscope (0.02 rad) prevented testing sensitivity to recoil energies below that value.

## Method

The group irradiated SSG detectors made of Sn granules (15–20 µm diameter) with a 70 MeV neutron beam at the Paul Scherrer Institute. Coincidences between the SSG signal and scattered neutrons detected by a scintillator hodoscope (covering 0.02–0.5 rad with 20 mrad FWHM resolution) were used to select elastic scattering events. The measured scattering-angle distributions were compared to Monte Carlo simulations based on the global heating model and an alternative model with a factor-of-two higher sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- Recoil energy thresholds of approximately 1 keV were measured for the first time in Sn SSG detectors (15–20 µm granules).
- At a magnetic threshold of h = 0.035, the cutoff in the measured recoil energy distribution corresponds to an energy threshold of 5 keV.
- At h = 0.025, the onset of the measured recoil energy distribution is at approximately 2 keV.
- The standard deviation of the distribution of coincident SSG signals above background is 25 ns.
- The accuracy in the energy measurement of the neutrons was ±8 MeV, dominated by the beam bunch width.
- The neutron beam had a repetition rate of 17 MHz and a bunch width of 2 ns.

## Context

This work extends previous SSG detector tests with minimum ionizing particles (Mips, 1992) and x-rays (Xray, 1992), and follows earlier neutron irradiation tests on Al SSG detectors (Neutron, 1993). The results show that Sn granules are approximately two times more sensitive to nuclear recoils than predicted by the global heating model, possibly due to local heating effects. This sensitivity to low recoil energies is encouraging for the use of SSG detectors in dark matter searches, as discussed in the context of WIMP detection (Pretzl, 1993; Gabutti, 1993).
