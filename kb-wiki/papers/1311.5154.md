# Interacting quark matter equation of state for compact stars

> Lattice QCD studies of the thermodynamics of the hot quark-gluon plasma (QGP) demonstrate the importance of accounting for the interactions of quarks and gluons, if one wants to investigate the phase structure of strongly interacting matter. Motivated by this observation and using state-of-the-art results from perturbative QCD, we construct a simple effective equation of state for cold quark matter that consistently incorporates the effects of interactions and furthermore includes a built-in estimate of the inherent systematic uncertainties. This goes beyond the MIT bag model description in a crucial way, yet leads to an equation of state that is equally straightforward to use.

# Interacting quark matter equation of state for compact stars

## Key claim

The paper constructs a simple analytic equation of state for cold quark matter based on perturbative QCD at order α_s^2, which includes a built-in estimate of systematic uncertainties via renormalization scale variation. The authors claim this EoS can produce pure quark stars with masses above 2 M_⊙, but only for certain choices of the renormalization scale parameter X; the result is a band of possible EoSs rather than a single curve, and the highest masses require the most favorable scale choices.

## Method

The authors take the three-loop perturbative QCD result for zero-temperature quark matter from Kurkela (2009), which includes a nonzero strange quark mass, and fit the pressure as a function of baryon chemical potential μ_B to a compact analytic function with five parameters. The fit is optimized to reproduce the pressure, quark number density, and speed of sound squared from the full perturbative calculation, with the renormalization scale parameter X = 3Λ̄/μ_B varied between 1 and 4 to estimate systematic uncertainty. The resulting EoS is then applied to electrically neutral, β-stable quark matter and used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for pure quark stars and for hybrid stars matched to the APR nucleonic EoS via Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit parameters for the pressure function are: c₁ = 0.9008, d₁ = 0.5034, d₂ = 1.452, ν₁ = 0.3553, ν₂ = 0.9101, with fit quality √χ² ≲ 0.03 in the region μ_B < 2 GeV, P(μ_B) > 0, and X ∈ [1,4].
- For the hybrid star matching at X = 3.205, the critical chemical potential where quark and nuclear matter pressures match is μ_c = 1.27 GeV, the pressure is P(μ_c) = 0.15 GeV/fm³, and the baryon number density at that point is N(μ_c) = 0.64/fm³.
- The strong coupling constant is taken as α_s(1.5 GeV) = 0.326 and the strange quark mass as m_s(2 GeV) = 0.938 GeV (note: this value appears to be a typo in the text; the standard value is ~0.095 GeV, but the paper states 0.938 GeV).
- Pure quark stars can reach masses in excess of 2 M_⊙, consistent with the observed pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD equation of state developed by Kurkela (2009) and Fraga (2001) to a simple analytic form suitable for compact star modeling, directly challenging the long-standing use of the MIT bag model (Farhi 1984) which neglects interactions. The MIT bag model had previously suggested that pure quark stars are unlikely to reach 2 M_⊙ and that hybrid stars are generically less massive than nucleonic ones (Weissenborn 2011), but this perturbative EoS shows that interactions can stiffen the EoS enough to reach higher masses. The result enables systematic studies of hybrid and quark stars with a first-principles-based EoS that includes realistic uncertainty estimates, and the authors note that a more systematic matching to hadronic EoSs is planned for a separate publication.
