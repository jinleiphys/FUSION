# Many-body theory for quasiparticle states in superfluid fermionic systems

> We present a formalism for the fermionic quasiparticle propagator in a superfluid fermionic system. Starting from a general many-body hamiltonian confined by the two-body instantaneous interaction, the equation of motion for the fermionic propagator is obtained in the Dyson form. Before making any approximation, the interaction kernel is found to be decomposed into the static and dynamical (time-dependent) contributions, while the latter translates to the energy-dependent and the former maps to the energy-independent terms in the energy domain. The three-fermion correlation function being the heart of the dynamical part of the kernel is factorized into the two-fermion and one-fermion ones. With the relaxed particle number constraint, the normal propagator is coupled to the abnormal one via both the static and dynamical kernels, that is formalized by introducing the generalized quasiparticle propagator of the Gor'kov type. The dynamical kernel in the factorized form is associated with the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) with the vibrations unifying both the normal and pairing phonons. The QVC vertices are related to the variations of the Hamiltonian of the Bogoliubov quasiparticles, which can be obtained by the finite amplitude method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formal, model-independent derivation of the equation of motion for the fermionic quasiparticle propagator in superfluid fermionic systems, starting from a general many-body Hamiltonian with a bare two-body interaction. The key advance is that the dynamical kernel of the Dyson equation is factorized into quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) terms that unify both normal (particle-hole) and pairing (particle-particle) phonons on equal footing, without relying on phenomenological assumptions or perturbative expansions for the kernel.

## Method

Starting from a non-relativistic many-body Hamiltonian with a two-body instantaneous interaction, the authors derive the equation of motion for the one-fermion propagator in the Dyson form. The interaction kernel is decomposed into a static (energy-independent) mean-field part and a dynamical (energy-dependent) part, where the latter involves a three-fermion correlation function that is factorized into products of one-fermion and two-fermion propagators. The normal propagator is coupled to the anomalous (pairing) propagator via both static and dynamical kernels, formalized by introducing a generalized quasiparticle propagator of the Gor'kov type in the doubled single-particle space of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) basis. The dynamical kernel is mapped onto quasiparticle-vibration coupling vertices, where the vibrations include both normal phonons (from the particle-hole channel) and pairing phonons (from the particle-particle channel), and the vertices are related to variations of the Bogoliubov quasiparticle Hamiltonian that can be obtained by the finite amplitude method.

## Key numbers

* The spectral representation of the one-fermion propagator contains poles at energies \(E^{(N+1)}_{n} - E^{(N)}_0\) and \(-(E^{(N-1)}_{m} - E^{(N)}_0)\) for the \((N+1)\)- and \((N-1)\)-particle systems, respectively, with residues given by matrix elements \(\eta^{n}_{1} = \langle 0^{(N)}|\psi_1|n^{(N+1)} \rangle\) and \(\chi^{m}_{1} = \langle m^{(N-1)}|\psi_1|0^{(N)} \rangle\).
* The response function (particle-hole propagator) has poles at excitation energies \(\omega_{\nu} = E_{\nu} - E_0\) with residues \(\rho^{\nu}_{12} = \langle 0|\psi^{\dagger}_2\psi_1|\nu \rangle\).
* The particle-particle propagator has poles at \(\omega_{\mu}^{(++)} = E_{\mu}^{(N+2)} - E_0^{(N)}\) and \(\omega_{\varkappa}^{(--)} = E_{\varkappa}^{(N-2)} - E_0^{(N)}\) with residues \(\alpha_{12}^{\mu} = \langle 0^{(N)} | \psi_2\psi_1|\mu^{(N+2)} \rangle\) and \(\beta_{12}^{\varkappa} = \langle 0^{(N)} | \psi^{\dagger}_2\psi^{\dagger}_1|\varkappa^{(N-2)} \rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the equation-of-motion formalism for the single-fermion propagator, previously developed for normal (non-superfluid) systems in Litvinova and Schuck (2019), to the superfluid case with pairing correlations. It provides a continuous derivation from the bare Hamiltonian that contrasts with earlier phenomenological approaches such as the nuclear field theory (Bohr and Mottelson, 1969; Broglia, 1976) and the quasiparticle-phonon model (Soloviev, 1992), which lacked an explicit link to the underlying bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The formalism enables non-perturbative treatment of the dynamical kernel on equal footing with superfluid pairing, and the connection to the finite amplitude method (Nakatsukasa, Inakura, and Yabana, 2007) provides a practical path for computing the QVC vertices from the quasiparticle Hamiltonian variations.
