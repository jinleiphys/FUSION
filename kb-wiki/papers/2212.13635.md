# Neutrino oscillations in Quantum Field Theory

> We propose a Quantum Field Theory (QFT) approach to neutrino oscillations in vacuum. The neutrino emission and detection are identified with the charged-current vertices of a single second-order Feynman diagram for the underlying process, enclosing neutrino propagation between these two points. The key point of our approach is the definition of the space-time setup typical for neutrino oscillation experiments, implying macroscopically large but finite volumes of the source and detector separated by a sufficiently large distance $L$. We derive an $L$-dependent master formula for the charged lepton production rate, which provides the QFT basis for the analysis of neutrino oscillations. Our formula depends on the underlying process and is not reducible to the conventional approach resorting to the concept of neutrino oscillation probability, which originates from non-relativistic quantum mechanics (QM). We demonstrate that for some particular choice of the underlying process our QFT formula approximately coincides with the conventional one under some assumptions.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Quantum Field Theory (QFT) approach to neutrino oscillations in vacuum that derives an L-dependent master formula for the charged lepton production rate, which is not reducible to the conventional concept of neutrino oscillation probability from non-relativistic quantum mechanics; they demonstrate that for some particular choice of the underlying process their QFT formula approximately coincides with the conventional one only under some assumptions.

## Method

The neutrino emission and detection are identified with the charged-current vertices of a single second-order Feynman diagram for the underlying process S+D → ℓ⁺_α + ℓ⁻_β + S' + D', enclosing neutrino propagation between these two points. The space-time setup is defined with macroscopically large but finite volumes of the source and detector separated by a sufficiently large distance L, with the requirement that the emission of ℓ⁻_β occurs after the emission of ℓ⁺_α (t₁ < t₂). The S-matrix element is computed using the neutrino propagator with the retarded part selected, and the integrations over space coordinates are limited to the finite volumes V_S and V_D of the source and detector, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The master formula contains the oscillatory factor e^{i(p_k - p_m)L}/(4πL²), where L is the distance between source and detector and p_k = √(E²_ν - m²_k) is the momentum of neutrino mass eigenstate k.
- For the illustrative process π⁺ + n → μ⁺ + e⁻ + p, the conventional production rate is given by Γ_{osc}^{π⁺n} = (1/2π²) G²_β (f_π/√2)² (m²_μ/m_π) E²_ν [P_{ν_μ ν_e}(E_ν)/(4πL²)] (g²_V + 3g²_A) p_e E_e.
- The neutrino energy in the rest frame of the decaying pion and target neutron is E_ν = m_π (1 - m²_μ/m²_π)/2.
- The finite-volume delta functions δ³_V(p) approach the usual δ³(p) in the limit V → ∞, with deviations ΔP ∼ ℓ^{-1}_{S,D} that are negligibly small for macroscopically large volumes.

## Context

This work extends the QFT approach with plane waves developed by Grimus and collaborators (1998-2019), where neutrino production and detection are part of a single Feynman diagram, and addresses the open question of whether neutrino oscillations can be consistently described in the standard QFT S-matrix formalism. The approach is free from ambiguities of the standard quantum-mechanical plane wave treatment related to equality of energies or momenta of different mass eigenstates and choice of reference frame, and it avoids the difficulties of wave-packet approaches (Kayser 1981, Giunti 1991, Beuthe 2002, Akhmedov 2010) regarding ill-defined flavor states and determination of wave packet size. The master formula enables a QFT-based analysis of neutrino oscillation data without resorting to the concept of oscillation probability from non-relativistic quantum mechanics.
