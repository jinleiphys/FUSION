# Radial-flow fluctuations in the geometrical-scaling framework

> We discuss radial-flow fluctuations using the $p_{\rm T}$-differential measure \(v_0(p_{\rm T})\), together with its $p_{\rm T}$-integrated counterpart \(v_0\), within the framework of geometrical scaling (GS), where the saturation momentum scale provides the characteristic scale for particle production. We show that the GS framework leads to results similar to those obtained from the momentum-rescaling model proposed by Jiangyong Jia. In the GS picture, event-by-event spectral fluctuations are governed by fluctuations of the saturation momentum scale; consequently, the single-mode ansatz introduced in Jia's model emerges naturally. We also show that the GS picture suggests a possible connection between transverse-momentum correlations and fluctuations of the emission region, which may be probed through Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) analyses. Using the string percolation model, which is closely related to GS, we estimate the multiplicity dependence of radial-flow fluctuations and propose the scaled quantity $A_0(N_{Δy}) \equiv v_0^2 N_{Δy}$, with $N_{Δy}=(dN/dy)Δy$, as a diagnostic observable for testing the role of effective flux-tube fluctuations.

## Key claim

The geometrical-scaling (GS) framework naturally reproduces the single-mode ansatz of Jia's momentum-rescaling model for radial-flow fluctuations, but only when the dominant fluctuation source is the saturation momentum scale; deviations of measured \(v_0(p_{\rm T})/v_0\) from the GS baseline may indicate additional fluctuation sources beyond saturation-scale fluctuations.

## Method

The author derived the \(p_{\rm T}\)-differential radial-flow fluctuation measure \(v_0(p_{\rm T})\) and its \(p_{\rm T}\)-integrated counterpart \(v_0\) within the GS framework, where transverse-momentum spectra are governed by a multiplicity-dependent saturation momentum scale \(Q_{\rm sat}\). By expanding the spectral fluctuation to first order in \(\delta Q_{\rm sat}\) and using the relation between fluctuations of \(Q_{\rm sat}\) and the effective area \(S^{*}_{\rm T}\), the author showed that the normalized spectral fluctuation takes the same factorized form as Jia's rescaling model. The string percolation model was then used to estimate the multiplicity dependence of these fluctuations by relating \(Q_{\rm sat}\) fluctuations to fluctuations of the string density \(\eta\).

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis-type scaling function parameters used for numerical estimates are \(q=1.145\) and \(\kappa=0.1100\), fixed from GS analysis of semi-inclusive \(\pi^\pm\) spectra in \(pp\) and \(p\)--Pb collisions.
- For \(pp\to \pi^\pm+X\) at \(\sqrt{s}=7.00\) TeV (ALICE data), the saturation momentum parametrization gives \(a_Q = -0.225\) and \(b_Q = 0.985\) for \(Q_{\rm sat} = a_Q + b_Q (dN/dy)^{1/6}\).
- For \(p\)--Pb\(\to \pi^\pm+X\) at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV (CMS data), the saturation momentum parametrization gives \(a_Q = 0.078\) and \(b_Q = 0.899\).
- The zero-crossing point of \(K_{\rm GS}(p_{\rm T})\) occurs around \(p_{\rm T}\simeq 0.5\)--\(0.7\) GeV/\(c\).
- For \(\lambda=0.22\), the mean string density scales as \(\eta \sim (W^{*})^{1/5}\) in the high-density limit.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical-scaling framework previously applied to semi-inclusive transverse-momentum spectra in \(pp\) and \(p\)--Pb collisions by Osada (2020) to the study of event-by-event radial-flow fluctuations. It directly builds on the momentum-rescaling model proposed by Jia (2025) and shows that the single-mode ansatz of that model emerges naturally from GS when saturation-scale fluctuations are the dominant source. The paper also connects to the string percolation model of Dias de Deus (2010) and Braun (2001) to estimate multiplicity scaling, and suggests that HBT analyses by Plumberg (2015) could probe the relation between transverse-momentum correlations and fluctuations of the emission region.
