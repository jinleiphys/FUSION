# Electromagnetic properties of baryon resonances

> Longitudinal and transverse transition form factors for most of the four-star nucleon resonances have been obtained from high-quality cross section data and polarization observables measured at MAMI, ELSA, BATES, GRAAL and CEBAF. As an application, we further show how the transition form factors can be used to obtain empirical transverse charge densities. Contour plots of the thus derived densities are shown and compared for the Roper and S11 nucleon resonances.

# Electromagnetic properties of baryon resonances

## Key claim
Transition form factors for four-star nucleon resonances below W=1.8 GeV have been extracted from electroproduction data using the unitary isobar model MAID, but the analysis at higher Q² (above 1 GeV²) for the N→Δ(1232) transition shows strong dependence on the chosen energy interval and background parametrization, indicating that further studies are required before the results can be considered definitive.

## Method
The MAID unitary isobar model was used to analyze all available pion electroproduction data from MAMI, ELSA, BATES, GRAAL, and CEBAF to extract longitudinal and transverse transition form factors for four-star nucleon resonances. Single-Q² data points were obtained for transitions from the proton to ten resonances including Δ(1232), P₁₁(1440), S₁₁(1535), D₁₃(1520), and others, with Q²-dependent parametrizations applied. For the neutron target, simpler parametrizations were used due to the smaller world database.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic form factor G_M* for N→Δ(1232) parameterized as G_M*(Q²) = 3 G_D(Q²) e^{-0.21 Q²/GeV²}, with G_D the standard dipole form factor
- For the Roper resonance P₁₁(1440), the transverse helicity amplitude A₁/₂(Q²) shows a zero crossing at Q² ≈ 0.7 GeV² and a maximum at Q² ≈ 2.0 GeV²
- The longitudinal Roper excitation S₁/₂(Q²) rises to large values around Q² ≈ 0.5 GeV²
- For S₁₁(1535) at Q²=0, MAID analyses find A₁/₂ values around 65 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}), while the JLab analysis obtains values around 90 for γ,π and 110 for γ,η; Shrestha and Manley report a small coupling of 59(3)
- The N→Δ(1232) magnetic form factor is known up to Q²=10 GeV²
- The ratio R_EM from MAID2007 stays always below zero, in contrast to predicted helicity conservation at the quark level

## Context
This work extends the unitary isobar model analyses of the Mainz group (MAID2007) and builds on coupled-channels analyses by the Giessen group (Shklyar, 2006), Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich, 2009), Jülich group (Doring, 2009), and EBAC group (Suzuki, 2010). The results are compared with the JLab dispersion-relation analysis of Aznauryan (2011) and with predictions from quark models and lattice QCD. The extracted transition form factors enable empirical transverse charge densities to be computed via Fourier-Bessel transformation, as demonstrated for the Roper and S₁₁(1535) resonances, allowing comparison of the dipole contributions from up and down quarks.
