# Neutrino-Deuteron Scattering in Effective Field Theory at Next-to-Next-to Leading Order

> We study the four channels associated with neutrino-deuteron breakup reactions at next-to-next to leading order in effective field theory. We find that the total cross-section is indeed converging for neutrino energies up to 20 MeV, and thus our calculations can provide constraints on theoretical uncertainties for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory. We stress the importance of a direct experimental measurement to high precision in at least one channel, in order to fix an axial two-body counterterm.

## Key claim

The total cross-section for neutrino-deuteron breakup reactions is indeed converging at next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) in effective field theory for neutrino energies up to 20 MeV, but a direct experimental measurement in at least one channel is required to fix an unknown axial two-body counterterm ($L_{1,A}$) that dominates theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear effective field theory without pions, using the power-counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, to compute all four channels of neutrino-deuteron breakup reactions (NC: $\nu d \rightarrow \nu np$, $\bar{\nu}d \rightarrow \bar{\nu}np$; CC: $\nu_e d \rightarrow e^- pp$, $\bar{\nu}_e d \rightarrow e^+ nn$) to NNLO. They incorporated Coulomb effects in the $pp$ final state via the Sommerfeld factor and fit the strong interaction parameters to the effective range expansion, with no new free parameters introduced at NNLO.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: $B = 2.2245$ MeV
- Deuteron binding momentum: $\gamma = 45.69$ MeV
- Axial coupling constant: $g_A = 1.26$
- Fermi constant: $G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- $np$ scattering length in $^1S_0$ channel: $a^{(^1S_0,np)} = -23.7$ fm
- $np$ effective range in $^1S_0$ channel: $r_0^{(^1S_0,np)} = 2.73$ fm (2% uncertainty)
- $nn$ scattering length: $a^{(^1S_0,nn)} = -18.5$ fm (few percent uncertainty)
- $nn$ effective range: $r_0^{(^1S_0,nn)} = 2.80$ fm
- Deuteron effective range: $\rho_d = 1.764$ fm
- Strange quark contribution to proton spin: $\Delta s = -0.17 \pm 0.17$
- Strange magnetic moment from SAMPLE experiment: $G_M^s(-0.1\text{ GeV}^2) = 0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19$ n.m.
- Two-body vector current parameters at $\mu = m_\pi$: $L_1 = 7.24$ fm$^4$, $L_2 = -0.149$ fm$^4$
- CKM matrix element: $|V_{ud}| = 0.975$
- A 2% uncertainty in $a^{(^1S_0,nn)}$ changes the $\nu$-$d$ breakup cross section at threshold by 3-4%

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of neutrino-deuteron scattering from next-to-leading order (NLO) as previously studied by Butler and Chen (2000) to NNLO, using the pionless EFT that has proven successful for high-precision calculations of processes like $np \rightarrow d\gamma$ as demonstrated by Rupak (2000). The calculations provide constraints on theoretical uncertainties for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and enable comparison with potential model calculations by Nakamura et al. (2000) and Ying, Haxton, and Henley (1998), which agree within a few percent at low energy. The results also have implications for $pp \rightarrow de^+\nu_e$, $NN \rightarrow NN\bar{\nu}\nu$, and parity-violating $\vec{e}$-$d$ scattering.
