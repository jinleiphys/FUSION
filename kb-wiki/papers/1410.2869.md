# Disentangling the nuclear shape coexistence in even-even Hg isotopes using the interacting boson model

> We intend to provide a consistent description of the even-even Hg isotopes, 172-200Hg, using the interacting boson model including configuration mixing. We pay special attention to the description of the shape of the nuclei and to its connection with the shape coexistence phenomenon.

# Disentangling the nuclear shape coexistence in even-even Hg isotopes using the interacting boson model

## Key claim
The interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) provides a consistent description of shape coexistence in even-even Hg isotopes from 172-200Hg, but the mean-field (intrinsic state) approach fails to correctly describe the ground state deformation evolution near mid-shell because it omits the full dynamics that are particularly important when two configurations lie close in energy.

## Method
The authors employed the interacting boson model including configuration mixing (IBM-CM) in a [N] ⊕ [N+2] boson space, where the Hamiltonian consists of extended consistent-Q (ECQF) Hamiltonians for each boson subspace plus a mixing term. They performed a fit to excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates for 172-200Hg to fix the Hamiltonian parameters, then analyzed the shape evolution using three approaches: (i) the IBM-CM coherent state (mean-field) formalism to extract β_IBM and γ_IBM; (ii) extraction of the quadrupole deformation parameter β from experimental B(E2) values using the rotational model formula; and (iii) calculation of quadrupole shape invariants q² from the IBM-CM wave functions.

## Key numbers
- The unperturbed regular configuration yields β_IBM around 0.5, while the unperturbed intruder configuration yields β_IBM around 1.0, for the Hg isotope chain.
- The full IBM-CM β_IBM varies between the regular value at the beginning and end of the shell and the intruder value at the mid-shell region (around 180-186Hg).
- The q² shape invariant differs by a factor of approximately 3 between the 0₁⁺ and 0₂⁺ states around the mid-shell region.
- The isotopes 180-186Hg present a prolate shape around mid-shell, while oblate shapes appear at the beginning and end of the shell.
- The intruder state never becomes the ground state in Hg, unlike the situation in Pt nuclei.

## Context
This work extends previous IBM-CM studies of Pt nuclei (García-Ramos et al., 2009, 2011, 2014) and Hg nuclei (García-Ramos et al., 2014) by focusing specifically on the shape evolution and its connection to shape coexistence. The paper directly compares IBM-CM results with recent Coulomb excitation experiments on 182-188Hg performed at REX-ISOLDE with the Miniball array (Bree et al., 2014), which provided detailed information on nuclear shapes. The quadrupole shape invariant analysis follows the model-independent approach of Kumar (1972) and Srebrny et al. (2011). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted rapid evolution of the 0₂⁺ state structure from intruder character near mid-shell to regular character from 190Hg onward.
