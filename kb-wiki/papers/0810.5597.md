# Gamow-Siegert functions and Darboux-deformed short range potentials

> Darboux-deformations of short range one-dimensional potentials are constructed by means of Gamow-Siegert functions (resonance states). Results include both Hermitian and non-Hermitian short range potentials which are exactly solvable. As illustration, the method is applied to square wells and barriers for which the transmission coefficient is a superposition of Fock-Breit-Wigner distributions. Resonance levels are calculated in the long lifetime limit by means of analytical and numerical approaches. The new complex potentials behave as an optical device which both refracts and absorbs light waves.

## Key claim

Darboux transformations using Gamow-Siegert functions (resonance states) can generate both Hermitian and non-Hermitian exactly solvable short-range potentials from simple square wells and barriers, but the construction is valid only in the long lifetime limit where the level width is much smaller than the level spacing.

## Method

The authors solve the one-dimensional stationary Schrodinger equation for square well and barrier potentials, then identify complex eigenvalues epsilon = E - i Gamma/2 corresponding to purely outgoing boundary conditions (Gamow-Siegert states). They apply Darboux (supersymmetric) transformations using these resonance wavefunctions as transformation functions, producing new complex potentials V_tilde = V + 2 beta' where beta = -d/dx ln(varphi_epsilon). By iterating the transformation with a pair of complex conjugate Gamow-Siegert functions, they obtain real short-range potentials that are strictly isospectral to the original.

## Key numbers

- For square wells in the long lifetime limit, resonance energies are given by E_m = [((n_inf + m)pi/(2 theta))^2 - 1] V_0, where theta = (b/2) sqrt(V_0), n_inf = ceil(2 theta/pi), and m = 0,1,2,... (equation c32)
- For square wells, the resonance width is Gamma/2 approximately = (4/b) sqrt(E) = 2v_+/b (equation c5)
- For square barriers, resonance energies are E_n approximately = V_0 [((2s+1)pi/2)^2 + 1] with s = 0,1,2,... and Gamma_s/2 = (2/theta)(E_s - V_0) (equation resona)
- Example numerical values for a square well with V_0 = 50, b = 14.2: first resonance epsilon_1 = 3.3029 - i 0.5119, second resonance epsilon_2 = 6.5823 - i 0.7227
- Example numerical values for a square well with V_0 = 16, b = 5: lowest decreasing function has kappa_0 = 1.7504 + i 0.7657

## Context

This work extends the use of Darboux (supersymmetric) transformations in quantum mechanics, which previously had been applied using complex eigenvalues of Hermitian Hamiltonians to produce non-Hermitian potentials with a single complex eigenvalue (Fernandez-Garcia and Rosas-Ortiz, 2003; Rosas-Ortiz, 2003). The paper fills a gap by studying Darboux-deformations of potentials with resonances, which was missing in the literature. The resulting complex potentials behave as optical devices that both refract and absorb light waves, and the method can be extended to s-waves in three dimensions by selecting odd solutions and adding an impenetrable wall along the negative axis.
