# On thermalization of a boost-invariant non Abelian plasma

> Using a holographic method, we further investigate the relaxation towards the hydrodynamic regime of a boost-invariant non-Abelian plasma taken out-of-equilibrium. In the dual description, the system is driven out-of-equilibrium by boundary sourcing, a deformation of the boundary metric, as proposed by Chesler and Yaffe. The effects of several deformation profiles on the bulk geometry are investigated by the analysis of the corresponding solutions of the Einstein equations. The time of restoration of the hydrodynamic regime is investigated: setting the effective temperature of the system at the end of the boundary quenching to $T_{eff}(τ^*)=500$ MeV, the hydrodynamic regime is reached after a lapse of time of ${\cal O}$(1 fm/c).

## Key claim

Using holographic boundary sourcing, the hydrodynamic regime is restored after a lapse of time of O(1 fm/c) after the end of the boundary distortion, but the thermalization time (when pressure isotropy is reached) is generally longer than the time when the energy density first matches the hydrodynamic form, and depends on the specific distortion profile.

## Method

The authors solve the 5D Einstein equations in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates for a boost-invariant metric, with time-dependent boundary conditions that deform the 4D boundary metric via a function γ(τ). They implement a numerical algorithm that evolves the metric functions A, Σ, and B in proper time τ, using asymptotic expansions at large radial coordinate r to extract the boundary stress-energy tensor components and the effective temperature from the apparent horizon condition Σ̇(r_h,τ)=0.

## Key numbers

- Setting the effective temperature at the end of boundary quenching to T_eff(τ*)=500 MeV, the hydrodynamic regime is reached after a lapse of time of O(1 fm/c)
- For model A with two distant pulses, the thermalization time τ_p (when p_∥/p_⊥ differs by less than 5% from the asymptotic NNLO expression) is τ_p=6.8, giving τ_p−τ*≃0.60 fm/c
- For model A with two overlapping pulses, the time difference is τ_p−τ*≃1.03 fm/c
- The condition for defining τ* is |ε(τ*)−ε_H(τ*)|/|ε(τ*)|=0.05, where ε_H is the hydrodynamic energy density from Eq. (hydroeps)
- The numerical time step used is dτ=10^−2
- The apparent horizon condition requires R_i(r_min,τ)<0.03 for the residual functions, and the asymptotic matching requires R_i(r_as,τ)<0.01

## Context

This work extends the holographic boundary sourcing method of Chesler and Yaffe (2009) to study more complex distortion profiles, including multiple pulses and combinations of short pulses with slow continuous deformations. The analysis of thermalization time and its dependence on the distortion structure is relevant for understanding pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy ion collisions, where the system is produced far from equilibrium and must relax to a hydrodynamic regime before the standard Bjorken (1982) flow description applies. The results enable future studies of more realistic collision scenarios, such as shock-wave collisions with lower symmetry.
