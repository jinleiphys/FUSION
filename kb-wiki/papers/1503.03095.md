# Neutron stars in the BPS Skyrme model: mean-field limit vs. full field theory

> Using a solitonic model of nuclear matter, the BPS Skyrme model, we compare neutron stars obtained in the full field theory, where gravitational back reaction is completely taken into account, with calculations in a mean-field approximation using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff approach. In the latter case, a mean-field-theory equation of state is derived from the original BPS field theory. We show that in the full field theory, where the energy density is non-constant even at equilibrium, there is no universal and coordinate independent equation of state of nuclear matter, in contrast to the mean-field approximation. We also study how neutron star properties are modified by going beyond mean field theory, and find that the differences between mean field theory and exact results can be considerable. Further, we compare both exact and mean-field results with some theoretical and phenomenological constraints on neutron star properties, demonstrating thus the relevance of our model even in its most simple version.

# Neutron stars in the BPS Skyrme model: mean-field limit vs. full field theory

## Key claim

The authors show that in the full field theory of the BPS Skyrme model coupled to gravity, there is no universal and coordinate-independent equation of state for nuclear matter, in contrast to the mean-field approximation; the differences between mean-field and exact results for neutron star properties can be considerable, but the model remains relevant for phenomenological constraints even in its simplest version.

## Method

The authors solve the Einstein equations coupled to the BPS Skyrme model in a spherically symmetric metric, using an axially symmetric ansatz for the Skyrme field, to obtain gravitating skyrmions (neutron stars) with full gravitational back-reaction. Separately, they derive a mean-field equation of state by averaging the energy density over the volume of the soliton, defined as the ratio of total energy to total volume, and then use this MF-EoS in the standard Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff approach. The comparison is performed for three choices of the potential: the step function potential, the Skyrme potential, and the Skyrme potential squared.

## Key numbers

* For the step function potential, the exact and mean-field equations of state both give $\rho = P + 2\mu^2$, with bag constants $B_\infty = B_0 = 2\mu^2$.
* For the Skyrme potential $\mathcal{U}_\pi$, the equilibrium bag constant is $B_0 = \frac{8}{5}\mu^2$ and the asymptotic bag constant is $B_\infty = 2\mu^2$.
* For the Skyrme potential squared $\mathcal{U}_\pi^2$, the equilibrium bag constant is $B_0 = \mu^2$ and the asymptotic bag constant is $B_\infty = \frac{5}{2}\mu^2$.
* The gravitational coupling constant is given as $\kappa^2 = 16\pi G = 6.654 \times 10^{-41} \, \text{fm} \, \text{MeV}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of neutron stars in the BPS Skyrme model (Adam et al., 2014) by systematically comparing full field-theoretic calculations with the mean-field TOV approach. The BPS Skyrme model is a solitonic effective field theory of nuclear matter that addresses the binding energy problem of the original Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961) and provides a perfect fluid energy-momentum tensor at the microscopic level, unlike other effective theories such as the Walecka model or the MIT bag model. The results enable quantitative assessment of the errors introduced by mean-field approximations in neutron star modeling and demonstrate that the BPS Skyrme model can serve as a laboratory for testing the validity of the TOV approach.
