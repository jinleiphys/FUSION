# Higher order Symmetric Cumulants

> We present the generalization of recently introduced observables for the studies of correlated fluctuations of different anisotropic flow amplitudes, dubbed Symmetric Cumulants. We introduce a new set of higher order observables and outline a unique way how the genuine multi-harmonic correlations can be extracted from multi-particle azimuthal correlators. We argue that correlations among flow amplitudes can be studied reliably with the general mathematical formalism of cumulants only if that formalism is applied directly on the flow amplitudes. We have tested all the desired properties of new observables with the carefully designed Toy Monte Carlo studies. By using the realistic iEBE-VISHNU model, we have demonstrated that their measurements are feasible and we have provided the predictions for the centrality dependence in Pb--Pb collisions at LHC energies. A separate study was performed for their values in the coordinate space. The new observables contain information which is inaccessible to individual flow amplitudes and correlated fluctuations of only two flow amplitudes, and therefore they provide further and independent constraints for the initial conditions and the properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Symmetric Cumulants (SC) to higher orders involving more than two flow amplitudes, arguing that genuine multi-harmonic correlations can be extracted reliably only if the cumulant formalism is applied directly on the flow amplitudes themselves, rather than on azimuthal angles as in prior approaches. The authors demonstrate that the traditional cumulant expansion on azimuthal angles produces expressions that depend on symmetry planes and therefore do not satisfy the fundamental properties of cumulants of flow amplitudes.

## Method

The authors define higher-order SC(k,l,m) by applying the general mathematical formalism of cumulants directly to the flow amplitudes v_n, then use the analytic relation between multi-particle azimuthal correlators and flow amplitudes to express these observables in terms of experimentally measurable azimuthal correlators. They test the properties of the new observables using Toy Monte Carlo simulations with controlled inputs and validate the feasibility of measurements using the realistic iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model for Pb–Pb collisions at LHC energies, also performing a separate study in coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- In the Toy Monte Carlo study with independently sampled v_1, v_2, v_3 (uniform in (0.03,0.1), (0.04,0.1), (0.05,0.1) respectively) and symmetry planes Ψ_1, Ψ_2 uniform in (0,2π) with Ψ_3 = (π/4 + 2Ψ_2 + Ψ_1)/3, the new cumulant SC(1,2,3) is consistent with 0 for 10000 events, while the old approach gives a non-zero result.
- The nonflow study uses N = 10^8 events with multiplicities M_initial = 25, 38, 50, 75, 100, 150, 200, 250, 500 and flow harmonics set to zero, introducing strong two-particle correlations by taking each particle twice.
- The difference between the new and old approaches for v_1, v_2, v_3 cumulants is shown to be non-negligible in mid-central and peripheral collisions in the iEBE-VISHNU model (Fig. 1).

## Context

This work extends the Symmetric Cumulants framework introduced in Bilandzic (2013) and first measured by ALICE (2016), which previously only addressed correlations between two flow amplitudes. The generalization to three or more amplitudes provides independent constraints on initial conditions and QGP properties that are inaccessible to individual flow amplitudes or two-amplitude correlations, building on theoretical studies by Niemi (2012) and others who used event-by-event flow amplitudes. The new approach corrects a subtle but important flaw in the traditional cumulant expansion on azimuthal angles (Borghini, 2001), enabling future experimental measurements of genuine multi-harmonic correlations.
