# Competitive effects of nuclear deformation and density dependence of $Λ\!N$ interaction

> Competitive effects of nuclear deformation and density dependence of $Λ\!N$-interaction in $Λ$ binding energies $B_Λ$ of hypernuclei are studied systematically on the basis of the baryon-baryon interaction model ESC including many-body effects. By using the $Λ\!N$ G-matrix interaction derived from ESC, we perform microscopic calculations of $B_Λ$ in $Λ$ hypernuclei within the framework of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics under the averaged-density approximation. The calculated values of $B_Λ$ reproduce experimental data within a few hundred keV in the wide mass regions from 9 to 51. It is found that competitive effects of nuclear deformation and density dependence of $Λ\!N$-interaction work decisively for fine tuning of $B_Λ$ values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fine tuning of Λ binding energies (B_Λ) in hypernuclei across a wide mass range (A=9 to 51) results from a competition between two opposing effects: nuclear deformation reduces the overlap between the Λ particle and the core nucleus (making B_Λ smaller), while the density dependence of the ΛN interaction makes the effective interaction more attractive at lower densities (making B_Λ larger), with the net effect depending on mass region.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of Λ binding energies using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) combined with ΛN G-matrix interactions derived from the Extended-Soft Core (ESC) baryon-baryon interaction model, including many-body effects via multi-pomeron exchange repulsion (MPP) and a phenomenological three-body attraction (TBA). The averaged-density approximation (ADA) was used to determine the Fermi momentum k_F for each hypernucleus, and generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations were performed with angular momentum projection to describe nuclear deformations and shape fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Calculated B_Λ values reproduce experimental data within about 200 keV for most hypernuclei from A=9 to A=51, with exceptions for ⁹_ΛBe, ¹⁵_ΛN, and ²⁸_ΛSi
- For ¹²_ΛB, B_Λ = 11.3 MeV with k_F = 1.07 fm⁻¹ in the full calculation (case A), compared to B_Λ = 9.7 MeV with k_F = 1.16 fm⁻¹ in the spherical-only calculation (case B), and the experimental value is 11.38 ± 0.02 MeV
- For ¹⁶_ΛO, the calculated B_Λ is 13.0 MeV with ADA and 12.3 MeV with LDA, compared to the experimental value of 12.96 ± 0.05 MeV
- For ⁸⁹_ΛY, the calculated B_Λ is 23.7 MeV with ADA and 23.6 MeV with LDA, compared to the experimental value of 23.65 ± 0.10 MeV
- The MPP+TBA parameters were set to g_P^(3) = 0.39, g_P^(4) = 0.0, and V_0 = -5.0, giving only minor effects on the results
- For ¹²_ΛB, the calculated B(E2; 5/2⁻₁ → 3/2⁻₁) in the core nucleus ¹¹B is 16 e² fm⁴, consistent with the experimental value of 14 ± 3 e² fm⁴

## Context

This work extends previous HyperAMD studies of p-shell and sd-pf shell hypernuclei (Isaka et al., 2014; 2015) by systematically analyzing the competition between nuclear deformation effects and the density dependence of the ΛN interaction across a wider mass range. The authors use the ESC08c(2012) interaction model from the Nijmegen group, which includes ΛN-ΣN coupling terms that produce strong density dependence. The results enable more precise predictions for hypernuclear structure and inform the study of hyperonic many-body effects relevant to neutron star equations of state, where the same MPP+TBA framework has been applied (Yamamoto et al., 2013; 2014).
