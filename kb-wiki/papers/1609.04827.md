# Updates to the p+p and A+A chemical freeze-out lines from the new experimental data

> We show that the new data on mean multiplicities measured in p+p and A+A collisions together with the updated list of resonances lead to the significant changes of the obtained freeze-out lines. The new A+A line gives much smaller temperatures at high collision energies and agrees with the values obtained at the LHC. The newly obtained p+p line is much closer to the A+A line than previously expected, and even touches it in the region where the $K^+/π^+$ horn appears in the data. It indicates that the temperatures that will be obtained in the beam energy and system size scan by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration might be very close. However, our analysis shows that the chemical potentials could be very different for the same energies in A+A and p+p. It adds more puzzles to the set of surprising coincidences at the energies close to the possible onset of deconfinement.

## Key claim

The new A+A chemical freeze-out line yields much lower temperatures at high collision energies than previous estimates, while the new p+p line is much closer to the A+A line than previously expected, even touching it in the region where the \(K^+/\pi^+\) horn appears; however, the chemical potentials remain very different between the two systems at the same energies.

## Method

The authors performed a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model fit to mean multiplicities from new p+p data (NA61/SHINE and HADES at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.2\)–17.3 GeV) and new/updated A+A data (HADES and NA49 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.2\)–17.3 GeV). They used the latest THERMUS resonance list with masses \(M \leq 2.4\) GeV (excluding \(\sigma\) and \(\kappa\) resonances), and applied the grand canonical ensemble for A+A and the full canonical ensemble for p+p with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness.

## Key numbers

- The new A+A freeze-out line parametrization gives \(T(\mu_B=0) \simeq 157\) MeV, consistent with LHC HRG results.
- The fit parameters for the A+A line are: \(a = 0.157\) GeV, \(b = 0.087\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c = 0.092\) GeV\(^{-3}\), \(d = 1.477\) GeV, \(e = 0.343\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The p+p and A+A lines touch in the region \(E_{\text{lab}} = 30\)–40\(A\) GeV (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7\)–9 GeV), where the \(K^+/\pi^+\) horn appears.
- At \(E_{\text{lab}} = 30A\) GeV, the chemical potential in A+A is larger than in p+p by 70 MeV; at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 40A\) GeV, the difference is 60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out analysis of Cleymans (2005) by incorporating new experimental data from NA61/SHINE, HADES, and updated NA49 results, as well as an expanded resonance list. The updated A+A line now agrees with LHC temperatures from Stachel (2013), while the p+p line is calculated for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 17.3\) GeV for the first time, following the earlier work of Becattini (1997) at higher energies. The results suggest that the upcoming NA61/SHINE beam energy and system size scan may find very similar freeze-out temperatures across system sizes, but with significantly different chemical potentials, adding puzzles to the interpretation of the onset of deconfinement.
