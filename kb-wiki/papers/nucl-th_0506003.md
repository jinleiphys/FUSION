# Isospin splitting of the nucleon mean field

> The isospin splitting of the nucleon mean field is derived from the Brueckner theory extended to asymmetric nuclear matter. The Argonne V18 has been adopted as bare interaction in combination with a microscopic three body force. The isospin splitting of the effective mass is determined from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock self-energy: It is linear acording to the Lane ansatz and such that $m^*_n > m^*_p$ for neutron-rich matter. The symmetry potential is also determined and a comparison is made with the predictions of the Dirac-Brueckner approach and the phenomenological interactions. The theoretical predictions are also compared with the empirical parametrizations of neutron and proton optical-model potentials based on the experimental nucleon-nucleus scattering and the phenomenological ones adopted in transport-model simulations of heavy-ion collisions. The direct contribution of the rearrangement term due to three-body forces to the single particle potential and symmetry potential is discussed.

## Key claim

The isospin splitting of the nucleon effective mass in neutron-rich matter is such that \(m^*_n > m^*_p\), and this splitting is linear in the asymmetry parameter \(\beta\) across the whole asymmetry range, but the result is conditional on the dominance of the \(k\)-mass (spatial nonlocality) over the \(e\)-mass (temporal nonlocality) in determining the total effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne V18 two-body interaction supplemented with a microscopic three-body force (TBF) constructed via the meson-exchange current approach. The Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved in the continuous choice for the auxiliary potential, and the TBF was incorporated as an effective density-dependent two-body interaction by averaging over the third nucleon using the defect function. The nucleon self-energy was decomposed into the BHF mean-field term, the core-polarization term, and the TBF rearrangement term, and the on-shell self-energy was used to extract the single-particle potentials, effective masses, and symmetry potential.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry potential at zero momentum and normal nuclear density is about 25 MeV, in agreement with the empirical range of 22 to 34 MeV extracted from nucleon-nucleus scattering data.
* The Lane potential extracted from experimental data for incident energies between 7 and 100 MeV has a value of about \(28 \pm 6\) MeV at \(k=0\) and decreases with a slope between 0.1 and 0.2.
* At density \(\rho = 0.3\) fm\(^{-3}\), the TBF rearrangement contribution to the single-particle potential is parametrized as \(\Delta U = 5.68 + 6.84 k^2\) in units of MeV.
* The BHF symmetry potential was calculated at three densities: \(\rho = 0.085\), 0.17, and 0.34 fm\(^{-3}\), for comparison with phenomenological parametrizations.

## Context

This work extends earlier BHF studies of asymmetric nuclear matter by Zuo et al. (1999, 2002a) by including a microscopic three-body force and providing a detailed analysis of the momentum and density dependence of the symmetry potential. It directly compares the microscopic symmetry potential with the phenomenological parametrizations of Das et al. (2003) used in transport-model simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and with the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock predictions of van Dalen et al. (2004) and Sammarruca et al. The results provide microscopic constraints for Skyrme-like effective interactions used in calculations far from the beta-stability line and for transport-model simulations of reactions with radioactive beams.
