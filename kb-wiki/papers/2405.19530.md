# Causal fermion states in magnetic field in a relativistic rotating frame and electromagnetic radiation by a rapidly rotating charge

> We consider the Dirac field uniformly rotating with angular velocity $Ω$ and also subject to the constant magnetic field $B$ directed along the rotation axis. The causal states are constrained to the interior of the light cylinder of radius $c/Ω$. When this radius is smaller than the system size, as in the quark-gluon plasma, the effect of the boundary on the fermion spectrum is critical. We derive the fermion spectrum and study its properties. We compute the intensity of the electromagnetic radiation emitted due to transitions between the fermion states. We study its dependence on energy and angular momentum for different values of the angular velocity and the magnetic field. Rotation has enormous impact on the electromagnetic radiation by the quark-gluon plasma with or without the magnetic field.

## Key claim
The paper extends previous work on rotating fermions in a magnetic field beyond the "slow rotation" approximation (where magnetic length is much smaller than the light cylinder radius) by imposing causal boundary conditions at the light cylinder surface r = c/Ω, which becomes critical when the system size exceeds this radius as in quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation for a uniformly rotating fermion field in a constant magnetic field aligned with the rotation axis, using cylindrical coordinates and imposing MIT boundary conditions at the light cylinder radius R = 1/Ω. They derive the fermion spectrum by solving for the eigenvalues λ from the boundary condition equation, then compute the electromagnetic radiation intensity from transitions between these causal states using leading-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- Light cylinder radius: R = c/Ω (with ℏ = c = 1), defining the causal boundary at ρ_R = |qB|/(2Ω²)
- In the unbounded limit (R → ∞), the principal quantum number λ becomes a non-negative integer n = 0, 1, 2, ... and the energy spectrum is E = ε√(p_z² + 2n|qB| + M²) + mΩ
- For the MIT boundary condition with α = -1, the lowest transverse momentum p_{T,1} = √(2|qB|λ₁) is shown for qB = +0.2 M² as a function of angular momentum m
- Energy levels are plotted for qB = -0.2 M² at p_z = 0 for various ρ_R values: 100, 10, 1, and 0.1

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Buzzegoli and Tuchin, 2022, 2023) that assumed slow rotation (√(qB) ≫ Ω) and ignored boundary effects. It builds on prior work on rotating quantum systems by Vilenkin (1980), Becattini (2007), Ambrus (2014-2021), and others, as well as studies of causal boundaries in rotating systems by Duffy (2002), Ebihara (2016), and Chernodub (2017). The MIT boundary condition approach follows Chodos (1974) and Shanker (1983) from early QCD confinement models, and was previously applied to rotating frames by Bezerra de Mello (2008), Becattini (2011), and Ambrus (2015). The results enable calculation of electromagnetic radiation from rapidly rotating quark-gluon plasma where the system size exceeds the light cylinder radius.
