# In-medium hadronization in the deconfined matter at RHIC and LHC

> We study the mechanism and probability of in-medium hadronization in the deconfined medium produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We show the likelihood of color-neutral objects to be formed inside the partonic fireball and the probability of these states to escape the medium with reduced interaction strength and energy loss. We will suggest specific measurements that are sensitive to the early degrees of freedom and show predictions for these measurements at RHIC and the LHC

# In-medium hadronization in the deconfined matter at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The authors propose that color-neutral pre-hadrons can form inside the deconfined medium well above the critical temperature (T = 1-1.5 T_c), not just at the hadronization surface, and that these pre-hadrons experience reduced energy loss due to color transparency. This claim is conditional on the assumption that high-momentum fragmentation processes, rather than bulk thermal hadronization, dominate particle production in the relevant kinematic regime (p_T > 6 GeV/c).

## Method
The authors calculate p_T-dependent formation times for pre-hadrons using light-cone variables based on the final hadron mass, deriving an intermediate complex formation time (τ_p') between the production time and the final formation time. They compare these formation times to the estimated QGP lifetime at RHIC (5±1 fm/c) and LHC (14±2 fm/c) to determine which hadron species form inside the medium. The nuclear suppression factors (R_AA) are then estimated under the assumption that hadrons formed early in the plasma show no suppression due to color transparency, while those formed after the plasma phase show maximum suppression from partonic energy loss.

## Key numbers
- QGP lifetime at RHIC: 5±1 fm/c (derived analytically, consistent with HBT and resonance yield measurements)
- QGP lifetime at LHC: 14±2 fm/c (estimated as roughly twice the RHIC lifetime)
- Minimum R_AA for pions at RHIC: 0.20±0.04 (based on average of PHENIX and STAR measurements)
- At RHIC: Kaons up to 10 GeV/c and protons up to 20 GeV/c are formed inside the medium
- Pre-hadron formation time calculation uses Λ_QCD ~ 200 MeV/c for the deviation from collinearity
- Cluster masses in HERWIG that best agree with correlations: on the order of 3 GeV/c²
- Full deconfinement from lattice Polyakov loop calculations: achieved only above 2.5 T_c

## Context
This work extends the earlier formation-time framework of Kopeliovich et al. (2000s) and the production/formation time distinction by Brodsky and Mueller (1980s), and builds on the authors' own prior publication (Bellwied, Markert, and collaborators, 2009). It contrasts with standard energy loss models (GLV, ASW, AMY) that assume fragmentation occurs only outside the medium, and with the quasi-particle approach of Cassing and Bratkovskaya (2000s) where colored dressed states evolve rather than color-neutral objects. The predictions enable experimental tests at RHIC and LHC through particle-specific nuclear suppression factors, p_T-broadening of identified particles in jets, and measurements of high-momentum resonance properties (yield, mass, width, lifetime).
