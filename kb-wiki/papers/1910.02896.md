# Complications in the interpretation of the charge asymmetry dependent $π$ flow for the chiral magnetic wave

> The charge asymmetry ($A_{\rm ch}$) dependence of the $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ elliptic flow difference, $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$, has been regarded as a sensitive observable for the possible chiral magnetic wave (CMW) in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this work, we first demonstrate that, due to non-flow backgrounds, the flow measurements by the Q-cumulant method using all charged particles as reference introduce a trivial linear term to $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$. The trivial slope can be negative in the triangle flow difference $Δv_{3}(A_{\rm ch})$ if the non-flow is dominated by back-to-back pairs. After eliminating the trivial term, we find that the non-flow between like-sign pairs gives rise to an additional positive slope to $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$ because of the larger dilution effect to $π^{+}$ ($π^{-}$) at positive (negative) $A_{\rm ch}$. We further find that the competition between different $π$ sources can introduce another non-trivial linear-$A_{\rm ch}$ term due to their different multiplicity fluctuations and anisotropic flows. We then study the effect of neutral cluster (resonance) decays as a mechanism for local charge conservation on the slope parameter of $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$. We find that the slope parameter is sensitive to the kinematics of those neutral clusters. Light resonances give positive slopes while heavy resonances give negative slopes. Local charge conservation from continuum cluster mass distribution can give a positive slope parameter comparable to experimental data. Our studies indicate that many non-CMW physics mechanisms can give rise to a $A_{\rm ch}$-dependent $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$ and the interpretation of $Δv_{2}(A_{\rm ch})$ in terms of the CMW is delicate.

## Key claim

Many non-CMW physics mechanisms, including non-flow correlations, multiple pion sources with different multiplicity fluctuations and anisotropic flows, and local charge conservation from neutral cluster decays, can each produce a linear charge-asymmetry dependence in the pion elliptic flow difference, making the interpretation of this observable as a signature of the chiral magnetic wave ambiguous.

## Method

The authors used toy Monte Carlo simulations to generate pions with Poisson multiplicity fluctuations, input anisotropic flows, and controlled non-flow correlations (like-sign or unlike-sign pairs with identical or back-to-back azimuthal angles). They also simulated resonance decays (\(\rho^0\), \(K_s\), \(\omega\), \(\eta\)) and neutral clusters with a continuum mass spectrum, applying acceptance cuts of \(0.15 < p_T < 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 1\), and calculated elliptic flow via the Q-cumulant method using single-sign reference particles to remove trivial linear terms.

## Key numbers

- The trivial slope from unlike-sign close-pair non-flow was \(r_{\rm triv} = 2.28\%\) for \(\Delta v_2(A_{\rm ch})\) in the toy model with input \(v_2 = 4\%\).
- Like-sign non-flow correlations (20% of pions paired with same sign) gave a positive slope \(r = 1.63\%\) for \(\Delta v_2(A_{\rm ch})\) after removing the trivial term.
- The slope parameter from multiple pion sources (primordial pions plus decays of \(K_s\), \(\rho^0\), \(\rho^\pm\), \(\omega\), \(\eta\)) with primordial-to-decay ratio 0.94 was approximately \(-0.4\%\).
- Neutral clusters with an exponential mass spectrum \(\rho(m) = \exp(-m/T_{\rm eff})\) with \(T_{\rm eff} = 300\) MeV gave a positive slope \(r = 1.11\%\) for \(\Delta v_2(A_{\rm ch})\).
- Replacing independent primordial pions with LCC-correlated pions from continuum-mass clusters in the multiple-source simulation yielded a slope \(r \sim 0.85\%\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of backgrounds to the chiral magnetic wave observable beyond the local charge conservation mechanism studied by Bzdak (2013) and the isospin chemical potential effect of Hatta (2015). It demonstrates that non-flow correlations, which were previously considered small for pion flow measurements, can significantly affect the small difference between \(\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\) elliptic flows. The results indicate that quantitative extraction of CMW signals requires detailed modeling of resonance decay kinematics, multiplicity fluctuations, and non-flow contributions, motivating more extensive background studies before firm conclusions can be drawn from experimental data.
