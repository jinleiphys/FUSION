# The two-nucleon system at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order

> We consider the two-nucleon system at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N^3LO) in chiral effective field theory. The two-nucleon potential at N^3LO consists of one-, two- and three-pion exchanges and a set of contact interactions with zero, two and four derivatives. In addition, one has to take into account various isospin-breaking and relativistic corrections. We employ spectral function regularization for the multi-pion exchanges. Within this framework, it is shown that the three-pion exchange contribution is negligibly small. The low-energy constants (LECs) related to pion-nucleon vertices are taken consistently from studies of pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory. The total of 26 four-nucleon LECs has been determined by a combined fit to some np and pp phase shifts from the Nijmegen analysis together with the nn scattering length. The description of nucleon-nucleon scattering and the deuteron observables at N^3LO is improved compared to the one at NLO and NNLO. The theoretical uncertainties in observables are estimated based on the variation of the cut-offs in the spectral function representation of the potential and in the regulator utilized in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key claim

The N³LO chiral two-nucleon potential, when regularized via spectral function regularization, yields an improved description of nucleon-nucleon scattering and deuteron observables compared to NLO and NNLO, but the three-pion exchange contribution is negligibly small and can be neglected; theoretical uncertainties are estimated by varying the cut-offs in the spectral function representation and in the regulator of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Method

The authors constructed the two-nucleon potential at N³LO in chiral effective field theory, including one-, two-, and three-pion exchanges plus contact interactions with up to four derivatives, along with isospin-breaking and relativistic corrections. They employed spectral function regularization for the multi-pion exchanges to separate long- and short-distance contributions, and determined the 26 four-nucleon low-energy constants by fitting to np and pp phase shifts from the Nijmegen analysis together with the nn scattering length. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved using a relativistic expression for the kinetic energy, and theoretical uncertainties were estimated by varying the cut-offs in the spectral function representation and the regulator.

## Key numbers

- The total of 26 four-nucleon low-energy constants (LECs) was determined by a combined fit to np and pp phase shifts from the Nijmegen analysis together with the nn scattering length.
- The three-pion exchange contribution reaches at most 2%–8% of the corresponding N³LO two-pion exchange contribution for distances r > 0.5 fm, depending on the choice of the spectral function cut-off.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was taken as g_πN = 13.1, corresponding to g_A = 1.29 (instead of 1.26) to account for the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy.
- The N³LO two-pion exchange potential includes contributions from the LECs c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄ and from the scale-independent LECs d̅₁, d̅₂, d̅₃, d̅₅, d̅₁₄, d̅₁₅.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to the two-nucleon system to N³LO, improving upon the earlier NLO and NNLO calculations by Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000, 2002). It differs from the N³LO potential of Entem and Machleidt (2003) by using spectral function regularization instead of dimensional regularization for two-pion exchange, by performing systematic variations of cut-offs to estimate theoretical uncertainties, and by employing a relativistic version of the Schrödinger equation. The results enable precision calculations in few-nucleon systems and provide a foundation for consistently deriving two- and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory.
