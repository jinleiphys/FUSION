# New Baryons in the Delta eta and Delta omega Channels

> The decays of excited nonstrange baryons into the final states Delta eta and Delta omega are examined in a relativized quark pair creation model. The wavefunctions and parameters of the model are fixed by previous calculations of N pi and N pi pi, etc., decays through various quasi-two body channels including N eta and N omega. Our results show that the combination of thresholds just below the region of interest and the isospin selectivity of these channels should allow the discovery of several new baryons in such experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the Delta eta and Delta omega decay channels, due to their isospin selectivity (only I=3/2 Delta* resonances can couple) and thresholds just below the mass region of interest, should allow the discovery of several new baryons that are "missing" from previous N pi analyses, provided the experimental difficulties of reconstructing these final states can be overcome.

## Method

The authors used a relativized quark pair creation model (3P0 model) with wavefunctions from the relativized quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986) to calculate decay amplitudes for excited nonstrange baryons into Delta eta and Delta omega final states. Model parameters were fixed by previous calculations of N pi, N pi pi, N eta, and N omega decays. The decay momentum was determined using PDG central masses for seen resonances and predicted masses for missing states, with integration over the Delta lineshape to account for states below nominal threshold.

## Key numbers

- Nominal Delta eta threshold: 1780 MeV; effective threshold (integrating over Delta lineshape): 1630 MeV
- Nominal Delta omega threshold: approximately 2010 MeV; effective threshold: approximately 1860 MeV
- Mass predictions for lightest missing Delta states: [Delta 1/2+]_1 around 1800-1850 MeV; [Delta 3/2+]_4 around 1950-2000 MeV
- Largest Delta eta amplitude among N=1,2 band states: Delta(2000)F35 (two-star state) with total amplitude 7.0 (+2.9/-5.1) MeV^(1/2)
- Missing [Delta 3/2+]_4(1985) amplitude in Delta eta: 4.3 (+1.9/-2.5) MeV^(1/2)
- Delta eta amplitude for [Delta 1/2+]_1(1835): 3.2 (+4.1/-3.1) MeV^(1/2)
- Delta omega amplitude for [Delta 9/2-]_1(2295): 9.8 (+2.2/-5.1) MeV^(1/2)
- Delta omega amplitude for [Delta 7/2-]_1(2230): 4.4 (+3.0/-2.4) MeV^(1/2)
- Delta omega amplitude for [Delta 9/2+]_2(2505): 4.3 (+1.5/-1.8) MeV^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of baryon decays (Capstick and Roberts, 1993, 1994) to the Delta eta and Delta omega channels, which had been previously considered only by Bijker, Iachello and Leviatan (1997) using an algebraic model with pointlike mesons. The paper addresses the problem of "missing" baryon states predicted by quark potential models (Isgur and Karl, 1978; Capstick and Isgur, 1986) that have weak N pi couplings and thus evade detection in N pi elastic scattering. The results enable experimental searches at Jefferson Lab (TJNAF) and the AGS Crystal Barrel detector, suggesting specific states that could be discovered or confirmed in these channels.
