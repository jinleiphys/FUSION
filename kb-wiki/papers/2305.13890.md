# Nonlocal optical potential in the inelastic deuteron scattering off $^{24}$Mg

> Nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potential with rotational quadrupole deformation enabling the excitation of the ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(2^+)$ state is developed; it fits well the proton-${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ elastic and inelastic differential cross section in the beam energy range from 30 to 45 MeV per nucleon. The inelastic deuteron-${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ scattering leading to the excited ${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}(2^+)$ state is studied in the same energy regime by solving the three-body Faddeev-type equations for transition operators. Effects of the optical potential nonlocality are evaluated by comparison with local models. Significant effects on the inelastic differential cross section are found at forward angles up to the first peak and at larger angles beyond the second peak. Nonlocal optical potential provides a simultaneous reasonable reproduction of the experimental data for the elastic and inelastic proton-${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ and deuteron-${}^{24}\mathrm{Mg}$ scattering, not achieved using local potentials.

## Key claim

Nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potentials with rotational quadrupole deformation provide a simultaneous reasonable reproduction of experimental data for elastic and inelastic proton-²⁴Mg and deuteron-²⁴Mg scattering, a result not achieved using local potentials; however, the most visible effects of nonlocality occur at forward angles below the first peak where no experimental data are available, and at larger angles beyond the second peak where nonlocal models are clearly favored over local ones.

## Method

The authors developed a nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potential with rotational quadrupole deformation, parametrized with Woods-Saxon radial functions and a Gaussian nonlocality function with range ρ = 1 fm, and fitted it to proton-²⁴Mg elastic and inelastic differential cross section data at beam energies 30.4, 34.9, 39.9, and 44.9 MeV. They then solved three-body Faddeev-type (AGS) equations for transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation to describe deuteron-²⁴Mg inelastic scattering at deuteron beam energies 60, 70, 80, and 90 MeV, using the fitted nucleon-nucleus potentials together with a CD Bonn neutron-proton potential.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ ranges from 0.50 to 0.57 across five parameter sets fitted to proton-²⁴Mg data
- Deformation length δ₂ = β₂ R_V0 varies within 5%, ranging from 1.56 to 1.64 fm
- Nonlocality range fixed to ρ = 1 fm
- For local potentials fitted for comparison, β₂ = 0.52 and 0.55, with δ₂ = 1.73 and 1.77 fm
- The ²⁴Mg(2⁺) excited state has excitation energy 1.369 MeV
- Beam energies studied: proton energies 30.4, 34.9, 39.9, and 44.9 MeV; deuteron energies 60, 70, 80, and 90 MeV
- Spin-orbit strength fixed to V_s = 7.5 MeV·fm² for nonlocal potentials and 6.0 MeV·fm² for local potentials

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of deuteron stripping and pickup reactions (Deltuva 2023) by applying nonlocal optical potentials with core excitation to inelastic deuteron scattering, a reaction type where nonlocality effects were previously unexplored. It improves upon earlier calculations using the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method (Chau 2011, Gomez-Ramos 2017) and Faddeev-type theory (Deltuva 2016), which used global optical potential parametrizations such as Chapel Hill 89 (CH89) and Koning-Delaroche (KD) that provided reasonable but imperfect descriptions of nucleon-²⁴Mg scattering data. The nonlocal potential enables a simultaneous description of both proton-²⁴Mg and deuteron-²⁴Mg scattering data that was not achieved in previous studies, and the nonlocality effect in elastic deuteron scattering is consistent with earlier work on ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca nuclei (Deltuva 2009).
