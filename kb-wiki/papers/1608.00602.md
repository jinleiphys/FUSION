# Compact stars with strongly coupled quark matter in a strong magnetic field

> Some time ago we have derived from the QCD Lagrangian an equation of state (EOS) for the cold quark matter, which can be considered an improved version of the MIT bag model EOS. Compared to the latter, our equation of state reaches higher values of the pressure at comparable baryon densities. This feature is due to perturbative corrections and also to non-perturbative effects. Later we applied this EOS to the study of compact stars, discussing the absolute stability of quark matter and computing the mass-radius relation for self-bound (strange) stars. We found maximum masses of the sequences with more than two solar masses, in agreement with the recent experimental observations. In the present work we include the magnetic field in the equation of state and study how it changes the stability conditions and the mass-radius curves.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously derived "mQCD" equation of state for cold quark matter to include strong magnetic fields up to \(5 \times 10^{17}\) G, and find that the maximum stellar masses remain above two solar masses even in the presence of such fields, provided the pressure anisotropy between parallel and perpendicular components remains modest (less than 10%).

## Method

The authors start from the QCD Lagrangian, split the gluon field into soft and hard components, and apply a mean-field approximation to the hard gluons, yielding an effective Lagrangian with a dynamical gluon mass \(m_G\) and a bag-like term \(\mathcal{B}_{QCD}\). They then solve the Dirac equation in a homogeneous magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis, obtaining Landau-level quantized energies for quarks and electrons, and compute the energy density, parallel pressure, and perpendicular pressure at zero temperature. The equation of state is used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations for non-rotating, spherically symmetric compact stars, imposing chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, baryon number conservation, and the absolute stability condition for strange quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stellar masses found with the mQCD equation of state: \(2.05\,M_\odot\) (mQCD) and \(1.89\,M_\odot\) (MIT bag model) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{16}\) G, with \(\rho_B = 2.6\,\rho_0\).
- For \(\mathcal{B}_{QCD}=53\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and fixed \(\xi\), the largest masses are \(2.22\,M_\odot\) (perpendicular pressure) and \(2.04\,M_\odot\) (parallel pressure) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G.
- For \(\mathcal{B}_{QCD}=61\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and fixed \(\xi\), the largest masses are \(2.06\,M_\odot\) (perpendicular) and \(1.93\,M_\odot\) (parallel) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G.
- For \(\xi=0.0015\) MeV\(^{-1}\) and fixed \(\mathcal{B}_{QCD}\), the largest masses are \(2.20\,M_\odot\) (perpendicular) and \(2.06\,M_\odot\) (parallel) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G.
- For the MIT bag model (\(\xi=0\) MeV\(^{-1}\)), the largest masses are \(2.08\,M_\odot\) (perpendicular) and \(1.92\,M_\odot\) (parallel) at \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G.
- The pressure anisotropy splitting starts at \(B \simeq 10^{17}\) G; at \(B = 5 \times 10^{17}\) G the difference between parallel and perpendicular pressures is less than 10%.
- The maximum baryon density for which a stability window exists is \(\rho_B \simeq 3.7\,\rho_0\); beyond this value no stable quark star is found.
- No stable quark star is found for magnetic fields above \(B \simeq 5 \times 10^{18}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier mQCD equation of state (Fogaca et al., 2012) to include strong magnetic fields, building on studies of magnetized quark matter in the MIT bag model and NJL model (e.g., Dexheimer et al., 2012; Paulucci et al., 2011). The results show that the mQCD model can support stars with masses above two solar masses even at fields up to \(5 \times 10^{17}\) G, consistent with observations of massive pulsars such as PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04\,M_\odot\)) and PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01 \pm 0.04\,M_\odot\)). The work enables future studies of magnetized strange quark stars, though it notes that for fields above \(10^{17}\) G the pressure anisotropy becomes significant, requiring beyond-TOV treatments for accurate modeling.
