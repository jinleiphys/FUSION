# Equation of State of nuclear matter in a Virial expansion of nucleons and nuclei

> We study the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter at subnuclear density in a Virial expansion for a nonideal gas. The gas consists of neutrons, protons, alpha particles, and 8980 species of nuclei with $A \ge 12$ and masses from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM). At very low density, the Virial expansion reduces to nuclear statistical equilibrium. At higher density, the Virial results match smoothly to the relativistic mean field results discussed in our previous paper. We tabulate the resulting EOS at over 73,000 grid points in the temperature range $T$ = 0.158 to 15.8 MeV, the density range $n_B$ = 10$^{-8}$ to 0.1 fm$^{-3}$, and the proton fraction range $Y_P$ = 0.05 to 0.56. In the future we plan to match these low density results to our earlier high density mean field results, and generate a full EOS table for use in supernova and neutron star merger simulations. This Virial EOS is exact in the low density limit.

## Key claim

The Virial expansion provides an equation of state (EOS) for subnuclear density nuclear matter that is exact in the low-density limit, but at higher densities it must be matched to relativistic mean field results via a thermodynamically consistent interpolation scheme, with the transition density depending on temperature and proton fraction.

## Method

The authors performed a Virial expansion to second order in fugacities for a nonideal gas composed of neutrons, protons, alpha particles, and 8980 species of heavy nuclei (A ≥ 12) with masses from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM). They included second virial coefficients for nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-alpha, and alpha-alpha elastic scattering, and incorporated Coulomb interactions between nuclei and the electron background via a Wigner-Seitz approximation. The resulting EOS was tabulated at 73,840 grid points across temperature T = 0.158 to 15.8 MeV, baryon density n_B = 10^{-8} to 0.1 fm^{-3}, and proton fraction Y_P = 0.05 to 0.56.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: T = 0.158 to 15.8 MeV (20 grid points)
- Baryon density range: n_B = 10^{-8} to 0.1 fm^{-3} (71 grid points)
- Proton fraction range: Y_P = 0.05 to 0.56 (52 grid points)
- Total grid points: 73,840 in the three-dimensional parameter space
- Computation required 1,000 CPU days on Indiana University's supercomputer clusters
- Transition from Virial to Hartree mean field EOS occurs at density approximately 3.98 × 10^{-3} fm^{-3} for T = 1 MeV and 3.16 MeV at Y_P = 0.3
- At T = 1 MeV, n_B = 10^{-4} fm^{-3}, Y_P = 0.2: total mass fraction of heavy nuclei is 56.5%
- At T = 1 MeV, n_B = 10^{-4} fm^{-3}, Y_P = 0.4: total mass fraction of heavy nuclei is 99.1%
- At T = 3.16 MeV, n_B = 10^{-3} fm^{-3}, Y_P = 0.4: total mass fraction of heavy nuclei is 22.3% (free neutrons 24.3%, free protons 6.3%, alpha particles 47.1%)

## Context

This work extends the Virial expansion approach previously applied to low-density matter with neutrons, protons, and alpha particles by Horowitz and Schwenk (2005) to include thousands of heavy nuclei species from the FRDM mass table. It provides a systematic improvement over nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) models used in earlier EOS tables such as Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and H. Shen, Toki, Oyamatsu, and Sumiyoshi (1998), which employed a single-nucleus approximation. The resulting Virial EOS is designed to be matched to the relativistic mean field results of Shen, Horowitz, and Teige (2010) for intermediate and high densities, enabling the generation of a full EOS table for use in supernova and neutron star merger simulations.
