# Isospin mixing and Fermi transitions: Selfconsistent deformed mean field calculations and beyond

> We study Fermi transitions and isospin mixing in an isotopic chain 70-78 Kr considering various approximations that use the same Skyrme-Hartree-Fock single particle basis. We study Coulomb effects as well as the effect of BCS and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) correlations. A measure of isospin mixing in the approximate ground state is defined by means of the expectation value of the isospin operator squared in N=Z nuclei (which is generalized to N different from Z nuclei). Starting from strict Hartree-Fock approach without Coulomb, it is shown that the isospin breaking is negligible, on the order of a few per thousand for (N-Z)=6, increasing to a few percent with Coulomb. Pairing correlations induce rather large isospin mixing and Fermi transitions of the forbidden type (beta- for N<Z, and beta+ for N>Z). The enhancement produced by BCS correlations is compensated to a large extent by QRPA correlations induced by isospin conserving residual interactions that tend to restore isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper shows that isospin mixing in the ground states of Kr isotopes is negligible in the Hartree-Fock approximation without Coulomb force, but pairing correlations induce large isospin mixing and forbidden Fermi transitions; however, this enhancement is largely compensated by QRPA correlations from isospin-conserving residual interactions, which tend to restore isospin symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (using the SG2 Skyrme force) calculations for the isotopic chain 70-78Kr, with and without Coulomb interactions and with and without BCS pairing correlations. They then extended the calculations to include proton-neutron QRPA correlations using a residual Fermi interaction of the form χ_F (τ₁·τ₂) derived consistently from the same Skyrme force, and computed Fermi transition strengths and the expectation value of the isospin operator squared as a measure of isospin mixing.

## Key numbers

- In the HF limit without Coulomb and without pairing, isospin mixing is zero for N=Z nuclei (⟨T⊥²⟩₀ = 0).
- With Coulomb force included in HF (no pairing), isospin mixing is on the order of a few percent for N≠Z isotopes.
- For ⁷²Kr (N=Z) with realistic pairing gaps (Δ ~ 1.5 MeV), ⟨T⊥²⟩₀ reaches a maximum of about 2.2.
- For ⁷⁴Kr with prolate deformation (β ~ 0.4) and no pairing, ⟨J⊥²⟩ ≈ 90; pairing reduces this to about 32 for Δ = 1.5 MeV.
- The Ikeda sum rule S_F⁻ - S_F⁺ = N - Z is always satisfied in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of β-decay strength functions in the A ≈ 60-80 region by the same authors (Alvarez-Rodriguez et al., 2004) and addresses the question of isospin breaking in selfconsistent mean field calculations, which is relevant to parity violation experiments on ¹²C (Donnelly, 1979) and tests of CKM matrix unitarity from superallowed Fermi transitions (Towner, 1992). The paper contrasts with shell-model calculations (e.g., Zuker, 1994) where isospin is an exact quantum number, and shows that while pairing correlations artificially enhance isospin mixing, QRPA correlations with consistent residual interactions largely restore isospin symmetry. The results enable more reliable calculations of Fermi transitions in proton-rich nuclei and clarify that isospin projection is not warranted for these systems, unlike angular momentum projection for deformed nuclei.
