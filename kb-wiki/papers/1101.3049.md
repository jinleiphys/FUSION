# Determining the nuclear form factor for detection of dark matter in the relativistic mean field theory

> In this work, we derive the nuclear form factor for the spin-independent collision between the WIMPs and nucleus in terms of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Comparison with the traditional form factors which are commonly used in literature is given and it is found that our results are slightly above that of the 2PF model by 4% to 8%, but deviate from the Helm form factor by 15% to 25% for the whole recoil energy spectrum of 0$\sim $ 100 keV. Moreover, taking Xe and Ge as examples, we show the dependence of the form factor on the recoil energy.

# Determining the nuclear form factor for detection of dark matter in the relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The nuclear form factor for spin-independent WIMP-nucleus scattering derived from relativistic mean field (RMF) theory yields results that are 4% to 8% above the two-parameter Fermi (2PF) model form factor, but deviate from the Helm form factor by 15% to 25% across the full recoil energy spectrum of 0 to 100 keV. The RMF-based form factor is claimed to be more accurate because it is derived from a self-consistent theoretical framework rather than from parameterized density models fitted to electron scattering data.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with nonlinear sigma and omega terms (the TM1 parameter set) to compute nuclear densities for several nuclei including 16O, 23Na, 40Ca, 72Ge, 127I, 132Xe, and 208Pb. They solved the Dirac equation for nucleons in the mean fields of sigma, omega, rho mesons and the electromagnetic field, iterating until self-consistency was achieved, then performed the Fourier transform of the resulting nuclear density to obtain the form factor F(q) for spin-independent WIMP-nucleus scattering. The RMF results were compared with the Helm form factor and the two-parameter Fermi (2PF) form factor using parameters from Duda (2006) and data from elastic electron scattering experiments.

## Key numbers

- For 72Ge at 10 keV recoil energy: RMF form factor squared |F(E)|^2 = 0.818, compared to 2PF = 0.802 and Helm = 0.710
- For 132Xe at 10 keV: RMF |F(E)|^2 = 0.576, compared to 2PF = 0.579 and Helm = 0.489
- For 72Ge at 50 keV: RMF |F(E)|^2 = 0.351, compared to 2PF = 0.324 and Helm = 0.278
- For 132Xe at 50 keV: RMF |F(E)|^2 = 0.035, compared to 2PF = 0.042 and Helm = 0.031
- For 72Ge at 100 keV: RMF |F(E)|^2 = 0.105, compared to 2PF = 0.098 and Helm = 0.083
- For 132Xe at 100 keV: RMF |F(E)|^2 = 0, same as 2PF and Helm
- RMF results are 2% to 8% above 2PF form factors for most of the energy region from 2 keV (momentum q approximately 0.1 fm^-1) to 200 keV (q approximately 0.9 fm^-1)
- RMF results are lower than 2PF by less than 1% at q less than or equal to 0.1 fm^-1
- RMF results are above the Helm form factor by about 15% to 25% at all recoil energy regions
- The TM1 parameter set used: M_N = 938 MeV, m_sigma = 511.198 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV, with coupling constants g_sigma = 10.0289, g_omega = 12.6139, g_rho = 4.6322

## Context

This work extends the application of relativistic mean field theory, originally developed by Walecka and successfully applied to nuclear ground-state observables by Ring (1990) and Meng (2006), to the problem of dark matter detection form factors. It provides a theoretically motivated alternative to the commonly used Helm form factor (Helm, 1956) and the two-parameter Fermi model (Jin, 1995; Duda, 2006), which are based on parameterized charge densities fitted to elastic electron scattering data. The RMF form factors enable more accurate extraction of the WIMP-nucleon cross section sigma_0 from direct detection experiments, particularly for detector materials such as Xe and Ge, and can serve as a reference for designing future dark matter detectors.
