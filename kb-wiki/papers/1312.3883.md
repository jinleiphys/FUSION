# Precise measurement of alpha-K for the 65.7-keV M4 transition in 119Sn: Extended test of internal-conversion theory

> We have measured the K-shell internal conversion coefficient, alpha-K, for the 65.7-keV M4 transition in 119Sn to be 1621(25). This result agrees well with Dirac-Fock calculations in which the effect of the K-shell atomic vacancy is accounted for, and disagrees with calculations in which the vacancy is ignored. This extends our precision tests of theory to Z = 50, the lowest Z yet measured.

## Key claim

The measured K-shell internal conversion coefficient for the 65.7-keV M4 transition in 119Sn, 1621(25), agrees with Dirac-Fock calculations that include the effect of the K-shell atomic vacancy and disagrees with calculations that ignore the vacancy, extending precision tests of internal-conversion theory to Z = 50, the lowest atomic number yet tested.

## Method

The authors activated a 6.8-micrometer-thick tin foil enriched to 98.8% in 118Sn with thermal neutrons to produce the 293.1-day isomer 119mSn, then recorded gamma-ray and x-ray spectra with a precisely calibrated HPGe detector after waiting nearly two years for short-lived impurities to decay. They extracted peak areas using the GF2 least-squares fitting program, corrected for impurity contributions from 182Ta, 75Se, 113Sn, and 125Sb using auxiliary measurements and known decay schemes, and determined the detector efficiency ratio at the relevant energies using a 109Cd calibration source and CYLTRAN Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key numbers

- Measured alpha_K for the 65.7-keV M4 transition in 119Sn: 1621(25), with a relative precision of ±1.5%
- The no-vacancy Dirac-Fock calculation gives alpha_K = 1544(1), which disagrees with the measurement by 3.1 standard deviations (Δ = +5.0(16)%)
- The frozen-orbital vacancy calculation gives alpha_K = 1618(1), agreeing with the measurement (Δ = +0.2(16)%)
- The SCF (self-consistent field of the ion) vacancy calculation gives alpha_K = 1603(1) (Δ = +1.1(16)%)
- The two vacancy-included calculations differ from the no-vacancy calculation by 4.8%
- The fluorescence yield for tin (Z=50) used was 0.860(4) from Scofield (1996)
- The efficiency ratio epsilon_gamma(65.7)/epsilon_K(25.8) was determined to be 1.043(8)

## Context

This work extends the authors' precision tests of internal-conversion theory from the previous range 55 < Z < 78 down to Z = 50, following their earlier measurements on 193Ir (Nica 2004, 2005), 134Cs (Nica 2007, 2008), 137Ba (Nica 2007, 2008), and 197Pt (Nica 2009). The results support the decision by Kibedi et al. (2008) to adopt the frozen-orbital approximation for the BrIcc database now used by the National Nuclear Data Center, and contradict the earlier systematic bias of 2-3% reported by Raman et al. (1973) when comparing measurements to Hager and Seltzer calculations.
