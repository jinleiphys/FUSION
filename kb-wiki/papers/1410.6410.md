# Four-body effects on 9Be + 208Pb scattering and fusion around the Coulomb barrier

> We investigate the 9Be + 208Pb elastic scattering and fusion at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The $^9$Be nucleus is described in a α+ α+ n three-body model, using the hyperspherical coordinate method. The scattering with $^{208}$Pb is then studied with the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, where the α+ α+ n continuum is approximated by a discrete number of pseudostates. Optical potentials for the $α+^{208}$Pb and $n+^{208}$Pb systems are taken from the literature. We present elastic-scattering and fusion cross sections at different energies, and investigate the convergence with respect to the truncation of the α+ α+ n continuum. A good agreement with experiment is obtained, considering that there is no parameter fitting. We show that continuum effects increase at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a full four-body CDCC treatment of 9Be (described as an α+α+n three-body system) scattering on 208Pb, using only literature optical potentials with no parameter fitting, reproduces experimental elastic scattering and fusion cross sections around the Coulomb barrier, with continuum effects becoming more important at lower energies.

## Method

The authors first solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for 9Be using the hyperspherical coordinate method with α+α and α+n interactions from the literature, adding a phenomenological three-body force to reproduce the experimental ground-state binding energy. They then applied the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method to the 9Be + 208Pb system, discretizing the α+α+n continuum into pseudostates and using α+208Pb and n+208Pb optical potentials from the literature to compute coupling potentials via Raynal-Revai coefficients. The coupled-channel equations were solved with R-matrix theory to obtain elastic scattering and total fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The 9Be ground state binding energy is −1.57 MeV with respect to the α+α+n threshold; the bare three-body model gave −3.12 MeV, requiring a three-body force with V0 = 2.7 MeV and ρ0 = 6 fm to reproduce the experimental value.
- The calculated 9Be r.m.s. radius is 2.36 fm (experiment: 2.45 ± 0.01 fm), quadrupole moment is 4.96 e·fm² (experiment: 5.29 ± 0.04 e·fm²), and magnetic moment is −1.33 μN (experiment: −1.18 μN).
- Elastic scattering calculations were performed at laboratory energies of 38, 44, 49, and 58 MeV, with a continuum cutoff energy of 10 MeV and including 9Be partial waves jπ = 3/2−, 5/2−, and 1/2+.
- Fusion cross sections were computed and compared to data from Dasgupta et al. (1999); the calculations overestimate the data at the lowest energies below the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC calculations that treated 9Be in a two-body cluster approximation (either 8Be+n or 5He+α), which suffered from the unbound nature of those subsystems and the lack of appropriate optical potentials. The three-body approach treats both configurations on equal footing and uses well-established α+208Pb and n+208Pb optical potentials. The results confirm earlier findings by Jha et al. (2014) that breakup effects enhance fusion below the Coulomb barrier, and the method enables future studies of other reactions involving three-body projectiles such as 6He or 11Li.
