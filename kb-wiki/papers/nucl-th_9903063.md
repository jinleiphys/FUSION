# Chemical and Thermal Freeze-Out Parameters from 1 to 200 A.GeV

> The present knowledge about hadrons produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions is compatible with chemical freeze-out happening when the energy density divided by the particle density reaches the value of 1 GeV. This observation is used to determine the energy dependence of the chemical freeze-out parameters T_{ch} and mu_B^{ch} for beam energies varying between 1 and 200 A.GeV. The consequences of this energy dependence are studied for various particle ratios. Predictions for particle ratios at beam energy 40 A.GeV are presented. The conditions for thermal freeze-out are also determined. These correspond either to an energy density of 45 MeV/fm**3 or to a particle density of 0.05/fm**3.

## Key claim

Chemical freeze-out in relativistic heavy ion collisions occurs when the average energy per hadron drops below 1 GeV, independent of beam energy, beam particle, or system size; this condition is used to extract the energy dependence of the chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch and baryon chemical potential mu_B^ch across the full range from 1 to 200 A.GeV.

## Method

The authors combine the phenomenological observation that all chemical freeze-out points correspond to an energy density divided by particle density of 1 GeV with a parameterization of the pion multiplicity per participant nucleon as a function of beam energy, taken from the work of Gazdzicki (1995). By intersecting the freeze-out curve (constant energy per particle) with the pion-per-participant curve at each beam energy, they extract T_ch and mu_B^ch for beam energies between 1 and 200 A.GeV. For thermal freeze-out, they compile existing results from transverse momentum spectra analyses and compare them to curves of constant energy density (45 MeV/fm^3) and constant particle density (0.05/fm^3).

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out occurs when energy density divided by particle density equals 1 GeV, independent of beam energy or system.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch converges toward a maximum value of approximately 160 MeV at high beam energies.
- Thermal freeze-out conditions correspond either to an energy density of 45 MeV/fm^3 or a particle density of 0.05/fm^3.
- The K+/pi+ ratio reaches a maximum value of about 0.2 and stays approximately constant beyond that point.
- The K+/K- ratio varies by two orders of magnitude over the energy range from 1 to 200 A.GeV.
- Predictions for particle ratios at beam energy 40 A.GeV are presented in Table 2 of the paper.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Cleymans and Redlich (1998) that chemical freeze-out points from GSI/SIS, BNL/AGS, and CERN/SPS all lie on a curve of constant energy per particle (1 GeV). It provides a systematic method to interpolate freeze-out parameters between existing experimental energies and to predict particle ratios at new beam energies, such as 40 A.GeV. The thermal freeze-out analysis compiles results from multiple experimental collaborations including NA49, WA98, and NA44, and shows that all available data are consistent with either a fixed energy density or fixed particle density at freeze-out, though the data cannot distinguish between these two possibilities.
