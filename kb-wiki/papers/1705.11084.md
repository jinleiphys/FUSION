# Dynamics of Fragment Formation in Neutron Rich Matter

> Background: Neutron stars are astronomical systems with nucleons submitted to extreme conditions. Due to the longer range coulomb repulsion between protons, the system has structural inhomogeneities. Several interactions tailored to reproduce nuclear matter plus screened Coulomb term reproduce these inhomogeneities known as nuclear pasta. These structural inhomogeneities, located in the crust of neutron stars, can also arise in expanding systems depending on the thermodynamic conditions (temperature, proton fraction, ...) and the expansion velocity. Purpose: We aim to find the dynamics of the fragments formation for expanding systems simulated according to the little big bang model. This expansion resembles the evolution of neutron stars merger. Method: We study the dynamics of the nucleons with semiclassical molecular dynamics models. Starting with an equilibrium configuration, we expand the system homogeneously until we arrive to an asymptotic configuration (i. e. very low final densities). We study, with four different cluster recognition algorithms, the fragment distribution throughout this expansion and the dynamics of the cluster formation. Results: Studying the topology of the equilibrium states, before the expansion, we reproduced the known pasta phases plus a novel phase we called pregnocchi, consisting of proton aggregates embedded in a neutron sea. We have identified different fragmentation regimes, depending on the initial temperature and fragment velocity. In particular, for the already mentioned pregnocchi, a neutron cloud surrounds the clusters during the early stages of the expansion, resulting in systems that give rise to configurations compatibles with the emergence of r-proccess. Conclusions: These calculations pave the way to a comparision between Earth experiments and neutron star studies.

## Key claim
The authors identify a novel pasta phase called "pregnocchi" (proton aggregates embedded in a neutron sea) that emerges only for certain potential parametrizations (New Medium and SSP) at low proton fractions, and show that during expansion these preexisting aggregates remain surrounded by a neutron cloud, creating conditions compatible with r-process nucleosynthesis. The abstract's claim of "configurations compatibles with the emergence of r-process" is hedged by the dependence on the specific cluster recognition algorithm used (ECRA being the most stable) and the expansion velocity.

## Method
The authors performed semiclassical molecular dynamics simulations of neutron-rich matter with N=5500 nucleons under periodic boundary conditions, using three different potential parametrizations (CMD, New Medium, and SSP) with screened Coulomb interaction (Thomas-Fermi approximation, screening length λ=20 fm). Starting from equilibrium configurations at densities ρ=0.05 fm⁻³ and ρ=0.08 fm⁻³ with proton fractions x=0.1 to x=0.4 and temperatures T=0.1 MeV to T=0.8 MeV, they expanded the simulation box isotropically at constant rates (fast: η̇=0.01 s⁻¹; slow: η̇=0.0001 s⁻¹) following the "microscopic big bang" method. Fragment distributions were analyzed using four cluster recognition algorithms: Minimum Spanning Tree (MST), Minimum Spanning Tree with Energy (MSTE), proton-MST with neutron cloud (MSTpC), and Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm with Binary Fusion Model (ECRA-BFM).

## Key numbers
- System size: N=5500 particles in cubical boxes with periodic boundary conditions
- Densities studied: ρ=0.05 fm⁻³ and ρ=0.08 fm⁻³
- Proton fractions: x=0.1, x=0.2, x=0.4
- Temperatures: T=0.1 MeV and T=0.8 MeV
- Screening length: λ=20 fm
- Expansion rates: fast η̇=0.01 s⁻¹ and slow η̇=0.0001 s⁻¹
- For pregnocchi at x=0.1, ρ=0.05 fm⁻³, T=0.8 MeV: slow expansion produced fragments up to mass 60 (20 protons); fast expansion produced fragments up to mass 20 (6 protons)
- For lasagna at x=0.4, ρ=0.05 fm⁻³, T=0.1 MeV: fast expansion had ~4% lone neutrons; slow expansion had ~0.1% lone neutrons
- Initial equilibrium density: ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E(ρ₀)=16 MeV/nucleon, compressibility 250 MeV
- Cutoff radius for nuclear potential: r_c=5.4 fm

## Context
This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of nuclear pasta phases by Dorso et al. (2012) and Alcain et al. (2014), who found new pasta phases and their neutrino opacity properties. It builds on the classical molecular dynamics (CMD) approach used in heavy-ion reaction studies by Chernomoretz et al. (2002) and the "little big bang" expansion method from Dorso and Strachan (1996). The study directly compares three potential parametrizations (CMD, New Medium, SSP) and shows that the choice of interaction qualitatively affects the pasta structures, with the New Medium and SSP potentials producing the novel "pregnocchi" phase not seen in CMD. The work enables future comparison between Earth-based heavy-ion experiments and neutron star merger simulations, particularly for understanding r-process nucleosynthesis conditions, and extends the expanding neutron star matter study by Caplan et al. (2015).
