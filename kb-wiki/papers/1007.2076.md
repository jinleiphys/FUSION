# A Modified Hard Thermal Loop Perturbation Theory

> Based on the external perturbation that disturbs the system only slightly from its equilibrium position we make the Taylor expansion of the pressure of a quark gas. It turns out that the first term was used in the literature to construct a Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) within the variation principle of the lowest order of the thermal mass parameter. Various thermodynamic quantities within the 1-loop HTLpt encountered overcounting of the leading order (LO) contribution and also required a separation scale for soft and hard momenta. Using same variational principle we reconstruct the HTLpt at the first derivative level of the pressure that takes into account the effect of the variation of the external source through the conserved density fluctuation. This modification markedly improves those quantities in 1-loop HTLpt in a simple way instead of pushing the calculation to a considerably more complicated 2-loop HTLpt. Moreover, the results also agree with those obtained in the 2-loop approximately self-consistent Φ-derivable Hard Thermal Loop resummation. We also discuss how this formalism can be extended for the higher order contributions

# A Modified Hard Thermal Loop Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The authors modify the standard Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) by constructing it at the first derivative level of the pressure with respect to an external source, rather than using the zeroth-order term alone. This modification corrects the overcounting of leading-order contributions and eliminates the need for an ad hoc separation scale between soft and hard momenta in 1-loop HTLpt, producing results that agree with the more complicated 2-loop approximately self-consistent Φ-derivable HTL resummation.

## Method
The authors start from a Taylor expansion of the pressure of a quark gas around an external perturbation that slightly disturbs the system from equilibrium. They identify the first derivative of the pressure with respect to the external source as the conserved density, and the second derivative as the conserved density fluctuation (susceptibility). Using the HTL Lagrangian with a covariant derivative that includes a background source term, they derive expressions for the quark number density, pressure, entropy density, and quark number susceptibility (QNS) at leading order in the modified 1-loop HTLpt, decomposing contributions into quasiparticle and Landau damping parts.

## Key numbers
- Quark number susceptibility in free case: χ^f = N_f T^2 (for N_c = 3, N_f flavors)
- Perturbative QNS expansion at μ=0: χ_p/χ_f = 1 - (1/2)(g/π)^2 + √(1 + N_f/6)(g/π)^3 - (3/4)(g/π)^4 log(1/g) + O(g^4)
- Quark thermal mass squared: m_q^2 = (g^2/6)(T^2 + μ^2/π^2)
- Free entropy density: S^f = N_c N_f (7π^2 T^3/45 + μ^2 T/3)

## Context
This work extends the HTLpt formalism developed by Andersen et al. (2000s) for pressure at the 1-loop level, which suffered from over-inclusion of order g^2 effects and required a separation scale. It also relates to the 2-loop approximately self-consistent Φ-derivable approach by Blaizot et al. (2000s), which produced correct leading-order and plasmon effects for thermodynamic quantities and QNS. The modified formalism enables consistent leading-order calculations of thermodynamic quantities and QNS without pushing to the more complicated 2-loop HTLpt, and the authors discuss how the formalism can be extended to higher orders.
