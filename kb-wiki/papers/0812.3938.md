# Perturbative nuclear physics

> We present a new formulation of effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions which treats pion interactions perturbatively, and we offer evidence that the expansion converges satisfactorily to third order in the expansion, which we have computed analytically for s and d wave NN scattering. Starting with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) expansion about the nontrivial fixed point corresponding to infinite NN scattering length, we cure the convergence problems with that theory by summing to all orders the singular short distance part of the pion tensor interaction. This method makes possible a host of high precision analytic few-body calculations in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The authors present a modified version of the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory expansion for nucleon-nucleon scattering that cures the convergence failure in the spin-triplet channel by summing to all orders the singular short-distance part of the pion tensor interaction, and they offer evidence that the expansion converges satisfactorily to third order (NNLO) for s- and d-wave scattering.

## Method

The authors replace the pion propagator with a modified version that adds two fictitious meson propagators: one with the same spin and isospin as the pion but with a heavy mass lambda and a negative sign (canceling the short-distance 1/r^3 tensor singularity), and another with isospin 1 and spin 0 that ensures the spin-singlet channel remains unchanged. They then compute all Feynman diagrams relevant for the 3S1, 3D1, and epsilon_1 partial wave channels to NNLO using dimensional regularization with renormalization scale mu = m_pi, treating lambda as O(Q) in the power counting.

## Key numbers

- The scale governing KSW expansion convergence is Lambda_NN = 16 pi f_pi^2 / (g_A^2 M) = 300 MeV
- The modified pion propagator uses a heavy mass scale lambda, with results shown for lambda = 750 MeV
- The coupling constants used are g_A ~ 1.25 and f_pi ~ 93 MeV
- The variation of phase shifts is studied over the range 600 MeV <= lambda <= 1000 MeV
- The KSW expansion fails to converge above p ~ 100 MeV in the spin-triplet channel
- The authors find that taking lambda ~ 750 MeV appears to optimize the expansion

## Context

This work extends the KSW expansion (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1996-1998) which successfully described spin-singlet NN scattering but failed to converge in the spin-triplet channel due to the singular tensor pion potential scaling as -1/r^3. The authors cure this by modifying the pion propagator in a manner reminiscent of Pauli-Villars regulation, making the lambda parameter analogous to the renormalization scale mu in perturbative QCD. This enables analytic few-body calculations to NNLO, with results that compare favorably to the NLO Weinberg expansion results of Epelbaum (1999) and the Nijmegen PWA93 partial wave analysis (Stoks, 1993).
