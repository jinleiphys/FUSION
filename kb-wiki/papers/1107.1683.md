# On Kinetic Theory of Energy Losses in Randomly Heterogeneous Medium

> We derive equation describing distribution of energy losses of the particle propagating in fractal medium with quenched and dynamic heterogeneities. We show that in the case of the medium with fractal dimension $2<D<3$ the losses $Δ$ are characterized by the sublinear anomalous dependence $Δ\sim x^α$ with power-law dependence on the distance $x$ from the surface and exponent $α=D-2$.

# On Kinetic Theory of Energy Losses in Randomly Heterogeneous Medium

## Key claim
The paper derives a generalized kinetic equation for the distribution of energy losses of a high-energy particle propagating through a fractal medium with quenched and dynamic heterogeneities, and shows that for fractal dimensions between 2 and 3, the average energy loss follows a sublinear anomalous power-law dependence on distance, with exponent α = D − 2, where D is the fractal dimension of the medium.

## Method
The authors formulate a microscopic model of energy loss as a sum of random positive energy losses at scattering events along a straight-line trajectory, with random distances between scattering centers governed by a probability density ψ(l). They derive a continuous-time-random-walk-like kinetic equation for the energy loss distribution f(Δ, x) and solve it using double Laplace transforms, assuming a fractal medium where the Laplace transform of the density of scatterings g(r) scales as (aq)^{−α} with α = D − 2. The solution is expressed in terms of the Wright function, and the average energy loss is computed analytically in the infrared limit x ≫ a.

## Key numbers
- For fractal dimension D in the range 2 < D < 3, the exponent α = D − 2 lies in the range 0 < α < 1.
- The average energy loss at depth x is ⟨Δ(x)⟩ = [ε̄ / Γ(1 + α)] (x/a)^{α}, where ε̄ is the average energy loss per scattering event and a is the scattering area.
- The distribution function f(Δ, x) = (1/ε̄)(a/x)^{α} W_α[ (Δ/ε̄)(a/x)^{α} ], where W_α is the Wright function.

## Context
This work extends the classical Landau theory of ionization energy losses in homogeneous amorphous media (Landau, 1944) to strongly inhomogeneous fractal media with power-law correlations. The approach is analogous to the Continuous Random Walk (CTRW) model used for thermal diffusion in fractal media (Klafter, Blumen, and Shlesinger, 1984; Shlesinger, 1984; Bouchaud, Koplik, and Widom, 1984; Isichenko and Volkov, 2003), but differs physically because the high-energy projectile follows a straight-line trajectory rather than a random walk. The results enable prediction of energy loss spectra in complex heterogeneous media where the structure function can be measured experimentally, with applications to radiation damage and particle propagation near critical points of phase transitions.
