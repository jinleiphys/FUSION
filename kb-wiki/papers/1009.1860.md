# Properties of the ground-state baryons in chiral perturbation theory

> We review recent progress in the understanding of low-energy baryon structure by means of chiral perturbation theory. In particular, we discuss the application of this formalism to the description of various properties such as the baryon-octet magnetic moments, the electromagnetic structure of decuplet resonances and the hyperon vector coupling $f_1(0)$. Moreover, we present the results on the chiral extrapolation of recent lattice QCD results on the lowest-lying baryon masses and we predict the corresponding baryonic sigma-terms.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (χPT) in a Lorentz covariant framework with a renormalization scheme that recovers power counting can achieve good convergence for low-energy baryon properties, in contrast to earlier heavy-baryon formulations that suffered from poor convergence and power-counting problems.

## Method

The authors apply the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) up to next-to-leading order (NLO) or next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), systematically including decuplet resonances via consistent couplings that remove unphysical spin-3/2 degrees of freedom. They fit the low-energy constants (LECs) to experimental data and to lattice QCD results from the PACS-CS and LHP collaborations, then use the fitted LECs to extrapolate baryon masses to the physical point and predict sigma terms via the Hellman-Feynman theorem.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole moments predicted for decuplet resonances: μ_Δ⁺⁺ = 6.0(1.0) μ_N and μ_Δ⁺ = 2.84(50) μ_N, with errors estimated from the NLO-to-LO ratio (50% of that ratio)
- Relative SU(3)_F-breaking corrections to the hyperon vector coupling f₁(0) (in %): ΛN channel = +0.1⁺¹·³₋₁·₀, ΣN channel = +8.7⁺⁴·²₋₃·₁, ΞΛ channel = +4.0⁺²·⁸₋₂·₁, ΞΣ channel = +1.7⁺²·²₋₁·₆
- Extracted CKM element V_us = 0.2176 ± 0.0029 ± Δ_V, where Δ_V accounts for systematic uncertainties from g₂
- Pion-nucleon sigma term from PACS-CS fit: σ_πN = 59(2)(17) MeV; from LHP fit: σ_πN = 61(2)(21) MeV
- Strange-nucleon sigma term from PACS-CS fit: σ_sN = −7(23)(25) MeV; from LHP fit: σ_sN = −4(20)(25) MeV
- Baryon-octet effective mass from experimental fit: M_B0^eff = 1.840(5) GeV; from PACS-CS fit: 1.76(7) GeV; from LHP fit: 1.85(8) GeV
- Baryon-decuplet effective mass from experimental fit: M_T0^eff = 1.519(2) GeV; from PACS-CS fit: 1.49(8) GeV; from LHP fit: 1.60(8) GeV

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz covariant BχPT approach previously developed by Fuchs et al. (2003) and Pascalutsa (2004) to systematically include decuplet resonances, addressing convergence problems that plagued earlier heavy-baryon formulations by Jenkins (1990, 1992) and infrared regularization by Becher (1999). The results enable model-independent extraction of V_us from hyperon semileptonic decays and provide chiral extrapolation tools for lattice QCD results on baryon masses, which had proven difficult to describe within heavy-baryon χPT as reported by the LHP and PACS-CS collaborations. The approach has been extended to heavy-light systems by Geng et al. (2010).
