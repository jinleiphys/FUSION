# Exact calculation of three-body contact interaction to second order

> For a system of fermions with a three-body contact interaction the second-order contributions to the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f)$ are calculated exactly. The three-particle scattering amplitude in the medium is derived in closed analytical form from the corresponding two-loop rescattering diagram. We compare the (genuine) second-order three-body contribution to $\bar E(k_f)\sim k_f^{10}$ with the second-order term due to the density-dependent effective two-body interaction, and find that the latter term dominates. The results of the present study are of interest for nuclear many-body calculations where chiral three-nucleon forces are treated beyond leading order via a density-dependent effective two-body interaction.

# Exact calculation of three-body contact interaction to second order

## Key claim

The genuine second-order three-body contribution to the energy per particle scales as \(k_f^{10}\) and is negative, but the second-order term from the density-dependent effective two-body interaction is repulsive and dominates by about a factor of two; however, the exact calculation shows that treating a three-body contact interaction solely through a density-dependent two-body interaction considerably overestimates second-order effects.

## Method

The author evaluates the two-loop rescattering diagram for three fermions in the medium, expanding the product of three Pauli-blocking factors into vacuum (\(B_0\)), one-body (\(B_1\)), and two-body (\(B_2\)) medium-correction terms. The vacuum divergence is renormalized with a Galilei-invariant three-body counterterm proportional to fourth powers of momenta, and the resulting in-medium scattering amplitude is integrated over three Fermi spheres to obtain the five-loop contribution to the energy per particle. The result is compared with the second-order contribution from the density-dependent effective two-body interaction derived by closing one nucleon line of the three-body contact vertex.

## Key numbers

- First-order energy per particle: \(\bar E(k_f) = -C_3 k_f^6 / (12\pi^4) = \Gamma_3 k_f^6\), with \(\Gamma_3 = -C_3/12\pi^4\).
- Density-dependent effective two-body coupling: \(\delta C_0(\rho) = C_3 k_f^3 / \pi^2\).
- Second-order three-body contribution: \(\bar E(k_f) = (37\pi/175) \Gamma_3^2 M k_f^{10} \{ \sqrt{3} \ln(k_f/\lambda_0) + \zeta_0 + \zeta_1 + \zeta_2 \} < 0\), with numerical constants \(\zeta_0 = -1.425\), \(\zeta_1 = -5.653\), \(\zeta_2 = -4.354 \pm 0.014\).
- Second-order contribution from density-dependent effective two-body interaction: \(\bar E(k_f) = (54/35) \Gamma_3^2 M k_f^{10} (11 - 2\ln 2) > 0\), with numerical factor \(14.83\).
- Numerical factor for genuine three-body second-order term: \(-7.59\) (excluding the logarithmic term).
- Maximum value of weighting function \(w(s)/8\) is \(2.65\) at \(s = 0.393\); maximum of \(\chi(x)\) is \(2.92\) at \(x = 0.621\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of three-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory, where leading-order three-body contact interactions are commonly mapped onto density-dependent effective two-body interactions for nuclear matter calculations (Achim 2007, Hebeler 2011). The exact second-order calculation for the simplest three-body contact interaction demonstrates that the density-dependent approximation overestimates second-order effects, supporting the need for complete second-order calculations with finite-range chiral three-nucleon forces (Epelbaum 2008, 3bodyn3lo). The results are relevant for improving nuclear many-body calculations that aim to reproduce empirical saturation properties of nuclear matter.
