# Direct and sequential radiative three-body reaction rates at low temperatures

> We investigate the low-temperature reaction rates for radiative capture processes of three particles. We compare direct and sequential capture mechanisms and rates using realistic phenomenological parametrizations of the corresponding photodissociation cross sections.Energy conservation prohibits sequential capture for energies smaller than that of the intermediate two-body structure. A finite width or a finite temperature allows this capture mechanism. We study generic effects of positions and widths of two- and three-body resonances for very low temperatures. We focus on nuclear reactions relevant for astrophysics, and we illustrate with realistic estimates for the $α$-$α$-$α$ and $α$-$α$-$n$ radiative capture processes. The direct capture mechanism leads to reaction rates which for temperatures smaller than 0.1 GK can be several orders of magnitude larger than those of the NACRE compilation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at very low temperatures (below about 0.1 GK), the direct three-body radiative capture mechanism can produce reaction rates several orders of magnitude larger than those predicted by the sequential two-step model, but this conclusion depends on the specific energies and widths of the intermediate two-body and three-body resonances, and the sequential model can still be used if finite resonance widths are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors compare direct and sequential radiative three-body capture rates using phenomenological parametrizations of photodissociation cross sections based on Breit-Wigner resonance shapes, with energy-dependent partial widths determined by penetration factors through Coulomb and centrifugal barriers. For the direct mechanism, the rate is computed from a single integral over the three-body photodissociation cross section, while the sequential mechanism involves a double integral over the two-body resonance formation cross section and the subsequent capture cross section. The comparison is performed for generic three-body systems with varying resonance energies and widths, and then applied to the specific cases of alpha-alpha-alpha (triple alpha) and alpha-alpha-neutron capture.

## Key numbers

- For three identical charged particles with three-body resonance energy E_R = 0.15 MeV and widths Gamma_R = 10^-5 MeV and Gamma_r = 10^-6 MeV, the ratio between direct and sequential rates reaches up to 12 orders of magnitude at T = 0.001 GK
- For the same system with E_r = 0.20 MeV (above E_R = 0.15 MeV) and Gamma_r = 10^-6 MeV, the sequential rate is up to 8 orders of magnitude smaller than the direct rate
- The direct capture mechanism leads to reaction rates that for temperatures smaller than 0.1 GK can be several orders of magnitude larger than those of the NACRE compilation
- For temperatures above about 0.03 GK, the sequential and direct rates agree when both resonances are narrow (Gamma_R = 10^-5 MeV, Gamma_r = 10^-6 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the comparison of direct versus sequential three-body capture mechanisms previously studied in the context of nuclear astrophysics, particularly for bridging the A=5 and A=8 gaps in nucleosynthesis. The authors build on formulations for sequential capture from Fowler (1967), Angulo (1999), and Sumiyoshi (2002), and on direct three-body methods from de Diego (2010) and Fedorov (2010). The paper enables systematic testing of whether the sequential approximation is reliable at low temperatures relevant for stellar environments, and provides a framework for computing rates when the intermediate two-body resonance is energetically inaccessible.
