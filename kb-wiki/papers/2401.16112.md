# Photoproduction of the $X(3872)$ beyond vector meson dominance: the open-charm coupled-channel mechanism

> Hidden-charm exotic hadrons will be searched for and investigated at future electron-ion colliders. For instance, the $X(3872)$ can be produced through the exclusive process $γp\to X(3872)p$. The vector meson dominance model has been commonly employed in estimating the cross sections of such processes. However, the coupled-channel production mechanism through open-charm meson-baryon intermediate states may play a crucial role. To assess the significance of such contributions, we estimate the cross section of the $γp\to X(3872)p$ reaction assuming the coupled-channel mechanism. For energies near the threshold, the total cross section is predicted to be of tens of nanobarns for $γp\to X(3872)p$, which can be measured at future experimental facilities. Furthermore, the open-charm coupled-channel mechanism leads to a distinct line shape of the total cross section that can be utilized to reveal the production dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the coupled-channel mechanism through open-charm meson-baryon intermediate states, rather than the commonly used vector meson dominance model, may dominate the near-threshold photoproduction of the X(3872) in the γp → X(3872)p reaction, and that this mechanism produces a distinct line shape in the total cross section that can be tested at future facilities.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians respecting heavy quark spin symmetry and evaluated the γp → X(3872)p scattering amplitude using a dispersive formalism, considering box diagrams with intermediate open-charm channels including Σc(2800)⁺D̄*⁰, Σc(2800)⁺⁺D*⁻, Λc(2860)⁺D̄*⁰, and Λc(2940)⁺D̄(*)⁰. They performed partial-wave projections using the JℓS basis, retaining S-wave for the open-charm systems and S-, P-, and D-waves for the γp and Xp systems, with monopole form factors applied to account for off-shell effects of exchanged particles.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for γp → X(3872)p near threshold is predicted to be of tens of nanobarns.
- The X(3872)p threshold is approximately 4810 MeV.
- The D̄⁰Λc(2940)⁺ threshold is 4804⁺²₋₁ MeV; the D̄*⁰Σc(2800)⁺ threshold is 4799⁺¹⁴₋₅ MeV; the D*⁻Σc(2800)⁺⁺ threshold is 4811⁺⁴₋₆ MeV.
- The coupling constants used include gγD⁰D*⁰ = 0.142, gγD*⁰D*⁰ = 0.852, gγD⁺D*⁻ = −0.035, gDpΣc = 1.57, gXD̄D* = 1.98, gD(*)pΛc1 = 9.91, and gD(*)pΛc2 = 11.25 (all in appropriate powers of GeV).
- The COMPASS collaboration measured an upper bound σγN→X(3872)N′ × BX(3872)→J/ψππ < 2.9 pb at 90% CL at an average γp center-of-mass energy of ⟨Wγp⟩ = 13.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel mechanism previously proposed by Du et al. (2020) for J/ψ photoproduction to the case of hidden-charm exotic hadrons, specifically the X(3872). The approach contrasts with the vector meson dominance model used by Albaladejo et al. (2020) and others, and is motivated by the observation that the GlueX collaboration data (2023) on J/ψ photoproduction show threshold cusp features consistent with coupled-channel expectations. The predictions provide falsifiable line shapes that can be tested at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider and the Electron-Ion Collider in China.
