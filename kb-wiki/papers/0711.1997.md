# s-Wave Approximation for Asymmetry in Nonmesonic Decay of Finite Hypernuclei

> We establish the bridge between the commonly used Nabetani-Ogaito-Sato-Kishimoto (NOSK) formula for the asymmetry parameter $a_Λ$ in the $\vecΛp \to np$ emission of polarized hypernuclei, and the shell model (SM) formalism for finite hypernuclei. We demonstrate that the s-wave approximation leads to a SM formula for $a_Λ$ that is as simple as the NOSK one, and that reproduces the exact results for $^5_Λ$He and $^{12}_Λ$C better than initially expected. The simplicity achieved here is indeed remarkable. The new formalism makes the theoretical evaluation of $a_Λ$ more transparent, and explains clearly why the one-meson exchange model is unable to account for the experimental data of $^5_Λ$He.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the s-wave approximation can be applied within the shell model formalism for finite hypernuclei, yielding a formula for the asymmetry parameter $a_\Lambda$ that is as simple as the Nabetani-Ogaito-Sato-Kishimoto (NOSK) formula and reproduces exact results for $^5_\Lambda$He and $^{12}_\Lambda$C better than initially expected, though the derivation relies on the assumption that all single-proton sub-shells in the core are completely filled.

## Method

The authors start from the exact shell model expression for the asymmetry parameter $a_\Lambda$ in finite hypernuclei, previously derived in their own work (Barbero et al., 2005). They then introduce the s-wave approximation by neglecting higher-order contributions from relative p-state (${\sf l}=1$) components, showing that the nuclear amplitudes ${\cal I}_\kappa(p;{\sf l}=0)$ become independent of the proton orbital ($1s_{1/2}$ or $1p_{3/2}$). Further approximations include neglecting kinematical and nonlocal effects, approximating the liberated energy as $\Delta = M_\Lambda - M_N$, assuming back-to-back emission, and factoring the amplitudes at $p = p_\Delta = \sqrt{M_N\Delta}$ out of the integrals, yielding a NOSK-like formula.

## Key numbers

- For $^5_\Lambda$He with the one-pion exchange model: exact $a_\Lambda^{exact} = -0.4354$, approximate $a_\Lambda^{approx} = -0.4351$
- For $^5_\Lambda$He with the full $(\pi,\eta,K)+(\rho,\omega,K^*)$ model: exact $a_\Lambda^{exact} = -0.5117$, approximate $a_\Lambda^{approx} = -0.5131$
- For $^{12}_\Lambda$C with the one-pion exchange model (without higher-order contributions): exact $a_\Lambda^{exact} = -0.4377$, approximate $a_\Lambda^{approx} = -0.4501$
- For $^{12}_\Lambda$C with the full $(\pi,\eta,K)+(\rho,\omega,K^*)$ model (without higher-order contributions): exact $a_\Lambda^{exact} = -0.5154$, approximate $a_\Lambda^{approx} = -0.5306$
- The higher-order contributions (${\sf l}=1$) are approximately 2% for parity-conserving transitions and 15% for parity-violating transitions
- The integrals $\J_0 \cong \J_1 \cong 2M_N p_\Delta$ with $p_\Delta = 815$ MeV
- The proton-induced decay rate satisfies $\Gamma_p(^{12}_\Lambda C) \cong 2 \Gamma_p(^5_\Lambda He)$ as a purely kinematical result

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell model formalism (Barbero et al., 2005) for the asymmetry parameter $a_\Lambda$ in nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei, establishing a bridge to the simpler NOSK formula (Nabetani et al., 1999) that was originally derived for the Fermi gas model. The new formalism makes the theoretical evaluation more transparent and explains why the one-meson exchange model cannot reproduce the experimental data for $^5_\Lambda$He, where measurements favor $a_\Lambda > 0$ while calculations yield negative values. The work enables future incorporation of chirally motivated $2\pi$-exchange mechanisms, as recently suggested by the Barcelona group (Chiang et al., 2007), and raises the open question of whether the formalism can be extended to other polarized hypernuclei such as $^{11}_\Lambda$B where the single-proton sub-shells are not completely filled.
