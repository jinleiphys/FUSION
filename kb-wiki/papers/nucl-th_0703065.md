# Four-body calculation of proton-3He scattering

> The four-body equations of Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas are solved, for the first time, for proton-${}^3\mathrm{He}$ scattering including the Coulomb interaction between the three protons using the method of screening and renormalization as it was done recently for proton-deuteron scattering. Various realistic two-nucleon potentials are used. Large Coulomb effects are seen on all observables. Comparison with data at different energies shows large deviations in the proton analyzing power but quite reasonable agreement in other observables. The effect of nucleon-nucleon magnetic moment interaction and correlations between $\pd$ and $\pHe$ analyzing powers are studied.

# Four-body calculation of proton-3He scattering

## Key claim

For the first time, the four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations are solved for proton-3He elastic scattering including the Coulomb interaction between the three protons via the screening and renormalization method, yielding converged results for observables at proton lab energies above 2 MeV; however, the proton analyzing power shows a 25–40% discrepancy at the peak compared to experimental data, even when using realistic two-nucleon potentials that describe other observables reasonably well.

## Method

The symmetrized momentum-space AGS equations for four nucleons are solved numerically without approximations beyond discretization of continuum variables on a finite momentum mesh, using the screening and renormalization approach for the Coulomb interaction between the three protons. The screened Coulomb potential is taken as w_R(r) = (α_e/r) exp[-(r/R)^4] with screening radius R up to 12 fm, and the renormalization factor Z_R = exp[-2i(σ_L - η_{LR})] is applied in the R → ∞ limit to recover the exact Coulomb amplitude. Calculations include isospin-singlet two-nucleon partial waves with total angular momentum I ≤ 4, isospin-triplet two-nucleon partial waves with orbital angular momentum l_x ≤ 7, three-nucleon partial waves with spectator orbital angular momentum l_y ≤ 7 and total angular momentum J ≤ 13/2, and four-nucleon partial waves with orbital angular momentum l_z ≤ 7 and all initial/final p-3He states with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 3.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius convergence: fully converged results obtained with R = 12 fm at proton lab energy E_p = 4 MeV; R = 8 and 10 fm results are very close to converged values
- 3He binding energies calculated with four potentials: AV18 gives 6.92 MeV, N3LO gives 7.13 MeV, CD Bonn gives 7.26 MeV, INOY04 gives 7.73 MeV; experimental value is 7.72 MeV
- Proton analyzing power discrepancy: 25–40% disagreement at the peak across all potentials
- Magnetic moment interaction effect on A_y: 5.3% increase toward data at E_p = 2.25 MeV; reduced to 2.7% at 4 MeV
- Calculations performed at proton lab energies E_p = 2.25, 4.0, and 5.54 MeV
- 2N magnetic moment interaction effect studied using AV18 potential

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization method previously applied to proton-deuteron scattering by Deltuva (2005) to the four-nucleon system, following earlier calculations of n-3H scattering by Deltuva (2007). It improves upon prior p-3He calculations by Viviani (2001), Fisher (2006), and Pfitzinger (2001) by including a greater number of two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon partial waves to achieve fully converged spin observables, and by using multiple realistic two-nucleon potentials (CD Bonn, AV18, INOY04, N3LO). The results enable future extension to inelastic reactions and use with nonlocal interactions, while the persistent discrepancy in proton analyzing power motivates further study including three-nucleon forces.
