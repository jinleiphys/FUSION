# Particle production from the Color Glass Condensate: proton-nucleus collisions in light of the HERA data

> We compute single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions consistently within the CGC framework. The parameters in the calculations are obtained from electron-proton DIS and standard nuclear geometry. We obtain a good description of the DIS data without an anomalous dimension in the initial condition of the BK evolution and get a good agreement with the available single inclusive proton-proton and proton-nucleus data.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions can be described consistently within the Color Glass Condensate framework using only parameters fitted to HERA deep inelastic scattering data, without requiring an anomalous dimension in the initial condition of the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution when a free parameter in the infrared cutoff of the dipole amplitude is allowed.

## Method
The authors compute single inclusive hadron production using the Color Glass Condensate framework, employing the dipole unintegrated gluon distribution obtained from fits to HERA combined reduced cross section data for the proton. For proton-nucleus collisions, the dipole-nucleus amplitude is constructed from the dipole-proton amplitude using the optical Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution, and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling is used for energy evolution.

## Key numbers
- The MV$^e$ model fit to HERA reduced cross section data yields $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.15$, with initial saturation scale $\qs^2 = 0.238$ GeV$^2$ and proton transverse area $\sigma_0/2 = 16.36$ mb
- The MV$^\gamma$ model fit gives $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.17$, with anomalous dimension $\gamma = 1.135$ and initial saturation scale $\qs^2 = 0.245$ GeV$^2$
- The standard MV model fit gives $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 2.76$, with initial saturation scale $\qs^2 = 0.139$ GeV$^2$
- A normalization factor $K \sim 2.5$ is required to match RHIC proton-proton data, while no $K$ factor is needed for LHC proton-proton data at $\sqrt{s}=7000$ GeV
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ is computed at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV for p+Pb collisions and compared with minimum bias ALICE data, showing $R_{pA}\to 1$ at midrapidity and large transverse momentum

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate phenomenology established by the AAMQS collaboration (Albacete et al., 2010) by demonstrating that a modified infrared cutoff in the McLerran-Venugopalan initial condition can replace the anomalous dimension parameter while maintaining good fits to HERA data. The calculation enables consistent predictions for the rapidity dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ in proton-lead collisions at the LHC, which is a solid prediction of the Color Glass Condensate framework that can be tested against future experimental data.
