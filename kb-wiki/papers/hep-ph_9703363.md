# Spin-Dependent Twist-Four Matrix Elements from g_1 Data in the Resonance Region

> Matrix elements of spin-dependent twist-four operators are extracted from recent data on the spin-dependent g_1 structure function of the proton and deuteron in the resonance region. We emphasize the need to include the elastic contributions to the first moments of the structure functions at Q^2 < 2 GeV^2. The coefficients of the 1/Q^2 corrections to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rules are found to be 0.04 \pm 0.02 and 0.03 \pm 0.04 GeV^2 for the proton and neutron, respectively.

## Key claim

The coefficients of the 1/Q^2 corrections to the Ellis-Jaffe sum rules are extracted from resonance-region g_1 data, but the extraction relies on subtracting the twist-two contribution which itself carries significant uncertainty from the value of alpha_s and the singlet axial charge Sigma_infinity; the results are therefore conditional on the accuracy of these inputs.

## Method

The first moments of the proton and deuteron g_1 structure functions measured by the E143 Collaboration at Q^2 = 0.5 and 1.2 GeV^2 were combined with elastic contributions calculated from nucleon form factors. The twist-two component was subtracted using three-loop perturbative QCD calculations, and the remaining higher-twist contribution was fitted to extract the twist-four matrix element f_2^N after subtracting target mass and twist-3 contributions. The analysis used data at Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 and included leading-logarithmic Q^2 evolution of the twist-four operators.

## Key numbers

- f_2^p = -0.10 ± 0.05 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 (proton twist-four matrix element)
- f_2^n = -0.07 ± 0.08 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 (neutron twist-four matrix element)
- mu_4^p = 0.04 ± 0.02 GeV^2 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 (coefficient of 1/Q^2 correction to proton Ellis-Jaffe sum rule)
- mu_4^n = 0.03 ± 0.04 GeV^2 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 (coefficient of 1/Q^2 correction to neutron Ellis-Jaffe sum rule)
- d_2^p = 0.0054 ± 0.0050 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 (twist-3 matrix element for proton)
- d_2^n = 0.0030 ± 0.020 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 (twist-3 matrix element for neutron, extracted from deuteron data with 5% D-state admixture)

## Context

This work extends the operator product expansion analysis of polarized deep-inelastic scattering to the resonance region, using parton-hadron duality to extract higher-twist matrix elements from low-Q^2 data. The results are compared with QCD sum rule calculations by Balitsky, Braun and Kolisnichenko (1989) and by Stein et al. (1995), as well as MIT bag model estimates by Ji and Unrau (1994). The analysis corrects earlier attempts by Anselmino, Ioffe and Leader (1989) and Burkert and Ioffe (1992) that omitted the elastic contribution required by the OPE. Future experiments at Jefferson Lab are expected to improve the neutron data and reduce the large uncertainties.
