# Compositeness of near-threshold states with repulsive Coulomb interaction combined with short-range potential

> We investigate the internal structure of near-threshold states in a system with a repulsive Coulomb interaction combined with a short-range potential, using the compositeness. We construct a model in which the eigenmomentum is expressed in terms of three observables: the Coulomb scattering length, the Coulomb effective range, and the Bohr radius. In the presence of the Coulomb interaction, a bound state directly goes into a resonance as parameters are varied, bypassing a virtual state, in contrast to the case with only the short-range interaction. We show that the compositeness of near-threshold states can be expressed solely in terms of these observables. When the magnitude of the Coulomb effective range is much smaller than that of the Bohr radius, both shallow bound states and near-threshold resonances exhibit common structures with large compositeness, reflecting the remnant of the low-energy universality.

# Compositeness of near-threshold states with repulsive Coulomb interaction combined with short-range potential

## Key claim
The compositeness of near-threshold states in a system with repulsive Coulomb plus short-range interactions can be expressed solely in terms of three observables: the Coulomb scattering length, the Coulomb effective range, and the Bohr radius. When the magnitude of the Coulomb effective range is much smaller than that of the Bohr radius, both shallow bound states and near-threshold resonances exhibit large compositeness, reflecting a remnant of low-energy universality, but the compositeness remains less than unity because the Coulomb interaction strongly affects states extremely close to threshold.

## Method
The authors construct a model in which two charged particles scatter and couple to a discrete state, with the self-energy derived for repulsive Coulomb interaction. The eigenmomentum is determined by solving the pole condition expressed in terms of the Coulomb scattering length \(a_s\), the Coulomb effective range \(r_e\), and the Bohr radius \(a_B\). The compositeness \(X\) is then calculated from the energy derivative of the self-energy and expressed in terms of these observables, with the probabilistic quantities \(\mathcal{X}\), \(\mathcal{Y}\), and \(\mathcal{Z}\) used for resonance states where the complex compositeness cannot be interpreted as a probability.

## Key numbers
- The Bohr radius is defined as \(a_B = 1/(\mu \alpha |Z_1 Z_2|)\) with \(\alpha \approx 1/137\), characterizing the strength of the Coulomb interaction.
- The pole trajectory is computed for fixed \(r_e / a_B = -0.1\) while varying \(a_B / a_s\).
- When \(|a_B / a_s| < |a_B / r_e|\) (the shaded region), the compositeness \(\mathcal{X}\) approaches unity, indicating composite-dominant structure.
- The vertical dotted line in the analysis corresponds to \(|a_B / a_s| = 1\), marking where the Coulomb interaction strongly affects near-threshold states and keeps \(\mathcal{X} < 1\).

## Context
This work extends the study of near-threshold states from systems with only short-range interactions (Braaten 2004, Naidon 2016) to systems that also include repulsive Coulomb interaction. In pure short-range systems, shallow bound states are composite-dominant while near-threshold resonances tend to be non-composite-dominant (Hyodo 2013, Matuschek 2020, Kinugawa 2024). The present paper shows that with Coulomb repulsion, a bound state directly transitions into a resonance as parameters vary, bypassing a virtual state, qualitatively different from the short-range-only case. This work enables quantitative analysis of internal structure for systems such as the \(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\)-\(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\) system (Lyu 2021) and the \({}^{8}\)Be nucleus (Braaten 2004, Higa 2008), where Coulomb repulsion plays a significant role in determining whether near-threshold states are bound or resonant.
