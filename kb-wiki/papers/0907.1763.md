# Causality and universality in low-energy quantum scattering

> We generalize Wigner's causality bounds and Bethe's integral formula for the effective range to arbitrary dimension and arbitrary angular momentum. Moreover, we discuss the impact of these constraints on the separation of low- and high-momentum scales and universality in low-energy quantum scattering.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Wigner's causality bounds and Bethe's integral formula for the effective range to arbitrary spatial dimension \(d\) and arbitrary angular momentum \(L\), showing that for \(2L+d \geq 4\) causal wave propagation prevents reaching the scale-invariant (unitarity) limit with finite-range interactions, forcing the effective range to appear as a second relevant low-energy parameter that cannot be tuned to zero without violating causality.

## Method

The authors consider two non-relativistic spinless particles in \(d\) dimensions with a rotationally-invariant, energy-independent, finite-range interaction. They construct the Wronskian of radial Schrödinger solutions at two different momenta, evaluate it at a radius \(r \geq R\) (the interaction range), and take the limits \(p' \rightarrow 0\) then \(p \rightarrow 0\) to derive a general expression for the effective range \(r_{L,d}\) in terms of an integral over the squared wavefunction and a boundary term \(b_{L,d}(r)\). The non-negativity of the integral yields an upper bound \(r_{L,d} \leq b_{L,d}(r)\) for any \(r \geq R\), which is the generalized causality bound.

## Key numbers

- For alpha-neutron scattering in three dimensions, the causality condition gives minimum interaction ranges: \(R \geq 1.1\) fm for the \(S_{1/2}\) channel, \(R \geq 2.6\) fm for the \(P_{1/2}\) channel, and \(R \geq 2.1\) fm for the \(P_{3/2}\) channel.
- For \(2L+d \geq 5\) and \(\left\vert a_{L,d}\right\vert \rightarrow \infty\), the upper bound on the effective range is \(r_{L,d} \leq -\frac{2\Gamma(L+\frac{d}{2}-2)\Gamma(L+\frac{d}{2}-1)}{\pi}\left( \frac{r}{2}\right)^{-2L-d+4}\).
- For \(L=0\) in three dimensions with a van der Waals tail, the effective range is \(r_0 = \left[ \Gamma\left( 1/4\right) \right]^2 \left( \beta_6 + 3K_0^{(2)}\beta_6^{-1}\right) /(3\pi)\).
- For \(L=1\) in three dimensions with a van der Waals tail, the effective range is \(r_1 = -36\left[ \Gamma\left( 3/4\right) \right]^2 \left( \beta_6^{-1} - 5K_1^{(2)}\beta_6^{-3}\right) /(5\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the original causality bounds of Wigner (1955) and Bethe's (1949) integral formula for the effective range to arbitrary dimension and angular momentum, generalizing the analysis of Phillips and Cohen (1996) who derived a Wigner bound for the S-wave effective range in three dimensions. The results confirm earlier findings by Bertulani (2002) for P-wave scattering in three dimensions based on renormalization arguments and by Barford (2002) for higher partial waves in the renormalization group framework. The paper enables further investigation of universality in few-body systems and provides constraints for effective field theories of interactions such as alpha-neutron scattering and P-wave Feshbach resonances in cold atoms.
