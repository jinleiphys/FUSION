# Hadron tomography in meson-pair production and gravitational form factors

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are 3-dimensional (3D) structure functions for hadrons, and they are important for solving the proton spin puzzle including partonic orbital-angular-momentum contributions. The $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs are generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). Here, $s$ and $t$ are Mandelstam variables. The GDAs can be studied in two-photon processes ($γ^* γ\to h \bar h$) at KEKB. A GDA describes the amplitude from quark and antiquark to the hadron pair $h \bar h$. In 2016, the Belle collaboration reported measurements for pion-pair production in electron-positron collision, and the pion GDAs were determined in this work by analyzing the Belle data. In our analysis, the pion GDAs are expressed by a few parameters, which are determined by analyzing the Belle data. From the obtained GDAs, form factors of energy-momentum tenor, so called gravitational form factors, are calculated for pion in the timelike region. The spacelike gravitational form factors are calculated from the timelike ones by using the dispersion relation. Then, the mass radius is calculated as 0.32-0.39 fm and the mechanical radius, defined by the slope of the form factor $Θ_1$, is calculated as 0.82-0.88 fm for the pion by using the spacelike form factors. This is the first study on gravitational form factors and radii of hadrons from actual experimental measurements. In 2019, the Belle II collaboration will start collecting data by the higher luminosity Super KEKB, so that the GDAs of other hadrons should also be investigated in the near future. Our studies are valuable in understanding 3D structure and gravitational properties of hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors determine pion generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) from Belle data on the two-photon process γ*γ → π⁰π⁰, and from these GDAs they extract the first experimental-based estimates of the pion's gravitational form factors and radii, with the caveat that the extracted mass radius (0.32–0.39 fm) and mechanical radius (0.82–0.88 fm) depend on the treatment of ππ scattering phase shifts above the KK threshold.

## Method

The authors parameterize the pion GDAs using a combination of continuum terms and resonance contributions (f₀(500) for S-wave, f₂(1270) for D-wave) with five free parameters, then fit these parameters to Belle differential cross section data for γ*γ → π⁰π⁰ at Q² = 17.23 GeV² and 24.25 GeV². From the fitted GDAs, they compute the timelike gravitational form factors Θ₁(s) and Θ₂(s) via the energy-momentum tensor matrix element, then convert these to spacelike form factors using a dispersion relation. The mass and mechanical radii are obtained from the slopes of the spacelike form factors at t = 0.

## Key numbers

- χ²/d.o.f. = 1.09 for the GDA fit to Belle data (Q² = 17.23 GeV² and 24.25 GeV², cosθ = 0.1 and 0.5)
- Mass radius √⟨r²⟩_mass = 0.32–0.39 fm from the spacelike form factor Θ₁(t)
- Mechanical radius √⟨r²⟩_mech = 0.82–0.88 fm from the spacelike form factor Θ₂(t)
- For comparison, the pion charge radius is √⟨r²⟩_charge = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm (Patrignani 2016)
- The f₂(1270) resonance peak appears at W ≈ 1.2 GeV in the cross section

## Context

This work extends the GDA formalism developed by Diehl (1998, 2000) and Polyakov (1998) to actual experimental data from the Belle collaboration (Masuda 2015), providing the first extraction of gravitational form factors and radii from measurements rather than models. The analysis uses the same parameterization approach as Kawamura (2013) and Kumano (2017), but now applied to the full Belle dataset. The results enable future studies of other hadrons' GDAs and gravitational properties using the higher-luminosity Super KEKB and Belle II data expected from 2019 onward.
