# How Uncertain Are Solar Neutrino Predictions?

> Solar neutrino fluxes and sound speeds are calculated using a systematic reevaluation of nuclear fusion rates. The largest uncertainties are identified and their effects on the solar neutrino fluxes are estimated.

## Key claim

The paper systematically reevaluates the uncertainties in solar neutrino flux predictions arising from nuclear fusion cross sections, concluding that these nuclear uncertainties are currently the largest recognized source of error in the neutrino predictions, but that helioseismological agreement with the standard solar model is so precise (rms fractional sound-speed difference of 0.001) that solar-physics explanations for the neutrino deficits would require discrepancies at least a factor of 30 larger than observed.

## Method

The authors constructed a standard solar model (BP98) using the nuclear reaction rates recommended by the 1997 Institute for Nuclear Theory (INT) workshop, combined with 1996 Livermore OPAL opacities, the OPAL equation of state, and updated electron and ion screening calculations. They propagated the uncertainties from each input parameter (nuclear cross sections, heavy-element abundance, opacity, luminosity, age, and diffusion coefficients) through the solar model to determine the resulting uncertainties in neutrino fluxes and capture rates for chlorine and gallium experiments. The model predictions were then compared with five independent helioseismological determinations of solar sound speeds.

## Key numbers

- pp neutrino flux: \(5.94 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\) with fractional uncertainty \(^{+0.01}_{-0.01}\)
- \(^8\)B neutrino flux: \(5.15 \times 10^{-4} \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\) with fractional uncertainty \(^{+0.19}_{-0.14}\)
- \(^7\)Be neutrino flux: \(4.80 \times 10^{-1} \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\) with fractional uncertainty \(^{+0.09}_{-0.09}\)
- Predicted chlorine capture rate: \(7.7^{+1.2}_{-1.0}\) SNU
- Predicted gallium capture rate: \(129^{+8}_{-6}\) SNU
- rms fractional difference between calculated and measured sound speeds: \(1.1 \times 10^{-3}\) for \(0.05 R_\odot < R < 0.95 R_\odot\); \(0.7 \times 10^{-3}\) in the solar core (\(0.05 R_\odot < R < 0.25 R_\odot\))
- Measured convective zone depth: \(R_{\text{CZ}} = (0.713 \pm 0.001) R_\odot\); model predicts \(0.714 R_\odot\)
- Measured surface helium abundance: \(Y_{\text{surf}} = 0.249 \pm 0.003\); model predicts \(0.243\)
- Using the Caltech (CIT) value for the \(^8\)B production cross section gives \(\phi(^8\text{B, CIT}) = 6.1^{+1.1}_{-0.9} \times 10^6 \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\) and chlorine rate \(8.8^{+1.4}_{-1.1}\) SNU

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic publication (Bahcall and Pinsonneault 1995) by incorporating the updated nuclear reaction rates from the 1997 INT workshop (Adelberger et al. 1998), which revised uncertainties upward for the \(^3\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\text{Be}\), \(^7\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^8\text{B}\), and \(^{14}\text{N}(p,\gamma)^{15}\text{O}\) reactions. The paper enables more precise particle-physics analyses of solar neutrino data by providing quantified uncertainties and an exportable computer code (exportrates.f) for propagating errors. It also identifies the \(^7\text{Be}+p\) cross section as the most important nuclear parameter needing improved laboratory measurement (to 5% accuracy) and the \(^3\text{He}-^4\text{He}\) cross section as the dominant uncertainty for the \(^7\)Be flux measurement planned by BOREXINO.
