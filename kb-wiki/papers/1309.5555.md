# Medium modifications of jet shapes in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV within a multiphase transport model

> Within a multiphase transport model, medium modifications of differential jet shapes are investigated in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The differential jet shapes are significantly modified by the strong interactions between jets and a partonic medium in Pb+Pb collisions relative to that in p+p collisions. The modifications are slightly weakened by the hadronization of coalescence, but strengthened by resonance decays in hadronic rescatterings. Subleading jets display larger medium modifications than leading jets, especially in central Pb+Pb collisions with large dijet asymmetries. These behaviors of medium modifications of differential jet shapes reflect a dynamical evolution of redistribution of jet energy inside a quenched jet cone in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Medium modifications of jet shapes in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV within a multiphase transport model

## Key claim
The differential jet shape in Pb+Pb collisions is significantly modified by strong interactions between jets and a partonic medium, but the abstract's claim of "significant modification" is qualified by the finding that the coalescence hadronization mechanism *reduces* the modifications, while resonance decays in hadronic rescatterings *strengthen* them; the final AMPT result underestimates the experimental data at small radius and overestimates it at large radius.

## Method
The study uses the AMPT model with string melting, including a dijet trigger of pT ~ 90 GeV/c from HIJING, parton cascade via ZPC with elastic scatterings only (no inelastic or radiative processes), coalescence hadronization, and hadronic rescatterings with resonance decays via the ART model. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-kt algorithm (R=0.3) using the same kinematic cuts as the CMS experiment (jet pT > 100 GeV/c, |η| between 0.3 and 2), and the differential jet shape ρ(r) is defined as the fraction of transverse momentum carried by particles with pT > 1 GeV/c in annuli of width δr=0.05 around the jet axis, with background estimated via the η-reflection method.

## Key numbers
- Dijet trigger: initial partons of pT ~ 90 GeV/c are used to generate jet showers.
- Jet reconstruction threshold: jet pT > 100 GeV/c within pseudorapidity 0.3 < |η| < 2.
- Partonic interaction cross sections: 1.5 mb for Pb+Pb collisions, 0 mb for p+p collisions.
- Jet cone size: R = 0.3; annulus width δr = 0.05 for differential jet shape.
- Dijet asymmetry selection: events divided by AJ = 0.3 cut, where AJ = (pT,1 - pT,2)/(pT,1 + pT,2); leading jet pT,1 > 100 GeV/c, subleading jet pT,2 > 40 GeV/c, Δφ1,2 > 2π/3.
- Centrality: most central Pb+Pb collisions (0-10%) and centrality bin 0-30% are analyzed.

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical studies of jet shape modifications by Vitev et al. (2008, 2009), who predicted medium-induced enhancement up to ~1.75 at the cone periphery using perturbative QCD and radiative energy loss, and by Wang and Zhu (2013), who found induced gluon radiations dominate over elastic scatterings in broadening the differential γ-jet shape within a linearized Boltzmann model without hadronization or hadronic phase. The present AMPT study includes both partonic and hadronic evolution stages, enabling investigation of how coalescence hadronization and resonance decays affect the jet shape modifications. The results enable future comparisons with CMS data (2012, 2013) and motivate inclusion of inelastic parton interactions and fragmentation as an alternative hadronization mechanism.
