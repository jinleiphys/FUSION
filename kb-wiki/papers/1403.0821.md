# On the critical line of 2+1 flavor QCD

> We determine the curvature of the (pseudo)critical line of QCD with $n_f$=2+1 staggered fermions at nonzero temperature and quark density, by analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials. Monte Carlo simulations are performed adopting the HISQ/tree action discretization, as implemented in the code by the MILC collaboration, suitably modified to include a nonzero imaginary baryon chemical potential. We work on a line of constant physics, as determined in Ref.\cite{Bazavov:2011nk}, adjusting the couplings so as to keep the strange quark mass $m_s$ fixed at its physical value, with a light to strange mass ratio $m_l/m_s=1/20$. In the present investigation we set the chemical potential at the same value for the three quark species, $μ_l=μ_s\equiv μ$. We explore lattices of different spatial extensions, $16^3\times 6$ and $24^3\times 6$, to check for finite size effects, and present results on a $32^3 \times 8$ lattice, to check for finite cut-off effects. We discuss our results for the curvature $κ$ of the critical line at $μ= 0$, which indicate $κ=0.018(4)$, and compare them with previous lattice determinations by alternative methods and with experimental determinations of the freeze-out curve.

## Key claim

The curvature κ of the QCD (pseudo)critical line at μ=0 is determined to be 0.018(4) using analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials, a value typically two to three standard deviations larger than previous lattice determinations and in better agreement with the freeze-out curvature from the standard statistical hadronization model.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations of 2+1 flavor QCD with rooted staggered quarks were performed using the HISQ/tree action, modified to include an imaginary baryon chemical potential common to all three quark species (μ_l=μ_s≡μ). The pseudocritical line was determined from the peak of the disconnected light quark chiral susceptibility on 16³×6, 24³×6, and 32³×8 lattices, with the strange quark mass fixed at its physical value and a light to strange mass ratio m_l/m_s=1/20, following the line of constant physics from Bazavov (2011).

## Key numbers

- Curvature κ = 0.018(4) for the pseudocritical line of 2+1 flavor QCD, obtained by analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials
- On the 16³×6 lattice: R_q = -1.63(22), κ = 0.0183(24)
- On the 24³×6 lattice: R_q = -1.51(25), κ = 0.0170(28)
- On the 32³×8 lattice: R_q = -1.70(29), κ = 0.0190(32)
- T_c(0) = 154(9) MeV, taken from Bazavov (2011) with the same action, yielding b = 0.117(27) GeV⁻¹ in the parametrization T_c(μ_B) = T_c(0) - b μ_B²

## Context

This work provides a new independent lattice determination of the QCD critical line curvature using the analytic continuation method with the HISQ/tree action, extending previous studies by Cea, Cosmai, and Papa (2010, 2012) and others. The result is compared with earlier lattice determinations by the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration (Endrodi, 2011) using Taylor expansion (κ=0.0089(14) and κ=0.0066(20)) and by the Bielefeld-BNL collaboration (Kaczmarek, 2011) (κ=0.0066(7)), as well as with freeze-out curve analyses by Cleymans (2005) and Becattini (2012). The discrepancy with previous lattice results suggests the need for systematic comparisons across different methods and discretizations, and enables future work on continuum extrapolation, extension to physical quark masses, and inclusion of unequal chemical potentials for light and strange quarks.
