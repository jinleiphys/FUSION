# What Chiral Symmetry Can Tell Us About Hadron Correlators in Matter

> The constraints imposed by chiral symmetry on hadron correlation functions in nuclear medium are discussed. It is shown that these constraints imply some structure of the in-medium hadron correlators, lead to the cancellation of the order $ρm_π$ term in the in-medium nucleon correlator and result in the effect of mixing of the chiral partners correlators, reflecting the phenomena of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The different scenarios of such restorations are briefly discussed.

# What Chiral Symmetry Can Tell Us About Hadron Correlators in Matter

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry constraints imply that in-medium hadron correlators exhibit mixing between chiral partners (e.g., vector-axial vector, sigma-pion), but the identity of the correlators at the point of restoration does not necessarily mean degeneracy of the effective masses of those partners, mass degeneracy is only one of several possible scenarios.

## Method

The author applies soft-pion theorems and current algebra commutation relations to derive the leading chiral corrections to in-medium two-point correlation functions for nucleon, vector (rho meson), axial-vector (A1 meson), scalar (sigma), and pseudoscalar (pion) channels. The corrections are expressed in terms of a mixing parameter xi that depends on the nuclear density, the pion-nucleon sigma term, the pion decay constant, and the pion mass. The analysis uses QCD sum rules with the Ioffe choice of nucleon interpolating current to show how the order rho m_pi term cancels in the in-medium nucleon correlator, bringing the sum rules into agreement with chiral symmetry constraints.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion of the pion-nucleon sigma term is given as sigma_{pi N} = A m_pi^2 - (9/16)(g_{pi N}/(2 M_N))^2 m_pi^3 + ...
- The in-medium two-quark condensate evolves to first order in density as <Psi|bar q q|Psi> = <0|bar q q|0> (1 - (sigma_{pi N}/(f_pi^2 m_pi^2)) rho)
- The mixing parameter xi for the nucleon correlator is xi = (rho * bar_sigma_{pi N})/(f_pi^2 m_pi^2), where bar_sigma_{pi N} is the leading nonanalytic part of the pion-nucleon sigma term
- The mixing parameter for the rho-A1 system is 4/3 times the one for the nucleon correlator
- The mixing parameter for the sigma-pi system is two times smaller than that for the rho-A1 system

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral symmetry constraints to in-medium hadron correlators, building on earlier QCD sum rule approaches by Drukarev and Cohen (1990s) and the soft-pion theorem framework. The mixing of chiral partner correlators was previously studied at finite temperatures by Dey (1980s) and at finite densities by Krippa (1990s). The results enable model-independent predictions for modifications of the dilepton spectrum in heavy-ion collisions, where additional enhancement near the A1 meson mass may appear alongside the rho meson peak, and suggest that the sigma-pi mixing effect could be observable in deeply bound pion states in heavy nuclei.
