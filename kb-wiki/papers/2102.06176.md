# Charged Lepton Flavor Violation at the EIC

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the potential sensitivity of the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) in the channel $ep\to τX$, within the model-independent framework of the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT). We compute the relevant cross sections to leading order in QCD and electroweak corrections and perform simulations of signal and SM background events in various $τ$ decay channels, suggesting simple cuts to enhance the associated estimated efficiencies. To assess the discovery potential of the EIC in $τ$-$e$ transitions, we study the sensitivity of other probes of this physics across a broad range of energy scales, from $pp \to e τX$ at the Large Hadron Collider to decays of $B$ mesons and $τ$ leptons, such as $τ\to e γ$, $τ\to e \ell^+ \ell^-$, and crucially the hadronic modes $τ\to e Y$ with $Y \in \{ π, K, ππ, K π, ...\}$. We find that electroweak dipole and four-fermion semi-leptonic operators involving light quarks are already strongly constrained by $τ$ decays, while operators involving the $c$ and $b$ quarks present more promising discovery potential for the EIC. An analysis of three models of leptoquarks confirms the expectations based on the SMEFT results. We also identify future directions needed to maximize the reach of the EIC in CLFV searches: these include an optimization of the $τ$ tagger in hadronic channels, an exploration of background suppression through tagging $b$ and $c$ jets in the final state, and a global fit by turning on all SMEFT couplings, which will likely reveal new discovery windows for the EIC.

## Key claim

The Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) has promising discovery potential for charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) in the channel \(ep \to \tau X\), but this potential is strongly dependent on the quark flavor involved: operators involving light quarks are already tightly constrained by existing \(\tau\) decay measurements, while operators involving charm and bottom quarks offer more promising discovery windows.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order QCD and electroweak cross sections for the CLFV process \(ep \to \tau X\) within the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) framework, then performed Monte Carlo simulations of signal and Standard Model background events across multiple \(\tau\) decay channels. They compared the EIC sensitivity to constraints from other probes including \(pp \to e\tau X\) at the LHC, \(\tau \to e\gamma\), \(\tau \to e\ell^+\ell^-\), and hadronic \(\tau\) decays such as \(\tau \to e Y\) with \(Y \in \{\pi, K, \pi\pi, K\pi, \ldots\}\), and also analyzed three specific leptoquark models. Renormalization group evolution was applied to connect operators at the TeV scale to low-energy observables.

## Context

This work extends the SMEFT analysis of CLFV to the EIC, complementing existing studies at the LHC and low-energy experiments. The authors compare the EIC sensitivity to constraints from \(\tau\) decays (such as \(\tau \to e\gamma\) and \(\tau \to e\ell^+\ell^-\)) and from LHC searches for \(pp \to e\tau X\), finding that operators involving light quarks are already strongly bounded by \(\tau\) decay measurements from experiments like Belle and BaBar. The analysis enables future optimization of \(\tau\) tagging in hadronic channels and exploration of background suppression through \(b\)- and \(c\)-jet tagging, as well as global fits of all SMEFT couplings to identify new discovery windows for the EIC.
