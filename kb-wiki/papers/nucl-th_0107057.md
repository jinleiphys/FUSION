# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction: Developing an Accurate NN Potential Based upon Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We present an accurate nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential based upon chiral effective Lagrangians. The model includes one- and two-pion exchange contributions up to chiral order three and contact terms (which represent the short range force) up to order four. Within this framework, the NN phase shifts below 300 MeV lab. energy and the properties of the deuteron are reproduced with high-precision. This chiral NN potential may serve as a reliable starting point for testing the chiral effective field theory approach in exact few-nucleon and microscopic nuclear many-body calculations.

# Chiral Symmetry and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction: Developing an Accurate NN Potential Based upon Chiral Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The authors construct a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential based on chiral effective field theory that reproduces NN phase shifts below 300 MeV lab energy and deuteron properties with high precision, but they explicitly note that the chiral model has "very little predictive power, less than the meson model," requiring approximately 46 parameters to achieve the fit. The approach is a "split approach" that uses chiral two-pion exchange only up to chiral order three and contact terms up to order four, because including two-pion exchange at order four would introduce "horrific mathematical expressions" and potentially irreparable discrepancies with empirical data.

## Method
The authors construct the NN potential using the heavy-baryon formulation of chiral perturbation theory, including one-pion exchange and two-pion exchange contributions up to chiral order three (calculated via covariant perturbation theory with dimensional regularization) plus contact terms up to chiral order four to represent short-range forces. The potential is regularized with exponential cutoffs (momentum-dependent) and then transformed to satisfy the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation, with parameters fitted to reproduce np scattering phase shifts and deuteron properties.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990), Ordonez (1992), and van Kolck (1994, 1999), with important contributions from Robilotta (1994), Kaiser et al. (1997, 1998, 1999), and Epelbaum et al. (1998). The authors note that previous chiral NN potentials were too inaccurate for reliable few-nucleon or many-body calculations, and they aim to match the quality of high-precision phenomenological potentials from the 1990s (Stoks 1994, Wiringa 1995, Machleidt 2001). The resulting chiral potential is the first to correctly predict the deuteron radius obtained by the isotope-shift method (Huber 1998), which all previous conventional potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijmegen) failed to reproduce. This enables exact few-body calculations and microscopic nuclear many-body theory using a chiral-based potential, though the authors caution that the model has limited predictive power compared to meson-exchange models.
