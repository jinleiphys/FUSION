# Maximal angular correlation in $γ-γ$ coincidences: a quantitative study

> The measurement of the angular distribution of maximally correlated annihilation gamma rays radiated in coincidence, like those emitted from a $^{22}\mathrm{Na}$ source, is a classic experiment that is nowadays ordinarily performed in Nuclear Physics laboratory classes. For the first time we present an analytic expression for such angular distribution, which can be easily tested and confronted with the laboratory measurements.

## Key claim

For the first time, an analytic expression is provided for the experimental angular distribution of coincidence counts from maximally correlated annihilation gamma rays (such as those from a ²²Na source), accounting for the finite size of the detectors. The expression is derived for a point-like source and identical detectors, and is intended to be tested against laboratory measurements rather than to directly measure physical parameters.

## Method

The author computes the solid angle corresponding to the intersection of two spherical caps on a sphere centered at the source: one cap corresponds to the fixed detector's field of view, and the other to the reflection of the rotating detector's field of view across the plane perpendicular to the fixed detector. The surface integral for this intersection area is evaluated in Cartesian coordinates, yielding an analytic formula for the geometrical efficiency factor ΔΩ/(4π) as a function of the rotation angle α and the detector half-angle β = arctan(r/d). The rate of coincidences is then given by C_C = A ε₁ ε₂ (ΔΩ/(4π)), where A is the source activity and ε₁, ε₂ are the detector intrinsic efficiencies.

## Key numbers

* The critical rotation angle at which the coincidence rate vanishes is α_crit = 2β, where β = arctan(r/d) is the half-angle subtended by each detector face at the source.
* For a typical laboratory configuration with β = π/16 (11.25°), the maximum geometrical efficiency at α = 0 is (1/2)(1 - cos(π/16)) ≈ 0.0096.
* The analytical result for the solid angle of intersection is ΔΩ(α, β) = 4(arccot(√2 sin(|α|/2) / √(cos α - cos 2β)) - cos β · arccot(√2 cos β sin(|α|/2) / √(cos α - cos 2β))), valid for |α| ≤ 2β.

## Context

This work fills a gap in the literature by providing an explicit analytic expression for the experimental angular distribution of coincidences from a ²²Na source, which previously could only be treated qualitatively or approximated by intersecting circles (valid only for parallel rays from a source at infinity). The result extends the classic treatments of angular correlations by Hamilton (1940), Biedenharn and Rose (1953), and the experimental textbook by Melissinos, and enables a quantitative fit of the coincidence rate as a function of rotation angle, allowing consistency checks against independently measured parameters such as β, source activity, and detector efficiencies.
