# Screening Effect in Quark-Hadron Mixed Phase

> Possibility of the structured mixed phases at first order phase transitions in neutron stars is reexamined by taking into account the charge screening effect. The Maxwell construction is shown to be not conflicted with the Gibbs conditions once the Coulomb potential is properly taken into account. Taking the hadron-quark deconfinement transition as an example, we explicitly demonstrate a mechanical instability of the geometrical structure of the structured mixed phase by the charge screening effect. In this case we have effectively the picture given by the Maxwell construction.

## Key claim

The Maxwell construction and the Gibbs conditions are not actually in conflict for first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, provided the Coulomb potential is properly included; the structured mixed phase (SMP) becomes mechanically unstable due to charge screening for physically realistic values of the surface tension, effectively recovering the Maxwell-construction picture.

## Method

The authors use a density-functional framework to describe the thermodynamics of non-uniform matter, treating the hadron-quark deconfinement transition with a sharp boundary between phases. They solve the Poisson equation self-consistently with the equations of motion for particle densities, including the Debye screening effect, and compute the total thermodynamic potential per Wigner-Seitz cell as a function of droplet radius and volume fraction.

## Key numbers

* Debye screening length in the quark phase: \(\lambda_D^q \simeq 5\) fm at a typical baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B \simeq 1\) GeV.
* The screening lengths for protons and electrons are of the same order as \(\lambda_D^q\).
* The minimum in the thermodynamic potential disappears for surface tension values between \(\beta_1 = 0.1\) and \(\beta_1 = 0.5\), corresponding to \(\sigma_{\rm QCD}\) between 10 and 50 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\).
* For volume fractions \(f = 0.01\) and \(f = 0.5\), the unscreened (Coulomb-only) minimum is recovered only in the limit \(f \ll 1\) and \(R \ll \lambda_D^I\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on structured mixed phases initiated by Glendenning (1992), who argued that the Gibbs conditions allow SMP in multi-component systems, and subsequent studies that assumed SMP exists in a wide density region. The authors show that earlier bulk calculations ignored finite-size effects and the Poisson equation, and that Debye screening (with \(\lambda_D \sim 5\) fm) destabilizes the geometrical structures for realistic surface tensions. The framework is applicable to other first-order transitions, such as the liquid-gas transition at subnuclear density studied by Maruyama and collaborators.
