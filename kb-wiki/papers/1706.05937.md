# Proton structure fluctuations: from HERA to the LHC

> We constrain the amount of event-by-event fluctuations of the proton by comparing with the HERA coherent and incoherent diffractive $J/Ψ$ production data. We find that the HERA measurements prefer large geometric fluctuations. We then include the constrained fluctuating proton shapes in viscous hydrodynamical simulations of the proton-nucleus collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, and find a good description of the mean transverse momentum and flow harmonics measured at the LHC.

## Key claim

The HERA diffractive J/psi production data require large event-by-event geometric fluctuations in the proton wave function; without these fluctuations the measured incoherent cross section would be underestimated by roughly two orders of magnitude. The constrained fluctuating proton shapes then allow a good description of the mean transverse momentum and flow harmonics measured in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC.

## Method

The authors model the proton as three hot spots whose positions are sampled from a Gaussian distribution with width B_qc, each hot spot having a Gaussian density profile of width B_q. They calculate diffractive J/psi production using the IPsat dipole model and the IP-Glasma framework, fitting B_qc, B_q, and an infrared cutoff parameter m to simultaneously describe the HERA coherent and incoherent diffractive J/psi production data. The constrained fluctuating proton shapes are then used as initial conditions for viscous hydrodynamical simulations (using the Music code) of proton-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, with the pre-thermal evolution described by classical Yang-Mills equations and hadronic afterburner treated by UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- B_qc = 3.0 GeV^{-2} (width of the Gaussian distribution for hot spot positions)
- B_q = 0.3 GeV^{-2} (width of the Gaussian density profile of each hot spot)
- m = 0.4 GeV (infrared cutoff parameter suppressing long distance Coulomb tails)
- sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV (center-of-mass energy for the proton-nucleus collisions)
- tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c (starting time for hydrodynamical evolution)
- T_switch = 155 MeV (switching temperature for hadronization via Cooper-Frye prescription)
- W = 75 GeV (center-of-mass energy for the diffractive J/psi production calculations)

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma framework, previously successful in describing heavy ion data at RHIC and LHC (Schenke 2012), to high-multiplicity proton-nucleus collisions. It directly constrains the previously free parameters describing proton shape fluctuations using HERA diffractive J/psi data (H1 Collaboration 2013), addressing the issue that without such fluctuations the explanation of large elliptic and triangular flow in proton-nucleus collisions is difficult (Schenke 2014). The results enable consistent quantitative tests of the hydrodynamical picture in small systems and have been extended to improve the description of exclusive J/psi production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at the LHC (Mantysaari 2017).
