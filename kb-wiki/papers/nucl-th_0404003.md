# Competition between isoscalar and isovector pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei

> We study the isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei. They are estimated from the double difference of binding energies for odd-odd N=Z nuclei and the odd-even mass difference for the neighboring odd-mass nuclei, respectively. The empirical and BCS calculations based on a T=0 and T=1 pairing model reproduce well the almost degeneracy of the lowest T=0 and T=1 states over a wide range of even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei. It is shown that this degeneracy is attributed to competition between the isoscalar and isovector pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei. The calculations give an interesting prediction that the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 82Nb has possibly the ground state with T=0.

## Key claim

The near-degeneracy of the lowest T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei is attributed to competition between isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) pairing correlations, and the paper predicts that the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 82Nb may have a T=0 ground state or nearly degenerate T=0 and T=1 states.

## Method

The authors extracted T=1 pairing correlations from the three-point odd-even mass difference Δ(3)n in odd-mass nuclei with N=Z+1, and extracted T=0 and T=1 proton-neutron pairing gaps from the double difference of binding energies for odd-odd N=Z nuclei. They performed BCS calculations using a pairing Hamiltonian that includes both T=1 and T=0 pairing interactions, with single-particle energies from a spherical Woods-Saxon potential and pairing strengths G=24.5/A and k0=244.5(1-1.67A-1/3)/A fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The T=1 pairing gap follows a global trend fitted by 5.18A-1/3 MeV, contrasting with the standard 12A-1/2 curve
- The T=1 pairing force strength used in BCS calculations is G=24.5/A
- The T=0 pairing force strength is k0=244.5(1-1.67A-1/3)/A
- The symmetry energy coefficient from the model is a(A)=134.4(1-1.52A-1/3) as determined by Duflo and Zuker
- The energy difference between T=0 and T=1 states is given by E(T=1)-E(T=0) ≈ k0 - 2Δ(3)n, where Δ(3)n is the odd-even mass difference

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton-neutron pairing by Satula (2001), Dobaczewski (2001), and Vogel (1996), who examined odd-even mass differences and pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei. The paper builds on the authors' own earlier work (Kaneko 2002) on double differences of binding energies and introduces a T=0 pairing interaction to explain the T=0 pairing gap that cannot be described by the T=1 pairing Hamiltonian alone. The results enable predictions for the isospin structure of ground states in heavy odd-odd N=Z nuclei beyond 78, particularly 82Nb, and suggest that strong proton-neutron correlations affect the odd-even mass differences in even-even N=Z nuclei.
