# Linear response theory in the continuum for deformed nuclei: Green's function vs. time-dependent Hartree-Fock with the absorbing-boundary condition

> The continuum random-phase approximation is extended to the one applicable to deformed nuclei. We propose two different approaches. One is based on the use of the three dimensional (3D) Green's function and the other is the small-amplitude TDHF with the absorbing-boundary condition. Both methods are based on the 3D Cartesian grid representation and applicable to systems without any symmetry on nuclear shape. The accuracy and identity of these two methods are examined with the BKN interaction. Using the full Skyrme energy functional in the small-amplitude TDHF approach, we study the isovector giant dipole states in the continuum for O-16 and for even-even Be isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper extends continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) to deformed nuclei by proposing two complementary methods, a 3D Green's function approach and a real-time time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) with absorbing-boundary condition (ABC), and demonstrates that the ABC method can reproduce the exact continuum results to within about 0.3–0.6 MeV in peak position, provided the absorber parameters are chosen appropriately for the energy range of interest.

## Method

The authors first construct a 3D continuum RPA by iteratively solving the linearized response equations using a 3D Cartesian grid, where the single-particle Green's function is built from a spherical reference potential plus a short-range deformed part via a Dyson equation. Separately, they perform small-amplitude real-time TDHF by applying an instantaneous external perturbation and propagating the occupied single-particle wave functions in time, using a complex absorbing potential of the form \(-i\tilde\eta(r)\) outside the inner region (\(R=8\) fm, \(\Delta r=12\) fm, \(\eta_0=10\) MeV) to enforce outgoing boundary conditions. Both methods are implemented on a 3D Cartesian grid with adaptive curvilinear coordinates to reduce computational cost in the outer region.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{16}\)O with the BKN interaction, the low-energy octupole resonance (LEOR) is a bound peak whose position is independent of box size, while the high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) embedded in the continuum requires a box size \(R \gtrsim 200\) fm in a discretized basis to match the continuum RPA result with \(\Gamma=0.5\) MeV smoothing.
* An empirical formula for the discretized RPA smoothing parameter is \(\Gamma \approx 3 (\hbar v / R)\); for \(R=20\) fm, \(\Gamma \gtrsim 8\) MeV is needed to reproduce the continuum calculation.
* The absorbing potential parameters \(\Delta r = 12\) fm and \(\eta_0 = 10\) MeV satisfy the no-reflection condition for particle energies \(7 < E < 60\) MeV.
* The real-time TDHF+ABC calculation for \(^{16}\)O octupole states shows a peak shift of about 0.3 MeV for LEOR and about 0.6 MeV for HEOR compared to the 3D Green's function method, attributed to the adaptive coordinate representation.
* The time evolution is computed up to \(t = 30\) MeV\(^{-1}\) with a time step \(\Delta t = 0.001\) MeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA formalism, originally developed for spherical nuclei by Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) and widely applied to magic nuclei, to deformed systems where explicit construction of the Green's function is extremely difficult. The authors' earlier work on molecular photoabsorption using TDDFT with ABC (Nakatsukasa and Yabana, 2001) and nuclear breakup reactions (Ueda, Yabana, and Nakatsukasa, 2002, 2004) provides the foundation for the ABC approach. The paper enables fully self-consistent continuum response calculations for deformed nuclei using Skyrme energy functionals, including time-odd density terms often omitted in prior RPA calculations, and opens the way to study collective excitations in weakly bound, deformed nuclei relevant to radioactive-ion-beam facilities.
