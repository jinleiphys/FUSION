# The Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky versus the Spherical Shell Model: A Comparative Study of pf-Shell Nuclei

> A comparative study is performed of a deformed mean field theory, represented by the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model, and the spherical shell model. Energy spectra, occupation numbers, B(E2)-values, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments in the light pf shell nuclei are calculated in the two models and compared. The result is also compared to available experimental data which are generally well described by the shell model. Although the Nilsson-Strutinsky calculation does not include pairing, both the subshell occupation numbers and quadrupole properties are found to be rather similar in the two models. It is also shown that ``unpaired'' shell model calculations produce very similar energies as the CNS at all spins. The role of the pairing energy in the description of backbending and signature splitting in odd-mass nuclei is also discussed.

## Key claim

The cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model, despite neglecting all pairing interactions, produces subshell occupation numbers and quadrupole properties that are rather similar to those from the spherical shell model for light pf-shell nuclei, but only when the CNS energies are normalized at high spin rather than at the ground state.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic comparison of the spherical shell model (using the code Antoine with the KB3 residual interaction in the full pf-shell space) and the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky model (using a modified oscillator potential with standard parameters, minimizing energy over deformation parameters ε, γ, and ε₄) for nuclei with mass numbers A=44-49 near the N=Z line. For the shell model, they also performed "unpaired" calculations where the T=0 and/or T=1 pairing forces were subtracted from the interaction, allowing direct comparison with the pairing-free CNS results. Energy spectra, occupation numbers, B(E2) values, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments were calculated and compared between the two models and with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state quadrupole deformations from CNS: ε ≈ 0.23 for ⁴⁸Cr, ε ≈ 0.19 for ⁴⁶Ti, and ε ≈ 0.15 for ⁴⁴Ti
- Measured B(E2) values for 2⁺ → 0⁺ transitions: 96 e²fm⁴ for ⁴⁴Ti (from half-life 3.97±0.28 ps), 190 e²fm⁴ for ⁴⁶Ti, and 311 e²fm⁴ for ⁴⁸Cr
- CNS calculated excitation energy of the 4⁻ state in ⁴⁸Cr: E_x(4⁻) = 3.65 MeV, compared to experimental value 3.53 MeV
- The yrast bands terminate at I=12⁺ for ⁴⁴Ti, I=14⁺ for ⁴⁶Ti, and I=16⁺ for ⁴⁸Cr, corresponding to 4, 6, and 8 valence particles in the f₇/₂ shell respectively
- Pairing force strengths used: G_{T=0} = -0.51 ħω and G_{T=1} = -0.32 ħω, where ħω = 40A^{-1/3} MeV
- CNS ground-state occupation of f₅/₂ shell in ⁴⁸Cr: 0.06 particles, compared to shell model prediction of 0.56 particles

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between the CNS and shell model for selected nuclei in the A≈40 region (Cr48triax, 2004; Ar36; Dong03, 2003) to a more systematic study of pf-shell nuclei between A=44 and 49. The spherical shell model calculations build on the extensive work by the Madrid-Strasbourg group reviewed by Caurier (2004), while the CNS approach follows the formalism of Afanasjev (1999) and earlier applications to terminating bands and superdeformed bands. The study enables assessment of the reliability of the CNS approach for heavier nuclei where full spherical shell model calculations are not feasible, and clarifies the role of T=0 versus T=1 pairing in backbending and signature splitting, particularly showing that T=1 pairing is the main cause of backbending at I≈10 in ⁴⁴Ti and ⁴⁶Ti.
