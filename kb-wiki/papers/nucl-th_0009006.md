# Signature of a Pairing Transition in the Heat Capacity of Finite Nuclei

> The heat capacity of iron isotopes is calculated within the interacting shell model using the complete $(pf+0g_{9/2})$-shell. We identify a signature of the pairing transition in the heat capacity that is correlated with the suppression of the number of spin-zero neutron pairs as the temperature increases. Our results are obtained by a novel method that significantly reduces the statistical errors in the heat capacity calculated by the shell model Monte Carlo approach. The Monte Carlo results are compared with finite-temperature Fermi gas and BCS calculations.

# Signature of a Pairing Transition in the Heat Capacity of Finite Nuclei

## Key claim
The heat capacity of even-mass iron isotopes exhibits a "shoulder" feature around T ∼ 0.7–0.8 MeV that correlates with the suppression of spin-zero neutron pairs, providing a signature of the pairing transition in finite nuclei despite the large fluctuations that suppress any sharp discontinuity.

## Method
The heat capacity was calculated using the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method in the complete (pf+0g9/2)-shell for iron isotopes 52–62Fe. A novel correlated-sampling technique was employed in which the energies at β ± δβ are computed using the same set of auxiliary fields, reducing statistical errors by nearly an order of magnitude compared to conventional uncorrelated sampling. The heat capacity was obtained from the numerical derivative C = −β² dE/dβ, and the number of J=0 neutron pairs was computed directly in SMMC as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers
- Statistical errors in the heat capacity of ⁵⁴Fe for T ∼ 0.5–1 MeV are reduced by almost an order of magnitude using the new correlated method compared to the conventional method.
- The deformation parameters δ for even iron isotopes ⁵²Fe–⁶²Fe are 0.225, 0.215, 0.244, 0.222, 0.230, and 0.220, respectively, extracted from SMMC-calculated 2₁⁺ excitation energies using the Bohr-Mottelson formula with r₀ = 1.27 fm.
- In the BCS calculation for ⁶⁰Fe, the neutron pairing transition temperature is Tⁿc ≈ 0.9 MeV and the proton pairing transition temperature is Tᵖc ≈ 1.2 MeV.
- The heat capacity of ⁵⁷Fe is significantly lower than that of both adjacent even-mass isotopes ⁵⁶Fe and ⁵⁸Fe at T ∼ 0.7 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pairing in finite nuclei using a schematic pairing model (RCP98) that found a shallow "kink" in the heat capacity of even-even nuclei when only static and small-amplitude quantal fluctuations were included. It also relates to experimental S-shaped heat capacities extracted from level density measurements in rare-earth nuclei. The new correlated-sampling SMMC method enables realistic calculations in large model spaces that were previously impractical due to large statistical errors, and the results show that despite strong fluctuations suppressing the BCS discontinuity, a shoulder in the heat capacity remains as a signature of the pairing transition.
