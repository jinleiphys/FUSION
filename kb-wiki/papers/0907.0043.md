# Global analysis of J/psi suppression in cold nuclear matter

> Interpreting the J/psi suppression reported in nucleus--nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC requires the quantitative understanding of cold nuclear matter effects, such as the inelastic rescattering of J/psi states in nuclei or the nuclear modification of parton densities. With respect to our former Glauber analysis, we include in the present work the new PHENIX d--Au measurements, and analyze as well all existing data using the EPS08 nuclear parton densities recently released. The largest suppression reported in the new PHENIX analysis leads in turn to an increase of sigma from 3.5 +/- 0.3 mb to 5.4 +/- 2.5 mb using proton PDF. The stronger x-dependence of the G^{A}/G^p ratio in EPS08 as compared to e.g. EKS98 shifts the cross section towards larger values at fixed target energies (x_2 ~ 0.1) while decreasing somehow the value extracted at RHIC (x_2 ~10^{-2}).

## Key claim

The paper re-extracts the effective J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section from a global analysis of cold nuclear matter effects, finding that the updated PHENIX d-Au data (which show larger suppression) increase the extracted cross section from 3.5 +/- 0.3 mb to 5.4 +/- 2.5 mb when using proton PDFs, but the result depends strongly on which nuclear parton density parametrization is used, with the EPS08 set giving a value of 2.2 +/- 2.2 mb for the same data.

## Method

The authors perform a Glauber-model analysis of J/psi production cross-section ratios in hadron-nucleus and lepton-nucleus collisions, fitting the J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section sigma from data taken at SPS, FNAL, HERA-B, RHIC, and NMC. They include the new PHENIX d-Au measurements and incorporate the EPS08 nuclear parton density parametrization, comparing results to those obtained with proton PDFs and other nPDF sets (nDS, nDSg, EKS98, HKN). The fit uses a chi-squared minimization that accounts for correlated normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- J/psi-N cross section from PHENIX data using proton PDF: 5.4 +/- 2.5 mb (previously 3.5 +/- 3.0 mb)
- J/psi-N cross section from PHENIX data using EPS08 nPDF: 2.2 +/- 2.2 mb (previously 1.3 +/- 2.5 mb)
- Global fit result using nDS nPDF: 3.5 +/- 0.2 (stat.) +/- 2.6 (syst.) mb
- Global fit result using EPS08 nPDF: 6.0 +/- 0.2 mb with chi-squared/ndf = 1.7
- Global fit result using EKS98 nPDF: 5.2 +/- 0.2 mb with chi-squared/ndf = 1.5
- Individual experiment results with EPS08: NA3 gives 5.2 +/- 0.2 mb, NA50 gives 7.8 +/- 0.5 mb, E866 gives 8.0 +/- 3.7 mb (chi-squared/ndf = 20.4), PHENIX gives 2.2 +/- 2.2 mb, NMC gives <= 2.0 mb
- Relative change from EKS98 to EPS08: PHENIX decreases by 29%, E866 increases by 51%, SPS experiments increase by 10-15%

## Context

This work extends the previous Glauber analysis by Arleo and Tram (2008) by including the re-analyzed PHENIX d-Au data and the new EPS08 nuclear parton densities. The results are compared to earlier global analyses by Gerschel and Hufner (1993) and Kharzeev et al. (1996), which gave significantly higher cross sections due to different data sets. The strong dependence on nPDF choice (EPS08 vs. EKS98 vs. nDS) highlights the need for better constraints on gluon nuclear parton distributions, particularly the anti-shadowing region at x ~ 0.1 and the shadowing region at x ~ 10^-2, to enable reliable interpretation of J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions as a quark-gluon plasma signal.
