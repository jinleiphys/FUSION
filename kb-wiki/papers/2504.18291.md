# Machine Learning-Based b-Jet Tagging in pp Collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV

> Studying heavy-flavor jets in pp collision is important since they can test pQCD calculations and be used as a reference for heavy-ion collisions. Jets in this analysis are reconstructed from charged particles using the anti-$k_{\mathrm{T}}$ algorithm with a resolution parameter $R=$ 0.4 and with pseudorapidity $|η|<$ 0.5. Beauty jets are tagged using a machine learning model that uses a convolutional neural network trained on information extracted from the jet, tracks, and secondary vertices. The results show that this model is superior compared to other traditional tagging methods.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a convolutional neural network (CNN) model trained on jet, track, and secondary vertex features achieves superior b-jet tagging performance compared to traditional cut-based methods (impact parameter and secondary vertex methods) in simulated proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, but only after applying smearing to mimic the ALICE detector resolution rather than using a full GEANT4 simulation.

## Method

Proton-proton collision events at sqrt(s)=13 TeV were generated using the PYTHIA8 Monte Carlo event generator with the Monash 2013 tune. Charged particles with transverse momentum above 0.15 GeV/c and pseudorapidity |eta|<0.9 were selected, and their momentum and distance-of-closest-approach (DCA) were smeared according to the ALICE ITS2 detector resolutions. Jets were reconstructed from these charged particles using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameter R=0.4. A three-branch CNN was trained on features from the top 10 tracks (sorted by signed DCA), the top 10 secondary vertices (sorted by decay length), and global jet parameters, using 350k b-jets as signal and 350k background jets (20% charm, 80% light flavor).

## Key numbers

- Jet reconstruction uses anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameter R=0.4 and pseudorapidity |eta|<0.5
- Track selection requires pT > 0.15 GeV/c and |eta| < 0.9
- Primary vertex required within |z| < 10 cm in the beam direction
- Momentum smearing parameters: a=0.005, b=8e-4 GeV/c, c=0.001 (GeV/c)^(-1) for the ALICE ITS2 resolution
- DCA smearing parameters: a=7 and 6 micrometers, b=23 and 25 micrometer*GeV/c for transverse and z directions respectively
- Secondary vertex reconstruction uses tracks with pT > 0.3 GeV/c
- Training used 40M PYTHIA8 events yielding approximately 350k b-jets
- Model trained with batch size 1000 and 300 epochs
- Area under the ROC curve (AUC) is approximately 0.966
- At 50% efficiency, the ML model achieves 30% higher purity compared to the impact parameter method
- At 70% efficiency, the model reaches 90% purity for high-pT jets

## Context

This work extends previous studies from ATLAS and CMS (ATLAS 2023, ATLAS 2022, Bols 2020) that demonstrated the superiority of machine learning models over traditional cut-based b-tagging methods. The traditional methods compared include the impact parameter method and secondary vertex method as implemented in ALICE (ALICE 2021). The paper uses PYTHIA8 with detector smearing rather than full GEANT4 simulation, keeping the analysis at a methodological level. This enables future application of the trained model to real data or heavy-ion collision environments, particularly for ALICE measurements of b-jet cross sections, fragmentation, and substructure.
