# The chiral transition and U(1)_A symmetry restoration from lattice QCD using Domain Wall Fermions

> We present results on both the restoration of the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry and the effective restoration of the anomalously broken U(1)_A symmetry in finite temperature QCD at zero chemical potential using lattice QCD. We employ domain wall fermions on lattices with fixed temporal extent N_τ= 8 and spatial extent N_σ= 16 in a temperature range of T = 139 - 195 MeV, corresponding to lattice spacings of a \approx 0.12 - 0.18 fm. In these calculations, we include two degenerate light quarks and a strange quark at fixed pion mass m_π= 200 MeV. The strange quark mass is set near its physical value. We also present results from a second set of finite temperature gauge configurations at the same volume and temporal extent with slightly heavier pion mass. To study chiral symmetry restoration, we calculate the chiral condensate, the disconnected chiral susceptibility, and susceptibilities in several meson channels of different quantum numbers. To study U(1)_A restoration, we calculate spatial correlators in the scalar and pseudo-scalar channels, as well as the corresponding susceptibilities. Furthermore, we also show results for the eigenvalue spectrum of the Dirac operator as a function of temperature, which can be connected to both U(1)_A and chiral symmetry restoration via Banks-Casher relations.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that the anomalously broken U(1)_A symmetry is effectively restored at temperatures above the chiral crossover, but this conclusion is hedged by the small physical volume (aspect ratio V^{1/3}T = 2) and the single lattice spacing used, leaving open the possibility that finite-volume effects could alter the observed degree of U(1)_A breaking.

## Method

The HotQCD Collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations using domain wall fermions on 16^3 × 8 lattices with the Iwasaki gauge action, both with and without a Dislocation Suppressing Determinant Ratio (DSDR) to control residual chiral symmetry breaking. They computed the chiral condensate, disconnected chiral susceptibility, and susceptibilities in scalar and pseudo-scalar meson channels across a temperature range of 139–195 MeV, using two degenerate light quarks at fixed pion mass m_π = 200 MeV and a physical strange quark mass. They also analyzed the eigenvalue spectrum of the Dirac operator and spatial screening correlators to probe both chiral and U(1)_A symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: T = 139–195 MeV, corresponding to lattice spacings a ≈ 0.12–0.18 fm
- Fixed pion mass: m_π = 200 MeV for the DSDR ensembles
- Light quark chiral condensate at T = 139 MeV: ⟨ψ̅_l ψ_l⟩/T^3 = 9.23(14) (DSDR ensemble)
- Light quark chiral condensate at T = 195 MeV: ⟨ψ̅_l ψ_l⟩/T^3 = 2.07(9) (DSDR ensemble)
- Disconnected chiral susceptibility (renormalized, MSbar scheme) at T = 139 MeV: χ_disc/T^2 = 17.2(1.4)
- Disconnected chiral susceptibility (renormalized, MSbar scheme) at T = 195 MeV: χ_disc/T^2 = 2.0(1.2)
- Subtracted chiral condensate Δ_{l,s}/T^3 = 10.30(14) at T = 139 MeV, decreasing to 0.34(9) at T = 195 MeV
- Chiral crossover temperature identified: T ∼ 160–170 MeV
- At highest temperatures (186 and 195 MeV), a gap in the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum below approximately 20 MeV is observed

## Context

This work extends earlier domain wall fermion studies of QCD thermodynamics by the RBC-Bielefeld Collaboration (Cheng 2009) which suffered from large residual chiral symmetry breaking, and it provides a direct comparison with staggered fermion results from the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov 2011) using HISQ and asqtad actions. The use of a chirally symmetric fermion formulation enables a cleaner separation of chiral and U(1)_A symmetry restoration effects, and the results enable future studies on larger volumes (32^3 and 48^3) to resolve finite-volume uncertainties and make quantitative comparisons with staggered fermion calculations.
