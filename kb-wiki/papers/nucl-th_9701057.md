# Probing nuclear expansion dynamics with $π^-/π^+$-spectra

> We study the dynamics of charged pions in the nuclear medium via the ratio of differential $π^-$- and $π^+$-spectra in a coupled-channel BUU (CBUU) approach. The relative energy shift of the charged pions is found to correlate with the pion freeze-out time in nucleus-nucleus collisions as well as with the impact parameter of the heavy-ion reaction. Furthermore, the long-range Coulomb force provides a 'clock' for the expansion of the hot nuclear system. Detailed comparisons with experimental data for $Au + Au$ at 1 GeV/A and $Ni + Ni$ at 2.0 GeV/A are presented.

# Probing nuclear expansion dynamics with π⁻/π⁺-spectra

## Key claim

The ratio of differential π⁻ to π⁺ spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies (1–2 GeV/A) can serve as a "clock" for the expansion dynamics of the hot nuclear system, because the long-range Coulomb force experienced by charged pions at freeze-out correlates linearly with their production time and momentum. However, this correlation holds only for pions with CMS momenta above about 50 MeV/c, and the effect is most pronounced for soft pions emitted in forward direction, where spectator matter enhances the Coulomb distortion.

## Method

The authors employed a coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (CBUU) transport approach that propagates nucleons, baryonic resonances up to 1.95 GeV/c², and mesons (π, η, ρ, σ) in a time-dependent mean-field plus Coulomb potential. The Coulomb potential was computed at each time step by solving the static Poisson equation in three dimensions using an Alternating-Direction Implicit Iterative algorithm, with boundary conditions from a multipole expansion up to the quadrupole moment. Pion freeze-out times and densities were extracted by tracking when pions from resonance decays ceased to undergo further strong interactions.

## Key numbers

- For Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A and b = 0 fm, the number of pions reaches its maximum (asymptotic) value at the end of the reaction, about a factor 1.2 higher than the maximum number of Δ(1232) resonances in the high-density phase.
- Soft pions (CMS momentum 0.2 GeV/c) are emitted on average approximately 10 fm/c later than hard pions with momenta of about 1.0 GeV/c, for central Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A.
- Pions with CMS momentum ≈ 1.0 GeV/c are emitted on average at 0.8 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density); after their production the fireball expands to ≈ 0.5 ρ₀ before most soft pions are emitted.
- The average Coulomb potential at freeze-out increases linearly with pion CMS momentum between 0.1 and 1.0 GeV/c for Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A.
- Pions created in peripheral collisions (b > 6 fm) feel a ≈ 15% stronger Coulomb potential than those produced in central collisions (b ≤ 6 fm) for Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A.
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio R for Au + Au at 1.0 GeV/A (including Coulomb effects) is ≈ 3.0 at CMS kinetic energy ≈ 50 MeV, decreasing to ≈ 0.8 at ≈ 300 MeV.
- Without Coulomb effects, the ratio R is constant at ≈ 1.85 ± 0.1 for Au + Au, consistent with the isospin-asymmetry formula R = (5N² + NZ)/(5Z² + NZ) which gives 1.95.
- For Ni + Ni at 2.0 GeV/A (85° < Θ_CMS < 135°), the ratio R is close to 1 and shows only a very moderate enhancement at low pion energy, reproduced within statistical errors up to ≈ 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CBUU transport approach of Teis (1996) to systematically study Coulomb effects on charged pion spectra, building on earlier suggestions by Muentz and by Bass and Li that kinematic shifts between π⁻ and π⁺ spectra could probe the baryon expansion dynamics. The authors compare their calculations to experimental data from the KaoS collaboration (Wagner) and the FOPI collaboration for Au + Au at 1 GeV/A and Ni + Ni at 2.0 GeV/A. The approach complements pion correlation measurements (used at AGS and SPS energies but less promising at SIS due to low multiplicities) and dilepton studies from ρ⁰ decays or π⁺π⁻ annihilation that probe the high-density phase on timescales of 1.3 fm/c. The authors note that hadronic selfenergies for pions (which are approximately the same for π⁺ and π⁻) should cancel in the ratio, making precise experimental data valuable for clarifying remaining questions about low-momentum behavior.
