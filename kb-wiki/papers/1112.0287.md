# Ab Initio Calculations of Medium-Mass Nuclei with Normal-Ordered Chiral NN+3N Interactions

> We study the use of truncated normal-ordered three-nucleon interactions in ab initio nuclear structure calculations starting from chiral two- plus three-nucleon Hamiltonians evolved consistently with the similarity renormalization group (SRG). We present three key steps: (i) a rigorous benchmark of the normal-ordering approximation in the importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) for 4-He, 16-O, and 40-Ca; (ii) a direct comparison of the IT-NCSM results with coupled-cluster calculations at the singles and doubles level (CCSD) for 16-O; and (iii) first applications of SRG-evolved chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians in CCSD for the medium-mass nuclei 16,24-O and 40,48-Ca. We show that the normal-ordered two-body approximation works very well beyond the lightest isotopes and opens a path for ab initio studies of medium-mass and heavy nuclei with chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions. At the same time we highlight the predictive power of chiral Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation for chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions works very well beyond the lightest isotopes, enabling ab initio studies of medium-mass and heavy nuclei, but the approximation's accuracy depends on the specific Hamiltonian, nucleus, and oscillator frequency, with the largest relative deviation of about 2% observed for 4-He.

## Method

The authors benchmarked the normal-ordering approximation by comparing ground-state energies and expectation values from importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) calculations using the exact 3N interaction versus the NO2B approximation for 4-He, 16-O, and 40-Ca. They then applied the NO2B approximation in coupled-cluster calculations at the singles and doubles level (CCSD) for 16-O, 24-O, 40-Ca, and 48-Ca, using chiral NN+3N Hamiltonians evolved with the similarity renormalization group (SRG) at various flow parameters.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of 16-O with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -120.2 (+0.8) MeV, with the change when going to alpha=0.08 fm^4 given in parentheses.
* Ground-state energy of 24-O with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -152.1 (+0.5) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 40-Ca with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -343 (+6) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 48-Ca with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -392 (+7) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 16-O with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian (modified 3N interaction, cutoff 400 MeV) at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -126.4 (-1.9) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 24-O with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian (modified 3N interaction, cutoff 400 MeV) at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -164.8 (-2.8) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 40-Ca with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian (modified 3N interaction, cutoff 400 MeV) at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -357 (-6) MeV.
* Ground-state energy of 48-Ca with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian (modified 3N interaction, cutoff 400 MeV) at alpha=0.04 fm^4: -403 (-8) MeV.
* For 4-He with NN+3N-induced interaction, the NO2B approximation leads to overbinding of about 0.4 MeV for the softest Hamiltonian (alpha=0.08 fm^4) and 0.6 MeV for the hardest Hamiltonian (alpha=0.04 fm^4).
* For 16-O with NN+3N-induced interaction, the NO2B approximation consistently overestimates binding energy by about 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the use of normal-ordered 3N interactions from light nuclei to medium-mass systems, building on prior studies of density-dependent NN interactions for nuclear matter and finite nuclei (e.g., Holt and Kaiser (2010), Hebeler and Schwenk (2010)). The results confirm and extend the systematics of SRG-evolved chiral Hamiltonians reported by Roth and Langhammer (2011) to heavier nuclei like 48-Ca. The demonstrated accuracy of the NO2B approximation opens the path for ab initio studies of medium-mass and heavy nuclei with chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions, with ongoing work on generalizing the approach to open-shell systems and multi-determinantal reference states.
