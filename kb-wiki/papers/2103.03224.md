# Neutron-neutron scattering length from the $^6$He$(p,pα)nn$ reaction

> We propose a novel method to measure the neutron-neutron scattering length using the $^{6}$He$(p,pα)nn$ reaction in inverse kinematics at high energies. The method is based on the final state interaction (FSI) between the neutrons after the sudden knockout of the $α$ particle. We show that the details of the neutron-neutron relative energy distribution allow for a precise extraction of the $s$-wave scattering length. We present the state-of-the-art in regard to the theory of this distribution. The distribution is calculated in two steps. First, we calculate the ground-state wave function of $^6$He as a $αn n$ three-body system. For this purpose we use Halo effective field theory (Halo EFT), which also provides uncertainty estimates for the results. We compare our results at this stage to model calculations done with the computer code FaCE. In a second step we determine the effects of the $nn$ FSI using the $nn$ t-matrix. We compare these FSI results to approximate FSI approaches based on standard FSI enhancement factors. While the final distribution is sensitive to the $nn$ scattering length, it depends only weakly on the effective range. Throughout we emphasize the impact of theoretical uncertainties on the neutron-neutron relative energy distribution, and discuss the extent to which those uncertainties limit the extraction of the neutron-neutron scattering length from the reaction $^{6}$He$(p,pα)nn$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the neutron-neutron scattering length can be extracted from the \(^{6}\mathrm{He}(p,p\alpha)nn\) reaction in inverse kinematics at high energies, but only under the condition that the sudden knockout approximation holds and that non-\(nn\) final-state interactions are suppressed by the chosen kinematics, with the authors noting that assessing the error induced by the sudden approximation is an important topic for future work.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutron-neutron relative-energy distribution in two steps: first, they compute the ground-state wave function of \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) as an \(\alpha n n\) three-body system using Halo effective field theory (Halo EFT) at leading order, comparing these results to model calculations performed with the computer code FaCE that uses local Gaussian two-body potentials and a three-body force. Second, they determine the effects of the \(nn\) final-state interaction using the \(nn\) t-matrix, comparing these results to approximate FSI approaches based on standard Watson-Migdal enhancement factors.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron separation energy of \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) (the binding energy of the \(\alpha n n\) three-body system) is \(B_3^{(0)} = \SI{0.975}{\meV}\).
* Varying the nominal \(nn\) scattering length of \(\SI{-18.7}{\fm}\) by \(\SI{2}{\fm}\) changes characteristic parts of the \(nn\) relative-energy distribution around \(E_{nn} = \SI{100}{\keV}\) by roughly 10\%.
* The effect of the \(nn\) effective range on the spectrum is less than 1\%.
* The contribution to the \(nn\) energy distribution for \(E_{nn} < 1\) MeV from two neutrons in a relative \(^3P_1\) wave is at least a factor of 30 smaller than that from the \(^1S_0\) wave.
* The accepted value for the neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a_{nn} = (-18.6 \pm 0.4)\) fm, while the Bonn group extracted \(a_{nn} = -16.3(4)\) fm and the TUNL group extracted \(a_{nn} = -18.7(7)\) fm from deuteron breakup reactions.

## Context

This work proposes a novel method to resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the Bonn and TUNL measurements of the neutron-neutron scattering length from deuteron breakup reactions, as well as the accepted value from pion capture experiments at PSI and Los Alamos. The method avoids complications of hadronic three-body final states and energy-dependent neutron detection efficiency corrections that plagued previous experiments. The approach extends Halo EFT calculations of \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) structure (Ji 2014, Gobel 2019) and could be cross-checked using the \(t(p,2p)2n\) knockout reaction; a proposal to carry out such an experiment has been approved at RIKEN.
