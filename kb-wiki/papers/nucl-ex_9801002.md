# Correlated forward-backward dissociation and neutron spectra as a luminosity monitor in heavy ion colliders

> Detection in zero degree calorimeters of the correlated forward-backward Coulomb or nuclear dissociation of two colliding nuclei is presented as a practical luminosity monitor in heavy ion colliders. Complementary predictions are given for total correlated Coulomb plus nuclear dissociation and for correlated forward-backward single neutrons from the giant dipole peak.

## Key claim

Detection of correlated forward-backward Coulomb or nuclear dissociation in zero degree calorimeters can serve as a practical luminosity monitor in heavy ion colliders, provided that the mutual Coulomb plus nuclear dissociation cross section is used to eliminate the problematic lower impact parameter cutoff dependence that limits the precision of pure mutual Coulomb dissociation calculations to about 5% uncertainty.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for mutual (forward-backward) dissociation of heavy ions using the Weizsacker-Williams method, which expresses heavy-ion dissociation in terms of experimentally known photo-dissociation cross sections. They combine this with a Glauber model calculation of nuclear dissociation, using a Poisson distribution to account for multiple excitations and unitarity corrections. The impact parameter integral is evaluated with a lower cutoff that merges into the nuclear surface when nuclear dissociation is included, making the combined cross section insensitive to parameter variation.

## Key numbers

- Mutual Coulomb plus nuclear dissociation cross section for 100 GeV + 100 GeV Au + Au at RHIC: 11 barns, with an estimated uncertainty of less than about 5%
- Single neutron peak from giant dipole resonance Coulomb dissociation for Au + Au at RHIC: 50.6 barns (integrated from 8.1 MeV to 24.0 MeV photon energy)
- Correlated forward-backward single neutron cross section for Au + Au at RHIC: 0.45 barns
- Correlated cross section for a single neutron in one detector and any neutral in the other for Au + Au at RHIC: 1.35 barns
- Mutual Coulomb dissociation cross section for Au + Au at RHIC (unitarity corrected, infinite photon energy cutoff): 3.90 barns for b0 = 15 fm, 3.19 barns for b0 = 17.32 fm
- Mutual Coulomb plus nuclear dissociation cross section for Pb + Pb at LHC: 14.85 barns (with R = 6.75 fm)
- Single neutron peak cross section for Pb + Pb at LHC: 105.91 barns (with R = 6.75 fm)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1996) and Baltz and White (1996) on Coulomb dissociation at RHIC, addressing the intrinsic limitation that pure mutual Coulomb dissociation calculations vary approximately as the inverse square of the lower impact parameter cutoff. The authors show that adding nuclear dissociation to the calculation removes this cutoff dependence, enabling absolute luminosity calibration at heavy ion colliders such as RHIC and LHC. The proposed method enables practical luminosity monitoring using zero degree calorimeters, with complementary approaches including the clean single neutron peak from giant dipole resonance excitation and the larger combined Coulomb plus nuclear dissociation signal.
