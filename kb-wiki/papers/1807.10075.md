# Pseudo-scalar {\boldm $ q\bar q $} bound states at finite temperatures within a Dyson-Schwinger--Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The combined Dyson-Schwinger--Bethe-Salpeter equations are employed at non-zero temperature. The truncations refer to a rainbow-ladder approximation augmented with an interaction kernel which facilitates a special temperature dependence. At low temperatures, $T \to 0$, we recover a quark propagator from the Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equation which delivers, e.g. mass functions $B$, quark renormalization wave function $A$, and two-quark condensate $\la q \bar q \ra$ smoothly interpolating to the $T = 0$ results, despite the broken O(4) symmetry in the heat bath and discrete Matsubara frequencies. Besides the Matsubara frequency difference entering the interaction kernel, often a Debye screening mass term is introduced when extending the $T = 0$ kernel to non-zero temperatures. At larger temperatures, however, we are forced to drop this Debye mass in the infra-red part of the longitudinal interaction kernel to keep the melting of the two-quark condensate in a range consistent with lattice QCD results. Utilizing that quark propagator for the first few hundred fermion Matsubara frequencies we evaluate the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function in the pseudo-scalar $ q \bar q$ channel for the lowest boson Matsubara frequencies and find a competition of $ q \bar q$ bound states and quasi-free two-quark states at $T = {\cal O}$ (100 MeV). This indication of pseudo-scalar meson dissociation below the anticipated QCD deconfinement temperature calls for an improvement of the approach, which is based on an interaction adjusted to the meson spectrum at $T = 0$.

# Pseudo-scalar \( q\bar q \) bound states at finite temperatures within a Dyson-Schwinger--Bethe-Salpeter approach

## Key claim

The authors find an indication of pseudo-scalar meson dissociation at temperatures of order 100 MeV, which is below the anticipated QCD deconfinement temperature; however, they explicitly state that this result "calls for an improvement of the approach," since the interaction kernel was adjusted to the meson spectrum at zero temperature and the calculation is limited to the first few hundred fermion Matsubara frequencies and the lowest boson Matsubara frequencies.

## Method

The authors solve the combined Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equation and homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation at finite temperature using the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism, with the Alkofer-Watson-Weigel (AWW) interaction kernel that retains only the infra-red term. They compute the quark propagator dressing functions \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) for the first few hundred fermion Matsubara frequencies, then evaluate the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function in the pseudo-scalar channel for the lowest boson Matsubara frequencies to extract pole masses of \( q\bar q \) bound states as functions of temperature. At larger temperatures, the Debye screening mass is dropped from the infra-red part of the longitudinal interaction kernel to keep the melting of the two-quark condensate consistent with lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature obtained by ignoring the Debye mass in the infra-red term is \(T_c \sim 135\) MeV, which is only about 10-15% smaller than lattice QCD results reported by Yoki and by Fischer and Renorm.
- The AWW model parameters used are: \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV, \(D = 1\) GeV\(^2\), light quark mass \(m_l = m_{u,d} = 5\) MeV, and strange quark mass \(m_s = 115\) MeV.
- The vacuum quark condensate from the AWW model lies in the range \(-\langle q\bar q \rangle_0 = [(0.244 - 0.251)\text{ GeV}]^3\).
- The Debye mass for \(N_f = 4\) active flavors is taken as \(m_g^2 = 16\pi^2 T^2 / 5\).
- The competition of \( q\bar q \) bound states and quasi-free two-quark states is found at \(T = \mathcal{O}(100\text{ MeV})\).

## Context

This work extends the combined Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter approach, previously applied successfully to meson spectroscopy at zero temperature by Maris and Tandy (1999), Alkofer, Watson, and Weigel (2002), and others, to finite temperatures. The authors note that earlier finite-temperature studies using the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation with a Debye mass in the infra-red kernel (e.g., Blank and Krassnigg) produced critical temperatures about 50% lower than lattice QCD predictions. The present work enables a qualitative study of meson dissociation temperatures, but the authors emphasize that a proper description of the full temperature dependence requires improvement of the interaction kernel, possibly by incorporating lattice QCD results or solving the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon propagator as done by Fischer and collaborators.
