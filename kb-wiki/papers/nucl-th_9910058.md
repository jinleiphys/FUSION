# Deuteron Formation in Heavy Ion Collisions within the Faddeev Approach

> We address the formation of correlations in a general nonequilibrium situation. As an example we calculate the formation of light composite particles in a heavy ion collision. In particular we study the formation of deuterons via three-body reactions in some detail. To calculate the relevant reaction rates we solve the Faddeev equation which is consistently modified to include the self energy corrections and Pauli blocking factors due to surrounding nucleons. We find that the time scales of the reaction as well as the number of emitted deuterons change as medium dependent rates are used in the calculation.

# Deuteron Formation in Heavy Ion Collisions within the Faddeev Approach

## Key claim
The authors find that the time scales of deuteron formation and breakup, as well as the number of emitted deuterons, change when medium-dependent reaction rates are used in the calculation rather than isolated (vacuum) rates, with the effect being significant at lower densities and momenta.

## Method
The authors solve an in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation for three-body correlations embedded in an uncorrelated medium, incorporating nucleon self-energies in Hartree-Fock approximation, Pauli blocking factors, and the Mott effect of the deuteron. The resulting in-medium transition matrix elements are used to calculate breakup cross sections, which are then inserted into linearized Boltzmann equations to extract deuteron breakup times and chemical relaxation times as functions of density, temperature, and momentum.

## Key numbers
- At a temperature of T=10 MeV and nuclear density n=0.007 fm^-3 (approximately n0/25, where n0 is normal nuclear matter density), the medium-dependent deuteron breakup time is shorter than the isolated breakup time, with the difference vanishing for larger momenta.
- The chemical relaxation time at T=10 MeV shows significant differences between isolated and in-medium cross sections, with the difference increasing with increasing density.
- The Mott momentum for deuterons at T=10 MeV and T=20 MeV is shown as a function of density, below which no bound state pole exists in the few-body equations.
- The total number of deuterons as a function of time is calculated for the collision 129Xe + 119Sn at 50 MeV/A laboratory energy, with results considered preliminary due to statistics and temperature.

## Context
This work extends the Beth-Uehling-Uhlenbeck approach to heavy ion collisions (Danielewicz 1991) by providing a microscopic calculation of in-medium three-body reaction rates rather than using experimental cross sections or Born/impulse approximations. The approach is derived from a cluster expansion and Dyson equation approach (Dukelsky 1998) and builds on earlier in-medium AGS equation developments (Beyer 1996, 1997). The results enable more accurate microscopic simulations of light composite particle formation in heavy ion collisions and may have consequences for the triton-to-helium-3 ratio in asymmetric nuclear matter, as well as applications to supernova collapse and neutron star matter.
