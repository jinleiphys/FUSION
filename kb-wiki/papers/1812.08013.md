# Clusters in light stable and exotic nuclei

> Since the discovery of molecular resonances in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C in the early sixties a great deal of research work has been undertaken to study alpha-clustering. Our knowledge on physics of nuclear molecules has increased considerably and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. Occurrence of "exotic" shapes and Bose-Einstein Condensates in light alpha-cluster nuclei are investigated. Various approaches of superdeformed/hyperdeformed shapes associated with quasimolecular resonant structures are discussed. The astrophysical reaction rate of 12C+12C is extracted from recent fusion measurements at deep subbarrier energies near the Gamov window. Evolution of clustering from stability to the drip-lines is examined.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current understanding of clustering in light stable and exotic nuclei, arguing that while the link between alpha-clustering, quasimolecular resonances, and extreme deformations (superdeformed/hyperdeformed shapes) is well established, the existence and astrophysical impact of a low-energy \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C resonance near the Gamow window (around E\(_{cm}\) = 1.5 MeV) remains unconfirmed and controversial, with recent direct measurements showing a possible resonance in the alpha channel but not in the proton channel.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental and theoretical work on alpha-clustering, including fusion measurements of \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C at deep subbarrier energies using the Stella apparatus with LaBr\(_3\) scintillators and double-sided silicon-strip detectors, employing particle-gamma coincidence techniques and nanosecond timing to minimize background. It also discusses the extension of the Ikeda diagram to neutron-rich nuclei by von Oertzen (2006), incorporating \(^{14}\)C cluster cores alongside \(^{16}\)O cores, and compares results with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) and Generator-Coordinate-Method (GCM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in \(^{12}\)C is an excited 0\(^+\) state at 7.654 MeV, described as a bosonic condensate or alpha-particle gas.
- A state in \(^{16}\)O equivalent to the Hoyle state is predicted at about 15.1 MeV (the 0\(^{+}_{6}\) state), approximately 700 keV above the 4\(\alpha\)-particle breakup threshold.
- Quasimolecular resonances in the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C reaction have widths of approximately 100 keV, indicating a \(^{24}\)Mg intermediate system with a lifetime significantly longer than the nuclear crossing time.
- A possible low-energy resonance in \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C is reported at E\(_{cm}\) = 2.14 MeV, with a predicted resonance around E\(_{cm}\) = 1.5 MeV that would drastically enhance energy production in explosive stellar events.
- The Gamow window for stellar temperatures is given as T = 0.9 GK (E\(_0\) = 2.25 \(\pm\) 0.46 MeV) and T = 0.5 GK (E\(_0\) = 1.5 \(\pm\) 0.3 MeV).
- An upper limit for the radiative partial width of a transition between 10\(^+\) and 8\(^+\) resonant states at E(\(^{12}\)C) = 32 MeV was reported as 1.2 \(\pm\) 10\(^{-5}\).

## Context

This review extends the classic Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, 1968) and its extension by von Oertzen (2006) for neutron-rich nuclei, and discusses recent experimental results from the Stella collaboration (Heine, 2018; Fruet, 2018) that challenge or confirm earlier measurements by Spillane (2007), Becker, and Aguilera. The paper enables future searches for Bose-Einstein Condensates in \(^{16}\)O and for the predicted low-energy \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C resonance that could impact models of stellar carbon burning, X-ray superbursts, and type Ia supernovae, as discussed by Wiescher and Kajino.
