# Spin distribution of fission fragments involving bending and wriggling modes

> This paper presents a theoretical description of the spin distributions of fragments from low-energy induced and spontaneous nuclear fission, expressed in an analytical form. The mechanism of pumping high spin values for deformed fission fragments is explained. The idea is that the source of the generation of high relative orbital moments and spins of the fragments are the transverse wriggling and bending vibrations of the pre-fragments, while the nucleus remains "cold" until the moment of fission. To verify this hypothesis, experimental distributions for the induced fission of $\rm ^{232}Th$ and $\rm ^{238}U$ nuclei, as well as the spontaneous fission of $\rm ^{252}Cf$, were compared. The results show reasonable agreement both in the magnitude of the mean spin values and in the sawtooth shape of the sip distribution with respect to the fragment mass number. The results are also compared with other approaches to the description of these quantities, and possible reasons for their discrepancies are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the spin distributions of primary fission fragments in low-energy induced and spontaneous fission can be described analytically using only zero-point transverse wriggling and bending vibrations of the pre-fragments, under the assumption that the nucleus remains "cold" until scission. The authors hedge that the bending vibration frequencies for the induced fission of Th-232 and U-238 are estimated by assuming they are similar to those for the spontaneous fission of Cf-252, which is acknowledged as "not quite correct" due to different stiffness coefficients and fission mechanisms.

## Method

The authors constructed an analytical probability distribution for fragment spins by treating wriggling and bending oscillations as independent collective vibrational modes near the scission point, using a Gaussian form for each mode's spin distribution. They calculated the moments of inertia for these modes using formulas from Nix (1965) and Shneidman (2002), with bending frequencies estimated from Shneidman (2002) for Cf-252 and extrapolated to Th-232 and U-238 using the relation omega_w = 2.5-2.6 omega_b from Nix (1965). The spin distribution for each fragment was then obtained by integrating over the other fragment's spin and the azimuthal angle, yielding a simple analytical expression. The model was tested against experimental data from Wilson (2021) for the induced fission of Th-232 and U-238 and the spontaneous fission of Cf-252.

## Key numbers

- For U-238(n,f), the calculated average spin values range from 3.66 hbar (Sn-130) to 8.26 hbar (Ce-150) for the light fragment, and from 4.53 hbar (Te-135) to 8.63 hbar (Ce-153) for the heavy fragment.
- For Th-232(n,f), the calculated average spin values range from 3.28 hbar (Sn-130) to 8.26 hbar (Ce-150) for the light fragment, and from 3.58 hbar (Sn-133) to 8.23 hbar (Ce-151) for the heavy fragment.
- For Cf-252(sf), the calculated average spin values range from 4.15 hbar (Sn-130) to 8.99 hbar (Md-154) for the light fragment, and from 5.51 hbar (Te-136) to 9.06 hbar (Ce-154) for the heavy fragment.
- The bending oscillation frequencies hbar*omega_b used in the model range from 0.7 MeV to 1.2 MeV across all three fissioning systems.
- The ratio of fragment moment of inertia to rigid-body moment of inertia ranges from 0.10 to 0.79 for nuclei far from magic, and 0.10 to 0.30 for nuclei near magic.

## Context

This work extends the quantum fission theory framework of Kadmensky (2002, 2003, 2005, 2008) by providing an analytical alternative to the statistical FREYA model of Verbeke (2015) and Randrup and Vogt (2021) and the time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) approach of Bulgac (2016, 2022) and Stetcu (2022). The authors note that both FREYA and TDDFT show a strong correlation between fragment spins that contradicts the experimental data of Wilson (2021), and that TDDFT calculations initially produced incorrect angular distributions that required correction by Scamps (2024). The analytical simplicity of the proposed approach is presented as an advantage over these computationally intensive methods, enabling straightforward estimation of spin distributions for primary fission fragments.
