# Neutron stars and quark matter

## Key claim

Recent observations of neutron star masses close to the maximum predicted by nucleonic equations of state (approximately 2.2 solar masses) begin to challenge our understanding of dense matter and constrain the possible presence of quark matter in neutron star interiors, but the question of whether quark degrees of freedom dominate in the deep cores remains open because the transition density from hadronic to quark matter is not reliably known.

## Method

The paper reviews the current state of neutron star physics by examining nucleonic equations of state derived from two-body potentials (Argonne A18) and three-body forces (Urbana UIX), then integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute mass-radius relations and maximum masses. The effects of quark matter are explored using the simple MIT bag model with bag constants of 122 and 200 MeV/fm^3, and the possibility of a mixed phase with quark droplets is considered. Observational mass determinations from binary pulsars and x-ray binaries are compared to these theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for the nucleonic equation of state (A18+delta v+UIX) is approximately 2.2 solar masses
- Without three-body forces, the maximum mass is approximately 1.6 solar masses, below some observed masses
- Neutron star radii from nucleonic models are in the range 10-12 km
- The neutron drip density is approximately 10^11 g/cm^3 (2x10^-4 fm^-3)
- Nuclear matter saturation density n0 is approximately 0.16 fm^-3
- The neutron star in PSR J0751+1807 has mass 2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses
- The neutron star in Vela X-1 has mass 1.86 +/- 0.33 solar masses
- The neutron star in Cyg X-2 has mass 1.78 +/- 0.23 solar masses
- The neutron star in EXO0748-676 has mass approximately 2.1 +/- 0.28 solar masses and radius approximately 13.8 +/- 1.8 km
- For the QPO system 4U1820-30 with upper frequency approximately 1070 Hz, the deduced mass is approximately 2.0 solar masses
- A typical quark droplet is estimated to have radius approximately 5 fm and contain approximately 100 u quarks and approximately 300 d and s quarks, with net negative charge approximately 150
- The transition from hadronic to quark matter is estimated in the range approximately 5-10 n0
- The onset of the droplet phase could be at density as low as approximately 2 n0

## Context

This review extends the line of work on neutron star structure using nucleonic equations of state developed by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988) and Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998), and confronts these models with recent mass measurements that challenge softer equations of state. The paper discusses the possibility of quark matter cores in neutron stars, building on earlier work by Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg, Pethick, and Staubo (1993) on mixed phases, and notes that lattice gauge theory calculations at non-zero baryon density by de Forcrand (2001) and by Schmidt, Fodor, and Katz (2002) suggest a phase transition in the range of 3-10 n0. The observations enable future work to constrain whether a distinct family of quark stars can exist and to determine the role of quark degrees of freedom in neutron star interiors.
