# Charmed Baryon $Λ_c$ in Nuclear Matter

> Density dependences of the mass and self-energies of $Λ_c$ in nulear matter are studied in the parity projected QCD sum rule. Effects of nuclear matter are taken into account through the quark and gluon condensates. It is found that the four-quark condensates give dominant contributions. As the density dependences of the four-quark condensates are not known well, we examine two hypotheses. One is based on the factorization hypothesis (F-type) and the other is derived from the perturbative chiral quark model (QM-type). The F-type strongly depends on density, while the QM-type gives a weaker dependence. It is found that, for the F-type dependence, the energy of $Λ_c$ increases as the density of nuclear matter grows, that is, $Λ_c$ feels repulsion. On the other hand, the QM-type predicts a weak attraction ($\sim 20$ MeV at the normal nuclear density) for $Λ_c$ in nuclear matter. We carry out a similar analysis of the $Λ$ hyperon and find that the F-type density dependence is too strong to explain the observed binding energy of $Λ$ in nuclei. Thus we conclude that the weak density dependence of the four-quark condensate is more realistic. The scalar and vector self-energies of $Λ_c$ for the QM-type dependence are found to be much smaller than those of the light baryons.

## Key claim

The in-medium behavior of the charmed baryon Λc depends critically on the poorly-known density dependence of the four-quark condensate: under the factorization hypothesis (F-type) the Λc feels repulsion in nuclear matter, while under the perturbative chiral quark model (QM-type) it feels a weak attraction of about 20 MeV at normal nuclear density, and comparison with the known binding of the Λ hyperon favors the QM-type (weak density dependence) as more realistic.

## Method

The authors performed a parity-projected QCD sum rule analysis of the Λc baryon in nuclear matter, including first-order αs corrections and using a Gaussian kernel. They calculated the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 7 and 8 condensates, incorporating nuclear matter effects through quark and gluon condensates in the linear density approximation. Two hypotheses for the density dependence of the four-quark condensate were examined: the factorization hypothesis (F-type) and the perturbative chiral quark model (QM-type), and the analysis was extended to the Λ hyperon for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The QM-type predicts a weak attraction of approximately 20 MeV at normal nuclear density for Λc in nuclear matter.
- The charm quark mass used is mc = 1.67 ± 0.07 GeV.
- The quark condensate in vacuum is ⟨q̄q⟩₀ = −(0.246 ± 0.002 GeV)³.
- The nucleon sigma term is σN = 45 MeV.
- The gluon condensate in vacuum is ⟨(αs/π)G²⟩₀ = 0.012 ± 0.0036 GeV⁴.
- The nucleon expectation value of the gluon condensate is ⟨(αs/π)G²⟩N = −0.65 ± 0.15 GeV.
- The parton distribution moments are A₂q = 0.62 ± 0.06 and A₃q = 0.15 ± 0.02.
- The nucleon expectation value of the mixed quark-gluon operator is ⟨q†gσ·Gq⟩N = −0.33 GeV².
- The strong coupling constant is taken as αs = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of Λc in nuclear matter by Wang (2011) and Azizi (2016), which gave inconsistent results (repulsion versus large attraction) and did not include αs corrections or parity projection. The authors include both αs corrections and parity projection, and compare with the known binding of the Λ hyperon in nuclei to discriminate between the two four-quark condensate models. This enables future studies of charmed baryon-nucleon interactions and the possibility of Λc-nucleus bound states, building on earlier work by Dover (1977), Bando (1981-1985), and more recent lattice QCD simulations by Miyamoto (2016).
