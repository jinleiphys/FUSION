# Color transparency in the reaction nuclear rho production by virtual photons

> We study the nuclear transparency $T$ for the exclusive reaction $γ^*+A\toρ+p+(A-1)^*$ at incident virtual photon energies $ν\ge$ 10 GeV %where the final state of the $A-1$-nucleon system is a one-hole state of the initial nucleus, to investigate the separate dependence on the photon virtuality, $Q^2$ and the 4-momentum-transfer-squared to the knocked-out proton, $t$. If the effects of color transparency are included, $T$ shows significant variation with $t$ even for small values of $Q^2$ for fixed values of the coherence length $l_c$, and also shows significant increase as $Q^2$ is increased at fixed $l_c$ and $t$. The value of $T$ is found to depend strongly on the phase space over which it is measured.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency \(T\) for the exclusive reaction \(\gamma^*+A\to\rho+p+(A-1)^*\) shows significant variation with the 4-momentum-transfer-squared to the knocked-out proton, \(t\), even for small values of the photon virtuality \(Q^2\) when the coherence length \(l_c\) is fixed, and also increases significantly with \(Q^2\) at fixed \(l_c\) and \(t\); however, the value of \(T\) depends strongly on the phase space over which it is measured, meaning that experimental extraction of color transparency signals requires careful control of kinematic cuts.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber model modified for particle production, incorporating the quantum diffusion model to include color transparency effects via position-dependent effective cross-sections for the outgoing \(\rho\) meson and proton. They calculated the production amplitude for the reaction where the residual \((A-1)\)-nucleon system is a one-hole state of the initial nucleus, using shell-model wavefunctions and the eikonal approximation for the outgoing proton. The transparency \(T\) was computed both at fixed missing momentum \(\mathbf{p}_m=0\) and integrated over a domain of \(\mathbf{p}_m\), for nuclei \(^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), over ranges of \(Q^2\), \(t\), and coherence length \(l_c\).

## Key numbers

* For nuclear matter density \(\rho \simeq 0.166\;\text{fm}^{-3}\) and proton-nucleon total cross-section \(\sigma = 40\;\text{mb}\), the mean-free path of an outgoing proton in the nucleus is \(l \simeq 1.5\;\text{fm}\).
* The coherence length is given by \(l_c = 2\nu / (Q^2 + m_V^2)\), where \(m_V\) is the vector meson mass.
* The formation length is estimated as \(l_h \simeq 2 p_h / (M_n^2 - M_h^2)\), with the mass-squared difference taken as \(M_n^2 - M_N^2 = M_n^2 - M_\rho^2 = 0.7\;\text{GeV}^2\) for both the proton and the \(\rho\) meson.
* The quantum diffusion model uses \(\langle k_t^2\rangle^{1/2} = 0.35\;\text{GeV}\) for the average transverse momentum of a quark in the hadron.
* The JLab experiment (fassi12) measured transparencies on \(^{12}\text{C}\) and \(^{56}\text{Fe}\) for \(Q^2\) from \(1.0\) to \(2.3\;\text{GeV}^2\), with coherence lengths varying from \(\sim 0.5\;\text{fm}\) to \(\sim 0.85\;\text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the semi-inclusive electroproduction calculation of Miller (2008) for \(\rho\) mesons, which used the Glauber model with color transparency but did not focus on the knocked-out proton. It contrasts with earlier searches for color transparency in \(A(e,e'p)\) reactions at SLAC (Makins 1994, O'Neill 1995) and JLab (Garrow 2002), which showed no indication of color transparency, and with the \((p,2p)\) experiments at Brookhaven (Carroll 1988) and later (Mardor 1998, Leksanov 2001) that showed a non-monotonic transparency versus \(t\). The results enable future experiments to design measurements of \(\rho\) electroproduction with proton knockout that isolate color transparency effects by holding \(l_c\) fixed while varying \(Q^2\) and \(t\), and by carefully controlling the phase space over which transparency is integrated.
