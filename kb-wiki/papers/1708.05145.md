# Goodness of Isospin in Neutron Rich Systems from the Fission Fragment Distribution

> We present the results of our calculations for the relative yields of neutron-rich fission fragments emitted in $^{208}$Pb ($^{18}$O, fission) reaction by using the concept of the conservation of isospin and compare with the experimental data. We take into account a range of isospin values allowed by the isospin algebra and assume that the fission fragments are formed in Isobaric Analog States. We also take into account the neutron multiplicity data for various neutron-emission channels in each partition, and use them to obtain the weight factors in calculating the yields. We then calculate the relative yields of the fission fragments. Our calculated results are able to reproduce the experimental trends reasonably well. This is the first direct evidence of the isospin conservation in neutron-rich systems and may prove a very useful tool in their studies.

# Goodness of Isospin in Neutron Rich Systems from the Fission Fragment Distribution

## Key claim
The relative yields of neutron-rich fission fragments from the 208Pb(18O, fission) reaction can be reproduced using only isospin conservation and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, without including shell effects, provided that the fission fragments are assumed to form in Isobaric Analog States and neutron multiplicity weight factors are incorporated. This provides the first direct evidence that isospin becomes a reasonably good quantum number in neutron-rich systems, consistent with the theoretical prediction of Lane and Soper (1962) that isospin impurity decreases with neutron excess by a factor of 2/(N-Z+2).

## Method
The authors calculated relative fission fragment yields for the 208Pb(18O, fission) reaction by assigning total isospin values to the compound nucleus, residual compound nucleus, and fragments using Kelson's conjectures, which assume that neutron emission favors excited states with T > T3 and that observed fragments form in Isobaric Analog States. They constructed all possible fragment pairs for each neutron-emission channel, computed the squared Clebsch-Gordan coefficients from the isospin part of the wavefunction, and weighted these intensities by normalized neutron multiplicity data taken from Bogachev et al. (2007) to obtain final relative yields.

## Key numbers
- The target 208Pb has T3 = 22 and projectile 18O has T3 = 1, giving the compound nucleus a unique isospin of T_CN = 23.
- For mass A = 100, the three isobars 100Ru, 100Mo, and 100Zr have T3 values of 6, 8, and 10 respectively; the authors assigned total isospin T = 10 to all three.
- The experimental data from Bogachev et al. (2007) have an estimated 10-30% error in total relative yields, leading to a minimum 10% error in partition-wise relative yields.
- The 98Zr isomer has a half-life of t1/2 = 1.7 microseconds, which lowers the experimental yield for that fragment.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Lane and Soper (1962), who showed that isospin impurity decreases with neutron excess, and applies Kelson's conjectures (1969) to assign isospin to fission fragments. It builds on the proposal by Jain et al. (2003) that major features of neutron-rich fission fragments could be obtained using isospin conservation, resolving the earlier ambiguity in assigning isospin to fragments. The calculations are compared with experimental data from Bogachev et al. (2007) and Banerjee et al. (2008), and the results suggest that isospin conservation can serve as a simple predictive tool for studying neutron-rich systems without requiring shell effect corrections, though the authors note that shell effects at A = 124 (Z = 50 closed shell) may explain discrepancies in the Zr-Sn partition.
