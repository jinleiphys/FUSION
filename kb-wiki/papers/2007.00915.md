# Dynamic scale anomalous transport in QCD with electromagnetic background

> We discuss phenomenological implications of the anomalous transport induced by the scale anomaly in QCD coupled to an electromagnetic (EM) field, based on a dilaton effective theory. The scale anomalous current emerges in a way perfectly analogous to the conformal transport current induced in a curved spacetime background, or the Nernst current in Dirac and Weyl semimetals -- both current forms are equivalent by a "Weyl transformation". We focus on a spatially homogeneous system of QCD hadron phase, which is expected to be created after the QCD phase transition and thermalization. We find that the EM field can induce a dynamic oscillatory dilaton field which in turn induces the scale anomalous current. As the phenomenological applications, we evaluate the dilepton and diphoton productions induced from the dynamic scale anomalous current, and find that those productions include a characteristic peak structure related to the dynamic oscillatory dilaton, which could be tested in heavy ion collisions. We also briefly discuss the out-of-equilibrium particle production created by a nonadiabatic dilaton oscillation, which happens in a way of the so-called tachyonic preheating mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a dynamic oscillatory scale anomalous current can be induced in QCD hadron phase by an electromagnetic field through a dilaton effective theory, but this result is conditional on assuming the lightest isosinglet scalar meson (presumably the f0(500)) behaves as a pseudo dilaton and that the system is spatially homogeneous, which is taken as a first approximation for heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors construct a dilaton effective Lagrangian using a conformal compensator field with a logarithmic potential that reproduces the partially conserved dilatation current (PCDC) relation, analogous to chiral perturbation theory. They then couple this dilaton theory to an electromagnetic field via the scale anomaly, assuming a soft-dilaton limit where the dilaton mass is much smaller than the dynamical quark mass scale, and analyze the resulting dynamic oscillatory behavior of the dilaton field in a homogeneous electromagnetic background.

## Key numbers

- The QCD hadron phase is thermalized at temperature ~100 MeV after the QCD phase transition
- The dilaton mass m_phi is counted as O(p) in the scale expansion, analogous to the pion mass m_pi ~ O(p)
- The dilaton decay constant f_phi appears in the potential V(chi) = (m_phi^2 f_phi^2 / 4) (chi/f_phi)^4 [log(chi/f_phi) - 1/4]
- The electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions are of order m_pi^2 with m_pi being the pion mass
- The ratio m_f0(500) / m_dyn ~ 500 MeV / 300 MeV is noted as approximately order one

## Context

This work extends the concept of scale anomalous transport previously discussed in QED in curved spacetime by Chernodub (2016, 2017) and in Dirac/Weyl semimetals (Nernst current), applying it dynamically to QCD hadron phase. The dilaton effective theory approach follows the dilaton-chiral perturbation theory framework established by Matsuzaki (2013) for nearly conformal gauge theories. The paper enables future experimental searches for characteristic peak structures in dilepton and diphoton production from heavy ion collisions, and connects to tachyonic preheating mechanisms studied in inflationary cosmology by Felder (2000).
