# Solving Bethe-Salpeter equation for two fermions in Minkowski space

> The method of solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, which we developed previously for spinless particles, is extended to a system of two fermions. The method is based on the Nakanishi integral representation of the amplitude and on projecting the equation on the light-front plane. The singularities in the projected two-fermion kernel are regularized without modifying the original Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The numerical solutions for the J=0 bound state with the scalar, pseudoscalar and massless vector exchange kernels are found. The stability of the scalar and positronium states without vertex form factor is discussed. Binding energies are in close agreement with the Euclidean results. Corresponding amplitudes in Minkowski space are obtained.

## Key claim

The method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, previously developed for spinless particles, is extended to two-fermion systems by using the Nakanishi integral representation and light-front projection, but the direct application to fermionic kernels introduces numerical difficulties from discontinuities that diverge as z approaches +/-1, which are resolved by a regularization procedure that multiplies the equation by an eta factor before projection.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for two fermions in a J=0 bound state is decomposed into four scalar functions multiplied by independent spin structures, each expressed via the Nakanishi integral representation. The equation is projected onto the light-front plane by replacing the momentum k with k + (omega/(omega dot p)) beta and integrating over beta, yielding a system of coupled two-dimensional integral equations for the Nakanishi weight functions g_i(gamma,z). To regularize singularities in the kernel that appear as discontinuities at z'=z which diverge when z approaches +/-1, the original equation is multiplied by an eta factor containing two scalar propagators with mass L before applying the light-front projection, producing smoother kernels that yield the same binding energies and weight functions independent of L.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for scalar exchange with mu=0.15 and Lambda=2: B=0.01 corresponds to g^2=7.813, B=0.50 corresponds to g^2=86.95
- Binding energies for scalar exchange with mu=0.50 and Lambda=2: B=0.01 corresponds to g^2=25.23, B=0.50 corresponds to g^2=157.4
- Binding energies for pseudoscalar exchange with mu=0.15 and Lambda=2: B=0.01 corresponds to g^2=224.8, B=0.50 corresponds to g^2=323.1
- Binding energies for pseudoscalar exchange with mu=0.50 and Lambda=2: B=0.01 corresponds to g^2=422.3, B=0.50 corresponds to g^2=525.9
- Binding energies for positronium (massless vector exchange) with mu=0.0 and Lambda=2: B=0.01 corresponds to g^2=3.265, B=0.50 corresponds to g^2=36.62
- Critical coupling constant for scalar exchange in the Bethe-Salpeter equation: g_c = 2*pi (approximately 6.28), compared to the light-front value of g_c=6.84 from a prior work by the same authors
- Critical coupling constant for positronium without vertex form factor: g_c = pi (approximately 3.14)
- Regularization parameter L=1.1 m used in calculations

## Context

This work extends the method previously developed for spinless particles (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2006) to two-fermion systems, building on the Nakanishi integral representation approach of Nakanishi (1963, 1965) and the light-front projection technique. The results for scalar exchange binding energies agree to four digits with Euclidean space calculations by Dorkin (2009), while pseudoscalar results show small discrepancies of about 0.5%. The method enables computation of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Minkowski space, which is necessary for calculating electromagnetic form factors where the Wick rotation fails, as shown in earlier work by Carbonell, Karmanov, and Mangin-Brinet (2007).
