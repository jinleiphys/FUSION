# Analysis of eta production using a generalized Lee model

> We have investigated the processes N($π$, $π$)N and N($π$, $η$)N close to eta threshold using a simple, nonrelativistic Lee model which has the advantage of being analytically solvable. It is then possible to study the Riemann sheets of the S-matrix and the behavior of its resonance poles especially close to threshold. A theoretical simulation of the experimental cusp effect at eta threshold leads to a characteristic distribution of poles on the Riemann sheets. We find a pole located in the $4^{th}$ Riemann sheet that up to now has not been discussed. It belongs to the cusp peak at eta threshold. In addition we obtain the surprising result using the Lee model that the resonance $S_{11}(1535)$ does not play a large role. The main features of the experimental data can be reproduced without explicitly introducing this resonance. Furthermore, we have also studied the reactions N($γ$, $π$)N and N($γ$, $η$)N and find reasonable agreement between the data and both models with and without the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance.

# Analysis of Eta Production using a Generalized Lee Model

## Key claim

Using a nonrelativistic Lee model that is analytically solvable, the authors find that the main features of experimental data for N(π, π)N and N(π, η)N near eta threshold can be reproduced without explicitly introducing the S₁₁(1535) resonance, suggesting that the importance of this resonance is commonly overestimated and that threshold effects (cusp) must be taken into account. The hedge is that the model is simple and nonrelativistic, and the authors state they find "reasonable agreement" rather than perfect description, with the goal being to investigate the interplay of cusp effect and resonance rather than perfect data description.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized Lee model in the Hilbert subspace with L = 0 and I = 1/2, describing interactions between the nucleon, two resonances (S₁₁(1535) and S₁₁(1650)), and two mesons (pion and eta), with both resonance interaction (meson+nucleon ↔ resonance) and contact interaction (nonresonant meson scattering). They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation analytically to obtain scattering amplitudes and the S-matrix, then fitted coupling constants and bare resonance masses to experimental partial wave analysis data from the VPI solution (SM90) for pion-nucleon scattering, and also studied photoproduction using perturbation theory. Two-pion production was accounted for by introducing an effective "two-pion" meson particle with a quadratic phase space density.

## Key numbers

- Eta threshold energy: 1486 MeV (center-of-mass)
- Cut-off parameter C chosen as 2000 MeV for meson-nucleon interaction
- Average masses used: m_π = 137 MeV, m_N = 939 MeV
- Resonance S₁₁(1650) pole in the 3rd sheet found as (1666-81i) MeV in the model with S₁₁(1535) and as (1671-57i) MeV in the model without S₁₁(1535), determined by speed plot technique
- The cusp peak at eta threshold shows a very narrow spike with a width of a few MeV

## Context

This work extends the Lee model approach (Lee 1954) to study the interplay between the S₁₁(1535) resonance and the cusp effect at eta threshold, building on earlier work by Hohler (1992, 1998) who questioned the existence of S₁₁(1535) and by Kaiser et al. (1997) who suggested it could be a quasi-bound state of kaon/Sigma-hyperon. The paper enables a clearer separation of threshold effects from genuine resonance contributions in eta production analyses, and provides a framework for studying pole trajectories on Riemann sheets that can be applied to other threshold phenomena in hadronic physics.
