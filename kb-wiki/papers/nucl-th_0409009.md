# Search for narrow six-quark states in the reactions γd -->πγNN

> We study the reactions γd --> πγNN with the aim to search for six-quark states, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. Such states predicted by a variety of QCD inspired models and recent evidence from Proton Linear Accelerator of INR (Moscow) strongly suggests the existence of such states with masses 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV. We propose an experiment at MAMI-B which will provide a unique opportunity to observe such dibaryon states in mass region up to 2000 MeV and determine their masses and quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The authors propose that narrow six-quark states (supernarrow dibaryons, SNDs) with masses below the pion production threshold can be observed in the reactions γd → πγNN at MAMI-B, provided that the nucleons in the deuteron overlap sufficiently (probability η ≈ 10⁻⁴) to form a six-quark state, and that the decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle.

## Method

The authors calculate the gauge-invariant amplitudes for photoproduction of isovector SNDs D(1,1⁻,0) and D(1,1⁺,1) in the reactions γd → π⁺D, γd → π⁻D, and γd → π⁰D, using diagrams where a pion is emitted from a six-quark state in the deuteron. They estimate the unknown coupling constants and the six-quark probability η from previous pd → ppX₁ data, then compute total and differential cross sections for photon energies 300–800 MeV. A GEANT3 simulation is performed for the Crystal Ball and TAPS detector setup at MAMI-B to estimate yields and background rejection.

## Key numbers

* Three narrow peaks observed in missing mass M_{pX₁} of pd → p + pX₁ at 1904 ± 2, 1926 ± 2, and 1942 ± 2 MeV, with widths equal to the experimental resolution (~5 MeV) and statistical significances of 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations, respectively.
* Corresponding peaks in missing mass M_{X₁} of the same reaction at 966 ± 2, 986 ± 2, and 1003 ± 2 MeV, differing from the neutron mass (939.6 MeV), supporting the interpretation that X₁ = γ + n.
* Calculated decay widths for D(1,1⁺,1) at M = 1.90 GeV: 0.51 eV; at M = 2.013 GeV: 109 eV (for η = 0.01; scaling to η ≈ 10⁻⁴ reduces widths by ~10²).
* Calculated total cross section for D(1,1⁻,0) production in γd → π⁺D: 50–160 nb for photon energies 300–800 MeV; for D(1,1⁺,1) in the same channel: ~50 nb.
* For γd → π⁻D, total cross sections reach 225 nb (D(1,1⁻,0)) and 110 nb (D(1,1⁺,1)) at M = 1904 MeV.
* Upper limit from TRIUMF pionic deuterium experiment: BR((π⁻d)_{atom} → γ d₁) < 6.7 × 10⁻⁶ (90% C.L.) for masses 1920–1980 MeV; authors' crude estimate gives ~5.4 × 10⁻⁷.
* Upper limit from Tamii et al. (2000): production cross section < 2 μb/sr for Γ_D << 1 MeV, and < 3.5 μb/sr for Γ_D = 3 MeV, in the mass region 1896–1914 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs) originally motivated by QCD-inspired models such as the MIT bag model (Mulders et al., 1980) and the chiral soliton model (Kopeliovich, 1995). It directly follows the experimental observation of three narrow peaks in pd → p + pX₁ at the INR Proton Linear Accelerator (Fil'kov et al., 2001–2004) and proposes a new photoproduction experiment at MAMI-B using the Crystal Ball and TAPS detectors. The reactions γd → πγNN offer a cleaner environment than previous hadronic experiments (e.g., Tamii et al., 2000, at RCNP) and could determine the quantum numbers of the SNDs, as well as clarify the nature of the exotic baryon states reported by Tatischeff et al. (1998) in pp → π⁺X.
