# Hard-Wall and Non-Uniform Lattice Monte Carlo Approaches to One-Dimensional Fermi Gases in a Harmonic Trap

> We present in detail two variants of the lattice Monte Carlo method aimed at tackling systems in external trapping potentials: a uniform-lattice approach with hard-wall boundary conditions, and a non-uniform Gauss-Hermite lattice approach. Using those two methods, we compute the ground-state energy and spatial density profile for systems of N=4 - 8 harmonically trapped fermions in one dimension. From the favorable comparison of both energies and density profiles (particularly in regions of low density), we conclude that the trapping potential is properly resolved by the hard-wall basis. Our work paves the way to higher dimensions and finite temperature analyses, as calculations with the hard-wall basis can be accelerated via fast Fourier transforms, the cost of unaccelerated methods is otherwise prohibitive due to the unfavorable scaling with system size.

## Key claim
The hard-wall boundary condition method on a uniform lattice can properly resolve the harmonic trapping potential for one-dimensional fermions, as demonstrated by favorable comparison with the more natural but computationally expensive Gauss-Hermite lattice approach, particularly in regions of low density.

## Method
The authors implemented two lattice Monte Carlo approaches for attractively interacting SU(2) fermions in a one-dimensional harmonic trap: a non-uniform Gauss-Hermite lattice that diagonalizes the harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian exactly, and a uniform lattice with hard-wall (infinite square well) boundary conditions where the kinetic energy is diagonal in the sine-wave basis. Both methods use a symmetric Suzuki-Trotter decomposition and Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to handle the two-body contact interaction, with the path integral evaluated via hybrid Monte Carlo. The hard-wall basis allows Fourier acceleration because the sine functions are linear combinations of plane waves, reducing computational scaling from O(V^2) to O(V ln V) in higher dimensions.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy per particle computed for N = 2, 4, 6, 8 particles in a harmonic trap, shown in units of the non-interacting case energy and in units of ℏω
- Density profiles computed for N = 4 and 8 particles for both noninteracting systems and interacting systems at coupling 2a_HO/a_0 ≈ 1.7
- The hard-wall basis reproduces the expected gaussian decay of density profiles over multiple orders of magnitude before breaking down at large distances due to the wall

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Gauss-Hermite lattice approach (Berger, Anderson, Drut) for trapped fermions by introducing a computationally cheaper hard-wall method. Most prior treatments of trapped 1D fermions combined the Bethe-ansatz solution with the local-density approximation (HomPlusLDA, BAplusLDA), while exact diagonalization approaches (ExactDiag) were limited to small systems. The hard-wall method enables ab initio Monte Carlo calculations that can be Fourier-accelerated, paving the way to higher dimensions and finite temperature analyses where the non-uniform method's O(V^2) scaling becomes prohibitive.
