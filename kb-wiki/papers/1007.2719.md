# Particle-particle random phase approximation applied to Beryllium isotopes

> This work is dedicated to the study of even-even 8-14 Be isotopes using the particle-particle Random Phase Approximation that accounts for two-body correlations in the core nucleus. A better description of energies and two-particle amplitudes is obtained in comparison with models assuming a neutron closed-shell (or subshell) core. A Wood-Saxon potential corrected by a phenomenological particle-vibration coupling term has been used for the neutron-core interaction and the D1S Gogny force for the neutron-neutron interaction. Calculated ground state properties as well as excited state ones are discussed and compared to experimental data. In particular, results suggest the same 2s_1/2-1p_1/2 shell inversion in 13Be as in 11Be.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the same 2s₁/₂-1p₁/₂ shell inversion observed in ¹¹Be also occurs in ¹³Be, based on the ability of the particle-particle Random Phase Approximation (pp-RPA) with a ¹²Be core to reproduce experimental ground-state properties and excited-state energies of ¹⁴Be only when this inversion is assumed.

## Method

The authors applied the particle-particle Random Phase Approximation (pp-RPA) to even-even ⁸⁻¹⁴Be isotopes, using either ¹⁰Be or ¹²Be as the reference core nucleus. The neutron-core interaction was described by a Wood-Saxon potential corrected with a phenomenological particle-vibration coupling term fitted to the ¹¹Be spectrum, while the neutron-neutron interaction used the D1S Gogny force. The pp-RPA equations were solved in a 20 fm box, and the spin-orbit term of the two-body interaction was included to describe excited states with different spins and parities.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ(¹²Be) = 3.62 MeV (theory) vs. 3.67 ± 0.01 MeV (experiment) from a ¹⁰Be core calculation.
- Two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ(¹⁴Be) = 1.29 MeV (theory, scenario B with inversion) vs. 1.26 ± 0.01 MeV (experiment).
- Root-mean-square radius of ¹²Be = 2.76 fm (theory) vs. 2.59 ± 0.06 fm (experiment).
- Root-mean-square radius of ¹⁴Be = 2.91 fm (theory, scenario B) vs. 3.10 ± 0.15 fm (experiment).
- Excitation energy of the 0⁺₂ state in ¹²Be = 2.48 MeV (theory) vs. 2.24 MeV (experiment).
- Excitation energy of the 1⁻₁ state in ¹²Be = 2.59 MeV (theory) vs. 2.68 ± 0.03 MeV (experiment), with B(E1) = 0.45 e²fm² (theory) vs. 0.051 ± 0.013 e²fm² (experiment).
- Relative energy of the 0⁺₂ state in ¹⁴Be = 1.26 MeV (scenario A) and 1.24 MeV (scenario B) vs. 1.22 ± 0.18 MeV (experiment).
- Relative energy of the 1⁻₁ state in ¹⁴Be = 1.8 MeV (theory, scenario B) vs. 1.8 ± 0.1 MeV (experiment from Labiche et al., 2001).
- B(E1) for the 1⁻₁ state in ¹⁴Be = 3.7 × 10⁻² e²fm² (theory, scenario B).
- Integrated B(E1) strength below E_r = 3.2 MeV in ¹⁴Be = 0.17 e²fm² (theory, scenario B).

## Context

This work extends the earlier pp-RPA study of Pacheco and Vinh Mau (2002) by including the spin-orbit interaction and applying the model to both ¹⁰Be and ¹²Be cores to describe ⁸⁻¹⁴Be isotopes. The results support the shell inversion hypothesis in ¹³Be previously proposed by Labiche et al. (1999) and Pacheco and Vinh Mau (2002), and are consistent with recent experimental data from Kondo et al. (2010). The model enables predictions for excited states and dipole transition probabilities that can be tested by future experiments, particularly to discriminate between the inversion and non-inversion scenarios for the 2s₁/₂-1p₁/₂ ordering in ¹³Be.
