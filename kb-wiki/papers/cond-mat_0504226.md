# Boson-Fermion pairing in a Boson-Fermion environment

> Propagation of a Boson-Fermion (B-F) pair in a B-F environment is considered. The possibility of formation of stable strongly correlated B-F pairs, embedded in the continuum, is pointed out. The new Fermi gas of correlated B-F pairs shows a strongly modified Fermi surface. The interaction between like particles is neglected in this exploratory study. Various physical situations where our new pairing mechanism could be of importance are invoked.

# Boson-Fermion pairing in a Boson-Fermion environment

## Key claim

The authors propose that in a mixture of free fermions and bosons with attractive boson-fermion interaction, stable strongly correlated boson-fermion pairs can form as sharp states embedded in the continuum at positive energies, analogous to Cooper pairs in a pure Fermi gas. This transition occurs for interaction strengths insufficient to form bound boson-fermion molecules in free space, and the resulting gas of composite fermions exhibits a strongly modified Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors study the propagation of a single boson-fermion pair in a homogeneous background gas of free fermions and spinless bosons at zero temperature, using a ladder approximation for the boson-fermion T-matrix. They employ a separable Yukawa-type interaction potential and solve the integral equation for the boson-fermion propagator, analyzing the pole structure of the T-matrix as a function of center-of-mass momentum. The fermion occupation numbers are then calculated via the single-particle spectral function using the quasiparticle approximation, with the self-energy expressed in terms of the T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Fermion density: n_F = 10^14 cm^{-3}; boson density: n_B = 0.004 n_F (for a ⁴⁰K-⁴¹K system with equal masses m = 0.649 × 10^{-22} g)
- The imaginary part of J_0 is zero below 2μ_f = 4.06 μK for P=0 (boson-fermion pair at rest)
- Critical interaction strength for disappearance of the boson-fermion pair shell: λ'_cr = 54.46
- Interaction strength for conversion of practically all original bosons into boson-fermion pairs: λ'_tot ≈ 58.75
- For λ' = 59, molecules appear in the momentum range 0 ≤ P ≲ 0.47 k_F
- Level crossing between collective and ordinary poles occurs at P ≈ k_F/2

## Context

This work extends the classic Cooper problem (Cooper 1956) to the boson-fermion channel, replacing one fermion species with spinless bosons. It builds on theoretical studies of boson-fermion mixtures (Suzuki, Roth, Capuzzi) and is motivated by experiments on ultracold atomic gases (BF1-BF4) and the observed collapse of boson-fermion mixtures (coll). The authors note related work by Kagan treating boson-fermion pairs in the molecular state rather than as collective pairs in the continuum. The approach is equivalent to a particle-particle RPA formulation in the boson-fermion channel, analogous to standard RPA for pure fermion systems. The results enable future studies of boson-fermion pairing in ultracold atomic gases, nuclear systems such as neutron stars with K⁻ mesons and nucleons forming Λ particles (Baldo), and quark-gluon plasma hadronization transitions (Aichelin).
