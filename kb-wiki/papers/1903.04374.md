# Doubly Heavy Baryons Expanded in $1/m_Q$

> Starting from the semirelativistic Hamiltonian for a doubly heavy baryon system ($QQq$) with Coulomb and linear confining scalar potentials, and operating the naive Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation on two heavy quarks, I construct a formulation how to calculate mass spectra and wave functions of doubly heavy baryons. Based on our formulation, their masses and wave functions are expanded in $1/m_Q$ with a heavy quark mass $m_Q$ and the $λ$ mode lowest-order equation is examined carefully to obtain a complete set of eigenfunctions. I claim that the $ρ$ mode wave function should be given by other nonrelativistic methods, e.g., the Godfrey-Isgur model to give a complete wave function for a doubly heavy baryon.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a formulation for calculating mass spectra and wave functions of doubly heavy baryons ($QQq$) by expanding in $1/m_Q$, but the $\rho$ mode wave function cannot be obtained from the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation and must instead be supplied by other nonrelativistic methods such as the Godfrey-Isgur model.

## Method
Starting from a semirelativistic Hamiltonian with Coulomb and linear confining scalar potentials, the author applies the naive Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani (FWT) transformation to the two heavy quarks in a doubly heavy baryon system. Using Jacobi coordinates $\rho$ and $\lambda$ to separate the heavy-heavy and heavy-light relative motions, the Hamiltonian, wave function, and energy eigenvalue are expanded in $1/m_Q$. The lowest-order $\lambda$ mode eigenvalue equation is solved explicitly, yielding angular eigenfunctions expressed in terms of spherical harmonics and a radial eigenvalue equation for the light quark interacting with the heavy diquark.

## Key numbers
- The mixing angle between $(1S1p)1/2^-(j_\lambda=1/2)$ and $(1S1p)1/2^-(j_\lambda=3/2)$ states is $\theta = \arctan(1/\sqrt{2}) = 35.3^\circ$ in the heavy quark limit.
- The $\pi^0$-$\eta$ mixing parameter is $\epsilon = 1.0 \times 10^{-2}$.
- The $K$ meson mass is given as 494-498 MeV for threshold considerations in decay processes.

## Context
This work extends the method previously applied to heavy-light mesons by Matsuki (1997) to doubly heavy baryons, using the same Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation technique. The paper discusses state classification schemes from Lu (2017) and Ebert (2002), and draws analogies to narrow-width $D_s(0^+,1^+)$ mesons to predict threshold behaviors for doubly heavy baryon decays such as $\Omega_{cc}(3/2^+)^+ \to \Omega_{cc}(1/2^-)^+ + \pi^0$ or $\Xi_{cc}(1/2^-)^{++} + K^-$. The formulation enables future calculations of complete doubly heavy baryon wave functions by combining the $\lambda$ mode solution from this work with $\rho$ mode wave functions from other nonrelativistic models.
