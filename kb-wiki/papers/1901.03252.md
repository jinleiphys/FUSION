# Phase Transitions in Neutron Stars

> In this paper we review the most common descriptions for the first order phase transition to deconfined quark matter in the core of neutron stars. We also present a new description of these phase transitions in the core of proto-neutron stars, in which more constraints are enforced so as to include trapped neutrinos. Finally, we calculate the emission of gravitational waves associated with deconfinement phase transitions, discuss the possibility of their detection, and how this would provide information about the equation of state of dense matter.

## Key claim

First-order phase transitions to deconfined quark matter in neutron stars can produce gravitational waves detectable by current and upcoming interferometers, but the amplitude estimates depend strongly on an uncertain efficiency parameter \(\eta\) (taken as \(10^{-4}\) in this work) and on whether the star is a cold neutron star or a hot proto-neutron star with trapped neutrinos.

## Method

The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a relativistic quantum model based on a non-linear realization of the SU(3) sigma model, to describe hadrons and three light quark flavors interacting via meson exchange. They construct equations of state for neutron star and proto-neutron star matter under both local and global conservation of electric charge and lepton fraction, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain stellar mass-radius relations. Finally, they estimate gravitational wave amplitudes and frequencies from the energy released when a metastable purely hadronic star transitions to a more compact hybrid star with the same baryon number, using the fundamental (\(f\)) mode frequency and damping time formulas.

## Key numbers

* For cold neutron stars, the estimated initial gravitational wave amplitude \(h_0\) ranges from \(2.2 \times 10^{-31}\) to \(5.9 \times 10^{-22}\) (scaled by \(\eta/10^{-4}\)), with frequency around \(2.0\) kHz.
* For proto-neutron stars, \(h_0\) ranges from \(8.0 \times 10^{-30}\) to \(2.4 \times 10^{-20}\) (scaled by \(\eta/10^{-4}\)), with frequency around \(1.3\) kHz.
* For proto-neutron stars, 83% of the 2572 cataloged pulsars have estimated initial signals above the LIGO/Virgo sensitivity limit; for cold neutron stars, 25% are above that limit.
* The highest estimated amplitude for a specific pulsar is \(h_0 = 1.9 \times 10^{-21}\).
* The shortest rotation period among selected pulsars is \(P = 1.396\) ms; the closest pulsar to Earth is at \(r = 90\) pc.
* The damping time scale \(\tau\) is proportional to the fourth power of the rotation period \(P\), ranging from milliseconds to years.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phase transitions in neutron stars by Glendenning (1992) and others, who introduced the Gibbs construction for mixed phases under global charge conservation, and the Maxwell construction for local charge neutrality. It also builds on the authors' own earlier work with the CMF model. The paper enables future gravitational wave searches to distinguish between different equations of state and to infer stellar radii, complementing electromagnetic observations. The results are consistent with Abdikamalov (2008), who used simpler equations of state but more sophisticated hydrodynamic simulations including rotation.
