# Analysis of Pion Photoproduction over the Delta Resonance Region

> A number of recent multipole analyses from Mainz, RPI, BNL and Virginia Tech (VPI) have focused on the first resonance region. One goal common to these studies was an improved set of Delta(1232) photo-decay amplitudes. There has been considerable debate over the differing results found for A(1/2), A(3/2) and the E2/M1 ratio. We show that a much more consistent set of values is possible if differences in the database are considered.

## Key claim
By critically examining the database used in multipole analyses of pion photoproduction over the Delta resonance region, the authors show that the previously conflicting values for the Delta(1232) photo-decay amplitudes and the E2/M1 ratio from Mainz, RPI, BNL, and Virginia Tech groups can be brought into much closer agreement, provided that differences in the fitted datasets are properly accounted for.

## Method
The authors performed energy-dependent and energy-independent multipole analyses of pion photoproduction data using a K-matrix approach, fitting to the full SAID database and to restricted subsets. They systematically tested the effect of removing pre-1980 differential cross section data, specifically two sets of Bonn pi0 p differential cross sections from the 1970s, and compared the resulting Delta(1232) photo-decay parameters with those from the Mainz, BNL, and RPI analyses.

## Key numbers
- A1/2 from the full SAID database fit (W500): -135(5) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- A3/2 from the full SAID database fit (W500): -250(8) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- E2/M1 ratio from the full SAID database fit (W500): -1.5(5)%
- E2/M1 ratio from the restricted database fit (F500): -2.6%
- E2/M1 ratio from the fit including BNL Sigma data (B500): -2.5%
- E2/M1 ratio from the Mainz analysis: -2.4%
- A1/2 from the Mainz analysis: -129(2) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- A3/2 from the Mainz analysis: -247(4) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Lattice prediction for A3/2: -195(34) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- BNL value for A3/2: (-268.9 +/- 2.8 +/- 4.9) x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- When the BNL group replaced their cross sections with Bonn cross sections, the E2/M1 ratio became -1.3%

## Context
This work extends the multipole analyses of the Delta(1232) resonance region performed by the Mainz group (Beck, 1990s), the BNL group (1990s), the RPI group (1990s), and the Virginia Tech group (1990s). The paper resolves the apparent disagreement among these analyses by showing that differences in the fitted databases, particularly the inclusion or exclusion of older Bonn pi0 p differential cross sections from the 1970s, are responsible for the spread in extracted E2/M1 ratios and photo-decay amplitudes. The results enable a more definitive comparison with lattice QCD predictions for the Delta(1232) photo-decay amplitudes and highlight the need for new wider-angle measurements of pi0 p photoproduction at Mainz to settle the E2/M1 ratio.
