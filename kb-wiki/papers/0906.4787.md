# Relativistic Viscous Fluid Dynamics and Non-Equilibrium Entropy

> Fluid dynamics corresponds to the dynamics of a substance in the long wavelength limit. Writing down all terms in a gradient (long wavelength) expansion up to second order for a relativistic system at vanishing charge density, one obtains the most general (causal) equations of motion for a fluid in the presence of shear and bulk viscosity, as well as the structure of the non-equilibrium entropy current. Requiring positivity of the divergence of the non-equilibrium entropy current relates some of its coefficients to those entering the equations of motion. I comment on possible applications of these results for conformal and non-conformal fluids.

## Key claim

The paper derives the most general causal equations of motion for a relativistic viscous fluid at vanishing charge density by writing down all terms in a gradient expansion up to second order, but notes that causality and hyperbolicity are only proven for small perturbations around equilibrium, not for strong nonlinear out-of-equilibrium situations.

## Method

The author systematically enumerates all independent gradient structures up to second order for a relativistic fluid at zero charge density, classifying them into scalars, vectors orthogonal to the fluid velocity, and symmetric traceless tensors orthogonal to the fluid velocity. The most general energy-momentum tensor and non-equilibrium entropy current are constructed from these structures, and the divergence of the entropy current is computed and required to be positive. Conditions relating the entropy current coefficients to the transport coefficients in the equations of motion are derived by demanding positivity of the entropy production.

## Key numbers

- The sound attenuation length is given by \(\Gamma = \left(\frac{2}{3} \frac{\eta}{\varepsilon+P} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{\zeta}{\varepsilon+P}\right)\), where \(\eta\) is shear viscosity and \(\zeta\) is bulk viscosity
- The high-wavenumber propagation speeds are \(\lim_{k\rightarrow \infty} \frac{d\,\omega_T(k)}{d\,k} = \sqrt{\frac{\eta}{\tau_\pi(\varepsilon+P)}}\) for the shear mode and \(\lim_{k\rightarrow \infty} \frac{d\,\omega_L(k)}{d\,k} = \sqrt{c_s^2 + \frac{4}{3}\frac{\eta}{\tau_\pi(\varepsilon+P)} + \frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi(\varepsilon+P)}}\) for the sound mode
- For conformal fluids, positivity of the entropy current divergence fixes three of five possible coefficients: \(B_1 = 2A_3\), \(A_3 = \kappa/(2T)\), and \(A_2 = -(2\kappa + \lambda_3)/(4T)\)
- For non-conformal fluids, eight of thirteen coefficient functions in the entropy current are fixed by positivity conditions, including \(A_5 = 0\), \(B_1 = 2A_3\), and \(B_3 = 2(1-3c_s^2)A_3\)

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Muronga (2003), Koide (2006), Baier (2007), and Betz (2008) to determine the precise form of relativistic viscous fluid equations, motivated by nuclear physics experiments on relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The paper builds on the causal second-order theories of Muller, Israel, and Stewart, and recovers and extends results from Bhattacharyya (2008) on the non-equilibrium entropy current. The results enable calculation of second-order transport coefficients via Kubo formulas from thermal correlators, and provide the framework for causal numerical simulations of relativistic viscous fluids in heavy-ion collisions.
