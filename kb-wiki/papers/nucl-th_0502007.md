# Search for possible way of producing super-heavy elements-Dynamic study on damped reactions of 244Pu+244Pu,238$U+238$U and 197Au+197Au

> By using the improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, the $^{244}$Pu+$^{244}$Pu, $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au reactions at the energy range of $E_{c.m.}$=800 $MeV$ to 2000 $MeV$ are studied. We find that the production probability of superheavy fragments(SHF) with $Z \geq$ 114 is much higher for $^{244}$Pu+$^{244}$Pu reaction compared with that of $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U reaction and no product of SHF is found for $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au. The production probability of SHF is narrowly peaked in incident energy dependence. The decay mechanism of the composite system of projectile and target and the time scale of decay process are explored. The binding energies of superheavy fragments are found to be broadly distributed and their shapes turn out to be exotic form.

## Key claim

The production probability of superheavy fragments (SHF) with Z >= 114 in the 244Pu+244Pu reaction is much higher than in the 238U+238U reaction, and no SHF are found for 197Au+197Au, but this result is obtained from a primary calculation using the ImQMD model with a specific parameter set (IQ2) and the precise location of the peak energy "may not be very definite."

## Method

The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model with a newly developed parameter set IQ2 to simulate central and semi-central collisions (impact parameters 1 fm and 3 fm) of 244Pu+244Pu, 238U+238U, and 197Au+197Au at center-of-mass energies from 800 MeV to 2000 MeV. For each energy and impact parameter, 500 simulation events were run, continuing until t=6000 fm/c if a superheavy fragment existed, or terminating earlier if none was found. The model was first validated by comparing calculated capture cross sections for 16O+208Pb and 48Ca+208Pb and charge distributions for 197Au+197Au at 35 AMeV against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 244Pu+244Pu, the peak production probability of SHF with Z >= 114 occurs at approximately E_cm = 1000 MeV; for 238U+238U, the peak is at approximately E_cm = 950 MeV.
- The yield of SHF in 238U+238U is only half of that in 244Pu+244Pu at impact parameter b=1 fm.
- No reaction event forming a product with Z >= 114 was found for 197Au+197Au in the present calculations.
- The binding energies of SHF reach about 7 MeV/nucleon on the large binding energy side.
- At t=1000 fm/c, more than 60 percent of events still have the two reaction partners sticking together; at 1200 fm/c, about 10-15 percent remain in this stage.
- The upper limit cross-section for producing superheavy elements from 238U+238U at E=7.5 MeV/nucleon was previously reported as about 2x10^{-32} cm^2 (Hildenbrand 1977).

## Context

This work extends the earlier experimental and theoretical studies of strongly damped collisions between massive nuclei, such as 238U+238U and 238U+248Cm, which were investigated in the 1970s and early 1980s by Herrmann (1979), Seaborg (1979), and others for searching superheavy nuclei. The authors note that the energies used in those earlier experiments (Hildenbrand 1977, Gaggeler 1980, Schadel 1978) were lower than the peak energy identified here for 238U+238U, suggesting that those experiments may have missed optimal conditions. The study enables future experimental searches for superheavy elements via damped reactions by identifying the narrow energy window needed and the advantage of using 244Pu over 238U, and it raises open questions about the structure and fission barriers of the exotic, strongly deformed SHF shapes (bubble-like and band-like) that were observed.
