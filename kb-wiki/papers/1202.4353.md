# Non-relativistic spectrum of two-color QCD at non-zero baryon density

> The heavy quarkonium spectrum of Two Color QCD (QC$_2$D) at non-zero quark chemical potential $μ$ and temperature $T$ with $μ/T\gg1$ has been calculated in both $S$- and $P$-wave channels using a lattice non-relativistic formulation of QC$_2$D. As $μ$ is varied, the quarkonium spectra reveal three separate regions, corroborating previous findings that there are three distinct physical regimes of QC$_2$D at low temperature and high baryon density: hadronic matter, quark/quarkyonic matter, and deconfined matter. The results are interpreted in terms of the formation of heavy-light $Qq$ states in the two-color baryonic medium.

## Key claim

The heavy quarkonium spectrum of two-color QCD at non-zero quark chemical potential and temperature with \(\mu/T\gg1\) reveals three distinct regimes as \(\mu\) is varied, corroborating previous findings of hadronic matter, quark/quarkyonic matter, and deconfined matter; however, the interpretation in terms of heavy-light \(Qq\) state formation is acknowledged by the authors as speculative and potentially over-elaborate.

## Method

The authors used a lattice non-relativistic formulation of two-color QCD (NRQC\(_2\)D) at \({\cal O}(v^4)\) to compute heavy quarkonium correlators for \(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\), \(^1P_0\), and \(^3P_1\) states. Background gauge field configurations were generated on \(16^3\times12\), \(12^3\times16\), and \(12^3\times24\) lattices at \(\beta=1.9\), \(\kappa=0.168\) with two dynamical flavors of Wilson quark, using a standard Hybrid Monte-Carlo algorithm with a diquark source term of magnitude \(j=0.02\) or \(0.04\). The heavy quark mass was chosen as \(Ma\ge3\) with \(n=1\) in the evolution equation, and Coulomb gauge fixing was applied for \(P\)-wave states.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.186(8)\) fm (\(= 1/1.060(45)\) GeV\(^{-1}\))
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi a = 0.68(1)\), with \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.80(1)\)
* Temperatures studied: \(T = 44, 66, 88\) MeV (corresponding to \(Ta = 1/24, 1/16, 1/12\))
* Chemical potential range: \(0 \le \mu a \le 1.1\)
* Transition chemical potentials (from prior work): \(\mu_o \simeq 360\) MeV, \(\mu_Q \simeq 530\) MeV, \(\mu_D \simeq 850\) MeV
* At \(\mu = 0.8a^{-1} \approx 850\) MeV, quark density \(n_q = 16\)–\(32\) fm\(^{-3}\) (35–70 times matter density)
* Downward energy shift for \(S\)-wave states: \(\Delta E \sim 40\) MeV for \(Ma=3.0\) in the plateau region \(\mu_1 < \mu < \mu_2\)
* At \(\mu a = 0.6\), \(\Delta E a = 0.4157(6), 0.4226(2), 0.4235(2)\) as \(Ta\) rises from \(1/24\) to \(1/12\)
* Deconfinement transition chemical potentials: \(\mu_D(T)a \approx 0.3, 0.55, 0.75\) for \(Ta = 1/12, 1/16, 1/24\) respectively

## Context

This work extends prior lattice studies of two-color QCD at non-zero baryon density by Hands, Kim, and Skullerud (2006, 2010, 2011), which identified three distinct regimes (BEC, BCS/quarkyonic, and deconfined) using thermodynamic observables. The present study uses heavy quarkonium as a probe, analogous to the use of quarkonium melting as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation in QCD (Matsui and Satz, 1986) and recent lattice studies of bottomonium at non-zero temperature (Aarts et al., 2010, 2011). The results enable future investigation of how heavy quark bound states behave in dense baryonic matter, potentially informing the phenomenology of compact stars and heavy-ion collisions, though the authors caution that their interpretation remains speculative.
