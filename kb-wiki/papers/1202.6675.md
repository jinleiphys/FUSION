# Photodisintegration of a Bound State on the Torus

> In this article the cross-section for the photodisintegration of a bound state is expressed, order by order in the multipole expansion, in terms of matrix elements between states living on the three-dimensional torus. The motivation is to make the process amenable to Monte-Carlo simulations. The case of the deuteron is discussed.

## Key claim

The cross-section for photodisintegration of a bound state can be expressed, order by order in the multipole expansion, in terms of matrix elements between states on the three-dimensional torus, making the process amenable to Monte-Carlo simulations; however, the naive correspondence between finite-volume and infinite-volume states must be examined individually for each process, and the derivation assumes a short-range potential that vanishes beyond a radius R < L/2.

## Method

The author considers a non-relativistic two-particle quantum mechanics model on a torus of side L, coupled perturbatively to an electromagnetic field. Using Luscher's relation between the discrete two-particle spectrum on the torus and infinite-volume scattering phases, the relative normalization between finite-volume wavefunctions (unit-normalized) and infinite-volume energy-normalized wavefunctions is derived for the lowest partial wave in a given cubic representation. This normalization factor, expressed in terms of the function F_ell(k,L), is then used to relate the finite-volume matrix element of the electromagnetic current to the infinite-volume photodisintegration cross-section in the dipole approximation and beyond.

## Key numbers

- The relation between finite- and infinite-volume p-wave wavefunctions is given by |psi_{1,m}(r)|^2 = (dk/dE) * (F_1(k,L)/(pi*k)) * |Psi_{1,m}(r)|^2 for r < L/2, where F_1(k,L) = k * d(delta_1)/dk + q*phi'(q) and q = kL/(2*pi).
- The dipole-approximation photodisintegration cross-section is sigma_{ell=1} = 4*pi^2*mu^2 * (e_b/m_b - e_a/m_a)^2 * omega_gamma * |epsilon_sigma * r_fi|^2, where mu is the reduced mass.
- The finite-volume matrix element in the dipole approximation relates to the infinite-volume one via |epsilon_sigma * r_fi|^2 = (dk/dE) * (F_1(k,L)/(pi*k)) * |epsilon_sigma * R_fi|^2, assuming equal masses.

## Context

This work extends Luscher's formalism (Luscher, 1986, 1990, 1991) for relating finite-volume spectra to scattering phases to the case of photodisintegration processes, providing a method to extract transition matrix elements from stationary observables on the torus. It contrasts with the approach of Detmold and Savage (2004), who used pionless effective field theory to determine low-energy constants for deuteron photodisintegration from lattice QCD, by instead offering a direct computation of low-multipole matrix elements at the cost of more stringent volume requirements. The method enables future lattice QCD calculations of photodisintegration cross-sections for systems such as the deuteron, and can be generalized to extract multipole matrix elements in several low-lying partial waves by measuring finite-volume matrix elements at multiple box sizes.
