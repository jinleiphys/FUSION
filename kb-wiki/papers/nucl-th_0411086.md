# Tensor correlation in 4He and its Effect on the doublet splitting in 5He

> We investigate the role of tensor correlation on the structures of \nuc{4}{He} and its effect on the doublet splitting in \nuc{5}{He}. We perform a configuration mixing calculation in the shell model type bases to represent the tensor correlation for $^4$He. It is found that our model describes the characteristics of the tensor correlation, which is represented by an admixture of the $0s_{1/2}$ configuration with a spatially modified $0p_{1/2}$ orbit. For $^5$He, we solve a coupled OCM equation for an extended $^4$He+$n$ model, while taking into account the tensor correlation in the $^4$He cluster. It is shown that the tensor correlation produces the Pauli blocking, in particular, for the $J^π={1/2}^-$ state, and its effect causes about half of the p-wave doublet splitting in $^5$He. This indicates that the strength of the effective spin-orbit interaction should be reduced by about half from the conventional one. We obtain a reliable $^4$He-$n$ interaction, including the tensor correlation, which further improves the behavior of the $d$- and $f$-wave phase shifts in the $^4$He+$n$ system.

## Key claim

The tensor correlation in 4He, represented by an admixture of the (0s1/2)4 configuration with a spatially modified (0p1/2)2 component, produces Pauli blocking that affects the 1/2- state of 5He more strongly than the 3/2- state, causing about half of the observed p-wave doublet splitting in 5He. This indicates that the strength of the effective spin-orbit interaction should be reduced by about half from the conventional value.

## Method

The authors performed a configuration mixing calculation for 4He using shell-model-type bases with harmonic oscillator wave functions, mixing the (0s)4 configuration with (0s)2(0p)2 configurations up to 2p-2h excitations, treating the length parameters b0s, b0p1/2, and b0p3/2 as variational parameters. For 5He, they solved a coupled orthogonality condition model (OCM) equation for an extended 4He+n model, taking into account the tensor correlation in the 4He cluster through the configuration mixing and treating the Pauli blocking between the valence neutron and the 0p orbital neutrons in the 4He cluster.

## Key numbers

- The 2p-2h component probability in 4He is 7.24% with the original tensor force strength and 16.81% when the tensor force matrix element is strengthened by 50%
- The D-state probability in 4He is 4.58% with the original tensor force and 10.87% with the strengthened tensor force
- The expectation value of the tensor force in 4He is -22.62 MeV with the original strength and -60.65 MeV with the strengthened tensor force
- The optimum variational length parameters for 4He with the original tensor force are (b0s, b0p) = (1.26, 0.77) fm, and with the strengthened tensor force are (1.14, 0.73) fm
- The matter radius of 4He is 1.34 fm with the original tensor force and 1.22 fm with the strengthened tensor force
- For the AK interaction, the 4He binding energy is -19.3 MeV with (b0s, b0p) = (1.12, 0.65) fm and a D-state probability of 6.3%
- For the GPT interaction, the 4He binding energy is -17.5 MeV with (b0s, b0p) = (1.36, 0.93) fm and a D-state probability of 5.1%
- The coupling matrix elements of the tensor force between the (0s)4 configuration and the (0p1/2)2 configuration are strongly enhanced for b0p/b0s approximately 0.4

## Context

This work extends the charge-parity projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) approach of Toki, Sugimoto, and Ikeda (2004) and the earlier configuration mixing calculation by Terasawa (1960), who found that internal excitation of 4He causes Pauli blocking in 5He producing orbital-state dependence. Nagata et al. (1959) also reproduced almost all of the doublet splitting using the tensor force of a pion theoretical one through coupled resonating group method (RGM) analysis. The present work clarifies that the (0p1/2)2 excitation in 4He is related to the pion-like 0- coupling nature of the tensor correlation, which was not clarified in previous studies, and provides a new 4He-n interaction including tensor correlation for studying d- and f-wave phase shifts in 4He+n scattering.
