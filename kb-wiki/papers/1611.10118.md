# More about the light baryon spectrum

> We discuss the light baryon spectrum obtained from a recent quark-diquark calculation, implementing non-pointlike diquarks that are self-consistently calculated from their Bethe-Salpeter equations. We examine the orbital angular momentum content in the baryons' rest frame and highlight the fact that baryons carry all possible values of L compatible with their spin, without the restriction P=(-1)^L which is only valid nonrelativistically. We furthermore investigate the meaning of complex conjugate eigenvalues of Bethe-Salpeter equations, their possible connection with 'anomalous' states, and we propose a method to eliminate them from the spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the light baryon spectrum obtained from a quark-diquark approach, using self-consistently calculated non-pointlike diquarks, agrees well with experimental data, but the authors qualify that the rainbow-ladder truncation performs well only for the N(1/2+) and Delta(3/2+) channels, while the remaining channels come out too low unless the strength in "bad" diquark channels is reduced.

## Method

The authors solved the covariant three-body Faddeev equation and its quark-diquark simplification using quarks and diquarks calculated from their Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations with full covariant tensor decomposition, employing a common rainbow-ladder interaction that models the qq and q-qbar kernel by an effective gluon exchange. They quantified the orbital angular momentum content of baryons from the contributions to the Bethe-Salpeter normalization in the baryon's rest frame, and investigated complex conjugate eigenvalues of Bethe-Salpeter equations by analyzing the spectral decomposition of the propagator matrix G and removing its negative eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying states in the N(1/2+), Delta(3/2+), and Delta(1/2+) channels carry sizeable L=0 components
- The N(1/2-), N(3/2-), Delta(3/2-), Delta(1/2-) channels are typically dominated by L=1
- The N(3/2+) channel is dominated by L=2
- For the Wick-Cutkosky model with equal constituent masses m1=m2=m and mu=0, the eigenvalues diverge at M=2m and the inverse eigenvalues vanish, except for anomalous states that do not
- In the Wick-Cutkosky model with unequal masses (m1/m2=4) and nonvanishing exchange mass (mu=m2), complex conjugate eigenvalue pairs split into two real branches, with the smaller eigenvalue bending to zero as M approaches 2m

## Context

This work extends the quark-diquark calculation of Eichmann (2016) by examining the orbital angular momentum content of baryons and investigating the meaning of complex conjugate eigenvalues in Bethe-Salpeter equations. The paper builds on the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) and earlier work on anomalous states by Ahlig (1998), showing that complex conjugate eigenvalues can be eliminated by removing negative eigenvalues of the propagator matrix G. The results enable future beyond-rainbow-ladder three-body calculations and clarify the interpretation of spurious states in the baryon spectrum.
