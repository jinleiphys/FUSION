> 3 pages, 1 figure; Talk given at Baryons'10, International Conference on Structure of Baryons, Dec. 7 - 11, 2010, Osaka, Japan

# Tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers - their production and related

## Key claim
Tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers are expected to exist based on the favored tetra-quark interpretation of \(D_{s0}^+(2317)\) and \(X(3872)\), and their production rates in \(B\) decays could be large enough to be observed, although the calculations are crude and model-dependent because no experimental input data for these exotic states is yet available.

## Method
The author classifies tetra-quark mesons into four groups based on flavor symmetry and antisymmetry, with color configurations of \(\mathbf{\bar 3}_c \times \mathbf{3}_c\) and \(\mathbf{6}_c \times \mathbf{\bar 6}_c\). Production rates for scalar and axial-vector tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers (such as \(C=2\), \(S=0\) or \(C=1\), \(S=-1\)) are estimated by comparing their quark-line diagrams to those of the known \(D_{s0}^+(2317)\) production in \(B\) decays, using the minimal \(q\bar q\) pair creation framework and crude quark counting for mass estimates.

## Key numbers
- Mass of \(\hat E^0\) (scalar meson with \(C = -S = +1\)): approximately 2.32 GeV, estimated using quark counting with \(m_c - m_s \simeq 1.0\) GeV and \(m_s - m_n \simeq 0.1\) GeV, taking \(m_{D_{s0}(2317)} \simeq 2317\) MeV and \(m_{X(3872)} \simeq 3872\) MeV as input.
- Mass of \(H_{Acc}\) (axial-vector meson with \(C=2\), \(S=0\), \(I=0\)): approximately 3.87 GeV.
- Mass of \(K_{Acc}\) (axial-vector meson with \(C=2\), \(S=1\), \(I=1/2\)): approximately 3.97 GeV.
- Masses of \(E_{A(cs)}\) and \(E_{A[cs]}\) (axial-vector mesons with \(C=1\), \(S=-1\)): approximately 2.97 GeV each.
- Predicted branching fractions for \(B_c^+ \rightarrow D^{*-} K_{Acc}^{++}\) and \(B_c^+ \rightarrow \bar D^{*0} K_{Acc}^+\): approximately \(10^{-(4 - 3)}\).
- Predicted branching fractions for \(B_u^- \rightarrow D^{*-} E^0_{A(cs)}\), \(B_u^- \rightarrow D^{*-} E^0_{A[cs]}\), \(B_u^- \rightarrow D^- \hat E^0\), and analogous \(\bar B_d^0\) decays: approximately \(10^{-(4-3)}\), about 10 times larger than \(Br(\bar B_d^0 \rightarrow K^- D_{s0}^+(2317))\).
- Suppression factor for \(H_{Acc}^+\) production relative to \(K_{Acc}\) production: approximately \(|V_{cd}/V_{cs}|^2 \simeq 0.05\).
- Measured branching fraction \(Br(B \rightarrow \bar D \tilde D_{s0}^+(2317)[D_s^+ \pi^0]) = (8.5^{+2.1}_{-1.9} \pm 2.6) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Measured branching fraction \(Br(B \rightarrow \bar D \tilde D_{s0}^+(2317)[D_s^{*+} \gamma]) = (2.5^{+2.0}_{-1.8} (< 7.5)) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Ratio \(R^\gamma \equiv Br(X(3872) \rightarrow \gamma J/\psi) / Br(X(3872) \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- J/\psi)\): \(0.14 \pm 0.05\) (Belle) and \(0.33 \pm 0.12\) (Babar).

## Context
This work extends the tetra-quark interpretation of \(D_{s0}^+(2317)\) and \(X(3872)\) (previously studied by Jaffe (1977), Terasaki (2003), and others) to predict the existence and production of mesons with exotic quantum numbers, such as \(C=2\) or \(C=-S=1\). The author argues that the tetra-quark model is favored over the charmonium interpretation for \(X(3872)\) and that the compact tetra-quark picture survives while the \(D^0 \bar D^{*0}\) molecular model (Toernquist) would be ruled out by prompt production data. The predicted masses for hidden-charm iso-triplet scalar mesons using the same quark counting method previously matched a peak at 3.2 GeV observed by Belle (2009), providing a consistency check. The work enables experimental searches for these exotic mesons in \(B\) decays, though detailed decay rate calculations are left as a future subject due to sensitivity to unknown masses near thresholds.
