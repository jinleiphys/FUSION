# Two-pion interferometry for the granular sources in the heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> We investigate the two-pion interferometry in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions in the granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets. The pion transverse momentum spectra and HBT radii of the granular sources agree well with the experimental data of the most central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at LHC. In the granular source model the larger initial system breakup time for the LHC collisions as compared to the RHIC collisions may lead to the larger HBT radii $R_{\rm out}$, $R_{\rm side}$, and $R_{\rm long}$. However, the large droplet transverse expansion and limited average relative emitting time of particles in the granular source lead to the ratio of the transverse HBT radii $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \sim 1$.

## Key claim

The granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets can consistently reproduce the pion transverse momentum spectra and HBT radii for both RHIC Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and LHC Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, but only if the initial system breakup time is taken to be larger for the LHC collisions (8.0 fm/c) than for the RHIC collisions (4.3 fm/c).

## Method

The authors model the particle-emitting source as a collection of many QGP droplets that form at a breakup time \(t_0\) after the initial thermalization time \(\tau_0\). Each droplet evolves hydrodynamically using two different equations of state (EOS1, a cross-over QGP-to-hadronic gas, and EOS2, S95p-PCE), and pions are emitted over a range of temperatures with separate direct and decay components. The two-pion correlation functions are computed in the longitudinal comoving system and fitted with a Gaussian form to extract the HBT radii \(R_{\rm out}\), \(R_{\rm side}\), and \(R_{\rm long}\) as functions of the transverse pair momentum \(k_T\).

## Key numbers

- For the RHIC granular source (Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV): thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.8\) fm/c, breakup time \(t_0 = 4.3\) fm/c, source transverse radius \({\cal R}_{\perp} = 6.5\) fm (EOS1) or 6.8 fm (EOS2), shell parameter \(\Delta{\cal R}_{\perp} = 3.5\) fm.
- For the LHC granular source (Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV): thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c, breakup time \(t_0 = 8.0\) fm/c, source transverse radius \({\cal R}_{\perp} = 7.8\) fm (EOS1) or 8.0 fm (EOS2), shell parameter \(\Delta{\cal R}_{\perp} = 5.4\) fm.
- Droplet transverse velocity parameters: \(a_T = 0.65\) (EOS1) or 0.68 (EOS2) for RHIC, and \(a_T = 0.65\) for both EOSs for LHC; \(b_T = 1.40\) for both energies and EOSs.
- Initial droplet radius standard deviation \(\sigma_d = 2.5\) fm; initial droplet temperature \(T_0 = 200\) MeV; transition temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV; direct emission fraction \(f_{\rm dir} = 0.85\); direct emission width \(\Delta T_{\rm dir} = 10\) MeV; decay emission width \(\Delta T_{\rm dec} = 90\) MeV.
- The ratio of transverse HBT radii \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \sim 1\) for both energies.

## Context

This work extends the granular source model previously developed by Zhang (2006, 2009) to explain RHIC HBT data from STAR (2005) and PHENIX (2004), and applies it for the first time to LHC Pb-Pb collisions using ALICE data (2011). The model addresses the long-standing "HBT puzzle" by showing that a fragmented source with limited relative emission time can produce \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} \sim 1\), contrary to simple hydrodynamic models. The results enable further studies of the relationship between source radii and emission time, and comparisons with other models that explain source functions for RHIC Au-Au collisions (PHENIX, 2008).
