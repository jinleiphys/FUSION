# LPM effect as the origin of the jet fragmentation scaling in heavy ion collisions

> We address a recent puzzling result from the LHC: the jet fragmentation functions measured in $PbPb$ and $pp$ collisions appear very similar in spite of a large medium-induced energy loss (we will call this "jet fragmentation scaling", JFS). To model the real-time non-perturbative effects in the propagation of a high energy jet through the strongly coupled QCD matter, we adopt an effective dimensionally reduced description in terms of the $(1+1)$ quasi-Abelian Schwinger theory. This theory is exactly soluble at any value of the coupling and shares with QCD the properties of dynamical generation of "mesons" with a finite mass and the screening of "quark" charge that are crucial for describing the transition of the jet into hadrons. We find that this approach describes quite well the vacuum jet fragmentation in $e^+e^-$ annihilation at $z\geq0.2$ at jet energies in the range of the LHC heavy ion measurements ($z$ is the ratio of hadron and jet momenta). In QCD medium, we find that the JFS is reproduced if the mean free path $λ$ of the jet is short, $λ\leq 0.3$ fm, which is in accord with the small shear viscosity inferred from the measurements of the collective flow. The JFS holds since at short mean free path the quantum interference (analogous to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect in QED) causes the produced mesons to have low momenta $p \sim m$, where $m \simeq 0.6$ GeV is the typical meson mass. Meanwhile the induced jet energy loss at short mean free path is much larger than naively expected in string models.

# LPM effect as the origin of the jet fragmentation scaling in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed similarity between jet fragmentation functions in PbPb and pp collisions at LHC (jet fragmentation scaling, JFS) can be reproduced if the mean free path of the jet in the medium is short (λ ≤ 0.3 fm), because the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect at strong coupling causes the produced mesons to have low momenta p ∼ m, even though the induced jet energy loss remains large. The authors emphasize that their treatment is model-dependent and can be questioned, but argue the LPM mechanism may be generic.

## Method

The authors adopt an effective (1+1)-dimensional quasi-Abelian Schwinger model, which is exactly soluble at any coupling and shares with QCD the properties of dynamical meson generation and charge screening. They compute the momentum distribution of produced mesons from a quark-antiquark source moving through the medium, modeling color exchange with the medium as static color sources at coordinates z_i. The hadron spectrum is obtained by summing over color sectors (suppressing inter-sector interference at large N_c), and the ratio of in-medium to vacuum fragmentation functions is evaluated for a jet energy of 100 GeV with multiple scatterings spaced by the mean free path.

## Key numbers

- Meson mass m = 0.6 GeV, obtained from fitting the model to e+e- → hadrons data at √s = 201.7 GeV (Abbiendi 2002).
- Matching scale Q0 = 2 GeV at which DGLAP evolution is matched onto the model.
- Mean free path threshold for JFS: λ ≤ 0.3 fm (equivalently λ ≤ m⁻¹).
- Medium length fixed at 4 fm for the fragmentation ratio calculation.
- Jet energy used for the fragmentation ratio calculation: E_jet = 100 GeV.
- Validity condition for the energy loss calculation: δE ≪ E_jet, requiring E_jet ≥ 100 GeV for λ ≤ 0.3 fm.
- The vacuum fragmentation spectrum at z ≥ 0.15 is reproduced reasonably well; the model falls below data at z ≤ 0.1.

## Context

This work addresses the puzzling CMS result (2011) that jet fragmentation functions in PbPb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair appear similar to pp collisions despite large medium-induced energy loss observed in di-jet imbalance (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011) and high-pT hadron suppression (Aamodt 2010). The approach extends earlier use of the Schwinger model for e+e- annihilation (1974) and for hadronization (Fujita 1989, Wong 1991), and incorporates the LPM effect (Landau 1953, Migdal 1956) at strong coupling, which is traditionally treated perturbatively (Baier 1996, Gyulassy 2000). The result enables a non-perturbative description of jet fragmentation in strongly coupled QCD matter consistent with the small shear viscosity inferred from collective flow measurements.
