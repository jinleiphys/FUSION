# Meson Photo- and Electroproduction

> Photo- and electroproduction of pions and eta-mesons is studied on the nucleon. The formalism of such reactions is presented including polarization degrees of freedom and theoretical approaches are discussed. In particular, the threshold and $Δ$-resonance region of pion photoproduction is considered and recent developments on low energy theorems (LET) and the E2/C2 quadrupole excitation of the $Δ$ are presented. For eta photoproduction we show our calculations for the threshold region of the new Mainz experiment and demonstrate the sensitivity of this reaction to the elementary $ηNN$ coupling and the resonance parameters of the $S_{11}(1535)$. In nuclear applications we point out the importance and advantage of light nuclei as $d$, $^3H$, $^3He$ and $^4He$ for the isospin structure of the reactions as well as for medium effects of the nucleon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper presents a comprehensive formalism for meson photo- and electroproduction on nucleons and light nuclei, arguing that while low-energy theorems (LET) for threshold pion photoproduction are now in agreement with experimental data after careful analysis, a strong energy variation of the s-wave amplitude over a range of 10 MeV above threshold remains unexplained and requires further experimental clarification.

## Method

The author develops the general formalism for coincidence cross sections of (e,e'π) and (e,e'η) reactions, including polarization degrees of freedom for the photon, target, and recoil nucleon, expressed in terms of response functions and CGLN amplitudes with multipole decomposition. Theoretical approaches are compared, including effective chiral Lagrangians with pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling, dispersion theoretical calculations, and dynamical coupled-channels models that satisfy unitarity by construction. For eta photoproduction, calculations are performed for the threshold region of the new Mainz experiment to study sensitivity to the ηNN coupling and S11(1535) resonance parameters.

## Context

This work extends the foundational formalism of Chew et al (1957) (CGLN amplitudes) and the low-energy theorems of Kroll and Ruderman (1954) and Fubini et al (1965), while addressing discrepancies between LET predictions and experimental data from Saclay (Maz86) and Mainz (Bec90) that were later resolved. The paper enables future experimental programs at modern electron accelerators with high duty factor, particularly the new Mainz experiment for eta photoproduction threshold studies and out-of-plane experiments for complete separation of structure functions, building on the dynamical model of Nozawa, Blankleider and Lee (1990) and the unitarized operator of Davidson et al (1991).
