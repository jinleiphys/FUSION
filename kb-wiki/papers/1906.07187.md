# Jet fragmentation functions for $Z$-tagged jets

> Recently the LHCb collaboration has measured both longitudinal and transverse momentum distribution of hadrons produced inside $Z$-tagged jets in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. These distributions are commonly referred to as jet fragmentation functions and are characterized by the longitudinal momentum fraction $z_h$ of the jet carried by the hadron and the transverse momentum $j_\perp$ with respect to the jet direction. We derive a QCD formalism within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory to describe these distributions and find that the $z_h$-dependence provides information on standard collinear fragmentation functions, while $j_\perp$-dependence probes transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions. We perform theoretical calculations and compare our results with the LHCb data. We find good agreement for the intermediate $z_h$ region. For $j_\perp$-dependence, we suggest binning in both $z_h$ and $j_\perp$, which would lead to a more direct probing of TMD fragmentation functions.

# Jet fragmentation functions for $Z$-tagged jets

## Key claim
The longitudinal momentum fraction ($z_h$) distribution of hadrons inside $Z$-tagged jets provides information on standard collinear fragmentation functions, while the transverse momentum ($j_\perp$) distribution probes transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions; however, the theoretical description agrees well with LHCb data only for the intermediate $z_h$ region (approximately $0.1 \lesssim z_h \lesssim 0.5$), and for the $j_\perp$-dependence a direct comparison with data is hindered because the experimental data are integrated over the entire $z_h$ range.

## Method
The authors derive a QCD factorization formalism within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for back-to-back $Z$-jet production, including resummation of large logarithms of the form $\alpha_s^n \ln^{2n}(p_T/q_T)$ and logarithms of the jet radius $\ln R$. They compute the differential cross section for hadron production inside $Z$-tagged jets using fragmenting jet functions matched onto standard collinear fragmentation functions for the $z_h$ distribution, and TMD fragmenting jet functions refactorized into TMD fragmentation functions and collinear-soft functions for the $j_\perp$ distribution. Numerical calculations are performed at next-to-leading order for the hard functions and at next-to-leading logarithmic level for the Sudakov factors, using NLO DSS fragmentation functions for charged hadrons.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: $\sqrt{s} = 8$ TeV for LHCb proton-proton collisions.
- Jet rapidity range: $2.5 < \eta_J < 4.0$; $Z$-boson rapidity range: $2.0 < \eta_Z < 4.5$.
- Jet radius parameter: $R = 0.5$ (anti-$k_T$ algorithm).
- Jet transverse momentum bins: $20 < p_{JT} < 30$ GeV, $30 < p_{JT} < 50$ GeV, and $50 < p_{JT} < 100$ GeV.
- The natural scale of the jet function is $\mu_J \sim p_{JT}R$.
- The default hard scale is $\mu = \sqrt{p_T^2 + m_Z^2}$.
- The quark jet function at NLO: $J_q(p_{JT}R, \mu) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} C_F \left(L^2 - \frac{3}{2}L + \frac{13}{4} - \frac{3\pi^2}{8} \right)$, where $L = \ln(p_{JT}R/\mu)$.
- Leading-order cusp anomalous dimensions: $\Gamma_0^q = 4C_F$, $\Gamma_0^g = 4C_A$; non-cusp: $\gamma_0^q = 6C_F$, $\gamma_0^g = 2\beta_0$ with $\beta_0 = \frac{11}{3}C_A - \frac{4}{3}T_F n_f$.

## Context
This work extends the SCET-based factorized framework previously developed for back-to-back photon-jet production by Buffing, Kang, Lee, and Xing (2018) to the case of $Z$-tagged jets, and generalizes it to include jet fragmentation functions. The formalism is related to the study of non-global logarithms by Chien, et al. (2019). The results enable future measurements of TMD fragmentation functions in vector-boson-tagged jet production in both proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the authors suggest that experimental binning in both $z_h$ and $j_\perp$ would allow more direct probing of TMD fragmentation functions.
