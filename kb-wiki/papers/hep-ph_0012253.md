# Subleading corrections to parity violating pion photoproduction

## Key claim

The photon asymmetry $B_\gamma$ for near-threshold parity violating (PV) pion photoproduction receives subleading chiral contributions that involve a new combination of PV couplings not included in previous analyses; if the leading PV coupling $h_\pi^1$ is suppressed relative to its "natural" size (as suggested by the $^{18}$F PV $\gamma$-decay measurement), then the subleading contributions may be comparable in magnitude to the leading term, contrary to the expectation from naturalness arguments that they would be only ~10% corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the photon asymmetry $B_\gamma$ for the reaction $\vec{\gamma} + p \rightarrow \pi^+ + n$ through ${\cal O}(p)$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT), including both the leading ${\cal O}(p^0)$ and subleading ${\cal O}(p)$ contributions. The subleading contributions arise from recoil corrections to the leading PV and parity-conserving amplitudes, as well as from new PV low-energy constants (LECs), specifically the combination $\bar C$ involving the PV $NN\pi\gamma$ contact interaction and the PV vector $\pi NN$ couplings $h_V^i$, which are shown to be distinct LECs that cannot be entirely eliminated by field redefinition at $3\pi$ order. Chiral loops and $\Delta(1232)$ intermediate states contribute only at ${\cal O}(p^2)$ and beyond, so the truncation at ${\cal O}(p)$ is complete.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order low-energy theorem for $B_\gamma$ at threshold is $B_\gamma(\omega_{\rm th},\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{2}F_\pi(\mu_p-\mu_n)}{g_A m_N} h_\pi^1$, where $g_\pi = 3.8 \times 10^{-8}$ sets the scale of the PV $\pi NN$ coupling in the absence of weak neutral currents.
- The chirally corrected low-energy theorem including subleading ${\cal O}(p)$ contributions is $B_\gamma(\omega_{\rm th},\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{2}F_\pi}{g_A m_N}[\mu_p-\mu_n(1+\frac{m_\pi}{m_N})]h_\pi^1 - \frac{4\sqrt{2}m_\pi}{g_A \Lambda_\chi}\bar C$, where $\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_\pi \sim 1$ GeV.
- The $^{18}$F PV $\gamma$-decay measurement gives $h_\pi^{\rm eff} = (0.73 \pm 2.3) g_\pi$, which is suppressed relative to the "natural" expectation of $\sim 10 g_\pi$ from the DDH SU(6)/quark model analysis.
- A vector meson saturation estimate yields $\bar C \sim -0.35 g_\pi$, consistent with the naturalness expectation $\bar C \sim g_\pi$.
- The DDH "best value" for $h_\pi^1$ is $|h_\pi^1| = 5 \times 10^{-7}$, corresponding to roughly $(7-12) \times g_\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Chen and Ji (2000), who computed $B_\gamma$ at leading order in HBCPT and proposed a measurement at Jefferson Lab, by including the complete set of ${\cal O}(p)$ subleading corrections. The results are relevant to understanding the "puzzle" of the suppressed $h_\pi^{\rm eff}$ value from the $^{18}$F PV $\gamma$-decay measurements (Haxton, 1980s; five independent groups), which conflicts with the naturalness estimate and with constraints from the cesium anapole moment (Boulder group, 1997) that imply $h_\pi^{\rm eff} \sim 10 g_\pi$. The new subleading contributions involve LECs that also appear in the radiative corrections to backward-angle PV $ep$ scattering measured by the SAMPLE collaboration, which were found to be larger than theoretical expectations (Zhu et al., 2000). The work enables experimental separation of the two PV LECs $h_\pi^1$ and $\bar C$ through measurements of $B_\gamma$ at Jefferson Lab, particularly if a null result at 20% precision would be consistent with the $^{18}$F constraints and would require additional measurements to disentangle the two couplings.
