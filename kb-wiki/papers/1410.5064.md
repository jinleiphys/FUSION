# Double parton interactions in $γp, γA$ collisions in the direct photon kinematics

> We derive expressions for the differential distributions and the total cross section of double- parton interaction in direct photon interaction with proton and nuclei. We demonstrate that in this case the cross section is more directly related to the nucleon generalized parton distribution than in the case of double parton interactions in the proton - proton collisions. We focus on the production of two dijets each containing charm (anticharm) quarks and carrying $x_1,x_2>0.2$ fractions of the photon momentum. Numerical results are presented for the case of $γp$ collisions at LHeC, HERA and in the ultraperipheral $AA$ and $pA$ collisions at the LHC. We find that the events of this kind would be abundantly produced at the LHeC. For $\sqrt{s}=1.3$ TeV the expected rate is $2\cdot 10^8$ events for the luminosity $10^{34}$ cm$^{-2}s^{-1}$, the running time of $10^6$ s and the transverse cutoff of $p_t>5$ GeV. This would make it feasible to use these processes for the model independent determination of two parton GPDs in nucleon and in nuclei. For HERA the total accumulated number of the events is also high, but efficiency of the detection of charm seems too low to study the process. We also find that a significant number of such double parton interactions should be produced in $p - Pb$ and $Pb- Pb$ collisions at the LHC: $\sim 6\cdot 10^4$ for $Pb-Pb$, and $\sim 7 \cdot 10^3$ for $p-Pb$ collisions for the same transverse momentum cutoff.

# Double parton interactions in γp, γA collisions in the direct photon kinematics

## Key claim

The cross section for double parton interactions (DPI) in direct photon–proton and photon–nucleus collisions is more directly related to the nucleon generalized parton distribution (GPD) than in pp collisions, because the only contributing mechanism is the 1⊗2 process (parton splitting), while the 2⊗2 mechanism is absent. The authors claim that for the LHeC at √s = 1.3 TeV, the expected event rate is 2×10⁸ events for a luminosity of 10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹, a running time of 10⁶ s, and a transverse momentum cutoff pₜ > 5 GeV, making model-independent determination of two-parton GPDs feasible.

## Method

The authors derived expressions for the differential distributions and total cross section of double-parton interactions in direct photon kinematics, focusing on the production of two dijets each containing a charm (or anticharm) quark, with momentum fractions x₁, x₂ > 0.2 of the photon momentum to ensure dominance of the direct photon mechanism. They calculated the cross section using the leading-log approximation (LLA), expressing it through the integral over the two-particle GPD of the nucleon, and convoluted the elementary γN cross section with the Weizsäcker–Williams photon flux for ep collisions at LHeC and HERA, and for ultraperipheral AA and pA collisions at the LHC. Numerical rates were obtained using GRV parton distribution functions and a two-gluon form factor parameterization from exclusive J/ψ diffractive photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- For γp collisions at LHeC (√s = 1.3 TeV, luminosity 10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹, running time 10⁶ s, pₜ > 5 GeV): expected 2×10⁸ MPI events; the ratio of MPI to dijet events is 0.045%.
- For HERA (√s = 300 GeV, integrated luminosity 1 fb⁻¹, pₜ > 5 GeV): expected 1.2×10⁵ MPI events; the ratio of MPI to dijet events is 0.0125%.
- For ultraperipheral Pb–Pb collisions at LHC (√s = 5.6 TeV, luminosity 10²⁷ cm⁻²s⁻¹, running time 10⁶ s, pₜ > 5 GeV): expected ~6×10⁴ MPI events; the ratio of MPI to dijet events is 0.037%.
- For ultraperipheral p–Pb collisions at LHC (√s = 9 TeV, luminosity 10²⁹ cm⁻²s⁻¹, running time 10⁶ s, pₜ > 5 GeV): expected ~6.6×10³ MPI events; the ratio of MPI to dijet events is 0.02%.
- The ratio of MPI to dijet events in AA collisions is a factor of ~2–3 larger than in pA collisions due to geometrical enhancement from scattering off two nucleons along the photon impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for multiparton interactions (MPI) developed for pp collisions by Treleani, Diehl, Frankfurt, and others (2000s) to the case of direct photon–hadron collisions, where the 2⊗2 mechanism is absent and only the 1⊗2 (parton splitting) mechanism contributes. The authors note that previous studies of MPI in γp collisions by BFS considered resolved photon kinematics, which is very different from the direct photon kinematics treated here. The results enable a nearly model-independent determination of two-parton GPDs in nucleons and nuclei, free from the ambiguities inherent in pp/pA scattering discussed by BDFS (2010s). The analysis also builds on the treatment of nuclear effects in pA collisions by Strikman and Treleani (2000s) and on the two-gluon form factor extracted from exclusive J/ψ photoproduction by Frankfurt (2000s).
