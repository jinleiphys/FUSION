# Recent theoretical developments on QCD matter at finite temperature and density

> QCD matter at finite temperature and density is a subject that has witnessed very impressive theoretical developments in the recent years. In this review I will discuss some new insights on the microscopic degrees of freedom of the QCD medium near the chiral crossover transition from lattice QCD. Latest high precision lattice data on the fluctuations and correlations between conserved charges like the baryon number, strangeness can help us to understand and distinguish between different models of interacting hadrons. Furthermore, the latest constraints on the location of the critical end-point and the curvature of the critical line will be discussed. In the later part of this review I will discuss about the insights on the thermal nature of the medium created in heavy ion collision experiments that have come from the theoretical analysis of the particle yields, and to what extent the lattice data on correlations and fluctuations of conserved charges can give us any information about the fireball at freezeout.

## Key claim

This review argues that lattice QCD techniques, particularly the Taylor series method and imaginary chemical potential formalism, have reliably determined the equation of state of QCD matter for baryon chemical potential to temperature ratios up to about 2.5, and that high-precision lattice data on fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges can distinguish between different hadron resonance gas models, but the location of the critical end-point remains constrained only to a region with temperature below 135 MeV and baryon chemical potential to temperature ratio above 2.5.

## Method

The review summarizes lattice QCD results obtained using two main approaches to circumvent the sign problem at finite baryon density: the Taylor series method, where thermodynamic quantities like pressure are expanded as a Taylor series in μ_B/T about μ_B=0, and the imaginary chemical potential method, where calculations are performed at imaginary μ_B and analytically continued to real μ_B. Results are presented for 2+1 flavor QCD using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) and stout improved staggered fermion discretizations, with strangeness neutrality constraints (n_S=0 and n_B/n_Q=0.4) implemented. The review also compares lattice results to hadron resonance gas model predictions and to experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The equation of state is reliably determined for μ_B/T ≲ 2.5 using expansion up to O(μ_B^6)
- The second-order curvature coefficient of the pseudo-critical line is κ_2^B ∼ 0.01 with error < 20%
- The fourth-order curvature coefficient κ_4^B ∼ 0, albeit with large systematic uncertainties from continuum extrapolation
- The critical end-point is constrained to μ_B/T > 2.5 for temperatures 135 ≤ T < 140 MeV
- The temperature of the critical end-point must satisfy T_CEP ≲ 135 MeV for successive χ_{2n}^B to be all positive
- Statistical fits to ALICE Pb-Pb data at √s = 2.76 TeV yield T_f ∼ 156 MeV and μ_B ∼ 0
- The proton yield deviates from statistical fits by 2.6 σ before including pion-nucleon interactions via S-matrix formalism

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD work on the phase diagram by groups including Bazavov (2017, 2020), Borsanyi (2018, 2020), and Bonati (2018), who have provided continuum estimates of Taylor coefficients and curvature coefficients. It contradicts the naive hadron resonance gas model constructed from experimentally known hadron states from the Particle Data Group, showing that such models fail to describe thermodynamic properties of strange and charm baryons as shown by Bazavov (2014). The work enables future constraints on the critical end-point location through higher-order cumulants (beyond χ_8^B) and improved understanding of chemical freezeout by comparing lattice data on cumulant ratios (such as R_31^B and R_42^B) to experimental net-proton cumulants from STAR, as demonstrated by Bazavov (2020).
