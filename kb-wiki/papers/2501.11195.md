# The Proton Radius Puzzle and Discrepancies in Proton Structure Measurements

> The proton radius puzzle remains a key challenge in modern physics, highlighting both the precision and limitations of current experimental and theoretical approaches. Recent studies, such as those by Xiong et al. and Bezginov et al., have consistently found a smaller proton radius of about 0.84 femtometers, in line with muonic hydrogen measurements, but discrepancies with earlier electron-proton scattering and atomic hydrogen spectroscopy persist. These unresolved differences reveal systematic errors or limitations in existing theoretical frameworks, as pointed out by Arrington and Sick. The divergence between results from muonic and electronic hydrogen measurements remains unexplained, with contributions from both experimental uncertainties and theoretical gaps. To address these challenges, a coordinated approach is needed-focused on reducing systematic uncertainties through advanced experimental setups, like improved scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab, and developing new theoretical models, such as those proposed by Alarćon et al. and Lin et al. Integrating experimental precision with theoretical rigor offers the best path toward resolving this enduring puzzle and providing deeper insights into the fundamental forces of nature.

# The Proton Radius Puzzle and Discrepancies in Proton Structure Measurements

## Key claim
The proton radius puzzle remains unresolved despite recent measurements from Xiong et al. and Bezginov et al. finding a smaller radius of about 0.84 femtometers consistent with muonic hydrogen results, because persistent discrepancies with earlier electron-proton scattering and atomic hydrogen spectroscopy data suggest either unidentified systematic errors in some experiments or limitations in existing theoretical frameworks such as Quantum Electrodynamics.

## Method
The paper reviews three primary experimental techniques for extracting the proton charge radius: atomic hydrogen spectroscopy (including continuous-wave laser spectroscopy, two-photon frequency comb spectroscopy, and the frequency-offset separated oscillatory field technique), muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (using pulsed laser spectroscopy of 2S-2P transitions in muonic hydrogen atoms formed by stopping negative muons in hydrogen gas), and electron-proton scattering experiments (measuring elastic scattering cross-sections and form factors at facilities including Mainz Microtron and Jefferson Lab). The review also covers reanalyses of existing scattering data using polynomial expansions and model-independent approaches incorporating analyticity constraints.

## Key numbers
- Pohl et al. (2010) muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement: proton radius r_p = 0.84184(67) fm, with transition frequency 49,881.88(76) GHz, deviating by 5 sigma from the then-CODATA value of 0.8768(69) fm
- Antognini et al. (2013) muonic hydrogen 2S-2P transitions: r_E = 0.84087(39) fm, with singlet transition nu_s = 54,611.16(1.00_stat)(0.30_sys) GHz and triplet transition nu_t = 49,881.35(0.57_stat)(0.30_sys) GHz
- Fleurbaey et al. (2018) atomic hydrogen 1S-3S spectroscopy: r_p = 0.877(13) fm, with transition frequency relative uncertainty of 9 x 10^{-13}
- Beyer et al. (2017) atomic hydrogen 2S-nP spectroscopy: r_p = 0.8764(89) fm
- Bezginov et al. (2019) FOSOF Lamb shift measurement: r_p = 0.833(10) fm, with Lamb shift uncertainty of plus or minus 3.2 kHz
- Grinin et al. (2020) two-photon frequency comb 1S-3S spectroscopy: r_p = 0.8482(38) fm, with centroid frequency f = 2,922,743,278,665.79(72) kHz and Rydberg constant R_inf = 10,973,731.568226(38) m^{-1}
- Bernauer et al. (2010) Mainz electron-proton scattering: r_p = 0.879(5_stat)(4_syst)(2_model)(4_group) fm, covering Q^2 from 0.004 to 1 (GeV/c)^2 with beam energies 180 to 855 MeV
- Zhan et al. (2011) Jefferson Lab polarized scattering: r_p = 0.875(10) fm, measuring form factor ratio across Q^2 = 0.30 to 0.70 (GeV/c)^2
- Xiong et al. (2019) Jefferson Lab electron-proton scattering: r_p = 0.831(7_stat)(12_syst) fm, with total systematic relative uncertainty of 1.4 percent
- Mihovilovic and Merkel ISR experiment: r_p = 0.836(17_stat)(59_syst)(3_model) fm, covering Q^2 from 1 x 10^{-3} to 4 x 10^{-3} (GeV/c)^2
- Griffioen et al. reanalysis: r_E = 0.840(16) fm using polynomial expansions below squared momentum transfers of 4 m_pi^2

## Context
This review synthesizes the ongoing proton radius puzzle that began with Pohl et al. (2010) reporting a significantly smaller radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy compared to the CODATA-recommended value from electron-proton scattering and atomic hydrogen spectroscopy. The paper extends discussions by Arrington and Sick (2015) regarding systematic errors in existing measurements and by Carlson (2015) on implications for Quantum Electrodynamics and the Standard Model. The review enables future coordinated experimental efforts at facilities like Jefferson Lab and theoretical developments such as those proposed by Alarcon et al. (2019) and Lin et al. (2022), while noting that lattice QCD calculations fall beyond its scope.
