# Construction of high-quality Nucleon-Nucleon potential models

> We present an updated version (Nijm93) of the Nijmegen soft-core potential, which gives a much better description of the np data than the older version (Nijm78). The chi^2 per datum is 1.87. The configuration-space and momentum-space versions of this potential are exactly equivalent; a unique feature among meson-theoretical potentials. We also present three new NN potential models: a non-local Reid-like Nijmegen potential (NijmI), a local version (NijmII), and an updated regularized version (Reid93) of the Reid soft-core potential. These three potentials all have a nearly optimal chi^2 per datum and can therefore be considered as alternative partial-wave analyses. All potentials contain the proper charge-dependent one-pion-exchange tail. Fortran code for the potentials can be obtained via anonymous ftp from thef-nym.sci.kun.nl

## Key claim

The paper presents four new nucleon-nucleon potential models that achieve significantly better fits to the combined pp and np scattering data below 350 MeV than previous potentials, with three of them (Nijm I, Nijm II, Reid93) reaching nearly optimal chi-squared per datum close to 1.0, though the updated Nijm93 soft-core potential still only achieves chi-squared per datum of 1.87, not the near-optimal value.

## Method

The authors constructed four potential models: an updated Nijmegen soft-core potential (Nijm93) with 14 free parameters fitted to both pp and np data, a non-local Reid-like Nijmegen potential (Nijm I), a local version (Nijm II), and a regularized update of the Reid soft-core potential (Reid93) using a dipole form factor to remove singularities at the origin. All potentials explicitly include charge-dependent one-pion-exchange with separate neutral-pion (134.9739 MeV) and charged-pion (139.5675 MeV) masses, and the Nijmegen potentials are constructed to be exactly equivalent in both configuration-space and momentum-space formulations. The parameters were optimized by minimizing chi-squared using the Nijmegen representation of the chi-squared hypersurface obtained from single-energy analyses of the scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Nijm93 potential achieves chi-squared per datum of 1.87 for the combined np and pp data below 350 MeV
- Nijm I potential achieves chi-squared per datum of 1.03, nearly matching the optimal value of 0.99 from the Nijmegen PWA93 partial-wave analysis
- The Nijmegen PWA93 partial-wave analysis used 39 parameters to reach chi-squared per datum of 0.99, while conventional potential models typically have only 10-15 free parameters
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant at the pion pole is taken as f_pi^2 = 0.075, based on previous partial-wave analyses
- The Reid93 potential uses a dipole cutoff parameter Lambda = 8 m_pi, where m_pi is the mean pion mass
- The neutral-pion mass is 134.9739 MeV and the charged-pion mass is 139.5675 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core potential (Nijm78) and the Reid soft-core potential (Reid68) to fit the much more accurate np scattering data that became available in the 1980s and 1990s, which older potentials could no longer describe adequately. The authors had previously shown in Stoks (1993) that only a few potentials (Reid68, Nijm78, and the Bonn pp potential) gave satisfactory fits to pp data, with chi-squared per datum around 1.9. The new potentials enable more reliable partial-wave analyses and provide an estimate of systematic errors in the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis by comparing phase parameters from different models that all achieve nearly optimal fits to the same database.
