# Quenching of spin operators in the calculation of radiative corrections for nuclear beta decay

> Calculations of the axial-vector component to the radiative correction for superallowed Fermi $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ nuclear beta decay are here modified with quenched rather than free-nucleon coupling constants for the axial-vector and electromagnetic interactions with nucleons. The result increases the deduced value of $V_{ud}$ but does not restore unitarity in the CKM matrix.

## Key claim
Using quenched (medium-modified) rather than free-nucleon coupling constants for the axial-vector and electromagnetic interactions in the radiative correction for superallowed Fermi \(0^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) beta decay increases the deduced value of \(V_{ud}\) but does not restore unitarity of the CKM matrix; the improvement in the fit quality is modest, and the quenching accounts for only about 20% of the observed \(Z\)-dependence in the corrected \(Ft\) values.

## Method
The author recalculates the nuclear-structure-dependent part of the radiative correction \(C = C_{\rm Born} + C_{\rm NS}\) for superallowed Fermi decays, replacing free-nucleon coupling constants with effective (quenched) values for the isoscalar spin operator \(g_S^{(0)}\) and the axial-vector coupling \(g_A\). Quenching factors \(q = g_{\rm eff}/g\) are taken from earlier core-polarization and meson-exchange calculations (Towner 1987) for nuclei from \(A=10\) to \(A=54\), and applied to both the one-body (Born) and two-body (nuclear-structure) contributions. The corrected \(Ft\) values for eight precision superallowed transitions are then fitted with one-parameter (constant \(Ft\)) and two-parameter (linear in daughter charge \(Z\)) functions.

## Key numbers
- The free-nucleon Born term value used is \(C_{\rm Born}({\rm free}) = 0.881 \pm 0.030\) (Towner 1992).
- For \(A=14\), the unquenched nuclear-structure correction is \(C_{\rm NS} = -1.15 \pm 0.30\); with quenching it becomes \(-0.88 \pm 0.23\).
- For \(A=26\), the unquenched \(C_{\rm NS} = 0.25 \pm 0.05\) becomes \(0.20 \pm 0.04\) with quenching.
- In the one-parameter fit, the corrected \(Ft(0)\) changes from \(3075.0 \pm 3.5\) s (unquenched) to \(3073.0 \pm 3.3\) s (quenched), and \(V_{ud}\) increases from \(0.9733 \pm 0.0007\) to \(0.9736 \pm 0.0007\).
- The unitarity sum \(V_{ud}^2 + V_{us}^2 + V_{ub}^2\) in the one-parameter fit changes from \(0.9959 \pm 0.0016\) (unquenched) to \(0.9965 \pm 0.0015\) (quenched).
- In the two-parameter fit, the slope \(a_1\) (the \(Z\)-dependence coefficient) is reduced from \((1.4 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\) to \((1.1 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Quenching factors used include, for example, \(q_A = 0.858\) for \(A=14\), \(q_A = 0.835\) for \(A=26\), and \(q_S^{(0)} = 0.873\) for \(A=14\).

## Context
This work extends the radiative correction calculations of Marciano and Sirlin (1986) and the nuclear-structure-dependent corrections of Jaus and Rasche (1990), Barker et al. (1992), and Towner (1992), which had introduced a \(Z\)-dependence in the corrected \(Ft\) values that worsened the CVC test and lowered \(V_{ud}\). By incorporating the well-known quenching of spin operators in nuclear matter (Brown and Wildenthal 1983; Towner 1987), the author shows that medium effects partially reduce the \(Z\)-dependence but do not resolve the unitarity deficit, leaving open the need for additional electromagnetic corrections or a re-examination of the CVC hypothesis.
