# Electric quadrupole transitions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential

> Eigenfunctions of the collective Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential have been obtained by using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) for both gamma-unstable and rotational structures. B(E2) transition rates have been calculated and compared to experimental data. Overall good agreement is obtained for transitions within the ground state band, while some interband transitions appear to be systematically underpredicted in gamma-unstable nuclei and overpredicted in rotational nuclei.

# Electric quadrupole transitions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential

## Key claim

The Morse potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian yields overall good agreement with experimental B(E2) transition rates for intraband transitions within the ground state band, but interband transitions are systematically underpredicted in γ-unstable nuclei and systematically overpredicted in rotational nuclei, based on calculations using parameters fitted only to energy spectra (not to B(E2) data).

## Method

The authors solved the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) for both γ-unstable (potential independent of γ) and rotational (axially symmetric prolate deformed, with γ-dependent potential approximated as harmonic) structures. Wave functions were obtained in closed form using Laguerre polynomials, and B(E2) transition rates were calculated from the resulting eigenfunctions using the standard quadrupole operator. Parameters were determined by fitting experimental energy spectra for 30 γ-unstable and 32 rotational nuclei, with no B(E2) data included in the fits.

## Key numbers

- 30 γ-unstable nuclei and 32 rotational nuclei were analyzed for B(E2) transitions
- For γ-unstable nuclei: B(E2) values for transitions within the ground state band show "good results" (no single numerical deviation quoted); interband transitions are "systematically lower than the experimental data"
- For rotational nuclei: intraband B(E2)s within the ground state band are "successful"; most interband B(E2)s are "systematically overpredicted"
- Normalization constant for the (nγ=0, K=0) state in the rotational case: N_{0,0} = sqrt(2c) for c ≥ 2, where the exponential term e^{-cπ²/3} becomes of order 10⁻⁴ or smaller
- Example γ-integral for ΔK=2 transition (n'γ=1, K'=2) → (nγ=0, K=0): C_{0,0,1,2} = (1/(6c²)) N_{0,0} N_{1,2}
- Both the Morse model and the deformation-dependent mass (DDM) Davidson model use the same number of free parameters: two parameters in the γ-unstable case, three parameters in the rotational case

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the Morse potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian (Boztosun and Karakoc, 2008) by completing the calculation of eigenfunctions and B(E2) transition rates. It builds on the critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello, 2000) and X(5) (Iachello, 2001), which used infinite-well potentials, and on earlier analytical solutions using Coulomb-like, Kratzer-like, β²ⁿ-type, and Davidson potentials (Fortunato, 2003; Arias, 2003; Bonatsos, 2004-2007). The results are compared to a recent deformation-dependent mass (DDM) Davidson approach (Bonatsos, 2009-2010), which uses a deformation-dependent mass instead of the constant mass employed here. The authors suggest that a DDM Kratzer potential should be studied next, as it would combine the deformation-dependent mass feature with the finite potential at large β characteristic of the Morse potential.
