# A soft-hard framework with exact four momentum conservation for small systems

> A new framework, called x-scape, for the combined study of both hard and soft transverse momentum sectors in high energy proton-proton ($p$-$p$) and proton-nucleus ($p$-$A$) collisions is set up. A dynamical initial state is set up using the 3d-Glauber model with transverse locations of hotspots within each incoming nucleon. A hard scattering that emanates from two colliding hotspots is carried out using the Pythia generator. Initial state radiation from the incoming hard partons is carried out in a new module called I-matter, which includes the longitudinal location of initial splits. The energy-momentum of both the initial hard partons and their associated beam remnants is removed from the hot spots, depleting the energy-momentum available for the formation of the bulk medium. Outgoing showers are simulated using the matter generator, and results are presented for both cases, allowing for and not allowing for energy loss. First comparisons between this hard-soft model and single inclusive hadron and jet data from $p$-$p$ and minimum bias $p$-$Pb$ collisions are presented. Single hadron spectra in $p$-$p$ are used to carry out a limited (in number of parameters) Bayesian calibration of the model. Fair comparisons with data are indicative of the utility of this new framework. Theoretical studies of the correlation between jet $p_T$ and event activity at mid and forward rapidity are carried out.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new framework, x-scape, that enforces exact event-by-event four-momentum conservation between hard and soft sectors in small collision systems, but the authors explicitly state that the current work only explores the minimal configuration of energy-momentum conservation without additional effects such as color transparency or corrections to energy loss, and that any further effects should be considered in addition to these basic constraints.

## Method

The framework combines a 3d-Glauber initial state model with transverse hotspots within nucleons, Pythia 8 for hard scattering generation, a new I-matter module for initial-state radiation including longitudinal splitting locations, and the matter generator for final-state showers. Energy and momentum of hard partons and their beam remnants are subtracted from the hotspots before the remaining energy is used for bulk medium evolution via MUSIC hydrodynamics and iSS particlization. The authors present results both with and without final-state energy loss, and perform a limited Bayesian calibration using single hadron spectra in proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic cross sections from the 3d-Glauber model: p+p at 200 GeV = 0.042 b, p+Au at 200 GeV = 1.75 b, Au+Au at 200 GeV = 6.89 b, p+p at 5020 GeV = 0.067 b, p+Pb at 5020 GeV = 2.16 b, Pb+Pb at 5020 GeV = 7.96 b
- High-pT particles are defined as those with pT greater than or approximately 8 GeV, and high-pT jets as those with pT greater than or approximately 20 GeV
- Experimental bounds on energy loss in p-Pb collisions place limits at Delta E less than or approximately 1% (ATLAS 2022) or pT less than or approximately 0.4 GeV out of jet cone (ALICE 2017)
- The nucleon transverse profile parameter BG controls the transverse size of the nucleon in the Gaussian profile T_n(r_perp) = 1/(2 pi B_G) exp(-r_perp^2/(2 B_G))

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework to small systems (p-p and p-A collisions), building on the observation of collective behavior in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at the LHC (ATLAS 2012, CMS 2012, ALICE 2012) and the subsequent complex picture of jet suppression and enhancement in p-Pb collisions (ATLAS 2014). The framework enables systematic studies of correlations between hard scattering and event activity, addressing open questions about QGP formation in small systems and the lack of significant energy loss for high-pT hadrons and jets, which previous measurements by ALICE (2014, 2017) and ATLAS (2016, 2022) have found difficult to reconcile with observed elliptic anisotropy at high pT.
