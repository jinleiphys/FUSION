# Model-independent study of the QCD sum rule for the pi NN coupling constant

> We reinvestigate the QCD sum rule for the pi NN coupling constant, g, starting from the vacuum-to-pion matrix element of the correlation function of the interpolating fields of two nucleons. We study in detail the physical content of the correlation function without referring to the effective theory. We consider the invariant correlation functions by splitting the correlation function into different Dirac structures. We show that the coefficients of the double-pole terms are proportional to g but that the coefficients of the single-pole terms are not determined by g. In the chiral limit the single-pole terms as well as the continuum terms are ill defined in the dispersion integral. Therefore, the use of naive QCD sum rules obtained from the invariant correlation functions is not justified. A possible procedure to avoid this difficulty is discussed.

## Key claim

The coefficients of the double-pole terms in the QCD sum rule for the πNN coupling constant are proportional to g, but the coefficients of the single-pole terms are not determined by g; in the chiral limit, the single-pole and continuum terms become ill-defined in the dispersion integral, so naive QCD sum rules from the invariant correlation functions are not justified.

## Method

The authors start from the vacuum-to-pion matrix element of the correlation function of two nucleon interpolating fields and split it into four Dirac structures (iγ₅, iγ₅ p̸/M, iγ₅ k̸/M, and γ₅σ_{μν}p^μk^ν/M²). They analyze the physical content of the correlation function without using effective Lagrangians, instead relying on general principles to classify the singularities. The vertex function and the pion-nucleon T-matrix are introduced to relate the correlation function to the πNN coupling constant, and the behavior of the absorptive and dispersive parts near the pion-nucleon threshold is examined.

## Key numbers

- The πNN coupling constant g is defined through the pole coefficient: at p² = M² and (p-k)² = M², the relation is ū(p̸-M)Π(p,k)(p̸-k̸-M)u = -ig ūγ₅u.
- The double-pole coefficients for the four Dirac structures are: -m_π²/2 g (iγ₅), 0 (iγ₅ p̸/M), M²g (iγ₅ k̸/M), and -M²g (γ₅σ_{μν}p^μk^ν/M²).
- In the chiral limit (m_π = 0), the derivative of the real part of the vertex function at threshold behaves as a finite constant, while for m_π ≠ 0 it diverges as infinity.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to the πNN coupling constant initiated by Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1985) and later refined by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1995) and Birse and Krippa (1996). It directly addresses the analysis of Kim, Lee and Oka (1999), who claimed that only the γ₅σ_{μν}p^μk^ν structure is independent of the pseudoscalar versus pseudovector coupling scheme. The authors show that tree-level effective Lagrangians are insufficient for the single-pole and continuum terms, and propose that a well-defined sum rule requires transforming the absorptive part to remove ill-defined terms, or using a projected correlation function approach.
