# Nuclear Matter from Effective Quark-Quark Interaction

> We study neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter with the quark-meson model for the two-nucleon interaction. The Bethe-Bruckner-Goldstone many-body theory is used to describe the correlations up to the three hole-line approximation with no extra parameters. At variance with other non-relativistic realistic interactions, the three hole-line contribution turns out to be non-negligible and to have a substantial saturation effect. The saturation point of nuclear matter, the compressibility, the symmetry energy and its slope are within the phenomenological constraints. Since the interaction also reproduces fairly well the properties of the three nucleon system, these results indicate that the explicit introduction of the quark degrees of freedom within the considered constituent quark model is expected to reduce the role of three-body forces.

## Key claim

The quark-meson (QM) two-nucleon interaction, when used in the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone many-body expansion up to three hole-line level, produces a saturation point, compressibility, symmetry energy, and slope that are within phenomenological constraints, but the three hole-line contribution is non-negligible and has a substantial saturation effect, unlike other non-relativistic realistic interactions. This suggests that explicitly introducing quark degrees of freedom may reduce the role of three-body forces, though the authors note that a direct derivation of three-body forces from the same quark model is left for future work.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson model (fss2) for the two-nucleon interaction, derived from an effective quark-quark interaction within the resonating-group-method (RGM) formalism, and applied the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) many-body theory up to the three hole-line approximation. They computed the equation of state (EOS) for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using both the continuous choice (CC) and gap choice (GC) for the single-particle auxiliary potential, solving the Bethe-Fadeev equations for the three-body scattering contribution. The energy dependence inherent to the RGM formalism was eliminated via an off-shell transformation using the norm kernel and a Gaussian representation of the fss2 interaction.

## Key numbers

- Saturation point for symmetric matter: energy per nucleon \(e_0 = -16.9\) MeV and density \(\rho_0 = 0.166\) fm\(^{-3}\) for the continuous choice (CC); \(e_0 = -16.06\) MeV and \(\rho_0 = 0.177\) fm\(^{-3}\) for the gap choice (GC).
- Compressibility at saturation: \(K = 228\) MeV for CC; \(K = 192\) MeV for GC.
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(S_0 = 34\) MeV for CC; \(S_0 = 33.7\) MeV for GC.
- Slope of symmetry energy: \(L = 54\) MeV for CC; \(L = 53\) MeV for GC.
- Ratio of three hole-line to two hole-line correlation energy around saturation: 0.15 for CC; 0.02 for GC.
- Wound parameter at saturation: 0.072 for the QM interaction; 0.037 for the Argonne v\(_{18}\) potential; contribution from the \(^3S_1 - ^3D_1\) channel is 0.009 for QM and 0.019 for Argonne v\(_{18}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the quark-model (QM) NN interaction of Fujiwara and collaborators (fss2), which reproduces few-body data (triton, hypertriton, alpha particle, nucleon-deuteron scattering) without three-body forces, to nuclear matter calculations. It contrasts with non-relativistic realistic potentials such as Argonne v\(_{18}\) (which require three-body forces to achieve saturation) and with chiral effective field theory approaches (where three-body forces are the dominant saturation mechanism). The results enable future work to explicitly derive three-body forces from the same quark model to check consistency, and they suggest that the off-shell behavior of the QM interaction, particularly its non-locality from quark exchange, is responsible for the different saturation mechanism compared to meson-exchange potentials.
