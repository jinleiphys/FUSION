# Cranked Relativistic Mean Field Description of Superdeformed Rotational Bands

> The cranked relativistic mean field theory is applied for a detailed investigation of eight superdeformed rotational bands observed in $^{151}$Tb. It is shown that this theory is able to reproduce reasonably well not only the dynamic moments of inertia $J^{(2)}$ of the observed bands but also the alignment properties of the single-particle orbitals.

## Key claim

Cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory can reproduce the dynamic moments of inertia \(J^{(2)}\) and the alignment properties of single-particle orbitals for eight superdeformed rotational bands in \(^{151}\)Tb, but the consistent interpretation of bands 7 and 8 in a pure single-particle picture remains difficult, possibly due to residual interactions neglected in the calculations.

## Method

The authors applied cranked relativistic mean field theory, in which the nucleus is described as Dirac spinors coupled to scalar \(\sigma\)-meson, isoscalar-vector \(\omega\)-meson, and isovector-vector \(\rho\)-meson fields, using the non-linear parameter set NL1. Pairing correlations were neglected because they play a minor role at high rotational frequencies (\(\Omega_x > 0.5\) MeV) for nuclei near \(^{152}\)Dy. The dynamic moments of inertia \(J^{(2)}\) and effective alignments \(i_{eff}\) (defined as the difference in spins between two bands at constant rotational frequency) were calculated for eight superdeformed bands in \(^{151}\)Tb and compared with experimental data and with results from the cranked Nilsson (CN) model and cranked Hartree-Fock approaches with Skyrme forces.

## Key numbers

- The effective alignment of the \(\pi [651]3/2(r=+i)\) orbital is described much better in CRMF theory than in the CN model (see Fig. 3 in Ragnarsson (1993)).
- The CRMF calculations suggest that the spins of the \(^{151}\)Tb(1) band relative to the \(^{152}\)Dy(1) band should be increased by \(2\hbar\) compared with the results of the CN calculations (Ragnarsson 1993).
- The effective alignment of the \(^{152}\)Dy(1) band relative to bands 5-8 in \(^{151}\)Tb shows a total gain in \(i_{eff}\) of approximately \(3\hbar\) as a function of rotational frequency.
- The energy of the lowest intraband \(\gamma\)-transition \(E_{\gamma}^0\) for band 1 is 726.5 keV; for band 2, 602.1 keV; for band 3, 681.5 keV; for band 4, 768.6 keV; for band 5, 709.3 keV; for band 6, 739 keV; for band 7, 758 keV; for band 8, 785 keV.
- The difference in charge quadrupole moments \(\Delta Q_0\) at \(\Omega_x = 0.5\) MeV relative to \(^{152}\)Dy(1) is \(-0.99\) eb for band 1, \(+0.20\) eb for band 2, \(-0.90\) eb for band 3, \(+0.24\) eb for band 4, \(-1.80\) eb for bands 5 and 6, and \(-1.51\) eb for bands 7 and 8.

## Context

This work extends the application of cranked relativistic mean field theory to the \(A \sim 140-150\) mass region, following earlier systematic investigations by Afanasjev, Konig, and Ring (1996) that focused on dynamic moments of inertia and charge quadrupole moments. The study directly compares with non-relativistic models such as the cranked Nilsson model (Ragnarsson 1993) and cranked Hartree-Fock approaches with Skyrme forces (Dobaczewski and Dudek 1995; Bender, Flocard, and Heenen 1996; Afanasjev 1997), and it tests the effective alignment method proposed by Ragnarsson (1991) as a more discriminating tool for configuration assignment. The results enable future systematic studies of alignment properties in the superdeformed minimum of the \(A \sim 140-150\) region, as planned in a forthcoming article by Afanasjev, Lalazissis, and Ring (1997).
