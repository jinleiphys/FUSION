# The influence of inelastic neutrino reactions with light nuclei on the standing accretion shock instability in core-collapse supernovae

> We perform numerical experiments to investigate the influence of inelastic neutrino reactions with light nuclei on the standing accretion shock instability (SASI). The time evolution of shock waves is calculated with a simple light-bulb approximation for the neutrino transport and a multi-nuclei equation of state. The neutrino absorptions and inelastic interactions with deuterons, tritons, helions and alpha particles are taken into account in the hydrodynamical simulations in addition to the ordinary charged-current interactions with nucleons. Axial symmetry is assumed but no equatorial symmetry is imposed. We show that the heating rates of deuterons reach as high as 10% of those of nucleons around the bottom of the gain region. On the other hand, alpha particles are heated near the shock wave, which is important when the shock wave expands and the density and temperature of matter become low. It is also found that the models with heating by light nuclei have different evolutions from those without it in the non-linear phase of SASI. This results is because matter in the gain region has a varying density and temperature and there appear sub-regions that are locally rich in deuterons and alpha particles. Although the light nuclei are never dominant heating sources and they work favorably for shock revival in some cases and unfavorably in other cases, they are non-negligible and warrant further investigation.

## Key claim

The inelastic neutrino reactions with light nuclei (deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles) are never dominant heating sources in the standing accretion shock instability (SASI) and can work favorably for shock revival in some cases and unfavorably in others, but they are non-negligible and warrant further investigation.

## Method

The authors performed 2D hydrodynamical simulations of the post-bounce phase in core-collapse supernovae using a light-bulb approximation for neutrino transport and a multi-nuclei equation of state that provides abundances of light nuclei up to mass number ~1000. They solved the fluid equations with additional heating terms for deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles, using energy-transfer coefficients from Nakamura (2009), O'Connor (2007), Arcones (2008), and Haxton (1988). Axial symmetry was assumed with no equatorial symmetry, and 300 radial mesh points (to 500 km) and 60 angular mesh points were used.

## Key numbers

- Deuterons have heating rates per baryon comparable to those of nucleons; their heating rates reach as high as ~10% of those of nucleons around the bottom of the gain region (e.g., at r=110 km along theta=180 degrees at t=200 ms, deuterons give 4.73 MeV/sec per baryon vs. nucleon pure heating of 631 MeV/sec per baryon)
- Alpha particles contribute ~10% of nucleon heating rates near the shock wave when the shock expands to ~400 km
- For the model with L52=5.2 and Mdot_sun=1.5, the shock radius is ~140 km and deuteron-heating is second dominant after nucleons in the gain region
- For the model with L52=6.2 and Mdot_sun=0.5, alpha particles contribute to heating alongside nucleons in the outer gain region
- At r=130 km for L52=5.2 and Mdot_sun=1.5, the heating rates per baryon are: Q_E=237.0 MeV/sec, Q_alpha=0.3643 MeV/sec, Q_d=3.264 MeV/sec
- Neutrino temperatures were set to (T_nu_e, T_nu_bar_e, T_nu_mu) = (4, 5, 10) MeV
- The inner boundary was set at the neutrino sphere radius, with outer boundary at 500 km
- The central mass M_in was fixed at 1.4 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the investigation of inelastic neutrino interactions with light nuclei beyond the earlier study by Ohnishi (2007), which only considered alpha particles as additional heating sources. The authors use their own multi-nuclei equation of state (Furusawa 2011, 2013) that includes deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles, unlike the Shen (1998, 2011) equation of state commonly used in supernova simulations which only includes alpha particles as light nuclei. The results enable future studies of how light nuclei affect the non-linear phase of SASI and shock revival, particularly in the context of multi-dimensional simulations with more realistic neutrino transport.
