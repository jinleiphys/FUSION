# Hyperon production in quasielastic $\barν_τ-$nucleon scattering

> The theoretical results for the total cross sections and polarization components of the $τ^{+}$ lepton produced in the charged current induced $|ΔS| = 1$ quasielastic $\barν_τ- N$ scattering leading to hyperons ($Λ, Σ$) have been presented assuming T invariance. The theoretical uncertainties arising due to the use of different vector, axial vector and pseudoscalar form factors as well as the effect of SU(3) symmetry breaking have been studied. We have also presented, for the first time, a comparison of the total cross sections for the production of $e,μ,τ$ leptons to facilitate the implications of lepton flavor universality~(LFU) in the $|ΔS| = 1$ quasielastic reactions induced by the antineutrinos of all flavors i.e., $ν_{l};~l=e,μ,τ$.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical calculations for total cross sections and tau+ lepton polarization in charged-current |ΔS| = 1 quasielastic antineutrino-nucleon scattering leading to hyperon production, assuming T invariance; the results are intended as a baseline within the Standard Model with implicit lepton flavor universality, against which future experimental deviations could signal lepton flavor universality violation.

## Method

The authors compute the differential and total cross sections and tau lepton polarization components for the reactions ν̄τ + p → τ+ + Λ (Σ0) and ν̄τ + n → τ+ + Σ−, using the standard electroweak matrix element with Cabibbo mixing angle θc = 13.1°. The vector form factors are related to nucleon electromagnetic form factors via SU(3) symmetry using the BBBA05 parameterization, while the axial vector form factor uses a dipole form with gA(0) = 1.267 and MA = 1.026 GeV; the pseudoscalar form factor is evaluated using both the Nambu and Marshak parameterizations. SU(3) symmetry breaking effects are studied using the models of Faessler et al. (2008) and Schlumpf, and a comparison of cross sections for e, μ, τ leptons is performed to examine lepton flavor universality.

## Key numbers

- The Cabibbo mixing angle used is θc = 13.1°.
- The axial vector dipole mass is MA = 1.026 GeV, with gA(0) = 1.267.
- The vector dipole mass for SU(3) breaking terms is MV = 0.84 GeV.
- The tau lepton mass is mτ = 1.776 GeV.
- The SU(3) symmetry breaking couplings at Q2 = 0 from Faessler et al. (2008) are: H1V(0) = −0.246, H2V(0) = 0.096, H3V(0) = 0.021, H4V(0) = 0.030; H1A(0) = −0.050, H2A(0) = 0.011, H3A(0) = −0.006, H4A(0) = 0.037.
- The Schlumpf model scaling factors for f1 are α = 0.976, 0.975, 0.975 for p→Λ, p→Σ0, n→Σ− transitions respectively; for g1 the factors are β = 1.072, 1.051, 1.056 respectively.
- SU(3) symmetry predicts dσ/dQ2 for p→Σ0 equals one-half of that for n→Σ−, and the longitudinal and perpendicular polarizations for these two channels are equal.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tau lepton polarization in neutrino-nucleon scattering by Fatima et al. (2018, 2020, 2021) and others (Kuzmin 2003, Hagiwara 2003) to the strangeness-changing (|ΔS| = 1) quasielastic channel, providing the first comparison of cross sections for all three lepton flavors (e, μ, τ) in these reactions. The results complement lepton flavor universality violation studies in semileptonic hyperon decays (He 2019) and in b-quark sector decays (LHCb 2021, BaBar, Belle), and are intended to facilitate future tests of lepton flavor universality in antineutrino-nucleus scattering experiments proposed by SHiP, DsTau, DUNE, and FASERν collaborations.
