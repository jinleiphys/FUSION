# Using spectator distributions to measure the initial geometry fluctuation

> A study of eccentricity ($\varepsilon_{n}$) fluctuations and its possible impact on final state momentum anisotropy ($v_{n}$) for symmetric collisions are presented in the framework of Glauber model. Effect of fluctuations of nucleon's position on the initial geometry has been studied using a new method, where the difference between oppositely moving spectators is taken as a measurement of eccentricity fluctuations. This study shows that higher harmonics ($n$ =3, 4 and 5) of eccentricity are less sensitive to fluctuations in transverse plane compared to the 2$^{nd}$ harmonic. Position fluctuations in transverse plane will increase $\varepsilon_{2}$ and hence possibly $v_{2}$ for the most central nucleus-nucleus collisions. For semi-central and peripheral collisions, the fluctuations have opposite effect, it deceases the eccentricity $\varepsilon_{2}$. The fluctuation of initial geometry can be studied in collider experiments by studying the spectator distribution on the both sides of the beam.

# Using spectator distributions to measure the initial geometry fluctuation

## Key claim

The difference in the number of spectators between the two colliding nuclei ($|S^{A}-S^{B}|$) can be used as a direct experimental observable to quantify event-by-event fluctuations of the initial collision geometry, with the claim that higher harmonics ($n=3,4,5$) of eccentricity are less sensitive to transverse-plane fluctuations than the second harmonic, contrary to the expectation that fluctuations generate those higher harmonics.

## Method

The study uses a Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) model to generate approximately 7 million Au+Au collision events at fixed impact parameters of $b = 1$, 5, and 9 fm, with a nucleon-nucleon cross-section of $\sigma_{NN} = 40$ mb. Events are grouped into sub-classes based on the absolute difference in spectator counts between the two nuclei ($|S^{A}-S^{B}|$), and the participant eccentricity moments $\varepsilon_n$ for $n=2$ through $5$ are calculated from the transverse positions of participating nucleons using the standard moment formula.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions at $b = 1$ fm, the maximum change in $\varepsilon_2$, $\varepsilon_3$, $\varepsilon_4$, and $\varepsilon_5$ with increasing $|S^{A}-S^{B}|$ is approximately 15%, 12%, 3%, and 7.5%, respectively.
- For semi-central collisions at $b = 5$ fm, the corresponding maximum changes are approximately 8.3%, 4.5%, 2%, and 3.5%.
- For peripheral collisions at $b = 9$ fm, the maximum changes are approximately 52%, 14%, 3%, and 8.5%.
- The maximum observed difference between $S^{A}$ and $S^{B}$ in a single event at $b = 5$ fm can be of order 50 spectators.
- The change in average number of participants ($\langle N_{part}\rangle$) across the $|S^{A}-S^{B}|$ range is less than 3%.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for the gold nucleus are radius $R = 6.38$ fm and diffuseness $d = 0.535$ fm.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of initial geometry fluctuations using the MCG model (Alver, 2008) and the AMPT model (Rihan, 2013), which showed that $v_2$ is more sensitive to fluctuations than $v_3$. The proposed spectator-difference method provides an experimental handle to select events with varying fluctuation magnitudes within a single centrality bin, enabling direct measurement of how fluctuations affect final-state flow harmonics $v_n$ at RHIC and LHC. The finding that $\varepsilon_2$ decreases with fluctuations in semi-central and peripheral collisions agrees with prior 3+1 viscous hydrodynamics calculations (v2flucth2, 2012). The method is intended for application to more realistic transport models and experimental data to study transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma.
