# Stability and causality criteria in linear mode analysis: stability means causality

> Causality and stability are fundamental requirements for the differential equations describing predictable relativistic many-body systems. In this work, we investigate the stability and causality criteria in linear mode analysis. We discuss the updated stability criterion in 3+1 dimensional systems and introduce the improved sufficient criterion for causality. Our findings clearly demonstrate that stability implies causality in linear mode analysis. Furthermore, based on the theorems present in this work, we conclude that if updated stability criterion and improved causality criterion are fulfilled in one inertial frame of reference (IFR), they hold for all IFR.

## Key claim

Stability across all inertial frames of reference (IFR) implies causality in linear mode analysis, but causality alone does not necessarily require stability; the authors prove this by showing that the updated stability criterion (Im ω ≤ |Im k| for complex k) is a sufficient condition for the improved causality criterion (Im ω ≤ |Im k| + b for large |k|).

## Method

The authors analyze linear partial differential equations of the form ∂_t φ + M(−i∂)φ = 0, where M is a polynomial of spatial derivatives, and consider plane-wave perturbations φ ∝ exp(−iωt + ik·x). They derive dispersion relations ω_i(k) from the characteristic polynomial det[ω + iM(k)] = 0. They then prove theorems connecting the updated stability criterion (Im ω ≤ |Im k| for k ∈ ℂ³) to an improved sufficient causality criterion (Im ω ≤ |Im k| + b for |k| > R) using contour integration and Cauchy's theorem, and demonstrate Lorentz covariance of both criteria.

## Key numbers

- The updated stability criterion: Im ω ≤ |Im k| for k ∈ ℂ³ (inequality (7) in the paper).
- The improved sufficient causality criterion: Im ω ≤ |Im k| + b for |k| > R, with R > 0 and b ∈ ℝ (inequality (11) in the paper).
- For isotropic systems, the asymptotic dispersion relations at k → ∞ take one of three forms: ω = c₁k + d₁ + O(|k|^a) with c₁ ∈ [−1,1]; ω = c₂k^(−2m−1) + d₂k^(−2m−2) + ... with Im c₂ = 0; or ω = c₃k^(−2m) + ... with Im c₃ ≤ 0 (theorems (16)-(18) in the paper).

## Context

This work extends the stability-causality discussion from 1+1-dimensional systems (Heller 2022, Gavassino 2023) to 3+1-dimensional relativistic systems, and provides rigorous proofs connecting the updated stability criterion to causality. The authors show that the conventional asymptotic causality criterion (lim_{|k|→∞} |Re ω|/|k| ≤ 1) is insufficient, giving the counterexample ω = k(1+i)/2 which satisfies the old criterion but is acausal. The results are model-independent and apply beyond relativistic hydrodynamics, complementing the "thermodynamic stability implies causality" result of Gavassino (2021) and the frame-independent stability analysis of Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2020).
