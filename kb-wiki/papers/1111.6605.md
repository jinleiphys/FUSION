# Fragmentation in Jets: Cone and Threshold Effects

> We study the fragmentation of (light) quarks and gluons to hadrons inside a jet of cone size R. This allows for a more exclusive analysis of fragmentation than is currently the case. The shape of semi-inclusive cross sections in the hadron energy fraction z is described by fragmenting jet functions (FJFs), which we calculate in terms of R and the jet energy E. We introduce a new joint resummation to sum the double logarithms of R and 1-z in the FJFs, which may similarly be applied to initial-state radiation at hadron colliders. Our results at next-to-leading logarithmic order indicate that the resummation of the threshold logarithms of 1-z is already important for z > 0.5 and improves the convergence of perturbation theory. Our framework may be used to analyze LHC and RHIC data and to test and tune Monte Carlo event generators.

## Key claim

The paper introduces fragmenting jet functions (FJFs) for cone jets of size R, and shows that the resummation of threshold logarithms of (1-z) is already important for z > 0.5 and improves the convergence of perturbation theory, with the caveat that this improvement holds to all orders only if the all-orders structure of the jet function anomalous dimension is established for jet algorithms (which is known for hemisphere invariant mass jets but not yet proven for cone algorithms).

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop matching coefficients for cone FJFs onto standard fragmentation functions (FFs) in the MSbar scheme, using the equivalence at one-loop that the cone restriction imposes an invariant mass constraint s ≤ min(z/(1-z), (1-z)/z) × 4E² tan²(R/2). They introduce a joint resummation that sums double logarithms of R and (1-z) by evaluating the FJF at the scale μ = 2(1-z)E tan(R/2), and apply this to both the diagonal (qq, gg) and off-diagonal (qg, gq) matching coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The cone restriction imposes s ≤ min(z/(1-z), (1-z)/z) × 4E² tan²(R/2) at one-loop order
- The dominant D_u^{π+}(z, μ=m_Z) is determined with uncertainties at the 10% level for z ≳ 0.5 (citing Hirai 2007)
- The FJF for u → π+ at E=100 GeV and R=0.4 shows threshold resummation becomes important at z ≳ 0.5
- The natural scale for joint resummation is μ = 2(1-z)E tan(R/2), which sums both logarithms of R and (1-z)
- The non-perturbative corrections scale like 1/R for small R (citing Dasgupta, Magnea, Salam 2008)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous introduction of fragmenting jet functions (Procura 2009) from hemisphere jets to cone jets, and builds on the factorization framework for cone jets developed by Ellis (2009, 2010) and Jouttenus (2009). The results enable more exclusive analysis of fragmentation at LHC and RHIC, allowing tests of Monte Carlo event generators and potentially improving determinations of fragmentation functions. The joint resummation technique also applies to initial-state radiation at hadron colliders via beam functions, where the scale μ² = (1-x)t would sum logarithms of t and (1-x).
