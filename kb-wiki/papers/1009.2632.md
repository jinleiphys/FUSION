# Renormalization of One Meson Exchange Potentials and Their Currents

> The Nucleon-Nucleon One Meson Exchange Potential, its wave functions and related Meson Exchange Currents are analyzed for point-like nucleons. The leading Nc contributions generate a local and energy independent potential which presents 1/r^3 singularities, requiring renormalization. We show how invoking suitable boundary conditions, neutron-proton phase shifts and deuteron properties become largely insensitive to the nucleon substructure and to the vector mesons. Actually, reasonable agreement with low energy data for realistic values of the coupling constants (e.g. SU(3) values) is found. The analysis along similar lines for the Meson Exchange Currents suggests that this renormalization scheme implies tremendous simplifications while complying with exact gauge invariance at any stage of the calculation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for point-like nucleons, the singular $1/r^3$ behavior of the One Meson Exchange Potential can be renormalized using boundary conditions at a short-distance cutoff, making neutron-proton phase shifts and deuteron properties largely insensitive to nucleon substructure and vector mesons, provided the same boundary conditions are used consistently for potentials, wave functions, and currents.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order large-$N_c$ nucleon-nucleon potential from one-boson exchange including $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $a_1$ mesons, which yields a local, energy-independent potential with $1/r^3$ singularities. They renormalize the potential in coordinate space by imposing a boundary condition at a short-distance cutoff $r_c$, making the Hamiltonian self-adjoint for $r > r_c$, and then compute phase shifts, deuteron properties, and electromagnetic form factors. The same boundary condition is applied consistently to meson exchange currents to preserve gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic D/S ratio $\eta = 0.02557$ for the $\pi\sigma\rho\omega a_1$ potential with AVMD coupling, compared to experimental value $0.0256(4)$
- Deuteron magnetic moment $\mu_d = 0.85744 \mu_N$ (experimental value)
- Neutron capture cross section $\sigma(np \to d\gamma) = 334.2(5) \text{ mb}$ (experimental value)
- Cutoff convergence achieved at $r_c \sim 0.3 \text{ fm}$
- Deuteron quadrupole moment $Q_d = 0.2792 \text{ fm}^2$ for $\pi\sigma\rho\omega a_1$ (AVMD), compared to experimental $0.2859(3) \text{ fm}^2$
- Deuteron D-state probability $P_D = 6.53\%$ for $\pi\sigma\rho\omega a_1$ (AVMD), compared to experimental $5.67(4)\%$
- $^1S_0$ phase shift reproduced for $m_\sigma \sim 500 \text{ MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to pion exchange by Cordon and Ruiz Arriola (2009) to include the full set of vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$, $a_1$) in the one-boson-exchange framework. It contrasts with phenomenological potentials such as NijmII and Reid93 (Stoks 1994) that use strong form factors to regularize short-distance behavior, and with chiral effective field theory potentials that do not satisfy large-$N_c$ scaling expectations (Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola 2008, 2009). The approach enables consistent calculation of meson exchange currents with exact gauge invariance, applicable to reactions like radiative neutron capture $n+p \to d+\gamma$.
