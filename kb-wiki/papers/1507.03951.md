# Bottomonia suppression in 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions

> We compute the QGP suppression of Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, and chi_b2 states in sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Using the suppression of each of these states, we estimate the inclusive R_AA for the Upsilon(1s) and Upsilon(2s) states as a function of N_part, y, and p_T including the effect of excited state feed down. We find that our model provides a reasonable description of preliminary CMS results for the N_part-, y-, and p_T-dependence of R_AA for both the Upsilon(1s) and Upsilon(2s). Comparing to our previous model predictions, we find a flatter rapidity dependence, thereby reducing some of the tension between our model and ALICE forward-rapidity results for Upsilon(1s) suppression.

## Key claim

The model provides a reasonable description of preliminary CMS results for the N_part-, y-, and p_T-dependence of R_AA for both Upsilon(1s) and Upsilon(2s), but there is still some lingering tension with ALICE forward-rapidity results for Upsilon(1s) suppression, although this tension is reduced compared to previous model predictions due to an updated centrality-averaging procedure.

## Method

The authors compute bottomonia suppression using a complex-valued heavy quark potential with real part derived from the internal energy and imaginary part from leading-order perturbative calculations, solving the 3D Schrodinger equation to obtain binding energies and thermal widths. The QGP background evolution is modeled using (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics with a spheroidal momentum-space anisotropy parameter, and the instantaneous decay rates are integrated over proper time to compute R_AA for each state. Inclusive R_AA for Upsilon(1s) and Upsilon(2s) are obtained by combining raw suppression factors with updated feed-down fractions from p-p data.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature for central collisions: T_0 in {552, 546, 544} MeV for 4pi eta/s in {1, 2, 3}, tuned to keep final charged particle multiplicity fixed
- Rest frame formation times: 0.2 fm/c (Upsilon(1s)), 0.4 fm/c (Upsilon(2s) and chi_b1), 0.6 fm/c (Upsilon(3s) and chi_b2)
- Feed-down fractions to Upsilon(1s): f_i^{1s} = {0.618, 0.105, 0.02, 0.207, 0.05} for Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(2s), Upsilon(3s), chi_b1, chi_b2 respectively
- Feed-down fractions to Upsilon(2s): f_i^{2s} = {0.5, 0.5} for Upsilon(2s) and Upsilon(3s)
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.3 fm/c
- Inelastic cross-section: sigma_NN = 62 mb
- Binary collision scaling factor: kappa_binary = 0.145

## Context

This work extends the model of Strickland (2011) by upgrading the background evolution to (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics, updating mixing fractions from recent ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb measurements, and correcting the centrality-averaging procedure. The results are compared with preliminary CMS data and ALICE forward-rapidity data from Abelev (2014). The model enables further constraints on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the QGP, finding values consistent with those obtained from collective flow analysis, supporting the picture of a nearly perfect fluid.
