# Spatially inhomogeneous condensate in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the isospin singlet pairing in asymmetric nuclear matter with nonzero total momentum of the condensate Cooper pairs. The quasiparticle excitation spectrum is fourfold split compared to the usual BCS spectrum of the symmetric, homogeneous matter. A twofold splitting of the spectrum into separate branches is due to the finite momentum of the condensate, the isospin asymmetry, or the finite quasiparticle lifetime. The coupling of the isospin singlet and triplet paired states leads to further twofold splitting of each of these branches. We solve the gap equation numerically in the isospin singlet channel in the case where the pairing in the isospin triplet channel is neglected and find nontrivial solutions with finite total momentum of the pairs. The corresponding phase assumes a periodic spatial structure which carries a isospin density wave at constant total number of particles. The phase transition from the BCS to the inhomogeneous superconducting phase is found to be first order and occurs when the density asymmetry is increased above 0.25. The transition from the inhomogeneous superconducting to the unpaired normal state is second order. The maximal values of the critical total momentum (in units of the Fermi momentum) and the critical density asymmetry at which condensate disappears are $P_c/p_F = 0.3$ and $α_c = 0.41$. The possible spatial forms of the ground state of the inhomogeneous superconducting phase are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in asymmetric nuclear matter, the isospin singlet pairing condensate can exist with nonzero total momentum of Cooper pairs, forming a spatially inhomogeneous (LOFF) phase, but this result depends on neglecting pairing in the isospin triplet channel and using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction without screening or single-particle spectrum renormalization.

## Method

The author solves the BCS gap equation numerically in the quasiclassical approximation for neutron-proton pairing in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) partial-wave channel, using a separable form of the Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction (PEST1). The calculation is performed at fixed total density \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and temperature \(\beta^{-1} = 3\) MeV, with the pairing interaction approximated by the bare interaction and quasiparticle effective masses set equal to their bare masses. The gap equation is solved simultaneously with the density normalization condition, and the free energy is minimized to identify the ground state.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap in symmetric matter at zero total momentum: \(\Delta_{00} = 14.38\) MeV at \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\beta^{-1} = 3\) MeV.
- Critical total momentum for vanishing of the condensate in symmetric matter: \(P_{c,0} = 0.558 p_F\).
- Critical isospin asymmetry for vanishing of the condensate at zero total momentum: \(\alpha_{c,0} = 0.37\).
- Onset of the LOFF phase occurs when density asymmetry exceeds \(\alpha = 0.25\).
- Maximum of the gap in the LOFF phase occurs at total momentum \(P \simeq 0.3 p_F\), independent of \(\alpha \ge 0.25\).
- Maximal critical total momentum for condensate disappearance: \(P_c/p_F = 0.3\).
- Maximal critical density asymmetry for condensate disappearance: \(\alpha_c = 0.41\).
- Free energy difference in symmetric zero-momentum case: \((\delta F_{00})_{\rho\beta} = -7.35\) MeV at \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\beta^{-1} = 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase concept, originally developed for metallic superconductors by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964), to asymmetric nuclear matter relevant for neutron stars and supernovae. It builds on earlier suggestions by Sedrakian (previous work) that the ground state of superconducting asymmetric nuclear matter at large asymmetries corresponds to a pair condensate with nonzero total momentum. The results enable further study of the spatial structure of the LOFF phase in nuclear matter, including possible cubic or layered configurations, and have implications for the cooling and rotation dynamics of neutron stars where neutron-proton pairing may occur.
