# Non-mesonic Weak Decays of Light Hypernuclei in the Direct Quark and the One-Pion Exchange Mechanisms

> Contributions of the direct quark mechanism are studied in the nonmesonic weak decays of light hypernuclei. The $ΛN \to NN$ transition is described by the one pion exchange mechanism and the direct quark mechanism, induced by the four-quark vertices in the effective weak Hamiltonian. By employing a realistic wave function of the $Λ$ inside the hypernuclei, nonmesonic decay rates of $^4_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$He, and $^5_Λ$He are calculated. The results show that the direct quark mechanism is significantly large and gives a large $ΔI =3/2$ contribution in the J=0 channel. The relative phase between the one-pion exchange and the direct-quark contributions is determined so that the effective weak Hamiltonian for quarks give both of them consistently. We find that the sum of these two contributions reproduce the current available experimental data fairly well.

## Key claim

The direct quark mechanism contributes significantly to nonmesonic weak decays of light hypernuclei and provides a large ΔI = 3/2 contribution in the J=0 channel, and when summed with the one-pion exchange contribution, the combined result reproduces available experimental data fairly well, though the relative phase between the two mechanisms is determined consistently from the effective weak Hamiltonian for quarks.

## Method

The authors calculated nonmesonic decay rates of ⁴_ΛH, ⁴_ΛHe, and ⁵_ΛHe by evaluating two-body transition matrix elements for the ΛN → NN process using both the one-pion exchange (OPE) mechanism and the direct quark (DQ) mechanism derived from the effective weak Hamiltonian with four-quark vertices. Realistic hypernuclear wave functions were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation variationally using the YNG potential folded from the Nijmegen ΛN potential model D, and the outgoing two-nucleon scattering states were described using the same Nijmegen model D with short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵_ΛHe in OPE with form factor and short-range correlation: total nonmesonic decay rate Γ_nm = 0.216 Γ_Λ (where Γ_Λ is the free Λ decay rate), with Γ_p = 0.191 Γ_Λ and Γ_n = 0.025 Γ_Λ, giving n-p ratio R_np = 0.130
- For ⁵_ΛHe in DQ with short-range correlation parameter C=0.5: total nonmesonic decay rate Γ_nm = 0.237 Γ_Λ, with Γ_p = 0.125 Γ_Λ and Γ_n = 0.112 Γ_Λ, giving n-p ratio R_np = 0.903
- The constituent quark mass used was m = 313 MeV (M_N/3) and the Gaussian parameter b = 0.5 fm
- The YNG potential wave function for ⁵_ΛHe gave a root-mean-square distance between α and Λ of 3.06 fm, compared to 2.69 fm for the one-range Gaussian potential
- The ground state energy obtained for ⁵_ΛHe was E = -3.08 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the direct quark mechanism for ΛN → NN transitions (Inoue, Oka, and Takeuchi, 1995-1997) by applying the DQ transition potential to actual hypernuclei rather than free two-baryon systems. It builds on earlier OPE-only calculations by other groups (Adams, 1967; Block and Dalitz, 1963; McKellar and Gibson, 1984; Ramos et al., 1997) and uses the realistic YNG wave functions developed by Motoba, Itonaga, and Yamamoto (1994) that previously improved agreement for pionic decays. The results enable future studies of heavier hypernuclei and suggest that both short-range quark effects and long-range pion exchange must be included to reproduce the observed neutron-to-proton ratio in nonmesonic decays.
