# Carroll hydrodynamics with spin

> We formulate Carroll hydrodynamics with the inclusion of a spin current. Our strategy relies on the fact that the $c\to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics yields the equations of Carroll hydrodynamics. Starting with the pre-ultralocal parametrization of the background geometry and the hydrodynamic degrees of freedom for a relativistic fluid endowed with a spin current, the $c\to 0$ limit produces Carroll hydrodynamics with spin. It is known that boost-invariant hydrodynamic models for ultrarelativistic fluids relevant for the physics of quark-gluon plasma, such as Bjorken and Gubser flow, are manifestations of Carroll hydrodynamics under appropriate geometric choices for the underlying Carrollian structure. In this work, we further this mapping between such boost-invariant models and Carroll hydrodynamics, now with the inclusion of a spin current.

## Key claim
The authors formulate Carroll hydrodynamics with a spin current by taking the \(c\to 0\) limit of relativistic spin hydrodynamics, and show that boost-invariant models of quark-gluon plasma (Bjorken and Gubser flow) extended to include spin are specific geometric realizations of this Carrollian framework.

## Method
Starting from the pre-ultralocal (PUL) parametrization of the background Lorentzian geometry and the fluid degrees of freedom (including the spin current), the authors impose the \(c\to 0\) limit on the conservation equations for the energy-momentum tensor and spin current of an ideal relativistic fluid. The resulting equations describe an ideal Carroll fluid with spin on a general Carrollian manifold. The mapping to Bjorken and Gubser flow with spin is then established by choosing appropriate Carroll geometric data and fluid variables.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma is \(\eta/s \approx 0.1 - 0.2\) just above the deconfinement temperature, close to the conjectured Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound of \(\eta/s \ge 1/4\pi\).
- The Levi-Civita connection decomposition includes terms of order \(c^{-2}\), \(c^0\), and \(c^2\) relative to the PUL connection, with explicit expressions for \(\overset{\tiny{(-2)}}{\G}{}^{\m}_{\n\lambda}\), \(C^{\m}_{\n\lambda}\), and \(\overset{\tiny{(2)}}{\G}{}^{\m}_{\n\lambda}\).
- The fluid four-velocity normalization condition in the Carroll limit becomes \(\hat{\mathfrak{u}}^\mu \vec{\mathfrak{u}}_\mu + 2 \hat{\mathfrak{u}}^\mu \ell_\mu = 0\), reducing the degrees of freedom in \(\hat{\mathfrak{u}}^\mu\) to three.

## Context
This work extends the mapping between Carroll hydrodynamics and boost-invariant heavy-ion collision models (Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1983) and Gubser flow (Gubser, 2010)) established by Bagchi et al. (2023) to include a spin current, which is relevant for explaining spin polarization of hyperons observed at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2017, 2018). It builds on prior formulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics by Gallegos et al. (2021), Hongo et al. (2021), and others, and on the pre-ultralocal parametrization approach of Hansen et al. (2021) and Armas et al. (2023). The work enables future studies of spin transport in Carrollian fluids and potential connections to condensed matter systems where spin hydrodynamics may produce observable effects.
