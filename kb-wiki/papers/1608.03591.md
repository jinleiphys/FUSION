# Particle Physics Models for the 17 MeV Anomaly in Beryllium Nuclear Decays

> The 6.8$σ$ anomaly in excited 8Be nuclear decays via internal pair creation is fit well by a new particle interpretation. In a previous analysis, we showed that a 17 MeV protophobic gauge boson provides a particle physics explanation of the anomaly consistent with all existing constraints. Here we begin with a review of the physics of internal pair creation in 8Be decays and the characteristics of the observed anomaly. To develop its particle interpretation, we provide an effective operator analysis for excited 8Be decays to particles with a variety of spins and parities and show that these considerations exclude simple models with scalar particles. We discuss the required couplings for a gauge boson to give the observed signal, highlighting the significant dependence on the precise mass of the boson and isospin mixing and breaking effects. We present anomaly-free extensions of the Standard Model that contain protophobic gauge bosons with the desired couplings to explain the 8Be anomaly. In the first model, the new force carrier is a U(1)B gauge boson that kinetically mixes with the photon; in the second model, it is a U(1)(B-L) gauge boson with a similar kinetic mixing. In both cases, the models predict relatively large charged lepton couplings ~ 0.001 that can resolve the discrepancy in the muon anomalous magnetic moment and are amenable to many experimental probes. The models also contain vectorlike leptons at the weak scale that may be accessible to near future LHC searches.

## Key claim

The 6.8σ anomaly in excited 8Be nuclear decays via internal pair creation is best explained by a new 17 MeV protophobic gauge boson, but the authors show that this interpretation depends critically on the precise mass of the boson and on isospin mixing and breaking effects in the 8Be nucleus, and that simple scalar particle models are excluded by parity considerations.

## Method

The authors performed an effective operator analysis for excited 8Be decays to new particles with various spins and parities, considering scalar, pseudoscalar, axial vector, and vector candidates. They then constructed anomaly-free extensions of the Standard Model containing protophobic gauge bosons, specifically a U(1)B gauge boson and a U(1)(B-L) gauge boson, each with kinetic mixing with the photon, and evaluated the required couplings against constraints from other experiments including π0 decays, beam dump searches, and neutrino scattering.

## Key numbers

- The anomaly in the opening angle distribution of electron-positron pairs from excited 8Be decays has a statistical significance of 6.8σ
- The best fit mass for the new particle is m_X = 16.7 ± 0.35 (stat) ± 0.5 (sys) MeV
- The best fit branching fraction ratio is Γ(8Be* → 8Be X) / Γ(8Be* → 8Be γ) × Br(X → e+e-) = 5.8 × 10^(-6)
- The fit to the new particle interpretation has χ²/dof = 1.07
- The models predict charged lepton couplings ∼ 0.001 that can resolve the discrepancy in the muon anomalous magnetic moment
- The 8Be* state has excitation energy 18.15 MeV and the 8Be*' state has excitation energy 17.64 MeV
- The branching fraction Br(8Be* → 8Be γ) ≈ 1.4 × 10^(-5)
- The ratio Br(8Be* → 8Be e+e-) / Br(8Be* → 8Be γ) ≈ 3.9 × 10^(-3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Feng et al., 2016) which first proposed a protophobic gauge boson explanation for the 8Be anomaly observed by Krasznahorkay et al. (2015) at the Atomki pair spectrometer. The paper provides a more detailed effective operator analysis that excludes scalar and pseudoscalar candidates, and presents two explicit anomaly-free Standard Model extensions. The models predict vectorlike leptons at the weak scale that may be accessible to near future LHC searches, and the relatively large charged lepton couplings can simultaneously address the muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy.
