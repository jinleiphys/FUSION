# Antisymmetrized random phase approximation for quasielastic scattering in nuclear matter: Non-relativistic potentials

> Many-body techniques for the calculation of quasielastic nuclear matter response functions in the fully antisymmetrized random phase approximation on a Hartree-Fock basis are discussed in detail. The methods presented here allow for an accurate evaluation of the response functions with little numerical effort. Formulae are given for a generic non-relativistic potential parameterized in terms of meson exchanges; on the other hand, relativistic kinematical effects have been accounted for.

## Key claim

The paper provides a detailed semi-analytical framework for calculating fully antisymmetrized random phase approximation (RPA) response functions in nuclear matter using non-relativistic potentials, with the caveat that the continued fraction expansion's convergence can only be tested by direct comparison between successive orders rather than by a general convergence theorem.

## Method

The author develops the polarization propagator for nuclear matter starting from the Galitskii-Migdal integral equation for the particle-hole four-point Green's function, then derives explicit expressions for the free Fermi gas response, the Hartree-Fock (HF) response using a biparabolic approximation for the self-energy, and the RPA response using a continued fraction expansion technique. The formalism accommodates a generic one-boson-exchange potential parameterized in terms of meson exchanges, with relativistic kinematical effects incorporated through a scaling variable transformation and Jacobian factors. The continued fraction expansion is pushed to second order to allow quantitative testing of the approximation's convergence.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum \(k_F = 195\) MeV/c used in the test calculations for the real part of the free polarization propagator
- The free polarization propagator \(\Pi^{(0)}(q,\omega)\) is evaluated at momentum transfers \(q = 500\) MeV/c and \(q = 1000\) MeV/c to test the relativistic approximation
- The non-relativistic scaling variable is defined as \(\psi_{\text{nr}} = \frac{1}{k_F}\left(\frac{\omega m_N}{q}-\frac{q}{2}\right)\)
- The relativistic scaling variable is defined as \(\psi_{\text{r}} = \frac{1}{k_F}\left[\frac{\omega m_N(1+\omega/2m_N)}{q}-\frac{q}{2}\right]\)
- The free response function is expressed as \(\Pi^{(0)}(q,\omega) = \frac{m_N}{q}\frac{k_F^2}{(2\pi)^2}\left[{\cal Q}^{(0)}(\psi)-{\cal Q}^{(0)}(\psi+\bar{q})\right]\) where \(\bar{q}=q/k_F\)

## Context

This work extends the continued fraction technique previously applied to finite nuclei (De Pace 1985, 1987) and nuclear matter (Alberico 1993, Barbaro 1994, 1996a, 1996b) by pushing the analytical calculation to second order in the CF expansion, enabling quantitative assessment of convergence. The formalism enables future studies of other many-body effects in quasielastic scattering by providing a computationally efficient starting point, and complements field-theoretical approaches such as the Walecka model (Walecka 1995) and alternative RPA methods (Shiomi 1989, Buballa 1991).
