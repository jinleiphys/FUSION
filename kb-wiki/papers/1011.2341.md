# Towards Microscopic Ab Initio Calculations of Astrophysical S-Factors

> Low energy capture cross sections are calculated within a microscopic many-body approach using an effective Hamiltonian derived from the Argonne V18 potential. The dynamics is treated within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) which uses a Gaussian wave-packet basis to represent the many-body states. A phase-shift equivalent effective interaction derived within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) that treats explicitly short-range central and tensor correlations is employed. As a first application the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be reaction is presented. Within the FMD approach the microscopic many-body wave functions of the 3/2- and 1/2- bound states in 7Be as well as the many-body scattering states in the 1/2+, 3/2+ and 5/2+ channels are calculated as eigenstates of the same microscopic effective Hamiltonian. Finally the S-factor is calculated from E1 transition matrix elements between the many-body scattering and bound states. For 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be the S-factor agrees very well, both in absolute normalization and energy dependence, with the recent experimental data from the Weizmann, LUNA, Seattle and ERNA experiments. For the 3H(alpha,gamma)7Li reaction the calculated S-factor is about 15% above the data.

## Key claim

The authors present the first *ab initio* calculation of the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be astrophysical S-factor using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, achieving agreement with recent experimental data without any adjustable parameters; however, the calculation for the analogous ³H(α,γ)⁷Li reaction overestimates the measured S-factor by about 15%.

## Method

The authors use Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) with a Gaussian wave-packet basis to construct fully antisymmetrized many-body wave functions for both bound and scattering states of the ⁷Be and ⁷Li systems. The effective Hamiltonian is derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), which treats short-range central and tensor correlations while preserving nucleon-nucleon phase shifts. The many-body Hilbert space includes both "frozen" cluster configurations (antisymmetrized products of ³He and ⁴He ground states at various separations) and polarized configurations obtained by variation after parity and angular momentum projection (VAP), with the microscopic R-matrix approach of the Brussels group used to match the relative wave functions to asymptotic Coulomb scattering solutions.

## Key numbers

- The ³He(α,γ)⁷Be S-factor agrees very well in absolute normalization and energy dependence with experimental data from the Weizmann Institute, LUNA collaboration, Seattle, and ERNA collaboration.
- For the ³H(α,γ)⁷Li reaction, the calculated S-factor is about 15% above the experimental data.
- With frozen configurations alone, the 3/2⁻ and 1/2⁻ states in ⁷Be are bound by only 240 keV and 10 keV, respectively; in the full Hilbert space including polarized configurations, the binding energies are 1.49 MeV and 1.31 MeV with respect to the cluster threshold.
- The calculated ⁷Be charge radius is 2.67 fm, in good agreement with the experimental value of 2.647(17) fm (Noertershaeuser 2009).

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster model calculations (Langanke 1986, Kajino 1986) and potential model approaches (Kim 1981, Mohr 2009) by performing a consistent *ab initio* calculation starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, which had not been possible previously. Earlier attempts using Variational Monte Carlo (Nollett 2001) and the No-Core Shell Model (Navratil 2007) only calculated asymptotic normalization coefficients from bound state wave functions. The approach enables future investigations of how composite polarizable many-body systems perform quantum tunneling, and raises the question of why the microscopic FMD model with a realistic nuclear force describes the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be data while other models fail.
