# The influence of the neutron skin and the asymmetry energy on the $π^-/π^+$ ratio

> We use the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (IQMD) to analyze the centrality dependence of the isospin ratio of pions, $π^-/π^+$. We find that the density dependence of the asymmetry potential, the Pauli blocking of the $Δ$-decay and the thickness of the neutron skin influence in different ways this observable. Using the centrality dependence of this ratio at different beam energies we can disentangle the different contributions and open the way for their experimental determination.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions can disentangle the contributions from the neutron skin thickness, the density dependence of the asymmetry energy, and the Pauli blocking of Δ-decay, provided that measurements are performed at multiple beam energies and centralities.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb at beam energies of 400 AMeV and 1 AGeV. They modified the nuclear initialization to vary the neutron skin thickness by assigning different radii for proton and neutron distributions, ranging from equal rms radii to a neutron skin of up to 0.4 fm. They then computed the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a function of impact parameter for each initialization, and separately varied the stiffness of the asymmetry energy (via the density exponent γ) and the treatment of Pauli blocking in Δ decay.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb at 400 AMeV, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central collisions is higher than at 1 AGeV, and differences between the equal-radii and neutron-skin initializations emerge even at central collisions.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca at 1 AGeV, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central collisions approaches a value near 1.75 (from a combinatorial estimate), while for ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb it approaches 2.
- The neutron skin thickness s was varied between 0.1 and 0.4 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb, with a typical value around 0.2 to 0.3 fm.
- At 400 AMeV, the asymmetry energy (asy-eos) influences the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central collisions, but the effect vanishes in peripheral collisions; at 1 AGeV, the differences vanish for all centralities.
- Pauli blocking of Δ decay enhances the π⁻/π⁺ ratio significantly at 400 AMeV for all centralities in ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb, but has no important role at 1 AGeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Hong and Danielewicz (2013), who introduced a momentum-dependent mean field to reproduce the π⁻/π⁺ ratio above 700 AMeV but found no dependence on the asymmetry energy at or below 400 AMeV. It also addresses conflicting results from Feng and Jin (2009), who found a strong dependence at 400 AMeV, and from Wei (2013), who found the neutron skin effect negligible for central collisions. The proposed centrality-dependent analysis enables simultaneous extraction of the neutron skin thickness (from peripheral collisions) and the density dependence of the asymmetry energy (from central collisions at lower beam energies), which are key inputs for understanding neutron star mergers and the mass-radius relation of neutron stars.
