# Role of final state interaction and of three-body force on the longitudinal response function of 4He

> We present an ab-initio calculation of the longitudinal electron scattering response function off 4He with two- and three-nucleon forces and compare to experimental data. The full four-body continuum dynamics is considered via the Lorentz integral transform method. The importance of the final state interaction is shown at various energies and momentum transfers q. The three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak by 10% for q between 300 and 500 MeV/c. Its effect increases significantly at lower q, up to about 40% at q=100 MeV/c. At very low q, however, data are missing.

## Key claim

The three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak of the longitudinal response function of 4He by 10% for momentum transfers between 300 and 500 MeV/c, but its effect increases significantly at lower momentum transfers, up to about 40% at q=100 MeV/c; however, at very low q experimental data are missing for comparison.

## Method

The authors performed an ab-initio calculation of the longitudinal electron scattering response function for 4He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, which allows full treatment of the four-body continuum dynamics. They employed the Arvonne V18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential with and without the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon force, expanding the wave functions in hyperspherical harmonics with a Kmax-dependent effective interaction (EIHH method). The response function was obtained by inverting the Lorentz integral transform, with the charge density operator expanded in Coulomb multipoles up to Jmax values ranging from 2 to 7 for q from 50 to 500 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Three-nucleon force reduces the quasi-elastic peak height by 10% for q between 300 and 500 MeV/c
- Three-nucleon force effect increases to about 26-27% reduction at q=100 MeV/c and q=50 MeV/c
- At q=100 MeV/c, the peak height with AV18 is 9.56 x 10^-3 MeV^-1 and with AV18+UIX is 7.11 x 10^-3 MeV^-1
- At q=500 MeV/c, the peak height with AV18 is 4.84 x 10^-3 MeV^-1 and with AV18+UIX is 4.36 x 10^-3 MeV^-1
- Ground state binding energy: 28.4 MeV with AV18+UIX, 24.3 MeV with AV18 alone
- The HH expansion used Kmax values of 12-16 for even J and 13-17 for odd J
- The smallest sigma_I value used in the LIT calculation was 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the longitudinal response function for 4He that used only central two-nucleon forces (ELO97, Sonia07) by incorporating realistic two-body potentials with three-nucleon forces. The LIT method had previously been applied to electroweak reactions in three-body systems (ELOT2000, Golak2002, ELOT2004, ELOT2005, Sara2008) and four-body systems (Doron2006, Doron2007). The results demonstrate that both final state interaction and three-nucleon forces are essential for agreement with experimental data, and suggest that the longitudinal response function at low momentum transfers (q <= 200 MeV/c) could serve as an electromagnetic observable to study three-nucleon forces, complementing purely hadronic probes.
