# Beyond the mean field in the particle-vibration coupling scheme

> The Energy Density Functional theory is one of the most used methods developed in nuclear structure. It is based on the assumption that the energy of the ground state is a functional only of the density profile. The method is extremely successful within the effective force approach, noticeably the Skyrme or Gogny forces, in reproducing the nuclear binding energies and other bulk properties along the whole mass table. Although the Density Functional is in this case represented formally as the Hartree-Fock mean field of an effective force, the corresponding single-particle states in general do not reproduce the phenomenology particularly well. To overcome this difficulty, a strategy has been developed where the effective force is adjusted to reproduce directly the single particle energies, trying to keep the ground state energy sufficiently well reproduced. An alternative route, that has been developed along several years, for solving this problem is to introduce the mean field fluctuations, as represented by the collective vibrations of the nuclear system, and their influence on the single particle dynamics and structure. This is the basis of the particle-vibration coupling model. In this paper we present a formal theory of the particle-vibration coupling model based on the Green' s function method. The theory extends to realistic effective forces the macroscopic particle-vibration coupling models and the (microscopic) Nuclear Field Theory. It is formalized within the functional derivative approach to many-body theory. An expansion in diagrams is devised for the single particle self-energy and the phonon propagator. Critical aspects of the particle-vibration coupling model are analysed in general. Applications at the lowest order of the expansion are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formal theory of the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model based on Green's function methods that extends macroscopic PVC models and the microscopic Nuclear Field Theory to realistic effective forces, but the approach is limited to cases where the phonons are assumed to be of small amplitude when density-dependent forces are used.

## Method

The authors derive coupled integral equations for the single-particle self-energy, Green's function, phonon propagator, and vertex function using the functional derivative approach to many-body theory. They propose a solution by iteration that generates a diagrammatic expansion, and apply the formalism at the lowest order to the nucleus ⁴⁰Ca using both density-independent (SV) and density-dependent Skyrme forces.

## Key numbers

- Calculations are performed for the nucleus ⁴⁰Ca, comparing results for a density-independent force (SV) and a density-dependent Skyrme force.
- The paper references Tables 4.3a and 4.3b of Ref. [CM] which contain an extensive review of results obtained in ²⁰⁸Pb by nine groups in the period 1968-1983.
- Recent fully self-consistent calculations cited include those for ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb in Ref. [Colo2010,inpress].

## Context

This work extends the particle-vibration coupling approach beyond phenomenological inputs used in earlier calculations (e.g., harmonic oscillator, Woods-Saxon, or Hartree-Fock potentials with various residual interactions as reviewed in Ref. [CM]) toward fully self-consistent treatments. It builds on the functional derivative method previously used in Refs. [LNC, HedLun, Hed] and provides a framework that can be compared with recent relativistic calculations by Ring and Afanasjev (Refs. [Ring, Afan1, Afan2]) and non-relativistic self-consistent calculations by Colo et al. (Ref. [Colo2010,inpress]). The formalism enables systematic inclusion of vertex corrections and conserving approximations beyond the standard RPA.
