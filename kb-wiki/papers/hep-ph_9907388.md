# A Review of pQCD Calculations of Electromagnetic Form Factors of Hadrons

> We review the current status of perturbative QCD calculation of hadronic electromagnetic form factors.

# A Review of pQCD Calculations of Electromagnetic Form Factors of Hadrons

## Key claim
The paper argues that while the normalization of hadronic electromagnetic form factors cannot be reliably predicted by leading-order perturbative QCD calculations, the asymptotic power-law dependence on momentum transfer \(Q^2\) is a robust and detailed dynamical prediction that agrees with data for \(Q\) as small as 2-3 GeV, independent of the choice of distribution amplitude and other theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors review the Brodsky-LePage factorization scheme for exclusive processes, which separates the process into a perturbatively calculable hard scattering kernel and a soft distribution amplitude. They then examine an improved factorization by Botts, Sterman, Li, and Sterman that retains the transverse momentum (\(k_T\)) dependence of the hard scattering and incorporates Sudakov form factors via renormalization group resummation of leading and next-to-leading logarithms, which renders the calculation infrared finite without an arbitrary cutoff. For the proton Dirac form factor \(F_1^p(Q^2)\), the authors discuss the choice of infrared cutoff parameter \(w\) in the Sudakov exponent and the use of model distribution amplitudes such as King-Sachrajda (KS) and Chernyak-Zhitnitsky (CZ).

## Key numbers
- The Sudakov form factor \(\exp(-S)\) is plotted for \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) with the QCD scale parameter \(\Lambda = 0.1\) GeV.
- For the pion form factor, the correct asymptotic \(Q^2\) behavior is seen beyond the scale of about \(Q = 1\) GeV, irrespective of the choice of wave function.
- For the proton, using the KS distribution amplitude with \(c = 1.14\) (where \(w = c/b_{max}\) and \(b_{max} = \max(b_l)\) for \(l=1,2,3\)) gives good agreement with data; the \(b_c\) dependence shows saturation at about \(b_c = 0.8/\Lambda\) with \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV.
- A model nonperturbative soft wave function with \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.271\) GeV\(^2\) is included in the proton calculation, and results with \(c = 1.4, 1.2, 1.0\) are shown at \(Q^2 = 35\) GeV\(^2\).
- Roughly 50% of the pion form factor contribution is regarded as perturbative, coming from the region where \(\alpha_s/\pi < 0.7\).

## Context
This review extends the Brodsky-LePage (1979) factorization approach to exclusive processes, which had been criticized because the momentum scales of exchanged gluons become small, making pQCD applicability doubtful. The work builds on the improved factorization by Botts and Sterman (1989) and Li and Sterman (1992) that includes Sudakov form factors and \(k_T\) dependence. For the proton, the paper addresses a controversy: Bolz et al. (1996) concluded that pQCD cannot fit the experimental form factor, but Kundu et al. (1997) reexamined the situation with a modified infrared cutoff and found agreement with data using the KS distribution amplitude. The review enables future work by establishing that the power-law dependence of form factors is a robust prediction, while emphasizing that higher-order calculations are needed for reliable normalizations.
