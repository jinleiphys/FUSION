# Neutrino Processes with Hot Nuclei in Supernovae

> In this paper, we calculate cross sections for charged-current neutrino-nucleus processes occuring under presupernova conditions. To treat thermal effects we extend self-consistent Skyrme-QRPA calculations to finite temperature by using the formalism of thermo field dynamics. The numerical results are presented for the sample nuclei, $^{56}$Fe and $^{82}$Ge

## Key claim

The paper calculates charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for presupernova conditions by extending self-consistent Skyrme-QRPA calculations to finite temperature using thermo field dynamics, avoiding reliance on the Brink hypothesis that the abstract does not mention as a limitation of prior approaches.

## Method

The authors extended the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) to finite temperature using the thermo field dynamics formalism (TQRPA), combined with the Skyrme energy density functional (SLy4, SGII, and SkM* parametrizations). They computed the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength functions for GT− and GT+ transitions in ⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge at zero temperature and at T = 1.72 MeV, then used these thermal strength functions to calculate cross sections for (νₑ, e⁻) and (ν̅ₑ, e⁺) reactions. The calculations account for electron chemical potential effects in the collapsing core.

## Key numbers

- GT strength functions were calculated at T = 0 and T = 1.72 MeV (2 × 10¹⁰ K) for ⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge using the SLy4 Skyrme force.
- For ⁸²Ge, the temperature rise lowers the GT₊ resonance peak by about 8 MeV.
- For ⁵⁶Fe, negative-energy GT₊ transitions from thermally excited states dominate the antineutrino absorption cross section for E(ν̅) < 5 MeV.
- The spread in cross sections computed with different Skyrme forces (SLy4, SGII, SkM*) is less than an order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the TQRPA approach previously applied to neutral-current reactions (Dzhioev, 2015) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus processes, and contrasts with large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations by Sampaio (2002) that partially rely on the Brink hypothesis and are limited to iron-group nuclei (A ≤ 65). The Skyrme-TQRPA method enables calculations for heavier, neutron-rich nuclei like ⁸²Ge that are important in core-collapse supernovae, and the results show that different Skyrme force parametrizations yield cross sections within an order of magnitude, suggesting robustness of the predictions.
