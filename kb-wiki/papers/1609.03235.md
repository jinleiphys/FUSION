# Possible strange hidden-charm pentaquarks from $Σ_c^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^*$ and $Ξ^{(',*)}_c\bar{D}^*$ interactions

> Using the one-boson-exchange model, we investigate the $Λ_c\bar{D}_s^*$, $Σ_c\bar{D}_s^*$, $Σ_c^*\bar{D}_s^*$, $Ξ_c\bar{D}^*$, $Ξ_c'\bar{D}^*$, and $Ξ_c^*\bar{D}^*$ interactions by considering the one-eta-exchange and/or one-pion-exchange contributions. We further predict the existence of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks. Promising candidates for hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks include a $Ξ_c'\bar{D}^*$ state with $0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ and the $Ξ_c^*\bar{D}^*$ states with $0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ and $0(\frac{3}{2}^-)$. Experimental searches for these predicted hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks are an interesting future research topic for experiments like LHCb.

## Key claim

Using the one-boson-exchange model, the authors predict the existence of strange hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks from the $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}_s^*$ and $\Xi^{(',*)}_c\bar{D}^*$ interactions, but the bound state solutions for the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}_s^*$ and $\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}_s^*$ systems require cutoff values larger than the typical estimate of 1-1.5 GeV, making their existence less certain than the $\Xi_c'\bar{D}^*$ and $\Xi_c^*\bar{D}^*$ states.

## Method

The authors employed the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to derive effective potentials for the $\Lambda_c\bar{D}_s^*$, $\Sigma_c\bar{D}_s^*$, $\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}_s^*$, $\Xi_c\bar{D}^*$, $\Xi_c'\bar{D}^*$, and $\Xi_c^*\bar{D}^*$ systems, considering one-pion-exchange (OPE) and one-eta-exchange (OEE) contributions. They constructed spin-orbit wave functions including S-wave and D-wave mixing, and solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation to find bound state solutions (binding energy and root-mean-square radius) by scanning the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ from 0.6 GeV to 5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}_s^*$ system with $I(J^P)=1(\frac{1}{2}^-)$: at $\Lambda=2.88$ GeV, binding energy $E=-0.53$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=3.78$ fm; at $\Lambda=3.08$ GeV, $E=-15.43$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=0.78$ fm.
- For the $\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}_s^*$ system with $I(J^P)=1(\frac{1}{2}^-)$: at $\Lambda=2.50$ GeV, $E=-1.59$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=2.31$ fm; at $\Lambda=2.60$ GeV, $E=-9.29$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=0.98$ fm.
- For the $\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}_s^*$ system with $I(J^P)=1(\frac{3}{2}^-)$: at $\Lambda=3.75$ GeV, $E=-0.32$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=4.52$ fm; at $\Lambda=3.95$ GeV, $E=-7.86$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=1.09$ fm.
- For the $\Xi_c'\bar{D}^*$ system with $I(J^P)=0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ (OPE only): at $\Lambda=1.14$ GeV, $E=-0.20$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=5.04$ fm; at $\Lambda=1.38$ GeV, $E=-13.02$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=0.97$ fm.
- For the $\Xi_c'\bar{D}^*$ system with $I(J^P)=0(\frac{1}{2}^-)$ (OPE and OEE combined): at $\Lambda=1.12$ GeV, $E=-0.43$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=4.18$ fm; at $\Lambda=1.32$ GeV, $E=-12.14$ MeV and $r_{RMS}=1.00$ fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks, such as the OBE model analysis of $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ by Chen et al. (2015, 2016) and the investigation of charm-strange molecular pentaquarks by Chen et al. (2016). It also builds on earlier theoretical predictions of strange hidden-charm pentaquarks by Wu et al. (2010, 2011) and Hofmann et al. (2005). The predicted $\Xi_c'\bar{D}^*$ and $\Xi_c^*\bar{D}^*$ molecular states with binding energies accessible at cutoff values around 1-1.5 GeV provide promising candidates for experimental searches at LHCb, particularly in channels such as $\Lambda_b\rightarrow J/\psi\Lambda(\pi^+\pi^-/\eta)$ as suggested by Karliner and Rosner (2016).
