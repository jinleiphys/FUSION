# Nucleon Polarizibilities for Virtual Photons

> We generalize the sum rules for the nucleon electric plus magnetic polarizability $Σ=α+β$ and for the nucleon spin-polarizability $γ$, to virtual photons with $Q^2>0$. The dominant low energy cross sections are represented in our calculation by one-pion-loop graphs of relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory and the $Δ(1232)$-resonance excitation. For the proton we find good agreement of the calculated $Σ_p(Q^2)$ with empirical values obtained from integrating up electroproduction data for $Q^2<0.4 GeV^2$. The proton spin-polarizability $γ_p(Q^2)$ switches sign around $Q^2= 0.4 GeV^2$ and it joins smoothly the "partonic" curve, extracted from polarized deep-inelastic scattering, around $Q^2=0.7 GeV^2$. For the neutron our predictions of $Σ_n(Q^2)$ and $γ_n(Q^2)$ agree reasonably well at $Q^2=0$ with existing determinations. Upcoming (polarized) electroproduction experiments will be able to test the generalized polarizability sum rules investigated here.

# Nucleon Polarizabilities for Virtual Photons

## Key claim
The authors generalize the Baldin sum rule for the electric plus magnetic polarizability Σ = α + β and the spin-polarizability γ to virtual photons with Q² > 0, finding that the low-energy hadronic calculation (pion-loops plus Δ-resonance) matches the downward-extrapolated partonic prediction at an intermediate scale Q² ≈ 0.5–0.7 GeV², but this matching is approximate and the neutron Σ_n(0) is overestimated by about 20% compared to the empirical value.

## Method
The authors compute the generalized polarizabilities Σ(Q²) and γ(Q²) by evaluating all 52 one-pion loop diagrams of relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory and the relativistic Δ(1232)-resonance excitation tree graph using the Rarita-Schwinger formalism. The off-shell parameter Y of the ΔNγ vertex is fixed to Y = 0 by requiring Σ_p(0) = 13.7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, and a phenomenological Δ → Nγ* transition form factor G_Δ(Q²) extracted from pion electroproduction data is employed. For the high-Q² partonic side, the integrals over the structure functions F₁(x) and g₁(x) are evaluated using existing parametrizations at Q² = 1 GeV².

## Key numbers
- Proton Σ_p(0) = (5.48 + 8.23) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ = 13.71 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, where the first number is from pion-loops and the second from Δ-excitation
- Neutron Σ_n(0) = (8.90 + 8.23) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ = 17.13 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, overestimating the empirical value (14.40 ± 0.66) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ by about 20%
- Proton spin-polarizability γ_p(0) = -1.34 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ and neutron γ_n(0) = -0.38 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ from semi-empirical determinations using pion photoproduction multipoles
- The proton spin-polarizability γ_p(Q²) switches sign around Q² = 0.4 GeV² and joins the partonic curve around Q² = 0.7 GeV²
- The integrals ∫₀¹ dx 2xF₁(x)/(1-x) evaluated at Q² = 1 GeV² give 0.453 for the proton and 0.313 for the neutron
- The integrals ∫₀¹ dx x² g₁(x)/(1-x) evaluated at Q² = 1 GeV² give 2.72 × 10⁻² for the proton and -1.52 × 10⁻³ for the neutron

## Context
This work extends the Baldin sum rule and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule framework to virtual photons, building on the real-photon calculations of chiral perturbation theory (Weise and collaborators, 1997) and the Δ(1232)-resonance treatment. The approach enables future tests using upcoming polarized electroproduction experiments at MAMI (Mainz), ELSA (Bonn), and TJNAF (Newport News), which will measure the helicity cross sections σ_{1/2,3/2}(ω, Q²) needed to evaluate the generalized sum rules empirically. The matching between the hadronic and partonic descriptions at intermediate Q² suggests that these integrated quantities are not highly sensitive to details of the nucleon excitation spectrum.
