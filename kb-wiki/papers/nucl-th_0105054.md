# In-medium broadening of nucleon resonances

> We analyze the effects of an in-medium broadening of nucleon resonances on the exclusive photoproduction of mesons on nuclei as well as on the total photoabsorption cross sections in a transport calculation. We show that the resonance widths observed in semi-inclusive photoproduction on nuclei are insensitive to an in-medium broadening of nucleon resonances. This is due to a simple effect: the sizeable width of the nuclear surface and Fermi motion.

## Key claim

The width of the resonant contribution observed in semi-inclusive single π⁰ photoproduction on nuclei is insensitive to an in-medium broadening of nucleon resonances, even though total photoabsorption cross sections directly reflect such broadening. This insensitivity arises from a purely geometric effect: the nuclear surface region dominates the semi-inclusive yield, and the density-dependent in-medium width shifts the production region outward to lower densities, keeping the observed width approximately constant.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel BUU (CBUU) transport calculation for semi-inclusive single π⁰ photoproduction on ⁴⁰Ca, using a relativistic spectral function for the D₁₃(1520) resonance with a density-dependent in-medium width Γ_med = Γ_m · ρ(r)/ρ₀. They also constructed an analytic folding model (Thomas-Fermi approximation) without final-state interactions to isolate the geometric effect, computing the cross section by integrating the elementary (γ,π) cross section over the nuclear volume and Fermi momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium width parameter Γ_m = 0.3 GeV was used as an example of strong medium modification, representing the summed effect of ρ meson mass shift, collisional broadening, and other density-dependent effects.
- In the CBUU calculation including broadening, only about 10–20% of all single π⁰ in the second resonance region stem from D₁₃(1520) decay.
- The observed full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the resonance in the one-pion photoproduction cross section on ⁴⁰Ca rises from 0.3 GeV at Γ_m = 0 to about 0.33 GeV at Γ_m = 300 MeV, then stays roughly constant, a change too small to be observed in data.
- Without Fermi motion, the FWHM levels off at a somewhat higher value than with Fermi motion included.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between the disappearance of resonance structures in total photoabsorption on nuclei (observed by Bianchi et al.) and the finding by Krusche et al. (2000) that the D₁₃(1520) width in semi-inclusive π⁰ production on nuclei is compatible with the free width when smeared over Fermi motion. Kondratyuk et al. and Alberico et al. had previously shown that the photoabsorption disappearance could be explained by an extra in-medium width of about 0.3 GeV. The present paper demonstrates that semi-inclusive and inclusive processes sample the resonance at different nuclear densities (surface vs. volume), explaining why photoabsorption scales with A while semi-inclusive π⁰ cross sections scale with A²/³. This finding has implications for planned photoproduction experiments searching for in-medium changes of hadronic properties.
