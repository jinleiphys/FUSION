# First non-zero measurement of a nuclear electric dipole moment

> This paper reports the first non-zero measurement of a nuclear electric dipole moment using a novel method based on the rate of change of a supercurrent first proposed in 2016~\cite{https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.1604.02152} and fleshed out in this current paper. The theory, experimental concept and implementation are described in detail. The non-zero nuclear electric dipole moment measured with over 1000 hours of data was that of $^{181}$Ta producing a best value $|d_\text{e}^\text{Ta}|=(3.39\pm0.31_\text{stat})\cdot10^{-32}e\cdot\text{cm}$ and $|d_\text{e}^\text{Ta}|=(3.39\pm3.18)\cdot10^{-32}e\cdot\text{cm}>0$ at 99.985\%CL. There is an uncertainty on the value of overall multiplicative parameters such as the self-inductance of the superconducting circuit ($\pm4\%$), the mutual inductance between the SQUID pickup coil and the sample wire ($\pm15\%$), and the magnitude of the solenoid current ($\pm5\%$). An upper-limit was estimated for the control element, $^{207}$Pb, $|d_\text{e}^\text{Pb}|\lesssim1.2\cdot10^{-31}e\cdot\text{cm}$ at 95\%CL.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first non-zero measurement of a nuclear electric dipole moment, using a novel method based on the rate of change of a supercurrent, with the result for \(^{181}\)Ta being statistically significant at 99.985% confidence level but subject to additional systematic uncertainties from multiplicative parameters such as self-inductance, mutual inductance, and solenoid current.

## Method

The experiment measures the nuclear electric dipole moment (NEDM) by detecting a supercurrent induced in a sample wire by the electrization field, a long-range electric field generated by nuclear EDMs in the penetration depth of a superconductor when an external magnetic field creates a non-zero nuclear magnetization. The sample wire (tantalum or lead) is placed against a SQUID that detects minute changes in the supercurrent, with the sample solenoid and a booster solenoid forming a single superconducting circuit inside an adiabatic demagnetization refrigeration system operating below 40 mK. Over 1000 hours of data were collected at fixed temperatures and zero-field conditions, with differenced datasets used to control for systematics from the salt pill current.

## Key numbers

* Best value for the tantalum nuclear EDM: \(|d_\text{e}^\text{Ta}| = (3.39 \pm 0.31_\text{stat}) \cdot 10^{-32} \, e \cdot \text{cm}\)
* Combined statistical and systematic result: \(|d_\text{e}^\text{Ta}| = (3.39 \pm 3.18) \cdot 10^{-32} \, e \cdot \text{cm} > 0\) at 99.985% confidence level
* Upper limit for the control element lead: \(|d_\text{e}^\text{Pb}| \lesssim 1.2 \cdot 10^{-31} \, e \cdot \text{cm}\) at 95% confidence level
* Systematic uncertainties: self-inductance of superconducting circuit (\(\pm 4\%\)), mutual inductance between SQUID pickup coil and sample wire (\(\pm 15\%\)), magnitude of solenoid current (\(\pm 5\%\))
* Ratio of free magnetizations: \(F_\text{edm}^\text{Ta} / F_\text{edm}^\text{Pb} = 18.8\)

## Context

This work extends the method first proposed by the author in 2016 (Prezeau, arXiv:1604.02152) for measuring nuclear EDMs without external electric fields, avoiding the Schiff screening that suppresses atomic EDM measurements in experiments such as those on \(^{199}\)Hg (Graner, 2016). The result enables a new approach to constrain CP-violating interactions in atoms, potentially allowing measurements of nuclear EDMs across many elements and detection of EDMs induced by axion background fields.
