# Multibaryon configurations in a simple chromomagnetic model

> In this work, we study the multibaryon configurations in a simple chromomagnetic model. We first construct the wave function of the multibaryon states using the multiquark configuration. We consider all possible quantum numbers assuming the spatial part of the wave function to be totally symmetric. Then, we calculate the color-spin factors for tetrabaryons, pentabaryons and hexabaryons in the flavor SU(3) breaking case.

## Key claim

The paper calculates color-spin factors for tetrabaryons, pentabaryons, and hexabaryons in a chromomagnetic model assuming the spatial wave function is totally symmetric, extending previous dibaryon and tribaryon calculations to four-, five-, and six-baryon configurations under flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author constructs multiquark wave functions for tetrabaryons (12 quarks), pentabaryons (15 quarks), and hexabaryons (18 quarks) with totally symmetric spatial parts, then computes the color-spin interaction operator \(H_{CS} = -\sum_{i<j} \lambda_i^c \lambda_j^c \sigma_i \cdot \sigma_j\) using the strange quark mass parameter \(\delta = 1 - m_u/m_s\). For each multibaryon configuration, the flavor and spin states are classified via Young tableaux and Clebsch-Gordan series, and the color-spin factors are presented as matrices when multiple states with the same quantum numbers mix.

## Key numbers

- For the tetrabaryon with no strange quarks (\(q^{12}\)), flavor \(\mathbf{\overline{28}}\) and spin \(S=0\): \(H_{CS} = 96\).
- For the tetrabaryon with one strange quark (\(q^{11}s\)), flavor \(\mathbf{\overline{35}}\) and spin \(S=1\): \(H_{CS} = \frac{224}{3} + \frac{16\delta}{3}\).
- For the tetrabaryon with one strange quark (\(q^{11}s\)), flavor \(\mathbf{\overline{28}}\) and spin \(S=0\): \(H_{CS} = 96 - 16\delta\).
- For the tetrabaryon with two strange quarks (\(q^{10}s^2\)), isospin \(I=1\), flavor \(\mathbf{27}\), spin \(S=2\): \(H_{CS} = 64 + \frac{8\delta^2}{3}\).
- For the tetrabaryon with two strange quarks (\(q^{10}s^2\)), isospin \(I=1\), flavor \(\mathbf{\overline{35}}\), spin \(S=1\): \(H_{CS} = \frac{224}{3} - \frac{32\delta}{3} + \frac{8\delta^2}{3}\).
- For the tetrabaryon with three strange quarks (\(q^9s^3\)), isospin \(I=\frac{3}{2}\), flavor \(\mathbf{10}\), spin \(S=3\): \(H_{CS} = 64 - 16\delta + 8\delta^2\).
- For the tetrabaryon with four strange quarks (\(q^8s^4\)), isospin \(I=2\), flavor \(\mathbf{27}\), spin \(S=2\): \(H_{CS} = 64 - 32\delta + \frac{56\delta^2}{3}\).
- For the tetrabaryon with four strange quarks (\(q^8s^4\)), isospin \(I=0\), flavor \(\mathbf{1}\), spin \(S=0\): \(H_{CS} = 24 - 16\delta + 19\delta^2\).

## Context

This work extends the chromomagnetic model calculations of Silvestre-Brac and Leandri (1992) for dibaryons and Park (2018, 2019, 2020) for tribaryons to tetrabaryons, pentabaryons, and hexabaryons. The results enable future studies of short-range four-body, five-body, and six-body nuclear forces using the quark model, particularly when flavor SU(3) symmetry is broken, and can be compared with lattice QCD calculations of multibaryon interactions.
