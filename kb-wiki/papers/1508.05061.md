# Towards a Faddeev-AGS description of $(d,p)$ reactions with heavy nuclei: Regularizing integrals with Coulomb functions

> The repulsive Coulomb force poses severe challenges when describing $(d, p)$ reactions for highly charged nuclei as a three-body problem. Casting Faddeev-AGS equations in a Coulomb basis avoids introducing screening of the Coulomb force. However, momentum space partial-wave $t$-matrix elements need to be evaluated in this basis. When those $t$-matrices are separable, the evaluation requires the folding of a form factor, depending on one momentum variable, with a momentum space partial-wave Coulomb function, which has a singular behavior at the external momentum $q$. We developed an improved regularization scheme to calculate Coulomb distorted form factors as the integral over the Coulomb function and complex nuclear form factors.

## Key claim

The authors developed an improved regularization scheme for calculating Coulomb distorted form factors that converges much faster than the prior approach of Upadhyay (2014), reducing the required number of integration points while achieving better accuracy, but the method is specifically designed for complex form factors arising from nucleon-nucleus potentials rather than the real form factors treated previously.

## Method

The authors split the folding integral for Coulomb distorted form factors into regularized and non-regularized parts, applying regularization in a finite neighborhood (Δ ∼ 10⁻³ fm⁻¹) around the singular point q, rather than the tiny neighborhood (Δ ∼ 10⁻⁶ fm⁻¹) used by Upadhyay (2014). They applied the Gel'fand-Shilov regularization technique to the singular part, expanding the integrand to first order in χ and deriving analytic expressions for the regularized integral, including the derivative of the hypergeometric function at χ = 0.

## Key numbers

- The new regularization scheme uses a finite neighborhood Δ ∼ 10⁻³ fm⁻¹ around the singular momentum q, compared to Δ ∼ 10⁻⁶ fm⁻¹ in Upadhyay (2014).
- The test calculation used a Yamaguchi-style real form factor with constant 2.7 fm⁻¹.
- The test system was p + ²⁰⁸Pb at E_c.m. = 6 MeV, corresponding to Coulomb parameter η = 5.3 and momentum q = 0.5 fm⁻¹.
- The integral I_a^b must be computed with relative discrepancy σ_k ≲ 10⁻⁴ to obtain even the first digit of the final result for this system.
- The non-regularized integrals with the new scheme (Δ = 10⁻³ fm⁻¹) converge faster than those with the old scheme (Δ = 10⁻⁶ fm⁻¹), as shown by error plots using Gauss-Legendre quadrature with 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, 1000, 2000, and 5000 points.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-AGS approach to (d,p) reactions with heavy nuclei, where the Coulomb screening technique encounters technical difficulties (Upadhyay 2012). The alternative Coulomb basis approach was suggested by Mukhamedzhanov (2012) using real two-body transition operators, but the present work generalizes the regularization to complex form factors needed for realistic nucleon-nucleus potentials. The improved numerical scheme enables progress toward implementing Faddeev-AGS equations in Coulomb basis including spin degrees of freedom, which is essential for describing (d,p) reactions with heavy nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb.
