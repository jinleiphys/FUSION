# Critical fluid dynamics in two and three dimensions

> We describe a numerical method for simulating stochastic fluid dynamics near a critical point in the Ising universality class. This theory is known as model H, and is expected to govern the non-equilibrium dynamics of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) near a possible critical endpoint of the phase transition between a hadron liquid and the quark-gluon plasma. The numerical algorithm is based on a Metropolis scheme, and automatically ensures that the distribution function of the hydrodynamic variables in equilibrium is independent of the transport coefficients and only governed by the microscopic free energy. We verify dynamic scaling near the critical point of a two and three-dimensional fluid and extract the associated critical exponent $z$. We find $z\simeq 3$ in three dimensions, and $z\simeq 2$ for a two-dimensional fluid. In a finite system, we observe a crossover between the mean field value $z=4$ and the true critical exponent $z\simeq 3$ ($z \simeq 2$ in $d=2$). This crossover is governed by the values of the correlation length and the renormalized shear viscosity.

# Critical fluid dynamics in two and three dimensions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Metropolis-based numerical algorithm for stochastic fluid dynamics (model H) can reproduce dynamic scaling near a critical point in both two and three dimensions, extracting critical exponents of $z\simeq 2$ in two dimensions and $z\simeq 3$ in three dimensions, but only after accounting for a crossover from the mean-field value $z=4$ that depends on the finite correlation length and renormalized shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors discretize the order parameter field $\phi$ and transverse momentum density $\vec\pi^T$ on a $d$-dimensional lattice and evolve them using a split scheme: advection terms are integrated with a third-order Runge-Kutta method using skew-symmetric derivatives to conserve kinetic energy, while dissipative and noise terms are combined into a single Metropolis accept-reject step that automatically satisfies fluctuation-dissipation relations. The Metropolis update transfers charge or momentum between neighboring lattice sites with Gaussian random amplitudes, and the change in the lattice Hamiltonian determines the acceptance probability, ensuring the equilibrium distribution depends only on the free energy functional and not on transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Dynamic critical exponent $z\simeq 3$ in three dimensions for model H
- Dynamic critical exponent $z\simeq 2$ in two dimensions for model H
- Mean-field crossover value $z=4$ observed in finite systems before the true critical exponent emerges
- The crossover is governed by the values of the correlation length and the renormalized shear viscosity

## Context

This work extends the Metropolis-based approach to stochastic fluid dynamics previously developed for model B (Chattopadhyay 2023) to the more complex model H, which couples a conserved order parameter to transverse momentum density. The method provides a direct numerical alternative to the $\epsilon$-expansion (Hohenberg and Halperin 1977) and functional renormalization group calculations (Canet 2006, Mesterhazy 2013) for determining dynamic critical exponents, and it enables future simulations of critical dynamics in expanding fluids relevant to heavy-ion collisions near a possible QCD critical endpoint.
