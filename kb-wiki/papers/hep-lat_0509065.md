# Magnetic polarizability of hadrons from lattice QCD in the background field method

> We present a calculation of hadron magnetic polarizability using the techniques of lattice QCD. This is carried out by introducing a uniform external magnetic field on the lattice and measuring the quadratic part of a hadron's mass shift. The calculation is performed on a $24^4$ lattice with standard Wilson actions at beta=6.0 (spacing $a=0.1$ fm) and pion mass down to about 500 MeV. Results are obtained for 30 particles covering the entire baryon octet ($n$, $p$, $Σ^0$, $Σ^-$, $Σ^+$, $Ξ^-$, $Ξ^0$, $Λ$) and decuplet ($Δ^0$, $Δ^-$, $Δ^+$, $Δ^{++}$, $Σ^{*0}$, $Σ^{*-}$, $Σ^{*+}$, $Ξ^{*0}$, $Ξ^{*-}$, $Ω^-$), plus selected mesons ($π^0$, $π^+$, $π^-$, $K^0$, $K^+$, ${K}^-$, $ρ^0$, $ρ^+$, $ρ^-$, $K^{*0}$, $K^{*+}$, $K^{*-}$). The results are compared with available values from experiments and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of hadron magnetic polarizabilities using the background field method, but the results are qualitative rather than quantitative because no chiral extrapolation is performed and the pion masses are limited to a minimum of about 500 MeV, far above the physical pion mass.

## Method
The authors introduced a uniform external magnetic field on a quenched 24^4 lattice by multiplying gauge link variables in the y-direction by an x-dependent phase factor, then measured the quadratic part of hadron mass shifts from two-point correlation functions. The magnetic polarizability beta was extracted by fitting the even-in-B mass shift to a polynomial in B^2, using six values of the magnetic field parameter eta and six different quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 1000 MeV down to 512 MeV. The calculation used standard Wilson quark actions at beta=6.0 with lattice spacing a=0.1 fm, analyzing 150 configurations.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses ranged from 1000 MeV down to 512 MeV across six kappa values (0.1515 to 0.1555)
- Lattice spacing a=0.1 fm on a 24^4 lattice at beta=6.0
- The weakest magnetic field magnitude was 2.46 x 10^13 tesla in physical units
- The ratio of proton mass shift to proton mass was estimated as 1.60 x 10^-5
- Neutron magnetic polarizability at the smallest pion mass: 17.0 +/- 1.1 x 10^-4 fm^3
- Proton magnetic polarizability at the smallest pion mass: 2.36 +/- 1.20 x 10^-4 fm^3
- Delta++ magnetic polarizability at the smallest pion mass: -63.1 +/- 1.9 x 10^-4 fm^3
- Omega- magnetic polarizability at the strange quark mass (kappa=0.1535): -12.4 +/- 0.2 x 10^-4 fm^3
- Sigma- magnetic polarizability at the smallest pion mass: -16.1 +/- 0.5 x 10^-4 fm^3
- Xi- magnetic polarizability at the smallest pion mass: -15.6 +/- 0.5 x 10^-4 fm^3

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD approach previously applied to electric polarizabilities by Christensen et al. (2004) and follows the background field method of Fiebig et al. (1989). The results are compared with experimental values from Compton scattering experiments on the proton (Illinois 1991, Mainz 1992, Saskatoon 1993/1995, MAMI 2001) and neutron (Schmiedmayer 1991, Kossert 2002, Kolb 2000, Hornidge 1999, Lundin 2002), as well as with theoretical predictions from chiral perturbation theory (BKM 1991-1993, Hemmert, Holstein 2005), quark models (Capstick 1992, Lipkin 1992, Dong 2005), and chiral soliton models (Cohen 1992, Scherer 1992). The calculation enables future chiral extrapolations and provides predictions for decuplet baryon polarizabilities that have no experimental measurements, particularly the Omega- magnetic polarizability which is predicted at -12.4 x 10^-4 fm^3.
