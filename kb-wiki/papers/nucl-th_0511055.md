# Study of the $О·$N scattering amplitude through the associated photoproduction of $П•$- and $О·$-mesons in the region of the N*(1535) resonance

> The $Оіp \to П•О·p$ reaction is studied in the kinematic region where the $О·p$ final state originates dominantly from the decay of the N*(1535) resonance. The threshold laboratory photon energy for this reaction (at the peak of the S11 resonance) is $E_Оі^{Lab} = $3 GeV. We will discuss it somewhat above threshold, at $E_Оі^{Lab}\simeq 4-5$ GeV, in order to reach lower (absolute) values of the squared 4-momentum transfer from the initial photon to the final $П•$-meson. In these conditions, we expect the t-channel $ПҖ^0$- and $О·$-meson exchanges to drive the dynamics underlying the $Оіp \to П•О·p$ process. The initial photon dissociates into the final $П•$-meson and a virtual pseudoscalar meson ($ПҖ^0$ or $О·$). The virtual pseudoscalar meson scatters from the proton target to produce the final $О·p$ state. The $ПҖ^0 p \to О·p$ and $О·p \to О·p$ amplitudes are derived in the framework of a coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering. We found the $О·$-meson exchange to be largely dominant. The $О·$-$ПҖ^0$ interference is of the order of $20 - 30 %$. The sign of this term is not known and has a significant influence on the results. The $ПҖN\to О·N$ amplitude being largely determined by data on the $ПҖ^- p \to О·n$ reaction, we found that the $Оіp \to П•О·p$ reaction cross section is rather directly related to the $О·$-nucleon scattering amplitude in the N*(1535) resonance region. Accurate data on the $Оіp \to П•О·p$ process would therefore put additional constraints on this still poorly known amplitude.

The authors calculate the Оіp вҶ’ ПҶО·p differential cross section assuming t-channel ПҖвҒ°- and О·-meson exchange mechanisms, using the anomalous interaction Lagrangian for the ПҶ-ПҮ-Оі vertices. The ПҖвҒ°p вҶ’ О·p and О·p вҶ’ О·p scattering amplitudes are taken from a unitary coupled-channel effective field theory model (Lutz, 2002) that reproduces pion- and photon-induced О· production data in the range 1.4 < вҲҡs < 1.8 GeV. The cross section is evaluated at laboratory photon energies of 4 and 5 GeV for О·p invariant masses of 1.49 GeV (near threshold) and 1.54 GeV (at the N*(1535) resonance peak), integrating over t from -1 GeVВІ to t_min.

## Context
This work extends the study of О·-nucleon scattering near threshold, where the scattering length a_О·N is poorly constrained (values range from ~0.3 to ~1 fm in the literature, as discussed by Green and Wycech). The authors propose the Оіp вҶ’ ПҶО·p reaction as a new probe of the О·N amplitude, complementing existing analyses of pion- and photon-induced О· production. The approach builds on the coupled-channel model of Lutz (2002) and the t-channel dominance observed in Оіp вҶ’ ПҶp data at 2.4 GeV (Barth; Mibe). Accurate measurements of this reaction could resolve uncertainties in the О·N scattering amplitude and inform the possibility of О·-nuclear quasi-bound states (Niskanen).
