# Study of the $η$N scattering amplitude through the associated photoproduction of $ϕ$- and $η$-mesons in the region of the N*(1535) resonance

> The $γp \to ϕηp$ reaction is studied in the kinematic region where the $ηp$ final state originates dominantly from the decay of the N*(1535) resonance. The threshold laboratory photon energy for this reaction (at the peak of the S11 resonance) is $E_γ^{Lab} = $3 GeV. We will discuss it somewhat above threshold, at $E_γ^{Lab}\simeq 4-5$ GeV, in order to reach lower (absolute) values of the squared 4-momentum transfer from the initial photon to the final $ϕ$-meson. In these conditions, we expect the t-channel $π^0$- and $η$-meson exchanges to drive the dynamics underlying the $γp \to ϕηp$ process. The initial photon dissociates into the final $ϕ$-meson and a virtual pseudoscalar meson ($π^0$ or $η$). The virtual pseudoscalar meson scatters from the proton target to produce the final $ηp$ state. The $π^0 p \to ηp$ and $ηp \to ηp$ amplitudes are derived in the framework of a coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering. We found the $η$-meson exchange to be largely dominant. The $η$-$π^0$ interference is of the order of $20 - 30 %$. The sign of this term is not known and has a significant influence on the results. The $πN\to ηN$ amplitude being largely determined by data on the $π^- p \to ηn$ reaction, we found that the $γp \to ϕηp$ reaction cross section is rather directly related to the $η$-nucleon scattering amplitude in the N*(1535) resonance region. Accurate data on the $γp \to ϕηp$ process would therefore put additional constraints on this still poorly known amplitude.

# Study of the ηN scattering amplitude through the associated photoproduction of φ- and η-mesons in the region of the N*(1535) resonance

## Key claim
The γp → φηp reaction cross section at laboratory photon energies of 4–5 GeV and low momentum transfer (|t| < 1 GeV²) is directly related to the η-nucleon scattering amplitude in the N*(1535) resonance region, provided the t-channel η-meson exchange dominates; however, the sign of the η-π⁰ interference term is not known and has a significant influence on the results, and the γηφ transition form factor is set to unity due to lack of information.

## Method
The authors calculate the γp → φηp differential cross section assuming t-channel π⁰- and η-meson exchange mechanisms, using the anomalous interaction Lagrangian for the φ-χ-γ vertices. The π⁰p → ηp and ηp → ηp scattering amplitudes are taken from a unitary coupled-channel effective field theory model (Lutz, 2002) that reproduces pion- and photon-induced η production data in the range 1.4 < √s < 1.8 GeV. The cross section is evaluated at laboratory photon energies of 4 and 5 GeV for ηp invariant masses of 1.49 GeV (near threshold) and 1.54 GeV (at the N*(1535) resonance peak), integrating over t from -1 GeV² to t_min.

## Key numbers
- Threshold laboratory photon energy for γp → φηp at the N*(1535) peak: E_γ^Lab = 3 GeV.
- At E_γ^Lab = 4 GeV, |t_min| = 0.38 GeV²; at E_γ^Lab = 5 GeV, |t_min| = 0.26 GeV².
- φ → π⁰γ decay width: Γ_φπ⁰γ = (5.24 ± 0.49) keV; φ → ηγ decay width: Γ_φηγ = (55.17 ± 1.71) keV.
- Extracted coupling constants: |g_φπγ| ≃ 0.13, |g_φηγ| ≃ 0.70 (ratio of squared couplings ~1/29).
- η-nucleon scattering length from the model: a_ηN = (1.03 + i 0.49) fm (Lutz, 2002).
- The η-π⁰ interference modifies the differential cross section by 20–30% near t_min.
- The π⁰-exchange contribution is smaller than the η-exchange contribution by approximately two orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the study of η-nucleon scattering near threshold, where the scattering length a_ηN is poorly constrained (values range from ~0.3 to ~1 fm in the literature, as discussed by Green and Wycech). The authors propose the γp → φηp reaction as a new probe of the ηN amplitude, complementing existing analyses of pion- and photon-induced η production. The approach builds on the coupled-channel model of Lutz (2002) and the t-channel dominance observed in γp → φp data at 2.4 GeV (Barth; Mibe). Accurate measurements of this reaction could resolve uncertainties in the ηN scattering amplitude and inform the possibility of η-nuclear quasi-bound states (Niskanen).
