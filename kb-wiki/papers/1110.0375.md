# $J/ψ\rightarrow p\bar{p}ϕ$ decay in the isobar resonance model

> Based on the effective Lagrangian approach, the $ J/ψ\to p \bar{p} ϕ$ decay is studied in an isobar resonance model with the assumption that the $ϕ$-meson is produced from intermediate nucleon resonances. The contributions from the $N^*_{1/2^-}(1535)$, $N^*_{3/2^+}(1900)$, $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ and $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ states are considered. In terms of the coupling constants $g^{2}_{ϕN N^{*}}$ and $g^{2}_{ϕN N^{*}}$ extracted from the data of the partial decay widths of the $N^*$s to the $Nπ$ channel, the reaction cross section of the $π^{-}p\rightarrow nϕ$ process and the partial decay widths of the $J/ψ\rightarrow p\bar{p}η$ and $J/ψ\rightarrow p\bar{n}π^{-}$ processes, respectively, the invariant mass spectrum and the Dalitz plot for $ J/ψ\to p \bar{p} ϕ$ are predicted. It is shown that there are two types of results. In the type I case, a large peak structure around 2.09GeV implies that a considerable mount of $Nϕ$ or $qqqs\bar s$ component may exist in the narrow-width $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ state, but for the wide-width $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ state, it has little $qqqs\bar s$ component. In the type II case, a small peak around 2.11GeV may only indicate the existence of a certain mount of $pϕ$ or $qqqs\bar s$ component in the narrow-width $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ state, but no information for the wide-width $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ state. Further BESIII data with high statistics would help us to distinguish the strange structures of these $N^*$s.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \phi$ decay can reveal whether certain nucleon resonances contain strange ($s\bar{s}$) components, but the interpretation depends on two distinct fitting scenarios (type I and type II) for the total widths of the $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ and $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ states, which cannot be distinguished without further BESIII data.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach within an isobar resonance model, assuming the $\phi$ meson is produced from intermediate nucleon resonances. They consider four $N^*$ states: $N^*_{1/2^-}(1535)$, $N^*_{3/2^+}(1900)$, $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$, and $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$. Coupling constants $g^2_{\phi N N^*}$ are extracted by fitting the total cross section data of the $\pi^- p \to n \phi$ reaction, while $g^2_{\psi N N^*}$ are extracted from BESII data on $J/\psi \to p \bar{n} \pi^-$ and $J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \eta$ decays; these are then used to predict the invariant mass spectrum and Dalitz plot for $J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \phi$.

## Key numbers

- The branching fraction of $J/\psi \to p \bar{n} \pi^-$ is $(2.09 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-3}$ and of $J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \eta$ is $(2.12 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-3}$, used to extract $g^2_{\psi N N^*}$.
- The $N^*_{1/2^-}(1535)$ provides about 50% to 70% of the contribution to the $\pi^- p \to n \phi$ cross section, with $g^2_{N^*(1535)N\phi}$ ranging from 1.1 to 1.4 (in units of $10^{-2}$).
- In type I (narrow $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ with width 95 MeV, wide $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ with width 260 MeV), a large peak around 2.09 GeV appears in the $p\phi$ invariant mass spectrum.
- In type II (wide $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ with width 350 MeV, narrow $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ with width 113 MeV), a small peak around 2.11 GeV appears.
- The $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ state contributes 9.8% to 25.7% of the $\pi^- p \to n \phi$ cross section depending on the case, with $g^2_{\phi NN^*}$ ranging from $9.46 \times 10^{-2}$ to $18.6 \times 10^{-2}$.
- The $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ contributes 3.3% to 5.6% of the cross section, with $g^2_{\phi NN^*}$ from $0.749 \times 10^{-2}$ to $1.37 \times 10^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the resonance model approach previously applied to $\pi^- p \to n \phi$ by Xie et al. (2008) to the $J/\psi \to p \bar{p} \phi$ decay, motivated by the DM2 Collaboration measurement from 1988 which had insufficient statistics to extract resonance information. The study aims to provide predictions for the upcoming high-statistics BESIII data (expected $3 \times 10^9$ $J/\psi$ events per year) to distinguish whether the $N^*_{1/2^-}(2090)$ or $N^*_{1/2^+}(2100)$ states contain significant $qqqs\bar{s}$ (pentaquark-like) components, building on earlier evidence for $s\bar{s}$ content in $N^*(1535)$ from its large $N\eta$ branching fraction (45-60%) and strong $K\Lambda$ coupling.
