# Functional-renormalization-group aided density-functional analysis for the correlation energy of the two-dimensional homogeneous electron gas

> The functional-renormalization-group aided density-functional theory (FRG-DFT) is applied to the two-dimensional homogeneous electron gas (2DHEG). The correlation energy of the 2DHEG is derived as a function of the Wigner-Seitz radius $ r_{\rm s} $ directly. We find that our correlation energy completely reproduces the exact behavior at the high-density limit. For finite density, the result of FRG-DFT shows good agreement with the Monte Carlo (MC) results in the high-density region, although the discrepancy between FRG-DFT and MC results becomes larger as the system becomes more dilute. Our study is the first example in which the FRG-DFT is applied to more-than-one-dimensional models, and shows that the FRG-DFT is a feasible and promising method even for the analysis of realistic models for quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim

The functional-renormalization-group aided density-functional theory (FRG-DFT) is applied to the two-dimensional homogeneous electron gas (2DHEG) for the first time in more than one dimension, and the resulting correlation energy reproduces the exact high-density limit and agrees well with Monte Carlo results at high densities, but the discrepancy grows as the system becomes more dilute.

## Method

The authors employ the FRG-DFT formalism for infinite systems, introducing a renormalization-group parameter λ that gradually turns on the Coulomb interaction from the non-interacting system (λ=0) to the fully interacting 2DHEG (λ=1). The flow equation for the effective action is truncated at second order in the vertex expansion, yielding coupled flow equations for the ground-state energy per particle and the two-point density-correlation function, which are solved analytically for the correlation energy. The chemical potential is adjusted throughout the flow to keep the electron density fixed at the target value.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy per particle for the non-interacting system is \( 1/(2r_s^2) \) in Hartree atomic units.
- The exchange energy per particle is \( -4\sqrt{2}/(3\pi r_s) \).
- The correlation energy derived by FRG-DFT exactly reproduces the Gell-Mann-Brueckner resummation result at the high-density limit (\( r_s \to 0 \)).
- The Wigner-Seitz radius is defined by the electron density as \( n = 1/(\pi r_s^2) \).
- The chemical potential at λ=0 is set to \( \mu_{\lambda=0} = \pi n = 1/r_s^2 \).

## Context

This work extends the FRG-DFT approach, previously applied only to zero-dimensional and one-dimensional systems (Kemler et al. 2013, Liang et al. 2018, Kemler et al. 2017, Yokota et al. 2018), to a two-dimensional model with long-range Coulomb interaction. The results are compared with Monte Carlo calculations by Tanatar and Ceperley (1989), Kwon et al. (1993), and Attaccalite et al. (2002). The study demonstrates that FRG-DFT is feasible for higher-dimensional systems, but the second-order vertex expansion is insufficient for accurate description of dilute systems, motivating the development of improved approximation schemes.
