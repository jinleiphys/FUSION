# Formation of $η$-mesic Nuclei Using the Recoilless (d,$^3$He) Reaction

> We propose to use the recoilless (d,$^3$He) reaction to produce $η$-mesic nuclei. This reaction has been used to observe deeply bound pionic states and proven to be powerful recently. We calculate $η$-mesic bound states in the nucleus using an optical potential and their formation cross section with the Green function method. Then, we carefully check the experimental feasibility. We find that $η$-mesic nuclei can be observed experimentally using the (d,$^3$He) reaction. We also mention the possibility to study the formation of $ω$-mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The recoilless (d,³He) reaction can produce η-mesic nuclei with observable cross sections, provided the η-nucleus optical potential is sufficiently attractive; the existence of such bound states remains theoretically controversial due to large uncertainties in the η-nucleon scattering length.

## Method

The authors calculated η-nucleus bound states by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a first-order in density optical potential derived from the η-nucleon scattering length. They then computed formation cross sections for the (d,³He) reaction using the Green function method of Morimatsu and Yazaki, with distorted waves treated in the eikonal approximation, and estimated experimental feasibility at GSI-SIS by including continuum background contributions from η production and π⁺π⁻ production.

## Key numbers

- η-nucleon scattering length estimates range from (0.717 ± 0.030) + i(0.263 ± 0.025) fm to (0.20 + i 0.26) fm, giving optical potentials of V(r) = -(86 + 32 i) ρ(r)/ρ₀ MeV and V(r) = -(62 + 30 i) ρ(r)/ρ₀ MeV respectively.
- Recoilless η production on ⁷Li requires incident deuteron kinetic energy T_d ≈ 3.6 GeV, achievable at GSI-SIS where maximum T_d is 4 GeV.
- The elementary p(d,³He)η cross section at the recoilless condition (T_p = 1.75 GeV) is 3 nb/sr in the center-of-mass frame, corresponding to approximately 150 nb/sr in the laboratory frame.
- Expected double-differential cross sections for η-mesic states on ⁷Li and ¹²C show peaks at Q-values near -552 MeV and -558 MeV respectively, with signal strengths of order 10-100 nb/sr/MeV.
- Continuum background levels are estimated at 2.7 nb/sr/MeV for ⁷Li and 3.4 nb/sr/MeV for ¹²C.
- With 100 hours of beam time at GSI (3×10¹⁰ deuterons/sec, 1 g/cm² target, Ω = 2.5×10⁻³ sr), the predicted peaks are clearly visible above background.

## Context

This work extends the recoilless (d,³He) technique previously used to observe deeply bound pionic states (Hayano et al., 1997; Gilg et al., 1998) to the search for η-mesic nuclei, which were first suggested theoretically by Haider and Liu but have not been observed experimentally. The method is complementary to studies of vector meson in-medium properties via dilepton decays and enables determination of the η-nucleus optical potential and the η mass shift in nuclear matter; the same technique could in principle be applied to ω-mesic nuclei, though no current facility can provide the required 10 GeV deuteron beam for recoilless ω production.
