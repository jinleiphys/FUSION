# Associated Strangeness Production in the Threshold Region

> Associated strangeness production close to threshold is a particularly clean example of how energy is converted into hadronic matter. Final results from the completed experiments of the PS185 collaboration at LEAR/CERN, using the antiproton-proton interaction to produce antihyperon-hyperon pairs, demonstrate that such studies form a powerful tool. First results from the ongoing investigations of the COSY-11 collaboration at COSY-J"ulich, measuring the strangeness dissociation into both hyperon-kaon and kaon-kaon pairs from proton-proton scattering, are presented and plans for further investigations are discussed. Finally, an interesting possibility for future studies of the strangeness production mechanism in the antiproton-proton interactions with antiproton beams of momenta >5 GeV/c is suggested.

# Associated Strangeness Production in the Threshold Region

## Key claim

The paper presents final results from the PS185 collaboration at LEAR/CERN on antihyperon-hyperon production and first results from the COSY-11 collaboration at COSY-Jülich on strangeness dissociation in proton-proton scattering, demonstrating that threshold-region measurements provide a clean tool for studying how energy converts into hadronic matter, though the theoretical interpretations remain incomplete and model-dependent.

## Method

The PS185 collaboration measured antiproton-proton reactions producing antihyperon-hyperon pairs (specifically \(\bar p p \rightarrow \bar Y Y \rightarrow \bar p \pi^+ p \pi^-\)) at the Low-Energy Antiproton Ring at CERN, extracting cross sections, polarization, spin correlations, and singlet fractions. The COSY-11 collaboration used an internal installation in a bending section of the COSY ring with a hydrogen cluster target, detecting positively charged reaction products bent to the inner region by a machine dipole using drift chambers and scintillators, to measure \(pp \rightarrow p K^+ \Lambda\) and \(pp \rightarrow pp K^+ K^-\) reactions near threshold.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar \Lambda \Lambda\) weighted mean singlet fraction is \(0.007 \pm 0.009\), indicating production in parallel-spin configurations.
- The normalized asymmetry parameter \(A = (\alpha + \bar \alpha)/(\alpha - \bar \alpha)\) for \(\Lambda (\bar \Lambda)\) decay is \(0.006 \pm 0.014\), consistent with CP conservation.
- For the \(pp \rightarrow pp K^+ K^-\) reaction at a beam momentum of 3.321 GeV/c (excess energy 6.1 MeV), two events were selected with a resulting cross section of approximately 500 pb.
- The COSY-11 beam momentum calibration achieved a deviation from nominal excess energy as little as 0.2 MeV for the \(pp \rightarrow p K^+ \Lambda\) data.
- LEAR provided antiproton beams with momenta up to 2 GeV/c (total energy \(\sqrt s = 2.43\) GeV), while COSY provides proton beams with momenta up to 3.4 GeV/c (total energy \(\sqrt s = 2.86\) GeV).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of strangeness production from antiproton-proton interactions at LEAR (PS185 collaboration, reported by Johansson (1997)) to proton-proton interactions at COSY-Jülich, adding threshold-region data points that fill the gap below the earlier lowest-energy bubble chamber measurement by Fickinger (1962) at approximately 160 MeV excess energy. The results enable comparisons with theoretical models including those of Randrup and Ko (1980), Schürmann and Zwermann (1988), Fäldt and Wilkin (1997), and Sibirtsev (1995), and provide input for understanding kaon production in heavy-ion collisions and hypernucleus formation. The paper also suggests future studies of triple associated strangeness production (\(\bar \Omega \Omega\) versus \(\Phi \Phi \Phi\)) at antiproton beam momenta above 5 GeV/c, following proposals by Dover (1991).
