# Gravitational Waves versus X and Gamma Ray Emission in a Short Gamma-Ray Burst

> The recent progress in the understanding the physical nature of neutron star equilibrium configurations and the first observational evidence of a genuinely short gamma-ray burst, GRB 090227B, allows to give an estimate of the gravitational waves versus the X and Gamma-ray emission in a short gamma-ray burst.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the gravitational wave emission from the binary neutron star progenitor of the short gamma-ray burst GRB 090227B, finding that the signal would be undetectable by Advanced LIGO at the burst's actual redshift of z=1.61, but would become detectable (SNR=5) only if the source were as close as z≈0.09, a condition that does not hold for this particular burst.

## Method

The authors first infer the binary neutron star parameters (masses, radii, crust masses) for GRB 090227B using the Fireshell model and the observed baryon load B=4.13×10⁻⁵, adopting globally neutral neutron star configurations from the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi (EMTF) equations with the NL3 nuclear parameterization. They then compute the gravitational wave emission during the inspiral phase using both the classical test-mass limit approximation and the effective one-body (EOB) formalism at 3PN and 4PN order, and evaluate the signal-to-noise ratio for detection by Advanced LIGO.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of GRB 090227B: E^{GRB}_{tot} = 2.83×10⁵³ erg
- Baryon load of GRB 090227B: B = (4.13±0.05)×10⁻⁵
- Redshift of GRB 090227B: z = 1.61
- Intrinsic duration of GRB 090227B: Δt = 0.35 s
- Average CBM density for GRB 090227B: ⟨n_{CBM}⟩ = 1.9×10⁻⁵ cm⁻³
- Lorentz factor at transparency for GRB 090227B: Γ_{tr} = 1.44×10⁴
- Critical mass for gravitational collapse (NL3): M_{crit} = 2.67 M⊙
- Inferred binary component masses (twin, NL3): M₁ = M₂ = 1.335 M⊙
- Inferred radii (NL3): R₁ = R₂ = 12.24 km
- Crust mass per component (NL3): M_{crust} ≈ 3.6×10⁻⁵ M⊙
- Crust thickness per component (NL3): ΔR_{crust} ≈ 0.47 km
- Contact frequency at source: f_c ≈ 1534.19 Hz
- Contact frequency at detector for z=1.61: f_{max} ≈ 587.81 Hz
- SNR at z=1.61 for Advanced LIGO: ⟨SNR⟩ ≈ 0.32
- Redshift for detectable signal (SNR=5): z ≈ 0.09
- Luminosity distance for detectable signal: d_L ≈ 381 Mpc
- Contact frequency at detector for z=0.09: f_{max} ≈ 1407.51 Hz

## Context

This work extends the Fireshell model analysis of GRB 090227B by Muccino et al. (2013) and the globally neutral neutron star theory developed by Rueda et al. (2011, 2012) and Belvedere et al. (2012). It provides the first quantitative estimate of gravitational wave emission from a genuine short GRB progenitor, showing that while the inspiral signal is too weak for detection at cosmological distances, future coincident observations of short GRBs and gravitational waves could probe neutron star structure. The paper contrasts its globally neutral neutron star crust masses (∼10⁻⁵ M⊙) with the much larger crust masses (10⁻³–10⁻² M⊙) from locally neutral TOV models used in earlier binary merger simulations by Rosswog et al. (2001) and Goriely et al. (2011).
