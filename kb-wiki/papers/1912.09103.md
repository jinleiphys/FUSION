# Diffusion processes involving multiple conserved charges: a first study from kinetic theory and implications to the fluid-dynamical modeling of heavy ion collisions

> The bulk nuclear matter produced in heavy ion collisions carries a multitude of conserved quantum numbers: electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness. Therefore, the diffusion processes associated to these conserved charges cannot occur independently and must be described in terms of a set of coupled diffusion equations. This physics is implemented by replacing the traditional diffusion coefficients for each conserved charge by a diffusion coefficient matrix, which quantifies the coupling between the conserved quantum numbers. The diagonal coefficients of this matrix are the usual charge diffusion coefficients, while the off-diagonal entries describe the diffusive coupling of the charge currents. In this paper, we show how to calculate this diffusion coefficient matrix from kinetic theory and provide results for a hadron resonance gas and a gas of partons. We further find that the off-diagonal entries can reach similar magnitudes compared to the diagonal entries. In order to provide some insight on the influence that the coupling between the net charge diffusion currents can have on heavy ion observables, we present first results for the diffusive evolution of a hadronic system in a simple (1+1)D-fluid dynamics approach, and study different configurations of the diffusion matrix.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that diffusion currents for baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in strongly interacting matter are coupled through a diffusion coefficient matrix, and that the off-diagonal entries of this matrix can reach magnitudes similar to the diagonal entries, potentially generating regions of non-zero net strangeness from initially net strangeness neutral matter in heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The authors compute the full diffusion coefficient matrix from relativistic kinetic theory using a first-order Chapman-Enskog expansion of the linearized Boltzmann equation for a multi-species gas with elastic binary collisions. They expand the first-order perturbation of the single-particle distribution function in powers of energy, solve the resulting matrix equation for the expansion coefficients, and then extract the diffusion coefficients via comparison to the Navier-Stokes form of the diffusion currents. Results are obtained for a hadron resonance gas and a gas of massless partons, and the authors also perform (1+1)D fluid-dynamical simulations to study the impact of the coupled diffusion on heavy ion observables.

## Key numbers
- The baryon chemical potential can reach values up to mu_B ~ 400 MeV at beam energies of sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
- The diffusion coefficient matrix is a 3x3 matrix with entries kappa_{qq'} for q,q' in {B, Q, S}, where the off-diagonal entries are found to reach similar magnitudes compared to the diagonal entries.
- The expansion of the perturbation function is truncated at order M, with the matrix M having dimensions [(N_species * M) x (N_species * M)], where N_species is the number of particle species.

## Context
This work extends the initial study of Greif (2017) which first computed the full matrix of diffusion coefficients for strongly interacting matter, providing more detailed computation methods and the first fluid-dynamic implementation. The paper builds on prior kinetic theory approaches to charge diffusion (such as those by Greif (2016) for electric conductivity) and addresses the need for coupled diffusion descriptions in lower-energy heavy ion collisions where net baryon density becomes significant, as studied in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and planned at FAIR and NICA facilities. The results enable more realistic fluid-dynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions that include the coupling between baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness diffusion currents.
