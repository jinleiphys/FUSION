# Early time dynamics far from equilibrium via holography

> We investigate the early time dynamics of heavy ion collisions studying the time evolution of the energy-momentum tensor as well as energy-momentum correlations within a uniformly thermalizing holographic QGP. From these quantities, we suggest a far-from equilibrium definition of shear viscosity, which is a crucial property of QCD matter as it significantly determines the generation of elliptic flow already at early times. During an exemplary initial heating phase of the holographic QGP the shear viscosity of entropy density ratio decreases down to 60%, followed by an overshoot to 110% of the near-equilibrium value, $η/s=1/(4π)$. Implications for the QCD QGP are discussed. Subsequently, we consider a holographic QGP which is Bjorken-expanding. Its energy-momentum tensor components have a known hydrodynamic attractor to which all time evolutions collapse independent of the initial conditions. Based on this, we propose a definition for a far from equilibrium speed of sound, and analytically compute its hydrodynamic attractor. Subjecting this Bjorken-expanding plasma to an external magnetic field and an axial chemical potential, we study the chiral magnetic effect far from equilibrium.

# Early time dynamics far from equilibrium via holography

## Key claim

The authors propose far-from-equilibrium definitions of shear viscosity and speed of sound in holographic plasmas, finding that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio can drop below 60% of the near-equilibrium value of 1/(4π) during early-time heating, which would imply larger elliptic flow generation at early times than current models assume. However, these results are obtained in N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory via holography, not in QCD directly, and the behavior depends on the specific heating time and final temperature parameters.

## Method

The authors model a thermalizing plasma using the AdS-Vaidya metric in holography, where horizon formation in the gravity dual corresponds to thermalization in the field theory. They compute the retarded shear correlator far from equilibrium by introducing a delta-source perturbation to the metric and performing a Wigner transform to obtain the frequency-dependent correlator. For the Bjorken-expanding plasma, they numerically solve the time-dependent Einstein equations following the method of Chesler (2010) and compute the speed of sound from derivatives of the energy-momentum tensor components.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases down to 60% of the near-equilibrium value 1/(4π) during an initial heating phase, then overshoots to 110% of 1/(4π)
- The example plasma heat-up starts at T_c = 155 MeV, ends at T_final = 310 MeV, rising over Δt = 0.3 fm (RHIC energies)
- The overshoot above 1/(4π) only exists for final temperatures T_final < 6.5 T_c
- The longitudinal speed of sound attractor is given analytically as C²_|| = 1/3 - (2/9)(A_0(w) + (w/4) ∂A_0(w)/∂w) with w = τT and pressure anisotropy attractor A_0(w) = (2530w - 276)/(3975w² - 570w + 120)
- All initial conditions for the speed of sound collapse to the attractor by τT < 1, and hydrodynamic expectations coincide with the attractor already at τT ≈ 0.5

## Context

This work extends the holographic exploration of far-from-equilibrium dynamics in N=4 SYM plasma, building on the hydrodynamic attractor concept from Spalinski (2017) and the far-from-equilibrium shear viscosity definition from Wondrak (2020). The results challenge Bayesian analyses such as Bernhard (2019) which may have underestimated early-time elliptic flow generation. The authors also compute the chiral magnetic effect far from equilibrium, extending work by Cartwright (2021). The paper enables future direct calculation of sound wave propagation from fluctuations around Bjorken-expanding holographic plasma and suggests the possibility of constructing an effective field theory of fluid dynamics far from equilibrium.
