# Soliton propagation in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We study the conditions for the formation and propagation of Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) solitons in nuclear matter. In a previous work we have derived a KdV equation from Euler and continuity equations in non-relativistic hydrodynamics. In the present contribution we extend our formalism to relativistic fluids. We present results for a given equation of state, which is based on quantum hadrodynamics (QHD).

# Soliton propagation in relativistic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) solitons can exist in relativistic nuclear fluids, provided the equation of state includes derivative couplings that produce a Laplacian term in the energy density; the relativistic generalization modifies the nonlinear coefficient from 3 to (3 - c_s^2), which reduces to the non-relativistic result only in the limit c_s^2 → 0.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Euler and continuity equations for a perfect fluid, rewrite them using the enthalpy per nucleon and the baryochemical potential, and then expand the baryon density and velocity in stretched coordinates around equilibrium values. By keeping terms up to second order in the expansion parameter σ, they derive a KdV equation for the first-order density perturbation ρ₁, using an energy density functional that includes a ∇²ρ_B term obtained from a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian with an added derivative coupling between the nucleon and vector meson fields.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound c_s² in realistic nuclear equations of state lies in the range 0.15–0.25, motivating the relativistic extension.
- The relativistic KdV equation has nonlinear coefficient (3 - c_s²), compared to 3 in the non-relativistic case.
- The soliton width λ is given by λ = (g_v / m_v²) × √[2ρ₀ / ((u - c_s) c_s M)], where g_v and m_v are the vector meson coupling and mass, ρ₀ is the equilibrium baryon density, M is the nucleon mass, and u is the perturbation propagation speed.
- The soliton amplitude in the relativistic case is (3(u - c_s)/c_s) × (3 - c_s²)⁻¹, compared to (u - c_s)/c_s in the non-relativistic case.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic KdV soliton formalism previously developed by the same authors (Fogaça and Navarra, earlier work) to relativistic fluids, updating earlier suggestions by other researchers (Fröbrich and Swiatecki, year not given) that KdV solitons might form in nuclear matter. The results rely on an equation of state derived from quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) as formulated by Lalazissis et al. (year not given) and are consistent with density functional theory approaches (Furnstahl, 1997). The formalism may be applicable to supersonic motion in hot dense matter produced at RHIC, such as jet quenching phenomena.
