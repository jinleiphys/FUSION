# Coupled channel study of $K^+Λ$ photoproduction

> A coupled channel model with $γN$, $KY$ and $πN$ channels has been used to analyze the recent data of $γp \to K^+ Λ$. The non-resonant interactions within the subspace $KY \oplus πN$ are derived from effective Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method. The direct photoproduction reaction is obtained from a chiral constituent quark model with $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ breaking. Missing baryon resonances issues are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
The coupled-channel model reproduces the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ data well, but the fit quality depends on which experimental dataset is used: fitting SAPHIR data alone yields a reduced $\chi^2$ of 1.3, while fitting CLAS data alone gives 2.1, and a simultaneous fit to all cross section and polarization data yields $\chi^2_{d.o.f} \approx 3$, indicating residual tensions between the two datasets.

## Method
The authors constructed a coupled-channel model including $\gamma N$, $KY$, and $\pi N$ channels for center-of-mass energies $W \approx 1.6$–$2.7$ GeV. Non-resonant meson-baryon interactions ($\pi N \to \pi N$, $\pi N \to KY$, $KY \to KY$) were derived from effective Lagrangians via a unitary transformation method, with parameters fixed by fitting $\pi N \to KY$ data first. The direct photoproduction amplitude was obtained from a chiral constituent quark model with $SU(6) \otimes O(3)$ symmetry breaking, including all 3- and 4-star resonances below 2 GeV with one adjustable parameter per resonance.

## Key numbers
- Reduced $\chi^2$ of 1.3 when fitting SAPHIR data alone for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$.
- Reduced $\chi^2$ of 2.1 when fitting CLAS data alone for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$.
- $\chi^2_{d.o.f} \approx 3$ when fitting all cross section and polarization asymmetry data simultaneously.
- Mass of the $3^{rd}$ $S_{11}$ resonance: M = 1.84 GeV, width $\Gamma$ = 283 MeV.
- Mass of the $3^{rd}$ $D_{13}$ resonance: M = 1.93 GeV, width $\Gamma$ = 252 MeV.
- Center-of-mass energy range studied: $W \approx 1.6$–$2.7$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel formalism of earlier studies (Julia-Diaz et al., 2004; 2005) by improving the derivation of meson-baryon intermediate states and incorporating the quark model of Capstick and Roberts (1994) and Saghai (2001) for direct and resonance photoproduction. The analysis addresses the missing baryon resonance problem by examining the role of a $3^{rd}$ $S_{11}$, $3^{rd}$ $P_{13}$, and $3^{rd}$ $D_{13}$ resonance; the $3^{rd}$ $P_{13}$ shows negligible influence, while the $3^{rd}$ $S_{11}$ and $3^{rd}$ $D_{13}$ have sizable effects, consistent with findings by other groups (Saghai, 2001; and a study on $D_{13}$, 2004). The results enable improved constraints on resonance properties from combined SAPHIR and CLAS datasets, though the discrepancy between the two datasets in total cross sections (likely due to different angular coverage and extrapolation methods) remains an open issue.
