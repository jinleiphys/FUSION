# Structural Studies of Many-Body Systems and (e,e'p) Reaction Cross Sections

> Studies of one-body density matrices (ODM) are performed in various correlation methods, such as the Jastrow method, the correlated basis function method, the Green's function method and the generator coordinate method aiming to extract the absolute spectroscopic factors and overlap functions (OF) for one-nucleon removal reactions from the ODM of the target nucleus. The advantage of this method is that it avoids the complicated task of calculating the total nuclear spectral function. The procedure for extracting bound-state OF's has been applied to make calculations of the cross sections of the $(e,e^{\prime}p)$ reaction on the closed-shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca as well as on the open-shell nucleus $^{32}$S consistently (using the same OF's) with the cross sections of $(p,d)$ and $(γ,p)$ reactions on the same nuclei. The analyses of the reaction cross sections and the spectroscopic factors and the comparison with the experimental data show the particular importance of these OF's, since they contain effects of nucleon correlations (short-range and/or long-range) which are accounted for to different extent in the theoretical methods considered.

# Structural Studies of Many-Body Systems and (e,e'p) Reaction Cross Sections

## Key claim

Overlap functions extracted from one-body density matrices (ODM) using various correlation methods can be used as realistic, parameter-free form factors to calculate absolute cross sections of one-nucleon removal reactions, but a reduction factor (attributed to long-range correlations not included in the ODM) must be applied to the calculated cross sections to reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors extracted bound-state overlap functions and spectroscopic factors from ground-state one-body density matrices of 16O, 40Ca, and 32S using five correlation methods: the Jastrow correlation method, the correlated basis function method, the Green's function method, and the generator coordinate method. These overlap functions were then used as form factors in nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation calculations of (e,e'p) reaction cross sections, with final-state interactions and Coulomb distortion of electron waves included via the code DWEEPY.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Stoitsov et al. (1996) and Di Marzio et al. (1997) that first applied the ODM-based extraction method to Jastrow-type overlap functions for (p,d) reactions. It provides a consistent analysis across multiple reaction channels ((e,e'p), (gamma,p), and (p,d)) on the same nuclei, using the same overlap functions. The results enable future tests of short-range correlations at large missing momenta and suggest that two-nucleon knockout reactions may better isolate short-range correlation effects at high missing energies.
