# How dense does parton matter get in Pb + Pb Collisions at the CERN SPS?

> We examine the qualitative features of parton production through materialization in heavy-ion collisions within perturbative QCD, and estimate the magnitude of the resulting parton density created during the early stage of the collisions. The implications for ``anomalous'' $J/ψ$ suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS are discussed. We argue that the A-dependence of absorption of $J/ψ$ by (partonic) comovers is steeper than assumed in most phenomenological models, because the absorption process is dominated by quasi-perturbative QCD interactions. Our argument is supported by results recently obtained in the framework of the parton cascade model. We predict significant ``anomalous'' suppression for Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS, but not for S+U collisions.

# How dense does parton matter get in Pb + Pb Collisions at the CERN SPS?

## Key claim
The authors argue that the density of comoving partons (comovers) responsible for J/ψ absorption in heavy-ion collisions grows with nuclear mass A as A^{2/3} (or (A₁A₂)^{1/3} for asymmetric systems), which is a much steeper A-dependence than assumed in most phenomenological models based on the Glauber approach. They predict significant "anomalous" J/ψ suppression for Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS, but not for S+U collisions, contingent on the critical transverse momentum p_crit lying below the perturbatively accessible range at SPS energies.

## Method
The authors use perturbative QCD to analyze mini-jet production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, deriving the A-dependence of parton densities from the nuclear geometry and parton distribution functions. They apply the condition for the onset of parton shadowing (Equation e3) to determine the critical transverse momentum p_crit, and then estimate the scaling of mini-jet yields above and below this threshold. The qualitative arguments are supported by quantitative results from the parton cascade model, which incorporates perturbative parton scattering with dynamic infrared cutoffs and a density-dependent clustering mechanism for parton-to-hadron transitions.

## Key numbers
- The critical transverse momentum for shadowing onset grows as p_crit ∼ A^{1/6}; for p_crit = 1 GeV with x f_{g/N}(x,1 GeV²) = 3 at x≈0.1 and α_s≈0.3, shadowing corrections become significant only for nuclei with A > 240.
- The integrated mini-jet yield for all momenta p_⊥ > p_crit grows linearly with A: ∫_{p_crit²}^{∞} dp_⊥² x dN(b)/dp_⊥²dx ∼ A.
- The parton cascade model predicts the initial partonic energy density ε_p reaches 5 GeV/fm³ at times τ < 1 fm/c in Pb+Pb collisions, corresponding to an initial temperature T_i ≈ 230 MeV.
- For S+U collisions, the predicted parton density is ε_p ≈ 2 GeV/fm³, just at the upper end of the transition region from lattice-QCD.
- The dissociation temperature for J/ψ due to color screening is T_D ≈ 1.2 T_c = 180-200 MeV, where T_c ≈ 150 MeV from lattice-QCD.
- The energy density produced by scattering partons at central rapidity scales as ε ∼ (A₁A₂)^{1/3}, growing by a factor of more than 2 between S+U and Pb+Pb, compared to only 30% increase in the usual Glauber model approach which predicts ε ∼ (A₁^{1/3}+A₂^{1/3}).

## Context
This work extends the qualitative arguments of Blaizot and Mueller (1987) on parton saturation and shadowing in heavy nuclei, applying them specifically to the problem of J/ψ suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration at CERN SPS. The authors contrast their approach with phenomenological models by Gavin and others that assume conventional absorption on light hadrons, and with the Glauber model predictions that yield a weaker A-dependence. The results enable a distinction between S+U and Pb+Pb collisions as systems that may or may not reach the deconfinement threshold, and support the interpretation that the "anomalous" J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb is caused by color screening in a dense partonic medium rather than hadronic comover absorption.
