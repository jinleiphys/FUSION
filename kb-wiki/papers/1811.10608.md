# Quark-hadron continuity beyond Ginzburg-Landau paradigm

> Quark-hadron continuity is a scenario that hadronic matter is continuously connected to color superconductor without phase transitions as the baryon chemical potential increases. This scenario is based on Landau's classification of phases since they have the same symmetry breaking pattern. We address the question whether this continuity is true as quantum phases of matter, which requires the treatment beyond Ginzburg-Landau description. To examine the topological nature of color superconductor, we derive a dual effective theory for $U(1)$ Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons and vortices of the color-flavor locked phase, and discuss the fate of emergent higher-form symmetries. The theory has the form of a topological $BF$ theory coupled to NG bosons, and fractional statistics of test quarks and vortices arises as a result of an emergent $\mathbb Z_3$ two-form symmetry. We find that this symmetry cannot be spontaneously broken, indicating that quark-hadron continuity is still a consistent scenario.

# Quark-hadron continuity beyond Ginzburg-Landau paradigm

## Key claim

Quark-hadron continuity between nucleon superfluidity and the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase remains a consistent scenario even when examined as quantum phases of matter beyond the Ginzburg-Landau paradigm, because the emergent $\mathbb{Z}_3$ two-form symmetry in the CFL phase cannot be spontaneously broken. The authors find that this symmetry is unbroken due to logarithmic confinement of vortices and the generalized Coleman-Mermin-Wagner theorem, meaning the CFL phase has a trivial topological structure.

## Method

The authors derive a dual effective field theory for the CFL phase starting from a gauged Ginzburg-Landau model with a diquark condensate, using Abelian duality to obtain a topological $BF$ theory coupled to massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons. They analyze the emergent higher-form symmetries of this effective theory, compute the braiding statistics between test quarks and CFL vortices via Wilson loop expectation values, and apply the generalized Coleman-Mermin-Wagner theorem to determine whether the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ two-form symmetry can be spontaneously broken in 4-dimensional spacetime.

## Key numbers

- The minimal superfluid circulation of vortices in the CFL phase is a fractional number $1/3$.
- The braiding phase between a CFL vortex $V_i$ and the physical Wilson loop $W_8(C)^2$ is $\exp(2\pi\im/3 \times \mathrm{link}(C,M_2))$, where $\mathrm{link}(C,M_2) \in \mathbb Z$ is the linking number.
- Under explicit $SU(3)_\mathrm{f}$ breaking with unequal gap parameters $\Delta_i$, the braiding phase becomes $\exp(2\pi\im g_3^2/(g_1^2+g_2^2+g_3^2) \times \mathrm{link}(C,M_2))$, where $g_i = 1/\Delta_i$, and is not quantized to $\mathbb{Z}_3$ unless $g_1=g_2=g_3$.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron continuity scenario originally proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1998), which was based on Landau's classification of phases by symmetry breaking patterns. It directly addresses the doubt raised by Cherman et al. (2018) that fractional statistics between test quarks and CFL vortices might indicate topological order incompatible with continuity. The authors show that the emergent $\mathbb{Z}_3$ two-form symmetry cannot be spontaneously broken, so the CFL phase has no deconfined topological excitations, preserving the possibility of continuity. This enables future work on whether the baryon chemical potential direction itself corresponds to a continuous deformation connecting the two phases, which requires dynamical understanding beyond symmetry arguments.
