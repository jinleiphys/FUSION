# Tensor charges and form factors of SU(3) baryons in the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model

> We investigate the tensor form factors of the baryon octet within the framework of the chiral quark-soliton model, emphasizing those of the nucleon, taking linear 1/N_c rotational as well as linear m_s corrections into account, and applying the symmetry-conserving quantization. We explicitly calculate the tensor form factors H_{T}^{q}(Q^{2}) corresponding to the generalized parton distributions H_{T}(x,ξ,t). The tensor form factors are obtained for the momentum transfer up to Q^{2}\leq1\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2} and at a renormalization scale of 0.36\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}. We find for the tensor charges δu=1.08, δd=-0.32, δs=-0.01 and discuss their physical consequences, comparing them with those from other models. Results for tensor charges for the baryon octet are also given.

## Key claim

The paper calculates tensor form factors and tensor charges of the baryon octet within the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model, taking linear 1/N_c rotational and linear m_s corrections with symmetry-conserving quantization, but the results are obtained only for momentum transfer up to Q^2 ≤ 1 GeV^2 and at a fixed renormalization scale of 0.36 GeV^2, with the hedgehog approximation and a constant constituent quark mass of 420 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the tensor form factors H_T^χ(Q^2) for the baryon octet by evaluating the nucleon matrix element of the quark tensor current in the Breit frame within the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM). The soliton is treated in the saddle-point approximation with a hedgehog pion field, and rotational zero-modes are quantized using symmetry-conserving quantization, while strange quark mass corrections are included linearly. The tensor form factors are extracted from the spatial part of the matrix element using spherical Bessel function expansions, with all model parameters fixed to meson masses and decay constants, leaving the constituent quark mass M=420 MeV as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Tensor charges at renormalization scale 0.36 GeV^2: δu = 1.08, δd = -0.32, δs = -0.01
- SU(3) χQSM tensor charges: g_T^0 = 0.76, g_T^3 = 1.40, g_T^8 = 0.45
- SU(2) χQSM tensor charges: g_T^0 = 0.75, g_T^3 = 1.44
- Axial-vector charges from same framework: g_A^0 = 0.45, g_A^3 = 1.18, g_A^8 = 0.35 (SU(3)); g_A^0 = 0.45, g_A^3 = 1.21 (SU(2))
- Momentum transfer range: Q^2 ≤ 1 GeV^2
- Renormalization scale: μ^2 = 0.36 GeV^2
- Constituent quark mass: M = 420 MeV
- Strange current quark mass: m_s = 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) χQSM calculations of tensor charges (TensorSU3, 2008) by including the symmetry-conserving quantization scheme and computing the full Q^2 dependence of the tensor form factors up to 1 GeV^2, whereas earlier work only gave the charges at Q^2=0. The results are compared with experimental extractions from SIDIS by Anselmino et al. (2009) who found δu=0.54 and δd=-0.23 at μ^2=0.8 GeV^2, and with lattice QCD calculations by Goeckeler et al. The model enables future calculation of the remaining tensor form factors E_T and H̃_T, and provides predictions for the full baryon octet that can be tested against future lattice QCD results and experimental measurements of transversity distributions.
