# Integrable wave function, describing space-time evolution of alpha-decay

> In the framework of decay theory of Goldberger and Watson we treat $α$-decay of nuclei as a transition caused by a residual interaction between the initial unperturbed bound state and the scattering states with alpha-particle. The integrable wave function for the $α$-decay is derived. The alpha-particle is described by the wave packet, having small amplitude inside the nucleus and exponentially growing in external region up to the $α$-wave front. The Moshinsky's distortions of the alpha-wave front are analyzed. It is found that the energy of the decaying level does not satisfy commonly accepted Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule for the quasibound levels. Only far from this condition the decay rate turns out to be determined by the Gamov's factor for the barrier penetrability. The derived general expression for the decay rate is approximated by the familiar quasiclassical formula.

## Key claim

The paper derives an integrable (square-integrable) wave function for alpha-decay within the Goldberger-Watson decay theory, showing that the energy of the decaying level does not satisfy the commonly accepted Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule for quasibound levels, and that only far from this condition is the decay rate determined by the Gamov factor for barrier penetrability.

## Method

The author applies the Feshbach projection-operator formalism to split the nuclear Hamiltonian into an unperturbed part \(H_0\) (with bound and scattering eigenfunctions) and a perturbation \(V'\) responsible for decay. The time evolution of the initial bound state \(\varphi_a\) (describing the parent nucleus without a pre-formed alpha-particle) is computed using the retarded Green's operator formalism of Goldberger and Watson, yielding an explicit wave packet expression for the alpha-particle that remains square-integrable. The WKB approximation is used to evaluate the radial scattering wave functions and to analyze the resonance condition and decay width.

## Key numbers

* The decay width \(\Gamma_r\) at resonance is given by \(\Gamma_r = e^{-2S_l} / [2 (d\alpha_l/dE)_r]\), where \(S_l\) is the action under the Coulomb barrier and \(\alpha_l\) is the WKB phase integral; the width scales as \(\Gamma_r \sim e^{-2S_l}\).
* The squared amplitude \(C_l^2\) far from the Bohr-Sommerfeld condition is \(C_l = (2/\pi)^{1/2} e^{-S_l} / (4 \sin\alpha_l)\), while at resonance \(C^2_{l,\text{res}} = 2 e^{2S_l} / \pi\).
* The probability of finding the parent nucleus in the initial state decays exponentially as \(P_a(t) = e^{-\Gamma t / \hbar}\).
* The asymptotic energy distribution of emitted alpha-particles is a Lorentzian: \(W_d(E, \infty) = (1/\pi) (\Gamma_d/2) / [(E-E_d)^2 + (\Gamma/2)^2]\).

## Context

This work extends the decay theory of Goldberger and Watson (1964) by constructing an explicit integrable wave function for alpha-decay, addressing the long-standing problem of non-normalizable Gamow states. It contrasts with the approach of Gamov (1928) and later treatments using complex energies or rigged Hilbert spaces, and with the wave-packet model of Sitenko, which assumed a pre-localized alpha-particle. The paper also critiques the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule used by Davydov and others for quasibound levels, showing it does not hold for the decaying state. The derived formalism enables a consistent description of alpha-decay within standard Hilbert space and can be extended to deformed nuclei via coupled-channels methods.
