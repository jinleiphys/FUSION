# Elliptic flow of hadrons in equal-velocity quark combination mechanism in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We apply a quark combination model with equal-velocity combination (EVC) approximation to study the elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions in a wide collision energy range ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 27 - 5020 GeV). Utilizing the simple relationship between $v_{2}$ of hadrons and those of quarks under EVC, we find that $v_{2}$ of up/down quarks obtained by experimental data of proton is consistent with that obtained by data of $Λ$ and $Ξ$. $v_{2}$ of strange quarks obtained by data of $Ω$ is consistent with that obtained by data of $Λ$ and $Ξ$, and at RHIC energies it is also consistent with that obtained by data of $ϕ$. This means that $v_{2}$ of these hadrons have a common quark-level source. Using data of $D^0$, we obtain $v_{2}$ of charm quarks with $p_T\lesssim 6$ GeV/c. We find that under EVC charm quark dominates $v_{2}$ of $D$ mesons at low $p_{T}$ but light-flavor quarks significantly contribute to $v_{2}$ of $D$ mesons in the range $3\lesssim p_{T}\lesssim8$ GeV/c. We predict $v_{2}$ of charmed baryons $Λ_{c}^{+}$ and $Ξ_{c}^{0}$ which show a significant enhancement at intermediate $p_{T}$ due to the double contribution of light-flavor quarks. The properties of the obtained quark $v_{2}$ under EVC are studied and a regularity for $v_{2}$ of quarks as the function of $p_{T}/m$ is found.

## Key claim

The equal-velocity combination (EVC) approximation for quark coalescence at hadronization can consistently describe the elliptic flow of identified hadrons across a wide collision energy range (27–5020 GeV), provided that the hadron's momentum is partitioned among its constituent quarks according to their masses rather than equally; the paper demonstrates this by showing that the extracted quark-level elliptic flows are consistent across different hadron species, though the phi meson at LHC energies requires an additional contribution from two-kaon coalescence to match data at intermediate transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors apply the equal-velocity combination (EVC) approximation of a quark combination model, where hadron elliptic flow is the sum of the elliptic flows of its constituent quarks evaluated at momentum fractions proportional to their constituent masses (e.g., \(x_i = m_i / \sum m_j\)). They invert these relations to extract the transverse-momentum-dependent elliptic flow of up/down, strange, and charm quarks from published experimental data of proton, Lambda, Xi, Omega, phi, and D0 mesons in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, checking consistency across different extraction channels.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range studied: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27\)–5020 GeV (27, 39, 62.4, 200, 2760, 5020 GeV)
- Constituent quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d \approx 0.3\)–0.33 GeV, \(m_s \approx 0.5\)–0.55 GeV, \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV; mass ratio \(r = m_s/m_u = 1.667\), \(r_{cu} = m_c/m_u = 5\), \(r_{cs} = m_c/m_s = 3\)
- Charm quark elliptic flow extracted for \(p_T \lesssim 6\) GeV/c from D0 data
- At LHC energies, about 20% of phi mesons with \(p_T \gtrsim 2.5\) GeV/c are estimated to come from two-kaon coalescence (\(z = 0.2\))
- In Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV (30–50% centrality), charm quark \(v_2\) reaches a peak value of about 0.13 at \(p_T \approx 2.5\) GeV/c, while light-flavor quarks peak at about 0.09 at \(p_T \approx 1.5\) GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the equal-velocity combination model to transverse momentum spectra (Song 2017, Zhang 2018, Song 2019) to the study of elliptic flow, testing the mechanism against the well-known number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling property observed by the STAR and ALICE collaborations (Adare 2006, Adamczyk 2015, Abelev 2014, Acharya 2018). The extracted quark-level elliptic flows enable predictions for charmed baryons (Lambda_c+, Xi_c0) that have not yet been measured, and the regularity found for quark \(v_2\) as a function of \(p_T/m\) provides a benchmark for hydrodynamic models such as AZHYDRO (Kolb 2000) and for future theoretical studies of heavy-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (Rapp 2009, Cao 2013).
