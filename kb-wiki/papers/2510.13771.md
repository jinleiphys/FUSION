# First simultaneous analysis of transverse momentum dependent and collinear parton distributions in the proton

> We present the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of unpolarized transverse momentum dependent (TMD) and collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the proton. Our study incorporates data from deep-inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan, inclusive weak boson, $W$+\,charm, and jet production involving PDFs, as well as TMD Drell-Yan and $Z$-boson production data from fixed target and collider experiments sensitive to both TMD and collinear distributions. The analysis is performed at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy for QCD resummation in TMD observables and next-to-leading order for observables described in collinear factorization. The combined analysis improves knowledge of both TMD and collinear PDFs, particularly in the sea-quark sector, providing a consistent simultaneous description of the aforementioned observables.

## Key claim
The first simultaneous global QCD analysis of unpolarized TMD and collinear PDFs in the proton, performed at N²LL accuracy for TMD observables and NLO for collinear observables, improves knowledge of both sectors particularly in the sea-quark sector, but the improvement is conditional on the specific dataset combination and the weak statistical correlation found between PDF and TMD parameters.

## Method
The analysis uses Bayesian Monte Carlo methodology with approximately 1000 replicas, fitting 35 collinear PDF parameters and 12 TMD parameters simultaneously to 4715 data points (4279 collinear, 436 TMD). The TMDs are constructed via the operator product expansion matching to collinear PDFs at small b_T with nonperturbative large-b_T modeling, using the ζ-prescription for TMD evolution with the Collins-Soper kernel parametrized nonperturbatively.

## Key numbers
- Total dataset: 4715 points (4279 collinear + 436 TMD)
- Total χ²/Npts = 1.10 for the combined JAM25_PDF+TMD analysis
- TMD data Z-score reduced from 3.72 (TMD-only) to 2.76 (combined), a reduction of approximately 1 unit
- ATLAS TMD data χ²/Npts improved from 2.07 to 1.78; CMS from 1.18 to 0.92
- Fixed target TMD data (E288, E605, E772) χ²/Npts = 1.31 in combined analysis
- Collinear PDF parameters: 35; TMD parameters: 12
- Input scale μ₀ = charm quark mass m_c = 1.28 GeV
- Strong coupling boundary condition α_s(M_Z) = 0.118
- B_NP = 1.5 GeV⁻¹ for b* prescription
- Kinematic cut for TMD data: q_T/Q < 0.2

## Context
This work extends the JAM Collaboration's Bayesian Monte Carlo methodology (Jimenez-Delgado 2013, Sato 2016, Ethier 2017, Cammarota 2020) by performing the first simultaneous fit of TMDs and collinear PDFs, whereas previous extractions treated them separately (Bacchetta 2017, 2019, 2022; Scimemi 2019; Moos 2020, 2025). The extracted Collins-Soper kernel agrees with lattice QCD results from Avkhadiev (2024) and other phenomenological extractions (Bacchetta 2025, Moos 2025). This enables future unified treatments including semi-inclusive DIS and e⁺e⁻ data for the Electron-Ion Collider.
