# Bulk viscosity due to kaons in color-flavor-locked quark matter

> We calculate the bulk viscosity of color-superconducting quark matter in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. We assume that the lightest bosons are the superfluid mode H and the kaons K0 and K+, and that there is no kaon condensate. We calculate the rate of strangeness-equilibrating processes that convert kaons into superfluid modes, and the resultant bulk viscosity. We find that for oscillations with a timescale of milliseconds, at temperatures T << 1 MeV, the CFL bulk viscosity is much less than that of unpaired quark matter, but at higher temperatures the bulk viscosity of CFL matter can become larger.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter is calculated under the assumption that there is no kaon condensate and that the lightest bosons are the superfluid mode H and the kaons K0 and K+. For oscillation timescales of milliseconds, the CFL bulk viscosity is much less than that of unpaired quark matter at temperatures T << 1 MeV, but can become larger at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors calculate the bulk viscosity by relating it to the rate of strangeness-equilibrating weak interaction processes that convert kaons into superfluid H modes. They derive the effective Lagrangian for the CFL phase including weak interactions, match the coupling constant G_ds to the Fermi constant G_F via a two-loop polarization diagram, and compute the K0 to H H decay rate using the H propagator with its self-energy. The bulk viscosity is then expressed in terms of the effective equilibration rate gamma_eff and a coefficient C derived from thermodynamic derivatives of the kaon and quark number densities.

## Key numbers

* The weak mixing coupling is G_ds = sqrt(2) V_ud V_us G_F, with V_ud V_us approximately 0.215(3)
* The H mode has velocity v_H^2 = 1/3 and the pion/kaon velocity v_pi^2 = 1/3
* The H decay constant is f_H^2 = (3/4)(mu^2/(2 pi^2))
* The pion decay constant is f_pi^2 = (21 - 8 log(2))/18 * (mu^2/(2 pi^2))
* The H self-energy at zero temperature scales as Pi_H^+(p) proportional to p^6/mu^4
* The H number density is n_H = zeta(3)/(pi^2 v_H^3) T^3
* The H free energy is Omega_H = -pi^2/(90 v_H^3) T^4

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity for unpaired quark matter by Madsen (1992) and Wang (1985) to the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase. It addresses the question of whether CFL quark matter could exist in compact star cores by providing a transport property relevant to r-mode damping, following the framework of Friedman (2001), Andersson (2002), and Kokkotas (2001). The calculation assumes no kaon condensation, which depends on uncertain instanton effects discussed by Schafer (2002), and enables future comparisons with observations of millisecond pulsars to potentially rule out or constrain the CFL phase.
