# Effective Field Theory for Fermi Systems in a large N expansion

> A system of fermions with short-range interactions at finite density is studied using the framework of effective field theory. The effective action formalism for fermions with auxiliary fields leads to a loop expansion in which particle-hole bubbles are resummed to all orders. For spin-independent interactions, the loop expansion is equivalent to a systematic expansion in 1/N, where "N" is the spin-isospin degeneracy g. Numerical results at next-to-leading order are presented and the connection to the Bose limit of this system is elucidated.

## Key claim

The loop expansion of the effective action for fermions with short-range, spin-independent interactions is equivalent to a systematic expansion in \(1/N\), where \(N\) is the spin-isospin degeneracy \(g\); this expansion is nonperturbative in the medium even when the description of free-space scattering remains perturbative, provided the product \(g k_F a_s / \pi\) is of order unity or greater.

## Method

The authors introduce an auxiliary field \(\sigma\) to rewrite the four-fermion contact interaction \(C_0 (\psi^\dagger \psi)^2\) as a Gaussian integral, then integrate out the fermion fields to obtain a generating functional for the \(\sigma\) field. The effective action \(\Gamma[\sigma_c]\) is expanded in a loop expansion around the classical field \(\sigma_c\), and the resulting \(\sigma\) propagator resums all fermion particle-hole bubble diagrams to all orders. For a uniform system, the leading-order effective potential is evaluated using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction (DR/MS), and the ground state energy density is obtained by solving the stationarity condition \(\delta\Gamma/\delta\sigma_c = 0\).

## Key numbers

- The number density of the system is \(n = g k_F^3 / (6\pi^2)\), where \(g\) is the spin-isospin degeneracy.
- The leading-order effective potential gives \(\sigma_c^0 = n\) at the stationary point.
- The low-density expansion of the energy density to \(\mathcal{O}(k_F^7)\) is:
  \[
  \mathcal{E} = n \frac{k_F^2}{2M} \left[ \frac{3}{5} + (g-1)\left\{ \frac{2}{3\pi}(k_F a_s) + \frac{4}{35\pi^2}(11-2\ln 2)(k_F a_s)^2 \right\} \right]
  \]
- The \(S\)-wave scattering length \(a_s\) and effective range \(r_s\) are related to the EFT coefficients by \(C_0 = 4\pi a_s / M\) and \(C_2 = C_0 a_s r_s / 2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of dilute Fermi systems from Furnstahl and Hammer (2000) to a nonperturbative regime by adopting a large-\(g\) expansion, which resums particle-hole bubbles to all orders. It provides a controlled framework for studying systems where \(g k_F a_s / \pi\) is not small, such as nuclear matter with \(g=4\), and elucidates the connection to the Bose limit first noted by Gentile (1940) and Shubert (1946) and later used by Brandow (1971) and Jackson and Wettig (1994). The approach enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections and stability analysis of the ground state.
