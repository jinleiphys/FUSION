# Substructures of heavy flavor jets in $pp$ and PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 TeV

> Groomed jet substructure measurements, the momentum splitting fraction $z_g$ and the groomed jet radius $R_g$, of inclusive, D$^0$-tagged and B$^0$-tagged jets in $pp$ and central PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV are predicted and investigated. Charged jets are constrained in a relatively low transverse momentum interval 15 $\leq p_{\rm T}^{\rm jet\ ch} <$ 30 GeV/$c$ where the QCD emissions are sensitive to mass effects. In $pp$ collisions, heavy flavor jets have less momentum-balanced splittings than inclusive jets and the larger quark mass is, the less momentum-balanced splittings are. B$^0$-tagged jets distribute at lager $R_g$ than both D$^0$-tagged and inclusive jets, while D$^0$-tagged jets distribute at smaller $R_g$ than inclusive jets. That can be explained by the competition between parton mass effects and flavour effects on splitting-angle distributions. In $A$+$A$ collisions, jet quenching effects further suppress the momentum-balanced splittings and enhance the large-angle splittings. Heavy flavor jets suffer more $z_g$ and less $R_g$ modifications due to jet-medium interactions. The mass hierarchy in $z_g$ of inclusive, D$^0$-tagged, B$^0$-tagged jets and competition between mass effects and Casimir colour factors in $R_g$ can also be observed in PbPb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in both proton-proton and lead-lead collisions, heavy flavor jets (tagged by D⁰ or B⁰ mesons) exhibit less momentum-balanced splittings than inclusive jets, with the effect increasing with quark mass; however, the groomed jet radius distributions show a competition between parton mass effects and Casimir color factor effects, meaning that D⁰-tagged jets can appear narrower than inclusive jets despite the mass effect, because inclusive jets are dominated by gluon-initiated jets which have wider splitting angles.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator with the Monash 2013 tune to simulate proton-proton collisions, and the SHELL model (Simulating Heavy quark Energy Loss with Langevin equations) coupled with a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model (CLVisc) to simulate in-medium parton evolution in lead-lead collisions. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (R=0.4) using FastJet 3.2.1, and the soft-drop grooming procedure was applied with parameters z_cut=0.1 and beta=0. The groomed momentum splitting fraction z_g and groomed jet radius R_g were computed for inclusive, D⁰-tagged, and B⁰-tagged charged jets in the transverse momentum interval 15 ≤ pT^jet,ch < 30 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momentum range: 15 ≤ pT^jet,ch < 30 GeV/c for all jet types in both pp and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV
- D⁰ meson mass: 1.86 GeV/c²; B⁰ meson mass: 5.28 GeV/c²
- Tagging heavy flavor meson transverse momentum range: 5 ≤ pT^HQ < 30 GeV/c with rapidity |y_HQ| < 0.8
- Jet pseudorapidity range: |η_jet| < 0.5
- Untagged rates (jets failing soft-drop condition) in pp collisions: inclusive jets 3.4%, D⁰-tagged jets 16.6%, B⁰-tagged jets 67.5%
- Untagged rates in PbPb collisions: inclusive jets 4.4%, D⁰-tagged jets 17.2%, B⁰-tagged jets 65.8%
- Jet transport coefficient q₀ = 1.5 GeV²/fm extracted from final-state hadron productions
- Spacial diffusion coefficient D_s(2πT) = 4, corresponding to D_s = 4/(2πT)
- Critical temperature for QGP: T_c = 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ALICE measurement of z_g and R_g for D⁰-tagged charged jets in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV (ALICE, 2022) to include predictions for B⁰-tagged jets and for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV. It builds on the SHELL model framework developed by Dai et al. (2018) and Wang et al. (2020) for simultaneous treatment of heavy and light parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The predictions enable systematic comparison of mass-dependent jet substructure modifications in heavy-ion collisions, isolating the competing effects of parton mass and Casimir color factors on splitting-angle distributions.
