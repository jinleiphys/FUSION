# Dynamic universality class of large-N gauge theories

> In dynamic critical phenomena, singular behaviors appear not only in the order parameter but also in the other transport coefficients (due to the mode-mode coupling). However, this effect has not been observed in the AdS/CFT duality. We point out that this mode-mode coupling is suppressed by 1/N^2 in the large-N gauge theories which correspond to model H in the classification of Hohenberg and Halperin. Thus, the effect cannot be seen in the classical supergravity approximation. We illustrate this point using the example of the N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory at a finite chemical potential. We also discuss the implications of this result to heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The mode-mode coupling effect that causes singular behavior in transport coefficients like shear viscosity and charge conductivity in dynamic critical phenomena is suppressed by a factor of 1/N_c^2 in large-N gauge theories, meaning this effect cannot be seen in the classical supergravity approximation of AdS/CFT and only emerges from one-loop computations.

## Method

The authors analyze the dynamic universality class of large-N gauge theories using the classification scheme of Hohenberg and Halperin, focusing on model H which includes conserved order parameters coupled to energy-momentum tensor. They perform a 1/N_c counting argument comparing the model B (diffusive) contribution to conductivity, which scales as O(N_c^2), with the model H (convective) contribution, which scales as O(1). They illustrate their argument using the N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory at finite chemical potential, whose gravity dual is the five-dimensional R-charged black hole.

## Key numbers

- The mode-mode coupling is suppressed by 1/N_c^2 in large-N gauge theories
- For the N=4 SYM, the R-charge susceptibility diverges at kappa=2, where chi = (N_c^2 T_0^2/8) * (2 + 5 kappa - kappa^2)/(2 - kappa)
- The R-charge conductivity remains finite at the critical point: lambda = (kappa + 2)^2 N_c^2 T_0 / (64 pi sqrt(1 + kappa))
- The shear viscosity remains finite at the critical point: eta = (pi N_c^2 T_0^3/8) * sqrt(1 + kappa)
- For d_s=3, model H dynamic exponents satisfy x_lambda + x_eta = 4 - d_s - eta, with x_lambda = 0.946 and x_eta = 0.054 (assuming eta = 0)
- The model H diffusion constant D_H ~ (32/(27 pi^2)) * (1/(N_c^2 T_c^2)) * (1/xi)
- The model B diffusion constant D_B ~ (1/(6 pi T_c)) * (2 - kappa)
- The correlation length near the critical point satisfies xi^{-2} ~ 0.12 (2 - kappa) (2 pi T_c)^2

## Context

This work extends the study of dynamic critical phenomena in AdS/CFT duality, which had previously been examined in papers by Maeda (2009), Buchel (2010), and Natsuume (2010). The authors resolve a puzzle where the N=4 SYM appeared to belong to model B rather than model H despite having conserved energy-momentum tensor, by showing the mode-mode coupling is a 1/N_c^2 effect invisible at leading order. The result has implications for heavy-ion collisions at the QCD critical end point, where Son and Stephanov (2004) argued QCD belongs to model H, but the 1/N_c^2 suppression raises questions about whether model H behavior is distinguishable from model B in real experiments.
