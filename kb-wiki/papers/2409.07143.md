# Resummed spin hydrodynamics from quantum kinetic theory

> In this work, the equations of dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics based on quantum kinetic theory are derived. Employing the inverse-Reynolds dominance (IReD) approach, a resummation scheme based on a power counting in Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers is constructed, leading to hydrodynamic equations that are accurate to second order. It is found that the spin dynamics can be characterized by eleven equations: six of them describe the evolution of the components of the spin potential, while the remaining five provide the equation of motion of a dissipative irreducible rank-two tensor. For a simple truncation, the first- and second-order transport coefficients are computed explicitly.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete set of dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamic equations from quantum kinetic theory using the inverse-Reynolds dominance (IReD) resummation scheme, reducing the number of dynamical variables from 30 to 11, but the derivation assumes spin effects are small (order hbar) and neglects feedback of spin on fluid flow, meaning phenomena like the Einstein-de Haas effect are omitted.

## Method

Starting from a quantum kinetic framework accurate to first order in an hbar-gradient expansion, the author expands the distribution function in irreducible moments in momentum space and develops a power-counting scheme in Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers (along with their quantum analogues) to close the infinite hierarchy of moment equations. The collision term is split into local and nonlocal parts, with the nonlocal part providing the mechanism for spin-orbital angular momentum exchange. The resulting equations of motion for the spin potential components (omega_0^mu and kappa_0^mu) and a dissipative rank-two tensor (t^mu^nu) are obtained by applying the IReD resummation procedure to the moment equations.

## Key numbers

* The spin dynamics is characterized by 11 equations: 6 for the components of the spin potential and 5 for a dissipative irreducible rank-two tensor.
* The relaxation time tau_omega (in units of the mean free path lambda_mfp) can become much larger than tau_kappa and tau_t, as noted from Ref. Wagner (2024).
* In the ultrarelativistic limit, all couplings to the tensor t^mu^nu vanish, so spin dynamics can be described through omega_0^mu and kappa_0^mu only.
* The first- and second-order transport coefficients are computed explicitly for a simple truncation in the ultra- and nonrelativistic limits, with values listed in Tables 1-3 and plotted in Figures 1-4.

## Context

This work extends the ideal-spin hydrodynamics analysis of Wagner (2024), which found that spin potential components follow damped wave equations with potentially large relaxation times, and the dissipative spin tensor analysis of Weickgenannt et al. (2022), which found relaxation times for dissipative spin degrees of freedom comparable to standard dissipative quantities. The resummed equations enable numerical studies of spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for explaining local polarization of Lambda baryons, and provide a relativistic generalization of the Bloch-Torrey equations for spin relaxation and diffusion.
