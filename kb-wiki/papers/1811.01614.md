# Temporal Correlators in the Continuous Time Formulation of Strong Coupling Lattice QCD

> We present results for lattice QCD in the limit of infinite gauge coupling on a discrete spatial but continuous Euclidean time lattice. A worm type Monte Carlo algorithm is applied in order to sample two-point functions which gives access to the measurement of mesonic temporal correlators. The continuous time limit, based on sending $N_τ\rightarrow \infty$ and the bare anistotropy to infinity while fixing the temperature in a non-perturbative setup, has various advantages: the algorithm is sign problem free, fast, and accumulates high statistics for correlation functions. Even though the measurement of temporal correlators requires the introduction of a binning in time direction, this discretization can be chosen to be by orders finer compared to discrete computations. For different spatial volumes, temporal correlators are measured at zero spatial momentum for a variety of mesonic operators. They are fitted to extract the pole masses and corresponding particles as a function of the temperature. We conclude discussing the possibility to extract transport coefficients from these correlators.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that temporal correlators and pole masses extracted from the continuous time (CT) formulation of strong coupling lattice QCD agree with discrete time results, while the CT approach removes the sign problem completely and allows a much finer temporal discretization than is practical in discrete time simulations.

## Method
A worm-type Monte Carlo algorithm was applied to sample monomer-monomer two-point correlation functions in the continuous Euclidean time limit of strong coupling QCD, where the gauge coupling is sent to infinity and the gauge fields are integrated out analytically. The CT limit is achieved by sending the temporal lattice extent \(N_\tau \rightarrow \infty\) and the bare anisotropy \(\gamma \rightarrow \infty\) while fixing the temperature \(aT = \kappa\gamma^2/N_\tau\). Temporal correlators at zero spatial momentum were constructed from binned histogram data for even and odd temporal distances, then fitted to extract pole masses for various mesonic channels using a cosh ansatz.

## Key numbers
- The chiral transition for SU(3) is located at \(aT \approx 1.403\).
- The non-perturbative anisotropy relation is \(\xi(\gamma) \simeq \kappa\gamma^2 + \gamma^2/(1+\lambda\gamma^4)\) with \(\kappa = 0.781(1)\) for SU(3).
- The Poisson process for spatial dimer emissions has decay constant \(\lambda = d_M(x,t)/4\), where \(d_M(x,t) = 2d - \sum_{\hat{\mu}} n_B(x+\hat{\mu})\).
- The CT partition function for \(N_c=3\) includes a vertex weight \(\hat{\nu}_\bot = 2/\sqrt{3}\).
- Pole masses were measured in units of \(M/T\) over a temperature range \(aT = \{1.0, \dots, 2.0\}\).

## Context
This work extends the strong coupling lattice QCD approach of Rossi (1984) and the continuous time formulation first proposed for quantum field theories by Beard and Wiese (1996). It builds on non-perturbative studies of the anisotropy parameter by Unger (2017) and uses a worm algorithm similar to the directed path algorithm introduced by Adams (2003). The results enable future extraction of transport coefficients such as the diffusion constant from spectral functions, and the method can be extended to finite quark masses, multiple flavors via a Hamiltonian formulation, and inclusion of \(\beta\)-corrections to move away from the strong coupling limit.
