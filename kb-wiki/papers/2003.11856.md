# Shadowing in inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section?

> Experimental results of inclusive hard-process cross sections in heavy-ion collisions conventionally lean on a normalization computed from Glauber models where the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section $σ_{\rm nn}^{\rm inel}$ -- a crucial input parameter -- is simply taken from proton-proton measurements. In this letter, using the computed electro-weak boson production cross sections in lead-lead collisions as a benchmark, we determine $σ_{\rm nn}^{\rm inel}$ from the recent ATLAS data. We find a significantly suppressed $σ_{\rm nn}^{\rm inel}$ relative to what is usually assumed, show the consequences for the centrality dependence of the cross sections, and address the phenomenon in an eikonal minijet model with nuclear shadowing.

## Key claim
The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section in lead-lead collisions at 5.02 TeV is significantly suppressed relative to the value measured in proton-proton collisions, with the suppression derived by comparing NNLO pQCD calculations for electroweak boson production to ATLAS data rather than assuming the proton-proton value.

## Method
The authors computed W± and Z boson production cross sections in lead-lead collisions at NNLO using the MCFM code with NNPDF3.1 proton PDFs and EPPS16 nuclear PDFs. They equated the theoretical nuclear modification ratio R_PbPb to the experimental ratio from ATLAS data, which depends on the mean nuclear overlap function ⟨T_AA⟩ obtained from the TGlauberMC Monte Carlo Glauber model. By requiring a match between theory and experiment, they performed a χ² fit to extract the value of σ_nn^inel, treating EPPS16 uncertainties as Gaussian correlated errors with 20 error-set pairs.

## Key numbers
- Fitted σ_nn^inel = 41.5^{+16.2}_{-12.0} mb at √s_nn = 5.02 TeV, compared to the nominal σ_pp^inel = 70 ± 5 mb from proton-proton measurements
- For 0-80% centrality, ⟨T_AA⟩ = 6.993 mb⁻¹ with σ_nn^inel = 70 mb versus 7.385 mb⁻¹ with the fitted σ_nn^inel = 41.5 mb
- For 0-100% centrality, ⟨T_AA⟩ = 5.605 mb⁻¹ with σ_nn^inel = 70 mb versus 5.923 mb⁻¹ with the fitted σ_nn^inel = 41.5 mb
- The χ² fit used 1+20 parameters (σ_nn^inel plus 20 EPPS16 error-set coefficients) with tolerance T = 1.645²

## Context
This work extends the proposal of Paukkunen (2010) to test Glauber model inputs using electroweak boson production as standard candles, now enabled by the few-percent precision of ATLAS Run II data (Aad 2019). The results contradict the standard practice of taking σ_nn^inel from proton-proton measurements as in the COMPETE analysis (Cudell 2002), and instead support predictions from eikonal minijet models with nuclear shadowing (Wang 1990). The finding resolves the unexpected enhancement in peripheral R_PbPb seen by ATLAS and enables more accurate normalization for jet quenching and partonic energy loss studies that rely on Eq. (1) for centrality-dependent cross sections.
