# The Lambda* N interaction and two-body bound state based on chiral dynamics

> The interaction of Lambda*=Lambda(1405) with a nucleon is studied from the viewpoint of chiral dynamics. We construct the coordinate space Lambda* N potential in the meson-exchange picture, which serves as a fundamental ingredient for the study of the few-body nuclear systems with a Lambda*, the Lambda*-hypernuclei. The coupling constants concerning Lambda* are determined based on the chiral unitary model picture for the meson-baryon scattering where Lambda* is described as a superposition of two resonance poles. Solving the coupled-channel two-body Lambda* N system, we find the higher energy Lambda* N state develops an s-wave quasi-bound state slightly below the threshold in the total spin S=0 channel, which acquires a finite width through the coupling to the lower energy Lambda* N channel. We show important roles of the Kbar exchange contribution to the Lambda* N potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Lambda^* N$ system (with $\Lambda^* = \Lambda(1405)$) can form an $s$-wave quasi-bound state slightly below threshold in the total spin $S=0$ channel, but this result depends on the specific chiral unitary model used to describe $\Lambda^*$ as a superposition of two resonance poles, and the bound state acquires a finite width through coupling between the two $\Lambda^* N$ channels.

## Method

The authors constructed a coordinate-space $\Lambda^* N$ potential using a one-boson-exchange picture with isoscalar $\sigma$ and $\omega$ meson exchanges and $\bar{K}$ exchange, where the $\Lambda^*$ is treated as an elementary particle with two resonance pole components ($\Lambda^*_1$ at 1427 MeV and $\Lambda^*_2$ at 1400 MeV) from the HNJH chiral unitary model. The coupling constants for the $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*\sigma$ and $\Lambda^*\Lambda^*\omega$ vertices were estimated from loop diagrams using the microscopic meson-baryon structure of $\Lambda^*$, and the $\Lambda^* N \bar{K}$ coupling was taken from the residues of the chiral unitary model poles. A two-channel coupled Schr\"odinger equation was solved for the $\Lambda^*_1 N$ and $\Lambda^*_2 N$ components.

## Key numbers

- The higher energy $\Lambda^*_1$ pole mass is 1427 MeV and the lower energy $\Lambda^*_2$ pole mass is 1400 MeV, from the HNJH chiral unitary model.
- The $\Lambda^* N \bar{K}$ coupling constant $g_{\Lambda^*_a N \bar{K}} / \sqrt{4\pi}$ is 0.55 for $\Lambda^*_1$ and 0.44 for $\Lambda^*_2$.
- The effective $\bar{K}$ mass used in the $\bar{K}$ exchange potential is 91 MeV for the $\Lambda^*_1 N$ diagonal component, 184 MeV for the $\Lambda^*_2 N$ diagonal component, and 146 MeV for the off-diagonal component.
- The volume integral of the $\Lambda^*_1 N$ potential is $-2602$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ for the $S=0$ channel and $4560$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ for the $S=1$ channel.
- The $\Lambda^* N \bar{K}$ coupling strength $g^2_{\Lambda^*NK}/4\pi$ is approximately 0.2-0.3, which is almost an order of magnitude larger than the value of 0.064 used in the earlier work by Arai (2007).

## Context

This work extends the "$\Lambda^*$-hypernuclei" picture proposed by Arai (2007), where multi-baryon systems with strangeness $S=-1$ are treated as composites of $\Lambda^*$ and nucleons, by replacing the phenomenological $\Lambda^* N$ interaction with one derived from chiral dynamics. The study provides a fundamental input for few-body calculations of $\bar{K}$-nuclei and complements the variational studies by Yamazaki (2007) and Dote (2008) which found that $\bar{K}N$ pairs in $\bar{K}NN$ bound states have large overlap with $\Lambda^*$. The results enable future rigorous calculations of $\Lambda^*$-hypernuclei and may help resolve quantitative discrepancies among theoretical predictions for $\bar{K}NN$ bound states.
