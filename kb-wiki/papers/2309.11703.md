# Causality and classical dispersion relations

> We explore the consequences of relativistic causality and covariant stability for short-wavelength dispersion relations in classical systems. For excitations described by a finite number of partial differential equations, as is the case in relativistic hydrodynamics, we give causality and covariant stability constraints on the excitation's frequency at large momenta.

# Causality and classical dispersion relations

## Key claim

The paper derives necessary and sufficient conditions for relativistic causality and covariant stability in classical theories described by a finite number of partial differential equations, focusing on constraints on dispersion relations at large momenta. The authors show that classical causality requires three conditions on the dispersion relations, not just the commonly used sub-luminal propagation speed, and that the large-momentum expansion of dispersion relations in causal classical theories must proceed with integer powers of 1/k before the first even-integer term.

## Method

The authors analyze linearized partial differential equations describing small excitations about an equilibrium state, obtaining dispersion relations from the polynomial spectral curve F(ω,k)=0. They apply the covariant stability condition Im(ω) ≤ |Im(k)| (which follows from microscopic causality in relativistic quantum theory) to constrain the large-momentum behavior of eigenfrequencies. Using Puiseux theorem for expansions of algebraic functions at infinity, they derive constraints on the leading-order behavior and the structure of subleading terms in the large-k expansion of ω(k).

## Key numbers

- The leading-order coefficient c₁ in the large-k expansion ω(k) ~ c₁k must satisfy 0 ≤ |c₁| ≤ 1, with c₁ real
- The large-k expansion takes the form ω(k) = Σ_{n=0}^{n₀} c_{1-2n} k^{1-2n} + c_{-2n₀} k^{-2n₀} + ..., where all c_{1-2n} are real and Im(c_{-2n₀}) ≤ 0
- For the diffusion equation with relaxation time τ, the dispersion relation ω(k) = -iDk²/(1+τDk²) interpolates between diffusive behavior at small k and constant ω = -i/τ at large k
- For the telegraph equation, the dispersion relation at short wavelength is ω(k) = ±c_s|k| + ... with 0 ≤ c_s ≤ 1
- For Müller-Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics of conformal fluids, causality is preserved for γ/τ < 1/2, where γ is the diffusion constant for transverse momentum density and τ is the stress relaxation time
- In strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, the large-k dispersion relations contain fractional powers: ω_n^±(k) = ±|k| ± c_n e^{∓iπ/3} (πT)^{4/3}|k|^{-1/3} + ..., which cannot be reproduced by any covariantly stable classical theory in 3+1 dimensions

## Context

This work extends the application of the covariant stability condition Im(ω) ≤ |Im(k)| (textbook material from relativistic quantum theory, as in Itzykson (1980)) to large-momentum dispersion relations in classical theories, complementing recent work by Heller (2022, 2023) who applied the same inequality to small-momentum expansions in relativistic hydrodynamics. The paper builds on the understanding that causal theories of relativistic hydrodynamics require non-hydrodynamic (gapped) modes, as appreciated by Geroch (1995), and provides a systematic algorithmic procedure for checking causality and covariant stability of linear classical theories. The results enable precise quantification of what classical theories can and cannot mimic from the large-momentum behavior of fundamental quantum field theories, and provide guidance for constructing causal effective descriptions such as relativistic hydrodynamics.
