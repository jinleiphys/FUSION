# Uniqueness of Fisher plots and their inferred (nuclear) liquid-vapor phase diagrams

> A detailed study of the lattice gas (Ising) model shows that the correct definition of cluster concentration in the vapor at coexistence with the liquid leads to Fisher plots that are unique, independent of fixed particle number density $ρ_{\text{fixed}}$, and completely reliable for the characterization of the liquid-vapor phase diagram of any van der Waals systems including nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Fisher plots of cluster concentrations from the lattice gas (Ising) model are unique and independent of fixed particle number density, but only when the vapor cluster concentrations are correctly defined using the area available to the vapor (excluding the liquid drop and its skin thickness) and only when the system is at liquid-vapor coexistence; previous claims of non-uniqueness arose from improper application of Fisher's theory.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional Ising model (lattice gas) calculations on an 80x80 square lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using the Heringa and Blote method. Three fixed magnetizations corresponding to mean densities of 0.2, 0.1, and 0.05 were studied, with ground-state liquid drops of 1280, 640, and 320 spins respectively. Vapor clusters were identified using the Coniglio-Klein algorithm to ensure physical (Ising) critical exponents, while the largest cluster was identified geometrically as the liquid drop. The vapor density was computed by summing cluster yields normalized to the area available to the vapor (total lattice area minus liquid drop area including skin thickness), and the pressure was obtained from the ideal gas law for clusters.

## Key numbers

- Three fixed mean densities studied: 0.2, 0.1, and 0.05 (in units of spins per lattice site), corresponding to ground-state liquid drops of 1280, 640, and 320 spins respectively on an L=80 lattice
- Critical temperature extracted from fits: 2.24 +/- 0.01 (density 0.2), 2.25 +/- 0.02 (density 0.1), and 2.25 +/- 0.03 (density 0.05), all consistent with the Onsager value of 2.26915
- Critical pressure extracted: 0.114 +/- 0.003 (density 0.2), 0.115 +/- 0.002 (density 0.1), and 0.12 +/- 0.01 (density 0.05), compared to the analytic value of 0.1097
- Compressibility factor C_F: 0.1021 +/- 0.0007 (density 0.2), 0.102 +/- 0.001 (density 0.1), and 0.102 +/- 0.001 (density 0.05), compared to the analytic value of 0.0966
- Surface energy coefficient c_0 from fits: 8.7 +/- 0.1 (density 0.2), 8.2 +/- 0.2 (density 0.1), and 7.8 +/- 0.2 (density 0.05)
- Reduced chi-squared values for the fits: 1.85 (density 0.2), 1.41 (density 0.1), and 1.56 (density 0.05)
- A shift of only about 0.45% in T_c is expected for the L=80 lattice due to finite size effects

## Context

This work extends the application of Fisher's droplet model to nuclear multifragmentation data by Moretto (2004) and Elliott (2002), and directly addresses claims by Gulminelli (2002) and D'Agostino (2003) that Ising cluster concentrations yield different critical points for different mean densities. The authors demonstrate that those claims resulted from improper definition of vapor cluster concentrations, and that with correct treatment (normalizing to the area available to the vapor rather than total area, and restricting analysis to coexistence conditions), the Fisher plot method yields unique phase diagrams consistent with the analytic Onsager solution. This enables reliable extraction of nuclear liquid-vapor phase diagrams from experimental multifragmentation data using the Fisher plot approach.
