# Complete experiment problem for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon in a truncated partial-wave analysis

> It is shown that within a truncated partial wave analysis for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons, it is possible to halve the number of independent partial amplitudes by taking into account parity conservation. In addition, within the framework of the proposed formalism, one can rather easily resolve the double discrete ambiguity arising from the symmetry under complex conjugation of the helicity amplitudes. This leads to essential simplifications of the complete experiment problem, at least as far as mathematical aspects are concerned.

# Complete experiment problem for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon in a truncated partial-wave analysis

## Key claim
Within a truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA) for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons, parity conservation can halve the number of independent partial amplitudes, and the double discrete ambiguity from complex conjugation of helicity amplitudes can be resolved, but only when the azimuthal angle dependence is split off as a phase factor, enabling relations between partial amplitudes at the same kinematic point rather than at different points as in the full spin-amplitude treatment.

## Method
The authors derive a partial-wave expansion of the transition amplitude for reactions such as γN → ππN, choosing the quantization axis along the photon momentum and splitting off the Φ dependence of the helicity amplitudes as a phase factor e^(iMΦ). Using the Wigner-Eckart theorem and sum rules for rotation matrices, they obtain a relation (Eq. 17) linking partial amplitudes t^(J-M)_{-ν-λ-μ} to t^(JM)_{νλμ} via a factor (-1)^M, which reduces the number of independent partial amplitudes by half. They then analyze the discrete ambiguity arising from complex conjugation of the helicity amplitudes and show it leaves the four single-polarization observables (I, beam, target, recoil) invariant while changing the sign of three others.

## Key numbers
- For J_max = 3/2, the total number of independent partial amplitudes is reduced from 40 to 20 by parity conservation.
- The total number of amplitudes t^(JM)_{νλμ} after applying the symmetry is 4(J_max + 1)^2 − 5, which for J_max = 3/2 gives 20 amplitudes.
- For the set of four single-polarization observables (I, T̂^l_00, Ŝ^0_11, P̂^0_y), truncation at J_max = 3/2 gives 53 independent expansion coefficients u^α_{jm′m} at each point (ω₁, ω₂) of the Dalitz plot, of which 39 can be chosen to fix all 20 complex amplitudes up to a common phase and discrete ambiguities.
- In the single-meson case for J_max = 3/2, the analogous complete set requires only 11 observables, compared to 79 observables needed for two-meson production at the same J_max.

## Context
This work extends the truncated partial-wave analysis approach previously developed for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (Grushin, Omelaenko, Tiator, Wunderlich, and others) to the more complex case of two pseudoscalar mesons. It contrasts with the earlier formalism of Fix and Arndt (FiArTrunc), where the quantization axis orthogonal to the reaction plane did not allow a simple parity relation at the same kinematic point. The results enable practical TPWA for reactions like γN → π⁰π⁰N at energies below the N(1520)3/2⁻ resonance, where partial waves with J ≤ 3/2 dominate according to analyses by Laget, Oset, Ochi, Prakhov, and Sarantsev. The formalism also resolves the double discrete ambiguity analogous to that found in single-meson photoproduction by Fix and Dementjev (FixDem).
