# The "soft ridge" -- is it initial-state geometry or modified jets?

> An $η$-elongated same-side 2D peak ("soft ridge") in minimum-bias angular correlations from heavy ion collisions has been attributed both to jet formation and to initial-state geometry structure coupled to radial flow. We consider evidence for both interpretations.

## Key claim

The same-side (SS) 2D peak in minimum-bias angular correlations from heavy ion collisions, often called the "soft ridge," can be fully explained as a manifestation of minimum-bias jets (minijets) rather than initial-state geometry coupled to radial flow; the paper argues that Fourier decomposition of the SS peak projected onto azimuth produces all the "higher harmonic flow" signals, and that the distinction between jet and nonjet structures depends on careful analysis of the peak's elongation on pseudorapidity.

## Method

The author compared two interpretations of the same-side 2D peak in minimum-bias angular correlations from Au-Au collisions at RHIC: a minijet interpretation based on 2D Gaussian fits to the peak, and a flow interpretation based on projecting the peak onto azimuth and fitting with Fourier series. The analysis used 2D model fits to separate the SS 2D peak from the nonjet azimuth quadrupole, exploiting the strong pseudorapidity dependence of the SS peak to distinguish jet-related multipoles from the nonjet quadrupole uniform in pseudorapidity. Centrality trends of peak parameters were compared to Glauber linear superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions and to pQCD jet calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 0-5% central 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, the SS 2D peak projected onto azimuth yields Fourier-derived flow coefficients: v2{2} = 0.026, v3{2} = 0.015, v4{2} = 0.007
- RMS residuals from the 2D model fit to the SS peak in 0-5% central Au-Au are approximately 0.5% of the SS peak amplitude
- The SS 2D peak amplitude varies as N_bin / n_ch approximately equal to nu = 2N_bin / N_part for Glauber linear superposition, with a sharp transition at nu approximately equal to 3
- The nonjet quadrupole A_Q{2D} varies as N_bin epsilon_opt^2 proportional to nu^4 epsilon_opt^2
- The SS 1D peak width for more-central 200 GeV Au-Au collisions is sigma_phiDelta = 0.65

## Context

This work extends the author's previous minijet analyses of angular correlations (Porter 2006, anomalous 2008, fragevo 2009, jetsyield 2010) and directly contradicts recent flow-based reinterpretations of the "soft ridge" (GMM 2010, Gunther 2010, Sorensen 2010). The paper enables a critical re-evaluation of "higher harmonic flow" results from both RHIC and LHC by showing they can be predicted from measured minijet systematics, and warns that ZYAM subtraction of jet-related quadrupoles effectively subtracts jets from jets, distorting the inferred jet modification in nuclear collisions.
