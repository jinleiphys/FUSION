# Constraints on shear stress tensor in viscous relativistic hydrodynamics

> We extend our hybrid model HydHSD by taking into account shear viscosity within the Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics. The influence of different forms of $π^{μν}$ constraints on observables is analyzed. We show that the form of the corresponding condition plays an important role for the sensitivity of viscous hydrodynamics to the ratio of shear viscosity to the entropy density, $η/s$. It is shown that the constraint used in the vHLLE model, results in most sensitivity of rapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra to a change of the $η/s$ ratio; however, their applicability for large values of $η/s$ is doubtful. On the contrary, the strict constraints from \cite{MNR2010} are very strong but most established. We also found that $η/s$ as a function of the collision energy probably has an extremum at $E_{\rm lab}=10.7$ AGeV. However, we obtain that any considered condition does not allow to reproduce simultaneously pion and proton experimental data within our model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the specific form of the constraint applied to the shear stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) in Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics critically determines the sensitivity of observables to the shear viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio \(\eta/s\); however, the authors find that no constraint they tested allows their hybrid model to simultaneously reproduce both pion and proton experimental data.

## Method

The authors extended their hybrid model HydHSD by incorporating shear viscosity within the Israel-Stewart framework, solving the conservation equations for energy, momentum, and baryon number together with relaxation-type equations for the shear stress tensor. Initial conditions for the hydrodynamic stage were generated from the Hadron String Dynamics (HSD) kinetic model, and the fluid-to-particle conversion was performed using the Cooper-Frye formula with a viscous correction to the distribution function. The numerical solution used the SHASTA algorithm for the ideal-fluid part and a centered second-order difference scheme for the shear stress propagation, with a strict rescaling condition (S-cond.) applied to keep the shear stress tensor components below a fraction \(C=0.3\) of the ideal-fluid tensor components.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at \(\Elab = 10.7\) AGeV, the ratio \(\eta/s\) as a function of collision energy "probably has an extremum" at that energy.
* The strict constraint (S-cond.) uses a predefined constant \(C=0.3\) to rescale \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) when \(q = \max_{\mu,\nu} |\pi^{\mu\nu}| / |T_{\rm id}^{\mu\nu}|\) exceeds this value.
* Starting times for hydrodynamic evolution range from \(t_{\rm start} = 7.9\) fm/\(c\) at \(\Elab = 6\) AGeV down to \(t_{\rm start} = 3.01\) fm/\(c\) at \(\Elab = 158\) AGeV.
* For Pb+Pb collisions at 40 AGeV, calculations with \(\eta/s = 0.01\) and \(\eta/s = 0.1\) show that the two-hump structure in the proton rapidity distribution becomes much more pronounced for the viscous case, while the height of the proton distribution at mid-rapidity (\(y=0\)) is almost independent of \(\eta/s\).
* The freeze-out temperature \(\Tfrz = 160\) MeV was used as a fixed value in the viscosity scan; lower \(\Tfrz\) values increase the humps in the proton \(y\)-distribution similarly to increasing \(\eta/s\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid model HydHSD (Khvorostukhin et al., 2015) from ideal hydrodynamics to include shear viscosity, following the Israel-Stewart framework. It compares different constraint prescriptions for the shear stress tensor, specifically the strict condition from MNR2010 and the condition used in the vHLLE model, finding that the vHLLE-type constraint gives the strongest sensitivity to \(\eta/s\) but is doubtful for large \(\eta/s\) values. The study enables future work to test whether a different equation of state or a different initial-state model could resolve the tension between simultaneous description of pion and proton data, which the current hadronic-only equation of state cannot achieve.
