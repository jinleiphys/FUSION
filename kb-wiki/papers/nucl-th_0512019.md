# Dipole and Quadrupole electroexcitations of the isovector T=1 particle-hole states in C-12

> Electroexcitations of the dominantly T=1 particle-hole states of C-12 are studied in the framework of the harmonic oscillator shell model. All possible T=1 single-particle-hole states of all allowed angular momenta are considered in a basis including single-particle states up to the 1f-2p shell. The Hamiltonian is diagnoalized in this space in the presence of the modified surface delta interaction. Correlation in the ground state wave functions by mixing more than one configuration is considered and shows a major contribution that leads to enhance the calculations of the form factors. A comparison with the experiment shows that this model is able to fit the location of states and a simple scaling of the results give a good fit to the experimental form factors.

## Key claim

The harmonic oscillator shell model with a modified surface delta interaction (MSDI) and configuration mixing in the ground state can reproduce the experimental form factors for dipole and quadrupole electroexcitations of T=1 states in C-12, but only after applying empirical scaling factors to the calculated amplitudes (a factor of 2 reduction for positive-parity states and a factor of 1.16 reduction for negative-parity states) and, for the M2 transition, using a reduced oscillator parameter.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the Hamiltonian in a Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) basis of all possible T=1 single-particle-hole states up to the 1f-2p shell, using the modified surface delta interaction (MSDI) with strength parameters A0=0.8 MeV, A1=1.0 MeV, B=0.7 MeV, and C=-0.3 MeV. Ground state correlations were included by mixing the closed 1s1/2 and 1p3/2 shell configuration with a higher configuration involving the 2s1/2 and 2p3/2 shells. The extended space calculations included particle-hole configurations where both the particle and hole could occupy either the original or the next-higher oscillator shell.

## Key numbers

* The 1+ state is calculated at 15.54 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 15.11 MeV; the 2+ state is calculated at 15.49 MeV, compared to 16.11 MeV experimentally; the 2- state is calculated at 18.19 MeV, compared to 16.58 MeV experimentally.
* The oscillator parameter b=1.64 fm was used for the M1, C2, and E2 form factor calculations, while b=1.35 fm was used for the M2 form factor.
* The M1 form factor data are described up to momentum transfer 1.7 fm^{-1} using extended space configurations.
* The Coulomb C2 form factor data are described for momentum transfer q less than or equal to 2.2 fm^{-1} using effective charges of 1.25e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons.
* The M2 form factor amplitudes were quenched by 86%, and a diffraction minimum was obtained at q approximately 0.6 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the earlier particle-hole calculations of Donnelly (1970) for C-12 by including a larger basis up to the 1f-2p shell and incorporating ground state correlations through configuration mixing. The results are compared with experimental data from Flanz et al. (1978), Deutschmann et al. (1983), and Sato et al. (1985), and the authors note that their M1 results are consistent with those of Sato et al. (1985) where meson exchange current contributions were found to be minor. The approach enables systematic testing of shell model predictions for electron scattering form factors across multiple multipolarities in light nuclei.
