# Octupole deformation in light actinides within an analytic quadrupole octupole axially symmetric model with Davidson potential

> The analytic quadrupole octupole axially symmetric model, which had successfully predicted 226Ra and 226Th as lying at the border between the regions of octupole deformation and octupole vibrations in the light actinides using an infinite well potential (AQOA-IW), is made applicable to a wider region of nuclei exhibiting octupole deformation, through the use of a Davidson potential (AQOA-D). Analytic expressions for energy spectra and B(E1), B(E2), B(E3) transition rates are derived. The spectra of 222-226Ra and 224,226Th are described in terms of the two parameters phi_0 (expressing the relative amount of octupole vs. quadrupole deformation) and beta_0 (the position of the minimum of the Davidson potential), while the recently determined B(EL) transition rates of 224Ra, presenting stable octupole deformation, are successfully reproduced. A procedure for gradually determining the parameters appearing in the B(EL) transitions from a minimum set of data, thus increasing the predictive power of the model, is outlined.

## Key claim

The analytic quadrupole octupole axially symmetric model with a Davidson potential (AQOA-D) extends the earlier AQOA-IW model, which used an infinite well potential and was limited to nuclei at the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations, to a wider region of nuclei exhibiting octupole deformation by introducing an additional free parameter, β₀ (the position of the minimum of the Davidson potential), while still describing spectra using only two parameters (φ₀ and β₀) after normalization to the first excited state.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian for quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom assuming axial symmetry, using a Davidson potential of the form β² + β₀⁴/β² for the β degree of freedom and two steep harmonic oscillators centered at ±φ₀ for the φ degree of freedom, achieving separation of variables. Analytic expressions for energy eigenvalues (involving Laguerre polynomials) and for B(E1), B(E2), and B(E3) transition rates are derived, with the φ₀ parameter expressing the relative amount of octupole versus quadrupole deformation. The model is applied to the spectra of ²²²⁻²²⁶Ra and ²²⁴,²²⁶Th, and a stepwise procedure is outlined for determining the parameters appearing in B(EL) transitions from a minimum set of experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the earlier AQOA-IW model (Bonatsos et al., 2015) which used an infinite well potential and successfully predicted ²²⁶Ra and ²²⁶Th as lying at the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations. The new AQOA-D model, by replacing the infinite well with a Davidson potential, becomes applicable to a wider region of light actinides including ²²²⁻²²⁴Ra and ²²⁴Th, which exhibit stable octupole deformation. The model successfully reproduces the recently measured electric transition probabilities of ²²⁴Ra (reported in 2013), which had also been addressed by the Bizzeti and Bizzeti-Sona approach (1988), and provides a framework for predicting B(EL) transition rates in other octupole-deformed nuclei from a minimum set of data.
