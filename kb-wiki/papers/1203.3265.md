# Azimuthal anisotropy in a jet absorption model with fluctuating initial geometry in heavy ion collisions

> The azimuthal anisotropy due to path-length dependent jet energy loss is studied in a simple jet absorption model that include event by event fluctuating Glauber geometry. Significant anisotropy coefficients v_n are observed for n=1,2 and 3, but they are very small for n>3. These coefficients are expected to result in a "ridge" for correlations between two independently produced jets. The correlations between the orientation of the n^{th}-order anisotropy induced by jet absorption (Φ_n^{QP}) and the n^{th}-order participant plane (Φ_n^{PP}) responsible for harmonic flow are studied. Tight correlations are observed for n=2 in mid-central collisions, but they weaken significantly for n\neq2. The correlations are positive for n>=3, but become negative in central collisions for n>3. The dispersion between Φ_n^{QP} and Φ_n^{PP} is expect to break the factorization of the Fourier coefficients from two-particle correlation v_{n,n} into the single particle v_n, and has important implications for the high-pT ridge phenomena.

# Azimuthal anisotropy in a jet absorption model with fluctuating initial geometry in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Significant azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v_n for n=1, 2, and 3 arise from path-length dependent jet energy loss in a simple jet absorption model with event-by-event fluctuating Glauber geometry, but these coefficients are very small for n>3; crucially, the orientation of the quenching plane (Φ_n^QP) and the participant plane (Φ_n^PP) are tightly correlated only for n=2 in mid-central collisions, with significant de-correlation for n≠2, which breaks the factorization of two-particle Fourier coefficients v_{n,n} into single-particle v_n.

## Method

The study uses a Monte Carlo Glauber model with event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions in Au ions (Woods-Saxon radius 6.38 fm, diffuseness 0.535 fm, nucleon-nucleon cross-section 42 mb) to generate participant density and binary collision density profiles. Back-to-back jet pairs are generated according to the binary collision density profile and propagated through the medium, with jet quenching implemented via exponential attenuation f = e^{-κ I}, where the matter integral I follows either a linear (m=1) or quadratic (m=2) path-length dependence motivated by radiative and AdS/CFT energy loss in a 1+1D medium. The absorption coefficient κ is tuned to reproduce R_AA ≈ 0.19 for 0-5% centrality π^0 data at RHIC, yielding κ = 0.1473 fm^{-1} for m=1 and 0.0968 fm^{-2} for m=2.

## Key numbers

- R_AA = 0.19 for 0-5% centrality π^0 data at RHIC, used to tune the absorption coefficient
- κ = 0.1473 fm^{-1} for m=1 (linear path-length dependence) and κ = 0.0968 fm^{-2} for m=2 (quadratic path-length dependence)
- v_n^QP signals are significant for n ≤ 3 (greater than 1%), while higher-order v_n^QP are smaller than 1%
- v_2^QP and v_4^QP–v_6^QP show strong centrality dependence; v_1^QP and v_3^QP show little centrality dependence for N_part > 100
- The reduction factor ⟨cos n(Φ_n^PP - Φ_n^QP)⟩ for n=2 reaches 15% (I_1) and 30% (I_2) in central collisions; for n=1 it reaches about 50% in mid-central collisions
- The predicted ridge amplitude from hard-hard correlations is on the order of 0.5-4% depending on centrality and path-length dependence type
- I_2 type path-length dependence induces nearly a factor of two larger v_n^QP for n=1 and n=3 compared to I_1

## Context

This work extends the simple jet absorption model of Drees, Feng, and Jia (2003) previously used to study single particle suppression R_AA, dihadron suppression I_AA, and v_2, now incorporating event-by-event fluctuating geometry to study higher-order harmonics. It contrasts with hydrodynamic approaches where v_n is driven by anisotropic pressure gradients (Ollitrault, 1992; Alver et al., 2010) and with pQCD energy loss calculations in event-by-event hydrodynamic backgrounds (Renk, 2011) that focused on event-averaged R_AA relative to the 2nd-order event plane. The study enables quantitative predictions for the breakdown of factorization in two-particle correlations at high pT and for the "hard-hard ridge" observable at LHC, with similarities to the cumulant expansion approach of Zhang and Liao (2012) but using Monte Carlo Glauber geometry and focusing on hard-hard rather than soft-hard correlations.
