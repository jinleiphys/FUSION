# Correlation between U/Th and Pb/Os abundance ratios and its application in nuclear cosmochronology

> The abundance ratios of radioactive elements U/Th and stable elements Pb/Os from the $r$-process are found to have a strong correlation. This correlation is quite robust with respect to astrophysical conditions. The U/Th-Pb/Os correlation is then applied to provide customized initial abundance ratios U/Th from the observed abundance ratios Pb/Os for six $r$-process enhanced metal-poor stars respectively. Ages of these six metal-poor stars are predicted by the U/Th chronometer, which are approximately between $11$ and $15$ Gyr. Their ages are compatible with the cosmic age of 13.8 billion years predicted from the cosmic microwave background radiation.

## Key claim

The paper claims a strong, robust correlation between the U/Th and Pb/Os abundance ratios from r-process nucleosynthesis that holds across varied astrophysical conditions, but the detailed correlation depends on the nuclear physics inputs (mass models) used, and the method provides customized initial U/Th ratios for individual stars rather than assuming universal r-process abundance patterns.

## Method

The authors performed r-process nucleosynthesis simulations using a parametric high-entropy wind (HEW) model with NucNet tools, varying the electron abundance Ye from 0.40 to 0.48 and the final entropy S_final from 280 to 400. Nuclear physics inputs were built from two mass tables (WS4 and DZ28), with (n,gamma) rates from TALYS-1.9 and beta-decay rates calculated consistently with each mass model. The U/Th-Pb/Os correlation was established by interpolating between simulation results at discrete entropy values, then applied to six r-process enhanced metal-poor stars by using observed Pb/Os ratios to determine customized initial U/Th ratios for age calculations via the U/Th chronometer.

## Key numbers

- The U/Th-Pb/Os correlation shows that for every increase of 1.0 in lg(Pb/Os), lg(U/Th) increases by 0.2
- The metal-poor star J2038-0023 has an observed Pb/Os ratio of 0.645, which yields an initial U/Th ratio of 0.561 from the correlation; its age is calculated as 14.14 Gyr (rough) and 13.85 Gyr (revised)
- Ages of the six metal-poor stars range approximately between 11 and 15 Gyr, consistent with the cosmic age of 13.8 Gyr from cosmic microwave background radiation
- For J2038-0023: rough age 14.14 Gyr (WS4), revised age 13.85 Gyr with uncertainty 0.09 Gyr (WS4); rough age 14.71 Gyr (DZ28), revised age 14.44 Gyr with uncertainty 0.08 Gyr (DZ28)
- For CS31082-001: rough age 12.86 Gyr (WS4), revised age 12.19 Gyr with uncertainty 0.10 Gyr (WS4)
- For BD+17°3248: rough age 12.59 Gyr (WS4), revised age 12.47 Gyr with uncertainty 0.10 Gyr (WS4)
- For HE1523-0901: rough age 12.43 Gyr (WS4), revised age 12.05 Gyr with uncertainty 0.07 Gyr (WS4)
- For CS29497-004: rough age 14.87 Gyr (WS4), revised age 14.33 Gyr with uncertainty 0.10 Gyr (WS4)
- For J0954+5246: rough age 12.44 Gyr (WS4), revised age 12.03 Gyr with uncertainty 0.09 Gyr (WS4)

## Context

This work extends previous nuclear cosmochronology studies that assumed universal r-process abundance patterns (Fowler 1960, Cowan 1999, Schatz 2002, Niu 2009) by providing star-specific initial abundances, building on the synchronization approach of Wu (2022) that constrained customized astrophysical conditions for each star. The U/Th-Pb/Os correlation enables age determinations for six metal-poor stars including two actinide boost stars (CS31082-001 and J0954+5246) that previously yielded unreasonable or negative ages under the universality assumption (Schatz 2002, Hill 2017). The results are consistent with ages from the Th-U-X chronometer of Wu (2022) and with the Planck Collaboration (2020) cosmic age of 13.8 Gyr, though some ages exceed the 11.4 Gyr cosmic age from Hubble constant measurements by Jee (2019).
