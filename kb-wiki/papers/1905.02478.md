# Spin-isospin Kondo effects for $ÎŁ_{c}$ and $ÎŁ_{c}^{\ast}$ baryons and $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}^{\ast}$ mesons

> We study the Kondo effect for a $ÎŁ_{c}$ ($ÎŁ_{c}^{\ast}$) baryon in nuclear matter. In terms of the spin and isospin ($\mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{spin}} \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{isospin}}$) symmetry, the heavy-quark spin symmetry and the S-wave interaction, we provide the general form of the Lagrangian for a $ÎŁ_{c}$ ($ÎŁ_{c}^{\ast}$) baryon and a nucleon. We analyze the renormalization equation at the one-loop level, and find that the coexistence of spin exchange and isospin exchange magnifies the Kondo effect in comparison with the case where the spin-exchange interaction and the isospin-exchange interaction exist separately. We demonstrate that the solution exists for the ideal sets of the coupling constants, including the $\mathrm{SU}(4)$ symmetry as an extension of the $\mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{spin}} \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{isospin}}$ symmetry. We also conduct a similar analysis for the Kondo effect of a $\bar{D}$ ($\bar{D}^{\ast}$) meson in nuclear matter. On the basis of the obtained result, we conjecture that there could exist a "mapping" from the heavy meson (baryon) in vacuum onto the heavy baryon (meson) in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coexistence of spin-exchange and isospin-exchange interactions between a $\Sigma_c$ ($\Sigma_c^\ast$) baryon and a nucleon in nuclear matter magnifies the Kondo effect compared to cases where each interaction exists separately, but this result depends on the specific values of the coupling constants and is demonstrated for ideal sets including SU(4) symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct the most general S-wave contact interaction Lagrangian for a $\Sigma_c$ ($\Sigma_c^\ast$) baryon and a nucleon, respecting SU(2)$_{\mathrm{spin}}$ Ă— SU(2)$_{\mathrm{isospin}}$ symmetry and heavy-quark spin symmetry in the heavy-quark mass limit. They analyze the renormalization group (RG) equations at one-loop level using the poor man's scaling method, deriving how the four independent coupling constants evolve with energy scale. A similar analysis is performed for $\bar{D}$ ($\bar{D}^\ast$) mesons in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The mass splitting between a $\Sigma_c$ baryon and a $\Sigma_c^\ast$ baryon is about 65 MeV, compared to their masses of 2286 MeV and 2520 MeV respectively, justifying the heavy-quark spin symmetry approximation.
- The mass splitting between a $\Sigma_b$ baryon and a $\Sigma_b^\ast$ baryon is about 20 MeV, compared to their masses of 5810 MeV and 5830 MeV respectively.
- The decay widths of $\Sigma_c \rightarrow \Lambda_c\pi$ and $\Sigma_c^\ast \rightarrow \Lambda_c\pi$ are around 2 MeV and 15 MeV respectively.
- The RG equation for a single SU($N$) non-Abelian interaction yields a Kondo scale $\Lambda_{\mathrm{K}} = e^{1/(2\rho_0 N g)}$ for negative coupling constant $g < 0$, where $\rho_0 \equiv m^{3/2}\sqrt{2\mu}/(2\pi^2)$ is the density of states at the Fermi surface.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Kondo effect for $\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}^\ast$ mesons in nuclear matter by Yasui (2013) and for $D_s^-$ and $D_s^{\ast -}$ mesons by Yasui (2016), now applying the formalism to $\Sigma_c$ and $\Sigma_c^\ast$ baryons. The analysis contrasts with the $\Lambda_c$ baryon case studied by Yasui (2018), where the light diquark has spin 0 and isospin 0, making it irrelevant for the Kondo effect. The results enable future investigation of transport coefficients (heat conductivity, electrical resistance, shear viscosity) and modifications of nuclear structure in atomic nuclei containing heavy baryons or mesons.
