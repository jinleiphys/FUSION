# Hard photon flow and photon-photon correlation in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

> Hard photons emitted from energetic heavy ion collisions are very interesting since they do not experience nuclear interaction, and therefore they are useful to explore properties of nuclear matter. We investigated hard photon production and its properties in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions with the help of the Blotzmann-Uehling-Ulenbeck model. Two components of hard photons are discussed: direct and thermal. The positive directed flow parameter and negative elliptic flow parameter of direct photons are demonstrated and they are anti-correlated to the flows of free protons. The dependencies of hard photon production and anisotropic parameters on impact parameter, beam energy, nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy are also discussed. Furthermore, we investigated the two-photon momentum correlation function from which the space-time structure information of the photon source could be extracted as well as the two-photon azimuthal correlation which could provide another good method to determine the elliptic flow parameter $v_{2}$ of direct hard photons.

## Key claim

Hard photons in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions can be separated into direct and thermal components, and the direct photons exhibit a positive directed flow parameter and negative elliptic flow parameter that are anti-correlated with the flows of free protons, but this conclusion depends on the specific definition of direct photons as those emitted before the first maximum expansion of the system (approximately 65 fm/c for Ca + Ca at 60A MeV).

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Ulenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate heavy-ion collisions, incorporating a Skyrme-parameterized mean field potential with three different nuclear equation-of-state compressibilities (200, 235, and 380 MeV) and several symmetry energy parametrizations. Hard photon production was calculated from individual proton-neutron bremsstrahlung collisions using the hard-sphere model, and photons were classified as direct or thermal based on their emission time relative to the first maximum expansion of the collision system. Anisotropic flow parameters (directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2) were extracted from Fourier expansions of the azimuthal distributions, and two-photon momentum correlation functions were computed to probe source space-time structure.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter for direct photons: 16.7 MeV; for thermal photons: 8.1 MeV (for inclusive 40Ca + 40Ca at 60A MeV with K=235 MeV, fitted with Eq. fit without 1/E factor)
- Slope parameter for direct photons: 22.5 MeV; for thermal photons: 9.8 MeV (same system, fitted with Eq. fit2 including 1/E factor, 35% and 20% higher respectively)
- Directed transverse flow of direct hard photons: +3.9 MeV/c; of free protons: -23.7 MeV/c (60A MeV Ca + Ca, 40-60% centrality, K=235 MeV)
- Elliptic flow v2 of direct photons: -2%; of free protons: +5.5% (same system and conditions)
- Thermal photons contribute less than 30% of the total hard photon yield in the present model

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hard photon production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions (Schutz, Cassing, Nifenecker, Bona, Wada, Martinez, Enterria) by providing the first detailed BUU-model calculations of anisotropic flow parameters for direct hard photons and their anti-correlation with free proton flows. The results enable future experimental measurements of hard photon flow at intermediate energies, which have not yet been performed, and suggest that thermal hard photon yields could serve as a probe of nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy, complementing earlier work on hard photon interferometry by Marques and Schutz.
