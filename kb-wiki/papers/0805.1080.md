# Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in strangelets at finite temperature

> A model for strangelets at finite temperature is built, in which the quark masses are dynamically generated. The dynamical chiral symmetry breaking inside strangelets at finite temperature is investigated. It is found that the chiral symmetry is going to break spontaneously inside the strangelets at some finite temperature, and then the masses of strange quarks inside the strangelets increase as the temperature rises. The phenomenon that strange quark mass increases with temperature after the chiral symmetry breaks spontaneously is illustrated by some typical numerical examples.

## Key claim

The authors find that inside strangelets at finite temperature, chiral symmetry breaks spontaneously at some critical temperature, and then the strange quark mass *increases* as temperature rises further, an "abnormal" behavior opposite to the usual expectation for bulk matter, which they attribute to volume expansion and finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors extend the NJL+MIT bag model (Yasui, 2008) to finite temperature by constructing a grand canonical partition function from the discrete single-particle eigenenergies of quarks confined in a spherical MIT bag, including vacuum energy contributions and the t'Hooft term for U(1)_A symmetry breaking. They solve the mean-field gap equations for the chiral condensates by minimizing the free energy at fixed baryon number, imposing zero external pressure to determine the bag radius, and use a momentum cutoff with a diffuseness parameter fitted to reproduce the average nucleon-delta mass.

## Key numbers

* Strangelets with baryon number A=240, 300, and 400 show spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking at critical temperatures of approximately 83 MeV, 57 MeV, and 43 MeV, respectively.
* For a strangelet with A=400 at a fixed radius of 4.55 fm, the strange quark mass decreases when T > 75 MeV, eventually approaching its current mass at higher temperature.
* The charge-mass ratio Z/A is about 0.1 before chiral symmetry breaking (matching the MIT bag model) and rises to about 0.2 at T=100 MeV for the strangelet with A=400.
* The vacuum energy density used in the model is epsilon_vac = -4.416 GeV/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the NJL+MIT bag model of Yasui (2008), which studied dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in strangelets at zero temperature, to finite temperature using a quantum statistical approach. It also incorporates the t'Hooft term neglected in that earlier model. The results contrast with the standard QCD phase diagram for bulk matter (Alford, 2001), where chiral symmetry restoration occurs with increasing temperature; the authors show that finite-size effects and volume expansion reverse this trend in strangelets. The findings are relevant for interpreting strangelet searches in heavy-ion collisions (Barrette, 2000) and for understanding the role of chiral symmetry in the stability of strange quark matter.
