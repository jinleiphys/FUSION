# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.III

> In scattering theory, the unitary limit is defined by an infinite scattering-length and a zero effective range, corresponding to a phase-shift π/2, independent of energy. This condition is satisfied by a rank-1 separable potential V(k,k')=-v(k)v(k') with v^{2}(k)=(4π)^{2}(Λ^{2}-k^{2})^{-1/2}, Λbeing the cut-off in momentum space.Previous calculations using a Pauli-corrected ladder summation to calculate the energy of a zero temperature many body system of spin 1/2 fermions with this interaction gave ξ=0.24 (in units of kinetic energy) independent of density and with Λ---->infinity. This value of ξis appreciably smaller than the experimental and that obtained from other calculations, most notably from Monte Carlo, which in principle would be the most reliable. Our previous work did however also show a strong dependence on effective range r_0 (with r_0=0 at unitarity). With an increase to r_0=1.0 the energy varied from ξ~0.38 at k_f=0.6 1/fm to ~0.45 at k_f=1.8 1/fm which is somewhat closer to the Monte-Carlo results. These previous calculations are here extended by including the effect of the previously neglected mean-field propagation, the dispersion correction. This is repulsive and found to increase drastically with decreasing effective range. It is large enough to suggest a revised value of ξ~0.4 <--> ~0.5 independent of r_0. Off-shell effects are also investigated by introducing a rank-2 (phase-shift equivalent) separable potential. Effects of 10% or more in energy could be demonstrated for r_0>0. It is pointed out that a computational cut-off in momentum-space brings in another scale in the in otherwise scale-less unitary problem.

# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.III

## Key claim

The previously reported value of ξ = 0.24 (energy in units of kinetic energy) for a unitary Fermi gas from ladder summation is revised upward to ξ ~ 0.4–0.5 when the previously neglected mean-field propagation (dispersion correction) is included, and this revised value is nearly independent of effective range r₀. The author suggests that there may not be a definite disagreement between Brueckner-type calculations and Monte Carlo results after all, provided higher-order terms are included.

## Method

The author extends previous Brueckner ladder calculations for spin-1/2 fermions at zero temperature by including the mean-field (dispersion) correction in hole-line propagation, using a rank-1 separable potential fitted to the unitary limit phase-shift δ(k) = π/2. Off-shell effects are investigated by constructing rank-2 phase-shift equivalent separable potentials using the Chadan-Fiedeldey method, varying the off-shell behavior while preserving on-shell phase-shifts. Calculations are performed with a momentum-space cutoff Λ = 10 fm⁻¹ and Fermi momentum k_f = 1 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- ξ = 0.24: the previously reported energy (in units of kinetic energy) from the analytic ladder calculation at the unitary limit (r₀ = 0, Λ → ∞), independent of density.
- With r₀ = 1.0 fm and no mean-field insertions, ξ varies from ~0.38 at k_f = 0.6 fm⁻¹ to ~0.45 at k_f = 1.8 fm⁻¹.
- With the dispersion correction included (standard choice of hole-line insertions), the revised ξ is ~0.4–0.5, independent of r₀.
- For r₀ = 0.25 fm, off-shell variations from rank-2 potentials produce ξ values ranging from 0.28 to 0.38, a variation of 10% or more.
- The wound integral I_w increases as r₀ decreases toward the unitary limit, while the mean field U_h is essentially independent of r₀.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations (Köhler 2007, 2008) which gave ξ = 0.24 using a Pauli-corrected ladder summation without mean-field propagation. That value was significantly smaller than experimental results and Monte Carlo calculations (Gehm 2003, Bartenstein 2004, Carlson 2003, Perali 2004, Heiselberg 2001, Baker 1999, Siu 2008, Dona 2010) which find ξ ~ 0.45. The present inclusion of the dispersion correction, a three-body effect analogous to the mechanism responsible for nuclear saturation in Brueckner theory, brings the ladder results closer to those other calculations. The author notes that Brueckner-type calculations by Siu (2008) and Dona (2010), which include ring diagrams and use different interactions, also give results similar to Monte Carlo, though direct comparison is not yet possible. The work highlights that a computational momentum-space cutoff introduces an additional scale in the otherwise scale-less unitary problem.
