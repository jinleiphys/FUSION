# Classical and Quantum Coherent State Description of Nucleon-Antinucleon Annihilation at Rest in the Skyrme Model with Omega Mesons

> We model the $N\bar{N}$ system at rest as a baryon number zero lump in the Skyrme model including the $ω$ field. We integrate the classical equations of motion from the highly non-perturbative annihilation region into the non-interacting radiation zone. Subsequent coherent state quantization of the radiation field gives a good description of the pion spectrum from annihilation at rest.

# Classical and Quantum Coherent State Description of Nucleon-Antinucleon Annihilation at Rest in the Skyrme Model with Omega Mesons

## Key claim
The authors model nucleon-antinucleon annihilation at rest as a classical pion and omega field burst emerging from a baryon-number-zero Skyrmion blob, then quantize the radiation using coherent states. The model reproduces the observed pion spectrum with no free parameters beyond those fixed by nucleon physics, though it yields only 23% of annihilations proceeding via vector mesons versus the empirical 40%, with the missing fraction attributed to rho mesons not yet included.

## Method
The authors construct a spherically symmetric initial "blob" of Skyrmionic matter with zero baryon number and total energy of 2 GeV, using the Skyrme Lagrangian modified to include omega mesons coupled to the baryon current. They integrate the classical equations of motion for the coupled pion field F(r,t) and omega fields ω₀ and ωᵣ from the annihilation region into the non-interacting radiation zone, then extract momentum distribution amplitudes f(k) for pions and g(k) for omegas. These classical amplitudes are used to construct quantum coherent states projected onto states of fixed four-momentum and isospin, from which pion number probabilities and mean meson numbers are computed.

## Key numbers
- Mean number of direct pions from classical fields: N_π = 4.4
- Mean number of omega mesons from classical fields: N_ω = 0.86
- Total mean number of pions (direct plus from omega decay): N_total = 7.0
- Percentage of secondary pions from omega decay: 37% (classical), 23% (with isospin and four-momentum projections)
- Pion decay constant used: f_π = 62.0 MeV
- Omega coupling parameter: β = 15.6
- Initial blob size parameter: a = 1/m_π
- Total initial energy: 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological treatment of annihilation into pions only (Amado 1993, Amado 1994) by including omega mesons in the Skyrme model dynamics, following the Skyrmion-anti-Skyrmion annihilation calculations of Sommermann (1992) and Shao, Amado (1993). The approach builds on the Skyrme model with omega coupling developed by Adkins and Nappi (1984) and uses coherent state quantization methods from Glauber (1963) with four-momentum projection from Horn and Silver (1971) and isospin projection from Botke (1988). The inclusion of omega mesons removes the even-odd pion number artifact present in the pions-only treatment, and the results enable future work on rho mesons, pion correlations, and annihilation in flight.
