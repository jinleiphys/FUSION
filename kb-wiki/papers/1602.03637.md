# Asymptotic normalization coefficients for ${\rm {^7Be}}+p\to{\rm{^8B}}$ from the peripheral ${\rm{^7Be}}$($d$,$n$)${\rm{^8B}}$ reaction and their astrophysical application

> The proton transfer ${\rm{^7Be}}$($d$,$n$)${\rm{^8B}}$ reaction at the energy of 4.5 MeV (c.m.) has been analysed within the modified DWBA. New estimates and their uncertainties are obtained for values of the asymptotic normalization coefficients for $p + {\rm{^7Be}} \to {\rm{^8B}}$, the astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ factor and the s-wave $p + {\rm{^7Be}}$ scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the ${\rm{^7Be}}(d,n){\rm{^8B}}$ reaction at 4.5 MeV (c.m.) is predominantly peripheral in the main peak region of the angular distribution, which allows extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for $p+{\rm{^7Be}}\to{\rm{^8B}}$ with reduced dependence on the geometric parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential, provided the condition that the calculated reduced cross section is independent of the single-particle ANC is satisfied within $\pm$1.5%.

## Method

The experimental differential cross sections for the ${\rm{^7Be}}(d,n){\rm{^8B}}$ reaction at 4.5 MeV (c.m.) from Das (2006) were reanalyzed within the modified distorted-wave Born approximation (MDWBA), where the differential cross section is expressed directly in terms of the ANCs for $p+{\rm{^7Be}}\to{\rm{^8B}}$ rather than spectroscopic factors. Four sets of optical potentials were used, obtained from global parametrizations and $\chi^2$ minimization fits to elastic $d+{\rm{^7Li}}$, $n+{\rm{^9Be}}$, and $n+{\rm{^{11}B}}$ scattering data in the forward hemisphere. The peripheral character was verified by testing the independence of the reduced cross section ${\cal{R}}_{j_B}$ on the single-particle ANC $b_{B;j_B}$ over a wide range of Woods-Saxon geometric parameters ($r_o$=1.10–1.40 fm, $a$=0.50–0.75 fm) and by calculating the dependence of the DWBA cross section on a cutoff radius $R_{cut}$.

## Key numbers

- The squared ANC for $p+n\to d$ is $C_{d;1/2}^2 = 0.774 \pm 0.018$ fm$^{-1}$, used as a fixed input.
- The single-particle ANC for the $s$-wave deuteron is fixed at $b_{d;1/2} = 0.942$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
- The ratio $\lambda = (C_{B;1/2}/C_{B;3/2})^2 = 0.125$ is taken from Tab06 and Trache (2003).
- The single-particle ANCs for ${\rm{^8B}}$ vary over $0.615 \le b_{B;3/2} \le 0.795$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and $0.596 \le b_{B;1/2} \le 0.782$ fm$^{-1/2}$ for the geometric parameter ranges used.
- The condition ${\cal{R}}_{j_B}(E_i,\theta;b_{B;j_B}) = f(E_i,\theta)$ is fulfilled within $\pm$1.5% for the 4.5 MeV data, whereas for the 5.8 MeV data from W.L. (1998) it is fulfilled only within $\pm$15.0%.
- The ratio $\tilde{R} = b_{B;1/2}/b_{B;3/2} = 0.9742 \pm 0.0065$ is practically independent of the geometric parameters.
- The binding energy of ${\rm{^8B}}$ in the (${\rm{^7Be}}+p$) channel is 137 keV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ${\rm{^7Be}}(d,n){\rm{^8B}}$ reaction by applying the modified DWBA to the more precise and peripheral data at 4.5 MeV from Das (2006), in contrast to earlier analyses at 5.8 MeV by W.L. (1998) and Ogata (2003) using standard DWBA or CDCCM, which yielded spectroscopic factors with large spreads (about 30%) due to sensitivity to optical potentials and bound-state parameters. The extracted ANCs are intended for use in estimating the astrophysical $S_{17}(0)$ factor for the ${\rm{^7Be}}(p,\gamma){\rm{^8B}}$ reaction, which is critical for predicting the solar ${\rm{^8B}}$ neutrino flux in standard solar models, and the results are compared with prior ANC determinations from Igam (2008) and Tab06.
