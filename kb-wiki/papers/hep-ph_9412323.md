# Rapidity-Dependent Low-pt Enhancement

## Key claim

The rapidity dependence of the low-pt enhancement (LPTE) is shown to be a sensitive measure of the longitudinal source size for longitudinally expanding finite systems, provided the system is finite in longitudinal extent and the expansion follows a scaling Bjorken profile; the enhancement vanishes in the limit of infinite longitudinal size.

## Method

The invariant momentum distribution (IMD) is calculated for a one-dimensionally expanding finite system using a Gaussian approximation for both the space-time rapidity distribution G(η) and the local thermal rapidity distribution J(m_t, η, y). The freeze-out hypersurface is described by a scaling Bjorken flow profile, and the Boltzmann approximation is applied to the local relativistic Bose-Einstein or Fermi-Dirac distributions. The resulting IMD yields an effective temperature T_e(y) that depends on rapidity, and the normalized ratio L_R(y, m_t) of the IMD at rapidity y to that at mid-rapidity is derived as a function of the longitudinal size Δη and freeze-out temperature T.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature at mid-rapidity for central S + Au reactions at 14.6 AGeV is T_* = 209 ± 10 MeV (from fit to E802 data).
- The freeze-out temperature from the same fit is T = 60 ± 418 MeV (not precisely determined due to experimental errors).
- The longitudinal size of the K⁺-emitting volume at freeze-out in central S + Au reactions at AGS is Δη_{K⁺} = 0.39 ± 0.18 space-time rapidity units.
- The fit to the E802 T_e(y) data yields χ²/NDF = 1.93.
- The mid-rapidity from the fit for the asymmetric S + Au collision is y_0 = 1.52 ± 0.25.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for longitudinally expanding finite systems developed in Csorgo (1994) for the invariant momentum distribution and Bose-Einstein correlation function. It addresses the open question from the experimental review by Gillo (1994) that theoretical models should explain the rapidity dependence observed in low-pt enhancement data. The results are compared to data from the NA35 collaboration for S + Pb collisions at CERN SPS (Mitchell, 1994) and the E802 collaboration for S + Au reactions at AGS (E802, 1994), and the rapidity-dependent effective temperature behavior is noted to be consistent with measurements from NA35, E802/E859, E810, and E814 collaborations as summarized by Stachel (1994). The method enables measurement of longitudinal source sizes that are otherwise difficult to access via HBT measurements (Csorgo, 1994; Csorgo, 1994).
