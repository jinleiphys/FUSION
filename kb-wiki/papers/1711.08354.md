# Pion Condensation by Rotation in a Magnetic field

> We show that the combined effects of a rotation plus a magnetic field can cause charged pion condensation. We suggest that this phenomenon may yield to observable effects in current heavy ion collisions at collider energies, where large magnetism and rotations are expected in off-central collisions.

## Key claim

The combined effects of a parallel rotation and magnetic field can induce charged pion condensation in the vacuum and at finite temperature, provided that the rotational chemical potential exceeds the effective pion mass in the Lowest Landau Level; this condensation may be observable in off-central heavy ion collisions if the magnetic field and rotation persist with sufficient strength through the freeze-out phase.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for charged pions in a rotating frame with a constant magnetic field parallel to the rotation axis, using both infinite-volume ladder operator methods and finite-volume circular wavefunctions with zero boundary conditions. They enforce strict charge conservation in a closed volume by introducing a charge chemical potential, and compute the Bose-Einstein condensate density by minimizing the energy per unit length including Coulomb repulsion between the charged rings and the magnetic back-reaction from the rotating condensate. The resulting formalism is applied to estimate the mean number of condensed pions at temperatures and rotations characteristic of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The vorticity measured by STAR in off-central AuAu collisions is Omega ~ (9 +/- 1) x 10^21 s^-1 ~ 0.05 m_pi.
- Large magnetic fields B ~ m_pi^2 are expected in off-central collisions (DIMA).
- For R ~ 10 fm and eB ~ m_pi^2, the Landau level degeneracy N = eB R^2 / 2 ~ 100/4 = 25, giving a rotational chemical potential mu_N = N Omega ~ 1.25 m_pi.
- Pion chemical potentials at freeze-out are mu_f ~ 0.5 m_pi at RHIC and mu_f ~ 0.86 m_pi at LHC (BEGUN), translating to effective chemical potentials mu_pi = mu_N + 2 mu_f ~ 1.96 m_pi and 2.98 m_pi respectively.
- The threshold for combined pi+ and pi- condensation in the LLL is 2 sqrt(2) m_pi.
- For N=25, the critical rotation for the l=N state is Omega_c = 1.69 sqrt(eB)/20, with Omega_c R = 0.59 < 1 (satisfying causality).
- The mean number of condensed pions is shown for temperatures 0.5 m_pi <= T <= 1.5 m_pi and rotations 0.04 m_pi <= Omega <= 0.06 m_pi.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charge density enhancement from combined rotation and magnetic fields in Dirac fermion systems (Vilenkin, Fukusumi et al., Liao, Fuku) and in NJL-type effective models (Liao, Fuku, Maxim). It specifically applies the mechanism to charged pions in heavy-ion collisions, where STAR (2017) has measured large vorticity and large magnetic fields are expected (DIMA). The predicted charged pion condensation may explain the large multi-pion correlations reported by ALICE (ABELEV) and could affect flow of charged particles and number fluctuations; the authors also suggest the effect may be probed in atomic physics, condensed matter (quantum Hall effect), and compact stars (magnetars).
