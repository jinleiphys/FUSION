# Continuum Lambda spectra for a 6Li_Lambda hypernucleus in the 6Li(K-, pi-) reaction

> We theoretically investigate Lambda production via a (K-, pi-) reaction on a 6Li target, using the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a Fermi-averaged K-n --> pi-Lambda amplitude. We calculate Lambda production spectra using the Green's function method for a 5Li nuclear core + Lambda system, employing a Lambda folding-model potential based on the 5Li nuclear density, which is constructed from alpha-p and 3He-d cluster wave functions. The results show that the calculated spectrum, which includes Lambda bound, resonance, and continuum states, agrees well with the experimental data from the (K-, pi-) reaction at p_K- = 790 MeV/c (0 deg.), where substitutional (0p_Lambda, 0p^{-1}_n) and (0s_Lambda, 0s^{-1}_n) configurations dominate in the near-recoilless reactions. A narrow peak corresponds to a high-lying excited state with spin-parity J^P= 1+ at E_Lambda= 13.8 MeV near the 3He + d + Lambda threshold, arising from interference effects between 5Li(3/2+)x(0s1/2)_Lambda and 5Li(1/2+)x(0s1/2)_Lambda components. This study offers a valuable framework for extracting essential information on the structure and production mechanisms of hypernuclear states from experimental data.

# Continuum Lambda spectra for a 6Li_Lambda hypernucleus in the 6Li(K-, pi-) reaction

## Key claim

The calculated Lambda production spectrum, which includes bound, resonance, and continuum states using the Green's function method with a Lambda folding-model potential based on 5Li nuclear densities from alpha-p and 3He-d cluster wave functions, agrees well with experimental data from the (K-, pi-) reaction at p_K- = 790 MeV/c (0 degrees), but only when the extended optimal Fermi-averaging (EOFA) method is used instead of standard Fermi-averaging (SFA) to resolve discrepancies in the elementary K-n to pi-Lambda amplitudes.

## Method

The authors employed the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a Fermi-averaged K-n to pi-Lambda amplitude, using the Green's function method to describe Lambda bound, resonance, and continuum states for a 5Li nuclear core + Lambda system. They constructed Lambda folding-model potentials from 5Li nuclear densities based on alpha-p and 3He-d cluster wave functions, and solved coupled-channel equations to obtain the Lambda production spectra, incorporating the EOFA method for the elementary amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The (K-, pi-) reaction was measured at p_K- = 790 MeV/c and theta_lab = 0 degrees, with a small momentum transfer of q approximately 60-80 MeV/c
- The ground state 1- is found at E_Lambda = -4.51 MeV and the 2- excited state at E_Lambda = -4.26 MeV, both below the 5Li(g.s.) + Lambda threshold
- A narrow peak corresponds to a high-lying excited state with spin-parity J^P = 1+ at E_Lambda = 13.8 MeV near the 3He + d + Lambda threshold, with a calculated width of 0.2 MeV
- The 1+_1 pole in the (alpha-p)-Lambda model space is located at E_Lambda = -0.45 - i 0.98 MeV on the [--] Riemann sheet
- The 1+_2 pole in the (alpha-p)-Lambda model space is at E_Lambda = 3.1 - i 2.9 MeV on the [--] sheet
- The 1+_1 and 1+_2 poles in the (3He-d)-Lambda model space reside at E_Lambda = 13.7 - i 0.1 MeV and E_Lambda = 16.3 - i 0.1 MeV on the [++] Riemann sheet
- The Lambda N effective interaction uses v^0_Lambda N = -21.19 MeV, b = 1.49 fm, and eta = -0.072
- The distance D between 3He and d clusters is taken as 1.8 fm, corresponding to an rms radius of 2.18 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of p-shell Lambda hypernuclei in (K-, pi-) reactions by Auerbach and Van Giai (1980) and shell-model descriptions by Majling (1980) and Millener (2007), as well as cluster-model descriptions by Motoba (1983, 1985) and Ohkura (1993). The study provides a framework for analyzing continuum spectra from hypernuclear reactions at J-PARC, including ongoing searches for the Xi hypernucleus 7_Xi_H by the J-PARC E75 Collaboration and proposed searches for 6_Xi_H. The method enables extraction of essential information on hypernuclear structure and production mechanisms from experimental data, particularly for understanding the role of continuum effects and interference between different 5Li core configurations.
