# Nuclear model effects in Charged Current neutrino--nucleus quasielastic scattering

> The quasielastic scattering of muon neutrinos on oxygen 16 is studied for neutrino energies between 200 MeV and 1 GeV using a relativistic shell model. Final state interactions are included within the distorted wave impulse approximation, by means of a relativistic optical potential, with and without imaginary part, and of a relativistic mean field potential. For comparison with experimental data the inclusive charged--current quasielastic cross section for $ν_μ$--$^{12}C$ scattering in the kinematical conditions of the LSND experiment at Los Alamos is also presented and briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that final state interaction (FSI) effects produce a sizeable reduction of the quasielastic cross section even at neutrino energies up to 1 GeV, contrary to the common assumption that FSI become negligible at such energies; however, the magnitude of the reduction depends strongly on whether the imaginary part of the optical potential is included, with the complex (absorptive) potential overestimating the reduction relative to inclusive data.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive charged-current quasielastic cross sections for muon neutrinos on oxygen-16 at incident energies of 200 MeV, 500 MeV, and 1 GeV using a Relativistic Shell Model (RSM) with bound nucleon states from a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach. Final state interactions are incorporated within the distorted wave impulse approximation using three treatments: a complex relativistic optical potential (ROP), a purely real ROP, and a continuum RMF potential; results are compared to the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and to a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model.

## Key numbers

- For the RSM with complex ROP, the reduction of the differential cross section near its maximum is about 60% for E_nu = 200 MeV and about 50% for E_nu = 500 MeV and E_nu = 1 GeV.
- For the RMF and real ROP models, the reduction near the maximum is about 30-40% for E_nu = 200 MeV and about 20% for E_nu = 500 MeV and E_nu = 1 GeV.
- The integrated cross section reduction from FSI in the RMF and real ROP models is about 15% at E_nu = 1 GeV.
- Resonant contributions to the integrated cross section from RMF and ROP models are about 10% at E_nu = 200 MeV, about 2% at 500 MeV, and about 1% at 1 GeV.
- For the LSND experiment on carbon-12, the flux-averaged integrated cross sections from the RSM are 20.5 (PWIA), 16.8 (RMF), and 15.1 (real ROP) in units of 10^{-40} cm^2, compared to the measured value of 10.6 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2.

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Shell Model previously applied to exclusive electron scattering and neutral current neutrino scattering (Alberico et al., 1997) to charged-current quasielastic scattering on oxygen-16, comparing with the relativistic Fermi gas model. The results show that nuclear model effects from shell structure are significant at low neutrino energies but diminish at 1 GeV, while FSI effects remain sizeable even at 1 GeV, contradicting the assumption in some earlier analyses (Bleve et al.) that FSI become negligible at such energies. The comparison with LSND data on carbon-12 indicates that the RMF treatment of FSI is favored over the real ROP or PWIA, but all calculations overestimate the measured cross section by approximately 50%, suggesting that additional reaction channels beyond quasielastic knockout may be important at these low energies.
