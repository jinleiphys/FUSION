# Anharmonic phonon excitations in subbarrier fusion reactions

> Recently measured high precision data of fusion excitation function have enabled a detailed study on the effects of nuclear collective excitations on fusion reactions. Using such highly accurate data of the $^{16}$O + $^{144,148}$Sm reactions, we discuss the anharmonic properties of collective phonon excitations in $^{144,148}$Sm nuclei. It is shown that subbarrier fusion reactions are strongly affected by the anharmonic effects and thus offer an alternative method to extract the static quadrupole moments of phonon states in a spherical nucleus.

# Anharmonic phonon excitations in subbarrier fusion reactions

## Key claim

Subbarrier fusion reactions are strongly affected by anharmonic effects in nuclear surface vibrations, and the analysis of fusion barrier distributions can serve as an alternative method to extract the static quadrupole moments of phonon states in spherical nuclei. The authors show that the previously reported contradiction between experimental data and coupled-channels calculations including double-phonon excitations in the harmonic limit can be resolved by incorporating anharmonicities, specifically through non-zero quadrupole moments of excited states and deviations of transition probabilities from the harmonic limit.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the 16O + 144,148Sm fusion reactions using the sdf-interacting boson model (IBM) in the vibrational limit to describe the anharmonic properties of quadrupole and octupole phonon excitations in the target nuclei. The coupling Hamiltonian included both nuclear and Coulomb parts with quadrupole and octupole operators containing χ parameters that generate non-zero quadrupole moments, and the calculations were carried out to all orders in the couplings. The χ parameters were determined by χ² fits to the experimental fusion excitation functions, and the resulting quadrupole moments of the first 2⁺ and 3⁻ states were extracted from the E2 operator using the effective charge determined from the experimental B(E2;0→2⁺₁) value.

## Key numbers

- For 144Sm: deformation parameters β₂ = 0.11 and β₃ = 0.21, estimated from electric transition probabilities
- For 144Sm: extracted χ parameters from fusion data fit: χ₂ = −3.30 ± 2.30, χ₂f = −2.48 ± 0.07, χ₃ = 2.87 ± 0.16
- For 144Sm: static quadrupole moment of the first 2⁺ state = −0.89 ± 0.63 b, and of the first 3⁻ state = −0.70 ± 0.02 b
- For 148Sm: static quadrupole moment of the first 2⁺ state = −1.00 ± 0.25 b, and of the first 3⁻ state = +1.52 ± 0.14 b
- For 148Sm: the quadrupole moment of the first 2⁺ state from fusion analysis (−1.00 ± 0.25 b) is very close to the value measured by Coulomb excitation technique (−0.97 ± 0.27 b, from Peker 1990)
- Effective charge for 144Sm: e_B = 0.16 eb, determined from experimental B(E2;0→2⁺₁) value

## Context

This work extends the analysis of subbarrier fusion reactions beyond the harmonic oscillator approximation used in earlier coupled-channels studies (Takigawa and Ikeda 1986; Kruppa et al. 1993) and the interacting boson model approach to fusion (Balantekin, Bennett, and Kuyucak 1993-1994). The paper addresses a specific puzzle: while the 58Ni + 60Ni reaction showed clear evidence for multi-phonon couplings (Stefanini et al. 1995), the 16O + 144Sm reaction appeared to contradict the inclusion of double-phonon excitations in the harmonic limit (Morton 1995; Leigh et al. 1995). The authors resolve this contradiction by showing that anharmonic effects are essential, and their analysis enables the determination of both the sign and magnitude of quadrupole moments of phonon states, complementing traditional Coulomb excitation techniques.
