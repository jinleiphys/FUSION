# Masses, decays and mixings of gluonia in QCD

> We compute the masses and decay widths of gluonia using QCD spectral sum rules and low-energy theorems. In the scalar sector, one finds a gluonium having a mass $M_G=(1.5\pm 0.2)$ GeV, which decays mainly into the $U(1)_A$ channels $ηη'$ and 4$π^0$. However, for a consistency of the whole approach, one needs broad-low mass gluonia (the $σ$ and its radial excitation), which couple strongly to the quark degrees of freedom similarly to the $η'$ of the $U(1)_A$ sector. Combining these results with the ones for the $\bar qq$ quarkonia, we present maximal quark-gluonium mixing schemes, which can provide quite a good description of the complex spectra and various decay widths of the observed scalar mesons $σ(1.0),f_0(0.98),f_0(1.37),f_0(1.5)$ and $f_J(1.71)$. In the tensor sector, the gluonium mass is found to be $M_T\simeq (2.05\pm 0.19)$ GeV, which makes the $ζ(2.2)$ a good $2^{++}$ gluonium candidate, even though we expect a rich population of $2^{++}$ gluonia in this region. In the pseudoscalar channel, the gluonium mass is found to be $M_P\simeq (2.05\pm 0.19)$ GeV, while we also show that the $E/ι(1.44)$ couples more weakly to the gluonic current than the $η'$, which can favour its interpretation as the first radial excitation of the $η'(0.96)$.

## Key claim
The paper computes gluonium (glueball) masses and decay widths using QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) and low-energy theorems (LET), finding a scalar gluonium mass of $M_G=(1.5\pm 0.2)$ GeV. However, for the consistency of the sum rule approach, the existence of broad, low-mass scalar gluonia (the $\sigma_B$ and its radial excitation) is required, which couple strongly to quark degrees of freedom, a condition the abstract does not explicitly state as a necessary outcome of the method.

## Method
The analysis uses QCD spectral sum rules (Laplace and finite energy sum rules) applied to two-point correlators built from gauge-invariant gluonic currents for the $0^{++}$, $2^{++}$, and $0^{-+}$ channels. The operator product expansion (OPE) is used, including contributions from the gluon condensate ($D=4$) and triple gluon condensate ($D=6$), with the perturbative series estimated to higher orders. Decay widths are then derived using low-energy theorems and vertex sum rules, combined with maximal quarkonium-gluonium mixing schemes to describe the observed scalar meson spectra.

## Key numbers
*   Scalar gluonium mass: $M_G = (1.50 \pm 0.19)$ GeV, decaying mainly into $\eta\eta'$ and $4\pi^0$.
*   Tensor gluonium mass: $M_T \simeq (2.0 \pm 0.1)$ GeV, identifying the $\zeta(2.2)$ as a good $2^{++}$ candidate.
*   Pseudoscalar gluonium mass: $M_P \simeq (2.05 \pm 0.19)$ GeV.
*   Upper bound on scalar gluonium mass: $M_G \leq (2.16 \pm 0.22)$ GeV.
*   Decay constant of the scalar gluonium $G(1.5)$: $f_G \simeq (390 \pm 145)$ MeV.
*   Decay width of the $\sigma_B$ into $\pi\pi$: $\Gamma(\sigma_B \rightarrow \pi\pi) \approx 0.7$ GeV for $M_{\sigma_B} \approx 1.0$ GeV.

## Context
This work updates and extends the earlier QSSR analyses of gluonia by Novikov et al. (NV) and Narison (SN), and the mixing schemes of Bramon and Narison (BN). It provides a comprehensive scheme for scalar, tensor, and pseudoscalar glueballs, offering predictions that can be compared with lattice QCD results from Sharpe et al. and experimental data from the GAMS and Crystal Barrel collaborations. The results enable a more detailed interpretation of observed scalar mesons like the $\sigma(1.0)$, $f_0(0.98)$, $f_0(1.37)$, $f_0(1.5)$, and $f_J(1.71)$ as mixtures of quarkonia and gluonia.
