# Multiple condensed phases in attractively interacting Bose systems

> We investigate a Bose gas with finite-range interaction using a scheme to eliminate unphysical processes in the T-matrix approximation. In this way the corrected T-matrix becomes suitable to calculate properties below the critical temperature. For attractive interaction, an Evans-Rashid transition occurs between a quasi-ideal Bose gas and a BCS-like phase with a gaped dispersion. The gap decreases with increasing density and vanishes at a critical density where the single-particle dispersion becomes linear for small momenta indicating Bose-Einstein condensation. The investigation of the pressure shows however, that the mentioned quantum phase transitions might be inaccessible due to a preceding first order transition.

# Multiple condensed phases in attractively interacting Bose systems

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Bose gas with attractive finite-range interaction can exhibit three distinct quantum phases, a quasi-ideal gas, a BCS-like gapped phase (Evans-Rashid transition), and a Bose-Einstein condensate with linear dispersion, but the pressure calculations show that these quantum phase transitions may be experimentally inaccessible because a first-order gas-liquid or gas-solid transition likely precedes them.

## Method

The authors employ a T-matrix (Bethe-Salpeter) approach with a scheme that eliminates unphysical repeated-collision processes in the Galitskii-Feynman approximation, using asymmetric self-consistency where one propagator is dressed and the other is not. They assume a homogeneous Bose gas with an s-type separable Yamaguchi interaction potential and solve the coupled equations for the self-energy, Green function, and density in the thermodynamic limit, extracting the binding energy, chemical potential, and gap parameter as functions of density and interaction strength.

## Key numbers

- For $^4$He: scattering length $a_0 = 93$ Å, effective range $r_0 = 7.298$ Å, vacuum binding energy $\omega_{00} = 129.6$ neV, bond length $\langle r \rangle = 49$ Å (experimental values: $\omega_{00} = (130 \pm 7)$ neV, $\langle r \rangle = (52 \pm 4)$ Å)
- For $^1$H: dissociation energy $D_0 = 4.478$ eV, bond length $\langle r \rangle = 0.7414$ Å
- The critical interaction strength in vacuum is $\lambda_{c0} = 8\pi\hbar^2/(m\gamma)$; the bound state vanishes for $|\lambda| < \lambda_{c0}$
- The energy scale set by the interaction range is $\varepsilon_\gamma = \hbar^2\gamma^2/2m$, with $\varepsilon_\gamma/k_B = 12$ K for $^1$H and $0.95$ K for $^4$He

## Context

This work extends the earlier Evans-Rashid transition theory (Evans and Rashid, 1973) for bosons, the bosonic analogue of the BCS transition, and addresses the Prange paradox identified by Kadanoff and Martin (1961) and later by Lipavský (2008), where symmetric self-consistent T-matrix approximations fail to give the correct gap dispersion in condensed phases. The results are very similar to those obtained by Stoof (1994), who showed that an attractively interacting Bose gas becomes liquid or solid before the Evans-Rashid transition or Bose condensation can occur. The approach enables simultaneous description of condensed and non-condensed phases on the same theoretical footing, and could be applied to systems near Feshbach resonances where BCS-like molecular Bose condensation is predicted.
