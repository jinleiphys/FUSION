# Charge-Dependent Correlations in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions and the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> We provide a phenomenological analysis of present experimental searches for local parity violation manifested through the Chiral Magnetic Effect. We introduce and discuss the relevant correlation functions used for the measurements. Our analysis of the available data from both RHIC and LHC shows that the present experimental evidence for the Chiral Magnetic Effect is rather ambiguous. We further discuss in some detail various background contributions due to conventional physics, which need to be understood quantitatively in order to draw a definitive conclusion about the existence of local parity violation in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The present experimental evidence for the Chiral Magnetic Effect from both RHIC and LHC data is rather ambiguous, and various background contributions from conventional physics must be understood quantitatively before a definitive conclusion about local parity violation in heavy ion collisions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors performed a phenomenological analysis of charge-dependent azimuthal correlation functions measured by the STAR, PHENIX, and ALICE collaborations, specifically the two-particle correlator ⟨cos(φᵢ−φⱼ)⟩ and the three-particle correlator ⟨cos(φᵢ+φⱼ−2φₖ)⟩ for same-charge and opposite-charge pairs. They also introduced an alternative observable, the Q̂ᶜ₁ vector analysis, which directly measures the charged dipole moment of the final-state hadron distribution in transverse momentum space. The analysis evaluated how the intrinsic single-particle distribution with a charge separation term contributes to these correlation functions and compared the predicted CME pattern with available data.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field in non-central heavy ion collisions is estimated to be of order mπ² ≈ 10¹⁸ Gauss (Skokov 2009, Bzdak 2011).
- For a pure CME signal with ΔΨCS = π/2, the same-charge correlators should satisfy γ++/−− = −d₁² < 0 and δ++/−− = +d₁² > 0, while opposite-charge correlators should satisfy γ+− = +d₁² > 0 and δ+− = −d₁² < 0.
- The STAR data for same-charge pairs show γ and δ approximately equal in magnitude but with the same (negative) sign, contrary to the CME prediction of opposite signs.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for searching for local parity violation via the Chiral Magnetic Effect, originally proposed by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and Voloshin (2004). The analysis critically evaluates the STAR collaboration's first measurements of charge-dependent correlations (STAR 2009) and demonstrates that the observed correlation patterns do not match the pure CME prediction. The paper enables future experimental and theoretical work by identifying the need for quantitative understanding of background effects from conventional physics, such as two-particle correlations and elliptic flow, before definitive conclusions can be drawn about the existence of local parity violation in heavy ion collisions.
