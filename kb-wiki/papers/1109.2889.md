# The Deuteron and Exotic Two-Body Bound States from Lattice QCD

> Results of a high-statistics, multi-volume Lattice QCD exploration of the deuteron, the di-neutron, the H-dibaryon, and the Xi-Xi- system at a pion mass of m ~ 390 MeV are presented. Calculations were performed with an anisotropic n_f = 2+1 Clover discretization in four lattice volumes of spatial extent L ~ 2.0, 2.5, 3.0 and 4.0 fm, with a lattice spacing of b_s ~ 0.123 fm in the spatial-direction, and b_t ~ b_s/3.5 in the time-direction. The Xi-Xi- is found to be bound by B_{Xi-Xi-} = 14.0(1.4)(6.7) MeV, consistent with expectations based upon phenomenological models and low-energy effective field theories constrained by nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering data at the physical light-quark masses. We find weak evidence that both the deuteron and the di-neutron are bound at this pion mass, with binding energies of B_d = 11(05)(12) MeV and B_{nn} = 7.1(5.2)(7.3) MeV, respectively. With an increased number of measurements and a refined analysis, the binding energy of the H-dibaryon is B_H = 13.2(1.8)(4.0) MeV at this pion mass, updating our previous result.

## Key claim

At a pion mass of approximately 390 MeV, lattice QCD calculations provide weak evidence (approximately 1-sigma significance after infinite-volume extrapolation) that both the deuteron and the di-neutron are bound, with binding energies of B_d = 11(05)(12) MeV and B_nn = 7.1(5.2)(7.3) MeV respectively, contradicting the trend suggested by most effective field theory and hadronic model analyses that the deuteron becomes less bound as quarks become heavier.

## Method

The NPLQCD Collaboration performed high-statistics lattice QCD calculations using an anisotropic n_f = 2+1 Clover discretization on four ensembles with spatial extents L ~ 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, and 4.0 fm, a spatial lattice spacing b_s ~ 0.123 fm, and anisotropy xi ~ 3.5. Two-baryon energy shifts were extracted from ratios of two-baryon to single-baryon correlation functions, and Luscher's method was used to relate finite-volume energy eigenvalues to infinite-volume binding energies through the effective range expansion and exponential volume extrapolation.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy at m_pi ~ 390 MeV: B_d = 11(05)(12) MeV (infinite-volume extrapolation), with B_d^(L=24) = 22.3 +/- 2.3 +/- 5.4 MeV and B_d^(L=32) = 14.9 +/- 2.3 +/- 5.8 MeV in the two largest volumes
* Di-neutron binding energy at m_pi ~ 390 MeV: B_nn = 7.1(5.2)(7.3) MeV (infinite-volume extrapolation), with B_nn^(L=24) = 10.4 +/- 2.6 +/- 3.1 MeV and B_nn^(L=32) = 8.3 +/- 2.2 +/- 3.3 MeV
* Xi-Xi- binding energy at m_pi ~ 390 MeV: B_{Xi-Xi-} = 14.0(1.4)(6.7) MeV
* H-dibaryon binding energy at m_pi ~ 390 MeV: B_H = 13.2(1.8)(4.0) MeV (updating the previous NPLQCD result)
* Physical deuteron binding energy: B_d = 2.224644(34) MeV (given for comparison)
* Pion mass of the calculation: m_pi ~ 390 MeV
* Spatial lattice spacing: b_s ~ 0.123 fm
* Lattice volumes: L ~ 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, and 4.0 fm, corresponding to m_pi L ~ 3.86, 4.82, 5.79, and 7.71

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's previous lattice QCD studies of multi-baryon systems (Beane 2009, Beane 2010) by providing refined H-dibaryon results and new evidence for Xi-Xi- binding, deuteron binding, and di-neutron binding at a pion mass of 390 MeV. The results conflict with the trend suggested by effective field theory analyses (Beane 2002, Epelbaum 2002) and hadronic models (Flambaum 2007) that the deuteron becomes less bound as quark masses increase, and enable future calculations at lighter pion masses and larger volumes to resolve the quark-mass dependence of nuclear binding, with implications for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints on time-variation of fundamental constants.
