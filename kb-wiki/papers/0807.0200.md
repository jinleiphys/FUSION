# Deconfinement phase transition in hybrid neutron stars from the Brueckner theory with three-body forces and a quark model with chiral mass scaling

> We study the properties of strange quark matter in equilibrium with normal nuclear matter. Instead of using the conventional bag model in quark sector, we achieve the confinement by a density-dependent quark mass derived from in-medium chiral condensates. In nuclear matter, we adopt the equation of state from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone approach with three-body forces. It is found that the mixed phase can occur, for a reasonable confinement parameter, near the normal nuclear saturation density, and goes over into pure quark matter at about 5 times the saturation. The onset of mixed and quark phases is compatible with the observed class of low-mass neutron stars, but it hinders the occurrence of kaon condensation.

## Key claim

The mixed hadron-quark phase can occur near normal nuclear saturation density for a reasonable confinement parameter, and transitions to pure quark matter at about 5 times saturation density, but this result depends on using a density-dependent quark mass model rather than the conventional bag model and is compatible only with low-mass neutron stars.

## Method

The authors combined a Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) equation of state for nuclear matter, including three-body forces constructed from meson-exchange currents with virtual nucleon resonance excitations and relativistic Z-diagrams, with a quark matter model where confinement is achieved by a density-dependent quark mass derived from in-medium chiral condensates (cubic scaling law). They solved the charge-neutrality and weak-equilibrium conditions for the quark phase and applied the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium between hadronic and quark matter, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase onset occurs near normal nuclear saturation density (approximately 0.16 fm^-3)
- Pure quark matter appears at about 5 times saturation density (approximately 0.8 fm^-3)
- For strange quark current mass m_s0 = 100 MeV, the confinement parameter D^(1/2) is constrained to the range 158-160 MeV by the Witten-Bodmer stability hypothesis
- The upper bound on D^(1/2) from vacuum condensate considerations is 270 MeV
- The lower bound on D^(1/2) is approximately 156 MeV when finite light quark masses are included
- The energy per baryon threshold for absolutely stable strange quark matter is 930 MeV (mass of ^56Fe divided by 56)
- The metastability threshold is 939 MeV (nucleon mass)

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent quark mass model (CDDM) developed by Peng et al. (2000, 2001) and Wen et al. (2005) to study deconfinement phase transitions in neutron stars, replacing the conventional bag model approach. It contrasts with results from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Schertler et al. (1999), where mixed quark phases could not appear at neutron-star densities. The finding that the mixed phase onset is compatible with low-mass neutron stars but hinders kaon condensation connects to the two-class neutron star scenario proposed by H. Heiselberg and M. Hjorth-Jensen (2000), where low-mass hybrid stars and high-mass hadronic stars may arise from different evolutionary paths.
