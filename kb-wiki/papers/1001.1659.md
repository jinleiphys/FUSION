# Remarks on the origin of Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson poles

> Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) poles are known to be connected with bound states and resonances. We discuss a new type of CDD pole associated with primitives i.e., poles of the P matrix that correspond to zeros of the D function on the unitary cut. Low's scattering equation is generalized for amplitudes with primitives. The relationship between the CDD poles and the primitives is illustrated by a description of the S-wave nucleon-nucleon phase shifts.

# Remarks on the origin of Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson poles

## Key claim
Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) poles can be associated not only with bound states and resonances, but also with a new type called primitives, poles of the P matrix that correspond to zeros of the D function on the unitary cut, which drive phase shifts downward and mimic repulsion, provided the constraint that Im D(s) is non-negative rather than strictly positive is adopted.

## Method
The author generalizes the Dyson model for scattering amplitudes by relaxing the condition Im D(s) > 0 to Im D(s) ≥ 0, allowing zeros of the D function on the unitary cut. A generalized Low scattering equation is derived that includes pole terms from both bound states and primitives. The formalism is applied to S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering in the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ channels using a relativistic extension of the quark compound bag (QCB) model, with a contact interaction added and the D function parametrized to reproduce experimental phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- In the ³S₁ channel, the phase shift vanishes at T_lab = 354 MeV, corresponding to k = 408 MeV; the effective interaction radius b = 1.52 fm.
- The primitive in the ³S₁ channel has mass M_α = 2047 MeV; the associated CDD pole is at M = 3203 MeV.
- In the ¹S₀ channel, the phase shift vanishes at T_lab = 265 MeV; b = 1.76 fm.
- The primitive in the ¹S₀ channel has mass M_α = 2006 MeV; the associated CDD pole is at M = 2916 MeV.
- The ¹S₀ scattering length a = 23.56 fm is used to fix the contact interaction parameter γ = 1 + b/a.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson model (Dyson, 1960) and the CDD pole formalism (Castillejo, Dalitz, and Dyson, 1956) to systems with repulsion, complementing the P-matrix approach of Jaffe and Low (1979) for identifying exotic multiquark states. The results are consistent with earlier QCB model calculations by Simonov (1981) and with the hybrid Lee model of Benjamins and van Dijk (1988), which can be reformulated in terms of one CDD pole and two compound states. The analysis enables improved modeling of short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions and the high-density equation of state of nuclear matter, relevant to heavy-ion collision data and neutron star observations.
