# Production of light hypernuclei with light-ion beams and targets

> Ion-ion collisions at relativistic energies have been shown recently to be a promising technique for the production of hypernuclei. In this article, we further investigate the production of light $Λ$ hypernuclei by use of a hybrid dynamical model, cascade-coalescence followed by Fermi breakup. The predictions are then compared with the available experimental data. The dependence of the production cross section upon the beam energy, beam mass number as well as different projectile-target combinations is investigated. In particular, we evaluate the yields and signal-over-background ratio in the invariant-mass spectrum for carbon projectiles impinging on hydrogen and carbon targets and various coincidence conditions in the experiment using the theoretical calculation as an input. It is found that comparing with carbon target, hydrogen target also leads to sizable hypernuclear yields, even for exotic species, and the hydrogen target could improve significantly signal-over-background ratio in some hypernuclear invariant mass studies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that hydrogen targets can produce sizable yields of light hypernuclei, including exotic species, and can significantly improve the signal-over-background ratio in invariant-mass studies compared to carbon targets, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of excitation-energy distribution in the de-excitation model and is benchmarked against only two available experimental datasets.

## Method

The authors used the Dubna intranuclear Cascade Model (DCM) to simulate ion-ion collisions, followed by a coalescence criterion for hyperon capture by nuclear fragments based on a potential of 30 MeV in cold nuclear matter at saturation density. The de-excitation of hot primary hyperresidues was described by the Fermi-breakup model for light systems (A ≤ 16), using a parameterized excitation-energy distribution or alternative uniform distributions to test sensitivity. Predictions were compared with Dubna data from the 1990s and HypHI data from 2015 for light-ion beams (³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li) on carbon targets, and then extended to ¹²C projectiles on hydrogen and carbon targets at energies from 1 to 10 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶Li + ¹²C at 2 GeV/nucleon, the calculated cross sections for ³_ΛH and ⁴_ΛH are 0.2 μb and 0.02 μb respectively (using parameterized excitation energy), compared to HypHI data of 3.9 ± 1.4 μb and 3.1 ± 1.0 μb.
- For ³He + ¹²C at 5.14 GeV/nucleon, the calculated ³_ΛH cross section is 0.63 μb (parameterized) versus Dubna data of 0.05⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·₀₂ μb.
- For ⁴He + ¹²C at 3.7 GeV/nucleon, the calculated ⁴_ΛH cross section is 0.19 μb (parameterized) versus Dubna data of 0.4⁺⁰·⁴₋₀·₂ μb.
- The total inelastic cross section for ¹²C + ¹²C at 2 GeV/nucleon is 908 mb; for ¹²C + ¹H at the same energy it is 268 mb, giving a ratio of approximately 3.4.
- The total yield of Λ hyperons calculated for ⁶Li + ¹²C at 2 GeV/nucleon is around 4.5 mb, larger than the HypHI acceptance-corrected result of 1.7 ± 0.8 mb.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Botvina et al. (2013, 2015, 2017) for hypernucleus production in peripheral ion collisions, using a hybrid cascade-coalescence and Fermi-breakup model. It directly benchmarks against the Dubna data from Avramenko et al. (1992) and the HypHI data from Rappold et al. (2015), finding satisfactory agreement with Dubna but significant underestimation of HypHI yields. The predictions for carbon projectiles on hydrogen and carbon targets at 1-10 GeV/nucleon enable future experiments at FAIR and HIAF to optimize target choice and beam energy for producing light hypernuclei, including exotic species like ⁶_ΛH and ⁸_ΛHe, and to improve signal-over-background ratios in invariant-mass measurements.
