# The impact of quark masses on pQCD thermodynamics

> We present results for several thermodynamic quantities within the next-to-leading order calculation of the thermodynamic potential in perturbative QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential including non-vanishing quark masses. These results are compared to lattice data and to higher-order optimized perturbative calculations to investigate the trend brought about by mass corrections.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD thermodynamic potential with non-vanishing quark masses provides a baseline that, while not quantitatively precise due to the poor convergence of the weak-coupling expansion, captures the trend of mass corrections and yields results reasonably close to lattice data within the interesting temperature regime, especially when running coupling and strange-quark mass are included.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential to order g^2 (NLO) in perturbative QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, including non-zero quark masses in the fermionic exchange diagram. The calculation is performed in the MSbar renormalization scheme, with the running of the strong coupling constant and the strange-quark mass implemented following Fraga (2004). The resulting pressure, energy density, entropy, trace anomaly, speed of sound, and quark number susceptibilities are evaluated numerically and compared to lattice data from Borsanyi (2012) and to higher-order optimized perturbative calculations such as Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) from Haque (2014) and dimensional reduction from Fraga (2013).

## Key numbers

* For 2+1 flavors at T=300 MeV, the difference in normalized pressure p/p_SB between massless quarks and quarks with m_f=200 MeV is roughly 9%.
* For 2+1 flavors with physical quark masses at T=150 MeV and mu_B=400 MeV, the difference in normalized pressure compared to massless quarks is approximately 11%.
* For 2 flavors with m_f=200 MeV at T=150 MeV and mu_B=400 MeV, the difference in normalized pressure compared to massless quarks amounts to almost 70%.
* The up and down quark masses are fixed to m_u=2.3 MeV and m_d=4.8 MeV.
* The strange-quark mass is set to m_s approximately 100 MeV at Lambda=2 GeV, with the invariant mass hat_m_s approximately 262 MeV and Lambda_MSbar approximately 380 MeV.
* The renormalization scale is chosen as Lambda=2*sqrt((pi T)^2+mu^2), with band uncertainties from varying Lambda by a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative calculations of the thermodynamic potential with massive quarks at zero temperature by Fraga (2004) and Kurkela (2009) to finite temperature and chemical potential at NLO. It also builds on the two-loop massive quark calculation at finite temperature by Laine (2006). The results enable comparisons to lattice QCD data at physical quark masses and provide a baseline for studying the role of heavier quarks (e.g., charm) in the early universe or in heavy-ion collisions, while also highlighting that susceptibilities are particularly sensitive to quark mass effects.
