# QCD strings and the thermodynamics of the metastable phase of QCD at large $N_c$

> The thermodyanmics of a metastable hadronic phase of QCD at large $N_C$ are related to properties of an effective QCD string. In particular, it is shown that in the large $N_c$ limit and near the maximum hadronic temperature, $T_H$, the energy density and pressure of the metastable phase scale as ${\cal E} \sim (T_H-T)^{-(D_\perp-6)/2}$ (for $D_\perp <6$) and $P \sim (T_H-T)^{-(D_\perp-4)/2}$ (for $D_\perp <4$) where $D_\perp$ is the effective number of transverse dimensions of the string theory. It is shown, however, that for the thermodynamic quantities of interest the limits $T \to T_H$ and $N_c \to \infty$ do not commute. The prospect of extracting $D_\perp$ via lattice simulations of the metastable hadronic phase at moderately large $N_c$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the large \(N_c\) limit of QCD, the energy density and pressure of the metastable hadronic phase diverge with power-law behavior near the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\), with exponents determined solely by the effective number of transverse dimensions \(D_\perp\) of the QCD string; however, the limits \(T \to T_H\) and \(N_c \to \infty\) do not commute, so the critical exponents for infinite \(N_c\) differ from those extracted at any finite \(N_c\).

## Method

The author models the metastable hadronic phase of large \(N_c\) pure gauge QCD as a non-interacting gas of glueballs, justified by the \(1/N_c^2\) scaling of glueball-glueball interactions. The asymptotic density of hadronic states is taken from string theory as \(\rho(m) \sim A (m/T_H)^{-2B} e^{m/T_H}\) with \(B = (D_\perp + 3)/4\). The integrals for the asymptotic energy density and pressure are evaluated near \(T_H\) using this Hagedorn spectrum, yielding power-law divergences in \((T_H - T)\) whose exponents depend on \(D_\perp\). The range of validity of the non-interacting gas approximation is then bounded by requiring that the pressure remain below order \(N_c^2\), where interactions become important.

## Key numbers

- Energy density scales as \(\mathcal{E} \sim (T_H - T)^{-(D_\perp - 6)/2}\) for \(D_\perp < 6\).
- Pressure scales as \(P \sim (T_H - T)^{-(D_\perp - 4)/2}\) for \(D_\perp < 4\).
- For the Nambu-Goto string (\(D_\perp = 2\)), the specific heat critical exponent is \(\alpha = 3\) (since \(\alpha = 4 - D_\perp/2\)).
- For \(D_\perp = 2\), the range of validity of the non-interacting gas approximation is \(c_1 T_H \ll T_H - T \ll T_H / N_c^2\), where \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are constants of order \(N_c^0\).
- For \(D_\perp < 4\), the upper bound of the validity range scales as \(T_H - T \ll T_H \, c_2 N_c^{-4/(4-D_\perp)}\).
- For \(D_\perp = 4\), the upper bound scales as \(T_H - T \ll T_H e^{-c_2 N_c^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn picture of a limiting temperature (Hagedorn, 1965) to the metastable hadronic phase of large \(N_c\) QCD, building on the understanding that large \(N_c\) QCD has a first-order deconfinement transition (Teper, 2005). It relates the thermodynamics near the maximum hadronic temperature to the effective QCD string, following the string-theoretic density of states derived by Dienes (1994). The analysis enables lattice simulations of pure glue theory at moderately large \(N_c\) (up to \(N_c = 12\), as in Teper, 2005) to extract the effective number of transverse dimensions \(D_\perp\) of the QCD string by measuring the power-law divergence of the energy density or specific heat in the metastable phase.
