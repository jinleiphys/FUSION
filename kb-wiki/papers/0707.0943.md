# On the nuclear reactions with participation of near-threshold baryon resonances

> The main features of nuclear reactions with participation of baryon resonances ($Δ$(1232), S(1535) and $Λ$(1405)), which are interpreted as a manifestation of the bound states of nucleus and corresponding meson are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that resonance-like behavior observed in nuclear reactions involving baryon resonances (Δ(1232), S(1535), Λ(1405)) may be explained either by the formation of bound "isonuclei" (meson-nucleus bound states) or by peripheral reaction mechanisms (triangle Feynman diagrams with virtual isobar production), and that current experimental data cannot definitively distinguish between these two interpretations.

## Method
The author reviews experimental data and theoretical calculations for π-nucleus, η-nucleus, and K-nucleus reactions near baryon resonance thresholds. For πd and π-12C reactions, the analysis compares pole diagrams (single-nucleon mechanism) with triangle diagrams (Δ-isobar rescattering on the intermediate nucleus) to explain resonance maxima, shifts, and broadening in total cross-sections. For K-nuclei, a phenomenological potential model for KN interaction is used to predict bound states of K-meson with light nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For π+d→pp reaction: resonance maximum at about 150 MeV (c.m.s.) with width of the order of 120 MeV.
- For πd elastic scattering at π-meson energies of about 200 MeV (l.s.): ratio (σ(π⁻d)−σ(π⁻p))/σ(π⁻p) ≈ 3.
- For π⁻+12C→π⁻n11C reaction: resonance maximum at kinetic π-meson energy of about 200 MeV (l.s.); shift of the order of 30 MeV to smaller incident π-meson energy explained by triangle diagram contribution.
- For Δ₃₃(1232) production on nuclei: pole position in πN amplitude corresponds to Re M(Δ₃₃) = 1210 MeV (off-shell).
- For S₁₁(1535) production: pole position Re M(S₁₁) = 1500 MeV.
- For K-nuclei: predicted binding energies for ³_KH(I=0,1) and ⁴_KH(I=1/2) from 20 MeV up to 100 MeV with widths of order several tens MeV.
- For tribaryon states S⁰(3115) and S⁺(3140): binding energies ~200 MeV, widths <20 MeV; statistical significance ~13σ for S(3115) and ~3.7σ for S(3140).
- For radiative transitions from K⁻-He atom to three-body K-nucleus states: estimated probabilities of order 10⁻³–10⁻⁴; for π-meson transitions: 10⁻¹–10⁻².

## Context
This work extends earlier experimental studies by Mescheryakov et al. (first experimental data on π⁺d→pp total cross-sections) and theoretical calculations using triangle Feynman diagrams for virtual isobar production. It reviews the ongoing debate between the "dinucleon resonance" (isonucleus) interpretation and the peripheral mechanism explanation for resonance maxima in πd and π-nucleus reactions. The paper also connects to experimental searches for η-nuclei (using photoproduction of S₁₁(1535) on 12C) and K-nuclei (based on potential model calculations and missing-mass measurements of tribaryon states), concluding that additional experimental and theoretical work is needed to separate nuclear reaction mechanism effects from genuine bound-state formation.
