# Effect of density dependent symmetry energy on Elliptical flow

> The effect of the density dependent symmetry energy on elliptical flow is studied using isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model(IQMD). We have used the reduced isospin-dependent cross-section with soft equation of state to study the sensitivity of elliptical flow towards symmetry energy. Aim of the present study is to pin down the Elliptical flow for the various forms of the density dependent symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The study finds that elliptical flow in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with stiffer forms of the symmetry energy (higher gamma) producing weaker squeeze-out flow for free nucleons, but the effect is less pronounced for light charged particles at the specific energy and system studied.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of Au-197 on Au-197 at 100 MeV/nucleon. They employed a reduced isospin-dependent cross-section with a soft equation of state and varied the density dependence of the symmetry energy using the parametrization E(rho) = E(rho_0) * (rho/rho_0)^gamma, with gamma values of 0.66, 1.33, and 2. Elliptical flow (v2) was calculated as the average of (p_x^2 - p_y^2)/(p_x^2 + p_y^2) and analyzed as a function of transverse momentum for both free nucleons and light charged particles.

## Key numbers

- System: Au-197 + Au-197 at incident energy of 100 MeV/nucleon, impact parameter b = 5 fm.
- Symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density: 32 MeV at rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- Three values of the stiffness parameter gamma were tested: 0.66, 1.33, and 2.
- Light charged particles (LCPs) were defined as fragments with mass number A between 2 and 4 inclusive.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the IQMD model to investigate how the density dependence of the symmetry energy affects collective flow observables in heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that elliptical flow is more sensitive to the symmetry energy than directed flow, and that the form and strength of the symmetry energy is a topic of active investigation in nuclear physics. The results enable future work to constrain the stiffness of the symmetry energy at densities above and below normal nuclear matter density by comparing simulations to experimental data.
