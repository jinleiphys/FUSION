# Role of isospin physics in supernova matter and neutron stars

> We investigate the liquid-gas phase transition of hot protoneutron stars shortly after their birth following supernova explosion and the composition and structure of hyperon-rich (proto)neutron stars within a relativistic mean-field model where the nuclear symmetry energy has been constrained from the measured neutron skin thickness of finite nuclei. Light clusters are abundantly formed with increasing temperature well inside the neutrino-sphere for an uniform supernova matter. Liquid-gas phase transition is found to suppress the cluster yield within the coexistence phase as well as decrease considerably the neutron-proton asymmetry over a wide density range. We find symmetry energy has a modest effect on the boundaries and the critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition, and the composition depends more sensitively on the number of trapped neutrinos and temperature of the protoneutron star. The influence of hyperons in the dense interior of stars makes the overall equation of state soft. However, neutrino trapping distinctly delays the appearance of hyperons due to abundance of electrons. We also find that a softer symmetry energy further makes the onset of hyperon less favorable. The resulting structures of the (proto)neutron stars with hyperons and with liquid-gas phase transition are discussed.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition in hot protoneutron star matter suppresses cluster formation and reduces neutron-proton asymmetry over a wide density range, but the symmetry energy has only a modest effect on the phase transition boundaries and critical temperature, while composition depends more sensitively on trapped neutrino fraction and temperature.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the FSUGold parameter set, with the nuclear symmetry energy constrained to a narrow range by fitting neutron skin thickness measurements of several nuclei. They included all charge states of the baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) and light clusters (deuteron, triton, helion, alpha) as explicit degrees of freedom with medium-modified binding energies, and applied Gibbs criteria for liquid-gas coexistence in neutrino-trapped protoneutron star matter under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition at nucleon density ~0.08 fm⁻³: T_c = 14.6 MeV for Λ_v = 0.03 coupling, increasing to T_c = 15.7 MeV when light clusters are included
- Symmetry energy at saturation density constrained to E_sym(ρ₀) = 32 ± 4 MeV, with density dependence bounded by 32(ρ/ρ₀)^0.7 ≤ E_sym(ρ) ≤ 32(ρ/ρ₀)^1.1 at subsaturation densities
- Hyperon potential depths used: U^(N)_Λ = -30 MeV (attractive), U^(N)_Ξ = -18 MeV (attractive), U^(N)_Σ = +30 MeV (repulsive)
- Fixed lepton fraction values studied: Y_Le = 0.1 and Y_Le = 0.4 for protoneutron star matter
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Neutrino mean free path: λ_ν ~ 10 cm compared to protoneutron star radii of about 10 km
- Central temperatures of protoneutron stars: T = 10-50 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter (Sharma 2009) and neutron star structure constrained by neutron skin measurements (Sharma 2008) to the astrophysical context of hot protoneutron stars with trapped neutrinos. The symmetry energy constraints from isospin diffusion data (Li 2008), isoscaling (Shetty 2007), and neutron skin thickness (Centelles 2009) are applied within the FSUGold RMF model to study both the low-density cluster formation regime and the high-density hyperonic matter regime. The results enable more realistic modeling of supernova dynamics, neutrino transport, and the Kelvin-Helmholtz evolution stage where protoneutron stars deleptonize over ~10 seconds into cold neutron stars.
