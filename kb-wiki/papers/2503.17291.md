# Linear sigma model with quarks and Polyakov loop in rotation: phase diagrams, Tolman-Ehrenfest law and mechanical properties

> We study the effect of rotation on the confining and chiral properties of QCD using the Polyakov-enhanced linear sigma model coupled to quarks. Working in the homogeneous approximation, we obtain the phase diagram at finite temperature, baryon density and angular frequency, taking into account the causality constraint enforced by the spectral boundary conditions at a cylindrical surface. We explicitly address various limits with respect to system size $R$, angular frequency $Ω$ and chemical potential $μ$. We demonstrate that, in this model, the critical temperatures of both the chiral restoration and the deconfinement transitions diminish in response to the increasing rotation, being in contradiction with the first-principle lattice results. We demonstrate that consistency between the thermodynamics of the model and the Tolman-Ehrenfest law is achieved in the limit of large volume. We also compute the mechanical characteristics of the rotating plasma, such as the moment of inertia and the $K_n$ shape coefficients describing the response of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the increase of angular velocity $Ω$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that, within the Polyakov-enhanced linear sigma model with quarks (PLSM$_q$) under the homogeneous approximation, the critical temperatures for both chiral restoration and deconfinement *decrease* with increasing rotation, which contradicts first-principle lattice QCD results that show the opposite trend; this discrepancy is a central finding rather than a resolved issue.

## Method

The authors employ the Polyakov-loop-extended linear sigma model coupled to two flavors of quarks (PLSM$_q$) in the mean-field approximation, treating the chiral condensate $\sigma$ and the Polyakov loop expectation values $L$ and $L^*$ as homogeneous quantities. They impose rigid rotation with constant angular velocity $\Omega$ and enforce the causality constraint ($\Omega R \le 1$) by enclosing the system in a cylinder of radius $R$ with spectral boundary conditions, which quantize the transverse fermionic momenta via roots of Bessel functions. The grand canonical potential is computed numerically, and the phase diagrams are obtained by minimizing the potential with respect to $\sigma$, $L$, and $L^*$ as functions of temperature $T$, baryon chemical potential $\mu$, angular frequency $\Omega$, and system size $R$.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum pion decay constant is set to $f_\pi = 0.093$ GeV, the pion mass to $m_\pi = 0.138$ GeV, the sigma meson mass to $m_\sigma = 0.600$ GeV, and the constituent quark mass in vacuum to $M_q = 0.307$ GeV, fixing the quark-meson coupling $g = M_q / f_\pi \simeq 3.3$.
* The deconfinement temperature $T_0$ is fixed at $0.27$ GeV, corresponding to the pure-glue SU(3) transition temperature.
* The Polyakov loop potential parameters are $a_0 = 3.51$, $a_1 = -2.47$, $a_2 = 15.2$, and $b_3 = -1.75$, with the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for gluon pressure giving $\nu_g = 16$ degrees of freedom.
* At the first-order phase transition in the pure-glue sector, the Polyakov loop jumps to $L^{(+)}_c \simeq 0.448$.

## Context

This work extends previous effective model studies of rotating QCD matter, such as those using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu, 1961) and the linear sigma model (Gell-Mann, 1960), which also predicted a decrease of critical temperatures with rotation (consistent with the Tolman-Ehrenfest effect), in contradiction with lattice QCD results (Braguta, 2020; Braguta, 2021) that show an increase. The paper enables a detailed quantitative comparison between effective model predictions and lattice data, and it provides a framework for computing mechanical properties (moment of inertia, shape coefficients) of rotating strongly interacting matter, which can be tested against future lattice calculations.
