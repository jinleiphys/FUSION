# Weak charge form factor and radius of 208Pb through parity violation in electron scattering

> We use distorted wave electron scattering calculations to extract the weak charge form factor F_W(q), the weak charge radius R_W, and the point neutron radius R_n, of 208Pb from the PREX parity violating asymmetry measurement. The form factor is the Fourier transform of the weak charge density at the average momentum transfer q=0.475 fm$^{-1}$. We find F_W(q) =0.204 \pm 0.028 (exp) \pm 0.001 (model). We use the Helm model to infer the weak radius from F_W(q). We find R_W= 5.826 \pm 0.181 (exp) \pm 0.027 (model) fm. Here the exp error includes PREX statistical and systematic errors, while the model error describes the uncertainty in R_W from uncertainties in the surface thickness σof the weak charge density. The weak radius is larger than the charge radius, implying a "weak charge skin" where the surface region is relatively enriched in weak charges compared to (electromagnetic) charges. We extract the point neutron radius R_n=5.751 \pm 0.175 (exp) \pm 0.026 (model) \pm 0.005 (strange) fm$, from R_W. Here there is only a very small error (strange) from possible strange quark contributions. We find R_n to be slightly smaller than R_W because of the nucleon's size. Finally, we find a neutron skin thickness of R_n-R_p=0.302\pm 0.175 (exp) \pm 0.026 (model) \pm 0.005 (strange) fm, where R_p is the point proton radius.

## Key claim

The weak charge form factor of 208Pb at the average momentum transfer q̄ = 0.475 fm⁻¹ is extracted from the PREX parity violating asymmetry measurement using distorted wave calculations, with the result being essentially model independent and insensitive to assumptions about the surface thickness of the weak charge density.

## Method

Distorted wave electron scattering calculations were performed for electrons moving in Coulomb and weak potentials, using a Wood-Saxon parameterization of the weak charge density to compute the parity violating asymmetry averaged over the experimental angular acceptance. The Wood-Saxon radius parameter R was adjusted until the calculated average asymmetry matched the PREX measurement, and the weak form factor F_W(q̄) was then obtained from the Fourier transform of the resulting weak charge density. The Helm model was subsequently used to infer the weak radius R_W from F_W(q̄), with the surface thickness σ constrained by fits to seven mean field model densities.

## Key numbers

- Parity violating asymmetry for 1.06 GeV electrons on 208Pb: A_Pb = 0.656 ± 0.060 (stat) ± 0.014 (syst) ppm
- Average momentum transfer: q̄ = 0.475 ± 0.003 fm⁻¹
- Weak charge form factor: F_W(q̄) = 0.204 ± 0.028 (exp) ± 0.001 (model)
- Weak charge radius: R_W = 5.826 ± 0.181 (exp) ± 0.027 (model) fm
- Point neutron radius: R_n = 5.751 ± 0.175 (exp) ± 0.026 (model) ± 0.005 (strange) fm
- Neutron skin thickness: R_n - R_p = 0.302 ± 0.175 (exp) ± 0.026 (model) ± 0.005 (strange) fm
- Weak charge skin: R_W - R_ch = 0.323 ± 0.181 (exp) ± 0.027 (model) fm
- Point proton radius: R_p = 5.449 fm (derived from charge radius R_ch = 5.503 fm)
- Weak charge of 208Pb: Q_W = -118.55

## Context

This work provides a second, more detailed analysis of the PREX measurement originally reported in PREX1 (2012), extracting the weak form factor and weak radius in addition to the neutron radius. The analysis uses the Helm model, previously applied to unpolarized electron scattering form factors by Helm (1956) and later authors, to relate the finite-q measurement to the rms radius, and clarifies the modest model assumptions needed. The results enable direct comparison of theoretical predictions for the weak form factor F_W(q̄) rather than just the neutron radius, and support the planned PREX-II measurement which aims to reduce the experimental error by about a factor of three.
