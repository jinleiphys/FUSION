# Surface Interaction Effects to a Klein-Gordon Particle Embedded in a Woods-Saxon Potential Well in terms of Thermodynamic Functions

> Recently, it has been investigated how the thermodynamic functions vary when the surface interactions are taken into account for a nucleon which is confined in a Woods-Saxon potential well, with a non-relativistic point of view. In this manuscript, the same problem is handled with a relativistic point of view. More precisely, the Klein-Gordon equation is solved in presence of mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential energy that is coupled to momentum and mass. Employing the continuity conditions the bound state energy spectra of an arbitrarily parameterized well are derived. It is observed that, when a term representing the surface effect is taken into account, the character of Helmholtz free energy and entropy versus temperature are modified in a similar fashion as this inclusion is done in the non-relativistic regime. Whereas it is found that this inclusion leads to different characters to internal energy and specific heat functions for relativistic and non-relativistic regimes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when surface interaction terms are included in a Woods-Saxon potential well, the Helmholtz free energy and entropy of a confined Klein-Gordon particle behave similarly to the non-relativistic case, but the internal energy and specific heat functions behave differently. This conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of potential parameters (α = 1 fm⁻¹, L = 6 fm, V₀ = 1.5 m₀c², W = 4 m₀c²) and the particle (neutral Kaon) used in the numerical example.

## Method

The author solves the one-dimensional Klein-Gordon equation with a mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon (GSWS) potential in the strong regime, using hypergeometric functions and continuity conditions at the boundary x = 0 to derive transcendental equations for the even and odd bound-state energy spectra. The energy eigenvalues are then obtained numerically via the Newton-Raphson method for an arbitrarily parameterized well. Using these spectra, the partition function is constructed and the thermodynamic functions (Helmholtz free energy, entropy, internal energy, and specific heat) are computed as functions of reduced temperature.

## Key numbers

- Neutral Kaon rest mass energy: 497.648 MeV
- For the Woods-Saxon (WS) well (no surface term): the lowest bound state energy is −495.211 MeV (n = 0) and the highest is 495.095 MeV (n = 28); the internal energy saturates to a positive value of 40.022 MeV
- For the generalized Woods-Saxon (GSWS) well (with surface term): the lowest bound state energy is −492.093 MeV (n = 0) and the highest is 476.444 MeV (n = 17); the internal energy saturates to a negative value of −7.387 MeV
- Potential parameters used: α = 1 fm⁻¹, L = 6 fm, V₀ = 1.5 m₀c², W = 4 m₀c²

## Context

This work extends the author's previous non-relativistic studies of thermodynamic functions for nucleons confined in Woods-Saxon and generalized Woods-Saxon potential wells (Lütfüoğlu 2016, 2017, 2018) to the relativistic regime using the Klein-Gordon equation. It also builds on prior solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation with Woods-Saxon potentials (Bayrak and Şahin 2015) and the use of the generalized Woods-Saxon potential in nuclear physics (Satchler book). The results enable comparison of relativistic versus non-relativistic thermodynamic behavior for confined spinless particles, and the author notes that three-dimensional solutions with angular momentum (using approximations such as Pekeris 1934 or Green and Aldrich 1976) remain an open question.
