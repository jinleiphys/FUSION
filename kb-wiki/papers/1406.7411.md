# Spontaneous CP violating quark scattering from asymmetric $Z\left(3\right)$ interfaces in QGP

> In this paper, we extend our earlier study of spontaneous CP violating scattering of quarks and anti-quarks from QCD $Z\left(3\right)$ domain walls for the situation when these walls have asymmetric profiles of the Polyakov loop order parameter $l(x)$. Dynamical quarks lead to explicit breaking of $Z(3)$ symmetry, which lifts the degeneracy of the $Z(3)$ vacua arising from spontaneous breaking of the $Z(3)$ symmetry in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase. Resulting domain walls have asymmetric profile of $l(x)$ (under reflection $x \rightarrow -x$ for a domain wall centered at the origin). We calculate the background gauge field profile $A_0$ associated with this domain wall profile. Interestingly, even with the asymmetric $l(x)$ profile, quark-antiquark scattering from the corresponding gauge field configuration does not reflect this asymmetry. We show that the expected asymmetry in scattering arises when we include the effect of asymmetric profile of $l(x)$ on the effective mass of quarks and antiquarks and calculate resultant scattering. We discuss the effects of such asymmetric Z(3) walls in generating quark and antiquark density fluctuations in cosmology, and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions e.g. event-by-event baryon fluctuations.

# Spontaneous CP violating quark scattering from asymmetric Z(3) interfaces in QGP

## Key claim

The authors show that even when dynamical quarks explicitly break Z(3) symmetry and produce asymmetric Polyakov loop profiles in Z(3) domain walls, the background gauge field configuration alone does not reflect this asymmetry in quark-antiquark scattering; the expected left-right asymmetry in scattering of quarks versus antiquarks only arises when the asymmetric profile of the Polyakov loop is also included in the effective mass of quarks and antiquarks.

## Method

The authors calculate the asymmetric profile of the Polyakov loop order parameter l(x) between Z(3) vacua by energy minimization using an effective potential that includes a linear term b₁ to represent explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks. They extract the background gauge field A₀ profile from the l(x) profile by inverting the Polyakov loop definition in Polyakov gauge, then solve the Dirac equation numerically using the Kalotas-Lee method for quarks and antiquarks scattering from both the A₀ configuration and the spatially-dependent effective mass derived from the l(x) profile.

## Key numbers

- For b₁ = 0.03 at T = 400 MeV: left-incident quark reflection coefficient R_q = 1.65437×10⁻⁶, right-incident quark reflection coefficient R_q = 0.00003366; left-incident antiquark reflection coefficient R_aq = 2.25671×10⁻⁶, right-incident antiquark reflection coefficient R_aq = 0.000376883
- For b₁ = 0.645 at T = 400 MeV: left-incident quark reflection coefficient R_q = 1.43314×10⁻¹⁰, right-incident quark reflection coefficient R_q = 0.00394808; left-incident antiquark reflection coefficient R_aq = 2.07835×10⁻⁷, right-incident antiquark reflection coefficient R_aq = 0.073885
- The height of the A₀ potential for b₁ = 0.645 is approximately 100 MeV less than for b₁ = 0.03
- The effective quark mass is modeled as m(x) = m_q + m₀(l₀ - |l(x)|) with m_q = 10 MeV for u,d quarks, m₀ = 300 MeV
- The energy splitting between Z(3) vacua from quark effects is ΔV ∼ (2/3)π²T⁴(N_l/N³)(N² - 2), giving ΔV ≈ 3T⁴ for N_l = 2 massless quarks

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Atreya 2011) of spontaneous CP-violating scattering from Z(3) domain walls by incorporating explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks, following the approach of Dumitru (2000, 2001, 2002) where the breaking is represented by a linear term in the Polyakov loop effective potential. The asymmetric scattering results enable new mechanisms for generating quark and antiquark density fluctuations in cosmology and event-by-event baryon fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier proposals by Korthals-Altes (1994) about CP violation from nontrivial gauge field configurations.
