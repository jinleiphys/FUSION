# Contribution of Two-Boson-Exchange with $Δ(1232)$ Excitation to Parity-Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> We study the leading electroweak corrections in the precision measurement of the strange form factors. Specifically, we calculate the two-boson-exchange (TBE), two-photon-exchange (TPE) plus $γZ$-exchange ($γZ$E), corrections with $Δ(1232)$ excitation to the parity-violating asymmetry of the elastic electron-proton scattering. The interplay between nucleon and $Δ$ contributions is found to depend strongly on the kinematics, as $δ_Δ$ begins as negligible at backward angles but becomes very large and negative and dominant at forward angles, while $δ_N$ always stays positive and decreases monotonically with increasing $ε$. The total TBE corrections to the extracted values of $G^{s}_{E}+βG^{s}_{M}$ in recent experiments of HAPPEX and G0 are, depending on kinematics, found to be large and range between 13% to -75% but small in the case of A4 experiments.

## Key claim

The two-boson-exchange (TBE) corrections with Δ(1232) excitation to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering are found to depend strongly on kinematics: the Δ contribution δ_Δ is negligible at backward angles but becomes very large, negative, and dominant at forward angles, while the nucleon contribution δ_N always stays positive and decreases monotonically with increasing ε. The total TBE corrections to the extracted strange form factors range from 13% to -75% for HAPPEX and G0 experiments, but are small (about -2.5%) for A4 experiments.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-boson-exchange (TPE plus γZ-exchange) corrections with Δ(1232) intermediate states to the parity-violating asymmetry A_PV in elastic electron-proton scattering using a simple hadronic model. The γNΔ and ZNΔ vertices were constructed with form factors determined from experimental data on γN→Δ and νN→μΔ, and the loop integrals were evaluated using FeynCalc and LoopTools with dipole form factors for ultraviolet regulation. The resulting corrections were then applied to extract the strange form factor combination G^s_E + βG^s_M from the HAPPEX, A4, and G0 experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic calculations of Zhou et al. (2007) and Tjon et al. (2008), which studied TBE corrections to A_PV with only elastic nucleon intermediate states, by including the Δ(1232) resonance which dominates low-energy hadron physics. The results show that Δ excitation significantly enhances the TBE corrections at forward angles where HAPPEX and G0 experiments operate, increasing the corrections to the extracted strange form factors from (-14.6%, -45.1%) to (-25.5%, -75.2%) for HAPPEX and from (7.7%, 13.6%) to (13.1%, 20.6%) for G0, compared to the earlier nucleon-only calculation. The findings raise the question of whether higher resonances should be included, and suggest that the dispersion relation approach of Gorchtein (2009) for the γZE correction to Q_weak may need extension to address the strong Q² dependence at low momentum transfer.
