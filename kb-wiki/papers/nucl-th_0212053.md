# Thermal model at RHIC, part II: elliptic flow and HBT radii

> We continue the analysis of the preceding talk with a discussion of the elliptic flow and the HBT pion correlation radii. It is shown that the thermal model can be extended to describe these phenomena. The description of the elliptic flow involves an appropriate deformation of the freeze-out hypersurface and flow velocity. The obtained results agree reasonably with the data for soft (< 2 GeV) transverse momenta. For the pionic HBT correlation radii the experimental feature that R_out/R_side ~ 1 is naturally obtained. The reproduction of individual R_side and R_out can be achieved with the inclusion of the excluded volume corrections, which effectively increase the radii by \~30%.

# Thermal model at RHIC, part II: elliptic flow and HBT radii

## Key claim
The thermal model with a single freeze-out can be extended to describe elliptic flow and HBT pion correlation radii at RHIC, but only for soft transverse momenta below 2 GeV; the description of HBT radii requires excluded-volume corrections that increase the geometric size parameters by approximately 30%, and the model cannot reproduce the saturation of elliptic flow at high momenta or reliably evaluate the longitudinal HBT radius.

## Method
The authors parameterize the freeze-out hypersurface with an azimuthal shape asymmetry (elongation out of the reaction plane, parameter ε) and a flow-velocity asymmetry (faster flow in the reaction plane, parameter δ). They first fit the size parameters τ and ρ_max to φ-averaged p⊥-spectra from PHENIX at √s_NN = 130 GeV for several centrality bins, then fix ε from theoretical estimates based on Kolb (2000) and fit δ to reproduce the measured elliptic flow coefficient v₂. For HBT radii, they compute the two-pion correlation function using the Bertsch-Pratt parameterization and include excluded-volume (Van der Waals) corrections with a common hard-sphere radius r = 0.6 fm, which rescales the geometric parameters by a factor S = 1.3.

## Key numbers
- The excluded-volume correction factor S = 1.3, obtained with r = 0.6 fm and Σᵢ P⁰ᵢ(T, μᵢ) = 80 MeV/fm³, increases the size parameters by ~30%.
- For the most central bin (0-5% centrality) at √s_NN = 130 GeV, the fitted size parameters are τ = 8.2 fm and ρ_max = 6.9 fm, with ρ_max/τ = 0.84 and maximum transverse flow β_⊥^max = 0.64.
- For the 60-92% centrality bin, τ = 2.3 fm and ρ_max = 2.0 fm, with ρ_max/τ = 0.87 and β_⊥^max = 0.66.
- The shape-asymmetry parameter ε takes values 0.1, 0.21, and 0.35 for centrality bins 0-15%, 15-30%, and 30-60%, respectively; the fitted flow-asymmetry parameter δ takes values 0.145, 0.34, and 0.35 for the same bins.
- The ratio R_out/R_side is close to 1 and drops below 1 at larger pair momentum k_⊥.

## Context
This work extends the thermal model with single freeze-out presented in the preceding talk (Broniowski, Baran, Florkowski 2002, part I) from particle ratios and p⊥-spectra to elliptic flow and HBT radii at RHIC. It contradicts earlier hydrodynamic simulations that predicted R_out/R_side significantly larger than 1, and instead reproduces the experimental finding that this ratio is close to or less than 1. The approach enables a unified description of soft hadron production, elliptic flow up to p⊥ ~ 2 GeV, and HBT radii using a small set of parameters (T, μ_B, τ, ρ_max, ε, δ), and has been shown to also work at √s_NN = 200 GeV and at SPS energies (Broniowski, Florkowski 2002).
