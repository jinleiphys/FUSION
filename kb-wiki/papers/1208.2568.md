# Chiral dynamics with vector fields: an application to $ππ$ and $πK$ scattering

> A theoretical study of Goldstone boson scattering based on the chiral Lagrangian with vector meson fields is presented. In application of a recently developed novel approach we extrapolate subthreshold partial-wave amplitudes into the physical region. The constraints set by micro-causality and coupled-channel unitarity are kept rigourously. It is shown that already the leading order subthreshold amplitudes lead to s- and p-wave $ππ$ and $πK$ phase shifts are in agreement with the experimental data up to about 1.2 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that leading-order subthreshold amplitudes from a chiral Lagrangian with explicit vector meson fields, when extrapolated via a novel unitarization scheme, produce s- and p-wave ππ and πK phase shifts in agreement with experimental data up to about 1.2 GeV, with the hedge that the convergence pattern depends on the truncation index n in the conformal expansion of the generalized potential.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized potential U(s) from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term plus s-, t-, and u-channel vector meson exchange) evaluated in the subthreshold region, then analytically continue it to higher energies using a conformal mapping with a truncation parameter n and a scale parameter Λ_s. The partial-wave scattering amplitudes T(s) are obtained as solutions of a non-linear integral equation that rigorously enforces micro-causality and coupled-channel unitarity, with CDD poles included explicitly for p-wave amplitudes where vector mesons appear as poles above threshold.

## Key numbers

- The chiral Lagrangian parameter f = 90 MeV, identified with the pion-decay constant at leading order.
- The product of coupling constants f_V h_P ≈ 0.23 GeV, determined from two-body decays of light vector mesons.
- The matching scale μ_M is identified with the smallest two-body threshold accessible in each (isospin, strangeness) sector.
- The scale parameter Λ_s is varied between 1.4 GeV and 1.8 GeV to produce error bands in the phase shift predictions.
- The approach reproduces experimental phase shifts up to √s ≈ 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1967, Gasser 1983) beyond the threshold region by incorporating light vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom, building on the resonance saturation mechanism (Ecker 1988) where low-energy constants of Q^4 counter terms are saturated by vector mesons. It applies a recently developed unitarization scheme (Gasparyan 2010) to Goldstone boson scattering, and supports the hadrogenesis conjecture (Lutz 2008, Lutz 2003, Terschlusen 2012) that identifies Goldstone bosons and light vector mesons as the relevant degrees of freedom for generating the meson spectrum. The results enable systematic description of scattering phases in the resonance region up to 1.2 GeV using only two leading-order parameters.
