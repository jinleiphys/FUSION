# Formation of large chunk of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions

> In terrestrial laboratory with existing accelerators, the maximum multinucleon system produced so far is limited to that with total nucleon number being less than the sum of two colliding nuclei. Such situation may hinder our studies on the properties of nuclear many-body system. Here a way of producing large multinucleon system via multi-nucleus collision device Collider Plus in terrestrial laboratory is proposed. It is shown that large chunk of nucleonic matter can be produced through nuclear ternary fusion reactions at lower beam energies and also large block of dense matter can be formed via ternary collisions of heavy nuclei at intermediate energies. These investigations may shed lights on the studies of the synthesis of superheavy elements and the properties of bulk superdense nuclear matter.

# Formation of large chunk of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
A proposed multi-nucleus collision device called Collider Plus could produce large multinucleon systems via ternary nuclear reactions, potentially enabling the synthesis of superheavy nuclei beyond Z=118 and the creation of dense matter with larger volume and longer lifetime than binary collisions, but the reaction probability is the product of two smaller probabilities and thus far below that of binary collisions.

## Method
The study uses an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate nuclear ternary reactions, where one nucleus is fixed as a target while two projectile nuclei collide with it from opposite directions. A Skyrme-type parametrization with ground-state compressibility K=230 MeV is used for the isoscalar single-nucleon potential, and a density-dependent symmetry energy of the form E_sym = 32(ρ/ρ_0)^γ with γ=1 or 3 is employed. Fragment formation is handled via a phase-space coalescence model with parameters P_0=260 MeV/c and R_0=3 fm.

## Key numbers
- In the ternary reaction ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U+⁴⁸Ca at 6A MeV beam energy, about 10⁻² isotope (Z=120, N=178) and about 10⁻⁴ isotope (Z=128, N=178) are produced.
- For head-on binary Au+Au collisions at 1.1A GeV, dense matter with densities above 3.4ρ₀ lasts about 5 fm/c; for head-on ternary Au+Au+Au collisions at 0.4A GeV, this lifetime increases to about 8 fm/c, which is about 60% longer.
- The sensitivity of the free neutron-to-proton ratio n/p to the symmetry energy in the 3Au system is about 3 times that in the 2Au system.
- The synthesized compound nucleus can exist beyond 30 fm/c (approximately 10⁻²² s) and can thus be considered a nucleus.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of ternary nuclear reactions using time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory by Umar et al. (2010) on triple-alpha reactions forming ¹²C, and time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) by Iwata et al. (2013) on ternary reactions of three ⁵⁶Fe nuclei. The proposed Collider Plus device aims to overcome the limitation that current terrestrial accelerators produce multinucleon systems with total nucleon number less than the sum of two colliding nuclei. The approach could enable synthesis of superheavy elements beyond Z=118 without requiring heavier, short-lived targets, and could provide more sensitive probes of the high-density nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy than conventional binary heavy-ion collisions.
