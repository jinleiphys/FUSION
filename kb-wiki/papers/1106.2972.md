# Nuclear reaction rates and the primordial nucleosynthesis

> The theoretical predictions of the primordial abundances of elements in the big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) are dominated by uncertainties in the input nuclear reaction rates. We investigate the effect of modifying these reaction rates on light element abundance yields in BBN by replacing the thirty-five reaction rates out of the existing eighty-eight. We have studied these yields as functions of evolution time or temperature. We find that using these new reaction rates results in only a little increase in helium mass fraction over that obtained previously in BBN calculations. This allows insights into the role of the nuclear reaction rates in the setting of the neutron-to-proton ratio during the BBN epoch. We observe that even with considerable nuclear physics uncertainties, most of these nuclear reactions have minimal effect on the standard BBN abundance yields of $^6$Li and $^7$Li.

## Key claim

Replacing thirty-five of the eighty-eight Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates in the standard BBN code with newer compilations (Angulo 1999 and Descouvemont 2004) produces only a negligible increase in the helium mass fraction and minimal changes to the final abundances of D, He-3, and Li-7, even when considerable nuclear physics uncertainties are present.

## Method

The authors modified the Kawano/Wagoner BBN code by substituting thirty-five Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates with rates from the compilations of Angulo (1999) and Descouvemont (2004), which use R-matrix analysis and standard statistical techniques for uncertainty evaluation. They then computed the primordial abundances of D, He-3, He-4, and Li-7 as functions of evolution time for both a test baryon-to-photon ratio (eta_10 = 3.162) and the WMAP value (eta_10 = 6.19), comparing the results to standard BBN predictions.

## Key numbers

- Helium-4 mass fraction at test density (eta_10 = 3.162): standard BBN gives 0.2410; this work gives 0.2411.
- Helium-4 mass fraction at WMAP density (eta_10 = 6.19): both standard BBN and this work give 0.2479.
- D/H at test density: standard BBN gives 7.250 x 10^-5; this work gives 7.277 x 10^-5.
- D/H at WMAP density: standard BBN gives 2.519 x 10^-5; this work gives 2.563 x 10^-5.
- He-3/H at test density: standard BBN gives 1.546 x 10^-5; this work gives 1.613 x 10^-5.
- He-3/H at WMAP density: standard BBN gives 1.033 x 10^-5; this work gives 1.058 x 10^-5.
- Li-7/H at test density: standard BBN gives 1.268 x 10^-10; this work gives 1.367 x 10^-10.
- Li-7/H at WMAP density: standard BBN gives 4.627 x 10^-10; this work gives 5.019 x 10^-10.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity studies of nuclear reaction rates in BBN performed by Nollett (2000), Cyburt (2002, 2004, 2008), and Serpico (2004), using the newer rate compilations of Angulo (1999) and Descouvemont (2004) that supersede the earlier compilations of Caughlan (1988) and Smith (1993). The finding that updated rates produce negligible changes to final abundances reinforces the conclusion of Boyd (2010) that adding new reactions has little effect, and suggests that the "lithium problems" likely require nonstandard BBN scenarios involving new particle physics, as discussed by Dodelson (1993, 1994).
