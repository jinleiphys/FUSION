# Integrated Azimuthal Correlations in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at CERN SPS

> Azimuthal correlations of particles produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS are discussed. The correlations quantified by the integral measure Phi are shown to be dominated by effects of collective flow.

# Integrated Azimuthal Correlations in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at CERN SPS

## Key claim

The integrated azimuthal correlations quantified by the measure Φφ in nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS are strongly dominated by collective flow effects, specifically directed and elliptic flow, rather than by other sources such as jets, resonance decays, or transverse momentum conservation. The claim is conditional on the forward rapidity window studied (1.1 ≤ yπ* ≤ 2.6 in center-of-mass) and the specific acceptance of the NA49 experiment.

## Method

The authors used the integral correlation measure Φφ, defined as √(⟨Z²⟩/⟨N⟩) − √(z²), where z is the deviation of a single particle's azimuthal angle from its inclusive average and Z is the event-wise sum. They compared experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration for p+p, C+C, Si+Si, and Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV with predictions from a Monte Carlo model that generated particle azimuthal angles from a distribution including directed flow (v₁) and elliptic flow (v₂) harmonics, using measured Fourier coefficients from Alt (2003). The model assumed Poissonian multiplicity distributions with average multiplicities measured in the same acceptance window, and a 15% admixture of protons among positively charged particles based on UrQMD model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Φφ reaches a maximum at ⟨Nw⟩ ≈ 50 wounded nucleons in Pb+Pb interactions at 158A GeV
- The measurement acceptance in transverse momentum was 0.005 ≤ pT ≤ 1.5 GeV/c
- The rapidity window in the laboratory frame was 4.0 ≤ yπ ≤ 5.5, corresponding to center-of-mass rapidity 1.1 ≤ yπ* ≤ 2.6
- Among positively charged particles, there was a 15% admixture of protons as estimated from the UrQMD model
- For the 7.2% most central Pb+Pb collisions, Φφ for positive particles was consistent with zero while for negative particles it was positive, with no observed collision energy dependence
- The UrQMD model produced vanishing values of Φφ within the experimental acceptance

## Context

This work extends the study of the Φ measure, previously applied to transverse momentum fluctuations by Anticic (2003, 2008) and electric charge fluctuations by Alt (2004), to azimuthal correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies. The analysis builds on the authors' earlier study (Cetner, 2010) that separately evaluated contributions from collective flow, resonance decays, jets, and transverse momentum conservation to the Φφ measure. The results enable future disentanglement of correlation sources in data from the NA49 and NA61 Collaborations, and demonstrate that the UrQMD model (Bass, 1998; Bleicher, 1999) lacks sufficient collective flow to reproduce the observed signal.
