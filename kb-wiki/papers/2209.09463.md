# Collective-model description of shape coexistence and intruder states in cadmium isotopes based on a relativistic energy density functional

> Low-energy structure of even-even $^{108-116}$Cd isotopes is analyzed using a collective model that is based on the nuclear density functional theory. Spectroscopic properties are computed by solving the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, with parameters determined by the constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov method employing a universal energy density functional and a pairing force. The collective Hamiltonian reproduces the observed quadrupole phonon states of vibrational character, which are based on the moderately deformed equilibrium minimum in the mean-field potential energy surface. In addition, the calculation yields a low-lying excited $0^+$ band and a $γ$-vibrational band that are associated with a deformed local minimum close in energy to the ground state, consistently with the empirical interpretation of these bands as intruder bands. Observed energy spectra, $B(E2)$, and $ρ^2(E0)$ values are, in general, reproduced reasonably well.

# Collective-model description of shape coexistence and intruder states in cadmium isotopes based on a relativistic energy density functional

## Key claim

The triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian derived from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov mean-field calculations reproduces the observed intruder bands in even-even 108-116Cd as arising from a deformed local minimum in the potential energy surface, but for 112,114Cd the calculation significantly underestimates the intruder 0+ and 2+ level energies and overestimates the B(E2) and ρ²(E0) transition probabilities by a factor of two to three, indicating the local triaxial minimum is too pronounced with the employed energy density functional.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) method with the DD-PC1 density-dependent point-coupling interaction and a separable finite-range pairing force (strength V₀ = 728 MeV fm³ for both protons and neutrons). Parameters of the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, deformation-dependent mass parameters, moments of inertia (computed via the Inglis-Belyaev formula), and collective potential, were determined from the RHB solutions. The collective Hamiltonian was then diagonalized to obtain excitation energies, E2 and E0 transition probabilities, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments for 108-116Cd.

## Key numbers

- Global minimum occurs at β ≈ 0.15 (weakly deformed prolate) for 108,110,112Cd, and at nearly spherical configuration for 114,116Cd
- Local minima appear at β ≳ 0.3 on both prolate (γ = 0°) and oblate (γ = 60°) sides; a triaxial local minimum at (β, γ) ≈ (0.35, 12°) is close in energy to the global minimum for 112,114Cd
- For 110Cd: calculated B(E2; 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) = 32 W.u., consistent with experimental value <40 W.u.; spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q(2⁺₁) = −0.53 e b, slightly larger in magnitude than experimental −0.40(3) e b
- For 112Cd: calculated B(E2; 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) = 167 W.u., a factor of three greater than measured 51 ± 14 W.u.; Q(2⁺₁) = −0.68 e b versus experimental −0.38 e b
- Pairing strength increased by 15% (to 837 MeV fm³) for both protons and neutrons shifts the global minimum to β ≈ 0.05 and makes the triaxial local minimum much less pronounced

## Context

This work extends the application of the relativistic density functional theory plus collective Hamiltonian approach to the cadmium isotopic chain, where shape coexistence between normal vibrational states and intruder 2p-2h proton excitations has been extensively studied experimentally and theoretically. The results are compared with symmetry-conserving configuration mixing calculations using the Gogny force (Garrett 2019, 2020), which generally overestimated excited 0⁺ energies, and with interacting boson model calculations incorporating configuration mixing (Nomura 2018, Leviatan 2018). The present approach enables a parameter-free description of spectroscopic observables without effective charges, but the systematic underestimation of intruder band energies for midshell nuclei points to limitations of the DD-PC1 functional in producing the correct deformation energy landscape, motivating future improvements to the energy density functional or inclusion of beyond-mean-field correlations.
