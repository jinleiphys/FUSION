# Adding new branches to the "Christmas tree" of the quasinormal spectrum of black branes

> In holography, quasinormal spectra of black branes coincide with the poles of retarded finite-temperature correlation functions of a dual quantum field theory in the limit of infinite number of relevant degrees of freedom such as colours. For asymptotically anti-de Sitter backgrounds, the spectra form a characteristic pattern in the complex frequency plane, colloquially known as the "Christmas tree". At infinite coupling, the tree has only one pair of branches. At large but finite coupling, the branches become more dense and lift up towards the real axis, consistent with the expectation of forming a branch cut in the limit of zero coupling. However, it is known that at zero coupling, the corresponding correlators generically have not one but multiple branch cuts separated by intervals proportional to the Matsubara frequency. This suggests the existence of multiple branches of the "Christmas tree" spectrum in dual gravity. In this note, we show numerically how these additional branches of the spectrum can emerge from the dual gravitational action with higher-derivative terms. This phenomenon appears to be robust, yet, reproducing the expected weak coupling behaviour of the correlators quantitatively implies the existence of certain constraints on the coefficients of the higher-derivative terms of the dual gravity theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows numerically that additional branches of the quasinormal spectrum of black branes (the "Christmas tree" pattern) can emerge from a dual gravitational action with higher-derivative terms, but reproducing the expected weak-coupling behavior of the correlators quantitatively implies constraints on the coefficients of those higher-derivative terms.

## Method

The authors consider a 5-dimensional bulk gravity action with higher-derivative terms organized as a power series of the Riemann tensor squared, truncated at second order, with parameters γ₁ and γ₂. They compute the quasinormal spectrum of gravitational fluctuations in the scalar channel (h_xy) by solving the perturbative equation of motion to order O(α²), where α is a small book-keeping parameter analogous to α' in string theory. The spectrum is studied numerically as a function of the parameters γ₂/γ₁ and α, as well as the spatial momentum q̃ = q/2πT.

## Key numbers

- At α=0, the quasinormal spectrum coincides with the known AdS-Schwarzschild black brane spectrum, with poles at w̃_n = (±1 - i)n for n=1,2,3,... at zero spatial momentum
- The Hawking temperature of the black brane is T = (r₀/π)[1 + (5/3)α - (40γ₂ + 605/18)α²]
- New branches emerge for α=0.02 and γ₂ values between 0.005 and 0.05 at q̃=0.5, with pole collisions occurring at γ₂ ≈ 0.0065-0.007
- At q̃=0, for γ₂=0.05 and α increasing from 0.01 to 0.08, the second pair of branches eventually crosses into the upper half-plane, developing an instability
- For negative γ₂ = -0.3 at α=0.02 and q̃=0, a large number of poles appear on the imaginary axis without forming extra Christmas tree branches

## Context

This work extends the study of quasinormal spectra of black branes in holography, where the spectra coincide with poles of retarded finite-temperature correlation functions in the dual quantum field theory. Prior work by Hartnoll (2005) showed that perturbative α' corrections cannot qualitatively alter the pole structure, while Grozdanov (2016) found that a "partial resummation" approach reveals additional poles on the imaginary axis. The present paper demonstrates that adding a second higher-derivative term (R²)² to the action can produce the multiple branch cuts expected at weak coupling, which were previously missing from the strong-coupling holographic description. This enables a potential mechanism for reconciling the zero-coupling and infinite-coupling singularity structures of thermal correlators.
