# The Equilibration of a Parton Plasma Created In Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the equilibration of a parton plasma in terms of its parton compositions and its state of thermalization. In studying the evolution of the plasma, one has to assume a small value of the strong coupling constant. This value is by no means fixed. By varying this only parameter in our calculation, we show the dependence of equilibration on its magnitude. It is shown that both kinetic and parton equilibration are faster with increasing coupling but the plasma cools much more rapidly resulting in shortened lifetime. The degree of equilibration improves significantly for quarks and antiquarks but not so for gluons and the total generated entropy is reduced. With a coupling depending on the average parton energy, there is additional acceleration in the equilibration during the evolution.

## Key claim

Both kinetic and parton equilibration of a parton plasma are faster with increasing strong coupling constant, but the plasma cools more rapidly resulting in a shortened lifetime; the degree of equilibration improves significantly for quarks and antiquarks but not so for gluons, and the total generated entropy is reduced.

## Method

The evolution of a parton plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions was studied using a method described in Wong (1997) with the same initial HIJING inputs as prior work. The strong coupling constant α_s was varied as the only free parameter, using fixed values of 0.3, 0.5, and 0.8, as well as a time-dependent coupling α_s^v derived from the one-loop formula α_s(Q) = 4π/β_0 ln(Q^2/Λ^2_QCD) where Q is given by the average parton energy. The state of equilibration was checked by tracking parton fugacities, the longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio p_L/p_T, and temperature estimates T.

## Key numbers

- For quark and antiquark fugacities, a factor of 1.76 or more was gained in the final l_q at both LHC and RHIC when using larger α_s compared to α_s=0.3.
- The evolution time varied from several fm/c to over 10 fm/c depending on initial conditions, with estimated temperature dropping by hundreds of MeV and average parton energy decreasing by over 1 GeV.
- A fixed value of α_s=0.3 corresponds to an average momentum transfer Q ~ 2 GeV and Λ_QCD ~ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chemical equilibration (Biro et al., Shuryak and Xiong) and kinetic equilibration (Shuryak, Geiger, Wong) that used a fixed α_s=0.3, by systematically varying the coupling and introducing a time-dependent coupling based on the average parton energy. The results show that the confining force helps the parton system equilibrate but at the price of earlier onset of the deconfinement phase transition, and that a time-dependent coupling leads to accelerated equilibration during the evolution. This enables future work to incorporate a more realistic, evolving coupling strength in parton plasma evolution models.
