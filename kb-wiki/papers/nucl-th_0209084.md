# Testing limits of statistical hadronization

> Much of the energy of the nuclei colliding at RHIC or SPS is converted into final state hadronic particles. About a quarter of this energy is in baryons and antibaryons. There are nearly 10 strange quark pairs per central rapidity participant. Do we really understand the hadronic particle yields? Do we need to introduce post-Fermi-model ideas such as chemical non-equilibrium in order to understand how a deconfined state hadronizes?

## Key claim

The paper argues that a chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model, which includes phase-space occupancy parameters (γ_q, γ_s) beyond the standard Fermi-Pomeranchuk equilibrium approach, is necessary to accurately describe the full set of hadronic particle yields measured at RHIC at √s_NN = 130 GeV, particularly the hyperon and multi-strange baryon yields.

## Method

The authors perform a global fit to 21 central-rapidity hadron ratios from RHIC-130 using a statistical hadronization model with up to six chemical parameters (chemical potentials μ_i and phase-space occupancy parameters γ_i for u,d,s quarks), reduced to three free parameters (temperature T, λ_q, γ_s) by imposing strangeness neutrality and maximal pion phase-space saturation (γ_q² = e^{m_π/T}). They compare the χ² quality of fits between chemical equilibrium (γ_q=γ_s=1) and chemical non-equilibrium scenarios, accounting for weak-decay feed-down corrections from Ξ and Λ hyperons.

## Key numbers

- The chemical non-equilibrium fit yields T = 140.3 ± 1.1 MeV (with 100% Ξ→Y cascading) or T = 142.5 ± 1.2 MeV (with 40% Ξ→Y cascading), compared to T = 165.8 ± 2.2 MeV for the chemical equilibrium fit.
- The ratio γ_s/γ_q = 1.50 ± 0.04 (100% cascading) or 1.48 ± 0.04 (40% cascading), corresponding to γ_s ≈ 2.5, while chemical equilibrium fixes γ_s/γ_q = 1.
- The specific strangeness content s/b ≈ 9.5 in the non-equilibrium fit is nearly 40% greater than the chemical equilibrium result of s/b = 7.0.
- The χ²/dof for the non-equilibrium fit is 10/(21-3) = 0.56 (100% cascading) or 25/(21-3) = 1.39 (40% cascading), compared to 188/(21-2) = 9.9 for the chemical equilibrium fit.
- The baryochemical potential μ_B = 28.5 MeV (non-equilibrium) versus 31.3 MeV (equilibrium).
- The antiproton-to-proton ratio is 0.71 ± 0.06, and the Ξ/Ξ ratio is 0.83 ± 0.08.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach originally developed by Fermi (1950) and Pomeranchuk (1951), and later refined by Hagedorn (1965, 1967, 1968, 1980) with the exponential hadronic mass spectrum and the concept of a limiting temperature T_H. The authors demonstrate that the standard chemical equilibrium Fermi model, which was adequate for SPS data, fails to describe the full set of RHIC-130 yields, particularly the Λ/Λ and Ξ/Ξ ratios. The non-equilibrium analysis enables extraction of the Wr´oblewski ratio (Wroblewski, 1985) and provides evidence for a low hadronization temperature (140-142 MeV) consistent with rapid QGP expansion (Rafelski, 2000; Csorgo, 2002), and yields strangeness content compatible with nearly saturated strangeness in the deconfined phase.
