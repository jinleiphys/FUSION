# Medium effects in antikaon-induced $╬×^-$ hyperon production on nuclei near threshold

> We study the antikaon-induced inclusive cascade $╬×^-$ hyperon production from $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei near threshold within a nuclear spectral function approach. The approach describes incoherent direct $╬×^-$ hyperon production in elementary ${K^-}p \to {K^+}╬×^-$ and ${K^-}n \to {K^0}╬×^-$ processes as well as takes into account the influence of the scalar nuclear $K^-$, $K^+$, $K^0$, $╬×^-$ and their Coulomb potentials on these processes. We calculate the absolute differential and total cross sections for the production of $╬×^-$ hyperons off these nuclei at laboratory angles $\le$ 45$^{\circ}$ by $K^-$ mesons with momenta of 1.0 and 1.3 GeV/c, which are close to the threshold momentum (1.05 GeV/c) for $╬×^-$ hyperon production on the free target nucleon at rest. We also calculate the momentum dependence of the transparency ratio for the $^{184}$W/$^{12}$C combination for $╬×^-$ hyperons at these $K^-$ beam momenta. We show that the $╬×^-$ differential and total (absolute and relative) production cross sections at the considered initial momenta reveal a distinct sensitivity to the variations in the scalar $╬×^-$ nuclear potential at saturation density $Žü_0$, studied in the paper, in the low-momentum region of 0.1--0.6 GeV/c. We also demonstrate that for the subthreshold $K^-$ meson momentum of 1.0 GeV/c there is a strong sensitivity of the transparency ratio for $╬×^-$ hyperons to the considered changes in the $╬×^-$ nuclear potential at all outgoing $╬×^-$ momenta as well. Therefore, the measurement of these absolute and relative observables in a dedicated experiment at the J-PARC will provide valuable information on the $╬×^-$ in-medium properties, which will be complementary to that deduced from the study of the inclusive ($K^-$,$K^+$) reactions at incident momenta of 1.6--1.8 GeV/c in the $╬×^-$ bound and quasi-free regions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the absolute differential and total cross sections for inclusive $\Xi^-$ hyperon production in $K^-$ + $^{12}$C and $K^-$ + $^{184}$W reactions at incident momenta of 1.0 and 1.3 GeV/c show distinct sensitivity to the scalar $\Xi^-$ nuclear potential at saturation density $\rho_0$ in the low-momentum region of 0.1ŌĆō0.6 GeV/c, and that the transparency ratio for the $^{184}$W/$^{12}$C combination at the subthreshold momentum of 1.0 GeV/c is strongly sensitive to this potential at all outgoing $\Xi^-$ momenta, a sensitivity that cannot be masked by uncertainties in the $\Xi^-N$ inelastic cross section.

## Method

The authors employ a nuclear spectral function approach within a first-collision model, describing incoherent direct $\Xi^-$ production in elementary $K^-p \to K^+\Xi^-$ and $K^-n \to K^0\Xi^-$ processes. They incorporate the influence of scalar nuclear potentials for $K^-$, $K^+$, $K^0$, and $\Xi^-$ as well as Coulomb potentials by using average in-medium masses, and account for the attenuation of the incident antikaon and final $\Xi^-$ via total $K^-N$ and inelastic $\Xi^-N$ cross sections. The model calculates differential and total cross sections for $\Xi^-$ production at laboratory angles $\le 45^\circ$ and the momentum dependence of the transparency ratio for the $^{184}$W/$^{12}$C combination, testing three scenarios for the $\Xi^-$ scalar potential ($U_{\Xi^-} = -14$, 0, and +14 MeV) and additional options ($-25$, $-15$, $-5$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of $\Xi^-$ in-medium properties from the inclusive ($K^-$,$K^+$) reactions at incident momenta of 1.6ŌĆō1.8 GeV/c (studied by KEK-PS E224, BNL-AGS E885, and BNL-E906) to near-threshold ($K^-$,$\Xi^-$) reactions at 1.0 and 1.3 GeV/c, which have cleaner dynamics due to smaller two-step process contributions. The predictions are intended as guidance for future dedicated experiments at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility (e.g., E70 and E03), and the results are compared with various theoretical predictions for the $\Xi^-$ nuclear potential, including those from chiral effective field theory (NLO and LO), lattice QCD (HAL-QCD), the Nijmegen ESC08c potential, the NLD potential, and the in-medium modified chiral soliton model, as well as with phenomenological potentials extracted from emulsion data (KEK-PS E373, J-PARC E07) and missing-mass measurements. The approach is based on a model previously developed for inclusive $\phi$ meson production in pion-induced reactions (Paryev, Ref. [71]).
