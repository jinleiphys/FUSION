# Description of single and double analog states in the f7/2 shell: The Ti isotopes

> The excitation energies of single analog states in even-odd Ti isotopes and double analog states in even-even Ti isotopes are microscopically described in a single j-shell formalism. A projection procedure for generalized BCS states has been used. As an alternative description a particle-core formalism is presented. The latter picture provides a two-parameter expression for excitation energies, which describes fairly well the data in four odd and three even isotopes of Ti.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the excitation energies of single analog (SA) states in even-odd Ti isotopes and double analog (DA) states in even-even Ti isotopes can be described using a single j-shell formalism with an isospin-invariant pairing Hamiltonian, and that the ratio of DA to SA excitation energies is approximately 2:1 only when the diagonalization spaces for the odd and even systems are equal.

## Method

The authors used a generalized BCS formalism with particle number and isospin projection to compute expectation values of an isospin-invariant pairing Hamiltonian for valence nucleons in the f7/2 shell. As an alternative, they employed a particle-core coupling model where the odd neutron is coupled to the even-even core via an isospin scalar interaction of strength F. The pairing strength G was fitted to reproduce experimental excitation energies for each isotope, and the particle-core strength F was then determined from the SA state energies.

## Key numbers

* Excitation energy of SA state in 43Ti: 4.340 MeV (calculated) vs 4.338 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of SA state in 45Ti: 4.178 MeV (calculated) vs 4.176 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of SA state in 47Ti: 7.191 MeV (calculated) vs 7.187 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of SA state in 49Ti: 8.725 MeV (calculated) vs 8.724 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of DA state in 44Ti: 9.345 MeV (calculated) vs 9.340 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of DA state in 46Ti: 14.150 MeV (calculated) vs 14.153 MeV (experimental)
* Excitation energy of DA state in 48Ti: 17.395 MeV (calculated) vs 17.398 MeV (experimental)
* Pairing strength G for 44Ti: 3.115 MeV; for 46Ti: 2.830 MeV; for 48Ti: 2.485 MeV
* Particle-core strength F for 43Ti: 1.669 MeV; for 45Ti: 1.723 MeV; for 47Ti: 1.392 MeV; for 49Ti: 1.239 MeV
* Linear fit for G in even-A isotopes: G = -0.156A + 9.979 MeV
* Linear fit for F in odd-A isotopes: F = -0.0717A + 1.9773 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier observations by Zamick and collaborators (Zamick and Devi, 1999; Devi et al., 2000) that in some Ti isotopes the DA excitation energy is twice the SA excitation energy in neighboring isotopes, providing a microscopic justification using a generalized BCS projection method. The paper also connects to the exact O(5) group theory solution for the pairing Hamiltonian (Takahashi, 1971; Flowers, 1952), showing that the projection procedure yields identical results to the group theory formula. The results enable simple analytical expressions for SA and DA excitation energies in terms of isospin T using only two free parameters (G and F), which can be applied to other f7/2 shell nuclei.
