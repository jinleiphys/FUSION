# Minijets in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at future colliders

> The role of minijet production as initial conditions for QGP production at $τ\sim 0.1 fm/c$ in nuclear collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies is discussed.

## Key claim

Minijet production from perturbative QCD, computed using collinear factorization and independent parton-parton scatterings, can provide the dominant initial conditions for quark-gluon plasma formation at tau ~ 0.1 fm/c in nuclear collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, but only if the minimum transverse momentum cutoff p0 is chosen around 2 GeV; at the LHC the gluon system becomes transversally saturated and effectively thermalized in the energy-per-particle sense, while at RHIC the soft component must also be included.

## Method

The author computes the integrated minijet cross section and transverse energy production in central Pb-Pb collisions using lowest-order perturbative QCD with collinear factorization, employing GRV-LO parton distributions and a minimum transverse momentum cutoff p0 = 2 GeV. The average number of semihard partons and their transverse energy in the central rapidity window |y| ≤ 0.5 are obtained from the nuclear overlap function T_AA(b) multiplied by the integrated jet cross section, without including a K-factor or nuclear shadowing effects. Saturation of minijet production is estimated by comparing the transverse area occupied by produced partons to the nuclear transverse area.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (sqrt(s) = 5500 AGeV) in central Pb-Pb collisions: average number of semihard partons N_PbPb = 4740 (4349 gluons, 199.9 quarks, 191.2 antiquarks) with total transverse energy E_T = 14170 GeV (12960 from gluons) at tau = 0.1 fm/c, p0 = 2 GeV, |y| ≤ 0.5
- At RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV) in central Pb-Pb collisions: average number of semihard partons N_PbPb = 120.6 (99.58 gluons, 12.96 quarks, 8.102 antiquarks) with total transverse energy E_T = 320.7 GeV (262.8 from gluons) at tau = 0.1 fm/c, p0 = 2 GeV, |y| ≤ 0.5
- Initial net baryon number density at tau = 0.1 fm/c: n_B-Bbar = 0.21 fm^{-3} for LHC and 0.12 fm^{-3} for RHIC, compared to nuclear matter density 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For LHC gluon system: energy per particle E_T^g / N_g = 2.98 GeV, corresponding to equilibrium temperature T_eq = 1.10 GeV
- Net baryon-to-entropy ratio at tau = 0.1 fm/c: (B-Bbar)/S ~ 1/5000 for LHC, ~ 2/1000 for RHIC
- Saturation condition for A=208 occurs near p_T ~ 2 GeV for LHC and near p_T ~ 1 GeV for RHIC

## Context

This work extends the earlier minijet approach of Blaizot and Mueller (1987) and Kajantie, Lindfors, and Landshoff (1987) by incorporating updated parton distributions from HERA data and providing quantitative predictions for LHC and RHIC conditions. The results enable hydrodynamical modeling of early QGP evolution with pQCD-based initial conditions, complementing event generators like HIJING by Wang and Gyulassy and the Parton Cascade Model by Geiger and Muller. The paper also discusses alternative approaches not based on collinear factorization, including the classical gluon field model of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and BFKL-based minijet production, concluding that collinearly factorized minijets dominate at p_T ≥ 2 GeV for LHC energies.
