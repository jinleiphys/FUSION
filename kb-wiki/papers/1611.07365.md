# Leading Neutrons From Polarized Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> Leading neutron production on protons is known to be subject to strong absorptive corrections, which have been under debate for a long time. On nuclear targets these corrections are significantly enhanced and push the partial cross sections of neutron production to the very periphery of the nucleus. As a result, the A-dependences of inclusive and diffractive neutron production turn out to be similar. The mechanism of π-a_1 interference, which successfully explained the observed single-spin asymmetry of neutrons in polarized pp interactions, is extended here to polarized pA collisions. Corrected for nuclear effects it explains well the magnitude and sign of the asymmetry A_N observed in inelastic events, resulting in a violent break up of the nucleus. However the excessive magnitude of A_N observed in the diffractive sample, remains a challenge.

# Leading Neutrons From Polarized Proton-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The mechanism of pi-a1 interference, which successfully explained the observed single-spin asymmetry of neutrons in polarized pp interactions, is extended here to polarized pA collisions and explains the magnitude and sign of the asymmetry A_N observed in inelastic events; however, the excessive magnitude of A_N observed in the diffractive sample remains a challenge.

## Method

The authors extend previously developed methods for calculating leading neutron production cross sections in pp collisions to nuclear targets, using the Glauber approximation with Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections calculated via the dipole representation. The single-spin asymmetry A_N arising from pi-a1 interference is computed by replacing the ratio of diffractive cross sections on protons with the corresponding ratio on nuclei, and the nuclear absorption factors are evaluated in impact parameter space with different treatments for inclusive, inelastic, and diffractive event samples defined by BBC detector conditions.

## Key numbers

- The calculations are performed at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for pAu collisions, with additional results shown for pPb at sqrt(s) = 5000 GeV
- The asymmetry A_N is evaluated at average q_T = 0.115 GeV and average z = 0.75
- The effective a1NN coupling relative to piNN coupling is g_{a1 NN}/g_{pi NN} = m_{a1}^2 f_pi / (2 m_N f_rho) approximately equal to 1/2, where f_pi = 0.93 m_pi and gamma_rho^2/4pi = 2.4
- The partial cross sections for inclusive and diffractive neutron production in pAu collisions are plotted as functions of impact parameter b_A, showing production only on the nuclear periphery

## Context

This work extends the pi-a1 interference mechanism previously developed for polarized pp collisions (Kopeliovich et al., 2011) to nuclear targets, building on the authors' earlier treatments of absorptive corrections in pp leading neutron production (Kopeliovich et al., 2008). The calculations incorporate Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections (Gribov, 1969) using the dipole representation (Kopeliovich et al., 1981; Kopeliovich, 2003). The results are compared with preliminary PHENIX data (Bazilevsky, 2016; Nakagawa, 2016) and show reasonable agreement for inelastic events, while the diffractive sample remains unexplained.
