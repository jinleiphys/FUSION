# Running the gamut of high energy nuclear collisions

> We present calculations of bulk properties and multiparticle correlations in a large variety of collision systems within a hybrid formalism consisting of IP-Glasma initial conditions, MUSIC viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and UrQMD microscopic hadronic transport. In particular, we study heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), including Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, and O+O collisions, and Au+Au, U+U, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and O+O collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We further study asymmetric systems, including p+Au, d+Au, $^3$He+Au, and p+Pb collisions at various energies as well as p+p collisions at 0.5 and 13 TeV. We describe experimental observables in all heavy ion systems well with one fixed set of parameters, validating the energy and system dependence of the framework. Many observables in the smaller systems are also well described, although they test the limits of the model. Calculations of O+O collisions provide predictions for potential future runs at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a single fixed set of model parameters, determined from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, can simultaneously describe bulk observables across a wide range of collision systems from heavy ions down to p+p, though the model begins to fail for the smallest systems, testing the limits of the hybrid framework.

## Method

The hybrid framework combines IP-Glasma initial conditions (based on the color glass condensate effective theory with sub-nucleonic hot-spot fluctuations), MUSIC viscous relativistic hydrodynamics (with temperature-dependent shear viscosity η/s=0.12 and bulk viscosity ζ/s parameterized as a Gaussian peak at T=0.16 GeV), and UrQMD microscopic hadronic transport for the low-temperature regime. The initial energy-momentum tensor from the Yang-Mills fields at τ=0.4 fm is transferred to hydrodynamics, and particles are sampled via the Cooper-Frye formula on a constant energy density hypersurface at e_sw=0.18 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.12 (constant)
- Bulk viscosity peak value: B_norm = 0.13, with peak temperature T_peak = 0.16 GeV
- Switching time from IP-Glasma to hydrodynamics: τ_switch = 0.4 fm
- Switching energy density for particle sampling: e_sw = 0.18 GeV/fm³
- Lattice spacing for IP-Glasma simulations: a = 0.04 fm, with lattice size L = 28 fm for large A+A collisions
- Normalization factor applied to initial T^{μν}: 0.235, corresponding to g ≈ 2.06
- Infrared regulator mass: m = 0.2 GeV
- Hot-spot width parameter: B_q = 0.3 GeV⁻², with hot-spot center distribution width B_qc = 4 GeV⁻²
- Log-normal fluctuation width for hot-spot normalization: σ = 0.6
- Woods-Saxon parameters for ²⁰⁸Pb: R = 6.62 fm, a = 0.546 fm, β₂ = 0, β₄ = 0
- Woods-Saxon parameters for ¹⁹⁷Au: R = 6.37 fm, a = 0.535 fm, β₂ = -0.13, β₄ = -0.03

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD hybrid framework previously applied to specific systems (Schenke 2012, Schenke 2013) by systematically testing it across the full range of collision systems from U+U down to p+p collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. It provides predictions for O+O collisions at 200 GeV and 5.02 TeV for potential future runs, and establishes a baseline for future investigations of QCD transport coefficients, jet quenching, electromagnetic probes, and the chiral magnetic effect.
