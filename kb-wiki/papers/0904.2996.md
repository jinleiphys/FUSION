# Pion Production in Heavy-ion Collisions in the 1 A GeV region

> Within the framework of the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, the pion emission in heavy-ion collisions in the region 1 A GeV is investigated systematically, in which the pion is considered to be mainly produced by the decay of resonances $\triangle$(1232) and N*(1440). The in-medium dependence and Coulomb effects of the pion production are included in the calculation. Total pion multiplicity and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ yields are calculated for the reaction $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au in central collisions for selected Skyrme parameters SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII and compared them with the measured data by the FOPI collaboration.

# Pion Production in Heavy-ion Collisions in the 1 A GeV region

## Key claim

The improved isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, using in-medium inelastic cross sections and including Coulomb effects, can reproduce the FOPI experimental data for total pion multiplicity and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 1 A GeV, but only for the SLy6 Skyrme parameter set; other parameter sets (SkP, Ska, SIII) show deviations, and even SLy6 slightly overpredicts total pion multiplicity at lower incident energies and underestimates it at higher energies.

## Method

The authors extended the ImIQMD model to include inelastic nucleon-nucleon channels producing Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, which subsequently decay into pions. They used an in-medium inelastic cross section scaled by the square of the ratio of effective to free reduced baryon-baryon mass, and an energy- and momentum-dependent decay width for the resonances. The model was applied to central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at incident energies around 1 A GeV, with four different Skyrme parameter sets (SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII) corresponding to different nuclear incompressibilities.

## Key numbers

- Total pion multiplicity and π⁻/π⁺ ratio calculated for central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 1 A GeV using Skyrme parameters SkP, SLy6, Ska, SIII, compared with FOPI data (Re07).
- The bare decay width Γ₀ = 0.12 GeV for Δ(1232) and Γ₀ = 0.2 GeV for N*(1440) resonances.
- Decay width parameters: a₁ = 22.48 (Δ) and 17.22 (N*), a₂ = 39.69, a₃ = 0.04 (Δ) and 0.09 (N*), with momentum p in GeV/c.
- Using the isobar model, the ratio π⁻/π⁺ = 1.95 from Δ resonance decay and π⁻/π⁺ = 1.7 from N* decay for the ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au system.

## Context

This work extends the ImIQMD model, previously applied to fusion dynamics near the Coulomb barrier (Feng 2005, Feng 2008, Wang 2004), to pion production in the 1 A GeV region. It addresses the known overprediction of total pion multiplicity by transport models using free nucleon-nucleon cross sections below 2 A GeV (Ko 2005). The calculated π⁻/π⁺ ratio is sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy at lower incident energies, complementing the IBUU04 model analysis (Xiao 2009) that favored a soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities. The results enable further investigations at the Cooling Storage Ring (CSR) energies in Lanzhou.
