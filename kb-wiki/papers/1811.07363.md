# Zero point energy of composite particles: The medium effects

> We analyze the zero point energy of composite particles (or resonances) which are dynamically created from relativistic fermions. We compare the zero point energies in medium to the vacuum one, taking into account the medium modification of the constituent particles. Treating composite particles as if quasi-particles, their zero point energies contain the quadratic and logarithmic UV divergences even after the vacuum subtraction. The coefficients of these divergences come from the difference between the vacuum and in-medium fermion propagators. We argue that such apparent divergences can be cancelled by consistently using fermion propagators to compute the quasi-particle contributions as well as their interplay, provided that the self-energies of the constituents at large momenta approach to the vacuum ones sufficiently fast. In the case of quantum chromodynamics, mesons and baryons, which may be induced or destroyed by medium effects, yield the in-medium divergences in the zero point energies, but the divergences are assembled to cancel with those from the quark zero-point energy. This is particularly important for unified descriptions of hadronic and quark matter which may be smoothly connected by the quark-hadron continuity.

## Key claim

The zero point energy of composite particles (or resonances) in medium contains quadratic and logarithmic UV divergences even after vacuum subtraction, but these apparent divergences can be cancelled by consistently using fermion propagators to compute both quasi-particle contributions and their interplay, provided that the self-energies of the constituents at large momenta approach the vacuum ones sufficiently fast.

## Method

The author analyzes the UV divergence structure of composite particle zero point energies using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) formalism, comparing in-medium and vacuum fermion propagators. The study decomposes the 2PI functional into contributions from changes in fermion bases and changes in chemical potential, examining how the interplay between single-particle and composite-particle contributions transfers UV divergences between them. The analysis uses the gaussian pair-fluctuation theory as a concrete example, where fermion pair fluctuations are added on top of mean field results.

## Key numbers

- The zero point energy integral scales as $\int d^3k \, E_{\rm comp}(k) = a \Lambda_{\rm UV}^4 + b m_{\rm comp}^2 \Lambda_{\rm UV}^2 + c m_{\rm comp}^4 \ln \Lambda_{\rm UV} + \cdots$, where $\Lambda_{\rm UV}$ is a UV scale much larger than the natural scale of the theory
- For momentum-dependent mass gaps with damping scale $\Lambda_{\rm damp}$, the zero point energy difference scales as $\sim (\mathcal{M}_{\rm med}^2 - \mathcal{M}_{\rm vac}^2) \Lambda_{\rm damp}^2 + (c_{\rm med}^2 - c_{\rm vac}^2) \ln \Lambda_{\rm UV}$
- The difference between medium and vacuum self-energies behaves as $\Sigma_{\rm med} - \Sigma_{\rm vac} \sim \Lambda_{\rm med}^2 / p$ after cancellation of leading components, where $\Lambda_{\rm med}$ is an IR scale in the medium

## Context

This work extends the analysis of composite particle contributions to equations of state in strongly correlated fermion systems, particularly for QCD at high baryon density where degrees of freedom change from hadrons to quarks (Baym 2017, Masuda 2012, Kojo 2014, Fukushima 2015). The paper addresses inconsistencies in the gaussian pair-fluctuation theory (NSR, Diener, Ohashi 2002) for relativistic fermions (Abuki 2006, Sun 2007), where incomplete treatment of the interplay between single-particle and composite contributions introduces UV divergences. The 2PI formalism developed by Luttinger-Ward (1960), Baym-Kadanoff (1961, 1962), and Cornwall-Jackiw-Toumbolis (1974) provides the framework for handling these divergences, with the renormalization program following vanHees (2001) and Blaizot (2003). The results enable unified descriptions of hadronic and quark matter that may be smoothly connected by quark-hadron continuity.
