# Chiral symmetry in nuclei

> Effective field theory is considered to provide a highly useful framework for connecting nuclear physics with the symmetries and dynamics of the underlying theory of strong interactions, QCD. Of many issues that are of great current interest in this domain, I concentrate here on two: (1) A new class of ab initio calculations of obsevables in two-nucleon systems; (2) Attempts to extend chiral perturbation calculations to higher-order terms.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) and chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) provide a framework for connecting nuclear dynamics to QCD, but straightforward chiral counting fails for nuclei because purely nucleonic intermediate states can have very low excitation energies that invalidate the ordinary counting rule; the paper therefore advocates a two-step procedure (nuclear chiral perturbation theory) where irreducible diagrams are summed to a specified chiral order and used as an effective operator in a nucleonic Hilbert space.

## Method

The author reviews the application of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) to two-nucleon systems, distinguishing between a "hybrid approach" (where transition operators from chiPT are sandwiched between phenomenological nuclear wavefunctions) and a formally consistent "ab initio" approach (where both transition operators and nuclear interactions are treated at the same chiral order). For the ab initio calculations, a next-to-leading-order (NLO) potential containing one-pion exchange and contact terms is regularized with a Gaussian momentum cutoff, and the low-energy constants are fixed by fitting scattering lengths, effective ranges, or deuteron observables. For the pp -> pp pi0 reaction near threshold, the author enumerates 20 topologically distinct types of nu=2 irreducible diagrams (seven relevant for the specific reaction) and computes their relative contributions to the leading-order two-body transition operator.

## Key numbers

- The momentum cutoff range for the pionless case is Lambda = 100-300 MeV; for the pionful case it is Lambda = 200-500 MeV.
- Without the pion, experimental phase shifts are reproduced well up to p approx 70 MeV; with the pion, up to p approx 140 MeV.
- For the pp -> pp pi0 reaction, the ratios R_n of nu=2 diagrams to the leading-order two-body operator are: vertex-correction-type diagrams give R_n = 0.1-0.2; two-pion diagrams of types Fig.2(b) and Fig.2(c) give |R_n| = 3-7; the pion-pion rescattering diagram Fig.2(d) gives |R_n| = 7-10.

## Context

This paper extends the program of applying chiral perturbation theory to nuclear systems, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1990) and the heavy-baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995). The hybrid approach tested the "chiral filter mechanism" of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978) and Rho (1991), with detailed calculations by Park, Min, and Rho (1995) for the n+p -> d+gamma reaction and by Park, Min, and Rho (1993) for first-forbidden beta-decay transitions. The ab initio calculations by Park, Kubodera, Min, and Rho (1999) represent a step beyond the hybrid approach, while the analysis of the pp -> pp pi0 reaction addresses the claim by Ivanov et al. (1998) of a significantly different pp fusion rate and the earlier work by Lee and Riska (1993) suggesting heavy-meson exchange contributions. The paper enables future NNLO calculations and distorted-wave treatments of pion production reactions.
