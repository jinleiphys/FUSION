# Bound states of the $D$-dimensional Schrödinger equation for the generalized Woods-Saxon potential

> In this paper, the approximate analitical solutions of the hyper-radial Schrödinger equation are obtained for the generalized Wood-Saxon potential by implementing the Pekeris approximation to surmount the centrifugal term. The energy eigenvalues and corresponding hyper-radial wave functions are found for any angular momentum case via the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) and Supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY QM) methods. Hence, the same expressions are obtained for the energy eigenvalues, and the expression of hyper-radial wave functions transformed each other is shown owing to these methods. Furthermore, a finite number energy spectrum depending on the depths of the potential well $V_{0}$ and $W$, the radial $n_{r}$ and $l$ orbital quantum numbers and parameters $D,a,R_{0}$ are also identified in detail. Finally, the bound state energies and the corresponding normalized hyper-radial wave functions for the neutron system of the a $^{56} Fe$ nucleus are calculated in $D=2$ and $D=3$, as well as the energy spectrum expressions of other highest dimensions are identified by using the energy spectrum of $D=2$ and $D=3$.

## Key claim

The paper claims to obtain approximate analytical solutions of the hyper-radial Schrödinger equation for the generalized Woods-Saxon potential in arbitrary dimension D, but only for bound states and only after applying the Pekeris approximation to the centrifugal term, which limits accuracy to the region near the minimum of the effective potential.

## Method

The authors reduce the D-dimensional Schrödinger equation to a one-dimensional radial equation using the substitution \(u(r)=r^{(D-1)/2}R(r)\) and the effective angular momentum \(\tilde{l}=l+(D-3)/2\). They apply the Pekeris approximation to expand the centrifugal potential \(\hbar^{2}\tilde{l}(\tilde{l}+1)/(2\mu r^{2})\) in a Taylor series around the minimum of the effective potential, retaining terms up to second order. The resulting differential equation is solved independently using both the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method and Supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY QM), yielding identical energy eigenvalue expressions and wave functions in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron system of \({}^{56}\)Fe, bound state energies are calculated in \(D=2\) and \(D=3\) dimensions (specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text).
- The condition for a finite number of bound states is \(0 \le n_r < \frac{1}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+\frac{8\mu a^2 W}{\hbar^2}+\frac{4\tilde{l}(\tilde{l}+1)a^2 C_2}{R_0^2}}-1\right)\), where \(n_r\) is the radial quantum number, \(a\) is the surface diffuseness, \(W\) is the surface potential depth, and \(R_0\) is the nuclear radius.
- The energy eigenvalue expression for \(l=0\) and \(D=3\) is \(E_{n_r}=\frac{V_0}{2}-\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu a^2}\left[\frac{1}{16}\left(\sqrt{1+\frac{8\mu a^2 W}{\hbar^2}}-2n_r-1\right)^2+\frac{(2\mu a^2 V_0/\hbar^2)^2}{(\sqrt{1+8\mu a^2 W/\hbar^2}-2n_r-1)^2}\right]\).
- The Pekeris approximation constants for \(x_e=0\) are \(C_0=1-4/\alpha+12/\alpha^2\), \(C_1=8/\alpha-48/\alpha^2\), \(C_2=48/\alpha^2\), where \(\alpha=R_0/a\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous solutions of the D-dimensional Schrödinger equation for the standard Woods-Saxon potential (Badalov 2015) to the generalized Woods-Saxon potential, which includes both volume and surface terms. It corrects errors in earlier applications of the NU method to the generalized Woods-Saxon potential noted by Berkdemir (2008) and an editorial comment. The results enable calculation of single-particle bound states in nuclei using a more realistic potential that can describe surface effects, and the authors note that the standard Woods-Saxon potential cannot produce bound states for \(n_r=0\), \(l=0\) in \(D=3\) and \(D=4\), motivating the use of the generalized form.
