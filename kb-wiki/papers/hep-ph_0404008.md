# Solution of the Kwiecinski evolution equations for unintegrated parton distributions using the Mellin transform

> The Kwiecinski equations for the QCD evolution of the unintegrated parton distributions in the transverse-coordinate space (b) are analyzed with the help of the Mellin-transform method. The equations are solved numerically in the general case, as well as in a small-b expansion which converges fast for b Lambda_QCD sufficiently small. We also discuss the asymptotic limit of large bQ and show that the distributions generated by the evolution decrease with b according to a power law. Numerical results are presented for the pion distributions with a simple valence-like initial condition at the low scale, following from chiral large-N_c quark models. We use two models: the Spectral Quark Model and the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Formal aspects of the equations, such as the analytic form of the b-dependent anomalous dimensions, their analytic structure, as well as the limits of unintegrated parton densities at x -> 0, x -> 1, and at large b, are discussed in detail. The effect of spreading of the transverse momentum with the increasing scale is confirmed, with <k_\perp^2> growing asymptotically as Q^2 alpha(Q^2). Approximate formulas for <k_\perp^2> for each parton species is given, which may be used in practical applications.

## Key claim

The Kwiecinski equations for unintegrated parton distributions can be solved efficiently using the Mellin-transform method, which diagonalizes the evolution in moment space and allows for analytic studies of limits such as small and large transverse coordinate b, as well as x approaching 0 or 1. The method is validated for pion distributions using initial conditions from chiral quark models, but the abstract's claim of a "simple" solution glosses over the need to handle b-dependent anomalous dimensions involving generalized hypergeometric functions and the requirement of numerical inverse Mellin transforms.

## Method

The authors apply the Mellin transform (x-moments) to the Kwiecinski evolution equations for unintegrated parton distributions in transverse-coordinate space, converting the integro-differential equations into ordinary differential equations for the moments with b-dependent anomalous dimensions. They solve these equations numerically for complex n-values along the Mellin contour, then perform the inverse Mellin transform via numerical integration with oscillatory functions to recover the x-space distributions. The initial conditions at Q0 = 313 MeV are taken from two chiral quark models (Spectral Quark Model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) with a factorized form where valence quarks are uniform in x and gluons/sea vanish, and the evolution switches from three to four flavors above the charm threshold at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

* Initial scale Q0 = 313 MeV from the momentum sum-rule of chiral quark models, with alpha(Q0^2)/(2 pi) ~ 0.3
* Average transverse momentum squared from non-perturbative profile: <k_perp^2>_NP^(SQM) = (544 MeV)^2 for the Spectral Quark Model (M_V = 770 MeV)
* Average transverse momentum squared from non-perturbative profile: <k_perp^2>_NP^(NJL) = (626 MeV)^2 for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (M = 280 MeV, Lambda = 871 MeV)
* Evolution carried from Q0 = 313 MeV up to Q = 2 GeV for numerical results shown in figures
* Charm-production threshold taken at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 for flavor switching
* Low-b expansion converges with 8 terms sufficient for x > 0.01 at b = 5 GeV^{-1} and Q = 2 GeV
* Large-b asymptotic formulas valid when Q b is larger than about 10|n|

## Context

This work extends the original studies of Kwiecinski (2001-2003) who derived the evolution equations for unintegrated parton distributions in transverse-coordinate space from the CCFM formalism, using Chebyshev interpolation for numerical solution. The authors provide an alternative Mellin-transform method that is faster and more stable, and they use genuine non-perturbative initial conditions from chiral quark models (Spectral Quark Model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) rather than guessed Gaussian profiles as in the earlier work of Kwiecinski (2003). The results enable practical applications of unintegrated parton distributions for the pion in processes such as Higgs boson production, W boson production, heavy flavor production, and hadron production in heavy-ion collisions, with approximate formulas for the average transverse momentum squared provided for each parton species.
