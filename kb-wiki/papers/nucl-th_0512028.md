# Insights on scalar mesons from their radiative decays

> We estimate the rates for radiative transitions of the lightest scalar mesons f_0(980) and a_0(980) to the vector mesons rho and omega. We argue that measurements of the radiative decays of those scalar mesons can provide important new information on their structure.

## Key claim

The authors argue that measuring the radiative decays of scalar mesons \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) to vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) can discriminate between competing models of their internal structure, because the quark-loop and kaon-loop mechanisms contribute at comparable magnitude in these decays, unlike in the previously studied \(\phi \to \gamma S\) decays where kaon loops dominate.

## Method

The authors calculated the transition matrix element \(M(p^2,q^2)\) for the \(S \to \gamma V\) vertex using three distinct mechanisms: (i) a nonrelativistic quark model with \(E1\) transitions for \(q\bar q\) states, (ii) an annihilation graph for compact \(qq\bar q\bar q\) states, and (iii) a kaon-loop diagram with pointlike scalar-kaon-kaon and vector-kaon-kaon vertices. They then estimated the decay widths for each mechanism using known coupling constants and symmetry relations, and compared the resulting patterns to those from \(\phi \to \gamma S\) and \(S \to \gamma\gamma\) decays.

## Key numbers

* Quark-loop estimate for \(a_0 \to \gamma \omega\): 125 keV, and for \(a_0 \to \gamma \rho\): 14 keV, based on \(SU(6)\) symmetry and the measured width \(\Gamma(f_1(1285) \to \gamma \rho) = 1320 \pm 312\) keV.
* Quark-loop estimate for \(f_0(n\bar n) \to \gamma \rho\): 125 keV, and for \(f_0(n\bar n) \to \gamma \omega\): 14 keV.
* Kaon-loop estimate for \(a_0/f_0 \to \gamma \rho/\omega\): 3.4 keV, using \(g_S^2/4\pi = 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(g_V^2/4\pi \approx 0.36\).
* Kaon-loop estimate for \(\phi \to \gamma S\): 0.6 keV, compared to experimental values \(\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma a_0) \simeq 0.3\) keV and \(\Gamma(\phi \to \gamma f_0) \simeq 1.9\) keV.
* Quark-loop estimate for \(f_0(980) \to \gamma\gamma\): 4.5 keV, derived from \(\Gamma(f_2(1270) \to \gamma\gamma) = 2.61 \pm 0.30\) keV and the ratio formula for \(^3P_0\) and \(^3P_2\) states.
* Kaon-loop estimate for \(S \to \gamma\gamma\): 0.24 keV, compared to experimental values \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(f_0(980)) = 0.39^{+0.10}_{-0.13}\) keV and \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(a_0(980)) = 0.30 \pm 0.10\) keV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the structure of the light scalar mesons \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\), which have been variously interpreted as \(q\bar q\) states, compact \(qq\bar q\bar q\) states (Jaffe, 1977), or loosely bound \(K\bar K\) molecules (Weinstein and Isgur, 1990; Juelich group). Previous studies of \(\phi \to \gamma S\) decays (Achasov and Ivanchenko, 1989) showed a dominant kaon-loop contribution but could not distinguish between molecular and compact four-quark scenarios due to limited phase space. The authors propose that measuring \(S \to \gamma V\) decays, where both quark and meson loops contribute comparably, can resolve this ambiguity and enable extraction of the scalar mesons' pole positions and coupling constants.
