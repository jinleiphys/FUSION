# Ab Initio No Core Shell Model - Recent Results and Further Prospects

> There has been significant recent progress in solving the long-standing problems of how nuclear shell structure and collective motion emerge from underlying microscopic inter-nucleon interactions. We review a selection of recent significant results within the ab initio No Core Shell Model (NCSM) closely tied to three major factors enabling this progress: (1) improved nuclear interactions that accurately describe the experimental two-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction data; (2) advances in algorithms to simulate the quantum many-body problem with strong interactions; and (3) continued rapid development of high-performance computers now capable of performing $20 \times 10^{15}$ floating point operations per second. We also comment on prospects for further developments.

## Key claim
The No Core Shell Model (NCSM) can demonstrate the emergence of collective rotational bands in light nuclei from realistic microscopic interactions without prior bias toward collective solutions, but the agreement with experiment depends on the specific interaction used and the inclusion of three-nucleon forces.

## Method
The authors performed No Core Full Configuration (NCFC) calculations using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in an M-scheme harmonic oscillator basis, with extrapolation to the infinite matrix limit. For 12C and neutron drops, they used chiral effective field theory NN plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions evolved via the similarity renormalization group (SRG). The many-body problem was solved as a large sparse matrix eigenvalue problem using the Lanczos algorithm, with computational accelerators and communication-overlapping techniques employed for efficiency.

## Key numbers
- The supercomputer used achieved 20 × 10^15 floating point operations per second (20 petaflops)
- For Be isotopes, 23 theoretical and experimental collective rotation parameters were compared across 6 isotopes
- Storing a 3N input interaction in the coupled-JT basis reduced file size from 33 GigaBytes to less than 0.5 GB
- GPU acceleration produced a speedup of 2.2x–2.7x in the many-nucleon Hamiltonian construction stage
- The overall calculation speedup from GPU acceleration was 1.2x–1.4x
- Over 80% parallel efficiency was achieved through overlapping communications and calculations in the Lanczos algorithm
- For 12C, calculations were performed at Nmax values up to 10 for natural parity and 11 for unnatural parity, with ℏΩ = 20 MeV
- Neutron drop calculations used a 10 MeV harmonic oscillator trap

## Context
This work extends the NCSM framework developed by Navratil (2000) and Barrett (2013) to demonstrate collective rotations in Be isotopes and 12C, building on prior NCFC extrapolation methods by Maris (2009). The chiral Hamiltonian results for 12C show that including initial 3N interactions improves agreement with experiment for most states, but the 1+, T=0 positive parity state worsens, indicating need for higher-order chiral interactions. The neutron drop results with chiral interactions lie between results from the Argonne v8' plus Urbana IX interaction and the Argonne v8' plus Illinois-7 interaction from Gandolfi (2010) and Pieper (2008), providing benchmarks for energy density functional development. The computational techniques developed enable future studies of heavier nuclei and neutrinoless double beta decay.
