# The balance of attractive and repulsive hadronic interactions: the influence of hadronic spectrum and excluded volume effects on lattice thermodynamics and consequences on experiments

> Repulsive hadronic interactions play a relevant role in the QCD dynamics, attractive ones being represented by resonance formation. In this study we propose different schemes in order to parameterise repulsive interactions, then being able to extract effective sizes of hadrons from fits to lattice QCD simulations. We find that allowing a difference between the strange and light sectors, strange particles are systematically smaller than light ones with equal mass. The very simple implementation of repulsive interactions would in principle allow to extract precise information about all hadronic species once corresponding lattice observables, sensitive to the species of interest, are provided. With the parameterisation which best reproduces lattice data there is also a good description of experimental yields measured by ALICE and STAR experiments.

## Key claim

The balance between attractive interactions (represented by resonance formation) and repulsive interactions (parameterized via excluded-volume effects) can simultaneously describe lattice QCD thermodynamics and experimental particle yields, provided that strange hadrons are assigned systematically smaller effective hard-core radii than light hadrons of equal mass.

## Method

The authors performed fits of the Hadron-Resonance Gas (HRG) model with excluded-volume (EV) repulsive interactions to lattice QCD data for pressure, interaction measure, and several fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon, electric charge, strangeness) in the temperature range 110–164 MeV. They employed different particle lists (PDG2005, PDG2014, PDG2016, and a Quark Model list) and parameterized repulsive interactions using schemes where hadronic radii were either fixed, proportional to mass, or inversely proportional to mass, with separate parameters for light and strange sectors. The best-fit radii from lattice data were then used to predict particle yields measured by ALICE at 2.76 TeV and STAR at 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Best fit to lattice data achieved with QM particle list and mass-proportional radii (2b scheme): \(\chi^2 = 1.714\), with proton radius \(r_p = 0.38 \pm 0.092\) fm and \(\Lambda\) radius \(r_\Lambda = 0.266 \pm 0.034\) fm.
* Without excluded-volume effects, \(\chi^2\) values for PDG2005, PDG2014, PDG2016, and QM lists were 49.645, 10.094, 9.331, and 16.312 respectively.
* Experimental charge radii: \(\pi^\pm\): \(0.672 \pm 0.008\) fm; \(K^\pm\): \(0.569 \pm 0.031\) fm; proton: \(0.8751 \pm 0.0061\) fm; \(\Sigma^-\): \(0.78 \pm 0.10\) fm.
* Freeze-out temperature from ALICE yields with QM list and EV-HRG: \(T = 150.5 \pm 1.7\) MeV, \(\mu_B = 5.2 \pm 6.8\) MeV, volume \(V = 6589.4 \pm 601.6\) fm\(^3\).
* Freeze-out temperature from STAR yields with QM list and EV-HRG: \(T = 155.6 \pm 1.7\) MeV, \(\mu_B = 30.7 \pm 8.6\) MeV, volume \(V = 2878.2 \pm 243.5\) fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the excluded-volume HRG approach previously applied to pure gauge SU(2) and SU(3) theories by Alba (2016) to full QCD with multiple flavors, and contrasts with the van der Waals HRG model of Vovchenko (2016, 2017) which treats mesons as point-like and uses a universal attraction term. The finding that strange hadrons have smaller effective radii than light hadrons is consistent with experimental charge radii and with expectations from the Quark Model. The results enable future tests against lattice data for charm degrees of freedom and against S-matrix approaches (Huovinen 2017, Lo 2017), and suggest that the non-monotonic behavior of higher-order baryon fluctuations on the lattice arises from repulsive interactions rather than criticality.
