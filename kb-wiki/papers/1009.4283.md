# Charge conservation in RHIC and contributiuons to local parity violation observables

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions provide laboratory environments from which one can study the creation of a novel state of matter, the quark gluon plasma. The existence of such a state is postulated to alter the mechanism and evolution of charge production, which then becomes manifest in charge correlations. We study the separation of balancing charges at kinetic freeze-out by analyzing recent result on balancing charge correlations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 {GeV}$. We find that balancing charges are emitted from significantly smaller regions in central collisions compared to peripheral collisions. The results indicate that charge diffusion is small and that the centrality dependence points to a change of the production mechanism. In addition we calculate the contributions from charge-balance correlations to STAR's local parity violation observable. We find that local charge conservation, when combined with elliptic flow, explains much of STAR's measurement.

## Key claim

Local charge conservation combined with elliptic flow explains much of STAR's local parity violation observable, but the authors find that the observed narrowing of charge balance functions for more central collisions cannot be explained by changes in temperature and flow alone and requires a smaller spatial separation of balancing charges at freeze-out.

## Method

The authors modified STAR's single-particle blast-wave model to incorporate local charge conservation by generating ensembles of particles with exactly conserved charges, where particles within an ensemble are assigned collective velocities following the blast-wave parameterization with the additional constraint of being emitted within a finite range of longitudinal rapidity (sigma_eta) and azimuthal angle (sigma_phi). They then extracted the separation of balancing charges by adjusting sigma_eta and sigma_phi to reproduce STAR's measured charge balance functions in relative pseudorapidity Delta_eta and relative azimuthal angle Delta_phi for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV across different centrality bins. Additionally, they calculated contributions from charge-balance correlations to STAR's local parity violation observable gamma_P using the same model.

## Key numbers

* For central collisions, the balancing charge separation in longitudinal rapidity sigma_eta is smallest and increases for higher impact parameters; the separation in azimuthal angle sigma_phi shows a similar qualitative behavior.
* The systematic uncertainties on the inferred sigma_eta and sigma_phi values are estimated to be on the order of 10-20 percent.
* For the 50-60 percent centrality bin, including final-state interaction corrections lowers the extracted sigma_phi by about ten percent.
* The analysis used acceptance cuts of 0.2 GeV/c < p_t < 2.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 1.0, matching the STAR measurement.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charge balance functions by Bass (2000) and Cheng (2004) and uses the most recent STAR measurements of charge balance functions from 2010. The authors distinguish between effects from changes in freeze-out temperature and collective flow versus effects from the spatial separation of balancing charges, which previous analyses could not separate. The results enable future experimental studies of more differential observables such as two-dimensional charge balance functions in phi and Delta_phi to further constrain the production mechanism of charge in the quark-gluon plasma.
