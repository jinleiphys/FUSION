# Universal parameters of the $Λ(1380)$, the $Λ(1405)$ and their isospin partners from a combined analysis of Lattice QCD and experimental results

> We perform a global analysis of lattice and experimental data on negative-strangeness meson-baryon scattering using a large set of variations of the theoretical framework based on the Chiral Unitary Approach. For the former, the Lüscher formalism is utilized taking into account all pertinent coupled-channel effects. Through this, systematic uncertainties related to data scarcity, potential ambiguities, and possible framework dependence are quantified for the first time. The implementation of information criteria and other statistical tools is discussed. As a final result we provide pole positions for isoscalar resonances at the physical and lattice points including statistical and systematic uncertainties. Predictions for the isovector states are also provided showing large uncertainties.

## Key claim
The two-pole structure of the isoscalar Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1380) resonances is confirmed by combined fits to lattice QCD and experimental data, but the systematic uncertainties from model variations and inconsistent older cross-section data are quantified for the first time, showing that the lattice input alone is insufficient to determine both pole positions accurately when model variations are considered.

## Method
The authors performed a global analysis using the Chiral Unitary Approach (UCHPT) with multiple model variations (different interaction kernels M1-M3 and regularization schemes S1-S3) to fit both finite-volume energy spectra from lattice QCD (using the Luscher formalism with coupled-channel effects) and experimental data including total cross sections, threshold ratios, and kaonic hydrogen shift from SIDDHARTA. The lattice energy levels were obtained from the CLS D200 ensemble with a pion mass of 0.2036 GeV, and the scattering amplitude was parameterized via a real symmetric K-matrix projected onto S-wave channels with isospin zero. Information criteria were used to assess model variations and quantify systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift: Delta E = 283 +/- 42 eV, Gamma/2 = 271 +/- 55 eV
- AMADEUS result: |f_{0+}^{pi- Lambda -> K- n}(sqrt(s)=1.4 GeV)| = 0.334 +/- 0.018 fm
- Threshold ratios: gamma = 2.38 +/- 0.04, R_c = 0.664 +/- 0.011, R_n = 0.189 +/- 0.015
- For model M3S1 at the physical point: Lambda(1380) pole at 1.342(+0.009/-0.010) - i 0.028(+0.013/-0.014) GeV; Lambda(1405) pole at 1.432(+0.002/-0.002) - i 0.025(+0.001/-0.001) GeV
- For model M3S1 at the lattice point: Lambda(1380) pole at 1.359(+0.010/-0.014) - i 0.100(+0.050/-0.050) GeV; Lambda(1405) pole at 1.460(+0.006/-0.005) - i 0.013(+0.005/-0.004) GeV
- For model M3S2 at the physical point: Lambda(1380) pole at 1.373(+0.002/-0.005) - i 0.082(+0.011/-0.009) GeV; Lambda(1405) pole at 1.423(+0.002/-0.001) - i 0.020(+0.001/-0.002) GeV

- 252 total cross section data points used from six channels with kaon momentum P_LAB <= 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends the two-pole interpretation of the Lambda(1405) originally proposed by Oller and Meissner (2000) and reviewed by Mai (2020), by combining for the first time lattice QCD results from the BaSc collaboration (2023) with experimental data in a global fit that quantifies systematic uncertainties from model dependence. The analysis uses the Chiral Unitary Approach framework similar to that of Kaiser (1996), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), and Ikeda (2012), but introduces multiple model variations to assess framework dependence. The results enable more reliable chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results to the physical point and provide predictions for isovector partner states, though with large uncertainties that highlight the need for improved experimental data, particularly in channels like K- p -> anti-K0 n where systematic discrepancies between experiments are identified.
