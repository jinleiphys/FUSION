# Thermal axion production in the primordial quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate the rate for thermal production of axions via scattering of quarks and gluons in the primordial quark-gluon plasma. To obtain a finite result in a gauge-invariant way that is consistent to leading order in the strong gauge coupling, we use systematic field theoretical methods such as hard thermal loop resummation and the Braaten-Yuan prescription. The thermally produced yield, the decoupling temperature, and the density parameter are computed for axions with a mass below 10 meV. In this regime, with a Peccei-Quinn scale above 6x10^8 GeV, the associated axion population can still be relativistic today and can coexist with the axion cold dark matter condensate.

## Key claim

For axion masses below 10 meV (Peccei-Quinn scale above 6 × 10^8 GeV), the thermal production rate from quark and gluon scattering in the primordial quark-gluon plasma is calculated to leading order in the strong gauge coupling using hard thermal loop resummation and the Braaten-Yuan prescription, yielding a finite gauge-invariant result that is most reliable for reheating temperatures much greater than 10^4 GeV where the coupling is small.

## Method

The authors computed the thermal production rate of axions with energies E ≳ T from 2 → 2 scattering processes (gluon-gluon, quark-antiquark, and quark-gluon) in the quark-gluon plasma using the model-independent axion-gluon interaction Lagrangian. They employed hard thermal loop resummation and the Braaten-Yuan prescription to separate soft gluon contributions (momentum transfer of order g_s T) from hard gluon contributions (momentum transfer of order T), summing both to obtain a cutoff-independent leading-order rate. The Boltzmann equation was then integrated from the reheating temperature T_R down to the temperature of radiation-matter equality to obtain the relic axion yield and density parameter.

## Key numbers

- Axion mass range considered: below 10 meV, corresponding to Peccei-Quinn scale f_PQ ≳ 6 × 10^8 GeV
- Integrated thermal production rate (collision term): W_a = [ζ(3) g_s^6 T^6] / [64π^7 f_PQ^2] × [ln(T^2/m_g^2) + 0.406]
- Relic axion yield today: Y_a^TP ≈ 18.6 g_s^6 ln(1.501/g_s) × (10^10 GeV / f_PQ)^2 × (T_R / 10^10 GeV)
- Axion decoupling temperature fit: T_D ≈ 9.6 × 10^6 GeV × (f_PQ / 10^10 GeV)^2.246
- Present axion temperature: T_a,0 = 0.332 T_0 ≈ 0.08 meV, where T_0 ≈ 0.235 meV is the present CMB temperature
- Axion mass relation: m_a ≈ 0.6 meV × (10^10 GeV / f_PQ)
- Present average momentum of thermal axions: ⟨p_a,0⟩ = 2.701 T_a,0
- Thermal relic axion yield (equilibrium): Y_a^eq ≈ 2.6 × 10^{-3}
- QCD Debye mass: m_D = √3 m_g, with m_g = g_s T √(N_c + n_f/2)/3 for N_c = 3 colors and n_f = 6 flavors

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axion decoupling from thermal equilibrium in the quark-gluon plasma by Masso (2002) and Sikivie (2006) by treating the case where axions were never in thermal equilibrium, using systematic gauge-invariant methods (hard thermal loop resummation from Braaten (1989) and the Braaten-Yuan prescription (1991)) rather than the simpler Debye mass screening approach. The calculation enables the first determination of the thermal axion abundance for cosmological scenarios with reheating temperatures below the decoupling temperature, and shows that the thermally produced axion population can coexist with the axion cold dark matter condensate from the misalignment mechanism (Sikivie 2006, Kim 2008), producing a Lee-Weinberg-type curve for the total axion density parameter.
