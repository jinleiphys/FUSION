# Novel scheme for parametrizing the chemical freeze-out surface in Heavy Ion Collision Experiments

> We introduce a new prescription for obtaining the chemical freeze-out parameters in the heavy-ion collision experiments using the Hadron Resonance Gas model. The scheme is found to reliably estimate the freeze-out parameters and predict the hadron yield ratios, which themselves were never used in the parametrization procedure.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new fitting procedure for extracting chemical freeze-out parameters from experimental hadron yields using the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, which replaces the conventional chi-squared minimization with four independent equations based on conserved and total charge ratios. The scheme is claimed to reliably predict hadron yield ratios that were never used in the parametrization procedure, though the authors note that for lower AGS energies they had to substitute one ratio (Lambda/proton) into the equations.

## Method

The authors solve four coupled equations numerically using Broyden's method: two equations fix the electric charge chemical potential and strangeness chemical potential via net-charge-to-net-baryon ratio and net-strangeness-to-total-strangeness constraints, while two new equations use the net baryon number normalized to total baryon number and net baryon number normalized to total hadron yield. The HRG model includes all hadrons up to 2 GeV, and the analysis uses mid-rapidity data for most central collisions from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments, with identified hadrons being pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, Lambdas, anti-Lambdas, Xis, and anti-Xis.

## Key numbers

* Freeze-out temperature increases with center-of-mass energy and approaches saturation, except at LHC energy where the temperature is lower due to lower proton yield
* Baryon chemical potential decreases with increasing center-of-mass energy, consistent with baryon stopping at low energies and baryon transparency at high energies
* Electric charge chemical potential remains negative throughout all energies, approaching zero for increasing center-of-mass energy
* The various charge ratios agree between model and data to an accuracy of 10^{-6} or better
* The pion ratio pi-/pi+ is greater than 1 for low center-of-mass energies and approaches 1 at higher collision energies

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of using the HRG model to characterize the chemical freeze-out surface in heavy-ion collisions, following the pioneering work of Cleymans and Redlich (1998) who argued for a universal freeze-out condition of 1 GeV average energy per hadron. The new approach contrasts with conventional chi-squared minimization methods used by Andronic et al. (2005), Becattini et al. (2005), and the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk et al., 2017), which the authors argue may bias results due to the choice of multiplicity ratios. The formalism enables a physically consistent alternative for analyzing data from upcoming heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as GSI and JINR.
