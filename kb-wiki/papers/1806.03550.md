# Modeling of two-particle femtoscopic correlations at top RHIC energy

> The spatial and temporal characteristics of particle emitting source produced in particle and/or nuclear collisions can be measured by using two-particle femtoscopic correlations. These correlations arise due to quantum statistics, Coulomb and strong final state interactions. In this paper we report on the calculations of like-sign pion femtoscopic correlations produced in p+p, p+Au, d+Au, Au+Au at top RHIC energy using Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model (UrQMD). Three-dimensional correlation functions are constructed using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization of the two-particle relative momentum. The correlation functions are studied in several transverse mass ranges. The emitting source radii of charged pions, Rout , Rside , Rlong , are obtained from Gaussian fit to the correlation functions and compared to data from the STAR and PHENIX experiments.

# Modeling of two-particle femtoscopic correlations at top RHIC energy

## Key claim
The UrQMD model reproduces the transverse-mass dependence of the femtoscopic radii R_side and R_long for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV in both qualitative and quantitative agreement with STAR and PHENIX data, but the model overestimates R_out, indicating that the emission duration of charged pions is not well reproduced.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate p+p, p+Au, d+Au, and central (0-10% centrality) Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. Since UrQMD does not contain quantum statistics correlations, the simulated phase-space points of charged pions at freeze-out were passed to a Correlation After Burner (CRAB) program. Three-dimensional correlation functions were constructed using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization in the longitudinally co-moving system, and Gaussian fits extracted the HBT radii R_out, R_side, and R_long in several transverse mass ranges.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √s_NN = 200 GeV for all systems (p+p, p+Au, d+Au, Au+Au)
- Centrality selection for p+Au, d+Au, Au+Au: 0-10% centrality
- Transverse mass range for correlation function projections shown: m_T = 0.2-0.4 GeV/c²
- Pseudorapidity cut applied: |η| < 1
- Ordering of source radii observed across all transverse mass ranges: R_i(Au+Au) > R_i(d+Au) > R_i(p+Au) ≥ R_i(p+p)

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic studies at top RHIC energy by comparing UrQMD model calculations to experimental data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The finding that R_out is overestimated by the model while R_side and R_long agree with data suggests that the UrQMD model correctly reproduces the geometrical size of the emitting region and system lifetime but overestimates the particle emission duration. The results provide estimates for future RHIC measurements and guidance for correcting transport properties in the UrQMD model.
