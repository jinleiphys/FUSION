# Revisiting the first-order QCD phase transition in dense strong interaction matter

> We revisit the phase structure and thermodynamics of QCD in the low temperature and high density region, where a strong, first-order phase transition is expected beyond the critical end point. By solving the quark gap equation in the continuum QCD approach, we reveal the coexistence of the multi-phases both in the microscopic dynamics of chiral symmetry breaking and also in the thermodynamic observables, which manifests the existence of spinodal decomposition during the first-order QCD phase transitions. We also analyse the interface structure of the co-exist Nambu and Wigner phases in the isothermal process during the first-order transition. In particular, the interface tension and interface entropy density are extracted from the isothermal trajectories, which further allows for an analysis on the formation of nuclear bubble, including the bubble radius and its stability at different temperatures. Our predictions may serve as useful inputs for further investigations in heavy-ion physics or astrophysics research.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spinodal decomposition, a multi-phase coexistence region with an intermediate solution branch between the Nambu (chiral symmetry broken) and Wigner (chiral symmetric) phases, is a genuine feature of first-order QCD phase transitions in the low-temperature, high-density region, going beyond mean-field approximations by using the continuum Dyson-Schwinger equations approach.

## Method

The authors solved the quark gap equation within the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) approach for Nf=2+1 flavor QCD, using a truncation scheme that satisfies Slavnov-Taylor identities and has been benchmarked against lattice QCD results at small baryon chemical potential. An improved iteration procedure inspired by the homotopy method was employed to find an intermediate solution branch for the quark propagator between the Nambu and Wigner phases. The gluonic background field was also computed to evaluate Polyakov loops, and a phenomenological Walecka model was incorporated via an excluded volume mechanism to account for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The light quark mass function at momentum (pi T, spatial 0) shows three distinct branches (Nambu, Intermediate, Wigner) at T=40 and 60 MeV, with the ordering Re M_l^N > Re M_l^I > Re M_l^W > 0
- The reduced chiral condensate Delta_{l,s} normalized to its vacuum value shows three branches at T=40 and 60 MeV as a function of baryon chemical potential
- The net-baryon number density was computed for temperatures from T=30 to 100 MeV in 10 MeV steps, showing multi-phase structure during the first-order transition
- The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition critical chemical potential is mu_B = 923 MeV at zero temperature, with nucleon saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} and binding energy 15.8 MeV
- The Walecka model parameters used: M_N = 938 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, g_sigma = 16.3 m_sigma / M_N, g_omega = 14.0 m_omega / m_N

## Context

This work extends previous functional QCD studies of the phase diagram by Gao (2020), Gunkel (2021), and Fu (2019) that predicted a first-order phase transition region, and confirms the spinodal decomposition picture suggested by effective model studies such as Xin (2014) and Jiang (2013) as well as a recent homotopy method study by Zheng (2023). The results provide inputs for heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities like HIAF, FAIR, and NICA, and for astrophysical studies of compact stars and primordial gravitational wave signatures.
