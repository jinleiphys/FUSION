# On the non-perturbative gluon mass and heavy quark physics

> We study a recently proposed gauge invariant, non-local pure gauge observable, which is equivalent to the gluon propagator in a certain gauge. The correlator describes a gluon coupled to static sources and decays with eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian, permitting a non-perturbative definition of a gluonic parton mass. Detailed numerical tests of the observable are performed in SU(2) gauge theories in 2+1 dimensions. In a Higgs model it reproduces the physical W-boson mass, while in a confining theory its non-local nature results in almost exclusive projection onto torelonic states. However, the gluon mass can also be related to the mass difference between the lowest gluelumps, i.e. gluon configurations bound to adjoint sources. Its value is computed in SU(2) pure gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions to be m_A=0.360(19)g^2, which plays an important role as "magnetic mass" of the four-dimensional theory at high temperatures. The same quantity is found to determine the mass splitting between vector and scalar mesons in the three-dimensional theory. In SU(3) pure gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions the corresponding gluelump splitting yields m_A\approx 370 MeV. Possible relations of this quantity with the QCD heavy meson spectrum are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper defines a non-perturbative gluon mass via the energy gap between the vacuum and the lowest excited eigenvalue of the Hamiltonian in the presence of static adjoint sources, but this mass is not related to an asymptotic particle state; rather it is a short-distance property of the static potential with the sources and flux in an adjoint state.

## Method

The author constructs a gauge-invariant, non-local Euclidean gluon correlator using eigenvectors of the spatial covariant Laplacian to define composite link and gluon fields, then relates this correlator to the gluelump spectrum (gluonic configurations bound to adjoint sources) via the transfer matrix formalism. Numerical lattice simulations are performed for SU(2) gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions, and the gluon mass is extracted from the mass difference between the lowest gluelump states without requiring gauge fixing or non-local fields.

## Key numbers

* In SU(2) pure gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions, the gluon mass is m_A = 0.360(19) g^2, where g^2 has dimension of mass and provides the scale.
* In SU(3) pure gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions, the corresponding gluelump splitting yields m_A ≈ 370 MeV, with a more precise literature value of 368(7) MeV from Glueball Masses (1999).
* In the SU(2) Higgs model in 2+1 dimensions, the gauge field propagator mass m_A equals the physical W-boson mass M_V within statistical errors at the tested point (x=0.0239, y=-0.02).

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative argument for a dynamical gluon mass by Bernard (1982) based on the adjoint static potential, and provides a concrete lattice implementation of the gauge-invariant gluon correlator proposed by Philipsen (2001). The computed gluon mass in SU(2) in 2+1 dimensions serves as the "magnetic mass" for the four-dimensional theory at high temperatures via dimensional reduction, and the same quantity determines the mass splitting between vector and scalar mesons in the three-dimensional theory. The results enable future studies of the relation between the gluon mass and the heavy meson spectrum in QCD, though the author notes that a careful study of the static limit is necessary to settle this question.
