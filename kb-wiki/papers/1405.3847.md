# Relativistic equation of state at subnuclear densities in the Thomas-Fermi approximation

> We study the non-uniform nuclear matter using the self-consistent Thomas--Fermi approximation with a relativistic mean-field model. The non-uniform matter is assumed to be composed of a lattice of heavy nuclei surrounded by dripped nucleons. At each temperature $T$, proton fraction $Y_p$, and baryon mass density $ρ_B$, we determine the thermodynamically favored state by minimizing the free energy with respect to the radius of the Wigner--Seitz cell, while the nucleon distribution in the cell can be determined self-consistently in the Thomas--Fermi approximation. A detailed comparison is made between the present results and previous calculations in the Thomas--Fermi approximation with a parameterized nucleon distribution that has been adopted in the widely used Shen EOS.

## Key claim

The self-consistent Thomas-Fermi (STF) approximation and the parameterized Thomas-Fermi (PTF) approximation produce very similar equations of state for non-uniform matter at subnuclear densities, with differences in free energy per baryon of about 0.2 MeV at low temperature (1 MeV) that are mainly attributable to the surface-energy parameter \(F_0\) used in PTF being too small (70 MeV fm⁵) compared to the self-consistent STF result.

## Method

The authors model non-uniform nuclear matter as a lattice of spherical heavy nuclei (droplet or bubble configuration) inside Wigner-Seitz cells, using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set. They solve the coupled mean-field equations self-consistently for the nucleon distributions at given temperature \(T\), proton fraction \(Y_p\), and baryon mass density \(\rho_B\), minimizing the free energy per baryon with respect to the cell radius \(R_C\). The results are compared to the earlier PTF approximation (Shen 2011), which used a parameterized nucleon distribution of the form \(n_i(r) = (n_i^{\text{in}} - n_i^{\text{out}})[1 - (r/R_i)^{t_i}]^3 + n_i^{\text{out}}\) and a gradient-energy term \(E_{\text{cell}}^g = \int F_0 |\nabla (n_n + n_p)|^2 d^3r\) with \(F_0 = 70\) MeV fm⁵.

## Key numbers

- Free energy per baryon difference between STF and PTF (\(F_0=70\)): 0.217 MeV at \(\rho_B = 10^{13.0}\) g cm⁻³, \(Y_p=0.3\), \(T=1\) MeV.
- Onset density of the bubble phase: above \(10^{13.9}\) g cm⁻³.
- Transition density where bubble phase delays uniform matter: \(\rho_B \sim 10^{14}\) g cm⁻³ (corresponding to \(n_B \sim 0.06\) fm⁻³).
- Gradient-energy parameter in PTF: \(F_0 = 70\) MeV fm⁵ (standard) and \(F_0 = 90\) MeV fm⁵ (tested for comparison).
- Maximum neutron-star mass predicted by TM1: \(2.18 M_\odot\) (from Shen 2011).

## Context

This work extends the Shen EOS (Shen 1998a, 1998b, 2011) by replacing the parameterized nucleon distribution with a self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculation, and by including the bubble phase in addition to the droplet phase. It directly compares to the PTF approximation used in the widely applied Shen EOS for supernova simulations. The results enable assessment of the accuracy of the simpler PTF method and show that the bubble phase can affect chemical potentials and delay the transition to uniform matter, though overall thermodynamic differences are small enough that the PTF approximation remains reasonable for core-collapse supernova simulations.
