# Generator Coordinate Method framework for Double Beta Decay

> We propose a consistent prescription for the derivation of the particle number and angular momentum projected QRPA (PQRPA) equation in the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) framework for calculation of NME's of double-beta decay of axially deformed nuclei. We derive closed formulae for the calculation of excitation energies and wave functions of the intermediate nucleus.

# Generator Coordinate Method framework for Double Beta Decay

## Key claim

The paper proposes a consistent prescription for deriving particle number and angular momentum projected QRPA (PQRPA) equations within the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) framework, specifically for calculating nuclear matrix elements of double-beta decay in axially deformed nuclei. The authors derive closed formulae for excitation energies and wave functions of the intermediate nucleus, but note that the approach relies on the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) and that the weight function in the GCM ansatz cannot be determined uniquely due to the overcomplete basis.

## Method

The authors start from a projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) ground state and construct charge-changing quasiparticle operators for the intermediate odd-odd nucleus. They apply the Generator Coordinate Method with particle number and angular momentum projection, using the Gaussian overlap approximation to transform the Hill-Wheeler integral equation into a differential form. The resulting PQRPA equation is derived by expanding the Hamiltonian kernel to second order in the generator coordinates, yielding matrices A and B that are computed using a recursive contraction formula analogous to Wick's theorem. The overcompleteness of the basis is handled by decomposing the norm overlap matrix M = WW† and retaining only non-zero eigenvalue components, which reduces the dimension of the PQRPA equation and removes spurious zero-energy modes.

## Key numbers

- The angular momentum projector for axially symmetric nuclei uses the factor (J + 1/2) multiplied by an integral over β from 0 to π, with sinβ dβ and the reduced Wigner d-function d^J_MK(β)
- The particle number projector for protons and neutrons involves double integrals over ζ and η from 0 to 2π, with factors e^(i(ζZ_0 + ηN_0)) and e^(i(ζΩ_Z + ηΩ_N)) respectively
- The recursive contraction method avoids explicit writing of (8-1)!! = 105 terms for the four-quasiparticle matrix elements
- The norm overlap expansion coefficients s_κ, M_κλ, and T_κλ are expressed as three-dimensional integrals over ω = {β, ζ, η} with integration measure ρ(ω) = sinβ d^J_MK(β) e^(i(ζZ_0 + ηN_0))

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the Generator Coordinate Method to double-beta decay, which had been used solely over PHFB calculations for configuration mixing effects (GCM applied to PHFB, prior work cited as bib8 and bib81). The approach differs from standard QRPA, the interacting shell model (cited as bib4, bib5), the interacting boson model (cited as bib6, bib7), and the projected Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov model (cited as bib9) by providing a consistent projection framework within the GCM. The derivation follows the procedure described by Federschmidt and Ring (cited as fed) for the PQRPA equations, with resolution hints from Jancovici and Schiff (cited as janc), and uses the contraction formalism of Hara and Iwasaki (cited as hara). The formalism enables future numerical calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in axially deformed nuclei, which would help extract neutrino properties from experimental data.
