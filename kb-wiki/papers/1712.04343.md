# A Subleading Power Operator Basis for the Scalar Quark Current

> Factorization theorems play a crucial role in our understanding of the strong interaction. For collider processes they are typically formulated at leading power and much less is known about power corrections in the $λ\ll 1$ expansion. Here we present a complete basis of power suppressed operators for a scalar quark current at $\mathcal{O}(λ^2)$ in the amplitude level power expansion in the Soft Collinear Effective Theory, demonstrating that helicity selection rules significantly simplify the construction. This basis applies for the production of any color singlet scalar in $q\bar{q}$ annihilation (such as $b \bar b \to H$). We also classify all operators which contribute to the cross section at $\mathcal{O}(λ^2)$ and perform matching calculations to determine their tree level Wilson coefficients. These results can be exploited to study power corrections in both resummed and fixed order perturbation theory, and for analyzing the factorization properties of gauge theory amplitudes and cross sections at subleading power.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete basis of power-suppressed hard-scattering operators at $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$ in the Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) power expansion for a scalar quark current, applicable to any color-singlet scalar produced in $q\bar{q}$ annihilation (such as $b\bar{b} \to H$). The construction is limited to the amplitude-level power expansion and applies specifically when the quarks are treated as massless for the purpose of organizing the operator basis.

## Method

The authors construct operators from collinear building blocks (quark fields $\chi_{n_i}$, gluon fields $\mathcal{B}_{n_i\perp}^\mu$, and perpendicular derivatives $\mathcal{P}_\perp^\mu$) using definite-helicity operators to simplify the basis. They classify all operators contributing at $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$ to both the amplitude and the cross section, and perform tree-level matching calculations to determine the Wilson coefficients for the process $q\bar{q} \to h$.

## Key numbers

* The power expansion parameter $\lambda \ll 1$ is used, with collinear momentum components scaling as $(n_i \cdot p, \bar{n}_i \cdot p, p_{n_i\perp}) \sim \bar{n}_i \cdot p \, (\lambda^2, 1, \lambda)$.
* The Higgs vacuum expectation value is $v = (\sqrt{2} G_F)^{-1/2} = 246$ GeV.
* The operator basis is constructed at $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$ in the amplitude-level power expansion, with hard scattering operators classified at $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^1)$ and $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$.
* The tree-level matching calculations determine Wilson coefficients for operators at $\mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$ for the scalar quark current.

## Context

This work extends the subleading-power operator basis approach previously applied to vector quark currents by Feige, Kolodrubetz, Moult, and Stewart (2017) and to color-singlet scalar production in gluon fusion by Moult, Stewart, and Vita (2017). The results provide the groundwork for deriving subleading-power factorization theorems for $q\bar{q} \to H$ processes, enabling systematic studies of power corrections in both resummed and fixed-order perturbation theory, and can be used to improve numerical accuracy in $N$-jettiness subtraction schemes for NNLO calculations.
