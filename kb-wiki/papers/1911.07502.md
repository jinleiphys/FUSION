# Vector-boson condensates, spin-triplet superfluidity of paired neutral and charged fermions, and $3P_2$ pairing of nucleons

> After reminding of properties of the condensate of the complex scalar field in the external uniform magnetic field $H$ focus is made on the study of phases of the complex neutral vector boson fields coupled with magnetic field by the Zeeman coupling and phases of the charged vector boson fields. These systems may behave as nonmagnetic and ferromagnetic superfluids and ordinary and ferromagnetic superconductors. Response of the ferromagnetic superfluid and superconducting systems occupying half of space on the external uniform static magnetic field $H$ is thoroughly studied. Then the spin-triplet pairing of neutral fermions at conserved spin is considered. Novel phases are found. In external magnetic field the phase with zero mean spin proves to be unstable to the formation of a phase with a non-zero spin. For $H>H_{\rm cr 2}$ the spin-triplet pairing and ferromagnetic superfluidity continue to exist above the "old" phase transition critical temperature $T_{\rm cr}$. For a certain parameter choice ferromagnetic superfluid phases are formed already for $H=0$, characterized by an own magnetic field $h$. Formation of domains is discussed. Next, spin-triplet pairing of charged fermions is studied. Novel phases are found with a ferromagnetic superconductivity. Then, the 3P$_2$ $nn$ pairing in neutron star matter is studied. Also a 3P$_2$ $pp$ pairing is considered. Numerical estimates are performed in the BCS weak coupling limit and beyond it for the $3P_2$ $nn$ and $pp$ pairings, as well as for the 3S$_1$ $np$ pairing.

## Key claim

For spin-triplet pairing of neutral fermions in an external magnetic field, the phase with zero mean spin is unstable to the formation of a phase with non-zero spin; for fields above a critical value \(H > H_{\rm cr 2}\), spin-triplet pairing and ferromagnetic superfluidity persist above the "old" phase transition critical temperature \(T_{\rm cr}\), and for certain parameter choices ferromagnetic superfluid phases form even at \(H = 0\) with an own magnetic field \(h\).

## Method

The author constructs Ginzburg-Landau free-energy functionals for complex neutral and charged vector boson fields and for spin-triplet Cooper pairs of neutral and charged fermions, incorporating Zeeman coupling, minimal coupling to the electromagnetic field, and self-interaction terms. The response of these systems to an external uniform static magnetic field is analyzed, including half-space boundary conditions, and the 3P\(_2\) pairing of neutrons and protons in neutron star matter is treated within the BCS weak coupling approximation and beyond it.

## Key numbers

- For the 3P\(_2\) \(nn\) pairing gap in neutron matter, values range from tiny values \(\lesssim 10\) keV up to values of the order of several MeV, with uncertainties due to three-body forces.
- The 3S\(_1\) \(np\) pairing gap in symmetric matter is estimated as large as \(\simeq 12\) MeV in BCS calculations, and with depletion of the Fermi surface taken into account, the maximum gap is \(\simeq 4\) MeV.
- The critical field for the second-order phase transition in the charged scalar field model is \(H_{\rm cr 2} = |m_{\rm ef}^2/e| = H_{\rm cr}\sqrt{2}\kappa\), where \(\kappa = \sqrt{l_h/l_\phi}\) is the Ginzburg-Landau parameter.
- The thermodynamical critical field for the charged scalar field is \(H_{\rm cr} = \sqrt{4\pi}|m_{\rm ef}^2|/\sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The magnetic field at the surface of ordinary pulsars reaches \(\sim (10^{12} - 10^{13})\) Gs, at magnetar surfaces \(\gtrsim 10^{15}\) Gs, and in neutron star interiors possibly up to \(\sim 10^{18}\) Gs.
- Magnetic fields in non-central heavy-ion collisions may reach \(\sim (10^{17} - 10^{19})\) Gs from GSI to LHC energies.
- The rotation frequency of millisecond pulsars can be as high as \(\sim 10^4\) Hz, and the rotation frequency of the fireball in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at freezeout is estimated as \(\sim 10^{22}\) Hz.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-triplet pairing and vector-boson condensates to include ferromagnetic superfluidity and superconductivity, building on prior treatments of 3P\(_2\) pairing in neutron stars by Tamagaki (1970), Takatsuka (1971), and Sauls and Serene (1978), and on the Ginzburg-Landau expansion up to 8th order by Yasui (2019). It also connects to studies of charged \(\rho\) meson condensation in dense isospin-asymmetric matter by Voskresensky (1997) and Kolomeitsev (2004), and to the instability of the \(W\) boson vacuum in strong magnetic fields by Chernodub (2010). The results enable predictions for the magnetic and rotational responses of neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision systems, and open the question of whether ferromagnetic superfluid phases can be realized in nuclear matter under extreme conditions.
