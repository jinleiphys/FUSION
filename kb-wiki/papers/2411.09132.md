# Bottomonium Properties in QGP from a Lattice-QCD Informed T-Matrix Approach

> Recent lattice quantum chromodynamics (lQCD) computations of bottomonium correlation functions with extended sources provide new insights into heavy-quark dynamics at distance scales which are of the order of the inverse temperature. We analyze these results employing the thermodynamic T-matrix approach, in a continued effort to interpret lQCD data for quarkonium correlation functions in a non-perturbative framework suitable for strongly coupled systems. Its key inputs are the in-medium driving kernel (potential) of the scattering equation and an interference function which implements 3-body effects in the quarkonium coupling to the thermal medium. A simultaneous description of lQCD results for the bottomonium correlators with extended operators and the previously analyzed Wilson line correlators only requires minor refinements of the potential but calls for stronger interference effects at larger separation of the bottom quark and antiquark. We then analyze the poles of the self-consistent T-matrices on the real axis to assess the survival of the various bound states. We estimate the pertinent temperatures where the poles disappear for the various bottomonium states and discuss the relation to the corresponding peaks in the bottomonium spectral functions. We also recalculate the spatial diffusion coefficient of the QGP and find it to be similar to that in our previous study.

# Bottomonium Properties in QGP from a Lattice-QCD Informed T-Matrix Approach

## Key claim
The thermodynamic T-matrix approach can simultaneously describe lattice QCD results for bottomonium correlators with extended operators and previously analyzed Wilson line correlators, but this requires only minor refinements of the in-medium potential while calling for stronger interference effects at larger quark-antiquark separation. The poles of the self-consistent T-matrices on the real axis are used to estimate the temperatures where bound states disappear, though the relation between pole disappearance and peaks in spectral functions is not straightforward.

## Method
The authors employ the thermodynamic T-matrix approach, which resums an infinite series of ladder diagrams to evaluate in-medium one- and two-body correlation functions in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma. The key inputs are an in-medium driving kernel (potential) for the scattering equation and an interference function that implements three-body effects in the quarkonium coupling to the thermal medium. The method involves reducing the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation to a one-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation through partial-wave expansion, solving it by discretizing three-momenta followed by matrix inversion, and iterating self-consistently with the light-parton sector to maintain consistency with the lattice QCD equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum bottomonium masses from the T-matrix are within 20 MeV of experimental values for almost all states below the open-bottom threshold, with the exception of the Upsilon(2S); for example, the Upsilon(1S) mass is calculated as 9.460 GeV compared to the experimental 9.460 GeV, and the eta_b(1S) mass is 9.417 GeV compared to 9.399 GeV experimentally.
- The vacuum potential parameters were adjusted to alpha_s = 0.31 and string tension sigma = 0.25 GeV^2, compared to previous values of 0.27 and 0.225 GeV^2 respectively.
- The mixing coefficient for scalar-vector confining potential is set to chi = 0.8, with a 20 percent admixture of Lorentz-vector structure.
- The bare bottom-quark mass is M_b^0 = 4.676 GeV and the constituent bottom-quark mass is M_b = 5.316 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous T-matrix studies by Liu et al. (2017) and Zhanduo Tang et al. (2023) that constrained the in-medium potential using lattice QCD data for heavy-quark free energy, quarkonium correlators, the equation of state, and static Wilson line correlators. The new analysis incorporates recent lattice QCD results from Larsen et al. (2019) for bottomonium correlators with extended operators, which have improved overlap with specific bottomonium states and greater sensitivity to in-medium effects at the scale of the temperature compared to point meson operators. The results enable more precise determinations of bottomonium spectral functions that can be used in transport simulations for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the spatial diffusion coefficient of the quark-gluon plasma is recalculated and found similar to the previous study.
