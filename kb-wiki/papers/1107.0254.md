# Bound state nature of the exotic Z_b states

> The assumption that the newly observed charged bottomonia states Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) are of molecular nature is confronted with the measured invariant mass distributions for the transitions of the Upsilon(5S) to the final states h_b pi^+ pi^- and h_b(2P) pi^+ pi^-. It is shown that the assumption that the Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) are B\bar B^*+{\rm c.c.} and B^*\bar B^* bound states, respectively, with very small binding energies is consistent with the data. The calculation is based on a power counting for bottom meson loops, which is explicitly given up to two-loop in the framework of a nonrelativistic effective field theory. We also show that if the Z_b states are of molecular nature, then the data should not be analyzed by using a Breit-Wigner parametrization.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the experimental data for the decays $\Upsilon(5S) \to h_b \pi^+ \pi^-$ and $\Upsilon(5S) \to h_b(2P) \pi^+ \pi^-$ are consistent with the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ being $S$-wave bound states of $B\bar B^*$+c.c. and $B^*\bar B^*$, respectively, with binding energies below the corresponding thresholds, contrary to the experimental analysis which reported masses slightly above threshold. The authors also show that if the $Z_b$ states are of molecular nature, a Breit-Wigner parametrization should not be used to analyze the data.

## Method
The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory for bottom meson loops, with a power counting scheme up to two-loop order. They compute the $Z_b$ propagator dressed by self-energy loops of $B\bar B^*$ or $B^*\bar B^*$, and fit the resulting invariant mass distributions for $h_b\pi^+$ and $h_b(2P)\pi^+$ to the Belle data, using the relation between the $Z_b$ coupling to bottom mesons and the binding energy for an $S$-wave loosely bound state.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit binding energies: $\Eng_{Z_b} = -4.7^{+2.2}_{-2.3}$ MeV and $\Eng_{Z_b'} = -0.11^{+0.06}_{-0.14}$ MeV, where negative values indicate bound states below the $B\bar B^*$ and $B^*\bar B^*$ thresholds, respectively.
- Best-fit coupling constants: $z_1 = 0.75^{+0.08}_{-0.11}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ for $Z_b$, $r_z = z_2/z_1 = -0.39^{+0.06}_{-0.07}$, and $g_1 z_1 = 0.40 \pm 0.06$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- Fit quality: $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 54.1/22 = 2.45$ for 27 data points (14 for $h_b\pi^+\pi^-$ and 13 for $h_b(2P)\pi^+\pi^-$).
- Predicted decay widths: $\Gamma(Z_b \to h_b\pi) = 140 (g_1 z_1 \text{ GeV})^2$ MeV and $\Gamma(Z_b' \to h_b\pi) = 169 (g_1 z_2 \text{ GeV})^2$ MeV; for $h_b(2P)\pi$: $\Gamma(Z_b \to h_b(2P)\pi) = 30 (g_1' z_1 \text{ GeV})^2$ MeV and $\Gamma(Z_b' \to h_b(2P)\pi) = 46 (g_1' z_2 \text{ GeV})^2$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation proposed by Bondar et al. (2011) for the $Z_b$ states, and confronts it with the Belle data (Adachi, 2011). It builds on earlier studies of near-threshold states such as the $X(3872)$ and on the power-counting formalism for heavy meson loops developed by Guo et al. (2010). The results enable future analyses that include non-resonant terms and other decay channels, and they caution against using Breit-Wigner parametrizations for dynamically generated states near threshold.
