# Probing chiral dynamics by charged-pion correlations

> The environment generated in the mid-rapidity region of a high-energy nuclear collision endows the pionic degrees of freedom with a time-dependent effective mass. Its specific evolution provides a mechanism for the production of back-to-back charge-conjugate pairs of soft pions which may present an observable signal of the non-equilibrium dynamics of the chiral order parameter.

## Key claim

The non-equilibrium evolution of the chiral order parameter in high-energy nuclear collisions endows pions with a time-dependent effective mass, which provides a mechanism for producing back-to-back charge-conjugate pairs of soft pions; this correlation signal may be observable and could serve as a probe of chiral dynamics, though the signal is partially obscured by background processes such as rho meson decays.

## Method

The author uses a quantum-field treatment within the effective mass approximation, where the time-dependent effective pion mass is derived from the linear sigma model. The Bogoliubov coefficients describing the mixing of forward-going positive pions with backward-going negative pions are computed from the mode functions determined by the equation of motion for the pion field. The analysis is first performed for a spatially uniform environment and then extended to non-uniform scenarios using a one-dimensional model with space- and time-dependent effective mass emulating the expansion of a system with initial temperature T0=240 MeV and initial radius R0=5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The amplification mechanism is effective for pion momenta below approximately 300 MeV/c, with the peak structure in the final occupancy occurring near k ≈ 260 MeV for a sigma mass mσ = 600 MeV.
- The initial effective mass is set to μi = 2m (where m is the free pion mass), with a decay time t0 = 5 fm/c and oscillation frequency ωosc = mσ.
- Three initial scenarios are considered with temperatures T0 = 220, 240, 260 MeV, corresponding to initial chiral order parameter values σ0 = 19, 26, 42 MeV respectively.
- The coarse-grained correlation coefficient saturates once the momentum cut-off k0 is above approximately 300 MeV/c.
- The amplification mechanism takes several to many fm/c to complete (approximately 5 fm/c).
- Rho meson decays produce pions with momenta of about 360 MeV/c, which is above the expected effect's upper limit of kmax ≈ 300 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical linear sigma model simulations by Randrup (2000) that identified transverse spectral enhancement and soft-pion multiplicity fluctuations as candidate DCC observables, and it builds on the quantum-field treatment developed by Randrup for non-uniform scenarios. The paper contrasts with the null results from experimental searches for neutral pion fraction broadening by MiniMax and WA98, and notes that the proposed correlation signal is absent in conventional event generators RQMD and HIJING as shown by Bleicher. The signal is expected to be relatively undisturbed during propagation due to the "Baked Alaska" scenario described by Bjorken, and the analysis suggests that rho meson decays do not significantly obscure the signal in the soft-pion domain.
