# Consistency of the effective-field-theory approach to the nucleon-nucleon interaction problem revisited

> It is argued that Weinberg's approach to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction problem within effective field theory provides a consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams. Within this scheme the NN potential is organized as an expansion in terms of small quantities like small external momenta and the pion mass (divided by the characteristic large scale of the effective theory). Physical observables to any given order in these small quantities are calculated from the solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger (or Schr\" odinger) equation.

## Key claim

Weinberg's approach to the nucleon-nucleon interaction problem within effective field theory provides a consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams, provided that the power counting is applied to renormalized diagrams as opposed to unrenormalized diagrams and counterterm contributions separately. The authors argue that the renormalization-group behavior of coupling constants does not lead to unnaturally large couplings in Weinberg's scheme, contrary to claims in the literature.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a nonrenormalizable NN potential derived from chiral effective field theory. They examine the structure of the potential as an expansion in small external momenta and the pion mass, and consider the cancellation of divergences generated by iterating the potential through counterterms. They estimate the running of renormalized couplings by calculating divergent parts of loop diagrams in dimensional regularization for the leading-order potential in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channels.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order potential includes the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.267\), the pion decay constant \(F = 92.4\) MeV, and the charged-pion mass \(M_\pi = 139.6\) MeV.
* For the \(^1S_0\) channel, the divergent part of the two-loop contribution proportional to \(C^2 g_A^2/(4F^2)\) is \(-\frac{1}{\epsilon}\frac{g_A^2 M_\pi^2 m_N^2}{256 \pi^2 F^2} C^2\), leading to a running coupling \(D(\mu) = D(\mu_0) + \frac{g_A^2 m_N^2}{256 \pi^2 F^2} C^2 \ln(\mu/\mu_0)\).
* Using the estimate \(C \approx 1/(110\ \text{MeV})^2\) from Kaplan (1998), the ratio \(D M_\pi^2 / C \approx M_\pi^2 / (430\ \text{MeV})^2\), indicating no enhancement of the coupling \(D\).
* For the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel, the coefficient of the logarithmically divergent part of a \(2n\)-loop ladder diagram is estimated as \(\sim \frac{1}{(146\ \text{MeV})^2} \left( \frac{q^2}{(1010\ \text{MeV})^2} \right)^n\), where \(q\) is a small momentum.

## Context

This paper defends Weinberg's original approach (Weinberg, 1990, 1991) against criticisms raised by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996, 1998) and others who proposed alternative power-counting schemes (KSW counting) due to perceived inconsistencies in renormalization-group behavior. The work extends earlier discussions by Lepage (1997) and Gegelia (1998, 2001) on the consistency of Weinberg's scheme, and it enables practical NN calculations using cutoff regularization with the cutoff chosen of order the characteristic large scale of the EFT (around 1 GeV), as done by Epelbaum (1999) and Entem (2003).
