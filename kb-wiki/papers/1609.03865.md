# Microscopic Calculations of Vortex-Nucleus Interaction in the Neutron Star Crust

> We investigate the dynamics of a quantized vortex and a nuclear impurity immersed in a neutron superfluid within a fully microscopic time-dependent three-dimensional approach. The magnitude and even the sign of the force between the quantized vortex and the nuclear impurity have been a matter of debate for over four decades. We determine that the vortex and the impurity repel at neutron densities, 0.014 fm$^{-3}$ and 0.031 fm$^{-3}$, which are relevant to the neutron star crust and the origin of glitches, while previous calculations have concluded that the force changes its sign between these two densities and predicted contradictory signs. The magnitude of the force increases with the density of neutron superfluid, while the magnitude of the pairing gap decreases in this density range.

## Key claim
The paper determines that the vortex and nuclear impurity repel at neutron densities of 0.014 fm$^{-3}$ and 0.031 fm$^{-3}$, contradicting previous calculations that predicted the force changes sign between these two densities and gave contradictory signs. The magnitude of the force increases with neutron superfluid density, while the magnitude of the pairing gap decreases in this density range.

## Method
The authors performed fully microscopic, three-dimensional, symmetry-unconstrained time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations on a periodic 3D lattice. They solved the TDSLDA equations (equivalent to time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations) in a box of 75 fm × 75 fm × 60 fm with 1.5 fm lattice spacing, using the FaNDF$^0$ functional for the normal part and a density-dependent pairing functional. They extracted the vortex-nucleus force by dragging a nuclear impurity with 50 protons at a constant velocity of 0.001c toward a vortex line and adjusting an external force to maintain constant velocity, then measuring the force required.

## Key numbers
- Neutron densities studied: 0.014 fm$^{-3}$ and 0.031 fm$^{-3}$, both relevant to the neutron star crust.
- Simulation box dimensions: 75 fm × 75 fm × 60 fm with lattice spacing 1.5 fm, giving momentum cutoff p_c ≈ 400 MeV/c.
- Dragging velocity of the nucleus: v_0 = 0.001c, far below the critical velocity to avoid phonon excitation.
- Time step: Δt = 0.054 fm/c.
- The velocity field inside the nucleus is about 0.005c, approximately 5 times larger than the dragging velocity.
- The extracted force is at least one order of magnitude larger than those predicted in a recent phenomenological analysis by Seveso (2016).

## Context
This work extends the first fully microscopic, three-dimensional, symmetry-unconstrained dynamical simulations reported by the same authors in a prior paper (Sekizawa et al., 2016, Ref. [vortex]) to extract the vortex-nucleus interaction. It contradicts previous calculations that predicted the force changes sign between the two densities studied, and it resolves a four-decade debate about the sign and magnitude of the force. The repulsive force is compatible with interstitial pinning, where vortices are trapped at positions maximizing separation from nuclei, as proposed by Link (2009). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star glitch mechanisms by providing a microscopically determined vortex-nucleus interaction.
