# The Evaluation of V_{ud}, Experiment and Theory

> The value of the V_{ud} matrix element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix can be derived from nuclear superallowed beta decays, neutron decay, and pion beta decay. We survey current world data for all three. Today, the most precise value of V_{ud} comes from the nuclear decays; however, the precision is limited not by experimental error but by the estimated uncertainty in theoretical corrections. Experimental uncertainty does limit the neutron-decay result, which, though statistically consistent with the nuclear result, is approximately a factor of three poorer in precision. The value obtained for $V_{ud}$ leads to a result that differs at the 98% confidence level from the unitarity condition for the CKM matrix. We examine the reliability of the small calculated corrections that have been applied to the data, and assess the likelihood of even higher quality nuclear data becoming available to confirm or deny the discrepancy. Some of the required experiments depend upon the availability of intense radioactive beams. Others are possible today.

## Key claim

The most precise value of $V_{ud}$ comes from nuclear superallowed beta decays, but the precision is limited not by experimental error but by the estimated uncertainty in theoretical corrections; the resulting unitarity sum for the first row of the CKM matrix differs from unity at the 98% confidence level, and the authors conclude that reasonable adjustments to the theoretical corrections are unlikely to resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The authors surveyed world data for nuclear superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ beta decays (nine nuclei from $^{10}$C to $^{54}$Co), free neutron decay, and pion beta decay, applying radiative corrections ($\delta_R$, $\Delta_R^V$) and isospin symmetry-breaking corrections ($\delta_C$) to extract $V_{ud}$ from each decay type. For the nuclear decays, they averaged $\mathcal{F}t$-values from over 100 independent measurements of Q-values, lifetimes, and branching ratios, using the average of two independent $\delta_C$ calculations (Towner and Hardy; Ormand and Brown) with assigned uncertainties reflecting the scatter between them.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear decay result: $|V_{ud}| = 0.9740 \pm 0.0005$, with unitarity sum $\sum_i V_{ui}^2 = 0.9968 \pm 0.0014$ (fails unity by 2.2 standard deviations)
- Neutron decay result: $|V_{ud}| = 0.9745 \pm 0.0016$, with unitarity sum $\sum_i V_{ui}^2 = 0.9978 \pm 0.0033$ (factor of three poorer precision than nuclear result)
- Pion beta decay result: $|V_{ud}| = 0.9670 \pm 0.0161$, with unitarity sum $\sum_i V_{ui}^2 = 0.9833 \pm 0.0311$
- Average $\mathcal{F}t$-value for nuclear decays: $3072.3 \pm 2.0$ s
- Nucleus-independent radiative correction: $\Delta_R^V = (2.40 \pm 0.08)\%$
- If experiment were the sole contributor to the nuclear $|V_{ud}|$ uncertainty, it would be only $\pm 0.0001$; the largest contributions come from $\Delta_R^V$ ($\pm 0.0004$) and $\delta_C$ ($\pm 0.0003$)
- To restore unitarity, the calculated radiative corrections would need to shift downward by 0.3% (one-quarter of their current value) or the isospin symmetry-breaking correction upward by 0.3% (over one-half their value)

## Context

This work extends the survey of superallowed beta decays by Towner and Hardy (1990, 2002) and updates the analysis presented at the WEIN conference (1998). The nuclear result for $V_{ud}$ produces a unitarity deficit that contradicts the Standard Model expectation, while the neutron decay result (consistent with unitarity) and pion beta decay result (also consistent but with large uncertainty) cannot resolve the discrepancy due to their larger error bars. The paper examines whether the unitarity problem can be fixed by adjustments to the theoretical corrections (Sirlin 1987; Jaus and Rasche 1987 for radiative corrections; Ormand and Brown 1995 for isospin corrections) and concludes it cannot, pointing to the need for either new experimental data from intense radioactive beams or speculative new physics beyond the Standard Model.
