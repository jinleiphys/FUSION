# The theta^+ baryon in soliton models: large Nc QCD and the validity of rigid-rotor quantization

> A light collective theta+ baryon state (with strangeness +1) was predicted via rigid-rotor collective quantization of SU(3) chiral soliton models. This paper explores the validity of this treatment. A number of rather general analyses suggest that predictions of exotic baryon properties based on this approximation do not follow from large Nc QCD. These include an analysis of the baryon's width, a comparison of the predictions with general large Nc consistency conditions of the Gervais-Sakita-Dashen-Manohar type; an application of the technique to QCD in the limit where the quarks are heavy; a comparison of this method with the vibration approach of Callan and Klebanov; and the 1/Nc scaling of the excitation energy. It is suggested that the origin of the problem lies in an implicit assumption in the that the collective motion is orthogonal to vibrational motion. While true for non-exotic motion, the Wess-Zumino term induces mixing at leading order between collective and vibrational motion with exotic quantum numbers. This suggests that successful phenomenological predictions of theta+ properties based on rigid-rotor quantization were accidental.

# The theta^+ baryon in soliton models: large Nc QCD and the validity of rigid-rotor quantization

## Key claim

The rigid-rotor collective quantization of SU(3) chiral soliton models, which predicted a light theta^+ baryon with strangeness +1, is formally invalid at large Nc because the Wess-Zumino term induces leading-order mixing between collective and vibrational motion for exotic quantum numbers, meaning the successful phenomenological predictions for the theta^+ were accidental rather than consequences of large Nc QCD.

## Method

The paper examines the validity of rigid-rotor quantization by analyzing the large Nc scaling of the collective Hamiltonian derived from SU(3) chiral soliton models, where the classical hedgehog configuration is rotated by time-dependent SU(3) rotations and quantized semiclassically. The analysis compares the rigid-rotor predictions against five independent large Nc consistency checks: the Nc scaling of the exotic baryon width, the Gervais-Sakita-Dashen-Manohar type consistency conditions, the heavy-quark limit of QCD, the Callan-Klebanov bound-state (vibrational) approach, and the 1/Nc scaling of the excitation energy. The paper also reexamines the derivation of the rigid-rotor quantization to identify where the implicit assumption of orthogonality between collective and vibrational motion fails for exotic states.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the anti-decuplet relative to the octet is given by M_{overline{10}} - M_8 = 3/(2 I_2) for Nc=3, but for general Nc it becomes M_{``overline{10}"} - M_{``8"} = (3 + Nc)/(4 I_2), which scales as Nc^0 (order unity) at large Nc rather than 1/Nc.
- The width of the theta^+ computed from rigid-rotor quantization scales as Gamma_{theta^+} ~ Nc^0, meaning it does not vanish as Nc goes to infinity, in contrast to non-exotic collective states like the Delta whose widths vanish at large Nc.
- The coupling constants in the collective operator for meson-baryon coupling scale as G_0 ~ Nc^{3/2} and G_1 ~ Nc^{1/2}, with the matrix element for theta^+ decay to nucleon plus kaon being of order Nc^0 after phase space factors.
- The non-exotic collective excitation energies scale as 1/Nc (since I_1 ~ Nc), giving M_{``10"} - M_{``8"} = 3/(2 I_1) ~ 1/Nc, while the exotic excitation energy remains finite at large Nc.

## Context

This paper extends the author's earlier work (Cohen, 2003) arguing that rigid-rotor quantization fails for exotic baryons, and addresses counterarguments by Diakonov and Petrov (2003) who claimed the method is accurate up to 1/Nc corrections. The analysis builds on the large Nc consistency relations of Gervais, Sakita, Dashen, and Manohar, and compares with the Callan-Klebanov bound-state approach (1985), which treats strangeness excitations as vibrational modes. Itzhaki, Klebanov, Ouyang, and Rastelli (IKOR) reached similar conclusions about the incompatibility of rigid-rotor and Callan-Klebanov treatments for exotic states, while Pobylitsa (2003) demonstrated the failure using an exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick toy model. The paper enables a formal understanding that exotic baryon predictions from rigid-rotor quantization are not model-independent consequences of large Nc QCD, and that alternative treatments (such as the Callan-Klebanov approach or explicit consideration of vibrational-collective mixing) are required for exotic states.
