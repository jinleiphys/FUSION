# Nonperturbative excitations in overoccupied gluon plasmas

> Motivated by the early-time dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, we extract gluonic spectral functions of overoccupied gauge theories far from equilibrium using classical-statistical lattice simulations and linear response theory. In 3+1 dimensions we find that the spectral function exhibits quasiparticle excitations at all momenta that are mostly consistent with perturbative hard-thermal loop predictions, while partially showing nonperturbative deviations. In contrast, the structure of excitations in 2+1 dimensions is nontrivial and nonperturbative. These nonperturbative interactions lead to broad excitation peaks in the spectral function, demonstrating the absence of soft quasiparticles in these theories. This also suggests that there may be significant nonperturbative corrections present in systems with large momentum anisotropy, which are relevant to phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

In 3+1 dimensions, the gluonic spectral function of overoccupied plasmas far from equilibrium exhibits quasiparticle excitations that are mostly consistent with perturbative hard-thermal loop (HTL) predictions, while partially showing nonperturbative deviations. In contrast, the structure of excitations in 2+1 dimensions is nontrivial and fully nonperturbative, with broad excitation peaks demonstrating the absence of soft quasiparticles in these theories.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Minkowski space-time, initializing systems with large occupation numbers f(p) ~ 1/g^2 for soft momenta below a characteristic scale Q. They extracted gluonic spectral functions using linear response theory, perturbing the fields with an instantaneous source at time t and solving linearized equations of motion for the response field. The spectral functions were computed at self-similar attractor states for isotropic 3+1 dimensional systems and for effectively 2+1 dimensional systems (corresponding to the limit of extreme momentum anisotropy), with results compared to perturbative HTL calculations.

## Key numbers

- For isotropic 3+1D plasmas, the damping rate scales as gamma_alpha(t,p) ~ g^2 T_*(t) ~ Q (Q t)^{-3/7} in the self-similar regime, with gamma_alpha(t,p) / omega(t,p=0) ~ (Q t)^{-2/7} decreasing with time
- For 2+1D plasmas, the damping rate satisfies gamma(t,p) ~ omega_plas(t) and gamma(t,p) ~ omega(t,p) for momenta p less than or approximately equal to omega_plas, with no scale separation
- The plasmon mass in 3+1D is approximately m ~ 0.15 Q
- For 2+1D and Glasma-like systems, the self-similar scaling exponents are beta = -1/5 and alpha = 3 beta
- For isotropic 3+1D systems, the self-similar scaling exponents are beta = -1/7 and alpha = 4 beta

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of gluonic spectral functions in overoccupied gauge theories (Boguslavski 2018) and self-similar attractors in 2+1 dimensional plasmas (Boguslavski 2019, 2021). The results show that while isotropic 3+1D plasmas are well described by HTL perturbation theory (Braaten 1990), 2+1D systems exhibit genuinely nonperturbative properties that HTL fails to reproduce, suggesting that significant nonperturbative corrections may be present in systems with large momentum anisotropy relevant to heavy-ion collisions. This enables future extensions to expanding and anisotropic plasmas to assess what may be lacking in current kinetic theory descriptions (Arnold 2002, Kurkela 2018, Fu 2021) and to study how nonperturbative properties affect transport coefficients (Boguslavski 2020).
