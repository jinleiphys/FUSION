# Cluster-shell competition and its effect on the $E0$ transition probability in $^{20}$Ne

> $^{20}$Ne has been known as a typical example of a nucleus with $α$ cluster structure ($^{16}$O+$α$ structure). However according to the spherical shell model, the spin-orbit interaction acts attractively for four nucleons outside of the $^{16}$O core, and this spin-orbit effect cannot be taken into account in the simple $α$ cluster models. We investigate how the $α$ cluster structure competes with independent particle motions of these four nucleons. The antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM) is a method to describe a transition from the $α$ cluster wave function to the $jj$-coupling shell model wave function. In this model, the cluster-shell transition is characterized by only two parameters; $R$ representing the distance between clusters and $Λ$ describing the breaking of $α$ clusters, and the contribution of the spin-orbit interaction, very important in the $jj$-coupling shell model, can be taken into account by changing $α$ clusters to quasi clusters. In this article, based on AQCM, we apply $^{16}$O plus one quasi cluster model for $^{20}$Ne. Here we focus on the $E0$ transition matrix element, which has been known as the quantity characterizing the cluster structure. The $E0$ transition matrix elements are sensitive to the change of the wave functions from $α$ cluster to $jj$-coupling shell model.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the $\alpha$ cluster structure in $^{20}$Ne competes with independent particle motions of the four valence nucleons, and shows that the $E0$ transition matrix element from the ground state to the second $0^+$ state is sensitive to this competition, with the calculated value agreeing with experiment only when the spin-orbit interaction strength is tuned to approximately $V_{ls} = 1770$ MeV.

## Method

The authors apply the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM) to $^{20}$Ne, describing the system as $^{16}$O plus one quasi cluster. The wave functions are characterized by two parameters: $R$ (distance between the quasi cluster and the $^{16}$O core) and $\Lambda$ (describing the breaking of the $\alpha$ cluster). They prepare GCM basis states with $R = 1,2,3,4,5,6,7$ fm and $\Lambda = 0, 1/3, 2/3, 1$, then superpose these states by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation, varying the strength of the spin-orbit interaction $V_{ls}$ from 0 to 3000 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Ground state $0^+$ energy at $V_{ls} = 0$ MeV: $-155.2$ MeV; at $V_{ls} = 3000$ MeV: $-166.3$ MeV (experimental value: $-160.6448$ MeV)
* Optimal GCM basis state at $V_{ls} = 0$ MeV: $R = 3$ fm, $\Lambda = 0$, energy $-153.6$ MeV
* Optimal GCM basis state at $V_{ls} = 3000$ MeV: $R = 1$ fm, $\Lambda = 2/3$, energy $-163.8$ MeV
* Experimental $E0$ transition matrix element from ground to second $0^+$ state: $6.914$ $e$ fm$^2$
* Calculated $E0$ transition matrix element at $V_{ls} = 0$ MeV: $10.0$ $e$ fm$^2$; agreement with experiment achieved at $V_{ls} = 1770$ MeV
* Level repulsion between third and fourth $0^+$ states occurs around $V_{ls} = 1000$ MeV; between second and third $0^+$ states around $V_{ls} = 1500$ MeV
* First $1^-$ state at $V_{ls} = 0$ MeV: $-150.3$ MeV; at $V_{ls} = 3000$ MeV: $-152.0$ MeV, with squared overlap of 0.85 with $R = 4$ fm, $\Lambda = 0$ basis state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AQCM study of $^{16}$O (Matsuno, 2015) where the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster state was affected by spin-orbit interaction, but here the focus is on changing the smaller cluster ($\alpha$) around the $^{16}$O core rather than the larger cluster. The paper builds on the traditional cluster model description of $^{20}$Ne by Horiuchi and the "container picture" by Zhou, and addresses the limitation that simple $\alpha$ cluster models cannot account for the spin-orbit interaction, which is crucial in the $jj$-coupling shell model. The results enable future studies of how cluster-shell competition affects electromagnetic transitions in other light nuclei.
