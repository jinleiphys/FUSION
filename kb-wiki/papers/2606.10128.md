# Determining universal spectra from probability distributions

> The probability distribution of a two-particle correlation function computed over background auxiliary field configurations, used to generate the interactions, has been shown to inform about the spectra of universal $n$-body clusters [1]. Here, we utilize two approaches, a numerical lattice computation and an analytic expansion in the limit of large numbers of identical species, in an attempt to refine the initial predictions. Exploratory calculations in these directions are presented, and future investigations laid out.

## Key claim

The probability distribution of a two-particle correlation function, computed over background auxiliary field configurations, can be used to extract the binding energies of universal $n$-body clusters; however, the initial log-normal prediction for the ratio $E_{2n}/E_4 = 1/2n(n-1)$ may require corrections, as direct calculations of $n$-body energies are systematically higher than this prediction.

## Method

The authors use two approaches to refine predictions for universal cluster spectra from probability distributions. First, they perform a numerical lattice computation of a system of particles interacting via exchange of bosonic auxiliary fields tuned to unitarity, measuring sample moments of the logarithm of the two-particle correlation function and extrapolating in inverse lattice size to remove discretization effects. Second, they develop an analytic field-theoretic expansion in the limit of a large number $N$ of identical fermion species, using the BCS channel formulation to compute the characteristic function and cumulants of the distribution of $\ln C_2(\phi,\tau)$ via a mean-field solution and diagrammatic expansion in $1/N$.

## Key numbers

* The preliminary linear extrapolation in $1/L$ indicates that the contribution to the cumulant expansion from $\kappa_3$ is of $\mathcal{O}(0.01\%)$ of the leading term.
* The leading-order cumulants in the large-$N$ expansion scale as $\kappa_n \propto \frac{g T}{V N}\xi^{n-2}$, where $\xi \equiv \frac{(M \tilde{g})^3}{\pi \Gamma(1/4)^4 N V}$.
* The beta function for the dimensionless coupling $\hat{g}$ is $\beta(\mu) = -\hat{g}(1-\hat{g})$, with fixed points at $\hat{g}=0$ (non-interacting) and $\hat{g}=1$ (unitarity).
* The non-trivial mean-field vacuum solution is $\phi_0 = 32\pi [\Gamma(5/4)]^4/(\tilde{g}^2 M^3)$.

## Context

This work extends the initial proposal of Nicholson (2012) that the $n$th moment of the two-particle correlation function distribution relates to the binding energy of a universal $2n$-body cluster tied to an Efimov trimer, with a log-normal distribution giving the specific ratio $E_{2n}/E_4 = 1/2n(n-1)$. The present study attempts to quantify deviations from this log-normal prediction by comparing with direct calculations from von Stecher (2010), Gattobigio (2014), Yan and Blume (2015), Kievsky (2017), and Carlson (2017), which show systematically higher energies. The results enable future work to compute corrections to the spectrum using either numerical lattice extrapolation or analytic large-$N$ perturbation theory, and to apply machine learning techniques to translate deviations from log-normal into spectral corrections.
