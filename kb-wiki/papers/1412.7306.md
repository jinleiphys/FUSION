# Effects of near-zero Dirac eigenmodes on axial U(1) symmetry at finite temperature

> We study the axial U(1)A symmetry of Nf = 2 QCD at finite temperature using the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum. The gauge configurations are generated employing the Mobius domain-wall fermion action on 16^3x8 and 32^3x8 lattices. The physical spatial size of these lattices is around 2 fm and 4 fm, respectively, and the simulated temperature is around 200 MeV, which is slightly above the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition. Although the Mobius domain-wall Dirac operator is expected to have a good chiral symmetry and our data actually show small values of the residual mass, we observe significant violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation for the low- lying eigenmodes of the Mobius domain-wall Dirac operator. Using the reweighting technique, we compute the overlap-Dirac operator spectrum on the same set of configurations and find a significant difference of the spectrum between the two Dirac operators for the low-lying eigenvalues. The overlap-Dirac spectrum shows a gap from zero, which is insensitive to the spacial volume.

## Key claim

The overlap-Dirac eigenvalue spectrum at temperatures slightly above the chiral transition shows a gap from zero that is insensitive to spatial volume, while the Möbius domain-wall Dirac operator spectrum shows small but non-zero eigenvalues near zero; the difference is attributed to significant violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation by low-lying eigenmodes of the Möbius domain-wall operator, even when the residual mass is small.

## Method

The authors generate \(N_f = 2\) QCD gauge configurations using the Möbius domain-wall fermion action on \(16^3 \times 8\) and \(32^3 \times 8\) lattices at temperatures around 180–200 MeV, slightly above the chiral transition. They compute the low-lying eigenvalue spectrum of the Möbius domain-wall Dirac operator and then, using a reweighting technique, compute the overlap-Dirac operator spectrum on the same configurations. The violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation is measured for each eigenmode via the quantity \(g_i\), and a low-mode reweighting approximation is used on the larger volume where full stochastic reweighting is not feasible.

## Key numbers

- Simulated temperature is around 200 MeV (slightly above the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition).
- Lattice spacings are estimated as 0.135 fm (at \(\beta=4.07\)) and 0.125 fm (at \(\beta=4.10\)).
- Quark masses used: \(am_\text{ud}=0.01\) (30 MeV or 32 MeV) and \(am_\text{ud}=0.001\) (3.0 or 3.2 MeV).
- Residual mass is kept at around or smaller than 1 MeV.
- For the reweighted overlap operator, the residual mass is \(<4\times 10^{-3}\) MeV.
- The overlap-Dirac spectrum shows a gap of approximately 20 MeV at the lightest quark mass on both volumes.
- The peak in the overlap spectrum at heavy quark mass (\(am_\text{ud}=0.01\)) gives \(a^3\rho(0)=0.00164\pm 0.00045\).
- For the low-mode reweighting, eigenvalues below \(\lambda \sim 100\) MeV are included; thresholds used are \(a\lambda_\text{th}=0.35\) (for \(L=16\)) and \(0.24\) (for \(L=32\)).

## Context

This work addresses a controversy between lattice QCD groups studying axial U(1) symmetry restoration above the chiral transition: JLQCD (Cossu 2013) and TWQCD (Chiu 2013) reported restoration using overlap or optimal domain-wall fermions, while LLNL/RBC (Bazavov 2012, Buchoff 2013) and Ohno (2012) found opposite results using domain-wall or staggered fermions on larger volumes. The present study systematically compares Möbius domain-wall and overlap Dirac spectra on the same configurations, identifying violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation in low modes of the domain-wall operator as a likely source of the discrepancy. The results support the existence of a gap in the Dirac spectrum above \(T_c\), which would imply restoration of axial U(1) symmetry in the chiral limit, and enable future studies using the low-mode reweighting technique on larger volumes.
