# Electromagnetic fields in small systems from a multiphase transport model

> We calculate the electromagnetic fields generated in small systems by using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. Compared to $A+A$ collisions, we find that the absolute electric and magnetic fields are not small in $p$+Au and $d$+Au collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and in $p$+Pb collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider. We study the centrality dependencies and the spatial distributions of electromagnetic fields. We further investigate the azimuthal fluctuations of the magnetic field and its correlation with the fluctuating geometry using event-by-event simulations. We find that the azimuthal correlation $\left\langle \cos2(Ψ_B - Ψ_{2}) \right\rangle$ between the magnetic field direction and the second harmonic participant plane is almost zero in small systems with high multiplicities, but not in those with low multiplicities. This indicates that the charge azimuthal correlation, $\left\langle \cos(ϕ_α+ϕ_β - 2Ψ_{RP}) \right\rangle$, is not a valid probe to study the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in small systems with high multiplicities. However, we suggest searching for possible CME effects in small systems with low multiplicities.

## Key claim
The absolute electric and magnetic fields in p+Au, d+Au, and p+Pb collisions are not small compared to A+A collisions, but the azimuthal correlation between the magnetic field direction and the second harmonic participant plane is almost zero in small systems with high multiplicities, making the traditional chiral magnetic effect observable invalid in those events; however, a nonzero correlation exists in low-multiplicity events, suggesting a possible search for the CME there.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate p+Au and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV. Electromagnetic fields were calculated from the spatial distribution of protons in the initial HIJING stage using the Liénard-Wiechert potentials, with all protons at least 0.3 fm from the center of mass included. The second harmonic participant plane was computed from the spatial distribution of partons after string melting, and event-by-event correlations between the magnetic field direction and participant plane were analyzed.

## Key numbers
- In p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the absolute magnetic field strength can reach the order of several m_pi^2 (where eB ~ m_pi^2 ~ 10^18 to 10^19 Gauss).
- For p+Au and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the correlation <cos2(Psi_B - Psi_2)> is almost zero when N_track > 10.
- For p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV, the correlation <cos2(Psi_B - Psi_2)> is close to zero when N_track > 50.
- The spatial distributions of electromagnetic fields in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV have magnitudes approximately 200/5020 ≈ 1/25 times smaller than those in p+Pb collisions when compared to p+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- For p+Pb collisions at b = 9 fm, the histogram of Psi_B - Psi_2 peaks at zero, indicating a nonzero correlation in very peripheral events.

## Context
This work extends calculations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Deng and Huang, 2012) to small collision systems, motivated by CMS measurements (Khachatryan, 2016; Sirunyan, 2017) showing similar charge azimuthal correlations in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions that challenge the CME interpretation. The study confirms earlier findings (Belmont, 2016) that the magnetic field direction and participant plane are uncorrelated in p+A collisions, but shows this holds only for high-multiplicity events. The results enable future experimental searches for the CME in low-multiplicity small system collisions, where a nonzero correlation between magnetic field and geometry exists.
