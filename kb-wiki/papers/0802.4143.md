# Spectral Continuity in Dense QCD

> The vector mesons in three-flavor quark matter with chiral and diquark condensates are studied using the in-medium QCD sum rules. The diquark condensate leads to a mass splitting between the flavor-octet and flavor-singlet channels. At high density, the singlet vector meson disappears from the low-energy spectrum, while the octet vector mesons survive as light excitations with a mass comparable to the fermion gap. A possible connection between the light gluonic modes and the flavor-octet vector mesons at high density is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper uses in-medium QCD sum rules to show that, in three-flavor quark matter with both chiral and diquark condensates, the flavor-octet vector mesons survive as light excitations at high density with a mass comparable to the fermion gap, while the flavor-singlet vector meson disappears from the low-energy spectrum above a critical chemical potential determined by the relative magnitude of the two condensates.

## Method

The authors compute the vector-current correlation function for flavor-octet and flavor-singlet channels in massless three-flavor quark matter using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to O(1/Q^6). They include four-quark condensates arising from both chiral (quark-antiquark) and diquark (quark-quark) pairing, factorize them using the factorization ansatz, and then match the OPE to a parametrized spectral function via finite energy sum rules to extract the vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- For the flavor-octet vector meson mass at intermediate density: \( m_V^{(8)} \simeq 100\ \text{MeV} \) for \(\mu = 500\ \text{MeV}\), \(\alpha_s \sim 1\), and \(\sigma \sim \varphi \sim (150\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- At asymptotic high density, the octet vector meson mass approaches \( m_{\rm V}^{(8)} \rightarrow \sqrt{20/3}\ \Delta \), where \(\Delta\) is the fermion gap.
- The critical chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm c}\) for the disappearance of the light singlet vector meson is determined by the condition \(\sigma^2 = (66/7) \varphi^2\).
- The singlet vector meson mass formula in the light-solution regime: \( (m_{\rm V}^{(1)})^2 \simeq \frac{1}{f} \frac{56\pi^3 \alpha_s}{81\mu^4} \left( \sigma^2 - \frac{66}{7} \varphi^2 \right) \), with \(f \equiv 2\pi^2 F/(2\mu)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of hadron-quark continuity in dense QCD, previously explored by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and by Hatsuda, Tachibana, Yamamoto, and Baym (2006) for pionic modes, to the vector meson sector. It provides a QCD sum-rule framework that connects low-density hadronic descriptions to the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase at high density, and suggests a connection between the light octet vector mesons and the light gluonic modes (CFL plasmons) found by Casalbuoni, Gatto, Nardulli, and Ruggieri (2001). The approach enables future studies of spectral continuity for other hadrons, such as flavored baryons, in dense matter.
