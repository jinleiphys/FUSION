# Flavor asymmetry in polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process

> We discuss the possibility of finding polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry in Drell-Yan processes. We find that the difference between polarized proton-proton and proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross sections should provide valuable information on the polarized flavor asymmetry. Numerical results indicate that the asymmetry effects are conspicuous especially in the large-x_F region. Our analysis is important for the transversity distributions because the flavor asymmetry cannot be found by inclusive lepton scattering and W-production processes.

## Key claim

The difference between polarized proton-proton and proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross sections can provide information on the polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry, with the effects being especially conspicuous in the large-\(x_F\) region; however, no actual experimental measurement is planned at this stage, and the proposal is limited by the difficulty of achieving longitudinal polarization of the deuteron due to its small magnetic moment.

## Method

The author derives expressions for the longitudinally-polarized and transversely-polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross-section differences in terms of polarized parton distributions, using a parton-model analysis that neglects higher-twist contributions and nuclear corrections. The ratio \(R_{pd} = \Delta_{(T)} \sigma_{pd} / [2 \, \Delta_{(T)} \sigma_{pp}]\) is then computed numerically using parton distributions from Ref. 7 at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), evolved to \(Q^2 = M_{\mu\mu} = 25\) GeV\(^2\) by leading-order DGLAP evolution, with the flavor asymmetry ratio \(r_{\bar q} \equiv \Delta_{(T)} \bar u / \Delta_{(T)} \bar d\) set to 0.7, 1.0, or 1.3.

## Key numbers

- The parton distributions are taken at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) and evolved to \(Q^2 = M_{\mu\mu} = 25\) GeV\(^2\).
- The center-of-mass energy for the fixed-target scenario is \(\sqrt{s} = 50\) GeV; collider options at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) and 500 GeV are also considered.
- The flavor asymmetry ratio \(r_{\bar q} \equiv \Delta_{(T)} \bar u / \Delta_{(T)} \bar d\) is varied among 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3.
- In the limit \(x_F \rightarrow +1\), the ratio \(R_{pd}\) becomes \(\frac{1}{2} [1 + \Delta_{(T)} \bar d(x_2) / \Delta_{(T)} \bar u(x_2)]_{x_2 \rightarrow 0}\); in the limit \(x_F \rightarrow -1\), it becomes \(\frac{1}{2} [1 + \Delta_{(T)} \bar d(x_1) / (4 \, \Delta_{(T)} \bar u(x_1))]_{x_1 \rightarrow 0}\), giving a deviation from 1 or from 5/8 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the unpolarized flavor asymmetry studies (Gottfried-sum-rule violation and proton-deuteron asymmetry in Drell-Yan experiments) to the polarized case, where previous parametrizations (AAC, 1998) assumed flavor-symmetric antiquark distributions. The analysis is important for transversity distributions because the flavor asymmetry cannot be found by inclusive lepton scattering and W-production processes due to the chiral-odd property; the paper proposes the only known method for extracting the transversity asymmetry \(\Delta_T \bar u / \Delta_T \bar d\). Future experimental possibilities are suggested at Fermilab, HERA-N, JHF, and RHIC, though longitudinal polarization of the deuteron at RHIC is noted as difficult.
