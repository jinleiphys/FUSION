# Estimation of the effect of hyperonic three-body forces on the maximum mass of neutron stars

> A model based on a microscopic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach of hyperonic matter supplemented with additional simple phenomenological density-dependent contact terms is employed to estimate the effect of hyperonic three-body forces on the maximum mass of neutron stars. Our results show that although hyperonic three-body forces can reconcile the maximum mass of hyperonic stars with the current limit of $1.4-1.5 M_\odot$, they are unable to provide the repulsion needed to make the maximum mass compatible with the observation of massive neutron stars, such as the recent measurements of the unusually high masses of the millisecond pulsars PSR J1614-2230 ($1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$) and PSR J1903+0327 ($1.667 \pm 0.021 M_\odot$).

## Key claim

Hyperonic three-body forces can raise the maximum mass of neutron stars containing hyperons to the "canonical" range of 1.4–1.5 solar masses, but even with the strongest reasonable hyperonic three-body forces the maximum mass reaches only about 1.60 solar masses, which is incompatible with the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙) and PSR J1903+0327 (1.667 ± 0.021 M⊙).

## Method

The authors start from a microscopic Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) calculation of hyperonic matter using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon force and the Nijmegen NSC89 hyperon-nucleon interaction. They then add phenomenological density-dependent contact terms of Skyrme-like form to the energy density to mimic the effect of nucleonic and hyperonic three-body forces, with parameters fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties and to the known binding energy of a single Λ hyperon in nuclear matter. The resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations for static neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hyperonic stars without hyperonic three-body forces (x=0): ranges from 1.27 M⊙ (for γNN=2) to 1.38 M⊙ (for γNN=3.5).
- Maximum mass of hyperonic stars with the strongest hyperonic three-body forces considered (γNN=3.5, x=1): 1.60 M⊙.
- Maximum mass of pure nucleonic stars (no hyperons) for the same parameter sets: ranges from 2.22 M⊙ (γNN=2) to 2.97 M⊙ (γNN=3.5).
- Central baryon density at maximum mass for the stiffest hyperonic case (γNN=3.5, x=1): 0.80 fm−3.
- Central speed of sound at maximum mass for the stiffest hyperonic case: 0.59 c.
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3; energy per particle at saturation: E/A = −16 MeV.
- Incompressibility coefficients K∞ for the four nucleonic parameter sets: 211, 236, 260, and 285 MeV.
- Binding energy of a single Λ in nuclear matter at saturation: −28 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations by Baldo (2000), Vidana (2000), and Hansel (2006) that showed hyperon softening reduces maximum masses below the canonical 1.4–1.5 M⊙ range. It directly tests the suggestion by Takatsuka that hyperonic three-body forces could solve this problem. The results establish a numerical lower limit of 1.27 M⊙ and an upper limit of 1.60 M⊙ for the maximum mass of hyperonic stars, motivating more realistic calculations of hyperonic three-body forces. The paper explicitly compares with the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010) and PSR J1903+0327 (Freire 2009).
