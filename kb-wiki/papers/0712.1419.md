# Updated Values of Running Quark and Lepton Masses

> Reliable values of quark and lepton masses are important for model building at a fundamental energy scale, such as the Fermi scale M_Z \approx 91.2 GeV and the would-be GUT scale Λ_GUT \sim 2 \times 10^16 GeV. Using the latest data given by the Particle Data Group, we update the running quark and charged-lepton masses at a number of interesting energy scales below and above M_Z. In particular, we take into account the possible new physics scale (μ\sim 1 TeV) to be explored by the LHC and the typical seesaw scales (μ\sim 10^9 GeV and μ\sim 10^12 GeV) which might be relevant to the generation of neutrino masses. For illustration, the running masses of three light Majorana neutrinos are also calculated. Our up-to-date table of running fermion masses are expected to be very useful for the study of flavor dynamics at various energy scales.

## Key claim

Using updated Particle Data Group (PDG) data and an improved running-matching-running scheme for flavor thresholds, the authors provide revised tables of running quark and charged-lepton masses at energy scales from below the Fermi scale up to the GUT scale, including new physics scales such as 1 TeV and typical seesaw scales (10^9 GeV and 10^12 GeV), with running masses of three light Majorana neutrinos also calculated for illustration.

## Method

The authors evolve quark masses using the renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the strong coupling and quark masses in the MS-bar scheme, with matching conditions at flavor thresholds μ = m_Q(m_Q) for Q = c, b, t. Charged-lepton masses are evolved using the RGE for the fine structure constant including QCD corrections. Above the Fermi scale, the Yukawa coupling matrices for up-type quarks, down-type quarks, charged leptons, and neutrinos are evolved using two-loop RGEs (one-loop for neutrinos) in both the Standard Model and the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model, with scheme conversion between MS-bar and DR-bar at M_Z.

## Key numbers

- Input light quark masses at μ = 2 GeV: m_u = 1.5–3.0 MeV, m_d = 3–7 MeV, m_s = 95 ± 25 MeV
- Input heavy quark masses: m_c(m_c) = 1.25 ± 0.09 GeV, m_b(m_b) = 4.20 ± 0.07 GeV
- Top quark pole mass: M_t = 172.5 ± 2.7 GeV
- Strong coupling at M_Z: α_s(M_Z) = 0.1176 ± 0.002
- Inverse fine structure constant at M_Z: α(M_Z)^{-1} = 127.918 ± 0.018
- Z boson mass: M_Z = 91.1876 ± 0.0021 GeV
- Charged-lepton pole masses: M_e = 0.510998918 ± 0.000000044 MeV, M_μ = 105.6583692 ± 0.0000094 MeV, M_τ = 1776.99^{+0.29}_{-0.26} MeV
- Neutrino mass-squared differences: Δm²_21 = (7.2–8.9) × 10^{-5} eV², |Δm²_32| = (1.7–3.3) × 10^{-3} eV² (99% CL)
- Cosmological bound on sum of neutrino masses: m_1 + m_2 + m_3 < 0.61 eV (95% CL)
- GUT scale: Λ_GUT ∼ 2 × 10^{16} GeV
- Strong coupling at bottom threshold: α_s^{(5)}(μ^{(5)}) = 0.223^{+0.008}_{-0.007} with μ^{(5)} = m_b(m_b)

## Context

This work updates the systematic estimate of running quark masses by Fusaoka and Koide (1998), incorporating new PDG data and improved treatment of flavor thresholds using the running-matching-running scheme from Kniehl and collaborators. The authors extend previous work by including new physics scales relevant for LHC physics (1 TeV) and seesaw mechanisms (10^9 GeV and 10^{12} GeV), and by calculating running masses for three light Majorana neutrinos. The tabulated results are intended as a reference for model building in flavor dynamics at various energy scales, from hadronic physics to GUT-scale phenomenology.
