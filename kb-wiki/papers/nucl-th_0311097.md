# Rotationally Invariant Hamiltonians for Nuclear Spectra Based on Quantum Algebras

> The rotational invariance under the usual physical angular momentum of the SUq(2) Hamiltonian for the description of rotational nuclear spectra is explicitly proved and a connection of this Hamiltonian to the formalisms of Amal'sky and Harris is provided. In addition, a new Hamiltonian for rotational spectra is introduced, based on the construction of irreducible tensor operators (ITO) under SUq(2) and use of q-deformed tensor products and q-deformed Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The rotational invariance of this SUq(2) ITO Hamiltonian under the usual physical angular momentum is explicitly proved, a simple closed expression for its energy spectrum (the ``hyperbolic tangent formula'') is introduced, and its connection to the Harris formalism is established. Numerical tests in a series of Th isotopes are provided.

# Rotationally Invariant Hamiltonians for Nuclear Spectra Based on Quantum Algebras

## Key claim

The SUq(2) Hamiltonian for rotational nuclear spectra is explicitly invariant under the usual physical angular momentum (su(2) algebra), contrary to earlier concerns that it might break spherical symmetry or isotropy of space; this invariance holds for the generic case where the deformation parameter q is not a root of unity.

## Method

The authors prove rotational invariance by constructing explicit realizations of the SUq(2) generators in terms of the usual su(2) angular momentum operators, showing that the SUq(2) Casimir operator commutes with all su(2) generators. They then express the SUq(2) Hamiltonian eigenvalues as an exact power series in l(l+1) using spherical Bessel functions, and derive a closed approximate form (the "hyperbolic tangent formula") from an irreducible tensor operator (ITO) construction under SUq(2) using q-deformed tensor products and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The SUq(2) Hamiltonian eigenvalues for the case q = e^(iτ) are given by E_ℓ^(τ) = A [sin(ℓτ) sin((ℓ+1)τ)] / sin²(τ) + E₀, where A = ħ²/(2J₀)
- The approximate expansion for small deformation (τ ≪ 1) yields E_ℓ^(τ) ≈ A [ℓ(ℓ+1) - (τ²/3){ℓ(ℓ+1)}² + (2τ⁴/45){ℓ(ℓ+1)}³ - (τ⁶/315){ℓ(ℓ+1)}⁴ + ...]
- The closed approximate formula from the ITO Hamiltonian is E_ℓ^(τ) ≈ A sin²(τ√[ℓ(ℓ+1)]) / τ², which connects to the Amal'sky formula E_ℓ = ε₀ sin²((π/N)√[ℓ(ℓ+1)]) with ε₀ ≈ 6.664 MeV
- The Harris formalism expansions derived from this approach give E = (A/(2τ)²)[(ωt)² + (1/4)(ωt)⁴ + (5/8)(ωt)⁶ + ...] and J/J₀ = 1 + (1/6)(ωt)² + (3/40)(ωt)⁴ + (5/112)(ωt)⁶ + ..., where t = 2J₀τ/ħ

## Context

This work extends the SUq(2) approach to rotational spectra introduced by Raychev, Roussev, and Smirnov (1990) and clarifies its relationship to the Variable Moment of Inertia model (Mariscotti, Scharff-Goldhaber, and Buck, 1969). The paper provides explicit connections to the Amal'sky formula (1990) for unified description of rotational spectra and to the Harris formalism (1965) for high-spin phenomena. The results enable systematic comparison of exact and approximate SUq(2) energy formulas with experimental spectra of Th isotopes, and with the Holmberg-Lipas formula (1968), which the authors note is probably the best two-parameter formula for rotational nuclear spectra according to Casten (1990).
