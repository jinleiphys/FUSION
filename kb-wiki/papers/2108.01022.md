# Engineering an Effective Three-spin Hamiltonian in Trapped-ion Systems for Applications in Quantum Simulation

> Trapped-ion quantum simulators, in analog and digital modes, are considered a primary candidate to achieve quantum advantage in quantum simulation and quantum computation. The underlying controlled ion-laser interactions induce all-to-all two-spin interactions via the collective modes of motion through Cirac-Zoller or Molmer-Sorensen schemes, leading to effective two-spin Hamiltonians, as well as two-qubit entangling gates. In this work, the Molmer-Sorensen scheme is extended to induce three-spin interactions via tailored first- and second-order spin-motion couplings. The scheme enables engineering single-, two-, and three-spin interactions, and can be tuned via an enhanced protocol to simulate purely three-spin dynamics. Analytical results for the effective evolution are presented, along with detailed numerical simulations of the full dynamics to support the accuracy and feasibility of the proposed scheme for near-term applications. With a focus on quantum simulation, the advantage of a direct analog implementation of three-spin dynamics is demonstrated via the example of matter-gauge interactions in the U(1) lattice gauge theory within the quantum link model. The mapping of degrees of freedom and strategies for scaling the three-spin scheme to larger systems, are detailed, along with a discussion of the expected outcome of the simulation of the quantum link model given realistic fidelities in the upcoming experiments. The applications of the three-spin scheme go beyond the lattice gauge theory example studied here and include studies of static and dynamical phase diagrams of strongly interacting condensed-matter systems modeled by two- and three-spin Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper extends the standard Mølmer-Sørensen scheme to engineer effective three-spin interactions in trapped-ion systems by using tailored first- and second-order spin-motion couplings, but the pure three-spin Hamiltonian is only achieved approximately when a "two-drive" scheme cancels the dominant single- and two-spin contributions, and the scheme's accuracy depends on operating in a regime where the detuning δ is the smallest frequency scale and contributions from off-resonant modes are suppressed.

## Method

The authors introduce two pairs of Raman laser beams with asymmetric detunings: one pair with a red sideband detuned by −δ from the center-of-mass mode frequency and a blue sideband detuned by +2δ, generating second-order spin-phonon couplings that produce effective three-spin interactions proportional to σ⁺ᵢσ⁺ⱼσ⁺ₖ. A second "mirror" drive with opposite detunings (red at +qδ and blue at −2qδ) is added to cancel the unwanted single- and two-spin contributions that scale as 1/δ, while the three-spin term scales as 1/δ² and survives. The effective Hamiltonian is derived via a Magnus expansion of the time-evolution operator, keeping only resonant contributions, and the full dynamics including phonon degrees of freedom are simulated numerically for a three-ion system to validate the scheme.

## Key numbers

- Three-spin coupling strength scales approximately as η⁴_com Ω²_r Ω_b / δ² in the single-mode (center-of-mass) limit, where η_com is the Lamb-Dicke parameter for the center-of-mass mode, Ω_r and Ω_b are Rabi frequencies, and δ is the detuning from the center-of-mass mode.
- Single- and two-spin couplings scale as η²_com Ω²_r / δ, making them larger than the three-spin coupling by a factor of approximately 1/(η²_com) in the small-δ regime.
- The two-drive scheme cancels single- and two-spin contributions by using a second set of lasers with Rabi frequencies scaled by √q and detunings scaled by qδ, where q is a non-rational constant to avoid resonant sideband transitions.

## Context

This work extends the Mølmer-Sørensen scheme (Molmer 1999) for two-spin interactions to three-spin interactions, building on a prior proposal by Bermudez et al. (2009) that used first- and second-order spin-phonon couplings to generate a σᶻᵢσᶻⱼσᶻₖ Hamiltonian. The present scheme differs by using asymmetric detunings and a mirror-drive cancellation technique that does not require addressing axial modes of motion. The engineered three-spin Hamiltonian enables analog quantum simulation of U(1) lattice gauge theories within the quantum link model, where matter-gauge interactions map to three-spin terms, and can also be applied to study phase diagrams of condensed-matter systems with two- and three-spin Hamiltonians.
