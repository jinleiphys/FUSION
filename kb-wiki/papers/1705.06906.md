# Integrated cross sections of high-energy $e^+e^-$ electroproduction by an electron in an atomic field

> The integrated cross sections of high-energy $e^+e^-$ electroproduction by an electron in an atomic field is studied. Importance of various contributions to these cross sections is discussed. It is shown that the Coulomb corrections are very important both for the differential cross section and for the integrated cross sections even for moderate values of the atomic charge number. This effect is mainly related to the interaction of the produced $e^+e^-$ pair with the atomic field. For the cross section differential over the electron transverse momentum the account for the interference of the amplitudes and the contribution of virtual bremsstrahlung is noticeable. The Coulomb corrections to scattering is larger than these two effects but essentially smaller than the Coulomb corrections to the amplitude of pair photoproduction by a virtual photon. However, in the cross section differential over the positron transverse momentum, there is a strong suppression of the effect of the Coulomb corrections to scattering.

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections to the total cross section of high-energy \(e^+e^-\) electroproduction by an electron in an atomic field are dominated by the interaction of the produced pair with the field, while the Coulomb corrections to scattering of the incoming and outgoing electrons are strongly suppressed in the total cross section and in the cross section differential over the positron transverse momentum, despite being significant in the cross section differential over the electron transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors used the analytical expression for the matrix element of the process \(e^- Z \to e^- e^+ e^- Z\) derived in their earlier work (Krachkov and Milstein, 2016) via the quasiclassical approximation, which includes the exact interaction of all charged particles with the atomic field. They introduced a parameter \(\lambda\) to switch on or off the interaction of the incoming and outgoing electrons with the atomic field, allowing them to isolate the Coulomb corrections to scattering. They then numerically integrated the differential cross section over the final particle momenta and energies to obtain the cross sections differential over the electron transverse momentum \(d\sigma/dp_{2\perp}\), differential over the positron transverse momentum \(d\sigma/dp_{4\perp}\), and the total cross section \(\sigma\), comparing the exact results with the Born approximation and with various approximate results (e.g., \(\lambda=0\), neglecting the interference between the two Feynman diagrams, and neglecting the virtual bremsstrahlung contributions \(T^{(0)}\) and \(\widetilde{T}^{(0)}\)).

## Key numbers

- For gold (\(Z=79\)) and incident electron energy \(\varepsilon_1 = 100 m\), the exact total cross section \(\sigma\) differs significantly from the Born result; the relative deviation \(\delta_2\) (approximate minus exact, divided by exact) for the result without the interference between amplitudes \(T\) and \(\widetilde{T}\) is about \(-0.15\) at \(\varepsilon_1/m = 100\) and decreases in magnitude at higher energies.
- For gold (\(Z=79\)) and \(\varepsilon_1 = 100 m\), the relative deviation \(\delta_2\) for the result without the contributions \(T^{(0)}\) and \(\widetilde{T}^{(0)}\) (virtual bremsstrahlung) is about \(-0.05\) at \(\varepsilon_1/m = 100\) and decreases at higher energies.
- For gold (\(Z=79\)) and \(\varepsilon_1 = 100 m\), the relative deviation \(\delta_2\) for the result with \(\lambda=0\) (neglecting Coulomb corrections to scattering) is about \(-0.02\) at \(\varepsilon_1/m = 100\) and decreases at higher energies.
- The cross section \(d\sigma/dp_{2\perp}\) for \(Z=79\) and \(\varepsilon_1=100 m\) is shown in units of \(\sigma_0/m = \alpha^2 \eta^2 / m^3\) (where \(\eta = Z\alpha\)), with the exact result peaking near \(p_{2\perp}/m \approx 1\) and exceeding the Born result by roughly a factor of 2 at the peak.
- The cross section \(d\sigma/dp_{4\perp}\) for \(Z=79\) and \(\varepsilon_1=100 m\) is shown in the same units; the exact result is about 50% larger than the Born result at the peak near \(p_{4\perp}/m \approx 1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the differential cross section (Krachkov and Milstein, 2016) to the integrated cross sections, and it parallels their study of electroproduction by heavy particles (Krachkov and Milstein, 2017), where a similar suppression of Coulomb corrections to scattering was found. The Born approximation results used for comparison date back to Racah (1937) and other early work (Bhabha, 1935; Bethe, Heitler, and others). The paper corrects a factor of two in the leading logarithmic approximation for the Coulomb corrections to the total cross section given in Krachkov and Milstein (2016), noting that the accuracy of such logarithmic approximations is low due to strong compensation between leading and next-to-leading terms (Lee and Milstein, 2009). The results are relevant for precision data analysis in detectors and for background estimates in new-physics searches.
