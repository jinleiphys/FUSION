# Exact conservation and breaking of pseudospin symmetry in single particle resonant states

> In this contribution we present some results on the study of pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in single particle resonant states. The PSS is a relativistic dynamical symmetry connected with the small component of the nucleon Dirac wave function. Many efforts have been made to study this symmetry in bound states. We recently gave a rigorous justification of the PSS in single particle resonant states by examining the zeros of Jost functions corresponding to the small components of the radial Dirac wave functions and phase shifts of continuum states [1, Phys. Rev. Lett. 109 (2012) 072501]. We have shown that the PSS in single particle resonant states in nuclei is conserved when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign. Examples of exact conservation and breaking of this symmetry in single particle resonances are given for spherical square-well and Woods-Saxon potentials.

## Key claim

The paper rigorously justifies that pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in single particle resonant states is exactly conserved when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign (i.e., when the sum of the scalar and vector potentials is zero or its derivative is zero), and provides numerical examples of both exact conservation and breaking of this symmetry.

## Method

The authors examined the zeros of Jost functions corresponding to the small components of radial Dirac wave functions for single particle states, treating bound and resonant states on the same footing by analytic continuation into the complex momentum plane. They derived the Jost function for spherical square-well potentials and also studied Woods-Saxon potentials, using the real stabilization method to obtain resonance parameters. For pseudospin doublets with different quantum numbers but the same pseudo-orbital angular momentum, they showed that the small components satisfy identical equations in the PSS limit, leading to identical Jost functions and thus paired resonant states.

## Key numbers

- For square-well potentials with D = 650 MeV and R = 7 fm, in the PSS limit (C = 0), all roots of the Jost function locate in the lower half plane with no bound states, demonstrating exact PSS conservation for resonant states.
- When C = -66 MeV (breaking the PSS limit), one bound state appears only for the h_{11/2} partner, and three pairs of PS partner resonant states show splitting of both energy and width.
- For Woods-Saxon potentials with V_0 - S_0 = 650 MeV, V_0 + S_0 = -66 MeV, a = 0.6 fm, and R = 7 fm (parameters connected with 208Pb from Guo (2005)), the splittings of energy and width for h_{11/2} and j_{13/2} are smaller than those from square-well potentials due to the smaller derivative of the sum potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of pseudospin symmetry, previously investigated in bound states by Ginocchio (1997) and Meng (1998, 1999), to single particle resonant states. It provides a rigorous justification using Jost functions, building on numerical studies of PSS in resonant states by Guo (2005, 2006) and Zhang (2006, 2007). The results enable future investigation of experimental evidence for PSS in resonant states, the breaking mechanism compared to bound states, and extension to anti-nucleon spectra, deformed systems, and the effect of Coulomb interactions.
