# Generalized rotational hamiltonians from nonlinear angular momentum algebras

> Higgs algebras are used to construct rotational Hamiltonians. The correspondence between the spectrum of a triaxial rotor and the spectrum of a cubic Higgs algebra is demonstrated. It is shown that a suitable choice of the parameters of the polynomial algebra allows for a precise identification of rotational properties. The harmonic limit is obtained by a contraction of the algebra, leading to a linear symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Hamiltonian of a triaxial rotor can be exactly reproduced by a particular finite-dimensional representation of the cubic Higgs algebra $sl^{(3)}(2,\mathbb{R})$, and that the harmonic (vibrational) limit of that rotor emerges as a contraction of the same algebra, provided the algebra parameters are chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors construct the most general Hamiltonian based on the cubic polynomial algebra $sl^{(3)}(2,\mathbb{R})$ and then select specific finite-dimensional representations (with step $q=2$) whose ladder-operator matrix elements match those of the triaxial rotor. For the vibrational limit, they perform a contraction of the rotor algebra to the harmonic oscillator algebra $h_4$ and embed the resulting Hamiltonian into an infinite-dimensional representation of $sl^{(3)}(2,\mathbb{R})$. The equivalence is verified by comparing matrix elements and by showing that the contracted Hamiltonian reproduces the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) treatment of the rotor at large angular momentum.

## Key numbers

* The deformation parameters for the finite-dimensional representation that reproduces the triaxial rotor are: $\alpha = -64 a^2$, $\beta = 192 a^2 c$, $\gamma = -8 a^2 (1 + 24 c^2 - 2 j(j+1))$, $\delta = 8 a^2 c (1 + 8 c^2 - 2 j(j+1))$, with $a = 1/(4I)$ and $c=0$.
* The RPA eigenfrequency for the harmonic limit of the triaxial rotor is $\omega = 2 I \sqrt{(A_3 - A_1)(A_3 - A_2)}$, where $I$ is the total angular momentum and $A_i = 1/(2\mathcal{I}_i)$ are the inertia parameters.
* The asymmetry parameter of the rotor is $\kappa = (2A_3 - A_1 - A_2)/(A_1 - A_2)$.
* The Casimir eigenvalue for the infinite-dimensional representation used in the harmonic limit is $\langle n | \mathcal{C} | n \rangle = 2 b^2 c (1 + 2c)$, which for $c=0$ gives $1/2$.

## Context

This work extends the mathematical study of Higgs algebras (Higgs, 1979; Debergh, 2000; Debergh, Ndimubandi, and Van den Bossche, 2003) to explicit nuclear-structure Hamiltonians, building on earlier schematic-model applications (Debergh and Stancu, 2001; Debergh and Van den Bossche, 2003). It establishes a direct algebraic correspondence between the cubic $sl^{(3)}(2,\mathbb{R})$ algebra and the standard triaxial rotor of Bohr and Mottelson (1975), and shows that the contraction procedure yields the same harmonic limit as the Holstein-Primakoff boson mapping and RPA formalism (Klein and Marshalek, 1991; Ring and Shuck, 1980). This enables future work on intermediate rotational-vibrational structures, potentially connecting to the newly discovered symmetries reported by McCutchan and Casten (2006).
