# Nuclear EoS with finite range interaction and the Constrained Dynamics

> The effects of the many-body correlations with respect the MF description characterizing the Constrained Molecular Dynamics are discussed in the case of finite and zero range effective microscopic interactions. In particular, for the system $^{64}Ni+^{48}Ca$ fusion-fission cross-sections, IMF production and transversal flow have been evaluated in the framework of the CoMD model at different incident energies. The results obtained significantly depend on whether the effects of the above-mentioned correlations are taken into account in the corresponding EoS.

# Nuclear EoS with finite range interaction and the Constrained Dynamics

## Key claim
The many-body correlations inherent in the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model, which are absent in mean-field (MF) descriptions, significantly affect the equation of state (EoS) and thereby alter predictions for fusion-fission cross-sections, intermediate mass fragment (IMF) production, and transverse flow in heavy ion collisions, but only when the effective interaction parameters are adjusted to account for these correlations rather than taken from the MF scheme.

## Method
The CoMD model was used to simulate $^{64}Ni+^{48}Ca$ collisions at incident energies from 10 to 150 AMeV, using a finite-range interaction (MDI) inspired by the Gogny force with range $\mu = 1.1$ fm and a zero-range interaction (ZR). The strength parameters for the effective interactions were obtained separately for three cases: MDI with parameters fitted to the CoMD correlations (MDI-MD), ZR with CoMD correlations (ZR-MD), and MDI with parameters obtained in the mean-field approximation (MDI-MF). Reaction mechanisms were identified from the $Z-V_p$ correlation plots for impact parameters $b = 0-5$ fm, and the nucleon transverse flow was evaluated at $b = 3$ fm.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.165$ fm$^{-3}$, binding energy $E(\rho_0) = -16$ MeV, incompressibility $K(\rho_0) = 240$ MeV, symmetry energy $E_{sym} = 30$ MeV, effective pairing energy $-2$ MeV per nucleon at saturation for mass around 100, relative effective mass $m^{*}_r = 0.67$, neutron-proton effective mass splitting $m^{*}_{rn} - m^{*}_{rp} = 0.4\beta$, slope parameter $L$ in the range $55 \div 105$ MeV.
- Surface correction coefficients: $C_s = -11$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^2$ (MDI-MD), $C_s = 3$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^2$ (ZR-MD), $C_s = -7$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^2$ (MDI-MF).
- IMF production cross-section ratio $R = \sigma_{IMF}^{MDI-MD} / \sigma_{IMF}^{ZR-MD}$ decreases with energy up to 70 AMeV, then increases from 100 to 150 AMeV.
- Fission probability enhanced for MDI-MD compared to ZR-MD at incident energies 25 and 35 AMeV.
- Balance energy disappears for MDI-MD at $b = 3$ fm, with positive transverse flow slopes across the explored energy range, while ZR-MD shows a balance energy.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of many-body correlations in nuclear matter EoS from Papa (Ref. npa, comd) to finite heavy-ion collision observables, comparing CoMD results with mean-field approaches that share the same microscopic effective interactions (Wolt, QMD approaches). The $^{64}Ni+^{48}Ca$ system was chosen because it has been experimentally investigated (Ref. exp3). The results enable future studies of how wave-packet dynamics and the Pauli principle produce correlations that modify the effective interaction beyond the mean-field picture, with implications for interpreting fusion, fission, and IMF production data at Fermi energies.
