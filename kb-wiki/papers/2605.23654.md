# Probing nuclear interactions à la Rutherford: Insights on $^4$He from $α$ scattering

> Nuclear interactions play a key role for the stability of atomic nuclei and stellar environments. Successful parametrization and models of these interactions, developed in the last decades, accurately reproduce all the proton and neutron scattering data, besides the properties of few-body nuclear systems. However, recent electron scattering results focusing on the first excited resonant state of $^4$He nucleus, reveal a puzzling situation suggesting potential gaps in our understanding of the nuclear phenomenology. Here, we report a new study of such $^4$He resonance by $^4$He + $^4$He scattering featuring data of unprecedented sensitivity and state-of-art analyses of the spectral line shape together with a phenomenological reaction modeling that incorporates the same nuclear densities employed in electron-scattering studies. Our analysis of the full set of experimental observables yields a reasonable description within the framework of current nuclear-interaction physics, thereby highlighting the need for further advancing the modeling of few-body open quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a new study of the 0⁺₂ resonance in ⁴He using α-α scattering yields a reasonable description within current nuclear-interaction physics, but the authors hedge that this highlights the need for further advancing the modeling of few-body open quantum systems rather than resolving the puzzle definitively.

## Method

The authors performed elastic and inelastic α + α scattering experiments at 53 MeV incident beam energy using the MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer and the OSCAR array of solid-state detectors at INFN-LNS in Catania. They reconstructed the ⁴He excitation energy from the p + ³H and n + ³He breakup channels, which were accessed for the first time in hadronic experiments, and applied a Fano analysis to extract the resonance parameters. The experimental data were compared with coupled-channel calculations using ab-initio densities from four different nuclear Hamiltonians, including chiral effective field theory (EFT) and simplified Hamiltonians, folded with the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- Resonance centroid energy E_r = 0.50 ± 0.02 MeV above the proton-emission threshold S_p = 19.813 MeV, corresponding to excitation energy E_x = 20.31 ± 0.02 MeV
- Resonance width Γ_r = 0.29 ± 0.04 MeV
- Signal-to-background ratio of approximately 12 for the 0⁺₂ state in the p + ³H channel
- Target thickness of ⁴He was 2.5 ± 0.13 μg cm⁻² (3.7×10¹⁷ atoms cm⁻²) for the elastic scattering experiment and 1.3 ± 0.1 μg cm⁻² (1.9×10¹⁷ atoms cm⁻²) for the inelastic scattering experiment
- The ³P₁ resonance at 24.25 MeV (E_r = 4.437 MeV above S_p) contributes to the spectrum

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the ⁴He 0⁺₂ resonance that showed a large spread in measured energy and width, with variations of about 0.6 MeV for E_r and 0.7 MeV for Γ_r across experiments using electrons (Frosch 1968, Walcher 1970, Kobschall 1983, Kegel 2023), protons (Williams 1966), and α-particles (Grotowski 1969, Baur 1981). The results disagree with earlier α-scattering measurements by Baur (1981) that reported a width of 0.89 MeV, but agree with the latest electron scattering experiment by Kegel (2023) on the width. The work enables future improvements in ab-initio reaction theory for light nuclei, particularly the long-term goal of constructing an ab-initio optical potential, and demonstrates that combining α-scattering with electron-scattering tightens sensitivity to microscopic nuclear physics.
