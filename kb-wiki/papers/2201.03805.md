# Study of threshold anomaly in elastic scattering of $^{14}$N by $^{56}$Fe and $^{90}$Zr

> We use a phenomenological nucleus-nucleus optical potential constructed in the light of a potential developed by Ginocchio to study the elastic angular distributions of different nuclear systems near Coulomb barrier. The differential cross section ratios of elastic to Rutherford are studied for systems $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe and $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr for several colliding energies. Our theoretical predictions are well competitive with the experimental data, and the significantly small imaginary part of the potential explains the elastic scattering cross section and threshold anomaly in both systems.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a phenomenological optical potential based on the Ginocchio potential, with a significantly smaller imaginary part than used in prior work by Williams (1975), can reproduce elastic scattering angular distributions and simultaneously exhibit the threshold anomaly for the tightly-bound projectile systems $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe and $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr. The hedge is that the threshold anomaly is demonstrated through the energy variation of the potential parameters $V_B$ and $V_{BW}$, not through a full dispersion-relation analysis of the volume integrals.

## Method
The authors constructed a complex nucleus-nucleus optical potential based on the analytically solvable Ginocchio potential, with separate real and imaginary parts each having a piecewise form defined inside and outside a radial matching point $R_0$ (or $R_{0W}$). They solved the Schrodinger equation to obtain scattering amplitudes and computed differential cross-section ratios $\sigma_{el}/\sigma_{Ruth}$ for $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe at four center-of-mass energies from 22.4 to 32.0 MeV and for $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr at five energies from 31.2 to 43.3 MeV. The potential parameters were tuned to fit experimental data from Williams (1975), with six parameters held energy-independent and four parameters ($V_B$, $B_0$, $B_2$, $V_{BW}$ for the Fe system; $V_B$, $B_0$, $W_2$, $V_{BW}$ for the Zr system) allowed to vary with energy.

## Key numbers
- For $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe: the ratio of imaginary to real potential at r=0 ranges from 5.3% at $E_{CM}$=22.4 MeV to 7.6% at $E_{CM}$=32.0 MeV, compared to 30% to 78.57% in Williams (1975).
- For $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr: the ratio of imaginary to real potential at r=0 ranges from 7.3% at $E_{CM}$=43.3 MeV to 9.9% at $E_{CM}$=34.6 MeV, compared to 30.68% to 175.2% in Williams (1975).
- The Coulomb barrier for $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe is about 25 MeV ($E_{CM}$) using the Bass formula.
- The Coulomb barrier for $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr is about 36 MeV ($E_{CM}$) using the Bass formula.
- For $^{14}$N+$^{56}$Fe, the real part parameter $V_B$ varies from 2.8 MeV at $E_{CM}$=22.4 MeV to 3.8 MeV at $E_{CM}$=25.6 MeV, then falls to 2.1 MeV at $E_{CM}$=32.0 MeV.
- For $^{14}$N+$^{90}$Zr, the real part parameter $V_B$ varies from 2.46 MeV at $E_{CM}$=31.2 MeV to 4.0 MeV at $E_{CM}$=34.6 MeV, then falls to 2.2 MeV at $E_{CM}$=43.3 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the application of the Ginocchio-based optical potential, previously used by Mallick (2006) for $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si and $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg, to heavier target systems with a tightly-bound projectile. It directly contradicts the analysis of Williams (1975), who used Woods-Saxon potentials with large imaginary parts (30-80% of the real part) and did not observe threshold anomaly for these systems. The results enable future studies of threshold anomaly in other tightly-bound projectile systems using potentials with small imaginary parts, and suggest that the non-trivial shape of the potential near the matching radius $R_0$ is essential for reproducing oscillatory structure in angular distributions without adding derivative terms as done by Boztsun (2002).
