# Equation of state and magnetic susceptibility of spin polarized isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> Properties of spin polarized isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are studied within the framework of the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock formalism. The single-particle potentials of neutrons and protons with spin up and down are determined for several values of the neutron and proton spin polarizations and the asymmetry parameter. It is found an almost linear and symmetric variation of the single-particle potentials as increasing these parameters. An analytic parametrization of the total energy per particle as a function of the asymmetry and spin polarizations is constructed. This parametrization is employed to compute the magnetic susceptibility of nuclear matter for several values of the asymmetry from neutron to symmetric matter. The results show no indication of a ferromagnetic transition at any density for any asymmetry of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors find no indication of a ferromagnetic transition at any density for any isospin asymmetry from symmetric to pure neutron matter, based on Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the Nijmegen NSC97e interaction. This conclusion is conditional on the specific many-body approximation and interaction model employed, and the authors note that a spin polarized state becomes less favorable as density increases.

## Method

The authors extended the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory to spin polarized isospin asymmetric nuclear matter with four distinct fermionic components (neutrons and protons with spin up and down). They solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation using the Nijmegen NSC97e realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the continuous prescription for the single-particle potential, to compute G-matrices for all spin and isospin combinations. From these, they calculated single-particle potentials, total energy per particle, and constructed an analytic parametrization of the energy as a function of density, asymmetry parameter β, and neutron and proton spin polarizations Sn and Sp, which was then used to compute the magnetic susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle potentials were evaluated at density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ for several values of Sn, Sp, and β (β = 0 and β = 0.5 shown)
- Total energy per particle was calculated for densities up to ρ = 0.8 fm⁻³ for neutron, asymmetric, and symmetric matter
- The analytic parametrization reproduces BHF results with deviations of at most 7-8% for the largest combinations of β, Sn, and Sp tested (β = 0.25, Sn = 0.3, Sp = 0.4; β = 0.5, Sn = 0.5, Sp = 0.25; β = 0.8, Sn = 0.6, Sp = 0.2)
- The ratio det(1/χ)/det(1/χF) was computed as a function of density for several values of β from neutron matter (β = 1) to symmetric matter (β = 0)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarized neutron matter by Vidana et al. (2002) using the Nijmegen II and Reid93 interactions, and by Fantoni et al. (2001) using auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo with Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX potentials, to include the effects of proton admixture and isospin asymmetry. The results contradict earlier findings by Kutschera and Wojcik (1989) and Bernardos et al. (1995) who suggested that the presence of protons favors ferromagnetic instability, and instead support the conclusion of Pandharipande et al. (1972) that no ferromagnetic transition occurs at densities relevant for neutron stars. The analytic parametrization and magnetic susceptibility results enable improved modeling of neutrino transport in supernovae and proto-neutron star simulations.
