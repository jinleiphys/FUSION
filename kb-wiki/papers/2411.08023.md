# How neutron star properties disfavor a nuclear chiral density wave

> Cold and dense matter may break rotational symmetry spontaneously and thus form an anisotropic phase in the interior of neutron stars. We consider the concrete example of an anisotropic chiral condensate in the form of a chiral density wave. Employing a nucleon-meson model and taking into account fermionic vacuum fluctuations, we improve and extend previous results by imposing the conditions of electric charge neutrality and electroweak equilibrium, by allowing for a more general form of the vector meson self-interactions, and by including properties of pure neutron matter into the fit of the model parameters. We find that the conditions inside neutron stars postpone the onset of the chiral density wave to larger densities compared to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. While this still allows for the construction of stars with an anisotropic core, we find that the chiral density wave is energetically preferred only in a corner of the parameter space where matter is too soft to generate stars with realistic masses. Therefore, taking into account constraints from astrophysical data, our calculation predicts an isotropic neutron star core.

## Key claim

The chiral density wave (CDW) is energetically preferred only in a corner of the parameter space where matter is too soft to generate neutron stars with realistic masses; therefore, taking into account constraints from astrophysical data, the calculation predicts an isotropic neutron star core, although the conditions inside neutron stars postpone the onset of the CDW to larger densities compared to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employ a nucleon-meson model with nucleonic degrees of freedom interacting via scalar and vector meson exchange, including rho meson exchange and a general form of vector meson self-interactions. They impose electric charge neutrality and electroweak equilibrium, include electrons and muons, and fit model parameters to properties of both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density and pure neutron matter at half saturation density from chiral effective field theory. The CDW is implemented via an ansatz where the sigma and neutral pion condensates oscillate spatially, and the free energy is computed at zero temperature including fermionic vacuum fluctuations with a renormalization scheme that ensures boundedness of the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.153\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(E_0 = -16.3\ \text{MeV}\), giving a chemical potential for baryon onset of \(\mu_0 = 922.7\ \text{MeV}\)
- Asymmetry energy of nuclear matter at saturation: \(S = 32\ \text{MeV}\)
- Binding energy of pure neutron matter: \(E_0 = +10\ \text{MeV}\) at \(n_B = 0.5 n_0\)
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 140\ \text{MeV}\); pion decay constant: \(f_\pi \simeq 92\ \text{MeV}\)
- Vacuum nucleon mass: \(m_N = 939\ \text{MeV}\); Yukawa coupling \(g_\sigma \simeq 10.2\) from \(m_N = g_\sigma f_\pi\)
- Vector meson masses: \(m_\omega \simeq m_\rho \simeq 782\ \text{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility range considered: \(K \simeq (200-300)\ \text{MeV}\), narrowed to \(K \simeq (200-270)\ \text{MeV}\)
- Effective nucleon Dirac mass at saturation: \(M_0 \simeq (0.7-0.8) m_N\)
- Minimal renormalization scale parameter: \(c_0 = e^{-1-2\pi^2/g_\sigma^2}\)

## Context

This work extends the recent study of Pitsinigkos (2023) on the CDW in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter by imposing neutron star conditions (charge neutrality, beta equilibrium) and including rho meson exchange and a more general vector meson self-interaction, following the approach of Alford (2022) for fitting pure neutron matter properties. It contrasts with previous studies of the CDW in quark-meson or Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models (e.g., Nickel 2009, Carignano 2011, Buballa 2015) by using a nucleon-based model that respects low-density nuclear matter properties, and it improves on earlier neutron star studies with a CDW (e.g., Buballa 2015, Carignano 2015) by constructing stars from a single model that simultaneously describes nuclear matter and allows a chiral transition. The results enable future work to explore whether other anisotropic phases or more complicated spatial structures might survive under neutron star constraints.
