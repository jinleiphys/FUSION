# The role of noise and dissipation in the hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma

> We discuss the role of noise and dissipation in the explosive spinodal decomposition scenario of hadron production during the chiral transition after a high-energy heavy ion collision. We use a Langevin description inspired by nonequilibrium field theory to perform real-time lattice simulations of the behavior of the chiral fields. Preliminary results for the interplay between additive and multiplicative noise terms, as well as for non-Markovian corrections, are also presented.

# The role of noise and dissipation in the hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Dissipation and noise effects, even at conservative levels, can dramatically delay the spinodal decomposition phase conversion of the quark-gluon plasma into hadrons, potentially preventing the "explosive" hadronization scenario if the expansion time is comparable to the dissipation-induced delay. The paper argues that the simple phenomenological Langevin equation commonly used misses richer dissipative structures (multiplicative noise, density-dependent dissipation, non-Markovian memory) that all tend to further retard thermalization.

## Method

The author performs real-time lattice simulations of the chiral order parameter (modeled as a real scalar field in a Landau-Ginzburg effective potential from the linear sigma model) using a Langevin equation with additive Gaussian white noise and a dissipation coefficient η. For the improved analysis, a λφ⁴ theory in the broken phase is simulated with an extended Langevin equation containing both additive and multiplicative noise terms (ξ₁ + φ ξ₂) and a density-dependent dissipation term (η₁ + η₂ φ²)∂φ/∂t. Non-Markovian corrections are studied via a Caldeira-Leggett quantum mechanical analog with a systematic expansion in time derivatives regulated by a frequency cutoff Ω.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the chiral transition is T_c ∼ 150 MeV (from lattice QCD, cited as Laermann (2003)).
- The spinodal temperature used in simulations is T_sp ≈ 108 MeV.
- The spinodal point (inflection point of the effective potential) occurs at φ₀ ≈ 0.162 T.
- Lattice simulations use a 64³ cubic lattice with spacing a = 0.91 fm and time step Δt = 0.001/T.
- For η = 2 T, dissipation retards the evolution of the average chiral field toward its vacuum value by approximately 100% compared to the η = 0 case.
- Expansion times for RHIC collisions are of order 5 fm/c.
- In the improved Langevin simulations (λφ⁴ theory), parameters are T = 1, λ = 1, μ = 1, lattice extent L = 16, time step Δt = 10⁻³, and the spinodal point of the bare double-well potential is φ_sp = 1/√3.

## Context

This work extends the explosive spinodal decomposition scenario for hadronization proposed by earlier studies (e.g., Scavenius (2001), Csernai (1995), Paech (2003)) by incorporating genuine dissipative effects that were largely neglected in previous treatments. It builds on the author's prior work (Fraga and Krein, cited as FK1) that first showed dissipation could be dramatic even for conservative assumptions, and now adds multiplicative noise and non-Markovian memory effects. The paper enables future development of a full microscopic nonequilibrium field-theoretic framework with controlled approximations for memory kernels and colored noise, which would be applicable to the chiral transition in heavy-ion collisions including plasma expansion and finite-size effects.
