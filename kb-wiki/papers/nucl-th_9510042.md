# Quantum Algebraic Symmetries in Nuclei and Molecules

> Various applications of quantum algebraic techniques in nuclear and molecular physics are briefly reviewed. Emphasis is put in the study of the symmetries of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies, which underly the structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, the Bloch--Brink $α$-cluster model and possibly the shell structure in deformed atomic clusters.

## Key claim

This review argues that the symmetries of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies (RHO) are described by a deformed version of the u(2) Lie algebra, but the paper explicitly notes that while the classical case is clear, "the situation at the quantum level, even in the two-dimensional case, is not as simple."

## Method

The authors construct a deformed u(2) algebra for the two-dimensional anisotropic harmonic oscillator with frequency ratio m/n (m and n coprime natural numbers) by defining creation and annihilation operators and then building generators S_0, S_+, S_- and H that are integrals of motion. They use the concept of a generalized deformed oscillator to find finite-dimensional representation modules, requiring that the structure function vanishes at the boundaries of the Fock space to determine allowed energy eigenvalues and the constant u. The "angular momentum" eigenvalues are then obtained via a Lanczos algorithm, yielding roots of a generalized Hermite polynomial equation.

## Key numbers

- The energy eigenvalues of the 2D anisotropic harmonic oscillator with frequency ratio m/n are given by E = N + (2p-1)/(2m) + (2q-1)/(2n), where p=1,2,...,m, q=1,2,...,n, and N=0,1,2,...; each eigenvalue has degeneracy N+1.
- For each value of N there are m*n energy levels, each with degeneracy N+1.
- In the symmetric case (m/n = 1/1), the "angular momentum" eigenvalues are uniformly distributed and differ by 2; in the general case they are non-uniformly distributed.
- The commutator [S_-, S_+] is a polynomial of S_0 of order m+n-1, reducing to the ordinary u(2) algebra when m=1, n=1.

## Context

This work reviews and extends prior studies of the 2D and 3D anisotropic harmonic oscillator by Bonatsos and collaborators, building on earlier investigations of superintegrable systems (e.g., the 1:2 and 1:3 frequency ratio cases studied by other authors). The deformed algebra formalism is intended to provide a unified description of the symmetries underlying superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, the Bloch-Brink alpha-cluster model, and possibly deformed atomic clusters, with the extension to three dimensions noted as work receiving attention.
