# Modification of Hadron Structure and Properties in Medium

> In the quest to understand QCD there are a number of outstanding challenges. Here we focus on one of these, namely what one expects to happen to the structure of a hadron when it is immersed in a nuclear medium. We argue that the necessary changes in the quark structure are intimately related to nuclear binding and saturation. Some of the potential experimental implications of these ideas are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the structure of a hadron must change when it is immersed in a nuclear medium, and that these changes in quark structure are intimately related to nuclear binding and saturation, but the author hedges that the reliable calculation of such effects (e.g., quark exchange or hidden color configurations) presents a formidable theoretical challenge.

## Method
The paper reviews the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where valence quarks in a bound nucleon interact directly with scalar and vector meson mean fields in nuclear matter. The scalar field modifies the Dirac equation for confined quarks, enhancing the lower Dirac component of the 1s wave function as density rises, which causes the effective sigma-nucleon coupling to decrease with increasing density. The paper also discusses an extension using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which constructs nucleons and mesons from quarks in a covariant, quark-level description where the Faddeev wave function of the bound nucleon is self-consistently modified in-medium.

## Key numbers
- The mean scalar field in a heavy nucleus within Quantum Hadro-dynamics (QHD) is of order 0.5 GeV, more than half the mass of the nucleon itself.
- Meson cloud effects die fairly rapidly once current quark masses rise above about 50 MeV (or equivalently the pion mass above 0.4 GeV).
- The QMC model with just 3 parameters performed extremely well in a study of more than 200 phenomenological Skyrme forces, many with 10-20 parameters fitted to various nuclear data.
- The predicted isovector EMC effect accounts for more than 1 sigma of the nominal deviation from the Standard Model in the NuTeV anomaly.

## Context
This work extends the quark-meson coupling model developed by Guichon and colleagues (Guichon, 1987; Guichon, 1995; Saito, 2005) to explain nuclear saturation and predict modifications of hadron structure in medium. It contrasts with the earlier work of Jaffe (1976) on the H di-baryon by including meson cloud corrections that are suppressed as hadron size increases, following the approach of Mulders and Thomas (1982). The paper enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade, including measurements of the polarized EMC effect and the isovector EMC effect, as well as parity-violating deep inelastic scattering and charged current DIS at a future electron-ion collider.
