# Hypernuclear constraints on $ΛN$ and $ΛNN$ interactions

> Recent work on using density dependent $Λ$-nuclear optical potentials in calculations of $Λ$-hypernuclear binding energies is reviewed. It is found that all known $Λ$ binding energies in the mass range $16 \leq A \leq 208$ are well fitted in terms of two interaction parameters: one, attractive, for the spin-averaged $ΛN$ interaction and another one, repulsive, for the $ΛNN$ interaction. The $ΛN$ interaction term by itself overbinds $Λ$ hypernuclei, in quantitative agreement with recent findings obtained in EFT and Femtoscopy studies. The strength of the $ΛNN$ interaction term is compatible with values required to resolve the hyperon puzzle.

# Hypernuclear constraints on $\Lambda N$ and $\Lambda NN$ interactions

## Key claim

All known $\Lambda$ binding energies in hypernuclei with mass numbers $16 \leq A \leq 208$ can be well fitted using a density-dependent optical potential with only two interaction parameters, an attractive spin-averaged $\Lambda N$ term and a repulsive $\Lambda NN$ term, but only if the $\Lambda NN$ interaction is suppressed for pairs involving excess neutrons, reflecting an isospin dependence of the three-body force.

## Method

The authors constructed a density-dependent $\Lambda$-nucleus optical potential $V_{\Lambda}^{\rm opt}(\rho)$ consisting of a two-body $\Lambda N$ term $V_{\Lambda}^{(2)}(\rho)$ and a three-body $\Lambda NN$ term $V_{\Lambda}^{(3)}(\rho)$, with the two-body term including explicit Pauli correlations via a Fermi-momentum-dependent renormalization factor. They fitted the strength parameters $b_0$ (attractive) and $B_0$ (repulsive) to experimental $\Lambda$ single-particle binding energies, first using only $^{16}_{~\Lambda}$N data and then performing $\chi^2$ fits across the full data set of 21 binding energies from $^{16}_{~\Lambda}$N to $^{208}_{~~\Lambda}$Pb. To account for the isospin dependence of the $\Lambda NN$ interaction, they introduced a suppression factor $F = [(2Z)^2 + (N-Z)^2]/A^2$ that reduces the three-body term for nuclei with neutron excess.

## Key numbers

- Fitted strength parameters from $\chi^2$ fit to 21 binding energies: $b_0 = 1.32 \pm 0.071$ fm, $B_0 = 0.164 \pm 0.020$ fm, with 100% correlation between the two parameters.
- Partial potential depths at nuclear-matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ from the 21-point fit: $D_\Lambda^{(2)} = -37.4 \mp 0.7$ MeV (two-body attractive), $D_\Lambda^{(3)} = 9.8 \pm 1.2$ MeV (three-body repulsive), total $D_\Lambda = -27.6 \pm 0.5$ MeV.
- From the 14-point fit to $1s_\Lambda$ and $1p_\Lambda$ states only: $D_\Lambda^{(2)} = -38.6 \mp 0.8$ MeV, $D_\Lambda^{(3)} = 11.5 \pm 1.4$ MeV, total $D_\Lambda = -27.1 \pm 0.6$ MeV.
- The suppression factor $F$ reaches as low as 0.67 for Pb ($A=208$).
- The predicted difference in $B_\Lambda$ between $^{48}_{~\Lambda}$K and $^{40}_{~\Lambda}$K due to the suppression factor is about 2.5 MeV for the $1s_\Lambda$ state and more than 2 MeV for the $1p_\Lambda$ state.
- The $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom for the full 21-point fit is 0.95.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous density-dependent optical potential methodology (Friedman and Gal, 2023a,b) and updates the comprehensive data analysis of Millener, Dover, and Gal (1988), which originally gave a limiting $B_\Lambda(A\to\infty)$ of 28 MeV now revised to 30 MeV. The finding that the $\Lambda N$ interaction alone overbinds hypernuclei by about 10 MeV agrees quantitatively with recent EFT calculations (e.g., Hildenbrandt et al., 2020; Hildenbrandt, Meißner, and Nogga, 2023) and with femtoscopy studies (Mihaylov et al., 2024). The repulsive $\Lambda NN$ strength derived here ($D_\Lambda^{(3)} \approx 10$ MeV) is compatible with the value required by Gerstung, Kaiser, and Weise (2020) to resolve the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics. The isospin dependence of the $\Lambda NN$ term can be tested in the approved JLab E12-15-008 experiment on $^{40,48}$Ca targets (Nakamura, 2022).
