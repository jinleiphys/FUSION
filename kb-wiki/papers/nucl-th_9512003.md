# Microscopic Structure of High-Spin Vibrational Excitations in Superdeformed 190,192,194Hg

> Microscopic RPA calculations based on the cranked shell model are performed to investigate the quadrupole and octupole correlations for excited superdeformed bands in 190Hg, 192Hg, and 194Hg. The K=2 octupole vibrations are predicted to be the lowest excitation modes at zero rotational frequency. At finite frequency, however, the interplay between rotation and vibrations produces different effects depending on neutron number: The lowest octupole phonon is rotationally aligned in 190Hg, is crossed by the aligned two-quasiparticle bands in 192Hg, and retains the K=2 octupole vibrational character up to the highest frequency in 194Hg. The gamma vibrations are predicted to be higher in energy and less collective than the octupole vibrations. From a comparison with the experimental dynamic moments of inertia, a new interpretation of the observed excited bands invoking the K=2 octupole vibrations is proposed, which suggests those octupole vibrations may be prevalent in SD Hg nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the lowest octupole vibrational modes in superdeformed Hg isotopes are K=2 octupole vibrations, but their behavior at finite rotational frequency depends strongly on neutron number: the octupole phonon becomes rotationally aligned in 190Hg, is crossed by aligned two-quasiparticle bands in 192Hg, and retains its K=2 vibrational character up to the highest frequency in 194Hg.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations based on the cranked Nilsson shell model, with an additional term restoring local Galilean invariance to correct spurious velocity-dependent effects from the l^2 term. They solved the coupled RPA dispersion equations using separable residual interactions (quadrupole, octupole, monopole pairing, and isovector dipole operators) in doubly-stretched coordinates, with a phenomenological prescription for pairing gaps that avoids the unphysical sudden collapse predicted by BCS theory.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation epsilon = 0.44 used for all three isotopes (190,192,194Hg)
- Pairing gaps at zero frequency: Delta_n(0) = 0.8 MeV for neutrons, Delta_p(0) = 0.6 MeV for protons
- Critical frequencies for pairing: hbar*omega_c = 0.5 MeV (neutrons), 0.3 MeV (protons)
- Octupole coupling strength factor f_3 = 1.0 reproduces experimental routhians for the lowest octupole state in 190Hg
- Quadrupole coupling strength factors f_2 = 1.007 (190Hg), 1.005 (192Hg), 1.005 (194Hg)
- Alignment of the pi j_{15/2} (alpha=-1/2) orbit reduced from i approx 6.5 hbar to i approx 4 hbar by the Galilean invariance correction term
- Crossing between the K=2 octupole band and the negative-parity two-quasiparticle band nu(7_1 x [642 3/2])_{alpha=0} occurs at hbar*omega approx 0.27 MeV in 190Hg
- Excitation energies of K=2 octupole states at zero frequency: approximately 1 MeV (from text reference to previous work)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RPA calculations for superdeformed states in 193Hg (Nakatsukasa et al. 1993) and 152Dy (Nakatsukasa 1995), and provides a unified microscopic description of the different behaviors observed experimentally in excited superdeformed bands of 190,192,194Hg. The predictions differ from generator-coordinate-method calculations by Skalski (1993), which found K=0 octupole states as the lowest and significantly higher excitation energies (about 2 MeV). The results enable interpretation of experimental data from Crowell et al. (1994, 1995), Fallon et al. (1995), and Cederwall et al. (1995), and suggest that octupole vibrations may be more prevalent in superdeformed Hg nuclei than previously expected.
