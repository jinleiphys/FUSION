# Multi-quark states

> The pentaquark state recently discovered has been discussed based on various quark model calculations. Odd parity for the state can not be ruled out theoretically because contributions related to non-trivial color structures have not been studied completely. Other multiquark states, especially dibaryons, have been discussed also. A strangeness -3 N$Ω$ dibaryon has been shown to have a width as small as 12-22 keV and should be detectable in $Ω$ high productivity reactions such as at RHIC, COMPAS and the planned JHF and FAIR projects.

## Key claim

The pentaquark state Theta+ (mass ~1540 MeV) may have odd parity, contrary to the positive parity predicted by the chiral soliton model, because contributions from nontrivial color structures in multiquark systems have not been fully studied; the ground state parity cannot be definitively assigned until these color confinement effects are understood.

## Method

The authors review experimental claims for the Theta+ pentaquark from eleven groups using photon-nucleus, proton-nucleus, neutrino, and deep inelastic scattering reactions, and discuss theoretical quark model calculations including the chiral soliton model, the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model, the Goldstone boson exchange model, and their own quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM). They perform variational calculations of pentaquark masses using the QDCSM with channel coupling and color screening, and apply the same model to dibaryon candidates in the u,d,s three-flavor world using an adiabatic approximation.

## Key numbers

- Theta+ pentaquark mass ~1540 MeV, width Gamma < 25 MeV (from experimental claims)
- NA49 collaboration claimed anti-decuplet partner Xi-- of Theta+
- HERA-H1 collaboration claimed charm pentaquark Theta_c
- Authors' QDCSM calculation: I=0 S-wave KN channel gives Theta+ mass of 1615 MeV in adiabatic approximation
- Authors' three-body variational calculation with QDCSM: minimum around 1.3-1.4 GeV for color screening parameter mu = 1.0-0.8 fm^{-2}
- Nonstrange dibaryon d*: mass 2165-2186 MeV, width 5.76-7.92 MeV, with IJ^p = 03^+
- Strangeness -3 NOmega dibaryon: mass 2549 MeV, width 12-22 keV, with IJ^P = 1/2 2^+
- Broad NDelta and DeltaDelta resonances: widths 150-250 MeV in energy range 2.1-2.2 GeV

## Context

This paper reviews the status of multiquark states following the 2003 claims of pentaquark discovery, extending earlier work by Jaffe (1977) on tetraquarks and dibaryons, and by Simonov's group on the quark compound bag model. The authors' QDCSM approach builds on earlier work by Goldman, Ping, Wang and collaborators on baryon-baryon interactions, and provides a framework to study nontrivial color structures that are absent in conventional mesons and baryons. The narrow NOmega dibaryon predicted here could be searched for at RHIC, COMPASS, JHF and FAIR facilities, while the pentaquark parity question motivates lattice QCD calculations with interpolating field operators that include hidden color components.
