# Cronin Effect in Hadron Production off Nuclei

> Recent data from RHIC for high-$p_T$ hadrons in gold-gold collisions raised again the long standing problem of quantitatively understanding the Cronin effect, i.e. nuclear enhancement of high-$p_T$ hadrons due to multiple interactions in nuclear matter. In nucleus-nucleus collisions this effect has to be reliably calculated as baseline for a signal of new physics in high-$p_T$ hadron production. The only possibility to test models is to compare with available data for $pA$ collisions, however, all existing models for the Cronin effect rely on a fit to the data to be explained. We develop a phenomenological description based on the light-cone QCD-dipole approach which allows to explain available data without fitting to them and to provide predictions for $pA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC. We point out that the mechanism causing Cronin effect drastically changes between the energies of fixed target experiments and RHIC-LHC. High-$p_T$ hadrons are produced incoherently on different nucleons at low energies, whereas the production amplitudes interfere if the energy is sufficiently high.

## Key claim

The paper develops a parameter-free phenomenological description of the Cronin effect (nuclear enhancement of high-\(p_T\) hadrons) using the light-cone QCD-dipole approach, claiming to explain available data without fitting to them, but only under the condition that the mechanism changes drastically between fixed-target energies (short coherence length, incoherent production) and RHIC/LHC energies (long coherence length, coherent production).

## Method

The authors model hadron production in \(pA\) collisions using two limiting regimes based on the coherence length \(l_c = \sqrt{s}/(m_N k_T)\). For short coherence length (\(l_c \ll R_A\)), they use QCD factorization with a modified beam parton distribution that includes transverse momentum broadening from the light-cone dipole cross section and energy loss. For long coherence length (\(l_c \gg R_A\)), they employ a light-cone dipole formalism for gluon radiation from a projectile gluon interacting with the nucleus, including nonperturbative interactions and gluon shadowing corrections. At RHIC energies, where the coherence length is intermediate, they interpolate between the two regimes using a nuclear longitudinal formfactor.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length formula: \(l_c = \sqrt{s} / (m_N k_T)\), where \(k_T\) is the parton transverse momentum.
- Dipole cross section parameters from DIS data: \(\sigma_0 = 23.03\ \text{mb}\), \(\lambda = 0.288\), \(x_0 = 3.04 \times 10^{-4}\), with saturation scale \(Q_s(x) = 1\ \text{GeV} \times (x_0/x)^{\lambda/2}\).
- Energy loss rate used: \(dE/dz = -2.5\ \text{GeV/fm}\).
- Regularization masses for hard scattering: gluon mass \(m_G = 0.8\ \text{GeV}\), quark mass \(m_q = 0.2\ \text{GeV}\).
- Nonperturbative interaction parameter for gluon wave function: \(r_0 = 0.3\ \text{fm}\).
- Predictions for LHC at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\ \text{TeV}\) and RHIC at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\ \text{GeV}\) are shown as ratios of \(pA\) to \(pp\) cross sections as functions of \(p_T\).
- At RHIC, the coherence length is \(l_c \sim 5\ \text{fm}\) within the \(p_T\)-range where the Cronin effect is appreciable.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone QCD-dipole approach previously applied to parton broadening (JKT) and gluon radiation (KM, KST1, KST2) to provide the first parameter-free description of the Cronin effect, contrasting with earlier models that relied on fitting to data (Ochiai, Wang, Levai). The paper enables reliable baseline calculations for the Cronin effect in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC, which is necessary to extract signals of new physics (e.g., final-state medium effects) from high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression data.
