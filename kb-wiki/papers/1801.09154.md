# Universality of Generalized Parton Distributions in Light-Front Holographic QCD

> The structure of generalized parton distributions is determined from light-front holographic QCD up to a universal reparametrization function $w(x)$ which incorporates Regge behavior at small $x$ and inclusive counting rules at $x \to 1$. A simple ansatz for $w(x)$ which fulfills these physics constraints with a single-parameter results in precise descriptions of both the nucleon and the pion quark distribution functions in comparison with global fits. The analytic structure of the amplitudes leads to a connection with the Veneziano model and hence to a nontrivial connection with Regge theory and the hadron spectrum.

# Universality of Generalized Parton Distributions in Light-Front Holographic QCD

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for both nucleons and pions can be described by a single universal reparametrization function w(x) within light-front holographic QCD, but only up to the condition that this function incorporates both Regge behavior at small x and inclusive counting rules at x → 1. The single-parameter ansatz for w(x) yields precise descriptions of parton distribution functions (PDFs) when compared to global fits, though the pion PDF at large x shows tension with some prior analyses.

## Method

The authors start from the light-front holographic QCD expression for form factors in terms of the Euler Beta function, then use reparametrization invariance to express GPDs at zero skewness in terms of a universal function w(x) that satisfies boundary conditions w(0)=0 and w(1)=1. They impose a simple ansatz w(x) = x^(1-x) exp[-a(1-x)^2] that enforces Regge behavior at small x and Drell-Yan counting rules at large x, with a single flavor-independent parameter a determined from the first moment of the GPD. The nucleon PDFs are constructed as superpositions of twist-3 and twist-4 contributions with an SU(6) breaking parameter r, while the pion PDF uses twist-2 and twist-4 terms with a meson cloud parameter γ; all PDFs are then evolved via DGLAP equations to higher scales for comparison with global fit data.

## Key numbers

- The universal scale λ is fixed from the ρ meson mass: √λ = κ = m_ρ/√2 = 0.548 GeV
- The single parameter a = 0.531 ± 0.037, determined from the first moments A_v^u(0) = 0.261 ± 0.005 and A_v^d(0) = 0.109 ± 0.005
- The initial scale for nucleon PDF evolution is μ_0 = 1.06 ± 0.15 GeV in the MSbar scheme at NNLO
- The initial scale for pion PDF evolution is μ_0 = 1.1 ± 0.2 GeV at NLO
- The SU(6) breaking parameter r is set to the maximum value r = 3/2 (constrained by positivity)
- The higher Fock component probabilities for the nucleon Pauli form factors are γ_p = 0.27 and γ_n = 0.38
- The meson cloud parameter for the pion is γ = 0.125
- The strong coupling constant at the Z-boson mass scale is set to α_s = 0.1182
- Heavy quark thresholds use MSbar masses m_c = 1.28 GeV and m_b = 4.18 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of nucleon form factors in light-front holographic QCD (Sufian et al., 2016) by shifting form factor poles to their physical locations, which modifies the Regge trajectory slope and intercept while preserving exclusive counting rules. The approach provides a unified framework connecting the hadron spectrum from light-front holographic QCD with parton distributions, and the analytic structure of the amplitudes leads to a connection with the Veneziano model and Regge theory. The results enable further studies of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions and Wigner distributions using the effective light-front wave functions derived here, while the unresolved issue of the pion PDF falloff at large x motivates future work including nonzero quark masses.
