# Neutron orbital structure from generalized parton distributions of 3He

> The generalized parton distribution H and E of the 3He nucleus, which could be measured in hard exclusive processes, such as coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering, are thoroughly analyzed in impulse approximation, within the Av18 interaction. It is found that their sum is dominated to a large extent by the neutron contribution: The peculiar spin structure of 3He makes this target unique for the extraction of the neutron information. This observation could allow to access for the first time, in dedicated experiments, the orbital angular momentum of the partons in the neutron.

## Key claim

The sum of the generalized parton distributions H and E for 3He is dominated to a large extent by the neutron contribution at low momentum transfer, but the proton contribution grows with increasing momentum transfer, reaching about 30% at Δ² = -0.15 GeV², which limits the clean extraction of neutron information to the near-forward region.

## Method

The authors performed an impulse approximation calculation of the GPDs H and E for 3He using the Av18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, evaluating spin-dependent non-diagonal spectral functions from realistic 3He wave functions. They computed the combination H+E for 3He in the nuclear Breit-frame, using a model for nucleonic GPDs that fulfills the general properties of GPDs, and checked their results against the experimental magnetic form factor of 3He.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic dipole moment of 3He is μ₃ ≈ -2.13 μ_N, close to the neutron value μ_n ≈ -1.91 μ_N, while the deuteron has F₂²(0) ≈ -0.14 μ_N
- The proton contribution to the nuclear GPD H+E is a few percent in the forward limit, grows to 15% at Δ² = -0.1 GeV², and reaches 30% at Δ² = -0.15 GeV² (with ξ = 0.1)
- The calculation reproduces the magnetic form factor of 3He for -Δ² ≲ 0.2 GeV², with agreement lost at higher momentum transfer
- The minimum excitation energy E_min = |E_3He| - |E_2H| = 5.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the impulse approximation calculation of 3He GPDs from Rinaldi and Scopetta (2011) to include the helicity-flip GPD E, which is necessary for accessing parton orbital angular momentum via Ji's sum rule (Ji, 1997). The approach builds on the established use of 3He as an effective polarized neutron target in deep inelastic scattering (Friar, 1993) and enables future coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab to extract neutron GPDs, complementing incoherent DVCS measurements on deuterium targets.
