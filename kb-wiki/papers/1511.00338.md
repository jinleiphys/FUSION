# Pion and kaon in the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach

> In the present work the Mott effect for pions and kaons is described within a Beth- Uhlenbeck approach on the basis of the PNJL model. The contribution of these degrees of freedom to the thermodynamics is encoded in the temperature dependence of their phase shifts. A comparison with results from $N_f = 2 + 1$ lattice QCD thermodynamics is performed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for mesonic correlations in the PNJL model to include the strange sector, enabling a description of the Mott effect for both pions and kaons; however, the model shows a discrepancy with lattice QCD data for temperatures above approximately 150 MeV, which the authors attribute primarily to the chiral transition temperature in the PNJL model being too high (around 250 MeV) compared to the lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature of 154 ± 9 MeV.

## Method

The authors employ a 3-flavor PNJL model with a polynomial Polyakov-loop potential and compute the thermodynamic potential in Gaussian approximation with a "no sea" approximation that removes zero-point energy terms. The mesonic contribution to the pressure is obtained from the temperature-dependent phase shifts of pions and kaons via the relativistic Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism, where the phase shifts are determined from the imaginary part of the logarithm of the inverse meson propagator. The model parameters are fixed to vacuum observables (constituent quark mass 367 MeV, pion mass 135 MeV, pion decay constant 92.4 MeV) and the total pressure is compared to N_f = 2+1 lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014).

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark mass: 367 MeV, obtained with bare quark masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 138.6 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 602 MeV, and scalar coupling G_S * Lambda^2 = 2.317.
* Vacuum pion mass: 135 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* Model results agree with lattice QCD data below T approximately 150 MeV; above this temperature a discrepancy opens up.
* The chiral transition temperature in the PNJL model is approximately 250 MeV, compared to the lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV from Kaczmarek (2011).
* Phase shifts are computed for temperatures from T = 150 MeV to 400 MeV for both pions and kaons.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously developed for pions in the PNJL model by Blaschke (2013, 2014) to include kaons, enabling comparison with N_f = 2+1 lattice QCD thermodynamics from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014). The approach builds on earlier studies of mesonic correlations in the PNJL model by Hansen (2006), Blaschke (2007), Radzhabov (2010), Wergieluk (2012), and Yamazaki (2012, 2013). The results enable future improvements by including higher-lying hadronic states, lowering the Mott temperature, and adding virial corrections for residual quark-gluon interactions in the plasma.
