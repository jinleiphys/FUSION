# Implementing the three-neutron quantization condition

> We describe in detail the implementation of the relativistic three-neutron finite-volume quantization condition derived in Ref. [1]. In particular, we show how the complications due to Wigner rotations acting on spins are included, and present concrete formulas for the case when the angular momenta within pairs is restricted to be less than 2. We describe the symmetries of the matrices appearing in the quantization condition, and decompose solutions into irreducible representations of the appropriate doubled finite-volume symmetry groups. We present an implementation of the three-particle K matrix, keeping the two lowest-order terms in the threshold expansion. We provide numerical predictions for the finite-volume spectrum for a setup with nearly physical parameters, including two-particle interactions that are based on experimental results. This exploratory study shows the how lattice QCD calculations of the three-neutron spectrum with sufficient precision can provide detailed information on both two- and three-particle interactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a detailed, practical implementation of the relativistic three-neutron finite-volume quantization condition derived in Draper (2023), including the treatment of Wigner rotations on spins, and shows that lattice QCD calculations of the three-neutron spectrum with sufficient precision can provide detailed information on both two- and three-particle interactions, though the formalism is valid only for isosymmetric QCD without QED effects and within a restricted energy range.

## Method

The authors implement the relativistic field theory (RFT) quantization condition for three neutrons in a cubic box of side L with periodic boundary conditions, building on the formalism of Draper (2023). They construct the matrices F, G, K_{2,L}, and K_{df,3} with indices for spectator momentum k, pair angular momentum (l,m), and spin components defined in the dimer-axis frame, including Wigner rotation matrices D^{(1/2)} to handle spin transformations between different pair rest frames. The two-particle K matrix is parametrized using phase shifts with a smooth cutoff function H(k) that turns off subthreshold contributions before the left-hand cut from pion exchange, and the three-particle K matrix is kept at the two lowest-order terms in the threshold expansion.

## Key numbers

* The quantization condition is valid for center-of-mass frame energies E* in the range M_N + sqrt(4 M_N^2 - M_pi^2) < E* < 3 M_N + M_pi, where M_N is the neutron mass and M_pi the pion mass.
* The left-hand cut branch point occurs at sigma_lhc = 4 M_N^2 - M_pi^2, which sets the lower limit for the cutoff function.
* The cutoff function H(k) uses a smooth transition with parameter epsilon_H, with calculations performed for epsilon_H = 0 and epsilon_H = 1.
* The angular momentum truncation is applied with l_max = 1, restricting pair angular momenta to be less than 2.
* The spin index runs over 2^3 = 8 values for the three spin-1/2 neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the three-particle quantization condition formalism from Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) and the three-neutron derivation of Draper (2023) to a practical numerical implementation, addressing the complications of Wigner rotations on spin degrees of freedom that are absent in spinless systems. It enables future lattice QCD calculations of three-neutron spectra to extract two- and three-neutron interactions, which are crucial for understanding neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star properties, complementing experimental efforts where three-neutron interactions have no direct experimental input.
