# Extended Superscaling of Electron Scattering from Nuclei

> An extended study of scaling of the first and second kinds for inclusive electron scattering from nuclei is presented. Emphasis is placed on the transverse response in the kinematic region lying above the quasielastic peak. In particular, for the region in which electroproduction of resonances is expected to be important, approximate scaling of the second kind is observed and the modest breaking of it is shown probably to be due to the role played by an inelastic version of the usual scaling variable.

## Key claim

The paper extends the study of superscaling in inclusive electron scattering to the region above the quasielastic peak, finding that approximate scaling of the second kind persists even in the resonance region where electroproduction of baryon resonances is expected, with the modest breaking of this scaling probably attributable to an inelastic version of the usual scaling variable rather than to a fundamental failure of the scaling concept.

## Method

The authors first updated the determination of the effective Fermi momentum \(k_F\) and an empirical energy shift \(E_{shift}\) for each nucleus by fitting the total inclusive cross section data in the region below the quasielastic peak (\(\psi' < 0\)). They then assumed that the longitudinal scaling function \(f_L\) is universal (superscales) based on previous work, used this to subtract the longitudinal contribution from the total cross section, and thereby isolated the transverse scaling function \(f_T\) for each nucleus across a wide range of kinematics. Finally, they defined a ratio \(\rho_{12}\) of modified transverse scaling functions (using a dipole form factor and weighting by total mass number \(A\) rather than by proton and neutron numbers separately) to quantify the degree of second-kind scaling violation in the region above the quasielastic peak.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momenta \(k_F\) determined for 10 nuclei: Lithium 165 MeV/c, Carbon 228 MeV/c, Magnesium 230 MeV/c, Aluminum 236 MeV/c, Calcium 241 MeV/c, Iron 241 MeV/c, Nickel 245 MeV/c, Tin 245 MeV/c, Gold 245 MeV/c, Lead 248 MeV/c.
- Energy shifts \(E_{shift}\) range from 15 MeV (Lithium) to 31 MeV (Lead).
- The ratio \(f_{Au}/f_{C}\) at \(\psi' = +1\) for 4 GeV data at 55 degrees is about 1.2, indicating roughly 20% violation of second-kind scaling in the resonance region.
- The ratio \(\rho_{12}\) for gold/carbon at momentum transfers of about 1.2–1.7 GeV/c stabilizes at roughly 5–10% above unity, with typical uncertainties of \(\pm 2\%\).
- The factor \(\overline{\mu}^{2}_{12}\) for gold relative to carbon is approximately 1.08, accounting for about half of the observed 20% rise in the ratio of \(f_T\) values.

## Context

This work extends the earlier superscaling analyses of Donnelly et al. (1999, 1999a), which demonstrated excellent second-kind scaling below the quasielastic peak but significant violations above it. The present study focuses on the transverse response in the \(\psi'>0\) region, where resonance excitation and meson production dominate, and introduces a ratio method analogous to that used in EMC effect studies. The results enable future work to model the scale-breaking mechanisms, such as meson-exchange currents and 2p-2h contributions investigated by Amaro et al. (2001) and correlations studied by Van Orden (1981), and provide a foundation for extending superscaling analyses to inelastic processes.
