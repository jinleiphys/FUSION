# Implications of a matter-radius measurement for the structure of Carbon-22

> We study Borromean 2n-halo nuclei using effective field theory. We compute the universal scaling function that relates the mean-square matter radius of the 2n halo to dimensionless ratios of two- and three-body energies. We use the experimental value of the rms matter radius of 22C measured by Tanaka et al. to put constraints on its 2n separation energy and the 20C-n virtual energy. We also explore the consequences of these constraints for the existence of excited Efimov states in this nucleus. We find that, for 22C to have an rms matter radius within 1-sigma of the experimental value, the two-neutron separation energy of 22C needs to be below 100 keV. Consequently, this three-body halo system can have an excited Efimov state only if the 20C-n system has a resonance within 1 keV of the scattering threshold.

# Implications of a matter-radius measurement for the structure of Carbon-22

## Key claim

For 22C to have an rms matter radius within 1-sigma of the experimental value measured by Tanaka et al. (2010), the two-neutron separation energy must be below 100 keV; consequently, an excited Efimov state can exist in this three-body halo system only if the 20C-n system has a resonance within 1 keV of the scattering threshold.

## Method

The authors use leading-order Halo Effective Field Theory (Halo EFT) with zero-range interactions to describe 22C as a Borromean 2n-halo nucleus composed of a 20C core and two valence neutrons. They solve the Faddeev equations for the three-body bound state and compute the universal scaling function f that relates the dimensionless product mB⟨r²⟩ to the ratios of two-body energies (Enn/B, Enc/B) and the mass ratio A. The theoretical uncertainty is estimated from the largest of √(mEnn)/Λ₀, √(2mEnc)/Λ₀, or √(2mB)/Λ₀, where Λ₀ is set by the 20C rms radius.

## Key numbers

- The experimental rms matter radius of 22C measured by Tanaka et al. (2010) is 5.4 ± 0.9 fm.
- The 20C core rms radius used in the calculation is 2.98 ± 0.05 fm, taken from Ozawa et al. (2000).
- The nn scattering length used is ann = (−18.7 ± 0.6) fm, from Trotter et al. (1999).
- The upper limit on the two-neutron separation energy B of 22C is 100 keV, independent of the 20C-n virtual energy.
- For an excited Efimov state to exist, the 20C-n virtual energy must be within 1 keV of the scattering threshold.
- At the unitary limit (Enn = Enc = 0), the dimensionless function f(0,0;A) for A=20 is approximately 15% lower than the value reported by Yamashita et al. (2004).

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT approach previously applied to 2n-halo nuclei by Canham et al. (2008, 2009) and the three-body EFT framework of Bedaque et al. (1998). It directly addresses the experimental measurement of 22C by Tanaka et al. (2010) and provides model-independent constraints that differ from earlier analyses: the results are a factor of two more restrictive than the binding-energy limit found by Fortune and Sherr (2012), and correct an incorrect assumption in the subtraction method used by Yamashita et al. (2011). The constraints enable future experimental searches for the 20C-n virtual state and Efimov states in neutron-rich carbon isotopes.
