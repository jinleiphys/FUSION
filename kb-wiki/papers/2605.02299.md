# Gluon production in the Color Glass Condensate from BCFW recursion

> In the Color Glass Condensate framework, the colliding projectiles are described as classical color currents. Gluon production at leading order in the coupling is obtained from the retarded solution of the classical Yang-Mills equations, with these currents acting as sources. However, while the final gluon spectrum is gauge invariant, the classical color field $A^μ$ from which it is obtained is gauge dependent. This makes the intermediate steps of this approach unnecessarily complicated, as some effort is spent to also determine the gauge dependent part of $A^μ$, which is then discarded when one calculates a gauge invariant observable. In this work, we use a generalization of BCFW recursion applicable to off-shell gluons in order to calculate directly the gauge independent gluon production amplitude. This approach allows all manipulations to be performed in terms of gauge invariant amplitudes instead of gauge dependent Feynman diagrams, and therefore provides a gauge agnostic way to obtain the result.

## Key claim

This work uses a generalization of BCFW recursion, applicable to off-shell gluons, to calculate the gauge-independent gluon production amplitude in the Color Glass Condensate framework, bypassing the need to solve gauge-dependent classical Yang-Mills equations. The method provides a gauge-agnostic derivation that manipulates only gauge-invariant objects, though the final results are not new.

## Method

The author applies an extension of the Britto-Cachazo-Feng-Witten (BCFW) recursion method to tree-level amplitudes containing off-shell gluons attached to color sources, using the spinor-helicity formalism and color decomposition. The recursion starts from 3-point amplitudes (including those with one or two off-shell gluons) and builds up to the full gluon production amplitude in the dilute-dense approximation, where one source density is weak compared to the other. The approach replaces the traditional method of solving classical Yang-Mills equations in a fixed gauge with a recursion that directly yields gauge-invariant amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The 3-point on-shell amplitudes used as recursion starting points are given by the Parke-Taylor formula, which is a one-term expression for MHV amplitudes with an arbitrary number of external gluons.
- The eikonal propagator for the color source is given by $\frac{\delta_{ab}}{v\cdot p}$, where $v^\mu$ is the direction of motion of the fast-moving projectile.
- The eikonal vertex connecting a gluon to a source line carries a factor $-g f^{abc} v^\mu$.

## Context

This work extends on-shell methods developed for scattering amplitudes, particularly the BCFW recursion of Britto, Cachazo, Feng, and Witten (2005), to the Color Glass Condensate framework where gluons attached to color sources are off-shell. It builds on prior extensions by van Hameren (2014, 2012), Bury (2017, 2015), and Kutak (2016) for off-shell gluons. The approach enables a gauge-agnostic calculation of gluon production that competes favorably with traditional Yang-Mills solutions in light-cone gauge, while providing a proof of gauge invariance.
