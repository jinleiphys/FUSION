# Larmor precession of the octet baryons in view of the general QCD parametrization

> We apply general QCD parametrization to describe an octet baryon at rest in a static and uniform magnetic field, in order to demonstrate a dynamical implementation of the hadron model. The derived evolution has the asymptote of Larmor precession, governed by an effective magnetic dipole moment coupling. The parameters appear in the effective magnetic dipole moment has been studied in the original works in kinematic settings. Here we show that the parameters have actual dynamical meaning, and are indeed measured by the process.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the general QCD parametrization (GP) framework, previously used for kinematic correspondences, has actual dynamical meaning: the parameters governing the effective magnetic dipole moment of octet baryons are measured by the Larmor precession process in a static and uniform magnetic field, provided the evolution time satisfies \(t \gg \tau_{(N)}\) where \(\tau_{(N)}\) is the characteristic time scale set by the energy separation from other states.

## Method

The authors apply general QCD parametrization to describe an octet baryon at rest in a static and uniform magnetic field. They expand the time evolution operator in a Dyson series, separate contributions from degenerate spin states of a single baryon species from those of all other states, and show that for times much larger than \(\tau_{(N)} \equiv \max\{\hbar/|E_i - E_{2N}|\}_{i \neq 2N, 2N-1}\), the effective Hamiltonian governing the evolution becomes \(H_0 + H_\mathrm{Lar}\), where \(H_\mathrm{Lar}\) is the projection of the interaction Hamiltonian onto the degenerate subspace. Using the GP correspondence operator \(V\), they map the exact QCD evolution onto a model Hamiltonian acting on NRQM model states.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic time scale for the asymptotic regime is \(\tau_{(N)} \equiv \max\{\hbar/|E_i - E_{2N}|\}_{i \neq 2N, 2N-1}\), where \(E_{2N}\) is the energy of the \(N\)th octet baryon and \(E_i\) are energies of all other states.
- The magnetic dipole moment of the octet baryons is parameterized by 7 parameters \(\tilde{g}_1, \ldots, \tilde{g}_7\) in the GP framework, with \(\tilde{g}_1\) and \(\tilde{g}_2\) being much larger than the remaining parameters due to the hierarchy.
- In the 2-parameter approximation (keeping only \(\tilde{g}_1\) and \(\tilde{g}_2\)), the NRQM prediction is reproduced via \(\tilde{g}_1 = -3\gamma_d \hbar/2\) and \(\tilde{g}_2 = 3(\gamma_d - \gamma_s)\hbar/2\), where \(\gamma_d = -\gamma_u/2\) and \(\gamma_s\) are the gyromagnetic ratios for constituent quarks.
- The hierarchy of parameters in the mass formula shows each flavor-breaking projection \(P_i^s\) gives a reduction factor \(\simeq 0.3\), and each pair of different indices in the same sum (gluon exchange) yields a reduction factor \(\simeq 0.37\).

## Context

This work extends the general QCD parametrization framework developed by Morpurgo (1989, 1990) and Dillon (2009) from kinematic settings to dynamical processes. The GP framework had previously explained why the nonrelativistic quark model (NRQM) works well for hadron masses and magnetic moments despite the "spin crisis" from polarized scattering experiments (Jaffe, 1995). The paper demonstrates that the parameters in the GP magnetic moment formula have actual dynamical meaning as measured by Larmor precession, enabling future extraction of physical properties of hadrons from dynamical processes and allowing GP to make detailed, testable predictions.
