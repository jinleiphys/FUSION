# Effective interactions in the sd shell

> We perform a quantitative study of the microscopic effective shell-model interactions in the valence sd shell, obtained from modern nucleon-nucleon potentials, chiral N3LO, JISP16 and Daejeon16, using No-Core Shell-Model wave functions and the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation. We investigate the monopole properties of those interactions in comparison with the phenomenological universal sd-shell interaction, USDB. Theoretical binding energies and low-energy spectra of O isotopes and of selected sd-shell nuclei, are presented. We conclude that there is a noticeable improvement in the quality of the effective interaction when it is derived from the Daejeon16 potential. We show that its proton-neutron centroids are consistent with those from USDB. We then propose monopole modifications of the Daejeon16 centroids in order to provide an adjusted interaction yielding significantly improved agreement with the experiment. A spin-tensor decomposition of two-body effective interactions is applied in order to extract more information on the structure of the centroids and to understand the reason for deficiencies arising from our current theoretical approximations. The issue of the possible role of the three-nucleon forces is addressed.

## Key claim
The Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential yields a noticeable improvement in the quality of the microscopic effective shell-model interaction for the sd shell compared to the chiral N3LO and JISP16 potentials, but the derived single-particle energies are deficient (with inverted d5/2 and s1/2 orbitals and a spin-orbit splitting about twice the empirical value) because Daejeon16 was fitted only to nuclei with A ≤ 16; the authors therefore adopt empirical single-particle energies and propose monopole modifications of the Daejeon16 centroids to achieve significantly improved agreement with experiment.

## Method
The authors derived effective valence-space shell-model interactions for the sd shell by performing No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations for 16O, 17O, 17F, and 18F using three nucleon-nucleon potentials (chiral N3LO, JISP16, and Daejeon16) with Nmax=4 and ħΩ=14 MeV, then applying the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) transformation to obtain effective one- and two-body operators for the sd valence space. They compared the monopole properties, binding energies, and low-energy spectra of oxygen isotopes and selected sd-shell nuclei against the phenomenological USDB interaction, and performed a spin-tensor decomposition of the two-body effective interactions to analyze the structure of the centroids.

## Key numbers
- The Daejeon16 potential yields neutron single-particle energies for A=18 of ε(ν1s1/2) = -3.576 MeV, ε(ν0d5/2) = -3.302 MeV, and ε(ν0d3/2) = 6.675 MeV, showing inversion of the d5/2 and s1/2 orbitals and a spin-orbit splitting of about 10 MeV (almost two times larger than the empirical value).
- The phenomenological USDB single-particle energies used for comparison are ε(0d5/2) = -3.9257 MeV, ε(1s1/2) = -3.2079 MeV, and ε(0d3/2) = 2.1117 MeV.
- The rms deviations in relative binding energies for oxygen isotopes are 1.9 MeV for DJ16th and 2.9 MeV for JISP16th when using theoretical single-particle energies.
- The NCSM ground state energy of 18F from the OLS-transformed Daejeon16 potential at Nmax=4 is -130.407 MeV for the 3+1 state and -130.400 MeV for the 1+1 state.

## Context
This work extends the proof-of-concept study by Dikmen (2015), which demonstrated that effective valence sd-shell interactions from NCSM wave functions reproduce exact NCSM energies for 18F and 19F, by applying the approach to heavier sd-shell nuclei and comparing with experimental data and the phenomenological USDB interaction from Richter (2008). The study addresses the long-standing challenge, dating to Kuo and Brown (1966), of constructing microscopic effective interactions from bare nucleon-nucleon potentials, and the finding that the main deficiency of such interactions is their monopole part, as noted by Poves and Zuker (1981). The results enable future work to incorporate three-nucleon forces and larger model spaces to achieve the stronger A-dependence observed in phenomenologically successful valence Hamiltonians.
