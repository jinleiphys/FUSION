# Bridging Electromagnetic and Gravitational Form Factors: Insights from LFHQCD

> We propose an efficacious approach to derive the generalized parton distributions for the pion and proton, based upon prior knowledge of their respective parton distribution functions (PDFs). Our method leverages on integral representations of the electromagnetic form factors derived from the light-front holographic QCD (LFHQCD) formalism, coupled with PDFs computed from continuum Schwinger functional methods at the hadronic scale. Using these techniques, we calculate gravitational form factors and associated mass distributions for each hadron. Remarkably, our calculations yield results that closely match recent lattice QCD simulations conducted near the physical pion mass. This work not only deepens our understanding of hadronic structure but also highlights the efficacy of the LFHQCD approach in modeling fundamental properties of hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to derive generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness for the pion and proton solely from prior knowledge of their parton distribution functions (PDFs), using the light-front holographic QCD (LFHQCD) formalism. The hedge is that the method relies on solving an inverse problem to determine a profile function from the PDF, and the resulting gravitational form factors are predictions that match lattice QCD results only when the PDFs are computed at the hadronic scale (approximately 0.331 GeV).

## Method

The authors use the integral representation of electromagnetic form factors from LFHQCD as an Euler Beta function, then invert Eq. (4) to determine a profile function w(x) from the valence-quark PDFs of the pion and proton, which were computed using continuum Schwinger functional methods (CSMs) at the hadronic scale. With w(x) determined, the zero-skewness GPD is constructed as H(x,t) = q(x) [w(x)]^{-t/4λ}, where λ is fixed by the rho meson mass. The gravitational form factor A(t) is then obtained from the first Mellin moment of this GPD, and the charge and mass densities are computed via two-dimensional Fourier transforms.

## Key numbers

* The mass scale √λ = 0.548 GeV, fixed by the rho meson mass as m_ρ/√2.
* The hadronic scale ζ_H ≈ 0.331 GeV, associated with the process-independent running coupling in QCD.
* The physical pion mass used in the calculation is m_π = 0.139 GeV; the lattice QCD simulations used for comparison have m_π^{lat} ≈ 0.170 GeV.
* For the pion, the charge density is maximal at b_⊥^{max} = 0.152 fm and the mass density at b_⊥^{max} = 0.11 fm.
* For the proton, the charge density is maximal at b_⊥^{max} = 0.261 fm and the mass density at b_⊥^{max} = 0.213 fm.
* The squared ratio of mass radius to charge radius (r_M/r_C)^2 = 0.605 for the pion and 0.593 for the proton.

## Context

This work extends the LFHQCD approach of Brodsky (2008) and de Teramond (2018) by using PDFs from CSMs calculations by Ding (2019) for the pion and Chang (2022) for the proton, rather than assuming an arbitrary profile function. The results are compared directly with recent lattice QCD simulations by Hackett (2023) for both pion and proton gravitational form factors, which were performed near the physical pion mass. The method enables future calculations of spin-related form factors and parton distributions, including the proton's gravitational form factor J(t) via the helicity-flip GPD.
