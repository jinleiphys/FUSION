# Gluon bremsstrahlung in finite media beyond multiple soft scattering approximation

> We revisit the calculation of the medium-induced gluon spectrum in a finite QCD and develop a new approach that goes beyond multiple soft scattering approximation. We show by expanding around the harmonic oscillator that the first two orders encompass the two known analytic limits: single hard and multiple soft scattering regimes, valid at high and low frequencies, respectively. Finally, we investigate the sensitivity of our results to the infrared and observe that for large media the spectrum is weakly dependent on the infrared medium scale.

## Key claim
The paper develops a new analytic approach for the medium-induced gluon spectrum in a finite QCD medium that goes beyond the multiple soft scattering approximation by expanding around the harmonic oscillator, showing that the first two orders recover both the single hard and multiple soft scattering limits, valid at high and low frequencies respectively, and that for large media the spectrum is weakly dependent on the infrared medium scale.

## Method
The author expands the scattering potential around the harmonic oscillator (HO) by splitting it into a soft part (resummed to all orders, yielding the BDMPS-Z approximation) and a hard part (treated as a perturbation). The next-to-leading order (NLO) correction is computed by solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation for the perturbed Green's function, using the HO Green's function as the leading-order solution. The resulting NLO contribution is evaluated analytically for a brick-shaped medium with static scattering centers (Gyulassy-Wang model) and compared to the GLV and BDMPS limits.

## Key numbers
- The characteristic frequency for the multiple soft scattering regime is \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2} \hat q L^2\), where \(\hat q\) is the transport coefficient.
- For \(\omega \ll \omega_c\), the BDMPS spectrum scales as \(\omega \frac{dI}{d\omega} \simeq 2 \bar\alpha \sqrt{\frac{\omega_c}{2\omega}}\).
- For \(\omega \gg \omega_c\), the BDMPS spectrum scales as \(\omega \frac{dI}{d\omega} \simeq 2 \bar\alpha \frac{1}{12} \left(\frac{\omega_c}{\omega}\right)^2\).
- For \(\omega \ll \frac{1}{2} \mu^2 L\), the GLV spectrum scales as \(\omega \frac{dI_{GLV}}{d\omega} \simeq \bar\alpha \frac{\hat q_0 L}{\mu^2} \ln\left(\frac{\mu^2 L}{2\omega}\right)\).
- For \(\omega \gg \frac{1}{2} \mu^2 L\), the GLV spectrum scales as \(\omega \frac{dI_{GLV}}{d\omega} \simeq \bar\alpha \frac{\hat q_0 L}{\mu^2} \frac{\pi}{4} \left(\frac{\mu^2 L}{2\omega}\right)\).
- The NLO correction in the soft regime (\(\omega \to 0\)) scales as \(\omega^{-1/2}\), suppressed by one power of the Coulomb logarithm \(\hat q_0 / \hat q \sim \ln^{-1}(\sqrt{\omega \hat q}/\mu^2)\).
- In the UV limit (\(\omega \to \infty\)), the NLO correction reduces to \(\frac{\pi}{4} \frac{L^2}{\omega}\), matching the GLV high-frequency limit.

## Context
This work extends the BDMPS-Z (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff-Zakharov) multiple soft scattering approximation and the GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) single hard scattering approximation by providing a unified analytic formula. It is inspired by Molière scattering theory (Moliere, 1947) and builds on prior work by Wiedemann (2000) showing that the BDMPS formalism encompasses the single hard scattering limit, and by Salgado and Wiedemann (2003) on the ASW model. The approach enables more controlled theoretical uncertainties in phenomenological studies of jet quenching, particularly for Monte Carlo event generators (e.g., Schenke 2009, Zapp 2008) that iterate the elementary radiative process to generate parton cascades.
