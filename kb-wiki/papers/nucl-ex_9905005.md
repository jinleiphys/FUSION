# Strangeness and Antibaryon Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We review the experimental results on strangeness and antibaryon production in heavy-ion collisions at the AGS and SPS. We argue that the observed enhancement in kaon production at the AGS is consistent with hadronic description, while the multistrange baryon and antibaryon results at the SPS may need physics beyond hadronic nature. We call the need for measurements of low energy antilambda-proton annihilation cross-section in the modeling of antilambda to antiproton ratio; large value of this ratio is observed in central heavy-ion collisions at the AGS and SPS. We speculate the importance of an excitation function measurement of the ratio.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement in kaon production at the AGS is consistent with hadronic description, while the multistrange baryon and antibaryon results at the SPS may need physics beyond hadronic nature; however, subtle differences exist between the AGS kaon data and the hadronic RQMD model predictions.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on strangeness and antibaryon production from AGS and SPS heavy-ion experiments (E802/859/866, E864, E878, E917, NA49, WA97). It compares measured yields and particle ratios to predictions from the hadronic transport model RQMD, including versions with and without secondary meson-induced interactions and with the "rope" mechanism for string fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Kaon yields per participant in Au+Au at AGS (sqrt(s)=4.74 GeV) and Si+A (sqrt(s)=5.39 GeV) show steady increase with number of participants (N_p); enhancement factor over isospin-weighted NN interactions is higher in Au+Au central collisions than Si+A for both K+ and K-
- In Pb+Pb at SPS, a hierarchy of particle ratios is observed at midrapidity: Omega/Xi > Xi/Lambda > phi/K; antiparticle ratios are systematically larger than particle ratios
- RQMD overpredicts phi/K by a factor of 1.5-2 and underpredicts Omega/Xi by a factor of 2 in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS
- The (anti-Lambda + anti-Sigma0 + 1.1 anti-Sigma+)/anti-p ratio in Au+Au at AGS has a most probable value of 3.5 in the 10% most central collisions
- Anti-p yield in Au+Au at AGS saturates or slightly decreases in central collisions, while anti-Lambda yield continues to increase

## Context

This review extends the discussion of strangeness enhancement as a QGP signature, originally proposed by Eggers (1991) and Kapusta (1986), by comparing AGS and SPS data to hadronic model calculations. The work calls for measurements of low-energy anti-lambda-proton annihilation cross-sections, which are needed to model the anti-lambda/anti-proton ratio observed in central collisions at both AGS and SPS. An excitation function measurement of this ratio is proposed as a potential new avenue for QGP search, since QGP formation and strong medium absorption would give different energy dependences.
