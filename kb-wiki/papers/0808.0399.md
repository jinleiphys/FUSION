# Shear Viscosity of a Non-Relativistic Conformal Gas in Two Dimensions

> The shear viscosity, eta, of a fermi gas with non-relativistic conformal symmetry in two spatial dimensions is investigated. We find that eta/s, s being the entropy density, diverges as a gas of free particles in this system. It is in contrast to the eta/s=1/(4 pi) found using non-relativistic AdS/CFT correspondence, which requires a strongly interacting CFT. It implies the unitary fermi gas in two spatial dimensions is not likely to have a weakly interacting gravity dual.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) for a two-component non-relativistic Fermi gas in two spatial dimensions diverges when the two-body binding energy is tuned to zero, in contrast to the eta/s = 1/(4 pi) predicted by the non-relativistic AdS/CFT correspondence for a strongly interacting conformal field theory.

## Method

The authors used an effective field theory (EFT) approach with a contact interaction Lagrangian for two-component non-relativistic fermions, computing the leading-order two-body scattering amplitude through bubble diagrams in dimensional regularization. They analyzed the scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame for spatial dimensions d=1, 2, and 3, and extracted the behavior of the coupling constant and amplitude as the two-body binding energy B approaches zero.

## Key numbers

- In d=2, the scattering amplitude takes the form A = -(4 pi/M) * 1/(ln[B/E] + i pi/2), which goes to zero for B=0 and E>0, indicating no interactions.
- In d=3, the unitarity limit corresponds to C0 approaching infinity, while in d=2, the B=0 limit yields a free system with eta/s diverging to infinity.
- The eta/s ratio for a strongly interacting CFT from non-relativistic AdS/CFT is 1/(4 pi), which the authors contrast with their result of eta/s approaching infinity for the d=2 unitary Fermi gas.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of non-relativistic conformal field theories (NRCFT) and their potential gravity duals via the AdS/CFT correspondence, as developed by Son (2008), Balasubramanian and McGreevy (2008), and others. The authors demonstrate that the unitary Fermi gas in two spatial dimensions, previously studied by Landau, Nieto (2001), and Nishida and Son (2006), behaves as a free system when the binding energy vanishes, contradicting the expectation from the NR AdS/CFT correspondence that such systems might have a weakly interacting gravity dual. This result implies that finding a strongly interacting NRCFT in d=2 that could exhibit the NR AdS/CFT correspondence remains an open problem.
