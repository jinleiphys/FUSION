# Anomalous Properties of Quadrupole Collective States in $^{136}$Te and beyond

> The ground and low-lying states of neutron-rich exotic Te and Sn isotopes are studied in terms of the nuclear shell model by the same Hamiltonian used for the spherical-deformed shape phase transition of Ba isotopes, without any adjustment. An anomalously small value is obtained for $B(E2;0^+_1\to 2^+_1)$ in $^{136}$Te, consistently with a recent experiment. The levels of $^{136}$Te up to yrast $12^+$ are shown to be in agreement with observed ones. It is pointed out that $^{136}$Te can be an exceptionally suitable case for studying mixed-symmetry 1$^+$, 2$^+$ and 3$^+$ states, and predictions are made for energies, M1 and E2 properties. Systematic trends of structure of heavier and more exotic Sn and Te isotopes beyond $^{136}$Te are studied by Monte Carlo Shell Model, presenting an unusual and very slow evolution of collectivity/deformation.

## Key claim

The anomalously small B(E2;0⁺₁→2⁺₁) value in ¹³⁶Te is explained by a shell-model Hamiltonian, used without adjustment from its successful description of Ba isotopes, as arising from weak proton-neutron coupling that produces an asymmetric wave function dominated by a neutron D-pair rather than a coherent proton-neutron mixture.

## Method

The authors performed conventional shell-model diagonalization using the OXBASH code for ¹³⁶Te, and Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) calculations with pair bases for heavier Te and Sn isotopes. The Hamiltonian, taken from prior work on Ba isotopes, includes monopole and quadrupole pairing interactions and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, with single-particle energies taken from experimental levels of ¹³³Sb and ¹³³Sn.

## Key numbers

- Experimental B(E2;0⁺₁→2⁺₁) for ¹³⁶Te is 0.103(15) e²b², while the modified Grodzins' rule gives 0.44(8) e²b²
- Calculated B(E2;0⁺₁→2⁺₁) for ¹³⁶Te is 0.15 e²b², compared to 0.096 e²b² for ¹³⁴Te and 0.027 e²b² for ¹³⁴Sn
- The 2⁺₁ wave function of ¹³⁶Te has a 0.82 amplitude for |Dν × Sπ⟩ and 0.45 amplitude for |Sν × Dπ⟩
- The 2⁺₂ → 2⁺₁ M1 transition has B(M1) = 1.04 μn², while B(E2; 2⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) is 0.001 e²b²
- Calculated B(M1; 0⁺₁ → 1⁺₁) is 1.14 μn²
- Monopole pairing strength parameters: g⁽⁰⁾ = 0.21 MeV for protons, 0.13 MeV for neutrons

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach previously applied to the spherical-deformed shape phase transition in Ba isotopes (Otsuka et al., 2001) to the neutron-rich Te and Sn isotopes beyond N=82. It contradicts the empirical NpNn rule of Casten (1985) and the modified Grodzins' rule (Raman et al., 2001) for ¹³⁶Te, and provides a microscopic explanation for the anomalously small B(E2) value observed by Radford et al. (2002). The results enable experimental searches for mixed-symmetry 1⁺, 2⁺, and 3⁺ states in ¹³⁶Te, which the authors identify as an exceptionally favorable case for such studies, and predict a very slow evolution of collectivity in heavier Te isotopes that deviates from empirical systematics.
