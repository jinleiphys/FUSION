# Low-energy $^3$He($帢$,$帢$)$^3$He elastic scattering and the $^3$He($帢$,$帠$)$^7$Be reaction

> The cross sections of the $^3$He($帢$,$帢$)$^3$He and $^3$He($帢$,$帠$)$^7$Be reactions are studied at low energies using a simple two-body model in combination with a double-folding potential. At very low energies the capture cross section is dominated by direct s-wave capture. However, at energies of several MeV the d-wave contribution increases, and the theoretical capture cross section depends sensitively on the strength of the L=2 potential. Whereas the description of the L=2 elastic phase shift requires a relatively weak potential strength, recently measured capture data can only be described with a significantly enhanced L=2 potential. A simultaneous description of the new experimental capture data and the elastic phase shifts is not possible within this model. Because of the dominating extranuclear capture, this conclusion holds in general for most theoretical models.

## Key claim

A simultaneous description of the new experimental capture data for the $^3$He($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^7$Be reaction and the elastic phase shifts from $^3$He($\alpha$,$\alpha$)$^3$He scattering is not possible within a simple two-body model using a double-folding potential; because of the dominating extranuclear capture, this conclusion holds in general for most theoretical models.

## Method

The author used a direct capture model with a double-folding potential derived from measured charge density distributions, adjusting the potential strength parameters $\lambda$ and $\lambda_{LS}$ to reproduce experimental elastic scattering phase shifts for $s$-waves, $d$-waves, $p$-waves, and $f$-waves from the literature. The scattering wave functions were then used without further adjustment to calculate the $^3$He($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^7$Be capture cross section via the overlap integral of the scattering wave function, the electromagnetic operator (E1, E2, M1), and the bound state wave function, with spectroscopic factors taken from theory.

## Key numbers

- The standard calculation yields an extrapolated astrophysical S-factor at zero energy $S(0) = 0.53$ keV b and normalized derivative $S'(0)/S(0) = -0.73$ MeV$^{-1}$, in agreement with the compilation of Adelberger et al. (1998) which recommends $S(0) = 0.53 \pm 0.05$ keV b and $S'(0)/S(0) = -0.57$ MeV$^{-1}$.
- The potential strength parameter for even partial waves is $\lambda^{\rm{even}} = 1.452$ with $\lambda_{LS}^{\rm{even}} = 0$, while for odd partial waves $\lambda^{\rm{odd}} = 1.830$ and $\lambda_{LS}^{\rm{odd}} = -0.173$.
- The calculated radiation width for the $7/2^-$ resonance at 2983 keV is $\Gamma_{\gamma} = 37$ meV with resonance strength $\omega \gamma = 150$ meV, compared to the experimental result of $\omega \gamma = 330 \pm 210$ meV from Levkov (2009).
- The calculated width of the $7/2^-$ resonance is $\Gamma_{\rm{calc}} = 145$ keV, compared to the experimental width $\Gamma_{\rm{exp}} = 175 \pm 7$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier direct capture analysis of Mohr (1993) for the $^3$He($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^7$Be reaction and the mirror $^3$H($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^7$Li system, using new experimental capture data from Levkov (2009) that extend to higher energies up to about 3.2 MeV. The study contradicts the assumption that a simple two-body model can simultaneously describe both elastic scattering and capture data, and it enables future work to develop more sophisticated models that can resolve the discrepancy between the $L=2$ potential strength required by elastic scattering and that required by capture data.
