# Unified treatment for in-medium light and heavy clusters with RMF models

> It was shown that light nuclei such as $^4$He, $^8$Be, and $^{12}$C can be well described by RMF models, which enables a unified description for nuclei with baryon numbers $A\gtrsim4$. In this work, we propose a hybrid treatment for investigating the clustering phenomenon in nuclear medium, where clusters ranging from light nuclei (e.g., $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He) to heavy ones (e.g., $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb) can be treated in a unified manner. In particular, assuming a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell, the clusters are fixed by solving the Dirac equations imposing the Dirichlet-Neumann boundary condition, while the nuclear medium are treated with Thomas-Fermi approximation and take constant densities. In the presence of nuclear medium, the clusters eventually become unbound as density increases, while the root-mean-square charge radii increase. For clusters with different proton and neutron numbers $N_p \neq N_n$, their binding energies varies with the proton fraction of nuclear medium, which are less significant for clusters with $N_p = N_n$. The uncertainties of density functionals on the clustering phenomenon are investigated as well adopting 8 different functionals. Based on the obtained results, an analytical formula describing the binding energies of in-medium clusters is then obtained. The results presented in this work should be useful to understand the clustering phenomenon in both heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid treatment for in-medium clusters that can handle both light and heavy nuclei (from \(^3\)H to \(^{208}\)Pb) in a unified manner, but the approach assumes a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell with Dirichlet-Neumann boundary conditions for clusters and constant-density Thomas-Fermi approximation for the nuclear medium, which may be inappropriate for heavy clusters where crystal structures become favorable at vanishing temperatures.

## Method

Clusters are fixed by solving the Dirac equations with Dirichlet-Neumann boundary conditions inside a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell, while the surrounding nuclear medium is treated with the Thomas-Fermi approximation assuming constant densities. The microscopic center-of-mass correction including both direct and exchange terms is adopted. The binding energies of in-medium clusters are then fitted to a polynomial formula in terms of the gas density \(n_{\mathrm{gas}}\) and proton fraction \(Y_p\).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for \(^4\)He in vacuum predicted by DD-LZ1: \(-6.832\) MeV (experimental: \(-7.074\) MeV)
- Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{208}\)Pb in vacuum predicted by DD-LZ1: \(-7.871\) MeV (experimental: \(-7.867\) MeV)
- Charge radius for \(^{40}\)Ca predicted by DD-LZ1: \(3.482\) fm (experimental: \(3.478\) fm)
- Charge radius for \(^{208}\)Pb predicted by DD-LZ1: \(5.497\) fm (experimental: \(5.501\) fm)
- In-medium reduction of Coulomb energy per nucleon for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(2.920\) MeV
- The charge radius of \(^8\)Be grows significantly at \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} \approx 0.0095\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), where the cluster becomes unbound
- Binding energies of \(^3\)H decrease drastically at \(n_{\mathrm{gas}} \approx 0.0008\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for non-linear functionals NL3, PK1, and TM1

## Context

This work extends the generalized relativistic density-functional (gRDF) approach of Typel (2010) and the excluded volume mechanism of Lattimer (1991) and Shen (2011) by providing a unified treatment for clusters with baryon numbers up to \(A=208\), whereas previous approaches were restricted to light nuclei with \(A \leq 16\). The results enable improved modeling of clustering in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star crusts, and the analytical formula for in-medium binding energies can be incorporated into nuclear statistical equilibrium models.
