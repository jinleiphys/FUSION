# Folding-model approach to reaction cross section of $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV (published in Results in Physics)

> Tanihata {\it et al.} determined matter radii $r_{m}(σ_{\rm I})$ for $^{4,6,8}$He from interaction cross sections $σ_{\rm I}$ of $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon, using the optical limit of the Glauber model. Lu {\it et al.} determined proton radii $r_{p}({\rm AIS})$ for $^{4,6,8}$He with the atomic isotope shifts (AIS). We investigate whether the Love-Franey $t$-matrix folding model is good for $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

The Love-Franey $t$-matrix folding model, when scaled by a factor of $F=0.9356$ determined from $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering, reproduces the experimental interaction cross sections for $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon, provided that the nuclear densities are scaled to match independently measured proton radii from atomic isotope shifts and neutron radii derived from matter radii.

## Method

The authors applied the Love-Franey $t$-matrix folding model to calculate reaction cross sections for $^{4,6,8}$He+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon. They first determined a normalization factor $F = \sigma_R(\text{LF})/\sigma_I(\text{exp})$ from $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering at the same energy. For the He isotopes, they constructed proton and neutron densities from Gogny-D1S HFB calculations, then scaled these densities to match the proton radii $r_p(\text{AIS})$ from atomic isotope shift measurements by Lu et al. (2013) and neutron radii $r_n(\sigma_I)$ derived from the matter radii $r_m(\sigma_I)$ of Tanihata et al. (1988) combined with the proton radii.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering at 790 MeV per nucleon, the ratio $F = \sigma_R(\text{LF})/\sigma_I(\text{exp}) = 0.9356$, where $\sigma_I(\text{exp}) = 856$ mb
- Proton radii from atomic isotope shifts: $r_p(\text{AIS}) = 1.462(6)$ fm for $^{4}$He, $1.934(9)$ fm for $^{6}$He, $1.881(17)$ fm for $^{8}$He
- Matter radii from Tanihata et al.: $r_m(\sigma_I) = 1.57(4)$ fm for $^{4}$He, $2.48(3)$ fm for $^{6}$He, $2.52(3)$ fm for $^{8}$He
- Neutron radii derived for $^{6,8}$He: $r_n(\sigma_I) = 2.71$ fm for $^{6}$He, $2.70$ fm for $^{8}$He
- The authors' extracted matter radii from the folding model: $r_m(\text{exp}) = 1.462(6)$ fm for $^{4}$He, $2.48(3)$ fm for $^{6}$He, $2.53(3)$ fm for $^{8}$He

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tanihata et al. (1988) and Ozawa et al. (2001), who determined matter radii from interaction cross sections using the optical limit of the Glauber model, by testing an alternative theoretical framework. The Love-Franey $t$-matrix folding model provides a more microscopic approach than the Glauber model's optical limit, and its success here enables future extraction of nuclear radii from reaction cross section measurements with a different theoretical foundation, potentially improving reliability through cross-validation between models.
