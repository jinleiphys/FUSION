# A dynamical model of surrogate reactions

> A new dynamical model is developed to describe the whole process of surrogate reactions; transfer of several nucleons at an initial stage, thermal equilibration of residues leading to washing out of shell effects and decay of populated compound nuclei are treated in a unified framework. Multi-dimensional Langevin equations are employed to describe time-evolution of collective coordinates with a time-dependent potential energy surface corresponding to different stages of surrogate reactions. The new model is capable of calculating spin distributions of the compound nuclei, one of the most important quantity in the surrogate technique. Furthermore, various observables of surrogate reactions can be calculated, e.g., energy and angular distribution of ejectile, and mass distributions of fission fragments. These features are important to assess validity of the proposed model itself, to understand mechanisms of the surrogate reactions and to determine unknown parameters of the model. It is found that spin distributions of compound nuclei produced in $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{240*}$U and $^{18}$O+$^{236}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{238*}$U reactions are equivalent and much less than 10$\hbar$, therefore satisfy conditions proposed by Chiba and Iwamoto (PRC 81, 044604(2010)) if they are used as a pair in the surrogate ratio method.

## Key claim

The authors develop a unified dynamical model for surrogate reactions that can calculate spin distributions of compound nuclei, and they find that the spin distributions for the two reactions $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{240*}$U and $^{18}$O+$^{236}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{238*}$U are equivalent and much less than 10$\hbar$, thereby satisfying the conditions proposed by Chiba and Iwamoto (2010) for the surrogate ratio method to work, but only if these reactions are used as a pair.

## Method

The model employs multi-dimensional Langevin equations to describe the time-evolution of collective coordinates (distance between centers, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) on a time-dependent potential energy surface that switches from a diabatic to an adiabatic form and changes from the total system mass ($^{256}$Fm) to the compound nucleus mass ($^{240}$U) as the reaction proceeds from the transfer stage to the decay stage. The nucleon transfer process is treated with a Langevin equation for the mass asymmetry parameter, and after the mono-nucleus state is reached, the full multi-dimensional Langevin equations with wall-and-window one-body dissipation and a hydrodynamical inertia tensor are used. The sliding friction parameter is calibrated by comparing calculated fission fragment mass distributions to experimental data for the $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U reaction at $E_{c.m.}=133.5$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The sliding friction value $\gamma_{tan}=5 \times 10^{-22}$ MeV s fm$^{-2}$ reproduces the experimental fission fragment mass distribution for $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U at $E_{c.m.}=133.5$ MeV very well.
- The spin distributions of compound nuclei produced in $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{240*}$U and $^{18}$O+$^{236}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{238*}$U reactions are much less than 10$\hbar$.
- The incident energy used for the spin distribution calculations of the $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{240}$U reaction is $E_{c.m.}=160$ MeV.
- The relaxation time for the diabatic-to-adiabatic potential transition is $\tau=10^{-21}$ sec, and the relaxation time for the neck parameter is $\tau_{\epsilon}=10^{-20}$ sec.
- The shell damping energy $E_d$ is chosen as 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the unified model of Zagrebaev and Greiner (2005, 2007) by introducing procedures to connect the transfer and decay stages of surrogate reactions, including switching potential energy surfaces between systems of very different mass numbers and adding temperature-dependent shell correction energy. The model enables testing the sufficient conditions for the surrogate ratio method proposed by Chiba and Iwamoto (2010), which previously assumed the existence of equivalent spin distributions without a dynamical calculation. The results suggest that the $^{18}$O+$^{238}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{240}$U and $^{18}$O+$^{236}$U $\rightarrow ^{16}$O+$^{238}$U reactions can be used as a pair in the surrogate ratio method, pending verification of the weak Weisskopf-Ewing condition by Hauser-Feshbach theory.
