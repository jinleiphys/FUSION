# Versatility of field theory motivated nuclear effective Lagrangian approach

> We analyze the results for infinite nuclear and neutron matter using the standard relativistic mean field model and its recent effective field theory motivated generalization. For the first time, we show quantitatively that the inclusion in the effective theory of vector meson self-interactions and scalar-vector cross-interactions explains naturally the recent experimental observations of the softness of the nuclear equation of state, without losing the advantages of the standard relativistic model for finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows quantitatively that the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model, specifically the G2 parameter set, naturally explains the experimentally observed softness of the nuclear equation of state at high densities without sacrificing the good description of finite nuclei achieved by standard RMF models, provided that vector meson self-interactions and scalar-vector cross-interactions are included.

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state (EOS) for infinite symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at zero temperature using both the standard RMF model (with parameter sets NL1 and NL3) and the E-RMF model (with parameter sets G1 and G2). They compared the resulting pressure-density relations to experimental constraints derived from elliptic and transverse flow observables in heavy-ion collisions, and also calculated neutron star masses and radii via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation and critical temperatures for the liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility of NL1 is K∞ = 212 MeV, yet its EOS is stiff at higher densities and does not follow the experimental trend.
- Neutron star radius predicted by G2: R = 10.08 km; by NL3: R = 15.47 km; experimental range: 10–12 km.
- Neutron star mass ratio predicted by G2: M/M⊙ = 2.05; by NL3: M/M⊙ = 2.78; experimental range: 1.5–2.5.
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter: G2 gives Tc = 14.2 MeV; NL3 gives Tc = 14.3 MeV; experimental range: 13–20 MeV.
- Critical temperature for asymmetric matter (asymmetry α = 0.2): G2 gives Tc = 13.7 MeV; NL3 gives Tc = 13.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the E-RMF approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (1996) by demonstrating its applicability to dense matter, contradicting the stiff EOS predictions of standard RMF parametrizations such as NL1 (Reinhard 1986) and NL3 (Lalazissis 1997). The results enable a unified description of finite nuclei and nuclear matter, including neutron star properties, and are consistent with the microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and the variational calculations of Akmal et al. using the Argonne v18 interaction.
