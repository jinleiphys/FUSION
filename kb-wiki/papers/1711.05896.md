# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors with non-local chiral effective Lagrangian

> The relativistic version of finite-range-regularisation is proposed. The covariant regulator is generated from the nonlocal Lagrangian. This nonlocal interaction is gauge invariant and is applied to study the nucleon electromagnetic form factors at momentum transfer up to 2 GeV$^2$. Both octet and decuplet intermediate states are included in the one loop calculation. Using a dipole regulator with $Λ$ around 0.85 GeV, the obtained form factors, electromagnetic radii as well as the ratios of the form factors are all comparable with the experimental data. This successful application of chiral effective Lagrangian to relatively large momentum transfer make it possible to further investigation of hadron quantities at high $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The authors propose a relativistic version of finite-range-regularisation (FRR) using a nonlocal, gauge-invariant chiral Lagrangian, and show that with a dipole regulator parameter Λ around 0.85 GeV, the nucleon electromagnetic form factors can be described up to momentum transfer Q² = 2 GeV², which is a much larger range than traditional chiral perturbation theory (valid only below Q² ≈ 0.1 GeV²).

## Method

The covariant regulator is generated from a nonlocal Lagrangian constructed by introducing a correlation function and a gauge link to preserve gauge invariance. One-loop calculations are performed including both octet and decuplet baryon intermediate states, with the dipole regulator \(\tilde{F}(k) = 1/(1 - k^2/\Lambda^2)^2\). The low-energy constant \(c_1\) is fitted to the experimental neutron magnetic moment (\(F_2^n(0) = -1.91\)), and the regulator parameter Λ is the only free parameter, varied to match the empirical nucleon form factor data.

## Key numbers

* Dipole regulator parameter Λ = 0.85 GeV yields calculated proton magnetic moment μ_p = 2.78 (experimental: 2.79) and neutron magnetic moment μ_n = -1.91 (experimental: -1.91).
* Calculated proton charge radius \(r_{Ep} = 0.857\) fm (experimental: 0.847 fm) and proton magnetic radius \(r_{Mp} = 0.848\) fm (experimental: 0.836 fm) for Λ = 0.85 GeV.
* Calculated neutron magnetic radius \(r_{Mn} = 0.867\) fm (experimental: 0.889 fm) and neutron charge radius squared \(r_{En}^2 = -0.077\) fm² (experimental: -0.113 fm²) for Λ = 0.85 GeV.
* Loop diagrams contribute approximately 25%–30% to the proton electromagnetic form factors and neutron magnetic form factor, with 70%–75% from tree-level contribution.
* The tree-level contribution to the proton magnetic moment at Q² = 0 is 2.11, and the loop contribution is 0.67, summing to 2.78.

## Context

This work extends the finite-range-regularisation approach, previously applied in non-relativistic heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (e.g., Young (2002), Leinweber (2003), Wang (2007)), to a relativistic framework that can reach Q² up to 2 GeV². Traditional chiral perturbation theory (Fuchs (2003)) is only valid for Q² < 0.1 GeV², and even with vector mesons included (Kubis (2000)) it only reaches Q² < 0.4 GeV². This method uses only two parameters (\(c_1\) and Λ) compared to six parameters in traditional ChPT, and enables future investigation of hadron quantities at high Q².
