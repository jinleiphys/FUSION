# Nuclear modification of forward $J/ψ$ production in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC

> We re-evaluate the nuclear suppression of forward $J/ψ$ production at high energy in the Color Glass Condensate framework. We use the collinear approximation for the projectile proton probed at large $x$ and an up to date dipole cross section fitted to HERA data to describe the target in proton-proton collisions. We show that using the Glauber approach to generalize the proton dipole cross section to the case of a nucleus target leads to a nuclear modification factor much closer to LHC data than previous estimates using the same framework.

# Nuclear modification of forward \(J/\psi\) production in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
Using the Glauber approach to generalize the proton dipole cross section to a nucleus target (rather than simply scaling the initial saturation scale by \(A^{1/3}\)) yields a nuclear modification factor \(R_\text{pA}\) much closer to LHC data than previous Color Glass Condensate estimates, though the predictions still slightly underestimate the measured ratio, especially at low transverse momentum.

## Method
The authors compute forward \(J/\psi\) production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV using the Color Glass Condensate hybrid model, where the projectile proton is treated with collinear parton distribution functions (MSTW 2008 LO) and the target is described by a dipole cross section evolved via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. The initial condition for the proton dipole is the MV\(^e\) parametrization fitted to HERA DIS data; for the nucleus, the dipole is obtained via a Glauber approach using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density, with explicit impact-parameter integration. Hadronization of the \(c\bar{c}\) pair into a \(J/\psi\) is treated using the color evaporation model.

## Key numbers
- The initial saturation scale squared for the proton dipole is \(Q_{s0}^2 = 0.060\) GeV\(^2\), with parameters \(C^2 = 7.2\), \(e_c = 18.9\), and \(\sigma_0/2 = 16.36\) mb, obtained by fitting HERA DIS data for \(Q^2 < 50\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x < 0.01\).
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters are \(d = 0.54\) fm and \(R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3})\) fm.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_\text{pPb}\) is computed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV as a function of rapidity \(Y\) and transverse momentum \(P_\perp\).
- The uncertainty band corresponds to varying the charm quark mass between 1.2 and 1.5 GeV and the factorization scale between \(M_\perp/2\) and \(2M_\perp\), where \(M_\perp = \sqrt{M^2 + P_\perp^2}\).

## Context
This work extends previous CGC calculations of forward \(J/\psi\) production by Fujii et al. (2013), which predicted a nuclear suppression much stronger than later LHC measurements. The improvement is motivated by the observation in single inclusive light hadron production by Lappi and Mantysaari (2013) that earlier CGC disagreements with LHC data were largely due to nuclear geometry effects. The results enable more reliable CGC-based predictions for quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions and can be refined by including more sophisticated hadronization treatments, such as non-relativistic QCD as used by Ma et al. (2015).
