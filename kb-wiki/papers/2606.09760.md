# Einstein-de Haas effect and induced rotation in an evolving magnetized QCD matter

> The Einstein-de Haas (EdH) effect describes the emergence of collective rotation driven by spin alignment under an external magnetic field. We investigate this effect in a dynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using a quasiparticle model (QPM). We compute the EdH-induced angular velocity $ω_{\mathrm{EdH}}$ as a function of temperature, proper time, and fireball radius. Our results show that $ω_{\mathrm{EdH}}$ grows with proper time and is consequently suppressed at higher temperatures. Near the QGP crossover temperature, $ω_{\mathrm{EdH}}$ attains a substantial, non-negligible magnitude. We identify a nontrivial crossing between the strong and weak magnetic field regimes that reflects the competition between spin alignment and the energy required to sustain orbital motion. This nontrivial crossing temperature separates a spin-dominated regime from an inertia-dominated regime of magnetic field-induced rotation. These findings establish the EdH effect as a manifestation of angular momentum conservation in magnetized QCD matter.

# Einstein-de Haas effect and induced rotation in an evolving magnetized QCD matter

## Key claim

The Einstein-de Haas effect generates a collective rotation in a dynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma with angular velocities comparable to typical vorticity inferred from hyperon polarization measurements, but only under the condition that the system is near the QCD crossover temperature and the magnetic field decay timescale coincides with the cooling timescale. The effect exhibits a nontrivial crossing temperature that separates a spin-dominated regime from an inertia-dominated regime, meaning stronger magnetic fields do not necessarily produce stronger vorticity generation.

## Method

The authors employ a quasiparticle model (QPM) treating quarks and gluons as massive particles with thermal masses that incorporate running coupling effects in a magnetic field, using Landau quantization and explicit Zeeman splitting for quarks. They assume boost-invariant longitudinal Bjorken expansion with temperature cooling T(tau) = T0 (tau0/tau)^(1/3) and an exponential decay of the magnetic field eB(tau) = eB0 exp(-tau/tau_B). The spin density is computed from the spin-only contribution to the pressure derivative with respect to the magnetic field, and the induced EdH angular velocity is obtained from total angular momentum conservation as omega_EdH = -S_z / (kappa epsilon R^2).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 350 MeV at thermalization time tau0 = 0.5 fm
- Deconfinement critical temperature Tc = 0.155 GeV
- Two initial magnetic field strengths: eB0 = m_pi^2 and eB0 = 3m_pi^2, with pion mass m_pi = 0.140 GeV
- Magnetic field decay constant tau_B = 5 +/- 2 fm
- Fireball radius R = 5 fm and geometric inertia factor kappa = 0.5
- Induced angular velocities omega_EdH in the range 0.05 to 2 MeV
- Crossing temperature between strong and weak field regimes at T approximately 0.28 GeV for mu_B = 0.1 GeV
- Bag constant B_vac = 0.0028 GeV^4
- Bare quark masses: m_u0 = 0.0023 GeV, m_d0 = 0.0048 GeV, m_s0 = 0.095 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the Einstein-de Haas effect in a static equilibrated hadron gas (Sahu 2026) to the dynamically expanding QGP phase, complementing their earlier investigation of the reciprocal Barnett effect in rotating QCD matter (Sahu 2025). The results establish that the EdH effect operates in both the partonic and hadronic phases of the fireball, and the predicted omega_EdH proportional to R^(-2) scaling offers a distinct experimental signature that can discriminate EdH rotation from conventional hydrodynamic vorticity. The findings enable future spin-hydrodynamic treatments and provide concrete phenomenological signatures for experimental measurements at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA.
