# Is Large Mixing Angle MSW the Solution of the Solar Neutrino Problems?

> Recent results on solar neutrinos provide hints that the LMA MSW solution could be correct. We perform accurate calculations for potential `smoking-gun' effects of the LMA solution in the SuperKamiokande solar neutrino experiment, including: (1) an almost constant reduction of the standard recoil electron energy spectrum (with a weak, < 10%, relative increase below 6.5 MeV); (2) an integrated difference in day-night rates (2% to 14%); (3) an approximately constant zenith-angle dependence of the nighttime event rate; (4) a new test for the difference in the shape of the equally-normalized day-night energy spectra (~ 1%); and (5) annual variations of the signal due to the regeneration effect (~ 6 times smaller than the integrated day-night effect). We also establish a relation between the integrated day-night asymmetry and the seasonal asymmetry due to LMA regeneration. As a cautionary example, we simulate the effect of an absolute energy calibration error on the shape (distortion) of the recoil energy spectrum. We compare LMA predictions with SuperKamiokande data and discuss the possibilities for distinguishing experimentally between LMA and vacuum oscillations.

## Key claim

The LMA MSW solution could be correct for solar neutrinos, but the paper emphasizes that the evidence is only suggestive: the observed day-night asymmetry is only about 1.6 sigma after 708 days of SuperKamiokande data, and the spectral distortion predicted by LMA is small enough that it could be mimicked by a 2-sigma energy calibration error or by an enhanced hep neutrino flux.

## Method

The authors performed accurate calculations of the LMA MSW solution's predictions for SuperKamiokande, convolving the neutrino survival probability with the 8B neutrino flux from the BP98 standard solar model, the differential cross-sections for electron and muon neutrino scattering, and the SuperKamiokande energy resolution function. They computed the recoil electron energy spectrum distortion, the day-night asymmetry integrated over all zenith angles, the zenith-angle dependence of the nighttime event rate, a new day-night spectrum test with equal normalization, and seasonal variations due to the regeneration effect. They also simulated the effect of an absolute energy calibration error on the apparent spectral shape.

## Key numbers

- Predicted integrated day-night asymmetry for LMA solutions: A_n-d = 0.02 to 0.14 (2% to 14%)
- Measured day-night asymmetry by SuperKamiokande after 708 days: A_n-d = 0.060 ± 0.036(stat) ± 0.008(syst)
- Best-fit oscillation parameters from combined fit of total rates and day-night asymmetry: Δm² = 2.7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², sin²2θ = 0.76, giving A_n-d = 8%
- Predicted LMA suppression of 8B neutrino rate at SuperKamiokande: Rate(measured, 8B) = 0.474 × Rate(BP98, 8B)
- Predicted range of capture rates for LMA solutions: ³⁷Cl = 2.9 ± 0.3 SNU, ⁷¹Ga = 71 ± 9 SNU
- For the day-night spectrum test with equal normalization, the average daytime spectrum between 5 MeV and 10 MeV is about 1.5% greater than the average nighttime spectrum for Δm² = 2 × 10⁻⁵ eV² and sin²2θ = 0.8
- The weak relative increase in the recoil electron spectrum below 6.5 MeV is less than 10%
- The SuperKamiokande energy calibration error is estimated as 0.8% at 10 MeV, i.e., δ = 80 keV (1 sigma); a 2-sigma (160 keV) error can produce a significant pseudo-slope
- The seasonal variation due to LMA regeneration is about 6 times smaller than the integrated day-night effect

## Context

This paper extends the authors' previous work (Bahcall, Krastev, Smirnov 1998) on global oscillation solutions to solar neutrino problems, focusing specifically on the LMA MSW solution. It provides detailed predictions for SuperKamiokande that can distinguish LMA from the SMA MSW solution and vacuum oscillations. The work enables future experimental tests: the day-night spectrum test is a new diagnostic, and the authors note that approximately 10 years of SuperKamiokande data would be needed to clearly observe the predicted low-energy spectral upturn. The paper also relates the LMA mass scale (about 10⁻² eV) to the atmospheric neutrino mass scale suggested by SuperKamiokande (Fukuda 1998).
