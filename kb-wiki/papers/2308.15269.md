# Simulation of 3+1D glasma in Milne coordinates I: Development of the framework

> We propose a new numerical method for $3+1$D glasma simulation using Milne coordinates. We formulate the classical Yang-Mills field and $3$D classical color current on a lattice at the initial proper time, specified as a moment just before the collision of the two nuclei. By solving the evolution equations, we extract observables of the $3$D glasma at later times. We demonstrate the efficiency of our method in terms of numerical cost and apply it to the central collisions of Au-Au. We also discuss possible further improvements of our method.

## Key claim
The authors propose a new numerical method for 3+1D glasma simulation using modified Milne coordinates, where the initial proper time is set just before the two nuclei collide (rather than at the collision moment), and the classical color current is treated as a 3D dynamical object that evolves via continuity equations. The method is claimed to be more numerically efficient than Minkowski-coordinate approaches because the Milne coordinate lattice does not need to be large enough to contain the outgoing nuclei.

## Method
The classical Yang-Mills field and 3D classical color current are discretized on a lattice in modified Milne coordinates at an initial proper time when the two nuclei are still separated. The initial gauge fields are constructed from Wilson lines of the color charge densities of each nucleus, and the initial currents are given by gauge-transformed color charge densities. The system is evolved using discretized equations of motion for the gauge fields and continuity equations for the currents, with the leap-frog method. The results are then transformed to usual Milne coordinates via a general coordinate transformation.

## Key numbers
- The simulation uses SU(2) gauge group on a lattice of size \(L^2_\perp \times L_{\tilde{\eta}}\).
- The initial proper time \(\tilde{\tau}_{\rm ini}\) is set sufficiently small such that the two nuclei do not overlap, requiring \(\tau_{\rm ini}/\sqrt{2} < x_{\rm c} - R/(\gamma\sqrt{2})\).
- The color charge density correlation uses a saturation scale \(Q_s\) and a 2D normal distribution with variance \(\sigma_\perp\) as an ultraviolet cutoff.
- An infrared cutoff \(m\) is introduced by multiplying the color charge density in momentum space by \(k^2_{{\rm lat},\perp}/(m^2 + k^2_{{\rm lat},\perp})\).
- The simulation parameters are chosen to be consistent with those in the prior work of 3DglasmaSS (2022), and the system is applied to central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the boost-invariant (2+1D) glasma description based on the McLerran-Venugopalan model to a full 3+1D simulation that includes rapidity dependence and the recoil effect of the nuclei. It is similar in spirit to the approach of 3DglasmaSS (2022), which performed simulations in Minkowski coordinates, but the authors claim their Milne-coordinate method requires less numerical resources because the lattice does not need to accommodate the outgoing nuclei in the longitudinal direction. The method enables future studies of glasma observables across a broader range of rapidity and can provide initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma.
