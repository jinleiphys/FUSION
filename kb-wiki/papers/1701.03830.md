# Revealing long-range multi-particle collectivity in small collision systems via subevent cumulants

> Multi-particle azimuthal cumulants, often used to study collective flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, have recently been applied in small collision systems such as $pp$ and $p$+A to extract the second-order azimuthal harmonic flow $v_2$. Recent observation of four-, six- and eight-particle cumulants with "correct sign" $c_2\{4\}<0, c_2\{6\}>0, c_2\{8\}<0$ and approximate equality of the inferred single-particle harmonic flow, $v_2\{4\}\approx v_2\{6\}\approx v_2\{8\}$, have been used as strong evidence for a collective emission of all soft particles produced in the collisions. We show that these relations in principle could be violated due to the non-Gaussianity in the event-by-event fluctuation of flow and/or non-flow. Furthermore, we show, using $pp$ events generated with the PYTHIA model, that $c_2\{2k\}$ obtained with standard cumulant method are dominated by non-flow from dijets. An alternative cumulant method based on two or more $η$-separated subevents is proposed to suppress the dijet contribution. The new method is shown to be able to recover a flow signal as low as 4\% imposed on the PYTHIA events, independently of how the event activity class is defined. Therefore the subevent cumulant method offers a more robust way of studying collectivity based on the existence of long-range azimuthal correlations between multiple distinct $η$ ranges. The prospect of using the subevent cumulants to study collective flow in A+A collisions, in particular its longitudinal dynamics, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the "correct sign" pattern of multi-particle cumulants (c₂{4}<0, c₂{6}>0, c₂{8}<0) and the approximate equality of v₂{4}≈v₂{6}≈v₂{8} are neither necessary nor sufficient evidence for collective flow in small collision systems, because these relations can be violated by non-Gaussian fluctuations of flow or non-flow, and can also be reproduced by non-flow correlations from dijets.

## Method

The authors generated 200 million proton-proton collisions at √s=13 TeV using the PYTHIA8 event generator, which contains non-flow correlations from jets and dijets but no genuine long-range collective flow. They calculated multi-particle azimuthal cumulants using the standard method and compared them with a proposed alternative method where particles are divided into two or three subevents separated in pseudorapidity (η). The subevent cumulants were constructed by requiring that the four particles come from different η ranges, thereby suppressing short-range dijet correlations. The performance of the subevent method was tested by embedding a known flow signal (as low as 4%) into the PYTHIA events using a flow afterburner and checking whether the method could recover the input v₂ value.

## Key numbers

- 200 million pp collisions at √s=13 TeV were generated with PYTHIA8 for the simulation.
- The three-subevent method was shown to recover a flow signal as low as 4% imposed on PYTHIA events.
- The statistical power of the two-subevent method is reduced by a factor of 2.7 compared to the standard method (M⁴/64 vs. M⁴/24 unique quadruplets), and the three-subevent method loses a factor of 2.3 (M⁴/54 vs. M⁴/24).
- For a two-component distribution with a=1/3, the cumulants take the values c_n{4}= (1/9)v₀⁴, c_n{6}= -(2/9)v₀⁶, and c_n{8}= (71/9)v₀⁸, demonstrating that the "correct sign" pattern can arise from non-flow.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of multi-particle cumulants in small collision systems, following observations by ATLAS (2016) that the sign-change point and magnitude of v₂{4} depend on how event classes are defined. The paper contradicts the interpretation in earlier work by Aad (2013), Khachatryan (2015), and Khachatryan (2016) that the "correct sign" pattern of cumulants is strong evidence for collective flow. The subevent cumulant method builds on an idea proposed by Adler (2002) for dividing particles into subevents, but repurposes it to suppress non-flow rather than to circumvent nested-loop calculations. The method enables future studies of longitudinal dynamics of collectivity in A+A collisions and provides a more robust way to isolate genuine long-range azimuthal correlations in small systems.
