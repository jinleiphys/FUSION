# Simple formula for leading $SU(3)$ irreducible representation for nucleons in an oscillator shell

> Applications of rotational $SU(3)$ symmetry in nuclei, using Elliott's $SU(3)$ or pseudo-$SU(3)$ or proxy-$SU(3)$ model, often need just the lowest or leading $SU(3)$ irreducible representation (irrep) $(λ_H, μ_H)$. For nucleons in an oscillator shell $η$, with ${\cal N}=(η+1)(η+2)/2$, we have the algebra $U(r{\cal N}) \supset [U({\cal N}) \supset SU(3)] \otimes SU(r)$; $r=2$ when there are only valence protons or neutrons and $r=4$ for nucleons with isospin $T$. Presented in this paper is a simple general formula for the leading $SU(3)$ irrep $(λ_H, μ_H)$ in any given irrep $\{f\}$ of $U({\cal N})$. Results are provided for $(λ_H, μ_H)$ irreps for $η$ values of interest in nuclei and for this for all allowed particle numbers. These results clearly show that prolate shape dominates over oblate shape in the shell model $SU(3)$ description.

# Simple formula for leading $SU(3)$ irreducible representation for nucleons in an oscillator shell

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple general formula for the leading $SU(3)$ irreducible representation $(\lambda_H, \mu_H)$ in any given irrep $\{f\}$ of $U(\mathcal{N})$, where $\mathcal{N}=(\eta+1)(\eta+2)/2$ for an oscillator shell $\eta$, applicable to both identical nucleons ($r=2$) and nucleons with isospin ($r=4$). The formula eliminates the need for detailed computer codes, though the author notes that such codes already exist and can generate the full reduction including all irreps with multiplicities.

## Method

The author derives a closed-form expression for $(\lambda_H, \mu_H)$ by summing over single-particle oscillator orbits labeled by $(n_z, n_x, n_y)$ quantum numbers, weighted by the occupation numbers $f_i$ from the $U(\mathcal{N})$ irrep $\{f\}$. For identical nucleons, the $U(\mathcal{N})$ irreps are $\{2^p\}$ for even particle number $m$ with spin $S=0$ and $\{2^p,1\}$ for odd $m$ with $S=1/2$. For proton-neutron systems with good spin-isospin $SU(4)$ symmetry, the $U(\mathcal{N})$ irrep $\{f\}$ is obtained as the conjugate of the lowest $U(4)$ irrep determined by particle number $m$ and isospin $T$, using well-known formulas for the $U(4)$ irreps.

## Key numbers

* For identical nucleons, prolate-to-oblate transition occurs at particle numbers $m=8$ for $\eta=2$, $m=15$ for $\eta=3$, $m=23$ for $\eta=4$, $m=34$ for $\eta=5$, and $m=47$ for $\eta=6$; these $m$ values are higher than the mid-shell values (6, 10, 15, 21, 28 respectively), breaking particle-hole symmetry.
* For proton-neutron systems with $SU(4)$ symmetry, prolate-to-oblate transition occurs at $m=16$ for $\eta=2$, $m=29$ for $\eta=3$, and $m=46$ for $\eta=4$.
* For heavy nuclei in the proxy-$SU(3)$ model with valence protons in the 50-82 shell ($\eta=4$) and neutrons in the 82-126 shell ($\eta=5$), oblate shape ($\lambda_H < \mu_H$) appears for $^{190,192,194}$W, $^{192,194,196}$Os, and $^{194,196,198}$Pt.
* The example nucleus $^{72}$Kr is discussed: with a $^{56}$Ni core and valence nucleons in $\eta=2$ (pseudo-$SU(3)$), the ground state is oblate; using proxy-$SU(3)$ with $\eta=3$ gives prolate shape, consistent with experimental indications of prolate with some oblate mixing.

## Context

This work extends the Elliott $SU(3)$ model (Elliott, 1958), pseudo-$SU(3)$ model (Hecht and Draayer, 1970s), and proxy-$SU(3)$ scheme (Bonatsos et al., 2010s) by providing a simple analytic formula for the leading $SU(3)$ irrep that previously required computer codes (Kota, 1990s; Draayer et al., 1990s). The formula enables rapid determination of prolate versus oblate shape dominance across nuclear chart regions, including heavy nuclei where protons and neutrons occupy different oscillator shells. The results enable systematic studies of prolate-oblate transitions in Ba to Pt isotopes and N=Z nuclei, with detailed experimental comparison deferred to a future publication. The formula is also noted to be applicable to interacting boson models IBM-2, IBM-3, and IBM-4.
