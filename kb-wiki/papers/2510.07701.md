# Gamow shell model calculations for the Thomas-Ehrman shift in new isotopes 21Al

> Proton-rich nuclei beyond the proton drip line exhibit unique phenomena, such as the Thomas-Ehrman shift (TES), providing valuable insights into nuclear stability and isospin symmetry breaking. The discovery of the lightest new isotope, 21Al, situated beyond the proton drip line, was recently reported in the experiment. In this study, we employ the Gamow shell model (GSM) to explore the TES in mirror pairs 21Al/21O, focusing on how this phenomenon affects the energy levels of these nuclei. Our calculations describe the ground state energies and reveal significant TES with large mirror energy differences in the excited mirror 1/2+ states in 21Al/21O. The large mirror energy difference is primarily due to the significant occupation of weakly bound or unbound s1/2 orbitals, resulting in the extended radial density distributions, and variations in Coulomb energy and nuclear interaction contributions between the mirror states. Additionally, the low-lying states of 21Al are also calculated with the GSM in coupled-channel (GSM-CC) representation, Furthermore, we also predict the cross-section of 20Mg(p, p) scattering, which serves as another candidate approach to study the unbound structure of 21Al in the experiment, offering a theoretical framework for studying the structure and reaction dynamics of 21Al in future experiments.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations reproduce the experimentally observed Thomas-Ehrman shift in the mirror pair 21Al/21O, with the large mirror energy difference in the excited 1/2+ states primarily attributed to the significant occupation of weakly bound or unbound s1/2 orbitals, which produce extended radial density distributions and variations in Coulomb and nuclear interaction contributions between the mirror states.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM) with a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonance, and complex-energy scattering states, using 16O as an inert core with valence nucleons occupying s1/2, p1/2,3/2, and d3/2,5/2 partial waves. The core-valence interaction was modeled via a Woods-Saxon potential, while the nucleon-nucleon interaction among valence nucleons was described by effective field theory (EFT) forces. The GSM in coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) was additionally used to calculate the 20Mg(p, p) scattering cross section as an alternative probe of the unbound structure of 21Al.

## Key numbers

* Ground state of 21Al: GSM calculation gives 1.03 MeV above the 20Mg ground state, in agreement with the experimentally measured value of 1.15 MeV (PhysRevC.110.L031301).
* Mirror energy difference (MED) for the 1/2+ state in 21Al/21O: GSM calculation gives -760 keV, compared to the experimental value of -870 keV.
* Width of the 1/2+ state in 21Al: GSM predicts 126 keV.
* Two-proton separation energy (S2p) for 22Si: GSM calculation gives 172 keV (bound ground state), compared to indirect mass measurements giving -108 (125) keV and -104 (201) keV from AME2003.
* Excitation energy of the 1/2+ state in 21Al: GSM gives 0.61 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.35 MeV.
* Excitation energy of the 1/2+ state in 21O: GSM gives 1.37 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.22 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous GSM studies of the Thomas-Ehrman shift in proton-dripline nuclei, including analyses of 16Ne and 18Mg (PhysRevC.103.044319), the A=16 mirror pairs 16F/16N and 16Ne/16C (PhysRevC.108.064316), and oxygen isotope mirror nuclei (GSM_MED). The calculations build on the recent experimental discovery of 21Al (PhysRevC.110.L031301) and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting its structure and reaction dynamics, including predictions for 20Mg(p, p) scattering that could guide future experiments.
