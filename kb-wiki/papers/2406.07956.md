# Ab initio calculations with a new local chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon force

> Ab initio calculations have achieved remarkable success in nuclear structure studies. Numerous works highlight the pivotal role of three-body forces in nuclear ab initio calculations. Concurrently, efforts have been made to replicate these calculations using only realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions. A novel local chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) NN interaction, distinct due to its weaker tensor force, has recently been established. This paper applies this local NN interaction in ab initio frameworks to calculate the low-lying spectra of p-shell light nuclei, particularly 10B, ground-state energies and shell evolution in oxygen isotopes. Results are compared with calculations utilizing nonlocal chiral N3LO NN and chiral NN +3N interactions. The ab initio calculations with the local N N potential accurately describe the spectra of p-shell nuclei, notably the 10B. Additionally, the neutron drip line for oxygen isotopes, with 24O as the drip line nucleus, is accurately reproduced in ab initio calculations with the local NN interaction. Calculations with the local NN interaction also reproduce the subshell closure at N = 14 and 16, albeit with a stronger shell gap compared to experimental data. However, the calculated charge radii based on the local NN interaction are underestimated compared with experimental data, which is similar to results from the nonlocal NN interaction. Consequently, the present ab initio calculations further indicate significant spin-orbit splitting effects with the new local NN potential, suggesting that 3N forces remain an important consideration.

## Key claim

The new local chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, which has a weaker tensor force than typical NN potentials, can reproduce the correct ordering of low-lying states in p-shell nuclei (notably 10B) and the neutron drip line of oxygen isotopes at 24O using only NN forces, without explicit three-nucleon (3N) interactions. However, the calculated charge radii are underestimated compared to experimental data, and the spin-orbit splitting effects are significantly larger than those from nonlocal NN interactions, indicating that 3N forces remain important for a complete description.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for light p-shell nuclei (A=6-12) and valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations for oxygen isotopes, using the local chiral N3LO NN potential with cutoff combination (Rπ, Rct) = (1.0, 0.70) fm and a regulator value of R = 1.0 fm with Vlow-k = 2.4 fm−1. For comparison, they also performed calculations with a nonlocal chiral N3LO NN potential softened by similarity renormalization group evolution with λSRG = 2.4 fm−1, and compared results to previous NCSM calculations with NN+3N interactions from Navratil (2007). NCSM results were extrapolated to infinite model space using an exponential form, while VS-IMSRG calculations used an ensemble normal-ordering technique with the Hamiltonian projected to the sd valence space above an 16O core.

## Key numbers

- The optimal harmonic oscillator frequency for local potential NCSM calculations was ℏω = 26 MeV for 10B, ℏω = 22 MeV for A=6-9 nuclei (except 9Li), ℏω = 24 MeV for 9Li and 10Be, and ℏω = 24 MeV for nonlocal calculations of 6Li and 10Be
- For VS-IMSRG calculations, ℏω = 24 MeV was employed
- The largest basis space truncation was Nmax = 10 for nuclei with A < 10 (except 6He at Nmax = 12), and Nmax = 8 for A ≥ 10 nuclei
- The neutron drip line for oxygen isotopes was accurately reproduced with 24O as the drip line nucleus
- Subshell closure at N = 14 and N = 16 was reproduced, albeit with a stronger shell gap compared to experimental data

## Context

This work extends the application of a recently developed local chiral N3LO NN potential (PhysRevC.107.034002, 2023) to ab initio calculations of p-shell nuclei and oxygen isotopes. Previous studies by Navratil (2007) and others demonstrated that 3N interactions are crucial for reproducing the correct ordering of low-lying states in 10B and the neutron drip line in oxygen isotopes. The results show that this local NN potential, with its weaker tensor force, can reproduce many features previously attributed to 3N forces, but the overestimated spin-orbit splitting and underestimated charge radii suggest that 3N forces remain necessary. This enables future work to systematically study the interplay between NN and 3N forces in chiral effective field theory, particularly for spin-orbit sensitive observables.
