# Helium as an Indicator of the Neutron-Star Merger Remnant Lifetime and its Potential for Equation of State Constraints

> The time until black hole formation in a binary neutron-star (NS) merger contains invaluable information about the nuclear equation of state (EoS) but has thus far been difficult to measure. We propose a new way to constrain the merger remnant's NS lifetime, which is based on the tendency of the NS remnant neutrino-driven winds to enrich the ejected material with helium. Based on the He I $λ1083.3$ nm line, we show that the feature around 800-1200 nm in AT2017gfo at 4.4 days seems inconsistent with a helium mass fraction of $X_{\mathrm{He}} \gtrsim 0.05$ in the polar ejecta. Our recent neutrino-hydrodynamic simulations of merger remnants are only compatible with this limit if the NS remnant collapses within 20-30 ms. Such a short lifetime implies that the total binary mass of GW170817, $M_\mathrm{\rm tot}$, lay close to the threshold binary mass for direct gravitational collapse, $M_\mathrm{thres}$, for which we estimate $M_{\mathrm{thres}}\lesssim 2.93 M_\odot$. This upper bound on $M_\mathrm{thres}$ yields upper limits on the radii and maximum mass of cold, non-rotating NSs, which rule out simultaneously large values for both quantities. In combination with causality arguments, this result implies a maximum NS mass of $M_\mathrm{max}\lesssim2.3 M_\odot$. The combination of all limits constrains the radii of 1.6 M$_\odot$ NSs to about 12$\pm$1 km for $M_\mathrm{max}$ = 2.0 M$_\odot$ and 11.5$\pm$1 km for $M_\mathrm{max}$ = 2.15 M$_\odot$. This $\sim2$ km allowable range then tightens significantly for $M_\mathrm{max}$ above $\approx2.15$ M$_\odot$. This rules out a significant number of current EoS models. The short NS lifetime also implies that a black-hole torus, not a highly magnetized NS, was the central engine powering the relativistic jet of GRB170817A. Our work motivates future developments... ## Key claim

The paper proposes that the absence of a strong helium spectral feature in the kilonova AT2017gfo at 4.4 days constrains the neutron-star merger remnant to have collapsed to a black hole within 20-30 ms, which in turn provides upper limits on neutron-star radii and maximum mass; the claim is conditional on the modeling assumptions that the observed 800-1200 nm feature is dominated by strontium rather than helium and that the electron density in the ejecta is not significantly lower than estimated.

## Method

The authors used non-local thermodynamic equilibrium (NLTE) collisional-radiative modeling of helium level populations in homologously expanding ejecta, combined with P Cygni profile synthesis, to determine the helium mass fraction consistent with the observed VLT/X-shooter spectrum of AT2017gfo at 4.4 days. They compared this observational upper limit on helium abundance to predictions from their own neutrino-hydrodynamic simulations of neutron-star merger remnants, which track helium production in neutrino-driven winds as a function of remnant lifetime before black-hole formation.

## Key numbers

- Helium mass fraction upper limit: \(X_{\mathrm{He}} \lesssim 0.05\) in the polar ejecta of AT2017gfo at 4.4 days (2-sigma conservative limit)
- Remnant lifetime constraint: neutron-star remnant must collapse within 20-30 ms to be compatible with the helium limit
- Threshold binary mass for direct gravitational collapse: \(M_{\mathrm{thres}} \lesssim 2.93 M_\odot\) for GW170817
- Maximum neutron-star mass: \(M_{\mathrm{max}} \lesssim 2.3 M_\odot\) from combining the threshold mass limit with causality arguments
- Radii of 1.6 \(M_\odot\) neutron stars: 12 \(\pm\) 1 km for \(M_{\mathrm{max}} = 2.0 M_\odot\) and 11.5 \(\pm\) 1 km for \(M_{\mathrm{max}} = 2.15 M_\odot\)
- Best-fit Sobolev optical depth for the He I 1083.3 nm line: \(\tau = 0.86 \pm 0.18\) at the photosphere

## Context

This work extends the argument by Bauswein (2017) that the brightness of AT2017gfo excludes prompt black-hole formation (giving lower limits on NS radii), and complements studies by Margalit (2017) and Shibata (2017) that used late-time electromagnetic signals and GRB timing to constrain the remnant lifetime. The method provides a new spectral diagnostic for the nuclear equation of state, enabling future constraints from kilonova spectra of additional merger events, and implies that a black-hole torus rather than a magnetized neutron star was the central engine of GRB170817A.
