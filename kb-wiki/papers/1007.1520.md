# Simple parametrization of $α$-decay spectroscopic factor in $150\le A\le200$ region

> Half life values for $α$-decay in the mass region $A=150-200$ have been calculated in the Microscopic Super Asymmetric Fission Model. The interaction between the $α$-particle and the daughter nucleus has been obtained in the double folding model with the microscopic interaction DDM3Y1. Theoretical densities for the nuclei involved have been calculated in Relativistic Mean Field approach with the Lagrangian density FSU Gold. Spectroscopic factors for $α$-decay, calculated as the ratios of the theoretically calculated and experimentally measured half life values, show a simple dependence on the mass number and the product of the number of valence protons and neutrons. The implications of the parameterization have been discussed. Finally, based on this simple relation, $α$-decay half life values in a number of nuclei have been predicted.

# Simple parametrization of α-decay spectroscopic factor in 150 ≤ A ≤ 200 region

## Key claim
The logarithm of the α-decay spectroscopic factor in the mass region A=150–200 can be parametrized as a simple linear function of the mass number A and the product of valence proton and neutron numbers (NpNn), but the fit requires excluding six outlier nuclei (196,198Rn, 196,190Po, 192Pb, and 188Pt) to achieve a χ² of 99.0 for 63 nuclei; the full 69-nucleus fit gives χ² = 308.

## Method
The author calculated α-decay half-lives using the Microscopic Super Asymmetric Fission Model with the WKB approximation for barrier penetration. The α–daughter interaction potential was obtained via double folding with the DDM3Y1 microscopic interaction, using nuclear densities from Relativistic Mean Field calculations with the FSU Gold Lagrangian density, assuming spherical symmetry. Spectroscopic factors were extracted as the ratio of theoretical to experimental half-lives, then fitted to the form log₁₀S = aA + bNpNn, where valence particle numbers are counted relative to magic numbers Z=50,82 and N=82,126.

## Key numbers
- Fitted parameters: a = −0.00928(8), b = 0.00786(37) from 63 nuclei (excluding 6 outliers), with χ² = 99.0
- Full 69-nucleus fit gives χ² = 308
- Predicted half-life for 188Po: 18.4 ms (Qα = 8.082 MeV)
- Predicted half-life for 178Pb: 4.2 ms (Qα = 7.790 MeV)
- Predicted half-life for 180W: 1.8×10¹⁷ years (Qα = 2.508 MeV), consistent with experimental value 1.1⁺⁸₋₄×10¹⁷ years
- Predicted half-life for 184Os: 1.3×10¹³ years (Qα = 2.963 MeV), slightly below the experimental lower limit of 5.6×10¹³ years
- Predicted branching ratio for 190Hg: 1.3×10⁻⁸%, below the experimental upper limit of 3.4×10⁻⁷%

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier study of α-decay spectroscopic factors in heavy nuclei (A > 208) using the same NpNn scheme (Gangopadhyay, 2009) into the lighter A=150–200 region. The parametrization builds on the Casten NpNn scheme (Casten, 1985), which has been used to systematize B(E2) values and deformation. The approach enables prediction of α-decay half-lives for unmeasured nuclei in this mass region, including candidates for double beta decay such as 184Os, and identifies nuclei where α-emission may be the dominant decay mode (e.g., 188Po, 178Pb).
