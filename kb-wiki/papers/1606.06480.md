# Emission of neutron-proton and proton-proton pairs in electron scattering induced by meson-exchange currents

> We use a relativistic model of meson-exchange currents to compute the proton-neutron and proton-proton yields in $(e,e')$ scattering from $^{12}$C in the 2p-2h channel. We compute the response functions and cross section with the relativistic Fermi gas model for a range of kinematics from intermediate to high momentum transfers. We find a large contribution of neutron-proton configurations in the initial state, as compared to proton-proton pairs. The different emission probabilities of distinct species of nucleon pairs are produced in our model only by meson-exchange currents, mainly by the $Δ$ isobar current. We also analyze the effect of the exchange contribution and show that the direct/exchange interference strongly affects the determination of the np/pp ratio.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange currents (MEC) alone, without short-range correlations (SRC), produce a np/pp pair emission ratio of about 6 for the kinematics of the Shneor et al. (2007) experiment, which is insufficient to explain the experimentally observed factor of 18±5 attributed to SRC; however, MEC contributions must be included in any analysis of the np/pp ratio to avoid misinterpretation.

## Method

The authors computed the inclusive 2p-2h response functions and cross sections for electron scattering on 12C using a relativistic Fermi gas model with fully relativistic meson-exchange currents, including pion-in-flight, seagull, and Δ(1232) isobar excitation diagrams. The 7D integral over hole and particle momenta was evaluated numerically without approximations, and the response functions were separated into np and pp charge channels with direct and exchange contributions included.

## Key numbers

- For q=600 MeV/c, the np transverse response is approximately a factor of 12 larger than the pp transverse response; for q=1000 MeV/c this factor reduces to about 6.
- The np/pp ratio for the kinematics of the Shneor et al. (2007) experiment (ω=865 MeV, Q²=2 (GeV/c)², q=1.65 GeV/c) is a factor of 6.
- The experimentally reported np/pp ratio from Shneor et al. (2007) and Subedi et al. (2008) is 18±5.
- The Δ excitation maximum occurs at about 1.12 GeV energy transfer.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic MEC model of De Pace et al. (2003) and Simo et al. (2016) to separate np and pp pair emission channels in electron scattering. It directly addresses the interpretation of experiments by Shneor et al. (2007) and Subedi et al. (2008) that reported a large np/pp ratio attributed to short-range correlations and tensor forces, as also studied in variational Monte Carlo calculations by Schiavilla et al. (2007) and Wiringa et al. (2014). The results enable future work to include correlation currents of the pionic type or effective interactions to disentangle MEC and SRC contributions in two-nucleon knockout reactions.
