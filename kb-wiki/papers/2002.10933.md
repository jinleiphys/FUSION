# Scaled variables and the quark-hadron duality

> The thermodynamic quantities of the ideal gas of hadrons and the $(2+1)$--flavor lattice QCD scaled by the effective degeneracy factors of the corresponding models are compared. We have found that in terms of the scaled variables the quark-hadron duality of the lattice QCD and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model disappears. However, we have unexpectedly revealed that the scaled variables lead to the quark-hadron duality of the lattice QCD and the quantum ideal gas of kaons and antikaons, namely, the ideal gas of those hadrons that contain all the three quarks $u,d,s$ and their antiquarks. Satisfactory agreement between the scaled results of the kaon ideal gas and the lattice QCD data is achieved at large values of the volume in the entire temperature range. In the ideal gas of kaons there is no any phase transition. Nevertheless, in our calculations the scaled thermodynamic quantities of the ideal gas and the lattice QCD follow the same qualitative behavior and are consistent with each other especially at high temperatures in the perturbative region.

## Key claim
The paper claims that after scaling thermodynamic quantities by each model's effective degeneracy factor, the standard quark-hadron duality between lattice QCD and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model disappears; however, a new duality unexpectedly emerges between lattice QCD and the ideal gas of kaons and antikaons, particularly at high temperatures in the perturbative region.

## Method
The author compared the energy density, pressure, trace anomaly, entropy density, and heat capacity of (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations with those of ideal gases of various hadron species (HRG model, nucleons, pions+eta, kaons) computed in the grand canonical ensemble at zero chemical potential. All thermodynamic quantities were divided by the effective degeneracy factor of each respective model (g_QCD = 47.5 for lattice QCD; g_HRG = 1251; g_K = 4 for kaons) to bring them to a common Stefan-Boltzmann limit, and the scaled results were compared across temperatures from approximately 100 to 500 MeV for volumes ranging from 1.1^3 to 10^3 fm^3.

## Key numbers
- The effective degeneracy factor for (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD is g_QCD = 47.5; for the HRG model it is g_HRG = 1251; for kaons (K±, K0, K̅0) it is g_K = 4.
- The common Stefan-Boltzmann limit for scaled energy density is π²/30; for scaled pressure it is π²/90; for scaled entropy density it is 2π²/45; for scaled heat capacity it is 2π²/15.
- At large volume V = 10^3 fm³, the scaled energy density of the kaon ideal gas describes lattice QCD data well at temperatures above 170 MeV.
- At small volume V = 1.2³ fm³ and temperatures above 250 MeV, the scaled energy density of the kaon ideal gas describes the HotQCD data well.
- The scaled pressure of the kaon ideal gas at V = 10^3 fm³ describes lattice QCD data well at temperatures above 200 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the standard comparison of lattice QCD equations of state with the hadron resonance gas model (Ratti 2011, Bazavov 2017, Andronic 2018), which shows quark-hadron duality at low temperatures. By introducing scaling by effective degeneracy factors, the author shows that the HRG duality disappears but is replaced by a surprising agreement between lattice QCD and the ideal gas of kaons only, which contain all three quark flavors (u, d, s) and their antiquarks. This enables a new perspective on how hadronic degrees of freedom relate to lattice QCD results, suggesting that the kaon sector alone captures the essential thermodynamic behavior of the full theory in the deconfined region.
