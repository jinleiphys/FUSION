# Shell Structure and $ρ$-Tensor Correlations in Density-Dependent Relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> A new effective interaction PKA1 with $ρ$-tensor couplings for the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory is presented. It is obtained by fitting selected empirical ground state and shell structure properties. It provides satisfactory descriptions of nuclear matter and the ground state properties of finite nuclei at the same quantitative level as recent DDRHF and RMF models. Significant improvement on the single-particle spectra is also found due to the inclusion of $ρ$-tensor couplings. As a result, PKA1 cures a common disease of the existing DDRHF and RMF Lagrangians, namely the artificial shells at 58 and 92, and recovers the realistic sub-shell closure at 64. Moreover, the proper spin-orbit splittings and well-conserved pseudo-spin symmetry are obtained with the new effective interaction PKA1. Due to the extra binding introduced by the $ρ$-tensor correlations, the balance between the nuclear attractions and the repulsions is changed and this constitutes the physical reason for the improvement of the nuclear shell structure.

## Key claim

The new effective interaction PKA1, which includes ρ-tensor couplings in density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory, cures the artificial shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92 that appear in existing DDRHF and RMF models, and recovers the realistic sub-shell closure at Z=64, but the improvement is conditional on the extra binding introduced by the ρ-tensor correlations changing the balance between nuclear attractions and repulsions.

## Method

The authors extended the DDRHF Lagrangian by adding ρ-tensor couplings (vector, tensor, and vector-tensor terms) to the existing σ, ω, ρ-vector, π-pseudo-vector, and photon channels. They fitted 12 free parameters (6 isoscalar, 6 isovector) to selected empirical ground-state properties (binding energies and charge radii of 14 reference nuclei), nuclear matter bulk properties, and shell-structure criteria including spin-orbit splittings in ¹⁶O and shell gaps at Z=58 and Z=64. The resulting interaction PKA1 was then used to calculate single-particle spectra, spin-orbit splittings, and two-proton separation energies for several nuclei, with comparisons to the DDRHF interaction PKO1 and the RMF interactions PK1 and DD-ME2.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.160 fm⁻³ for PKA1 (compared to 0.152 fm⁻³ for PKO1 and DD-ME2)
- Nuclear matter binding energy E_B = -15.83 MeV, compression modulus K = 229.96 MeV, symmetry energy J = 36.02 MeV for PKA1
- Effective masses for PKA1: M_R* = 0.663, M_NR* = 0.681, M_S* = 0.547
- Root-mean-square deviation for binding energies of 14 reference nuclei: Δ = 1.6847 MeV (0.19% relative) for PKA1, compared to 1.8787 MeV (0.30%) for PKO1 and 2.3495 MeV (0.34%) for DD-ME2
- Root-mean-square deviation for charge radii of 14 reference nuclei: Δ = 0.0342 fm (1.03%) for PKA1, compared to 0.0341 fm (1.01%) for PKO1 and 0.0298 fm (0.94%) for DD-ME2
- Spin-orbit splitting of ν1p in ¹⁶O: 6.055 MeV (PKA1) vs. 6.18 MeV (experiment)
- Spin-orbit splitting of ν1f in ⁴⁸Ca: 8.512 MeV (PKA1) vs. 8.01 MeV (experiment)
- Spin-orbit splitting of ν1g in ⁹⁰Zr: 8.608 MeV (PKA1) vs. 7.07 MeV (experiment)
- Spin-orbit splitting of ν1i in ²⁰⁸Pb: 7.936 MeV (PKA1) vs. 5.81 MeV (experiment)
- ρ-meson contributions to spin-orbit splittings in PKA1 are about 20% of total on average, growing to 30% or higher for high-j orbits near the Fermi surface

## Context

This work extends the DDRHF framework of Long et al. (2006) by adding ρ-tensor couplings, which were absent in previous DDRHF parametrizations (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) and in RMF models such as PK1 (Long et al., 2004) and DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al., 2005). The artificial shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92 were previously noted in RMF calculations by Geng et al. (2006) and in DDRHF calculations. The inclusion of ρ-tensor correlations enables the correct reproduction of the sub-shell closure at Z=64 and eliminates spurious shell gaps, while also improving the ordering of single-particle levels in ²⁰⁸Pb (e.g., the neutron 2g₉/₂ and 1i₁₁/₂ states). The work suggests that particle-vibration coupling, not included here, may account for systematic overestimations of some spin-orbit splittings.
