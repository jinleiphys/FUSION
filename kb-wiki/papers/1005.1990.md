# Beyond universality in three-body recombination: an Effective Field Theory treatment

> We discuss the impact of a finite effective range on three-body systems interacting through a large two-body scattering length. By employing a perturbative analysis in an effective field theory well suited to this scale hierarchy we find that an additional three-body parameter is required for consistent renormalization once range corrections are considered. This allows us to extend previously discussed universal relations between different observables in the recombination of cold atoms to account for the presence of a finite effective range. We show that such range corrections allow us to simultaneously describe the positive and negative scattering-length loss features observed in recombination with Lithium-7 atoms by the Bar-Ilan group. They do not, however, significantly reduce the disagreement between the universal relations and the data of the Rice group on Lithium-7 recombination at positive and negative scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the effective field theory (EFT) for systems with a large two-body scattering length, an additional three-body parameter is required for consistent renormalization when finite effective range corrections are included. This extra parameter allows simultaneous description of recombination features on both sides of a Feshbach resonance for Lithium-7 atoms in the Bar-Ilan experiment, but does not resolve the disagreement between universal predictions and the Rice group data.

## Method

The authors employed a perturbative EFT expansion in the small parameters ℓ/|a| and ℓk, where ℓ is the range of the underlying interaction, a is the two-body scattering length, and k is the momentum scale. They included the two-body effective range rs perturbatively at NLO and derived the integral equation for the atom-dimer scattering amplitude, incorporating a new three-body counterterm H₁₁ that depends on 1/a. The three-body recombination length ρ₃ was then computed from the elastic atom-dimer scattering amplitudes for ⁷Li in two different hyperfine states, using the magnetic-field dependence of a and rs from the experiments.

## Key numbers

- The recombination minimum for ⁷Li in the |F=1 m_F=0⟩ state was measured at a*₀ ≈ 1160 a_B (Bohr radii), and the recombination maximum at a*' ≈ -264(11) a_B (Gross et al., 2009).
- The LO universal ratio prediction is a*' = -0.21 a*₀.
- For the |F=1 m_F=1⟩ state (Rice group), the first recombination maximum on the a<0 side is a*'⁽¹⁾ = -6301 a_B and the second is a*'⁽²⁾ = -298 a_B.
- The NLO prediction for the atom-dimer resonance scattering length a* in the |F=1 m_F=0⟩ state is a* = (210 ± 44) a_B.
- For the |F=1 m_F=1⟩ state, the NLO prediction for the second recombination minimum is a*₀ = (1348 ± 151) a_B, which disagrees with the experimental result a*₀ = (2676 ± 67 ± 128) a_B from Pollack et al. (2009).
- The NLO prediction for the trimer binding momentum at unitarity in the |F=1 m_F=1⟩ state is κ* = (0.242 ± 0.030) × 10⁻³ a_B⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the universal Efimov relations derived by Braaten and Hammer (2004) and the leading-order EFT treatment of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) by including finite effective range corrections perturbatively. It builds on earlier range-correction studies by Platter et al. (2008) and Thogerson et al. (2008), but differs by treating the range perturbatively and identifying the need for an additional three-body parameter. The results enable systematic NLO predictions for three-body recombination in cold atoms with known effective ranges, and suggest that the discrepancy in the Rice group data (Pollack et al., 2009) cannot be explained by range corrections alone, motivating further investigation into experimental conditions.
