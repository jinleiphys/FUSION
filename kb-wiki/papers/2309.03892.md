# A novel approach to the global analysis of proton form factors in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We present a novel method for the global analysis of elastic electron-proton scattering when combining data sets from experiments with different overall normalization uncertainties. The method is a modification of one employed by the NNPDF collaboration in the fitting of parton distribution data. This method is an alternative to the 'penalty trick' method traditionally employed in global fits to proton electric and magnetic form factors, while avoiding the biases inherent in that approach. We discuss issues that arise when extending the method to nonlinear models. For data with $Q^2>1$ GeV$^2$ we find relatively minor differences to traditional model fits when the normalization uncertainties from different experiments are correctly accounted for. We discuss implications of this method for the well-known discrepancy between the form factor ratio $G_E/G_M$ extracted from the Rosenbluth and polarization transfer techniques.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the iterated model fit (IMF) method, an unbiased alternative to the traditional "penalty trick" for combining data sets with different normalization uncertainties in global fits of proton form factors. For data with Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, the method yields relatively minor differences to traditional model fits when normalization uncertainties are correctly accounted for, but may reduce the apparent disagreement between form factor ratios extracted from Rosenbluth and polarization transfer techniques.

## Method

The authors modify the NNPDF collaboration's t_0 method (Ball 2010) to handle data at multiple kinematics by constructing a covariance matrix for each experiment that includes a term proportional to the product of best-guess model values times the squared normalization uncertainty. They generate Monte Carlo replica data sets by first pulling replica data from normal distributions based on point-to-point uncertainties, then multiplying by normalization factors pulled from normal distributions centered at 1 with standard deviations given by the experimental normalization uncertainties. The procedure iterates: replica fits are averaged to produce an updated best-guess model, which is fed back into the covariance matrix until convergence. For nonlinear models, the authors average the fitted parameters directly rather than averaging the functions, accepting this as an approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the linear model fit to three data sets (Walker 1994, Andivahis 1994) with Q^2 from 1 to 8.83 GeV^2: chi^2/d.o.f. = 28.1/42 for IMF versus 28.3/39 for penalty trick
- For the nonlinear z-expansion model fit to nine data sets (GMp12 collaboration, Christy 2022) with Q^2 < 16 GeV^2 (115 points): chi^2/d.o.f. = 76.5/107 for IMF versus 76.9/98 for penalty trick
- IMF convergence required 2 iterations with 1000 replicas for the linear model, and 5 iterations with 1500 replicas for the nonlinear model
- In the nonlinear fit, about 10% of replica form factor fits were discarded because they changed sign in the region Q^2 < 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the t_0 method developed by the NNPDF collaboration (Ball 2010) for parton distribution fitting to the domain of elastic proton form factors, providing an alternative to the penalty trick method used in prior analyses by Gramolin and Nikolenko (2016) and the GMp12 collaboration (Christy 2022). The authors note that Bernauer et al. (2014) and recent work on the proton radius (Lin 2022) also use the penalty trick, and they plan to reanalyze those fits with the IMF method. The paper enables future combined global analyses of cross section and polarization transfer data including two-photon exchange corrections, which are considered the most viable explanation for the form factor ratio discrepancy.
