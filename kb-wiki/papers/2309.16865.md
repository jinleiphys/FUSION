# A Bayesian study of quark models in view of recent astrophysical constraints

> In this work, we perform a comparative analysis between the density-dependent quark model and the vector MIT bag model using Bayesian analysis. We use the equations of state generated by these two models to describe quark stars. We impose four recent observational astrophysical constraints on both models to determine their model-dependent parameters in an optimized manner assuming that the compact objects observed are composed entirely of self-bound quarks. The restrictions are aimed at producing stars with maximum masses $2 - 2.35$ M$_\odot$ and a mass-radii diagram compatible with the observed pulsars: PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0952-0607, PSR J0030+0451 and the compact object XMMU J173203.3-344518. With this analysis, the parameter dependence of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) of both models is restricted.

## Key claim

The paper uses Bayesian analysis to constrain the parameters of two quark matter models (density-dependent quark model and vector MIT bag model) under the assumption that observed compact objects are composed entirely of self-bound strange quarks, finding that the vector MIT bag model produces stiffer equations of state than the density-dependent quark model when subjected to the same astrophysical constraints.

## Method

The authors generated equations of state for cold, beta-equilibrated strange quark matter using both the density-dependent quark model (with parameters C and D) and the vector MIT bag model (with parameters B, G_V, and b_4). They applied Bayesian inference with uniform priors and Gaussian likelihoods, using the emcee Markov chain Monte Carlo sampler, to constrain model parameters against mass and radius measurements of four compact objects: PSR J0952-0607, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, and XMMU J173203.3-344518. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for each equation of state.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0952-0607 mass: 2.35 ± 0.17 solar masses (no radius constraint)
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.072 (+0.067/-0.066) solar masses, radius: 12.39 (+1.30/-0.98) km
- PSR J0030+0451 mass: 1.34 (+0.15/-0.16) solar masses, radius: 12.71 (+1.14/-1.19) km
- XMMU J173203.3-344518 mass: 0.77 (+0.20/-0.17) solar masses, radius: 10.4 (+0.86/-0.78) km
- For the vector MIT bag model, the selected parameter sets yield maximum masses from 2.21 to 2.54 solar masses, with radii at 1.4 solar masses ranging from 11.43 to 12.38 km
- For the density-dependent quark model, the selected parameter sets yield maximum masses from 1.91 to 2.18 solar masses, with radii at 1.4 solar masses ranging from 12.46 to 14.41 km
- The vector MIT bag model tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses ranges from 633 to 1078
- The density-dependent quark model tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses ranges from 534 to 2163

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron star equations of state (such as those by Zhu (2023), Takatsy (2023), and De (2023)) by specifically testing two quark matter models under the assumption that the observed compact objects are pure quark stars rather than hybrid or hadronic stars. The study uses recent NICER measurements of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451 from Riley (2021) and Riley (2019), along with the high-mass pulsar PSR J0952-0607 from Romani (2022) and the low-mass candidate XMMU J173203.3-344518 from Doroshenko (2022). The results enable future comparisons between quark star and hybrid star scenarios, and provide constrained parameter ranges for both the density-dependent quark model (originally developed by Wu (2008) and Xia (2014)) and the vector MIT bag model (as formulated by Lopes (2021)).
