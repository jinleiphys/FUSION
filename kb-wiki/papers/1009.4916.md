# Renormalized interactions with a realistic single particle basis

> Neutron-rich isotopes in the sdpf space with Z < 15 require modifications to derived effective interactions to agree with experimental data away from stability. A quantitative justification is given for these modifications due to the weakly bound nature of model space orbits via a procedure using realistic radial wavefunctions and realistic NN interactions. The long tail of the radial wavefunction for loosely bound single particle orbits causes a reduction in the size of matrix elements involving those orbits, most notably for pairing matrix elements, resulting in a more condensed level spacing in shell model calculations. Example calculations are shown for 36Si and 38Si.

# Renormalized interactions with a realistic single particle basis

## Key claim

The modifications to effective interactions needed for neutron-rich isotopes in the sdpf space with Z ≤ 14 can be quantitatively justified by using realistic radial wavefunctions from Skyrme Hartree-Fock rather than harmonic oscillator wavefunctions, because the long tail of loosely bound single particle orbits reduces the size of two-body matrix elements, particularly pairing matrix elements, by amounts that are suggestive of but not identical to the empirical 300 keV reduction used in the SDPF-U interaction.

## Method

The authors start with the N³LO chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at 500 MeV cutoff, renormalize it via a v_low_k similarity transformation with a sharp cutoff Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹, and then use Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory to second order with excitations up to 6ħω to renormalize into the sdpf model space (sd proton orbits and pf neutron orbits). They compare three bases for the renormalization: harmonic oscillator (HO), Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) using the Skxtb interaction, and a mixed core-polarization (CP) basis with SHF energies but HO wavefunctions, applied to both a stable ⁴⁰Ca target and a neutron-rich ³⁴Si target.

## Key numbers

- The N³LO interaction is renormalized with a sharp cutoff Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹.
- For ³⁴Si, the p₃/₂ orbit is bound by 400 keV and the p₁/₂ orbit by 269 keV; the f₇/₂ orbit is bound by 2.6 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator component R^HO_11 makes up only 80% of the p₃/₂ and 78% of the p₁/₂ SHF radial wavefunctions; the f₇/₂ orbit is 99% represented by R^HO_03.
- The expansion of SHF wavefunctions in the HO basis includes orbits up to (2n+l)=9, giving n_max=3 for f orbits and n_max=4 for p orbits, capturing over 99% of f strength but only 93% (p₃/₂) and 92% (p₁/₂) of p strength.
- Neutron-neutron J=0 pairing matrix elements are reduced by an average of 213 keV when using ³⁴Si as the target nucleus in the SHF basis compared to ⁴⁰Ca.
- The total matrix element ⟨f₇/₂ f₇/₂ | V | f₇/₂ f₇/₂⟩_J=0 is -1.985 MeV for ³⁴Si in SHF basis versus -2.282 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca in SHF basis, a 13% reduction.
- The total matrix element ⟨p₃/₂ p₃/₂ | V | p₃/₂ p₃/₂⟩_J=0 is -0.944 MeV for ³⁴Si in SHF basis versus -1.270 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca in SHF basis, approximately a 26% reduction.
- The binding energy of ³⁶Si changes by nearly 500 keV depending on which renormalization procedure is used.
- The rms deviation between experiment and theory for ³⁶Si with ³⁴Si as target in the SHF basis is 214 keV for the four states shown.
- The experimental binding energy of ³⁶Si relative to ³⁴Si is 140 keV higher than previously measured.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach of Kuo et al. (various years), who noted reduction of core polarization in the harmonic oscillator basis and used different oscillator parameters for core and valence nucleons in halo nuclei, and of Ogawa et al. (various years), who found a 10%-30% reduction in matrix elements involving loosely bound orbits using a Woods-Saxon basis but without including core polarization. The paper provides a microscopic justification for the empirical Z-dependent modification of neutron-neutron pairing matrix elements in the SDPF-U interaction of Nowacki and Poves (various years), which reduced pairing by 300 keV for Z ≤ 14. The results enable more accurate shell model calculations for neutron-rich silicon isotopes and suggest that inclusion of three-nucleon forces and improved single particle energies from energy density functionals are needed for higher accuracy.
