# Charmed Mesons Have No Discernable Color-Coulomb Attraction

> Starting with a confining linear Lorentz scalar potential V_s and a Lorentz vector potential V_v which is also linear but has in addition a color-Coulomb attraction piece, -alpha_s/r, we solve the Dirac equation for the ground-state c- and u-quark wave functions. Then, convolving V_v with the u-quark density, we find that the Coulomb attraction mostly disappears, making an essentially linear barV_v for the c-quark. A similar convolution using the c-quark density also leads to an essentially linear tildeV_v for the u-quark. For bound cbar-c charmonia, where one must solve using a reduced mass for the c-quarks, we also find an essentially linear widehatV_v. Thus, the relativistic quark model describes how the charmed-meson mass spectrum avoids the need for a color-Coulomb attraction.

# Charmed Mesons Have No Discernable Color-Coulomb Attraction

## Key claim
The color-Coulomb attraction piece in the vector potential, when convolved with the quark density of the other bound quark, becomes essentially linear and indistinguishable from a purely confining potential for charmed mesons and charmonia. The authors qualify that this result depends on the still relatively light mass of the charm quark and may not hold for bottom quarks.

## Method
The authors solve the Dirac equation for ground-state c- and u-quark wave functions using a confining linear Lorentz scalar potential and a Lorentz vector potential that is also linear but includes a color-Coulomb attraction term, -α_s/r. Following the Friar-Negele approach for muonic atoms, they then convolve the vector potential with the u-quark density to obtain the effective potential for the c-quark, and similarly convolve with the c-quark density for the u-quark. The procedure is repeated for charmonium states using the reduced mass of the c-quarks.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.550 GeV
- Reduced mass for charmonium calculations: m_c/2 = 0.775 GeV
- κ² = 0.9 GeV/fm, corresponding to κ = 2.136 fm⁻¹
- Smoothing scale x_D = 0.16 (corresponding to R_D ~ 0.3 fm for the D(1869) meson)
- α_s = 0.4 used in the color-Coulomb potential
- c-quark eigenenergy with original potentials: E_c(1s) = 1.339 GeV (about 200 MeV below its mass)
- c-quark eigenenergy with convolved potential: E_c(1s) = 1.534 GeV
- Linear fit to convolved vector potential for c-quark: slope a₀ = 1.139, displacement a₁ = -1.498
- Linear fit to convolved vector potential for u-quark: slope a₀ = 1.218, displacement a₁ = -1.673
- Linear fit to convolved vector potential for charmonia: slope a₀ = 1.225, displacement a₁ = -1.688
- The charmonium spectrum is reproduced to within approximately ±50 MeV

## Context
This work extends the relativistic quark model approach to charmed mesons, building on the non-relativistic potential model studies of charmonium by Rosner and others, which successfully described states with linear confining potentials without evidence for color-Coulomb contributions. The method follows the relativistic treatment of the Hydrogen atom by Friar and Negele, applying the convolution technique to smear the Coulomb singularity. The results are consistent with the good spectral results found in non-relativistic and relativistic models for charmonium using linear confining potentials. The authors intend to next examine bottom quark states to determine whether the color-Coulomb contribution becomes discernible there.
