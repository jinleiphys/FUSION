# Counting statistics in finite Fermi systems: illustrations with the atomic nucleus

> We analyze here in details the probability to find a given number of particles in a finite volume inside a normal or superfluid finite system. This probability, also known as counting statistics, is obtained using projection operator techniques directly linked to the characteristic function of the probability distribution. The method is illustrated in atomic nuclei. The nature of the particle number fluctuations from small to large volumes compared to the system size are carefully analyzed in three cases: normal systems, superfluid systems and superfluid systems with total particle number restoration. The transition from Poissonian distribution in the small volume limit to Gaussian fluctuations as the number of particles participating to the fluctuations increases, is analyzed both in the interior and at the surface of the system. While the restoration of total number of particles is not necessary for small volume, we show that it affects the counting statistics as soon as more than very few particles are involved.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the counting statistics (probability distribution of particle number in a finite volume) in finite Fermi systems transitions from a Poisson distribution for very small volumes to a Gaussian distribution as the volume increases, and that restoration of the total particle number symmetry in superfluid systems is unnecessary for small volumes but significantly affects the counting statistics when more than very few particles are involved.

## Method

The authors compute the probability distribution P(N) for finding N particles in a finite volume Ω using projection operator techniques linked to the characteristic function F(φ) = ⟨e^{iφN_Ω}⟩, which is the Fourier transform of the probability distribution. For superfluid systems described by BCS-type quasi-particle vacua, the characteristic function is evaluated using Pfaffian methods, and the analysis is extended to include simultaneous projection on both the volume particle number and the total particle number to restore U(1) symmetry. The method is applied to a self-bound ⁴⁰Ca nucleus described by a simplified Skyrme density functional with variable pairing gap, considering both interior volumes (spheres centered at the nucleus) and surface volumes.

## Key numbers

- The average inter-particle distance d is defined from the saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ through ρ₀ = 1/d³.
- The single-particle gap near the Fermi energy for ⁴⁰Ca is Δε = 6.73 MeV.
- Pairing gaps are varied from Δ/Δε = 0.01 (no pairing limit) to Δ/Δε = 2.0.
- The ratio μ₄/μ₂² becomes close to 3.0 (indicating Gaussian behavior) when the sphere radius approaches R/d ≃ 1.
- For very small volumes (R/d = 0.5), the probability distribution is nearly independent of both pairing and total particle number projection.
- For volumes with R/d > 1, centered moments μ₂ and μ₄ are strongly reduced after total particle number projection compared to the unprojected case.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of particle number fluctuations from infinite or large Fermi systems (as studied in atomic gases by Astrakharchik (2007), Castin (2008), and Belzig (2007)) to finite mesoscopic systems like atomic nuclei, where finite-size effects are significant. The projection operator technique connects to standard nuclear many-body methods (Ring and Schuck, 1980; Blaizot and Ripka, 1986) and to recent applications in nuclear reactions (Simenel, 2010; Sekizawa, 2014; Bulgac, 2019). The results enable direct calculation of full counting statistics in finite superfluid nuclei and clarify when symmetry restoration is necessary for accurate fluctuation predictions.
