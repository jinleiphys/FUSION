# Properties of hadronic systems according to the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics

> The non-extensive self-consistent theory describing the thermodynamics of hadronic systems at high temperatures is used to derive some thermodynamical quantities, as pressure, entropy, speed of sound and trace-anomaly. The calculations are free of fitting parameters, and the results are compared to lattice QCD calculations, showing a good agreement between theory and data up to temperatures around 175 MeV. Above this temperature the effects of a singularity in the partition function at To = 192 MeV results in a divergent behaviour in respect with the lattice calculation.

## Key claim

The non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics, using parameters fixed by experimental transverse-momentum distributions and the hadron mass spectrum (no free parameters), reproduces lattice QCD results for bulk thermodynamic functions up to temperatures around 175 MeV; above that temperature, a singularity in the partition function at T₀ = 192 MeV causes divergence from lattice data.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for pressure, entropy density, energy density, trace-anomaly, specific heat, and speed of sound from the non-extensive self-consistent partition function, which uses a power-law mass spectrum and the q-exponential function. A linear mapping τ = (τ₀/T₀) T between the effective temperature τ and the lattice-QCD temperature T is established, and the resulting thermodynamic functions are compared directly to three sets of lattice QCD data (Borsanyi et al. 2010 and Cheng et al. 2008) without any fitting.

## Key numbers

- Limiting effective temperature τ₀ = (60.7 ± 0.5) MeV, limiting entropic index q₀ = 1.138 ± 0.006, and γ = (5 ± 3) × 10⁻³ GeV³/², all determined from experimental pT-distributions and hadron spectrum.
- Hagedorn temperature T₀ = T_H = (192 ± 15) MeV, with constant c = -(950 ± 10) MeV from the relation τ₀ = T₀ + (q-1)c.
- Good agreement between theory and lattice QCD up to temperatures around 175 MeV; deviations begin near T ~ 185 MeV for normalized pressure and near T ~ 175 MeV for the trace-anomaly.
- The speed of sound goes to zero at the critical temperature T₀ = 192 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics developed by Deppman (2009) and connects it to lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi et al. (2010) and Cheng et al. (2008). It contrasts with standard hadron resonance gas (HRG) models (Broniowski, Chatterjee, Megias, Arriola) which use Hagedorn's exponential mass spectrum and require fitting to lattice data, whereas the present approach uses parameters fixed by experimental data. The results enable a parameter-free bridge between experimental observables from high-energy collisions and lattice QCD thermodynamics, and suggest that a power-law mass spectrum is consistent with lattice results within non-extensive statistics.
