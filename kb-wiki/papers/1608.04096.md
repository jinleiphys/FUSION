# Precise Determination of the U-235 Reactor Antineutrino Cross Section per Fission

> We investigate which among the reactor antineutrino fluxes from the decays of the fission products of $^{235}\text{U}$, $^{238}\text{U}$, $^{239}\text{Pu}$, and $^{241}\text{Pu}$ may be responsible for the reactor antineutrino anomaly if the anomaly is due to a miscalculation of the antineutrino fluxes. We find that it is very likely that at least the calculation of the $^{235}\text{U}$ flux must be revised. From the fit of the data we obtain the precise determination $σ_{f,235} = ( 6.33 \pm 0.08 ) \times 10^{-43} \, \text{cm}^2 / \text{fission}$ of the $^{235}\text{U}$ cross section per fission, which is more precise than the calculated value and differs from it by $2.2σ$. The cross sections per fission of the other fluxes have large uncertainties and in practice their values are undetermined by the fit.

# Precise Determination of the U-235 Reactor Antineutrino Cross Section per Fission

## Key claim

If the reactor antineutrino anomaly is due to a miscalculation of the antineutrino fluxes rather than oscillations to sterile neutrinos, then at least the calculation of the U-235 flux must be revised; the fit yields a U-235 cross section per fission that is more precise than the calculated value and differs from it by 2.2 sigma, but the cross sections per fission of the other three isotopes (U-238, Pu-239, Pu-241) remain undetermined with large uncertainties.

## Method

The author performed a least-squares fit of the measured-to-predicted rate ratios from 26 reactor antineutrino experiments (including Bugey, Rovno, Gosgen, ILL, Krasnoyarsk, SRP, Nucifer, Chooz, Palo Verde, Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz) to extract correction coefficients for the antineutrino fluxes of U-235, U-238, Pu-239, and Pu-241. To prevent unphysical best-fit values for the Pu isotopes, a penalty term was added to the chi-squared function that constrained the correction coefficients to reasonable intervals around unity using a 5% uncertainty for U-235, Pu-239, and Pu-241, and a 10% uncertainty for U-238.

## Key numbers

- The U-235 cross section per fission determined from the fit with penalty terms is sigma_f,235 = (6.33 +/- 0.08) x 10^-43 cm^2/fission, which differs from the calculated Saclay+Huber value of (6.69 +/- 0.14) x 10^-43 cm^2/fission by 2.2 sigma.
- The correction coefficient for U-235 from the penalized fit is r_235 = 0.946 +/- 0.012.
- The correction coefficients for the other isotopes from the penalized fit are r_238 = 0.908 +/- 0.077, r_239 = 0.956 +/- 0.041, and r_241 = 0.990 +/- 0.049.
- The fit with penalty terms has chi-squared_min = 16.5 with 22 degrees of freedom (78% goodness of fit), while the null hypothesis (all r_k = 1) has chi-squared = 97.8 with 26 degrees of freedom.
- The calculated Saclay+Huber cross sections per fission (in units of 10^-43 cm^2/fission) are: U-235 = 6.69 (2.11% uncertainty), U-238 = 10.10 (8.15%), Pu-239 = 4.40 (2.45%), Pu-241 = 6.03 (2.15%).

## Context

This work addresses the reactor antineutrino anomaly identified by Mention (2011), which arose from the 2011 flux recalculation by Mueller (2011) and Huber (2011) that was about 3% higher than the previous estimate from Schreckenbach (1985) and Hahn (1989). The analysis extends earlier fits by the Saclay group and Zhang (2013) by including updated data from Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz, and Nucifer, and by applying penalty terms to keep the Pu and U-238 corrections physically reasonable. The results enable future research reactor experiments such as PROSPECT, SoLid, and STEREO to improve the determination of the U-235 cross section per fission and to test whether the 5 MeV spectral bump observed by RENO, Double Chooz, and Daya Bay also originates from U-235.
