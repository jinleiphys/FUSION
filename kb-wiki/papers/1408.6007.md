# Pigmy resonance in monopole response of neutron-rich Ni isotopes ?

> The RPA monopole strength as well as the unperturbed particle-hole excitation strength is studied, in which the strength in the continuum is properly treated without discretizing unbound particle spectra. The model is the sef-consistent Hartree-Fock calculation and the RPA Green's function method with Skyrme interactions. Numerical examples are the Ni-isotopes, especially $^{68}_{28}$Ni$_{40}$, in which an experimental observation of low-lying socalled "pigmy resonance" with an appreciable amount of monopole strength at 12.9 $\pm$ 1.0 MeV was recently reported. In the present study it is concluded that sharp monopole peaks with the width of the order of 1 MeV can hardly be expected for $^{68}$Ni in that energy region. Instead, a broad shoulder of monopole strength consisting of neutron excitations to non-resonant one-particle states (called "threshold strength") with relatively low angular-momenta $(\ell, j)$ is obtained in the continuum energy region above the particle threshold, which is considerably lower than that of isoscalar giant monopole resonance. In the case of monopole excitations of $^{68}$Ni there are no unperturbed particle-hole states below 20 MeV, in which the particle expresses a neutron (or proton) resonant state. It is emphasized that in the theoretical estimate a proper treatment of the continuum is extremely important.

# Pigmy resonance in monopole response of neutron-rich Ni isotopes ?

## Key claim

The authors argue that the recently reported "pigmy resonance" in the monopole response of ⁶⁸Ni at 12.9 ± 1.0 MeV is unlikely to be a sharp resonance peak with width of order 1 MeV; instead, the low-energy monopole strength in this neutron-rich nucleus arises from broad "threshold strength" consisting of neutron excitations to non-resonant one-particle states, forming a broad shoulder rather than a distinct resonance.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations followed by random phase approximation (RPA) using the Green's function method in coordinate space with Skyrme interactions (SLy4), treating the continuum properly without discretizing unbound particle spectra. They compute the isoscalar monopole strength distribution for neutron-rich Ni isotopes, particularly ⁶⁸Ni and ⁷⁸Ni, and analyze the contributions from individual occupied neutron hole orbits to the unperturbed particle-hole strength.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally reported low-lying monopole strength in ⁶⁸Ni is at 12.9 ± 1.0 MeV (Vandebrouck et al., 2014), with the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid at 21.1 ± 1.9 MeV.
- The neutron separation energy S_n for ⁶⁸Ni is experimentally 7.79 MeV; the SLy4 interaction gives 8.92 MeV, equal to the binding energy of the least-bound neutrons in the 1f₅/₂ orbit.
- Calculated neutron binding energies in ⁶⁸Ni (SLy4): 2p₁/₂ at −9.25 MeV, 2p₃/₂ at −11.39 MeV, 1f₇/₂ at −16.45 MeV.
- The proton separation energy S_p for ⁶⁸Ni is experimentally 15.43 MeV; SLy4 gives 14.9 MeV.
- For ⁷⁸Ni, the neutron separation energy from SLy4 is 5.88 MeV, equal to the binding energy of the least-bound neutrons in the 1g₉/₂ orbit.
- One-particle resonant energies in the HF potential of ⁶⁸Ni (SLy4): 2d₅/₂ neutron at −0.71 MeV (bound), 2d₃/₂ neutron at +1.13 MeV (width 0.54 MeV), giving a spin-orbit splitting of 1.84 MeV for 2d neutrons.
- For protons in ⁶⁸Ni: 2d₅/₂ at +1.27 MeV, 2d₃/₂ at +3.23 MeV, giving a spin-orbit splitting of 1.96 MeV for 2d protons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of response functions in unstable nuclei using HF+RPA with Skyrme interactions in coordinate space (Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang, 1997), particularly the analysis of threshold strength in neutron-rich systems. It directly contradicts the interpretation of a discrete low-energy monopole resonance in ⁶⁸Ni as reported by Vandebrouck et al. (2014) and the RPA calculation using discretized particle spectra by Ebata and Kido (2011). The paper emphasizes that proper treatment of the continuum is essential for reliable calculations in neutron-rich nuclei, and that discretization methods can produce artificial discrete states that do not correspond to physical one-particle resonances. The results enable future experimental work to better characterize the broad threshold strength rather than searching for narrow monopole resonances in this energy region.
