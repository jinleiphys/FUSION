# Strangeness dynamics in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collision

> We investigate hadron production as well as transverse hadron spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions from 2 $A\cdot$GeV to 21.3 $A\cdot$TeV within two independent transport approaches (UrQMD and HSD) that are based on quark, diquark, string and hadronic degrees of freedom. The comparison to experimental data demonstrates that both approaches agree quite well with each other and with the experimental data on hadron production. The enhancement of pion production in central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions relative to scaled $pp$ collisions (the 'kink') is well described by both approaches without involving any phase transition. However, the maximum in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio at 20 to 30 A$\cdot$GeV (the 'horn') is missed by $\sim$ 40%. A comparison to the transverse mass spectra from $pp$ and C+C (or Si+Si) reactions shows the reliability of the transport models for light systems. For central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at bombarding energies above $\sim$ 5 A$\cdot$GeV, however, the measured $K^{\pm}$ $m_{T}$-spectra have a larger inverse slope parameter than expected from the calculations. The approximately constant slope of $K^\pm$ spectra at SPS (the 'step') is not reproduced either. Thus the pressure generated by hadronic interactions in the transport models above $\sim$ 5 A$\cdot$GeV is lower than observed in the experimental data. This finding suggests that the additional pressure - as expected from lattice QCD calculations at finite quark chemical potential and temperature - might be generated by strong interactions in the early pre-hadronic/partonic phase of central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions.

## Key claim

The hadronic transport models UrQMD and HSD, which include only hadronic and string degrees of freedom, fail to reproduce the experimentally observed hardening of kaon transverse mass spectra and the maximum in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio (the 'horn') in central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions above \(\sim 5\) A\(\cdot\)GeV, suggesting that additional pressure from pre-hadronic or partonic degrees of freedom is required already at these energies.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using two independent transport models, UrQMD (v. 1.3) and HSD, which describe nucleus-nucleus collisions using quark, diquark, string, and hadronic degrees of freedom without explicit parton-parton scattering. They computed hadron multiplicities, particle ratios, and transverse mass spectra for central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions across bombarding energies from 2 A\(\cdot\)GeV to 21.3 A\(\cdot\)TeV and compared these results to experimental data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio maximum at 20 to 30 A\(\cdot\)GeV (the 'horn') is missed by \(\sim 40\%\) in both transport models.
- Differences between the two transport models for particle yields are less than 20%; maximum deviations between models and experimental data are less than \(\sim 30\%\).
- At 160 A\(\cdot\)GeV, including string-string interactions with a transverse string radius \(R_s \leq 0.25\) fm gives a hardening of kaon spectra by about 15%, still significantly less than observed in data.
- The inverse slope parameter \(T\) for \(K^\pm\) mesons from transport models shows no 'step' (constant slope above \(\sim 30\) A\(\cdot\)GeV) as seen in experimental data.
- The critical temperature \(T_c\) from lattice QCD calculations is in the range of 160 MeV, with energy density in the order of 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) or below.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the RQMD model (Sorge, 1990s) and the AMPT model (Ko, 2000s) by systematically comparing two independent hadronic transport approaches to experimental data across a wide energy range. The findings contradict interpretations that the 'horn' and 'step' in kaon observables can be explained by hadronic rescattering alone, as proposed in statistical model analyses (Cleymans, 2000s; Rafelski and Müller, 1980s). The results support the need for pre-hadronic or partonic degrees of freedom in the early collision phase, consistent with lattice QCD predictions (Fodor and Katz, 2002; Karsch, 2002) and with studies of elliptic flow at RHIC energies that are underestimated by \(\sim 30\%\) in HSD.
