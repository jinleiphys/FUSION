# Condensation of interacting scalar bosons at finite temperatures

> Thermodynamical properties of an interacting system of scalar bosons at finite temperatures are studied within the framework of a field-theoretical model containing the attractive and repulsive self-interaction terms. Self-consistency relations between the effective mass and thermodynamic functions are derived in the mean-field approximation. We show that for a sufficiently strong attractive interaction a first-order phase transition develops in the system via the formation of a scalar condensate. An interesting prediction of this model is that the condensed phase appears within a finite temperature interval and is characterized by a constant scalar density of Bose particles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a sufficiently strong attractive self-interaction (parameter κ > 1), a system of scalar bosons at zero chemical potential undergoes a first-order phase transition to a scalar Bose condensate phase, but this condensed phase exists only within a finite temperature interval rather than extending to arbitrarily low temperatures.

## Method

The authors formulate a field-theoretical model with a real scalar field using a Skyrme-like interaction Lagrangian containing attractive φ⁴ and repulsive φ⁶ terms. They derive thermodynamic functions in the mean-field approximation, obtaining self-consistency relations between the effective mass M(σ) and the scalar density σ, and solve the coupled gap equations for σ(T) and M(T) at zero chemical potential. The onset of scalar Bose condensation is identified with the condition M(σ) = 0, leading to massless quasiparticles in the condensed phase.

## Key numbers

- For the illustrative case with pion-like parameters (m = 140 MeV, g = 3, b = 25 m⁻²) and κ = 1.1, the scalar density in the condensed phase is σ₂ ≈ 0.312 m².
- The maximum temperature for the condensed phase is T₂ = √(12σ₂/g) ≈ 156 MeV.
- The critical temperature of the first-order phase transition is T_c ≈ 80 MeV.
- At T_c, the scalar density jumps from σ_c ≈ 0.022 m² to σ₂.
- The entropy density in the condensed phase satisfies s ≈ 3.60 n, where n is the particle number density.
- The limiting scalar density for massless quasiparticles is σ_lim(T) = gT²/12.

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of dense bosonic systems, such as the pion gas with repulsive φ⁴ interaction considered by Vos (2018) and Skyrme-like models by Anchishkin (2019), to include both attractive and repulsive self-interactions. It builds on the pioneering work on pion condensation by Migdal and coworkers (1972-1990) and on thermodynamic consistency conditions from Gorenstein (1995). The model predicts a "triangular phase diagram" where the scalar condensate exists only in a finite temperature window, which differs from conventional Bose-Einstein condensation at low temperatures. The authors note that future work should incorporate the special nature of pions as Goldstone bosons within the linear sigma model.
