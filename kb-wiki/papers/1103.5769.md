# Dijet asymmetry at the Large Hadron Collider

> The MARTINI numerical simulation allows for direct comparison of theoretical model calculations and the latest results for dijet asymmetry from the ATLAS and CMS collaborations. In this paper, partons are simulated as undergoing radiative and collisional processes throughout the evolution of central lead-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Using hydrodynamical background evolution determined by a simulation which fits well with the data on charged particle multiplicities from ALICE and a value of $α_s\approx 0.25-0.3$, the dijet asymmetry is found to be consistent with partonic energy loss in a hot, strongly-interacting medium.

## Key claim
The dijet asymmetry observed by ATLAS and CMS in lead-lead collisions at the LHC is consistent with partonic energy loss in a hot, strongly-interacting medium, but only when using a specific range of the strong coupling constant (α_s ≈ 0.25–0.3) and a hydrodynamic background that reproduces ALICE charged-particle multiplicities.

## Method
The authors used the MARTINI Monte Carlo simulation, which solves rate equations for radiative and collisional QCD processes at finite temperature, to evolve high-energy partons sampled from PYTHIA event generation through a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic background (MUSIC) for central lead-lead collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. The evolved partons were hadronized and reconstructed into jets using the anti-k_t algorithm (R=0.4) via FastJet, applying the same kinematic cuts as the ATLAS and CMS analyses. The only free parameter in the model was α_s, varied among 0.25, 0.27, and 0.3.

## Key numbers
- The hydrodynamic simulation used an impact parameter of b = 2.31 fm to reproduce the 0–10% centrality class multiplicities.
- ATLAS analysis required leading jet transverse energy E_T > 100 GeV and opposing jet E_T > 25 GeV.
- CMS analysis required leading jet E_T1 > 120 GeV and opposing jet E_T2 > 50 GeV.
- The strong coupling constant was varied over α_s ≈ 0.25–0.3.
- The collision energy was √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for each nucleon-nucleon collision.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of jet quenching at RHIC to the higher-energy LHC environment, where far more energetic jets are kinematically accessible. It builds on prior theoretical approaches including the radiative energy loss models of Majumder (2011) and Che et al. (2011), the full jet shower evolution study by Qin and Müller (2010), and the dijet asymmetry analysis by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2010). The results enable future investigations of event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations and medium viscosity effects on jet observables.
