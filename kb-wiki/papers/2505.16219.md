# Hidden-Charm Tetraquarks in a Mixture Model: Coupled-Channel Analysis with $c\bar{c}$ and Hadronic Molecular Components

> The nature of the $X(3872)$ and other exotic hadrons has been a subject of extensive investigation since the first observation of the $X(3872)$ in 2003. While various theoretical models have been proposed, including hadronic molecular and compact tetraquark interpretations, some experimental evidence suggests that the $X(3872)$ may be a mixture state of a hadronic molecule and a $c\bar{c}$ core. In this work, we perform a systematic study of the hidden-charm tetraquark candidates $X(3860)$, $X(3872)$, and $Z(3930)$ using a coupled-channel model that incorporates both $c\bar{c}$ states and $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ hadronic molecular components. The $c\bar{c}$ sector is described based on the constituent quark model predictions for the $χ_{cJ}(2P)$ ($J = 0, 1, 2$) states, while the meson-meson interactions are modeled using pseudoscalar and vector meson exchange potentials. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce the masses of the $X(3872)$ and $Z(3930)$, and the resulting framework is used to predict the mass and structure of the $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ state associated with the $X(3860)$. Our results support the mixture interpretation of these exotic hadrons, exhibiting strong attractions from the transition potential between $c\bar{c}$ and $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ components. The molecular component is found to dominate in the $X(3872)$, while the $c\bar{c}$ component plays a more prominent role in the $X(3860)$ and $Z(3930)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the X(3872), X(3860), and Z(3930) are mixture states of a bare charmonium core (the χcJ(2P) states predicted by constituent quark models) and D(*)D̅(*) hadronic molecular components, with the mixture interpretation supported by strong attractions from the transition potential between these components, but the specific composition varies: the molecular component dominates in the X(3872) while the c c̅ component plays a more prominent role in the X(3860) and Z(3930).

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation that couples bare χcJ(2P) states (J=0,1,2) from constituent quark models with D(*)D̅(*) hadronic molecular channels, including D-wave configurations and DsD̅s channels. The meson-meson interactions were modeled using one-boson-exchange potentials derived from heavy meson chiral Lagrangians, incorporating π, η, K, ρ, ω, K*, and φ exchanges with a cutoff parameter α varied between 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3. The transition potential between the c c̅ core and hadronic molecule components was introduced with two free parameters (gcc̅ and Λq), and the model parameters were fixed to reproduce the observed masses of the X(3872) and Z(3930).

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass: 3871.64 ± 0.06 MeV (JPC = 1++), used as input to fix model parameters
- Z(3930) mass: 3922.5 ± 1.0 MeV (JPC = 2++), used as input to fix model parameters
- X(3860) mass: 3862+50-35 MeV (JPC = 0++), the predicted state with large experimental uncertainties
- Pion decay constant fπ ≈ 93 MeV, used in the heavy meson chiral Lagrangian
- Cutoff parameter Λmeson = 220 MeV · α + mmeson, with α values of 0.7, 1.0, and 1.3 used to study cutoff dependence

## Context

This work extends the mixture model approach developed by Takizawa (2012) and Yamaguchi (2019) by incorporating more realistic hadron-hadron interactions between D(*) and D̅(*) mesons through pseudoscalar and vector meson exchange potentials, rather than simpler contact interactions. The study systematically analyzes the spin partners of the X(3872) (the X(3860) with JPC=0++ and Z(3930) with JPC=2++) which have been reported by Belle, BaBar, and LHCb experiments, and predicts the mass and structure of the X(3860) which currently has large experimental uncertainties. The results enable future experimental searches for the X(3860) with improved precision and provide a framework for understanding the internal structure of hidden-charm tetraquark candidates.
