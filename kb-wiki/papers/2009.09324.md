# Coupled-channel treatment of $^7\mathrm{Li}(n,γ)^8\mathrm{Li}$ in effective field theory

> The E1 contribution to the capture reaction $^7\mathrm{Li}(n,γ)^8\mathrm{Li}$ is calculated at low energies. We employ a coupled-channel formalism to account for the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ excited core contribution. We develop a halo effective field theory power counting where capture in the spin $S=2$ channel is enhanced over the $S=1$ channel. A next-to-leading order calculation is presented where the excited core contribution is shown to affect only the overall normalization of the cross section. The momentum dependence of the capture cross section, as a consequence, is the same in a theory with or without the excited $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ degree of freedom at this order of the calculation. The kinematical signature of the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ core is negligible at momenta below 1 MeV and significant only beyond the $3^+$ resonance energy, though still compatible with a next-to-next-to-leading order correction. We compare our formalism with a previous halo effective field theory calculation [Zhang, Nollett, and Phillips, Phys. Rev. C 89, 024613 (2014)] that also treated the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ core as an explicit degree of freedom. Our formal expressions and analysis disagree with this earlier work in several aspects.

## Key claim

The excited $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ core contribution to the $^7\mathrm{Li}(n,\gamma)^8\mathrm{Li}$ capture cross section affects only the overall normalization at next-to-leading order, meaning the momentum dependence of the cross section is the same whether or not the excited core is included as an explicit degree of freedom at this order; the kinematical signature of the excited core is negligible below 1 MeV and only becomes significant beyond the $3^+$ resonance energy, where it remains compatible with a next-to-next-to-leading order correction.

## Method

The authors develop a halo effective field theory (halo EFT) with a coupled-channel formalism that includes the $\frac{1}{2}^-$ excited state of $^7\mathrm{Li}$ ($^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$) as an explicit degree of freedom, alongside the $\frac{3}{2}^-$ ground state core and the neutron. They construct two EFTs: one without the excited core (EFT$_\text{gs}$) and one with it (EFT$_\star$), using auxiliary dimer fields to mediate neutron-core interactions and performing coupled-channel calculations for $^3S_1$-$^3S_1^\star$ and $^3P_2$-$^3P_2^\star$ mixing. The E1 capture cross section is calculated at next-to-leading order, with the power counting arranged so that capture in the spin $S=2$ channel is enhanced over the $S=1$ channel.

## Key numbers

- Binding momenta of $^8\mathrm{Li}$ states: $\gamma_0 \approx 57.78$ MeV for the $2^+$ ground state with the $^7\mathrm{Li}$ core, $\gamma_\star \approx 64.22$ MeV for the $2^+$ ground state with the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ core, $\gamma_1 \approx 41.56$ MeV for the $1^+$ excited state with the $^7\mathrm{Li}$ core, $\gamma_{1\star} \approx 50.12$ MeV for the $1^+$ excited state with the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ core.
- Excitation energy of $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$: $E_\star = 0.47761$ MeV, corresponding to a low-momentum scale $\gamma_\Delta = \sqrt{2\mu E_\star} \approx 28.0$ MeV.
- Scattering length in the $^3S_1$ channel: $a_0^{(1)} = 0.87 \pm 0.07$ fm.
- The breakdown scale $\Lambda$ is estimated at 100-150 MeV from pion physics and core breakup, with a possible larger value $\Lambda \gtrsim 200$ MeV from $p$-wave effective momenta; the ratio $Q/\Lambda \sim 1/3$ is assumed.

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT treatment of $^7\mathrm{Li}(n,\gamma)^8\mathrm{Li}$ from Rupak (2011) and Fernando (2011), which did not include the $^7\mathrm{Li}^\star$ excited core explicitly, by incorporating a coupled-channel formalism similar to Cohen (2004) and Lensky (2011). It directly addresses criticisms raised by Zhang, Nollett, and Phillips (2014, Phys. Rev. C 89, 024613), who argued that the excited core must be included at leading order given its excitation energy of 0.5 MeV is smaller than the neutron separation energy of $^8\mathrm{Li}$ (2 MeV). The present analysis disagrees with several aspects of the Zhang et al. formalism and shows that the excited core contribution is a next-to-leading order effect that only changes the overall normalization, not the momentum dependence, of the cross section. This enables future work to incorporate M1 capture from the $3^+$ resonance and extend the coupled-channel treatment to additional $p$-wave channels.
