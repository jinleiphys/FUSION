# Bulk viscosity transport coefficients in neutron star mergers

> We compute first and second-order bulk-viscous transport properties due to weak-interaction processes in $npe$ matter in the neutrino transparent regime. The transport coefficients characterize the out-of-beta-equilibrium pressure corrections, which depend on the weak-interaction rates and the equation of state. We calculate these coefficients for realistic equations of state and show they are sensitive to changes in the nuclear symmetry energy $J$ and its slope $L$.

## Key claim
The paper computes first- and second-order bulk-viscous transport coefficients for neutrino-transparent npe matter in neutron star mergers, showing that these coefficients depend explicitly on the bulk viscous scalar (the out-of-equilibrium pressure correction) and are sensitive to the nuclear symmetry energy J and its slope L, with the caveat that the results apply specifically to the neutrino transparent regime where weak interactions are not fast enough to restore beta equilibrium.

## Method
The authors use a resummed Israel-Stewart theory derived from a two-component reactive fluid mixture, where the bulk scalar Pi = P - P_eq represents deviations from beta equilibrium. They compute the transport coefficients (bulk viscosity zeta and relaxation time tau_Pi) for npe matter using Walecka-like relativistic mean-field models with parameters consistent with mass-radius observations, considering direct and modified Urca processes in the neutrino transparent regime. The calculations systematically vary the nuclear symmetry energy J and its slope L within existing constraints to explore their impact on the transport coefficients.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density n_sat = 0.148 fm^{-3} (used as reference density for symmetry energy expansion)
- Temperature T = 2 MeV (used for all displayed results at saturation density)
- The symmetry energy is modeled as E_sym(n_B) = J + (L/3)(n_B/n_sat - 1) + higher order terms, where J is the symmetry energy at saturation and L its slope
- Results shown at two times saturation density for bulk pressure nonlinearity (Figure 1)

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Gavassino (2023) who showed that a two-component reactive fluid mixture can be rewritten as a resummed Israel-Stewart theory with transport coefficients depending on the bulk viscous scalar. It builds on prior work by Alford (2017, 2018, 2021, 2023) on bulk-viscous dissipation in neutron star mergers and the damping of density oscillations in the post-merger phase. The results enable future studies of gravitational-wave signatures from bulk-viscous effects in neutron star mergers, as discussed by Most (2021, 2022) and Chabanov (2023).
