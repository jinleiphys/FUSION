# Constraining the Low-$p_T$ $η/π^0$ Ratio for Direct-Photon Analyses with Blast-Wave Fits to $π$, $K$, and $p$ Spectra

> We predict the $η/π^0$ ratio at low $p_T$ ($p_T \lesssim 3~\mathrm{GeV}/c$) using the measured charged $K/π$ ratio and model input from a blast-wave framework with feeddown contributions. This approach can provide improved, data-constrained background estimates for direct-photon and dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions. In this approach, the explicit modeling of radial flow and hadronic feeddown enables an uncertainty estimate for the low-$p_T$ extrapolation of the $η/π^0$ ratio. Using central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV as an example, we find that the $η$-related decay-photon uncertainty at $p_T \approx 1~\mathrm{GeV}/c$ is of order 10\% of the expected direct-photon signal.

# Constraining the Low-$p_T$ $\eta/\pi^0$ Ratio for Direct-Photon Analyses with Blast-Wave Fits to $\pi$, $K$, and $p$ Spectra

## Key claim
The $\eta/\pi^0$ ratio at low $p_T$ ($p_T \lesssim 3$ GeV/$c$) can be predicted using the measured charged $K/\pi$ ratio combined with a blast-wave model that includes resonance feeddown, enabling an uncertainty estimate for the low-$p_T$ extrapolation. The method yields predictions numerically close to the Ren-Drees approach, but makes the underlying physical ingredients (radial flow, feeddown contributions, particle-mass dependence) explicit.

## Method
The authors determine the low-$p_T$ $\eta/\pi^0$ ratio by multiplying a model double ratio $[(\eta/\pi^0)/(K/\pi)]_{\mathrm{model}}$ from a blast-wave fit (with feeddown from short-lived resonances) by the measured charged $K/\pi$ ratio. The blast-wave model parameters (kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_\mathrm{kin}$, surface velocity $\beta_s$, radial flow profile exponent $n$) are obtained from simultaneous fits to measured $\pi$, $K$, and $p$ spectra in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. Feeddown kernels are precomputed using PYTHIA 8 decay simulations for hadrons up to $m=2$ GeV/$c^2$ with proper decay length $c\tau < 1$ mm, augmented with additional hadron states from the Thermal-FIST PDG-2025 hadron list.

## Key numbers
- The $\eta$-related decay-photon uncertainty at $p_T \approx 1$ GeV/$c$ is of order 10% of the expected direct-photon signal in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV.
- The overall feeddown contribution to the $\eta$ spectrum is about 40% for $p_T < 3$ GeV/$c$ in 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV.
- For $p_T \gtrsim 5$ GeV/$c$, the $\eta/\pi^0$ ratio reaches an approximately constant value of $r = 0.487 \pm 0.024$ in nucleus-nucleus collisions, independent of collision energy and centrality.
- The flow-scaling construction gives an effective radial-flow velocity of $\beta = 0.6972$ for 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, obtained from a scan minimizing the spread of scaled hadron spectra in the fit interval $0.5 < p_T < 2.5$ GeV/$c$.
- A relative uncertainty of $\pm 5\%$ is assigned to the double ratio $(\eta/\pi^0)/(K^+/\pi^+)$ from the blast-wave plus feeddown approach, based on comparison with the FluiduM hydrodynamic simulation.

## Context
This work extends previous approaches to constraining the low-$p_T$ $\eta/\pi^0$ ratio, including $m_T$ scaling used by WA98 (2000) and PHENIX (2008), the Tsallis blast-wave model used by STAR (2016), and the Ren-Drees method (2021) which assumes $\eta/\pi^0$ in AA collisions equals the pp ratio scaled by the ratio of $K/\pi$ in AA to pp. The new method improves upon these by explicitly modeling radial flow and hadronic feeddown within a unified framework, enabling controlled extrapolation of the $\eta$ meson spectrum toward low $p_T$. This approach can provide improved, data-constrained background estimates for direct-photon and dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions, addressing a typical bottleneck in such analyses.
