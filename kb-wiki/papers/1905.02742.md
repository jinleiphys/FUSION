# Partonic angular momentum in the nucleon's chiral periphery

> We study the nucleon's partonic angular momentum (AM) content at peripheral transverse distances $b = \mathcal{O}(M_π^{-1})$, where the structure is governed by chiral dynamics. We compute the nucleon form factors of the energy-momentum tensor in chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) and construct the transverse densities of AM at fixed light-front time. In the periphery the spin density is suppressed, and the AM is predominantly orbital. In the first-quantized representation of ChEFT in light-front form, the field-theoretical AM density coincides with the quantum-mechanical orbital AM density of the soft pions in the nucleon's periphery.

## Key claim

At peripheral transverse distances of order the inverse pion mass, the nucleon's partonic angular momentum density is predominantly orbital, while the spin density is suppressed; this conclusion relies only on chiral symmetry, the quantum numbers of the angular momentum operators, and the restriction to peripheral distances, and is therefore robust.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon form factors of the energy-momentum tensor in chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) and constructed the transverse densities of angular momentum at fixed light-front time. The calculation was performed in two ways: (I) by computing the two-pion spectral functions of the invariant form factors A and B in the invariant formulation of ChEFT and constructing the peripheral densities through a dispersion relation; (II) by computing the nucleon matrix element of the EMT component T^{+T} in the light-front formulation of ChEFT and constructing the angular momentum density through overlap integrals of light-front wave functions describing the transition of the initial nucleon to intermediate pion-baryon states (B = N, Δ).

## Key numbers

- The peripheral densities decay at large b as ∼exp(−2 M_π b) for the "leading" densities (ρ_A, ρ_B) and at least ∼exp(−3 M_π b) for the "suppressed" ones (ρ_S, ρ_D).
- The contribution from intermediate N states dominates the peripheral AM density and is positive; the contribution from intermediate Δ states is negative and numerically small.
- In the large-N_c limit, the intermediate N and Δ contributions become equal and opposite: ⟨L^z⟩[B=Δ] = −⟨L^z⟩[B=N], ensuring the total peripheral AM density scales as O(N_c^0) at b ∼ M_π^{-1}.
- The ChEFT expressions are applicable only at large distances b ≳ 2 M_π^{-1}; at smaller distances the densities are strongly modified by ππ rescattering effects.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of peripheral nucleon structure using ChEFT, which had computed transverse charge and current densities (Strikman 2010, Granados 2013, 2015, 2016) and mechanical properties at large distances. The results validate the concept of transverse angular momentum densities at fixed light-front time (Lorce 2017, Adhikari 2016) and provide a simple mechanical picture of peripheral nucleon structure. The approach enables future calculations of the T^{++} current in the nucleon's periphery, transverse angular momentum densities for transverse nucleon polarization, and inclusion of ππ rescattering effects through unitarity-based methods (Granados 2017, Alarcon 2017).
