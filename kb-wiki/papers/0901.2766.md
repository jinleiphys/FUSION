# Nonperturbative $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ NN scattering in pionless EFT

> The rigorous on-shell $T$-matrices for $NN$ scattering in the coupled channels $^3S_1$$-$$^3D_1$ are briefly presented in the context of EFT($\notπ$) with the contact potentials truncated at order $Δ=4$. The nonperturbative features of renormalization are highlighted and elaborated. A simple scenario for EFT power counting's and renormalization prescriptions is also presented with its consequences being roughly analyzed and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nonperturbative renormalization features previously demonstrated in the uncoupled \(^1S_0\) channel are universal and also hold in the coupled \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels, but only within the context of contact potentials truncated at order \(\Delta=4\) in pionless EFT (EFT(\(\not\!\pi\))). The key hedge is that the inverse \(T\)-matrix separates into a universal divergent part \(\mathcal{I}_0\) plus a real rational part that is independent of \(\mathcal{I}_0\), a property that is proven only for contact potentials and may not extend to pionful EFT without further work.

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) for coupled-channel \(NN\) scattering in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) partial waves using contact potentials truncated at chiral order \(\Delta=4\). By factorizing the potentials and \(T\)-matrices into a column vector \(U(q) = (1, q^2, q^4)^T\) and matrices \(\lambda\) and \(\tau\), the LSE reduces to an algebraic matrix equation. The on-shell \(T\)-matrices are then expressed in closed form, and their inverse is shown to satisfy \(\partial_{\mathcal{I}_0}(\mathbf{T}^{-1} - \mathcal{I}_0 \mathbf{I}) = 0\), isolating the divergent integral \(\mathcal{I}_0\) from the real rational parts.

## Key numbers

* The contact EFT couplings scale as \(C_{\Delta;\ldots}/C_{0;ss} \sim \Lambda^{-\Delta}\), with \(\Lambda\) the upper EFT scale.
* The divergent integral \(\mathcal{I}_0\) is parametrized as \(\mathcal{I}_0 = J_0 + iMp/(4\pi)\), where \(J_0\) is a real constant and \(p = \sqrt{ME}\).
* In the simple scenario, \(J_0 \sim (M/(4\pi))\Lambda\) and \(J_{2n+1} \sim (M/(4\pi))(\epsilon\Lambda)^{2n+1}\) with \(\epsilon \ll 1\).
* For the \(^3S_1\) channel, the scattering length is unnatural: \(1/a \sim \Lambda \mathcal{O}(\epsilon)\), while the effective range is natural: \(r_e \sim \mathcal{O}(1)/\Lambda\).
* For the \(^3D_1\) channel, the scattering length is also unnatural: \(1/a \sim \Lambda^5 \mathcal{O}(\epsilon)\), due to cancellation in the numerator of the rational term in \(1/T_{dd}\).

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative renormalization analysis of Yang (2007) from the uncoupled \(^1S_0\) channel to the coupled \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels, confirming the universality of the \(\mathcal{I}_0\)-independence property argued in Yang (2008). It contrasts with the partly perturbative KSW approach (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998), where the fine-tuning occurs only at lowest order and the scale \(J_0\) is an ordinary running parameter, whereas here it is a renormalization-group invariant. The results enable future comparisons with other power-counting schemes such as those of Weinberg (1990) and provide a framework for treating nonperturbative divergences in short-range interactions, potentially including pion-exchange potentials.
