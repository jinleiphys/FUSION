# Coupled-channel calculation of bound and resonant spectra of Lambda-9Be and Lambda-13C hypernuclei

> A Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) approach has been used to analyze the spectra of two hyper-nuclear systems, Lambda-9Be and Lambda-13C. The splitting of the two odd-parity excited levels (1/2^- and 3/2^-) at 11 MeV excitation in Lambda-13C is driven mainly by the weak Lambda-nucleus spin-orbit force, but the splittings of the 3/2^+ and 5/2^+ levels in both Lambda-9Be and Lambda-13C have a different origin. These cases appear to be dominated by coupling to the collective 2+ states of the core nuclei. Using simple phenomenological potentials as input to the MCAS method, the observed splitting and level ordering in Lambda-9Be is reproduced with the addition of a weak spin-spin interaction acting between the hyperon and the spin of the excited target. With no such spin-spin interaction, the level ordering in Lambda-9Be is inverted with respect to that currently observed. In both hyper-nuclei, our calculations suggest that there are additional low-lying resonant states in the Lambda-nucleus continua.

## Key claim

The splitting and ordering of the positive-parity doublet (3/2+ and 5/2+) in Lambda-9Be can be reproduced by the MCAS method only when a weak spin-spin interaction (V_sI = -0.1 MeV) is added between the hyperon and the spin of the excited target; without this term, the level ordering is inverted relative to the observed spectrum, even though the magnitude of the splitting remains consistent with experiment.

## Method

The authors applied the Multi-Channel Algebraic Scattering (MCAS) method, which solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space using expansions of the interaction potentials in finite sums of energy-independent separable terms built from sturmian functions. They modeled the hypernuclei as a Lambda hyperon coupled to the ground state and low-lying collective excitations (specifically the 2+ states) of the core nuclei (Be-8 and C-12), using phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials with central, spin-orbit, and spin-spin components. The deformation of the core was treated via a rotational model with a quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2, and the Pauli principle was not applied since the hyperon is distinguishable from nucleons.

## Key numbers

- For Lambda-9Be: ground state (1/2+) binding energy calculated as -6.73 MeV (Case 1 and Case 2), compared to experimental -6.71 MeV (emulsion) or -5.99 MeV (KEK E336 experiment)
- For Lambda-9Be: 3/2+ state at -3.63 MeV and 5/2+ state at -3.70 MeV (Case 2 with spin-spin), compared to experimental -3.64 MeV and -3.69 MeV respectively
- For Lambda-9Be: predicted resonant states at 4.68-4.92 MeV above threshold with widths of 3.8-4.9 keV (7/2+ and 9/2+)
- For Lambda-13C: 3/2+ state at -7.08 MeV and 5/2+ state at -7.24 MeV (Case 2), compared to experimental -6.81 MeV for both (unresolved doublet)
- For Lambda-13C: ground state (1/2+) at -11.68 MeV, compared to experimental -11.69 MeV
- For Lambda-13C: predicted odd-parity resonant states between +3.02 MeV and +4.66 MeV above threshold, with widths ranging from less than 0.1 keV to 0.23 MeV
- Lambda-nucleus central potential depth: -26.4 MeV for Be-8 core (R0=2.3 fm) and -28.9 to -30.4 MeV for C-12 core (R0=2.6 fm)
- Lambda-nucleus spin-orbit strength: 0.35 MeV for both systems
- Deformation parameters: beta_2 = 0.7 for Be-8, beta_2 = -0.52 for C-12

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus systems by Amos (2003) and Canton (2006), to hypernuclear systems by scaling the nucleon-core interaction parameters (reducing central depth by about 30%, radius by 15%, and spin-orbit by an order of magnitude). It contrasts with cluster-model calculations by Hiyama (2000), who treated Lambda-9Be as an alpha-alpha-Lambda three-body system, and with shell-model calculations by Millener (2005, 2009), which included Lambda-Sigma coupling. The results suggest that collective core excitations play a dominant role in the positive-parity doublet splittings, contrary to mean-field approaches that found collectivity less important in hypernuclei (Ma 2008). The predicted low-lying resonant states in the Lambda-nucleus continua could be targets for future experimental searches with high-precision gamma-ray spectroscopy.
