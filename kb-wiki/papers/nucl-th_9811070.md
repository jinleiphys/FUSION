# J/psi and eta_c in the nuclear medium: QCD sum rule approach

> We investigate the masses of the lowest c\bar{c} states, the J/psi and eta_c, in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules. Up to dimension four, the differences between the operator product expansions in vacuum and in medium arise from the density-dependent change in the gluon condensate and from a new contribution proportional to the nucleon expectation value of the twist-2 gluon operator. Both terms together give an attractive shift of about 5-10 MeV to the J/psi and eta_c masses in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis predicts an attractive mass shift of about 5-10 MeV for both the J/psi and eta_c in nuclear matter at normal nuclear density, with the caveat that this result relies on the operator product expansion truncated at dimension four and the linear low-density approximation for in-medium condensates.

## Method

The authors compute the current-current correlation function for vector (J/psi) and pseudoscalar (eta_c) currents in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules, expanding the operator product expansion up to dimension four. They use the linear low-density approximation to relate in-medium condensates to nucleon expectation values, including both the scalar gluon condensate and a new contribution from the twist-2 gluon operator. The mass is extracted from ratios of moments of the polarization function evaluated at a fixed Euclidean point, using the same moment range (n=7 to 11) and parameter choices as the vacuum analysis of Reinders et al. (1981, 1985).

## Key numbers

- Mass shift for J/psi in nuclear matter at normal density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}): Delta m_psi = -7 MeV
- Mass shift for eta_c in nuclear matter at normal density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}): Delta m_eta_c = -5 MeV
- Effective gluon condensate change at normal nuclear density: <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_0 -> <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_0 (1 - 0.06 rho_N / rho_0)
- Vacuum gluon condensate value: <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_0 = (0.35 GeV)^4
- Twist-2 gluon parameter: A_G(8 m_c^2) = 0.9, representing twice the momentum fraction carried by gluons in the nucleon
- Resulting J/psi- and eta_c-nucleon scattering lengths: a = (0.1-0.2) fm
- Chiral limit nucleon mass used: m_N^0 = 750 MeV
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.24 GeV
- Strong coupling used: alpha_s(8 m_c^2) = 0.21

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach, previously applied to light quark systems such as nucleons (Drukarev and Levin, 1991; Hatsuda, 1991; Tomozawa, 1991) and vector mesons (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992), to heavy quark systems where uncertainties from four-quark condensates are absent. The predicted mass shifts are surprisingly close to those reported in earlier calculations by Luke et al. (1992), Brodsky et al. (1997), and Teramond et al. (1998) using different methods. The results enable more reliable estimates of charmonium-nucleon interactions under normal nuclear conditions, which is important for interpreting J/psi suppression signals in heavy-ion collisions and for assessing the possibility of charmonium bound states with light nuclei as proposed by Brodsky et al. (1990).
