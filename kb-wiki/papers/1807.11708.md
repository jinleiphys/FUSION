# Fermionic spectral functions with the Functional Renormalization Group

> We present first results on the calculation of fermionic spectral functions from analytically continued flow equations within the Functional Renormalization Group approach. Our method is based on the same analytic continuation from imaginary to real frequencies that was developed and used previously for bosonic spectral functions. In order to demonstrate the applicability of the method also for fermionic correlations we apply it here to the real-time quark propagator in the quark-meson model and calculate the corresponding quark spectral functions in the vacuum.

## Key claim

The authors present the first FRG calculation of fermionic spectral functions obtained from analytically continued flow equations, demonstrating the method's applicability to the quark propagator in the quark-meson model, but restrict themselves to vacuum and vanishing external spatial momenta as a first step.

## Method

The authors solve the Wetterich flow equation for the effective potential of the quark-meson model using both a grid method and a Taylor-expansion method, then derive and solve analytically continued flow equations for the retarded quark two-point function's dressing functions \(B_k(\omega)\) and \(C_k(\omega)\) directly in the real-frequency domain. The analytic continuation is performed by treating the Euclidean energy variable as a general complex argument \(z = ip_0\) with \(z = \omega + i\epsilon\) to obtain retarded boundary conditions, using three-dimensional regulator functions that allow analytic integration over the energy component.

## Key numbers

- UV cutoff scale \(\Lambda = 1000\) MeV; UV parameters: \(m_\Lambda/\Lambda = 0.794\), \(\lambda_\Lambda = 2.00\), \(c/\Lambda^3 = 0.00175\), Yukawa coupling \(h = 3.2\)
- IR observables from grid method: \(\sigma_0 \equiv f_\pi = 93.5\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(m_\sigma = 509\) MeV, \(m_\psi = 299\) MeV
- IR observables from Taylor method: \(\sigma_0 \equiv f_\pi = 90.1\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV, \(m_\sigma = 534\) MeV, \(m_\psi = 288\) MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale occurs at \(k \approx 600\) MeV where pion and sigma masses become different
- UV initial condition for mass dressing function: \(B_{k=\Lambda} = h\sigma_{0,\text{IR}} = 299\) MeV (grid method) versus \(B_{k=\Lambda} = h\sigma_{0,\text{UV}} = 8.9\) MeV (Taylor method)

## Context

This work extends the analytic continuation method previously developed for bosonic spectral functions in the quark-meson model by Tripolt et al. (2014, 2017) to fermionic correlations, enabling direct calculation of real-time quark propagators without numerical reconstruction methods such as Bayesian approaches used by Qin et al. (2013) and Fischer, Pawlowski, Rothkopf et al. (2017) for Dyson-Schwinger equations. The approach enables future extension to finite temperature and density, and provides a framework for studying quark spectral functions that can be compared with results from Euclidean FRG calculations by Cyrol, Mitter, Pawlowski et al. (2017).
