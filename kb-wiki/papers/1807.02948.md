# van der Waals hadron resonance gas and QCD phase diagram

> Taking into account the recently developed van der Waals (VDW) like equation of state (EoS) for grand canonical ensemble of fermions, the temperature dependent profiles of normalized entropy density ($s /T^3$) and the ratio of shear viscosity and entropy density ($η/ s$) for hadron resonance gas have been evaluated. The VDW parameters, corresponding to interactions between (anti)baryons, have been obtained by contrasting lattice EoS for QCD matter at finite chemical potentials ($μ_{B}$) and for $T \le$ 160 MeV. The temperature and chemical potential dependent study of $s /T^3$ and $η/s$ for hadron gas, by signalling onsets of first order phase transition and crossover in the hadronic phase of QCD matter, helps in understanding the QCD phase diagram in the ($T, μ_{B}$) - plane. An estimation of probable location of critical point matches predictions from other recent studies.

# van der Waals hadron resonance gas and QCD phase diagram

## Key claim

The temperature-dependent profiles of normalized entropy density ($s/T^3$) and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($\eta/s$) for hadron resonance gas, calculated using a van der Waals equation of state with interaction constants obtained by fitting lattice QCD data at finite baryon chemical potential ($\mu_B/T = 1.0$ and $2.0$), can identify the onset of first-order phase transition and crossover in the hadronic phase, thereby estimating the probable location of the QCD critical point, but only for the hadronic phase, not the full QCD phase diagram including partonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors constructed a van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model where attractive and repulsive interactions between (anti)baryons are described by van der Waals constants $a$ and $b$, while meson repulsion is treated via excluded volume with a hard-core radius $r_M = 0.2$ fm. The VDW parameters were obtained by simultaneously fitting lattice QCD results for pressure and energy density at $\mu_B/T = 1.0$ and $2.0$ for temperatures $T \le 160$ MeV, yielding $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.15/26$. Shear viscosity was calculated using an analytical formula from molecular kinetic theory for a multi-component gas, and entropy density was computed from the VDWHRG thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- VDW interaction constants obtained from lattice fit: $a = 1250$ MeV fm$^3$ and $b = 5.75$ fm$^3$ (corresponding to baryon hard-core radius $\sim 0.62$ fm)
- Meson hard-core radius fixed at $r_M = 0.2$ fm
- $\eta/s$ at $\mu_B = 0$ and $T = 160$ MeV (crossover temperature) is approximately $0.69$
- For interaction constants $a = 329$ MeV fm$^3$ and $b = 3.42$ fm$^3$ (nuclear matter ground state), the point between first-order transition and crossover appears at $T \simeq 19.5$ MeV and $\mu_B \simeq 910$ MeV
- For the lattice-fitted constants ($a = 1250$ MeV fm$^3$, $b = 5.75$ fm$^3$), the point between first-order transition and crossover appears at $T \simeq 62.1$ MeV and $\mu_B \simeq 720$ MeV
- Probable QCD critical point estimated at $T \sim 65$ MeV and $\mu_B \sim 715$ MeV
- Lattice results exclude the possibility of a critical point at $T \gtrsim 135$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals HRG approach previously applied to nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions (refs. 24, 25) and to QCD at zero chemical potential (ref. 26), by fitting interaction constants directly to lattice QCD data at finite $\mu_B$ from ref. 22. The study complements prior estimates of the QCD critical point in VDWHRG models (ref. 27) and addresses the need for hadronic-phase equations of state for interpreting Beam Energy Scan data at RHIC, as well as future experiments at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI. The results enable identification of regions in the QCD phase diagram where first-order phase transition and crossover occur, though the authors note that quantitative $\eta/s$ values from their approximate kinetic theory formula should be interpreted cautiously.
