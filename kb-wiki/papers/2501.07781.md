# Self-similar inverse cascade from generalized symmetries

> We investigate the role of generalized symmetries in driving non-equilibrium and non-linear phenomena, specifically focusing on turbulent systems. While conventional turbulence studies have revealed inverse cascades driven by conserved quantities integrated over the entire space, such as helicity in three spatial dimensions, the influence of higher-form symmetries, whose conserved charges are defined by integration over subspaces, remains largely unexplored. We demonstrate a novel mechanism where higher-form symmetries naturally induce a self-similar inverse cascade. Taking axion electrodynamics with non-linear topological interaction as a paradigmatic example, we show that the conserved charge associated with its 1-form symmetry drives the system toward large-scale coherent structures through a universal scaling behavior characterized by analytically determined scaling exponents. Our findings suggest that higher-form symmetries can provide a fundamental organizing principle for understanding non-equilibrium phenomena and the emergence of coherent structures in turbulent systems.

# Self-similar inverse cascade from generalized symmetries

## Key claim

Higher-form symmetries, whose conserved charges are defined by integration over subspaces rather than the entire volume, can naturally induce a self-similar inverse cascade in turbulent systems. Using axion electrodynamics with a nonlinear topological interaction as a paradigmatic example, the authors show that the conserved charge associated with its 1-form symmetry drives the system toward large-scale coherent structures through a universal scaling behavior, but only when dissipative effects are included and the system is coupled to gapped charged matter with no Ohmic current.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the reduced (1+1)-dimensional equations of motion for axion electrodynamics in momentum space, assuming homogeneity along two spatial directions and periodic boundary conditions along the remaining direction. They include a dissipative term for the axion field as a phenomenological low-energy effective theory assumption, and analyze the late-time evolution of spectral functions for energy and topological charge to extract scaling exponents from a self-similar ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Scaling exponents: alpha = 1, beta = 1/2, characterizing the late-time evolution of the system
- The instability parameter A(t) decays as t^{-1/2} at late times
- The spectral function g(t,k) scales as g(t,k) ~ t * g-tilde(t^{1/2} k)
- Numerical simulation parameters: L = 1000, N_k = 200, sigma = 1.0, e = 1.0, v = 1
- Initial conserved charge N = 24 pi^2 L, giving initial A(0) approximately 3.0
- The dispersion relation shows unstable modes in the infrared region 0 < |k_1| < C e E-bar_3 / v

## Context

This work extends the concept of generalized symmetries, introduced by Gaiotto (2014), from equilibrium physics into strongly nonlinear non-equilibrium phenomena. It builds on prior studies of the chiral plasma instability (CPI) by Joyce (1997) and Akamatsu (2013), where helicity conservation drives inverse cascade, and generalizes that mechanism to higher-form symmetries. The scaling exponents found here coincide with those observed in turbulence after CPI by Tashiro (2012), Hirono (2015), Yamamoto (2016), Buividovich (2015), and Mace (2019), suggesting a possible broader universality class. The work enables future investigation of whether similar dynamics occur in condensed matter systems with emergent axion fields and in cosmological settings such as gauge field amplification during inflation.
