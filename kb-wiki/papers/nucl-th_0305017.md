# BBN and Lambda_QCD

> Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) has increasingly become the tool of choice for investigating the permitted variation of fundamental constants during the earliest epochs of the Universe. Here we present a BBN calculation that has been modified to permit changes in the QCD scale, Lambda_QCD. The primary effects of changing the QCD scale upon BBN are through the deuteron binding energy, B_D, and the neutron-proton mass difference, delta-m_np, which both play crucial roles in determining the primordial abundances. In this paper, we show how a simplified BBN calculation allows us to restrict the nuclear data we need to just B_D and delta-m_np yet still gives useful results so that any variation in Lambda_QCD may be constrained via the corresponding shifts in B_D and delta-m_np by using the current estimates of the primordial deuterium abundance and helium mass fraction. The simplification predicts the helium-4 and deuterium abundances to within 1% and 50% respectively when compared with the results of a standard BBN code. But Lambda_QCD also affects much of remaining required nuclear input so this method introduces a systematic error into the calculation and we find a degeneracy between B_D and delta-m_np. We show how increased understanding of the relationship of the pion mass and/or B_D to other nuclear parameters, such as the binding energy of tritium and the cross section of T + D -> He4 + n, would yield constraints upon any change in B_D and delta-m_np at the 10% level.

## Key claim

The authors present a simplified BBN calculation that restricts the nuclear data needed to just the deuteron binding energy (B_D) and the neutron-proton mass difference (δm_np) to constrain variations in Λ_QCD, but acknowledge that this method introduces a systematic error and a degeneracy between B_D and δm_np because Λ_QCD also affects much of the remaining required nuclear input.

## Method

The authors modified a standard BBN code to allow changes in the QCD scale Λ_QCD, focusing on how variations in Λ_QCD propagate through the deuteron binding energy B_D and the neutron-proton mass difference δm_np. They used a simplified BBN calculation that treats only these two parameters as free, while keeping all other nuclear inputs fixed at their standard values, and compared the predicted primordial abundances of helium-4 and deuterium to observational constraints. The neutron-proton interconversion rates were calculated using Born approximation expressions that include δm_np, while the deuterium formation rate (n + p → D + γ) was taken from low-energy effective field theory as a function of B_D.

## Key numbers

- The simplified BBN calculation predicts helium-4 abundance to within 1% and deuterium abundance to within 50% when compared with the results of a standard BBN code.
- The freeze-out temperature for neutron/proton weak equilibrium is T_np ~ 1.2 MeV, determined by δm_np.
- The temperature at which BBN begins (deuterium abundance peaks) is T_BBN ≈ B_D/26 = 86 keV, corresponding to t_BBN = 180 s.
- The neutron-proton mass difference is approximately δm_np = 1.29 MeV, with the electromagnetic contribution αM_elm ≈ 0.76 MeV and the down-up quark mass difference approximately 2 MeV.
- The time-temperature relationship before electron/positron annihilation is t T² = 0.74 MeV² s, and after annihilation becomes t T_γ² = 1.32 MeV² s.
- The baryon-to-photon ratio used is η = 5.6 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The relationship between shifts in Λ_QCD and the fine structure constant is given as ΔΛ_QCD/Λ_QCD ~ 30 Δα/α.
- Improved understanding of the relationship of the pion mass and/or B_D to other nuclear parameters would yield constraints upon any change in B_D and δm_np at the 10% level.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fundamental constant variation in BBN, particularly those by Bergström, Iguri and Rubinstein (1999) and Nollett and Lopez (2002) on varying fine structure constant, and by Flambaum and Shuryak (2002) on quark masses and Λ_QCD constraints from BBN and the Oklo reactor. The authors build on the relationship between Λ_QCD and other constants from Langacker (2001) and Calmet (2002), who found ΔΛ_QCD/Λ_QCD ~ 30 Δα/α in grand unified theories. The paper enables future work to break the degeneracy between B_D and δm_np by incorporating improved nuclear physics understanding of how the pion mass affects other nuclear parameters such as the tritium binding energy and the T + D → He4 + n cross section.
