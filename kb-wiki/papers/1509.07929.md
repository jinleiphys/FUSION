# Perturbative results for two and three particle threshold energies in finite volume

> We calculate the energy of the state closest to threshold for two and three identical, spinless particles confined to a cubic spatial volume with periodic boundary conditions and with zero total momentum in the finite-volume frame. The calculation is performed in relativistic quantum field theory with particles coupled via a $λϕ^4$ interaction, and we work through order $λ^3$. The energy shifts begin at ${\cal O}(1/L^3)$, and we keep subleading terms proportional to $1/L^4$, $1/L^5$ and $1/L^6$. These terms allow a non-trivial check of the results obtained from quantization conditions that hold for arbitrary interactions, namely that of Lüscher for two particles and our recently developed formalism for three particles. We also compare to previously obtained results based on non-relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the energy shift of the state closest to threshold for two and three identical, spinless particles in a cubic volume through order λ³ in λφ⁴ theory, keeping terms up to 1/L⁶, to provide a non-trivial check of the general quantization conditions of Lüscher (for two particles) and the authors' recently developed formalism for three particles, with the caveat that the 1/L⁶ term for three particles cannot serve as a check because the three-particle interaction enters at this order and there is no a priori relation between non-relativistic and relativistic three-body interactions.

## Method

The authors compute the finite-volume correlation functions C₂(τ) and C₃(τ) for even and odd particle-number states in λφ⁴ theory through order λ₀³, using Feynman diagrams with zero total momentum and periodic boundary conditions on a cubic volume of side L. They isolate the threshold state by removing exponentially growing far-subthreshold and exponentially falling excited-state contributions by hand, then extract the threshold energy shift ΔEⱼ,ₜₕᵣ from the constant and linear terms in a τ-expansion of the subtracted correlator, converting finite-volume momentum sums to integrals plus power-law residues using methods from Lüscher (1986, 1991) and Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015).

## Key numbers

- The leading two-particle threshold energy shift at order λ₀ is ΔE₂,ₜₕᵣ⁽¹⁾ = -1/(8m²L³), from the connected single-vertex diagram.
- The two-particle energy shift at order λ₀² includes a term proportional to 1/(256π²m³L⁴) from the finite-volume residue ℐ/(256π²m³L⁴) and a term 5/(256m⁵L⁶).
- The two-particle constant term at order λ₀² includes a 1/L² contribution -𝒥/(1024π⁴m²L²) and a 1/L³ contribution A₂/(16m³L³) + I⁽⁰·²⁾/(64m³L³) with I⁽⁰·²⁾ = 1/(8π).
- The three-particle threshold energy shift begins at O(1/L³), with subleading terms kept through O(1/L⁶).
- The subtracted three-particle amplitude at threshold is calculated through cubic order using the subtraction defined in Hansen and Sharpe (2015).

## Context

This work extends the general finite-volume quantization conditions of Lüscher (1986, 1991) for two particles and Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) for three particles by providing explicit perturbative checks in λφ⁴ theory. The results agree with non-relativistic quantum mechanics calculations by Beane (2007) and Tan (2007) for the 1/L³, 1/L⁴, and 1/L⁵ terms, but the 1/L⁶ term for three particles cannot be compared due to scheme dependence of the three-body interaction. The calculation enables future tests of the three-particle formalism and provides a method for determining the relation between relativistic and non-relativistic three-body interactions.
