# Numerical study of the density of states for the bag model

> The density of states for an extended MIT bag model is studied numerically by using a parameterized smooth representation which provides the best fit to the numerical data. It is found that the mass dependence of the surface term in the density of states agrees with that derived from multi-reflection theory calculation. The mass dependence of the curvature term in the density of states is extracted for finite values of the quark mass. The scaling properties of the data, assumed in the parameterization, are studied. The difference between the parameterized smooth representation determined by a best fit and the results derived from direct smoothing of the numerical data are discussed. The fluctuations in the density of states are also discussed. We provide a smooth representation of the density of states of a spherical cavity for non-relativistic particles.

# Numerical study of the density of states for the bag model

## Key claim
The mass dependence of the surface term in the density of states for a spherical MIT bag agrees with multi-reflection theory to within 1-2% for most values of m/k, but the curvature term extracted from numerical data is a fluctuating function of m/k that contains a global maximum, contradicting the monotonic ansatz proposed by Madsen (1993, 1994). The volume term is confirmed to be independent of m/k to a very good approximation.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the MIT bag boundary condition for Dirac quarks in a spherical cavity to obtain discrete eigenmomenta q_{nκ} for each combination of radial quantum number n and spin-orbit quantum number κ. They counted the total number of quark states N(k) up to a cutoff momentum k for various bag radii R and quark masses m, then fitted the data to a smooth ansatz N̅(k) = A(m/k)(kR)³ + B(m/k)(kR)² + C(m/k)(kR) using the method of minimum squares, extracting the coefficients A, B, and C as functions of m/k. The consistency of the procedure was checked by repeating calculations with different cutoff momenta (25, 50, 100, 200, 400, and 800 MeV) while keeping m/k and kR fixed.

## Key numbers
- Volume term coefficient A = 7.074 × 10⁻² g, differing from the multi-reflection theory value 2g/(9π) by 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵
- Relative difference between the numerically fitted surface term B(m/k) and the multi-reflection theory surface term B_MRT(m/k) is around 1-2% for most values of m/k
- For the non-relativistic case with boundary condition u_ξ(kR)=0, the best smooth fit is N(k) = (2g/(9π))(kR)³ - 0.1289g(kR)² + 1.0144g(kR) - 60.319g
- The fluctuating residual term N_res^(0) relative to the smooth representation is approximately 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁶ of N̅(k)

## Context
This work extends the numerical evaluation of quark states in the MIT bag model by Gilson and Jaffe (1993), who studied only a fixed quark mass of 150 MeV in the non-relativistic limit m>k. The authors provide the first systematic numerical extraction of the mass dependence of the surface and curvature terms across the full range of m/k, confirming the multi-reflection theory surface term of Balian and Bloch (1970) and Berger and Jaffe (1987) while correcting the curvature term ansatz of Madsen (1993, 1994). The resulting smooth representation of the density of states enables more accurate calculations of thermodynamic quantities for strangelets and other finite quark matter systems, though the authors note that a non-spherical bag representation is needed for droplets at high angular momentum produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
