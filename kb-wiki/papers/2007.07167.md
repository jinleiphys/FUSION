# Multi-particle quantum-statistical correlation functions in a Hubble-expanding hadron gas

> Quantum-statistical correlation measurements in high-energy physics represent an important tool to obtain information about the space-time structure of the particle-emitting source. There are several final state effects which may modify the measured femtoscopic correlation functions. One of these may be the interaction of the investigated particles with the expanding hadron gas, consisting of the other final state particles. This may cause the trajectories - and hence the phases - of the quantum-correlated pairs to be modified compared to free streaming. The resulting effect and could be interpreted as an Aharonov-Bohm-like phenomenon, in the sense that the possible paths of a quantum-correlated pair represent a closed loop, with an internally present field caused by the hadron gas. In this paper, the possible role of the effect in heavy-ion experiments is presented with analytical calculations and a simple numerical model. The modification of the strength of multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions is investigated, and the is found that in case of sufficiently large source density, this effect may play a non-negligible role.

# Multi-particle quantum-statistical correlation functions in a Hubble-expanding hadron gas

## Key claim
The interaction of quantum-correlated particle pairs with the surrounding expanding hadron gas can modify their trajectories and phases compared to free streaming, producing an Aharonov-Bohm-like effect that may non-negligibly suppress multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlation strengths, but only in cases of sufficiently large source density.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions for two-, three-, and four-particle Bose-Einstein correlation strengths in the presence of random phase shifts acquired by particles propagating through a fluctuating external field. They then construct a toy model of a Hubble-expanding hadron gas with normally distributed charged particles, simulate 8 million event-by-event configurations to compute the distribution of time-of-flight shifts for a probe particle, and convert these time shifts into phase-shift distributions to estimate the momentum-dependent suppression of correlation strengths.

## Key numbers
- For a scenario with \(N_c = 1000\) charged particles and source radius \(R = 1.5\) fm, the two-particle correlation strength \(\lambda_2\) shows a decrease from unity at zero momentum, with the strongest suppression occurring for \(m_t - m_\pi\) values between approximately 40 and 130 MeV/\(c\), before returning to the default value at higher momenta.
- For a more realistic scenario with \(N_c = 500\) and \(R = 5\) fm, the modification of \(\lambda_2\) and \(\lambda_3\) is much smaller, on the order of a few per mille.
- The width of the time-shift distribution \(\sigma_t\) decreases with increasing probe particle momentum, with absolute values differing by nearly one order of magnitude between the two scenarios.
- The three-particle correlation strength at zero relative momentum in the presence of random phases is given by \(\lambda_3 = 3e^{-2\sigma^2} + 2e^{-3\sigma^2}\), where \(\sigma\) is the width of the phase-shift distribution.

## Context
This work extends the core-halo model of Csorgo (1994) and the treatment of partially coherent pion production by Csorgo (1999) by identifying a new mechanism that can suppress correlation strengths: random phase shifts from the hadron gas environment. The effect is analogous to the Aharonov-Bohm effect (Aharonov, 1959) and could complicate the interpretation of \(\lambda_2\) and \(\lambda_3\) measurements used to study chiral symmetry restoration via the \(\eta'\) meson (Kapusta, 1995; Vance, 1998; Csorgo, 2009; Adare, 2017). The authors suggest that this phenomenon should be accounted for when drawing conclusions about coherence or core fractions from femtoscopic data, and their toy model provides a framework for future quantitative estimates.
