# Images of the Origin of Mass

> Recent years have brought considerable progress with studies of the bound-state problem in continuum QCD. A small part of that made with Dyson Schwinger equations is highlighted herein. Topics covered include: opportunities provided by precision experimental studies of the (far) valence region; and capitalising upon new data on hadron elastic and transition form factors.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is responsible for approximately 98% of the proton's mass, and its effects can be exposed in observables such as parton distribution functions, distribution amplitudes, and hadron form factors, with the pion's valence-quark distribution now seen as a success for the unification of nonperturbative and perturbative QCD studies.

## Method

The paper reviews computations using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in continuum QCD, employing two different truncation schemes: rainbow-ladder (RL), the leading-order in a systematic symmetry-preserving scheme, and a DCSB-improved (DB) kernel that incorporates nonperturbative effects omitted in RL truncation. The pion's leading-twist two-particle distribution amplitude is computed via a projection of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter wave function onto the light-front, and the pion electromagnetic form factor is computed using a refinement of Nakanishi methods to reach arbitrarily large momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

- DCSB is responsible for approximately 98% of the proton's mass.
- At a renormalisation scale ζ=2 GeV, dressed valence-quarks carry 45% of the pion's light-front momentum: 2⟨x⟩^π_{ζ2} = 0.45, with glue carrying 44% and sea-quarks 11%.
- Even at LHC energies, 25% of the pion's momentum is carried by dressed-valence quarks, 21% by sea-quarks, and 54% by glue.
- The asymptotic form φ^asy_π(x) remains a poor approximation to the true pion PDA even at ζ=200 GeV.
- The pion electromagnetic form factor prediction from DSEs shows that on Q^2 ≳ 8 GeV^2 one should see quark-counting rules and QCD scaling violations.
- The charge radius of a given diquark correlation is typically 10% larger than its mesonic analogue.
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p may exhibit a zero whose location is a direct measure of the quark-quark interaction; the prediction is made that G_E^n(Q^2) > G_E^p(Q^2) on Q^2 > 4 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based computations of Maris and Tandy (2000) for the pion form factor, which were limited to Q^2 ≤ 4 GeV^2, to arbitrarily large momentum transfers. It also builds upon the lattice-QCD results of Cloet et al. (2013) and Segovia et al. (2013) for the pion PDA. The paper enables future experimental tests at JLab12, including measurements of the pion's electromagnetic form factor and the proton's electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio, as well as planned Drell-Yan studies at Fermilab and a possible Electron-Ion Collider. The connection between elastic form factors and valence-quark PDFs at x≃1, developed by Wilson et al. (2011) and Roberts et al. (2013), provides a framework for discriminating between contemporary pictures of nucleon structure through measurements of nucleon longitudinal spin asymmetries on x ≳ 0.8.
