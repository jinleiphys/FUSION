# QCD sum rules for the pseudoscalar decay constants - To constrain the strange quark mass

> We study the higher order corrections of quark masses to the Gell-Mann$-$Oakes$-$Renner (GOR) relation by constructing QCD sum rules exclusively for pseudoscalar mesons from the axial-vector correlation function, $i \int d^4x~ e^{ip\cdot x} < 0| T[A_μ(x) A_ν(0)] | 0>$. To project out the pseudoscalar meson contributions, we apply $-p^μp^ν/p^2$ to this correlation function and construct sum rules for the decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons, $f_π, f_k$ and $f_{η_8}$. The OPE is proportional to quark masses due to PCAC. To leading order in quark mass, each sum rule reproduces the corresponding GOR relation. For kaon and $η_8$, the deviation from the GOR relation due to higher orders in quark mass is found to be substantial. But the deviation gives better agreements with the phenomenology. Our sum rule provides a sensitive relation between $f_K$ and $m_s$, which stringently constrain the value for $m_s$. To reproduce the experimental value for $f_K$, $m_s$ is found to be 186 MeV at 1 GeV scale. The $f_{η_8}$ sum rule also supports this finding.

# QCD sum rules for the pseudoscalar decay constants - To constrain the strange quark mass

## Key claim

The higher-order quark mass corrections to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relation for kaon and eta_8 are substantial (20-30% level), and by using the experimental kaon decay constant as input, the strange quark mass is constrained to 186 MeV at the 1 GeV scale, which then consistently reproduces the eta_8 decay constant from phenomenology.

## Method

The author constructs QCD sum rules from the axial-vector correlation function projected with -p^mu p^nu/p^2, which isolates pseudoscalar meson contributions and removes axial-vector meson contamination. The operator product expansion (OPE) is calculated up to dimension 6, including perturbative terms and quark condensate contributions, while gluon condensate and mixed condensate contributions vanish due to the projection. Sum rules are derived for pion, kaon, and eta_8 decay constants, and the strange quark mass is extracted by requiring the kaon sum rule to reproduce the experimental f_K.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass: m_s = 186.5 ± 8.5 MeV at 1 GeV scale, extracted from the kaon sum rule using f_K^expt ~ 160 MeV, with uncertainty from the strange quark condensate ratio ⟨s̄ s⟩ = (0.8 ± 0.1)⟨q̄ q⟩.
- Pion decay constant from the sum rule deviates from the GOR relation by about 3%, confirming the small correction in the light quark sector.
- Kaon GOR relation with conventional parameters (m_s = 150 MeV, ⟨s̄ s⟩ = 0.8⟨q̄ q⟩) yields f_K = 116 MeV, much smaller than the experimental value of ~160 MeV.
- Eta_8 decay constant from the sum rule using m_s = 186 MeV gives f_eta8 = 160 MeV at M^2 = 1 GeV^2, consistent with the literature range 157-165 MeV.
- The higher-order corrections shift the kaon decay constant by 20-25% upward from the kaon GOR relation value.
- The eta_8 sum rule shows a 30% correction upward from the eta_8 GOR relation.

## Context

This work extends the GOR relation framework by incorporating higher-order quark mass corrections through QCD sum rules, addressing the known failure of the kaon GOR relation to reproduce f_K with conventional strange quark masses. Prior work by Ioffe (1981) and Reinders, Rubinstein, and Yazaki (1983) established standard QCD sum rule parameters, while Choe and Lee (1998) calculated f_K via sum rules but did not systematically study higher-order corrections. The extracted m_s = 186 MeV is larger than the conventional 150 MeV value and the hadronic tau decay result of 159 MeV from Kambor and Maltman (2000), but consistent with the current algebra ratio m_s = 175 ± 25 MeV from Leutwyler (1996). The result enables a more reliable determination of the strange quark mass from pseudoscalar meson properties and can be tested in future calculations including alpha_s corrections to the perturbative part.
