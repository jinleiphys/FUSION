# Kaon Energies in Dense Matter

> We discuss the role of kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon correlations in kaon condensation in dense matter. Correlations raise the threshold density for kaon condensation, possibly to densities higher than those encountered in stable neutron stars.

# Kaon Energies in Dense Matter

## Key claim

Correlations between kaons and nucleons, and between nucleons themselves, raise the threshold density for kaon condensation in dense matter, possibly to densities higher than those encountered in stable neutron stars. The Lenz approximation overestimates the attraction felt by a K- in neutron star matter at core densities by roughly a factor of 2, because in-medium correlations differ substantially from those in a dilute gas.

## Method

The authors expand the kaon energy expression from the chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian of Brown, Lee, Rho, and Thorsson (BLRT) in powers of nucleon density, identifying the first-order term as the Lenz potential proportional to K-N scattering lengths. They then construct simple square-well and Yukawa potentials fitted to the K-n and K-p scattering lengths, compute the K-n relative wave function at zero energy, and estimate the Hartree potential (which neglects correlations) as an upper bound on the attraction. Second-order contributions from neutron density fluctuations are estimated using the static density-density response function from the many-body calculations of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981).

## Key numbers

- The threshold density for kaon condensation in the BLRT mean-field treatment was found to be approximately 3-4 times nuclear matter density, where nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3.
- The central density of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is about 4 rho_0 according to Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988).
- The K-n scattering length used is a_{K-n} = -0.41 fm, and the K-p scattering length is a_{K-p} = -0.85 fm, from the BLRT parameter set.
- For a square-well potential of radius 0.7 fm fitted to the scattering lengths, the well depths are 126.2 MeV for neutrons and 181 MeV for protons; for radius 1.0 fm, depths are 49.5 MeV and 75.6 MeV respectively.
- For a Yukawa potential with rho meson mass 770 MeV, the coupling strength V_0 is -795 MeV for neutrons and -1075 MeV for protons.
- At density 4 rho_0, second-order estimates of the kaon energy shift are 7 MeV (square well, R=1 fm), 16 MeV (square well, R=0.7 fm), and 46 MeV (Yukawa potential).
- The factor 1 - a_{K-N}/R exceeds 1.41 for neutrons and 1.85 for protons with the given scattering lengths and R < 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of kaon condensation by Brown, Lee, Rho, and Thorsson (1994) by incorporating the effects of kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon correlations that were neglected in that earlier approach. The authors argue that the Lenz potential, which is exact only at low densities, overestimates the attraction at neutron star core densities, and that a Hartree treatment gives a more realistic estimate. The results suggest that kaon condensation may not occur in stable neutron stars, contrary to earlier conclusions, but the authors note that uncertainties in the kaon-nucleon interaction, density dependence of the pion decay constant, and the electron chemical potential at high densities prevent a definitive ruling out. The work motivates more complete many-body calculations, such as the lowest order constrained variational method, to be reported separately.
