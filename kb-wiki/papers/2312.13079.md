# Hadron Production in Ultra-relativistic Nuclear Collisions and Finite Baryon-Size Effects

> We investigate relative hadron yield production of various like and unlike mass particles in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions by employing a statistical thermal model with finite-sized baryons (antibaryons) to imitate the hard-core repulsive interactions leading to the excluded volume type effect. A strong evidence of strangeness suppression relative to the non-strange ones, mainly pions, particularly at higher energies is also observed. This study also indicates that at chemical freeze-out the particle ratios and strangeness suppression in the system obtained theoretically are sensitive to baryonic (antibaryonic) hard-core radius ($r_B$). A comparison with earlier analysis involving the strangeness suppression effect is made where baryons and antibaryons were treated as point-like particles. The available experimental data showing energy dependence of various particle ratios are well described throughout the range of centre-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$). The value of hard-core radius between 0.76 to 0.79 fm is found to fit the data quite well using $χ^{2}$ minimization technique. Two different chemical freeze-out stages are found where the earlier one belongs to baryonic (hyperonic), antibaryonic (antihyperonic) states and the later one to mesonic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating finite hard-core radii for baryons and antibaryons (between 0.76 and 0.79 fm) into a statistical thermal model with excluded volume effects provides a good description of experimental particle ratios across a wide range of collision energies, and that this finite-size treatment reverses the previously reported trend of strangeness suppression factor increasing with energy.

## Method

The authors employed a grand-canonical statistical thermal model (hadron resonance gas) where baryons and antibaryons are assigned a finite hard-core radius to mimic excluded volume repulsion, while mesons remain point-like. They fitted the antiproton-to-proton ratio as a function of center-of-mass energy to determine the baryon chemical potential and temperature parametrization, then applied the same baryon radius to all baryonic species. Strangeness suppression was incorporated via a factor gamma_s applied per strange quark, and the model was compared to experimental data for multiple particle ratios from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit baryon hard-core radius for antiproton/proton ratio: 0.78 fm
- Best-fit baryon hard-core radius for antilambda/lambda ratio: 0.76 fm
- Best-fit baryon hard-core radius for anticascade/cascade ratio: 0.79 fm
- Best-fit baryon hard-core radius for antiomega/omega ratio: 0.78 fm
- Baryon chemical potential decreases from approximately 600 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 5 GeV to approximately 20 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 200 GeV
- Baryon freeze-out temperature saturates at approximately 163 MeV for sqrt(s_NN) beyond 200 GeV
- Meson freeze-out temperature parameter c = 153 MeV (versus 163 MeV for baryons)
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s approaches an asymptotic value of 0.75 at high energies
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom values: 0.95 (pbar/p), 1.34 (Lambdabar/Lambda), 1.89 (Xibar/Xi), 0.38 (Omegabar/Omega), 0.11 (K-/pi-), 1.20 (K+/pi+), 0.85 (Xi+/pi-)

## Context

This work extends prior statistical thermal model analyses by Cleymans and Redlich (1998) and others that treated hadrons as point-like particles, showing that including finite baryon size reverses the previously reported increasing trend of the strangeness suppression factor with collision energy. The double chemical freeze-out scenario (baryons at higher temperature, mesons at lower temperature) is consistent with earlier suggestions by Choi and Lee (2003) and supports the idea of multiple freeze-out stages. The results enable more refined thermal model analyses of heavy-ion collision data across the full energy range from AGS to LHC.
