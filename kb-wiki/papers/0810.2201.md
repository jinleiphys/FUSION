# Lightest Scalar Resonances and the Dynamics of the γγ\toππReactions

> The high-statistics Belle data on the γγ\toπ^+π^- and γγ\toπ^0π^0 reactions have been jointly analyzed. The main dynamical mechanisms of these reactions for energies below 1.5 GeV have been revealed. It has been shown that the direct coupling constants of the σ(600) and f_0(980) resonances with a γγpair are small and that the σ(600)\toγγand f_0(980)\toγγdecays are four-quark transitions due primarily to π^+π^- and K^+K^- loop mechanisms, respectively. The role of the chiral shielding of the σ(600) resonance is emphasized. The widths of the f_0(980)\toγγand σ(600)\toγγdecays averaged over the resonance mass distributions, as well as the width of the f_2(1270)\toγγdecay, are estimated as (Γ_{f_0\toγγ})_{ππ}\approx 0.19 keV, (Γ_{σ\toγγ})_{ππ}\approx 0.45 keV, and Γ_{f_2\toγγ}(m^2_{f_2})\approx 3.8$ keV.

## Key claim

The direct coupling constants of the σ(600) and f₀(980) resonances to a γγ pair are small, implying that their two-photon decays are dominated by four-quark transitions via π⁺π⁻ and K⁺K⁻ loop mechanisms, respectively, rather than by direct quark-antiquark annihilation.

## Method

The authors performed a joint analysis of high-statistics Belle data on the γγ → π⁺π⁻ and γγ → π⁰π⁰ reactions for center-of-mass energies below 1.5 GeV. They constructed a model for the helicity and partial amplitudes that includes Born contributions from point-like charged pion and kaon exchanges, modified in the S and D₂ waves by strong final-state interactions, plus direct resonance-photon couplings. The S-wave amplitudes incorporate mixed σ(600) and f₀(980) resonances with a chiral-shielding background, and the D₂-wave amplitudes include the f₂(1270) resonance with its ππ and γγ couplings.

## Key numbers

- ⟨Γ_{f₀→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.19 keV, the width of the f₀(980) → γγ decay averaged over the resonance mass distribution in the ππ channel.
- ⟨Γ_{σ→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.45 keV, the width of the σ(600) → γγ decay averaged over the resonance mass distribution in the ππ channel.
- Γ_{f₂→γγ}(m²_{f₂}) ≈ 3.8 keV, the width of the f₂(1270) → γγ decay at the resonance mass.
- Direct coupling constants yield Γ^{(0)}_{σ→γγ}(m²_σ) = 0.012 keV and Γ^{(0)}_{f₀→γγ}(m²_{f₀}) = 0.008 keV in the π⁰π⁰-only fit, confirming their smallness.
- In the joint fit including form factors, the direct coupling g^{(0)}_{σγγ} = -0.049 GeV⁻¹ (negligible width) and g^{(0)}_{f₀γγ} = 0.718 GeV⁻¹ (Γ^{(0)}_{f₀→γγ} ≈ 0.01 keV).
- f₂(1270) parameters from the joint fit: mass m_{f₂} = 1.272 GeV, total width Γ^{tot}_{f₂}(m²_{f₂}) = 0.196 GeV, Γ_{f₂→γγ}(m_{f₂}) = 3.84 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical investigations of scalar mesons in γγ collisions (Achasov and Shestakov, 1990s–2000s) and confirms the four-quark model prediction (Achasov and Devyanin, 1982) that the f₀(980) and a₀(980) resonances are suppressed in γγ production. The joint analysis of the high-statistics Belle data (2007–2008) resolves a long-standing tension between the π⁺π⁻ and π⁰π⁰ channels by introducing form factors that modify the Born amplitudes, while preserving the conclusion that the scalar resonances couple to photons primarily through charged meson loops rather than direct quark-antiquark annihilation. The results enable refined tests of the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons and provide input for chiral perturbation theory and nonperturbative QCD studies.
