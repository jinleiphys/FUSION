# Nuclear fusion catalyzed by doubly charged scalars: Implications for energy production

> A number of popular extensions of the Standard Model of particle physics predict the existence of doubly charged scalar particles $X^{\pm\pm}$. Such particles may be long-lived or even stable. If exist, $X^{--}$ could form atomic bound states with light nuclei and catalyze their fusion by essentially eliminating the Coulomb barrier between them. Such an $X$-catalyzed fusion ($X$CF) process does not require high temperatures or pressure and may have important applications for energy production. A similar process of muon-catalyzed fusion ($μ$CF) has been shown not to be a viable source of energy because of the sticking of negative muons to helium nuclei produced in the fusion of hydrogen isotopes, which stops the catalytic process. We analyze $X$CF in deuterium environments and show that the $X$-particles can only stick to $^6$Li nuclei, which are produced in the third-stage reactions downstream in the catalytic cycle. The corresponding sticking probability is very low, and, before getting bound to $^6$Li, each $X$-particle can catalyze $\sim 3.5\cdot 10^{9}$ fusion cycles, producing $\sim 7\cdot 10^{4}$ TeV of energy. We also discuss the ways of reactivating the $X$-particles from the Coulomb-bound (${\rm ^6Li}X$) states, which would allow re-using them in $X$CF reactions.

## Key claim

Doubly charged scalar particles \(X^{--}\) can catalyze nuclear fusion in deuterium environments with a catalytic poisoning probability so low that each particle could produce \(\sim 7\times 10^{4}\) TeV of energy before being bound to a \(^6\)Li nucleus, but this viability depends on the \(X\)-particles being sufficiently long-lived (lifetime \(\tau_X \gtrsim 10^2\) s) and on the possibility of reactivating those that do become bound.

## Method

The author analyzes the kinetics of \(X\)-catalyzed fusion (\(X\)CF) in deuterium by calculating the branching ratios of fusion reactions at three successive stages: (I) \(dd\) fusion producing \(^3\)He, \(^3\)H, or \(^4\)He; (II) fusion of \(^3\)He or \(^3\)H with a deuteron to produce \(^4\)He; and (III) fusion of \(^4\)He with a deuteron to produce \(^6\)Li. The sticking probability of \(X\) to \(^6\)Li is computed from these branching ratios, and the total number of fusion cycles and energy output per \(X\)-particle are derived from the net reaction \(4d \to {^6\text{Li}} + p + n + 23.1\) MeV. The author also estimates formation times of \(X\)-atomic systems and discusses reactivation of \(X\) from \((^6\text{Li}X)\) ions via beam stripping.

## Key numbers

* Each \(X\)-particle can catalyze \(\sim 3.5 \times 10^{9}\) fusion cycles before sticking to a \(^6\)Li nucleus.
* Each \(X\)-particle produces \(\sim 7 \times 10^{4}\) TeV of energy before catalytic poisoning.
* The fraction of \(X\)-particles that end up bound to \(^6\)Li is \(\sim 2.8 \times 10^{-10}\).
* The net energy release of the full catalytic cycle is \(23.1\) MeV per four deuterons converted to \(^6\)Li, a proton, and a neutron.
* The Coulomb binding energy of \(X\) to \(^6\)Li is about \(2\) MeV.
* The moderation time of \(X\)-particles at liquid hydrogen density (\(4.25 \times 10^{22}\) nuclei/cm\(^3\), \(T \simeq 20\) K) is \(\tau \simeq 6 \times 10^{-11}\) s; in deuterium gas at \(0^\circ\)C and 1 bar it is \(\sim 4.8 \times 10^{-8}\) s.
* The cross section for producing an \(X\)-particle with mass \(m_X \simeq 1\) TeV/\(c^2\) and \(\beta = 0.3\) is \(\sim 1\) fb.
* To strip \(X\) from \((^6\text{Li}X)\) ions by scattering on target nuclei with average atomic number \(A \simeq 40\), the required beam energy is \(\sim 0.05\) GeV, and the stripping cross section is \(\gtrsim 0.1\) b.

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon-catalyzed fusion (\(\mu\)CF), which was shown not to be viable for energy production because muons stick to helium nuclei with probability \(\omega_s \simeq 10^{-2}\), yielding only \(\sim 1.7\) GeV per muon (Jackson 1957). Prior analyses of catalysis by singly charged or fractionally charged particles (Zeldovich 1957, Ioffe 1979, Hamaguchi 2006) also found fatal catalytic poisoning. The author demonstrates that doubly charged scalars avoid this problem because they stick only to \(^6\)Li, with a much lower probability, and that reactivation of bound \(X\)-particles via beam stripping is energetically favorable. The paper motivates continued experimental searches for doubly charged scalars and suggests cosmological implications for primordial nucleosynthesis and dark matter.
