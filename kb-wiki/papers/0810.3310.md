# Lowest order constrained variational calculation of polarized neutron matter at finite temperature

> Some properties of the polarized neutron matter at finite temperature has been studied using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the $AV_{18}$ potential. Our results indicate that spontaneous transition to the ferromagnetic phase does not occur. Effective mass, free energy, magnetic susceptibility, entropy and the equation of state of the polarized neutron matter at finite temperature are also calculated. A comparison is also made between our results and those of other many-body techniques.

## Key claim

Using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 potential, the authors find no indication of a spontaneous transition to a ferromagnetic phase in polarized neutron matter at finite temperature, in agreement with some prior many-body calculations but contradicting others. The claim is conditional on the specific method and potential used, and the authors note that the free energy curves for different polarizations never cross, which would be required for such a transition.

## Method

The authors apply the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to a system of interacting neutrons described by a Jastrow-type trial wave function, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential. They perform a cluster expansion of the energy functional truncated at the two-body term, minimize the two-body energy subject to a normalization constraint, and treat the effective masses of spin-up and spin-down neutrons as variational parameters that minimize the free energy. The single-particle energies use a quadratic approximation with momentum-independent effective masses, and the chemical potentials are determined numerically from the Fermi-Dirac distribution at each temperature, density, and spin polarization.

## Key numbers

- At density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and T = 10 MeV, the ratio of effective masses for fully polarized to unpolarized neutron matter is m₁*(δ=1.0)/m₁,₂*(δ=0.0) = 1.22
- At density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and T = 20 MeV, the ratio of effective masses for fully polarized to unpolarized neutron matter is m₁*(δ=1.0)/m₁,₂*(δ=0.0) = 1.18
- The condition for preventing anomalous entropy behavior, m*(ρ,δ=1.0)/m*(ρ,δ=0.0) < 2^(2/3), is satisfied at all densities explored
- The free energy per particle increases with both density and polarization, with the minimum occurring for the unpolarized case (δ = 0.0) at all densities and temperatures considered (T = 10 and T = 20 MeV)
- The ratio χ_F/χ (magnetic susceptibility relative to free Fermi gas) increases monotonically with density at T = 10 and T = 20 MeV, showing no magnetic instability

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature LOCV calculations of polarized neutron matter (Bordbar and Bigdeli, 2007) to finite temperature, and compares results with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of Bombaci et al. (2006) using the same AV18 potential. The results support the conclusion that no ferromagnetic transition occurs, consistent with Bombaci et al. (2006) and recent Monte Carlo calculations (Fantoni et al., 2004), but contradicting earlier work using Skyrme-like interactions (Rios and Polls, 2005) and the D1P Gogny force (Lopez-Val et al., 2006) which found ferromagnetic transitions at certain densities. The calculations are relevant for understanding magnetic field generation in protoneutron stars, where interior temperatures are of order 20-50 MeV.
