# Calculation of the correlation, relativistic and QED corrections to the total electron binding energy in atoms and their nuclear charge dependence

> We present relativistic many-body calculations of total electron binding energy of neutral atoms up to element $Z=120$. Binding energy for ions may be found by subtracting known ionization potentials. Accuracy of the results for $17<Z \le105$ significantly exceeds that in NIST tables (there are no data for $Z>105$ there). We fit numerical results for binding energies by analytical function of $Z$. We also calculate numerical values and determine dependence on $Z$ of the correlation corrections, Dirac and Breit relativistic corrections and quantum electrodynamics (QED) corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents relativistic many-body calculations of total electron binding energies for neutral atoms up to Z=120, claiming accuracy that significantly exceeds NIST tables for 17 < Z ≤ 103, but the actual hedge is that for Z < 18 the NIST data (based on measurements) remain more accurate and are used only for testing, while for Z > 103 no NIST data exist for comparison.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) method including Breit and QED potentials self-consistently to generate basis wave functions and obtain the mean-field total electron binding energy. Correlation corrections are calculated using the linearized single-double coupled cluster (SD) method for closed-shell atoms up to Z=56, and the second-order perturbation theory for heavier atoms (Z > 56), with an interpolation formula for open-shell atoms. The Dirac, Breit, and QED corrections are computed numerically and fitted to analytical functions of nuclear charge Z.

## Key numbers

- For He (Z=2), the calculated total electron binding energy is 2.90404 a.u., compared to the NIST value of 2.90339 a.u., a difference of 0.02%
- For Ne (Z=10), the calculated total is 129.053 a.u., compared to NIST 129.0525(7) a.u., within experimental error of 0.0005%
- For Ba (Z=56), the SD correlation correction is 2.9926 a.u., while the second-order correction is 3.108 a.u., a relative difference of 0.0014%
- The Breit correction is fitted as δE_Breit = -8.822 Z^1.08885 × 10^(-6) E^(1), with fitting accuracy ~1% for Z from 18 to 120
- The QED correction is fitted as δE_QED = -7.687 Z^1.08887 × 10^(-6) E^(1), with fitting accuracy ~1% for Z from 18 to 120
- The Dirac relativistic correction reaches ~17% of the total binding energy for superheavy elements with Z > 100
- For No (Z=102), the calculated total binding energy is 36658.3 a.u., compared to NIST 36600(300) a.u., reducing the error by up to 37 times

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of total electron binding energies using the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Slater method (HACCM.76) and multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock approach (Santos), which did not include correlation corrections, and provides systematic correlation corrections for all neutral atoms up to Z=120. The results enable more precise extraction of bare nuclear masses from atomic mass measurements, which is critical for testing nuclear models, determining nuclear stability boundaries, and supporting neutrino mass measurements and searches for new physics via isotope shifts.
