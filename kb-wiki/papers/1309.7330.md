# Relativistic Hard-Scattering and Tsallis Fits to pT Spectra in pp Collisions at the LHC

> Motivated by the good Tsallis fits to the high-pT spectra in pp collisions at the LHC, we study the relativistic hard-scattering model and obtain an approximate analytical expression for the differential hard-scattering cross section at η~ 0. The power-law behaviour of the transverse spectra, in the form of dσ/dpT^2 \propto 1/pT^n, gives a power index n in the range of 4.5-5.5 for jet production as predicted by pQCD, after the dependencies of the structure functions and the running coupling constant are properly taken into account. The power indices for hadron production n are slightly greater than those for jet production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the success of Tsallis fits to high-\(p_T\) spectra in \(pp\) collisions arises from the underlying power-law behavior of the parton-parton scattering cross section (\(\alpha_s^2/p_T^4\)), but the power index \(n\) extracted from data is not the naive pQCD value of 4; it is modified by structure functions, running coupling, and fragmentation, yielding \(n \sim 4.5\)–5.5 for jets and \(n \sim 6\) for hadrons.

## Method

The authors derive an approximate analytical expression for the differential hard-scattering cross section at pseudorapidity \(\eta \sim 0\) using the saddle point integration method, assuming parton distribution functions of the form \(xG(x) \propto (1-x)^g\). They then fit this expression to experimental jet and hadron transverse momentum spectra from D0, ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS collaborations, extracting the power index \(n\) for each dataset. For hadron production, they introduce a regularization of the power law using either a linear or quadratic dependence on transverse mass \(m_T\) and account for jet fragmentation by relating the parent jet transverse momentum to the observed hadron \(p_T\) via \(\bar{c}_T = 2.3 \, p_T\).

## Key numbers

* Power index for jet production in \(\bar{p}p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=1.80\) TeV (D0, \(R=0.7\), \(|\eta|<0.7\)): \(n = 4.39\).
* Power index for jet production in \(\bar{p}p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.63\) TeV (D0, \(R=0.7\), \(|\eta|<0.7\)): \(n = 4.47\).
* Power index for jet production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV (ALICE, \(R=0.2\), \(|\eta|<0.5\)): \(n = 4.78\).
* Power index for jet production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV (ALICE, \(R=0.4\), \(|\eta|<0.5\)): \(n = 4.98\).
* Power index for jet production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV (CMS, \(R=0.5\), \(|\eta|<0.5\)): \(n = 5.39\).
* Power index for hadron production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV (linear \(m_T\) regularization): \(n = 5.69\), with \(m_{T0} = 0.804\) GeV.
* Power index for hadron production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.9\) TeV (linear \(m_T\) regularization): \(n = 5.86\), with \(m_{T0} = 0.634\) GeV.
* Power index for hadron production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV (quadratic \(m_T^2\) regularization): \(n = 5.45\), with \(m_{T0} = 1.09\) GeV.
* Power index for hadron production in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=0.9\) TeV (quadratic \(m_T^2\) regularization): \(n = 5.49\), with \(m_{T0} = 0.837\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic hard-scattering model of Blankenbecler, Brodsky, and Gunion (1974) and earlier work by Wong (1994, 1998, 2013) to provide an analytical approximation for the differential cross section at midrapidity. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the naive pQCD prediction of \(n \sim 4\) and the larger index \(n \sim 6.6\) found in Tsallis fits to hadron spectra by Wong (2012). The results enable a more fundamental QCD-based interpretation of the parameters in Tsallis distributions, showing that the power index is not a universal constant but is increased by fragmentation and showering processes. The paper also notes that multiple parton scattering (studied by Kwiecinski, 1987; Calucci and Treleani, 1990; Gyulassy, 2001) does not significantly alter the power index at high \(p_T\) in minimum-bias events.
