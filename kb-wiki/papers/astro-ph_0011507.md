# Presupernova Evolution with Improved Rates for Weak Interactions

> Recent shell-model calculations of weak-interaction rates for nuclei in the mass range A = 45 - 65 have resulted in substantial revisions to the hitherto standard set of Fuller, Fowler, & Newman (FFN). In particular, key electron-capture rates, such as that for Co60 are much smaller. We consider here the effects of these revised rates on the presupernova (post-oxygen burning) evolution of massive stars in the mass range 11 to 40 M_sun. Moreover, we include, for the first time in models by our group, the effects of modern rates for beta-decay in addition to electron capture and positron emission. Values for the central electron mole number at the time of iron core collapse in the new models are typically larger, by delta Y_e = 0.005 to 0.015, than those of Woosley & Weaver 1995, with a tendency for the more massive models to display larger differences. About half of this change is a consequence of including beta-decay; the other half, result of the smaller rates for electron capture. Unlike what might be expected solely on basis of the larger Y_e's, the new iron core masses are systematically smaller owing to a decrease in the entropy in the outer iron core. The changes in iron core mass range from zero to 0.1 M_sun. We also observe, as predicted by Aufderheide et al. (1994), a tendency towards beta-equilibrium just prior to the collapse of the core, and the subsequent loss of that equilibrium as core collapse proceeds. We discuss the key weak reaction rates, both beta-decay and electron-capture, responsible for the evolution of Y_e and make suggestions for future measurements.

## Key claim

The new shell-model weak-interaction rates (LMP) produce central electron mole numbers (Ye) at iron core collapse that are larger by ΔYe = 0.005 to 0.015 compared to the Woosley & Weaver (1995) models, but contrary to naive expectation, the iron core masses are systematically smaller (by up to 0.1 Msun) due to decreased entropy in the outer iron core.

## Method

The authors used a modified version of the KEPLER implicit hydrodynamics code to evolve 34 stellar models in the mass range 11 to 40 Msun from oxygen depletion through silicon burning and core collapse. They compared three rate sets: the Woosley & Weaver (1995) rates (which effectively neglected beta-decay), a full implementation of the Fuller, Fowler, & Newman (FFN) rates including beta-decay, and the new LMP rates (shell-model rates from Langanke & Martinez-Pinedo for A=45-65, Oda et al. for A<40, and FFN for A=40-44). The presupernova model was defined as the time when collapse velocity near the iron core edge first reached 1000 km/s.

## Key numbers

- Central Ye at collapse for 15 Msun: 0.432 (LMP rates), 0.430 (FFN rates), 0.423 (WW model)
- Central Ye at collapse for 25 Msun: 0.445 (LMP rates), 0.438 (FFN rates), 0.430 (WW model)
- Central Ye at collapse for 40 Msun: 0.447 (LMP rates), 0.439 (FFN rates), 0.430 (WW model)
- Change in central Ye relative to WW models: ΔYe = 0.005 to 0.015, with larger differences for more massive models
- Change in iron core mass: zero to 0.1 Msun (larger changes for higher mass stars)
- Central entropies at presupernova time using WW models: 0.73 (15 Msun), 0.97 (25 Msun), and 1.10 (40 Msun) in units of kB per baryon

## Context

This work extends the presupernova evolution models of Woosley & Weaver (1995) by incorporating the shell-model weak-interaction rates of Langanke & Martinez-Pinedo (2000) for pf-shell nuclei, which substantially revise the earlier FFN rates (Fuller, Fowler, & Newman, 1980-1985). The new rates confirm the prediction of Aufderheide et al. (1994) that beta-equilibrium is approached just prior to core collapse. The results enable more accurate predictions of iron core structure and composition for supernova explosion modeling, and the authors identify key weak reaction rates (particularly electron capture on specific iron and nickel isotopes) that warrant future laboratory measurements.
