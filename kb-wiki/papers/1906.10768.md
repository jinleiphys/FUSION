# DAB-MOD sensitivity study of heavy flavor $R_{AA}$ and azimuthal anisotropies based on beam energy, initial conditions, hadronization, and suppression mechanisms

> Heavy flavor probes provide important information about the in-medium properties of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we investigate the effects of 2D+1 event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds on the nuclear suppression factor and momentum anisotropies of heavy flavor mesons and non-photonic electrons. Using the state-of-the-art D and B mesons modular simulation code (called "DAB-MOD"), we perform a systematic comparison of different transport equations in the same background, including a few energy loss models --- with and without energy loss fluctuations --- and a relativistic Langevin model with different drag parametrizations. We present the resulting D and B mesons $R_{AA}$, $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ as well as multi-particle cumulants, in AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=200\, \text{GeV}$ and PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=2.76 \, \text{TeV}$ and $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=5.02 \, \text{TeV}$, and compare them to the available experimental data. The $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ ratio, which is known to be a powerful probe of the initial conditions and flow fluctuations in the soft sector, is also studied in the context of heavy flavor. We also investigate the correlations between the transverse anisotropies of heavy mesons and all charged particles to better understand how heavy quarks couple to the hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma. We study the influence that different initial conditions and the implementation of heavy-light quark coalescence has on our results.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates how different modeling choices, beam energy, initial conditions, hadronization via coalescence, and energy loss mechanisms, affect heavy flavor observables, but it does not aim to constrain any single model; rather, it quantifies the sensitivity of the nuclear suppression factor and azimuthal anisotropies to these choices, noting that the results depend on the specific combination of assumptions.

## Method

The authors used the DAB-MOD modular Monte Carlo simulation to propagate charm and bottom quarks through 2+1D event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds generated by v-USPhydro, coupled to either MCKLN or Trento initial conditions. They compared several transport models: parametric energy loss models (with and without energy loss fluctuations) and a relativistic Langevin model with two different drag parametrizations (Moore & Teaney and Gossiaux & Aichelin), and also implemented heavy-light quark coalescence. Heavy quark initial momenta were sampled from FONLL pQCD calculations, and the spatial distribution followed the initial energy density of each hydrodynamic event.

## Key numbers

*   D and B meson R_AA, v_2, v_3, and v_4 were calculated in AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.
*   The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) was set to 0.08 for MCKLN and 0.05 for Trento in AuAu 200 GeV; for PbPb 2.76 TeV, eta/s=0.11 (MCKLN) and 0.05 (Trento); for PbPb 5.02 TeV, eta/s=0.05 (MCKLN) and 0.047 (Trento).
*   The hydrodynamic evolution started at an initial time tau_0=0.6 fm.
*   Approximately 1000–2000 hydrodynamic events per 10% centrality range were used, with each event oversampled to ~10^7 heavy quarks.
*   The v_2{4}/v_2{2} ratio was computed for all three beam energies and compared to STAR and ALICE data; MCKLN captured STAR data well at RHIC, while Trento provided a nearly perfect fit to LHC run 2 data.

## Context

This work extends previous heavy flavor studies by Prado (2016) and the DUKE/SUBATECH collaboration (Cao 2015, Nahrgang 2014) by performing a systematic comparison of multiple transport models within the same hydrodynamic background, rather than focusing on a single model. It also builds on the soft-sector initial condition studies of Giacalone (2017) and Alba (2017) by applying the v_2{4}/v_2{2} ratio, a known probe of initial conditions and flow fluctuations, to the heavy flavor sector for the first time. The results enable future work to disentangle the effects of initial conditions, energy loss mechanisms, and hadronization on heavy flavor observables, and to use heavy flavor multi-particle cumulants as additional constraints on QGP properties.
