# $^{12}$C properties with evolved chiral three-nucleon interactions

> We investigate selected static and transition properties of $^{12}$C using ab initio No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) methods with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. We adopt the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) to assist convergence including up to three-nucleon (3N) contributions. We examine the dependences of the $^{12}$C observables on the SRG evolution scale and on the model-space parameters. We obtain nearly converged low-lying excitation spectra. We compare results of the full NCSM with the Importance Truncated NCSM in large model spaces for benchmarking purposes. We highlight the effects of the chiral 3N interaction on several spectroscopic observables. The agreement of some observables with experiment is improved significantly by the inclusion of 3N interactions, e.g., the B(M1) from the first $J^πT = 1^+ 1$ state to the ground state. However, in some cases the agreement deteriorates, e.g., for the excitation energy of the first $1^+ 0$ state, leaving room for improved next-generation chiral Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The inclusion of chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions significantly improves agreement with experiment for some observables in 12C, such as the B(M1) transition from the first 1+1 state to the ground state, but deteriorates agreement for others, such as the excitation energy of the first 1+0 state, indicating that next-generation chiral Hamiltonians are needed.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations for 12C using chiral two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions evolved via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) to soften the Hamiltonian and improve convergence. They systematically varied the SRG flow parameter, the harmonic-oscillator basis frequency, and the model-space truncation parameter Nmax to examine convergence, and benchmarked full NCSM results against Importance Truncated NCSM (IT-NCSM) results in large model spaces.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 12C with chiral NN+3N interactions at Nmax=(8,9): -95.761 MeV (NCSM) and -95.662(45) MeV (IT-NCSM), compared to experimental -92.161 MeV
- Excitation energy of the first 2+0 state with NN+3N: 3.962 MeV (NCSM at Nmax=(8,9)), compared to experimental 4.439 MeV
- Excitation energy of the first 1+0 state with NN+3N: 8.998 MeV (NCSM at Nmax=(8,9)), compared to experimental 12.710 MeV
- B(M1) from the 1+1 state to the ground state with NN+3N: 1.109 mu_N^2 (NCSM at Nmax=(8,9)), compared to experimental 0.951(20) mu_N^2
- B(E2) from the 2+0 state to the ground state with NN+3N: 4.210 e^2 fm^4 (NCSM at Nmax=(8,9)), compared to experimental 7.59(42) e^2 fm^4
- Point-proton rms radius of the ground state with NN+3N: 2.149 fm (NCSM at Nmax=(8,9)), compared to experimental 2.35(2) fm

## Context

This work extends earlier NCSM calculations of 12C with chiral NN+3N interactions by Navratil (2007) that used the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation and reached only Nmax=6 basis spaces, by employing SRG evolution and reaching Nmax=8 spaces with both natural and unnatural parity states. The results confirm the strong enhancement of the B(M1) from the 1+1 state by 3N interactions previously reported by Hayes (2003) using the Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99) interaction, and provide benchmarks for future calculations with next-generation chiral Hamiltonians at N3LO being developed by the LENPIC collaboration.
