# Critical fluctuations in models with van der Waals interactions

> Particle number fluctuations are considered within the van der Waals (VDW) equation, which contains both attractive (mean-field) and repulsive (eigenvolume) interactions. The VDW equation is used to calculate the scaled variance of particle number fluctuations in generic Boltzmann VDW system and in nuclear matter. The strongly intensive measures $Δ[E^*,N]$ and $Σ[E^*,N]$ of the particle number and excitation energy fluctuations are also considered, and, similarly, show singular behavior near the critical point. The $Δ[E^*,N]$ measure is shown to attain both positive and negative values in the vicinity of critical point. Based on universality argument, similar behavior is expected to occur in the vicinity of the QCD critical point.

# Critical fluctuations in models with van der Waals interactions

## Key claim
The van der Waals equation of state, extended to the grand canonical ensemble and including quantum statistics, predicts that strongly intensive measures of particle number and excitation energy fluctuations diverge at the critical point, with the Δ[E*,N] measure taking both positive and negative values in its vicinity. Based on universality arguments, similar singular behavior is expected near the QCD critical point, though the present calculations are performed for Boltzmann and Fermi statistics rather than for QCD directly.

## Method
The authors extend the classical van der Waals equation to the grand canonical ensemble by deriving the chemical potential from the free energy, and incorporate quantum statistics by replacing the ideal gas free energy with that of an ideal quantum gas. They then compute the scaled variance ω[N] of particle number fluctuations and the strongly intensive measures Δ[E*,N] and Σ[E*,N] for two systems: a generic Boltzmann van der Waals gas and nuclear matter described with Fermi statistics for nucleons, using van der Waals parameters a = 329 MeV fm³ and b = 3.42 fm³ for nucleons following VDWgen (2015).

## Key numbers
- For nuclear matter, the van der Waals parameters used are a = 329 MeV fm³ and b = 3.42 fm³ for nucleons, taken from VDWgen (2015).
- The scaled variance ω[N] is close to unity at small densities in the gaseous phase, close to zero at large densities in the liquid phase, and diverges at the critical point for both the Boltzmann van der Waals gas and nuclear matter.
- Both Δ[E*,N] and Σ[E*,N] diverge at the critical point in nuclear matter, with Δ[E*,N] attaining negative values in the vicinity of the critical point.

## Context
This work extends the study of critical fluctuations in van der Waals systems previously developed by VDWgen (2015) to include strongly intensive measures Δ[E*,N] and Σ[E*,N], which were suggested by SI (2015) as probes insensitive to volume fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. The results are relevant to the NA61/SHINE collaboration's search for the QCD critical point, and the authors note that van der Waals interactions should also play a role in the full hadron resonance gas, as recently demonstrated for the crossover region at μB = 0 in VDWHRG (2016). Further studies could clarify the role of van der Waals interactions in the confined phase of QCD.
