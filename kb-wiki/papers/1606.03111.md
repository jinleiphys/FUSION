# Dependence of two-proton radioactivity on nuclear pairing models

> Sensitivity of two-proton emitting decay to nuclear pairing correlation is discussed within a time-dependent three-body model. We focus on the $^6$Be nucleus assuming $α+ p + p$ configuration, and its decay process is described as a time-evolution of the three-body resonance state. For a proton-proton subsystem, a schematic density-dependent contact (SDDC) pairing model is employed. From the time-dependent calculation, we observed the exponential decay rule of a two-proton emission. It is shown that the density dependence does not play a major role in determining the decay width, which can be controlled only by the asymptotic strength of the pairing interaction. This asymptotic pairing sensitivity can be understood in terms of the dynamics of the wave function driven by the three-body Hamiltonian, by monitoring the time-dependent density distribution. With this simple SDDC pairing model, there remains an impossible trinity problem: it cannot simultaneously reproduce the empirical $Q$ value, decay width, and the nucleon-nucleon scattering length. This problem suggests that a further sophistication of the theoretical pairing model is necessary, utilizing the two-proton radioactivity data as the reference quantities.

## Key claim

The two-proton decay width of 6Be is sensitive to the asymptotic strength of the pairing interaction but not to its density dependence near the core, yet the simple schematic density-dependent contact (SDDC) pairing model cannot simultaneously reproduce the empirical Q value, decay width, and nucleon-nucleon scattering length (the "impossible trinity problem").

## Method

The authors employed a time-dependent three-body model describing 6Be as an alpha core plus two valence protons, with the decay process treated as time-evolution of a three-body resonance state. The proton-proton pairing interaction was modeled using a schematic density-dependent contact (SDDC) potential with an energy cutoff of 40 MeV, and the initial state was prepared using a confining potential method. The time-dependent Schrodinger equation was solved by expanding the wave function on a discretized basis of single-particle states up to the (h11/2) channel within a radial box of 80 fm.

## Key numbers

- The empirical Q value for 6Be two-proton decay is 1371 +/- 5 keV (from Ajzenberg-Selove 1988)
- The experimental two-proton decay width is 92 +/- 6 keV (from Ajzenberg-Selove 1988)
- With the surface SDDC pairing model (eta=1.04), the calculated Q value is 1370.7 keV and the decay width is 34.7 keV, underestimating the experimental width
- The spin-singlet component of the decay width with SDDC is 33.4 keV, while the spin-triplet component is 1.3 keV
- With the finite-range Minnesota pairing model, the decay width is 88.2 keV (spin-singlet 87.1 keV, spin-triplet 1.1 keV), in better agreement with experiment
- The pairing correlation energy for the surface SDDC model is -6.28 MeV, comprising -1.46 MeV from the recoil term and -4.82 MeV from the pairing interaction
- The vacuum scattering length used to fix the bare strength is a0 = -18.5 fm
- The bare strength w0 for the SDDC model is -767.398 MeV.fm^3

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent three-body model developed by Oishi (2014) to systematically study how different pairing models affect two-proton radioactivity observables. It directly compares the zero-range SDDC pairing model with the finite-range Minnesota pairing model used in Oishi (2014), finding that while both produce similar correlated diproton emission dynamics, they yield significantly different decay widths. The results suggest that two-proton radioactivity data can serve as a new reference for validating and sophisticating nuclear pairing models beyond what is possible with bound-state observables alone, and point toward the need for more sophisticated density-dependence parameterizations (Yamagami 2012) or three-body forces (Myo 2001).
