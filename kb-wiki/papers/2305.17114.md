# Impact of initial fluctuations and nuclear deformations in isobar collisions

> Relativistic isobar ($^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr) collisions have revealed intricate differences in their nuclear size and shape, inspiring unconventional studies of nuclear structure using relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this study, we investigate the relative differences in the mean multiplicity ($R_{\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle}$) and the second- ($R_{ε_{2}}$) and third-order eccentricity ($R_{ε_{3}}$) between isobar collisions using initial state models. It is found that initial fluctuations and nuclear deformations have negligible effects on $R_{\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle}$ in most central collisions, while both are important for the $R_{ε_{2}}$ and $R_{ε_{3}}$, the degree of which is sensitive to the underlying nucleonic or sub-nucleonic degree of freedom. These features, compared to real data, may probe the particle production mechanism and the physics underlying nuclear structure.

## Key claim

Initial fluctuations and nuclear deformations have negligible effects on the relative difference in mean multiplicity ($R_{\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle}$) in most central collisions, but both are important for the second- and third-order eccentricity ratios ($R_{\epsilon_{2}}$ and $R_{\epsilon_{3}}$), with the degree of sensitivity depending on whether nucleonic or sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom are used.

## Method

The authors used the Optical Glauber model and Monte Carlo Glauber model to calculate the relative differences in mean multiplicity and eccentricities between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. Nuclear density profiles were taken from energy density functional theory calculations with Woods-Saxon parameterizations, including quadrupole deformation $\beta_2=0.16$ for Ru and octupole deformation $\beta_3=0.20$ for Zr. The study compared results under nucleon participant and quark participant assumptions, using GPU parallel computing for high-precision calculations.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections: $\sigma_X^{\rm RuRu}=4.369$ barn and $\sigma_X^{\rm ZrZr}=4.551$ barn for Optical Glauber; $\sigma_X^{\rm MC,RuRu}=4.206$ barn and $\sigma_X^{\rm MC,ZrZr}=4.348$ barn for Monte Carlo Glauber (4.46% and 3.73% smaller, respectively)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Ru: $R=5.093$ fm, $a=0.487$ fm (spherical) or $a=0.471$ fm (deformed with $\beta_2=0.16$)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Zr: $R=5.022$ fm, $a=0.538$ fm (spherical) or $a=0.517$ fm (deformed with $\beta_3=0.20$)
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: $\sigma_{\rm NN}=42$ mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- The difference in $R_{\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle}$ between spherical and deformed cases is approximately 10%, corresponding to an uncertainty on the extracted slope parameter of symmetry energy of about 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isobar collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider by the STAR Collaboration (STAR 2021), which showed sizable differences in multiplicity distribution, elliptic flow, and triangular flow between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions. The study builds on earlier work by Xu (2017), Zhang (2021), and Li (2019) that used isobar collisions to probe nuclear structure parameters, and by Jia (2022) and Nijs (2021) on quantitative assessments of isobar size and shape differences. The results enable more precise extraction of the symmetry energy slope parameter $L(\rho)$ and nuclear deformation parameters from experimental data, and may help distinguish between different particle production mechanisms in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
