# Constraining Nuclear Symmetry Energy with Multi-messenger Resonant Shattering Flares

> Much effort is devoted to measuring the nuclear symmetry energy through neutron star (NS) and nuclear observables. Since matter in the NS core may be non-hadronic, observables like radii and tidal deformability may not provide reliable constraints on properties of nucleonic matter. We demonstrate that coincident timing of a resonant shattering flare (RSF) and gravitational wave signal during binary NS inspiral probes the crust-core transition region and provides constraints on the symmetry energy comparable to terrestrial nuclear experiments. We show that nuclear masses, RSFs and measurements of NS radii and tidal deformabilities constrain different density ranges of the EOS, providing complementary probes.

# Constraining Nuclear Symmetry Energy with Multi-messenger Resonant Shattering Flares

## Key claim
A single coincident detection of a resonant shattering flare (RSF) and gravitational wave signal during binary neutron star inspiral can constrain the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy (L) with precision comparable to terrestrial nuclear experiments, but only if the measurement probes the crust-core transition region where nucleonic physics is dominant rather than the core where non-hadronic degrees of freedom may invalidate nucleonic equations of state.

## Method
The authors construct neutron star equations of state using an extended Skyrme energy density functional parameterized by the symmetry energy parameters J, L, and K_sym for densities up to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density, coupled with a piecewise polytrope at higher densities to represent unknown core physics. They perform Bayesian inference via Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling of the five model parameters (J, L, K_sym, and two polytropic indices) using an injected RSF frequency of 250 Hz with a conservative uncertainty, and compare constraints from RSF data alone, from astrophysical data (NICER mass-radius measurements and GW170817 tidal deformability), and from nuclear binding energies.

## Key numbers
- The injected RSF frequency used for the primary analysis is f_RSF = 250 Hz, produced by a neutron star with mass 1.4 solar masses
- The uncertainty in the measured i-mode frequency is approximately delta_f ~ 3.7 Hz for a chirp mass of 1.2 solar masses at f_RSF = 100 Hz
- Nuclear saturation density is n_s ~ 0.16 fm^-3
- The prior ranges for symmetry energy parameters are: 25 < J < 45 MeV, 0 < L < 200 MeV, -600 < K_sym < 200 MeV
- An injected frequency of f_RSF = 350 Hz results in a shift of the J-L constraint region of approximately -20 MeV in L compared to the 250 Hz injection
- The i-mode frequency is mainly dependent on matter properties around 0.5 times nuclear saturation density

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies on resonant shattering flares (Neill et al., 2022) and the i-mode as a candidate for triggering RSFs, building on the original RSF mechanism proposed by Tsang (2012, 2013). The paper addresses the concern raised by Baym (2018) and others that core-dependent observables like radii and tidal deformability may not reliably constrain nucleonic symmetry energy if non-hadronic matter exists in the inner core. The results show that RSF constraints on L are complementary to constraints from nuclear masses (which primarily constrain J, as in Kortelainen 2010), from heavy ion collisions (Tsang 2009), and from dipole polarizability experiments (Roca-Maza 2013). The approach enables independent probes of the crust and core, with upcoming LIGO/Virgo observing runs potentially yielding the first multi-messenger RSF detection.
