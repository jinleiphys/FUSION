# Energy weighted sums for collective excitations in nuclear Fermi-liquid

> Model independent, $m_1$, adiabatic, $m_{-1}$, and high-energy, $m_3$, energy weighted sums for the isoscalar and isovector nuclear excitations are investigated within the framework of the kinetic theory adopted to the description of a two-component nuclear Fermi-liquid. For both the adiabatic and scaling approaches, the connection of the EWS $m_{-1}$ and $m_3$ to the nuclear stiffness coefficients and the first- and zero-sound velocity is established. We study the enhancement factor $κ_{I}$ in the energy weighted sum $m'_1$ for the isovector excitations and provide the reasonable explanation of the experimental exceeding of the 100% exhaustion of sum $m'_1$ for the isovector giant dipole resonances. We show the dependence of the enhancement factor $κ_{I}$ on the nuclear mass number $A$ and analyse its dependence on the Landau's isovector amplitude $F'_1$.

# Energy weighted sums for collective excitations in nuclear Fermi-liquid

## Key claim

The paper shows that the enhancement factor $\kappa_I$ in the isovector energy-weighted sum $m'_1$ depends on both the nuclear mass number $A$ and the Landau isovector amplitude $F'_1$, providing an explanation for why experimental data can exceed 100% exhaustion of the sum rule for isovector giant dipole resonances, a result that depends on the velocity dependence of effective nuclear forces.

## Method

The authors use the linearized Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation for a two-component nuclear Fermi-liquid to derive the linear response function and the energy-weighted sums $m_{-1}$, $m_1$, and $m_3$ for both isoscalar and isovector excitations. They expand the distribution function distortion in multipoles of the Fermi surface and truncate the recurrence chain at different orders to obtain the adiabatic (first-sound) and scaling (zero-sound) approximations. For finite nuclei, they impose boundary conditions at a sharp nuclear surface using the Euler equation and pressure tensor formalism.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear incompressibility coefficient: $K \approx 220$ MeV (from Eq. 46)
- Coefficient of isotopic symmetry: $C_{sym} = \frac{2}{3}\varepsilon_F (1+F'_0) \approx 60$ MeV (from Eq. 67)
- Adiabatic sound velocity for isoscalar excitations: $\tilde{u}_1 = \sqrt{(1+F_0)p_F^2/(3mm^*)}$ (Eq. 51)
- Scaling sound velocity for isoscalar excitations: $\tilde{u}_3 = \sqrt{(9/5+F_0)p_F^2/(3mm^*)}$ (Eq. 51)
- Enhancement factor definition: $1+\kappa_I = (1+F'_1/3)/(1+F_1/3)$ (Eq. 72)
- Isovector sum $m'_1 = \hbar^2 (A/2m') q^2$ where $m' = m/(1+\kappa_I)$ (Eq. 71)
- Adiabatic isovector energy centroid: $\tilde{E}'_1 = \hbar \sqrt{C_{sym}/m'} \, q$ (Eq. 74)
- Scaling isovector energy centroid: $\tilde{E}'_3 = \hbar \sqrt{C'_{sym}/m'} \, q$ where $C'_{sym} = C_{sym} + 8\varepsilon_F/15$ (Eqs. 71, 74)

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Kolomietz and collaborators (Ko, KoSh) to systematically connect energy-weighted sums to nuclear transport coefficients for both isoscalar and isovector channels. It builds on the Landau Fermi-liquid theory (Abrikosov and Khalatnikov) and the scaling approximation used in earlier studies of giant resonances (Lipparini and Stringari, 1989; Bohigas, Lane, and Martorell). The results enable a consistent treatment of the enhancement factor in isovector sum rules, addressing the long-standing puzzle of experimental sum-rule exhaustion exceeding 100% for the giant dipole resonance, and provide a framework for extracting Landau parameters from measured resonance energies.
