# Radial Oscillations and Stability of Neutron Stars with Antikaon Condensates

> Radial oscillations provide a direct probe of the stability and compressibility of neutron stars and are highly sensitive to the equation of state of dense matter. In this work, we investigate the impact of antikaon condensates on the radial oscillation properties of neutron stars. We model neutron star matter using equations of state with a wide range of stiffness. For this purpose, both non-linear and density-dependent relativistic mean-field frameworks are employed to develop equations of state that are consistent with current astrophysical constraints. We further consider the emergence of antikaon condensates ($K^-$ and $\bar{K}^0$) in the stellar core, which modifies the pressure--energy density relation of dense matter. We find that the nature of the transition from nuclear matter to the condensed phase is sensitive to the antikaon optical potential depth and underlying equation of state. We compute the fundamental and higher-order radial oscillation modes for neutron stars containing antikaon condensates over a range of antikaon optical potential depths. Our results demonstrate that the antikaon optical potential depth plays a decisive role in governing the systematic shifts observed in the radial oscillation frequencies, while also significantly reducing the stability limits and maximum masses of neutron stars. These imprints of antikaon condensation on radial oscillation spectra provide a promising avenue for future multi-messenger observations and high-frequency gravitational-wave searches to directly probe and constrain the internal composition and equation of state of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the antikaon optical potential depth plays a decisive role in governing systematic shifts in radial oscillation frequencies of neutron stars containing antikaon condensates, while also significantly reducing stability limits and maximum masses, but these effects are conditional on the specific underlying equation of state and the nature of the transition from nuclear matter to the condensed phase.

## Method

The authors model neutron star matter using both non-linear (NL) and density-dependent (DD) relativistic mean-field (RMF) frameworks, employing six parametrizations (NL3, GMT, IUFSU, DD2, PKDD, DDMEδ) to generate equations of state with varying stiffness. They incorporate antikaon condensates (K⁻ and K̄⁰) via minimal coupling, vary the antikaon optical potential depth between -160 and -120 MeV, and compute the fundamental and higher-order radial oscillation modes by solving the linearized Einstein field equations for radially perturbed, spherically symmetric stellar configurations.

## Key numbers

- Antikaon optical potential depth varied in the range -160 ≤ U_K̄ ≤ -120 MeV across all six RMF parametrizations
- Bare nucleon mass fixed at 939 MeV for NL-RMF models; 939 MeV (DD2), 938.565 MeV (PKDD), and 939.573 MeV (DDMEδ) for DD-RMF models
- Vacuum masses of antikaons: 493.69 MeV for K⁻ and 497.611 MeV for K̄⁰
- Nuclear saturation densities range from 0.145 fm⁻³ (GMT) to 0.155 fm⁻³ (IUFSU); binding energies per nucleon range from -16.020 MeV (DD2) to -16.400 MeV (IUFSU)
- Incompressibility moduli range from 219.100 MeV (DDMEδ) to 281.000 MeV (GMT); symmetry energy coefficients range from 31.30 MeV (IUFSU) to 37.40 MeV (NL3)
- Effective-to-bare nucleon mass ratios range from 0.5625 (DD2) to 0.6340 (GMT)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of radial oscillations in neutron stars containing hyperons, deconfined quark matter, and Δ baryons (Kokkotas 2000, Panotopoulos 2018, Sagun 2020, Sun 2021, Sen 2022, Li 2022, Routaray 2022, Rather 2023-2024, Jyothilakshmi 2025) by providing the first systematic analysis of radial oscillations in neutron stars with antikaon condensates across a wide range of equation-of-state stiffness. The study builds on earlier investigations of antikaon condensation in neutron stars (Lalazissis 1996, Pal 2000, Fattoyev 2010, Pons 2000, Banik 2000-2002, Gupta 2013, Char 2014, Batra 2017, Kundu 2022, Thapa 2020-2021) and enables future multi-messenger observations and high-frequency gravitational-wave searches with third-generation detectors like Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope to directly probe the internal composition of neutron stars.
