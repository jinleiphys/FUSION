# Cottingham formula for the electromagnetic self-energy contribution to M_p - M_n

> We provide an update of the determination of the electromagnetic self-energy contribution to M_p - M_n based upon Cottingham's Formula. A technical oversight in the literature is uncovered: the application of the Cottingham Formula requires the use of a subtracted dispersion integral; an argument to evade the subtraction function was presented; the argument was based on false assumptions about the scaling violations of the parton model, a point first mentioned by J. C. Collins. We elucidate this point and utilize low-energy effective theory to relate the unknown subtraction function to the nucleon isovector magnetic polarizability. This allows us to provide the first reliable determination of dM = 1.30(03)(47) MeV [1].

# Cottingham formula for the electromagnetic self-energy contribution to M_p - M_n

## Key claim
The electromagnetic self-energy contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is determined to be 1.30(03)(47) MeV, but this result depends on modeling the unknown subtraction function in the Cottingham formula; the previous determination by Gasser and Leutwyler (1975) of 0.76(30) MeV is invalidated because it relied on false assumptions about scaling violations of the parton model, as first pointed out by J. C. Collins.

## Method
The authors apply the Cottingham formula, which relates the electromagnetic self-energy to an integral of the forward Compton scattering tensor, using a once-subtracted dispersion relation for the scalar function T_1 that requires a subtraction function T_1(0,Q^2). They evaluate the elastic contribution using the Kelly parameterization of elastic form factors, the inelastic contribution from parameterizations of resonance and scaling regions of nucleon structure functions, and model the unknown subtraction function by relating its low-Q^2 behavior to the nucleon isovector magnetic polarizability via heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, connecting to high-Q^2 behavior using the operator product expansion.

## Key numbers
- The total electromagnetic self-energy contribution: dM^gamma = 1.30(03)(47) MeV, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second systematic from modeling the subtraction function
- Elastic contribution: dM^el = 1.39(02) MeV
- Elastic subtraction term: dM^sub_el = -0.62(02) MeV
- Inelastic contribution: dM^inel = 0.057(16) MeV
- Inelastic subtraction term: dM^sub_inel = +0.47(47) MeV
- The experimental proton-neutron mass difference: M_p - M_n = -1.29333217(42) MeV
- The lattice QCD determination of the quark mass contribution: dM^{m_d - m_u}_{LQCD} = -2.53(40) MeV
- The isovector magnetic polarizability used: beta_M^{p-n} = -1.0 +/- 1.0 x 10^{-4} fm^3
- The counterterm contribution estimated as less than 0.02 MeV

## Context
This work corrects a technical oversight in the Cottingham formula determination by Gasser and Leutwyler (1975), who assumed power-law corrections to the Callan-Gross relation that allowed evasion of the subtraction function; the authors show that QCD corrections are only suppressed by the strong coupling constant, invalidating that argument. The result enables an independent determination of the light quark mass difference m_d - m_u when combined with lattice QCD calculations, and the same unknown subtraction function also contributes to muonic hydrogen energy levels as studied by Pachucki (1996), Carlson (2011), Birse (2012), and Miller (2012).
