# Periodic interference structures in the time-like proton form factor

> An intriguing and elusive feature of the timelike hadron form factor is the possible presence of an imaginary part associated to rescattering processes. We find evidence of that in the recent and precise data on the proton timelike form factor measured by the BABAR collaboration. By plotting these data as a function of the 3-momentum of the relative motion of the final proton and antiproton, a systematic sinusoidal modulation is highlighted in the near-threshold region. Our analysis attributes this pattern to rescattering processes at a relative distance of 0.7-1.5 fm between the centers of the forming hadrons. This distance implies a large fraction of inelastic processes in $\bar{p}p$ interactions, and a large imaginary part in the related $e^+e^- \rightarrow \bar{p}p$ reaction because of unitarity.

# Periodic interference structures in the time-like proton form factor

## Key claim
The paper claims evidence for a systematic sinusoidal modulation in the BABAR proton time-like form factor data when plotted as a function of the 3-momentum of the relative motion of the final proton and antiproton, which the authors attribute to rescattering processes. The claim is hedged by the condition that the modulation is detectable only in the near-threshold region (q² < 10 GeV²) and that the relative magnitude of about 10% is constant in this range, suggesting but not proving the modulation could persist at higher q² in future data.

## Method
The authors plot the BABAR collaboration data on the generalized proton time-like form factor as a function of the 3-momentum p of one hadron in the frame where the other is at rest, defined from the total energy. They subtract a smooth regular background fit (the BABAR parametrization) from the data and fit the residual with a damped cosine function A exp(-B p) cos(C p + D) using χ² minimization via the Minuit package. The Fourier transform of the fitted function is then analyzed in coordinate space to extract the distance scale of the rescattering processes.

## Key numbers
- The distance between the first, second, and third maxima of the oscillation is Δp ≈ 1.14 GeV.
- The oscillation amplitude parameter A = 0.05 ± 0.01 (dimensionless, relative to the form factor magnitude).
- The damping parameter B = 0.7 ± 0.2 GeV⁻¹, giving a damping range 1/B ≈ 1.4 GeV.
- The oscillation frequency parameter C = 5.5 ± 0.2 GeV⁻¹.
- The relative size of the oscillating term over the regular background is ∼ 10%.
- The rescattering processes are attributed to a distance range of 0.7–1.5 fm between the centers of the forming hadrons.
- The fit χ²/n.d.f. = 1.2.
- The regular background fit uses parameters A = 7.7 GeV⁻⁴ and m_a² = 14.8 GeV² from the BABAR parametrization.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of time-like proton form factors using the precise BABAR data (Lees 2013) and provides evidence for the long-sought imaginary part associated with rescattering processes, which had been predicted theoretically but remained elusive. The analysis builds on prior studies of proton-antiproton interactions at LEAR (Zenoni 1999) and AD (Bianconi 2011), which established large inelastic cross sections near threshold, and on Watson's final state theorem (Watson 1952) connecting unitarity to the imaginary part. The results enable future tests with more precise data from BESIII (BEPCII) and PANDA (FAIR), and challenge models that analytically continue space-like form factors to the time-like region, such as vector meson dominance approaches (Bijker 2004, Adamuscin 2005) and dispersion relation methods (Belushkin 2006, Lomon 2012).
