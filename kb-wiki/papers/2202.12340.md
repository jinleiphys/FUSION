# Basic Elements for Simulations of Standard Model Physics with Quantum Annealers: Multigrid and Clock States

> We explore the potential of D-Wave's quantum annealers for computing some of the basic components required for quantum simulations of Standard Model physics. By implementing a basic multigrid (including "zooming") and specializing Feynman-clock algorithms, D-Wave's Advantage is used to study harmonic and anharmonic oscillators relevant for lattice scalar field theories and effective field theories, the time evolution of a single plaquette of SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice gauge field theory, and the dynamics of flavor entanglement in four neutrino systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that D-Wave's quantum annealers can compute basic components needed for quantum simulations of Standard Model physics, but the results are limited to small test systems (single-site oscillators, one SU(3) plaquette, four-neutrino systems) and the precision depends on careful tuning of parameters such as the Lagrange multiplier eta, the number of bits per coefficient K, and the number of zoom steps.

## Method

The authors map Hamiltonian ground-state and time-evolution problems onto quadratic unconstrained binary optimization (QUBO) problems by expanding wavefunctions in a finite orthonormal basis with coefficients represented in fixed-point binary form. They implement an adaptive zooming algorithm that iteratively refines the search range for coefficients, a multigrid procedure that solves coarser discretizations first and interpolates solutions to finer grids, and a generalized Feynman-clock algorithm for time evolution. These methods are executed on D-Wave's neal simulator and on the D-Wave Advantage system 4.1 quantum annealer.

## Key numbers

- Harmonic oscillator ground state energy deviation from digitized value: (4.0 +2.6 -1.2) x 10^{-6} (lattice units) with 8 zoom steps, K=3, N_A=10^3, n_s=64 states, phi_max=5
- Harmonic oscillator first excited state energy deviation: (5.2 +2.5 -1.7) x 10^{-6} (lattice units) with 8 zoom steps, K=3, N_A=10^3, n_s=64
- Harmonic oscillator fifth excited state energy deviation: (4.7 +2.3 -1.6) x 10^{-6} (lattice units) with 8 zoom steps, K=3, N_A=10^3, n_s=64
- Digitized harmonic oscillator ground state energy matches exact continuum value (0.5 lattice units) to better than 3.5 x 10^{-11} with n_s=64, phi_max=5
- Ground state wavefunction fidelity: digitized wavefunction reproduces continuum wavefunction to better than 10^{-6}, neal results reproduce digitized wavefunction to better than 10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the approach of Rahman et al. (2021) who first simulated SU(2) plaquette systems on quantum annealers, and Chang et al. (2019) who developed adaptive eigenvalue solvers for D-Wave systems. The paper adds multigrid techniques inspired by classical numerical methods (Brandt, 1977) and generalizes the Feynman-clock algorithm to arbitrary Hermitian matrices. The results enable future work on using quantum annealers as preconditioners for universal quantum computers and on simulating larger lattice gauge theory systems, though scaling to physically relevant system sizes remains an open challenge.
