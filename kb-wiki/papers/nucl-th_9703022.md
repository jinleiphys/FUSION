# Comment on ``Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter''

> In a recent paper, Eletsky and Ioffe estimated the energy shift of $ρ$-meson with high momentum (q) in nuclei using the Glauber approximation. They also made remarks on the low q vector mesons in medium. In this Comment, we will discuss the second point and show that (i) mean-field physics for the mass shift of low-q mesons is overlooked in the paper, and (ii) the short distant operator product expansion is relevant to analyze the low-q mesons in QCD sum rules contrary to the claim in the paper.

# Comment on "Meson Masses in Nuclear Matter"

## Key claim

The mean-field physics for the mass shift of low-momentum vector mesons in nuclear matter is overlooked in Eletsky and Ioffe (1997), and contrary to their claim, the short-distance operator product expansion (OPE) is relevant for analyzing low-momentum mesons in QCD sum rules.

## Method

The authors analyze the polarization function (generalized optical potential) for vector mesons in nuclear matter, showing that at low momentum where |q|^{-1} >> d (inter-nucleon distance), multiple scattering requires averaging the in-medium forward scattering amplitude over the Fermi sea. They compare two OPE approaches: the short-distance expansion (where Q^2 and Q^2/(q·P)^2 go to infinity with finite ratio) and the light-cone expansion (where Q^2 and Q^2/q·P go to infinity with finite ratio), arguing that for q ≈ 0 with P = 0, the short-distance expansion is the natural generalization of vacuum QCD sum rules via a fixed-q dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The polarization function at extremely low density reduces to Π(ω, q) = -4π F(ω, q; 0) ρ, where ρ is the nuclear density and F is the forward scattering amplitude
- The expansion parameter at low density is Λ_QCD^l p_F^m |q|^n / ω^(l+n+m), where p_F is the Fermi momentum
- The OPE analysis keeps contributions up to dimension 6 operators

## Context

This Comment responds to Eletsky and Ioffe (1997), who estimated the energy shift of high-momentum rho mesons using the Glauber approximation. The authors argue that for low-momentum mesons, the mean-field plus RPA treatment employed in hadronic model calculations (Jean, Piekarewicz and Williams, 1994) is most appropriate, and that the short-distance OPE approach developed in Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Hatsuda, Lee and Shiomi (1995) is valid for low-momentum mesons. The work by Drukarev and Levin (1991) using light-cone expansion with fixed s kinematics is criticized as inappropriate for extracting mean-field physics because it inevitably gives a large q limit.
