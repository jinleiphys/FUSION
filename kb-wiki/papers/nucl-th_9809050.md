# Not Strange but Bizarre Physics from the SAMPLE Experiment

> Since the report of the SAMPLE Collaboration suggesting the strange-quark contribution to nucleon magnetic moments, G_M^s(0), may be greater than zero, numerous models have appeared supporting positive values for G_M^s(0). In this paper the bizarre physics associated with G_M^s(0) > 0 is illustrated. Using new lattice QCD results, our best estimate for G_M^s(0) shifts slightly from G_M^s(0) = -0.75 +/- 0.30 mu_N, to G_M^s(0) = -0.62 +/- 0.26 mu_N.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the SAMPLE Collaboration's suggestion of a positive strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, \(G_M^s(0) > 0\), would require bizarre and physically implausible properties of QCD, such as an enhancement of the heavier strange-quark loop moment relative to the lighter down-quark loop moment or a valence-quark ratio \(u_n/u_\Xi\) much larger than 1; the author's updated lattice QCD estimate instead gives a large negative value.

## Method

The author derives two exact expressions for \(G_M^s(0)\) in terms of experimentally measured octet baryon magnetic moments (p, n, \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Sigma^-\), \(\Xi^0\), \(\Xi^-\)) and two quark-sector ratios: the strange-to-down sea-quark-loop ratio \(^{\ell}R_d^s\) and the valence-quark environment-sensitivity ratio \(u_n/u_\Xi\). These expressions are then analyzed by plotting the resulting surface for \(G_M^s(0)\) as a function of these ratios, and the regions consistent with positive versus negative \(G_M^s(0)\) are compared to expectations from baryon mass ratios and lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

* The SAMPLE Collaboration measured \(G_M^s(0.1\ {\rm GeV}^2) = +0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19\ \mu_N\) (statistical, systematic, and theoretical uncertainties, respectively).
* The author's previous lattice QCD estimate was \(G_M^s(0) = -0.75 \pm 0.30\ \mu_N\).
* Using new lattice QCD results, the updated best estimate is \(G_M^s(0) = -0.62 \pm 0.26\ \mu_N\).
* The lattice QCD result for the valence-quark ratio is \(u_n/u_\Xi = 0.72 \pm 0.46\).
* The best estimate for the sea-quark-loop ratio is \(^{\ell}R_d^s = 0.55 \pm 0.06\).
* The experimentally measured baryon magnetic moments used in the expressions are given as 3.673 \(\mu_N\) and 3.618 \(\mu_N\) for the first combination, and -1.033 \(\mu_N\) and -0.599 \(\mu_N\) for the second combination (in units of nuclear magnetons).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Leinweber, 1997) of the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, which used lattice QCD to estimate \(G_M^s(0) = -0.75 \pm 0.30\ \mu_N\). It directly responds to the SAMPLE Collaboration's experimental result suggesting a positive value, and contrasts the bizarre QCD properties required for \(G_M^s(0) > 0\) with the more natural negative solution. The paper enables future experiments to anticipate a large negative value near \(-0.6\ \mu_N\) and provides a framework for interpreting new measurements of \(G_M^s(0)\) in terms of quark-sector ratios.
