# Ab initio many-body calculations of nucleon scattering on 4He, 7Li, 7Be, 12C and 16O

> We combine a recently developed ab initio many-body approach capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states, the ab initio NCSM/RGM, with an importance truncation scheme for the cluster eigenstate basis and demostrate its applicability to nuclei with mass numbers as high as 17. Using soft similarity renormalization group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions, we first calculate nucleon-4He phase shifts, cross sections and analyzing power. Next, we investigate nucleon scattering on 7Li, 7Be, 12C and 16O in coupled-channel NCSM/RGM calculations that include low-lying excited states of these nuclei. We check the convergence of phase shifts with the basis size and study A=8, 13, and 17 bound and unbound states. Our calculations predict low-lying resonances in 8Li and 8B that have not been experimentally clearly identified yet. We are able to reproduce reasonably well the structure of the A=13 low lying states. However, we find that A=17 states cannot be described without an improved treatment of 16O one-particle-one-hole excitations and alpha clustering.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the _ab initio_ NCSM/RGM approach, combined with an importance truncation scheme for the cluster eigenstate basis, can be applied to nucleon scattering on nuclei with mass numbers up to 17, but they find that A=17 states cannot be described without an improved treatment of 16O one-particle-one-hole excitations and alpha clustering.

## Method

The authors combine the _ab initio_ no-core shell model (NCSM) with the resonating-group method (RGM) to describe both bound and scattering states of light nuclei. They use soft similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions and employ an importance truncation scheme based on many-body perturbation theory to reduce the computational complexity of the NCSM calculations for cluster eigenstates. The one- and two-body densities from the importance truncated NCSM calculations are used to obtain the NCSM/RGM integration kernels, which are then solved as coupled-channel Schrodinger equations for the relative motion wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-4He phase shifts were calculated using HO basis expansion up to N_max=17 for the localized parts of the NCSM/RGM integration kernels and for the 4He ground- and first-excited 0+0 wave functions
- The SRG-N3LO NN potential used a cutoff of 2.02 fm^-1
- Calculations employed soft SRG evolved chiral N3LO NN potential that allowed convergence within about 14-16 hbar-Omega HO excitations in the basis expansion
- Nucleon-alpha scattering calculations covered the energy range approximately 6-19 MeV for differential cross sections and analyzing powers
- The IT-NCSM(seq) scheme started with full NCSM calculation in 2 hbar-Omega and sequentially constructed importance truncated spaces up to higher N_max values

## Context

This work extends the _ab initio_ NCSM/RGM approach previously applied to n-3H, n-4He, n-10Be, and p-3,4He scattering (Navratil et al., 2009) to heavier target nuclei including 7Li, 7Be, 12C, and 16O. The importance truncation scheme (Roth, 2009) enables calculations in larger model spaces than previously possible with the full NCSM. The paper enables future studies of nucleon scattering on p-shell nuclei using realistic interactions, while identifying that A=17 systems require further methodological improvements to account for 16O one-particle-one-hole excitations and alpha clustering.
