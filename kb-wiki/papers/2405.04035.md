# Absence of binding of heavy fermions by two light atoms in two dimensions

> By developing the mean-field theory valid for large $N$, we investigate the problem of two light fermions interacting via a zero-range potential with $N$ heavy fermions in two dimensions. We obtain numerical evidence that this system is never fully bound. It always splits into droplets containing a single light atom. This is in contrast to the one-dimensional case where any number of heavy and light fermions can be bound together.

# Absence of binding of heavy fermions by two light atoms in two dimensions

## Key claim
The authors provide numerical evidence, based on mean-field theory valid for large $N$ and large mass ratio $M/m$, that a system of two light fermions interacting via zero-range forces with $N$ heavy fermions in two dimensions is never fully bound; it always splits into separate $N+1$-type droplets containing a single light atom each, with any excess heavy atoms forming a free gas. The authors hedge that they cannot exclude binding for finite $N$ due to beyond-mean-field effects, nor for clusters with more than two light atoms.

## Method
The authors develop a mean-field Hartree-Fock energy functional for $N$ heavy and $N_l$ light fermions, using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for the heavy component (valid for $N\gg 1$) and solving coupled equations for the light-atom orbitals and heavy-atom density. They include a beyond-mean-field correction (second-order perturbation theory) to break the scaling degeneracy of the two-dimensional mean-field solutions and determine cluster energies and sizes. For the $N+2$ case, they solve the mean-field equations numerically on a lattice in a square box of variable size $L\times L$, monitoring how the density profile and cluster populations change with $L$ to test for self-binding.

## Key numbers
- The $N+1$ cluster in two dimensions exists for $\alpha = 4\pi N^2/(M/m) < 11.7$, corresponding to a critical heavy-atom number $N_c = 0.965 (M/m)^{1/2}$.
- The $2+1$ trimer in two dimensions emerges for mass ratio $M/m > 3.33$; the $3+1$ tetramer for $M/m > 3.38$; the $4+1$ pentamer for $M/m > 5.14$.
- The leading-order cluster energy scales as $E \approx -m^{-1}a^{-2} e^{-4\pi N/\gamma_c}$, where $\gamma_c$ is the critical coupling from the mean-field solution.
- Numerical box sizes up to $L=16$ (in units of $1/\sqrt{2m|\epsilon_1|}$) were used, with lattices up to $450\times 450$ grid points; the distance between clusters grows linearly with $L$, indicating no binding.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous mean-field studies of $N+1$ clusters in one and two dimensions (Tononi 2022, Givois 2023) and of $N+N_l$ clusters in one dimension (Givois 2023), where binding of multiple light atoms was found possible. The present two-dimensional result contrasts sharply with the one-dimensional case, where $N+2$ systems can form bound chains of droplets. The finding also contrasts with exact few-body results for small $N$ in two dimensions (Pricoupenko and Pedri for the trimer; Liu 2022 for tetramer and pentamer), which show binding thresholds at specific mass ratios. The work enables future studies of whether $N+N_l$ clusters with $N_l>2$ might become bound in the mean-field regime, and of beyond-mean-field effects for finite $N$.
