# 2-Nucleon 1-Loop Corrections to Pion Double Charge Exchange within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> One-loop corrections at the two-nucleon level to Pion Double Charge exchange (DCX) scattering off a nuclear target at threshold, are calculated within the framework of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT). An estimate for the (2-nucleon) 1-loop correction is obtained in the static limit and using an impulse approximation. We find a small (1.6%) increase relative to the leading order tree graphs.

## Key claim

One-loop corrections at the two-nucleon level to pion double charge exchange (DCX) scattering at threshold are calculated within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) and found to produce only a small (1.6%) increase relative to the leading order tree graphs, in contrast to the large one-loop corrections previously found for single-nucleon processes.

## Method

The authors evaluate the eight connected two-nucleon one-loop Feynman diagrams for pion DCX (π⁺ + n + n → π⁻ + p + p) using the leading-order HBChPT Lagrangian, with the static limit for nucleon velocity parameters and an impulse approximation. The loop integrals are computed using dimensional regularization expanded around d=4, and the amplitudes are evaluated at the kinematic point P² = M_π² (where P is the momentum transfer between nucleons) for spin-singlet nuclear states. The ultraviolet-divergent portions are identified and the finite parts are extracted numerically using a renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Total tree-graph amplitude at P² = M_π²: ⟨S=0|T_total[tree]|S=0⟩ = -4.81 × 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻⁴
- Total one-loop amplitude (UV-finite part): ⟨S=0|T_total(1-loop)|S=0⟩ = -(7.9 ± 1.6) × 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻⁴ for μ = 1 ± 0.5 GeV
- One-loop correction relative to tree graphs: 1.6% increase
- Individual tree graph amplitudes: contact graph = -1.42 × 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻⁴; pole graph = -6.3 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻⁴; double-scattering graph = -2.67 × 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻⁴
- Input constants: M_π = 139.57 MeV, F_π = 93 MeV, g_A = 1.26
- Expected chiral suppression factor: M_π²/(4πF_π)² ≈ 0.014

## Context

This work extends the application of HBChPT from single-nucleon processes (where one-loop vertex corrections to π-N and π-π interactions give 10-30% and ~25% corrections respectively, as found by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995)) to two-nucleon processes with pion loops. The tree-level DCX calculation is shown to agree with the earlier work of Koltun and Johnson (1995) using the Olsson-Turner effective Lagrangian. The small one-loop correction found here enables future systematic chiral perturbation theory calculations for multi-nucleon pion reactions, establishing that the chiral expansion converges rapidly for two-nucleon processes despite the larger corrections seen at the single-nucleon level.
