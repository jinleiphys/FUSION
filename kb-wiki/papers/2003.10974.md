# Generalizing the relativistic quantization condition to include all three-pion isospin channels

> We present a generalization of the relativistic, finite-volume, three-particle quantization condition for non-identical pions in isosymmetric QCD. The resulting formalism allows one to use discrete finite-volume energies, determined using lattice QCD, to constrain scattering amplitudes for all possible values of two- and three-pion isospin. As for the case of identical pions considered previously, the result splits into two steps: The first defines a non-perturbative function with roots equal to the allowed energies, $E_n(L)$, in a given cubic volume with side-length $L$. This function depends on an intermediate three-body quantity, denoted $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$, which can thus be constrained from lattice QCD input. The second step is a set of integral equations relating $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ to the physical scattering amplitude, $\mathcal M_3$. Both of the key relations, $E_n(L) \leftrightarrow \mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ and $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}\leftrightarrow \mathcal M_3$, are shown to be block-diagonal in the basis of definite three-pion isospin, $I_{πππ}$, so that one in fact recovers four independent relations, corresponding to $I_{πππ}=0,1,2,3$. We also provide the generalized threshold expansion of $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}$ for all channels, as well as parameterizations for all three-pion resonances present for $I_{πππ}=0$ and $I_{πππ}=1$. As an example of the utility of the generalized formalism, we present a toy implementation of the quantization condition for $I_{πππ}=0$, focusing on the quantum numbers of the $ω$ and $h_1$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the relativistic finite-volume three-particle quantization condition from identical pions to all three-pion isospin channels in isosymmetric QCD, showing that the formalism block-diagonalizes into four independent relations for total isospin \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=0,1,2,3\), but the practical implementation requires truncation in angular momentum and relies on parametrizations of the intermediate three-body quantity \(\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}\) that must be constrained from lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors extend the relativistic effective field theory (RFT) approach of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) by promoting all building blocks of the finite-volume correlator, including the Bethe-Salpeter kernels, the two-particle K-matrix \(\mathcal{K}_2\), and the three-particle quantity \(\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}\), to \(7\times 7\) matrices on the space of neutral three-pion flavor configurations. They then perform a change of basis to states with definite two- and three-pion isospin, which block-diagonalizes the quantization condition into four independent relations, one for each allowed total isospin, with dimensionalities 1, 3, 2, and 1 respectively. The relation between \(\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df},3}\) and the physical scattering amplitude \(\mathcal{M}_3\) is similarly block-diagonalized, and the authors provide threshold expansions and resonant parametrizations for all channels.

## Key numbers

* The quantization condition holds for energies in the range \(m^2 < E^2 - \boldsymbol{P}^2 < (5m)^2\), where \(m\) is the pion mass, neglecting corrections of order \(e^{-mL}\).
* The three-pion isospin decomposition yields multiplicities: \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=0\) (1 irrep), \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=1\) (3 irreps), \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=2\) (2 irreps), \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=3\) (1 irrep), for a total of 7 irreducible representations from the product \(\mathbf{1} \otimes \mathbf{1} \otimes \mathbf{1}\).
* The two-pion subchannel isospin content for each total isospin: \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=0\) has only \(I_{\pi\pi}=1\); \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=1\) has \(I_{\pi\pi}=0,1,2\); \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=2\) has \(I_{\pi\pi}=1,2\); \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=3\) has only \(I_{\pi\pi}=2\).
* The toy implementation for \(I_{\pi\pi\pi}=0\) focuses on the quantum numbers of the \(\omega\) and \(h_1\) resonances, with finite-volume energies exhibiting avoided level crossings associated with cascading resonant decays such as \(h_1 \to \rho\pi \to \pi\pi\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quantization condition for identical particles developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) and the subsequent generalizations by Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe (2017, 2018) to non-identical pions, enabling lattice QCD studies of three-pion systems with any total isospin. It complements alternative approaches such as nonrelativistic effective field theory (Hammer et al., 2017) and finite-volume unitarity (Mai et al., 2017, 2018), and it enables the extraction of scattering amplitudes for experimentally relevant resonances like the \(\omega\) and \(h_1\) that decay to three pions, going beyond the identical-particle case previously applied to three-\(\pi^+\) systems by Blanton et al. (2019).
