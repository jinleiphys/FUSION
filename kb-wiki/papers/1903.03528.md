# Hard dilepton production from a weakly magnetized hot QCD medium

> We have computed the hard dilepton production rate from a weakly magnetized deconfined QCD medium within one-loop photon self-energy by considering one hard and one thermomagnetic resummed quark propagator in the loop. In the presence of the magnetic field, the resummed propagator leads to four quasiparticle modes. The production of hard dileptons consists of rates when all four quasiquarks originating from the poles of the propagator individually annihilate with a hard quark coming from a bare propagator in the loop. Besides these, there are also contributions from a mixture of pole and Landau cut part. In weak field approximation, the magnetic field appears as a perturbative correction to the thermal contribution. Since the calculation is very involved, for a first effort as well as for simplicity, we obtained the rate up to first order in the magnetic field, i.e., ${\cal O}[(eB)]$, which causes a marginal improvement over that in the absence of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The hard dilepton production rate from a weakly magnetized deconfined QCD medium is computed to first order in the magnetic field, i.e., \({\cal O}[(eB)]\), which yields only a marginal improvement over the rate in the absence of magnetic field, as the calculation is limited to a first effort with one hard and one thermomagnetic resummed quark propagator in the loop.

## Method

The authors compute the hard dilepton production rate from the imaginary part of the one-loop photon self-energy, using one hard (bare) quark propagator and one thermomagnetic resummed quark propagator in the loop, with the virtual photon at rest in the plasma rest frame (\(\mathbf{p}=0\)). The resummed propagator in a weak magnetic field yields four quasiparticle modes (two from \(L^2=0\) and two from \(R^2=0\)), and the rate includes contributions from pole-pole annihilation processes (e.g., \(q q_{L(+)} \to \gamma^* \to l^+l^-\)) and pole-cut contributions from the Landau damping region. The calculation is performed up to first order in the magnetic field using a weak-field expansion of the quark propagator, with the thermomagnetic mass given by \(M^{2}(T,m_f,q_{f}B) = \frac{q_{f}B}{16\pi^{2}}[\ln(2)-\frac{T}{m_f}\frac{\pi}{2}]\).

## Key numbers

* The thermal mass is given by \(m_{th}^{2}=\frac{1}{8}C_{F}g^{2}T^{2}\).
* The thermomagnetic mass is \(M^{2}(T,m_f,q_{f}B) = \frac{q_{f}B}{16\pi^{2}}[\ln(2)-\frac{T}{m_f}\frac{\pi}{2}]\).
* The Born rate for dilepton production is \(\left.\frac{dR}{d^4xd^4P}\right|_{\rm born} = \sum_f\(\frac{q_f}{e}\)^2\frac{\alpha^2}{4\pi^4}n_F^2(E/2)\).
* The pole-pole contribution to the dilepton rate at zero magnetic field involves integrals over \(k\) with delta functions \(\delta(E-\omega_+-k)\) and \(\delta(E-\omega_-+k)\), where \(\omega_\pm\) are the HTL quasiparticle energies.
* The pole-cut contribution at zero magnetic field involves an integral over \(x = \omega/m_{th}\) and \(y = k/m_{th}\) with a theta function \(\Theta(2y-E_s)\), where \(E_s = E/m_{th}\).

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop (HTL) approach for dilepton production from a hot QCD medium, as developed by Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan (1990) and Turbide, Rapp, and Gale (2006), to include a weak background magnetic field. It builds on prior calculations of dilepton rates in magnetized media using weak-field approximations by Bandyopadhyay, Farias, and collaborators (2016, 2017) and the Ritus eigenfunction method by Sadooghi and Taghinavaz (2016). The results enable future calculations of hard dilepton production at higher orders in the magnetic field and provide a baseline for comparing with experimental signatures of the chiral magnetic effect and inverse magnetic catalysis in noncentral heavy-ion collisions.
