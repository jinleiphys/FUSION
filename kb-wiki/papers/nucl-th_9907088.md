# Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov Approximation for Finite Systems

> Some general features of the spectrum of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations are examined. Special attention is paid to the asymptotic behavior of the single quasiparticle wave functions (s.qp.w.fs.), matter density distribution and density of the pair condensate. It is shown that due to the coupling between hole and particle states,the deeply bound hole states acquire a width and have to be treated as continuum states. The proper normalization of the s.qp.w.fs. is discussed.

# Hartree–Fock–Bogoliubov Approximation for Finite Systems

## Key claim
Due to the coupling between hole and particle states mediated by the pair condensate, deeply bound hole states acquire a width and must be treated as continuum states rather than bound states, even though the matter density and pair condensate density remain finite. This holds specifically when the two-body interaction has finite range; approximations using a constant or zero-range pairing field lead to unphysical divergences in the pair condensate density.

## Method
The paper derives the Hartree–Fock–Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for a finite system with a finite-range two-body interaction, starting from the quasiparticle vacuum definition and the stationarity of the total energy. It analyzes the asymptotic behavior of the single-quasiparticle wave functions (s.qp.w.fs.) by solving the coupled equations in the exterior region, using perturbation theory near a bound state to show how a discrete hole state spreads into a resonance. The normalization conditions are re-examined: for energies within the interval λ < E < −λ the normalization is a Kronecker symbol (discrete states), while for |E| > |λ| it is a Dirac delta function (continuum states). Three illustrative models are solved: constant pairing (BCS), square-well potentials, and surface delta pairing.

## Key numbers
- The chemical potential λ is negative (λ < 0), and the interval for bound quasiparticle states is λ < E < −λ.
- The width of a resonant hole state is given by Γ(E) = 2π |⟨φ₀|Δ|u₀E⟩|², where φ₀ is the unperturbed bound hole state and u₀E is the continuum particle state.
- The matter density ρ decays asymptotically as exp[2√(2m|λ|/ħ²) r].
- The pair condensate density Φ decays asymptotically as exp[√(2·2m|λ|/ħ²) r], i.e., with a longer tail than the matter density.
- For energies E < λ, the v-component of the s.qp.w.f. decays as exp[−√(2m|2λ|/ħ²) r] while the u-component remains oscillatory (O(1)).
- The integral of the occupation number probability density n(E) over all energies satisfies ∫₋∞^∞ n(E) dE = 1.

## Context
This work extends the HFB formalism, originally developed for infinite systems by N.N. Bogoliubov and others, to finite nuclei where boundary conditions are critical. It contradicts the common practice of treating all s.qp. states as bound (as in the constant-pairing BCS approximation of Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer) and shows that such approximations produce a divergent pair condensate density. The paper enables proper self-consistent HFB calculations for finite nuclei by specifying the correct asymptotic forms and normalization rules; it notes that existing HFB results (e.g., by H.J. Mang and collaborators, or by D. Gogny) may not correspond to the true solution and require re-examination.
