# 3-D structure of the Glasma initial state -- Breaking boost-invariance by collisions of extended shock waves in classical Yang-Mills theory

> We simulate the 3+1 D classical Yang-Mills dynamics of the collisions of longitudinally extended nuclei, described by eikonal color charges in the Color Glass Condensate framework. By varying the longitudinal thickness of the colliding nuclei, we discuss the violations of boost invariance and explore how the boost invariant high-energy limit is approached. Subsequently, we develop a more realistic model of the three dimensional color charge distributions that connects longitudinal and transverse fluctuations to the $x$ and $k_\bot$ dependence of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, and explore the resulting rapidity profiles and their longitudinal fluctuations.

## Key claim

By simulating 3+1D classical Yang-Mills dynamics for collisions of longitudinally extended nuclei, the authors demonstrate that boost invariance is broken for finite longitudinal thickness and is only recovered in the limit of infinitely thin shocks; the violations of boost invariance are controlled by the dimensionless parameter \(Q_s R_\gamma\), where \(R_\gamma\) is the longitudinal thickness of the nuclei.

## Method

The authors discretize the classical Yang-Mills Hamiltonian in temporal axial gauge on a 3D lattice and solve the equations of motion for gauge fields, electric fields, and eikonal color currents using a leap-frog algorithm. Initial conditions are constructed from color charge distributions sampled via the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model in the transverse plane, multiplied by a Gaussian profile \(T(z)\) of width \(R_\gamma\) in the longitudinal direction, and the subsequent collision dynamics is simulated numerically for the SU(2) gauge group.

## Key numbers

* For the thin-shock limit (\(Q_s R_\gamma = 0.0625\)), the longitudinal electric field \(E_z^2\) at mid-rapidity after the collision approaches the boost-invariant result, while for thicker nuclei (\(Q_s R_\gamma = 0.5\)) the peak field strength is reduced and the rise time is delayed.
* The transverse magnetic field \(B_\perp^2\) at the center of the collision shows a delayed onset and reduced peak value as \(Q_s R_\gamma\) increases from 0.0625 to 0.5, compared to the boost-invariant limit.
* Simulations use lattice parameters \(N_\perp = 128\), \(N_z = 2048\), with \(Q_s a_\perp = 0.125\) and \(R_\gamma / a_z = 16\).
* The infrared regulator in the MV model is set to \(m/Q_s = 1\) and the ultraviolet cutoff to \(\Lambda/Q_s = 5\).

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012) and earlier 3+1D studies using colored particle-in-cell methods (Gelfand 2016, Ipp 2017, Ipp 2020) by providing a fully field-theoretic lattice simulation of collisions with finite longitudinal extent. It enables first-principles studies of longitudinal fluctuations and rapidity profiles in heavy-ion collisions, complementing phenomenological models such as those of Bozek (2015) and Pang (2016).
