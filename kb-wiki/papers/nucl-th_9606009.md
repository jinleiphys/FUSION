# Meson-exchange Model for $πN$ scattering and $γN -> πN$ reaction

> An effective Hamiltonian consisting of bare $Δ\leftrightarrowπN$, $γN$ vertex interactions and energy-independent meson-exchange $πN \leftrightarrow πN, γN$ transition operators is derived by applying a unitary transformation to a model Lagrangian with $N,Δ,π$, $ρ$, $ω$, and $γ$ fields. With appropraite phenomenological form factors and coupling constants for $ρ$ and $Δ$, the model can give a good description of $πN$ scattering phase shifts up to the $Δ$ excitation energy region. It is shown that the best reproduction of the recent LEGS data of the photon-asymmetry ratios in $γp \rightarrow π^0 p$ reactions provides rather restricted constraints on the coupling strengths $G_E$ of the electric $E2$ and $G_M$ of the magnetic $M1$ transitions of the bare $Δ\leftrightarrow γN$ vertex and the less well-determined coupling constant $g_{ωNN}$ of $ω$ meson. Within the ranges that $G_M = 1.9 \pm 0.05$, $G_E = 0.0 \pm 0.025$, and $7 \leq g_{ωNN}\leq 10.5$, the predicted differential cross sections and photon-asymmetry ratios are in an overall good agreement with the data of $γp \rightarrow π^0 p$, $γp \rightarrow π^+ n$, and $γn\rightarrow π^- p$ reactions from 180 MeV to the $Δ$ excitation region. The predicted $M_{1^+}$ and $E_{1^+}$ multipole amplitudes are also in good agreement with the empirical values determined by the amplitude analyses. The constructed effective Hamiltonian is free of the nucleon renormlization problem and hence is suitable for nuclear many-body calculations. ## Key claim

The paper claims that a meson-exchange dynamical model, derived via unitary transformation from a Lagrangian with N, Δ, π, ρ, ω, and γ fields, can simultaneously describe πN scattering and pion photoproduction up to the Δ excitation region, but only within the restricted ranges G_M = 1.9 ± 0.05, G_E = 0.0 ± 0.025, and 7 ≤ g_ωNN ≤ 10.5, where the best reproduction of LEGS photon-asymmetry data provides the constraints.

## Method

The authors applied the Fukuda-Sawada-Taketani (FST) unitary transformation method to a model Lagrangian containing N, Δ, π, ρ, ω, and γ fields, deriving an effective Hamiltonian with bare Δ ↔ πN and Δ ↔ γN vertex interactions and energy-independent meson-exchange πN ↔ πN and γN ↔ πN transition operators. The hadronic part was fixed by fitting πN scattering phase shifts from Arndt (1990), Höhler (1983), and Koch (1980) using seven parameters for the meson-exchange πN potential and the Δ ↔ πN vertex. The three remaining parameters (G_M, G_E, and g_ωNN) were then determined by fitting the LEGS data on photon-asymmetry ratios in γp → π⁰p, and the resulting model was tested against extensive differential cross section and asymmetry data for γp → π⁰p, γp → π⁺n, and γn → π⁻p from 180 MeV to the Δ region.

## Key numbers

- G_M = 1.9 ± 0.05 for the magnetic M1 transition of the bare γN → Δ vertex
- G_E = 0.0 ± 0.025 for the electric E2 transition of the bare γN → Δ vertex
- g_ωNN between 7 and 10.5 for the ωNN coupling constant
- E2/M1 ratio for the γN → Δ transition: (0.0 ± 1.3)% for the bare vertex and (-1.8 ± 0.9)% for the dressed vertex
- The model applies from 180 MeV up to the Δ excitation energy region (approximately 300-400 MeV photon energy)
- Seven parameters were used to define the vertices of the meson-exchange πN potential and the Δ ↔ πN transition

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange approach of Pearce and Jennings (1991), Yang and Holstein (1990), and the Jülich group (1992) for πN scattering to also describe pion photoproduction, improving upon earlier dynamical models by Tanabe and Ohta (1985), Yang and Lee (1985), and Nozawa, Blankleider, and Lee (1990) that used phenomenological separable potentials with no dynamical relation to the photoproduction operator. The model enables consistent nuclear many-body calculations of Δ excitation in intermediate-energy reactions, building on the πNN Hamiltonian of Lee (1986), and provides a dynamical justification for the K-matrix method used by Olsson (1974), Davidson and Mukhopadhyay (1990, 1991), and Li (1993) to extract γN → Δ transition amplitudes from data.
