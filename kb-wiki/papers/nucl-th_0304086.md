# Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance of Stable Nuclei in a Consistent Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> A fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation is applied to study the systematic behavior of the isovector giant dipole resonance of nuclei along the $β$-stability line in order to test the effective Lagrangians recently developed. The centroid energies of response functions of the isovector giant dipole resonance for stable nuclei are compared with the corresponding experimental data and the good agreement is obtained. It is found that the effective Lagrangian with an appropriate nuclear symmetry energy, which can well describe the ground state properties of nuclei, could also reproduce the isovector giant dipole resonance of nuclei along the $β$-stability line.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) using effective Lagrangians with an appropriate nuclear symmetry energy can reproduce the centroid energies of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IV GDR) for stable nuclei along the beta-stability line, but the agreement is good only for heavy and medium-weight nuclei; for light nuclei with mass number less than 40, the theoretical results do not agree well with experimental data.

## Method

The authors applied a fully consistent relativistic RPA, where both the relativistic mean field wave functions and the particle-hole residual interactions are derived from the same effective Lagrangian. They calculated the response functions of the isovector dipole operator for over 130 stable nuclei with mass numbers from 12 to 240, using the NL3 parameter set, and extracted the centroid energies from the first moment of the response function integrated up to 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter symmetry energy values for the four parameter sets: NL-SH (36.1 MeV), TM1 (36.9 MeV), NL3 (37.4 MeV), and NL1 (43.6 MeV).
- Centroid energy of IV GDR for 208Pb: 13.16 MeV (NL3) compared to experimental 13.3 ± 0.1 MeV.
- Centroid energy of IV GDR for 116Sn: 15.77 MeV (NL3) compared to experimental 15.7 ± 0.2 MeV.
- Centroid energy of IV GDR for 90Zr: 17.19 MeV (NL3) compared to experimental 16.5 ± 0.2 MeV.
- Centroid energy of IV GDR for 40Ca: 19.57 MeV (NL3) compared to experimental 19.8 ± 0.5 MeV.
- The empirical formula for the peak energy of IV GDR: E_m = 31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6} (in MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic RPA calculations by Ma (1997), Ma, Toki, and Giai (1997), and Giai, Ma, and Toki (1999) from double magic nuclei to a systematic study of over 130 stable nuclei along the beta-stability line. It tests the NL3 parameter set from Lalazissis, Konig, and Ring (1997) against the experimental data compiled by Berman and Fultz (1975), and finds that the effective Lagrangian with appropriate symmetry energy can simultaneously describe ground state properties and the IV GDR, though the relationship between the GDR excitation energy and the nuclear symmetry energy is not strongly constrained.
