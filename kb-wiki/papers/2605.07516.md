# Collinear matching for leading power gluon transverse momentum distributions

> We compute the tree-level and one-loop matching relations for leading power gluon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions. At tree-level, working within the spinor formalism, we focus on twist-2 and twist-3 contributions, deriving the complete series of mass corrections for both T-even and T-odd distributions. At one-loop accuracy, we extend the parton-in-parton framework to include contributions beyond the leading term in the small-b expansion. Applying this methodology to the gluon sector, we obtain for the first time the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation for the gluon worm-gear T distribution. Furthermore, we develop a method to include the mass corrections in one-loop results and provide a closed-form expression for the mass series suitable for numerical implementations.

## Key claim
The paper computes tree-level and one-loop matching relations for leading-power gluon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), including for the first time the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation for the gluon worm-gear T distribution, but the results are limited to the Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) gauge link configuration and assume factorization as a working hypothesis rather than a proven theorem.

## Method
The authors perform an operator product expansion (OPE) of the gluon-gluon TMD correlator around zero transverse separation, using twist-decomposition within the spinor formalism to isolate leading-twist (LT) and next-to-leading twist (NLT) contributions. At tree-level, they Taylor-expand the correlator and apply projection operators to extract mass corrections for both T-even and T-odd distributions. At one-loop, they extend the parton-in-parton framework beyond the leading term in the small-b expansion, computing matching coefficients for the gluon sector and developing a method to include hadron mass corrections at one-loop accuracy.

## Key numbers
- The tree-level matching for the unpolarized gluon TMD \(f_1^g(x,b)\) is given by a series involving terms \((x^2 M^2 b^2 / 4)^k\) with \(k \ge 1\), where \(M\) is the hadron mass and \(b\) is the transverse separation.
- The Sivers function \(f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,b)\) matching involves the NLT gluon PDFs \(F_2^+\) and \(F_4^+\) evaluated at \((-x,0,x)\), with a series expansion in \((x^2 M^2 b^2 / 4)^k\) for \(k \ge 1\).
- The one-loop matching coefficients are computed for the gluon sector, providing the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation for the gluon worm-gear T distribution \(g_{1T}^g\) for the first time.
- A closed-form expression for the mass series is provided, suitable for numerical implementations.

## Context
This work extends the matching relations for gluon TMDs, which were previously known only at tree-level for mass corrections (Moos, 2020; Rodini, 2023) and at three-loop order for unpolarized and helicity distributions (Ebert, 2020; Luo, 2020; Zhu, 2025), but not for the full set of gluon TMDs at one-loop. The results enable improved phenomenological analyses of gluon-sensitive processes at the LHC and future EIC, particularly for observables involving linearly polarized gluons and the gluon Sivers function, and provide a framework for systematic inclusion of higher-twist and mass corrections in TMD factorization.
