# The K/pi ratio and the lifespan of the fireball

> We construct a hadronic kinetic model for description of excitation functions of multiplicity ratios K+/pi+, K-/pi-, and Lambda/pi. It is shown that the model is able to describe data rather well under the assumption that the total lifespan of the fireball is decreasing function of the collision energy for SPS energies above 30 AGeV. Thus in order to identify the onset of deconfinement the proposed model will have to be checked against other kinds of data until hadronic description can be safely falsified.

## Key claim

A purely hadronic kinetic model can reproduce the observed "horn" in the $K^+/\pi^+$ excitation function, provided the total lifespan of the fireball is assumed to decrease with increasing collision energy for SPS energies above 30 $A$GeV. This means the horn data alone do not suffice to reject hadronic models or claim evidence for deconfinement.

## Method

The authors construct a hadronic kinetic model that parametrizes the fireball expansion in two stages: an accelerating phase with quadratic time dependence followed by a scaling phase with power-law decrease of baryon density. They calculate the time evolution of $K^+$, $K^0$, $K^{*+}$, and $K^{*0}$ densities using a master equation that includes production and annihilation reactions (associated production with hyperons, meson reactions for $K\bar K$, $K^*$ formation and decay, and baryon-baryon reactions), while treating non-strange species and $S<0$ particles under equilibrium assumptions. The model varies the total lifespan and initial energy density as free parameters to explore different evolution scenarios.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at 30 $A$GeV, the final $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio from the kinetic calculation (before resonance decay feed-down) is about a factor of 2 larger than the data value of 0.23.
- In the selected parameter sets, total lifespan $\tau_T$ values are: 25 fm/$c$ at 11.6 $A$GeV, 25 fm/$c$ at 30 $A$GeV, 20 fm/$c$ at 40 $A$GeV, 15 fm/$c$ at 80 $A$GeV, and 15 fm/$c$ at 158 $A$GeV.
- Initial energy densities $\varepsilon_0$ in the selected sets range from 1 GeV/fm$^3$ (at 11.6 $A$GeV) to 2.75 GeV/fm$^3$ (at 158 $A$GeV).
- Final state temperatures from the simulations range from 114.7 MeV (at 11.6 $A$GeV) to 153.6 MeV (at 158 $A$GeV), compared to chemical freeze-out temperatures from Becattini (2006) of 118.1 MeV to 157.8 MeV.
- At the highest SPS energy, the $\bar \Lambda/\Lambda$ multiplicity ratio is around 10%, giving an upper estimate for the error from neglecting antibaryons.

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic kinetic studies of strangeness production (Koch, Muller, and Rafelski; Knoll; Braun-Munzinger and Koch) by using a parametrized fireball evolution rather than a hydrodynamic calculation, motivated by the fact that hydrodynamic simulations do not reproduce femtoscopic fireball sizes at breakup. The model successfully reproduces the "horn" in $K^+/\pi^+$, $K^-/\pi^-$, and $\Lambda/\pi$ excitation functions that had been interpreted by some authors (using the Statistical Model of Early Phase) as evidence for deconfinement. The authors argue that to falsify hadronic descriptions, the model must be tested against other data such as single-particle spectra, correlations, total abundances, and particularly dilepton spectra, which are sensitive to the total lifespan.
