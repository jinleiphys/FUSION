# Gluons as Goldstone Bosons when Flavor Symmetry is Broken Spontaneously

> The mechanism where flavor symmetry is broken spontaneously is discussed within a scalar QCD model with effective three-meson couplings. For sufficiently large coupling the model is unstable with respect to quantum loops from mesonic vacuum polarization. It is argued that color and gluons naturally can account for the Goldstone degrees of freedom expected when flavor symmetry is spontaneously broken.

# Gluons as Goldstone Bosons when Flavor Symmetry is Broken Spontaneously

## Key claim

For sufficiently large three-meson couplings, the flavor-symmetric solution to the self-consistency equations becomes unstable and a stable flavor-asymmetric solution emerges; the Goldstone degrees of freedom expected from spontaneous flavor symmetry breaking can be identified with the longitudinal and/or scalar confined gluons of QCD, provided the color gauge group remains unbroken.

## Method

The author constructs a scalar QCD model with two scalar nonets of opposite charge conjugation, coupled through F-type and D-type trilinear meson interactions, and computes the one-loop mesonic vacuum polarization contributions to the inverse propagator. The self-consistency condition requires that the pole masses appearing in the loop functions equal the masses determined by the zeros of the inverse propagator; the stability of the symmetric solution is analyzed by examining the response to small mass variations, leading to an inequality involving the coupling strength and derivatives of the loop function F.

## Key numbers

- The instability condition is given by \( N_f (g_F^2 + g_D^2)/(4\pi) > [\partial F/\partial s + \partial F/\partial m_1^2]^{-1} \) evaluated at \( s = m_1^2 = m_2^2 \), where the left-hand side is positive for any reasonable function F.
- For the unitarity-cut model with S-wave threshold behavior and \( \Lambda \to \infty \), the bound approaches 7.94, with \( \partial F/\partial s|_{s=m_1=m_2} = 1/\pi - 2/\sqrt{27} \) and \( \partial F/\partial m_1^2|_{s=m_1=m_2} = 1/\sqrt{27} \).
- For P-wave threshold behavior (as in \( \rho \to \pi\pi \)), the bound is stricter by more than an order of magnitude.
- The physical couplings \( g_{\rho\pi\pi} \) and \( g_{\sigma\pi\pi} \) (with \( m_\sigma \approx \Gamma_\sigma \approx 0.5 \) GeV) lie well above the instability bound for all realistic cutoffs \( \Lambda \).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier preprints (Tornqvist, 1996) on spontaneous flavor symmetry breaking via quantum effects from trilinear meson couplings, where no explicit quark mass splitting term is needed in the Lagrangian. The mechanism contrasts with the conventional approach (Leutwyler, 1994) where flavor symmetry is broken by adding non-degenerate quark masses by hand. The paper suggests that the spontaneous breaking mechanism, when applied to an SU6 model involving pseudoscalar and vector nonets, can produce reasonable \( \pi \)-\( K \)-\( \eta \)-\( \eta' \) and \( \rho \)-\( \omega \)-\( K^* \)-\( \phi \) splittings determined only by the \( \pi \) and \( \rho \) masses, and opens the possibility of understanding the hadron mass spectrum with three nearly massless and three massive bare quarks.
