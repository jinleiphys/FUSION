# Nucleon-Nucleon Partial Wave Analysis to 2.5 GeV

> A partial-wave analysis of NN elastic scattering data has been completed. This analysis covers an expanded energy range, from threshold to a laboratory kinetic energy of 2.5 GeV, in order to include recent elastic pp scattering data produced by the EDDA collaboration at COSY. The results of both single-energy and energy-dependent analyses are described.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Partial Wave Analysis to 2.5 GeV

## Key claim

The partial-wave analysis extends elastic pp scattering fits to 2.5 GeV laboratory kinetic energy and finds smooth, structureless amplitudes above 1.6 GeV, with no evidence for the dibaryon resonance conjectured near 2.7 GeV center-of-mass energy; however, the authors note the high-energy region was constrained mainly by cross section data, so the solution should be considered a guide to expected amplitudes rather than definitive.

## Method

Single-energy and energy-dependent partial-wave analyses were performed on NN elastic scattering data from threshold to 2.5 GeV laboratory kinetic energy, using a modified K-matrix parameterization scheme to address degeneracy issues in the basis functions at higher energies. The database was expanded to include recent EDDA collaboration differential cross sections from COSY (540–2520 MeV, 35°–90° cm angle), along with reexamined higher-energy data from ANL (450 points) and Saclay (about 900 points). The np database was not significantly increased, so the I=0 analysis was not extended.

## Key numbers

- Energy range extended from 1.6 GeV (previous analysis) to 2.5 GeV laboratory kinetic energy
- EDDA data contributed 2121 differential cross section points from 540 MeV to 2520 MeV proton kinetic energy
- COSY data gave χ²/datum of 1.07 from SM97 fit and 5.6 from NM97 fit (without COSY data)
- Net χ² increased by 3498 when 2121 COSY data were added to NM97 and re-analyzed to produce SM97
- Total pp database: 14,873 data points (0–2520 MeV range); total np database: 10,854 data points (0–2000 MeV range)
- SM97 fit to pp data (0–2520 MeV): χ² = 26,460 over 14,873 data points
- SM97 fit to np data (0–2000 MeV): χ² = 17,440 over 10,854 data points
- A hypothetical ¹S₀ resonance structure was tested at W_r = 2.7 GeV with elastic width Γ_e = 16 MeV and inelastic width Γ_i = 67 MeV

## Context

This work updates the previous SM94 partial-wave analysis by Arndt et al. (1994) which covered up to 1.6 GeV, extending the range to include EDDA collaboration data from COSY. The analysis addresses claims of a broad dibaryon resonance near 2.7 GeV center-of-mass energy suggested by Hoshizaki (1990s) and a sharp structure reported in the polarization observable A_yy, but finds no evidence for resonant behavior in the unpolarized cross sections. The results enable future comparisons with polarization measurements expected from Saturne II and COSY, and provide a baseline amplitude set for searches for localized structures in spin-dependent observables.
