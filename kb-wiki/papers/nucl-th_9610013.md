# QCD Sum Rule for $Λ$(1405)

> Motivated by the recently constructed interpolation field for S$_{11}$(1535), we propose a new interpolating field for $Λ$(1405). Using this current, we calculate the mass of $Λ$(1405) based on the conventional QCD sum rule analysis. By calculating the Wilson coefficients up to dimension 8 operators and taking into account the mass corrections from s-quark, we find the calculated mass of $Λ$(1405) to be very close to its experimental value.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a newly constructed interpolating field for the Lambda(1405) resonance, designed to match its quark configuration in the bag model or nonrelativistic quark model, yields a QCD sum rule mass prediction of 1.42 GeV, which agrees with the experimental value of 1.405 GeV within 2 percent. The hedge is that this agreement depends on choosing the parameter t = -2.781 and the quark-gluon condensate parameter lambda_q^2 = 0.435 GeV^2, values previously optimized for the S_11(1535) resonance.

## Method

The authors construct an interpolating field for Lambda(1405) by applying a covariant derivative (z * D) to one quark field in the standard Lambda interpolating field, placing one quark in a p-wave and two quarks in s-wave states as suggested by the bag model and nonrelativistic quark model. They calculate the two-point correlation function in the operator product expansion up to dimension 8 operators, including s-quark mass corrections, and perform a conventional QCD sum rule analysis with Borel transformation. The parameter t in the interpolating field is optimized by requiring zero coupling to the positive-parity ground state Lambda(1115), following the method of Lee and Kim (1996) and Jido, Kodama, and Oka (1996).

## Key numbers

- Calculated mass of Lambda(1405): M_Lambda* = 1.42 GeV, compared to experimental value 1.405 GeV (agreement within 2 percent)
- Optimal mixing parameter: t = -2.781
- Quark condensate: <q_bar q> = -(0.23 GeV)^3
- Gluon condensate: <(alpha_s/pi) G^2> = (0.35 GeV)^4
- s-quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- Quark-gluon condensate parameter: lambda_q^2 = 0.435 GeV^2 (taken from previous S_11 work)
- Continuum thresholds: s_+ = (1.6)^2 GeV^2 (positive parity), s_- = (1.67)^2 GeV^2 (negative parity)

## Context

This work extends the method of Lee and Kim (1996) for constructing interpolating fields for negative-parity excited baryons, previously applied to the S_11(1535) nucleon resonance, to the strange baryon sector. The approach contrasts with standard QCD sum rule analyses that use Ioffe currents obtained via SU(3) transformation from the nucleon current. The resulting interpolating field is expected to enable future studies of other spectral properties of Lambda(1405), such as its coupling strengths and decay properties.
