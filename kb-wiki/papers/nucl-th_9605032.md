# Virtual Compton Scattering from the Proton and the Properties of Nucleon Excited States

> We calculate the $N^*$ contributions to the generalized polarizabilities of the proton in virtual Compton scattering. The following nucleon excitations are included: $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1520)$, $N^*(1700)$, $螖(1232)$, $螖^*(1620)$ and $螖^*(1700)$. The relationship between nucleon structure parameters, $N^*$ properties and the generalized polarizabilities of the proton is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the contributions of seven nucleon and Delta excited states to the ten generalized polarizabilities (GP's) of the proton in virtual Compton scattering, using the Non-Relativistic Quark Model (NRQM) as a guide. The authors note that the NRQM estimate should be a useful guide to the analysis of soon available experimental data, but that different models (such as bag models) may not give exactly the same properties for the nucleon and its excitations.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic tensor for virtual Compton scattering in the low-energy regime (below pion production threshold) using the NRQM with wave functions from Isgur and Karl (1978). They separate the hadronic tensor into a leading term and a recoil term, where the recoil corrections contribute only when the final photon is magnetic. The generalized polarizabilities are then obtained by partial wave projection of the hadronic tensor, with the final expressions given analytically for all ten independent GP's.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass parameter: \( m_q = 350 \) MeV
- Harmonic oscillator size parameter: \( \alpha = 320 \) MeV
- Mixing parameters for the \( ^28 \) representation of the P-wave intermediate states: \( a_X = 0.85 \) for \( N^*(1535) \), \( -0.53 \) for \( N^*(1650) \), \( 0.99 \) for \( N^*(1520) \), \( 0.11 \) for \( N^*(1700) \), \( 1.0 \) for \( \Delta^*(1620) \), and \( 1.0 \) for \( \Delta^*(1700) \)
- The \( ^48 \) component of the excited state wavefunctions contributes nothing to the proton GP's because of the isospin factor \( (1-2\tau) \), but does contribute in the neutron case

## Context

This work extends the initial evaluation of the generalized polarizabilities by Guichon et al. (1995), which neglected all recoil effects and found only 7 non-zero GP's. The present calculation includes recoil corrections and studies the sensitivity of the GP's to the mass spectrum of the nucleon excited states and the hadron size parameter \( \alpha \). The results are intended to guide the analysis of upcoming experimental data from CEBAF and MAMI (Audit et al., 1993) on the \( p(e,e'p)\gamma \) reaction.
