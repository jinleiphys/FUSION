# Role of the tensor force in induced fission of 240Pu

> Results: We find that the tensor force affects the height of fission barriers and the double-humped structure of fission path of 240Pu. On the PES, the fission valley becomes larger in the (Q20, Q30) plane, and the triaxial deformation is suppressed around the outer barrier after considering the tensor force. Incorporating the tensor force into the dynamical process enhances the difference in shape evolution between two asymmetric channels. More interestingly, the charge distribution from TDHF with double PNP calculations shows a strong odd-even effect after including the tensor force. In addition, the total mass and charge distributions of fission fragments show a slight shift towards larger asymmetry and are more consistent with the experiments when tensor components are included. We also find that the tensor force enhances energy gaps of the deformed shells for heavy fragments. Moreover, the TKEs of fragments are in accord with the experiments, and after incorporating the tensor force, we observe a higher concentration of the calculated TKEs for heavy fragments at Z = 52 and Z = 56. Conclusions: The tensor force plays a role in both static and dynamical processes in nuclear fission, improving the accuracy of theoretical descriptions within the current framework. Our calculations have shown that the fission-barrier height with SLy5t is closer to the empirical value than that of SLy5. Additionally, the inclusion of the tensor force not only manifests the odd-even effect in charge distribution but also improves the description of total mass and charge distributions.

## Key claim

The tensor force, when included perturbatively in the Skyrme density functional SLy5t, improves the description of both static and dynamical fission of 240Pu compared to the baseline SLy5 without tensor terms, but the improvements are conditional: the fission-barrier height with SLy5t is closer to the empirical value than that of SLy5, and the charge distribution shows a strong odd-even effect only after including the tensor force.

## Method

The authors used constrained Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing (CHF+BCS) to generate two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PES) in the (Q20, Q30) plane for 240Pu, employing the Skyrme functionals SLy5 and SLy5t. They then performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) simulations with double particle number projection (PNP) to model the dynamical fission process from saddle to scission, extracting fragment mass, charge, and total kinetic energy (TKE) distributions. The tensor force effects were isolated by comparing results from SLy5 (no tensor) and SLy5t (with tensor terms added perturbatively).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy: -1784.83 MeV (SLy5) vs -1792.24 MeV (SLy5t); after center-of-mass correction, SLy5t gives -1811.02 MeV, closer to the empirical value of -1813.45 MeV from AME2020.
- Inner barrier height: SLy5 gives -1775.52 MeV at Q20 = 21.0 b; SLy5t gives -1782.47 MeV at Q20 = 19.0 b, with triaxial deformation Q22 = 2.0 b appearing only in the SLy5t case.
- Outer barrier height: SLy5 gives -1774.70 MeV at Q20 = 43.0 b, Q30 = 11.4 b3/2, Q22 = 0.8 b; SLy5t gives -1782.85 MeV at Q20 = 39.0 b, Q30 = 9.1 b3/2, Q22 = 0.7 b.
- Energy difference between SLy5 and SLy5t beyond the fission barrier: about 2.5 MeV when Q20 > 67 b.
- Triaxial deformation around the outer barrier: Q22 = 0.9 b for SLy5, reduced to 0.7 b for SLy5t.
- The TKEs of fragments are in accord with experiments, with a higher concentration of calculated TKEs for heavy fragments at Z = 52 and Z = 56 after incorporating the tensor force.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of induced fission of 240Pu using SLy5 without tensor forces (Huang 2024) and is the first to incorporate tensor force effects into TDHF with double PNP for dynamical fission calculations. It builds on static HFB studies by Bernard (2020) that showed tensor terms change barrier heights and PES topology for thorium isotopes, and on earlier TDHF fission studies by Scamps (2015, 2018), Bulgac (2016, 2019, 2021), and others that did not include tensor forces. The results enable more accurate microscopic descriptions of fission fragment distributions and suggest that tensor force effects are essential for reproducing experimental charge odd-even staggering and fragment asymmetry.
