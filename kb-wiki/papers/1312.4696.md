# Nuclear matter response function with a central plus tensor Landau interaction

> We present a method to obtain response functions in the random phase approximation (RPA) based on a residual interaction described in terms of Landau parameters with central plus tensor contributions. The response functions keep the explicit momentum dependence of the RPA, in contrast with the traditional Landau approximation. Results for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are presented using Landau parameters derived from finite-range interactions, both phenomenological and microscopic. We study the convergence of response functions as the number of Landau parameters is increased.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to calculate RPA response functions using a residual interaction expanded in Landau parameters that include both central and tensor contributions, keeping the full momentum dependence rather than taking the traditional long-wavelength (Landau) limit. The authors show that the Landau approximation is only valid for small transferred momenta (approximately \(k = q/(2k_F) \lesssim 0.1\)), and that convergence of the RPA response with increasing number of Landau parameters is generally achieved at \(\ell_{max}=2\) except in the \(S=1, M=0, I=0\) channel where tensor terms cause slower convergence.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole propagator using a residual interaction expanded in Legendre polynomials with Landau parameters for central (\(f_\ell, f'_\ell, g_\ell, g'_\ell\)) and tensor (\(h_\ell, h'_\ell\)) channels, truncating at \(\ell_{max}=3\). They obtain the RPA response function by numerically solving a coupled system of momentum-averaged equations derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using Landau parameters extracted from two phenomenological finite-range interactions (Gogny D1MT and M3Y-P2) and two microscopic calculations (correlated basis function with Argonne V18 and chiral effective field theory).

## Key numbers

- Density used for all calculations: \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) (symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter)
- For Gogny D1MT in SNM, the dimensionless Landau parameters at \(\ell=0\) are: \(F_0 = -0.310\), \(F'_0 = 0.724\), \(G_0 = -0.037\), \(G'_0 = 0.731\), \(H_0 = 0.306\), \(H'_0 = -0.102\)
- For M3Y-P2 in SNM at \(\ell=0\): \(F_0 = -0.383\), \(F'_0 = 0.620\), \(G_0 = 0.112\), \(G'_0 = 1.008\), \(H_0 = 0.043\), \(H'_0 = -0.015\)
- For CBF in PNM at \(\ell=0\): \(F^{(n)}_0 = -0.263\), \(G^{(n)}_0 = 0.914\), \(H^{(n)}_0 = 0.071\)
- For CEFT in PNM at \(\ell=0\) (at \(k_F = 1.7\) fm\(^{-1}\)): \(F^{(n)}_0 = 0.554\), \(G^{(n)}_0 = 0.837\), \(H^{(n)}_0 = 0.191\)
- The Landau approximation is nearly identical to RPA at \(k = 0.1\) for \(\nu\) below the peak, but at \(k = 0.5\) there is significant redistribution of strength
- Convergence of RPA response is achieved at \(\ell_{max}=2\) for most channels, except the \(S=1, M=0, I=0\) channel with D1MT

## Context

This work extends the method previously developed by Garrido et al. (1992), Margueron et al. (2006), Davesne et al. (2009), and Navarro et al. (2013) to obtain RPA response functions, now including tensor contributions in the Landau parameter expansion. The approach provides a simpler alternative to full Skyrme-based RPA calculations (Davesne et al., 2009; Pastore et al., 2012) and enables systematic studies of neutrino transport in dense matter, where the momentum dependence beyond the Landau limit is important. The results establish quantitative limits on the validity of the Landau approximation used in earlier work by Iwamoto and Pethick (1982) and Benhar et al. (2013) for neutrino mean-free path calculations.
