# Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory for neutron drops

> Neutron drops confined in an external field are studied in the framework of relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The ground state energies and radii of neutron drops with even numbers from $N = 4$ to $N=50$ are calculated and compared with results obtained from other nonrelativistic \textit{ab initio} calculations and from relativistic density functional theory. Special attention has been paid to the magic numbers and to the sub-shell closures. The single-particle energies are investigated and the monopole effect of the tensor force on the evolutions of the spin-orbit and the pseudospin-orbit splittings is discussed. The results provide interesting insight of neutron rich systems and can form an important guide for future density functionals.

## Key claim

Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory, using only the bare two-nucleon interaction without three-nucleon forces, reproduces neutron drop ground-state energies and radii that are comparable to nonrelativistic *ab initio* calculations that do include three-nucleon interactions, but the results depend significantly on the choice of the bare interaction and the external trap parameters.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Bethe-Goldstone equation for neutron drops confined in a spherical harmonic oscillator trap with strength ℏω = 10 MeV, using the Bonn A, B, and C one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon interactions. The self-consistent single-particle basis is obtained by iterating the relativistic Hartree-Fock equation with the G-matrix, using a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis in a box of 8 fm with 0.05 fm mesh, and an energy cut-off of 900 MeV. Only the isospin channel T_z = 1 is included, and the filling approximation is used for open shells.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of N=8 neutron drop: 130.0 MeV (Bonn A), 130.2 MeV (Bonn B), 130.3 MeV (Bonn C) in a HO trap with ℏω = 10 MeV
- Total energy of N=20 neutron drop: 418.1 MeV (Bonn A), 419.3 MeV (Bonn B), 419.7 MeV (Bonn C)
- Total energy of N=28 neutron drop: 663.9 MeV (Bonn A), 666.3 MeV (Bonn B), 666.9 MeV (Bonn C)
- RMS radius of N=8 neutron drop: 2.61 fm (Bonn A, B, C)
- RMS radius of N=20 neutron drop: 3.02 fm (Bonn A), 3.03 fm (Bonn B, C)
- RMS radius of N=28 neutron drop: 3.19 fm (Bonn A), 3.20 fm (Bonn B, C)
- RMS radius of N=50 neutron drop with Bonn A: 3.61 fm
- Gap between 1f5/2 (or 2p1/2) and 2p3/2 single-particle states in N=34 drop: 3.047 MeV (Bonn A), compared to 0.401 MeV (NL3) to 2.127 MeV (DD-ME2) for relativistic density functionals
- Convergence achieved at energy cut-off ε_cut = 900 MeV, faster than the 1100 MeV needed for finite nuclei

## Context

This work extends the RBHF framework, previously applied to finite nuclei by Shen et al. (2016, 2017), to the neutron drop model which was first studied by quantum Monte Carlo methods by Pudliner et al. (1996) and later systematically by Gandolfi et al. (2011), Maris et al. (2013), and Tews et al. (2016). The results are compared with nonrelativistic *ab initio* calculations (QMC, NCSM, coupled-cluster) using interactions such as AV8', AV8'+UIX, AV8'+IL7, chiral 2N+3N forces, and JISP16, as well as with relativistic density functionals (NL3, PK1, DD-ME2, PKDD, PC-PK1, PKO1). The work provides microscopic reference data that can guide the improvement of relativistic density functionals for neutron-rich systems, particularly regarding the isovector channel and the tensor-force effects on shell structure.
