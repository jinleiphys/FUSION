# Effects of strong magnetic fields on neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluidity with spin-orbit interactions

> We discuss neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ phases in the core of neutron stars in strong magnetic field (magnetars). The neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ pairing provides a wide variety of condensates, such as the uniaxial nematic and (D$_{2}$ and D$_{4}$) biaxial nematic, with different symmetries stemming from the combinations of spin and momentum. Based on the spin-orbital angular momentum coupling and the spin-magnetic field coupling of the neutrons, we derive the Ginzburg-Landau equation containing higher order terms of the magnetic field. We investigate the phase diagram of the neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluidity, and find that the D$_{2}$ biaxial nematic phase is extended by the higher order terms of the magnetic field. We also discuss the thermodynamic properties, the heat capacity and the spin susceptibility.

## Key claim

The authors find that including higher-order terms of the magnetic field in the Ginzburg-Landau equation extends the region of the D₂ biaxial nematic phase in the temperature-magnetic field phase diagram of neutron ³P₂ superfluidity, but note that the magnetic field strength in magnetars (γₙB/(πT_c0) ≈ 0.02) is sufficient to cover all three nematic phases (UN, D₂-BN, and D₄-BN).

## Method

Starting from the LS potential for neutron-neutron interactions in the ³P₂ channel, the authors derive a Ginzburg-Landau free energy density using bosonization, one-loop approximation, and quasi-classical approximation for momentum integrals. They include terms up to O(B⁴A²) and O(B²A⁴) in the magnetic field expansion, which were omitted in prior work. The condensate is parametrized in diagonal form as A = A₀ diag(r, -1-r, 1) with A₀ ≥ 0 and -1 ≤ r ≤ -1/2, and the phase diagram is obtained by minimizing the free energy as a function of temperature T and magnetic field B.

## Key numbers

- Neutron mass: m = 939 MeV
- Nuclear matter density: n = 0.17 fm⁻³ (Fermi momentum p_F = 338 MeV)
- Critical temperature: T_c0 = 0.2 MeV
- Gyromagnetic ratio of neutron: γₙ = 1.2 × 10⁻¹³ MeV/T (in natural units ℏ = c = 1)
- Landau parameter: F₀ᵃ = -0.75
- Magnetar surface magnetic field: ≈ 10¹⁵ G (or 10¹¹ T)
- Magnetar field strength parameter: γₙB/(πT_c0) ≈ 0.02

## Context

This work extends prior Ginzburg-Landau treatments of neutron ³P₂ superfluidity (Masuda 2015, Masuda 2016) that included only the lowest-order magnetic field terms, by incorporating higher-order magnetic field contributions. The results connect to studies of topological properties in neutron ³P₂ phases (Masuda 2015, Masuda 2016, Mizushima 2016, Chatterjee 2016) and provide thermodynamic quantities (heat capacity and spin susceptibility) that could be used to probe the internal structure of magnetars. Future work could examine competition with neutron ¹S₀ superfluidity, hyperon matter, and quark matter phases.
