# Double-$Λ$ hypernuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory

> We study the properties of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory, which has been successfully used for the description of stable and unstable nuclei. With the meson-hyperon couplings determined by the experimental binding energies of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, we present a self-consistent calculation of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory, and discuss the influence of hyperons on the nuclear core. The contribution of two mesons with dominant strange quark components (scalar $σ^*$ and vector $ϕ$) to the $ΛΛ$ binding energy of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei is examined.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, with meson-hyperon couplings fixed by single-Λ hypernuclei data, can reproduce the weak ΛΛ interaction (ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 1 MeV) suggested by the Nagara event for ⁶_ΛΛHe, but only if the center-of-mass correction is treated microscopically; the contributions from the strange mesons σ* and φ largely cancel, making model 2 (with those mesons) almost identical to model 1 (without them) for double-Λ hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent RMF calculations for spherical double-Λ hypernuclei across a wide mass range, using two nucleonic parameter sets (TM1 and NL-SH). For the Λ hyperon, the ω coupling was fixed at the quark-model value R_ω = 2/3, the σ coupling R_σ = 0.621 was fitted to experimental single-Λ binding energies, and the tensor coupling f^Λ_ω = -g^Λ_ω was included. In model 2, the φ coupling was set to the quark-model value R_φ = -√2/3, while the σ* coupling R_σ* was adjusted to reproduce the Nagara-event bond energy ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 1 MeV for ⁶_ΛΛHe. The center-of-mass correction was treated microscopically using the expectation value of the total momentum operator.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ binding energy B_ΛΛ(⁶_ΛΛHe) = 5.52 MeV (model 1, TM1) and 7.25 ± 0.19^{+0.18}_{-0.11} MeV (experimental Nagara event).
- ΛΛ bond energy ΔB_ΛΛ(⁶_ΛΛHe) = 1.08 MeV (model 1, NL-SH, microscopic c.m. correction) and 0.73 MeV (same model, simple A^{-1/3} c.m. approximation).
- Rearrangement energy E_R for ⁶_ΛΛHe = 3.54 MeV (TM1, model 1), compared to B_ΛΛ = 5.52 MeV; for ⁴²_ΛΛCa, E_R = 0.29 MeV compared to B_ΛΛ = 38.15 MeV.
- Center-of-mass correction for ⁶_ΛΛHe = 10.25 MeV (microscopic, NL-SH, model 1) versus 16.92 MeV (simple A^{-1/3} approximation).
- For R_σ* ≈ 0.75, the extra binding from σ* and φ in model 2 is about 4 MeV for ⁶_ΛΛHe but less than 1 MeV for ²¹⁰_ΛΛPb.
- Maximum neutron star mass with R_σ* = 0.57 (weak ΛΛ) is about 0.1 M_⊙ larger than with R_σ* = 0.75 (strong ΛΛ).

## Context

This work extends RMF studies of single-Λ hypernuclei (e.g., earlier works by the same group and others using TM1 and NL-SH parameter sets) to double-Λ systems, addressing the weak ΛΛ interaction implied by the Nagara event (Takahashi et al., 2001). It contrasts with earlier cluster-model and Faddeev calculations for light hypernuclei, and with the earlier strong ΛΛ interaction (ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 5 MeV) deduced from older emulsion data. The results enable more reliable predictions for multi-strange systems and neutron-star equations of state, showing that the weak ΛΛ interaction leads to a slightly stiffer EOS than the strong one.
