# Nonmesonic weak decay of charmed hypernuclei

> We present a study of the nonmesonic weak decay (NMWD) of charmed hypernuclei using a relativistic formalism. We work within the framework of the independent particle shell model and employ a ($π$,$K$) one-meson-exchange model for the decay dynamics. We implement a fully relativistic treatment of nuclear recoil. Numerical results are obtained for the one-neutron-induced transition NMWD rates of the $_{Λ^{+}_{c}}^{12}$N. The effect of nuclear recoil is sizable and goes in the direction to decrease the nuclear decay rate. We found that the NMWD decay rate of $_{Λ^{+}_{c}}^{12}$N is of the same order of magnitude as the partial decay rate for the corresponding mesonic decay $Λ^+_c \rightarrow Λ+ π^+$, suggesting the feasibility of experimental detection of such heavy-flavor nuclear processes.

## Key claim

The nonmesonic weak decay (NMWD) rate of the charmed hypernucleus \(^{12}_{\Lambda^{+}_{c}}\mathrm{N}\) is of the same order of magnitude as the partial decay rate for the corresponding mesonic decay \(\Lambda^{+}_c \rightarrow \Lambda + \pi^{+}\), suggesting experimental detection of such heavy-flavor nuclear processes may be feasible, but only when a fully relativistic treatment of nuclear recoil is included, which sizably decreases the decay rate.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic independent-particle shell model with Dirac single-particle wave functions obtained from mean-field potentials (NL3 Lagrangian for nucleons, SU(4) symmetry for \(\Lambda^{+}_c\) couplings). The decay dynamics was described by a one-meson-exchange model including pion and kaon exchanges, with soft monopole form factors (cutoffs \(\Lambda_\pi = 1.3\) GeV, \(\Lambda_K = 1.2\) GeV). Numerical calculations were performed for the one-neutron-induced transition \(\Lambda^{+}_c + n \rightarrow \Lambda + p\) in the specific charmed hypernucleus \(^{12}_{\Lambda^{+}_{c}}\mathrm{N}\), with the \(\Lambda^{+}_c\) in the \(1s_{1/2}\) state and the neutron hole in the \(1p_{3/2}\) state of the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) core.

## Key numbers

* The free \(\Lambda^{+}_c\) lifetime is \(\tau_{\Lambda^{+}_{c}} = (2.00 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-13}\) s, corresponding to a total decay rate \(\Gamma(\Lambda^{+}_c \rightarrow \mathrm{all}) = (3.29 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-9}\) MeV.
* The partial width for the mesonic decay \(\Lambda^{+}_c \rightarrow \Lambda + \pi^{+}\) is \(\Gamma(\Lambda^{+}_c \rightarrow \Lambda + \pi) = (4.28 \pm 0.26) \times 10^{-11}\) MeV, with a branching ratio of 1.07%.
* The energy liberated in the charmed hyperon decay is \(\Delta_c = M_{\Lambda^{+}_c} - M_{\Lambda} = 1.170\) GeV, compared to \(\Delta_s = M_{\Lambda} - M_N = 177\) MeV for strange hyperons.
* The strong coupling constants used are \(g_{NN\pi} = 13.3\) and \(g_{N\Lambda K} = -14.1\); the weak parity-conserving and parity-violating amplitudes are \(A_\pi = -1.56\), \(B_\pi = 6.63\) (from CLEO collaboration), and \(A_K = -0.95\), \(B_K = 9.17\) (from theoretical predictions), all in units of \(G_F m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism previously developed by Fontoura et al. (2015) for the NMWD of \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei to the case of charmed \(\Lambda^{+}_c\) hypernuclei. It builds on earlier suggestions by Bunyatov et al. (1992) that \(\Lambda^{+}_c\) hypernuclei may decay nonmesonically, and on decades of theoretical work on charmed nuclear bound states by Tsushima et al. (2002, 2003), Shyam et al. (2016), and others. The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like FAIR and JPARC, where charmed hypernuclei might be produced, and motivate further studies of SU(4) flavor symmetry breaking effects on the meson-\(\Lambda^{+}_c\) couplings.
