# Density and temperature dependence of nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section

> The in-medium neutron-proton, proton-proton (neutron-neutron) elastic scattering cross sections ($σ_{np}^{*}$, $σ_{pp(nn)}^{*}$) are studied based on the effective Lagrangian of density dependent relativistic hadron theory in which the $δ$[$a_0(980)$] meson is included. Our study shows that at low densities the $σ_{np}^*$ is about 3-4 times larger than $σ_{pp(nn)}^*$ and at densities higher than the normal density the isospin effect is almost washed out. Because of coupling to $δ$ meson the $σ_{nn}^*$ and $σ_{pp}^*$ are different in isospin asymmetric medium following the splitting of the proton and neutron mass. The isospin effect on the density dependence of the in-medium nucleon elastic cross section is dominantly contributed by the isovector $δ$ and $ρ$ mesons. The temperature effect on the $σ_{np}^*$ and $σ_{pp(nn)}^*$ is studied. It is shown that the temperature effect is weaker compared with the density effect but it becomes obvious as density increases.

## Key claim

The in-medium neutron-proton elastic cross section is about 3-4 times larger than the proton-proton (or neutron-neutron) cross section at low densities, but this isospin effect is almost washed out at densities at or above normal nuclear density; the temperature effect on these cross sections is weaker than the density effect but becomes more noticeable as density increases.

## Method

The authors calculated in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections using an effective Lagrangian from density-dependent relativistic hadron theory that includes the scalar-isovector delta meson [a0(980)] in addition to the sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They solved self-consistently for the effective proton and neutron masses in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, then computed the cross sections from the meson-exchange diagrams including the delta meson contributions and crossing terms, using a form factor at each nucleon-meson-nucleon vertex.

## Key numbers

- At low densities, the ratio sigma*_np / sigma*_pp(nn) is about 3-4, decreasing to approximately 1 at normal density (rho_0 = 0.18 fm^-3) and above.
- The cut-off masses used in the form factors are: Lambda_sigma = 1200 MeV, Lambda_omega = 808 MeV, Lambda_delta = 1000 MeV, Lambda_rho = 800 MeV.
- The meson masses used are: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_delta = 983 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV.
- The saturation properties of the model are: binding energy E/A = 16 MeV, incompressibility K = 282 MeV, effective mass ratio M*/M_0 = 0.554, symmetry energy a_sym = 26.1 MeV.
- Temperature effects were studied at T = 0, 50, 100, and 150 MeV for a proton fraction Y_p = 0.3 at kinetic energy E_K = 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Li et al. (2000) based on the QHD-II model (which included only the rho meson for isovector channels) by adding the scalar-isovector delta meson, motivated by studies of asymmetric nuclear matter by Kubis (1997) and Hofmann et al. (2001). The results differ significantly from the earlier QHD-II calculations, where sigma*_np depended weakly on density; the inclusion of the delta meson produces a strong density dependence for both sigma*_np and sigma*_pp(nn). This work enables more accurate transport model simulations of neutron-rich nuclear collisions at intermediate energies, particularly for rare-isotope beams at facilities like NSCL/MSU and future RIA and SIS-200.
