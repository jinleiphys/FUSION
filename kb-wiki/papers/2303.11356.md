# Strongly interacting matter exhibits deconfined behavior in massive neutron stars

> Neutron-star cores contain matter at the highest densities in our Universe. This highly compressed matter may undergo a phase transition where nuclear matter melts into deconfined quark matter, liberating its constituent quarks and gluons. Quark matter exhibits an approximate conformal symmetry, predicting a specific form for its equation of state (EoS), but it is currently unknown whether the transition takes place inside at least some physical neutron stars. Here, we quantify this likelihood by combining information from astrophysical observations and theoretical calculations. Using Bayesian inference, we demonstrate that in the cores of maximally massive stars, the EoS is consistent with quark matter. We do this by establishing approximate conformal symmetry restoration with high credence at the highest densities probed and demonstrating that the number of active degrees of freedom is consistent with deconfined matter. The remaining likelihood is observed to correspond to EoSs exhibiting phase-transition-like behavior, treated as arbitrarily rapid crossovers in our framework.

## Key claim

The authors use Bayesian inference to show that the equation of state in the cores of maximally massive neutron stars is consistent with deconfined quark matter with approximately 88% posterior probability (or 75% using a more conservative Gaussian process method), but they explicitly note that the remaining likelihood corresponds to equations of state exhibiting phase-transition-like behavior, treated as arbitrarily rapid crossovers in their framework.

## Method

The analysis combines a low-density equation of state from Chiral Effective Field Theory (CEFT) up to approximately 1.2 times nuclear saturation density with a high-density perturbative QCD constraint, connected by either a parametric four-segment speed-of-sound interpolation or a non-parametric Gaussian process regression. The prior ensemble of equations of state is conditioned using a likelihood function incorporating 12 simultaneous neutron-star mass-radius measurements, including mass measurements of the heaviest pulsars, tidal deformability constraints from GW170817, and X-ray observations from NICER and other collaborations, with Bayesian inference performed via Markov-Chain-Monte-Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- The posterior probability for conformalized matter (defined by the criterion d_c < 0.2) in maximally massive stars is approximately 88% using the four-segment speed-of-sound interpolation and 75% using the Gaussian process regression
- The corresponding probabilities for 2 solar mass and 1.4 solar mass neutron stars are only 11% and 0%, respectively
- The normalized pressure p/p_free in the cores of maximally massive neutron stars is 0.40 ± 0.03 with 68% credence, corresponding to an effective number of degrees of freedom N_eff = 3.6 ± 0.3
- Using the less conservative criterion gamma < 1.75 from earlier work, the posterior probability of conformalization in maximally massive stars would be 99.8% for the speed-of-sound interpolation and 97.8% for the Gaussian process ensemble
- The conformality criterion threshold is set at d_c = 0.2, which is below the lower bound for first-order phase transitions at 1/(3*sqrt(2)) ≈ 0.236

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analyses (Annala 2017, Annala 2019, Annala 2021) by generalizing to a Bayesian framework that simultaneously incorporates 12 neutron-star mass-radius measurements and high-density constraints from perturbative QCD, which earlier studies by Komoltsev (2021) and Gorda (2022) showed significantly constrain the neutron-star-matter equation of state down to realistic core densities. The results enable quantitative statements about the likelihood of a hadron-to-quark-matter transition within stable neutron stars, with the authors noting that just as in heavy-ion collisions where multiple lines of evidence established the production of deconfined quark-gluon plasma, additional observables beyond the equation of state would be desirable to compellingly establish the existence of quark matter in neutron-star cores.
