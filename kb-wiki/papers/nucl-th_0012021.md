# Relativistic Effects in Electroweak Nuclear Responses

> The electroweak response functions for inclusive electron scattering are calculated in the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, both in the quasi-elastic and in the $Δ$ peak regions. The impact of relativistic kinematics at high momentum transfer is investigated through an expansion in the initial nucleonic momentum, which is however exact in the four-momentum of the exchanged boson. The same expansion is applied to the meson exchange currents in the particle-hole sector: it is shown that the non-relativistic currents can be corrected by simple kinematical factors to account for relativity. The left-right asymmetry measured via polarized electron scattering is finally evaluated in the quasi-elastic and $Δ$ peaks.

# Relativistic Effects in Electroweak Nuclear Responses

## Key claim
The Relativistic Fermi Gas model provides a consistent framework for calculating electroweak response functions in both the quasi-elastic and Delta resonance regions, and a new non-relativistic expansion of nuclear currents to first order in the bound nucleon momentum yields an excellent approximation to the exact relativistic results for any value of energy and momentum transfer, provided the full dependence on the exchanged boson four-momentum is retained.

## Method
The electroweak response functions for inclusive electron scattering were calculated analytically within the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, treating the nucleus as a collection of free nucleons described by positive-energy Dirac spinors. A new non-relativistic expansion was developed by expanding the single-nucleon electromagnetic current and the N-to-Delta transition current in powers of the parameter eta = p/m_N (the bound nucleon momentum over the nucleon mass), truncating at linear order while retaining the exact dependence on the dimensionless momentum transfer kappa and energy transfer lambda. The same expansion was applied to meson exchange currents (pion-in-flight and seagull currents) in the particle-hole sector.

## Key numbers
- The asymmetry for inclusive electron scattering is roughly of order 10^{-5}, with absolute value growing with |Q^2|.
- The normalization factor A_0 in the asymmetry formula is approximately 3.1 x 10^{-4} times tau (where tau = kappa^2 - lambda^2).
- The Delta longitudinal response is about 15% of the corresponding quasi-elastic response at q = 1 GeV/c in the absence of a Coulomb form factor, and the Coulomb form factor can enhance this ratio by almost a factor of 2.
- The asymmetry in the Delta resonance region at q = 350 MeV/c and scattering angle theta = 10 degrees is increased by about 10% due to the axial N-to-Delta response.
- The Fermi momentum for carbon-12 is given as k_F = 225 MeV/c.
- The expansion parameter eta_F (Fermi momentum over nucleon mass) is typically about 1/4.
- The new expansion was tested at q = 2 GeV/c for carbon-12, where the approximate results are nearly indistinguishable from the exact relativistic calculation.

## Context
This work extends the Relativistic Fermi Gas model calculations of electroweak response functions previously developed by the author and collaborators (Barbaro, 2000; Amaro, 1998) to include a systematic treatment of relativistic effects through a new expansion method. The approach enables the inclusion of relativistic effects in standard non-relativistic models through simple kinematical factors, addressing the need for relativistic treatments at high momentum transfer where non-relativistic calculations become inadequate. The results enable future measurements of the left-right asymmetry in polarized electron scattering to extract information about the N-to-Delta transition form factors and the axial response, particularly at momentum transfers around 300-400 MeV/c and 1 GeV/c, where the asymmetry shows a dramatic increase when transitioning from the quasi-elastic peak to the Delta region at small electron scattering angles.
