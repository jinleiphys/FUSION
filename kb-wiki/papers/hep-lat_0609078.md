# Spin-dependent potentials from lattice QCD

> The spin-dependent corrections to the static inter-quark potential are phenomenologically relevant to describing the fine and hyperfine spin splitting of the heavy quarkonium spectra. We investigate these corrections, which are represented as the field strength correlators on the quark-antiquark source, in SU(3) lattice gauge theory. We use the Polyakov loop correlation function as the quark-antiquark source, and by employing the multi-level algorithm, we obtain remarkably clean signals for these corrections up to intermediate distances of around 0.6 fm. Our observation suggests several new features of the corrections.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the multi-level algorithm applied to Polyakov loop correlation functions yields remarkably clean signals for spin-dependent potentials up to intermediate distances of around 0.6 fm, whereas previous lattice studies suffered from large numerical errors that obscured the behavior of these potentials already at intermediate distances.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-dependent corrections to the static inter-quark potential in SU(3) lattice gauge theory using the quenched approximation, employing the Polyakov loop correlation function as the quark-antiquark source. They use the multi-level algorithm to evaluate the field strength correlators on the lattice, then fit these correlators to their spectral representation to extract the matrix elements and energy gaps, which are inserted into the expressions for the spin-dependent potentials V1'(r), V2'(r), V3(r), and V4(r). The Huntley-Michael procedure is applied to renormalize the field strength operators on the lattice.

## Key numbers

* At β=6.0 (a ≈ 0.093 fm), the static potential V0(rI) was computed on 16⁴, 20⁴, and 20³×40 lattices for distances r/a = 2–9 (2–6 for spin-dependent potentials), with 90, 82, and 33 configurations respectively.
* At β=6.3 (a ≈ 0.059 fm) on a 24⁴ lattice, the static potential was computed for distances r/a = 2–8 (2–6 for spin-dependent potentials) with 39 configurations.
* The physical lattice spacing was fixed using the Sommer scale with r0 = 0.5 fm.
* The optimal sublattice size a × Ntsl was empirically found to be 0.3–0.4 fm for suppressing statistical fluctuations.
* At β=6.0 on the 20³×40 lattice with Ntsl = 4 (Nsub = 8), the memory requirement was about 2 GB (49 work space units, where 1 wsu = 42 MB).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice determinations of spin-dependent potentials from the 1980s and 1990s by deForcrand (1985), Michael (1985), Huntley (1986), Campostrini (1986, 1987), Ford (1988), Koike (1989), Born (1993), and Bali (1996, 1997), which suffered from large numerical errors at intermediate distances. The authors employ the multi-level algorithm of Luscher (2001, 2002) and the Polyakov loop correlation function to avoid the systematic errors associated with the Wilson loop approach used in previous studies. The results enable more accurate phenomenological applications to the fine and hyperfine splitting of heavy quarkonium spectra, and the authors plan to address the matching coefficient in future studies.
