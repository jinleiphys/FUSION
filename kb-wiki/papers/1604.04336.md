# $ϕ$-meson production at forward/backward rapidity in high-energy nuclear collisions from a multiphase transport model

> Within the framework of a multiphase transport model (AMPT), the $ϕ$-meson production is studied in d+Au collisions at \srt = {200} GeV in the forward (d-going, $1.2<y<2.2$) and backward (Au-going, $-2.2<y<-1.2$) direction. The AMPT model with string melting version (parton cascade turning-on) describes the experimental data well, while the pure hadronic transport scenario of the AMPT model underestimates the $ϕ$-meson production rate in comparison with the data. Detailed investigations including the rapidity, transverse momentum and collision system size dependencies of $ϕ$-meson nuclear modification factor indicate that a combination of the initial state effect and a follow-up parton cascade is required in the AMPT model to describe the data. Similar calculations are also present in p+Pb collisions at \srt = {5.02} TeV and p+p collisions at \srt = {2.76} TeV. The findings from a comparison of AMPT model study with the data are consistent with that at RHIC energy.

# φ-meson production at forward/backward rapidity in high-energy nuclear collisions from a multiphase transport model

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting (parton cascade turned on) describes φ-meson production data in d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, but only in the forward (d-going or p-going) direction; in the backward (Au-going or Pb-going) direction the model systematically underpredicts the yield at high transverse momentum (pT > 1.5–1.7 GeV/c), possibly due to small current quark masses used in the model.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (versions 1.26 default and 2.26 string melting) with a parton scattering cross section of 1.5 mb, QCD coupling αs = 0.33, screening mass μ = 3.2 fm⁻¹, and Lund string fragmentation parameters α = 0.5, b = 0.9 GeV⁻². They simulated φ-meson production in d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (forward rapidity 1.2 < y < 2.2, backward rapidity -2.2 < y < -1.2), p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, and p+p collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, comparing invariant yields and nuclear modification factors RdAu and RAA with PHENIX and ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- In d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the default AMPT model underestimates φ-meson production by about 40% compared to the string melting version and experimental data in the d-going direction.
- The ratio of φ-meson invariant yield between the string melting version and default AMPT version is close to 0.6 and independent of pT within statistical uncertainty in d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- In d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the string melting AMPT describes data well up to pT = 1.5 GeV/c in the Au-going direction, then underestimates at higher pT.
- In p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the string melting AMPT describes data up to pT = 1.7 GeV/c in the Pb-going direction, then systematically underpredicts at higher pT.
- The nuclear modification factor RdAu for φ mesons in d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV is consistent with unity at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.35), enhanced in the Au-going direction, and suppressed in the d-going direction.
- In Au+Au and Cu+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the φ-meson RAA increases at low pT and starts to decrease at pT > 1.2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT model studies of identified particle production in d+Au collisions at mid-rapidity by Lin and Ko (2002), where final state interactions dominated the pT spectra of pions, kaons, and protons. The study addresses the limited theoretical understanding of φ-meson suppression at forward rapidity observed by PHENIX (2014) in d+Au collisions and by ALICE (2014) in p+Pb collisions. The findings enable separation of cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear shadowing, Cronin enhancement, initial-state parton energy loss) from hot matter effects in small collision systems, and show that a combination of initial state effects and parton cascade is required to describe the data.
