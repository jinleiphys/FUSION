# Description of eta-distributions at RHIC energies in terms of the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process and relativistic diffusion equations

> Assuming the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process and relativistic diffusion equations with three sources at y = 0, we analyze eta-distributions of d+Au collision as well as Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130 GeV and 200 GeV.

## Key claim
The Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process combined with relativistic diffusion equations, using three sources at y=0, can describe eta-distributions in both d+Au and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, but the model requires the ratio of weight factors for projectile, target, and central sources to be determined by specific criteria on the mass-to-transverse-momentum parameters.

## Method
The authors model particle production using relativistic diffusion equations for projectile and target fragmentation sources (with drift toward y=0) and an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process for the central gluon source. They fit the resulting three-component formula, including Jacobian transformations between rapidity and pseudorapidity, to PHOBOS Collaboration data on dn/deta for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 and 200 GeV (centrality cuts 6-15% and 15-25%) and for d+Au collisions at 200 GeV, using the CERN MINUIT program to determine weight factors and evolution parameters.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at 130 GeV, centrality 6-15%: evolution parameter p=0.52±0.21, central variance V0^2=5.17±0.95, target variance VT^2=1.31±0.26, chi^2/d.o.f.=0.64/48
- For Au+Au at 130 GeV, centrality 15-25%: p=0.62±0.13, V0^2=5.98±1.03, VT^2=1.34±0.30, chi^2/d.o.f.=0.82/48
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, centrality 6-15%: p=0.78±0.12, V0^2=5.64±1.42, VT^2=1.43±0.25, chi^2/d.o.f.=0.50/48
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, centrality 15-25%: p=0.66±0.16, V0^2=6.06±1.67, VT^2=1.59±0.28, chi^2/d.o.f.=0.54/48
- For Au+Au, the ratio of weight factors is c_P : c_0 : c_T = 1 : 6-7 : 1 at minimum chi^2
- For d+Au at 200 GeV: p=0.76, V0^2=4.27±0.64, VP^2=0.165±0.159, VT^2=7.56±1.38, c_0=39.8±5.4, c_P=0.529±0.077, chi^2/d.o.f.=5.1/45
- For d+Au, the weight factor ratio is c_P : c_0 : c_T = 0.53 : 40 : 53, with c_T/c_P fixed at 100 based on the mass ratio m_d : m_Au = 1 : 100

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous two-source model (Biyajima 2002, 2003) by adding a third source at y=0 and using relativistic diffusion equations for the fragmentation sources instead of simple Gaussian forms. The model successfully describes PHOBOS data for both Au+Au and d+Au collisions, and the authors note that the analytical form can be applied to other systems such as Si+Al and S+Au collisions in future work.
