# Three-body model for the complete fusion of a two-cluster composite projectile with a heavy target

> The purpose of this work is to show that within a three-body description, the complete fusion process and the target excitation by the projectile can be taken into account by introducing a three-body optical potential in the formalism. We give a schematic description of such a potential and points to ways of testing the validity of the CDCC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent three-body description of complete fusion for a two-cluster projectile requires introducing a three-body optical potential, which is missing in standard three-body models that only use two-body optical potentials; the authors provide a schematic form for this potential but do not calculate it, leaving its evaluation as a challenge.

## Method

The authors formulate the fusion of a projectile \(a = b + x\) with a target \(A\) using a Feshbach projection operator framework within a three-body model. They derive expressions for incomplete fusion cross sections in terms of the imaginary parts of the two-body optical potentials \(W_{xA}\) and \(W_{bA}\), and propose a schematic three-body optical potential \(U_{Axb}\) to account for complete fusion. The complete fusion cross section is then expressed as proportional to the matrix element of the three-body imaginary potential \(W_{3B}\) with the full three-body wave function.

## Key numbers

- The incomplete fusion cross section for participant \(x\) is given by \(\sigma_{IF}^{(x)} = \frac{\pi}{k^2} \sum_{l_x} (2l_x+1) \langle T_x(l_x)(1-T_b(l_b)) \rangle\) in the Glauber limit.
- The incomplete fusion cross section is proportional to \(\int \langle \Psi^{(+)} | \chi_b^{(-)} \rangle (\vec{r}_x) W_{xA} (\vec{r}_x) \langle \chi_b^{(-)} | \Psi^{(+)} \rangle d\vec{r}_x\) (Eq. sigmaIF_1) and similarly for the other cluster.
- The complete fusion cross section is proportional to \(\langle \Psi^{(+)} | W_{3B} | \Psi^{(+)} \rangle\) (Eq. sigmaIF_3), where \(W_{3B}\) is the imaginary part of the three-body optical potential.

## Context

This work extends the three-body formalism for incomplete fusion developed by Austern (1987), Ichimura (1990), Hussein and McVoy (1985), and Udagawa and Tamura (1986), which did not address complete fusion. It also builds on the earlier work of Carlson (1998) on incomplete fusion. The proposed three-body optical potential is intended to enable a consistent calculation of complete fusion and to test the validity of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) model, which the authors note consistently fails to account for incomplete fusion as shown by Diaz-Torres (2002).
