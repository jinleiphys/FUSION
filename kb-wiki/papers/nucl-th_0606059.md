# Collective modes in relativistic npe matter at finite temperature

> Isospin and density waves in neutral neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter are studied within a relativistic mean-field hadron model at finite temperature with the inclusion of the electromagnetic field. The dispersion relation is calculated and the collective modes are obtained. The unstable modes are discussed and the spinodals, which separate the stable from the unstable regions, are shown for different values of the momentum transfer at various temperatures. The critical temperatures are compared with the ones obtained in a system without electrons. The largest critical temperature, 12.39 MeV, occurs for a proton fraction y_p=0.47. For y_p=0.3 we get $T_{cr}$ =5 MeV and for y_p>0.495 $T_cr\lesssim 8$ MeV. It is shown that at finite temperature the distillation effect in asymmetric matter is not so efficient and that electron effects are particularly important for small momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the dynamical instability region of neutral npe matter persists up to higher temperatures than predicted by thermodynamic calculations, with a maximum critical temperature of 12.39 MeV at proton fraction y_p=0.47, but only when electrons are included dynamically rather than through a screening approximation; the critical temperature and the efficiency of the distillation effect depend strongly on the momentum transfer k and on whether electrons are treated as an independent fluctuating component.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Vlasov equation for a system of protons, neutrons, and electrons interacting through scalar-isoscalar (sigma), vector-isoscalar (omega), vector-isovector (rho), and electromagnetic fields within the NL3 parametrization of the relativistic mean-field model. They compute the dispersion relation for longitudinal collective modes by solving a 5x5 matrix determinant, identifying unstable modes (imaginary frequencies) and mapping the spinodal boundaries as functions of baryon density, proton fraction, temperature, and momentum transfer k. The analysis compares results with and without electrons, and for different values of k from 5 MeV to 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum critical temperature T_cr = 12.39 MeV occurs at proton fraction y_p = 0.47 for momentum transfer k = 75 MeV.
- For y_p = 0.3, the critical temperature is T_cr = 5 MeV (at k = 10 MeV).
- For y_p > 0.495, the critical temperature is T_cr less than or approximately 8 MeV (for k > 180 MeV).
- The saturation density rho_0 for the NL3 parametrization is 0.148 fm^{-3}.
- At T = 0 MeV, the most unstable mode occurs at k = 145 MeV, corresponding to a modulation wavelength lambda approximately 9 fm.
- For T = 10 MeV, unstable modes exist only for k between approximately 30 MeV and 150 MeV.
- For k = 8 MeV, the critical proton fraction can be as low as y_p = 0.22 with T_cr approximately 4.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature dynamical instability analysis of npe matter by Brito, Providencia, Santos, Avancini, Menezes, and Chomaz (2005) to finite temperature, and contrasts with thermodynamic spinodal calculations by the same group (2004) which found that the instability region disappeared above 2.4 MeV. The results are relevant for understanding nuclear pasta phases in neutron star crusts and supernova matter, and connect to quantum molecular dynamics simulations by Watanabe et al. (2004) which gave critical temperatures of approximately 6 MeV for y_p=0.5 and approximately 5 MeV for y_p=0.3. The findings enable improved modeling of neutrino mean free paths in warm stellar matter, particularly for the low-energy collective modes that may revive the stalled supernova shock.
