# Radiative pion capture in nuclei: a continuum shell-model approach

> The radiative pion capture process in nuclei is approached by using a continuum shell-model description of the nucleus, together with a phenomenological treatment of the two particle-two hole effects. It is found that these effects play an important role to reproduce the observed experimental photon energy distribution. This distribution as well as the integrated one depends significantly on the details of the mean field potential. This makes this process interesting to investigate the nuclear structure dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a continuum shell-model description of radiative pion capture, combined with a phenomenological treatment of two particle-two hole (2p2h) effects, can reproduce the experimental photon energy distribution, but only when the 2p2h correlations are included; the pure one-body shell-model calculation alone fails to reproduce the low-energy tail of the spectrum.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential photon distribution for radiative pion capture from closed-shell nuclei (12C, 16O, 40Ca) using a continuum shell-model framework where final states are described as a neutron particle in the continuum coupled to a proton hole. They incorporate 2p2h correlation effects by folding the pure one-body shell-model differential rate with the imaginary part of the particle-hole propagator, using a complex self-energy taken from previous work on quasielastic electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- In 40Ca, convergence of the photon multipole sum is achieved with four or five multipoles for photon energies around the pion mass.
- The integrated ratio R(gamma) for 12C is 1.79% (continuum 0.62% plus discrete 1.17%), compared to experimental values of 1.84 +/- 0.08%, 1.92 +/- 0.19%, and 1.6 +/- 0.1%.
- For 16O, the total integrated ratio R(gamma) is 1.72% (continuum 0.92% plus discrete 0.80%), versus experimental values of 2.27 +/- 0.24% and 2.24 +/- 0.48%.
- For 40Ca, the total integrated ratio R(gamma) is 2.04% (continuum 1.17% plus discrete 0.87%), compared to the experimental value of 1.82 +/- 0.05%.
- In 16O, the area under the differential rate curve with 2p2h correlations is 9.2 x 10^-3, while without correlations it is 9.8 x 10^-3, showing less than 10% change in the integrated rate.
- The Gaussian convolution width used to smooth resonance structures in oxygen and calcium is 1.5 MeV.
- The shell-model gives integrated RPC rates with an uncertainty of about 20-25%, based on variations of the Woods-Saxon potential parameters.

## Context

This work extends the continuum shell-model approach previously developed for quasielastic electron scattering (Amaro 1993, 1994) to the radiative pion capture process. It contrasts with the Fermi gas model of Chiang (1990), which could not reproduce the low-energy tail of the photon distribution due to limited momentum components, and with earlier shell-model calculations by Vergados (1975), Eramzhyam (1977), Ohtsuka and Ohtsubo (1978), and Wunsch (1980) that used different potentials for particles and holes, losing orthogonality. The approach enables future investigation of nuclear structure dynamics through the sensitivity of the photon distribution to the mean field potential and 2p2h correlations.
