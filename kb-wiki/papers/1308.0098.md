# Probing gluon dynamics by charm and bottom mesons in nuclear medium from heavy meson effective theory with 1/M-corrections

> We consider heavy mesons with charm and bottom in nuclear medium. We construct the effective Lagrangian with axial-vector coupling including 1/M-corrections for the heavy meson mass $M$ by following the velocity-rearrangement invariance. As an application, we consider heavy mesons, $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ and $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{\ast}$ mesons for charm and $\mathrm{B}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{\ast}$ mesons for bottom, bound in nuclear matter, and we discuss their in-medium masses modified by the interaction with nucleons via pion exchanges including the 1/M-corrections. The mass modifications are affected by the gluon dynamics in nuclear medium. By comparison with the heavy quark effective theory, we find that the effects of scale anomaly become suppressed in nuclear medium. We also find that the contributions from the chromoelectric gluon are enhanced in nuclear medium, while those from the chromomagnetic gluon are reduced. We propose to use heavy mesons as probes to research the gluon fields in nuclear medium in experimental studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium masses of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{(\ast)}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{(\ast)}$ mesons in nuclear matter, computed via heavy meson effective theory with $1/M$-corrections up to $\mathcal{O}(1/M^1)$, reveal that the effects of scale anomaly become suppressed while chromoelectric gluon contributions are enhanced and chromomagnetic gluon contributions are reduced, but this conclusion depends on the specific hadronic model used to evaluate the matrix elements and the correspondence between the $1/M$-expansion and the $1/m_{\mathrm{Q}}$-expansion in heavy quark effective theory.

## Method

The authors constructed the heavy meson effective Lagrangian for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{(\ast)}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{(\ast)}$ mesons interacting with nucleons via pion exchanges, including axial-vector coupling with $1/M$-corrections derived from velocity-rearrangement invariance. They then applied this Lagrangian to calculate the in-medium masses of these mesons in nuclear matter at zero temperature, and extracted the medium-modified matrix elements $\bar{\Lambda}(\rho)$, $\lambda_{1}(\rho)$, and $\lambda_{2}(\rho;m_{\mathrm{Q}})$ by fitting the computed masses to the heavy quark effective theory mass formula expanded in $1/m_{\mathrm{Q}}$.

## Key numbers

- The mass formula for a heavy hadron H containing a heavy quark Q is $M_{\mathrm{H}} = m_{\mathrm{Q}} + \bar{\Lambda} - \frac{\lambda_{1}}{2m_{\mathrm{Q}}} + 4\vec{S}_{\mathrm{Q}} \!\cdot\! \vec{S}_{\mathrm{L}} \frac{\lambda_{2}(m_{\mathrm{Q}})}{2m_{\mathrm{Q}}} + \mathcal{O}(1/m_{\mathrm{Q}}^2)$, where $\bar{\Lambda}$, $\lambda_{1}$, and $\lambda_{2}(m_{\mathrm{Q}})$ are matrix elements related to the scale anomaly, chromoelectric gluon field, and chromomagnetic gluon field, respectively.
- The heavy meson effective theory uses the averaged mass $M = (M_{\mathrm{P}} + 3M_{\mathrm{P}^{\ast}})/4$ for the pseudoscalar P meson and vector P$^{\ast}$ meson, with the correspondence $1/M = 1/m_{\mathrm{Q}} + \mathcal{O}(1/m_{\mathrm{Q}}^2)$.
- The ratios $\bar{\Lambda}(\rho)/\bar{\Lambda}$, $\lambda_{1}(\rho)/\lambda_{1}$, and $\lambda_{2}(\rho;m_{\mathrm{Q}})/\lambda_{2}(m_{\mathrm{Q}})$ quantify the medium modifications of the scale anomaly and chromoelectric and chromomagnetic gluon fields, respectively, but no numerical values for these ratios are given in the text.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\bar{\mathrm{D}}^{(\ast)}$ and $\mathrm{B}^{(\ast)}$ mesons in nuclear matter by Yasui and Sudoh (2012, 2013) and others using quark-meson coupling models (Tsushima 1998, 2002), QCD sum rules (Hilger 2008, 2010), mean-field methods (Mishra 2003, 2008; Kumar 2010, 2011), and coupled-channel approaches (Lutz 2005; Tolos 2007; Garcia-Recio 2011), which did not fully include all $\mathcal{O}(1/m_{\mathrm{Q}})$ terms. The paper enables future experimental studies using heavy mesons as probes of gluon dynamics in nuclear medium, and the formalism can be applied to baryons containing a heavy quark as well as to nonuniform medium and few-body systems.
