# The QCD Trace Anomaly at Strong Coupling from M-Theory

> In this short note we address the issue of obtaining the temperature dependence of the QCD conformal anomaly from a top-down holographic dual consistent with very recent lattice results for both, T<Tc and T>Tc. As the holographic dual, we use the M-theory uplift of the SYZ type IIA mirror obtained in arXiv:1306.4339 at finite gauge/string coupling (as part of the 'MQGP' limit) of the type IIB holographic dual of large-N thermal QCD of arXiv:0902.1540. We also show that after a tuning of the (small) Ouyang embedding parameter and radius of a blown-up S^2 when expressed in terms of the horizon radius, a QCD deconfinement temperature Tc = 150 MeV from a Hawking-Page phase transition at vanishing baryon chemical potential, consistent with lattice QCD in the heavy-quark limit, can be obtained.

## Key claim
The authors claim to obtain the temperature dependence of the QCD trace anomaly from a top-down M-theory holographic dual that is consistent with recent lattice results for both T < Tc and T > Tc, but this agreement is achieved only after tuning the Ouyang embedding parameter and the blown-up S^2 radius expressed in terms of the horizon radius, and is justified in the heavy-quark limit.

## Method
The authors use the M-theory uplift of the SYZ type IIA mirror (obtained in the MQGP limit) of the type IIB holographic dual of large-N thermal QCD constructed by Dasgupta et al. (2009). They compute the QCD trace anomaly from the D=11 supergravity action including O(R^4) higher derivative corrections, evaluating the free energy and its temperature derivative for both the high-temperature (black hole) and low-temperature (thermal) backgrounds. The deconfinement temperature Tc = 150 MeV is obtained from a Hawking-Page phase transition at vanishing baryon chemical potential, with the glueball mass from lattice QCD used to fix the IR cutoff.

## Key numbers
- Tc = 150 MeV from a Hawking-Page phase transition at zero baryon chemical potential, consistent with lattice QCD in the heavy-quark limit, obtained after tuning the Ouyang embedding parameter and blown-up S^2 radius with parameters alpha = 0.6 and delta = 1.008.
- The lightest 0++ scalar glueball mass is approximately 1,700 MeV from lattice calculations, used to fix the IR cutoff r0 via m_{0++} ≈ 4 r0 / L^2.
- The horizon radius r_h scales as exp[-1/(3(6π)^(1/3)(g_s N_f)^(2/3)(g_s M^2/N)^(1/3))], implying a very small r_h and hence large |μ_Ouyang| in the MQGP limit.

## Context
This work extends the top-down holographic approach to thermal QCD initiated by Dasgupta et al. (2009) for the type IIB dual and by Misra et al. (2013) for the M-theory uplift in the MQGP limit. It fills a gap in the literature by providing a lattice-consistent computation of the QCD trace anomaly from a top-down M-theory dual valid for all temperatures, whereas previous attempts such as Nitti et al. used bottom-up holographic models to match older lattice results. The results enable further holographic studies of the QCD equation of state and transport coefficients at intermediate coupling for comparison with heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
