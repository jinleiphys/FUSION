# Eta electroproduction on nuclei in the nucleon resonance region

> We investigate eta electroproduction on nuclei for Q^2=2.4 and 3.6 GeV^2 in the framework of a coupled-channel BUU transport model. We analyze the importance of final state interactions and side feeding and compare with findings drawn from eta photoproduction. It is shown that in contrast to photoproduction the influence of etas stemming from secondary processes becomes important at high Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in eta electroproduction on nuclei at high $Q^2$ (2.4 and 3.6 GeV²), final state interactions (FSI) change from being primarily absorptive (as in photoproduction) to becoming a significant source of additional eta production through secondary processes, a qualitative difference that the abstract states but does not quantify.

## Method

The authors used a semi-classical coupled-channel BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate eta electroproduction on ⁴⁰Ca nuclei at Q² = 2.4 and 3.6 GeV². The elementary γ*N → ηN reaction was modeled via a Breit-Wigner parametrization for the S₁₁(1535) resonance, with the transversal photocoupling helicity amplitude A_T determined from fits to exclusive data from Armstrong et al. (2003). Final state interactions were treated using the BUU equation with 29 nucleon resonances from Manley and Saleski (1992), and for high-energy collisions (invariant masses above 2 GeV) the string model Fritiof was used to determine final-state particle content.

## Key numbers

- Q² values studied: 2.4 and 3.6 GeV², with corresponding electron beam energies of 3.2 GeV and 4 GeV respectively, matching the kinematics of Armstrong et al. (2003)
- The S₁₁(1535) resonance pole mass used: μ = 1.535 GeV
- For photoproduction (Q² = 0), the S₁₁ resonance can be excited by a real photon with energy E_γ ≈ 0.787 GeV
- The momentum transfer at the S₁₁ resonance peak is q_γ ≈ 2.6 GeV for Q² = 2.4 GeV² and q_γ ≈ 3.3 GeV for Q² = 3.6 GeV²
- For the F₁₅(1680) resonance, helicity amplitudes a factor of ~2 larger than those in Stoler (1993) were needed to saturate the inclusive data from Brasse et al. (1976)
- For the neutron sector, the relation σ_n = 2/3 σ_p was used for the γ*n → S₁₁ channel, following Krusche and the authors' prior photoproduction work

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel BUU calculations of eta photoproduction on nuclei (Lehr and Mosel, 2003) to the electroproduction case at higher Q², using the same model framework as their earlier study of pion electroproduction at low Q² ≤ 0.8 GeV² (Lehr et al., 2003). The results show that at high Q², the interpretation of eta production in terms of in-medium S₁₁(1535) properties becomes more complicated because secondary eta production channels in the FSI become important, a finding that parallels similar side-feeding effects seen in high-energy photo production of K⁺ (Effenberger et al., 1999) and ρ mesons (Falter et al., 2003) on nuclei.
