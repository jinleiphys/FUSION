# On the Efimov Effect in Higher Partial Waves

> Using the framework of effective field theory, we present a detailed study of the Efimov effect in higher partial waves for systems of two identical particles and a third distinguishable particle. Depending on the total angular momentum $L$, the two identical particles must be bosons or fermions. We derive analytical expressions for the elastic and inelastic atom-dimer scattering cross sections as well as the atom-dimer relaxation rate at the dimer breakup threshold. For the experimentally most relevant case of P-waves, we numerically calculate the atom-dimer scattering cross sections and relaxation rates as a function of the scattering length, three-body parameter, and mass ratio for energies away from the breakup threshold.

## Key claim

The Efimov effect can occur in higher partial waves for heteronuclear systems of two identical particles and a third distinguishable particle, but only when the mass ratio exceeds a critical value that depends on the angular momentum; for the experimentally relevant P-wave case with fermionic identical particles, the critical mass ratio is $\delta^{-1}_{c,1}=13.61$, and the effect only appears when the two identical particles are fermions for odd angular momentum channels.

## Method

Using an effective field theory framework with a Lagrangian describing resonant S-wave interactions between unlike particles and negligible interactions between like particles, the authors derived a generalized Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation for the off-shell atom-dimer scattering amplitude projected onto total angular momentum $L$. They solved this equation analytically at the dimer breakup threshold and numerically for energies below threshold, focusing on the P-wave case, to obtain elastic and inelastic atom-dimer scattering cross sections and relaxation rates as functions of scattering length, three-body parameter, and mass ratio.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass ratio for P-wave Efimov effect: $\delta^{-1}_{c,1}=13.61$
- Critical mass ratio for D-wave Efimov effect: $\delta^{-1}_{c,2}=38.63$
- Mass ratios for possible experimental mixtures showing P-wave Efimov effect: $^6$Li-$^{87}$Sr = 14.5, $^6$Li-$^{137}$Ba = 22.8, $^6$Li-$^{167}$Er = 27.8, $^6$Li-$^{171}$Yb = 28.5, $^6$Li-$^{173}$Yb = 28.8, $^7$Li-$^{137}$Ba = 19.6, $^7$Li-$^{167}$Er = 23.9, $^7$Li-$^{171}$Yb = 24.4, $^7$Li-$^{173}$Yb = 24.7
- For the S-wave case in a K-Rb mixture, the experimental value $a_*/|a_-|=2.7$ was measured by Barontini et al. (2009), while the universal prediction for this system is $a_*/|a_-|=0.52$
- For fermions in the P-wave channel without Efimov effect, one three-body bound state exists for $8.17260<\delta^{-1}<12.91743$ and two three-body bound states for $12.91743<\delta^{-1}<13.6069657$, as found by Kartavtsev and Malykh (2007)
- The coefficient $c_1(\delta,k)$ approaches $-8.7\pm 0.2$ for $k\rightarrow 0$ for all considered mass ratios

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the Efimov effect, originally predicted by Efimov (1970) for identical bosons in S-waves, to higher partial waves in heteronuclear systems. It builds on the effective field theory framework developed by Helfrich and Hammer (2010) for S-wave heteronuclear systems and generalizes the scaling factor calculations of Nielsen et al. (2001) and the critical mass ratio determinations of Petrov (2003) and Kartavtsev and Malykh (2007). The results enable experimental searches for the P-wave Efimov effect in Bose-Fermi mixtures such as lithium-ytterbium or lithium-strontium combinations, where the mass ratios exceed the critical value of 13.61, and provide predictions for atom-dimer relaxation rates that could be measured in cold atom experiments.
