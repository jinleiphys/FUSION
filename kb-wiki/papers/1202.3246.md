# Nonlinear statistical effects in relativistic mean field theory

> We investigate the relativistic mean field theory of nuclear matter at finite temperature and baryon density taking into account of nonlinear statistical effects, characterized by power-law quantum distributions. The analysis is performed by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number and electric charge fraction. We show that such nonlinear statistical effects play a crucial role in the equation of state and in the formation of mixed phase also for small deviations from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (characterized by the Tsallis nonextensive parameter \(q\) close to 1) significantly modify the relativistic mean field equation of state for nuclear matter and the hadron-quark mixed phase, but the authors treat \(q\) as a free parameter that in principle should depend on physical conditions such as interaction length and collision frequency.

## Method

The authors apply Tsallis' nonextensive statistical mechanics to a relativistic mean field model of hadronic matter (with nucleons interacting via \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields) and to a MIT bag model for quark-gluon matter. They impose Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number and electric charge fraction to construct the hadron-quark mixed phase, solving the field equations self-consistently with \(q\)-deformed fermion and boson distributions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density used as reference: \(\rho_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Vacuum baryon mass: \(M = 939\) MeV.
- Bag constant set to \(B^{1/4} = 190\) MeV.
- Critical phase transition temperature from lattice calculations cited as \(T_c \approx 170\) MeV, with critical energy density \(\epsilon_c \approx 1\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- For \(q > 1\), both the first and second critical baryon densities of the mixed phase are "sensibly reduced" relative to the \(q=1\) case (exact numerical reductions not given in the text).
- At \(T = 90\) MeV, pressure as a function of baryon density is stiffer in the pure hadronic phase for \(q > 1\) but shows a strong softening in the mixed phase.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nonextensive statistics in high-energy physics (Tsallis, 1988; Bediaga, 2000; Beck, 2000; Rafelski, 2001; Wilk, 2000; Biro, 2005) and applies them to relativistic mean field theory (Walecka, 1974; Boguta, 1977; Glendenning, 1985) and the MIT bag model (Chodos, 1974). It builds on earlier investigations of nonextensive effects on the hadronic equation of state (Silva, 2009) and enables future work to connect the nonextensive parameter \(q\) to microscopic quantities such as mean interparticle interaction length, screening length, and collision frequency in the parton plasma.
