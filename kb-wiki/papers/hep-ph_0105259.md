# Perturbative QCD and factorization of coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron

> We analyze the predictions of perturbative QCD for pion photoproduction on the deuteron, gamma D -> pi^0 D, at large momentum transfer using the reduced amplitude formalism. The cluster decomposition of the deuteron wave function at small binding only allows the nuclear coherent process to proceed if each nucleon absorbs an equal fraction of the overall momentum transfer. Furthermore, each nucleon must scatter while remaining close to its mass shell. Thus the nuclear photoproduction amplitude, M_{gamma D -> pi^0 D}(u,t), factorizes as a product of three factors: (1) the nucleon photoproduction amplitude, M_{gamma N_1 -> pi^0 N_1}(u/4,t/4), at half of the overall momentum transfer, (2) a nucleon form factor, F_{N_2}(t/4), at half the overall momentum transfer, and (3) the reduced deuteron form factor, f_d(t), which according to perturbative QCD, has the same monopole falloff as a meson form factor. A comparison with the recent JLAB data for gamma D -> pi^0 D of Meekins et al. [Phys. Rev. C 60, 052201 (1999)] and the available gamma p -> pi^0 p data shows good agreement between the perturbative QCD prediction and experiment over a large range of momentum transfers and center of mass angles. The reduced amplitude prediction is consistent with the constituent counting rule, p^11_T M_{gamma D -> pi^0 D} -> F(theta_cm), at large momentum transfer. This is found to be consistent with measurements for photon lab energies E_gamma > 3 GeV at theta_cm=90 degrees and $\elab > 10$ GeV at 136 degrees.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD predictions for coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron, using a new reduced amplitude factorization that includes nonperturbative effects in the pion production process itself, show good qualitative agreement with JLAB data without needing to invoke anomalous contributions such as odderon exchange, though the normalization constant C' is fixed by the highest-energy data point and the factorization is expected to be less accurate at lower energies.

## Method

The authors applied the cluster decomposition theorem for the deuteron wave function at small binding to factorize the nuclear photoproduction amplitude M_{gamma D -> pi^0 D}(u,t) into a product of three factors: the nucleon photoproduction amplitude at half the overall momentum transfer, a nucleon form factor at half the overall momentum transfer, and the reduced deuteron form factor f_d(t). They then compared the predicted invariant amplitudes, computed from available gamma p -> pi^0 p data using the factorization formula, with the JLAB gamma D -> pi^0 D data of Meekins et al. (1999) at center-of-mass angles of 90 degrees and 136 degrees.

## Key numbers

- The normalization constant C' in the factorization formula was fixed by the data point at photon lab energy E_lab = 4 GeV at theta_cm = 90 degrees, yielding C' approximately 0.8.
- The reduced deuteron form factor was parameterized as f_d(t) approximately 2.14 / [1 - t/(0.28 GeV^2)], as determined from analyses of elastic deuteron form factors by Arnold.
- The nucleon form factor was taken as [1 - t/(0.71 GeV^2)]^{-2}.
- The virtuality of the struck-quark propagator at E_lab = 4 GeV is approximately |t|/3 approximately 2 GeV^2.
- The constituent counting rule predicts p^{11}_T M_{gamma D -> pi^0 D} -> F(theta_cm) at large momentum transfer, consistent with measurements for photon lab energies E_lab > 3 GeV at theta_cm = 90 degrees and E_lab > 10 GeV at 136 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the reduced amplitude formalism previously applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering by Brodsky and Ji (1986) and to deuteron photodisintegration by Brodsky and Hiller (1983), improving upon the older reduced nuclear amplitude factorization by including nonperturbative effects in the pion production process itself. The analysis was motivated by JLAB data from Meekins et al. (1999) that appeared inconsistent with both constituent-counting rules and the older reduced amplitude approach, and the new factorization resolves this discrepancy without invoking odderon exchange as proposed by Ginzburg, Ivanov, and Serbo (1993). The results enable further tests of perturbative QCD in exclusive nuclear processes at higher energies and suggest the need for additional measurements on both proton and deuteron targets.
