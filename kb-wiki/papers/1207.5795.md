# Instabilities of an anisotropically expanding non-Abelian plasma: 3D+3V discretized hard-loop simulations

> We study the (3+1)-dimensional evolution of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in the presence of a longitudinally expanding background of hard particles using the discretized hard loop framework. The free streaming background dynamically generates a momentum-space anisotropic distribution which is unstable to the rapid growth of chromomagnetic and chromoelectric fields. These fields produce longitudinal pressure that works to isotropize the system. Extrapolating our results to energies probed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions we find, however, that a pressure anisotropy persists for a few fm/c. In addition, on time scales relevant to heavy-ion collisions we observe continued growth of plasma instabilities in the strongly non-Abelian regime. Finally, we find that the longitudinal energy spectrum is well-described by a Boltzmann distribution with increasing temperature at intermediate time scales.

# Instabilities of an anisotropically expanding non-Abelian plasma: 3D+3V discretized hard-loop simulations

## Key claim

The chromo-Weibel instability in a longitudinally expanding non-Abelian plasma does **not** saturate at the soft scale on timescales relevant for heavy-ion collisions (a few fm/c), and the longitudinal energy spectrum becomes Boltzmann-distributed rather than exhibiting a power-law Kolmogorov-type spectrum, but the authors caution that the back-reaction of hard particles on the soft background field is not included and may slow isotropization at late times.

## Method

The authors performed 3D+3V real-time simulations of the hard-loop (HL) equations of motion for an SU(2) gauge theory on a lattice with transverse spacing $a$, longitudinal spacing $a_\eta$, and temporal spacing $\epsilon$, using a leapfrog algorithm. The hard particles were approximated as a longitudinally free-streaming ensemble that dynamically generates a momentum-space anisotropic distribution; initial seed fields were provided by transverse current fluctuations in the auxiliary $\overline{\mathcal W}$ fields with a longitudinal mode cutoff $\Lambda_\nu$, rather than by initial gauge field fluctuations. The simulations used a velocity-space lattice of size $N_u \times N_\phi = 128 \times 32$ and a spatial lattice of $N_\perp = 40$ sites in the transverse directions and $N_\eta = 128$ sites in the rapidity direction.

## Key numbers

- Canonical lattice parameters: $N_T = 40$, $N_\eta = 128$, $a_\eta = 0.025$, $a = Q_s^{-1}$, $\tau_0 = Q_s^{-1}$; transverse infrared cutoff $k_{\rm min} = 0.157 \, Q_s$, transverse ultraviolet cutoff $k_{\rm max} = \pi Q_s$, longitudinal infrared cutoff $\nu_{\rm min} = 1.96$, longitudinal ultraviolet cutoff $\nu_{\rm max} = 125.7$.
- Debye mass prescription: $m_D^2 \tau_{\rm iso}\tau_0 = 1.285$ using $c = 2\ln2$ from Kovchegov (2000) and $Q_s \tau_0 = 1$.
- For RHIC energies: $\tau_0 = Q_s^{-1} \sim 0.14$ fm/c with $Q_s \sim 1.4$ GeV; for LHC energies: $\tau_0 \sim 0.1$ fm/c with $Q_s \sim 2$ GeV.
- Extrapolated isotropization time for current fluctuation amplitude $\Delta = 0.8$: $\sim 6.5$ fm/c; for $\Delta = 1.6$ (consistent with quantum fluctuations): $\sim 5$ fm/c.
- Velocity-space convergence test used $N_u \times N_\phi = 128 \times 32$ with temporal step $\epsilon = 10^{-3}$ fm/c, spatial lattice $32^2 \times 32$, $a = 0.1$ fm, $a_\eta = 0.025$, $\tau_0 = 0.1$ fm/c, $\tau_{\rm iso} = 0.01$ fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous 1D+3V hard-loop simulations by Rebhan (2008) and the semianalytic stability analysis of Romatschke (2006) and Rebhan (2009) to a full 3D+3V treatment of the expanding non-Abelian plasma. The finding of no soft-scale saturation is consistent with fixed-anisotropy analyses by Bodeker (2007) and Arnold (2007). The observed exponential longitudinal energy spectrum at early times is not in contradiction with the pure Yang-Mills simulations of Fukushima (2011), which found a power-law spectrum only at much later times ($\tau \gtrsim 150$ fm/c). The results enable future studies using classical Yang-Mills and Boltzmann-Vlasov simulations that include the back-reaction of hard particles, and suggest that longitudinal thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma may occur on timescales of order 1 fm/c.
