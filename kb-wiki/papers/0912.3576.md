# Nucleon Properties from Approximating Chiral Quark Sigma Model

> We apply the approximating chiral quark model. This chiral quark model is based on an effective Lagrangian which the interactions between quarks via sigma and pions mesons. The field equations have been solved in the mean field approximation for the hedgehog baryon state. Good results are obtained for nucleon properties in comparison with original model.

## Key claim

The approximating chiral quark sigma model, which includes an additional gradient term (the A-term) in the Lagrangian, yields improved nucleon properties compared to the original Birse and Banerjee (1985) model, particularly reducing the sigma commutator by about 30%, but only for specific choices of quark mass and sigma meson mass.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled field equations for the sigma and pion mesons and the Dirac equation for quarks in the mean-field approximation using the hedgehog baryon ansatz. The meson fields were treated as time-independent classical fields, and the implicit integral equations were solved by iteration to self-consistency using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method for the Dirac equations. The model includes an additional A-term in the Lagrangian that breaks renormalizability but modifies the meson dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: 2.68 nuclear magnetons at m_q = 462 MeV, m_sigma = 900 MeV, compared to 2.79 (experiment) and 2.87 (Birse and Banerjee 1985)
- Neutron magnetic moment: -2.08 nuclear magnetons at m_q = 462 MeV, m_sigma = 900 MeV, compared to -1.91 (experiment) and -2.29 (Birse and Banerjee 1985)
- Sigma commutator sigma(pi N): 56 MeV at m_q = 400 MeV, m_sigma = 900 MeV, compared to 92 MeV in Birse and Banerjee (1985)
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A(0): 1.78 at m_q = 462 MeV, m_sigma = 900 MeV, compared to 1.86 (Birse and Banerjee 1985) and 1.25 (experiment)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant g_pi NN(0) * m_pi / (2 M_N): 1.38 at m_q = 462 MeV, m_sigma = 900 MeV, compared to 1.53 (Birse and Banerjee 1985) and 1.0 (experiment)

## Context

This work extends the approximating chiral quark model of Broniowski and Golli (2003) by calculating additional nucleon properties (sigma commutator, axial-vector coupling, and pion-nucleon coupling) that were not computed in that earlier study. The results improve upon the original linear sigma model of Birse and Banerjee (1985), particularly for the sigma commutator, but the axial-vector coupling constant remains significantly above the experimental value, indicating the need for quantum effects or additional mesonic interactions in future work.
