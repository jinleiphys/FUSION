# Nonextensive hydrodynamics of boost-invariant plasmas

> We use quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics to study the non-conformal and non-extensive dynamics of a system undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion. To introduce nonextensivity, we use an underlying Tsallis distribution with a time-dependent nonextensivity parameter $q$. By taking moments of the quasiparticle Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation, we obtain dynamical equations which allow us to determine the time evolution of all microscopic parameters including $q$. We compare numerical solutions for bulk observables obtained using the nonextensive evolution with results obtained using quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics with a Boltzmann distribution function ($q \rightarrow 1$). We show that the evolution of the temperature, pressure ratio, and scaled energy density, are quite insensitive to which distribution function is assumed. However, we find significant differences in the early-time evolution of the bulk pressure which are observed for even small deviations from the Boltzmann distribution function. Finally, we discuss the existence of non-conformal hydrodynamic attractors for the longitudinal and transverse pressures, the bulk and shear viscous corrections, and the nonextensivity parameter $q$.

# Nonextensive hydrodynamics of boost-invariant plasmas

## Key claim

The evolution of temperature, pressure ratio, and scaled energy density in quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics is quite insensitive to whether a Tsallis or Boltzmann distribution function is assumed; however, significant differences appear in the early-time evolution of the bulk pressure even for very small deviations from the Boltzmann distribution (δq ~ 0.001), and non-conformal hydrodynamic attractors exist for both extensive and non-extensive statistics.

## Method

The authors used 0+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics with a temperature-dependent thermal mass matched to lattice QCD, solving the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation by taking zeroth, first, and second moments. They introduced nonextensivity via a Tsallis distribution with a time-dependent nonextensivity parameter q, obtaining five coupled dynamical equations for λ, T, α_x, α_z, and q. Numerical solutions were evolved from initial conditions T₀=600 MeV, α_x=α_z=1 at τ₀=0.25 fm/c, with initial q(τ₀)=1.15 for the Tsallis case.

## Key numbers

- Initial effective temperature: T₀ = 600 MeV at proper time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c
- Maximum difference in scaled energy density between Tsallis and Boltzmann approaches: roughly 1% at late times
- Maximum difference in pressure anisotropy between Tsallis and Boltzmann approaches: roughly 10% at τ ~ 0.5 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios studied: 4πη/s = 1 and 4πη/s = 5
- Convergence of longitudinal and transverse pressure ratios to universal attractor occurs at τ ~ 4 fm/c (τ/τ_eq ~ 4)
- Convergence of bulk pressure Π/P_eq occurs only for τ/τ_eq > 10 (τ ~ 20 fm/c)
- Tsallis parameter q converges to unity at τ/τ_eq ~ 5
- At τ₀, τT = 0.15 and τ/τ_eq = 0.628217; at late times (τ = 100 fm/c), τT ~ 15 and τ/τ_eq ~ 35
- Fitted δq values from experimental data cited: δq = 0.015 (Au+Au 200 GeV, 10-20% centrality), δq = 0.086 (60-80% centrality), δq = 0.1363 (Pb-Pb 2.76 TeV, 5-10% centrality), δq ~ 0.12-0.14 (p-p collisions)
- In massless limit, number density convergence interval: 0 ≤ q ≤ 1.5

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics framework previously developed for 0+1D boost-invariant systems with Boltzmann statistics (Alqahtani, Demir, Strickland, 2015) to include Tsallis non-extensive statistics. The motivation comes from the observation that 3+1D aHydroQP predictions using Boltzmann distributions at freeze-out fall faster than experimental data at intermediate p_T ~ 3 GeV, while Tsallis-based fits better reproduce the power-law tails seen in data. The results enable future modification of existing 3+1D aHydroQP codes to incorporate Tsallis statistics for studying heavy-ion observables, particularly hadron spectra at intermediate transverse momentum, and suggest that the late-time value of q can be constrained without full dynamical simulation.
