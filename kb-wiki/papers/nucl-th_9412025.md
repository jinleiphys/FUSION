# Radiative Neutron-Proton Capture in Effective Chiral Lagrangians

> We calculate the cross-section for the thermal $n+p\rightarrow d+γ$ process in chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order using heavy-fermion formalism. The exchange current correction is found to be $(4.5\pm 0.3)~\%$ in amplitude and the chiral perturbation at one-loop order gives the cross section $σ_{th}^{np}=(334\pm 2)\ {\mbox mb}$ which is in agreement with the experimental value $(334.2\pm 0.5)\ {\mbox mb}$. Together with the axial charge transitions, this provides a strong support for the power of chiral Lagrangians for nuclear physics.

# Radiative Neutron-Proton Capture in Effective Chiral Lagrangians

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNL) reproduces the thermal neutron-proton capture cross section to within 1% of experiment, provided a short-distance cutoff is introduced at internucleon distances around 0.5 fm. The exchange current correction is found to be (4.5 ± 0.3)% in amplitude, confirming that the dominant contributions come from "generalized tree" operators rather than genuine loop corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the isovector magnetic moment operator for the n+p → d+γ process using heavy-fermion chiral perturbation theory to NNL order (ν = +1 relative to the leading two-body current). They separate the two-body current into three classes: the "generalized tree" operators (one-pion exchange with resonance-saturated counter terms from ω and Δ exchange), and genuine two-pion exchange loop diagrams. The matrix elements are evaluated using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential for the deuteron and scattering wave functions, with a short-range cutoff r_c applied to regularize zero-range contributions.

## Key numbers

- Exchange current correction in amplitude: (4.5 ± 0.3)%
- Chiral perturbation theory cross section: σ_th^np = (334 ± 2) mb
- Experimental cross section: (334.2 ± 0.5) mb
- Impulse approximation cross section: σ_imp = 305.6 mb (about 9.6% below experiment)
- Argonne v18 predicted scattering length: a_s^th = -23.732 fm (experimental: a_s^exp = -23.739 ± 0.008 fm)
- Counter-term constants from resonance saturation: c̄_ω ≃ 0.1021 (from ω → πγ decay, g_ω = 17.55) and c̄_Δ ≃ 0.1667 (from NΔ transition, μ_T = -7.7 ± 0.5, C = -1.73)
- Theoretical prediction with cutoff dependence: σ_χPT = 334 ± 3 mb
- Cutoff value giving exact agreement with experiment: r_c ≃ 0.5 fm
- Genuine one-loop correction: less than 0.6% of the single-particle matrix element

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of exchange currents by Riska and Brown (1972), who computed two-pion-exchange and resonance diagrams to explain the ~10% discrepancy between impulse approximation and experiment. It also formalizes the current-algebra approach of Chemtob and Rho (1971) within modern chiral perturbation theory, following the framework for nuclear forces developed by Weinberg (1990-1992) and Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994). The authors had previously applied the same chiral order to axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei (Park, Min, and Rho, 1993), where a similar "chiral filter" mechanism was observed. This calculation demonstrates that chiral perturbation theory can quantitatively describe nuclear electromagnetic processes and enables systematic extension to higher orders, with the conjecture that at O(Q^4) the appropriate cutoff would be r_c ~ 1/m_ω ~ 0.3 fm.
