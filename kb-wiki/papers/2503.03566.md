# Four-dimensional QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials

> Exploration of the QCD phase diagram is pivotal in particle and nuclear physics. We construct a full four-dimensional equation of state of QCD with net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness by extending the NEOS model beyond the conventional two-dimensional approximation. Lattice QCD calculations based on the Taylor expansion method and the hadron resonance gas model are considered for the construction. We also develop an efficient numerical method for applying the four-dimensional equation of state to relativistic hydrodynamic simulations, which can be used for the analysis of nuclear collisions at beam energy scan energies and for different nuclear species at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

# Four-dimensional QCD equation of state at finite chemical potentials

## Key claim
The authors construct a full four-dimensional equation of state of QCD with net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness by extending the NEOS model, but the construction relies on a smooth crossover matching between lattice QCD Taylor expansion results and the hadron resonance gas model around a temperature that depends on baryon chemical potential, meaning the model is not valid at very large fugacities where the Taylor expansion does not converge.

## Method
The hybrid QCD equation of state model, NEOS-4d, is built by combining lattice QCD results from the Taylor expansion method (using all susceptibilities up to fourth order, with parametrized sixth-order susceptibilities for thermodynamic consistency) with the hadron resonance gas model for hadrons and resonances lighter than 2 GeV. The pressure from the two descriptions is smoothly connected using a hyperbolic tangent switching function with a chemical-potential-dependent crossover temperature. The authors introduce new variables based on the temperature and chemical potentials of a three-flavor parton gas to enable efficient tabulation of the equation of state for use in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key numbers
- The crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is set to a = 0.16 GeV, with a width parameter Delta T_c = 0.1 * T_c(0)
- The chemical-potential-dependent crossover temperature uses parameters b = 0.139 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.053 GeV^{-3}, and d = 0.4
- The parton gas equation of state gives energy density e = (19*pi^2/12) * T_tilde^4 for the three-flavor case
- Constant s/n_B trajectories are shown for values 420, 144, 51, and 30, corresponding to collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 62.4, 19.6, and 14.5 GeV respectively
- The trajectories are cut off when mu_B exceeds 0.6 GeV
- In the parton gas limit, diagonal susceptibilities approach chi_2^S = 1, chi_2^Q = 2/3, and chi_2^B = 1/3

## Context
This work extends the NEOS model (Monnai 2019, Monnai 2021) beyond the conventional two-dimensional approximation to include all three conserved charges (baryon, electric charge, strangeness) simultaneously. It builds on lattice QCD calculations (Bazavov 2014, Bazavov 2017) and the hadron resonance gas model, and provides an efficient numerical method for applying the four-dimensional equation of state to relativistic hydrodynamic simulations. The results enable exploration of the QCD phase diagram at beam energy scan energies at RHIC, including the effects of varying n_Q/n_B ratios from 0 to 1 that correspond to neutron-rich and proton-rich regions of the initial collision geometry.
