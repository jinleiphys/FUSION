# Effective potential for relativistic scattering

## Key claim

The effective potential for relativistic scattering in the Sine-Gordon model is frame-dependent (laboratory versus centre-of-mass frame), but in both frames it closely resembles the zero-momentum potential of the equivalent Ruijsenaars-Schneider model, provided one uses quantum inverse scattering with singular potentials.

## Method

The authors apply quantum inverse scattering theory to singular potentials, specifically the \(1/\sinh^2 x\) potential, to construct effective potentials for the Sine-Gordon model. They compute these effective potentials separately in the laboratory frame and the centre-of-mass frame, then compare them to the zero-momentum potential of the Ruijsenaars-Schneider model.

## Key numbers

- The effective potential in the laboratory frame is derived using the Sine-Gordon S-matrix, with the coupling constant related to the sine-Gordon parameter \(\beta\) (the paper does not quote a specific numerical value for \(\beta\) in the main text provided).
- The effective potential in the centre-of-mass frame is calculated and shown to be frame-dependent relative to the laboratory frame result.
- The zero-momentum potential of the Ruijsenaars-Schneider model is used as the reference potential to which both frame-dependent effective potentials are compared.

## Context

This work extends the quantum inverse scattering approach for singular potentials, building on prior treatments of the \(1/\sinh^2 x\) potential and the Sine-Gordon S-matrix. The frame dependence of the effective potential is a new result that contrasts with non-relativistic scattering, and it enables further study of relativistic effects in integrable quantum field theories. The paper explicitly references the Ruijsenaars-Schneider model as the equivalent zero-momentum system.
