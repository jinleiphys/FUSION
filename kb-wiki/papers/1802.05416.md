# Magnetic moments of the lowest-lying singly heavy baryons

> A light baryon is viewed as $N_c$ valence quarks bound by meson mean fields in the large $N_c$ limit. In much the same way a singly heavy baryon is regarded as $N_c-1$ valence quarks bound by the same mean fields, which makes it possible to use the properties of light baryons to investigate those of the heavy baryons. A heavy quark being regarded as a static color source in the limit of the infinitely heavy quark mass, the magnetic moments of the heavy baryon are determined entirely by the chiral soliton consisting of a light-quark pair. The magnetic moments of the baryon sextet are obtained by using the parameters fixed in the light-baryon sector. In this mean-field approach, the numerical results of the magnetic moments of the baryon sextet with spin $3/2$ are just 3/2 larger than those with spin $1/2$. The magnetic moments of the bottom baryons are the same as those of the corresponding charmed baryons.

# Magnetic moments of the lowest-lying singly heavy baryons

## Key claim
The magnetic moments of the baryon sextet with spin 3/2 are exactly 3/2 larger than those with spin 1/2, and the magnetic moments of bottom baryons are identical to those of the corresponding charmed baryons, because the heavy quark contribution is ignored in the limit of infinitely heavy quark mass.

## Method
The authors employed the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM), treating a singly heavy baryon as N_c-1 valence light quarks bound by meson mean fields with the heavy quark as a static color source. All dynamical parameters (w_1 through w_6) were fixed using experimental data on the magnetic moments of the light baryon octet from prior work by Yang (2015), with no additional free parameters introduced. The magnetic moments were computed by sandwiching the collective operator between heavy baryon wave functions that include rotational 1/N_c corrections and linear m_s (strange quark mass) corrections, with the soliton wave functions coupled to the heavy quark spin via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers
- μ(Σ_c^{++}) = 2.15 ± 0.1 μ_N (nuclear magnetons) for the spin-1/2 charmed sextet
- μ(Σ_c^0) = -1.24 ± 0.05 μ_N for the spin-1/2 charmed sextet
- μ(Ω_c^0) = -0.85 ± 0.05 μ_N for the spin-1/2 charmed sextet
- μ(Σ_c^{*++}) = 3.22 ± 0.15 μ_N for the spin-3/2 charmed sextet
- μ(Ω_c^{*0}) = -1.28 ± 0.08 μ_N for the spin-3/2 charmed sextet
- Effects of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking contribute approximately 5-10% to most magnetic moments, except for Σ_c^0 and Ω_c^0 where effects are around 15-20%
- The scaling factor σ used to compensate for the valence-sea separation was determined as σ ≈ 0.85 from Kim (2017)

## Context
This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model previously applied to light baryons by Kim (1995, 1997) and Yang (2015) to the singly heavy baryon sector, following the formalism developed by Yang (2016) for heavy baryon masses. The results are compared with the Skyrme model in bound-state approaches by Oh (1991) and Scholl and Weigel (2003), the relativistic quark model by Faessler (2006), and lattice QCD by Can (2013, 2015) and Bahtiyar (2016). The present results are systematically larger than lattice QCD values except for the Σ_c^{++} magnetic moment, but are consistent with the relativistic quark model. The same approach can be extended to compute transition magnetic moments for radiative decays and to study doubly heavy baryons with N_c-2 valence quarks.
