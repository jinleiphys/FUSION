# Description of nuclei in the A$\sim$100 mass region with the interacting boson model

> Even--even nuclei in the $A\sim100$ mass region are investigated within the framework of the interacting boson model-1 ({IBM-1}). The study includes energy spectra and electric quadrupole transition properties of zirconium, molybdenum, ruthenium and palladium isotopes with neutron number $N\geq52$. A global parametrization of the {IBM-1} Hamiltonian is found leading to a description of about 300 collective levels in 30 nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation from the observed level energies of 120 keV. The importance of the $d_{5/2}$ subshell closure at neutron number N=56 is pointed out. The geometric character of the nuclei can be visualized by plotting the potential energy surface $V(β,γ)$ obtained from the {IBM-1} Hamiltonian in the classical limit. The parametrization established on the basis of known elements is used to predict properties of the unknown, neutron-rich isotopes $^{106}$Zr, $^{112}$Mo, $^{116}$Ru and $^{122}$Pd.

## Key claim
The paper presents a global IBM-1 parametrization for the A~100 mass region that describes about 300 collective levels in 30 nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation of 120 keV, but this fit explicitly excludes zirconium isotopes with neutron number N<60 because the N=56 subshell closure produces strong deviations that the simple parametrization cannot capture.

## Method
The authors used the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) with a Hamiltonian containing six parameters (epsilon, kappa, kappa', kappa'', lambda, and chi) and assumed a linear dependence of kappa, kappa', and lambda on the fractional filling of the neutron and proton valence shells, while epsilon and kappa'' were kept constant. They performed a unified fit to energy levels and E2 transition rates of even-even Zr, Mo, Ru, and Pd isotopes with N>=52, minimizing the root-mean-square deviation, and used the consistent-Q formalism for E2 calculations. The geometric character was visualized through potential energy surfaces V(beta,gamma) obtained from the classical limit of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square deviation from observed level energies: 120 keV for 335 collective levels in 31 nuclei (excluding Zr with N<60)
- Optimal quadrupole parameter chi = -0.20, determined from minimization of rms deviations in both energies and B(E2) values
- Boson effective charge e_b = 0.097 e b, adjusted to reproduce 2_1+ -> 0_1+ and 4_1+ -> 2_1+ transitions
- The 2_1+ level in 96Zr is observed at 1751 keV while calculated at 444 keV, illustrating the N=56 subshell closure effect
- Predicted unknown neutron-rich isotopes: 106Zr, 112Mo, 116Ru, and 122Pd

## Context
This work extends the multi-nucleus IBM-1 parametrization approach of Casten et al. (1985) based on the N_nu N_pi scheme, using more refined Hamiltonians to better reproduce observed nuclear properties. It has similar aims to the work of Nomura et al. (2008) who connect IBM with mean-field models, but here the long-term goal is to draw inspiration from the shell-model connection. The study enables spectroscopic predictions for unknown neutron-rich nuclei in the A~100 region by extrapolation of the parametrization, and is sensitive enough to detect precursor effects of shell closures (such as the N=56 d_5/2 subshell closure) that could be useful for exploring exotic regions of the nuclear chart.
