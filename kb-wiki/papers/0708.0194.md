# Isomer triggering via nuclear excitation by electron capture

> Triggering of long-lived nuclear isomeric states via coupling to the atomic shells in the process of nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) is studied. NEEC occurring in highly-charged ions can excite the isomeric state to a triggering level that subsequently decays to the ground state. We present total cross sections for NEEC isomer triggering considering experimentally confirmed low-lying triggering levels and reaction rates based on realistic experimental parameters in ion storage rings. A comparison with other isomer triggering mechanisms shows that, among these, NEEC is the most efficient.

# Isomer triggering via nuclear excitation by electron capture

## Key claim

Nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) can be the most efficient isomer triggering mechanism among those investigated, provided that low-lying triggering levels are available; the efficiency is particularly pronounced for high-atomic-number isotopes, and for the specific case of $^{242\rm{m}}$Am the NEEC resonance strength is six orders of magnitude larger than the x-ray photoexcitation strength, contradicting earlier qualitative estimates that had assumed a partial internal conversion coefficient many orders of magnitude too low.

## Method

The authors calculated total resonance strengths for NEEC isomer triggering by modeling the resonant capture of a free electron into a highly-charged ion with simultaneous nuclear excitation from the isomeric state to a triggering level, followed by branching-ratio-weighted decay to a final state that avoids repopulating the isomer. The electron-nucleus interaction was treated relativistically with finite nuclear size, nuclear matrix elements were expressed via experimental reduced transition probabilities (or Weisskopf estimates where unavailable), electronic wavefunctions were computed with the GRASP92 package, and internal conversion rates were derived from NEEC rates via detailed balance; photoexcitation resonance strengths were calculated for comparison using the same triggering levels.

## Key numbers

- $^{242}_{95}$Am: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 3.6 \times 10^{-3}$ b eV for capture into the $5p_{3/2}$ orbital at continuum electron energy $E_c = 0.135$ keV; x-ray resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm x\,ray} = 2.4 \times 10^{-8}$ b eV; partial internal conversion coefficient for the $5p$ orbital $\alpha_{5p} = 6 \times 10^5$, more than ten orders of magnitude larger than the value assumed in Zader (prior work).
- $^{235}_{92}$U: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 1.3 \times 10^{-1}$ b eV for capture into $2p_{1/2}$ at $E_c = 21.992$ keV; x-ray strength $1.3 \times 10^{-2}$ b eV.
- $^{189}_{76}$Os: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 1.2 \times 10^{-3}$ b eV for capture into $1s_{1/2}$ at $E_c = 131.050$ keV; x-ray strength $2.2 \times 10^{-2}$ b eV (the only case where photoexcitation exceeds NEEC).
- $^{152}_{63}$Eu: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 3.4 \times 10^{-4}$ b eV for capture into $2s_{1/2}$ at $E_c = 5.204$ keV; x-ray strength $6.5 \times 10^{-5}$ b eV.
- $^{204}_{92}$Pb: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 4.9 \times 10^{-5}$ b eV for capture into $2p_{3/2}$ at $E_c = 55.138$ keV; x-ray strength $8.7 \times 10^{-6}$ b eV.
- $^{93}_{42}$Mo: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 9.1 \times 10^{-6}$ b eV for capture into $3p_{3/2}$ at $E_c = 2.113$ keV; x-ray strength $1.4 \times 10^{-8}$ b eV.
- $^{178}_{72}$Hf: NEEC resonance strength $S^{I\to F}_{\rm NEEC} = 2.0 \times 10^{-7}$ b eV for capture into $1s_{1/2}$ at $E_c = 51.373$ keV; x-ray strength $5.4 \times 10^{-8}$ b eV.
- Estimated reaction rates for NEEC triggering in a storage ring with $10^6$ isomeric ions and electron density $10^{15}$ cm$^{-2}$: $^{235\rm{m}}$U: $6.5 \times 10^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$; $^{189\rm{m}}$Os: $1.1 \times 10^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$; $^{178\rm{m2}}$Hf: $1.5 \times 10^{-7}$ s$^{-1}$.
- $^{180\rm{m}}$Ta isomer lifetime: $t_{1/2} \ge 1.2 \times 10^{15}$ yr.

## Context

This work extends the study of isomer triggering mechanisms beyond photoabsorption (Kara_Carr, Carr_Kara, prior work), Coulomb excitation (Hayes, Kara_Carr5), and earlier qualitative NEEC estimates by Zader (prior work) that were off by many orders of magnitude due to unknown internal conversion coefficients. The results contradict the conclusion of Zader (prior work) that NEEC is less favorable than other mechanisms for $^{242\rm{m}}$Am, and instead show NEEC is the most efficient among the mechanisms compared. The paper enables experimental searches for NEEC itself (which had not been conclusively observed at the time) by proposing isomer triggering as a background-reduced signature, and identifies candidate isotopes for storage-ring experiments at facilities such as FAIR (SIS100) and electron beam ion traps.
