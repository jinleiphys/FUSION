# Parton rescattering effect on the charged hadron forward-backward multiplicity correlation in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt s$=200 GeV

> The parton rescattering effect on the charged hadron forward-backward multiplicity correlation in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt s$=200 GeV is studied by a parton and hadron cascade model, PACIAE, based on the PYTHIA model. The calculated multiplicity and pseudorapidity distribution of the final state charged hadron are well compared with experimental data. It turned out that the final state charged hadron pseudorapidity distribution are different from the initial state charged partons. The parton rescattering effect on the charged hadron forward-backward multiplicity correlation increases with increasing parton rescattering strength in the center pseudorapidity region ($|η| <1$). However, this effect becomes weaker in the outer pseudorapidity region ($|η|> 1$).

# Parton rescattering effect on the charged hadron forward-backward multiplicity correlation in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV

## Key claim

The parton rescattering effect on the charged hadron forward-backward multiplicity correlation increases with increasing parton rescattering strength in the central pseudorapidity region (|eta| < 1), but this effect becomes weaker in the outer pseudorapidity region (|eta| > 1); however, the effect is not as strong as expected from the increasing fraction of participating charged partons because the average total number of initial state charged partons is only around 6 and only 2-to-2 parton rescattering processes are considered.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade modEl) model, which is based on PYTHIA, to simulate pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They designed three scenarios for parton rescattering in the parton evolution stage: no rescattering (K=0), weak rescattering (K=1, mu^2=0.4 GeV^2/c^4, with about 18% of charged partons participating), and strong rescattering (K=3, mu^2=0.1 GeV^2/c^4, with about 58% of charged partons participating). The forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength b was calculated as a function of the center distance between forward and backward pseudorapidity bins (Delta eta) using the standard covariance definition.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity (|eta| <= 5.4) for no parton rescattering: 21.2 (final hadronic state), compared to experimental data of 19.9 +/- 2.2 (PHOBOS inelastic pp data)
- Charged particle multiplicity for 18% parton rescattering: 21.7 (final hadronic state, 18.5 for inelastic collisions)
- Charged particle multiplicity for 58% parton rescattering: 21.9 (final hadronic state, 18.7 for inelastic collisions)
- Initial state charged parton multiplicity: 5.96 (no rescattering), 6.13 (18% rescattering), 6.15 (58% rescattering), in full eta phase space
- Parton rescattering parameters: weak case uses K=1 and mu^2=0.4 GeV^2/c^4; strong case uses K=3 and mu^2=0.1 GeV^2/c^4

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of forward-backward multiplicity correlations using the PYTHIA model by Yan et al. (2008), which found that a factor of 3-4 discrepancy between UA5 antiproton-proton data and STAR pp data could be attributed to differences in detector acceptances. It also responds to the work of Hwa and Yang (2007), who argued that back-to-back parton scattering could explain final state hadronic covariance without detailed dynamical inputs. The study uses the PACIAE model developed by Sa et al. (2007) to investigate whether parton rescattering in the evolution stage significantly modifies the correlation strength, and it enables future comparisons with nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies and pp collisions at LHC energies where stronger parton rescattering effects are expected.
