# Recent developments at finite density on the lattice

> Some recent developments to handle the numerical sign problem in QCD and related theories at nonzero density are reviewed. In this contribution I focus on changing the integration order to soften the severity of the sign problem, the density of states, and the extension into the complex plane (complex Langevin dynamics and Lefshetz thimbles).

# Recent developments at finite density on the lattice

## Key claim
The numerical sign problem in QCD at nonzero density can be mitigated but not eliminated by changing the order of integration, using density-of-states methods, or extending the configuration space into the complex plane; each approach has limitations and requires careful a posteriori verification, and no single method has yet yielded a first-principles determination of the QCD phase diagram.

## Method
The paper reviews three classes of lattice techniques for handling the complex fermion determinant at nonzero chemical potential: (1) changing the integration order by performing gauge-link integrals before fermion integration, starting from the strong-coupling limit (β=0) and adding O(β) corrections via reweighting or worm-type algorithms; (2) density-of-states methods using constrained simulations, specifically the local linear relaxation (LLR) approach that samples the density ρ(x) over many orders of magnitude in bins with forced sampling; (3) complexification methods including complex Langevin dynamics (with gauge cooling for nonabelian theories) and integration along Lefschetz thimbles, which explore the complexified configuration space.

## Key numbers
- In the strong-coupling limit (β=0) with O(β) corrections included, the critical temperature aT_c decreases with increasing β, while the tricritical point depends only weakly on β; the critical endpoint of the nuclear transition shifts to smaller temperature at nonzero β.
- In the Z(3) spin model at nonzero chemical potential, the density of states extends over more than 60 orders of magnitude, with |x| (net particle density) up to 5500, while standard histograms only cover |x| ≲ 1000.
- In QCD with a hopping parameter expansion to O(κ^50), complex Langevin results for the quark density at μ=0.7 and 0.8 on an 8^4 lattice converge to the full QCD result as the truncation order increases.

## Context
This review extends the discussion of the sign problem from the foundational review by deForcrand (2010) and complements other recent reviews by Aarts (2013), Chandrasekharan (2013), Ejiri (2013), Gattringer (2014), and Sexty (2014). The strong-coupling expansion with O(β) corrections builds on work by Karsch (1988) and deForcrand (2009), while the Hubbard-Stratonovich approach to decouple plaquettes was originally discussed by Fabricius (1984) and recently taken up by Vairinhos (2014). The LLR density-of-states method was introduced by Langfeld, Lucini and Rago (2012) and applied to finite density in the Z(3) model by Langfeld (2014) and Lucini (2014). Complex Langevin results for full QCD were first obtained by Sexty (2013), and the comparison between complex Langevin and Lefschetz thimbles in simple models was performed by Aarts (2013, 2014). The review identifies open questions about convergence of the strong-coupling expansion at O(β^2), behavior of density-of-states methods at large μ and near transitions, and the need for continued caution in complex Langevin applications to full QCD.
