# Long-range azimuthal correlations in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions from the incoherent scattering of partons

> We show that the incoherent elastic scattering of partons, as present in a multi-phase transport model (AMPT), with a modest parton-parton cross-section of $σ=1.5 - 3$ mb, naturally explains the long-range two-particle azimuthal correlation as observed in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider.

# Long-range azimuthal correlations in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions from the incoherent scattering of partons

## Key claim
The incoherent elastic scattering of partons in a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) with a parton-parton cross-section of σ = 1.5–3 mb naturally generates the long-range two-particle azimuthal correlation in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC, contradicting an earlier negative result reported by the CMS Collaboration (2012).

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with the string melting mechanism, which converts all excited strings into quarks and antiquarks, to simulate p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV and p+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV. The parton cascade included only two-body elastic scatterings with a cross-section obtained from pQCD with a screening mass, followed by hadronization via a simple coalescence model and a relativistic hadronic transport model. The two-particle density function was calculated as a function of relative azimuthal angle Δφ and pseudorapidity separation Δη, averaged over 2 < |Δη| < 4, and compared to CMS data using the zero-yield-at-minimum (ZYAM) method to remove constant background.

## Key numbers
- Parton-parton cross-section of σ = 1.5 mb was used for the main results, with values of 0, 0.5, 1.5, and 3 mb tested for comparison.
- In p+Pb collisions, the partonic stage lifetime τ scales as τ ∼ N_track^α with α ∼ 1/2; for N_track = 50, 100, 200 the lifetime τ ≈ 1, 1.4, 1.7 fm, respectively.
- In p+p collisions, τ grows slowly from τ ≈ 0.6 fm for N_track = 10 to τ ≈ 0.8 fm for N_track = 100.
- The near-side peak at Δφ = 0 is best visible for transverse momentum range 1 < p_T < 2 GeV/c.
- The AMPT model reproduced CMS p_T spectra for pions, kaons, and protons in p+Pb collisions within 20% accuracy.

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model studies that previously showed long-range azimuthal correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV (Xu 2011) to the smaller systems of p+p and p+Pb collisions. It provides an alternative to both hydrodynamic descriptions (Bozek 2011, Shuryak 2013, Werner 2013) and Color Glass Condensate approaches (Dusling 2013) for understanding the long-range correlations observed by CMS (2010, 2012) and ALICE (2012). The key advantage over hydrodynamics is that no local thermalization is assumed, while the approach uses a single essential parameter (σ = 1.5–3 mb) compared to the CGC framework where the saturation scale is fitted separately for each multiplicity and colliding system. The results enable further investigation of elliptic and triangular Fourier coefficients in p+p, p+A, and peripheral A+A collisions.
