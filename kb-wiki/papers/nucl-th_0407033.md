# Full-Coupled Channel Approach to Doubly Strange $s$-Shell Hypernuclei

> We describe {\it ab initio} calculations of doubly strange, $S=-2$, $s$-shell hypernuclei ($^4_{ΛΛ}$H, $^5_{ΛΛ}$H, $^5_{ΛΛ}$He and $^6_{ΛΛ}$He) as a first attempt to explore the few-body problem of the {\it full}-coupled channel scheme for these systems. The wave function includes $ΛΛ$, $ΛΣ$, $NΞ$ and $ΣΣ$ channels. Minnesota $NN$, D2$^\prime$ $YN$, and simulated $YY$ potentials based on the Nijmegen hard-core model, are used. Bound state solutions of these systems are obtained. We find that a set of phenomenological $B_8B_8$ interactions among the octet baryons in $S=0, -1$ and -2 sectors, which is consistent with all of the available experimental binding energies of $S=0, -1$ and -2 $s$-shell (hyper-)nuclei, can predict a particle stable bound state of $^4_{ΛΛ}$H. For $^5_{ΛΛ}$H and $^5_{ΛΛ}$He, $ΛN-ΣN$ and $ΞN-ΛΣ$ potentials enhance the net $ΛΛ-NΞ$ coupling, and a large $Ξ$ probability is obtained even for a weaker $ΛΛ-NΞ$ potential.

## Key claim

A set of phenomenological baryon-baryon interactions among the octet baryons in S=0, -1 and -2 sectors, which is consistent with the Nagara event and all experimental binding energies of S=0 and -1 s-shell (hyper-)nuclei, can predict a particle stable bound state of ^4_ΛΛH, with the caveat that the mND_S potential gives an energy only 0.02 MeV below the ^3_ΛH + Λ threshold.

## Method

The authors performed full-coupled channel ab initio calculations for doubly strange S=-2 s-shell hypernuclei (^4_ΛΛH, ^5_ΛΛH, ^5_ΛΛHe, ^6_ΛΛHe) using the stochastic variational method. The wave function included four isospin-basis components: ΛΛ, ΛΣ, NΞ, and ΣΣ channels. The Minnesota NN potential, D2' YN potential, and simulated YY potentials based on the Nijmegen hard-core model (ND and NF) were used, with the Hamiltonian given by a 4x4 matrix of diagonal and transition potentials.

## Key numbers

- The Λ separation energies calculated for S=-1 hypernuclei using the D2' potential are: 0.056 MeV for ^3_ΛH, 2.23 MeV for ^4_ΛH, 2.17 MeV for ^4_ΛHe, 0.91 MeV for ^4_ΛH*, 0.89 MeV for ^4_ΛHe*, and 3.18 MeV for ^5_ΛHe
- The ΛΛ separation energies B_ΛΛ for S=-2 hypernuclei using the ND_S potential are: 0.107 MeV for ^4_ΛΛH, 4.04 MeV for ^5_ΛΛH, 3.96 MeV for ^5_ΛΛHe, and 7.93 MeV for ^6_ΛΛHe
- The ΛΛ separation energies using the mND_S potential (modified ND_S with ΛΛ diagonal strength reduced by factor 0.8) are: 0.058 MeV for ^4_ΛΛH, 3.74 MeV for ^5_ΛΛH, 3.66 MeV for ^5_ΛΛHe, and 7.53 MeV for ^6_ΛΛHe
- The experimental B_ΛΛ for ^6_ΛΛHe from the Nagara event is 7.25 ± 0.19^{+0.18}_{-0.11} MeV
- The calculated ΔB_ΛΛ(^6_ΛΛHe) using ND_S is 1.58 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.01 ± 0.20^{+0.18}_{-0.11} MeV
- The scattering length and effective range parameters for the YY potentials are: (a_s, r_s) = (-1.37 fm, 4.98 fm) for ND_S, (-0.91 fm, 6.25 fm) for mND_S, and (-0.40 fm, 12.13 fm) for NF_S
- For NF_S, the probabilities of non-ΛΛ components are: (P_NΞ, P_ΛΣ, P_ΣΣ) = (0.58%, 0.37%, 0.03%) for ^4_ΛΛH, (3.10%, 2.10%, 0.10%) for ^5_ΛΛH, and (1.33%, 1.13%, 0.10%) for ^6_ΛΛHe
- The P_NΞ probability for ^5_ΛΛH using mND_S is 4.55%, larger than the 3.10% obtained with NF_S despite the weaker ΛΛ-NΞ coupling in ND

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational calculations (Nemura 2003) and addresses a conflict with Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations (Filikhin and Gal 2002) that found no bound state for ^4_ΛΛH. The full-coupled channel approach goes beyond earlier treatments (Afnan and Gibson 2003; Yamada 2004) that only included ΛΛ-NΞ-ΣΣ coupling in the ^1S_0 channel, by also incorporating ΛN-ΣN and NΞ-ΛΣ transitions in the ^3S_1 channel. The results enable future experimental searches at J-PARC to test predictions for the lightest doubly strange hypernuclei and clarify the nature of ΛΛ-NΞ coupling in A=5 systems.
