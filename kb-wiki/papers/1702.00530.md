# Effects of meson-nucleon dynamics in a relativistic approach to medium-mass nuclei

> Recent developments in the relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) are reviewed. Based on the covariant meson-nucleon Lagrangian of quantum hadrodynamics, RNFT connects consistently the high-energy scale of heavy mesons, medium-energy range of pion and the low-energy domain of emergent collective vibrations (phonons) in a parameter-free way. Mesons and phonons build up the effective interaction in various channels, in particular, the phonon-exchange part takes care of the retardation effects, which are of great importance for the fragmentation of single-particle states, spreading of collective giant resonances and soft modes, quenching and beta-decay rates with significant consequences for astrophysics and for the theory of weak processes in nuclei. As examples, the recently discovered impact of isospin dynamics on the nuclear shell structure and the isospin-flip pairing vibrations are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including isovector (pion and rho-meson) dynamics beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation in the relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) produces additional shifts of dominant single-particle states in medium-mass nuclei, amounting from a few hundred keV to 1 MeV, though the impact is generally weaker than that of isoscalar phonons except for some states far from the Fermi surface.

## Method
The authors extend the relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) by incorporating isospin-flip phonons (proton-neutron and neutron-proton) into the nucleonic self-energy alongside the traditional isoscalar phonons. The self-energy is computed in a diagonal approximation using vertices and frequencies extracted from the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) for both isoscalar and isovector channels, and the Dyson equation is solved to obtain fragmented single-particle energies and spectroscopic factors. For the pairing vibrations, the proton-neutron particle-particle RRPA (pn-pp-RRPA) and the proton-neutron particle-particle relativistic time blocking approximation (pn-pp-RTBA) are applied to compute the response to deuteron-addition operators.

## Key numbers
- Shifts of dominant single-particle states from inclusion of pion and rho-meson dynamics amount from a few hundred keV to 1 MeV (e.g., the proton state 1f7/2 in 132Sn shows a significant effect due to strength redistribution).
- The pn-pp-RRPA calculations for 56Ni use a realistic Landau-Migdal parameter value g' = 0.6, with variations down to g' = 0 to test sensitivity.
- The pn-pp-RTBA calculations for 56Ni with g' = 0.6 show fragmentation and redistribution of deuteron-addition strength to lower energies compared to pn-pp-RRPA.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) developed by Litvinova and collaborators over the past decade, which itself builds on quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) and the original non-relativistic nuclear field theory (NFT) of Bes, Broglia, Bortignon, and others. The inclusion of isovector phonon dynamics goes beyond earlier RNFT implementations that only included isoscalar phonons (Litvinova 2015, 2016), and the investigation of proton-neutron pairing vibrations opens a new direction for understanding isospin-flip pairing mechanisms and their influence on nuclear structure observables.
