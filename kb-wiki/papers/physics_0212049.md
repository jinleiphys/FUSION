# Statistical Analysis of Composite Spectra

> We consider nearest neighbor spacing distributions of composite ensembles of levels. These are obtained by combining independently unfolded sequences of levels containing only few levels each. Two problems arise in the spectral analysis of such data. One problem lies in fitting the nearest neighbor spacing distribution to the histogram of level spacings obtained from the data. We show that the method of Bayesian inference is superior to this procedure. The second problem occurs when one unfolds such short sequences. We show that the unfolding procedure generically leads to an overestimate of the chaoticity parameter. This trend is absent in the presence of long-range level correlations. Thus, composite ensembles of levels from a system with long-range spectral stiffness yield reliable information about the chaotic behavior of the system.

# Statistical Analysis of Composite Spectra

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when analyzing composite ensembles of short level sequences (containing only 5–10 levels each), the unfolding procedure generically leads to an overestimate of the chaoticity parameter, but this bias is absent when the underlying system possesses long-range spectral rigidity (GOE-type stiffness). The authors show that Bayesian inference is superior to chi-squared histogram fitting for determining the chaoticity parameter from such data.

## Method

The authors generated surrogate ensembles of statistically independent spacings with a known chaoticity parameter f_t = 0.60 using a random-number generator, and constructed composite GOE ensembles from experimental microwave resonator data (Alt et al., 1997) with known f_t = 0.52. Short sequences of 5–24 levels were artificially folded using a power-law transformation (E' = aE^b with a = 0.36, b = 2.82) and then unfolded using either a two-parameter or three-parameter constant-temperature formula. The chaoticity parameter was inferred both by chi-squared fits to histograms (bin sizes 0.2 and 0.3) and by Bayesian inference using Jeffreys' prior, with the best-fit value taken as the mean of the posterior distribution.

## Key numbers

* Surrogate ensemble with true f_t = 0.60: Bayesian inference gave f = 0.67 ± 0.14 (N=50), 0.61 ± 0.13 (N=100), and 0.63 ± 0.07 (N=200), correctly reproducing the true value within error
* Surrogate ensemble after two-parameter unfolding: Bayesian inference gave f = 0.88 ± 0.10 (N=50), 0.83 ± 0.08 (N=100), and 0.77 ± 0.06 (N=200), significantly overestimating the true value
* Surrogate ensemble after three-parameter unfolding: Bayesian inference gave f = 0.91 ± 0.08 (N=50), 0.86 ± 0.08 (N=100), and 0.78 ± 0.06 (N=200)
* GOE ensemble with true f_t = 0.52: Bayesian inference gave f = 0.56 ± 0.14 (N=50), 0.52 ± 0.10 (N=100), and 0.55 ± 0.07 (N=200), correctly reproducing the true value
* GOE ensemble after two-parameter unfolding: f = 0.59 ± 0.14 (N=50), 0.56 ± 0.09 (N=100), and 0.58 ± 0.09 (N=200)
* GOE ensemble after three-parameter unfolding: f = 0.62 ± 0.13 (N=50), 0.58 ± 0.09 (N=100), and 0.62 ± 0.06 (N=200)
* Chi-squared fit to 0.2-bin histogram of surrogate ensemble (N=50): f = 0.90 ± 0.12, compared to Bayesian result of 0.67 ± 0.14

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis methods previously applied to coupled microwave resonators by Barbosa and Dembo to the problem of composite nuclear spectra, where sequences of levels of the same spin and parity typically contain only 5–10 levels (as in the ground-state domain analyses by von Egidy (1986), Mitchell (1988), Raman (1991), and Shriner (1991)). The paper addresses a systematic bias in the unfolding procedure that had been implicitly assumed negligible in prior analyses of nuclear level statistics. The finding that long-range spectral rigidity protects against unfolding-induced bias enables more reliable extraction of chaoticity parameters from experimental nuclear and atomic spectra, and suggests that a fully Bayesian treatment integrating over unfolding parameters (as outlined in Harney, 2002) would yield correct error intervals even for systems without GOE rigidity.
