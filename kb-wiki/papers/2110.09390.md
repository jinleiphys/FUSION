# Importance truncation for the in-medium similarity renormalization group

> Ab initio nuclear many-body frameworks require extensive computational resources, especially when targeting heavier nuclei. Importance-truncation (IT) techniques allow to significantly reduce the dimensionality of the problem by neglecting unimportant contributions to the solution of the many-body problem. In this work, we apply IT methods to the nonperturbative in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) approach and investigate the induced errors for ground-state energies in different mass regimes based on different nuclear Hamiltonians. We study various importance measures, which define the IT selection, and identify two measures that perform best, resulting in only small errors to the full IMSRG(2) calculations even for sizable compression ratios. The neglected contributions are accounted for in a perturbative way and serve as an estimate of the IT-induced error. Overall we find that the IT-IMSRG(2) performs well across all systems considered, while the largest compression ratios for a given error can be achieved when using soft Hamiltonians and for large single-particle bases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that importance truncation (IT) applied to the in-medium similarity renormalization group at the two-body truncation level (IT-IMSRG(2)) can significantly reduce computational costs while introducing only small errors, provided the truncation is restricted to the hppp and pppp two-body interaction blocks and the neglected contributions are accounted for perturbatively.

## Method

The authors apply importance truncation to the two-body matrix elements of the Hamiltonian in the IMSRG(2) framework by defining a mask based on an importance measure and a threshold, keeping only matrix elements above the threshold throughout the flow. They test multiple importance measures, including the magnitude of the matrix element and a perturbatively motivated measure incorporating energy denominators, and they treat the neglected matrix elements via a second-order perturbative correction. The IT-IMSRG(2) calculations are performed in a natural-orbital (NAT) single-particle basis using the Magnus expansion approach to solve the flow equation.

## Key numbers

- For the 1.8/2.0 EM interaction in a model space of e_max=10, the correlation energy of 40Ca is E_corr = -96.88 MeV; for the N3LO 500 Hamiltonian, E_corr = -303.02 MeV.
- A compression ratio of R_C=70 in an e_max=14 model space corresponds to truncating approximately 485.7 million of the total 492.7 million two-body matrix elements, leaving 7 million nonvanishing matrix elements (roughly equivalent to an e_max=8 model space).
- For 4He in the optimized e_NAT=14 NAT basis truncated to e_max=12 using the 1.8/2.0 EM interaction, the IT-IMSRG(2) results are compared to full IMSRG(2) and MBPT up to third order, with the perturbative correction shown as a band.
- Truncating all interaction blocks in 40Ca with the N3LO 500 Hamiltonian leads to a relative error on the correlation energy of nearly 10% at the full compression limit.

## Context

This work extends importance-truncation techniques, previously applied in the no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) by Roth (2007, 2009) and in Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory by Tichai (2019), to the nonperturbative IMSRG framework. The approach is motivated by the need to reduce computational and storage costs for ab initio calculations of heavier nuclei (above A ~ 100), deformed nuclei, and calculations including higher-order many-body terms. The findings enable future development of an IT-IMSRG solver using sparse storage formats to realize the computational benefits, and the method is expected to be most efficient for soft Hamiltonians and large single-particle bases.
