# Corrections from space-time dependent electromagnetic fields to Wigner functions and spin polarization

> We have derived the Wigner equations at global equilibrium with constant vorticity but space-time dependent electromagnetic fields up to second order in semiclassical expansion. We obtain the new second-order contributions to the charge currents and energy-momentum tensor from the varying electromagnetic fields. We also compute the new corrections to the spin polarization pesudo-vector from both contant and varying electromagnetic fields. We also find that the space-time dependent electromagnetic field provides a tighter constraint on the solutions of Wigner functions in global equilibrium compared with constant electromagnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order semiclassical corrections to Wigner functions, charge currents, energy-momentum tensor, and spin polarization from space-time dependent electromagnetic fields, finding that the varying fields impose tighter constraints on the Wigner function solutions at global equilibrium compared to the constant-field case, but the specific solution presented assumes a particular choice for the undetermined second-order distribution function.

## Method

The authors solve the Wigner equation for chiral fermions under a background electromagnetic field using a semiclassical expansion up to second order in ℏ, with the operators expanded via spherical Bessel functions. They impose global equilibrium conditions with constant thermal vorticity but allow space-time dependent electromagnetic fields, then integrate the resulting chiral Wigner functions over momentum to obtain vector and axial currents, energy-momentum tensor, and spin polarization pseudo-vector. The second-order contributions are separated into terms present already for constant fields and new terms arising from derivatives of the electromagnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The first-order chiral current receives contributions \(j_s^{(1)\mu} = \frac{s}{12\pi^2}(\pi^2 T^2 + 3\mu_s^2)\omega^\mu + \frac{s\mu_s}{4\pi^2}B^\mu\) from vorticity and magnetic field
- The second-order current includes a new derivative-dependent term \(\Delta^{\textrm{II}}j_s^{(2)\mu} = -\frac{1}{12}\left(\kappa_s^\epsilon + \frac{1}{\pi^2}\right)\partial_\lambda F^{\mu\lambda} + \frac{1}{24\pi^2}u_\nu u^\mu \partial_\lambda F^{\lambda\nu} + \frac{1}{48\pi^2}u_\nu u^\lambda \partial_\lambda F^{\mu\nu}\)
- The coefficient \(\kappa_s^\epsilon\) contains a divergent term \(-\frac{1}{\pi^2}\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon} + \ln 2 + \frac{1}{2}\ln\pi + \psi\left(\frac{3}{2}\right) - \ln T + \hat{\kappa}_s\right]\) requiring dimensional regularization with \(\epsilon = 4-d\)
- The electromagnetic field decreases by one order of magnitude within 0.2 fm/c at the beginning of heavy ion collisions and varies steeply within 10 fm in spatial distribution

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function approach of Gao (2012), Yang (2020), and Hidaka (2022) from constant electromagnetic fields to space-time dependent fields, addressing a gap in the literature where only a few works (Kharzeev 2011, Satow 2014, Mameda 2023) have discussed second-order derivative terms of electromagnetic fields. The results enable more realistic modeling of chiral transport and spin polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where electromagnetic fields vary rapidly in space and time, and provide a foundation for studying the coupling between thermal vorticity and varying electromagnetic fields at second order.
