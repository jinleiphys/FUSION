# Measurement of hadron masses in 2-color finite density QCD

> We investigate hadron spectra in 2-color QCD using lattice simulation with $N_{f}=2$ at low temperature and finite density in which there appears not only the hadronic phase but also the superfluid phase. We first calculate the pion and rho meson spectrum, which is well-known from previous works. The spectral ordering of these mesons flips around the quark chemical potential $μ=m^{0}_π/2$ ($m^{0}_π$: the pion mass at $μ=0$), where the phase transition between the hadronic and superfluid phases occurs. For $μ\gtrsim m^{0}_π/2$, the effective mass for the pion linearly increases while the one for the rho meson monotonically decreases. Furthermore, we measure hadron spectra with the isospin $I=0$ and the angular momentum $J^{P}=0^{\pm}$. The effective masses for the meson, diquark, and antidiquark with the same quantum number become degenerate just below $μ= m^{0}_π/2$, and the three hadrons have the same mass in the superfluid phase. It suggests that mixing occurs between spectra associating with mesons and baryons due to the $U(1)_{B}$ symmetry breaking. This phenomenon can be explained in the linear sigma model with the approximate $SU(4)$ Pauli-Gursey symmetry.

## Key claim

The spectral ordering of the pion and rho meson flips around the quark chemical potential μ = m⁰_π/2 (the phase transition point between hadronic and superfluid phases), and for μ ≳ m⁰_π/2 the pion effective mass increases linearly while the rho meson mass monotonically decreases. In the I=0, Jᴾ=0± channels, the effective masses for the meson, diquark, and antidiquark become degenerate just below μ = m⁰_π/2 and remain equal in the superfluid phase, suggesting mixing due to U(1)_B symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of 2-color QCD with N_f=2 using the Iwasaki gauge action and two-flavor Wilson fermion action, adding a quark number operator and diquark source term to the fermion action. They computed 2-point correlation functions for meson, diquark, and antidiquark operators in the I=1 (pion and rho meson) and I=0, Jᴾ=0± channels on 32⁴ lattices at β=0.8 and κ=0.159, corresponding to m⁰_π/m⁰_ρ ≈ 0.81 and lattice spacing a ≈ 0.16 fm. Effective masses were extracted by fitting the 2-point functions with single cosh, single exponential, or double cosh functions depending on the symmetry and noise properties of each channel.

## Key numbers

- m⁰_π ≈ 738 MeV (pion mass at μ=0)
- m⁰_π/m⁰_ρ ≈ 0.81 (ratio of pion to rho meson mass at μ=0)
- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.16 fm, temperature T ≈ 0.19 T_c with T_c assumed to be 200 MeV
- Phase transition between hadronic and superfluid phases occurs around μ/m⁰_π = 0.5
- For μ/m⁰_π ≤ 0.32, both pion and rho meson masses are independent of μ
- For μ/m⁰_π ≥ 0.40, pion mass starts to increase while rho meson mass slightly increases then decreases
- At μ/m⁰_π = 0.81, the pion 2-point function slope is steeper than the rho meson slope, indicating pion becomes heavier than rho meson in the superfluid phase
- In the I=0, 0⁺ channel at zero chemical potential, diquark and antidiquark masses equal the pion mass; the I=0, 0⁻ meson also has the same mass as the pion

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of 2-color QCD at finite density (Muroya et al., 2002; Hands et al., 2007; Wilhelm et al., 2019) that observed nontrivial features in the superfluid phase including spectral ordering changes and pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone modes. The results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Kogut et al., 1999, 2000) for the linear increase of the pion mass in the superfluid phase, and with analytical studies of dense 3-color QCD (Brown and Rho, 1991; Hatsuda and Lee, 1991) predicting decreasing rho meson mass at high density. The observed meson-diquark-antidiquark mixing in the superfluid phase is explained by the linear sigma model with approximate SU(4) Pauli-Gursey symmetry (Suenaga et al., 2022). Future work requires taking the j→0 limit of the diquark source term and including disconnected diagrams to study U(1)_A anomaly effects.
