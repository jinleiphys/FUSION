# Schematic Interactions with Many Degeneracies

> In previous works we examined the spectra for systems of 2 protons and 2 neutrons, in a single j shell calculation, by obtaining matrix elements from experiment. More recently we considered schematic interactions in the same model space. We continue in this vein here. The present work and the former can be regarded as 2 bookends on a bookshelf.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a new schematic interaction (0122) for single-j shell calculations of two-proton-two-neutron systems, which produces a collapse of the spectrum at high angular momentum with many degenerate levels, in contrast to their earlier 0123 interaction that yielded equally spaced high-spin states. The two interactions are presented as "bookends" representing extreme behaviors, with the claim that the splitting between the highest-spin states is linear in the parameter C for the generalized interaction SET3 = {0,0,1,0,2,0,C,0}.

## Method

The authors perform shell-model calculations for systems of two protons and two neutrons in a single-j shell, using schematic two-body interactions defined by the energies E(J) for even-J (T=1) states. The new 0122 interaction sets E(J)=0 for odd J and E(J)=J/2 for even J up to J=(2j-3), with the highest even J value E(2j-1) set equal to E(2j-3). They compute energy spectra and wave functions for the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells, and compare results with the earlier 0123 interaction and with realistic MBZE matrix elements taken from experiment.

## Key numbers

- For 44Ti with the 0122 interaction: I=12+ and I=10+ states are degenerate at 4.7840 MeV excitation energy
- For 44Ti with the 0123 interaction: equally spaced spectrum from I=6 onward with 1.5 MeV gaps (I=6 at 3.1498, I=8 at 4.6498, I=10 at 6.1498, I=12 at 7.6498 MeV)
- For 44Ti with MBZE interaction: I=12+ at 7.7022 MeV, I=10+ at 7.3839 MeV (splitting 0.3183 MeV)
- For 44Ti with the 0122 interaction: I=8+ states appear at 3.5221, 4.7840 (degenerate doublet), 5.6691, 6.4942, and 10.3078 MeV
- For the generalized interaction SET3 in f7/2: the I=12+ to I=10+ splitting equals 1.5C MeV
- For 52Fe with INTb interaction: E(12)-E(10) = -0.122 MeV (inverted), compared to +0.282 MeV for 44Ti with INTa
- For 44Ti: 12+ state half-life is 2.1 ns; for 52Fe: 12+ state half-life is 45.9 s

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of schematic interactions in single-j shell calculations (Kingan, Quinonez, Zamick, 2015-2016) and contrasts with the realistic MBZE interaction derived from experimental matrix elements of 42Sc (Escuderos et al., 1985). The new 0122 interaction produces degeneracies that exaggerate the behavior seen in real nuclei like 44Ti and 52Fe, where the 12+ state can become isomeric due to level inversion. The results connect to earlier work on seniority and pairing interactions (Flowers and Edmonds, 1950s) and to analytical results by Neergaard (2015), and may help interpret complex shell-model spectra by identifying "traces" of schematic behavior in realistic calculations.
