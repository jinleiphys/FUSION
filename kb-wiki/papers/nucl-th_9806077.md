# The elementary p(p,p'π^{+})n reaction

> A detailed study of the elementary p(p,p$'π^{+}$)n reaction is presented using the delta isobar model. In this model, in the first step one of the two protons in the initial state gets excited to $Δ$. This, in the second step, decays into a nucleon and a pion. For the $pp \to NΔ$ step the parametrized form of the DWBA t-matrix of Jain and Santra, which reproduces most of the available data on $pp \to nΔ^{++}$, is used. The cross-sections studied include the outgoing proton momentum spectra in coincidence with the pion, the outgoing pion momentum spectra and the integrated total cross-section. We find that all the calculated numbers are in good agreement with the corresponding measured cross sections.

# The elementary p(p,p'π⁺)n reaction

## Key claim

The delta isobar model, using a parametrized DWBA t-matrix for the pp→NΔ step based on one-pion-exchange with a cutoff parameter Λ_π = 650 MeV/c, reproduces the measured exclusive proton and pion momentum spectra and total cross sections for the p(p,p'π⁺)n reaction, but the agreement for the pion momentum spectrum excludes a peak around 550 MeV/c that may arise from final-state neutron-proton resonance structure.

## Method

The authors model the p(p,p'π⁺)n reaction as a two-step process: first, one proton excites to a Δ isobar via the pp→NΔ transition described by a parametrized DWBA t-matrix (Jain and Santra, 1992) using one-pion-exchange with Λ_π = 650 MeV/c; second, the Δ decays into a nucleon and pion via a pseudovector non-relativistic Lagrangian. The calculation includes both Δ⁺⁺ and Δ⁺ intermediate states, direct and exchange diagrams for the initial protons, and uses a free Δ propagator with energy-dependent width (Γ₀ = 120 MeV, γ = 200 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Λ_π = 650 MeV/c: the cutoff parameter used in the parametrized one-pion-exchange potential for the pp→NΔ transition
- Δ⁺⁺ contribution dominates the cross section; Δ⁺ contributes only 5-10%
- Exchange diagrams contribute at the level of 10-20% and reduce the cross section
- Beam energy for exclusive measurements: 800 MeV (LAMPF data from Hancock et al., 1983)
- Free Δ resonance parameters: m_Δ = 1232 MeV, Γ₀ = 120 MeV, γ = 200 MeV
- Coupling constants: f = 1.008 (πNN vertex), f* = 2.156 (πNΔ vertex)
- Pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier DWBA analyses of the pp→nΔ⁺⁺ reaction by Jain and Santra (1990, 1992) to the kinematically complete p(p,p'π⁺)n data from LAMPF (Hancock et al., 1983), providing a more stringent test than the inclusive missing-mass data used previously. The calculations contrast with those of Engel et al. (1996), which used plane waves and omitted distortion effects in the entrance and exit channels. The authors argue that one-pion-exchange alone suffices for spin-averaged cross sections in the delta region, consistent with the conclusions of Dmitriev (1986) and Jain and Santra (1990), while noting that rho-exchange remains controversial based on spin-transfer measurements by Prout et al. (1994) and Ellegaard et al. (1989). The work enables future studies of delta dynamics in nuclear media, building on the authors' earlier work on delta decay in nuclear matter (Jain and Kundu, 1996).
