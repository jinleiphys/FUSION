# $N$-boson spectrum from a Discrete Scale Invariance

> We present the analysis of the $N$-boson spectrum computed using a soft two-body potential the strength of which has been varied in order to cover an extended range of positive and negative values of the two-body scattering length $a$ close to the unitary limit. The spectrum shows a tree structure of two states, one shallow and one deep, attached to the ground-state of the system with one less particle. It is governed by an unique universal function, $Δ(ξ)$, already known in the case of three bosons. In the three-particle system the angle $ξ$, determined by the ratio of the two- and three-body binding energies $E_3/E_2=\tan^2ξ$, characterizes the Discrete Scale Invariance of the system. Extending the definition of the angle to the $N$-body system as $E_N/E_2=\tan^2ξ$, we study the $N$-boson spectrum in terms of this variable. The analysis of the results, obtained for up to $N=16$ bosons, allows us to extract a general formula for the energy levels of the system close to the unitary limit. Interestingly, a linear dependence of the universal function as a function of $N$ is observed at fixed values of $a$. We show that the finite-range nature of the calculations results in the range corrections that generate a shift of the linear relation between the scattering length $a$ and a particular form of the universal function. We also comment on the limits of applicability of the universal relations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $N$-boson spectrum for up to $N=16$ particles, computed with a finite-range soft two-body potential, follows a universal tree structure governed by the same universal function $\Delta(\xi)$ that describes three-boson systems, but only for the $n=0$ branch where bound states exist; the finite-range nature of the interaction introduces range corrections that shift the linear relation between the scattering length and the universal function, limiting the validity of universal relations as the system becomes more compact.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation using the Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) method for $N$-boson systems with $N$ up to 16, using a two-body Gaussian (TBG) potential with strength varied to cover positive and negative scattering lengths near the unitary limit. They extracted the universal function $y(\xi) = e^{-\Delta(\xi)/2s_0}/\cos\xi$ from the computed energies and fitted it to a linear form $y = \kappa_N^m a_B + \Gamma_N^m$ for each $N$ and each level $m$ (ground state $m=0$, first excited state $m=1$). The parameters $\kappa_N^m$ (scale parameters) and $\Gamma_N^m$ (finite-range shift parameters) were then analyzed as functions of $N$.

## Key numbers

- For $N=3$, the ground state branch ($n=0$) gives $\kappa_3^0 = 0.0488$ a.u.$^{-1}$ and $\Gamma_3^0 = 0.869$, while the first excited branch ($n=1$) gives $\kappa_3^1 = 0.00212$ a.u.$^{-1}$ and $\Gamma_3^1 = 0.0840$, with a fit $\chi^2 \approx 0.1$.
- The ratio $\kappa_3^0/\kappa_3^1 \approx 23.0$ from the finite-range calculation, compared to the zero-range prediction of $e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7$.
- At the unitary limit, the ratio $E_4^{n,0}/E_3^0 \approx 4.611$ and $E_4^{n,1}/E_3^0 \approx 1.002$ from prior zero-range theory; the present finite-range calculation gives $\kappa_4^0/\kappa_3^0 = 2.42$ and $\kappa_4^1/\kappa_3^0 \approx 1.05$.
- The threshold values for the three-body ground state and first excited state disappearing into the three-atom continuum are $a_3^{0,-} \approx -44$ a.u. and $a_3^{1,-} \approx -745$ a.u.
- The crossing point coordinates for the ground states are $a_B^0 = 7.077$ a.u. and $\Gamma_0 = 0.768$; for the excited states, $a_B^1 = 7.304$ a.u. and $\Gamma_1 = 0.887$.
- For $N=4$ and $N=5$, the linear relation $\kappa_N^m = \kappa_4^m + (N-4)(\kappa_5^m - \kappa_4^m)$ describes the $N$-dependence of the scale parameters.
- The quantity $\kappa_N^m a_N^{m,-}$ at the $N$-body continuum threshold is approximately $-2.33$ for $m=0$ and $-2.45$ for $m=1$, compared to the zero-range value of $-1.56$.

## Context

This work extends the Efimov physics framework, originally established for three-boson systems by Efimov (1970, 1971) and reviewed by Braaten and Hammer (2006), to $N$-boson systems with up to 16 particles. It builds on prior studies of four-body systems by Hammer and Platter (2007), Stecher et al. (2009), and Deltuva (2011), which identified a two-level structure attached to each Efimov trimer, and on the work of Stecher et al. (2010) and Gattobigio et al. (2012) that studied universal ratios for $N \leq 13$ and $N \leq 6$, respectively. The paper enables a parameterization of the $N$-boson spectrum using only four parameters per level (two $\kappa_N^m$ values and the crossing point coordinates) and establishes that the tree structure of two states per $N$ holds only for bound states ($n=0$ branch), with excited states becoming unbound for $N > 7$ at the $N$-body continuum threshold.
