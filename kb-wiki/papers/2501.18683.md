# Visualizing How the Structure of Large-Radius Jets Shapes Their Wakes

> The ATLAS collaboration has introduced and implemented a strategy for selecting and analyzing large-radius jets composed of skinny $R=0.2$ subjets in heavy ion collisions at the LHC. We show how measurements of these jets teach us about the resolution length $L_{\rm res}$ of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and can teach us how jet substructure shapes the wakes that jets excite in the QGP droplets through which they pass. We use Hybrid Model calculations to reproduce measurements of $R_{AA}$ for large-radius jets in PbPb collisions, and study their dependence on the angle between the two skinny subjets involved in the final reclustering step of an $R=1$ jet. We show how these observables can constrain the value of $L_{\rm res}$ and demonstrate that the ATLAS data rule out any picture in which an entire parton shower loses energy coherently as if it were a single entity. Determining the degree to which the QGP can resolve partons within a jet is central to the broader program of using jet quenching measurements to probe QGP. We make further use of this setup by analyzing the response of the medium to the passage of large-radius $R=2$ jets containing two skinny subjets in gamma-jet events. We introduce novel jet-shape observables that allow us to visualize the angular shape of the soft hadrons originating from the wakes that wide jets with two skinny subjets excite in a droplet of QGP, as a function of the angular separation between the subjets. We find that even when they are $\sim 0.8- 1$ radian apart, a single broad wake is produced. Only when the two subjets are even farther apart is the presence of two sub-wakes revealed. We show that the way in which jet structure shapes jet wakes can be visualized with similar clarity in experiments by using only those hadrons with low $p_T$. These observables thus offer a new and distinctive way of seeing jet wakes in heavy ion collision data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ATLAS data on large-radius jet suppression in PbPb collisions rule out the picture where an entire parton shower loses energy coherently as a single entity (infinite resolution length), and that novel jet-shape observables using low-\(p_T\) hadrons can visualize how the angular separation between two skinny subjets within a large-radius jet determines whether they produce a single broad wake or two distinct sub-wakes in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors use the Hybrid Strong/Weak Coupling Model to simulate PbPb collisions at LHC energies, first reconstructing skinny anti-\(k_t\) \(R=0.2\) subjets and then reclustering them into large-radius jets of \(R=1.0\) or \(R=2.0\). They compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for these reclustered jets as a function of the angular separation \(\Delta R_{12}\) between the two hardest subjets, for different values of the QGP resolution length \(L_{\rm res}\). For wake visualization, they introduce jet-shape observables using only soft hadrons (0.7–1.0 GeV) within \(\Delta R=2.0\) of the jet axis in \(\gamma\)-jet events, and study how the two-dimensional angular distribution of wake hadrons changes with the rapidity separation \(\Delta y_{12}\) between the two subjets.

## Key numbers

* ATLAS data on \(R_{AA}\) for reclustered \(R=1.0\) jets in PbPb collisions rule out \(L_{\rm res}=\infty\); the data are inconsistent with any picture where an entire parton shower loses energy coherently as a single colored object.
* For reclustered \(R=2.0\) jets with two skinny subjets separated by 0.8 to 1.0 radians, a single broad wake is produced; only when the two subjets are even farther apart do two distinct sub-wakes appear.
* The wake visualization uses hadrons with \(0.7 < p_T < 1.0\) GeV within a radius of \(\Delta R = 2.0\) around the reclustered \(R=2.0\) jet axis; this range captures the wake while minimizing contamination from fragmentation hadrons.
* In inclusive jet samples (without \(\gamma\)-tagging), the positive wake from the two subjets is smaller than in \(\gamma\)-jet samples due to selection bias toward less-quenched jets, and the negative wake from away-side jets distorts the measured shape.

## Context

This work extends the ATLAS collaboration's (2023) measurements of large-radius jet suppression using reclustered skinny subjets in PbPb collisions, and builds on the Hybrid Model framework developed by Casalderrey-Solana and collaborators. The resolution length concept connects to prior work on color coherence in jet quenching. The new jet-shape observables enable experimental visualization of jet wakes in heavy ion collisions, complementing earlier wake studies using \(Z\)-hadron correlations by CMS (2024). The results open the possibility for ATLAS and CMS to directly image how jet substructure shapes the medium response, using \(\gamma\)-jet events to avoid contamination from away-side jet wakes.
