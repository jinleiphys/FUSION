# Threshold Expansion and Dimensionally Regularised NRQCD

> A Lagrangean and a set of Feynman rules are presented for non-relativistic QFT's with manifest power counting in the heavy particle velocity $v$. A régime is identified in which energies and momenta are of order $Mv$. It is neither identical to the ultrasoft régime corresponding to radiative processes with energies and momenta of order $Mv^2$, nor to the potential régime with on shell heavy particles and Coulomb binding. In this soft régime, massless particles are on shell, and heavy particle propagators become static. Examples show that it contributes to one- and two-loop corrections of scattering and production amplitudes near threshold. Hence, NRQFT agrees with the results of threshold expansion. A simple example also demonstrates the power of dimensional regularisation in NRQFT.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent low-energy Lagrangian for non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) with manifest velocity power counting exists, but only if a previously overlooked "soft regime" (with energies and momenta of order \(Mv\)) is included alongside the potential regime (\(T\sim Mv^2, |\vec{p}|\sim Mv\)) and ultrasoft regime (\(k_0\sim|\vec{k}|\sim Mv^2\)). The abstract's claim of agreement with threshold expansion is conditional on this additional regime being incorporated into the Feynman rules.

## Method

The author identifies three distinct kinematic regimes (soft, potential, ultrasoft) by examining all possible particle poles in non-relativistic propagators given the two scales \(Mv\) and \(Mv^2\). For each regime, space-time coordinates and fields are rescaled so that typical momenta become dimensionless, establishing explicit velocity power counting. A Lagrangian and Feynman rules are then constructed for a simplified scalar model (heavy complex scalar "quark" coupled to a massless real scalar "gluon"), including multipole expansions for vertices coupling particles from different regimes. The validity is checked by matching NRQFT to relativistic theory in one- and two-loop examples from Beneke and Smirnov (1997).

## Key numbers

* Soft regime: energies and momenta of order \(Mv\) (where \(v=\beta\gamma\ll 1\) is the relativistic generalization of relative particle velocity).
* Potential regime: kinetic energy \(T\sim Mv^2\), momentum \(|\vec{p}|\sim Mv\).
* Ultrasoft regime: energies and momenta of order \(Mv^2\).
* Soft quark propagator (static, to lowest order): \(\frac{i}{T+i\epsilon}\).
* Potential gluon propagator (instantaneous): \(\frac{i}{-\vec{k}^2+i\epsilon}\).
* The \(\Phi_\mathrm{p}\Phi_\mathrm{p}A_\mathrm{p}\) vertex scales as \(\mathcal{O}(1/\sqrt{v})\).
* The \(\Phi_\mathrm{s}\Phi_\mathrm{s}A_\mathrm{s}\) vertex scales as \(\mathcal{O}(v^0)\).
* Soft loop momenta scale as \([\mathrm{d}^4 k_\mathrm{s}]\sim v^4\); potential loop momenta scale as \([\mathrm{d}^4 k_\mathrm{p}]\sim v^5\).

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle raised by Beneke and Smirnov (1997), who showed that existing velocity rescaling rules (Luke and Manohar, 1992; Grinstein and Rothstein, 1992; Luke and Savage, 1997; Labelle, 1997) failed to reproduce the correct two-gluon exchange contribution to Coulomb scattering near threshold. The paper extends NRQCD by adding the soft regime, which cannot be mimicked by potential gluon exchange or contact terms. It enables a self-consistent formulation of dimensionally regularised NRQCD with explicit velocity power counting, and connects to Heavy Quark Effective Theory (Isgur and Wise, 1989; 1990) as a subset of the soft regime. A future publication (Griesshammer, 1998) is promised to extend the formalism to gauge theories and fermions.
