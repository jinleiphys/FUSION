# Entropy production in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We use experimentally measured identified particle spectra and Hanbury Brown-Twiss radii to determine the entropy per unit rapidity $dS/dy$ produced in $\sqrt{s} = 7$ TeV pp and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions. We find that $dS/dy = 11335 \pm 1188$ in 0-10% Pb-Pb, $dS/dy = 135.7 \pm 17.9$ in high-multiplicity pp, and $dS/dy = 37.8 \pm 3.7$ in minimum bias pp collisions and compare the corresponding entropy per charged particle $(dS/dy)/(dN_{\rm ch}/dy)$ to predictions of statistical models. Finally, we use the QCD kinetic theory pre-equilibrium and viscous hydrodynamics to model entropy production in the collision and reconstruct the average temperature profile at $τ_0 = 1$ fm/$c$ for high multiplicity pp and Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The authors use experimentally measured particle spectra and Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii to determine the entropy per unit rapidity in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, finding that the entropy per charged particle in central Pb-Pb collisions agrees with earlier results at RHIC energies, while the values for pp collisions are lower than statistical model predictions, though the deviations are within 1-2 sigma.

## Method

The entropy per unit rapidity is calculated using the Pal-Pratt method, which combines identified particle transverse momentum spectra with three-dimensional source radii from HBT correlations to reconstruct the phase-space density at kinetic freeze-out. The authors sum the entropy contributions from pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, xi and omega baryons, plus estimated contributions from unmeasured particles (neutrons, neutral kaons, eta, eta-prime, and sigma baryons) using Pythia 8.2 simulations with hadron yields at T = 156 MeV. For Pb-Pb collisions, the series expansion of the entropy integral is truncated at fourth order, while for pp collisions where pion phase-space density exceeds unity, an eighth-order polynomial approximation is used.

## Key numbers

* Total entropy per unit rapidity dS/dy = 11335 ± 1188 in 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
* Total entropy per unit rapidity dS/dy = 135.7 ± 17.9 in high-multiplicity pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
* Total entropy per unit rapidity dS/dy = 37.8 ± 3.7 in minimum bias pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
* Entropy per charged particle S/N_ch = 6.7 ± 0.8 in 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
* Entropy per charged particle S/N_ch = 5.4 ± 0.7 in high-multiplicity pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
* Entropy per charged particle S/N_ch = 5.2 ± 0.5 in minimum bias pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
* Initial entropy density s(tau_0) = 82.3 fm^{-3} at tau_0 = 1 fm/c for 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions, corresponding to temperature T(tau_0) ≈ 340 MeV
* Initial energy density e(tau_0) ≈ 13.9 GeV/fm^3 from Bjorken formula for 0-10% central Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the entropy determination method of Pal and Pratt (2003), who analyzed Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV and found dS/dy = 4451, to LHC energies in both Pb-Pb and pp collisions. The authors compare their measured entropy per charged particle to predictions from hadron resonance gas models by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2017) and Vovchenko and Stocker (2019), finding agreement within 1-2 sigma. The results enable reconstruction of initial temperature profiles at tau_0 = 1 fm/c using QCD kinetic theory pre-equilibrium evolution (Kurkela et al., 2018) and viscous hydrodynamics (Floerchinger et al., 2018), providing constraints on the early-time dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma.
