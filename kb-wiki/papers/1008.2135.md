# Heavy pseudoscalar mesons in a Schwinger-Dyson--Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The mass spectrum of heavy pseudoscalar mesons, described as quark-antiquark bound systems, is considered within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with momentum-dependent masses of the constituents. This dependence is found by solving the Schwinger-Dyson equation for quark propagators in rainbow-ladder approximation. Such an approximation is known to provide both a fast convergence of numerical methods and accurate results for lightest mesons. However, as the meson mass increases, the method becomes less stable and special attention must be devoted to details of numerical means of solving the corresponding equations. We focus on the pseudoscalar sector and show that our numerical scheme describes fairly accurately the $π$, $K$, $D$, $D_s$ and $η_c$ ground states. Excited states are considered as well. Our calculations are directly related to the future physics programme at FAIR.

# Heavy pseudoscalar mesons in a Schwinger-Dyson–Bethe-Salpeter approach

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder approximation to the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with only two effective parameters for the gluon propagator and three bare quark masses, reproduces the ground-state masses of pi, K, D, D_s, and eta_c mesons fairly accurately, but the method becomes less stable as meson mass increases and requires special numerical treatment for heavy states.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for quark propagators in Euclidean space using a rainbow-ladder approximation with a phenomenological gluon propagator consisting only of an infrared term parameterized by two parameters (omega and D). The quark propagator functions A(p) and B(p) are expanded in hyperspherical harmonics (Chebyshev polynomials) to reduce the four-dimensional integral equation to a system of one-dimensional integral equations solved by iteration. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons is then solved using the momentum-dependent quark masses obtained from the Schwinger-Dyson equation, with the vertex function expanded in an orthogonal set of basis functions and further decomposed into hyperspherical harmonics, yielding a system of homogeneous linear equations whose determinant zeros give bound-state masses. For heavy mesons (M > 1 GeV), the quark propagator must be evaluated at complex momenta, which is handled by solving the Schwinger-Dyson equation along a parabolic contour using Cauchy's theorem.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state masses obtained: pi^0 at 135 MeV, K at 497 MeV, D^pm at 1870 MeV, D_s^pm at 1970 MeV, eta_c at 2980 MeV
- Effective parameters used: omega = 0.5 GeV, D = 16 GeV^{-2}
- Bare quark masses: u/d = 0.005 GeV, s = 0.115 GeV, c = 1.0 GeV
- First excited u\bar{u} state found at 1080 MeV
- First excited c\bar{u} state found at approximately 2530 MeV
- The approach predicts an s\bar{s} "ghost" state around 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the combined Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter formalism, previously successful for light mesons (Roberts, Alkofer, and collaborators), to the charm sector including heavy pseudoscalar mesons and excited states. It employs the same two-parameter infrared gluon propagator ansatz from Roberts and uses the parabolic contour method of Fisher to handle complex momenta needed for heavy mesons. The results are directly relevant to the FAIR research programme (CBM and PANDA collaborations) and complement calculations by Krassnigg and Souchlas using similar approaches. The obtained partial Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes enable future calculations of decay processes, transition form factors, and in-medium meson properties relevant to experiments such as HADES.
