# Quantum binding energies in the Skyrme model

> A major problem in the Skyrme model is that the binding energy of skyrmions, which model nuclei, is too high by an order of magnitude. We show that the most popular solution to this problem, to construct models with zero classical binding energy, still produces large binding energies when spin energy is included. We argue that it is thus necessary to include quantum effects. We calculate the binding energy of skyrmions including the most simple quantum correction, that of vibrational modes in a harmonic approximation. We show that this can give physically reasonable results for nucleon numbers N=1-8 thanks to a remarkable cancellation between the strongly binding classical energy and the strongly unbinding zero-point energy from vibrational modes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even in "BPS Skyrme models" designed to have zero classical binding energy, the binding energy remains too large by an order of magnitude once spin energy from rigid-body quantization is included; only by including vibrational quantum corrections in a harmonic approximation can the Skyrme model produce binding energies of the correct order of magnitude (a few MeV per nucleon) for nucleon numbers N=1-8.

## Method

The authors quantize skyrmions by including both zero modes (translational, rotational, and isorotational) and vibrational normal modes in a harmonic approximation, using previously calculated vibrational spectra for N=1-8 at dimensionless pion mass m̃=1. The total energy of an N-skyrmion is the sum of its classical mass, spin/isospin energy from rigid-body quantization, and the zero-point energy of its 7N-9 vibrational modes (with a breathing mode for N=1). The parameters Fπ and e are calibrated by setting the 1-skyrmion mass equal to the neutron mass and minimizing the binding energy error for N=2-8.

## Key numbers

- The spin energy of the 1-skyrmion from rigid-body quantization is 73.5 MeV, which alone is an order of magnitude larger than the true binding energy of the alpha particle (7.1 MeV)
- The classical binding energy per nucleon is approximately 75 MeV, an order of magnitude too large
- With the harmonic quantization, the calibrated parameters are Fπ = 51.8 MeV and e = 2.42, giving a 1-skyrmion mass of 941 MeV
- The resulting binding energies per nucleon for N=2-8 are: 0.82 MeV (N=2), -0.57 MeV (N=3), 6.99 MeV (N=4), 7.22 MeV (N=5), 5.11 MeV (N=6), 7.88 MeV (N=7), 5.16 MeV (N=8), compared to experimental values of 1.11, 2.7, 7.08, 5.39, 5.33, 4.83, and 7.06 MeV respectively
- The dimensionless average vibrational frequencies ω̄_N range from 1.200 (N=1) down to 0.676 (N=8)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem first pointed out by Braaten (1988) that Skyrme model binding energies are an order of magnitude too large. Previous attempts to solve this problem focused on constructing BPS-type models with low classical binding energy, including modifications by Gillard (2015), Adam (2010), Sutcliffe (2010), and Gudnason (2020). The authors show that such classical solutions are insufficient because spin energy from the standard rigid-body quantization of Adkins (1983) and Manko (2007) still produces binding energies an order of magnitude too large. By including vibrational modes calculated by Gudnason (2018), the paper demonstrates that a remarkable cancellation between classical binding and zero-point vibrational energy can yield realistic results, opening the door to using vibrational spectra rather than classical binding energies for calibrating the Skyrme model and suggesting new approaches to problems in nuclear matter and neutron star crusts.
