# Comparison of elastic electron or positron scattering from proton-rich nuclei

> We investigate the cross sections of the elastic electron or positron scattering from $^{208}$Pb, $^{12}$C, $^{12,16}$O and $^{28, 32}$S by the relativistic partial-wave expansion method using the static charge density distribution from the self-consistent relativistic mean field model and also calculate the charge form factor for $^{12, 16}$O and $^{28, 32}$S. The numerical results are compared with the available data. Calculations indicate that the extended charge density distributions of $^{12}$O and $^{28}$S have observable effects on the cross sections of the electron or positron scattering as well as the charge form factors.

## Key claim

The combination of the relativistic partial-wave expansion method with the self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) model can predict elastic electron and positron scattering cross sections and charge form factors for stable nuclei, and the extended charge density distributions of the proton-rich nuclei $^{12}$O and $^{28}$S produce observable shifts in diffraction minima and amplitude deviations in cross sections and form factors compared to their stable isotopes $^{16}$O and $^{32}$S, but only at momentum transfers above a certain threshold.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic partial-wave expansion method to solve the Dirac equation for electrons and positrons scattering from a spherically symmetric static Coulomb potential derived from nuclear charge density distributions. The charge densities for $^{208}$Pb, $^{12}$C, $^{12,16}$O, and $^{28,32}$S were obtained from the self-consistent RMF model with NL-SH force parameters. Differential cross sections were computed from direct and spin-flip scattering amplitudes, and charge form factors were obtained by dividing the cross sections by the Mott cross section.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb and $^{12}$C, theoretical cross sections and the ratio $R = (d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^{+}} / (d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^{-}}$ were compared with experimental data from Breton (1991) and showed general agreement.
- For $^{16}$O and $^{32}$S, calculated electron scattering cross sections agreed with experimental data from Sick (1970) and Li (1974) in the range of low and moderate momentum transfer ($q \leq 3$ fm$^{-1}$), with deviations at higher momentum transfers.
- The first diffraction minimum of the form factor for $^{12}$O and the second minimum for $^{28}$S shift outward markedly compared to $^{16}$O and $^{32}$S, with the shifts and amplitude deviations large enough to be experimentally observable.
- The cross section differences $D(q) = [(d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^-} - (d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^+}] / [(d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^-} + (d\sigma/d\Omega)|_{e^+}]$ for stable and unstable isotopes coincide at low momentum transfers, indicating observability only above a certain momentum transfer value.

## Context

This work extends the application of the relativistic partial-wave expansion method, originally developed by Yennie (1954), and the RMF model, which has been shown by Gambhir (1990), Horowitz (1981), Ma (1994), and Ren (1999) to reproduce binding energies, separation energies, and charge radii, to proton-rich unstable nuclei. The calculations support theoretical predictions of proton halos in $^{26,27,28}$P by Ren (1996) and experimental evidence from Navin (1998), and they demonstrate that elastic electron or positron scattering at future facilities such as GSI/Germany (Haik, 2005) and RIKEN/Japan (Suda, 2004) can be used to study charge density distributions of proton drip-line nuclei.
