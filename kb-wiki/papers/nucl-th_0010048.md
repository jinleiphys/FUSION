# Covariant nucleon electromagnetic form factors from the Goldstone-boson-exchange quark model

> We present a study of proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors for the recently proposed Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model. Results for charge radii, magnetic moments, and electric as well as magnetic form factors are reported. The calculations are performed in a covariant framework using the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics. All the predictions by the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model are found in remarkably good agreement with existing experimental data.

## Key claim
The Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model, when combined with point-form relativistic quantum mechanics, predicts nucleon electromagnetic form factors in remarkably good agreement with experimental data without needing constituent quark form factors or other phenomenological parameters beyond the quark model wave functions. The hedge is that this agreement holds for the range of momentum transfers considered (up to about 1 GeV^2 for the dipole form), and that a small discrepancy remains in the magnetic moments.

## Method
The authors solved the three-quark Hamiltonian of the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model using the stochastic variational method to obtain nucleon wave functions in the center-of-momentum frame. They then calculated electromagnetic form factors in the point-form spectator approximation, applying Lorentz boosts to the wave functions via velocity states and using only one-body currents with pointlike constituent quarks (f1=1, f2=0). The calculations were performed in the standard Breit frame with momentum transfer only in the z-direction.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge radius squared: 0.75 fm^2 (PFSA prediction) vs experimental 0.774(27) fm^2 and 0.780(25) fm^2
- Neutron charge radius squared: -0.12 fm^2 (PFSA prediction) vs experimental -0.113(7) fm^2
- Proton magnetic moment: 2.64 nuclear magnetons (PFSA prediction) vs experimental 2.792847337(29) n.m.
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.67 n.m. (PFSA prediction) vs experimental -1.91304270(5) n.m.
- Nucleon mass with confinement only: 1353 MeV (compared to physical 939 MeV)
- Proton electric form factor matches dipole form for Q^2 less than about 1 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model of Glozman (1998) from baryon spectroscopy to electromagnetic observables, using the point-form relativistic quantum mechanics formalism developed by Klink (1998). It contrasts with previous relativistic studies by Cardarelli (1995), Szczepaniak (1995), and Coester (1997) that required constituent quark form factors to match experimental data. The results enable further studies of electromagnetic transitions and baryon resonance decays within the same framework.
