# Probing quark transversity GPDs in diffractive photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron

> Transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can be probed in diffractive electro- and photoproduction of two vector mesons on a hadron in kinematics where the two vector mesons are separated by a large rapidity gap. We report on calculations for this process in the case of coherent $ρ^0-ω$ meson production on a deuteron target. Our cross section results show that an electron-ion collider with deuteron beams and forward detectors could probe deuteron transversity GPDs.

# Probing quark transversity GPDs in diffractive photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron

## Key claim
Diffractive electro- and photoproduction of two vector mesons (specifically coherent ρ⁰-ω production) on a deuteron target can probe deuteron transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs), provided an electron-ion collider with deuteron beams and forward detectors is available. The cross section results are calculated at leading twist and leading order in the collinear approximation, and the target does not need to be polarized to access transversity GPDs.

## Method
The authors compute the differential cross section for the reaction γ⁽*⁾ D → ρ⁰_ L ω D' in kinematics where the two vector mesons are separated by a large rapidity gap, using QCD factorization at leading twist and leading order. The amplitude is written as a convolution of an impact factor for the photon-to-ρ⁰ transition (via two-gluon exchange, the Born approximation of the hard pomeron) and the amplitude of the pomeron-deuteron interaction ℙ D → ω D', where the deuteron GPDs enter. Deuteron GPDs are computed using a convolution model that separates nucleon and nuclear structure via light-front quantization, employing the AV18 deuteron radial wave functions, the PARTONS framework for nucleon vector GPDs (MMS13 model), and the Goloskokov-Kroll model for nucleon transversity GPDs.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross sections are evaluated at the minimum momentum transfer squared t_min = -0.33 GeV², with skewness ξ = 0.15.
- For longitudinally polarized ω mesons (probing deuteron vector GPDs), photoproduction cross sections are larger than electroproduction cross sections at all shown p_T² values.
- For transversely polarized ω mesons (probing deuteron transversity GPDs), cross sections are smaller than those for longitudinally polarized ω mesons at the same kinematics.
- The differential cross section dσ/(dt dp²_T dξ) is independent of total invariant mass s at leading twist and leading order.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of diffractive double vector meson production, first considered for double ρ production on the proton by Ivanov (2002) and Enberg (2006), to the case of coherent production on a deuteron target. It builds on the authors' prior introduction of transversity GPDs for spin-1 hadrons (Cosyn 2018) and studies of their polynomiality properties (Cosyn 2018). The results enable experimental searches for deuteron transversity GPDs at future electron-ion colliders (Boer 2011, Accardi 2012), though the basic convolution model used violates polynomiality of deuteron GPDs due to truncation of the Fock space, which the authors note requires future extension.
