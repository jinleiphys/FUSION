# Chiral Symmetry transition in the Linear Sigma Model with quarks: Counting effective QCD degrees of freedom from low to high temperature

> We use the linear sigma model coupled to quarks, together with a plausible location of the critical end point (CEP), to study the chiral symmetry transition in the QCD phase diagram. We compute the effective potential at finite temperature and density up to the contribution of the ring diagrams, both in the low and high temperature limits, and use it to compute the pressure and the position of the CEP. In the high temperature regime, by comparing to results from extrapolated lattice data, we determine the model coupling constants. Demanding that the CEP remains in the same location when described in the high temperature limit, we determine again the couplings and the pressure for the low temperature regime. We show that this procedure gives an average description of the lattice QCD results for the pressure and that the change from the low to the high temperature domains in this quantity can be attributed to the change in the coupling constants which in turn we link to the change in the effective degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq), when used with two different sets of coupling constants for the low- and high-temperature regimes, can reproduce the average behavior of lattice QCD results for the pressure, and the change in coupling constants between regimes can be interpreted as a change in the effective degrees of freedom from hadronic to partonic.

## Method

The authors computed the finite-temperature and finite-density effective potential for the LSMq up to the ring diagram order, separately in the high-temperature and low-temperature limits. In the high-temperature regime, the coupling constants (lambda and g) were determined by comparing to extrapolated lattice data and requiring a crossover transition at mu=0. In the low-temperature regime, the couplings were determined by demanding that the critical end point (CEP) location matches the one found in the high-temperature approximation. The pressure was then computed from the effective potential at v=0 in both regimes and compared to lattice QCD results for two light flavors.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for mu=0: T_c ~ 170 MeV (taken as input for two-flavor chiral limit, slightly larger than the N_f=2+1 lattice value)
- High-temperature coupling constants: lambda = 0.86, g = 1.11
- Low-temperature coupling constants: lambda = 2.4, g = 1.65 (with T_c/a = 1/2, corresponding to m_sigma ~ 540 MeV)
- CEP location from high-temperature approximation: within the region (mu_CEP/T_c, T_CEP/T_c) ~ (1.0-1.4, 0.9-0.95) as found by lattice-inspired calculations (Sharma)
- Pressure at low temperature: P/T^4 ~ 2 (average value up to the largest T/T_c reached)
- Pressure at high temperature: P/T^4 ~ 3 (average value)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the LSMq phase diagram (Ayala 2014, Ayala 2015) by explicitly computing the effective potential in both low- and high-temperature limits and linking the change in coupling constants to the change in effective degrees of freedom. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD calculations (HotQCD 2014) that are hindered by the sign problem at finite chemical potential, and with other effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL) and Polyakov-loop extended NJL model (pNJL). The results enable a consistent description of the chiral phase transition across a wide temperature range using a single effective model, and support the use of the LSMq as an analytical tool for locating the CEP in the QCD phase diagram.
