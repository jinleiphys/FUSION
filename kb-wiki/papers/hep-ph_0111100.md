# Pion Propagation near the QCD Chiral Phase Transition

> We point out that, in analogy with spin waves in antiferromagnets, all parameters describing the real-time propagation of soft pions at temperatures below the QCD chiral phase transition can be expressed in terms of static correlators. This allows, in principle, the determination of the soft pion dispersion relation on the lattice. Using scaling and universality arguments, we determine the critical behavior of the parameters of pion propagation. We predict that when the critical temperature is approached from below, the pole mass of the pion drops despite the growth of the pion screening mass. This fact is attributed to the decrease of the pion velocity near the phase transition.

## Key claim
The real-time dispersion relation of soft pions below the QCD chiral phase transition can be expressed entirely in terms of static (Euclidean) correlators measurable on the lattice, and the pion pole mass drops as the critical temperature is approached from below, even though the screening mass grows.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for soft pions at finite temperature, with separate temporal and spatial decay constants \(f_t\) and \(f_s\) due to the loss of Lorentz invariance. They match the partition function and its derivatives between the effective theory and QCD to relate the Lagrangian parameters to static correlators: the axial isospin susceptibility \(\chi_{I5}\) gives \(f_t\), the static pion correlator gives the screening mass \(m\) and decay constant \(f = f_s\), and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation links the condensate to \(m\) and \(f_s\). Using scaling and universality arguments for the O(4) sigma-model in three dimensions, they derive the critical exponents for \(f\), \(u\), \(m\), and the pole mass \(m_p\).

## Key numbers
- The O(4) critical exponent \(\eta \approx 0.03\) in \(d=3\) dimensions.
- The O(4) exponents \(\nu \approx 0.73\) and \(\beta \approx 0.38\) in \(d=3\).
- The pion velocity scales as \(u^2 \sim t^\nu\) with \(t = (T_c - T)/T_c\).
- The screening mass scales as \(m^2 \sim m_q\, t^{\beta - \nu}\) (growing as \(T \to T_c\)).
- The pole mass scales as \(m_p^2 \sim m_q\, t^\beta\) (dropping as \(T \to T_c\)).
- At \(T = T_c\), the screening mass scales as \(m^2 \sim m_q^{1 - (\nu - \beta)/\beta\delta}\) and the pole mass as \(m_p^2 \sim m_q^{1 + 1/\delta}\) with \(\delta \approx 5\).
- The estimated shift in the pion pole mass near \(T_c\) is \(\Delta m \approx -0.3\, m_\pi\), giving a pion multiplicity enhancement of roughly \(\exp(-\Delta m / T_{\rm ch}) \approx 1.3\) for a freezeout temperature \(T_{\rm ch} \sim 170\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the analogy between QCD and antiferromagnets, previously used for spin waves by Halperin and Hohenberg (1969), to the real-time propagation of pions. It contradicts the naive expectation that the pion mass increases uniformly near \(T_c\) by showing that the pole mass drops while the screening mass grows. The results enable lattice determination of the soft pion dispersion relation via static correlators and suggest observable consequences for heavy-ion collisions, such as enhanced low-momentum pion production and possible Cherenkov radiation. The approach builds on earlier effective Lagrangian studies by Pisarski and Tytgat (1996) and Shuryak (1990).
