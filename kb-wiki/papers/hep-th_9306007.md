# Transport Properties of Solitons

> We calculate in this article the transport coefficients which characterize the dynamics of solitons in quantum field theory using the methods of dissipative quantum systems. We show how the damping and diffusion coefficients of soliton-like excitations can be calculated using the integral functional formalism. The model obtained in this article has new features which cannot be obtained in the standard models of dissipation in quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that solitons in quantum field theory move as Brownian particles at low energies due to scattering off mesons, and that the damping and diffusion coefficients are temperature-dependent, unlike the standard oscillator model of quantum dissipation where the damping time is temperature-independent.

## Method

The authors quantize a scalar field theory in 1+1 dimensions with a degenerate potential using the collective coordinate method, obtaining a Hamiltonian where the soliton couples to mesonic modes through a momentum-dependent interaction. They then apply the Feynman-Vernon influence functional formalism to derive the reduced density operator for the soliton, treating the mesons as a thermal reservoir. Using the Born approximation for small soliton velocities and a Markovian approximation for times much longer than the meson correlation time, they extract the damping and diffusion coefficients from the resulting equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- The classical soliton mass is given by \( M = V[\phi_s] = \frac{1}{g^2} \int d^2x \left(\frac{d\phi_s}{dx}\right)^2 \)
- The damping function in the Markovian limit is \( \gamma(t) = \overline{\gamma}(T) \delta(t) \), where \( \overline{\gamma}(T) \) is temperature-dependent
- The diffusion coefficient in momentum space is \( D(t) = \hbar \frac{d^2 \Gamma_R}{dt^2} = -\hbar^2\theta(t) \sum^\infty_{n,m=1} g^2_{nm} \overline{n}_n(\Omega_n - \Omega_m)^2 \cos(\Omega_n - \Omega_m)t \)
- The thermal occupation number of mesons is \( \overline{n}_n = \left(e^{\beta \hbar \Omega_n} - 1\right)^{-1} \) with \( \beta = 1/(K_B T) \)

## Context

This work extends the quantum theory of solitons developed by Rajaraman (1982) and the dissipative quantum systems framework of Caldeira and Leggett (1983) by linking these two previously separate fields. The authors show that their model produces results that cannot be obtained from the standard oscillator model of dissipation, where the damping time is independent of temperature. The approach enables calculation of transport properties for soliton-like excitations from a microscopic perspective, and the authors note that the same Hamiltonian structure appears in polaron dynamics, referencing their own prior work on that system (Castro Neto and Caldeira, 1992).
