# Systematic study of the symmetry energy coefficient in finite nuclei

> The symmetry energy coefficients in finite nuclei have been studied systematically with a covariant density functional theory (DFT) and compared with the values calculated using several available mass tables. Due to the contamination of shell effect, the nuclear symmetry energy coefficients extracted from the binding energies have large fluctuations around the nuclei with double magic numbers. The size of this contamination is shown to be smaller for the nuclei with larger isospin value. After subtracting the shell effect with the Strutinsky method, the obtained nuclear symmetry energy coefficients with different isospin values are shown to decrease smoothly with the mass number $A$ and are subsequently fitted to the relation $\dfrac{4a_{\rm sym}}{A}=\dfrac{b_v}{A}-\dfrac{b_s}{A^{4/3}}$. The resultant volume $b_v$ and surface $b_s$ coefficients from axially deformed covariant DFT calculations are $121.73$ and $197.98$ MeV respectively. The ratio $b_s/b_v=1.63$ is in good agreement with the value derived from the previous calculations with the non-relativistic Skyrme energy functionals. The coefficients $b_v$ and $b_s$ corresponding to several available mass tables are also extracted. It is shown that there is a strong linear correlation between the volume $b_v$ and surface $b_s$ coefficients and the ratios $b_s/b_v$ are in between $1.6-2.0$ for all the cases.

## Key claim

After subtracting shell effects via the Strutinsky method, the symmetry energy coefficients extracted from covariant density functional theory (with the PC-PK1 interaction) decrease smoothly with mass number and can be fitted to a volume-plus-surface formula; the resulting volume and surface coefficients depend strongly on whether axial deformation is included, and the spherical calculation alone gives a volume coefficient (104.25 MeV) that is significantly lower than the empirical value for infinite nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1, solving the Dirac equation in a harmonic-oscillator basis with 20 shells for spherical and 16 shells for axially deformed calculations. Pairing correlations were treated in the BCS approximation with a density-independent delta force, and the center-of-mass correction was included microscopically. The symmetry energy coefficient for each nucleus was extracted from the binding-energy difference between isobars after subtracting the Coulomb energy (modeled as a uniformly charged droplet), and shell corrections were removed using the Strutinsky method. The resulting coefficients for nuclei with isospin values T = 2, 4, ..., 12 were then fitted to the relation 4a_sym/A = b_v/A - b_s/A^(4/3) using the Levenberg-Marquardt method.

## Key numbers

* Volume coefficient b_v = 121.73 MeV and surface coefficient b_s = 197.98 MeV from axially deformed covariant DFT calculations with PC-PK1.
* Ratio b_s/b_v = 1.63 from the axially deformed PC-PK1 calculation.
* Spherical PC-PK1 calculation gave b_v = 104.25 MeV and b_s = 132.38 MeV.
* All extracted ratios b_s/b_v from various mass tables and calculations lie between 1.6 and 2.0.
* A strong linear correlation between b_v and b_s was found: b_s = 3.28 b_v - 218 MeV.
* The empirical value for the volume coefficient of infinite nuclear matter is b_v = 128 ± 16 MeV (corresponding to a_sym = 32 ± 4 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy in finite nuclei using non-relativistic Skyrme energy functionals (Satula 2006) and earlier relativistic calculations with the PK1 interaction (Ban 2006), which did not include shell corrections or axial deformation. The authors show that after properly subtracting shell effects and including axial deformation, the covariant DFT with PC-PK1 yields a surface-to-volume ratio (1.63) consistent with the non-relativistic Skyrme result of Satula (2006), and that the linear correlation between b_v and b_s agrees with the findings of Dieperink (2007). The results enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy from finite-nucleus data and provide constraints for the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to neutron-star properties.
