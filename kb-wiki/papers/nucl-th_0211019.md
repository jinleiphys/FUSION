# Relativity in polarized electron scattering observables

> Coincidence scattering of polarized electrons from nuclei with polarization transfer to outgoing nucleons is studied within the context of relativistic mean field theory. Effects introduced by the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of the bound and scattered nucleon wave functions are analyzed for the polarized response functions and transferred polarization asymmetries. Results obtained by projecting out the negative-energy components are compared with the fully-relativistic calculation. The crucial role played by the relativistic dynamics in some spin-dependent observables is clearly manifested even for low/medium values of the missing momentum. Kinematical relativistic effects are also analyzed. A discussion of the factorization approach and the mechanism for its breakdown is also briefly presented.

## Key claim

Dynamical relativistic effects, specifically the enhancement of the lower components of Dirac wave functions for bound and scattered nucleons, significantly affect spin-dependent observables in polarized electron scattering even at low/medium missing momentum values (below 300 MeV/c), contrary to the expectation that such effects only matter at high missing momenta.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for the \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{N})B\) reaction using relativistic mean field theory to describe bound and ejected nucleon wave functions as Dirac solutions with scalar and vector potentials. They compared fully-relativistic results with calculations where negative-energy components were projected out, with the effective momentum approximation (EMA), and with non-relativistic calculations using a Pauli-reduced current operator, analyzing the transferred polarization responses and asymmetries for the \(1p_{1/2}\) and \(1p_{3/2}\) shells in \(^{16}\)O.

## Key numbers

- Kinematics used: momentum transfer \(q = 1\) GeV/c, energy transfer \(\omega = 440\) MeV, forward electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 23^\circ\).
- Fully-relativistic and positive-energy projected results do not differ appreciably for missing momentum values up to \(\sim 300\) MeV/c; deviations become visible for \(p > 300\) MeV/c.
- For the \(p_{1/2}\) shell, a difference between relativistic and projected results is observed at very small missing momentum values, attributed to the quantum number \(\overline{\ell}=0\) in the lower component of the bound state wave function.
- Two polarized responses, \(R^{TL'}_s\) and \(R^{T'}_l\), are very insensitive to dynamical and kinematical relativistic effects, while \(R^{TL'}_l\) and \(R^{T'}_s\) show clear sensitivity.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of relativistic effects in unpolarized \((e,e'p)\) reactions (Caballero 1998, Udias 1999, Udias 2001) and in polarized responses within the plane-wave limit (Crispin 2000) by incorporating final-state interactions through RDWIA. The results contradict the standard factorized PWIA approach and show that the spin-orbit coupling in the final state breaks down factorization completely for polarized observables, while the left-right asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) shows a richer structure in relativistic calculations that is lost in non-relativistic and EMA approaches. This enables future comparisons with experimental data from polarized \((e,e'\vec{N})\) experiments at intermediate energies.
