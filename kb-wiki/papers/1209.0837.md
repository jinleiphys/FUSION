# The magnetic form factor of the deuteron in chiral effective field theory

> We calculate the magnetic form factor of the deuteron up to O(eP^4) in the chiral EFT expansion of the electromagnetic current operator. The two LECs which enter the two-body part of the isoscalar NN three-current operator are fit to experimental data, and the resulting values are of natural size. The O(eP^4) description of G_M agrees with data for momentum transfers Q^2 < 0.35 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory (χEFT) calculation of the deuteron magnetic form factor G_M at order eP^4, which includes the first two-body isoscalar current contributions, agrees with experimental data for momentum transfers Q^2 < 0.35 GeV^2, provided two low-energy constants (LECs) are fitted to data; the expansion converges well for Q^2 < 0.25 GeV^2 when NNLO wave functions are used.

## Method

The authors computed the deuteron magnetic form factor G_M in the Breit frame using χEFT deuteron wave functions from NLO and NNLO potentials with various cutoffs, combined with the electromagnetic current operator expanded to O(eP^4). The two-body isoscalar current operator at this order contains a long-range one-pion-exchange term proportional to the LEC d̅_9 and a short-range contact term proportional to L_2; these two LECs were determined by fitting to the deuteron magnetic moment and to experimental G_M data up to momentum transfer |q| = 400 MeV. Matrix elements were evaluated via Monte Carlo integration with importance sampling, using 2730 sample points and 3000 runs to achieve better than 1% precision.

## Key numbers

- The O(eP^4) description of G_M agrees with data for momentum transfers Q^2 < 0.35 GeV^2.
- The chiral expansion for G_M converges well for Q^2 < 0.25 GeV^2 when NNLO wave functions are employed.
- The breakdown scale of the EFT expansion is estimated at |q| = 600 MeV, where the short-distance two-body contribution becomes equal in magnitude to the impulse-approximation result.
- Fitted values of d̅_9 range from -0.011 to 0.018 GeV^{-2} across different cutoff combinations at NLO and NNLO.
- Fitted values of L_2 range from 0.028 to 0.892 GeV^{-4} across different cutoff combinations at NLO and NNLO.
- The Monte Carlo integration achieved better than 1% precision throughout the momentum range 0–800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier χEFT calculations of deuteron form factors by Phillips (2003, 2006) and Walzl (2001), which studied the charge and quadrupole form factors G_C and G_Q at O(eP^4) but did not include the two-body isoscalar current contributions to G_M. The present calculation uses the two-nucleon current operators derived by Kolling (2009, 2011) and determines the two LECs d̅_9 and L_2 from data, complementing earlier work on magnetic moments by Song (2007). Together with the results of Phillips (2006), this provides a complete set of elastic electron-deuteron form factors at O(eP^4) in χEFT, enabling systematic tests of the theory against experimental data up to Q^2 ≈ 0.35 GeV^2.
