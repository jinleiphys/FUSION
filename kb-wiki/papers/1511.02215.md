# Operator analysis of effective spin-flavor interactions for L=1 excited baryons

> We match the non-relativistic quark model, with both flavor dependent and flavor independent effective quark-quark interactions, to the spin-flavor operator basis of the 1/Nc expansion for the L=1 non-strange baryons. We obtain analytic expressions for the coefficients of the 1/Nc operators in terms of radial integrals that depend on the shape and relative strength of the spin-spin, spin-orbit and tensor interactions of the model, which are left unspecified. We obtain several new, parameter-free relations between the seven masses and the two mixing angles that can discriminate between different spin-flavor structures of the effective quark-quark interaction. We discuss in detail how a general parametrization of the mass matrix depends on the mixing angles and is constrained by the assumptions on the effective quark-quark interaction. We find that, within the present experimental uncertainties, consistency with the best values of the mixing angles as determined by a recent global fit to masses and decays does not exclude any of the two most extreme possibilities of flavor dependent (independent) quark-quark interactions, as generated by meson (gluon) exchange interactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, within current experimental uncertainties, the mixing angles of L=1 non-strange excited baryons are consistent with both flavor-dependent (one-meson exchange) and flavor-independent (one-gluon exchange) effective quark-quark interactions, meaning neither extreme possibility is excluded by the mass data alone.

## Method

The authors match a non-relativistic quark model with general two-body spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions to the spin-flavor operator basis of the 1/N_c expansion for L=1 non-strange baryons. They compute matrix elements in a harmonic oscillator basis, leaving the radial dependence of the interactions unspecified, and derive analytic expressions for the 1/N_c operator coefficients in terms of radial integrals. The resulting mass matrix is then diagonalized to obtain parameter-free relations between the seven baryon masses and two mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- The seven masses considered (in MeV): N_{1/2}(1535) = 1535 ± 10, N'_{1/2}(1650) = 1658 ± 13, N_{3/2}(1520) = 1515 ± 5, N'_{3/2}(1700) = 1700 ± 50, N_{5/2}(1675) = 1675 ± 5, Δ_{1/2}(1620) = 1630 ± 30, Δ_{3/2}(1700) = 1710 ± 40 (from PDG 2014).
- The two mixing angle solutions from the OGE+OME relations R1 and R2: sol–A = (0.80 ± 0.32, 0.01 ± 0.21) and sol–B = (−0.04 ± 0.68, −0.23 ± 0.17) in radians.
- The best-fit mixing angles from a global fit to masses and decays (deUrreta 2013) are (0.49 ± 0.29, −0.13 ± 0.17) in radians.
- Accuracy of mass relations (in %): for sol–A, R1 = 0.00, R2 = 0.00, R3 = 1.89, R4 = 0.35, R5 = 0.52; for sol–B, R1 = 0.00, R2 = 0.00, R3 = 0.17, R4 = 0.15, R5 = 1.08.

## Context

This work extends the matching of quark model interactions to the 1/N_c operator expansion initiated by Pirjol and collaborators (2007, 2008) and the specific Isgur-Karl model analysis by Galeta (2009) and Pirjol (2010). It generalizes previous studies by including all three components of the quark-quark interaction (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor) with the most general spin-flavor structure. The results enable discrimination between one-gluon exchange and one-meson exchange interaction mechanisms in excited baryons, and the derived mass-angle relations can be tested against future lattice QCD calculations and more precise experimental data on baryon masses and decays.
