# Fully Perturbative Calculation of $nd$ Scattering to Next-to-next-to-leading-order

> We introduce a new technique to calculate perturbative corrections to neutron-deuteron ($nd$) scattering that does not require calculation of the full off-shell scattering amplitude. Its relation to the more familiar partial-resummation technique is explained. Also included is a calculation of the SD-mixing term that occurs at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory $\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$\xspace. Using the new technique with the SD-mixing term a complete strictly perturbative phase-shift analysis of $nd$ scattering is performed up to NNLO including eigen-phases and mixing angles. This is compared to potential model calculations and good agreement is found with the eigen-phases and some of the mixing angles at low energies.

# Fully Perturbative Calculation of $nd$ Scattering to Next-to-next-to-leading-order

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new technique for calculating perturbative corrections to neutron-deuteron scattering that avoids computing the full off-shell leading-order scattering amplitude, enabling a strictly perturbative phase-shift analysis up to next-to-next-to-leading-order in pionless effective field theory. The method works by absorbing higher-order contributions into the inhomogeneous term of an integral equation whose kernel remains the same at each order, provided that the lower-order amplitudes are already known.

## Method

The author develops a perturbative expansion in pionless effective field theory (EFT$_{\not{\pi}}$) for neutron-deuteron scattering, using an auxiliary-field (dibaryon) formalism with both spin-triplet and spin-singlet dibaryon propagators. At each order (LO, NLO, NNLO), the integral equation for the partial-wave scattering amplitude retains the same LO kernel, while the inhomogeneous term is built from previously calculated lower-order amplitudes and the appropriate order-by-order corrections to the dibaryon propagators. The integral equations are solved numerically using the Hetherington-Schick method with a contour rotation into the complex plane, and the doublet S-wave channel additionally requires three-body forces to render the kernel compact and fix the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding momentum: $\gamma_t = 45.7025$ MeV
- Deuteron effective range parameter (pole expansion): $\rho_t = 1.65$ fm
- Virtual-bound state binding momentum in ${}^{1}\!S_0$ channel: $\gamma_s = -7.8902$ MeV
- Residue about the virtual-bound state pole in ${}^{1}\!S_0$: $Z_s = 0.9015$
- Doublet S-wave neutron-deuteron scattering length: $a_{1/2} = 0.65$ fm
- Triton binding energy used to fit the three-body force $H_2$: $B_d = -8.48$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of pionless effective field theory calculations for three-nucleon systems, which previously relied on a partial-resummation technique (Bedaque et al., 1999; Gabbiani et al., 1999) that summed certain classes of diagrams to all orders and produced unphysical negative imaginary parts in the quartet S-wave phase shift above deuteron breakup threshold. The new fully perturbative method avoids these issues and is analogous to the approach used by Ji and Phillips (2012) for a trimer of Helium-4 atoms in short-range effective field theory, but is here applied to neutron-deuteron scattering including all partial waves up to G-waves. The results enable future strictly perturbative calculations of three-nucleon observables at higher orders without the numerical expense of computing the full off-shell LO amplitude.
