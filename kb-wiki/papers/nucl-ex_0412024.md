# The reaction 2H(p,pp)n in three kinematical configurations at E_p = 16 MeV

> We measured the cross sections of the $^2$H(p,pp)n breakup reaction at E$_p$=16 MeV in three kinematical configurations: the np final-state interaction (FSI), the co-planar star (CST), and an intermediate-star (IST) geometry. The cross sections are compared with theoretical predictions based on the CD Bonn potential alone and combined with the updated 2$π$-exchange Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (TM99'), calculated without inclusion of the Coulomb interaction. The resulting excellent agreement between data and pure CD Bonn predictions in the FSI testifies to the smallness of three-nucleon force (3NF) effects as well as the insignificance of the Coulomb force for this particular configuration and energy. The CST also agrees well whereas the IST results show small deviations between measurements and theory seen before in the pd breakup space-star geometries which point to possible Coulomb effects. An additional comparison with EFT predictions (without 3NF) up to order N$^3$LO shows excellent agreement in the FSI case and a rather similar agreement as for CD Bonn in the CST and IST situations.

## Key claim

The cross-section data for the $^2$H(p,pp)n breakup reaction at 16 MeV show excellent agreement with pure CD Bonn predictions in the np final-state interaction (FSI) and co-planar star (CST) configurations, but small deviations in the intermediate-star (IST) geometry that point to possible Coulomb effects; the theoretical calculations deliberately omitted the Coulomb interaction, so the agreement in FSI and CST may be fortuitous.

## Method

The authors measured kinematically complete cross sections for the $^2$H(p,pp)n breakup reaction at a proton laboratory energy of 16.0 MeV using unpolarized protons from a tandem Van de Graaff accelerator incident on solid deuterated polyethylene targets. Coincident protons were detected with silicon surface barrier detectors arranged in three specific geometries: np final-state interaction (FSI), co-planar star (CST), and intermediate-star (IST). The experimental cross sections were compared to Faddeev calculations in momentum space using the CD Bonn potential alone and combined with the updated Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (TM99'), as well as to chiral effective field theory (EFT) predictions up to order N$^3$LO, all without inclusion of the Coulomb interaction.

## Key numbers

- Monitor reaction elastic cross section at 16.0 MeV and $\Theta=30^\circ$: $(83.219 \pm 1.248)$ mb/sr, used for absolute normalization.
- Bin widths for S-curve projections: 200 keV for FSI, 400 keV for CST and IST.
- Typical relative statistical errors after background subtraction: between 1% and 3%.
- Finite-geometry correction in FSI peaks: at most 1.2% reduction in cross section.
- Effects of the TM99' three-nucleon force on cross sections: smaller than $\approx 2\%$ for FSI peaks, CST, and IST geometries; up to $\approx 10\%$ in the region between two np FSI peaks.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of three-nucleon breakup at low energies, particularly the nd space-star puzzle where modern NN forces give too small cross sections (Stewart 1989, Strate 1989, Gebhardt 1993, Setze 1996, Zhou 2001) and the pd space-star data that lie below nd predictions, suggesting Coulomb effects (Rauprich 1991, Grossmann 1996, Patberg 1996, Ishikawa 2003). The IST and CST configurations had not been explored in pd breakup at low energies prior to this measurement. The results enable future comparisons with EFT calculations that include consistent three-nucleon forces at N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO, which are stated to be underway.
