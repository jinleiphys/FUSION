# Resonant State in Helium-4 Lambda

> In a recent experiment E906 at the BNL-AGS, a search for light S=-2 hypernuclei, strong evidence was found for the nuclide Hydrogen-4 double Lambda. One of the most striking components of this data was the appearance of a narrow low-momentum pi- line at k(pi-) = 104-105 MeV/c. This was ascribed to the decay of Hydrogen-4 double Lambda into a resonant state in Helium-4 Lambda. The existence of such a state is shown to be plausible and its characteristics are delineated.

## Key claim

The narrow low-momentum π⁻ line at 104–105 MeV/c observed in the E906 experiment can be explained by a resonant state in ⁴_ΛHe, provided that the resonance arises naturally from a potential that also reproduces the known ground-state binding of ⁴_ΛHe; the existence of such a state is shown to be plausible rather than definitively proven.

## Method

The authors construct a surface Saxon-Woods potential to represent the average interaction between a proton and a ³_ΛH cluster, with parameters constrained to reproduce the 7.75 MeV proton separation energy in the ground state of ⁴_ΛHe. They then solve for p-wave resonances in this potential using a resonance code, varying the radius parameter and the ground-state binding energy to test stability. The resulting resonance energy and width are compared to the observed π⁻ momentum peak, and the ΛΛ pairing energy ΔB_ΛΛ is extracted from the kinematics of the decay chain.

## Key numbers

- The p-wave resonance appears at ε = 1.18 MeV with a width of 1.00 MeV for a potential with radius parameter r₀ = 1.40 fm and depth V₀ = 28.09 MeV.
- For r₀ = 1.45 fm, the resonance parameters are ε = 0.97 MeV and width 0.70 MeV (V₀ = 27.68 MeV).
- Fixing the proton separation energy at 7.18 MeV (averaged over the 0⁺,1⁺ ground-state doublet) with r₀ = 1.45 fm gives ε = 1.19 MeV and width 1.04 MeV (V₀ = 26.69 MeV).
- The ΛΛ pairing energy ΔB_ΛΛ is estimated as 0.34 MeV for r₀ = 1.40 fm (ε_R = 1.18 MeV) and 0.55 MeV for r₀ = 1.45 fm (ε_R = 0.97 MeV), both yielding a decay momentum centroid at k* = 104.5 MeV/c.
- The two-body decay to ⁴_ΛHe(1⁺) gives a predicted π⁻ momentum of 116.5 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the BNL-AGS experiment E906 (2003), which found evidence for ⁴_ΛΛH and a narrow π⁻ line at 104–105 MeV/c. The authors build on shell-model calculations by Kumagai-Fuse and Okabe (2002) for the weak decay of ⁴_ΛΛH, and contrast their extracted ΔB_ΛΛ (~0.3–0.6 MeV) with the larger value of 1.0 ± 0.38 MeV found by Takahashi et al. for ⁶_ΛΛHe. The results enable future experiments with better resolution to separate the predicted 116.5 MeV/c line from the known 114.3 MeV/c line of ³_ΛH decay, providing clearer evidence for ⁴_ΛΛH.
