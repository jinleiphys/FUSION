# Nucleon-nucleon interaction in a chiral SU(3) quark model revisited

> A dynamical investigation of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction by using the resonating group method (RGM) in a chiral SU(3) quark model has been revisited. The considered quark-quark interaction includes, besides the one-gluon exchange (OGE) and the phenomenological confinement potential, the nonet scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchanges derived from the spontaneous SU(3) chiral symmetry breaking. The physical consistency requirement that the wave functions of single baryons satisfy the minimums of the Hamiltonian has been strictly imposed in determination of the model parameters. The calculated masses of the octet and decuplet baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, and the NN scattering phase shifts up to a total angular momentum J=6 are in satisfactory agreement with the experiments.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that imposing the variational principle (requiring each single-baryon wave function to minimize its own Hamiltonian) in a chiral SU(3) quark model yields satisfactory agreement with experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering data and baryon masses, but only after the model parameters are refitted to simultaneously satisfy these constraints, a condition not enforced in prior quark-model studies.

## Method

The resonating group method (RGM) is applied to the six-quark nucleon-nucleon system within a chiral SU(3) quark model whose quark-quark interaction includes one-gluon exchange (OGE), a phenomenological confinement potential (tested in both linear and quadratic forms), and nonet scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchanges derived from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Unlike earlier calculations, the harmonic-oscillator size parameter \(b_u\) for each baryon is determined variationally by minimizing the baryon mass with respect to \(b_u\), and the nine adjustable model parameters (OGE couplings, confinement strengths, zero-point energies, and the sigma meson mass) are then fitted to simultaneously reproduce the octet and decuplet baryon ground-state masses, the deuteron binding energy, and the NN scattering phase shifts up to total angular momentum \(J=6\).

## Key numbers

* Baryon ground-state masses: all eight octet and decuplet states (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ, Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω) are reproduced exactly to their experimental values (e.g., N at 939 MeV, Δ at 1232 MeV, Ω at 1672 MeV) in both linear and quadratic confinement models.
* Deuteron binding energy: -2.215 MeV (linear confinement) and -2.218 MeV (quadratic confinement), compared to the experimental value of -2.224 MeV.
* Size parameter \(b_u\) for the nucleon: 0.474 fm (linear confinement) and 0.472 fm (quadratic confinement); for the Δ baryon: 0.642 fm (linear) and 0.588 fm (quadratic), showing significant variation across baryon species.
* OGE coupling constants: \(g_u = 1.06\) and \(g_s = 1.16\) (linear confinement); \(g_u = 0.98\) and \(g_s = 1.07\) (quadratic confinement).
* Sigma meson mass: 608 MeV (linear confinement) and 625 MeV (quadratic confinement).

## Context

This work revisits the chiral SU(3) quark model framework previously developed by Zhang (1997) and Dai (2003), correcting a key inconsistency: earlier studies used a single fixed size parameter for all baryons, which violated the variational principle for baryons other than the nucleon. By enforcing that each baryon's wave function minimizes its own Hamiltonian, a condition advocated by Ohta (1982) and Liu (1982), the authors obtain a parameter set that simultaneously describes single-baryon properties and NN scattering without introducing additional channels. This consistent treatment is intended to provide a more reliable foundation for extending the model to other baryon-baryon systems, particularly the search for dibaryons, which the authors state will be the subject of future work.
