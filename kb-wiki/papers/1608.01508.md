# Photoproduction of the scalar mesons $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ off the nucleon

> We investigate photoproduction of the scalar mesona off the nucleon, using the effective Lagrangians and the Regge approach. We first study $f_0(980)$ photoproduction, replacing the Feynman propagator with the Regge ones for the $ρ$-meson exchange in the $t$-channel. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the experimental data on the differential cross section. We then apply the same method to $f_0(500)$ or $σ$ photoproduction with the same parameters for the Regge propagator. Since the threshold energy of the $f_0(500)$ production is rather low, $N^*$ resonances, which can decay into the nucleon and two pions in the isoscalar and scalar state, can come into play in the $s$-channel. To examine the effects of the $N^*$ resonances, we set up two models to look into the respective contribution of the $N^*$ resonances. We find that in particular $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1680)$ play important roles in $f_0(500)$ photoproduction. We discuss the physical implications of the present results.

## Key claim

The authors model photoproduction of scalar mesons \(f_0(500)\) and \(f_0(980)\) off the nucleon using effective Lagrangians combined with a Regge approach, but the results for \(f_0(500)\) depend on the poorly known mass of that meson and on the choice of which \(N^*\) resonances are included, with the Roper resonance \(N(1440)\) and \(N(1680)\) playing important roles.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level Feynman amplitudes for \(\gamma p \to f_0 p\) using effective Lagrangians, replacing the \(t\)-channel \(\rho\)-meson Feynman propagator with a Regge propagator to describe data at higher photon energies (3.0–3.8 GeV). For \(f_0(980)\) photoproduction, only nucleon exchange in the \(s\)- and \(u\)-channels plus the Reggeized \(\rho\) exchange are used; for \(f_0(500)\) photoproduction, \(N^*\) resonances that decay into \((\pi\pi)_{S\text{-wave}}^{I=0}N\) are added in the \(s\)-channel, with two models: Model I includes only spin-1/2 resonances (\(N(1440)\), \(N(1535)\), \(N(1650)\), \(N(1710)\)), while Model II additionally includes spin-3/2 and spin-5/2 resonances (\(N(1520)\), \(N(1675)\), \(N(1680)\)). Coupling constants are fixed from known decay widths and helicity amplitudes from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- \(f_0(980)\) photoproduction differential cross section is compared to CLAS data at photon energies \(E_\gamma = 3.0\)–\(3.8\) GeV and momentum transfer squared \(-t\) between \(0.4\) and \(1.0\) GeV\(^2\).
- The Regge trajectory for the \(\rho\) meson is \(\alpha_\rho(t) = 0.55 + 0.8t\).
- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho NN} = 3.25\) and \(\kappa_\rho = 6.1\) are used for the \(\rho NN\) vertex.
- The \(f_0(980)NN\) coupling constant is taken as \(g_{f_0(980)NN} = 5.8\).
- The \(f_0(500)NN\) coupling constant is taken as \(g_{f_0(500)NN} = 8.46\) (from the full Bonn potential with \(m_{\sigma'} = 550\) MeV).
- The photocoupling constant for \(\rho\)-meson exchange is \(g_{\gamma f_0(500) \rho} = 0.25\) (from SND Collaboration) and \(g_{\gamma f_0(980) \rho} \approx 0.21\) (from molecular \(K\bar{K}\) model).
- Strong coupling constants for \(N^* \to f_0 N\) decays are determined: \(g_{f_0NN(1440)} = \pm 3.88\), \(g_{f_0NN(1535)} = \pm 2.55\), \(g_{f_0NN(1650)} = \pm 0.96\), \(g_{f_0NN(1710)} = \pm 0.15\), \(g_{f_0NN(1520)} = \pm 0.85\), \(g_{f_0NN(1675)} = \pm 9.85\), \(g_{f_0NN(1680)} = \pm 2.29\).
- Photocoupling constants for \(N^*\) resonances are listed: for \(N(1440)\), \(f_1 = 0.47\); for \(N(1535)\), \(f_1 = 0.81\); for \(N(1650)\), \(f_1 = 0.28\); for \(N(1710)\), \(f_1 = -0.24\); for \(N(1520)\), \(f_1 = 4.63\), \(f_2 = -4.92\); for \(N(1675)\), \(f_1 = -1.34\), \(f_2 = -2.28\); for \(N(1680)\), \(f_1 = 15.24\), \(f_2 = -13.47\).

## Context

This work extends the study of scalar meson photoproduction beyond earlier analyses such as Donnachie (2015), who applied a Regge approach to \(f_0(980)\) photoproduction with a different parameter set, and is motivated by CLAS data on \(\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p\) in the \(S\)-wave (Battaglieri 2008, 2009). The results enable future coupled-channel calculations of \(\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p\) that include the \(K\bar{K}\) channel and the full correlated \(\pi\pi\) amplitude, which are necessary for a quantitative understanding of scalar meson production.
