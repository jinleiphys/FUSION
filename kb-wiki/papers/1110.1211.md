# Collisional energy losses of heavy quarks in relativistic nuclear collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We investigate the energy losses of $b$ and $c$ quarks at hard collisions with gluons and light quarks in quark-gluon plasma produced in central $Au + Au$ collisions at RHIC energy($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV). As the coupling constant at hard collision in the perturbative QCD is limited by Landau pole at momenta $Q^{2} = Λ^{2}$, we use the analytic models of QCD, where the Landau pole is absent. We calculate the energy losses of $b$ and $c$ quarks in analytic model of QCD at $Q^{2} \ge Λ^{2}$. At calculations we use the effective quasiparticle model. We use at calculations of total nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{b+c}$ the fragmentation of $b$ and $c$ quarks into heavy hadrons with accounting theirs energy losses. We show that this factor gives the considerably suppression of heavy $B$ and $D$ mesons in the region of middle meanings of $p_{\perp}$. The experimental data \cite{2} show, that nonphotonic electrons have the analogous suppression in the same region of $p_{\perp}$.

# Collisional energy losses of heavy quarks in relativistic nuclear collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{b+c}$ for heavy $B$ and $D$ mesons in central $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV) can be described using collisional energy losses of $b$ and $c$ quarks computed with an analytic QCD running coupling that avoids the Landau pole at $Q^2 = \Lambda^2$, provided the lower momentum-transfer boundary for hard collisions is taken as $|t| \ge \Lambda^2$ rather than the perturbative region.

## Method

The author calculated collisional energy losses of $b$ and $c$ quarks scattering with gluons and light quarks in an expanding quark-gluon plasma, using the analytic invariant charge $\alpha_{an}^{(1)}(q^2)$ from the one-loop analytic QCD model of Shirkov and Solovtsov, which has no Landau pole. The plasma evolution was modeled with an effective quasiparticle model using thermal masses $m_g(T)$ and $m_q(T)$ determined by a two-loop effective coupling $G^2(T,T_c)$, with initial temperature $T_0 = 410.6$ MeV and initial time $\tau_0 = 0.405 m_\pi^{-1}$ at RHIC energy. The total nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{b+c}$ was computed by convolving initial $b$ and $c$ quark distributions from FONLL calculations with the energy-loss-modified Peterson fragmentation functions (with parameters $\epsilon_b = 0.002$, $\epsilon_c = 0.02$) and including the measured $B$ decay contribution of approximately 50% to non-photonic electrons.

## Key numbers

- Initial plasma temperature: $T_0 = 410.6$ MeV; initial time: $\tau_0 = 0.405 m_\pi^{-1}$ for central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Heavy quark masses used: $M_b = 4.75$ GeV, $M_c = 1.3$ GeV
- Summary energy loss for $b$ quark from $bg$ collisions: $(\Delta E/E)_{tot}^{b} = 0.112$; for $c$ quark from $cg$ collisions: $(\Delta E/E)_{tot}^{c} = 0.357$ (at $p_{b,c} = 8$ GeV)
- Summary energy loss for $b$ quark from $bq$ collisions: $(\Delta E/E)_{tot}^{b} = 0.097$; for $c$ quark from $cq$ collisions: $(\Delta E/E)_{tot}^{c} = 0.309$ (at $p_{b,c} = 8$ GeV)
- Nuclear modification factor at $p_{\perp}^{H} = 6.9$ GeV/c: $R_{AA}^{b+c} = 0.281$ (including both gluon and light quark collisions)
- Nuclear modification factor values for $p_{\perp}^{H}$ from 5.5 to 8.5 GeV/c range from $R_{AA}^{Q(g+q)} = 0.235$ (at 5.5 GeV/c) to $R_{AA}^{Q(g+q)} = 0.335$ (at 8.5 GeV/c)
- QCD scale constant: $\Lambda = 200$ MeV
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: $\epsilon_b = 0.002$, $\epsilon_c = 0.02$
- $B$ decay contribution to non-photonic electrons: $e_B/(e_B + e_D) = 0.52 \pm 0.03$ at $p_{\perp} \ge 5$ GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the approach of collisional energy loss calculations for heavy quarks in QGP (following the quasiparticle model framework of earlier works by the author and collaborators) by replacing the perturbative QCD running coupling with an analytic model that removes the Landau pole singularity. The calculation uses the analytic invariant charge model of Shirkov and Solovtsov (2000s) and compares the resulting nuclear modification factor with experimental data from the STAR collaboration (2000s) for non-photonic electrons in central $Au+Au$ collisions. The results enable future comparisons with other energy-loss models and fragmentation functions, and the author notes plans for analogous calculations with alternative fragmentation parameterizations.
