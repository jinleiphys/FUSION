# Causality of the Einstein-Israel-Stewart Theory with Bulk Viscosity

> We prove that Einstein's equations coupled to equations of Israel-Stewart-type, describing the dynamics of a relativistic fluid with bulk viscosity and nonzero baryon charge (without shear viscosity or baryon diffusion) dynamically coupled to gravity, are causal in the full nonlinear regime. We also show that these equations can be written as a first-order symmetric hyperbolic system, implying local existence and uniqueness of solutions to the equations of motion. We use an arbitrary equation of state and do not make any simplifying symmetry or near-equilibrium assumption, requiring only physically natural conditions on the fields. These results pave the way for the inclusion of bulk viscosity effects in simulations of gravitational-wave signals coming from neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The paper proves that Einstein's equations coupled to Israel-Stewart-type equations for a relativistic fluid with bulk viscosity and nonzero baryon charge (without shear viscosity or baryon diffusion) are causal in the full nonlinear regime, provided the condition $\left[\frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi} + n \left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial n}\right)_\varepsilon\right]\frac{1}{\varepsilon+P+\Pi} \leq 1- \left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial \varepsilon}\right)_n$ holds. This condition is a nonlinear generalization of the known linear stability condition around equilibrium.

## Method

The authors analyze the principal part of the Einstein-Israel-Stewart system, consisting of energy-momentum conservation equations, a bulk scalar relaxation equation of the form $\tau_\Pi u^\alpha \nabla_\alpha \Pi + \Pi + \lambda \Pi^2+ \zeta \nabla_\alpha u^\alpha=0$, baryon number conservation, and Einstein's equations. They compute the characteristic speeds of the system and show they are bounded by the speed of light under the stated condition, establishing causality. They then rewrite the fluid equations as a first-order symmetric hyperbolic (FOSH) system by constructing symmetric matrices $\mathcal{A}^\mu$ with $\mathcal{A}^0$ positive definite, which implies local existence and uniqueness of solutions via standard theorems.

## Key numbers

- The causality condition involves the ratio $\zeta/\tau_\Pi$ (bulk viscosity over relaxation time), the energy density $\varepsilon$, the equilibrium pressure $P$, the bulk scalar $\Pi$, the baryon density $n$, and the partial derivatives $(\partial P/\partial \varepsilon)_n$ and $(\partial P/\partial n)_\varepsilon$; no specific numerical values are given for these quantities.
- The nuclear saturation density is cited as approximately $0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ as a reference for the density regime of neutron star mergers.
- The typical time scale for gravitational wave damping in neutron star mergers is stated as approximately ten milliseconds, against which viscous transport time scales were previously compared.

## Context

This work extends the known linear stability and causality results for Israel-Stewart theory around equilibrium (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1983, 1985; Denicol et al., 2008; Pu et al., 2009) to the full nonlinear regime, including dynamical coupling to gravity. It also provides the first FOSH formulation and existence/uniqueness proof for such theories in the nonlinear regime. The results enable the inclusion of bulk viscosity effects in numerical simulations of neutron star mergers, which had been identified as necessary by Alford et al. (2017) but previously impossible due to the lack of a causal formulation. The methods apply to generalized Israel-Stewart theories from Denicol et al. (2012) and Baier et al. (2007), and the authors note that the case considered here is the first example of a relativistic viscous fluid theory satisfying causality, local existence, uniqueness, linear stability, and microscopic derivability simultaneously.
