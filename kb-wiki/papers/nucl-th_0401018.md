# A Possible Extension of a Trial State in the TDHF Theory with Canonical Form in the Lipkin Model

> With the aim of the extension of the TDHF theory in the canonical form in the Lipkin model, the trial state for the variation is constructed, which is an extension of the Slater determinant. The canonicity condition is imposed to formulate the variational approach in the canonical form. A possible solution of the canonicity condition is given and the zero-point fluctuation induced by the uncertainty principle is investigated. As an application, the ground state energy is evaluated.

## Key claim
The paper constructs an extended trial state for the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory in the Lipkin model that goes beyond the Slater determinant, but only for the specific case where the canonicity condition admits a particular solution; the ground state energy is evaluated under the additional imposition of a minimum uncertainty relation, not from the variational principle alone.

## Method
The authors start from the Lipkin model with 2Ω fermions in two levels and represent the Hamiltonian using quasi-spin operators. They first construct a Slater determinant as an su(2)-coherent state, then extend it to a quasi-spin squeezed state by applying an exponential operator exp(B B*²/2) to the Slater determinant, analogous to boson squeezed states. The canonicity condition is imposed to define canonical variables (X*,X) and (Y*,Y), and a specific solution is derived for the parameters A and B in terms of these variables. The ground state energy is then calculated by imposing the minimum uncertainty relation (ΔQ·ΔP = ħ/2 |⟨R⟩|) to determine the squeezed variable n_Y, using two different methods: Method 1 determines n_X from energy minimization then n_Y from uncertainty; Method 2 determines n_Y from uncertainty first then minimizes energy with respect to n_X.

## Key numbers
- The system size is Ω = 8 (2Ω = 16 fermions) with single-particle energy splitting ε = 1.
- The ground state energy is evaluated as a function of the dimensionless coupling χ' (where χ' = χ/ε), with the phase transition point at χ' = 1.
- The expectation value ⟨N⟩ for the squeezed state is 2X*X + 4Y*Y, and ⟨1 - N/2Ω⟩ = 1 - X*X/Ω - 2Y*Y/Ω.
- The square of the quasi-spin for the squeezed state satisfies ⟨S_x²⟩ + ⟨S_y²⟩ + ⟨S_z²⟩ = Ω(Ω+1) = 72 for Ω=8.
- The normalization factor Ψ(B*B) for the squeezed state is given by a series: Ψ(B*B) = 1 + Σ_{n=1}^{2Ω} (2n-1)!!/(2^n·n!) · Π_{k=1}^{2n-1} (1 - k/2Ω) (B*B)^n.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quasi-spin squeezed state approach to the Lipkin model (Tsue, Yamamura, and Kuriyama, 1996) by formulating it within the canonical TDHF framework, which was originally developed for Slater determinants by Yamamura and Kuriyama (1987) and by Marumori, Maskawa, Sakata, and Kuriyama (1980). The squeezed state approach reproduces the exact ground state energy near the phase transition point better than the standard Slater determinant (Hartree-Fock) approximation, and the results are compared with exact eigenvalues. The method enables future time-dependent studies of nuclear many-body dynamics beyond the TDHF approximation, incorporating zero-point fluctuations from the uncertainty principle.
