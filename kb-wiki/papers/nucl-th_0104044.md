# Signature inversion in axially deformed $^{160,162}$Tm

> The microscopic analysis of experimental data in $^{160,162}$Tm is presented within the two-quasiparticle-phonon model. The model includes the interaction between odd quasiparticles and their coupling with core vibrations. The coupling explains naturally the attenuation of the Coriolis interaction in rotating odd-odd nuclei. It is shown that the competition between the Coriolis and neutron-proton interactions is responsible for the signature inversion phenomenon.

## Key claim

The signature inversion in axially deformed \(^{160,162}\)Tm is explained by the competition between the Coriolis interaction and the neutron-proton interaction between odd quasiparticles, with the coupling of quasiparticles to core vibrations providing a natural attenuation of the Coriolis interaction without requiring an ad hoc attenuation factor.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic two-quasiparticle-plus-phonon-plus-rotor model for axially deformed nuclei. They first solved random phase approximation (RPA) equations to determine the structure and energies of two-quasiparticle phonons describing low-lying vibrational states of the even-even core, then solved the variational problem for the intrinsic Hamiltonian to obtain amplitudes of neutron-proton two-quasiparticle components and their coupling with core vibrations, and finally diagonalised the full Hamiltonian coupling intrinsic and rotational terms via the Coriolis interaction. The single-particle mean field was approximated by the Nilsson Hamiltonian, and the residual neutron-proton interaction was taken as a delta force with parameters fitted from systematics of Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting and Newby shifts for rare-earth nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium \(\gamma\) deformation for \(^{160,162}\)Tm is very small, \(\gamma \sim 4^\circ\), obtained from self-consistent cranking Hartree-Fock with Skyrme III interaction.
- The quadrupole momentum \(Q_{20}\) changes from 1314 fm\(^2\) to 1268 fm\(^2\) in \(^{160}\)Tm and from 1399 fm\(^2\) to 1375 fm\(^2\) in \(^{162}\)Tm over rotational frequencies 0.1–0.25 MeV.
- The optimal inertial parameter \(\hbar^2/2\mathcal{J}\) is 11.2 keV for the ground rotational band and approximately 11 keV for all other bands.
- The calculated Newby shift \(\Delta E^{(N)}\) for the \(K^\pi=0^-(\pi 1/2[411]\otimes\nu 1/2[530])\) band in \(^{160}\)Tm is 120 keV, close to the optimal fitted value of 135 keV.
- The calculated Newby shift \(\Delta E^{(N)}\) for \(^{162}\)Tm is approximately 100 keV, slightly larger than the fitted value of 88 keV.
- The signature inversion point is reproduced at \(I\sim 18\hbar\) in \(^{160}\)Tm and at \(I\sim 16\hbar\) in \(^{162}\)Tm.
- The neutron-proton interaction parameters used are \(u_0 = -3.504\) MeV and \(u_1 = -0.876\) MeV.
- The phonon components reduce the strength of the Coriolis interaction by approximately 25%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic analysis of signature inversion in odd-odd nuclei of the \(A\sim 160\) region, specifically addressing the \(\pi(h_{11/2})\otimes\nu(i_{13/2})\) band studied systematically in earlier work (Author 1990s). It contradicts the prediction from the Cranked Shell Model (Bengtsson 1980s) that signature inversion requires triaxiality and is confined to \(62<Z<70\), since the authors find signature inversion in axially deformed nuclei with \(Z=69\). The mechanism differs from that in \(^{72}\)Br (Pleiter 1990s) where triaxiality is responsible. The work enables future studies of electromagnetic transitions to probe phonon components in excited states of odd-odd nuclei and their role in signature inversion.
