# Electromagnetic triangle anomaly and neutral pion condensation in QCD vacuum

> We study the QCD vacuum structure under the influence of an electromagnetic field with a nonzero second Lorentz invariant $I_2=\vec{E}\cdot{\vec B}$. We show that the presence of $I_2$ can induce neutral pion ($π^0$) condensation in the QCD vacuum through the electromagnetic triangle anomaly. Within the frameworks of chiral perturbation theory at leading small-momenta expansion as well as the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at leading $1/N_c$ expansion, we quantify the dependence of the $π^0$ condensate on $I_2$. The stability of the $π^0$-condensed vacuum against the Schwinger charged pair production due to electric field is also discussed.

# Electromagnetic triangle anomaly and neutral pion condensation in QCD vacuum

## Key claim
The presence of a nonzero second Lorentz invariant I2 = E·B can induce neutral pion (π0) condensation in the QCD vacuum through the electromagnetic triangle anomaly, but this condensed vacuum is only physically meaningful when the electric field is weak enough that Schwinger pair production of charged pions is strongly suppressed.

## Method
The authors study the QCD vacuum under parallel electric and magnetic fields using two complementary frameworks: chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at leading order in the small-momenta expansion including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at leading order in the 1/Nc expansion (mean-field approximation). They minimize the effective potential to obtain the π0 condensate as a function of I2, and analyze the stability of the resulting vacuum against Schwinger pair production of charged pions.

## Key numbers
- The π0 condensate for |I2| < I2c is given by π0/m* = Nc/(4π2 fπ2 mπ2) (qu2 - qd2) E·B, where qu = 2e/3 and qd = -e/3 are the u and d quark charges
- The critical value I2c = 4π2 fπ2 mπ2 / [Nc(qu2 - qd2)] above which the condensate is entirely in the π0 direction
- In the NJL model, when I2^(1/4) ≳ 0.28 GeV, the chiral symmetry breaking is driven solely by the π0 condensate
- The charged pion mass in the presence of a magnetic field becomes mπ±2 = mπ2 + |eB|
- The stability condition for the π0-condensed vacuum is |eE| ≪ mπ2 + |eB|

## Context
This work extends studies of QCD vacuum modification by electromagnetic fields, particularly the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking (Gusynin 1994, Gusynin 1995, Miransky 2015) and the weakening of chiral symmetry breaking by electric fields (Klevansky 1989, Babansky 1997, Cohen 2007). The neutral pion condensation proposed here differs from disoriented chiral condensates (Mohanty 2005) which are far-from-equilibrium transient phenomena, while this is a near-equilibrium stationary state. The results may be testable in lattice QCD using strategies similar to those used at finite baryon chemical potential (D'Elia 2012), and could have implications for heavy-ion collisions where large I2 with opposite signs above and below the reaction plane may exist (Deng 2012).
