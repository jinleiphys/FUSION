# Examination of the adiabatic approximation for $(d,p)$ reactions

> Background: Deuteron-induced one-neutron transfer reactions have been used to extract single-particle properties of nuclei, and the adiabatic (AD) approximation is often used to simply treat the deuteron breakup states. Purpose: The primary goal is to examine the validity of the AD approximation for the ($d,p$) reaction systematically. We clarify also the role of the closed-channels often ignored in the description of breakup reactions. Methods: We calculate the ($d,p$) cross sections with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for 128 reactions systems and compare the results with those obtained by the CDCC calculation with the AD approximation. Effect of the closed channels are investigated by ignoring the closed channels in CDCC. Results: The AD approximation affects in general the ($d,p$) cross section by less than 20 %, but some exceptional nonadiabatic cases for which the AD approximation breaks down are found. The closed channels turn out to give significant effects on the cross section at deuteron energies less than about 10 MeV. Conclusions: The use of the AD approximation in the description of the ($d,p$) reaction can be justified in many cases, with the uncertainty of less than about 20 %. The existence of some nonadiabatic cases nevertheless should be realized. The neglect of the closed channels without confirming the convergence of the CDCC result is not recommended.

## Key claim

The adiabatic (AD) approximation affects the ($d,p$) cross section by less than 20% in general and by about 35% at most, but some exceptional nonadiabatic cases exist where the approximation clearly breaks down; the validity depends on the incident deuteron energy, the neutron separation energy of the residual nucleus, and the target Coulomb barrier height.

## Method

The authors calculated ($d,p$) differential cross sections using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for 128 reaction systems, varying four target nuclei (20Ne, 40Ca, 100Zr, 200Hg), four incident deuteron energies (5, 10, 20, 40 MeV), four transferred angular momenta (ℓf = 0, 1, 2, 3), and two neutron separation energies (Sn = 0.1 and 8.0 MeV). They compared the full CDCC results with those from CDCC where the adiabatic approximation was imposed by setting all p-n continuum state energies equal to the deuteron ground-state energy (CDCC-AD). The effect of closed channels was investigated by repeating CDCC calculations with the maximum p-n linear momentum kmax determined by the incident deuteron energy, thereby excluding closed channels.

## Key numbers

- The AD approximation affects the ($d,p$) cross section by less than 20% in general, and by about 35% at most.
- For 40Ca(d,p)41Ca(2s1/2) at Ed = 40 MeV with Sn = 0.1 MeV, the deuteron elastic breakup cross section with CDCC-AD was 107 mb and with CDCC was 73 mb.
- Closed channels give significant effects on the cross section at deuteron energies less than about 10 MeV.
- The adiabatic factor SAD for 100Zr(d,p)101Zr(2s1/2) at Ed = 5 MeV with Sn = 0.1 MeV was 0.96.
- The adiabatic factor SAD for 40Ca(d,p)41Ca(2s1/2) at Ed = 40 MeV with Sn = 8 MeV was 0.87.
- For 40Ca(d,p)41Ca(2s1/2) at Ed = 5 MeV with Sn = 0.1 MeV, SAD was 1.08 (marked *3, indicating shape deviation despite SAD near unity).

## Context

This work systematically extends previous numerical tests of the adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) performed by Nunes and Deltuva (2011), Upadhyay et al. (2012), and Pang and Mukhamedzhanov (2014) for a few reaction systems, by examining 128 systems covering a range of targets, energies, and separation energies. The study also builds on the finding by Ogata and Yoshida (2016) that closed channels are crucial for accurate deuteron breakup cross sections at low energies. The results enable practitioners to assess when the AD approximation is reliable for extracting single-particle properties from ($d,p$) reactions and warn against neglecting closed channels without convergence checks.
