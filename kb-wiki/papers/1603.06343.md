# Anomalous Hyperfine Structure of the $^{229}$Th Ground-State Doublet in Muonic Atom

> The hyperfine splitting of the ground and low-energy $3/2^+(7.8 \pm 0.5$ eV) levels in the $^{229}$Th nucleus in muonic atom ($μ^-_{1S_{1/2}}{}^{229}$Th$)^*$ has been calculated considering the distribution of the nuclear magnetization in the framework of collective nuclear model with the wave functions of the Nilsson model for the unpaired neutron. It is shown that (a) the hyperfine splitting of the $3/2^+$ isomeric state is anomalously weak, and the reduction of hyperfine structure in comparison with the model of a point nuclear magnetic dipole is close to 100\%, (b) partial inversion of levels of the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet and spontaneous decay of the ground state with excitation of the isomeric state take place, (c) the energy of the isomeric transition lies in the range of 120--140 eV, which makes possible the direct observation of the transition, registration of conversion electrons and measurement of the nuclear matrix element.

## Key claim

The hyperfine splitting of the isomeric $3/2^+$ state in a muonic $^{229}$Th atom is anomalously weak, with a reduction close to 100% compared to the point-dipole model, and partial inversion of the ground-state doublet sublevels occurs, leading to spontaneous decay of the ground state to the isomeric state; however, these effects depend critically on the intrinsic $g_K$ factor and the reduced $M1$ transition probability, which have significant uncertainties.

## Method

The magnetic hyperfine (MHF) splitting of the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet in a muonic atom ($\mu^-_{1S_{1/2}}{}^{229}$Th$)^*$ was calculated by solving the Dirac equations for the muon wave function in the field of a deformed thorium nucleus with a Fermi-shaped proton density. The interaction energy between the muon current and the distributed nuclear magnetization was computed using the collective nuclear model with Nilsson-model wave functions for the unpaired neutron in the ground $5/2^+[633]$ and isomeric $3/2^+[631]$ states, including the Bohr-Weisskopf effect and mixing of $F=2$ sublevels via the dynamic nuclear-size (penetration) effect.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field at the center of the $^{229}$Th nucleus from the muon: about 23 GT (from Fermi contact interaction).
- Ground-state magnetic moment: $\mu_{gr}=0.45$ (in nuclear magnetons); isomeric-state magnetic moment: $\mu_{is}=-0.076$.
- Reduction of MHF structure compared to point-dipole model: about 56% for the $5/2^+(0.0)$ ground state.
- Mixing matrix element between $F=2$ sublevels: $\mathcal{E} \simeq 150$ eV.
- Reduced probability of the nuclear isomeric transition: $B_{W.u.}(M1;3/2^+\rightarrow{}5/2^+) = 3.0\times10^{-2}$ in Weisskopf units.
- Mixing coefficient $b \simeq 0.47$ for the $F=2$ sublevels.
- Probability of transition 1 (ground-state sublevel decay): $2.8\times10^{-11}$ eV; internal conversion coefficient $\alpha_{M1}=6.6\times10^5$; total width $\Gamma_{tot}=1.8\times10^{-5}$ eV; half-life of $|5/2^+,F=3\rangle$ sublevel less than $2.5\times10^{-11}$ s.
- Radiation width of transition 2: $1.1\times10^{-14}$ eV; total width $7.0\times10^{-7}$ eV ($\alpha_{M1}=6.0\times10^7$); probability of isomeric state excitation from ground-state decay: 3-4%.
- Expected formation rate of isomeric nuclei: $N_{is}\simeq3\times10^3$ per second at modern muon factories producing $10^5$ muonic atoms per second.
- Energy of the isomeric transition lies in the range of 120-140 eV.

## Context

This work extends the Bohr-Weisskopf treatment of finite nuclear size effects on hyperfine structure (Bohr 1950) and the Le Bellac formalism for muonic atoms (Le Bellac 1963) to the specific case of the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet. It builds on prior measurements of the isomeric state energy (Beck 2007; Wense 2016) and the $g$-factors of thorium (Bemis 1988; Kroger 1976), and it enables experimental identification of the isomeric transition via conversion electrons in muonic atoms, as well as potential optical studies of the hyperfine sublevels if the $g_K$ factor and $B(M1)$ fall within the optical-range parameter space identified in the paper.
