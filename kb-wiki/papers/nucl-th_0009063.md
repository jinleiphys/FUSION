# Will Strangeness Win the Prize?

> Five groups have made predictions involving the production of strange hadrons and entered them in a competition set up by Barbara Jacak, Xin-Nian Wang and myself in the spring of 1998 for the purpose of comparing to first year physics results from RHIC. These predictions are summarized and evaluated.

# Will Strangeness Win the Prize?

## Key claim

Five groups submitted predictions for strange hadron production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC before any data were taken, and the paper summarizes and evaluates these predictions without declaring a winner; the most exciting outcome would be something that was not predicted at all.

## Method

The paper reviews five competition entries for strange hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at the full RHIC beam energy of 100 GeV per nucleon, categorized into three approaches: a phase-space thermal model (Rafelski and Letessier), two hydrodynamic models (Dumitru and Rischke; Bass and Dumitru), and two microscopic transport models (Zhang, Ko, Li and Lin using ZPC+ART; Bleicher using UrQMD). Each entry specified parameters such as temperature, chemical potentials, initial conditions, and freeze-out criteria, and the paper compares their predictions for hadron yields, rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Phase-space model (Rafelski and Letessier): temperature fixed at 150 MeV; extrapolated from SPS fitted values of T = 160 ± 10 MeV and μ_B = 200 ± 70 MeV.
- Dumitru and Rischke hydrodynamic model: initial energy density of 17 GeV/fm³, baryon density 2.3 times normal nuclear matter density, formation time 0.6 fm/c, initial temperature 300 MeV, light quark chemical potential 47 MeV, critical temperature for first-order phase transition 160 MeV.
- Bass and Dumitru hydrodynamic model: transverse radius R_T = 6 fm, formation time 1/10 of that, spectra computed at isotherm of 130 MeV.
- Zhang, Ko, Li and Lin parton cascade: kaon abundance enhanced by about 50% relative to HIJING input.
- UrQMD (Bleicher): predictions for directed flow v₁ and elliptic flow v₂ for pions and kaons, with flow coefficients greater for pions than kaons.
- Rafelski and Letessier predicted hadron ratios: p/p̄ = 1.19, Λ/p = 1.74, Ξ⁻/Λ = 0.19, Ω/Ξ⁻ = 0.147, K⁺/K⁻ = 1.05 (for γ_q = 1.25, λ_q = 1.03).
- Rafelski and Letessier predicted central rapidity densities dN/dy: for γ_q = 1.25, λ_q = 1.03: b = 17, p = 25, p̄ = 21, Λ+Σ⁰ = 44, Ξ = 17, Ω = 1.2.

## Context

This paper summarizes a competition organized at the INT workshop "Probes of Dense Matter in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions" in spring 1998, with predictions submitted before RHIC data were taken. It extends the tradition of thermal model fits to SPS data (Cleymans, this proceedings) and hydrodynamic models dating back to Landau in the 1950s, and it enables direct comparison of pre-diction versus measurement once RHIC results become available. The paper does not declare a winner but notes that the judges would announce the winner in summer 2001.
