# Pseudoscalar and scalar meson masses at finite temperature

> The composite operator formalism is applied to QCD at finite temperature to calculate the masses of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. In particular the ratio of the sigma mass to the pion mass is an interesting measure of the degree of chiral symmetry breaking at different temperatures. We calculate the temperature T* at which M_sigma(T) < 2M_pi(T), above which the sigma partial width into two pions vanishes. We find T*=0.95T_c (where T_c is the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition), within the full effective potential given by the formalism. We find that an expansion a-la Landau of the effective potential around the critical point in the limit of small quark mass provides for a very good determination of T*.

# Pseudoscalar and scalar meson masses at finite temperature

## Key claim
The temperature at which the sigma meson mass becomes less than twice the pion mass, causing the sigma's strong decay width into two pions to vanish, is T* = 0.95 T_c when computed using the full effective potential, and this result is nearly identical to that obtained from a simpler Landau expansion of the effective potential around the critical point in the small-quark-mass limit.

## Method
The authors apply the composite operator formalism to QCD at finite temperature, using an effective action for composite operators with a variational Ansatz for the fermion self-energy. They compute the scalar and pseudoscalar meson masses from second derivatives of the effective potential evaluated at its minimum, using parameters (M = 280 MeV, m_u = 8 MeV, m_d = 11 MeV, m_s = 181 MeV) obtained from fits to zero-temperature QCD observables including the pion mass and decay constant, kaon mass, and kaon electromagnetic mass difference.

## Key numbers
- T* = 0.95 T_c ≈ 98 MeV (from full effective potential calculation)
- T* = 0.93 T_c ≈ 95 MeV (from leading-order-in-quark-mass numerical calculation)
- T_c ≈ 103 MeV (critical temperature for chiral phase transition)
- M_π ≈ 135 MeV, M_σ ≈ 630 MeV at T = 0
- Quark masses: m_u = 8 MeV, m_d = 11 MeV, m = (m_u + m_d)/2 = 9.5 MeV, m_s = 181 MeV
- Mass scale M = 280 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV
- Fermion condensate at T = 0: ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_0 ≈ (-197 MeV)^3 (massless quarks), ≈ (-200 MeV)^3 (massive quarks)
- Landau expansion coefficients at T_c: a_4(T_c) = 0.0033, b_1(T_c) = 1.25, a_s(T_c) = -7.28

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous application of the composite operator formalism to finite-temperature QCD, where they had already studied the temperature dependence of f_π (Barducci et al., 1995). The calculation of the sigma-to-pion mass ratio provides a quantitative measure of chiral symmetry restoration, complementing earlier studies using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and by Florkowski and Friman (1994). The result that T* lies close to T_c supports the expectation that the sigma meson becomes a narrow resonance at high temperature, potentially observable through its electromagnetic decay channel, as suggested by Hatsuda and Kunihiro and by Pisarski (1994).
