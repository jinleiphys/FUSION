# Eulerian Rotations of Deformed Nuclei for TDDFT Calculations

> We discuss three practical methods for performing Eulerian rotations of Slater determinants in a three-dimensional Cartesian geometry. In addition to the straightforward application of the active form of the quantum mechanical rotation operator, we introduce two methods using a passive position-space rotation followed by an active spin-space rotation, one after variation and the other before variation. These methods can be used to initialize reactions involving deformed nuclei where a particular alignment of the deformed nuclei with respect to the collision axis is desired. We show that doing the rotation before the variation is the most efficient way of generating such initial states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for initializing time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations of deformed nuclei at arbitrary orientations, performing a passive position-space rotation of the initial guess followed by an active spin-space rotation *before* the variational Hartree-Fock (HF) minimization is the most accurate and efficient method, though the authors note that the "rotation after variation" methods are also viable with sufficient basis-spline order.

## Method

The authors implemented and compared three methods for Eulerian rotation of Slater determinants in a three-dimensional Cartesian geometry using basis-spline collocation: (1) direct active rotation via the quantum mechanical rotation operator applied after HF variation, using small angular steps and Taylor expansion; (2) passive position-space rotation via spline interpolation followed by active spin-space rotation, applied after HF variation; and (3) passive position-space rotation of the initial guess followed by active spin-space rotation, applied *before* HF variation. All calculations used the Skyrme SLy4 effective interaction and a deformed \(^{20}\)Ne nucleus on a \(30^3\) lattice with box size \((-10, +10)\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{20}\)Ne: \(E_B = -157.26\) MeV (exact value for unrotated nucleus aligned with code axes)
- Root-mean-square radius: \(r_{rms} = 2.92\) fm (exact value)
- Least-bound neutron single-particle energy: \(\epsilon_n = -13.24\) MeV (exact value)
- Least-bound proton single-particle energy: \(\epsilon_p = -9.35\) MeV (exact value)
- For the "rotation before variation" method at Euler angles \((45^\circ, 45^\circ, 45^\circ)\) with basis-spline order \(M=9\): \(E_B = -157.26\) MeV, \(r_{rms} = 2.92\) fm, \(\epsilon_n = -13.24\) MeV, \(\epsilon_p = -9.35\) MeV (all exact to the precision shown)
- For the direct active rotation method at \((45^\circ, 45^\circ, 45^\circ)\) with \(M=9\): \(E_B = -157.26\) MeV, \(\epsilon_p = -9.34\) MeV (slight deviation from exact)
- For the passive rotation after variation method at \((45^\circ, 45^\circ, 45^\circ)\) with \(M=9\): \(E_B = -157.25\) MeV, \(\epsilon_p = -9.34\) MeV (slight deviation)
- Quadrupole deformation of \(^{20}\)Ne: \(28.54\) fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the methodology for three-dimensional, unrestricted HF and TDHF calculations developed by Umar et al. (1991) and Umar and Oberacker (2006), providing practical tools for initializing reactions involving deformed nuclei at arbitrary orientations. The techniques are directly applicable to angular momentum projection calculations in nuclear structure (Bender and Heenen, 2003) and to time-dependent Hartree-Fock studies of heavy-ion collisions (Simenel, 2012). The "rotation before variation" method enables systematic studies of fusion and deep-inelastic collisions as a function of nuclear orientation, which was previously difficult to implement in fully three-dimensional TDDFT codes.
