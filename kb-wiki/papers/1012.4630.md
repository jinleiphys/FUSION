# On the nature of the X(3872)

> We present recent studies of charmonium multiquark states. We use different interacting models and numerical methods to study deeply bound four-quark states and meson-meson molecules. No deeply bound four-quark states are found in our analysis. A nice description of the X(3872) is obtained as a $D\bar{D}^*-J/Ψω$ coupled channel state.

## Key claim

The X(3872) can be described as a \(D\bar{D}^*-J/\Psi\omega\) coupled channel state, but no deeply bound four-quark states are found in the analysis; only a few channels may present observable resonances or slightly bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body problem using an infinite expansion of the four-quark wave function in terms of hyperspherical harmonics. Close to threshold, they solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to identify attractive channels that could contain a meson-meson molecule, allowing coupling between two-meson systems (e.g., \(D\bar{D}^*\) coupled to \(J/\Psi\omega\)) and using the same interacting Hamiltonian for both two- and four-quark systems.

## Key numbers

- The \(D\bar{D}^*\) system with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}(I)=1^{++}(0)\) shows a bound state slightly below the threshold for charmed meson production, corresponding to the X(3872).
- A \(\Psi(2S)\pi^+\) peak was observed at about 4430 MeV/c\(^2\) by Belle in \(\bar B^0 \to \Psi(2S)\pi^+K^-\) decays.
- Two resonances in \(\Xi_{c1} \pi^+\) were reported by Belle at masses of about 4050 and 4250 MeV/c\(^2\) from the \(B^0 \to \Xi_{c1} \pi^+K^-\) decay.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of charmonium spectroscopy that noted an overpopulation of states beyond the conventional \(c\bar c\) spectrum, as discussed by Bug10 and others. It contradicts the expectation of deeply bound compact tetraquarks, instead supporting a molecular interpretation. The results enable future searches for four-quark bottomonium states, which the authors predict should have larger binding energies due to heavy quark symmetry.
