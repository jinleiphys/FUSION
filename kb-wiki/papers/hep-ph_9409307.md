# Chiral Anomaly Tests

> For the gamma-pion interaction, the perturbative expansion of the effective chiral Lagrangian (chi-PT) can be limited to terms quartic in momenta and masses (O(p4)), or to higher order. The abnormal intrinsic parity (chiral anomaly) component of the lagrangian leads to interesting predictions for the processes pi0 -----> 2 gamma and gamma ----->3 pi. These are described by the amplitudes F_{pi} and F_{3pi}, respectively. We demonstrate that the O(p4) value of F_{3pi} disagrees with existing data, while the O(p6) value is nearly consistent. We describe how Fermilab experiment E781 can get improved data for tests of the chiral anomaly.

# Chiral Anomaly Tests

## Key claim
The O(p⁴) chiral perturbation theory prediction for the γ → 3π amplitude F₃π disagrees with existing Serpukhov data by at least two standard deviations, while the O(p⁶) prediction is nearly consistent when both experimental and theoretical uncertainties are considered. The paper argues that the earlier conclusion by Antipov et al. that the chiral anomaly prediction is confirmed is not supported by the data.

## Method
The author compares the O(p⁴) and O(p⁶) chiral perturbation theory predictions for the anomalous amplitudes Fπ (π⁰ → 2γ) and F₃π (γ → 3π) against existing experimental data from the Serpukhov experiment of Antipov et al., which used the Primakoff reaction π⁻ + Z → π⁻' + π⁰ + Z' with 40 GeV pions on a nuclear target. The author then calculates expected event rates for the proposed Fermilab experiment E781 using a 600 GeV pion beam on a carbon target, estimating cross sections and statistical precision for both the Primakoff reaction and the alternative reaction π⁻ + e → π⁻' + π⁰ + e'.

## Key numbers
- Fπ (O(p⁴) prediction): 0.025 GeV⁻¹, in agreement with experiment
- F₃π (O(p⁴) prediction): 9.7 ± 0.2 GeV⁻³, using Nc = 3 and f = 92.4 ± 0.2 MeV
- F₃π (Antipov et al. measurement): 12.9 ± 0.9 (stat) ± 0.5 (sys) GeV⁻³, from approximately 200 events at Serpukhov with 40 GeV pions, covering -t < 2 × 10⁻³ (GeV/c)² and s < 10 mπ²
- F₃π (O(p⁶) prediction): 10.7 ± 0.5 GeV⁻³, including one-loop and O(p⁶) corrections via vector meson dominance
- Expected cross section for Primakoff reaction at 600 GeV on C¹² target: approximately 100 nb for s interval 4-10 mπ²
- Expected number of two-pion events in E781: approximately 2 × 10⁴, from an estimated 3 × 10¹⁰ pion interactions
- Expected cross section for π⁻ + e → π⁻' + π⁰ + e' on carbon: roughly 10 nb per atom, yielding approximately 2000 events

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bijnens et al. (1990s) for the anomalous sector, specifically the Wess-Zumino-Witten terms, and directly contradicts the conclusion of Antipov et al. (1986) that the O(p⁴) chiral anomaly prediction for γ → 3π was confirmed by their Serpukhov data. The paper motivates the Fermilab E781 experiment (Russ, 1990s) as a means to resolve the discrepancy with improved statistics and reduced systematic uncertainties, and also notes complementary approaches at CEBAF and low-energy e⁺e⁻ colliders. The higher energy (600 GeV) and lower s-range in E781 are expected to suppress strong interaction backgrounds that plagued the earlier measurement.
