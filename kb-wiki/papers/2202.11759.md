# Ab initio informed evaluation of the radiative capture of protons on $^7$Be

> The radiative capture of protons by $^7$Be, which is the source of $^8$B that $β$-decays emitting the majority of solar neutrinos measured on earth, has not yet been measured at astrophysically relevant energies. The recommended value for its zero-energy S-factor, $S_{17}$(0) = 20.8$\pm$(0.7)exp$\pm$(1.4)theory eV$\cdot$b, relies on theoretical extrapolations from higher-energy measurements, a process that leads to significant uncertainty. We performed a set of first-principle (or, ab initio) calculations of the $^7$Be($p$, $γ$)$^8$B reaction to provide an independent prediction of the low-energy S-factor with quantified uncertainties. We demonstrate underlying features in the predicted S-factor allowing the combination of theoretical calculations and measurements to produce an evaluated S-factor of $S_{17}$(0) = 19.8$\pm$0.3 eV$\cdot$b. We expect the calculations and uncertainty quantification process described here to set a new standard for the evaluation of light-ion astrophysical reactions.

## Key claim

The authors combine ab initio calculations with experimental data to produce an evaluated zero-energy S-factor of S17(0) = 19.8 ± 0.3 eV·b, but this result depends on a specific treatment of experimental covariances that weights each experiment equally rather than treating all data points as uncorrelated.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations of the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction using multiple chiral effective field theory (χEFT) nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions at different truncation orders (N2LO, N3LO, N4LO, and N3LO*). They applied a phenomenological correction (NCSMC-pheno) to adjust the 8B ground state binding energy and the 1+ and 3+ resonance positions to match experimental values. The relationship between the S-factor at zero energy and at higher energies was then used to perform a regression analysis combining theoretical predictions with experimental measurements.

## Key numbers

- Recommended zero-energy S-factor from the literature: S17(0) = 20.8 ± 0.7 (expt) ± 1.4 (theory) eV·b
- Evaluated zero-energy S-factor from this work: S17(0) = 19.8 ± 0.3 eV·b
- 8B ground state binding energy: 136(1) keV
- Linear relationship slope between S17(0) and sum of squared ANCs: 38.53 ± 1.45 eV·b·fm
- Regression using E_c.m. ≤ 0.5 MeV data with equal experiment weighting yields S17(0) = 19.8 ± 0.2 eV·b
- Regression using 0.8 ≤ E_c.m. ≤ 1.5 MeV data with equal experiment weighting yields S17(0) = 19.8 ± 0.2 eV·b
- Asymptotic normalization coefficients from the N4LO+3N*_lnl interaction: C_{p1/2} = 0.343 fm^{-1/2}, C_{p3/2} = 0.621 fm^{-1/2}
- Scattering lengths from the N4LO+3N*_lnl interaction: a1 = 1.3(1) fm, a2 = -5.0(1) fm

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction (Navratil 2011) by including the discrete portion of the NCSMC ansatz, which yields scattering length signs consistent with recent measurements (PhysRevC.99.045807) unlike earlier calculations. The new evaluation protocol combining theoretical calculations with experimental data enables a reduction in the theoretical uncertainty of S17(0) by an order of magnitude compared to the recommended value from SolarFusion2, and demonstrates the need for high-order (N4LO and above) chiral calculations and proper treatment of experimental covariances in future evaluations of thermonuclear reaction rates.
