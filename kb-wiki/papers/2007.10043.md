# Prompt-delayed $γ$-ray spectroscopy of neutron-rich $^{119,121}$In isotopes

> The fusion and transfer induced fission reaction $^{9}$Be($^{238}$U,~f) with 6.2 MeV/u beam energy, using a unique setup consisting of AGATA, VAMOS++ and EXOGAM detectors, was used to populate through the fission process and study the neutron-rich $^{119,121}$In isotopes. This setup enabled the prompt-delayed $γ$-ray spectroscopy of isotopes in the time range of $100~\rm{ns} - 200~μ\rm{s}$. In the odd-$A$ $^{119,121}$In isotopes, indications of a short half-life $19/2^{-}$ isomeric state, in addition to the previously known $25/2^{+}$ isomeric state, were observed from the present data. Further, new prompt transitions above the $25/2^{+}$ isomer in $^{121}$In were identified along with reevaluation of its half-life. The experimental data were compared with the theoretical results obtained in the framework of large-scale shell-model calculations in a restricted model space. The $\langle πg_{9/2} νh_{11/2};I \arrowvert \hat{\mathcal{H}}\arrowvert πg_{9/2} νh_{11/2};I\rangle$ two-body matrix elements of residual interaction were modified to explain the excitation energies and the $B(E2)$ transition probabilities in the neutron-rich In isotopes. The (i) decreasing trend of $E(29/2^{+}) - E(25/2^{+})$ in odd-In (with dominant configuration $πg_{9/2}^{-1}νh_{11/2}^{-2}$ and maximum aligned spin of $29/2^{+}$) and (ii) increasing trend of $E(27/2^{+}) - E(23/2^{+})$ in odd-Sb (with dominant configuration $πg_{7/2}^{+1}νh_{11/2}^{-2}$ and maximum aligned spin of $27/2^{+}$) with increasing neutron number could be understood as a consequence of hole-hole and particle-hole interactions, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper reports new prompt gamma-ray transitions above the previously known (25/2+) isomeric state in 121In and a reevaluation of its half-life, while also finding indications of a short-lived (19/2-) isomeric state in both 119In and 121In, with the caveat that the (19/2-) half-life in 119In is estimated to be between 3 ns and 10 ns, below the direct sensitivity of the experimental setup.

## Method

The neutron-rich 119,121In isotopes were populated via fusion- and transfer-induced fission using a 238U beam at 6.2 MeV/u on a 9Be target at GANIL. The experimental setup combined the AGATA gamma-ray tracking array for prompt gamma detection, the VAMOS++ spectrometer for isotopic identification of fission fragments, and the EXOGAM array at the focal plane for delayed gamma-ray detection, enabling spectroscopy in the time range of 100 ns to 200 microseconds. Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed in a restricted model space with modified two-body matrix elements of the residual interaction to reproduce the experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of the (25/2+) state in 119In: T1/2 = 270(11) ns, yielding B(E2; 25/2+ -> 21/2+) = 19(1) e2fm4
- Half-life of the (25/2+) state in 121In: T1/2 = 7.3(2) microseconds, yielding B(E2; 25/2+ -> 21/2+) = 3.3(1) e2fm4
- Half-life estimate for the (19/2-) state in 119In: 3 ns < T1/2 < 10 ns
- Calculated B(E2; 25/2+ -> 21/2+) values from modified shell-model interaction: 19.5 e2fm4 for 119In and 12.5 e2fm4 for 121In
- Modifications to the two-body matrix elements ⟨πg9/2 νh11/2; I | H | πg9/2 νh11/2; I⟩: -200 keV for I=6, -400 keV for I=8, and +500 keV for I=10

## Context

This work extends the study of high-spin isomeric states in neutron-rich In isotopes near the Z=50 shell closure, following earlier measurements by Lu (2002) and Rejmund (2010) that reported conflicting half-lives for the (25/2+) state in 121In (350(50) ns and 17(2) microseconds respectively). The new results resolve this discrepancy and, combined with shell-model calculations, reveal opposite trends in the energy evolution of maximally aligned states in In versus Sb isotopes, which the authors attribute to the hole-hole versus particle-hole nature of the proton-neutron interaction. The findings enable systematic comparisons of B(E2) values across the isotopic chains of Sn, In, and Sb, showing that the present experimental values for In follow the expected parabolic behavior inherited from Sn isotopes, in contrast to earlier reported values.
