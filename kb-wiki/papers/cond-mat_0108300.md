# Ground states of a system of interacting particles in a parabolic trap

## Key claim
Within the lowest Landau level approximation (weak interaction limit where the two-body interaction is much smaller than the oscillator frequency), the authors develop a method to identify exact ground states of a 2D system of interacting bosons in a parabolic trap without solving the full spectrum, provided the interaction potential belongs to a broad universality class satisfying a set of integral inequalities.

## Method
The authors decompose the projected interaction operator into a sum of two terms: one term \(V_0\) whose ground state can be found analytically, and a remainder \(V_S\) that is required to be non-negative definite. By constructing a specific class of basis states \(|0_{L,v}\rangle\) that are annihilated by \(V_S\) and are eigenstates of \(V_0\), they show these states become exact ground states of the full Hamiltonian in each sector of total angular momentum \(L\) and collective angular momentum \(v\). The sufficient condition for \(V_S \geq 0\) is reduced to a set of integral inequalities involving the interaction potential \(V(r)\).

## Key numbers
- The condition \(V(r) \ll \hbar \omega\) defines the lowest Landau level approximation, where \(\omega\) is the oscillator frequency of the trapping potential.
- The effective Hamiltonian is \(H = \omega (L + N) + \tilde{V}\), with \(\hbar = m = 1\).
- The term \(V_0\) is given by \(V_0 = (N/2)[(N-1)s_0 - (L - v)(s_1 - s_2)]\), where \(s_0 = \int_0^\infty dt\, e^{-t} V(\sqrt{2t})\) and \(s_1 - s_2 = \int_0^\infty dt\, e^{-t} \left(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{t^2}{4}\right) V(\sqrt{2t})\).
- The sufficient condition for the ground state property is the set of integral inequalities: \(\int_0^\infty dt\, V(r) e^{-r^2/2} \left[ \frac{r^{4n}}{2^{2n}(2n)!} - 1 + n\left(1 - \frac{r^4}{8}\right) \right] \geq 0\) for integer \(n\) with \(0 \leq 2n \leq L\).
- The allowed values of the collective angular momentum \(v\) are \(0, 1, 2, \dots, L-2, L\).

## Context
This work extends the method previously introduced by the same authors (Vorov and Hussein, 2000) for identifying ground states via operator decomposition, applying it specifically to 2D bosonic systems in a parabolic trap within the lowest Landau level approximation. The results enable exact determination of ground states for a broad class of repulsive interactions, including Gaussian, delta-function, and 2D log-Coulomb potentials, without numerical diagonalization of the full Hamiltonian. The authors note that generalization to fermionic systems is possible, opening the door to exact treatments of quantum dots, trapped Bose-Einstein condensates, and electrons in magnetic fields.
