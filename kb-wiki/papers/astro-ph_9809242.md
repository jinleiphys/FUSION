# Neutrino Capture and r-Process Nucleosynthesis

> We explore neutrino capture during r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven ejecta from nascent neutron stars. We focus on the interplay between charged-current weak interactions and element synthesis, and we delineate the important role of equilibrium nuclear dynamics. During the period of coexistence of free nucleons and light and/or heavy nuclei, electron neutrino capture inhibits the r-process. At all stages, capture on free neutrons has a larger impact than capture on nuclei. However, neutrino capture on heavey nuclei by itself, if it is very strong, is also detrimental to the r-process until large nuclear equilibrium clusters break down and the classical neutron-capture phase of the r-process begins. The sensitivity of the r-process to neutrino irradiation means that neutrino-capture effects can strongly constrain the r-process site, neutrino physics, or both. These results apply also to r-process scenarios other than neutrino-heated winds.

## Key claim

Neutrino capture during r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven ejecta is generally detrimental to the r-process, contrary to earlier suggestions that it could accelerate the process; the paper shows that even if necessary conditions for the r-process (high entropy, neutron richness) are attained, they are not sufficient to guarantee a viable r-process because neutrino capture on free neutrons depletes the neutron supply and neutrino capture on heavy nuclei within quasi-equilibrium clusters does not accelerate but rather limits the r-process.

## Method

The authors performed nucleosynthesis calculations using the Clemson nucleosynthesis code, a fully implicit single-network code with over 3000 nuclear species from neutrons and protons to actinide nuclei, including isotopes from the proton-drip to neutron-drip lines. They computed neutrino capture cross sections using a single-particle shell model with Fermi and Gamow-Teller transitions, assuming Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectra with zero chemical potential, and modeled the neutrino-driven wind expansion with constant entropy, homologous outflow, and an expansion timescale of 0.3 seconds. Eight models were run with different combinations of neutrino capture on free nucleons and heavy nuclei enabled or disabled below various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino capture cross section on a free neutron for black body neutrinos with temperature \(T_{\nu_e} = 3.5\) MeV: \(1.6 \times 10^{-41}\ {\rm cm}^2\)
- Neutrino capture cross section on a neutron bound inside \(^{130}\)Cd for the same neutrinos: \(8.6 \times 10^{-43}\ {\rm cm}^2\)
- Neutrino temperatures used: \(kT_{\nu_e} = 3.5\) MeV and \(kT_{\bar{\nu}_e} = 4.0\) MeV
- Electron neutrino luminosity: \(10^{51}\) ergs/s; electron antineutrino luminosity: \(4 \times 10^{51}\) ergs/s
- Initial conditions: radius \(r_0 = 5.6\) km, entropy 110\(k\) per nucleon, density \(2\times 10^8\) g/cm\(^3\), expansion timescale \(\tau = 0.3\) s, initial temperature \(T_9 \approx 40\)
- Final reaction freezeout occurred at \(T_9 \approx 0.5\), radius \(r \approx 1000\) km, density about 35 g/cc
- In models 0, 1, and 2, the ratio of free neutrons to heavy seed nuclei \(R\) was about 70 at \(T_9 = 3\)
- In model 0, the number of free neutrons per nucleon at \(T_9 = 3\) was 0.38, while neutrons locked in heavy nuclei was 0.34

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Meyer et al. (1992), who included neutral-current spallation of neutrons from nuclei, and by Fuller and Meyer (1995) and McLaughlin, Fuller, and Wilson (1996), who identified the strong "alpha effect" in which the electron fraction grows rapidly as \(^4\)He nuclei assemble in a large neutrino flux. The paper contradicts the proposal by Qian et al. (1997) that neutrino capture could accelerate the r-process by acting like beta decay to move nuclei to higher charge. The results show that neutrino capture effects can strongly constrain the r-process site and neutrino physics, and apply to r-process scenarios beyond neutrino-heated winds.
