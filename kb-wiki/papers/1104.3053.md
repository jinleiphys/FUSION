# Entropy scaling and thermalization in hadron-hadron collisions at LHC

> The recent LHC data have once more brought the issue of the formation of thermalized state of matter in hadron-hadron collisions into the forefront. In this letter, we have shown that the scaling of the information entropy of the chaotically produced particles is valid up to recently available data from p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 2.36 TeV obtained by ALICE experiment. We predict that at the highest energies projected for LHC, almost all the particles will be produced chaotically, indicating that a collective behavior should be observed in hadronic collisions, as indicated by one of present authors quite some time ago.

## Key claim

The authors show that the scaling of information entropy from a two-component model (chaotic plus coherent sources) holds for non-single-diffractive p+p collisions up to the highest available LHC energy of sqrt(s) = 2.36 TeV, and they predict that at the projected LHC energies of 7 TeV and 14 TeV, the chaotic fraction will approach unity, indicating thermalization.

## Method

The authors analyze multiplicity distributions from p+p collisions using a two-component model where particle emission comes from a convolution of a chaotic source (Planck-Polya distribution) and a coherent source (Poisson distribution). They compute the information entropy S for symmetric pseudorapidity intervals at various collision energies and verify that the scaled entropy S/eta_max as a function of the scaled variable xi = eta_c/eta_max follows a single universal curve. They then extrapolate the average chaotic multiplicity and average total multiplicity to higher energies using power-law fits.

## Key numbers

- The entropy scaling law is validated up to sqrt(s) = 2.36 TeV from ALICE p+p data
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, predicted average chaotic multiplicity ranges from 6.6(2) to 16(1) depending on xi, with average coherent multiplicity consistent with zero (0.1(3) to 1(1))
- At sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, predicted average chaotic multiplicity ranges from 9.1(4) to 21(2) depending on xi, with average coherent multiplicity consistent with zero (-0.4(6) to 1(1))
- Power-law fit parameters for chaotic multiplicity: exponent c = 0.47(2) at xi = 0.051362, c = 0.45(1) at xi = 0.102724, c = 0.43(2) at xi = 0.133542
- Power-law fit parameters for total multiplicity: exponent c = 0.37(2) at xi = 0.051362, c = 0.36(1) at xi = 0.102724, c = 0.35(2) at xi = 0.133542

## Context

This work extends the entropy scaling approach of Carruthers (1989) from SPS energies (up to 900 GeV) to the LHC energy of 2.36 TeV, and builds on the two-component model of Fowler and collaborators for multiplicity distributions. The prediction that chaotic (thermalized) production dominates at LHC energies suggests that collective behavior may be observable in hadron-hadron collisions, which would enable tests of thermalization in simpler systems than heavy-ion collisions.
