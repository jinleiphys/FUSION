# Alpha-muon sticking and chaos in muon-catalysed "in flight" d-t fusion

> We discuss the alpha-muon sticking coefficient in the muon-catalysed ``in flight" d-t fusion in the framework of the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model. Especially the influence of muonic chaotic dynamics on the sticking coefficient is brought into focus. The chaotic motion of the muon affects not only the fusion cross section but also the $μ-α$ sticking coefficient. Chaotic systems lead to larger enhancements with respect to regular systems because of the reduction of the tunneling region. Moreover they give smaller sticking probabilities than those of regular events. By utilizing a characteristic of the chaotic dynamics one can avoid losing the muon in the $μ$CF cycle. We propose the application of the so-called ``microwave ionization of a Rydberg atom" to the present case which could lead to the enhancement of the reactivation process by using X-rays.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chaotic dynamics of the muon during muon-catalysed d-t fusion can reduce the alpha-muon sticking probability compared to regular dynamics, and that this chaotic behavior can be exploited to strip the stuck muon using an external oscillating X-ray field, potentially improving the muon recycling efficiency. The authors hedge that their study does not include medium effects important for determining the precise reactivation coefficient in actual experimental setups.

## Method

The authors used the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model to simulate the "in flight" d-t fusion reaction tμ + d → (tdμ)⁺ → ⁴He⁺⁺ + μ + n + Q, starting from a ground-state muonic tritium configuration. They computed the enhancement factor of the fusion cross section due to the muon, the initial muon sticking probability on the alpha particle using phase-space distributions at the internal classical turning point, and simulated muon stripping by applying a linearly polarized oscillatory electric field with frequency twice the muon's angular frequency in the muonic helium ion.

## Key numbers

- Average binding energy of ground state muonic tritium atom: BE_{tμ} = -2.73 keV, in agreement with the scaled hydrogen value of -2.71 keV
- Screening potential U_μ = 8.3 keV, derived from the energy difference between the ground state muonic tritium and ground state muonic ⁵He
- Estimated "in flight" d-t fusion rate at liquid hydrogen density: λ^{dt} ∼ 2.8 × 10⁴ s⁻¹, in agreement with an independent result of 0.5 × 10⁵ s⁻¹ from Melezhik
- For the adiabatic limit assumption (muon in ground state of ⁵He at fusion), the sticking probability is 5.6% for d-t fusion, 32.4% for d-d fusion, and 7.84% for t-t fusion
- Direct measurement of initial sticking coefficient ω_0 = 0.69 ± 0.40 ± 0.14% from Davies
- Effective sticking probability ω_s^{eff} varies from 0.64% to 0.86 ± 0.01% as temperature varies from 16K to 5K, from Kawamura
- At incident energy 0.18 keV, the average enhancement factor f̄_μ = 1.1 × 10²⁷; a regular event had f_μ = 4.1 × 10¹⁹ and r_a^μ/r_a^0 = 0.15; a chaotic event had f_μ = 2.7 × 10³¹ and r_a^μ/r_a^0 = 0.06
- The fundamental frequencies for the ground and first excited state of muonic He ion correspond to wavelengths of 0.11 nm and 0.44 nm, respectively

## Context

This work extends the study of muon-catalysed fusion (μCF) by applying the CoMD approach, previously used for electron screening problems by Kimura and Bonasera, to investigate the role of chaotic muon dynamics in the sticking process. The paper addresses the discrepancy between theoretical estimates and experimental measurements of the initial sticking coefficient ω_0 reported by Davies and others, and proposes a novel method for muon reactivation inspired by microwave ionization of Rydberg atoms studied by Koch, van Leeuwen, and others. The results enable future investigation of temperature-dependent phenomena in the muon cycling rate and sticking probability observed experimentally by Bom and Kawamura, and suggest that controlled chaos via X-ray irradiation could enhance the reactivation process in the μCF cycle.
