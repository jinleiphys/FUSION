# A first estimate of triply heavy baryon masses from the pNRQCD perturbative static potential

> Within pNRQCD we compute the masses of spin-averaged triply heavy baryons using the now-available NNLO pNRQCD potentials and three-body variational approach. We focus in particular on the role of the purely three-body interaction in perturbation theory. This we find to be reasonably small and of the order 25 MeV Our prediction for the Omega_ccc baryon mass is 4900(250) in keeping with other approaches. We propose to search for this hitherto unobserved state at B factories by examining the end point of the recoil spectrum against triple charm.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first estimate of triply heavy baryon masses using perturbative pNRQCD potentials, finding that the purely three-body interaction contributes only about 25 MeV to the total mass, and that the predicted Omega_ccc mass of 4900(250) MeV is consistent with other approaches, though the uncertainty is large.

## Method

The authors employ the NNLO pNRQCD static potential for three heavy quarks, combining the two-body Delta-shaped potential with the intrinsic three-body force that first appears at NNLO. They use a variational approach to solve the three-body Schroedinger equation, first fitting the strong coupling constant and quark masses (charm and bottom) to five meson observables: spin-averaged charmonium and bottomonium 1S masses, the bottomonium 1P spin-averaged mass, the Upsilon(2S) mass, and the J/psi to eta_c gamma radiative width, plus the B_c mass. The running coupling is evolved from the Z boson pole using the NNLO renormalization group equation, with the coupling frozen at low scales (400-600 MeV) to avoid Landau pole singularities.

## Key numbers

- The purely three-body interaction contributes approximately 25 MeV to the triply heavy baryon masses
- Predicted Omega_ccc baryon mass: 4900(250) MeV
- Spin-averaged charmonium 1S mass used as input: 3067.7(3) MeV
- Spin-averaged bottomonium 1S mass used as input: 9443(1) MeV
- Spin-averaged bottomonium 1P mass used as input: 9899.9(3) MeV
- B_c spin-averaged mass used as input: 6317(8) MeV
- J/psi to eta_c gamma radiative width used as input: 1.6(4) keV
- Strong coupling at Z boson pole fixed to world average: 0.1184(7) (NLO and NNLO)
- Strong coupling at 5 GeV (N_f=4): 0.204(2) NLO, 0.2136(6) NNLO
- Strong coupling at 1.6 GeV (N_f=3): 0.295(4) NLO, 0.336(2) NNLO
- Strong coupling at 0.8 GeV: 0.417(8) NLO, 0.574(7) NNLO
- Charm quark pole mass from fits: approximately 1700-2000 MeV
- Bottom quark pole mass from fits: approximately 4980-5381 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD framework, previously applied to quarkonium systems by Brambilla et al. (2004, 2010) and others, to triply heavy baryons using the three-body potential computed to NNLO by Brambilla et al. (2009). The study is timely because the three-body potential has only recently become available, and the Omega_ccc baryon remains unobserved. The results enable experimental searches at B factories by examining the endpoint of the recoil spectrum against triple charm, and provide a perturbative QCD baseline for comparison with lattice QCD studies such as those by Alexandrou et al. (2002) and Bakry et al. (2011), which found the Delta-shaped potential dominant at short distances.
