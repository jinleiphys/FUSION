# Real-time simulation of jet energy loss and entropy production in high-energy scattering with matter

> In analogy to high-energy nuclear scattering experiments, we study a real-time scattering process between a propagating state and a dense target in $1+1$-d massive QED. In our setup, we identify three distinct regimes that qualitatively characterize the evolution: for a dilute medium, the incoming probe state evolves nearly ballistically; in an intermediate setting, it traverses the matter, locally exciting it; and for dense targets, one approaches a black-disk limit, where the matter acts as a strong wall potential. We find evidence that the probe's energy loss rate scales linearly with the path length in the medium, and we study how the entanglement entropy reveals the mixing between the probe and medium states. With the goal of one day replicating high-energy nuclear experiments in quantum devices, we briefly discuss how the current tensor network-based simulations can be translated to a quantum simulator.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in a 1+1-dimensional massive QED (Schwinger model) simulation, the energy loss rate of a probe jet state scales linearly with path length through a dense target medium, but this result is qualified by the observation that it only holds in regimes where the jet is not fully back-scattered (Q < 0.9), and the linear scaling is extracted from polynomial fits to lattice data rather than being directly measured.

## Method
The authors simulate a real-time scattering process between a meson wave-packet (jet) and a compact matter state created by an external electric field in the 1+1-d Schwinger model, using tensor network methods (matrix product states) with the time-dependent variational principle algorithm. The system is prepared by quenching the ground state, inserting a Gaussian wave-packet jet state with momentum |k| = a^{-1} on a 60-site lattice with lattice spacing a=1, coupling ga=2, and fermion mass ma=0.1, then evolving the system for total simulation time t_measurement a^{-1}=160. The matter state is created by an external electric field with charge 0 ≤ |Q| ≤ 1 located between lattice sites 27 and 33, turned off at t* a^{-1}=6, and the jet energy loss is computed by subtracting the vacuum contribution from the full simulation.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: N=60 sites, with initial electric field between sites 27 and 33
- Coupling constant: g a = 2, fermion mass: m a = 0.1 (reduced to m a = 0.0001 in the target region before t*)
- External charge range: 0 ≤ |Q| ≤ 1
- Turn-off time of external field: t* a^{-1} = 6
- Total simulation time: t_measurement a^{-1} = 160
- Scattering time: approximately 60 a
- Maximal bond dimension in simulations: D ~ 460 (below maximum allowed D=800)
- Transition to black-disk limit occurs at Q ≥ 0.9

## Context
This work extends previous real-time scattering studies in the Schwinger model, particularly the meson wave-packet scattering work of Pichler (2015) and Papaefstathiou (2024), by introducing a composite matter target rather than scattering identical states. The three identified regimes (ballistic propagation, intermediate excitation, and black-disk limit) provide a 1+1-d analog to QCD phenomena in heavy ion collisions and deep inelastic scattering, as described by Gribov (1969) and Bjorken (1973). The linear path-length dependence of energy loss resembles the QCD bremsstrahlung findings of Baier (1998) and Zakharov (1997), and the results enable future translation of these tensor network simulations to quantum simulator platforms for studying real-time non-perturbative dynamics.
