# Non-Gaussian fluctuation dynamics in relativistic fluids

> We consider non-equilibrium evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations within relativistic hydrodynamics relevant for the QCD critical point search in heavy-ion collision experiments. We rely on the hierarchy of relaxation time scales, which emerges in the hydrodynamic regime near the critical point, to focus on the slowest mode such as the fluctuations of specific entropy, whose equilibrium magnitude, non-Gaussianity and typical relaxation time are increasing as the critical point is approached. We derive evolution equations for the non-Gaussian correlators of this diffusive mode in an arbitrary relativistic hydrodynamic flow. We compare with the simpler case of the stochastic diffusion on a static homogeneous background and identify terms which are specific to the case of the full hydrodynamics with pressure fluctuations and flow.

## Key claim

The authors derive evolution equations for non-Gaussian (three- and four-point) connected correlation functions of the slowest diffusive mode (specific entropy) in relativistic hydrodynamic flow, extending the Hydro+ formalism beyond Gaussian fluctuations, but they restrict to "tree-level" terms and assume faster hydrodynamic modes (such as pressure fluctuations) can be treated as equilibrated, which nonetheless modify the equations via nonlinear mode coupling.

## Method

The authors expand stochastic relativistic hydrodynamic equations in powers of fluctuation magnitude around a background flow, using the confluent formalism for Lorentz covariance and a multipoint Wigner transform to express n-point correlators in terms of midpoint coordinates and wave vectors. They focus on the specific entropy mode \(m = s/n\) (entropy per baryon), which is the slowest diffusive mode near the QCD critical point, and derive evolution equations for two-, three-, and four-point connected correlation functions by truncating the infinite hierarchy at leading order in the small parameters \(\varepsilon\) (fluctuation magnitude) and \(\varepsilon_q\) (gradient expansion). The derivation accounts for multiplicative noise and the coupling to faster modes like pressure fluctuations through nonlinearities in the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The small parameter controlling fluctuation magnitude is \(\varepsilon \equiv (\xi/\ell)^3 \sim (q\xi)^3\), where \(\xi\) is the correlation length and \(\ell \sim 1/q\) is the homogeneity scale.
- The magnitude of fluctuations is suppressed by \(\sqrt{\varepsilon}\), and non-Gaussian connected correlators of order \(n>2\) are suppressed by \(\varepsilon^{n-1}\).
- The Onsager operator \(Q\) scales as \(\varepsilon_q^2 \varepsilon\), where \(\varepsilon_q \equiv q \ell_{\rm mic}\) with \(\ell_{\rm mic}\) the microscopic scale.
- The system size in heavy-ion collisions is \(\mathcal O(10 \text{ fm})\), large compared to the typical correlation length \(\xi\) (a fraction of fm).

## Context

This work extends the Hydro+ formalism of Stephanov (2018) and the deterministic (hydrokinetic) approach of An, Basar, Stephanov, and Yee (2019-2022) from Gaussian to non-Gaussian fluctuation measures, building on the earlier treatment of nonlinear charge diffusion at constant temperature by An (2021). It enables quantitative predictions for non-Gaussian fluctuation observables (e.g., skewness and kurtosis) in heavy-ion collisions near the QCD critical point, where critical slowing down makes non-equilibrium evolution essential, and complements stochastic approaches such as those of Nahrgang (2018).
