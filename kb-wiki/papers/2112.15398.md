# Critical behavior towards the chiral limit at vanishing and non-vanishing chemical potentials

> We study the scaling behavior of the (2+1)-flavor QCD crossover region towards the chiral limit with smaller-than-physical light quark mass gauge ensembles, generated using the HISQ fermion discretization. At zero chemical potential, we study the fluctuations of conserved charges and their correlations with the chiral condensate, towards the chiral limit. We analyse the role of universal and regular contributions to the above quantities. We find a preliminary estimate of the leading curvature coefficient of the chiral phase transition line using scaling arguments.

# Critical behavior towards the chiral limit at vanishing and non-vanishing chemical potentials

## Key claim
The (2+1)-flavor QCD crossover region shows scaling behavior consistent with a second-order chiral phase transition belonging to the 3d O(2) universality class (O(4) in the continuum limit), but the regular (non-universal) contributions to observables such as the strange mass derivative of the light quark condensate remain non-negligible at the light quark masses studied, preventing a clean scaling collapse. The leading curvature coefficient of the chiral phase transition line appears to change only weakly as the chiral limit is approached.

## Method
The authors generated gauge ensembles using the HISQ fermion discretization with tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action at fixed temporal extent N_tau = 8, with light quark masses ranging from physical (m_l = m_s/27) down to m_s/160, corresponding to pion masses from 140 MeV to 58 MeV. They computed second, fourth, and sixth order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) at zero chemical potential, as well as mixed observables involving derivatives of the light quark chiral condensate with respect to both the strange quark mass and chemical potentials. The curvature of the chiral phase transition line was estimated by taking the ratio of the second chemical potential derivative to the temperature derivative of the chiral condensate at the chiral transition temperature.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses of the ensembles: 140 MeV (m_l = m_s/27), 110 MeV (m_l = m_s/40), 80 MeV (m_l = m_s/80), and 58 MeV (m_l = m_s/160)
- Chiral phase transition temperature at N_tau = 8: T_c^(N_tau=8) approximately 144 MeV (from HotQCD 2019)
- Pseudo-critical temperature at physical mass and N_tau = 8: T_pc^(N_tau=8) approximately 161 MeV
- Scale setting uses kaon decay constant f_K = 156.1/sqrt(2) MeV (from Bazavov 2010)
- Aspect ratios N_sigma/N_tau in the range 4-7 for the largest simulated volumes
- The singular part of second order cumulants at T = T_c scales linearly with H^((1-alpha)/(beta*delta)) = H^0.61 using 3d O(2) critical exponents

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition towards the chiral limit, building on the determination of T_c approximately 132 MeV in the chiral limit by HotQCD (2019) and on scaling analyses using 3d O(2) and O(4) universality class scaling functions from Engels (2000, 2011) and Friman (2011). The results are compared to continuum extrapolated curvature coefficients at physical mass from HotQCD (2018). The preliminary curvature estimates in the chiral limit are relevant for locating the tricritical point and the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, as discussed by Hatta (2002). The authors plan future scaling analyses using known O(2) scaling functions and studies at multiple lattice spacings and volumes to take continuum and thermodynamic limits.
