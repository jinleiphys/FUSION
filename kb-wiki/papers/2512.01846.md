# Many-channel microscopic cluster model of $^{8}$Be: S-factors

> We investigate low--energy astrophysical $S$ factors for reactions proceeding through the $^{8}$Be compound system with entrance channels $p+{}^{7}$Li, $n+{}^{7}$Be, and $d+{}^{6}$Li. Using the same microscopic many--channel three--cluster framework as in our previous study of the high--lying $^{8}$Be spectrum, we calculate $S(E)$ for $^{7}$Li($p,α)^{4}$He, $^{7}$Be($n,α)^{4}$He, $^{7}$Be($n,p)^{7}$Li, $^{6}$Li($d,α)^{4}$He, $^{6}$Li($d,p)^{7}$Li, and $^{6}$Li($d,n)^{7}$Be in the energy range relevant for primordial and stellar nucleosynthesis. For the mirror pair $^{7}$Li($p,α)^{4}$He / $^{7}$Be($n,α)^{4}$He and for $^{7}$Be($n,p)^{7}$Li the calculated $S$ factors reproduce both the absolute scale and the low--energy trends of the experimental data within their quoted uncertainties, whereas the absolute $S$ factors for the deuteron--induced channels on $^{6}$Li are underestimated at low energy, consistent with the shifted $^{6}$Li+$d$ threshold and the absence of a broad subthreshold $2^{+}$ structure in the present implementation. A partial--wave analysis identifies the dominant $J^π$ contributions in each channel and relates them to specific $^{8}$Be resonances, while demonstrating that cluster polarization, previously shown to be crucial for the $^{8}$Be spectrum, is likewise essential for the normalization and energy dependence of several $S$ factors. Evaluating $S(E)$ at appropriate Gamow energies, we obtain a hierarchy of reaction channels that quantifies the relative importance of neutron-- and deuteron--induced processes for the production and destruction of $^{7}$Li and $^{7}$Be.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified microscopic many-channel three-cluster model can reproduce the absolute scale and low-energy trends of astrophysical S-factors for reactions proceeding through the compound nucleus ⁸Be, but only for the mirror pair ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He / ⁷Be(n,α)⁴He and for ⁷Be(n,p)⁷Li within experimental uncertainties; the deuteron-induced channels on ⁶Li are systematically underestimated at low energy due to a shifted ⁶Li+d threshold and the absence of a broad subthreshold 2⁺ structure in the present implementation.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic many-channel three-cluster model that includes all eight binary partitions of ⁸Be, with the total wave function written as a superposition of channel wave functions describing each binary partition. They solved the Schrödinger equation with the Hasegawa-Nagata nucleon-nucleon potential, using the same parameters as in their previous study of the high-lying ⁸Be spectrum (Paper I), and extracted astrophysical S-factors from the scattering S-matrix elements for six reactions at low entrance-channel energies relevant for primordial and stellar nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

* Gamow peak energy for the p+⁷Li channel: E₀ = 209 keV with width ΔE₀ = 277 keV at T = 0.8 GK
* Gamow peak energy for the d+⁶Li channel: E₀ = 250 keV with width ΔE₀ = 303 keV at T = 0.8 GK
* Gamow peak energy for the n+⁷Be channel: E₀ = 103 keV with width ΔE₀ = 95 keV at T = 0.8 GK (using l=1 for the effective-energy window)
* A 2⁺ resonance in ⁸Be is located at E ≈ 1.27 MeV above the ⁷Be+n threshold in the microscopic calculation
* A 3⁺ resonance lies about 0.53 MeV above the ⁷Be+n threshold, generating a peak in the ⁷Be(n,p₀)⁷Li S-factor near E_cm ≈ 0.5 MeV
* A 1⁻ resonance at E ≈ 0.45 MeV above the ⁷Be+n threshold dominates the ⁷Be(n,p₁)⁷Li channel

## Context

This work extends the microscopic many-channel three-cluster framework previously applied to the high-lying ⁸Be spectrum (Zhaba, Lashko, Vasilevsky, 2025, Paper I) to low-energy astrophysical S-factors. It addresses a gap left by earlier microscopic calculations, such as those by Descouvemont and Baye (1994) who treated ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He in a generator-coordinate three-cluster model but overestimated both the absolute scale and low-energy slope, and by DWBA analyses for ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He (Yamamuro, 1995) and ⁶Li(d,α)⁴He (Ruprecht, 2004). The results enable a unified microscopic description of six reactions through ⁸Be, providing a hierarchy of reaction channels at Gamow energies that quantifies the relative importance of neutron- and deuteron-induced processes for the production and destruction of ⁷Li and ⁷Be, relevant to the cosmological lithium problem.
