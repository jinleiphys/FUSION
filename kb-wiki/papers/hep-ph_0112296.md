# Lectures on Chiral Disorder in QCD

> I explain the concept that light quarks diffuse in the QCD vacuum following the spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry. I exploit the striking analogy to disordered electrons in metals, identifying, among others, the universal regime described by random matrix theory, diffusive regime described by chiral perturbation theory and the crossover between these two domains.

## Key claim

Light quarks in the QCD vacuum behave analogously to disordered electrons in metals, with the spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry arising from a diffusive mechanism; the analogy identifies four distinct spectral regimes (ballistic, diffusive, ergodic, quantum) whose boundaries are determined by the system volume and the pion mass, but the detailed microscopic nature of the disorder remains unknown.

## Method

The author constructs a return probability for quarks at zero virtuality by analytically continuing the pion correlation function from chiral perturbation theory, using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and the Banks-Casher relation to connect the spectral density of the Dirac operator to the quark condensate. The diffusion constant is extracted from low-energy theorems without assuming any specific model of the gluonic disorder, yielding an effective description in terms of soft "diffuson" modes that are identified with pions. The hierarchy of spectral scales is then mapped by comparing the Schwinger proper time (dual to the Dirac eigenvalue) to the diffusion time scales familiar from mesoscopic physics.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion constant of the QCD vacuum: \( D = F_\pi^2 / |\langle \bar{q}q \rangle| \sim 0.22 \) fm
- Thouless virtuality (crossover between ergodic and diffusive regimes): \( \Lambda_c = D/L^2 = F_\pi^2/(\Sigma \sqrt{V}) \), where \( \Sigma = |\langle \bar{q}q \rangle| \)
- Coherence length of the QCD vacuum: \( L_{\text{coh}} = \sqrt{D/\gamma} = 1/M_\pi \), with \( \gamma = 2m \)
- Mean level spacing in the ergodic regime scales as \( \Delta \sim 1/V \)
- Borderline between diffusive and ballistic regimes is given by twice the constituent quark mass, approximately the mass of the \( \rho \) meson (770 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the analogy between QCD and disordered systems pioneered by the instanton model of Diakonov and Petrov and the numerical simulations of Shuryak, providing a unified framework that connects random matrix theory (universal regime) with chiral perturbation theory (diffusive regime). The analysis builds on the Banks-Casher relation and the finite-volume chiral perturbation theory of Gasser and Leutwyler, showing that the zero-mode approximation in that framework corresponds to the ergodic regime of the Dirac spectrum. The results enable systematic classification of spectral fluctuations in lattice QCD calculations and suggest possible multi-critical scaling in the presence of external parameters such as temperature or density.
