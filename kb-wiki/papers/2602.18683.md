# Nucleon Size Independence of Hadronic Nucleus-Nucleus Cross Sections

> Recent proposals to constrain the nucleon size using hadronic cross sections ($σ_{\rm AA}$) conflict with collective flow data. I demonstrate this dependence is an artifact of ``geometric inflation,'' where smearing point-like nucleons unintentionally dilutes the nuclear surface. By implementing a self-consistent framework that preserves the global nuclear density, I show that $σ_{\rm AA}$ is essentially insensitive to the nucleon width. This establishes $σ_{\rm AA}$ as a robust probe of the nuclear surface rather than the sub-nucleon scale. Utilizing this property, I extract a neutron skin thickness for $^{208}$Pb of $Δr_{\rm np} \in [0, 0.24]$~fm, providing an unconventional way to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy using high-energy hadronic observables.

# Nucleon Size Independence of Hadronic Nucleus-Nucleus Cross Sections

## Key claim
The previously reported sensitivity of the hadronic nucleus-nucleus cross section ($\sigma_{\rm AA}$) to nucleon size is an artifact of "geometric inflation," where smearing point-like nucleons with finite-width Gaussian profiles unintentionally dilutes the nuclear surface. When a self-consistent framework preserving the global nuclear density is implemented, $\sigma_{\rm AA}$ becomes essentially insensitive to the nucleon width, establishing it as a robust probe of the nuclear surface rather than sub-nucleon structure.

## Method
The author demonstrates that the standard Monte Carlo sampling of nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution, followed by convolution with Gaussian kernels of width $w$, produces a matter profile broader than the target distribution. To recover the target Woods-Saxon density, the sampling distribution for nucleon centers is modified via an inverse Weierstrass transform, using a Woods-Saxon form with corrected parameters ($\tilde{R}$, $\tilde{a}$) constrained by matching the first three radial moments of the convolved density to the target distribution. The corrected parameters are applied to compute $\sigma_{\rm AA}$ for $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, and the neutron skin thickness is extracted by comparing the calculated cross section with ALICE measurements.

## Key numbers
- $\sigma_{\rm AA} \simeq 7.43$ b for $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV when using corrected sampling parameters, essentially independent of nucleon width $w$
- ALICE measurement: $\sigma_{\rm AA}=7.67\pm0.25$ b for $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- Extracted neutron skin thickness for $^{208}$Pb: $\Delta r_{\rm np} \in [0, 0.24]$ fm
- For $w$ identified with proton charge radius ($r_{\rm ch,p}=0.8414$ fm), ALICE data imply $\Delta r_{\rm np} \in [0, 0.176]$ fm
- For $w \approx 0.5$ fm (gluonic radius), $\Delta r_{\rm np} \in [0.069, 0.243]$ fm
- Nuclear symmetry energy slope constraint: $L \in [-69, 97]$ MeV, derived from the linear relation $\Delta r_{\rm np} \approx 0.101 + 0.00147L$
- Physical upper bound for nucleon width in $^{208}$Pb: $w \approx 0.9$ fm
- Corrected Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{208}$Pb with Gaussian kernel at $r_p = r_{\rm ch,p}$: $\tilde{R} \approx 6.672$ fm, $\tilde{a} \approx 0.456$ fm
- Cross section variation with nucleon profile shape: $\sigma_{\rm AA}=7.41$ b (Gaussian), $7.55$ b (Dipole), $7.60$ b (Monopole) at fixed $r_p = r_{\rm ch,p}$

## Context
This work resolves a tension between Bayesian analyses that favor $w \sim 1$ fm (Bernhard 2019, JETSCAPE 2020, Nijs 2020) and proposals to constrain nucleon size using $\sigma_{\rm AA}$ that yield $w \approx 0.4$–$0.5$ fm (Nijs 2022). The geometric inflation artifact is well-documented in quantum molecular dynamics for intermediate-energy reactions (TMEP 2016, Yang 2021, Xiang 2025) and in extracting point-proton densities from charge distributions (Klos 2007, Salcedo 1987, Oset 1989), but had been overlooked in relativistic heavy-ion simulations. The self-consistent framework enables $\sigma_{\rm AA}$ to serve as a precision probe of the neutron skin thickness, providing an unconventional constraint on the nuclear symmetry energy slope $L$ that is consistent with global constraints (Tsang 2012, Li 2021) and compatible with the lower bound of the PREX-II measurement (Adhikari 2021, Reed 2021). Future electron–ion collider data (AbdulKhalek 2021) could further constrain the nucleon's gluonic shape to reduce the $\sim 0.2$ b systematic uncertainty from nucleon profile shape.
