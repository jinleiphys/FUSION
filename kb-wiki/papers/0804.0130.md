# Role of deformation on giant resonances within the QRPA approach and the Gogny force

> Fully consistent axially-symmetric-deformed Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations have been performed, in which the same Gogny D1S effective force has been used for both the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov mean field and the QRPA approaches. Giant resonances calculated in deformed $^{26-28}$Si and $^{22-24}$Mg nuclei as well as in the spherical $^{30}$Si and $^{28}$Mg isotopes are presented. Theoretical results for isovector-dipole and isoscalar monopole, quadrupole, and octupole responses are presented and the impact of the intrinsic nuclear deformation is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fully consistent axially-symmetric-deformed QRPA calculations using the Gogny D1S force can reproduce giant resonance properties in light s-d shell nuclei, but the mean energies of isoscalar monopole and quadrupole resonances are found to be nearly independent of mass number, contrary to the standard A⁻¹/³ systematic laws, and the theoretical GQR energies overestimate experimental values by 10-20% due to the effective mass of the D1S interaction.

## Method

Fully consistent axially-symmetric-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the Gogny D1S effective force, followed by Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations using the same force in all particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole channels. The HFB equations were solved in a harmonic oscillator basis of 9 major shells with high convergence accuracy, and all quasi-particle states were used to generate the two-quasi-particle excitation set without energy or occupation probability cuts. The QRPA equations were solved separately for each K^π block, and states of good angular momentum J were obtained by angular momentum projection of the intrinsic excitations.

## Key numbers

- In spherical nuclei ²⁸Mg and ³⁰Si, a highly collective isovector dipole state is found at 23 MeV, with two less collective components around 20 MeV and 25 MeV
- In ²⁴Mg, the K^π=0^- QRPA state is at 19.35 MeV (dominated by 1d5/2→1f7/2 transitions) and the |K|^π=1^- state is at 27.44 MeV (dominated by 1p3/2→2s1/2 transitions)
- The width of the GDR in ²⁴Mg is found to be 9±1 MeV, in fair agreement with experiment
- Theoretical IVGDR mean energies range from 22.80 MeV (²⁸Si) to 23.18 MeV (²⁴Mg), roughly constant across isotopes, while systematic values vary from 21.7 MeV (³⁰Si) to 23.4 MeV (²²Mg)
- In prolate ²²⁻²⁴Mg nuclei, high-energy |K|^π=1^- dipole components exhaust 54% of the EWSR and low-energy K=0 components exhaust 29%
- In oblate nuclei, high-energy K=0 dipole components exhaust around 35% of the EWSR and low-energy components around 50%
- Theoretical GMR mean energies range from 21.06 MeV (²⁴Mg) to 21.99 MeV (²⁶Mg), compared to experimental values of 21.0±0.6 MeV (²⁴Mg) and 21.25±0.38 MeV (²⁸Si)
- Theoretical GQR mean energies range from 20.41 MeV (²⁸Si) to 21.44 MeV (³⁰Si), compared to experimental values of 16.9±0.6 MeV (²⁴Mg) and 18.54±0.25 MeV (²⁸Si)
- In ²⁴Mg, the theoretical GQR energy (exhausting 90% of EWSR) is 3.64 MeV larger than the experimental value (exhausting 72±10% of EWSR)
- In ²⁸Si, the theoretical GQR energy (exhausting 91% of EWSR) is 1.87 MeV larger than the experimental value (exhausting 68±9% of EWSR)
- Theoretical LEOR mean energies range from 11.18 MeV (²⁸Mg) to 13.49 MeV (²⁴Mg), larger than systematic values of 30A⁻¹/³ MeV
- Theoretical HEOR mean energies range from 34.48 MeV (²²Mg) to 35.72 MeV (²⁶Mg)

## Context

This work extends the fully consistent QRPA approach using the Gogny force to deformed nuclei, building on earlier spherical RPA and QRPA calculations by Thouless (1961), Blaizot and Ripka, and others. The results are compared with experimental data on ²⁴Mg and ²⁸Si from Youngblood et al. (2002, 2007) and with macroscopic model predictions reviewed by Bortignon, Bracco, and Broglia (1998). The approach enables systematic studies of giant resonances across the nuclear chart without adjustable parameters, and the authors note that the mismatch with experimental GQR energies is likely due to the effective mass (m*/m=0.7) of the D1S interaction.
