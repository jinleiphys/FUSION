# Quark-meson coupling model with short-range quark-quark interactions

> Short-range quark-quark correlations are introduced into the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model phenomenologically. We study the effect of the correlations on the structure of the nucleon in dense nuclear matter. With the addition of correlations, the saturation curve for symmetric nuclear matter is much improved at high density.

## Key claim

Short-range quark-quark correlations, introduced phenomenologically into the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model via scalar- and vector-type contact interactions weighted by a geometrical overlap probability, improve the saturation curve for symmetric nuclear matter at high density, bringing it into the empirical region.

## Method

The authors add scalar- and vector-type contact interactions between quarks in different nucleons to the QMC Lagrangian, treating them in mean-field approximation (MFA). The probability for nucleon bags to overlap is calculated using a simple geometrical approach based on the MIT bag radius, with the two-nucleon correlation function taken from Miller and Spencer (1985). The Dirac equation for a quark in a nucleon bag is then solved with an effective quark mass modified by both the usual sigma-meson field and the scalar-type short-range correlation, and the total energy per nucleon is computed self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Free nucleon mass: \(M = 939\) MeV.
- Bare quark mass: \(m_q = 5\) MeV.
- Free-space bag radius: \(R_0 = 0.8\) fm.
- Bag constant: \(B^{1/4} = 170.0\) MeV; bag parameter \(z = 3.295\).
- Dimensionless coupling constants for short-range correlations: \(\bar{f}_s^q = 40\), \(\bar{f}_v^q = 8\).
- For correlation length \(\ell_c = 0.75\) fm: \(\alpha = 1.05\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(\beta = 0.617\) fm\(^{-2}\), wound integral \(\kappa = 0.12\) at \(\rho_0\), \(g_\sigma^2 = 50.09\), \(g_\omega^2 = 37.22\), incompressibility \(K = 360\) MeV.
- For correlation length \(\ell_c = 0.7\) fm: \(\alpha = 1.20\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(\beta = 0.708\) fm\(^{-2}\), wound integral \(\kappa = 0.10\) at \(\rho_0\), \(g_\sigma^2 = 48.11\), \(g_\omega^2 = 33.80\), incompressibility \(K = 370\) MeV.
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: \(M^\star/M \sim 0.84\).
- Free-space rms radius of the quark wave function: \(r_q = 0.5824\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the original QMC model of Guichon (1988) and its subsequent developments, which were limited to low and moderate densities by the assumption of non-overlapping nucleon bags. By introducing short-range quark-quark correlations, the model now reproduces the empirical saturation curve at high density, where earlier QMC calculations deviated. The approach enables future studies of nuclear matter at high density, including the region near the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, and suggests connections to higher-order terms in chiral effective Lagrangians.
