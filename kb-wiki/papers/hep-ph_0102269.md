# Strangeness in the nucleon: neutrino-nucleon and polarized electron-nucleon scattering

> After the EMC and subsequent experiments at CERN, SLAC and DESY on the deep inelastic scattering of polarized leptons on polarized nucleons, it is now established that the Q^2=0 value of the axial strange form factor of the nucleon, a quantity which is connected with the spin of the proton and is quite relevant from the theoretical point of view, is relatively large. In this review we consider different methods and observables that allow to obtain information on the strange axial and vector form factors of the nucleon at different values of Q^2. These methods are based on the investigation of the Neutral Current induced effects such as the P-odd asymmetry in the scattering of polarized electrons on protons and nuclei, the elastic neutrino (antineutrino) scattering on protons and the quasi-elastic neutrino (antineutrino) scattering on nuclei. We discuss in details the phenomenology of these processes and the existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that the axial strange form factor of the nucleon at \(Q^2=0\) is relatively large, but stresses that this conclusion depends on the renormalization scheme and on NLO QCD corrections; the extracted matrix element \(\langle p|\bar{s}\gamma_{\alpha}\gamma_5 s|p\rangle\) is scale-dependent and its numerical value is obtained only after applying specific QCD corrections to the polarized deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The authors review the determination of strange vector and axial form factors of the nucleon by analyzing neutral-current observables: the parity-violating asymmetry in polarized electron-nucleon and electron-nucleus scattering, elastic neutrino (antineutrino) scattering on protons, and quasi-elastic neutrino (antineutrino) scattering on nuclei. They derive the relevant hadronic matrix elements using the Standard Model neutral-current Lagrangian, isospin symmetry, and the known electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon, then discuss the phenomenological extraction of strange form factors from existing experimental data.

## Context

This review extends the analysis of nucleon strangeness beyond the deep inelastic scattering results from EMC (Ashman 1989), SMC (Adeva 1998), SLAC (Abe 1995, 1998; Anthony 2000), and DESY HERMES (1997, 1998) by considering neutral-current processes at lower \(Q^2\). It builds on the theoretical framework of chiral perturbation theory (Gasser 1991) and the operator product expansion (Politz 1974) to connect strange form factors to observables in elastic neutrino scattering and polarized electron scattering, enabling future experiments to extract the \(Q^2\) dependence of the strange vector and axial form factors.
