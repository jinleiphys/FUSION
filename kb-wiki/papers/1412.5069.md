# Thermodynamics of pairing transition in hot nuclei

> The pairing correlations in hot nuclei $^{162}$Dy are investigated in terms of the thermodynamical properties by covariant density functional theory. The heat capacities $C_V$ are evaluated in the canonical ensemble theory and the paring correlations are treated by a shell-model-like approach, in which the particle number is conserved exactly. A S-shaped heat capacity curve, which agrees qualitatively with the experimental data, has been obtained and analyzed in details. It is found that the one-pair-broken states play crucial roles in the appearance of the S shape of the heat capacity curve. Moreover, due to the effect of the particle-number conservation, the pairing gap varies smoothly with the temperature, which indicates a gradual transition from the superfluid to the normal state.

## Key claim

The S-shaped heat capacity curve in hot nuclei, which is often interpreted as a fingerprint of a superfluid-to-normal phase transition, arises primarily from the contribution of one-pair-broken (seniority s=2) states, and the transition is gradual rather than sharp when particle number is conserved exactly.

## Method

The authors used covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PK1 effective interaction to obtain single-particle states for the nucleus 162Dy. Pairing correlations were treated by the shell-model-like approach (SLAP), which diagonalizes a pairing Hamiltonian in a multi-particle configuration space with exact particle-number conservation and exact blocking effects. Thermodynamic quantities including heat capacity, entropy, and level density were then evaluated in the canonical ensemble using the resulting many-body wave functions and excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength for neutrons was fixed to G_n = 0.29 MeV and for protons to G_p = 0.32 MeV, with an energy cutoff E_c = 30 MeV.
- The critical temperature estimated from experimental data for 161,162Dy is T_c ≈ 0.5 MeV (cited from Schiller (2001)).
- The heat capacity is nearly zero at temperatures T ≤ 0.35 MeV due to the pairing gap.
- The inflection point in the heat capacity curve occurs at T ≈ 0.75 MeV.
- The dimensions of the proton and neutron multi-particle configuration spaces are about 3×10^5 and 5×10^5, respectively.
- Six valence particle pairs (highest seniority 12) were included for both neutrons and protons.
- The pairing gap calculated by SLAP does not vanish up to T = 1 MeV, indicating a gradual transition.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the S-shaped heat capacity in hot nuclei, which had been observed experimentally for 161,162Dy, 171,172Yb (Schiller, 2001) and 166,167Er (Melby, 2001), and had been reproduced by mean-field models including finite-temperature BCS (Sano, 1963; Goodman, 1981) and finite-temperature HFB (Goodman, 1986). The present approach improves upon these by using the shell-model-like approach within CDFT (Meng, 2006), which conserves particle number exactly, in contrast to standard BCS or HFB. The results show a smooth variation of the pairing gap with temperature, similar to particle-number projected approaches such as variation-after-projection BCS (Gambacurta, 2013), but with exact treatment of blocking effects. This enables a detailed microscopic analysis of the seniority components contributing to the heat capacity and level density.
