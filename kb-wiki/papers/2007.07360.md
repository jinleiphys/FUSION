# Effective Field Theory for Two-Body Systems with Shallow S-Wave Resonances

> Resonances are of particular importance to the scattering of composite particles in quantum mechanics. We build an effective field theory for two-body scattering which includes a low-energy $S$-wave resonance. Our starting point is the most general Lagrangian with short-range interactions. We demonstrate that these interactions can be organized into various orders so as to generate a systematic expansion for an $S$ matrix with two low-energy poles. The pole positions are restricted by renormalization at leading order, where the common feature is a non-positive effective range. We carry out the expansion explicitly to next-to-leading order and illustrate how it systematically accounts for the results of a toy model -- a spherical well with a delta shell at its border.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for two-body S-wave scattering that can describe shallow resonances, but only when the effective range is non-positive ($r_0 \le 0$), which is a constraint from renormalization that the abstract mentions but does not emphasize as a limiting condition. The authors argue that this is not a flaw but rather the correct power counting for a system with two fine-tunings (large scattering length and large effective range), as opposed to the single fine-tuning case relevant for nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors construct the most general Lagrangian with short-range contact interactions for a single particle species, then organize the low-energy constants (LECs) according to a power counting where both the zero-derivative ($C_0$) and two-derivative ($C_2$) interactions are treated nonperturbatively at leading order (LO), while higher-derivative interactions enter perturbatively at next-to-leading order (NLO) and beyond. They solve the Schrodinger equation exactly for the LO potential with a momentum cutoff regulator, renormalize by matching to the effective-range expansion (ERE) parameters $a_0$ and $r_0$, then treat the NLO four-derivative interaction in distorted-wave perturbation theory. The expansion is carried out explicitly to NLO and tested against a toy model consisting of a spherical well with a delta shell at its border.

## Key numbers

- The LO scattering amplitude reproduces the first two terms of the ERE: $k\cot\delta_0^{(0)}(k) = -1/a_0 + (r_0/2)k^2$, with $a_0 \sim 1/M_{lo}$ and $r_0 \sim 1/M_{lo}$ for a shallow resonance
- The renormalization condition forces $r_0 < 0$ for real LECs, consistent with the Wigner bound $r_0 \leq 2R(1 - R/a_0 + R^2/3a_0^2)$ where $R \sim 1/\Lambda$ is the range
- The LO error in $k\cot\delta_0^{(0)}(k)$ is estimated as $\Delta(k\cot\delta_0^{(0)}(k)) = (r_0^2/4\theta_1\Lambda)k^4$, which is $\mathcal{O}(M_{lo}/M_{hi})$ relative to the LO result when $\Lambda \sim M_{hi}$
- The LECs scale as $C_0 = \mathcal{O}(1/M_{lo})$, $C_2 = \mathcal{O}(1/M_{lo}^3)$, and $C_{n\ge 4} = \mathcal{O}(1/M_{lo}^{n/2+2}M_{hi}^{n/2-1})$ for the resonant case
- At NLO, the shape parameter scales as $|P_0| \sim M_{lo}/M_{hi}$

## Context

This work extends the EFT framework for shallow S-wave states beyond the well-studied case of a single bound or virtual state (van Kolck 1997, Kaplan 1998) to the case of two shallow poles including resonances. It builds on earlier work by Beane, Cohen, and Phillips (1997) who showed that iterating both $C_0$ and $C_2$ to all orders requires $r_0 \le 0$ for renormalizability, which they viewed as problematic for nuclear physics; the present paper reinterprets this constraint as the signature of a different fine-tuning regime appropriate for resonant systems. The momentum-dependent interactions developed here are intended to be more easily incorporated into ab initio methods for few-body systems than the energy-dependent "dimeron" field approaches used previously (Bertulani 2002, Bedaque 2003, Higa 2008). Future work aims to include the Coulomb interaction to describe phenomenological S-wave resonances such as the $^8$Be ground state in alpha-alpha scattering.
