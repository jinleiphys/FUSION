# Stochastic Pionisation in Hot Quark-Gluon Matter

> We present a microscopic approach to dynamical pionisation of the hot quark-gluon matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The time evolution of the system is described assuming that quarks undergo Brownian motion in a thermal bath provided by the gluons. The rate of hadronization as well as the time dependence of the temperature of the system are seen to be quite sensitive to the QCD $Λ$ parameter. Even in a non-equilibrium scenario, we find that there appears a clear hint of a first order phase transition.

# Stochastic Pionisation in Hot Quark-Gluon Matter

## Key claim

Even in a non-equilibrium scenario, the microscopic stochastic hadronization process yields a clear hint of an apparent first order phase transition, but the authors stress this is only an approximate transition with no unique demarcation between phases, and the critical temperature of approximately 215 MeV is model-dependent, deriving from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model.

## Method

The authors model quarks as Brownian particles moving in a thermal gluonic bath, with hadronization treated via Smoluchowski's theory of coagulation: pions form when a quark and an antiquark with appropriate quantum numbers come within a sphere of influence whose radius is the pion screening length from the NJL model. Rate equations for pion and quark number densities, including pion decay back to quarks and Bjorken longitudinal expansion, are solved self-consistently with energy-momentum conservation for a system initialized at RHIC conditions (initial gluon thermalisation time 0.3 fm, temperature 500 MeV, quark fugacity 0.15).

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: gluon thermalisation time \( t_g = 0.3 \) fm, temperature \( T = 500 \) MeV, quark ratio \( r_q = 0.15 \), gluon ratio \( r_g = 1 \), pion ratio \( r_\pi = 0 \)
- Pion decay width vanishes around \( T = 215 \) MeV, corresponding to \( t \sim 3.5 \) fm
- For \( \Lambda = 0.2 \) or 0.3 GeV, the crossover from quark-dominated to pion-dominated system occurs at \( t \geq 4 \) fm; for \( \Lambda = 0.4 \) GeV, this happens at \( t \sim 6 \) fm
- For \( \Lambda = 0.4 \) GeV, the constant-temperature mixed phase persists up to 9 fm before cooling resumes
- Current quark mass taken as \( m_q(\text{curr}) = 10 \) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Alam, Raha, and Sinha (1994, 1996) and Alam et al. (1997) on non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma evolution, applying a Brownian motion picture to the hadronization problem. It contrasts with bubble nucleation models by Csernai and Kapusta (1992) that assume thermodynamic equilibrium, and with other semi-microscopic approaches such as the NJL model calculations by Rehberg, Klevansky, and Hufner (1996), string phenomenology codes by Werner (1993), and parton cascade models by Geiger and Ellis (1995). The results enable further investigation of non-perturbative modes persisting in the high-temperature phase and the existence of colour degrees of freedom at lower temperatures, which the authors identify as urgent issues for QGP diagnostics.
