# Description of the critical point symmetry in 124Te by IBM-2

> Based on the neutron and proton degrees of freedom, the low-lying energy levels, the $E2$, $M1$ and $E0$ transition strengths of nucleus $^{124}$Te have been calculated by the neutron-proton interacting boson model. The calculated results are quite consistent with the experimental data. By comparing the key observables of states at the critical point of $\textrm{U}_{πν}(5)$--$\textrm{O}_{πν}(6)$ transition with experimental data and calculated results, we show that the $^{124}$Te is a possible nucleus at the critical point of the second order phase transition from vibration to unstable rotation and such a critical point contains somewhat triaxial rotation. The 0$^+_2$ state of $^{124}$Te can be interpreted as the lowest state of the first excited family of intrinsic levels in the critical point symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors claim that \(^{124}\)Te is a possible nucleus at the critical point of the second-order phase transition from vibration (U\(_{\pi\nu}\)(5)) to \(\gamma\)-unstable rotation (O\(_{\pi\nu}\)(6)) in the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2), and that this critical point contains some triaxial rotation; the claim is hedged by noting that the description is based on comparing key observables with predictions for the infinite-boson-number limit and that the calculated \(M1\) strengths are systematically slightly larger than experiment, suggesting the actual nucleus may have somewhat less SU\(^*_{\pi\nu}\)(3) character than the model.

## Method

The authors performed numerical diagonalization of an IBM-2 Hamiltonian (including \(d\)-boson energies, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, dipole proton-neutron interaction, and Majorana terms) using the NPBOS code, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental low-lying energy levels and electromagnetic transition strengths of \(^{124}\)Te. They then compared the calculated energy ratios and \(B(E2)\), \(B(M1)\), and \(\rho^2(E0)\) transition strengths to experimental data and to the predicted key observables at the critical point of the U\(_{\pi\nu}\)(5)–O\(_{\pi\nu}\)(6) transition.

## Key numbers

* Energy ratio \(R_{4_1/2_1} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\): experimental 2.07, calculated 2.06, critical-point prediction 2.20.
* Energy ratio \(R_{2_2/2_1} = E(2^+_2)/E(2^+_1)\): experimental 2.20, calculated 2.13, critical-point prediction 2.20.
* Energy ratio \(R_{0_2/2_1} = E(0^+_2)/E(2^+_1)\): experimental 2.75, calculated 2.35, critical-point prediction 3.03.
* \(B(E2)\) ratio \(R_{B,42} = B(E2,4^+_1\rightarrow2^+_1)/B(E2,2^+_1\rightarrow0^+_1)\): experimental 1.74, calculated 1.55, critical-point prediction 1.68.
* \(B(E2)\) ratio \(R_{B,02} = B(E2,0^+_2\rightarrow2^+_1)/B(E2,2^+_1\rightarrow0^+_1)\): experimental 0.65, calculated 0.90, critical-point prediction 0.86.
* Electric monopole strength \(\rho^2(E0,0^+_2\rightarrow0^+_1)\times10^3\): experimental \(12\pm3\), calculated 11.00.
* \(B(M1,1^+_1\rightarrow0^+_1)\): experimental 0.04 \(\mu^2_N\), calculated 0.029 \(\mu^2_N\); for the O\(_{\pi\nu}\)(6) limit the value is 0.10 \(\mu^2_N\) and for the U\(_{\pi\nu}\)(5) limit the \(B(M1,1^+_1\rightarrow0^+_2)\) is 0.50 \(\mu^2_N\) (with \(g_\pi=1\), \(g_\nu=0\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies that debated whether \(^{124}\)Te exhibits U(5), O(6), or \(E(5)\)-like behavior (e.g., Hicks (2017), Clark (2004), Sabria (2016), Ghit (2008)) by applying the IBM-2 framework to explicitly include neutron-proton degrees of freedom and to test the critical-point predictions of the U\(_{\pi\nu}\)(5)–O\(_{\pi\nu}\)(6) transition. The results support the interpretation of the \(0^+_2\) state as the lowest state of the first excited family of intrinsic levels in the critical-point symmetry, and the calculated \(\rho^2(E0)\) value resolves a long-standing puzzle about shape coexistence in \(^{124}\)Te. This enables future studies of other Te isotopes and nuclei near the same phase transition using IBM-2 with explicit proton-neutron degrees of freedom.
