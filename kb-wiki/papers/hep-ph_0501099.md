# The QCD Pomeron in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions: IV. Photonuclear Production of Vector Mesons

> The photonuclear production of vector mesons in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is investigated within the QCD color dipole picture, with particular emphasis on the saturation model. The integrated cross section and the rapidity distribution for the A + A --> V + A + A (V = rho, omega, phi, J/Psi) process are computed and theoretical estimates for scattering on both light and heavy nuclei are given for energies of RHIC and LHC. A comparison with the recent STAR data on coherent production of rho mesons is also presented. Furthermore, we calculate the photoproduction of vector mesons in proton-proton collisions at RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The QCD color dipole picture with the saturation model, when extended to nuclear targets via the Glauber-Gribov formalism, provides predictions for vector meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions that are consistent with the STAR data on coherent rho production at RHIC, but the authors note that other approaches with very distinct assumptions also describe the same HERA data, so the description of the QCD Pomeron remains an open question.

## Method

The authors compute the photonuclear production of vector mesons (rho, omega, phi, J/Psi) in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions using the QCD color dipole picture, where the process is factorized into the photon fluctuation into a quark-antiquark pair and the dipole-nucleus cross section. The dipole-proton cross section is taken from the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff saturation model, extended to nuclear targets using the Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering formalism with a 3-parameter Fermi nuclear density distribution. The vector meson wavefunctions are modeled using the DGKP approach with Gaussian dependence on the dipole transverse separation, and the photonuclear cross section is obtained by convoluting the photon flux from the Weizsacker-Williams method with the gamma-A to V-A cross section.

## Key numbers

* Integrated cross section for AuAu to AuAu rho at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): 876 mb
* Integrated cross section for AuAu to AuAu J/Psi at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): 476 microbarn
* Integrated cross section for PbPb to PbPb rho at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5500 GeV): 10069 mb
* Integrated cross section for PbPb to PbPb J/Psi at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5500 GeV): 41.5 mb
* Integrated cross section for SiSi to SiSi rho at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): 6.74 mb
* Integrated cross section for CaCa to CaCa rho at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5500 GeV): 128 mb
* Maximum gamma-N center-of-mass energy W_gamma_N reaches 950 GeV for LHC heavy ion mode (gamma_L=2930)
* Saturation model parameters from fit to HERA data: sigma_0=23.03 mb (3-flavor), lambda=0.288, x_0=3.04e-4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of nuclear exclusive vector meson production for future electron-nucleus colliders (Goncalves and Machado, 2004) to ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. It provides a partonic-basis alternative to the hadronic-basis calculations using vector meson dominance (VDM) by Klein and Nystrand (1999) and the GVDM plus Glauber-Gribov approach of Strikman and collaborators. The results enable comparison with the first STAR data on coherent rho production in gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV and provide predictions for future measurements at LHC energies, where saturation effects are expected to be amplified due to the nuclear saturation scale Q_sA^2 proportional to A^(1/3).
