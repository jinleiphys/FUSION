# Elliptic flow at SPS and RHIC: from kinetic transport to hydrodynamics

> Anisotropic transverse flow is studied in Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions at SPS and RHIC energies. The centrality and transverse momentum dependence at midrapidity of the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 is calculated in the hydrodynamic and low density limits. Hydrodynamics is found to agree well with the RHIC data for semicentral collisions up to transverse momenta of 1-1.5 GeV/c, but it considerably overestimates the measured elliptic flow at SPS energies. The low density limit LDL is inconsistent with the measured magnitude of v_2 at RHIC energies and with the shape of its p_t-dependence at both RHIC and SPS energies. The success of the hydrodynamic model points to very rapid thermalization in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and provides a serious challenge for kinetic approaches based on classical scattering of on-shell particles.

# Elliptic Flow at SPS and RHIC: From Kinetic Transport to Hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic calculations quantitatively reproduce the centrality and transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow measured at RHIC for semicentral collisions up to transverse momenta of 1-1.5 GeV/c, but considerably overestimate the measured elliptic flow at SPS energies; the low density limit (LDL) is inconsistent with the measured magnitude of v2 at RHIC and with the shape of its pT-dependence at both energies, pointing to very rapid thermalization at RHIC but raising questions about whether classical scattering of on-shell particles can describe the approach to thermalization.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic calculations assuming boost-invariant longitudinal flow, using two equations of state (EOS Q with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at Tc=165 MeV, and EOS H with only hadronic resonances) and varying freeze-out temperatures between 120 and 140 MeV. They also computed elliptic flow in the low density limit (LDL) of kinetic theory using the first-order perturbative expression from Heiselberg and Levy (1999), estimating transport cross sections and resonance decay corrections, and compared both sets of calculations to NA49 data for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=17 A GeV and STAR data for Au+Au at sqrt(s)=130 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density for central collisions at SPS: epsilon0 = 9.0 GeV/fm^3 (EOS Q) or 10.0 GeV/fm^3 (EOS H); at RHIC: epsilon0 = 23.0 GeV/fm^3 (EOS Q) or 22.3 GeV/fm^3 (EOS H)
- Thermalization time tau0 = 0.8 fm/c at SPS and 0.6 fm/c at RHIC
- Initial temperature T0 = 257 MeV (SPS, EOS Q) and 334 MeV (RHIC, EOS Q)
- Charged particle multiplicity density at midrapidity for central collisions: dNch/dy = 390 (SPS, EOS Q) and 670 (RHIC, EOS Q)
- Resonance decays reduce pion elliptic flow by 18-25% at Tf approx 140 MeV and by 8-15% at Tf approx 120 MeV
- Hydrodynamic model reproduces RHIC data up to impact parameters of about 7 fm and transverse momenta up to about 1.5 GeV/c
- LDL gives about 50% more elliptic flow at RHIC than at SPS, reflecting the ratio of charged multiplicity densities (dNch/dy approx 630 at RHIC vs 410 at SPS for central collisions)
- LDL underpredicts pT-averaged v2 at RHIC and fails to reproduce the approximately linear rise of v2 with pT seen in data at both energies

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic calculations of Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999, 2000) and the low density limit approach of Heiselberg and Levy (1999) to compare directly with the first RHIC data from STAR (Ackermann et al., 2000) and SPS data from NA49 (Appelshäuser et al., 1998; Poskanzer et al., 1999). The results challenge existing parton cascade calculations (Zhang, Gyulassy, and Ko, 1999; Molnar, 2000) and hadronic cascade codes such as UrQMD (Bleicher and Stöcker, 2000), which underpredict the RHIC elliptic flow by about a factor of 4-5 when averaged over pT. The agreement with hydrodynamics enables future quantitative characterization of the quark-gluon plasma equation of state and discrimination between equations of state with and without a phase transition, provided that single-particle spectra and pT-dependent v2 at different centralities are measured.
