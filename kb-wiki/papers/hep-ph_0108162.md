# Validity Of The Vafa-Witten Proof On Absence Of Spontaneous Parity Breaking In QCD

> The vacuum energy response of a quantum field theory is studied as a function of complex external fields coupled to the symmetry-breaking observables of the theory. It is found that the energy density is insensitive to spontaneous symmetry breaking effects along a special direction in the complex plane. In particular, in the case of vector-like gauge theories such as QCD, the change in energy generated by coupling to pure-gluonic parity-odd operators is always a growing function of the external fields along the real axis, independent of the mode in which symmetry is realized. In light of this, although there is little doubt that parity is not spontaneously broken in massive QCD, the validity of the Vafa and Witten proof is, however, questionable.

# Validity Of The Vafa-Witten Proof On Absence Of Spontaneous Parity Breaking In QCD

## Key claim

The Vafa-Witten proof that spontaneous parity breaking cannot occur in vector-like gauge theories such as QCD is invalid because the vacuum energy response is insensitive to spontaneous symmetry breaking effects along the real axis in the complex plane of external fields, which is precisely the direction Vafa and Witten used to argue that parity must be conserved. Although there is little doubt that parity is not spontaneously broken in massive QCD, the proof itself is not satisfactory.

## Method

The author analyzes the vacuum energy density as a function of complex external fields coupled to symmetry-breaking observables, distinguishing between "paramagnetic" observables (where the Euclidean action gains a real term) and "diamagnetic" observables (where the Euclidean action gains an imaginary term). For diamagnetic observables such as pure-gluonic parity-odd operators, the author shows that the energy response along the real axis is always a growing quadratic function regardless of whether symmetry is broken or not, because spontaneous symmetry breaking effects only manifest along the imaginary axis. The analysis uses the Lee-Yang theory of phase transitions, where partition function zeros on the imaginary axis coalesce into a logarithmic cut as the four-volume goes to infinity.

## Key numbers

- The second-order derivative of the vacuum energy at lambda=0 for paramagnetic observables is given by d^2 E/d lambda^2 = - integral [D phi] X^2 exp(-S_E) / integral [D phi] exp(-S_E), which is negative definite because X^2 and the integration measure are both positive.
- For diamagnetic observables, the vacuum energy along the real axis takes the form Re E(Re lambda) = E(0) + (1/2) alpha (Re lambda)^2, with the same quadratic growth in both symmetric and symmetry-breaking phases.
- In the symmetry-breaking phase along the imaginary axis, the vacuum energy takes the form Re E(i Im lambda) = E(0) - (1/2) alpha (Im lambda)^2, identical to the symmetric phase result.

## Context

This paper directly challenges the foundational proof by Vafa and Witten (1984) that spontaneous parity breaking cannot occur in vector-like gauge theories. It extends earlier criticisms by Azcoiti and Galante (1999), who argued that the vacuum energy density in the presence of external symmetry-breaking sources does not exist, and by Cohen (2001), who showed the proof does not apply to finite-temperature QCD. The work also relates to Aoki's (1984) finding of a parity-breaking phase in lattice QCD with Wilson fermions, and to Sharpe and Singleton's (1998) argument that quark-field observables have different properties from pure gluonic operators. The analysis enables future investigations of spontaneous parity breaking by examining the vacuum energy response along the imaginary axis of the external field rather than the real axis.
