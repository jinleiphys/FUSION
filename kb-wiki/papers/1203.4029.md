# Revisiting the production of charmonium plus a light meson at PANDA

## Key claim

The form factor (FF) contribution to the $pp\pi$ and $pp\Psi$ interaction vertices cannot be ignored when calculating charmonium-plus-light-meson production cross sections in low-energy $p\bar{p}$ annihilation; with a monopole FF cutoff $\Lambda = 1.9$ GeV, the calculated $p\bar{p}\to \pi^0 J/\psi$ cross section agrees with the E760 data, whereas the calculation without FF overestimates it.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level proton-exchange diagrams (t-channel and u-channel) to compute amplitudes for $p\bar{p}\to m\Psi$ where $m$ is a light meson ($\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\omega$, $\rho$) and $\Psi$ is a charmonium state ($\eta_c$, $J/\psi$, $\psi'$, $\chi_{c0}$, $\chi_{c1}$). They introduce a monopole form factor $\mathcal{F}(q_i^2)=(\Lambda^2-m_i^2)/(\Lambda^2-q_i^2)$ at each interaction vertex to account for the off-shell structure of the exchanged proton, and compare results with and without this FF. For the $pp\omega$ and $pp\rho$ vertices, both Dirac ($g_\omega$, $g_\rho$) and Pauli ($\kappa_\omega$, $\kappa_\rho$) coupling constants are included, and several published sets of these constants are tested.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for $pp\pi$ is taken as $g_{NN\pi}=13.5$.
- The $p\bar{p}\Psi$ coupling constants used are: $g_{p\bar p \eta_c} = (19.0 \pm 3.2)\times 10^{-3}$, $g_{p\bar p J/\psi} = (1.62 \pm 0.03)\times 10^{-3}$, $g_{p\bar p \psi'} = (0.97 \pm 0.04)\times 10^{-3}$, $g_{p\bar p \chi_{c0}} = (5.42 \pm 0.37)\times 10^{-3}$, $g_{p\bar p \chi_{c1}} = (1.03 \pm 0.07)\times 10^{-3}$.
- The monopole FF cutoff that reproduces the E760 data for $p\bar{p}\to \pi^0 J/\psi$ at $E_{cm}=3.5$ GeV is $\Lambda=1.9$ GeV.
- For $p\bar{p}\to \omega\Psi$ with $\Lambda=1.9$ GeV and $(g_\omega, \kappa_\omega)=(12.2, -0.12)$, the total cross section of $p\bar{p}\to \omega\eta_c$ is the largest among all charmonium channels; the $J/\psi$ production cross section is roughly 50 times smaller than that of $\eta_c$, while the $\chi_{c0}$ production cross section is 5 times larger than that of $J/\psi$.
- The coupling constant for $pp\eta$ is taken as $g_{pp\eta}=11.5$; for $pp\rho$, the Bonn full model values $(g_\rho, \kappa_\rho)=(3.249, 6.1)$ are used.
- Hadron masses used: $m_{\pi^0}=135.0$ MeV, $m_{\omega}=782.7$ MeV, $m_{p}=938.3$ MeV, $m_{\eta_c}=2980.3$ MeV, $m_{J/\psi}=3096.9$ MeV, $m_{\chi_{c0}}=3414.8$ MeV, $m_{\chi_{c1}}=3510.7$ MeV, $m_{\psi'}=3686.1$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the initial-state light-meson emission model of Barnes and Li (2006) for $p\bar{p}\to \pi^0\Psi$ by adding monopole form factors at the $ppm$ and $pp\Psi$ vertices, which was listed as a future development in that earlier work. It also extends the approach to vector meson channels ($\omega$, $\rho$) using coupling constants from the Paris, Nijmegen, and Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials, as well as from pion photoproduction (Sato, 1996), nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Mergell, 1995), QCD sum rules (Zhu, 1999), and the $^3P_0$ quark model (Downum, 2006). The results provide predictions for the forthcoming $\overline{\mbox{P}}$ANDA experiment at FAIR, and show that $p\bar{p}\to \omega\Psi$ can serve as an ideal channel to test different theoretical values of the $pp\omega$ Dirac and Pauli coupling constants.
