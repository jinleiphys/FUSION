# Anisotropic Hydrodynamics for Rapidly Expanding Systems

> We exactly solve the relaxation-time approximation Boltzmann equation for a system which is transversely homogeneous and undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. We compare the resulting exact numerical solution with approximate solutions available in the anisotropic hydrodynamics and second order viscous hydrodynamics frameworks. In all cases studied, we find that the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework is a better approximation to the exact solution than traditional viscous hydrodynamical approaches.

## Key claim

The anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework is a better approximation to the exact solution of the relaxation-time approximation Boltzmann equation than traditional second-order viscous hydrodynamical approaches (Israel-Stewart and DNMR) for a transversely homogeneous, boost-invariant system undergoing longitudinal expansion, across all studied values of shear viscosity to entropy ratio and initial momentum-space anisotropy.

## Method

The authors exactly solved the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) Boltzmann equation for a system of massless particles that is transversely homogeneous and undergoing boost-invariant (0+1-dimensional) longitudinal expansion. They obtained an integral equation for the energy density, which was solved numerically via iteration, and compared the resulting exact numerical solutions for the pressure anisotropy and particle production with approximate solutions from the aHydro framework and two second-order viscous hydrodynamics frameworks: the Israel-Stewart (IS) prescription and the full second-order (DNMR) prescription.

## Key numbers

- For an isotropic initial condition with $\xi_0 = 0$ and $T_0 = 600$ MeV at $\tau_0 = 0.25$ fm/c, the aHydro approximation was closer to the exact solution than IS and DNMR for all three values of $4\pi\eta/{\cal S} \in \{1, 3, 10\}$.
- For an anisotropic initial condition with $\xi_0 = 10$ and $T_0 = 600$ MeV at $\tau_0 = 0.25$ fm/c, the aHydro approximation was again closer to the exact solution for all three values of $4\pi\eta/{\cal S} \in \{1, 3, 10\}$.
- For the case $4\pi\eta/{\cal S} = 10$ with isotropic initial conditions, the IS approximation predicted a negative longitudinal pressure for the majority of the time shown.
- The correct relaxation time in the RTA is $\tau_{\rm eq} = 5 \eta/(T {\cal S})$, not $\tau_{\rm eq} = 6 \eta/(T {\cal S})$; the latter value (obtained from the Grad-Israel-Stewart truncation) disagrees with the exact solution at early and late times.
- The particle production measure $\Delta_n = \tau_f/\tau_0 \, n(\tau_f)/n(\tau_0) - 1$ (with freeze-out temperature $T_f = 150$ MeV, starting from $\xi_0 = 0$, $T_0 = 600$ MeV, $\tau_0 = 0.25$ fm/c) was monotonically increasing with $\eta/{\cal S}$ for both IS and DNMR, while the aHydro framework showed the correct qualitative behavior (vanishing in both the ideal and free-streaming limits).

## Context

This work extends the exact RTA Boltzmann solution originally obtained by Baym (1984) to arbitrary initial conditions at $\tau_0 \neq 0$ and time-dependent relaxation times, providing a benchmark for comparing approximate dynamical frameworks. The aHydro framework, developed by Florkowski, Martinez, Ryblewski, and Strickland (2010-2012), is shown to outperform both the Israel-Stewart approximation (Israel and Stewart, 1976, 1979) and the full second-order viscous hydrodynamics treatment (Denicol, 2012) in all cases studied. The exact solutions obtained here can be used to create improved approximation frameworks that account for deviations from the spheroidal form of the distribution function.
