# Scalar correlations in a quark plasma and low mass dilepton production

> We investigate possible consequences of resonant scalar interactions for dilepton production from a quark plasma at the chiral phase transition. It is found that this production mechanism is strongly suppressed compared to the Born process and has no significance for present experiments.

## Key claim

Resonant scalar (sigma) correlations in a quark plasma at the chiral phase transition can enhance the spectral density in the scalar channel by 1–2 orders of magnitude, but the resulting dilepton production rate is still strongly suppressed relative to the perturbative thermal Born process and cannot explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES collaboration.

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature and chemical potential to compute the scalar isoscalar polarization operator and the sigma-to-photon transition integral. They evaluate the ratio of the resonant scalar correlation cross section to the Born cross section for dilepton production from quark-antiquark annihilation, using two representative sets of thermodynamic parameters near and above the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers

* For set (a): T = 170 MeV, μ = 80 MeV, constituent quark mass m = 150 MeV, sigma mass m_σ = 330 MeV; the sigma propagator is strongly peaked near the two-quark threshold.
* For set (b): T = 240 MeV, μ = 110 MeV, constituent quark mass m = 33 MeV, sigma mass m_σ = 340 MeV; chiral symmetry is almost restored.
* The resonant cross section is suppressed by at least two orders of magnitude relative to the Born process for all kinematical conditions studied.
* The NJL parameters used are: scalar coupling constant K = 9.45 GeV^{-2}, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 660 MeV, current quark mass m_0 = 5.35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Weldon (1992) that a scalar resonance in a hot pion gas could decay into lepton pairs, but examines the mechanism in the quark phase rather than the hadron phase. The standard explanation for the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES collaboration involves in-medium modification of the rho resonance (Li, Ko and Brown, 1995; Chanfray, Rapp and Wambach, 1996), but the authors find that scalar correlations from the chiral phase transition do not provide an alternative explanation. The result rules out resonant scalar q-qbar interactions as a significant source for present dilepton experiments, directing future work toward other mechanisms such as vector meson modifications.
