# Gauge- and frame-independent decomposition of nucleon spin

> In a recent paper, we have shown that the way of gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin is not necessarily unique, but there still exists a preferable decomposition from the observational viewpoint. What was not complete in this argument is a fully satisfactory answer to the following questions. Does the proposed gauge-invariant decomposition, especially the decomposition of the gluon total angular momentum into its spin and orbital parts, correspond to observables which can be extracted from high-energy deep-inelastic-scattering measurements ? Is this decomposition not only gauge-invariant but also Lorentz frame-independent, so that it is legitimately thought to reflect an intrinsic property of the nucleon ? We show that we can answer both of these questions affirmatively, by making full use of a gauge-invariant decomposition of covariant angular momentum tensor of QCD in an arbitrary Lorentz frame.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin, including the separation of gluon total angular momentum into spin and orbital parts, can be made both gauge-invariant and Lorentz frame-independent, and that the resulting quark and gluon intrinsic spin contributions correspond to observables extracted from polarized deep-inelastic-scattering measurements.

## Method

The author constructs a gauge-invariant decomposition of the covariant angular-momentum tensor of QCD in an arbitrary Lorentz frame by splitting the gluon field into physical and pure-gauge parts, without fixing gauge explicitly. The forward matrix element of the Pauli-Lubansky vector, expressed in terms of this covariant angular-momentum tensor and the nucleon momentum, is then used to obtain a gauge- and frame-independent decomposition of the nucleon spin. Two distinct decompositions (I and II) are presented, differing only in the orbital angular momentum parts while sharing identical spin contributions for both quarks and gluons.

## Key numbers

* The net longitudinal quark polarization Δq has been fairly precisely determined through high-statistics measurements of the deuteron spin structure function by COMPASS (2005, 2007) and HERMES (2007), giving the portion of nucleon spin from intrinsic quark spin as around 1/3.
* The flavor-singlet axial charge a_q^(0) enters the forward matrix element of the quark spin part as M a_q^(0) ε^(μνλσ) s_σ, where M is the nucleon mass and s_σ is the spin vector.
* In the MS-bar renormalization scheme, a_q^(0) can be identified with Δq; in the Adler-Bardeen scheme, a_q^(0) = Δq - 2 n_f (α_s / 4π) Δg, where n_f is the number of quark flavors and Δg the net gluon polarization.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Wakamatsu, 2010) showing that gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin is not unique, and addresses unresolved questions about observability and frame-independence. It builds on the gauge-invariant decomposition approach of Chen et al. (2008, 2009) and the Ji decomposition (1997), while also relating to the earlier gauge-variant decomposition of Jaffe and Manohar (1990). The paper enables a rigorous connection between theoretical spin decompositions and experimental measurements from polarized deep-inelastic scattering and generalized parton distribution analyses.
