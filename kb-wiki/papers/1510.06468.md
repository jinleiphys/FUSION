# Heavy flavor electron $R_\text{AA}$ and $v_2$ in event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamics

> In this work we investigate how event-by-event hydrodynamics fluctuations affect the nuclear suppression factor and elliptic flow of heavy flavor mesons and non-photonic electrons. We use a 2D+1 Lagrangian ideal hydrodynamic code on an event-by-event basis in order to compute local temperature and flow profiles. Using a strong coupling inspired energy loss parametrization on top of the evolving space-time energy density distributions we are able to propagate the heavy quarks inside the medium until the freeze-out temperature is reached and a Pythia modeling of hadronization takes place. The resulting D$^0$ and heavy-flavor electron yield is compared with recent experimental data for $R_\text{AA}$ and $v_2$ from the STAR and Phenix collaborations. In addition we present preditions for the higher order Fourier harmonic coefficients $v_3(p_T)$ of heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC's $\sqrt{S_\text{NN}} = 200$ GeV collisions.

## Key claim

Event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations, when combined with a strong-coupling-inspired energy loss parametrization, can reproduce the measured nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v_2) of heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC and LHC energies, though the calculated v_2 remains below the experimental data, indicating the energy loss model requires improvement.

## Method

The authors used a 2D+1 Lagrangian ideal hydrodynamic code (Smoothed Particles Hydrodynamics) on an event-by-event basis to compute local temperature and flow profiles from initial conditions generated by a modified PHOBOS Glauber Monte Carlo. Heavy quarks were propagated through the evolving medium using a strong-coupling-inspired energy loss parametrization (dE/dx proportional to v*gamma(v)*T^2) until the freeze-out temperature of 140 MeV, followed by Peterson fragmentation and Pythia 8 modeling of hadronization and decay into electrons.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature set to 140 MeV
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts from tau = 1.0 fm/c
- SPH smoothing parameter h = 0.3 fm
- RHIC collision energy: sqrt(S_NN) = 200 GeV
- LHC collision energy: sqrt(S_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The calculated v_2 for heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC (0-60% centrality) is below experimental data from STAR (2-corr and 4-corr methods)
- The calculated v_3 for heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC (0-60% centrality) is presented as a prediction, with values up to approximately 0.01 at p_T around 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic approach previously developed by Andrade (2013, 2014) to heavy flavor observables, using the energy loss parametrization derived from AdS/CFT correspondence by Gubser (2006). The results are compared with experimental data from STAR (Abelev 2007, Adamczyk) and PHENIX (Adare 2007) collaborations. The modular framework enables future testing of different energy loss models, hydrodynamic codes, or fragmentation functions to improve the description of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
