# Model calculations of doubly closed shell nuclei in CBF theory III. j-j coupling and isospin dependence

> Correlated Basis Function theory and Fermi Hypernetted Chain technique are extended to study medium-heavy, doubly closed shell nuclei in j-j coupling scheme, with different single particle wave functions for protons and neutrons and isospin dependent two-body correlations. Central semirealistic interactions are used. Ground state energies, one-body densities, distribution functions and momentum distributions are calculated for 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb nuclei. The values of the ground state energies provided by isospin dependent correlations are lower than those obtained with isospin independent correlations. In finite nuclear systems, the two--body Euler equations provide correlation functions variationally more effective than those obtained with the same technique in infinite nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper extends Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory and Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) techniques to medium-heavy doubly closed shell nuclei using a j-j coupling scheme with separate proton and neutron single-particle wave functions and isospin-dependent two-body correlations, but the results are obtained with central semirealistic interactions (v4 potentials without tensor components) and therefore cannot be directly compared with experimental data.

## Method

The authors generalized the FHNC formalism to treat nuclei as mixtures of protons and neutrons with different populations, introducing a new type of statistical link (ρ₀,A) that appears in j-j coupling due to spin-orbit splitting, which connects nucleons with antiparallel third spin components. Ground-state wave functions were constructed as correlated wave functions using a Slater determinant of single-particle wave functions from a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential with spin-orbit term, multiplied by an isospin-dependent correlation operator. The expectation values of energy, one-body densities, distribution functions, and momentum distributions were computed using FHNC/0 approximation (neglecting elementary diagrams), with both Gaussian variational correlations and Euler-Lagrange correlations obtained by minimizing the energy at second order of the cluster expansion.

## Context

This work extends previous CBF-FHNC calculations by Co' et al. (1992, 1994) that treated N=Z nuclei in l-s coupling without Coulomb interaction, now incorporating j-j coupling necessary for heavier nuclei like ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb where neutron number exceeds proton number. The calculations use the S3 interaction of Afnan and Tang (1968) supplemented with repulsive terms as discussed by Guardiola (1981), and single-particle wave functions from Woods-Saxon potentials with parameters from Ring and Schuck (1978). This represents a step toward fully microscopic ab initio calculations of heavy nuclei starting from realistic nuclear Hamiltonians, enabling future work with realistic interactions including tensor components.
