# Unified description of Bjorken and Landau 1+1 hydrodynamics

> We propose a generalization of the Bjorken in-out Ansatz for fluid trajectories which, when applied to the (1+1) hydrodynamic equations, generates a one-parameter family of analytic solutions interpolating between the boost-invariant Bjorken picture and the non boost-invariant one by Landau. This parameter characterises the proper-time scale when the fluid velocities approach the in-out Ansatz. We discuss the resulting rapidity distribution of entropy for various freeze-out conditions and compare it with the original Bjorken and Landau results.

# Unified description of Bjorken and Landau 1+1 hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The paper proposes a one-parameter family of analytic solutions to (1+1)-dimensional relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics that interpolates between the boost-invariant Bjorken picture and the non-boost-invariant Landau picture, where the single parameter $h$ sets the proper-time scale at which fluid velocities approach the Bjorken in-out Ansatz; the interpolation is exact only in the limits $h \to 0$ (Bjorken) and $h$ fixed with $z_\pm \to \infty$ (Landau asymptotic), not for all intermediate values.

## Method

The authors introduce a generalized in-out Ansatz for fluid trajectories, $2y = \log f_+(z_+) - \log f_-(z_-)$, where $y$ is rapidity and $z_\pm = t \pm z$ are light-cone coordinates, and impose the perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations with an equation of state $\epsilon = g p$ (sound velocity $1/\sqrt{g}$). Solving the resulting differential equation $f f'' = A^2/2$ yields analytic expressions for pressure, entropy density, and rapidity as functions of $z_+$ and $z_-$ in terms of a single scale parameter $h = H/A$. The entropy distribution $dS/dy$ is then evaluated for three different freeze-out conditions: fixed time $t$, fixed proper time $\tau$, and fixed temperature $T$.

## Key numbers

- The parameter $h$ (with dimension of length) characterizes the proper-time scale at which the fluid velocity approaches the Bjorken in-out Ansatz; the ratio $y/\eta$ is plotted versus $\tau/h$ for several values of $\eta$.
- For small proper times, $y \approx (\tau/2h)^2 \sinh 2\eta$, showing the fluid starts at rest (Landau "full stopping" limit) at $\tau/h \to 0$.
- For freeze-out at fixed proper time $\tau$, the entropy distribution $dS/dy$ is compared with the Landau approximate formula $dS/dy = S_0 \, e^{\sqrt{L^2 - y^2}}$; curves are shown for various values of $\tau/h$.
- For freeze-out at fixed temperature, the distribution $dS/dy$ is plotted for $\theta \geq 2$, where $\theta = \log(T_0/T_F)$; the distribution is close to Gaussian but with a slope smaller than required by data unless $g$ is larger (smaller sound speed).
- The freeze-out surface at fixed temperature becomes time-like at rapidity $y_m = 2g\theta/(g+1)$.

## Context

This work extends the classic Bjorken (1970) boost-invariant hydrodynamic picture and the Landau (1953) "full-stopping" hydrodynamic model by providing a one-parameter analytic family that smoothly connects both limits. It contrasts with other recent (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic solutions by Csorgo and by Pratt, which do not share the same smooth interpolation property. The results enable systematic comparison of freeze-out prescriptions (fixed time, fixed proper time, fixed temperature) and their effect on the observable rapidity distribution of entropy, and suggest future extensions to more flexible initial conditions (relaxing the point-like initial fluid) and to gauge/gravity correspondence applications.
