# Measuring Quark-Gluon-Plasma Thermalization Time with Dileptons

> We calculate the medium dilepton yield from a quark-gluon plasma which has a time-dependent local momentum-space anisotropy. A phenomenological model for the hard momentum scale, p_hard(tau), and plasma anisotropy parameter, xi(tau), is constructed which interpolates between free streaming behavior at early times (tau << tau_iso) and ideal hydrodynamic behavior at late times (tau >> tau_iso). We show that high-energy dilepton production is sensitive to the assumed plasma isotropization time, tau_iso, and can therefore be used to experimentally determine the time of onset for hydrodynamic expansion of a quark-gluon plasma and the magnitude of expected early-time momentum-space anisotropies.

## Key claim

High-energy dilepton yields from a quark-gluon plasma are sensitive to the assumed plasma isotropization time, tau_iso, and can therefore be used to experimentally determine the time of onset for hydrodynamic expansion, but only at LHC energies where the signal-to-background ratio is favorable; at RHIC energies the sensitivity is reduced due to poor signal-to-background.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological model for the time-dependent hard momentum scale p_hard(tau) and momentum-space anisotropy parameter xi(tau) that interpolates between longitudinal free streaming at early times (tau << tau_iso) and ideal hydrodynamic expansion at late times (tau >> tau_iso). They then computed the leading-order medium dilepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation, folding it with anisotropic phase-space distribution functions parameterized by p_hard(tau) and xi(tau), and integrated over the space-time evolution of a Pb-Pb collision at LHC energy.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC energies, the nuclear saturation scale Q_s is about 1.5 GeV, implying a formation time tau_0 of about 0.2 fm/c; for LHC energies, Q_s is about 2 GeV, implying tau_0 of about 0.1 fm/c.
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV, the calculation used tau_0 = 0.088 fm/c, T_0 = 845 MeV, T_c = 160 MeV, and R_T = 7.1 fm.
- At p_T = 5 GeV, the expected medium dilepton yield varies by nearly an order of magnitude depending on the assumed plasma isotropization time.
- At 1 fm/c, approximately 94% of all p_T = 4 GeV dileptons and 98% of p_T = 5 GeV dileptons have already been produced.
- The measurable effect on dilepton production is predicted to occur between 3 GeV and 8 GeV in invariant mass and transverse momentum at LHC energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Teaney 2000, Huovinen 2001, Hirano 2002) that assumed ideal hydrodynamic behavior starting at very early times (tau less than about 1 fm/c), and addresses the open question of when the quark-gluon plasma actually becomes isotropic enough for hydrodynamics to apply. The authors propose dilepton production as an independent experimental probe of the isotropization time, complementing existing constraints from elliptic flow and viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Romatschke 2007, Song 2007). The results enable future experimental determination of QGP initial conditions at LHC and can be extended to study finite chemical potentials, collisional broadening, and finite viscosity effects.
