# Centrality dependence of multiplicities in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> We compute the centrality dependence of multiplicities of particles produced in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at various energies and atomic numbers. The computation is carried out in perturbative QCD with saturated densities of produced gluons and by including effects of nuclear geometry. Numbers are given for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicities in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions can be computed from perturbative QCD with a local saturation criterion, yielding predictions that are essentially constant in the number of participants for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, provided the model is restricted to impact parameters where the saturation scale remains above 0.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors extend the pQCD + saturation model from central (b=0) to non-central collisions by generalizing the saturation condition to a local one: at each transverse position s in the nuclear overlap region, the density of produced partons equals p0^2/pi, defining a local saturation momentum psat(b,s). The initial multiplicity at fixed impact parameter b is then obtained by integrating psat^2(b,s)/pi over the overlap area, including only regions where psat >= 0.5 GeV. The impact parameter is converted to the experimentally accessible number of participants Npart using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution and the inelastic pp cross section (35-42 mb depending on energy).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale psat ~ 1 GeV at RHIC and ~ 2 GeV at LHC for central collisions
- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV, the initial proper time is tau ~ 1/psat ~ 0.2 fm
- The contribution to the multiplicity integral from psat between 0.5 and 0.7 GeV is only about a 4% increase in N_AA
- The model is applied up to b=1.8 R_A, corresponding to Npart ~ 30, and b=1.6 R_A corresponds to Npart ~ 50
- The relation between initial transverse energy and multiplicity is E_T(b)/N(b) ~ 1.35 * <psat(b)>
- The conversion from initial gluons to final charged particles uses N_ch ~ (2/3) N_f and dN/deta ~ 0.9 dN/dy, with final-to-initial ratio N_f/N_AA ~ 3.6/4

## Context

This work extends the pQCD + saturation model of Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, and Tuominen (2000) from central to non-central collisions, enabling predictions in a three-parameter space of atomic number A, center-of-mass energy sqrt(s), and impact parameter b (or number of participants). The centrality dependence of multiplicity was proposed by Wang and Gyulassy (2000) as a way to distinguish between models, noting that the ratio N_ch/N_part grows with decreasing b in their estimate while it decreases in the HIJING Monte Carlo model. The results agree with first PHOBOS data at sqrt(s)=56 and 130 GeV for central collisions, and the constant behavior of N_ch per participant pair as a function of N_part is a prediction that can be tested against future data and other models such as classical field computations by Kovchegov and collaborators.
