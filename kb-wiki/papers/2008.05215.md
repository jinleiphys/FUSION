# Parity violation in proton-deuteron scattering

> The effects of parity violation in the interaction of relativistic polarized protons and deuterons are discussed. Within Glauber's approach, estimates are obtained for P-odd asymmetries in the total and elastic scattering cross sections, in the deuteron dissociation cross section, and in the inelastic cross section with meson production in a final state. It is shown that, from the point of view of the magnitude of the P-odd effects, the interaction of polarized deuterons with unpolarized protons has an advantage over the interaction of polarized protons with unpolarized deuterons. A significant P-odd asymmetry was found in the dissociation channel of the polarized deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that parity-violating (P-odd) asymmetries in proton-deuteron scattering at NICA collider energies are larger for the interaction of polarized deuterons with unpolarized protons than for polarized protons with unpolarized deuterons, with the largest asymmetry found in the deuteron dissociation channel; however, the authors note that the accuracy of calculating the radiative corrections to the weak Hamiltonian is not high, so the numerical results are qualitative rather than precise predictions.

## Method

The authors apply Glauber's multiple-scattering approach to compute P-odd corrections to the total, elastic, quasi-elastic (deuteron dissociation), and inelastic cross sections for proton-deuteron scattering at high energies. They parameterize the strong interaction amplitudes using standard eikonal forms with s-channel helicity conservation and incorporate weak interaction amplitudes from their prior work on nucleon-nucleon scattering (Milstein, Nikolaev, Salnikov 2020), which include radiative corrections due to strong charge-exchange interactions. The deuteron is treated as a loosely bound system using a square-well wave function for the form factor, and the Franco-Glauber technique is employed to handle quasi-elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- Total strong cross section for pd scattering: σ_s,tot^pd = 96 mb, with Glauber screening correction Δσ_G = 4 mb
- Elastic strong cross section for pd scattering: σ_s,el^pd = 20 mb
- Quasi-elastic (dissociation) strong cross section for pd scattering: σ_s,qel^pd = 22.4 mb
- For polarized deuteron (λ_d=1) on unpolarized proton: total P-odd asymmetry A_tot^pd = 2×10^{-8}, elastic asymmetry A_el^pd = 3.5×10^{-8}, quasi-elastic asymmetry A_qel^pd = 6×10^{-8}
- For polarized proton (λ_p=1) on unpolarized deuteron: total P-odd asymmetry A_tot^pd = -0.9×10^{-8}, elastic asymmetry A_el^pd = -3×10^{-8}, quasi-elastic asymmetry A_qel^pd = -10^{-8}
- Weak interaction parameters for nucleon-nucleon scattering: c_pp = 5 nb, c_pn = -7.8 nb, Λ = 1 GeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV
- Strong interaction parameters: ε = -0.5, σ_s,tot = 50 mb, B = 9 GeV^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of P-odd asymmetries in nucleon-nucleon scattering (Milstein, Nikolaev, Salnikov 2020) to the proton-deuteron system, motivated by planned polarization experiments at the NICA collider (Kekelidze 2016, Savin 2014). The results contradict the expectation that inelastic channels would dominate, instead showing that elastic and quasi-elastic scattering yield larger asymmetries, similar to the enhancement found in elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering. The findings enable experimental planning for parity violation searches at NICA, particularly using polarized deuterons which avoid the spin resonance problems that affect polarized protons in the NICA energy range, and suggest that detection of recoil protons from a jet hydrogen target (Nikitin) can separate elastic and quasi-elastic events.
