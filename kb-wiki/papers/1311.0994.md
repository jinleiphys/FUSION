# P wave bottomonium spectral functions in the QGP from lattice NRQCD

> We present an overview of bottomonium spectral functions in the quark-gluon plasma, obtained by the FASTSUM collaboration, using lattice QCD simulations with two light quark flavours on anisotropic lattices. The bottom quark is treated nonrelativistically. While we find that the S wave ground states survive up to the highest temperature we consider, we have strong indications that P wave states melt immediately above Tc.

## Key claim
The paper claims that P wave bottomonium states (χb, hb) melt immediately above the deconfinement temperature Tc, while S wave ground states (Υ, ηb) survive up to at least 2.09 Tc; however, this conclusion is drawn from lattice ensembles at a single lattice spacing and requires verification on finer spatial lattices.

## Method
The FASTSUM collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations with two light quark flavours (Nf=2) on highly anisotropic lattices (spatial-to-temporal lattice spacing ratio ξ=6), using a fixed spatial lattice spacing as≈0.162 fm and temporal extents Nτ from 80 down to 16 to achieve temperatures from 0.42 Tc to 2.09 Tc. The bottom quark was treated nonrelativistically using O(v4) NRQCD, and spectral functions were reconstructed from Euclidean correlators via the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), with correlator relative errors below 4×10−4 in S wave channels.

## Key numbers
- Lattice anisotropy: ξ = as/aτ = 6, with aτ−1 = 7.35(3) GeV
- Spatial lattice spacing: as ≈ 0.162 fm
- Temperature range: T = 90 MeV (0.42 Tc) to 458 MeV (2.09 Tc)
- Light quark pion-to-rho mass ratio: mπ/mρ ≈ 0.54
- In S wave channels, temperature effects on correlators are at most a few percent; in P wave channels, effects reach up to 25 percent
- S wave ground state (Υ) survives up to at least T = 2.09 Tc (458 MeV)
- P wave ground state (χb1) shows no plateau in effective masses above T = 1.05 Tc (230 MeV), interpreted as immediate melting
- The Υ ground state peak position corresponds to ω = 8.57 GeV in the spectral function plots (after additive shift)

## Context
This work extends the FASTSUM collaboration's previous studies of bottomonium spectral functions in the QGP (Aarts 2010, Aarts 2011, Aarts 2012) by comparing S wave and P wave channels systematically. The results confirm the expected melting pattern from effective field theory computations (Brambilla 2010) and are consistent with LHC observations of excited state suppression (Chatrchyan 2011). The findings enable future studies on finer spatial lattices and with Nf=2+1 dynamical quark flavours to verify robustness, and may inform the use of bottomonium as a thermometer for the QGP created at RHIC and the LHC.
