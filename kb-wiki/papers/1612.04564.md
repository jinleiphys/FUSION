# Behavior of universal critical parameters in the QCD phase diagram

> We determine the dependence of important parameters for critical fluctuations on temperature and baryon chemical potential in the QCD phase diagram. The analysis is based on an identification of the fluctuations of the order parameter obtained from the Ising model equation of state and the Ginzburg-Landau effective potential approach. The impact of the mapping from Ising model variables to QCD thermodynamics is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper determines the dependence of universal critical parameters (the dimensionless cubic and quartic interaction strengths \(\tilde{\lambda}_3\) and \(\tilde{\lambda}_4\)) on temperature and baryon chemical potential in the QCD phase diagram, but the mapping from Ising model variables to QCD thermodynamics is strongly model-dependent and not universal.

## Method

The authors identify fluctuations of the order parameter obtained from the three-dimensional Ising model equation of state (using the linear parametric representation of Schofield (1969)) with those from a Ginzburg-Landau effective potential approach. This identification yields parametric expressions for the correlation length \(\xi\) and the dimensionless interaction strengths \(\tilde{\lambda}_3\) and \(\tilde{\lambda}_4\) in terms of the auxiliary spin model variables \(R\) and \(\theta\). A linear mapping from the Ising variables \(r\) and \(h\) to QCD variables (baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) and temperature \(T\)) is then employed, with the \(r\)-axis oriented tangentially to the first-order transition line at the critical point and the \(h\)-axis parallel to the \(T\)-axis.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponents used are \(\beta = 1/3\) and \(\delta = 5\), consistent with the three-dimensional Ising universality class.
- The parameter \(\tilde{\lambda}_3\) is an odd function of \(h\); at \(h=0\) for \(r<0\), it approaches \(\tilde{\lambda}_3 \to \pm 7\,C/6^{1/4}\) as \(h\to 0^{\pm}\), where \(C = (T^2 H_0 / M_0^5)^{1/4}\).
- The parameter difference \(2\tilde{\lambda}_3^2 - \tilde{\lambda}_4\) is positive for all \(r<0\) with maximum value \(128\,C^2/27\) at \(h=0\); for \(r>0\) it becomes negative in an interval \(-h_0 \leq h \leq h_0\) with minimum value \(-4\,C^2/9\) at \(h=0\).
- The correlation length \(\xi\) diverges as \(R \to 0\) (the critical point), scaling as \(\xi = \sqrt{M_0/H_0} \, R^{-2/3} (3+2\theta^2)^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nonaka and Asakawa (2004) and Bluhm et al. (2006) for constructing an equation of state with a critical point for QCD matter, by explicitly computing the behavior of the cubic and quartic coupling parameters in the scaling region. The results provide input for dynamical studies of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, similar to the work of Nahrgang et al. (2011) and Herold et al. (2016), and are relevant for interpreting data from the RHIC beam energy scan and the NA61 experiment at CERN-SPS.
