# New Insights into Supradense Matter from Dissecting Scaled Stellar Structure Equations

> The strong-field gravity in General Relativity (GR) realized in neutron stars (NSs) renders the Equation of State (EOS) $P(\varepsilon)$ of supradense neutron star (NS) matter to be essentially nonlinear and refines the upper bound for $ϕ\equiv P/\varepsilon$ to be much smaller than the Special Relativity (SR) requirement with linear EOSs, where $P$ and $\varepsilon$ are respectively the pressure and energy density of the system considered. Specifically, a tight bound $ϕ\lesssim0.374$ is obtained by anatomizing perturbatively the intrinsic structures of the scaled Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff (TOV) equations without using any input nuclear EOS. New insights gained from this novel analysis provide EOS-model independent constraints on properties (e.g., density profiles of the sound speed squared $s^2=\d P/\d\varepsilon$ and trace anomaly $Δ=1/3-ϕ$) of cold supradense matter in NS cores. Using the gravity-matter duality in theories describing NSs, we investigate the impact of gravity on supradense matter EOS in NSs. In particular, we show that the NS mass $M_{\rm{NS}}$, radius $R$ and its compactness $ξ\equiv M_{\rm{NS}}/R$ scale with certain combinations of its central pressure and energy density (encapsulating its central EOS). Thus, observational data on these properties of NSs can straightforwardly constrain NS central EOSs without relying on any specific nuclear EOS-model.

## Key claim

By perturbatively analyzing the scaled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations without using any input nuclear equation of state (EOS), the authors derive an upper bound on the ratio \(\phi \equiv P/\varepsilon\) of pressure to energy density for supradense matter in neutron star (NS) cores that is much stricter than the Special Relativity (SR) bound of \(\phi \leq 1\); the bound is \(\phi \lesssim 0.374\), applicable specifically near the centers of stable NSs and especially at the maximum-mass (TOV) configuration.

## Method

The authors rewrite the TOV equations in dimensionless form using the central energy density \(\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}\) to define mass and length scales, then perform a perturbative expansion of the scaled pressure \(\widehat{P}\), energy density \(\widehat{\varepsilon}\), and mass \(\widehat{M}\) in even powers of the scaled radial coordinate \(\widehat{r}\) near the NS center. By imposing the causality condition \(s_{\rm{c}}^2 \leq 1\) on the central speed of sound squared derived from the TOV configuration condition \(\mathrm{d}M_{\rm{NS}}/\mathrm{d}\varepsilon_{\rm{c}} = 0\), they obtain an upper bound on the central ratio \(\x \equiv P_{\rm{c}}/\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}\). They generalize this bound to finite radii and to stable NSs away from the TOV configuration by analyzing the radial behavior of \(\phi = \widehat{P}/\widehat{\varepsilon}\).

## Key numbers

*   The upper bound for the central ratio \(\x \equiv P_{\rm{c}}/\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}\) at the TOV configuration is \(\x \lesssim 0.374\).
*   The scaling relation for the maximum NS radius is \(R_{\rm{max}}/\mathrm{km} \approx 1050_{-30}^{+30} \times (\nu_{\rm{c}} / \mathrm{fm}^{3/2} / \mathrm{MeV}^{1/2}) + 0.64_{-0.25}^{+0.25}\), where \(\nu_{\rm{c}} \equiv \varepsilon_{\rm{c}}^{-1/2} [\x/(1+3\x^2+4\x)]^{1/2}\).
*   The scaling relation for the maximum NS mass is \(M_{\rm{NS}}^{\rm{max}}/M_{\odot} \approx 1730_{-30}^{+30} \times (\Gamma_{\rm{c}} / \mathrm{fm}^{3/2} / \mathrm{MeV}^{1/2}) - 0.106_{-0.035}^{+0.035}\), where \(\Gamma_{\rm{c}} \equiv \varepsilon_{\rm{c}}^{-1/2} [\x/(1+3\x^2+4\x)]^{3/2}\).
*   The Pearson correlation coefficients for the radius and mass scaling relations are 0.958 and 0.986, respectively, based on 104 EOS samples.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to constrain the EOS of supradense matter in NS cores, which has been a major science driver in nuclear physics and multimessenger astronomy since the discovery of GW170817 (Abbott 2017) and NICER mass-radius measurements for pulsars like PSR J0740+6620 (Fonseca 2021, Riley 2021). The derived bound \(\phi \lesssim 0.374\) is an EOS-model-independent result from GR structure equations, contrasting with earlier model-dependent estimates such as the minimum-period EOS of Koranda (1997) which gave \(\x \approx 0.55\). The scaling relations for NS mass and radius in terms of central EOS parameters enable direct constraints from observational data without relying on specific nuclear EOS models, and the bound on \(\phi\) implies a lower bound on the trace anomaly \(\Delta = 1/3 - \phi\) of approximately \(-0.04\) in NS cores.
