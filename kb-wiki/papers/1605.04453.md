# Heavy Quark Entropy shift: From the Hadron Resonance Gas to Power Corrections

> A heavy quark placed in the medium modifies its specific heat. Using a renormalization group argument we show a low energy theorem in terms of the defect in the trace of the energy-momentum tensor which allows the unambiguous determination of the corresponding entropy shift after imposing the third principle of thermodynamics for degenerate states. We show how recent lattice QCD data can be understood in the confined phase in terms of a single-heavy hadronic spectrum and above the phase transition through power corrections which are analyzed by means of a dimension 2 gluon condensate of the dimensionally reduced theory.

## Key claim

The entropy shift of a heavy quark in the medium can be unambiguously determined from lattice QCD data using a renormalization group argument and the third principle of thermodynamics, provided one accounts for missing singly heavy hadronic states in the confined phase and power corrections from a dimension-2 gluon condensate above the phase transition.

## Method

The authors derived a low-energy theorem relating the entropy shift to the defect in the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, then integrated it with boundary conditions set by the third principle of thermodynamics for degenerate states. They compared the resulting entropy shift to recent TUM lattice data (Bazavov 2016) across a wide temperature range, modeling the confined phase with a hadron resonance gas including singly heavy mesons and baryons from the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Capstick, and modeling the high-temperature phase with power corrections from a dimension-2 gluon condensate of the dimensionally reduced theory.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: \(T_c = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from the TUM lattice calculation (Bazavov 2016)
- Temperature range of TUM lattice data: \(125\ \mathrm{MeV} \le T \le 6000\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Fit of the dimension-2 condensate for \(T_c \le T \le 5000\ \mathrm{MeV}\): \(\langle A^2 \rangle^{\mathrm{NP}} / (2 T_c^2) = 3.40(2)\), with \(\Delta S_{\mathrm{pert}}(T) = 0.195(3)\)
- Fit quality: \(\chi^2 = 108\) for \(N_{\mathrm{dat}} = 92\), giving \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.2\)
- Low-temperature boundary condition: \(\Delta S_Q(0) = \log(2 N_f)\) with \(N_f = 2\) light degenerate flavors
- High-temperature boundary condition: \(\Delta S_Q(\infty) = \log(N_c)\)
- Hagedorn-Polyakov temperature for \(b\)-quarks: \(T_H = 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of studies (Megias 2005, 2007, 2009) that identified inverse power corrections in temperature dominating above the phase transition, explained by dimension-2 condensates of the dimensionally reduced theory. It also builds on their hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop in the confined phase (Megias 2012, 2013) and the recent TUM lattice calculation with physical quark masses in 2+1 flavors (Bazavov 2016), which found that perturbative QCD to NNLO (Berwein 2015) might only set in at 5800 MeV. The analysis enables a deeper understanding of power corrections above the phase transition and suggests missing singly heavy hadronic states in the confined phase.
