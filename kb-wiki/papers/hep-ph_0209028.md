# Hadron correlators, spectral functions and thermal dilepton rates from lattice QCD

> We discuss information on thermal modifications of hadron properties which can be extracted from the structure of Euclidean correlation functions of hadronic currents as well as more direct information obtained through the reconstruction of the spectral functions based on the Maximum Entropy Method.

# Hadron correlators, spectral functions and thermal dilepton rates from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The Euclidean correlation functions and MEM-reconstructed spectral functions for light mesons show that a strong low-energy correlation persists above the deconfinement temperature, with the pseudo-scalar channel exhibiting a broad "resonance" rather than a delta-function peak, while heavy quark bound states show channel-dependent survival: pseudo-scalar and vector states appear bound at 1.5 T_c, but scalar and axial-vector states dissolve at T_c.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations on 64³×16 lattices using non-perturbatively improved Wilson fermions, computing Euclidean time correlation functions for pseudo-scalar and vector meson channels at various temperatures. Spectral functions were reconstructed from these correlators using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), and the vector spectral function was used to compute the thermal dilepton production rate at vanishing momentum.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass below T_c: approximately 500 MeV (corresponding to the non-zero quark mass used in calculations below T_c)
- Lattice size: 64³×16 (spatial × temporal lattice points)
- G_V(1/2T, T)/T³ = 2.23 ± 0.05 at T/T_c = 1.5
- G_V(1/2T, T)/T³ = 2.21 ± 0.05 at T/T_c = 3.0
- Heavy quark pseudo-scalar mass at zero temperature: close to 2.5 GeV (chosen for the heavy quark study)
- Heavy quark vector (J/ψ) and pseudo-scalar (η_c) states appear bound at 1.5 T_c

## Context
This work extends the application of MEM to finite-temperature lattice QCD, building on prior zero-temperature MEM analyses by Asakawa (2000) and Nakahara (1999) and earlier finite-temperature work by Karsch (2001). The thermal dilepton rate results are compared to leading-order perturbative (Born) and HTL-resummed perturbative calculations by Braaten (1990s), showing discrepancies in the infrared regime. The heavy quark bound state analysis addresses potential model predictions by Satz (2000s) regarding J/ψ and χ_c survival across the QCD phase transition. The results enable further lattice studies of transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma and refined calculations of dilepton production rates.
