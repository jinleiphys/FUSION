# Partial dynamical symmetry in quantum Hamiltonians with higher-order terms

> A generic procedure is proposed to construct many-body quantum Hamiltonians with partial dynamical symmetry. It is based on a tensor decomposition of the Hamiltonian and allows the construction of a hierarchy of interactions that have selected classes of solvable states. The method is illustrated in the SO(6) limit of the interacting boson model of atomic nuclei and applied to the nucleus $^{196}$Pt.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generic method to construct many-body quantum Hamiltonians that have a partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) of type (a), where part of the eigenspectrum retains all the dynamical symmetry quantum numbers, and shows that the existence of such PDS is closely related to the order of the interaction among the particles. The method is illustrated in the SO(6) limit of the interacting boson model (IBM) and applied to the nucleus \(^{196}\)Pt, where the PDS Hamiltonian preserves solvability of the ground band states while breaking the symmetry in excited bands.

## Method

The authors construct PDS Hamiltonians by identifying \(n\)-particle annihilation operators with definite tensor character under the algebra chain that annihilate a specific subset of states (those with a given SO(6) irrep \(\langle\Sigma_0\rangle\)). Any number-conserving normal-ordered interaction built from such annihilation operators and their Hermitian conjugates preserves the solvability of those selected states. The method is applied to the SO(6) limit of the IBM, where tensor decompositions of two-body, three-body, and four-body operators are carried out using SO(6) coupling, leading to a hierarchy of interactions \(\hat{P}_+^k \hat{n}_s \hat{P}_-^k\) and \(\hat{P}_+^k \hat{V}_p \hat{P}_-^k\) that retain SO(6) dynamical symmetry in states with \(\Sigma=N\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{196}\)Pt with boson number \(N=6\), the SO(6)-DS anharmonicity of bandhead states is \(R=-0.29\), while the empirical value is \(R=-0.70\).
- The PDS Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}_{\rm PDS}=\hat{H}_{\rm DS}+\eta\hat{P}_{+}\hat{n}_s\hat{P}_{-}\) with parameters \(A=122.9\) keV, \(B=44.0\) keV, \(C=17.9\) keV, and \(\eta=34.9\) keV yields a calculated anharmonicity \(R=-0.63\), much closer to the empirical value.
- The \(0^+_1\), \(0^+_3\), and \(0^+_4\) levels of \(^{196}\)Pt are at excitation energies 0, 1403, and 1823 keV, respectively.
- The ground band states (\(\Sigma=N=6\)) remain pure in the PDS calculation; the first-excited band head has SO(6) decomposition \(\Sigma=4\): 76.5%, \(\Sigma=2\): 16.1%, \(\Sigma=0\): 7.4%.
- The E2 operator parameters are \(e_{\rm b}=0.151\) \(e\)b and \(\chi=0.29\); the \(2^+_1\rightarrow0^+_1\) transition has \(B(E2)=0.274\) \(e^2\)b\(^2\) (experiment) and 0.274 \(e^2\)b\(^2\) (both DS and PDS).

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), previously introduced for two-body interactions in the SU(3) limit of the IBM by Alhassid and Leviatan (1992) and Leviatan (1996), to higher-order interactions in the SO(6) limit. The method addresses the failure of the exact SO(6) dynamical symmetry to reproduce the large anharmonicity of excited bands in \(^{196}\)Pt, as noted by Cizewski (1978) and Ramos (2000), who concluded that cubic terms are needed. The PDS construction enables the systematic inclusion of higher-order terms that break the symmetry in excited bands while preserving the successful description of the ground band, and it can be applied to any quantal system of interacting bosons or fermions.
