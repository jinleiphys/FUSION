# Finite lifetime effects on the photon production from a quark-gluon plasma

> We use the real-time Keldysh formalism to investigate finite lifetime effects on the photon emission from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We provide an ansatz which eliminates the divergent contribution from the vacuum polarization and renders the photon spectrum UV-finite if the time evolution of the QGP is described in a suitable manner.

## Key claim

Finite lifetime effects can make first-order photon emission processes (kinematically forbidden in equilibrium) dominate over higher-order equilibrium contributions at high momentum, but the resulting spectrum is only rendered UV-finite if the quark-gluon plasma is both switched on and switched off in a sufficiently smooth manner; with an instantaneous switch-off the spectrum remains non-integrable.

## Method

The authors used the real-time Keldysh formalism to compute the photon production rate from a homogeneous but non-stationary quark-gluon plasma, modeling the finite lifetime via time-dependent occupation numbers of the quarks. They decomposed the one-loop photon self-energy into vacuum polarization and medium contributions, showing that the vacuum polarization is evaluated on-shell and thus does not contribute to the photon yield. They then numerically evaluated the photon spectrum for a QGP at fixed temperature, comparing different switching functions for the turn-on and turn-off of the plasma, including instantaneous, exponential, and smoother profiles.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the QGP: \(T = 0.3\) GeV.
- Turn-on timescale: \(\tau_F = 1\) fm/c; turn-off (hadronization) timescale: \(\tau_H = 1\) fm/c; plasma lifetime: \(\tau_L = 4\) fm/c.
- For a QGP that is switched on but not switched off, the photon spectrum decays as \(\sim 1/k^3\) for all switching functions, making it non-integrable.
- For a QGP that is also switched off, an instantaneous switch-off still yields a \(\sim 1/k^3\) decay, while an exponential switch-off (\(g_2\)) yields \(\sim 1/k^5\) and a smoother switch-off (\(g_3\)) yields \(\sim 1/k^7\), rendering the spectrum UV-finite.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Boyanovsky et al. (2000, 2001) who first identified first-order photon processes from a finite-lifetime QGP but encountered divergent vacuum polarization and UV non-integrability. It also addresses criticisms by Fraga et al. (2003) that the earlier ansatz was inadequate. The present paper provides a renormalization-free ansatz that eliminates the vacuum polarization divergence and shows that UV-finite spectra are achievable for realistic (smooth) switching scenarios, enabling future work on infrared singularities and a general proof of UV-finiteness.
