# Study of identified particle production as a function of transverse event activity classifier, $S_{T}$ in p$-$p collisions

> A new observable, $S_{T}$, is introduced in terms of the sum of the transverse momentum of charged particles ($\sum_{i} p_{T_{i}}$ ) produced in proton proton (p$-$p) collisions at LHC energies to probe the underlying events (UE). The UE are defined as those aspects of proton-proton collisions that are not attributed to the primary hard scattering process, but rather to the accompanying interactions of the rest of the proton. The conventional approach of studying underlying events is usually carried out by defining topological regions with respect to the leading particle in an event. The transverse region is generally sensitive to UE and various classifiers have been used to discriminate the extent of UE activity regions. The production of identified particles like $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, p , $K_{S}^{0}$, and $Λ^{0}$ are studied in different ranges of transverse activity classifier in p$-$p collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13 $ TeV using pQCD inspired PYTHIA 8 event generator. A comparative analysis of the identified particle spectra, mean multiplicity and mean transverse momentum has been carried out with respect to $S_{T}$ and the performance of this new observable is gauged by comparing the results with previously defined $R_{T}$ observable.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a new transverse event activity classifier, $S_{T}$, defined as the scalar sum of transverse momentum of charged particles in the transverse region normalized by its mean, and claims it provides a useful alternative to the existing $R_{T}$ classifier for probing underlying event activity in p-p collisions, though the study is performed using only the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator rather than experimental data.

## Method
The authors generated p-p collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV using the PYTHIA 8.308 event generator with color reconnection and rope hadronization models. Events were selected with leading charged particle transverse momentum $p_{T}^{lead}$ between 5 and 40 GeV/c to ensure a plateau region in underlying event observables. The kinematic region was divided into toward, away, and transverse topological regions based on the azimuthal angle difference from the leading particle, and identified particle production ($\pi^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, p, $K_{S}^{0}$, $\Lambda^{0}$) was studied as a function of both $S_{T}$ and $R_{T}$ classifiers.

## Key numbers
- p-p collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV were simulated using PYTHIA 8.308
- Events were selected with $p_{T}^{lead}$ in the range 5 GeV/c to 40 GeV/c to ensure a plateau region in UE observables
- Kinematic cuts of $p_{T} \geq 0.15$ GeV/c and $|\eta| \leq 0.8$ were applied to approximate experimental sensitivity
- The crossing point for mean $p_{T}$ of charged particles in the transverse region with the away and toward regions occurs at $S_{T} \sim 1.5$ and $R_{T} \sim 1.5$
- For $V_{0}$ particles ($K_{S}^{0}$, $\Lambda^{0}$), the crossing point with the away region occurs at $S_{T}, R_{T} \sim 2.5$

## Context
This work extends the study of underlying event activity in small collision systems, motivated by observations of heavy-ion-like phenomena (strangeness enhancement, collectivity, anisotropic flows) in high-multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions reported by ALICE (2017), CMS (2010, 2012), and ATLAS (2016). The new $S_{T}$ classifier is compared with the previously defined $R_{T}$ classifier proposed by Sjostrand and Skands (2004) and used by ALICE (2012). The results provide a baseline for future experimental measurements at LHC energies and can help constrain Monte Carlo models of underlying event dynamics.
