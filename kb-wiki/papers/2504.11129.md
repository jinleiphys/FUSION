# $R$-matrix type parametrization of the Jost function for extracting the resonance parameters from scattering data

> A new method is proposed for fitting non-relativistic binary-scattering data and for extracting the parameters of possible quantum resonances in the compound system that is formed during the collision. The method combines the well-known $R$-matrix approach with the analysis based on the semi-analytic representation of the Jost functions. It is shown that such a combination has the advantages of both these approaches, namely, the number of the fitting parameters remains relatively small (as for the $R$-matrix approach) and the proper analytic structure of the $S$-matrix is preserved (as for the Jost function method). It is also shown that the new formalism, although closely related to the $R$-matrix method, has the benefit of no dependence on an arbitrary channel radius. The efficiency and accuracy of the proposed method are tested using a model single-channel potential. Artificial ``experimental'' data generated with this potential are fitted, and its known resonances are successfully recovered as zeros of the Jost function on the appropriate sheet of the Riemann surface of the energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid fitting method that combines the small number of fitting parameters characteristic of the R-matrix approach with the correct analytic structure of the S-matrix guaranteed by the Jost function formalism, and claims that this new method has the additional benefit of eliminating dependence on an arbitrary channel radius, unlike the standard R-matrix method.

## Method

The authors parametrize the single-valued, analytic functions A_l(E) and B_l(E) in the semi-analytic Jost function representation using polynomial expansions based on the R-matrix form, with real coefficients alpha_nl and beta_nl as fitting parameters and energies E_nl chosen near suspected resonances. They test the method by generating artificial total cross-section data from a model single-channel Noro-Taylor potential with a Coulomb tail, fitting these data using the parametrization with N=3 and l_max=2 (24 fitting parameters), and then locating resonance poles as zeros of the incoming Jost function on the appropriate unphysical Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

* The artificial data set consisted of 40 data points for the total cross-section in the energy range 1.7 < E < 5 (units with hbar=c=1, mu=1).
* The fitting used N=3 terms in the polynomial expansions, giving M=2(N+1)(l_max+1)=24 adjustable parameters for l_max=2.
* The first S-wave resonance was chosen at E_nl=1.78 and the second at E_nl=4.0; the P-wave resonance at E_nl=3.85 and another at E_nl=4.75; background energies included E_nl=0.
* The fitted resonance energies (not explicitly listed numerically in the truncated text) are reported to be in good agreement with the exact values calculated from the potential.

## Context

This work extends the Jost-function fitting method of Rakityansky (2013) and Vaandrager (2016, 2019, 2020), which used Taylor series parametrization requiring many parameters, by incorporating the R-matrix parametrization of Wigner (1947) and Descouvemont (2010). The new method overcomes the original Jost-function method's drawback of requiring a large number of fitting parameters (typically a few dozen) and the R-matrix method's drawbacks of dependence on an arbitrary channel radius and obscure analytic structure. The approach enables simultaneous extraction of multiple resonances from a single fit of total cross-section data, which the original method could not achieve for the test potential.
