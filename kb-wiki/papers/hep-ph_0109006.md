# From SPS to RHIC: Breaking the Barrier to the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> After 15 years of heavy-ion collision experiments at the AGS and SPS, the recent turn-on of RHIC has initiated a new stage of quark-gluon plasma studies. I review the evidence for deconfined quark-gluon matter at SPS energies and the recent confirmation of some of the key ideas by the new RHIC data. Measurements of the elliptic flow at RHIC provide strong evidence for efficient thermalization during the very early partonic collision stage, resulting in a well-developed quark-gluon plasma with almost ideal fluid-dynamical collective behaviour and a lifetime of several fm/c.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the combined evidence from SPS and early RHIC heavy-ion experiments is fully consistent with the formation of a quark-gluon plasma, but it hedges that at SPS energies only the region at and slightly above the critical temperature can be probed, while RHIC data provide the first strong evidence for thermalization in the partonic phase itself, based on elliptic flow measurements.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results from SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV) and RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s)=130 and 200 A GeV) and compares them to theoretical predictions from lattice QCD, hydrodynamic models, and hadronic rescattering simulations. The analysis focuses on four key observables: hadron yields and chemical freeze-out temperatures, J/psi suppression patterns, dilepton spectra from rho meson decays, and elliptic flow coefficients as functions of centrality and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* Critical energy density for deconfinement: about 0.6 GeV/fm^3 from lattice QCD (Karsch)
* Critical temperature Tc approximately 170 MeV +/- 10% from lattice QCD
* Initial energy density for central Pb+Pb at SPS: 3.2 +/- 0.3 GeV/fm^3 at tau_0=1 fm/c (Bjorken estimate)
* Initial energy density at RHIC (sqrt(s)=130 A GeV): 60% larger than SPS value; additional 15% increase at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV
* Initial temperature at SPS: approximately 210-220 MeV; at RHIC possibly as high as 350 MeV
* Chemical freeze-out temperature at SPS: T_chem=168 MeV, mu_B=266 MeV from 18 particle ratios
* Chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC: T_chem approximately 175 MeV, mu_B approximately 50 MeV
* Kinetic freeze-out temperature at SPS: T_f approximately 100 MeV with mean transverse flow velocity v_perp approximately 0.55 c
* Kinetic freeze-out temperature at RHIC: T_f approximately 125 MeV with 20% larger radial flow than SPS
* Freeze-out energy density: approximately 50 MeV/fm^3
* Strangeness enhancement: triply strange Omega baryons enhanced by factor 17 relative to pBe collisions
* Elliptic flow thermalization time at RHIC: approximately 0.5 fm/c from hydrodynamic calculations

## Context

This review extends the line of work from SPS experiments (NA38/NA50, CERES/NA45, WA97, WA98) and compares their findings to the first RHIC results from PHENIX, STAR, and PHOBOS. The paper builds on lattice QCD predictions by Karsch (2000) and earlier theoretical work on QGP signatures by Rafelski (1982), Matsui and Satz (1986), and Shuryak (1978). It enables the next stage of detailed QGP property studies at RHIC and future LHC experiments, particularly for electromagnetic radiation and jet quenching measurements.
