# Direct photons in Pb+Pb at CERN-SPS from microscopic transport theory

> Direct photon production in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energy is calculated within the relativistic microscopic transport model UrQMD, and within distinctly different versions of relativistic hydrodynamics. We find that in UrQMD the local momentum distributions of the secondaries are strongly elongated along the beam axis initially. Therefore, the pre-equilibrium contribution dominates the photon spectrum at transverse momenta above $\approx 1.5$ GeV. The hydrodynamics prediction of a strong correlation between the temperature and radial expansion velocities on the one hand and the slope of the transverse momentum distribution of direct photons on the other hand thus is not recovered in UrQMD. The rapidity distribution of direct photons in UrQMD reveals that the initial conditions for the longitudinal expansion of the photon source (the meson ``fluid'') resemble rather boostinvariance than Landau-like flow.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium contribution from meson-meson collisions with large longitudinal rapidity difference dominates the direct photon spectrum at transverse momenta above about 1.5 GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energy, contradicting the hydrodynamics prediction of a strong correlation between source temperature and the slope of the photon transverse momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors calculated direct photon production in central Pb+Pb collisions at 160A GeV using the relativistic microscopic transport model UrQMD, which includes resonance excitation, string fragmentation, and secondary interactions without assuming thermal or chemical equilibrium. They compared these results to three different hydrodynamic models: a boost-invariant scaling expansion, a Landau-type one-dimensional expansion, and a three-fluid model, all incorporating a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma at T_C=160 MeV. Photon production rates from meson-meson reactions (ππ→ργ, πρ→πγ, πη→πγ) were computed explicitly in UrQMD using differential cross sections from Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991), while hydrodynamic calculations used the thermal rate from the same reference.

## Key numbers

- Pre-equilibrium meson-meson collisions with rapidity gap |Δy_z|>3 dominate direct photon emission for transverse momenta k_T > 1.5 GeV in UrQMD
- Exponential fit of the photon spectrum in the region 2 GeV ≤ k_T ≤ 3 GeV yields an effective temperature of 260 MeV for both the three-fluid model and Landau expansion, and 210 MeV for the Bjorken expansion
- The Bjorken expansion assumed an initial temperature of 300 MeV and initial proper time τ_0 = 0.22 fm/c
- The Landau expansion assumed an initial longitudinal extension of 2L = 2R_Pb/γ_CM ≈ 1.4 fm
- The bag constant for the QGP phase transition was chosen as B^{1/4} = 235 MeV, giving a critical temperature T_C = 160 MeV
- The strong coupling constant was fixed at α_S = g^2/4π = 0.4 for the photon rate calculations
- In UrQMD, the temperature extracted from the nearly isotropic collision spectrum (|Δy|<3) was T = 180 ± 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Srivastava and Sinha (1994), Sollfrank et al. (1997), and Dumitru et al. (1995) that used hydrodynamic models to predict direct photon production, and contrasts with the transport approach of Bratkovskaya and Cassing (1997). The finding that pre-equilibrium contributions dominate high-k_T photons challenges the use of photon slope as a thermometer of the thermalized source, a correlation emphasized in hydrodynamic calculations by Seibert (1993) and others. The results enable future experimental tests at SPS energies, particularly through measurement of the photon rapidity distribution, which UrQMD predicts to be flatter than in Landau or three-fluid models, more closely resembling boost-invariant expansion.
