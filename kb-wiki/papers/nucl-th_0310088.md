# Quark-Hadron Duality and Scaling in Reduced QCD

> We introduce a generalization of 1+1 dimensional large Nc QCD, which we refer to as ``reduced'' QCD, or rQCD. In this model gluons and quark momenta live in 1+1 dimensions only, but the quark spin and all other particles (leptons, and the photon) live in the full 1+3 dimensions. The bound states of quarks and antiquarks are identical to those originally described by 't Hooft (except that there are new transversely polarized states previously excluded), so the model is exactly soluable. However, significant differences arise when the model is applied to electromagnetic interactions. After reviewing the strongly interacting sector of the theory, we discuss deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in this model, and show that the new states with transverse polarization give the Callan-Gross relation and remove the pathological features of the original 1+1 dimensional description. We conclude that rQCD gives a satisfactory description of the phenomenology and provides a deep understanding of both duality and DIS.

## Key claim

The authors introduce "reduced QCD" (rQCD), a generalization of 1+1 dimensional large Nc QCD where gluons and quark momenta live in 1+1 dimensions but quark spin and all other particles live in full 1+3 dimensions, and claim that this model gives a satisfactory description of deep inelastic scattering phenomenology and provides a deep understanding of both duality and scaling, provided that the new transversely polarized states (previously excluded) are included.

## Method

The authors construct rQCD by extending the 't Hooft model so that quark spin degrees of freedom and the photon live in 1+3 dimensions while gluons and quark momenta remain confined to 1+1 dimensions. They solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator in the large Nc limit using the rainbow approximation, then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for q\bar{q} bound states numerically using modified cubic splines with fractional-power boundary conditions. They compute the electromagnetic current and structure functions for deep inelastic scattering, including contributions from the three degenerate spin states (γ−, γ−γx, and γ−γy).

- The chiral limit (m₀ = 0) gives exact ground state solution M₀ = 0 with wave function Φ₀(z,r) = 1 for all z
- The bound state masses follow a linear Regge trajectory M_n² ∝ nπg² up to approximately n ∼ 60

## Context

This work extends the original 't Hooft model (1974) of large Nc QCD in 1+1 dimensions, which Einhorn (1975) had previously applied to deep inelastic scattering and found pathological results (DIS cross section proportional to the square of undressed quark masses, approaching zero in the chiral limit, and no Callan-Gross relation). The authors' earlier toy model of scalar and spinor fields in 1+1 dimensions (Batiz and Gross, 2003) had shown that extending spinor degrees of freedom to 1+3 dimensions could recover the Callan-Gross relation. The present work applies this same extension to the 't Hooft model, removing the pathological features and enabling future numerical studies of the approach to scaling in the DIS limit (planned for a subsequent paper, Batiz and Gross, 2004).
