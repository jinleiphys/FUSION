# Study of radioactivity built-up and decay with singles time-stamped data

> Time-stamped data have been used to estimate the decay half-lives of radioactive 118mSb and 64,66,68Ga nuclei. These nuclei are populated through the reaction 4He (Elab = 32 MeV) + nat In and p (Elab = 10 MeV) + natZn, respectively. The g -rays emitted by the excited daughter nuclei are detected by a single high purity germanium (HPGe) detector and the data are acquired using a CAEN 5780M desktop digitizer. Half-lives of different orders of magnitudes for the above mentioned nuclei are obtained from both the decay of parent and the growth of daughter nuclei. It is observed that the half-lives obtained from both these techniques match fairly well and also are consistent with the reported values. It is demonstrated that the time-stamped data can be useful while studying certain half-lives which are too long for measurement using in-beam electronic technique and too short for decay studies in off-beam methods. The data also have a special relevance for constant monitoring of beam current and beam-tuning.

# Study of radioactivity built-up and decay with singles time-stamped data

## Key claim
Time-stamped singles data from a digital data acquisition system can reliably measure nuclear half-lives ranging from minutes to days, bridging the gap between in-beam electronic timing (too short) and off-beam decay studies (too long), provided the event bin size is optimized to balance statistical precision and number of data points.

## Method
The authors irradiated natural indium with a 32 MeV alpha beam and natural zinc with a 10 MeV proton beam at the Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre, Kolkata. Gamma rays from decaying daughter nuclei were detected by a single HPGe detector coupled to a CAEN 5780M desktop digitizer (14 bit, 100 MS/s), which recorded time-stamped list-mode data with 10 ns resolution. Half-lives were extracted both from the exponential decay of parent nuclei and from the growth curves of daughter nuclei, using event binning (1M or 3M events per bin) rather than fixed time intervals, with fits performed via chi-squared minimization.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of 118mSb: 4.92(2) h (from linear fit of 1050 keV decay gamma, 1M event bin), compared to adopted value of 5.00(2) h
- Half-life of 64Ga: 2.61 ± 0.15 min, compared to adopted value of 2.627 ± 0.012 min
- Half-life of 66Ga: 9.82 ± 1.18 h, compared to adopted value of 9.49 ± 0.03 h
- Half-life of 68Ga: 70.30 ± 3.57 min, compared to adopted value of 67.71 ± 0.08 min
- Time resolution of digitizer time-stamp: 10 ns
- Digitizer sampling rate: 100 MHz (100 MS/s)
- Digitizer resolution: 14 bit, 16k channels
- Beam energies: 4He at 32 MeV (lab); proton at 10 MeV (lab)

## Context
This work extends the use of digital pulse processing (DPP) methods, which have been stimulated by advances in flash ADCs and FPGAs (Li et al.; Ne et al.), to half-life measurements that are too long for conventional in-beam electronic timing and too short for off-beam decay studies. The results are compared with adopted values from the National Nuclear Data Center (NNDC) and with earlier work by Bolotin. The technique enables constant monitoring of beam current and beam tuning during in-beam experiments, and the authors suggest it could be tested for lifetime measurements in the range of tens of nanoseconds in future work.
