# New look at geometrical scaling

> In this note we discuss emergence of geometrical scaling (firstly proposed for deep inelastic collisions) in pp scattering at the LHC and in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. After discussing general properties of geometrical scaling (GS) we focus on simple signatures of GS, namely on ratios of the $p_{\rm T}$ values at which multiplicity spectra for different energies are equal.

# New look at geometrical scaling

## Key claim

Geometrical scaling (GS), originally proposed for deep inelastic scattering, also emerges in proton-proton scattering at the LHC and in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, but the paper presents this as a qualitative observation based on ratios of transverse momenta at equal multiplicities, noting that more detailed studies are needed for quantitative confirmation.

## Method

The author constructs ratios S^{p_T}_{W1/W2} of transverse momenta at which charged-particle multiplicity spectra are equal for two different collision energies, using CMS non-single diffractive pp spectra at 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV and PHOBOS Au-Au spectra at 62.4 and 200 GeV. These ratios are compared to the prediction from geometrical scaling, (W1/W2)^{λ/(2+λ)} with λ = 0.27, where the saturation scale Q_s^2 follows a power law in x. The method requires interpolating one spectrum (here the 7 TeV data) to find matching p_T values.

## Key numbers

- λ = 0.27, the power used in the saturation scale Q_s^2(x) = Q_0^2 (x/x_0)^{-λ}, with Q_0 ~ 1 GeV and x_0 ~ 10^{-3}
- For pp collisions, S^{p_T}_{7 TeV/0.9 TeV} and S^{p_T}_{7 TeV/2.36 TeV} are plotted as functions of log(N_ch), showing approximate constancy over a wide range of N_ch
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC, S^{p_T}_{200 GeV/62.4 GeV} is shown for two centrality classes: 0-6% and 45-50%, with a line corresponding to λ = 0.27
- The predicted ratio from GS is S^{p_T}_{W1/W2} = (W1/W2)^{λ/(2+λ)}; for λ = 0.27, this gives specific numerical values for each energy pair

## Context

This work extends the concept of geometrical scaling, first proposed by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Wusthoff (2000) for deep inelastic scattering and based on the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) saturation model, to hadronic collisions. It builds on earlier work by McLerran (2010) and Praszalowicz (2011) on GS in pp collisions, and on studies of saturation in heavy ion collisions by Kharzeev (2000) and McLerran (2010). The method enables a simple qualitative test of GS across different collision systems and energies, but the author notes that quantitative studies require analyzing ratios R_{W1/W2} of the spectra themselves, and that future LHC data at higher energies and for different nuclei will allow further tests of A-dependence of the saturation scale.
