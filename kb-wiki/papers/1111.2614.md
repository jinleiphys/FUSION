# Nucleon axial and pseudoscalar form factors from the covariant Faddeev equation

> We compute the axial and pseudoscalar form factors of the nucleon in the Dyson-Schwinger approach. To this end, we solve a covariant three-body Faddeev equation for the nucleon wave function and determine the matrix elements of the axialvector and pseudoscalar isotriplet currents. Our only input is a well-established and phenomenologically successful ansatz for the nonperturbative quark-gluon interaction. As a consequence of the axial Ward-Takahashi identity that is respected at the quark level, the Goldberger-Treiman relation is reproduced for all current-quark masses. We discuss the timelike pole structure of the quark-antiquark vertices that enters the nucleon matrix elements and determines the momentum dependence of the form factors. Our result for the axial charge underestimates the experimental value by 20-25% which might be a signal of missing pion-cloud contributions. The axial and pseudoscalar form factors agree with phenomenological and lattice data in the momentum range above Q^2 ~ 1...2 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The authors compute nucleon axial and pseudoscalar form factors using a covariant three-body Faddeev equation in rainbow-ladder truncation, finding that the axial charge underestimates the experimental value by 20-25%, which they interpret as a possible signal of missing pion-cloud contributions rather than a failure of the underlying approach.

## Method

The authors solve a covariant three-body Faddeev equation for the nucleon bound-state amplitude using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark-gluon interaction, with the effective coupling taken from Maris and Tandy (1999). They then compute the nucleon matrix elements of the axialvector and pseudoscalar isotriplet currents by "gauging" the three-quark scattering matrix, which yields impulse-approximation diagrams and gluon-exchange kernel contributions. The dressed axialvector and pseudoscalar quark-antiquark vertices are determined from their inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations rather than from model ansatze used in earlier quark-diquark approaches.

## Key numbers

* Axial charge \(g_A\): the computed value underestimates the experimental value by 20-25%
* Axial and pseudoscalar form factors agree with phenomenological and lattice data in the momentum range above \(Q^2 \sim 1\dots 2\) GeV\(^2\)
* The nucleon bound-state amplitude contains approximately 30% p-wave admixture and roughly 1% d-wave contribution in the canonical normalization
* The infrared scale \(\Lambda\) is set via the experimental pion decay constant; the effective coupling uses \(\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD}=0.234\) GeV and \(\Lambda_t=1\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the three-body Faddeev approach previously applied to nucleon and \(\Delta\) masses (Eichmann 2009) and nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Eichmann 2011) to the axial and pseudoscalar channels. It improves upon earlier quark-diquark model calculations (Hellstern 1997, Oettel 2000) which faced complications from seagull diagrams and chiral Ward identity requirements that prevented full axial form factor calculations. The results enable future studies of pion-cloud contributions and provide a framework for computing nucleon structure at all momentum scales within the Dyson-Schwinger approach.
