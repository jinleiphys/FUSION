# Experimental constraints on $γ$-ray strength function of $^{72}$Ga from $^{72}\mathrm{Ga(n,γ)^{72}Ga}$ capture data and reevaluation of Maxwellian-averaged cross sections of $^{71}$Ga

> The $γ$-ray strength function of medium-mass neutron rich nuclei $^{72}$Ga has been extracted from the statistical Hauser-Feshbach analysis of the available capture data of $^{71}\mathrm{Ga}(n,γ){}^{72}\mathrm{Ga}$ over the 0.01 - 3 MeV energy range with the required nuclear level density (NLD) of the $^{72}$Ga constraint from the work of R. Santra et al.,[\href{https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevC.107.064611}{Physical Review C 107, 064611 (2023)}]. The Gogny D1M model for the E1 and M1 strength functions, including low-energy upbends of $^{72}$Ga nuclei, is experimentally constrained in the present work. Subsequently, the Maxwellian-averaged cross section(MACS) of $^{71}\mathrm{Ga}$ has been reevaluated based on the present $γ$-ray strength function. It is found that the present MACS value at \(kT = 30\) keV is 115.35$^{+11.92}_{-10.44}$ mb, which is consistent with previous work.

# Experimental constraints on γ-ray strength function of ⁷²Ga from ⁷²Ga(n,γ)⁷²Ga capture data and reevaluation of Maxwellian-averaged cross sections of ⁷¹Ga

## Key claim
The Gogny D1M HFB+QRPA model for the E1 and M1 γ-ray strength function of ⁷²Ga, including low-energy upbend contributions, has been experimentally constrained by fitting to available ⁷¹Ga(n,γ)⁷²Ga capture data over 0.01–3 MeV, yielding a Maxwellian-averaged cross section (MACS) at kT = 30 keV of 115.35⁺¹¹·⁹²₋₁₀·₄₄ mb that is consistent with most prior work, but the extracted γSF carries significant uncertainty primarily from the input nuclear level density of ⁷²Ga rather than from the capture data themselves.

## Method
The authors performed statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculations using the TALYS 2.0 code on available ⁷¹Ga(n,γ)⁷²Ga capture data over the neutron energy range 0.01–3 MeV. The nuclear level density of ⁷²Ga was taken from the experimental work of Santra et al. (2023) for excitation energies 3.33–151.33 MeV, with the constant-temperature model (T = 0.88 MeV, E₀ = −2.16 MeV) used below 3.33 MeV. A two-stage χ² minimization, global Differential Evolution followed by local MIGRAD refinement, was used to fit five free parameters (N, f₀, ε₀, C, η) that modify the Gogny D1M HFB+QRPA E1 and M1 strength functions including low-energy upbend contributions.

## Key numbers
- MACS of ⁷¹Ga at kT = 30 keV: 115.35⁺¹¹·⁹²₋₁₀·₄₄ mb
- Best-fit χ²/dof = 5.84 for the γSF parameterization
- E1 contributes 67.63% and M1 contributes 32.37% of the capture cross section
- Best-fit parameters: N = 1.29 ± 0.02; f₀ = (1.00 ± 0.13) × 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻⁴; ε₀ = 3.00 ± 0.23 MeV; C = (1.00 ± 0.02) × 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻³; η = 0.85 ± 0.01 MeV⁻¹
- Uncertainty in γSF from n-OPM variation is ≤ 5%
- The Gogny D1M HFB+QRPA model gave χ²/Ndf = 6.57 before the upbend parameterization

## Context
This work extends the statistical-model approach to constraining γ-ray strength functions, previously applied to ⁹⁰Zr by Netterdon et al. (2015) via (p,γ) data, to the medium-mass neutron-rich nucleus ⁷²Ga using (n,γ) capture data. The experimentally constrained γSF of ⁷²Ga is compared with available data for ⁷³,⁷⁴Ge from Ren et al. (2016), showing agreement in the 2–3 MeV range but a slightly higher overall slope. The reevaluated MACS resolves discrepancies among prior values ranging from 50 mb (Woosley et al., 1978) to 131 mb (Goriely et al., 2002) and is consistent with the KADoNiS v3.0 value of 123(8) mb and the recent measurement of 105(7) mb by Tessler et al. (2022). The results enable improved s-process nucleosynthesis calculations for gallium isotopes, where a 50% change in the neutron capture cross section on one gallium isotope can alter s-abundances of following isotopes by up to 20% in shell carbon burning.
