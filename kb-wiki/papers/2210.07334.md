# Light-nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions within a thermodynamical approach

> We present results of simulations of light-nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions within updated Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) final State interactions (THESEUS). The simulations were performed for Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions in the collision energy range of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 6.4--19.6 GeV. The light-nuclei production is treated within the thermodynamical approach on equal basis with hadrons. The only additional parameter related to the light nuclei is the energy density of late freeze-out that imitates afterburner stage of the collision because the light nuclei do not participate in the UrQMD evolution. This parameter is fixed from the condition of the best reproduction of the proton transverse-momentum spectrum after the UrQMD afterburner by that at the late freeze-out. The updated THESEUS results in not perfect, but a reasonable reproduction of data on bulk observables of the light nuclei, especially their functional dependence on the collision energy and light-nucleus mass. Various ratios, $d/p$, $t/p$, $t/d$, and $N(t)\times N(p)/N^2(d)$, are also considered. The directed flow of light nuclei turns out to be more involved. Apparently, it requires explicit treatment of the afterburner evolution of light nuclei that violates the kinetic equilibrium. Imperfect reproduction of the light-nuclei data leaves room for medium effects in produced light nuclei.

## Key claim
The updated THESEUS model, using a thermodynamical approach for light-nuclei production with no additional free parameters (except for a late freeze-out energy density that imitates the afterburner), can reasonably reproduce the functional dependence of light-nuclei yields on collision energy and mass, but systematically overestimates the absolute yields and fails to describe the directed flow, indicating that explicit treatment of the afterburner for light nuclei is required.

## Method
The authors used the Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD final State interactions (THESEUS) to simulate Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 6.4--19.6 GeV. Light nuclei were treated thermodynamically on an equal basis with hadrons, but were not passed through the UrQMD afterburner. To imitate the missing afterburner effects, the freeze-out energy density for light nuclei was lowered from the conventional 0.4 GeV/fm$^3$ to 0.2 GeV/fm$^3$, a value chosen by fitting the proton transverse-momentum spectrum after the UrQMD afterburner.

## Key numbers
- The late freeze-out energy density used to imitate the afterburner for light nuclei was set to $\varepsilon_{\rm frz}=$ 0.2 GeV/fm$^3$, compared to the conventional value of 0.4 GeV/fm$^3$.
- Simulations were performed for Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions in the collision energy range of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 6.4--19.6 GeV.
- The model systematically overestimates the $d/p$, $t/p$, and $t/d$ midrapidity ratios for central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions compared to STAR data (STAR 2022).
- The calculated $N(t)\times N(p)/N^2(d)$ ratio overestimates the final STAR data but is generally below the preliminary STAR data, which used UrQMD for weak-decay feed-down subtraction.

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic approach to light-nuclei production, previously successful in statistical models (Andronic 2010, Vovchenko 2020), by implementing it within a dynamical 3FD+UrQMD framework (THESEUS). It contrasts with coalescence models (Ivanov 2017) which require tuned parameters for each nucleus and energy, and with transport models like SMASH (Oliinychenko 2018) that treat deuterons microscopically. The results enable future work to focus on improving the afterburner treatment for light nuclei and investigating the role of medium effects, particularly to clarify the non-monotonic behavior of the $N(t)\times N(p)/N^2(d)$ ratio observed by STAR near 20-30 GeV.
