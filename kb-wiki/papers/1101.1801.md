# Bulk viscosity and energy-momentum correlations in high energy hadron collisions

> We show how the measurement of appropriately constructed particle-energy/momentum correlations allows access to the bulk viscosity of strongly interacting hadron matter in heavy ion collisions. This measurement can be performed by the LHC and RHIC experiments in events with high-particle multiplicity, following up on existing estimates of the shear viscosity based on elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the bulk viscosity of strongly interacting hadron matter can be extracted from measurements of particle energy-momentum correlations in high-multiplicity heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC, but the method requires reconstruction of the trace of the stress-energy tensor (needing at least three correlation components) and depends on knowledge of the temperature and freeze-out time from other measurements, with the obtained value serving as a lower bound due to detector inefficiencies.

## Method

The authors derive a relation between the bulk viscosity and fluctuations of the stress-energy tensor trace using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, then connect these fluctuations to experimentally accessible particle momentum correlations by constructing observables from sums over particle pairs of quantities like (p_r^2/E) and (p_z^2/E). They separate the stochastic force contribution from the hydrodynamic part by exploiting the different time-dependence of their correlation functions (delta-function versus exponential decay), and provide relativistic boost corrections to transform the fluid rest-frame formulas to the laboratory frame. A Monte Carlo study using ALICE transverse momentum distributions for pp collisions at 900 GeV and PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV evaluates the systematic error from particle mixing between different fluid elements under various kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at 900 GeV with a cut of P_perp > 0.3 GeV and Delta phi = +/- 60 degrees, approximately 35.7% of thermally emitted particles are lost from the kinematic bin, introducing a systematic theory uncertainty
- For PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV with the same cuts (P_perp > 0.3 GeV, Delta phi = +/- 60 degrees), approximately 34.3% of particles mix with other fluid cells
- The ALICE pp transverse momentum distribution at 900 GeV is fit by 11.47 exp(-4.10 P_perp) for P_perp < 1.7 GeV and 0.25 P_perp^(-5.95) for P_perp > 1.7 GeV
- The ALICE PbPb transverse momentum distribution at 2.76 TeV is fit by 8.90 x 10^5 exp(-2.93 P_perp) for P_perp < 2.0 GeV and 2.00 x 10^5 P_perp^(-6.29) for P_perp > 2.0 GeV
- The final expression for bulk viscosity depends on the freeze-out time tau_f squared and the temperature T, with the observable constructed from particle momenta and energies

## Context

This work extends the shear viscosity measurement method via momentum correlations developed by Gavin and Abdel-Aziz (2006, 2007) to the bulk viscosity, which is more challenging because it requires reconstruction of the full stress-energy tensor trace rather than a single component. The paper builds on the established consensus that the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions behaves as a strongly interacting fluid with very low shear viscosity over entropy ratio eta/s approximately 0.1, confirmed at LHC by Lacey (2010). The method enables experimental access to the bulk viscosity, which is expected to have a maximum near the phase transition (Karsch 2007, Fernandez-Fraile 2008) and may be comparable to shear viscosity in that region, unlike the usual assumption eta much greater than zeta in fluid mechanics.
