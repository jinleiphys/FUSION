# $^3$He spin-dependent structure functions within the relativistic Light-Front Hamiltonian dynamics

> $^3$He spin-dependent structure functions, $g^3_1(x)$ and $g^3_2(x)$, which parametrize the hadronic tensor in polarized deep-inelastic scattering, were evaluated within the Poincaré covariant light-front framework. The Bakamjian-Thomas construction of the Poincaré generators allows us to make use of a realistic $^3$He wave function, obtained from refined nuclear phenomenological potentials. The same approach was already successfully applied to the $^3$He and $^4$He unpolarized deep-inelastic scattering. To investigate the neutron polarized structure functions, $g^n_1$ and $g^n_2$, a readily implementable procedure, aimed at extracting the neutron spin structure functions from those of $^3$He, is shown to hold. Moreover, the first moment of $g^3_1(x)$ was evaluated, aiming at providing a valuable check of the Bjorken sum rule. The present analysis is relevant for experiments nvolving polarized beams planned at the future facilities, like the Electron Ion Colliders.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Poincaré-covariant light-front calculation of the \(^3\)He spin-dependent structure functions \(g_1^3(x)\) and \(g_2^3(x)\) yields results that are nearly indistinguishable from a non-relativistic calculation using the same nuclear potential, meaning that relativistic effects are too small to be resolved by current experimental precision, but the covariant framework provides a theoretically sound baseline for future high-precision experiments at Electron-Ion Colliders.

## Method

The authors evaluate the nuclear spin-dependent structure functions \(g_1^3(x)\) and \(g_2^3(x)\) within the impulse approximation using a Poincaré-covariant light-front Hamiltonian dynamics framework, employing the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of the Poincaré generators. A realistic \(^3\)He wave function from the Argonne v18 phenomenological potential is used, and the nuclear structure functions are obtained via a convolution of the spin-dependent light-front spectral function (related to transverse momentum dependent distributions) with free-nucleon structure functions, taken in the Bjorken limit. The free-nucleon \(g_1^N\) is taken from the GRSV NLO parametrization, and \(g_2^N\) is obtained via the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation.

## Key numbers

* Effective nucleon polarizations in \(^3\)He from the covariant calculation: \(p_1^n \simeq 0.873\), \(p_1^p \simeq -0.0230\), \(p_2^n \simeq 0.873\), \(p_2^p \simeq -0.0245\); the non-relativistic values are \(p_1^n \simeq 0.893\), \(p_1^p \simeq -0.0212\).
* First moment of the \(^3\)He spin structure function: \(\Gamma_3 = -0.0604\) from direct calculation; \(\Gamma_3^{\text{pol}} = -0.0611\) from the approximation using effective polarizations, a difference of about 1\%.
* Extracted first moment of the neutron from the GRSV parametrization at \(Q^2 = 10~\text{GeV}^2\): \(\Gamma^{\text{GRSV}}_n = -0.063\) and \(\bar \Gamma_n = -0.0622\).
* The approximation of Eq. (13) for extracting the neutron structure function from \(^3\)He data works to better than 3\% difference in the relevant \(x\) region up to \(x = 0.9\).

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant light-front approach previously applied to unpolarized deep-inelastic scattering on \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He (Pace 2022, Fornetti 2023) to the spin-dependent case. It validates the neutron extraction procedure originally proposed in a non-covariant framework (Ciofi degli Atti 1993), which has been used by experimental collaborations such as E142, and shows that the covariant effective polarizations yield a slightly improved extraction. The results provide a theoretically robust foundation for analyzing future polarized \(^3\)He data from the Electron-Ion Collider and for testing the Bjorken sum rule.
