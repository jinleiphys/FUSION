# Nuclear suppression at RHIC and LHC in Glauber-Gribov approach

> The approach to problem of nuclear shadowing based on Gribov Reggeon calculus is presented. Here the total cross section of $h A$ interaction is found in a parameter-free description, employing the new data on the gluon density of the Pomeron, measured with high precision at HERA, as input. The model is then applied for calculation of $J/ψ$ production in $d Au$ collisions at top RHIC energy. It is shown that the theoretical estimates are in a very good agreement with the PHENIX data, and further predictions for the $J/ψ$ suppression in $p Pb$ collisions at coming soon LHC are made.

# Nuclear suppression at RHIC and LHC in Glauber-Gribov approach

## Key claim
The observed reduction of nuclear absorption for J/ψ production in dAu collisions at RHIC can be explained as a signal of the onset of coherent scattering for heavy-state production within Gribov Reggeon theory, rather than as a decrease in the absorptive cross section itself. The model provides a parameter-free description of nuclear shadowing that agrees with PHENIX data, but the agreement is limited to mid- and forward rapidity; at backward rapidity the model does not include anti-shadowing effects, which are assumed to be a 10% effect.

## Method
The total hadron-nucleus cross section is expanded in an infinite series of multiple scattering contributions using Gribov Reggeon calculus, where the double rescattering term is linked to diffractive deep inelastic scattering (DDIS) data. Higher-order rescatterings are summed via the Schwimmer unitarization of fan diagrams with triple-Pomeron interactions. The gluon shadowing is calculated by inserting the diffractive gluon distribution from HERA measurements and the CTEQ6M gluon distribution into the unitarized cross section formula, with nuclear density profiles taken from a 3-parameter Woods-Saxon parameterization.

## Key numbers
- The critical energy for onset of coherent scattering for J/ψ is given by s_M = (M_QQ^2 / x_+) * (R_A m_N / sqrt(3)), where M_QQ is the heavy quarkonium mass; for J/ψ at RHIC this scale falls within the RHIC energy range.
- The integration limits for diffractive mass are M^2_min = 4m_π^2 = 0.08 GeV^2 and M^2_max chosen such that x_Pom ≤ 0.1, which guarantees disappearance of nuclear shadowing at x ~ 0.1.
- The deuteron form factor is approximated as F_D(t) = exp(at) with a = 40 GeV^{-2}.
- At LHC, gluon shadowing should reduce the nuclear modification factor for J/ψ to R_pPb^{J/ψ} ≃ 0.6 at mid-rapidity and to R_pPb^{J/ψ} ≃ 0.5 at forward rapidities for minimum bias pPb collisions.
- The absorptive cross section extracted from PHENIX data is 1-2 mb, smaller than at lower energies.

## Context
This work extends the Gribov Reggeon theory approach of Capella (1998), Armesto (2003), and Tywoniuk (2007) to J/ψ production, using new HERA measurements of the gluon diffractive parton density as input. It contradicts predictions by Braun (1998) and Bedjidian (2003) that absorptive effects would increase with rising collision energy. The model enables predictions for J/ψ suppression in pPb collisions at LHC and provides a crucial input for correct treatment of final-state effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions. A similar approach with a simpler parameterization of nuclear shadowing was considered by Capella (2006).
