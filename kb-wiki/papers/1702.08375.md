# Amplitude reconstruction from complete electroproduction experiments and truncated partial-wave expansions

> We compare the methods of amplitude reconstruction, for a complete experiment and a truncated partial-wave analysis, applied to the electroproduction of pseudoscalar mesons. We give examples which show, in detail, how the amplitude reconstruction (observables measured at a single energy and angle) is related to a truncated partial-wave analysis (observables measured at a single energy and a number of angles). A connection is made to existing data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for pseudoscalar meson electroproduction, a complete experiment analysis (CEA) determining six complex amplitudes at a single energy and angle and a truncated partial-wave analysis (TPWA) using data at a single energy and multiple angles are equivalent only when the number of partial waves is small enough that the CEA and TPWA require the same number of measurements; for higher partial waves, the TPWA requires measurements at multiple angles and can be solved with fewer distinct observable types than the CEA.

## Method

The authors compare CEA and TPWA approaches by constructing explicit complete sets of polarization observables (response functions) from the 36 possible in electroproduction, testing them with Mathematica NSolve for exact integer-algebra solutions at low partial-wave truncations (L=0, J=1/2, L=1) and with NMinimize random-search minimization for L=2. They extend Omelaenko's linear-factor decomposition of transversity amplitudes to include the longitudinal amplitudes b5 and b6, introducing a root parameterization with 2L complex roots gamma_i and a normalization coefficient d_{2L} for the longitudinal sector.

## Key numbers

- For the simplest case (L=0, two S-wave multipoles E_{0+} and L_{0+}), only 3 measurements at a single angle are needed for both CEA and TPWA: R_T^{00}, R_{LT}^{0y}, R_{LT'}^{0y}
- For J=1/2 (four S,P-wave multipoles: E_{0+}, M_{1-}, L_{0+}, L_{1-}), 8 measurements at a single angle suffice for both CEA and TPWA; alternatively 8 measurements using only 5 distinct observables at 2 angles work for TPWA
- For the full set of 7 S,P-wave multipoles (L=0,1 including all electric, magnetic, and longitudinal), 14 measurements are required; a minimal set uses 6 observables at 2 angles
- For L=2 with 6 multipoles (E_{0+}, M_{1-}, E_{1+}, E_{2-}, L_{0+}, L_{1-}), 12 measurements at a single angle work for both CEA and TPWA; alternatively 12 measurements using only 5 observables at 3 angles suffice for TPWA
- The total number of real parameters to determine in the most general electroproduction case is 12L+1, with 6L+1 complex multipoles (E, M, L) for truncation order L

## Context

This work generalizes the authors' previous study of CEA and TPWA in photoproduction (Tiator et al., 2016) to electroproduction, where longitudinal amplitudes associated with the virtual photon complicate the spin structure. It extends the earlier analysis by Dmitrasinovic, Donnelly and Gross (1986), who examined the CEA for electroproduction and concluded that a complete set could be constructed without requiring both target and recoil polarization simultaneously. The paper enables practical planning of electroproduction experiments at facilities like JLab, showing that Rosenbluth separation of transverse and longitudinal response functions is not necessary for TPWA (the combination R_T + epsilon_L R_L can be used instead), and provides explicit minimal sets of observables for various truncation levels.
