# Structure of the orbital excited $N^*$ from the Schwinger-Dyson equations

> We present progress in the evaluation of $γ^* N \rightarrow N^*(1535)$ transition form factors in a quark-diquark picture of these baryons. Our analysis is based upon the fully-consistent treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction, embedded in the interlaced formalism of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress toward computing the $\gamma^* N \rightarrow N^*(1535)$ transition form factors using a quark-diquark picture with a symmetry-preserving vector $\times$ vector contact interaction, but the results are preliminary and the authors note that the common assumption that the $S_{1/2}$ helicity amplitude contribution is negligible at high $Q^2$ may be a poor approximation.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction (CI) embedded in the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations framework. They solve the gap equation for the quark propagator, the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons and diquarks, and the Faddeev equation for baryons in a quark-diquark picture, including spin-orbit repulsion via phenomenological couplings. The quark-photon vertex is obtained from an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation with an added anomalous electromagnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass from gap equation: $M = 0.368$ GeV, using current quark mass $m_{u/d} = 0.007$ GeV, $\alpha_{IR} = 0.93\pi$, $m_G = 0.8$ GeV, $\Lambda_{IR} = 0.24$ GeV, $\Lambda_{UV} = 0.905$ GeV
- Computed meson masses: $m_\pi = 0.14$ GeV, $m_\rho = 0.93$ GeV, $m_\sigma = 1.22$ GeV, $m_{a_1} = 1.37$ GeV
- Computed diquark masses: $0^+$ diquark $0.78$ GeV, $1^+$ diquark $1.06$ GeV, $0^-$ diquark $1.15$ GeV, $1^-$ diquark $1.33$ GeV
- Computed baryon masses within CI: $m_N = 1.14$ GeV, $m_{N^*} = 1.82$ GeV
- Diquark composition of $N(940)$: $77\%$ $0^+$, $22\%$ $1^+$, $<1\%$ $0^-$, $<1\%$ $1^-$
- Diquark composition of $N^*(1535)$: $12\%$ $0^+$, $<1\%$ $1^+$, $84\%$ $0^-$, $3.5\%$ $1^-$
- Spin-orbit repulsion couplings: $g_{SO}^{1,0} = 0.25, 0.32$ for mesons, multiplied by $1.8$ for corresponding diquarks; diquark breakup factor $g_{DB} = 0.1$ for opposite-parity diquarks in baryons
- Anomalous electromagnetic moment parameter: $\zeta = 1/2$

## Context

This work extends the quark-diquark approach of Eichmann (2011) and the contact interaction framework of Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010), Roberts (2010), and Wilson (2011) to the parity-partner transition $N \rightarrow N^*(1535)$. It builds on the diquark correlation studies of Lu (2017) and Chen (2017). The calculations are intended to provide benchmarks for forthcoming experimental results from the 12 GeV upgrade of Jefferson Laboratory, which will extend the $Q^2$ range to around 12 GeV$^2$, complementing existing data from MAID2007 (Drechsel, 2007), MAID analyses (Tiator, 2009), and JLab Hall C (Dalton, 2008).
