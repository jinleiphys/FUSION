# The non-perturbative QCD Debye mass from a Wilson line operator

> According to a proposal by Arnold and Yaffe, the non-perturbative g^2T-contribution to the Debye mass in the deconfined QCD plasma phase can be determined from a single Wilson line operator in the three-dimensional pure SU(3) gauge theory. We extend a previous SU(2) measurement of this quantity to the physical SU(3) case. We find a numerical coefficient which is more accurate and smaller than that obtained previously with another method, but still very large compared with the naive expectation: the correction is larger than the leading term up to T ~ 10^7 T_c, corresponding to g^2 ~ 0.4. At moderate temperatures T ~ 2 T_c, a consistent picture emerges where the Debye mass is m_D ~ 6T, the lightest gauge invariant screening mass in the system is ~ 3T, and the purely magnetic operators couple dominantly to a scale ~ 6T. Electric (~ gT) and magnetic (~ g^2T) scales are therefore strongly overlapping close to the phase transition, and the colour-electric fields play an essential role in the dynamics.

# The non-perturbative QCD Debye mass from a Wilson line operator

## Key claim

The non-perturbative $g^2T$ contribution to the Debye mass in the deconfined QCD plasma phase is determined from a single Wilson line operator in the three-dimensional pure SU(3) gauge theory, extending a previous SU(2) measurement. The correction is larger than the leading term up to $T \sim 10^7 T_c$, corresponding to $g^2 \sim 0.4$, meaning that at moderate temperatures the electric ($\sim gT$) and magnetic ($\sim g^2T$) scales are strongly overlapping.

## Method

The Debye mass is defined via gauge-invariant operators odd under a time-reflection symmetry ${\cal R}_z A_0^a \to -A_0^a$, following the proposal of Arnold and Yaffe. The measurement is reduced to evaluating a correlation function of field-strength operators connected by an adjoint Wilson line in the pure 3d SU(3) gauge theory, using lattice simulations with clover-type operators and variational smearing to project onto the lightest state. The measured mass $m$ from the exponential falloff is combined with perturbative matching to extract the coefficient $c_N$ of the $g^2T$ term in the physical Debye mass.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2): continuum extrapolated coefficient $c_2 = 1.14(4)$ (from fits to $\beta_G \ge 12$ with $\chi^2$/d.o.f. = 0.03).
- For SU(3): continuum extrapolated coefficient $c_3 = 1.65(6)$ (from quadratic fit to all $\beta_G$ values with $\chi^2$/d.o.f. = 1.25).
- At $T \sim 2 T_c$ with $g_3^2 \approx 2.7T$, the Debye mass is $m_D \sim 6T$.
- The correction exceeds the leading term up to $T \sim 10^7 T_c$, where $g^2 \sim 0.4$.
- At $T \sim 2 T_c$, the lightest gauge-invariant screening mass (from $A_0^a A_0^a$ bound state) is $\sim 3T$, and purely magnetic operators couple dominantly to a scale $\sim 6T$.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) measurement of Laine and Philipsen (1999) to the physical SU(3) case, using the Wilson line operator approach proposed by Arnold and Yaffe. The results are considerably smaller than those obtained by Kajantie et al. (1997) using operators in the 3d SU(N) + adjoint Higgs theory, who found $c_2 = 1.58(20)$ and $c_3 = 2.46(15)$. The present values scale well with $N$ and agree qualitatively with direct 4d simulations by Datta and Gupta (1999), who found $m(A_1^{-+}) \approx 6.3(2)T$ for the electric sector mass. The results enable reliable construction of dimensionally reduced effective theories down to $T \sim 2 T_c$, provided $A_0$ is kept in the action rather than integrated out.
