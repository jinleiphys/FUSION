# Two-pion exchange as a leading-order contribution in chiral effective field theory

> Pion exchange is the central ingredient to nucleon-nucleon interactions used in nuclear structure calculations, and one pion exchange (OPE) enters at leading order in chiral effective field theory. In the $^{2S+1}L_J={^1S_0}$ partial wave, however, OPE and a contact term needed for proper renormalization fail to produce the qualitative, and quantitative, features of the scattering phase shifts. Cutoff variation also revealed a surprisingly low breakdown momentum of about 330 MeV in this partial wave. Here we show that potentials consisting of OPE, two pion exchange (TPE), and a single contact address these problems and yield accurate and renormalization group (RG) invariant phase shifts in the $^1S_0$ partial wave. We demonstrate that a leading-order potential with TPE can be systematically improved by adding a contact quadratic in momenta. For momentum cutoffs below about 500 MeV, the removal of relevant physics from TPE loops needs to be compensated by additional contacts to keep RG invariance. Inclusion of the $Δ$ isobar degree of freedom in the potential does not change the strong contributions of TPE.

## Key claim

The paper shows that promoting two-pion exchange (TPE) to leading order alongside one-pion exchange (OPE) and a single contact term in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave yields accurate and renormalization-group-invariant phase shifts, contrary to the standard Weinberg power counting where TPE enters only at next-to-leading order. The authors hedge that this promotion is justified by the unexpectedly large matrix elements of TPE potentials that formally enter at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors constructed nucleon-nucleon potentials in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave consisting of OPE, leading TPE (NLO in Weinberg counting), and subleading TPE (NNLO in Weinberg counting), plus a single contact term \(V_{\rm ct}^{(0)}\) adjusted to reproduce the reference phase shift at laboratory energy \(E_0 = 15\) MeV. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation non-perturbatively for these potentials regularized with an exponential cutoff \(f(x)=e^{-x^n}\) with \(n=3\) and varied the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) from low values up to 4 GeV. They also tested systematic improvement by adding a contact quadratic in momenta \(V_{\rm ct}^{(2)}\) both non-perturbatively and perturbatively, and repeated the analysis with \(\Delta\) isobar degrees of freedom included in the TPE.

## Key numbers

* The breakdown momentum for OPE plus a single contact in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave was found to be \(\Lambda_\text{b} \approx 330\) MeV, but with TPE promoted to leading order the breakdown momentum increases to \(\Lambda_\text{b} \approx 500-520\) MeV.
* The relevant scale of the TPE interaction is estimated as \(\Lambda_\text{TPE} \equiv \sqrt{2 m_\pi m_N} \approx 510\) MeV, below which TPE physics is removed by the cutoff.
* The leading-order potential \(V_{\pi,\pi\pi} + V_{\rm ct}^{(0)}\) yields RG-invariant phase shifts for cutoffs above about 750 MeV, while for cutoffs \(\Lambda \lesssim 500\) MeV the removal of TPE physics must be compensated by additional contacts.
* The dimensionless ratio \(C \Lambda_\text{b}^2 / \tilde{C} \approx -4\) for the non-perturbative inclusion of the subleading contact, consistent with naive dimensional analysis expectations of order 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Lepage (1997), Birse (2006), and Valderrama (2011) that OPE plus contacts alone yields a surprisingly low breakdown momentum of about 200-330 MeV in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave, questioning the consistency of leading-order chiral EFT. It also builds on the proposal by Birse (2010) to promote TPE to leading order, providing the first rigorous analysis. The results enable a consistent leading-order chiral EFT description of the \(^1S_0\) partial wave with a breakdown momentum compatible with the general assumption of 500-1000 MeV, and show that chiral EFTs with cutoffs below about 500 MeV are effectively "two-pion-less" EFTs requiring additional contacts for proper renormalization.
