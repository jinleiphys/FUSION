# Effect of charmed meson loops on charmonium transitions

> The effects of intermediate charmed mesons on charmonium transitions with the emission of one pion or eta are studied systematically. Based on a non-relativistic effective field theory we show that charmed meson loops are enhanced compared to the corresponding tree-level contributions for transitions between two S-wave charmonia as well as for transitions between two P-wave charmonia. On the contrary, for the transitions between one S-wave and one P-wave charmonium state, the loops need to be analyzed case by case and often appear to be suppressed. The relation to and possible implications for an effective Lagrangian approach are also discussed. This study at the same time provides a cross check for the numerical evaluations.

## Key claim

Charmed meson loop contributions are enhanced relative to tree-level contributions for transitions between two S-wave charmonia and between two P-wave charmonia, but are suppressed for transitions between one S-wave and one P-wave charmonium state, meaning the loops must be analyzed case by case.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) to systematically calculate charmed meson triangle-loop diagrams for charmonium transitions with emission of one pion or eta. They compared these loop contributions to tree-level amplitudes derived from chiral effective Lagrangians that incorporate quark mass differences and electromagnetic effects, finding that electromagnetic contributions vanish at leading order.

## Key numbers

- The velocity of the intermediate charmed meson in below-threshold decays is approximately v ≈ 0.5 for most decays studied, leading to loop enhancement by a factor of 1/v compared to tree-level contributions.
- The tree-level amplitude for S-wave to S-wave transitions scales as M_tree^SS ~ (1/m_c) q δ, where δ denotes the quark mass difference.
- The tree-level amplitude for P-wave to P-wave transitions scales as M_tree^PP ~ (1/Λ_QCD) q δ.
- The tree-level amplitude for S-wave to P-wave transitions scales as M_tree^SP ~ δ.
- The π⁰-η mixing angle at leading order in chiral perturbation theory is ε_{π⁰η} = 0.01.
- The charmonium mass difference for decays h_c' → J/ψ π⁰(η) and χ_c0' → η_c π⁰(η) exceeds 800 MeV, of order O(Λ_χ) with Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV, beyond the range of chiral expansion convergence.

## Context

This work extends the NREFT formalism introduced by Guo et al. (2009) for ψ' → J/ψ π⁰(η) decays to a systematic study of all S-wave and P-wave charmonium transitions with one pion or eta emission. It contradicts the assumption that tree-level quark mass difference terms dominate these decays, showing that charmed meson loops provide the leading-order contribution for S-wave to S-wave and P-wave to P-wave transitions. This enables future extraction of light quark mass ratios from bottomonium transitions where loop contributions are suppressed, as suggested by Guo et al. (2010).
