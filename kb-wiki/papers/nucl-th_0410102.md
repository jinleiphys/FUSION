# Numerical study of the hadron-quark mixed phase

> The Coulomb screening effect and the finite-size effect such as surface tension are figured out in the hadron-quark deconfinement phase transition. We study the mixed phase of the quark droplets immersed in hadron matter. We see that the droplet phase is mechanically unstable if the surface tension is strong enough. Once the Coulomb potential is properly taken into account, we could effectively satisfy the condition for charge chemical equilibrium in the Maxwell construction. As a result, we suggest the Maxwell construction revives the physical meaning effectively.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that, when the Coulomb screening effect is properly included by numerically solving the full Poisson equation, the Maxwell construction may effectively regain physical meaning for the hadron-quark deconfinement phase transition, even though a naive application of the Maxwell construction fails to satisfy charge chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors used density functional theory to model quark droplets embedded in hadron matter within Wigner-Seitz cells, imposing total charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium. They employed the bag model with a bag constant of 120 MeV/fm³ for the quark phase and an effective potential parametrized to reproduce nuclear matter saturation for the hadron phase, treating the surface tension as a free parameter. The equations of motion for constituent densities and the Coulomb potential were solved numerically, including the full Poisson equation without linearized approximations.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: B = 120 MeV/fm³, equal to that used in Heiselberg et al.
- Surface tension values treated as free parameters: σ = 40 MeV/fm² and σ = 60 MeV/fm² are shown in figures.
- Baryon chemical potential μ_B = 1232 MeV used for the density profile example and thermodynamic potential calculation.
- Volume fraction f = (R/R_W)³ = 0.01 used in the example density profiles.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analytical study by Voskresensky et al. (previous work), which used a linearized approximation for the Poisson equation to examine the Coulomb screening effect in the hadron-quark mixed phase. It directly addresses the claim by Glendenning that the Maxwell construction is inappropriate for the deconfinement phase transition due to multiple chemical potentials, and the finding by Heiselberg et al. that the mixed phase is energetically disfavored for large surface tension. The results suggest that the mixed phase region in neutron star cores may be narrow, and the authors note that further study of other geometrical structures (rod, slab, tube, bubble) is needed to complete the phase diagram.
