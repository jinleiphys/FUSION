# Testing the mechanism of QGP-induced energy loss

> We present an analytic model of jet quenching, based on the (D)GLV energy loss formalism, to describe the system size dependence of QGP-induced parton absorption in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Numerical simulations of the transverse momentum dependence of jet quenching are given for central Au+Au and Cu+Cu reactions. Low pT dijet correlations are shown to be sensitive to the reappearance of the lost energy as soft hadrons. At high pT we find that the attenuation of dihadrons is similar to that of single inclusive particles. Comparison to recent data from PHENIX and STAR is given as test of the jet quenching theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic model of jet quenching based on the (D)GLV energy loss formalism, claiming that the fractional energy loss scales as \(\epsilon \propto A^{2/3} \propto N_{\text{part}}^{2/3}\), but this scaling holds only in the limit of large parton energy \(2E/\mu^2 L \gg 1\) and under the assumption of (1+1)D Bjorken expansion.

## Method

The authors develop an analytic model combining power-law partonic cross sections with the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) energy loss formalism extended by Djordjevic et al. for heavy quarks. They compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) from the fractional energy loss \(\epsilon = \Delta E/E\) and its scaling with system size, then perform numerical simulations of single inclusive hadron production and dihadron correlations for central Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. The model includes initial-state multiple scattering, nuclear shadowing, and medium-induced gluon splitting, with the energy redistribution from lost energy into soft hadrons treated as a parameter-free prediction.

## Key numbers

- Fractional energy loss scales as \(\epsilon \propto A^{2/3} \propto N_{\text{part}}^{2/3}\) for systems from Be+Be to U+U at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is approximately \(p_T\) independent in the range \(5\ \text{GeV} < p_T < 20\ \text{GeV}\) for central Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.
- Effective gluon rapidity densities used in calculations: \(dN^g/dy = 1150\) for central Au+Au and \(dN^g/dy = 370\) for central Cu+Cu collisions.
- Ratio of single inclusive to double inclusive hadron suppression \(R_{AA}^{h_1} / R_{AA}^{h_1 h_2} \sim 1.5\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, and \(\sim 1.2 - 1.35\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62\) GeV.
- Nuclear shadowing effects are small, with \(|S(x,Q^2)-1| < 0.2\), and dynamical power corrections are negligible for \(p_T > 5\) GeV hadron production.

## Context

This work extends the GLV energy loss formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 1999) and its heavy quark extension by Djordjevic et al. (2003) to provide a systematic analytic description of system size dependence in jet quenching. It enables tomographic determination of QGP properties by comparing theoretical predictions to experimental data from PHENIX (Adler 2003, Shimomura 2005) and STAR (Adler 2002, Dunlop 2005, Magestro 2005). The agreement with data indicates the QGP created in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is not fully opaque, and the parameter-free prediction of energy redistribution into soft hadrons provides a testable signature for future dihadron correlation measurements.
