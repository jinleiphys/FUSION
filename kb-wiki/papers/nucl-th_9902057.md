# On the Backbending Mechanism of $^{48}$Cr

> The mechanism of backbending in $^{48}$Cr is investigated in terms of the Projected Shell Model and the Generator Coordinate Method. It is shown that both methods are reasonable shell model truncation schemes. These two quite different quantum mechanical approaches lead to a similar conclusion that the backbending is due to a band crossing involving an excited band which is built on simultaneously broken neutron and proton pairs in the ``intruder'' subshell $f_{7/2}$. It is pointed out that this type of band crossing is usually known to cause the second backbending in rare-earth nuclei.

## Key claim

The backbending in \(^{48}\)Cr is due to a band crossing involving an excited band built on simultaneously broken neutron and proton pairs in the \(f_{7/2}\) intruder subshell, rather than the usual crossing between the ground band and a two-quasiparticle band that causes the first backbending in rare-earth nuclei. The authors note that this type of band crossing is usually known to cause the second backbending in rare-earth nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Projected Shell Model (PSM) calculations using a Nilsson+BCS deformed quasiparticle basis at deformation \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.25\), with a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions. They also performed Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations using constrained Hartree-Fock states with the same Hamiltonian as the full \(pf\)-shell model, projecting onto good angular momentum and diagonalizing. Band diagrams were constructed to visualize band crossings before configuration mixing.

## Key numbers

- Deformation of \(^{48}\)Cr near ground state: \(\beta \approx 0.28\), corresponding to \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.25\), from experimental lifetime measurements.
- Monopole pairing strength: \(G_M = [22.5 - 18.0(N-Z)/A]/A\) for neutrons and \(G_M = 22.5/A\) for protons.
- Quadrupole pairing strength proportionality constant: fixed to 0.20.
- GCM calculation used 66 mesh points in the \(q\)-\(\gamma\) parameter space.
- Effective charges used for B(E2) calculations: 0.5\(e\) for neutrons and 1.5\(e\) for protons.
- Backbending occurs around spin 10 and 12, seen as a sudden drop in \(\gamma\)-ray energy \(E_\gamma = E(I) - E(I-2)\).
- The \(f_{7/2}\) intruder subshell in light nuclei limits maximal spin alignment to \(J=6\), compared to \(J=12\) in the \(i_{13/2}\) subshell of rare-earth nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the full \(pf\)-shell model calculation by the Strasbourg-Madrid collaboration (1997) and the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov description of \(^{48}\)Cr, providing a quantum-mechanical interpretation of the backbending mechanism. The authors contrast their conclusion with a recent paper by Sakata based on CHFB which claims the backbending is not due to level crossing. The PSM and GCM results enable a unified description where the backbending in light nuclei is analogous to the second backbending in rare-earth nuclei, and the GCM formalism offers a new way to simultaneously describe normally deformed and superdeformed bands.
