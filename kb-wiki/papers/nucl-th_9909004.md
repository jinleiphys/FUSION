# Strange Form Factors and Sum Rules of Baryon Decuplet

> Treating perturbatively the flavor symmetry breaking effects resided in the wave function as well as in the SU(3) Lagrangian, we calculate the strange form factors of decuplet baryons. The higher dimensional representation mixing components in the baryon wave function of the deccuplet baryons are found to yield the important contributions to the strange form factors, especially of the $Δ$ and $Σ^*$ baryons. The model independent sum rules among the baryon decuplet magnetic moments are also derived.

## Key claim

The strange form factors of decuplet baryons receive important contributions from higher-dimensional representation mixing in the baryon wave function, especially for the Δ and Σ* baryons, when flavor symmetry breaking is treated perturbatively in the chiral bag model.

## Method

The authors treat SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking perturbatively in the chiral bag model (CBM), including both explicit symmetry breaking in the Lagrangian (via meson mass and decay constant differences) and implicit symmetry breaking via higher-dimensional irreducible representation (IR) mixing in the baryon wave function. The baryon decuplet wave functions are expanded in the minimal multi-quark Fock space restricted to the 10, 27, and 35 representations, with mixing coefficients determined by first-order perturbation theory. Magnetic moments and strange form factors are then computed from the electromagnetic currents decomposed into u-, d-, and s-flavor components.

## Key numbers

- μ_Δ^{++} = 1.29 μ_p (CBM prediction) compared to experimental value μ_Δ^{++}^{exp} = (1.62 ± 0.18) μ_p
- μ_Ω^{-} = -1.75 n.m. (CBM prediction) compared to experimental value μ_Ω^{-}^{exp} = -1.94 ± 0.17 ± 0.14 n.m.
- Proton strange form factor F_{2p}^{(s)} = 0.30 n.m. (CBM prediction) compared to SAMPLE Collaboration result F_{2p}^{(s),exp} = 0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19 n.m.
- Bag radius R ~ 0.6 fm corresponding to magic angle θ(R) = π/2
- Inertia parameters used: M = 0.66, N = 6.00, N' = 0.52, P = 1.11, Q = 1.27, mI_2 = 3.96

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CBM calculations of baryon octet strange form factors (Hong and Park, 1998; Hong, Park, and Min, 1998) to the baryon decuplet. It addresses the lingering question of strangeness content in baryons raised by the EMC experiment, and the SAMPLE collaboration's measurement of the proton strange form factor. The model-independent sum rules derived for decuplet magnetic moments hold across multiple approaches including the nonrelativistic quark model, chiral quark soliton model (Kim, 1998), and chiral perturbation theory (Butler, 1993), and enable future comparisons with lattice gauge theory (Leinweber, 1991) and experimental measurements of decuplet magnetic moments.
