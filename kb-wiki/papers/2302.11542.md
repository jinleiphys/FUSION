# Unified tetraquark equations

> We derive covariant equations describing the tetraquark in terms of an admixture of two-body states $D\bar D$ (diquark-antidiquark), $MM$ (meson-meson), and three-body-like states $q\bar q (T_{q\bar q})$, $q q (T_{\bar q\bar q})$, and $\bar q\bar q (T_{qq})$ where two of the quarks are spectators while the other two are interacting (their t matrices denoted correspondingly as $T_{q\bar q}$, $T_{\bar q\bar q}$, and $T_{qq}$). This has been achieved by describing the $qq\bar q\bar q$ system using the Faddeev-like four-body equations of Khvedelidze and Kvinikhidze [Theor. Math. Phys. 90, 62 (1992)] while retaining all two-body interactions (in contrast to previous works where terms involving isolated two-quark scattering were neglected). As such, our formulation, is able to unify seemingly unrelated models of the tetraquark, like, for example, the $D\bar D$ model of the Moscow group [Faustov et al., Universe 7, 94 (2021)] and the coupled channel $D \bar D-MM$ model of the Giessen group [Heupel et al., Phys. Lett. B718, 545 (2012)].

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified set of covariant tetraquark equations that include all two-body interactions in the four-body kernel, in contrast to previous works that neglected terms involving isolated two-quark scattering; this unification encompasses both the diquark-antidiquark model of the Moscow group and the coupled-channel meson-meson/diquark-antidiquark model of the Giessen group as special limiting cases.

## Method

The authors start from the Faddeev-like four-body equations of Khvedelidze and Kvinikhidze (1992) for the \(qq\bar q\bar q\) system with distinguishable quarks, retaining all pairwise interactions. They then antisymmetrize for two identical quarks and two identical antiquarks, and introduce separable approximations for the two-body t matrices in the product term \(T_a T_{a'}\) to expose three-body-like states where two quarks are spectators while the other two interact. The resulting coupled-channel equations incorporate the previously neglected \(q\bar q(T_{q\bar q})\), \(qq(T_{\bar q\bar q})\), and \(\bar q\bar q(T_{qq})\) contributions alongside the standard meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark channels.

## Key numbers

- The four-body kernel \(K^d\) is expressed as a sum of three terms \(K^d_1, K^d_2, K^d_3\) corresponding to the three possible two-pair partitions \((13,24)\), \((14,23)\), and \((12,34)\).
- The t matrix for each partition is given by \(T^d_\A = T^d_a + T^d_{a'} + T^d_a T^d_{a'}\), where the first two terms (isolated two-body t matrices) were neglected in the Giessen group's derivation (Heupel et al., 2012).
- The bound state form factor \(\Phi\) for identical quarks satisfies \(\Phi = \frac{1}{4} K G_0^{(4)} \Phi\), with the factor \(1/4\) arising from combinatorial factors of \(1/2\) each for identical quarks and identical antiquarks.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-like four-body formalism of Khvedelidze and Kvinikhidze (1992) to the tetraquark problem, and directly addresses the discrepancy between the Moscow group's diquark-antidiquark model (Faustov et al., 2021) and the Giessen group's coupled-channel model (Heupel et al., 2012). By retaining all two-body interaction terms that the Giessen group neglected, the unified equations enable a more complete assessment of the relative roles of diquark-antidiquark and meson-meson components in tetraquark structure, and can be applied to future calculations of heavy tetraquark masses and compositions.
