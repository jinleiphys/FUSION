# rho-rho-N and rho-rho-Delta molecules with $J^P=5/2^{+}$ and $J^P=7/2^{+}$

> The rho-rho-N and rho-rho-Delta three-body systems have been studied within the framework of the fixed center approximation of Faddeev equation. The rho-rho interaction in isospin I=0, spin S=2 is strongly attractive, and so are the N-rho, Delta-rho interactions. This leads to bound states of both rho-rho-N and rho-rho-Delta. We find peaks of the modulus squared of the scattering matrix around 2227 MeV for rho-rho-N, and 2372 MeV for rho-rho-Delta. Yet, the strength of the peak for the rho-rho-N amplitude is much smaller than for rho-rho-Delta, weakening the case for a rho-rho-N bound state, or a dominant rho-rho-N component. A discussion is made on how these states can be searched for in present programs looking for multimeson final states in different reactions.

## Key claim
The paper finds peaks in the modulus squared of the scattering matrix around 2227 MeV for the rho-rho-N system and 2372 MeV for the rho-rho-Delta system, but the strength of the peak for the rho-rho-N amplitude is much smaller than for rho-rho-Delta, weakening the case for a rho-rho-N bound state or a dominant rho-rho-N component.

## Method
The three-body systems were studied within the fixed center approximation (FCA) of the Faddeev equation, where two rho mesons are assumed to form a strongly bound cluster (the f2(1270) resonance) and the third particle (N or Delta) interacts with the cluster via multiple scattering. The rho-rho interaction in isospin I=0, spin S=2 is strongly attractive and leads to the f2(1270) bound state, and the N-rho and Delta-rho interactions are taken from unitarized amplitudes in coupled channels using the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The width of the f2(1270) and the decay widths of the vector mesons and decuplet baryons are incorporated via convolution integrals over their mass distributions.

## Key numbers
- Peak in |T|^2 for the rho-rho-N system around 2227 MeV with a width of 100 MeV (using cutoff Lambda = 875 MeV).
- Peak in |T|^2 for the rho-rho-Delta system around 2372 MeV with a width of about 25 MeV (after convolution for the f2(1270) width).
- The strength of |T|^2 at the peak for the rho-rho-Delta system is about 200 times bigger than for the rho-rho-N system; the integrated strength is about 40 times bigger.
- The f2(1270) resonance has a decay width of 157 MeV.
- The cutoff parameter Lambda used for the rho-rho interaction is 875 MeV.

## Context
This work extends studies of two-meson-one-baryon systems that have successfully described low-lying baryonic resonances, such as the chiral unitary approach for J^P=1/2^- states (Mag05) and J^P=3/2^- states (Lutz, Sarkar), and earlier Faddeev calculations for the pi-rho-Delta system (Xie and Pedro) that explained a Delta(1/2^+) state around 1750 MeV. The results suggest that the Delta(2390)(7/2^+) observed in two independent experiments (Cutkosky, Hoehler) with masses of 2350 +/- 100 MeV and 2425 +/- 60 MeV could correspond to the rho-rho-Delta bound state found here, though the narrow width of about 25 MeV from this calculation is not compatible with the experimental width of around 300 +/- 100 MeV. The work enables experimental searches for these states in multimeson final states at facilities such as Hall D of Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and LEPS.
