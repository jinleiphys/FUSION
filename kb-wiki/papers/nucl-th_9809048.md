# Relativistic current densities for bound spin-orbit partners and the longitudinal-transverse response in (e,e'p) processes

> Scalar, baryon and vector--current densities in coordinate and momentum space are calculated within a relativistic mean--field model. The role of the low components of the bound nucleon wave function is investigated in detail for different spin--orbit partner shells. We show that the relative importance of the negative--energy projection components can be explained from the different quantum numbers involved in the relativistic wave function. This fact has proved to be important in the analysis of various electron scattering observables even for low--medium values of the bound nucleon momentum.

# Relativistic current densities for bound spin-orbit partners and the longitudinal-transverse response in (e,e'p) processes

## Key claim

The large deviations (up to a factor of three) in the longitudinal-transverse (TL) response function predictions produced by different current operators and gauges occur specifically for the jack-knifed states (p1/2, d3/2) but not for the stretched states (p3/2, d5/2), a genuine relativistic effect stemming from the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of the bound nucleon wave function.

## Method

Scalar, baryon, and vector-current densities in coordinate and momentum space were calculated within a relativistic mean-field model (Walecka model with S-V potentials) using the TIMORA code with parameters from Horowitz and Serot (1981). The longitudinal-transverse response function R^TL for one-nucleon knockout was computed in the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) for spin-orbit partner shells (p1/2 and p3/2 in 16O; d3/2 and d5/2 in 40Ca) using three different gauges (Landau, Coulomb, Weyl) and two current operators (CC1 and CC2 following de Forest's notation), with the response separated into positive energy projection, negative energy projection, and crossed term contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the p1/2 shell in 16O: ratio R^TL(CC1(0))/R^TL(CC2(0)) is approximately 1.5, and R^TL(CC1(3))/R^TL(CC2(3)) is approximately 2, with the ratio between CC1(3) and CC2(0) results reaching a factor of 3
- For the p3/2 shell in 16O: the ratio between CC1(3) and CC2(0) results reaches at most 1.2, with most cases showing less than 10% variation
- Kinematics used: quasielastic conditions with momentum transfer q = 500 MeV/c and energy transfer omega = 131.56 MeV
- For stretched states the theoretical uncertainty is at most 20% (largest ratio between CC1(3) and CC2(0) calculations), compared to up to 300% for jack-knifed states
- The contribution of the lower component f_kappa to the normalization of the baryon density is less than 3% in all cases considered

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Caballero et al., 1998) which investigated negative energy projections (NEP) of relativistic bound nucleon wave functions in (e,e'p) responses, focusing on the p1/2 shell in 16O where variations of up to a factor of three were found in R^TL. The present paper examines whether this large effect is general or particular to the p1/2 shell by extending the analysis to p3/2, d3/2, and d5/2 orbitals. The work is motivated by the success of relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations in interpreting (e,e'p) data (Udias et al., 1993, 1995; Jin et al., 1992) and addresses controversy surrounding TL responses from exclusive quasielastic experiments. The results enable identification of which nuclear orbitals are most sensitive to relativistic effects and current operator ambiguities, with implications for future RDWIA calculations where final-state interactions are expected to differ by at most 4% between spin-orbit partners at the TL response maximum (as noted in progress reports by the authors).
