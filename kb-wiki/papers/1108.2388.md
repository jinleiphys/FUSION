# Collisional Processes in Supersymmetric Plasma

> Collisional processes in ultrarelativistic N=1 SUSY QED plasma are studied and compared to those in an electromagnetic plasma of electrons, positrons and photons. Cross sections of all binary interactions which occur in the supersymmetric plasma at the order of e^4 are computed. Some processes, in particular the Compton scattering on selectrons, appear to be independent of momentum transfer and thus they are qualitatively different from processes in an electromagnetic plasma. It suggests that transport properties of the SUSY plasma are different than those of its non-supersymmetric counterpart. Energy loss and momentum broadening of a particle traversing the supersymmetric plasma are discussed in detail and the characteristics are shown to be surprisingly similar to those of QED plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that while the differential cross sections of binary processes in N=1 SUSY QED plasma are qualitatively different from those in QED plasma, with some processes being independent of momentum transfer, the resulting energy loss and momentum broadening of a test particle are surprisingly similar to those in ordinary electromagnetic plasma, up to logarithmic corrections.

## Method

The authors computed all binary interaction cross sections in ultrarelativistic N=1 SUSY QED plasma at order e^4 (alpha^2), using the Mandelstam invariants s, t, u. They then derived the collisional energy loss and transverse momentum broadening (hat q) of a high-energy selectron traversing an equilibrium plasma, considering only scattering on photons as a demonstration, and performed the phase-space integrals analytically using the Bose-Einstein distribution for plasma photons.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss of a high-energy selectron (E >> T) in SUSY QED plasma: dE/dx = -e^4 / (2^5 * 3 * pi) * T^2
- Energy loss of an energetic muon in QED plasma (for comparison): dE/dx = -e^4 / (48 pi^3) * T^2 * (ln(E/(eT)) + 2.031)
- Momentum broadening of a high-energy selectron in SUSY QED plasma: hat q = e^4 / (12 pi^3) * T^3 * [zeta(3) + (pi^4/45) * (T/E)], where zeta(3) ≈ 1.202
- The cross section for Compton scattering on selectrons (process No. 25): d sigma / dt = 4 pi alpha^2 / s^2, independent of momentum transfer
- The cross section for photino-electron scattering (process No. 6): d sigma / dt = 4 pi alpha^2 / s^2, independent of momentum transfer

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of collective excitations in N=1 SUSY QED plasma (Czajka and Mrowczynski, 2010), which found surprising similarity to QED plasma. It contrasts with the expectation that the qualitatively different cross sections (e.g., isotropic Compton scattering on selectrons versus the small-angle-dominated Compton scattering on electrons) would lead to different transport properties. The results enable comparison with strongly coupled theories studied via AdS/CFT duality (Aharony, 1999; Son, 2007; Janik, 2010) and with earlier weak-coupling studies of supersymmetric plasmas (Caron-Huot, 2006; Huot, 2006; Blaizot, 2006; Chesler, 2006, 2009). The energy loss result is compared to the QED plasma calculation by Braaten (1991).
