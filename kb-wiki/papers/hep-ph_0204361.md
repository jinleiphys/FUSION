# Elliptic flow of colored glass in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We compute the elliptic flow generated by classical gluon fields in a high energy nuclear collision. The classical gluon fields are described by a typical momentum scale, the saturation scale Lambda_s, which is, for RHIC energies, of the order of 1-2 GeV. A significant elliptic flow is generated only over time scales on the order of the system size R. The flow is dominated by soft modes $p_{T} \sim Lambda_s / 4$ which linearize at very late times $tau \sim R \gg 1/Lambda_s$. We discuss the implications of our result for the theoretical interpretation of the RHIC data.

## Key claim

Classical gluon fields described by the Color Glass Condensate generate a significant elliptic flow only over time scales on the order of the system size R, much longer than the characteristic time 1/Lambda_s, and the flow is dominated by soft modes with p_T approximately Lambda_s/4; the classical contribution to v_2 contributes only about 50% of the measured v_2 for various centralities at RHIC.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions with boost invariance, modeling a gold nucleus as a sphere of radius R filled with randomly distributed nucleons. They computed the momentum anisotropy parameter alpha and the elliptic flow parameter v_2 from the classical gauge fields by two methods: direct Coulomb gauge computation and a cooling (relaxation) method, for peripheral collisions at impact parameter b/2R = 0.5.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Lambda_s for RHIC energies is of order 1-2 GeV.
- For a gold nucleus, the nuclear radius R is approximately 6.5 fm.
- The momentum anisotropy parameter alpha saturates at about 1% at a proper time on the order of the system size R.
- The elliptic flow v_2 is peaked at transverse momentum p_T approximately Lambda_s/4.
- The classical contribution to v_2 contributes only about 50% of the measured v_2 for various centralities.
- Results were computed for values of Lambda_{s0}R = 18.5, 37, and 74, spanning the RHIC-LHC energy range.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the numerical gluon production calculations of Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2001) to compute elliptic flow in non-central collisions. The results show that classical fields alone cannot fully explain the RHIC elliptic flow data from the STAR collaboration (2001), suggesting that additional contributions from hard mode scattering and possibly hydrodynamic evolution are needed, as discussed in the context of partonic transport models by Molnar and Gyulassy (2001) and hydrodynamic calculations by Kolb, Huovinen, Heinz, and others (2001).
