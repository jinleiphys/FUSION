# Composition of fluctuations of different observables

> We demonstrate that description of fluctuations observed in multiparticle production processes using Tsallis statistics approach (in which fluctuations are described by the nonextensivity parameter q) leads to a specific sum rule for parameters q seen in different observables which can be verified experimentally.

# Composition of fluctuations of different observables

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that fluctuations in multiparticle production processes described by Tsallis statistics lead to a specific sum rule connecting the nonextensivity parameter q obtained from different observables (multiplicity distributions, rapidity distributions, and transverse mass distributions), which can be verified experimentally. The sum rule explains why q values extracted from different distributions in nuclear collisions differ from each other and from those seen in proton-antiproton collisions.

## Method
The authors analyze experimental data from NA49 on Pb+Pb collisions at various energies, fitting transverse mass distributions dN/dμ_T and rapidity distributions dN/dy using the Tsallis distribution h_q(E). They derive a sum rule by decomposing fluctuations of the variable z = μ_T/T (used in rapidity fits) into contributions from fluctuations of transverse mass μ_T and fluctuations of temperature T, using the relation Var(z)/⟨z⟩^2 = Var(μ_T)/⟨μ_T⟩^2 + Var(T)/⟨T⟩^2. The parameter q from multiplicity distributions P(N) (fitted to negative binomial form) is compared with q values from rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, with corrections applied for fluctuations of μ_T.

## Key numbers
- For p-pbar collisions at 200, 540, and 900 GeV, fits to p_T spectra give (T_T [GeV]; q_T) = (0.134;1.095), (0.135;1.105), and (0.14;1.11) respectively
- For p-pbar collisions at the same energies, fits to rapidity distributions give (T_L; q_L) = (11.74;1.2), (20.39;1.26), and (30.79;1.29)
- The relation q_L - 1 = -0.104 + 0.058 ln sqrt(s) describes the energy dependence of q from rapidity distributions in p-pbar collisions
- For Pb+Pb collisions, q from multiplicity distributions follows q - 1 = (1/(a N_P))(1 - N_P/A) with a = 0.98, where N_P is number of projectile participants and A is mass number
- For p-pbar at 540 GeV, the correlation between T and q in rapidity fits gives T(q)/μ_T = 56.5 - 14.5 q, with Δq = 0.22 between the corrected and uncorrected values

## Context
This work extends the Tsallis statistics approach to multiparticle production previously applied by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000) to describe temperature fluctuations via a diffusion model, and by the PHENIX Collaboration (2004) and CMS Collaboration at LHC. The paper addresses the discrepancy between q values extracted from different observables in nuclear collisions compared to proton-antiproton collisions, where earlier work by the authors (compq) had found q_L - 1 matched 1/k from negative binomial fits. The sum rule enables future experimental verification using complete datasets from LHC experiments, particularly ALICE, and clarifies why fluctuations in multiplicity distributions appear smaller than those in rapidity or transverse momentum distributions for heavy-ion collisions.
