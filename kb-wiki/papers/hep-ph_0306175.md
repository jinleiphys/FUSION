# Final state interactions in nonleptonic hyperon decays

> We investigate the importance of final state interactions in weak nonleptonic hyperon decays within a relativistic chiral unitary approach based on coupled channels. The effective potentials for meson-baryon scattering are derived from a chiral effective Lagrangian and iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation, which generates the low lying baryon resonances dynamically. The inclusion of final state interactions decreases the discrepancy between theory and experiment for both s and p waves. Our study indicates that contributions from higher order terms of the weak effective Lagrangian may play an important role in these decays.

## Key claim

Final state interactions in nonleptonic hyperon decays produce sizeable effects (much larger than the anticipated 10% level), but even after including them via a relativistic chiral unitary approach, a satisfactory simultaneous fit to both s and p waves is not achieved, indicating that contributions from higher order terms of the weak effective Lagrangian may play an important role.

## Method

The authors derived effective potentials for meson-baryon scattering from a chiral effective Lagrangian at leading and next-to-leading order, then iterated these potentials in a Bethe-Salpeter equation to generate baryon resonances dynamically. The strong interaction parameters were constrained by fitting to experimental pion-nucleon phase shifts, after which the two unknown weak low-energy constants (d and f) were fitted to the decay observables (widths, asymmetry parameters, and phases) of the seven nonleptonic hyperon decays.

## Key numbers

- Weak couplings from the fit including final state interactions: d = 0.31 × 10⁻⁷ GeV, f = -0.38 × 10⁻⁷ GeV
- Tree-level fit to s waves only gave d = 0.17 × 10⁻⁷ GeV, f = -0.4 × 10⁻⁷ GeV
- For Λ → p π⁻: experimental width 1.597 ± 0.012 μeV, α = 0.642 ± 0.013, φ = -6.5° ± 3.5°; fit gave Γ = 1.70 μeV, α = 0.28, φ = -1.0°
- For Σ⁺ → n π⁺: experimental width 3.966 ± 0.013 μeV, α = 0.068 ± 0.013, φ = 167° ± 20°; fit gave Γ = 0.82 μeV, α = -0.86, φ = 59.2°
- For Ξ⁻ → Λ π⁻: experimental width 4.016 ± 0.037 μeV, α = -0.456 ± 0.014, φ = 4° ± 4°; fit gave Γ = 4.61 μeV, α = -0.27, φ = 1.76°
- Strong interaction parameters used: b_D = 0.066 GeV⁻¹, b_F = -0.185 GeV⁻¹, b_0 = -0.254 GeV⁻¹, regularization scale μ = 1.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory studies of nonleptonic hyperon decays by Jenkins (1992) and Borasoy and Holstein (1999), which could not simultaneously fit s and p waves without introducing many unknown low-energy constants. The authors use a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to meson-baryon scattering by Kaiser, Weise, and collaborators, and to radiative φ decays by Oller (2001). The results show that final state interactions alone cannot resolve the long-standing s-p wave puzzle, suggesting that higher-order weak counterterms (beyond the leading d and f couplings) are necessary, enabling future work to incorporate such terms systematically.
