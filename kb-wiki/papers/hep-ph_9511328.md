# Need for the intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling in multipion production processes

> We give reasons in support of the use of an effective intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling Hamiltonian for describing the properties of the pion multiplicity distribution and the corresponding factorial moments within the thermal-density matrix approach.We explain the appearance of the negative-binomial (NB) distribution for pions and the well-known empi- rical relation of Wroblewski.Our model Hamiltonian is written as a linear combination of the generators of the SU(1,1) group.We find the generating function for the pion multiplicity distribution at finite temperature T and discuss the properties of the second-order factorial moment.Also, we show that an intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling generates the squeezed states of the pion field. At T=0, these squeezed states become an inherent property of the NB distribution.

# Need for the intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling in multipion production processes

## Key claim

The authors argue that an effective intensity-dependent pion-nucleon coupling Hamiltonian, with SU(1,1) dynamical symmetry, can explain the negative-binomial (NB) multiplicity distribution for pions and the empirical Wroblewski relation, but only if the coupling constant increases linearly with the average pion multiplicity (or temperature), making the coupling energy-dependent rather than a fixed parameter.

## Method

The authors model the pion field using a thermal-density operator approach, where the effective Hamiltonian is written as a linear combination of SU(1,1) group generators via the Holstein-Primakoff realization. They compute the generating function for the pion multiplicity distribution at finite temperature T by applying a unitary S-matrix transformation to the free-pion thermal state, and derive expressions for the average multiplicity, dispersion, and second-order factorial moment. The squeezing properties of the pion field are analyzed using Hermitian quadrature operators at T=0.

## Key numbers

- The average number of thermal pions is given by $\bar{n}_{T} = 1/(e^{\beta \omega} - 1)$.
- For the standard linear coupling Hamiltonian, the two-pion correlation function is $g^{(2)} = 2 - (\gamma/(1+\gamma))^2$, where $\gamma = \bar{n}_C/\bar{n}_T$.
- At T=0, the NB distribution shape parameter is $2\lambda$, with $\lambda \ge 3/2$ required for consistency with data.
- The coefficient $A_2(T)$ in the dispersion relation $D^2 = A_0(T) + A_1(T)\langle n \rangle + A_2(T)\langle n \rangle^2$ is given by equation (48); data on negatively charged particles in pp collisions suggest $A_2(T) = 1/3$, which restricts $\bar{n}_T < 1$ for all $\lambda \ge 3/2$.
- At T=0, the Wroblewski relation gives $A = (2\lambda)^{-1/2}$ and $B = (\lambda/2)^{1/2}$ for the coefficients in $d(\theta) \approx A\bar{n}(\theta) + B$ when $\bar{n}(\theta) \gg 1$.
- At T=0, the dispersion relation for the NB distribution is $D^2/\langle n \rangle^2 = 1/(2\lambda) + 1/\bar{n}(\theta)$.
- For $T \to \infty$, $D^2/\langle n \rangle^2 = 1 + \text{th}^2(2\theta)$.

## Context

This work extends the thermal-density matrix approach to multipion production, addressing the failure of the standard linear pion-nucleon coupling Hamiltonian (which produces only displaced thermal states) to reproduce the Wroblewski relation. The authors build on the observation that the NB distribution describes pp and p-pbar collision data from 22 to 900 GeV (as reported by the UA5 collaboration and others), and that KNO scaling is violated at higher energies. The model provides an alternative to the clan-structure interpretation of the NB distribution (Giovannini and Van Hove, 1986) and offers a dynamical mechanism for generating squeezed states of the pion field, which could be extended to include two-pion coupling terms as studied for photon fields (Kim and others, 1989).
