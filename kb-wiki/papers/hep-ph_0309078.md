# Analytic structure of the gluon and quark propagators in Landau gauge QCD

> In Landau gauge QCD the infrared behavior of the propagator of transverse gluons can be analytically determined to be a power law from Dyson-Schwinger equations. This propagator clearly shows positivity violation, indicating the absence of the transverse gluons from the physical spectrum, i.e. gluon confinement. A simple analytic structure for the gluon propagator is proposed capturing all important features. We provide arguments that the Landau gauge quark propagator possesses a singularity on the real timelike axis. For this propagator we find a positive definite Schwinger function.

## Key claim

The Landau gauge gluon propagator violates reflection positivity (indicating gluon confinement), while the quark propagator yields a positive definite Schwinger function, suggesting that quark confinement is not manifest at the level of the propagator. The authors qualify that the quark propagator's dominant singularity lies on or very close to the real timelike axis at a scale of 350 to 500 MeV, but the exact nature of this singularity and sub-dominant structures cannot be reliably determined with current methods.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the ghost, gluon, and quark propagators in Landau gauge QCD using a self-consistent truncation scheme that neglects the four-gluon interaction and employs ansatze for the ghost-gluon, quark-gluon, and three-gluon vertices. For the quark-gluon vertex, the non-Abelian part was factored out and the Curtis-Pennington vertex was used, which includes a term proportional to the sum of quark momenta that enhances dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The resulting propagators were tested against Osterwalder-Schrader reflection positivity by computing the Schwinger function via Fourier transformation.

## Key numbers

- The infrared exponent for the gluon and ghost dressing functions is κ = (93 - √1201)/98 ≈ 0.595 in the specific truncation used.
- The infrared fixed point for the running coupling is α_S(0) ≈ 2.972.
- The gluon Schwinger function Δ_g(t) has a zero at t ≈ 5 GeV^{-1} ≈ 1 fm and becomes negative for larger Euclidean times.
- The fit to the gluon renormalization function uses Λ_QCD = 510 MeV and a normalization parameter w = 2.5.
- The dominant singularity of the quark propagator occurs at a scale m ∼ 350 to 500 MeV, with the lower estimate from lattice data and the upper estimate from DSE solutions.

## Context

This work extends previous DSE studies by von Smekal (1997) and Mandula (1998) on gluon positivity violation, and provides an analytic parameterization of the gluon propagator that satisfies axioms of local quantum field theory except positivity. The results confirm the Kugo-Ojima confinement scenario (Kugo 1979) by showing that transverse gluons are not in the physical spectrum, while quarks may be confined through a different mechanism not visible at the propagator level. The analytic fit for the gluon propagator and the analysis of quark propagator singularities enable future direct solution of DSEs over the complex momentum plane to determine the detailed analytic structure of the quark propagator.
