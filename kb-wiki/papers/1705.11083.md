# From bare to renormalized order parameter in gauge space: structure and reactions

> The physical reason why one can calculate with similar accuracy, as compared to the experimental data, the absolute cross section associated with two-nucleon transfer processes between members of pairing rotational bands, making use of simple BCS (constant matrix elements) or of many-body (Nambu-Gorkov (NG), nuclear field theory (NFT)) spectroscopic amplitudes, is not immediately obvious. Restoration of spontaneous symmetry breaking and associated emergent generalised rigidity in gauge space provides the answer, and points to a new emergence: a physical sum rule resulting from the intertwining of structure and reaction processes and closely connected with the central role induced pairing interaction plays in structure together with the fact that successive transfer dominates Cooper pair tunnelling.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the absolute cross sections for two-nucleon transfer reactions between members of pairing rotational bands can be calculated with similar accuracy using either simple BCS (constant matrix elements) or many-body (Nambu-Gorkov, nuclear field theory) spectroscopic amplitudes because restoration of spontaneous symmetry breaking and emergent generalized rigidity in gauge space leads to a physical sum rule, with the caveat that this equivalence relies on the fact that successive transfer dominates Cooper pair tunnelling and that the induced pairing interaction plays a central role in structure.

## Method

The authors employ nuclear field theory (NFT) within the Nambu-Gorkov framework, applying it to superfluid nuclei such as the Sn isotopes. They calculate the order parameter \(\alpha_0\) (the number of Cooper pairs in the condensate) from BCS occupation amplitudes, then relate it to the density of neutron levels at the Fermi energy and to the induced pairing interaction mediated by particle-vibration coupling. The analysis connects these structure quantities to two-nucleon transfer cross sections through the relation \(\sigma \sim |\alpha_0|^2\), and estimates the Cooper pair correlation length \(\xi_{ind}\) to demonstrate that successive transfer fully probes nuclear pair correlations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{120}\)Sn, the order parameter \(\alpha_0 = 5-6\) from detailed microscopic calculations (compared to a single-\(j\) shell estimate of 8).
- The induced pairing gap \(\Delta_{ind} \approx g_{pv} \alpha_0 = \Delta_{exp}/2 \approx 0.8\) MeV for superfluid Sn isotopes.
- The mass enhancement factor \(\lambda = g_{pv} N(0) \approx 0.4\), leading to an effective mass \(m_{\omega} = m(1+\lambda) \approx 1.4 m\).
- The density of neutron levels at the Fermi energy for one spin orientation in \(^{120}\)Sn is \(N(0) \approx 4\) MeV\(^{-1}\), obtained from \(N(0) \approx \frac{1}{2} \alpha_0\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- The induced pairing correlation length \(\xi_{ind} \approx 24\) fm, using \(v_F/c = 0.3\), \(\lambda = 0.4\), and \(\alpha_0 = 8\).
- The generalized pair quantality parameter \(q_{\xi_{ind}} \approx 0.04\), indicating strong correlation of Cooper pair partners.
- For \(^{120}\)Sn, the two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n} = 15.6\) MeV, while the one-neutron separation energy \(S_{1n} = 9.1\) MeV, giving \((2\times S_{1n}) - S_{2n} = 2.6\) MeV.
- The low-lying quadrupole vibrational state of \(^{120}\)Sn has a \(B(E2)\) value of 890 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\) from SLy4 QRPA, renormalized to 2150 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\) in NFT, compared to the experimental value of 2030 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the broken-symmetry restoration paradigm developed by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Bes and Broglia (1966) for pairing rotations in nuclei, and builds on the nuclear field theory framework previously applied to two-nucleon transfer reactions by Potel et al. (2013). The paper directly addresses the puzzle raised by earlier calculations (Idini, 2015) that simple BCS and many-body Nambu-Gorkov approaches yield similar absolute cross sections for two-nucleon transfer, providing a physical explanation based on the dominance of successive transfer and the role of the induced pairing interaction. The results enable a unified treatment of structure and reactions in superfluid nuclei, with implications for understanding Cooper pair tunnelling in finite quantum systems and for interpreting transfer reaction data in terms of the nuclear superfluid order parameter.
