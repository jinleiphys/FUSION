# Pairing in Asymmetric Many-Fermion Systems: Functional Renormalisation Group Approach

> Functional renormalisation group approach is applied to a imbalanced many- fermion system with a short-range attractive force. Composite boson field is introduced to describe the pairing between different flavour fermions. A set of approximate flow equations for the effective couplings is derived and solved. We identify the critical values of mass and particle number density mismatch when the system undergoes a phase transition to a normal state and determine the phase diagram both at unitary regime and nearby.

# Pairing in Asymmetric Many-Fermion Systems: Functional Renormalisation Group Approach

## Key claim
The functional renormalisation group (FRG) approach, using a simple ansatz for the effective action expanded to third order in the boson field bilinears, predicts that the phase transition from superfluid to normal state in imbalanced many-fermion systems is of first order at zero temperature, and that the Sarma phase (gapless excitations) never occurs under the conditions studied. The quantitative agreement with experimental data is reasonable but the theoretical critical line lies systematically above the experimental fit, indicating room for improvement in the truncation.

## Method
The authors apply the FRG formalism to a two-flavour fermion system with short-range attractive interactions, introducing a composite boson field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to describe pairing. They adopt a simple ansatz for the effective action with running couplings, expand the effective potential near its minimum up to order ρ³ (where ρ = φ†φ), and derive approximate flow equations for the effective couplings including both bosonic and fermionic fluctuations. The flow is evolved at fixed particle densities with running chemical potentials, using regulators of the Litim type for both bosons and fermions, with parameters relevant to ultracold atomic gases and nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential mismatch for the unitary regime: δμ_crit = 0.68 μ, above which the minimum of the effective potential drops to zero, indicating a first-order phase transition.
- For neutron matter parameters (scattering length a_nn ≈ -18.6 fm), the critical chemical potential mismatch is δμ_c = 0.33 μ, compared to QMC-based results of δμ_c = 0.27 μ.
- Superfluid gap at unitarity (1/p_F a = 0) for small density imbalance α = 0.03: calculated Δ = 0.55 (dimensionless), experimental Δ = 0.44.
- Superfluid gap at 1/p_F a = -0.25 for α = 0.03: calculated Δ = 0.27, experimental Δ = 0.22.
- Higher-order couplings contribute corrections at the level of 18-20 percent.
- The critical scale for the transition to the broken phase is k_cr ≈ (p₁ + p₂)/2, and is practically insensitive to the starting scale when chosen above 10 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends the FRG approach previously applied to balanced many-fermion systems (Krippa, 2013) to the imbalanced case, addressing limitations of mean-field treatments that are unreliable for strongly interacting fermions. The Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit (Clogston and Chandrasekhar, 1962) gave δμ = 0.71 Δ₀ for the BCS limit, while this work finds δμ_crit = 0.68 μ at unitarity. The results contradict the possibility of a Sarma phase at zero temperature, consistent with the interpretation of the system as an inhomogeneous mixture of gapped and normal phases (Bedaque et al., 2003). The phase diagram is compared to experimental data from Shin et al. (2008), and the calculations suggest that including particle-hole channel corrections (Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov corrections) as done for balanced systems by Diehl et al. (2007) would bring theory closer to experiment. The formalism enables future studies of combined mass/density asymmetry including the LOFF phase.
