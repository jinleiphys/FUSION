# Triangularity and Dipole Asymmetry in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We introduce a cumulant expansion to parameterize possible initial conditions in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We show that the cumulant expansion converges and that it can systematically reproduce the results of Glauber type initial conditions. At third order in the gradient expansion, the cumulants characterize the triangularity $<r^3 \cos3(ϕ- ψ_{3,3})>$ and the dipole asymmetry $<r^3 \cos(ϕ- ψ_{1,3})>$ of the initial entropy distribution. We show that for mid-peripheral collisions the orientation angle of the dipole asymmetry $ψ_{1,3}$ has a $20%$ preference out of plane. This leads to a small net $v_1$ out of plane. In peripheral and mid-central collisions the orientation angles $ψ_{1,3}$ and $ψ_{3,3}$ are strongly correlated. We study the ideal hydrodynamic response to these cumulants and determine the associated $v_1/ε_1$ and $v_3/ε_3$ for a massless ideal gas equation of state. $v_1$ and $v_3$ develop towards the edge of the nucleus, and consequently the final spectra are more sensitive to the viscous dynamics of freezeout. The hydrodynamic calculations for $v_3$ are compared to Alver and Roland fit of two particle correlation functions. Finally, we propose to measure the $v_1$ associated with the dipole asymmetry and the correlations between $ψ_{1,3}$ and $ψ_{3,3}$ by measuring a two particle correlation with respect to the participant plane, $<\cos(ϕ_a - 3ϕ_b + 2Ψ_{PP})>$. The hydrodynamic prediction for this correlation function is several times larger than a correlation currently measured by the STAR collaboration, $<\cos(ϕ_a + ϕ_\b - 2Ψ_{PP})>$.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a cumulant expansion to parameterize fluctuating initial conditions in relativistic heavy ion collisions, showing that at third order in the gradient expansion the dipole asymmetry and triangularity characterize the initial entropy distribution, and that for mid-peripheral collisions the dipole asymmetry orientation angle has a 20% preference out of plane, leading to a small net directed flow out of plane.

## Method

The authors developed a cumulant expansion of the Fourier transform of the initial entropy density distribution, expanding to third order in the gradient expansion to characterize the dipole asymmetry (parameterized by `<r^3 cos(phi - psi_{1,3})>`) and triangularity (parameterized by `<r^3 cos3(phi - psi_{3,3})>`). They used 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics with a massless ideal gas equation of state to compute the linear hydrodynamic response to these cumulants, and estimated the magnitudes and correlations of the cumulants using the PHOBOS Monte Carlo Glauber code.

## Key numbers

- The dipole asymmetry orientation angle psi_{1,3} has a 20% preference out of plane for mid-peripheral collisions
- The dipole asymmetry epsilon_1 is about a factor of two smaller than the triangularity epsilon_3 across the range of participant numbers studied
- The conditional probability distribution fit parameter C = 0.53 for impact parameter b = 7.6 fm and C = 0.56 for b = 10.5 fm
- The hydrodynamic prediction for the correlation function <cos(phi_alpha - 3phi_beta + 2Psi_{PP})> is several times larger than the STAR correlation <cos(phi_alpha + phi_beta - 2Psi_{PP})>

## Context

This work extends the approach of Alver and Roland (2010), who provided the first compelling explanation of two-particle correlation features (the "ridge" and "shoulder") as arising from collective response to fluctuating initial conditions, and builds on event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations by Takahashi et al. (2009) that showed flow from fluctuating initial conditions can describe two-particle correlations. The paper enables a systematic framework where linear response to specified cumulants can be calculated with hydrodynamics and combined with Glauber model event-by-event cumulants for comparison to data, and proposes specific experimental measurements to test the geometric interpretation of two-particle correlations at RHIC.
