# Deep crustal heating for realistic compositions of thermonuclear ashes

> The deep crustal heating, associated with exothermal nuclear reactions, is believed to be a key parameter for describing the thermal evolution of accreting neutron stars. In this paper, we present the first thermodynamically consistent calculations of the crustal heating for realistic compositions of thermonuclear ashes. In contrast to previous studies based on the traditional approach, we account for neutron hydrostatic/diffusion (nHD) equilibrium condition imposed by superfluidity of neutrons in a major part of the inner crust and rapid diffusion in the remaining part of the inner crust. We apply a simplified reaction network to model nuclear evolution of various multi-component thermonuclear burning ashes (superburst, KEPLER, and extreme rp-process ashes) in the outer crust and calculate the deep crustal heating energy release Q, parametrized by the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, P_{oi}. Using the general thermodynamic arguments, we set a lower limit on Q, Q>0.13-0.2 MeV per baryon (an actual value depends on the ash composition and the employed mass model).

## Key claim
The paper presents the first thermodynamically consistent calculations of deep crustal heating for realistic multi-component thermonuclear ashes, accounting for the neutron hydrostatic/diffusion (nHD) equilibrium condition in the inner crust. The authors treat the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface, P_oi, as a free parameter and set a lower limit on the total heat release Q of Q > 0.13-0.2 MeV per baryon, with the actual value depending on ash composition and mass model.

## Method
The authors applied a simplified reaction network to model nuclear evolution of three representative ash compositions (superburst, KEPLER, and extreme rp-process ashes) in the outer crust of an accreting neutron star. They used experimental nuclear masses from AME2020 combined with three theoretical mass models (FRDM92, FRDM12, and HFB24) and calculated the heat release Q as a function of the pressure P_oi at the outer-inner crust interface, using thermodynamic arguments from GC21 to separate outer crust modeling from inner crust uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Lower limit on total deep crustal heating: Q > 0.13-0.2 MeV per baryon (depending on ash composition and mass model)
- For P_oi = P_nd^(cat) (catalyzed neutron drip pressure), Q ~ 0.54 MeV per baryon for superburst and KEPLER ashes, and Q ~ 0.24 MeV per baryon for extreme rp-process ashes
- Heat release in outer crust at P_oi = P_nd^(cat): Q_o ~ 0.14-0.15 MeV per baryon for extreme rp-process ashes (abundance peak at A=106), and Q_o ~ 0.15-0.27 MeV per baryon for ashes with A~60
- Lower bound on P_oi from requiring Q_inner > 0: P_oi > 6.5 x 10^29 dyn cm^-2 for superburst and KEPLER ashes, and P_oi > 7.4 x 10^29 dyn cm^-2 for extreme rp-process ashes
- For superburst ashes at P_oi ~ 8 x 10^29 dyn cm^-2, Q_o predicted by HFB24 and FRDM92 mass models differs by a factor of 1.3 (about 0.05 MeV per accreted baryon)

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamically consistent nHD approach developed by Gusakov and Chugunov (2020) and GC21, which corrects the traditional approach used by Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 2003, 2008), Lau et al. (2018), and Fantina et al. (2018) that neglected neutron redistribution in the inner crust. The results enable future determination of P_oi through accurate modeling of crust evolution during accretion, and provide composition-dependent heating profiles needed for modeling thermal evolution of accreting neutron stars and interpreting quiescent thermal emission observations.
