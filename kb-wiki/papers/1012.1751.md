# Possibility of Antimagnetic Rotation in odd-A Cd isotopes

> The classical particle plus rotor model for antimagnetic rotation (AMR) has been used to investigate the possibility of observation of AMR in the high spin levels of $^{105,\,107,\,109}$Cd. The calculated I($ω$) plot and B(E2) values have been compared with the available experimental data.

# Possibility of Antimagnetic Rotation in odd-A Cd isotopes

## Key claim

The classical particle plus rotor model for antimagnetic rotation (AMR) predicts that high-spin levels in $^{105}$Cd beyond 31/2 $\hbar$ originate from an interplay of AMR and collective rotation, but the same model indicates that the high-spin yrast levels of $^{109}$Cd do **not** originate due to AMR, contrary to what might be expected from the observation of AMR in their even-even neighbors.

## Method

The authors applied the classical particle-rotor model for AMR, which treats two proton holes in the $g_{9/2}$ orbital and neutrons in $h_{11/2}$/$g_{7/2}$/$d_{5/2}$ orbitals, with the shears angle $\theta$ between the proton-hole and neutron angular momenta as the only degree of freedom. The total energy includes a rotational term, particle-hole interactions ($V_{\pi\nu}=1.2$ MeV), and hole-hole interactions ($V_{\pi\pi}=0.2$ MeV), with the moment of inertia $\Im$ and core moment of inertia $\Im_{rot}$ fixed from experimental data. Theoretical $I(\omega)$ plots and B(E2) values were calculated for $^{105,107,109}$Cd and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{109}$Cd: band head spin $j_\nu = 27/2\ \hbar$; $I_{sh}^{max} = 47/2\ \hbar$; $I_{max} = 51/2\ \hbar$; $\Im = 6$ MeV$^{-1}\ \hbar^2$; $\Im_{rot} = 15$ MeV$^{-1}\ \hbar^2$; band head frequency shift of 0.51 MeV.
- For $^{105}$Cd: band head spin $j_\nu = 23/2\ \hbar$; $I_{sh}^{max} = 39/2\ \hbar$; $\Im = 11.2$ MeV$^{-1}\ \hbar^2$; $\Im_{rot} = 14$ MeV$^{-1}\ \hbar^2$; band head frequency shift of approximately 0.4 MeV; AMR configuration becomes yrast around $\hbar\omega = 0.60$ MeV corresponding to the 35/2 $\hbar$ level.
- Predicted B(E2) values for $^{105}$Cd: 0.15 $(eb)^2$ at 31/2 $\hbar$ ($\theta = 73.5^\circ$), 0.12 $(eb)^2$ at 35/2 $\hbar$ ($\theta = 64.5^\circ$), and 0.07 $(eb)^2$ at 39/2 $\hbar$ ($\theta = 54^\circ$).
- For $^{109}$Cd, the measured B(E2) values are about twice as large as those predicted by the model.
- For $^{107}$Cd, the negative parity yrast sequence is known only up to 31/2 $\hbar$; calculations were performed for three possible values of $\Im\omega_{sh}|_{(\theta=0^\circ)} = 2\ \hbar$, $4\ \hbar$, and $6\ \hbar$ with $\Im_{rot} = 14$ MeV$^{-1}\ \hbar^2$.
- The measured $\Im^{(2)}$/B(E2) ratio for $^{109}$Cd was found to be around 165 $\hbar^2$ MeV$^{-1}\ (eb)^{-2}$, an order of magnitude larger than expected for a well deformed rotor.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of AMR previously performed for even-even Cd isotopes ($^{106,108,110}$Cd) by the same authors (Santosh and Chattopadhyay, 2010) using the same classical particle-rotor model framework. The calculations complement those for even-even Cd isotopes where $V_{\pi\pi}=1.2$ MeV and $V_{\pi\nu}=0.2$ MeV were used for all Cd isotopes from $^{105}$Cd to $^{110}$Cd. The results for $^{109}$Cd contradict the earlier conclusion by Chiara (2007) that the high-spin levels originate from AMR, while the predictions for $^{105}$Cd and $^{107}$Cd provide testable hypotheses that require future B(E2) measurements to confirm the presence of AMR in these odd-A nuclei.
