# True Dynamical and Gauge Structures of the QCD Ground State and the Singular Gluon Fileds

> We convincingly argue that the true dynamical and gauge structure of the QCD ground state is much more complicated than its Lagrangian exact gauge symmetry supposes to be. The dynamical source of these complications has been identified with the tadpole/seagull term, which renormalized version called, the mass gap. Its true dynamical role is hidden in the QCD ground state, but it is explicitly present in the full gluon self-energy. To disclose it the splintering between the transverse conditions for the full gluon self-energy and its subtracted counterpart has been derived. The equation of motion for the full gluon propagator on account of the mass gap was given, which allows to fix the dynamical and gauge structures by a newly-introduced generalized gauge. A novel non-perturbative analytical method, the mass gap approach was developed for QCD and its groud state as well. We have discovered a general, non-perturbative singular solution for the full gluon propagator valid in the whole gluon momentum range, while dominating at large distances over all the other possible solutions. It accommodates all the severe infrared singularities, which may appear in the QCD ground state due to the self-interaction of massless gluon modes. A corresponding non-pertubative multiplicative infrared renormalization program has been formulated. The resulting full gluon propagator prevents gluons to appear at large distances as physical states (confinement of color gluons). Our approach also explains the scale violation in the asymptotic freedom regime, and why the rising potential between heavy quarks is only linear one.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the true dynamical and gauge structure of the QCD ground state is more complicated than the exact gauge symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian suggests, because the tadpole/seagull term (whose renormalized version is called the mass gap) is explicitly present in the full gluon self-energy and cannot be removed by the transverse conditions that preserve the exact gauge symmetry in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors analyze the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) equation for the full gluon propagator, focusing on the transversity conditions for the full gluon self-energy and its subtracted counterpart. They derive the splintering between these transverse conditions and show that preserving the exact gauge symmetry requires setting the mass gap to zero, which hides the tadpole term. They then formulate a non-perturbative "mass gap approach" in a newly-introduced generalized gauge, deriving a singular solution for the full gluon propagator that accommodates severe infrared singularities and developing a multiplicative infrared renormalization program.

## Key numbers

- The mass gap parameter \(\Delta^2(D)\) appears as a quadratically UV divergent but regularized constant in the full gluon self-energy, with contributions \(\Delta^2_q\), \(\Delta^2_g(D)\), and \(\Delta^2_t(D)\) from quark, gluon, and tadpole terms respectively (equation 3.7).
- The transverse condition for the full gluon self-energy yields \(q_\rho q_\sigma \Pi_{\rho\sigma}(q; D) = (\xi_0 - \xi)/(\xi \xi_0) (q^2)^2\) (equation 3.5), and for the subtracted counterpart \(q_\rho q_\sigma \Pi^{(s)}_{\rho\sigma}(q; D) = (\xi_0 - \xi)/(\xi \xi_0) (q^2)^2 - q^2 \Delta^2(D)\) (equation 3.8).
- When the exact gauge symmetry is preserved, the condition \(\xi = \xi_0\) is required (equation 3.18), and all mass scale parameters including \(\Delta^2(D)\) must be set to zero (equation 3.14).

## Context

This work extends the mass gap concept, originally introduced by Jaffe and Witten (2000) within the Hamiltonian formalism, to the QCD ground state (vacuum). It contradicts the standard perturbative treatment where the tadpole term is removed by gauge invariance, and instead argues that the tadpole term is the dynamical source of complications in the QCD vacuum. The approach enables a non-perturbative description of gluon confinement (preventing gluons from appearing as physical states at large distances) and explains the linear rising potential between heavy quarks, as well as scale violation in the asymptotic freedom regime.
