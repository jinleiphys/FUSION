# Effective Potential Approach to Quark Ferromagnetization in High Density Quark Matter

> A possibility of spontaneous magnetization in high density symmetric quark matter is investigated using the NJL type effective model of QCD by means of an effective potential with respect to an auxiliary field. It is shown that the quark ferromagnetic condensate has non-vanishing value at high baryon density due to the tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a quark ferromagnetic condensate (spontaneous spin alignment) can have a non-zero value at high baryon density in symmetric quark matter, but only when the tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks is included in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type effective model; the effect does not appear from the scalar or vector interactions alone.

## Method

The authors start from an NJL-type Lagrangian density that includes a tensor interaction term, and use the auxiliary field method to introduce a field \(F_k\) corresponding to the ferromagnetic condensate. They derive the effective potential for this condensate at finite temperature and chemical potential using the Matsubara formalism, then specialize to zero temperature and finite chemical potential to obtain analytic expressions for the effective potential in the regimes \(F > \mu\) and \(F < \mu\). The gap equation \(\delta V/\delta F = 0\) is solved to find the minimum of the effective potential as a function of the quark chemical potential \(\mu\).

## Key numbers

* The critical quark chemical potential for the onset of ferromagnetization is \(\mu_{\rm cr} = 0.406\) GeV for the tensor coupling strength \(G = 20\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* At this critical chemical potential, the critical baryon density is about \(3.47\) times the normal nuclear density \(\rho_0\).
* For \(G = 15\) GeV\(^{-2}\), the critical baryon density is \(5.34\,\rho_0\) with \(\mu_{\rm cr} = 0.468\) GeV; for \(G = 25\) GeV\(^{-2}\), it is \(2.48\,\rho_0\) with \(\mu_{\rm cr} = 0.363\) GeV.
* The estimated strength of the "reduced" magnetic field at high density is of order \(10^{15}\) to \(10^{16}\) T.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bohr, da Providencia, and Providencia (Bohr1, Bohr2) that considered ferromagnetic condensate in quark matter using a thermodynamic treatment of the NJL model with tensor interaction. The present paper re-derives the result using a field-theoretical effective potential approach with the auxiliary field method, which enables extension to finite temperature. The results are relevant for understanding the possible origin of very large magnetic fields in magnetars (neutron stars with extremely strong magnetic fields) and could be extended to study competition or coexistence with color superconductivity, as previously considered by other authors (add3, add4).
