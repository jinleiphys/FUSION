# Global variables for various centralities at RHIC: a Cracow model approach

> The final $p_{T}$-spectra measured at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ and 200 GeV are fitted within the Cracow single-freeze-out model. Then the global variables like the transverse energy at midrapidity, the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity and the total multiplicity of charged particles are evaluated. The predictions agree fairly well with the experimental data. The centrality independence of the total number of charged particles per participant pair has been also reproduced.

## Key claim

The Cracow single-freeze-out model, with geometric parameters fitted to final RHIC data on identified charged hadron pT spectra, reproduces the centrality independence of the total number of charged particles per participant pair to within approximately 10%, despite the fact that the geometric parameters were fitted to midrapidity spectra while the total multiplicity is measured over the full rapidity range.

## Method

The single-freeze-out model assumes simultaneous chemical and thermal freeze-out on a hypersurface defined by constant proper time tau, with four-velocity proportional to the coordinate. Thermal parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B) were taken from independent fits to particle yield ratios, while geometric parameters (tau and rho_max) were determined by chi-squared fits to final pT spectra of pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, and antip from PHENIX at 130 and 200 GeV, and from STAR and BRAHMS at 200 GeV, for multiple centrality bins. Global variables (dE_T/deta at midrapidity, dN_ch/deta at midrapidity, and total N_ch) were then computed from the fitted model parameters.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV: for N_part=347.7, rho_max=6.50+/-0.27 fm, tau=8.23+/-0.23 fm, beta_perp_max=0.62, chi^2/NDF=0.52
- PHENIX at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV: for N_part=14.3, rho_max=1.68+/-0.19 fm, tau=3.14+/-0.22 fm, beta_perp_max=0.47, chi^2/NDF=1.32
- PHENIX at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=351.4, rho_max=8.46+/-0.10 fm, tau=8.84+/-0.08 fm, beta_perp_max=0.69, chi^2/NDF=0.80
- PHENIX at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=6.3, rho_max=1.71+/-0.06 fm, tau=2.40+/-0.05 fm, beta_perp_max=0.58, chi^2/NDF=1.40
- STAR at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=352, rho_max=9.22+/-0.18 fm, tau=7.13+/-0.06 fm, beta_perp_max=0.79, chi^2/NDF=0.29
- STAR at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=14, rho_max=2.37+/-0.08 fm, tau=3.32+/-0.06 fm, beta_perp_max=0.58, chi^2/NDF=0.87
- BRAHMS at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=357, rho_max=8.75+/-0.16 fm, tau=8.38+/-0.11 fm, beta_perp_max=0.72, chi^2/NDF=0.50
- BRAHMS at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: for N_part=62, rho_max=4.42+/-0.12 fm, tau=4.95+/-0.10 fm, beta_perp_max=0.67, chi^2/NDF=0.61
- Predicted total charged-particle multiplicity per participant pair is roughly constant within N_part approx 60-360, with only approx 10% deviation in the BRAHMS case
- Predicted absolute values of total N_ch per participant pair agree with PHOBOS data within approx 10%
- For dE_T/deta at midrapidity per participant pair at 200 GeV, the most central STAR point is overestimated by approx 17%

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of the single-freeze-out model by Baran et al. (2003) and Broniowski et al. (2002) by fitting to final (rather than preliminary) RHIC data from PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS, and by including previously unfitted centrality bins and the BRAHMS spectra. The model reproduces the centrality independence of total charged-particle multiplicity per participant pair observed by PHOBOS (Back et al., 2003), which is surprising because the geometric parameters were fitted to midrapidity spectra only. The results enable further tests of the single-freeze-out scenario and provide a consistent framework for predicting global observables from pT spectra fits.
