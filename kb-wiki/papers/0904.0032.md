# A light-cone wavefunction approach to open heavy flavor dynamics in QCD matter

> We calculate the lowest order charm and beauty parton distribution functions in and fragmentation functions into D and B mesons using the operator definitions of factorized perturbative QCD. In the vacuum, we find the leading corrections that arise from the structure of the final-state hadrons. Quark-antiquark potentials extracted from the lattice are employed to demonstrate the existence of open heavy flavor bound-state solutions in the QGP in the vicinity of the critical temperature. We provide first results for the in-medium modification of the heavy quark distribution and decay probabilities in a co-moving plasma. In an improved perturbative QCD description of heavy flavor dynamics in the thermal medium, we combine D and B meson formation and dissociation with parton-level charm and beauty quark quenching to obtain predictions for the heavy meson and non-photonic electron suppression in Cu+Cu and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and the LHC, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first calculation of thermal modifications to heavy quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs) for open heavy flavor, showing that heavy-light quark bound-state solutions of the Dirac equation persist in a co-moving quark-gluon plasma up to temperatures of approximately 1.6 times the critical temperature, which motivates combining partonic energy loss with meson dissociation to describe heavy flavor suppression.

## Method

The authors calculate charm and beauty quark PDFs and fragmentation functions into D and B mesons using operator definitions of factorized perturbative QCD, expressing them in terms of light-cone wavefunctions. They evaluate heavy meson wavefunctions in vacuum and near the QCD phase transition using quark-antiquark potentials extracted from lattice QCD, then combine D and B meson formation and dissociation with parton-level charm and beauty quark quenching in an improved perturbative QCD description of heavy flavor dynamics in the thermal medium.

## Key numbers

- Bound-state solutions persist to temperatures of approximately 1.6 times the critical temperature (T_c)
- For central Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, gluon rapidity densities are dN^g/dy = 250 and 1050, respectively
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, expected gluon rapidity density is dN^g/dy = 2800
- The formation time of the QGP is taken as tau_0 = 0.6 fm at RHIC and tau_0 = 0.3 fm at the LHC
- The squared transverse momentum broadening from initial-state scattering uses mu^2 = 0.12 GeV^2, with lambda_g = (C_F/C_A) lambda_q = 1 fm
- The effective coupling constant for jet-medium interactions is alpha_s = 0.28-0.32
- The mean parton transverse momentum squared in nucleon-nucleon collisions is <k_Ta,b^2>_NN = 0.9 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (Zhang 2003, Wicks 2007) and heavy meson dissociation approaches (Adil 2006, Dominguez 2008) by providing the first theoretical evaluation of thermal modifications to heavy quark PDFs and FFs. It enables a combined treatment of partonic and hadronic aspects of open heavy flavor suppression in heavy ion collisions, addressing the known discrepancy between partonic energy loss calculations and the measured non-photonic electron suppression at RHIC (Adare 2006, Abelev 2006).
