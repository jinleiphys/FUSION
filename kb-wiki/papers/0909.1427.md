# Temperature dependence of magnetic susceptibility of nuclear matter: lowest order constrained variational calculations

> In this paper we study the magnetic susceptibility and other thermodynamic properties of the polarized nuclear matter at finite temperature using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method employing the $AV_{18}$ potential. Our results show a monotonic behavior for the magnetic susceptibility which indicates that the spontaneous transition to the ferromagnetic phase does not occur for this system.

## Key claim

The magnetic susceptibility of hot symmetrical nuclear matter, calculated with the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method using the AV18 potential, shows a monotonic behavior with no indication of a spontaneous transition to a ferromagnetic phase, contrary to some earlier calculations that predicted such a transition for certain densities.

## Method

The authors applied the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to polarized symmetrical nuclear matter at finite temperature, using the AV18 two-body potential. They minimized the free energy per nucleon with respect to variations in the effective masses and correlation functions, subject to a normalization constraint, and solved the resulting coupled and uncoupled Euler-Lagrange differential equations to obtain the correlation functions and thermodynamic properties.

## Key numbers

- At a density of 0.5 fm^{-3} and temperature 20 MeV, the ratio of the effective mass of fully polarized nuclear matter to that of unpolarized nuclear matter is 1.24, which is below the limiting value of 2^{2/3} that would indicate anomalous entropy behavior.
- The ratio chi_F/chi (magnetic susceptibility relative to a noninteracting Fermi gas) is shown as a function of temperature at a density of 0.16 fm^{-3} and as a function of density at temperature 20 MeV; the ratio is always positive and monotonically increasing, with no crossing to negative values that would signal a ferromagnetic instability.
- Free energy per nucleon is presented for temperatures of 10 and 20 MeV across various spin polarizations and densities; the free energy increases with density and polarization and decreases with temperature.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations for polarized neutron matter at finite temperature (Bigdeli et al., 2009) and for polarized symmetrical nuclear matter at zero temperature (Bigdeli et al., 2008) to the finite-temperature case. It contradicts earlier results that found ferromagnetic transitions in neutron matter using some interactions (Brown, 1970; Rice, 1975; Pandharipande, 1976; Marcos, 1982; Vidana, 2002) and aligns with other modern calculations using realistic two-body and three-body forces that show no ferromagnetic transition (Kutschera, 1990; Fantoni, 2001; Vidaña, 2002; Bombaci, 2006). The results are relevant for understanding the magnetic field generation in protoneutron stars, where interior temperatures are of order 20-50 MeV.
