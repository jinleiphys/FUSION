# Elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies in cluster effective field theory

> The elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies is studied employing an effective field theory in which the $α$ and $^{12}$C states are treated as elementary-like fields. We discuss scales of the theory at stellar energy region that the ${}^{12}$C($α$, $γ$)$^{16}$O process occurs, and then obtain an expression of the elastic scattering amplitudes in terms of effective range parameters. Using experimental data of the phase shifts for $l=0,1,2$ channels at low energies, for which the resonance regions are avoided, we fix values of the parameters and find that the phase shifts at the low energies are well reproduced by using three effective range parameters for each channel. Furthermore, we discuss problems and uncertainties of the present approach when the amplitudes are extrapolated to the stellar energy region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that elastic α-¹²C scattering phase shifts for l=0, 1, 2 channels at low energies can be well reproduced using three effective range parameters per channel in a cluster effective field theory, but the extrapolation of the scattering amplitudes to the stellar Gamow peak energy (T_G ≈ 0.3 MeV) suffers from significant uncertainties and cannot qualitatively reproduce the enhancement of the S-factor for the E1 and E2 channels reported in prior work.

## Method

The author constructs an effective field theory treating α and ¹²C as elementary-like fields, with resonance energies (4.89, 2.42, and 2.68 MeV for the 0₂⁺, 1₂⁻, and 2₂⁺ states, respectively) as the large energy scales. Scattering amplitudes for l=0, 1, 2 partial waves are expressed in terms of three effective range parameters (γ_l, r_l, P_l) per channel, and these parameters are fitted to experimental phase shift data from Plaga et al. (1987) and Tischhauser et al. (2009) in energy ranges below the resonances, avoiding the resonance regions.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow peak energy for the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O process is T_G ≈ 0.3 MeV, corresponding to a typical momentum scale Q ≈ 41 MeV.
- The large momentum scale of the theory is Λ_H ≈ 150 MeV, giving an expansion parameter Q/Λ_H ≈ 1/3 at T_G.
- For the l=0 channel, fitted effective range parameters include γ_0 values ranging from 0.058 ± 0.058 MeV (set S0) to -0.008 ± 0.001 MeV (set S3), r_0 ≈ 0.27 fm, and P_0 ≈ -0.04 fm³.
- For the l=1 channel, fitted parameters include γ_1 values from (-2.53 ± 1.09) × 10³ MeV³ (set P0) to (-4.57 ± 0.38) × 10³ MeV³ (set P2), r_1 ≈ 0.405 fm⁻¹, and P_1 ≈ -0.645 fm.
- For the l=2 channel, fitted parameters include r_2 ≈ 0.53 fm⁻³ and P_2 ≈ -5.5 fm⁻¹ (with γ_2 set to zero).
- The subthreshold bound states of ¹⁶O lie at T = -0.045 MeV (1₁⁻) and T = -0.24 MeV (2₁⁺).

## Context

This work extends the application of cluster effective field theory to elastic α-¹²C scattering, aiming toward a model-independent theoretical framework for the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction, which determines the ¹²C/¹⁶O ratio in stellar helium burning. The approach follows earlier EFT studies of nuclear astrophysics reactions such as p(n,γ)d at BBN energies and pp fusion in the Sun, where theoretical uncertainties below 1% were achieved. The paper identifies that incorporating the subthreshold 1₁⁻ and 2₁⁺ states is difficult in the present effective range expansion framework, and the extrapolation to T_G shows significant uncertainties that must be resolved before the EFT can achieve its stated goal of about 5% theoretical uncertainty in the S-factor.
