# Production of resonances in a thermal model

> We discuss the pi+ pi- invariant-mass correlations and the resonance production in a thermal model with single freeze-out. The predictions are confronted with the data from the STAR Collaboration.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out thermal model, which assumes equal chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures, can reproduce the gross features of the STAR $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant-mass correlation data, but the model's predicted $\rho$ peak position is shifted down by about 30 MeV due to thermal effects and kinematic cuts, leaving a few tens of MeV unexplained; the authors therefore also present calculations with the $\rho$ mass moved down by 9%.

## Method

The authors compute $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant-mass correlations from decays of neutral resonances ($\rho$, $K_S^0$, $\omega$, $\eta$, $\eta'$, $f_0/\sigma$, $f_2$) using a phase-shift formula for the volume density of resonances in a thermal model with single freeze-out. The freeze-out hypersurface has a Buda-Lund-like shape with Hubble flow, and the model parameters (temperature $T$, baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$, invariant time $\tau$, transverse size $\rho_{\rm max}$) are fixed by fitting particle abundance ratios and transverse-momentum spectra. The full calculation includes collective flow, kinematic cuts, decays of higher resonances, and detector efficiency filtering.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the thermal parameters are $T=160$ MeV and $\mu_B=26$ MeV.
- For a static source, spectra are shown at $T=165$ MeV and $T=110$ MeV.
- Thermal effects and kinematic cuts shift the $\rho$ peak down by about 30 MeV.
- With the $\rho$ mass moved down by 9%, the model uses $m^*_\rho = 700$ MeV as an alternative to the standard 770 MeV.
- For $m^*_\rho = 770$ MeV: $T = 165.6 \pm 4.5$ MeV, $\mu_B = 28.5 \pm 3.7$ MeV; predicted $\rho^0/\pi^- = 0.114 \pm 0.002$ (experiment: $0.183 \pm 0.028$ for 40-80% centrality).
- For $m^*_\rho = 700$ MeV: $T = 167.6 \pm 4.6$ MeV, $\mu_B = 28.9 \pm 3.8$ MeV; predicted $\rho^0/\pi^- = 0.135 \pm 0.001$.
- Predicted $K^*(892)/K^- = 0.33 \pm 0.01$ for both $\rho$ mass choices (experiment ranges from $0.205 \pm 0.033$ for 0-10% to $0.269 \pm 0.047$ for 50-80% centrality).
- Predicted $\Lambda(1520)/\Lambda = 0.061 \pm 0.002$ (experiment: $0.022 \pm 0.010$ for 0-7% to $0.062 \pm 0.027$ for 60-80% centrality).

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski and Florkowski (2002) to resonance production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, confronting predictions with STAR data. The phase-shift formalism was also applied by Pratt and Bauer in a similar analysis. The results enable further refinement of thermal models by showing that the $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant-mass spectrum can serve as a hadronic thermometer, and that the "cooling" effect from higher resonance decays and flow mimics a lower freeze-out temperature.
