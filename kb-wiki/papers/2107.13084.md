# Charm-baryon semileptonic decays and the strange $Λ^*$ resonances: New insights from lattice QCD

> Understanding the properties of the strange $Λ^*$ baryon resonances is a long-standing and fascinating problem. $Λ_c$ charm-baryon semileptonic weak decays to these resonances are highly sensitive to their internal structure and can be used to test theoretical models. We have performed the first lattice-QCD computation of the form factors governing $Λ_c$ semileptonic decays to a $Λ^*$ resonance: the $Λ^*(1520)$, which has negative parity and spin $3/2$. Here we present the resulting Standard-Model predictions of the $Λ_c\toΛ^*(1520)\ell^+ν_\ell$ differential and integrated decay rates as well as angular observables. Furthermore, by combining the recent BESIII measurement of the $Λ_c \to X e^+ ν_e$ inclusive semipositronic branching fraction [Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 251801 (2018)] with lattice-QCD predictions of the $Λ_c \to Λe^+ ν_e$, $Λ_c \to n e^+ ν_e$, and $Λ_c \to Λ^*(1520) e^+ ν_e$ decay rates, we obtain an upper limit on the sum of the branching fractions to all other semipositronic final states. In particular, this upper limit constrains the $Λ_c\toΛ^*(1405)e^+ ν_e$ branching fraction to be very small, which may be another hint for a molecular structure of the $Λ^*(1405)$.

# Charm-baryon semileptonic decays and the strange Lambda* resonances: New insights from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The first lattice-QCD computation of form factors for Lambda_c semileptonic decays to a Lambda* resonance (the Lambda*(1520)) yields Standard-Model predictions for decay rates and angular observables; by combining these with the BESIII inclusive measurement and lattice predictions for other exclusive channels, the authors obtain an upper limit on the sum of branching fractions to all other semipositronic final states, which constrains the Lambda_c to Lambda*(1405) e+ nu_e branching fraction to be very small, potentially supporting a molecular structure for the Lambda*(1405), but only if the preference for a small residual branching fraction is strengthened with higher-precision data.

## Method

The authors performed lattice-QCD calculations of the Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) transition form factors using an interpolating field with spatial structure corresponding to total quark orbital angular momentum L=1, enabling them to access the narrow, spin-3/2, negative-parity resonance. They computed the differential and integrated decay rates and angular observables for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) ell+ nu_ell in the full kinematic range, and combined these results with previous lattice-QCD predictions for Lambda_c to Lambda e+ nu_e and Lambda_c to n e+ nu_e together with the BESIII inclusive branching fraction measurement to derive upper limits on the sum of all other semipositronic decay modes.

## Key numbers

- Integrated decay rate for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) e+ nu_e divided by |V_cs|^2: 0.00267(39)(18) ps^{-1}
- Integrated decay rate for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) mu+ nu_mu divided by |V_cs|^2: 0.00239(34)(16) ps^{-1}
- Branching fraction for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) e+ nu_e: 0.0512(82)(8) percent
- Branching fraction for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1520) mu+ nu_mu: 0.0458(72)(7) percent
- Sum of lattice-QCD branching fractions for Lambda_c to Lambda e+ nu_e, n e+ nu_e, and Lambda*(1520) e+ nu_e: 4.32(23)(7) percent
- BESIII inclusive branching fraction Lambda_c to X e+ nu_e: 3.95(34)(9) percent
- Upper limit on branching fraction for Lambda_c to X e+ nu_e with X not equal to Lambda, n, or Lambda*(1520): less than 0.15 percent at 68 percent confidence level; less than 0.39 percent at 90 percent confidence level
- Quark-model predictions for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1405) e+ nu_e branching fraction: 0.24 percent (Hussain 2017) and 0.31 percent (Li 2021), both above the 68 percent confidence level upper limit
- Molecular-model prediction for Lambda_c to Lambda*(1405) e+ nu_e branching fraction: 0.002 percent to 0.005 percent (Ikeno 2015)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice-QCD methodology for Lambda_b to Lambda*(1520) transitions (Meinel 2020, Meinel 2021) to the charm sector, enabling the first ab initio calculation of Lambda_c semileptonic decays to a Lambda* resonance. The results are compared with quark-model predictions (Hussain 2017) and molecular-model calculations (Ikeno 2015) for the Lambda*(1405), and the derived upper limits on the Lambda_c to Lambda*(1405) branching fraction favor the molecular interpretation over the three-quark bound-state picture. The analysis also uses prior lattice-QCD results for Lambda_c to Lambda (Meinel 2016) and Lambda_c to n (Meinel 2017) decays, and the BESIII inclusive measurement (Ablikim 2018). Future higher-precision measurements of the inclusive branching fraction and of Lambda_c to Lambda e+ nu_e could strengthen the constraints on the structure of the Lambda*(1405).
