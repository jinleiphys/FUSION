# Hierarchy of azimuthal anisotropy harmonics in collisions of small systems from the Color Glass Condensate

> We demonstrate that the striking systematics of two-particle azimuthal Fourier harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ in ultrarelativistic collisions of protons, deuterons and helium-3 ions off gold nuclei measured by the PHENIX Collaboration [arXiv:1805.02973] at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) is reproduced in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory. This contradicts the claim in [arXiv:1805.02973] that their data rules out initial state based explanations. The underlying systematics of the effect, as discussed previously in [arXiv:1705.00745,arXiv:1706.06260,arXiv:1801.09704], arise from the differing structure of strong color correlations between gluon domains of size $1/Q_S$ at fine ($p_\perp \gtrapprox Q_S$) or coarser ($p_\perp \lessapprox Q_S$) transverse momentum resolution. Further tests of the limits of validity of this framework can be carried out in light-heavy ion collisions at both RHIC and the Large Hadron Collider. Such measurements also offer novel opportunities for further exploration of the role of the surprisingly large short-range nuclear correlations measured at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory reproduces the systematics of the second and third Fourier harmonics ($v_2$ and $v_3$) measured by PHENIX in p+Au, d+Au, and ³He+Au collisions, contradicting the claim that the data rules out initial-state-based explanations.

## Method

The authors performed dilute-dense CGC EFT computations including the leading contribution and the first non-trivial saturation correction to the color charge density of the projectile, with the target treated to all orders. The projectile and target color sources were placed using Glauber sampling with the IP-Sat model, and parameters were fixed by minimizing deviations from the measured multiplicity distribution in d+Au collisions from STAR. The two-particle anisotropy coefficients $v_n^2\{2\}(p_\perp)$ were then computed for 0-5% centrality events in each system at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The mean of the ratio $Q_S/g^2\mu$ was taken to be 0.5, the variance of Gaussian fluctuations of $\ln(Q_S^2)$ was taken to be $\sigma=0.5$, and the infrared cutoff scale for color fields was taken to be $m=0.3$ GeV.
- The PHENIX event plane resolution was 6.7% for p+Au and 5.7% for d+Au.
- The $p_\perp$ bin width used was $\Delta=0.5$ GeV, matching the PHENIX analysis.
- The computation was performed for collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the toy model computations of Dusling (2017) that showed quarks from small light ions scatter off color domains in the heavy ion target, and the dense-dense CGC computations of Schenke (2015). It directly contradicts the claim by the PHENIX Collaboration (2018) that their data rules out initial-state explanations. The framework enables further tests in light-heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and offers opportunities to explore short-range nuclear correlations measured at Jefferson Lab.
