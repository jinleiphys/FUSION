# Critical point of QCD at finite T and μ, lattice results for physical quark masses

> A critical point (E) is expected in QCD on the temperature (T) versus baryonic chemical potential (μ) plane. Using a recently proposed lattice method for μ\neq 0 we study dynamical QCD with n_f=2+1 staggered quarks of physical masses on L_t=4 lattices. Our result for the critical point is T_E=162 \pm 2 MeV and μ_E= 360 \pm 40 MeV. For the critical temperature at μ=0 we obtained T_c=164 \pm 2 MeV. This work extends our previous study [Z. Fodor and S.D.Katz, JHEP 0203 (2002) 014] by two means. It decreases the light quark masses (m_{u,d}) by a factor of three down to their physical values. # Critical point of QCD at finite T and μ, lattice results for physical quark masses

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with physical quark masses on a finite lattice (L_t=4), the critical endpoint (E) on the QCD phase diagram is located at T_E=162±2 MeV and μ_E=360±40 MeV, but the continuum extrapolation is still missing, so these results are not yet the final continuum-limit values.

## Method

The authors used overlap-improving multi-parameter reweighting to generate QCD configurations at μ=0 and reweight them to finite μ, then analyzed the volume dependence of Lee-Yang zeros on the complex gauge coupling (β) plane to distinguish crossover from first-order phase transition. They performed dynamical QCD with n_f=2+1 staggered quarks of physical masses on L_t=4 lattices with spatial extensions L_s=6,8,10,12, and set the physical scale using T=0 measurements of m_π, m_K, m_ρ, and the Sommer scale R_0.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint temperature: T_E = 162 ± 2 MeV
- Critical endpoint baryonic chemical potential: μ_E = 360 ± 40 MeV
- Critical temperature at μ=0: T_c = 164 ± 2 MeV
- Endpoint in lattice units: μ_end = 0.1825(75)
- Curvature of the crossover line: C = 0.0032(1) in T/T_c = 1 − C μ_B²/T_c²
- Pion-to-rho mass ratio at T≠0 parameters: 0.188(2) (physical value 0.179)
- Pion-to-kaon mass ratio at T≠0 parameters: 0.267(1) (physical value 0.277)
- Light quark masses decreased by a factor of 3 compared to previous study (Fodor and Katz, JHEP 0203 (2002) 014), from approximately three times physical down to physical values
- Largest volume increased by a factor of 3 compared to that previous study
- Computational effort 140 times larger than the previous analysis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Fodor and Katz, JHEP 0203 (2002) 014) by reducing light quark masses to physical values and increasing the largest volume by a factor of three, confirming that increasing volume did not change results while decreasing quark masses reduced μ_E as expected. The result is consistent with earlier model calculations (Halasz et al., 1998) that suggested μ_E ≈ 700 MeV for infinite strange quark mass, but gives a significantly lower value for physical quark masses. The missing continuum extrapolation is identified as the most important remaining step, though the authors note that decreasing lattice spacing by a factor of 2 would increase CPU costs by approximately three orders of magnitude.
