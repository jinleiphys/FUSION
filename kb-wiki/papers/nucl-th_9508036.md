# Treatment of Pionic Modes at the Nuclear Surface for Transport Descriptions

> Dispersion relations and amplitudes of collective pionic modes are derived in a pi + nucleon-hole + delta-hole model for use in transport descriptions by means of a local density approximation. It is discussed how pionic modes can be converted to real particles when penetrating the nuclear surface and how earlier treatments can be improved. When the surface is stationary only free pions emerge. The time-dependent situation is also addressed, as is the conversion of non-physical (i.e. unperturbed delta-hole) modes to real particles when the nuclear density vanishes. A simplified one-dimensional scenario is used to investigate the reflection and transmission of pionic modes at the nuclear surface. It is found that reflection of pionic modes is rather unlikely, but the process can be incorporated into transport descriptions by the use of approximate local transmission coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper derives dispersion relations and amplitudes for collective pionic modes (quasimesons) in a pi + nucleon-hole + delta-hole model for use in transport descriptions, but the treatment is restricted to zero temperature and omits the delta width in the dispersion relations, with the justification that these simplifications are acceptable at the low densities and temperatures expected at the nuclear surface.

## Method

The authors employ a Green's function technique within the RPA approximation to calculate spin-isospin modes in infinite nuclear matter, using a cubic box normalization with effective p-wave interactions at the N pi N and N pi Delta vertices and short-range interactions at nucleon-hole vertices. They then apply a local density approximation to extend these results to inhomogeneous systems, and investigate the conversion of collective pionic modes to real particles at the nuclear surface using a simplified one-dimensional scenario. The dispersion relations and amplitudes of the two collective pionic modes (tilde_pi_1 and tilde_pi_2) are parametrized as functions of momentum and density for implementation in transport simulations.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: rho^0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}
- The lower collective mode (tilde_pi_1) starts at hbar omega = m_pi c^2 at q = 0 and enters the Delta N^{-1} region around q approximately 360 MeV/c
- The upper collective mode (tilde_pi_2) starts slightly above hbar omega approximately m_Delta c^2 - m_N c^2 at q = 0
- The non-collective NN^{-1} modes have energies in the region 0 <= hbar omega <= q^2/(2 m_N*) + q p_F/m_N* for q < 2 p_F
- The non-collective Delta N^{-1} states have energies constrained to the band m_Delta - m_N + q^2/(2 m_Delta) - q p_F/m_Delta <= hbar omega <= m_Delta - m_N + q^2/(2 m_Delta) + q p_F/m_Delta
- Partial and total Delta widths are presented for a Delta with momentum 300 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the pi + NN^{-1} + Delta N^{-1} model developed in a prior reference (Helgesson and Randrup, 1995) to address the conceptual problems of converting quasimesons to real particles at the nuclear surface, where the density approaches zero. It improves upon earlier treatments by the Giessen group (1993) and the Texas group (1994), which either used effective dispersion relations with distorted in-medium effects or allowed modes to escape as unperturbed Delta N^{-1} states that were then converted to free deltas. The paper enables more accurate incorporation of in-medium pionic properties into transport descriptions such as BUU and QMD for heavy-ion collisions at bombarding energies from a few hundred MeV to several GeV per nucleon.
