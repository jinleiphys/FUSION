# Inferring the Equation of State from Neutron Star Observables via Machine Learning

> We have conducted an extensive study using a diverse set of equations of state (EoSs) to uncover strong relationships between neutron star (NS) observables and the underlying EoS parameters using symbolic regression method. These EoS models, derived from a mix of agnostic and physics-based approaches, considered neutron stars composed of nucleons, hyperons, and other exotic degrees of freedom in beta equilibrium. The maximum mass of a NS is found to be strongly correlated with the pressure and baryon density at an energy density of approximately 800 MeV.fm$^{-3}$. We have also demonstrated that the EoS can be expressed as a function of radius and tidal deformability within the NS mass range 1-2$M_\odot$. These insights offer a promising and efficient framework to decode the dense matter EoS directly from the accurate knowledge of NS observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the maximum mass of a neutron star can be predicted from the pressure and baryon density at a fixed energy density of approximately 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\) using a quadratic formula derived via symbolic regression, and that the equation of state can be expressed as a function of radius and tidal deformability for neutron stars in the mass range 1-2 \(M_\odot\). The hedge is that these relationships are strongest for nucleonic equations of state and degrade for agnostic models, with the correlation between pressure and maximum mass dropping from 0.85 at 600 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\) to 0.8 at 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\) for the combined dataset.

## Method

The authors assembled seven distinct sets of equations of state (EoS) for cold dense neutron star matter, including nucleonic relativistic mean-field models (NL), hyperonic extensions (NL-hyp), models from the CompOSE database, speed-of-sound extrapolation models (CSE), piecewise-polytropic models (PWP), density-dependent Bayesian models (DDB), and spectral representation models (SR). They applied the Python Symbolic Regression (PySR) algorithm to approximately half a million iterations, searching for mathematical relationships between EoS quantities (pressure, baryon density, energy density) and neutron star observables (mass, radius, tidal deformability) computed from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The optimal equations were selected based on the highest Pearson correlation coefficient and smallest relative error.

## Key numbers

* The maximum mass of a neutron star can be expressed as \(M_{\rm max}/M_\odot = -4.0946 + 7.9151 Q - 2.2312 Q^2\), where \(Q = 1.23326\times 10^{-6} (P/({\rm MeV.fm^{-3}}) + 0.77003) / (\rho/{\rm fm^{-3}})\) evaluated at an energy density of 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\), with a relative error below 4\% for over 90\% of the 700 EoS models tested.
* The Pearson correlation coefficient between pressure and maximum mass peaks at 0.85 for the combined dataset at an energy density of 600 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\), and drops to 0.8 at 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\); individual set correlations at 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\) range from 0.99 (DDB) down to 0.37 (CSE).
* Using the fitted formula instead of solving the TOV equations during Bayesian inference reduced CPU time from 971.10 CPU hours to 127.50 CPU hours, a reduction factor of more than seven, while producing nearly identical posterior distributions for the equation of state and mass-radius relation.
* The baryon density at an energy density of 800 MeV.fm\(^{-3}\) spans from 0.63 fm\(^{-3}\) (minimum) to 0.77 fm\(^{-3}\) (maximum) across the 700 EoS models.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to find quasi-universal relations between neutron star properties and the dense matter equation of state, such as those by Lattimer (2012) and Hebeler (2013), by applying symbolic regression to a broader and more diverse set of EoS models than previously considered. The authors build on machine learning approaches to neutron star physics, including neural network studies by Ferreira (2021) and Bayesian inference frameworks by Malik (2022), and demonstrate that their derived formula can dramatically accelerate Bayesian parameter estimation by bypassing repeated TOV integrations. The results enable more efficient large-scale inference of the dense matter EoS from astrophysical observations, particularly for nucleonic models where the correlations are strongest.
