# Duality Between the Weak and Strong Interaction Limits for Randomly Interacting Fermions

> We establish the existence of a duality transformation for generic models of interacting fermions with two-body interactions. The eigenstates at weak and strong interaction U possess similar statistical properties when expressed in the U=0 and U=infinity eigenstates bases respectively. This implies the existence of a duality point U_d where the eigenstates have the same spreading in both bases. U_d is surrounded by an interval of finite width which is characterized by a non Lorentzian spreading of the strength function in both bases. Scaling arguments predict the survival of this intermediate regime as the number of particles is increased.

# Duality Between the Weak and Strong Interaction Limits for Randomly Interacting Fermions

## Key claim
The authors establish a duality transformation for the deformed Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) such that eigenstate properties at weak and strong interaction U are statistically equivalent when expressed in the U=0 and U=∞ eigenstate bases respectively, but the duality point U_d lies outside both Lorentzian regimes and is surrounded by a finite-width intermediate regime where the strength function is non-Lorentzian in both bases.

## Method
The authors perform exact numerical diagonalization of the deformed TBRE Hamiltonian for systems of up to N=3432 basis states (n=7 particles in m=14 orbitals), averaging over 20 to 500 realizations per parameter set and keeping only the middle 33% of the spectrum. They compute the strength function, participation ratios, inverse moments, and structural entropy in both the U=0 and U=∞ eigenstate bases as functions of the interaction strength U, and compare numerical results to Golden rule predictions for the Lorentzian regime widths.

## Key numbers
- The duality point occurs at U_d ≈ 1.11 Δ for m=12, n=5, where the participation ratios in both bases cross at a universal value ξ₂⁽⁰⁾ = ξ₂⁽∞⁾ ≈ 0.8 ξ₂^GOE, independent of n and m.
- The Golden rule width for the U=∞ basis scales as Γ⁽∞⁾ ∝ n(m-n)Δ³/U², confirmed numerically for n ∈ [3,6] and m ∈ [10,14].
- The Lorentzian regime in the U=0 basis requires Δ/√(nN) ≪ U < Δ/√n, while in the U=∞ basis it requires Δ < U < √N Δ.
- The intermediate non-Lorentzian regime is bounded by Δ/√n < U_d < Δ, giving an exponent β ∈ [0.3;0.5] for the n-dependence U_d ∝ Δ n⁻ᵝ, compatible with the Golden rule estimate U_d ∼ Δ/n¹/⁴.
- The structural entropy at the duality point approaches the GOE asymptotic value S_str^GOE = 0.3689.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the crossover from weak to strong interaction in fermionic systems by Aberg (1990), Kusnezov (1996), Berkovits (1999), Waintal (1999), Song (2000), and Sahu (2001), and addresses the reversed crossover from strong to weak interaction. The duality transformation is analogous to that found in one-dimensional quasi-periodic lattices by Aubry (1979) and in tight-binding models for strongly correlated fermions by Florian (1999), but differs fundamentally in that the duality point is protected by a finite-width intermediate regime rather than a sharp transition. The results corroborate the conclusions of Aberg (1990) that the crossover to fully developed chaos in Fock space is smooth rather than a sharp Anderson-like transition, contrary to claims by Altshuler (1997) and Song (2000). The work enables further investigation of quasiparticle excitations in the strong interaction limit and suggests that the duality is specific to two-body interactions, with different scaling expected for k-body interactions with k>2.
