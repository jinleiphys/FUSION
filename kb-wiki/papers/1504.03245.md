# Properties of nuclei in the nobelium region studied within the covariant, Skyrme, and Gogny energy density functionals

> We calculate properties of the ground and excited states of nuclei in the nobelium region for proton and neutron numbers of 92 <= Z <= 104 and 144 <= N <= 156, respectively. We use three different energy-density-functional (EDF) approaches, based on covariant, Skyrme, and Gogny functionals, each within two different parameter sets. A comparative analysis of the results obtained for odd-even mass staggerings, quasiparticle spectra, and moments of inertia allows us to identify single-particle and shell effects that are characteristic to these different models and to illustrate possible systematic uncertainties related to using the EDF modelling

## Key claim
The paper systematically compares six energy-density-functional (EDF) models (covariant NL1 and NL3*, Skyrme SLy4 and UNEDF2, Gogny D1S and D1M) for nuclei in the nobelium region, finding that while overall single-particle level positions and deformation dependence are fairly similar across models, significant differences in deformed shell gaps and spherical shell structure exist, particularly for UNEDF2, which illustrate systematic uncertainties inherent to EDF modelling rather than providing a definitive prediction.

## Method
Ground states of even-even and odd-mass nuclei with proton numbers Z=92–104 and neutron numbers N=144–156 were calculated using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) or relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) methods with full self-consistent blocking for odd-mass nuclei. Low-lying quasiparticle spectra of odd-mass nuclei and low-spin moments of inertia of even-even nuclei were determined, and the results were analyzed for odd-even mass staggerings, two-particle mass staggerings, and excitation energies to identify single-particle and shell effects characteristic to each model.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strengths for SLy4: neutron V_n = -1240 MeV fm^3, proton V_p = -1575 MeV fm^3, adjusted to three-point gaps of ^247Cf (N=149) and ^249Bk (Z=97)
- Pairing strengths for UNEDF2: neutron V_n = -233.889 MeV fm^3, proton V_p = -280.330 MeV fm^3, adjusted to experimental Δ^(3)_n and Δ^(3)_p in ^251Cf and ^249Bk respectively
- For SLy4 calculations: cubic box of 32^3 fm^3 with step size 0.8 fm, 20×20×20 mesh under triaxial symmetry
- For UNEDF2: 680 deformed harmonic-oscillator basis states with frequencies ℏΩ_x = ℏΩ_y = 8.4826549 MeV and ℏΩ_z = 6.4653456 MeV
- For covariant EDFs: basis deformation β_0=0.3, γ=0°, oscillator frequency ℏω_0=41A^(-1/3) MeV, fermionic shells up to N_F=14 and bosonic shells up to N_B=20
- For ^251Cf with SLy4: a low-lying ⟨Ĵ_∥⟩^π = 1/2^+ level at 45 keV excitation energy above the calculated ⟨Ĵ_∥⟩^π = 3/2^+ ground state (with cranking constraint); without constraint the 1/2^+ level is lowered by about 180 keV and becomes ground state
- For SLy4: spherical orbital 1j_15/2^- is lowered by about 2 MeV and 1i_11/2^+ is raised by about 2 MeV in UNEDF2 compared to SLy4

## Context
This work extends systematic comparisons of EDF approaches previously applied to rare-earth and actinide regions, notably the single-particle spectra studies of Afanasiev and Shawaqfeh (2011) using covariant EDFs NL1 and NL3*, and the Skyrme HFB calculations of Bender et al. (2003) and others. The results enable assessment of extrapolation reliability toward superheavy nuclei by quantifying model-dependent uncertainties in deformed shell gaps at Z=98, Z=104, N=150, and N=152, with the notable exception of NL3* showing additional gaps at Z=102 and N=148. The study also highlights the need for improved treatment of pairing and time-odd fields, as indicated by the work of Robledo (2014) on odd-odd systems.
