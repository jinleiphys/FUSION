# Non-trivial effect for the large radius at the dripline of Oxygen isotopes

> The anomalous large radii are exotic phenomena observed around the neutron dripline. Around the neutron dripline, the weak binding of the last bound neutron(s) causes the drastic increase of the radius, which is called neutron halo structure. Although the nucleus $^{24}$O is located at the dripline of Oxygen isotopes, the separation energies of one and two neutron(s) are 4.19 MeV and 6.92 MeV, respectively. In spite of this enough binding, the enhancement of the matter radius is observed. In this study, we microscopically describe the structure change of $^{22}$O core in $^{24}$O and explain the observed large radius based on the cluster model. Two degrees of freedom for the large radius; the relative distances among four $α$ clusters and size of each $α$ cluster are examined, where Tohsaki interaction, which has finite range three-body terms is employed. The nucleus $^{24}$O has the almost the same amount of the clusterization compared with $^{22}$O, but the expansion of each $α$ cluster plays an important role. When two neutrons are added to $^{22}$O at the center, the expansion of each $α$ clusters is energetically more favored than enhancing the clustering for reducing the kinetic energy of the neutrons. The calculated rms matter radius of $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O are 2.75 fm and 2.92 fm, respectively. Although these are slightly smaller than the experimental values, the jump at $^{24}$O from $^{22}$O is reproduced.

## Key claim

The observed large matter radius of $^{24}$O, despite its relatively strong neutron separation energies (4.19 MeV for one neutron and 6.92 MeV for two neutrons), is explained not by enhanced clustering of the four $\alpha$ clusters in the $^{16}$O core but by the expansion of each individual $\alpha$ cluster when two neutrons are added to $^{22}$O at the center.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM) with a tetrahedron configuration of four $\alpha$ clusters for the $^{16}$O core, three di-neutron clusters transformed into quasi clusters for the six valence neutrons (becoming $(d_{5/2})^6$), and one additional di-neutron at the center for the last two neutrons (becoming $(2s)^2$). The Tohsaki interaction (with finite-range three-body terms) was used for the central potential, and the G3RS interaction for the spin-orbit part, with the size parameter $\nu$ and cluster distances $R$ and $R'$ variationally determined.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of $^{24}$O: 4.19 MeV; two-neutron separation energy: 6.92 MeV.
- Calculated rms matter radius of $^{22}$O: 2.75 fm; calculated rms matter radius of $^{24}$O: 2.92 fm.
- Experimental matter radius of $^{22}$O: 2.88 $\pm$ 0.06 fm; experimental matter radius of $^{24}$O: 3.19 $\pm$ 0.13 fm.
- Optimal size parameter $\nu$ for $^{24}$O: 0.16 fm$^{-2}$, corresponding to $b = 1.77$ fm; for $^{22}$O: 0.18 fm$^{-2}$, corresponding to $b = 1.67$ fm.
- Ground state energy of $^{24}$O from generator coordinate method: $-165.80$ MeV (experimental: $-168.97$ MeV).
- Expectation value of the one-body spin-orbit operator $\sum_i {\bf l_i} \cdot {\bf s_i}$ at the shell model limit: 6 (in units of $\hbar^2$) at $\Lambda = 1$, confirming the $(d_{5/2})^6$ configuration.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on the anomalous large radius of $^{24}$O (Ozawa 2001) and the proposal that the $^{22}$O core size increases when two neutrons are added (PhysRevLett.88.142502, PhysRevC.73.034318). It contrasts with the authors' previous findings in Be isotopes (PhysRevC.61.044306, PhysRevC.62.034301), where added neutrons enhanced $\alpha$-$\alpha$ clustering; in Oxygen isotopes, mean-field features dominate and the expansion of individual $\alpha$ clusters is energetically favored. The results enable further microscopic studies of core polarization effects at the neutron dripline.
