# Model-independent femtoscopic Levy imaging for elastic proton-proton scattering

> A model independent Levy expansion method is introduced to describe nearly Levy shaped squared moduli of Fourier transforms. We apply this method to precisely characterize the most recent elastic scattering data of proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV. The results reveal a substructure of protons, which is found to be significantly larger and darker in high energy proton-proton collisions at the TeV scale, as compared to a rather faint and apparently overlooked sub-structure, that we also identify in elastic proton-proton scattering at the ISR energy range of $\sqrt{s} = 23 - 62$ GeV.

# Model-independent femtoscopic Levy imaging for elastic proton-proton scattering

## Key claim

The paper introduces a model-independent Levy expansion method to describe elastic proton-proton scattering data and claims to reveal a substructure inside protons that is significantly larger and darker at LHC energies (13 TeV) compared to a much fainter substructure at ISR energies (23-62 GeV), but the authors caution that the method cannot determine the detailed physical meaning of these observed signatures.

## Method

The authors develop an orthonormal series expansion using Levy polynomials to fit the differential cross-section data of elastic proton-proton scattering, where the squared modulus of the Fourier-transformed elastic scattering amplitude is expanded around a symmetric Levy-stable source distribution. They apply this expansion to TOTEM data at 13 TeV and to earlier ISR data, then use the fitted amplitude to reconstruct the impact parameter dependent shadow (inelasticity) profile function via the eikonal formalism and Glauber's multiple diffraction theory.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, the Levy expansion fit to TOTEM data yields a confidence level CL = 2%, with data spanning 10 orders of magnitude on the vertical scale
- The maximal measured four-momentum transfer at 13 TeV is -t_max approximately 4 GeV^2, corresponding to a minimal spatial resolution of about 0.1 fm
- Substructure total cross-section at ISR energies (23.5 to 62.5 GeV): ranges from 0.24 +/- 0.03 mb at 23.5 GeV to 0.30 +/- 0.03 mb at 62.5 GeV
- Substructure total cross-section at LHC energies: 5.2 +/- 0.7 mb at 7 TeV and 8.9 +/- 0.30 mb at 13 TeV
- Substructure elastic cross-section at LHC energies: 0.24 +/- 0.06 mb at 7 TeV and 0.63 +/- 0.04 mb at 13 TeV
- Levy scale parameter R for substructure at ISR energies: approximately 0.30 to 0.32 fm across all energies
- Levy scale parameter R for substructure at LHC energies: 0.49 +/- 0.01 fm at 7 TeV and 0.51 +/- 0.01 fm at 13 TeV
- At ISR energies, the maximum shadow profile P(b=0) of the substructure is about 0.05

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Csorgo 2018) where a fixed Levy exponent alpha = 0.9 gave statistically acceptable descriptions of elastic pp scattering from 23.5 GeV to 13 TeV. The method unites Glauber's multiple diffraction theory (Glauber 1984) with orthonormal Levy series expansions previously used in Bose-Einstein correlation analysis (Csorgo 1999, Novak 2016). The results are consistent with the Bialas-Bzdak quark-diquark picture of baryons and with AdS/QCD calculations by Brodsky and collaborators (2017), but the authors emphasize the model-independent nature of their imaging and note that other quantitative models could explain the observed substructure signatures.
