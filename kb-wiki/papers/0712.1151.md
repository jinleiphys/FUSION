# Effect of the Coulomb interaction in A(d,p) fragmentation

> In the framework of Glauber-Sitenko model we calculate contribution of Coulomb interaction in cross-section of A(d,p) reaction at high energy and zero angle. It is demonstrated that such effect significantly increases the differential cross section only at peak, where the proton momentum $p$ is near half of the deuteron momentum $p_d$ in lab. frame, $p \sim \frac12 p_d$. The Coulomb interaction do not change the results in the high momentum region, where quark effects should be taken into account.

## Key claim

Within the Glauber-Sitenko model, the Coulomb interaction significantly increases the differential cross section of the A(d,p) reaction at high energy and zero angle only at the peak where the proton momentum p is near half the deuteron momentum p_d in the lab frame (p ~ 1/2 p_d); the Coulomb effect does not change results in the high momentum region where quark effects should be taken into account.

## Method

The authors calculate the Coulomb correction to the inclusive 12C(d,p) break-up cross section by adding Coulomb and screening phase shifts to the strong interaction phase function in the Glauber-Sitenko formalism. They use the Bertocchi-Treleani model modified by a deuteron wave function that includes Pauli principle effects at the constituent quark level (generating effective P-wave components), and evaluate the invariant differential cross section at zero proton angle for a deuteron momentum of 9.1 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Total p-12C cross section used: σ = 340 mb.
- Slope parameter for nucleon-nucleus scattering: β² = 69.3 (GeV/c)².
- For 12C, the harmonic oscillator parameter a₀ = 1.60 fm.
- The Coulomb effect strongly increases the cross section at the peak near q ~ 0 (proton momentum in deuteron rest frame), but becomes negligibly small at q > 100 MeV/c.
- Quark effects are assumed to be significant at q > 200 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the modified Bertocchi-Treleani model of Kobushkin (1998), which described break-up data using a deuteron wave function with Pauli principle effects at the constituent quark level, by now including the Coulomb interaction via the Ahkiezer and Sitenko approach. The results show that Coulomb corrections are confined to the peak region and do not affect the high-momentum region where quark effects (from NN*, N*N, N*N* components) are important, enabling cleaner separation of short-range nuclear structure effects in future analyses.
