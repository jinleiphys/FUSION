# Impact of Dynamical Fermions on Centre Vortex Structure

> This presentation examines the centre vortex structure of Monte-Carlo generated gauge-field configurations using modern visualisation techniques. This time, the manner in which light dynamical fermion degrees of freedom impact the centre-vortex structure is explored. Focusing on the thin vortices identified by plaquettes having a non-trivial centre phase, the vortex structure is illustrated through 3D renderings of oriented plaquettes. The impact of light dynamical fermions is not subtle, changing both the density of vortices and the complexity of the vortex structures observed. The role of vortex branching points in full QCD is highlighted in the survey of results presented.

## Key claim

The impact of light dynamical fermions on centre-vortex structure is "not subtle," changing both the density of vortices and the complexity of the observed vortex structures, with a particular enhancement of branching points in full QCD compared to pure-gauge theory.

## Method

The authors generated Monte-Carlo gauge-field configurations on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice, using a renormalisation-group improved Iwasaki gauge action with \(\beta = 1.90\) for dynamical-fermion ensembles (PACS-CS \((2+1)\)-flavour full-QCD ensembles with non-perturbatively \({\cal O}(a)\)-improved Wilson quarks) and a matched pure-gauge ensemble with \(\beta = 2.58\). They identified centre vortices by fixing to Maximal Centre Gauge, projecting link variables to the centre elements of \(SU(3)\) (the three cube roots of unity), and then locating plaquettes with nontrivial centre charge; the resulting vortex structure was visualised through 3D renderings of oriented plaquettes as jets on the dual lattice.

## Key numbers

- The average number of vortices composing the primary percolating cluster in \(32^2 \times 64\) spatial slices was \(3,277 \pm 156\) for pure-gauge theory and \(5,924 \pm 239\) for full QCD, based on an ensemble of 200 configurations with 32 three-dimensional slices each.
- The lattice spacing for the dynamical-fermion ensemble was \(a = 0.0933\) fm, set using the Sommer parameter with \(r_0 = 0.4921\) fm; the pure-gauge ensemble had a matched spacing of \(a = 0.100\) fm.
- The pion mass for the lightest \(u\)- and \(d\)-quark-mass ensemble was 156 MeV.
- There are \(32^2 \times 64 \times 3 = 196,608\) spatial plaquettes on these lattices, making vortex presence a relatively rare occurrence.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of centre vortices in pure \(SU(3)\) gauge theory, which had shown that vortices underpin confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (t Hooft 1977; Greensite 2003; Langfeld 2003; Trewartha 2015), by introducing light dynamical fermion degrees of freedom via the PACS-CS ensembles (Aoki 2008). The results show that dynamical fermions radically alter the vortex structure, approximately doubling the size of the percolating vortex cluster and increasing the number of branching points (monopole-antimonopole pairs), enabling future quantitative studies of path-length distributions and branching probabilities in full QCD.
