# Generalized Faddeev equations in the AGS form for deuteron stripping with explicit inclusion of target excitations and Coulomb interaction

> Theoretical description of reactions in general, and the theory for $(d,p)$ reactions, in particular, needs to advance into the new century. Here deuteron stripping processes off a target nucleus consisting of ${A}$ nucleons are treated within the framework of the few-body integral equations theory. The generalized Faddeev equations in the AGS form, which take into account the target excitations, with realistic optical potentials provide the most advanced and complete description of the deuteron stripping. The main problem in practical application of such equations is the screening of the Coulomb potential, which works only for light nuclei. In this paper we present a new formulation of the Faddeev equations in the AGS form taking into account the target excitations with explicit inclusion of the Coulomb interaction. By projecting the $(A+2)$-body operators onto target states, matrix three-body integral equations are derived which allow for the incorporation of the excited states of the target nucleons. Using the explicit equations for the partial Coulomb scattering wave functions in the momentum space we present the AGS equations in the Coulomb distorted wave representation without screening procedure. We also use the explicit expression for the off-shell two-body Coulomb scattering $T$-matrix which is needed to calculate the effective potentials in the AGS equations. The integrals containing the off-shell Coulomb T-matrix are regularized to make the obtained equations suitable for calculations. For $NN$ and nucleon-target nuclear interactions we assume the separable potentials what significantly simplifies solution of the AGS equations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new formulation of generalized Faddeev equations in the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form for deuteron stripping that explicitly includes both target excitations and the Coulomb interaction, avoiding the screening procedure that previously limited applicability to light nuclei with charge Z ≤ 20.

## Method

The authors project (A+2)-body operators onto target states to derive matrix three-body integral equations that incorporate excited states of the target nucleus. They use explicit partial Coulomb scattering wave functions in momentum space to present the AGS equations in a Coulomb distorted wave representation without screening, and regularize integrals containing the off-shell Coulomb T-matrix to make the equations suitable for calculations. For NN and nucleon-target nuclear interactions, separable potentials are assumed to simplify solution of the AGS equations.

## Key numbers

- The screening procedure for Coulomb potential previously worked only for targets with charge Z ≤ 20; the new formulation removes this limitation.
- The formalism is developed for deuteron stripping on a target of A nucleons, with the target able to exist in N internal states (ρ = 1, 2, ..., N), where ρ = 1 corresponds to the ground state with ε¹ = 0.
- The sum rule for spectroscopic factors in the three-body model without antisymmetrization is Σ S = 1; with antisymmetrization in isospin formalism it becomes Σ S = A + 1, and separately for neutrons or protons it becomes N_A + 1 or Z_A + 1 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Faddeev/AGS formalism previously developed by Alt et al. (2007) for deuteron stripping on ¹²C at bombarding energies of 4.66, 15, and 56 MeV, which neglected Coulomb interaction except for approximate distortion factors. It also builds on the screening-based AGS calculations by Deltuva et al. (2009) and comparisons with the adiabatic distorted wave approach (ADWA) by Nunes and Filomena Deltuva (2011), which neglected target excitations. The new formulation enables application to deuteron stripping on heavier nuclei, relevant for studying (n,γ) processes on exotic nuclei for nuclear astrophysics.
