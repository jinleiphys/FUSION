# Moments and power corrections of longitudinal and transverse proton structure functions from lattice QCD

> We present a simultaneous extraction of the moments of $F_2$ and $F_L$ structure functions of the proton for a range of photon virtuality, $Q^2$. This is achieved by computing the forward Compton amplitude on the lattice utilizing the second-order Feynman-Hellmann theorem. Our calculations are performed on configurations with two different lattice spacings and volumes, all at the $SU(3)$ symmetric point. We find the moments of $F_{2}$ and $F_{L}$ in good agreement with experiment. Power corrections turn out to be significant. This is the first time the $Q^2$ dependence of the lowest moment of $F_2$ has been quantified.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of the Q² dependence of the lowest moment of the proton structure function F₂, using the forward Compton amplitude computed via the second-order Feynman-Hellmann theorem. The results are obtained at the SU(3) symmetric point with unphysical quark masses (mπ ≈ 420–470 MeV) and without chiral, infinite volume, or continuum extrapolations, yet show good agreement with experimental moments.

## Method
The forward Compton amplitude was computed on the lattice by applying the second-order Feynman-Hellmann theorem to perturbed two-point correlation functions, using vector current insertions on valence quarks only (quark-line connected contributions). The Compton structure functions ℱ₁ and ℱ₂ were isolated from the tensor components T₃₃ and T₀₀+T₃₃, and their ω-dependence was fit simultaneously in a Bayesian framework to extract the first few Mellin moments of F₂ and FL. Calculations were performed on two ensembles with lattice spacings a = 0.074(2) fm and 0.068(3) fm, volumes 32³×64 and 48³×96, at the SU(3) symmetric point with pion masses of approximately 470 MeV and 420 MeV respectively.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacings: a = 0.074(2) fm (β=5.50) and a = 0.068(3) fm (β=5.65)
- Pion masses: mπ ≈ 470 MeV (32³×64 ensemble) and mπ ≈ 420 MeV (48³×96 ensemble)
- Q² range covered: approximately 1 GeV² to 7 GeV²
- For the proton lowest moment of F₂, the asymptotic (leading-twist) value M₂,p⁽²⁾ = 0.135(6) and the power correction coefficient C₂,p⁽²⁾/M_N² = 0.091(11)
- For the uu contribution: M₂,uu⁽²⁾ = 0.268(13), C₂,uu⁽²⁾/M_N² = 0.206(24)
- For the dd contribution: M₂,dd⁽²⁾ = 0.146(7), C₂,dd⁽²⁾/M_N² = 0.024(14)
- For the ud (mixed-flavour) contribution: M₂,ud⁽²⁾ = 0.000(0), C₂,ud⁽²⁾/M_N² = 0.007(3)

## Context
This work extends the Feynman-Hellmann approach previously applied to the F₁ structure function (PhysRevLett.118.242001, PhysRevD.102.114505) to simultaneously extract moments of F₂ and FL. It provides the first lattice quantification of the Q² dependence of the lowest moment of F₂, including significant power corrections up to Q² ≈ 5 GeV² that are larger than anticipated in theoretical estimates (Alekhin 2012, Dasgupta 1996). The results enable future studies to constrain higher-twist effects by comparing direct determinations of FL moments with their twist-2 components, and the methodology can be extended to spin-dependent structure functions and parity-violating weak currents.
