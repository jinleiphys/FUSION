# Finite range and upper branch effects on itinerant ferromagnetism in repulsive Fermi gases: Bethe-Goldstone ladder resummation approach

> We investigate the ferromagnetic transition in repulsive Fermi gases at zero temperature with upper branch and effective range effects. Based on a general effective Lagrangian that reproduces precisely the two-body $s$-wave scattering phase shift, we obtain a nonperturbative expression of the energy density as a function of the polarization by using the Bethe-Goldstone ladder resummation. For hard sphere potential, the predicted critical gas parameter $k_{\rm F}a=0.816$ and the spin susceptibility agree well with the results from fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo calculations. In general, positive and negative effective ranges have opposite effects on the critical gas parameter $k_{\rm F}a$: While a positive effective range reduces the critical gas parameter, a negative effective range increases it. For attractive potential or Feshbach resonance model, the many-body upper branch exhibits an energy maximum at $k_{\rm F}a=α$ with $α=1.34$ from the Bethe-Goldstone ladder resummation, which is qualitatively consistent with experimental results. The many-body T-matrix has a positive-energy pole for $k_{\rm F}a>α$ and it becomes impossible to distinguish the bound state and the scattering state. These positive-energy bound states become occupied and therefore the upper branch reaches an energy maximum at $k_{\rm F}a=α$. In the zero range limit, there exists a narrow window ($0.86<k_{\rm F}a<1.56$) for the ferromagnetic phase. At sufficiently large negative effective range, the ferromagnetic phase disappears. On the other hand, the appearance of positive-energy bound state resonantly enhances the two-body decay rate around $k_{\rm F}a=α$ and may prevent the study of equilibrium phases and ferromagnetism of the upper branch Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ferromagnetic phase transition in repulsive Fermi gases is qualitatively altered by nonperturbative effects beyond second-order perturbation theory: the higher-order contributions change the sign of the coefficient of the nonanalytic term in the Ginzburg-Landau free energy from positive to negative around the critical point, making the transition second-order rather than first-order, but only for the zero-range limit where a narrow window (0.86 < k_F a < 1.56) for the ferromagnetic phase exists, while sufficiently large negative effective range causes the ferromagnetic phase to disappear entirely.

## Method

The author constructed a general effective Lagrangian that reproduces the exact two-body s-wave scattering phase shift for a given interaction potential, then derived a nonperturbative expression for the energy density as a function of spin polarization by resumming the particle-particle ladder diagrams to all orders using the Bethe-Goldstone method. The in-medium particle-particle bubble was decomposed into vacuum and medium parts, with the vacuum part regularized using dimensional regularization with the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme, and the resulting geometric series was summed to obtain the interaction energy density. The dimensionless function f(x) was expressed in terms of the exact phase shift via the function F(s,t) = -1/[t cot delta(t k_F) + (1/pi) R(s,t)], where R(s,t) contains the medium effects.

## Key numbers

- For hard sphere potential, the predicted critical gas parameter is k_F a = 0.816
- The many-body upper branch exhibits an energy maximum at k_F a = alpha with alpha = 1.34 from the Bethe-Goldstone ladder resummation
- In the zero range limit, there exists a narrow window 0.86 < k_F a < 1.56 for the ferromagnetic phase
- The naive mean-field or Hartree-Fock approximation predicts a critical gas parameter k_F a = pi/2
- Second-order perturbation theory predicts a first-order FMPT at k_F a = 1.054
- The perturbative result for chi_0/chi vanishes at k_F a = 1.058
- From the nonperturbative result in the zero range limit, a second-order phase transition occurs at k_F a = 0.858

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Goldstone ladder resummation approach previously applied by He and collaborators to study ferromagnetism in repulsive Fermi gases, and it addresses the theoretical challenge of understanding the many-body upper branch at low temperature and its influence on the ferromagnetic phase transition. The paper contradicts the perturbative prediction of a first-order transition from Belitz, Kirkpatrick, and Vojta (2005) and from second-order calculations by Kanno (1970), finding instead a second-order transition when ladder diagrams are resummed to all orders. The results enable future studies of equilibrium phases of the upper-branch Fermi gas, though the author notes that positive-energy bound states resonantly enhance the two-body decay rate around k_F a = alpha and may prevent experimental study of these phases.
