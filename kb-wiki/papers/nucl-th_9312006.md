# A Quark Model of Lambdabar-Lambda Production of Pbar-P Interactions

> A quark model which includes both scalar and vector contributions to the reaction mechanism (SV quark model) is used in a DWBA calculation of $\bar ΛΛ$ production in $\bar p p$ interractions. Total and differential cross-sections, polarizations, depolarizations, and spin-correlattion coefficients are computed for laboratory momenta from threshold to 1695 MeV/c. The free parameters of the calculation are the scalar and vector strengths, a quark cluster size parameter,and the parameters of the unknown $\bar ΛΛ$ potentials. Good agreement with experiment is found for constructive interference of the scalar and vector terms, and for $\bar ΛΛ$ potentials which differ from those suggested by several authors on the basis of SU(3) arguments. The fit to the data is better than that obtained by other quark models, which use only scalar $or$ vector annihilation terms. The agreement with experiment is also better than that found in meson-exchange models. The recent suggestion[1] that measurement of the depolarization parameter $D_{nn}$ can be used to discriminatebetween meson-exchange and quark models is examined in detail. We conclude that a measurement of $D_{nn}$ will provide a test of which of these models, as presently constructed,is the more appropriate description of strangeness production in the $\bar p p \ rightarrow \bar ΛΛ$ reaction.

## Key claim

The SV quark model, which includes both scalar and vector contributions to the reaction mechanism, provides a better fit to experimental data for $\bar p p \rightarrow \bar \Lambda \Lambda$ than models using only scalar or vector terms alone, and also outperforms meson-exchange models, but only when the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ potentials are allowed to differ from those expected from SU(3) symmetry arguments.

## Method

A distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculation was performed using a quark model with both scalar (``^3P_0") and vector (``^3S_1") terms for $\bar q q$ annihilation and creation. The $\bar p p$ initial-state interaction used the Kohno-Weise potential, while the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ final-state interaction was modeled with a potential of similar form whose parameters were varied in a nine-parameter fit to PS185 data. Minimization programs including Monte Carlo, simplex, gradient, and simulated annealing techniques searched for best-fit values of the scalar and vector strengths, a quark cluster size parameter, and six parameters of the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ potential.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory momenta studied: from threshold to 1695 MeV/c, with data at 1436, 1437, 1445, 1477, 1508, 1546, 1642, and 1695 MeV/c.
- Best global fit achieved with combined scalar and vector terms gave $\chi^2$ per data point of 3.2 for 356 data points.
- The $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ annihilation potential strength $W_{\bar \Lambda \Lambda}^{(0)} = -700$ MeV was chosen to fit the production cross-section.
- The $\bar p p$ imaginary potential parameters: $r_0 = 0.55$ fm, $a = 0.2$ fm, $W_{\bar N N}^{(0)} = -1.2$ GeV.
- The diffuseness parameter $a_w$ of the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ annihilation potential was fixed at 0.01 fm after searches found minima for very small values.
- The vector quark model predicts $D_{nn} = 2/3$ in Born approximation, while meson-exchange predicts $D_{nn} = -1$ in Born approximation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SV quark model (Alberg et al., 1988, 1990) and provides a more complete calculation than earlier quark models by Kohno and Weise (1986) using only vector terms, and Furui and Faessler (1987) using scalar or vector terms separately. It contradicts the SU(3)-based expectations for the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ potential derived from the Nijmegen YN potential (Nagels, Rijken, and de Swart, 1975-1979). The paper examines the suggestion by Haidenbauer et al. (1992) that the depolarization parameter $D_{nn}$ can discriminate between quark and meson-exchange models, and confirms that the difference persists for the SV model. The results enable future tests at higher momenta and for production of $\bar \Sigma \Lambda$, $\bar \Lambda \Sigma$, and $\bar \Sigma \Sigma$ pairs.
