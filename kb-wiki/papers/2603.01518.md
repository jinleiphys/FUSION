# Threshold Cusp Structures in the Presence of Isospin Symmetry Breaking

> We study the behavior of the cusp structures focusing on the isospin-breaking effects. The properties of the near-threshold exotic hadrons are encoded in the shapes of the cusp structures. In hadron scattering, it is often the case that the thresholds of isospin partner channels are located within a narrow energy region. To analyze the scattering in such systems, it is therefore essential to study the cusp structures that emerge at two closely separated thresholds with isospin symmetry breaking. In this study, we propose a practical representation of the scattering amplitude and show that the two neighboring cusp structures are related through the isospin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two neighboring threshold cusp structures in hadron scattering are related through isospin symmetry, and that when isospin-breaking effects are small, the cusps at the $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ thresholds exhibit similar shapes and merge into a single cusp in the isospin-symmetric limit; however, for larger isospin breaking, the sharpness of the two cusps can differ significantly.

## Method

The authors construct a 3x3 scattering amplitude matrix for the $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ coupled-channels system using an isospin-symmetric $K$-matrix with four real parameters, where isospin symmetry breaking enters only through the momentum matrix $\hat{p}$ due to the small threshold energy difference $\Delta_{\Sigma N} \sim 2$ MeV between the $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ channels. They expand the $s$-wave cross section near each threshold in terms of the channel momentum and analyze the slopes determined by the complex scattering lengths $a_i$ and constants $b_i$. Numerical calculations are performed for two cases: a simplified Flatté-type model with a single scattering length $a_{\Sigma N} = -1.0 - i\,0.8$ fm, and a more realistic case using spin-triplet scattering lengths from chiral EFT (NLO19 with cutoff $\Lambda=500$ MeV) taken from Haidenbauer (2019).

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between $\Sigma^+ n$ and $\Sigma^0 p$ thresholds is $\Delta_{\Sigma N} \sim 2$ MeV.
- For the simplified model, the scattering length is set to $a_{\Sigma N} = -1.0 - i\,0.8$ fm, yielding charge-basis scattering lengths $a_{\Sigma^+n} = -0.65 - i\,0.46$ fm and $a_{\Sigma^0p} = -0.26 - i\,0.27$ fm.
- For the chiral EFT case (NLO19, $\Lambda=500$ MeV), the isospin-basis scattering lengths are $a_{1/2} = 0.95 - i\,4.8$ fm and $a_{3/2} = -0.42$ fm, with $C_1 = 2.0$ fm.
- In the chiral EFT case, the isospin-broken charge-basis scattering lengths are $a_{\Sigma^+n} = -0.48 - i\,3.0$ fm and $a_{\Sigma^0p} = 0.52 - i\,0.92$ fm.
- For the chiral EFT case, the sum $a_{\Sigma^+n} + a_{\Sigma^0p} = 0.04 - i\,3.92$ fm differs from the isospin-symmetric $a_{\Sigma N} = 1.4 - i\,4.8$ fm by approximately $\sim 1$ fm.
- The slope parameters for the chiral EFT case are $a_{\Sigma^+n} - b_{\Sigma^+n} = -2.7 - i\,3.0$ fm and $a_{\Sigma^0p} - b_{\Sigma^0p} = -0.54 - i\,0.71$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of threshold cusp structures in exotic hadron physics, building on prior analyses of cusp shapes and scattering lengths by Esposito (2021), LHCb (2021), Baru (2004), Guo (2019), and the authors' own previous work (Sone 2024, 2025). The paper uses the $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ coupled-channels system as a concrete example, with realistic spin-triplet scattering lengths taken from chiral EFT calculations by Haidenbauer (2019, 2021, 2023). The results enable future experimental studies of near-threshold exotic hadrons to interpret cusp structures in the presence of isospin symmetry breaking, particularly in systems where isospin partner thresholds lie within a few MeV of each other.
