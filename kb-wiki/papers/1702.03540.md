# Elliptic flow of $ϕ$-meson at intermediate $p_{T}$: Influence of mass versus quark number

> We have studied elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) of $ϕ$-mesons in the framework of a multi phase transport (AMPT) model at LHC energy. In the realms of AMPT model we observe $ϕ$-mesons at intermediate transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) deviate from the previously observed (at RHIC) particle type grouping of $v_{2}$ according to the number of quark content i.e, baryons and mesons. Recent results from the ALICE Collaboration have shown that $ϕ$-meson and proton $v_{2}$ has a similar trend, possibly indicating that particle type grouping might be due to the mass of the particles and not the quark content. A stronger radial boost at LHC compared to RHIC seems to offer a consistent explanation to such observation. However, recalling that $ϕ$-mesons decouple from the hadronic medium before additional radial flow is build-up in the hadronic phase, similar pattern in $ϕ$-meson and proton $v_{2}$ may not be due to radial flow alone. Our study reveals that models incorporating $ϕ$-meson production from $K\bar{K}$ fusion in the hadronic rescattering phase also predict a comparable magnitude of $ϕ$-meson and proton $v_{2}$ particularly in the intermediate region of $p_{T}$. Whereas, $v_{2}$ of $ϕ$-mesons created in the partonic phase is in agreement with quark-coalescence motivated baryon-meson grouping of hadron $v_{2}$. This observation seems to provide a plausible alternative interpretation for the apparent mass-like behaviour of $ϕ$-meson $v_{2}$. We have also observed a violation of hydrodynamical mass ordering between proton and $ϕ$-meson $v_{2}$ further supporting that $ϕ$-mesons are negligibly affected by the collective radial flow in the hadronic phase due to the small in-medium hadronic interaction cross sections.

# Elliptic flow of φ-meson at intermediate pT: Influence of mass versus quark number

## Key claim

The apparent mass-like behavior of φ-meson elliptic flow (v2) at LHC energies, where φ-meson v2 tracks proton v2 rather than following the baryon-meson grouping expected from quark coalescence, may be explained by φ-meson regeneration via K‾K fusion in the hadronic rescattering phase rather than by radial flow alone. The authors argue that φ-mesons created in the partonic phase via s‾s coalescence do follow the quark-coalescence baryon-meson grouping, and that the observed deviation is a hadronic rescattering effect.

## Method

The authors used the string melting (SM) version of the AMPT (A Multi Phase Transport) model to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, with parton scattering cross sections of 1.5 mb and 3 mb. They calculated v2(pT) for pions, kaons, φ-mesons, and protons in the 20-40% centrality class, varying the hadronic cascade time from 0.6 fm/c to 30 fm/c to isolate the effects of hadronic rescattering. Additionally, they selectively turned off the K‾K → φ production channel in the hadronic phase to distinguish the contributions of partonically-produced ("primordial") φ-mesons from those regenerated hadronically.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross sections used: 1.5 mb and 3 mb, with strong coupling constant αs fixed at 0.33 and Debye screening mass μ tuned to 3.22 fm⁻¹ and 2.265 fm⁻¹ respectively
- Hadronic cascade times varied from 0.6 fm/c (labeled "w/o had. rescatt.") to 30 fm/c (labeled "w/ had. rescatt.")
- Elastic scattering cross sections in the model: φ-meson with nucleons set to 8 mb, φ-meson with other mesons (π, K, ρ) set to 5 mb
- Violation of hydrodynamical mass ordering observed below pT = 1.5 GeV/c, where v2^proton < v2^φ despite m_proton < m_φ
- At intermediate pT > 1.5 GeV/c, v2 of "primordial" φ-mesons (excluding K‾K fusion) coincides with results from calculations with hadronic rescattering turned off

## Context

This work extends the discussion of baryon-meson grouping of v2 at intermediate pT, previously established at RHIC energies via quark coalescence (Adler et al., 2003; Adams et al., 2004), to LHC energies where ALICE results (Abelev et al., 2015) showed φ-meson v2 shifting toward the baryon band. The authors challenge the interpretation that this shift is due to stronger radial boost at LHC (as argued in hybrid model calculations by Heinz et al., 2013), noting that φ-mesons decouple from the hadronic medium before additional radial flow builds up due to their small hadronic interaction cross sections (OZI suppression). The finding that φ-meson regeneration from K‾K fusion in the hadronic phase can produce the apparent mass-like behavior offers an alternative interpretation and suggests that partonically-produced φ-mesons remain consistent with quark-coalescence expectations. The observed violation of hydrodynamical mass ordering between protons and φ-mesons below 1.5 GeV/c, previously predicted by the same AMPT model for RHIC (Chen et al., 2008) and confirmed by RHIC data, could not yet be verified at LHC due to lack of data below 0.9 GeV/c in pT.
