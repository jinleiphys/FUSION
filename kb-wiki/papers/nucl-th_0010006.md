# Unified optical-model approach to low-energy antiproton annihilation on nuclei and to antiprotonic atoms

> A successful unified description of $\bar p$ nuclear interactions near E=0 is achieved using a $\bar p$ optical potential within a folding model, $V_{\rm opt} \sim \bar v * ρ$, where a $\bar p p$ potential $\bar v$ is folded with the nuclear density $ρ$. The potential $\bar v$ fits very well the measured $\bar p p$ annihilation cross sections at low energies ($p_L < 200$ MeV/c) and the $1s$ and $2p$ spin-averaged level shifts and widths for the $\bar p$H atom. The density-folded optical potential $V_{\rm opt}$ reproduces satisfactorily the strong-interaction level shifts and widths over the entire periodic table, for $A > 10$, as well as the few low energy $\bar p$ annihilation cross sections measured on Ne. Both $\bar v$ and $V_{\rm opt}$ are found to be highly absorptive, which leads to a saturation of reaction cross sections in hydrogen and on nuclei. Predictions are made for $\bar p$ annihilation cross sections over the entire periodic table at these very low energies and the systematics of the calculated cross sections as function of $A$, $Z$ and $E$ is discussed and explained in terms of a Coulomb-modified strong-absorption model. Finally, optical potentials which fit simultaneously low-energy $\bar p - ^4$He observables for $E < 0$ as well as for $E > 0$ are used to assess the reliability of extracting Coulomb modified $\bar p$ nuclear scattering lengths directly from the data. The relationship between different kinds of scattering lengths is discussed and previously published systematics of the $\bar p$ nuclear scattering lengths is updated.

## Key claim
A unified optical potential, derived by folding a $\bar p p$ potential with the nuclear density, can simultaneously describe both the strong-interaction level shifts and widths of antiprotonic atoms (for $A > 10$) and low-energy antiproton annihilation cross sections on nuclei, provided the strength of the folded potential is renormalized to about 2/3 of the free $\bar p p$ strength.

## Method
The authors constructed a $\bar p$-nucleus optical potential $V_{\rm opt}$ by folding a complex $\bar p p$ potential $\bar v$ (fitted to $\bar p p$ annihilation cross sections and $\bar p$H atomic data) with the nuclear density $\rho$. This density-folded potential was then used in a Klein-Gordon equation to calculate both the bound-state properties (level shifts and widths) of antiprotonic atoms and the total reaction cross sections for low-energy $\bar p$ scattering on nuclei. The strength of the folded potential was renormalized by a common factor to fit the atomic data for nuclei with $A > 10$.

## Key numbers
- The $\bar p p$ potential (G) has a Gaussian range $a_G = 1.5 \pm 0.15$ fm and a strength parameter $\bar b_0 = -0.15 \pm 0.15 + i 1.8 \pm 0.06$ fm, yielding a combined $\chi^2$ of 3.8 for 6 cross-section and 3 atomic data points.
- The density-folded $\bar p$-nucleus optical potential (F) has a strength parameter $b_0 = -0.1 + i 1.2$ fm, which is 2/3 of the $\bar p p$ strength $\bar b_0$, and fits all $\bar p$ atoms with $A > 10$ with a $\chi^2$ of 2.4 per point.
- For the $\bar p$-$^4$He system, the optical potential (F') yields a Coulomb-modified $s$-wave scattering length $a_0^{(sc)} = 1.851 - i 0.630$ fm, in contrast to the value ${\rm Im}~a_0^{(sc)} = -0.36 \pm 0.03 ({\rm stat.})^{+0.19}_{-0.11} ({\rm syst.})$ fm derived by Protasov et al. (2000) using a scattering-length approximation.
- The average radius $<r>$ for the density-folded potential (F) at $p_L = 37.6$ MeV/c is parameterized as $<r> = 1.840 + 1.120 A^{1/3}$ fm, holding to better than 2% for $p_L \lesssim 100$ MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier optical model studies (Friedman, Gal, and Batty, 2000) by providing a full analysis of the $A$, $Z$, and energy dependence of annihilation cross sections, and by demonstrating that the same potential can cross the $E=0$ borderline between bound atomic states and scattering. It contradicts the claim of Protasov et al. (2000) that the Coulomb-modified $s$-wave scattering length for $\bar p$-$^4$He can be determined in a model-independent way, showing instead that the $p$-wave effective range is important. The results enable reliable predictions of $\bar p$ annihilation cross sections across the periodic table and update the systematics of $\bar p$ nuclear scattering lengths previously published by Batty (1983).
