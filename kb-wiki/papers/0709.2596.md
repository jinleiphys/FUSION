# Chiral symmetry, Confinement and Nuclear Matter properties

> We discuss the possible influence of fundamental QCD properties such as spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and nucleon substructure on nuclear matter properties. We propose a chiral version of the relativistic $σ-ω$ model in which the attractive background scalar field is associated with the chiral invariant field governing the radial fluctuations of the quark condensate. Nuclear matter stability is ensured once the scalar response of the nucleon depending on the quark confinement mechanism is properly incorporated. The needed parameters are estimated from lattice results and a satisfactory description of bulk properties follows, the only really free parameter being the $ωNN$ coupling constant. Pion loops can be also incorporated to obtain in a consistent way the finite density chiral susceptibilities. A good description of the asymmetry energy is obtained once the full rho meson exchange and Fock terms are included.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a chiral version of the relativistic σ-ω model, where the attractive scalar field is identified with the chiral invariant field governing radial fluctuations of the quark condensate, can describe nuclear matter properties only once the scalar response of the nucleon (depending on the quark confinement mechanism) is properly incorporated; without this response, the model fails both QCD tests (nucleon scalar susceptibility) and nuclear matter stability.

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian in which the scalar field s (the fluctuation of the chiral radius around fπ) is identified with the sigma meson of Walecka-type relativistic mean-field models. They incorporate the nucleon scalar response κNS (the second derivative of the nucleon mass with respect to the scalar field) as a phenomenological parameter C, estimated from lattice QCD extrapolations of the nucleon mass. The energy density of symmetric nuclear matter is minimized at Hartree level including the scalar potential, vector ω meson repulsion, and the scalar response; pion loops are then added via RPA ring approximation to compute chiral susceptibilities, and finally relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations include full ρ meson exchange and Fock terms for all mesons (σ, ω, π, ρ) to describe asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate vacuum value: ⟨q̄q⟩vac ≈ -(240 MeV)³
- Pion decay constant: fπ = 94 MeV
- Constituent quark mass from NJL model: MQ ≈ 350 MeV
- Lattice QCD extrapolation parameters for nucleon mass: a₂ ≈ 1.5 GeV⁻¹, a₄ ≈ -0.5 GeV⁻³
- Non-pionic sigma term from lattice analysis: σN^(non-pion) ≈ 29 MeV
- Total sigma term (including pion cloud): σN ≈ 50 MeV
- Sigma mass from lattice comparison: mσ = 800 MeV (used in earlier work) or 850 MeV (used in final fit)
- Dimensionless scalar response parameter from lattice: C = 1.25; best fit for saturation: C ≈ 1 (Hartree) or C = 1.75 (RHF)
- Omega-NN coupling constant: gω = 6.8 (Hartree with improved response) or gω = 6.35 (RHF with VDM rho)
- Incompressibility: K = 270 MeV (Hartree) or K = 242 MeV (RHF)
- Asymmetry energy parameter: aS = 30.2 MeV (RHF with VDM rho, gρ=2.65, κρ=3.7); aS = 45 MeV (strong rho scenario, κρ=6.6)
- Sigma mass drop at 2.5 ρ₀: only about 100 MeV (with scalar response); without response, sigma mass would nearly vanish at that density

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka (1986) by incorporating chiral symmetry and nucleon substructure, addressing the long-standing problem of the chiral status of Walecka theories (Koch and Miller, 1974; Birse and Teller, 2001). It builds on the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and uses lattice QCD extrapolation techniques from Thomas et al. (2004) to constrain the scalar response. The model enables consistent calculation of in-medium chiral susceptibilities and provides a framework for future studies of finite nuclei (especially spin-orbit splitting) and neutron star properties.
