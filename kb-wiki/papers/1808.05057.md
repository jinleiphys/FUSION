# Test of semi-local duality in a large $N_C$ framework

> In this paper we test the semi-local duality based on the method of Ref.[1] for calculating final-state interactions at varying number of colors ($N_C$). We compute the amplitudes by dispersion relations that respect analyticity and coupled channel unitarity, as well as accurately describing experiment. The $N_C$ dependence of the $ππ\toππ$ scattering amplitudes is obtained by comparing these amplitudes to the one of chiral perturbation theory. The semi-local duality is investigated by varying $N_C$. Our results show that the semi-local duality is not violated when $N_C$ is large. At large $N_C$, the contributions of the $f_2(1270)$, the $f_0(980)$ and the $f_0(1370)$ cancel that of the $ρ(770)$ in the finite energy sum rules, while the $f_0(500)$ has almost no effect. This gives further credit to the method developed in Ref.[1] for investigating the $N_C$ dependence of hadron-hadron scattering with final-state interactions. This study is also helpful to understand the structure of the scalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper tests semi-local duality in ππ scattering at large number of colors (N_C) using a dispersive method for final-state interactions, finding that semi-local duality is not violated when N_C is large (up to N_C=180), with the contributions of the f_2(1270), f_0(980), and f_0(1370) canceling that of the ρ(770) in finite energy sum rules, while the f_0(500) has almost no effect.

## Method

The authors compute ππ → ππ scattering amplitudes using dispersion relations with Omnès functions that respect analyticity and coupled channel unitarity, fitting to experimental data up to s=4 GeV² and to chiral perturbation theory amplitudes. The N_C dependence is introduced by scaling the phase according to tan φ(s,N_C) = (3/N_C) tan φ(s) and the polynomial part as P(s,N_C) = (3/N_C) P(s), following the method of Dai (2017). Semi-local duality is tested by computing finite energy sum rule ratios R^I_n(t) and F^{II'}_n(t) for varying N_C from 3 up to 180, comparing resonance contributions to Regge exchange expectations.

## Context

This work extends the method of Dai (2017) for studying N_C dependence of hadron-hadron scattering with final-state interactions to test semi-local duality, following the pioneering work of Peláez (2004, 2006) and Ruiz de Elvira (2011) who tested semi-local duality using unitarized chiral perturbation theory up to N_C=30. The results give further credit to the dispersive method for investigating N_C dependence and are helpful for understanding the structure of scalar mesons, particularly whether the f_0(500) contains a sub-dominant q̄q component as suggested by earlier work.
