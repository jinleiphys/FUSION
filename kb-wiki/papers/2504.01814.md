# Energy Density Functional of Confined Quarks: an Improved Ansatz

> Density Functional Theory (DFT) is a robust framework for modeling interacting many-body systems, including the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter. Many models, however, rely on energy functionals based on assumptions that have not been rigorously validated. We critically analyze a commonly used ansatz for confinement, where the energy functional scales with density as $U \propto n^{\frac{2}{3}}$ . Our findings, derived from a systematic non-local energy functional, reveal that this scaling does not capture the dynamics of confinement. Instead, the energy functional evolves from $n^2$ at low densities to $n$ at high densities, governed by an infrared cutoff. These results suggest that models relying on such assumptions should be revisited to ensure more reliable EoS construction.

# Energy Density Functional of Confined Quarks: an Improved Ansatz

## Key claim
The commonly used ansatz for confinement, where the energy functional scales as \(U \propto n^{2/3}\), is not supported by a systematic non-local energy functional analysis. Instead, the energy functional evolves from \(n^2\) at low densities to \(n\) at high densities, governed by an infrared cutoff \(\mu_{\rm IR}\), suggesting that models relying on the \(n^{2/3}\) scaling should be revisited.

## Method
The authors evaluate a non-local energy functional \(U[\hat{n}] = \int d^3p d^3q \, V(\vec{p}-\vec{q}) \, \hat{n}(\vec{p})\hat{n}(\vec{q})\) using a confining potential \(V(\vec{q}) = 8\pi b / (\vec{q}^{\,4} + \mu_{\rm IR}^4)\), where \(b\) is the string tension and \(\mu_{\rm IR}\) is an infrared regulator. They compute the functional for a degenerate Fermi gas in its ground state, with occupation \(\hat{n}(\vec{k}) = \theta(k_F - |\vec{k}|)\), and derive analytic asymptotic expressions for the integral \(\mathcal{J}\) in the limits \(k_F \ll \mu_{\rm IR}\) (low density) and \(k_F \gg \mu_{\rm IR}\) (high density).

## Key numbers
- At low densities (\(k_F \ll \mu_{\rm IR}\)), the energy functional scales as \(\mathcal{J} \approx b \, k_F^6 / (9 \mu_{\rm IR}^4) \propto b \, n^2 \, \mu_{\rm IR}^{-4}\).
- At high densities (\(k_F \gg \mu_{\rm IR}\)), the energy functional scales as \(\mathcal{J} \approx (\pi+4/3)/12 \, b \, k_F^3 / \mu_{\rm IR} \propto b \, n \, \mu_{\rm IR}^{-1}\).
- The infrared cutoff is set to \(\mu_{\rm IR} \approx 0.15\) GeV, corresponding to a string-breaking scale of approximately 1.3 fm.
- The high-density limit is attained only at exceedingly large densities, highlighting the slow approach to this regime.

## Context
This work critically examines the confinement ansatz proposed by DB01 and used in subsequent studies such as Bastian (2020), Ivanytskyi (2022), and Bastian (2023), which assumed \(U \propto n^{2/3}\) based on dimensional analysis arguments from Ropke (1986). The authors demonstrate that non-local interactions introduce a characteristic scale \(\mu_{\rm IR}\) that alters the density dependence, contradicting the earlier scaling. The findings call for reevaluation of existing dense matter equation of state models that incorporate confinement effects, and point toward Coulomb gauge QCD as a more rigorous framework for connecting confinement to density functionals.
