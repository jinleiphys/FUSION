# Evidence for a high entropy phase in nuclear collisions

> We determine the entropy per baryon content of the central reaction region in terms of the charged particle multiplicity. We study the consistency of our findings with recent data on strange anti-baryon production at 200 GeV A in S -> A collisions (A=200) assuming formation of a central fireball. Hadron gas models which do not invoke strong medium modifications of hadron masses do not provide enough entropy and are inconsistent with the combined experimental results. In contrast the quark-gluon plasma hypothesis explains them naturally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the combined experimental data on charged particle multiplicity and strange anti-baryon production in 200 GeV/A S→A collisions (A~200) are inconsistent with a conventional hadron gas (HG) fireball model, but are naturally explained by the formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with high entropy per baryon. The key hedge is that this conclusion depends on assuming the formation of a central fireball and that the hadron gas models considered do not invoke strong medium modifications of hadron masses.

## Method

The authors relate the observable D_Q (the ratio of net charge multiplicity to total charged multiplicity) to the entropy per baryon (S/B) of the fireball, using a thermal model with chemical potentials for u, d, and s quarks and a strangeness saturation factor γ_s. They compute D_Q and S/B for a hadron gas with zero net strangeness and λ_s=1±0.05 (as required by observed strange baryon/anti-baryon ratios), then compare these predictions to experimental data from EMU05 (D_Q) and WA85 (anti-baryon ratios) for S–Pb and S–W collisions at 200 GeV/A.

## Key numbers

- D_Q (central rapidity, S–Pb, EMU05): 0.088 ± 0.007, implying specific entropy S/B ≈ 50.
- Hadron gas model with μ_B = 340 MeV (from strange baryon analysis) gives D_Q ≈ 0.25, implying S/B ≈ 21.
- QGP model with T = 210 MeV, μ_B = 340 MeV, α_s = 0.6 gives S/QGP/B = 47.
- For S–S collisions at 200 GeV/A: D_Q ≈ 0.065 (25% error), implying S/B = 65–75.
- Measured anti-Lambda/Lambda ratio (S–W, WA85): 0.13 ± 0.03 (central rapidity, m⊥ > 1.7 GeV).
- Hadron gas at T=150 MeV with μ_s=0 requires μ_B ≈ 900 MeV, giving anti-Lambda/Lambda ≈ 0.0003.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Rafelski (1991) showing that the strange particle source has symmetric strangeness phase space, and by Letessier, Tounsi, and Rafelski (1992) on entropy differences between QGP and HG. It directly contradicts the hadron gas interpretation of the NA35 and WA85 data attempted by Davis et al. (1991), who found that a HG at T=170 MeV and μ_B=257 MeV underpredicted the π/K ratio by nearly a factor 2. The results enable future experiments with Au and Pb beams to test the QGP formation hypothesis in baryon-rich environments.
