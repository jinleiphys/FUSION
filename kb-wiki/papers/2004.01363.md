# Existence of higher nodal band states with $α$+$^{48}$Ca cluster structure in $^{52}$Ti

> It is shown that recently observed $α$ cluster states a few MeV above the $α$ threshold energy in $^{52}$Ti correspond to the higher nodal band states with the $α$+$^{48}$Ca cluster structure, i.e. a vibrational mode in which the intercluster relative motion is excited. The existence of the higher nodal states in the $^{48}$Ca core region in addition to the well-known higher nodal states in $^{20}$Ne and $^{44}$Ti reinforces the importance of the concept of vibrational motion due to clustering even in medium-weight nuclei with a $jj$-shell closed core. The higher nodal band and the shell-like ground band in $^{52}$Ti are described in a unified way by a Luneburg lens-like deep local potential due to the Pauli principle, which explains the emergence of backward angle anomaly (anomalous large angle scattering (ALAS)) at low energies, prerainbows at intermediate energies and nuclear rainbows at high energies in $α$+$^{48}$Ca scattering. The existence of a $K=0^-$ $α$ cluster band analog to $^{44}$Ti midway between the ground band and the higher nodal band is inevitably predicted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that three newly observed α cluster states in \(^{52}\)Ti, a few MeV above the α threshold, correspond to the \(N=14\) higher nodal band states of the α+\(^{48}\)Ca cluster structure, but this identification depends on the use of a specific deep local potential that reproduces both the cluster bands and the scattering data across a wide energy range.

## Method

The author performed optical model analyses of α+\(^{48}\)Ca elastic scattering angular distributions at incident energies from 18 to 100 MeV, using a Woods-Saxon squared real potential and imaginary potentials with volume and surface absorption terms. The real potential was then used in bound-state and resonance calculations to determine the energies, widths, intercluster radii, and \(B(E2)\) values for the α+\(^{48}\)Ca cluster bands in \(^{52}\)Ti, with the Pauli principle enforced by excluding states with \(N=2n+L < 12\).

## Key numbers

- The ground band states (\(N=12\)) are calculated at excitation energies: \(0^+\) at 0.0 MeV, \(2^+\) at 0.31 MeV, \(4^+\) at 0.93 MeV, \(6^+\) at 1.83 MeV, \(8^+\) at 2.99 MeV, and \(10^+\) at 4.39 MeV.
- The \(N=14\) higher nodal band states are calculated at excitation energies: \(0^+\) at 4.66 MeV, \(2^+\) at 5.12 MeV, and \(4^+\) at 6.16 MeV, matching the three newly observed states.
- The ratio \(R=(E_x(4^+)-E_x(0^+))/(E_x(2^+)-E_x(0^+)) \simeq 3.4\) for the observed states agrees with the theoretical value of 3.2 for the \(N=14\) band.
- The rotational constant \(k \simeq 57\) keV for the observed states is close to the theoretical \(k = 69\) keV for the \(N=14\) band.
- The intercluster rms radii for the \(N=14\) band states are 5.99 fm (\(0^+\)), 5.95 fm (\(2^+\)), 5.84 fm (\(4^+\)), 5.65 fm (\(6^+\)), and 5.36 fm (\(8^+\)), all larger than the sum of the free α and \(^{48}\)Ca radii (5.15 fm).
- The calculated \(B(E2)\) values for the ground band are 108 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(2^+ \rightarrow 0^+\)), 142 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(4^+ \rightarrow 2^+\)), and 134 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (\(6^+ \rightarrow 4^+\)), compared with experimental values of 86\(^{+5}_{-4}\), 109\(^{+16}_{-13}\), and 100\(^{+7}_{-6}\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\), respectively.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair for the real potential at 29 MeV is \(J_v = 342\) MeV fm\(^3\), comparable to 350 MeV fm\(^3\) for the α+\(^{40}\)Ca system.

## Context

This work extends the α cluster model successfully applied to \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{44}\)Ti (Michel 1986, Michel 1988, Ohkubo 1988) to the medium-weight nucleus \(^{52}\)Ti with a \(jj\)-shell closed \(^{48}\)Ca core. It contradicts the earlier microscopic GCM calculation by Langanke (1982) which used a shallower potential and could not reproduce the ground band. The identification of the \(N=14\) higher nodal band and the prediction of a \(K=0^-\) band (\(N=13\)) midway between the ground band and the higher nodal band enable future experimental searches for the parity-doublet partner, which would further confirm α clustering in this mass region.
