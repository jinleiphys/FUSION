# Nucleon number dependence of the onset of anomalous J/psi suppression and the dynamics of nuclear collisions

> We point out that data on the onset of anomalous J/psi suppression as a function of nucleon numbers A and B could provide information on the dynamics of nuclear interactions. In particular the models of anomalous J/psi suppression by Blaizot and Ollitrault (BO) and by Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi and Satz (KLNS) are based on different assumptions on the dynamics of nuclear collisions and lead to different predictions of the dependence of the onset of anomalous J/psi suppression on nucleon numbers of colliding nuclei. The data on this onset as function of A and B could become a tool for the study of the dynamics of nuclear collisions and bring further evidence on J/psi suppression by new form of hadronic matter, possibly Quark-Gluon Plasma. In particular we propose to study J/ψsuppression in A+Pb interaction with nucleon number of A between 58 and 73 or a bit higher.

# Nucleon number dependence of the onset of anomalous J/psi suppression and the dynamics of nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The onset of anomalous J/psi suppression as a function of the nucleon numbers A and B of colliding nuclei can discriminate between the Blaizot-Ollitrault (BO) and Kharzeev-Lourenco-Nardi-Satz (KLNS) models, provided that the data are taken for A+Pb collisions with A between 57 and 73; the presence or absence of anomalous suppression in that range would respectively support the BO or KLNS picture of high-energy-density formation.

## Method

The authors compare two models of anomalous J/psi suppression, BO, which uses the density of participating nucleons per unit transverse area as the criterion for Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) formation, and KLNS, which uses the interaction density (ratio of nucleon-nucleon collisions to wounded nucleons) as the criterion. They fix the critical thresholds in each model by fitting to NA50 Pb-Pb data at 158 GeV per nucleon, then compute the survival probability of J/psi as a function of transverse energy for various A+Pb systems using a sharp-sphere nuclear model with a Gerschel-Hufner absorption term (sigma_abs = 7 mb) and a step-function suppression for tubes exceeding the critical density.

## Key numbers

- Critical threshold in the BO scheme: kappa_BO,c = 9.75 (dimensionless), obtained by fitting to Pb-Pb data.
- Critical threshold in the KLNS scheme: kappa_K,c = 2.43 (dimensionless), obtained by fitting to Pb-Pb data.
- For the academic S-U onset scenario: kappa_BO,c = 9.312 and kappa_K,c = 2.086.
- The proposed range for the nucleon number A in A+Pb collisions is 57 ≤ A ≤ 73, including nuclei such as Ni-59, Cu-64, and Zn-65.
- The transverse energy is related to the number of wounded nucleons as E_T(b) = 0.325 GeV × N_w(b).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of the BO model (Blaizot and Ollitrault, 1996) and the KLNS model (Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi, and Satz, 1996) for anomalous J/psi suppression, which had previously been tested only in Pb-Pb collisions by the NA50 Collaboration at CERN SPS. The authors show that Pb-Pb data alone cannot distinguish the two schemes, but propose that A+Pb collisions with A in the range 57–73 provide a decisive test. The study uses the Gerschel-Hufner mechanism (Gerschel and Hufner, 1988) for normal nuclear absorption and notes that the Dual Parton Model (Armesto, Capella, and Ferreiro, 1999) suggests a contribution to transverse energy proportional to the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions. A positive result would support the wounded-nucleon picture of energy density, while a null result would favor the interaction-density picture and provide further evidence for J/psi suppression by a new form of hadronic matter, possibly Quark-Gluon Plasma.
