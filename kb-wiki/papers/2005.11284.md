# Model comparison for initial density fluctuations in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Four models for the initial conditions of a fluid dynamic description of high energy heavy ion collisions are analysed and compared. We study expectation values and event-by-event fluctuations in the initial transverse energy density profiles from Pb-Pb collisions. Specifically, introducing a Fourier-Bessel mode expansion for fluctuations, we determine expectation values and two-mode correlation functions of the expansion coefficients. The analytically solveable independent point-sources model is compared to an initial state model based on Glauber theory and two models based on the Color Glass Condensate framework. We find that the large wavelength modes of all investigated models show universal properties for central collisions and also discuss to which extent general properties of initial conditions can be understood analytically.

## Key claim

The large-wavelength (small azimuthal and radial mode number) fluctuations in initial transverse energy density profiles from Pb-Pb collisions show universal properties across four different initial condition models for central collisions, but this universality breaks down for non-central collisions and for shorter-wavelength modes.

## Method

The authors introduce a Fourier-Bessel mode expansion for fluctuations of the initial transverse energy density around an event-averaged background field, using cylindrical Bessel functions with Neumann boundary conditions to define radial modes. They compute expectation values and two-mode correlation functions of the expansion coefficients for four models: the independent point-sources model (IPSM), the TrENTo Monte Carlo Glauber model, a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model in the large-Nc limit, and the Magma variation of CGC. For TrENTo, they generate event ensembles on a 10 x 10 femtometer grid with 0.2 femtometer spacing using parameters tuned to LHC Pb-Pb data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, then convert entropy density to energy density by raising to the 4/3 power and rotate each event by a random reaction-plane angle.

## Key numbers

- TrENTo simulation uses a grid of 10 x 10 femtometer squared with grid spacing 0.2 femtometer
- TrENTo parameters: reduced thickness parameter p = 0, fluctuation parameter k = 1.4, nucleon width sigma = 0.6 femtometer, normalization factor N = 16, inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_inel_NN = 6.4 femtometer squared
- Impact parameters for TrENTo are sampled from 0 to 20 femtometer
- The normalization condition for the ensemble-averaged transverse energy density is integral over transverse plane of <epsilon(r,phi)> = 1
- For the IPSM, the one-point function expectation value for the (m=0, l=1) coefficient is 1/pi

## Context

This work extends the mode-expansion approach for initial condition fluctuations developed in Floerchinger (2014) and Floerchinger (2018) to compare four specific models: the IPSM (Alver 2008, Bhalerao 2006, Bzdak 2013), TrENTo (Moreland 2015), CGC (Albacete 2019), and Magma (Bhalerao 2019, Giacalone 2019). The comparison enables future work using response functions to propagate these initial fluctuations through fluid dynamics, allowing model discrimination from final-state observables without expensive event-by-event simulations. The paper also discusses how non-linear field transformations between entropy and energy density (using the thermodynamic relation delta_epsilon = T * delta_s) affect the comparison of models formulated in different variables.
