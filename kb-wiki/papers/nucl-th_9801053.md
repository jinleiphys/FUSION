# Vector meson propagation in dense matter

> The properties of vector mesons in nuclear matter are discussed. Results for the momentum dependence of the $ρ$-meson self energy in matter due to $ρN$ interactions are presented, and consequences for the production rate of $e^+e^-$ pairs in hot and dense matter are discussed. I also examine the constraints imposed by elementary processes on the widths of $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons in nuclear matter, and conclude that in recent models the in-medium widths of these mesons are overestimated.

# Vector meson propagation in dense matter

## Key claim
The in-medium widths of ρ and ω mesons are overestimated in recent models; constraints from elementary processes like πN → VN imply much smaller in-medium broadening than predicted by models that extrapolate from the photon point to the vector-meson mass shell.

## Method
The ρ-meson self energy in nuclear matter is computed using baryon resonance contributions (N*(1720) and Δ(1905)) in a static approximation, yielding a momentum-dependent self energy that vanishes for a ρ at rest. The low-density theorem is used to relate vector-meson–nucleon forward scattering amplitudes to in-medium self energies, and the imaginary parts of these amplitudes are extracted from experimental data on pion-induced vector-meson production (π⁻p → ωn, π⁻p → ρ⁰n, π⁺p → ρ⁺p) via unitarity and detailed balance.

## Key numbers
- Imaginary part of the ωN scattering length from the model of Klingl et al.: Im a_{ωN→ωN} = 2.1 fm, corresponding to ΔΓ_ω ≃ 390 MeV at nuclear matter density ρ₀.
- Imaginary part of the ωN scattering length extracted from π⁻p → ωn data: Im a_{eff} = 0.03 fm (after Fermi averaging), giving ΔΓ_ω^{(πN)} = 9 MeV at ρ₀.
- Imaginary part of the ρN scattering length from Klingl et al.: Im a_{ρN→ρN} = 1.62 fm, corresponding to ΔΓ_ρ ≃ 300 MeV at ρ₀.
- πN contribution to the ρ width from data: ΔΓ_ρ^{(πN)} = 9–17 MeV at ρ₀, with total (including πΔ channel) estimated at ΔΓ_ρ = 45–85 MeV at ρ₀.
- In the model of Herrmann et al., the ρ width enhancement at ρ₀ is ΔΓ_ρ ≃ 150 MeV.
- The ω-meson mass shift in the revised model of Klingl et al. is ≃ 120 MeV downward, with width enhancement ≃ 30 MeV at ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of vector-meson in-medium properties triggered by the Brown–Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) and QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee, which predicted strong mass reductions. It directly contradicts the large in-medium widths found by Klingl, Kaiser and Weise (1997) and provides constraints that had not been implemented in the many-body calculations of Rapp et al. and Herrmann et al., which attribute the low-mass lepton-pair enhancement observed by the CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations to ρ-meson broadening. The results enable a more reliable determination of vector-meson self energies by requiring models to reproduce pion-induced production data near threshold rather than relying on extrapolations from the photon point.
