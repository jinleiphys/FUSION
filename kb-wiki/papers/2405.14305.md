# High-precision spectroscopy of $^{20}$O benchmarking ab-initio calculations in light nuclei

> The excited states of unstable $^{20}$O were investigated via $γ$-ray spectroscopy following the $^{19}$O$(d,p)^{20}$O reaction at 8 $A$MeV. By exploiting the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method, the lifetime of the 2$^+_2$ and 3$^+_1$ states were firmly established. From the $γ$-ray branching and E2/M1 mixing ratios for transitions deexciting the 2$^+_2$ and 3$^+_1$ states, the B(E2) and B(M1) were determined. Various chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, describing the nuclear properties beyond ground states, along with a standard USDB interaction, were compared with the experimentally obtained data. Such a comparison for a large set of $γ$-ray transition probabilities with the valence space in medium similarity renormalization group ab-initio calculations was performed for the first time in a nucleus far from stability. It was shown that the ab-initio approaches using chiral EFT forces are challenged by detailed high-precision spectroscopic properties of nuclei. The reduced transition probabilities were found to be a very constraining test of the performance of the ab-initio models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-precision measurements of electromagnetic transition probabilities in $^{20}$O provide a very constraining test of ab-initio calculations using chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, but the ab-initio approaches are challenged by these detailed spectroscopic properties and systematically underestimate B(E2) values, likely due to the restriction to the VS-IMSRG(2) level which leaves out many-particle-many-hole correlations.

## Method

The $^{20}$O nucleus was populated via the $^{19}$O$(d,p)^{20}$O direct reaction in inverse kinematics at 8 $A$MeV using a radioactive beam from the SPIRAL1 accelerator at GANIL. The Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM) was employed with a CD$_2$+Au backed target to measure lifetimes of the 2$^+_2$ and 3$^+_1$ states, using triple coincidences between the VAMOS++ magnetic spectrometer, the MUGAST charged-particle array, and the AGATA gamma-ray tracking array. From the measured lifetimes, gamma-ray branching ratios, and E2/M1 mixing ratios, the B(E2) and B(M1) reduced transition probabilities were extracted and compared to valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) ab-initio calculations using three different chiral EFT Hamiltonians (1.8/2.0(EM), N$^3$LO$_{lnl}$, N$^2$LO$_{GO}$) and to standard shell-model calculations with the USDB interaction.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the 2$^+_2$ state: 70(10) fs (70(14) fs for 90% confidence)
- Lifetime of the 3$^+_1$ state: 54(9) fs (54(12) fs for 90% confidence)
- Branching ratios for 2$^+_2$ decays: 0.88(1) to 2$^+_1$ and 0.12(1) to 0$^+_1$
- Branching ratios for 3$^+_1$ decays: 0.28(1) to 2$^+_2$ and 0.72(1) to 2$^+_1$
- Experimental B(E2;2$^+_1 \to 0^+_1$) = 5.9(2) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Experimental B(E2;2$^+_2 \to 0^+_1$) = 1.3(2) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Experimental B(E2;2$^+_2 \to 2^+_1$) = 4(2) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Experimental B(M1;2$^+_2 \to 2^+_1$) = 0.05(2) $\mu_N^2$
- Experimental B(E2;3$^+_1 \to 2^+_1$) = 0.32(7) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Experimental B(M1;3$^+_1 \to 2^+_1$) = 0.016(4) $\mu_N^2$
- Experimental B(E2;3$^+_1 \to 2^+_2$) = 0.7(2) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Experimental B(M1;3$^+_1 \to 2^+_2$) = 0.19(4) $\mu_N^2$
- Mixing ratio for 2$^+_2 \to 2^+_1$ transition: -0.18(8) from Young (1981)

## Context

This work extends previous lifetime measurements of the 2$^+_2$ state in $^{20}$O by Ciemala (2020) which gave 150$^{+80}_{-30}$ fs, finding a shorter lifetime of 70(10) fs with improved feeding-free analysis using the $(d,p)$ reaction. It provides the first comprehensive comparison of a large set of gamma-ray transition probabilities in a nucleus far from stability with VS-IMSRG ab-initio calculations using chiral EFT forces, following earlier work on $^{21}$O by Heil (2020) and studies of three-body forces in oxygen isotopes by Otsuka (2010). The results enable future benchmarking of ab-initio calculations in exotic nuclei at next-generation radioactive beam facilities, with improvements needed such as including meson exchange currents and releasing the VS-IMSRG(2) restriction to incorporate many-particle-many-hole correlations.
