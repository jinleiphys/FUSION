# The role of the elemental nature of A=3 nuclei in neutron-rich nuclei

> The idea of treating the trinucleon systems as elementary entities in the elementary particle model (EPM) as an Effective Field Theory has been a success in explaining the weak charge-changing processes in nuclei. The EPM results are found to be as good as those obtained from nuclear microscopic models using two- and three-body forces. We extend this concept to investigate the validity of the elemental nature of $A=3$ nuclei through studies of nuclear structure of neutron-rich nuclei. By treating neutron-rich nuclei as primarily made up of tritons as its building blocks, we extract one- and two-triton separation energies of these nuclei. Calculations have been performed here within relativistic mean field (RMF) models with latest interactions. Clear evidence arises of a new shell structure with well-defined predictions of new magic nuclei. These unique predictions have been consolidated by standard one- and two-neutron separation energy calculations. The binding energy per nucleon plots of these nuclei also confirm these predictions. We make unambiguos prediction of six magic nuclei: $_{\:\:8}^{24}{\rm O}_{16}$, $_{20}^{60}{\rm Ca}_{40}$, $_{\:\:35}^{105}{\rm Br}_{70}$, $_{\:\:41}^{123}{\rm Nb}_{82}$, $_{\:\:63}^{189}{\rm Eu}_{126}$ and $_{\:\:92}^{276}{\rm U}_{184}$.

## Key claim

By treating neutron-rich nuclei as bound states of tritons (A=3 nuclei) and extracting one- and two-triton separation energies from relativistic mean field calculations, the authors predict six new magic nuclei, but this magicity is an effective manifestation of underlying neutron and proton magic numbers being translated into triton numbers, with the caveat that for lighter nuclei protons dominate while for heavier nuclei neutrons decide the effective magicity.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field (RMF) models with NL3*, NL3, and TM1 interactions to calculate binding energies of N=2Z nuclei for Z ranging from 5 to 120. They extracted one- and two-triton separation energies from these binding energies, treating neutron-rich nuclei as composed of triton clusters, and identified magic numbers from peaks in the separation energy plots. The results were consolidated by standard one- and two-neutron separation energy calculations and binding energy per nucleon plots for the same nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Six magic nuclei predicted: $^{24}_{\:8}{\rm O}_{16}$, $^{60}_{20}{\rm Ca}_{40}$, $^{105}_{\:35}{\rm Br}_{70}$, $^{123}_{\:41}{\rm Nb}_{82}$, $^{189}_{\:63}{\rm Eu}_{126}$, and $^{276}_{\:92}{\rm U}_{184}$
- The inter-triton cluster bond energy for $^{24}{\rm O}$ (8 tritons, 19 bonds) is 5.30 MeV, extracted from experimental binding energy of 100.64 MeV minus 8.48 MeV per triton
- For $^{60}_{20}{\rm Ca}_{40}$, the RMF binding energy is 466.997 MeV with a quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.00180$
- For $^{276}_{\:92}{\rm U}_{184}$, the RMF binding energy is 1951.805 MeV with $\beta_2 = 0.00005$
- The experimental binding energies for N=2Z nuclei are only available up to $^{51}_{17}{\rm Cl}_{34}$ (17 triton bound system)

## Context

This work extends the Elementary Particle Model (EPM) approach of Kim and Primakoff (1965), which treats the ($^3$He, $^3$H) pair as elementary entities, from weak interaction processes to nuclear structure of neutron-rich nuclei. It also builds on the SU$_{\mathcal{A}}$(2) nusospin group proposed by Abbas (2001, 2004, 2005, 2016), which treats the (helion, triton) pair analogously to the (proton, neutron) isospin pair. The prediction of $^{60}_{20}{\rm Ca}_{40}$ as doubly magic agrees with earlier mean field calculations by Terasaki (2006) and Meng (2002), though those found the N=40 shell gap less pronounced than at N=28 or 50. The superheavy prediction of $^{276}_{\:92}{\rm U}_{184}$ with N=184 magicity (associated with Z=92 rather than Z=120 as in other models) enables experimental searches for new shell structure in neutron-rich and superheavy nuclei.
