# On the convergence of multi-channel effective interactions

> A detailed analysis of convergence properties of the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki iteration method, which is used for the calculation of low-momentum effective potentials Vlowk is presented. After summarizing different modifications of the iteration method for one-flavor channel we introduce a simple model in order to study the generalization of the iteration method to multi-flavor channels. The failure of a straightforward generalization is discussed. The introduction of a channel-dependent cutoff cures the conceptual and technical problems. This novel method has already been applied successfully for realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki (ALS) iteration method, which converges for one-flavor channels, fails for multi-flavor channels with different masses because the necessary convergence condition (that the largest P-space eigenvalue be smaller than the smallest Q-space eigenvalue) is violated; introducing a channel-dependent momentum cutoff that truncates each channel’s kinetic energy to the same energy restores convergence.

## Method

The authors constructed a simple solvable two-flavor-channel model with a Yukawa potential and different masses (m1=9, m2=13, dimensionless) to mimic the convergence problems of realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions. They applied the standard ALS iteration and its modified version (with a diagonal shift matrix M) to this model, monitoring the norms of the iteration terms x_n and the decoupling-equation deviation d_n, as well as the evolution of P-space eigenvalues with iteration number. They then introduced a channel-dependent cutoff that equates the kinetic energies of the two channels at the cutoff boundary, recalculated the model eigenvalues, and re-ran the iterations.

## Key numbers

* The model Hamiltonian’s eight exact eigenvalues (with a single momentum cutoff Λ=5) are: 9.55, 14.50, 15.05, 24.04, 13.64, 17.76, 18.20, 25.88 (dimensionless units).
* With the channel-dependent cutoff (Λ1=5 for channel 1, Λ2≈3.1 for channel 2), the eight exact eigenvalues become: 9.55, 14.50, 15.05, 24.04, 12.96, 14.56, 14.88, 20.22 (dimensionless units).
* The mass parameters in the model are m1=9 and m2=13, with a fixed mass m0=8 used to define reduced masses μi = m0 mi/(m0+mi).
* The momentum cutoff for the P-space is ΛP=5 and for the Q-space ΛQ=10 (dimensionless).

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum potential Vlowk approach of Bogner et al. (2003) and the ALS iteration method of Lee (1980), Suzuki (1980), and Andreozzi to multi-flavor channels such as the hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction with strangeness S=-1. The failure of a straightforward generalization is demonstrated and cured by the channel-dependent cutoff, which has already been applied successfully to realistic YN interactions (Wagner et al., 2006). This enables systematic calculations of low-momentum effective interactions for systems like ΛN ↔ ΣN transitions, where a mass difference of about 78 MeV causes convergence problems.
