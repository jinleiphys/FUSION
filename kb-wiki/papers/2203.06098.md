# Complex scaling flows in the quench dynamics of interacting particles

> Many-body systems driven out of equilibrium can exhibit scaling flows of the quantum state. For a sudden quench to resonant interactions between particles we construct a new class of analytical scaling solutions for the time evolved wave function with a complex scale parameter. These solutions determine the exact dynamical scaling of observables such as the pair correlation function, the contact and the fidelity. We give explicit examples of the nonequilibrium dynamics for two trapped fermions or bosons quenched to unitarity, for ideal Bose polarons, and for resonantly interacting, Borromean three-body systems. These solutions reveal universal scaling properties of interacting many-body systems that arise from the buildup of correlations at short times after the quench.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new class of analytical scaling solutions for the time-evolved wave function after a sudden quench to resonant interactions, using a complex scale parameter. The key hedge is that these solutions apply specifically to the short-time dynamics dominated by few-body correlations, before many-body time scales are reached, and are demonstrated for trapped few-particle systems rather than generic many-body systems.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for particles in a harmonic trap after a sudden quench from noninteracting to resonant (unitary) interactions, using the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition for contact interactions. They express the quench dynamics as a complex scaling transformation of a single stationary state with negative energy, solving the Ermakov equation with a complex scale parameter λ(t)=√(e^{2iω₀t}-1). The general solution is derived in hyperspherical coordinates for N particles, where the hyperradial wave function takes the analytical form F(R,t)=e^{-iE₀t}F₀(R)[1-Γ(-s,R²/(e^{2iω₀t}-1))/Γ(-s)].

## Key numbers

- For two particles quenched to unitarity, the contact scales as C(t)=32/(√π ℓ) |sin ω₀t|, reaching maximum C_max=32/√π ℓ at quarter period ω₀t=π/2
- The Loschmidt echo for two particles shows anomalous short-time scaling M(t)=1-(8/3π)|ω₀t|^{3/2}+O(t^{5/2})
- At quarter period ω₀t=π/2, the pair correlation develops a node at r₀≈1.306930 ℓ
- The N-body contact generalizes to C^{(N)}(t)=[2^{2s+1}/(s! s²)] |sin ω₀t|^{2s}, rising as |t|^{2s} for short times
- The wave function overlap deviates from unity as t^{1+s} for short times, with s=1/2 for two particles

## Context

This work extends the scaling dynamics approach of Pitaevskii and Rosch (1997) and Werner and Castin (2006) for harmonically trapped systems with SO(2,1) symmetry, applying it to interaction quenches rather than trap frequency changes. The complex scaling method is reminiscent of the complex scaling technique used by Balslev and Combes (1971) to represent resonances as single states of complex energy. The analytical solutions enable exact calculation of dynamical observables like the contact and fidelity for quenched systems, and the authors show how the short-time dynamics of few-body correlations constrain effective fluid descriptions of strongly correlated quantum gases.
