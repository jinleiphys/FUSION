# Transversity generalized parton distributions for the deuteron

> Transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) appear as scalar functions in the decomposition of off-forward quark-quark and gluon-gluon correlators with a parton helicity flip. For a spin 1 hadron, we find 9 transversity GPDs for both quarks and gluons at leading twist 2. We study these twist-2 chiral odd quark transversity GPDs for the deuteron in a light cone convolution model, based on the impulse approximation, and using the lowest Fock-space state for the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper identifies 9 transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for both quarks and gluons in a spin-1 hadron at leading twist-2, and computes the quark transversity GPDs for the deuteron using a light-cone convolution model based on the impulse approximation, but explicitly leaves phenomenology, polynomiality properties, and gluon transversity GPD calculations for future work.

## Method

The authors decompose the tensor quark-quark and gluon-gluon correlators into independent tensor structures built from available four-vectors, imposing hermiticity, light-front parity, and light-front time-reversal constraints to obtain 9 transversity GPDs for each parton type. They then compute the deuteron quark transversity GPDs using a convolution formalism where the deuteron light-front wave function (including Melosh rotations and S- and D-wave components) is combined with the nucleon chiral-odd quark GPDs in the impulse approximation, with the spectator nucleon treated as on-shell.

## Key numbers

- 9 transversity GPDs are found for both quarks and gluons at leading twist-2 for a spin-1 hadron
- The forward limit of the quark transversity GPD \(H^{qT}_1(x,0,0)\) connects to the collinear parton distribution function \(h_1(x)\)
- The forward limit of the gluon transversity GPD \(H^{gT}_5(x,0,0)\) connects to the structure function \(x\Delta\) from Jaffe (1989) or the collinear pdf \(xh_{1TT}(x)\) from Boer (2016)
- First moments of GPDs \(H^{qT}_i\) for \(i\in\{2,3,5,8\}\) and \(H^{gT}_i\) for \(i\in\{2,3,8\}\) integrate to zero
- The deuteron light-front wave function uses radial components \(\phi_l(k)\) for \(l=0\) (S-wave) and \(l=2\) (D-wave)

## Context

This work extends the study of deuteron GPDs beyond the helicity non-flip sector defined by Berger (2001) and the DVCS/DEMP calculations by Kirchner (2003) and Cano (2003), which left the transversity sector unexplored. The results enable future phenomenological studies of coherent exclusive processes on the deuteron at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provide the foundation for connecting deuteron structure to nucleon structure through sum rules and generalized form factors.
