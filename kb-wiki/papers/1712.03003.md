# Two-body contributions to the effective mass in nuclear effective interactions

> Starting from general expressions of well-chosen symmetric nuclear matter quantities derived for both zero- and finite-range effective theories, we derive the contributions to the effective mass. We first show that, independently of the range, the two-body contribution is enough to describe correctly the saturation mechanism but gives an effective mass value around $m^*/m \simeq 0.4$. Then, we show that the full interaction (by instance, an effective two-body density-dependent term on top of the pure two-body term) is needed to reach the accepted value $m^*/m \simeq 0.7-0.8$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for any effective nuclear interaction containing only two-body terms (zero- or finite-range), the effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter is inevitably close to \(m^*/m \simeq 0.4\) when the saturation point properties are set to their commonly accepted values; a density-dependent term (simulating three-body effects) is required to reach the empirically accepted range \(m^*/m \simeq 0.7-0.8\).

## Method

The authors derived general analytical expressions for the energy per particle, compression modulus, and effective mass at saturation for both zero-range Skyrme-type interactions (up to N3LO) and finite-range interactions (e.g., Gogny and Nakada forms). By taking a specific linear combination of these quantities, they eliminated the leading coupling constants and isolated the effective mass, showing that finite-range effects beyond N2LO contribute negligibly (less than about 0.5% for most interactions). They then added a standard two-body density-dependent term and computed its effect on the effective mass.

## Key numbers

* Pure two-body interactions (e.g., SV, SHZ2, B1, D1) yield \(m^*/m \simeq 0.38-0.48\); for SV specifically: \(m^*/m = 0.383\) with \(k_F = 1.319\) fm\(^{-1}\), \({\cal E}_0 = -16.05\) MeV, \(K_\infty = 306\) MeV.
* For the M3Y-P2-N3LO interaction, the finite-range correction \(\Delta_{FR}\) contributes 0.0126 to \(m/m^*\), changing \(m^*/m\) from 0.4149 to 0.4127 (less than 0.5% effect).
* Including a density-dependent term raises the effective mass to typical values \(m^*/m \simeq 0.7\) (e.g., SIII: 0.763, SkM\(^*\): 0.789, SLy5: 0.697, D1S: 0.697).
* The Weisskopf relation \(m/m^* = 3/2 - (5/2)({\cal E}_0/\varepsilon_F)\) gives \(m^*/m \simeq 0.4\) for \({\cal E}_0 = -16\) MeV and \(k_F = 1.33\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work generalizes the earlier observation by Weisskopf (1957) that a quadratic momentum-dependent mean field forces a low effective mass, and extends it to arbitrary two-body interactions including finite-range forms. It confirms and quantifies the correlation between effective mass, incompressibility, and density-dependence power \(\alpha\) previously noted for Skyrme forces by Chabanat et al. (1997). The results explain why all pure two-body interactions (SV, SHZ2, B1, etc.) give \(m^*/m \approx 0.4\) and justify the necessity of density-dependent terms in modern Skyrme (SIII, SkM\(^*\), SLy5) and Gogny (D1, D1S, D1M) interactions. The derived relation between \(t_3\) and \(\alpha\) provides a constraint for future fitting of effective interactions.
