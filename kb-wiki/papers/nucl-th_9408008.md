# The Spin-Dependent Structure Functions of Nuclei in the Meson-Nucleon Theory

> A theoretical approach to the investigation of spin-dependent structure functions in deep inelastic scattering of polarized leptons off polarized nuclei, based on the effective meson-nucleon theory and operator product expansion method, is proposed and applied to deuteron and $^3He$. The explicit forms of the moments of the deuteron and $^3He$ spin-dependent structure functions are found and numerical estimates of the influence of nuclear structure effects are presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a theoretical framework for calculating spin-dependent structure functions of polarized nuclei (deuteron and $^3$He) using the operator product expansion within an effective meson-nucleon theory, but the approach relies on a perturbative expansion in the meson-nucleon coupling constant and a non-relativistic reduction, meaning the results are valid only within these well-defined approximations and require phenomenological input for the Wilson coefficients.

## Method

The authors extend the operator product expansion method to polarized deep inelastic scattering by including axial twist-two operators constructed from nucleon fields interacting with meson fields via one-boson-exchange interactions. They perform a non-relativistic reduction of these operators up to second order in the pion-nucleon coupling constant, compute the matrix elements for the nucleon ground state using the Tamm-Dancoff method, and then evaluate the moments of the spin-dependent structure functions for the deuteron and $^3$He. The inverse Mellin transform is used to reconstruct the nuclear spin-dependent structure functions in the form of a convolution of the nucleon structure function with effective nucleon distributions in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken sum rule computed from E142 data gives $\int_0^1 (g_1^p - g_1^n) dx = 0.148 \pm 0.022$, compared to the theoretical prediction of $0.187 \pm 0.004$.
- The SMC Collaboration estimated the first moment of the isoscalar nucleon spin structure function as ${\sf M}_n \equiv \int_0^1 g_1^N dx = 0.023 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.015$.
- Combining SMC and EMC data gives the Bjorken sum rule as $\int_0^1 (g_1^p - g_1^n) dx = 0.20 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.04$.
- The EMC collaboration obtained $\int_0^1 g_1^p(x) dx = 0.126 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.015$ for the proton.

## Context

This work extends the meson-nucleon theory approach previously applied to spin-independent structure functions by Kazakov, Umnikov, and collaborators (1990s) to the spin-dependent case, providing a rigorous OPE-based derivation of the convolution formula that includes binding effects. The method enables quantitative estimates of nuclear structure corrections needed for extracting neutron spin structure functions from polarized deuteron and $^3$He targets, which is essential for testing the Bjorken sum rule in experiments at CERN (SMC), SLAC (E142), and DESY. The results are compared with the conventional convolution approach of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (1990s) and with relativistic light cone calculations by Sauer and collaborators (1990s).
