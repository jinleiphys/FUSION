# $Λ(1405)$ mediated triangle singularity in the $K^-d\to pΣ^-$ reaction

> We study for the first time the $pΣ^-\to K^-d$ and $K^-d\to pΣ^-$ reactions close to threshold and show that they are driven by a triangle mechanism, with the $Λ(1405)$, a proton and a neutron as intermediate states, which develops a triangle singularity close to the $\bar{K}d$ threshold. We find that a mechanism involving virtual pion exchange and the $K^-p\toπ^+Σ^-$ amplitude dominates over another one involving kaon exchange and the $K^-p\to K^-p$ amplitude. Moreover, of the two $Λ(1405)$ states, the one with higher mass around $1420$ MeV, gives the largest contribution to the process. We show that the cross section, well within measurable range, is very sensitive to different models that, while reproducing $\bar{K}N$ observables above threshold, provide different extrapolations of the $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes below threshold. The observables of this reaction will provide new constraints on the theoretical models, leading to more reliable extrapolations of the $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes below threshold and to more accurate predictions of the $Λ(1405)$ state of lower mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $K^-d\to p\Sigma^-$ reaction near threshold is driven by a triangle mechanism involving the $\Lambda(1405)$, a proton, and a neutron, and that the cross section is highly sensitive to different theoretical models of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction below threshold. The hedge is that this sensitivity only holds because the reaction probes $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, where models that agree above threshold diverge significantly.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Feynman diagrams for the $p\Sigma^-\to K^-d$ and $K^-d\to p\Sigma^-$ reactions, which involve a triangle loop with a $\Lambda(1405)$, a proton, and a neutron as intermediate states. They compute the amplitudes for two mechanisms: one with virtual pion exchange and the $K^-p\to\pi^+\Sigma^-$ amplitude, and another with kaon exchange and the $K^-p\to K^-p$ amplitude. The cross section is obtained by integrating over the loop momentum and summing over spin transitions, using the deuteron wave function from the Bonn model and form factors for the pseudoscalar exchanges.

## Key numbers

- The triangle singularity is predicted to occur around $\sqrt{s}=2380$ MeV, a few MeV above the $K^-d$ threshold.
- The coupling constant for the deuteron to $pn$ is $g_d = (2\pi)^{(3/2)} 2.67 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1/2}$, with a cutoff $q_\mathrm{max}=240$ MeV.
- The higher-mass $\Lambda(1405)$ pole is at $(1426, 16)$ MeV (mass, half-width), with couplings $g_{\Lambda^*,\bar{K}N} = -2.5 + i\,0.94$ and $g_{\Lambda^*,\pi\Sigma} = 0.42 - i\,1.4$.
- The lower-mass $\Lambda(1405)$ pole is at $(1390, 66)$ MeV, with couplings $g_{\Lambda^*,\bar{K}N} = 1.2 + i\,1.7$ and $g_{\Lambda^*,\pi\Sigma} = -2.5 - i\,1.5$.
- The cross section differences between models reach up to about $100\%$ (factor of two) near threshold, with the Oset-Ramos and Feijoo-Magas-Ramos models giving nearly identical results, while the Ciepl\'y-Smejkal model differs by about $100\%$ and the Roca-Oset model by about $40\%$.
- Using the Bonn or Paris deuteron wave functions reduces the cross section by about $23\%$ compared to the $\theta$-function prescription.

## Context

This work extends the triangle singularity mechanism previously applied to the $pp\to\pi^+d$ reaction (Ikeno et al., 2019) to the $K^-d\to p\Sigma^-$ reaction, where the $\Lambda(1405)$ plays the role of the $\Delta(1232)$. It aims to provide new constraints on theoretical models of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction below threshold, which are currently poorly constrained despite fitting above-threshold data (e.g., models by Oset-Ramos, Roca-Oset, Ciepl\'y-Smejkal, and Feijoo-Magas-Ramos). The reaction could be measured at facilities like DAFNE, J-PARC, or Jefferson Lab, and would help narrow predictions for the lower-mass pole of the $\Lambda(1405)$ and inform studies of $\bar{K}$ bound states in nuclei.
