# Testing of coalescence mechanism in high energy heavy ion collisions using two-particle correlations with identified particle trigger

> In central Au-Au collisions at top RHIC energy, two particle correlation measurements with identified hadron trigger have shown attenuation of near side proton triggered jet-like yield at intermediate transverse momentum ($p{_T}$), 2$< p{_T} <$ 6 GeV/$\it{c}$. The attenuation has been attributed to the anomalous baryon enhancement observed in the single inclusive measurements at the same $p{_T}$ range. The enhancement has been found to be in agreement with the models invoking coalescence of quarks as a mechanism of hadronization. Baryon enhancement has also been observed at LHC in the single inclusive spectra. We study the consequence of such an enhancement on two particle correlations at LHC energy within the framework of A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model that implements quark coalescence as a mode of hadronization. In this paper we have calculated the proton over pion ratio and the near side per trigger yield associated to pion and proton triggers at intermediate $p{_T}$ from String Melting (SM) version of AMPT. Results obtained are contrasted with the AMPT Default (Def.) which does not include coalescence. Baryon enhancement has been observed in AMPT SM at intermediate $p{_T}$. Near side jet-like correlated yield associated to baryon (proton) trigger in the momentum region where baryon generation is enhanced is found to be suppressed as compared to the corresponding yields for the meson (pion) trigger in most central Pb-Pb events. No such effect has been found in the Default version of AMPT.

# Testing of coalescence mechanism in high energy heavy ion collisions using two-particle correlations with identified particle trigger

## Key claim

The near-side jet-like yield associated with proton triggers is suppressed compared to pion triggers in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies when trigger particles are selected from the intermediate transverse momentum region where baryon enhancement is observed, but only in the String Melting version of AMPT that implements quark coalescence; the Default version without coalescence shows no such species-dependent suppression.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model in both its String Melting (SM) version, which implements quark coalescence for hadronization, and its Default version, which uses Lund string fragmentation. They generated Pb-Pb collision events at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and constructed two-particle azimuthal correlation functions with identified pion and proton/antiproton triggers in the intermediate pT range 1.8–3.0 GeV/c and associated particles in 1.0–1.8 GeV/c. The near-side jet-like yield was extracted by subtracting the long-range (flow-modulated) background from the short-range correlation region using the Δη-gap method.

## Key numbers

- Trigger particle pT range: 1.8 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c; associated particle pT range: 1.0 < pT < 1.8 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity acceptance: |η| < 1.0 for all particles
- Parton scattering cross section set to 1.5 mb (αs = 0.33, μ = 3.22 fm⁻¹)
- p/π ratio in AMPT SM reaches maximum at approximately 2 GeV/c in 0–5% most central collisions
- Ratio of proton trigger yield to pion trigger yield (Yp/Yπ) at intermediate pT (1.8–3.0 GeV/c) shows a dip below unity with centrality, while at high pT (3.0–8.0 GeV/c) the ratio remains consistent with unity and independent of centrality
- Yields from Default version are independent of particle species and centrality (multiplied by 1.5 in figure for visibility)

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon enhancement observed in single inclusive measurements at RHIC by PHENIX and STAR (PHENIX 2004, STAR 2014) to two-particle correlation measurements at LHC energies. The authors test whether the coalescence mechanism, which successfully describes the anomalous baryon-to-meson enhancement at intermediate pT, also produces the predicted "trigger dilution" effect in correlation measurements. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental data from LHC and RHIC, and suggest that the difference in jet yield between baryon-hadron and meson-hadron correlations arises from competition between jet-medium interplay and dilution due to quark recombination. The study uses the AMPT model as developed by Lin et al. (2005) and compares with prior PHENIX correlation measurements at √sNN = 200 GeV.
