# Relating Quark Confinement and Chiral Symmetry Breaking in QCD

> We study the relation between quark confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. Using lattice QCD formalism, we analytically express the various "confinement indicators", such as the Polyakov loop, its fluctuations, the Wilson loop, the inter-quark potential and the string tension, in terms of the Dirac eigenmodes. In the Dirac spectral representation, there appears a power of the Dirac eigenvalue $λ_n$ such as $λ_n^{N_t-1}$, which behaves as a reduction factor for small $λ_n$. Consequently, since this reduction factor cannot be cancelled, the low-lying Dirac eigenmodes give negligibly small contribution to the confinement quantities,while they are essential for chiral symmetry breaking. These relations indicate no direct, one-to-one correspondence between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. In other words, there is some independence of quark confinement from chiral symmetry breaking, which can generally lead to different transition temperatures/densities for deconfinement and chiral restoration. We also investigate the Polyakov loop in terms of the eigenmodes of the Wilson, the clover and the domain-wall fermion kernels, respectively, and find the similar results. The independence of quark confinement from chiral symmetry breaking seems to be natural, because confinement is realized independently of quark masses and heavy quarks are also confined even without the chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper analytically shows that low-lying Dirac eigenmodes give negligibly small contribution to confinement indicators such as the Polyakov loop, Wilson loop, and string tension, while these same modes are essential for chiral symmetry breaking, indicating no direct one-to-one correspondence between quark confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. The authors hedge that this independence can generally lead to different transition temperatures or densities for deconfinement and chiral restoration, but does not rule out approximate coincidence in specific regimes.

## Method

Using lattice QCD formalism on a temporally odd-number lattice (where the temporal lattice size \(N_t\) is odd), the authors analytically express the Polyakov loop, its fluctuations, the Wilson loop, the inter-quark potential, and the string tension in terms of Dirac eigenmodes via the functional trace \(\mathrm{Tr}_{c,\gamma}(\hat{U}_4 \hat{\Slash D}^{N_t-1})\). They then numerically evaluate the Dirac spectral sum and compare it to direct lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculations in both confined and deconfined phases for quenched SU(3) gauge theory, also testing the effect of removing low-lying Dirac modes below an infrared cutoff.

## Key numbers

* Polyakov loop \(L_P\) in the confined phase (\(\beta=5.6\), \(10^3\times5\) lattice): real part ranges from \(-0.00807\) to \(0.0139\) across configurations; after removing Dirac modes below \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}}\simeq 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\), the IR-cut value \((L_P)_{\mathrm{IR-cut}}\) matches \(L_P\) to within \(\sim 0.00002\) or better.
* Polyakov loop in the deconfined phase (\(\beta=5.7\), \(10^3\times3\) lattice): real part ranges from \(0.305\) to \(0.337\); after the same IR cutoff, \((L_P)_{\mathrm{IR-cut}}\) matches \(L_P\) to within \(\sim 0.003\) or better.
* Chiral condensate after removing Dirac modes below \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}}\simeq 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in the confined phase: \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle_{\Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}}} / \langle \bar{q}q\rangle \simeq 0.02\) for physical current-quark mass \(m_q \simeq 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), indicating near chiral restoration.
* Polyakov-loop fluctuation ratio \(R_{\mathrm{conf}}(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IRcut}}) \equiv R_A(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IRcut}})/R_A\) remains near 1.0 for all \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IRcut}}\) up to \(\sim 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\) in the confined phase, while the chiral condensate ratio \(R_{\mathrm{chiral}}(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IRcut}})\) drops to near zero over the same range.

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher (1980) identification of low-lying Dirac modes as essential for chiral symmetry breaking, and the lattice QCD studies of the Polyakov loop and deconfinement by several groups (e.g., Aoki et al. 2006, Bazavov et al. 2016). It contradicts the suggestion from QCD-monopole studies (Suganuma et al. 1995, Miyamura 1995, Woloshyn 1995) that confinement and chiral symmetry breaking are simultaneously lost when monopoles are removed, by showing that the two phenomena can be independent. The derived Dirac-mode representation of confinement indicators enables future studies of deconfinement and chiral restoration at finite temperature and density, and can be applied to SU(\(N_c\)) gauge theories with any color number, with or without dynamical quarks.
