# Revisiting the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem in nuclear matter

> An assessment of the magnitude of the rearrangement contribution to the Fermi energy and to the binding energy per particle is carried out in symmetric nuclear matter by extending the G-matrix framework. The restoration of the thermodynamic consistency or, equivalently, the fulfillment of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper assesses the magnitude of the rearrangement contribution to the Fermi energy and binding energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter, showing that including this contribution within an extended G-matrix framework can restore the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, but only if the rearrangement is applied to a narrow region of momenta near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors start from a self-consistent G-matrix calculation using the Bonn potential for the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, solving the G-matrix equation with continuous single-particle energies across the Fermi surface. They then compute the rearrangement contribution to the single-particle energy by performing a functional derivative of the total energy with respect to the momentum distribution, following Landau's theory of Fermi liquids, and evaluate the quantity A(k_F) which encodes the density dependence of the G-matrix elements. Finally, they introduce a parameter k_QP to restrict the rearrangement contribution to a region near the Fermi surface, and adjust this parameter to satisfy the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle from the self-consistent G-matrix calculation: -16.1 MeV, but at a density of 0.25 fm^-3 (k_F = 1.55 fm^-1)
- Violation of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem: 17.2 MeV, with the Fermi energy being -33.3 MeV
- The quantity A(k_F) is well approximated by the linear fit A = 150ρ + 15 MeV fm, where ρ is the density in fm^-3
- Positive rearrangement contribution to the Fermi energy at the minimum of the binding energy: about 25 MeV
- With rearrangement included and k_QP = 0.09 k_F, the binding energy per particle becomes -13.15 MeV at a saturation density of about 0.19 fm^-3 (k_F = 1.41 fm^-1)
- At k_F = 1.41 fm^-1, the rearrangement contribution is about 20 MeV for the Fermi energy and about 1.8 MeV for the binding energy per particle
- Compression modulus predicted with rearrangement: about 150 MeV, compared to about 120 MeV in pure BHF

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework by including rearrangement contributions beyond the mean field, addressing the violation of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem previously noted by Baldo et al. (1990) and Brueckner and Goldman (1960). The results are compared with those of Brueckner et al. (1960), who found about 12 MeV and 1.5 MeV for the Fermi and mean energy rearrangement contributions respectively, and with Bozek (2001), who found about 12 MeV and 1 MeV. The approach enables a thermodynamically consistent treatment of nuclear matter that brings the saturation density closer to the experimental value of 0.17 fm^-3, while also providing a framework for incorporating quasi-particle concepts from Landau's theory into nuclear matter calculations.
