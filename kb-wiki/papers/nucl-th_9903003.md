# Renormalization Group Flow and Equation of State of Quarks and Mesons

> Nonperturbative flow equations within an effective linear sigma model coupled to constituent quarks for two quark flavors are derived and solved. A heat kernel regularization is employed for a renormalization group improved effective potential. We determine the initial values of the coupling constants in the effective potential at zero temperature. Solving the evolution equations with the same initial values at finite temperature in the chiral limit, we find a second order phase transition at T_c \approx 150 MeV. Due to the smooth decoupling of massive modes, we can directly link the low-temperature four-dimensional theory to the three-dimensional high-temperature theory. We calculate the equation of state in the chiral limit and for finite pion masses and determine universal critical exponents.

## Key claim
Using a renormalization-group-improved one-loop effective potential with heat-kernel regularization, the authors find a second-order chiral phase transition at T_c ≈ 150 MeV in the chiral limit, but note that the linear sigma model lacks gluon degrees of freedom and therefore cannot fully model QCD at high temperature.

## Method
The authors derive nonperturbative flow equations for the effective potential of a two-flavor linear sigma model coupled to constituent quarks, using a heat-kernel infrared cutoff function. They solve the evolution equations for the running couplings (mass parameter, quartic coupling, and vacuum expectation value) at zero temperature to fix initial conditions, then extend the flow equations to finite temperature via Matsubara summation. The equation of state is computed both in the chiral limit and for finite pion masses, and critical exponents are extracted from the scaling behavior near the transition.

## Key numbers
- T_c ≈ 150 MeV for the chiral phase transition in the chiral limit (second order)
- Ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 1.2 GeV, with chiral symmetry breaking scale k_χSB ≈ 0.8 GeV
- Initial values at k = Λ: m²_k=Λ = (550 MeV)², λ_k=Λ = 40
- Yukawa coupling fixed to g = 3.2 (constant in k) to yield constituent quark mass ≈ 300 MeV
- Vacuum expectation value at k = 0: φ_k=0 = 93 MeV = f_π

## Context
This work extends the renormalization-group flow approach of Wetterich (1993) to a linear sigma model with quarks, using a heat-kernel regularization that yields analytic threshold functions for the smooth decoupling of massive modes. The method reproduces the chiral perturbation theory results at low temperatures and provides a nonperturbative treatment of the infrared divergences near T_c that plague finite-order perturbation theory. The results enable calculation of the equation of state for two-flavor QCD in a simplified model, though the authors note that the high-temperature behavior lacks gluonic degrees of freedom present in full QCD.
