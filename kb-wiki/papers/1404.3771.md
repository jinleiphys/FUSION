# Near Mass Degeneracy in the Scalar Meson Sector: Implications for $B^*_{(s)0}$ and $B'_{(s)1}$ Mesons

> The empirical observation of near degeneracy of scalar mesons above 1 GeV, namely, the mass of the strange-flavor scalar meson is similar to that of the non-strange one, is at variance with the naive expectation of the quark model. Qualitatively, the approximate mass degeneracy can be understood as a consequence of self-energy effects due to strong coupled channels which will push down the mass of the heavy scalar meson in the strange sector more than that in the non-strange partner. However, it works in the conventional model without heavy quark expansion, but not in the approach of heavy meson chiral perturbation theory as mass degeneracy and the physical masses of $D_{s0}^*$ and $D_0^*$ cannot be accounted for simultaneously. In the heavy quark limit, near mass degeneracy observed in the scalar charm sector will imply the same phenomenon in the $B$ system. We have the prediction $M_{B_0^*}\approx M_{B_{s0}^*}\approx 5715\,{\rm MeV}+δΔ_S$ based on heavy quark symmetry and the leading-order QCD correction, where $δΔ_S$ arises from $1/m_Q$ corrections. A crude estimate indicates that $δΔ_S$ is of order $-35$ MeV or less. We stress that the closeness of $B_{s0}^*$ and $B_0^*$ masses implied by heavy quark symmetry is not spoiled by $1/m_Q$ or QCD corrections. The mass-shift effect on $K_0^*(1430)$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the near mass degeneracy observed in scalar charm mesons (where the strange-flavor $D_{s0}^*$ and non-strange $D_0^*$ have similar masses) should also occur in the bottom sector, leading to the prediction that $B_{s0}^*$ and $B_0^*$ masses are approximately equal. However, this conclusion is conditional: it holds in the conventional model without heavy quark expansion but fails in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HMChPT), where the mass degeneracy and physical masses of $D_{s0}^*$ and $D_0^*$ cannot be accounted for simultaneously.

## Method

The authors calculated self-energy corrections to scalar meson masses from hadronic loops (coupling to two pseudoscalar mesons) using two frameworks: HMChPT and a conventional field theory model with derivative coupling. For the conventional model, they determined the strong coupling constant $g = 0.55 \pm 0.04$ from the measured width of $D_0^*(2400)^0$ and applied renormalization scales of 1.85 GeV for charm and 5.28 GeV for bottom sectors. They then solved the on-shell conditions to obtain physical masses and widths for $B_{s0}^*$, $B_0^*$, $D_{s0}^*$, and $D_0^*$ using bare masses from quark model calculations by Godfrey and Di Pierro.

## Key numbers

- Predicted masses: $M_{B_0^*} \approx M_{B_{s0}^*} \approx 5715\,{\rm MeV} + \delta\Delta_S$, where $\delta\Delta_S$ is of order $-35$ MeV or less (from heavy quark symmetry and leading-order QCD correction)
- In the conventional model with bare masses from Godfrey: $M_{B_{s0}^*} = 5725$ MeV, $M_{B_0^*} = 5723$ MeV, with mass shifts of $-105$ MeV and $-37$ MeV respectively
- In the conventional model with bare masses from Di Pierro: $M_{B_{s0}^*} = 5719$ MeV, $M_{B_0^*} = 5710$ MeV, with mass shifts of $-85$ MeV and $+4$ MeV respectively
- Predicted width of $B_0^*$: 71 MeV (Godfrey bare masses) or 68 MeV (Di Pierro bare masses)
- Measured masses used for comparison: $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ at $2317.8 \pm 0.6$ MeV, $D_0^*(2400)^0$ at $2318 \pm 29$ MeV
- Measured masses of light scalar mesons: $a_0(1450)$ at $1474 \pm 19$ MeV, $K_0^*(1430)$ at $1425 \pm 50$ MeV
- Coupling constant extracted: $g = 0.55 \pm 0.04$ from $D_0^*(2400)^0$ width of $267 \pm 40$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of mass shifts due to hadronic loops in scalar mesons, building on the approach of Guo, Krewald, and Meissner (2009) who showed that self-energy contributions can significantly lower scalar meson masses. The authors find that HMChPT (used by Guo et al.) fails to reproduce the near degeneracy of $D_{s0}^*$ and $D_0^*$ simultaneously, while the conventional model succeeds. The prediction of near-degenerate $B_{s0}^*$ and $B_0^*$ masses provides a testable target for future experimental searches, as these bottom scalar mesons have not yet been observed. The work also discusses the near degeneracy of $K_0^*(1430)$ and $a_0(1450)$ in the light scalar sector, noting that lattice calculations by Mathur (2007) already suggest this unusual SU(3) symmetry.
