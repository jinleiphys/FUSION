# A chiral symmetric relativistic mean field model with logarithmic sigma potential

> We develop a chiral symmetric relativistic mean field model with logarithmic sigma potential derived in the strong coupling limit of the lattice QCD. We find that both of the nuclear matter and finite nuclei are well described in the present model. The normal vacuum is found to have global stability at zero and finite baryon densities, and an equation of state with moderate stiffness (K ~ 280 MeV) is obtained. The binding energies and charge radii of Z closed even-even nuclei are well reproduced in a wide mass range from C to Pb isotopes, except for the underestimates of binding energies in several jj closed nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors develop a chiral symmetric relativistic mean field model using a logarithmic sigma potential derived from the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD, and find that both nuclear matter and finite nuclei are well described, with the normal vacuum having global stability at zero and finite baryon densities, a condition that previous chiral RMF models failed to satisfy without additional repulsive mechanisms.

## Method

The authors construct a flavor SU(2) chirally symmetric RMF Lagrangian containing nucleons coupled to sigma, pion, omega, and rho meson fields, with the sigma potential taken from the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD (a logarithmic term, \(b_\sigma\phi^2 - a_\sigma\log\phi^2 - c_\sigma\sigma\)) plus a phenomenological omega self-interaction term \((\omega_\mu\omega^\mu)^2\). Two parameters (\(g_\omega\) and \(c_\omega\)) are fitted to the nuclear matter saturation point at \((\rho_0, E/A) = (0.145\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}, -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV})\), while the sigma mass \(m_\sigma\) and rho coupling \(g_\rho\) are determined by reproducing binding energies of Sn and Pb isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility: \(K \simeq 279\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(m_\sigma \sim 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (minimum value); the allowed mass region giving \(K < 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) is \(460\ \mathrm{MeV} \lesssim m_\sigma \lesssim 540\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Saturation density and binding energy used for fitting: \(\rho_0 = 0.145\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E/A = -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Sigma expectation value at saturation density: \(\sigma \simeq 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(\rho_B \simeq \rho_0\) in the SCL model.
- Adopted sigma mass for the typical parameter set: \(m_\sigma = 502.63\ \mathrm{MeV}\), giving \(K = 279.14\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to construct chiral RMF models that avoid the "abnormal vacuum" problem, the collapse to a chirally restored state at low density, which plagued the linear sigma model (Lee and Wick, 1974; Lee and Margulies, 1975; Boguta, 1983). Previous solutions included baryon loop renormalization (Boguta and Levinson, 1975), sigma-omega coupling (Boguta, 1983; Ogawa, 2004), higher-order sigma terms (Sahu and Ohnishi, 2000), and glueball fields (Heide, 1994; Furnstahl, 1993, 1995; Mishustin, 1993; Papazoglow, 1997, 1998). The present model derives its logarithmic potential directly from the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD (Kawamoto and Smit, 1981; Nishida, 2004; Kogetsu et al., 2006), providing a QCD-inspired alternative that yields incompressibility and finite-nuclei properties comparable to successful phenomenological models such as NL1, NL3, and TM1.
