# Neutrino-nucleus interactions: open questions and future projects

> We discuss various issues concerning the interactions of nuclei with neutrinos of low impinging energies (i.e. having several tens of MeV to a few hundred MeV) of interest for particle physics, nuclear physics and astrophysics. We focus, in particular, on open questions as well as possible strategies to obtain more experimental information. The option of a low-energy beta-beam facility is extensively discussed. We also mention its potential concerning the neutrino magnetic moment.

# Neutrino-nucleus interactions: open questions and future projects

## Key claim
A low-energy beta-beam facility could address open questions in neutrino-nucleus interactions for neutrinos in the tens-of-MeV to few-hundred-MeV range, but the achievable event rates depend critically on the ion intensities and storage ring parameters that are still under feasibility study. The potential to improve direct limits on the neutrino magnetic moment by almost an order of magnitude is conditional on achieving very intense neutrino sources and very low threshold detectors.

## Method
The paper reviews the current status of neutrino-nucleus interaction measurements and modeling, comparing conventional neutrino sources (pion and muon decay) with the proposed beta-beam approach where accelerated beta-decaying ions produce pure electron neutrino or antineutrino beams. Event rates are calculated using an analytical formula for the boosted neutrino flux integrated over the detector geometry and storage ring parameters, applied to deuteron, oxygen, iron, and lead targets. For the neutrino magnetic moment study, the expected number of neutrino-electron scattering events is computed by averaging the total (weak plus electromagnetic) cross section with the neutrino flux from a static source of beta-beam ions.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino-deuteron reaction theoretical calculations reach the few percent precision, but the quantity $L_{1,A}$ remains unknown.
- For a low-energy beta-beam with $\gamma=14$, using $2 \times 10^{13}~\bar{\nu}$/s (from $^{6}$He decay) and $8 \times 10^{11}~\nu$/s (from $^{18}$Ne decay), the predicted event rates per year on a 35-ton deuteron target are 2363 ($\nu$ + D) and 25779 ($\bar\nu$ + D) for a small ring (L=450 m, D=150 m), and 180 and 1956 respectively for a large ring (L=7 km, D=2.5 km).
- For a 952-ton oxygen target, the predicted rates are 6054 ($\nu$ + $^{16}$O) and 82645 ($\bar\nu$ + $^{16}$O) per year for the small ring, and 734 and 9453 for the large ring.
- For a 250-ton iron target, the predicted rate is 20768 ($\nu$ + $^{56}$Fe) per year for the small ring and 1611 for the large ring.
- For a 360-ton lead target, the predicted rate is 103707 ($\nu$ + $^{208}$Pb) per year for the small ring and 7922 for the large ring.
- Current best direct limits on the neutrino magnetic moment from reactor experiments are $\mu_{\nu} < 1.0-4 \times 10^{-10} \mu_B$ at 90% C.L.
- With $10^{15}$ $^{6}$He/s collected inside a $4\pi$ detector, a vanishing magnetic moment yields about 170 events in the 0.1 MeV to 1 MeV range per year and 3 events in the 1 keV to 10 keV range per year; with a magnetic moment of $5 \times 10^{-11} \mu_B$, these increase to 210 and 55 respectively.

## Context
This review extends the discussion of neutrino-nucleus interactions from the well-studied cases of deuteron (relevant for SNO) and carbon (where a discrepancy between experiment and theory persists) to oxygen, iron, and lead targets needed for next-generation detectors like Hyper-K or UNO. The beta-beam concept, originally proposed by Zucchelli (2002) for high-energy neutrino oscillation studies, is here adapted for low-energy nuclear physics applications, complementing the conventional ORLAND proposal and the high-energy Minerva experiment. The work enables future feasibility studies within the EURISOL project and provides a framework for designing experiments to measure neutrino-nucleus cross sections and potentially improve limits on the neutrino magnetic moment.
