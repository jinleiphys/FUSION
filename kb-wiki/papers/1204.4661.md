# Lifetime Measurements in $^{182,186}$Pt}

> Lifetimes in the yrast bands of the nuclei $^{182,186}$Pt have been measured using the Doppler-shift Recoil Distance technique. The results in both cases {\em viz.} a sharp increase in B(E2) values at very low spins, may be interpreted as resulting from a mixing between two bands of different quadrupole deformations.

## Key claim

A sharp increase in B(E2) values at very low spins in both \(^{182}\text{Pt}\) and \(^{186}\text{Pt}\) may be interpreted as resulting from mixing between two bands of different quadrupole deformations, but the authors emphasize that this two-band mixing interpretation might not be unique and that other models (such as IBA-1) can also reproduce the observed B(E2) values.

## Method

Lifetimes of yrast states were measured using the Doppler-shift Recoil Distance Method (RDM) at the ATLAS facility. The reactions \(^{154}\text{Sm}(^{36}\text{S},4n)^{186}\text{Pt}\) at 167 MeV and \(^{122}\text{Sn}(^{64}\text{Ni},4n)^{182}\text{Pt}\) at 295 MeV were used, with gamma-ray spectra recorded by twelve Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors. Decay curves were fitted simultaneously using the code LIFETIME, with corrections for cascade feeding, detector efficiencies, internal conversion, and relativistic effects.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the 2\(^+\) state in \(^{186}\text{Pt}\) is consistent with the adopted value from Raman et al. (compilation).
- Deformation \(\beta_2\) increases from 0.20 for the 2\(^+\) state to 0.25 for the 6\(^+\) state in \(^{186}\text{Pt}\).
- Deformation \(\beta_2\) increases from 0.19 for the 2\(^+\) state to 0.25 for the 6\(^+\) state in \(^{182}\text{Pt}\).
- Band-mixing interaction V\(_{int}\) = 259 keV for \(^{186}\text{Pt}\) and 237 keV for \(^{182}\text{Pt}\).
- Ground-state band in \(^{186}\text{Pt}\) is composed of 56% B1 (larger deformation) and 44% B2 (smaller deformation).
- Ground-state band in \(^{182}\text{Pt}\) is composed of 66% B1 and 34% B2.
- Measurable lifetime range: \(\sim\)2 ps to \(\geq\)1 ns.
- Target-to-stopper distances ranged from 15 \(\mu\)m (\(^{186}\text{Pt}\)) and 9 \(\mu\)m (\(^{182}\text{Pt}\)) to \(\sim\)10000 \(\mu\)m.

## Context

This work extends previous lifetime measurements in \(^{184}\text{Pt}\) (Garg et al.) to the neighboring isotopes \(^{182}\text{Pt}\) and \(^{186}\text{Pt}\), providing new data for understanding shape coexistence in the light Pt region (Z=78). The results support the band-mixing interpretation proposed by Dracoulis et al. for \(^{176-178}\text{Pt}\) and are consistent with configuration-mixing calculations by García-Ramos et al. (2011). The measurements enable further tests of competing theoretical approaches, including the Interacting Boson Model and the General Collective Model, and motivate future lifetime measurements of non-yrast states to distinguish between models.
