# Investigating Heavy-flavor vs Light-flavor Puzzle with Event Topology and Multiplicity in Proton+Proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8

> Heavy-flavored hadrons are unique probes to study the properties of hot and dense QCD medium produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. Transverse spherocity is one of the event-topology variables used to separate jetty and isotropic events from the pool of event samples. This study aims to understand the production dynamics of heavy-flavors through the transverse momentum spectra, double differential yield and mean transverse momentum of J/$ψ$, $\rm D^{0}$ and $Λ_{c}^{+}$ as a function of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse spherocity. Further to investigate the possibility of hardonization of the charm quarks, transverse spherocity dependence ratios like $Λ_{c}^{+}$/$\rm D^{0}$ and $Λ^{0}$/$K^{-}$ are studied. For the current analysis, the events are generated by using 4C tuned PYTHIA8 for pp at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV, which is quite successful in explaining the heavy-flavor particle production at the LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse spherocity, an event-topology variable, can separate jetty and isotropic events in proton-proton collisions and reveals a "heavy-flavor vs light-flavor puzzle": the baryon-to-meson ratio for heavy-flavor hadrons (Λc+/D0) is higher in jetty events, while the opposite trend holds for light-flavor hadrons (Λ0/K−), suggesting different production dynamics. This conclusion is based on PYTHIA8 simulations with the 4C tune, not on experimental data.

## Method

The authors generated 100 million events for J/ψ and approximately 50 million events each for D0 and Λc+ in proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV using the PYTHIA8 event generator with the 4C tune, including multi-parton interactions and color reconnection. They classified events as jetty (lowest 20% of transverse spherocity distribution) or isotropic (highest 20%) for minimum-bias and six charged-particle multiplicity classes, then analyzed the transverse momentum spectra, self-normalized yields, mean transverse momentum, and baryon-to-meson ratios for J/ψ, D0, and Λc+ as functions of multiplicity and spherocity.

## Key numbers

*   The crossing point where jetty-event production dominates over isotropic-event production for Λc+ and J/ψ is at a lower pT than for D0 in minimum-bias collisions; for high-multiplicity events, the crossing points for all three particles are similar.
*   The relative yield and relative mean transverse momentum of D0, J/ψ, and Λc+ increase with charged-particle multiplicity; for jetty events these quantities are higher than for isotropic events.
*   The relative mean transverse momentum of the studied particles for isotropic events stays systematically below the spherocity-integrated values for low-multiplicity events and approaches the spherocity-integrated values with increasing multiplicity.
*   The Λc+/D0 ratio in jetty events is higher than in isotropic events for minimum-bias pp collisions.
*   The Λ0/K− ratio shows the opposite trend: higher for isotropic events than for jetty events for pT > 4 GeV/c in minimum-bias collisions.

## Context

This work extends studies of event-topology dependence in small systems, following prior use of transverse spherocity for light-flavor particles by ALICE (Acharya 2019) and for heavy-flavor production in PYTHIA8 by Khatun (2019) and Khuntia (2018). The paper provides a PYTHIA8-based baseline for future experimental measurements with the upgraded ALICE ITS3 detector in LHC Run 3 and Run 4, aiming to disentangle hard (jetty) and soft (isotropic) production mechanisms in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions.
