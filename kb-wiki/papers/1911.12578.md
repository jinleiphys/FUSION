# Improved many-body expansions from eigenvector continuation

> Quantum many-body theory has witnessed tremendous progress in various fields, ranging from atomic and solid-state physics to quantum chemistry and nuclear structure. Due to the inherent computational burden linked to the ab initio treatment of microscopic fermionic systems, it is desirable to obtain accurate results through low-order perturbation theory. In atomic nuclei however, effects such as strong short-range repulsion between nucleons can spoil the convergence of the expansion and make the reliability of perturbation theory unclear. Mathematicians have devised an extensive machinery to overcome the problem of divergent expansions by making use of so-called resummation methods. In large-scale many-body applications such schemes are often of limited use since no a priori analytical knowledge of the expansion is available. We present here eigenvector continuation as an alternative resummation tool that is both efficient and reliable because it is based on robust and simple mathematical principles.

## Key claim

Eigenvector continuation (EC) can serve as a non-parametric resummation tool for many-body perturbation theory that yields rapidly converging results even when the underlying perturbative expansion diverges, provided the Hamiltonian depends smoothly on a control parameter and the targeted eigenstate remains in a low-dimensional manifold.

## Method

The authors partition the Hamiltonian as H = H0 + c H1, where c is a scaling parameter, and generate a low-dimensional manifold of auxiliary states by solving the A-body Schrodinger equation for H(c_i) at several values c_i within the radius of convergence of perturbation theory. They then diagonalize the physical Hamiltonian H(1) within this manifold by computing (P+1)x(P+1) Hamiltonian and norm matrices from perturbative state corrections up to order P and solving the resulting secular equation. The method is applied to two nuclear systems: the closed-shell nucleus 3H using standard many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) and the open-shell nucleus 18O using Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) with a symmetry-breaking reference state.

## Key numbers

* For 3H with the unevolved EM500 interaction, the MBPT series diverges beyond P >= 8, while EC provides rapid monotonic convergence toward the exact configuration interaction (CI) result.
* For 3H with SRG-evolved potential at lambda = 2.0 fm^-1, EC and Pade results at P=4 deviate from the CI result by below 5%.
* For 18O with SRG parameter lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 and a truncated harmonic-oscillator basis at e_max = 4, EC based on BMBPT provides sub-percent error relative to the CI result for P >= 1.
* The EC method yields a variational upper bound to the exact ground-state energy at each perturbative order.

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation technique introduced by Frame (2018) and Konig (2019) from simple model systems to realistic nuclear many-body calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions. It directly addresses the convergence problems of many-body perturbation theory that arise from strong short-range repulsion (ultraviolet correlations) and from infrared correlations in open-shell nuclei, problems previously tackled with methods such as coupled-cluster theory (Hagen, 2014), self-consistent Green's function theory (Dickhoff, 2004), and the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama, 2011). The method enables future fully realistic ab initio calculations of mid-mass closed- and open-shell nuclei with two- and three-nucleon interactions, and can be extended to coupled-cluster theory (Ekstrom, 2019) and infinite nuclear matter.
