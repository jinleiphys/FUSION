# The Chiral Quark Condensate and the Bag Constant with Finite Chemical Potential

> A method for obtaining the low chemical potential dependence of the dressed quark propagator from an effective quark-quark interaction model is developed. From this the chemical potential dependence of the chiral quark condensate and bag constant are evaluated. It is found that the chiral order parameter decreases with the increasing chemical potential $μ$, which is qualitatively different from the results given by some authors in the literature. The reason for this discrepancy is discussed.

# The Chiral Quark Condensate and the Bag Constant with Finite Chemical Potential

## Key claim

Within the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) at mean-field level, the chiral quark condensate decreases monotonically with increasing chemical potential μ up to a critical value, contrary to earlier results by Maris, Roberts, and Schmidt (1998) and Bender et al. (1998) which found the condensate increasing with μ before dropping discontinuously. This discrepancy arises because those earlier works assumed a specific functional form for the dressed quark propagator that the present authors argue is insufficiently general.

## Method

The authors start from the GCM Euclidean action at finite chemical potential in the chiral limit, using a Feynman-like gauge ansatz for the dressed gluon propagator with no explicit μ-dependence. They expand the inverse dressed quark propagator to first order in μ around μ=0, obtaining the nonperturbative dressed vertex Γ₄ via an inhomogeneous ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation that satisfies the vector Ward identity. Using two different parameterized model gluon propagators (an exponential infrared form and a rational infrared form, both with a perturbative ultraviolet tail matched to one-loop running), they numerically solve the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equations for the scalar and vector self-energy functions in both the Nambu-Goldstone (B≠0) and Wigner (B≡0) phases, then compute the μ-dependent quark propagator, chiral condensate, and bag constant.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant at zero chemical potential is B(0)^(1/4) = 0.131–0.152 GeV, comparable to the commonly used value (0.145 GeV)^4 in bag-like models.
- The critical chemical potential for the first-order chiral phase transition is μ_c = 270–375 MeV.
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit used to fix model parameters is f_π = 87 MeV.
- The QCD scale parameter is Λ_QCD = 200 MeV, with d = 12/(33-2N_f) = 12/27 for the ultraviolet tail.
- Model parameters for gluon propagator (1): Δ = 0.002–0.200 GeV², χ = 1.45–1.65 GeV; for gluon propagator (2): Δ = 10⁻⁷–10⁻¹ GeV⁴, χ = 0.83–1.83 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the GCM framework of Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Roberts and Williams (1994) to finite chemical potential, building on the approach of Blaschke, Roberts, and Schmidt (1998) for studying chiral symmetry restoration. The authors find qualitatively different behavior of the chiral condensate compared to Maris, Roberts, and Schmidt (1998) and Bender et al. (1998), who used a propagator ansatz depending only on (p₄+iμ)² and |p|². The method enables future studies of the phase transition between confined (Nambu-Goldstone) and deconfined (Wigner) phases, and the results are consistent with those of Weise (1996) and Klevansky (1992) from other approaches.
