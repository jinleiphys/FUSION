# Constraints on Coupling Constants through Charged $Σ$ Photoproduction

> The few available data for the reactions $γp \rightarrow K^{0} Σ^{+}$ and $γn \rightarrow K^{+} Σ^{-}$ are compared to models developed for the processes $γp \rightarrow K^{+} Σ^{0}$ and $γp \rightarrow K^{+} Λ$. It is found that some of these phenomenological models overpredict the measurements by up to a factor of 100. Fitting the data for all of these reactions leads to drastically reduced Born coupling constants.

# Constraints on Coupling Constants through Charged Σ Photoproduction

## Key claim

Existing phenomenological models for kaon photoproduction that were fitted only to the $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ and $K^{+}\Lambda$ channels overpredict the available total cross section data for the charged $\Sigma$ channels ($\gamma p \rightarrow K^{0}\Sigma^{+}$ and $\gamma n \rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{-}$) by up to a factor of 100; including these charged $\Sigma$ data in a global fit forces the Born coupling constants $g_{K\Sigma N}$ and $g_{K\Lambda N}$ to drastically reduced values, making the process resonance-dominated rather than Born-dominated.

## Method

The authors extended existing diagrammatic models for kaon photoproduction to include all four isospin channels of $\Sigma$ photoproduction, using pseudoscalar (PS) coupling for the kaon-nucleon-hyperon vertices. They performed fits to the combined data set of $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ photo- and electroproduction data (86 and 96 points below 2.2 GeV, respectively) together with the few total cross section measurements for $K^{+}\Sigma^{-}$ and $K^{0}\Sigma^{+}$, incorporating Born terms, $\Lambda$ and $K^{*}$ exchange, and the nucleon resonances $S_{11}(1650)$ and $P_{11}(1710)$ along with the $\Delta$ resonances $S_{31}(1900)$ and $P_{31}(1910)$.

## Key numbers

- The model of Bennhold (1989) overpredicts the charged $\Sigma$ cross sections by up to a factor of 100 (two orders of magnitude).
- The SU(3) range for the leading coupling constant in $\Sigma$ electromagnetic production is $0.9 < |g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{4\pi}| < 1.3$.
- Fitting all data simultaneously (set III) yields $g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 0.130$, compared to $g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 1.30$ from fitting only the $K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ data (set II), a reduction by almost a factor of 10.
- The ratio of neutral to charged $K^{*}K\gamma$ transition moments is $|g_{K^{*0}K^{0}\gamma}/g_{K^{*+}K^{+}\gamma}| = 1.53 \pm 0.20$, with the neutral amplitude having opposite sign to the charged amplitude according to the cloudy bag model.
- The measured decay widths are $\Gamma_{K^{*+} \rightarrow K^{+}\gamma} = 50 \pm 5$ keV and $\Gamma_{K^{*0} \rightarrow K^{0}\gamma} = 117 \pm 10$ keV.
- The $\chi^{2}/N$ values for the three fits are: set I (Bennhold 1989) = 3.15, set II (excluding charged $\Sigma$ data) = 2.67, set III (including all data) = 5.30.

## Context

This work extends the analyses of kaon photoproduction that had previously focused almost exclusively on the $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Lambda$ and $\gamma p \rightarrow K^{+}\Sigma^{0}$ reactions (Adelseck, Bennhold, and Wright 1985; Adelseck and Wright 1988; Tanabe, Kohno, and Bennhold 1989; Bennhold 1989; Adelseck and Saghai 1990; Williams, Ji, and Cotanch 1992). The authors show that the charged $\Sigma$ channels provide crucial constraints that were missing from earlier fits, and that the extracted Born coupling constants fall significantly below both the SU(3) predictions and the values obtained from hadronic $YN$ scattering and Nijmegen/Juelich potentials. The results enable future analyses to incorporate all isospin channels, and the authors note that upcoming data from CEBAF (CLAS) and Bonn will be essential to resolve the remaining ambiguities about which resonances dominate the process.
