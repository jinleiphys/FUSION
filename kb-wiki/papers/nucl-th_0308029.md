# Cronin effect vs. geometrical shadowing in d+Au collisions at RHIC

> Multiple initial state parton interactions in p(d)+Au collisions are calculated in a Glauber-Eikonal formalism. The convolution of perturbative QCD parton-nucleon cross sections predicts naturally the competing pattern of low-pT suppression due geometrical shadowing, and a moderate-pT Cronin enhancement of hadron spectra. The formal equivalence to recent classical Yang-Mills calculations is demonstrated, but our approach is shown to be more general in the large x>0.01 domain because it automatically incorporates the finite kinematic constraints of both quark and gluon processes in the fragmentation regions, and accounts for the observed spectra in elementary pp-->π+X processes in the RHIC energy range, sqrt{s} = 20-200 GeV. The Glauber-Eikonal formalism can be used as a baseline to extract the magnitude of dynamical shadowing effects from the experimental data at differente centralities and pseudo-rapidities.

## Key claim

The Glauber-Eikonal formalism predicts a natural competition between low-\(p_T\) suppression from geometrical shadowing and moderate-\(p_T\) Cronin enhancement in d+Au collisions, but the authors note that this baseline cannot a priori distinguish geometrical shadowing from genuine dynamical (non-linear) shadowing, so the formalism is intended as a baseline to extract the magnitude of dynamical shadowing from experimental data.

## Method

The authors compute multiple initial-state parton interactions in p(d)+Au collisions by numerically evaluating the full Glauber-Eikonal series via convolution of perturbative QCD parton-nucleon cross sections, without introducing a phenomenological \(p_T\)-dependent nuclear broadening of intrinsic \(k_T\). They first fit the infrared regulator \(p_0\) and a \(K\)-factor to reproduce \(pp \rightarrow \pi X\) spectra at \(\sqrt{s}=27.4\) GeV and 200 GeV, including a Gaussian intrinsic \(k_T\) smearing with \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.52\) GeV\(^2\), then compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{dAu}\) for \(\pi^0\) production at midrapidity using the same parameters.

## Key numbers

* At \(\sqrt{s}=27.4\) GeV, the fitted infrared regulator is \(p_0 = 0.70 \pm 0.1\) GeV and the \(K\)-factor is \(1.07 \pm 0.02\) for scale choice \(Q_p = Q_h = m_T/2\) with \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.52\) GeV\(^2\).
* At \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the fitted infrared regulator is \(p_0 = 1.0 \pm 0.1\) GeV and the \(K\)-factor is \(0.99 \pm 0.03\) for scale choice \(Q_p = Q_h = m_T/2\) with \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.52\) GeV\(^2\).
* The Cronin ratio for \(\pi^0\) in minimum-bias d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV is compatible with PHENIX data across the whole \(p_T\) range, with a slight overestimate at \(p_T \lesssim 2\) GeV.
* The parton-level Cronin ratio peaks at \(p_T \simeq 6\) GeV, significantly larger than the \(p_T \simeq 3\) GeV found in the MV model of Ref. [GJ03].

## Context

This work extends the traditional Glauber-Eikonal approach, previously evaluated only up to the three-scattering term at \(\sqrt{s} \leq 40\) GeV (e.g., Straub 1992), to RHIC energies by numerically summing the full multiple-scattering series. It contrasts with approximated GE models (e.g., Vitev 2003) that add a random Gaussian kick and with classical Yang-Mills/McLerran-Venugopalan models (e.g., Gyulassy 2003) that ignore finite-\(x>0.01\) kinematics and cannot reproduce the absolute \(pp\) spectra. The formalism enables extraction of dynamical shadowing from RHIC data by providing a unitarity-conserving baseline that isolates geometrical shadowing.
