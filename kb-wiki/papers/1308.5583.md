# Mass dependence of nuclear short- range correlations and the EMC effect

> We sketch an approximate method to quantify the number of correlated pairs in any nucleus $A$. It is based on counting independent-particle model (IPM) nucleon-nucleon pairs in a relative $S$-state with no radial excitation. We show that IPM pairs with those quantum numbers are most prone to short-range correlations and are at the origin of the high-momentum tail of the nuclear momentum distributions. Our method allows to compute the $a_2$ ratios extracted from inclusive electron scattering. Furthermore, our results reproduce the observed linear correlation between the number of correlated pairs and the magnitude of the EMC effect. We show that the width of the pair center-of-mass distribution in exclusive two-nucleon knockout yields information on the quantum numbers of the pairs.

## Key claim

The number of short-range correlated pairs in any nucleus can be approximately quantified by counting independent-particle model (IPM) nucleon-nucleon pairs in a relative S-state with no radial excitation (n=0, l=0), and this counting reproduces the observed linear correlation between correlated pair abundance and the magnitude of the EMC effect, though the calculations tend to overestimate the measured a2 ratios at high A.

## Method

The authors start from a harmonic oscillator basis for the Slater determinant of an independent-particle model wave function, then transform two-particle wave functions to relative and center-of-mass coordinates using Talmi-Moshinsky brackets. They identify IPM pairs with relative quantum numbers (n=0, l=0) as those most prone to short-range correlations, since central and tensor correlation operators project onto pairs with substantial close-proximity probability. For a2 ratios, they count only proton-neutron pairs in a spin triplet (S=1) state; for the EMC correlation, they count all pp, np, and nn pairs.

## Key numbers

- The width of the center-of-mass distribution for l=0 pp pairs in 12C is 154 MeV, compared to 140 MeV for all l values (a difference of 14 MeV wider for close-proximity pairs)
- The a2 ratios are computed for nuclei relative to 4He and compared to experimental data from Hen (2012); calculations agree for light and medium nuclei but overestimate at high A
- The EMC effect magnitude (quantified by slope -dR/dxB for Bjorken 0.35 < xB < 0.7) shows a linear correlation with the computed number of correlated NN pairs per nucleon relative to the deuteron

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approximate method (Vanhalst 2011, 2012) for quantifying correlated pairs in nuclei, building on the correlation operator approach of Pieper (1992). It addresses the experimentally observed linear correlation between the EMC effect and inclusive electron scattering ratios (Weinstein 2010, Hen 2013), suggesting both phenomena share a common origin in high-density or high-virtuality fluctuations. The method enables parameter-free predictions of the EMC effect for any nucleus A and provides testable predictions for the center-of-mass momentum distribution width in exclusive A(e,e'NN) reactions.
