# Effective Field Theory and the Gamow Shell Model: The 6He Halo Nucleus

> We combine Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory (H/CEFT) and the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) to describe the $0^+$ ground state of $\rm{^6He}$ as a three-body halo system. We use two-body interactions for the neutron-alpha particle and two-neutron pairs obtained from H/CEFT at leading order, with parameters determined from scattering in the p$_{3/2}$ and s$_0$ channels, respectively. The three-body dynamics of the system is solved using the GSM formalism, where the continuum states are incorporated in the shell model valence space. We find that in the absence of three-body forces the system collapses, since the binding energy of the ground state diverges as cutoffs are increased. We show that addition at leading order of a three-body force with a single parameter is sufficient for proper renormalization and to fix the binding energy to its experimental value.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a leading-order Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory description of the 6He ground state as a three-body n+n+alpha system requires a three-body force for proper renormalization; without it, the ground-state binding energy diverges as cutoffs are increased, indicating a collapse analogous to the Thomas effect.

## Method

The authors combined Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory (H/CEFT) at leading order with the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for the 0+ ground state of 6He. Two-body interactions for the neutron-alpha (p3/2 channel) and neutron-neutron (1s0 channel) pairs were derived from H/CEFT with parameters fixed by effective range expansions, and the three-body dynamics was solved using a Berggren basis of single-particle states generated by the n-alpha potential, with the GSM formalism incorporating continuum states in the valence space.

## Key numbers

- The n-alpha p3/2 resonance momentum from the effective range expansion is k_res = (0.174824 - 0.031319 i) fm^-1
- The n-alpha scattering volume is a_nα = -62.951 fm^3 and the effective momentum is r_nα = -0.8819 fm^-1
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a_nn = -18.7 fm
- The experimental 6He ground-state energy is E_6gs = -0.98 MeV
- At cutoff Λ_nn = 1.6 fm^-1, the ground-state energy goes from -0.034 MeV at Λ_nα = 2.1 fm^-1 to -0.475 MeV at Λ_nα = 6.1 fm^-1 to -0.400 MeV at Λ_nα = 12.1 fm^-1
- At cutoff Λ_nn = 2.5 fm^-1, the energy goes from -0.182 MeV to -2.251 MeV to -2.524 MeV over the same Λ_nα range
- The three-body force coupling constant D(Λ_nnα) crosses zero at cutoff Λ_0 ≈ 2.9 fm^-1, where two-body-only calculations reproduce the experimental binding energy

## Context

This work extends H/CEFT, previously applied to two-body systems like n-alpha scattering by Bertulani et al., to the three-body halo nucleus 6He. It builds on prior GSM calculations of helium isotopes by Michel et al. using phenomenological potentials, and on EFT studies of three-body systems with s-wave interactions by Bedaque et al. that demonstrated the necessity of three-body forces for renormalization-group invariance. The results enable systematic, model-independent calculations of other halo nuclei and open the question of whether Efimov-like states can exist in systems with p-wave interactions.
