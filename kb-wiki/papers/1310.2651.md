# Explanation and Prediction of Observables using Continuum Strong QCD

> The last five years have brought considerable progress in the study of the bound-state problem in continuum quantum field theory. We highlight a subset of that progress; viz., that made within the context of Dyson Schwinger equation analyses of cold, sparse hadrons. Our focus is primarily on advances in the reliable computation, explanation and prediction of quantities that are truly measurable; but we also review aspects of a new paradigm that has condensates contained within hadrons, and explain that the asymptotic form of parton distribution amplitudes and functions are practically unreachable with terrestrial facilities. Given the pace of expansion in experiment and improvement in theory, it appears possible that the next five years will bring profound growth in our store of knowledge about hadrons and nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) on heavy nuclei can reveal the flavour dependence of the EMC effect in the valence quark region, and that the existence of a zero in the proton's electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio is causally connected to the rate at which dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is active, with the zero disappearing if the chiral condensate falls to roughly 80% of its unperturbed value.

## Method

The authors used the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute nuclear-matter quark distribution functions, incorporating isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, and isovector-vector mean fields whose strengths were determined by fitting the empirical saturation energy and density of symmetric nuclear matter and the nuclear matter symmetry energy. For the proton form factor calculation, they introduced a damping factor α into the dressed-quark propagator to accelerate the transition between constituent-like and parton-like behaviour, then solved the Faddeev equation and computed the proton's elastic form factors using a dressed-quark-photon vertex that includes a momentum-dependent anomalous magnetic moment distribution.

## Key numbers

- The isovector mean field leads to flavour-dependent effects in nuclei like lead and gold that are ≥ 5% in the valence quark region
- For α=1, the ratio μ_p G_E/G_M exhibits a zero at Q² ≈ 8 GeV²
- When the chiral condensate falls to roughly 80% of its unperturbed value, the zero disappears from the computed result on Q² > 0
- The nucleon mass drops by less than 1% as α is increased from 1.0 to 2.0
- The weak mixing angle value used is sin²θ_W = 0.2227 ± 0.004 (on-shell renormalisation scheme)
- For N > Z nuclei, R^γ < R^{γZ} on the domain x_A ≥ 0.3

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach to nuclear structure by Cloet et al. (2009, 2012) and provides a framework for interpreting the NuTeV measurement of sin²θ_W by Zeller et al. (2001). The proposed PVDIS measurements at JLab 12 would complement alternative methods to access quark substructure of nuclei, such as measurement of the EMC effect for spin structure functions by Cloet et al. (2005, 2006), and would provide a timely test of models that aim to describe the modification of nucleon structure functions in-medium, while charged-current reactions at an electron-ion collider (Thomas, 2009) or Drell-Yan reactions (Dutta, 2010; Chang, 2011) will not be possible for at least ten to twenty years.
