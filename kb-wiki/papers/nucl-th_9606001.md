# Baryon Stopping and Strangeness Production in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The stopping behaviour of baryons in massive heavy ion collisions (at SPS, RHIC and LHC) is investigated within different microscopic models. At SPS-energies the predictions range from full stopping to virtually total transparency. Experimental data are indicating strong stopping. The initial baryo-chemical potentials and temperatures at collider energies and their impact on the formation probability of strange baryon clusters and strangelets are discussed.

# Baryon Stopping and Strangeness Production in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper argues that at SPS energies, experimental data indicate strong baryon stopping, ruling out early models that predicted transparency; however, at RHIC and LHC energies, a finite net-baryon number at midrapidity may persist even in very central collisions, and this residual baryon density could enable the formation of strange baryon clusters and strangelets through a strangeness separation mechanism during QGP hadronization.

## Method
The authors use the FRITIOF 7.02 event generator to calculate rapidity distributions of net-baryons, hyperons, and pions for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (200 AGeV/c), RHIC, and LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 6.5 TeV). They compare these predictions with results from the ATTILA and RQMD models. For strangelet formation, they apply a two-phase thermodynamic model of a hadronizing QGP droplet in mechanical, chemical, and thermal equilibrium, following the approach of Greiner and Stoecker (1991), to study the time evolution of baryon density and strangeness fraction under collider conditions.

## Key numbers
- At LHC, for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 6.5 TeV, the probability for net-baryon number fluctuations larger than +/- 20 in bins of one unit of rapidity width is about 15%.
- For a single event with dN_B/dy = 30 at midrapidity, the entropy per baryon number S/A_B is approximately 500, assuming each pion carries 3.6 units of entropy.
- For a QGP droplet with initial baryon number A_B_init = 30 and S/A_init = 200, the baryon density increases by more than one order of magnitude during evolution, and the chemical potential rises from mu_i = 16 MeV to mu_f > 200 MeV.
- The strange quark chemical potential evolves from mu_s_i = 0 to mu_s_f approximately 400 MeV.
- The string tension used in FRITIOF is 1 GeV/fm, with quark production ratios u : d : s : di = 1 : 1 : 0.3 : 0.1, corresponding to m_s = 280 MeV; an enhanced string tension of 2 GeV/fm yields ratios u : d : s : di = 1 : 1 : 0.55 : 0.32.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of baryon stopping by Sorge et al. (1989) and Mattiello et al. (1989), and directly contradicts predictions from ATTILA and FRITIOF 1.7, which showed baryon-free midrapidity at SPS energies. The paper builds on the strangelet distillation mechanism of Greiner and Stoecker (1991) and applies it to the collider regime for the first time. It enables future experimental searches for strangelets and MEMOs (metastable exotic multistrange objects) at RHIC and LHC, and highlights the need for models incorporating string-string interactions to improve predictions of strange particle yields.
