# Scaled transverse-momentum spectra as a probe of collective dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate a scaling property of transverse-momentum spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions obtained by removing the global scales of multiplicity and mean transverse momentum. The resulting dimensionless observable isolates the intrinsic shape of the spectrum and reveals an approximate universality across collision centralities, systems, and energies. Hydrodynamic simulations reproduce this scaling on an event-by-event basis, indicating that it may originate from the collective dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma. Using Gaussian-process emulators trained on the JETSCAPE hybrid model, we perform a Bayesian analysis incorporating the scaled spectra as observables. The results demonstrate that the spectral shape provides independent constraints on key properties of the medium, including pre-equilibrium dynamics and initial-state granularity, while exposing tensions with parameter regions preferred by traditional $p_T$-integrated observables. We further explore an analogous scaling of transverse-mass spectra and observe a comparable universality across centralities and hadron species. These results suggest that scaled spectra provide a powerful new probe of collective dynamics and offer complementary constraints for the quantitative characterization of QCD matter created in heavy-ion collisions.

# Scaled transverse-momentum spectra as a probe of collective dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The scaled transverse-momentum observable \(U(x_T) \equiv \langle p_T \rangle / N \cdot dN/dp_T\) isolates the intrinsic shape of the particle spectrum and reveals an approximate universality across collision centralities, systems, and energies, but this scaling is not exact and breaks down for certain combinations of model parameters, particularly in peripheral collisions where initial-state granularity and pre-equilibrium dynamics dominate the deviations.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using Gaussian-process emulators trained on the JETSCAPE hybrid model to compare the scaled spectra \(U(x_T)\) with ALICE Pb–Pb data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV. They computed first-order Sobol indices from a global sensitivity analysis to identify which model parameters govern the shape of \(U(x_T)\) and its deviations from universality across centrality classes. The deviation from perfect universality was quantified by taking pairwise differences in \(U(x_T)\) between each centrality and the most central (0–5%) reference, evaluated as root-mean-square error across 41 \(x_T\) bins.

## Key numbers

- The scaled-spectra calibration favors small nucleon widths of \(w \sim 0.6\) fm (granular initial conditions), whereas the \(p_T\)-integrated calibration prefers smoother profiles with \(w \sim 1.0\) fm.
- The analysis used ALICE Pb–Pb data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV for centralities 0–5% and 50–60%.
- The deviation from universality was evaluated across 41 \(x_T\) bins for each centrality class.
- The Gaussian nucleon width \(w\) is the dominant contributor to non-universality in peripheral collisions, while the free-streaming relaxation time \(\tau_R\) and maximum bulk viscosity \((\zeta/s)_{\rm max}\) contribute more significantly for mid-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the scaling observable proposed by the ExTrEMe Collaboration (ExTrEMe 2024) and builds on the JETSCAPE Bayesian analysis framework (JETSCAPE 2020) that previously relied on \(p_T\)-integrated observables such as \(dN/dy\), \(\langle p_T \rangle\), and flow harmonics \(v_n\). The results reveal a tension between parameter regions preferred by scaled spectra and those favored by traditional integrated observables, indicating that the spectral shape provides independent constraints on pre-equilibrium dynamics and initial-state granularity. The findings enable future studies of the limits of collectivity in small collision systems and the transition from soft hydrodynamic physics to semi-hard regimes where non-equilibrium contributions become non-negligible (\(p_T \gtrsim 1.5\)–2 GeV).
