# Weakly bound molecules trapped with discrete scaling symmetries

> When the scattering length is proportional to the distance from the center of the system, two particles are shown to be trapped about the center. Furthermore, their spectrum exhibits discrete scale invariance, whose scale factor is controlled by the slope of the scattering length. While this resembles the Efimov effect, our system has a number of advantages when realized with ultracold atoms. We also elucidate how the emergent discrete scaling symmetry is violated for more than two bosons, which may shed new light on Efimov physics. Our system thus serves as a tunable model system to investigate universal physics involving scale invariance, quantum anomaly, and renormalization group limit cycle, which are important in a broad range of quantum physics.

# Weakly bound molecules trapped with discrete scaling symmetries

## Key claim

Two particles interacting via a short-range force can form an infinite tower of bound states exhibiting discrete scale invariance when the scattering length is made proportional to the distance from the center of the system, but only for specific ranges of the slope parameter c; for example, in one dimension the odd-parity channel only has solutions for 0 < c < π√(M/4μ), while the even-parity channel has solutions for any c > 0.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Schrödinger equation in one dimension with a zero-range interaction whose strength depends on the center-of-mass coordinate X through a space-dependent scattering length a(X) = c|X|. They regularize the divergence at X=0 with a sharp or smooth cutoff, derive an integral equation for the wave function in momentum space, and find analytic solutions in terms of hyperbolic functions. For more than two identical bosons, they use a Born-Oppenheimer approximation and lattice Hamiltonian calculations to compute resonance energies and scaling factors.

## Key numbers

- Scale factor for two bosons in one dimension at c=0.25: λ₂ ≈ 1.482(2), extracted from lattice calculations
- Scale factor for three bosons in one dimension at c=0.25: λ₃ ≈ 1.172(2), extracted from lattice calculations
- Scale factor for four bosons in one dimension at c=0.25: λ₄ ≈ 1.10(2), extracted from lattice calculations
- Born-Oppenheimer approximation predictions for N=2,3,4 at c=0.25: λ₂ ≈ 1.481, λ₃ ≈ 1.174, λ₄ ≈ 1.092
- In one dimension, odd-parity bound states exist only for 0 < c < π√(M/4μ)
- In two dimensions, sℓ has a solution when c√(4μ/M) < exp[-Ψ((ℓ+1)/2)] ≈ 7.124, 1.781, 0.9642, 0.6552 for ℓ = 0, 1, 2, 3 respectively
- In three dimensions, sℓ has a solution when c√(4μ/M) < [Γ((ℓ+1)/2)/Γ((ℓ+2)/2)]² ≈ 3.142, 1.273, 0.7854, 0.5659 for ℓ = 0, 1, 2, 3 respectively

## Context

This work extends the concept of discrete scale invariance from the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970), which requires three particles in three dimensions, to a two-particle system in one, two, or three dimensions. The authors propose that the scaling trap overcomes common difficulties in ultracold-atom experiments of Efimov physics, such as the instability of three-body bound states and the fixed scale factor ≈22.7. The system enables experimental study of quantum anomalies and renormalization group limit cycles (Wilson 2005), and the authors show that for more than two bosons the discrete scaling symmetry is violated due to incommensurate scaling laws between different particle-number sectors, which may shed new light on longstanding questions in Efimov physics (Braaten 2006, Ferlaino 2010).
