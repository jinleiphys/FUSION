# QCD constraints on isospin-dense matter and the nuclear equation of state

> Understanding the behavior of dense hadronic matter is a central goal in nuclear physics as it governs the nature and dynamics of astrophysical objects such as supernovae and neutron stars. Because of the non-perturbative nature of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), little is known rigorously about hadronic matter in these extreme conditions. Here, lattice QCD calculations are used to compute thermodynamic quantities and the equation of state of QCD over a wide range of isospin chemical potentials with controlled systematic uncertainties. Agreement is seen with chiral perturbation theory when the chemical potential is small. Comparison to perturbative QCD at large chemical potential allows for an estimate of the gap in the superconducting phase, and this quantity is seen to agree with perturbative determinations. Since the partition function for an isospin chemical potential, $μ_I$, bounds the partition function for a baryon chemical potential $μ_B=3μ_I/2$, these calculations also provide rigorous non-perturbative QCD bounds on the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state over a wide range of baryon densities for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first non-perturbative QCD constraint on the nuclear equation of state with complete quantification of systematic uncertainties, using lattice QCD calculations of isospin-dense matter to bound the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state. The hedge is that these bounds do not significantly constrain phenomenological nuclear equations of state within their typical uncertainties, but they are independent of modeling uncertainties that enter in regions unconstrained by nuclear structure or perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors compute energies of systems with isospin charge n from 1 to 6144 using two-point correlation functions on four ensembles of gauge field configurations with Nf=2+1 flavors of clover fermions. From these energies, they determine the pressure, energy density, and speed of sound using thermodynamic relations, then extrapolate to the continuum limit and physical quark masses using fits with terms of order a^2, a^2 mu_I, a^2 mu_I^2, and (m_pi^2 - m_pi^2_physical). A Bayesian model mixing approach combining chiral perturbation theory, lattice QCD, and perturbative QCD via a Gaussian Process provides the complete zero-temperature equation of state for isospin-dense matter.

## Key numbers

- Lattice calculations span isospin charges n in {1,...,6144} on ensembles with lattice spacings a = 0.091(1) fm and a = 0.070(1) fm
- Pion masses on the ensembles are 169(3) MeV, 169(2) MeV, 164(3) MeV, and 125(4) MeV
- The speed of sound exceeds the conformal limit of c_s^2/c^2 = 1/3 over a wide range of isospin chemical potential
- The superconducting gap extracted from comparing lattice QCD pressure with NNLO perturbative QCD agrees with the perturbative form for mu_I in [1500, 3250] MeV
- The Stefan-Boltzmann pressure for a free Fermi gas is P_SB = mu_I^4 / (32 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the proof-of-principle single-lattice-spacing results of Abbott (2023) by adding continuum extrapolation, physical quark mass interpolation, and improved statistical precision. It agrees with chiral perturbation theory at small chemical potentials (Son 2001, Kogut 2001) and with perturbative QCD at large chemical potentials (Fujimoto 2023), while providing more precise determinations of the superconducting gap than perturbative scale variation alone. The results enable rigorous QCD bounds on the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state via path-integral inequalities (Cohen 2003, Cohen 2004), and provide constraints for phenomenological frameworks studying the QCD phase diagram and neutron star interiors.
