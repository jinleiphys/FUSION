# Quark Coulomb Interactions and the Mass Difference of Mirror Nuclei

> We study the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly in the binding energy of mirror nuclei at high density by adding a single neutron or proton to a quark gluon plasma. In this high-density limit we find an anomaly equal to two-thirds of the Coulomb exchange energy of a proton. This effect is dominated by quark electromagnetic interactions---rather than by the up-down quark mass difference. At normal density we calculate the Coulomb energy of neutron matter using a string-flip quark model. We find a nonzero Coulomb energy because of the neutron's charged constituents. This effect could make a significant contribution to the ONS anomaly.

# Quark Coulomb Interactions and the Mass Difference of Mirror Nuclei

## Key claim

At high density, the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly in mirror-nucleus binding energies is dominated by quark electromagnetic interactions rather than by the up-down quark mass difference, with a magnitude equal to two-thirds of the proton Coulomb exchange energy; at normal density, a string-flip quark model calculation suggests that the nonzero Coulomb energy of neutron matter from its charged quark constituents could make a significant contribution to the anomaly, though the authors note this result is model-dependent and somewhat smaller than the observed anomaly.

## Method

The authors first analyze the ONS anomaly in the high-density limit by comparing the Coulomb exchange energy of adding a single proton versus a single neutron to a quark-gluon plasma, using the asymptotic freedom property that quarks behave as a nearly free Fermi gas at high density. They then perform Monte Carlo simulations of pure neutron matter at normal density using a nonrelativistic three-quark string-flip model with harmonic confining potentials, where the variational wave function is antisymmetric under quark exchange and satisfies cluster separability. The total Coulomb energy is computed for systems of 96 and 264 quarks in a box with antiperiodic boundary conditions, and the change in Coulomb energy per neutron relative to the isolated neutron self-energy is extracted as a function of baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton mass difference in free space is ΔM = 1.29 MeV.
- The Coulomb exchange energy of an added proton in a Fermi gas of elementary nucleons is V_p = -e^2 k_F / π.
- The Coulomb exchange energy of an added neutron in a quark-gluon plasma is V_n^(q) = -(2/3) e^2 k_F / π, exactly two-thirds that of a proton.
- The electromagnetic self-energy of an isolated neutron in the string-flip model is V_n^0 = -0.446 MeV, using α = 1/137 and a proton charge radius of 0.86 fm.
- At normal neutron density ρ_n = 0.08 fm^{-3} and N = 96 quarks, the change in Coulomb energy per neutron is ΔV = -78 ± 1 keV.
- The observed ONS anomaly is of order 200 keV in mass 15 and 300 keV in mass 39.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, first identified by Okamoto (1964) and Nolen and Schiffer (1969), which is the discrepancy between calculated and measured binding-energy differences of mirror nuclei. The authors extend prior work by Brown (1998), who noted the anomaly's magnitude approximately equals the Coulomb exchange energy, and by Blunden and Iqbal (1987), who calculated contributions from ρ-ω mixing to charge symmetry breaking. The high-density limit analysis clarifies the relative roles of electromagnetic effects versus the up-down quark mass difference studied by Nakamura and coworkers (1996). The string-flip model calculation at normal density provides a first estimate of quark electromagnetic contributions to the anomaly and suggests directions for more elaborated quark models with spin, flavor, and color degrees of freedom.
