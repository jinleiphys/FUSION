# Review of experimental and theoretical status of the proton radius puzzle

> The discrepancy between the measured Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen and expectations from electron-proton scattering and regular hydrogen spectroscopy has become known as the proton radius puzzle, whose most "mundane" resolution requires a $> 5 σ$ shift in the value of the fundamental Rydberg constant. I briefly review the status of spectroscopic and scattering measurements, recent theoretical developments, and implications for fundamental physics.

## Key claim

The proton radius puzzle is a 5.6 sigma discrepancy between the proton charge radius measured from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and the radius obtained from electron-proton scattering combined with regular hydrogen spectroscopy; the most mundane resolution would require a greater than 5 sigma shift in the value of the fundamental Rydberg constant.

## Method

This is a review paper summarizing the experimental status of the proton radius puzzle circa 2016, including spectroscopic measurements in regular and muonic hydrogen, electron-proton scattering data, and theoretical developments in form factor parameterizations and radiative corrections. The author discusses reanalyses of the Mainz A1 collaboration dataset using z-expansion fits for form factor nonlinearities, and examines the treatment of two-photon exchange contributions and radiative corrections in both electron-proton scattering and muonic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius from CODATA 2014 electron combination: r_E^p = 0.8751(61) fm
- Proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen (CREMA 2013): r_E^p = 0.84087(26)(29) fm
- Original 2010 discrepancy: 6.9 sigma between CODATA 2010 value r_E^p = 0.8775(51) and CREMA 2010 value r_E^p = 0.84184(36)(56)
- Proton charge radius from muonic deuterium combined with hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift: r_E^p(mu D) = 0.83562(21) fm
- The two-pion threshold for form factor analyticity: Q^2_max = 4 m_pi^2
- Wavefunction enhancement factor for muonic versus electronic hydrogen: (m_mu/m_e)^3 approximately (200)^3

## Context

This review extends the discussion of the proton radius puzzle following the original CREMA measurement by Pohl (2010) and the CODATA adjustments by Mohr (2012, 2015). The paper summarizes reanalyses of the Mainz A1 scattering data by Lee (2015) using z-expansion fits, and discusses theoretical developments in two-photon exchange calculations for muonic hydrogen by Birse and McGovern (2012) and Hill (2016). The work enables future experimental tests including the PRad experiment at JLab, the MUSE muon-proton scattering experiment, and new measurements of muonic helium isotopes.
