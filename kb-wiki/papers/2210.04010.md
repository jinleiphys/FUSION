# Soft gluon emission from heavy quark scattering in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We apply the Low's theorem to soft gluon emission from heavy quark scattering in the nonperturbative strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP). The sQGP is described in terms of the dynamical quasi-particles and adjusted to reproduce the EoS from lQCD at finite temperature and chemical potential. Since the emitted gluon is soft and of long wavelength, it does not provide information on the detailed structure of the scattering, and only the emission from incoming and outgoing partons is enough. It simplifies the calculations making the scattering amplitude factorizable into the elastic scattering and the emission of soft gluon. Imposing a proper upper limit on the emitted gluon energy, we obtain the guage-invariant scattering cross sections of heavy quarks with the massive partons of the medium as well as their transport coefficients (momentum drag and diffusion) in the QGP and compare with those from the elastic scattering without gluon emission.

## Key claim

Low's theorem for soft gluon emission is extended to heavy quark scattering in a nonperturbative strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) described by the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), but the soft gluon approximation is valid only when the emitted gluon energy is much smaller than the momenta of the scattering partons and when the scattering is not nearly forward, because the leading-order approximation neglects gluon emission from the exchanged virtual gluon which becomes comparable near forward scattering.

## Method

The authors apply Low's theorem to soft gluon emission from heavy quark scattering with light quarks and gluons in the sQGP, where the medium is described by the DQPM with massive off-shell quasiparticles whose properties are adjusted to reproduce lattice QCD equation of state. The scattering amplitude is factorized into an elastic scattering part and a soft gluon emission factor, keeping only the leading order in (q/p) where q is the gluon momentum and p is the parton momentum. The authors verify that the resulting amplitudes satisfy the Slavnov-Taylor identities for quark-quark and quark-gluon scatterings, ensuring gauge invariance, and then compute the scattering cross sections and transport coefficients (momentum drag and diffusion) for heavy quarks with soft gluon emission.

## Key numbers

- The soft gluon emission diagrams from external legs are of order 1/q, while emission from the exchanged gluon is of order 1/p = 1/q * (q/p), with q/p much less than 1
- The color factor for the transition amplitude squared in quark-quark scattering includes terms proportional to (N_c^2 - 1) and (N_c^2 - 2) where N_c = 3
- The propagator for pseudoscalar particles is given as G(p) = i/(p^2 - m^2 + i*epsilon) and the electromagnetic vertex as V^mu(p+q,p) = -iQ(2p+q)^mu
- The fermion propagator is G(p) = i*(p-slash + m)/(p^2 - m^2 + i*epsilon) and the gluon-quark vertex is V_{ij}^{mu,a}(p+q,p) = ig*gamma^mu*T_{ij}^a

## Context

This work extends the soft photon approximation from QED (Low 1958) to QCD for soft gluon emission in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma, building on the DQPM framework developed by Cassing (2009), Bratkovskaya (2011), and others for describing partonic interactions with massive quasiparticles. Previous studies of soft gluon emission by Gunion (1981), Djordjevic (2003), and Lushozi (2019) focused on forward scattering of energetic partons, while this work treats all scattering angles systematically. The results enable inclusion of radiative energy loss for heavy quarks in the PHSD transport approach, which previously only included collisional energy loss (Berrehrah 2013, 2014; Song 2015), and are relevant for understanding the nuclear modification factor of heavy flavor in heavy-ion collisions.
