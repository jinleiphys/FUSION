# Nuclear charge radii in Bayesian neural networks revisited

> In this work, a refined Bayesian neural network (BNN) based approach with six inputs including the proton number, mass number, and engineered features associated with the pairing effect, shell effect, isospin effect, and ``abnormal" shape staggering effect of $^{181,183,185}$Hg, is proposed to accurately describe nuclear charge radii. The new approach is able to well describe the charge radii of atomic nuclei with $A\ge40$ and $Z\ge20$. The standard root-mean-square (rms) deviation is $0.014$ fm for both the training and validation data. In particular, the predicted charge radii of proton-rich and neutron-rich calcium isotopes are found in good agreement with data. We further demonstrate the reliability of the BNN approach by investigating the variations of the rms deviation with extrapolation distances, mass numbers, and isospin asymmetries.

# Nuclear charge radii in Bayesian neural networks revisited

## Key claim
The refined Bayesian neural network (BNN) with six engineered input features achieves a root-mean-square deviation of 0.014 fm for both training and validation data, but this performance depends on explicitly labeling the "abnormal" shape-staggering nuclei ¹⁸¹,¹⁸³,¹⁸⁵Hg as a separate feature to prevent these rare events from distorting the network.

## Method
The authors combine a three-parameter nuclear charge radius formula (NP) with a Bayesian neural network having one hidden layer of 34 neurons and six input features: proton number Z, mass number A, a pairing term δ, a promiscuity factor P related to shell effects, an isospin asymmetry term I², and a local indicator LI that flags the three mercury isotopes with known shape staggering. The network is trained on 820 experimental charge radii from Angeli (2013) for nuclei with A≥40 and Z≥20, then validated on 113 newer data points from Goodacre (2020) and Li (2021). Predictions are obtained via Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling, and the authors define a special root-mean-square deviation (SRMSD) that accounts for the BNN's uncertainty estimates.

## Key numbers
- RMSD of 0.0140 fm for the training set and 0.0139 fm for the validation set using the D6 (NP-BNN with 6 inputs) model
- RMSD of the D6 model is 46% of the NP formula's RMSD (0.0300 fm) on the validation set
- SRMSD of 0.0094 fm for the D6 model on the validation set, compared to 0.0142 fm for the D4 model
- The D4 model's validation RMSD (0.0187 fm) is 31% larger than its training RMSD (0.0143 fm), while the D6 model shows no such increase
- RMSD of 0.014 fm achieved in this work, compared to 0.023 fm for ANNs (Akkoyun 2012, Wu 2020), 0.02 fm for naive Bayesian classifier (Ma 2020), 0.03 fm for kernel ridge regression (Ma 2022), 0.026 fm for earlier BNN (Utama 2016), and 0.019 fm for the previous D4 model (Dong 2021)

## Context
This work extends the NP-BNN4 (D4) model of Dong (2021) by adding two new engineered features: an isospin asymmetry term I² and a local indicator LI for the shape-staggering mercury isotopes ¹⁸¹,¹⁸³,¹⁸⁵Hg observed by Marsh (2018). The D4 model showed validation RMSD 30% larger than training RMSD and poor predictions for neutron-deficient calcium and thallium isotopes. The new D6 model resolves these issues and achieves the lowest reported RMSD among machine learning approaches to charge radii, including comparisons with ANNs (Akkoyun 2012, Wu 2020), kernel ridge regression (Ma 2022), and earlier BNN methods (Utama 2016). The approach enables more reliable extrapolations to super-heavy nuclei and neutron-rich regions where experimental data are scarce.
