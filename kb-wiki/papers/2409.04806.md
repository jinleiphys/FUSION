# Medium-induced parton splitting in expanding QCD matter

> Using the approach derived by Caron-Huot and Gale from the BDMPS-Z formalism, we obtain medium-induced parton splitting rates in a Bjorken expanding QCD matter. We compare the rate with the case of a static medium and investigate the impact of the medium expansion on the splitting rate. We also consider the leading order in the opacity expansion and the harmonic oscillator solution. These approximations are recovered in the limits of small and large formation times, respectively.

## Key claim

Medium-induced parton splitting rates in a Bjorken expanding QCD matter are suppressed compared to the static medium case, with the suppression being more pronounced for larger expansion exponents and at late times; the full rate is consistent with the opacity expansion (OE) for small formation times and with the harmonic oscillator (HO) solution for large formation times, but the paper notes that for small initial times the full rate remains below the OE results, indicating a failure of the opacity expansion.

## Method

The splitting rates are obtained by solving the two-dimensional quantum evolution equation derived by Caron-Huot and Gale from the BDMPS-Z formalism, extended to a Bjorken expanding medium where the temperature evolves as T(t) = T0 (t0/(t+t0))^(alpha/3). The evolution equation for the interaction picture wave function is solved numerically using an Euler scheme with a time-dependent collision integral and a time-dependent initial condition obtained from a differential equation in inverse time. The full rate is compared to the leading order in the opacity expansion (single scattering) and to the harmonic oscillator solution valid in the deep LPM regime, with the latter obtained from the analytic spectrum using Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- Splitting rate of a gluon with momentum P = 16 GeV to a gluon of momentum fraction z = 0.01 and z = 0.5, with coupling constant g = sqrt(4*pi*0.3) ~ 1.94 and temperature T = 0.4 GeV
- Initial times t0 = 0.3 fm/c and t0 = 0.1 fm/c used for comparison
- Expansion exponents alpha = 1, 0.4, 0.01, 10^(-1), 10^(-2), and 10^(-4) considered
- For alpha = 0.01, the splitting rate aligns with the static medium results, serving as a cross-check
- For hard splitting z = 0.5 with alpha = 1, the suppression is more pronounced than for soft splitting z = 0.01
- The deep LPM regime is reached at late times for momentum fractions z >= 0.1

## Context

This work extends the Caron-Huot and Gale formalism (CaronHuot:2010bp) from static media to Bjorken expanding media, building on the BDMPS-Z framework (Baier:1994bd, Zakharov:1996fv) and the AMY formalism (Arnold:2008iy) which integrated out time dependence for infinite media. The approach differs from that of Andres (2023) by providing a simpler numerical implementation. The results serve as a foundation for future studies aiming to obtain a full resummation of the in-medium shower using either Monte Carlo simulations (Schenke:2009gb) or effective kinetic descriptions (Mehtar-Tani:2022zwf).
