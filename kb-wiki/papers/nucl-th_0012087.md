# Few-Body Correlations in Fermi Systems

> I present recent results on few-body correlations in nuclear matter at finite temperatures and densities. In particular I focus on the four-body correlations. The alpha-particle is the strongest bound state. The investigation is done based on effective in-medium few-body equations that consistently include self energy corrections and Pauli blocking effects. I address the question of four-body condensates as well.

# Few-Body Correlations in Fermi Systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a systematic framework for treating few-body correlations in nuclear matter at finite temperatures and densities, claiming that the alpha-particle is the strongest bound state and that four-body condensates (quartetting) are likely to occur at higher critical temperatures than two-body pairing. However, the author notes that the situation for positive chemical potentials remains unclear because the four-body AGS equation develops poles related to zeros in the energy denominator, unlike the two-body case where the numerator also vanishes.

## Method
The author derives effective in-medium few-body equations using an equal-time Dyson equation formalism, treating the medium as uncorrelated and neglecting backward-propagating particles. The resulting two-, three-, and four-body equations incorporate self-energy corrections for masses and Pauli blocking effects, and are solved using an energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) generalized for non-symmetric potentials. The in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations are formulated for both scattering and bound-state problems, with the two-body t-matrix derived from the Feynman-Galitskii equation.

## Key numbers
- Temperature T = 10 MeV used for three-body break-up cross section calculations at different nuclear matter densities
- Triton binding energy calculated as a function of nuclear matter density at T = 10 MeV and T = 20 MeV
- Binding energies of two-, three-, and four-body systems shown for T = 10 MeV and center-of-mass momentum P_cm = 0
- Mott density of the alpha-particle: n_Mott = 0.19 fm^-3 at T = 10 MeV and P_cm = 0
- Binding energy of the isolated triton differs by 10% between two different potentials studied, yet the Mott density is practically at the same place after renormalization
- Asymmetric nuclear matter ratio N_p/N_n = 0.72 (for the 129Xe + 119Sn reaction) compensates the Coulomb effect on helium Mott density

## Context
This work extends the author's previous few-body calculations in nuclear matter (Beyer 1996, Beyer 1999) to include four-body correlations and the possibility of alpha-particle condensation. The formalism builds on the Dyson equation approach of Dukelsky, Ropke, and Schuck (1998) and the in-medium AGS equations developed by Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (1967, 1972, 1974, 1975). The results for critical temperatures of quartetting are compared to the variational treatment of Ropke, Schnell, Schuck, and Noziere (1998), with the present work suggesting that the exact four-body treatment may yield different results for the rapid fall of critical temperature at positive chemical potentials. The approach enables systematic investigation of cluster formation in astrophysical contexts such as neutron star structure and heavy-ion collisions.
