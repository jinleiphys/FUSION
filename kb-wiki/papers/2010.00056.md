# Review of the ${\mathbf Λ}$(1405): A curious case of a strange-ness resonance

> During its long-lasting history, the $Λ(1405)$ has become a benchmark for our understanding of the $\rm SU(3)$ hadron dynamics. Starting with its theoretical prediction and later experimental verification, until the most recent debates on the existence of the second broad pole, it emerged as a fruitful research area sparking many theoretical and experimental developments. This review intends to provide the reader with the current status of research on the $Λ(1405)$-resonance, reflecting on historical, experimental and theoretical developments. A common database for experimental results and a comparison of most recent theoretical approaches will be provided in the last two parts of this manuscript.

## Key claim

The Λ(1405) resonance exhibits a double-pole structure in the complex energy plane, a feature that arises from the non-perturbative dynamics of the coupled πΣ and K̅N channels, though the existence of the second broad pole remains debated and depends on the theoretical framework used for the analytic continuation of the scattering amplitude.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental constraints on the Λ(1405) from total cross sections of K⁻p → {K⁻p, K̅⁰n, π⁰/⁺/⁻Σ⁰/⁻/⁺} reactions measured in bubble chamber experiments at CERN, LBNL, BNL, and Rutherford Laboratory from the 1960s through the 1980s, along with kaonic hydrogen X-ray measurements from the SIDDHARTA collaboration. On the theory side, the review compares results from chiral unitary models, dynamical coupled-channel models, and potential models that constrain the scattering amplitude via unitarity and chiral perturbation theory, then analytically continue to the complex energy plane to extract pole positions.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1405) resonance mass was first observed at 1405 MeV in the πΣ mass plots of K⁻p → Σππ reactions (Alston 1961, Bastien 1961)
- The SIDDHARTA collaboration measured the kaonic hydrogen 1s state energy shift ΔE = 283 ± 42 eV and width Γ/2 = 271 ± 55 eV (Bazzi 2011)
- Threshold ratios from kaonic hydrogen experiments: γ = 2.38 ± 0.04, R_c = 0.664 ± 0.011, R_n = 0.189 ± 0.015 (Nowak 1978, Tovee 1971)
- The spin-parity J^P = 1/2⁻ was confirmed by the CLAS collaboration in photo-induced reactions (Moriya 2014)
- The K̅N scattering length from NNLO SU(3) chiral perturbation theory gives a_{K̅N}^{I=0} = (+1.11 + 0.22i) fm, while the SIDDHARTA/Deser-type relation gives a_{K̅N}^{I=0} ≈ (−0.53 + 0.77i) fm

## Context

This review extends the earlier reviews by Sakurai (1960), Dalitz (1963), Hyodo (2011), and Gal (2016) on strangeness S = −1 baryon resonances, and provides a common database of experimental results for future theoretical comparisons. The work enables improved extraction of Λ(1405) pole parameters from upcoming experiments such as SIDDHARTA-2 at DAΦNE (Curceanu 2020) and the Hall D neutral kaon beam experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (Amaryan 2020), which will help resolve the current disagreement between theoretical models in the K⁻n channel.
