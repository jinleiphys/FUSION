# Flavored exotic multibaryons and hypernuclei in topological soliton models

> The energies of baryon states with positive strangeness, or anti-charm (-beauty) are estimated in chiral soliton approach, in the "rigid oscillator" version of the bound state soliton model proposed by Klebanov and Westerberg. Positive strangeness states can appear as relatively narrow nuclear levels (Theta-hypernuclei), the states with heavy anti-flavors can be bound with respect to strong interactions in the original Skyrme variant of the model (SK4 variant). The binding energies of anti-flavored states are estimated also in the variant of the model with 6-th order term in chiral derivatives in the lagrangian as solitons stabilizer (SK6 variant). The latter variant is less attractive, and nuclear states with anti-charm or anti-beauty can be unstable relative to strong interactions. The chances to get bound hypernuclei with heavy antiflavors are greater within "nuclear variant" of the model with rescaled model parameter (Skyrme constant e or e' decreased by ~30%) which is expected to be valid for baryon numbers greater than B ~10. The rational map approximation is used to describe multiskyrmions with baryon number up to ~30 and to calculate the quantities necessary for their quantization (moments of inertia, sigma-term, etc.).

## Key claim

The paper estimates that positive-strangeness multibaryons (Theta-hypernuclei) can appear as relatively narrow nuclear levels, while states with heavy antiflavors (anti-charm, anti-beauty) can be bound with respect to strong interactions in the original Skyrme variant (SK4) but may be unstable in the variant with a sixth-order term (SK6); the chances for bound heavy-antiflavored hypernuclei increase within a "nuclear variant" of the model where the Skyrme constant is decreased by about 30%.

## Method

The authors use the rigid oscillator version of the bound state soliton model, originally proposed by Klebanov and Westerberg, to calculate flavor and antiflavor excitation energies for baryon numbers up to about 30. They employ the rational map approximation to describe multiskyrmion configurations and compute static characteristics (moments of inertia, sigma-terms) for both the SK4 variant (with the Skyrme term as stabilizer) and the SK6 variant (with a sixth-order derivative term as stabilizer). The collective coordinate quantization procedure is applied, including the Wess-Zumino term, to obtain the Hamiltonian and excitation frequencies for strangeness, anti-charm, and anti-beauty states.

## Key numbers

- For the SK4 variant with e=4.12, the flavor moment of inertia Theta_F^(0) for B=1 is 2.05 GeV^{-1}, and for B=32 it is 86.4 GeV^{-1}.
- For the SK6 variant with e'=4.11, the flavor moment of inertia Theta_F^(0) for B=1 is 2.28 GeV^{-1}, and for B=32 it is 87.4 GeV^{-1}.
- For the nuclear variant (rescaled parameters, e=3.00 in SK4), Theta_F^(0) for B=1 is 4.66 GeV^{-1}, and for B=32 it is 191 GeV^{-1}.
- The ratios F_D/F_pi are 1.22 for kaons and 2.28^{+1.4}_{-1.1} for D-mesons.
- The Skyrme parameter e is close to 4 in numerical fits of hyperon spectra; the nuclear variant uses e=3.00 for SK4 and e'=2.84 for SK6.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of exotic baryon states within chiral soliton models, particularly the rigid oscillator approach of Klebanov and Westerberg (1996) and earlier estimates for baryon numbers up to 8 by Kopeliovich et al. (2003). It builds on the prediction of the Theta+ pentaquark by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and subsequent experimental searches. The paper enables future studies of multibaryon systems with positive strangeness (Theta-hypernuclei) and heavy antiflavors, and it provides a framework for comparing the SK4 and SK6 variants of the Skyrme model for baryon numbers up to about 30.
