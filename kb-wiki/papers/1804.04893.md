# Interplay of drag by hot matter and electromagnetic force on the directed flow of heavy quarks

> Rapidity-odd directed flow in heavy ion collisions can originate from two very distinct sources in the collision dynamics i. an initial tilt of the fireball in the reaction plane that generates directed flow of the constituents independent of their charges, and ii. the Lorentz force due to the strong primordial electromagnetic field that drives the flow in opposite directions for constituents carrying unlike sign charges. We study the directed flow of open charm mesons $D^0$ and $\overline{D^0}$ in the presence of both these sources of directed flow. The drag from the tilted matter dominates over the Lorentz force resulting in same sign flow for both $D^0$ and $\overline{D^0}$, albeit of different magnitudes. Their average directed flow is about ten times larger than their difference. This charge splitting in the directed flow is a sensitive probe of the electrical conductivity of the produced medium. We further study their beam energy dependence; while the average directed flow shows a decreasing trend, the charge splitting remains flat from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=60$ GeV to $5$ TeV.

## Key claim

The drag from the tilted fireball dominates over the Lorentz force from the electromagnetic field, resulting in same-sign directed flow for both D^0 and anti-D^0 mesons, with their average directed flow about ten times larger than their difference; the charge splitting in the directed flow is a sensitive probe of the electrical conductivity of the produced medium.

## Method

The authors combine a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic evolution (using the vHLLE code) with Langevin dynamics for heavy quarks, including both the drag from the expanding tilted fireball and the Lorentz force from the electromagnetic field. The initial entropy density is modeled using a Glauber approach with asymmetric entropy deposition to produce the fireball tilt, and the electromagnetic fields are calculated from spectator protons with a constant electrical conductivity. Heavy quark momenta are sampled from PYTHIA p+p events, and D^0 and anti-D^0 mesons are produced via Peterson fragmentation at the end of the Langevin evolution.

## Key numbers

- The average directed flow slope at midrapidity (dv_1/deta)^avg is about ten times larger than the charge splitting slope (dv_1/deta)^diff for D^0 and anti-D^0 mesons.
- As the electrical conductivity sigma is raised from 0.011 to 0.035 fm^{-1}, the charge splitting slope (dv_1/deta)^diff at midrapidity increases by 400% for tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c and by 25% for tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- As the initial time tau_0 is raised from 0.2 to 0.6 fm/c, the average directed flow slope (dv_1/deta)^avg falls by about 20%.
- The charge splitting of the D meson directed flow remains flat from sqrt(s_NN) = 60 GeV to 5 TeV for sigma = 0.023 fm^{-1} and tau_0 = 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark directed flow from the tilted fireball alone (Chatterjee 2017) and from electromagnetic fields alone (Das 2016) to their combined effect. The results enable extraction of the electrical conductivity of hot QCD matter from the charge splitting of D meson directed flow, and predict a weak energy dependence of this splitting from RHIC to LHC energies. The authors note that preliminary STAR data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV show qualitative agreement with their predictions, while preliminary ALICE data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV indicate a much larger charge splitting than their model can explain with similar parameters.
