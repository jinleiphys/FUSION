# Heavy Meson Observables and Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) studies show that the b-quark mass-function is approximately constant, and that this is true to a lesser extent for the c-quark. These observations provide the basis for a study of the leptonic and semileptonic decays of heavy pseudoscalar mesons using a ``heavy-quark'' limit of the DSEs. When exact, this limit reduces the number of independent form factors. Semileptonic decays with light mesons in the final state are also accessible because the DSEs provide a description of light-quark propagation characteristics and light-meson structure. A description of B-meson decays is straightforward, however, the study of decays involving the D-meson indicates that c-quark mass-corrections are quantitatively important.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework, when combined with a heavy-quark limit, can provide a quantitatively reliable description of B-meson observables, but for D-meson observables the heavy-quark approximation is inadequate and requires corrections of 15-20% because the c-quark mass-function is not sufficiently constant.

## Method

The authors solved the quark DSE with an infrared-enhanced gluon propagator interaction and current-quark masses to obtain dressed-quark propagators, then employed a heavy-quark limit (neglecting 1/hat-M corrections) for the b- and c-quark propagators while retaining full momentum-dependent light-quark propagators. Heavy-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes were modeled with a Gaussian Ansatz containing two free parameters (binding energy E and width Lambda), and light-meson amplitudes were taken from an algebraic parametrisation previously fitted to light-hadron observables. The hadronic current for semileptonic decays was computed in impulse approximation, and the two heavy-quark parameters were fixed by requiring f_B = 0.170 GeV and fitting to several B -> pi form factor data points.

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach previously applied to light mesons by Roberts et al. (1996) and Maris and Roberts (1997) into the heavy-meson sector, using a heavy-quark limit analogous to the heavy-quark effective theory framework of Isgur and Wise (1990). The results enable correlation of heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light semileptonic decays within a single nonperturbative framework, and the calculated form factors can be used to extract CKM matrix elements from experimental decay rates. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD simulations (e.g., from the UKQCD collaboration cited as "latt") and vector meson dominance models, providing a continuum method that incorporates dressed-quark substructure and can be systematically improved by including 1/hat-M_c corrections.
