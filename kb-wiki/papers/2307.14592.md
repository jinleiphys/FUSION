# Short-range correlations and momentum distributions in mirror nuclei 3H and 3He

> Motivated by recent high-energy electron and $\rm ^3H$ and $\rm ^3He$ nuclei scattering experiment in Jefferson Lab (Nature 609, 41 (2022)), the short-range correlations (SRCs) between nucleon pairs for 3-nucleon systems are microscopically studied using realistic $NN$ 2-body interaction and two-Gaussian type $NNN$ 3-body interaction. The wave functions of both $\rm ^3H$ and $\rm ^3He$ are obtained by solving 3-body Schrödinger equations using Gaussian expansion method (GEM). The differences of one-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon momentum distributions between $\rm ^3H$ and $\rm ^3He$ are analyzed in detail. The results show that the percentages of $pn$-SRC pairs are significantly enhanced as compared with those of $nn(pp)$-SRC ones in $\rm ^3H$ and $\rm ^3He$ nuclei, which is consistent with the experimental findings.

## Key claim

The authors show that in both ³H and ³He, the percentages of proton-neutron short-range correlation (pn-SRC) pairs are significantly enhanced relative to nn- or pp-SRC pairs, yielding a ratio of about 1.62–1.63, which is smaller than the experimental value of 2.17⁺⁰·²⁵₋₀·₂₀ reported by the Jefferson Lab experiment (Li, 2022) because the calculation sums over all center-of-mass momenta of the nucleon pair, whereas the experiment selects pairs with near-zero center-of-mass momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for ³H and ³He using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with the Argonne v8' (AV8') two-nucleon interaction plus a two-Gaussian three-nucleon interaction. One-body and two-body momentum distributions are obtained by Fourier transforming the correlated coordinate-space wave functions, and short-range correlation (SRC) pairs are defined as those with relative momentum k > 1.33 fm⁻¹ (the Fermi momentum of saturated nuclear matter). The percentages of SRC and non-SRC pairs are then computed for each nucleon-pair type.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ³H: –8.44 MeV (with AV8' + 3-body interaction); experimental value is –8.48 MeV.
- Binding energy of ³He: –7.76 MeV (with AV8' + 3-body interaction); experimental value is –7.72 MeV.
- Proton root-mean-square radius of ³H: 1.597 fm (with AV8' + 3-body interaction); experimental value is 1.59 fm.
- Proton root-mean-square radius of ³He: 1.770 fm (with AV8' + 3-body interaction); experimental value is 1.76 fm.
- Percentage of pn-SRC pairs in ³H: 6.43%; percentage of nn-SRC pairs in ³H: 3.98%; ratio P_pn/P_nn = 1.62.
- Percentage of pn-SRC pairs in ³He: 6.33%; percentage of pp-SRC pairs in ³He: 3.89%; ratio P_pn/P_pp = 1.63.
- At k = 5.0 fm⁻¹, the ratio ρ_p/ρ_n for ³H is approximately 1.25.

## Context

This work is a microscopic ab initio calculation motivated by the Jefferson Lab experiment (Li, 2022) that measured pn- and pp-SRC ratios in ³H and ³He, finding a ratio of 2.17⁺⁰·²⁵₋₀·₂₀ relative to the pair-counting prediction. The present results are consistent with the experimental finding that pn-SRCs dominate over nn/pp-SRCs, and the smaller theoretical ratio (1.62–1.63) is attributed to summing over all center-of-mass momenta of the pair, whereas the experiment selects pairs with Q ≈ 0. The calculations extend prior variational Monte Carlo work (Piarulli, 2022) and can be extended to heavier systems to study the isospin dependence of short-range correlations and the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.
