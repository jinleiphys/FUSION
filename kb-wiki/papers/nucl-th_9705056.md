# Probes for the early reaction dynamics of heavy-ion collisions at AGS and SPS

> We discuss the early evolution of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions within a multi-fluid dynamical model. In particular, we show that due to the finite mean-free path of the particles compression shock waves are smeared out considerably as compared to the one-fluid limit. Also, the maximal energy density of the baryons is much lower. We discuss the time scale of kinetic equilibration of the baryons in the central region and its relevance for directed flow. Finally, thermal emission of direct photons from the fluid of produced particles is calculated within the three-fluid model and two other simple expansion models. It is shown that the transverse momentum and rapidity spectra of photons give clue to the cooling law and the early rapidity distribution of the photon source.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, the finite mean-free path of nucleons (due to the total NN cross section) causes compression shock waves to be smeared out considerably compared to the one-fluid limit, and that the maximal energy density of the baryons is much lower than in that limit; this kinetic non-equilibrium in the early stage weakens the conversion of initial longitudinal momentum into transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors employ a three-fluid hydrodynamical model (projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and produced particles) with local friction forces derived from NN-interaction data, propagating all three fluids in (3+1)-dimensional space-time. They compare the compression stage, kinetic equilibration time, directed flow, and thermal photon emission from the third fluid against the one-fluid limit and two other expansion models (scaling hydrodynamics and Landau expansion). The equation of state for the third fluid uses an ideal QGP described by the MIT bag model above T_C = 160 MeV, matched via Gibbs conditions to a hadronic phase below that temperature.

## Key numbers

- The time scale for kinetic equilibration of projectile and target nucleons in the central region is t_CM^eq = 2R_Pb / gamma_CM = 1.5 fm/c for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS.
- The maximum directed nucleon flow <p_x^dir/N>(y) for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (b = 3 fm) is on the order of 40-50 MeV.
- In the three-fluid model, the third fluid locally reaches energy densities up to approximately 10 GeV/fm^3 for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS.
- The maximum temperature of the third fluid in the three-fluid model reaches up to 300 MeV at CERN-SPS.
- Fitting the photon transverse momentum spectrum in the region 2 GeV <= k_T <= 3 GeV by an exponential distribution gives "T" = 260 MeV in the three-fluid model and for the Landau expansion, and "T" = 210 MeV for the scaling expansion.
- The initial temperature assumed for the scaling and Landau expansion models is 300 MeV, with an initial time of tau_0 = 0.22 fm for the boost-invariant expansion.
- The ground-state energy density of nuclear matter is epsilon_0 = 0.15 GeV/fm^3, and the ground-state baryon density is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends earlier multi-fluid models (Previous, year not specified) by implementing three fluids (projectile, target, and produced particles) with local interactions based on NN-interactions and (3+1)-dimensional propagation. It contrasts with one-fluid hydrodynamics (Scheid, year not specified; Rischke, year not specified) where shock discontinuities appear and the baryon and energy flow through the symmetry plane vanish. The results enable future investigation of whether directed flow at SPS is more sensitive to the equation of state of produced particles than to that of the nucleons themselves, and whether rescattering of produced particles with projectile and target nucleons (neglected in the present model) is essential for quantitative comparison to experimental data (Gutbrod, year not specified).
