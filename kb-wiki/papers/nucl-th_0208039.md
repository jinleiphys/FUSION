# Electron Quasielastic Scattering at High Energy from $^{56}$Fe, What Suppression?

> Quasielastic electron scattering $(e,e')$ from $^{56}$Fe is calculated at large electron energies (2-4 GeV) and large three momentum transfer (0.5-1.5 GeV/c). We use a relativistic mean-field single particle model for the bound and continuum nucleon wavefunctions based on the $σ-ω$ model and we include the effects of electron Coulomb distortion in the calculation. The calculations are compared to high energy data from SLAC and more recent data from Jefferson Laboratory, particularly for kinematics where the energy transfer is less than 500 to 600 MeV and the quasielastic process is expected to dominate the cross section. The effects of the predicted weakening of the strong scalar and vector potentials of the $σ-ω$ model at high energy are investigated. Possible evidence for `longitudinal suppression' or modifications of nucleon form factors in the medium is considered, but neither is necessary to explain the quasielastic data for four momentum transfers less than 1 (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic mean-field single particle model with energy-dependent scalar and vector potentials, the authors find no evidence for `longitudinal suppression' or modification of nucleon form factors in the nuclear medium when comparing to quasielastic electron scattering data from $^{56}$Fe at high energies, provided the four-momentum transfer is less than 1 (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive quasielastic $(e,e')$ cross sections for $^{56}$Fe using a relativistic mean-field model based on the $\sigma-\omega$ model for bound and continuum nucleon wavefunctions, with electron Coulomb distortion effects included via an approximate treatment that yields an $r$-dependent momentum transfer. They compare calculations using constant scalar and vector potentials against calculations using energy-dependent potentials (parametrized from global fits to proton-nucleus scattering by Cooper et al. (1993)) for the outgoing nucleon, and also test the effect of current non-conservation by evaluating the $z$-component of the nuclear current directly in the Lorentz gauge.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energies of 2.02 GeV and 3.595 GeV (SLAC data) and 4.045 GeV (JLAB data) were used, with three-momentum transfers of 0.5-1.5 GeV/c and energy transfers below 500-600 MeV.
- For 500 MeV outgoing nucleons, the scalar potential scaling factor $f_S$ is 0.70 and the vector potential scaling factor $f_V$ is 0.59, based on the parametrization $f_S=0.97-0.66x+0.28x^2$ and $f_V=0.97-0.91x+0.30x^2$ where $x=T_p/M$.
- At $Q^2=0.25$ (GeV/c)$^2$, proposed medium-modified form factors would cause $G_E$ to drop by 11% and $G_M$ to increase by 3%; at $Q^2=1.0$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $G_E$ would drop by 51% and $G_M$ would decrease by 16%.
- The standard dipole form factor parameter $\Lambda^2$ is 0.71 (GeV/c)$^2$ for free nucleons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic mean-field analyses of quasielastic scattering from $^{40}$Ca (Williamson et al., 1997) and $^{208}$Pb (Kim et al., 2001) to higher energies, comparing with SLAC data (Day et al., 1987) and JLAB data (Arrington et al., 1999). The results contradict earlier claims of longitudinal suppression based on non-relativistic calculations (Meziani et al., 1985) and support Jourdan's (1996) conclusion that the Coulomb sum rule is obeyed. The calculations enable improved separation of quasielastic from inelastic contributions in scaling studies at large $Q^2$, and suggest that proposed medium modifications of nucleon form factors (Cheon and Jeong, 1992; Saito et al., 1999) are not required to describe the data.
