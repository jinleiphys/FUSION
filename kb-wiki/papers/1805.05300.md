# Estimate of the CME signal in heavy-ion collisions from measurements relative to the participant and spectator flow planes

> An interpretation of the charge dependent correlations sensitive to the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) -- the separation of the electric charges along the system magnetic field (across the reaction plane) -- is ambiguous due to a possible large background (non-CME) effects. The background contribution is proportional to the elliptic flow $v_2$; it is the largest in measurements relative to the participant plane, and is smaller in measurements relative to the flow plane determined by spectators, where the CME signal, on opposite, is likely larger. In this note I discuss a possible strategy for corresponding experimental measurements, and list and evaluate different assumptions related to this approach.

## Key claim

Measuring the ratio of the charge-dependent correlator to elliptic flow relative to the spectator flow plane versus the participant flow plane can isolate the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) signal from background, but this requires the assumption that the background contribution is proportional to elliptic flow and that the spectator plane and magnetic field plane coincide in midcentral collisions.

## Method

The author derives the ratio of the charge-dependent three-particle correlator (Δγ) to elliptic flow (v₂), measured relative to both the participant plane and the spectator plane. By taking the double ratio of these two measurements, the background contribution cancels if it is proportional to v₂, leaving a term proportional to the fraction of the CME signal in the participant-plane correlator. The author then evaluates several assumptions about the relative orientations of the participant plane, spectator plane, and magnetic field plane to relate this double ratio to the CME fraction.

## Key numbers

- In midcentral collisions (centrality ~40-50%), the spectator flow plane is very close to the true reaction plane, as measured by v₂{4}; in central collisions (<10% centrality), the spectator plane is close to the participant plane.
- Experimentally, v₂ measured relative to the participant plane is larger than v₂ measured relative to the spectator plane by about 15%.
- To estimate a CME fractional contribution to the gamma correlator (f_CME_PP) at the level of ~5%, the double ratio of the correlator-to-v₂ ratios must be measured with an accuracy better than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Xu (2017) who attempted to estimate the CME signal from existing measurements and make predictions for isobar collisions at RHIC. The paper provides a more detailed evaluation of the role of flow fluctuations and explicitly lists the assumptions required, building on the gamma correlator method introduced by Voloshin (2004) and the background decomposition used by the ALICE and CMS collaborations (2017) in their Event Shape Engineering analyses. The proposed measurement strategy could enable a cleaner separation of the CME signal from background in heavy-ion collision data.
