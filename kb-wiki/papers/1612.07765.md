# Implications of Missing Resonances in Heavy Ions Collisions

> Between 2004 until today many new resonances in the QCD spectrum have been added by the Particle Data Group. However, it is still possible that there are further states that have yet to be measured, as predicted by Lattice QCD calculations and Quark Models. In this paper I review how these missing states would influence the field of heavy-ion collisions. Additionally, the quantum numbers that characterize these missing states are also discussed.

# Implications of Missing Resonances in Heavy Ions Collisions

## Key claim
Missing QCD resonances, as predicted by Lattice QCD and Quark Models, could influence heavy-ion collision observables including transport coefficients, chemical equilibration times, and elliptic flow, but additional states beyond the current PDG listings are unlikely to strongly affect comparisons with Lattice QCD thermodynamic quantities or freeze-out temperatures.

## Method
The author reviews the hadron resonance gas model, which treats an interacting gas of hadrons as non-interacting particles plus their resonances, and compares its predictions to Lattice QCD calculations of pressure, energy density, and entropy. The mass spectra of known hadrons from the PDG 2004 and 2016 listings are fitted to an exponential Hagedorn mass spectrum separately by particle families (light mesons, light baryons, strange mesons, strange baryons with |S|=1-3) to identify gaps and variations in the Hagedorn temperature. Dynamical multi-body interaction models using hypothetical Hagedorn states are then compared to experimental particle ratios from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers
- Light mesons and baryons are fitted to a Hagedorn temperature of T_H = 155 MeV, with light mesons deviating from exponential mass spectrum around M ~ 1.5 GeV and light baryons around M ~ 2 GeV
- Strange hadrons require a higher Hagedorn temperature of T_H = 175 MeV for fitting, except Omega baryons which require T_H = 300 MeV
- Light and strange hadrons from the PDG are measured up to m ~ 2.5 GeV
- The Hagedorn temperature T_H = 165 MeV is given as a reference value such that T_c ≤ T_H
- A gap in strange mesons is identified in the mass range m = 1-1.5 GeV
- |S|=1 and |S|=2 baryons fit exponential mass spectra up to M ~ 2 GeV and M ~ 1.7 GeV, respectively

## Context
This review extends work by Noronha-Hostler (2008, 2012) on missing resonances affecting transport coefficients in the hadron gas phase, and by Bazavov (2014) on missing strange baryons from Lattice QCD. It builds on Hagedorn's (1965) proposal of an exponentially increasing mass spectrum with a limiting temperature, and on earlier studies by Broniowski (2004), Chatterjee (2009), and Lo (2015) that separated known hadrons by families to extract individual mass spectra. The paper enables future systematic searches for missing resonances, particularly strange mesons between 1-1.5 GeV and |S|=3 baryons, and calls for a standardized database linking hadron spectroscopy to heavy-ion collision codes.
