# The $πNN$ and $πNN(1535)$ couplings in QCD

> The light cone QCD sum rules are derived for the $πNN$ coupling $g_{πNN}$ and $πNN(1535)$ coupling $g_{πNN^\ast}$. The contribution from the excited states and continuum is subtracted cleanly through double Borel transformation. We first improve the original sum rule for $g_{πNN}$ and determine the value of the pion wave function at the middle point $ϕ_π(u_0 ={1\over 2})=(1.5\pm 0.2)$ at $μ=1$GeV, which is a fundamental nonperturbative quantity like the quark condensate. Our calculation shows that the $πN N(1535)$ coupling is strongly suppressed.

## Key claim

The light cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR) are derived for the πNN coupling gπNN and the πNN(1535) coupling gπNN*, with the contribution from excited states and continuum subtracted cleanly through double Borel transformation. The authors improve the original sum rule for gπNN and determine the pion wave function at the middle point φπ(u0 = 1/2) = 1.5 ± 0.2 at μ = 1 GeV, and find that the πNN(1535) coupling is strongly suppressed.

## Method

The authors study the two-point correlation function of two nucleon currents sandwiched between the vacuum and the pion state, using the Ioffe nucleon interpolating field. They perform an operator product expansion on the light cone, keeping terms up to twist four, and apply a double Borel transformation with respect to the two external momenta p1² and p2² to subtract excited state and continuum contributions. For the πNN(1535) coupling, they use a general nucleon interpolating field with mixing parameter s and focus on the chiral odd tensor structure p̂γ5, which isolates the N* → N transition.

## Key numbers

- φπ(u0 = 1/2, μ = 1 GeV) = 1.5 ± 0.2, determined using the experimentally known gπNN = 13.5 as input
- gπNN* = (−)(0.08 ± 0.03) from the sum rule analysis, with a more conservative estimate of (−)(0.08 ± 0.06)
- The working interval for the Borel parameter M² in the gπNN analysis is 0.9 GeV² ≤ M² ≤ 1.8 GeV²
- The continuum threshold s0 = 2.25 GeV² for the nucleon sum rule
- The continuum threshold s1 = 3.2 GeV² for the N(1535) sum rule
- Parameters used: a = 0.55 GeV³, ⟨gs²G²⟩ = 0.48 GeV⁴, m0² = 0.8 GeV², mN = 0.938 GeV, mN* = 1.535 GeV, λN(s = −1) = 0.013 GeV³, λN*(s = 0.8) = 0.27 GeV³

## Context

This work extends the light cone QCD sum rule approach previously applied to pion couplings with heavy mesons and baryons (Belyaev 1995, Zhu 1997) to the πNN system, improving upon the earlier sum rule of Belyaev and Zyablyuk (1995) by including gluon condensate and quark-gluon mixed condensate contributions. The result for gπNN* is consistent with the coupled channel analysis of the Julich group (1997) and the chiral symmetry argument of Jido, Oka, and Hosaka (1998), but contradicts the estimate of Kim and Lee (1997) who found gπNN* ∼ 1.5. The determination of φπ(1/2) = 1.5 ± 0.2 supports the asymptotic form of the pion wave function and provides a fundamental nonperturbative quantity analogous to the quark condensate for use in other QCD sum rule analyses.
