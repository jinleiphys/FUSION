# Confronting axial-vector form factor from lattice QCD with MINERvA antineutrino-proton data

> We compare recent MINERvA antineutrino-hydrogen charged-current measurements to phenomenological predictions of the axial-vector form factor based on fits to all available electron scattering and deuterium bubble-chamber data and to representative lattice-QCD (LQCD) determination by the PNDME Collaboration. While there is $1$--$2σ$ agreement in the cross section with MINERvA data for each bin in $Q^2$, we identify three regions with different relevance and opportunity for LQCD predictions. For $Q^2 \lesssim 0.2~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, the phenomenological extractions have large number of data points and LQCD is competitive, while MINERvA data have large errors. For $0.2~\mathrm{GeV}^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, LQCD is competitive with the MINERvA determination, and both give values larger than from phenomenological extraction. For $Q^2 > 1~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, the MINERvA data are the most precise. Our analysis indicates that with improving precision of MINERvA-like experiments and LQCD data, the uncertainty in the nucleon axial-vector form factor will be steadily reduced.

# Confronting axial-vector form factor from lattice QCD with MINERvA antineutrino-proton data

## Key claim

The cross sections obtained using the PNDME lattice-QCD axial-vector form factor are in good agreement with the MINERvA hydrogen data within the Q² range of validity of these LQCD calculations (Q² ≲ 1 GeV²), showing 1–2σ agreement per bin, but a χ² test reveals approximately 2.5σ tension between the LQCD and fits to the deuterium bubble-chamber data.

## Method

The authors compared the MINERvA antineutrino-hydrogen charged-current elastic cross-section data to predictions based on the axial-vector form factor from (i) fits to deuterium bubble-chamber data using a z-expansion parameterization (Meyer 2016) and (ii) the PNDME lattice-QCD calculation (Jang 2023). They computed differential cross sections using the standard Llewellyn-Smith decomposition in terms of vector and axial-vector form factors, applying the same kinematic cuts as the MINERvA measurement (muon angle θμ ≤ 20°, momentum 1.5 GeV ≤ pμ ≤ 20 GeV), and performed χ² tests using the full covariance matrices over fifteen Q² bins with Q² ≲ 1 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The χ² test comparing MINERvA data to LQCD predictions gave χ² = 11 for 15 degrees of freedom; comparing MINERvA to deuterium predictions gave χ² = 12 for 15 degrees of freedom, both statistically insignificant.
- The χ²/d.o.f. between LQCD and deuterium predictions was approximately 6, implying about 2.5σ tension.
- The PNDME LQCD calculation analyzed 13 ensembles with lattice spacing 0.057 fm < a < 0.151 fm and pion masses Mπ ≈ 135, 220, 310 MeV.
- The PNDME results have ≲ 10% uncertainty in the Euclidean momentum transfer squared region 0 < Q² < 1 GeV².
- The kinematic limit for total cross sections with Q²max ≈ 1 GeV² corresponds to neutrino energy Eν ≲ 0.84 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between lattice-QCD calculations of the nucleon axial-vector form factor and experimental measurements, following the recent MINERvA measurement (MINERvA 2023) that isolated antineutrino scattering off hydrogen atoms free from nuclear corrections. The authors identify three Q² regions with different opportunities: for Q² ≲ 0.2 GeV², phenomenological extractions from deuterium data (Meyer 2016) have many data points and LQCD is competitive; for 0.2 GeV² ≲ Q² ≲ 1 GeV², LQCD is competitive with MINERvA and both give values larger than the phenomenological extraction; for Q² > 1 GeV², MINERvA data are the most precise. The analysis indicates that with improving precision of MINERvA-like experiments and LQCD data, the uncertainty in the nucleon axial-vector form factor will be steadily reduced, which is critical for neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE, T2K, and NOvA.
