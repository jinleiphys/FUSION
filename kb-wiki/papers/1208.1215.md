# Spontaneous fission modes and lifetimes of super-heavy elements in the nuclear density functional theory

> Lifetimes of super-heavy (SH) nuclei are primarily governed by alpha decay and spontaneous fission (SF). Here we study the competing decay modes of even-even SH isotopes with 108 <= Z <= 126 and 148 <= N <= 188 using the state-of-the-art self-consistent nuclear density functional theory framework capable of describing the competition between nuclear attraction and electrostatic repulsion. The collective mass tensor of the fissioning superfluid nucleus is computed by means of the cranking approximation to the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach. Along the path to fission, our calculations allow for the simultaneous breaking of axial and space inversion symmetries; this may result in lowering SF lifetimes by more than seven orders of magnitude in some cases. We predict two competing SF modes: reflection-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric.The shortest-lived SH isotopes decay by SF; they are expected to lie in a narrow corridor formed by $^{280}$Hs, $^{284}$Fl, and $^{284}_{118}$Uuo that separates the regions of SH nuclei synthesized in "cold fusion" and "hot fusion" reactions. The region of long-lived SH nuclei is expected to be centered on $^{294}$Ds with a total half-life of ?1.5 days.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that spontaneous fission (SF) half-lives of super-heavy nuclei can be lowered by more than seven orders of magnitude when the calculations allow for the simultaneous breaking of axial and space inversion symmetries along the fission path, a condition that previous studies often omitted. The authors claim that the region of long-lived super-heavy nuclei is centered on $^{294}$Ds with a total half-life of approximately 1.5 days, but this result depends on the specific scaling factor (0.7) applied to the collective ground state energy.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SkM* energy density functional and a density-dependent mixed pairing interaction, with the symmetry-unrestricted solver HFODD. The collective mass tensor was computed using the cranking approximation to the adiabatic time-dependent HFB (ATDHFB) approach, and fission pathways were explored by constraining multipole moments $Q_{20}$, $Q_{30}$, $Q_{22}$, and $Q_{40}$ to allow for elongation, reflection-asymmetry, triaxiality, and necking. The WKB approximation was used to compute penetrabilities and SF half-lives from the action integral along static fission pathways.

## Key numbers

- The SF half-life of $^{306}122$ is reduced from $10^{13.82}$ s (axially symmetric sEF) to $10^{9.39}$ s (triaxial sEF) and finally to $10^{6.22}$ s (reflection-asymmetric aEF), an overall reduction of about seven orders of magnitude.
- The maximum predicted SF half-life among the studied nuclei is $T_{sf} = 10^{7.76}$ s for $^{298}$Fl, with similar values for $^{300}$Lv and $^{302}_{120}$Udn.
- The shortest SF half-lives reach down to $10^{-10}$ s for nuclei in a narrow corridor formed by $^{280}$Hs, $^{284}$Fl, and $^{284}_{118}$Uuo.
- The predicted total half-life of $^{294}$Ds is $10^{5.13}$ s, approximately 1.5 days.
- The inner barrier of $^{306}122$ is reduced by approximately 3 MeV by triaxiality, and the outer barrier is reduced by about 1 MeV.
- The pairing strengths used were $V_{n0} = -268.9$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_{p0} = -332.5$ MeV fm$^3$, adjusted to reproduce pairing gaps in $^{252}$Fm.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field studies of super-heavy nuclei by Cwiok (1996) and others, and directly addresses the limitations of earlier microscopic-macroscopic calculations by Smolanczuk (1995, 1997) and axial HFB+D1S calculations by Warda (2012), which overestimated SF half-lives by many orders of magnitude due to imposed symmetry constraints. The results are consistent with axial Skyrme HF+BCS calculations by Erler and Schindzielorz (2009, 2012) regarding the corridor of maximum SF instability. The predictions enable experimental guidance for identifying decay modes of super-heavy nuclei synthesized in hot-fusion and cold-fusion reactions, and provide a benchmark for future calculations incorporating dynamical treatment of penetrability with multiple collective coordinates and improved energy density functionals.
