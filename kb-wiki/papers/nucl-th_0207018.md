# Anomalous J/psi suppression and charmonium dissociation cross sections

> We study $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energies in hadronic matter with energy- and temperature-dependent charmonium dissociation cross sections calculated in the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson. We find that the variation of J/$ψ$ survival probability from peripheral to central collisions can be explained as induced by hadronic matter absorption in central collisions.

## Key claim

The variation of J/ψ survival probability from peripheral to central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energies can be explained as induced by hadronic matter absorption in central collisions, without requiring a quark-gluon plasma, provided that the charmonium dissociation cross sections are taken to be temperature-dependent and rise with increasing temperature.

## Method

The authors calculated π-J/ψ, π-χc1, and π-χc2 dissociation cross sections using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson, with a temperature-dependent inter-quark potential inferred from lattice gauge results of Karsch et al. (2000). They then incorporated these cross sections into a model of Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV that includes nuclear absorption, pion formation and thermalization times, and the expansion and freeze-out of hadronic matter, fitting the adjustable thermalization time and transverse expansion velocity to NA50 data on B(J/ψ)σ(J/ψ)/σ(DY) versus transverse energy ET.

## Key numbers

- Peak π-J/ψ dissociation cross section at T=0 is about 1.1 mb; at freeze-out temperature Tfz=0.139 GeV it rises to 6.5 mb; at thermalization temperature Ttherm=0.16 GeV it is even higher.
- Peak π-χc1 dissociation cross section at T=0 is about 1.6 mb; at Tfz=0.139 GeV it rises to 4.1 mb.
- Peak π-χc2 dissociation cross section at T=0 is about 1.9 mb; at Tfz=0.139 GeV it rises to 4.8 mb.
- Average cross sections at thermalization time for the most central collision (b=0 fm) are: ⟨σπJ/ψ⟩ = 3.47 mb, ⟨σπχc1⟩ = 2.74 mb, ⟨σπχc2⟩ = 2.95 mb.
- Critical temperatures for spontaneous dissociation: Td/Tc = 0.99 for J/ψ, 0.90 for χc1, 0.91 for χc2, and 0.50 for ψ'.
- The thermalization temperature is Ttherm=0.16 GeV and freeze-out temperature is Tfz=0.139 GeV, with critical temperature Tc=0.175 GeV.
- For b>8.9 fm, no thermalization is attained and nuclear absorption (σNabs=4.2 mb) dominates J/ψ suppression.
- The constant ψ' dissociation cross section used to represent experimental data is σπψ'=12 mb.

## Context

This work extends the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson (1992, 1993) for charmonium dissociation cross sections by incorporating temperature dependence from lattice gauge results of Karsch et al. (2000). It contrasts with earlier parton-model approaches by Kharzeev and Satz (1996) that gave much smaller low-energy cross sections, and with meson-exchange models by Matinyan and Muller (1998), Haglin (2000), Lin and Ko (2000), Song and Lee (2001), and Navarra et al. (2001). The results enable comparison with NA50 and future NA60 data to test whether additional suppression beyond hadronic absorption, such as from quark-gluon plasma formation, is needed at large transverse energies.
