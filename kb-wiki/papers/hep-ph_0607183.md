# The model of particle production by strong external sources

> Using some knowledge of multiplicity disributions for high energy reactions, it is possible to propose a simple analytical model of particle production by strong external sources. The model describes qualitatively most peculiar properties of the distributions. The generating function of the distribution varies so drastically as it can happen at phase transitions.

# The model of particle production by strong external sources

## Key claim
The paper proposes a simple analytical model for particle production by strong external sources that reproduces the qualitative features of multiplicity distributions observed in high-energy reactions, including the oscillations of cumulant moments predicted by QCD. However, the model is explicitly phenomenological and relies on a guessed ansatz for the coefficients \(b_r\) that has not yet been derived from first-principles field theory calculations.

## Method
The author starts from a real scalar \(\phi^3\) field theory with a strong time-dependent external source \(j\), which serves as a toy model for the Color Glass Condensate formalism. Using the general expression for the generating function of the multiplicity distribution derived in prior work, the author proposes a two-parameter ansatz for the cut-diagram coefficients \(b_r = g^2 (x^r / r!) \cos[\phi(r-1)]\), where \(x\) and \(\phi\) are energy-dependent parameters. The generating function, cumulant moments, average multiplicity, and multiplicity distribution are then computed analytically from this ansatz.

## Key numbers
- For \(e^+e^-\) collisions at LEP energies, the first zero of cumulant moments \(q_1^{(0)}\) is located near \(q = 4\), and the average total multiplicity is about 40.
- The model parameters that approximately reproduce these values are \(x = 3\) and \(\phi = \pi/12\).
- The distance between neighboring zeros of cumulant moments is \(\Delta q^{(0)} = \pi / \phi = 12\).
- For the chosen parameters, the first extremum of the generating function is at \(u_1 = 2\), with \(G(2) \approx \exp[(\pi/12) e^6] \gg 1\); the next minimum is at \(u_2 = 6\), with \(G(6) \approx \exp[-(\pi/12) e^{18}] \ll 1\).
- The average number of cut subdiagrams for these parameters is \(\langle n_{cut} \rangle \approx 14\), and the average number of particles in a single cut subgraph is \(\langle n \rangle_1 \approx 3\).

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate-inspired approach of scalar field theory with strong external sources developed by Gribov, Vershkov, and others (Gribov and Vershkov, 2004; Gribov, Vershkov, and others, 2005). It directly addresses the QCD prediction of oscillating cumulant moments found by Dremin (1994) and Dremin and Nechitailo (1993, 1994), which was confirmed experimentally in reviews by Dremin and Gary (2001). The model enables future approximate computations of the cut-diagram coefficients \(b_r\) from Feynman diagrams, and its generating function behavior near \(u=2\) suggests a connection to phase transitions in statistical mechanics, analogous to the Lee-Yang theory of phase transitions (Lee and Yang, 1952).
