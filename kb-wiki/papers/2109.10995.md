# Charmonium in nuclear matter and nuclei

> We present results for the $η_c$-nucleus bound state energies for various nuclei using an effective Lagrangians approach. The attractive potentials for the $η_c$ in the nuclear medium originate from the medium-modified intermediate $D D^{*}$ state in the $η_c$ self energy, using the local density approximation. Our results suggest that the $η_c$ should form bound states with all the nuclei considered

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the ηc meson should form bound states with all nuclei considered (⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ¹⁹⁷Au, ²⁰⁸Pb), but the precise bound state energies depend on the cutoff parameter ΛD in the phenomenological vertex form factor, which introduces an uncertainty of several MeV.

## Method
The ηc self-energy in nuclear matter was computed from the medium-modified intermediate D D* state using an effective Lagrangian approach, with the in-medium D and D* meson masses calculated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The ηc mass shift as a function of nuclear density was obtained by solving the mass equation with a regulated self-energy integral, using a phenomenological form factor with cutoff ΛD varied between 1500 and 3000 MeV. Using the local density approximation, ηc-nucleus potentials were constructed from the density-dependent mass shift and nuclear density profiles, and bound state energies were obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation.

## Context
This work extends the approach previously applied to J/Ψ by Krein (2010) and Tsushima (2011), and to ηc by Cobos-Martinez (2020), where nuclear mean fields modify the intermediate charmed meson states to produce attraction. The results suggest that ηc-nucleus bound states should exist despite the OZI suppression of light-quark meson exchange, following the original suggestion by Brodsky (1989). The next step is to include an imaginary part to the ηc potentials to account for the ηc natural width of 32 MeV, which may affect observability of the predicted bound states.
