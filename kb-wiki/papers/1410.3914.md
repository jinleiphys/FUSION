# Unified Description of Efficiency Correction and Error Estimation for Moments of Conserved Quantities in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We provide a unified description of efficiency correction and error estimation for moments of conserved quantifies in heavy-ion collisions. Moments and cumulants are expressed in terms of the factorial moments, which can be easily corrected for the efficiency effect. By deriving the covariance between factorial moments, one can obtain the general error formula for the efficiency corrected moments based on the error propagation derived from the Delta theorem. The skellam distribution based Monto Carlo simulation is used to test the Delta theorem and Bootstrap error estimation methods. The statistical errors calculated from the two methods can well reflect the statistical fluctuations of the efficiency corrected moments.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified mathematical framework for correcting measured moments and cumulants of conserved quantities (e.g., net-proton number) for finite detector efficiency and simultaneously estimating their statistical errors, but the derivation assumes that the detection efficiency can be treated as a binomial response and that the efficiency values themselves are known with small uncertainties (relative errors ~5%).

## Method

The author expresses moments and cumulants of net-proton distributions in terms of bivariate factorial moments of proton and anti-proton numbers, which are corrected for efficiency by dividing by powers of the detection efficiency. The Delta theorem from statistics is then applied to derive analytical error propagation formulae for efficiency-corrected moments, using the covariance between factorial moments expressed via Stirling numbers of the first and second kind. The method is extended to phase-space-dependent efficiency by dividing the phase space into sub-regions with constant efficiency and connecting multivariate factorial moments across sub-regions. A Skellam-distribution-based Monte Carlo simulation is used to test the Delta theorem and Bootstrap error estimation methods.

## Key numbers

- The detection efficiency can be obtained with relative uncertainties of ~5% from Monte Carlo embedding techniques.
- The STAR Collaboration has published moment data for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, with data at 14.5 GeV taken in 2014.
- Explicit efficiency correction formulae are given for cumulants C1 through C4 of net-proton distributions for the constant-efficiency case (Eq. 13 in the paper).

## Context

This work extends earlier efficiency correction methods for moment analysis discussed by Voker (year not given) and the STAR Collaboration (2013, 2014), and builds on the Delta theorem error estimation approach previously applied only for unity efficiency (Luo, 2012). The unified description enables experimental analyses at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan program to correct for detector efficiency and compute statistical errors analytically rather than via computationally intensive Bootstrap resampling, which is important for the statistics-hungry search for the QCD critical point.
