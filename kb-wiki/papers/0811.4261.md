# Neutron transition strengths of $2^+_1$ states in the neutron rich Oxygen isotopes determined from inelastic proton scattering

> A coupled-channel analysis of the $^{18,20,22}$O$(p,p')$ data has been performed to determine the neutron transition strengths of 2$^+_1$ states in Oxygen targets, using the microscopic optical potential and inelastic form factor calculated in the folding model. A complex density- and \emph{isospin} dependent version of the CDM3Y6 interaction was constructed, based on the Brueckner-Hatree-Fock calculation of nuclear matter, for the folding model input. Given an accurate isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction, the isoscalar ($δ_0$) and isovector ($δ_1$) deformation lengths of 2$^+_1$ states in $^{18,20,22}$O have been extracted from the folding model analysis of the $(p,p')$ data. A specific $N$-dependence of $δ_0$ and $δ_1$ has been established which can be linked to the neutron shell closure occurring at $N$ approaching 16. The strongest isovector deformation was found for 2$^+_1$ state in $^{20}$O, with $δ_1$ about 2.5 times larger than $δ_0$, which indicates a strong core polarization by the valence neutrons in $^{20}$O. The ratios of the neutron/proton transition matrix elements ($M_n/M_p$) determined for 2$^+_1$ states in $^{18,20}$O have been compared to those deduced from the mirror symmetry, using the measured $B(E2)$ values of 2$^+_1$ states in the proton rich $^{18}$Ne and $^{20}$Mg nuclei, to discuss the isospin impurity in the $2^+_1$ excitation of the $A=18,T=1$ and $A=20,T=2$ isobars.

# Neutron transition strengths of 2⁺₁ states in the neutron rich Oxygen isotopes determined from inelastic proton scattering

## Key claim

The isoscalar (δ₀) and isovector (δ₁) deformation lengths of 2⁺₁ states in ¹⁸,²⁰,²²O have been extracted from a folding model analysis of (p,p') data using a complex density- and isospin-dependent CDM3Y6 interaction, with the caveat that the real isovector density dependence required an enhancement factor of 1.3 to account for the observed charge-exchange (p,n) data. The strongest isovector deformation was found for the 2⁺₁ state in ²⁰O, with δ₁ about 2.5 times larger than δ₀, indicating strong core polarization by valence neutrons, but the Mₙ/Mₚ ratio of 3.24 ± 0.20 for ²⁰O is significantly lower than the earlier estimate of approximately 4.2 from the same group's prior work.

## Method

A coupled-channel (CC) analysis of elastic and inelastic proton scattering data on ¹⁸,²⁰,²²O targets was performed using microscopic optical potentials and inelastic form factors calculated in the single-folding model. The effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was a complex, density- and isospin-dependent version of the CDM3Y6 interaction, with the isovector density dependence constructed based on Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter by Jeukenne, Lejeune and Mahaux (JLM). The proton deformation length δₚ was fixed by reproducing experimental B(E2) values, while the neutron deformation length δₙ was the only free parameter adjusted to fit the inelastic scattering cross sections, using both Bohr-Mottelson collective model transition densities and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) transition densities from Khan et al. (2002).

## Key numbers

- ¹⁸O 2⁺₁ state: δₚ = 1.04 ± 0.02 fm, δₙ = 1.23 ± 0.06 fm, Mₙ/Mₚ = 1.55 ± 0.08, δ₀ = 1.15 ± 0.04 fm, δ₁ = 1.87 ± 0.27 fm, M₁/M₀ = 0.22 ± 0.03
- ²⁰O 2⁺₁ state: δₚ = 0.82 ± 0.03 fm, δₙ = 1.95 ± 0.10 fm, Mₙ/Mₚ = 3.24 ± 0.20, δ₀ = 1.52 ± 0.06 fm, δ₁ = 3.75 ± 0.26 fm, M₁/M₀ = 0.59 ± 0.05
- ²²O 2⁺₁ state: δₚ = 0.70 ± 0.12 fm, δₙ = 0.91 ± 0.05 fm, Mₙ/Mₚ = 1.81 ± 0.33, δ₀ = 0.85 ± 0.05 fm, δ₁ = 1.14 ± 0.16 fm, M₁/M₀ = 0.43 ± 0.07
- The B(E2↑) value used for ¹⁸O was 45.1 ± 2.0 e²fm⁴ from Raman et al. (2001)
- The B(E3↑) value used for the 3⁻ state in ¹⁸O was 1120 ± 11 e²fm⁶ from Spear (1989)
- The renormalization factor for the real isovector density dependence of the CDM3Y6 interaction was set to 1.3

## Context

This work extends the earlier folding model analysis by Khoa et al. (2003) of the same ¹⁸,²⁰O(p,p') data, which had used a simpler isovector density dependence and found Mₙ/Mₚ ≈ 4.2 for ²⁰O. The revised analysis was motivated by a measurement of Coulomb excitation of ²⁰Mg by Iwasa et al. (2008), which suggested Mₙ/Mₚ ≈ 2.5 for ²⁰O based on mirror symmetry, raising questions about either the accuracy of the (p,p') analysis method or isospin impurity. The present results contradict the mirror-symmetry prediction for ¹⁸O, where the mirror method gives Mₙ/Mₚ ≈ 2.44 ± 0.18 while the (p,p') analysis yields 1.55 ± 0.08, suggesting isospin impurity in the A=18, T=1 isobars. The results establish a specific N-dependence of δ₀ and δ₁ linked to the neutron shell closure at N approaching 16, and enable future studies of core polarization effects in neutron-rich nuclei using inelastic proton scattering in inverse kinematics with radioactive beams.
