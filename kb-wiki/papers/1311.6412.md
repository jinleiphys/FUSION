# Collective dynamics of the p-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We review the signatures for the soft collective dynamics in highest-multiplicity ultrarelativistic p-Pb collisions and show that the effects are well described in a three-stage model, consisting of the event-by-event Glauber initial conditions, viscous 3+1D hydrodynamics, and statistical hadronization. In particular, we discuss the ridges in two-particle correlations in the relative azimuth and pseudorapidity, the elliptic and triangular flow coefficients, and the mass hierarchy of observables sensitive to flow.

# Collective dynamics of the p-Pb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The highest-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at the LHC exhibit signatures of soft collective dynamics that are well described by a three-stage model (Glauber initial conditions, viscous 3+1D hydrodynamics, and statistical hadronization), but the model departs from experimental data for lower multiplicity events, indicating that dissipative effects or direct production from the corona become important and that the collective approach has limits of validity.

## Method
The authors used a three-stage approach: event-by-event Glauber initial conditions implemented in GLISSANDO, followed by event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics for the intermediate evolution, and finally statistical hadronization at freezeout using THERMINATOR. They compared the model results to experimental data from the LHC for p-Pb collisions, analyzing two-particle correlation functions, harmonic flow coefficients, and mass hierarchy of identified particles.

## Key numbers
- The rms size of Glauber initial conditions for p-Pb collisions with fixed participant number N_p=18 is not more than twice smaller than the corresponding size for peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, while the p-Pb system is more dense.
- Elliptic flow coefficient v_2{2, |Δη > 2|} and triangular flow coefficient v_3{2, |Δη > 2|} from the model show fair agreement with CMS data for highest-multiplicity events, with departure for lower centrality classes.
- The model reproduces the mass hierarchy in mean transverse momentum of identified particles (protons higher than kaons, kaons higher than pions) in agreement with data from Abelev (2013), while HIJING 2.1 simulations show much smaller splitting with no collective effects.
- The differential elliptic flow v_2{2} for pions, kaons, and protons as a function of transverse momentum shows agreement with data from ABELEV (2013).

## Context
This work extends the collective flow framework previously established for ultra-relativistic A-A collisions at RHIC and the LHC to the smaller p-Pb system, showing clear analogies between the two systems. The authors provide an alternative to the color-glass approach of Dusling (2012, 2013) for explaining the ridge structures. The results enable using p-Pb collisions as a test ground for the onset of collective dynamics and for studying the limits of applicability of hydrodynamics, with lower multiplicity events requiring more involved modeling that accounts for corona nucleon production.
