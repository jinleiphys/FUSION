# Complex Scaled Spectrum Completeness for Coupled Channels

> The Complex Scaling Method (CSM) provides scattering wave functions which regularize resonances and suggest a resolution of the identity in terms of such resonances, completed by the bound states and a smoothed continuum. But, in the case of inelastic scattering with many channels, the existence of such a resolution under complex scaling is still debated. Taking advantage of results obtained earlier for the two channel case, this paper proposes a representation in which the convergence of a resolution of the identity can be more easily tested. The representation is valid for any finite number of coupled channels for inelastic scattering without rearrangement.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a representation (the "P representation") in which the convergence of a resolution of the identity under Complex Scaling can be more easily tested for any finite number of coupled channels in inelastic scattering without rearrangement, but the proof is restricted to well-separated channels with normal square root threshold singularities and short-ranged forces.

## Method

The authors generalize a two-channel method to N channels by introducing a "P representation" that maps the lowest and highest channel momenta via rational formulae k = P + Q²/P and K = P - Q²/P (with Q = e^(iθ)), opening the cuts for these two channels while leaving intermediate channel cuts closed. They then construct a contour integral of the Green's function in the P plane, evaluating three contributions: the upper semicircle at infinity (giving the multichannel identity), the continuum contributions from each channel (giving pseudoprojectors), and the residues at poles (giving bound states and resonances).

## Key numbers

- The highest channel threshold is normalized to E_N* = 4 for technical convenience.
- The locus of thresholds in the P plane satisfies |P| = 1, a simple result derived from the vanishing of both real and imaginary parts of k_j².
- The angular positions of thresholds in the P plane are η = θ + π/2 for the lowest channel and η = θ for the highest channel, with intermediate thresholds lying between these values.
- The function sin2η + sin(4θ-2η) decreases monotonically as η increases from θ to θ+π/2, giving a unique solution for each intermediate threshold E_j*.

## Context

This work extends earlier results for the two-channel case (Giraud, Kato, Ohnishi, 2004) to any finite number of coupled channels, building on the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) framework established by Aguilar, Balslev, and Combes (1971). The paper addresses the debated question of whether a resolution of the identity exists under complex scaling for multichannel inelastic scattering, and provides a representation that enables testing convergence for sum rules, level densities, and similar observables. The approach contrasts with R-matrix methods (reviewed by Barrett, Robson, and Tobocman) and time-dependent wave packet descriptions (Kukulin, Krasnopolsky, and Horáček; Michel and Liotta) that avoid complex scaling.
