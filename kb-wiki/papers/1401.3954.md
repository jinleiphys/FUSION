# The nucleon spin decomposition: news and experimental implications

> Recently, many nucleon spin decompositions have been proposed in the literature, creating a lot of confusion. This revived in particular old controversies regarding the measurability of theoretically defined quantities. We propose a brief overview of the different decompositions, discuss the sufficient requirements for measurability and stress the experimental implications.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Stueckelberg/background/path dependence issue that plagues gauge-invariant nucleon spin decompositions is in practice solved by the framework used to analyze actual experiments, and that no particular decomposition should be discarded from a purely physical point of view, although some appear easier to determine experimentally.

## Method

The author reviews the four main kinds of proton spin decompositions (Jaffe-Manohar, Chen et al., Ji, and Wakamatsu) and compares their definitions of quark/gluon spin and orbital angular momentum (OAM) contributions. The paper then examines the Chen et al. approach of splitting the gauge potential into pure-gauge and physical parts, discusses its non-uniqueness via Stueckelberg transformations, and links the experimental resolution of this ambiguity to QCD factorization theorems where Wilson lines run along the light-front direction.

## Key numbers

- The quark spin contribution is common to all four decompositions: \(\uvec S^q_\text{JM}=\uvec S^q_\text{Ji}=\uvec S^q_\text{Chen}=\uvec S^q_\text{Wak}=\int\ud^3x\,\psi^\dag\tfrac{1}{2}\uvec\Sigma\psi\).
- The potential OAM difference between Chen et al. and Wakamatsu decompositions is given by \(\uvec L_\text{pot}=-\int\ud^3x\,(\uvec{\mathcal D}\cdot\uvec E)^a\,\uvec x\times\uvec A^a_\phys=\uvec L^g_\text{Wak}-\uvec L^g_\text{Chen}=\uvec L^q_\text{Chen}-\uvec L^q_\text{Wak}\).
- The Ji decomposition does not split total gluon angular momentum: \(\uvec J^g_\text{Ji}=\uvec S^g_\text{Wak}+\uvec L^g_\text{Wak}=\int\ud^3x\,\uvec x\times(\uvec E^a\times\uvec B^a)\).

## Context

This work extends the debate sparked by Chen et al. (2008, 2009), who challenged textbook knowledge by providing a gauge-invariant decomposition of gluon angular momentum into spin and OAM contributions. The paper builds on the review by Leader (2013) and the author's own prior work (Lorce, 2012) to clarify that the experimental framework based on QCD factorization theorems naturally selects the condition \(A^+_\phys(x)=0\), which allows interpretation of the measured quantity \(\Delta G\) as gauge-invariant gluon spin and links OAM to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum dependent GPDs (GTMDs).
