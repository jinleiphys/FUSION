# Transverse Structure of Nucleon Parton Distributions from Lattice QCD

> This work presents the first calculation in lattice QCD of three moments of spin-averaged and spin-polarized generalized parton distributions in the proton. It is shown that the slope of the associated generalized form factors decreases significantly as the moment increases, indicating that the transverse size of the light-cone quark distribution decreases as the momentum fraction of the struck parton increases.

## Key claim

The slope of the generalized form factors A_{n0}(t) and \tilde A_{n0}(t) decreases significantly as the moment index n increases, indicating that the transverse size of the light-cone quark distribution decreases as the momentum fraction of the struck parton increases, but this result is obtained in the "heavy pion world" (pion masses of 744 and 897 MeV) and the authors note that the commonly used factorization Ansatz H(x,0,t)=Q(x)F(t) is fundamentally wrong in this regime, with no argument that it should be restored for lighter quarks.

## Method

The authors calculated the generalized form factors A_{(n=1,2,3),0}(t) and \tilde A_{(n=1,2,3),0}(t) from off-diagonal nucleon matrix elements of the tower of twist-two operators in full lattice QCD using unquenched SESAM configurations on 16^3 x 32 lattices. They used all combinations of momenta that produce the same four-momentum transfer t below 3.5 GeV, calculated all hypercubic lattice operators and index combinations that produce the same continuum generalized form factors, and extracted statistically accurate measurements from an overdetermined set of equations.

## Key numbers

- For the flavor non-singlet combination at m_pi = 897 MeV: A_{1,0}^{u-d}(0) = 1.000 +/- 0.001 with dipole mass m_d = 1.470 +/- 0.031 GeV and transverse rms radius = 0.380 +/- 0.008 fm
- For the flavor non-singlet combination at m_pi = 897 MeV: A_{3,0}^{u-d}(0) = 0.060 +/- 0.008 with dipole mass m_d = 3.857 +/- 0.494 GeV and transverse rms radius = 0.145 +/- 0.019 fm
- The non-singlet transverse size drops 62% from 0.38 fm for n=1 to 0.14 fm for n=3 at m_pi = 897 MeV
- For the flavor singlet combination at m_pi = 897 MeV: A_{1,0}^{u+d}(0) = 2.998 +/- 0.002 with transverse rms radius = 0.463 +/- 0.005 fm
- For the flavor singlet combination at m_pi = 897 MeV: A_{3,0}^{u+d}(0) = 0.155 +/- 0.018 with transverse rms radius = 0.266 +/- 0.019 fm
- The singlet size drops 43% from 0.46 fm for n=1 to 0.27 fm for n=3 at m_pi = 897 MeV
- For the spin-dependent flavor non-singlet at m_pi = 897 MeV: \tilde A_{1,0}^{u-d}(0) = 1.195 +/- 0.014 with transverse rms radius = 0.302 +/- 0.005 fm
- For the spin-dependent flavor non-singlet at m_pi = 897 MeV: \tilde A_{3,0}^{u-d}(0) = 0.123 +/- 0.004 with transverse rms radius = 0.250 +/- 0.010 fm
- The transverse charge radius at m_pi = 897 MeV is 0.48 fm, compared to the experimental transverse size of 0.72 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Burkardt (2000) connecting generalized parton distributions to impact parameter dependent parton distributions, and provides the first lattice QCD calculation of three moments of both spin-averaged and spin-polarized generalized parton distributions in the proton. The results for n=2 moments are consistent with those of Gockeler (2003). The calculations enable future work extending to the chiral regime of realistic quark masses using hybrid calculations of dynamical staggered sea quarks and domain wall valence quarks, as explored by Schroers (2003), and will complement experimental measurements of the full x, xi, and t dependence of H(x,xi,t) and \tilde H(x,xi,t).
