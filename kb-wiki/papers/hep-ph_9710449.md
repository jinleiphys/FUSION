# Spin-dependent Polarizability of Nucleon with Dispersion Relation in the Skyrme Model

> We calculate the spin-dependent polarizability of the nucleon in the Skyrme model. The result is compared with that of a heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory(HBChPT), and is shown to be the same as that of HBChPT up to the $Δ$-pole terms in the narrow width limit of the $Δ$ state and with the experimental physical constants. The effect of the $Δ+π$ channel is rather small and is numerically quite similar to that of the $Δ$ loop in the HBChPT. The electric and magnetic polarizabilities are recalculated using the transverse photon and a consistent inclusion of the $Δ$ width.

# Spin-dependent Polarizability of Nucleon with Dispersion Relation in the Skyrme Model

## Key claim

The spin-dependent polarizability of the nucleon calculated in the Skyrme model agrees with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) results, but only up to the Delta-pole terms and only in the narrow width limit of the Delta state when experimental physical constants are used; with a finite Delta width, the numerical value changes significantly.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-dependent polarizability gamma using a once-subtracted dispersion relation that integrates the helicity-difference photo-absorption cross section (sigma_{1/2} - sigma_{3/2}) over photon energy. The photo-absorption cross sections are calculated from Born amplitudes for the processes gamma+N -> pi+N and gamma+N -> pi+Delta in the Skyrme model, including electric and magnetic Born terms with explicit treatment of the Delta resonance width. The electric and magnetic polarizabilities are also recalculated using transverse photons with consistent inclusion of the Delta width.

## Key numbers

- The spin-dependent polarizability gamma for parameter Set III (empirical constants) is -0.1 x 10^{-4} fm^4, compared to the HBChPT result of [4.5 (N loop) - 4.0 (Delta pole) - 0.4 (Delta loop)] x 10^{-4} fm^4
- In the narrow width limit, gamma_M^N = -4.0 x 10^{-4} fm^4; with finite width, gamma_M^N = -2.5 x 10^{-4} fm^4
- The electric Born contribution gamma_E^N = 5.0 x 10^{-4} fm^4 for Set III, matching the N-loop term in HBChPT
- The Delta width Gamma_Delta = 145 MeV at q = 227 MeV using the experimental G_{NNpi}
- The magnetic polarizability beta for Set III is 9.8 x 10^{-4} fm^3, compared to the HBChPT result of [1.2 (N loop) + 12 (Delta pole) + 1.5 (Delta loop)] x 10^{-4} fm^3
- The electric polarizability alpha for Set III is 22.1 x 10^{-4} fm^3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of electric and magnetic polarizabilities in the Skyrme model (Saito, 1995) to the spin-dependent polarizability gamma, and compares directly with HBChPT calculations by Bernard et al. (1992) and Hemmert et al. (1997). The Skyrme model treats the N and Delta states as equal rotational levels, unlike HBChPT where the Delta is introduced as an explicit degree of freedom. The results enable a consistent treatment of the Delta width in dispersion-relation calculations of nucleon polarizabilities, resolving discrepancies found in earlier work where the width was inconsistently included.
