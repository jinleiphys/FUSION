# Phase Space and Dynamical Fluctuations of Kaon--to--Pion Ratios

> The dynamical fluctuations of kaon--to--pion ratios have been studied over a wide range of center--of--mass energies $\sqrt{s}$. Based on changing phase space volume which apparently is the consequence of phase transition from hadrons to quark--gluon plasma at large $\sqrt{s}$, single--particle distribution function $f$ is assumed to be rather modified. Varying $f$ and phase space volume are implemented in the grand--canonical partition function, especially at large $\sqrt{s}$, so that hadron resonance gas model, when taking into account the experimental acceptance and quark phase space occupation factor, turns to be able to reproduce the dynamical fluctuations over the entire range of $\sqrt{s}$.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, when modified by a changing phase space volume and a modified single-particle distribution function, can reproduce the dynamical fluctuations of kaon-to-pion ratios over the entire range of center-of-mass energies, but only if the quark phase space occupation factor and a phase space factor are both implemented; the model does not provide a theory for the energy dependence of these modifications, which would require QCD.

## Method

The author modifies the grand-canonical partition function of the hadron resonance gas model by introducing a phase space factor Q that accounts for the change in phase space volume when the system transitions from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma at high center-of-mass energies. The single-particle distribution function is derived from a Boltzmann-Vlasov master equation and expressed in a Tsallis-like form, with the phase space factor Q appearing as a measure of non-extensivity. The dynamical fluctuations of the (K++K-)/(pi++pi-) ratio are then computed from the susceptibilities, including contributions from resonance decays and branching ratios, and compared with experimental data from SPS and RHIC.

## Key numbers

* The dynamical fluctuations of (K++K-)/(pi++pi-) show a non-monotonic behavior over the full range of center-of-mass energies sqrt(s), with a prompt rise at sqrt(s) approximately 17 GeV.
* The HRG model with only the quark phase space occupation factor gamma reproduces SPS data (below sqrt(s) ~ 17 GeV) but underestimates RHIC data; the upper curve including the phase space factor Q matches RHIC data.
* The phase space factor Q is roughly estimated as Q(x,k) approximately exp(2 omega x k) and is kept unchanged with varying sqrt(s).
* The ratio s/T^3 (entropy density over temperature cubed) is assumed constant at chemical freeze-out.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of particle ratio fluctuations using the HRG model (Tawfik, 2009) and addresses the inability of the standard HRG with only the quark phase space occupation factor to describe RHIC data. The concept of phase space dominance originates from Fermi (1950), and the non-monotonic behavior of kaon-to-pion fluctuations is related to the "horn" at top SPS energy discussed in prior work on kaon production. The results enable a phenomenological connection between dynamical fluctuations and the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, and the author notes that the non-extensivity parameter q in the Tsallis distribution will be related to the phase space factor Q in future work.
