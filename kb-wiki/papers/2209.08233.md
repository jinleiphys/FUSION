# Fluctuations of conserved charges in hydrodynamics and molecular dynamics

> I present an overview of recent theoretical results on fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions obtained in relativistic hydrodynamics and molecular dynamics frameworks. In particular, I discuss the constraints on the location of the QCD critical point based on comparisons of experimental data on proton number cumulants with precision calculations of non-critical contributions. Recent developments on critical fluctuations in molecular dynamics simulations are covered as well.

## Key claim

The analysis of (net-)proton number cumulants across collision energies disfavors the existence of the QCD critical point at small baryon densities (μ_B/T < 2–3), but the data at lower energies (√s_NN ≲ 20 GeV) show excess proton correlations over non-critical baselines that require further analysis before a firm conclusion on the critical point location can be reached.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of non-critical contributions to proton number fluctuations, specifically baryon number conservation and repulsive interactions modeled via excluded volume, implemented at the Cooper-Frye particlization stage of relativistic hydrodynamic simulations (MUSIC code). For lower energies, a Siemens-Rasmussen-like fireball model is used to extract baryon number susceptibilities from fits to proton cumulant data. Separately, classical molecular dynamics simulations of the Lennard-Jones fluid are performed in a periodic box within the microcanonical ensemble to study particle number fluctuations near the critical point, with fluctuations analyzed in both coordinate and momentum subspaces.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions), the normalized net-proton variance κ₂[p−p̄]/⟨p+p̄⟩ is sensitive to baryon conservation range and baryon annihilation; local baryon conservation across Δy_cor ∼ 3 units of rapidity can describe the data.
- At RHIC-BES energies (√s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV), experimental data are quantitatively consistent with non-critical baryon conservation and repulsion at √s_NN ≳ 20 GeV.
- At √s_NN = 2.4 GeV (HADES), the proton scaled variance κ₂/κ₁ > 2, and the data are described by a thermal emission model with baryon number susceptibilities exhibiting the hierarchy χ₄ᴮ ≫ −χ₃ᴮ ≫ χ₂ᴮ ≫ χ₁ᴮ, with freeze-out at T ∼ 70 MeV and μ_B ∼ 850–900 MeV.
- In Lennard-Jones molecular dynamics simulations near the critical point (T ≃ 1.06 T_c, n ≃ n_c), the grand-canonical scaled variance ω^gce ∼ 7 is approached as system size increases, but critical fluctuations are completely washed out when analyzing momentum-space cuts.

## Context

This review extends the line of work comparing experimental proton number cumulant data from ALICE (2019), STAR (2020, 2021), NA61/SHINE (2017), and HADES (2020) with non-critical baseline calculations from hydrodynamics (Vovchenko 2021) and molecular dynamics (Kuznietsov 2022). The results constrain the QCD critical point location to the baryon-rich regime (√s_NN ≲ 20 GeV), consistent with lattice QCD constraints (Bazavov 2017, Borsanyi 2021), and motivate future development of hydrodynamic frameworks with critical fluctuations (Stephanov 2017, Pradeep 2022) and expanding-system molecular dynamics simulations.
