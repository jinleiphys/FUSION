# Meson spin alignment and baryon polarization from coalescence with spin-vorticity non-equilibrium

> We estimate both the polarization of spin $1/2$ baryons and the spin density matrix coefficients of spin $1$ mesons using a thermal model that incorporates vorticity and polarization to describe quarks, along with a coalescence formalism where spin and vorticity are not in equilibrium. We find that while our model is not predictive due to its considerable number of free parameters, it has the potential to explain several seemingly puzzling features of experimental spin measurements, such as the absence of baryon polarization alongside significant vector meson spin alignment. We suggest the measuring meson spin off-diagonal matrix elements, and examining the dependence of polarization observables on azimuthal angles, as methods to falsify this model and gain insights into the freezeout details of baryon and meson spin structure.

## Key claim
The authors show that a thermal model with spin-vorticity non-equilibrium combined with coalescence can potentially explain the puzzling coexistence of absent baryon polarization with significant vector meson spin alignment, but they explicitly state the model is not predictive due to its considerable number of free parameters.

## Method
The authors combine a blast wave model augmented with thermal vorticity (from FLO) with a coalescence formalism for hadron production (from kayman1) to compute both the spin density matrix elements of vector mesons and the polarization of spin-1/2 baryons. The model incorporates two distinct vorticities: a global vorticity formed in the initial state and a local vorticity formed on the time-scale of elliptic flow, with the latter less equilibrated with spin than the former. The coalescence equations are convoluted with the blast wave distribution to obtain the full momentum, rapidity, and angular dependence of the observables.

## Key numbers
- The blast wave model parameters (delta and epsilon) are extracted from a fit to 130 GeV spectra and elliptic flow data from bro and FLO.
- The authors reproduce results from xia by setting theta_r = Delta-psi, phi_r = -pi/2, and Omega/T = 1/5 for L = 0.
- The longitudinal polarization chi_{1/2} for baryons is defined as (rho^B_{1/2,1/2} - rho^B_{-1/2,-1/2})/(rho^B_{1/2,1/2} + rho^B_{-1/2,-1/2}).

## Context
This work extends the coalescence model for vector mesons from kayman1 and kayman2 to include baryons, and combines it with the blast wave vorticity model from FLO. It addresses phenomenological puzzles noted in the literature: local polarization having the wrong sign (review) and vector meson spin alignment not being described by theoretical models (vector), which has been interpreted as suggesting new physics such as classical fields (classic0, classic1, classic2, classic3) or spin distillation (kayman2). The authors suggest that measuring meson spin off-diagonal matrix elements and examining the azimuthal angle dependence of polarization observables could falsify their model and provide insights into freezeout details of baryon and meson spin structure.
