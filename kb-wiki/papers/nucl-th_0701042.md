# Short-range Three-Nucleon Forces Effects on Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering

> Effects of three-nucleon forces arising from the exchange of a pion and a scalar-isoscalar object among three nucleons on nucleon-deuteron scattering observables are studied.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether three-nucleon forces (3NFs) from pion–scalar-isoscalar exchange can improve the description of nucleon-deuteron scattering observables, particularly tensor analyzing powers, where the standard two-pion-exchange 3NF alone gives insufficient or undesirable contributions.

## Method

The author constructs coordinate-space models of π–S 3NFs, including π–σ exchange via the Roper resonance, Z-diagrams with pseudovector or pseudoscalar πNN coupling, linear and nonlinear σ models, and π–effective-2π exchange. These are added to the Argonne V18 two-nucleon force and the Brazil 2πE 3NF in Faddeev calculations of the triton binding energy and nucleon-deuteron scattering observables at 3 MeV and 28 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The Argonne V18 two-nucleon force underbinds the triton (³H) by 0.85 MeV.
- The Brazil 2πE 3NF gives an additional attraction of -1.75 MeV to the triton.
- The (π–σ)Z,PV 3NF contributes +0.47 MeV to the triton energy (repulsive relative to the 2πE attraction).
- The (π–2π) 3NF contributes +0.74 MeV to the triton energy (repulsive).
- The (π–σ)N* 3NF contributes -0.32 MeV (attractive).
- The (π–σ)Z,PS 3NF contributes -1.04 MeV (attractive).
- The linear σ-model 3NF contributes -1.99 MeV (attractive).
- The nonlinear σ-model 3NF contributes +0.23 MeV (repulsive).
- Cutoff masses used: Λπ = 800 MeV, Λσ = 1300 MeV (except Λσ = 800 MeV for the π–2π model in scattering calculations).
- The π–2π model uses parameters: α₀₀⁺ = 3.68, gs = 4.36, ms = 393 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author’s previous studies (Ishikawa 2003, 2004) showing that tensor components in the standard two-pion-exchange 3NF cause problems for tensor analyzing powers in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering. The π–S 3NF models provide tensor interactions with different characteristics that can partially compensate the 2πE 3NF effects. The results suggest that further measurements of T₂₁(θ) and ΔσT – ΔσL at various energies could help determine which additional 3NF components are needed beyond the 2πE 3NF.
