# AdS/CFT and the Double-Copy: A Unified Perspective Across Various Phenomenological Models

> This study utilizes theoretical tools including the double copy relation and AdS/CFT correspondence. Applying analogies, we establish a system to discuss the associations and equivalence relations among QCD, QED, and various (mixed) gauge field theory based quantum many-body effective models. By employing mathematical techniques on the background spacetime, these relations are extended to extreme regimes while ensuring model independence.

## Key claim

This work proposes that the double copy relation and AdS/CFT correspondence can be unified into a single phenomenological framework that spans QCD, QED, and various gauge-theory-based quantum many-body effective models, but the claim is explicitly hedged: the framework is built on analogies and mathematical techniques applied to background spacetime, and the author states it ensures "model independence" only while simultaneously acknowledging that the construction remains "phenomenologically oriented" and requires "some additional string-theoretic ingredients" to approach a general quantum field theory.

## Method

The author first reviews the double copy relation for scattering amplitudes and classical observables, then extends it to position space using convolution operators and light-front holographic QCD to encode loop contributions into an effective running coupling. The AdS/CFT correspondence is employed to relate bulk gauge fields to boundary conformal field theory correlators via bulk-to-boundary propagators and dilaton modifications, and the two frameworks are combined by constructing a "degenerate" version where the double copy partner field acts as a modification operator that incorporates loop-level contributions through dilaton factors \(e^{-L\phi}\). The resulting unified expression is then applied to derive correlation functions and form factors for various QCD matter states (hadronic, nuclear pasta, color-superconducting) and QED-based quasiparticle effective theories.

## Key numbers

- Running coupling in light-front holographic QCD: \(a_s \approx a_s(0) e^{-\kappa^2 z^2}\) with \(\beta(a_s) = -\beta_0 a_s^2/(2\pi) - \beta_1 a_s^3/(2\pi)^2 - \beta_2 a_s^4/(2\pi)^3 - \cdots\)
- Bulk-to-boundary propagator: \(\tilde{V}(Q^2, z) = |\lambda| z^2 \int_0^1 \frac{dx}{(1-x)^2} x^{Q^2/4|\lambda|} e^{-|\lambda| z^2 x/(1-x)}\)
- Form factor in AdS bulk: \(\mathcal{F}(0,z) = F_0 C_\Delta e^{-\kappa^2 z^2/2} (\kappa z)^{2\Delta} U(\Delta/2, \Delta, \kappa^2 z^2)\) with \(C_\Delta = \Gamma(\Delta/2) \kappa^{-\Delta} / (c_\Delta \Gamma(\Delta))\) and \(c_\Delta = \Gamma(\Delta) / (\pi^{d/2} \Gamma(\Delta - d/2))\)
- Chromomagnetic screening mass in color-superconducting state: \(\kappa = \sqrt{ \frac{g^2 \mu_q^2}{2\pi^2} \left(1 - \frac{\Delta^2}{\mu_q^2} K\left(\frac{\Delta}{\mu_q}\right)\right) }\) where \(K(x) = \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin t}{t} e^{-xt} dt\)
- QCD scale parameter: \(\Lambda^2 = \mu^2 e^{-4\pi/(\beta_0 g_0^2)}\) at \(\beta_0\) order

## Context

This work extends prior developments in double copy theory (Bern 2010, Lin 2022) and AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1998, Witten 1998) by attempting to merge them into a single phenomenological framework applicable across different interaction strengths and energy scales. It builds on light-front holographic QCD (Brodsky 2010) for non-perturbative QCD characterization, and references prior work on double copy in AdS space (Alday 2011, Bissi 2012, Goncalves 2021) and on nuclear pasta configurations (Schneider 2016, Caplan 2022). The framework aims to enable analytical tools from one effective model to be transferred to others, potentially providing a unified description spanning from hadronic states through nuclear pasta to color-superconducting matter, but the author acknowledges the construction remains phenomenological and requires further verification.
