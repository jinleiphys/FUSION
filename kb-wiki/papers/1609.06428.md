# Coupled-channels description of the 40Ca+58,64Ni transfer and fusion reactions

> Preliminary experimental data for nucleon transfer reactions of the 40Ca+58Ni and 40Ca+64Ni systems are analyzed with the coupled- channels approach. It is shown that a simple treatment for the transfer in the coupled-channels method cannot reproduce simultaneously the transfer probabilities and the sub-barrier enhancement of fusion cross sections.

# Coupled-channels description of the 40Ca+58,64Ni transfer and fusion reactions

## Key claim

A simple treatment of transfer in the coupled-channels method cannot simultaneously reproduce the measured transfer probabilities and the sub-barrier enhancement of fusion cross sections for the 40Ca+64Ni system, although it works for the 40Ca+58Ni system where the transfer effect is too small to enhance fusion.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using the CCFULL program modified to compute neutron and proton transfer probabilities, with Woods-Saxon real and imaginary potentials and coupling parameters adjusted to reproduce experimental transfer data. Transfer probabilities were computed as the ratio of transfer cross sections to elastic plus inelastic cross sections, and the model assumed pair transfer is a sum of direct pair transfer and sequential transfer with the sequential coupling equal to the single transfer coupling. The calculations included excitation to the first 3- state in 40Ca and the first 2+ states in 58,64Ni.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca+58Ni: neutron transfer coupling constant a01 = 1.96 fm with Q01 = -3.85 MeV; proton transfer a01 = 1.96 fm with Q01 = -4.909 MeV
- For 40Ca+64Ni: neutron transfer coupling constant a01 = 1.16 fm with Q01 = -1.29 MeV; proton transfer a01 = 1.16 fm with Q01 = -0.874 MeV
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: V0 = 60.41 MeV for 40Ca+58Ni and V0 = 68.70 MeV for 40Ca+64Ni, with diffuseness a = 0.66 fm for both systems
- Transfer probabilities for 40Ca+64Ni are about one order of magnitude higher than for 40Ca+58Ni, as expected from the Q-value
- When fitting transfer coupling to reproduce fusion cross sections instead, the predicted transfer probabilities are overestimated by about a factor of 10

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Scamps (2015) on the 96Zr+40Ca reaction, where a slight underestimation of fusion cross sections was found at low energies, and contrasts with the results of Esbensen (2016) who showed that including proton transfer channels could perfectly reproduce fusion cross sections. The present analysis tests this approach on the 40Ca+58,64Ni systems measured by Bourgin (2014), where TDHF theory by Bourgin (2016) indicated the major difference between the two systems is due to neutron transfer. The results suggest that more complex models may be necessary, as the current coupling scheme treats collective excitations and transfer channels as independent degrees of freedom, which may be a limitation of the method used by Scamps (2015), Esbensen (2016), and Esbensen (1989).
