# Excitation Function of Energy Density and Partonic Degrees of Freedom in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We estimate the energy density pile-up at mid-rapidity in central Pb+Pb collisions from 2 - 200 GeV/nucleon. The energy density is decomposed into hadronic and partonic contributions. A detailed analysis of the collision dynamics in the framework of a microscopic transport model shows the importance of partonic degrees of freedom and rescattering of leading (di)quarks in the early phase of the reaction for lab-energies > 30 GeV/nucleon. In Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon the energy density reaches up to 4 GeV/fm^3, 95% of which are contained in partonic degrees of freedom.

# Excitation Function of Energy Density and Partonic Degrees of Freedom in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon reaches up to 4 GeV/fm³, with 95% contained in partonic degrees of freedom, but this does not imply formation of an equilibrated Quark-Gluon Plasma, the partonic phase in the UrQMD model is modeled as an incoherent superposition of non-interacting partonic constituents that retain their original correlation into hadrons, and deconfinement is not implemented.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) microscopic transport model, which represents hadrons as Gaussian wave packets in phase space propagated via Hamilton's equations of motion, with 55 baryon species and 32 meson species including resonances. Particle production at high energies is modeled via string fragmentation, where particles produced during string fragmentation are assigned a formation time; all contributions from hadrons within their formation time are termed "partonic" and those from fully formed hadrons are termed "hadronic". Energy densities in the central reaction zone were obtained by summing analytically over all Gaussian hadrons around mid-rapidity (y_cm ± 1) at the locations of these hadrons and then averaging over these energy densities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum total energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon: 4 GeV/fm³ (at t ≈ 1 fm/c, around midrapidity Δy = 1)
- 95% of the energy density at 160 GeV/nucleon is contained in partonic degrees of freedom
- At 40 GeV/nucleon, the maximum fraction of partonic energy density exceeds 90% of the total energy density
- At 10.6 GeV/nucleon, the number of constituent quarks propagating through high partonic energy density (ε_Q ≥ 2 GeV/fm³) is negligible
- At 160 GeV/nucleon, more than 1000 valence quarks are present over a time scale of Δt ≈ 2 fm/c at ε_Q ≥ 2 GeV/fm³
- Without rapidity cut, energy densities during the early reaction stage (t ≈ 1 fm/c) can reach 20 GeV/fm³
- Energy density calculated by summing over particles in a central cell (2×2×1 fm³) can yield up to 30 GeV/fm³

## Context
This work extends the use of hadronic transport models to CERN/SPS energies, where energy density estimates had been claimed to exceed the critical energy density for a deconfinement phase transition from Lattice Gauge Theory (Blum 1995, Boyd 1995, Laermann 1996). The study demonstrates that hadronic transport models contain implicit partonic degrees of freedom through string production and fragmentation (Andersson 1987, Sjostrand 1994), and that these partonic degrees of freedom dominate both the energy density and collision dynamics in the first 5 fm/c at beam energies above 30 GeV/nucleon. The results enable future work comparing this approach with parton cascade models (Geiger 1992) and models incorporating explicit color dynamics and increasing cross sections for partonic constituents (Gerland 1998), to determine whether the partonic phase resembles a non-interacting free streaming parton gas or a strongly interacting, nearly equilibrated soup of partons.
