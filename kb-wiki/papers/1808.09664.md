# Interactions between vector mesons and dynamically generated resonances

> The $ρρ$ interaction and the corresponding dynamically generated bound states are revisited. We demonstrate that an improved unitarization method is necessary to study the pole structures of amplitudes outside the near-threshold region. In this work, we extend the study of the covariant $ρρ$ scattering in a unitarized chiral theory to the $S$-wave interactions for the whole vector-meson nonet. We demonstrate that there are unphysical left-hand cuts in the on-shell factorization approach of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. This is in conflict with the correct analytic behavior and makes the so-obtained poles, corresponding to possible bound states or resonances, unreliable. To avoid this difficulty, we employ the first iterated solution of the $N/D$ method and investigate the possible dynamically generated resonances from vector-vector interactions. A comparison with the results from the nonrelativistic calculation is provided as well.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the on-shell factorization approach of the Bethe-Salpeter equation introduces unphysical left-hand cuts that make the resulting poles unreliable, and they employ the first iterated solution of the N/D method to investigate dynamically generated resonances from vector-vector interactions, finding that while a bound state pole corresponding to the f0(1370) exists, no pole can be associated with the f2(1270).

## Method

The authors use the hidden local symmetry approach with the parameter a=2 to derive tree-level vector-vector scattering amplitudes for the entire vector-meson nonet (ρ, K*, ω, φ) in the S-wave, projecting the amplitudes onto partial waves with total angular momentum J=0, 1, and 2. They compare two unitarization methods: the on-shell factorization of the Bethe-Salpeter equation and the first iterated solution of the N/D dispersion relation, applying these to single-channel and coupled-channel systems classified by strangeness S and isospin I.

## Key numbers

- The ρρ interaction in the (I,J)=(0,0) channel produces a bound state assigned to the f0(1370), while no bound state is found in the (I,J)=(0,2) channel despite the attractive strength at threshold being twice that of the scalar sector
- The binding momentum for the f2(1270) in the nonrelativistic calculation reaches about 445 MeV, indicating relativistic effects must be considered
- The pion decay constant is taken as Fπ = 93 MeV (an older value used for comparison with earlier results)
- The left-hand cut develops at s ≤ 3 Mρ² when the Mandelstam variable t ≥ Mρ²

## Context

This work extends the covariant ρρ scattering study of Gulmez (2016) to the full vector-meson nonet, correcting the nonrelativistic treatment of Molina (2008) which found both f0(1370) and f2(1270) as ρρ bound states. The authors show that the tensor bound state disappears when relativistic effects and proper treatment of left-hand cuts are included, consistent with the qq̄ picture of the f2(1270) supported by experimental data on γγ → ππ from Dai (2014) and Mori (2007). The work enables a more reliable determination of which hadronic resonances are dynamically generated from vector-vector interactions, and provides a framework for extending such studies to coupled channels with proper analytic properties.
