# Renormalization of nuclear chiral effective field theory with non-perturbative leading order interactions

> We extend the renormalizability study of the formulation of chiral effective field theory with a finite cutoff, applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering, by taking into account non-perturbative effects. We consider the nucleon-nucleon interaction up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. The leading-order interaction is treated non-perturbatively. In contrast to the previously considered case when the leading-order interaction was assumed to be perturbative, new features related to the renormalization of the effective field theory are revealed. In particular, more severe constraints on the leading-order potential are formulated, which can enforce the renormalizability and the correct power counting for the next-to-leading order amplitude. To illustrate our theoretical findings, several partial waves in the nucleon-nucleon scattering, $^3P_0$, $^3S_1-{^3D_1}$ and $^1S_0$ are analyzed numerically. The cutoff dependence and the convergence of the chiral expansion for those channels are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous renormalization analysis of chiral EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering from the perturbative regime (where the series in the leading-order potential converges) to the non-perturbative regime (where it does not), finding that the renormalizability conditions and power-counting constraints derived earlier for the perturbative case remain valid, but with the caveat that for "mild" regulators the NLO cutoff must be kept finite (though possibly larger than the LO cutoff) rather than arbitrarily large.

## Method

The authors apply the Fredholm method to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering in the partial-wave basis, treating the leading-order (LO) potential non-perturbatively. They derive bounds on the Fredholm determinant and minor using Hadamard's inequality, and analyze the renormalization of the next-to-leading-order (NLO) amplitude via the Bogoliubov-Parasiuk-Hepp-Zimmermann (BPHZ) subtraction procedure, considering both "mild" regulators (where the LO potential falls as 1/q² at large momentum) and "standard" regulators (e.g., Gaussian or power-law form factors). Numerical calculations are performed for the ³P₀, ³S₁-³D₁, and ¹S₀ partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The LO potential scale is set by V₀,max = 8π²/(m_N Λ_V), where Λ_V is of order the hard scale Λ_b (the breakdown scale of chiral EFT, roughly the ρ-meson mass).
- The bound on the Fredholm determinant D is estimated as |D| ≤ M_D ≈ √2 exp(eΣ²/2), where Σ = M_Σ Λ/Λ_V is a dimensionless parameter; for Σ = 1, M_D ≈ 5.
- The maximal term in the Fredholm determinant series occurs at n_Dmax ≈ e Σ², with the bound |D^[n]| ≤ exp(e Σ²/2)/√(2π e) Σ.
- The bound on the minor Y is |Y_ji(p',p;p_on)| ≤ (8π² M_Ymax)/(Λ_V |p'|²) g(p') h(p), with Y_max ≤ √2 e Σ exp(e Σ²/2).
- For the ¹S₀ and ³S₁-³D₁ channels, the parameter Σ is expected to be close to 1 due to the shallowness of the (quasi) bound states.
- In numerical calculations, the relative error δ_n D/D becomes less than 1% for n = 3 or 4 in most cases.

## Context

This work extends the rigorous renormalization analysis of Gasparyan and Epelbaum (2021) from the perturbative regime (where the LO potential series converges) to the non-perturbative regime relevant for the ¹S₀, ³S₁-³D₁, and ³P₀ partial waves in nucleon-nucleon scattering. It builds on the Weinberg power-counting framework (Weinberg, 1990, 1991) and addresses the long-standing issue of cutoff dependence and power-counting violation in chiral EFT with finite cutoffs, complementing practical high-accuracy calculations by Reinert et al. (2017, 2020) and Entem et al. (2017). The results enable systematic uncertainty estimates for non-perturbative channels and clarify the conditions under which renormalization can be achieved with different regulator choices.
