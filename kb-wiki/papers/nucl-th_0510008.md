# Power-law tailed spectra from equilibrium

> We propose that power-law tailed hadron spectra may be viewed as stemming from a matter in an unconventional equilibrium state typical for non-extensive thermodynamics. A non-extensive Boltzmann equation, which is able to form such spectra as a stationary solution, is utilized as a rough model of quark matter hadronization. Basic ideas about non-extensive simulation of the QCD equation of state on the lattice are presented.

## Key claim

Power-law tailed hadron spectra may be viewed as stemming from a matter in an unconventional equilibrium state typical for non-extensive thermodynamics, rather than requiring separate pQCD explanations for the high-\(p_T\) region; the non-extensive Boltzmann equation can form such spectra as a stationary solution.

## Method

The authors generalize the Boltzmann equation by replacing the standard additive energy composition rule with a nontrivial associative composition rule \(E_{12} = h(E_1, E_2)\), which maps to simple addition via a function \(X(E)\). They simulate a non-extensive Boltzmann equation (NEBE) for massless partons evolving from initially boosted Fermi spheres, sampling color-singlet pairs with probability \(1/9\) to produce a pion-like spectrum. Separately, they propose a lattice strategy for QCD using a superstatistics approach, where the Tsallis expectation value is obtained by averaging over Gamma-distributed timelike link lengths \(\theta = a_t/a_s\), leading to a K-Bessel function weight factor for Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pion \(p_T\)-spectrum from RHIC AuAu collisions at 200 GeV shows a non-exponential fit already in the \(p_T\)-region between 1 and 4 GeV, and the extrapolation of this fit almost coincides with the fit to the whole observed range between 1 and 12 GeV.
- The Hagedorn temperature is given by \(T_H = E_c / d\) in the approach where Tsallis-distributed quarks are folded into mesonic and baryonic distributions.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model tradition of Hagedorn (1960s) and the search for quark matter assuming local thermal equilibrium in an exploding fireball (Cser, 1990s; TOR-LECT). It contrasts with the traditional approach that explains the power-law tail at very high \(p_T\) by pQCD calculations, noting that PHENIX data show a power-law part exceeding the scaled pQCD yield. The paper enables a unified non-extensive thermodynamic description of hadron spectra from soft to hard scales, and proposes a practical lattice strategy for computing Tsallis expectation values in QCD, requiring new Monte Carlo simulations rather than reusing old data.
