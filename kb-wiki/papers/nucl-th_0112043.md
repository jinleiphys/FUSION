# Strange particle production at RHIC in a single-freeze-out model

> Strange particle ratios and pT-spectra are calculated in a thermal model with single freeze-out, previously used successfully to describe non-strange particle production at RHIC. The model and the recently released data for phi, Lambda, anti-Lambda, and K*(892) are in very satisfactory agreement, showing that the thermal approach can be used to describe the strangeness production at RHIC.

## Key claim

The thermal model with single freeze-out, previously successful for non-strange particles at RHIC, also describes strange particle production at RHIC without any extra parameters such as strangeness suppression factors, provided the model includes complete treatment of hadronic resonances and the freeze-out hypersurface is defined by constant proper time with Hubble-like expansion.

## Method

The authors used a thermal model with single freeze-out (chemical and thermal freeze-out occur simultaneously) at temperature T = 165 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B = 41 MeV, with a boost-invariant freeze-out hypersurface defined by constant proper time τ = 7.66 fm and transverse size ρ_max = 6.69 fm. The four-velocity of the hydrodynamic expansion at freeze-out was taken proportional to the coordinate (Hubble-like expansion), with maximum transverse fluid velocity β_⊥^max = 0.66 and average ⟨β_⊥⟩ = 0.49. Particle spectra were computed using the Cooper-Frye formula with complete treatment of hadronic resonances from the Particle Data Table and full feeding from weak decays, using parameters previously fitted to the p_⊥ spectra of pions, kaons, and antiprotons without any refitting.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 165 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B = 41 MeV, used as thermal parameters
- Invariant time τ = 7.66 fm and transverse size ρ_max = 6.69 fm, fitted to p_⊥ spectra of pions, kaons, and antiprotons
- Maximum transverse fluid velocity β_⊥^max = 0.66, average ⟨β_⊥⟩ = 0.49
- Λ/p ratio: model gives 0.47, experiment gives 0.49 ± 0.03
- φ/h⁻ ratio: model gives 0.019, experiment gives 0.021 ± 0.001
- φ/K⁻ ratio: model gives 0.15, experiment gives 0.10–0.16
- K*⁰/h⁻ ratio: model gives 0.046, experiment gives 0.042 ± 0.004 ± 0.01
- K*⁰/K ratio: model gives 0.33, experiment gives 0.26 ± 0.03 ± 0.07
- φ/K*⁰ ratio: model gives 0.42, experiment gives 0.49 ± 0.05 ± 0.12
- Ω⁻/Ξ⁻ ratio: 0.18
- Ξ⁻/Σ⁻ ratio: 0.55
- Σ⁻/Λ ratio: 0.20
- J/ψ/π⁻ ratio: 0.12 × 10⁻⁶
- Asymptotic inverse slope parameter λ_∞ ≃ 363 MeV
- Excluded-volume scaling factor S = 1.3 with r = 0.6 fm, S = 1.6 with r = 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski and Florkowski (2001), previously applied to non-strange particles at RHIC, to strange particle production. The results contradict the need for separate chemical and thermal freeze-out scenarios (Heinz, 1999) and support the picture of a very short expansion time between the two freeze-outs, as evidenced by the successful reconstruction of K*(892)⁰ states by the STAR Collaboration. The model enables future comparison with HBT correlation radii and analysis of non-central events, and the authors note that the excluded-volume corrections (Braun-Munzinger, 1999; Gorenstein, 1995) could resolve discrepancies between the fitted geometric parameters and experimentally measured HBT radii.
