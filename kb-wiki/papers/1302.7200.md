# The Born series for $S$-wave quartet $nd$ scattering at small cutoff values

> Perturbative expansions, the Born series, of the scattering length and the amplitude of $S$-wave neutron-deuteron scattering for spin quartet channel below deuteron breakup threshold are studied in pionless effective field theory at small cutoff values. A three-body contact interaction is introduced when the integral equation is solved using the small cutoffs. After renormalizing the three-body interaction by using the scattering length, we expand the integral equation as the ordinary and inverted Born series. We find that the scattering length and the phase shift are considerably well reproduced with a few terms of the inverted Born series at relatively large cutoff values, $Λ\simeq 100$ MeV.

## Key claim

The inverted Born series reproduces the scattering length and phase shift of S-wave quartet nd scattering with only a few terms at cutoff values up to about 100 MeV, whereas the ordinary Born series converges only at much smaller cutoffs (about 10 MeV). The three-body contact interaction becomes necessary only when the cutoff is reduced below about 160 MeV, where the short-range length scale exceeds 1.24 fm and the Pauli principle suppression weakens.

## Method

The author solved the integral equation for S-wave neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin quartet channel using pionless effective field theory with a sharp momentum cutoff. A three-body contact interaction was introduced and its coupling constant renormalized to reproduce the known scattering length a4 = 6.34 fm. The integral equation was then expanded in both the ordinary Born series (around the weak-coupling limit) and the inverted Born series (around the unitary limit, i.e., the inverse of the amplitude) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Key numbers

- Scattering length a4 = 6.34 fm (theoretical value at cutoff Lambda = 197 MeV), consistent with experimental value 6.35 ± 0.02 fm
- Deuteron binding momentum gamma = 45.7 MeV; effective range rho_d = 1.76 fm
- In the limit Lambda → 0, the scattering length without three-body force becomes 19.19 fm
- The three-body coupling g(0) = -12.56 fm^2 reproduces a4 at zero cutoff
- The ordinary Born series reproduces a4 only for cutoff up to about 10 MeV; the inverted Born series reproduces a4 for cutoff up to about 100 MeV
- The inverted Born series up to NLO reproduces the phase shift well at Lambda = 60 MeV; up to NNLO convergence is faster

## Context

This work extends the study of perturbative expansions in pionless effective field theory for three-nucleon systems, specifically revisiting the spin quartet nd channel previously treated by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 2000) where no cutoff dependence was reported at Lambda ~ m_pi. The author connects this to the renormalization group fixed-point analysis of Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1999), where the trivial fixed point corresponds to the weak-coupling limit and the non-trivial fixed point to the unitary limit. The inverted Born series approach could enable perturbative treatment of interactions near the unitary limit, relevant for halo/cluster effective field theories and studies of exotic nuclei near the drip line.
