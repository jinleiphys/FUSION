# Impact of scale, nuclear PDF and temperature variations on the interpretation of medium-modified jet production data from the LHC

> In this paper we present a study of in-medium jet modifications performed with JEWEL and PYTHIA 6.4, focusing on the uncertainties related to variations of the perturbative scales and nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) and on the impact of the initial and crossover temperature variations of the medium. The simulations are compared to LHC data for the jet spectrum and the nuclear modification factor. We assess the interplay between the choice of nuclear PDFs and different medium parameters and study the impact of nuclear PDFs and the medium on the jet structure via the Lund plane.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that JEWEL simulations can describe LHC jet data across multiple experiments and energies, but the agreement depends on correlated variations of nuclear PDFs and medium parameters; in particular, a good description of central PbPb collisions requires either a low gluon density or a higher initial temperature, and these two effects can only be disentangled using measurements at different centralities.

## Method

The authors used the JEWEL Monte Carlo event generator (based on PYTHIA 6.4) to simulate jet production in pp and PbPb collisions, implementing a 1D Bjorken hydrodynamic medium evolution with Glauber geometry. They compared the simulated jet spectra and nuclear modification factors to ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS data at center-of-mass energies of 2.76 and 5.02 TeV per nucleon, evaluating uncertainties from perturbative scale variations (seven-point method), nuclear PDFs (nCTEQ15 and EPPS16 sets), and variations of the initial temperature and crossover temperature of the medium.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature from lattice QCD is T_C ≈ 156 MeV (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2018), while JEWEL uses a default T_C = 170 MeV for its 1D Bjorken expansion.
- The default initial temperature in JEWEL is T_I = 360 MeV, with formation time τ_I = 0.6 fm, chosen to match the value in Shen (2012).
- Nuclear PDF uncertainties are fairly constant at about ±15% as estimated by both nCTEQ15 and EPPS16.
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for jets at low p_T ≈ 40 GeV lies around 0.3 (ALICE, 0-10% centrality), rising to 0.6 at p_T ≈ 1 TeV (ATLAS, 0-10% centrality).
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are σ_inel^NN = 61.8 mb for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and σ_inel^NN = 67.6 mb for √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, with uncertainties of 0.9 mb and 0.6 mb respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet quenching using JEWEL (Zapp 2008, 2012, 2013; Kunnawalkam Elayavalli 2017) by systematically evaluating the interplay between nuclear PDF uncertainties and medium parameter variations. The study enables more precise extraction of QGP properties from jet data by demonstrating that initial-state nuclear PDF uncertainties must be accounted for when constraining medium parameters like initial temperature, and that the 1D Bjorken expansion in JEWEL may overestimate the interaction of the medium with faster jets compared to full 3D hydrodynamic models.
