# Degenerate distributions in complex Langevin dynamics: one-dimensional QCD at finite chemical potential

> We demonstrate analytically that complex Langevin dynamics can solve the sign problem in one-dimensional QCD in the thermodynamic limit. In particular, it is shown that the contributions from the complex and highly oscillating spectral density of the Dirac operator to the chiral condensate are taken into account correctly. We find an infinite number of classical fixed points of the Langevin flow in the thermodynamic limit. The correct solution originates from a continuum of degenerate distributions in the complexified space.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics correctly evaluates the chiral condensate in one-dimensional QCD at finite chemical potential by reproducing the contributions from the highly oscillatory complex spectral density of the Dirac operator, even though the dynamics is not ergodic and yields a continuum of degenerate stationary distributions in the complexified space.

## Method

The authors cast the chiral condensate as an integral over the eigenvalue density of the Dirac operator, which is a complex and rapidly oscillating function on an ellipse in the complex plane. They then complexify the angular variable alpha to x+iy and apply the discretized complex Langevin equations with real noise, solving numerically for the condensate at various lattice sizes n and comparing to the exact analytic result.

## Key numbers

- For n=8, 16, 32, and 48 with mu=2, mu_c=1, the condensate values from complex Langevin dynamics are 0.6479(2), 0.6483(3), 0.6483(4), and 0.6478(3) respectively, compared to the exact result of 0.648054.
- The average phase factor (severity of sign problem) is shown for n=4, 10, 16 as a function of mu/mu_c, with the sign problem severe when |mu| > |mu_c|.
- In the thermodynamic limit n to infinity, the chiral condensate becomes lim Sigma = sign(mu_c)/cosh(mu_c).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of complex Langevin dynamics as a method to evade the sign problem, previously demonstrated for the relativistic Bose gas by Aarts (2008, 2009) and for heavy dense QCD by Aarts (2008). It directly tests the method against exact results from one-dimensional QCD, which was shown by Ravagli (2007) to contain the same features of the sign and Silver Blaze problems as four-dimensional QCD. The paper establishes a first link between complex oscillations of the Dirac spectral density and complex Langevin dynamics, and demonstrates that the difficulties with erroneous convergence found in Aarts (2009, 2010) are not intrinsically related to the sign problem.
