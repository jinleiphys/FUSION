# Dominant $2πγ$-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction: Spin-spin and tensor potentials

> We calculate at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory the electromagnetic corrections to the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction that is generated by the isovector spin-flip $ππNN$ contact-vertex proportional to the large low-energy constant $c_4\simeq 3.4 $GeV$^{-1}$. We find that the respective $2πγ$-exchange potentials contain sizeable isospin-breaking components which reach up to -4% of corresponding isovector $2π$-exchange potentials. The typical values of these novel charge-independence breaking spin-spin and tensor potentials are $-0.11 $MeV and $0.09 $MeV, at a nucleon distance of $r=m_π^{-1}=1.4 $fm. The charge-symmetry breaking spin-spin and tensor potentials come out a factor of 2.4 smaller. Our analytical results for these presumably dominant isospin-violating spin-spin and tensor NN-forces are in a form such that they can be easily implemented into phase-shift analyses and few-body calculations.

## Key claim
The paper calculates that the two-pion–photon-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction, generated by the isovector spin-flip ππNN contact-vertex proportional to the low-energy constant c₄ ≈ 3.4 GeV⁻¹, produces charge-independence breaking spin-spin and tensor potentials reaching up to -4% of the corresponding isovector two-pion-exchange potentials. The abstract’s claim of “sizeable” components is hedged by the condition that these results are the dominant contributions from this specific vertex, and the charge-symmetry breaking potentials are a factor of 2.4 smaller.

## Method
The author computed at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory the electromagnetic corrections to the two-pion exchange NN interaction, using the Cutkosky cutting rule to evaluate the spectral functions of 70 two-loop 2πγ-exchange diagrams. The spectral functions were integrated over the Lorentz-invariant ππγ three-particle phase space in the heavy-nucleon limit, and the coordinate-space potentials were obtained via a modified Laplace transformation. An infrared singularity from soft photon emission was regularized using a “plus”-prescription analogous to that used for parton splitting functions.

## Key numbers
- Charge-independence breaking spin-spin potential: -0.11 MeV at nucleon distance r = m_π⁻¹ = 1.4 fm.
- Charge-independence breaking tensor potential: 0.09 MeV at nucleon distance r = m_π⁻¹ = 1.4 fm.
- Charge-symmetry breaking spin-spin and tensor potentials are a factor of 2.4 smaller than the charge-independence breaking ones.
- The isospin-violating components reach up to -4% of the corresponding isovector 2π-exchange potentials.
- The low-energy constant c₄ = 3.4 GeV⁻¹; the fine-structure constant α = 1/137.036; the pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV; the charged pion mass m_π = 139.57 MeV; the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.3.

## Context
This work extends the author’s previous calculation (Kaiser, 2006) of electromagnetic corrections to the two-pion exchange NN interaction generated by the c₃ vertex, which produced central potentials of 0.3 MeV at 1.4 fm. It also builds on the leading-order pion-photon exchange calculations by Van Kolck et al. and the isospin-breaking two-pion exchange potentials derived by Epelbaum et al. The results provide analytical forms for these isospin-violating spin-spin and tensor potentials that can be implemented into NN phase-shift analyses and few-body calculations, enabling tests of their phenomenological relevance.
