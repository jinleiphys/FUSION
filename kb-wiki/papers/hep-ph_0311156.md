# Photon production from a thermalized quark gluon plasma: quantum kinetics and nonperturbative aspects

> We study the production of photons from a quark gluon plasma in local thermal equilibrium by introducing a non-perturbative formulation of the real time evolution of the density matrix. The main ingredient is the real time effective action for the electromagnetic field to $\mathcal{O}(α_{em})$ and to all orders in $α_s$. The real time evolution is completely determined by the solution of a \emph{classical stochastic} non-local Langevin equation which provides a Dyson-like resummation of the perturbative expansion. The Langevin equation is solved in closed form by Laplace transform in terms of the thermal photon polarization. A quantum kinetic description emerges directly from this formulation. We find that photons with $k \lesssim 200 ~{Mev}$ \emph{thermalize} as plasmon quasiparticles in the plasma on time scales $t \sim 10-20 ~{fm}/c$ which is of the order of the lifetime of the QGP expected at RHIC and LHC. We then obtain the direct photon yield to lowest order in $α_{em}$ and to leading logarithmic order in $α_s$ in a \emph{uniform} expansion valid at all time. The yield during a QGP lifetime $t \sim 10 ~{fm}/c$ is systematically larger than that obtained with the equilibrium formulation and the spectrum features a distinct flattening for $k \gtrsim 2.5 ~{Gev}$. We discuss the window of reliability of our results, the theoretical uncertainties in \emph{any} treatment of photon emission from a QGP in LTE and the shortcomings of the customary S-matrix approach.

## Key claim

Photon production from a quark gluon plasma in local thermal equilibrium is studied using a non-perturbative real-time formulation that goes beyond the standard S-matrix approach; the yield during a finite QGP lifetime of about 10 fm/c is systematically larger than that obtained from equilibrium formulations, with the spectrum showing a distinct flattening for momenta above approximately 2.5 GeV, though the authors note that the reliability of any LTE-based calculation is suspect for momenta above 4-5 GeV.

## Method

The authors derive a real-time effective action for the electromagnetic field to order alpha_em and to all orders in alpha_s by integrating out quark and gluon degrees of freedom from an initial density matrix describing a QGP in local thermal equilibrium. This effective action leads to a classical stochastic non-local Langevin equation for the transverse gauge field, which is solved in closed form by Laplace transform in terms of the thermal photon polarization. The photon distribution function is then obtained from this solution, representing a Dyson-like resummation of the perturbative expansion that is uniformly valid at all times.

## Key numbers

* Photons with momentum k less than or approximately 200 MeV thermalize as plasmon quasiparticles on time scales of 10-20 fm/c, comparable to the expected QGP lifetime at RHIC and LHC.
* The direct photon yield calculated over a QGP lifetime of approximately 10 fm/c is systematically larger than the equilibrium formulation result by a factor between 2 and 4 for intermediate momenta 200 MeV to 2 GeV.
* The spectrum flattens at an energy scale of approximately 2.5 GeV, becoming dramatically larger than yields from the S-matrix formulation.
* The theoretically reliable range for LTE-based calculations is identified as 200 MeV to approximately 5 GeV, with the region above 4-5 GeV being sensitive to initial conditions and pre-equilibrium stage modeling.

## Context

This work extends real-time studies of photon production from a QGP with finite lifetime reported in Wangphoton and Nuestro, which showed that contributions subleading in the infinite-lifetime limit become comparable to or larger than S-matrix results during the finite QGP lifetime. The authors also reference Renk (2003) regarding sensitivity of the high-momentum spectrum to initial conditions. The formulation enables a quantum kinetic description that includes photon thermalization and propagation as quasiparticles, addressing shortcomings of the customary S-matrix approach which assumes infinite lifetime and treats quarks and gluons as asymptotic states.
