# Influence of Vector Mesons on the f0(600) Decay Width in a Linear Sigma Model with Global Chiral Invariance

> We consider a globally invariant chiral Lagrangian that contains vector and axial-vector mesons. We compute the f0(600) decay width into two pions and pion-pion scattering lengths and compare with the corresponding results in which the (axial-)vector degrees of freedom decouple. We show that the role of vector mesons has a great impact on these quantities.

## Key claim
The inclusion of vector and axial-vector mesons in a globally invariant chiral Lagrangian drastically reduces the computed decay width of the f0(600) resonance into two pions, making it impossible to simultaneously describe both ππ scattering lengths and the f0(600) decay width, contrary to the model without these degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors constructed a SU(2)r × SU(2)l chirally symmetric Lagrangian containing scalar (σ, a0), pseudoscalar (π, ηN), vector (ρ, ω), and axial-vector (a1, f1) mesons. They fixed eleven Lagrangian parameters using experimental masses of π, ηN, a0, ρ, and a1, the pion decay constant fπ, and the decay widths Γρ→ππ and Γf1→a0π, leaving three independent parameters (mσ, Z, h1). They then computed the f0(600) → ππ decay width and ππ scattering lengths a0^0 and a0^2 both with and without the (axial-)vector mesons (the latter limit obtained by setting g1 → 0, Z → 1, and h1 = h2 = h3 = 0).

## Key numbers
- The σ decay width with vector mesons included is not larger than approximately 300 MeV even for mσ ≅ 650 MeV, and for mσ ≤ 450 MeV (required by scattering length a0^0) the width is smaller than about 100 MeV.
- Without vector mesons, for mσ ∈ [500, 620] MeV, the σ decay width Γσ ∈ [350, 600] MeV, in agreement with Leutwyler et al. (Γσ/2 = 272^{+9}_{-12.5} MeV) and Peláez et al. (Γσ/2 = 255 ± 16 MeV).
- The fitted parameters with vector mesons are: Z = 1.67 ± 0.20, h1 = -68^{+36}_{-15}, and mσ = 332^{+168}_{-53} MeV (with mσ ∈ [279, 500] MeV from the a0^0 constraint).
- The experimental scattering lengths used are a0^0 = 0.218 ± 0.020 and a0^2 = -0.0457 ± 0.0125 from the NA48/2 collaboration.

## Context
This work extends the Linear Sigma Model by including vector and axial-vector mesons (ρ, ω, a1, f1) alongside the usual scalar and pseudoscalar fields, following earlier formulations by Boguta (1983) and the Mainz group. It contradicts the successful description of f0(600) phenomenology obtained without vector mesons, as in the work of Leutwyler (2008) and Peláez (2004). The results suggest that identifying the scalar fields with the heavier {f0(1370), a0(1450)} states, as done by Amsler (1995) and Fariborz (2005), may resolve the discrepancy, with the light f0(600) interpreted as a tetraquark state mixing with quarkonia.
