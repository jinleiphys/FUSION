# X-ray dichroism and orbital anapoles

> We present a theoretical analysis of photo-absorption spectroscopies in noncentrosymmetric systems, covering both x-ray and optical regions. Integrated dichroic spectra are interpreted using microscopic effective operators, which are obtained by coupling the orbital angular momentum to the orbital anapole. Symmetry arguments afford a classification of valence-electron states in the presence of parity nonconserving hybridisation. Enantiomorphism is identified by a two-particle chiral operator.

# X-ray Dichroism and Orbital Anapoles

## Key claim

The paper provides a theoretical framework showing that integrated dichroic spectra in noncentrosymmetric systems can be interpreted using microscopic effective operators constructed by coupling the orbital angular momentum to the orbital anapole, but the analysis is restricted to dichroic spectra integrated over a finite energy range corresponding to the two partners of a spin-orbit split inner-shell (j± = lc ± 1/2) and relies on a localised (atomic) model.

## Method

The authors employ a spectrum-generating algebra (SGA) approach based on the de Sitter algebra so(3,2) and its subgroup so(3,1), realised by the orbital angular momentum operator L and the orbital anapole operator Ω⁻. They use the Racah-Wigner calculus to relate integrated dichroic spectra to ground-state expectation values of effective orbital operators, which are constructed by coupling L (space-even) and Ω⁻ (space-odd) to form space-odd irreducible tensors. The analysis covers E1-E2 interference for X-ray natural circular dichroism (XNCD) and X-ray nonreciprocal linear dichroism (XNRLD), as well as E1-M1 interference for optical natural circular dichroism (ONCD).

## Key numbers

- The integrated XNCD spectrum expression contains the prefactor 8π²α/(3ħc)(2lc+1), where α = e²/ħc
- The integrated XNRLD spectrum expression contains the prefactor 8π²α/(ħc)(2lc+1)
- The integrated ONCD spectrum expression contains the prefactor 4π²α/(mc)
- The "magic angle" for detecting the toroidal (orbital anapole) contribution is given as k̂B̂ ≃ 39.23°
- The orbital anapole operator is defined as Ω⁻ = (n × L − L × n)/2 = i[n, L²]/2
- The one-particle chiral operator L·Ω⁻ = 0 (zero eigenvalue at one-particle level)
- The two-particle chiral operator for the D^(1/2,1/2) ⊗ D^(1/2,1/2) case takes the form LT·Ω⁻T = 2i[N(1)·N(2) − N†(1)·N†(2)]

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Carra and Benoist (2000) on de Sitter algebras to interpret two recent experiments: XNCD observed in α-LiIO₃ at iodine L edges (Goulon, 1998) and in Na₃Nd(digly)₃·2NaBF₄·6H₂O at the Nd L₃ edge (Alagna, 1998), and XNRLD detected at the vanadium K edge in Cr-doped V₂O₃ (Goulon, 2000). The approach builds on the concept of spectrum-generating algebras originally introduced in nuclear physics (Goshen and Lipkin, 1959) and particle physics (Dothan, Gell-Mann, and Ne'eman, 1965; Dothan and Ne'eman, 1966), and uses Trammell's expansion (1953) to connect the abstract formulation to the electron orbital current. The work enables a symmetry-based classification of valence-electron states in noncentrosymmetric systems and identifies enantiomorphism via a two-particle chiral operator measurable by optical natural circular dichroism.
