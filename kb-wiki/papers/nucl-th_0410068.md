# Pentaquark baryon production in nuclear reactions

> Using a hadronic model with empirical coupling constants and form factors, we have evaluated the cross sections for the production of exotic pentaquark $Θ^+$ and/or $Ξ_5^+$ and $Ξ_5^{--}$ in reactions induced by photons, nucleons, pions, and kaons on nucleon targets. We have also predicted the $Θ^+$ yield in relativistic heavy ion collisions using a kinetic model that takes into account both $Θ^+$ production from the coalescence of quarks and antiquarks in the quark-gluon plasma and the effects due to subsequent hadronic absorption and regeneration.

## Key claim

Using a hadronic model with empirical coupling constants and form factors, the authors evaluate cross sections for pentaquark production in elementary reactions and heavy ion collisions, but the results depend strongly on the assumed parity and width of the pentaquark, with cross sections smaller by about an order of magnitude if the pentaquark has negative parity.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic model with empirical coupling constants and form factors to evaluate cross sections for pentaquark Θ⁺ production in photon-nucleon, proton-proton, pion-nucleon, and kaon-nucleon reactions, as well as for Ξ₅⁺ and Ξ₅⁻⁻ production in photon-nucleon reactions. For heavy ion collisions, they employed a kinetic model combining quark coalescence from the quark-gluon plasma with subsequent hadronic absorption and regeneration using an expanding fireball model.

## Key numbers

- Θ⁺ production cross section in γn → K⁻Θ⁺ at photon energy Eγ = 3 GeV: about 30 nb with K* exchange and 15 nb without K* exchange (for positive parity Θ⁺ with width 1 MeV)
- Θ⁺ production cross section in γp → K̄⁰Θ⁺ at Eγ = 3 GeV: about 35 nb, comparable to the revised SAPHIR measurement of about 50 nb
- Θ⁺ production cross section in pp → Σ⁺Θ⁺ at center-of-mass energy √s = 2.75 GeV (beam momentum 2.95 GeV/c): 0.3 μb with gK*NΘ = 1.0, comparable to the 0.4 μb measured in the COSY experiment
- Θ⁺ production cross sections in πN → K̄Θ⁺: 0.5-5 μb at center-of-mass energy 0.2 GeV above threshold
- Θ⁺ production cross sections in KN → πΘ⁺: 20-75 μb at center-of-mass energy 0.2 GeV above threshold
- Ξ₅⁺ and Ξ₅⁻⁻ production cross sections in γp → K⁰K⁰Ξ₅⁺ and γn → K⁺K⁺Ξ₅⁻⁻: 0.01-0.1 nb at photon energy Eγ = 4.5 GeV
- Θ⁺ yield in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV from quark coalescence: about 0.19 for Θ⁺ radius ~0.9 fm
- Final Θ⁺ yield after hadronic effects: about 0.5 for ΓΘ = 20 MeV, about 0.2 for ΓΘ = 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pentaquark production mechanisms by Oh et al. (2004) and Nam et al. (2004) using a hadronic model with empirical coupling constants and form factors. The results provide cross sections useful for planning experiments at facilities such as KEK in Japan and for interpreting data from the SAPHIR experiment at Bonn and the COSY experiment. The heavy ion predictions extend the quark coalescence model of Greco et al. (2003) and the statistical model of Randrup (2004) to pentaquark production at RHIC.
