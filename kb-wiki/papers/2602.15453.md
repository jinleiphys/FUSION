# Perturbative calculations of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering in chiral effective field theory

> We develop a framework for calculating nucleon-deuteron scattering using strict perturbation theory for treating subleading interactions in chiral effective field theory (ChEFT). Rather than using direct evaluations in the distorted-wave expansion, our approach solves a hierarchy of integral equations to obtain subleading scattering amplitudes. A benchmark with the wave packet continuum-discretization is performed. This framework benefits from the fact that the renormalization-group invariance chiral forces involves only a limited number of two-body partial waves at leading order. We use it to calculate nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering differential cross sections and analyzing powers up to next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The authors develop a framework for calculating nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering using strict perturbation theory for subleading chiral interactions, where the key enabling feature is that the renormalization-group invariant leading-order chiral forces involve only a limited number of two-body partial waves, allowing the perturbative hierarchy to reuse the same integral kernel at all orders.

## Method

The Faddeev equation is solved in momentum space using a deformed contour integration technique to avoid singularities from the free propagator, the two-body t-matrix, and the deuteron pole. The leading-order (LO) potentials are treated nonperturbatively in only three NN partial waves (³S₁-³D₁, ¹S₀, and ³P₀), while all other channels are treated at next-to-leading order (NLO) using a fixed-kernel perturbation theory (FKPT) approach. The FKPT method solves a hierarchy of integral equations where the kernel remains identical to the LO kernel at all orders, with only the driving terms changing at each order.

## Key numbers

* Phase shifts and mixing angles at nucleon laboratory energies of 3, 14, and 30 MeV for Jᴾ = ½⁺, ½⁻, and ³⁄₂⁺ are benchmarked against the wave-packet continuum-discretization (WPCD) method, with discrepancies less than one percent.
* The contour deformation uses a rotation angle of 10 degrees and 48 mesh points for the numerical calculations.
* A cutoff value Λ = 400 MeV is used for the LO potentials in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁-³D₁ channels during benchmarking.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of subleading chiral interactions developed for the Skorniakov–Ter-Martirosian equation by Vanasse (2013) and for deuteron longitudinal response by Andis et al. (2025). The power counting follows the renormalization-group invariant scheme of Long (2011, 2012) and Wu (2018), which has been applied to NN scattering, electroweak processes by Shi (2022) and Liu (2022), and triton properties by Thim (2025). The framework enables systematic perturbative calculations of three-nucleon scattering observables up to higher orders in chiral effective field theory without requiring full nonperturbative solutions at each order.
