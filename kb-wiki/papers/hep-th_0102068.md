# Stability of bound states in the light-front Yukawa model

> We show that in the system of two fermions interacting by scalar exchange, the solutions for J$^π$=$0^+$ bound states are stable without any cutoff regularization for coupling constant below some critical value.

## Key claim

For the two-fermion system with scalar exchange in light-front dynamics, the $J^\pi=0^+$ bound state solutions are stable (cutoff-independent as the cutoff tends to infinity) only for coupling constants below a critical value; above that critical value the system collapses even without the most singular kernel in the Hamiltonian matrix.

## Method

The authors solved the light-front dynamics equations for two fermions interacting via scalar exchange in the ladder approximation, using the explicitly covariant version of light-front dynamics with the state vector defined on the plane $\omega\cdot x=0$, $\omega^2=0$. They transformed the equations into a representation with variables $k$ and $\theta$ where the kinetic energy is quadratic in $k$ and the kernels are smooth in $\theta$, then analyzed the asymptotic behavior of the kernels at large momenta and performed numerical calculations with a cutoff $k_{max}$.

## Key numbers

- For the one-channel problem with kernel $K_{11}$ only, the critical coupling constant $\alpha_c$ is found numerically to lie in the range $3 < \alpha_c < 4$ (with constituent mass $m=1$ and exchange mass $\mu=0.25$).
- For $\alpha=3$ (below $\alpha_c$), the mass squared $M^2$ converges as $k_{max}$ increases; for $\alpha=4$ (above $\alpha_c$), $M^2$ decreases logarithmically with $k_{max}$ and becomes negative.
- For the full two-channel $J=0$ state at $\alpha=1.184$, $M^2$ varies by only 0.1% when changing $k_{max}$ from 50 to 600, demonstrating stability.
- The critical value from a non-majorating analysis gives $\alpha_c = \pi$, compared to $\pi/2$ from a majorating analysis that replaces $\sqrt{\gamma}$ by 1.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of bound states in the Yukawa model previously studied by Glazek and collaborators (Glazek, 1990s) using the Tamm-Dancoff method, where the $J=0^+$ state was found to be cutoff-dependent and required renormalization. The authors show that in the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics, the $J=0$ state is stable below a critical coupling, contradicting the earlier conclusion of logarithmic divergence. The $J=1,a=0$ state is confirmed to be always unstable, in agreement with Glazek's results. This work enables a clearer separation of physical bound states from cutoff artifacts and provides a foundation for renormalization procedures in light-front Yukawa models.
