# Fluidity of the system produced in relativistic pp and heavy-ion collisions: Hadron resonance gas model approach

> We have estimated the dimensionless parameters such as Reynolds number ($Re$), Knudsen number ($Kn$) and Mach number ($Ma$) for a multi-hadron system by using the excluded volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG) model along with Hagedorn mass spectrum to include higher resonances in the system. The size dependence of these parameters indicate that the system formed in proton+proton collisions may achieve thermal equilibrium making it unsuitable as a benchmark to analyze the properties of the system produced in heavy ion collisions at similar energies. While the magnitude of $Kn$ can be used to study the degree of thermalization and applicability of inviscid hydrodynamics, the variations of $Re$ and $Ma$ with temperature ($T$) and baryonic chemical potential ($μ_B$) assist to understand the change in the nature of the flow in the system. Indeed the nature of flow changes from laminar to turbulent as $Re$ increases and the system is characterized as incompressible for low $Ma (<<1)$ and compressible for larger $Ma$. $Ma$ can also be used to understand whether the flow is subsonic or supersonic.

## Key claim

The system formed in proton+proton collisions may achieve thermal equilibrium, making it unsuitable as a benchmark for analyzing the properties of the system produced in heavy-ion collisions at similar energies, based on the size dependence of the Knudsen, Reynolds, and Mach numbers estimated using the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model with Hagedorn mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors used the excluded volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG) model with a Hagedorn mass spectrum to include higher resonances, incorporating finite-size effects via a lower momentum cutoff. They calculated shear viscosity from the Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation, treating hadrons as hard spheres of equal radius 0.3 fm, and then derived the Knudsen number (Kn), Reynolds number (Re), and Mach number (Ma) as functions of temperature, baryonic chemical potential, and system size.

## Key numbers

- Knudsen number Kn ≈ 0.3 for central collisions and 0.5 for semi-central collisions of Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV, from prior hydrodynamic model estimates cited in the paper.
- Reynolds number Re varies between 10 and 100 for a system of size 5 fm at temperature T = 300 MeV with fluid velocity in the range 0.1–1 (in units of c=1), from prior work cited in the paper.
- The AdS/CFT bound for η/s is 1/4π, used as the perfect fluid limit reference.
- System radii chosen for analysis: R = 1.5, 3, 5, 7, and 10 fm, covering from high-multiplicity pp to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
- Hard-core radius for all hadrons set to rh = 0.3 fm.
- Hagedorn temperature TH = 180 MeV used in the mass spectrum parametrization.
- Average flow velocities taken as ⟨v⟩ = 0.3c, 0.5c, and 0.7c, with results shown for ⟨v⟩ = 0.5c.
- Flow becomes turbulent for Re ≳ 2000, while the values obtained in this study indicate laminar flow.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in hadron gas models, comparing favorably with η/s values from Gorenstein (2008) using EVHRG with relativistic molecular kinetic theory, and with results from Denicol (2013), Kadam (2019), and Kadam (2019) using Chapman-Enskog theory and relativistic mean-field HRG. The speed of sound squared is compared with lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2013). The findings challenge the use of pp collisions as a baseline for heavy-ion collisions and enable future studies of thermalization thresholds and hydrodynamic applicability in small systems at LHC energies.
