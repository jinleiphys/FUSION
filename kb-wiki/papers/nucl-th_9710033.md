# On the role of the effective interaction in quasi-elastic electron scattering calculations

> The role played by the effective residual interaction in the transverse nuclear response for quasi-free electron scattering is discussed. The analysis is done by comparing different calculations performed in the Random--Phase Approximation and Ring Approximation frameworks. The importance of the exchange terms in this energy region is investigated and the changes on the nuclear responses due to the modification of the interaction are evaluated. The calculated quasi-elastic responses show clear indication of their sensibility to the details of the interaction and this imposes the necessity of a more careful study of the role of the different channels of the interaction in this excitation region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the effective residual interaction used in quasi-elastic electron scattering calculations must be fixed within the same effective theory (e.g., Random-Phase Approximation or Ring Approximation) in which it is later applied; otherwise, the calculated transverse nuclear responses can show large, uncontrolled discrepancies, though these differences reduce with increasing momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors calculated transverse (e,e') response functions for ⁴⁰Ca at three momentum transfers (q = 300, 410, and 550 MeV/c) using both the Random-Phase Approximation (RPA), which includes exchange terms, and the Ring Approximation (RA), which omits them. They compared results obtained with the Jülich-Stony Brook interaction (which includes a reduction factor r=0.4 on the ρ-exchange potential) and a modified interaction without that reduction factor, and also refitted the Landau-Migdal parameters g₀ and g₀′ to reproduce the energies and B-values of two 1⁺ states in ²⁰⁸Pb (at 5.85 and 7.30 MeV) within each theory separately.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: q = 300, 410, and 550 MeV/c for transverse responses in ⁴⁰Ca.
- Landau-Migdal parameters for the Jülich-Stony Brook interaction: g₀ = 0.57, g₀′ = 0.717, with C₀ = 386.04 MeV fm³.
- When refitting V^II_res within RPA: g₀ = 0.470, g₀′ = 0.760.
- When refitting V^II_res within RA: g₀ = 0.038, g₀′ = 0.717.
- Fermi momentum used for all calculations: k_F = 235 MeV/c.
- The g₀ contribution in RA is suppressed by a factor of approximately μ_s²/μ_v² ≈ 1/28 relative to the g₀′ contribution.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of effective interactions from low-energy nuclear structure (where such studies are common) into the quasi-elastic peak region, where the role of the interaction had not been examined in detail. It directly builds on the Jülich-Stony Brook interaction parameterization of Speth, Klemt, Wambach, and Brown (1980), which was fitted to low-energy magnetic properties in the lead region, and on prior RPA and RA calculations by Alberico et al. (1984), Gil, Nieves, and Oset (1997), and Bauer, Ramos, and Polls (1996). The results enable a systematic approach to developing a unified framework for calculating nuclear responses across different energy and momentum regimes, and they highlight the need to investigate the tensor piece of the interaction and its interplay with meson-exchange currents, final state interactions, and short-range correlations.
