# Hybrid Equations of State for Neutron Stars with Hyperons and Deltas

> In this contribution, we describe new chemically-equilibrated charge-neutral hybrid equations of state for neutron stars. They present a first-order phase transition to quark matter and differentiate by the particle population considered and how these particles interact. While some equations of state contain just nucleons and up, down-quarks, others also contain hyperons, Delta baryons, and strange quarks. The hybrid equations of state, together with corresponding hadronic ones, are available on the CompOSE repository and can be used for different astrophysical applications.

# Hybrid Equations of State for Neutron Stars with Hyperons and Deltas

## Key claim
The paper presents 16 new equations of state (8 hadronic and 8 hybrid) for neutron stars that include a first-order phase transition to quark matter, but the phase transition is deliberately weakened (with smaller density jumps) compared to earlier versions of the Chiral Mean Field model, which allows hybrid stars to remain stable without requiring mixed phases. The claim that hybrid stars reach masses above 2 solar masses holds only for the subset of equations of state that include both omega-rho and omega^4 vector interactions (EoS 5 through 8).

## Method
The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, an effective relativistic model that includes chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement to quark matter via a first-order phase transition at zero temperature. They generate 8 hadronic and 8 hybrid equations of state by varying the particle composition (nucleons only vs. including hyperons, Delta baryons, and strange quarks) and by adding omega-rho mixed vector-isoscalar vector-isovector interactions and/or omega^4 higher-order vector-isoscalar interactions. The equations of state are combined with a unified crust from Gulminelli and Raduta, then solved via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron-star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of isospin-symmetric matter: n_B = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B = -16 MeV
- Compressibility at saturation: K = 300 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 30 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope: L = 88 MeV (L = 75 MeV when omega-rho interactions are included)
- Hyperon potentials at saturation: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = 5 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV (with omega^4: U_Lambda = -27 MeV, U_Sigma = 6 MeV, U_Xi = -17 MeV, U_Delta = -64 MeV)
- Phase transition densities for hybrid EoS range from n_B = 0.433 fm^-3 (EoS 2) to n_B = 0.689 fm^-3 (EoS 7)
- Maximum hadronic star masses range from 2.00 M_sun (EoS 3) to 2.13 M_sun (EoS 2)
- Maximum hybrid star masses range from 1.96 M_sun (EoS 1 and 2) to 2.02 M_sun (EoS 5 and 7)
- Radius of 1.4 M_sun star ranges from 13.15 km (EoS 3) to 13.67 km (EoS 2)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability of 1.4 M_sun star ranges from 702 (EoS 3) to 904 (EoS 2)

## Context
This work extends the Chiral Mean Field model previously developed by Papazoglou (1998), Dexheimer (2008), and Roark (2018) by applying a modified quark description from Dexheimer (2020) that produces weaker first-order phase transitions. The results are orthogonal to those of Rikovska-Stone (2007) and Weissenborn (2011), who predict neutron stars with Xi hyperons but no Sigma hyperons, whereas this model finds Sigma^- and Lambda hyperons but no Xi. The 16 equations of state are made publicly available on the CompOSE repository (Oertel 2016, Typel 2013) for use in astrophysical simulations, including neutron-star merger simulations that are already underway.
