# Recent progress on nucleon form factors

> The form factors of the nucleon provide key information on nucleon properties. When confronted with precisely measured observables from experiments, they serve as benchmark quantities for lattice calculations. On the other hand lattice determinations may serve as vital theory input for the interpretation of experiments, e.g. in neutrino-nucleus scattering. I review recent progress in the calculation of nucleon form factors on the lattice and its relevance to future experiments.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors have reached a level of precision where they can serve as vital theory input for interpreting experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, but the most dominant remaining systematic uncertainty is excited-state contamination, which must be shown to be under control for every analysis.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors, focusing on the extraction of ground-state matrix elements from two- and three-point correlation functions using ratios that cancel overlap factors. The analysis covers multiple approaches to handle excited-state contamination: multi-state fits (with fixed or free parameters from two-point functions), the summation method, and constraints from chiral perturbation theory or the PCAC relation. The review compares results from different collaborations for the vector, axialvector, and strange electromagnetic form factors, examining how choices of fit forms (dipole, z-expansion, effective field theory) and treatment of systematics affect final values.

## Key numbers

- The weighted average of recent lattice determinations for the axial radius squared is ⟨r_A²⟩^average = 0.365(25) fm² (from z-expansion or direct extractions, with systematic errors added in quadrature)
- The PDG-style unconstrained weighted average for the strange magnetic moment is μ^{s,average} = -0.0193(54)
- The average strange electric radius squared is ⟨(r_E^s)²⟩^average = -0.00484(54) fm²
- The average strange magnetic radius squared is ⟨(r_M^s)²⟩^average = -0.0167(53) fm²
- A determination of the axial radius to 20% accuracy is sufficient to render the theoretical uncertainty due to r_A in neutron quasi-elastic cross sections a subdominant contribution

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice nucleon form factor calculations discussed in Ottnad (2020) and compares results from multiple collaborations including PNDME (Jang 2019), Mainz21 (Djukanovic 2021), ETMC (Alexandrou 2018, 2020), PACS (Shintani 2018, Ishikawa 2021), RQCD (2019), and NME (Park 2021). The work enables improved interpretation of upcoming neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments at DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, and addresses the proton radius puzzle where lattice determinations tend toward the smaller value from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) but cannot yet rule out the larger ep-scattering value from A1 (2010).
