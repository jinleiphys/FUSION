# Jets and high-$p_{T}$ probes measured in the STAR experiment

> Hard probes created through large momentum transfers are used to study the properties of QCD matter created in heavy-ion collisions, by comparing the measurements to those in p+p collisions. Jets, and the "quenching" or suppression of jets in the medium created in heavy-ion collisions, are studied through various different observables. We present the most recent measurements from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, with p+p collisions as the reference, by the STAR Collaboration. The observables are semi-inclusive charged jets and di-jet transverse momentum imbalance. Additionally, correlation measurements of direct photon-hadron and neutral pion-hadron are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that at RHIC's top energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, the energy lost by jets in the quark-gluon plasma is redistributed within a cone radius of R=0.4, in contrast to LHC measurements where energy is transported to larger angles; however, the expected difference in suppression between direct photon-triggered and neutral pion-triggered hadron correlations due to color factor and path length dependence is not observed within current experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured three observables in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV using p+p collisions as reference: (i) jet-like correlations of direct photon-hadron and neutral pion-hadron pairs, quantifying medium modification via the ratio $I_{AA}$ of per-trigger yields; (ii) semi-inclusive recoil charged jet spectra on the away side of a high-$p_T$ charged hadron trigger (9 < $p_T^{trig}$ < 30 GeV/c), using a mixed-event technique to subtract uncorrelated background and unfolding for instrumental effects; (iii) di-jet transverse momentum imbalance $A_J = (p_{T,lead} - p_{T,sublead})/(p_{T,lead} + p_{T,sublead})$ for jets reconstructed with the anti-$k_T$ algorithm using charged tracks from the TPC and neutral energy from the BEMC.

## Key numbers

- For semi-inclusive recoil charged jets with R=0.3, the horizontal shift in $p_{T,jet}^{ch}$ spectra between central and peripheral collisions is $-5.0 \pm 0.5$ GeV/c for $p_{T,jet}^{ch} > 10$ GeV/c
- For semi-inclusive recoil charged jets with R=0.5, the horizontal shift is $-2.3 \pm 0.2$ GeV/c for $p_{T,jet}^{ch} > 10$ GeV/c
- The semi-inclusive recoil charged jet spectra show approximately 80% suppression in central collisions relative to peripheral collisions with R=0.3 for $p_{T,jet}^{ch} > 10$ GeV/c
- Direct photon triggers and neutral pion triggers are selected with 12 < $p_T^{trig}$ < 20 GeV/c, with associated charged tracks having $p_T^{assoc}$ > 1.2 GeV/c to reach $z_T = p_T^{assoc}/p_T^{trig}$ values down to 0.1
- Di-jet analysis requires leading jet $p_T$ > 20 GeV/c and subleading jet $p_T$ > 10 GeV/c with $|\phi_{lead} - \phi_{sublead} - \pi| < 0.4$
- The high tower trigger requires uncorrected transverse energy $E_T > 5.4$ GeV in the BEMC

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of jet suppression and di-jet modification at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2003-2015) and provides a systematic comparison of three complementary jet observables. The semi-inclusive recoil jet measurement using a mixed-event background subtraction technique follows methods developed by ALICE (2012) and STAR (2015). The results enable quantitative comparison with theoretical models such as those by Zhang, Owens, Wang, Wang (ZOWW), Wang, Qin, and the YaJEM framework, and motivate further measurements of neutral-triggered jets and jet substructure (soft drop grooming) at RHIC energies.
