# Probing in-medium effect via giant dipole resonance in the extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> Rather than using the geometric method employed in the original Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) model, this article employs a stochastic approach to analyze the collision term and examine the width of the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) in ${}^{208}$Pb. Based on the ``soft" EQMD model, which we recently developed, the response and strength functions are self-consistently determined for various symmetry energy coefficients and in-medium reduction factor values. The results confirm that the peak position and GDR width in ${}^{208}$Pb are highly sensitive to the symmetry energy and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon ({\it NN}) cross section. This provides an opportunity to study the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and the medium effect. A significant reduction in free {\it NN} elastic cross sections within the medium is necessary to accurately reproduce the GDR width, as demonstrated by a comparison with the evaluation data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the width of the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) in \({}^{208}\)Pb, calculated using a stochastic collision method within the "soft" Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) model, is highly sensitive to both the symmetry energy coefficient and the in-medium reduction of the free nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross section; a significant reduction of the free NN cross section in the medium is necessary to reproduce the experimental GDR width, a condition not met by the original geometric collision approach.

## Method

The authors replaced the original geometric collision term in the EQMD model with a stochastic (mean-free-path) method, using the free NN elastic scattering cross sections parameterized by Cugnon (1982). They applied a dipole excitation operator to a ground-state \({}^{208}\)Pb nucleus, evolved the system in time, and extracted the GDR strength function via a Fourier transform of the dipole moment. The calculations used the "soft" EQMD model with SLy7 Skyrme parameters, and the symmetry energy coefficient and an in-medium reduction factor for the NN cross section were varied to fit the evaluated GDR data.

## Key numbers

- The GDR width calculated with the original geometric approach underestimates the experimental value by at least **1 MeV**.
- The symmetry energy coefficient \(c_s\) required to match the evaluated GDR peak position is close to **33 MeV**.
- The free NN elastic cross section for the pp/nn system at low incident momentum (\(p_\text{lab} < 0.1\) GeV/\(c\)) is fixed at **628.367 mb**.
- The free NN elastic cross section for the pn system at low incident momentum (\(p_\text{lab} < 0.05\) GeV/\(c\)) is fixed at **3627.89 mb**.
- The maximum cross section in the original EQMD model is **100 mb**, which is about **3 b** lower than the free pn cross section and about **500 mb** lower than the free pp cross section in the low-energy region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the "soft" EQMD model (Shi et al., 2024), which corrected the mean-field incompressibility, by now incorporating a more realistic collision term. It directly addresses the inability of the original geometric collision method (Maruyama et al., 1996) to reproduce the GDR width, a finding consistent with studies using the Lattice BUU model (Wang et al., 2020; Song et al., 2023) that showed the GDR width is sensitive to the in-medium NN cross section. The results enable a new method to constrain the in-medium NN cross section and the symmetry energy at low energies using GDR data.
