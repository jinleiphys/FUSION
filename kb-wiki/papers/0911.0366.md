# Quadrupole collective states within the Bohr collective Hamiltonian

> The article reviews the general version of the Bohr collective model for the description of quadrupole collective states, including a detailed study the model's kinematics. The general form of the classical and quantum Bohr Hamiltonian is presented together with a discussion of the tensor structure of the collective wave functions and with a short review of various methods of solving the Bohr Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation.The methods of derivation of the classical and quantum Bohr Hamiltonian from the microscopic many-body theory are recalled and the microscopic approach to the Bohr Hamiltonian is applied to interpret collective properties of 12 heavy even-even nuclei in the Hf-Hg region.

## Key claim

This review paper presents the general form of the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole collective states, emphasizing that the model can be treated as an algebraic collective model rather than necessarily requiring a geometrical interpretation of the quadrupole tensor coordinates.

## Method

The paper provides a comprehensive kinematic analysis of quadrupole collective coordinates, including both laboratory-frame tensor variables and intrinsic-frame deformation parameters (beta and gamma), along with the associated momentum operators and angular momentum components. It then presents the general structure of the classical and quantum Bohr Hamiltonian, discusses the tensor structure of collective wave functions, and reviews methods for solving the Bohr Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation. Finally, it recalls the Adiabatic Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (ATDHFB) theory and the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) as microscopic approaches for deriving the Bohr Hamiltonian, and applies the microscopic approach to interpret collective properties of twelve heavy even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Jacobian of the transformation from laboratory to intrinsic coordinates is proportional to beta^3 sin(3 gamma), where beta is the deformation parameter (0 <= beta < infinity) and gamma is the angular deformation parameter (-pi <= gamma <= pi)
- The group of transformations of the intrinsic coordinate system contains 48 transformations (the octahedral group O_h), generated by three Bohr rotations (R_1, R_2, R_3) and inversion P
- An arbitrary symmetric quadrupole bitensor field has six independent nonvanishing intrinsic components (not fifteen), expressed in terms of six scalar functions m_0, m_1, m_2, m_2', m_3, and m_4
- The elementary tensors of the quadrupole tensor include two quadrupole tensors, two scalars (sigma_0 = beta^2 and chi_0 = beta^3 cos(3 gamma)), and one octupole tensor

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Bohr (1952) and Bohr and Mottelson (1953) on the collective model, as well as the generalization to large-amplitude quadrupole excitations by Belyaev (1965) and Kumar and Baranger (1967). It provides a unified treatment of the kinematics and dynamics of the Bohr Hamiltonian, enabling future microscopic calculations of collective properties in heavy nuclei through methods such as ATDHFB and GCM, as illustrated by the application to twelve even-even nuclei in the Hf-Hg region.
