# Nucleon structure from 2+1f dynamical DWF lattice QCD at nearly physical pion mass

> Current status of nucleon structure calculations with joint RBC and UKQCD 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermions (DWF) lattice QCD is reported: Two ensembles with pion mass of about (m_π=170) MeV and 250 MeV are used. The lattice cutoff is set at about 1.4 GeV, allowing a large spatial volume of about (L=4.6) fm across while maintaining a sufficiently small residual breaking of chiral symmetry with the dislocation-suppressing-determinant-ratio (DSDR) gauge action. We calculate all the isovector form factors and some low moments of isovector structure functions. We confirm the finite-size effect in isovector axialvector-current form factors, in particular the deficit in the axial charge and its scaling in terms of (m_πL), that we reported from our earlier calculation at heavier pion masses.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the isovector axial charge \(g_A/g_V\) from lattice QCD at nearly physical pion mass (170 MeV and 250 MeV) remains below the experimental value of 1.2701(25) and shows a finite-size effect scaling with \(m_\pi L\), consistent with earlier calculations at heavier pion masses, but the authors caution that the statistics are still preliminary and the dependence may not yet be conclusively attributed to a pion cloud.

## Method

The RBC and UKQCD collaborations used 2+1-flavor dynamical domain-wall fermions (DWF) with a combined Iwasaki and dislocation-suppressing-determinant-ratio (DSDR) gauge action at a lattice cutoff of about 1.4 GeV and spatial volume of about (4.6 fm)^3. They generated two ensembles with pion masses of approximately 170 MeV and 250 MeV, analyzed about 40 configurations from the heavy ensemble and 60 from the light ensemble, and calculated isovector form factors and low moments of isovector structure functions using nucleon source-sink separation of 9 lattice units (about 1.3 fm).

## Key numbers

- Pion masses: approximately 170 MeV and 250 MeV for the two ensembles.
- Lattice cutoff: about 1.4 GeV (preliminary estimate of 1.368(7) GeV).
- Spatial volume: about (4.6 fm)^3.
- Nucleon mass estimates: 0.721(13) lattice units (about 0.98 GeV) for the lighter ensemble and 0.763(10) lattice units (about 1.05 GeV) for the heavier ensemble.
- Isovector vector charge \(g_V\): 1.44(3) for the heavy ensemble and 1.44(2) for the light ensemble.
- Non-perturbative renormalization factor \(Z_V = g_V^{-1} = 0.69(2)\), consistent with axial-current renormalization \(Z_A = 0.6871(4)\) from the meson sector.
- Isovector axial to vector charge ratio \(g_A/g_V\): 1.10(8) for the heavy ensemble and 1.06(11) for the light ensemble, compared to the experimental value of 1.2701(25).
- Finite-size scaling parameter \(m_\pi L\): 5.8 for the 250 MeV ensemble and 4.2 for the 170 MeV ensemble.

## Context

This work extends the RBC and UKQCD collaborations' earlier nucleon structure calculations at heavier pion masses (down to 330 MeV) with simple Iwasaki gauge action at 1.7 GeV cutoff (Yamazaki 2008, Yamazaki 2009, Aoki 2010), where strong finite-size dependence in axialvector form factors was first observed. The new ensembles at lower pion mass and larger spatial volume (4.6 fm) allow the authors to test the scaling of the axial charge deficit with \(m_\pi L\) and to study low moments of structure functions, which showed no finite-size effect in earlier work. The results enable future combined chiral and continuum limit studies for baryons, though the authors note that such an analysis is not yet attempted due to poor understanding of chiral and finite-size corrections for baryons.
