# Second-forbidden nonunique $β^-$ decays of $^{59,60}$Fe: Possible candidates for $g_A$ sensitive electron spectral-shape measurements

> In this work, we present a theoretical study of the electron spectral shapes for the second-forbidden nonunique $β^-$-decay transitions $^{59}\textrm{Fe}(3/2^-)\to\,^{59}\textrm{Co}(7/2^-)$ and $^{60}\textrm{Fe}(0^+)\to\,^{60}\textrm{Co}(2^+)$ in the framework of the nuclear shell model. We have computed the involved wave functions by carrying out a complete $0\hbarω$ calculation in the full $fp$ model space using the KB3G and GXPF1A effective interactions. When compared with the available data, these interactions predict the low-energy spectra and electromagnetic properties of the involved nuclei quite successfully. This success paves the way for the computations of the $β$-decay properties, and comparison with the available data. We have computed the electron spectral shapes of the mentioned decay transitions as functions of the value of the weak axial coupling $g_{\rm A}$. By comparing these computed shapes with the measured spectral shapes allows then to extract the effective value of $g_{\rm A}$ for these decay transitions. This procedure, coined the spectrum-shape method (SSM) in several earlier studies, complements the method of determining the value of $g_{\rm A}$ by reproducing the (partial) half-lives of decay transitions. Here we have enhanced the original SSM by constraining the value of the relativistic vector matrix element, $^V\mathcal{M}^{(0)}_{KK-11}$, using the conserved vector-current hypothesis (CVC) as a starting point. We hope that this finding would be a strong incentive to measure the spectral shapes in the future.

# Second-forbidden nonunique β⁻ decays of ⁵⁹,⁶⁰Fe: Possible candidates for g_A sensitive electron spectral-shape measurements

## Key claim

The electron spectral shapes of the second-forbidden nonunique β⁻ decays ⁵⁹Fe(3/2⁻)→⁵⁹Co(7/2⁻) and ⁶⁰Fe(0⁺)→⁶⁰Co(2⁺) depend sensitively on the effective value of the weak axial coupling g_A when the small relativistic vector matrix element is constrained by fitting to the measured partial half-life, but only when using this method rather than the CVC-constrained approach, because the 0ħω shell-model calculation in the full fp model space yields a vanishing value for this matrix element that must be supplied externally.

## Method

The authors performed complete 0ħω nuclear shell-model calculations in the full fp model space using the KB3G and GXPF1A effective interactions to compute wave functions and one-body transition densities for the two second-forbidden nonunique β⁻ decay transitions. They computed electron spectral shapes as functions of g_A using the formalism of Behrens and Bühring, including next-to-leading-order corrections, and enhanced the spectrum-shape method by constraining the relativistic vector matrix element ⁵Vℳ⁽⁰⁾_{KK-11} either through the conserved vector-current hypothesis or by using it as a fit parameter to reproduce the measured partial half-life for each value of g_A.

## Key numbers

- Q value for ⁵⁹Fe decay: 1.565 MeV; branching ratio: 0.18(4)%; partial half-life: 67.72 years
- Q value for ⁶⁰Fe decay: 0.178 MeV; branching ratio: 100%; partial half-life: 2.62 × 10⁶ years
- CVC-constrained s-NME (⁵Vℳ⁽⁰⁾_{KK-11}) for ⁵⁹Fe: 0.2164 (KB3G) and 0.1921 (GXPF1A); for ⁶⁰Fe: 0.2862 (KB3G) and 0.3055 (GXPF1A)
- Half-life-fitted s-NME at g_A = 0.80 for ⁵⁹Fe: 0.1037 (KB3G) and 0.0950 (GXPF1A); for ⁶⁰Fe: 0.1143 (KB3G) and 0.0919 (GXPF1A)
- Computed first 2⁺ state in ⁶⁰Fe: 0.788 MeV (KB3G) and 0.817 MeV (GXPF1A), compared to experimental value 0.823 MeV
- Experimental B(E2; 2⁺→0⁺) in ⁶⁰Fe: 13.6(14) W.u.; computed: 15.53 W.u. (KB3G) and 18.89 W.u. (GXPF1A)

## Context

This work extends the spectrum-shape method previously applied to ²⁴Na and ³⁶Cl by Kumar et al. (2020) and to ²⁰F by Kirsebom et al. (2019), and complements earlier SSM studies of ⁸⁷Rb, ⁹⁴Nb, ⁹⁸Tc, ⁹⁹Tc, ¹¹³Cd, and ¹¹⁵In by Haaranen et al. (2016, 2017) and Kostensalo et al. (2017). The calculations improve upon a restricted-space study of ⁵⁹Fe by Suhonen (2019) and a truncated-space calculation of ⁶⁰Fe by Kostensalo et al. (2017), using complete 0ħω calculations in the full fp model space. The results provide theoretical motivation for future experimental measurements of the electron spectral shapes, which could enable extraction of the effective g_A value in this mass region.
