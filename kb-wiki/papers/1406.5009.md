# Nuclear collective dynamics within Vlasov approach

> We discuss, in an investigation based on Vlasov equation, the properties of the isovector modes in nuclear matter and atomic nuclei in relation with the symmetry energy. We obtain numerically the dipole response and determine the strength function for various systems, including a chain of Sn isotopes. We consider for the symmetry energy three parametrizations with density providing similar values at saturation but which manifest very different slopes around this point. In this way we can explore how the slope affects the collective response of finite nuclear systems. We focus first on the dipole polarizability and show that while the model is able to describe the expected mass dependence, A^{5/3}, it also demonstrates that this quantity is sensitive to the slope parameter of the symmetry energy. Then, by considering the Sn isotopic chain, we investigate the emergence of a collective mode, the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR), when the number of neutrons in excess increases. We show that the total energy-weighted sum rule exhausted by this mode has a linear dependence with the square of isospin I=(N-Z)/A, again sensitive to the slope of the symmetry energy with density. Therefore the polarization effects in the isovector density have to play an important role in the dynamics of PDR. These results provide additional hints in the investigations aiming to extract the properties of symmetry energy below saturation.

## Key claim

The Vlasov approach can reproduce the expected mass dependence of the dipole polarizability (A^{5/3}) and shows that both the polarizability and the energy-weighted sum rule of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance depend on the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy, even when the symmetry energy at saturation is held nearly constant.

## Method

The authors solved two coupled Vlasov equations for protons and neutrons using a test-particle method with Skyrme-like (SKM*) mean-field parametrizations. They applied a dipole boost initial condition to simulate the collective response of finite nuclei, extracted the strength function from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment, and integrated the response over the low-energy region to isolate the Pygmy Dipole Resonance contribution.

## Key numbers

- For the asystiff equation of state, the symmetry energy at saturation is E_sym/A = 28.3 MeV and the slope parameter L = 72 MeV.
- For the asysoft case, the slope parameter L = 14.4 MeV.
- For the asysuperstiff case, the slope parameter L = 96.6 MeV.
- For the isovector zero sound mode in nuclear matter with C = 32 MeV, the solution gives s = omega/(k v_F) approximately 1.08.
- For the charge mean square radius of 208Pb, the model gives R_p = 5.45 fm, compared to the experimental value of 5.50 fm.
- For 124Sn, the model gives R_p = 4.59 fm, compared to the experimental value of 4.67 fm.
- For 68Ni, the experimental dipole polarizability is 3.14 fm^3; the model gives values from 4.1 fm^3 (asysoft) to 5.7 fm^3 (asysuperstiff).
- For 208Pb, the experimental dipole polarizability is around 20.1 fm^3; the model gives values from 21.1 fm^3 (asysoft) to 28.6 fm^3 (asysuperstiff).
- The energy centroid of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance is described by the parametrization 41 A^{-1/3}.
- The difference between the numerically estimated first moment m_1 and the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule was below 5% in all cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier Vlasov-based studies of isovector modes (Baran et al. 2012, 2013) and provides a semiclassical complement to quantum approaches such as relativistic quasiparticle RPA (Paar et al. 2007, 2009) and time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations (Brinjevic et al. 2006). The results enable further constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation by comparing dipole polarizability and PDR sum-rule predictions with experimental data from systems like 68Ni (Rossi et al. 2013) and 208Pb (Tamii et al. 2011).
