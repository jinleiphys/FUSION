# Short-range repulsion and isospin dependence in the KN system

> The short-range properties of the KN interaction are studied within the meson-exchange model of the Juelich group. Specifically, dynamical explanations for the phenomenological short-range repulsion, required in this model for achieving agreement with the empirical KN data, are explored. Evidence is found that contributions from the exchange of a heavy scalar-isovector meson (a_0(980)) as well as from genuine quark-gluon exchange processes are needed. Taking both mechanisms into account a satisfactory description of the KN phase shifts can be obtained without resorting to phenomenological pieces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the phenomenological short-range repulsive contribution ("σ_rep") required in the Jülich meson-exchange model of the KN interaction can be explained by a combination of a_0(980) meson exchange and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) quark-gluon processes, rather than being a purely phenomenological input.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid model combining the Jülich meson-exchange KN potential with a nonrelativistic quark model calculation of the KN interaction from quark interchange and one-gluon-exchange, including Coulomb, spin-spin, spin-orbit, and confining interactions. They replaced the phenomenological σ_rep (mass 1.2 GeV) in the Jülich model with OGE contributions and added a_0(980) scalar-isovector meson exchange, then solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain phase shifts for comparison with empirical data.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological σ_rep in the original Jülich model had a mass of about 1.2 GeV
- The a_0(980) meson has a mass of about 1 GeV
- Quark model parameters used: m_q = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.55 GeV, α_s/m_q^2 = 0.6/(0.33)^2 GeV^2, nucleon size parameter α = 0.4 GeV, kaon size parameter β = 0.35 GeV
- String tension of confining potential: σ = 0.18 GeV^2
- Physical masses used: M_N = 0.940 GeV, M_K = 0.495 GeV
- The KKω coupling constant in the original Jülich model had to be increased about 60% above the SU(3) symmetry value to describe S-wave data

## Context

This work extends the Jülich group's meson-exchange model for KN scattering (Jülich 1995, 1998) which required a phenomenological σ_rep to fit data. It also builds on quark model calculations of the KN interaction by Barnes and Swanson (1992) and Silvestre-Brac and collaborators (1995-1998), who found that OGE alone could not simultaneously describe both isospin channels. The hybrid approach enables a description of KN phase shifts without phenomenological short-range pieces, and the authors suggest future work should test this model on the anti-kaon-nucleon (Kbar N) system to further validate the approach.
