# Density and isospin asymmetry dependence of high-momentum components

> We study the one-body momentum distribution at different densities in nuclear matter, with special emphasis on its components at high momentum. Explicit calculations for finite neutron-proton asymmetry, based on the ladder self-consistent Green's function approach, allow us to access the isospin dependence of momentum distributions and elucidate their role in neutron-rich systems. Comparisons with the deuteron momentum distribution indicate that a substantial proportion of high-momentum components are dominated by tensor correlations. We identify the density dependence of these tensor correlations in the momentum distributions. Further, we find that high-momentum components are determined by the density of each sub-species and we provide a new isospin asymmetry scaling of these components. We use different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions to quantify the model dependence of our results.

## Key claim

High-momentum components in asymmetric nuclear matter are determined by the density of each nucleon sub-species, and the isospin asymmetry dependence of these components follows a linear scaling relation that holds in the tensor-dominated momentum region (400-550 MeV), with the minority species being more correlated.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations using a ladder summation of diagrams in the two-body sector, solving an in-medium Lippmann-Schwinger equation for neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton channels. They computed one-body momentum distributions at finite temperature (T=5 MeV) for various total densities (0.04 to 0.24 fm⁻³) and isospin asymmetries, using four different nucleon-nucleon interactions: Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, N3LO chiral potential, and Av4' (which lacks tensor terms). The momentum distributions were normalized to unity and compared to the corresponding deuteron momentum distribution for each interaction.

## Key numbers

- The tensor-dominated momentum region is defined as k = 400-550 MeV, with high-momentum components studied up to approximately 850 MeV
- At saturation density (ρ=0.16 fm⁻³), the ratio a₂ (integrated average of n(k)/n_d(k) in the tensor-dominated region) ranges from approximately 2 to 2.7 for the three phase-shift equivalent interactions at half saturation
- The density dependence of a₂ follows a power law a₂ = b₁ ρ^{b₂} with b₁=7-10 and b₂=0.4-0.5, yielding values between approximately 1 at low densities and 5 at large densities
- The linear scaling parameter γ(ρ) is negative and shows similar values across different phase-shift equivalent interactions, with the scaling relation n_τ(k; ρ, β) = [1 ± γ(ρ) β] × n_τ(k; ρ, 0) holding at densities up to ρ=0.24 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous SCGF studies of low-momentum depletion in asymmetric matter (Rios 2009a) and complements microscopic analyses of high-momentum components' impact on isovector properties (Xu 2011, Vidana 2011, Carbone 2012). The results provide a microscopic foundation for scaling arguments proposed by Frankfurt (2009), Sargsian (2011, 2012), and others, while contradicting the power-law scaling n_τ ∼ [1 ± β]^{-γ} with γ∼1 suggested by Sargsian (2012). The findings enable improved modeling of neutron-rich systems and neutron-star matter, where extrapolations to high isospin asymmetry require the explicit linear dependence identified here.
