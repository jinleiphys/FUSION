# Refined Glauber model versus Faddeev calculations and experimental data for $pd$ spin observables

> Spin-dependent observables in intermediate-energy $pd$ elastic scattering within the framework of the refined Glauber model are considered. The improvements include an account of all ten $pp$ and $pn$ helicity amplitudes at respective energies constructed on the basis of modern phase-shift analysis, accurate deuteron wave functions taken from the modern $NN$ force model and account of charge-exchange effects. Predictions of the refined diffraction model for the differential cross section and analyzing powers are compared with exact three-body Faddeev calculations and the recent experimental data. An amazingly good agreement between the results of both theoretical approaches as well as between the refined Glauber model and experiment in a wide angular range not only for differential cross section but also for vector and tensor analyzing powers has been found in the first time. Possible reasons for this agreement are discussed.

# Refined Glauber model versus Faddeev calculations and experimental data for $pd$ spin observables

## Key claim

The refined Glauber model, when incorporating all ten $pp$ and $pn$ helicity amplitudes from modern phase-shift analysis, accurate deuteron wave functions, and charge-exchange effects, shows "amazingly good agreement" with exact three-body Faddeev calculations and experimental data for $pd$ elastic scattering observables at intermediate energies, but this agreement is demonstrated primarily for transferred momenta squared $|t| \lesssim 0.35$ (GeV/$c)^2$ and the agreement for tensor analyzing powers at lower energies ($T_p \simeq 250$ MeV) is notably less good than for differential cross sections and vector analyzing powers.

## Method

The authors generalized the conventional Glauber diffraction model for $pd$ elastic scattering by incorporating all five $pp$ and five $pn$ helicity amplitudes (reconstructed from modern phase-shift analysis via the SAID database), using high-precision deuteron $S$- and $D$-wave functions from the CD-Bonn $NN$ potential model, and including double charge-exchange contributions. All input $NN$ amplitudes and deuteron wave functions were parametrized as multi-Gaussian series (five terms each) to enable fully analytical derivation of all 12 invariant $pd$ amplitudes, from which differential cross sections and analyzing powers were computed. The predictions were compared with exact three-body Faddeev calculations and experimental data at incident proton energies of 250 MeV, 440 MeV, and 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section and analyzing powers calculated at $T_p = 250$ MeV, $T_p = 440$ MeV, and $T_p = 1$ GeV (equivalent proton energy for deuteron observables)
- Good agreement between refined Glauber model, Faddeev calculations, and experiment observed for $|t| \lesssim 0.35$ (GeV/$c)^2$
- At $T_p = 250$ MeV, comparison with Faddeev results using CD-Bonn potential and experimental data from Ref. [EXP250] is shown for differential cross section and proton analyzing power
- At $T_p = 440$ MeV, Glauber results compared with Faddeev calculation at 440 MeV and experimental data at $T_p = 425$ MeV for differential cross section; for proton analyzing power, comparison uses Faddeev result and experimental data at $T_p = 392$ MeV
- At $T_p = 1$ GeV, comparison with experimental data from Refs. [EXPDS1] and [EXPAD1] shown for differential cross section and deuteron vector and tensor analyzing powers
- The ratio of spin-dependent $NN$ amplitudes to spin-independent ones decreases strongly as energy rises, making the approximation of neglecting higher-order spin-$D$-wave products (terms with $k+l \geq 3$) accurate at $T_p \sim 1$ GeV but potentially unsatisfied at $T_p \sim 100$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber model studies by incorporating full spin dependence of $NN$ amplitudes, deuteron $D$-wave, and isospin effects (charge exchange) simultaneously, whereas previous works had treated these contributions separately (KSP, FSP for spin dependence; HD, GD for deuteron $D$-wave; WEX, GEX for charge exchange). The authors compare their refined diffraction model predictions with exact three-body Faddeev calculations, which had previously been solved only for incident energies below 350 MeV (FC250, EXP248), and note that a similar comparison by Elster (ELSTER) using a simple central MT-III potential found agreement only at $T_N \gtrsim 1$ GeV, whereas the present work finds good agreement already at $T_p \simeq 250$ MeV when realistic input is used. The results enable a potential inverse scattering problem: using precise $pd$ experimental data to extract poorly known $nn$ or $pn$ scattering amplitudes at intermediate energies.
