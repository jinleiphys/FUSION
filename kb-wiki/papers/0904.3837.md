# Particle Production within the Quark Meson Coupling Model

> Quark meson coupling (QMC) models can be successfully applied to the description of compact star properties in nuclear astrophysics as well as to nuclear matter. In the regime of hot hadronic matter very few calculations exist using the QMC model, in particular when applied to particle yields in heavy ion collisions. In the present work, we identify the free energy of the bag with the effective mass of the baryons and we calculate the particle production yields on a Au+Au collision at RHIC with the QMC model and compare them with results obtained previously with other relativistic models. A smaller temperature for the fireball, T=132 MeV, is obtained due to the smaller effective baryon masses predicted by QMC. QMC was also applied to the description of particle yields at SPS in Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, when the effective baryon mass is identified with the free energy of the MIT bag (rather than the bag energy), predicts a smaller freeze-out temperature of T=132 MeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, compared to other relativistic models, because QMC yields smaller effective baryon masses at finite temperature.

## Method

The authors modeled nuclear matter as a system of non-overlapping MIT bags interacting through scalar and vector mean fields (σ, ω, ρ) in the mean-field approximation. They identified the effective mass of each baryon with the free energy of its bag, solved the self-consistent equations for the meson fields and quark chemical potentials at finite temperature, and performed a χ² fit to experimental particle ratios from Au+Au collisions at √s=130 GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions (SPS) to determine freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature for Au+Au at RHIC: T = 132 MeV (QMC), compared to 149 MeV (NL3) and 146.6 MeV (TW)
- Baryon chemical potential for Au+Au at RHIC: μ_b = 32.5 MeV (QMC), compared to 47.5 MeV (NL3) and 62.8 MeV (TW)
- Freeze-out density for Au+Au at RHIC: ρ = 3.08 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³ (QMC), compared to 8.37 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³ (NL3) and 4.90 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³ (TW)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at freeze-out: M*_N/M_N = 0.78 (QMC), compared to 0.88 (NL3) and 0.87 (TW)
- χ² value for Au+Au fit: 27.56 (QMC), compared to 23.94 (NL3) and 22.18 (TW)
- For Pb+Pb at SPS: QMC gave T = 130 MeV, μ_b = 167.5 MeV, χ² = 216

## Context

This work extends previous studies of particle production in heavy ion collisions using relativistic mean-field models, specifically the NL3 parametrization (Lalazissis et al., 1997) and the TW density-dependent hadronic model (Typel and Wolter, 1999) as reported in earlier work by the same group. It also contrasts with the thermal model of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) which gave T=174±7 MeV, and the chiral model of Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2002) which gave T=155 MeV. The QMC model's lower freeze-out temperature and smaller effective masses suggest that the freeze-out occurs below the critical temperature for the phase transition to a massless baryon phase. The approach enables testing the QMC model in the high-temperature, low-density regime relevant to heavy ion collisions, though the simplified treatment of mesons as free particles limits the quality of fits, especially for SPS data.
