# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei with local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

> Local chiral effective field theory interactions have recently been developed and used in the context of quantum Monte Carlo few- and many-body methods for nuclear physics. In this work, we go over detailed features of local chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions and examine their effect on properties of the deuteron, paying special attention to the perturbativeness of the expansion. We then turn to three-nucleon interactions, focusing on operator ambiguities and their interplay with regulator effects. We then discuss the nuclear Green's function Monte Carlo method, going over both wave-function correlations and approximations for the two- and three-body propagators. Following this, we present a range of results on light nuclei: Binding energies and distribution functions are contrasted and compared, starting from several different microscopic interactions.

## Key claim

Local chiral effective field theory interactions can be constructed up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) that are fully local in coordinate space, enabling their use in quantum Monte Carlo methods, but the resulting predictions for light nuclei carry truncation uncertainties estimated at approximately 50% and 67% degree-of-belief intervals at NLO and N2LO respectively, and the deuteron binding energy is consistent with experiment only when these uncertainties are taken into account.

## Method

The authors constructed local chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions by removing nonlocalities through coordinate-space regulator functions and by exploiting Fierz ambiguities to select local contact operators up to N2LO. They then implemented these interactions in Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for light nuclei, using spectral function regularization with cutoff 1000 MeV for two-pion-exchange contributions and fitting the 3N low-energy constants c_D and c_E to A >= 3 experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy at N2LO: 2.21(2) MeV for R0 = 1.0 fm and 2.20(3) MeV for R0 = 1.2 fm (experimental: 2.224575(9) MeV)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio eta_d: 0.0263(3) for R0 = 1.0 fm and 0.0267(6) for R0 = 1.2 fm (experimental: 0.0256(4))
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d: 0.286(5) fm^2 for R0 = 1.0 fm and 0.289(6) fm^2 for R0 = 1.2 fm (experimental: 0.2859(3) fm^2)
- Deuteron root-mean-square matter radius: 1.97(2) fm for R0 = 1.0 fm and 1.97(3) fm for R0 = 1.2 fm (experimental: 1.9660(68) fm)
- Coordinate-space cutoff range adopted: 1.0 to 1.2 fm, corresponding to momentum scales approximately 500 MeV and 400 MeV respectively
- Spectral function regularization cutoff: 1000 MeV, with increasing from 1 to 1.4 GeV changing He-4 binding energy by less than approximately 2%

## Context

This work extends the development of local chiral interactions by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and Tews et al. (2016) to detailed calculations of light nuclei, providing a systematic alternative to phenomenological models such as the Argonne v18 interaction (Wiringa, 1995) and the Urbana/Illinois three-nucleon forces (Pudliner, 1997; Pieper, 2001). The results enable benchmark comparisons with other ab initio methods including the no-core shell model (Barrett, 2013), coupled-cluster method (Hagen, 2013, 2015), and in-medium similarity renormalization group (Hergert, 2015), and provide a foundation for future calculations of neutron matter and neutron-rich systems with controlled chiral EFT uncertainties.
