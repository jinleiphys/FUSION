# Threshold energy for sub-barrier fusion hindrance phenomenon

> The relationship between the threshold energy for a deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance phenomenon and the energy at which the regime of interaction changes (the turning-off of the nuclear forces and friction) in the sub-barrier capture process, is studied within the quantum diffusion approach. The quasielastic barrier distribution is shown to be a useful tool to clarify whether the slope of capture cross section changes at sub-barrier energies.

# Wiki Page: Threshold Energy for Sub-Barrier Fusion Hindrance Phenomenon

## Key claim
The threshold energy \(E_s\) for deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance coincides with the energy \(E_{ch}\) at which the regime of interaction changes (nuclear forces and friction turn off) in the sub-barrier capture process, as demonstrated within the quantum diffusion approach. The quasielastic barrier distribution is predicted to show a sharp change in slope below this threshold energy, which could serve as an experimental signature of the regime change.

## Method
The authors employed the quantum diffusion approach, treating capture in terms of a single collective variable (relative distance between colliding nuclei) with fluctuation and dissipation effects modeling channel couplings. They calculated the energy \(E_{ch}\) where the external turning point \(R_{ex}\) equals the interaction radius \(R_{int}=R_b+1.1\) fm (where nucleon density reaches about 10% of saturation density), and compared it with experimental threshold energies \(E_s\) for fusion hindrance across a range of reactions. The quasielastic barrier distribution \(D_{qe}(E_{\rm c.m.})=dP_{cap}(E_{\rm c.m.},J=0)/dE_{\rm c.m.}\) was computed from the capture probability to predict the slope change.

## Key numbers
- For reactions including \(^{4}\)He + \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{58}\)Ni + \(^{54}\)Fe, \(^{48}\)Ca + \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{90,96}\)Zr, \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{90,96}\)Zr, \(^{58}\)Ni + \(^{58,60,64}\)Ni, \(^{60}\)Ni + \(^{89}\)Y, \(^{64}\)Ni + \(^{64}\)Ni, \(^{100}\)Mo, \(^{90}\)Zr+\(^{90}\)Zr, and \(^{16}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{238}\)U, the values \(E_s\) and \(E_{ch}\) almost coincide and linearly increase with \(Z_1Z_2[A_1A_2/(A_1+A_2)]^{1/2}\).
- The interaction radius is defined as \(R_{int}=R_b+1.1\) fm, where \(R_b\) is the Coulomb barrier position.
- The nuclear forces start acting when the nucleon density reaches approximately 10% of saturation density at distance \(R_{int}\).

## Context
This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by the same group (Sargsyan, Adamian, Antonenko, and collaborators, 2009-2012) for describing heavy-ion capture reactions above and below the Coulomb barrier. It provides a theoretical explanation for the fusion hindrance phenomenon observed experimentally by Jiang and collaborators (2002-2006) and discussed by Gomes and Bertulani. The predicted sharp change in quasielastic barrier distribution slope offers a new experimental tool to identify the regime change, complementing direct capture measurements. The authors suggest that reactions with spherical nuclei such as \(^{4}\)He, \(^{16}\)O + Sn, \(^{144}\)Sm, \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{48,40}\)Ca, \(^{36}\)S + \(^{90}\)Zr are preferable for experimental verification.
