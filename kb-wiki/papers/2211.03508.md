# A coupled-channel system with anomalous thresholds and unitarity

> We consider the isospin one-half example system, with $D \,π, D\,η, D_s \bar K, D^* π, D^*η, D^*\bar K$ coupled channels in the $J^P = 1^-$ partial wave, chosen such that various phenomena that come with the opening of an anomalous threshold can be illustrated in a step-wise procedure by a suitable variation of up, down and strange quark masses. We use a set of LEC in the chiral Lagrangian that were adjusted to a large set of Lattice QCD results. The six phase shifts and inelasticity parameters are presented for various choices of the pion mass. For a pion mass of 150 MeV there are no anomalous thresholds encountered. The small change from 150 MeV to 145 MeV pion mass causes a dramatic impact of the anomalous threshold on the phase shifts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a small change in the pion mass from 150 MeV to 145 MeV causes a dramatic impact on the phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in a coupled-channel system with \(J^P = 1^-\) quantum numbers, specifically due to the opening of an anomalous threshold in the \(D^* \pi \leftrightarrow D^* \eta\) channel from u-channel exchange of the \(D\) meson.

## Method

The authors constructed a six-channel partial-wave reaction amplitude for the isospin one-half system with channels \(D \pi, D \eta, D_s \bar K, D^* \pi, D^* \eta, D^* \bar K\) in the \(J^P = 1^-\) partial wave. They used a set of low-energy constants (LEC) from the chiral Lagrangian, previously adjusted to a large set of Lattice QCD results, and solved the nonlinear coupled-channel integral equation with the Generalized Potential Approach (GPA) extended to handle anomalous thresholds as developed in Lutz (2015) and Lutz (2018). The quark masses were varied such that the kaon mass remained at its physical value while the pion mass was changed, and the integration contours in the integral equation were modified to avoid crossing of left-hand and right-hand cut lines.

## Key numbers

- For a pion mass of 150 MeV, no anomalous thresholds are encountered in the system.
- Reducing the pion mass from 150 MeV to 145 MeV causes a dramatic impact on the phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in the \(D \pi\) and \(D^* \pi\) channels.
- The anomalous left-hand cut in the \(D^* \pi \leftrightarrow D^* \eta\) channel develops from u-channel exchange of the \(D\) meson at pion masses smaller than about 150 MeV.
- At the physical pion mass, the left-hand cut in the \(D^* \pi \to D^* \pi\) channel has branch points above the \(D^* \pi\) threshold.

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Potential Approach (GPA) introduced by Gasparyan (2010, 2011, 2012) and Danilkin (2010) to handle anomalous thresholds in coupled-channel systems, building on the formal developments of Lutz (2015, 2018). It contrasts with earlier p-wave studies that did not account for such thresholds, and it enables chiral extrapolations of Lattice QCD results from unphysically large quark masses down to the physical point, where small changes in the pion mass can cause dramatic effects in phase shifts and potentially the dynamic generation of p-wave resonances.
