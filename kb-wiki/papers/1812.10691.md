# Basics of doubly heavy tetraquarks

> We outline the most important results regarding the stability of doubly heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$ with an adequate treatment of the four-body dynamics. We consider both color-mixing and spin-dependent effects. Our results are straightforwardly applied to the case of all-heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$. We conclude that the stability is favored in the limit $M_Q/m_q \gg 1$ pointing to the stability of the $bb\bar u\bar d$ state and the instability of all-heavy tetraquarks.

# Basics of doubly heavy tetraquarks

## Key claim
Doubly heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$ become bound only when the heavy-to-light mass ratio $M_Q/m_q$ is sufficiently large, with the $bb\bar u\bar d$ state predicted stable but all-heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$ unstable; the stability of $cc\bar u\bar d$ is marginal and requires both color-mixing and spin-spin interactions to be treated accurately in a full four-body calculation.

## Method
The authors solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for tetraquarks using a Hamiltonian with pairwise Coulomb-plus-linear confining potentials and a regularized Breit-Fermi spin-spin interaction (the AL1 potential). They compare exact variational solutions (using correlated Gaussians or hyperspherical expansions) with the diquark-antidiquark cluster approximation, and analyze the effects of color-mixing between $\bar 33$ and $6\bar 6$ configurations and of spin-dependent corrections on the binding energy as a function of the mass ratio $M_Q/m_q$.

## Key numbers
- The $bb\bar u\bar d$ system acquires a binding energy of approximately 150 MeV when the spin-spin term is included.
- The hydrogen-molecule analog ($M^+ m^+ M^- m^-$) becomes unstable for a mass ratio $M/m \geq 2.2$.
- For the $cc\bar u\bar d$ system, binding requires both color mixing and spin-spin interaction, and a hyperspherical expansion up to grand orbital momentum $K_{\rm{max}} \approx 12$ is needed for convergence.
- The all-heavy tetraquark $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$ satisfies a Hall-Post lower bound $E_{\rm min}(H_4) \ge 2\,E_{\rm min}(Q\bar Q)$, demonstrating its instability.

## Context
This work extends the pioneering study of Ader, Richard and Taxil (1982) showing that $QQ\bar q\bar q$ configurations become more bound as $M_Q/m_q$ increases, and addresses the current consensus on a stable axial-vector doubly bottom tetraquark found in lattice QCD by Francis et al. (2017) and in diquark-model analyses by Karliner and Rosner (2017). The authors caution that the diquark approximation can overestimate binding, as shown in Richard (2018), and that the all-heavy tetraquark case differs from the positronium molecule because the color algebra introduces larger disorder in the interaction strengths. The results enable future precise calculations of $cc\bar u\bar d$ near threshold, where its decay modes ($DD\pi$ or $DD\gamma$) depend on the exact binding energy.
