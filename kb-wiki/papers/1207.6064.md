# Neutron degeneracy and plasma physics effects on radiative neutron captures in neutron star crust

> We consider the astrophysical reaction rates for radiative neutron capture reactions ($n,γ$) in the crust of a neutron star. The presence of degenerate neutrons at high densities (mainly in the inner crust) can drastically affect the reaction rates. Standard rates assuming a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for neutrons can underestimate the rates by several orders of magnitude. We derive simple analytical expressions for reaction rates at a variety of conditions with account for neutron degeneracy. We also discuss the plasma effects on the outgoing radiative transition channel in neutron radiative capture reactions and show that these effects can also increase the reaction rates by a few orders of magnitude. In addition, using detailed balance, we analyze the effects of neutron degeneracy and plasma physics on reverse ($γ,n$) photodisintegration. We discuss the dependence of the reaction rates on temperature and neutron chemical potential and outline the efficiency of these reactions in the neutron star crust.

## Key claim

Neutron degeneracy in the inner crust of neutron stars can enhance radiative neutron capture reaction rates by several orders of magnitude compared to standard Maxwell-Boltzmann-based rates, and plasma effects on the radiative transition channel can further increase the rates by a few orders of magnitude, with the combined correction factor depending on temperature, neutron chemical potential, and reaction threshold.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the ratio \(R_n\) of the reaction rate coefficient calculated with a Fermi-Dirac neutron distribution to that calculated with a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, using a power-law approximation for the neutron capture cross section \(\sigma(E) = \sigma_a (E-E_0)^\nu\). They employed the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model code TALYS-1.2 to compute cross sections for specific reactions, using the HFB-17 mass model, microscopic level densities, and a quasiparticle random-phase-approximation-based E1 gamma-ray strength function. For plasma effects, they introduced a correction factor \(R_\mathrm{pl}\) based on an analytic approximation for the enhancement of electric dipole radiative transitions in a degenerate electron plasma, and combined it with the degeneracy factor to obtain the total correction \(R_{n,\mathrm{pl}}\).

## Key numbers

- Neutron Fermi energy in ground-state matter at \(\rho = 6.2 \times 10^{12}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) is approximately 2.6 MeV.
- The neutron drip density is \(\rho \sim 4 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) for cold-catalyzed crust and \(\rho \sim 6 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) for accreted crust.
- For the \(^{46}\mathrm{Mg}(n,\gamma)^{47}\mathrm{Mg}\) reaction, the threshold energy is \(E_0 = 4.06\) MeV and the power-law index is \(\nu = 3.5\) with \(E_\mathrm{max} \approx 6\) MeV.
- For the \(^{39}\mathrm{Mg}(n,\gamma)^{40}\mathrm{Mg}\) reaction, the power-law index is \(\nu = -0.6\) with \(E_\mathrm{max} \approx 0.1\) MeV.
- The plasma enhancement factor for electric dipole transitions is approximated as \(\mathcal{R}_{\gamma e1}(\omega) \approx 1 + 3.03 (\omega_p/\omega)^2\), with maximum error 17% at \(\omega = \omega_p\).
- For the \(^{62}\mathrm{Ca}(n,\gamma)^{63}\mathrm{Ca}\) reaction, the threshold is \(E_0 = 0.78\) MeV and the power-law approximation holds up to \(E_\mathrm{max} \approx 10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of neutron capture reactions in neutron star crusts beyond the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann approach used in reaction network simulations (e.g., Rauscher and Thielemann 2000) by incorporating neutron degeneracy, which becomes important above the neutron drip density. The plasma effects on radiative transitions build on the analysis of Shternin and Yakovlev (2009) for degenerate electron plasmas. The derived analytical correction factors enable future detailed reaction network calculations for nucleosynthesis and crustal heating in accreting neutron stars, and the authors note that full inclusion of plasma effects in Hauser-Feshbach calculations remains a computational project for the future.
