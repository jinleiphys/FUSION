# Microscopic calculations and energy expansions for neutron-rich matter

> We investigate asymmetric nuclear matter with two- and three-nucleon interactions based on chiral effective field theory, where three-body forces are fit only to light nuclei. Focusing on neutron-rich matter, we calculate the energy for different proton fractions and include estimates of the theoretical uncertainty. We use our ab-initio results to test the quadratic expansion around symmetric matter with the symmetry energy term, and confirm its validity for highly asymmetric systems. Our calculations are in remarkable agreement with an empirical parametrization for the energy density. These findings are very useful for astrophysical applications and for developing new equations of state.

## Key claim

The quadratic expansion of the energy of asymmetric nuclear matter around symmetric matter using the symmetry energy term is confirmed to be a very good approximation even for highly neutron-rich systems (proton fractions up to 0.15), based on ab-initio calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions where three-nucleon forces are fit only to light nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated the energy of asymmetric nuclear matter for densities up to 0.2 fm^{-3} and proton fractions up to 0.15 using low-momentum NN interactions evolved from the N^3LO 500 MeV potential and leading N^2LO 3N forces. They included NN interactions at Hartree-Fock and second-order perturbative levels and 3N interactions at Hartree-Fock level, approximating the contributions by keeping only terms with zero, one, or two proton lines (neglecting contributions from two or more protons). The theoretical uncertainty was estimated by varying the cutoff scales and the c_i couplings across seven interaction sets.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}: E_sym = 30.8 ± 0.8 MeV (increasing to 31.2 ± 1.0 MeV when including small contributions from two or more proton lines)
- Symmetry energy at n = 0.05 fm^{-3}: E_sym = 15.8 ± 0.2 MeV
- Symmetry energy at n = 0.10 fm^{-3}: E_sym = 24.0 ± 0.2 MeV
- Neglected contributions from two or more proton lines at saturation density for x = 0.15: E_pp/A = -0.9 MeV (2.4% of NN interaction energy) and [E_ppn + E_ppp]/A = 0.6 MeV (7.2% of 3N interaction energy)
- For x = 0.05 at saturation density: E_pp/A = -0.2 MeV (0.4%) and [E_ppn + E_ppp]/A = 0.1 MeV (1.0%)

## Context

This work presents the first calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter using chiral effective field theory interactions with three-nucleon forces fit only to few-body data, extending prior work on neutron matter by Hebeler (2009) and symmetric matter by Hebeler (2010). The results are compared to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Zuo (2012) using the Argonne v_18 potential and to in-medium chiral perturbation theory by Fiorilla (2011). The symmetry energy extracted from these calculations shows remarkable agreement with experimental constraints from isobaric analog states and neutron skins analyzed by Danielewicz (2013), and the empirical parametrization used by Hebeler (2013) to extrapolate from neutron matter to asymmetric matter for astrophysical applications is validated within uncertainties.
