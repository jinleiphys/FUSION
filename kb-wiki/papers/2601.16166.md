# String Breaking and Glueball Dynamics in $2+1$D Quantum Link Electrodynamics

> At the heart of quark confinement and hadronization, the physics of flux strings has recently become a focal point in the field of quantum simulation of high-energy physics (HEP). Despite considerable progress, a detailed understanding of the behavior of flux strings in quantum simulation-relevant lattice formulations of gauge theories has remained limited to the lowest truncations of the gauge field, which are severely limited in their ability to draw conclusions about the quantum field theory limit. Here, we employ tensor network simulations to investigate the behavior of flux strings in a quantum link formulation of $2+1$D quantum electrodynamics (QED) with a spin-$1$ representation of the gauge field. We first map out the ground-state phase diagram of this model in the presence of two spatially separated static charges, revealing distinct microscopic processes responsible for string breaking, including a two-stage breaking mechanism not possible in the spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ formulation. Starting in different initial product state string configurations, we then explore far-from-equilibrium quench dynamics across various parameter regimes, demonstrating genuine $2+1$D real-time string breaking and glueball-like bound state formation, with the latter not possible in the spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ formulation. In and out of equilibrium, we consider different values and placements of the static charges. Finally, we provide efficient qudit circuits for a quantum simulation experiment in which our results can be observed in state-of-the-art ion-trap setups. Our findings lay the groundwork for quantum simulations of flux strings towards the quantum field theory limit.

## Key claim

Using tensor network simulations of a $2+1$D U(1) quantum link model with a spin-1 representation of the gauge field, the authors demonstrate that string breaking and glueball-like bound state formation occur in genuine $2+1$D real-time dynamics only when a finite plaquette term is present, and that a two-stage string-breaking mechanism exists for static charges of magnitude $q=2$ that is not possible in the spin-1/2 formulation.

## Method

The authors employ density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) simulations using matrix product states with bond dimensions up to $\chi = 600$ to compute ground states of the $2+1$D U(1) quantum link model on a $16 \times 6$ rectangular lattice with open (periodic) boundary conditions along the $x$ ($y$) direction. They enforce static charges of magnitude $q=1$ and $q=2$ at specific lattice sites through large energy penalty terms, and monitor the vacuum-subtracted matter density and von Neumann entanglement entropy as functions of the electric coupling $g_E$ at fixed magnetic coupling $g_B$. For out-of-equilibrium dynamics, they prepare initial product state string configurations and perform quench dynamics across various parameter regimes.

## Key numbers

- Static charges of magnitude $q=1$ and $q=2$ are placed at opposite corners of a rectangular patch of dimensions $l_1 = 5$ and $l_2 = 4$ on a lattice of size $L_x = 16$ and $L_y = 6$
- Ground-state calculations use bond dimensions up to $\chi = 600$ for convergence
- For $q=1$ static charges, the jump in vacuum-subtracted matter density at the string-breaking transition has magnitude precisely two, consistent with creation of one dynamical matter pair
- For $q=2$ static charges, two successive jumps in vacuum-subtracted matter density occur, each of magnitude two, corresponding to two distinct string-breaking transitions
- The mass parameter is set to $m = 6$ throughout the equilibrium calculations
- String-breaking transitions occur at electric coupling values around $g_E/\kappa = 3.0$ to $5.0$ depending on the magnetic coupling $g_B$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flux strings in quantum link models that were limited to spin-1/2 representations, such as the QuEra experiment by Gonzalez-Cuadra (2025) which observed string breaking on a hexagonal lattice without a plaquette term, resulting in effectively 1+1D dynamics. The spin-1 formulation lifts fundamental limitations of the spin-1/2 case, including the inability to realize a two-stage string-breaking mechanism and the impossibility of dynamically forming glueball-like closed loops. The two-stage string-breaking mechanism observed here is analogous to results in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory studied through Monte Carlo techniques by Pepe (2009). The findings enable future quantum simulation experiments using qudit processors, for which the authors provide efficient qudit circuits compatible with state-of-the-art ion-trap setups.
