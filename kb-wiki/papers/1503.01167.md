# The isospin quartic term in the kinetic energy of neutron-rich nucleonic matter

> The energy of a free gas of neutrons and protons is well known to be approximately isospin parabolic with a negligibly small quartic term of only $0.45$ MeV at the saturation density of nuclear matter $ρ_0=0.16/\rm{fm}^3$. Using an isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution including a high (low) momentum tail (depletion) with its shape parameters constrained by recent high-energy electron scattering and medium-energy nuclear photodisintegration experiments as well as the state-of-the-art calculations of the deuteron wave function and the equation of state of pure neutron matter near the unitary limit within several modern microscopic many-body theories, we show for the first time that the kinetic energy of interacting nucleons in neutron-rich nucleonic matter has a significant quartic term of $7.18\pm2.52\,\rm{MeV}$. Such a large quartic term has significant ramifications in determining the equation of state of neutron-rich nucleonic matter using both terrestrial and astrophysical observables.

# The isospin quartic term in the kinetic energy of neutron-rich nucleonic matter

## Key claim

The kinetic energy of interacting nucleons in neutron-rich nucleonic matter has a significant isospin quartic term of 7.18 ± 2.52 MeV at saturation density, about 16 times the free Fermi gas prediction of 0.45 MeV, but this result depends on the assumption that Tan's adiabatic sweep theorem relating the high-momentum tail strength to the equation of state is valid up to saturation density for pure neutron matter, which the authors acknowledge has not been proven.

## Method

The authors constructed an isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution with a high-momentum tail (HMT) of the form C_J / |k|^4 and a low-momentum depletion region, parameterized by four quantities (depletion Δ_J, momentum-dependence strength β_J, HMT amplitude C_J, and cutoff φ_J) expanded linearly in isospin asymmetry δ. They constrained these parameters using experimental data on high-momentum tail fractions in symmetric nuclear matter (28% ± 4%) and pure neutron matter (1.5% ± 0.5%) from electron scattering and photodisintegration experiments, together with the equation of state of pure neutron matter near the unitary limit from Tan's universal relations and modern many-body calculations. The kinetic energy per nucleon was then expanded in powers of δ^2 to extract the quartic coefficient E^{kin}_{sym,4}(ρ_0).

## Key numbers

- E^{kin}_{sym,4}(ρ_0) = 7.18 ± 2.52 MeV for interacting nucleons at saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- E^{kin}_{sym}(ρ_0) = -14.28 ± 11.59 MeV (kinetic symmetry energy, negative due to HMT effects)
- E^{kin}_0(ρ_0) = 39.77 ± 8.13 MeV (kinetic energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter)
- Ratio |E^{kin}_{sym,4}(ρ_0) / E^{kin}_{sym}(ρ_0)| ≈ 52% ± 26%, compared to 3.7% in the free Fermi gas
- Free Fermi gas quartic term: E^{kin}_{sym,4}(ρ_0) = 0.45 MeV at ρ_0
- HMT amplitude in symmetric nuclear matter: C_0 ≈ 0.161 ± 0.015 (average of two experimental constraints)
- HMT cutoff in symmetric nuclear matter: φ_0 = 2.38 ± 0.56
- HMT fraction in symmetric nuclear matter: x^{HMT}_{SNM} = 28% ± 4%
- HMT fraction in pure neutron matter: x^{HMT}_{PNM} = 1.5% ± 0.5%
- Isospin dependence of HMT amplitude: C_1 = -0.25 ± 0.07
- Isospin dependence of HMT cutoff: φ_1 = -0.56 ± 0.10

## Context

This work extends studies of short-range correlations in nucleonic matter by Hen et al. (2014, 2015) and Weiss et al. (2015), who established the high-momentum tail fractions and C/|k|^4 behavior from electron scattering and photodisintegration experiments. It contradicts the long-standing assumption that the kinetic energy of asymmetric nuclear matter follows the isospin parabolic law (quadratic in δ) as in the free Fermi gas model, which had been verified to high accuracy by Bombaci and Lombardo (1991) for the total equation of state but not separately for the kinetic part. The finding enables future studies of isospin-quartic effects in heavy-ion collisions using transport models, and it provides a necessary correction for extracting the potential part of the symmetry energy from terrestrial and astrophysical observables, particularly for determining the proton fraction in β-equilibrium and the critical density for the direct URCA process in neutron stars as studied by Sjöberg (1974) and subsequent authors.
