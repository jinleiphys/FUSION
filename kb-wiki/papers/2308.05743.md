# "QGP Signatures" Revisited

> We revisit the graphic table of QCD signatures in our 1996 Annual Reviews article "The Search for the Quark-Gluon Plasma" and assess the progress that has been made since its publication towards providing quantitative evidence for the formation of a quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and its characteristic properties.

## Key claim

The qualitative signatures for quark-gluon plasma formation proposed in 1996 have been largely confirmed by data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC, but the transition is a smooth crossover rather than a first-order phase transition, so observable changes are more gradual than originally anticipated; the critical energy density is now known from lattice QCD to be approximately 0.3-0.4 GeV/fm³ rather than the previously assumed value somewhat below 1 GeV/fm³.

## Method

The authors revisit the graphic table of QCD signatures from their 1996 Annual Reviews article and assess progress by comparing experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC heavy-ion collisions against the original qualitative expectations for strangeness enhancement, quarkonium suppression, and other observables. They convert measured charged-particle multiplicities into estimates of initial energy density using the Bjorken model with isentropic expansion and lattice-QCD thermodynamic relations, covering collision energies from 7.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV for the heaviest collision systems (Au+Au and Pb+Pb).

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density from lattice QCD: ε_c ≈ 0.3-0.4 GeV/fm³ (depending on definition of pseudocritical temperature T_c)
- Initial energy density at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV approximately coincides with the threshold for QGP production
- Strangeness fugacity γ_s reaches unity at top RHIC and LHC energies, confirming full saturation of strange quark density at hadronization
- R_AA(J/ψ) at SPS and RHIC: approximately 0.4, fairly flat across energies from SPS through RHIC
- R_AA(J/ψ) at LHC: approximately 0.8-0.9 for p_T-integrated mid-rapidity production
- R_AA(ψ')/R_AA(J/ψ) ≈ 0.5 at LHC (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV), showing sequential suppression
- Entropy per unit rapidity for Pb+Pb at LHC: dS/dy ≈ 12,500 (from dN_ch/dη ≈ 1600)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from thermal fits: T_c = 156.6 MeV
- QGP formation time choice: τ_ini ≈ 0.6 fm/c for √s_NN ≥ 45 GeV

## Context

This paper extends the authors' own 1996 review (Harris and Müller, 1996) "The Search for the Quark-Gluon Plasma" and assesses progress against the original signature expectations. The work confirms predictions of strangeness saturation from Rafelski (1982) and sequential quarkonium melting from Matsui and Satz (1986), while showing that regeneration of charmonium at LHC energies (Braun-Munzinger, 2000; Thews, 2000) explains the reduced suppression of J/ψ at high energies. The results enable future dynamical modeling of QGP formation and evolution, with Bayesian inference techniques now being applied to extract underlying physics from data.
