# Strongly Interacting Matter at Finite Chemical Potential : Hybrid Model Approach

> Search for a proper and realistic equation of state (EOS) for strongly interacting matter used in the study of the QCD phase diagram still appears as a challenging problem. Recently, we constructed a hybrid model description for the quark gluon plasma (QGP) as well as hadron gas (HG) phases where we used an excluded volume model for HG and a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model for the QGP phase. The hybrid model suitably describes the recent lattice results of various thermodynamical as well as transport properties of the QCD matter at zero baryon chemical potential ($μ_{B}$). In this paper, we extend our investigations further in obtaining the properties of QCD matter at finite value of $μ_{B}$ and compare our results with the most recent results of lattice QCD calculation.

## Key claim

The hybrid model, combining an excluded-volume hadron gas with a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle QGP, reproduces lattice QCD results for thermodynamic quantities at finite baryon chemical potential, but only in specific temperature ranges; at high chemical potential (μB = 400 MeV) the model shows a first-order phase transition discontinuity where lattice results still indicate a crossover, and the model's predictions exceed lattice data at intermediate temperatures.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state using an excluded-volume model for the hadron gas phase (with hard-core baryon radius r = 0.8 fm and mesons treated as ideal gas) and a thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model for the QGP phase (with temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent effective masses for quarks and gluons). They impose net strangeness neutrality and compute isentropic trajectories at fixed S/N values (300, 100, 45, 30) on the T-μB plane, then calculate thermodynamic quantities (p/T⁴, ε/T⁴, s/T³, trace anomaly, c_s²) along these trajectories and at fixed μB = 400 MeV, comparing with lattice results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Hard-core baryon radius: r = 0.8 fm
- Critical point (CP) located at S/N ≈ 60, corresponding to T = 158 MeV and μB ≈ 170 MeV, giving √sNN ≈ 21 GeV
- Isentropic trajectories computed at S/N = 300, 100, 45, and 30, where S/N = 300 corresponds to RHIC energy √sNN = 200 GeV
- At μB = 400 MeV, the model shows a first-order phase transition at T = 160 MeV
- Trace anomaly remains significant up to T = 500 MeV
- Square of speed of sound shows a shallow minimum around T ≈ 160-170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid model (Srivastava and Singh, 2011) from zero baryon chemical potential to finite μB, comparing with lattice results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2012). The model provides an intuitive picture for crossover transitions at high RHIC energies and suggests that baryonic hard-core repulsion is crucially responsible for the existence of a critical point on the phase boundary. The results enable future investigations into the QCD equation of state and the precise location of the critical point, though the model's discontinuity at μB = 400 MeV contrasts with lattice results that still show crossover behavior at that chemical potential.
