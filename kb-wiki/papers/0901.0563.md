# Weak Interaction in Holographic QCD

> We present a recent proposal of a simple prescription for including low-energy weak-interactions into the framework of holographic QCD, based on the standard AdS/CFT dictionary of double-trace deformations. This opens a new perspective on phenomenological applications of holographic QCD: calculating weak observables in the strongly coupled regime of QCD. The idea is general enough to be implemented in any holographic model. Its efficiency and usefulness are demonstrated by performing few exemplar calculations of weak reactions involving mesons and baryons, within the Sakai-Sugimoto and hard/soft wall holographic models.

## Key claim

The authors propose a general prescription for incorporating low-energy weak interactions into any holographic QCD model by treating the effective four-fermi vertices as double-trace deformations of the boundary QCD Lagrangian, which translates into specific boundary conditions for the 5D gauge fields. The claim is that this approach can reproduce known current-algebra results and provide quantitative estimates of coupling constants to about 10-20% accuracy, though the examples shown are limited to the zero-momentum-transfer limit.

## Method

The authors start from the effective Fermi weak interaction Lagrangian, which is quadratic in the SU(2)_L and electromagnetic currents. Using the AdS/CFT dictionary, each current corresponds to a 5D gauge field A_mu(x,r) whose near-boundary behavior has a non-normalizable coefficient set by the functional derivative of the weak interaction with respect to that current, and a normalizable mode corresponding to the current expectation value. The equations of motion come from the chosen holographic QCD model (Sakai-Sugimoto or Hard/Soft Wall), and solving them with these boundary conditions yields the physical current at low energies. For baryons, an effective instanton model calibrated to the nucleon mass is used to describe the nucleon's coupling to the gauge fields.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion decay: the prescription reproduces the current-algebra result with the coupling involving the pion decay constant f_pi, giving the interaction term -2 G_F f_pi (partial_mu pi^-)(bar l_L gamma^mu nu_L)
- Neutron beta-decay in the Sakai-Sugimoto model: predicted axial constant g_A approximately 1.30, compared to the experimental value g_A = 1.2695(29), corresponding to an under-prediction of about 4% in the neutron half-life
- Neutron beta-decay in the hard wall model: the axial constant is given by g_A = (0.33 + 1.02 D), where D is a parameter measured in GeV^{-1}
- Parity non-conserving pion-nucleon interaction: the zero-momentum-transfer coupling is -2 G_F f_pi (bar p gamma^mu n) (partial_mu pi^+), identical to the current-algebra and chiral perturbation theory result

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD framework, previously applied mainly to pure QCD and electromagnetic couplings in models such as the Sakai-Sugimoto model (2005) and Hard/Soft Wall models (2005-2006), to include weak interactions. The prescription is general enough to be implemented in any holographic model and enables calculations of weak observables in the strongly coupled regime of QCD. The approach recovers results from current algebra and chiral perturbation theory at zero momentum transfer, but the authors claim it can provide predictions for finite momentum transfer and for couplings of excited mesons (such as rho, omega) that are beyond the reach of chiral algebra, with results to be reported in future work.
