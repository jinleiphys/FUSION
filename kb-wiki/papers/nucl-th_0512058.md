# Mixed Representation RPA Calculation for Octupole Excitations on Superdeformed Sates in the 40Ca and Neutron-Rich Sulfur Regions

> By means of the mixed representation RPA based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean field, we investigate low-frequency octupole excitations built on the superdeformed (SD) states in the N=Z nuclei around 40Ca and the neutron-rich Sulfur isotopes. The RPA calculation is carried out fully self-consistently on the three-dimensional Cartesian mesh in a box, and yields a number of low-frequency octupole vibrations built on the SD states in 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca and 44Ti. In particular, a strongly collective K^π=1^- octupole vibration is suggested to appear on top of the SD state in 40Ca. For 48,50S close to the neutron drip line, we find that the low-lying state created by the excitation of a single neutron from a loosely bound low Omega level to a high Omega resonance level acquires an extremely strong octupole transition strength due to the spatially very extended structure of the particle-hole wave functions.

# Mixed Representation RPA Calculation for Octupole Excitations on Superdeformed States in the 40Ca and Neutron-Rich Sulfur Regions

## Key claim

The paper suggests that low-frequency octupole vibrations built on superdeformed (SD) states appear in the 40Ca region and neutron-rich Sulfur isotopes, with the prediction that a strongly collective K^π=1^- octupole vibration exists on top of the SD state in 40Ca, and that extremely strong octupole transition strengths arise in 48,50S near the neutron drip line due to spatially extended particle-hole wave functions, though the authors note that the triaxiality of the SD mean field in 40Ca (γ=7°) may cause K-mixing and splitting of modes, and that the peaks above the neutron-emission threshold in 48,50S are associated with discretized non-resonant continuum states and cannot be given definite physical meanings.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh in a box, using the SIII, SkM*, and SLy4 Skyrme interactions, and then solved the random phase approximation (RPA) equations in the mixed representation, where particles above the Fermi surface are described in coordinate representation and holes in the HF single-particle basis. The RPA eigenvalue equations were solved using the conjugate gradient method, with the calculation assuming reflection symmetry about three planes and using a mesh spacing of Δx = 0.6 fm in a box of size 17.4 × 17.4 × 29.4 fm³.

## Key numbers

- For 32S: a K^π=2^- collective mode at 2.6 MeV above the SD band head with isoscalar octupole transition strength of 30 W.u. (1 W.u. ≈ 61 fm⁶ for 32S)
- For 36Ar: a K^π=2^- mode at 3.9 MeV (27 W.u.) and a K^π=1^- mode at 4.0 MeV (25 W.u.) (1 W.u. ≈ 77 fm⁶ for 36Ar)
- For 40Ca: a K^π=1^- mode at about 1.2 MeV with isoscalar octupole transition strength of 29 W.u. (1 W.u. ≈ 95 fm⁶ for 40Ca), and a K^π=0^- mode at about 3.3 MeV with 82 W.u.
- For 44Ti (with SIII interaction, including triaxiality): a "K^π=0^-" collective mode at 2.4 MeV (27 W.u.) and a "K^π=2^-" doublet at 3.0 and 3.5 MeV (24 and 16 W.u., respectively) (1 W.u. ≈ 115 fm⁶ for 44Ti)
- For 36S: a K^π=1^- mode at 2.6 MeV (13 W.u.) and a K^π=2^- mode at 4.2 MeV (16 W.u.) (1 W.u. ≈ 77 fm⁶ for 36S)
- For 48S: a K^π=1^- mode at 0.51 MeV with 31 W.u. (1 W.u. ≈ 137 fm⁶ for 48S), with unperturbed energy 0.65 MeV and unperturbed strength 7.7 W.u.
- For 50S: a K^π=2^- mode at 2.5 MeV with 55 W.u. (1 W.u. ≈ 149 fm⁶ for 50S), with unperturbed excitation energy 3.0 MeV and unperturbed strength 22 W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field calculations by Dudek (1990), Skalski (1992), and Butler (1996) and quasiparticle RPA studies by Mizutori (1991, 1993) that indicated SD nuclei are soft against octupole deformations, and builds on the discovery of SD bands in the 40Ca region by Svensson (2000, 2001) and Ideguchi (2001). The mixed representation RPA approach, developed by Lemmer (1968), Bertsch (1983), and Muta (2002), allows fully self-consistent treatment of deformed nuclei without an upper energy cutoff for particles, overcoming limitations of continuum RPA methods restricted to spherical nuclei. The results enable experimental searches for low-frequency octupole vibrations on known SD 0+ states in the 40Ca region and predict a new SD region in neutron-rich Sulfur isotopes near the neutron drip line, where the extremely strong octupole transitions from loosely bound states to resonance levels could be studied as soft collective modes unique to unstable nuclei.
