# On holographic thermalization and gravitational collapse of tachyonic scalar fields

> In this paper we study the thermalization of a spatially homogeneous system in a strongly coupled CFT. The non-equilibrium initial state is created by switching on a relevant perturbation in the CFT vacuum during Delta t >= t >= -Delta t. Via AdS/CFT, the thermalization process corresponds to the gravitational collapse of a tachyonic scalar field (m^2 = -3) in the Poincare patch of AdS_5. In the limit Delta t < 0.02/T, the thermalization time t_T is found to be quantitatively the same as that of a non-equilibrium state created by a marginal perturbation discussed in Ref. [5]. In the case Delta t >= 1/T, we also obtain double-collapse solutions but with a non-equilibrium intermediate state at t = 0. In all the cases our results show that the system thermalizes in a typical time t_T ~ O(1)/T. Besides, a conserved energy-moment current in the bulk is found, which helps understand the qualitative difference of the collapse process in the Poincare patch from that in global AdS[9, 10].

## Key claim

The thermalization time of a strongly coupled CFT after a relevant perturbation is quantitatively the same as for a marginal perturbation when the source duration is very short (Δt < 0.02/T), and in all cases the system thermalizes in a typical time t_T ~ O(1)/T, regardless of whether the perturbation is marginal or relevant.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Einstein-Klein-Gordon equations for a tachyonic scalar field with m² = -3 in the Poincaré patch of AdS₅, using Schwarzschild-like coordinates. The scalar source at the boundary is a Gaussian pulse φ₀(t) = (ε/a) exp(-a t²), where Δt = 1/√a characterizes the duration of the perturbation. The bulk geometry is reconstructed from the back-reaction of the scalar field, and the thermalization time t_T is defined as the first time when min f(t_T, u) = 0.01, where f is the metric function.

## Key numbers

- For narrow waves with Δt < 0.02/T, the relative difference in the dimensionless thermalization time g_t = t_T T between the tachyonic (m² = -3) and massless scalar cases is less than 0.8%.
- The black hole mass for narrow waves scales as M_bh ∝ ε²/a, specifically M_bh = 4π ε²/(3a) for the tachyonic case with m² = -3.
- For a broad wave with (a, ε) = (0.3, 0.5), the thermalization time is t_T = 1.85 = 0.83/T, with apparent horizon location u₀ = 1.09.
- The perturbative result for the black hole mass M_bh = 4π ε²/(3a) is valid for Δt < 0.3/T.

## Context

This work extends the holographic thermalization studies of a massless scalar field (marginal perturbation) in Ref. [5] (PaperI) to the case of a tachyonic scalar field (relevant perturbation) with m² = -3 in AdS₅. The paper also contrasts the collapse process in the Poincaré patch with that in global AdS studied by Bizon (2001, 2002). The results enable a systematic comparison of thermalization times for different types of perturbations and confirm that the thermalization time is always of order 1/T, independent of whether the perturbation is marginal or relevant.
