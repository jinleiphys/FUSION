# Nucleon-to-Resonance Form Factors at Large Photon Virtualities

> We present a unified description of elastic and transition form factors involving the nucleon and its resonances; in particular, the $N(1440)$, $Δ(1232)$ and $Δ(1600)$. We compare predictions made using a framework built upon a Faddeev equation kernel and interaction vertices that possess QCD-kindred momentum dependence with results obtained using a confining, symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact-interaction in a widely-used leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. This comparison explains that the contact-interaction framework produces hard form factors, curtails some quark orbital angular momentum correlations within a baryon, and suppresses two-loop diagrams in the elastic and transition electromagnetic currents. Such defects are rectified in our QCD-kindred framework and, by contrasting the results obtained for the same observables in both theoretical schemes, shows those objects which are most sensitive to the momentum dependence of elementary quantities in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified description of elastic and transition form factors for the nucleon and its resonances, but the calculations are limited to the "dressed-quark core" of these baryons, explicitly omitting meson-baryon final-state interactions (MB FSIs) that are expected to contribute materially at low photon virtualities (Q² less than about 2 GeV²).

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation using a quark-diquark approximation, where the diquark correlations are non-pointlike and fully dynamical. They compare results from two frameworks: a QCD-kindred approach with momentum-dependent interaction vertices, and a contact-interaction (CI) model in rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The electromagnetic current is constructed from six terms probing quarks and diquarks separately, and form factors are extracted using projection operators.

## Key numbers

- Computed dressed-quark core masses: m_N(940) = 1.19 GeV, m_N(1440) = 1.73 GeV, m_Δ(1232) = 1.35 GeV, m_Δ(1600) = 1.79 GeV
- Light-quark scalar diquark mass m_{0+} = 0.79 GeV; axial-vector diquark mass m_{1+} = 0.89 GeV
- Meson-baryon FSIs reduce the nucleon core mass by approximately 0.2 GeV, the Δ(1232) core mass by approximately 0.16 GeV, and the Roper resonance core mass by 0.3 GeV
- CLAS 12 will deliver resonance electroproduction data out to Q² ≈ 12 GeV²
- Existing CLAS data cover Q² < 6.0 GeV² for most resonances up to 1.8 GeV
- The zero crossing in the Pauli transition form factor F₂* for γ*p → N(1440) is always present, with its precise location depending on meson-cloud estimation
- For γ*p → Δ(1600), empirical results are only available at the real-photon point (Q²=0) for G_M* and G_E*
- Future electro-excitation N → Δ(1600) amplitudes will become available at 2.0 GeV² < Q² < 5.0 GeV²

## Context

This work extends prior Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of baryon structure by Segovia (2014, 2015, 2016), Chen (2017, 2018, 2019), and others, providing a unified treatment of nucleon elastic form factors and transitions to the Roper resonance N(1440), Δ(1232), and Δ(1600). The calculations contradict quark-model predictions from Capstick (2000) and Crede (2013) that placed the first positive-parity excitation above the first negative-parity state. The results enable interpretation of upcoming CLAS 12 data at higher Q², particularly to test whether dynamical chiral symmetry breaking shows environmental sensitivity across different baryon states and whether diquark correlations with different quantum numbers appear in higher excitations.
