# The enigmatic $Δ(1600)$ resonance

> Our recently proposed model of the $Δ(1600)$ resonance, in which the dominant component is a quasi-bound state of the $Δ(1232)$ and the pion, is confronted with a similar model of the $N^*(1440)$ resonance as its counterpart in the P11 partial wave. We stress an essentially different mechanism responsible for generating the two resonances.

# The enigmatic Δ(1600) resonance

## Key claim
The Δ(1600) resonance is primarily a quasi-bound state of the Δ(1232) and the pion, which is a fundamentally different mechanism from that generating the N*(1440) resonance, despite the two resonances appearing as counterparts in the P11 and P33 partial waves. The radially excited three-quark state plays only a minor role in the formation of the Δ(1600), in contrast to the N*(1440) where the three-quark resonant state strongly influences the width.

## Method
The authors use a coupled-channel chiral quark model with πN, πΔ, and σN channels, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the meson amplitude with a separable kernel approximation. The Cloudy Bag Model (CBM) fixes the quark-pion vertices, while the s-wave σ-baryon vertex is introduced phenomenologically with a free coupling constant gσ. The Laurent-Pietarinen expansion extracts S-matrix poles in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers
- For the N*(1440) resonance, starting with bare masses of 1750 MeV and 2000 MeV for the three-quark configuration, both curves end up at the same point with mass and width consistent with PDG values; at g/g0=1 the width corresponds to Im W = 180 MeV.
- For the Δ(1600) in the πN system alone, at R = 0.123 fm the mass and width agree well with PDG values; at R = 0.050 fm the system becomes bound.
- The physically sensible region for the Δ(1600) is around R ≈ 0.8 fm, where the bag radius corresponds to a cutoff energy of approximately 550 MeV.
- The bare mass of the first radial excitation of the Δ(1232) is fixed at 2.2 GeV, approximately 1 GeV above the (1s)³ configuration.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of the N*(1440) resonance (Golli, PRC 2018) by applying a similar coupled-channel approach to the Δ(1600). The results contradict the naive expectation that both resonances arise from the same three-quark configuration mechanism. The model builds on the Chew-Low (1956) conjecture that the Δ(1232) arises from πN attraction, and uses lattice QCD results from Lang (2016) and Kiratidis (2017) that found no sizable three-quark component below about 1.7 GeV. This work enables future studies of the different dynamical origins of low-lying baryon resonances in the P11 and P33 partial waves.
