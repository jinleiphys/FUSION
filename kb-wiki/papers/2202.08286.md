# DIS physics at the EIC and LHeC and connections to the future LHC and $ν$A programs

> Deeply-inelastic scattering (DIS) stands to enter a golden age with the prospect of precision programs at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and Large Hadron-electron Collider (LHeC). While these programs will be of considerable importance to resolving longstanding issues in (non)perturbative QCD as well as hadronic and nuclear structure, they will also have valuable implications for a wider range of physics at the Energy and Intensity Frontiers, including at the High-Luminosity LHC (HL-LHC) and future $νA$ facilities. In this plenary contribution, we highlight a number of salient examples of the potential HEP impact from the complementary EIC and LHeC programs drawn from their respective Yellow Report and Whitepaper. Interested readers are encouraged to consult the extensive studies and literature from which these examples are taken for more detail.

## Key claim
The EIC and LHeC DIS programs will have valuable implications for a wider range of physics at the Energy and Intensity Frontiers, including at the High-Luminosity LHC and future neutrino-nucleus facilities, but the paper is a plenary overview drawing from existing Yellow Report and Whitepaper studies rather than presenting new original results.

## Method
The paper reviews the projected kinematical coverage and physics capabilities of the EIC and LHeC programs, comparing their complementary regions in the (x, Q^2) plane. It summarizes projected PDF uncertainty reductions from EIC pseudodata (100 fb^-1) and LHeC pseudodata, and illustrates the downstream impact on Higgs production cross sections, W-mass determinations, sin^2θ_W extractions, and strong coupling α_s constraints using the CT18 NNLO PDF framework and the PDFSense methodology.

## Key numbers
- EIC will achieve center-of-mass energies 20 ≤ √s ≤ 140 GeV with electron energy E_e ≤ 18 GeV and proton energy E_p ≤ 275 GeV
- EIC integrated luminosity ∫dt ℒ ∼ 1 ab^-1, with pseudodata sets assuming 100 fb^-1
- EIC can reduce u_v PDF uncertainty by a factor of ∼5 at x ∼ 0.8 at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- EIC can improve α_s(M_Z) precision by ∼40% over CT18 NNLO uncertainty
- LHeC will achieve √s = 1.2, 1.3 TeV with E_e = 50, 60 GeV and E_p = 7 TeV
- LHeC integrated luminosity ∫dt ℒ ∼ 1-2 ab^-1
- LHeC can reduce W-mass PDF uncertainty to Δm_W = ±1.6 MeV, compared to Δm_W = ±9 MeV in contemporary ATLAS-based determinations
- LHeC can probe x ≥ 5 × 10^-5 and Q^2 ∼ 10^5-6 GeV^2

## Context
This paper extends the discussions from the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek 2021) and LHeC Whitepaper (LHeC 2020) by connecting their projected DIS capabilities to the HL-LHC program and future neutrino facilities like DUNE. It highlights complementarity with fixed-target Drell-Yan measurements from SeaQuest (E906) and with ongoing work in the CT18 NNLO PDF framework (Hou 2019). The paper enables future coordinated efforts within the Snowmass 2021 exercises to incorporate DIS knowledge into simulations, event-generator calculations, and detector design.
