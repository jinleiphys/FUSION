# Nuclear pairing: basic phenomena revisited

> I review the phenomena associated with pairing in nuclear physics, most prominently the ubiquitous presence of odd-even mass differences and the properties of the excitation spectra, very different for even-even and odd-A nuclei. There are also significant dynamical effects of pairing, visible in the inertias associated with nuclear rotation and large-amplitude shape deformation.

# Nuclear pairing: basic phenomena revisited

## Key claim

The paper reviews pairing phenomena in nuclear physics, arguing that while odd-even mass differences and excitation spectra differences between even-even and odd-A nuclei are the most prominent signatures of pairing, the dynamical effects of pairing, particularly on rotational inertia and large-amplitude shape deformation, are also significant and often underestimated in simple BCS treatments.

## Method

The author compiles experimental binding energies from the 2003 mass table (Audi and Wapstra) and excitation spectra from nuclear databases to extract neutron and proton pairing gaps using the three-point formula Δ⁽³⁾. The systematics of these gaps are fitted to simple parameterizations (constant, c/A¹/², and c₁/A + c₂), and the results are compared with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using Skyrme energy functionals and contact pairing interactions. The paper also examines rotational inertia in deformed nuclei via the R₄₂ ratio and the first 2⁺ excitation energies, and discusses large-amplitude collective motion in fission and exotic decay.

## Key numbers

- Odd-neutron pairing gaps average about 1 MeV, with a constant fit giving 1.04 ± 0.31 MeV (443 data points); even-neutron gaps average 1.32 ± 0.42 MeV (442 data points)
- Odd-proton gaps fit to 0.96 ± 0.28 MeV (418 data points); even-proton gaps fit to 1.64 ± 0.46 MeV (407 data points)
- The c/A¹/² fit for neutrons gives 12/A¹/² ± 0.25 MeV (odd) and 12/A¹/² ± 0.28 MeV (even)
- The c₁/A + c₂ fit for odd neutrons gives 24/A + 0.82 ± 0.27 MeV; for even neutrons gives 41/A + 0.94 ± 0.31 MeV
- The Fermi gas single-particle level density estimate is approximately A/100 MeV⁻¹; the observed average first-excited-state spacing in odd-A nuclei is about a factor of 10 smaller
- In deformed nuclei, experimental 2⁺ excitation energies are systematically higher than the rigid-rotor prediction by a factor of about 2, implying moments of inertia about half the rigid value
- The HFB calculation reproduces deformed 2⁺ energies with rms errors of only ±10%
- The pairing enhancement factor for configuration mixing under the barrier is 2Δ²/G², which can be an order of magnitude or more
- Observed lifetimes for ²³⁴U decay channels range over 12 orders of magnitude; theory including the enhancement factor reproduces them to within one or two orders of magnitude

## Context

This review extends the classic understanding of nuclear pairing from early BCS applications (Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines, 1958) and the Grodzins systematics (1962) for quadrupole collectivity. It contrasts with the simpler constant-gap picture by showing systematic mass-number dependence and shell effects at magic numbers N=28, 50, 82, 126, and N=14. The paper enables future work on time-dependent HFB theory for fission dynamics and on the role of induced interactions from low-frequency phonons, as estimated by Brink and collaborators (2005). The discussion of spin-triplet pairing connects to the Wigner energy along the N=Z line (Lunney, 2003).
