# The decays omega(782), phi(1020) to 5 pi in the hidden local symmetry approach

> The decays omega -> 2pi^+ 2pi^- pi^0 and omega -> pi^+ pi^- 3pi^0 are reconsidered in the hidden local symmetry approach (HLS) added with the anomalous terms. The decay amplitudes are analyzed in detail, paying the special attention to the Adler condition of vanishing the whole amplitude at vanishing momentum of any final pion. Combining the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule applied to the five pion final state, with the Adler condition, we calculate also the phi -> 2pi^+ 2pi^-pi^0 and phi -> pi^+ pi^- 3pi^0 decay amplitudes. The partial widths of the above decays are evaluated, and the excitation curves in e^+e^- annihilation are obtained, assuming reasonable particular relations among the parameters characterizing the anomalous terms of the HLS Lagrangian. The evaluated branching ratios B(phi -> pi^+ pi^- 3pi^0)~2x10^{-7} and B(phi -> 2pi^+ 2pi^- pi^0)~7x10^{-7} are such that with the luminosity L=500 pb^{-1} attained at DAFNE phi factory, one may already possess about 1685 events of the decays phi -> 5pi.

## Key claim

The branching ratios for omega(782) and phi(1020) decays to five pions are evaluated in the hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach, assuming specific relations among the arbitrary constants c1, c2, c3 that characterize the anomalous terms of the HLS Lagrangian, namely c1 = c3, c2 = 0, and a = 2, which satisfy both the condition of no point-like omega to three pion amplitude and the condition c1 + c2 - c3 = 0.

## Method

The decay amplitudes for omega to pi+ pi- 3pi0 and omega to 2pi+ 2pi- pi0 were constructed from the nonanomalous and anomalous parts of the HLS Lagrangian, including contributions from diagrams with intermediate rho meson decays to four pions, pion pole terms, and contact terms. The Adler condition (vanishing of the full amplitude when any final pion momentum goes to zero) was verified for both decay channels as a control of the calculation. For phi decays, the amplitudes were obtained by combining the OZI rule applied to the five pion final state with the Adler condition, using an effective Lagrangian approach.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio B(omega -> pi+ pi- 3pi0) at the omega pole mass: 3.6 x 10^{-9}
* Branching ratio B(omega -> 2pi+ 2pi- pi0) at the omega pole mass: 3.5 x 10^{-9}
* Branching ratio B(phi -> pi+ pi- 3pi0): approximately 2 x 10^{-7}
* Branching ratio B(phi -> 2pi+ 2pi- pi0): approximately 7 x 10^{-7}
* With luminosity L = 500 pb^{-1} at the DAFNE phi factory, about 1685 events of phi -> 5pi decays are expected
* The rho-pi-pi coupling constant g_{rho-pi-pi} = 5.9 from the KSRF relation, or g = g_{rho-pi-pi} = 6.00 +/- 0.01 assuming a = 2
* The constant c3 extracted from the omega to 3pi partial width: c3 = 0.99 +/- 0.01

## Context

This work extends previous evaluations by Achasov (2000) of omega to 5pi branching ratios, finding values more than three times larger due to the inclusion of non-resonant diagrams (from diagrams Fig. 3, 4, 8, 9) that were previously neglected; these non-resonant contributions amount to 13-14% of the total but are almost in phase with the resonant contributions, leading to constructive interference. The results enable experimental searches for these rare decays at the DAFNE phi factory, where the predicted phi to 5pi branching ratios of order 10^{-7} should yield observable event rates. The HLS approach relates contributions that would otherwise appear unrelated, and the specific parameter choices (c1 = c3, c2 = 0) are consistent with older chiral models by Schechter that predicted c3 = 1.
