# Coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions

> We have investigated the neutrino induced coherent pion production reaction at low and intermediate energies including pion, nucleon and Delta(1232) resonance. Medium effects in the production mechanism and the distortion of the pion wave function are included in the calculation. We find a strong reduction of the cross section due to these effects and also substantial modifications in the energy distributions of the final pion. The dependence on the atomic number is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects (density-dependent Delta selfenergy and pion distortion via a microscopic optical potential) cause a strong reduction of the coherent pion production cross section and substantial modifications in the final pion energy distributions, with the reduction being around 35% from Delta medium modifications alone, and further suppression from pion distortion.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic amplitude for neutrino-induced coherent pion production on isospin-symmetric nuclei, including direct and crossed Delta(1232) excitation as well as nucleon-pole terms, with the nuclear current obtained as a coherent sum over all nucleons in a local Fermi gas. Medium effects are incorporated by adding a density-dependent selfenergy to the Delta propagator and by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a microscopic optical potential based on the Delta-hole model for pion distortion.

## Key numbers

- The Delta medium modification reduces the cross section by around 35%.
- The eikonal approximation for pion distortion fails at pion momenta below 400 MeV/c.
- The dominant direct Delta excitation mechanism accounts for most of the cross section at neutrino energy around 1 GeV.
- The cross section is proportional to the square of the axial form factor C5^A(0), taken as 1.2 in this work.
- The amplitude is proportional to an effective number of participants P = Z + N/3 (where Z and N are proton and neutron numbers), but the full model shows a quenched dependence on P due to pion absorption and nuclear form factor effects.

## Context

This work extends and improves earlier calculations by Kelkar et al. (1996) and Singh et al. (2006) by including a more complete set of hadronic mechanisms and a realistic treatment of nuclear medium effects. The study is motivated by the K2K experiment (Hasegawa, 2005) which found a deficit of forward muons at average neutrino energy 1.3 GeV, suggesting overestimation of charged-current coherent pion production, and by the need to understand neutral-current coherent pion production as a background for neutrino oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE. The results enable more accurate predictions for current and future neutrino-nucleus experiments at low and intermediate energies.
