# Improved Constraints on Sterile Neutrinos in the MeV to GeV Mass Range

> Improved upper bounds are presented on the coupling $|U_{e4}|^2$ of an electron to a sterile neutrino $ν_4$ from analyses of data on nuclear and particle decays, including superallowed nuclear beta decays, the ratios $R^{(π)}_{e/μ}=BR(π^+ \to e^+ ν_e)/BR(π^+ \to μ^+ ν_μ)$, $R^{(K)}_{e/μ}$, $R^{(D_s)}_{e/τ}$, and $B^+_{e 2}$ decay, covering the mass range from MeV to GeV.

# Improved Constraints on Sterile Neutrinos in the MeV to GeV Mass Range

## Key claim
Improved upper bounds on the coupling $|U_{e4}|^2$ of an electron to a sterile neutrino $\nu_4$ are derived from analyses of nuclear and particle decay data, covering the mass range from 0.5 MeV to approximately 1 GeV. These bounds are purely kinematic and do not rely on model-dependent assumptions about visible neutrino decay modes, making them complementary to constraints from neutrino decay searches.

## Method
The authors analyze superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ nuclear beta decays by using the mutual consistency of corrected ${\cal F}t$ values to derive upper limits on $|U_{e4}|^2$ for $\nu_4$ masses up to 9.4 MeV. They also analyze ratios of leptonic decay branching ratios $R^{(M)}_{e/\mu}$ for $\pi^+$, $K^+$, $D_s^+$, and $B^+$ mesons, comparing experimental measurements to Standard Model predictions to extract bounds on $|U_{e4}|^2$ in both the kinematically allowed and forbidden regions for $\nu_4$ emission. For $B^+$ decays, direct peak-search limits on $BR(B^+ \to e^+ \nu_4)$ are used.

## Key numbers
- $|U_{e4}|^2 < 4 \times 10^{-4}$ for $\nu_4$ masses in the range $0.5\ {\rm MeV} \lesssim m_{\nu_4} < 9.4$ MeV, derived from superallowed beta decay data using Hardy and Towner (2018) analysis (90% C.L.)
- $|U_{e4}|^2 < 2.7 \times 10^{-4}$ for the same mass range, derived using Ramsey-Musolf analysis (90% C.L.)
- For $m_{\nu_4}=30$ MeV, the upper bound is $|U_{e4}|^2 < 0.8 \times 10^{-6}$ from the PIENU analysis of $R^{(\pi)}_{e/\mu}$
- For $m_{\nu_4} > 139$ MeV (pion forbidden region), the flat upper bound from PIENU-H is $|U_{e4}|^2 < 1.4 \times 10^{-3}$
- For $m_{\nu_4} > 493$ MeV (kaon forbidden region), the flat upper bound from KENU-H is $|U_{e4}|^2 < 1.0 \times 10^{-2}$
- From $D_s^+ \to e^+ \nu_4$ analysis, $\bar\rho(\delta_e^{(D_s)},\delta^{(D_s)}_{\nu_4})$ ranges from $1.37 \times 10^5$ at $m_{\nu_4}=0.191$ GeV to $1.83 \times 10^6$ at $m_{\nu_4}=0.85$ GeV
- From $B^+ \to e^+ \nu_4$ peak search, the upper limit on $BR(B^+ \to e^+ \nu_4)$ is $2.5 \times 10^{-6}$ for $m_{\nu_4}$ from 0.1 GeV to 1.4 GeV, increasing to $7 \times 10^{-6}$ for $m_{\nu_4}$ from 1.4 GeV to 1.8 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous bounds from kink searches in Kurie plots by Deutsch (1990) and from superallowed beta decay analyses by Hardy and Towner (1990), improving them by factors of 5-10 using updated $|V_{ud}|$ determinations. The analysis of meson decay ratios builds on the theoretical framework of Shrock (1980, 1981) and uses recent experimental results from PIENU (2015), NA62 (2018), CLEO, BABAR, Belle, and BES III. The bounds are complementary to cosmological constraints and to searches for visible neutrino decays such as PS191, and the authors identify new peak-search experiments for $D_s^+ \to e^+ \nu_4$ and $D^+ \to e^+ \nu_4$ at BES III and $B^+ \to e^+ \nu_4$ at Belle II as promising avenues for further improvement.
