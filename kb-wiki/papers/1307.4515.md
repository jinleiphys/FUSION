# Centrality dependence of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV

> We have used the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence model to investigate the centrality dependence of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production based on the $6.2\times 10^7$ hadronic final states generated by the PACIAE model in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=200$ GeV in $|y| <1$ and $p_T<5$ acceptances. It turned out that the yields of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton strongly depend on the centrality, i.e. their yields decrease rapidly with the increase of centrality bins; but their yield ratios are independent on centrality. These theoretical results are consistent with the STAR and PHENIX data. Furthermore, centrality distribution of $d$ ($\bar d$), $^3{He}$ ($^3{\bar{He}}$) and $_{\barΛ}^3H$ ($\bar{_{\barΛ}^3H}$) follows Gaussian distributions. This means that light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton are primarily produced in the central collisions.

## Key claim

The yields of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV depend strongly on centrality, decreasing rapidly as centrality increases, but their yield ratios remain independent of centrality; the centrality distributions of these particles follow Gaussian shapes, indicating they are primarily produced in central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to generate 6.2 × 10^7 hadronic final states for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with |y| < 1 and p_T < 5 GeV/c acceptance. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase space coalescence (DCPC) model to these final states to reconstruct light (anti)nuclei (deuteron, anti-deuteron, helium-3, anti-helium-3) and (anti)hypertriton by requiring that constituent particles (p, n, Λ) lie within a spatial radius R_0 and invariant mass window Δm of the cluster. The PACIAE model parameters (K factor, parj(1), parj(2), parj(3)) were tuned to reproduce STAR data on Λ, anti-Λ, Ξ⁻, and anti-Ξ⁻ yields across centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- 6.2 × 10^7 hadronic final states were generated by the PACIAE model for the analysis.
- The anti-deuteron to deuteron yield ratio is 0.402 in 0-5% centrality and 0.405 in 0-100% centrality, compared to STAR values of 0.394 (0-12%) and 0.428 (0-80%).
- The anti-helium-3 to helium-3 yield ratio is 0.418 in 0-5% centrality and 0.419 in 0-100% centrality, compared to a STAR mixed-centrality value of 0.45 ± 0.18 ± 0.07.
- The anti-hypertriton to hypertriton yield ratio is 0.451 in 0-5% centrality and 0.507 in 0-100% centrality, compared to a STAR mixed-centrality value of 0.49 ± 0.18 ± 0.07.
- The hypertriton to helium-3 yield ratio is 0.756 in 0-5% centrality and 0.677 in 0-100% centrality.
- The 3σ width of the Gaussian centrality distribution is (44.7 ± 1.2)% for deuterons, (47.7 ± 1.2)% for anti-deuterons, (35.1 ± 1.2)% for helium-3, (37.2 ± 0.9)% for anti-helium-3, (34.2 ± 1.2)% for hypertriton, and (36.6 ± 0.9)% for anti-hypertriton.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PACIAE+DCPC studies of light nuclei production in pp collisions at 7 TeV and in 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (Chen et al., 2013) by systematically investigating the centrality dependence across multiple bins (0-5%, 0-12%, 0-30%, 0-50%, 0-80%, 0-100%). It addresses a gap noted in prior experimental analyses by STAR (2009, 2010) and PHENIX (2005), which used event samples with different centrality selections, and theoretical studies by Ma et al. and Steinheimer et al. that focused only on most central collisions. The results enable direct comparison between theoretical calculations and experimental data across different centrality bins by introducing a normalized yield that converts yields in any centrality bin to the minimum-bias equivalent.
