# Kaon condensation and composition of neutron star matter in modified quark-meson coupling model

> We use the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model to study the composition profile of neutron star matter and compare the results with those calculated by quantum hadrodynamics (QHD). Both MQMC and QHD model parameters are adjusted to produce exactly the same saturation properties so that we can investigate the model dependences of the matter composition at high densities. We consider the possibility of deep kaon optical potential and find that the composition of matter is very sensitive to the interaction strength of kaons with matter. The onset densities of the kaon condensation are studied in detail by varying the kaon optical potentials. We find that the MQMC model produces the kaon condensation at lower densities than QHD. The presence of kaon condensation changes drastically the population of octet baryons and leptons. Once the kaon condensation takes place, the population of kaons builds up very quickly, and kaons become the dominant component of the matter. We find that the $ω$-meson plays an important role in increasing the kaon population and suppressing the hyperon population.

## Key claim

The composition of neutron star matter, particularly the onset density of kaon condensation, is highly sensitive to the kaon optical potential depth and shows significant model dependence between the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model and quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), even when both models are calibrated to identical saturation properties.

## Method

The authors employed the MQMC model, treating octet baryons as MIT bags with density-dependent bag constants, and compared results with a QHD model where baryons are point particles. Both models were calibrated to produce identical saturation properties (density 0.17 fm^-3, binding energy 16 MeV, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, effective nucleon mass ratio 0.78, and compression modulus 285.5 MeV). The kaon optical potential U_K- at saturation density was varied from -80 MeV to -160 MeV to determine the coupling constant g_sigma K, and the composition of neutron star matter was computed under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3, with binding energy E_b/A = 16 MeV and symmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass ratio at saturation: m*_N/m_N = 0.78, compression modulus K = 285.5 MeV
- For U_K- = -120 MeV: onset density rho_crit = 5.9 rho_0 (MQMC) vs 9.8 rho_0 (QHD)
- For U_K- = -140 MeV: onset density rho_crit = 3.8 rho_0 (MQMC) vs 4.3 rho_0 (QHD)
- For U_K- = -160 MeV: onset density rho_crit = 3.0 rho_0 (MQMC) vs 3.3 rho_0 (QHD)
- Bag radius for all octet baryons: R_0 = 0.6 fm; bare quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- Quark-meson coupling constants in MQMC: g_sigma^q = 1.0, g_omega^q = 2.71, g_rho^q = 7.88; g'_sigma^B = 2.27

## Context

This work extends prior studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Glendenning 1997, Knorren et al. 1995, Banik and Bandyopadhyay 2001) by systematically comparing the MQMC model with QHD under identical saturation conditions. The results show that the MQMC model predicts kaon condensation at lower densities than QHD, and that the omega meson plays a crucial role in rapidly increasing kaon population while suppressing hyperons. The findings are relevant to interpreting neutron star mass observations (around 1.5 solar masses from binary pulsar data, with possible heavier pulsars in the 1.8-2.0 solar mass range) and to understanding the role of exotic degrees of freedom in dense matter. The work also connects to experimental searches for deeply bound kaonic nuclei (Akaishi and Yamazaki 2002, KEK experiments, FINUDA collaboration).
