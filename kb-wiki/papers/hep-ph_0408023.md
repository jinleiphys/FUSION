# Nuclear parton distribution functions and their effects on sin^2 theta_W anomaly

> Nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) are investigated by analyzing the data on structure functions F_2^A and Drell-Yan cross sections sigma_{DY}^{pA}. An important point of this analysis is to show uncertainties of the NPDFs by the Hessian method. The analysis indicates that the uncertainties are large for antiquark distributions at x>0.2 and gluon distributions in the whole x region. We also discuss a nuclear effect on the NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly as an application.

## Key claim

The NuTeV anomaly in the weak mixing angle (sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 ± 0.0009 versus the global value 0.2227 ± 0.0004) might be explained by nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions, specifically the difference between u_v and d_v nuclear corrections, but the authors' preliminary results show large uncertainties that prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed a χ² analysis of 951 data points on nuclear structure functions F₂^A and Drell-Yan cross sections σ_DY^{pA} for 17 nuclei from deuteron to lead. Nuclear parton distribution functions were parameterized as nucleonic PDFs multiplied by weight functions w_i(x,A,Z) with a cubic polynomial in x and an A-dependent factor, evolved at leading order from Q₀² = 1 GeV² to experimental Q² values. Uncertainties were estimated using the Hessian method with Δχ² = 10.427 for nine parameters, corresponding to a one-sigma confidence region.

## Key numbers

- Total data points analyzed: 951, with minimum χ² = 1489.8
- NuTeV sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), compared to global value 0.2227 ± 0.0004
- Initial Q² for PDF evolution: Q₀² = 1 GeV²
- Δχ² = 10.427 used for uncertainty estimation with nine parameters
- Nuclei analyzed: D, ⁴He, Li, Be, C, N, Al, Ca, Fe, Cu, Kr, Ag, Sn, Xe, W, Au, Pb (A = 2 to approximately 208)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier 2001 NPDF analysis (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai, 2001) by adding Drell-Yan and HERMES data, including charm-quark distributions, and providing the first serious uncertainty estimate for nuclear PDFs using the Hessian method. The analysis enables quantitative studies of nuclear corrections in neutrino scattering, particularly for the NuTeV sin²θ_W anomaly, and identifies the need for new data to constrain antiquark distributions at x > 0.2 and gluon distributions across all x.
