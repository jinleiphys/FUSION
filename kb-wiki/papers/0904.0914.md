# On the density dependence of single-proton and two-proton knockout reactions under quasifree conditions

> We consider high-energy quasifree single- and two-proton knockout reactions induced by electrons and protons and address the question what target-nucleus densities can be effectively probed after correcting for nuclear attenuation (initial- and final-state interactions). Our calculations refer to ejected proton kinetic energies of 1.5 GeV, the reactions (e,e'p), (γ,pp) and (p,2p) and a carbon target. It is shown that each of the three reactions is characterized by a distinctive sensitivity to the density of the target nucleus. The bulk of the (γ,pp) strength stems from the high-density regions in the deep nuclear interior. Despite the strong attenuation, sizable densities can be probed by (p,2p) provided that the energy resolution allows one to pick nucleons from s orbits. The effective mean densities that can be probed in high-energy (e,e'p) are of the order of 30-50% of the nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper shows that each of the three quasifree proton knockout reactions, (e,e'p), (γ,pp), and (p,2p), probes a distinctively different range of target-nucleus densities after accounting for nuclear attenuation, but the abstract's claim that (p,2p) can probe "sizable densities" is conditional on the energy resolution being sufficient to select nucleons from s orbits specifically.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) to compute the distorted momentum distributions for single-proton and two-proton knockout from a carbon-12 target at an ejected proton kinetic energy of 1.5 GeV. They introduced a function δ(r,θ) that encodes the contribution from each infinitesimal radial and angular interval to the cross section, and defined an average effective density ρ̅ by weighting the target density with this δ function. Calculations were performed for (e,e'p), (γ,pp), and (p,2p) reactions under kinematic conditions tuned to probe the maxima of the undisturbed momentum distributions for various orbital combinations.

## Key numbers

- For (e,e'p) from the s1/2 orbit, the average effective density probed with RMSGA is 0.086 fm⁻³; for the p3/2 orbit it is 0.038 fm⁻³.
- For (p,2p) from the s1/2 orbit, the average effective density probed with RMSGA is 0.055 fm⁻³; for the p3/2 orbit it is 0.025 fm⁻³.
- For (e,e'pp) from the (s1/2-s1/2) orbits, the average effective density probed with RMSGA is 0.135 fm⁻³; for (p3/2-p3/2) it is 0.075 fm⁻³; for (s1/2-p3/2) it is 0.098 fm⁻³.
- The nuclear saturation density ρ₀ is taken as 0.17 fm⁻³; the (p,2p) reaction from the s1/2 orbit probes an effective mean density of approximately 0.33 ρ₀.
- The effective mean densities probed in high-energy (e,e'p) are of the order of 30-50% of the nuclear saturation density.
- The proton root-mean-square radius in carbon-12 from elastic electron scattering is 2.464 ± 0.012 fm.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) framework previously developed by Ryckebusch (2003) to systematically compare the density sensitivity of electromagnetic and hadronic knockout reactions. The (p,2p) results for carbon at 1 GeV incoming protons agree with the DWIA calculations of PhysRevC.69.024604. The findings are relevant for ongoing and planned experiments at high-energy radioactive beam facilities (Aumann 2007, Kobayashi 2008) that aim to study the equation of state for nuclei far from equilibrium, and for searches of medium modifications of nucleon properties at small distance scales.
