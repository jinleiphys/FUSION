# Dynamical correlations across momentum scales in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Experimental probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) generated in heavy-ion collisions span a broad range in momentum scale: low transverse momentum (low $p_T$) measurements probe collective dynamics, while high $p_T$ measurements probe the response to QGP excitation by jets (jet quenching). However, the dynamical interplay between QGP collective dynamics and jet quenching is currently poorly understood. We present a new framework for exploring dynamical correlations across momentum scales in heavy-ion collisions, based on the $p_T$-differential radial-flow observable $v_0(p_T)$. Measured $v_0(p_T)$ phenomenology is traced to the evolution in strength and coherence of distinct underlying fluctuation modes. We then propose new experimental observables to quantify this evolution. The eigenvalue ratio $λ_2/λ_1$ of the reference-aligned covariance matrix $V_0$ is shown to measure the effective fluctuation rank, while the $p_T$-dependence of the corresponding eigenvectors maps the evolution from a single coherent soft mode to multi-mode dynamics including coalescence and jet quenching. These observables map the soft-mid-hard correlation structure and provide a unified description of the collective-to-partonic transition in the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the rise-plateau-downturn structure of the radial-flow observable \(v_0(p_T)\) in heavy-ion collisions can be traced to a sequence of coherent soft, partially coherent coalescence, and anti-correlated hard fluctuation modes, and that the eigenvalue ratio \(\lambda_2/\lambda_1\) of the reference-aligned covariance matrix serves as an experimentally accessible "order parameter" for the effective number of active fluctuation modes. The hedge is that this framework is demonstrated using a simplified three-component model that reproduces LHC data, but the dynamical origin of the observed \(v_0(p_T)\) features had previously remained unclear.

## Method

The authors model the inclusive transverse-momentum spectrum (\(p_T < 15\) GeV) as a superposition of three sectors: a soft blast-wave baseline, an intermediate coalescence bridge using an effective quark kernel, and a hard fragmentation tail described by a quenched power law. Event-by-event fluctuations are introduced by correlated variations of the soft and mid-\(p_T\) parameters drawn from a four-dimensional multivariate normal distribution, with two deterministic schemes (amplitude modulation and slope modulation) coupling the hard yield to the soft background. The reference-aligned covariance matrix \(V_0(p_{T1},p_{T2})\) is then constructed from projected fluctuations \(\delta n(p_T)\,\delta[p_T]_{\rm ref}\), and its eigen-decomposition is used to compute the factorization ratio \(r(p_{T1},p_{T2})\) and the eigenvalue ratio \(\lambda_2/\lambda_1\).

## Key numbers

* Soft sector parameters: kinetic temperature \(T_{\rm kin} = 0.094\) GeV, surface velocity \(\beta_s = 0.897\), profile exponent \(n = 0.739\), with dispersions \(\sigma_{\beta_s} = 0.012\) and \(\sigma_{T_{\rm kin}} = 0.002\) GeV, and intra-sector correlation \(\rho_{\beta T}^{\rm (soft)} = -0.5\).
* Mid (coalescence) sector parameters: quark slope \(T_q = 0.36\) GeV, flow exponent \(\eta = 1.8\), reference velocity \(\beta_{\rm ref} = 0.65\), tail scale \(p_s = 4.7\) GeV, tail exponent \(\delta = 1.2\), with dispersions \(\sigma_{\beta_{\rm mid}} = 0.04\) and \(\sigma_{T_q} = 0.03\) GeV, intra-sector correlation \(\rho_{\beta T}^{\rm (mid)} = -0.55\), and inter-sector correlation \(\rho_{\rm sm} = 0.5\).
* Hard sector parameters: scale \(p_0 = 2.0\) GeV, power index \(n_0 = 5.8\), slope fluctuation width \(\sigma_n = 0.1\), velocity coupling weight \(w_\beta = 2.0\), temperature coupling weight \(w_T = 0.0\).
* For soft-only fluctuations, the eigenvalue ratio \(\lambda_2/\lambda_1 \simeq 0.01\) for all particle species; including mid-\(p_T\) fluctuations increases \(\lambda_2/\lambda_1\) fourfold to \(\sim 0.04\); including hard-sector fluctuations increases \(\lambda_2/\lambda_1\) to \(\sim 0.1\).
* The analysis focuses on Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV for the 30-40% centrality class, with current measurements extending to \(p_T \sim 10\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the radial-flow observable \(v_0(p_T)\) introduced by Gardim (2019) and Schenke (2020), and responds to recent LHC measurements by ATLAS (2025) and ALICE (2025) that revealed the rise-plateau-downturn structure and species hierarchy. The framework provides a unified description of the collective-to-partonic transition in the QGP, connecting soft hydrodynamic calculations (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) with jet quenching studies (Cunqueiro 2021, Apolinario 2022, Wang 2025) and coalescence models (Fries 2003, Greco 2003, Molnar 2003). The proposed eigenmode analysis of the covariance matrix enables systematic measurements of soft-mid-hard correlations across collision systems and beam energies, including potential application to RHIC Beam Energy Scan data.
