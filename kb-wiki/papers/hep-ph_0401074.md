# Threshold production of the $Θ^+$ in a polarized proton reaction

> We compute cross sections of $Θ^+$ production near threshold for a polarized proton reaction, $\vec p \vec p \to Σ^+ Θ^+$ which was recently proposed to determine unambiguously the parity of $Θ^+$. Within theoretical uncertainties cross sections for the allowed spin configuration are estimated; it is of order of one microbarn for the positive parity $Θ^+$ and about 1/10 microbarn for the negative parity $Θ^+$ in the threshold energy region where the s-wave component dominates.

## Key claim
The cross section for the polarized proton reaction \(\vec p \vec p \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+\) near threshold is estimated to be of order 1 microbarn for positive parity \(\Theta^+\) and about 0.1 microbarn for negative parity \(\Theta^+\), but these values carry theoretical uncertainties from unknown coupling constants and form-factor choices, and the s-wave dominance that underpins the parity-determination selection rule is verified only up to \(\sqrt{s} \lesssim 2750\) MeV.

## Method
The authors computed Born diagrams for pseudoscalar kaon (\(K\)) and vector \(K^*\) exchange, using effective interaction Lagrangians with \(\gamma_5\) structure depending on the assumed parity of \(\Theta^+\). Coupling constants for the \(\Sigma^+\) vertex were taken from previous analyses (\(g_{KN\Sigma}=3.54\), \(g_{K^*N\Sigma}=-2.46\), \(g^{T}_{K^*N\Sigma}=1.15\)), while the unknown \(\Theta^+\) couplings were varied: \(g_{KN\Theta}=3.78\) (fixed by \(\Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to KN}=15\) MeV), \(|g_{K^*N\Theta}| = |g_{KN\Theta}|/2\), and tensor couplings varied within \(|g_{K^*N\Theta}^T| \leq 2|g_{K^*N\Theta}|\). A monopole form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV was applied, and total and differential cross sections were evaluated near the threshold energy \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm th} = 2729.4\) MeV.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for positive parity \(\Theta^+\) (allowed spin \(S=0\)): order of 1 microbarn near threshold.
- Total cross section for negative parity \(\Theta^+\) (allowed spin \(S=1\)): order of 0.1 microbarn near threshold.
- Threshold energy \(\sqrt{s}_{\rm th} = 2729.4\) MeV.
- Coupling constants used: \(g_{KN\Sigma} = 3.54\), \(g_{K^*N\Sigma} = -2.46\), \(g^{T}_{K^*N\Sigma} = 1.15\), \(g_{KN\Theta} = 3.78\) (from \(\Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to KN} = 15\) MeV).
- Form factor cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV.
- Allowed channel cross sections scale as \((s - s_{\rm th})^{1/2}\) (s-wave); forbidden channels scale as \((s - s_{\rm th})^{3/2}\) (p-wave) with suppression factor \(\sim k/m_K \sim 0.1\).

## Context
This work extends the parity-determination method proposed by Thomas (2003) for the reaction \(\vec p \vec p \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+\), which relies on s-wave dominance and Fermi statistics to link the initial proton spin state to the \(\Theta^+\) parity. The calculations confirm that s-wave dominance holds up to \(\sqrt{s} \approx 2750\) MeV and provide cross-section estimates needed to design experiments. The results also connect to earlier photoproduction and hadron-induced reaction studies (e.g., by the same authors) and to the spin-correlation observable \(A_{xx}\) discussed by Hanhart (2003). The small cross sections and forward-peaked kinematics pose experimental challenges for fixed-target facilities such as COSY.
