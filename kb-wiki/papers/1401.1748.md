# Testing the Perey Effect

> Background: The effects of non-local potentials have historically been approximately included by applying a correction factor to the solution of the corresponding equation for the local equivalent interaction. This is usually referred to as the Perey correction factor. Purpose: In this work we investigate the validity of the Perey correction factor for single-channel bound and scattering states, as well as in transfer (p,d) cross sections. Method: We solve the scattering and bound state equations for non-local interactions of the Perey-Buck type, through an iterative method. Using the distorted wave Born approximation, we construct the T-matrix for (p,d) on 17O, 41Ca, 49Ca, 127Sn, 133Sn, and 209Pb at 20 and 50 MeV. Results: We found that for bound states, the Perey corrected wave function resulting from the local equation agreed well with that from the non-local equation in the interior region, but discrepancies were found in the surface and peripheral regions. Overall, the Perey correction factor was adequate for scattering states, with the exception of a few partial waves corresponding to the grazing impact parameters. These differences proved to be important for transfer reactions. Conclusions: The Perey correction factor does offer an improvement over taking a direct local equivalent solution. However, if the desired accuracy is to be better than 10%, the exact solution of the non-local equation should be pursued.

## Key claim

The Perey correction factor (PCF) improves upon using a direct local equivalent solution for non-local potentials of the Perey-Buck type, but if the desired accuracy is better than 10%, the exact solution of the non-local equation should be pursued, as the PCF fails for partial waves corresponding to grazing impact parameters and in the surface and peripheral regions of bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the scattering and bound state equations for non-local interactions of the Perey-Buck type using an iterative method, for neutron states bound to 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 126Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb, and for (p,p) and (p,d) reactions on 17O, 41Ca, 49Ca, 127Sn, 133Sn, and 209Pb at 20 and 50 MeV. They constructed local equivalent potentials by fitting elastic scattering angular distributions from the non-local solutions, then applied the Perey correction factor to the local wave functions and compared them to the full non-local wave functions. Using the distorted wave Born approximation, they constructed the T-matrix for (p,d) transfer reactions and compared angular distributions from the full non-local calculation, the local equivalent calculation, and the Perey-corrected calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Perey effect by Austern (1965) and Fiedeldey (1966), who first demonstrated the reduction of wave function amplitude in the nuclear interior due to non-local potentials, and by Perey and Buck (1962), who introduced the Perey-Buck form of the non-local potential. The study directly tests the Perey correction factor derived by Austern (1970) for three-dimensional cases, and complements recent work by Timofeyuk and Johnson (2013) who studied non-local effects in (d,p) reactions within the Adiabatic Distorted Wave Approximation. The results enable future work to determine when approximate treatments of non-locality are sufficient and when exact solutions of the non-local equation are necessary, particularly for transfer reactions where accuracy better than 10% is required.
