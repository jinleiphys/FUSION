# Virtual axion-like particle complement to Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger action

> We modify action in an external electromagnetic field to include effects of virtual axion-like particle (ALP) excitations. A measurable addition to QED-Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger (EHS) action is obtained and incorporated into experimental constraints placed on ALP mass and coupling to two photons. The regime of these constraints in which the ALP vacuum effect surpasses the EHS effect is characterized. We show that probing of the virtual vacuum effect offers an alternative method in search for physics related to ALPs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that virtual axion-like particle (ALP) fluctuations produce a measurable addition to the QED Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger (EHS) action, and that this ALP vacuum effect can surpass the QED vacuum effect for ALP coupling-to-mass ratios satisfying \(G_\mathrm{A}^2/m_\mathrm{A}^2 \geq 28\alpha^2/(45 m_e^4)\). The claim is conditional: the ALP contribution only dominates over the EHS effect in a specific region of the ALP parameter space that is not excluded by existing astrophysical constraints.

## Method

The authors start from the effective QED action including Maxwell, EHS, and ALP terms, then integrate out the ALP field by completing the square in the Lagrangian, which yields an effective \(P^2\) term (where \(P = \mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{B}\)) that adds to the existing EHS \(P^2\) term. They compare the coefficient of this new term to the corresponding coefficient from the EHS low-field expansion, deriving the condition for the ALP effect to equal or exceed the QED effect. This condition is then plotted onto the existing experimental constraint diagram for ALP mass and coupling from the Particle Data Group (Tanabashi 2018).

## Key numbers

- The condition for ALP effect to equal or exceed QED effect: \(G_\mathrm{A}^2/m_\mathrm{A}^2 \geq 28\alpha^2/(45 m_e^4)\), which translates to \(\log_{10}(G_\mathrm{A}[\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}]) \geq \log_{10}(m_\mathrm{A}[\mathrm{eV}]) - 4.66\).
- For the pion, the virtual effect adds a factor of \(1 + \mathcal{O}(10^{-10})\) to the \(P^2\) coefficient, a negligible addition.
- The PVLAS constraint corresponds to \(G_\mathrm{A}/m_\mathrm{A} \sim 10^{-7}\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1} / 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV}\), which is about 20 times the strength of the QED-EHS effect.
- The critical field for the electron loop is \(E_c = m_e^2 c^3 / e\hbar = 1.3 \times 10^{18}\,\mathrm{V/m}\).
- For ALP mass \(m_\mathrm{A} = 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV}\), the Compton wavelength is \(\lambdabar_c = 2 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{m}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective action (Heisenberg 1935, Schwinger 1951) by adding virtual ALP fluctuations, complementing the QED vacuum polarization from electron loops. It contrasts with the cases of pions and axions, where the fixed product of decay constant and mass (for pions, \(f_\pi = 93\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_\pi \sim 135\,\mathrm{MeV}\)) makes the virtual effect negligible. The results are incorporated into the experimental constraint diagram from the Particle Data Group (Tanabashi 2018), adding a new "virtual effect" region that includes the PVLAS vacuum birefringence measurements (Zavattini 2005, 2007; Della Valle 2015). This enables a new detection method for ALPs that does not rely on real ALP propagation, relevant for experiments at facilities like ELI (Gies 2008, Dunne 2008) and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Ruffini 2009, Rafelski 2016).
