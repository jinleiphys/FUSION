# The Interference Term between the Spin and Orbital Contributions to M1 Transitions

> We study the cross-correlation between the spin and orbital parts of magnetic dipole transitions M1 in both isoscalar and isovector channels. In particular, we closely examine certain cases where $\sum B(M1)$ is very close to $\sum B(M1)_σ + \sum B(M1)_l$, implying a cancellation of the summed interference terms. We gain some insight into this problem by considering special cases approaching the SU(3) limit, and by examining the behaviour of single-particle transitions at the beginning and towards the end of the s-d shell.

# The Interference Term between the Spin and Orbital Contributions to M1 Transitions

## Key claim

For isovector M1 transitions in N=Z nuclei, the summed interference term between spin and orbital contributions nearly vanishes (θ close to 90°) when the system is near the SU(3) or U(4) symmetry limit, but this cancellation is not exact and depends on the strength of the spin-orbit interaction; for isoscalar transitions, the interference is always maximally destructive (θ = 180°) due to the vanishing of the total angular momentum operator matrix element.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the (x,y) interaction, where x and y scale the two-body spin-orbit and tensor interaction strengths respectively, with V_c(r) containing the central interaction. They computed summed B(M1) strengths, B(M1)_l (orbital), and B(M1)_σ (spin) for transitions from ground states to excited 1+ states in s-d shell nuclei, and defined an interference angle θ via the relation ΣB(M1) = ΣB(M1)_σ + ΣB(M1)_l + 2√(ΣB(M1)_l)√(ΣB(M1)_σ)cos(θ). They examined the dependence of θ on the spin-orbit and tensor interaction strengths by varying x and y, and studied single-particle transitions for j = l ± 1/2 cases.

## Key numbers

- For isoscalar transitions in ²⁸Si (x=1, y=1), ΣB(M1) = 1.6876 × 10⁻² μ_N², with θ = 180° for all nuclei from ²⁰Ne to ³⁶Ar.
- For isovector transitions in ²⁰Ne (x=1, y=1), θ = 90.00°, increasing steadily to 95.40° for ³⁶Ar.
- For isovector transitions in ²⁸Si (x=1, y=1), ΣB(M1) = 4.4449 μ_N², ΣB(M1)_l = 2.2424 μ_N², ΣB(M1)_σ = 2.3911 μ_N², with θ = 92.30°.
- For ²²Ne, the T=1 → T=1 transitions give θ = 85.90°, while T=1 → T=2 transitions give θ = 102.4°, with the combined total giving θ = 88.70°.
- For ²⁸Si with x=0, y=0 (no spin-orbit or tensor), θ = 90.03°; with x=1, y=0, θ = 94.93°; with x=1, y=1, θ = 92.33°; with x=1.5, y=0.5, θ = 98.31°.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study of M1 transitions in s-d shell nuclei (Fayache et al., 1997), which focused on how spin-orbit and tensor interactions affect transition strengths. The present paper quantifies the cross-correlation between spin and orbital contributions that was briefly noted in that earlier work. The results connect to the SU(3) model of Elliott (1958) and the U(4) limit of Wigner (1937), showing that deviations from these symmetries produce systematic departures from the cancellation of interference terms. The findings enable a more nuanced understanding of how effective interactions inside nuclei modify the balance between spin and orbital M1 strength, particularly relevant for interpreting experimental M1 data in terms of nuclear structure and symmetry breaking.
