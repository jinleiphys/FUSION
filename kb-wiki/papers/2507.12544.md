# Parton distribution and fragmentation functions with massive gluons

> The correct description of the hadron's structure requires understanding how quarks and gluons form the observable hadrons and how they are distributed within them. Two key nonperturbative quantities encapsulate this information: parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs). The former define a probabilistic light-front momentum distribution of partons within a hadron, whereas the latter describe the hadronization process of high-energy partons. Computing these functions analytically poses significant challenges, as it demands models that accurately incorporate the nonperturbative infrared dynamics of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). In this work, we compute the pion PDF and its elementary and full FFs using the Curci-Ferrari (CF) model. This model enables the exploration of nonperturbative QCD effects by introducing a gluon-mass scale within the Landau gauge QCD Lagrangian. The two-point quark and gluon correlation functions derived from the CF model agree well with lattice QCD results and reproduce the pion decay constant in the chiral limit, consistent with chiral perturbation theory. The resulting pion PDF and FFs computed with the CF quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude are in good qualitative and quantitative agreement with those obtained using the Qin-Chang model, a benchmark approach to nonperturbative QCD. These findings support the broader applicability of the Curci-Ferrari model in hadron phenomenology.

## Key claim
The Curci-Ferrari model, which introduces a gluon-mass term into the QCD Lagrangian, produces pion parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions that are in good qualitative and quantitative agreement with those obtained using the established Qin-Chang model, supporting the broader applicability of the Curci-Ferrari model in hadron phenomenology.

## Method
The authors computed the pion PDF and elementary FF using the cut-diagram formalism with dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes derived from the Curci-Ferrari model in the chiral limit. The quark propagators and BSAs were parametrized using Padé approximants and Nakanishi integral representations, and the resulting distributions were compared with those obtained from the Qin-Chang model within the rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The full fragmentation functions were then obtained via the quark-jet model of Field and Feynman, which accounts for cascading hadron emissions through Volterra integral equations.

- The QC model parameters include omega = 0.5 GeV and kappa = (omega D)^(1/3) = 0.8 GeV, with Lambda_QCD = 0.234 GeV.
- The BSA in the CF model is parametrized with three terms per amplitude; for the E_pi amplitude, the parameters include Lambda1 = -67.08, Lambda2 = -1.134, Lambda3 = 3.15, with exponents n1 = 4, n2 = 5, n3 = 6.
- The QC model BSA for E_pi uses Lambda = 1.267 GeV, with U1 = 2.571, U2 = -1.572, sigma1 = 2.136, sigma2 = 1.889, and exponents n1 = 4, n2 = 5.
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is reproduced consistent with chiral perturbation theory (no explicit numerical value given in the text for f_pi).

## Context
This work extends the application of the Curci-Ferrari model, previously used to describe two-point Green functions in QCD (Tissier 2011, Pelaez 2013-2022), to the computation of parton distribution and fragmentation functions. The results are compared with those from the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach using the Qin-Chang model (Qin 2011) and earlier NJL model calculations (Ito 2009). The agreement between the CF and QC predictions demonstrates that the CF model can describe physical observables beyond two-point correlation functions, enabling future applications to other hadronic observables and processes.
