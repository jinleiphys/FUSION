# Dynamic modeling for heavy-ion collisions

> Recent theory progresses in (3+1)D dynamical descriptions of relativistic nuclear collisions at finite baryon density are reviewed. Heavy-ion collisions at different collision energies produce strongly coupled nuclear matter to probe the phase structure of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Dynamical frameworks serve as a quantitative tool to study properties of hot QCD matter and map collisions to the QCD phase diagram. Outstanding challenges are highlighted when confronting theoretical models with the current and forthcoming experimental measurements from the RHIC beam energy scan program.

# Dynamic modeling for heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper reviews recent progress in (3+1)D dynamical descriptions of relativistic nuclear collisions at finite baryon density, but notes that while hybrid hydrodynamics + hadronic transport frameworks successfully describe many flow measurements, they currently underestimate multi-strangeness baryon ratios for collision energies below 40 GeV, suggesting these baryons may have a higher chemical freeze-out energy density than non-strange hadrons.

## Method
The review examines hybrid dynamical frameworks that combine relativistic hydrodynamics with hadronic transport approaches to model the full evolution of heavy-ion collisions from initial energy deposition through QGP expansion to hadronic freeze-out. It focuses on 3D initial-state models with dynamical initialization schemes that incorporate baryon stopping and strangeness production, comparing model calculations of identified particle yield ratios against experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program across collision energies from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the crossover from hadron gas to QGP at zero net baryon density is T_pc = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (HotQCD, 2018).
- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV, the fireball reaches T_max ~ 250 MeV and mu_B,max ~ 200 MeV at mid-rapidity, while forward rapidity regions reach mu_B ~ 300 MeV.
- The dynamical evolution at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV shows energy-momentum and baryon charge depositions lasting about 1.5-2 fm/c, with hydrodynamic expansion continuing up to 10.5 fm/c before full hadronization.
- Model calculations use a constant switching energy density e_sw = 0.26 GeV/fm^3 for particlization.
- The strangeness neutrality condition n_S = 0 results in a linear correlation mu_S ~ mu_B/3.
- Relativistic heavy-ion collisions with sqrt(s_NN) >= 20 GeV probe net baryon chemical potential up to ~300 MeV where QGP and hadronic phases connect via smooth crossover.

## Context
This review extends the hybrid dynamical framework developed by Shen (2017) and collaborators, which combines 3D MC-Glauber initial conditions with string deceleration models, and builds upon prior work by Karpenko (2015), Du (2018), and others on dynamical initialization schemes. The paper highlights that the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program has driven theory developments toward (3+1)D modeling, and that Bayesian inference methods (Bernhard, 2019; JETSCAPE, 2020; Nijs, 2020) are now being adopted to systematically constrain QGP properties. The review identifies outstanding challenges for future fixed-target experiments at RHIC and the FAIR/NICA programs that will probe beyond mu_B = 400 MeV, where lattice QCD guidance on the equation of state is limited and searches for the QCD critical point and first-order phase transition require reliable baseline expectations without critical phenomena.
