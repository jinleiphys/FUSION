# Helicity Dependent Distribution Functions of the Proton and $Λ$ and $Σ^0$ Baryons

> Using continuum Schwinger function methods, a coherent set of predictions for proton, $Λ$ and $Σ^0$ distribution functions (DFs) has been made available -- both helicity dependent and unpolarised. The results and comparisons between them reveal impacts of diquark correlations and SU$(3)$-flavour symmetry breaking, some of which are highlighted in this contribution. For instance: in-proton ratios of helicity-dependent/unpolarised valence-quark DFs are presented; it is highlighted that, were it not for the presence of axialvector diquarks in the $Σ^0$, the valence strange quark would carry none of the $Σ^0$ spin; and the sign and size of polarised gluon DFs is discussed -- at a scale typical of modern measurements, gluon partons carry roughly 40% of each octet baryon's spin.

# Helicity Dependent Distribution Functions of the Proton and Λ and Σ⁰ Baryons

## Key claim
Using continuum Schwinger function methods within a vector × vector contact interaction (SCI) framework, the authors present a coherent set of predictions for helicity-dependent and unpolarised distribution functions of the proton, Λ, and Σ⁰ baryons, with the caveat that SCI predictions serve as a guide to results that would be obtained from more realistic momentum-dependent interactions.

## Method
The study employs a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector × vector contact interaction (SCI) realised via a quark + dynamical diquark picture of baryons, solving the Faddeev equation for baryon bound-state amplitudes. Distribution functions are calculated for valence quarks, gluons, and four-flavour separated sea quarks, both unpolarised and helicity-dependent, at a scale ζ = 2 GeV. The spin-flavour wave functions of Λ and Σ⁰ are constructed from scalar (J^P = 0⁺) and axialvector (J^P = 1⁺) diquark correlations, with the Λ having two isoscalar-scalar components and one isovector-axialvector, while the Σ⁰ has one isoscalar-scalar and two isovector-axialvector components.

## Key numbers
- Gluon partons carry roughly 40% of each octet baryon's spin at a scale typical of modern measurements.
- At ζ₂, compared with the proton value, quarks carry 12% more of the Λ spin and 3% less of the Σ⁰ spin.
- The net glue contribution to each baryon's spin is the same, approximately 40%.
- Quark orbital angular momentum in the Λ-baryon is only about 55% of that in Σ⁰ and proton.
- The Higgs-boson generated renormalisation group invariant quark current masses sum to approximately 13 MeV, which is 0.014 m_N.
- The in-nucleon expectation value of the RGI mass term is approximately 60 MeV.
- The emergent hadron mass component is approximately 0.94 m_N.
- The RGI gluon mass scale is 0.43(1) GeV.
- The process-independent QCD effective charge at infrared momenta is α̂(k²=0)/π = 0.97(4).

## Context
This work extends the systematic programme of continuum Schwinger function method predictions for baryon distribution functions, building on prior calculations for pion, kaon, and proton DFs by Cui (2020), Chang (2022), Lu (2022), Cheng (2023), and Yu (2025). The SCI analysis of Λ and Σ⁰ DFs by Yu (2025) reveals the impacts of diquark correlations and SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking, showing that Higgs-driven mass differences between light and strange quarks are largely masked by emergent hadron mass effects. The results enable future comparisons with data from Jefferson Laboratory and provide a foundation for predictions of spin transfer in deep-inelastic Λ electroproduction, where data exist from HERMES (1999) and Schnell (2024) but reliable fragmentation functions are still needed.
