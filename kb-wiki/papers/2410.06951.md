# Exclusive rare semileptonic decays of $B$ and $B_c$ mesons in the relativistic independent quark model

> We investigate the exclusive rare semileptonic decays: $B(B_c)\to K(D_{(s)})l\bar{l}/Σν_l\bar{ν_l}$ ($l=μ, τ$) in the framework of relativistic independent quark (RIQ) model based on an average flavor independent confining potential in equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. The invariant weak form factors, parametrising the matrix elements between participating meson states are calculated in the parent meson rest frame. The momentum transfer dependence of the form factors is reliably determined in the whole accessible kinematical range: $q^2_{min}\leq 0\leq q^2_{max}$. Our predicted branching fractions for $B\to Kμ^+μ^-/τ^+τ^-(Σν_l\bar{ν_l})$, $B_c\to D(D_s)μ^+μ^-$, $B_c\to D(D_s)τ^+τ^-$ and $B_c\to D(D_s)Σν_l\bar{ν_l}$, obtained in order of $10^{-7}(10^{-6})$, $10^{-9}(10^{-7})$, $10^{-9}(10^{-8})$ and $10^{-8}(10^{-7})$, respectively are in reasonable agreement with other Standard Model predictions and Lattice QCD results. The averaged values of the lepton polarization asymmetries for decay modes are obtained as $\langle P_L(B\to Kμ^+μ^-)\rangle$=-0.97, $\langle P_L(B_c\to D_{(s)}μ^+μ^-)\rangle$=-0.972, $\langle P_L(B\to Kτ^+τ^-)\rangle$=-0.224, $\langle P_L(B_c\to Dτ^+τ^-)\rangle$=-0.275 and $\langle P_L(B_c\to D_sτ^+τ^-)\rangle$=-0.194.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their relativistic independent quark (RIQ) model predictions for branching fractions of exclusive rare semileptonic decays of \(B\) and \(B_c\) mesons are in reasonable agreement with other Standard Model predictions and Lattice QCD results, but the abstract's hedge is that the form factors are calculated in the parent meson rest frame and the momentum transfer dependence is determined in the whole accessible kinematical range without extrapolation, unlike other approaches that use monopole/dipole/Gaussian ansatz.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic independent quark (RIQ) model, based on an average flavor independent confining potential in equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form, to calculate the invariant weak form factors \(f_+(q^2)\), \(f_-(q^2)\), and \(f_T(q^2)\) for the transitions \(B \to K\) and \(B_c \to D(D_s)\). These form factors were then used with the effective electroweak Hamiltonian and Wilson coefficients to compute differential decay rates, branching fractions, and averaged lepton polarization asymmetries for the decays to \(\mu^+\mu^-\), \(\tau^+\tau^-\), and \(\Sigma\nu_l\bar{\nu_l}\) final states.

## Key numbers

* Branching fraction for \(B \to K\mu^+\mu^-/\tau^+\tau^-(\Sigma\nu_l\bar{\nu_l})\) obtained in order of \(10^{-7}(10^{-6})\).
* Branching fraction for \(B_c \to D(D_s)\mu^+\mu^-\) obtained in order of \(10^{-9}(10^{-7})\).
* Branching fraction for \(B_c \to D(D_s)\tau^+\tau^-\) obtained in order of \(10^{-9}(10^{-8})\).
* Branching fraction for \(B_c \to D(D_s)\Sigma\nu_l\bar{\nu_l}\) obtained in order of \(10^{-8}(10^{-7})\).
* Averaged lepton polarization asymmetry \(\langle P_L(B \to K\mu^+\mu^-)\rangle = -0.97\).
* Averaged lepton polarization asymmetry \(\langle P_L(B_c \to D_{(s)}\mu^+\mu^-)\rangle = -0.972\).
* Averaged lepton polarization asymmetry \(\langle P_L(B \to K\tau^+\tau^-)\rangle = -0.224\).
* Averaged lepton polarization asymmetry \(\langle P_L(B_c \to D\tau^+\tau^-)\rangle = -0.275\).
* Averaged lepton polarization asymmetry \(\langle P_L(B_c \to D_s\tau^+\tau^-)\rangle = -0.194\).

## Context

This work extends the RIQ model, previously applied to static properties and semileptonic decays of heavy-flavored mesons by Barik et al. (1996, 1997, 2009) and Nayak et al. (2021, 2022), to rare semileptonic decays governed by flavor-changing neutral currents. The predictions are compared with other Standard Model approaches such as the relativistic constituent quark model (Faessler 2002, Ebert 2010), light front quark model (Choi 2002, 2010), light cone sum rules (Ball 2005, Khodjamirian 2010), and lattice QCD (Bouchard 2013, Bailey 2016, Parrott 2023). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from LHCb and Belle II, particularly for the \(B_c\) sector where data is scant.
