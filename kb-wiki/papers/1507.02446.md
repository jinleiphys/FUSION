# Photon emission in neutral current interactions at the T2K experiment

> We have applied a microscopic model for single photon emission in neutral current interactions on nucleons and nuclei to determine the number and distributions of such events at the Super-Kamiokande detector, for the flux and beam exposure of the T2K experiment in neutrino mode. These reactions represent an irreducible background in electron-(anti)neutrino appearance measurements aimed at a precise measurement of mixing angle $θ_{13}$ and the $CP$ violating phase. We have obtained a total number of photon events that is twice larger than the one from the NEUT event generator (version 5.1.4.2) used in the analysis of T2K data. Detailed comparisons of energy and angular distributions for the $ν_μ$ and $\barν_μ$ fluxes have also been performed.

# Photon emission in neutral current interactions at the T2K experiment

## Key claim
The microscopic model predicts a total number of NCγ events at Super-Kamiokande that is twice larger than the NEUT event generator (version 5.1.4.2) used in T2K analysis, but the shape differences in energy and angular distributions between the two models are not significant; the normalization discrepancy arises primarily from the strength of the Δ(1232) radiative decay rather than from missing non-Δ amplitudes or nuclear corrections.

## Method
The authors applied a microscopic many-body model for single photon emission in neutral current interactions on nucleons and nuclei, including baryon-pole terms (nucleon, Δ(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535)), crossed baryon-pole terms, and t-channel pion exchange. For nuclear targets, they treated incoherent reactions using a local density approximation with one-particle-one-hole excitations and coherent reactions with summed amplitudes over all nucleons, including in-medium Δ(1232) broadening. They folded the cross sections with the T2K neutrino flux at Super-Kamiokande for a total of 6.57 × 10²⁰ protons on target in neutrino mode.

## Key numbers
- Total predicted NCγ events: 0.427 ± 0.050 (68% CL), composed of 0.412 ± 0.049 from νμ and 0.015 ± 0.002 from ν̄μ
- NEUT 5.1.4.2 prediction: 0.217 events total (0.209 from νμ, 0.008 from ν̄μ)
- Super-Kamiokande fiducial mass: 2.25 × 10¹⁰ grams
- Protons on target: 6.57 × 10²⁰
- Median neutrino energy of T2K flux at SK: 630 MeV
- Photon energy distribution peaks at Eγ ∼ 200–300 MeV for the incoherent channel
- Only 3% of predicted events arise from the ν̄μ contamination of the νμ beam

## Context
This work extends the microscopic model of Wang (2013) for NCγ reactions to predict event rates at Super-Kamiokande for the T2K experiment, following earlier applications to the MiniBooNE anomaly. The results contradict the NEUT generator (Hayato, 2009) used in T2K analysis, which predicts half as many events. The model is consistent with MiniBooNE in situ estimates from NUANCE tuned to NCπ⁰ measurements (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2009). This work enables improved background estimates for future δCP measurements and suggests that NEUT underestimates the irreducible NCγ background by a factor of two.
