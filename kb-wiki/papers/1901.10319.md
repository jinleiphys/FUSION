# Spin partners $W_{bJ}$ from the line shapes of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$

> In a recent paper Phys.Rev. D98, 074023 (2018), the most up-to-date experimental data for all measured production and decay channels of the bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ were analysed in a field-theoretical coupled-channel approach which respects analyticity and unitarity and incorporates both the pion exchange as well as a short-ranged potential nonperturbatively. All parameters of the interaction were fixed directly from data, and pole positions for both $Z_b$ states were determined. In this work we employ the same approach to predict in a parameter-free way the pole positions and the line shapes in the elastic and inelastic channels of the (still to be discovered) spin partners of the $Z_b$ states. They are conventionally referred to as $W_{bJ}$'s with the quantum numbers $J^{PC}=J^{++}$ ($J=0,1,2$). It is demonstrated that the results of our most advanced pionful fit, which gives the best $χ^2/{\rm d.o.f.}$ for the data in the $Z_b$ channels, are consistent with all $W_{bJ}$ states being above-threshold resonances which manifest themselves as well pronounced hump structures in the line shapes. On the contrary, in the pionless approach, all $W_{bJ}$'s are virtual states which can be seen as enhanced threshold cusps in the inelastic line shapes. Since the two above scenarios provide different imprints on the observables, the role of the one-pion exchange in the $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ systems can be inferred from the once available experimental data directly.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the spin partner states \(W_{bJ}\) (with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=J^{++}\) for \(J=0,1,2\)) of the known \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) will appear as above-threshold resonances with pronounced hump structures in their line shapes if the one-pion exchange is included in the potential, but as virtual states with enhanced threshold cusps if pion exchange is omitted; the actual experimental observation of these states will therefore discriminate between the two scenarios.

## Method

The authors employ a field-theoretical coupled-channel approach based on an effective Lagrangian consistent with chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetry, where the potential includes one-pion exchange, one-eta exchange, and short-range contact interactions up to order \(\mathcal{O}(Q^2)\). All low-energy constants (elastic contact couplings, inelastic couplings to \(\Upsilon(nS)\pi\) and \(h_b(mP)\pi\) channels, and \(S\)-\(D\) counter terms) were previously fixed from a combined fit to the experimental line shapes of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) in the \(J^{PC}=1^{+-}\) channel; these parameters are then translated via heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints to the \(W_{bJ}\) channels without any additional fitting. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved nonperturbatively to obtain the \(T\)-matrix and the differential production rates for both elastic (\(B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}\)) and inelastic (\(\Upsilon(nS)\pi\), \(h_b(mP)\pi\)) channels.

## Key numbers

* The binding momentum scale generated by the \(B^*\)-\(B\) mass difference is \(p_{\rm typ} = \sqrt{m\,\delta} \simeq 500\) MeV, where \(\delta = m_* - m \approx 45\) MeV.
* The \(B^*B\pi\) coupling constant is taken as \(g_b = g_c \approx 0.57\), extracted from the \(D^{*+}\to D^0\pi^+\) decay width with final-state momentum \(q = 39\) MeV.
* The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
* The hard scale of the chiral EFT is \(\Lambda_h \approx 1\) GeV, and the heavy-quark spin symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/M_b \approx 0.04\).
* The \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}\) for the best pionful fit to the \(Z_b\) data is reported as the best among the fits considered, though the exact numerical value is not given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) states performed by Wang et al. (2018) in Phys.Rev. D98, 074023, where all interaction parameters were fixed from experimental line shapes. It improves upon an earlier study by Baru et al. (2017) by using parameters directly fitted to data rather than masses from other analyses, by including inelastic channels for the \(W_{bJ}\) predictions, and by investigating the renormalisation programme with the \(S\)-\(D\) counter term. The predictions enable Belle-II to test the role of one-pion exchange in the \(B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}\) systems by measuring the line shapes of the \(W_{bJ}\) states, which will distinguish between the pionful (resonance) and pionless (virtual state) scenarios.
