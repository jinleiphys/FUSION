# Towards a test particle description of transport processes for states with continuous mass spectra

> Based on a first order gradient expansion a consistent transport equation is derived for a nonrelativistic system beyond the quasiparticle approximation, i.e. for a regime where the dynamically generated width of the states is allowed to be large. An exactly conserved quantity is identified which is interpreted as an effective particle number obtained by coarse graining. Using a test particle ansatz for this conserved quantity allows to rewrite the transport equation into equations of motion for test particles. The two-body collision terms are formulated in terms of the test particles which gain non-trivial renormalization factors due to the coarse graining process.

## Key claim

The paper derives a consistent transport equation for nonrelativistic systems beyond the quasiparticle approximation, where the dynamically generated width of states is allowed to be large, but the derivation is restricted to first order gradient expansion and the nonrelativistic case only, with possible generalizations to relativistic systems left as a future comment.

## Method

Starting from the exact nonrelativistic Kadanoff-Baym equations for two-point functions, the author performs a Wigner transformation and a first order gradient expansion in the center-of-mass variable, keeping terms up to one derivative. From the resulting six equations for three quantities (the generalized phase-space density \(S^<\), the spectral function \(\mathcal{A}\), and the real part of the retarded propagator \(\mathrm{Re}\bar D^{\mathrm{ret}}\)), the author identifies redundant equations and constraints, ultimately reducing them to a single transport equation for \(S^<\) together with algebraic expressions for \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathrm{Re}\bar D^{\mathrm{ret}}\). A conserved quantity is isolated from this transport equation, and a test particle ansatz is made for this conserved quantity to rewrite the transport equation into equations of motion for test particles, with two-body collision terms formulated in terms of test particles that gain renormalization factors from coarse graining.

## Key numbers

* The spectral function normalization is \(\int \frac{dp_0}{2\pi} \mathcal{A}(X,p) = 1\) (Eq. 3.17).
* The spectral function is given by \(\mathcal{A}(X,p) = \Gamma(X,p) / \left[ \left( p_0 - \vec{p}^2/(2m) - \mathrm{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\mathrm{ret}}(X,p) \right)^2 + \frac{1}{4} \Gamma^2(X,p) \right]\) (Eq. 4.7).
* The real part of the retarded propagator is \(\mathrm{Re}\bar D^{\mathrm{ret}}(X,p) = \left( p_0 - \vec{p}^2/(2m) - \mathrm{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\mathrm{ret}}(X,p) \right) / \left[ \left( p_0 - \vec{p}^2/(2m) - \mathrm{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\mathrm{ret}}(X,p) \right)^2 + \frac{1}{4} \Gamma^2(X,p) \right]\) (Eq. 4.8).
* The particle number is given by \(N(t) = \int d^3x \int \frac{d^4p}{(2\pi)^4} S^<(t,\vec{x};p)\) (Eq. 3.18).

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym formalism beyond the quasiparticle regime treated in earlier works such as Danielewicz (1984) and Botermans and Malfliet (1990), addressing the need for off-shell transport in heavy-ion collisions where collision rates are high and resonances have large decay widths, as noted by Henning (1999), Ivanov, Knoll, and Voskresensky (1999), and Cassing and Juchem (1999). The formalism enables a test particle description of transport processes for states with continuous mass spectra, providing a foundation for numerical simulations of non-equilibrium many-body systems where the quasiparticle approximation fails, and the author notes that the approach can be generalized to relativistic systems.
