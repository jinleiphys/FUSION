# Unified Resummation of Soft Gluon Radiation in Heavy Meson Pair Photoproduction

> We develop a unified resummation framework for heavy-meson pair photoproduction that treats soft-gluon radiation in a massive scheme for $|\boldsymbol q| \lesssim m_Q$ and is consistent with the massless limit $m_Q \ll |\boldsymbol q|$, where $\boldsymbol q$ denotes the transverse momentum of the pair and $m_Q$ the quark mass. This framework describes the full correlation regime $|\boldsymbol q| \ll |\boldsymbol P|$, with $\boldsymbol P$ the pair's relative transverse momentum. Next, within the Color Glass Condensate framework, we quantify the impact of soft gluon radiation on two important phenomenological observables, namely, the azimuthal $Δϕ$ correlation and the harmonic asymmetries $\langle \cos(2nϕ_{qP})\rangle$. Our results show that the resummation provides an excellent description of the H1 $Δϕ$ data. We then find that the angular-asymmetry harmonics are sizable and their ratios in $γA$ to $γp$ collisions are highly sensitive to gluon saturation. We further find a clear mass hierarchy in both observables, indicating that heavy-quark masses suppress soft radiation and thereby enhance genuine small-$x$ saturation signals. Our work demonstrates that heavy-quark pair photoproduction provides a quantitative probe of gluon saturation, directly measurable in forthcoming EIC and UPC measurements. Finally, the unified resummation is readily extendable to other processes involving massive particles and jets in both $eA$ and $pA$ collisions.

# Unified Resummation of Soft Gluon Radiation in Heavy Meson Pair Photoproduction

## Key claim
The authors develop a unified resummation framework for heavy-meson pair photoproduction that interpolates between the massive scheme (for $|\boldsymbol q| \lesssim m_Q$) and the massless limit (for $m_Q \ll |\boldsymbol q|$), enabling the first quantitative description of H1 $\Delta\phi$ data. The framework claims that heavy-quark masses suppress soft radiation and thereby enhance genuine small-$x$ saturation signals, but this enhancement is conditional on the specific observable and harmonic order, with higher harmonics showing stronger saturation sensitivity.

## Method
The authors compute the one-loop eikonal radiation kernel for $\gamma + g \rightarrow Q + \bar{Q} + X$ in the back-to-back region ($|\boldsymbol q| \ll |\boldsymbol P|$), decomposing it into Fourier coefficients $\mathcal{S}_{r2n}(\boldsymbol{k}_g)$. They unify the massive scheme (where $m_Q$ regulates collinear divergences for $|\boldsymbol{k}_g| \leq m_Q$) and the massless scheme (where $m_Q \to 0$ for $m_Q \ll |\boldsymbol{k}_g|$) via a smooth crossover in coordinate space, yielding resummed Sudakov factors and harmonic coefficients $C_{2n}(\mu_b)$. The Color Glass Condensate framework is employed with the WW gluon distribution evolved via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, and heavy-quark hadronization is modeled using the Peterson model.

## Key numbers
- For the nuclear modification factor $\mathcal{R}$ in UPCs at $\sqrt{s}=5.36$ TeV: $\mathcal{R}|_{m_c}\simeq 0.85 > \mathcal{R}|_{m_b}\simeq 0.80$ in the deep back-to-back region ($\Delta\phi \to \pi$), showing a clear mass hierarchy.
- For D-meson pair photoproduction at the EIC with $Q^2=1$ GeV$^2$, the ratio $\langle \cos(2\phi_{qP})\rangle_{\gamma A}/\langle \cos(2\phi_{qP})\rangle_{\gamma p}$ stays in the range $0.5$--$0.6$, indicating strong saturation-induced suppression even after including Sudakov effects.
- The azimuthal asymmetries exhibit $n$-ordering: $\langle \cos(2\phi_{qP})\rangle > \langle \cos(4\phi_{qP})\rangle > \langle \cos(6\phi_{qP})\rangle$, with ratios showing increasingly strong suppression at higher harmonic order.
- The initial condition for rcBK evolution uses $Q_{s0}^2=0.16$ GeV$^2$ for the proton and $Q_{s0A}^2=5\,Q_{s0}^2$ for the heavy nucleus, with heavy-quark masses $m_c=1.2$ GeV and $m_b=4.5$ GeV.
- The non-perturbative Sudakov function uses $b_{\rm max}=1.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, $Q_0^2=2.4$ GeV$^2$, and the NP coefficient $\mathcal{C}_{\rm NP}$ varied in the range $1 \le \mathcal{C}_{\rm NP} \le 2$.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of soft-gluon radiation in heavy-particle production (Catani 1990, Zhu 2013, Klein 2018, Klein 2020, Hatta 2021, Shao 2023) and azimuthal asymmetries in the CGC framework (Metz 2011, Dominguez 2011, Dumitru 2015, Boer 2017, Zhao 2021, Caucal 2023). It resolves the challenge posed by earlier treatments of $\gamma\gamma \to l^+l^-$ (Klein 2018, Hatta 2021, Shao 2023, Shi 2024) where distinct Sudakov factors were needed for $\Delta\phi$ distributions versus azimuthal asymmetries, by providing a unified resummation that describes both observables consistently. The framework enables quantitative predictions for heavy-meson pair photoproduction at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider and in ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC, establishing these channels as probes of gluon saturation.
