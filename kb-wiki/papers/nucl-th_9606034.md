# On the Extraction of the Neutron Spin Structure Functions and the Gerasimov -- Drell -- Hearn Integral from $^3\vec {He}(\vec e, e')X$ data

> Nuclear effects in polarized inelastic electron scattering off polarized $^3He$ are discussed; in the kinematics of future experiments at CEBAF, Fermi motion effects are found to be much larger than in deep inelastic scattering. It is shown that improperly describing nuclear dynamics would lead to the extraction of unreliable neutron spin structure functions. On the other hand side, a simple and workable equation relating the Gerasimov -- Drell -- Hearn Integral for the neutron to the corresponding quantity for $^3He$ is proposed.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects, particularly Fermi motion, are much larger in the resonance region than in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) for polarized electron scattering off polarized $^3He$, and improperly describing nuclear dynamics would lead to unreliable extracted neutron spin structure functions; however, a simple equation using effective nucleon polarizations can relate the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) integral for the neutron to that for $^3He$ with at most 5% difference.

## Method

The authors calculated the $^3He$ spin structure function $g_1^{^3He}$ in the resonance region ($W_{th} < \tilde{W} < 2$ GeV) using a convolution model based on the spin-dependent spectral function of a nucleon inside $^3He$, with the nucleon spin structure functions $g_1^N$ obtained from a model by Burkert that parametrizes resonance contributions ($P_{33}(1232)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $F_{15}(1680)$) and includes a non-resonant background from the single pion Born term. The GDH integral for $^3He$ was then computed and compared to the free neutron GDH integral using the effective polarization approximation.

## Key numbers

- The effective neutron polarization in $^3He$ is $p_n = 0.88$ and the effective proton polarization is $p_p = -0.030$, as obtained from the $S'$ and $D$ wave probabilities in the $^3He$ ground state.
- In DIS kinematics, the extracted neutron spin structure function $\tilde{g}_1^n$ using the effective polarization formula differs from the free neutron $g_1^n$ by at most 4%.
- The GDH integral for the neutron extracted from $^3He$ data using the effective polarization formula differs from the free neutron GDH integral by at most 5%.
- At $Q^2 \simeq 1$ GeV$^2$, the $\Delta$ resonance ($P_{33}(1232)$) is produced with an average 3-momentum $|\vec{p}_\Delta| \simeq 6$ fm$^{-1}$; at $Q^2 \simeq 0.5$ GeV$^2$, $|\vec{p}_\Delta| \simeq 4.5$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The GDH sum rule prediction for the neutron at $Q^2=0$ is $-0.60$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear effects on the deuteron spin structure function (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1995) to the $^3He$ case, specifically for the kinematics of planned CEBAF experiments covering $0.2$ GeV $\leq \nu \leq 3$ GeV and $0.15$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 2$ GeV$^2$. The authors show that while the simple effective polarization formula used in DIS (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1993) fails for the resonance region structure functions themselves, it remains valid for the integrated GDH integral, enabling extraction of the neutron GDH integral from $^3He$ data. This is particularly important because existing analyses of unpolarized photoproduction data disagree with the GDH sum rule prediction for the neutron, unlike the proton case.
