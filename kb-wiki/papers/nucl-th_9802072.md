# The electron screening in OH-->F transition both for bound and scattering states

> We estimate the nuclear fusion rates for the transition OH-->F from the ground and excited molecular states and also consider effect of screening of electric charges of nuclei by bound electrons in the case of colliding atoms O and H. We show that the fusion rate for the molecular state is vanishingly small while the electron screening drastically changes the rate for the colliding atoms at kinetic energies up to hundreds of keV. The latter may be of consequence for some astrophysical applications.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the nuclear fusion rate for the molecular state of OH is vanishingly small and undetectable, but that atomic electron screening drastically changes the fusion rate for colliding atoms at kinetic energies up to hundreds of keV, which may be consequential for astrophysical applications. The key hedge is that the calculations are restricted to electromagnetic forces and do not include nuclear forces or the associated nuclear resonance, which could later be included in an astrophysical S-factor.

## Method

The authors solved Hartree-Fock-Roothaan equations for the OH molecule with nine electrons to obtain the effective potential between the two nuclei as a function of internuclear distance. Using this potential, they numerically integrated the radial Schrodinger equation for relative nuclear motion to obtain wave functions for both bound vibrational states and scattering states. For scattering states, they compared the wave function at zero separation (which determines fusion probability) for screened nuclei versus bare Coulomb nuclei across a range of kinetic energies.

## Key numbers

- The molecular energy minimum occurs at R = 2.2 a.u., with calculated energy E_min = -149.86 Ry, which is 0.988 of the experimental value of -151.56 Ry.
- The vibrational energy spacing is Delta E = 0.04 Ry = 0.54 eV.
- For the ground vibrational state (N=0), the wave function at zero separation is Psi(0) = 1.0 x 10^{-113} a.u.
- For the 16th excited vibrational state (N=16), Psi(0) = 4.7 x 10^{-105} a.u.
- For colliding atoms at a kinetic energy of 1.4 keV (the medium energy in the Sun), the ratio Psi(0)/Psi_Coul(0) is approximately 10^8.
- For colliding atoms at 10 keV, the ratio is about 9, and it tends to 1 at about 1 MeV.
- The electron screening energy U for the OH system is approximately 648 eV.

## Context

This work extends earlier speculation by Belyaev (1996) about enhanced fusion rates from molecular states due to energy degeneracy, but uses a more rigorous Hartree-Fock-Roothaan approach rather than simple analytic wave functions. The authors argue that the commonly used Debye-Huckel approximation for screening in stellar plasmas is irrelevant for stars like the Sun because the Debye radius is atomic scale while the relevant internuclear distances are nuclear scale, a point previously discussed by Dar and Shaviv (1996). The results suggest that atomic electron screening must be included in calculations of thermonuclear reaction rates for heavy enough ions, and the authors believe this effect will be common for other nuclear transitions.
