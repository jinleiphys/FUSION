# New method for calculating electromagnetic effects in semileptonic beta-decays of mesons

> We construct several classes of hadronic matrix elements and relate them to the low-energy constants in Chiral Perturbation Theory that describe the electromagnetic effects in the semileptonic beta decay of the pion and the kaon. We propose to calculate them using lattice QCD, and argue that such a calculation will make an immediate impact to a number of interesting topics at the precision frontier, including the outstanding anomalies in $|V_{us}|$ and the top-row Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix unitarity.

## Key claim

The authors construct a method to determine the low-energy constants (LECs) in Chiral Perturbation Theory that describe electromagnetic effects in semileptonic beta decays of pions and kaons using lattice QCD calculations, arguing that this will reduce the theoretical uncertainty in the extraction of \(|V_{us}|\) and help resolve the current tension with Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix unitarity, though the actual implementation requires calculating specific hadronic matrix elements that have not yet been fully computed for the kaon system.

## Method

The authors derive matching relations between two representations of electroweak radiative corrections: Sirlin's representation, which expresses corrections in terms of the axial \(\gamma W\) box diagram, and the Chiral Perturbation Theory representation, which expresses corrections in terms of low-energy constants \(\{K_i, X_i\}\). They propose calculating the axial \(\gamma W\) box diagram for both \(\pi_{e3}\) and \(K_{e3}^0\) decays on the lattice, which fixes the LECs \(X_1\) and \(X_6^\mathrm{phys}\) that describe lepton-hadron electromagnetic interactions. For the remaining lepton-free LECs, they construct four-point correlation functions that enable a direct lattice determination.

## Key numbers

- The current CKM unitarity test from the 2020 PDG gives \(|V_{ud}|^2+|V_{us}|^2+|V_{ub}|^2 = 0.9985(3)_{V_{ud}}(4)_{V_{us}}\), compared to 0.9994(4)_{V_{ud}}(4)_{V_{us}} from the 2018 PDG
- \(|V_{us}|\) extracted from kaon semileptonic decay (\(K_{l3}\)) is 0.2231(4)_\mathrm{exp+RCs}(6)_\mathrm{lattice} (with \(N_f=2+1+1\)), while from kaon leptonic decay (\(K_{\mu2}\)) it is 0.2252(5) (with \(N_f=2+1+1\))
- The short-distance electroweak factor \(S_\mathrm{EW} = 1.0229(3)\) for kaon semileptonic decays
- The Fermi constant \(G_F = 1.1663787(6) \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\)
- The perturbative QCD correction \(a_\mathrm{pQCD} \approx 0.068\) and the higher-order QED resummation \(\delta_\mathrm{HO}^\mathrm{QED} = 0.0010(3)\)
- The \(\mathcal{O}(e^2p^2)\) ChPT calculation of \(\delta_\mathrm{em}^{Kl}\) for \(K_{e3}^0\) gives \(0.99 \pm 0.19_{e^{2}p^{4}} \pm 0.11_{\mathrm{LEC}}\) percent, and for \(K_{e3}^{\pm}\) gives \(0.10 \pm 0.19_{e^{2}p^{4}} \pm 0.16_{\mathrm{LEC}}\) percent

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to radiative corrections previously applied to the neutron and pion systems, specifically building on the calculation of the axial \(\gamma W\) box diagram for \(\pi_{e3}\) by Feng et al. (2020) which achieved 1% accuracy. It addresses the outstanding discrepancy between \(|V_{us}|\) values extracted from kaon semileptonic and leptonic decays, and aims to reduce the theoretical uncertainty in the ratio \(R_V = \Gamma_{K_{l3}}/\Gamma_{\pi_{e3}}\) introduced by Czarnecki et al. (2019). The method enables first-principles determination of LECs that were previously only estimated using resonance models by Ananthanarayan (2004) and Descotes-Genon (2005) with no rigorous error analysis, and could help resolve the 3\(\sigma\) tension in top-row CKM unitarity.
