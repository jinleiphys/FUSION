# Study of the gamma p --> pi0 pi0 p reaction with the Crystal Ball/TAPS at the Mainz

> The gamma p --> pi0 pi0 p reaction has been measured from threshold to 1.4 GeV using the Crystal Ball and TAPS photon spectrometers together with the photon tagging facility at the Mainz Microtron. The experimental results include total and differential cross sections as well as specific angular distributions, which were used to extract partial-wave amplitudes. In particular, the energy region below the D13(1520) resonance was studied.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the dynamics of double-pion photoproduction below the D13(1520) resonance are not well understood, and that the measured angular distributions indicate a persistence of J=3/2 waves at energies where previous models predicted a rapid fall of the D13(1520) contribution, suggesting additional strong contributions from 3/2- and/or 3/2+ states.

## Method

The gamma p to pi0 pi0 p reaction was measured from threshold to 1.4 GeV using the Crystal Ball and TAPS photon spectrometers at the Mainz Microtron, with photon energies tagged by the Glasgow tagging spectrometer. Events with four photons detected were identified via kinematic-fit techniques, and the experimental acceptance was determined using Monte Carlo simulations incorporating Delta and D13 intermediate states. The angular distributions of the incident photon in a coordinate system aligned with the final-state decay plane were expanded in spherical harmonics to extract partial-wave amplitudes, with the analysis restricted to waves with J <= 3/2 for energies below 0.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Total cross sections were measured from threshold to E_gamma = 1402 MeV, with data taken using 855-MeV and 1508-MeV electron beams.
* The average acceptance for the 855-MeV beam data was close to 60% for the entire energy range; for the 1508-MeV beam data, acceptance decreased smoothly from 55% at E_gamma = 617 MeV to 42% at E_gamma = 1400 MeV.
* Systematic uncertainties in total and differential cross sections were estimated to be not larger than 6%.
* The D13(1520) contribution to the total cross section is seen as the first peak at E_gamma approximately 730 MeV.
* The background from gamma p to pi0 eta p events was estimated to reach only 0.5% at incident-photon energies of 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of double-pion photoproduction at MAMI, ELSA, and GRAAL (Braghieri 1996, Harter 1997, Wolf 2000, Ahrens 2002, Assafiri 2003, Kotulla 2004, Ahrens 2005, Strauch 2005, Ahrens 2007, Thoma 2008, Sarantsev 2008, Krambrich 2009) by providing higher-statistics data with finer energy binning. The analysis applies a formalism developed by Fix and Arenhoevel (2005) for partial-wave analysis of two-pseudoscalar photoproduction, similar to approaches used for inelastic pion-nucleon scattering (Arnold 1978, Morgan 1974). The results enable improved determination of the partial-wave content of the reaction, particularly testing the role of the D33(1700) resonance proposed by Sarantsev (2008) and constraining models such as those by Oset (1987), Ochi (1997), and Fix and Arenhoevel (2005).
