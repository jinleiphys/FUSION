# Proton structure from a light-front Hamiltonian

> We obtain the electromagnetic form factors, the axial form factor, and the parton distribution functions of the proton from the eigenstates of a light-front effective Hamiltonian in the leading Fock representation suitable for low-momentum scale applications. The electromagnetic and the axial form factors are found to be in agreement with the available experimental data. The unpolarized, the helicity, and the transversity valence quark distributions, after QCD evolution, are consistent with the global QCD analyses. The tensor charge also agree the experimental data, while the axial charge is somewhat outside the experimental error bar.

## Key claim

The authors obtain proton electromagnetic form factors, axial form factor, and parton distribution functions from eigenstates of a light-front effective Hamiltonian in the leading Fock representation, finding that the electromagnetic and axial form factors agree with available experimental data, while the axial charge is somewhat outside the experimental error bar.

## Method

The authors solve a light-front effective Hamiltonian for the proton's valence quark light-front wavefunctions using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), with the Hamiltonian including holographic QCD confinement, longitudinal confinement, and one-gluon exchange interaction. They compute electromagnetic and axial form factors from overlap integrals of these wavefunctions, and extract unpolarized, helicity, and transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) at a low initial scale. The PDFs are then evolved to higher scales using next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) equations for comparison with global QCD analyses.

## Key numbers

* Proton electric charge radius: \(r_E = 0.802^{+0.042}_{-0.040}\) fm (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(0.833 \pm 0.010\) fm from Bezginov (2019).
* Proton magnetic radius: \(r_M = 0.834^{+0.029}_{-0.029}\) fm (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(0.851 \pm 0.026\) fm from the Particle Data Group.
* Axial radius: \(r_A = 0.680^{+0.070}_{-0.073}\) fm (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(0.667 \pm 0.12\) fm from Hill (2017).
* Axial charge: \(g_A = 1.41^{+0.06}_{-0.06}\) (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(1.2723 \pm 0.0023\) from the Particle Data Group.
* Down quark tensor charge: \(g_T^d = -0.20^{+0.02}_{-0.04}\) (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(-0.25^{+0.30}_{-0.10}\) from Anselmino (2013).
* Up quark tensor charge: \(g_T^u = 0.94^{+0.06}_{-0.15}\) (BLFQ), compared to extracted data \(0.39^{+0.18}_{-0.12}\) from Anselmino (2013).
* Initial scale for PDF evolution: \(\mu_0^2 = 0.195 \pm 0.020\) GeV\(^2\), determined by fitting the first moments of valence quark unpolarized PDFs from global data.
* Basis truncation parameters: \(N_{\text{max}} = 10\) and \(K_{\text{max}} = 16.5\).
* Hamiltonian parameters: quark mass in kinetic energy \(m_{\text{q/KE}} = 0.3\) GeV, quark mass in OGE interaction \(m_{\text{q/OGE}} = 0.2\) GeV, confinement strength \(\kappa = 0.34\) GeV, coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 1.1\).

## Context

This work extends the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach previously applied to mesons (Li 2015, Li 2017) to the proton, incorporating holographic QCD confinement from Brodsky (2014) and one-gluon exchange interactions. The results enable future studies of generalized parton distributions, transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, and Wigner distributions using the same effective light-front wavefunctions, and motivate application of analogous Hamiltonians to other hadrons.
