# $ np \leftrightarrow dγ$ reactions calculated up to $E_γ=20$ MeV

> We calculate the electromagnetic dipole transition cross sections for the $np \rightarrow dγ$ and $ dγ\rightarrow np$ reactions over a broad range of energies. We use the LENPIC nucleon-nucleon interaction obtained from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N4LO) and effective electromagnetic dipole transition operators obtained from the same $χ$EFT up to N2LO. Our results agree with existing experiments. We get results at energies for which experimental data and/or modern theoretical calculations have not been reported. In this study, we utilize a new approach, namely, our adaptation of the Efros [V. D. Efros, Phys. Rev. C 99, 034620 (2019)] method that is prospective for future many-body applications in calculations of bound and continuum state wave functions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the electromagnetic dipole transition cross sections for the \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\) and \(d\gamma \rightarrow np\) reactions using a modern chiral effective field theory interaction and operators, and they claim their results agree with existing experiments; however, the abstract glosses over that at some specific energies (e.g., \(E = 1.2625 \cdot 10^{-8}\) MeV) they have a discrepancy of a few percent with experiment, which they attribute to the need for a higher chiral order in the current operator.

## Method

The authors use the LENPIC nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory up to N4LO and effective electromagnetic dipole transition operators from the same chiral EFT up to N2LO. They employ their adaptation of the Efros method, which uses an oscillator basis to construct scattering wave functions with a limited set of short-range functions and a truncated potential energy matrix, to calculate the \(M1\) and \(E1\) contributions to the capture and photodisintegration cross sections. The deuteron bound state is obtained by locating the \(S\)-matrix pole in the coupled \(^3SD_1\) partial waves.

## Key numbers

*   Deuteron binding energy: \(E_B = -2.2232\) MeV, compared to the experimental value of \(-2.224575(9)\) MeV.
*   Quadrupole moment: \(Q = 0.2723\) fm\(^2\), compared to the experimental value of \(0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\).
*   \(s\)-wave asymptotic normalization coefficient: \(A_s = 0.8846\) fm\(^{-1/2}\), consistent with the experimental value of \(0.8846(9)\) fm\(^{-1/2}\).
*   Asymptotic \(D/S\) state ratio: \(\eta = 0.0176\), deviating from the experimental value of \(0.0256(4)\).
*   For the \(M1\) capture cross section, converged results are obtained using \(v\) ranging from 9 to 15 short-range functions; for the \(E1\) capture cross section, \(v\) ranging from 2 to 6 gives convergence, except at \(E = 17.78\) MeV where \(v = 10\) is needed.
*   For scattering states, \(N_{\text{max}} = 50\) for the \(M1\) contribution and \(N_{\text{max}} = 20\) for the \(E1\) contribution provide cross section accuracy to within 1% in most cases and within 2% in a few cases.

## Context

This work extends the previous results of DuWeijieHORSE for the \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\) reaction to higher energies, up to \(E_\gamma = 20\) MeV, and also provides results for the inverse photodisintegration reaction. The calculations use a modern chiral EFT interaction and operators, and the authors note that for some energy ranges, no experimental or theoretical results have been reported previously. The paper demonstrates the applicability of the Efros method, which is intended to be a promising approach for future many-body applications in combination with the no-core shell model, enabling calculations of scattering states and reactions in light nuclei where other methods like the HORSE method are impractical.
