# Electro-magnetic field fluctuation and its correlation with the participant plane in Au+Au and isobaric collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> Intense transient electric ({\bf E}) and magnetic ({\bf B}) fields are produced in the high energy heavy-ion collisions. The electromagnetic fields produced in such high-energy heavy-ion collisions are proposed to give rise to a multitude of exciting phenomenon including the Chiral Magnetic Effect. We use a Monte Carlo (MC) Glauber model to calculate the electric and magnetic fields, more specifically their scalar product $\bf{E}\cdot\bf{B}$, as a function of space-time on an event-by-event basis for the Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV for different centrality classes. We also calculate the same for the isobars Ruthenium and Zirconium at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. In the QED sector $\bf{E}\cdot\bf{B}$ acts as a source of Chiral Separation Effect, Chiral Magnetic Wave, etc., which are associated phenomena to the Chiral Magnetic Effect. We also study the relationships between the electromagnetic symmetry plane angle defined by $\bf{E}\cdot\bf{B}$ ($ψ_{E.B}$) and the participant plane angle $ψ_{P}$ defined from the participating nucleons for the second-fifth order harmonics.

## Key claim

The scalar product **E·B**, which acts as a source term for chiral effects in heavy-ion collisions, shows a non-trivial centrality dependence and its symmetry plane angle correlates with the participant plane angle for higher-order harmonics (n=2-5), but the correlation strength depends on both centrality and the harmonic order.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model to sample nucleon positions in Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, including nuclear deformation parameters for the isobars. Electromagnetic fields were calculated from the Lorentz transformation of the Coulomb field of each proton, assuming straight-line trajectories with a cutoff of 0.3 fm to avoid singularities, and the scalar product **E·B** was computed on an event-by-event basis at various space-time points. The **E·B** symmetry plane angle ψ_EB was defined using the spatial distribution of **E·B** weighted by r^n, and its correlation with the participant plane angle ψ_P (defined from participating nucleon positions) was studied for the second through fifth harmonic orders.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength at √sNN=200 GeV is B ∼ 10^18-10^19 Gauss (stated as known from prior work)
- Event-averaged fields satisfy eB ≈ eE ∼ 10 m_π^2 at top RHIC energy for Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV
- For Au nucleus: R = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm, β2=β4=0
- For Ru: β2=0.158, R=5.085 fm, a=0.46 fm; for Zr: β2=0.08, R=5.02 fm, a=0.46 fm
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_NN = 42 mb at √sNN=200 GeV
- Centrality bins used: 0%-5% (b=0-3.2 fm), 40%-50% (b=9.3-10.6 fm), 70%-80% (b=12.2-13.5 fm)
- For t=z=0, **E·B** is almost flat up to impact parameter b∼10 fm, then falls rapidly
- At t=0.5 fm and z=±0.1 fm, **E·B** is approximately six orders of magnitude smaller than at t=z=0
- Charged particle multiplicity parameters: x=0.13, k=1.7, μ=2.08 for |η|<0.5

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Ref6, Ref7, Huang 2015) by focusing on the scalar product **E·B** rather than the fields individually, and by introducing a novel **E·B** symmetry plane angle to study correlations with the participant plane. The study is motivated by the Chiral Magnetic Effect and related phenomena (Chiral Separation Effect, Chiral Magnetic Wave) where **E·B** acts as a source term for chirality pumping. The results enable more realistic estimates of CME signals by showing that the **E·B** distribution has non-trivial angular correlations with the fireball geometry, which could affect experimental searches for charge separation relative to the reaction plane.
