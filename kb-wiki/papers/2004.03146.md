# Tensor and pairing interactions within the QMC energy density functional

> In the latest version of the QMC model, QMC$π$-III-T, the density functional is improved to include the tensor component quadratic in the spin-current and a pairing interaction derived in the QMC framework. Traditional pairing strengths are expressed in terms of the QMC parameters and the parameters of the model optimised. A variety of nuclear observables are calculated with the final set of parameters. The inclusion of the tensor component improves the predictions for ground-state bulk properties, while it has a small effect on the single-particle spectra. Further, its effect on the deformation of selected nuclei is found to improve the energies of doubly-magic nuclei at sphericity. Changes in the energy curves along the Zr chain with increasing deformation are investigated in detail. The new pairing functional is also applied to the study of neutron shell gaps, where it leads to improved predictions for subshell closures in the superheavy region.

## Key claim

The QMCπ-III-T density functional includes tensor terms quadratic in the spin-current and a pairing interaction derived within the QMC framework, both without introducing new free parameters; the tensor contribution is expected to be small because of strong cancellations between the sigma and omega meson contributions in the like-particle channel.

## Method

The QMC Hamiltonian was solved using an HF+BCS code (SkyAx) allowing axially-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric shapes, with five parameters (three couplings Gσ, Gω, Gρ, the sigma self-coupling λ3, and the sigma meson mass mσ) fitted to binding energies and rms charge radii of 70 magic nuclei (129 data points) using the POUNDeRS derivative-free optimisation algorithm. The tensor terms quadratic in the spin-current density J were included from both the central and spin-dependent parts of the QMC functional, and the pairing interaction was derived from the QMC Hamiltonian itself rather than using traditional parametrisations with separate pairing parameters.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy rms residual for 746 even-even nuclei: 1.74 MeV (QMCπ-III-T), compared to 2.17 MeV (QMCπ-III without tensor) and 2.39 MeV (QMCπ-II)
- Charge radius rms residual for 346 even-even nuclei: 0.028 fm (QMCπ-III-T), unchanged from QMCπ-III without tensor
- Nuclear matter saturation density ρ0 = 0.15 ± 0.01 fm⁻³, binding energy E0 = -15.7 ± 0.2 MeV, symmetry energy asym = 29 ± 1 MeV, incompressibility K0 = 233 ± 2 MeV
- Like-particle tensor strength α = 55.2 MeV·fm⁵, proton-neutron tensor strength β = -6.3 MeV·fm⁵ (both from QMCπ-III-T, not separately fitted)
- Ground-state deformation β2 for ¹⁰⁰Zr: 0.45 (QMCπ-III-T), 0.44 (QMCπ-III), 0.36 (FRDM), 0.36 (experiment)

## Context

This work extends the QMCπ-II model (Martinez 2019) by adding tensor terms that arise naturally from the spin-dependent part of the QMC effective interaction, unlike approaches such as Lesinski (2007), Zalewski (2008), Bender (2009), or Shi (2017) which introduce additional tensor parameters. The QMC-derived pairing interaction replaces traditional density-dependent delta interactions (DDDI) or constant delta forces, eliminating the need for separate pairing strength parameters. The results enable future studies of superheavy nuclei where the new pairing functional improves predictions for subshell closures, and the tensor terms improve ground-state bulk properties while having small effects on single-particle spectra.
