# Color superconductivity and speed of sound in the two-flavor quark-meson diquark model

> We discuss the properties of the two-flavor quark-meson diquark (QMD) model as a renormalizable low-energy model for QCD in the 2SC phase of QCD. The effective degrees of freedom are the mesons (sigma and pions), quarks, and diquarks. Some of the parameters of the model can be determined by expressing them in terms of the vacuum meson masses and the pion decay constant using the on-shell renormalization scheme. The remaining parameters are considered free, although they in principle can be calculated from QCD. The thermodynamic potential is calculated in a mean-field approximation taking only quark loops into account. In this approximation, we derive a set of renormalization group equations for the running masses and couplings. The solutions to these equations are used to improve the thermodynamic potential $Ω$ and thereby thermodynamic quantities. Four parameter sets are chosen and the phase diagram in the $\barμ$--$T$ plane is obtained (with $\barμ={1\over3}μ_B$). We also calculate the speed of sound $c_s$ as a function of $\barμ$ at vanishing temperature. For large values of $\barμ$, the speed of sound approaches the conformal limit $c_s={1\over\sqrt{3}}$ from above, in disagreement with perturbative calculations, but agreement with hard-dense-loop resummed perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The speed of sound in the two-flavor quark-meson diquark (QMD) model approaches the conformal limit \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\) from above at large baryon chemical potential, in disagreement with standard perturbative QCD calculations but in agreement with hard-dense-loop resummed perturbation theory; this result depends on the inclusion of quark-loop corrections which cause the diquark gap to approach a constant value rather than growing linearly with chemical potential.

## Method

The authors construct a renormalizable low-energy effective Lagrangian for the 2SC phase of QCD containing mesons (sigma and pions), quarks, and diquarks as degrees of freedom. They compute the thermodynamic potential in a mean-field approximation that includes only one-loop quark contributions, using dimensional regularization and the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) renormalization scheme. Parameters in the meson sector are fixed by matching to vacuum meson masses and the pion decay constant using the on-shell renormalization scheme, while the diquark-sector couplings are treated as free parameters; four parameter sets are selected for numerical study. The renormalization group equations for running masses and couplings are derived and solved to improve the thermodynamic potential, from which the phase diagram and speed of sound are obtained.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound approaches the conformal limit \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\) from above at large \(\bar{\mu}\), with the asymptotic form \(c_s^2 \approx \frac{1}{3}\left(1 + \frac{2}{3}\frac{g_{\Delta,0}^2\bar{\Delta}_0^2}{\bar{\mu}^2}\right)\).
- The diquark gap \(\bar{\Delta}_0\) at large \(\bar{\mu}\) is given by \(\bar{\Delta}_0^2 = \frac{m_q^2}{g_{\Delta,0}^2} e^{\frac{(4\pi)^2}{4g_{\Delta,0}^2} - 1}\), approaching a constant independent of \(\bar{\mu}\).
- The pressure at high density is \(p = \frac{8}{(4\pi)^2}\bar{\mu}^4 + \frac{16g_{\Delta,0}^2}{(4\pi)^2}\bar{\mu}^2\bar{\Delta}_0^2\).
- The energy density at high density is \(\epsilon = \frac{24}{(4\pi)^2}\bar{\mu}^4 + \frac{16g_{\Delta,0}^2}{(4\pi)^2}\bar{\mu}^2\bar{\Delta}_0^2\).

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group studies of the QMD model by Pawlowski (2015) and Braun (2015) to a mean-field treatment with renormalization group improvement, providing a renormalizable alternative to the nonrenormalizable NJL model commonly used for color superconductivity (see reviews by Shovkovy (2005) and Buballa (2005)). The result that the speed of sound approaches the conformal limit from above contrasts with standard perturbative QCD calculations (Freedman and McLerran (1977), Baluni (1978)) but agrees with hard-dense-loop resummed perturbation theory; this has implications for constraining the equation of state of dense matter relevant to compact stars.
