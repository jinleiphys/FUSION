# Chiral thermodynamics of nuclear matter

> The free energy and the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are calculated at finite temperature up to three loop order in the framework of in-medium chiral perturbation theory, systematically incorporating one- and two-pion exchange dynamics to this order. Effects from the two-pion exchange with explicit Δ-isobar excitation are included, as well as three-body forces. We construct the phase diagram of nuclear matter for different proton fractions x_p and investigate the dependence of nuclear matter properties on the isospin-asymmetry. A detailed study of the liquid-gas phase transition is performed. For isospin-symmetric nuclear matter we find a critical temperature of 15.1 MeV; as the isospin-asymmetry is increased, the liquid-gas coexistence region decreases until it disappears at x_p \approx 0.05, while nuclear matter becomes unbound at x_p \approx 0.12. The quadratic expansion of the free energy in the asymmetry parameter δ is a good approximation at low temperature even for large δ. An estimate of chiral four-body correlations in nuclear and neutron matter is also performed and the corrections from such four-body interactions are found to be small.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the free energy and equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature using in-medium chiral perturbation theory up to three-loop order, finding that the liquid-gas phase transition disappears at a proton fraction of approximately 0.053 and that nuclear matter becomes unbound at a proton fraction of approximately 0.12, though the authors note their framework is oversimplified at low densities where nuclear clustering occurs.

## Method

The free energy density was computed as a sum of convolution integrals of momentum-space kernels with finite-temperature nucleon distribution functions, incorporating one- and two-pion exchange dynamics, explicit Delta-isobar excitation, and three-body forces up to three-loop order. Short-distance physics was encoded in contact terms with coefficients fixed to reproduce the saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter at -16 MeV and the asymmetry energy of 34 MeV at the saturation point. The calculation was extended to finite temperature by replacing zero-temperature Fermi distributions with thermal distributions and including an anomalous Fock term from second-order one-pion exchange that vanishes at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter: 15.1 MeV
- Critical density for symmetric nuclear matter: 0.053 fm^-3
- Critical pressure for symmetric nuclear matter: 0.261 MeV fm^-3
- Critical chemical potential for symmetric nuclear matter: 914.7 MeV
- Saturation density at T=0 for symmetric nuclear matter: 0.157 fm^-3, corresponding to Fermi momentum k_F0 = 262 MeV
- Binding energy at saturation for symmetric nuclear matter: -16.0 MeV
- Compression modulus K extracted from T=0 curve: approximately 300 MeV
- Asymmetry energy at saturation density: 34.0 MeV (imposed to fix contact terms)
- Slope parameter L extracted from asymmetry energy expansion: 90.1 MeV
- Isobaric compressibility K_tau: -388 MeV
- Proton fraction at which liquid-gas coexistence disappears: approximately 0.053
- Proton fraction at which nuclear matter becomes unbound at T=0: approximately 0.12
- Critical temperature variation with parameter changes: 14.6 to 15.8 MeV (plus or minus 5 percent)

## Context

This work extends the chiral three-loop calculation of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter from Kaiser et al. (2002, 2003) to the isospin-asymmetric case, providing a systematic investigation of the phase diagram for different proton-to-neutron ratios. The results enable comparisons with empirical constraints from multifragmentation measurements that locate the critical temperature between 15 and 20 MeV (Karnaukhov) and with relativistic mean-field models (Vretenar, Cao) that give asymmetry energy values between 33 and 37 MeV, while also providing predictions for neutron-rich matter relevant to neutron star physics.
