# Universal Two-Body Spectra of Ultracold Harmonically Trapped Atoms in Two and Three Dimensions

> We consider the spectrum of two ultracold harmonically trapped atoms interacting via short-range interactions. The Green's function approach is used to unify the two and three dimensional cases. We derive criteria for the universality of the spectrum, i.e. its independence of the details of the short-range interaction. The results in three dimensions are examplified for narrow s-wave Feshbach resonances and we show how effective range corrections can modify the rearrangement of the level structure. However, this requires extremely narrow resonances or very tight traps that are not currently experimentally available. In the two-dimensional case we discuss the p-wave channel in detail and demonstrate how the non-universality of the spectrum arises within the Green's function approach. We then show that the spectrum is not particularly sensitive to the short-distance details in the case when the two-body interaction has a bound state.

# Universal Two-Body Spectra of Ultracold Harmonically Trapped Atoms in Two and Three Dimensions

## Key claim

The two-body spectrum in a harmonic trap is universal (independent of short-range interaction details) only for s-wave and p-wave channels in three dimensions and only for s-wave (m=0) in two dimensions; for higher partial waves, the spectrum depends on short-distance physics. However, observing effective range corrections to the level structure in three dimensions requires extremely narrow Feshbach resonances or very tight traps that are not currently experimentally available.

## Method

The author uses a Green's function approach within a two-channel formalism to derive the eigenspectrum of two ultracold atoms in an isotropic harmonic trap as a function of the scattering phase shift. For three dimensions, the derivation covers general angular momentum l-wave cases and relates the trap spectrum to free-particle scattering properties through the regularized Green's function. For two dimensions, the same approach is applied to derive spectra for s-wave (m=0) and p-wave (m=1) channels, using generic forms of the phase shift including effective range corrections.

## Key numbers

- For three dimensions, the universal spectrum formula is given by: Γ(3/4 + l/2 - E/2ℏω)/Γ(1/4 - l/2 - E/2ℏω) = (-1)^(l+1)/2^(2l+1) (kb)^(2l+1) cot δ(k)
- The criterion for universality for l≥2 is: (r₀/b)^(2l-3) ≫ [(2l+1)/(2l-3)]·[(2l-1)!!]²/4 · |tan δ_l(k)|/(kb)^(2l+1)
- For the 3D s-wave Feshbach model, the eigenvalue equation is: x = (1/2)(a_bg^s/b)(|r_e0^s|/b)(E/ℏω) - [(a_bg^s/b)f(E)]/[1 - (a_bg^s/b)f(E)], where x = (B-B₀)/ΔB and f(E) = √2 Γ(3/4 - E/2ℏω)/Γ(1/4 - E/2ℏω)
- For 2D s-wave, the eigenvalue equation is: γ + (1/2)ψ(-ν₀) + (r_e^s)²/b²(ν₀ + 1/2) = ln[b/a_2D^s]
- For 2D p-wave with A=0 (bound state at zero energy), the eigenvalue equation is: γ + (1/2)ψ(-ν₁) + (r_e²)/b²(ν₁ + 1) + 1/(4ν₁ + 4) = ln[b/a_2D^p]
- For typical traps, b ~ 1 μm, and the smallest values used for a_bg^s/b are 0.01

## Context

This work extends the foundational exact solution of Busch et al. (1998) for two atoms in a harmonic trap using a zero-range pseudopotential, and the Green's function approaches of Castin (2007), Idziaszek (2006), and Yip (2008). The paper unifies the two and three dimensional cases and provides criteria for when the universal spectrum breaks down for higher partial waves, which was previously discussed for d-waves by Suzuki (2009). The results enable quantitative understanding of when effective range corrections become important in narrow Feshbach resonances, relevant for experiments in microtraps such as those reported by Serwane et al. (2011), and for two-dimensional setups of current experimental interest.
