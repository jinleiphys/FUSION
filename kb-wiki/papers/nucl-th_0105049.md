# Pairing correlations. Part 1: description of odd nuclei in mean-field theories

> In order to extract informations on pairing correlations in nuclei from experimental mass differences, the different contributions to odd-even mass differences are investigated within the Skyrme HFB method. In this first paper, the description of odd nuclei within HFB is discussed since it is the key point for the understanding of the above mentioned contributions. To go from an even nucleus to an odd one, the advantage of a two steps process is demonstrated and its physical content is discussed. New results concerning time-reversal symmetry breaking in odd-nuclei are also reported. PACS: 21.10Dr; 21.10.Hw; 21.30.-x. Keywords: Mean-field theories; Pairing correlations; odd nuclei;

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a proper description of odd nuclei within the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) method requires a two-step process: first constructing a fully paired state with the correct odd particle number, then creating a quasiparticle excitation on that state, which resolves the particle-number inconsistency that arises when creating a quasiparticle directly on the even neighbor ground state.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme HFB calculations (using the SLy4 Skyrme force for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force for the particle-particle channel) along the cerium isotopic chain from 118Ce to 166Ce. Each odd nucleus was calculated twice: first as a fully paired HFB vacuum with an odd average neutron number (HFBE state), then with the fully self-consistent HFB scheme including blocking. The energy difference between these two calculations was decomposed into a polarization contribution from time-reversal symmetry breaking and a contribution from the blocking of pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The polarization energy (Epol = EHF - EHFE) in the zero-pairing limit ranged from 48 keV for 155Ce to 223 keV for 147Ce along the cerium isotopic line.
- The approximation (e_n - e_nbar)/4 for the polarization energy in the HF case showed very good agreement with the full calculation across the entire cerium isotopic chain.
- Time-odd terms of the interaction were responsible for roughly 2/3 of the time-reversal symmetry breaking effect for all cerium isotopes studied.
- The large polarization energy for 147Ce (223 keV) was associated with blocking of the down-sloping Nilsson orbital [660]1/2 originating from the 1i13/2 shell.
- Large polarization energies for 123Ce and 141Ce corresponded to the [541]1/2 blocked orbitals coming from the 2f7/2 spherical shell.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative quasiparticle creation approach introduced by Ring et al. (1980) and used by Satula and Wyss (1994), providing a formal justification for using an odd fully paired state as the reference vacuum rather than the even neighbor ground state. The two-step procedure builds on the density operator formalism developed by Balian, Flocard, and Veneroni (1985) for fermionic superconducting systems at finite temperature, and was previously used by Bendjellal et al. (1992) as a definition of the smooth part of the microscopic binding energy in studies of odd-even mass staggering. The results enable a clean decomposition of the different physical contributions to odd-even mass differences, which is essential for extracting pairing correlations from experimental mass data.
