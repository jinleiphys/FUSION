# Semiclassical approximation to the Hartree-Fock method in finite nuclei

> In this paper we review the semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi theory for describing the ground-state properties of nuclei. The binding energies calculated in this approach do not contain shell effects and, in this sense, they are analogous to those obtained from the mass formula. We discuss some techniques for incorporating the shell effects which are missing in the semiclassical calculation, such as the so-called expectation value method and the Kohn-Sham scheme. We present numerical applications for effective zero-range Skyrme forces and finite-range Gogny forces.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) semiclassical approximation to the Hartree-Fock method, showing that the semiclassical binding energies do not contain shell effects and are analogous to those from the mass formula; the shell effects can be incorporated perturbatively through techniques such as the expectation value method or the Kohn-Sham scheme, with the caveat that the ETF approximation at order \(\hbar^2\) overbinds nuclei and gives smaller root-mean-square radii than the full quantal Hartree-Fock results.

## Method

The authors derive the ETF energy functional up to \(\hbar^2\) order for a non-local Hartree-Fock potential, starting from the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion of the one-body density matrix and expressing the kinetic energy, exchange energy, and spin-orbit densities in terms of the local particle density and its gradients. They then solve the resulting Euler-Lagrange equations variationally to obtain semiclassical densities and binding energies for zero-range Skyrme forces (using the SLy4 parametrization) and finite-range Gogny forces (using the D1 parametrization), and compare these results with full quantal Hartree-Fock calculations. For the Gogny force, they also implement a Kohn-Sham scheme where the \(\hbar^2\) exchange energy density is used as the exchange-correlation functional.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical ETF approach previously developed by Brack and Quentin (1980s) and by Centelles et al. (1990s) for Skyrme forces, and applies it to finite-range Gogny forces following the density-matrix method of Soubbotin and Viñas (2000s). The paper enables future calculations where shell effects can be added perturbatively to the semiclassical ETF results, either through the Strutinsky method or the expectation value method, providing a computationally simpler alternative to full Hartree-Fock calculations for heavy nuclei.
