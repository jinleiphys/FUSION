# First Measurement of the $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$ Transition Strength in $^7$Be: Testing Ab Initio Predictions for $A=7$ Nuclei

> Electromagnetic observables are able to give insight into collective and emergent features in nuclei, including nuclear clustering. These observables also provide strong constraints for ab initio theory, but comparison of these observables between theory and experiment can be difficult due to the lack of convergence for relevant calculated values, such as $E2$ transition strengths. By comparing the ratios of $E2$ transition strengths for mirror transitions, we find that a wide range of ab initio calculations give robust and consistent predictions for this ratio. To experimentally test the validity of these ab initio predictions, we performed a Coulomb excitation experiment to measure the $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$ transition strength in $^7$Be for the first time. A $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$ value of $26(6)(3) \, e^2 \mathrm{fm}^4$ was deduced from the measured Coulomb excitation cross section. This result is used with the experimentally known $^7$Li $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$ value to provide an experimental ratio to compare with the ab initio predictions. Our experimental value is consistent with the theoretical ratios within $1 σ$ uncertainty, giving experimental support for the value of these ratios. Further work in both theory and experiment can give insight into the robustness of these ratios and their physical meaning.

## Key claim

The ratio of $E2$ transition strengths between mirror nuclei $^7$Be and $^7$Li provides a robust and well-converged test of ab initio nuclear theory, even though the individual absolute $E2$ strengths are not converged in no-core shell model calculations. The experimentally measured ratio is consistent with theoretical predictions within $1\sigma$ uncertainty, but the result carries significant systematic uncertainty from the $E1$ dipole polarizability contribution and limited statistics.

## Method

A Coulomb excitation experiment was performed using a radioactive $^7$Be beam produced via the $^6$Li($^2$H,n)$^7$Be reaction at the University of Notre Dame Nuclear Science Laboratory, with the beam separated using the TwinSol superconducting solenoid system. The $^7$Be beam at 31.3(10) MeV was scattered off a 1 $\mu$m-thick Au foil at 77% of the Coulomb barrier, and de-excitation $\gamma$ rays from the $1/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-$ transition were detected in coincidence with scattered $^7$Be ions using six HPGe clover detectors and an annular Si detector. The $B(E2)$ value was extracted using the Winther-De Boer Coulomb excitation code combined with Geant4 simulations to account for beam spot size and offset effects.

## Key numbers

- $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$ in $^7$Be: $26(6)_{\mathrm{stat}}(3)_{\mathrm{syst}} \, e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$, deduced from Coulomb excitation cross section
- $^7$Be beam energy: 31.3(10) MeV, at 77% of the Coulomb barrier for Au target
- $\gamma$-ray peak at 431 keV with FWHM of 10 keV, yielding 30(6) counts
- Beam rate on target: $8.8(4) \times 10^4$ particles per second
- HPGe detector array efficiency: 1.4% at 500 keV; energy resolution 2.8 keV at 1408 keV
- NCSM ratio predictions for $^7$Be/$^7$Li $B(E2)$: 3.04 (JISP16), 3.06 (Daejeon16), 3.10 (LENPIC N$^2$LO), 3.13 (EM N$^3$LO NCSM), 2.81 (EM N$^3$LO NCSMC)
- GFMC predictions for absolute $B(E2)$ in $^7$Li: 8.09(17) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL2 Type I), 8.15(20) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL2 Type II), 7.81(45) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL7)
- GFMC predictions for absolute $B(E2)$ in $^7$Be: 25.6(3) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL2 Type I), 27.5(8) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL2 Type II), 22.2(11) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (AV18+IL7)
- Known $^7$Li $B(E2; 3/2^- \rightarrow 1/2^-)$: 8.3(5) $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio studies of $E2$ transition strengths in light nuclei, particularly the $A=10$ system where quantum Monte Carlo calculations could not consistently reproduce $E2$ strengths across isobars (McCutchan 2012). The use of mirror-nuclei ratios was motivated by earlier NCSM observations that $E2$ strength ratios within rotational bands converge rapidly even when absolute values do not (Caprio 2015). The measurement enables direct experimental testing of ab initio predictions from multiple approaches including GFMC (Pervin 2007, Pastore 2013) and NCSM with various interactions (JISP16, Daejeon16, LENPIC, EM N$^3$LO), and opens the door for further studies of the physical meaning and robustness of these ratios across other mirror systems.
