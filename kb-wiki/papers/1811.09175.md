# Asymptotic theory of charged particle transfer reactions at low energies and nuclear astrophysics

> A new asymptotic theory is proposed for the peripheral sub- and above-barrier transfer $A$($x$, $y$)$B$ reaction within the three-body ($A$, $a$ and $y$) model, where $ x$= $y$ + $a$, $B$= $A$ + $a$ and $ a$ is a transferred particle. In the asymptotic theory, the allowance of the contribution of the three-body ($A$, $a$ and $y$) Coulomb dynamics of the transfer mechanism to the peripheral partial amplitudes for the partial wave $l_i>>$ 1 and of the Coulomb-nuclear distorted effects in the entrance and exit channels is done in a correct manner within the framework of the dispersion theory and the conventional distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), respectively. It is shown that the proposed asymptotic theory makes it possible to test the accuracy of taking into account the the three-body Coulomb dynamics of the transfer mechanism in the modified DWBA. The results of the analysis of the differential cross sections of the specific proton and triton transfer reactions at above- and sub-barrier energies are presented. New estimates and their uncertainties are obtained for values of the asymptotic normalization coefficients for ${\rm{^9Be}} \,+p \to\,{\rm{^{10}B}}$, ${\rm{^{11}B}}\,+\,p\to\,{\rm{^{12}C}}$, ${\rm{^{16}O}} \,+\,p \to\, {\rm{^{17}F}}$ and ${\rm{^{19}F}}\to\, {\rm{^{16}O}} \,+\,t$ as well as for the direct astrophysical $S$ factors at stellar energy of the radiative capture ${\rm{^9Be(}}p,\,γ{\rm{)^{10}B}} $, ${\rm{^{11}B(}}p,\,γ{\rm{)^{12}C}} $ and ${\rm{^{16}O(}}p,\,γ{\rm{)^{17}F}}$ reactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new asymptotic theory for peripheral charged-particle transfer reactions that correctly accounts for the three-body Coulomb dynamics of the transfer mechanism, which the modified distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) may handle inaccurately, particularly for weakly bound residual nuclei of astrophysical interest.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the reaction amplitude and differential cross section of peripheral A(x,y)B transfer reactions within a hybrid framework combining the DWBA approach and the dispersion peripheral model. They separate the singular part of the amplitude near the nearest singularity at cosθ = ξ, which governs the peripheral partial amplitudes for partial waves l_i >> 1, and incorporate the three-body Coulomb dynamics through the Coulomb Green's function operator G_C while treating Coulomb-nuclear distortions in the entrance and exit channels via the optical model. The theory is applied to analyze differential cross sections of specific proton and triton transfer reactions at above- and sub-barrier energies.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic theory previously developed for peripheral neutron transfer reactions induced by heavy ions at above-barrier energies (KMYa1988) to the more complicated case of charged particle transfer reactions where three-body Coulomb dynamics must be correctly treated. The theory addresses limitations of the modified DWBA approach (Ar96, Mukh2) which, as shown in earlier work (Yarm2013, Mukh10, Yar97, Igam072), may yield inaccurate ANC values for weakly bound states of astrophysical interest because the first-order perturbation treatment of the Coulomb polarization operator is not guaranteed to be small. The proposed asymptotic theory enables testing the accuracy of three-body Coulomb dynamics treatment in modified DWBA and can be applied to strong sub-barrier transfer reactions where peripheral partial waves are strongly suppressed.
