# From QCD to nuclear matter saturation

> We discuss a relativistic chiral theory of nuclear matter with $σ$ and $ω$ exchange using a formulation of the $σ$ model in which all the chiral constraints are automatically fulfilled. We establish a relation between the nuclear response to the scalar field and the QCD one which includes the nucleonic parts. It allows a comparison between nuclear and QCD information. Going beyond the mean field approach we introduce the effects of the pion loops supplemented by the short-range interaction. The corresponding Landau-Migdal parameters are taken from spin-isospin physics results. The parameters linked to the scalar meson exchange are extracted from lattice QCD results. These inputs lead to a reasonable description of the saturation properties, illustrating the link between QCD and nuclear physics. We also derive from the corresponding equation of state the density dependence of the quark condensate and of the QCD susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a direct, model-independent link between QCD quantities (quark condensate, scalar susceptibility) and the parameters governing nuclear attraction in the sigma-omega model, but only after introducing a phenomenological nucleon scalar response (kappa_NS) that cancels the overly large tadpole contribution which otherwise prevents nuclear matter saturation.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral theory of nuclear matter using a nonlinear representation of the sigma model with sigma and omega exchange, incorporating pion loops and short-range interactions parameterized by Landau-Migdal parameters taken from spin-isospin physics (Ichimura et al., 2006). They extract the scalar meson exchange parameters from lattice QCD results on the nucleon mass evolution with quark mass, and introduce a nucleon scalar response term (kappa_NS) to cancel the excessive tadpole contribution. The equation of state is then used to derive the density dependence of the quark condensate and QCD susceptibilities via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key numbers

- Sigma mass m_sigma = 850 MeV (adjusted from lattice value of 800 MeV), corresponding to non-pionic sigma commutator sigma_N^(non-pion) = 26 MeV
- Non-pionic sigma commutator from lattice expansion: sigma_N^(non-pion) = 29 MeV, dominated by the a_2 term (a_2 = 1.5 GeV^{-1})
- Lattice expansion coefficient a_4 = -0.5 GeV^{-3}; the sigma model without nucleon response gives a_4 = -3.4 GeV^{-3}, showing the need for cancellation
- Dimensionless nucleon scalar response parameter C = 0.985 (fitted to saturation), compared to lattice-indicated value C = 1.25
- Pion-nucleon form factor cutoff Lambda = 7 m_pi, giving pionic sigma commutator contribution of 22 MeV; total sigma_N = 51 MeV
- Landau-Migdal parameters from Ichimura et al. (2006): g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NDelta = 0.3, g'_DeltaDelta = 0.5
- Correlation energy per particle: -17.4 MeV (transverse channel -9.5 MeV, longitudinal channel -7.9 MeV)
- Omega-nucleon coupling constant g_omega = 8.0 (free parameter)
- Scalar coupling constant g_S = M_N/f_pi = 10

## Context

This work extends the relativistic sigma-omega model of Walecka and Serot (1986) by incorporating chiral constraints and QCD information, building on the authors' previous work (Ericson, Chanfray, 2002) that identified the scalar mean field with the chiral singlet field. It addresses the known failure of the linear sigma model to produce saturation (Kerman, Miller, 1974) by introducing a nucleon scalar response analogous to the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988). The approach enables a consistent description of both nuclear matter saturation properties and QCD quantities (quark condensate, scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities), and provides a framework for extracting nucleon structure information from lattice QCD data on the nucleon mass evolution with quark mass (Thomas et al., 2004).
