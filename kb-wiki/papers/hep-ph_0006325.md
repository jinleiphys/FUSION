# Baryon Number Flow in High-Energy Collisions

> It is not obvious which partons in the proton carry its baryon number (BN). We present arguments that BN is associated with a specific topology of gluonic fields, rather than with the valence quarks. The BN distribution is easily confused with the difference between the quark and antiquark distributions. We argue, however, that they have quite different x-dependences. The distribution of BN asymmetry distribution is nearly constant at small x while q(x)-\bar q(x) \propto \sqrt{x}. This constancy of BN produces energy independence of the \bar pp annihilation cross section at high energies. Recent measurement of the baryon asymmetry at small x at HERA confirms this expectation. The BN asymmetry at mid-rapidities in heavy ion collisions is substantially enhanced by multiple interactions, as has been observed in recent experiments at the SPS. The same gluonic mechanism of BN stopping increases the production rate for cascade hyperons in a good accord with data. We expect nearly the same as at SPS amount of BN stopped in higher energy collisions at RHIC and LHC, which is, however, spread ove larger rapidity intervals.

# Baryon Number Flow in High-Energy Collisions

## Key claim

The baryon number of a proton is carried by a specific topology of gluonic fields (a string junction) rather than by valence quarks, and this gluonic mechanism produces a BN distribution that is nearly constant at small x (proportional to 1/x) rather than following the valence quark distribution which vanishes as sqrt(x). The authors argue that this explains the energy independence of the antiproton-proton annihilation cross section at high energies and accounts for baryon stopping observed in heavy ion collisions at the SPS.

## Method

The authors analyze the x-dependence of baryon number asymmetry in high-energy collisions using both theoretical arguments from QCD and comparisons with experimental data. They examine the string junction model from dual topological theories and perturbative QCD calculations of gluon exchange in color-decuplet states, and compare predictions for baryon asymmetry in gamma-proton collisions at HERA, hyperon production in pion-nucleus collisions at E791, and baryon stopping in lead-lead collisions at the SPS NA49 experiment.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic antiproton-proton annihilation cross section is predicted to be approximately 1-2 mb from both nonperturbative string junction and perturbative QCD approaches
- Analysis of multiplicity distributions in pp and pbar-p collisions yields sigma_ann(pbar-p) = 1.5 ± 0.1 mb, confirmed as energy independent across lab energies from 10 to 1480 GeV
- The predicted baryon asymmetry for meson-nucleon collisions is A_BN = 7%, using sigma_ann = 1.5 mb and sigma_in(MN) = 20 mb
- H1 Collaboration at HERA measured A_BN(x=10^-3) = 8 ± 1 ± 2.5% in gamma-p interactions, in good agreement with the predicted value
- The ratio of cascade hyperon to non-strange baryon stopping in central Pb-Pb collisions at SPS is R(Xi/N) = 0.063 ± 0.006 (NA49 experiment)
- The pure gluonic mechanism predicts R(Xi/N) ≈ 0.067, while mechanisms associating BN with valence quarks predict R(Xi/N) ≈ 0.022

## Context

This work extends the string junction concept originally proposed by Rossi and Veneziano (1970s) and developed in dual topological theories and Regge theory, arguing that BN is associated with gluonic field topology rather than valence quarks. The paper contradicts the naive expectation that valence quarks carry BN and that baryon number should be swept to fragmentation regions in high-energy collisions. The results enable predictions for baryon stopping at RHIC and LHC, where the authors expect nearly the same amount of BN stopping as at SPS but spread over larger rapidity intervals. The analysis of hyperon production ratios provides a sensitive test to distinguish between the gluonic string junction mechanism and preasymptotic quark-exchange mechanisms.
