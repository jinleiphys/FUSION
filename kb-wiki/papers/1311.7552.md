# Identification of non-ordinary mesons from the dispersive connection between their poles and their Regge trajectories: the f0(500) resonance

> We show how the Regge trajectory of a resonance can be obtained from its pole in a scattering process and analytic constraints in the complex angular momentum plane. The method is suited for resonances that dominate an elastic scattering amplitude. In particular, from the rho(770) resonance pole in pion-pion scattering, we obtain its linear Regge trajectory, characteristic of ordinary quark-antiquark states. In contrast, the f0(500) pole -the sigma meson- which dominates scalar isoscalar pion-pion scattering, yields a non-linear trajectory with a much smaller slope at the f0(500) mass. Conversely, imposing a linear Regge trajectory for the f0(500), with a slope of typical size, yields an elastic amplitude at odds with the data. This provides strong support for the non-ordinary nature of the sigma meson.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Regge trajectory of a resonance can be obtained from its pole in a scattering process using analytic constraints in the complex angular momentum plane, and that the resulting trajectory for the f0(500) (sigma meson) is nonlinear with a slope about two orders of magnitude smaller than that of the rho(770), providing strong support for the non-ordinary nature of the sigma meson. The hedge is that the method is suited only for resonances that dominate an elastic scattering amplitude, and the authors cannot specify what mechanism produces the f0(500) beyond noting it is different from ordinary quark-antiquark states.

## Method

The authors use analytic S-matrix theory to derive coupled dispersion relations for the Regge trajectory alpha(s) and residue beta(s) from a single Regge pole dominating an elastic partial wave. They solve these integral equations iteratively, fitting only the pole position and residue of the rho(770) and f0(500) resonances in pion-pion scattering, with the f0(500) case modified to include the Adler zero required by chiral symmetry. The input pole parameters are taken from a model-independent dispersive analysis of pi-pi scattering data by Garcia-Martin et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- rho(770) pole position: sqrt(s_rho) = 763.7 (+1.7/-1.5) - i 73.2 (+1.0/-1.1) MeV; residue |g_rho| = 6.01 (+0.04/-0.07)
- f0(500) pole position: sqrt(s_sigma) = 457 (+14/-13) - i 279 (+11/-7) MeV; residue |g_sigma| = 3.59 (+0.11/-0.13) GeV
- rho(770) trajectory intercept: alpha_rho(0) = 0.520 +/- 0.002
- rho(770) trajectory slope: alpha'_rho = 0.902 +/- 0.004 GeV^{-2}
- f0(500) trajectory intercept: alpha_sigma(0) = -0.090 (+0.004/-0.012)
- f0(500) trajectory slope: alpha'_sigma = 0.002 (+0.050/-0.001) GeV^{-2}
- Fitted rho(770) pole from the method: sqrt(s_rho) = 762.7 - i 73.5 MeV, |g_rho| = 5.99
- Fitted f0(500) pole from the method: sqrt(s_sigma) = 461 - i 281 MeV, |g_sigma| = 3.51 GeV

## Context

This work extends the constrained Regge-pole amplitude formalism of Chu and collaborators (1969) to extract Regge trajectories from single resonance poles in elastic scattering. It directly addresses the longstanding debate about the nature of the f0(500) (sigma meson), which previous studies by Jaffe (1976) and Pelaez (2006) suggested may not be an ordinary quark-antiquark meson, and which Anisovich et al. (2000) excluded from linear trajectory fits because it does not fit well into that classification. The method enables future studies of other controversial resonances such as the K*(892) vector and K(800) (kappa) scalar, as well as investigations of the N_c or quark mass dependence of Regge trajectories.
