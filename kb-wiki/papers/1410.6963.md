# Electromagnetic Polarizabilities of Mesons

> The Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) has been very successful in describing low-energy hadronic properties in the non-perturbative regime of Quantum Chromodynamics. The results of ChPT, many of which are currently under active experimental investigation, provide stringent predictions of many fundamental properties of hadrons, including quantities such as electromagnetic polarizabilities. Yet, even for the simplest hadronic system, a pion, we still have a broad spectrum of polarizability measurements (MARK II, VENUS, ALEPH, TPC/2g, CELLO, Belle, Crystal Ball). The meson polarizability can be accessed through Compton scattering, so we can measure it through Primakoff reaction. This paper will provide an analysis of the CHPT predictions of the SU(3) meson electromagnetic polarizabilities and outline their relationship to the Primakoff cross section at the kinematics relevant to the planned JLab experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to the case for baryons, SU(3) meson electric and magnetic polarizabilities exhibit strong energy dependence with similar slopes across all mesons, and that the photon fusion cross section for charged kaons is highly sensitive to the choice of static versus dynamic polarizability, which could enable model discrimination.

## Method

The authors compute Compton structure functions A(s,t) and B(s,t) using the Computational Hadronic Model (CHM) for the SU(3) octet of mesons, extending to lightest vector mesons. They then extract dynamical electric and magnetic polarizabilities from these structure functions via the relations α(s,t) = -(1/8πm)(A + (s-3m²)/t B) and β(s,t) = (1/8πm)(A + (s-m²)/t B). These polarizabilities are inserted into the expression for the photon fusion cross section σ(γγ → P P̅) to study the impact on Primakoff two-meson photo-production.

## Key numbers

* Proton electric polarizability: α_p = (11.2 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³; magnetic polarizability: β_p = (2.5 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from PDG compilation).
* Neutron electric polarizability: α_n = (11.6 ± 1.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³; magnetic polarizability: β_n = (3.7 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from PDG compilation).
* Meson lifetimes range from 10⁻¹⁷ to 10⁻⁸ seconds.
* The photon fusion cross section σ(γγ → P P̅) is evaluated for |cos θ| < 0.6, with results shown for invariant masses of the produced meson pair in GeV.

## Context

This work extends Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) calculations of meson polarizabilities, which were completed for pions up to order O(p⁶) by Gasser et al. (2006) and for kaons up to order O(p⁴) by Guerrero (1999). The authors use their Computational Hadronic Model to update these calculations for the full SU(3) octet and to study the energy dependence of polarizabilities and their impact on cross sections relevant to the planned GlueX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory. The paper enables future extraction of charged kaon polarizability from measurements of the γγ → K⁺K⁻ cross section, which could test effective models of low-energy QCD.
