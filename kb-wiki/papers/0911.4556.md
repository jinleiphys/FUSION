# A lattice based equation of state and $ϕ$ meson production in $\sqrt{s}$=6-200 GeV Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions

> In a boost-invariant hydrodynamic model, we have analyzed $ϕ$ meson production in Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions, in the centre of mass energy range $\sqrt{s}$= 6-200 GeV. Hydrodynamic evolution is governed by a lattice based equation of state with a confinement-deconfinement {\em cross over} at $T_{co}$=196 MeV. We also look for the threshold energy above which the confined matter undergoes a deconfinement transition. Analysis indicate that above a threshold energy, ${\sqrt{s}}_{th}$=$13.65 \pm 3.06$ GeV, the fluid medium crosses over from a confined phase to a deconfined phase.

# A lattice based equation of state and φ meson production in √s=6-200 GeV Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions

## Key claim

Ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice-based equation of state featuring a confinement-deconfinement cross-over at T_co=196 MeV can reasonably explain φ meson production across √s=6-200 GeV, but only if the fluid crosses over from confined to deconfined phase above a threshold energy of √s_th=13.65±3.06 GeV; the analysis assumes boost-invariance and zero baryon density even at lower SPS energies where these approximations are known to be violated.

## Method

The authors solved boost-invariant (2+1D) ideal hydrodynamic equations using the AZHYDRO code, with initial energy density at τ_i=0.6 fm distributed as a linear combination of participant and collision number profiles from a Glauber model. The equation of state combined a hadronic resonance gas (all resonances below 2.5 GeV) at low temperature with a parameterization of lattice QCD entropy density from Cheng et al. (2007), joined by a hyperbolic tangent switching function centered at T_co=196 MeV. φ meson transverse momentum spectra from NA49 (Pb+Pb at √s=6.3-17.3 GeV) and STAR (Au+Au at √s=62, 130, 200 GeV) were fitted by varying the central energy density ε₀, with freeze-out at T_F=150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cross-over temperature: T_co=196 MeV, from lattice simulation of Cheng et al. (2007)
- Threshold energy for deconfinement: √s_th=13.65±3.06 GeV, extracted from a tanh fit to the ratio ⟨N⟩/(0.5·N_part·ΔY·T³)
- Transition width: Δ√s_th=11.28±5.93 GeV, indicating a sharp transition over a narrow energy range
- Central energy densities fitted: from ε₀=1.32 GeV/fm³ (Pb+Pb at √s=6.3 GeV) to ε₀=31.2 GeV/fm³ (Au+Au at √s=200 GeV)
- Ratio of experimental to predicted φ multiplicity: ranges from 2.39±1.43 (√s=6.3 GeV) down to 1.03±0.09 (√s=200 GeV)
- Ratio of experimental to predicted mean p_T: ranges from 1.73±0.12 (√s=7.6 GeV) to 0.91±0.06 (√s=200 GeV)
- Degeneracy increase factor: the ratio ⟨N⟩/(N_part·ΔY·T³) increases by a factor of ~4 from low SPS to RHIC energy, matching the ~4.5 increase in s/T³ in the lattice+HRG EOS over 150-400 MeV

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic analysis of φ meson production from RHIC energies (STAR collaboration, 2007-2008) down to SPS energies (NA49 collaboration, Alt 2008), covering the full range √s=6-200 GeV. It contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic calculations using first-order phase transition equations of state, and with the work of Huovinen (2005) who found that a cross-over EOS performed worse than first-order EOS for elliptic flow. The extracted threshold energy near the top SPS energy aligns with the first evidence for QGP formation reported by Heinz (2000). The analysis enables future RHIC energy scan programs (Caines 2009, Odyniec 2008) to narrow the threshold uncertainty by filling the gap between √s=17.3 and 62 GeV. The authors note that using a finite baryon density EOS would lower the cross-over temperature and sharpen the transition, but such lattice simulations were not yet available for application.
