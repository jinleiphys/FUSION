# Nuclear Shape Fluctuations in Fermi-Liquid Drop Model

> Within the nuclear Fermi-liquid drop model, quantum and thermal fluctuations are considered by use of the Landau-Vlasov-Langevin equation. The spectral correlation function of the nuclear surface fluctuations is evaluated in a simple model of an incompressible and irrotational Fermi liquid. The dependence of the spectral correlation function on the dynamical Fermi-surface distortion is established. The temperature at which the eigenvibrations become overdamped is calculated. It is shown that, for realistic values of the relaxation time parameter and in the high temperature regime, there is a particular eigenmode of the Fermi liquid drop where the restoring force is exclusively due to the dynamical Fermi-surface distortion.

## Key claim

Within the nuclear Fermi-liquid drop model, the spectral correlation function of nuclear surface fluctuations depends on dynamical Fermi-surface distortion, and for realistic relaxation time parameters in the high temperature regime there exists a particular eigenmode where the restoring force is exclusively due to this dynamical Fermi-surface distortion; however, this mode is damped too strongly to cause a visible peak in the fluctuation spectrum for collision parameters below 25 MeV.

## Method

The authors derived a Langevin equation for nuclear shape variables starting from the Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation with a random force, incorporating dynamical Fermi-surface distortion up to multipolarity l=2. They adopted a collision integral that includes memory effects through a frequency-dependent relaxation time, combining contributions from two-body interparticle collisions and one-body dissipation from nucleon collisions with the moving nuclear surface. The spectral correlation function for shape fluctuations was then evaluated using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem for an incompressible and irrotational Fermi liquid drop with a sharp boundary.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature where the liquid drop stiffness coefficient vanishes for quadrupole deformation (L=2) in 208Pb is T_in^(LD) = 7.72 MeV.
- The limiting temperature T_lim where eigenexcitations become overdamped is 9.5 MeV for beta = 3.4 MeV.
- For beta larger than about 2.3 MeV, the transition between underdamped and overdamped modes occurs in the presence of an unstable mode.
- The collisional relaxation time parameter beta ranges from 2.4 to 19.8 MeV based on different estimates; specific values used include beta = 1.26 MeV (lower limit), 3.4 MeV, and 9.2 MeV.
- The one-body dissipation parameter lambda/r_0 = 3.011 for beta = 3.4 MeV and lambda/r_0 = 3.551 for beta = 9.2 MeV, fixed by fitting the experimental half-width of the giant quadrupole resonance in cold 208Pb.
- The surface energy coefficient b_S = 17.2 * ((T_C^2 - T^2)/(T_C^2 + T^2))^(5/4) MeV with critical temperature T_C = 18 MeV; the Coulomb coefficient b_C = 0.7 * (1 - x_C T^2) MeV with x_C = 0.76 * 10^(-3) MeV^(-2).
- The nuclear radius parameter r_0 = 1.12 fm.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear Fermi-liquid drop model of Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1959) by incorporating quantum and thermal fluctuations through the Landau-Vlasov-Langevin equation, building on prior treatments of nuclear viscosity and relaxation by Landau and Pomeranchuk (1958) and Kolomietz et al. (1995). The approach enables future determination of model parameters from comparison with experimental gamma-ray spectra, and shows that in a finite nuclear Fermi-liquid the first-sound regime is not reached; instead, eigenmotion becomes overdamped at a temperature T_lim below the instability temperature T_in^(LD), contradicting expectations from infinite matter treatments.
