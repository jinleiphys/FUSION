# Influence of Impact Parameter Fluctuations on Transverse Momentum Fluctuations

> The preliminary NA49 results on the energy dependence of transverse momentum fluctuations over the whole SPS energy range exhibit an unexpected effect. The $Φ_{p_{T}}$ fluctuation measure - used by the NA49 experiment - manifests a different behavior for different charge combinations. Whereas the $Φ_{p_{T}}$ is consistent with zero and independent of energy for negatively charged particles, it significantly increases for both all charged and positively charged particles at lower SPS energies. The string-hadronic model UrQMD is applied here to explain this effect. The UrQMD simulations show that the number of protons is strongly correlated with impact parameter and that the event-by-event impact parameter fluctuations are responsible for the event-by-event transverse momentum fluctuations of positively charged and all charged particles where protons are included. The observations presented in this article are important for all experiments measuring event-by-event fluctuations, especially for those using all charged particles in the analysis. The results can be also crucial for detectors with acceptances extending to the beam/target spectator domains.

## Key claim

The increased transverse momentum fluctuations observed by NA49 for positively charged particles at lower SPS energies are not a signature of new physics (such as the QCD critical point) but instead arise from a trivial correlation between the number of protons in the forward hemisphere and the number of protons near the production region, driven by event-by-event impact parameter fluctuations within the fixed centrality bin.

## Method

The author analyzed preliminary NA49 data on the $\Phi_{p_{T}}$ fluctuation measure for central Pb+Pb collisions at five SPS energies (20A, 30A, 40A, 80A, and 158A GeV) and compared them to events generated by the UrQMD string-hadronic transport model with identical kinematic and acceptance restrictions (forward rapidity $1.1 < y^*_{\pi} < 2.6$, transverse momentum $0.005 < p_T < 1.5$ GeV/c, and the same azimuthal acceptance curves). Within the UrQMD model, the particle composition was examined separately for different species, and the dependence of $\Phi_{p_{T}}$ on centrality selection and on rapidity cuts was studied to isolate the role of protons and impact parameter fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The NA49 centrality selection for all five energies was the most central 7.2% of the total inelastic cross section, corresponding to an impact parameter range $0 < b < 4.35$ fm in UrQMD.
- Within the NA49 forward-rapidity acceptance, the fraction of protons among positively charged particles was 48.7% at 20A GeV and 20.6% at 158A GeV; the total multiplicity of final-state protons remained nearly constant between 11.1 and 13.2 across all five energies.
- For minimum bias 20A GeV UrQMD events at `4$\pi$' acceptance, $\Phi_{p_{T}}$ for protons only was $1506 \pm 5.0$ MeV/c, while for pions ($\pi^{+}$ and $\pi^{-}$) it was $-0.3 \pm 1.4$ MeV/c.
- For the 7.2% most central 20A GeV UrQMD events at `4$\pi$' acceptance, $\Phi_{p_{T}}$ for protons only was approximately 130 MeV/c; this value was reduced to near zero when the centrality was restricted to the most central 1% of interactions.
- The beam rapidity in the center-of-mass system for 20A GeV interactions was $y^{*}_{beam}=1.88$.

## Context

This work extends the NA49 study of system-size dependence of transverse momentum fluctuations (Grebieszkow and NA49 Collaboration, 2006) by examining the energy dependence, which revealed an unexpected charge-sign asymmetry. The analysis uses the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999) to demonstrate that the effect is a trivial consequence of impact parameter fluctuations rather than a signal of the QCD critical point as proposed by theoretical arguments (Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, 1998). The results imply that experiments measuring event-by-event fluctuations, particularly those using all charged particles or with acceptance extending to spectator domains, must apply centrality restrictions or rapidity cuts to avoid this trivial correlation; the author notes that RHIC experiments using only midrapidity are largely insensitive to this effect.
