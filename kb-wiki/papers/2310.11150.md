# $R$-dependence of jet observables with JEWEL+v-USPhydro

> The $R$-dependence of jet observables provides a new tool in understanding the interplay between the jet energy-loss mechanism and medium response in heavy-ion collisions. This work applies the Monte Carlo events generator JEWEL and PYTHIA, coupled with $\rm T_{R}ENTo$ initial conditions and the state-of-the-art (2+1)D v-USPhydro, for the simulation of jet distributions and substructure observables for lead-lead collisions at LHC energy scales. We present the jet nuclear modification $R_{AA}$ and anisotropic flow coefficients $v_{n=2,3}$ varying the jet cone radius $R$, in the context of anti-$k_T$ jets, in addition to leading subjet fragmentation. The calculations indicate the impacts of the hydrodynamic evolution and weakly-coupled medium response, given by recoils, on the distributions. Results are compared to experimental data in a wide range of jet $p_T$ and collision centrality, and displayed along large jets ($R \ge 0.6$) predictions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that coupling JEWEL with a realistic (2+1)D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic medium (v-USPhydro) significantly improves the description of the jet cone radius dependence of jet observables in lead-lead collisions, but only when recoiling scattering centers are included for large jets (R >= 0.6); for smaller jets (R <= 0.4) the inclusion of recoils is not imperative.

## Method

The authors coupled the JEWEL 2.2.0 Monte Carlo event generator (based on the BDMPS-Z formalism for parton showers and medium interactions) with T_R_ENTo initial conditions and the (2+1)D v-USPhydro viscous hydrodynamic code, replacing JEWEL's original smooth Glauber+Bjorken medium description. They computed the nuclear modification factor R_AA, elliptic flow v_2, triangular flow v_3, and leading subjet fragmentation distributions for anti-k_T jets in PbPb collisions at LHC energies, varying the jet cone radius R and comparing results with and without recoiling scattering centers (using the 4MomSub procedure). Each model variant (with and without recoils) was independently tuned to ATLAS central R_AA results to remove predictability biases.

## Key numbers

- R_AA ratios for 10-30% centrality at 5.02 TeV: the model with recoils describes the experimental R trend from CMS (2021) for jet cone radii up to R = 0.6, while the original JEWEL model could not reproduce the R-dependence for large jets (R >= 0.6)
- v_2{2} for 30-50% centrality: shows the exact opposite R-dependence behavior from R_AA, with no further differences from the inclusion of recoils
- v_3{2} for 0-5% central collisions: both model variants (with and without recoils) yield values lower than ATLAS data (2021); v_3{2} with recoils is approximately zero
- Leading subjet fragmentation ratio between subjet radii r=0.1 and r=0.2 for 0-10% central PbPb collisions: the model describes ALICE data (2022) within experimental uncertainties, with little modification from the addition of recoils

## Context

This work extends the JEWEL+hydrodynamic coupling previously developed by Barreto (2022) and Canedo (2020), addressing the inability of JEWEL's original simplistic medium to reproduce R-dependent R_AA results for large jets (R >= 0.6) as measured by ALICE (2023) and CMS (2021). The results enable future studies of missing effects in the jet-medium interplay, particularly the decorrelation between soft and jet symmetry planes indicated by the v_3{2} comparison to ATLAS data (2021), and provide publicly available code for further phenomenological investigations of jet quenching and medium response.
