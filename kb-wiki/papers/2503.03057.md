# Interacting mesons as degrees of freedom in a chiral model

> We study the equation of state of hot and dense hadronic matter using an extended Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model framework where the addition is the inclusion of interactions of thermally excited mesons. This is implemented by calculating the in-medium masses of pseudoscalar and vector mesons, obtained through the explicit chiral symmetry-breaking and vector interaction terms in the Lagrangian, respectively, prior to applying the mean-field approximation. As a result, the in-medium meson contributions generate a feedback term to the CMF's equations of motion, which then modifies the equation of state. With this improvement, we quantify the effect on the equation of state of strongly interacting matter through comparisons with state-of-the-art lattice QCD results and other hadronic models like the Hadron Resonance Gas model. We find that the results of the updated hadronic CMF model with an improved meson description (mCMF) provide a better agreement with lattice-QCD data for thermodynamic state variables across a wide range of temperatures and baryon chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The extended Chiral Mean Field (mCMF) model, which includes in-medium masses for thermally excited pseudoscalar and vector mesons via explicit chiral symmetry-breaking and vector interaction terms, yields better agreement with lattice QCD thermodynamic data across a wide range of temperatures and baryon chemical potentials than the original CMF model, but only when derivative couplings and self-interactions of pseudoscalar mesons are excluded, consistent with the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors extended the hadronic Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model by calculating in-medium masses of pseudoscalar mesons (π, η, η′, K) and vector mesons (ω, ρ, K*, φ) from the second derivative of the interaction potential with respect to the respective meson fields, using only the explicit chiral symmetry-breaking and vector interaction terms in the Lagrangian. These field-dependent meson masses were then used in the thermal meson contribution to the grand canonical potential, generating a feedback term in the mean-field equations of motion that modifies the equation of state for isospin-symmetric matter at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar meson in-medium masses follow the relation m*²_π = m²_π × (σ/σ₀), where σ₀ = −f_π is the vacuum expectation value of the scalar σ field.
* The pseudocritical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at zero baryon chemical potential is approximately T_pc ≈ 158 MeV (cited from Borsanyi 2020).
* Hyperon potentials in nuclear matter at saturation density include: Λ at −28.06 MeV, Σ at +5.51 MeV, Ξ at −15.67 MeV, Δ at −82 MeV, Σ* at +5.70 MeV, Ξ* at −8.32 MeV, and Ω⁻ at +28.70 MeV.
* The model parameters include m_HO₃ = 0.8061 for hyperon octet potentials, g_X_D = 3.54, α_DX = 0.248, V_Δ = 1, m₀ = 190 MeV, and m_HD₃ = 1.9 for the baryon decuplet.

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model of Dexheimer (2008, 2009) and Kumar (2024) by including, for the first time, in-medium masses for thermal mesons that generate feedback on the mean-field equations, whereas previous CMF versions treated thermal mesons as non-interacting with vacuum masses. The approach differs from earlier studies using the nonlinear chiral SU(3) model (Kumar 2018, 2020; Mishra 2004) that included derivative couplings and pseudoscalar-baryon interactions; here only explicit symmetry-breaking and vector interaction terms are retained. The improved mCMF model enables more accurate comparisons with lattice QCD results and the Hadron Resonance Gas model for the equation of state at high temperature and low baryon chemical potential, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
