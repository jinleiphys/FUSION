# Hadronic Screening in Improved Taste

> We present our results on meson and nucleon screening masses in finite temperature two flavour QCD using smeared staggered valence quarks and staggered thin-link sea quarks with different lattice spacings and quark masses. We investigate optimization of smearing by observing its effects on the infrared (IR) and ultraviolet (UV) components of gluon and quark fields. The application of smearing to screening at finite temperature also provides a transparent window into the mechanism of the interplay of smearing and chiral symmetry. The improved hadronic operators show that above the finite temperature cross over, T_c, screening masses are consistent with weak-coupling predictions. There is also evidence for a rapid opening up of a spectral gap of the Dirac operator immediately above T_c.

## Key claim

Screening masses of mesons and nucleons in finite-temperature two-flavor QCD, when measured using smeared staggered valence quarks, become consistent with weak-coupling predictions above the crossover temperature \(T_c\), contrary to earlier lattice results that showed strong deviations.

## Method

The authors generated gauge configurations with the Wilson gauge action and two flavors of thin-link staggered sea quarks using the R-algorithm, on lattices with \(N_t=4\) and spatial sizes \(N_s=8, 12, 16, 24\) at bare quark mass \(am=0.015\) (set N), plus two older ensembles (sets O and P) along lines of constant pion mass. They applied one level of smearing (APE, HYP, Stout, or HEX) to valence quark propagators and optimized the smearing parameter \(\epsilon\) by minimizing UV power suppression, conjugate gradient iteration count, or the maximum eigenvalue of \(D^\dagger D\). Screening correlators for mesons (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector) and the nucleon were computed using Coulomb gauge fixed wall-sources and fit to hyperbolic cosine forms, with masses extracted via bootstrap and covariance-aware fits.

## Key numbers

* For set N at \(T=0\) with \(\beta=5.2746\) and \(am=0.015\), the plaquette value is \(P=0.4873(4)\).
* The optimum smearing parameter for APE and HYP schemes is \(\epsilon=0.6\); for HEX \(\epsilon=0.15\); for Stout \(\epsilon=0.1\), nearly independent of lattice spacing.
* At \(T_c\), the negative chiral projection \(\prm{V_t}(z=1/T)\) is \((6\pm8)\times10^{-3}\) with thin-link valence quarks, but becomes \((0\pm2)\times10^{-3}\) with optimal HYP smearing.
* At \(T_c\), \(\prm S(z=1/T) = -3.3\pm0.1\) with thin-link valence, improving to \(-0.57\pm0.04\) with optimal HYP smearing.
* The ensemble-averaged smallest eigenvalue of the massless staggered Dirac operator, \(\langle\lambda_0\rangle\), rises by two orders of magnitude between \(T_c\) and \(1.06T_c\) for the smeared operator.
* The free theory screening mass for mesons on sets N and O is \(5.27\) (in units of \(T\)), and for baryons \(7.90\); on set P the values are \(5.78\) (mesons) and \(8.67\) (baryons).
* The weak-coupling prediction for meson screening masses is \(\mu = \mu_{FFT} + \frac43\alpha_s[1+2E_0]T\) with \(E_0=0.3824\) for two flavors in the dimensional reduction scheme.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of screening masses that found strong deviations from weak-coupling predictions using staggered quarks (Kogut et al., FFT mass, quasi, Cheng, Edwin) and closer but still imperfect agreement with Wilson quarks (Brandt, DR, HTL). The authors show that the discrepancy is largely a valence quark artifact, as previously suggested by quenched studies (scrnp). The results enable reliable weak-coupling descriptions of fermionic screening masses above \(T_c\) and constrain models of thermal effects on hadrons near the QCD crossover. The rapid opening of a spectral gap in the Dirac operator immediately above \(T_c\) (also studied by Lacaze et al.) provides a mechanism for the improved behavior.
