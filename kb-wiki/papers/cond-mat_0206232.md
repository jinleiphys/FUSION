# Efimov States in a Bose-Einstein Condensate near a Feshbach Resonance

> Recent experiments with Bose-Einstein condensates of 85Rb atoms near a Feshbach resonance have produced evidence for a condensate of diatomic molecules coexisting with the atom condensate. It should also be possible to create condensates of the triatomic molecules predicted by Efimov coexisting with the atom and dimer condensates. The smoking gun for the trimer condensate would be oscillatory dependence of observables on the binding energy of the trimer. It may also be possible to deduce the existence of the trimer condensate from the spectra of the bursts of atoms and dimers created in the disappearance of the trimers.

# Efimov States in a Bose-Einstein Condensate near a Feshbach Resonance

## Key claim

It should be possible to create condensates of Efimov triatomic molecules coexisting with atom and dimer condensates in 85Rb Bose-Einstein condensates near a Feshbach resonance, provided the scattering length during the hold time exceeds a critical value a_* (the scattering length at which an Efimov state appears at the atom-dimer threshold). The smoking gun for such a trimer condensate would be oscillatory dependence of observables on the trimer binding energy, superimposed on the known oscillations from the dimer condensate.

## Method

The authors analyze the time-dependent magnetic field sequence used in the 85Rb experiments of Donley et al. (2002), which involved pulses to large scattering lengths (up to approximately 2500 a_0) and variable holding times at intermediate scattering lengths. They extend this protocol by considering the coherent transfer of atoms from the atom condensate into dimer and trimer condensates during the pulses, and calculate the phase accumulation of each condensate during the hold time. They derive the expected oscillatory signatures in the remnant condensate number, burst atom number, and missing atom number as functions of the hold time, and compute the energy distributions of atoms emitted when the scattering length is suddenly changed.

## Key numbers

- The dimer binding energy in the limit of large scattering length a is |E_2| = ħ²/(ma²)
- New Efimov states appear below the atom-dimer threshold at scattering length values that differ by multiples of 22.7: a_*, 22.7a_*, 515a_*, ...
- For the 85Rb experiment of Donley et al. (2002), the initial condensate contained N ≈ 16,000 atoms at a scattering length a_init ≈ 10 a_0 (where a_0 is the Bohr radius)
- The maximum scattering length reached in the experiment was a_max ≈ 2500 a_0
- The burst atoms had a typical energy scale of 150 nK
- The lowest oscillation frequency observed in the experiment was 10 kHz, corresponding to a_hold ≈ 2000 a_0
- For a > 0, the condition |E_2| = ħ²/(ma²) is satisfied to within 20% at a = 925 a_0 (B = 158.5 G)
- A reasonable range for the 3-body parameter is 925 a_0 < a_* < 21,000 a_0, with 50% probability that a_* < 4400 a_0
- The atom-dimer scattering length is a_12 = [1.46 + 2.15 cot(s_0 ln(a/a_*))] a, where s_0 = 1.00624
- The probability that a dimer remains a dimer after a sudden change from scattering length a to a' is P[D → D] = 4aa'/(a+a')² for a' > 0
- The mean energy of atoms from dimer dissociation is ⟨E⟩ = (2a+|a'|)/(2|a'|) |E_2|
- The 3-body recombination rate coefficient into the shallow dimer is α = 67.1 sin²[s_0 ln(a/a_*) + 1.63] ħ a⁴/m, which vanishes at a = 0.20a_*, 4.5a_*, ...

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework developed for resonant Bose-Einstein condensates to include Efimov trimers, building on the experimental work of Donley et al. (2002) who observed evidence for a molecular dimer condensate coexisting with an atom condensate in 85Rb. The authors apply Efimov's universal theory (Efimov 1971) for three-body bound states near a two-body resonance, together with the renormalization group approach of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) and the atom-dimer scattering results of Braaten and Hammer (2002) and Bedaque, Braaten, and Hammer (2000). The work enables experimental searches for trimer condensates in existing 85Rb setups, and if successful, would provide dramatic confirmation of Efimov physics in cold atoms and open access to the rich physics of the resonant BEC.
