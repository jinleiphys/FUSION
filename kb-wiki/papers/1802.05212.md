# First Extraction of Transversity from a Global Analysis of Electron-Proton and Proton-Proton Data

> We present the first extraction of the transversity distribution in the framework of collinear factorization based on the global analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering off transversely polarized targets and in proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton. The extraction relies on the knowledge of di-hadron fragmentation functions, which are taken from the analysis of electron-positron annihilation data. For the first time, the chiral-odd transversity is extracted from a global analysis similar to what is usually done for the chiral-even spin-averaged and helicity distributions. The knowledge of transversity is important for, among other things, detecting possible signals of new physics in high-precision low-energy experiments.

## Key claim

The authors present the first extraction of the transversity parton distribution function (PDF) from a global analysis that simultaneously fits data from deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and proton-proton collisions, using collinear factorization and di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) determined from electron-positron annihilation data; the extraction is performed at leading order (LO) in the strong coupling constant and is limited to the valence quark components due to isospin symmetry assumptions on the DiFFs.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of azimuthal asymmetries in pion-pair production from three processes: semi-inclusive DIS off transversely polarized protons and deuterons (HERMES and COMPASS data), back-to-back pion-pair production in electron-positron annihilation (BELLE data), and proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton (STAR data). The transversity PDF was parametrized at a starting scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 using a flexible functional form constrained by the Soffer inequality, with the unpolarized PDF f1 taken from MSTW08 and the helicity PDF g1 from DSSV; the DiFFs were taken from a previous analysis of BELLE data. The fit used 600 bootstrap replicas to estimate statistical uncertainties, and the gluon DiFF uncertainty was explored by testing three different assumptions for D1^g.

## Key numbers

* Global fit achieved chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.08 +/- 0.09 for 46 data bins and 10 free parameters.
* SIDIS data contributed approximately 38% of the global chi^2, with about 76% of that from COMPASS data; STAR data contributed the remaining 62%, dominated by the |P_hT| bins (about 70%).
* The tensor charge for the up quark at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 is delta u = 0.39(10) at 90% confidence level.
* The tensor charge for the down quark at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 is delta d = -0.11(26) at 90% confidence level.
* The isovector tensor charge g_T = delta u - delta d at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 is 0.53(25) at 90% confidence level.
* The truncated tensor charge (restricted to the experimental x range [0.0065, 0.133]) at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 is delta tilde u = 0.32(8) and delta tilde d = -0.10(22).
* The SIDIS data spanned average x values from 0.0065 to 0.133, corresponding to average Q^2 from 1.232 to 31.5 GeV^2; STAR data covered pseudorapidity -0.84 to 0.84, |P_hT| from 3 to 13 GeV, and M_h from 0.3 to 1.2 GeV at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of transversity from di-hadron SIDIS data alone (Bacchetta et al., 2012; Radici et al., 2015) by including for the first time proton-proton collision data from the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk et al., 2015), enabling a global analysis analogous to those routinely performed for spin-averaged and helicity PDFs. The results show improved precision for the up quark transversity compared to both the previous di-hadron analysis and the Collins-effect extractions (Anselmino et al., 2013; Kang et al., 2015), while the down quark remains sensitive to the unconstrained gluon DiFF. The extracted isovector tensor charge appears incompatible with most lattice QCD calculations (Bhattacharya et al., 2015), motivating further work on both phenomenological extractions and lattice simulations to enable searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model in precision low-energy experiments such as neutron beta decay.
