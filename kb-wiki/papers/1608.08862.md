# Towards generating a new supernova equation of state: A systematic analysis of cold hybrid stars

> The hadron-quark phase transition in core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe) has the potential to trigger explosions in otherwise nonexploding models. However, those hybrid supernova equations of state (EOS) shown to trigger an explosion do not support the observational 2 M$_\odot$ neutron star maximum mass constraint. In this work, we analyze cold hybrid stars by the means of a systematic parameter scan for the phase transition properties, with the aim to develop a new hybrid supernova EOS. The hadronic phase is described with the state-of-the-art supernova EOS HS(DD2), and quark matter by an EOS with a constant speed of sound (CSS) of $c_{QM}^2=1/3$. We find promising cases which meet the 2 M$_\odot$ criterion and are interesting for CCSN explosions. We show that the very simple CSS EOS is transferable into the well-known thermodynamic bag model, important for future application in CCSN simulations. In the second part, the occurrence of reconfinement and multiple phase transitions is discussed. In the last part, the influence of hyperons in our parameter scan is studied. Including hyperons no change in the general behavior is found, except for overall lower maximum masses. In both cases (with and without hyperons) we find that quark matter with $c_{QM}^2=1/3$ can increase the maximum mass only if reconfinement is suppressed or if quark matter is absolutely stable.

## Key claim
The authors find that quark matter with a constant speed of sound squared of 1/3 can increase the maximum mass of hybrid stars only if reconfinement is suppressed or if quark matter is absolutely stable, and that promising configurations meeting the 2 solar mass criterion exist for future supernova equations of state, but only in a restricted parameter region where the transition pressure is either very low or very high relative to the hadronic energy density.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic parameter scan of cold hybrid stars using a Maxwell construction between the hadronic supernova equation of state HS(DD2) and a constant-speed-of-sound quark matter equation of state with c_QM^2 = 1/3. They varied the transition pressure p_trans from 1 to 800 MeV/fm^3 and the energy density discontinuity Delta_epsilon/epsilon_trans from 0 to 1.3 (and up to 3 in an extended scan), solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to classify each configuration into one of four hybrid star types (A, B, C, D) and determine its maximum mass. The analysis was repeated with the hyperonic equation of state BHB(Lambda phi) to study the effect of hyperons.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of the pure hadronic HS(DD2) equation of state: 2.42 solar masses
- Maximum mass of the hyperonic BHB(Lambda phi) equation of state: 2.11 solar masses
- Transition pressure range for the main scan: 1 to 800 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to baryon densities from approximately 0.1 to 1.02 fm^-3
- Ratio p_trans/epsilon_trans range: 0.01 to 0.55
- Ratio Delta_epsilon/epsilon_trans range: 0 to 1.3 (main scan) and 0 to 3 (extended scan)
- The 2 solar mass contour is reached at p_trans/epsilon_trans greater than approximately 0.22 (case 1) or less than approximately 0.02 (case 2) for any Delta_epsilon/epsilon_trans
- For p_trans/epsilon_trans between approximately 0.02 and 0.22, Delta_epsilon/epsilon_trans is limited to low values to satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint
- At the lowest p_trans and Delta_epsilon, maximum masses exceed 3 solar masses when reconfinement is ignored
- The Seidov criterion for stability at the onset of quark matter: Delta_epsilon_crit/epsilon_trans = 1/2 + 3/2 * p_trans/epsilon_trans

## Context
This work extends the parameter scan approach of Alford et al. (2013, 2016) by using the state-of-the-art supernova equation of state HS(DD2) instead of the softer HLPS or stiffer NL3 hadronic equations of state, and by explicitly addressing the problem of reconfinement that was not considered in those earlier scans. The study is motivated by the QCD-phase-transition supernova mechanism of Sagert et al. (2009), which showed explosions but used hybrid equations of state with maximum masses below 2 solar masses. The results enable the future construction of a new hybrid supernova equation of state that simultaneously satisfies the 2 solar mass neutron star constraint and shows a pronounced third family branch favorable for triggering supernova explosions, while also providing a systematic mapping between the constant-speed-of-sound parametrization and the thermodynamic bag model for use in finite-temperature simulations.
