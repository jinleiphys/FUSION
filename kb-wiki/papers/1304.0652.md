# Elementary excitations of a Higgs-Yukawa system

> This work investigates the physics of elementary excitations for the so-called relativistic quantum scalar plasma system, also known as the Higgs-Yukawa system. Following the Nemes-Piza-Kerman-Lin many-body procedure, the Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) equations were obtained for this model by linearizing the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations of motion around equilibrium. The resulting equations have a closed solution, from which the spectrum of excitation modes are studied. We show that the RPA oscillatory modes give the one-boson and two-fermion states of the theory. The results indicate the existence of bound states in certain regions in the phase diagram. Applying these results to recent LHC observations concerning the mass of the Higgs boson, we determine limits for the intensity of the coupling constant g of the Higgs-Yukawa model, in the RPA mean-field approximation, for three decay channels of the Higgs boson. Finally, we verify that, within our approximations, only Higgs bosons with masses larger than 190 GeV/c^2 can decay into top quarks.

## Key claim

The Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) oscillatory modes of the Higgs-Yukawa system yield both one-boson and two-fermion states, and bound states exist in certain regions of the phase diagram; however, within the RPA mean-field approximation, only Higgs bosons with masses larger than 190 GeV/c² can decay into top quarks.

## Method

The authors linearized the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations of motion around the equilibrium stationary solution of the Higgs-Yukawa model, following the Nemes-Piza-Kerman-Lin many-body procedure. The resulting coupled linear oscillator equations were solved analytically as a coupled-channel scattering problem with a separable potential, yielding closed expressions for the two-fermion wave function and the scattering matrix. Renormalization was performed using counterterms from prior work (Takano Natti et al., 1998) plus a wave-function renormalization constant chosen to cancel logarithmic divergences.

## Key numbers

- Only Higgs bosons with masses larger than 190 GeV/c² can decay into top quarks, within the RPA mean-field approximation.
- The wave-function renormalization constant is chosen as \( Z \equiv 4\pi g^2 L(m) \), where \( L(m) \equiv \int_{\bf k} 1/[2{\bf k}^2 ({\bf k}^2+m^2)^{1/2}] \).
- The finite expression for the scattering matrix denominator is \( \Delta^{+}(\omega) = -\pi\mu^2/(g^2 m^2) + F(\omega) - \theta(\omega-4\bar{m}^2)(i/8\pi)[\omega^2-4\bar{m}^2] \), where \( F(\omega) \) is given by Eqs. (75a)-(75b) in the text.
- The stationary condition for the condensate is \( (\pi/2)\mu^2\langle\phi\rangle|_{\rm eq} - g\bar{m}^3[\ln(\bar{m}/m) + 1/2] = 0 \).

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative mean-field framework developed by Takano Natti et al. (1998) for the Higgs-Yukawa system, which itself built on the non-relativistic nuclear many-body dynamics approach of Nemes and Toledo Piza (1982). The RPA analysis provides a non-perturbative alternative to lattice studies (Higgs 2012) and 1/N-expansion methods (Higgs 2011) for investigating bound states and decay channels. The results enable constraints on the Higgs-Yukawa coupling constant from LHC Higgs boson observations, and the framework can be extended to finite-density and finite-temperature calculations beyond mean-field approximation.
