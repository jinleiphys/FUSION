# Exploring Anisotropic flow via the Boltzmann Transport Equation Employing the Tsallis Blast Wave Description at LHC energies

> Anisotropic flows $i.e.$ azimuthal anisotropies in particle production are one of the important probes in characterizing the properties of the strongly interacting matter created in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. These observables are sensitive to both the transport properties as well as the equation of state (EOS) of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) matter. We have adopted the Boltzmann transport equation (BTE) in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to describe the experimental data for harmonic flows such as elliptic flow ($v_2$), triangular flow ($v_3$), quadrangular flow ($v_4$) obtained in heavy-ion collisions at Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. In this analysis, we have used Tsallis statistics as an initial distribution and the Tsallis Blast wave (TBW) description is used as the equilibrium distribution function while describing the evolution of the particle production in BTE. We have fitted the transverse momentum spectra, $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ of identified hadrons such as pion, kaon, and proton for Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions at the LHC energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV, respectively for various centralities. Our study offers a comparative analysis between the two distinct collision systems operating at comparable collision energies. The present formulation successfully fits the experimental data for $p_T$-spectra upto $p_T$ = 8 GeV and effectively explains the anisotropic flows data upto $p_T$ = 10 GeV with a very favourable $χ^2/ndf$. We observe that the average transverse flow velocity ($<β_r>$) and the kinetic freeze-out temperature ($T$) extracted in our analysis decrease as we go towards the peripheral collisions. The azimuthal modulation amplitudes ($ρ_a$) exhibit an increasing pattern as one moves from central to peripheral collisions in both the Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe nuclei interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Boltzmann Transport Equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a Tsallis distribution as the initial state and the Tsallis Blast Wave description as the equilibrium distribution, can simultaneously fit the transverse momentum spectra up to 8 GeV and the anisotropic flow coefficients v2, v3, and v4 up to 10 GeV for identified hadrons in Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions at LHC energies, but this success is contingent on treating the kinetic freeze-out temperature as a fixed parameter that decreases from central to peripheral collisions, contrary to conventional Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave results.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann Transport Equation in the relaxation time approximation, using the Tsallis non-extensive distribution as the initial distribution function and the Tsallis Blast Wave model as the equilibrium distribution function. They fitted the transverse momentum spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients v2, v3, and v4 for pions, kaons, and protons in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV across multiple centrality classes, using chi-squared minimization via the ROOT TF1 class. The analysis assumed Bjorken longitudinal expansion, mid-rapidity conditions, and a single freeze-out hyper-surface for all identified hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Transverse momentum spectra fitted successfully up to pT = 8 GeV for all identified hadrons in both collision systems.
- Anisotropic flow data (v2, v3, v4) explained effectively up to pT = 10 GeV.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature for the most central Pb-Pb collisions: T = 0.110 GeV; for most central Xe-Xe collisions: T = 0.106 GeV.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature for peripheral Pb-Pb collisions: T = 0.096 GeV; for peripheral Xe-Xe collisions: T = 0.090 GeV.
- Average transverse flow velocity and kinetic freeze-out temperature decrease when moving from central to peripheral collisions in both systems.
- Azimuthal modulation amplitudes (rho_a) for v2, v3, and v4 increase from central to peripheral collisions in both Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that used the Boltzmann Transport Equation with the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave model (Tripathy 2017), which could only explain pT-spectra up to 5 GeV, by replacing the equilibrium distribution with the Tsallis Blast Wave model to reach higher pT. It also builds on the Tsallis Blast Wave model studies of Che (2020), which described spectra up to 3 GeV, and Tang (2008), which applied the TBW model to Au-Au collisions at RHIC. The present formulation enables a comparative analysis of anisotropic flow between Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collision systems at comparable LHC energies and provides a framework for extracting freeze-out parameters across centralities.
