# Two-proton capture on the $^{68}$Se nucleus with a new self-consistent cluster model

> We investigate the two-proton capture reaction of the prominent rapid proton capture waiting point nucleus, $^{68}$Se, that produces the borromean nucleus $^{70}$Kr ($^{68}$Se$+p+p$). We apply a recently formulated general model where the core nucleus, $^{68}$Se, is treated in the mean-field approximation and the three-body problem of the two valence protons and the core is solved exactly. The same Skyrme interaction is used to find core-nucleon and core valence-proton interactions. We calculate $E2$ electromagnetic two-proton dissociation and capture cross sections, and derive the temperature dependent capture rates. We vary the unknown $2^+$ resonance energy without changing any of the structures computed self-consistently for both core and valence particles. We find rates increasing quickly with temperature below $2-4$~GK after which we find rates varying by less than a factor of two independent of $2^+$ resonance energy. The capture mechanism is sequential through the $f_{5/2}$ proton-core resonance, but the continuum background contributes significantly.

## Key claim

The two-proton capture rate on the waiting-point nucleus ⁶⁸Se increases rapidly with temperature below 2–4 GK, but above that range the rate varies by less than a factor of two regardless of the unknown 2⁺ resonance energy; the capture mechanism is sequential through the f₅/₂ proton-core resonance, with a significant contribution from the continuum background.

## Method

The core nucleus ⁶⁸Se was treated in the mean-field approximation using the Skyrme interaction SLy4, and the three-body problem of the core plus two valence protons was solved exactly via adiabatic expansion of the Faddeev equations in hyperspherical coordinates. The same Skyrme interaction was used to derive the core-nucleon and core-valence-proton interactions self-consistently. E2 electromagnetic two-proton dissociation and capture cross sections were calculated, and the temperature-dependent capture rates were obtained by averaging over a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution.

## Key numbers

- The ⁶⁸Se+p f₅/₂ resonance energy was set to 0.6 MeV, based on experimental data from San Andres (2014).
- The ⁷⁰Kr 0⁺ ground-state energy was fixed at −1.34 MeV, from systematics of Audi (2012).
- The Skyrme strength parameters t_i were scaled by a factor of 0.9515 to reproduce the known f₅/₂ resonance energy.
- The three-body Gaussian potential strength S₀ was set to −17.5 MeV to reproduce the 0⁺ ground-state energy; for the 2⁺ state, S₀ was varied from −35.05 MeV to −26.22 MeV to produce resonance energies from near zero to 4.0 MeV.
- The 2⁺ resonance energies were varied over 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, and 4.0 MeV.
- Above a few GK, the average capture rate is roughly 6 × 10⁻¹¹ cm⁶ [N_A mol]⁻² s⁻¹.
- The background contribution (without resonance) is smaller than the full rate by only about a factor of two above approximately 4 GK (∼0.34 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the three-body model of Hove (2016) by using a self-consistent mean-field treatment of the core, rather than phenomenological proton-core potentials, to study the two-proton capture on the rp-process waiting point ⁶⁸Se. The results enable more reliable input for astrophysical network calculations of nucleosynthesis beyond the iron peak, particularly for borromean proton-dripline nuclei where two-proton capture can bypass beta-decay waiting points. The approach can now be applied to other waiting-point nuclei, as the same effective nucleon-nucleon interaction is used for both core and valence particles.
