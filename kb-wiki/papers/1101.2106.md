# General integral relations for the description of scattering states using the hyperspherical adiabatic basis

> In this work we investigate 1+2 reactions within the framework of the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. To this aim two integral relations, derived from the Kohn variational principle, are used. A detailed derivation of these relations is shown. The expressions derived are general, not restricted to relative $s$ partial waves, and with applicability in multichannel reactions. The convergence of the ${\cal K}$-matrix in terms of the adiabatic potentials is investigated. Together with a simple model case used as a test for the method, we show results for the collision of a $^4$He atom on a \dimer dimer (only the elastic channel open), and for collisions involving a $^6$Li and two $^4$He atoms (two channels open).

## Key claim

The paper derives general integral relations, based on the Kohn variational principle, that allow extraction of the K-matrix (or S-matrix) for 1+2 reactions using only the internal part of the scattering wave function, removing the need for asymptotic matching at large hyperradii. The method works for multichannel reactions with arbitrary relative partial waves, but is restricted to energies below the breakup threshold.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrodinger equation using the hyperspherical adiabatic (HA) expansion, where the wave function is expanded in angular eigenfunctions obtained from a fixed-rho eigenvalue problem. They then apply two integral relations derived from the Kohn variational principle to compute the K-matrix elements from the trial wave function, avoiding the slow convergence that occurs when imposing plane-wave boundary conditions directly in hyperradial coordinates. The method is tested on a model system equivalent to a two-body reaction, then applied to elastic scattering of a helium-4 atom on a helium-4 dimer, and finally to collisions involving lithium-6 and two helium-4 atoms with two open channels.

## Context

This work extends the integral relations approach introduced by Barletta et al. (2009) from single-channel elastic s-wave scattering to multichannel reactions with arbitrary relative angular momenta. It addresses the slow convergence problem identified in the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion for scattering states, where the asymptotic plane wave in Jacobi coordinates (k_y y) does not match the hyperradial plane wave (k_y rho) at any finite rho. The method enables efficient calculation of scattering observables for three-body systems with hard-core interactions, such as helium cluster collisions, and is applicable to 1+N reactions with more than three particles provided the N-body bound state wave function is known.
