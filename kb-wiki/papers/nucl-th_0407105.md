# Polarization transfer in 4He(e,e'p) and 16O(e,e'p) in a relativistic Glauber model

## Key claim

The relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) predictions for polarization-transfer components in 4He(e,e'p) and 16O(e,e'p) closely match those of a relativistic plane-wave model, indicating that final-state interactions have only a minor impact on these observables, provided small proton missing momenta are probed in quasi-elastic kinematics. Short-range correlations also play a modest role (typically 1% at 200 MeV/c missing momentum), but the authors find that including medium-modified electromagnetic form factors from the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model lowers the double ratio R by nearly 8%, improving agreement with data at lower Q2 values.

## Method

The authors computed polarization-transfer components within the RMSGA framework, which adopts relativistic single-particle wave functions from the Hartree approximation to the sigma-omega model (W1 parametrization) and relativistic electron-nucleon couplings using the CC2 current operator. The Dirac-Glauber phase describing final-state interactions was computed rigorously (without truncating the multiple-scattering series) using profile functions parameterized from proton-nucleon scattering data, with each residual nucleon treated as a frozen scattering center with its unique relativistic wave function. Medium-modified electromagnetic form factors were implemented using QMC model predictions (bag radius 0.8 fm) and a modified Skyrme model, with the density-dependent form factors incorporated into the current operator via the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Short-range correlation effects on polarization-transfer components are typically of order 1% at a missing momentum of 200 MeV/c in 4He
- At Q2 = 0.4 and 0.5 (GeV/c)2, both RMSGA and RPWIA overestimate the double ratio R by nearly 10%, with R approximately 1 for zero recoil momentum
- Implementing QMC medium-modified form factors lowers the computed double ratio R by almost 8%, resulting in better agreement with data at Q2 = 0.4-0.5 (GeV/c)2
- At Q2 >= 1.5 (GeV/c)2, the averaged medium magnetic form factor in 4He is 10% larger than the free one according to the QMC model
- The 16O data are restricted to Q2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)2
- The 4He data cover the range 0.5 <= Q2 <= 2.6 (GeV/c)2 from Jefferson Laboratory
- For Q2 <= 1 (GeV/c)2, RMSGA overestimates the ratio R by about 10%, while RDWIA overestimates by 5-7%

## Context

This work extends the RMSGA framework previously developed by the same group (Lava, Ryckebusch, Van Overmeire, 2003) for nuclear transparencies to the study of polarization-transfer observables, and directly compares with experimental data from Malov (2000) for 16O and Dieterich (2001) and Strauch (2003) for 4He. The results are contrasted with relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations by Udias (1993) and others, finding that RMSGA attributes smaller effects to final-state interactions than RDWIA does. The paper enables systematic comparison of Glauber-based and optical-potential approaches for describing polarization transfer at higher Q2 where optical potentials become less natural, and tests medium modification predictions from the QMC model (Guichon, 1988; Lu, 1998, 1999) and a modified Skyrme model (Yakhshiev, 2002; Meissner, 2003) against world data.
