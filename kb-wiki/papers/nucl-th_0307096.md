# Lambda-Lambda bond energy from the Nijmegen potentials

> The $ΛΛ$ bond energy $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ in $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei is obtained from a $G$-matrix calculation which includes the coupling between the $ΛΛ$, $ΞN$ and $ΣΣ$ channels, as well as the effect of Pauli blocking to all orders. The Nijmegen NSC97e model is used as bare baryon-baryon interaction in the strangeness $S=-2$ sector. The $ΛΛ$-$ΞN$ coupling increases substantially the bond energy with respect to the uncoupled $ΛΛ$ case. However, the additional incorporation of the $ΣΣ$ channel, which couples simultaneously to $ΛΛ$ and $ΞN$ states, has a surprisingly drastic effect and reduces the bond energy down to a value closer to that obtained in an uncoupled calculation. We find that a complete treatment of Pauli blocking reduces the repulsive effect on the bond energy to about half of what was claimed before.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the full three-channel (ΛΛ, ΞN, ΣΣ) G-matrix calculation using the Nijmegen NSC97e potential yields a ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe of 0.28 MeV, which is much smaller than the two-channel (ΛΛ, ΞN) result of 0.78 MeV, and that a complete treatment of Pauli blocking reduces the bond energy by about half of what was claimed in earlier work by Myint (2003).

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in infinite nuclear matter, including coupling between ΛΛ, ΞN, and ΣΣ channels, using the Nijmegen NSC97e potential as the bare baryon-baryon interaction in the strangeness S=-2 sector. The ΛΛ bond energy in finite hypernuclei was approximated by the diagonal ¹S₀ ΛΛ G-matrix element evaluated at the average nuclear density and starting energy appropriate for each hypernucleus, with the Λ single-particle wave function obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential fitted to reproduce experimental Λ binding energies.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe for the uncoupled ΛΛ channel: 0.16 MeV
- ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe for the two-channel (ΛΛ, ΞN) calculation: 0.78 MeV
- ΛΛ bond energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe for the full three-channel (ΛΛ, ΞN, ΣΣ) calculation: 0.28 MeV
- ΛΛ scattering length for the three-channel case: -0.49 fm
- ΛΛ bond energy in ¹⁰_ΛΛBe for the three-channel calculation: 0.22 MeV
- ΛΛ bond energy in ¹³_ΛΛB for the three-channel calculation: 0.11 MeV
- Experimental ΛΛ bond energy for ⁶_ΛΛHe: 1.01 (+0.38, -0.31) MeV (Takahashi 2001)
- Experimental ΛΛ bond energy for ¹⁰_ΛΛBe: 4.2 ± 0.4 MeV (Danysz 1963)
- Experimental ΛΛ bond energy for ¹³_ΛΛB: 4.8 ± 0.7 MeV (Aoki 1991)

## Context

This work extends earlier G-matrix calculations by Carr (1997) and variational calculations by Yamada (2004) by treating the coupling to ΣΣ states explicitly and including Pauli blocking to all orders, rather than in a truncated second-order approximation as done by Myint (2003). The results confirm the incompatibility between experimental binding energies of light double-Λ hypernuclei reported by Filikhin and Gal (2002), and suggest that the ΣΣ channel, usually neglected, has a surprisingly large repulsive effect that reduces the bond energy substantially. The work enables future studies of three-channel S=-2 interactions and highlights the need for new experiments to clarify the experimental situation.
