# Inference of the low-energy constants in $Δ$-full chiral effective field theory including a correlated truncation error

> We sample the posterior probability distributions of the low-energy constants (LECs) in $Δ$-full chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to third order. We use eigenvector continuation for fast and accurate emulation of the likelihood and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo to draw effectively independent samples from the posteriors. Our Bayesian inference is conditioned on the Granada database of neutron-proton ($np$) cross sections and polarizations. We use priors grounded in $χ$EFT assumptions and a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering data. We model correlated EFT truncation errors using a two-feature Gaussian process, and find correlation lengths for $np$ scattering energies and angles in the ranges 45--83 MeV and 24--39 degrees, respectively. These correlations yield a non-diagonal covariance matrix and reduce the number of independent scattering data with a factor of 8 and 4 at the second and third chiral orders, respectively. The relatively small difference between the second and third order predictions in $Δ$-full $χ$EFT suppresses the marginal variance of the truncation error and the effects of its correlation structure. Our results are particularly important for analyzing the predictive capabilities in \textit{ab initio} nuclear theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that modeling correlated EFT truncation errors using a two-feature Gaussian process reduces the number of independent neutron-proton scattering data by factors of 8 and 4 at second and third chiral orders respectively, though the relatively small difference between second and third order predictions in Delta-full chiral effective field theory suppresses the marginal variance of the truncation error and the effects of its correlation structure.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference of the low-energy constants (LECs) in Delta-full chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), conditioned on the Granada database of neutron-proton cross sections and polarizations. They used eigenvector continuation for fast emulation of the likelihood and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo to draw independent samples from the posterior distributions, modeling correlated EFT truncation errors with a two-feature Gaussian process across scattering energies and angles.

## Key numbers

- Correlation lengths for np scattering energies found in the range 45--83 MeV across different observable types
- Correlation lengths for np scattering angles found in the range 24--39 degrees across different observable types
- Number of independent scattering data reduced by a factor of 8 at second chiral order (NLO) and a factor of 4 at third chiral order (NNLO)
- Regulator cutoff Lambda = 450 MeV used for the nonlocally regulated potential
- Hard scale Lambda_chi = 600 MeV assumed as a point estimate
- Training data consisted of approximately 25--50 points uniformly distributed across 4--6 energies in the range T_lab in [30, 290] MeV and 5--10 angles in the range theta in [0, 180] degrees
- Maximum a posteriori marginal variance cbar_sq values ranged from 0.22^2 (for N_zz^y polarization) to 1.95^2 (for sigma_L total cross section)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian LEC estimation in Delta-less chiral effective field theory by Svensson, Ekstrom, and Forssen (2021, 2022) and Wesolowski (2021), where an uncorrelated truncation error was employed. The correlated truncation error model follows the Gaussian process approach proposed by Melendez (2019), and the results are particularly important for analyzing predictive capabilities in ab initio nuclear theory, enabling more robust uncertainty quantification for nuclear interactions.
