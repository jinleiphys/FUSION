# J/psi+Z production at the LHC

> We briefly review recent results which we have obtained in the study of J/psi+Z production at the LHC. Considering our NLO computation in the Colour Evaporation Model (CEM) as an upper theory limit for the single-parton-scattering contributions, we claim that the existing data set from ATLAS points at a dominant double-parton-scattering contribution with an effective cross section smaller than that for jet-related observables. As a side product of our analysis, we have computed, for the first time, the one-loop QCD corrections to the J/psi P_T-differential cross section in the CEM.

# J/psi+Z production at the LHC

## Key claim
The existing ATLAS data for J/psi+Z production point to a dominant double-parton-scattering (DPS) contribution with an effective cross section smaller than that for jet-related observables, using the NLO Colour Evaporation Model (CEM) computation as an upper theory limit for the single-parton-scattering (SPS) contributions.

## Method
The authors performed NLO QCD computations in the Colour Evaporation Model (CEM) using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, integrating the heavy-quark pair production cross section over the invariant-mass range between 2m_c and the open-heavy-flavour threshold, multiplied by a phenomenological probability factor. They fitted the CEM parameters to 8 TeV ATLAS single-J/psi data to obtain the prompt J/psi probability factors at LO and NLO, then applied these to J/psi+Z production at the LHC, combining SPS (CEM) and DPS contributions to compare with ATLAS data.

## Key numbers
- The prompt J/psi probability factor at LO: P^{LO, prompt}_{J/psi} = 0.014 ± 0.001, fitted to 8 TeV ATLAS data with m_c = 1.27 GeV.
- The prompt J/psi probability factor at NLO: P^{NLO, prompt}_{J/psi} = 0.009 ± 0.0004, fitted to 8 TeV ATLAS data with m_c = 1.27 GeV.
- The K factor affecting the P_T slope for single J/psi production is close to 1.6.
- The effective cross section sigma_eff extracted from J/psi+Z data is 4.7^{+2.4}_{-1.5} mb.
- The effective cross section from di-J/psi data is 8.2 ± 2.0 ± 2.9 mb.
- The central scale mu_0 used is the transverse mass of the J/psi in J/psi + parton, with scale variations within 1/2 mu_0 to 2 mu_0.

## Context
This work extends previous NLO predictions for J/psi+Z production by Mao (2011) and Gong (2012), which found that ATLAS data showed larger SPS yields than expected if DPS rates assumed sigma_eff on the order of 15 mb. The authors show that the CEM yield, as an upper limit of SPS contributions, does not agree with DPS-subtracted data using sigma_eff = 15 mb, but can accommodate sigma_eff as low as 5 mb. This enables future studies to use quarkonium-associated-production observables to extract smaller effective cross sections, consistent with findings from di-J/psi data by Lansberg (2014).
