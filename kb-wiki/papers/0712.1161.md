# Spin effects in elastic backward P-D scattering

> The elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering is analyzed including both relativistic effects in the deuteron and the reaction mechanism. It is shown that inclusion of the graphs corresponding to the emission, rescattering and absorption of the virtual pion by a deuteron nucleon in addition to the one-nucleon exchange graph allows a rather satisfactory description of all the experimental data on the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power of the deuteron and transfer polarization in this reaction.

# Spin effects in elastic backward P-D scattering

## Key claim

Inclusion of one-pion exchange graphs (emission, rescattering, and absorption of a virtual pion by a deuteron nucleon) in addition to the relativistic one-nucleon exchange (RONE) graph allows a satisfactory description of all experimental data on differential cross section, tensor analyzing power, and transfer polarization, but only up to initial deuteron momenta of 7 GeV/c, and the contribution of these pion-exchange graphs decreases at higher momenta.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic invariant calculation of elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering including both the one-nucleon exchange graph (RONE) and higher-order graphs corresponding to virtual pion emission, rescattering, and absorption by a nucleon in the deuteron. The pion-exchange graphs were computed using a monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter of about 1 GeV/c, and the pi-N amplitude was taken from pi-N phase shift analysis. Calculations were performed using both the Reid soft core and the Argon-18 N-N potential deuteron wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The RONE model describes differential cross section and tensor analyzing power T20 at initial deuteron momenta up to 1.5 GeV/c
- The transfer polarization kappa0 is not described by RONE in the wide interval of deuteron momenta 1 GeV/c < p_d^{l.s.} < 4 GeV/c
- The total calculation including one-pion exchange graphs describes experimental data at initial deuteron momenta up to 7 GeV/c
- The monopole form factor for the virtual pion used a cutoff parameter of about 1 GeV/c
- The RONE fails at kinetic energies of backward scattered protons T_p > 0.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier one-nucleon exchange models (KermKissl 1969; BagGross 1969) and the relativistic ONE (IL 2001) which could not describe all observables at higher energies. It builds on the suggestion by Barry (1972) that high-order pion-exchange graphs become sizable at energies where Delta-isobar creation at the pi-N vertex is possible, and on similar findings for deuteron stripping reactions (DL 1990; DN 1989). The results enable future investigation of six-quark admixtures in the deuteron wave function, which may become important at initial momenta beyond 7 GeV/c where the pion-exchange contribution decreases.
