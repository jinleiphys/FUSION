# Constraining nuclear parameters using Gravitational waves from f-mode Oscillations in Neutron Stars

> Gravitational waves (GW) emanating from unstable quasi-normal modes in Neutron Stars (NS) could be accessible with the improved sensitivity of the current GW detectors or with the next-generation GW detectors and, therefore, can be employed to study the NS interior. Assuming f-mode excitation in isolated pulsars with typical energy of pulsar glitches and considering potential f-mode GW candidates for A+ (upgraded LIGO detectors operating at 5th observation run design sensitivity) and Einstein Telescope (ET), we demonstrate the inverse problem of NS asteroseismology within a Bayesian formalism to constrain the nuclear parameters and NS Equation of State (EOS). We describe the NS interior within relativistic mean field formalism. Taking the example of glitching pulsars, we find that for a single event in A+ and ET, among the nuclear parameters, the nucleon effective mass ($m^*$) within 90\% credible interval (CI) can be restricted within $10\%$ and $5\%$, respectively. At the same time, the incompressibility ($K$) and the slope of the symmetry energy ($L$) are only loosely constrained. Considering multiple (10) events in A+ and ET, all the nuclear parameters are well constrained, especially $m^*$, which can be constrained to 3\% and 2\% in A+ and ET, respectively. Uncertainty in the observables of a $1.4M_{\odot}$ NS such as radius ($R_{1.4M_{\odot}}$), f-mode frequency ($f_{1.4M_{\odot}}$), damping time ($τ_{1.4M_{\odot}}$) and a few EOS properties including squared speed of sound ($c_s^2$) are also estimated.

## Key claim

Assuming f-mode excitation in isolated pulsars with energy typical of pulsar glitches, the nucleon effective mass ($m^*$) can be constrained within 10% (A+ detectors) and 5% (Einstein Telescope) from a single gravitational wave event, while the incompressibility ($K$) and slope of the symmetry energy ($L$) remain only loosely constrained; with ten events, all nuclear parameters become well constrained, with $m^*$ reaching 3% and 2% precision respectively.

## Method

The authors model gravitational wave signals from f-mode oscillations as damped sinusoids, using glitch data from the Jodrell Bank Glitch Catalogue and pulsar properties from the ATNF Pulsar Catalogue. They assign masses to glitching pulsars from a uniform distribution and compute f-mode frequencies and damping times by solving linearized perturbation equations in general relativity for the PCSB0 equation of state within a relativistic mean field formalism. Bayesian parameter estimation with bilby and nested sampling is performed for simulated events in A+ (upgraded LIGO O5 design sensitivity) and Einstein Telescope configurations, then the joint posteriors of frequency and damping time are used to constrain nuclear parameters through a second Bayesian step using universal relations.

## Key numbers

- For a single event from the Vela pulsar (glitch size $\Delta\nu/\nu = 3.1\times 10^{-6}$, assigned mass $\sim 1.84 M_\odot$), the f-mode frequency is recovered within $\leq 1\%$ (90% credible interval) in both A+ and ET configurations
- For the same single event, the damping time uncertainty is $\sim 27\%$ in A+ and $\sim 10\%$ in ET (90% credible interval)
- With a single event, $m^*/m_N$ is constrained to $0.643^{+0.055}_{-0.060}$ in A+ and $0.653^{+0.038}_{-0.037}$ in ET (90% credible interval), corresponding to approximately 10% and 5% precision respectively
- With ten events in A+, $m^*/m_N$ is constrained to $0.640^{+0.021}_{-0.020}$ (3% precision); with ten events in ET, $0.648^{+0.011}_{-0.010}$ (2% precision)
- For a canonical $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star from a single event in A+: radius $13.30^{+0.74}_{-0.57}$ km (6% precision), f-mode frequency $1588^{+96}_{-111}$ Hz (7% precision), damping time $0.285^{+0.045}_{-0.032}$ s (18% precision)
- For a canonical $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star from a single event in ET: radius $13.20^{+0.58}_{-0.39}$ km (4% precision), f-mode frequency $1607^{+41}_{-70}$ Hz (4% precision), damping time $0.278^{+0.026}_{-0.014}$ s (9% precision)

## Context

This work extends the inverse asteroseismology approach of Ho (2020) and Volkel (2021, 2022) by applying Bayesian inference to constrain nuclear parameters from f-mode gravitational wave signals, using realistic detector configurations and actual glitching pulsar data. The methodology enables future gravitational wave detections from isolated neutron stars to directly constrain the relativistic mean field parameters of nuclear matter, particularly the nucleon effective mass, and can be combined with other astrophysical observations to further reduce uncertainties on the equation of state.
