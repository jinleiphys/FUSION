# Collective excitations in neutron-star crusts

> We explore the spectrum of low-energy collective excitations in the crust of a neutron star, especially in the inner region where neutron-proton clusters are immersed in a sea of superfluid neutrons. The speeds of the different modes are calculated systematically from the nuclear energy density functional theory using a Skyrme functional fitted to essentially all experimental atomic mass data.

## Key claim

The coupling of the neutron superfluid to the strain field (parameter \(L\)) substantially reduces the speed of the highest longitudinal mixed mode in the inner crust, especially in the shallowest layers, but the thermal properties of the crust (heat capacity and thermal conductivity) remain almost unchanged compared to previous estimates that neglected this coupling.

## Method

The authors linearize elasto-hydrodynamic equations for the inner crust, treating it as an isotropic solid with a neutron superfluid permeating a Coulomb lattice of neutron-proton clusters. They compute the speeds of transverse and longitudinal collective modes using the band theory of solids to account for entrainment effects, and estimate the coupling coefficient \(L\) from the total bulk modulus using Eq. (4). The microscopic input (composition, conduction neutron density, and mean-field potentials) is taken from the ETFSI method with the BSk14 Skyrme interaction, which fits experimental atomic masses with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.73 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At average baryon density \(\bar n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\), the speed of the highest longitudinal mixed mode \(v_{+}\) is 3.75 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) when coupling is included, compared to 5.56 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without coupling (a reduction of about 33%).
- At \(\bar n = 0.001\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 3.50 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 4.58 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.005\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 3.86 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 4.80 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 4.40 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 5.20 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 5.35 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 5.84 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.03\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 6.20 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 6.56 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 6.96 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 7.39 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 7.68 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 8.31 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.06\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 8.37 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 9.29 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.07\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 9.07 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 10.3 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- At \(\bar n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(v_{+}\) is 9.82 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) with coupling versus 11.4 \(\times 10^{-2}c\) without.
- The heat capacity of the highest longitudinal mixed mode at temperature \(T = 10^7\) K near the neutron-drip transition is enhanced by about a factor of 3 compared to previous estimates that neglected the coupling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Chamel et al., 2013) by including the coupling of the neutron superfluid to the strain field, which was previously set to zero. It builds on the band theory of solids to treat entrainment effects arising from Bragg scattering of unbound neutrons by the Coulomb lattice, going beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation used in earlier mean-field studies (Grasso et al., 2008; Baroni et al., 2010). The results enable more accurate calculations of the thermal evolution of neutron stars, although the thermal properties of the crust remain dominated by transverse phonons and electrons.
