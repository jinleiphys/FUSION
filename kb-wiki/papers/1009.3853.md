# Decay properties of the heavy-light mesons

> We study the decay properties of a heavy-light meson. We reformulate the decay amplitudes for the heavy-light systems and find a new way to calculate decay rates. Applying this formulation, we find a new sum rule for the radiative decays of one heavy-light meson into another, $H_1\to H_2+γ$ with various combinations of $H_i$.

## Key claim
The paper reformulates decay amplitudes for heavy-light mesons to be Lorentz-invariant, deriving a new sum rule for radiative decays in the heavy quark symmetric limit; the sum rule relates sums of decay widths of mixed \(1^+\) states to those of \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states, but the authors note that experimental verification is still pending.

## Method
The authors construct decay amplitudes in the Breit frame, where initial and final mesons move with equal and opposite velocities, using Lorentz-boosted wave functions derived from rest-frame wave functions. They compute form factors for vector and axial-vector currents for transitions involving one photon or one chiral particle, with initial states up to spin \(2^+\). The sum rules are obtained analytically in the heavy quark symmetric limit by evaluating these form factors with explicit wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit for \(\Gamma(D_{s1}(2460) \to D_s(1968) + \gamma) < 630\) keV.
- Upper limit for \(\Gamma(D_{s1}(2460) \to D_{s1}^*(2112) + \gamma) < 280\) keV.
- The sum rule for the \(``^3P_1"\) state gives \(\Gamma(1^+ \to 0^- + \gamma) + \Gamma(1^+ \to 1^- + \gamma) = \Gamma(0^+ \to 1^- + \gamma) = \frac{2}{9}\frac{e^2 k^3}{2\pi} \left[ \int dr (u_2^{-1} u_1^1 + v_2^{-1} v_1^1) \right]^2\).
- The sum rule for the \(``^1P_1"\) state gives \(\Gamma(1^+ \to 0^- + \gamma) + \Gamma(1^+ \to 1^- + \gamma) = \Gamma(2^+ \to 1^- + \gamma) = \frac{2}{9}\frac{e^2 k^3}{2\pi} \left[ \int dr (u_2^{-1} u_1^{-2} + v_2^{-1} v_1^{-2}) \right]^2\).

## Context
This work extends earlier approaches by Goity and Roberts (1999) and Di Pierro and Eichten (2001), which used a non-Lorentz-covariant dipole expansion, and by Bardeen, Eichten, and Hill (2001), which employed an effective chiral Lagrangian without meson wave functions. The new Lorentz-invariant formulation enables calculation of radiative and pionic decay rates for heavy-light mesons using explicit wave functions, and the derived sum rules provide testable predictions for states like \(D_{s0}(2317)\), \(D_{s1}(2460)\), \(D_{s1}(2536)\), and \(D_{s2}(2573)\) that await experimental confirmation.
