# Inhomogeneous Superconductivity in Condensed Matter and QCD

> Inhomogeneous superconductivity arises when the species participating in the pairing phenomenon have different Fermi surfaces with a large enough separation. In these conditions it could be more favorable for each of the pairing fermions to stay close to its Fermi surface and, differently from the usual BCS state, for the Cooper pair to have a non zero total momentum. For this reason in this state the gap varies in space, the ground state is inhomogeneous and a crystalline structure might be formed. This situation was considered for the first time by Fulde, Ferrell, Larkin and Ovchinnikov, and the corresponding state is called LOFF. The spontaneous breaking of the space symmetries in the vacuum state is a characteristic feature of this phase and is associated to the presence of long wave-length excitations of zero mass. The situation described here is of interest both in solid state and in elementary particle physics, in particular in Quantum Chromo-Dynamics at high density and small temperature. In this review we present the theoretical approach to the LOFF state and its phenomenological applications using the language of the effective field theories.

## Key claim

Inhomogeneous superconductivity (the LOFF state) can arise when two fermion species participating in pairing have sufficiently different Fermi surfaces, making it energetically favorable for Cooper pairs to carry non-zero total momentum and produce a spatially modulated gap, rather than forming the usual uniform BCS condensate.

## Method

The authors develop a unified theoretical formalism based on Nambu-Gor'kov (NG) equations and effective field theory to describe the LOFF state in both condensed matter and QCD contexts. They derive the gap equation and grand potential for fermions with different chemical potentials, perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion up to sixth order in the gap parameter to study the phase diagram, and analyze the phonon effective Lagrangians for different crystalline structures (single plane wave and cubic). The formalism is applied to two-flavor QCD at high density and to condensed matter systems with paramagnetic impurities or magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The BCS gap at zero temperature and zero chemical potential difference is given by Δ₀ = 2δ exp(-2/ρg) in the weak coupling limit, where ρ = p_F²/(π² v_F) is the density of states and δ is the ultraviolet cutoff of order the Debye frequency.
- The homogeneous BCS state becomes unstable at δμ = Δ₀/√2, where δμ is the chemical potential difference between the two fermion species.
- At T=0, the gap equation for homogeneous superconductors with δμ ≠ 0 yields two solutions: Δ = Δ₀ (independent of δμ) and Δ² = 2δμΔ₀ - Δ₀², with the first solution having lower grand potential for δμ < Δ₀/√2.
- The grand potential difference between the BCS state and the normal state at δμ = 0 is Ω = -(ρ/4)Δ₀² in the weak coupling limit.

## Context

This review extends the original work of Fulde and Ferrell (1964) and Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) on inhomogeneous superconductivity in metals, and connects it to more recent developments in color superconductivity in QCD at high density, as initiated by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000). The paper provides a unified effective field theory framework using Nambu-Gor'kov equations and High Density Effective Theory (HDET), enabling cross-fertilization between condensed matter and particle physics communities. The formalism enables future study of the LOFF phase in heavy fermion superconductors, organic superconductors, high-Tc materials, and neutron star interiors (particularly for explaining pulsar glitches).
