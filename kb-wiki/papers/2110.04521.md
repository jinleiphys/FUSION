# Interglueball potential in lattice SU(N) gauge theories

> The dynamics of the glueballs is important in the context of the experimental search as well as for understanding the non-Abelian gauge theory. The glueballs of the dark $SU(N_c)$ Yang-Mills theory are also good candidates of dark matter. In this proceedings contribution, we report on the result of the lattice calculation of the interglueball potential of the Yang-Mills theory with the color numbers $N_c=2,3,4$, with a detailed inspection of the systematics due to the discretization.

# Interglueball potential in lattice SU(N) gauge theories

## Key claim
The interglueball potential in SU(2), SU(3), and SU(4) Yang-Mills theories is attractive and similar in size across different color numbers, but the expected large-N_c scaling behavior (proportional to 1/N_c^2) could not be clearly resolved due to discretization systematics that remain significant despite the subtraction of the centrifugal force artifact.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(2), SU(3), and SU(4) Yang-Mills theories using the standard plaquette action with multiple lattice spacings, generating configurations via the pseudo-heat-bath method. They defined the 0^{++} glueball operator using spatial plaquette operators with APE smearing, and extracted the interglueball potential from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using the time-dependent HAL QCD method, which avoids the need for ground state saturation. The centrifugal force term was subtracted to remove unphysical contributions from higher partial waves, and the cluster decomposition error reduction technique (CDERT) was applied to improve statistical accuracy.

## Key numbers
- For SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, the Yukawa fit of the interglueball potential gives V_1 = -231 ± 8 (chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.3).
- For SU(2), the 2-Gaussian fit yields V_1 = (-8.5 ± 0.5) Lambda and V_2 = (-26.6 ± 2.6) Lambda (chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.9).
- The low-energy scattering cross section for SU(2) glueballs is sigma_{phi phi} = (2.5 - 4.7) Lambda^{-2} from the Yukawa fit and (14 - 51) Lambda^{-2} from the 2-Gaussian fit.
- Combining the scattering cross section with dark matter constraints gives a lower limit on the SU(2) Yang-Mills scale parameter Lambda > 60 MeV.
- Lattice spacings used range from a sqrt(sigma) = 0.608(16) (beta=2.1, SU(2)) to a sqrt(sigma) = 0.1876(12) (beta=6.1, SU(3)), corresponding to a in units of Lambda^{-1} from 0.356(27) to 0.101(8).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice calculation of interglueball potentials (Yamanaka 2019) by applying the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009, HAL QCD Collaboration 2012) with centrifugal force subtraction to reduce lattice artifacts. The results are compared with the strong coupling expansion estimate (Munster 1984) and the large-N_c scaling expectation (Lucini 2001, Lucini 2012, Hernandez 2020). The findings motivate future calculations using improved actions with better rotational symmetry to reduce discretization errors, and enable constraints on dark matter models where SU(N_c) Yang-Mills glueballs serve as dark matter candidates.
