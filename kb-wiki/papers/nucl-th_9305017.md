# Momentum and Coordinate Space Three-nucleon Potentials

> In this paper we give explicit formulae in momentum and coordinate space for the three-nucleon potentials due to $ρ$ and $π$ meson exchange, derived from off-mass-shell meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes which are constrained by the symmetries of QCD and by the experimental data. Those potentials have already been applied to nuclear matter calculations. Here we display additional terms which appear to be the most important for nuclear structure. The potentials are decomposed in a way that separates the contributions of different physical mechanisms involved in the meson-nucleon amplitudes. The same type of decomposition is presented for the $π- π$ TM force: the $Δ$, the chiral symmetry breaking and the nucleon pair terms are isolated.

## Key claim

The paper provides explicit momentum- and coordinate-space formulae for three-nucleon potentials arising from rho-pi and rho-rho meson exchange, derived from off-mass-shell meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes constrained by QCD symmetries and experimental data; the authors emphasize that the displayed terms are those believed to be "the most important for nuclear structure," and that the decomposition isolates contributions from different physical mechanisms (Delta isobar, chiral symmetry breaking, and nucleon pair terms) within the same framework used for the earlier Tucson-Melbourne (TM) pi-pi force.

## Method

The authors construct the three-nucleon potentials by performing a non-relativistic reduction of the Feynman diagrams for two-meson exchange, using the three-body T-matrix defined via the S-matrix with Dirac spinors normalized to u-bar u = 1. The intermediate meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes (pi N -> pi N, rho N -> pi N, and rho N -> rho N) are obtained through current-algebra Ward identities and vector dominance, then expanded in powers of the exchanged three-momenta, retaining terms up to second order. The resulting potentials are decomposed into contributions from the backward-propagating nucleon ("Z-graph"), the Delta isobar, and chiral symmetry breaking terms, with explicit separation of the isospin-even and isospin-odd channels.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma term sigma/f_pi^2 = 1.03 mu^{-1} (updated Karlsruhe data) and 1.13 mu^{-1} (earlier data), where mu = 139.6 MeV (charged pion mass) or 138 MeV (SU(2) average).
- The coefficient b (for the q-vector dot q-prime term in the pi-pi force) = -2.62 mu^{-3} (updated data) or -2.58 mu^{-3} (earlier data); the Delta contribution b_Delta = c_2 = -1.54 mu^{-3}, and the chiral symmetry breaking part b_sigma = -1.08 mu^{-3} (updated) or -1.04 mu^{-3} (earlier).
- The coefficient c (for the q^2 + q'^2 term in the pi-pi force) = 0.91 mu^{-3} (updated) or 1.0 mu^{-3} (earlier); the Z-graph contribution c_z = -0.15 mu^{-3}.
- The coefficient d (for the spin-flip term in the pi-pi force) = -0.75 mu^{-3}; the Delta contribution d_Delta = -0.36 mu^{-3}, the current-algebra part d_ca = -0.24 mu^{-3}, and the Z-graph part d_z = -0.15 mu^{-3}.
- The on-mass-shell kappa_rho (Pauli coupling of the rho to the nucleon) = 6.6 (Hohler and Peitarinen value).
- The constant D_0 (leading term in the expansion of the Delta contribution to the spin-flip amplitude B^-) = 4.87 mu^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) family of three-nucleon forces, previously limited to pi-pi exchange (Coon et al., 1979; Coon and Gloeckle), to include rho-pi and rho-rho exchange derived from the same current-algebra and Ward-identity principles. The paper provides the explicit forms needed for few-body calculations, addressing the need identified by nuclear matter studies (Ellis et al.) where rho exchange was found to screen pi-exchange effects, and by triton calculations (Chen et al.; Sasakawa et al.) where the TM pi-pi force alone produced overbinding. The potentials enable direct comparison between the Hannover approach (based on two-body NN -> NDelta transition potentials) and the Tucson-Melbourne approach, and are intended for application to three-nucleon scattering calculations (Witala et al.) and alpha-particle binding energy studies (Kamada et al.; Carlson; Wiringa).
