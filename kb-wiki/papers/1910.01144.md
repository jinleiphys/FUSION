# A model for neutrino-nucleus interactions in the GeV region

> We review the recent progress in modelling neutrino-nucleus scattering, in a framework based on scaling which describes simultaneously the nuclear response to electromagnetic and weak probes. The study is relevant for the analysis of neutrino oscillation data and the design of the next generation experiments Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE.

# A model for neutrino-nucleus interactions in the GeV region

## Key claim

The SuSAv2 (SuperScaling Approach version 2) model provides very good agreement with inclusive electron and neutrino scattering data within present error bars, but the author stresses that this agreement is not sufficient to assure the level of control of nuclear effects required by future experiments Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE, and that further studies of semi-inclusive reactions are needed.

## Method

The model is based on scaling and superscaling of inclusive (e,e') data, using relativistic mean field (RMF) framework to evaluate scaling functions for different nuclear response channels. In the quasi-elastic region, the impulse approximation is employed with nucleon bound states as solutions of the Dirac equation in scalar and vector potentials, while final state interactions are described by the same potentials with a "blending function" that mixes plane-wave impulse approximation and RMF approaches depending on momentum transfer. Two-body currents mediated by pions (MEC) are included to account for 2p2h excitations in the dip region between the quasi-elastic and Delta resonance peaks.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy E = 2.222 GeV and scattering angle θ = 15.541 deg for the Jefferson Lab electron scattering data on 40Ar and 48Ti shown in the comparison
- Average neutrino energy <E> ~ 600 MeV for the T2K experiment data compared with SuSAv2 predictions
- Average antineutrino energy <E> ~ 3.5 GeV for the MINERvA experiment data compared with SuSAv2 predictions
- The model parameters are fitted once and for all to electron scattering data on 12C

## Context

This work reviews the SuSAv2 model developed in Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) and Megias (2016), which extends the scaling-based approach of Donnelly (1999) and Amaro (2004) to simultaneously describe nuclear responses to electromagnetic and weak probes. The model has been implemented in the GENIE generator (Dolan 2019) with implementation in NEUT in progress, and is relevant for the analysis of neutrino oscillation data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA collaborations, as well as for the design of the next-generation experiments Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE. The paper identifies semi-inclusive reactions as the next major challenge for nuclear theorists in this field, as they are more sensitive to model details than inclusive measurements.
