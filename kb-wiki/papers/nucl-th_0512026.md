# Nuclear collective tunneling transitions between Hartree states deformed in quadrupole symmetry

> In a nucleus which has two Hartree states deformed in quadrupole symmetry, i.e., prolate state and oblate state, the nuclear residual interaction derived in the present theory beyond the Hartree approximation acts as the restoring force for the spherical symmetry of the nuclear system to be recovered so that the deformed nucleus makes collective tunneling transitions between prolate states and oblate states. We derive the Hamiltonian that is effective not only for the Hartree states but also for the collective tunneling transitions between the Hartree states. Solving the secular equation for the effective Hamiltonian on the basis states expressed in terms of SU(5) algebra, we analyze the nuclear collective tunneling transitions between a prolate state and an oblate state in the real-time description. The collective tunneling transitions are discussed to proceed through tri-axially asymmetric deformed states.

# Nuclear Collective Tunneling Transitions between Hartree States Deformed in Quadrupole Symmetry

## Key claim

The nuclear residual interaction derived beyond the Hartree approximation acts as a restoring force for spherical symmetry, enabling collective tunneling transitions between prolate and oblate Hartree states in nuclei such as ²⁸Si; these transitions proceed through tri-axially asymmetric deformed intermediate states, and the tunneling probability depends on the orientation of the final oblate state relative to the initial prolate polarization axis.

## Method

The authors derived an effective Hamiltonian from a non-relativistic meson-nucleon field theory by quantizing the meson field into a spherically symmetric principal mean field and quadrupole tunneling steering fields. They expressed the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction between d-state nucleons in terms of SU(5) algebra generators, classified the nuclear states of 12 valence nucleons in ²⁸Si into 490-dimensional (oblate, configuration d⁸s⁴), 490*-dimensional (prolate, configuration d¹²), and 1176-dimensional (intermediate, configuration d¹⁰s²) representations, and solved the secular equation for the total Hamiltonian in the combined representation space.

## Key numbers

- Energy splitting ΔE between the almost degenerate ground state and first excited state for L=0: 1.33 κ (where κ is the quadrupole-quadrupole coupling constant, of order 0.1 MeV for s-d shell nuclei)
- Energy splitting ΔE for L=2: 0.75 κ
- Energy splitting ΔE for L=4: 0.93 κ
- Ground state energy of the nuclear Hamiltonian for L=0: -280.47 κ
- First excited state energy for L=0: -279.14 κ
- Ground state energy for L=2: -265.46 κ
- First excited state energy for L=2: -264.71 κ
- Ground state energy for L=4: -233.87 κ
- First excited state energy for L=4: -232.94 κ
- Transition probability from z-prolate state to x- and y-oblate states is suppressed by about 40 percent compared to transition to z-oblate state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation (Kohmura, Kohmura1) of collective tunneling between deformed Hartree states by solving the secular equation in the SU(5) representation space and providing real-time transition probabilities. The approach builds on the Bohr and Mottelson collective model and the SU(3) symmetry framework of Elliott, but extends to SU(5) algebra to accommodate the full quadrupole degrees of freedom. The results enable quantitative predictions for shape coexistence and shape mixing in s-d shell nuclei such as ²⁸Si, and the formalism can be applied to other nuclei with competing prolate and oblate Hartree states.
