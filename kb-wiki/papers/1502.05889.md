# Gamow-Teller properties of the double beta-decay partners 116Cd(Sn) and 150Nd(Sm)

> The two Gamow-Teller (GT) branches connecting the double-beta decay partners (116Cd, 116Sn) and (150Nd, 150Sm) with the intermediate nuclei 116In and 150Pm are studied within a microscopic approach based on a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation built on a Skyrme selfconsistent mean field with pairing correlations and spin-isospin residual forces. The results are compared with the experimental GT strength distributions extracted from charge-exchange reactions. Combining the two branches, the nuclear matrix elements for the two-neutrino double-beta decay are evaluated and compared to experimental values derived from the measured half-lives.

## Key claim

The deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) built on a Skyrme selfconsistent mean field can simultaneously reproduce the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions from charge-exchange reactions and the two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for the 116Cd(Sn) and 150Nd(Sm) systems, but the agreement depends on the choice of nuclear deformation (prolate shapes generally perform best) and the treatment of the energy denominator in the matrix element.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme interactions (SLy4, SGII, Sk3) including BCS pairing correlations, then solved the QRPA equations on the deformed ground-state basis with separable spin-isospin residual interactions in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. They computed GT strength distributions for both the beta-minus and beta-plus branches connecting the double-beta decay partners to the intermediate nuclei 116In and 150Pm, and evaluated the two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements using two different energy denominator prescriptions (D1 and D2). The results were compared with experimental GT strengths extracted from charge-exchange reactions and with experimental matrix elements derived from measured half-lives.

## Key numbers

- For 116Cd, the experimental two-neutrino double-beta decay half-life is (2.8 +/- 0.2) x 10^19 yr, yielding nuclear matrix elements M_GT = 0.138 MeV^-1 (using bare g_A = 1.273) or 0.223 MeV^-1 (using quenched g_A = 1)
- For 150Nd, the experimental half-life is (8.2 +/- 0.9) x 10^18 yr, yielding M_GT = 0.070 MeV^-1 (bare g_A = 1.273) or 0.114 MeV^-1 (quenched g_A = 1)
- The phase-space factors used are G = 2.764 x 10^-18 yr^-1 for 116Cd and G = 3.643 x 10^-17 yr^-1 for 150Nd
- The coupling constants used in the QRPA are chi_ph^GT = 3.0/A^0.7 MeV and kappa_pp^GT = 1.8/A^0.7 MeV
- A quenching factor q = g_A / g_A,bare = 0.79 is applied to the GT strength calculations
- For 116In, the experimental Q-values are Q_EC = 0.463 MeV and Q_beta- = 3.276 MeV; for 150Pm, Q_EC = 0.083 MeV and Q_beta- = 3.456 MeV, with the first 1+ state at 0.11 MeV excitation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deformed QRPA studies on A=76, 128, 130 double-beta decay partners (Sarriguren 2012, 2013) to the 116Cd(Sn) and 150Nd(Sm) systems, motivated by recent charge-exchange reaction experiments by Sasano (2012) and Guess (2011). The results are compared with spherical QRPA calculations using Woods-Saxon bases and Bonn-A interactions (Suhonen 2014), as well as with deformed QRPA calculations using realistic CD-Bonn interactions on Woods-Saxon potentials (Yousef 2009, Fang 2010). The work enables future systematic studies of deformation effects on the effective axial-vector coupling constant g_A, which is needed to resolve discrepancies between quenching factors required by single-beta and double-beta decay observables.
