# Screening enhancement factors for laboratory CNO and rp astrophysical reactions

> Cross sections of laboratory CNO and rp astrophysical reactions are enhanced due to the presence of the multi-electron cloud that surrounds the target nuclei. As a result the relevant astrophysical factors are overestimated unless corrected appropriately. This study gives both an estimate of the error committed if screening effects are not taken into account and a rough profile of the laboratory energy thresholds at which the screening effect appears. The results indicate that, for most practical purposes, screening corrections to past relevant experiments can be disregarded. Regarding future experiments, however, screening corrections to the CNO reactions will certainly be of importance as they are closely related to the solar neutrino fluxes and the rp process. Moreover, according to the present results, screening effects will have to be taken into account particularly by the current and future LUNA experiments, where screened astrophysical factors will be enhanced to a significant degree.

## Key claim

Electron screening corrections to laboratory CNO and rp astrophysical reactions can be disregarded for most past experiments, but will be important for future experiments (particularly those by the LUNA collaboration) where screened astrophysical factors will be enhanced to a significant degree; the actual importance of screening corrections depends on their size relative to other experimental errors.

## Method

The author extends a previously derived analytic screening enhancement factor (Liolios, 2001) for binary multielectron reactions to proton-induced reactions relevant to the CNO cycle and rp process. The screening enhancement factor is bounded between the sudden limit (SL) and adiabatic limit (AL), with formulas derived in the Thomas-Fermi model accounting for exchange, ionization, thermal, and relativistic effects of the atomic cloud. For targets with atomic number Z1 > 8, the gap between the two limits narrows considerably, and the screening enhancement factor can be accurately described by a single exponential expression.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 14N(p,γ)15O at E_cm = 93 keV, the screening enhancement factor f = 1.083, corresponding to an 8.3% overestimate of the astrophysical factor S(0)
- For the reaction 14N(p,γ)15O at 70 keV (planned LUNA experiment), screening corrections will be approximately 13%
- For the reaction 19F(p,αγ)16O at E_cm = 188.8 keV, the screening correction is 5% (S_exp = 5 ± 4 MeV·b corrected to S_exp/f = 4.7 ± 4 MeV·b)
- For the reaction 86Sr(p,γ)87Y at the lowest measured energy E_cm = 1477 keV, the screening overestimate is 6.7%
- The most effective energy of interaction for proton-induced reactions is given by E0 ≈ 1.22 (Z1 T6)^(2/3) keV

## Context

This work extends the screening correction method of Liolios (2001) from non-resonant CNO reactions to more advanced proton-induced reactions including those with resonances, such as rp-process reactions. The study addresses the screening puzzle where experimental screening energies consistently exceed theoretical predictions (Langanke and Rolfs, 1989; Engstler et al., 1988), and recent experiments in metals (Raiola et al., 2002) show the same discrepancy. The results enable more accurate determination of astrophysical S-factors for future low-energy experiments, particularly relevant to the LUNA collaboration's measurements of 14N(p,γ)15O, which directly impact solar neutrino flux predictions from the CNO cycle (Bahcall et al., 2002) and rp-process nucleosynthesis.
