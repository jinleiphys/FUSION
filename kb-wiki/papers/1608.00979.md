# On the spin excitation energy of the nucleon in the Skyrme model

> In the Skyrme model of nucleons and nuclei, the spin excitation energy of the nucleon is traditionally calculated by a fit of the rigid rotor quantization of spin/isospin of the fundamental Skyrmion (the hedgehog) to the masses of the nucleon and the Delta resonance. The resulting, quite large spin excitation energy of the nucleon of about $ 73\, \mbox{MeV}$ is, however, rather difficult to reconcile with the small binding energies of physical nuclei, among other problems. Here we argue that a more reliable value for the spin excitation energy of the nucleon, compatible with many physical constraints, is about $ 16\, \mbox{MeV}$. The fit of the rigid rotor to the Delta, on the other hand, is problematic in any case, because it implies the use of a nonrelativistic method for a highly relativistic system.

# On the spin excitation energy of the nucleon in the Skyrme model

## Key claim

The traditional rigid rotor quantization fit of the Skyrme model to the nucleon and Delta resonance masses, which yields a spin excitation energy of about 73 MeV, is unreliable because it applies a nonrelativistic method to a highly relativistic system (the Delta). The authors argue that a more realistic value for the nucleon spin excitation energy, compatible with nuclear binding energies and other physical constraints, is approximately 16 MeV.

## Method

The authors derive a self-consistent relation between the nucleon mass, the root-mean-square nucleon radius, and the spin excitation energy using the moment of inertia for a spherically symmetric energy distribution. They then compare the spin excitation energy to the binding energy per nucleon of infinite nuclear matter and to the volume term in the semi-empirical mass formula (Weizsacker formula). Finally, they compute the classical rotational velocities implied by the rigid rotor quantization for both the nucleon and Delta cases to assess the validity of the nonrelativistic approximation.

## Key numbers

- The traditional spin excitation energy from the Delta fit is 73.2 MeV, obtained from M_Delta - M_N = 4 M_s with M_Delta = 1232 MeV and M_N = 938.9 MeV.
- The proposed spin excitation energy from the self-consistent relation is 15.2 MeV, using M_N = 938.9 MeV, R_rms = 1.25 fm, and hbar = 197.3 MeV fm.
- The binding energy per nucleon of infinite nuclear matter is approximately 16 MeV.
- The volume coefficient in the Weizsacker formula is a_V = 15.5 MeV.
- For M_s = 16 MeV, the nucleon classical rotational frequency is omega_N = 0.187 fm^{-1}, giving equatorial velocities of about 0.2 c.
- For M_s = 73.2 MeV, the nucleon rotational frequency is omega_N = 0.857 fm^{-1} and the Delta rotational frequency is omega_Delta = 1.916 fm^{-1}, firmly in the relativistic regime.
- A fit to lithium-6 properties in the standard Skyrme model gives F_pi = 75.20 MeV, e = 3.263, and M_s = 21.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model analysis of Adkins (1983) who established the traditional rigid rotor quantization and the 73 MeV spin excitation energy from the Delta fit. The authors argue that this fit is problematic, consistent with earlier concerns about relativistic corrections raised by HaKi. The paper builds on recent developments in generalized Skyrme models with small classical binding energies (BPS-type models) and connects to the successful description of nuclear spectra in small nuclei by Wood (2015) and Halcrow and King (2016). The proposed 16 MeV value aligns the Skyrme model with nuclear binding energy systematics from the semi-empirical mass formula and infinite nuclear matter properties.
