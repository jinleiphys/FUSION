# Statistical Production of $B_c$ Mesons in Heavy-Ion Collisions at the LHC Energy

> The recombination production of $B_c$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC energy is facilitated by the abundant and highly thermalized charm ($c$) quarks transported in the deconfined medium created. We study the production of $B_c$ mesons via $c$ and bottom ($b$) quark recombination in a statistical fashion by placing $B_c$ in the position of a member of the family of open $b$ hadrons, which allows us to make quantitative predictions for the modifications of the production fraction ($f_c$) of $B_c$ mesons and its relative production to $B$ mesons in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions with respect to proton-proton ($pp$) collisions at the same energy. The statistical production yield of $B_c$ mesons is converted into the transverse momentum ($p_T$) distribution with the shape computed from resonance recombination using the $c$- and $b$-quark phase space distributions that have been simulated via Langevin diffusion and constrained by open $c$- and $b$-hadron observables. Supplemented with the component fragmented from $b$-quark spectrum that dominates at high $p_T$, the total $p_T$ spectrum of $B_c$ mesons is obtained and converted into the $p_T$ dependent nuclear modification factor ($R_{\rm AA}$). Both $f_c$ and the integrated $R_{\rm AA}$ exhibit a $\sim5$-fold enhancement in central Pb-Pb collisions relative to the $pp$ reference. Comparison with data measured by the CMS experiment shows decent agreement within theoretical and experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

The recombination of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma leads to a factor of approximately 5 enhancement of $B_c$ meson production in central Pb-Pb collisions relative to proton-proton collisions, but this enhancement is conditional on the high degree of thermalization of charm quarks and is most pronounced at low transverse momentum, tending toward the $pp$ baseline at high $p_T$.

## Method

The authors treat $B_c$ as a member of the open bottom hadron family and compute its absolute production yield in Pb-Pb collisions using the canonical ensemble version of the statistical hadronization model (SHM), which exactly conserves charm and bottom quark numbers. The SHM yield is converted into a transverse momentum ($p_T$) distribution using the resonance recombination model with charm and bottom quark phase space distributions from Langevin diffusion simulations constrained by open heavy-flavor hadron data, and this is supplemented at high $p_T$ by a fragmentation component from $b$ quarks using the Braaten fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- $B_c^-/B^-$ ratio reaches approximately 0.043 in central (0-20%) Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, compared to $0.00790\pm0.00186$ in $pp$ collisions at the same energy
- $B_c^-$ production fraction $f_c$ is approximately 0.013 in central Pb-Pb collisions, compared to $0.00258\pm0.00062$ in $pp$ collisions
- The integrated nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ for $B_c^-$ reaches approximately 5 in central and semicentral Pb-Pb collisions in the full $p_T$ range
- $dN_{c\bar{c}}/dy \sim 20$ charm and $dN_{b\bar{b}}/dy \sim 0.9$ bottom quarks per unit rapidity at mid-rapidity in central Pb-Pb collisions
- $B_c^-$ integrated cross section in $pp$ collisions: $d\sigma^{B_c^-}/dy = 0.104\pm0.0245\ \mu$b at mid-rapidity
- $B_c^-/B^-$ ratio in $pp$ collisions: $0.00790\pm0.00186$

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach previously applied to charmonia and charm hadrons by Braun-Munzinger (2000) and Andronic (2006, 2021) to the case of $B_c$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with transport models by Liu (2012) and Zhao (2022) and instantaneous coalescence models by Chen (2021) that rely on in-medium heavy quark potential inputs. The results enable quantitative predictions for $B_c$ production as a probe of quark-gluon plasma properties and provide a framework for comparing with CMS experimental data (2022) that show hints of $B_c$ enhancement in Pb-Pb collisions.
