# Tractable $\textit{a}$ $\textit{priori}$ Dimensionality Reduction for Quantum Dynamics

> In this short letter, I present a powerful application in dimensionality reduction of the lesser-used Jacobi-Davidson algorithm for the generalized eigenvalue decomposition. When combined with matrix-free implementations of relevant operators, this technique allows for the computation of the dynamics of an arbitrary quantum state to be done in $\mathcal{O}(n)$ time, where $n$ is the size of the original Hilbert space.

## Key claim

The Jacobi-Davidson algorithm, modified to target eigenpairs by the magnitude of their overlap with a specific initial state rather than by eigenvalue, enables an optimal dimensionality reduction for quantum dynamics that scales as O(n) in time and memory, where n is the size of the original Hilbert space, provided the Hamiltonian and operators admit matrix-free implementations.

## Method

The author reorders the energy eigenstates by the magnitude of their overlap with the initial state, then uses a modified Jacobi-Davidson algorithm to compute only the k eigenpairs with the largest overlaps. These k eigenvectors form a projector onto a reduced k-dimensional subspace, within which all dynamics (time evolution and observable expectation values) are computed exactly, while the error in the full space is bounded analytically. The method is demonstrated on a non-integrable generalized long-range Ising model with power-law couplings.

## Key numbers

- For a 14-site spin chain (Hilbert space size n = 16384), the approximation used only k = 8 basis states (less than 0.05% of the full space).
- Relative error in the observable S_x^2/N was on the order of 0.01% for the p = 0.1 case and 1% for the p = 0.9 case.
- The time complexity of the method is O(kn) with matrix-free implementations, compared to O(n^3) for full exact diagonalization.

## Context

This work extends the standard exact diagonalization approach used in No-Core Shell Model and Gamow Shell Model calculations (Barrett 2015) by providing a tractable method for true time evolution rather than just static properties. The technique addresses limitations of other methods such as Matrix Product States, which break down for highly entangled states, and improves upon typical Arnoldi-type eigensolvers that cannot target eigenpairs by overlap with a specific state. The method enables exact verification of approximate techniques and may be applied to condensed matter systems with diverging entanglement and to nuclear dynamics calculations.
