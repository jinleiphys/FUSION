# Quantum Simulation of Lattice QCD with Improved Hamiltonians

> Quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories are anticipated to directly probe the real time dynamics of QCD, but scale unfavorably with the required truncation of the gauge fields. Improved Hamiltonians are derived to correct for the effects of gauge field truncations on the SU(3) Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian. It is shown in $1+1D$ that this enables low chromo-electric field truncations to quantitatively reproduce features of the untruncated theory over a range of couplings and quark masses. In $3+1D$, an improved Hamiltonian is derived for lattice QCD with staggered massless fermions. It is shown in the strong coupling limit that the spectrum qualitatively reproduces aspects of two flavor QCD and simulations of a small system are performed on IBM's {\tt Perth} quantum processor.

## Key claim

Improved Hamiltonians derived via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) can correct for the effects of truncating the SU(3) gauge field Hilbert space, enabling low chromo-electric field truncations to quantitatively reproduce features of the untruncated theory, but only when the operator size truncation in the IMSRG is larger than the correlation length of the system.

## Method

The author derives improved Hamiltonians for the SU(3) Kogut-Susskind lattice gauge theory by applying the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to decouple low and high chromo-electric energy subspaces, then truncating to the low-energy subspace. For larger systems, the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) is used with a truncation on operator size, where the SRG flow is solved on a small lattice and matched to a translationally invariant Hamiltonian on a larger lattice. The performance of these improved Hamiltonians is tested using density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculations for the baryon mass and chromo-electric energy density in 1+1D, and a quantum simulation of meson time evolution is performed on IBM's Perth quantum processor.

## Key numbers

- For the 1+1D massless case at coupling g=2, the relative error in the baryon mass computed with improved Hamiltonians saturates with system size and can be reduced to the percent level by increasing the operator size truncation in the IMSRG.
- For the 1+1D massless case at coupling g=1, the relative error in the baryon mass grows uncontrollably with lattice size for the two-staggered-site IMSRG truncation, but increasing the operator size truncation reduces the error to controllable levels.
- The quantum simulation of a meson on three physical sites (5 qubits) with g=1, m=1 used 50 circuits per Trotter step with 50 calibration circuits, each sampled 10,000 times on IBM's Perth processor.
- The meson mass was computed on a lattice of 2 physical sites (4 staggered) for g=1 and various quark masses m, with the improved Hamiltonian in Eq. (SRG2Links) matching the exact result.

## Context

This work extends the application of similarity renormalization group techniques, previously used in low-energy nuclear physics (PhysRevC.75.061001) and light-front field theories (Glazek 1997, Wegner 2001), to correct for gauge field truncation errors in lattice gauge theory quantum simulations. It builds on prior studies of gauge field digitization (Klco 2019, Ciavarella 2021, Tong 2022) and Schrieffer-Wolff perturbation theory approaches (Schrieffer 1966, Bravyi 2011), demonstrating that SRG-based improved Hamiltonians avoid the convergence issues of perturbative strong-coupling expansions. The work enables quantum simulations of lattice QCD with significantly reduced qubit requirements, as demonstrated by the 5-qubit simulation of meson dynamics on IBM hardware, compared to the 18 qubits needed for the full theory.
