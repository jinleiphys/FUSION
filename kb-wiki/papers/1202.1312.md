# Excited-state spectroscopy of triply-bottom baryons from lattice QCD

> The spectrum of baryons containing three b quarks is calculated in nonperturbative QCD, using the lattice regularization. The energies of ten excited bbb states with J^P = 1/2^+, 3/2^+, 5/2^+, 7/2^+, 1/2^-, and 3/2^- are determined with high precision. A domain-wall action is used for the up-, down- and strange quarks, and the bottom quarks are implemented with NRQCD. The computations are done at lattice spacings of a \approx 0.11 fm and a \approx 0.08 fm, and the results demonstrate the improvement of rotational symmetry as a is reduced. A large lattice volume of (2.7 fm)^3 is used, and extrapolations of the bbb spectrum to realistic values of the light sea-quark masses are performed. All spin-dependent energy splittings are resolved with total uncertainties of order 1 MeV, and the dependence of these splittings on the couplings in the NRQCD action is analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of excited-state energies of triply-bottom baryons, resolving all spin-dependent energy splittings with total uncertainties of order 1 MeV, but the results depend on the specific choices of NRQCD operator coefficients and the extrapolation to physical light-quark masses.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD with domain-wall fermions for the up, down, and strange sea quarks and nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the bottom quarks, including spin-dependent terms through order v^6 in the NRQCD action. Baryon interpolating operators with definite continuum angular momentum J were constructed using up to two covariant derivatives and then subduced to irreducible representations of the double cover of the octahedral group. Two-point correlation functions were computed on seven ensembles of gauge field configurations generated by the RBC/UKQCD collaboration at lattice spacings of approximately 0.11 fm and 0.08 fm, with a spatial volume of (2.7 fm)^3, and the results were extrapolated to physical light-quark masses.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacings used: a ≈ 0.11 fm and a ≈ 0.08 fm (from ensembles with gauge couplings β = 2.13 and β = 2.25, respectively)
- Spatial lattice volume: (2.7 fm)^3 for all ensembles (24^3 × 64 at β = 2.13, 32^3 × 64 at β = 2.25)
- Pion masses on the ensembles range from 0.2950(40) GeV to 0.641(15) GeV
- Smearing radius for bottom quark fields: r_S ≈ 0.14 fm
- All spin-dependent energy splittings resolved with total uncertainties of order 1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the earlier lattice QCD calculation of the Ω_bbb ground-state mass by Meinel (2010) to include excited states, and provides the first nonperturbative QCD test of model-dependent predictions from quark models (e.g., Roberts 2007, Flynn 2011) and potential NRQCD calculations (e.g., Brambilla 2009, Llanes-Estrada 2011). The results enable future comparisons with experimental searches for triply-bottom baryons at the LHC, and the methodology can be applied to other triply-heavy baryon systems.
