# Constraining neutron superfluidity with $r$-mode physics

> We constrain the parameters of neutron superfluidity in the cores of neutron stars making use of the recently proposed effect of resonance stabilization of $r$-modes. To this end, we, for the first time, calculate the finite-temperature $r$-mode spectra for realistic models of rotating superfluid neutron stars, accounting for both muons and neutron-proton entrainment in their interiors. We find that the ordinary (normal) $r$-mode exhibits avoided crossings with superfluid $r$-modes at certain stellar temperatures and spin frequencies. Near the avoided crossings, the normal $r$-mode dissipates strongly, which leads to substantial suppression of the $r$-mode instability there. The extreme sensitivity of the positions of avoided crossings to the neutron superfluidity model allows us to constrain the latter by confronting the calculated spectra with observations of rapidly rotating neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Key claim

The resonance stabilization of normal $r$-modes by superfluid modes can quantitatively explain the observed population of neutron stars in the classical instability window, but only if the neutron superfluid critical temperature profile is sufficiently wide (model I); narrower profiles (model II) fail to stabilize moderately heated sources unless those sources have implausibly low masses.

## Method

The authors calculated finite-temperature $r$-mode spectra for rotating superfluid neutron stars with npeμ composition, using the Cowling approximation and Newtonian framework, expanding oscillation equations to first order in spin frequency Ω and treating neutron-proton entrainment as a small perturbation. They considered two realistic equations of state (APR and BSk24) and two models of the neutron superfluid critical temperature profile, then compared the predicted avoided crossing frequencies ν_{n0,sα}(T^∞) with observational data on neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Key numbers

- The maximum neutron critical temperature in the region where neutron and proton superfluids coexist is constrained to T_{cn max} ∼ (3–6)×10^8 K, with the adopted models corresponding to the upper limit of this range.
- The redshifted maximum critical temperature T_{cn max}^∞ varies in the range ∼ (2.5–4)×10^8 K depending on neutron star mass and equation of state.
- The classical instability window is calculated for a neutron star with mass M = 1.8 M⊙, showing stability strips along the avoided crossing curves ν_{n0,sα}(T^∞).
- Avoided crossing n0,s0 occurs at unrealistically high spin frequencies except when T^∞ approaches T_{cn max}^∞ ∼ (2–4)×10^8 K, where ν_{n0,s0} drops nearly vertically to zero.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological resonance stabilization scenario proposed by Gusakov et al. (2014a, 2014b) by developing it into a quantitative theory with realistic microphysics, including neutron-proton entrainment and muons. It contradicts the classical r-mode instability scenario (Andersson 1998; Friedman and Morsink 1998) which predicted that rapidly rotating neutron stars should not be observed in the instability window. The results enable future constraints on both neutron superfluidity models and the equation of state of superdense matter, though the authors note that other stabilization mechanisms (Ekman layer dissipation, bulk viscosity in hyperon/quark matter, enhanced mutual friction) may also operate.
