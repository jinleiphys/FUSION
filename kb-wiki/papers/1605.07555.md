# Competition between fermions and bosons in nuclear matter at low densities and finite temperatures

> We derive the free energy for fermions and bosons from fragmentation data. Inspired by the symmetry and pairing energy of the Weizsacker mass formula we obtain the free energy of fermions (nucleons) and bosons (alphas and deuterons) using Landau's free energy approach. We confirm previously obtained results for fermions and show that the free energy for alpha particles is negative and very close to the free energy for ideal Bose gases. Deuterons behave more similarly to fermions (positive free energy) rather than bosons. This is due to their low binding energy, which makes them very 'fragile', i.e., easily formed and destroyed. We show that the α-particle fraction is dominant at all temperatures and densities explored in this work. This is consistent with their negative free energy, which favors clusterization of nuclear matter into α-particles at subsaturation densities and finite temperatures. The role of finite open systems and Coulomb repulsion is addressed.

## Key claim

The free energy density for alpha particles is negative and very close to that of an ideal Bose gas, while deuterons exhibit a positive free energy density more similar to fermions, which the authors attribute to the low binding energy of deuterons making them "fragile" (easily formed and destroyed). The alpha-particle fraction is dominant at all temperatures and densities explored, consistent with their negative free energy favoring clusterization into alphas at subsaturation densities and finite temperatures.

## Method

The authors reconstructed quasiprojectiles (QP) from 35 MeV/A beams of 64Zn, 70Zn, and 64Ni on self-supporting targets of the same isotopes, using the 4π NIMROD-ISiS setup at Texas A&M University. Temperatures and densities of the QP were determined using the quantum-fluctuation method from transverse momentum quadrupole fluctuations, average multiplicities, and multiplicity fluctuations, with Coulomb corrections applied. The free energy per nucleon for fragments was derived using Landau's free energy approach, expanded in the neutron-proton asymmetry m, and for N=Z fragments (bosons) a pairing-inspired parametrization was used.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent τ was taken as 2.3 ± 0.1 from prior work.
- For the Landau fit at excitation energy 5.5 MeV/A, the fitted parameters were a = 16.289 ± 0.024, b = -102.871 ± 0.058, and H/T = 0.808 ± 0.002, with c fixed at 115.
- Estimated statistical errors on temperature and density were better than 3%; errors on free energy for protons were 10%, while for deuterons and alphas they were better than 3%.
- Coulomb corrections lowered temperature values by almost 2 MeV, with small effects on densities; uncertainty in source charge gave ±2% for densities and ±6% for temperatures.
- The QP mass range was constrained to 54 ≤ A_QP ≤ 64, with excitation energy bins from 2.5 to 9.5 MeV/A in 1 MeV/A steps.

## Context

This work extends prior analyses of nuclear phase transitions using Landau free energy techniques (Mabiala et al. 2013, 2014) and the quantum-fluctuation method for temperature and density determination (Zheng et al. 2010, 2012, 2013, 2014). The results are compared to ideal Bose gas calculations from Landau and Lifshitz, and to experimentally derived Mott points for deuterons and alphas from Hagel et al. (2012). The findings are relevant for understanding clusterization in low-density nuclear matter, with implications for astrophysical phenomena such as supernovae and neutron star crusts, and for the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
