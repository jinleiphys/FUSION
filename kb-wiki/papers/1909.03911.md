# $J/ψ$ Production Dynamics: Event shape, Multiplicity and Rapidity dependence in Proton+Proton Collisions at LHC energies using PYTHIA8

> High-multiplicity pp collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies have created special importance in view of the Underlying Event (UE) observables. The recent results of LHC, such as long range angular correlation, flow-like patterns, strangeness enhancement etc. in high multiplicity events are not yet completely understood. In the same direction, the understanding of multiplicity dependence of J/$ψ$ production is highly necessary. Transverse spherocity, which is an event shape variable, helps to investigate the particle production by isolating the hard and the soft components. In the present study, we have investigated the multiplicity dependence of J/$ψ$ production at mid-rapidity and forward rapidity through the transverse spherocity analysis and tried to understand the role of jets by separating the isotropic and jetty events from the minimum bias collisions. We have analyzed the J/$ψ$ production at the mid-rapidity and forward rapidities via dielectron and dimuon channels, respectively using 4C tuned PYTHIA8 event generator. The analysis has been performed in two different center-of-mass energies: $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 and 13 TeV, to see the energy dependence of jet contribution to the multiplicity dependence study of J/$ψ$ production. Furthermore, we have studied the production dynamics through the dependence of thermodynamic parameters on event multiplicity and transverse spherocity.

# J/ψ Production Dynamics: Event shape, Multiplicity and Rapidity dependence in Proton+Proton Collisions at LHC energies using PYTHIA8

## Key claim
Using PYTHIA8 simulations with the 4C tune, the authors find that the jet contribution to J/ψ production is larger at mid-rapidity than at forward rapidity and is independent of center-of-mass energy, but the paper's conclusions are conditional on the specific PYTHIA8 tune and the event classification scheme (lowest 20% spherocity for jetty, highest 20% for isotropic events).

## Method
The authors generated inelastic non-diffractive pp collisions using PYTHIA8.2 with the 4C tune at √s = 5.02 and 13 TeV, simulating J/ψ → e⁺e⁻ at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.9) and J/ψ → μ⁺μ⁻ at forward rapidity (2.5 < y < 4.0). Events were classified by transverse spherocity (S₀) of charged hadrons at mid-rapidity (|η| < 1.0) into jetty (lowest 20%, 0 < S₀ < 0.37) and isotropic (highest 20%, 0.71 < S₀ < 1) event classes. The transverse momentum spectra of J/ψ were fitted with the Tsallis non-extensive distribution to extract thermodynamic parameters (temperature T and non-extensive parameter q) as functions of multiplicity, rapidity, and event shape.

## Key numbers
- Crossing point (p_T where jetty and isotropic event contributions cross) at mid-rapidity for √s = 5.02 TeV: ranges from 0.50 ± 0.50 GeV/c (multiplicity 5-10) to 6.50 ± 0.82 GeV/c (multiplicity 40-150)
- Crossing point at forward rapidity for √s = 5.02 TeV: ranges from 6.00 ± 0.57 GeV/c (multiplicity 5-10) to 9.50 ± 0.90 GeV/c (multiplicity 40-150)
- Crossing point at mid-rapidity for √s = 13 TeV: ranges from 1.50 ± 0.50 GeV/c (multiplicity 5-10) to 7.00 ± 0.68 GeV/c (multiplicity 40-150)
- Crossing point at forward rapidity for √s = 13 TeV: ranges from 6.50 ± 0.53 GeV/c (multiplicity 5-10) to 10.00 ± 0.69 GeV/c (multiplicity 40-150)
- Event statistics: 6.1×10⁸ (forward) and 1.22×10⁸ (mid) events at √s = 5.02 TeV; 9.6×10⁸ (forward) and 1.14×10⁸ (mid) events at √s = 13 TeV
- Tsallis non-extensive q-parameter is consistent around 1.0-1.2 across all energies, rapidities, and multiplicities
- Minimum p_T cut-off of 0.5 GeV/c applied to avoid QCD divergences; minimum 5 charged particles with p_T > 0.15 GeV/c required for spherocity calculation

## Context
This work extends the ALICE experimental studies of multiplicity-dependent J/ψ production (Abelev 2012, Adamova 2017) by using transverse spherocity to separate jetty and isotropic events, addressing the question of whether QGP-like effects in high-multiplicity pp collisions arise from collectivity or from jet biases. The study uses PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune, which was previously shown to reproduce ALICE J/ψ cross-section data at √s = 5.02 TeV. The results support the ALICE observation (Thakur 2019) that J/ψ production as a function of multiplicity is independent of √s at a given rapidity, and suggest that the different multiplicity-dependent trends at mid and forward rapidity are due to different jet contributions. The work enables future experimental separation of hard and soft contributions to quarkonium production in small systems.
