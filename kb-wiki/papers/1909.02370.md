# Improvement for Color Glass Condensate factorization: single hadron production in pA collisions at next-to-leading order

> High order calculation at semi-hard scale is very important, but a satisfactory calculation framework is still missing. We propose a systematic method to regularize rapidity divergences in the CGC factorization, which makes higher order calculation rigorous and straight forward. By applying this method to single hadron production in pA collision, we find the kinematic constraint effect introduced by hand in previous works comes out automatically, but with different values. The difference is crucial for our next-to-leading order (NLO) result to have a smaller theoretical uncertainty comparing with LO result, which makes high order calculation in CGC factorization to be useful. As a byproduct, the negativity problem found in literature can also be overcome in our framework by a proper choosing of factorization scale.

## Key claim

The authors propose a systematic rapidity regularization method for Color Glass Condensate (CGC) factorization that makes higher-order calculations rigorous, and they find that the kinematic constraint effect introduced by hand in previous works (Watanabe 2015, Iancu 2016) emerges automatically but with different values, which is crucial for their next-to-leading order (NLO) result to have a smaller theoretical uncertainty compared to the leading order (LO) result.

## Method

The authors modify light-cone energy denominators by shifting their power from 1 to 1+η and multiplying by a dimensional factor (X_f p_A^-)^η, then perform minimal subtraction for the resulting 1/η rapidity divergence. They apply this regularization to single inclusive hadron production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions at NLO, using collinear factorization for the proton parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs) while treating the target-nucleus interaction with CGC effective theory. The dipole amplitudes are obtained by solving the leading-log Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling corrections (rcBK), using parameters from Fujii (2013).

## Key numbers

*   For π⁻ meson production at LHC with √s = 5020 GeV and y_h = 1.65, the NLO results remain positive even when p_{h⊥} is as large as 19 GeV, whereas in Watanabe (2015) the positive region only extends from p_{h⊥} = 2.5-3 GeV to 6 GeV.
*   The typical theoretical uncertainty at LO is 30%-50%, while at NLO it is less than 10%-20%, as estimated by varying the factorization scale parameter κ from 0.5 to 2.
*   For the numerical calculation, the factorization scale X_f is chosen as X_f = κ \bar{X} with κ = 1, and X_max = 0.01 is used to avoid X_f becoming too large as ξ → 1.

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation for single hadron production in pA collisions originally performed by Chirilli (2011, 2012), which suffered from negative cross sections at high transverse momentum as noted by Stasto (2013). Previous attempts to solve the negativity problem by Watanabe (2015) and Iancu (2016) introduced kinematic constraints by hand, whereas this paper provides a systematic regularization framework that automatically generates such constraints. The method enables estimation of theoretical uncertainties through factorization scale variation, which was not possible in those fixed-scale approaches, and it can be applied to any process in CGC factorization at NLO and beyond.
