# The Hagedorn spectrum and large $N_c$ QCD in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions

> We show that a Hagedorn spectrum (i.e., spectrum where the number of hadrons grows exponentially with the mass) emerges automatically in large $N_c$ QCD in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions. The approach is based on the study of Euclidean space correlation functions for composite operators constructed from quark and gluon fields and exploits the fact that the short time behavior of the correlators is known in QCD. The demonstration relies on one critical assumption: that perturbation theory accurately describes the trace of the logarithm of a matrix of point-to-point correlation functions in the regime where the perturbative corrections to the asymptotically free value are small.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Hagedorn spectrum (exponential growth in the number of hadrons with mass) emerges automatically in large \(N_c\) QCD in both 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions, but only under the critical assumption that perturbation theory accurately describes the trace of the logarithm of a matrix of point-to-point correlation functions in the regime where perturbative corrections to the asymptotically free value are small.

## Method

The authors construct sequences of sets of single-color-trace, color-singlet local composite operators built from quark and gluon fields, where the number of operators in the \(n^{\rm th}\) set grows as \(2^n\). They study the Euclidean-time correlation matrix of these operators and derive an inequality relating the negative logarithmic derivative of its trace to the sum of the masses of the lowest \(2^n\) mesons. Using asymptotic freedom at short times and analyzing perturbative corrections, they show that this quantity grows at most linearly with \(n\), which forces the number of hadrons to grow at least exponentially with mass, establishing the Hagedorn spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The number of operators in the \(n^{\rm th}\) set is \(N = 2^n\).
* In 3+1 dimensions, the naive mass dimension of each current in set \({\cal S}_n\) is \(4n+3\); in 2+1 dimensions it is \(12n+3\).
* The asymptotically free trace of the logarithmic derivative is \((2^n)(8n+6)/\tau\) for 3+1 dimensions and \((2^n)(24n+6)/\tau\) for 2+1 dimensions.
* The Hagedorn temperature bound is \(T_H \leq 8\log_2(e)/\tau_0\) for 3+1 dimensions and \(T_H \leq 24\log_2(e)/\tau_0\) for 2+1 dimensions, where \(\tau_0\) is a time scale independent of \(n\).
* A fit to nonstrange meson masses from the Particle Data Group up to 2300 MeV yields a Hagedorn temperature of 426 MeV, which is almost 2.5 times larger than the critical temperature \(T_C\) from lattice gauge calculations.

## Context

This work generalizes an earlier argument by Cohen (2011) that applied only to 3+1 dimensions, extending it to 2+1 dimensions and providing a complete demonstration of the perturbative correction scaling. It builds on the approach of Kogan and Zhitnitsky, who computed the spectrum of large \(N_c\) QCD in 1+1 dimensions with adjoint fermions and found Hagedorn behavior. The result enables a rigorous theoretical foundation for the Hagedorn spectrum in large \(N_c\) QCD without assuming stringy dynamics, and it provides a bound on the Hagedorn temperature in terms of the short-time regime where asymptotic freedom applies.
