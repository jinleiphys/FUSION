# Quantum chromodynamics with advanced computing

> We survey results in lattice quantum chromodynamics from groups in the USQCD Collaboration. The main focus is on physics, but many aspects of the discussion are aimed at an audience of computational physicists.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations from the USQCD Collaboration provide non-perturbative determinations of Standard Model parameters and hadronic properties, but the abstract's focus on physics results glosses over the fact that these calculations require enormous computational resources (integrals of dimension greater than 10^8) and are carried out in two distinct steps: first generating gluon field ensembles using supercomputers, then mining those ensembles for physics.

## Method

The authors survey lattice QCD results obtained by discretizing space-time on a finite grid, replacing continuous functional integrals with multi-dimensional integrals evaluated via Monte Carlo importance sampling with weight det M exp(-S_gluons). Quark fields are handled by integrating out Grassmann variables analytically, yielding a sparse matrix M of dimension exceeding 10^8. Calculations proceed in two steps: generating ensembles of gluon fields using leadership-class computers, then computing hadronic observables from selected rows of M^{-1} on capacity computing systems.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling constant from lattice QCD: alpha_s = 0.1170 ± 0.0012 (evaluated at distance r = hbar/m_Z0 c, from hadron spectrum)
- Up quark mass: m_u = 1.9 ± 0.2 MeV/c^2
- Down quark mass: m_d = 4.6 ± 0.3 MeV/c^2
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 88 ± 5 MeV/c^2
- Pion decay constant from MILC: f_pi = 128 ± 3 MeV; from HPQCD: f_pi = 132 ± 2 MeV (experiment: 130.7 ± 0.4 MeV)
- D_s decay constant from HPQCD: f_Ds = 241 ± 3 MeV (experiment: 277 ± 9 MeV, showing 3.8 sigma discrepancy)
- Axial charge of nucleon from lattice QCD: g_A = 1.21 ± 0.08 (Ref. Edwards 2005) and 1.20 ± 0.07 (Ref. Yamazaki 2008) (experiment: 1.269 ± 0.003)
- pi K scattering length for isospin 1/2: a_{1/2} m_pi c/hbar = +0.1725 ± 0.0017^{+0.0023}_{-0.0156}
- pi K scattering length for isospin 3/2: a_{3/2} m_pi c/hbar = -0.0574 ± 0.0016^{+0.0024}_{-0.0058}
- Confining force: F_QCD ≈ 800 MeV fm^{-1} ≈ 15,000 N

## Context

This survey extends the program of lattice QCD calculations from the USQCD Collaboration, building on Wilson's 1974 formulation of lattice gauge theory and subsequent developments in renormalization (Lepage 1989) and algorithms. The results enable precise tests of the Standard Model through CKM unitarity constraints, provide non-perturbative input for interpreting experiments at Jefferson Lab, RHIC, and the LHC, and make predictions (such as pi K scattering lengths) awaiting experimental confirmation at facilities like DiRAC. The work also sets the stage for computing nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions from first principles, with implications for neutron star physics.
