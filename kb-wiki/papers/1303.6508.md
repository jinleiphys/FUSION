# Superscaling in electron-nucleus scattering and its link to CC and NC QE neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The superscaling approach (SuSA) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, based on the assumed universality of the scaling function for electromagnetic and weak interactions, is reviewed. The predictions of the SuSA model for bot CC and NC differential and total cross sections are presented and compared with the MiniBooNE data. The role of scaling violations, in particular the contribution of meson exchange currents in the two-particle two-hole sector, is explored.

## Key claim

The superscaling approach (SuSA) model, which uses world electron scattering data to predict neutrino cross sections, underestimates MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic data when the standard axial mass (M_A ≈ 1 GeV/c²) is used; the inclusion of two-particle two-hole meson-exchange currents improves agreement but does not fully resolve the discrepancy, and correlation diagrams required by gauge invariance are still missing.

## Method

The authors review the SuSA model, which assumes universality of the scaling function extracted from electron-nucleus scattering for weak interactions, and apply it to both charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering on ¹²C and CH₂ targets. They compare SuSA predictions with and without the addition of fully relativistic two-particle two-hole meson-exchange current contributions to the MiniBooNE data, and also compare with the relativistic mean field model. For neutral-current reactions, they examine the sensitivity of the proton-to-nucleon cross-section ratio to the strange axial form factor g_A^(s).

## Key numbers

- Standard axial mass value M_A ≈ 1 GeV/c² versus the ad hoc value M_A ≈ 1.35 GeV/c² needed to fit MiniBooNE data with the relativistic Fermi gas model
- SuSA neutral-current cross section off CH₂ is about 20% smaller than the relativistic Fermi gas prediction
- The ratio (νp → νp)/(νN → νN) is reported for reconstructed nucleon kinetic energy T_rec from 350 to 800 MeV
- MiniBooNE neutrino energy conditions: E_ν ≈ 0.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SuSA model, originally developed by Amaro et al. (2004) from electron scattering superscaling properties (Alberico 1988, Day 1990, Donnelly 1998), to both charged-current and neutral-current neutrino reactions. It directly addresses the "axial mass puzzle" raised by MiniBooNE results (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010, 2013), where Martini (2010) and Nieves (2011) proposed two-particle two-hole mechanisms as an explanation. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for experiments from MiniBooNE up to several GeV (NOMAD, MINERvA) and demonstrate that neutral-current data are more sensitive to final-state interaction treatment than charged-current MiniBooNE data where the ejected nucleon is not observed.
