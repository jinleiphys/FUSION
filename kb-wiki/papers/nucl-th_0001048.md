# Dileptons and hadron spectral functions in heavy-ion collisions

> We briefly review the dilepton experiments at BEVALAC/SIS and SPS energies for p+p, p+A and A+A collisions as well as our present understanding of the data within transport theoretical simulations. Since dileptons from p+A and A+A collisions in particular probe the in-medium spectral functions of vector mesons, a novel semiclassical off-shell transport approach is introduced on the basis of the Kadanoff-Baym equations that describes the dynamical evolution of broad hadron spectral functions. The implications of the off-shell dynamics -- relative to the conventional on-shell transport dynamics -- are discussed for proton spectra, high energy $γ$, pion, kaon and antikaon production from GANIL to AGS energies in comparison to experimental data.

## Key claim

Dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions probe in-medium vector meson spectral functions, but a novel semiclassical off-shell transport approach based on the Kadanoff-Baym equations is necessary to describe the dynamical evolution of broad hadron spectral functions; the abstract's claim of "understanding" is qualified by the paper's conclusion that the "DLS puzzle" (a factor of 6-7 discrepancy for Ca+Ca at 1 A GeV) might be solved within off-shell dynamics only if there is a strong coupling of low-mass rho mesons to baryons at high baryon density, which remains speculation.

## Method

The author derives a semiclassical off-shell transport theory from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for scalar bosons, keeping only first-order gradient terms in the Wigner representation, and obtains algebraic solutions for the spectral function A_XP = Gamma_XP / [(P^2 - M_0^2 - Re Sigma^ret_XP)^2 + Gamma_XP^2/4]. A testparticle ansatz is used for the Green function F_XP = A_XP N_XP, yielding equations of motion for testparticles in eight-dimensional phase-space that include terms proportional to gradients of the width Gamma_XP, which vanish in the quasiparticle limit. The collision terms are approximated by taking on-shell transition amplitudes from the conventional HSD approach corrected by final phase-space for off-shell masses.

## Key numbers

* The e+e- invariant mass spectra for Ca+Ca at 1 A GeV are underestimated in the regime 0.2 GeV <= M <= 0.6 GeV by a factor of 6-7 when using vacuum spectral functions for rho and omega mesons, and by a factor of 2-3 within the "dropping mass" and "melting" rho scenarios (DLS puzzle).
* At SIS energies (1-2 A GeV), the enhancement of K- for Ni+Ni reactions at 1.8 A GeV amounts to a factor of about 2 due to off-shell effects.
* At GANIL energies (95 A GeV), off-shell effects are most pronounced in high momentum proton spectra and high energy photon production, demonstrated in comparison to gamma spectra from the TAPS collaboration for Ar+Au at 95 A GeV.
* At AGS energies of 11 A GeV, no substantial effects from off-shell dynamics could be established within numerical accuracy on rapidity distributions and transverse mass spectra.

## Context

This work extends the conventional on-shell transport approaches (e.g., HSD by Cassing (1999) and UrQMD) by incorporating off-shell dynamics derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equations (Kadanoff and Baym, 1962), building on formal developments by Botermans and Malfliet and by the author's earlier work (Cassing and Juchem, 1999). The approach addresses the "DLS puzzle" where the DLS Collaboration data at 1 A GeV cannot be explained by the "dropping mass" scenario (Brown and Rho) or the "melting" rho scenario (Rapp and Wambach, 1997, 1999) that work at SPS energies; it enables future studies of vector meson spectral functions at high baryon density (2-3 rho_0 for more than 10 fm/c) at energies from 2-10 A GeV, where the scalar quark condensate is expected to approach zero.
