# Measurement of anisotropic radial flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We suggest the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse (radial) rapidity of the final state particles as a more direct measure of the transverse motion of the source than the standard azimuthal multiplicity distribution. Using a sample generated by the AMPT model with string melting, we demonstrate that the azimuthal amplitude of the suggested distribution characterizes the anisotropic radial flow, and coincides with the parameter of anisotropic radial rapidity extracted from a generalized blast-wave parametrization.

## Key claim

The azimuthal amplitude of the mean transverse rapidity distribution directly characterizes the anisotropic radial flow in non-central collisions, and this amplitude coincides with the anisotropic radial rapidity parameter extracted from a generalized blast-wave parametrization, but only when using the AMPT model with string melting for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors generated a sample of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using the AMPT model with string melting. They constructed the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity of final state hadrons within a longitudinal rapidity window of |y_L|<0.1, and fitted it with a Fourier expansion to extract the isotropic and anisotropic components. They then performed simultaneous fits of the transverse momentum spectra for six particle species (pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, and anti-p) and their differential elliptic flow v2(pT) using a generalized blast-wave parametrization with a Tsallis distribution, extracting the temperature, isotropic radial flow rapidity, and anisotropic radial flow rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Isotropic mean rapidity from the azimuthal distribution: y_T0 = 1.3371 +/- 0.0001 (minimum bias sample)
- Anisotropic amplitude from the azimuthal distribution: y_T2 = 0.0334 +/- 0.0002 (minimum bias sample)
- Blast-wave fit results: T = 96.1 +/- 1.0 MeV, rho_0 = 0.73 +/- 0.01, rho_2 = 0.035 +/- 0.003 (minimum bias sample)
- The anisotropic radial rapidity parameter rho_2 = 0.035 +/- 0.003 is very close to y_T2 = 0.0334 +/- 0.0002

## Context

This work extends the standard approach of using azimuthal multiplicity distributions and Fourier coefficients (v2) to study flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions, as developed by Voloshin, Poskanzer, and Snellings (2008). The proposed observable provides a more direct measure of the transverse motion of the source than multiplicity-based methods, and is important for hydrodynamic calculations and for direct measurements of shear viscosity in relativistic heavy ion collisions, as discussed by Wang et al. (2009). The method enables experimental determination of anisotropic radial flow parameters without requiring blast-wave fitting of spectra and elliptic flow.
