# Nuclear Lattice Simulations with Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We study nuclear and neutron matter by combining chiral effective field theory with non-perturbative lattice methods. In our approach nucleons and pions are treated as point particles on a lattice. This allows us to probe larger volumes, lower temperatures, and greater nuclear densities than in lattice QCD. The low energy interactions of these particles are governed by chiral effective theory and operator coefficients are determined by fitting to zero temperature few-body scattering data. Any dependence on the lattice spacing can be understood from the renormalization group and absorbed by renormalizing operator coefficients. In this way we have a realistic simulation of many-body nuclear phenomena with no free parameters, a systematic expansion, and a clear theoretical connection to QCD. We present results for hot neutron matter at temperatures 20 to 40 MeV and densities below twice nuclear matter density.

## Key claim

The authors combine chiral effective field theory with non-perturbative lattice methods to simulate nuclear and neutron matter, treating nucleons and pions as point particles on a lattice. The approach claims to have "no free parameters" and a "systematic expansion" with a clear connection to QCD, but this is conditional on finding a window of lattice cutoffs where many-body results are independent of the cutoff up to higher-order terms, a condition they state must be verified numerically.

## Method

The authors construct a Euclidean lattice action for neutrons and neutral pions using chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting, including lowest-order interactions (pion-nucleon coupling, neutron contact term) and a temporally-improved discretization to reduce dependence on the temporal lattice spacing. They compute the partition function via Monte Carlo sampling of the functional integral, with operator coefficients determined by fitting to zero-temperature few-body scattering data. The neutron contact interaction is treated using a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary field.

## Key numbers

* Hot neutron matter was studied at temperatures between 20 and 40 MeV.
* Densities considered were below twice nuclear matter density.
* The pion decay constant used is \(F_{\pi}^{phys} \approx 183\) MeV.
* The lattice spacing \(a\) is chosen so that \(m_{\pi} < \pi a^{-1} < \Lambda_{\chi}\), where \(\Lambda_{\chi}\) is the chiral symmetry breaking scale.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Muller, Koonin, Seki, and van Kolck (1999), who first studied infinite nuclear and neutron matter on a spatial lattice using an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, by including pion degrees of freedom and using chiral effective field theory with Weinberg power counting. It contrasts with traditional many-body methods such as Green's function Monte Carlo used by Carlson (2003), Wiringa (2002), and Pieper (2002), which are not directly grounded in QCD. The paper enables future studies of nuclear matter at finite density and temperature with a systematic error estimate, and the authors note that exact chiral symmetry on the lattice and inclusion of charged nucleons and pions will be investigated in future work.
