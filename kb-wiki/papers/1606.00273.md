# Influence of short-range correlations in neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Background: Nuclear short-range correlations (SRCs) are corrections to mean-field wave functions connected with the short-distance behavior of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. These SRCs provide corrections to lepton- nucleus cross sections as computed in the impulse approximation (IA). Purpose: We want to investigate the influence of SRCs on the one-nucleon (1N) and two-nucleon (2N) knockout channel for muon-neutrino induced processes on a $^{12}$C target at energies relevant for contemporary measurements. Method: The model adopted in this work, corrects the impulse approximation for SRCs by shifting the com- plexity induced by the SRCs from the wave functions to the operators. Due to the local character of the SRCs, it is argued that the expansion of these operators can be truncated at a low order. Results: The model is compared with electron-scattering data, and two-particle two-hole responses are presented for neutrino scattering. The contributions from the vector and axial-vector parts of the nuclear current as well as the central, tensor and spin-isospin part of the SRCs are studied. Conclusions: Nuclear SRCs affect the 1N knockout channel and give rise to 2N knockout. The exclusive neutrino-induced 2N knockout cross section of SRC pairs is shown and the 2N knockout contribution to the QE signal is calculated. The strength occurs as a broad background which extends into the dip region.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRCs) provide corrections to the impulse approximation for neutrino-nucleus scattering, and their effect can be captured by shifting complexity from the nuclear wave functions to the operators, with the expansion truncated at low order due to the local character of SRCs.

## Method

The authors compute transition matrix elements of the one-body nuclear current between correlated nuclear states, translating this into an effective operator with one- and two-body parts acting on uncorrelated Hartree-Fock wave functions. Continuum and bound-state wave functions are computed using a Hartree-Fock method with the same Skyrme-type interaction, ensuring orthogonality of initial and final states. The model accounts for central, tensor, and spin-isospin components of the short-range correlations, and is applied to both one-nucleon (1N) and two-nucleon (2N) knockout channels for electron and muon-neutrino scattering on a carbon-12 target.

## Key numbers

- For exclusive 2N knockout in 12C(nu_mu, mu- N_a N_b) at E_nu_mu = 750 MeV, E_mu = 550 MeV, theta_mu = 15 degrees, and T_p = 50 MeV, most strength resides in a region with initial pair center-of-mass momentum P_12 less than 300 MeV/c.
- The semi-exclusive 12C(nu_mu, mu- p) cross section is studied at E_nu_mu = 750 MeV, E_mu = 550 MeV, for three muon scattering angles with in-plane kinematics (phi_p = 0 degrees).
- For electron scattering, the longitudinal (W_CC) and transverse (W_T) responses for 1p1h contributions to 12C(e,e') are shown for three values of the momentum transfer q, compared with data from Barreau (1983) and Jourdan (1996).

## Context

This work extends the Ghent group's model for photoinduced and electroinduced 1p1h and 2p2h reactions (Ryckebusch 1993, Ryckebusch 1997, Janssen 1999) into the weak sector for neutrino-nucleus scattering. It contrasts with the Fermi-gas-based approaches of Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011) by using a Hartree-Fock framework that preserves shell structure, and with the SuSA approach (Amaro 2004) by explicitly including SRCs rather than absorbing them into a phenomenological scaling function. The results enable predictions for exclusive neutrino-nucleus measurements being pursued by the ArgoNeuT, MicroBooNE, DUNE, MINERvA, and NOvA collaborations.
