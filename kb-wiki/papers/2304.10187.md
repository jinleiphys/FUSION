# $ Δ$ baryon spectroscopy in lattice QCD

> A variational analysis is performed within the framework of lattice QCD to extract the masses of the spin-3/2 positive parity $ Δ^+ $ baryons, including radial excitations. $2+1$ flavour dynamical gauge-field configurations provided by the PACS-CS collaboration via the ILDG are considered. To improve our interpolator basis, we perform an iterative process of source and sink smearing and solve a generalised eigenvalue problem using the resulting fermion operators. We obtain a clear signal for the ground and first excited states at a light quark mass corresponding to $ m_π= 413 $ MeV. Furthermore, we show that one can use the eigenvectors obtained in this method to investigate the nature of these states, allowing us to classify our results as $ 1s $ and $ 2s $ states for the ground and first excited states respectively. Finally, we briefly highlight the method of Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory which can be used to make comparison with quark model expectations.

# Δ Baryon Spectroscopy in Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The variational analysis extracts masses for the ground and first excited spin-3/2 positive parity Δ⁺ baryons at a single light quark mass corresponding to mπ = 413 MeV, but the first excited state mass (2.160 GeV) sits higher than the expected Δ(1600) resonance, and the authors note that a simple 3-quark interpolating field does not appear to see this state.

## Method
A variational analysis was performed on 2+1 flavour dynamical gauge-field configurations from the PACS-CS collaboration (32³×64 lattice, a = 0.0961 fm). An iterative Gaussian smearing process was applied to source and sink interpolating fields at four smearing levels (16, 35, 100, 200 sweeps), and a generalised eigenvalue problem was solved to extract masses. Spin projection to isolate J = 3/2 states and parity projection to select positive parity were applied prior to constructing effective mass functions and performing covariance matrix fits.

## Key numbers
- Ground state Δ mass: mΔ⁰ = 1.439 ± 0.007 GeV, with χ²/dof = 0.972
- First excited state Δ mass: mΔ¹ = 2.160 ± 0.029 GeV, with χ²/dof = 0.925
- Pion mass at the simulated quark mass: mπ = 413 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0961(13) fm, with Sommer parameter r₀ = 0.4921(64) fm
- Smearing parameter: α = 0.7, with δt = 2 used in effective mass construction
- Variational parameters: t₀ = 18, Δt = 2, relative to source at tₛ = 16

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of the Δ baryon spectrum, building on techniques developed for the nucleon spectrum by Mahbub (2013) and using spin projection methods from Zanotti (2003). The results are compared with those from the PACS-CS Collaboration (2008), Khan et al. (2021), and the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Bulava 2010) at similar quark masses. The authors aim to provide finite-volume lattice results that can be confronted with Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) analyses, which have been applied to the Roper resonance by Wu (2017) and to the low-lying Δ spectrum by Abell (2021) and Hall (2013), to bridge lattice QCD data with experimental observables.
