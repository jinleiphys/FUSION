# Evolution Equations for Connected and Disconnected Sea Parton Distributions

> It has been revealed from the path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor that there are connected sea and disconnected sea partons. The former is responsible for the Gottfried sum rule violation primarily and evolves the same way as the valence. Therefore, the DGLAP evolution equations can be extended to accommodate them separately. We discuss its consequences and implications vis-a-vis lattice calculations.

# Evolution Equations for Connected and Disconnected Sea Parton Distributions

## Key claim

The DGLAP evolution equations can be extended to separately accommodate connected sea (CS) and disconnected sea (DS) parton distributions, where the CS evolves like valence quarks and the DS evolves like flavor-singlet sea, enabling direct comparison with lattice QCD calculations of connected and disconnected insertion matrix elements.

## Method

The author derives extended NNLO DGLAP evolution equations by classifying parton degrees of freedom according to the topology of quark paths in the Euclidean path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor: connected insertions (CI) yield valence and CS partons for u and d flavors, while disconnected insertions (DI) yield DS partons for u, d, s, and c. The operator product expansion shows that CI operators do not mix with glue operators, while DI operators do, leading to separate evolution equations where CS evolves like valence and DS can evolve from gluons but cannot evolve into valence or CS. The extended set of 11 equations reduces to the standard 9 DGLAP equations under linear combinations.

## Key numbers

- The NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule gives $\int^1_0 dx [F^p_2(x)-F^n_2(x)]/x = 0.235 \pm 0.026$, a 4 sigma deviation from the Gottfried sum rule value of 1/3
- The x-integrated difference of sea quarks is $\int^1_0 [\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)] dx = 0.148 \pm 0.039$
- The lattice result for the ratio $R = \langle x\rangle_{s+\bar{s}} / \langle x\rangle_{u+\bar{u}}(DI) = 0.857(40)$ is used to extract CS partons at $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$
- The CS partons are extracted in the region $0.03 < x < 0.4$ at $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the standard DGLAP evolution framework (Moch, Vermaseren, Vogt 2004; Curci, Catani, Grazzini 2008) by incorporating the path-integral classification of sea partons developed by Liu (1993, 1998, 1999), which showed that Gottfried sum rule violation comes exclusively from connected sea partons at the isospin symmetry limit. The separation of CS and DS enables direct comparison with lattice QCD calculations of moments from connected and disconnected insertions (Deka 2013; Yang 2016; Alexandrou 2016), and can help constrain global PDF analyses that currently lump CS and DS together as total sea. The approach builds on an earlier attempt to separate CS from DS (Liu 2012) that combined CT10 global fitting, HERMES data on strangeness, and lattice results.
