# How Robust is the N = 34 Subshell Closure? First Spectroscopy of $^{52}$Ar

> The first $γ$-ray spectroscopy of $^{52}$Ar, with the neutron number N = 34, was measured using the $^{53}$K(p,2p) one-proton removal reaction at $\sim$210 MeV/u at the RIBF facility. The 2$^{+}_{1}$ excitation energy is found at 1656(18) keV, the highest among the Ar isotopes with N $>$ 20. This result is the first experimental signature of the persistence of the N = 34 subshell closure beyond $^{54}$Ca, i.e., below the magic proton number Z = 20. Shell-model calculations with phenomenological and chiral-effective-field-theory interactions both reproduce the measured 2$^{+}_{1}$ systematics of neutron-rich Ar isotopes, and support a N = 34 subshell closure in $^{52}$Ar.

## Key claim

The first gamma-ray spectroscopy of 52Ar shows a 2+1 excitation energy of 1656(18) keV, the highest among argon isotopes with neutron number N > 20, providing experimental evidence that the N = 34 subshell closure persists below the magic proton number Z = 20, though the authors note that there is no direct correlation between the measured 2+1 energy and the size of the shell gap defined by effective single-particle energies.

## Method

The experiment used a 53K(p,2p) one-proton removal reaction at approximately 210 MeV/u at the RIBF facility, with a 53K secondary beam impinging on a liquid hydrogen target. Reaction residues were identified using the BigRIPS fragment separator and SAMURAI spectrometer, while de-excitation gamma rays were measured by the DALI2+ spectrometer with Doppler correction enabled by reaction vertex reconstruction from the MINOS time projection chamber.

## Key numbers

- 2+1 excitation energy in 52Ar: 1656(18) keV, the highest among Ar isotopes with N > 20
- 2+2 state at 2295(39) keV with 3-sigma significance
- Inclusive cross section for 53K(p,2p)52Ar: 1.9(1) mb
- Partial cross sections: 0.9(2) mb to the 1656 keV state, 0.4(1) mb to the 2295 keV state, and 0.7(3) mb to the ground state
- 438 counts of 52Ar accumulated over seven days of data taking
- 5-sigma significance level for the 1656 keV transition
- MINOS efficiency to detect at least one of the two protons: 92(3) percent

## Context

This work extends the study of N = 34 subshell closure beyond 54Ca, where it was previously suggested by Steppenbeck (2013) and confirmed by mass measurements of 55-57Ca by Michimasa (2018). The measured 2+1 systematics show a pronounced enhancement at N = 34 relative to N = 32 in argon, unlike the decreasing trend observed in calcium, titanium, and chromium isotopes. The results enable benchmarking of many-body methods including valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) and coupled-cluster calculations, and support shell-model predictions of 48Si as a new doubly magic nucleus.
