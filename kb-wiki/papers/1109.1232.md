# A critical look at the role of the bare parameters in the renormalization of Phi-derivable approximations

> We revisit the renormalization of Phi-derivable approximations from a slightly different point of view than the one which is usually followed in previous works. We pay particular attention to the question of the existence of a solution to the self-consistent equation that defines the two-point function in the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism and to the fact that some of the ultraviolet divergences which appear if one formally expands the solution in powers of the bare coupling do not always appear as divergences at the level of the solution itself. We discuss these issues using a particular truncation of the Phi functional, namely the simplest truncation which brings non-trivial momentum and field dependence to the two-point function.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in Phi-derivable approximations, some ultraviolet divergences that appear when one formally expands the self-consistent solution in powers of the bare coupling do not actually appear as divergences in the solution itself, and that the existence of a solution to the self-consistent two-point function equation in the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism is not guaranteed for all values of the bare parameters.

## Method

The authors revisit the renormalization of Phi-derivable approximations using a particular truncation of the Phi functional, specifically the simplest truncation that introduces nontrivial momentum and field dependence into the two-point function. They analyze the self-consistent equation for the two-point function and examine the ultraviolet behavior by comparing the solution obtained from a "Lambda-flow" (a differential equation in the cutoff) with an iterative expansion in powers of the bare coupling.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the renormalization of Phi-derivable approximations by focusing on the existence of solutions to the self-consistent gap equation, a question that earlier treatments (such as those by van Hees and Knoll, and by Blaizot, Iancu, and Reinosa) had not fully addressed. The paper shows that the standard iterative expansion in the bare coupling can produce spurious divergences that are absent in the actual self-consistent solution, and that the renormalization procedure must account for the fact that the self-consistent solution may cease to exist beyond a critical cutoff. This enables a more careful approach to renormalizing non-perturbative approximations in quantum field theory, particularly for applications to thermal field theory and the study of phase transitions.
