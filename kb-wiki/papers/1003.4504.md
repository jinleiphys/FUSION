# Study of the near threshold pp-->ppK+K- reaction in view of the K+K final state interaction

> Measurements of the pp-->ppK+K- reaction, performed near the kinematical threshold with the experiment COSY-11 at the Cooler Synchrotron COSY, reveal a significant discrepancy between obtained excitation function and theoretical expectations neglecting interactions of kaons. In order to deepen our knowledge about the low energy dynamics of the ppKK system we investigated population of events for the pp-->ppK+K- reaction as a function of the invariant masses of two particle subsystems. Based for the first time on the low-energy K+K- invariant mass distributions and the generalized Dalitz plot analysis, we estimated the scattering length for the K+K- interaction.

## Key claim

The analysis of the near-threshold \(pp\to ppK^+K^-\) reaction provides the first estimate of the \(K^+K^-\) scattering length from low-energy invariant mass distributions, but the result carries large uncertainties and cannot distinguish between isospin I=0 and I=1 states of the \(K^+K^-\) system.

## Method

The COSY-11 collaboration measured the \(pp\to ppK^+K^-\) reaction at excess energies Q = 10 MeV and Q = 28 MeV using the Cooler Synchrotron COSY. Events were analyzed via generalized Dalitz (Goldhaber) plots of the \(K^+K^-\) and \(pp\) invariant mass distributions, corrected for acceptance and detection efficiency. The experimental distributions were compared to Monte Carlo simulations that included final state interactions in the \(pp\), \(pK^-\), and \(K^+K^-\) subsystems, with the \(K^+K^-\) scattering length treated as a free parameter and determined using a Poisson likelihood \(\chi^2\) statistic.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to the experimental data yields \(\left|Re(a_{K^+K^-})\right| = 0.5^{+4}_{-0.5}\) fm and \(Im(a_{K^+K^-}) = 3 \pm 3\) fm.
- Data were collected at two excess energies: Q = 10 MeV (27 events) and Q = 28 MeV (30 events).
- The total cross section at threshold is more than seven orders of magnitude smaller than the total proton-proton production cross section.
- The four-body phase space calculation (no final state interactions) differs from data by two orders of magnitude at Q = 10 MeV and by a factor of about five at Q = 28 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by the COSY-11 collaboration (Wolke, Quentmeier, Winter), ANKE, and DISTO, which had revealed a discrepancy between the measured excitation function and theoretical expectations neglecting kaon interactions. Previous calculations including only \(pp\) and \(pK^-\) final state interactions (ANKE collaboration) failed to describe data very close to threshold, motivating the inclusion of the \(K^+K^-\) interaction. The result enables future studies of the scalar resonances \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\), whose interpretation as \(K\bar{K}\) molecules depends on the strength of the \(K\bar{K}\) interaction.
