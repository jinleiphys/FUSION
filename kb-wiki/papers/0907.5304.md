# Bound-Free Electron-Positron Pair Production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The bound-free electron-positron pair production is considered for relativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular, cross sections are calculated for the pair production with the simultaneous capture of the electron into the 1s ground state of one of the ions and for energies that are relevant for the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Colliders (LHC). In the framework of perturbation theory, we applied Monte-Carlo integration techniques to compute the lowest-order Feynman diagrams amplitudes by using Darwin wave functions for the bound states of the elec- trons and Sommerfeld-Maue wave functions for the continuum states of the positrons. Calculations were performed especially for the collision of Au + Au at 100 GeV/nucleon and Pb + Pb at 3400 GeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The bound-free electron-positron pair production (BFPP) cross sections calculated using lowest-order perturbation theory with Darwin wave functions for bound electrons and Sommerfeld-Maue wave functions for continuum positrons agree well with prior computations at RHIC energies but are about 20% lower at LHC energies, with the distortion of the positron wave function due to electron shielding found to be negligible.

## Method

The authors applied Monte-Carlo integration techniques to compute the lowest-order Feynman diagrams (direct and crossed terms) for BFPP in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. They used Darwin wave functions for the bound 1s electron states and Sommerfeld-Maue wave functions for the continuum positron states, incorporating a normalization constant to account for distortion of the positron wave function near the capturing nucleus. The cross sections were obtained by integrating over impact parameter and summing over positron momenta, with the integrands tested on approximately 10 million randomly chosen positions for convergence.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at 100 GeV/nucleon (RHIC): BFPP cross section = 94.5 barn, in excellent agreement with the 94.9 barn from Meier et al.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 2957 GeV (LHC): BFPP cross section = 202 barn, compared to 225 barn from Meier et al. (about 20% lower)
- BFPP cross sections increase by about 3 orders of magnitude when going from nuclear charge Z = 10 to Z = 90 at both 100 GeV/nucleon and 3400 GeV/nucleon
- BFPP cross section for capture into the 1s ground state is suppressed by about three orders of magnitude compared to free electron-positron pair production for all transverse momenta between 0.1 and 100 MeV/c
- Total numerical errors in the computations are estimated to be less than or approximately five percent

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown and Weneser (mid-1990s), who used large-basis coupled-channel Dirac-equation methods and derived a simple formula sigma_BFPP = A ln(gamma) + B for ultra-relativistic energies. The results are compared with the Feynman-Monte Carlo computations by Rhoades-Brown and coworkers, which found cross sections about a factor of 3 larger than the Weizsacker-Williams calculations by Baur and Bertulani. The present calculations verify the expected scaling behavior: BFPP cross sections scale approximately with (Z alpha)^8 ln(gamma), while free pair production scales with (Z alpha)^4 ln^3(gamma). These results are relevant for predicting beam losses at RHIC and LHC facilities, where BFPP can produce a separate beam of one-electron ions that may strike the beam-pipe about 140 meters from the interaction point.
