# Hadronic structure on the light-front VIII. Light scalar and vector mesons

> We use the QCD instanton vacuum model to discuss the emergence of the light scalar and vector mesons on the light front. We take into account both the instanton and anti-instanton single and molecular interactions on the light quarks, in the form of non-local effective interactions. Although the molecular induced interactions are suppressed by a power of the packing fraction, they are still sufficient to bind the vector mesons, while keeping most of the scalar spectrum relatively unchanged. We explicitly derive the light front distribution amplitudes (DAs) and partonic functions (PDFs) for the scalar and vector mesons, and compare them after pertinent QCD evolution, to the available empirical and lattice measured counterparts. The Dirac electric form factors for both the pion and rho meson are derived, and shown to compare well with current data.

## Key claim

The QCD instanton vacuum model, including both single instanton/anti-instanton and molecular (instanton-anti-instanton) interactions, can generate light vector mesons on the light front even though the molecular interactions are suppressed by a power of the packing fraction relative to the single-instanton interactions; the molecular interactions are sufficient to bind vector mesons while leaving most of the scalar spectrum relatively unchanged.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD instanton vacuum model with non-local effective interactions derived from single instantons and anti-instantons (the 't Hooft determinantal interactions) and from instanton-anti-instanton molecular configurations. They construct the light front Hamiltonian in leading order in 1/N_c, derive bound state equations for scalar and vector mesons, and solve them to obtain light front wavefunctions. Using these wavefunctions, they compute distribution amplitudes (DAs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), and Dirac electric form factors, then evolve the DAs and PDFs using QCD evolution and compare to empirical data and lattice results.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size: ρ ≈ 0.31 fm
- Mean instanton plus anti-instanton density: n_{I+Ī} ∼ 1 fm^{-4}
- Critical scalar coupling for chiral symmetry breaking in the chiral limit: g^{cr}_S ≈ 2.981 π² ρ², which corresponds to approximately 72.64 GeV^{-2} for ρ ≈ (636 MeV)^{-1}
- The ratio of molecular to single-instanton coupling: G_{IĪ} = G_I² ξ / (128π⁴ ρ²), where ξ is a positive dimensionless hopping parameter

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series on hadronic structure on the light front (Shuryak 2021, 2022; Liu 2023) by incorporating instanton-anti-instanton molecular interactions to generate vector mesons, which the single-instanton 't Hooft interactions alone cannot bind. The approach contrasts with phenomenological light cone formulations (Jia 2018, Moita 2021, Aliev 2009) and covariant formulations (Maris 2000, Hutauruk 2016, Bashir 2012) by deriving the effective interactions from the QCD instanton vacuum rather than from purely phenomenological models. The results enable future comparisons of light front wavefunctions from the instanton vacuum with lattice QCD simulations using the LaMET procedure (Ji 2013, Zhang 2017) and with empirical parametrizations of PDFs.
