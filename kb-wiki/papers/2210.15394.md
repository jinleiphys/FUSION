# Cumulants from short-range correlations and baryon number conservation - next-to-leading order

> We calculate the baryon number cumulants within acceptance with short-range correlations and global baryon number conservation in terms of cumulants in the whole system without baryon conservation. We extract leading and next-to-leading order terms of the large baryon number limit approximation. Our results extend the findings of Refs. [1,2]. These approximations are checked to be very close to the exact results.

## Key claim

The paper derives next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to baryon number cumulants in a finite acceptance, assuming short-range correlations and global baryon number conservation, expressed in terms of cumulants in the whole system without baryon conservation; these NLO terms significantly improve the approximation of exact results compared to leading-order (LO) terms alone, but the authors note that the short-range correlation strengths cannot assume arbitrary values.

## Method

The authors start from the factorial cumulant generating function for a subsystem with baryon number conservation, assuming short-range correlations parameterized by strengths α_k. They approximate this generating function by expanding in powers of the α_k and then compute the B-th derivative using the general Leibniz formula. The resulting factorial cumulants are converted to cumulants via Stirling numbers of the second kind, and the leading and next-to-leading order terms in a large baryon number B expansion are extracted by multiplying cumulants by appropriate powers of (κ₂^(G))² and taking limits as B → ∞. The results are expressed in terms of global cumulants κ_n^(G) (without baryon conservation) and the acceptance fraction f.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order term for the second cumulant: κ₂^(1,B,LO) = f̄ f κ₂^(G), where f̄ = 1-f and f is the fraction of particles in the acceptance.
- The next-to-leading order term for the second cumulant: κ₂^(1,B,NLO) = (1/2) f̄ f [ (κ₃^(G))² - κ₂^(G) κ₄^(G) ] / (κ₂^(G))².
- The leading-order term for the third cumulant: κ₃^(1,B,LO) = f̄ f (1-2f) κ₃^(G).
- The next-to-leading order term for the third cumulant: κ₃^(1,B,NLO) = (1/2) f f̄ (1-2f) [ κ₃^(G) κ₄^(G) - κ₂^(G) κ₅^(G) ] / (κ₂^(G))².
- The leading-order term for the fourth cumulant: κ₄^(1,B,LO) = f f̄ [ κ₄^(G) - 3f f̄ ( κ₄^(G) + (κ₃^(G))² / κ₂^(G) ) ].
- The next-to-leading order term for the fourth cumulant is given by a lengthy expression involving κ₂^(G) through κ₆^(G) (Eq. (eq:k4-nlo) in the paper).
- The approximations are checked against exact results for B up to 90, with example parameters α_k = 0.1 (1/2)^(k-2) for k=2,...,6, α₁=1, and f=0.25.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper Barej (2022) which derived analytic baryon number factorial cumulant generating functions and leading-order results, and also reproduces and extends the relations between cumulants in a subsystem and cumulants in the whole system without baryon conservation originally obtained by Vovchenko (2020). The new NLO corrections improve the large-B approximation, making it applicable to lower collision energies where antibaryons are neglected; the method could be extended to include antibaryons and other long-range correlations.
