# On the accuracy of using Fokker Planck equation in heavy ion collision

> Application of Fokker-Planck equation to heavy quark transport in the evolving medium created in heavy ion collision is critically scrutinised. We realise that the approach introduces a moderate uncertainty in drag and diffusion coefficients culminating in huge ambiguity in the theoretical prediction of nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$. Quantitative estimation of the error is presented by considering recent developments in this field.

# On the accuracy of using Fokker Planck equation in heavy ion collision

## Key claim
The Fokker-Planck equation introduces a moderate uncertainty (6-15%) in drag and diffusion coefficients when applied to heavy quark transport in rapidly evolving quark-gluon plasma, which propagates into a huge ambiguity (more than 10-18%) in the theoretical prediction of the nuclear modification factor R_AA. The approach is only justified when the medium evolution time scale is much larger than the observation time scale of the Fokker-Planck equation, a condition not satisfied in heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The authors critically examine the time scales relevant to heavy quark transport in quark-gluon plasma: collision time, mean free time, and relaxation time of heavy quarks, as well as the mean free time of medium particles. They compute these time scales using the heavy quark-gluon differential scattering cross-section and Debye mass, then compare them to the observation time scale required for the Fokker-Planck equation to be valid. The uncertainty in drag and diffusion coefficients arising from temperature variation within the observation time window is estimated, and the resulting proportional error in R_AA is derived from the solution to the one-dimensional Fokker-Planck equation.

## Key numbers
- Collision time of heavy quarks varies from 0.3 to 0.2 fm in the temperature range 200-400 MeV (from average transverse momentum transfer), or 0.5-0.25 fm for soft gluon exchange processes.
- Mean free time of heavy quarks lies between 1.5 and 0.99 fm in the same temperature range.
- Relaxation time of heavy quarks is around 1.45-0.72 fm for the temperature range 200-400 MeV.
- Mean free time of medium (bath) particles ranges from 1 to 0.5 fm for the same temperature variation.
- For an observation time of 0.5 fm, the corresponding temperature change in heavy ion collision is about 15-20 MeV.
- Uncertainty in drag and diffusion coefficients is estimated as 6-10% according to some literature, or more than 10-15% according to others.
- The minimum percentage error in the prediction of R_AA is more than 10-18%, depending on different temperature dependences of transport coefficients.

## Context
This work critically scrutinizes the standard approach of using the Fokker-Planck equation with time-dependent transport coefficients for heavy quark transport in evolving quark-gluon plasma, as previously employed by Svetitsky (1988), Rapp (2005), and others. The authors show that the time scales involved (collision time, mean free time, relaxation time) are of the same order, violating the hierarchy required for the Fokker-Planck equation to be valid even for a static medium. This extends concerns raised about the applicability of transport equations to rapidly evolving systems. The paper suggests that alternative approaches, such as modeling heavy quarks as open quantum systems as recently devised for bottomonium by Borghini (2014), may be necessary to mitigate the incompatibility of time scales.
