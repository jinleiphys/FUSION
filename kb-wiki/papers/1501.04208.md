# Parity violation in quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering within the relativistic impulse approximation

> We study parity violation in quasielastic (QE) electron-nucleus scattering using the relativistic impulse approximation. Different fully relativistic approaches have been considered to estimate the effects associated with the final-state interactions. We have computed the parity-violating quasielastic (PVQE) asymmetry and have analyzed its sensitivity to the different ingredients that enter in the description of the reaction mechanism: final-state interactions, nucleon off-shellness effects, current gauge ambiguities. Particular attention has been paid to the description of the weak neutral current form factors. The PVQE asymmetry is proven to be an excellent observable when the goal is to get precise information on the axial-vector sector of the weak neutral current. Specifically, from measurements of the asymmetry at backward scattering angles good knowledge of the radiative corrections entering in the isovector axial-vector sector can be gained. Finally, scaling properties shown by the interference $γ-Z$ nuclear responses are also analyzed.

## Key claim

The parity-violating quasielastic (PVQE) asymmetry is shown to be an excellent observable for extracting precise information on the axial-vector sector of the weak neutral current, particularly the isovector axial-vector radiative corrections, provided measurements are taken at backward scattering angles where nuclear-model uncertainties from final-state interactions and off-shell effects are minimized (dispersion below 5% near the quasielastic peak).

## Method

The authors computed inclusive PVQE electron-nucleus scattering cross sections and the helicity asymmetry within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for bound nucleon wave functions. Final-state interactions (FSI) were incorporated by describing the outgoing nucleon as a scattered solution of the Dirac equation with the same scalar and vector potentials used for bound states (RMF-FSI model), and results were compared with the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), the effective asymptotic momentum approximation (EMA), and the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model. The weak neutral current form factors were constructed from electromagnetic form factors (GKex model) and standard axial-vector parameters, with radiative corrections taken from Liu (2007), and the sensitivity to off-shell ambiguities was tested using the CC1/CC2 prescriptions and Landau, Coulomb, and Weyl gauge choices.

## Key numbers

- The transverse PV responses (̃R^T and ̃R^{T'}) show a reduction of ~20% in their maximum when FSI are included (RMF-FSI vs RPWIA) at q=0.5 GeV and q=1.0 GeV.
- The positive-energy projection (EMA) reduces the transverse PV responses by ~6% relative to the full RMF-FSI result.
- The longitudinal PV response ̃R^L is approximately one order of magnitude smaller than the transverse PV responses.
- At forward scattering angles (θ_e=15°), the dispersion between RPWIA, EMA, and RMF-FSI models is lower than ~1% near the quasielastic peak (ω~150 MeV at q=500 MeV; ω~500 MeV at q=1000 MeV).
- At backward scattering angles (θ_e=140°), the dispersion between models near the quasielastic peak is smaller than ~5%.
- The difference between RFG and RMF-FSI results near the quasielastic peak is lower than 7% at q=500 MeV and decreases for increasing q.
- The PVQE asymmetry for different target nuclei (¹⁶O, ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca) shows differences up to ~10% at ω~0.25 GeV and q=1 GeV.
- The axial-vector form factor at Q²=0 is G_A^{ep}(0)=1.04±0.44, with the uncertainty dominated by the radiative correction R_A^{T=1}=-0.258±0.34 from Liu (2007).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity violation in elastic electron-proton scattering (Gonzalez-Jimenez 2013, 2014; Moreno 2014) to the quasielastic regime with nuclear targets, where the complex nuclear structure introduces additional uncertainties from FSI, off-shell effects, and gauge ambiguities. The results show that the PVQE asymmetry at backward angles is robust against nuclear-model uncertainties, enabling it to complement elastic PVep measurements for determining the isovector axial-vector radiative corrections, while forward-angle measurements are more sensitive to two-body current contributions noted by Amaro (2002), Alberico (1993), and Barbaro (1994). The scaling properties of the interference γ-Z nuclear responses are also analyzed, extending the scaling studies of Caballero (2006) to the parity-violating sector.
