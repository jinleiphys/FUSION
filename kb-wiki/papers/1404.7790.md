# Heavy quark collisional energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma including finite relaxation time

> In this paper, we calculate the soft-collisional energy loss of heavy quarks traversing the viscous quark-gluon plasma including the effects of a finite relaxation time $τ_π$ on the energy loss. We find that the collisional energy loss depends appreciably on $τ_π$ . In particular, for typical values of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio, we show that the energy loss obtained using $τ_π$ = 0 can be $\sim$ 10$\%$ larger than the one obtained using $τ_π$ = 0. Moreover, we find that the energy loss obtained using the kinetic theory expression for $τ_π$ is much larger that the one obtained with the $τ_π$ derived from the Anti de Sitter/Conformal Field Theory correspondence. Our results may be relevant in the modeling of heavy quark evolution through the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma depends appreciably on the finite relaxation time tau_pi, with the effect being largest when using the kinetic theory expression for tau_pi rather than the AdS/CFT expression; for typical viscosity-to-entropy ratios at RHIC and LHC, the difference in energy loss between tau_pi = 0 and tau_pi from kinetic theory is at most 10 percent.

## Method

The authors compute the soft-collisional energy loss of a charm quark traversing an isotropic, non-expanding viscous quark-gluon plasma by linearizing Wong's equations and using the QGP polarization tensor derived from an effective hydrodynamic formalism based on the Entropy Production Variational Method. The energy loss is obtained from the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions evaluated at the Landau damping condition omega = k dot v, with the polarization tensor incorporating a finite relaxation time tau_pi through an effective shear viscosity eta_eff = eta / [1 + i tau_pi (k^alpha u_alpha)]. Results are compared for three cases: tau_pi = 0, tau_pi from Boltzmann kinetic theory (tau_pi = 5 eta/(s T)), and tau_pi from the AdS/CFT correspondence (tau_pi = (2 - ln 2)/(2 pi T)).

## Key numbers

- For a charm quark (mass 1.27 GeV) at temperature T = 0.3 GeV and coupling g = 0.2, the energy loss in the ideal fluid case can be roughly 25 percent larger than in the viscous case with eta/s = 3/(4 pi).
- For typical values of eta/s at RHIC and LHC (0.08 to 0.24), the impact of tau_pi from kinetic theory on dE/dx is at most 10 percent.
- For eta/s less than 0.35, the energy loss calculated including tau_pi from kinetic theory can be up to 20 percent larger than the tau_pi = 0 case.
- The ratio of energy loss for a quark at v = 0.7c to that at v = 0.99c is approximately 0.7, slightly increasing with eta/s.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark collisional energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma by including the effect of a finite relaxation time tau_pi, which had not been considered before in this context. The results are consistent with those of Jiang et al. (2014) for viscous corrections without tau_pi, and the authors note that the effect of tau_pi on energy deposition modifies the diffusive propagation of the quark itself, potentially affecting observables such as quarkonium suppression. The work enables discrimination between the kinetic theory and AdS/CFT models for tau_pi through their different predictions for how energy loss depends on eta/s and quark momentum.
