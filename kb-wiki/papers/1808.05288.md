# Processes that break baryon number by two units and the Majorana nature of the neutrino

> We employ the simplest possible models of scalar-fermion interactions that are consistent with the gauge symmetries of the Standard Model and permit no proton decay to analyze the connections possible among processes that break baryon number by two units. In this context we show how the observation of $n$-${\bar n}$ oscillations and of a pattern of particular nucleon-antinucleon conversion processes --- all accessible through e-d scattering --- namely, selecting from $e^- p \to e^+ {\bar p}$, $e^- p \to {\bar n} {\bar ν}$, $e^- n \to {\bar p} {\bar ν}$, and $e^- n \to e^- {\bar n} $ would reveal that the decay $π^- π^- \to e^- e^-$ must occur also. This latter process is the leading contribution to neutrinoless double beta decay in nuclei mediated by new short-distance physics, in contrast to that mediated by light Majorana neutrino exchange. The inferred existence of $π^- π^- \to e^- e^-$ would also reveal the Majorana nature of the neutrino, though the absence of this inference would not preclude it.

## Key claim

The paper shows that observing a specific pattern of baryon-number-violating processes (n-nbar oscillations plus particular nucleon-antinucleon conversion modes accessible through electron-deuteron scattering) would necessarily imply the existence of the short-distance process π⁻π⁻ → e⁻e⁻, which would reveal the Majorana nature of the neutrino; however, the absence of such an inference would not preclude the neutrino being Majorana.

## Method

The authors construct minimal scalar-fermion models consistent with Standard Model gauge symmetries that permit baryon-number violation by two units (ΔB=2) but forbid proton decay (ΔB=1) at tree level. They enumerate all possible scalar representations and their interactions, then systematically classify which combinations of scalars generate n-nbar oscillations, nucleon-antinucleon conversion processes accessible through e-d scattering, and the ΔL=2 process π⁻π⁻ → e⁻e⁻ that drives short-distance neutrinoless double beta decay. By analyzing which scalar content appears in both ΔB=2 and ΔL=2 models, they identify patterns of observable processes that would reveal the existence of the additional scalars needed for π⁻π⁻ → e⁻e⁻.

## Key numbers

* Neutron Majorana mass limit from non-observation of n-nbar oscillations: 2 × 10⁻³³ GeV at 90% CL (from Abe 2011)
* Existing collider constraints on color-sextet scalars: masses of O(500 GeV) with O(1) couplings from t-quark final state studies
* Scalars coupling to dileptons with masses below 8 GeV are not excluded by CMS same-sign dilepton searches due to b-hadron decay backgrounds
* The e-p conversion processes should be appreciable if scalars possess masses of O(1-10 GeV)
* Earlier claimed limit on scalar mass scale of 1.6 TeV from pp → e⁺e⁺ in ¹⁶O neglects Coulomb repulsion, which should weaken that bound by orders of magnitude
* H-nbarH oscillation limit from astrophysical gamma-ray observations is weakened by a factor of 10⁸ when accounting for Galactic magnetic fields of about 1 nT in cold HI clouds and no less than 0.1 nT in the warm interstellar medium

## Context

This work extends the minimal scalar models of Arnold, Fornal, and Wise (2012) that generate ΔB=2 processes without proton decay, by adding ΔL=2 interactions to study connections between baryon-number and lepton-number violation. It builds on the framework of Babu and Mohapatra (2014), who showed in SO(10) grand unified theory that observing n-nbar oscillations and a ΔB=1 process could imply a Majorana neutrino, but here the authors demonstrate such a connection without requiring proton decay. The paper enables experimental programs at the European Spallation Source (Milstead 2015) and electron-deuteron scattering facilities to use patterns of ΔB=2 discovery to probe the short-distance mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay, complementing direct searches in nuclei.
