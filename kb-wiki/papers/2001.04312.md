# The Mass of Short-Range Correlated Nucleon

> The nucleon properties and structure should be modified by short-range correlations (SRC) among nucleons. By analyzing SRC ratio data, we extract the mass of nucleon in the SRC pair and the expected number of pn-SRC pairs in deuterium, under the assumption that the SRC nucleon mass is universal in different nuclei. The nucleon mass of a two-nucleon SRC pair is $m_{\text{SRC}}=852\pm 18$ MeV, and the number of pn-SRC pairs in deuterium is $n^{\text{d}}_{\text{SRC}}=0.021\pm 0.005$. The mass deficit of the strongly overlapping nucleon can be explained by the trace anomaly contribution to the mass in QCD or alternatively by the vacuum energy in the MIT bag model.

# The Mass of Short-Range Correlated Nucleon

## Key claim

Under the assumption that the short-range correlation (SRC) nucleon mass is universal across different nuclei, the nucleon in a two-nucleon SRC pair has a mass of 852 ± 18 MeV, corresponding to a mass deficit of 86 ± 18 MeV relative to the free nucleon mass; this result depends on the validity of the universal-mass assumption and on the interpretation of SRC ratio data from inclusive electron-nucleus scattering.

## Method

The authors analyzed the correlation between nuclear mass per nucleon and the measured SRC cross-section ratio a₂(A) for several nuclei, using a linear fit based on a decomposition of nuclear mass into contributions from SRC nucleons and mean-field nucleons. The nuclear masses were taken from atomic mass evaluations, and the a₂ data were combined from two analyses of experimental measurements at Jefferson Lab. The fit yielded two free parameters: the SRC nucleon mass m_SRC and the number of pn-SRC pairs in deuterium n^d_SRC.

## Key numbers

- SRC nucleon mass: m_SRC = 852 ± 18 MeV (0.915 ± 0.019 u)
- Number of pn-SRC pairs in deuterium: n^d_SRC = 0.021 ± 0.005
- Mean-field nucleon mass: m_MF = 939.9 ± 5 MeV (1.009 ± 0.005 u)
- Mass deficit per nucleon in NN-SRC: 86 ± 18 MeV
- For ¹²C: number of pn-SRC pairs n_SRC^C = 0.58 ± 0.14, corresponding to 9.7% ± 2.4% of nucleons in NN-SRC state
- Fit quality: χ²/N = 95/9 = 10.5; reduced to χ²/N = 1.6/9 = 0.2 when including 20% model uncertainties in a₂ data

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations in nuclei, building on inclusive measurements of quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering by Frankfurt (1993), Egiyan (2005), Fomin (2011), and Schmookler (2019), and on the generalized contact formalism developed by Weiss (2015, 2016, 2020). The extracted SRC nucleon mass is compared with predictions from the MIT bag model (Chodos 1974) and lattice QCD calculations of the trace anomaly (Yang 2018). The result enables experimental constraints on the QCD decomposition of nucleon mass and on the tensor force responsible for pn-SRC pair formation, and can be tested with future ab initio calculations or improved GCF models.
