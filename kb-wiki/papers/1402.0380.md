# Study of nucleonic matter with a consistent two- and three-body perturbative chiral interaction

> We calculate perturbatively the energy per nucleon in infinite nuclear matter with a chiral N3LO (next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order) two-body potential plus a N2LO three-body force (3BF). The 3BF low-energy constants which cannot be constrained by two-body observables are chosen such as to reproduce the A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element. This enables to study the nuclear matter equation of state in a parameter-free approach.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a chiral N3LO two-body potential plus a N2LO three-body force, with the three-body low-energy constants fixed solely from A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element, can reproduce empirical nuclear matter saturation properties without any additional adjustment. The hedge is that the third-order particle-hole diagram was included only for symmetric nuclear matter, not for pure neutron matter.

## Method
The authors performed perturbative many-body calculations of infinite nuclear matter using a chiral N3LO two-nucleon potential with cutoff Lambda = 414 MeV, combined with a N2LO three-nucleon force. The three-body force was incorporated as a density-dependent two-body potential by summing one nucleon over the filled Fermi sea, and the ground-state energy per nucleon was computed from Goldstone diagrams up to third order in the interaction. The low-energy constants c_D and c_E of the three-body force were fixed by fitting the A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element using hyperspherical harmonics wave functions.

## Key numbers
- c_D = -0.40 GeV^{-1} and c_E = -0.07 GeV^{-1} from fitting A=3 binding energies and triton Gamow-Teller matrix element
- SNM incompressibility K_0 = 279 MeV, compared to empirical value 230 +/- 30 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L = 68 MeV, compared to empirical value centered at 70 MeV
- Cutoff parameter Lambda = 414 MeV used in the chiral potential

## Context
This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory studies of nuclear matter by using a consistent treatment of two- and three-body forces with low-energy constants constrained only by few-body systems. The approach contrasts with earlier calculations where three-body force parameters were adjusted to nuclear matter properties directly. The results enable parameter-free predictions of the nuclear matter equation of state that can be applied to neutron star structure and supernova simulations, and they build on the method of fixing c_D and c_E from triton beta-decay as suggested by Gardestig and Phillips (2006) and developed by Gazit (2008) and Marcucci (2012).
