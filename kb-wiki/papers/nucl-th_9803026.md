# Symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter in the relativistic approach at finite temperatures

> The properties of hot matter are studied in the frame of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. The equations are solved self-consistently in the full Dirac space. For the interaction we used the potentials given by Brockmann and Machleidt. The obtained critical temperatures are smaller than in most of the nonrelativistic investigations. We also calculated the thermodynamic properties of hot matter in the relativistic Hartree--Fock approximation, where the force parameters were adjusted to the outcome of the relativistic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock calculations at zero temperature. Here, one obtains higher critical temperatures, which are comparable with other relativistic calculations in the Hartree scheme.

# Symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter in the relativistic approach at finite temperatures

## Key claim
The critical temperatures for the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter obtained from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations in the full Dirac space are smaller than those from most nonrelativistic investigations, but the authors caution that BHF calculations face numerical convergence problems at low densities and may not be fully applicable in that regime.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations self-consistently in the full Dirac space for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures, using the Brockmann-Machleidt one-boson-exchange potentials (potential B). They also performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations where the force parameters were adjusted to reproduce the RBHF results at zero temperature. The equations were solved with the momentum dependence of the self-energies taken into account, neglecting the imaginary part of the self-energy which was found to be small.

## Key numbers
- Saturation properties of potential B at zero temperature: binding energy E/A = -15.73 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.172 fm⁻³, incompressibility K = 249 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.8 MeV
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter in RBHF: T_c = 10.4 MeV
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter in RHF: T_c = 15.2 MeV
- Critical density for symmetric matter in RBHF: slightly above 1/3 of the saturation density ρ₀
- For comparison, nonrelativistic calculations give critical temperatures in the range 14 to 22 MeV with critical density about 1/3 ρ₀
- For comparison, relativistic Hartree calculations give T_c = 14.4 MeV with ρ_c = 0.31 ρ₀ (Ref. [9]) and T_c ≅ 14 MeV (Ref. [10])
- For asymmetric matter in a recent nonrelativistic calculation: T_c = 20.8 MeV (ρ_c = 0.39 ρ₀) for symmetric matter, decreasing to 8.0 MeV (ρ_c = 0.24 ρ₀) for asymmetry δ = 0.75

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism, previously applied at zero temperature by the authors (Huber, Weber, Weigel, 1995) and others, to finite temperatures. It contrasts with the majority of nonrelativistic treatments which yield higher critical temperatures, and with the Groningen group's RBHF calculation (Ref. [12]) which used a nonunique ansatz for the T-matrix in terms of five Fermi invariants. The authors' approach follows the original scheme of the Brooklyn group, solving in the full Dirac space to avoid ambiguous self-energy results. The findings enable more reliable equations of state for proto-neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that the symmetry energy remains monotonic with density at finite temperatures, contrary to some nonrelativistic equations of state with non-monotonic symmetry energy (Ref. [24]).
