# Quantum Monte Carlo Studies of Relativistic Effects in Light Nuclei

> Relativistic Hamiltonians are defined as the sum of relativistic one-body kinetic energy, two- and three-body potentials and their boost corrections. In this work we use the variational Monte Carlo method to study two kinds of relativistic effects in the binding energy of 3H and 4He. The first is due to the nonlocalities in the relativistic kinetic energy and relativistic one-pion exchange potential (OPEP), and the second is from boost interaction. The OPEP contribution is reduced by about 15% by the relativistic nonlocality, which may also have significant effects on pion exchange currents. However, almost all of this reduction is canceled by changes in the kinetic energy and other interaction terms, and the total effect of the nonlocalities on the binding energy is very small. The boost interactions, on the other hand, give repulsive contributions of 0.4 (1.9) MeV in 3H (4He) and account for 37% of the phenomenological part of the three-nucleon interaction needed in the nonrelativistic Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The relativistic nonlocalities in the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) and kinetic energy individually produce significant effects (e.g., a ~15% reduction in OPEP contribution), but these largely cancel, making the total effect on binding energy very small; the boost interactions, however, give repulsive contributions of 0.4 MeV in ³H and 1.9 MeV in ⁴He, accounting for ~37% of the phenomenological three-nucleon interaction needed in nonrelativistic Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic Hamiltonian in the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics, consisting of relativistic one-body kinetic energy, a two-body potential with relativistic OPEP (nonlocal in configuration space) and a phenomenological part fitted to phase shifts, plus boost corrections expanded to leading order in P²/4m². They used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) methods with Metropolis sampling to evaluate expectation values of this Hamiltonian for ³H and ⁴He, employing variational wave functions built from pair and triplet correlation operators, with relativistic correlation functions approximated by nonrelativistic ones plus a variational parameter λ.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic nonlocality reduces the OPEP contribution in the deuteron by ~2.6 MeV, while the relativistic kinetic energy is smaller by ~ -1 MeV, giving a net effect of 1.6 MeV canceled by changes in the phenomenological potential.
- The boost interactions give repulsive contributions of 0.4 MeV in ³H and 1.9 MeV in ⁴He.
- The boost interactions account for ~37% of the phenomenological part of the three-nucleon interaction needed in nonrelativistic Hamiltonians.
- In the deuteron, the leading relativistic correction to OPEP from the expansion in Q and q variables is 2.7 MeV, the third term gives -0.18 MeV, and the exact value is 2.59 MeV.
- The difference in ⟨H_R⟩ between using a nonrelativistic wave function (λ=0) and a relativistic one (λ=1) for the deuteron is ~0.04 MeV, or about 2% of the binding energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of relativistic effects in light nuclei using quantum Monte Carlo methods (Carlson 1993, Forest 1995) by including, for the first time, the nonlocalities induced by relativistic effects in the one-pion-exchange potential. The results show that while boost interactions provide a significant repulsive contribution that helps explain the underbinding seen in nonrelativistic calculations for A=6-8 nuclei by Pudliner (1995), the nonlocality effects largely cancel. This enables future studies of larger nuclei and provides a framework for incorporating relativistic effects consistently in nuclear Hamiltonians used for electron-nucleus scattering at multi-GeV facilities like TJNAF.
