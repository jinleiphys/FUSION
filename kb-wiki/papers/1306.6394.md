# Dilepton Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The properties of electromagnetic radiation from hot fireballs as created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are reviewed. We first outline how the medium effects in the electromagnetic spectral function, which governs thermal production rates, relate to the (partial) restoration of chiral symmetry. In particular, we show how chiral and QCD sum rules, together with constraints from lattice QCD, can render these relations quantitative. Turning to dilepton data, we elaborate on updates in the space-time evolution and quark-gluon plasma emission rates from lattice-QCD calculations. With a now available excitation function in dilepton spectra from the RHIC beam-energy scan connecting down to SPS energies, we argue that a consistent interpretation of dilepton data emerges. Combining well-constrained space-time evolutions with state-of-the-art emission rates identifies most of the radiation to emanate from around the pseudo-critical temperature, and thus confirms resonance melting as the prevalent mechanism in this regime, compatible with chiral restoration. Recent measurements of a relatively soft slope and large elliptic flow in direct-photon spectra at RHIC and LHC lend further support to this picture.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent interpretation of dilepton and direct-photon data from heavy-ion collisions emerges when combining well-constrained space-time evolutions with state-of-the-art emission rates, identifying most radiation as emanating from around the pseudo-critical temperature and confirming resonance melting as the prevalent mechanism compatible with chiral restoration. The hedge is that this interpretation is "compatible with" rather than proven by chiral restoration, and that a full theoretical understanding of direct-photon elliptic flow has not yet been achieved.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations of in-medium vector-meson spectral functions using effective hadronic Lagrangians with chiral pion interactions and resonance excitations, implemented into finite-temperature field theory. These are combined with quark-gluon plasma (QGP) emission rates from lattice QCD and hard-thermal-loop approaches, then folded over thermal fireball models constrained by hadron data (yields, spectra, and elliptic flow) to compare with dilepton and photon measurements from SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson broadening amounts to a few hundred MeV at hadronic densities of 0.2 fm⁻³
- The pseudo-critical temperature is T_pc ≈ 170 MeV
- Fireball lifetime extracted from NA60 dimuon data: τ_FB = 6.5 ± 1 fm/c
- Fireball cooling from T_i ≈ 200 MeV to T_fo ≈ 120 MeV
- Direct-photon inverse slopes: T_eff = 221 ± 27 MeV at RHIC and 301 ± 51 MeV at LHC
- Average radial flow velocity β = 0.3–0.4 leads to emission temperatures T ≈ 160–200 MeV
- Bulk elliptic flow at RHIC takes about 5 fm/c to develop most of its strength

## Context

This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic radiation from hot QCD matter, building on prior calculations of in-medium vector spectral functions (Rapp 2009, Leupold 2009) and Weinberg sum rules (Weinberg 1967, Das 1967). It connects to lattice QCD results (Borsanyi 2010, Ding 2010, Brandt 2012) and experimental data from NA60 (Arnaldi 2006), CERES/NA45 (Agakichiev 2005), STAR (Geurts 2012), and PHENIX (Adare 2008). The work enables future tests through dilepton elliptic flow measurements and precision mass spectra at collider energies.
