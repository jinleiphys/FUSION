# Model independent determination of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift and proton radius

> We obtain a model independent expression for the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift. This expression includes the leading logarithmic ${\cal O}(m_μα^6)$ terms, as well as the leading ${\cal O}(m_μα^5 \frac{m_μ^2}{m_ρ^2})$ hadronic effects. The latter are controlled by the chiral theory, which allows for their model independent determination. In this paper we give the missing piece for their complete expression including the pion and Delta particles. Out of this analysis and the experimental measurement of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift we determine the electromagnetic proton radius: $r_p=0.8412(15)$ fm. This number is at 6.8$σ$ variance with respect to the CODATA value. The accuracy of our result is limited by uncomputed terms of ${\cal O}(m_μα^5\frac{m_μ^3}{m_ρ^3},m_μα^6)$. This parametric control of the uncertainties allows us to obtain a model independent determination of the error, which is dominated by hadronic effects.

# Model independent determination of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift and proton radius

## Key claim

The authors obtain a model-independent expression for the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift that includes the leading logarithmic O(m_μ α^6) terms and the leading O(m_μ α^5 m_μ^2/m_ρ^2) hadronic effects controlled by chiral theory, yielding a proton radius r_p = 0.8412(15) fm that is at 6.8σ variance with the CODATA value, but with an uncertainty dominated by hadronic effects and limited by uncomputed terms of O(m_μ α^5 m_μ^3/m_ρ^3, m_μ α^6).

## Method

The authors use a combined effective field theory framework of Heavy Baryon Effective Theory (HBET), Non-Relativistic QED (NRQED), and potential NRQED (pNRQED) to compute the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift. They integrate out the pion and Delta particles by matching HBET to NRQED, then integrate out the scale m_μ α to obtain pNRQED, where the spectrum is computed using non-relativistic quantum mechanics perturbation theory combined with perturbative quantum field theory for ultrasoft photons. The hadronic contributions to the two-photon exchange term are computed using chiral perturbation theory including Delta particle effects, which provides the missing piece for a complete model-independent determination.

## Key numbers

- Experimental muonic hydrogen Lamb shift: ΔE^exp = 202.3706(23) meV
- Determined proton radius: r_p = 0.8412(15) fm
- Discrepancy with CODATA value: 6.8σ
- Two-photon exchange contribution (Born + polarizability): ΔE^TPE_LO+NLO = 34.4(12.5) μeV, comprising Born term 8.3(4.3) μeV and polarizability term 26.2(10.0) μeV
- Pure chiral Born correction: 10.08 μeV; Delta-related Born correction: -1.81 μeV
- Pure chiral polarizability correction: 18.51 μeV; Delta-related polarizability correction: 7.67 μeV
- Final Lamb shift expression: ΔE = 206.070(13) - 5.2271(7) (r_p^2/fm^2) meV, with an additional hadronic term of 0.0455(125) meV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to muonic hydrogen developed by Pineda (2004) and Nevado (2007), providing the first complete model-independent computation of the hadronic two-photon exchange contribution including Delta particle effects. The result confirms the proton radius puzzle first reported by Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013), showing a 6.8σ discrepancy with the CODATA value from Mohr (2012). The analysis enables a parametric control of uncertainties that is impossible in dispersion relation approaches, which require model-dependent subtractions as emphasized by Hill (2011), and improves upon the chiral perturbation theory computation of Alarcon (2013) by including Delta contributions and maintaining proper power counting.
