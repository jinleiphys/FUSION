# Singularity-Free Breit Equation from Constraint Two-Body Dirac Equations

> We examine the relation between two approaches to the quantum relativistic two-body problem: (1) the Breit equation, and (2) the two-body Dirac equations derived from constraint dynamics. The Breit equation is known to be pathological when singularities appear at finite separations $r$ in the reduced set of coupled equations for attractive potentials even when the potentials themselves are not singular there. They then give rise to unphysical bound states and resonances. In contrast, the two-body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics do not have these pathologies in many nonperturbative treatments. To understand these marked differences, we first express these contraint equations in a hyperbolic form. These coupled equations are then re-cast into two equivalent equations: (1) a covariant Breit-like equation with potentials that are exponential functions of certain ``generator'' functions, and (2) a covariant orthogonality constraint on the relative momentum. This reduction enables us to show in a transparent way that finite-$r$ singularities do not appear as long as the the exponential structure is not tampered with and the exponential generators of the interaction are themselves nonsingular for finite $r$. These Dirac or Breit equations, free of the structural singularities which plague the usual Breit equation, can then be used safely under all circumstances, encompassing numerous applications in the fields of particle, nuclear, and atomic physics which involve highly relativistic and strong binding configurations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the constraint two-body Dirac equations, when recast into a covariant Breit-like equation with an exponential interaction structure, are free of the finite-separation pole singularities that plague the original Breit equation, provided the exponential generators are themselves nonsingular for finite \(r\) and the exponential structure is not destroyed by approximations.

## Method

The authors first express the constraint two-body Dirac equations in a hyperbolic form using a generator function \(\Delta\) that depends only on the spacelike separation \(x_\perp\). They then recast these coupled equations into two equivalent equations: a covariant Breit-like equation \(w \exp(2\mathcal{D})\Psi = (H_{10}+H_{20})\Psi\) and a covariant orthogonality constraint \(P \cdot p \, \Psi = 0\). The interaction appears exponentiated as \(\exp(2\mathcal{D})\), where \(2\mathcal{D}\) contains all eight interaction types (scalar, time-like vector, space-like vector, pseudoscalar, pseudovector, tensor) as commuting operators built from the generator functions \(L, J, \mathcal{G}, C, H, I, Y, \mathcal{F}\) and additional tensor terms \(N, T, S, R\).

## Key numbers

- The total c.m. energy \(w\) for the \(e^+e^-\) system in the \(^1J_J\) states satisfies a Sommerfeld formula: \(w = m\sqrt{2+2 / (1+\alpha^2/(n+\sqrt{(J+1/2)^2-\alpha^2}-J-1/2)^2)}\), which expands to \(w = 2m - m\alpha^2/(4n^2) - m\alpha^4/(2n^3(2J+1)) + (11/64)m\alpha^4/n^4 + O(\alpha^6)\).
- The nonrelativistic limit of the "third law" condition for scalar interactions gives \(m_1 S_1 = m_2 S_2\) when \(|S_i| \ll m_i\).
- The constituent on-shell c.m. energies are defined as \(\epsilon_1 = (w^2 + m_1^2 - m_2^2)/(2w)\) and \(\epsilon_2 = (w^2 + m_2^2 - m_1^2)/(2w)\).

## Context

This work extends the constraint dynamics approach of Crater and Van Alstine (1982, 1987) and relates it to the older Breit equation (also known as the Kemmer-Fermi-Yang equation), which is known to produce unphysical bound states and resonances due to pole singularities at finite separations as shown by Wong (1993) and others (Childers 1982, Kolsrud 1968). The paper shows that the exponential interaction structure arising from the relativistic "third law" condition eliminates these singularities, enabling safe nonperturbative use of the resulting Breit-like equations in particle, nuclear, and atomic physics for highly relativistic and strong binding configurations. The work also connects to the treatment of Sazdjian (1987) and Mourad and Sazdjian (1995) on Poincaré invariance and charge conjugation symmetry.
