# The QED of Bernabéu-Tarrach sumrule for electric polarizability and its implication for the Lamb shift

> We attempt to rehabilitate a sum rule (proposed long ago by Bernabéu and Tarrach) which relates the electric polarizability of a particle to the total photoabsorption of quasi-real longitudinally polarized photons by that particle. We discuss its perturbative verification in QED, which is largely responsible for the scepticism about its validity. The failure of the QED test can be understood via the Sugawara-Kanazawa theorem and is due to the non-vanishing contour contribution in the pertinent dispersion relation. We show another example where this contribution is absent and the perturbative test works exactly. On the empirical side, we show that the sum rule gives a reasonable estimate of the $πN$-channel contribution to the proton electric polarizability. If this sum rule is valid indeed, there should be a sum rule for the so-called ``subtraction function'' entering the data-driven calculations of the polarizability effects in the Lamb shift. We have written down a possible sum rule for the subtraction function and verified it in a perturbative calculation.

## Key claim

The Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule for the electric polarizability is valid if convergent, despite previous perturbative QED tests that appeared to disprove it; the failure in QED arises from a non-vanishing contour contribution in the dispersion relation at infinite energy, which is an artifact of the perturbative calculation rather than a fundamental flaw in the sum rule.

## Method

The authors revisit the derivation of the Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule from forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) dispersion relations and the optical theorem. They perform perturbative tests in leading-order QED and in leading-order baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT), computing both sides of the sum rule for the π⁺n and π⁰p channels. They also evaluate the sum rule empirically using the MAID model for single-pion photoproduction on the proton, and derive a new sum rule for the subtraction function relevant to Lamb shift calculations, which they verify in χPT and in the naïve parton model.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous magnetic moment term for the proton in the Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule is α_em κ_p²/(4M_p³) ≈ 0.54 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The full χPT integral for the Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule gives I_BT(Λ → ∞) ≈ 7 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, yielding a proton electric polarizability of about 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The PDG value for the proton electric polarizability is α_E1^p = (11.2 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- For the Baldin sum rule, the single-πN-channel value is about 11.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, compared to the inclusive result of (14 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.

## Context

This work attempts to rehabilitate the Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule, which was previously discredited by Llanta and Tarrach (1978) through a perturbative QED test and later dismissed by L'vov (1998). The sum rule is a virtual-photon analog of the Baldin sum rule (Baldin, 1960), which relates the sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities to real-photon absorption. If validated, the Bernabéu-Tarrach sum rule would enable separate data-driven determinations of the proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities, helping resolve the current controversy between Compton scattering experiments at HIγS (Li, 2022) and MAMI (A2 Collaboration, 2021). It would also provide a sum rule for the subtraction function entering the proton-structure contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen, which is a significant uncertainty in extracting the proton charge radius from spectroscopy (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013).
