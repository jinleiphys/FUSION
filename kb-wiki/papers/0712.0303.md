# Baryon-Strangeness correlations in Parton/Hadron transport model for Au + Au collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 GeV

> Baryon-strangeness correlation (C$_{BS}$) has been investigated with a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) in $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. The centrality dependence of C$_{BS}$ is presented within the model, from partonic phase to hadronic matter. We find that the system still reserve partial predicted signatures of C$_{BS}$ after parton coalescence. But after hadronic rescattering, the predicted signatures will be obliterated completely. So it seems that both coalescence hadronization process and hadronic rescattering are responsible for the disappearance of the C$_{BS}$ signatures.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the baryon-strangeness correlation coefficient C_BS retains partial signatures of a partonic phase after coalescence hadronization, but hadronic rescattering obliterates those signatures completely, suggesting that both processes together are responsible for the disappearance of the predicted C_BS signal in final-state hadrons.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which includes initial conditions from HIJING, parton cascade via ZPC with a 10 mb cross section, hadronization via quark coalescence, and hadronic rescattering via the ART model. They computed C_BS as a function of the number of participant nucleons (N_part) for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, comparing results from the string melting AMPT (which produces a partonic phase) with the default AMPT (which uses string fragmentation and no partonic phase), both with and without hadronic rescattering, for pseudorapidity windows eta_max = 0.1 and 0.5.

## Key numbers

- For the string melting AMPT without hadronic rescattering at eta_max = 0.5, C_BS increases from peripheral toward central collisions, reaching about 0.6 for central collisions and then flattening.
- For the default AMPT without hadronic rescattering at eta_max = 0.5, C_BS stays roughly constant at about 0.35.
- For the default AMPT with hadronic rescattering in central collisions, C_BS is about 0.4, close to the UrQMD result.
- For a particle subset including only kaons and protons, C_BS goes down to about 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Koch et al. (2005) to use C_BS as a probe of deconfined matter in heavy-ion collisions, and compares with lattice QCD predictions that C_BS is approximately 1 above T_C. The results are consistent with earlier UrQMD studies by Haussler et al. (2006), which also found that hadronic rescattering washes out the partonic signal. The study shows that both coalescence hadronization and hadronic rescattering must be accounted for when interpreting C_BS measurements, and suggests that no existing fluctuation probe of the deconfined matter survives to the final state in experiments.
