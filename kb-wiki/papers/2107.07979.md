# On the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039 A from LIGO/Virgo and NICER

> We perform a Bayesian analysis of neutrons star moment of inertia by utilizing the available gravitational-wave data from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817 and GW190425) and mass-radius measurements from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (PSR J0030+0415 and PSR J0740+6620), incorporating the possible phase transition in the pulsar inner core. We find that the moment of inertia of pulsar A in the double pulsar binary J0737-3039 is $\sim1.30\times10^{45}\,{\rm g\,cm^2}$, which only slightly depends on the employed hadronic equation of states. We also demonstrate how a moment of inertia measurement would improve our knowledge of the equation of state and the mass-radius relation for neutron stars and discuss whether a quark deconfinement phase transition is supported by the available data and forthcoming data that could be consistent with this hypothesis. We find that if pulsar A is a quark star, that its moment of inertia is a large value of $\sim1.55\times10^{45}\,{\rm g\,cm^2}$ suggesting the possibility of distinguishing it from (hybrid-)neutron stars with measurements of PSR J0737-3039A moment of inertia. We finally demonstrate the moment-of-inertia-compactness universal relations and provide analytical fits for both (hybrid-)neutron star and quark star results based on our analysis.

## Key claim

The moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039 A is predicted to be approximately 1.30 x 10^45 g cm^2, but this value depends on whether the star is a pure neutron star, a hybrid star with a quark core, or a pure quark star, with the quark star scenario yielding a larger value of approximately 1.55 x 10^45 g cm^2 that could be distinguished by future measurements.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using a 3D parameter space for the equation of state based on the constant-speed-of-sound parameterization, incorporating a sharp first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark phases. They used gravitational-wave data from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817 and GW190425) and mass-radius measurements from NICER (PSR J0030+0415 and PSR J0740+6620), sampling the posterior distribution with nested sampling software PyMultiNest to obtain approximately 3,000 posterior samples of the CSS model parameters for both QMF+CSS and DD2+CSS hadronic equation of state models.

## Key numbers

- Moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039 A: 1.27_{-0.14}^{+0.18} x 10^45 g cm^2 (QMF+CSS) and 1.29_{-0.15}^{+0.26} x 10^45 g cm^2 (DD2+CSS) at 90% credible interval
- If pulsar A is a quark star, moment of inertia: approximately 1.55 x 10^45 g cm^2
- Maximum neutron star mass from combined analysis: 2.23_{-0.21}^{+0.41} solar masses at 90% credible interval
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass star: 11.61_{-0.76}^{+0.75} km (QMF+CSS) and 11.89_{-0.93}^{+1.15} km (DD2+CSS) at 90% credible interval
- Current 90% upper limit from double pulsar timing: 3 x 10^45 g cm^2 (Kramer 2021)
- Scaled moment of inertia I/M^{3/2} lies within range approximately 35-50 km^2/solar mass^{1/2}

## Context

This work extends previous predictions of the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039 A by Landry and Kumar (2018), Jiang and Yagi (2020), and Silva et al. (2021) by explicitly including the possibility of a phase transition in the equation of state prior and incorporating additional data from GW190425 and the NICER measurement of PSR J0740+6620. The results enable future discrimination between pure neutron stars, hybrid stars with quark cores, and pure quark stars once a 10% precision measurement of the moment of inertia becomes available within the next decade, as anticipated by Hu et al. (2020).
