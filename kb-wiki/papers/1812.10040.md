# Fusion barrier distribution and superheavy elements

> The barrier distribution, originated from channel coupling effects in heavy-ion fusion reactions, has been extracted experimentally for many systems using either a fusion excitation function or an excitation function of large-angle quasi-elastic scattering. In this article, we discuss an application of the latter method to the $^{48}$Ca+$^{248}$Cm system, which is relevant to hot fusion reactions to synthesize superheavy elements. To this end, we carry out coupled-channels calculations for this system, taking into account the deformation of the target nucleus, and discuss the role of deformation in a formation of evaporation residues.

# Fusion barrier distribution and superheavy elements

## Key claim
The quasi-elastic barrier distribution for the ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁸Cm system, extracted using the GARIS separator at RIKEN, shows that the maximum of evaporation residue cross sections originates from side collisions (θ=π/2), confirming the notion of compactness proposed by Hinde et al. (1995), but this conclusion depends on the specific deformation parameters and the inclusion of neutron transfer channels which only produce minor modifications.

## Method
Coupled-channels calculations were performed for the ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁸Cm system using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with depth V₀=-105 MeV, range r₀=1.18 fm, and surface diffuseness a=0.6 fm. The calculations included the deformation of the ²⁴⁸Cm target nucleus (β₂=0.297, β₄=0.04 with radius parameter R_T=1.2×248¹/³ fm), octupole vibrational excitation in ⁴⁸Ca at 4.51 MeV (β₃=0.175), and a 1-neutron pick-up channel with ground-state-to-ground-state Q value Q_gg=-1.06 MeV. The quasi-elastic barrier distribution was defined as D_qel(E) = -d/dE(σ_qel(E,π)/σ_R(E,π)), and the experimental data were obtained by detecting recoiled target-like particles at π using the GARIS separator.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters for ²⁴⁸Cm: β₂=0.297 and β₄=0.04, with radius parameter R_T=1.2×248¹/³ fm
- Octupole vibrational excitation in ⁴⁸Ca at 4.51 MeV with deformation parameter β₃=0.175
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: V₀=-105 MeV, r₀=1.18 fm, a=0.6 fm
- Q value for 1-neutron pick-up channel: Q_gg=-1.06 MeV
- Evaporation residue cross sections peak at energies around 211 MeV, slightly above the barrier height for side collision

## Context
This work extends the experimental extraction of quasi-elastic barrier distributions, previously applied to many systems by Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991) and others, to the ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁸Cm system relevant for synthesizing superheavy element Z=116 (Lv). The measurements using GARIS at RIKEN, which was previously used to synthesize element Z=113 (Nihonium) by Morita et al. (2012), provide cleaner data than earlier attempts by Mitsuoka et al. (2007) and Ntshangase et al. (2007). The results enable quantitative connection between entrance channel barrier distributions and evaporation residue formation, and the extended fusion-by-diffusion model developed by Hagino et al. (2018) provides a theoretical framework for understanding the role of target deformation in superheavy element synthesis.
