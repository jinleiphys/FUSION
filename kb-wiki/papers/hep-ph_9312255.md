# The Goldberger -- Treiman Relation, $g_A$ and $g_{πNN}$ at $T\neq 0$

> The Goldberger-Treiman relation is shown to persist in the chiral limit at finite temperatures to order $O(T^2)$. The $T$ dependence of $g_A$ turns out to be the same as for $F_π$, $g_{A}(T)=g_{A}(0)(1-T^2/12F^2)$, while $g_{πNN}$ is temperature independent to this order. The baryon octet ${\cal D}$ and ${\cal F}$ couplings also behave as $F_π$ if only pions are massless in the pseudoscalar meson octet.

## Key claim

The Goldberger–Treiman relation persists at finite temperature in the chiral limit to order \(O(T^2)\), with the axial coupling \(g_A\) acquiring the same temperature dependence as the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\), while the pion–nucleon coupling \(g_{\pi NN}\) remains unchanged to this order.

## Method

The authors use the method of effective chiral Lagrangians, treating the low-temperature environment as a gas of massless thermal pions. They compute the finite-temperature corrections to the axial current–nucleon matrix element by attaching a single thermal pion loop to the pole diagram of the Goldberger–Treiman relation, evaluating diagrams for pion residue, wave function renormalization, and \(\pi N\) interactions. The analysis is extended to the \(SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R\) baryon octet, where the \({\cal D}\) and \({\cal F}\) couplings are treated analogously.

## Key numbers

* The axial coupling at finite temperature is \(g_A(T) = g_A(0) \left(1 - T^2 / 12 F^2\right)\), where \(F \simeq 90\) MeV is the pion decay constant in the chiral limit.
* The pion decay constant at finite temperature is \(F^2(T) = F^2(0) \left[1 - (1/6) (T^2 / F^2)\right]\).
* The thermal pion propagator gives \(\Delta_T(0) = T^2 / 12\).
* The pion mass in the chiral limit is not renormalized at this order; the quoted result from prior work is \(m_\pi(T) = m_\pi(0) (1 + T^2 / 48 F^2)\).
* For the baryon octet, the ratios obey \({\cal D}(T)/{\cal D}(0) = {\cal F}(T)/{\cal F}(0) = g_A(T)/g_A(0) = F_\pi(T)/F_\pi(0) = 1 - T^2 / 12 F_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature analysis of chiral symmetry relations, following earlier studies of hadron masses and condensates in the pion gas approximation (Eletsky and Ioffe, 1993; Leutwyler and Smilga, 1990) and the demonstration that the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation holds to order \(O(T^2)\) (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1987). The result that \(g_{\pi NN}\) is temperature-independent to leading order is consistent with earlier model calculations using chiral mean field theory (Robinson, 1991), QCD sum rules (Indumathi, 1992), and the topological chiral soliton model (Manton and Blaizot, 1993). The paper suggests that other hadronic couplings involving pions, such as \(\omega\rho\pi\), may also exhibit weak temperature dependence, a question the authors plan to address in future work.
