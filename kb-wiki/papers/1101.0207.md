# Radiative Neutron Capture on Lithium-7

> The radiative neutron capture on lithium-7 is calculated model independently using a low energy halo effective field theory. The cross section is expressed in terms of scattering parameters directly related to the S-matrix element. The cross section depends on the poorly known p-wave effective range parameter r. This constitutes the leading order uncertainty in traditional model calculations. It is explicitly demonstrated by comparing with potential model calculations. A single parameter fit describes the low energy data extremely well and yields r = -1.47 fm^{-1}.

## Key claim
The radiative neutron capture cross section on lithium-7 is calculated model-independently using halo effective field theory, but the leading-order result depends on the poorly known p-wave effective range parameter r₁, which constitutes the dominant theoretical uncertainty in traditional potential model calculations.

## Method
The authors treat the ⁷Li nucleus as point-like and construct a halo effective field theory for the n-⁷Li system, expanding interactions in powers of low momentum. The s-wave initial state interactions are treated perturbatively using scattering lengths, while the p-wave final state (⁸Li) is treated as a shallow bound state requiring non-perturbative resummation of two EFT interactions via a dimer formalism. The E1 capture cross section is calculated analytically by gauging the ⁷Li core momentum, and the result is expressed in terms of effective range expansion parameters directly related to S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Binding momentum of ⁸Li: γ = 57.8 MeV (from binding energy B ≈ 2.03 MeV)
- Scattering lengths: a₀⁽²⁾ = -3.63 ± 0.05 fm (⁵S₂ channel), a₀⁽¹⁾ = 0.87 ± 0.07 fm (³S₁ channel)
- Ratio of spin-channel contributions at zero energy: σ(⁵P₂)/[σ(⁵P₂)+σ(³P₂)] ≈ 0.81
- Fitted effective range r₁ = -1.47 fm⁻¹ when including both spin channels 1 and 2 (fit to data from Blackmon (1996) in energy range Eₙ ∼ 2-700 eV)
- Fitted effective range r₁ = -1.83 fm⁻¹ when including only spin channel 2 contribution
- Potential model results can be reproduced in EFT with r₁ in range -0.46 fm⁻¹ ≤ r₁ ≤ -0.3 fm⁻¹

## Context
This work extends halo effective field theory techniques, previously applied to few-nucleon systems (Rupak 2000), to the A=8 halo system ⁸Li, which is the isospin mirror of ⁸B important for solar neutrino physics. The reaction ⁷Li(n,γ)⁸Li is also relevant for inhomogeneous Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and constrains astrophysical models (Kawano 1991). The authors explicitly compare with potential model calculations by Tombrello (1965) and Davids-Typel (2004), demonstrating that different Woods-Saxon parameter choices reproducing the same binding energy yield different effective ranges and thus different cross sections. This work enables a model-independent treatment of the mirror reaction ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B by incorporating Coulomb interactions systematically, as has been done for proton fusion in EFT (Kong 1999).
