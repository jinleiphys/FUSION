# Fermi Liquid parameters for dense nuclear matter in Effective Chiral Model

> We calculate relativistic Fermi liquid parameters (RFLPs) for the description of the properties of dense nuclear matter (DNM) using Effective Chiral Model. Analytical expressions of Fermi liquid parameters (FLPs) are presented both for the direct and exchange contributions. We present a comparative study of perturbative calculation with mean field (MF) results. Moreover we go beyond the MF so as to estimate the pionic contribution to the FLPs. Finally, we use these parameters to estimate some of the bulk quantities like incompressibility, sound velocity, symmetry energy etc. for DNM interacting via exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ meson. In addition, we also calculate the energy densities and the binding energy curve for the nuclear matter. Results for the latter have been found to be consistent with two loop calculations reported recently within the same model.

# Fermi Liquid parameters for dense nuclear matter in Effective Chiral Model

## Key claim
The relativistic Fermi liquid parameters (RFLPs) for dense nuclear matter are calculated using an Effective Chiral Model, with the claim that the mean field (MF) results differ significantly from perturbative results at high density, while the pionic contributions to the Fermi liquid parameters are evaluated beyond the mean field level. The hedge is that the results for binding energy are consistent with two-loop calculations reported recently within the same model, but only for the specific parameter set designated as M0A.

## Method
The authors calculate relativistic Fermi liquid parameters (RFLPs) by evaluating direct and exchange contributions to the forward scattering amplitude from sigma, omega, and pion meson exchange using a chirally invariant Lagrangian. They perform both perturbative calculations and mean field (MF) calculations, where in the MF approximation meson fields are replaced by their vacuum expectation values and the effective nucleon mass M* is determined self-consistently. The exchange contributions are evaluated analytically, and the combination f0 - (1/3)f1 is used to compute chemical potential, energy density, incompressibility, sound velocity, and symmetry energy for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- At normal matter density (rho0 = 0.148 fm^-3), the chemical potential from MF calculation is 861.07 MeV and from perturbative calculation is 832.64 MeV.
- At saturation density, the total energy from perturbative calculation is 886.43 MeV and from MF calculation is 893.31 MeV.
- For sigma meson exchange at rho = rho0: F0^MF = -8.65, F0^ex = 3.61, F0^tot = -5.04, F1^ex = 0.875.
- For omega meson exchange at rho = rho0: F0^MF = 7.35, F0^ex = -1.91, F0^tot = 5.44, F1^ex = -0.932.
- The coupling parameters used are: m_sigma/M = 0.54, g_sigma/(4pi) = 0.7936; m_omega/M = 0.8328, g_omega/(4pi) = 0.9681.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Fermi liquid theory originally developed by Baym and Chin (1976) using the Walecka model, and the MF calculations follow the approach of Matsui (1981) who included rho and pi mesons but only at the MF level where pions do not contribute. The present calculation goes beyond MF to include pionic contributions to the FLPs, and the results for exchange energy are compared with the direct evaluation of loop diagrams by Hu et al. (2007) within the same chiral effective field theory model. This enables future calculations of bulk nuclear matter properties such as incompressibility, sound velocity, and symmetry energy using the full set of relativistic Fermi liquid parameters including exchange and pionic contributions.
