# Ab initio approach to s-shell hypernuclei 3H_Lambda, 4H_Lambda, 4He_Lambda and 5He_Lambda with a Lambda N-Sigma N interaction

> Variational calculations for s-shell hypernuclei are performed by explicitly including $Σ$ degrees of freedom. Four sets of YN interactions (SC97d(S), SC97e(S), SC97f(S) and SC89(S)) are used. The bound-state solution of $_Λ^5$He is obtained and a large energy expectation value of the tensor $ΛN-ΣN$ transition part is found. The internal energy of the $^4$He subsystem is strongly affected by the presence of a $Λ$ particle with the strong tensor $ΛN-ΣN$ transition potential.

# Ab initio approach to s-shell hypernuclei ³ΛH, ⁴ΛH, ⁴ΛHe and ⁵ΛHe with a ΛN-ΣN interaction

## Key claim

The bound-state solution of ⁵ΛHe is obtained for the first time in an ab initio calculation that explicitly includes Σ degrees of freedom, resolving the long-standing "anomalous small binding" problem. However, none of the four YN interaction models employed reproduces all experimental Λ separation energies precisely; the SC97e(S) interaction gives values closest to experiment among those tested.

## Method

Variational calculations for A=3-5 hypernuclei were performed using a correlated Gaussian basis with global vector representation, explicitly including both Λ and Σ components in the trial wavefunction. The Hamiltonian was constructed as a 2×2 matrix coupling Λ and Σ channels, using the G3RS potential for the NN interaction and four Nijmegen soft-core derived YN interactions (SC97d(S), SC97e(S), SC97f(S), SC89(S)) with Gaussian form factors fitted to reproduce the low-energy S-matrix of the original interactions. Linear variational parameters were determined by the Ritz variational principle, and nonlinear parameters were optimized stochastically.

## Key numbers

- Λ separation energy BΛ(⁵ΛHe) = 3.17 MeV (SC97d(S)), 2.75 MeV (SC97e(S)), 2.10 MeV (SC97f(S)), 0.35 MeV (SC89(S)); experimental value is 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV
- Λ separation energy BΛ(³ΛH) = 0.01 MeV (SC97d(S)), 0.10 MeV (SC97e(S)), 0.18 MeV (SC97f(S)), 0.37 MeV (SC89(S)); experimental value is 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV
- Λ separation energy BΛ(⁴ΛH) = 1.67 MeV (SC97d(S)), 2.06 MeV (SC97e(S)), 2.16 MeV (SC97f(S)), 2.55 MeV (SC89(S)); experimental value is 2.04 ± 0.04 MeV
- Λ separation energy BΛ(⁴ΛHe) = 1.62 MeV (SC97d(S)), 2.02 MeV (SC97e(S)), 2.11 MeV (SC97f(S)), 2.47 MeV (SC89(S)); experimental value is 2.39 ± 0.03 MeV
- Σ probability PΣ(⁵ΛHe) = 2.04% (SC97d(S)), 1.55% (SC97e(S)), 1.87% (SC97f(S)), 1.33% (SC89(S))
- Root-mean-square radii from center-of-mass of ⁴He in ⁵ΛHe: N = 1.5 fm, Λ = 2.9 fm, Σ = 1.6 fm (using SC97e(S))
- Energy expectation value of tensor ΛN-ΣN transition part in ⁵ΛHe: approximately -20 MeV (using SC97e(S))
- Internal energy change of ⁴He subsystem in ⁵ΛHe relative to isolated ⁴He: ΔEc ≈ 4.7 MeV
- Reduction of tensor NN interaction energy in ⁵ΛHe relative to isolated ⁴He: approximately 2.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous few-body studies of A=3,4 hypernuclei by Miyagawa (1995), Hiyama (2001), and Nogga (2001) to include the five-body system ⁵ΛHe, which had resisted bound-state solutions in earlier attempts. Prior variational Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson (1991) using the NSC89 interaction failed to find a bound ⁵ΛHe state. The study resolves the "anomalous small binding" problem noted by Dalitz, Herndon and Tang (1972) and discussed by Gibson and Hungerford (1995), showing that explicit inclusion of Σ degrees of freedom with strong tensor ΛN-ΣN coupling produces binding. The finding that the ⁴He core is not rigid but undergoes significant internal energy change (ΔEc ≈ 4.7 MeV) has implications for double-Λ hypernuclei such as ⁶ΛΛHe studied by Takahashi (2001), where similar core polarization effects are expected to be even larger.
