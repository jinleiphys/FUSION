# Charge and Matter Form Factors of Two-Neutron Halo Nuclei in Halo Effective Field Theory at Next-to-leading-order

> Using halo effective field theory (EFT), an expansion in $R_{core}/R_{halo}$, where $R_{core}$ is the radius of the core and $R_{halo}$ the radius of the halo nucleus, we calculate the charge and neutron form factors of the two-neutron halo nuclei $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, and $^{22}$C to next-to-leading-order (NLO) by treating them as an effective three-body system. From the form factors we extract the point charge and point matter radii, inter-neutron distance, and neutron opening angle. Agreement is found with existing experimental extractions. Results are given for the point charge and point matter radii for arbitrary neutron core scattering effective range, $ρ_{cn}$, that can be used for predictions once $ρ_{cn}$ is measured. Estimates for $ρ_{cn}$ are also used to make NLO predictions. Finally, our point charge radii are compared to other halo-EFT predictions, and setting the core mass equal to the neutron mass our point charge radius is found to agree with an analytical prediction in the unitary limit.

# Charge and Matter Form Factors of Two-Neutron Halo Nuclei in Halo Effective Field Theory at Next-to-leading-order

## Key claim

Halo effective field theory (EFT) calculations of charge and matter form factors for the two-neutron halo nuclei 11Li, 14Be, and 22C at next-to-leading-order (NLO) agree with existing experimental extractions, but the NLO predictions for point charge and point matter radii depend on the unknown neutron-core scattering effective range rho_cn; the paper provides results for arbitrary rho_cn that can be used once this quantity is measured, and also uses naturalness estimates for rho_cn to make NLO predictions.

## Method

The authors treat two-neutron halo nuclei as effective three-body systems (core plus two neutrons) in halo EFT, expanding in the ratio R_core/R_halo. They calculate charge and neutron form factors at LO and NLO by solving coupled integral equations for the trimer vertex function, adding range corrections perturbatively (unlike prior work that resummed range corrections to all orders). From the form factors expanded in powers of momentum transfer Q^2, they extract point charge radii, point matter radii, inter-neutron distances, and neutron opening angles.

## Key numbers

- For 11Li: LO point charge radius = 2.43 fm, NLO point charge radius = 2.46 fm (using naturalness estimate for rho_cn); LO point matter radius = 2.97 fm, NLO point matter radius = 3.00 fm
- For 14Be: LO point charge radius = 2.46 fm, NLO point charge radius = 2.50 fm; LO point matter radius = 3.07 fm, NLO point matter radius = 3.11 fm
- For 22C: LO point charge radius = 2.79 fm, NLO point charge radius = 2.84 fm; LO point matter radius = 3.63 fm, NLO point matter radius = 3.68 fm
- The nn scattering length a_nn = -18.7(6) fm and effective range rho_nn = 2.75(11) fm are used as inputs
- The nn virtual bound state momentum gamma_nn = -9.87 MeV and residue Z_nn = 1.16
- The neutron charge radius squared is taken as -0.115(4) fm^2 from Agashe (2014)
- The binding energies used: B_cn for 10Li = -0.026(13) MeV, for 13Be = -0.510 MeV, for 21C = -0.01(47) MeV; B_cnn for 11Li = 0.3693(6) MeV, for 14Be = 1.27(13) MeV, for 22C = 0.11(6) MeV

## Context

This work extends the LO halo-EFT calculations of Canham and Hammer (2008, 2009) for two-neutron halo nuclei by adding range corrections perturbatively at NLO, rather than resumming them to all orders as in the earlier NLO work. It also corrects the LO point charge radius results of Hagen et al. (2013), showing that their analytical functions contained an error that led to disagreement with the known analytical solution in the unitary and equal-mass limit. The results enable future predictions of charge and matter radii once the neutron-core effective range rho_cn is measured experimentally, and the formalism is equally applicable to cold atom systems and low-energy few-nucleon systems via universality.
