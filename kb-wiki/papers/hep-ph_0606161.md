# Open Problems in Hydrodynamical Approach to Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss some open problems in hydrodynamical approach to the relativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular, we propose a new, very simple alternative approach to the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics of Israel and Stewart.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while ideal hydrodynamics successfully describes collective flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, several open problems (initial conditions, event-by-event fluctuations, finite size effects, viscosity, and freeze-out) must be resolved before quantitative conclusions about the quark-gluon plasma can be drawn. The authors propose a new alternative to the Israel-Stewart second-order dissipative hydrodynamics that uses integral expressions with a relaxation time to maintain causality while keeping the simpler structure of the Landau formulation.

## Method

The authors review the standard relativistic ideal hydrodynamics framework and then list five open problems: initial conditions (early thermalization via Weibel instabilities or pre-thermalization), event-by-event fluctuations from initial state variations, finite size effects (introducing gradient terms analogous to the Weizsäcker term), viscosity effects (comparing ideal fluid predictions to data at SPS and RHIC), and final hadron spectra from freeze-out (sudden freeze-out vs. continuous emission). For the viscosity problem, they derive a causal dissipative hydrodynamics by replacing the instantaneous Landau-type viscous relations with retarded integral expressions using an exponential relaxation kernel, converting the parabolic diffusion equations into hyperbolic telegraph-type equations.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear ground state surface thickness is of the order of 3 fm, used to illustrate the finite size effect where the hydrodynamical inhomogeneity scale λ_Hydro = |(1/ρ)∇ρ|⁻¹ must be much larger than the mean free path for hydrodynamics to be valid.
- The parameter characterizing the finite size approximation is x = λ_micro / λ_Hydro, with hydrodynamics valid for x ≪ 1.
- The relaxation time τ_relax is introduced in the telegraphic equation τ_relax ∂²n/∂t² + ∂n/∂t = ζ ∇²n to convert the parabolic diffusion equation to a hyperbolic causal equation.
- The viscous terms in the proposed formalism are given by differential equations such as Π = -ζ ∂_α u^α + γ dΠ/dτ, where γ is the relaxation coefficient and d/dτ = u^μ ∂_μ is the proper time derivative.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of relativistic heavy ion hydrodynamics following the success of ideal fluid descriptions at RHIC, as reported by Gyulassy (2004) and others. It contrasts the observed elliptic flow at SPS energies (below ideal fluid predictions) with RHIC data (matching ideal fluid), referencing the work of Hirano and Gyulassy on entropy density effects. The proposed alternative to Israel-Stewart (1976) dissipative hydrodynamics aims to provide a simpler causal framework that can be implemented in existing hydro codes such as SPHERIO, enabling more precise extraction of QGP properties. The paper also connects to work on pre-thermalization in scalar field models (Berges, 2004), Weibel instabilities in QCD (Randrup, 2003; Arnold, 2005), and the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound on viscosity from string theory (Son, 2005).
