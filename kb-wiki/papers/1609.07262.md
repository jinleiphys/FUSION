# Applicability of pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data in global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions

> Despite the success of modern nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) in describing nuclear hard-process data, they still suffer from large uncertainties. One of the poorly constrained features is the possible asymmetry in nuclear modifications of valence $u$ and $d$ quarks. We study the possibility of using pion-nucleus Drell-Yan dilepton data as a new constraint in the global analysis of nPDFs. We find that the nuclear cross-section ratios from the NA3, NA10 and E615 experiments can be used without imposing significant new theoretical uncertainties and, in particular, that these datasets may have some constraining power on the $u$/$d$ -asymmetry in nuclei.

## Key claim

Pion-nucleus Drell-Yan dilepton data from the NA3, NA10 and E615 experiments can be used in global nPDF analyses without imposing significant new theoretical uncertainties, and these datasets may have some constraining power on the asymmetry between nuclear modifications of valence u and d quarks, but the data are not precise enough to completely pin down this difference.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) cross-section computations for pion-nucleus Drell-Yan dilepton production using the MCFM 7.0.1 code, comparing predictions from the EPS09 and nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs (with CT14 and nCTEQ15 free-proton PDFs respectively) against data from the NA3, NA10 and E615 experiments. They tested the sensitivity to pion PDFs by using both GRV and SMRS pion parton distributions, applied an isospin correction to the NA10 data, and accounted for the 6% normalization uncertainty in the NA10 datasets by minimizing a chi-squared function to determine optimal normalization factors.

## Key numbers

- NA3 experiment: mass range 4.1 GeV < M < 8.5 GeV at sqrt(s) = 16.8 GeV
- NA10 experiment: two beam energies, 286 GeV (sqrt(s) = 23.2 GeV) with mass range 4.2 GeV < M < 15 GeV, and 140 GeV (sqrt(s) = 16.2 GeV) with mass range 4.35 GeV < M < 15 GeV, both excluding the Upsilon peak region 8.5 GeV < M < 11 GeV
- E615 experiment: mass range 4.05 GeV < M < 8.55 GeV at sqrt(s) = 21.7 GeV, with an additional kinematic cut x1 > 0.36
- NA10 normalization uncertainty quoted as sigma_N^data = 6%
- Optimal normalization factor for NA10 286 GeV data: 1.044 (EPS09) and 1.058 (nCTEQ15)
- Optimal normalization factor for NA10 140 GeV data: 1.125 (EPS09) and 1.141 (nCTEQ15)
- Omega experiment: sqrt(s) = 8.7 GeV, data shown as a function of lepton pair invariant mass for x_F > 0

## Context

This work extends the global nPDF analyses of EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and nCTEQ15 (Kovarik 2015), which have large uncertainties in the flavor dependence of valence quark nuclear modifications. The study follows up on a suggestion by Dutta (2010) that pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data could constrain the u/d asymmetry in nuclei. The authors find that the NA3, NA10 and E615 data are compatible with modern nPDFs and will be included in the successor of the EPS09 analysis (EPPS16), but contrary to Dutta (2010), they find that u/d-symmetric nuclear modifications (EPS09) agree well with the data, while the most extreme flavor-asymmetric nCTEQ15 error sets are disfavored.
