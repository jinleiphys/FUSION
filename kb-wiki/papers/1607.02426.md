# Janus-Facedness of the Pion: Analytic Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Models

> Inversion enables the construction of interaction potentials underlying - under fortunate circumstances even analytic - instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter descriptions of all lightest pseudoscalar mesons as quark-antiquark bound states of Goldstone-boson nature.

## Key claim
Inversion techniques can analytically retrieve the central interquark potential underlying instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter descriptions of light pseudoscalar mesons as quark-antiquark bound states of Goldstone-boson nature, but only under the simplifying assumptions of instantaneous interactions, free quark propagation with a constituent mass, and a Fierz-symmetric kernel.

## Method
The authors start from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for a quark-antiquark bound state and impose instantaneous interactions and free constituent-quark propagation, reducing the equation to a Salpeter equation for a three-dimensional wave function. For a spin-singlet state with equal constituent masses, the Salpeter amplitude has two independent components; in the strictly massless (Goldstone) case with bound-state mass zero, the system decouples to a single integral equation for one component. The interquark potential is then obtained analytically by Fourier-Bessel transforming the kinetic term and dividing by the configuration-space Salpeter component, using an ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude constrained by Dyson-Schwinger studies of the quark mass function and axiomatic confinement criteria.

## Key numbers
- For the chiral-limit quark mass function parametrization: \( M(\underline{k}) = 0.708\;\text{GeV} \exp(-\underline{k}^2 / 0.655\;\text{GeV}^2) + 0.0706\;\text{GeV} / [1 + (\underline{k}^2 / 0.487\;\text{GeV}^2)^{1.48}]^{0.752} \), with the product of exponents \( 1.48 \times 0.752 \approx 1.1 \) close to unity as required by large-momentum behavior.
- For the numerically determined potential, constituent quark mass values used: \( m = 0 \), \( m = 0.35\;\text{GeV} \), \( m = 0.5\;\text{GeV} \), \( m = 1.0\;\text{GeV} \), and \( m = 1.69\;\text{GeV} \).
- For the analytic potential with massless quarks (\( m = 0 \)) and mixing parameter \( \eta \neq -1 \), the potential at the spatial origin behaves as \( V(r) \to \text{const} / (r \ln r) \to -\infty \) for \( r \to 0 \).
- For the analytic potential with constituent mass \( m = \mu \), the potential at large distances behaves as \( V(r) \to -\text{const} / \sqrt{r} \to 0 \) for \( r \to \infty \).

## Context
This work extends the inversion approach of Lucha (2013) to construct analytic instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter models for all light pseudoscalar mesons, building on earlier studies of the Salpeter equation (Lucha 2007) and Dyson-Schwinger analyses of the quark propagator (Maris and Roberts 1997). The analytic results are compared with numerical outcomes (Lucha 2016) and can be refined by dropping the free-quark propagation assumption (Lucha 2005) to incorporate full quark mass function and wave-function renormalization effects (Lucha 2016). The approach enables rigorous analytic relationships between QCD-inspired interactions and Goldstone-boson bound-state solutions.
