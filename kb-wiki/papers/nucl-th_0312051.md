# In-medium effects on the $K^-/K^+$ ratio at GSI

> The in-medium modifications on the $K^-/K^+$ ratio produced at GSI are studied. Particular attention is paid to the properties of antikaons, which determine the chemical potential and temperature at freeze-out conditions. Different approaches have been considered: non-interacting $K^-$, on-shell self-energy and single-particle spectral density. We observe that the full off-shell approach reproduces the "broad-band equilibration" advocated by Brown being the spectral density crucial to explain an enhanced $K^-/K^+$ ratio.

# In-medium effects on the $K^-/K^+$ ratio at GSI

## Key claim

The full off-shell approach incorporating the $K^-$ spectral density reproduces the "broad-band equilibration" advocated by Brown et al., but the resulting $K^-/K^+$ ratio is in excess by a factor of 2 with respect to the experimental value, and the corresponding freeze-out temperatures are too low to be compatible with experiment.

## Method

The authors used a statistical model with canonical strangeness conservation to compute the $K^-/K^+$ ratio, incorporating in-medium effects on antikaons through three different approaches: a non-interacting free gas, an on-shell self-energy using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock single-particle potential, and a full off-shell treatment including the $K^-$ spectral density with s-wave and p-wave contributions from $YN^{-1}$ excitations. The temperature and baryonic chemical potential were determined by fixing the $K^+/K^-$ ratio to values near the experimental one for Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- The $K^+/K^-$ ratio was fixed to 30, close to the experimental value for Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV.
- When using the full $K^-$ spectral density with both s- and p-wave contributions, a band of chemical potentials up to 850 MeV appears for $T \approx 35$ MeV, but this temperature is too low to be compatible with experiment.
- A ratio of approximately 15 gives a more plausible experimental temperature of $T \approx 70$ MeV when the full $K^-$ spectral density is used.
- The $K^-/K^+$ ratio from the full spectral density approach exceeds the experimental value by a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Cleymans (2000) and the "broad-band equilibration" concept introduced by Brown, Rho, and Song (2000) to explain the centrality independence of the $K^-/K^+$ ratio observed in C+C, Ni+Ni, and Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV by Barth (2000). The study shows that while the full spectral density reproduces the broad-band feature, it overproduces $K^-$ mesons compared to experiment, suggesting that non-equilibrium evolution of $K^-$ in the medium may be needed to resolve the discrepancy.
