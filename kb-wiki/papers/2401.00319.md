# Measurements of $p-Λ$ and $d-Λ$ correlations in 3 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR

> Heavy-ion collisions provide a unique opportunity to explore nucleon-hyperon (N-Y) interactions through two-particle correlations. The $p-Λ$ and $d-Λ$ correlations shed light on both N-Y two-body and N-N-Y three-body interactions, which is crucial for understanding neutron star properties. We present the high precision measurement of $p-Λ$ and the first measurement of $d-Λ$ correlation with $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=$ 3 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR. Using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism, we characterized emission source size, the scattering length ($f_0$), and the effective range ($d_0$) of $p-Λ$ and $d-Λ$ interactions. Using the $f_0$ and $d_0$ extracted from two spin states in $d-Λ$ correlation, the parameters from the doublet state indicate the hypertriton binding energy is consistent with the current average of world measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first experimental measurement of $d-\Lambda$ correlations and a high-precision measurement of $p-\Lambda$ correlations in 3 GeV Au+Au collisions, using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism to extract scattering parameters; the hypertriton binding energy derived from the doublet-state parameters is consistent with the current world average, but the quoted binding energy range of 0.04 to 0.33 MeV is given at 95% confidence level rather than as a single central value.

## Method

The STAR detector at RHIC analyzed approximately 250M Au+Au collision events at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3$ GeV collected in 2018 under fixed-target setup. Particles were identified using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detector, with $\Lambda$ hyperons reconstructed via the KFParticle package from their $p+\pi^-$ decay products. The correlation functions were measured as a function of $k^*$ (momentum in the pair rest frame) and fitted simultaneously across centrality and rapidity bins using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism to extract the emission source size ($R_G$), scattering length ($f_0$), and effective range ($d_0$) for both $p-\Lambda$ and $d-\Lambda$ systems, with spin-separated fits for the $d-\Lambda$ doublet and quartet states.

## Key numbers

- Spin-averaged $p-\Lambda$ scattering length: $f_0 = 2.32^{+0.12}_{-0.11}$ fm
- Spin-averaged $p-\Lambda$ effective range: $d_0 = 3.5^{+2.7}_{-1.3}$ fm
- $d-\Lambda$ doublet state scattering length: $f_0(D) = -20^{+3}_{-3}$ fm
- $d-\Lambda$ doublet state effective range: $d_0(D) = 3^{+2}_{-1}$ fm
- $d-\Lambda$ quartet state scattering length: $f_0(Q) = 16^{+2}_{-1}$ fm
- $d-\Lambda$ quartet state effective range: $d_0(Q) = 2^{+1}_{-1}$ fm
- Hypertriton binding energy from $d-\Lambda$ doublet parameters: $B_{\Lambda} = [0.04, 0.33]$ MeV at 95% CL
- $4\sim8\%$ of $d-\Lambda$ entries at $k^* < 100$ MeV/c in 10-20% centrality come from $^3_{\Lambda}H$ decay

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of nucleon-hyperon interactions using two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions, as reviewed by Fabbietti (2020), and provides the first experimental data on $d-\Lambda$ correlations. The extracted $d-\Lambda$ scattering parameters enable a new method to constrain the structure of the hypertriton ($^3_{\Lambda}H$) via the Bethe formula from Effective Range Expansion, complementing world average measurements compiled by Chen (2023). The results test theoretical predictions for $p-\Lambda$ spin states from Wang (1999) and $d-\Lambda$ spin states from Haidenbauer (2020), and the observed source size trends (larger in central collisions, slightly smaller for $d-\Lambda$ than $p-\Lambda$) agree with naive collision dynamics expectations.
