# Superscaling analysis of the Coulomb Sum Rule in quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering

> The Coulomb sum rule for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering in $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca and $^{56}$Fe is analyzed based on scaling and superscaling properties. Results obtained in the relativistic impulse approximation with various descriptions of the final state interactions are shown. A comparison with experimental data measured at Bates and Saclay is provided. The theoretical description based on strong scalar and vector terms present in the relativistic mean field, which has been shown to reproduce the experimental asymmetric superscaling function, leads to results that are in fair agreement with Bates data while it sizeably overestimates Saclay data. We find that the Coulomb sum rule for a momentum transfer $q\geq 500$ $MeV/c$ saturates to a value close to 0.9, being very similar for the three nuclear systems considered. This is in accordance with Bates data, which indicates that these show no significative quenching in the longitudinal response.

## Key claim

The Coulomb sum rule (CSR) for quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering on 12C, 40Ca, and 56Fe saturates to a value close to 0.9 for momentum transfers q >= 500 MeV/c, but this result depends on the theoretical description of final state interactions: the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, which reproduces the asymmetric experimental superscaling function, yields this saturation value, while the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) gives saturation near 1.0.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations of the inclusive quasielastic (e,e') cross section and longitudinal response for 12C, 40Ca, and 56Fe, using Dirac-Hartree bound states from a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach. They compared three descriptions of final state interactions (FSI): the RMF with the same scalar and vector potentials for initial and final states, the real part of the relativistic optical potential (rROP), and the plane wave limit (RPWIA) with no FSI. The Coulomb sum rule was evaluated both from the standard experimental expression integrating the longitudinal response over energy transfer and from an alternative expression based on the superscaling function.

## Key numbers

- CSR saturates to approximately 0.9 for q = 0.5 and 0.7 GeV/c for all three nuclei (12C, 40Ca, 56Fe) in the RMF model
- CSR saturates to approximately 0.7 for q = 0.3 GeV/c in the RMF model
- RPWIA yields CSR saturation near 1.0 for all q values and both 12C and 40Ca
- For 40Ca at q = 300 MeV/c, the RMF CSR integrated to the Bates energy cutoff (omega_max = 140 MeV) gives 0.6917, while the total RMF CSR is 0.7493 (7.7% difference)
- For 40Ca at q = 500 MeV/c, the RMF CSR integrated to the Saclay energy cutoff (omega_max = 290 MeV) gives 0.8335, while the total RMF CSR is 0.8825 (5.6% difference)
- The rROP model reaches a maximum CSR of approximately 0.95 at q = 0.4 GeV/c, decreasing to approximately 0.9 at higher q

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of quasielastic electron scattering developed by Donnelly, Sick, and collaborators (Day 1990, Donnelly and Sick 1999, Martinez et al. 2002) to address the longstanding controversy between Bates and Saclay experimental results on the Coulomb sum rule. Jourdan (1996) concluded from Bates data that the CSR saturates at 100% with no quenching, while Morgenstern and Meziani (2001) from Saclay data found significant quenching interpreted as medium modification of nucleon properties. The RMF model, previously shown to reproduce the asymmetric experimental superscaling function (Caballero et al. 2005, Martinez et al. 2006), reconciles these datasets by showing that the apparent quenching depends on the energy transfer cutoff used in the experimental integration, with the RMF results agreeing with Bates data but overestimating Saclay data. This analysis enables future comparison with the high-statistics JLAB experiment (CSR-JLAB, currently under analysis).
