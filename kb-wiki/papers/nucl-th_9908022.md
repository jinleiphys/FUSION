# A variational calculation of particle-antiparticle bound states in the scalar Yukawa model

> We consider particle-antiparticle bound states in the scalar Yukawa (Wick-Cutkosky) model. The variational method in the Hamiltonian formalism of quantum field theory is employed. A reformulation of the model is studied, in which covariant Green's functions are used to solve for the mediating field in terms of the particle fields. A simple Fock-state variational ansatz is used to derive a relativistic equation for the particle-antiparticle states. This equation contains one-quantum-exchange and virtual-annihilation interactions. It is shown that analytic solutions of this equation can be obtained for the simplified case where only the virtual annihilation interaction is retained. More generally, numerical and perturbative solutions of the equation are obtained for the massive and massless-exchange cases. We compare our results with various Bethe-Salpeter-based calculations.

## Key claim

The variational method applied to the scalar Yukawa (Wick-Cutkosky) model yields a relativistic two-body equation that includes both one-quantum-exchange and virtual-annihilation interactions, and the resulting binding energies differ substantially from ladder Bethe-Salpeter calculations, particularly at stronger coupling, though the simple Fock-state ansatz used cannot incorporate crossed-ladder effects.

## Method

The authors reformulate the scalar Yukawa model by using covariant Green's functions to solve for the mediating chion field in terms of the particle fields, then construct the Hamiltonian in normal-ordered form. A simple two-particle Fock-state trial state with an adjustable function is used in the variational principle to derive a relativistic momentum-space wave equation for particle-antiparticle bound states, which is then solved analytically for the annihilation-only case, perturbatively for the massless-exchange case, and numerically for the massive and massless cases using variational trial wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy expansion for massless exchange: \(E_{n\ell}(\alpha) = 2m - \frac{1}{4} m \alpha^2 \frac{1}{n^2} - \frac{1}{16}m\alpha^4 \left[ \frac{2}{(2\ell+1)n^3} - \frac{3}{4n^4} \right] + \frac{1}{8}m\alpha^4\left[ \frac{4}{(2\ell+1)n^3} - \frac{1}{n^4} \right] + \Delta E_{anni} + O(\alpha^5)\), where \(\Delta E_{anni} = \frac{1}{8} m \alpha^4 \frac{1}{n^3}\delta_{\ell 0} + O(\alpha^5)\)
- Minimum coupling for binding in annihilation-only case: \(\alpha_0 = 8\pi/(\pi+2) = 4.88812\) when \(\mu\) just exceeds \(2m\)
- For massless exchange with annihilation only, the cross section in units of \(\pi/m^2\) starts at \(64\alpha^2/(16+[1+4/\pi]\alpha)^2\) at \(q=0\) and asymptotes to \(\alpha^2/(4q^6)\) as \(q \to \infty\)
- The maximum cross section for massless exchange annihilation-only case asymptotes to \(64\pi^2/(\pi+4)^2 = 12.3848\) as \(\alpha \to \infty\)

## Context

This work extends the variational Hamiltonian approach to bound states in quantum field theory, following the reformulation method of Darewych (1996) and earlier variational work by Schiff, Coleman and Weinberg, Jackiw, and others. The results are compared extensively with the classic Wick-Cutkosky Bethe-Salpeter ladder solutions (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954), light-cone calculations of Ji and Furnstahl (1993), quasipotential results from Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996) including Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze, Gross, and equal-time approximations, and the Feynman-Schwinger formulation. The paper enables future work incorporating more sophisticated Fock-state ansatze to include crossed-ladder effects, which Theussl and Desplanques (1998) have shown are important for accurate binding energies.
