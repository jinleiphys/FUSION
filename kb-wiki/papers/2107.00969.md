# X(3872) Production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Heavy-ion collisions provide a unique opportunity to study the nature of X(3872) compared with electron-positron and proton-proton (antiproton) collisions. We investigate the centrality and momentum dependence of X(3872) in heavy-ion collisions via the Langevin equation and instant coalescence model (LICM). When X(3872) is treated as a compact tetraquark state, the tetraquarks are produced via the coalescence of heavy and light quarks near the quantum chromodynamic (QCD) phase transition due to the restoration of the heavy quark potential at $T\rightarrow T_c$. In the molecular scenario, loosely bound X(3872) is produced via the coalescence of $D^0$-$\bar D^{*0}$ mesons in a hadronic medium after kinetic freeze-out. We employ the LICM to explain both $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ production as a benchmark. Then we give predictions regarding X(3872) production and the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{X(3872)}$. We find that the total yield of tetraquark is several times larger than the molecular production in Pb-Pb collisions. Although the geometric size of the molecule is huge, the coalescence probability is small due to strict constraints on the relative momentum between $D^0$ and $\bar D^{*0}$ in the molecular Wigner function, which significantly suppresses the molecular yield.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, the total yield of X(3872) as a compact tetraquark state is several times larger than the yield as a loosely bound hadronic molecule, because the molecular coalescence probability is strongly suppressed by the strict constraints on the relative momentum between D⁰ and D̅*⁰ in the molecular Wigner function, despite the molecule's large geometric size.

## Method

The authors employ a Langevin equation to simulate the Brownian motion of charm quarks in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and D mesons in the hadronic medium, coupled with an instantaneous coalescence model (ICM) to form hadrons. For the tetraquark scenario, X(3872) is produced via coalescence of heavy and light quarks near the QCD phase transition temperature T_c; for the molecular scenario, it is produced via coalescence of D⁰ and D̅*⁰ mesons after kinetic freeze-out. The (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model MUSIC is used to simulate the bulk medium expansion, and the test particle Monte Carlo method numerically solves the combined Langevin equation and ICM (LICM).

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV; D⁰ meson mass set to m_D⁰ = 1.875 GeV; D̅*⁰ meson mass set to m_D̅*⁰ = 2.007 GeV.
- Spatial diffusion coefficient for charm quarks in QGP: D_s(2πT) = 5; for D mesons in hadronic medium: D_s(2πT) = 8.
- Critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV; initial maximum temperature at center of hot medium in most central collisions T_0(τ_0, x_T=0) = 510 MeV; hydrodynamic evolution starts at τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- Charm quark production cross-section in pp collisions: dσ_pp^{c̅c}/dy = 1.165 mb.
- Root-mean-square radius of J/ψ: √⟨r²⟩_{J/ψ} = 0.54 fm; root-mean-square radius of D mesons: √⟨r²⟩_D = 0.43 fm.
- Tetraquark root-mean-square radius assumed to be 0.3–0.5 fm; coalescence temperature of tetraquark T_{c̅cqq̅→X} ≃ 1.2 T_c.
- Molecular state binding energies range from 3.3 keV to 1600.3 keV depending on cutoff parameter Λ (0.55–0.579); corresponding mean radii ⟨r⟩ range from 2.47 fm to 22.60 fm.
- Coalescence temperature of molecular X(3872) set to kinetic freeze-out temperature T_mole ≃ T_kin ≃ 0.14 GeV.
- Hadronization ratios: H_{c→D⁰} = 9.5%; H_{c→D*⁰} = 20%.
- Statistical factor for tetraquark coalescence: g_{X(3872)} = 1/432; for J/ψ: g_M = 1/12.
- Charm pair number reduced by approximately 28% due to shadowing effect in centrality 0-20%.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin equation and instantaneous coalescence model (LICM) previously applied to charmonium and D meson production in heavy-ion collisions (Chen 2018, Zhao 2016, He 2014) to the exotic hadron X(3872). It directly addresses the debate on whether X(3872) is a compact tetraquark or a loosely bound hadronic molecule (Guo 2017, Esposito 2016, Nakamura 2019), motivated by recent LHCb (2020) and CMS (2021) measurements of the X(3872)/ψ(2S) yield ratio in pp and Pb-Pb collisions. The predictions enable experimental discrimination between the two internal structure scenarios through centrality and transverse momentum dependence of X(3872) production in heavy-ion collisions.
