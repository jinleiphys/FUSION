# Green's Functions in the Color Field of a Large Nucleus

> We compute the Green's functions for scalars, fermions and vectors in the color field associated with the infinite momentum frame wavefunction of a large nucleus. Expectation values of this wavefunction can be computed by integrating over random orientations of the valence quark charge density. This relates the Green's functions to correlation functions of a two dimensional, ultraviolet finite, field theory. We show how one can compute the sea quark distribution functions, and explictly compute them in the kinematic range of transverse momenta, $α_s^2 μ^2 << k_t^2 << μ^2$, where $μ^2$ is the average color charge squared per unit area. When $m_{quark}^2 << μ^2 \sim A^{1/3}$, the sea quark contribution to the infinite momentum frame wave function saturates at a value that is the same as that for massless sea quarks.

## Key claim

The paper computes Green's functions for scalars, fermions, and vectors in the classical color field of a large nucleus in the infinite momentum frame, with the explicit caveat that the results are restricted to transverse momenta \(k_t \ll \mu\) (where \(\mu^2\) is the average color charge squared per unit area) because quantum corrections become large when \(k_t \sim \mu\).

## Method

The authors treat valence quarks as static, classical sources of color charge along the light cone, with a Gaussian distribution for the charge density. They solve the classical equations of motion for the gauge field in light cone gauge, obtaining a pure gauge field for \(x^- > 0\) that is discontinuous at \(x^- = 0\). The Green's functions are constructed by matching plane wave solutions across this discontinuity, then averaging over random color orientations of the valence quark sources using a two-dimensional field theory measure. The sea quark distribution is extracted from the fermion propagator via a relation between propagators and distribution functions.

## Key numbers

* The average color charge squared per unit area is \(\mu^2 = 1.1\, A^{1/3}~\text{fm}^{-2}\).
* The sea quark distribution per unit rapidity per unit transverse area is \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2}\frac{dN_{sea}}{dy} = \frac{2(N_c^2-1)}{\pi^2} \mu^2 \alpha_s^2 \ln(1/\alpha_s)\), valid in the kinematic range \(\alpha_s^2 \mu^2 \ll k_t^2 \ll \mu^2\).
* The gluon distribution per unit rapidity per unit transverse area from the same momentum range is \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2}\frac{dN_{glue}}{dy} = \frac{2 \alpha_s \ln(1/\alpha_s) \mu^2 (N_c^2-1)}{\pi}\).
* The ratio of intrinsic sea quarks to glue in the nuclear wavefunction is suppressed by a factor \(\alpha_s/\pi\).
* The sea quark distribution saturates for quark masses satisfying \(m_{quark}^2 \ll \mu^2 \sim A^{1/3}\), giving the same result as for massless sea quarks.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous papers (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) which argued that quark and gluon distribution functions for large nuclei at small Bjorken \(x\) are computable in a weakly coupled many-body theory. The results provide explicit Green's functions that enable computation of first radiative corrections to distribution functions, potentially revealing a Lipatov enhancement (Lipatov, 1976). The formalism also enables future Altarelli-Parisi analysis to connect these computations to experimental structure function measurements, particularly in the region \(\mu^2 \gg Q^2 \gg \Lambda^2_{QCD}\) where intrinsic transverse momentum is important.
