# The charge radii and the decay rates of the pseudoscalar meson

> The charge radii and the decay rates of the pion and kaons are calculated, using the relativistic equation of motion with a linear potential. Those physical quantities are quite well explained with the current quark masses in the case of the pion. It is found that the Van Royen-Weisskopf paradox can be cleared out only in the linear potential model by considering the color degree of freedom of the quark in the meson. The physical quantities for the kaon are not so satisfactory that it should be required to reconsider the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element in the kaon decay.

# The charge radii and the decay rates of the pseudoscalar meson

## Key claim

The linear confinement potential model, when applied to the relativistic Klein-Gordon equation, can simultaneously explain the charge radii and decay rates of the pion using current quark masses and a color factor of 1/9 to resolve the Van Royen-Weisskopf paradox; however, the same approach fails to give consistent results for the kaon, suggesting that the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element |V_us| may need to be reconsidered.

## Method

The author solves the Klein-Gordon equation for a two-body quark-antiquark system in the center-of-mass frame, using a linear confinement potential V_eff = kr. The wavefunction solution is an Airy function of the form Ψ(r) = C exp(-a r^(3/2)). Charge radii are computed from the form factor expansion of the charge density distribution, and decay rates for π^- → μ^- + ν̅ and K^- → μ^- + ν̅ are calculated from the invariant amplitude using the weak interaction with the appropriate CKM matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses used: m_u = 10 MeV, m_d = 28.61 MeV, m_s = 160 MeV (second column of Table); alternative set: m_u = 1.5 MeV, m_d = 4.29 MeV, m_s = 14.77 MeV (third column of Table)
- Linear potential parameter k = 34655.5 MeV^2 for the first quark mass set, corresponding to α_s = 13.35
- Linear potential parameter k = 5057.66 MeV^2 for the second quark mass set, corresponding to α_s = 34.0
- The symmetric factor S = 1/(4 × 9) resolves the Van Royen-Weisskopf paradox, where 1/4 accounts for the spin singlet state and 1/9 accounts for the color singlet state
- For the kaon, the CKM matrix element |V_us| is estimated as 0.101, which lies outside the standard range of 0.217-0.224
- The u- and d-quark mass ratio extracted from the pion data is 0.35
- The u- and s-quark mass ratio for the kaon is calculated as 0.065 when the kaon decay constant is forced to the experimental value
- For comparison: the Coulomb potential model gives decay constants of 1.55 MeV (pion) and 2.77 MeV (kaon); the harmonic oscillator potential gives 274.2 MeV (pion) and 583.9 MeV (kaon)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous attempt to derive a quark confinement potential from QCD (Ko, 1999) and addresses difficulties in earlier quark model calculations by Weiss, Kokkedee, Lucha, Allen, and Krutov, particularly the Van Royen-Weisskopf paradox where the spatial wavefunction |Ψ(0)|^2 from charge radius calculations was 19 times larger than that from decay rate calculations. The linear potential model is shown to give consistent results for the pion, unlike the Coulomb or harmonic oscillator potentials, but the kaon results remain unsatisfactory. The paper suggests that the K_e3 decay K^+ → π^0 + e^+ + ν should be reconsidered with an elegant quark model, and that the Kobayashi-Maskawa original parameterization of the CKM matrix may need scrutiny. A new relation f_π/f_K ≈ M_π/M_K is proposed from the numerical calculations.
