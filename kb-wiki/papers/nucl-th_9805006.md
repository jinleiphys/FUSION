# New analytic solutions of the non-relativistic hydrodynamical equations

> New solutions are found for the non-relativistic hydrodynamical equations. These solutions describe expanding matter with a Gaussian density profile. In the simplest case, thermal equilibrium is maintained without any interaction, the energy is conserved, and the process is isentropic. More general solutions are also obtained that describe explosions driven by heat production, or contraction of the matter caused by energy loss.

# New analytic solutions of the non-relativistic hydrodynamical equations

## Key claim
The paper presents exact solutions to the non-relativistic hydrodynamical equations for a spherically expanding Gaussian density profile, where in the simplest case thermal equilibrium is maintained without any collisions (free-streaming Knudsen gas). The solutions are conditional on a specific initial condition with spherical symmetry and a radial initial flow pattern, and the Gaussian shape is preserved under convolution.

## Method
The authors solve the continuity equation, Euler equation, and energy conservation equation for an ideal gas equation of state, assuming a Gaussian particle density profile with time-dependent width. They first obtain an adiabatic solution with constant total entropy, then generalize by adding a source term to the energy balance equation proportional to local internal energy to model heat production or loss. An algorithm is provided to generate infinitely many new solutions by choosing a functional form for the scale factor φ(t) and deriving the corresponding temperature and source functions.

## Key numbers
- The particle density is given by n(r,t) = N/(2πR²(t))^(3/2) exp(-r²/(2R²(t))), where R(t) is the time-dependent Gaussian width
- The temperature evolves as T(t) = T₀/φ(t) with φ(t) = (1 + (t-t₀)/τ)² + (T₀/T_G)((t-t₀)/τ)², where T_G = m u₀² and u₀ = R₀/τ is the characteristic expansion rate
- The single-particle momentum spectrum is N₁(p) = N/(2πmT_*)^(3/2) exp(-p²/(2mT_*)) with effective temperature T_* = T₀ + m u₀²
- The total energy is E_tot = (3/2) N T_* = constant, with heat energy E_heat = (3/2) N T(t) and flow energy E_flow = E_tot - E_heat
- The two-particle correlation function radius parameter satisfies 1/R_*² = 1/R_T² + 1/R₀² = (1/R₀²)(1 + m u₀²/T₀)
- For the exponential solution with constant heat source j, the temperature and radius evolve as T(t) = T₀ exp(j(t-t₀)/2) and R²(t) = R₀² exp(j(t-t₀)/2), with j = ±√(16 T₀/(m R₀²))

## Context
This work extends the exact hydrodynamical solution of Zimanyi, Bondorf and Garpman (1978) by introducing a different temperature and density profile while maintaining the same flow field pattern. The solutions are applicable to non-relativistic heavy ion collisions and provide analytic tools to study the time evolution of strongly interacting matter. The paper enables analytic calculation of single-particle and two-particle spectra and correlation functions, and the algorithm for generating solutions with heat production or loss opens the door to modeling explosive expansion or radiative cooling in hydrodynamical systems.
