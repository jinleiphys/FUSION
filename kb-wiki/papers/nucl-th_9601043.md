# Nuclear Properties for Astrophysical Applications

> We tabulate the ground-state odd-proton and odd-neutron spins and parities, proton and neutron pairing gaps, binding energy, one- and two-neutron separation energies, quantities related to beta-delayed one- and two-neutron emission probabilities, beta-decay energy release and half-life with respect to Gamow-Teller decay, one- and two-proton separation energies, and alpha-decay energy release and half-life for 8979 nuclei ranging from oxygen-16 to Z = 136, A = 339 and extending from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. Single-particle level diagrams and other quantities are also presented in graphical form. The starting point of our present work is a study of nuclear ground-state masses and deformations based on the finite-range droplet model and folded-Yukawa single-particle potential published in a previous issue of Atomic Data and Nuclear Data Tables. The beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities and Gamow-Teller beta-decay rates are obtained from a quasi-particle random-phase approximation with single-particle levels and wave functions at the calculated nuclear ground-state shapes as input quantities.

# Nuclear Properties for Astrophysical Applications

## Key claim

The authors present a comprehensive tabulation of nuclear ground-state properties for 8979 nuclei from oxygen-16 to Z=136, A=339, based on the finite-range droplet model (FRDM 1992) and folded-Yukawa single-particle potential, with beta-decay properties calculated via a quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) using the calculated ground-state shapes as input; the reliability of the mass model for nuclei far from stability is assessed through simulations showing only a 2% increase in model error when predicting 351 new masses measured between 1977 and 1989.

## Method

The authors calculated ground-state masses and deformations using the finite-range droplet model (FRDM 1992) with a folded-Yukawa single-particle potential, then derived binding energies, separation energies, and decay energy releases from mass differences. Beta-decay half-lives and beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities were obtained from a quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) incorporating the Gamow-Teller residual interaction with strength 23 MeV/A, using single-particle levels and wave functions at the calculated nuclear ground-state deformations. Alpha-decay half-lives were estimated using the Viola-Seaborg relationship with constants determined by Sobiczewski, Patyk, and Cwiok.

## Key numbers

- 8979 nuclei tabulated, ranging from oxygen-16 to Z=136, A=339, extending from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line
- Effective pairing-interaction constant r = 3.2 MeV adopted for the entire nuclear chart, revised from the earlier value of 3.3 MeV
- Critical deformation epsilon_crit = 0.15 used to distinguish between deformed and spherical spin assignments for odd-nucleon spins and parities
- Gamow-Teller force strength chi_GT = 23 MeV/A used in the QRPA calculations
- Constant B = 4131 s used for Gamow-Teller beta-decay half-life calculations
- Viola-Seaborg constants: a = +1.66175, b = -8.5166, c = -0.20228, d = -33.9069 for alpha-decay half-life estimation
- Electron binding energy approximated by a_el * Z^2.39 with a_el = 1.433 x 10^-5 MeV
- Atomic mass unit u = 931.5014 MeV used for consistency with the 1989 mass evaluation
- In simulations, 351 new masses measured between 1977 and 1989 were calculated with only a 2% increase in model error
- For superheavy nuclei such as 288110 and 290110, the difference in mass predictions between FRDM (1992) and a limited adjustment was approximately 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mass calculation (Moller, Nix, 1992) published in Atomic Data and Nuclear Data Tables, which provided ground-state masses and deformations for the same set of 8979 nuclei using the FRDM (1992) model. The paper enables astrophysical r-process and rp-process calculations by providing beta-decay half-lives, beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities, and other nuclear properties essential for nucleosynthesis modeling. The authors note that a new mass evaluation containing 217 new masses not included in the 1989 data set (Audi, 1989) has become available, allowing direct assessment of model reliability without simulations.
