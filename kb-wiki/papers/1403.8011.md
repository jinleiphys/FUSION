# Supernova Equation of State with an extended SU(3) Quark-Meson Model

> The quark-meson model is investigated for the two- and three-flavor case extended by contributions of vector mesons under conditions encountered in core-collapse supernova matter. Typical temperature ranges, densities and electron fractions, as found in core-collapse supernova simulations, are studied by implementing charge neutrality and local beta-equilibrium with respect to weak interactions. Within this framework, we analyze the resulting phase diagram and equation of state (EoS) and investigate the impact of undetermined parameters of the model. The EoS turns out to be relatively independent on the entropy per baryon but there are significant changes when going from the two-flavor to the three-flavor case due to the nontrivial contribution from the strange quarks which stay massive even at high densities. While an increasing vector meson coupling constant leads to a substantial stiffening of the EoS, we find that the impact of changing the scalar meson mass is equally strong and results in a softening of the EoS for increasing values.

## Key claim

The equation of state (EoS) from the extended SU(3) quark-meson model is relatively independent of entropy per baryon but shows significant changes when going from two-flavor to three-flavor cases, with the impact of varying the scalar meson mass being equally strong as varying the vector meson coupling constant, though in the opposite direction (softening versus stiffening).

## Method

The authors extended the standard SU(2) and SU(3) quark-meson (linear sigma) models by including vector meson contributions (omega, rho, and phi mesons) in a mean-field approximation. They imposed charge neutrality and local beta-equilibrium with respect to weak interactions, fixing the electron-to-baryon ratio Ye to values typical of core-collapse supernova simulations (standard Ye = 0.2), and solved the gap equations for the scalar condensates and vector meson fields at finite temperature and baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Standard values used: Ye = 0.2, entropy per baryon s = 1.0 kB/baryon, vector coupling g_omega = 3.0, sigma meson mass m_sigma = 600 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92 MeV; kaon decay constant f_K = 110 MeV
- Constituent light quark mass fixed to m_l = 300 MeV
- Critical endpoint for both flavor cases lies at approximately T ≈ 80 MeV
- For g_omega = 9.0, the critical endpoint vanishes and the phase transition becomes a crossover at all temperatures
- Sigma meson mass varied in the range 400–1000 MeV; vector coupling varied in the range g_omega = 1–10
- Electron fraction Ye varied in the range 0.0–0.5; entropy per baryon explored in the interval 0.5–4 kB/baryon

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter in supernovae that used the MIT bag model (Sagert 2008, Fischer 2010) or the Polyakov-loop extended NJL model (Fischer 2011) by employing a chiral effective model with explicit scalar and vector meson degrees of freedom. The results enable more realistic input for core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations, particularly by quantifying how undetermined parameters (vector coupling and sigma meson mass) affect the phase diagram and EoS, and by demonstrating that the strange quark remains massive even at high densities under supernova conditions.
