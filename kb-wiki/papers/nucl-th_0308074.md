# $ΛN \to NN$ weak interaction in effective field theory

> The nonleptonic weak $|ΔS|=1$ $ΛN$ interaction, responsible for the dominant nonmesonic decay of all but the lightest hypernuclei, is studied in the framework of an effective field theory. The long-range physics is described through tree-level exchange of the SU(3) Goldstone bosons, while the short-range potential is parametrized in terms of the lowest-order contact terms. We obtain reasonable fits to available weak hypernuclear decay rates and quote the values for the parity-violating asymmetry as predicted by the present effective field theory.

# ΛN → NN weak interaction in effective field theory

## Key claim

The paper presents an exploratory study of whether effective field theory (EFT) methods can describe the nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei, finding that leading-order contact terms of natural size can reproduce total and partial decay rates, but the two existing experimental asymmetry values for A=5 and A=11 hypernuclei cannot be fitted simultaneously with this set of contact terms.

## Method

The long-range part of the weak ΛN → NN interaction is described through tree-level exchange of pion and kaon mesons (the SU(3) Goldstone bosons), while the short-range part is parametrized by lowest-order contact operators in a momentum expansion. The contact potential includes leading-order parity-conserving (order p⁰) and leading-order parity-violating (order q/M_N) terms, regularized with a Gaussian smearing of range δ ≈ 0.36 fm. The calculation uses shell-model wave functions for the initial hypernucleus with correlated ΛN wave functions from G-matrix calculations and NN final-state interactions from the Nijmegen soft-core potential.

## Key numbers

- Energy release at threshold: ≈ 177 MeV, corresponding to nucleon momenta ≈ 417 MeV
- Strong πNN coupling: g_{NNπ} = 13.16
- Weak ΛNπ parity-violating amplitude: 1.05 (in units of G_F m_π² = 2.21 × 10⁻⁷)
- Weak ΛNπ parity-conserving amplitude: -7.15 (in same units)
- Strong ΛNK coupling (Nijmegen NSC97f): g_{ΛNK} = -17.66
- Strong ΣNK coupling (Nijmegen NSC97f): g_{ΣNK} = -5.38
- Gaussian smearing range: δ ≈ 0.36 fm (from δ ∼ √2 m_ρ⁻¹)
- Harmonic oscillator parameters for ¹²_ΛC and ¹¹_ΛB: b_N = 1.64 fm, b_Λ = 1.87 fm
- Harmonic oscillator parameters for ⁵_ΛHe: b_N = 1.4 fm, b_Λ = 1.85 fm
- Total decay rate Γ(⁵_ΛHe) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.44 (experiment: 0.41 ± 0.14, 0.50 ± 0.07)
- n/p ratio (⁵_ΛHe) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.55 (experiment: 0.93 ± 0.55, 0.50 ± 0.10)
- Asymmetry α_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.24 (experiment: 0.24 ± 0.22)
- Total decay rate Γ(¹²_ΛC) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.93 (experiment: 1.14 ± 0.2, 0.89 ± 0.15, 0.83 ± 0.11)
- n/p ratio (¹²_ΛC) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.77 (experiment: 0.87 ± 0.23)
- Asymmetry A(¹²_ΛC) with π+K+LO PC+PV: 0.02 (experiment: -0.01 ± 0.10)
- Isoscalar contact coefficient C_IS (LO PC+PV fit): 4.68 ± 0.67
- Isoscalar contact coefficient C_IS (fit including α_Λ): 5.97 ± 0.86

## Context

This work extends meson-exchange approaches to hypernuclear weak decay (Parreno, Ramos, Bennhold 1997; Dubach et al. 1996) by applying EFT methods previously successful in the non-strange NN sector (Beane et al. 2001; van Kolck 1999). It builds on a study by Jun (2001) that added a Fermi V-A interaction to one-pion exchange. The paper finds that leading-order contact terms can reproduce decay rates but cannot simultaneously fit asymmetry data from ⁵_ΛHe and ¹¹_ΛB, identifying this as an issue for future experiments to resolve. The next generation of high-precision weak decay data currently under analysis is expected to provide much improved constraints.
