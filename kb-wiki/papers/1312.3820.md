# Production and ratio of $π$, K, p and $Λ$ in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> The particle production and their ratios for $π$, $K$, $p$, and $Λ$ are studied in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV based on a blast-wave model with thermal equilibrium mechanism. The transverse momentum spectra of the above mentioned particles at the kinetic freeze-out stage are discussed. The modification of the inverse slope of pion transverse momentum spectrum due to resonance decay has also been investigated. In addition, we found that the anti-particles to particles ratio as well as kaons to pions ratio agree with the data by the LHC-ALICE Collaboration reasonably well, while the $p/π$ ratio is overestimated by a factor of 1.5, similar to those from other thermal model calculations. It is found that the ratios of $p/π$ and $K/π$ are dominated by the radial flow but slightly affected by the baryon chemical potential. Our study thus constrains the parameters at the chemical and kinetic freeze-out stages within the framework of thermal model in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV, and will help better understand the properties of the dense and hot matter created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at freeze-out stage.

## Key claim

The blast-wave model with thermal equilibrium can reproduce the transverse momentum spectra and particle ratios for pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the model overestimates the p/π ratio by a factor of 1.5, similar to other thermal model calculations, indicating a systematic limitation of the thermal approach for baryon production.

## Method

The authors used a blast-wave model assuming local thermal equilibrium and radial expansion to compute the phase-space distribution of hadrons at freeze-out. Particle yields were calculated from the chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T_ch, baryon chemical potential μ_B, strangeness chemical potential μ_S) using Eq. (1), while transverse momentum spectra were obtained from the kinetic freeze-out parameters (temperature T_kin and radial flow parameter ρ_0) using the Wigner function formalism of Eq. (2). The model calculations were compared with ALICE experimental data for central (0-5%) and peripheral (70-80%) Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Baryon chemical potential μ_B = 0.1 MeV gives anti-particle to particle ratios compatible with unity, consistent with ALICE data showing π⁻/π⁺ = 0.998 and K⁻/K⁺ = 1.00 for 0-5% centrality
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch = 160 MeV for central collisions and T_ch = 150 MeV for peripheral collisions
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T_kin = 99 MeV with ρ_0 = 1 for central collisions; T_kin = 140 MeV with ρ_0 = 0.78 for peripheral collisions
- The p/π ratio is overestimated by a factor of 1.5 compared to ALICE data
- Local inverse slope T_loc for protons and kaons converges to approximately 0.45 GeV/c at high p_T (above 1 GeV/c for K and 2 GeV/c for p)
- Resonance decay contributes 60-80% of total pion yield in central collisions, but only 60% at low p_T decreasing to 40% at high p_T in peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends thermal model studies previously applied at RHIC energies (such as the blast-wave model by Retiere and Lisa (2004)) to LHC energies, and is comparable to other thermal model calculations (Thermal-PMB) that also overestimate the p/π ratio. The results constrain the chemical and kinetic freeze-out parameters for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and the finding that the p/π ratio is systematically overestimated by thermal models points to the need for more sophisticated treatment of baryon production mechanisms, possibly involving non-equilibrium effects or additional hadronic interactions beyond the thermal equilibrium assumption.
