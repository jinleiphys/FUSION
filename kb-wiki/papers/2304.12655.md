# Accurate relativistic density functional for exchange energy of atomic nuclei

> The inclusion of nucleonic exchange energy has been a long-standing challenge for the relativistic density functional theory (RDFT) in nuclear physics. We propose an orbital-dependent relativistic Kohn-Sham density functional theory to incorporate the exchange energy with local Lorentz scalar and vector potentials. The relativistic optimized effective potential equations for the local exchange potentials are derived and solved efficiently. The obtained binding energies and charge radii for nuclei are benchmarked with the results given by the traditional relativistic Hartree-Fock approach, which involves complicated nonlocal potentials. It demonstrates that the present framework is not only accurate but also efficient.

## Key claim

The authors construct, for the first time, an orbital-dependent relativistic density functional for the nuclear relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) energy, in which the single-particle RHF orbitals are replaced with Kohn-Sham orbitals and the exchange potentials are fully local. The framework is benchmarked against traditional RHF results and is claimed to be both accurate and efficient, though the ground-state energies obtained via the relativistic Kohn-Sham (RKS) method are expected to be slightly higher than those of RHF because the former is a constrained optimization using only local auxiliary potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the RHF energy derived from an effective Lagrangian with nucleons interacting via σ, ω, and ρ mesons and the photon. They replace the occupied single-particle states with Kohn-Sham orbitals obtained by solving the relativistic Kohn-Sham equation with local Lorentz scalar and vector potentials. The local exchange potentials are determined by solving the relativistic optimized effective potential (ROEP) equations, which are derived via the chain rule of functional differentiation and consist of a pair of coupled equations for the scalar and vector channels; the orbital-shift terms in the ROEP equations are neglected (consistent with the relativistic Krieger-Li-Iafrate approximation), and the equations are solved iteratively until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Total energy per nucleon as a function of iteration step is shown for ¹⁶O, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb; the RKS and RHF results converge to almost the same value with a similar convergence pattern.
- The total computing time for the RKS calculations is about one third of that for the RHF calculations (shown in inset clocks for ¹⁶O, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the energy difference between the RKS and RHF results can reach 5.7 MeV when the energy-shift term is neglected.
- The RKS calculation for ¹⁶O reproduces the correct long-range Coulomb behavior of the proton potential, decaying as ∼(Z−1)e²/r (Z is the proton number), in contrast to the Hartree potential which decays as ∼Ze²/r due to self-interaction.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic density functional theory (RDFT) by incorporating exchange energy, which has been a long-standing challenge; previous RDFT functionals contained only kinetic and Hartree energies and could not directly relate to realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The approach is benchmarked against the traditional relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) method with the PKO2 effective interaction (Long, 2008) and is inspired by the optimized effective potential method widely used in Coulombic systems (Engel, 1995; Ködderitzsch, 2008). The new framework enables extension to deformed nuclei on three-dimensional lattices and provides a path toward an ab initio relativistic energy density functional based on realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions such as Bonn potentials (Machleidt, 1989) and relativistic chiral interactions (Ren, 2018).
