# Photoelectric disintegration of 16O

> The photoelectric cross section of $^{16}$O($γ$,$α$)$^{12}$C is estimated to be larger than the radiative capture cross section of $^{12}$C($α$ ,$γ$)$^{16}$O. The predicted cross section and the angular distribution of $α$-particle are illustrated for the future experiment. The cross section just above the $α$-particle threshold is found to be dominated by the $E$2 excitation.

## Key claim

The photoelectric cross section of the 16O(γ,α)12C reaction is estimated to be larger than the radiative capture cross section of 12C(α,γ)16O, with the cross section just above the α-particle threshold dominated by the E2 excitation rather than the E1 transition that has been presumed important in prior work.

## Method

The author used the reciprocity theorem to relate the photodisintegration cross section of 16O(γ,α)12C to the capture cross section of the inverse 12C(α,γ)16O reaction. The same potential model as in Katsuma (2008) was employed to calculate the cross sections and angular distributions, with results multiplied by the Gamow factor exp(2πη) to compensate for the rapid energy variation near the α-particle threshold.

## Key numbers

- At Eγ ≈ 8.41 MeV (corresponding to Ec.m. = 1.25 MeV), σγα ≈ 50 × σαγ, making the photoelectric cross section about 50 times larger than the capture cross section.
- At Eγ = 8.0 MeV, the differential cross section at 90° is 3.11×10−12 b/sr, the integrated E1 cross section is 2.60×10−11 b, and exp(2πη) = 5.72×109.
- At Eγ = 8.5 MeV, the differential cross section at 90° is 4.22×10−10 b/sr, the integrated E1 cross section is 3.53×10−9 b, and exp(2πη) = 5.27×107.
- At Eγ = 9.0 MeV, the differential cross section at 90° is 1.19×10−8 b/sr, the integrated E1 cross section is 9.98×10−8 b, and exp(2πη) = 3.88×106.
- At Eγ = 9.5 MeV, the differential cross section at 90° is 3.47×10−7 b/sr, the integrated E1 cross section is 2.91×10−6 b, and exp(2πη) = 6.94×105.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous potential model studies (Katsuma 2008, 2014b) which predicted that the low-energy cross section of the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction is dominated by E2 transition, contrary to the belief that the subthreshold 1−1 state at Ex = 7.12 MeV couples strongly with the 1−2 state at Ex = 9.585 MeV to enhance the E1 cross section. The predicted angular distributions and cross sections are intended to guide forthcoming experimental projects (Gai 2014, Ahmad 2013) using high-intensity laser photonuclear reactions, which could determine the 12C(α,γ)16O cross section more accurately and reduce the large uncertainties in helium-burning nucleosynthesis rates.
