# Description of quadrupole collectivity in N ~ 20 nuclei with techniques beyond the mean field

> Properties of the ground and several collective excited states of the light nuclei ^{30,32,34}Mg are described in the framework of the angular momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method using the quadrupole moment as collective coordinate and the Gogny force as the effective interaction. The calculated excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities agree reasonably well with experiment. The results clearly indicate that both the restoration of the rotational symmetry and the quadrupole dynamics are key ingredients for the description of the properties of the above mentioned nuclei.

# Description of quadrupole collectivity in N ~ 20 nuclei with techniques beyond the mean field

## Key claim

The angular momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method (AMP-GCM) with the Gogny D1S interaction reproduces the isotopic trend of excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities in 30,32,34Mg, but the calculated 2+ excitation energies are systematically larger than experimental values by a factor of roughly 1.5; this discrepancy is attributed to the use of projection after variation (PAV) rather than the more complete projection before variation (PBV), and applying a quenching factor of 0.7 to the rotational bands brings the results into much better agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed angular momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method (AMP-GCM) calculations using the mass quadrupole moment q20 as the collective coordinate, with intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions generated by the Gogny force (D1S parameterization) expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis of 10 major shells. The Hill-Wheeler equation was solved for each angular momentum I to obtain collective wave functions and energies for the ground and excited bands, with the projected norm and Hamiltonian kernels computed using the angular momentum projector P^I_00 restricted to K=0 axially symmetric configurations. To estimate the effect of projection before variation (PBV), self-consistent cranking (SCC) calculations were performed for selected configurations and compared with AMP results to extract a quenching factor of 0.7 for the rotational band energies.

## Key numbers

* For 32Mg, the calculated 0+1 to 2+1 excitation energy is 1.46 MeV (uncorrected) versus the experimental value of 0.885 MeV; applying the 0.7 quenching factor gives 1.02 MeV.
* For 34Mg, the calculated 0+1 to 2+1 excitation energy is 1.02 MeV (uncorrected) versus the Monte Carlo Shell Model prediction of 0.75 MeV; applying the 0.7 quenching factor gives 0.71 MeV.
* For 30Mg, the calculated 0+1 to 2+1 excitation energy is 2.15 MeV (uncorrected) versus the experimental value of 1.482 MeV; applying the 0.7 quenching factor gives 1.50 MeV.
* The B(E2,0+1 to 2+1) transition probabilities are 229 e2fm4 for 30Mg, 395 e2fm4 for 32Mg, and 525 e2fm4 for 34Mg, compared to the experimental value of 454 +/- 78 e2fm4 for 32Mg.
* The energy gain from quadrupole fluctuations at I=0 is 0.9 MeV for 30Mg, 1.0 MeV for 32Mg, and 0.7 MeV for 34Mg.
* The two-neutron separation energies S(2n) from the AMP-GCM calculation are 7.8 MeV for 32Mg and 6.13 MeV for 34Mg, compared to experimental values of 8.056 MeV and 6.896 MeV respectively.
* The spherical neutron sd-pf shell gap (Delta epsilon_f7/2-d3/2) for 32Mg with D1S is 5.4 MeV, and the f7/2-p3/2 gap is 1.8 MeV.
* For the D1 parameterization of the Gogny force in 32Mg, the B(E2,0+1 to 2+1) is 138 e2fm4 and the 2+ excitation energy is 2.25 MeV; for D1' these values are 299 e2fm4 and 1.67 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous angular momentum projected (AMP) energy landscape study of 30-34Mg and 32-38Si (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2000) by incorporating configuration mixing via the Generator Coordinate Method, which was necessary because two coexistent configurations (prolate and oblate) with comparable energy were found in all nuclei except 34Mg. The results are consistent with the shell model picture of Poves (1987-1994) where deformed ground states in this region arise from 2p-2h neutron excitations into the fp shell, and the calculated B(E2) values agree well with the Monte Carlo Shell Model predictions of Utsumo (1999). The work enables future full projection before variation calculations, which would require triaxial projection and are identified as the next step to improve agreement with experimental excitation energies, particularly for the 2+ states.
