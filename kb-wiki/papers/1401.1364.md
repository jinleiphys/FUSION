# Elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions within a partonic transport model

> The quark gluon plasma produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions exhibits remarkable features. It behaves like a nearly perfect liquid with a small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and leads to the quenching of highly energetic particles. We show that both effects can be understood for the first time within one common framework. Employing the parton cascade Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings (BAMPS), the microscopic interactions and the space-time evolution of the quark gluon plasma are calculated by solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation. Based on cross sections obtained from perturbative QCD with explicitly taking the running coupling into account, we calculate the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. With only one single parameter associated with coherence effects of medium-induced gluon radiation, the experimental data of both observables can be understood on a microscopic level. Furthermore, we show that perturbative QCD interactions with a running coupling lead to a sufficiently small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark gluon plasma, which provides a microscopic explanation for the observations stated by hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the near-perfect liquid behavior (small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio) and jet quenching (nuclear modification factor) of the quark-gluon plasma can be understood simultaneously within a single microscopic framework, using perturbative QCD cross sections with a running coupling and a single parameter X=0.3 associated with coherence effects of medium-induced gluon radiation.

## Method

The authors employed the parton cascade Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings (BAMPS) to solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for on-shell partons, including all 2→2 and 2↔3 processes for light quarks (u,d,s) and gluons with pQCD cross sections. The running coupling α_s was evaluated at the momentum transfer of each interaction, and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect was implemented via a Θ-function requiring the formation time of emitted gluons to be smaller than the mean free path, with the parameter X calibrated to nuclear modification factor data at RHIC. The nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 were calculated at both RHIC and LHC energies, and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s was computed via the Green-Kubo relation in a static medium.

## Key numbers

- LPM parameter X=0.3 gives best agreement with neutral pion R_AA data at RHIC
- At RHIC, the hadron curve from fragmentation lies between gluon and light quark curves; gluons are considerably stronger suppressed than light quarks due to larger color factor
- At LHC, charged hadron R_AA at a given p_T has approximately the same value as the parton R_AA at twice as large p_T
- Partonic freeze-out energy density set to ε = 0.6 GeV/fm^3 for elliptic flow calculation
- The η/s ratio at T=0.2 GeV for n_f=0 (gluon-dominated QGP) is approximately 0.2
- Gluon and quark fugacity at freeze-out at LHC take values of approximately 0.9 and 0.5, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous BAMPS calculations for elliptic flow (Xu 2007, Xu 2008, Xu 2010, Fochler 2011) and nuclear modification factor (Fochler 2008, Fochler 2010, Fochler 2011) by including improved Gunion-Bertsch cross sections and a running coupling for all channels. The results provide a microscopic explanation for the small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted from dissipative hydrodynamic models (Gale 2012), and enable future studies of improved LPM effect treatments, inclusion of quantum statistics, and investigations of fully reconstructed jets and heavy flavor within the same framework.
