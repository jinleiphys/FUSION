# Extracting dynamics in the fusion of neutron-rich light nuclei

> The dependence of fusion dynamics on neutron excess for light nuclei is extracted. This is accomplished by comparing the average fusion cross-section at energies just above the fusion barrier for $^{12-15}$C + $^{12}$C with measurements of the interaction cross-section from high evergy collisions. The experimental results indicate that the fusion cross-section associated with dynamics increases with increasing neutron excess. Calculations with a time-dependent Hartree-Fock model fail to describe the observed trend.

## Key claim

The fusion cross-section associated with dynamics increases with increasing neutron excess for light carbon isotopes, but a time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) model fails to describe this observed trend, suggesting an important aspect of the dynamics is not incorporated in the model.

## Method

The authors compared average above-barrier fusion cross-sections for 12-15C + 12C (measured using an active target approach at ANL, with data taken from Carnelli (2015) and Calderon (2015)) with interaction cross-sections from high-energy collisions at E/A ~ 900 MeV taken from Ozawa (2001). The difference between the average fusion cross-section and the interaction cross-section was defined as the cross-section due to fusion dynamics. TDHF calculations using the Sky3D code with a SLy4 interaction were performed for 12,14,22C + 12C and compared to the experimental results.

## Key numbers

- The interaction cross-section for 14C is ~850 mb, while for 16C it jumps to ~1050 mb, reflecting the completion of the 1p1/2 shell at N=8.
- The average above-barrier fusion cross-section was calculated over the energy interval 14 MeV ≤ E_cm ≤ 17 MeV, designated as ⟨σ_F⟩_14^17.
- The TDHF model predicts a constant dynamical contribution of approximately 280 mb above the geometric size, independent of neutron excess.
- The experimental data for ⟨σ_DYNAMICS⟩ shows a linear increase with neutron excess that the TDHF model fails to reproduce.

## Context

This work extends the study of fusion dynamics for neutron-rich light nuclei, building on prior measurements of fusion excitation functions for carbon isotopes by Carnelli (2015) and Calderon (2015), and interaction cross-sections by Ozawa (2001) and Tanihata (1985). The approach of comparing average fusion cross-sections with interaction cross-sections provides a novel method to isolate the dynamical contribution to fusion. The failure of the TDHF model to describe the observed trend indicates that an important aspect of the dynamics is not incorporated in the model, motivating future theoretical developments and measurements for more neutron-rich nuclei.
