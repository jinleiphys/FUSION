# A new effective interaction for the trapped fermi gas: the BEC-BCS crossover

> We extend a recently introduced separable interaction for the unitary trapped Fermi gas to all values of the scattering length. We derive closed expressions for the interaction matrix elements and the two-particle eigenvectors and analytically demonstrate the convergence of this interaction to the zero-range two-body pseudopotential for s-wave scattering. We apply this effective interaction to the three- and four-particle systems along the BEC-BCS crossover, and find that their low-lying energies exhibit convergence in the regularization parameter that is much faster than for the conventional renormalized contact interaction. We find similar convergence properties of the three-particle free energy at unitarity.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously introduced separable effective interaction for the unitary trapped Fermi gas to all values of the scattering length, claiming that the low-lying energies of three- and four-particle systems converge substantially faster in the regularization parameter than for the conventional renormalized contact interaction, though this faster convergence is demonstrated only for systems up to four particles and the convergence is not always monotonic.

## Method

The authors construct a separable effective interaction in the relative harmonic oscillator basis by choosing coefficients \(f_n\) such that the two-particle Hamiltonian reproduces the lowest \(q+1\) relative energies \(\varepsilon_0,\ldots,\varepsilon_q\) for any scattering length \(a\), using closed-form expressions derived from a Cauchy matrix inversion. They then apply this interaction in configuration-interaction (CI) calculations for three- and four-particle systems in a harmonic trap, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a many-particle basis of Slater determinants constructed from single-particle harmonic oscillator states up to shell \(N_{\text{max}}\), and study convergence as a function of both the regularization parameter \(q\) and the model space size \(N_{\text{max}}\).

## Key numbers

* For the three-particle system at unitarity, the lowest \(L^\pi=0^+\) state has exact value \(E=4.6662\ldots\hbar\omega\); with the effective interaction at \(q=4\) the energy is \(E=4.6602\,\hbar\omega\) (0.14% error), while at \(q=5\) it is \(E=4.6597\,\hbar\omega\).
* For the three-particle \(0^+_3\) state at unitarity, the effective interaction at \(q=4\) gives \(6.716\,\hbar\omega\) (0.7% error from exact \(6.6662\ldots\hbar\omega\)), whereas the renormalized contact interaction at \(q=4\) gives \(6.984\,\hbar\omega\) (5% error).
* For the four-particle \(0^+_1\) state at unitarity, the effective interaction at \(q=5\) gives \(E=7.036\,\hbar\omega\), compared to \(E=7.010\,\hbar\omega\) from Daily (2010) (0.4% difference).
* For the four-particle \(1^+_0\) state at unitarity, the effective interaction at \(q=5\) gives \(6.582\,\hbar\omega\), within error of the value \(6.588(20)\,\hbar\omega\) from Daily (2010).
* At \(q=4\) and \(N_{\text{max}}=13\), three-particle energies are converged in \(N_{\text{max}}\) to about \(0.0003\%\) in the BCS regime (\(a_{\text{osc}}/a=-0.8\)), \(0.002\%\) at unitarity, and \(0.01\%\) in the BEC regime (\(a_{\text{osc}}/a=0.8\)).
* At \(q=4\) and \(N_{\text{max}}=11\), four-particle energies are converged in \(N_{\text{max}}\) to about \(0.05\%\) at \(a_{\text{osc}}/a=-0.8\) and \(0.2\%\) at \(a_{\text{osc}}/a=0.8\).
* The asymptotic relative difference between the effective interaction two-particle eigenvector components and the exact ones scales as \(1/q\) for large \(q\), compared to \(1/\sqrt{q}\) for the renormalized contact interaction.

## Context

This work extends the separable effective interaction approach introduced by Alhassid (2008) for the unitary limit to arbitrary scattering lengths along the BEC-BCS crossover. It improves upon the slow convergence of the conventional renormalized contact interaction used in earlier CI calculations by Stetcu (2008) and Alhassid (2008), and provides an alternative to the effective field theory approach of Stetcu (2010) that includes perturbative higher-order interactions. The method enables more accurate few-body calculations for trapped Fermi gases, which can serve as benchmarks for quantum Monte Carlo methods and provide input for virial expansion approaches such as those of Liu (2009).
