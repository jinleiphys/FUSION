# Neutron-rich isotope production for $Z\geq 98$ in ${}^{238} \mathrm{U}+{ }^{248} \mathrm{Cm}$ reaction

> Background: Multi-nucleon transfer (MNT) reactions in actinide systems are a promising method to synthesize transuranium neutron-rich elements. Appropriate theoretical approaches are needed to understand the mechanism behind MNT. Purpose: This work aims to produce neutron-rich isotopes in the super-heavy region through the ${}^{238} \mathrm{U}+{ }^{248} \mathrm{Cm}$ system. We employ a microscopic approach to elucidate reaction mechanisms, and predict new isotope production that expands the known nuclear chart. Methods: The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, including fluctuations and correlations, is used to explain the primary cross-sections in MNT reactions based on the quasi-fission and inverse quasi-fission processes, and a statistical de-excitation model with GEMINI++ code to calculate the secondary fragment cross-sections Results: The calculated cross-sections using SMF and GEMINI++ explain available experimental results for the ${}^{238} \mathrm{U}+{ }^{248} \mathrm{Cm}$ system at $E_\mathrm{c.m.}=898.7$~MeV energy. This shows the effectiveness and applicability of the quantal diffusion approach based on the SMF theory in heavy-ion collisions. Conclusions: Production of transuranium neutron-rich elements with a proton number up to $Z=$101 are obtained with sizable cross-sections. Theoretical results calculated for the Z=102-105 region, for which there are no experimental data, show that the cross-section values would be lower than the microbarn level. SMF theory does not contain any adjustable parameters other than the standard parameters of the energy density functional used in the TDHF theory and is an important approach for the microscopic understanding of reaction mechanisms.

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, combined with the GEMINI++ statistical de-excitation code, can reproduce available experimental cross-sections for the 238U+248Cm system at Ec.m.=898.7 MeV and predict production of neutron-rich isotopes up to Z=101 with sizable cross-sections, while cross-sections for Z=102-105 are predicted to be below the microbarn level.

## Method

The authors employed the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, which includes fluctuations and correlations beyond mean-field theory, to calculate primary fragment cross-sections from multi-nucleon transfer (MNT) reactions based on quasi-fission and inverse quasi-fission processes. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional provided mean drift paths and transport coefficients for four distinct initial collision geometries (tip-tip, tip-side, side-tip, side-side). The excited primary fragments were then cooled using the statistical de-excitation code GEMINI++, which implements the Hauser-Feshbach formalism for light particle evaporation, the Bohr-Wheeler and Moretto formalisms for fission, and the Blatt-Weisskopf approach for gamma radiation.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: Ec.m.=898.7 MeV for the 238U+248Cm system
- Neutron dispersion σNN values range from approximately 1.1 to 9.2 across angular momenta ℓ=0-600ℏ for tip-tip geometry
- Proton dispersion σZZ values range from approximately 0.4 to 5.8 across angular momenta ℓ=0-600ℏ for tip-tip geometry
- Isovector curvature parameter α values: 0.177 (XX and YX geometries), 0.108 (XY), 0.120 (YY) for the 250U+236Cm system
- Isoscalar curvature parameter β values: 0.004 (XX, XY, YY geometries), 0.010 (YX) for the 238U+248Cm system
- The isoscalar path produces approximately a φ=31° angle with the horizontal neutron axis
- Production of transuranium neutron-rich elements with proton number up to Z=101 obtained with sizable cross-sections
- Cross-section values for Z=102-105 region predicted to be lower than the microbarn level

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach previously applied by Ayik et al. (2018, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2023) and Yilmaz et al. (2020) to one of the heaviest available actinide systems, 238U+248Cm, which has been studied experimentally by Kratz et al. (2013, 2015) and Schaedel et al. (1982) and theoretically by Peng et al. (2022), Zhao et al. (2023), and Zagrebaev et al. (2008). The results demonstrate that the SMF approach, which contains no adjustable parameters beyond the standard energy density functional parameters used in TDHF, can predict production cross-sections for neutron-rich isotopes up to Z=101 and provides a microscopic framework for understanding reaction mechanisms in the super-heavy region. The calculations for Z=102-105 (No, Lr, Rf, Db) provide predictions where no experimental data currently exist, guiding future experimental searches.
