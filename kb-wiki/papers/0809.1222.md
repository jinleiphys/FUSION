# Quark deconfinement phase transition for improved quark mass density-dependent model

> By using the finite temperature quantum field theory, we calculate the finite temperature effective potential and extend the improved quark mass density-dependent model to finite temperature. It is shown that this model can not only describe the saturation properties of nuclear matter, but also explain the quark deconfinement phase transition successfully. The critical temperature is given and the effect of $ω$- meson is addressed.

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model can simultaneously describe both the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the quark deconfinement phase transition, with a critical temperature of 127 MeV when the omega-meson field is included; the omega-meson's repulsive interaction lowers the critical temperature compared to the case without it.

## Method

The authors extended their previous zero-temperature IQMDD model to finite temperature using the finite-temperature quantum field theory formalism of Dolan and Jackiw, computing the one-loop effective potential under mean-field approximation. They solved the coupled differential equations for the quark spinor components and the sigma and omega meson fields self-consistently, with the sigma field forming a Friedberg-Lee soliton bag. The bag constant B(T) was defined as the difference between the effective potential at the false vacuum and the physical vacuum, and the critical temperature was determined by the condition B(T_C)=0.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for quark deconfinement: T_C = 127 MeV (with omega-meson field included)
- Critical temperature without omega-meson field: T_C = 140 MeV (higher than the 127 MeV value with omega-meson)
- Bag constant at zero temperature: B = 174 MeV fm^{-3}
- Omega-meson mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Sigma-meson mass: m_sigma = 509 MeV
- Coupling constants: f = 5.45 (quark-sigma coupling), g = 3.37 (quark-omega coupling)
- Nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV
- Sigma self-interaction parameter: b = -8400 MeV
- Effective potential was computed at temperatures T = 0 MeV, T = 80 MeV, T = 127 MeV, and T = 204 MeV, showing a first-order phase transition

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IQMDD model (Wu and Su, 2006) which introduced sigma and omega mesons to mimic quark interactions but did not study deconfinement. It contrasts with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988), which uses MIT bag boundary conditions that permanently confine quarks and cannot describe deconfinement. The IQMDD model replaces the MIT bag with a Friedberg-Lee soliton bag whose spontaneous symmetry breaking is restored at high temperature, enabling the study of deconfinement; the results are similar to those of the FL model without omega-meson (Refs. 22, 25-27) but with the omega field lowering the critical temperature. The next step is to add rho and pi mesons to address isospin dependence and chiral symmetry.
