# Pair production with electron capture in peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy ions

> The production of electron-positron pairs with the capture of the electron in an atomic orbital is investigated for the conditions of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider LHC). Dirac wave functions for the leptons are used, taking corrections to orders of Z.alpha into account. The dependence on the transverse momentum transfer is studied and the accuracy of the equivalent photon approximation is discussed as a function of the nuclear charge.

## Key claim

The cross section for electron-positron pair production with K-shell electron capture in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be calculated using Dirac wavefunctions with first-order corrections in Zα, and this approach yields results that agree closely with calculations using full Sommerfeld-Maue wavefunctions, except that a commonly used approximate formula from Bertulani (1988) underestimates the cross section by up to a factor of 5 for large nuclear charges (Z ∼ 80–90).

## Method

The authors used a semiclassical approach with straight-line trajectories for the colliding ions, working in the rest frame of the nucleus where the electron is captured. The positron wavefunction was treated as a plane wave plus a first-order correction term proportional to Ze², with the normalization constant accounting for distortion to all orders, while the electron was described by a distorted wavefunction based on the non-relativistic hydrogenic wavefunction. The cross section was derived by integrating the square of the first-order probability amplitude over impact parameters, separating the result into transverse, longitudinal, and interference components of virtual photons, and comparing with the equivalent photon approximation and with results using Sommerfeld-Maue wavefunctions from Bertulani (1988).

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au beams at RHIC (100 GeV/nucleon), the calculated total cross section is σ = 229 b.
- For Pb+Pb beams at LHC (3 TeV/nucleon), the calculated total cross section is σ = 562 b.
- The approximate formula from Bertulani (1988) (equation labeled bbb) disagrees with the exact integration by a factor of 5 for large Z (Z ∼ 80–90), while agreeing for low Z (Z ≲ 15).
- The numerical integration of the exact expression and the Bertulani (1988) differential formula (equation labeled dsigde) agree within 2% of each other.
- For Au+Au at RHIC, a previous numerical calculation using full Dirac wavefunctions by Baltz et al. (1993) gave 114 b, which is about 2 times smaller than the present result and about 2 times larger than the Bertulani (1988) approximate formula.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of pair production with electron capture from Bertulani (1988) by using Dirac wavefunctions with first-order Zα corrections rather than Sommerfeld-Maue wavefunctions, and shows that the earlier approximate analytical formula underestimates cross sections for heavy nuclei. The results enable improved predictions for beam luminosity loss at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that atomic screening (not included here) would reduce cross sections by a factor of 2–4 compared to experimental data from Krause et al. (1998). The approach also provides a direct connection to photo-production cross sections and confirms the findings of Sauter (1931) for the inverse process of positron annihilation with K-shell electrons.
