# Energy-Energy Correlators in $e^+e^-$ and Deep Inelastic Scattering

> We study energy-energy correlators (EECs) in $e^+e^-$ annihilation and deep inelastic lepton-hadron scattering (DIS), focusing on aspects of nonperturbative physics in these observables. We introduce the EEC jet functions and investigate the infrared (IR) behavior of both small-angle EECs and angle-integrated EECs by performing explicit one-loop calculations. The factorization and universality of the EECs in these processes are demonstrated. A matching scheme is proposed to smoothly connect kinematic regions where different scaling behaviors with jet energy are observed. In combination with the next-to-leading order correction, this matching provides a good description of the EEC data and PYTHIA simulations in high-energy $e^+e^-$ annihilation. Predictions for DIS processes for future electron-ion collider kinematics are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that energy-energy correlators (EECs) in both \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) can be factorized in terms of universal EEC jet functions, but only after a matching scheme is introduced to connect the perturbative and nonperturbative scaling regions; the abstract's claim of "good description" is qualified by the need to fit a nonperturbative parameter \(\Lambda_i\) to data and by the fact that the leading-logarithmic approximation alone fails at low transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors introduce integrated and unintegrated EEC jet functions derived from di-hadron fragmentation functions, summing over all hadrons weighted by their energy fractions. They perform explicit one-loop calculations in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and DIS to study the infrared behavior of small-angle and angle-integrated EECs, deriving evolution equations in Fourier \(b_T\)-space. A matching scheme is proposed that uses a \(b_*\) prescription and an exponential nonperturbative model to smoothly connect the perturbative region (where the anomalous dimension is \(\gamma^{(3)}\)) to the nonperturbative region (where it becomes \(\gamma^{(5)}\)), with the matching parameter \(\mu_0\) representing the scale where the low-\(q_T\) behavior takes over.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order integrated EEC for \(e^+e^-\) annihilation is \(\Sigma_2^{(0)} = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \Gamma_q(Q))\), where \(\Gamma_q\) is the quark EEC jet function.
* The anomalous dimension matrix at \(N=3\) for \(n_f=5\) has eigenvalues approximately 6.14 and 2.51, indicating \(\Gamma_{q,g}(\mu) \to 1\) as \(\mu \to \infty\).
* Input values for the integrated EEC jet functions at \(\mu_{\rm init}=2\) GeV are \(\Gamma_q(\mu_{\rm init})=0.754\) and \(\Gamma_g(\mu_{\rm init})=0.824\), derived from \(\Gamma'_q(\mu_{\rm init})=0.246\) and \(\Gamma'_g(\mu_{\rm init})=0.176\) from globally fitted fragmentation functions (Gao 2024).
* The nonperturbative parameter \(\Lambda_q \approx \Lambda_g = 4.37\) GeV is chosen to describe near-side EECs in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation.
* The matching coefficient \({\cal C}_{qq}(\mu)\) decreases by about 20% when \(\mu\) increases from 50 GeV to 1 TeV.
* The \(b_*\) prescription uses \(b_{\rm max} = 1.5\ {\rm GeV}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach for EECs established by Dixon (2019) and the di-hadron fragmentation function formalism of deFlorian (2003), while also building on the TMD resummation framework of Collins (1984). The paper provides explicit one-loop calculations that complement recent alternative approaches using di-hadron fragmentation for near-side EECs by Lee (2025) and Kang (2025). The results enable predictions for DIS at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and offer a framework for precision studies of hadronization effects in EEC observables across different collision systems.
