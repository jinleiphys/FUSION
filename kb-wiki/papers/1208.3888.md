# Different scenarios of topological phase transitions in homogeneous neutron matter

> We study different scenarios of topological phase transitions in the vicinity of the π^0 condensation point in neutron matter. The transitions occur between the Fermi-liquid state and a topologically different one with two sheets of the Fermi surface. Two possibilities of a rearrangement of quasiparticle degrees of freedom are shown: the first-order topological phase transition and the second-order one. The order of the phase transition is found to be strongly dependent on the value of the critical wave vector of the soft π^0 mode. The thermodynamics of the system is also studied. It is shown that the topology of the quasiparticle momentum distribution is mainly determined by the neutron matter density, while the temperature T is essential in a narrow density region. A simple explanation of the first-order topological phase transition at T=0 is given.

## Key claim

The order of the topological phase transition between the Fermi-liquid state and a state with two sheets of the Fermi surface in neutron matter near the π⁰ condensation point depends strongly on the value of the critical wave vector q_c of the soft π⁰ mode; when q_c < p_F the transition is second-order, but when q_c ≳ p_F it becomes first-order.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Landau-Migdal quasiparticle equations (the Landau relation for the quasiparticle spectrum, the Fermi-Dirac distribution at finite temperature, and the density normalization condition) using a semi-microscopic quasiparticle interaction function derived from the exchange of a soft π⁰ collective mode. The interaction function was constructed from the renormalized π⁰-nucleon vertex and the pion polarization operator, with two parameter sets for the Δ-resonance-hole contribution that both reproduce the critical density ρ_c ≈ 0.2 fm⁻³ but give different critical wave vectors q_c. The equations were solved by a contracting iterations method, with T=0 modeled by T = 10⁻⁵ ε_F⁰.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for π⁰ condensation: ρ_c ≈ 0.2 fm⁻³, consistent with microscopic calculations by Wiringa and by Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998).
- For the second-order transition (q_c = 0.74 p_F), the bifurcation occurs at Fermi momentum p_F^b ≈ 2.562 m_π.
- For the first-order transition (q_c ≈ p_F), the first-order phase transition occurs at p_F^(1st) ≈ 2.573 m_π, with coexisting states in the interval 2.57 m_π ≲ p_F ≲ 2.58 m_π.
- The temperature-driven first-order transition occurs at T₁ ≈ 6.2 × 10⁻² ε_F⁰ ≈ 5 MeV for the system at p_F = 2.572 m_π.
- The critical Fermi momenta for the two parametrizations are p_Fc ≈ 2.602 m_π and p_Fc ≈ 2.598 m_π, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of topological phase transitions in neutron matter near the π⁰ condensation point by Voskr (2000) and Baldo (2004), which treated the quasiparticle interaction with phenomenological parameters. The authors show that the order of the transition depends on the critical wave vector q_c, a quantity not accurately known from microscopic calculations. The results are relevant for neutron star cooling, as the appearance of a low-momentum sheet of the Fermi surface can enable direct Urca processes, a mechanism previously considered by Voskr (2000) and Khod (2004) for enhanced cooling of neutron stars such as Vela, Geminga, and 3C58.
