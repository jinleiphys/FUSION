# Excluded volume effects in the hybrid star EoS

> In this contribution, we outline a new 2-phase description of the quark-nuclear matter hybrid equation of state that takes into account effects of phase space occupation (excluded volume) in both, the hadronic and the quark matter phases. For the nuclear matter phase, the reduction of the available volume at increasing density leads to a stiffening, while for the quark matter phase a reduction of the effective string tension in the confining density functional is obtained. The deconfinement phase transition in the resulting hybrid equation of state is sensitive to both excluded volume effects. As an application, we consider matter under compact star constraints of electric neutrality and $β$-equilibrium. We obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars that fulfill the $2M_\odot$ constraint and exhibit the high-mass twin phenomenon. Both features depend sensitively on the excluded volume.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that including excluded volume effects in both the hadronic and quark matter phases of a hybrid equation of state can produce high-mass twin star configurations that satisfy the 2 solar mass constraint, but the occurrence and prominence of this twin phenomenon depends sensitively on the excluded volume parameter.

## Method
The authors construct a two-phase hybrid equation of state for quark-nuclear matter at zero temperature under neutron star conditions of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium. For the hadronic phase, they apply an excluded volume prescription to the DD2 equation of state, while for the quark phase they use a relativistic density functional approach based on the string-flip model with a density-dependent effective string tension that scales with the available volume fraction. The deconfinement phase transition is determined by the intersection of the hadronic and quark matter branches in the pressure-chemical potential plane, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Hybrid star masses of 1.8–2.3 solar masses and radii of 11–14 km are obtained for the third family branch, well separated from the hadronic star branch radii of 15.0–15.5 km in the same mass range.
- For excluded volume parameter v_ex = 0, the 2 solar mass constraint is not fulfilled.
- The inverse linear excluded volume scheme uses parameters (v_ex = 2.0 fm^3, D_0 = 220 MeV, a = 0.25, b = 0.04) for the SFM-C configuration.
- The Gaussian excluded volume scheme uses parameters (v_ex = 2.0 fm^3, D_0 = 190 MeV, a = 0.29, b = 0.01) for the SFM-B configuration.

## Context
This work extends the string-flip model approach of Horowitz (1985) and Ropke (1986) by giving it a relativistic form as in Khvorostukin (2006), and builds on the necessary conditions for high-mass twin stars identified by Benic (2014). The paper enables future observational programmes aimed at sufficiently accurate neutron star radius measurements to test the existence of a third family of compact stars, which would imply a strong first-order phase transition in compact star matter at zero temperature and thus a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
