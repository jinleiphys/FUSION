# The influence of $δ$ meson on the isospin splitting of in-medium $NN\to NΔ$ cross sections

> The isospin splitting of the in-medium $NN\rightarrow NΔ$ cross sections in asymmetric nuclear medium are investigated in the framework of the one-boson exchange model by including $δ$ and $ρ$ mesons. Our results show that the medium correction factors $R=σ_{ NN\rightarrow NΔ}^*/σ_{NN\rightarrow NΔ}^{\text{free}}$ have $R_{pp \to nΔ^{++}} < R_{nn \to pΔ^{-}}$ and $R_{NN \to NΔ^{+}} <R_{NN \to NΔ^{0}}$ by using the without-$δ$ sets. By including the $δ$ meson, it appears the totally opposite results in the $R$ for different channels, i.e., $R_{pp \to nΔ^{++}} > R_{nn \to pΔ^{-}}$ and $R_{NN \to NΔ^{+}} >R_{NN \to NΔ^{0}}$.

## Key claim

The isospin splitting of the in-medium correction factor \(R = \sigma^*_{NN\to N\Delta}/\sigma^{\text{free}}_{NN\to N\Delta}\) reverses sign when the \(\delta\) meson is included in the one-boson exchange model: without the \(\delta\) meson, \(R_{pp \to n\Delta^{++}} < R_{nn \to p\Delta^{-}}\) and \(R_{NN \to N\Delta^{+}} < R_{NN \to N\Delta^{0}}\), but with the \(\delta\) meson the ordering becomes \(R_{pp \to n\Delta^{++}} > R_{nn \to p\Delta^{-}}\) and \(R_{NN \to N\Delta^{+}} > R_{NN \to N\Delta^{0}}\). This reversal depends on the specific relativistic mean-field parameter set and is most pronounced near the threshold energy (\(E_{\text{beam}} = 0.4\) GeV).

## Method

The in-medium \(NN \to N\Delta\) cross sections were calculated using the one-boson exchange model with a relativistic Lagrangian that includes \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\delta\), and \(\pi\) mesons, employing both nonlinear (NL) and density-dependent (DD) relativistic mean-field parameter sets. Six parameter sets were used: three without the \(\delta\) meson (NL\(\rho\)-\(\Delta\), DDME2-\(\Delta\), DDRH\(\rho\)-\(\Delta\)) and three with the \(\delta\) meson (NL\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\), DDME\(\delta\)-\(\Delta\), DDRH\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\)). The in-medium cross sections were obtained by replacing free masses and momenta with effective Dirac masses and kinetic momenta from the mean-field solutions, averaging over the \(\Delta\) mass distribution via a spectral function, and applying form factors to account for finite baryon size and high-order contributions.

## Key numbers

- The medium correction factor \(R\) was evaluated at a beam energy of \(E_{\text{beam}} = 0.4\) GeV (near threshold) for isospin asymmetry \(\alpha = 0.2\).
- For the without-\(\delta\) sets, the ordering was \(R_{pp\to n\Delta^{++}} < R_{Np\to N\Delta^{+}} < R_{Nn\to N\Delta^{0}} < R_{nn\to p\Delta^{-}}\); for the with-\(\delta\) sets, the ordering reversed to \(R_{pp\to n\Delta^{++}} > R_{nn\to p\Delta^{-}}\) and \(R_{NN\to N\Delta^{+}} > R_{NN\to N\Delta^{0}}\).
- The effective mass splittings at \(\alpha = 0.2\) for the with-\(\delta\) sets were: \(\Delta m^*_N = (m^*_p - m^*_n)/m_N = 0.0312\) (NL\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\)), 0.0236 (DDME\(\delta\)-\(\Delta\)), 0.0265 (DDRH\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\)); \(\Delta m^*_\Delta = (m^*_{\Delta^{++}} - m^*_{\Delta^+})/m_\Delta = 0.0079\) (NL\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\)), 0.0060 (DDME\(\delta\)-\(\Delta\)), 0.0068 (DDRH\(\rho\delta\)-\(\Delta\)).
- The free-space \(pp \to n\Delta^{++}\) cross section data from Baldini (1987) were used to fit the cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta}\) (410–419 MeV) and \(\Lambda_{\rho NN}\) (580–1000 MeV) for each set.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium \(NN \to N\Delta\) cross sections by Li and Li (2017) and Cui et al. (2018, 2019), who investigated isospin splitting from effective mass differences and threshold effects but did not systematically compare parameter sets with and without the \(\delta\) meson. The results show that the \(\delta\) meson reverses the isospin splitting of the medium correction factor, which is important because most relativistic mean-field parameter sets (about 95%) and transport models such as BUU (Larionov, 2003) and RVUU (Song, 2015) omit the \(\delta\) meson. This reversal can affect predictions of pion observables (e.g., the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio) in heavy-ion collisions, which are used to probe the symmetry energy at high density.
