# Relativistic constraints on 3N contact interactions

> In this paper we analyze the relativistic corrections to the leading order three-nucleon (3N) contact interactions. These boost corrections are derived first from the nonrelativistic reduction of covariant Lagrangians and later from the Poincaré algebra constraints on nonrelativistic theories. We show that in order to describe the 3N potential in reference frames other than the center-of-mass frame, the inclusion of five additional terms with fixed coefficients is required. These terms will be relevant in systems with mass number A>3. How they will affect EFT calculations of binding energies and scattering observables in these systems should be investigated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that to describe the three-nucleon (3N) contact potential in reference frames other than the center-of-mass frame, five additional momentum-dependent terms with fixed coefficients must be included, and these boost corrections will be relevant in systems with mass number A > 3.

## Method

The authors derived relativistic corrections to leading-order 3N contact interactions using two complementary approaches: first, by performing a nonrelativistic reduction of covariant Lagrangians containing 92 relativistic contact operators, and second, by imposing Poincare algebra constraints on nonrelativistic theories. The nonrelativistic expansions were rewritten in a minimal basis of operators using Fierz relations, yielding the frame-dependent potential expressed in terms of the overall momentum P.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order 3N contact potential in a general frame contains 13 center-of-mass operators O_1 through O_13 and 5 additional P-dependent operators O_1* through O_5* with fixed coefficients, as shown in Eq. (1)
- The relativistic correction O_P^(0) is given by the combination 6 O_0 + (1/(4m^2)) * ((2/3) O_1* + O_2*), which starts at order Q^0

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of relativistic corrections to two-nucleon contact forces up to order 1/m^2 by Girlanda and Schiavilla (2010) and up to 1/m^4 by Filandri and collaborators (2023), which identified two free LECs parameterizing momentum-dependent NN interactions. The study also builds on the classification of subleading 3N contact forces by Girlanda (2011), which identified 13 independent operators in the 3N center-of-mass frame. The results enable future EFT calculations of binding energies and scattering observables in systems with A > 3, where unexplained discrepancies between theory and experiment exist, and may help address the p-d A_y puzzle.
