# Bottom and Charm Production at LHC and RHIC

> We study $b\bar{b}$ and $c\bar{c}$ production and the influence of nuclear shadowing at LHC and RHIC energies. We find a significant reduction in the production cross section of both charm and bottom at RHIC and LHC. Bound states such as $Υ$ and $J/Ψ$ are suppressed by this reduction in the charm production cross sections. Therefore, $J/Ψ$ suppression may not be useful as a signature for the quark gluon plasma.

# Bottom and Charm Production at LHC and RHIC

## Key claim

The reduction in heavy quark production cross sections due to nuclear shadowing of gluon distribution functions is significant enough that J/Ψ suppression at RHIC and LHC may be caused by this depletion of initial partons rather than by the existence of a quark-gluon plasma, but the authors caution that a full calculation requires including a hadronization model and secondary charm production from minijet rescattering before any final conclusion can be drawn.

## Method

The authors compute leading-order gluon fusion processes g+g → Q+Q̅ for charm and bottom quark production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC (Au-Au at √s = 200 GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb at √s = 5.5 TeV), using perturbative QCD with the GRV98 set of gluon distribution functions for free nucleons and a strong shadowing parametrization from Hammon et al. for bound nucleons in a nucleus with A = 208. The cross sections per nucleon-nucleon collision are obtained by integrating the partonic cross section over the gluon structure functions with and without shadowing effects.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy, the reduction in the c̅c production cross section due to shadowing is about a factor of 10.
- The ratio of heavy-quark production cross sections with and without shadowing is close to 1 for top quark production at LHC (M_Q ~ 200 GeV).
- The shadowing effect on charm production at LHC is much larger than that on bottom production.
- At RHIC energy, the difference in c̅c production cross section between the shadowed and unshadowed cases is not very large but still significant.
- The results are per nucleon-nucleon collision; to obtain total nucleus-nucleus cross sections, one multiplies by A², and the Glauber geometrical factor T(0) ~ A²/(πR_A²) for central collisions behaves like A^(4/3).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear shadowing effects on parton distribution functions, previously developed by Hammon et al. and based on the parton fusion formalism of Qiu, to heavy quark production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The paper directly challenges the interpretation of J/Ψ suppression as a signature for quark-gluon plasma, as proposed by Satz and others, by showing that initial-state shadowing alone can produce significant suppression. The authors note that secondary charm production from minijet rescattering in the pre-equilibrium stage must still be calculated, as it could potentially lead to J/Ψ enhancement rather than suppression.
