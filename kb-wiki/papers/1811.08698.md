# Towards a holographic quark-hadron continuity

> We study dense nuclear and quark matter within a single microscopic approach, namely the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model. Nuclear matter is described via instantons in the bulk, and we show that instanton interactions are crucial for a continuous connection of chirally broken and chirally symmetric phases. The continuous path from nuclear to quark matter includes metastable and unstable stationary points of the potential, while the actual chiral phase transition remains of first order, as in earlier approximations. We show that the model parameters can be chosen to reproduce low-density properties of nuclear matter and observe a non-monotonic behavior of the speed of sound as a function of the baryon chemical potential, as suggested by constraints from QCD and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The authors show that within the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model, instanton interactions are necessary to continuously connect chirally broken nuclear matter and chirally symmetric quark matter in order parameter space, but the actual chiral phase transition between stable phases remains first order as in earlier approximations.

## Method

The authors employ the gauge/gravity duality using the Sakai-Sugimoto model in the decompactified limit with deconfined geometry, describing nuclear matter via instanton solutions on flavor branes and quark matter via strings attached to disconnected flavor branes. They construct a many-instanton approximation using the exact flat-space two-instanton solution from the ADHM construction, incorporating two-body interactions through a nearest-neighbor approximation on a regular lattice, and solve the equations of motion for the abelian gauge field and flavor brane embedding dynamically.

## Key numbers

- The instanton width parameters are given by \(\rho_0 = 6\sqrt{2\pi/\sqrt{5}} \simeq 10.06\) and \(\gamma_0 = 2\sqrt{2/3} \simeq 1.633\) in the large-'t Hooft coupling expansion
- The critical deconfinement temperature is \(T_c^{\rm deconf} = M_{\rm KK}/(2\pi)\) for all chemical potentials
- The chiral phase transition temperature at zero chemical potential is \(T_c^{\rm chiral} \simeq 0.15/L\), requiring \(\ell/\pi \lesssim 0.31\) for the deconfined geometry to be relevant
- The single instanton has topological charge 1, verified by \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty dz \, q_0(z) = 1\)
- The two-instanton solution has topological charge 2, verified by \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty dz \, q_{\rm int}(d,z) = 2\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Preis (2016) by accounting for instanton interactions in the bulk, which were neglected in that earlier treatment. The study addresses the quark-hadron continuity question previously explored by Schafer (1998), Alford (1999), Hatsuda (2006), and others, and is motivated by neutron star physics where thermodynamic properties depend on the nature of the nuclear-to-quark matter transition. The results enable future inclusion of quark masses and more realistic modeling of dense matter in neutron stars, complementing approaches that combine separate nuclear and quark matter models as done by Masuda (2012) and others.
