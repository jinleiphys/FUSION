# Structure functions in polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes

> Polarized proton-deuteron (pd) Drell-Yan processes are investigated for studying new spin structure of a spin-1 hadron. Our formalism indicates that there exist many structure functions: 108 in general and 22 in the \vec Q_T-integrated case. A naive parton model suggests that at least one of them should be related to the tensor polarized distributions, which have not been measured at all. There are some experimental possibilities at FNAL, HERA, and RHIC to study the polarized pd reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes can reveal new tensor-polarized antiquark distributions (specifically a \(b_1\)-type distribution and a newly named \(c_1\) distribution) that are inaccessible in electron-deuteron scattering, but the analysis is limited to a naive parton model that neglects \(O(1/Q)\) contributions.

## Method

The author develops a general formalism for polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes using two independent methods: the Jacob-Wick helicity formalism with spin density matrices, and an expansion of the hadron tensor in momentum and spin vectors. A naive parton-model analysis is then performed to relate the structure functions to parton distributions, integrating over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(\vec Q_T\) to reduce the number of finite structure functions.

## Key numbers

- 108 structure functions exist in the general polarized pd Drell-Yan formalism, compared to 48 in the pp Drell-Yan.
- 22 structure functions survive after integrating over \(\vec Q_T\), compared to 11 in the pp case.
- In the naive parton model with \(\vec Q_T\) integration, only 4 finite structure functions remain (unpolarized, longitudinally-polarized, transversity, and tensor-polarized).
- 15 distinct spin asymmetry combinations are identified for the pd Drell-Yan, including new quadrupole asymmetries \(A_{UQ_0}\), \(A_{UQ_1}\), \(A_{LQ_1}\), \(A_{TQ_1}\), \(A_{UQ_2}\), \(A_{LQ_2}\), and \(A_{TQ_2}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of polarized proton-proton Drell-Yan processes (which underlies the RHIC-SPIN project) to the proton-deuteron case, building on the author's prior studies (Kumano, 1999, Refs. hk1 and hk2). It addresses the limitation of electron-deuteron scattering, which cannot determine antiquark tensor distributions precisely (as illustrated by the Gottfried sum rule violation and the E866 Drell-Yan experiments that determined \(\bar u/\bar d\)). The formalism enables future experimental searches at FNAL, HERA-N, and a possible next-generation RHIC project, though significant theoretical and numerical work remains to assess feasibility.
