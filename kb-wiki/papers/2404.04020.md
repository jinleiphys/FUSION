# Unified equations of state for cold nonaccreting neutron stars with Brussels-Montreal functionals. V. Improved parametrization of the nucleon density distributions

> We previously studied the inner crust and the pasta mantle of a neutron star within the 4th-order extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach with consistent proton shell corrections added perturbatively via the Strutinsky integral (SI) theorem together with the contribution due to pairing. To speed up the computations and avoid numerical problems, we adopted parametrized nucleon density distributions. However, the errors incurred by the choice of the parametrization are expected to become more significant as the mean baryon number density is increased, especially in the pasta mantle where the differences in the energy per nucleon of the different phases are very small, typically a few keV. To improve the description of these exotic structures, we discuss the important features that a nuclear profile should fulfill and introduce two new parametrizations. Performing calculations using the BSk24 functional, we find that these parametrizations lead to lower ETF energy solutions for all pasta phases than the parametrization we adopted before and more accurately reproduce the exact equilibrium nucleon density distributions obtained from unconstrained variational calculations. Within the ETFSI method, all parametrizations predict the same composition in the region with quasi-spherical clusters. However, the two new parametrizations lead to a different mantle structure at mean baryon densities above about 0.07 fm^-3, at which point lasagna is energetically favored. Interestingly, spherical clusters reappear in the pasta region. The inverted pasta phases such as bucatini and Swiss cheese are still found in the densest region above the core in all cases.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the choice of parametrized nucleon density profiles significantly affects the predicted structure of the neutron star pasta mantle, and introduces two new parametrizations (SoftD and 3FD) that yield lower ETF energies and more accurately reproduce exact equilibrium profiles than the previously used StrD parametrization, particularly at mean baryon densities above about 0.07 fm^-3 where lasagna becomes energetically favored.

## Method
The authors performed fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations using the BSk24 functional for five pasta phases (spheres, spaghetti, lasagna, bucatini, Swiss cheese) with three different parametrizations of nucleon density distributions: the previously used StrD parametrization, and two new parametrizations called SoftD and 3FD. They then added proton shell corrections perturbatively via the Strutinsky Integral (SI) theorem to obtain full ETFSI results, comparing the equilibrium phase sequences and energy differences across parametrizations.

## Key numbers
- The onset of pasta phases shifts from approximately 0.05 fm^-3 for StrD to approximately 0.052-0.053 fm^-3 for the two new parametrizations
- The crust-core transition density is approximately 0.082 fm^-3 for all parametrizations
- Energy differences between new parametrizations and StrD reach a maximum of about 2-3 keV per nucleon for spheres and spaghetti near approximately 0.074 fm^-3
- Energy reduction for inverted phases does not exceed approximately 1 keV per nucleon
- Largest deviations of order less than 4-5 keV per nucleon are found in the lasagna phase
- Lasagna appears in the density range approximately 0.07 fm^-3 to 0.08 fm^-3 with the new parametrizations
- Proton drip occurs at approximately 0.078 fm^-3
- At density 0.065 fm^-3, the equilibrium proton number for spheres is Z=40 for all parametrizations

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous ETFSI studies of the neutron star inner crust and pasta mantle (Pearson+20, Pearson+22, Shchechilin+sym23) by improving the parametrization of nucleon density distributions. The new parametrizations address limitations of the StrD profile introduced in Onsi+08, which did not satisfy the symmetry condition between lasagna and anti-lasagna and could not describe certain inverted phase configurations. The results enable more reliable predictions of pasta mantle structure for use in studies of neutrino emission, thermal and electrical conductivities, bulk viscosity, and elastic properties of neutron star matter.
