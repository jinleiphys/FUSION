# Penta-quark States with Strangeness, Hidden Charm and Beauty

> The classical quenched quark models with three constituent quarks provide a good description for the baryon spatial ground states, but fail to reproduce the spectrum of baryon excited states. More and more evidences suggest that unquenched effects with multi-quark dynamics are necessary ingredients to solve the problem. Several new hyperon resonances reported recently could fit in the picture of penta-quark states. Based on this picture, some new hyperon excited states were predicted to exist; meanwhile with extension from strangeness to charm and beauty, super-heavy narrow $N^*$ and $Λ^*$ resonances with hidden charm or beauty were predicted to be around 4.3 and 11 GeV, respectively. Recently, two of such $N^*$ with hidden charm might have been observed by the LHCb experiment. More of those states are expected to be observed in near future. This opens a new window in order to study hadronic dynamics for the multi-quark states.

## Key claim

The classical quenched three-quark constituent model fails to reproduce the baryon excited-state spectrum, and unquenched effects with multi-quark dynamics, specifically penta-quark components, are necessary to resolve longstanding puzzles such as the mass-order reversal of the lowest excited baryons; extending this picture from strangeness to hidden charm and beauty predicts super-heavy narrow \(N^*\) and \(\Lambda^*\) resonances around 4.3 GeV and 11 GeV, two of which may have been observed by LHCb.

## Method

The authors review coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering models (Valencia and EBAC approaches) that solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation or a three-dimensional scattering equation for pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon channels, including \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c\), \(\bar{D}\Lambda_c\), \(\eta_c N\), \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\), \(\bar{D}^*\Lambda_c\), and \(J/\psi N\) for hidden charm, and analogous channels with \(b\) quarks for hidden beauty. They also consider a diquark-diquark-antiquark colored cluster model using chromomagnetic, flavor-spin, and instanton-induced interactions, and a breathing-model Hamiltonian with instanton-induced \(q\bar{q}\) creation for the \(\Omega^*\) system.

## Key numbers

- The lowest \(\Omega^*\) is predicted around 1780 MeV with \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\) from the NJL and instanton-induced interaction model, compared to the quenched model prediction of around 2020 MeV.
- From the Valencia approach for hidden charm: a \(N^*\) resonance from \(PB\) channels at mass 4261 MeV with width 56.9 MeV; a \(N^*\) from \(VB\) channels at 4412 MeV with width 47.3 MeV; \(\Lambda^*\) resonances from \(PB\) channels at 4209 MeV (width 32.4 MeV) and 4394 MeV (width 43.3 MeV); \(\Lambda^*\) resonances from \(VB\) channels at 4368 MeV (width 28.0 MeV) and 4544 MeV (width 36.6 MeV).
- For hidden beauty, masses are predicted around 11 GeV with widths of only a few MeV.
- The LHCb observed \(P_c^+(4380)\) with mass \(4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\) MeV and width \(205 \pm 18 \pm 86\) MeV, and \(P_c^+(4450)\) with mass \(4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5\) MeV and width \(39 \pm 5 \pm 19\) MeV.
- The branching ratio of \(\Psi(2S) \to \Omega \bar{\Omega}\) measured by BESII is \((5 \pm 2) \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously applied to strangeness baryons (e.g., \(N^*(1535)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) with large penta-quark components) to the charm and beauty sectors, building on predictions from Refs. [Ncc1, Ncc2] and the Valencia approach of Oset et al. It directly addresses the mass-order reversal problem for the lowest excited baryons (e.g., \(N^*(1535)\) heavier than \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) and \(N^*(1440)\)) and the failure of quenched models to explain the scalar meson nonet. The predictions of super-heavy narrow resonances enable experimental searches at facilities such as CEBAF (12 GeV upgrade), PANDA/FAIR, JPARC, and BESIII, and the LHCb observation of two \(P_c^+\) states provides the first possible evidence for penta-quark states with hidden charm.
