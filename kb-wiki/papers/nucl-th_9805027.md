# Spatial Variation of Nuclear Structure Functions and Heavy Quark Production

> We explore how nuclear modifications to the free nucleon structure functions (also known as shadowing) affect heavy quark production in collisions at different impact parameters. We assume that the nuclear modifications arise from a density dependent effect such as gluon recombination and are thus proportional to the local density. We calculate the dependence of charm and bottom quark production on impact parameter and show that density dependent modifications can lead to significant reductions in the heavy quark production cross sections in central relative to peripheral interactions.

# Spatial Variation of Nuclear Structure Functions and Heavy Quark Production

## Key claim

Assuming that nuclear shadowing modifications to parton distributions are proportional to the local nuclear density, the heavy quark production cross section in central nucleus-nucleus collisions can be significantly reduced relative to peripheral collisions, with the effect varying by up to approximately 50% depending on collision energy and the shadowing parameterization used.

## Method

The authors calculate leading-order charm and bottom quark production cross sections in heavy-ion collisions as a function of impact parameter, using the GRV 94 LO, GRV 94 HO, and MRS G nucleon parton distributions with quark masses m_c = 1.3 GeV and m_b = 4.75 GeV. They incorporate spatial dependence by assuming that nuclear modifications to the parton densities are proportional to the local nuclear density described by a Woods-Saxon distribution, using two shadowing parameterizations (S_1, which is Q^2-independent, and S_2, which includes Q^2 evolution) normalized to reproduce the impact-parameter-integrated cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Charm production in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV): cross section decreases by nearly 50% relative to the no-shadowing case when spatial dependence is included
- Charm production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV): cross section decreases by about 30% relative to the no-shadowing case
- Charm production in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV): cross section enhanced by 12% with the S_2 parameterization due to antishadowing
- Bottom production at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV): approximately 25% shadowing at b approximately 0 with S_2, compared with 60% for charm at the same impact parameter
- Bottom production at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV): 2% enhancement in central collisions due to antishadowing for S_2, 9% depletion for S_1
- Total cross sections integrated over impact parameter for GRV 94 LO at LHC: charm = 7440 microbarn per nucleon pair (S=1), 3910 (S_1), 3410 (S_2); bottom = 212 microbarn per nucleon pair (S=1), 120 (S_1), 160 (S_2)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear shadowing in heavy quark production by Gavin, McGaughey, Ruuskanen and Vogt (1996) that did not include spatial dependence, and builds on the authors' own previous work (Emel'yanov et al., 1997) on spatial dependence of shadowing at RHIC energies. The results show that using peripheral collisions as a baseline for comparison to central collisions can lead to significant errors if spatial dependence of shadowing is not accounted for, which is important for interpreting quark-gluon plasma signatures in experiments such as NA50 at CERN. The approach enables future studies of similar impact-parameter-dependent effects in other hard processes such as J/psi and Drell-Yan production.
