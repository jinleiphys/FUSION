# Domain growth and fluctuations during quenched transition to QGP in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We model the initial confinement-deconfinement transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a rapid quench in view of expected rapid thermalization to a QGP state. The transition is studied using the Polyakov loop model, with the initial field configuration (in the confining phase) covering a small neighborhood of the confining vacuum $l \simeq 0$, as appropriate for $T < T_c$. Quench is implemented by evolving this initial configuration with the effective potential at a temperature $T > T_c$. We study the formation of Z(3) domain structure and its evolution during the transition as $l$ rolls down in different directions from the top of the central hill in the effective potential of $l$. When explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking effects (arising from dynamical quark effects) are small, then we find well defined Z(3) domains which coarsen in time. Remarkably, the magnitude plot of $l$ shows vacuum bubble like configurations arising during the quench. This first order transition like behavior occurs even though there is no metastable vacuum separated by a barrier from the true vacuum for the parameter values used. When the initial field configuration everywhere rolls down roughly along the same direction (as will happen with large explicit symmetry breaking) then we do not find such bubble-like configurations. However, in this case we find huge oscillations of $l$ with large length scales. We show that such large oscillations can lead to large fluctuations in the evolution of flow anisotropies compared to the equilibrium transition case.

# Domain growth and fluctuations during quenched transition to QGP in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors model the confinement-deconfinement transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a rapid quench rather than an equilibrium transition, and show that the resulting dynamics depends critically on the strength of explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks. When explicit symmetry breaking is small, well-defined Z(3) domains form and coarsen, and surprisingly, bubble-like configurations appear in the magnitude of the Polyakov loop even though no metastable vacuum or potential barrier exists at the quench temperature used.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional field-theoretic simulations of the Polyakov loop order parameter using an effective potential fitted to lattice results for pure SU(3) gauge theory, with a linear term added to represent explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks. The initial field configuration was taken as a small patch around the confining vacuum at l ≈ 0, then evolved with the effective potential at a quench temperature T₀ = 400 MeV (or 500 MeV for some runs) using the time-dependent equation of motion in Minkowski space with Bjorken longitudinal expansion. The temperature was decreased as τ⁻¹/³, and simulations used a 2000 × 2000 lattice with 20 fm × 20 fm physical size and Δx = 0.01 fm.

## Key numbers

- Quench temperature T₀ = 400 MeV (and 500 MeV for bubble studies), with initial proper time τ₀ = 1 fm
- Transition temperature T_c = 182 MeV from the parameter values used
- Z(3) domains grow to size of order 2 fm within about 1 fm after quench (at τ = 2.0 fm), and to about 4 fm within another 1 fm (at τ = 2.8 fm)
- Magnitude of Polyakov loop l at early times (τ = 2.0 fm) is about 0.08 times its vacuum expectation value, reaching about 0.4 times vev at τ = 2.8 fm
- Smallest reasonable Z(3) domain sizes found are of order 1-2 fm at very early times
- Initial field magnitude taken as ε = 0.01 times the vacuum expectation value of l
- For flow anisotropy studies, initial temperature profile eccentricity of 0.5 was used, corresponding to ε_x = -0.143 for uniform energy density
- Lattice of 1000 × 1000 with physical size 25 fm × 25 fm used for flow anisotropy calculations
- Woods-Saxon temperature profile with diameter of about 16 fm used for Au-Au collision modeling
- Transverse expansion with uniform acceleration of 0.015 c per fm applied

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Gupta and Srivastava, earlier works) that modeled the transition as a first-order equilibrium process with bubble nucleation, appropriate for large chemical potential. The quench approach is motivated by the very short thermalization time (less than 1 fm) observed at RHIC and LHC, making equilibrium dynamics unlikely. The results contrast with simulations by Dumitru and collaborators who studied spinodal decomposition in the Polyakov loop model with a focus on fluctuations and long-wavelength mode growth, whereas this work emphasizes detailed domain coarsening, bubble-like structures, and effects on flow anisotropies. The findings enable future investigation of parametric resonance and novel particle production mechanisms during early QGP evolution, and raise questions about the validity of assuming equilibrium initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of elliptic flow.
