# Hydrodynamics from quantum fields: a regularized expansion from the Wigner distribution

> Second-order relativistic hydrodynamics is surprisingly predictive, even in the presence of large gradients. The hydrodynamic expansion from the method of moments does not require a gradient expansion, but it is intrinsically bound to the classic nature of relativistic kinetic theory. In this work a modified version of the method of moments is applied the Wigner distribution (the quantum precursor of the distribution function) to recover a systematically improvable hydrodynamic expansion, avoiding the divergences that would otherwise appear in the quantum case. The convergence of the regularized expansion is checked numerically in a far from equilibrium, distant from the kinetic limit case.

## Key claim

The method of moments can be generalized from classical relativistic kinetic theory to the quantum Wigner distribution by introducing a regularization parameter ζ, yielding a systematically improvable hydrodynamic expansion that avoids the infrared divergences that would otherwise appear in the quantum case; the regularized expansion converges numerically even in a far-from-equilibrium, off-shell regime where the gradient expansion would be expected to fail.

## Method

The author generalizes the method of moments by replacing the on-shell momentum integral with an integral over all four-momentum and introducing a Gaussian regularization factor exp(-ζ(k·u)²) in the definition of the tensor moments φ_n^{μ₁···μₛ} of the Wigner distribution. The exact evolution equations for these regularized moments are derived from the quantum kinetic equation for the Wigner function, and the regularization parameter ζ is integrated out to recover well-defined equations for the stress-energy tensor and its higher-order residual moments. The convergence of the expansion is tested numerically in a system with Bjorken symmetry, using a relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel and an initial Wigner distribution that is far off-shell and far from equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_in = 600 MeV, proper time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c, and shear viscosity parameter 4π\bar{η} = 3, chosen to mimic heavy-ion collision conditions
- Knudsen number τ_R(τ₀)θ(τ₀) ≈ 1.6, indicating large gradients where a gradient expansion would be expected to fail
- Initial relative error in the first residual moment for longitudinal pressure ΔR_L^(1)/R_L^(1) = 40%, and for transverse pressure ΔR_T^(1)/R_T^(1) = 275%
- Despite large residual moment errors, the error in the time derivative of the longitudinal pressure is Δ\dot{P}_L/\dot{P}_L ≈ 22% and of the transverse pressure Δ\dot{P}_T/\dot{P}_T ≈ 15%
- The trace anomaly time derivative shows a much larger initial error of ≈ 121%

## Context

This work extends the method of moments approach developed by Denicol (2012) and collaborators for classical relativistic kinetic theory to the quantum domain using the Wigner distribution, which is the quantum precursor of the classical distribution function. The regularization scheme was introduced earlier by Tinti (2018) to handle similar infrared divergences in the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation. The numerical test generalizes the exactly solvable Bjorken-flow system of Florkowski (2013) to the quantum case, providing a controlled setting to verify convergence. This enables a systematic, first-principles derivation of relativistic hydrodynamics from quantum field theory without relying on the classical Boltzmann equation, which is expected to be valid only for weakly interacting asymptotic particle states and may not apply near the quark-gluon plasma cross-over region.
