# Enhanced subbarrier fusion for proton halo nuclei

> In this short note we use a simple model to describe the dynamical effects of break-up processes in the subbarrier fusion involving weakly bound nuclei. We model two similar cases involving either a neutron or a proton halo nucleus, both schematically coupled to the break-up channels. We find that the decrease of the coulomb barrier in the proton break-up channel leads, ceteris paribus, to a larger enhancement of the subbarier fusion probabilities with respect to the neutron-halo case.

# Enhanced subbarrier fusion for proton halo nuclei

## Key claim

Using a simplified two-channel model that couples the entrance channel to a single representative break-up channel, the authors find that the reduction of the Coulomb barrier in the proton break-up channel leads to a larger enhancement of subbarrier fusion probabilities for proton-halo nuclei compared to neutron-halo nuclei, *ceteris paribus*. The enhancement is conditional on the effective Q-value being positive for the proton case and negative for the neutron case at energies around or below the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors construct a two-channel coupled-channels model for the fusion of $^{8}$B (proton halo) and $^{11}$Be (neutron halo) with a $^{58}$Ni target. The first channel is the entrance channel; the second channel represents the full set of continuum break-up channels, where the ejected nucleon is neglected and the ion-ion potential is rescaled accordingly. The nuclear potentials are taken from the Broglia and Winther proximity parameterization, the coupling potential is a derivative Woods-Saxon form with 10% of the proximity potential strength, and the coupled equations are solved under incoming-wave boundary conditions (IWBC).

## Key numbers

- One-proton separation energy for $^{8}$B: $S_{1p} = 0.136$ MeV
- One-neutron separation energy for $^{11}$Be: $S_{1n} = 0.504$ MeV
- Average excitation energy $\langle E^* \rangle$ for $^{8}$B: 0.5 MeV; for $^{11}$Be: 0.4 MeV
- Incoming energy used for $^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni: 20 MeV; for $^{11}$Be+$^{58}$Ni: 17 MeV
- Effective Q-value for the proton break-up case ($^{8}$B): $Q_{eff} = +1.97$ MeV
- Effective Q-value for the neutron break-up case ($^{11}$Be): $Q_{eff} = -1.12$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of subbarrier fusion dynamics for weakly bound nuclei, building on the coupled-channels framework of Bal98, Das83b, and Hag12. The results are consistent with the time-dependent approach of Nakatsukasa et al. (2004) and help explain the enhancement observed experimentally for the proton halo nucleus $^{8}$B by Aguilera et al. (2011). The model shows that the proton break-up case behaves analogously to fusion with positive Q-values, while the neutron break-up case resembles negative Q-value systems, as discussed in Das97 and Das83b.
