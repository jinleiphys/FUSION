# Effect of three-body forces on response functions in infinite neutron matter

> We study the impact of three-body forces on the response functions of cold neutron matter. These response functions are determined in the random phase approximation (RPA) from a residual interaction expressed in terms of Landau parameters. Special attention is paid to the non-central part, including all terms allowed by the relevant symmetries. Using Landau parameters derived from realistic nuclear two- and three-body forces grounded in chiral effective field theory, we find that the three-body term has a strong impact on the excited states of the system and in the static and long-wavelength limit of the response functions for which a new exact formula is established.

# Effect of three-body forces on response functions in infinite neutron matter

## Key claim

Three-body forces have a strong impact on the excited states and static response functions of cold neutron matter, but the effect of the center-of-mass tensor and cross-vector terms in the quasiparticle interaction is negligible in the static limit due to an approximate cancellation between the exchange and center-of-mass tensor contributions, even though these noncentral terms are of the same magnitude as the standard exchange tensor term individually.

## Method

The authors derived Landau parameters from chiral effective field theory two- and three-body forces at momentum-space cutoffs of 414, 450, and 500 MeV, including all noncentral terms (exchange tensor, center-of-mass tensor, and cross-vector interactions) allowed by symmetries. They then computed the response functions of infinite neutron matter in the random phase approximation (RPA) by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equations analytically, using the quasiparticle interaction expanded in Legendre polynomials up to ℓ_max=3.

## Key numbers

- Landau parameters were calculated at a density corresponding to k_F=1.68 fm⁻¹ for three cutoff values: Λ=414, 450, and 500 MeV; for Λ=450 MeV, the central parameters include F₀=0.223, G₀=0.877, and noncentral parameters include H̃₀=0.177, K̃₀=-0.107, L̃₀=-0.155.
- The static spin susceptibility in the S=1 channel is given by χ_HF(0)/χ_RPA^(S=1)(0)=1+G₀+T₁/T₂, where T₁ and T₂ involve combinations of H̃_ℓ, K̃_ℓ, and L̃_ℓ parameters up to ℓ=3.
- Without three-body forces, the S=0 static susceptibility (compressibility) at ρ=0.24 fm⁻³ is approximately four times larger than the complete case including three-body forces.
- The S=1 channel static susceptibility differs by about 20% at ρ=0.24 fm⁻³ when three-body forces are removed.
- Response functions were computed at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and q/k_F=0.5 for k_F=1.68 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the RPA response function formalism of Pas13d to include center-of-mass tensor and cross-vector interactions (first identified by Sch04 and later applied by Ols04 and Hol13). It uses chiral effective field theory interactions (Cor13, Cor14) that have been shown to produce realistic equations of state for neutron and nuclear matter. The results enable more accurate calculations of neutrino and anti-neutrino propagation in neutron star matter (Sawyer75, Iwamoto82, Reddy98, Shen13, Pas12a), and demonstrate that three-body forces are essential for avoiding instabilities in the response functions that appear when only two-body forces are used.
