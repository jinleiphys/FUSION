# $\cos2ϕ_t$ azimuthal asymmetry in back-to-back $J/ψ$-jet production in $e~p\rightarrow e~J/ψ~Jet~ X$ at the EIC

> In this article, we investigate the $\cos2ϕ_t$ azimuthal asymmetry in $e ~p\rightarrow e ~J/ψ~Jet~ X$, where the $J/ψ$-jet pair is almost back-to-back in the transverse plane, within the framework of the generalized parton model(GPM). We use non-relativistic QCD(NRQCD) to calculate the $J/ψ$ production amplitude and incorporate both color singlet(CS) and color octet(CO) contributions to the asymmetry. We estimate the asymmetry using different parameterizations of the gluon TMDs in the kinematics that can be accessed at the future electron-ion collider (EIC) and also investigate the impact of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) evolution on the asymmetry. We present the contributions coming from different states to the asymmetry in NRQCD.

## Key claim

The $\cos2\phi_t$ azimuthal asymmetry in back-to-back $J/\psi$-jet production at the EIC can probe the linearly polarized gluon TMD, but only the Weizsacker-Williams (WW) type gluon TMDs contribute to this process, and the asymmetry depends on the specific parameterization of the gluon TMDs and on TMD evolution effects.

## Method

The authors calculated the $\cos2\phi_t$ asymmetry in $e p \rightarrow e J/\psi \text{ Jet } X$ using the generalized parton model (GPM) with TMD factorization, assuming the $J/\psi$-jet pair is almost back-to-back in the transverse plane. They used non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) to compute the $J/\psi$ production amplitude, including both color singlet ($^3S_1^{(1)}$) and color octet ($^3S_1^{(8)}$, $^1S_0^{(8)}$, $^3P_J^{(8)}$) contributions, and employed two different sets of long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) from Sharma and Chao. The asymmetry was estimated using different parameterizations of the gluon TMDs, and the impact of TMD evolution on the asymmetry was investigated.

## Key numbers

- The LDME set from Ref. Sharma gives $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{1}S_0)|0\rangle = 1.8 \pm 0.87 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{3}S_1)|0\rangle = 0.13 \pm 0.13 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_1^{J/\psi}(^{3}S_1)|0\rangle = 1.2 \times 10^2 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, and $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{3}P_0)|0\rangle/m_c^2 = 1.8 \pm 0.87 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$.
- The LDME set from Ref. Chao gives $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{1}S_0)|0\rangle = 8.9 \pm 0.98 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{3}S_1)|0\rangle = 0.30 \pm 0.12 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_1^{J/\psi}(^{3}S_1)|0\rangle = 1.2 \times 10^2 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, and $\langle0|\mathcal{O}_8^{J/\psi}(^{3}P_0)|0\rangle/m_c^2 = 0.56 \pm 0.21 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$.
- The charmonium velocity parameter is $v \approx 0.3$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\cos2\phi_t$ asymmetry in $J/\psi$ production in unpolarized $ep$ collisions (Mukherjee 2017) and in back-to-back $J/\psi$-jet production (DAlesio 2019), where the upper bound of the asymmetry was investigated. The present calculation uses recent parameterizations of gluon TMDs and includes TMD evolution effects, which were not addressed in the earlier bound study. The results are relevant for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) at Brookhaven National Lab, where the process can probe WW-type gluon TMDs over a wide range of scales by varying the invariant mass of the $J/\psi$-jet pair.
