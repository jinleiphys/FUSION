# Medium Effects of Low Energy Pions

> Fits of pion-nucleus potentials to large sets of pionic atom data reveal departures of parameter values from the corresponding free $πN$ parameters. These medium effects can be quantitatively reproduced by a chiral-motivated model where the pion decay constant is modified in the medium or by including the empirical on-shell energy dependence of the amplitudes. No consistency is obtained between pionic atoms and the free $πN$ interaction when an extreme off-shell chiral model is used. The role of the size of data sets is briefly discussed.

# Medium Effects of Low Energy Pions

## Key claim

Global fits to pionic atom data show that the in-medium isovector s-wave amplitude b1 is more repulsive than the free πN value by three standard deviations, but this medium effect can be quantitatively reproduced either by a chiral-motivated model where the pion decay constant is modified in the medium or by including the empirical on-shell energy dependence of the amplitudes; however, no consistency is obtained when an extreme off-shell chiral model is used.

## Method

The author performed fits of the standard Ericson-Ericson optical potential to a global set of 100 pionic atom data points from 20Ne to 238U, including deeply bound 1s states in 205Pb and 115,119,123Sn. Neutron density distributions were parameterized using the rms radius difference rn−rp = α(N−Z)/A + η, with α varied between 1.0 and 1.5 fm, and the isovector s-wave amplitude b1 was treated as a free parameter. The fits were then repeated with b1 replaced by a density-dependent form derived from a medium-modified pion decay constant, and separately with energy-dependent amplitudes implementing minimal substitution E → E − Vc using both chiral off-shell and empirical on-shell πN amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The free πN isovector s-wave amplitude b1 at threshold is −0.0885 +0.0010/−0.0021 mπ−1 from pionic hydrogen measurements (SBG 2001).
- The conventional potential fit with α=1.5 fm yields χ² per point of 1.7 for 100 data points, but b1 is more repulsive than the free value by at least two error bars (three error bars for α=1.25 fm).
- In the medium-modified pion decay constant model, b1(ρ) = b1(0)/(1 − 2.3ρ) for σ=50 MeV, with ρ in fm−3.
- For the chiral off-shell energy dependence, the fit to pionic atom data deteriorates significantly, with the χ² minimum occurring at unacceptably large α values.
- When ReB0 is constrained to zero, the χ² increases by 10 units per degree of freedom for the large data sets, and b0 deviates sharply from the free πN value for all data sets.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the "anomalous" s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms first noted by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997), where the repulsive s-wave potential inside nuclei is typically twice as strong as expected from the free πN interaction. The paper tests the model of Weise (2001) where partial restoration of chiral symmetry modifies the pion decay constant in dense matter, and the alternative approach of Ericson and Tauscher (1982) using empirical on-shell energy dependence. It contradicts the off-shell chiral expansion approach of Kolomeitsev, Kaiser, and Weise (2003) which fails to reproduce pionic atom data. The results enable future analyses to incorporate medium-modified amplitudes or on-shell energy dependence when fitting pionic atom data, and demonstrate that constraining ReB0 to zero as done in some recent analyses of deeply bound states leads to unreliable results.
