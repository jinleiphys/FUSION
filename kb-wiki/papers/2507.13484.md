# Quark + Diquark Description of Nucleon Elastic Electromagnetic Form Factors

> Working with a Poincaré-covariant quark + diquark, $q(qq)$, Faddeev equation approach to nucleon structure, a refined symmetry preserving current for electron + nucleon elastic scattering is developed. The parameters in the interaction current are chosen to ensure that the $q(qq)$ picture reproduces selected results from contemporary $3$-body analyses of nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors. Although the subset of fitted results is small, the $q(qq)$ picture reproduces almost all the $3$-body predictions and often results in better agreement with available data. Notably, the $q(qq)$ framework predicts a zero in $G_E^p/G_M^p$, the absence of such a zero in $G_E^n/G_M^n$, and a zero in the proton's $d$-quark Dirac form factor. Derived $q(qq)$ results for proton flavour-separated light-front-transverse number and anomalous magnetisation densities are also discussed. With the $q(qq)$ framework thus newly benchmarked, one may proceed to comparisons with a broader array of $3$-body results. This may enable new steps to be made toward answering an important question, viz. is the quark + fully-interacting diquark picture of baryon structure only a useful phenomenology or does it come close to expressing robust features of baryon structure?

## Key claim

The quark + diquark Faddeev equation approach, when equipped with a refined symmetry-preserving electromagnetic current whose parameters are fitted to a small subset of three-body results (the proton electric form factor on $x\in(0,4.5)$ and the ratio $\mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ on $x\in(2,4.5)$), reproduces almost all three-body predictions for nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors and often yields better agreement with available data than the three-body approach itself.

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré-covariant quark + diquark Faddeev equation for the nucleon bound-state amplitude, using a dressed-quark propagator parametrised to exhibit emergent hadron mass and including both isoscalar-scalar ($0^+$) and isovector-axialvector ($1^+$) diquark correlations. They construct a six-diagram electromagnetic current for electron-nucleon elastic scattering, employing a new unamputated photon-quark vertex Ansatz with six parameters and photon-diquark vertices with two diquark radius parameters, and determine these eight parameters via a least-squares fit to selected three-body results from Yao et al. (2024) for the proton.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark current mass: $m_q = 5.08$ MeV, yielding Euclidean constituent-quark mass $M_q^E = 0.33$ GeV (ratio $M_q^E/m_q = 65$)
- Scalar diquark mass: $m_{[ud]_{0^+}} = 0.79$ GeV; axialvector diquark mass: $m_{\{uu\}_{1^+}} = m_{\{ud\}_{1^+}} = m_{\{dd\}_{1^+}} = 0.89$ GeV
- Scalar diquark coupling: $g_{0^+} = 14.94$; axialvector diquark coupling: $g_{1^+} = 12.98$ (ratio $g^2_{1^+}/g^2_{0^+} \simeq 0.75$)
- Faddeev equation yields nucleon dressed-quark core mass: $m_N = 1.18$ GeV
- The fit to three-body results uses data on $x=Q^2/m_N^2 \in (0,4.5)$ for $G_E^p$ and $x\in(2,4.5)$ for $\mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$

## Context

This work extends the quark + diquark Faddeev equation approach previously used by Chen et al. (2018), Lu et al. (2019), Cui et al. (2020, 2021), and Chen et al. (2021, 2023) by updating the electromagnetic current to reproduce the direct three-body (three valence-quark) results of Yao et al. (2024) for nucleon form factors. The three-body approach, first solved ab initio by Eichmann (2009), remains computationally challenging and has not been widely applied to nucleon-to-resonance transitions; the newly benchmarked quark + diquark framework can now be used to extend studies of resonance electroproduction and to identify empirical signals for diquark correlations within nucleons by comparing predictions of the two approaches.
