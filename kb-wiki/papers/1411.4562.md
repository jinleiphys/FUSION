# Improving proton-induced one-nucleon removal in intranuclear cascade

> It is a well-established fact that intranuclear-cascade models generally fail to consistently reproduce the cross sections for one-proton and one-neutron removal from stable nuclei by a high-energy proton beam. We use simple shell-model calculations to investigate the reasons of this deficiency. We find that a refined description of the neutron skin and of the energy density in the nuclear surface is crucial for the aforementioned observables, and that neither ingredient is sufficient if taken separately. As a by-product, the predictions for removal of several nucleons are also improved by the refined treatment.

## Key claim

Intranuclear-cascade models systematically overestimate one-proton removal cross sections from heavy stable nuclei by factors up to 3-4, while giving reasonable predictions for one-neutron removal; the authors show that simultaneously refining both the neutron skin description and the energy density in the nuclear surface is necessary to improve these predictions, and neither ingredient alone is sufficient.

## Method

The authors performed simple shell-model calculations for the target nucleus to obtain realistic radial density profiles and energy-dependent occupation probabilities for protons and neutrons, then incorporated these into the Liège Intranuclear Cascade model (INCL++) coupled with the ABLA07 de-excitation model. They decoupled the neutron and proton spatial density parameters in INCL to match shell-model Woods-Saxon fits, and introduced a "fuzziness" parameter to allow deeply-bound nucleons to appear in the nuclear surface region, thereby increasing the excitation energy deposited in single-collision knockout events.

## Key numbers

- One-proton removal cross sections from heavy nuclei are overestimated by a factor of 3-4 in standard INCL/ABLA calculations
- The neutron skin thickness in lead from shell-model calculations is 0.26 fm (radius difference) with diffuseness difference 0.09 fm, compared to the experimentally accepted value of about 0.2 fm
- For calcium-40, the shell model yields a thin proton skin of -0.08 fm (radius difference) with diffuseness difference -0.02 fm
- INCL/GEMINI++ calculations give one-nucleon removal cross sections within 20% of INCL/ABLA values, confirming de-excitation model dependence is mild
- The Fermi momentum used in INCL is p_F = 270 MeV/c
- The effective neutron-evaporation threshold S*_n is slightly larger than the neutron separation energy S_n

## Context

This work extends the INCL model family (Boudard 2006, Mancusi 2014) by addressing a known deficiency in describing peripheral knockout reactions. The failure of semi-classical INC models for one-nucleon removal had been noted previously (Jacob 1998, Audirac 2004), and similar observations about "cold fragmentation" in peripheral nucleus-nucleus reactions were made by Benlliure (2001). The refined model enables improved predictions for spallation residue production, particularly for proton-rich channels, and the approach could be generalized to other dynamical models such as BUU or QMD approaches.
