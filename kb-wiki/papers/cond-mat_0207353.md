# Relativistic Nonextensive Thermodynamics

> Starting from the basic prescriptions of the Tsallis' nonextensive thermostatistics, i.e. generalized entropy and normalized q-expectation values, we study the relativistic nonextensive thermodynamics and derive a Boltzmann transport equation that implies the validity of the H-theorem where a local nonextensive four-entropy density is considered. Macroscopic thermodynamic functions and the equation of state for a perfect gas are derived at the equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent relativistic nonextensive thermodynamics can be formulated using Tsallis' thermostatistics with normalized q-expectation values, but the macroscopic equation of state for a perfect gas (p = n k_B T) and the energy per particle in the non-relativistic limit show no explicit dependence on the deformation parameter q, meaning nonextensive effects are not directly detectable in these macroscopic thermodynamic variables.

## Method

The author starts from Tsallis' generalized entropy and normalized q-mean expectation values to define relativistic macroscopic variables: the particle four-flow N^μ and the energy-momentum tensor T^{μν}. A relativistic Boltzmann equation is derived with a q-deformed streaming term p^μ ∂_μ f^q and a q-deformed collision term C_q, incorporating a generalized molecular chaos hypothesis h_q[f,f_1] = e_q(ln_q f + ln_q f_1). The equilibrium distribution is obtained by requiring vanishing entropy production, yielding a Tsallis-like distribution f_eq(p) = [1 - (1-q) p^μ U_μ / (k_B T)]^{1/(1-q)} / Z_q, from which thermodynamic functions and the equation of state are derived using q-modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The particle density is expressed as n = (4π / Z_q) m^2 k_B T K_2(q,z), where z = m/k_B T and K_2(q,z) is a q-modified Bessel function of the second kind.
- The equilibrium pressure is p = (4π / Z_q) m^2 (k_B T)^2 K_2(q,z), leading to the equation of state p = n k_B T.
- The energy density is ε = (4π / Z_q) m^4 [3 K_2(q,z)/z^2 + K_1(q,z)/z].
- The energy per particle is e = 3 k_B T + m K_1(q,z) / K_2(q,z), which in the non-relativistic limit (p ≪ 1) reduces to e ≃ m + (3/2) k_B T.

## Context

This work extends Tsallis' nonextensive thermostatistics (Tsallis, 1988) into the relativistic regime, building on prior formulations of normalized q-mean expectation values (Mendes, 1998; Plastino and Plastino, 1995). It differs from the non-relativistic formulation of Lima (2001) by requiring that both the streaming term and the collision term in the Boltzmann equation depend on the deformation parameter q to ensure conservation laws. The results enable future studies of nonextensive effects in high-energy physics, particularly in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where effects may appear in microscopic observables such as particle distributions and correlation functions rather than in macroscopic thermodynamic variables.
