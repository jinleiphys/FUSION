# Two-gluon one-photon vertex in a magnetic field and its explicit one-loop approximation in the intermediate field strength regime

> We find the general structure for the two-gluon one-photon vertex in the presence of a constant magnetic field. We show that, when accounting for the symmetries satisfied by the strong and electromagnetic interactions under parity, charge conjugation and gluon interchange, and for gluons and photons on mass-shell, there exist only three possible tensor structures that span the vertex. These correspond to external products of the polarization vectors for each of the particles in the vertex. We also explicitly compute the one-loop approximation to this vertex in the intermediate field strength regime, which is the most appropriate one to describe possible effects of the presence of a magnetic field to enhance photon emission during pre-equilibrium in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that the most favored direction for the photon to propagate is in the plane transverse to the field, which is consistent with a positive contribution to $ν_2$ and may help to understand the larger than expected elliptic flow coefficient measured in this kind of reactions.

## Key claim
The authors derive the general tensor structure for the two-gluon one-photon vertex in a constant magnetic field and show that, for on-shell gluons and photons, only three independent tensor structures survive. They compute the one-loop approximation in the intermediate field strength regime, where the photon energy is larger than the magnetic field strength, and find that photon propagation is most favored in the plane transverse to the field, which may help explain the unexpectedly large elliptic flow coefficient measured in peripheral heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors construct the vertex from external products of polarization vectors for each gauge boson, using the Ritus basis that accounts for the magnetic field direction. They impose constraints from gauge invariance (transversality), gluon exchange symmetry, and parity/charge conjugation invariance to reduce the 27 possible tensor structures to 18 for off-shell bosons, and further to 3 for on-shell bosons. They then compute the one-loop approximation by evaluating fermion triangle diagrams in configuration space using Schwinger's proper time representation for fermion propagators in a constant magnetic field, performing Gaussian integrations over internal momenta in the intermediate field strength regime.

## Key numbers
- The peak magnetic field intensity inferred at RHIC energies is of order \(B\sim 10^{19}\) G (from STAR collaboration measurements).
- The vertex reduces to exactly three independent tensor structures for on-shell gluons and photons, corresponding to the coefficients \(a_1^{++}\), \(a_2^{++}\), and \(a_{10}^{++}\).
- The coefficients show a \(\sin^2\theta\) dependence (where \(\theta\) is the angle between photon propagation and magnetic field direction), meaning the most favored photon propagation direction is at \(\theta = \pi/2\) (transverse to the field).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of gluon fusion and splitting channels for photon production during pre-equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions (Ayala 2017, Ayala 2019, Ayala 2022), which either limited accuracy to low transverse momentum or did not account for the full tensor structure with symmetry properties. The vertex derived here is an essential ingredient for computing matrix elements of gluon fusion/splitting processes, which are enhanced relative to quark processes at pre-equilibrium because gluon occupation numbers exceed quark occupation numbers by a factor of \(\alpha_s^2\) (Baier 2000). The results may help resolve the "photon puzzle", the unexpectedly large elliptic flow coefficient \(\nu_2\) of direct photons measured by PHENIX (2011, 2015, 2018) and ALICE (2018), by providing a mechanism for anisotropic photon emission from magnetic field effects without requiring late-stage flow.
