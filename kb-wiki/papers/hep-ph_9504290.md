# Phenomenology of $x_F$ Dependence of Quarkonium Production in Proton-Nucleus Interactions

> We present a phenomenological study of the $x_F$ dependence of quarkonium production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. The \xf~dependence of comover contributions is introduced to account for the observed quarkonium suppression at low $x_F$. Combining comover contributions, nuclear shadowing effect, energy loss mechanism and nuclear absorption together we reproduce the overall \xf~dependence of E772/E789 data.

# Phenomenology of $x_F$ Dependence of Quarkonium Production in Proton-Nucleus Interactions

## Key claim
The paper claims that by introducing an $x_F$-dependent comover contribution, combined with nuclear shadowing, energy loss, and nuclear absorption, the overall $x_F$ dependence of E772/E789 data can be reproduced; however, the authors note that a fairly high comover density (maximum about 6 fm$^{-3}$) is needed, which may imply some new mechanism is required at low $x_F$.

## Method
The authors model quarkonium ($J/\psi$, $\Upsilon$) production in proton-nucleus collisions at 800 GeV/c by combining four mechanisms: comover interactions with an $x_F$-dependent density extrapolated from $p-p$ data using the independent cluster model, nuclear absorption with a resonance-nucleon cross section scaled by radius, nuclear shadowing following Qiu (1987) and Vogt (1991), and energy loss following Gavin (1992) with normalization after Khakzad (1993). The comover density is related to charged particle rapidity distributions fitted to data at $\sqrt{s}=45.2$ GeV, and the $x_F$ dependence enters by assuming only comovers with rapidity close to the quarkonium rapidity cause breakup.

## Key numbers
- Best fit parameters for $J/\psi$ production: comover absorption cross section $\sigma_{co} = 4.0$ mb and comover formation time $\tau_0 = 0.8$ fm/c, corresponding to a maximum comover density of about 6 fm$^{-3}$.
- Energy loss value used to fit large $x_F$ data: $dE/dZ \simeq 0.5$ GeV/fm, compared to an upper bound of $dE/dZ \simeq 1.5$ GeV/fm from Gavin (1992).
- Nuclear absorption cross section for $\psi N$: $\sigma_{\psi N} = 6$ mb, with formation times $\tau_{\psi}=0.89$ fm and $\tau_{\Upsilon}=0.76$ fm.
- Charged particle rapidity distribution fit parameters at $\sqrt{s}=45.2$ GeV: $W=1.75$, $\eta_0=1.5$, $\delta=2.9$.
- Comover formation time range considered: $0.4-1.2$ fm/c.
- Effective proper time $\tau_f = r_0/c_s$ with $r_0 = 1.2$ fm and $c_s \sim 1/\sqrt{3}$.

## Context
This work extends phenomenological studies of quarkonium suppression in proton-nucleus collisions, addressing the inability of nuclear absorption alone (Gavin 1994) to explain the data. It improves upon earlier comover models (Vogt 1991, Gavin 1990) by introducing an $x_F$-dependent comover distribution, and combines this with energy loss (Gavin 1992) using a smaller energy loss value consistent with other considerations (Brodsky 1993). The paper notes that nuclear antishadowing (Liu 1994) would lead to enhanced $J/\psi$ production at low $x_F$, contradicting the E772/E789 data, and that absorption in confined hadronic matter is excluded for nucleus-nucleus collisions (Khakzad 1994), suggesting the need for further study of low $x_F$ suppression mechanisms.
