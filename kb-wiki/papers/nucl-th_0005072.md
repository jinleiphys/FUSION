# Quantum Hadrodynamics: Evolution and Revolution

> The underlying philosophy and motivation for quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), namely, relativistic field theories of nuclear phenomena featuring manifest covariance, have evolved over the last quarter century in response to successes, failures, and sharp criticisms. A recent revolution in QHD, based on modern effective field theory and density functional theory perspectives, explains the successes, provides antidotes to the failures, rebuts the criticisms, and focuses the arguments in favor of a covariant representation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the original motivation for renormalizable quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) lagrangians has been superseded by a modern effective field theory (EFT) and density functional theory (DFT) perspective, which explains the successes of mean-field calculations, provides antidotes to earlier failures involving quantum vacuum contributions, and rebuts criticisms of the covariant approach, provided one accepts that the scalar field in successful QHD models is not the chiral partner of the pion but an independent chiral-singlet degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors trace the historical evolution of QHD from renormalizable field theories (exemplified by the Walecka model) through the difficulties encountered when computing quantum loops and attempting to incorporate linear chiral symmetry via the Sigma model. They then present the modern reinterpretation: QHD lagrangians are treated as nonrenormalizable effective field theories with a breakdown scale of roughly 600 MeV, organized by naive dimensional analysis (NDA) and naturalness, and the mean-field approximation is justified as a Kohn-Sham realization of covariant density functional theory.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale Lambda for the effective field theory is roughly 600 MeV.
- The pion decay constant f_pi is approximately 94 MeV, used in NDA power counting.
- The scalar and vector mean fields in nuclear matter at equilibrium density are roughly several hundred MeV.
- The singlet scattering length in the 1S0 NN channel is roughly (8 MeV)^{-1}, signaling an almost-bound state near zero energy.
- The 1S0 NN phase shift becomes repulsive at about 250 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work initiated by Walecka (1974) on renormalizable QHD and responds to criticisms raised by Brodsky (1984), Negele (1985), and Brown (1987) in earlier issues of Comments on Modern Physics. It builds on the effective field theory framework of Weinberg (1967, 1990) and the naive dimensional analysis of Georgi and Manohar (1984, 1993), and it connects to the density functional theory perspective articulated by Kohn and Sham (1965). The revolution described enables a systematic, covariant treatment of nuclear many-body problems that is consistent with chiral symmetry and avoids the pathologies of renormalizable loop calculations.
