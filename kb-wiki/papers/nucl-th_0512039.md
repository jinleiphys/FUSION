# Two-component model for the deuteron electromagnetic structure

> We suggest a simple phenomenological parametrization for all three deuteron electromagnetic form factors, and show that a good fit on the available data, with a minimal number of parameters, can be obtained. The present description of the deuteron electromagnetic structure is based on two components with different radii, one corresponding to two nucleons separated by $\simeq $2 fm, and a standard isoscalar contribution, saturated by $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons, only.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a simple phenomenological parametrization for all three deuteron electromagnetic form factors (charge, magnetic, and quadrupole) using a two-component model with a minimal number of parameters, achieving a good fit to available data for momentum transfers up to Q² ≤ 2 GeV², but the authors note that the parametrization cannot be reliably extended to higher Q² and that the region above 2 GeV² may represent a transition from hadronic to quark degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors generalize a two-component parametrization previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Iachello 1973, updated by Bijker 2004) to the deuteron case. The model combines a core component described by a function g_i(Q²) = 1/(1+γ_i Q²)^δ_i, representing two nucleons separated by approximately 2 fm, with a meson-cloud component F_i(Q²) saturated by ω and φ meson contributions. The three deuteron form factors G_c, G_q, and G_m are fitted to experimental data extracted from elastic electron-deuteron scattering (structure functions A and B and tensor polarization t_20) using a χ² minimization that accounts for asymmetric experimental errors.

## Key numbers
- The charge form factor G_c has a zero at Q²_0c ≈ 0.7 GeV²; the magnetic form factor G_m has a zero at Q²_0m ≈ 2 GeV².
- For the global fit with common parameters for all three form factors: δ = 1.04 ± 0.03, γ = 12.1 ± 0.5 GeV⁻² (first solution, χ²/ndf = 1.1); δ = 1.05 ± 0.03, γ = 12.1 ± 0.5 GeV⁻² (second solution, χ²/ndf = 1.5).
- The two-nucleon component radius derived from the fit is R_2N = 1.7 fm, corresponding to m₀² ≈ 0.08 GeV².
- Normalization values at Q² = 0: G_c(0) = 1, G_q(0) = 25.83 (dimensionless), G_m(0) = 1.714 (dimensionless).

## Context
This work extends the two-component model of Iachello (1973) and Bijker (2004) from nucleon electromagnetic form factors to the deuteron, providing a simpler alternative to previous deuteron form factor parametrizations that required 12 to 33 parameters (such as those in the world-data compilation by the authors themselves). The parametrization is intended for use in calculations of deuteron electromagnetic processes (elastic scattering, electrodisintegration, pion production) and can be extended to the time-like region, which the authors indicate as a forthcoming work.
