# Conventional and Unconventional Pairing and Condensates in Dilute Nuclear Matter

> This contribution will survey recent progress toward an understanding of diverse pairing phenomena in dilute nuclear matter at small and moderate isospin asymmetry, with results of potential relevance to supernova envelopes and proto-neutron stars. Application of {\it ab initio} many-body techniques has revealed a rich array of temperature-density phase diagrams, indexed by isospin asymmetry, which feature both conventional and unconventional superfluid phases. At low density there exist a homogeneous translationally invariant BCS phase, a homogeneous LOFF phase violating translational invariance, and an inhomogeneous translationally invariant phase-separated BCS phase. The transition from the BCS to the BEC phases is characterized in terms of the evolution, from weak to strong coupling, of the pairing gap, condensate wave function, and quasiparticle occupation numbers and spectra. Additionally, a schematic formal analysis of pairing in neutron matter at low to moderate densities is presented that establishes conditions for the emergence of both conventional and unconventional pairing solutions and encompasses the possibility of dineutron formation.

## Key claim

This survey paper argues that dilute nuclear matter at small to moderate isospin asymmetry exhibits a rich phase diagram featuring both conventional BCS pairing and unconventional phases (LOFF and phase-separated BCS/BEC), with the caveat that polarization effects and clustering beyond deuterons are neglected in the primary numerical calculations, and the results are therefore schematic rather than fully predictive for astrophysical environments.

## Method

The authors solve coupled BCS gap equations and density equations self-consistently in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) partial wave using the Paris potential as the bare pairing interaction, with the nuclear mean field modeled by a Skyrme density functional (SkIII and SLy4 parametrizations). The gap equation is generalized to allow Cooper pairs with nonzero center-of-mass momentum for the LOFF phase, and free-energy minimization determines the ground state among BCS, LOFF, phase-separated BCS, and normal phases across temperature-density-asymmetry space. A separate schematic analysis based on the Thouless criterion examines the possibility of dineutron formation in neutron matter.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), used as reference for density parameter \(\log(\rho/\rho_0)\)
* Scattering length for the \(^1S_0\) neutron-neutron partial wave: \(a = -18.95\) fm, with a resonance at 0.067 MeV that just fails to support a bound dineutron in vacuum
* A tetracritical point occurs at \(\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.22\) and \(T = 2.85\) MeV for isospin asymmetry \(\alpha = 0.255\)
* The LOFF phase disappears at \(\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.65\) and \(T = 0\) for \(\alpha = 0.62\)
* Representative points for weak, intermediate, and strong coupling regimes: WCR at \(T = 0.5\) MeV, \(\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.5\); ICR at \(T = 0.5\) MeV, \(\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -1.5\); SCR at \(T = 0.2\) MeV, \(\log(\rho/\rho_0) = -2.5\)
* A dineutron would require an effective in-medium mass \(M^*\) of about 5% over the bare mass \(M\) to bind in pure neutron matter

## Context

This work reviews and extends a body of recent findings on dilute nuclear matter phase diagrams, building on earlier studies of BCS-BEC crossovers by Eagles (1969), Schrieffer (1973), Alm (1993), Baldo (1995), Stein (1995), and Lombardo (2001), as well as more recent work by Mao (2009), Heckel (2010), Huang (2010), Jin (2010), Sedrakian (2013), and others. The numerical results use a Malfliet-Tjon interaction model for symmetric matter and the Paris potential for asymmetric matter, with the LOFF phase analysis following the framework of Larkin, Ovchinnikov, Fulde, and Ferrell (1964). The schematic dineutron analysis supplements correlated-basis theory calculations by Krotscheck's group (2014) and addresses the long-standing question of whether dineutron correlations can produce bound states in medium, with potential relevance to supernova envelopes and proto-neutron stars.
