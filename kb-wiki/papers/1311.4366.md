# Deformation effects in toroidal and compression dipole excitations of $^{170}$Yb: Skyrme-RPA analysis

> The effect of nuclear deformation on the isoscalar toroidal and compression dipole modes in prolate $^{170}$Yb is studied in the framework of the random-phase-approximation method with a representative set of Skyrme forces (SV-bas, SLy6, SkM$^*$ and SkI3). It is shown that the deformation crucially redistributes the strength of both modes. The compression mode has the same sequence of $μ$=0 and 1 branches as the isovector giant dipole resonance where for prolate nuclei the $μ=0$ mode is lower in energy ($μ$ being the projection of the axial momentum of the mode). Instead, the toroidal mode exhibits an anomalous (opposite) sequence where the $μ$=1 branch precedes the $μ$=0 one.

## Key claim

Deformation in prolate \(^{170}\)Yb crucially redistributes the strength of both isoscalar toroidal and compression dipole modes, but the toroidal mode exhibits an anomalous sequence where the \(\mu=1\) branch lies lower in energy than the \(\mu=0\) branch, opposite to the standard ordering seen in the isovector giant dipole resonance and the compression mode.

## Method

The study employed the separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) method with the Skyrme energy-density functional, using four different Skyrme forces (SV-bas, SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) that cover a range of isoscalar effective masses from 0.58 to 0.90. Calculations were performed in a 2D cylindrical coordinate representation with a mesh size of 0.7 fm and a box of 21 fm, with pairing treated at the BCS level using delta forces. The configuration space included all two-quasiparticle dipole states up to approximately 175 MeV to exhaust nearly 100% of the relevant energy-weighted sum rules.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter \(\beta_2 = 0.34-0.35\) and quadrupole moment \(Q_2 \approx 8.5\) b were obtained for \(^{170}\)Yb across all four Skyrme forces, compared to experimental values \(\beta_2^{\mathrm{exp}} = 0.32\) and \(Q_2^{\mathrm{exp}} = 7.6\) b.
- The compression mode main peak shifts from 26-30 MeV in the spherical case to 22-26 MeV with deformation, with deformation splitting varying from approximately 7 MeV for SV-bas to approximately 2 MeV for SkI3.
- The toroidal mode strength is mainly concentrated at 5-20 MeV, with deformation shifting strength to below 7-8 MeV.
- The spurious center-of-mass peak comes down to 1.5-3.0 MeV depending on the Skyrme force for the compression mode and close to zero for the toroidal mode.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of deformation effects in compression and toroidal modes for Sm isotopes (Kvasil et al., 2013) to the rare-earth axial nucleus \(^{170}\)Yb. The anomalous ordering of \(\mu=0\) and \(\mu=1\) branches in the toroidal mode, where \(\mu=1\) lies lower than \(\mu=0\) in prolate nuclei, contrasts with the standard sequence observed in the isovector giant dipole resonance and the compression mode. The results enable further microscopic analysis of the different flow characters (vortical for toroidal, irrotational for compression and giant dipole resonance) and their impact on the low-energy pygmy dipole resonance region.
