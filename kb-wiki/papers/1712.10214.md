# Neutrino emission from Cooper pairs at finite temperatures

> A brief review is given of the current state of the problem of neutrino pair emission through neutral weak currents caused by the Cooper pairs breaking and formation (PBF) in superfluid baryon matter at thermal equilibrium. The cases of singlet-state pairing with isotropic superfluid gap and spin-triplet pairing with anisotropic gap are analyzed with allowance for the anomalous weak interactions caused by superfluidity. It is shown that taking into account the anomalous weak interactions in both the vector and axial channels is very important for a correct description of neutrino energy losses through the PBF processes. The anomalous contributions lead to an almost complete suppression of the PBF neutrino emission in spin-singlet superfluids and strong reduction of the PBF neutrino losses in the spin-triplet superfluid neutron matter, which considerably slows down the cooling rate of neutron stars with superfluid cores.

## Key claim

Taking into account the anomalous weak interactions (off-diagonal vertices in the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism) in both the vector and axial channels is essential for a correct description of neutrino energy losses from Cooper pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes; these anomalous contributions lead to an almost complete suppression of PBF neutrino emission in spin-singlet superfluids and a strong reduction in spin-triplet superfluid neutron matter, which considerably slows down the cooling rate of neutron stars with superfluid cores.

## Method

The author reviews the derivation of neutrino emissivity from PBF processes in superfluid baryon matter using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, where the effective vertex of nucleon interactions with an external neutrino field is a 2x2 matrix in particle-hole space with off-diagonal entries representing anomalous weak interactions. The weak polarization tensor of the medium is computed in the long-wave limit (k → 0) for both the vector and axial channels, separately analyzing singlet-state pairing (¹S₀) with an isotropic gap and spin-triplet pairing (³P₂) with an anisotropic gap, using Dyson equations for the anomalous vertices and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to obtain the total energy loss rate.

## Key numbers

- For spin-singlet (¹S₀) pairing, the PBF neutrino emission through the vector channel is suppressed proportionally to v_F⁴ (where v_F is the Fermi velocity, small in non-relativistic systems), and the axial channel contribution is proportional to v_F² due to small relativistic effects, making the PBF neutrino energy losses negligible in practice.
- For spin-triplet (³P₂) pairing with M_J = 0, the anomalous axial-vector vertices are given by B_1 = √(3/2) Δ ω / (ω² - Δ²/5) e* and B_{-1} = √(3/2) Δ ω / (ω² - Δ²/5) e, where Δ is the temperature-dependent gap amplitude and ω is the total neutrino pair energy.
- The vector channel polarization tensor Π_00^V(ω, 0) = 0 exactly in the long-wave limit, as required by current conservation, proving that the neutrino emissivity via the vector channel obtained in earlier work (Yakovlev 1999) is inconsistent.

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier work (Leinson 2006, 2008, 2010, 2012) showing that the PBF neutrino emission from ¹S₀ superfluids is practically absent, contradicting the earlier calculations by Flowers et al. (1976) and Yakovlev et al. (1999) which overestimated the emissivity. The suppression of ¹S₀ PBF emission was first understood in the context of neutron star crust cooling by Gupta et al. (2007), resolving contradictions with superburst observations by Cumming et al. (2006). For ³P₂ pairing in the neutron star core, the minimal cooling paradigm (Page 2009) previously reduced the emissivity by only about 30% by neglecting anomalous axial-vector interactions; this review shows that full inclusion of anomalous contributions leads to stronger suppression, and simulations by Han et al. (2017) predict higher surface temperatures as a result.
