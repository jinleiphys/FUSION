# Hadronization within the Non-Extensive Approach and the Evolution of the Parameters

> We review transverse momentum distributions of various identified charged particles stemming from high energy collisions fitted by various non-extensive distributions as well as by the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. We investigate the best-fit formula with the obtained $χ^2/ndf$ values. We find that the physical mass and $\sqrt{s}$ scaling become more explicit with heavier produced hadrons in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions. The spectral shape parameters, in particular the temperature $T$ and the non-extensive Tsallis parameter $q$, do exhibit an almost linear dependence with the centrality-dependence in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that non-extensive Tsallis distributions (with parameters \(T\) and \(q\)) provide good fits to transverse momentum spectra of identified charged hadrons across a wide range of collision energies and systems, but the authors hedge that the choice of fitting function (whether using \(m_T-m\), \(m_T\), or \(p_T\) as the variable) affects the extracted parameters, and that the classical Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution fails to describe the high-\(p_T\) tails.

## Method

The authors fitted six different distribution functions (one Boltzmann-Gibbs and five non-extensive Tsallis-type formulas with different variable choices: \(m_T-m\), \(m_T\), and \(p_T\)) to published transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons from proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\) GeV, 200 GeV, 900 GeV, 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV, and 7 TeV, as well as from \(pPb\) collisions at 5.02 TeV and \(PbPb\) collisions at 2.76 TeV. They compared the goodness-of-fit via \(\chi^2/ndf\) values and analyzed the scaling of the fitted temperature \(T\) and Tsallis parameter \(q\) with center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}\) and particle mass \(m\).

## Key numbers

- Fitting \(p_T\) ranges for \(pp\) collisions: pions from 0.3-2.9 GeV/c (at 62.4 GeV) up to 0.1-20 GeV/c (at 7 TeV); kaons from 0.4-2 GeV/c (at 62.4 GeV) up to 0.2-20 GeV/c (at 7 TeV); protons from 0.6-3.6 GeV/c (at 62.4 GeV) up to 0.3-20 GeV/c (at 7 TeV).
- For \(pp\) at 62.4 GeV, the mean transverse mass \(\langle m_T \rangle\) from non-extensive fits for pions was 0.395\(\pm\)0.001 GeV/c\(^2\), for kaons 0.769\(\pm\)0.003 GeV/c\(^2\), and for protons 1.169\(\pm\)0.001 GeV/c\(^2\), compared to experimental data values of 0.395\(\pm\)0.015, 0.740\(\pm\)0.013, and 1.176\(\pm\)0.032 GeV/c\(^2\) respectively.
- For \(pp\) at 7 TeV, the mean transverse mass \(\langle m_T \rangle\) from non-extensive fits for pions was 0.503\(\pm\)0.001 GeV/c\(^2\), for kaons 0.963\(\pm\)0.0003 GeV/c\(^2\), and for protons 1.364\(\pm\)0.0005 GeV/c\(^2\), compared to experimental data values of 0.500\(\pm\)0.007, 0.892\(\pm\)0.0308, and 1.424\(\pm\)0.0515 GeV/c\(^2\) respectively.
- The fitting temperature \(T\) for pions in \(pp\) collisions using function \(f_1\) followed \(T = T_0 + T_1 \ln\sqrt{s}\) with \(T_0 = 0.112\) GeV and \(T_1 = 0.002\) GeV; for protons the same function gave \(T_0 = 0.050\) GeV and \(T_1 = 0.020\) GeV.
- The Tsallis parameter \(q\) for pions in \(pp\) collisions using function \(f_1\) followed \(q = q_0 + q_1 \ln\sqrt{s}\) with \(q_0 = 1.042\) and \(q_1 = 0.012\); for protons the same function gave \(q_0 = 1.080\) and \(q_1 = 0.006\).

## Context

This work extends the application of non-extensive Tsallis statistics to hadronization in high-energy collisions, building on prior studies by Hagedorn (1995) and various experimental collaborations (PHENIX and ALICE). The paper systematically compares multiple functional forms of the Tsallis distribution that have been used in the literature, including those from Lavagno (2002) and others, and demonstrates that the choice of variable (\(m_T-m\) vs. \(m_T\) vs. \(p_T\)) affects the extracted temperature and \(q\) parameters. The findings enable future studies to select the most physically motivated fitting function for extracting thermodynamic parameters from experimental spectra, and suggest that the temperature and \(q\) parameter scale logarithmically with \(\sqrt{s}/m\), which may guide theoretical understanding of non-extensive effects in QCD.
