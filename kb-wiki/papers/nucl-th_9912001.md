# Single Boson Images Via an Extended Holstein Primakoff Mapping

> The Holstein-Primakoff mapping for pairs of bosons is extended in order to accommodate single boson mapping. The proposed extension allows a variety of applications and especially puts the formalism at finite temperature on firm grounds. The new mapping is applied to the O(N+1) anharmonic oscillator with global symmetry broken down to O(N). It is explicitly demonstrated that N-Goldstone modes appear. This result generalizes the Holstein-Primakoff mapping for interacting boson as developed in ref.[1].

# Single Boson Images Via an Extended Holstein Primakoff Mapping

## Key claim

The paper extends the Holstein-Primakoff mapping for boson pairs to also accommodate single boson images, which was previously impossible; this extension is necessary to unambiguously define the two-point function for the Goldstone mode and to place the formalism at finite temperature on firm theoretical grounds, but the demonstration is performed only for the O(N+1) anharmonic oscillator in the 1/N-expansion to leading order, with infrared divergences deliberately disregarded.

## Method

The author derives an extended Holstein-Primakoff mapping for bosons by starting from the ten generators of the non-compact Sp(4) group, then contracting the algebra to the semidirect product group Sp(2) ⊗ N(1) via an Inönü-Wigner contraction, which yields images for both single bosons and boson pairs. The mapping is applied to the O(N+1) anharmonic oscillator with global symmetry broken to O(N), using a coherent variational ground state with two condensates, and the Hamiltonian is expanded in powers of 1/N to leading order. The resulting bilinear Hamiltonian is diagonalized via a generalized Bogoliubov rotation (RPA equations of motion) to extract the excitation spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The Goldstone mode frequency is given by ℰ_π² = ω² (d − √(1 + d²))⁴ = ω² + 4 g s² + (2g/ℰ_π) = η/s, which vanishes in the exact symmetry limit (η = 0) for finite condensate s ≠ 0.
- The sigma-mode frequency is ℰ_σ² = ω² + 12 g s² + (2g/ω)(d + √(1 + d²))².
- The constant z_N in the mapping function Γ_N(m) is fixed to z_N = N − 2 by requiring the residue of the pion two-point function at the pole to be 2N Γ_N²(0) = 1.
- The RPA phonon frequencies Ω_ν satisfy Ω_ν² = η/s + (8 g s²) / (1 − (4g/ℰ_π) * 1/(Ω_ν² − 4ℰ_π²)), with a pair of zero-energy solutions in the exact symmetry limit (η = 0).

## Context

This work extends the Holstein-Primakoff mapping for boson pairs previously applied to the O(N) vector model by Aouissat, Schuck, and Wambach (1998), which could only provide RPA phonon modes and lacked single-boson images. The extension is inspired by Marshalek's earlier extension of the Holstein-Primakoff mapping for fermions to treat both even and odd nuclei, and by the work of Klein, Cohen, Li, Rafelski, and Rafelski on mapping the SO(5) group generators. The result enables unambiguous computation of all n-point functions at next-to-leading order in the 1/N-expansion and provides a natural extension to finite temperature, which the author states will be addressed in a forthcoming publication.
