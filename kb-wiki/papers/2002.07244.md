# Fission Fragment Excitation Energy Sharing Beyond Scission

> A simplified, though realistic, model describing two receding and accelerating fission fragments, due to their mutual Coulomb repulsion, shows that fission fragments share excitation energy well after they ceased to exchange nucleons. This mechanism leads to a lower total kinetic energy of the fission fragments, particularly if the pygmy resonances in the fission fragments are excited. Even though the emphasis here is on fission, similar arguments apply to fragments in heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

Fission fragments can exchange several MeV of excitation energy through Coulomb interactions well after scission (when nucleon exchange has ceased), contradicting the long-held assumption that total kinetic energy (TKE) is fully determined at scission and that intrinsic excitation energies are fixed at that point.

## Method

The author develops a simplified classical model treating fission fragments as incompressible neutron and proton fluids with harmonic restoring forces (dipole oscillations) and mutual Coulomb repulsion, solving the equations of motion for the relative fragment coordinate and the intrinsic dipole coordinates. The model includes damping of dipole oscillations and is compared with time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) simulations of induced fission of 236U using the SeaLL1 energy density functional.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu induced fission with average fragment charges Z_h=53 and Z_L=41, the initial Coulomb energy is e²Z₁Z₂/η = 176.2 MeV
- Dipole mode energies of approximately 6 MeV are observed in TDDFT simulations for the fission fragments
- For undamped oscillations with ℏω=6 MeV and initially polarized fragments, the intrinsic excitation energy reaches several MeV at large separations
- For strongly damped cases (ℏγ₁=ℏγ₂=6 MeV), the internal excitation energy increases by up to a few MeV beyond that acquired during saddle-to-scission descent
- Scission occurs when the separation between fragment centers of mass exceeds approximately 21 fm for 236U

## Context

This work extends the author's previous TDDFT studies (Bulgac 2016, 2019, 2020) that demonstrated fission fragment deformation properties evolve after scission, contradicting decades-old assumptions in the field. It contradicts phenomenological models (Brosa 1990, Sierk 2017, Usang 2019) and time-dependent generator coordinate method calculations (Regnier 2016, 2019) that assume fragments have significant kinetic energy at scission and that TKE is determined at that point. The mechanism enables reinterpretation of TKE measurements as signatures of post-scission energy exchange rather than solely scission configurations, and suggests similar effects occur in heavy-ion reaction fragments.
