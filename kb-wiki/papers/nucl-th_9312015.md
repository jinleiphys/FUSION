# Semiclassical Quark Model Of the Nuclear Matter Response

> The longitudinal response function $R(q,w)$\ of nuclear matter is calculated in a semiclassical quark model. The model has a many- body string-flip potential that confines quarks into hadrons and avoids color van der Waals forces. Molecular dynamics simulations are used to calculate $R(q,w)$\ in one space dimension for a variety of momentum transfers $q$\ and excitation energies $w$. The response function decreases with density (compared to a free hadron response) because of quark exchange between hadrons.

## Key claim

The longitudinal response function of nuclear matter, calculated in a semiclassical quark model with a string-flip potential, decreases with density compared to a free hadron response because of quark exchange between hadrons; this decrease is presented as a suggestion that quark exchange effects may somewhat decrease the strength of traditional hadronic calculations, rather than as a definitive quantitative prediction.

## Method

The authors used a one-dimensional string-flip potential model that confines quarks into two-quark hadrons via harmonic oscillator links, with the minimum pairing of quarks determined at each time step. Molecular dynamics simulations were performed for systems of 8 and 16 quarks with periodic boundary conditions, using a fictitious temperature derived from a quantum variational calculation to simulate zero-point motion. The longitudinal response function R(q,w) was computed from the Fourier transform of the autocorrelation function of the one-body density, and compared to analytic expressions for free quark and free hadron gases.

## Key numbers

- The response function was calculated for densities ranging from ρ = 0.043 to ρ = 1.59 (in units where quark mass m, spring constant k, and Planck's constant ħ are set to 1).
- Momentum transfers used include q = 0.034, 0.33, 0.32, 0.65, 1.30, 0.70, 1.25, 0.17 (in inverse length units where ħ²/(2mk)¹/⁴ = 0.841 sets the length scale).
- The root mean square separation of quarks in a ground state hadron is ⟨x²⟩¹/² = 0.841 (ħ²/(mk))¹/⁴.
- Energy conservation in the molecular dynamics integration was maintained to better than one percent using a time step of 0.01.
- Finite size effects were tested by comparing N=8 and N=16 quark systems at ρ = 0.211 and ρ = 0.414, finding very small differences at low density and somewhat larger but still small differences at higher density.

## Context

This work extends the string-flip quark model of Horowitz, Moniz, and Negele (1985) and Lenz et al. (1986) from ground state calculations to dynamical response functions, using a semiclassical approximation to avoid the statistical noise that prevents direct real-time path integral Monte Carlo calculations. The authors note that a previous attempt by Horowitz (1985) calculated the Coulomb sum (energy integral of the response) but could not separate nucleon excitations from quark excitations; the present work provides the full energy-dependent response. The results suggest that quark exchange effects may decrease the strength of traditional hadronic calculations of the nuclear response, potentially explaining deficits in the experimental Coulomb sum and Gamow-Teller strength, though no evidence for new collective modes (quark giant resonances) was found in this simplified model without spin, flavor, or color degrees of freedom.
