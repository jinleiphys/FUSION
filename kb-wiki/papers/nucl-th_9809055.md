# Tensor interaction in Hartree--Fock calculations

> The contribution of the tensor interaction in Hartree-Fock calculations for closed shell nuclei is studied. The investigation is done neglecting the spin-orbit component of the force. As expected from nuclear matter estimates we find that the effect of the tensor interaction is negligible in the case of spin-isospin saturated nuclei and also for medium and heavy nuclei with closed shells. In light nuclei with only one of the two spin- orbit partners filled, such as 12C and 14C, the tensor interaction plays an important role.

## Key claim

The tensor interaction's contribution to Hartree-Fock binding energies is negligible for nuclei with fully occupied spin-orbit partner levels (spin-isospin saturated nuclei and medium-to-heavy closed-shell nuclei), but it plays an important role in light nuclei such as 12C and 14C where only one of the two spin-orbit partners is filled; however, this conclusion is conditional on having neglected the spin-orbit component of the force entirely.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations for closed-shell nuclei using the Brink and Boeker B1 finite-range central interaction, to which they added tensor and tensor-isospin terms taken from the realistic Argonne v14 potential and from the phenomenological Julich-Stony Brook (JSB) interaction. They solved the Hartree-Fock equations iteratively using a plane-wave expansion method, starting from single-particle wave functions generated by a Saxon-Woods potential without spin-orbit or Coulomb terms, and compared binding energies and single-particle energies across nuclei from 4He to 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 16O with the Argonne v14 potential alone (without B1 central part): 52.08 MeV (unbound)
- Binding energy of 40Ca with the Argonne v14 potential alone: 217.18 MeV (unbound)
- For 48Ca, the change in binding energy when using the T6 tensor force (fitted to reproduce 12C binding) relative to the B1-only result is approximately 2%
- The B1 interaction was originally constructed to reproduce experimental binding energies of 4He, 16O, and 40Ca

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear matter estimates (Wir88) that suggested tensor effects would be small in symmetric nuclear matter, and it contradicts the common practice of neglecting tensor terms in mean-field effective interactions. The authors note that Random Phase Approximation calculations (Spe80) had already shown tensor terms are needed to describe magnetic excitations, and they cite Nak84 showing that effective tensor forces from Brueckner G-matrix calculations are similar to bare nucleon-nucleon tensor terms. The paper enables future construction of finite-range effective interactions that include tensor, spin-orbit, and density-dependent terms for use across the full isotope table.
