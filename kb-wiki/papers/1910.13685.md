# Color transparency of $K^+$ meson produced in the inclusive $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction on nuclei

> Color transparency is studied for the $K^+$ meson produced due to large four-momentum transfer in the $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction on nuclei. The variation of the $K^+$ meson color transparency ($K^+$CT) with the photon virtuality, and kaon momentum is investigated. The calculated results for $K^+$CT are compared with the data reported from Jefferson Laboratory.

# Color transparency of $K^+$ meson produced in the inclusive $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction on nuclei

## Key claim

Color transparency of the $K^+$ meson, calculated using the quantum diffusion model with a hadron formation length parameter $\Delta M^2 = 0.3$ GeV$^2$, reproduces the Jefferson Laboratory nuclear transparency data for $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au, whereas conventional Glauber model calculations using the free-space kaon-nucleon total cross section significantly underestimate those data.

## Method

The nuclear transparency $T_A(K^+)$ for the inclusive $(e,e^\prime)$ reaction on $d$, $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au was calculated using the Glauber model with an optical potential approach. The effective kaon-nucleon total cross section $\sigma^{K^+N}_{t,eff}$ was implemented via the quantum diffusion model, which depends on the photon virtuality $Q^2$, the hadron formation length $l_h$, and the path length $l_z$ traversed by the kaon in the nucleus. The nuclear density distributions were taken from electron scattering data (harmonic oscillator Gaussian for $^{12}$C, two-parameter Fermi for $^{63}$Cu and $^{197}$Au, and Paris potential for deuteron).

## Key numbers

- The calculated $T_A(K^+)$ using $\Delta M^2 = 0.3$ GeV$^2$ agrees well with JLab data for all nuclei studied ($^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, $^{197}$Au) in the $Q^2$ range of a few GeV.
- The hadron formation length is given by $l_h = 2k_h / \Delta M^2$, where $\Delta M^2$ is uncertain between 0.25 and 1.4 GeV$^2$; the best fit to data uses $\Delta M^2 = 0.3$ GeV$^2$.
- The kaon momentum range considered is approximately 3 to 9.5 GeV/$c$, accessible in 11 GeV JLab kinematics.
- The small-angle elastic scattering cross section $\Delta \sigma_{el}^{K^+A}$ uses an angular aperture $\Delta \Omega = 6.7$ msr, but its contribution to $T_A(K^+)$ is negligible.
- The transverse momentum of the (anti)quark is $\langle k_t^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.35$ GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the study of color transparency from pions (Clasie et al., 2007) and rho mesons (Howell et al., 2007; Kopeliovich et al., 2001) to the $K^+$ meson, motivated by the failure of conventional nuclear physics to explain JLab data (Nuru et al., 2014). The calculation uses the quantum diffusion model of Farrar et al. (1988) and follows the approach of Kaskulov et al. (2009) for pionic color transparency. The results enable future experimental tests of color transparency for kaons at higher momenta accessible at 11 GeV JLab and at facilities such as J-PARC.
