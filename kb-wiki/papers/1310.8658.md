# Theoretical study of the ${}^{4}\rm{He}(γ,p)^3\rm{H}$ and ${}^{4}\rm{He}(γ,n)^3\rm{He}$ reactions

> {\it Ab initio} calculation of the total cross section for the reactions $^{4}\rm{He}(γ,p)^3\rm{H}$ and $^{4}\rm{He}(γ,n)^3\rm{He}$ is presented, using state-of-the-art nuclear forces. The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method is applied, which allows exact treatment of the final state interaction (FSI). The dynamic equations are solved using the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method. In this calculation of the cross sections the three-nucleon force is fully taken into account, except in the source term of the LIT equation for the FSI transition matrix element.

## Key claim

The authors present the first *ab initio* calculation of the exclusive two-body photodisintegration cross sections for ⁴He using a state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonian, but the three-nucleon force is omitted from the source term of the Lorentz integral transform equation for the final-state interaction transition matrix element, leading to a discrepancy between the sum of exclusive cross sections and the inclusive cross section.

## Method

The authors apply the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method to treat the final-state interaction exactly, solving the dynamic equations with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method. They use the unretarded dipole approximation and include isospin-symmetry breaking in the calculation. The three-nucleon force (UIX) is included in the Hamiltonian but omitted from the source term of the exclusive LIT equation for the final-state interaction.

## Key numbers

- The sum of the exclusive cross sections σ_{n,³He} + σ_{p,³H} calculated with the KMTI-III nucleon-nucleon potential agrees excellently with the inclusive cross section up to approximately 34 MeV, above which three- and four-body breakup effects become visible.
- For the AV18/UIX potential model, the sum of the exclusive cross sections does not reproduce the inclusive cross section, indicating the role of the three-nucleon force in the final-state interaction is not negligible.
- The authors estimate an error of the order of 1% in the cross section from convergence of the LIT and variance in the inversion process.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Quaglioni (2004) that used a semi-realistic Malfliet-Tjon potential without three-nucleon forces, and by Hori (2012) that used nucleon-nucleon interactions with ad-hoc three-nucleon forces. The results enable future inclusion of the three-nucleon force in the source term of the exclusive LIT equation and can be applied to nucleon-nucleus scattering reactions.
