# Ultraviolet energy dependence of particle production sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The energy dependence of particle production sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated from RHIC to LHC energies. Whereas charged-hadron production in the fragmentation sources follows a ln(s_NN/s_0) law, particle production in the mid-rapidity gluon-gluon source exhibits a much stronger dependence proportional to ln^3(s_NN/s_0), and becomes dominant between RHIC and LHC energies. The production of particles with pseudorapidities beyond the beam rapidity is also discussed.

## Key claim
Charged-hadron production in the mid-rapidity gluon-gluon source follows a cubic-log dependence proportional to ln^3(s_NN/s_0), not a power law as initially proposed in Wolschin (2013), and this dependence only approximates a power law in the limited energy range of about 100 GeV to 2.76 TeV.

## Method
The author uses the nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM), a Fokker-Planck-type transport equation with three sources (two fragmentation sources and one mid-rapidity gluon-gluon source), to fit charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions from central AuAu collisions at RHIC (19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) and PbPb collisions at LHC (2.76 TeV and extrapolated to 5.52 TeV). The particle content of each source is extracted by minimizing the analytical solutions of the transport equation against available pseudorapidity data via chi-squared fits.

## Key numbers
- Total charged-hadron production follows a power law: N_ch^tot = 1.1 x 10^4 (s_NN/s_0)^0.23 with s_0 = 1 TeV^2
- Fragmentation source particle content: N_ch^qg = 695 x ln(s_NN/s_0) with s_0 = 100 GeV^2
- Mid-rapidity gluon-gluon source: N_ch^gg = 7.5 x ln^3(s_NN/s_0) with s_0 = 169 GeV^2
- Predicted total charged-hadron yield at 5.125 TeV (upcoming 2015 LHC run): N_ch^tot = 23,327, with central source contributing N_ch^gg = 12,811
- Total charged-hadron yield at 2.76 TeV from RDM: N_ch^tot = 17,327; ALICE collaboration extrapolated value: 17,146 +/- 722
- Mid-rapidity yield density: dN_ch^tot/deta at eta~0 = 1.15 x 10^3 (s_NN/s_0)^0.165 with s_0 = 1 TeV^2
- Width of gluon-gluon source in rapidity at 2.76 TeV: Gamma_gg = 6.24 (RDM), compared to Landau result Gamma_gg^Landau = 6.36 in eta

## Context
This work extends the relativistic diffusion model analysis of Wolschin (2013) by correcting the energy dependence of the gluon-gluon source from a power law to a cubic-log dependence, corroborated by independent investigations of charged-particle and transverse energy production by Sahoo and Mishra (2014). The result contrasts with the thermal/statistical hadronization model of Braun-Munzinger et al. and Becattini et al., which does not describe the plateau in rapidity distributions or limiting fragmentation at RHIC and LHC energies. The cubic-log dependence satisfies the Froissart bound and is consistent with theoretical calculations of gg scattering in a classical color field by Cheung and Chiu (2011). The analysis also shows that Landau hydrodynamics fails for the overall pseudorapidity distribution at LHC energies but may still describe the mid-rapidity source.
