# Effects on the hadron propagators due to $k_μk_ν$ terms in the vector meson propagator

> In an approximation where the baryon current conservation is violated, the contribution of the $k_μk_ν$ terms in the vector meson propagator may not vanish. Their effects on the baryon and meson spectral functions and on the consequences of self-consistency are studied in the relativistic self-consistent Hartree-Fock approximation by means of the $σ-ω$ model. Two cases where the $k_μk_ν$ terms are and are not neglected are compared. It is found that there is a marked change in the baryon spectral function which becomes more peaked in the latter case. Such a change remains even by a proper readjustment of parameters. The effects of self-consistency in the $σ-ω$ model are qualitatively the same in both cases, though quantitatively there is some significant difference.

# Effects on the hadron propagators due to $k_\mu k_\nu$ terms in the vector meson propagator

## Key claim

In the relativistic self-consistent Hartree-Fock (RSCHF) approximation using the $\sigma-\omega$ model, including the $k_\mu k_\nu$ terms in the vector meson propagator (which are usually neglected based on baryon current conservation) produces a marked change in the baryon spectral function that becomes more peaked, and this change cannot be removed by a proper readjustment of parameters. The effect of self-consistency remains qualitatively the same whether or not these terms are included, though with quantitative differences.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the renormalized baryon and meson propagators in the $\sigma-\omega$ model using the RSCHF approximation, comparing two cases: case I where the $k_\mu k_\nu$ terms in the $\omega$-meson propagator are neglected, and case II where they are properly included following a rule proposed in earlier work (Zhang and Wu, 2000). They studied three different approximation schemes (potential P, extended potential EP, and fully self-consistent FSC) and computed the baryon spectral weight functions $\alpha(k^2)$ and $\beta(k^2)$ as well as the meson spectral functions $\rho_s(k^2)$ and $\rho_v(k^2)$ using on-shell renormalization conditions for baryons and intermediate renormalization conditions for mesons.

## Key numbers

- Baryon true mass $M_t = 4.7585\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, scalar meson mass $m_s = 2.6353\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, vector meson mass $m_v = 3.9680\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$
- Scalar coupling $\overline{g}_s^2 = g_s^2 / 16\pi^2 = 0.5263$
- Vector coupling $\overline{g}_v^2 = g_v^2 / 8\pi^2 = 1.3685$
- For an intermediate vector coupling $\overline{g}_v^2 = 0.3400$, the effect of self-consistency on the baryon spectral functions was examined, showing only a small increase in peak height

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Krein, Nielsen, Puff, and Wilets (1990s) who found that the baryon spectral function could become negative in the $\omega$-meson-only case when $k_\mu k_\nu$ terms were neglected, and by Zhang and Wu (2000) who showed those negative values were caused by neglecting these terms. The present paper extends the analysis to the $\sigma-\omega$ model with both scalar and vector mesons, showing that even when other mesons are present, the $k_\mu k_\nu$ terms significantly affect the baryon spectral function. The results indicate that in approximations violating baryon current conservation (such as RSCHF with bare vertices), these terms cannot be safely neglected, and future work should pursue calculations using vertices consistent with the Ward-Takahashi identity to determine whether simpler approximations can substitute for that more rigorous approach.
