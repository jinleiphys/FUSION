# Crystalline Phases in QCD

> For decades now, low-energy models of QCD have shown indications that a crystalline quark phase could be stable at high chemical potentials. Beyond models, however, there are numerous difficulties in investigating such a hypothesis in full QCD, such as the sign problem. Functional methods do not suffer from the sign problem, and thus, can access the high--$μ$ side of the QCD phase diagram. The main tool used to look for signs of inhomogeneous/crystalline phases in low-energy models is the so-called ``stability analysis''. In this talk, I show how the standard stability analysis was generalised to be applicable in any theory, including QCD.

## Key claim
Low-energy models of QCD have long indicated that a crystalline quark phase could be stable at high chemical potentials, but the author shows that the standard stability analysis used to search for such phases can be generalized to be applicable in any theory, including full QCD, despite the non-locality of the quark self-energy and the complexity of the free-energy functional.

## Method
The author generalizes the stability analysis by expanding the 2PI effective action of QCD around the homogeneous chiral quark propagator and computing the quadratic contribution to the free energy from small inhomogeneous perturbations. The perturbation to the quark self-energy is written as a product of a chiral-breaking mass term and a translation-breaking function of momentum difference, allowing the sign of the free-energy shift to be governed by a reduced functional that is independent of the specific shape of the inhomogeneous perturbation. The analysis is performed for three models of the running coupling (Maris-Tandy with and without UV log-tail, and Qin-Chang) by fixing test-function parameters via a complex saddle-point condition along the second-order chiral transition and left spinodal lines.

## Key numbers
- For the Maris-Tandy model with UV log-tail, the model parameters are \(\eta = 1.8\) and \(\Lambda_\text{MT} = 720\) MeV.
- For the Qin-Chang model, the parameters are \(\omega_\text{QC} = 600\) MeV and \(D = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the UV log-tail, \(\Lambda_\text{QCD} = 234\) MeV, \(m_t = 500\) MeV, and \(N_F = 4\).
- The test-function for the mass perturbation uses five parameters \(L_1\) through \(L_5\) and an imaginary part proportional to \(L_3\); the parameters are fixed by the complex saddle-point condition along the line where \(\tilde\Omega^{(2)}(0) = 0\).
- For the full Maris-Tandy model, \(\tilde\Omega^{(2)}\) becomes negative for finite momentum difference \(d\) at lower temperatures along the homogeneous stability boundary, indicating instability toward inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Context
This work extends the stability analysis technique previously applied to QCD-inspired models such as the quark-meson model, NJL model, and Gross-Neveu model (Buballa 2014, Nickel 2009, Thies 2006) to full QCD using functional methods. It builds on the first Dyson-Schwinger equation solution for an inhomogeneous phase (Muller 2013) and addresses concerns raised by Pannullo (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024) and Winstel (2022, 2024) that model results might be artificial or washed out by fluctuations. The method is complementary to the new approach in Motta (2024) which bypasses the saddle-point search and confirms that the instability persists across the first-order transition line, enabling future work with closer-to-QCD truncations.
