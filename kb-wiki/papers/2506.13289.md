# $0νββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements under Left-Right symmetric model from the spherical quasi-particle random phase approximation method with realistic force

> We perform the calculation of nuclear matrix elements for the neutrinoless double beta decays under a Left-Right symmetric model mediated by light neutrino, and we adopt the spherical quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) approach with realistic force. For eight nuclei: $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te and $^{136}$Xe, related nuclear matrix elements are given. We analyze each term and the details of contributions of different parts are also given. For the $q$ term, we find that the weak-magnetism components of the nucleon current contribute equally as other components such as axial-vector. We also discuss the influence of short-range correlations on these NMEs. It is found that $R$ term are more sensitive to the short range correlation than other terms due to the large portion of the contribution from high exchange momenta.

## Key claim
The paper calculates nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay under the Left-Right symmetric model using the spherical quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with realistic force, finding that weak-magnetism components of the nucleon current contribute equally to axial-vector components in the q term, contrary to earlier assumptions that these components were suppressed.

## Method
The authors employed the spherical proton-neutron quasi-particle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from the Brueckner G-matrix, using a large model space (N=0-7). They solved BCS equations for initial and final even-even nuclei to obtain quasi-particle energies and occupation amplitudes, then constructed QRPA phonon excitations for intermediate odd-odd nuclei. The NMEs were computed by summing over all intermediate states, incorporating short-range correlations (SRC) through Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn parametrizations, and including vector, axial-vector, weak-magnetism, and induced pseudo-scalar form factors with momentum dependence.

## Key numbers
- For 76Ge: total M_GT = 4.667 (AV18 SRC), 5.169 (CD-Bonn SRC), 5.024 (no SRC)
- For 76Ge: M_qGT (q+ total) = 4.364 (AV18), 4.611 (CD-Bonn), 4.237 (no SRC); M_qGT (q- total) = 1.671 (AV18), 1.682 (CD-Bonn), 1.440 (no SRC)
- For 76Ge: RGT = 8.873 (AV18), 11.240 (CD-Bonn), 12.756 (no SRC)
- For 136Xe: total M_GT = 2.028 (AV18), 2.261 (CD-Bonn), 2.179 (no SRC)
- For 136Xe: M_qGT (q+ total) = 1.992 (AV18), 2.107 (CD-Bonn), 1.916 (no SRC); M_qGT (q- total) = 0.667 (AV18), 0.671 (CD-Bonn), 0.549 (no SRC)
- For 136Xe: RGT = 5.632 (AV18), 7.151 (CD-Bonn), 8.137 (no SRC)
- Pairing strengths g_pair range from 0.66 to 0.99 across the eight nuclei
- Isovector particle-particle parameters g_pp^(T=1) range from 0.77 to 0.92; isoscalar parameters g_pp^(T=0) range from 0.53 to 0.66

## Context
This work extends previous QRPA calculations of LRSM NMEs by Simkovic (2017) and others, which neglected weak-magnetism contributions, and confirms recent LSSM findings by Fang (2024) that weak-magnetism components are essential. The calculations provide NMEs for eight candidate nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe) under the mass, lambda, and eta mechanisms, enabling more accurate extraction of lepton-number-violating parameters from future experimental half-life measurements. The results also show that R terms are more sensitive to short-range correlations than other terms due to large contributions from high exchange momenta.
