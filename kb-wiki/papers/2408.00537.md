# Bayesian analysis of (3+1)D relativistic nuclear dynamics with the RHIC beam energy scan data

> This work presents a Bayesian inference study for relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider. The theoretical model simulates event-by-event (3+1)D collision dynamics using hydrodynamics and hadronic transport theory. We analyze the model's 20-dimensional posterior distributions obtained using three model emulators with different accuracy and demonstrate the essential role of training an accurate model emulator in the Bayesian analysis. Our analysis provides robust constraints on the Quark-Gluon Plasma's transport properties and various aspects of (3+1)D relativistic nuclear dynamics. By running full model simulations with 100 parameter sets sampled from the posterior distribution, we make predictions for $p_{\rm T}$-differential observables and estimate their systematic theory uncertainty. A sensitivity analysis is performed to elucidate how individual experimental observables respond to different model parameters, providing useful physics insights into the phenomenological model for heavy-ion collisions.

# Bayesian analysis of (3+1)D relativistic nuclear dynamics with the RHIC beam energy scan data

## Key claim
The Bayesian inference provides robust constraints on the Quark-Gluon Plasma's transport properties and (3+1)D relativistic nuclear dynamics, but the quality of these constraints depends critically on using an accurate model emulator: the PCSK emulator gives stronger constraints (KL divergence of 24.6) than the Scikit GP emulator (KL divergence of 20.9), and the Bayes factor shows strong evidence favoring the PCSK emulator over the Scikit GP emulator.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using the iEBE-MUSIC framework, which couples a 3D-Glauber initial state model with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) and hadronic transport (UrQMD) for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV. They trained Gaussian Process emulators (PCSK and Scikit-learn) on 1,000 design points from a Maximum Projection Latin Hypercube Design plus 95 High Probability Posterior points, then used Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the pocoMC package to explore the 20-dimensional parameter space against STAR and PHOBOS experimental data.

## Key numbers
- 20 model parameters were constrained using Bayesian inference with uniform priors
- KL divergence for PCSK emulator (LHD + HPP): 24.6; for Scikit GP: 20.9; for PCSK (LHD only): 22.4
- Bayes factor ln(B) = 6.94 +/- 0.03 for PCSK (LHD + HPP) versus Scikit GP, indicating strong evidence favoring PCSK
- Bayes factor ln(B) = -1.41 +/- 0.03 for PCSK (LHD + HPP) versus PCSK (LHD only), indicating weak evidence favoring the LHD-only training
- QGP specific shear viscosity at mu_B = 0 GeV (eta_0) prior range: [0.001, 0.3]; at mu_B = 0.2 GeV (eta_2) prior range: [0.001, 0.3]; at mu_B = 0.4 GeV (eta_4) prior range: [0.001, 0.3]
- QGP specific bulk viscosity maximum (zeta_max) prior range: [0, 0.2]; peak temperature T_zeta,0 prior range: [0.15, 0.25] GeV
- Switching energy density e_sw prior range: [0.15, 0.5] GeV/fm^3
- Rapidity loss fluctuation parameter sigma_yloss constrained to approximately 0.3, smaller than the value 0.6 used in Shen (2022)
- alpha_shadowing constrained to approximately 0.15, indicating 15% of binary collisions are shadowed
- alpha_rem = 0.54 +/- 0.15
- lambda_B approximately 0.15
- B_G = 15.99 (+7.14/-9.66) GeV^-2, corresponding to nucleon mass width between 0.5 and 0.95 fm at 90% confidence level
- 544 experimental data points were used in the Bayesian inference
- 100 posterior parameter sets were used for full model simulations: 1,000 minimum bias events at 200 GeV, 2,000 at 19.6 GeV, and 3,000 at 7.7 GeV per parameter set

## Context
This work expands upon the Bayesian inference analysis of Shen (2023) by using a more accurate Gaussian Process emulator (PCSK) compared to the Scikit GP emulator used previously, and by including 95 additional high-probability posterior points in the training dataset. The study extends previous phenomenological extractions of QGP transport properties from the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020) and Bernhard (2019) to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program at finite baryon density, requiring full (3+1)D simulations. The results enable predictions for pT-differential observables with quantified systematic theory uncertainties and provide sensitivity analysis linking individual experimental observables to specific model parameters, which can guide future experimental measurements and model refinements.
