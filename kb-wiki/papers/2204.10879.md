# Event-by-event anti-deuteron multiplicity fluctuation in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> Using the nucleon coalescence model based on kinetic freeze-out nucleons from the hybrid model of MUSIC hydrodynamics and UrQMD hadronic transport, we study the production of anti-deuteron and its event-by-event fluctuation in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We find a clear suppression of the anti-deuteron to antiproton yield ratio in peripheral collisions, which is in accordance with the measurements from the ALICE Collaboration. Also found is a Poissonian event-by-event fluctuation of the anti-deuteron multiplicity distribution in all collision centralities, which is different from the prediction of a simple coalescence model calculation that assumes all antiproton and antineutron pairs to have the same probability to form anti-deuterons. We further find a small negative correlation between the anti-deuteron and antiproton multiplicity distributions as a result of the baryon conservation.

## Key claim

Using a realistic coalescence model that includes baryon conservation and a Wigner-function-based formation probability, the authors find that the event-by-event anti-deuteron multiplicity distribution in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV is consistent with the Poisson limit for all centralities, contrary to the prediction of a simple coalescence model that assumes all antiproton and antineutron pairs have the same formation probability.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model MUSIC with the hadronic transport model UrQMD to generate kinetic freeze-out nucleons from \(2\times 10^6\) events. Anti-deuteron yields were then calculated event-by-event using a nucleon coalescence model where the formation probability is given by the Wigner function of the anti-deuteron internal wave function, with a size parameter \(\sigma_{\bar{d}} = 2.26\) fm. Baryon conservation was enforced by sequentially forming anti-deuterons from antiproton-antineutron pairs and removing the paired antinucleons from further coalescence.

## Key numbers

- The size parameter in the Wigner function is \(\sigma_{\bar{d}} = \sqrt{4/3} \, r_{\bar{d}} \approx 2.26\) fm, related to the anti-deuteron root-mean-squared radius.
- The scaled moment \(C_2/C_1\) of the anti-deuteron multiplicity distribution is consistent with the Poisson limit of 1.0 for all collision centralities and pseudorapidity acceptance windows.
- The mean correlation coefficient \(\rho_{\bar{p}\bar{d}}\) between antiproton and anti-deuteron multiplicities, averaged over all centralities, is approximately \(-4.2 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(|\eta| < 0.1\) and \(-8.5 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(|\eta| < 0.8\).
- The correlation between antiproton and antineutron multiplicities at kinetic freeze-out is close to zero, reaching about 0.012 only for central collisions at large pseudorapidity acceptance windows.
- In the simple coalescence model (Model B of Feckova (2016)), the scaled moment is approximately \(C_2/C_1 \approx 1 + 2\langle N_{\bar{d}}\rangle / \langle N_{\bar{p}}\rangle\), exceeding the Poisson limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of anti-deuteron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions beyond event-averaged yields to event-by-event fluctuations, which provide a more sensitive probe of production mechanisms. The results contradict the prediction of a simple coalescence model by Feckova (2016) that assumed equal formation probability for all antiproton-antineutron pairs, which gave deviations from Poisson statistics. The findings provide quantitative benchmarks for comparison with ALICE Collaboration measurements and help distinguish between the coalescence model and the statistical hadronization model (SHM), which also predicts Poissonian fluctuations in the grand canonical ensemble but may show canonical suppression effects as in Vovchenko (2018).
