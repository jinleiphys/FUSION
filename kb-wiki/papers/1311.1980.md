# Factorization of exclusive electroinduced two-nucleon knockout

> We investigate the factorization properties of the exclusive electroinduced two-nucleon knockout reaction $A(e,e'pN)$. A factorized expression for the cross section is derived and the conditions for factorization are studied. The $A(e,e'pN)$ cross section is shown to be proportional to the conditional center-of-mass (c.m.) momentum distribution for close-proximity pairs in a state with zero relative orbital momentum and zero radial quantum number. The width of this conditional c.m. momentum distribution is larger than the one corresponding with the full c.m. momentum distribution. It is shown that the final-state interactions (FSIs) only moderately affect the shape of the factorization function for the $A(e,e'pN)$ cross sections. Another prediction of the proposed factorization is that the mass dependence of the $A(e,e'pp)$ $[A(e,e'pn)]$ cross sections is much softer than $\frac{Z(Z-1)}{2}$ $[NZ]$.

## Key claim

The exclusive $A(e,e'pN)$ cross section factorizes and becomes proportional to the conditional center-of-mass (c.m.) momentum distribution for close-proximity pairs in a state with zero relative orbital momentum and zero radial quantum number ($nl=00$), provided that final-state interactions (FSIs) only moderately affect the shape of the factorization function.

## Method

The authors derived a factorized expression for the eightfold $A(e,e'pN)$ cross section using a non-relativistic reaction model in the spectator approximation, with the effect of short-range correlations implemented through a two-body cluster approximation. They computed the conditional pair c.m. momentum distributions for pp and pn pairs in $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{208}$Pb using harmonic oscillator (HO) and Woods-Saxon (WS) single-particle bases, and performed Monte Carlo simulations for $A(e,e'pp)$ processes in kinematics accessible at Jefferson Laboratory Hall A and Hall B detectors.

## Key numbers

- The width $\sigma$ of the $P_{2,x}(P_{12,x}|nl=00)$ distribution for pp pairs in $^{12}$C is 156 MeV (HO basis) and 158 MeV (WS basis); for $^{208}$Pb it is 178 MeV (HO) and 177 MeV (WS).
- The width $\sigma$ of the full $P_{2,x}(P_{12,x})$ distribution for pp pairs in $^{12}$C is 140 MeV (HO) and 142 MeV (WS); for $^{208}$Pb it is 145 MeV (HO) and 146 MeV (WS).
- The excess kurtosis $\kappa$ of $P_{2,x}(P_{12,x}|nl=00)$ for pp pairs in $^{12}$C is $-0.25$ (HO) and $-0.28$ (WS); for $^{208}$Pb it is $-0.58$ (HO) and $-0.63$ (WS).
- The mass dependence of the $A(e,e'pp)$ and $A(e,e'pn)$ cross sections is predicted to be much softer than $\frac{Z(Z-1)}{2}$ and $NZ$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Ryckebusch (1996) for $A(e,e'pp)$ factorization to include FSIs and relax the zero-range approximation, building on prior studies of short-range correlations in nuclei from inclusive $A(e,e')$ measurements by Egiyan (2003, 2005) and Fomin (2011), and exclusive $A(e,e'pN)$ measurements by Shneor (2007) and Subedi (2008). The results enable more quantitative extraction of short-range correlation information from exclusive two-nucleon knockout experiments at Jefferson Laboratory, and provide predictions for the mass and isospin dependence of the cross sections that can be tested against future data.
