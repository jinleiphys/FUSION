# On Determining $α_s(m_Z)$ from Dijets in $e^+e^-$ Thrust

> We update a previous N$^3$LL$^\prime$+${\cal O}(α_s^3)$ determination of the strong coupling from a global fit to thrust data by including newly available perturbative ingredients, upgrading the renormalization scales to include a fully canonical scaling region, and implementing the log resummation in a way which ensures the integrated cross section is unaffected by the the leading $1/Q$ hadronization power corrections. Detailed discussions are provided concerning the stability of the results under variations of the fit range and the importance of summing up higher-order logarithmic terms for convergence and stability. We show that high-precision results can be achieved even when carrying out a more conservative fit by restricting the dataset to a region which is more clearly dominated by dijet events. This leads to $α_s(m_Z) = 0.1136 \pm 0.0012$ with $χ^2/{\rm dof}=0.86$, fully compatible with earlier results using a larger fit range. We also demonstrate that a number of additional effects associated to power corrections have a small impact on this fit result, including modifications to the renormalon substraction scheme for dijet power corrections and the inclusion of three-jet power correction models. The fit is also shown to provide very good agreement with data outside the fit range.

## Key claim

The paper updates a previous N³LL′+𝒪(αₛ³) determination of the strong coupling from thrust data, finding αₛ(m_Z) = 0.1136 ± 0.0012 with χ²/dof = 0.86 from a more conservative fit restricted to a dijet-dominated region, which is fully compatible with earlier results using a larger fit range but remains several standard deviations below the world average of about 0.118.

## Method

The authors compute the e⁺e⁻ thrust distribution using a factorization theorem in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that separates hard, jet, and soft functions, with resummation of Sudakov logarithms at N³LL′ order and full inclusion of 𝒪(αₛ³) fixed-order corrections. They implement a canonical profile function for renormalization scales, a gap subtraction scheme to remove the leading renormalon in the dijet power correction parameter Ω₁, and a shape function for non-perturbative hadronization effects, then perform a global fit to experimental thrust data from multiple e⁺e⁻ experiments at various center-of-mass energies.

## Context

This work extends the 2010 analysis of Abbate et al. (2010) by incorporating newly available perturbative ingredients (four-loop cusp anomalous dimension, three-loop jet and soft function coefficients), upgrading to canonical profile functions from Hoang et al. (2014), and implementing log resummation that preserves the integrated cross section OPE. The result addresses concerns raised by Luisoni et al. (2020) and Caola et al. (2021, 2022) regarding three-jet power corrections, demonstrating that such effects have small impact on the fit, and enables future high-precision determinations of αₛ from dijet event shapes with controlled theoretical uncertainties.
