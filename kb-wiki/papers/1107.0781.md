# Dynamical coupled-channel study of K* K*bar and omega phi states in a chiral quark model

> A dynamical coupled-channel study of K* K*bar state with isospin 0 and omega phi state is performed within both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a satisfactory description of the energies of the octet and decuplet baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts, and the hyperon-nucleon (YN) cross sections. The results show that the interactions of K* K*bar states are attractive, which consequently result in K* K*bar bound states with the binding energies of about 10-70 MeV, and contrarily, no omega phi bound state is obtained. The channel coupling effect of K* K*bar and omega phi is found to be considerably large, which makes the binding of K* K*bar 5-45 MeV deeper. The plausible interpretation of f_0(1710) and X(1812) being K* K*bar dominated states is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a dynamically generated $K^*\bar K^*$ bound state with spin $S=0$ and energy around 1720-1727 MeV is obtained in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, but only when vector meson exchanges are included; no bound state is found in the original chiral SU(3) quark model without vector meson exchanges, and no $\omega\phi$ bound state is found in either model.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical coupled-channel study of $K^*\bar K^*$ and $\omega\phi$ systems with isospin $I=0$ and spin-parity $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$ by solving the resonating group method (RGM) equation within both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model. All model parameters were taken from previous work that described baryon ground state energies, deuteron binding energy, nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and hyperon-nucleon cross sections, with no free adjustable parameters in the present calculation. The channel coupling between $K^*\bar K^*$ and $\omega\phi$ was investigated by solving coupled-channel RGM equations for both scattering and bound state problems.

## Key numbers

- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (single-channel, spin $S=0$): 28 MeV (Model II, $f_{chv}/g_{chv}=0$) and 16 MeV (Model III, $f_{chv}/g_{chv}=2/3$)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (single-channel, spin $S=1$): 61 MeV (Model II) and 42 MeV (Model III)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (single-channel, spin $S=2$): 71 MeV (Model II) and 44 MeV (Model III)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energy in the original chiral SU(3) quark model (single-channel, spin $S=2$): 11 MeV; no bound states for $S=0$ or $S=1$
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (coupled-channel, spin $S=0$): 64 MeV (Model II) and 57 MeV (Model III)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (coupled-channel, spin $S=1$): 69 MeV (Model II) and 46 MeV (Model III)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energies in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model (coupled-channel, spin $S=2$): 100 MeV (Model II) and 89 MeV (Model III)
- $K^*\bar K^*$ binding energy in the original chiral SU(3) quark model (coupled-channel, spin $S=2$): 55 MeV; no bound states for $S=0$ or $S=1$
- Channel coupling effect deepens $K^*\bar K^*$ binding by 5-45 MeV
- $K^*\bar K^*$ bound state energy for spin $S=0$ in extended chiral SU(3) quark model: 1720-1727 MeV
- $\omega\phi$ threshold is 18 MeV higher than $K^*\bar K^*$ threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson-vector meson interactions performed with a chiral unitary approach by Geng et al. (2010), which found $f_0(1710)$ as a dynamically generated $K^*\bar K^*$ dominated state, and re-scattering calculations by Zhao et al. (2006) that suggested $X(1812)$ could arise from $K^*\bar K^*$ re-scattering. The results support the interpretation of $f_0(1710)$ as a $K^*\bar K^*$ dominated state and suggest that $X(1812)$ observed in $J/\psi \to \gamma \omega \phi$ may originate from the tail of $f_0(1710)$ through $K^*\bar K^*$ re-scattering. Future work on decay widths and branching ratios is needed for further identification of these states.
