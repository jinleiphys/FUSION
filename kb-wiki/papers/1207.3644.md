# Nuclear charge radii and electric monopole transitions in the interacting boson model

> The interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello is applied to calculate nuclear charge radii and electric monopole transitions of even-even nuclei in the rare-earth region. Consistent operators are used for the two observables. A relation between summed M1 strength and $ρ({\rm E0})$ values is pointed out.

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) can account for nuclear charge radii and electric monopole (E0) transition data in the rare-earth region, provided that the same effective nucleon charges are used consistently in the operators for both observables. The paper points out a correlation between summed M1 strength and rho(E0) values, but the main claim is conditional on the assumption that the effective charges in the charge radius and E0 transition operators are identical.

## Method

The IBM-1 (interacting boson model) was applied to calculate charge radii and E0 transitions for even-even nuclei in the rare-earth region from Ce (Z=58) to W (Z=74). A consistent operator formalism was used where the charge radius operator was taken as the most general scalar expression linear in the generators of U(6), and the E0 transition operator was derived from it using the relation between effective charges. Structural information for ground and excited states was obtained by adjusting an IBM-1 Hamiltonian to observed spectra, following a procedure similar to Garcia-Ramos et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

* Isotope shift parameter eta = 0.50 fm^2, kept constant for all isotopes from Ce to W
* Root-mean square deviation for energy calculations across entire isotopic chains: typically of order 100 keV
* Largest isotope shift peaks occur for the differences between N=90 and N=88 isotopes: specifically for Sm-152 minus Sm-150, Gd-154 minus Gd-152, and Dy-156 minus Dy-154
* Isomer shifts in Sm and Gd isotopes are an order of magnitude smaller in the deformed region than in the spherical region

## Context

This work extends the IBM framework of Arima and Iachello (1976) to systematically calculate both charge radii and E0 transitions across the rare-earth region, building on earlier studies by Zerguine et al. (2008, 2012). It addresses the long-standing question of whether excited 0+ states in deformed nuclei correspond to beta vibrations (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) or alternative interpretations such as pairing isomers (Kulp et al., 2003, 2005) or shape coexistence (Kulp et al., 2008). The consistent operator approach enables future tests of the correlation between E0 strength and summed M1 strength in deformed nuclei.
