# Unitarized chiral perturbation theory of hadrons

> An exposition is made of recent developments using techniques of unitary chiral perturbation theory, $U χP T$, which allows one to extend predictions using chiral Lagrangians to higher energies than ordinary chiral perturbation theory, including the region of low lying mesonic and baryonic resonances, some of which are dynamically generated in the approach. Results for meson meson scattering, pion and kaon form factors and meson baryon scattering are shown. Applications are done for nuclear problems showing the results for the kaon and eta selfenergies, phi renormalization in a nuclear medium and $σ$ renormalization in the medium, comparing results with recent experiments.

# Unitarized chiral perturbation theory of hadrons

## Key claim

Unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UχPT) extends the predictive range of standard chiral perturbation theory to energies including the low-lying mesonic and baryonic resonances, some of which are dynamically generated rather than introduced as explicit degrees of freedom; the approach works only when the kernel is factorized on shell and the loop integrals are regularized with a cutoff of order 1 GeV or subtraction constants of natural size (around -2).

## Method

The method combines the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian amplitudes with the Bethe-Salpeter equation, factorizing the kernel on shell and using a cutoff (typically 650 MeV to 1 GeV) or equivalent subtraction constants to regularize the loop integrals. For meson-meson scattering, the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) and the N/D method are also employed, where the real part of the inverse T-matrix is expanded in powers of momenta using second-order chiral Lagrangians. The approach is extended to coupled channels for meson-baryon scattering, with unitarity imposed via dispersion relations for the inverse amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The σ meson appears around 500 MeV as the first pole limiting convergence of standard χPT for s-wave meson-meson scattering
- A cutoff of order 1 GeV is used to regularize loops in the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV (Oller 1997)
- Subtraction constants of order -2 are obtained from the relation a_i(μ) = -2 log(1 + sqrt(1 + m_i^2/μ^2)) with μ around 650 MeV to 1 GeV
- The η selfenergy potential at threshold is (54 - i29) MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- The η bound state in 24Mg has 13 MeV binding energy with a half width of order 16 MeV
- The φ meson width in nuclear medium increases by a factor of 5 to 10 times the free φ width
- The K¯ selfenergy gives a moderate attraction of order 40 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (Ramos 1999)

## Context

This work extends the earlier Lippmann-Schwinger approach of Kaiser (1995) for πN and K¯N scattering, and the Bethe-Salpeter treatment of meson-meson scattering by Oller (1997) and meson-baryon scattering by Oset (1997). It systematizes the N/D method for meson-baryon systems as developed by Oller (2000), and clarifies the distinction between genuine resonances (such as ρ and K*, which require explicit bare vector meson exchange in the kernel) and dynamically generated resonances (such as σ, f0(980), a0(980), κ(900), Λ(1405), and N*(1535), which emerge purely from unitarizing the lowest-order amplitudes). The approach enables predictions for kaon and eta selfenergies in nuclear matter, φ meson renormalization in medium, and σ meson renormalization in nuclei, with the latter confirmed by photoproduction experiments at Mainz (Messchendorp 2002).
