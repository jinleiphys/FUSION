# On the time modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI experiments

> According to experimental data at GSI, the rates of the number of daughter ions, produced by the nuclear K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of the H-like heavy ions with one electron in the K-shell, such as 142Pr58+, 142Pm60+ and 122I52+, are modulated in time with periods T_(EC)of the order of a few seconds, obeying an A-scaling T_(EC)= A/20 s, where A is the mass number of the mother nuclei, and with amplitudes a^(EC)_d ~ 0.21. We show that these data can be explained in terms of the interference of two massive neutrino mass-eigenstates. The appearance of the interference term is due to overlap of massive neutrino mass-eigenstate energies and of the wave functions of the daughter ions in two-body decay channels, caused by the energy and momentum uncertainties introduced by time differential detection of the daughter ions in GSI experiments.

## Key claim

The observed time modulation of K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI can be explained by interference of two massive neutrino mass-eigenstates, but only if the energy and momentum uncertainties introduced by the time differential detection of daughter ions (with time resolution τ_d ≈ 320 ms) cause an overlap of the neutrino mass-eigenstate energies and daughter ion wave functions.

## Method

The authors calculated the EC-decay amplitude as a coherent sum over massive neutrino mass-eigenstates using time-dependent perturbation theory, with the daughter ion described as a wave packet to account for the finite time resolution of detection. They then derived the time-dependent EC-decay rate λ_EC(t) = λ_EC (1 + a_EC cos(ω_21 t)), where ω_21 = Δm²_21 / (2 M_m) in the rest frame, and transformed to the laboratory frame using the Lorentz factor γ = 1.43. The amplitude a_EC = sin 2θ_12 was compared to the experimentally observed modulation amplitude a^(EC)_d ≈ 0.21, and the discrepancy was attributed to stochastic processes (such as preceding electromagnetic decays from hyperfine excited states) that destroy coherence for a fraction of mother ions.

## Key numbers

- Modulation periods measured at GSI: T_EC = 7.10(22) s for ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺, 7.06(8) s for ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, and 6.11(3) s for ¹²²I⁵²⁺
- Modulation amplitudes measured at GSI: a^(EC)_d = 0.23(4) for ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺, 0.18(3) for ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, and 0.22(2) for ¹²²I⁵²⁺
- Quadratic neutrino mass differences extracted from GSI data: (Δm²_21)_GSI = 2.20(7)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺), 2.18(3)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺), 2.19(1)×10⁻⁴ eV² (¹²²I⁵²⁺), with combined value 2.19×10⁻⁴ eV²
- Time resolution of daughter ion detection: τ_d ≈ 320 ms, giving energy uncertainty δE_d ≈ 1.29×10⁻¹⁴ eV and momentum uncertainty |δq_d| ≈ 1.82×10⁻¹⁴ eV
- Upper limit on β⁺-decay modulation amplitude: a_(β⁺) < 0.03 for ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺
- Lorentz factor relating laboratory and center-of-mass frames: γ = 1.43
- Phenomenological scaling: T_EC = A/20 s, with κ = 0.050(4) s

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of GSI experimental data (GSI2-GSI5, 2008-2009) on modulated EC-decay rates of H-like heavy ions, previously proposed by Giunti (2008) and Kienert et al. (2008) to arise from quantum beats of closely spaced mass-eigenstates of the mother ions. The authors' neutrino interference mechanism reproduces the observed A-scaling of modulation periods (T_EC ∝ A) that the alternative mass-splitting hypothesis could not explain, and also accounts for the absence of modulation in the β⁺-decay branch. The extracted (Δm²_21)_GSI ≈ 2.19×10⁻⁴ eV² is 2.9 times larger than the KamLAND value of 7.59(21)×10⁻⁵ eV² (KamLAND Collaboration, 2008), which the authors suggest may be resolved by neutrino mass corrections from interactions with strong Coulomb fields of daughter ions. The work enables further tests using EC-decays of He-like heavy ions and bound-state β⁻-decays of bare ions, which lack hyperfine structure and could clarify the role of stochastic coherence loss.
