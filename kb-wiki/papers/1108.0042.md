# Swallowtail Band Structure of the Superfluid Fermi Gas in an Optical Lattice

> We investigate the energy band structure of the superfluid flow of ultracold dilute Fermi gases in a one-dimensional optical lattice along the BCS to BEC crossover within a mean-field approach. In each side of the crossover region, a loop structure (swallowtail) appears in the Bloch energy band of the superfluid above a critical value of the interaction strength. The width of the swallowtail is largest near unitarity. Across the critical value of the interaction strength, the profiles of density and pairing field change more drastically in the BCS side than in the BEC side. It is found that along with the appearance of the swallowtail, there exists a narrow band in the quasiparticle energy spectrum close to the chemical potential and the incompressibility of the Fermi gas consequently experiences a profound dip in the BCS side, unlike in the BEC side.

## Key claim
Swallowtail loop structures appear in the Bloch energy band of a superfluid Fermi gas in a one-dimensional optical lattice on both sides of the BCS-BEC crossover, but only above a critical interaction strength; the width of the swallowtail is largest near unitarity, and the associated changes in density and pairing field are more drastic on the BCS side than on the BEC side.

## Method
The authors solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently for a two-component unpolarized dilute Fermi gas with s-wave contact interactions in a one-dimensional optical lattice of the form \(V_{\rm ext}(z)=sE_{\rm R}\sin^2 q_{\rm B}z\). They impose a supercurrent with wavevector \(Q=P/\hbar\) and use a Bloch decomposition to compute the energy per particle in the lowest Bloch band as a function of quasimomentum \(P\) for various values of the interaction parameter \(1/k_{\rm F}a_s\) along the BCS-BEC crossover. For comparison, they also solve the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) equation for bosonic dimers of mass \(m_b=2m\) and scattering length \(a_b=2a_s\) in the BEC limit.

## Key numbers
- The swallowtail half-width reaches a maximum near unitarity (\(1/k_{\rm F}a_s=0\)) for the parameters \(s=0.1\) and \(E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=2.5\).
- In the BCS side, the swallowtail appears above a critical value \(1/k_{\rm F}a_s \approx -0.62\) for \(s=0.1\) and \(E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=2.5\).
- The half-width of the swallowtail predicted by the GP equation vanishes at \(1/k_{\rm F}a_s \simeq 10.6\) for \(s=0.1\) and \(E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=2.5\).
- In the BCS side, for \(0.1 \le s \le 0.5\) at \(E_{\rm F}/E_{\rm R}=5\), the critical value of \(1/k_{\rm F}a_s\) changes within 30%.

## Context
This work extends the study of swallowtail band structures, previously known for Bose-Einstein condensates (Wu and Niu, 2000; Diakonov et al., 2002), to superfluid Fermi gases along the BCS-BEC crossover. It shows that swallowtails exist in fermionic superfluids with unique features distinct from the bosonic case, such as a narrow quasiparticle band near the chemical potential and a pronounced dip in incompressibility on the BCS side. The results enable experimental proposals for controlling directed motion of Fermi gases via time modulation of the band shape, and may have implications for superfluid neutrons in neutron star crusts.
