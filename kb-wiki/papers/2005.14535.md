# Speed of sound and quark confinement inside neutron stars

> Several observations of high-mass neutron stars (NSs), as well as the first historic detection of the binary neutron star merger GW170817, have delivered stringent constraints on the equation of state (EoS) of cold and dense matter. Recent studies suggest that, in order to simultaneously accommodate a $2M_\odot$ NS and the upper limit on the compactness, the pressure has to swiftly increase with density and the corresponding speed of sound likely exceeds the conformal limit. In this work, we employ a unified description of hadron-quark matter, the hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model, to investigate the EoS under NS conditions. We show that the dynamical confining mechanism of the model plays an important role in explaining the observed properties of NSs.

## Key claim

The hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model shows that the dynamical confining mechanism, which suppresses quark degrees of freedom at low densities, provides the rapid stiffening of the equation of state needed to simultaneously satisfy the high-mass neutron star constraint and the compactness limit from GW170817; this stiffening is required at densities inside a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, even before the hadron-to-quark phase transition occurs.

## Method

The authors employ the hybrid QMN model, which unifies hadronic and quark degrees of freedom within a single framework, incorporating a parity-doublet structure for nucleons and a statistical confinement mechanism via an auxiliary scalar field that acts as a momentum cutoff in Fermi-Dirac distributions. They compute the equation of state under neutron-star conditions (beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality) for four values of the confinement parameter (alpha b0 = 350, 370, 400, 450 MeV) with a fixed parity-doublet mass parameter m0 = 700 MeV, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and speed-of-sound profiles.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with binding energy E/A - m+ = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, and symmetry energy E_sym = 31 MeV.
- For alpha b0 = 350 MeV, the chiral phase transition occurs in the density range 1.82–2.60 rho0; for alpha b0 = 370 MeV, 2.14–2.76 rho0; for alpha b0 = 400 MeV, 2.61–2.92 rho0; for alpha b0 = 450 MeV, the transition is a smooth crossover at 3.56 rho0.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: radius R = 13.22 km, central density 1.59 rho0, maximal speed of sound c_s^2 = 0.468 (alpha b0 = 350 MeV); R = 12.79 km, 1.85 rho0, c_s^2 = 0.459 (370 MeV); R = 12.41 km, 2.23 rho0, c_s^2 = 0.480 (400 MeV); R = 12.29 km, 2.53 rho0, c_s^2 = 0.295 (450 MeV).
- The chiral phase transitions occur at masses around 1.8 solar masses, within the mass-radius region accessible by the GW190425 gravitational-wave event.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the hybrid QMN model (Marczenko 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020) and addresses the tension between the high-mass neutron star constraint (requiring stiff equations of state) and the compactness limit from GW170817 (favoring soft pressure), as quantified by Reed (2019) who derived a lower bound on the maximal speed of sound. The results show that the dynamical confinement mechanism produces the required stiffening at densities just above saturation, similar to effects seen in relativistic density-functional models with excluded nucleon volume (Typel 2016) and constant-speed-of-sound hybrid equations of state (Alford 2017). The model enables further study of multi-messenger astronomy and heavy-ion collisions, where a signature of chiral symmetry restoration in dilepton production was recently proposed (Sasaki 2019).
