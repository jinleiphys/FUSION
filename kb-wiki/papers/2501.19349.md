# Viscous effects of a hot QGP medium in time dependent magnetic field and their phenomenological significance

> In this work, we have studied, for the first time, the impact of a realistic picture of a time dependent electric and magnetic field on the shear and bulk viscosities of the medium. Both the electric and magnetic fields are considered to be exponentially decaying with time, and the study is valid in the regime where the magnetic field strength is weak ($eB\ll T^2$). The evaluation has been done in the kinetic theory framework wherein we have solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation time approximation collision kernel. We have shown that the constant weak field results as well as the $B=0$ results in the literature can be obtained as special cases of our general results. We have observed that the shear and bulk viscosities increase with time or equivalently, decrease with the strength of the magnetic field. To connect these observations with experiments, we have calculated the thermalization time, shear viscosity to entropy ratio ($η/s$), and bulk viscosity to entropy ratio ($ζ/s$).

# Viscous effects of a hot QGP medium in time dependent magnetic field and their phenomenological significance

## Key claim

The authors claim that for the first time, shear and bulk viscosities of a QGP medium have been calculated under exponentially decaying electric and magnetic fields, but only in the weak-field regime (eB ≪ T²) and assuming a static medium where time dependence enters exclusively through the external electromagnetic fields, not through medium expansion.

## Method

The relativistic Boltzmann transport equation was solved within the relaxation time approximation collision kernel, using an ansatz for the deviation from equilibrium distribution that incorporates time-dependent electromagnetic fields. The electric and magnetic fields were taken as exponentially decaying with time, B = B₀ exp(-t/τ_B) and E = E₀ exp(-t/τ_B), and the coupled differential equations for the magnetic-field-dependent components of the distribution function were solved using a matrix method. The shear and bulk viscosities were extracted by comparing the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor with the standard viscous tensor decomposition, including both quark and gluon contributions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: eB ∼ 10⁻¹ m_π² (∼10¹⁷ Gauss) for super proton synchrotron energies, eB ∼ m_π² for RHIC energies, and eB ∼ 15 m_π² for LHC energies (from prior work cited as Skokov (2009))
- Shear viscosity was computed at t = 2 fm/c with qB₀ = 0.01 GeV², μ = 0.01 GeV, and various temperatures
- Bulk viscosity was computed at t = 2 fm/c with qB₀ = 0.01 GeV², μ = 0.1 GeV, and various temperatures
- Magnetic field decay rate τ_B values used include 1 fm/c, 4 fm/c, and others in contour plots
- Temperature values shown include T = 0.2 GeV and T = 0.3 GeV in various figures
- Chemical potential values shown include μ = 0.01 GeV and μ = 0.1 GeV in various figures

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in constant magnetic fields, such as shear and bulk viscosities calculated using kinetic theory by Rath (2021) and others, and time-dependent magnetic field studies by K (2021, 2023) where the cyclotron frequency was previously taken equal to the inverse decay rate. The authors show that their general results reduce to the constant weak-field results and the B = 0 results from the literature as special cases. This enables more realistic hydrodynamic modeling of QGP in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where magnetic fields are known to decay with time, potentially affecting observables such as elliptic flow and transverse momentum spectra.
