# Nuclear disintegration induced by virtual photons at heavy-ion colliders

> A model of electromagnetic interaction of relativistic heavy ions which makes it possible to calculate the inclusive and exclusive characteristics of such interactions is presented. The multistep model includes (1) the single and double virtual photon absorption by a nucleus, (2) intranuclear cascades of produced hadrons and (3) statistical decay of excited residual nuclei described by evaporation, fission and multifragmentation mechanisms. Components of the model were verified by comparison of calculation results with experimental data obtained with monoenergetic photons and heavy ions at KEK-Tanashi Synchrotron and CERN SPS accelerator, respectively.

# Nuclear disintegration induced by virtual photons at heavy-ion colliders

## Key claim
The RELDIS model can predict inclusive and exclusive characteristics of electromagnetic dissociation of relativistic heavy ions, but the model's validity depends on the assumption that virtual photons from the Lorentz-boosted Coulomb field are nearly real (with $Q^2 \leq 1/R^2$) and that photonuclear reaction data from real monoenergetic photons can be used to predict electromagnetic dissociation processes.

## Method
The multistep model uses the Weizsäcker-Williams method of equivalent photons to treat the Coulomb field of a relativistic nucleus as a spectrum of virtual photons, then calculates single and double virtual photon absorption cross sections using photoabsorption data. The absorbed photons initiate an intranuclear cascade of hadrons, followed by statistical decay of the excited residual nucleus via evaporation, fission, or multifragmentation, with the entire process simulated by the RELDIS Monte Carlo code.

## Key numbers
- Mean neutron multiplicities in electromagnetic dissociation: 4.2 for PbPb collisions at SPS (158A GeV beam), 8.8 for PbPb at LHC (2.75A+2.75A TeV beams), and 7.2 for AuAu at RHIC (100A+100A GeV)
- The virtual photon spectrum extends up to maximum energy $E_{\gamma}^{max} \sim \gamma/R$, with the minimum photon energy starting at the photoneutron threshold of approximately 7 MeV
- In the Giant Resonance region, photoabsorption occurs for photon energies $6 \leq E_{\gamma} \leq 30$ MeV
- For heavy nuclei absorbing photons of several GeV, up to 95% of the photon energy is released in fast particles on average
- Multifragmentation dominates when excitation energy exceeds 3-4 MeV/nucleon
- Up to 20 neutrons may be emitted in the $\Delta Z = -2$ fragmentation channel for PbPb collisions at SPS

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of relativistic Coulomb excitation described by Bertulani and Baur, and applies the Weizsäcker-Williams method to heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The model contradicts the simpler picture assumed by Hirzebruch (where neutron and proton emission rates above the photoproton threshold were determined by the N/Z ratio of the fragmenting nucleus), instead showing that multiple neutron emission dominates in charge-changing channels. The results enable predictions for designing large-rapidity detectors and zero-degree calorimeters at RHIC and LHC, and provide a basis for separating electromagnetic and nuclear contributions in fragmentation measurements.
