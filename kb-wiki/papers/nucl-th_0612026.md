# Chiral pion-nucleon dynamics in finite nuclei: spin-isospin excitations

> The nuclear density functional framework, based on chiral dynamics and the symmetry breaking pattern of low-energy QCD, is extended to the description of collective nuclear excitations. Starting from the relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian previously introduced [Nucl. Phys. A770 (2006) 1], the proton-neutron (quasiparticle) random phase approximation is formulated and applied to investigate the role of chiral pion-nucleon dynamics in excitation modes involving spin and isospin degrees of freedom, e.g. isobaric analog states and Gamow-Teller resonances.

# Chiral pion-nucleon dynamics in finite nuclei: spin-isospin excitations

## Key claim
The nuclear density functional framework based on chiral dynamics and low-energy QCD symmetry breaking can be extended to describe collective spin-isospin excitations in finite nuclei, with the density-dependent Landau-Migdal parameter \(g_0'(k_f)\) fine-tuned to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb, while the remaining isovector couplings are completely determined by chiral pion-nucleon dynamics without further adjustment.

## Method
The authors formulate the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-QRPA) starting from the relativistic point-coupling Hartree-Bogoliubov model (FKVW) previously developed for ground-state properties. The residual particle-hole interaction includes isovector contact terms from the FKVW functional, direct one-pion exchange with \(g_A = 1.3\) and \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and a zero-range Landau-Migdal term whose density dependence is calculated from in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The \(T=1\) pairing channel uses the Gogny D1S force, while the \(T=0\) pairing channel uses a two-Gaussian interaction with strength \(V_0 = 250\) MeV. The short-distance constant \(b_5^{\sigma\tau}\) in the Landau-Migdal parameter is adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance energy in \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers
- Gamow-Teller resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb: experimental position 19.2 MeV, reproduced with \(b_5^{\sigma\tau} = -7.30\)
- Landau-Migdal parameter expansion coefficients: \(c_1 = -1.80\ \text{fm}^2 - (\pi^2/\Lambda^2) b_3\), \(c_2 = 1.17\ \text{fm}^2\), \(c_3 = -9.84\ \text{fm}^2 + (\pi^2/\Lambda^2) b_5^{\sigma\tau}\), \(c_4 = 21.13\ \text{fm}^2 - (\pi^2/\Lambda^2) b_6^{\sigma\tau}\), with \(\Lambda = 2\pi f_\pi \approx 0.58\) GeV
- Short-distance constants: \(b_3 = -2.93\), \(b_5^{\sigma\tau} = -7.30\), \(b_6^{\sigma\tau} = -8.45\)
- Isobaric analog resonance excitation energies: \(^{48}\)Ca calculated vs. experimental 7.2 MeV; \(^{90}\)Zr calculated vs. experimental 12.0 MeV; \(^{208}\)Pb calculated vs. experimental 18.8 MeV
- Gamow-Teller resonance excitation energies: \(^{48}\)Ca calculated vs. experimental 10.5 MeV; \(^{90}\)Zr calculated vs. experimental 15.6 MeV; \(^{208}\)Pb calculated vs. experimental 19.2 MeV
- Fermi sum rule exhausted to better than 99.75%
- Ikeda sum rule exhausted with 8-10% contribution from configurations including negative-energy Dirac sea states
- Pairing interaction ranges: \(\mu_1 = 1.2\) fm, \(\mu_2 = 0.7\) fm; relative strengths \(g_1 = 1\), \(g_2 = -2\); overall strength \(V_0 = 250\) MeV
- Lorentzian width for strength distributions: 1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the FKVW density functional approach (Finelli et al., 2006) from ground-state properties to charge-exchange excitations, building on the in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculation of Landau-Migdal parameters (Kaiser, 2006). The PN-QRPA implementation follows the formalism of Paar et al. (2004) and Niksic et al. (2002, 2005), but replaces phenomenological meson-exchange interactions (such as DD-ME1 used in Paar et al., 2004) with the chirally motivated point-coupling interaction. The results enable a consistent microscopic description of both ground states and collective excitations within a single chiral effective field theory framework, with the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly noted as absent due to the omission of the attractive Coulomb exchange term.
