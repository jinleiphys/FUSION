# A Pattern for the Flavor Dependence of the Quark-Gluon Interaction

> A flavor dependent kernel is constructed based on the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach of Quantum Chromodynamics. The quark-antiquark interaction is composed of a flavor dependent infrared part and a flavor independent ultraviolet part. Our model gives a successful and unified description of the light, heavy and heavy-light ground pseudoscalar and vector mesons. For the first time, our model shows that the infrared enhanced quark-antiquark interaction is stronger and wider for the lighter quark.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a flavor-dependent quark-antiquark interaction kernel within the rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, showing for the first time that the infrared-enhanced interaction is stronger and wider for lighter quarks, but only under the condition that the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is preserved to within 3% for all pseudoscalar mesons considered.

## Method

The authors build a flavor-dependent effective interaction kernel composed of a flavor-dependent infrared Gaussian term and a flavor-independent ultraviolet term that reproduces perturbative QCD. They solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using 12 free parameters (3 per flavor: current quark mass, interaction strength parameter D_f, and interaction width ω_f) fitted to 11 observables from π, K, η_c, η_b, D, D_s, and B mesons. The flavor-dependent interaction for a quark-antiquark pair of flavors f and g is taken as 𝒢^{fg}_{IR}(s) = 8π² (D_f/ω_f²)(D_g/ω_g²) exp(-s/(ω_f ω_g)), with the remaining meson masses and decay constants predicted.

## Key numbers

- For ω_u ∈ [0.45, 0.55] GeV, pseudoscalar meson masses are almost unchanged and decay constants vary within 1.2%
- M_{B_s} deviates from experiment by only 0.01 GeV (5.377 GeV predicted vs 5.367 GeV experimental)
- M_{B_c} error reduced from 0.11 GeV in original rainbow-ladder to less than 0.02 GeV (6.290 GeV predicted vs 6.275 GeV experimental)
- Decay constants f_D, f_{D_s}, f_B, f_{B_s}, f_{B_c} all deviate from lattice QCD by less than 6%
- For vector mesons, mass deviation is about 6% for ρ meson (0.724 GeV predicted vs 0.775 GeV experimental), decreasing to about 1% for heavy mesons
- Decay constant deviation is about 12% for φ meson (0.191 GeV predicted vs 0.170 GeV lattice), decreasing to less than 7% for heavy mesons
- For B*_c meson: M_{B*_c} ≈ 6.357 GeV and f_{B*_c} ≈ 0.305 GeV (compared to original rainbow-ladder values of ≈ 6.54 GeV and ≈ 0.43 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach, which was phenomenologically successful for light mesons (Maris 1997, Maris 1998, Maris 1999, Qin 2011) and heavy quarkonia (Chen 2017, Qin 2018) but failed for heavy-light mesons (Nguyen 2011, Hilger 2017). The flavor-dependent kernel solves a problem that had been surveyed for 20 years (Ivanov 1999, Roberts 2004, Ivanov 2007, Gomez-Rocha 2015, Gomez-Rocha 2016, Binosi 2019). The approach enables unified description of light, heavy, and heavy-light mesons, and is expected to be applicable to baryons (e.g., double charm baryons via Faddeev approach) and to scattering processes such as the B to π transition form factor.
