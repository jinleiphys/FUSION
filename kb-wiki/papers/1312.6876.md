# Systematic expansion for infrared oscillator basis extrapolations

> Recent work has demonstrated that the infrared effects of harmonic oscillator basis truncations are well approximated by imposing a partial-wave Dirichlet boundary condition at a properly identified radius L. This led to formulas for extrapolating the corresponding energy E_L and other observables to infinite L and thus infinite basis size. Here we reconsider the energy for a two-body system with a Dirichlet boundary condition at L to identify and test a consistent and systematic expansion for E_L that depends only on observables. We also generalize the energy extrapolation formula to nonzero angular momentum, and apply it to the deuteron. Formulas given previously for extrapolating the radius are derived in detail.

## Key claim

The infrared effects of harmonic oscillator basis truncations can be systematically expanded in powers of \(e^{-2\kappa_\infty L}\) using an analytic continuation of the S-matrix to complex momentum, yielding energy corrections that depend only on observables such as the asymptotic normalization coefficient and effective range parameters; the previously published NLO formula is corrected by including the S-matrix zero at \(-i\kappa_\infty\).

## Method

The authors derive a transcendental equation relating the binding momentum \(k_L\) for a two-body system with a Dirichlet boundary condition at radius \(L\) to the analytic continuation of the partial-wave S-matrix, \(e^{-2k_L L} = [s_0(i k_L)]^{-1}\). They expand the S-matrix about the bound-state pole using an effective range expansion centered at the binding momentum \(\kappa_\infty\), substitute a systematic expansion for \(k_L\) in powers of \(e^{-2\kappa_\infty L}\), and equate terms order-by-order to obtain the energy correction \(\Delta E_L\) up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The formulas are tested against exact solutions for square-well potentials and against realistic deuteron calculations using a chiral N\(^3\)LO potential in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis, with the oscillator parameter \(\hbar\Omega\) restricted to values above 49 MeV to eliminate ultraviolet contamination.

## Key numbers

* Square well of depth \(V_0=4\) (units with \(\hbar^2/\mu=1\), lengths in units of \(R\)): exact infinite-L energy \(\Einf = -1.5088\) (in units of \(1/R^2\)).
* Square well simulating deuteron binding energy: \(\Einf = -0.1321\) (units with \(R=1\)).
* Deuteron calculated with chiral N\(^3\)LO (500 MeV) potential: oscillator parameter restricted to \(\hbar\Omega > 49\) MeV to eliminate UV contamination.
* For \(l=2\) energy correction: \([\Delta E]_{\rm LO} = \kappa_\infty (\gamma_\infty^{(2)})^2 [(\kappa_\infty L)^2 + 3\kappa_\infty L + 3]/[(\kappa_\infty L)^2 - 3\kappa_\infty L + 3] e^{-2\kappa_\infty L}\).
* For \(l=1\) energy correction: \([\Delta E]_{\rm LO} = \kappa_\infty (\gamma_\infty^{(1)})^2 (\kappa_\infty L + 1)/(\kappa_\infty L - 1) e^{-2\kappa_\infty L}\).
* Effective range parameter \(w_2 = 0.389\) used for deuteron (from Phillips (1999)).
* Oscillator length \(L_2 = \sqrt{2(N+3/2+2)}b\) where \(b = \sqrt{\hbar/(\mu\Omega)}\) and \(N=2n+l\).
* Smallest zeros of spherical Bessel functions: \(x_0=\pi\), \(x_1\approx 4.49341\), \(x_2\approx 5.76346\).

## Context

This work extends the infrared extrapolation framework of Furnstahl (2012) and More (2013), which showed that harmonic oscillator basis truncations effectively impose a Dirichlet boundary condition at radius \(L\). The present paper provides a systematic expansion for the energy correction to NLO using S-matrix methods, correcting an inconsistent higher-order formula from earlier work, and generalizes the extrapolation to nonzero orbital angular momentum \(l>0\). The approach is analogous to Luscher's formula (1985) for lattice calculations but adapted to oscillator bases, and it enables controlled extrapolations of bound-state energies from finite-basis calculations in nuclear structure, with well-defined theoretical uncertainties. The formulas are validated against square-well models and applied to the deuteron using the chiral potential of Entem (2003).
