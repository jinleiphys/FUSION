# Strangeness production from SPS to LHC

> Global strangeness production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC is reviewed. Special emphasis is put on the comparison with the statistical model and the canonical suppression mechanism. It is shown that recent RHIC data on strange particle production as a function of centrality can be explained by a superposition of a fully equilibrated hadron gas and particle emission from single independent nucleon-nucleon collisions in the outer corona.

## Key claim

The observed strangeness enhancement from peripheral to central heavy ion collisions can be explained primarily by a geometrical core-corona superposition of a fully equilibrated hadron gas (from the core) and particle emission from single independent nucleon-nucleon collisions (from the corona), with canonical suppression playing a role only in the most peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors compare statistical model fits to measured particle multiplicities and rapidity densities from SPS and RHIC experiments, using the strangeness undersaturation parameter gamma_s as a diagnostic. They develop a core-corona superposition model where the midrapidity density of each particle species is expressed as a sum of contributions from single nucleon-nucleon collisions (corona) and a fully equilibrated hadron gas (core), with the number of single NN collisions estimated from Glauber Monte-Carlo calculations. The model is tested against STAR data on phi meson and hyperon production as a function of centrality in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The pion rapidity distribution at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV (top SPS energy) has a dispersion width of 1.3, while that of a single fireball at rest with T = 125 MeV is about 0.8.
- The measured rapidity width at RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is 2.1, which is reasonably larger than 0.8.
- The ratio N_s/N_W for the number of single NN collisions is fitted for N_W > 10 by the formula N_s/N_W = 1/N_W^0.08 - 0.58.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC is constant at 165 MeV across all centralities.
- At SPS, gamma_s is approximately 0.85 in central collisions, significantly lower than at RHIC where gamma_s attains its maximal value of 1.

## Context

This work extends the core-corona superposition idea previously applied by Becattini and collaborators (2004) to central C-C, Si-Si, and Pb-Pb collisions at top SPS energy, and by Bozek (2005) to reproduce the K/pi ratio as a function of centrality in Au-Au collisions. The paper contradicts the canonical suppression mechanism as the primary explanation for strangeness undersaturation, arguing that the strangeness correlation volume would need to reach saturation only in central RHIC collisions, which the authors consider an unnatural coincidence. The results enable a geometrical interpretation of strangeness enhancement and suggest that the constancy of chemical freeze-out temperature with centrality, first observed by the STAR collaboration, is hard to reconcile with collisional thermalization as pointed out by Heinz (2005).
