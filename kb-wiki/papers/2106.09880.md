# Accelerated quantum Monte Carlo with mitigated error on noisy quantum computer

> Quantum Monte Carlo and quantum simulation are both important tools for understanding quantum many-body systems. As a classical algorithm, quantum Monte Carlo suffers from the sign problem, preventing its application to most fermion systems and real time dynamics. In this paper, we introduce a novel non-variational algorithm using quantum simulation as a subroutine to accelerate quantum Monte Carlo by easing the sign problem. The quantum subroutine can be implemented with shallow circuits and, by incorporating error mitigation, can reduce the Monte Carlo variance by several orders of magnitude even when the circuit noise is significant. As such, the proposed quantum algorithm is applicable to near-term noisy quantum hardware.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm (quantum-circuit Monte Carlo, QCMC) that uses shallow quantum circuits as a subroutine to ease the sign problem in quantum Monte Carlo, claiming that the Monte Carlo variance can be reduced by several orders of magnitude even when the quantum computer has significant noise, provided the gate error rate is below a threshold determined by the classical algorithm's variance growth rate.

## Method
The authors express the real-time evolution operator as an exact summation over unitary operators with complex coefficients, using two series of formulas: Pauli-operator-expansion (POE) and leading-order-rotation (LOR) formulas derived from Lie-Trotter-Suzuki product formulas. The quantum computer evaluates transition amplitudes for randomly sampled circuit configurations, while the classical computer performs the Monte Carlo summation; error mitigation is applied via probabilistic error cancellation or forward-backward circuits to handle noise on near-term hardware.

## Key numbers
- For the second-order LOR formula, the rate of increase of variance has an upper bound gamma approximately equal to 2.45 times epsilon to the power 0.82, where epsilon is the total gate error rate of one elementary Lie-Trotter-Suzuki product.
- Compared with Green's function Monte Carlo (which has gamma_c = 1 for a large class of qubit Hamiltonians), QCMC reduces the variance by a factor of approximately 4 times 10 to the 4 when h_tot t = 4 and epsilon = 0.1.
- The second-order LOR formula converges as O(Delta t to the 6), which is faster than the fourth-order product formula.
- The normalisation factor C_A for the zeroth-order POE formula has C_L = h_tot Delta t and C_T = e to the (h_tot Delta t) minus (1 + h_tot Delta t).

## Context
This work extends the line of hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for many-body simulation, specifically addressing the sign problem that limits classical quantum Monte Carlo methods such as Green's function Monte Carlo (Carlson 2015), auxiliary-field Monte Carlo (Blankenbecler 1981, Lee 2009), and diagrammatic Monte Carlo (Houcke 2010). The algorithm is designed for near-term noisy quantum hardware, in contrast to fault-tolerant approaches like those of Berry (2015) and Babbush (2018), and avoids the barren plateau problem of variational quantum algorithms (McClean 2018). The framework enables future work on eigenstate and finite-temperature problems via quantum phase estimation and eigenenergy filtering.
