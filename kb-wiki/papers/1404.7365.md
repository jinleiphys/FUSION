# Dual Origin of Pairing in Nuclei

> An essentially "complete" description of the low-energy nuclear structure of the superfluid nucleus $^{120}$Sn and of its odd-$A$ neighbors is provided by the observations carried out with the help of Coulomb excitation and of one-- and of two-- particle transfer reactions, specific probes of vibrations, quasiparticle and pairing degrees of freedom respectively, and of their mutual couplings. These experimental findings are used to stringently test the predictions of a similarly "complete" description of $^{119,120,121}$Sn carried out in terms of elementary modes of excitation which, through their interweaving, melt together into effective fields, each displaying properties reflecting that of all others, there individuality resulting from the actual relative importance of each one. Its implementation is done by solving the Nambu-Gor'kov equations including, for the first time, all medium polarization effects resulting from the interweaving of quasiparticles, spin and surface vibrations, taking into account, within the framework of nuclear field theory (NFT), the variety of processes leading to self-energy, vertex and Pauli principle corrections, and to the induced pairing interaction. Theory provides an overall quantitative account of the experimental findings. From these results one can, not only obtain strong circumstantial evidence for the inevitability for the dual origin of pairing in nuclei but also, extract information which can be used at profit to quantitatively disentangle the contributions to pairing correlations in general and to the pairing gap in particular, arising from the bare and from the induced pairing interactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides strong circumstantial evidence that pairing in typical superfluid nuclei like \(^{119,120,121}\)Sn has a dual origin, in which the bare nucleon-nucleon pairing interaction and the pairing interaction induced by the exchange of vibrational modes play an essentially equal role, with the caveat that density modes contribute attractively while spin modes contribute repulsively, partially canceling each other.

## Method

The authors solved the Nambu-Gor'kov equations for \(^{120}\)Sn and its odd-\(A\) neighbors, including for the first time all medium polarization effects from the interweaving of quasiparticles, spin vibrations, and surface vibrations within the framework of nuclear field theory (NFT). They diagonalized the bare pairing interaction \(v^{bare}_p\) in the BCS approximation using a Hartree-Fock basis from the SLy4 interaction, calculated vibrations via QRPA with separable multipole-multipole interactions and the spin-dependent part of SLy4, and then computed self-energy, vertex, and Pauli principle corrections to obtain the dressed quasiparticle states and the induced pairing interaction \(v^{ind}_p\). The total effective pairing interaction \(v^{eff}_p = v^{bare}_p + v^{ind}_p\) was then used to solve the Nambu-Gor'kov equations self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The strength of the bare pairing interaction was fixed to \(G = 0.22\) MeV, fitting the pairing gap calculated by Lesinski et al. (2012).
- The contributions of \(v^{bare}_p\) and \(v^{ind}_p\) to the pairing gap \(\tilde \Delta_{\nu}\) are about equal.
- The BCS pairing gap calculated with \(v^{bare}_p\) alone is \(\Delta^{BCS} = 1.08\) MeV (horizontal line in Fig. 2b).
- The empirical pairing gap from the three-point odd-even mass difference is 1.46 MeV (arrow on vertical axis in Fig. 2b).
- For the \(^{120}\)Sn(p,d)\(^{119}\)Sn(5/2\(^+\)) reaction, theory predicts four \(d_{5/2}\) fragments below 2 MeV with summed integrated cross section \(\sum^4_{i=1} \sigma(2^{\circ}-55^{\circ}) \approx 6.2\) mb, while experiment observes five fragments with \(\sum^5_{i=1} \sigma(2^{\circ}-55^{\circ}) \approx 8 \pm 2\) mb.
- For the \(^{120}\)Sn(p,t)\(^{118}\)Sn(gs) two-neutron transfer reaction, theory gives an integrated cross section \(\sigma(7.6^{\circ}-69.7^{\circ}) = 2360\) \(\mu\)b, reproducing the experimental value of \(2250 \pm 338\) \(\mu\)b within errors.

## Context

This work extends the NFT renormalization program pioneered by Bohr, Mottelson, Bes, and coworkers (Mottelson, 1976) and provides the first complete implementation of that program for a superfluid nucleus. It builds on prior calculations of pairing in \(^{120}\)Sn by Dobaczewski (2013), Avogadro (2013), Afdeenkov (2013), and Tarpanov (2014), and uses the bare pairing gap from Lesinski et al. (2012) and Holt (2013). The results enable a quantitative protocol to disentangle the contributions of the bare and induced pairing interactions to pairing correlations and the pairing gap, using observables such as multiplet splitting, \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities, and quasiparticle fragmentation in transfer reactions.
