# Parity Doubling and SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R Restoration in the Hadron Spectrum

> We construct the most general nonlinear representation of chiral SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R broken down spontaneously to the isospin SU(2), on a pair of hadrons of same spin and isospin and opposite parity. We show that any such representation is equivalent, through a hadron field transformation, to two irreducible representations on two hadrons of opposite parity with different masses and axial couplings. This implies that chiral symmetry realized in the Nambu-Goldstone mode does not predict the existence of degenerate multiplets of hadrons of opposite parity nor any relations between their couplings or masses.

# Parity Doubling and SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R Restoration in the Hadron Spectrum

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry realized in the Nambu-Goldstone mode (with massless pions) does not predict degenerate multiplets of hadrons of opposite parity, nor any relations between their masses or axial couplings, because any nonlinear representation mixing opposite-parity states can be transformed into two decoupled standard nonlinear representations with independent masses and couplings. The authors show that parity doubling, if observed, must be a manifestation of additional dynamics beyond chiral symmetry alone, not a consequence of the SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R symmetry of QCD.

## Method

The authors construct the most general nonlinear representation of chiral SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R broken spontaneously to isospin SU(2) on a pair of hadrons with same spin and isospin but opposite parity, parameterized by three functions (h_1, h_2, v) of the pion field squared. They impose the Jacobi identity to derive consistency conditions that restrict these functions to lie on an ellipse at each value of the pion field, then demonstrate that any such representation can be mapped via a continuous field redefinition to the standard nonlinear (parity-conserving) realization where opposite-parity states transform independently. They illustrate the result by constructing the explicit Lagrangian for spin-1/2 baryons transforming linearly under axial rotations and showing that chirally invariant operators of dimension d ≤ 4 break the degeneracy and allow arbitrary axial couplings.

## Key numbers

- The consistency conditions from the Jacobi identity yield two equations: 2vf + h_1^2 + xv^2 = 1 and vg + 2v'(f + xg) + h_1h_2 = v^2, where x = π^aπ^a and f, g define the pion transformation.
- The linear representation corresponds to (h_1, h_2, v) = (1, 0, 0) or equivalently θ(x) = ±arctan(x/f(x)).
- The standard nonlinear (SNL) representation corresponds to (h_1, h_2, v) = (0, 0, v_0(x)) with v_0(x) = 1 in the chosen convention, or θ(x) = 0.
- The explicit field redefinition mapping the linear representation to two decoupled nonlinear fields is: \tilde B = (B - 2iπ^a t^a B')/√(1+π^2), \tilde B' = (B' - 2iπ^a t^a B)/√(1+π^2).
- The mass eigenstates after including the chirally invariant m_1 term have masses m_0 - m_1 and m_0 + m_1, with the off-diagonal axial charge equal to 1 - c_3.

## Context

This paper extends the classic analysis of Coleman, Callan, Wess, and Zumino (1969) on nonlinear representations of Lie algebras, providing an explicit construction that clarifies why parity doubling cannot follow from chiral symmetry alone. It directly contradicts claims in the literature (e.g., parity doubling papers cited as "paritydoubling" and "chiraldoubling") that chiral symmetry restoration explains the approximate degeneracy of opposite-parity hadrons. The work enables a companion paper (Jaffe, Pirjol, Scardicchio, 2005) that examines the actual data on parity doubling among baryons and considers alternative dynamical explanations, including restoration of U(1)_A symmetry. A note added after publication clarifies that hadrons which do not couple to pions could still form linear representations, as proposed by Glozman and collaborators.
