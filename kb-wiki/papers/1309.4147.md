# $N^*(1920)(1/2^+)$ state in the $NK\bar{K}$ system

> We study the three body $N \bar{K} K$ system by using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, taking the interaction between $N$ and $\bar{K}$, $N$ and $K$, and $\bar{K}$ and $K$ from the chiral unitary approach. Our results suggest that a $N\bar{K}K$ hadron state, with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$, and mass around 1920 MeV, can be formed.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a resonant $N\bar{K}K$ hadron state with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$ and mass around 1920 MeV can be formed, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the result depends on the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations and on using two-body interactions derived from the chiral unitary approach, and the authors note that peaks around 1950 MeV seen in one configuration may be diluted when other wave function components are considered.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body $N\bar{K}K$ system using the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating the $f_0/a_0(980)$ meson as a bound state of $\bar{K}K$ and the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a bound state of $\bar{K}N$. They used unitarized two-body scattering amplitudes for $N\bar{K}$, $NK$, and $\bar{K}K$ from the chiral unitary approach, and computed the total three-body scattering amplitude $T$ for total isospin $I=1/2$ and spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$, identifying resonances as peaks in $|T|^2$.

## Key numbers

- A clear peak in the $Na_0 \to Na_0$ scattering amplitude appears around 1915 MeV.
- A clear peak in the $K\Lambda(1405) \to K\Lambda(1405)$ scattering amplitude appears around 1925 MeV.
- The ratio $|\frac{M_{a_0(980)}}{m_K} T_{K\Lambda(1405) \to K\Lambda(1405)}|^2 \simeq 4 |T_{Na_0 \to Na_0}|^2$ indicates the $K\Lambda(1405)$ configuration is dominant.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $N\bar{K}K$ system that found a bound state with $I=1/2$ and $J^P=1/2^+$ using nonrelativistic three-body variational calculations (Jido, 2010) and by solving Faddeev equations in a coupled channel approach (Alberto, 2010). The result supports the existence of a $N^*$ state around 1920 MeV and enables further investigation of three-body hadron resonances using the FCA method, which has previously been applied to multi-$\rho$ states and the $\pi\Delta\rho$ system (Xie, 2011).
