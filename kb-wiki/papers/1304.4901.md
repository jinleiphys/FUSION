# Lifetime and fragment correlations for the two-neutron decay of $^{26}$O ground state

> The structure and decay of $^{26}$O are investigated in a three-body $^{24}$O+$n+n$ model suitable for studies of the long-lived (including radioactivity timescale) states. We have found extremely strong effect of the subbarrier configuration mixing on the decay width of true $2n$ emitters due to core recoil and neutron-neutron final state interaction. This effect is far exceeding analogous effect in the true $2p$ emitters. Our calculations provide reasonably narrow boundaries for the lifetime vs.\ decay energy dependence for the true $2n$ emission. An upper limit of $\sim 1$ keV for the decay energy of the unbound $^{26}$O is inferred based on the recent experimental lifetime value.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the decay width of true two-neutron emitters like $^{26}$O is dramatically increased by subbarrier configuration mixing due to core recoil and neutron-neutron final state interaction, an effect far exceeding analogous effects in true two-proton emitters; based on the experimental lifetime from Kohley (2013), the authors infer an upper limit of approximately 1 keV for the decay energy of $^{26}$O.

## Method

The authors use a three-body hyperspherical harmonics cluster model for the $^{24}$O+$n$+$n$ system, solving the homogeneous Schrödinger equation with box boundary conditions to find eigenfunctions, then solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation with an arbitrary width to obtain a wave function with pure outgoing three-body asymptotics, and finally computing the actual width from the outgoing flux and internal normalization. Simplified Woods-Saxon and Gaussian potentials are used for the $^{24}$O-$n$ and $n$-$n$ interactions, with parameters adjusted to reproduce known properties of $^{25}$O and to explore the effects of core recoil, $n$-$n$ final state interaction, and occupied orbital repulsion.

## Key numbers

- The experimental half-life of $^{26}$O is reported as $T_{1/2}=4.5^{+1.1}_{-1.5}$(stat)$\pm 3$(sys) ps by Kohley (2013).
- An upper limit of approximately 1 keV is inferred for the decay energy of $^{26}$O based on the experimental lifetime.
- The ground state decay energy of $^{25}$O is $770^{+20}_{-10}$ keV with a width of $172 \pm 30$ keV, from Hoffman (2008).
- Experimental upper limits for the decay energy of $^{26}$O are $150^{+50}_{-150}$ keV from Lunderberg (2012) and less than 120 keV from Caesar (2012).
- The calculated valence neutron rms radius in $^{26}$O is around 5.7 fm.
- The width calculations show a factor of 50 difference between the "strong repulsion" and "no repulsion in $p$-wave" cases.
- The $n$-$n$ potential parameters are $V_0=-31$ MeV and $r_0=1.8$ fm; the $d$-wave $^{24}$O-$n$ potential has depth $V_d=-35.5$ MeV, radius $r_0=3.5$ fm, and diffuseness $a=0.75$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the semianalytical model of Grigorenko (2011) for true two-neutron emitters by performing detailed three-body calculations for $^{26}$O, which was identified as a candidate for two-neutron radioactivity. The results contradict the earlier bound-state prediction for $^{26}$O by Ren (1995) and provide much narrower lifetime-versus-energy boundaries than the earlier estimates. The study enables future experimental searches for two-neutron radioactivity using precision measurements of neutron angular correlations in reactions with relativistic secondary beams, as proposed by Grigorenko (2011).
