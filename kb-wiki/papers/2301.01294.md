# Energy dependence of heavy-ion initial condition in isobar collisions

> Collisions of isobar nuclei, those with the same mass number but different structure parameters, provide a new way to probe the initial condition of the heavy ion collisions. Using transport model simulation of $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions at two energies $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=0.2$ TeV and 5.02 TeV, where $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr nuclei have significantly different deformations and radial profiles, we identify sources of eccentricities contributing independently to the final state harmonic flow $v_n$. The efficacy for flow generation differs among these sources, and explains the modest energy dependence of the isobar ratios of $v_n$. Additionally, a significant component of $v_n$ is found to be uncorrelated with the eccentricity but is instead generated dynamically during system evolution. Experimental measurement of these ratios at the LHC energy and comparison with RHIC energy can provide insight into the collision-energy dependence of the initial condition.

## Key claim

The isobar ratios of harmonic flow $v_n$ between $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions show a modest energy dependence between $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=0.2$ TeV and 5.02 TeV, with stronger sensitivity to nuclear structure differences at the higher energy, but the ratios are robust probes because the effects of nuclear structure are captured entirely by the participant-plane flow component $v_{n,\mathrm{pp}}$ and not by the flow component uncorrelated with initial eccentricity.

## Method

The authors performed transport model simulations using the AMPT model (string-melting mode with 3.0 mb partonic cross-section) for $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=0.2$ TeV and 5.02 TeV. They varied four nuclear structure parameters (quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$, octupole deformation $\beta_3$, half-width radius $R_0$, and surface diffuseness $a_0$) in five configurations to isolate the influence of each parameter on the charged particle multiplicity distribution $p(N_{\mathrm{ch}})$, harmonic flow $v_2$ and $v_3$, and their isobar ratios. They decomposed the flow into components correlated with the participant-plane eccentricity ($v_{n,\mathrm{pp}}$), the reaction-plane eccentricity ($v_{n,\mathrm{rp}}$), and a residual component $\delta_n$ uncorrelated with initial geometry.

## Key numbers

- The charged particle multiplicity at 1% centrality at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV is a factor of 3.9 larger than at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=0.2$ TeV, requiring a scale factor of 1/3.9 to match the $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ distributions at the two energies.
- The $v_n\{2\}$ values are significantly larger at the LHC energy than at RHIC energy, with $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_3\{2\}$ decreasing by about 20-30% from $|\eta|<1$ to $3.5<|\eta|<5$ at the LHC.
- The response coefficients for elliptic flow differ between the LHC and RHIC by about 20-30%, with $k_2\{\mathrm{rp}\}$ and $k_2\{\beta_3\}$ showing larger values toward central collisions.
- The values of $\varepsilon_n\{\beta_n\}$ and $v_n\{\beta_n\}$ increase nearly linearly with $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ for both $\beta_2=0.16$ and $\beta_3=0.2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isobar collisions by the STAR Collaboration (Jia 2021, Xu 2021, Nijs 2021) and theoretical work by Giacalone (2021) and Bally (2022), which established that isobar ratios of bulk observables are sensitive to nuclear structure differences and insensitive to final-state effects. The paper provides the first systematic study of the energy dependence of these ratios using the AMPT model, showing that the response coefficients for different sources of eccentricity have different values and different energy dependencies. This enables future experimental measurements at the LHC to be compared with RHIC data from the STAR Collaboration to probe the collision-energy dependence of the initial condition, and suggests that similar studies in smaller systems such as $p$/$d$/$^{3}$He+Au collisions could further constrain the role of dynamical flow generation.
