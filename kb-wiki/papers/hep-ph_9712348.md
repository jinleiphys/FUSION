# Using Drell-Yan Processes to Probe Nucleon and Meson Structure Functions

> We investigate how Drell-Yan processes can be used to measure the magnitude of flavor symmetry violation in the proton sea. We examine the utility of the following beams: protons, charged pions, and charged kaons. In each case we present an approximate expression for the Drell-Yan asymmetry. Using currently available parton distributions, we locate those kinematic regions which provide the greatest information on the quantity $\bar{d}^p(x) - \bar{u}^p(x)$. If sufficiently intense $K^+$ beams were available, they could provide an efficient measurement of this quantity. Finally we present and discuss sets of sum rules for the Drell-Yan processes.

# Using Drell-Yan Processes to Probe Nucleon and Meson Structure Functions

## Key claim

Drell-Yan asymmetries measured with proton and charged meson beams on hydrogen and deuterium targets can reveal the magnitude of flavor symmetry violation in the proton sea, quantified by $\bar{d}^p(x) - \bar{u}^p(x)$, but the sensitivity depends critically on the choice of projectile: proton and $K^+$ beams are effective probes, while $K^-$, $\pi^+$, and $\pi^-$ beams are insensitive because their valence antiquark content dilutes the signal relative to the noise from valence quark distributions.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the Drell-Yan asymmetry $A^{DY}_B(x_1,x_2) = 2\sigma_{Bp}/\sigma_{BD} - 1$ for beams of protons, charged pions, and charged kaons incident on hydrogen and deuterium targets, using charge symmetry to relate neutron and proton parton distributions. They computed these asymmetries numerically using the Gluck, Reya and Vogt (GRV) parton distributions at $Q^2 = 20$ GeV$^2$, and produced contour plots over the kinematic variables $x_1$ (beam momentum fraction) and $x_2$ (target momentum fraction) to identify regions maximizing sensitivity to $\bar{d}^p(x) - \bar{u}^p(x)$. They also derived sum rules relating integrals of Drell-Yan cross section differences to valence parton distributions in the projectile mesons.

## Key numbers

- The NA51 collaboration measured $\bar{u}^p(x)/\bar{d}^p(x) = 0.51 \pm 0.04 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.05 (\text{syst})$ at $\langle x \rangle = 0.18$, providing the experimental benchmark for flavor asymmetry in the proton sea.
- For a proton beam at $x_1 = x_2 = 0.2$, the predicted asymmetry $A^{DY}_p$ is approximately $-10\%$, consistent with the NA51 measurement; at $x_1 = 0.3$ and $x_2 = 0.2$, the asymmetry would be approximately $-20\%$, while at $x_1 = 0.1$ and $x_2 = 0.2$ it would be nearly zero.
- For a $K^+$ beam with equal-$x$ kinematics, the asymmetry $A^{DY}_{K^+}$ shows a large difference (up to approximately 0.3-0.4) between the flavor-asymmetric and flavor-symmetric cases for $x$ between 0.1 and 0.4, making $K^+$ an excellent probe if sufficiently intense beams were available.
- The sum rule $\int \int (H^{DY}_{\bar{p}p} - H^{DY}_{pp}) dx_1 dx_2 = 17/9$ is presented, with the note that QCD corrections multiply all sum rule right-hand sides by the Drell-Yan $K$ factor of approximately 2.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Ellis and Stirling (1991) to use proton-induced Drell-Yan processes for measuring the flavor asymmetry in the proton sea, which was motivated by the New Muon Collaboration's discovery of a violation of the Gottfried sum rule (Amaudruz et al., 1991; Arneodo et al., 1994). The authors build on the NA51 measurement (Baldit et al., 1994) and the contemporaneous E866 experiment at FNAL (Hawker et al., 1997) by systematically comparing the utility of different beam species. The paper enables future experiments with kaon beams, particularly after the Main Injector upgrade at FNAL scheduled for 1999, to measure both the proton sea asymmetry and the poorly known kaon structure functions. The sum rules extend earlier work by Hwa (1978) and Sarma (1980) on isolating valence contributions in Drell-Yan processes.
