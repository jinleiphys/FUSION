# Probing Transverse-Momentum Dependent Evolution With Groomed Jets

> We propose an observable which involves measuring the properties (transverse momentum $p_{h\perp}$ and energy fraction $z_h$) of an identified hadron inside a groomed jet. The jet is identified with an anti-kT/CA algorithm and is groomed by implementing the modified mass drop procedure with an energy cut-off parameter $z_{cut}$. The transverse momentum of the hadron inside the jet is measured with respect to the groomed jet axis. We obtain a factorization theorem in the framework of Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), to define a Transverse Momentum Dependent Fragmenting Jet Function (TMDFJF). The TMDFJF is factorized into collinear and collinear soft modes by matching onto SCET$_+$. We resum large logarithms in $E_J/p_{h\perp}$, where $E_J$ is the ungroomed jet energy, to NLL accuracy and apply this formalism for computing the shape of the $p_{h\perp}$ distribution of a pion produced in an $e^+ +e^-$ collision. We observe that the introduction of grooming makes this observable insensitive to non-global logarithms and particularly sensitive to non-perturbative physics of the transverse momentum dependent evolution at low values of $p_{h\perp}$, which can be probed in the variation of the cut-off parameter $z_{cut}$ of the groomer. We discuss how this observable can be used to distinguish between non-perturbative models that describe universal TMD evolution and provide a window into the three dimensional structure of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the transverse momentum spectrum of an identified hadron inside a groomed jet (using the modified mass drop procedure) provides direct access to the universal transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) evolution, because the grooming procedure removes sensitivity to non-global logarithms and makes the observable particularly sensitive to non-perturbative TMD physics, which can be probed by varying the grooming parameter \(z_{\text{cut}}\).

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem in Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the cross section of an identified hadron inside a jet that has been groomed using the modified mass drop/soft-drop algorithm with energy cutoff parameter \(z_{\text{cut}}\). They define a Transverse Momentum Dependent Fragmenting Jet Function (TMDFJF) which is further factorized into collinear and collinear-soft modes by matching onto SCET\(_+\). Large logarithms in \(E_J/p_{h\perp}\) (where \(E_J\) is the ungroomed jet energy) are resummed to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, and the formalism is applied to compute the \(p_{h\perp}\) distribution of a pion produced in \(e^+e^-\) collisions.

## Key numbers

* The grooming parameter \(z_{\text{cut}}\) is formally taken to satisfy \(z_{\text{cut}} \ll 1\), with typical choice \(z_{\text{cut}} \sim 0.1\).
* The resummation is performed to NLL accuracy, using the cusp anomalous dimension coefficients \(\Gamma_0 = 4\) and \(\Gamma_1 = 4C_A(67/9 - \pi^2/3) - (80/9)T_R n_f\).
* Non-perturbative contributions are stated to be highly suppressed for \(k_T \gtrsim 4\) GeV, allowing the resummed spectrum to be evaluated directly in momentum space at NLL accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the study of TMD evolution from traditional processes (Drell-Yan, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and \(e^+e^-\) hemisphere jets) to groomed jets at hadron colliders, where non-global logarithms and underlying event contamination previously threatened universality. It builds on the factorization arguments of Frye et al. (2016) for groomed jet substructure and the SCET framework of Chiu et al. (2012) for TMD evolution. The observable enables distinguishing between non-perturbative models of universal TMD evolution and provides a window into the three-dimensional structure of hadrons, with the grooming parameter \(z_{\text{cut}}\) serving as a tunable probe of non-perturbative physics.
