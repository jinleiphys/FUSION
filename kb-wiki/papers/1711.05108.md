# Covariant Extension of the GPD overlap representation at low Fock states

> We present a novel approach to compute Generalized Parton Distributions within the Lightfront Wave Function overlap framework. We show how to systematically extend Generalized Parton Distributions computed within the DGLAP region to the ERBL one, fulfilling at the same time both the polynomiality and positivity conditions. We exemplify our method using pion Lightfront Wave Functions inspired by recent results of non-perturbative continuum techniques and algebraic nucleon Lightfront Wave Functions. We also test the robustness of our algorithm on reggeized phenomenological parameterizations. This approach paves the way to a better understanding of the nucleon structure from non-perturbative techniques and to a unification of Generalized Parton Distributions and Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Functions phenomenology through Lightfront Wave Functions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method to extend Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) computed in the DGLAP kinematic region to the ERBL region using the overlap representation of Lightfront Wave Functions, while simultaneously satisfying both polynomiality and positivity conditions, a problem that previous models had only solved in particular cases.

## Method

The authors exploit the mathematical equivalence between polynomiality of GPD Mellin moments and the Radon transform representation via Double Distributions (DDs). They develop a numerical algorithm that inverts the Radon transform with incomplete data, taking GPDs computed from Lightfront Wave Function overlaps in the DGLAP region as input and producing a covariant extension into the ERBL region. The method is tested on pion Lightfront Wave Functions derived from Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approaches, algebraic nucleon Lightfront Wave Functions, and reggeized phenomenological parameterizations.

## Context

This work extends the covariant extension approach previously applied only in particular cases by Hwang (2007) and Mueller (2014) to a general solution applicable to any Lightfront Wave Function model. It addresses the long-standing tension between positivity (naturally satisfied in Fock state overlap representations) and polynomiality (naturally satisfied in Double Distribution approaches), which had forced model builders to favor one property at the risk of violating the other. The method enables future unification of GPD and Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Function phenomenology through Lightfront Wave Functions, and provides a framework for building GPD models that satisfy all theoretical constraints simultaneously, which is directly relevant to experimental programs at Jefferson Laboratory and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
