# Kinetic equations and anisotropic hydrodynamics for quark and gluon fluids

> The mixture of quark and gluon fluids is studied in a one-dimensional boost-invariant setup using the set of relativistic kinetic equations treated in the relaxation time approximation. Effects of a finite quark mass, non-zero baryon number density, and quantum statistics are discussed. Comparisons between the exact kinetic-theory results and anisotropic hydrodynamics predictions are performed and a very good agreement between the two are found.

## Key claim

The paper claims that anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) predictions for the pressure anisotropy of a quark-gluon mixture show "very good agreement" with exact kinetic-theory results, but this agreement is demonstrated only for a one-dimensional boost-invariant setup with a constant relaxation time and specific initial conditions, and the best agreement is found using a specific choice of the mixing parameter (alpha=1 or alpha=0) in the zeroth-moment equations.

## Method

The authors solve coupled relativistic kinetic equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) with a constant relaxation time, assuming Bjorken flow symmetry (boost-invariance and transverse homogeneity). They obtain exact formal solutions for the distribution functions and use these to compute thermodynamic quantities such as energy density, transverse pressure, and longitudinal pressure. Separately, they formulate an anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework by taking zeroth, first, and second moments of the kinetic equations with the Romatschke-Strickland (RS) anisotropic distribution functions as leading-order ansatz, and close the system using Landau matching conditions for baryon number and energy density.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass values used: m = 300 MeV and m = 1 MeV (gluons always massless)
- Initial baryon number densities: B0 = 0.001 fm^{-3} and B0 = 1 fm^{-3}
- Initial transverse momentum scales for quarks and gluons: Lambda_Q^0 = Lambda_G^0 = 1 GeV
- Constant relaxation time: tau_eq = 0.25 fm
- Initial anisotropy parameters: oblate-oblate configuration with xi_Q^0 = 1 and xi_G^0 = 10; prolate-prolate configuration with xi_Q^0 = -0.5 and xi_G^0 = -0.25
- Degeneracy ratio r = k_G/k_Q = 4/3

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quark-gluon mixtures within kinetic theory (Florkowski 2012, Florkowski 2013, Florkowski 2014, Florkowski 2015) by including finite quark mass, non-zero baryon number density, and quantum statistics effects. It also builds on the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski (2010) and Martinez (2010), which treats large pressure anisotropy at leading order in the hydrodynamic expansion. The results enable testing the validity of hydrodynamic formalisms for early-stage quark-gluon plasma evolution in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and show that aHydro reproduces exact kinetic theory results better than standard Navier-Stokes viscous hydrodynamics in the far-from-equilibrium regime.
