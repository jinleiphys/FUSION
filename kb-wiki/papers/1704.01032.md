# Strong-coupling corrections to ground-state properties of a superfluid Fermi gas

> We theoretically present an economical and convenient way to study ground-state properties of a strongly interacting superfluid Fermi gas. Our strategy is that complicated strong-coupling calculations are used only to evaluate quantum fluctuation corrections to the chemical potential $μ$. Then, without any further strong-coupling calculations, we calculate the compressibility, sound velocity, internal energy, pressure, and Tan's contact, from the calculated $μ$ without loss of accuracy, by using exact thermodynamic identities. Using a recent precise measurement of $μ$ in a superfluid $^6$Li Fermi gas, we show that an extended $T$-matrix approximation (ETMA) is suitable for our purpose, especially in the BCS-unitary regime, where our results indicate that many-body corrections are dominated by superfluid fluctuations. Since precise determinations of physical quantities are not always easy in cold Fermi gas physics, our approach would greatly reduce experimental and theoretical efforts toward the understanding of ground-state properties of this strongly interacting Fermi system.

## Key claim

The authors propose that ground-state properties of a strongly interacting superfluid Fermi gas can be obtained with high accuracy by using an extended T-matrix approximation (ETMA) only to compute the chemical potential, then deriving all other quantities from exact thermodynamic identities, provided the ETMA chemical potential agrees with experimental data in the BCS-unitary regime.

## Method

The authors first compute the chemical potential μ and superfluid order parameter Δ self-consistently within ETMA, which includes superfluid fluctuations via a 2×2-matrix self-energy in the Nambu representation. They then use exact thermodynamic identities to derive the isothermal compressibility, sound velocity, internal energy, pressure, and Tan's contact from the calculated μ without further strong-coupling calculations. The ETMA results are compared to experimental data on a ⁶Li superfluid Fermi gas at T/T_F ≈ 0.06, as well as to the BCS-Leggett mean-field theory, the non-selfconsistent T-matrix approximation (TMA), and the Luttinger-Ward approach.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter from ETMA: ξ_B = 0.381, compared to experimental value ξ_B = 0.376(4) from Ku et al.
- ETMA Tan's contact at unitarity: C/k_F⁴ = 0.098, compared to experimental value C/k_F⁴ = 0.107(3) from Hoinka et al. and quantum Monte Carlo result C/k_F⁴ = 0.0996(34)
- Luttinger-Ward approach gives μ(T=0)/ε_F = 0.36 at unitarity, slightly below the experimental value μ/ε_F = 0.38 from Horikoshi et al. and Ku et al.
- ETMA sound velocity agrees with Bragg spectroscopy experiment by Vale et al. in the BCS-unitary regime

## Context

This work extends the BCS-Leggett mean-field theory (Leggett, 1980) and the non-selfconsistent T-matrix approximation (TMA) by including superfluid fluctuations through ETMA, which better reproduces the experimental chemical potential measured by Horikoshi et al. (2017). The approach enables efficient computation of multiple ground-state observables from a single strong-coupling calculation, reducing experimental and theoretical effort. The results are relevant for quantum simulation of neutron-star interiors, where the low-density region is expected to resemble a strongly interacting superfluid Fermi gas, though the authors note that the effective range differs between cold atoms and neutron matter.
