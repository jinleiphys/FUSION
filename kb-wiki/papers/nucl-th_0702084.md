# LHC Predictions from an extended theory with Elastic, Inelastic, and Path Length Fluctuating Energy Loss

> We present the LHC predictions for the WHDG model of radiative, elastic, and path length fluctuating energy loss. We find the pT dependence of RAA is qualitatively very different from AWS-based energy loss extrapolations to the LHC; the large pT reach of the year one data at the LHC should suffice to distinguish between the two. We also discuss the importance of requiring a first elastic scatter before any medium-induced elastic or radiative loss occurs, a necessary physical effect not considered in any previous models.

## Key claim

The WHDG model, which includes radiative, elastic, and path length fluctuating energy loss, predicts a qualitatively different transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor R_AA at the LHC compared to AWS-based models; the large pT reach of year-one LHC data should suffice to distinguish between the two approaches, provided the medium density increases only modestly (by a factor of approximately 2-3) from RHIC to LHC.

## Method

The authors compute R_AA for pions at LHC energies by convolving the probability distributions for radiative and elastic energy loss, then averaging over a realistic Glauber geometry with Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles. They compare their results to two implementations of the AWS (Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann) model, which uses a nonperturbatively large transport coefficient q-hat proportional to the three-fourths root of the energy density. The WHDG model also introduces a new physical effect: the requirement that a parton jet must undergo at least one elastic scatter before any medium-induced energy loss can occur, implemented via a probability P_0 = exp(-N_c) for no in-medium energy loss.

## Key numbers

- For the WHDG model at RHIC, the conservative input parameters are dN_g/dy = 1000 and alpha_s = 0.3, which reproduce PHENIX pion R_AA data.
- The AWS models require q-hat = 100 and q-hat = 68 (in unspecified units) as input parameters to fit RHIC data, corresponding to c values of approximately 8-20 in the relation q-hat = c * epsilon^(3/4), compared to pQCD estimates giving c approximately 2.
- The LHC medium density scaling used in AWS models makes the LHC approximately 7 times more dense than RHIC, based on EKRT-type scaling.
- Including the P_0 (probability of no in-medium energy loss) physics accounts for 50% of R_AA at fixed alpha_s = 0.3; allowing alpha_s to run as alpha_s(q^2 = 2 pi T(z)) reduces P_0 by a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the WHDG model previously applied to RHIC data (Wicks 2005, Horowitz 2006) by providing explicit LHC predictions and comparing them to AWS-based models from Eskola (2004) and Dainese (2004). The paper also introduces a previously neglected physical effect: the requirement of a first elastic scatter before any medium-induced energy loss can occur, which was not considered in any prior models including those of Vitev (2005) and Wang (2004). The results enable experimental discrimination between energy loss mechanisms using the large pT lever arm of LHC pion data, and motivate future work relaxing the fixed coupling constant approximation.
