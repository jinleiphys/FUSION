# Quarkyonic Chiral Spirals in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Approach

> We explore the inhomogeneous QCD phases at finite density and temperature using a (3+1)-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the large Nc limit with an additional attractive tensor-tensor interaction channel. For single modulated solutions, the problem reduces to solving the gap equation of a Chiral Gross-Neveu (NJL2) theory, whose minimum solution is a periodic array of chirally twisted kinks. At zero temperature, the minimum solution reduces to the quarkyonic chiral spiral found in quarkyonic matter. The connection between the quarkyonic matter and our (3+1)-dimensional NJL model is rooted on the fact that the first reduces to (1+1)-dimensional QCD and the second to the NJL2 theory, both of which have continuous chiral symmetry and asymptotic freedom. Our findings can be useful to obtain a reliable qualitative picture of the QCD phase diagram and the location of the QCD critical point in the technically challenging region of intermediate densities and temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a (3+1)-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an additional attractive tensor-tensor interaction channel, in the large Nc limit, produces a ground state that is a periodic array of chirally twisted kinks; at zero temperature this solution reduces to the quarkyonic chiral spiral found in quarkyonic matter, resolving a standing discrepancy between two previously proposed inhomogeneous solutions for QCD at intermediate densities.

## Method

The authors start with a two-flavor chiral invariant NJL Lagrangian in (3+1) dimensions that includes both conventional scalar/pseudo-scalar interactions and an additional tensor-tensor four-fermion interaction term. In the mean-field approximation and considering single-modulated condensates along the z-direction, they reduce the problem to solving the gap equation of a (1+1)-dimensional Chiral Gross-Neveu (NJL2) theory. Using a resolvent approach, they find the general solution as a periodic array of chirally twisted kinks, and perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion to determine the energetically favored solution near the chiral transition curve.

## Key numbers

- The general solution for the condensate M*(z) is specified by four parameters: a scale λ, a phase parameter q, an angular parameter θ (θ ∈ [0, 4K']), and the elliptic parameter ν (0 ≤ ν ≤ 1).
- At zero temperature, the energetically favored solution is the chiral spiral with q = μ.
- The Ginzburg-Landau expansion up to α₃ order yields the equation M*' - i(2α₂/α₃)M* = 0, whose solution is a chiral spiral M* = λ exp(i(2α₂/α₃)z).

## Context

This work resolves a standing problem between two different inhomogeneous solutions proposed for large Nc QCD at intermediate densities: the quarkyonic chiral spiral found in quarkyonic matter by the authors of that approach, and a real crystalline soliton solution obtained using a (3+1)-D NJL theory with only conventional scalar and pseudo-scalar interactions by Nickel (2009). The modified NJL model presented here contains the Nickel (2009) model as a particular case and is consistent with the quarkyonic matter results, suggesting this NJL model is more physically appropriate for describing the intermediate density region of QCD. The findings enable exploration of the μ-T plane region occupied by the inhomogeneous phase and the possible location of the QCD critical point.
