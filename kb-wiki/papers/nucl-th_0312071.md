# Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei and stranger systems

> Recent experiments on production of Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei have renewed interest in extracting the Lambda-Lambda interaction from the few events identified since the inception of this field forty years ago. Few-body calculations relating to this issue are reviewed, particularly with respect to the possibility that A=4 marks the onset of Lambda-Lambda binding in nuclei. The Nijmegen soft-core model potentials NSC97 qualitatively agree with the strength of the Lambda-Lambda interaction deduced from the newly determined binding energy of Lambda-Lambda-6He. Applying the extended NSC97 model to stranger nuclear systems suggests that A=6 marks the onset of Xi binding, with a particle stable Lambda-Xi-6He, and that strange hadronic matter is robustly bound.

# Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei and stranger systems

## Key claim
The Nijmegen soft-core model potentials NSC97 qualitatively agree with the strength of the Lambda-Lambda interaction deduced from the newly determined binding energy of Lambda-Lambda-6He, but the onset of Lambda-Lambda binding in nuclei may occur at A=4 only under specific conditions on the Lambda-N interaction strength, and the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations yield no bound state for Lambda-Lambda-4H over a wide range of Lambda-Lambda interactions including that corresponding to the measured binding energy of Lambda-Lambda-6He.

## Method
Few-body Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations were performed for light Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei (A=4,5,6,10) using s-wave two-body potentials fitted to low-energy scattering parameters or subsystem binding energies, with the Nijmegen soft-core model NSC97 providing the underlying hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. For stranger systems, three-body Faddeev calculations for alpha-Lambda-Xi systems were used to assess Xi binding, and relativistic mean field calculations extended model NSC97 to bulk strange hadronic matter over the full range of strangeness fraction.

## Key numbers
- The new KEK E373 candidate for Lambda-Lambda-6He has a Lambda-Lambda pairing energy Delta B_LambdaLambda ~ 1 MeV, substantially lower than the ~4.5 MeV deduced from the older event.
- The four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation for Lambda-Lambda-4H yields no bound state for a wide range of Lambda-Lambda interactions, including that corresponding to B_LambdaLambda(Lambda-Lambda-6He) ~ 1 MeV.
- For Lambda-Lambda-10Be, the four-body alpha-alpha-Lambda-Lambda model gives Delta B_LambdaLambda ~ 1.5 MeV in the limit V_LambdaLambda -> 0, and the two solid points for NSC97 versions are short of reproducing the old B_LambdaLambda(Lambda-Lambda-10Be) by about 2.3 +/- 0.4 MeV.
- For strange hadronic matter at strangeness fraction f_S=0.8, the global minimum binding energy occurs at baryon density rho_B=0.27 fm^-3, with a shallow local minimum at rho_B=0.72 fm^-3; at f_S=1.0 the relationship reverses in a first-order phase transition.
- Binding energies per baryon in strange hadronic matter reach as much as 80 MeV for the N-Sigma-Xi dominated phase.

## Context
This review extends the work of the Nijmegen group (Rijken, 2001) on one-boson-exchange models for the baryon-baryon interaction using SU(3)-flavor symmetry, and addresses the inconsistency between the old Lambda-Lambda-10Be event (Danysz, 1963) and the old Lambda-Lambda-6He event (Prowse, 1966) that was resolved by the new KEK E373 data (Takahashi, 2001). The calculations build on earlier Faddeev work by Filikhin and Gal (2002) and are challenged by the stochastic variational calculations of Nemura et al. (2003), who argue that the Lambda-N interaction in the 3S channel should be weaker than used by Filikhin and Gal. The results enable future experimental searches for the lightest particle-stable S=-3 hypernucleus, Lambda-Xi-6He, and connect to studies of phase transitions to hyperon matter in neutron stars (Schaffner-Bielich et al., 2002).
