# Persistence of the N = 50 shell closure over the isotopic chains of Sc, Ti, V and Cr nuclei using relativistic energy density functional

> The analytical expression of the density-dependent binding energy per nucleon for the relativistic mean field (RMF), also known as the relativistic energy density functional (Relativistic-EDF), is used to obtain the isospin-dependent symmetry energy and its components for the isotopic chain of Sc, Ti, V, and Cr nuclei. The procedure of the coherent density fluctuation model is employed to formulate the Relativistic-EDF and Brueckner energy density functional (Brueckner-EDF) at local density. A few signatures of shell and/or sub-shell closure are observed in the symmetry energy and its components, i.e., surface and volume symmetry energy, far from the beta-stable region for odd-A Sc and V, and even-even Ti and Cr nuclei with non-linear NL3 and G3 parameter sets. A comparison is made with the results obtained from Relativistic-EDF and Brueckner-EDF with both NL3 and G3 for the considered isotopic chains. We find Relativistic-EDF outperforms the Brueckner-EDF in predicting the shell and/or sub-shell closure of neutron-rich isotopes at N = 50 for these atomic nuclei. Moreover, a relative comparison has been made for the results obtained with the non-linear NL3 and G3 parameter sets.

## Key claim
The relativistic energy density functional (Relativistic-EDF) outperforms the Brueckner energy density functional (Brueckner-EDF) in predicting shell and/or sub-shell closure at N = 50 for neutron-rich isotopes of Sc, Ti, V, and Cr, but the evidence is not uniform across all isotopic chains and parameter sets: a clear kink at N = 50 appears for Sc with both NL3 and G3, for Ti only with NL3, while for V and Cr only minor kinks appear with NL3 and no clear evidence with G3.

## Method
The authors used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to fold nuclear matter symmetry energy from two energy density functionals (Relativistic-EDF and Brueckner-EDF) with the total density distributions of spherical nuclei obtained from self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations. The symmetry energy S and its volume (S_V) and surface (S_S) components were computed for the isotopic chains of Sc (Z=21), Ti (Z=22), V (Z=23), and Cr (Z=24) using the non-linear NL3 and G3 parameter sets. The weight function from CDFM was used to translate nuclear matter quantities from momentum space to coordinate space at local density.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy S for Sc isotopes with Relativistic-EDF and NL3: 34.1 to 40.3 MeV; with G3: 25.8 to 30.7 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for Ti isotopes with Relativistic-EDF and NL3: 33.3 to 40.9 MeV; with G3: 25.9 to 31.0 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for V isotopes with Relativistic-EDF and NL3: 34.8 to 41.5 MeV; with G3: 25.2 to 31.2 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for Cr isotopes with Relativistic-EDF and NL3: 34.8 to 42.1 MeV; with G3: 25.2 to 31.5 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for Sc isotopes with Brueckner-EDF and NL3: 24.4 to 27.7 MeV; with G3: 22.2 to 27.4 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for Ti isotopes with Brueckner-EDF and NL3: 24.3 to 27.9 MeV; with G3: 22.1 to 27.6 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for V isotopes with Brueckner-EDF and NL3: 24.1 to 28.1 MeV; with G3: 21.8 to 27.7 MeV.
- Symmetry energy S for Cr isotopes with Brueckner-EDF and NL3: 24.0 to 28.4 MeV; with G3: 21.7 to 27.9 MeV.
- Root-mean-square deviation for binding energy of 70 spherical nuclei: 2.977 MeV for NL3, 2.308 MeV for G3.
- Root-mean-square deviation of charge radii for eight closed-shell nuclei: 0.021 fm for NL3, 0.018 fm for G3.

## Context
This work extends the application of the relativistic energy density functional within the coherent density fluctuation model, previously used for doubly magic nuclei and the Pb isotopic chain by Akumal (2021) and Jeet (2022), to the medium-mass isotopic chains of Sc, Ti, V, and Cr. It addresses the Coester band problem of the classical Brueckner prescription, which fails to replicate the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. The results enable further study of shell and sub-shell closures near N = 50 and N = 40 in neutron-rich regions, complementing experimental efforts at radioactive ion beam facilities and theoretical work on new magic numbers in the calcium region by Leis (2021), Janssens (2002), and others.
