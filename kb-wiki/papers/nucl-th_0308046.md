# Proton-neutron pairing in the deformed BCS approach

> We examine isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations for the ground state of even-even Ge isotopes with mass number A=64-76 within the deformed BCS approach. For N=Z 64Ge the BCS solution with only T=0 proton-neutron pairs is found. For other nuclear systems (N>Z) a coexistence of a T=0 and T=1 pairs in the BCS wave function is observed. A problem of fixing of strengths of isoscalar and isovector pairing interactions is addressed. A dependence of number of like and unlike pairs in the BCS ground state on the difference between number of neutrons and protons is discussed. We found that for nuclei with N much bigger than Z the effect of proton-neutron pairing is small but not negligible.

## Key claim

The paper claims that T=0 proton-neutron pairing correlations are not negligible even for medium-heavy nuclei with large neutron excess (N much larger than Z), contrary to the general belief that such correlations are restricted to the vicinity of the N=Z line. This conclusion depends on the procedure used to fix the pairing strengths: when adjusted to experimental odd-even mass differences, the effect persists for all Ge isotopes studied (A=64-76), but when using commonly prescribed pairing strengths (G=16/A MeV for T=1 and G=20/A MeV for T=0), the T=0 pairing solution disappears for N-Z ≥ 8.

## Method

The authors performed generalized deformed BCS calculations for even-even Ge isotopes (A=64-76) using an axially-symmetric Woods-Saxon potential with quadrupole (β₂) and hexadecapole (β₄) deformation parameters from the literature. The nuclear Hamiltonian included monopole (K=0) proton-proton, neutron-neutron, isovector (T=1) proton-neutron, and isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing interactions. Pairing strengths were adjusted to reproduce empirical pairing gaps (Δ_p^emp, Δ_n^emp) and the proton-neutron interaction energy (δ_pn^emp) derived from experimental nuclear masses via Taylor series expansions.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Ge (N=Z), the BCS solution with only T=0 proton-neutron pairs was found; the like-particle pairing gaps Δ_pp and Δ_nn vanish above a critical ratio G_pn/G_ττ, with G_ττ assumed to be 0.250 MeV.
- For ⁷⁰Ge (N>Z), the like-particle pairing modes coexist with proton-neutron pairing; G_ττ was assumed to be 0.229 MeV.
- The average pairing gaps from the traditional model are given as Δ̄_τ = 12 MeV/A^{1/2} and δ̄_pn = 20 MeV/A; from Vogel et al. as Δ̄_τ = (7.2 - 44(N-Z)²/A²) MeV/A^{1/3} and δ̄_pn = 31 MeV/A.
- For ⁷⁴Ge in phase iii (with T=0 pn-pairing), the number of pp pairs <N_pp> reaches a maximum of about 7.6 and nn pairs <N_nn> about 4.8, while <N_pn^{T=0}> decreases with increasing N-Z.
- For ⁷⁶Ge (N-Z=12), the largest differences among G_pp^{T=1}, G_nn^{T=1}, and G_pn^{T=0} forces are visible; the T=0 pn-pairing effect is small but not negligible.
- Using the commonly prescribed strengths (G_pp^{T=1}=G_nn^{T=1}=16/A MeV, G_pn^{T=0}=20/A MeV), the T=0 pairing solution disappears for N-Z ≥ 8.

## Context

This work extends earlier generalized pairing formalisms by Chen and Goswami (1967) and Goodman (1968, 1970, 1972) to include deformation degrees of freedom, and applies the deformed BCS approach to Ge isotopes. It contrasts with the general belief that proton-neutron pairing correlations are restricted to the N=Z line, as found in schematic model studies by Engel et al. (1996, 1997) and Satula and Wyss (1997, 2000). The results enable future studies of beta and double beta decay matrix elements within a coupled deformed BCS plus QRPA approach, as noted by Simkovic et al. (2003) and in related work by Cheoun et al. (1995) and Pantis et al. (1996).
