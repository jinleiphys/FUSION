# Parton rearrangement and fast thermalization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> The implications of parton rearrangement processes on the dynamics of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions have been investigated. A microscopic transport approach, namely the quark gluon string model (QGSM) which has been extended for a locally density-dependent partonic rearrangement and fusion procedure served as the tool for this investigations. The model emulates effectively the dynamics of a strongly coupled quark plasma and final hadronic interactions. Main QGSM results on anisotropic flow components v1 and v2 at top RHIC energy are compiled. Predictions for the pseudorapidity dependence of directed and elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions under LHC conditions are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that implementing locally density-dependent parton rearrangement and fusion processes in the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) drives the system to fast kinetic equilibrium and improves the description of anisotropic flow, but this is a model emulation of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma rather than a first-principles QCD calculation.

## Method

The authors extended the standard QGSM, a microscopic string cascade transport approach using Monte-Carlo simulation, by adding a dynamical mechanism where above a critical local particle density, hadronic correlations are decomposed into constituent partons which then rearrange into new hadron-like correlations. A quark-antiquark pair of the same flavor may additionally annihilate during rearrangement, effectively implementing a 3-to-2 reaction. The model was applied to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, and the resulting anisotropic flow components v1 and v2 were compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Local equilibration time reduced by a factor of 5: from approximately 10 fm/c to approximately 2 fm/c in the overlap zone of Au+Au collisions
- Au+Au collisions simulated at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at RHIC
- Pb+Pb collisions simulated at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV for LHC predictions
- LHC predictions made for impact parameters b = 2.3 fm and b = 8 fm
- Elliptic flow v2 around midrapidity under LHC conditions predicted to increase by about 10-20% compared to RHIC results

## Context

This work extends earlier QGSM studies by Bleibel (2006, 2007) and Burau (2004) by implementing parton rearrangement to address the fast thermalization problem in heavy-ion collisions. The results are compared to PHOBOS data (Back, 2005) and to hydrodynamic approaches such as the hydro-cascade hybrid model of Hirano (2005) and parton cascade approaches (Xu, 2007). The predictions suggest that the hydrodynamical limit will be reached under LHC conditions, enabling tests of whether the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma picture holds at higher energies.
