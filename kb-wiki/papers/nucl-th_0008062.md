# Direct-decay properties of charge-exchange spin giant resonances

> An extended continuum-RPA approach is applied to describe direct-decay properties of spin giant resonances in $^{208}$Bi and $^{90}$Nb. Partial branching ratios for direct proton decay from these resonances are evaluated. The branching ratio for $γ$-decay from the spin-dipole resonance to the Gamow-Teller resonance (main peak) is estimated. The saturation-like behaviour of the mean doorway-state spreading width in $^{208}$Pb is discussed in connection with the branching ratio for direct proton decay from the spin-monopole resonance and the Gamow-Teller strength distribution.

## Key claim

The extended continuum-RPA approach can reproduce measured partial branching ratios for direct proton decay from the Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole resonances in 208Bi and 90Nb, but the calculated values depend on a phenomenological smearing parameter (mean doorway-state spreading width) that must be adjusted for each resonance to match the experimental total width.

## Method

The authors apply a continuum-RPA (CRPA) approach using a phenomenological mean field and Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction, with the isovector part of the mean field and the Coulomb field calculated self-consistently. The coupling of doorway states to many-quasiparticle configurations is described phenomenologically via a smearing parameter I (mean doorway-state spreading width), which is adjusted for each giant resonance to reproduce the experimental total width in calculations of the energy-averaged strength function. Partial branching ratios for direct proton decay are then evaluated from the CRPA equations with the substitution of the excitation energy by omega + iI/2.

## Context

This work extends the CRPA approach originally proposed in Refs. [6,7] and extended in Refs. [8,9] to describe direct-decay properties of charge-exchange spin giant resonances, motivated by coincidence experiments at RCNP (Osaka) and KVI (Groningen) on (3He,tp) and (3He,tgamma) reactions. The calculated branching ratios for direct proton decay from the GTR and SDR in 208Bi show good agreement with experimental data from Refs. [1,2], and the results for the IVSMR indicate that proton direct decay is the main decay channel for these monopole resonances, consistent with observations in Ref. [4]. The calculated gamma-decay branching ratios from the SDR to the GTR are expected to be compared with upcoming data from (3He,tgamma) coincidence experiments (Ref. [5]), and the GT strength distribution calculation can be compared with (pn) data from Ref. [13] to verify the particle-hole structure.
