# The electric dipole moment of the neutron from 2+1 flavor lattice QCD

> We compute the electric dipole moment d_n of the neutron from a fully dynamical simulation of lattice QCD with 2+1 flavors of clover fermions and nonvanishing theta term. The latter is rotated into the pseudoscalar density in the fermionic action using the axial anomaly. To make the action real, the vacuum angle theta is taken to be purely imaginary. The physical value of d_n is obtained by analytic continuation. We find d_n = -3.8(2)(9) x 10^{-16} [theta e cm], which, when combined with the experimental limit on d_n, leads to the upper bound theta < 7.6 x 10^{-11}.

## Key claim
The paper computes the neutron electric dipole moment from lattice QCD with 2+1 flavors of clover fermions at nonvanishing theta term, but the vacuum angle theta is taken to be purely imaginary to make the action real, and the physical value is obtained by analytic continuation. The result is conditional on the assumption that the theory is analytic in the vicinity of theta=0.

## Method
The theta term is chirally rotated into a flavor-singlet pseudoscalar density in the fermionic action using the axial anomaly, with the vacuum angle taken purely imaginary to keep the action real. Simulations are performed on 24^3 x 48 lattices at beta=5.50 (a=0.074 fm) using the SLiNC action with nonperturbatively determined clover coefficient, generating ensembles at two sets of quark masses along the constant singlet quark mass line. The electric dipole moment is extracted from three-point functions of the nucleon with the local vector current, using the ratio method to obtain the form factor F_3 at zero momentum transfer, followed by analytic continuation back to real theta.

## Key numbers
- d_n = -0.0039(2)(9) [e fm theta] at the physical point, corresponding to d_n = -3.9(2)(9) x 10^{-16} [theta e cm]
- Upper bound on the vacuum angle: |theta| < 7.4 x 10^{-11} (from combining with experimental bound |d_N^n| <= 2.9 x 10^{-13} e fm)
- At the symmetric point (m_pi = 465(13) MeV, m_K = 465(13) MeV): d_n = -0.0297(38) [e fm theta]
- At the intermediate point (m_pi = 360(10) MeV, m_K = 505(14) MeV): d_n = -0.0215(25) [e fm theta]
- Lattice spacing a = 0.074(2) fm at beta=5.50
- Low-energy constant from chiral perturbation theory fit: w_a(mu=1 GeV) = 0.04(1) GeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment by Shintani (2008, 2012) and Berruto (2005), which used the theta term absorbed into the observable rather than the action, and which encountered difficulties with nonvanishing topological susceptibility at finite lattice spacing. The approach follows the method of Horsley (2008) for imaginary theta simulations and uses the chiral perturbation theory framework of Guo (2012) for extrapolation to the physical point. The results enable a direct lattice QCD determination of the strong CP parameter theta from the experimental neutron EDM bound, and pave the way for future simulations on larger lattices with smaller pion masses and lattice spacings.
