# Resolving the Quenched ${g_A}$ Puzzle in Nuclei and Nuclear Matter

> The long-standing puzzle of the quenched $g_A$ in nuclei -- and in dense matter -- is shown to have a simple resolution in a scale-symmetric HLS chiral Lagrangian at the Fermi-liquid fixed point. This resolution exposes scale-chiral symmetry, hidden in QCD in the vacuum, emerging in nuclear matter from low density to high compact-star density. It has important implications on "first principles" approaches to nuclear physics, such as the role of multi-body exchange currents in Gamow-Teller matrix elements in nuclei and in neutrinoless double $β$ decays for going Beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing puzzle of the quenched axial coupling constant \(g_A\) in nuclei (where the effective value \(g_A^{\rm eff} \approx 1\)) is resolved by strong particle-hole correlations captured in a scale-symmetric hidden local symmetry (HLS) chiral Lagrangian at the Fermi-liquid fixed point, without requiring multi-body exchange currents or baryon resonance degrees of freedom. The author argues that the "first-principles" resolution claimed by Gysbers et al. (2019) using standard chiral EFT with pions only (S\(\chi\)EFT\(_\pi\)) is "highly questionable if not unfounded" because the higher-order chiral-filter unprotected terms cannot be controlled at the truncation order used.

## Method

The author constructs a scale-chiral hidden local symmetric Lagrangian (\(bs\)HLS) that includes nucleons, pions, vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)) as hidden gauge fields, and a scalar dilaton \(\chi\) (associated with \(f_0(500)\)) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of scale symmetry, with a cutoff above the vector-meson mass \(\sim 700\) MeV. This Lagrangian is treated in the mean-field approximation, which is shown to be equivalent to the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory in the limit \(\bar{N} \equiv k_F/(\Lambda - k_F) \to \infty\). The quenching factor \(q^L = g_A^L / g_A\) is then calculated from the Landau interaction parameters and the in-medium pion decay constant ratio \(\Phi = f_\pi^\ast / f_\pi\), using the expression \(g_A^{\rm L}/ g_A \approx (1 - \frac{1}{3} \Phi \tilde{F}_1^\pi)^{-2}\), where \(\tilde{F}_1^\pi\) is the pion Fock term contribution to the Landau parameter.

## Key numbers

* The quenching factor predicted at nuclear matter density is \(q^L \simeq 0.79\), which agrees with the experimental value for \(^{100}\)Sn of \(q_{exp} = 0.75(2)\) from the super-allowed Gamow-Teller decay measurement.
* The effective axial coupling constant from the calculation is \(g_A^{\rm eff} = 0.95 - 1.08\), matching the range from the experimental quenching factor \(q_{B_{\rm GT,ESPM}} = 0.75(2)\).
* The theoretical prediction for the ratio \(\epsilon = g_A^{\rm eff}_t / g_A\) for the time component of the axial current in nuclear matter is \(\epsilon_{theory} = 2.0 \pm 0.2\), which agrees with experimental measurements in the Pb region (\(A=205-212\)) giving \(\epsilon_{exp} = 2.01 \pm 0.05\).
* The prediction for the nuclear anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio is \(\delta g_l^p (n_0) \simeq 0.21\), agreeing with the measured value in the Pb region of \(\delta g_l^{\rm proton} = 0.23 \pm 0.03\).

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid fixed-point approach to the axial coupling problem, originally developed by Friman and Rho (1980s), and contrasts it with the standard chiral EFT approach (S\(\chi\)EFT\(_\pi\)) used by Gysbers et al. (2019) which claims resolution via two- and three-body weak currents. The author argues that the \(bs\)HLS approach captures the effect of high loop-orders in S\(\chi\)EFT\(_\pi\) at tree order, and that the quenching factor \(\sim 0.75\) is given mostly by strong particle-hole correlations without multi-body currents or baryon resonances. The result enables a connection between the quenching in finite nuclei and the emergence of scale symmetry at high density in compact stars, where the same Lagrangian predicts \(g_A^\ast \to 1\) at the dilaton-limit fixed point, consistent with the precocious onset of conformal sound velocity above \(3n_0\) as discussed in the author's review (MR-review, 2019).
