# Proton Spin Decomposition via QCD Sum Rules

> We present a novel approach for investigating the spin structure of hadrons based on the two-point function in quantum field theory. In a rotating frame, we derive two independent expressions of the two-point function and identify their equivalence, which allows for a complete decomposition of the total spin of a composite system into the angular momenta of its constituent particles. Applying this approach to the proton, we analyze its spin structure in the massless quark limit. Our results indicate that the quark spin contribution accounts for approximately 27$\%$ of the total proton spin at a low-energy scale, significantly deviating from the prediction of the non-relativistic quark model.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the equivalence between two methods of computing the two-point function in a rotating frame allows a complete decomposition of the proton's total spin into contributions from quark spin, quark orbital angular momentum, and gluon total angular momentum, but only in the massless quark limit and at a low-energy scale where the quark condensate is determined (approximately 0.5-1 GeV).

## Method

The authors analyze the operator product expansion (OPE) of the two-point function for a composite field that couples to the proton, restricting to five Feynman diagrams: one leading perturbative diagram and four non-perturbative diagrams involving the quark condensate and gluon condensate. They evaluate the two-point function in a rotating frame using two independent methods, Method A (direct insertion of angular momentum operators) and Method B (Lorentz transformation of the field), and exploit their equivalence to extract the spin decomposition via QCD sum rules, using a Borel transformation to isolate the ground-state proton contribution.

## Key numbers

- Quark spin contribution accounts for 27.1 ± 0.4% of the total proton spin at the low-energy scale where the quark condensate is determined.
- Quark orbital angular momentum contributes 78.3 ± 0.5% of the total proton spin.
- Gluon total angular momentum contributes -5.4 ± 0.8% of the total proton spin.
- Up-quark spin contributes 27.3 ± 1.1% and down-quark spin contributes -0.2 ± 1.5% of the total proton spin.
- Up-quark orbital angular momentum contributes 96.4 ± 0.6% and down-quark orbital angular momentum contributes -18.2 ± 0.6% of the total proton spin.
- The analysis uses condensate values: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.24 GeV)^3 and ⟨G₀⟩ = 0.012 GeV^4, with threshold parameter s₀ = 3 GeV² and Borel mass range M² ∈ [0.8, 1.6] GeV².

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kim (2022) from spin-1 heavy quarkonium to the spin-1/2 proton, and addresses the "proton spin puzzle" first identified by the EMC experiment in the late 1980s, which showed that quarks contribute only about 30% of the proton spin. The results appear to contradict lattice simulations by the LHPC (2010), Gockeler (2003), Alexandrou (2013), and Deka (2013) at higher scales (μ ≈ 2 GeV), but the authors argue that QCD evolution to lower scales (μ ≈ 0.5-1 GeV) can reverse the signs of orbital angular momentum components and shift gluon contributions negative, making their results qualitatively consistent with expectations at low energy.
