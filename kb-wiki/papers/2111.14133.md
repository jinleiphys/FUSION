# A Pfaffian formulation for matrix elements of three-body operators in multiple quasi-particle configurations

> We present a Pfaffian formula to calculate matrix elements of three-body operators in symmetry-restoration beyond-mean-field methods, including the case of multiple quasi-particle configurations. Detailed derivation based on [Mizusaki et al., Phys. Lett. B 715, 219 (2012)] and [Hu et al., Phys. Lett. B 734, 162 (2014)] is provided, and potential applications in generator coordinate method with chiral interactions, as well as in study of nuclear matrix elements in neutrinoless double beta decay are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Pfaffian formula for calculating matrix elements of three-body operators in symmetry-restoration beyond-mean-field methods, applicable to both single quasi-particle vacuum and multiple quasi-particle configurations, with the derivation relying on the generalized Wick's theorem and Hara's prescription rather than providing a single compact Pfaffian expression.

## Method

The authors start from the generalized Pfaffian formula for norm overlaps derived by Hu et al. (2014) and apply Hara's prescription to classify the contractions in the matrix element of a three-body operator into four classes: contractions among the six creation/annihilation operators themselves, contractions linking one pair of these operators with the quasi-particle operators, contractions linking two pairs, and contractions linking all three pairs. Each class is expressed as a sum over Pfaffians of sub-matrices of the original skew-symmetric matrix, with coefficients built from the three-body interaction tensor and basic contractions involving particle creation and annihilation operators.

## Key numbers

- The three-body operator is expressed in second quantization as a sum over M single-particle states: $\hat{V}^{(3)} = \sum_{\mu\nu\delta \omega\rho\gamma}^M W_{\mu\nu\delta \omega\rho\gamma} \hat{c}^\dag_{\mu} \hat{c}^\dag_{\nu} \hat{c}^\dag_{\delta} \hat{c}_{\gamma} \hat{c}_{\rho} \hat{c}_{\omega}$, where the factor 1/36 is absorbed into the anti-symmetric matrix W.
- The Pfaffian formula for norm overlaps (Eq. 4) requires a $(2N+6)\times(2N+6)$ skew-symmetric matrix for the three-body case, where 2N is the number of quasi-particle operators and 6 comes from the three creation and three annihilation operators.
- The final expression for the three-body matrix element (Eq. 30) contains four terms: $O^{(0)}$ with a single Pfaffian of the $2N\times2N$ matrix S, $O^{(1)}$ with sums over pairs of indices requiring Pfaffians of $(2N-2)\times(2N-2)$ sub-matrices, $O^{(2)}$ with sums over quadruples of indices requiring $(2N-4)\times(2N-4)$ sub-matrices, and $O^{(3)}$ with sums over sextuples of indices requiring $(2N-6)\times(2N-6)$ sub-matrices.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian formulations developed by Robledo (2009, 2011) for norm overlaps, by Mizusaki et al. (2012, 2013) for multi-quasi-particle norm overlaps, and by Hu et al. (2014) for one-body and two-body operator matrix elements, to the case of three-body operators. The formula enables generator coordinate method calculations with chiral three-body forces and evaluation of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay involving chiral two-body currents, which require three-body transition operators.
