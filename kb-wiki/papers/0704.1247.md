# A Rational Approach to Resonance Saturation in large-Nc QCD

> We point out that resonance saturation in QCD can be understood in the large-Nc limit from the mathematical theory of Pade Approximants to meromorphic functions. These approximants are rational functions which encompass any saturation with a finite number of resonances as a particular example, explaining several results which have appeared in the literature. We review the main properties of Pade Approximants with the help of a toy model for the <VV-AA> two-point correlator, paying particular attention to the relationship among the Chiral Expansion, the Operator Product Expansion and the resonance spectrum. In passing, we also comment on an old proposal made by Migdal in 1977 which has recently attracted much attention in the context of AdS/QCD models. Finally, we apply the simplest Pade Approximant to the <VV-AA> correlator in the real case of QCD. The general conclusion is that a rational approximant may reliably describe a Green's function in the Euclidean, but the same is not true in the Minkowski regime due to the appearance of unphysical poles and/or residues.

## Key claim

Resonance saturation in large-Nc QCD can be understood as a Pade Approximant to the meromorphic Green's function, but the approximation reliably describes the Euclidean regime only; in the Minkowski regime, unphysical poles and/or residues appear, so masses and decay constants extracted from the approximant are unreliable except possibly for the lowest-lying states.

## Method

The authors reinterpret the approximation of large-Nc QCD Green's functions by a finite number of resonances as a Pade Approximant (PA) to a meromorphic function. They test this interpretation on a toy model for the ⟨VV-AA⟩ two-point correlator, whose spectrum consists of an infinite tower of resonances with masses following a Regge trajectory and constant decay constants, expressed analytically in terms of digamma functions. They construct PAs of various orders (including P^0_2 and P^50_52) from the chiral expansion coefficients and compare the approximants' poles, residues, and large-momentum expansions to the exact model results.

## Key numbers

* The simplest PA, P^0_2, yields a complex-conjugate pole pair at z_R = 0.6550 + i 0.1732 (in GeV^2 units) and a residue r_R^2 = 3.379 × 10^{-3} GeV^2; this residue approximates the OPE coefficient C_{-4} = -2.592 × 10^{-3} GeV^4 to within about 30%.
* The high-order PA P^50_52 reproduces the first OPE coefficient C_{-4} with 52 decimal figures of accuracy, C_{-6} with 48 figures, and C_{-8} with 45 figures.
* The first pole and residue of P^50_52 match the ρ meson mass (0.767 GeV) and decay constant (F_ρ = 133.884 MeV) to 193 decimal places, but the 25th real pole agrees with the physical mass only to 3 decimal figures and its residue is 29 times the true value.
* The integral I_π = 4.78719 × 10^{-3} (related to the electromagnetic pion mass difference) is reproduced by P^50_52 with more than 42 decimal figures.

## Context

This work extends the Minimal Hadronic Approximation (MHA) approach of Peris et al. (2000) and the resonance saturation ideas of Ecker et al. (1989) by providing a mathematical foundation in Pade theory. It explains discrepancies found between the short-distance constraints of Knecht et al. (1998) and the fit results of Friot et al. (2004) for the vector and axial-vector masses. The analysis also criticizes Migdal's 1977 proposal to extract the QCD spectrum from the OPE via PAs, noting that different spectra can produce the same parton-model logarithm, and extends this criticism to AdS/QCD models such as Erlich et al. (2005). The toy model used was previously studied by Shifman et al. (2000) and Duality et al. (2004) for testing sum-rule methods.
