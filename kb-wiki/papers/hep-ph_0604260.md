# Fate of the inert three-flavor, spin-zero color-superconducting phases

> I investigate some of the inert phases in three-flavor, spin-zero color-superconducting quark matter: the CFL phase (the analogue of the B phase in superfluid $^3\rm He$), the A and A* phases, and the 2SC and sSC phases. I compute the pressure of these phases with and without the neutrality condition. It is shown that the 2SC phase is identical to the A* phase up to a color rotation. The CFL phase is the energetically favored phase except for a small region of intermediate densities where the 2SC/A* phase is favored.

## Key claim

The CFL phase is the energetically favored ground state of three-flavor, spin-zero color-superconducting quark matter except for a small region of intermediate densities where the 2SC/A* phase is favored; the A* phase is shown to be identical to the 2SC phase up to a color rotation, which was not the case for the analogous phases in superfluid helium-3.

## Method

The author computed the pressure difference between each color-superconducting phase and normal quark matter, first without imposing neutrality using the QCD gap equation formalism from Refs. (spinn0, spin0), then with color and electric charge neutrality using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. For the neutral case, tadpoles were evaluated to determine the required chemical potentials (mu_8 for the A phase; mu_2 and mu_8 for the A* phase), and the mean-field thermodynamic potential was calculated including diquark condensates, chiral condensates, and lepton contributions.

## Key numbers

- The pressure difference for the A phase and A* phase without neutrality: Delta p = 4 alpha, where alpha = (mu^4 / (4 pi^2)) b^2 b_0'^2 exp(-pi / bar{g})
- The pressure difference for the sSC phase without neutrality: Delta p = (8 / 2^(1/2)) alpha
- The pressure difference for the CFL phase without neutrality: Delta p = (12 / 2^(1/3)) alpha
- The pressure difference for the 2SC phase without neutrality: Delta p = 4 alpha
- NJL model parameters used: m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 1.835, K Lambda^5 = 12.36, Lambda = 602.3 MeV
- Diquark coupling strength set to G_D = (3/4) G_S

## Context

This work extends the study of inert phases from one-flavor color superconductivity and superfluid helium-3 (Refs. spinn0, inert) to three-flavor, spin-zero color superconductivity. It confirms that the CFL phase dominates at high densities as found by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999), while at intermediate densities the 2SC/A* phase can be favored. The identification of the A* phase as equivalent to the 2SC phase via a color rotation resolves a question from the superfluid helium-3 analogy and enables future work to focus on the CFL and 2SC phases as the only distinct inert phases in this system.
