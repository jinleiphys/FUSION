# Analysis of polarized $^{16}$O$(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ observables within the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation

> Recoil nucleon transferred polarization observables in coincidence quasielastic electron scattering are studied within the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation. Results for response functions and polarization asymmetries are discussed for proton knockout from $p_{1/2}$, $p_{3/2}$ and $s_{1/2}$ shells in $^{16}$O. The impact of spinor distortion is examined by comparing the fully relativistic calculation with results obtained by projecting out the negative-energy components. In particular, a careful analysis of effects linked to the description of the bound and scattered relativistic nucleon wave functions is presented. The high sensitivity of some polarization observables to the dynamical enhancement of the lower components, already shown within the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation, is proven to be maintained in the relativistic distorted wave approach. Semi-relativistic approaches based on the effective momentum approximation are also studied. Finally, comparison with experimental data and a brief analysis of effects linked to medium modified form factors is presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the high sensitivity of certain transferred polarization observables to the dynamical enhancement of the lower components of relativistic nucleon wave functions, previously established within the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), is maintained when full final-state interactions are included via the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). However, the authors caution that equally acceptable relativistic potentials and alternative current operator descriptions exist, so the resulting variations in polarization observables define a minimal theoretical uncertainty that must be smaller than any claimed medium modification effects.

## Method

The authors performed RDWIA calculations for proton knockout from the \(p_{1/2}\), \(p_{3/2}\), and \(s_{1/2}\) shells in \(^{16}\)O, using bound nucleon wave functions from the NLSH relativistic mean-field parameterization and the EDAIO relativistic optical potential for the outgoing nucleon. They compared fully relativistic results with those obtained by projecting out negative-energy components (the "projected approach") and with the effective momentum approximation (EMA-noSV), and also evaluated semi-relativistic current operators expanded to first order in missing momentum over nucleon mass. The transferred polarization asymmetries \(P'_l\) and \(P'_s\) were computed for fixed kinematics (\(q=1\) GeV/c, \(\omega=439\) MeV, \(|Q^2|=0.8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)) and compared across these approaches.

## Key numbers

- Kinematics: \(q = 1\) GeV/c, \(\omega = 439\) MeV, yielding \(|Q^2| = 0.8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\); electron beam energy \(\varepsilon_{beam} = 2.445\) GeV, electron scattering angle \(\theta_e = 23.4^\circ\).
- For the \(p_{1/2}\) shell at missing momentum \(p \simeq 100\) MeV/c, final-state interactions reduce individual response functions by \(\sim 50\)–\(60\%\) for \(R^{TL'}_l\) and \(\sim 25\%\) for \(R^{TL'}_s\) and \(R^{T'}_l\).
- For the \(p_{1/2}\) shell at \(p \simeq 100\) MeV/c, the difference between RPWIA and RDWIA for the sideways transferred polarization \(P'_s\) is of the order of \(20\)–\(25\%\).
- The Coulomb distortion of electron wave functions was accounted for using an effective momentum approximation with a nuclear Coulomb potential of 3.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPWIA studies by Martinez et al. (2002, 2003) that demonstrated the importance of negative-energy components of the bound nucleon for polarized responses and transferred polarization asymmetries, now incorporating realistic final-state interactions through relativistic optical potentials. It also builds on prior RDWIA analyses of cross sections and response functions by Udias et al. (1993–2001) and on semi-relativistic approaches developed by Amaro et al. (1996–1998) and Jeschonnek (1998). The results enable meaningful comparisons with recent double-polarized \((\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) experiments on \(^{16}\)O (Malov, 2000) and \(^{4}\)He (Dieterich, 2001; Strauch, 2002), and establish a baseline theoretical uncertainty against which possible medium modifications of nucleon form factors must be judged.
