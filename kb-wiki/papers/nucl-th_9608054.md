# Magnetically Catalyzed Fusion

> We calculate the reaction cross-sections for the fusion of hydrogen and deuterium in strong magnetic fields as are believed to exist in the atmospheres of neutron stars. We find that in the presence of a strong magnetic field ($B \gsim 10^{12}$G), the reaction rates are many orders of magnitude higher than in the unmagnetized case. The fusion of both protons and deuterons are important over a neutron star's lifetime for ultrastrong magnetic fields ($B \sim 10^{16}$G). The enhancement may have dramatic effects on thermonuclear runaways and bursts on the surfaces of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that in strong magnetic fields (B ≳ 10¹² G), nuclear fusion cross-sections for hydrogen and deuterium are enhanced by many orders of magnitude compared to the unmagnetized case, but the enhancement is qualified by the fact that proton-proton fusion becomes important only over cosmological timescales even for ultrastrong fields (B ∼ 10¹⁶ G), while deuteron-proton fusion proceeds on much shorter timescales.

## Method

The authors model hydrogen atoms in strong magnetic fields using a variational approach with Gaussian trial wavefunctions for both the perpendicular (Landau) and axial electron coordinates, obtaining electron density distributions and screening potentials. They then compute the total interaction potential between two hydrogen atoms (including direct Coulomb terms and electron exchange antisymmetrization) and use the WKB approximation to calculate transmission probabilities through the potential barrier. These transmission probabilities are combined with standard S-factors for the p(p,e⁺ν)D and D(p,γ)³He reactions, and the ground-state fraction of hydrogen atoms is estimated using a modified Saha equation that accounts for quantizing magnetic fields and finite perpendicular momentum cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- For B = 4.7 × 10¹² G, the variational calculation gives a ground-state binding energy of 17.1 Ry for m=0 (compared to 18.6 Ry from Ruder et al.), with α = a_z/a_H = 7.77
- For B = 4.7 × 10¹⁶ G, the binding energy is 97.7 Ry for m=0 with α = 265
- The screening potential at the center of the electron cloud for B = 9.8 × 10¹² G is approximately 0.25 e²/a_H
- The p(p,e⁺ν)D reaction has S₀ = 4.38 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV-barn and S₁ = 11.2 MeV⁻¹ at low energies
- The D(p,γ)³He reaction has S₀ = 2.5 × 10⁻⁷ MeV-barn and S₁ = 27.8 MeV⁻¹
- For B = 10¹⁶ G, the p-p reaction timescale exceeds 10¹⁰ years below temperatures of about one million degrees
- For B = 10¹⁶ G, the D(p,γ)³He reaction timescale is less than one year over the interesting range of densities and temperatures
- For B = 10¹² G and 10¹⁴ G, only the deuterium reaction proceeds at a significant rate

## Context

This work extends the study of atomic structure in strong magnetic fields by Ruder et al. (1994) and the Saha equation formalism for magnetized hydrogen by Lai and Salpeter (1995) to calculate nuclear fusion rates in neutron star atmospheres. The results show that magnetic catalysis of fusion could dramatically affect thermonuclear runaways and bursts on neutron star surfaces, and may provide a steady energy source that erodes the hydrogen fuel supply over millions of years. The paper enables future work on fusion in ultrastrong magnetic fields and suggests that for heavier atoms (Z>1) the screening will be less effective due to electrons occupying higher m-levels.
