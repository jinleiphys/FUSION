# Study of Spectral Modification of Hadrons through Electromagnetic Probes

> The medium modification of vector meson properties and its effects on thermal photon and dilepton emission rates from hot/dense hadronic matter are discussed. The role of spectral changes of hadrons in explaining the WA98 photon data and the CERES/NA45 dilepton data with similar initial conditions is demonstrated.

## Key claim

Medium modification of vector meson properties (mass shift and/or broadening) can simultaneously explain both the WA98 photon data and the CERES/NA45 dilepton data from CERN SPS heavy-ion collisions, but it is difficult to distinguish whether the initial state is a quark-gluon plasma or a chirally symmetric hadronic phase with masses approaching zero at a temperature of about 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute thermal photon and dilepton emission rates from a hot/dense medium using the imaginary part of the retarded electromagnetic current correlation function, incorporating vector meson dominance. They model medium effects via two scenarios: universal scaling of hadron masses with temperature and baryon density (Nambu scaling with exponent λ=1/2), and the Quantum Hadrodynamic (QHD) model where vector meson self-energies arise from coupling to nucleonic excitations. The space-time evolution of the collision is treated with (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics assuming boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry, with initial conditions constrained by pion multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for QGP initial state: T_i = 190 MeV (photon analysis, Fig. 1) and T_i = 180–200 MeV (dilepton analysis)
- Initial temperature for hadronic initial state with universal scaling: T_i = 200 MeV (photon analysis) and T_i = 190–200 MeV (dilepton analysis)
- Freeze-out temperature: T_F = 120 MeV
- Initial thermalization time: τ_i = 1 fm/c
- Pion multiplicity used for initial temperature: dN_π/dy = 700 (for photon analysis)
- Dilepton analysis uses charged particle multiplicities ⟨N_ch⟩ = 250 and ⟨N_ch⟩ = 350
- Dilepton kinematic cuts: p_T > 200 MeV/c, opening angle Θ_ee > 35 mrad
- The ρ-peak in dilepton spectra shifts toward lower invariant mass for universal scaling compared to QHD model

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sarkar and collaborators (2001) that explained WA98 photon data with medium effects, and by Rapp and others (1997) who explained CERES dilepton enhancement via ρ meson broadening. The paper shows that both the WA98 photon data and the CERES/NA45 dilepton data can be reproduced with similar initial conditions (T_i ∼ 200 MeV) using either a QGP initial state or a hadronic initial state with universal mass scaling, making it difficult to distinguish between the two scenarios. The results enable future work to refine the equation of state and to test whether the photon emission rate from QGP (Kapusta 1991, Aurenche 1998) remains valid for strong coupling (g ∼ 2 at T ∼ 200 MeV).
