# Spectral Representation of Thermal OTO Correlators

> We study the spectral representation of finite temperature, out of time ordered (OTO) correlators on the multi-time-fold generalised Schwinger-Keldysh contour. We write the contour-ordered correlators as a sum over time-order permutations acting on a funda- mental array of Wightman correlators. We decompose this Wightman array in a basis of column vectors, which provide a natural generalisation of the familiar retarded-advanced basis in the finite temperature Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. The coefficients of this de- composition take the form of generalised spectral functions, which are Fourier transforms of nested and double commutators. Our construction extends a variety of classical results on spectral functions in the SK formalism at finite temperature to the OTO case.

## Key claim

The paper extends the spectral representation and column-vector/retarded-advanced (RA) basis formalism, previously developed for standard finite-temperature Schwinger-Keldysh (SK) two-point and three-point correlators, to out-of-time-ordered (OTO) correlators on a multi-time-fold generalized SK contour, with the hedge that the construction works for non-zero external frequencies and receives contact-term corrections at special loci where any frequency goes to zero.

## Method

The authors write contour-ordered correlators on a generalized SK contour with \(k\) time-folds as a sum over time-order permutations acting on a fundamental array of Wightman correlators. They decompose this Wightman array in a basis of column vectors that generalize the retarded-advanced basis, where the coefficients of the decomposition are generalized spectral functions defined as Fourier transforms of nested and double commutators. The basis is chosen so that, on a contour with \(k\) time-folds, there are \(k\) "retarded" combinations that can appear only in the causal past of other operators and \(k\) "advanced" combinations that can appear only in the causal future, implementing largest-time and smallest-time equations.

## Key numbers

* For a free real scalar field at finite temperature, the spectral function is \(\rho_p \equiv 2\pi\ \text{sign}(p^0)\delta(p^2+m^2) = \frac{2\pi}{2\omega_p} [\delta(\omega-\omega_p)-\delta(\omega+\omega_p)]\) with \(\omega_p=\sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2+m^2}\).
* The Bose-Einstein factor is \(\bose_p \equiv 1/(e^{\beta p^0}-1)\), obeying \(1+\bose_p+\bose_{-p}=0\) and \(\bose_p=e^{-\beta p^0}(1+\bose_p)\).
* For a generalized SK contour with \(k=2\) time-folds, the Wightman array \(\Mtildetwo\) is a \(4\times 4\) matrix; the dual basis consists of four column vectors \(\ebP{1},\ebP{2},\ebF{1},\ebF{2}\) given explicitly in Eq. (2pt:25), each containing entries of \(\bose(\omega)\) and \(1+\bose(\omega)\).
* The row vectors that annihilate the future-most index are \(\eF{1}(\omega_1)\equiv (-1,1,0,0)\) and \(\eF{2}(\omega_1)\equiv (0,0,-1,1)\); the row vectors that annihilate the past-most index are \(\eP{1}(\omega_2)\equiv (1,0,0,-e^{-\beta\omega_2})\) and \(\eP{2}(\omega_2)\equiv (0,-1,1,0)\).

## Context

This work extends classical results on spectral functions in the finite-temperature Schwinger-Keldysh formalism (Evans 1990, 1991; Carrington 1996; Hou 1998, 1999; Chu 1993; Henning 1993, 1995) to the out-of-time-ordered case, building on the recognition by Haehl (2017) that OTO spectral functions are needed to reconstruct all n-point Wightman correlators. The formalism enables efficient perturbative computations of OTO correlators in thermal quantum field theory, analogous to how the retarded-advanced basis simplified SK perturbation theory, and provides a framework for systematic diagrammatic calculations that avoids the proliferation of fields and vertices that plagues naive approaches.
