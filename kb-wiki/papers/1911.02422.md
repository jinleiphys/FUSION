# Microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling model for spectroscopy of odd-mass nuclei with octupole correlations

> [Background] Predictions of spectroscopic properties of low-lying states are critical for nuclear structure studies. Theoretical methods can be particularly involved for odd-mass nuclei because of the interplay between the unpaired nucleon and collective degrees of freedom. Only a few models have been developed for systems in which octupole collective degrees of freedom play a role. [Purpose] We aim to predict spectroscopic properties of odd-mass nuclei characterized by octupole shape deformation, employing a model that describes single-particle and collective degrees of freedom within the same microscopic framework. [Method] A microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling (CQC) model based on the covariant density functional theory is developed, which includes collective excitations of even-mass core nuclei and single-particle states of the odd nucleon, calculated using a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian combined with a constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. [Results] Model predictions for low-energy excitation spectra and transition rates of odd-mass radium isotopes $^{223, 225, 227}$Ra are shown to be in good agreement with available data. [Conclusions] A microscopic CQC model based on covariant density functional theory has been developed for odd-mass nuclei characterized by both quadrupole and octupole shape deformations. Theoretical results reproduce data in odd-mass Ra isotopes and provide useful predictions for future studies of octupole correlations in nuclei and related phenomena.

## Key claim
The paper develops a microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling (CQC) model that includes both quadrupole and octupole collective degrees of freedom for odd-mass nuclei, but the model's free parameters (Fermi surface and coupling strengths) must be adjusted to reproduce experimental ground-state spin and parity or excitation energies of the lowest levels, meaning the predictions are not fully parameter-free.

## Method
The authors construct a CQC Hamiltonian in the laboratory frame, coupling spherical single-neutron states from a constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculation with collective states of even-even cores obtained from a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH) solved on the deformation energy surface. The configuration space includes both particle-like states coupled to the lighter even-even neighbor (A-1) and hole-like states coupled to the heavier neighbor (A+1), with quadrupole-quadrupole and octupole-octupole interactions mediating the coupling. The RHB calculation uses the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional for the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing force for the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers
* For the core nuclei $^{222-228}$Ra, all exhibit a global minimum with octupole deformation $\beta_3 > 0.1$ and octupole deformation energy $\Delta E_{\rm oct} > 0.4$ MeV; $^{226}$Ra shows the deepest octupole minimum with $\Delta E_{\rm oct} \approx 0.94$ MeV.
* For $^{223}$Ra, the ground-state band and its parity partner show a very small energy difference of approximately 50 keV between the two bandheads.
* For $^{223}$Ra, the theoretical bandheads of bands 3 and 4 are approximately 130 keV lower than the corresponding experimental levels.
* The CQC model parameters adjusted for $^{223}$Ra are: Fermi surface $\varepsilon_f = -6.36$ MeV, quadrupole coupling strength $\chi_2 = 5.5$ MeV/$b^2$, and octupole coupling strength $\chi_3 = 4.0$ MeV/$b^3$.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling model of Quan (2017), previously applied to quadrupole-deformed odd-mass nuclei, to include octupole correlations in reflection-asymmetric systems. It builds on covariant density functional theory calculations using the PC-PK1 functional and the quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian framework developed by Li (2013) and Xia (2017). The model enables predictions for odd-mass radium isotopes $^{223,225,227}$Ra, with $^{225}$Ra being a key candidate for searches for a permanent electric dipole moment, and provides a microscopic alternative to approaches such as the interacting boson-fermion model extended to octupole degrees of freedom by Nomura (2018).
