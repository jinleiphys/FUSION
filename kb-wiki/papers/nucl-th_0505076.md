# The unitary-model-operator approach to nuclear many-body problems

> Microscopic nuclear structure calculations have been performed within the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach. Ground-state and single-particle energies are calculated for nuclei around ^{14}C, ^{16}O and ^{40}Ca with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions.

# The unitary-model-operator approach to nuclear many-body problems

## Key claim
The unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) can produce ground-state and single-particle energies for nuclei up to A≈40 using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions, but the calculated binding energies are systematically less bound than experiment because three-body cluster terms and genuine three-body forces are not yet included in the results presented.

## Method
The UMOA derives a Hermitian, energy-independent effective interaction through a two-step unitary transformation of the original Hamiltonian, first in a large model space to handle short-range correlations and then in a smaller model space that eliminates two-particle two-hole (2p2h) excitations. The effective interaction is determined self-consistently with the one-body potential for both particle and hole states, and the final ground-state energies are obtained by diagonalizing the transformed Hamiltonian including 1p1h excitations from the unperturbed ground state.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁶O with the CD-Bonn potential: −115.61 MeV (experimental: −127.62 MeV); binding energy per nucleon: 7.23 MeV (experimental: 7.98 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁶O with the N³LO potential: −110.00 MeV; with Nijm I: −104.25 MeV; with Nijm 93: −99.69 MeV
- Unperturbed ground-state energy of ¹⁶O for ρ₁=12 and ℏΩ=15 MeV using the CD-Bonn potential: −38.40 MeV after first-step calculation, −104.12 MeV after second-step calculation
- Three-body cluster effect for the CD-Bonn potential in ¹⁶O: approximately −4 MeV
- Convergent result for ⁴⁰Ca ground-state energy obtained at ρ₁=16 and ℏΩ=14 MeV with the CD-Bonn potential

## Context
This work extends the UMOA, which is a Hermitian-type coupled-cluster method (Coester 1958, Kümmel 1978), to nuclei heavier than the p-shell, where the no-core shell model (Navratil 2000) and Green's function Monte Carlo (Pieper 2001) are computationally limited to A≈12. The results for ¹⁶O show the same ordering of interaction strengths as Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for ⁴He by Nogga (2002). The approach enables systematic microscopic calculations for neutron-rich oxygen isotopes and Λ hypernuclei, and the authors identify the inclusion of genuine three-body forces and higher-order cluster terms as the next step to resolve the remaining underbinding.
