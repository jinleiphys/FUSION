# Pion and photon couplings of N* resonances from scattering on the proton

> Results of a relativistic model for pion- and photon-induced reactions on the proton are presented. The model is crossing symmetric and gauge invariant. The nucleon resonances P_11 (1440), P_11 (1710), D_13 (1520), S_11 (1535), S_11 (1650), P_33 (1232), P_33 (1600), S_31 (1620), and D_33 (1700) have been included explicitly in the calculation. Unitarity within the channel space πN + γN below the two-pion production threshold has been achieved by using the K-matrix approach. Strong and electromagnetic coupling parameters of the N* resonances have been determined from a fit to the pion-nucleon phase shifts, pion-photoproduction multipoles and Compton-scattering cross sections. The model is shown to describe simultaneously most of the available data. Results for the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified relativistic K-matrix model that simultaneously describes pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, and Compton scattering on the proton, extracting strong and electromagnetic couplings of nine nucleon resonances below about 1.7 GeV. The model achieves unitarity in the πN ⊕ γN channel space below the two-pion production threshold, but the description of the P11 partial wave required an additional resonance at 1.71 GeV because a single Roper resonance at 1.44 GeV failed to reproduce the phase shift.

## Method

The authors constructed a crossing-symmetric and gauge-invariant relativistic effective Lagrangian model using the K-matrix formalism to enforce two-body unitarity in the πN and γN channel space. Tree-level amplitudes included s- and u-channel exchange of the nucleon and nine N* resonances, plus t-channel meson exchanges (σ, ρ, π, ω), with parameters fitted simultaneously to Virginia Tech single-energy partial-wave amplitudes for πN phase shifts up to Tπ=820 MeV, pion-photoproduction multipoles up to Eγ=805 MeV, and Compton scattering cross sections from Saskatoon and Mainz. Off-shell form factors with cutoff Λ=1.2 GeV were applied to resonance propagators, and additional decay widths for two-pion and Nη channels were included for resonances above threshold.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon coupling constant fixed to gπNN = 13.02
- ρ(770) meson couplings: gππρ = 6.07, gρNN = 4.12, κρ = 1.79
- σ(760) product coupling: gππσ gNNσ = 4.7
- σγγ coupling: gσγγ gNNσ = -5.65, corresponding to σ→γγ decay width Γ = 10.15 keV
- π0γγ coupling from axial anomaly: gπ0γγ = 0.038
- Proton electric polarizability: α(0) = 5.64 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (underestimated compared to experimental global average α(0) = 12.1(0.8)(0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Proton magnetic polarizability: β(0) = 2.59 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (consistent with experimental β(0) = 2.1(0.8)(0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Sum of polarizabilities: α(0)+β(0) = 8.23 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Difference of polarizabilities: α(0)-β(0) = 3.05 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- P33(1232) resonance: pole mass 1224 MeV, πN coupling g = 2.050, off-shell parameter aπ = -0.265
- D13(1520) resonance: pole mass 1505 MeV, πN coupling g = 1.909, off-shell parameter aπ = -0.910
- S11(1535) resonance: pole mass 1535 MeV, πN coupling g = 2.200, additional width Γ0 = 80 MeV
- P11(1440) resonance: pole mass 1520 MeV, πN coupling g = 6.272, additional width Γ0 = 372 MeV

## Context

This work extends the K-matrix approach previously applied only in the Δ(1232) region by Scholten et al. (1996) to energies up to 1.7 GeV, explicitly including heavier nucleon resonances. It contrasts with fixed-t dispersion relation approaches (Baldin 1960, L'vov 1979) that cannot easily extract resonance electromagnetic decay parameters, and with tree-level calculations by Garcilaso and Moya de Guerra (1993) and Feuster and Mosel (1997) that did not simultaneously fit all three reaction channels. The model enables extraction of N* → Nγ couplings from combined data, though the underestimated electric polarizability suggests the σ-meson approximation for two-pion exchange is too crude, pointing toward improvements needed in modeling the continuum.
