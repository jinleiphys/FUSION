# Event-by-Event Fluctuations from Decay of a Polyakov Loop Condensate

> A model for particle production at the deconfining phase transition in QCD is developed, as the semi-classical decay of a condensate for the Polyakov loop. In such a model, generically particle production, as measured on an event-by-event basis, exhibits significant deviations from statistical behavior.

## Key claim

Particle production from the semi-classical decay of a Polyakov loop condensate near the deconfining phase transition in QCD generates event-by-event fluctuations in the average pion momentum that are significantly larger than purely statistical fluctuations, provided the system is not averaged over too many independent domains.

## Method

The authors model the deconfining phase transition as an instantaneous quench from an initial temperature above T_c to a temperature below T_c, then evolve the Polyakov loop field classically under a mean-field potential fitted to lattice data for the pure glue theory. The oscillating Polyakov loop couples to a chirally symmetric pion field via a Hartree-type interaction, and the resulting pion production is computed by solving the mode equations for the pion field with parametric resonance, including backreaction from the produced pions on the Polyakov loop dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The time-averaged root-mean-square fluctuation of the average pion momentum in a single domain is approximately 10.3% (from Fig. 4, RMS fluctuation of average pion momentum relative to mean).
- The total pion density at time t = 4/f_pi after the quench is approximately 0.35 per fm^3.
- The estimated number of pions per unit rapidity dN/dy is approximately 460 for a nucleus with A ~ 200 and R ~ 7 fm.
- Averaging over approximately 300 domains reduces the RMS fluctuation of the average momentum per pion to about 0.6%.
- If the domain size is ~2 fm instead of ~1 fm, there are about 40 domains per unit rapidity, and the RMS fluctuation increases to about 1.7%.
- The RMS fluctuation of the pion multiplicity in a single domain during the last 1 fm/c is approximately 24%; for 300 domains this gives fluctuations of about 1.4%.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field theory for the Polyakov loop proposed by Pisarski (2000) to particle production, drawing an analogy to preheating in inflationary cosmology (Traschen and Brandenberger, 1990; Kofman, Linde, and Starobinsky, 1997). The model contrasts with Disoriented Chiral Condensates (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993), where the condensate energy density is small relative to the total, and with critical fluctuations at a conjectured QCD critical endpoint (Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, 1998), which require tuning to a specific temperature and chemical potential and produce particles at low momentum. The results enable experimental searches for non-statistical fluctuations at RHIC and LHC, with the prediction that fluctuations should be similar at both colliders and that charge fluctuations should be large (like hadrons, not quarks), in contrast to quasi-particle models (Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller, 2000; Jeon and Koch, 2000).
