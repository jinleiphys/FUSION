# Density functional theory for fermions close to the unitary regime

> We consider interacting Fermi systems close to the unitary regime and compute the corrections to the energy density that are due to a large scattering length and a small effective range. Our approach exploits the universality of the density functional and determines the corrections from the analyical results for the harmonically trapped two-body system. The corrections due to the finite scattering length compare well with the result of Monte Carlo simulations. We also apply our results to symmetric neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading corrections to the energy density of a unitary Fermi gas due to a large but finite scattering length and a small effective range, using the universality of the density functional fitted to the exactly solvable harmonically trapped two-fermion system; the authors estimate a systematic error of 5%-15% on their derived coefficients based on the deviation of their earlier DFT estimate for the universal constant ξ from Monte Carlo results.

## Method

The authors exploit the universality of the density functional by fitting the coefficients of a local density approximation (LDA) energy functional to the analytically known energy eigenvalues of the harmonically trapped two-fermion system. The two-body problem is solved via the parabolic cylinder function, with the boundary condition at the origin expressed through the effective range expansion of the s-wave phase shift. The coefficients c₁ (for the 1/(aρ¹/³) correction) and c₂ (for the r₀ρ¹/³ correction) are determined by inserting the exact two-body density at unitarity into the LDA functional, integrating over space, and equating the result to the exact energy shift from the two-body solution.

## Key numbers

- Universal constant ξ ≈ 0.44 ± 0.01 (Carlson et al., 2003), ξ ≈ 0.42 ± 0.01 (Astrakharchik et al., 2004), and ξ ≈ 0.42 (Bulgac et al., 2005)
- Coefficient c₁ for the 1/(aρ¹/³) correction: c₁ = −0.244 (this work); Monte Carlo results yield c₁ ≈ −0.28 (Chang et al., 2004; Astrakharchik et al., 2004)
- Coefficient c₂ for the r₀ρ¹/³ correction: c₂ = 0.142 (this work, main result)
- Systematic correction to ξ from finite effective range in Monte Carlo calculations: c₂ r₀ ρ¹/³ ≈ 0.007 for Carlson et al. (2003) where r₀ ρ¹/³ ≈ 0.05, and ≈ 0.001 for Astrakharchik et al. (2004) where r₀ ρ¹/³ = 0.01
- For neutron matter: scattering length a = −18.3 fm, effective range r₀ = 2.7 fm

## Context

This work extends the density functional approach of Papenbrock (2005), which determined the universal constant ξ from the two-body system, to include corrections from finite scattering length and effective range. The results for c₁ agree within 13% with Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003) and Astrakharchik et al. (2004), and with the analytical result of Steele (2000). The derived coefficient c₂ enables a systematic correction to Monte Carlo extractions of ξ and is applied to symmetric neutron matter, where the DFT result is compared to the equation of state of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981).
