# Pasta properties of the neutron star within effective relativistic mean-field model

> We study the properties of pasta structures and their influence on the neutron star observables employing the effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model. The compressible liquid drop model is used to incorporate the finite size effects, considering the possibility of nonspherical structures in the inner crust. The unified equation of states are constructed for several E-RMF parameters to study various properties such as pasta mass and thickness in the neutron star's crust. The majority of the pasta properties are sensitive to the symmetry energy in the subsaturation density region. Using the results from Monte Carlo simulations, we estimate the shear modulus of the crust in the context of quasiperiodic oscillations from soft gamma-ray repeaters and calculate the frequency of fundamental torsional oscillation mode in the inner crust. Global properties of the neutron star such as mass-radius profile, the moment of inertia, crustal mass, crustal thickness, and fractional crustal moment of inertia are worked out. The results are consistent with various observational and theoretical constraints.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the properties of nuclear pasta structures (their mass, thickness, and sequence of shapes) in the inner crust of neutron stars are primarily sensitive to the symmetry energy in the subsaturation density region, but this sensitivity is mediated by the specific parametrization of surface and curvature energy fitted to each effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model, with the exact sequence of pasta shapes (spheres, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) depending on whether the crust-core transition density is higher or lower.

## Method

The authors used the compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) within the Wigner-Seitz approximation to compute finite-size effects (surface, curvature, Coulomb energies) for five pasta geometries (sphere, rod, slab, tube, bubble) in the inner crust. They constructed unified equations of state by combining the CLDM inner crust with an outer crust based on the BPS variational formalism using the AME2020 atomic mass evaluation and HFB-26 extrapolated masses, and with a homogeneous core described by thirteen E-RMF parameter sets (BKA24, FSU2, FSUGarnet, G1, G2, G3, GL97, IUFSU, IUFSU*, IOPB-I, SINPA, SINPB, TM1). The equilibrium pasta phase at each baryon density was determined by minimizing the total energy per unit volume, and the resulting unified EoSs were used to solve the TOV equations for global neutron star properties and to compute the shear modulus and fundamental torsional oscillation frequency via Monte Carlo simulation results.

## Key numbers

* The spherical geometry dominates for the majority of the inner crust, extending up to a baryon density of approximately 0.05 fm\(^{-3}\) from the outer crust boundary.
* The relative pasta layer thickness is given by \(\Delta R_p / \Delta R_c \approx (\mu_c - \mu_p) / (\mu_c - \mu_0)\), and the relative pasta layer mass by \(\Delta M_p / \Delta M_c \approx 1 - P_p / P_c\), where \(\mu_c\), \(\mu_p\), \(\mu_0\) are baryon chemical potentials and \(P_p\), \(P_c\) are pressures at the pasta layer bottom and crust-core transition, respectively.
* The shear modulus of the BCC Coulomb lattice including electron screening is \(\mu = 0.1194 (1 - 0.010 Z^{2/3}) (\rho_i (Ze)^2 / a)\), where \(\rho_i\) is the density of nuclei, \(Ze\) the charge, and \(a = R_{WS}\).
* The frequency of the fundamental torsional oscillation mode is \(\omega_0^2 \approx e^{2\nu} V_s^2 (l-1)(l+2) / (2 R R_c)\), where \(e^{2\nu} = 1 - 2M/R\), \(R\) is the star radius, \(R_c\) the crust radius, and \(l\) the angular quantum number.
* The surface tension parameter \(p\) was taken as 3, and the curvature parameter \(\alpha\) as 5.5; varying \(p\) from 2.5 to 3.5 did not change the number of pasta structures but slightly increased the density at which they occur.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Parmar 2022) which considered only spherical shapes in the inner crust, by now including nonspherical pasta configurations (rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) using the same CLDM approach. It builds on the Bayesian inference framework of Newton (2021) and Carreau (2019) for crust properties, and on the shear modulus and torsional oscillation formalism of Gearheart (2011) and Sotani (2012, 2013) in the context of quasiperiodic oscillations from soft gamma repeaters. The results enable future Bayesian studies of neutron star crust observables by providing a computationally fast CLDM method that reproduces crust properties at a level comparable to more expensive Thomas-Fermi calculations (Newton 2013) and Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations (Newton 2022).
