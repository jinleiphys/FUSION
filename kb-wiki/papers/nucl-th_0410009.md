# Dissociation of hadrons in quark matter within finite temperature field theory approach on the light front

> We present a relativistic three-body equation to investigate the properties of nucleons in hot and dense nuclear/quark matter. Within the light front approach we utilize a zero-range interaction to study the three-body dynamics. The relativistic in-medium equation is derived within a systematic Dyson equation approach that includes the dominant medium effects due to Pauli blocking and self energy corrections. We present the in-medium nucleon mass and calculate the dissociation of the three-body system.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic three-body equation for nucleons in hot and dense quark matter, but the results are limited to a zero-range (contact) interaction and an invariant cut-off regularization scheme, meaning the dissociation lines are parameter-dependent and not directly predictive of real QCD.

## Method

The authors derive a relativistic three-body equation on the light front using a Dyson equation approach, incorporating medium effects via Pauli blocking factors (Fermi distribution functions) and self-energy corrections to the quark mass (calculated within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model). They solve the equation for a zero-range interaction with an invariant cut-off Λ, fitting the isolated three-body mass to the proton mass (using a quark mass of 336 MeV) to fix the coupling strength λ(Λ). The in-medium binding energy B₃(T,μ) is then computed, and the Mott lines (where B₃=0) are extracted for two cut-off values.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass set to m = 336 MeV for fitting to the proton mass.
- Two cut-off values used for in-medium calculations: Λ = 4m and Λ = 8m.
- The chiral phase transition temperature T_c is cited as 150 to 175 MeV from lattice calculations (Karsch 2000).
- The binding energy B₃(T,μ) is shown to decrease with increasing chemical potential at constant temperature (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given in text).
- The dissociation (Mott) lines are shown for Λ=4m and Λ=8m (Fig. 2), with different transition temperatures at zero density depending on the cut-off.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of relativistic three-body bound states with contact interactions (Mattiello 2003) to finite temperature and density, using a light-front Dyson approach. The results are compared to the chiral phase transition calculated within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model on the light front (Mattiello, chiral). The Mott lines for nucleon dissociation lie in the broken phase, and the difference between the Mott line and the chiral transition may indicate a confinement-deconfinement region. The approach enables future studies of three-quark correlations in the QCD phase diagram, though a treatment of spins is left for later work.
