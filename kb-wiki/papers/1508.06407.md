# Nuclear incompressibility parameters evaluated from isoscalar giant monopole resonance of $N=Z$, $A=100,132$ nuclide and Sn isotopes

> The isoscaler giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) are computed using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) with five kinds of Skyrme parameter sets (SGII, SkM$^*$, SLy4, SkT3 and SkI3). To extract the nuclear matter property from finite system, ISGMRs of $N$=$Z$ ($Z$=20 - 50), isobar even-even nuclide for $A$=100, 132 and Sn isotopes are analysed systematically. The magnitude relation of nuclear incompressibility-parameter ($K_\infty$) among Skyrme parameter sets, can be corresponded to the peak positions of GMR in spherical isotopes over $A$=80. The parameters ($K_{\rm surf}, K_Ï„$ and $K_{\rm Coul}$) which appear in expansion of the finite nucleus incompressibility $K_A$, are determined for each Skyrme parameter. From the comparison experimental data whole mass region and the present results, they indicate that the isospin dependent term $K_Ï„$ is filtered as -305$\pm$10 MeV. The incompressibility parameters of {\it infinite} system corresponding to our results is $K_Ï„^\infty$=-340$\pm$35, $K_\infty$=225$\pm$11, and $K_{\rm sym}$=-138$\pm$18 MeV.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent term of the finite-nucleus incompressibility expansion, $K_\tau$, is determined to be -305$\pm$10 MeV from comparison of Cb-TDHFB calculations with experimental ISGMR data across the whole mass region, though the analysis relies on the assumptions of Eq. (EGMR) and Eq. (KAex) and the mass dependence of experimental $E_{\rm GMR}$ deviates from a simple $A^{-1/3}$ scaling.

## Method

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) was computed using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory in three-dimensional coordinate space with five Skyrme parameter sets (SGII, SkM$^*$, SLy4, SkT3, SkI3). The centroid energies $\bar{E}_{\rm GMR}$ were evaluated using the $m_1/m_0$ moment ratio of the strength function, and the finite-nucleus incompressibility $K_A$ was extracted via $E_{\rm GMR}=\sqrt{\hbar^2 K_A / m \langle r^2 \rangle}$. The expansion coefficients $K_{\rm surf}$, $K_\tau$, and $K_{\rm Coul}$ in $K_A = K_\infty + K_{\rm surf}A^{-1/3} + K_\tau ((N-Z)/A)^2 + K_{\rm Coul} Z^2 A^{-4/3}$ were determined by chi-square fitting to $N=Z$ nuclide results (for $K_{\rm surf}$ and $K_{\rm Coul}$) and to $A=100,132$ isobar results (for $K_\tau$), excluding deformed nuclei from the analysis.

## Key numbers

- $K_\tau$ = -305$\pm$10 MeV (isospin-dependent term of finite-nucleus incompressibility, filtered from comparison with experimental data across the whole mass region)
- $K_\tau^\infty$ = -340$\pm$35 MeV (infinite-system isospin incompressibility parameter)
- $K_\infty$ = 225$\pm$11 MeV (infinite-system symmetric-matter incompressibility)
- $K_{\rm sym}$ = -138$\pm$18 MeV (symmetry incompressibility)
- For SGII: $K_\infty$ = 214.6 MeV, $K_{\rm surf}$ = -273.7 MeV, $K_\tau$ = -295.9 MeV, $K_{\rm Coul}$ = -4.52 MeV (case without double-magic nuclei)
- For SkM$^*$: $K_\infty$ = 216.6 MeV, $K_{\rm surf}$ = -285.3 MeV, $K_\tau$ = -282.0 MeV, $K_{\rm Coul}$ = -4.76 MeV (case without double-magic nuclei)
- For SLy4: $K_\infty$ = 229.9 MeV, $K_{\rm surf}$ = -318.4 MeV, $K_\tau$ = -314.3 MeV, $K_{\rm Coul}$ = -4.73 MeV (case without double-magic nuclei)
- For SkT3: $K_\infty$ = 235.7 MeV, $K_{\rm surf}$ = -326.1 MeV, $K_\tau$ = -305.1 MeV, $K_{\rm Coul}$ = -4.54 MeV (case without double-magic nuclei)
- For SkI3: $K_\infty$ = 258.2 MeV, $K_{\rm surf}$ = -372.6 MeV, $K_\tau$ = -352.0 MeV, $K_{\rm Coul}$ = -4.75 MeV (case without double-magic nuclei)
- The expansion Eq. (KAex) with determined coefficients reproduces the directly calculated $\bar{E}_{\rm GMR}$ of Sn isotopes within 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of nuclear incompressibility from ISGMR measurements using the $K_A$ expansion approach, following the framework of Blaizot (1980) and similar analyses by Li et al. (2007), Patel et al. (2012, 2013), and Stone et al. (2014). The results narrow the range of $K_\tau$ compared to previous studies and provide constraints on the infinite-matter incompressibility parameters $K_\infty$, $K_\tau^\infty$, and $K_{\rm sym}$. The finding that the experimental $E_{\rm GMR}$ mass dependence deviates from $A^{-1/3}$ scaling, requiring an additional $A^{-1/6}$ term analogous to the Goldhaber-Teller model for the giant dipole resonance, suggests that the simple one-phonon picture underlying Eq. (EGMR) may need refinement for heavy nuclei. This enables future work to test the extracted parameters against other nuclear observables such as neutron-skin thickness and dipole polarizability, and to improve the connection between finite-nucleus measurements and the nuclear equation of state for astrophysical applications.
