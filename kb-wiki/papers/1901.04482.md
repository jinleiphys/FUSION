> This report summarises the main findings of the QCD Working Group in the CERN Physics Beyond Colliders Study.

# Physics Beyond Colliders: QCD Working Group Report

## Key claim

The COMPASS++ project proposes a "New QCD facility at the M2 beam line of the CERN SPS" that could address open questions in hadron structure, but the full program requires 11-12 years of running time and depends critically on the development of RF-separated hadron beams, which would only become available after Long Shutdown 3 (LS3) at the earliest.

## Method

The report surveys proposed measurements at the existing M2 beam line using muon, conventional hadron, and RF-separated hadron beams, including muon-proton elastic scattering for the proton charge radius, pion-induced Drell-Yan for pion parton distribution functions (PDFs), kaon polarisability via the Primakov reaction, and strange-meson spectroscopy. Performance projections are based on Monte Carlo simulations and comparisons to existing data from COMPASS, MAMI, and other experiments, with feasibility studies conducted in parallel by the PBC Conventional Beams activity.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius extraction: target precision of 0.01 fm from electric form factor measurement in the Q² range 0.001 GeV² < Q² < 0.02 GeV²
- Pion-induced Drell-Yan: 280 days of running at 190 GeV beam energy on carbon target would allow improved pion PDF extraction for x_π > 0.1
- Kaon polarisability: projected statistical precision of 0.03 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (5% error relative to one-loop χPT prediction of α_K = 0.64 × 10⁻⁴ fm³) from 5 × 10¹² kaons at 100 GeV
- Kaon Primakov reaction: estimated 6 × 10⁵ K⁻γ events in kinematic range 0.1 < x_γ < 0.6 and M(K⁻γ) < 0.8 GeV after one year
- RF-separated beam intensity: 5 × 10⁶ s⁻¹ for kaon beams at ~100 GeV
- Total budget estimate: 10 to 20 million CHF for new detectors and spectrometer upgrades
- Collaboration size: approximately 250 physicists

## Context

This report extends the COMPASS-II program (Gautheron 2010) and addresses the proton charge radius puzzle that emerged from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010), which disagreed with earlier electron-scattering and hydrogen-spectroscopy results. The proposed measurements would complement ongoing efforts including MUSE at PSI (Gilman 2017), PRad at Jefferson Lab (Gasparian 2011), and future measurements at MAMI and MAGIX/MESA, while the kaon polarisability and strange-meson spectroscopy programs would provide unique benchmarks for low-energy QCD in the three-flavour sector that no other existing or planned facility can perform.
