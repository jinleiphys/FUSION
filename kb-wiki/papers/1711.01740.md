# Semiclassical theory of strong localization for quantum thermalization

> We introduce a semiclassical theory for strong localization that may arise in the context of many-body thermalization. As a minimal model for thermalization we consider a few-site Bose-Hubbard model consisting of two weakly interacting subsystems that can exchange particles. The occupation of a subsystem ($x$) satisfies in the classical treatment a Fokker-Planck equation with a diffusion coefficient $D(x)$. We demonstrate that it is possible to deduce from the classical description a quantum breaktime $t^*$, and hence the manifestations of a strong localization effect. For this purpose it is essential to take the geometry of the energy shell into account, and to make a distinction between different notions of phasespace exploration.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a semiclassical theory for strong quantum localization in many-body thermalization, arguing that the quantum breaktime \(t^*\), and hence the localization volume, can be deduced from a classical Fokker-Planck description by accounting for the geometry of the energy shell and distinguishing between phase-space "spreading" and "exploration," provided the system is classically chaotic within the relevant energy range.

## Method

The authors study a four-site Bose-Hubbard model consisting of a chaotic trimer weakly coupled to a monomer, with \(N=60\) bosons and an effective Planck constant \(1/N\). They compute the classical diffusion coefficient \(D(x)\) from a Fokker-Planck equation for the trimer occupation \(x\), and derive a quantum breaktime \(t^*\) by formulating a quantum-classical correspondence (QCC) condition that compares the semiclassical exploration volume \(\Omega^{\text{cl}}_t\) with the quantum exploration \(\mathcal{N}^{\text{sc}}_t\), incorporating the energy-shell width \(\mathcal{N}_E\) and the number of Planck cells \(\Omega_E\) intersecting a given energy surface. The localization measure \(\mathcal{F}^{\text{s}} = \Omega^{\text{qm}}_\infty / \Omega^{\text{sc}}_\infty\) is then computed from quantum and semiclassical saturation profiles.

## Key numbers

- The effective Planck constant is \(1/N\) with \(N=60\) bosons.
- The quantum ergodicity measure \(\mathcal{F}^{\text{qm}}_\text{erg} = 1/3\) is used to account for universal quantum fluctuations of the GOE type.
- Strong localization is observed for initial trimer occupations in the ranges \(25 \leq x_0 \leq 29\) and \(x_0 \geq 56\) (out of \(N=60\) total particles), at scaled energy \(\varepsilon_0 = 1.181\).
- The semiclassical spreading volume \(\Omega^{\text{sc}}_E\) is given by \(\sqrt{\mathcal{N}_E^2 + \Omega_E^2}\), where \(\mathcal{N}_E\) is the number of unperturbed states in the energy shell and \(\Omega_E\) is the number of Planck cells intersecting a typical energy surface.

## Context

This work extends the neglected paradigm of quantum localization as a breakdown of quantum-classical correspondence, previously applied to Anderson-type models in \(d=1,2,3\) dimensions by the authors of brk1, brk2, brk3, brk4 (1990s), and adapts it to a minimal many-body thermalization model. It builds on preliminary numerical evidence for localization in the four-site Bose-Hubbard model from tmn (2015), and uses phase-space exploration concepts from Heller (1980) and scar theory. The theory enables the semiclassical determination of the mobility edge and localization volume without explicit interference calculations, and is essential for discussing long-time localization in high-dimensional chaotic systems where Arnold diffusion prevents classical localization.
