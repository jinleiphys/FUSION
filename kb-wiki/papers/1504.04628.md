# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors in two-flavour QCD

> We present results for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, including the momentum transfer dependence and derived quantities (charge radii and magnetic moment). The analysis is performed using O(a) improved Wilson fermions in Nf=2 QCD measured on the CLS ensembles. Particular focus is placed on a systematic evaluation of the influence of excited states in three-point correlation functions, which lead to a biased evaluation, if not accounted for correctly. We argue that the use of summed operator insertions and fit ansätze including excited states allow us to suppress and control this effect. We employ a novel method to perform joint chiral and continuum extrapolations, by fitting the form factors directly to the expressions of covariant baryonic chiral effective field theory. The final results for the charge radii and magnetic moment from our lattice calculations include, for the first time, a full error budget. We find that our estimates are compatible with experimental results within their overall uncertainties.

## Key claim

The authors present lattice QCD results for nucleon electromagnetic form factors that are compatible with experimental values within overall uncertainties, but they emphasize that this agreement is achieved only after a systematic evaluation and control of excited-state contamination in three-point correlation functions, using summed operator insertions and fit ansatze including excited states.

## Method

The analysis uses O(a) improved Wilson fermions in Nf=2 QCD on CLS ensembles at multiple lattice spacings and pion masses. The form factors are extracted from three-point correlation functions using three methods: a plateau method at source-sink separation of 1.1 fm, a summation method, and explicit two-state fits. A novel joint chiral and continuum extrapolation is performed by fitting the form factors directly to expressions from covariant baryonic chiral effective field theory.

- On the B6 ensemble (a=0.079 fm, m_pi=268 MeV): G_E at Q^2=0.104 GeV^2 is 0.826(0.008) from plateau method, 0.785(0.023) from summation, and 0.760(0.018) from two-state fit

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of nucleon form factors by groups such as LHPC (Bratt et al., 2010), QCDSF/UKQCD (Gockeler et al., 2010), and ETM (Alexandrou et al., 2011), with a particular focus on controlling excited-state contamination that had been identified as a potential source of systematic bias. The use of covariant baryonic chiral effective field theory for joint chiral and continuum extrapolations represents a methodological advance over earlier approaches that used polynomial fits. The results enable more reliable comparisons with experimental data from the A1 Collaboration at Mainz (Bernauer et al., 2010, 2014) and Jefferson Lab (Jones et al., 2000; Gayou et al., 2002; Punjabi et al., 2005; Puckett et al., 2010), and provide a foundation for future calculations with dynamical strange quarks and at lighter pion masses.
