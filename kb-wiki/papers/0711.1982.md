# The physical meaning of scattering matrix singularities in coupled-channel formalisms

> The physical meaning of bare and dressed scattering matrix singularities has been investigated. Special attention has been attributed to the role of well known invariance of scattering matrix with respect to the field transformation of the effective Lagrangian. Examples of evaluating bare and dressed quantities in various models are given.

## Key claim

Bare scattering matrix singularities (such as bare masses of resonances) extracted from coupled-channel formalisms are inherently model-dependent and cannot be given a unique physical meaning, because the invariance of the scattering matrix under field transformations of the effective Lagrangian makes it impossible to separate off-shell effects from contact terms in a model-independent way.

## Method

The authors examine the field-theoretic equivalence theorem, which states that observables are unchanged under nonlinear field redefinitions, and apply it to a toy model of two nucleon-like states (N and R) coupled to a scalar field (sigma). They compare two different interaction Lagrangians that produce the same on-shell observables but yield different self-energies for the R field at one-loop order, demonstrating that bare masses extracted from fits to data depend on the choice of Lagrangian. They also illustrate the same principle using chiral perturbation theory for pi-pi scattering, showing that different parameterizations of the pion field give identical on-shell scattering amplitudes but different off-shell behavior, with the difference canceled by contact terms in processes such as bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

* The toy model uses two nucleon-like states (N and R) coupled to a scalar field (sigma) with coupling constant g and bare mass M_R^0 for the R field.
* In the chiral perturbation theory example, the pion mass M_pi and decay constant F_pi appear as parameters; the pi-pi scattering amplitude at lowest order contains terms proportional to (s - M_pi^2)/F_pi^2.
* The pseudoscalar density coupling constant G_pi is shown to equal 2 B F_pi, where B is related to the quark condensate in the chiral limit.

## Context

This paper is a workshop summary from the BRAG2007 pre-meeting of the NSTAR2007 Workshop, collecting different perspectives on the relation between bare and dressed scattering matrix singularities. It extends the long-standing discussion of how to compare quark model predictions (which give bare/quenched mass spectra) with experimental scattering matrix poles, following earlier work by Hoehler on the model dependence of Breit-Wigner parameters and by Scherer and Fearing (1998) on the unmeasurability of off-shell effects. The conclusion that dressed scattering matrix poles are the only model-independent meeting point between theory and experiment enables a clear separation: bare quantities from coupled-channel models remain legitimate only within a well-defined model, and future work must account for hadronic mass shifts produced by off-shell ambiguities.
