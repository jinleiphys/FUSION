# The phase diagram in $T-μ-N_c$ space

> We examine the phase diagram of hadronic matter when the number of colors, as well as temperature and density, are varied. We show that in this regime several new phase transitions are possible, and we examine issues related to these transitions.

# The phase diagram in $T-\mu-N_c$ space

## Key claim
The paper argues that varying the number of colors $N_c$ alongside temperature and density reveals several new phase transitions in hadronic matter, but these results are conjectural and the connections between the three different approaches presented (Van der Waals model, percolation theory, and holographic quantum gravity) remain speculative rather than rigorously established.

## Method
The authors use three separate theoretical approaches: (1) a Van der Waals equation of state with $N_c$-dependent parameters to interpolate between nuclear matter at $N_c=3$ and quarkyonic matter at large $N_c$; (2) a percolation model where the probability of quark exchange between neighboring baryons depends on $N_c$, with the link probability scaling as $N_c^{-2}$ for gluon exchange; (3) a holographic calculation in $AdS_3$ where quantum gravity corrections smear the black hole singularity to a Gaussian of width $\ell$, with the ratio $q = \ell/L_{ADS}$ playing the role of $N_f/N_c$ in the dual gauge theory.

## Key numbers
- $\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 200-300$ MeV (the $N_c$-invariant scale of the theory)
- $N_N \sim \order{10}$ is required to interpolate between the nuclear phase diagram at $N_c=3$ ($\rho_c \ll \Lambda_{QCD}^3$, $T_c \ll \Lambda_{QCD}$) and the quarkyonic phase diagram at $N_c \rightarrow \infty$ ($\rho_c \sim \Lambda_{QCD}^3$, $T_c \sim \Lambda_{QCD}$)
- 3D bond-percolation thresholds: $p_c \sim 0.25$ for simple-cubic, $0.18$ for body-centered-cubic, and $0.12$ for hexagonal-close-packed lattice
- Critical $q = \ell/L_{ADS} \simeq 0.18$ for the Hawking-Page phase transition to become a cross-over in the holographic calculation
- The critical number of colors for percolation is significantly larger than three for $r_T \sim \Lambda_{QCD}^{-1}$, $\lambda \sim 1$ (shown in contour plots covering $2 \le N_c \le 80$)

## Context
This work extends the large-$N_c$ limit approach of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) by examining the phase diagram when $N_c$ is varied continuously rather than taken to infinity. It addresses known discrepancies between large-$N_c$ predictions and $N_c=3$ phenomenology: deconfinement is a phase transition at $N_c=\infty$ but a cross-over at $N_c=3$ (Pollatsek, 2004), and nuclear matter is a strongly bound crystal at large $N_c$ (Klebanov, 1990) but a liquid at $N_c=3$. The paper connects to the concept of quarkyonic matter (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007) and suggests that percolation phenomena may provide a mechanism for the transition between these regimes. The holographic calculation relates to the Hawking-Page transition (Hawking and Page, 1983) and its dual interpretation as the deconfinement transition in gauge/string duality (Maldacena, 1998).
