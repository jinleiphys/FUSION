# Global polarization of $Λ$, $Ξ^{-}$, and $Ω^{-}$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC BES-II energies

> We investigate the global spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and the multi-strange hyperons $Ξ^{-}$ and $Ω^{-}$ in Au+Au collisions across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan II (BES-II) energy range, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$--$27$ GeV. The polarization is computed using the modified Cooper--Frye formula, which includes contributions from thermal vorticity, the thermal shear tensor, and the gradient of the baryon chemical potential, combined with the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic framework CLVisc with SMASH initial conditions. We present the global polarization as a function of collision energy, centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity. We find that the global polarization of $Ω^{-}$ is systematically larger than those of $Λ$ and $Ξ^{-}$ because of its larger spin quantum number, but it remains below the central value of the recent STAR measurement. This discrepancy may suggest that additional mechanisms, such as spin correlations among strange quarks inside the $Ω^{-}$, could contribute to the observed $Ω^{-}$ polarization. We also find that the global-polarization splitting between hyperons and anti-hyperons increases toward lower collision energies and is dominated by the chemical-potential-gradient contribution.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Omega- hyperons is systematically larger than that of Lambda and Xi- due to its larger spin quantum number (3/2), but the computed values remain below the central value of the recent STAR measurement, suggesting that additional mechanisms such as spin correlations among strange quarks inside the Omega- may contribute to the observed polarization.

## Method

The authors employed the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic framework CLVisc with SMASH initial conditions to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7-27 GeV. The global spin polarization was computed using the modified Cooper-Frye formula, which includes contributions from thermal vorticity, the thermal shear tensor, and the gradient of the baryon chemical potential. Feed-down effects were incorporated following Refs. Becattini (2016), Karpenko (2016), Li (2017), Xia (2019), and Li (2021).

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of all three hyperon species (Lambda, Xi-, Omega-) increases as collision energy decreases from sqrt(s_NN)=27 GeV down to 7.7 GeV in 20-50% central Au+Au collisions
- For Omega- hyperons, the simple estimate gives P_Omega approximately 5P_Lambda/3 when mass differences and feed-down effects are neglected, based on spin quantum number S=3/2 for Omega- versus S=1/2 for Lambda
- Hyperon masses used: m_Lambda=1.116 GeV, m_Xi=1.322 GeV, m_Omega=1.672 GeV
- Freeze-out energy density chosen as epsilon_f=0.4 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to T_f=158 MeV in the zero-chemical-potential limit
- Kinematic cuts: |Y|<1.5 and 0.5<p_T<6 GeV/c for the energy dependence results
- Hydrodynamic initial proper time tau_0 ranges from 3.2 fm at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV to 1.0 fm at sqrt(s_NN)=27 GeV, with R=6.38 fm for the Au nucleus radius
- Specific shear viscosity C_eta_v varies from 0.20 at lower energies to 0.12 at sqrt(s_NN)=27 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Lambda hyperon polarization (Becattini 2013, Karpenko 2016, Li 2017, Wu 2022) to multi-strange hyperons Xi- and Omega- across the RHIC BES-II energy range. It provides systematic predictions for the global polarization as functions of collision energy, centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity, enabling comparison with upcoming high-statistics STAR BES-II data. The finding that computed Omega- polarization remains below the experimental central value points toward the need for future theoretical work on short-range spin correlations among strange quarks inside the Omega-, as suggested by Lv (2024), Zhang (2024), and Lv (2026).
