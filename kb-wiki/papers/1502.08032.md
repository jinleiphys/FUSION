# Pion Production in High-Energy Neutrino Reactions with Nuclei

> [Background] A quantitative understanding of neutrino interactions with nuclei is needed for precision era neutrino long baseline experiments (MINOS, NOvA, DUNE) which all use nuclear targets. Pion production is the dominant reaction channel at the energies of these experiments. [Purpose] Investigate the influence of nuclear effects on neutrino-induced pion production cross sections and compare predictions for pion-production with available data. [Method] The Giessen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model is used for the description of all incohrent channels in neutrino-nucleus reactions. [Results] Differential cross sections for charged and neutral pion production for the MINER$ν$A neutrino and antineutrino flux are calculated. An estimate for the coherent cross section is obtained from a comparison of data with theoretical results. The invariant mass ($W$) distribution of the $Δ$ resonances produced is analyzed. [Conclusions] Final state interactions affect the pion kinetic energy spectra significantly. The data for charged pion production at MINER$ν$A are compatible with the results of calculations using elementary data taken from an old Argonne National Laboratory experiment. Remaining differences can be attributed to coherent production; the data for antineutrino induced neutral pion production, where no coherent contribution is present, are reproduced quite well. In contrast, the MiniBooNE data, taken at a lower energy, are consistently higher than the theoretical description. The analysis of $W$-distributions shows that experimental cuts on this quantity lead to shape distortions for nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model reproduces the MINERνA charged-pion data reasonably well when using elementary cross sections from the older Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) experiment, but only after attributing the remaining discrepancies to coherent pion production; the MiniBooNE data at lower energies are consistently higher than the theoretical description, indicating an inconsistency between the two experimental datasets.

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann–Uehling–Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model factorizes neutrino-nucleus reactions into an initial interaction with a bound, Fermi-moving nucleon followed by final-state interactions (FSI) described via a numerical implementation of the Kadanoff-Baym equations. The nuclear ground state is a local relativistic Fermi gas with a coordinate- and momentum-dependent potential; resonance production uses vector form factors from MAID electron-scattering data and axial form factors fitted to elementary pion-production data from ANL or BNL, with the ANL set taken as default. Fixed-energy cross sections are folded with the MINERνA flux (1.5–10 GeV) and, for charged-pion comparisons, a cut on reconstructed invariant mass \(W_{\text{rec}} < 1.4\) GeV is applied.

## Key numbers

* Charged-pion kinetic energy distribution (neutrino, CH target, MINERνA flux, \(W_{\text{rec}} < 1.4\) GeV): the calculated cross section with ANL input lies at the lower end of the experimental error bars for \(T_\pi > 0.1\) GeV; the biggest disagreement is for \(T_\pi < 0.1\) GeV.
* Angular distribution of charged pions (same conditions): at \(\theta \approx 30^\circ\) the calculated cross section is about 1/2 of the measured one; agreement is good for \(\theta \gtrsim 50^\circ\).
* Difference between experimental and calculated charged-pion kinetic energy distribution: a modest peak at about 0.14 GeV and a clear peak at about 0.04 GeV, with the difference rising to about \(0.5 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\)/GeV at 0.04 GeV.
* Integral of the difference (charged pions, \(W_{\text{rec}} < 1.4\) GeV): about \(1.9 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\), compared to the MINERνA measured coherent cross section of about \(3.5 \times 10^{-39}\) cm\(^2\) (without the \(W\) cut).
* Charged-pion spectrum without \(W_{\text{rec}}\) cut is larger by about a factor of 1.7 at the maximum compared to the cut spectrum.
* Neutral-pion production (antineutrino, no \(W\) cut): the calculated angular distribution reproduces the MINERνA data quite well, with no coherent contribution present in this channel.
* Calculated MINERνA charged-pion cross section is roughly a factor of 1.5 larger than the calculated MiniBooNE cross section (both with ANL input, no \(W\) cut).

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model calculations of Lalakulich et al. (2012) and Mosel (2014) to a detailed comparison with MINERνA data, using the ANL elementary cross sections favored by the reanalysis of Wilkinson et al. (2014). It contrasts with the MiniBooNE results, which are consistently higher than GiBUU predictions, and with the work of Yu et al. (2014) that uses the Adler-Nussinov-Paschos model and fails to describe the MINERνA data due to missing pion-nucleon interactions. The analysis enables improved tuning of event generators for DUNE and highlights the need for a complete theory of coherent pion production, as the Rein-Sehgal model implemented in GENIE and NEUT shows poor agreement with MINERνA coherent data (Higuera et al., 2014).
