# A Matrix Element for Chaotic Tunnelling Rates and Scarring Intensities

> It is shown that tunnelling splittings in ergodic double wells and resonant widths in ergodic metastable wells can be approximated as easily-calculated matrix elements involving the wavefunction in the neighbourhood of a certain real orbit. This orbit is a continuation of the complex orbit which crosses the barrier with minimum imaginary action. The matrix element is computed by integrating across the orbit in a surface of section representation, and uses only the wavefunction in the allowed region and the stability properties of the orbit. When the real orbit is periodic, the matrix element is a natural measure of the degree of scarring of the wavefunction. This scarring measure is canonically invariant and independent of the choice of surface of section, within semiclassical error. The result can alternatively be interpretated as the autocorrelation function of the state with respect to a transfer operator which quantises a certain complex surface of section mapping. The formula provides an efficient numerical method to compute tunnelling rates while avoiding the need for the exceedingly precise diagonalisation endemic to numerical tunnelling calculations.

## Key claim

Tunnelling splittings in ergodic double wells and resonant widths in ergodic metastable wells can be approximated as easily-calculated matrix elements involving the wavefunction in the neighbourhood of a certain real orbit, provided the wavefunction is already known from numerical diagonalisation or other means; the formula avoids the need for the exceedingly precise diagonalisation endemic to numerical tunnelling calculations.

## Method

The authors start from Herring's formula, which expresses the tunnelling splitting as a surface integral of the wavefunction and its normal derivative across a section separating the two wells. They then use semiclassical Green's functions constructed from complex trajectories (following the approach of Auerbach and Kivelson and of Wilkinson and Hannay) to extend the wavefunction from the allowed region into the forbidden region. The splitting is expressed as a matrix element computed by integrating across a real orbit in a surface of section representation, using only the wavefunction in the allowed region and the stability properties of the orbit; this real orbit is a continuation of the complex bounce orbit that crosses the barrier with minimum imaginary action.

## Key numbers

* The mean splitting function is given by \( f_0(E) = \frac{1}{\pi} \frac{e^{-K_0/\hbar}}{\sqrt{(-1)^{d-1}\chi_\R \det(W_0 - I)}} \), where \(K_0\) is the imaginary action of the bounce orbit, \(W_0\) is the complex monodromy matrix, \(d\) is the dimension, and \(\chi_\R\) is a sign fixed by the symmetry operation.
* The normalised splittings are defined as \( y_n = \frac{\rho_0(E_n)}{f_0(E_n)} \Delta E_n \), where \(\rho_0(E_n)\) is the average density of states within one well and \(f_0(E_n)\) is the mean splitting function; these normalised splittings have average value of unity by construction.
* The potential used for numerical testing is \( V(x,y) = (x^2-1)^4 + x^2y^2 + \mu y^2 \) with \(\mu = 0.1\), forming two wells symmetric in \(x\) separated by a saddle at the origin with energy \(E=1\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Auerbach and Kivelson and of Wilkinson and Hannay, who used semiclassical Green's functions from complex trajectories to calculate spectral tunnelling effects. The paper provides a formula that relates tunnelling rates directly to the morphology of the wavefunction in the allowed region, which can serve as a starting point for statistical analyses and for semiclassical analysis using Green's functions. When the underlying trajectory is periodic, the matrix element becomes a scarring weight of the wavefunction, quantifying the connection between scarring and tunnelling that has been argued to mediate current-voltage characteristics in tunnelling diodes under crossed electric and magnetic fields.
