# Hard Photoproduction at HERA

> In view of possible photoproduction studies in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, we briefly review the present theoretical understanding of photons and hard photoproduction processes at HERA, discussing the production of jets, light and heavy hadrons, quarkonia, and prompt photons. We address in particular the extraction of the strong coupling constant from photon structure function and inclusive jet measurements, the infrared safety and computing time of jet definitions, the sensitivity of dijet cross sections on the parton densities in the photon, factorization breaking in diffractive dijet production, the treatment of the heavy-quark mass in charm production, the relevance of the color-octet mechanism for quarkonium production, and isolation criteria for prompt photons.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical understanding of hard photoproduction at HERA, arguing that while many QCD-based predictions (e.g., for jet cross sections, heavy-quark production, and prompt photons) show reasonable agreement with data, significant open questions remain in diffractive and non-relativistic regimes, and these will likely persist until a new electron-hadron collider is built.

## Method

The author reviews the QCD-improved parton model for photoproduction, distinguishing direct and resolved photon interactions, and discusses the factorization of collinear singularities at next-to-leading order (NLO). The review covers inclusive and diffractive jet production, light and heavy hadron production (including charm and quarkonia), and prompt photon production, comparing theoretical predictions (e.g., fixed-order, variable-flavor-number, and NRQCD calculations) to HERA data from the H1 and ZEUS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The Weizsacker-Williams approximation for the photon flux includes subleading non-logarithmic terms that modify the cross section typically by 5% (Kes 1975).
- The longitudinally invariant \(k_T\) clustering algorithm scales numerically with the cubic power of the number \(N\) of particles; the FastJet code scales only with \(N\ln N\) (Cacciari 2006).
- In diffractive dijet photoproduction, a suppression factor \(S\) of 0.34 is applied to resolved processes only (Kaidalov 2003).
- For \(J/\psi\) production, the color-singlet contribution shows an order-of-magnitude discrepancy with the measured \(p_T\)-spectrum at the Tevatron.
- The photon isolation criterion uses a cone of size \(R\) and limits hadronic energy to \(E_{(T)}^{\rm had} < \epsilon_{(T)} E_{(T),\gamma}\).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed for photoproduction at HERA (Klasen 2002) and addresses open questions in diffractive factorization breaking (Klasen 2004a, 2004b, 2005) and heavy-quark production (Cacciari 2001, Kniehl 2005). It enables future studies at the LHC via ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions and motivates the eventual construction of a new electron-hadron collider such as eRHIC or an International Linear Collider.
