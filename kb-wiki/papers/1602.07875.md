# The Skyrme-TQRPA calculations of electron capture on hot nuclei in pre-supernova environment

> We combine the thermal QRPA approach with the Skyrme energy density functional theory (Skyrme-TQRPA) for modelling the process of electron capture on nuclei in supernova environment. For a sample nucleus, $^{56}$Fe, the Skyrme-TQRPA approach is applied to analyze thermal effects on the strength function of GT$_+$ transitions which dominate electron capture at $E_e\le 30$~MeV. Several Skyrme interactions are used in order to verify the sensitivity of the obtained results to the Skyrme force parameters. Finite-temperature cross sections are calculated and the results are compared with those of the other model calculations.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-TQRPA approach predicts that the Brink hypothesis (which assumes GT$_+$ strength distributions on excited states are identical to the ground state) fails for hot nuclei, and that downward (exoergic) GT$_+$ transitions from thermally excited states become possible at finite temperature, enabling electron capture at arbitrarily low incident electron energies.

## Method

The authors combine the thermal quasiparticle random-phase approximation (TQRPA) with Skyrme energy density functional theory using a finite-rank separable approximation for the residual particle-hole interaction. They solve finite-temperature BCS equations to obtain thermal quasiparticles, then diagonalize the thermal Hamiltonian to find charge-exchange phonon states, from which the temperature-dependent GT$_+$ strength function and electron capture cross sections are computed for the sample nucleus $^{56}$Fe.

## Key numbers

- The GT$_+$ resonance in $^{56}$Fe shifts downward by about 1.5 MeV when temperature increases to 1 MeV, due primarily to vanishing of pairing correlations above the critical temperature $T_\mathrm{cr} \approx 0.8$ MeV.
- At $T=0$, the proton and neutron pairing gaps for $^{56}$Fe are $\Delta_p = 1.57$ MeV and $\Delta_n = 1.36$ MeV, respectively.
- The ground-state reaction threshold $Q = M(^{56}\mathrm{Mn}) - M(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}) = 4.21$ MeV.
- The total GT$_+$ strength at $T=0$ from BCS calculations is $S_+ \approx 10\text{--}11$, while the experimental value is $S_+ = 2.9 \pm 0.3$ (requiring a quenching factor of 0.74 applied to the axial coupling constant $g_A = -1.26$ to obtain $g_A^* = -0.93$).
- The difference between FTRPA and TQRPA transition energies is $E_\mathrm{FTRPA} - E_\mathrm{TQRPA} = 2.92$ MeV, explaining the $\sim 3$ MeV shift in cross sections between the two methods.
- The spread in Skyrme-TQRPA cross sections across different Skyrme forces (SLy4, SGII, SkM*) is less than an order of magnitude at low energies and temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TQRPA calculations (Dzhioev et al., 2009, 2010) that used a phenomenological QPM Hamiltonian with Saxon-Woods mean field, by implementing self-consistent Skyrme energy density functionals for improved predictive power, especially for nuclei far from stability. The results contradict the Brink hypothesis used in large-scale shell-model calculations (Caurier et al., 1999; Langanke et al., 2001) and show that finite-temperature RPA approaches (Paar et al., 2011; Fantina et al., 2012; Niu et al., 2013) miss the downward GT$_+$ transitions that dominate low-energy electron capture. The method enables future calculations for neutron-rich nuclei beyond the reach of shell-model approaches, and can be extended by including phonon coupling or nuclear deformation.
