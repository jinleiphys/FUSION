# Simple effective interaction: Infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei

> The mean field properties and equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter are studied by using a simple effective interaction which has a single finite range Gaussian term. The study of finite nuclei with this effective interaction is done by means of constructing a quasilocal energy density functional for which the single particle equations take the form of Skryme-Hartree-Fock equations. The predictions of binding energies and charge radii of spherical nuclei are found to be compatible with the results of standard models as well as experimental data.

## Key claim

The Simple Effective Interaction (SEI) with a single finite-range Gaussian term can reproduce finite nuclei properties with quality comparable to traditional Skyrme, Gogny, and M3Y forces, provided the two remaining parameters not fixed by nuclear matter constraints are determined from finite nuclei experimental data.

## Method

The authors first determine nine of the eleven SEI parameters from infinite nuclear matter properties, including saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and the momentum dependence of the mean field constrained by optical potential data. They then construct a quasilocal energy density functional using a semiclassical ℏ-expansion of the density matrix, which reduces the single-particle equations to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock form. The two remaining parameters (t₀ and x₀) are fixed by fitting binding energies and charge radii of spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.157 fm⁻³
- Energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter e(ρ₀) = -16.0 MeV
- Incompressibility K(ρ₀) = 245 MeV (obtained with γ = 1/2)
- Effective mass at saturation m*/m = 0.709
- Symmetry energy E_s(ρ₀) = 35.0 MeV
- Slope parameter L(ρ₀) = 76.3 MeV (within the range 70 ± 15 MeV from FRDM predictions)
- Exchange strength ε_ex = -94.46 MeV and range α = 0.7596 fm
- Parameter b = 0.5914 fm³
- Spin symmetry energy E_σ(ρ₀) = 26.55 MeV
- Spin-isospin symmetry energy E_{τσ}(ρ₀) = 23.09 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the SEI with Yukawa, Gaussian, and exponential form factors (Behera et al., 1998, 2002) from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei, enabling consistent description of both regimes. The results are compared with microscopic calculations using the UV14+UVII interaction (Wiringa), DBHF calculations with the Bonn potential (Samanta et al., 2010), and various Gogny forces (D1, D1S, D1M) as well as M3Y-P5 (Nakada, 2008). The approach enables future calculations of spin and spin-isospin properties of nuclear matter and provides a framework for describing finite nuclei with a momentum-dependent mean field that agrees with experimental optical potential data.
