# Elliptic flow of thermal photons from event-by-event hydrodynamic model

> Elliptic flow of direct photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions is believed to be dominated by contribution from thermal radiation of quark gluon plasma up to $p_T$ $\sim 5$ GeV/$c$, although other sources start outshining the thermal contribution at already smaller values of $p_T$ in the direct photon spectrum. The elliptic flow of thermal photons from ideal hydrodynamics considering a smooth initial density distribution under-predicts the PHENIX direct photon data from 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC by a large margin in the range $1 < p_T < 5$ GeV/$c$. However, a significant enhancement of thermal photon production due to fluctuations in the initial QCD matter density distributions is expected. We show that such fluctuations result in substantially larger photon elliptic flow for $p_T > 2.5 $ GeV/$c$ compared to a smooth initial-state-averaged density profile. The results from event-by-event hydrodynamics are found to be sensitive to the fluctuation size parameter. However, the effects of initial state fluctuations are insufficient to account for the discrepancy to the PHENIX data for direct photon elliptic flow. Furthermore, the photon $v_2$ is reduced even more when we include the NLO pQCD prompt photon component. We also calculate the spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons for 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC and for the 0--40% centrality bin. Thermal photons from event-by-event hydrodynamics along with prompt photons from NLO pQCD calculations explain the ALICE preliminary direct photon data well in the region $p_T \ge 2.5$ GeV/c. Similar to RHIC, the elliptic flow results at LHC are again found to be much smaller than the ALICE preliminary $v_2$ data.

## Key claim

Initial-state density fluctuations in event-by-event hydrodynamics produce substantially larger thermal photon elliptic flow for $p_T > 2.5$ GeV/$c$ compared to smooth initial-state-averaged profiles, but this enhancement remains insufficient to explain the PHENIX direct photon $v_2$ data from 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and the discrepancy persists even before including the diluting effect of prompt photons.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with longitudinal boost invariance using the SHASTA algorithm, initializing entropy density via a Monte Carlo Glauber wounded-nucleon model with Gaussian smearing (size parameter $\sigma$ varied from 0.4 to 1.0 fm). Thermal photon emission was computed by integrating leading-order partonic rates from Arnold-Moore-Yaffe and hadronic rates from Turbet et al. over the space-time evolution, with instantaneous transition at 170 MeV. Prompt photon spectra were calculated at NLO pQCD using the INCNLO package with CTEQ6.6M PDFs and EPS09s nuclear modifications, and the total direct photon $v_2$ was obtained by weighting thermal and prompt contributions.

## Key numbers

- For 200A GeV Au+Au at RHIC, with $\sigma = 0.4$ fm, the reaction-plane $v_2$ at $p_T = 4$ GeV/$c$ is about 3 times larger than the result from smooth initial-state-averaged IC.
- The average emission time for thermal photons at $p_T = 5$ GeV/$c$ is $\langle \tau \rangle = 0.6$ fm/$c$ with $\sigma = 0.4$ fm, compared to $\langle \tau \rangle = 0.3$ fm/$c$ with $\sigma = 1.0$ fm.
- Inclusion of prompt photons reduces the $v_2$ from fluctuating IC by approximately 25% at $p_T = 2$ GeV/$c$ and more than 50% at $p_T = 4$ GeV/$c$ at RHIC.
- For 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb at LHC (0-40% centrality), thermal photons from fluctuating IC plus prompt photons explain the ALICE preliminary direct photon spectrum well for $p_T \ge 2.5$ GeV/$c$.
- The initial time for hydrodynamics was $\tau_0 = 0.17$ fm/$c$ for RHIC and $\tau_0 = 0.14$ fm/$c$ for LHC, with entropy normalization constants $K = 102$ fm$^{-1}$ and $K = 250$ fm$^{-1}$ respectively.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used was $\sigma_{NN} = 42$ mb for RHIC and 64 mb for LHC.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Chatterjee et al. (2013) that showed event-by-event hydrodynamics enhances thermal photon production, and by the same group on hadronic elliptic flow with fluctuating initial conditions (Holopainen et al., 2011). It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between smooth-IC hydrodynamic predictions and PHENIX direct photon $v_2$ data (PHENIX Collaboration, 2012), finding that fluctuations alone cannot resolve the tension. The results enable systematic investigation of whether viscous effects, as discussed by Dion et al. (2013), or other unconventional mechanisms are needed to explain the experimental data at both RHIC and LHC energies.
