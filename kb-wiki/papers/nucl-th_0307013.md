# Effect of the bound nucleon form factors on charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We study the effect of bound nucleon form factors on charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering. The bound nucleon form factors of the vector and axial-vector currents are calculated in the quark-meson coupling model. We compute the inclusive $^{12}$C($ν_μ,μ^-$)$X$ cross sections using a relativistic Fermi gas model with the calculated bound nucleon form factors. The effect of the bound nucleon form factors for this reaction is a reduction of $\sim$8% for the total cross section, relative to that calculated with the free nucleon form factors.

## Key claim

The bound nucleon form factors, calculated in the quark-meson coupling model, reduce the total charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section for \(^{12}\)C by approximately 8% relative to calculations using free nucleon form factors; this reduction is additional to standard nuclear-structure corrections and is not sensitive to the choice of effective nucleon mass or binding energy parameter.

## Method

The authors computed the inclusive \(^{12}\)C(\(\nu_\mu,\mu^-\))\(X\) cross section using a relativistic Fermi gas model, implementing bound nucleon vector and axial-vector form factors derived from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The bound form factors were obtained by scaling empirical free-nucleon parameterizations with ratios of QMC-calculated in-medium form factors to free-space improved cloudy bag model form factors. The differential cross section was averaged over the LSND muon neutrino spectrum (0 to 300 MeV), and total cross sections were computed for several choices of effective nucleon mass and binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The reduction in total cross section due to bound nucleon form factors is \(\sim\)8% for \(^{12}\)C, relative to the free-nucleon form factor calculation.
- For the bound nucleon form factor \(F_2^*(Q^2)\) in \(^{12}\)C at \(Q^2 = 0.15\) GeV\(^2\), the enhancement relative to free is \(\sim\)8%; for \(G_E^{p*}(Q^2)\) the quenching is \(\sim\)4%.
- The effective nucleon mass in \(^{12}\)C used in the calculation is \(m_N^* = 802.8\) MeV, corresponding to a Fermi momentum \(k_F = 225\) MeV and baryon density \(\rho_B = 0.668 \rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- The reduction rate \(R(\delta\sigma)\) is 7.7% for both \(m_N\) and \(m_N^*\) with \(E_B = 0\); for \(E_B = 20, 25, 30\) MeV, \(R(\delta\sigma) = 8.1\%, 7.6\%, 7.5\%\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of bound nucleon form factors from electromagnetic scattering (e.g., polarized (\( \vec{e}, e' \vec{p} \)) experiments on \(^{16}\)O and \(^4\)He at MAMI and Jefferson Lab, which found a \(\sim\)10% difference in the \(G_E^p/G_M^p\) ratio in \(^4\)He) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering. The authors note that existing calculations for the LSND \(^{12}\)C(\(\nu_\mu,\mu^-\))\(X\) total cross section overestimate the data by 30% to 100% (LSND, Kolbe). The \(\sim\)8% reduction from bound nucleon form factors is presented as an additional correction beyond standard nuclear-structure effects (e.g., meson exchange currents, RPA correlations), and the authors suggest that more precise calculations including RPA (Hungchong Kim) are needed for definitive conclusions.
