# Chirally symmetric but confined hadrons at finite density

> At a critical finite chemical potential and low temperature QCD undergoes the chiral restoration phase transition. The folklore tradition is that simultaneously hadrons are deconfined and there appears the quark matter. We demonstrate that it is possible to have confined but chirally symmetric hadrons at a finite chemical potential and hence beyond the chiral restoration point at a finite chemical potential and low temperature there could exist a chirally symmetric matter consisting of chirally symmetric but confined hadrons. If it does happen in QCD, then the QCD phase diagram should be reconsidered with obvious implications for heavy ion programs and astrophysics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that it is possible to have confined but chirally symmetric hadrons at a finite chemical potential, contradicting the folklore that chiral restoration and deconfinement occur simultaneously. They explicitly hedge that whether such a phase actually exists in QCD remains an open question.

## Method

The authors use a manifestly chirally-symmetric and confining model in four dimensions, which is a generalization of the 1+1 dimensional 't Hooft model (QCD in the large N_c limit). They solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy in the rainbow approximation with an instantaneous linear confining potential, then include a finite quark chemical potential at zero temperature by Pauli-blocking quark momenta below the Fermi momentum. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for quark-antiquark bound states to obtain the meson spectrum above the critical chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate in the vacuum: \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = (-0.231\sqrt{\sigma})^3\)
- Critical chemical potential for chiral restoration: \(\mu = 0.2\sqrt{\sigma}\) (the value at which the spectrum above the critical point is shown)
- Meson masses are reported in units of \(\sqrt{\sigma}\) for multiplets with J=0 and J=2 at \(\mu = 0.2\sqrt{\sigma}\) (exact numerical values for individual meson masses are not given in the text)

## Context

This work extends the qualitative large N_c arguments of McLerran and Pisarski (2007) that a confining but chirally symmetric phase might exist at reasonably large chemical potential and low temperature. It contradicts the naive intuition and typical Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models, which are not confining and therefore cannot address whether chiral restoration implies deconfinement. The result enables reconsideration of the QCD phase diagram with implications for heavy ion programs and astrophysics, particularly because chirally-symmetric hadrons would have only short-range interactions (decoupling from Goldstone bosons) and a different weak decay rate due to vanishing axial charge.
