# Further Evidence For Zero Crossing On The Three Gluon Vertex

> The three gluon one particle irreducible function is investigated using lattice QCD simulations over a large region of momentum in the Landau gauge for four dimensional pure Yang-Mills equations and the SU(3) gauge group. The results favor a zero crossing of the gluon form factor for momenta in the range $220 - 260$ MeV. This zero crossing is required to happen in order to have a properly defined set of Dyson-Schwinger equations. It is also shown that in the high momentum region the lattice results are compatible with the predictions of renormalisation group improved perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The lattice results favor a zero crossing of the three-gluon form factor for momenta in the range 220–260 MeV, which is required for a properly defined set of Dyson-Schwinger equations; however, the data point at 216 MeV shows the form factor becoming negative only within 2.2 standard deviations, and the zero crossing is not directly observed but inferred from the trend of the data.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in four dimensions using the standard Wilson action at β = 6.0, with lattices of size 64⁴ and 80⁴ (lattice spacing a = 0.102 fm). Gauge configurations were generated using a combined Monte Carlo sweep of seven over-relaxation updates with four heat bath updates, then rotated to the minimal Landau gauge by maximizing a gauge-fixing functional using the Fourier accelerated steepest descent method. The three-gluon one-particle irreducible (1PI) function was accessed via the complete three-point Green's function for the kinematic configuration where one gluon momentum vanishes (p, 0, -p), extracting the form factor Γ(p²) from the ratio of the three-point function to the gluon propagator squared times the zero-momentum propagator.

## Key numbers

- The smallest momentum on the 64⁴ lattice is p_min = 191 MeV; on the 80⁴ lattice, p_min = 153 MeV.
- At p = 216 MeV, the form factor Γ(p²) = -0.80(37) (from the 64⁴ lattice with 2000 configurations), becoming negative but compatible with zero only within 2.2 standard deviations.
- At p = 270 MeV, Γ(p²) = 0.171(73) (64⁴ lattice); at p = 264 MeV, Γ(p²) = 0.58(43) (80⁴ lattice with 279 configurations).
- The zero crossing is estimated to occur for momenta below approximately 250 MeV.
- The lattice spacing is a = 0.102 fm, corresponding to 1/a = 1.943 GeV, measured from the string tension (Bali 1993).
- The gluon anomalous dimension is γ = 13/22; the three-gluon anomalous dimension is γ_3g = 17/44; the combined exponent for the UV combination is γ' = -35/44.

## Context

This work extends recent lattice evidence for a zero crossing of the three-gluon vertex in SU(3) Yang-Mills (Athenodorou 2016) and confirms earlier observations in SU(2) simulations (Cucchieri 2006, Cucchieri 2008) and in Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Aguilar 2014, Blum 2014, Eichmann 2014). The results provide further lattice support for the requirement that the three-gluon vertex must change sign to render the gluon Dyson-Schwinger equation finite (Binosi 2013, Aguilar 2014). The high-momentum data validate the predictions of renormalization group improved perturbation theory above approximately 2.5 GeV, and the work enables improved parametrizations of the three-gluon vertex for continuum approaches to QCD.
