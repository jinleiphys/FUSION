# Joule heating in the cooling of magnetized neutron stars

> We present 2D simulations of the cooling of neutron stars with strong magnetic fields (B \geq 10^{13} G). We solve the diffusion equation in axial symmetry including the state of the art microphysics that controls the cooling such as slow/fast neutrino processes, superfluidity, as well as possible heating mechanisms. We study how the cooling curves depend on the the magnetic field strength and geometry. Special attention is given to discuss the influence of magnetic field decay. We show that Joule heating effects are very large and in some cases control the thermal evolution. We characterize the temperature anisotropy induced by the magnetic field for the early and late stages of the evolution of isolated neutron stars.

# Joule heating in the cooling of magnetized neutron stars

## Key claim

Joule heating from magnetic field decay is shown to be very large and in some cases controls the thermal evolution of neutron stars with magnetic fields B ≥ 10¹³ G, but the effect depends on the assumed Ohmic and Hall decay timescales and the field geometry, and the inverted temperature distribution found at the base of the envelope does not automatically propagate to the surface due to the blanketing effect of the envelope.

## Method

The authors performed 2D simulations of neutron star cooling in axial symmetry by solving the diffusion equation with an anisotropic thermal conductivity tensor, where the ratio of parallel to perpendicular conductivity depends on the magnetization parameter ω_B τ. They considered two baseline mass models (1.35 M_⊙ and 1.63 M_⊙) with crustal confined magnetic fields, included slow/fast neutrino processes and superfluidity, and modeled magnetic field decay using an approximate solution of the diffusion equation with Ohmic and Hall timescales.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: B ≥ 10¹³ G, with a specific example of B₀ = 5 × 10¹⁴ G used in Joule heating simulations
- Two neutron star masses: Model A at M = 1.35 M_⊙ (minimal cooling, modified Urca) and Model B at M = 1.63 M_⊙ (fast cooling, direct Urca)
- Ohmic decay timescale set to τ_Ohm = 10⁶ yr in the Joule heating simulations
- Inverted temperature distribution at the base of the envelope (at 10⁹ g/cm³) persists for t ≲ 500 yr for Model A

## Context

This work extends the first 2D simulations of magnetized neutron star cooling presented by Aguilera et al. (2007) and builds on earlier proposals by Geppert et al. (2004) and Azorín et al. (2006) that crustal confined magnetic fields with strengths above 10¹³ G could produce surface thermal anisotropy. The results show that magnetic field decay as a heat source can strongly affect thermal evolution, requiring reinterpretation of observations of magnetars, high-field radio pulsars, and radio-quiet isolated neutron stars, and motivate a fully coupled magneto-thermal evolution as a next step.
