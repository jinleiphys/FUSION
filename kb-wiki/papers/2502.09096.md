# Low-energy spectra of nobelium isotopes: Skyrme random-phase-approximation analysis

> Low-energy spectra in the isotopic chain $^{250-262}$No are systematically investigated within the fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) using Skyrme forces SLy4, SLy6, SkM* and SVbas. QRPA states of multipolarity $λμ$=20, 22, 30, 31, 32, 33, 43, 44 and 98 are considered. The main attention is paid to isotopes $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No where the most extensive experimental spectroscopic information is available. In these two nuclei, a reasonable description of $K^π=8^-, 2^-$and $3^+$ isomers is obtained with forces SLy4 and SLy6. The disputed $8^-$ isomer in $^{254}$No is assigned as neutron two-quasiparticle configuration $nn[734\uparrow,613\uparrow]$. The isomers are additionally analyzed using Skyrme functionals UNEDF1, UNEDF2 and UNEDF1$^{\rm SO}$. At the energies 1.2 - 1.4 MeV, the 2qp $K$-isomers $4^-, 7^-$ in $^{252}$No and $4^-, 6^-, 7^-$ in $^{254}$No are also predicted. In $^{254}$No, the $K^π=3^+$ isomer should be accompanied by the nearby $K^π=4^+$ counterpart. It is shown that, in the chain $^{250-262}$No, some features of $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No should exhibit essential irregularities caused by a noticeable shell gap in the neutron single-particle spectrum and corresponding reduction of the neutron pairing. In particular, low-energy pairing-vibrational $K^π=0^+$ states in $^{252,254}$No are predicted.

## Key claim

The disputed $8^-$ isomer in $^{254}$No is assigned as the neutron two-quasiparticle configuration $nn[734\uparrow,613\uparrow]$, based on fully self-consistent QRPA calculations with the SLy6 Skyrme force, though the description of this state is significantly worse than for $^{252}$No and all three Skyrme forces overestimate its excitation energy.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) calculations using Skyrme forces SLy6, SkM*, and SVbas, treating both mean field and residual interaction from the same functional. They considered QRPA states of multipolarities $\lambda\mu$=20, 22, 30, 31, 32, 33, 43, 44, and 98 across the isotopic chain $^{250-262}$No, with detailed analysis of low-energy non-rotational states in $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No. The pairing was treated within the BCS scheme using zero-range pairing interaction, with volume pairing for SLy6 and SkM* and surface density-dependent pairing for SVbas.

## Key numbers

- The $8^-$ isomer in $^{252}$No is calculated at 1.361 MeV (SLy6), 1.330 MeV (SkM*), and 1.913 MeV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 1.254 MeV.
- The $8^-$ isomer in $^{254}$No is calculated at 1.747 MeV (SLy6), 1.554 MeV (SkM*), and 1.994 MeV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 1.295 MeV.
- The neutron pairing gap $\Delta_n$ in $^{254}$No drops to 0.05 MeV for SLy6, compared to 0.36 MeV for SkM* and 0.67 MeV for SVbas, while the experimental five-point gap is 0.71 MeV.
- The calculated $E(2^+_1)$ energies for $^{252}$No are 39 keV (SLy6), 40 keV (SkM*), and 52 keV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 46 keV.
- The calculated $E(2^+_1)$ energies for $^{254}$No are 38 keV (SLy6), 37 keV (SkM*), and 50 keV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 44 keV.
- The moments of inertia for $^{252}$No are 76.8 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SLy6), 74.1 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SkM*), and 58.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 64.7 $\hbar^2$/MeV.
- The moments of inertia for $^{254}$No are 78.3 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SLy6), 80.8 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SkM*), and 59.3 $\hbar^2$/MeV (SVbas), compared to the experimental value of 67.9 $\hbar^2$/MeV.
- The neutron mass staggering $\delta^{(3)}_{2n}$ shows a peak at A=254, confirming the existence of a neutron shell gap at N=152.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent and non-self-consistent studies of K-isomers in nobelium isotopes, including calculations with Woods-Saxon potentials (Soloviev 1991, Jolos 2011), cranking shell model with particle-number projection (He 2020), and various density functional theory approaches (Dobaczewski 2015). The paper provides the first systematic fully self-consistent QRPA analysis of low-energy non-rotational states across the $^{250-262}$No chain, predicting pairing-vibrational $K^\pi=0^+$ states in $^{252,254}$No and additional 2qp K-isomers at 1.2-1.4 MeV. The results enable future experimental searches for these predicted states and further theoretical work incorporating particle-number projection to improve description of the $8^-$ isomer in $^{254}$No.
