# Radiative decay and electromagnetic moments in $^{229}$Th determined within nuclear DFT

> Using the nuclear DFT approach with symmetry breaking and restoration, we investigate the electromagnetic properties of the ground and isomeric states in $^{229}$Th. We determine the magnetic dipole transition strength B(M1; $3/2^+_1\rightarrow 5/2^+_1)$ between these two states and discuss the effects of parity breaking, configuration mixing, and time-odd core polarization. We also determine the corresponding spectroscopic magnetic dipole and octupole, and electric quadrupole moments. Because the octupole deformability of the Skyrme functionals used here is not described in sufficient detail, we analyze the results using a set of Skyrme functionals and perform a regression aligned with the measured electric octupole moments of neighboring even-even nuclei. Without parameter adjustment, the results compare favorably with the experimental data but also indicate the need to systematically adjust the octupole degrees of freedom in future functional parametrizations.

## Key claim

Using nuclear DFT with symmetry breaking and restoration, the authors present the first parameter-free analysis of the electromagnetic properties of the ground and isomeric states in $^{229}$Th, but the results depend on a regression aligned with measured electric octupole moments of neighboring even-even nuclei because the Skyrme functionals used do not describe octupole deformability in sufficient detail.

## Method

The authors applied a multi-reference DFT (MR-DFT) approach with configuration interaction, using seven Skyrme functionals (SkXc, SkM*, UNEDF1, SIII, SkO', SLy4, UNEDF0) with pairing interaction implemented in the code HFODD. They blocked specific odd-neutron axial quasiparticle configurations in $^{229}$Th, relaxed parity constraints to obtain deformed symmetry-breaking solutions with non-zero intrinsic octupole moments, projected these solutions onto good parity and angular momentum, and formed mixed wave functions by solving Hill-Wheeler equations. Calculations were performed in two variants: with time-even (TE) terms only and with both time-even and time-odd (TE+TO) terms included, using Landau parameters adjusted to experimental magnetic moments or Gamow-Teller strengths.

## Key numbers

- B(M1; 3/2+_1 -> 5/2+_1) = 0.04(3) $\mu_N^2$ from TE+TO regression aligned with $^{226}$Ra data, and 0.03(2) $\mu_N^2$ from TE+TO regression aligned with $^{230}$Th data, compared to experimental value 0.0388(12) $\mu_N^2$
- Electric quadrupole moment Q(5/2+_1) = 2.98(5) b from DFT, compared to experimental 3.11(2) b (4% deviation)
- Electric quadrupole moment Q(3/2+_1) = 1.728(12) b from DFT, compared to experimental 1.77(1) b (2.4% deviation)
- Magnetic dipole moment $\mu$(5/2+_1) = 0.3(3) $\mu_N$ from DFT, compared to experimental 0.366(6) $\mu_N$ (30% deviation)
- Magnetic dipole moment $\mu$(3/2+_1) = -0.18(7) $\mu_N$ from DFT, compared to experimental -0.378(8) $\mu_N$ (50% deviation)
- Magnetic octupole moment $\Omega$(5/2+_1) = -0.054(10) $\mu_N$ b (prediction, no experimental value)
- Magnetic octupole moment $\Omega$(3/2+_1) = 0.129(6) $\mu_N$ b (prediction, no experimental value)
- Intrinsic octupole moments used for regression: Q3_0($^{226}$Ra) = 1080(30) e fm^3 and Q3_0($^{230}$Th) = 800(40) e fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts on $^{229}$Th that used phenomenological models (PhysRevC.92.054324), core-plus-particle models (PhysRevC.73.044326, PhysRevLett.118.212501), and projected shell models (Che25), which relied on adjusted parameters, effective charges, or effective g factors. It also goes beyond earlier parameter-free self-consistent approaches that neglected octupole deformation (PhysRevC.79.064303), time-reversal and signature symmetry breaking, parity and rotational symmetry restoration, or configuration interaction. The results enable future improvements by demonstrating the need to systematically adjust octupole degrees of freedom in functional parametrizations and to address self-interaction and self-pairing problems in existing Skyrme functionals.
