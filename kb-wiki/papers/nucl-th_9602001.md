# Using Faddeev Differential Equations to Calculate Three-Body Resonances

> Algorithm, based on explicit representations for analytic continuation of the T-matrix Faddeev components on unphysical sheets, is worked out for calculations of resonances in the three-body quantum problem. According to the representations, poles of T-matrix, scattering matrix and Green function on unphysical sheets, interpreted as resonances, coincide with those complex energy values where appropriate truncations of the scattering matrix have zero as eigenvalue. Scattering amplitudes on the physical sheet, necessary to construct scattering matrix, are calculated on the basis of the Faddeev differential equations. The algorithm developed is applied to search for the resonances in the $nnp$ system and in a model three-boson system.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algorithm for calculating three-body resonances by reducing the problem to finding zeros of a truncated scattering matrix on the physical sheet, rather than directly searching for poles on unphysical sheets. The method is restricted to resonances on two-body unphysical sheets (where the index l0=0) because reliable numerical methods for the (3→2,3) processes needed for three-body sheets are not yet developed.

## Method

The authors use explicit representations for analytic continuation of the T-matrix Faddeev components onto unphysical sheets, which express the T-matrix, scattering matrix, and resolvent on a given unphysical sheet Π_l in terms of the same quantities on the physical sheet and a truncation S_l(z) of the three-body scattering matrix. The scattering amplitudes on the physical sheet needed to construct S_l(z) are computed by solving the Faddeev differential equations in configuration space, extended to complex energy values. For the nnp system, the authors restrict to the two-body unphysical sheet Π_(0,1) and compute only elastic nd-scattering amplitudes in the s-wave approximation, using the Faddeev integro-differential equations for both quartet (S=3/2) and doublet (S=1/2) spin states.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron energy threshold is denoted as E_d, with the continuum spectrum interval between E_d and the breakup threshold at z=0 being the region where the physical sheet Π_0 connects to the two-body unphysical sheet Π_(0,1)
- The partial Laplacian in the Faddeev equations includes the term L(L+1)/y^2 for the centrifugal barrier, where L is the total orbital angular momentum
- The geometric function h^L in the integral coupling term contains a factor (4/√3) and involves Legendre polynomials P_k(u) of order k

## Context

This work extends the explicit representations for analytic continuation of the T-matrix developed by Motovilov (1993, 1994) to practical numerical calculations, using the Faddeev differential equation approach of Merkuriev, Faddeev, and collaborators. The method contrasts with the complex scaling approach of Balslev and Combes (1971), which requires interaction potentials to be analytic functions of coordinates. The algorithm enables calculation of resonances on two-body unphysical sheets for three-body systems, but the authors note that extension to three-body unphysical sheets would require reliable methods for computing (3→2,3) amplitudes, which are not yet available even for real energies.
