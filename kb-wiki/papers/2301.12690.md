# High-Density behavior of symmetry energy and speed of sound in the dense matter within an effective chiral model

> With an effective chiral model, we investigate how the mesonic cross couplings $σ-ρ$ and $ω-ρ$ affect the density content of the symmetry energy and its higher-order slope parameters. Earlier mentioned cross-couplings are crucial to controlling the density content of symmetry energy. For this purpose, we did a case study for different values of the symmetry energy $J_1$, defined at density 0.1 fm$^{-3}$ in the range (23.4 - 25.2) for a fixed value of the slope of the symmetry energy $L_0 = 60$ MeV at saturation density and investigate its effect on the higher-order coefficients and their influence on the underlying equation of state. We found that the model with $J_1= 24.6$ MeV is more favorable with the pure neutron matter (PNM) constraints obtained from $χ$EFT calculations. In addition, we show that all of our models predict a monotonically increasing speed of sound up to four times the saturation density. The speed of sound decreases/saturates above that point and approaches the conformal limit approximately $\sqrt{1/3}~c$ at the center of the maximum mass star.

# High-Density behavior of symmetry energy and speed of sound in the dense matter within an effective chiral model

## Key claim

The authors show that within an effective chiral model, the mesonic cross-couplings σ-ρ and ω-ρ can be tuned to produce symmetry energy values at 0.1 fm⁻³ (J₁) in the range 23.4–25.2 MeV while fixing the slope parameter L₀ = 60 MeV at saturation density, and that the specific model with J₁ = 24.6 MeV best matches pure neutron matter constraints from χEFT calculations. All models predict a monotonically increasing speed of sound up to four times saturation density, after which it decreases or saturates toward the conformal limit of approximately √(1/3) c at the center of the maximum mass star.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field effective chiral Lagrangian including scalar (σ), vector (ω), and isovector (ρ) mesons with higher-order scalar self-interactions and mesonic cross-couplings (σ-ρ and ω-ρ). They fix the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state using parameters from prior work (Malik 2017) and then tune three parameters (C_ρ, η₁, η₂) to obtain different values of the symmetry energy J₁ at density 0.1 fm⁻³ and J₀ at saturation density, while keeping the slope L₀ fixed at 60 MeV. The resulting equations of state are used to compute symmetry energy slope parameters (L, K_sym, Q_sym, Z_sym) and the speed of sound in beta-equilibrated matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³; binding energy E₀ = −16.0 MeV; nuclear incompressibility K = 231 MeV; effective nucleon mass m*/m = 0.87
- Symmetry energy J₁ values studied: 23.4, 24.0, 24.6, and 25.2 MeV (at density 0.1 fm⁻³)
- Slope parameter L₀ fixed at 60 MeV for all models at saturation density
- J₀ values range from 31.5 to 34 MeV across the parameter sets
- K_sym,₀ values range from −247.63 to −76.66 MeV; Q_sym,₀ values range from −37.344 to 310.06 MeV; Z_sym,₀ values range from −2624.05 to 6205.68 MeV
- K_τ values range from −520.32 to −346.07 MeV
- Speed of sound increases monotonically up to 4ρ₀, then approaches √(1/3) c ≈ 0.577 c at higher densities
- Nucleon mass = 939 MeV; ω meson mass = 783 MeV; σ meson mass = 444.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the effective chiral model (Malik 2017; Jha 2006, 2008) by systematically investigating how mesonic cross-couplings control the density dependence of symmetry energy and its higher-order slope parameters. The results are compared with several relativistic mean-field models including NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), IUFSU (Fattoyev 2010), BSP (Agrawal 2012), and BKA22 (Agrawal 2010), as well as constraints from heavy-ion collision flow experiments (Danielewicz 2002), isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2013), χEFT pure neutron matter calculations (Hebeler 2013), and astrophysical plus pQCD constraints on the speed of sound (Kurkela 2022; Gorda 2022). The work enables future studies of neutron star structure and composition using a chiral model that reproduces both terrestrial nuclear constraints and astrophysical observations.
