# Theoretical uncertainties of the elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering observables

> Theoretical uncertainties of various types are discussed for the nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering observables at the incoming nucleon laboratory energies up to 200 MeV. We are especially interested in the statistical errors arising from uncertainties of parameters of a nucleon-nucleon interaction. The obtained uncertainties of the differential cross section and numerous scattering observables are in general small, grow with the reaction energy and amount up to a few percent at 200 MeV. We compare these uncertainties with the other types of theoretical errors like truncation errors, numerical uncertainties and uncertainties arising from using the various models of nuclear interaction. We find the latter ones to be dominant source of uncertainties of modern predictions for the three-nucleon scattering observables. To perform above mentioned studies we use the One-Pion-Exchange Gaussian potential derived by the Granada group, for which the covariance matrix of its parameters is known, and solve the Faddeev equation for the nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering. Thus beside studying theoretical uncertainties we also show a description of the nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering data by the One-Pion-Exchange Gaussian model and compare it with results obtained with other nucleon-nucleon potentials, including chiral N$^4$LO forces from the Bochum-Bonn and Moscow(Idaho)-Salamanca groups. In this way we confirm the usefulness and high quality of the One-Pion-Exchange Gaussian force.

# Theoretical uncertainties of the elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering observables

## Key claim

The statistical uncertainties arising from nucleon-nucleon potential parameter errors propagate to nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering observables and are generally small (up to a few percent at 200 MeV), but the dominant source of theoretical uncertainty in modern three-nucleon scattering predictions is the choice of nuclear interaction model rather than parameter statistics.

## Method

The authors solved the momentum-space Faddeev equation for nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering using the One-Pion-Exchange Gaussian potential from the Granada group, for which the covariance matrix of its 42 parameters is known. They sampled 50 sets of potential parameters from the multivariate normal distribution, computed observables for each set, and used the half-width of the central 68% of predictions (half of Delta_68%) as the estimator of statistical uncertainty. They compared these statistical errors with truncation errors, numerical uncertainties, and model dependence from using different nucleon-nucleon potentials including AV18, chiral N^4LO forces from the Bochum-Bonn group, and chiral N^4LO forces from the Moscow(Idaho)-Salamanca group.

## Key numbers

- Statistical uncertainty of the differential cross section at E=13 MeV: relative sample standard deviation ranges from 0.061% at theta_cm=75 degrees to 0.356% at theta_cm=120 degrees
- Statistical uncertainty of the differential cross section at E=200 MeV: relative sample standard deviation ranges from 0.325% at theta_cm=30 degrees to 1.356% at theta_cm=165 degrees
- Difference between OPE-Gaussian and AV18 predictions at the cross section minimum: approximately 3.9% at E=13 MeV and approximately 3.5% at E=200 MeV
- Difference between OPE-Gaussian and AV18 predictions for the spin correlation coefficient C_xx,y-C_yy,y at E=13 MeV: approximately 19% at the minimum, while the statistical error of the OPE-Gaussian result is approximately 2%
- Difference between OPE-Gaussian and AV18 predictions for the spin transfer coefficient K_yz^x' at E=65 MeV: approximately 23%, while the statistical error is approximately 3%
- Chi-squared per data point for the OPE-Gaussian force: 1.06
- Chi-squared per data point for the chiral N^4LO potential from Machleidt and collaborators at Lambda=500 MeV: 1.15
- Chi-squared per data point for the chiral N^4LO potential from Epelbaum and collaborators at R=0.9 fm: approximately 1.14

## Context

This work extends the estimation of theoretical uncertainties from the two-nucleon system into the three-nucleon scattering regime, building on the statistical analysis framework developed by the Granada group (Navarro Perez and collaborators, 2013-2014) for nucleon-nucleon potentials. Previous uncertainty estimates in three-nucleon reactions relied on comparing predictions from different interaction models (Witala and collaborators, 2001) or benchmark calculations, while truncation error prescriptions for chiral effective field theory were proposed by Epelbaum and collaborators (2015) and applied to three-nucleon systems by Binder and collaborators (2016). The study enables future comparisons between theoretical and experimental uncertainties in three-nucleon scattering, particularly as experimental precision continues to improve, and demonstrates that the OPE-Gaussian force provides a high-quality description comparable to the AV18 potential while offering the advantage of known parameter covariances.
