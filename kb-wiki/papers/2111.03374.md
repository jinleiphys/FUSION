# Building a realistic neutron star from holography

> We employ the recently improved description of dense baryonic matter within the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model to construct neutron stars. In contrast to previous holographic approaches, the presence of an isospin asymmetry allows us to implement beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality. As a consequence, we are able to model the crust of the star within the same formalism and compute the location of the crust-core transition dynamically. After showing that a simple pointlike approximation for the baryons fails to satisfy astrophysical constraints, we demonstrate that our improved description does account for neutron stars that meet the current experimental constraints for mass, radius, and tidal deformability. However, we also point out tensions in the parameter fit and large-$N_c$ artifacts and discuss how to potentially resolve them in the future.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the improved homogeneous ansatz for baryonic matter in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, which includes isospin asymmetry, can produce neutron stars satisfying current experimental constraints for mass, radius, and tidal deformability, but they also identify tensions in the parameter fit and large-\(N_c\) artifacts that remain unresolved.

## Method
The authors use the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic approach based on type-IIA string theory, to compute the equation of state of dense baryonic matter. They employ a homogeneous ansatz for the non-abelian gauge fields that includes isospin asymmetry, allowing them to implement beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality by adding a lepton gas. The resulting equation of state is then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities of neutron stars, including a dynamically determined crust-core transition.

## Key numbers
- The heaviest known neutron star has a mass of about \(2.1\,M_\odot\) (NANOGrav 2019), and if the compact object from GW190814 is a neutron star, the lower limit for the maximal mass is even \(2.5\,M_\odot\) or higher.
- The tidal deformability for a roughly \(1.4\,M_\odot\) star from GW170817 is constrained to \(70 \lesssim \Lambda_{1.4} \lesssim 580\).
- NICER radius estimates for the heaviest neutron star are \((11.4 - 13.7)\,{\rm km}\) (Riley 2021) and \((12.2 - 16.3)\,{\rm km}\) (Miller 2021); for a roughly \(1.4\,M_\odot\) star, estimates are \((11.5 - 13.9)\,{\rm km}\) (Riley 2019) and \((12.0 - 14.3)\,{\rm km}\) (Miller 2019).
- In the pointlike approximation, fitting to the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \simeq 93\,{\rm MeV}\) and nucleon mass \(m_N \simeq 939\,{\rm MeV}\) yields \(K \simeq 0.842\,{\rm GeV}\), giving a maximal mass \(M_{\rm max} \simeq 3.0\,M_\odot\) (2 layers) and \(\Lambda_{1.4} \simeq 6.6\times 10^4\), far exceeding constraints.
- The largest possible mass compatible with the tidal deformability constraint in the pointlike approximation is about \(1.62\,M_\odot\) (2 layers) or \(1.67\,M_\odot\) (1 layer).

## Context
This work extends previous holographic studies of neutron stars using the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, which previously employed a pointlike approximation for baryons (Bergman 2007) or a homogeneous ansatz without isospin (Rozali 2007, Elliot-Ripley 2016). The key advance is the inclusion of isospin asymmetry, enabling beta equilibrium and charge neutrality, which was absent in earlier holographic approaches. This enables a unified description of the crust and core within the same formalism, and the results can be compared to constraints from gravitational wave events (LIGO/Virgo 2017, 2020), NICER observations (Riley 2019, Miller 2019, Riley 2021, Miller 2021), and the mass measurement of the heaviest neutron star (NANOGrav 2019). The paper also identifies tensions in the parameter fit and large-\(N_c\) artifacts that need resolution in future work.
