# Chern-Simons current and local parity violation in hot QCD matter

> Non-Abelian gauge theories "live" in a space-time with non-trivial topology that can be characterized by an odd-dimensional Chern-Simons form. In QCD, Chern-Simons form is induced by the chiral anomaly and the presence of topological solutions; it opens a possibility for the breaking of P and CP invariances in strong interactions ("the strong CP problem"). While there is apparently no global P and CP violation in QCD, here I argue that topological fluctuations in hot quark-gluon matter can become directly observable in the presence of a very intense external magnetic field by inducing local P- and CP- odd effects. These phenomena can be described by using the Maxwell-Chern-Simons electrodynamics as an effective theory. Local P and CP violation in hot QCD matter can be observed in experiment through the "chiral magnetic effect" - the separation of electric charge along the axis of magnetic field that is created by the colliding relativistic ions. There is a recent evidence for the electric charge separation relative to the reaction plane of heavy ion collisions from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC.

# Chern-Simons current and local parity violation in hot QCD matter

## Key claim

Topological fluctuations in hot quark-gluon matter can become directly observable in the presence of a very intense external magnetic field by inducing local P- and CP-odd effects, even though there is no global P and CP violation in QCD. The observable signature is the "chiral magnetic effect", the separation of electric charge along the axis of the magnetic field created by colliding relativistic ions.

## Method

The author derives an effective Maxwell-Chern-Simons (axion) electrodynamics by coupling QCD to electromagnetism, introducing a pseudo-scalar field θ to describe topological charge distribution. The resulting equations of motion (Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations) are analyzed for two regimes: static domains where θ is constant, yielding charge densities on domain walls; and time-dependent domains where ∂θ/∂t is nonzero, yielding an induced current along the magnetic field. The analysis also extends to finite baryon density, where charge separation can occur without a magnetic field if angular momentum is present.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant in the Maxwell-Chern-Simons Lagrangian is c = Σ_f q_f² e² / (2π²), where q_f are the quark electric charges.
- For a static domain with magnetic field B, the upper domain wall acquires charge density per unit area (Q/S)_up = + c θ B, and the lower wall acquires (Q/S)_down = − c θ B.
- For a time-dependent domain, the induced current is J = − (e² / (2π²)) ∂θ/∂t B.
- The domain size is on the order of the QCD scale, L ∼ Λ_QCD⁻¹, S ∼ Λ_QCD⁻², requiring an extremely strong magnetic field eB ∼ Λ_QCD² to observe an electric dipole moment.
- At finite baryon density μ, the induced charge density on topological domain walls is ρ_B = N_c (e μ / (2π²)) (∇θ · Ω), where Ω is the angular velocity of the rotating system.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kharzeev (2004), Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), and Kharzeev (2009) on the chiral magnetic effect, building on earlier studies of axion electrodynamics by Wilczek (1987), Carroll (1989), and Sikivie (1984). The author notes that the chiral magnetic effect has been observed on the lattice by Buividovich (2009), and that electromagnetic fields of the required strength can be created in heavy ion collisions as shown by Kharzeev (2007) and Skokov (2009). The analysis enables experimental searches for local parity violation through charge separation measurements, with the STAR Collaboration at RHIC having presented evidence for the P-odd charge separation effect reported by Voloshin (2009). The mechanism requires a deconfined, chirally symmetric phase for sphaleron transitions to turn on and for quarks to separate by distances comparable to the system size.
