# Unexpected properties of the $^{33}$S($α$,p)$^{36}$Cl reaction cross section at low energies

> New experimental data for the $^{33}$S($α$,p)$^{36}$Cl reaction show a very unusual energy dependence. Contrary to common findings for many other $α$-induced reactions, statistical model calculations underestimate the measured cross sections at very low energies. The relatively huge cross sections at these low energies require a significant amount of single-particle strength in the measured energy range which exceeds by far 100% as soon as the additional strength from the competing $^{33}$S($α$,n)$^{36}$Ar reaction is taken into account. In addition, the new data deviate from a general trend for the energy dependence of $α$-induced reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The new experimental data for the ³³S(α,p)³⁶Cl reaction show a very unusual energy dependence where statistical model calculations underestimate the measured cross sections at very low energies, contrary to the typical behavior for α-induced reactions where calculations overestimate data below the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental data from Bowers et al. (2013) measured at the University of Notre Dame using the activation technique with a ³³S beam in inverse kinematics on a ⁴He gas cell, with ³⁶Cl production determined by accelerator mass spectrometry. The author compared these data to statistical model calculations using the TALYS and NON-SMOKER codes, and derived an upper limit for the reaction cross section from single-particle strength estimates using the narrow-resonance formalism. The data were also compared to a systematics of reduced cross sections versus reduced energies for many α-induced reactions.

## Key numbers

- At center-of-mass energy E = 3.503 MeV (ΔE = 0.476 MeV), the experimental cross section is 2.4 ± 0.3 mb, which corresponds to 17% of the single-particle strength summed over all partial waves
- At E = 4.568 MeV (ΔE = 0.465 MeV), the experimental cross section is 37 ± 5 mb, corresponding to 14% of the single-particle strength
- At E = 5.632 MeV (ΔE = 0.454 MeV), the experimental cross section is 105 ± 8 mb, corresponding to 7% of the single-particle strength
- At E = 6.751 MeV (ΔE = 0.378 MeV), the experimental cross section is 199 ± 16 mb, corresponding to 4% of the single-particle strength
- At E = 8.400 MeV (ΔE = 0.454 MeV), the experimental cross section is 330 ± 40 mb, corresponding to 3% of the single-particle strength
- The summed α-strength over the five measured intervals reaches 45% of the single-particle strength for all partial waves; assuming smooth energy dependence between data points leads to about 100%
- The ³³S(α,p)³⁶Cl reaction has Q = -1.93 MeV; the competing ³³S(α,n)³⁶Ar reaction has Q = -2.00 MeV

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Bowers et al. (2013), which focused on production of short-lived radionuclides like ³⁶Cl in the early solar system. The findings contradict the typical behavior observed for many α-induced reactions where statistical model calculations overestimate cross sections at low energies, as documented in studies by Somorjai et al. (1998), Gyürky et al. (2006, 2010), and others. The results challenge the widely used α-nucleus potential of McFadden and Satchler (1966) and the systematics of reduced cross sections established by Mohr (2013), and if confirmed, would require significant revision of theoretical models for α-induced reactions in light nuclei.
