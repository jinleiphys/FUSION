# Chaos in QCD? Gap equations and their fractal properties

> We discuss how iterative solutions of QCD inspired gap-equations at finite chemical potential show domains of chaotic behavior as well as non-chaotic domains which represent one or the other of the only two -- usually distinct -- positive mass gap solutions with broken or restored chiral symmetry, respectively. In the iterative approach gap solutions exist which exhibit restored chiral symmetry beyond a certain dynamical cut-off energy. A chirally broken, non-chaotic domain with no emergent mass poles and hence with no quasi-particle excitations exists below this energy cutoff. The transition domain between these two energy separated domains is chaotic. As a result, the dispersion relation is that of quarks with restored chiral symmetry, cut at a dynamical energy scale, determined by fractal structures. We argue that the chaotic origin of the infrared cut-off could hint at a chaotic nature of confinement and the deconfinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that iterating QCD-inspired gap equations at finite chemical potential produces a single, unique gap solution that transitions between chirally broken (massive, no mass pole) and chirally restored (bare-mass, with mass pole) domains through a chaotic region, rather than yielding distinct phase solutions as traditionally assumed.

## Method

The authors iteratively solved the algebraic gap equations of the Munczek-Nemirovsky (MN) model, which uses a contact interaction (delta-function in momentum space) for the dressed gluon propagator, starting from the non-interacting solution (A=1, B=m). They mapped the complex plane of the squared momentum variable \(\tilde p^2 = z_R^2 + i z_I^2\) to identify regions of stable, periodic, and chaotic behavior of the scalar gap B after hundreds of iterations, and then examined the resulting mass-pole structure in Minkowski metric at finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Coupling strength set to \(\eta = 1.09\) GeV, following the original MN model (PhysRevD.28.181).
- For bare quark mass \(m = 10\) MeV, the outer chaotic boundary ellipse parameters are \((z_{R,0}^2, R_R^2, R_I^2) = (0.98, 1.26, 0.77)\) MeV\(^2\); for \(m = 100\) MeV, \((0.99, 1.28, 0.80)\) MeV\(^2\).
- The inner almond-shaped region parameters for \(m = 10\) MeV are \((1.115, 1.085, 0.310)\) MeV\(^2\); for \(m = 100\) MeV, \((1.150, 1.100, 0.340)\) MeV\(^2\).
- Critical chemical potential for complete disappearance of the infrared energy gap: \(\mu_{C,IR} \approx 625\) MeV for \(m = 100\) MeV, and \(\approx 540\) MeV for \(m = 10\) MeV.
- Onset chemical potential for quasi-particle pole occupation: \(\mu_m \approx 359\) MeV for \(m = 10\) MeV, and \(\approx 432\) MeV for \(m = 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the exploration of chaotic behavior in gap equations initiated by Martinez (2019), who found chaos emerging in strongly coupled systems. It applies iterative analysis to the Munczek-Nemirovsky model (PhysRevD.28.181), a confining model with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and contrasts with traditional approaches that treat distinct analytic gap solutions (Nambu and Wigner phases) as separate phases compared by pressure, as done by Klaehn (2009) and Chen (2008). The results suggest that confinement may be understood as an emergent fractal phenomenon, with the chaotic domain providing a dynamical infrared cutoff that suppresses quasi-particle excitations.
