# Shear viscosity of a superfluid Fermi gas in the unitarity limit

> We compute the shear viscosity of a superfluid atomic Fermi gas in the unitarity limit. The unitarity limit is characterized by a divergent scattering length between the atoms, and it has been argued that this will result in a very small viscosity. We show that in the low temperature T limit the shear viscosity scales as xi^5/T^5, where the universal parameter 'xi' relates the chemical potential and the Fermi energy, mu=xi E_F. Combined with the high temperature expansions of the viscosity our results suggest that the viscosity has a minimum near the critical temperature T_c. A naive extrapolation indicates that the minimum value of the ratio of viscosity over entropy density is within a factor of ~ 5 of the proposed lower bound hbar/(4πk_B).

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of a superfluid Fermi gas in the unitarity limit scales as \(\xi^5/T^5\) at low temperatures, and a naive extrapolation combining this low-temperature result with high-temperature expansions suggests the minimum value of the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio is within a factor of about 5 of the conjectured lower bound \(\hbar/(4\pi k_B)\), but the phonon calculation is not reliable near the critical temperature where the minimum likely occurs.

## Method

The authors derived an effective Lagrangian for phonons (Nambu-Goldstone bosons) in the superfluid phase using Galilean and gauge invariance, with the universal parameter \(\xi\) (Bertsch parameter) relating chemical potential to Fermi energy. They computed the shear viscosity by solving the linearized Boltzmann equation for phonon binary collisions using a variational method with orthogonal polynomial trial functions, evaluating the five-dimensional phase space integrals with Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio scales as \(\eta/s = 7.7 \times 10^{-6} \xi^5 (T_F/T)^8\) for the low-temperature superfluid phase, with \(\xi = 0.4\).
* The critical temperature for superfluidity is \(T_c = (0.29 \pm 0.02) T_F\).
* The conjectured lower bound for \(\eta/s\) is \(\hbar/(4\pi k_B)\).
* The naive extrapolation indicates the minimum \(\eta/s\) is within a factor of \(\sim 5\) of the bound.
* In the high temperature limit \(T \gg T_c\), the viscosity is \(\eta \approx (15/32\sqrt{\pi})(m T)^{3/2}\).
* The universal parameter \(\xi\) is in the range \(0.3-0.4\) from numerical calculations (Carlson 2003, Chang 2004, Astrakharchik 2004).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of shear viscosity in strongly interacting Fermi gases from the high-temperature regime (Bruun 2005) into the low-temperature superfluid phase, closely paralleling calculations for liquid Helium (Khalatnikov, Maris) and the color-flavor-locked phase of dense quark matter (Manuel 2004). The results enable comparison with experimental data on collective excitation damping in unitarity Fermi gases (Kinast 2005) and test the conjectured viscosity bound (Kovtun 2004) in a non-relativistic strongly interacting system.
