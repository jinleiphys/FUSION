# Measuring initial temperature through photon to dilepton ratio in heavy ion collision

> Theoretical calculation of transverse momentum($p_T$) distribution of thermal photons and dileptons originating from ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions suffer from several uncertainties since the evaluation of these spectra needs various inputs which are not yet known unambiguously. In the present work the ratio of the $p_T$ spectra of thermal photons to lepton pairs has been evaluated and it is shown that the ratio is insensitive to some of these parameters.

# Measuring initial temperature through photon to dilepton ratio in heavy ion collision

## Key claim

The ratio of the transverse momentum spectra of thermal photons to dileptons is insensitive to uncertainties in parameters such as initial temperature, thermalisation time, equation of state, critical temperature, and freeze-out temperature, provided the dilepton invariant mass is integrated from twice the pion mass to the phi meson mass. The ratio reaches a plateau for transverse momenta above 1 GeV, making it a robust observable for extracting the initial temperature of the system formed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors computed the invariant transverse momentum distributions of thermal photons and dileptons by convolving production rates from the quark-gluon plasma, mixed phase, and hadronic phase with a space-time evolution described by ideal relativistic hydrodynamics assuming longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry. Photon rates were taken to O(ααs) from Arnold et al., dilepton rates from the plasma phase to O(α²αs) from Altherr and Thoma, and hadronic phase contributions included decays of vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ). The ratio Rem = (d²Nγ/d²pT dy)/(d²Nγ*/d²pT dy) at y=0 was evaluated for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies using both bag model and lattice equations of state.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy, changing Tc from 192 MeV to 170 MeV changes the photon yield by 8.5% and the dilepton yield by 13% at pT = 2 GeV, while Rem changes by only 5%.
- Input parameters for SPS: dN/dy = 700, τi = 1 fm, Ti = 0.2 GeV, Tf = 120 MeV.
- Input parameters for RHIC: dN/dy = 1100, τi = 0.2 fm, Ti = 0.4 GeV, Tf = 120 MeV.
- Input parameters for LHC: dN/dy = 2100, τi = 0.08 fm, Ti = 0.70 GeV, Tf = 120 MeV; and dN/dy = 4000, τi = 0.08 fm, Ti = 0.85 GeV, Tf = 120 MeV.
- The ratio Rem reaches a plateau for pT above 1 GeV when the dilepton invariant mass is integrated from M = 2mπ to mφ.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Alam et al. that reproduced the photon spectra measured by WA98 and PHENIX and the dilepton spectra measured by CERES and NA60 at SPS and RHIC energies. The ratio approach addresses the problem that individual thermal photon and dilepton spectra depend strongly on poorly known inputs such as initial temperature, thermalisation time, and equation of state, as noted by Larry, Gale and Kapusta, Weldon, and others. The insensitivity of the ratio to the critical temperature and other parameters enables a more reliable extraction of the initial temperature from experimental data at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, and the results can be compared with lattice equation of state calculations from the MILC collaboration.
