# How gauge covariance of the fermion and boson propagators in QED constrain the effective fermion-boson vertex

> We derive the gauge covariance requirement imposed on the QED fermion-photon three-point function within the framework of a spectral representation for fermion propagators. When satisfied, such requirement ensures solutions to the fermion propagator Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) in any covariant gauge with arbitrary numbers of spacetime dimensions to be consistent with the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation (LKFT). The general result has been verified by the special cases of three and four dimensions. Additionally, we present the condition that ensures the vacuum polarization is independent of the gauge parameter. As an illustration, we show how the Gauge Technique dimensionally regularized in 4D does not satisfy the covariance requirement.

## Key claim

The paper derives necessary and sufficient conditions on the QED fermion-photon three-point function (the effective vertex) that ensure solutions to the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the fermion and photon propagators are gauge covariant, meaning they satisfy the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation (LKFT) in any covariant gauge. The authors show that satisfying the Ward-Green-Takahashi identity alone is not sufficient for gauge covariance.

## Method

The authors use a spectral representation for the fermion propagator, which provides a bijective mapping between the propagator in the complex momentum plane and its spectral functions. They rewrite the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the fermion propagator as a linear integral equation in terms of spectral functions, encoded in a matrix operator Omega. The Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation is expressed as an exponential operator acting on the spectral functions, using fractional calculus (Riemann-Liouville operators). By requiring that the SDE solution in any gauge equals the LKFT-transformed solution from the Landau gauge, they derive a consistency condition that Omega must satisfy. A similar condition is derived for the photon propagator SDE by requiring the vacuum polarization to be gauge-parameter independent.

## Key numbers

* The LKFT kernel for the fermion propagator involves the parameter nu = alpha*xi/(4*pi), where xi is the covariant gauge parameter and alpha is the fine-structure constant.
* The Riemann-Liouville fractional derivative operator D^epsilon appears, where epsilon is defined by the number of spacetime dimensions d = 4 - 2*epsilon.
* The operator phi_n (Eq. 3.13) contains the factor Gamma(epsilon)*(4*pi*mu^2/p^2)^epsilon * Gamma(1-epsilon)/Gamma(1+epsilon), which is singular as epsilon approaches 0.
* The exponential operator K_j acting on a power-law function z^beta yields a series with terms proportional to z^{beta + m*epsilon} (Eq. 3.14), with coefficients involving Gamma functions of beta, n, and epsilon.

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the fermion-boson vertex in QED using gauge covariance, building on the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformation (Zumino, 1959) and the Gauge Technique ansatz of Salam, Delbourgo, and Strathdee (1963-1964). It contrasts with simpler truncations like the Maris-Tandy ansatz (Maris, 1999), which the authors show cannot be gauge covariant because the mass function changes incorrectly with gauge parameter. The results enable systematic checks of whether any proposed vertex ansatz (such as the Gauge Technique, which is shown to fail the consistency requirement in quenched 4D) respects the gauge covariance of propagator SDE solutions.
