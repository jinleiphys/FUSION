# Quark model study of the triton bound stat

> The three-nucleon bound state problem is studied employing nucleon-nucleon potentials derived from a basic quark-quark interaction. We analyze the effects of the nonlocalities generated by the quark model. The calculated triton binding energies indicate that quark-model nonlocalities can yield additional binding in the order of few hundred keV.

# Quark model study of the triton bound state

## Key claim
Quark-model nonlocalities in the nucleon-nucleon interaction can yield additional binding in the three-nucleon system on the order of up to 200 keV, but the effect is clearly separable only for the $^1S_0$ partial wave; the $^3S_1-{^3D_1}$ contribution is obscured by imperfect phase equivalence between the local and nonlocal models.

## Method
The authors derived two nucleon-nucleon potentials from the same constituent quark model: a nonlocal potential via the resonating group method (RGM) and a local potential via the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. They fine-tuned parameters to achieve near phase-shift equivalence between the two models, then performed five-channel Faddeev calculations of the triton binding energy in momentum space using separable finite-rank expansions (EST method) of rank 6-8 for $^1S_0$ and rank 6 for $^3S_1-{^3D_1}$.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy with the local potential: -7.572 MeV
- Triton binding energy with the nonlocal potential: -7.715 MeV
- Difference in triton binding energy between local and nonlocal models: approximately 150 keV (stated as up to 200 keV in the summary)
- Deuteron binding energy: -2.2245 MeV (local) and -2.2242 MeV (nonlocal)
- $^1S_0$ scattering length: -23.758 fm (local) and -23.759 fm (nonlocal)
- $^3S_1$ scattering length: 5.464 fm (local) and 5.461 fm (nonlocal)
- D-state probability in the deuteron: 4.79% (local) and 4.85% (nonlocal)

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nonlocal effects in the three-nucleon system, which had primarily explored nonlocalities from meson-exchange pictures (Gibson, Haidenbauer, Machleidt, Sammarruca, and Elster, all circa 1990s). Only one prior study (Takeuchi and Amghar, 1992) had addressed nonlocalities from quark substructure. The results show that quark-model nonlocalities produce binding enhancements comparable in magnitude to those from meson-exchange nonlocalities, and the approach enables systematic comparison of local versus nonlocal treatments of the same underlying quark dynamics.
