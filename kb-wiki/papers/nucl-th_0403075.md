# Improved effective momentum approximation for quasielastic (e,e') scattering off highly charged nuclei

> We address the problem of including Coulomb distortion effects in inclusive quasielastic (e,e') reactions using an improved version of the effective momentum approximation. Arguments are given that a simple modification of the effective momentum approximation, which is no longer applicable in its original form in the case of highly charged nuclei, leads to very good results. A comparison of the improved effective momentum method with exact calculations is given. A critical remark concerns recent experiments with inclusive quasielastic positron scattering.

# Improved effective momentum approximation for quasielastic (e,e') scattering off highly charged nuclei

## Key claim
The standard effective momentum approximation (EMA) systematically underestimates quasielastic electron scattering cross sections for heavy nuclei, but a simple modification called EMA', using a momentum shift of 2/3 of the central potential value for the cross section calculation while retaining the full central potential value for the focusing factor, yields results in excellent agreement with exact distorted-wave calculations.

## Method
The authors compared the standard EMA, which uses a momentum shift Δk = -V₀/c based on the central electrostatic potential V₀ of the nucleus, with exact Dirac wave function calculations by Kim et al. (2000). They proposed EMA', which uses Δk' = -2V₀/3c for the plane-wave cross section calculation (reflecting that most nucleons are near the nuclear surface) while retaining Δk = -V₀/c for the focusing factor. They validated the approximation by interpolating from exact results and by performing eikonal approximation calculations for electron and positron scattering on ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the central potential energy V₀ = -26.6 MeV, calculated from the formula V₀ = -3αZ/(2r_c) with r_c = [1.1 A^{1/3} + 0.86 A^{-1/3}] fm.
- Standard EMA uses Δk = 26.6 MeV/c; EMA' uses Δk' = (2/3) × 26.6 MeV/c ≈ 17.7 MeV/c for the momentum shift in the cross section.
- For electrons at initial energy ε_i = 485 MeV and scattering angle Θ_e = 60°, EMA' gave excellent agreement with exact calculations across varying energy loss ω.
- For electrons at ε_i = 310 MeV and Θ_e = 143°, EMA' showed even better agreement in the low energy transfer region.
- For the positron experiment analysis, the authors used initial energies of 385 MeV for electrons and 420 MeV for positrons, corresponding to a momentum shift from 385 MeV/c to 402.5 MeV/c for electrons and from 420 MeV/c to 402.5 MeV/c for positrons.
- The peak values of the total response functions from Gueye et al. (1999) could differ by about 15 per cent, and the overall normalization uncertainty is 15 to 20 per cent.

## Context
This work extends the effective momentum approximation originally developed for light nuclei by Knoll (1974) and Lenz and Rosenfelder (1971), and addresses its failure for heavy nuclei as demonstrated by exact calculations from Kim et al. (2000). The improved EMA' method enables reliable analysis of quasielastic electron scattering data from highly charged nuclei without the computational expense of full distorted-wave calculations. The authors also critically reexamine positron scattering experiments by Gueye et al. (1999) on ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb, suggesting that the data analysis should be revised due to normalization uncertainties and the inadequacy of standard EMA for extracting response functions.
