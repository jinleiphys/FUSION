# Top Jets in the Peak Region: Factorization Analysis with NLL Resummation

> We consider top-quarks produced at large energy in e+e- collisions, and address the question of what top-mass can be measured from reconstruction. The production process is characterized by well separated scales: the center-of-mass energy, Q, the top mass, m, the top decay width, Gamma_t, and also LambdaQCD; scales which can be disentangled with effective theory methods. In particular we show how the mass measurement depends on the way in which soft radiation is treated, and that this can shift the mass peak by an amount of order Q LambdaQCD/m. We sum large logs for Q >> m >> Gamma_t > LambdaQCD and demonstrate that the renormalization group ties together the jet and soft interactions below the scale m. Necessary conditions for the invariant mass spectrum to be protected from large logs are formulated. Results for the cross-section are presented at next-to-leading order with next-to-leading-log resummation, for invariant masses in the peak region and the tail region. Using our results we also predict the thrust distribution for massive quark jets at NLL order for large thrust. We demonstrate that soft radiation can be precisely controlled using data on massless jet production, and that in principle, a short distance mass parameter can be measured using jets with precision better than LambdaQCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the top quark mass can be measured from jet reconstruction in e+e- collisions with precision better than Lambda_QCD, but the mass peak can be shifted by an amount of order Q*Lambda_QCD/m depending on how soft radiation is treated, and this shift must be controlled through a short-distance mass definition.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem for the double differential invariant mass distribution of top and antitop jets in e+e- -> t tbar at center-of-mass energies Q >> m, using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) matched onto Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) generalized for unstable particles. They compute the hard functions H_Q and H_m, the jet functions B_+ and B_-, and the soft function S at one-loop order in alpha_s, and perform next-to-leading-log (NLL) resummation of large logarithms between the scales Q, m, Gamma_t, and Lambda_QCD using renormalization group evolution. The analysis covers both the peak region (M_t^2 - m^2 ~ m*Gamma) and the tail region (M_t^2 - m^2 >> m*Gamma) of the invariant mass distribution.

## Key numbers

- Top quark width Gamma_t^SM ~ 1.4 GeV (Standard Model value)
- Top mass from CDF and D0 measurements: m_t = 170.9 +/- 1.8 GeV
- Power corrections to the factorization theorem are suppressed by alpha_s*m/Q, m^2/Q^2, Gamma_t/m, or s_{t,tbar}/m^2
- In the tail region, nonperturbative corrections scale as O(Lambda_QCD*Q/s_{t,tbar})
- The hadronic scale Lambda ~ 0.5 GeV is used as the scale where interactions become non-perturbative

## Context

This work extends the factorization formalism introduced in Fleming et al. (2007) for top jet invariant mass distributions, providing explicit one-loop calculations and NLL resummation that were not present in the earlier conceptual paper. The approach builds on SCET methods developed by Bauer et al. (2000-2003) and HQET techniques from Manohar and Wise (2000), and is analogous to factorization theorems for massless event shapes studied by Korchemsky et al. (1994-2000) and Bauer et al. (2002-2003). The results enable precision top mass measurements at a future linear collider with uncertainty below Lambda_QCD, and the soft function formalism can be calibrated using data from massless jet production.
