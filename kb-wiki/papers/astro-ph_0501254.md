# Lattice QCD Constraints on Hybrid and Quark Stars

> A QCD-motivated dynamical-quasiparticle model with parameters adjusted to reproduce the lattice-QCD equation of state is extrapolated from region of high temperatures and moderate baryonic densities to the domain of high baryonic densities and zero temperature. The resulting equation of state matched with realistic hadronic equations of state predicts a phase transition into the quark phase at higher densities than those reachable in neutron star interiors. This excludes the possibility of the existence of hybrid (hadron-quark) stars. Pure quark stars are possible and have low masses, small radii and very high central densities. Similar results are obtained for a simple bag model with massive quarks, fitted to reproduce the same lattice results. Self-bound quark matter is also excluded within these models. Uncertainties in the present extrapolation re discussed. Comparison with standard bag models is made.

## Key claim

The paper uses a QCD-motivated dynamical-quasiparticle model, fitted to lattice QCD data at high temperature and moderate baryon density, to extrapolate to cold dense matter; it finds that the predicted phase transition to quark matter occurs at densities higher than those reachable in neutron star interiors, thereby excluding hybrid stars, but pure quark stars remain possible with low masses, small radii, and very high central densities, though the authors note significant uncertainties in the extrapolation.

## Method

The authors extrapolate a dynamical-quasiparticle (DQ) model, with parameters fitted to (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results at finite chemical potential, to zero temperature and high baryon density. They solve the mean-field equations for effective quark and gluon masses and the bag parameter, then compute the equation of state (EoS) for cold, beta-equilibrated, electrically neutral matter. The resulting quark-matter EoS is matched to a realistic hadronic EoS (the HHJ parameterization of the Urbana-Argonne A18+δv+UIX* EoS) using a Maxwell construction to determine the phase transition density.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the DQ model fit is \(T_C = 195\) MeV, slightly above the lattice value of 175 MeV.
- For the "1-loop" DQ version, an overall normalization factor \(N_{\rm norm} = 0.9\) is applied to match lattice pressure.
- For the "2-loop" DQ version, the auxiliary function \(f_{\rm 2-loop} \simeq 2.6\) in the temperature range \(T_C\) to \(3T_C\).
- The "heavy-bag" model uses quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 330\) MeV, \(m_s = 450\) MeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 600\) MeV, and bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 183\) MeV.
- The "light-bag-155" model uses \(B^{1/4} = 155\) MeV; the "light-bag-200" model uses \(B^{1/4} = 200\) MeV.
- The hadronic EoS (HHJ) is valid for densities \(n \lesssim 4 n_0\), where \(n_0\) is nuclear saturation density.
- A neutron star mass measurement of \(M_{\rm J0751+1807} = 2.2 \pm 0.2 M_\odot\) at 95% confidence is cited.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model approach of Peshier, Kämpfer, and Soff (2000), who fitted to earlier lattice data and found quark stars possible only for bag constants \(B^{1/4} \leq 180-200\) MeV, but did not consider hybrid stars. The present study uses newer lattice data from the (2+1)-flavor simulations and a different dynamical-quasiparticle model that explicitly includes confinement via the absence of solutions below a certain temperature-chemical potential combination. The results contradict many earlier bag-model studies (e.g., those using the "light-bag-155" or "light-bag-200" parameter sets) that predicted hybrid stars or self-bound quark matter; instead, the lattice-constrained models exclude hybrid stars and self-bound matter, while allowing pure quark stars only with extreme properties. The work enables future studies to refine the extrapolation by including color-superconducting pairing effects (2SC and CFL phases) and to test the predictions against upcoming neutron star observations.
