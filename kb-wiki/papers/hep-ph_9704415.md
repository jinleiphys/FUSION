# Elastic pion-nucleon Scattering to O(p^3) in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The elastic pi-N scattering amplitude in the isospin limit is calculated in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, up to the third order. Threshold parameters like scattering lengths, volumes, effective ranges, etc. are compared with data. All relevant low energy constants are fixed from the available pion-nucleon data. A clear improvement in the description of data is observed, when going from the first two orders in the chiral expansion to the third one. The importance of even higher orders is suggested by the result.

## Key claim

The elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in the isospin limit is calculated to third order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), and a clear improvement in the description of data is observed compared to first and second order, but the results suggest that even higher orders are still important for a fully satisfactory description.

## Method

The full elastic pi-N scattering amplitude was computed in HBCHPT up to O(p^3) by summing tree-level diagrams from the effective Lagrangian at orders 1, 2, and 3, together with one-loop diagrams from the lowest-order Lagrangian and counterterm contributions. All relevant low-energy constants (LECs) appearing in the amplitude were fixed by fitting to available pion-nucleon scattering data, rather than estimated from resonance saturation as in prior work. The calculation was performed strictly within the HBCHPT framework, including the effects of heavy source components in the nucleon external leg factors.

## Key numbers

* The S-wave pi-N scattering lengths receive corrections at O(p^3) that improve agreement with data; the paper reports the LECs b_i^r(mu) determined from fits, with specific numerical values given for the finite parts (e.g., b_1 through b_19) at the scale mu = M_pi.
* The pion decay constant in the chiral limit F is related to the physical F_pi by F = F_pi (1 - M_pi^2/(16 pi^2 F_pi^2) * bar l_4), where bar l_4 is a dimensionless low-energy constant.
* The nucleon mass shift delta m is given by delta m = -4 M_pi^2/m_N * a_3 - 3 g_A^2 M_pi^3/(32 pi F_pi^2), where a_3 is a second-order LEC.
* The renormalized LECs l_i^r(mu) and b_i^r(mu) are expressed in terms of scale-invariant quantities bar l_i and tilde b_i, with the scale dependence given by l_i^r(mu) = l_i^r(mu_0) + gamma_i/(4 pi)^2 * ln(mu_0/mu) and b_i^r(mu) = b_i^r(mu_0) + beta_i * ln(mu_0/mu).

## Context

This work extends earlier HBCHPT calculations of pi-N scattering by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1993, 1995, 1996) which determined some LECs from resonance saturation rather than direct data fits. The author fixes all LECs from experimental pi-N data, providing a more data-driven determination. The results enable future calculations at O(p^4) and beyond, and the explicit separation of polynomial and unitary parts of the amplitude provides a framework for systematic improvement.
