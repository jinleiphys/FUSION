# Correlation femtoscopy

> The basics of correlation femtoscopy, recent results from femtoscopy in relativistic heavy ion collisions and their consequences are shortly reviewed.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the basics and recent results of correlation femtoscopy in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the standard two-particle approximation and smoothness assumptions may not be justified for rare pairs associated with large phase-space density fluctuations or in low-energy heavy ion reactions where particles are produced in a mean field of residual nuclei.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism of two-particle momentum correlations at small relative momenta, where the correlation function is defined as the ratio of the measured two-particle distribution to a reference distribution obtained by mixing particles from different events. The space-time information is extracted based on assumptions of small mean freeze-out phase space density, smoothness of the momentum dependence of one-particle emission probabilities, independent or incoherent particle emission, and the equal-time approximation for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The correlation functions are analyzed in terms of Gaussian correlation radii in the out-side-longitudinal coordinate system and their dependence on pair rapidity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* For central lead-lead or gold-gold collisions at the highest SPS energy, the mean freeze-out phase space density for pions with \(p_t > 0.2\) GeV/\(c\) is substantially smaller than unity, pointing to negligible multiboson effects in this \(p_t\)-region.
* At RHIC, the correlation data point to a kinetic freeze-out temperature somewhat below the pion mass, a strong transverse flow with mean transverse flow velocity exceeding half the velocity of light, a short evolution time of 8-10 fm/\(c\), and a very short emission duration of about 2-3 fm/\(c\).
* Fits of the \(p\Lambda\) correlation functions from experiments at AGS, RHIC, and SPS yield correlation radii of 3-4 fm, in agreement with radii obtained from \(pp\) correlations in the same experiments.
* In a preliminary analysis of NA49 \(\pi^+\pi^-\) correlation data from central \(Pb+Pb\) 158 AGeV collisions using the RQMD model, the fitted strong interaction scale appeared to be \(0.63 \pm 0.08\), significantly lower than unity.
* The RQMD simulation of central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at 158 AGeV yields for \(\pi^\pm p\) systems: \(\langle\Delta x\rangle \doteq -5.2\) fm, \(\langle\Delta t\rangle \doteq 2.9\) fm/\(c\), \(\langle\Delta x^*\rangle \doteq -8.5\) fm.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on correlation femtoscopy by Kopylov and Podgoretsky (reviewed in 1989) and the development of the formalism including final state interaction effects by various authors (Koonin, 1977; Gyulassy et al., 1979; Lednicky and Lyuboshitz, 1981; Boal and Shillcock, 1986; Bowler, 1991). The paper discusses recent results from relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC, particularly the weak energy dependence of correlation radii that contradicts traditional hydrodynamic models (Soff et al., 2001; Hirano and Tsuda, 2002) and may indicate explosive particle production or early hadronization. The results enable further studies of collective flows, relative space-time asymmetries in particle production, and strong interactions between specific particles such as \(\Lambda\Lambda\) and \(\bar{p}\Lambda\) systems that are hardly accessible by other means.
