# Muon capture on light nuclei

> This work investigates the muon capture reactions 2H(μ^-,ν_μ)nn and 3He(μ^-,ν_μ)3H and the contribution to their total capture rates arising from the axial two-body currents obtained imposing the partially-conserved-axial-current (PCAC) hypothesis. The initial and final A=2 and 3 nuclear wave functions are obtained from the Argonne v_{18} two-nucleon potential, in combination with the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential in the case of A=3. The weak current consists of vector and axial components derived in chiral effective field theory. The low-energy constant entering the vector (axial) component is determined by reproducting the isovector combination of the trinucleon magnetic moment (Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium beta-decay). The total capture rates are 393.1(8) s^{-1} for A=2 and 1488(9) s^{-1} for A=3, where the uncertainties arise from the adopted fitting procedure.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding the "potential current" terms required by the partially-conserved-axial-current (PCAC) hypothesis to the chiral effective field theory weak current operator changes the total muon capture rates on deuteron and helium-3 by less than 0.1%, well below the theoretical uncertainties, provided the low-energy constant \(d_R\) is refitted to reproduce the tritium beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Method

The authors calculated the total capture rates for the reactions \(^2\)H(\(\mu^-,\nu_\mu\))\(nn\) and \(^3\)He(\(\mu^-,\nu_\mu\))\(^3\)H using nuclear wave functions from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon potential (with the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential for \(A=3\)) and a weak current operator derived in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N\(^3\)LO. They added the axial two-body "potential current" terms from Mosconi (2005) to the chiral current operator, refitted the low-energy constant \(d_R\) to the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element of tritium beta-decay, and compared the resulting rates to those of Marcucci (2010) which omitted these terms.

## Key numbers

- Total muon capture rate on deuteron (\(^2\)H): 393.1(8) s\(^{-1}\), with the uncertainty from the fitting procedure.
- Total muon capture rate on helium-3 (\(^3\)He): 1488(9) s\(^{-1}\), with the uncertainty from the fitting procedure.
- The low-energy constant \(d_R\) for the axial contact term, refitted to reproduce the tritium beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element, is 1.54(8); this differs by about 13% from the value 1.75(8) in Marcucci (2010) without potential currents.
- The difference in total rates between the present work and Marcucci (2010) is \(\leq 0.1\%\); the largest partial-wave difference is 2% in the \(^3P_0\) contribution to the deuteron capture rate.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory calculations of muon capture on light nuclei by Marcucci (2010) to include the "potential current" axial two-body terms required by PCAC, which were previously studied by Mosconi (2005) and applied to muon capture on deuteron by Ricci (2010). The results show that these potential currents have negligible effect on the total capture rates when the weak current is consistently constrained by tritium beta-decay, and the authors expect similarly tiny contributions in weak capture reactions of astrophysical interest at low momentum transfer.
