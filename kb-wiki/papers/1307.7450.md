# Hyperon Stars in Strong Magnetic Fields

> We investigate the effects of strong magnetic fields on the properties of hyperon stars. The matter is described by a hadronic model with parametric coupling. The matter is considered to be at zero temperature, charge neutral, beta-equilibrated, containing the baryonic octet, electrons and muons. The charged particles have their orbital motions Landau-quantized in the presence of strong magnetic fields (SMF). Two parametrisations of a chemical potential dependent static magnetic field are considered, reaching $1-2 \times 10^{18}\,G$ in the center of the star. Finally, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass-radius relation and population of the stars.

## Key claim
The paper claims that strong magnetic fields (reaching \(1-2 \times 10^{18}\,\text{G}\) in the stellar center) can stiffen the equation of state of hyperon stars, allowing maximum masses up to \(2.03 M_\odot\), but this result depends on the specific parametrization of the density-dependent magnetic field and the choice of hyperonic coupling scheme.

## Method
The authors model neutron star matter at zero temperature, charge neutral, and beta-equilibrated, containing the full baryon octet, electrons, and muons. Charged particles have their orbital motions Landau-quantized in a strong magnetic field, and the hadronic matter is described by a relativistic mean-field model with parametric coupling that includes many-body forces. Two parametrizations of a chemical-potential-dependent static magnetic field are used, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass-radius relation and particle populations.

## Key numbers
- Central magnetic field strengths reach \(1-2 \times 10^{18}\,\text{G}\) in the star.
- Surface magnetic field is set to \(B_{\text{surf}} = 10^{15}\,\text{G}\).
- The \(\Lambda\)-hypernucleus binding energy used is \((B/A)_\Lambda = -28\,\text{MeV}\) at saturation density, with \(\chi_{\sigma\Lambda} = 0.75\).
- The maximum mass obtained for a magnetic hyperon star is \(2.03 M_\odot\).
- The density-dependent magnetic field parameters are \(a = 2.5\) and \(b = 4.35 \times 10^{-7}\).
- Three values of the effective nucleon mass ratio are considered: \(m^*/m_N = 0.70, 0.75, 0.78\) (corresponding to \(\lambda = 0.06, 0.10, 0.14\)).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of magnetized neutron stars (Lattimer and others) by including hyperons and a density-dependent magnetic field profile. The suppression of hyperon populations in strong magnetic fields agrees with earlier findings by Lattimer. The results enable future modeling of hyperon stars with realistic magnetic field profiles and could be compared to observations of massive neutron stars to constrain hyperonic coupling schemes.
