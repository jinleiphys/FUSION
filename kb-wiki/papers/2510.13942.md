# Studying hard probe dynamics in QGP using effective field theory

> An effective field theory framework is developed to study the interaction of heavy quarks in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The latter is treated as a relativistic non-dissipative colorless fluid which can be studied using a derivatively coupled effective field theory based on previous work. Coupling this to heavy quarks provides systematic way to obtain the interaction between the heavy quark and phonons, excitations of the fluid. In particular we calculate the decay width of heavy quark to phonon and phonon-heavy quark scattering in a thermal medium.

## Key claim

The authors develop an effective field theory (phi-EFT) framework for heavy quarks interacting with a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma treated as a relativistic non-dissipative fluid, but the collective quark-fluid interaction is only the dominant mechanism for momentum exchange when the UV scale Lambda_h is greater than or comparable to the temperature T (Lambda_h >= T); when Lambda_h is much smaller than T, interactions with microscopic constituents dominate instead.

## Method

The authors construct a derivatively coupled effective field theory for a perfect fluid using comoving coordinate scalar fields phi^I, whose excitations are phonons with speed of sound c_s. They couple heavy quarks to the fluid by expanding the QCD Lagrangian with functions of the scalar X = J^mu J_mu, where J^mu is the fluid current, and systematically expand around equilibrium to obtain phonon-quark interaction vertices suppressed by powers of the UV scale Lambda_h. They then compute the 2-to-2 heavy quark-phonon scattering cross section using the leading-order diagrams (suppressed by Lambda_h^2) and neglect the subleading point-like two-quark-two-phonon interaction (suppressed by Lambda_h^3).

## Key numbers

* The UV scale Lambda_h is estimated to lie in the range 3 T less than or similar to Lambda_h less than or similar to 6 T for realistic to strong coupling, based on the sound attenuation length and the first non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode.
* For weakly coupled QCD with thermal coupling g(T) approximately 3.2, the kinematic shear viscosity is eta/s approximately 0.14, giving a phonon momentum constraint k less than or similar to 5 T.
* For strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory at infinite 't Hooft coupling, the speed of sound is c_s = 1/sqrt(3) and the shear viscosity is eta/s = 1/(4 pi), giving a phonon momentum constraint k much less than 2 sqrt(3) pi T approximately 11 T.
* The first non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode in the shear channel gives the constraint k much less than omega_1/c_s = 1.56 (2 pi T) sqrt(3) approximately 17 T.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory framework for relativistic hydrodynamics developed by Endlich (2010), Dubovsky (2011), Grozdanov (2013), and others by coupling heavy quark probes to the fluid phonon degrees of freedom. It builds on prior work by Gupta (2023) but provides a more complete treatment of all operators. The framework enables systematic calculation of heavy quark energy loss and momentum broadening in strongly coupled QGP, complementing holographic calculations by Friess (2006), Gubser (2007), and Chesler (2007), as well as perturbative QCD calculations by Neufeld (2008, 2010). The authors note that recent CMS observations (2025) of fluid response to energetic probes motivate this theoretical development.
