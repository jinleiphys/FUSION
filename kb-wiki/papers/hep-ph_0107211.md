# Illuminating Dense Quark Matter

> We imagine shining light on a lump of cold dense quark matter, in the CFL phase and therefore a transparent insulator. We calculate the angles of reflection and refraction, and the intensity of the reflected and refracted light. Although the only potentially observable context for this phenomenon (reflection of light from and refraction of light through an illuminated quark star) is unlikely to be realized, our calculation casts new light on the old idea that confinement makes the QCD vacuum behave as if filled with a condensate of color-magnetic monopoles.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the reflection and refraction of ordinary light incident on a lump of cold dense quark matter in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, showing that the CFL phase behaves as a transparent dielectric with a modified index of refraction; although the observable context (illuminated quark stars) is unlikely to be realized, the analysis provides a derivation of boundary conditions that support the idea that the QCD vacuum behaves as if filled with a condensate of color-magnetic monopoles.

## Method

The authors treat the CFL phase as a transparent insulator with a rotated photon degree of freedom, characterized by a mixing angle θ between the ordinary photon and the eighth gluon. They solve the classical electromagnetic boundary value problem at a planar vacuum-CFL interface, matching ordinary electromagnetic fields in vacuum to rotated (tilde-Q) electromagnetic fields inside the CFL phase, using four boundary conditions derived from the Meissner effect for X-bosons in the CFL phase and from the dual Meissner effect description of confinement in the QCD vacuum. They treat two independent linear polarizations of the incident light and compute the reflection and transmission coefficients, verifying energy conservation via the Poynting vector.

## Key numbers

- The fermionic gap Δ in the CFL phase is likely of order tens to 100 MeV at astrophysically accessible densities, with quark chemical potential μ ∼ (350–500) MeV.
- The mixing angle θ is small, perhaps of order 1/20, because at accessible densities the gluons are strongly coupled (g²/4π ∼ 1) and photons are weakly coupled (e²/4π ≈ 1/137).
- The index of refraction of CFL quark matter is ṋ = √(ε̃/ε₀); for μ/Δ ∼ (4–10), one obtains ṋ ∼ (1.02–1.1).
- The rotated photon charge is ẽ = e cosθ, which is less than e.
- The dielectric constant in the CFL phase is ε̃ = ε₀ (1 + (8α/9π) cos²θ μ²/Δ²), valid to leading order in α and for ω, k ≪ Δ.
- The magnetic permeability in the CFL phase remains unchanged to leading order: μ̃ = μ₀.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the partial Meissner effect for static magnetic fields in CFL matter (ABRflux) to the case of time-varying electromagnetic fields. The authors show that the boundary conditions motivated by the dual superconductor picture of confinement (tHooft) can be derived from the single easily-derived boundary condition (ParallelH) together with Snell's law and energy conservation, thereby illuminating the connection between dense quark matter and the QCD vacuum. The results enable future studies of optical properties of quark stars and provide a new perspective on the nature of confinement.
