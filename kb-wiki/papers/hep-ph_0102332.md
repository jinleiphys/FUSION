# Verifying the Kugo-Ojima Confinement Criterion in Landau Gauge Yang-Mills Theory

> Expanding the Landau gauge gluon and ghost two-point functions in a power series we investigate their infrared behavior. The corresponding powers are constrained through the ghost Dyson-Schwinger equation by exploiting multiplicative renormalizability. Without recourse to any specific truncation we demonstrate that the infrared powers of the gluon and ghost propagators are uniquely related to each other. Constraints for these powers are derived, and the resulting infrared enhancement of the ghost propagator signals that the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion is fulfilled in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The leading infrared powers of the gluon and ghost propagators are uniquely related by the relation \(\epsilon_0 = -2\delta_0\), and the ghost dressing function exponent \(\delta_0\) is shown to be negative, which implies the Kugo–Ojima confinement criterion is fulfilled in Landau gauge Yang–Mills theory. This result is derived without recourse to any specific truncation of the Dyson–Schwinger equations, relying instead on multiplicative renormalizability and the non-renormalization of the ghost-gluon vertex.

## Method

The authors expand the Landau gauge gluon and ghost dressing functions \(Z(p^2)\) and \(G(p^2)\) as power series in \(p^2/\mu^2\) with exponents \(\epsilon_n\) and \(\delta_n\), and similarly expand the ghost-gluon vertex. They insert these expansions into the ghost Dyson–Schwinger equation, separate the ultraviolet and infrared parts of the momentum integral, and use multiplicative renormalizability to match the leading powers. The ultraviolet part of the integral is shown to determine the renormalization constant \(\widetilde Z_3\), and by requiring consistency of the asymptotic series, the relation \(\epsilon_0 = -2\delta_0\) is derived. The sign of \(\delta_0\) is then fixed by requiring positivity of the ghost dressing function coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The leading infrared exponents satisfy \(\epsilon_0 = -2\delta_0\), relating the gluon and ghost dressing functions.
- The ghost exponent \(\delta_0\) is constrained to be negative (\(\delta_0 < 0\)), implying the ghost propagator is more singular than a massless pole in the infrared.
- A lower bound \(\delta_0 > -1\) is cited from a generalized masslessness condition (Alkofer 2000), which implies the gluon exponent satisfies \(2 > \epsilon_0 > 0\).
- The explicit evaluation of the leading-order integral yields the inequality \(-\frac{3}{4} \frac{C_A g^2}{16\pi^2} e_0 d_0^2 \frac{1}{\delta_0} > 0\), where \(C_A = N_c\) is the color factor.

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of infrared behavior in Landau gauge Yang–Mills theory beyond previous truncation schemes, such as those of von Smekal (1997) and Atkinson (1998), which had suggested the Kugo–Ojima criterion might hold but were sensitive to truncation. The result directly verifies the Kugo–Ojima confinement criterion (Kugo 1979, Kugo 1995) by purely analytical methods, and it is correlated with the Oehme–Zimmermann superconvergence relations (Oehme 1980). The analysis enables future work, such as Watson (2001), to investigate whether an infrared constant gluon propagator can be excluded by also considering the gluon Dyson–Schwinger equation.
