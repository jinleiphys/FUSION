# Neutrinoless $ββ$-Decay in DCEQTDA

> We have recently developed a nuclear model, which is a natural extension of the $pn$-QRPA model, specially designed to describe double charge exchange (DCE) processes generated by two-body DCE transition operators. It is based on the Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) for $pn$ and $2p2n$ excitations in intermediate and final nuclei, respectively, and will be called DCEQTDA. As such, this model, having the same number of free parameters as the $pn$-QRPA, also brings into play the excitations of four quasiparticles to build up the final nuclear states, which are then used to evaluate the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for all $0^+$ and $2^+$ final states, including resonances, and not just for the ground state as in $pn$-QRPA. In addition, it allows us to evaluate: (a) the values of $Q_{ββ}$, (b) the excitation energies in final nuclei, and (c) the DCE sum rules, which are fulfilled in the DCEQTDA. So far, this model has been used mainly to calculate double beta decays with the emission of two neutrinos ($2νββ$-decay). Here, we extend it to the study of these processes when no neutrinos are emitted ($0νββ$-decay), evaluating them in a series of nuclei, but paying special attention to (i) $^{76}$Se, which have been measured recently in the GERDA and MAJORANA experiments, and (ii) $^{124}$Te, for which the first direct observation of the double electron capture $2ν$ has been performed with the XENON1T dark matter detector. We obtain good agreement with the data for both the ground state and the excited states. The validity of the DCEQTDA model is checked by comparing the calculation with the experimental data for the $2νββ$ NMEs, and for the $Q_{ββ}$, in a series of nuclei.

## Key claim

The DCEQTDA model, an extension of the pn-QRPA that includes four-quasiparticle excitations and the Pauli principle, can reproduce the measured 2νββ-decay half-lives and Q-values for ⁷⁶Ge and ¹²⁴Xe, and it predicts that the 0νββ-decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) and the double charge-exchange reaction NMEs are of the same order of magnitude even in the region of giant resonances, though the model's Q-values deviate from experiment by up to 0.8 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct the DCEQTDA model by representing the initial, intermediate, and final nuclei as the BCS vacuum, two-quasiparticle (pn) excitations, and four-quasiparticle (2p2n) excitations, respectively, solving the resulting eigenvalue problem. They evaluate the 0νββ-decay NMEs within the closure approximation using a δ-force residual interaction, with parameters fixed by the Partial SU(4) Symmetry Restoration condition (PSU4SR) and, for ⁷⁶Ge, an alternative parameter set (P2) that fits the measured ground-state 2νββ NME. The model uses a large single-particle space (9 levels for ⁷⁶Ge, 7 for ¹²⁴Xe) to satisfy the DCE sum rules, and it calculates NMEs for all 0⁺ and 2⁺ final states, not just the ground state.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se, the calculated Qββ⁻ is 1.239 MeV (P1) and 1.314 MeV (P2), compared to the experimental value of 2.039 MeV.
- For ¹²⁴Xe → ¹²⁴Te, the calculated Qee is 4.071 MeV (P1), compared to the experimental value of 2.864 MeV.
- The 2νββ NME for the 0⁺₁ ground state of ⁷⁶Se is 56.4 × 10⁻³ (P1) and 103 × 10⁻³ (P2), with the experimental value being 107 × 10⁻³.
- The 2νββ half-life for the 0⁺₁ state in ⁷⁶Se is 6.8 × 10²¹ yr (P1) and 2.0 × 10²¹ yr (P2), compared to the experimental value of 1.88 × 10²¹ yr.
- For ¹²⁴Xe → ¹²⁴Te, the 2νee half-life for the 0⁺₁ state is 1.51 × 10²² yr (P1), compared to the experimental value of (1.8 ± 0.5) × 10²² yr.
- The number of four-quasiparticle states used in ⁷⁶Se is 2045 states for 0⁺ and 8456 states for 2⁺; in ¹²⁴Te it is 1146 states for 0⁺ and 4918 states for 2⁺.

## Context

This work extends the DCEQTDA model, previously applied to 2νββ-decay (Fer20), to the 0νββ-decay of ⁷⁶Ge and ¹²⁴Xe, which are relevant to the GERDA, MAJORANA, and XENON1T experiments. The model improves upon the standard pn-QRPA (Vog86, Civ87, Tom87) by allowing calculations for excited final states and satisfying DCE sum rules (Vog88, Mut92, Zhe89), and it is compared to the Multiple-Commutator Model (Aun96) and Shell Model (Kos22, perez2019two) calculations. The results enable future comparisons between 0νββ-decay NMEs and double charge-exchange reaction NMEs measured by the NUMEN experiment (Len19, Cav20).
