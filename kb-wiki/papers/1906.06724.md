# Fusion hindrance effects in laser-induced non-neutral plasmas

> Inertial confinement fusion hotspots and cluster Coulomb explosion plasmas may develop a positive net electric charge. The Coulomb barrier penetrability and the rate of nuclear fusion reactions at ultra-low energies ($\lesssim 10$ keV) are altered by such an environment. These effects are here studied via the screening potential approach. Approximate analytical results are developed by evaluating the average screening potential for some scenarios of interest. It is found that fusion is hindered for reactions between thermal fuel nuclei, while an enhancement is expected for secondary and "beam-target" reactions. Depending on the plasma conditions, the variations can be relevant even for relatively small net charges (several % difference or more in the fusion rate for an average net charge per nucleus of $10^{-5}$ proton charges).

## Key claim

Fusion reactions between thermal fuel nuclei in a positively charged plasma are hindered (rate decreases), while secondary and "beam-target" reactions are enhanced; these effects can be relevant even for an average net charge per nucleus as small as 10⁻⁵ proton charges, producing several percent difference or more in the fusion rate depending on plasma conditions.

## Method

The authors model the plasma as a uniform sphere with constant net charge density and Maxwell-Boltzmann reactant energy distribution. They compute the screening potential, the kinetic energy transferred from the charged environment to the reactants during their approach, by linking the distance reactants travel before fusion to an appropriate mean free path (Coulomb scattering mean free path for inertial confinement fusion; cluster geometrical cross-section for Coulomb explosion). The average screening potential is then used to estimate enhancement factors for the reaction rate per particle pair and average cross-section via the saddle-point approximation.

## Key numbers

- The average net charge per nucleus considered is 10⁻⁵ proton charges, which yields "several % difference or more" in the fusion rate.
- The study applies to ultra-low collision energies less than 10 keV.
- For inertial confinement fusion systems, the Coulomb logarithm is approximated as ln Λ = 10 throughout.
- For Coulomb explosion systems, the mean free path is λ_CE = N_ic^(1/3) / (π r_s² n_i), with N_ic = 10³ nuclei per cluster and r_s = 0.17 nm.
- Inertial confinement fusion hotspot densities are approximately 10²⁵ atoms/cm³ (citing Hurricane 2014 at NIF), while Coulomb explosion densities are approximately 10¹⁸ atoms/cm³ (citing Lattuada 2016).

## Context

This work extends the screening potential approach of Salpeter (1954) for neutral plasmas to the case of non-neutral plasmas, which may occur in inertial confinement fusion hotspots (challenging the neutrality assumption in hydrodynamic simulations such as Ramis 2016 and Hoffman 2015) and in cluster Coulomb explosion systems (Lattuada 2016, Zhang 2017, Quevedo 2018). The only prior work the authors identify that relaxes the neutrality assumption is a molecular dynamics simulation by Bonasera (2004). The results enable future hydrodynamic models that do not assume charge neutrality and can predict the time evolution of net charge in laser-induced plasmas.
