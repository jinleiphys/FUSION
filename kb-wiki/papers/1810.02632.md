# Cluster structures in $^{12}$C from global energy density functionals

> Spectroscopic properties of low-lying states and cluster structures in $^{12}$C are analyzed in a "beyond mean-field framework" based on global energy density functionals (EDFs). To build symmetry-conserving collective states, axially-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric solutions of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations are first projected onto good values of angular momentum, particle number, and parity. Configuration mixing is implemented using the generator coordinate method formalism. It is shown that such a global microscopic approach, based on a relativistic EDF, can account for the main spectroscopic features of $^{12}$C, including the ground-state and linear-chain bands as well as, to a certain approximation, the excitation energy of the Hoyle state. The calculated form factors reproduce reasonably well the available experimental values, and display an accuracy comparable to that of dedicated microscopic cluster models.

### Key claim
The paper shows that a global relativistic energy density functional (EDF), with no parameters tuned to light nuclei, can reproduce the main spectroscopic features of 12C including the ground-state band and linear-chain bands, and can approximate the Hoyle state excitation energy to within about 800 keV of the experimental value, though the model space is restricted to axial and reflection-asymmetric shapes and cannot capture triaxial configurations important for the Hoyle state.

### Method
Axially-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric reference states were generated as solutions to the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations using the DD-PC1 functional. These states were projected onto good angular momentum, particle number, and parity, then mixed via the generator coordinate method (GCM) over a grid of quadrupole (β2) and octupole (β3) deformations. The resulting collective wave functions were used to compute spectroscopic properties, including transition strengths and electron scattering form factors.

### Key numbers
- Excitation energy of the 2₁⁺ state: 4.3 MeV (experimental: 4.44 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the 4₁⁺ state: 13.9 MeV (experimental: 14.08 MeV)
- Ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 3.23 (experimental: 3.17)
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 8.0 e²fm⁴ (reproduces experimental value)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q_spec(2₁⁺) = 5.0 e fm² (experimental: 7.1 ± 2.5 e fm²)
- B(E2; 4₁⁺ → 2₁⁺) = 15.5 e²fm⁴ (agrees with AMD prediction of 16 e²fm⁴)
- B(E2; 2₃⁺ → 0₃⁺) = 130.6 e²fm⁴ for the linear-chain band
- Hoyle state (0₂⁺) excitation energy: about 800 keV above experimental value
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₂⁺) = 1.7 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 2.6 ± 0.4 e²fm⁴)
- B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 0₁⁺) = 1.3 e²fm⁴ (THSR prediction: 2.0-2.5 e²fm⁴, experimental: 0.73 ± 0.13 e²fm⁴)
- Binding energy of symmetry-restored oblate minimum: E_B = -92.16 MeV

### Context
This work extends beyond-mean-field relativistic EDF calculations (Zhou 2016, Marevic 2018) to 12C, complementing earlier mean-field studies of clustering by Ebran (2012, 2014, 2018). It provides a global microscopic alternative to dedicated cluster models such as AMD (Kanada-En'yo 2007), FMD (Neff 2004, Chernykh 2007), and THSR (Tohsaki 2001, Funaki 2015), with comparable accuracy for form factors and ground-state band properties. The approach enables future studies of triaxial configurations and bent-arm structures, which are needed to fully describe the Hoyle state and linear-chain stability.
