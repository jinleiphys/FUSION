# Hadronic weak charges and parity-violating forward Compton scattering

> Parity-violating elastic electron-nucleon scattering at low momentum transfer allows one to access the nucleon's weak charge, the vector coupling of the $Z$-boson to the nucleon. In the Standard Model and at tree level, the weak charge of the proton is related to the weak mixing angle and accidentally suppressed, $Q_W^{p,\,{\rm tree}}=1-4\sin^2θ_W\approx0.07$. Modern experiments aim at extracting $Q_W^p$ at $\sim1\%$ accuracy. Similarly, parity non-conservation in atoms allows to access the weak charge of atomic nuclei. We consider a novel class of radiative corrections, an exchange of two photons with parity violation in the hadronic/nuclear system. These corrections may affect the extraction of $\sin^2θ_W$ from the experimental data at the relevant level of precision because they are affected by long-range interactions similar to other parity-violating radiative corrections, such as, e.g., the $γZ$-exchange, which has obtained much attention recently. We show that the significance of this new correction increases with the beam energy in parity-violating electron scattering, but the general properties of the parity-violating forward Compton amplitude protect the formal definition of the weak charge as a limit at zero-momentum transfer and zero-energy. We also discuss the relevance of the new correction for upcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a novel class of radiative corrections to parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering, two-photon exchange with parity violation occurring in the hadronic or nuclear system, which may affect the extraction of the weak mixing angle at the percent-level precision of modern experiments. However, the authors show that the general properties of the parity-violating forward Compton amplitude, specifically a superconvergence relation, protect the formal definition of the weak charge as a limit at zero momentum transfer and zero energy, preventing a catastrophic logarithmic divergence.

## Method

The authors calculate the imaginary part of the forward parity-violating two-photon exchange box diagram using a dispersion relation approach, expressing it in terms of the hadronic structure function \(F_3^{\gamma\gamma}\) which arises from parity-violating hadronic interactions. They then reconstruct the real part via forward dispersion relations, and extend the analysis beyond the forward limit to obtain the leading \(t\)-dependence. The elastic contribution is evaluated using the nucleon anapole moment parametrized by \(a_0\), while the inelastic contribution is treated via a sum rule that exploits the superconvergence relation for the parity-violating real Compton amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The tree-level weak charge of the proton is \(Q_W^{p,\,{\rm tree}} = 1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W \approx 0.07\), accidentally suppressed in the Standard Model.
* Modern experiments aim to extract \(Q_W^p\) at \(\sim 1\%\) accuracy.
* The anapole moment parameter is \(a_0 = -(0.74 \pm 1.38) \times 10^{-6} \, \text{GeV}^{-2}\), corresponding to a shift and uncertainty of the modulus of the axial charge \(G_A\) by \(-18(35)\,\%\).
* The elastic contribution from the parity-violating two-photon exchange reduces the elastic \(\gamma Z\)-box correction by 18%, with an uncertainty of 44% of the corrected value of \(G_A + \delta G_A^{ep}\).
* For the nuclear anapole moment contribution to the weak charge of \(^{133}\text{Cs}\), the naive estimate is \(\delta Q_W^{\rm Nucl} \lesssim 10^{-3}\), safely negligible.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radiative corrections to parity-violating electron scattering, specifically the \(\gamma Z\)-box correction studied by Gorchtein (2008) and subsequently by Blunden (2011), Carlson (2012), and others. The parity-violating two-photon exchange correction has not been studied before in this context. The results are relevant for the Q-Weak experiment at Jefferson Lab (Androic 2013) and the P2 experiment at the MESA facility in Mainz (Becker 2013), as well as for atomic parity-violation experiments. The superconvergence relation for the parity-violating Compton amplitude, previously stated by Lukaszuk (2002) and Kurek (2004), is proven here in relativistic chiral perturbation theory and used to construct a self-consistent model.
