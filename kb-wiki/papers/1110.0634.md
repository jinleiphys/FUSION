# Systematics of 2+ states in C isotopes from the ab initio no-core shell model

> We study low-lying states of even carbon isotopes in the range A = 10 - 20 within the large- scale no-core shell model (NCSM). Using several accurate nucleon-nucleon (NN) as well as NN plus three-nucleon (NNN) interactions, we calculate excitation energies of the lowest 2+ state, the electromagnetic B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) transition rates, the 2+1 quadrupole moments as well as se- lected electromagnetic transitions among other states. Recent experimental campaigns to measure 2+-state lifetimes indicate an interesting evolution of nuclear structure that pose a challenge to reproduce theoretically from first principles. Our calculations do not include any effective charges or other fitting parameters. However, calculated results extrapolated to infinite model spaces are also presented. The model-dependence of those results is discussed. Overall, we find a good agree- ment with the experimentally observed trends, although our extrapolated B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) value for 16C is lower compared to the most recent measurements. Relative transition strengths from higher excited states are investigated and the influence of NNN forces is discussed. In particular for 16C we find a remarkable sensitivity of the transition rates from higher excited states to the details of the nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that *ab initio* no-core shell model calculations, using realistic nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions without effective charges, reproduce the experimental trends of electric quadrupole observables across even carbon isotopes from A=10 to A=20, but the extrapolated B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) value for 16C is lower than the most recent measurements, and the transition rates from higher excited states in 16C show remarkable sensitivity to the inclusion of three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for even carbon isotopes 10-20C using several realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (CD-Bonn 2000, INOY, and chiral N3LO) as well as chiral NN plus three-nucleon (NNN) interactions. They solved the many-body problem in a harmonic-oscillator basis truncated by the maximum number of HO excitations (Nmax), using the Antoine shell model code and the NSuite package for importance-truncated NCSM calculations, with Okubo-Lee-Suzuki or similarity renormalization group transformations to soften the Hamiltonians. Calculated results for excitation energies, B(E2) transition rates, and quadrupole moments were extrapolated to infinite model spaces using constrained fits to multiple HO-frequency sequences and linear fits in 1/Nmax.

## Key numbers

- For 12C, the extrapolated quadrupole moment Q(2+1) = +6.2 ± 0.2 e fm² (constrained fit) compared to experimental +6 ± 3 e fm²
- For 16C, the extrapolated B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) = 2.2 ± 0.9 e² fm⁴ (constrained fit) compared to experimental values 2.6 ± 0.9 and 4.15 ± 0.73 e² fm⁴
- For 18C, the extrapolated B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) = 4.2 ± 0.4 e² fm⁴ (constrained fit) compared to experimental 4.3 ± 1.2 e² fm⁴
- For 20C, the extrapolated B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) = 4.8 ± 1.1 e² fm⁴ (constrained fit) compared to experimental upper limit <5.7 e² fm⁴
- The largest matrix dimension was D = 1.4 × 10⁹ for 18C in full Nmax-space calculations
- For 16C with the chiral NN+NNN interaction, the relative B(E2; 2+2 → 0+1) / B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) = 0.11, compared to 2.2 with CD-Bonn 2000
- The optimal HO frequency range for all observables using the CD-Bonn 2000 interaction was 10-14 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of carbon isotopes by Fujii (2007) and Ma (2010) that used effective charges or core polarization approaches, by performing parameter-free *ab initio* calculations with realistic interactions. The calculations address the experimental puzzle of quenched E2 strengths in neutron-rich carbon isotopes reported by Imai (2004), Wiedeking (2008), and Ong (2008), and demonstrate that three-nucleon forces are essential to reproduce the observed suppression of the 2+2 → 0+1 transition in 16C seen in the experiment by Petri (2012). The results enable future systematic studies of electromagnetic observables in light nuclei using chiral interactions without adjustable parameters.
