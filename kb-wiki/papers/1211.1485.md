# Improved dispersive analysis of the scalar form factor of the nucleon

> We present a coupled system of integral equations for the pi pi --> Nbar N and Kbar K --> Nbar N S-waves derived from Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering. We discuss the solution of the corresponding two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnes problem and apply the results to a dispersive analysis of the scalar form factor of the nucleon fully including Kbar K intermediate states. In particular, we determine the corrections Delta_sigma and Delta_D, which are needed for the extraction of the pion-nucleon sigma term from pi N scattering, and show that the difference Delta_D - Delta_sigma=(-1.8 +/- 0.2) MeV is insensitive to the input pi N parameters.

# Improved dispersive analysis of the scalar form factor of the nucleon

## Key claim
The difference Δ_D − Δ_σ = (−1.8 ± 0.2) MeV is insensitive to the input πN parameters, improving upon the previous dispersive determination of (−3.3 ± 0.2) MeV by fully including K̅K intermediate states and using modern ππ phase shifts.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem for the ππ → N̅N and K̅K → N̅N S-waves, derived from Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering. The scalar form factor of the nucleon is then obtained from unsubtracted and once-subtracted dispersion relations using the spectral function constructed from the two-channel unitarity relation, with input including ππ phase shifts from an extended Roy-equation analysis, πN partial waves from the KH80 analysis, and chiral perturbation theory at O(p^4) for the meson form factor normalizations.

## Key numbers
- Δ_D − Δ_σ = (−1.8 ± 0.2) MeV, with the uncertainty reduced compared to the individual corrections due to cancellation of πN parameter dependence
- Δ_σ = (13.9 ± 0.3) MeV at the reference πN coupling constant g^2/4π = 14.28, with linear dependence coefficients Z_1 = 0.36 MeV, Z_2 = 0.57 MeV, Z_3 = 12.0 MeV, Z_4 = −0.81 MeV for variations of πN parameters
- Δ_D = (12.1 ± 0.3) MeV at the same reference point, with coefficients Z̃_1 = 0.42 MeV, Z̃_2 = 0.67 MeV, Z̃_3 = 12.0 MeV, Z̃_4 = −0.77 MeV
- F_π^S(0) = (0.984 ± 0.006) M_π^2 from chiral perturbation theory at O(p^4)
- F_K^S(0) = (0.4 to 0.6) M_π^2, with the lower and upper bounds used to assess the sensitivity of the scalar pion form factor near the K̅K threshold

## Context
This work extends the dispersive analysis of Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1990) by updating the ππ phase shifts and fully including K̅K intermediate states in the two-channel formalism, which were previously treated only approximately. The improved determination of Δ_D − Δ_σ enables a more precise extraction of the pion-nucleon sigma term σ_πN from πN scattering data via the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem, with the remainder Δ_R estimated at |Δ_R| ≲ 2 MeV from chiral perturbation theory (Becher, Leutwyler 2001). The results also provide input for future analyses of the scalar form factor at higher energies and for studies of the nucleon mass decomposition.
