# Elastic and Transition Form Factors in DSEs

> A symmetry preserving framework for the study of continuum Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is obtained from a truncated solution of the QCD equations of motion or QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs). A nonperturbative solution of the DSEs enables the study of, e.g., hadrons as composites of dressed-quarks and dressed-gluons, the phenomena of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB), and therefrom an articulation of any connection between them. It is within this context that we present a unified study of Nucleon, Delta and Roper elastic and transition form factors, and compare predictions made using a framework built upon a Faddeev equation kernel and interaction vertices that possess QCD-like momentum dependence with results obtained using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact-interaction.

## Key claim

A unified Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework can describe the dressed-quark core of the Nucleon, Delta, and Roper resonances, but the predictions agree with experimental data only at momentum transfers above approximately \(Q^2 \gtrsim 3 m_N^2\); at lower momenta, omitted meson-cloud contributions dominate the form factors.

## Method

The author solves a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation in a quark-plus-diquark picture, using a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations with QCD-like momentum-dependent propagators and vertices. Elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors are computed for the Nucleon, Delta, and Roper, and the results are compared with those from a symmetry-preserving vector\(\otimes\)vector contact-interaction (CI) model.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon mass from the Faddeev equation: \(M_{\rm nucleon} = 1.18\ {\rm GeV}\) (dressed-quark core, before meson-cloud dressing).
* Roper (first radial excitation) mass from the Faddeev equation: \(M_{\rm Roper} = 1.73\ {\rm GeV}\) (dressed-quark core); the meson-undressed bare-Roper mass from Ref. Suzuki (2009) is \(1.76\ {\rm GeV}\).
* The dominant scalar-diquark component \(u[ud]\) contributes \(62\%\) of the proton's normalisation.
* The QCD-kindred prediction for the magnetic transition form factor \(G_M^{\ast}\) agrees with data for \(x = Q^2/M_N^2 \gtrsim 0.4\).
* Meson-cloud contributions provide up to \(35\%\) of the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta\) magnetic form factor for \(x \lesssim 2\).
* The Roper transition form factors from the QCD-kindred framework agree with data for \(x \gtrsim 2\); the inferred meson-cloud contribution falls to \(20\%\) of its maximum by \(x=2\).

## Context

This work extends a series of DSE-based studies by Segovia et al. (2013–2015) on Nucleon, Delta, and Roper form factors, comparing a QCD-kindred interaction with a contact-interaction model. It contrasts with dynamical coupled-channels approaches (e.g., Suzuki 2009; Kamano 2013) that include meson-baryon final-state interactions to dress the bare baryon. The results enable future extraction of the dressed-quark core from experimental data at high momentum transfers, particularly at Jefferson Lab, and discriminate between competing models of the quark-quark interaction.
