# The influence of Coulomb on the liquid-gas phase transition and nuclear multifragmentation

> The liquid-gas phase transition is analyzed from the topologic properties of the event distribution in the obervables space. A multi-canonical formalism allows to directly relate the standard phase transition with neutral particles to the case where the non saturating Coulomb interaction is present, and to interpret the effect of Coulomb as a deformation of the probability distributions and a rotation of the order parameter. This formalism is applied to a statistical multifragmentation model and consequences for the nuclear multifragmentation phase transitions are drawn.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction does not fundamentally alter the underlying density of states or the convexity anomalies that define the first-order liquid-gas phase transition; instead, it deforms the probability distribution in the observable space and rotates the order parameter away from the total energy axis, which can make the phase transition appear suppressed or reduced when only the total energy projection is examined.

## Method

The authors introduce a multi-canonical formalism with two Lagrange multipliers (βₙ for the nuclear energy and βc for the Coulomb energy) that allows continuous interpolation between the charged system (βₙ = βc) and the uncharged system (βc = 0). They apply this formalism to a statistical multifragmentation model using a Metropolis Monte Carlo simulation in the isobar ensemble, generating event distributions in the (Eₙ, Vc) plane for nuclear sources of different sizes and at various temperatures and pressures.

## Key numbers

- For a system of total mass number A=50 and atomic number Z=23 at subcritical pressure λ=3×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³: the uncharged case shows a first-order phase transition at βₙ⁻¹=3.7 MeV, while the charged case requires a lower temperature βₙ⁻¹=βc⁻¹=3.1 MeV to achieve similar relative peak heights
- For the same A=50, Z=23 system at higher pressure λ=5×10⁻³ fm⁻³: the uncharged case is shown at βₙ⁻¹=7.5 MeV, and the charged case at βₙ⁻¹=βc⁻¹=6.7 MeV
- For a heavy system of A=200 and atomic number Z=82 at λ=1.26×10⁻³ fm⁻³: the uncharged case is shown at βₙ⁻¹=5.6 MeV; charged cases are shown at βₙ⁻¹=βc⁻¹=3.0, 3.7, 4.4, 4.8, and 5.0 MeV, with a partially screened charge case at βₙ⁻¹=5.0 MeV and effective charge q̄=0.14

## Context

This work extends the topological approach to phase transitions in finite systems developed by Chomaz et al. (2001) and the multi-canonical interpolation method of Challa and Hetherington (1988). It directly addresses the long-standing problem of connecting nuclear multifragmentation experiments on charged nuclei to the thermodynamics of uncharged nuclear matter, which had previously been treated only as a theoretical construction. The formalism enables reconstruction of the uncharged system's entropy from charged-system event distributions via reweighting, though the authors note this becomes prohibitively difficult for heavy systems (A=200) where the Coulomb distortion is severe and the transition becomes a crossover rather than a first-order phase transition.
