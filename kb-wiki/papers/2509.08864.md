# Attractor of hydrodynamic attractors

> Understanding how hydrodynamics emerges rapidly in the medium produced by relativistic heavy-ion collisions remains a key theoretical challenge. While the attractor solution -- manifesting as a non-thermal fixed point during the early evolution stage -- offers a potential explanation, it does not fully account for how far-from-equilibrium systems quickly approach near-equilibrium states. In this Letter, we demonstrate that the attractor in a higher-order hydrodynamic theory converges to the same solution as a second-order theory before reaching the Navier--Stokes limit. This finding suggests that commonly used second-order hydrodynamic equations, provided they incorporate the correct transport coefficients, are adequate to approximate the system's behavior starting from an intermediate time -- even when using a higher-order theory that would be more suitable for describing the far-from-equilibrium evolution.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that attractor solutions in higher-order hydrodynamic theories converge to the same attractor as second-order theories before reaching the Navier-Stokes limit, implying that second-order hydrodynamics with correct transport coefficients can approximate system behavior from intermediate times even when a higher-order theory would be more suitable for far-from-equilibrium evolution.

## Method

The authors study conformal, boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous systems and compare attractor solutions across multiple hydrodynamic frameworks: second-order theories (MIS, DNMR), third-order theories (PJP), and higher-order coupled theories (BD, YJG) that incorporate four-index non-equilibrium moments. They perform slow-roll expansions in powers of 1/w for the anisotropy parameter φ and the higher-rank moment Φ, and numerically solve the attractor equations to compare convergence behavior across theories with different transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- All theories with identical second-order coefficients (c_{2,1}=10/21, c_{2,2}=0) converge to a universal attractor by w ~ 20, where w = τT is the scaled proper time
- The MIS theory, with different second-order parameters, converges with the other theories by w ~ 100
- All theories approach the Navier-Stokes limit by w ~ 400
- The second-order slow-roll coefficient A₂ = (16Cη/9)((2/3 - c_{2,1})Cτ + (16/9)c_{2,2}Cη), where Cη = 1/(4π) and Cτ = 5Cη
- Third-order transport coefficients (c_{3,1}, c_{3,2}, c_{φ,Φ}, c_{Φ,φ}, c_{Φ,Φ}, c_{Φ,2}) appear only in A₃ and higher terms of the slow-roll expansion

## Context

This work extends the attractor concept introduced by Heller and Spalinski (2015), who showed that solutions of second-order hydrodynamic equations rapidly converge to universal attractors regardless of initial viscous corrections. The authors bridge a conceptual gap by showing that higher-order theories (BD, YJG) converge to the same attractor as second-order theories (DNMR, PJP) when they share identical second-order transport coefficients. This enables simplified practical applications of hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that second-order hydrodynamics with proper coefficients suffices to approximate the complete dynamics for studying late-time evolution of the energy-momentum tensor.
