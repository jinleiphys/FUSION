# The third Zemach moment and the size of the proton

> To resolve the puzzle of the proton size raised from the recent result of muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, De Rújula has proposed that a large value of the third Zemach moment $< r^3_{p}>{(2)}$ of the proton to be the solution. His suggestion has been criticized by many groups based on the $ep$ scattering data at low $Q^2$ regime. However, if there is a "thorn" or "lump" in the electric form factor of the proton $G_{E}(Q^2)$ at extremely low $Q^2$ regime, then the third Zemach moment $< r^3_{p}>{(2)}$ would be as large as De Rújula suggested. In this article, we show that the existence of such a "thorn" or "lump" has not been completely excluded, although tightly restricted, by the current data of $ep$ elastic scattering. We also suggest a more sophisticated global fitting procedure of $G_{E}(Q^2)$ for the future fitting.

# The third Zemach moment and the size of the proton

## Key claim
A "thorn" or "lump" in the proton electric form factor at extremely low $Q^2$ (below the lowest measured $Q^2$ of 0.004 GeV$^2$) could produce a third Zemach moment large enough to resolve the proton radius puzzle, and such a structure has not been completely excluded by current $ep$ elastic scattering data, although it is tightly restricted.

## Method
The authors construct a modified electric form factor $G_E(Q^2)$ by adding a Gaussian "thorn" function $\Delta G_E(Q^2) = K_1 \exp[-(Q^2 - K_2)^2 / K_3^4]$ to existing parametrizations (Alberico, Kelly, and the inverse-polynomial fit from the Mainz A1 collaboration). They compute the resulting third Zemach moment $\langle r^3_p \rangle_{(2)}$ via the integral formula $\langle r^3_p \rangle_{(2)} = \frac{48}{\pi} \int_0^\infty \frac{d\mathbf{q}}{\mathbf{q}^4} [G_E^2(\mathbf{q}) - \frac{\mathbf{q}^2}{3} \langle r^2_p \rangle - 1]$ and check whether the deviation from the original fit stays below the 0.2% experimental uncertainty of the Mainz data.

## Key numbers
- The inverse-polynomial fit from Mainz alone gives $\langle r^3 \rangle_{(2)} = 2.96667$ fm$^3$.
- With a "thorn" of height $K_1 = -0.0016962$, peak position $K_2 = 0.001$ GeV, and width $K_3 = 0.0221336$ GeV, the modified fit yields $\langle r^3_p \rangle_{(2)} = 47.3646$ fm$^3$ and $\Delta L = 5.95 \times 10^{-4}$ meV, well below the experimental uncertainty of $3 \times 10^{-3}$ meV.
- The deviation $R(Q^2)$ between the modified and original inverse-polynomial fits ranges from 0% to 0.17%, within the Mainz statistical errors of below 0.2%.
- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift formula is $L^{th}(\text{meV}) = 209.9779 - 5.2262 \langle r_p^2 \rangle + 0.00913 \langle r^3_p \rangle_{(2)}$, with the experimental value $L^{exp} = 206.2949 \pm 0.0032$ meV.
- De Rujula's proposed large third Zemach moment is 36.59 fm$^3$, about fifteen times larger than the dipole-form value.

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle, where the charge radius from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift (Pohl, 2010) is $0.84184(67)$ fm, significantly smaller than the CODATA value of $0.8768(69)$ fm and the $ep$ scattering value of $0.879(5)_{\text{stat}}(4)_{\text{syst}}(2)_{\text{model}}(4)_{\text{group}}$ fm (A1 collaboration). De Rujula (2010) proposed a large third Zemach moment as a solution, which was criticized by Cloet, Walcher, and Ron based on $ep$ scattering data. The authors show that a "lump" in $G_E(Q^2)$ below $Q^2 = 0.004$ GeV$^2$ can produce the required large moment while staying within the Mainz data uncertainties. This enables future global fits using a combined parametrization with a Gaussian term, though the authors note that chiral perturbation theory (Pineda) predicts $\langle r^3_p \rangle_{(2)} \approx 2-3$ fm$^3$, much smaller than their values, and that Carroll (2011) disagrees with their conclusions.
