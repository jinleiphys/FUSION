# Hydrodynamic attractor in the non-conformal Bjorken flow

> Non-conformal attractor behavior is studied by solving non-conformal second order viscous hydrodynamics with respect to boost-invariant plasmas. Numerical solutions of the relative decay rate of the enthalpy density, the inverse shear and bulk Reynolds numbers, all exhibit universal patterns towards an ideal fluid description at late stages, demonstrating the existence of non-conformal hydrodynamic attractor. However, as a generic feature, the non-conformal hydrodynamic attractor emerges only at late times, which differs drastically from the behavior observed in conformal systems. The absence of the early-time attractor in non-conformal fluids is a consequence of the early-time instable mode, which arises from a positive-valued eigen-mode associated with the free-streaming dynamics. To support practical simulations of hydrodynamics in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, early-time attractor can be restored in a mixture of the shear and the bulk viscous corrections, in which early-time instable modes cancel approximately. Early-time non-conformal attractor is also approachable, if the quadratic coupling between the bulk pressure and the expansion rate is enhanced by at least a factor of two through the transport coefficient $δ_{ΠΠ}$.

## Key claim

Non-conformal hydrodynamic attractor exists only at late times, not at early times, which differs drastically from conformal systems; the absence of early-time attractor is a generic feature that prevails as long as the conformal symmetry breaking parameter epsilon is greater than zero.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled ordinary differential equations of non-conformal second-order Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) viscous hydrodynamics for a boost-invariant (Bjorken) plasma, using a linear equation of state with constant speed of sound. They analyzed the evolution of three dimensionless quantities, the relative decay rate of enthalpy density, the inverse shear Reynolds number, and the inverse bulk Reynolds number, as functions of the inverse Knudsen number w, and examined the asymptotic gradient expansion of these quantities to study the attractor behavior.

## Key numbers

- The conformal symmetry breaking parameter epsilon (deviation of speed of sound squared from 1/3) is taken as epsilon = 0.01 for numerical solutions, consistent with lattice QCD calculations showing epsilon ~ 10^{-2} at high energy densities.
- The first-order transport coefficient alpha_Pi (bulk channel) is given by alpha_Pi = 14.55 epsilon^2 + O(z_*^5), where z_* = m/Q_s ~ 10^{-3} is the ratio of quark mass to saturation scale.
- The second-order transport coefficients at leading order in z_* are: gamma_1 = 38/21, gamma_2 = 4/5, gamma_3 = (8/5)epsilon, gamma_4 = 2/3.
- The gradient expansion coefficients show linear growth of consecutive ratios with slope S_g ≈ S_k ≈ S_q ≈ 1 and intercept beta_g ≈ beta_k ≈ beta_q ≈ 0.66.
- The ideal fluid fixed point gives g_0 = -4/3 + epsilon for the relative decay rate of enthalpy density.

## Context

This work extends the conformal hydrodynamic attractor studies of Heller (2015), Romatschke (2017), and Basar (2015) to non-conformal systems, and confirms the recent findings of Chattopadhyay (2021) and Jaiswal (2021) that early-time attractor is absent in non-conformal fluids. The paper proposes two strategies to restore early-time attractor: mixing shear and bulk viscous corrections to cancel instable modes, or enhancing the quadratic coupling between bulk pressure and expansion rate (transport coefficient delta_PiPi) by at least a factor of two. This is relevant for practical hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, where early-time initialization is essential.
