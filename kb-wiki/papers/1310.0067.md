# $N^*$'s and $Δ$'s generated in vector, pseudoscalar meson-baryon systems

> We have recently studied coupled channel interactions of vector and pseudoscalar mesons with octet baryons having total strangeness null and spin-parity $1/2^-$. We, thus, considered $πN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$, $K Σ$, $ρN$, $ωN$, $ϕN$, $K^* Λ$, and $K^*Σ$ with total isospin 1/2 and 3/2. The interactions between pseudoscalar mesons and baryons are obtained by using the Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem. For the case of vector mesons, we calculate $s$-, $t$-, $u$-exchange diagrams and a contact term. The subtraction constants, required to calculate the loop-function in the scattering equations, are constrained by fitting the available experimental data on some of the reactions with pseudoscalar meson-baryon final states. We end up finding resonances which can be related to $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$ (with a double pole structure), $N^*(1895)$ and $Δ(1620)$. We conclude that these resonances can be, at least partly, interpreted as dynamically generated resonances and that the vector mesons play an important role in determining the dynamical origin of the low-lying $N^*$ and $Δ$ states.

## Key claim

The resonances N*(1535), N*(1650) (with a double pole structure), N*(1895), and Δ(1620) can be *at least partly* interpreted as dynamically generated resonances, with vector meson contributions playing an important role; the subtraction constants used differ from those of a natural renormalization scheme, indicating that something is missing in the present formalism (such as quark components or other hadron channels).

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel approach including πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, ρN, ωN, φN, K*Λ, and K*Σ channels with total isospin 1/2 and 3/2. Pseudoscalar meson-baryon interactions were obtained using the Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem, while vector meson-baryon interactions were calculated from s-, t-, and u-exchange diagrams plus a contact term derived from hidden local symmetry. Subtraction constants for the dimensional regularization of loop functions were constrained by fitting experimental data on πN amplitudes and the reactions π⁻p → ηn and π⁻p → K⁰Λ.

## Key numbers

- Pole found at 1504 − i55 MeV, decaying with branching ratio 43% to πN and 55% to ηN, in good agreement with N*(1535) properties.
- Double pole structure for N*(1650): pole positions at 1668 − i28 MeV and 1673 − i67 MeV.
- Two additional isospin 1/2 poles found at 1801 − i96 MeV and 1912 − i54 MeV, grouped under N*(1895).
- A pole in the isospin 3/2 configuration related to Δ(1600).
- Subtraction constants used: πN = −1.955, ηN = −0.777, KΛ = −4.476, KΣ = −1.945, ρN = −0.45, ωN = −0.955, φN = −2.972, K*Λ = −0.184, K*Σ = −1.152.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of vector meson-baryon systems (Khemchandani et al., earlier works) and their inclusion of pseudoscalar mesons in the strangeness −1 sector, where hyperon resonances were dynamically generated. It builds on effective field theories relying on chiral and hidden local symmetries (Bando, earlier work) and contrasts with other studies of N* and Δ resonances (Juelich group, Lutz, Valencia group, and others). The results enable further investigation into the missing components (quark degrees of freedom, additional hadron channels) that would fully account for the dynamical generation of these low-lying N* and Δ states.
