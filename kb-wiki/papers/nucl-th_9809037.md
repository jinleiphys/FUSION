# On isovector meson exchange currents in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We investigate the nonrelativistic reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold energies. To this end, two assumptions have been used in the calculations: 1) the static approximation and 2) the one iteration approximation. Within these assumptions it is possible to recover the nonrelativistic result including a systematic extension to relativistic corrections. We find that the so-called pair current term can be constructed from the $P$-wave contribution of the deuteron Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The form factor that enters into the calculation of the pair current is constrained by the manifestly gauge independent matrix elements.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for deuteron electrodisintegration near threshold, under the static approximation and the one iteration approximation, yields an analytical expression that recovers the nonrelativistic impulse approximation plus a correction term that is analytically identical to the pion pair current contribution, with the pair current term constructed from the P-wave contribution of the deuteron Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the deuteron electrodisintegration reaction near threshold, restricting to the transition to the final \(^1S_0\) state. They employed two approximations: the static approximation (neglecting retardation and Lorentz boost effects) and the one iteration approximation (solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation by a single iteration starting from nonrelativistic wave functions as input). The \(k_0\) integration was performed by closing the contour in the upper half-plane, and a \(k/m\) expansion was applied to extract the leading nonrelativistic terms. The resulting expressions for the transition form factors \(V_{1,2}(s,q^2)\) were evaluated in terms of partial-wave vertex functions for the deuteron (\(^3S_1^{++}\), \(^3D_1^{++}\), \(^3P_1^e\), \(^3P_1^o\)) and the \(np\) pair (\(^1S_0^{++}\), \(^3P_0^e\), \(^3P_0^o\)).

## Key numbers

* The scalar-exchange model used a coupling constant \(g_{NR}=8.37\) for the nonrelativistic case and \(g=17.00\) for the Bethe-Salpeter equation to achieve the same deuteron binding energy of \(\epsilon=2.23\) MeV.
* The difference between the exact solution and the one iteration approximation for the \(^3S_1^{++}\) vertex function did not exceed 5% in the relevant region \(p=0\dots0.5\) GeV and \(p_4=-0.1\dots0.1\) GeV.
* The momentum transfer squared threshold for failure of the nonrelativistic impulse approximation is given as \(-q^2 > 9\) fm\(^{-2}\), where experimental data do not show the deep minimum present in calculations.
* The scalar meson mass used in the model was \(\mu = 0.2\,m\) (where \(m\) is the nucleon mass).

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach to deuteron electrodisintegration, building on prior covariant treatments by Karmanov (1996) and earlier work on static electromagnetic properties by Kaptari, Klevansky, and Beyer (1997). It addresses the long-standing problem of gauge invariance and nucleon form factor choice in meson exchange current calculations, which was highlighted by Ritz, Goller, and Gloeckle (1996) and by Arenhoevel (1991). The result enables a systematic connection between fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter calculations and nonrelativistic meson exchange current models, providing a framework to include relativistic corrections consistently.
