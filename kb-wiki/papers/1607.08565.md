# Charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions within the SuSAv2-MEC approach

> We present a detailed study of charged-current (CC) neutrino-nucleus reactions in a fully relativis- tic framework and comparisons with recent experiments spanning an energy range from hundreds of MeV up to 100 GeV within the SuperScaling Approach, which is based on the analysis of electron- nucleus scattering data and has been recently improved with the inclusion of Relativistic Mean Field theory effects. We also evaluate and discuss the impact of two-particle two-hole meson-exchange currents (2p-2h MEC) on neutrino-nucleus interactions through the analysis of two-particle two-hole axial and vector contributions to weak response functions in a fully relativistic Fermi gas. The results show a fairly good agreement with experimental data over the whole range of neutrino energies.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-MEC model, which combines a superscaling approach with relativistic mean field theory and fully relativistic two-particle two-hole meson-exchange currents (2p-2h MEC), achieves "fairly good agreement" with charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering data from MiniBooNE, MINERvA, T2K, and SciBooNE across an energy range from hundreds of MeV to 100 GeV, but the model tends to overpredict data at the most forward angles (cos theta_mu between 0.9 and 1.0) where quasi-free scattering is questionable.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections using the SuSAv2 model, which incorporates relativistic mean field (RMF) effects at low-to-intermediate momentum transfer and transitions to relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) at high momentum transfer via a q-dependent blending function. They added fully relativistic 2p-2h MEC contributions including vector, axial, and vector-axial interference terms in longitudinal and transverse channels, computed from an exact microscopic calculation of seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta-pole operators. The 2p-2h MEC responses were parametrized for efficient implementation, and the model was extended to the Delta-resonance region by subtracting QE+MEC contributions from experimental electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE neutrino flux mean energy: 788 MeV; antineutrino flux mean energy: 665 MeV
- 2p-2h MEC contributions increase the pure QE response by approximately 25-35% at the maximum, relative to the pure QE response
- The 2p-2h MEC responses attain their largest values at energy transfer omega between 0.3 and 0.8 GeV and momentum transfer q between 0.3 and 1.0 GeV/c (for E_nu = 3 GeV)
- The vector-axial interference contribution to the 2p-2h MEC cross section reaches its maximum around E_nu approximately 1 GeV
- The axial term in the transverse 2p-2h MEC response is larger than the vector term at q less than 800 MeV/c, while the opposite occurs at higher kinematics

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2 approach previously validated against electron-nucleus scattering data (Megias 2016) to neutrino-nucleus reactions, incorporating for the first time fully relativistic axial 2p-2h MEC contributions alongside vector contributions. The model addresses discrepancies between MiniBooNE CCQE data and impulse approximation predictions that motivated earlier studies by Martini (2009), Amaro (2010), Nieves (2011), and Lalakulich (2012), who showed that multinucleon excitations could explain the data without tuning the axial mass parameter. The fully relativistic treatment of all response channels (vector-vector, axial-axial, and vector-axial interference) distinguishes this work from previous non-relativistic approaches that assumed axial-axial contributions equal vector-vector ones and the interference T' response proportional to the transverse vector-vector response. The results enable implementation of the model in Monte Carlo generators for current neutrino oscillation experiments.
