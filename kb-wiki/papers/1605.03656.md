# Effects of the high-momentum tail of nucleon momentum distribution on the isospin-sensitive observables

> Based on the Isospin-dependent transport model Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU), effects of the high momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distribution in colliding nuclei on some isospin-sensitive observables are studied in the semi-central $^{197}\rm {Au}+^{197}\rm {Au}$ reactions at incident beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the nucleon transverse flow, the difference of neutron and proton transverse flows, the nucleon elliptic flow and free neutron to proton ratio are all less sensitive to the HMT, while the isospin-sensitive nucleon elliptic flow difference is clearly affected by the HMT. Except at very high kinetic energies, the kinetic energy distributions of $π^{-}$, $π^{+}$ and charged pion ratio $π^{-}/π^{+}$ are all sensitive to the HMT.

# Effects of the high-momentum tail of nucleon momentum distribution on the isospin-sensitive observables

## Key claim
In semi-central 197Au+197Au reactions at 400 MeV/nucleon, the high-momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distribution in colliding nuclei has little effect on nucleon transverse flow, the difference of neutron and proton transverse flows, nucleon elliptic flow, and the free neutron-to-proton ratio; however, the isospin-sensitive nucleon elliptic flow difference and the kinetic energy distributions of pions (except at very high kinetic energies) are clearly affected by the HMT.

## Method
The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with an isospin- and momentum-dependent mean-field single nucleon potential. The HMT was incorporated into the initial nucleon momentum distribution of 197Au nuclei using the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the AV18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force, and the effect of the HMT on the single-particle potential was also included consistently. Simulations were performed for semi-central 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 7.2 fm.

## Key numbers
- About 20% of nucleons form strongly correlated nucleon pairs with large relative momentum and small center-of-mass momentum relative to the Fermi momentum.
- The number of neutron-proton pairs in the HMT is about 18 times that of proton-proton or neutron-neutron pairs.
- The HMT roughly exhibits a C/k^4 form for momenta above the Fermi momentum.
- The impact parameter used was b = 7.2 fm, selected to match FOPI-LAND data for Au+Au at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- Free nucleons were identified when their local densities were less than ρ0/8.

## Context
This work extends prior studies that incorporated HMT effects into transport models by modifying the symmetry potential in the nuclear mean-field potential (Hen et al., 2015; Baliga et al., 2015). It addresses the fact that most isospin-dependent transport models (Danielewicz, 2007; Lynch, 2009; Wolter, 2009; Xiao, 2009; Li, 2013) rarely included HMT effects. The findings are relevant for interpreting observables used to probe the nuclear symmetry energy, such as the free neutron/proton ratio (Li et al., 1997), the π−/π+ ratio (Yong, 2006), and nucleon transverse flow (Yong, 2006). The results suggest that the difference of neutron and proton transverse flows and the π−/π+ ratio at higher kinetic energies are recommendable as HMT-insensitive probes of the symmetry energy.
