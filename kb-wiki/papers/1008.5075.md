# Hagedorn States and Thermalization

> In recent years Hagedorn states have been used to explain the physics close to the critical temperature within a hadron gas. Because of their large decay widths these massive resonances lower $η/s$ to near the AdS/CFT limit within the hadron gas phase. A comparison of the Hagedorn model to recent lattice results is made and it is found that for both Tc =176 MeV and Tc=196 MeV, the hadrons can reach chemical equilibrium almost immediately, well before the chemical freeze-out temperatures found in thermal fits for a hadron gas without Hagedorn states. In this paper we also observe the effects of Hagedorn States on the $K^+/π^+$ horn seen at AGS, SPS, and RHIC.

## Key claim

Hagedorn states can drive hadrons into chemical equilibrium almost immediately after the phase transition, within approximately 1-3 fm/c, but only for critical temperatures near 176 or 196 MeV; the effect is negligible for the K+/π+ horn because the relevant chemical freeze-out temperatures (124-160 MeV) lie well below the Hagedorn temperature.

## Method

The authors solve coupled rate equations for pions, Hagedorn states, and X-Xbar pairs (where X includes protons, kaons, Lambdas, and Omegas) under a Bjorken-expanding fireball with constant total entropy. They use a Hagedorn mass spectrum with an exponential form, volume corrections from repulsive interactions, and decay widths extrapolated from PDG data. The system is evolved from initial temperatures Tc=176 MeV or Tc=196 MeV down to T=110 MeV, with four different initial fugacity configurations for pions, Hagedorn states, and X-Xbar pairs.

## Key numbers

- Chemical equilibration times: Δτ ≈ 1-3 fm/c
- Two critical temperatures considered: Tc = 176 MeV and Tc = 196 MeV
- Hagedorn mass spectrum parameters: M0 = 2 GeV, mr² = 0.5 GeV, maximum mass M = 12 GeV
- For Tc = 196 MeV: A = 0.5 GeV^(3/2), B = (340 MeV)^4
- For Tc = 176 MeV: A = 0.1 GeV^(3/2), B = (300 MeV)^4
- Decay widths range from 250 MeV to 1800 MeV, following Γ_i = 0.15 m_i - 0.0584
- Average number of pions from Hagedorn decay: ⟨n_i⟩ ≈ 3 to 34; with X-Xbar pair: ⟨n_i,x⟩ ≈ 2 to 7
- Initial radius r0 = 7.1 fm at t0 ≈ 2 fm/c for Tc = 196 MeV; t0 ≈ 4 fm/c for Tc = 176 MeV
- Total pion number at mid-rapidity: ∑N_π = 874
- Entropy per pion: S_π/N_π ≈ 5.5
- Expansion parameters: v0 = 0.5, a0 = 0.025

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Greiner (2004) and Noronha-Hostler (2007, 2009) that proposed Hagedorn states as catalysts for rapid chemical equilibration in hadron gas, and by Noronha-Hostler (2008) showing Hagedorn states lower η/s near the AdS/CFT bound. It contradicts the assumption that hadrons must be born in chemical equilibrium from QGP, as argued by Stock (1999). The results enable implementation of Hagedorn states into transport models such as UrQMD, and motivate further study of lower critical temperatures suggested by recent lattice calculations (Borsanyi 2010).
