# Nuclear EMC effect through $\barΛ/Λ$ production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering processes

> We calculate $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hadron production cross sections in charged lepton semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off nuclear target ($A$, using iron $\mathrm{Fe}$ as an example) and deuteron ($D$) target. The results show that the ratio $(\barΛ^{A}/Λ^{A})/(\barΛ^{D}/Λ^{D})$ is sensitive to the sea quark content of the nucleus. We adopt three different models to take the nuclear EMC effect into account. The ratio $(\barΛ^{A}/Λ^{A})/(\barΛ^{D}/Λ^{D})$ is predicted to be different by these different models.

## Key claim

The ratio \((\bar{\Lambda}^{A}/\Lambda^{A})/(\bar{\Lambda}^{D}/\Lambda^{D})\) in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off iron versus deuterium is predicted to be sensitive to the sea quark content of the nucleus, but the three models (pion excess, quark-cluster, and rescaling) are not fully resolved by available data; the ratio can discriminate them only "to some extent" because the error bands of the quark-cluster model are wide.

## Method

The authors calculated \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) production cross sections in charged-lepton semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off iron (Fe) and deuterium (D) targets at \(Q^{2}=5~\mathrm{GeV^{2}}\). They adopted three nuclear EMC-effect models (pion excess, quark-cluster, and rescaling), fitting each model's free parameter to combined EMC and BCDMS experimental data on \(F_{2}^{A}/F_{2}^{D}\) using MINUIT. Fragmentation functions for \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) were obtained via the Gribov-Lipatov relation and the AKK parametrization, with nuclear modifications described by the Arleo energy-loss model.

## Key numbers

* Fitted pion excess model parameter \(\langle n_{\pi}\rangle = 0.166 \pm 0.095\) for iron, from combined EMC and BCDMS data.
* Fitted quark-cluster model parameter \(f = 0.2524 \pm 0.2079\) for iron, from combined EMC and BCDMS data.
* Fitted rescaling model parameter \(\xi_{A} = 1.5475^{+0.959}_{-0.529}\) for iron, from combined EMC and BCDMS data.
* Energy-loss model parameters: gluon transport coefficient \(\hat{q}=0.72~\mathrm{GeV/fm^{2}}\), quark energy \(\nu=12~\mathrm{GeV}\), and nuclear radius \(R\cong1.17A^{1/3}~\mathrm{fm}\) for iron.
* All calculations performed at \(Q^{2}=5~\mathrm{GeV^{2}}\) in the region \(0.1<x<0.7\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Lu and Ma (2006) that first proposed the \((\bar{\Lambda}^{A}/\Lambda^{A})/(\bar{\Lambda}^{D}/\Lambda^{D})\) ratio as a probe of nuclear sea quarks, but improves the numerical analysis using updated \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) fragmentation functions from Du et al. (2017) and more careful fitting of the three EMC models (pion excess model from Llewellyn Smith (1983) and Ericson and Thomas (1983); quark-cluster model from Carlson and Havens (1983); rescaling model from Close, Roberts, and Ross (1983)). The results suggest that future SIDIS measurements of \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) production could help discriminate among these competing pictures of the nuclear EMC effect.
