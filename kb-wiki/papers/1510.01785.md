# Ground state properties of even-even and odd Nd,Ce and Sm isotopes in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method

> In this work, we have studied ground-state properties of both even-even and odd Nd isotopes within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with SLy5 Skyrme force in which the pairing strength has been generalized with a new proposed formula. We calculated bind- ing energies, two-neutron separation energies, quadrupole deformation, charge, neutron and proton radii. Similar calculations have been carried out for Ce and Sm in order to verify the validity of our pairing strength formula. The results have been compared with available experimental data, the results of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations based on the D1S Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and predictions of some nuclear models such as Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new generalized formula for the pairing strength, V0^(n,p) = 170.95 * A^(1/6) (in MeV), derived by fitting to the pairing strengths that reproduce experimental ground-state energies of Nd isotopes, and claim that this formula improves the agreement with experimental binding energies, separation energies, radii, and deformations for Nd, Ce, and Sm isotopes compared to direct HFB calculations without the formula.

## Method

The authors performed axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SLy5 Skyrme functional and a mixed surface-volume zero-range pairing interaction, implemented in the HFBTHO (v2.00d) code with a transformed harmonic oscillator basis of 16 shells and 500 states. For each Nd isotope from A=124 to 161, the pairing strength V0^(n,p) (identical for neutrons and protons) was tuned to reproduce the experimental ground-state energy, and the resulting values were fitted to a function of mass number A to obtain the proposed formula. The formula was then tested by calculating ground-state properties for the same Nd isotopes and for Ce (A=119-157) and Sm (A=128-165) isotopes, with results compared to experimental data, HFB calculations using the D1S Gogny force, the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the NL3 functional.

## Key numbers

* The proposed pairing strength formula is V0^(n,p) = 170.95 * A^(1/6) MeV, with a mean deviation of about 5.05 MeV between the fitted and exact values.
* For Nd isotopes, the maximal error in binding energy per nucleon is about 0.019 MeV per particle, corresponding to approximately 2.697 MeV for the total binding energy.
* For Ce isotopes, the approximately maximal error per particle is 0.018 MeV; for Sm isotopes, it is 0.020 MeV.
* For the A=140 isobar chain, the mean absolute error between experimental total binding energies and the present work is 1.436 MeV, compared to 0.347 MeV for RMF, 1.076 MeV for FRDM, 3.123 MeV for HFB with D1S Gogny, and 7.392 MeV for direct HFB calculations.
* The maximal errors in two-neutron separation energy (S2n) for Nd, Ce, and Sm are 1.4782 MeV, 1.29951 MeV, and 1.44676 MeV, respectively.
* The difference between neutron and proton rms radii reaches 0.242 fm for 161Nd, 0.257 fm for 157Ce, and 0.228 fm for 165Sm.
* The quadrupole deformation beta2 saturates near 0.29 for Nd, 0.30 for Sm, and 0.26 for Ce isotopes above N=82.

## Context

This work extends previous HFB studies of Nd isotopes using the HFBTHO code (Stoitsov, 2005) and the D1S Gogny force (Amedee, 2010) by introducing a new mass-dependent pairing strength formula for the SLy5 Skyrme interaction. The results are compared with the Finite Range Droplet Model (Moller, 1997) and Relativistic Mean Field theory with NL3 functional (Lalazissis, 1997). The proposed formula enables systematic calculations of ground-state properties across isotopic chains without case-by-case tuning of the pairing strength, and the authors suggest it could be applied to study shape coexistence and mixing phenomena using beyond-mean-field methods such as the Generator Coordinate Method or the Bohr Hamiltonian.
