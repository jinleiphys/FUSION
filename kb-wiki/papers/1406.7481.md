# Predicting exotic molecular states composed of nucleon and P-wave charmed meson

> In this work, we study the interaction between a nucleon and a $P$-wave charmed meson in the $T$ doublet by exchanging a pion. Our calculations indicate that a nucleon and a $P$-wave charmed meson with $J^P=0^+$ or $J^P=1^+$ in the $T$ doublet can form bound states. We propose the experimental search for these exotic molecular states near the $D_1(2420)N$ and $D_2^*(2460)N$ thresholds, where Belle, LHCb and the forthcoming Belle II have the discovery potential for them.

## Key claim

The authors predict that a nucleon and a P-wave charmed meson in the T doublet (with J^P = 0^+ or 1^+) can form bound molecular states via one-pion exchange, but the existence of these states depends on the sign of the product g_{πNN} k and on the cutoff parameter Λ being near 1 GeV, with only specific isospin and total angular momentum combinations yielding loosely bound candidates.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians for the interaction of charmed mesons in the T doublet (D_1 with J^P=1^+ and D_2^* with J^P=2^+) with nucleons via one-pion exchange, using the heavy quark limit and chiral symmetry. They derived the OPE effective potentials in momentum space via the Breit approximation, then performed Fourier transformation to obtain coordinate-space potentials with a monopole form factor (cutoff Λ around 1 GeV). The coupled-channel Schrodinger equation was solved numerically using the FESSDE program to find bound state solutions for D_1N and D_2^*N systems with various isospin (I=0,1) and total angular momentum (J=1/2, 3/2, 5/2) quantum numbers, considering both pure channels and coupled D_1N/D_2^*N channels.

## Key numbers

- For the isoscalar D_1N state with J=1/2 (Case I, Λ=1.00 GeV): binding energy E = -7.15 MeV, root-mean-square radius r_RMS = 1.60 fm
- For the isoscalar D_2^*N state with J=3/2 (Case I, Λ=1.00 GeV): binding energy E = -4.17 MeV, root-mean-square radius r_RMS = 2.06 fm
- For the isoscalar D_1N/D_2^*N coupled system with J=1/2 (Case I, Λ=0.99 GeV): binding energy E = -8.17 MeV, r_RMS = 1.54 fm, with 98.58% probability in the D_1N channel
- For the isoscalar D_1N/D_2^*N coupled system with J=3/2 (Case I, Λ=1.305 GeV): binding energy E = -23.23 MeV, r_RMS = 0.63 fm, with 97.14% probability in the D_2^*N channel
- The coupling constant k was taken as 0.59, and g^2_{πNN}/(4π) = 13.6
- The pion decay constant f_π = 132 MeV
- D_1 masses: 2423.4 MeV (charged), 2421.3 MeV (neutral); D_2^* masses: 2464.4 MeV (charged), 2462.6 MeV (neutral)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon-charmed meson molecular states, which had already examined S-wave charmed mesons (H doublet: D, D^*) and S-wave scalar charmed mesons (S doublet: D_0^*) to explain observed states like Λ_c(2940), Σ_c(2800), and X(3250). The authors specifically target the T doublet (P-wave charmed mesons D_1 and D_2^*), which had not been systematically studied for molecular state formation with nucleons. The predictions provide specific thresholds (D_1(2420)N and D_2^*(2460)N) and decay channels (such as DN, D^*N, Σ_cπ, and three-body modes like Σ_cππ and Λ_cππ) for experimental searches at Belle, LHCb, and the forthcoming Belle II.
