# Correlations and charge distributions of medium heavy nuclei

> The effects of long- and short-range correlations on the charge distributions of some medium and heavy nuclei are investigated. The long-range correlations are treated within the Random Phase Approximation framework and the short-range correlations with a model inspired to the Correlation Basis Function theory. The two type of correlations produce effects of the same order of magnitude. A comparison with the empirical charge distribution difference between 206Pb and 205Tl shows the need of including both correlations to obtain a good description of the data.

## Key claim

Long-range correlations (RPA) and short-range correlations (CBF-based model) produce effects of the same order of magnitude on charge distributions of medium-heavy nuclei, and both must be included simultaneously to reproduce the empirical charge density difference between 206Pb and 205Tl, contrary to the implication that either type alone might suffice.

## Method

The authors calculated charge density distributions for 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb using a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis. Long-range correlations (LRC) were treated within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) framework using two different effective interactions (Landau-Migdal and Jülich-Stony Brook), while short-range correlations (SRC) were evaluated using a first-order cluster expansion model inspired by Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory, with correlation functions taken from Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) calculations using realistic interactions. The charge distribution difference between 206Pb and 205Tl was computed by combining LRC effects treated within the open-shell structure and SRC effects approximated from the closed-shell 208Pb-207Tl system.

## Key numbers

- RMS charge radii (in fm) for 16O: IPM = 2.64, SRC = 2.70, LRC = 2.98
- RMS charge radii (in fm) for 40Ca: IPM = 3.25, SRC = 3.31, LRC = 3.57
- RMS charge radii (in fm) for 208Pb: IPM = 5.46, SRC = 5.52, LRC = 5.59
- Occupation probability of the 3s1/2 state in 208Pb: LRC = 0.80, SRC = 0.92 (compared to 0.6 from (e,e'p) data of Qui (1986) and 0.7 from a later DWBA re-analysis by Udias (1993))
- B(E3;0→3) for 208Pb: 7.536 × 10^5 e²fm⁶ with LM interaction, 7.489 × 10^5 e²fm⁶ with JSB interaction (experimental value from Bohr (1975))
- B(E3;0→3) for 16O: 0.97 × 10² e²fm⁶ (LM), 1.17 × 10³ e²fm⁶ (JSB), experimental 1.5 × 10³ e²fm⁶ (Bohr 1975)
- B(E3;0→3) for 40Ca: 1.1762 × 10⁴ e²fm⁶ (LM), 1.22 × 10⁴ e²fm⁶ (JSB), experimental 1.70 × 10⁴ e²fm⁶ (Bohr 1975)
- Gaussian correlation parameters: A = 0.7, B = 2.2 fm⁻²

## Context

This work extends earlier FHNC calculations by Fabrocini (2000, 2001) and Mok (2000) that showed a lack of some correlation effects (likely LRC) in doubly magic nuclei. It also builds on RPA studies of occupation numbers by Len (1990) and Gogny (1979), and on the Landau-Migdal formalism for charge distributions developed by Co (1986, 1987). The key advance is demonstrating that both LRC and SRC contribute comparably and must be combined to describe the empirical 206Pb-205Tl charge difference measured by Cavedon (1982) and Frois (1983), enabling future calculations that treat both correlation types on equal footing within a consistent many-body framework.
