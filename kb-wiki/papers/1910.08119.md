# Baryon preclustering at the freeze-out of heavy-ion collisions and light-nuclei production

> Following the idea of nucleon clustering and light-nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions close to the QCD critical-end point, we address the quantum effects affecting the interaction of several nucleons at finite temperature. For this aim we use the $K$-harmonics method to four-nucleon states ($α$ particle), and also develop a novel semiclassical "flucton" method at finite temperature, based on certain classical paths in Euclidean time, and apply it to two- and four-particle configurations. To study possible effects on the light-nuclei production close to the QCD critical point, we also made such calculations with modified internuclear potentials. For heavy-ion experiments, we propose new measurements of light-nuclei multiplicity ratios which may show enhancements due to baryon preclustering. We point out the special role of the $\mathcal{O}(50)$ four-nucleon excitations of $α$-particle, feeding into the final multiplicities of $d,t$, $^3$He and $^4$He, and propose to directly look for their two-body decays.

## Key claim

The paper argues that enhanced light-nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions near the QCD critical point can arise from baryon preclustering driven by modified internucleon potentials, but only if quantum corrections are included via a novel semiclassical "flucton" method; the abstract's claim of observable enhancements is conditional on the existence of the QCD critical point and on the in-medium reduction of the sigma-meson mass from 500 MeV to about 285 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the K-harmonics method to solve the four-nucleon Schrodinger equation for the alpha particle, obtaining 40 lowest eigenstates including a previously missed second bound state. They also developed a semiclassical "flucton" method at finite temperature based on periodic classical paths in Euclidean (Matsubara) time, applying it to two-, three-, and four-nucleon systems in a tetrahedral configuration. Both methods were applied with the Serot-Walecka potential using the vacuum sigma mass (500 MeV) and a reduced sigma mass (285 MeV) to simulate conditions near the QCD critical point.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of the four-nucleon system (alpha particle) from K-harmonics: E0 = -27.8 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -28.3 MeV
- Second bound state (0+) of the four-nucleon system: E1 = -2.8 MeV, corresponding to a binding energy of -8.1 MeV relative to the experimental ground state
- For the modified potential with doubled attractive coefficient, six radial bound states were found with energies: -226.1, -120.1, -52.6, -17.3, -3.4, -0.1 MeV
- Correlation volume for two nucleons at T=100 MeV: veff = 5.3 fm^3 for the unmodified potential (m_sigma=500 MeV) and veff = 151 fm^3 for the modified potential (m_sigma=285 MeV)
- Ratio of potential depth to temperature for the unmodified potential: |V(r_min)|/T ~ 1/3 to 1/2; for the modified potential: |V(r_min)|/T ~ 2-3
- The density matrix magnitude for four nucleons at T=100 MeV increases from ~0.4 to ~12 when switching from unmodified to modified potential

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous classical Molecular Dynamics study (Shuryak 2018) by adding quantum corrections to baryon preclustering, which were previously neglected. It addresses the same experimental observations from the STAR collaboration at RHIC, the large scaled kurtosis of net-proton distributions and the apparent increase of tritium production relative to deuterium at sqrt(s_NN)=20-30 GeV, that motivated the earlier work. The paper enables new experimental measurements of light-nuclei multiplicity ratios (such as t p/d^2) as potential signals for the QCD critical point, and proposes searching for two-body decays of the approximately 50 four-nucleon excited states of the alpha particle that feed into final deuteron, triton, helium-3, and helium-4 multiplicities.
