# High Density Quark Matter under Stress

> We study the effect of SU(3) flavor breaking on high density quark matter. We discuss, in particular, the effect a non-zero electron chemical potential and a finite strange quark mass. We argue that these perturbations trigger pion or kaon condensation. The critical chemical potential behaves as $μ_e\sim\sqrt{m m_s} Δ/p_F$ and the critical strange quark mass as $m_s \sim m^{1/3} Δ^{2/3}$, where $m$ is the light quark mass, $Δ$ is the gap, and $p_F$ is the Fermi momentum. We note that parametrically, both the critical $μ_e$ and $m_s^2/(2p_F)$ are much smaller than the gap.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter, perturbations from a non-zero electron chemical potential or a finite strange quark mass trigger pion or kaon condensation rather than destroying superfluidity, provided the perturbations remain below the unlocking transition. The critical values for condensation are parametrically much smaller than the superconducting gap.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for the pseudo-Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase, matching it to perturbative QCD at high density. They compute the masses of pions and kaons from the effective Lagrangian including both O(M^2) and O(M^4) quark mass terms, then determine the conditions under which these mesons become massless and condense. They also analyze the response using linear response theory and a thermodynamic potential with charge neutrality constraints.

## Key numbers

- Critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation: m_s ~ m^{1/3} Δ^{2/3}, where m is the light quark mass and Δ is the gap.
- Critical electron chemical potential for meson condensation: μ_e ~ sqrt(m m_s) Δ / p_F.
- For p_F = 500 MeV with Δ and f_π calculated to leading order in perturbation theory, the K^0 becomes massless for m_s ≈ 60 MeV; varying the renormalization scale between p_F/2 and 2p_F gives critical strange quark masses between 39 MeV and 67 MeV.
- The pion decay constant in the CFL phase: f_π^2 = (21 - 8 log 2)/18 × μ^2/(2π^2).
- The hypercharge density in the kaon condensed phase satisfies n_Y < Δ p_F^2/(2π), equal to the particle density within a strip of width Δ around the Fermi surface.
- In normal quark matter with charge neutrality, the electron chemical potential is μ_e ≈ m_s^2/(4p_F).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of CFL quark matter by Alford et al. (1999) and Schafer (1999) that first examined the effects of a non-zero strange quark mass and predicted an unlocking transition to 2+1SC matter at m_s ~ sqrt(p_F Δ). The present paper analyzes the regime below that unlocking transition and shows that the system responds by forming kaon or pion condensates, analogous to chiral rotation of the CFL order parameter. This enables more realistic modeling of neutron star cores, where charge neutrality and finite strange quark mass are unavoidable, and connects to the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferell (LOFF) phase studied by Alford et al. (2001) in the vicinity of the unlocking transition.
