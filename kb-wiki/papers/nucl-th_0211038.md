# Neutron star structure in a quark model with excluded volume correction

> We study the effects of the finite size of baryons on the equation of state of homogeneous hadronic matter. The finite extension of hadrons is introduced in order to improve the performance of field theoretical models at very high densities. We simulate the in-medium averaged baryon-baryon strong repulsion at very short distances by introducing a Van der Waals like normalization of the fields. This is done in the framework of the Quark Meson Coupling model, that allows to take care of the quark structure of baryons. Since within this model the confinement volume evolves with the fields configuration, the treatment is not equivalent to a simple hard-core potential. We investigate the phase transition to quark matter and the structure of neutron stars. We have found significant corrections at high densities.

## Key claim

The finite-size (excluded volume) correction to baryons in the Quark Meson Coupling model significantly modifies the equation of state at high densities, enabling a phase transition to quark matter before bag overlap occurs, whereas without this correction the non-overlapping bag assumption breaks down before pure quark matter can be reached.

## Method

The authors introduce a Van der Waals-like excluded volume correction by renormalizing the baryon field normalization with an available volume factor, replacing the total volume V by V' = V - Σ_b N^b v_b, where v_b = 4√2 R_b^3 is the effective volume per baryon. This is implemented within the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, where baryons are MIT bags containing three valence quarks that interact via σ, ω, and ρ meson exchange in mean-field approximation, and the bag radius evolves dynamically with density. The resulting equation of state is used to compute neutron star structure via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, including a mixed phase of hadronic and deconfined quark matter with global charge and baryon number conservation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation: E_b = -16 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: a_s = 35 MeV
- Bag constant values used: B^{1/4} = 169.93, 187.83, and 210.85 MeV, corresponding to proton bag radii R_p = 0.8, 0.7, and 0.6 fm respectively
- Maximum neutron star masses: 1.51 ≤ M_max/M_⊙ ≤ 1.88 (above the experimental lower limit of 1.44 M_⊙ for binary pulsars)
- Maximum star radii: 12 km ≤ R ≤ 13 km, slightly larger when excluded volume corrections are included
- In the excluded volume case, the hadronic contribution n_H lies always below 5.6 n_0
- Energy density in the mixed phase ranges from 0.3 to 1.1 GeV fm^{-3} for case (b)-CC and from 0.6 to 2.0 GeV fm^{-3} for case (c)-CC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Aguirre and De Paoli, 2002) of excluded volume corrections in nuclear matter with lambda hyperons to include the full baryon octet and neutron star structure. It contrasts with the standard QMC approach (Guichon, 1989; Saito and Thomas, 1994) where the bag equilibrium condition is ∂M_b*/∂R_b = 0, and with the approach of Pal et al. (1999) where the bag constant depends on the scalar meson field and bag radii increase with density leading to early overlap. The excluded volume correction enables a consistent description of the hadron-to-quark matter phase transition at densities where the non-overlapping bag assumption would otherwise fail, and produces neutron star masses compatible with the observational lower bound from binary pulsars.
