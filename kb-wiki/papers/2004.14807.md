# Spin versus Helicity Equilibration Times and Lagrangian for Strange Quarks in Rotating Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Measurements of the net polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have stimulated much interest in how strange quarks might align their spin with the vorticity of the matter created in heavy ion collisions. We calculate the Lagrangian in the rest frame of a fluid element undergoing rotation with angular velocity $ω$ including photon and gluon fields. There is an additional coupling between the quarks and the gauge fields proportional to $ω$, but this vertex does not change the spin of the quarks. We also show that the times to equilibrate quark helicity and spin parallel to the vorticity are the same so long as $ω$ is small compared to the temperature.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the equilibration times for strange quark helicity and for spin parallel to the vorticity are equal, provided the vorticity is small compared to the temperature (which holds in heavy ion collisions). There is no additional quark-gauge field coupling that would flip the spin or helicity beyond the mechanisms already considered in prior work.

## Method
The authors use a tetrad formalism to derive the Dirac Lagrangian in the local rest frame of a rotating fluid element, including electromagnetic and SU(N) gauge fields. They compute the single-particle Hamiltonian from the Dirac equation and examine the spin-rotation coupling term. They then compare helicity eigenstates to spin eigenstates quantized along the vorticity direction using both two-component Pauli spinors and four-component Dirac spinors, and analyze the relaxation-time approximation to the Boltzmann equation to relate the equilibration times.

## Key numbers
- The vorticity extracted from STAR data is ω = (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s^{-1}, corresponding to ω = 6 ± 1 MeV, with a systematic error of a factor of two.
- The temperature in heavy ion collisions is T ≥ 150 MeV, so ω/T is at most about 0.04.
- The correction to the equilibration time from vorticity effects on the equilibrium distribution functions is at most 0.02% and is negligible.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kapusta, Rrapaj, Rudaz, 2018, 2019) on strange quark spin equilibration mechanisms in rotating quark-gluon plasma, where they found that perturbative QCD scattering and vorticity fluctuations gave equilibration times too long for heavy ion collisions, while the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with axial symmetry breaking could equilibrate constituent strange quarks below about 170 MeV. The present paper rules out additional spin-flipping couplings in the fundamental Lagrangian and confirms that helicity and spin equilibration times are equivalent under the relevant conditions. It enables future work to focus on the previously identified mechanisms without concern for new vertices or time-scale differences.
