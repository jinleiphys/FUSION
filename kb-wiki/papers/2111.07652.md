# Comparison of heavy ion transport simulations: Ag+Ag collisions at Elab = 1.58 AGeV

> We compare the microscopic transport models UrQMD, PHSD, PHQMD, and SMASH to make predictions for the upcoming Ag+Ag data at $E_\mathrm{lab}=1.58$~$A$GeV ($\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.55$~GeV) by the HADES collaboration. We study multiplicities, spectra and effective source temperatures of protons, $π^{\pm,0}$, $K^\pm$, the $η$, $Λ+Σ^0$ and the $Ξ^-$ within these models. Despite variations in the detailed implementation of the dynamics in the different models, the employed transport approaches all show consistent multiplicities of the bulk of investigated hadrons. The main differences are in the $Ξ^-$ production, which is treated differently between UrQMD/SMASH on one side employing high mass resonance states with explicit decays to $\mathrm{Resonance}\rightarrow Ξ+K+K$ in contrast to PHSD/PHQMD which account only non-resonant $Ξ$ production channels. A comparison of the spectra, summarized by effective source temperatures, shows that all models provide similar source temperatures around $T_\mathrm{source}=80-95$~MeV, and show substantial radial flow on the order of $\langle v_T\rangle=0.22c-0.3c$ even for such a small system.

## Key claim

Despite variations in the detailed implementation of dynamics across the four transport models, all approaches show consistent multiplicities for the bulk of investigated hadrons in Ag+Ag collisions at 1.58 AGeV, with the main difference being a factor of 10-15 higher yields for the double-strange Xi- hyperon in UrQMD and SMASH compared to PHSD and PHQMD due to the inclusion of heavy resonance decays in the former two models.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic transport simulations of Ag+Ag collisions at Elab = 1.58 AGeV (sqrt(sNN) = 2.55 GeV) using four models: UrQMD v3.5, PHSD v4.5, PHQMD, and SMASH, all at 0-10% centrality determined by an impact parameter cut of bmax = 3.6 fm from the hard sphere approximation. They compared integrated 4pi multiplicities, rapidity distributions, transverse mass spectra, and extracted effective source temperatures by fitting the inverse slope parameters of transverse mass distributions to a thermal source relation T_inv(y) = T_eff / cosh(y). The effective temperatures were then plotted as a function of hadron mass to extract the true source temperature and average transverse flow velocity via a linear fit of the form T_eff = T_source + (1/3) m <v_T>^2.

## Key numbers

* Integrated hadron multiplicities for protons, pions, and eta mesons are consistent across all four models for 0-10% central Ag+Ag collisions at Elab = 1.58 AGeV
* Xi- yields in UrQMD and SMASH are a factor of approximately 10-15 higher than in PHSD and PHQMD simulations
* Low transverse mass pions (m_T - m_0 < 300 MeV) show inverse slope parameters T(y_cm) = 60 +/- 10 MeV across all models
* High transverse mass pions (300 < m_T - m_0 < 800 MeV) show inverse slope parameters T(y_cm) = 80 +/- 10 MeV across all models
* Effective source temperatures T_source extracted from the zero-flow limit range from 80-95 MeV across all models
* Average transverse flow velocities range from <v_T> = 0.22c to 0.3c for the Ag+Ag system
* UrQMD with hard EoS yields effective temperatures T_eff of 97 MeV for high-m_T pions, 99 MeV for K+, 105 MeV for K-, 116 MeV for Lambda+Sigma0, and 122 MeV for protons
* PHSD with middle EoS yields effective temperatures T_eff of 86 MeV for high-m_T pions, 106 MeV for K+, 90 MeV for K-, 94 MeV for Lambda+Sigma0, and 106 MeV for protons

## Context

This work provides predictions for upcoming HADES collaboration data on Ag+Ag collisions at 1.58 AGeV, extending previous studies of heavy-ion transport model comparisons at SIS energies. The finding that all models yield similar source temperatures around 80-95 MeV is consistent with earlier studies at HADES energies by FOPI (1997) and Reichert (2020) for similar system sizes. The observed radial flow velocity of 0.22c-0.3c for Ag+Ag is smaller than the value of 0.4c extracted for Au+Au reactions by Harabasz (2020), as expected from the smaller system size. The smaller effective temperatures of Lambda and Xi baryons compared to the flow fit, observed consistently across UrQMD, PHSD, and PHQMD, was previously found by vanHecke (1998) and attributed to early decoupling of multi-strange baryons from the fireball, also confirmed by WA97 (1998).
