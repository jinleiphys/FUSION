# Decuplet to octet baryon transitions in chiral perturbation theory

> We have systematically investigated the decuplet (T) to octet (B) baryon ($T\rightarrow Bγ$) transition magnetic moments to the next-to-next-to-leading order and electric quadruple moments to the next-to-leading order in the framework of the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Our calculation includes the contributions from both the intermediate decuplet and octet baryon states in the loops. Our results show reasonably good convergence of the chiral expansion and agreement with the experimental data. The analytical expressions may be useful to the chiral extrapolation of the lattice simulations of the decuplet electromagnetic properties.

## Key claim

The authors systematically compute the decuplet-to-octet baryon transition magnetic moments to next-to-next-to-leading order (O(p³)) and electric quadrupole moments to next-to-leading order (O(p²)) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding "reasonably good convergence of the chiral expansion and agreement with the experimental data", though the agreement is qualitative rather than precise for all channels, and several low-energy constants must be fitted.

## Method

The calculation uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with dimensional regularization and the modified minimal subtraction scheme. Both intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states are included in the one-loop diagrams, and the chiral order is counted using the standard power-counting formula Dχ = 4NL − 2IM − IB + Σ nNn. The tree-level contributions come from O(p²) and O(p⁴) electromagnetic Lagrangians, while the loop diagrams (sixteen in total) are evaluated at O(p³) and O(p⁴). The second-order strong-interaction Lagrangian for the φφTN vertex introduces two independent low-energy constants, g̃t1 and g̃t2, after eliminating redundant terms.

## Key numbers

* The octet-decuplet mass splitting used is δ = 0.2937 GeV (isospin symmetry limit).
* The φNT coupling constant is C = −1.2 ± 0.1; the φTT coupling constant is H = −2.2 ± 0.6 (Butler 1992).
* Pseudoscalar meson masses used: mπ = 0.140 GeV, mK = 0.494 GeV, mη = 0.550 GeV.
* Averaged octet baryon mass MB = 1.158 GeV; averaged decuplet baryon mass MT = 1.452 GeV.
* The pion decay constant fπ ≈ 92.4 MeV; fK ≈ 113 MeV; fη ≈ 116 MeV.
* The octet magnetic moment LECs from earlier work (Meissner 1997) are bD = 3.9 and bF = 3.0.

## Context

This work extends earlier HBChPT calculations of T→Bγ transitions by Butler, Savage, and Springer (1993) at next-to-leading order, and the small-scale expansion (SSE) calculation by Gellas et al. (1998) at O(ε³). It also goes beyond the quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory study by Arndt (2003) and the non-relativistic SSE analysis by Gail (2005). The analytical expressions are intended to enable chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations of decuplet electromagnetic properties, such as those by Alexandrou (2003, 2004) and Leinweber (1992).
