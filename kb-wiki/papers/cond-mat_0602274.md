# Induced P-wave Superfluidity in Asymmetric Fermi Gases

> We show that two new intra-species P-wave superfluid phases appear in two-component asymmetric Fermi systems with short-range S-wave interactions. In the BEC limit, phonons of the molecular BEC induce P-wave superfluidity in the excess fermions. In the BCS limit, density fluctuations induce P-wave superfluidity in both the majority and the minority species. These phases may be realized in experiments with spin-polarized Fermi gases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that attractive intra-species P-wave interactions are induced between gapless fermions in asymmetric two-component Fermi gases with short-range S-wave interactions, leading to the formation of P-wave superfluids. The authors propose that previously suggested normal Fermi-liquid phases and breached pair phases are replaced by P-wave superfluids at zero temperature, but note that in the asymptotic regions (both in asymmetry and coupling) the gaps are exponentially suppressed, so thermodynamic properties will still be dominated by the underlying Fermi-liquid/breached pair nature.

## Method

The authors calculate the induced interactions that give rise to P-wave superfluidity in the controlled limit of small scattering length, using single-phonon exchange in the BEC regime and density-fluctuation exchange (via the static Lindhard function) in the BCS regime. They estimate the dependence of the P-wave gap Δ_P on physical parameters using weak-coupling BCS-type expressions, projecting the induced interactions onto the P-wave (l=1) Legendre component. The analysis is performed for both the BEC limit (where excess fermions interact with a molecular BEC) and the BCS limit (where both majority and minority species are present).

## Key numbers

- In the BEC regime, the P-wave gap for small asymmetries (n_F/n_b ≪ k_F a ≪ 1) is Δ_P/ε_F ∼ exp(-0.44 n_b/n_F), independent of the scattering length.
- The maximal P-wave gap in the BEC regime occurs at n_F/n_b ≈ 0.44 k_F a ≪ 1, with Δ_P^max/ε_F ∼ exp(-5.6/(k_F a)).
- The ratio of critical temperatures for the maximal P-wave gap to BEC condensation is T_c^P,max/T_c^BEC ∼ (k_F a)^(2/3) exp(-5.6/(k_F a)).
- In the BCS regime, the majority component P-wave gap for large asymmetries (k_u ≫ k_d) is Δ_P^↑/ε_u ∼ exp(-3π^2/(2 (2 k_d a)^2 ln(k_u/k_d)) × k_u/k_d).
- The minority component P-wave gap for large asymmetries is Δ_P^↓/ε_d ∼ exp(-18π^2/((2 k_d a)^2) × k_u/k_d).
- The maximum majority P-wave gap in the BCS regime occurs at k_d ≈ 0.77 k_u, with Δ_P^↑,max/ε_u ∼ exp(-π^2/(0.11 (2 k_u a)^2)).
- The function R_1(x) for the P-wave projection has a maximum of R(1.86) = 0.104.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of asymmetric Fermi gases beyond previously proposed phases such as anisotropic/inhomogeneous superfluid states with crystalline structure (LOFF) by Fulde and Ferrell (1964) and Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1965), deformed Fermi surfaces by Muther and Sedrakian (2002), and homogeneous gapless (breached pair) superfluids by Liu and Wilczek (2002), Gubankova et al. (2003), and Forbes et al. (2004). The paper builds on the Kohn-Luttinger (1965) insight that all Fermi surfaces are unstable at zero temperature in the presence of interactions, and on induced interaction calculations for symmetric Fermi systems by Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961), Heiselberg et al. (2000), and for P-wave pairing in neutron stars by Schwenk et al. (2003). The results suggest that these P-wave phases could be observed in experiments with spin-polarized Fermi gases approaching the S-wave Feshbach resonance, particularly from the BEC regime.
