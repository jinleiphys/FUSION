# Unusual statistics of interference effects in neutron scattering from compound nuclei

> We consider interference effects between p-wave resonance scattering amplitude and background s-wave amplitude in low-energy neutron scattering from a heavy nucleus which goes through the compound nucleus stage. The first effect is in the difference between the forward and backward scattering cross sections. Because of the chaotic nature of the compound states, this effect is a random variable with zero mean. However, a statistical consideration shows that the probability distribution of this effect does not obey the standard central limit theorem. That is, the probability density for the effect averaged over n resonances does not become a Gaussian distribution with the variance decreasing as 1/sqrt(n) (``violation'' of the theorem!). We derive the probability distribution of the effect and the limit distribution of the average. It is found that the width of this distribution does not decrease with the increase of n, i.e., fluctuations are not suppressed by averaging. Furthermore, we consider the correlation between the neutron spin and the scattering plane and find that this effect, although much smaller, shows fluctuations which actually increase upon averaging over many measurements. Limits of the effects due to finite resonance widths are also considered. In the appendix we present a simple derivation of the limit theorem for the average of random variables with infinite variances.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the probability distribution of the forward-backward asymmetry in neutron scattering from compound nuclei does not obey the standard central limit theorem: the width of the distribution for the average over n resonances does not decrease as 1/√n, and for the spin-correlation effect the fluctuations actually increase with n, provided the distance to the resonance ε is greater than the resonance width Γ_p.

## Method

The authors derive the probability distribution of two interference effects in low-energy neutron scattering from heavy nuclei: the p_i·p_f correlation (forward-backward asymmetry) and the σ·(p_i×p_f) correlation (spin asymmetry relative to the scattering plane). They model the p-wave resonance capture widths using the Porter-Thomas distribution and the distance to the nearest p-wave resonance using the Wigner distribution, then compute the asymptotic behavior of the resulting probability densities and apply limit theorems for sums of random variables with infinite variances.

## Key numbers

- For the first correlation (p_i·p_f), the asymptotic probability density f(x) ∝ 1/x² for large x, and the limit distribution of the average X is a Cauchy distribution F_n(X) = X_c/[π(X²+X_c²)] with X_c = πγ̄/βD = πg_pΓ̄_p^(n)/AkD, independent of n.
- For the second correlation (σ·(p_i×p_f)), the asymptotic probability density f(x) ∝ x⁻³/² for large x, and the limit distribution of the average X is F_n(X) = √(nx₀/π) e^{-nx₀/X} / X³/², with typical values growing as X ∼ nx₀ where x₀ = 2γ̄/βD².
- The unusual statistics are valid for n < D/Γ_p; for heavy non-fissionable nuclei D/Γ_p ∼ 500.
- The second correlation effect is smaller than the first by a factor Γ_p/D ∼ 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of unusual statistics previously applied to parity-violating asymmetries in neutron scattering by Flambaum (1994), which also showed violations of the central limit theorem due to infinite variance distributions. The present paper examines more conventional interference effects that do not involve the weak interaction, specifically the p_i·p_f and σ·(p_i×p_f) correlations. The results enable experimental searches for these effects in low-energy neutron scattering across many isotopes, where averaging over measurements would not suppress fluctuations as normally expected.
