# On the low x behaviour of nuclear shadowing

> We calculate the x dependence of nuclear shadowing at moderate values of Q^2 by using HERA diffractive data and, for consistency, F2 parameterization of ZEUS. We show that no decrease of shadowing occurs down to very low x (x = 10^-4).

## Key claim

The authors calculate that nuclear shadowing does not decrease down to very low x (x ≈ 10⁻⁴) at moderate Q², contradicting an earlier prediction that shadowing would reach its maximum near x ≈ 10⁻² and then decrease. This conclusion is conditional on using HERA diffractive data rather than the restrictive asymptotic assumptions of the prior work.

## Method

The authors use the Gribov relation between nuclear shadowing and diffraction dissociation, incorporating the ZEUS parametrization of the diffractive structure function F₂ᴰ⁽³⁾ and the ZEUS parametrization of F₂ᴺ for consistency. They integrate the diffractive structure function over xₚₒₘ and impact parameter, including an eikonal factor for multiple rescattering in heavy nuclei, and use a Fermi-type nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The exponent of the pomeron flux in the ZEUS parametrization is a = 1.30 ± 0.08 (+0.08/-0.14), with A = 0.018 ± 0.001 ± 0.005 and C = 0.57 ± 0.12 ± 0.22
- A more recent preliminary analysis gives a ~ 1.1-1.2 in the Q² range 10-20 GeV²
- The effective cross section for multiple rescattering is taken as σ_eff = 10 mb (with variations to 12 and 15 mb shown)
- The slopes used for the t-dependence are B_s ≈ 6 GeV⁻² and B_v ≈ 12 GeV⁻², with α'_Pom ≈ 0.5
- The experimental upper cutoff on xₚₒₘ is xₚₒₘᶜ = 0.04

## Context

This work extends the Gribov relation between nuclear shadowing and diffraction dissociation (Gribov, 1969) and directly contradicts the prediction of Boris (1997) that shadowing would decrease above x ≈ 10⁻². The authors argue that the earlier result relied on two questionable assumptions: a simple power-like parametrization of the shadowing term with α_Pom(0) ≈ 1.1, and keeping the diffractive mass M² fixed equal to Q². The present calculation enables a model-independent prediction that any decrease of shadowing occurs below x ≈ 10⁻⁴, outside the kinematic range accessible at HERA.
