# Some thoughts on critical and tricritical points and deconfinement

> We start with a discussion of a phase diagram and three special collision energies: (i) the first touching of the mixed phase; (ii) the softest point; (iii) the critical point. We then proceed to more details about the role a massless critical mode should play in the particle interaction and the elliptic flow. We then enter a discussion of whether high density QGP is or is not strongly coupled, and if so what one can say about color superconductivity domain using universality and data on trapped ultracold fermionic atoms. At the end, we briefly discuss deconfinement, or more specifically on whether there are precursors of the QCD strings at $T>T_c$, as well as possible role of magnetically charged excitations (monopoles and dyons).

# Some thoughts on critical and tricritical points and deconfinement

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD phase diagram contains three special collision energies, the first touching of the mixed phase, the softest point, and the critical endpoint, and that near the critical point the massless critical mode (a sigma-omega mixture) should dramatically increase attractive mean-field potentials for baryons and vector mesons while making pion interactions more repulsive, with observable consequences for collective flows. The author further proposes that color superconductivity (CS) in dense quark matter may be strongly coupled and that universality arguments from ultracold fermionic atom experiments can constrain its critical temperature, but that even at maximal coupling no heavy ion collisions can reach the CS domain.

## Method

The paper reviews the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane, identifying three special points along cooling paths for heavy ion collisions: the first touch of the phase boundary, the softest point (minimum p/epsilon ratio), and the critical endpoint E. The author discusses the Landau-Ginzburg approach to critical phenomena near the endpoint, focusing on the critical mode (sigma field) and its effects on mean-field potentials for baryons, vector mesons, and pions. For color superconductivity, the author uses universality arguments to relate the critical temperature of strongly coupled fermionic systems to the Fermi energy, extracting a universal constant from trapped atom experiments (alpha_Tc = 0.35) and applying it to quark matter to estimate the slope of the CS critical line and upper bounds on Tc. The paper also discusses deconfinement precursors, including lattice results on static quark-antiquark potentials and the possible role of magnetic monopoles and dyons in generating QCD strings above Tc.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for color superconductivity from universality arguments is bounded above by Tc < 70 MeV, based on the intersection of the critical line with the chemical freezeout line.
- The universal constant relating critical temperature to Fermi energy in strongly coupled fermionic systems is alpha_Tc = Tc/EF = 0.35 (from damping measurements) or 0.27 (from specific heat measurements), with a spread of about 20% as an estimate of uncertainties.
- The second transition temperature associated with disappearance of pairs is T2/TF ≈ 0.7-0.8.
- The slope of the CS critical line is predicted as dTc/dμ = alpha_Tc/3 ≈ 0.1.
- The disappearance of pairing (dashed lines) is expected below T2 = 150 MeV.
- The energy of associated matter at T = Tc for large separation of static quarks reaches about E = 3-4 GeV, with entropy as large as S ≈ 10.
- The omega meson mass shift in nuclear matter is about 14%, and the rho meson shift in peripheral AuAu collisions at RHIC is about 10%.
- The critical endpoint is estimated to be around ELab = 20-40 GeV*A, the softest point around 10-20 GeV*A, and the first touch point around ELab ∼ 5-6 GeV*A.
- The diquark gap from instanton-based calculations is Δ ∼ 100 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the author's prior work with Zahed on zero-binding lines (Shuryak and Zahed, 2004) and with Stephanov and Rajagopal on critical point signals (Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, 1998), including the focusing effect and event-by-event fluctuations. It also builds on the Hung and Shuryak (1995) study of adiabatic cooling paths and the softest point in hydrodynamics. The discussion of color superconductivity uses universality arguments to connect with experimental data on trapped ultracold fermionic atoms from the Duke group (Kinast et al., 2004, 2005) and the MIT group (quantized vortices, 2005). The deconfinement discussion references lattice results on static potentials and the Seiberg-Witten (1994) solution for N=2 SUSY YM as a working example of confinement via monopole condensation, as well as molecular dynamics studies of monopole plasmas by Liao and Shuryak (2006). The paper enables future experimental searches at RHIC high-density runs and FAIR for the three special collision energies, and suggests that transport properties from atomic systems might be related to heavy ion collision hydrodynamics via universality.
