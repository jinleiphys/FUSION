# Effects of formation properties in one-proton radioactivity

> It is shown that the proton formation probability, extracted from experimental data corresponding to one-proton radioactivity, is divided into two regions when plotted as a function of an universal parameter. This parameter is derived from a microscopic description of the decay process. In this way we explain the systematics of proton emission half-lives. At the same time the formation probability is shown to be a useful quantity to determine the deformation property of the mother nucleus.

## Key claim

The proton formation probability extracted from experimental one-proton radioactivity data separates into two distinct regions when plotted against a universal parameter derived from a microscopic description, and this separation is primarily caused by the deformation of the mother nucleus rather than by variations in the Q-value or single-particle wave functions.

## Method

The authors fitted a four-parameter universal decay law (UDL) formula, log T₁/₂ = aχ' + bρ' + d l(l+1)/ρ' + c, to 44 experimental proton decay events from the compilation of Blank (2008) and Darby (2011). They then extracted the formation amplitudes |RF(R)|² from experimental half-lives using the expression log |RF(R)|⁻² = log T₁/₂(Expt.) - log[(ln2/ν)|H⁺_l(χ,ρ)|²], and plotted these as a function of the parameter ρ' = √(A Z_d (A_d¹/³ + 1)). The BCS occupation numbers u and single-proton wave functions were calculated using Woods-Saxon potentials with universal and Cherpunov parameters to interpret the observed trends.

## Key numbers

- The UDL fit to all 44 proton decay events (parameter set I) reproduces experimental half-lives with a root-mean-square deviation σ = 0.44; the half-lives are reproduced within an average factor of three.
- For nuclei with N < 75 (parameter set II), the fit gives σ = 0.344; for nuclei with N ≥ 75, σ = 0.235.
- Most experimental data are reproduced within a factor of 4 (0.25 ≤ R ≤ 4), where R = T₁/₂(Expt.)/T₁/₂(Cal.).
- A strong transition in formation probability occurs at ρ' = 20.5, corresponding to the nucleus ¹⁴⁴Tm.
- The formation probability |RF(R)|² for ¹⁴⁴Tm is 0.111 fm⁻¹, the maximum value among the tabulated nuclei.
- For the isomeric h₁₁/₂ hole state in ¹⁷⁷Tl, the experimental half-life is underestimated by the UDL by a factor of about 8.

## Context

This work extends the universal decay law (UDL) approach previously applied to α decay by Qi (2008) to the case of one-proton radioactivity, and explains the systematics of proton emission half-lives that earlier treatments (e.g., Delion (2006a)) had described using a linear correlation between reduced half-life and the Coulomb parameter. The finding that formation probabilities separate into two regions based on deformation parallels a similar "clustering" observed by Delion (2009) using the fragmentation potential as the determining variable. The results enable the use of extracted formation probabilities as a tool to determine the deformation properties of proton-unstable nuclei near the drip line.
