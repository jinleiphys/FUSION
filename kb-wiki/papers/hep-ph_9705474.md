# Two flavor chiral phase transition from nonperturbative flow equations

> We employ nonperturbative flow equations to compute the equation of state for two flavor QCD within an effective quark meson model. This yields the temperature and quark mass dependence of quantities like the chiral condensate or the pion mass. A precision estimate of the universal critical equation of state for the three-dimensional O(4) Heisenberg model is presented. We explicitly connect the O(4) universal behavior near the critical temperature and zero quark mass with the physics at zero temperature and a realistic pion mass. For realistic quark masses the pion correlation length near T_c turns out to be smaller than its zero temperature value.

## Key claim

The paper connects the universal O(4) critical behavior near the chiral phase transition temperature and zero quark mass with the physics at zero temperature and realistic pion mass, finding that for realistic quark masses the pion correlation length near T_c is actually smaller than its zero temperature value, contrary to earlier expectations of large fluctuations.

## Method

The authors employ nonperturbative flow equations (the exact renormalization group for the effective average action) to compute the equation of state for two-flavor QCD within an effective quark-meson model. They solve the flow for a truncation including a general scalar potential, running wave function renormalizations, and a Yukawa coupling, using an infrared cutoff that smoothly decouples massive Matsubara modes as temperature increases. The initial conditions at the compositeness scale k_Phi ~ 600 MeV are fixed by requiring f_pi = 92.4 MeV for m_pi = 135 MeV and a constituent quark mass in the range 300-350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the chiral limit: T_c = 100.7 MeV
- Pion mass at zero temperature for realistic quark masses: m_pi(T=0) = 135 MeV
- Pion mass at the pseudocritical temperature for realistic quark masses: m_pi(T_pc) ~ 1.7 * m_pi(0) ~ 230 MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature for realistic quark masses (m_pi=135 MeV): T_pc ~ 130 MeV (from inflection point of condensate)
- Pseudocritical temperature for m_pi=45 MeV: T_pc ~ 110 MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature for m_pi=230 MeV: T_pc ~ 150 MeV
- Critical exponent nu = 0.787, with amplitude xi^+ = 0.270 for the correlation length above T_c
- Chiral condensate normalized at scale k_Phi ~ 620 MeV

## Context

This work extends the O(4) universality arguments of Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) and Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) by providing the explicit connection between universal critical behavior and realistic quark masses that those earlier studies lacked. The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice QCD simulations for two flavors, which typically use larger pion masses (e.g., 230 MeV) and need analytic guidance for extrapolation to the chiral limit. The finding that the pion correlation length decreases near T_c for realistic quark masses contradicts earlier speculation about large correlation lengths and disoriented chiral condensates, though the authors note that a tricritical behavior remains possible for three flavors.
