# Maximum entropy kinetic matching conditions for heavy-ion collisions

> Coupling hadronic kinetic theory models to fluid dynamics in phenomenological studies of heavy ion collisions requires a prescription for ``particlization''. Existing particlization models are based on implicit or explicit assumptions about the microscopic degrees of freedom that go beyond the information provided by the preceding fluid dynamical history. We propose an alternative prescription which uses only macroscopic information provided by the hydrodynamic output. This method follows directly from the connections between information theory and statistical mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a particlization prescription for heavy-ion collisions that uses only macroscopic information from the preceding fluid dynamic evolution, avoiding implicit or explicit assumptions about microscopic degrees of freedom; the method follows from the connection between information theory and statistical mechanics, and the resulting maximum-entropy distribution reduces to the linearized Chapman-Enskog form in the Relaxation Time Approximation when viscous stresses are weak.

## Method

The authors maximize the kinetic entropy density four-current subject to constraints from the energy-momentum tensor components (energy density, isotropic pressure, and shear stress) using Lagrange multipliers, without invoking any microscopic equation of motion or ad hoc ansatz for the momentum dependence of viscous corrections. The resulting maximum-entropy distribution function is expressed in terms of seven Lagrange multipliers that are determined by solving the matching conditions for energy density, total isotropic pressure, and shear stress tensor. For the case of vanishing shear stress, the authors solve the coupled integral equations numerically using a hadron resonance gas that includes all resonances propagated in the UrQMD hadronic afterburner.

## Key numbers

- Temperature used for comparison: T = 0.15 GeV
- Bulk pressure magnitude used in comparison: Π = -p_eq / 3 (one-third of the equilibrium pressure)
- For pions, the viscous corrections become negative except at very low momenta p < 50 MeV
- For protons at p = 0, the maximum-entropy distribution is about 20% larger than the Chapman-Enskog RTA distribution and more than a factor 2 larger than the equilibrium distribution
- The Chapman-Enskog RTA linear bulk correction becomes larger than the ideal part (|δf_h,CE| ≳ f_h,eq) at moderate momenta |p| ~ 1 GeV for the chosen bulk pressure

## Context

This work extends the maximum-entropy principle of Jaynes (1957) to the particlization problem in heavy-ion collisions, providing an alternative to existing approaches such as the Grad 14-moment method (Israel 1976, Monnai 2009) and the first-order Chapman-Enskog method with Relaxation Time Approximation (Jaiswal 2014). The maximum-entropy distribution differs from modified equilibrium distributions (Pratt 2010, McNelis 2019) and anisotropic equilibrium distributions (Florkowski 2013, Alqahtani 2017) at second and higher order in dissipative stresses, while matching the Chapman-Enskog RTA form exactly at linear order. This method enables particlization without ad hoc regulation of viscous corrections to maintain positivity of distribution functions, and provides a thermodynamic relation for the non-equilibrium entropy density in terms of hydrodynamic fields and their conjugate variables.
