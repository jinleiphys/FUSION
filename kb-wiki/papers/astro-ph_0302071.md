# Supernova Observation Via Neutrino-Nucleus Elastic Scattering in the CLEAN Detector

> Development of large mass detectors for low-energy neutrinos and dark matter may allow supernova detection via neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering. An elastic-scattering detector could observe a few, or more, events per ton for a galactic supernova at 10 kpc ($3.1 \times 10^{20}$ m). This large yield, a factor of at least 20 greater than that for existing light-water detectors, arises because of the very large coherent cross section and the sensitivity to all flavors of neutrinos and antineutrinos. An elastic scattering detector can provide important information on the flux and spectrum of $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ from supernovae. We consider many detectors and a range of target materials from $^4$He to $^{208}$Pb. Monte Carlo simulations of low-energy backgrounds are presented for the liquid-neon-based Cryogenic Low Energy Astrophysics with Noble gases (CLEAN) detector. The simulated background is much smaller than the expected signal from a galactic supernova.

# Supernova Observation Via Neutrino-Nucleus Elastic Scattering in the CLEAN Detector

## Key claim

A detector based on neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering could observe a few or more events per ton for a galactic supernova at 10 kpc, a factor of at least 20 greater than existing light-water detectors, but this yield depends on the assumed supernova neutrino spectrum and the achievable detector threshold, with significant uncertainty in the ν_x temperature (estimated to range from approximately 6 to 8 MeV).

## Method

The authors folded coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections with a standard model supernova neutrino spectrum using Boltzmann distributions at temperatures of 3.5, 5, and 8 MeV for ν_e, anti-ν_e, and ν_x respectively, assuming equal energy partition among all six neutrino flavors. They calculated recoil spectra and total yields for a range of target materials from ^4He to ^208Pb, then performed Monte Carlo simulations of radioactive backgrounds for the liquid-neon-based CLEAN detector, modeling gamma and x-ray emission from photomultiplier glass and wavelength shifter substrates.

## Key numbers

- Total yield for ^20Ne (CLEAN target): 3.99 events per ton, dominated by 3.08 ν_x events, with 0.38 ν_e and 0.53 anti-ν_e events
- Total yield for ^132Xe: 31 events per ton; for ^208Pb: 47.5 events per ton with average recoil energy of only 2.6 keV
- For CLEAN with 100 tons active mass and 5 keV threshold: expected supernova signal of 330 Ne elastic events above 5 keV, compared to 62 ± 8 background events in 10 seconds
- For CLEAN with 70 tons fiducial mass and 21 keV threshold: expected 140 Ne events above 21 keV, compared to 2.6 ± 1.6 background events in 10 seconds
- ^14C background in carbon-based detectors: approximately 2 decays per ton during 10 seconds at a concentration of 10^-18
- Assumed quenching factor for liquid neon: 0.25 (based on similarity to liquid xenon, where measurements range from 22% to 43%)
- Supernova assumed at distance 10 kpc (3.1 × 10^20 m) radiating total energy 3 × 10^53 ergs

## Context

This work extends the concept of supernova detection via coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, building on earlier proposals by Freedman (1974) and Cabrera (1985), and positions CLEAN as a complement to existing detectors such as Super-Kamiokande (which measures anti-ν_e) and KamLAND (which may detect neutrino-proton elastic scattering as proposed by Beacom (2002)). The paper enables future comparison of ν_x spectral information from elastic scattering with flavor-dependent signals from other detectors, potentially providing a baseline for observing neutrino oscillations by comparing the flavor-blind elastic scattering signal to theoretical simulations and measurements from water Cherenkov detectors.
