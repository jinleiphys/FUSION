# Heavy flavor transport in the QGP medium

> I review recent developments on heavy flavor transport in the QGP medium, along two directions. The first is the transport of individual open heavy quarks. Leveraging the tools of heavy quark effective theory, recent work revealed a novel connection between the evolution equation of the heavy quark phase space distribution, the conditions for kinetic equilibration to take place, and a non-perturbatively defined expectation value of a Wilson loop characterized by the heavy quark velocity. Using these developments, I discuss results from a first exploration of the ensuing stopping and equilibration dynamics of heavy quarks in a strongly coupled environment. The second direction is the transport of quarkonia, bound states of heavy quarks, where novel Generalized Gluon Distributions have been recently defined and calculated in the framework of potential non-relativistic QCD. I discuss their application to the dynamics of quarkonium in heavy-ion collisions, highlighting the need to account for non-Markovian effects in the interactions between the heavy quark pair and quark-gluon plasma -- which have thus far resisted a systematic characterization.

## Key claim

Recent theoretical developments using Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) and potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) have established novel non-perturbative connections between heavy quark transport in quark-gluon plasma and field-theoretic quantities such as Wilson loops and Generalized Gluon Distributions, but the practical application of these quantities to phenomenology remains limited by the need to account for non-Markovian effects that have thus far resisted systematic characterization.

## Method

For open heavy flavor, the author derives a Kolmogorov evolution equation for the heavy quark phase space distribution whose kernel is explicitly determined by a Wilson loop expectation value, calculated in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory as a proxy for QCD. For quarkonium, the author formulates the in-medium dynamics using an open quantum systems framework coupled to pNRQCD, where the relevant gauge-invariant correlation functions (Generalized Gluon Distributions) are defined in terms of chromoelectric field correlators and have been studied analytically in QCD at next-to-leading order (NLO), in strongly coupled N=4 SYM, and recently on the lattice in Euclidean time.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of heavy quark mass over temperature was chosen as M/T = 20, typical for a b quark in a heavy-ion collision, for the time evolution study of the heavy quark phase space distribution.
- The Euclidean lattice study of Generalized Gluon Distributions (Brambilla 2025) indicates that real-time reconstruction must extend to frequencies up to at least |omega| ~ 1 GeV to cover all binding energies of bottomonium (bbar) and charmonium (cbar) states.
- At zero momentum, the transport coefficients satisfy kappa_T(0) = kappa_L(0) = 2 T M eta_D(0), where the first equality follows from isotropy and the second from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for heavy flavor transport beyond simple Langevin/Fokker-Planck models (reviewed by Rapp 2018) and Boltzmann transport approaches (Das 2013), which have relied on the Einstein relation that is known not to hold in the heavy quark limit of QCD (Moore 2004, Gubser 2006). The new Kolmogorov equation formulation (Rajagopal 2025) guarantees kinetic equilibration through a KMS relation satisfied by the Wilson loop, while the Generalized Gluon Distributions (Nijs 2023) provide a systematic framework for quarkonium dynamics that can be constrained by lattice calculations (Brambilla 2025) and compared with experimental data from CMS (2023), ALICE (2023), LHCb (2024), and STAR (2025). The next step requires developing theoretical tools to handle non-Markovian effects in the quarkonium-QGP interactions, which have been shown in strongly coupled setups to encode all relevant physics (Nijs 2023).
