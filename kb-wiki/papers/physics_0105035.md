# Schroedinger uncertainty relation and its minimization states

> An introductory survey on the Schroedinger uncertainty relation and its minimization states is presented with minimal number of formulas and some historical points. The case of the two canonical observables, position and momentum, is discussed in greater detail: basic properties of the two subsets of minimization states (canonical squeezed and coherent states) are reviewed and compared. The case of two non-canonical observables is breafly outlined. Stanfard SU(1,1) and SU(2) group-related coherent states can be defined as states that minimize Schroedinger inequality for the three pairs of generators simultaneously. The symmetry of the Heisenberg and Schroedinger relations is also discussed, and two natural generalizations to the cases of several observables and several states are noted.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Schrödinger uncertainty relation is more general and precise than the Heisenberg-Robertson relation, but notes that despite these advantages it has been absent from almost all textbooks, with interest renewed only in the last two decades in connection with squeezed states.

## Method

The paper presents an introductory survey reviewing the historical development of uncertainty relations from Heisenberg (1927) through Kennard and Weyl to Robertson (1929) and Schrödinger (1930). It examines the mathematical properties of states that minimize the Heisenberg and Schrödinger inequalities for canonical observables (position and momentum), and extends the analysis to non-canonical observables including the generators of SU(1,1) and SU(2) groups. The author derives the condition for minimization as eigenstates of complex linear combinations of the observables, and discusses the covariance properties of the inequalities under linear transformations.

## Key numbers

* Planck constant: h-bar = 1.05 x 10^-27 erg.sec (given in historical context)
* Heisenberg-Weyl-Kennard inequality for position and momentum: (Delta p_x)^2 (Delta x)^2 >= h-bar/4
* For dimensionless observables q and p: (Delta q)^2 (Delta p)^2 >= 1/4
* Schrödinger inequality for dimensionless q and p: (Delta q)^2 (Delta p)^2 - (Delta qp)^2 >= 1/4
* In Glauber coherent states: (Delta q)^2 = 1/2 and (Delta p)^2 = 1/2 (minimizing Heisenberg inequality)
* In squeezed states: Delta p = e^{-s}/sqrt(2) and Delta q = e^{s}/sqrt(2) for real parameter s
* Energy levels of harmonic oscillator: E_n = h-bar omega (n + 1/2), n = 0,1,...
* Mean energy in coherent state |alpha>: h-bar omega (|alpha|^2 + 1/2)
* Photon distribution in coherent states: P_n(alpha) = |alpha|^{2n} exp(-|alpha|^2)/n!

## Context

This paper extends the foundational work of Heisenberg (1927), Kennard and Weyl, Robertson (1929), and Schrödinger (1930) on uncertainty relations. It connects to the discovery of coherent states by Klauder, Glauber, and Sudarshan (1963) and squeezed states by Stoler (1970) and Caves (1981). The work enables further investigation of states that minimize uncertainty relations for non-canonical observables, with applications to improving interferometric measurement accuracy as proposed by Caves (1981) for gravitational wave detection.
