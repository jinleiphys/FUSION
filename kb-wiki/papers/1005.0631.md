# Gluon saturation and inclusive hadron production at LHC

> In high density QCD the hadron production stems from decay of mini-jets that have the transverse momenta of the order of the saturation scale. It is shown in this paper that this idea is able to describe in a unique fashion both the inclusive hadron production for \sqrt{s} \geq 546 GeV including the first data from LHC and the deep inelastic scattering at HERA. Recently reported data from ALICE, CMS and ATLAS including inclusive charged-hadron transverse-momentum and multiplicity distribution in pp collisions are well described in our approach. We provide predictions for the upcoming LHC measurements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that inclusive hadron production in high-energy proton-proton collisions can be described using gluon saturation physics (Color Glass Condensate) across a wide energy range from \(\sqrt{s} \geq 546\) GeV to LHC energies, but this description relies on the assumption that \(k_t\)-factorization remains valid for dense-dense system scattering, which the authors acknowledge is not rigorously proven and must be treated as an assumption.

## Method

The authors use \(k_t\)-factorization to compute mini-jet production cross-sections from unintegrated gluon densities, which are derived from a phenomenological impact-parameter-dependent color dipole scattering amplitude that satisfies the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. The dipole amplitude model (Eqs. CA5-CA6) incorporates geometric scaling and saturation behavior, with parameters fixed by fitting to HERA deep inelastic scattering data at low Bjorken-\(x\). The mini-jet spectrum is converted to hadron distributions using Local Parton-Hadron Duality, with the non-singlet diffractive cross-section obtained from the average impact parameter of mini-jet production rather than from soft interaction models.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at midrapidity \(\eta=0\) and \(p_T=1\) GeV for central collisions \(b=0\): \(Q_s \approx 0.6 \div 0.8\) GeV across the energy range considered
- Mini-jet mass: \(m_{jet} \approx 0.4 \div 0.5\) GeV, derived from \(m_{jet}^2 \simeq 2 \mu p_T\) with \(\mu \approx m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV
- Normalization factor \(\frac{KC}{M} = 2.32\) determined from a fit to charged particle multiplicity at \(\sqrt{s} = 546\) GeV
- Impact-parameter profile parameter: \(B_{CGC} = 7.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\) from fitting diffractive processes at HERA
- Average impact parameter of jet production: \(\langle \vec b^2_{jet} \rangle \approx 2.5 B_{CGC}\), slightly increasing with energy
- For leading-order BFKL kernel with frozen QCD coupling: \(1 - \gamma_{cr} = 0.63\), \(\lambda = 4.88 \bar{\alpha}_s\), \(\kappa = 9.9\)

## Context

This work extends the KLN approach (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi, 2001-2005) for heavy-ion and hadron-nucleus collisions to proton-proton scattering, improving it by using a dipole amplitude that satisfies the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation and explicitly includes impact-parameter dependence of the saturation scale. The model simultaneously describes HERA deep inelastic scattering data and LHC hadron production data, supporting the universality of saturation physics. The approach enables predictions for higher LHC energies (7, 10, and 14 TeV) and for multiplicity-dependent observables such as the average transverse momentum as a function of charged particle multiplicity.
