# Cosmological consequences of first-order general-relativistic viscous fluid dynamics

> We investigate the out-of-equilibrium dynamics of viscous fluids in a spatially flat Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker cosmology using the most general causal and stable viscous energy-momentum tensor defined at first order in spacetime derivatives. In this new framework a pressureless viscous fluid having density $ρ$ can evolve to an asymptotic future solution in which the Hubble parameter approaches a constant while $ρ\rightarrow 0$, even in the absence of a cosmological constant (i.e., $Λ= 0$). Thus, while viscous effects in this model drive an accelerated expansion of the universe, the density of the viscous component itself vanishes, leaving behind only the acceleration. This behavior emerges as a consequence of causality in first-order theories of relativistic fluid dynamics and it is fully consistent with Einstein's equations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a pressureless viscous fluid in a spatially flat FLRW cosmology, described by a causal first-order relativistic viscous fluid theory (BDNK), can drive an accelerated expansion even without a cosmological constant, but the equilibrium energy density of the fluid asymptotically decays to zero rather than approaching a constant value, a behavior the authors call the "Cheshire Cat" mechanism.

## Method

The authors couple the most general causal and stable first-order viscous energy-momentum tensor (BDNK formalism) to Einstein's equations in a spatially flat FLRW metric without a cosmological constant. They derive the modified Friedmann equations for a pressureless fluid (w=0) and analyze the fixed points and stability of the resulting dynamical system, using specific choices of transport coefficients that satisfy causality, well-posedness, and stability conditions from prior work (Bemfica et al. 2019, Kovtun 2019).

## Key numbers

* For a constant non-negative w ≪ 1 with η ≥ 0 and ζ = 2η w (1/3 - w), the asymptotic constant Hubble parameter is H₀ = ζ/(1+w), while the energy density decays as ρ(t) = ρ₀ β / [(ρ₀+β) exp(tβ/α) - ρ₀], where α = 4η w/(1+w) and β = α H₀ (3-w)/(2w).
* For conformal radiation (w=1/3), the Hubble parameter obeys H(t) = H₀/(1+2H₀ t), identical to the ideal fluid case, and the energy density satisfies ρ = 3H².
* In the Landau-Lifshitz theory (χ₁=χ₂=χ₃=0, χ₄=-ζ), a constant energy density implies a constant Hubble rate H = ζ (positive for ζ>0), but this theory is acausal and unstable.

## Context

This work extends the BDNK first-order viscous fluid formalism (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha 2018; Kovtun 2019) to cosmological settings, contrasting with earlier acausal approaches such as Eckart (1940) and Landau-Lifshitz (1959) theories, which are known to be unstable (Hiscock and Lindblom 1985). It also differs from Israel-Stewart second-order theories, whose nonlinear well-posedness remains an open question except in special cases (Bemfica et al. 2019). The results enable future investigations of cosmological perturbations in a causally consistent viscous framework, which previous unified viscous dark matter models (e.g., Li and Barrow 2009; Velten et al. 2012) could not reliably address due to acausality.
