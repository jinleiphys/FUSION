# Classification of color superconductivity by one-gluon exchange helicity amplitudes and renormalization group equations

> Quark matter at high baryon density exhibits diverse pairing patterns classified by color, flavor, and angular momentum quantum numbers. We compute one-gluon exchange (OGE) helicity amplitudes and introduce a nonrelativistic classification of the pairing channel, justified by the channel decomposition in a Lorentz-noninvariant medium and the decoupling of renormalization group flows at leading order. We find the new attractive channel in OGE; the medium effects can render the vacuum-repulsive color $\boldsymbol{6}$ channel attractive in the spin-triplet sector. For color $\boldsymbol{\bar{3}}$ with antisymmetric flavor, the dominant pairing is ${}^1S_0$, while for symmetric flavor the same-helicity $^1P_1$ prevails over $^3S_1$, revising the conventional single-flavor picture. With a mismatch of the Fermi momenta, $^1S_0$ channel, leading to color-flavor locked or two-flavor color superconductor, remains most stable when the separation is small, and the color-spin locked pairing becomes favored as the mismatch gets large. We suggest there are possible quark-hadron continuity in certain cases as expected in the literature.

# Classification of color superconductivity by one-gluon exchange helicity amplitudes and renormalization group equations

## Key claim

The paper claims that the vacuum-repulsive color sextet ($\boldsymbol{6}_c$) channel can become attractive in the spin-triplet sector due to medium effects, specifically the dominance of chromomagnetic over chromoelectric gluons in dense matter; this attraction is much stronger than that from the Kohn-Luttinger mechanism considered in prior work by Schafer (2006), Kumamoto (2024), and Fujimoto (2025).

## Method

The author computes one-gluon exchange (OGE) helicity amplitudes for quark-quark scattering near the Fermi surface using the hard dense loop (HDL) effective theory, then decomposes these amplitudes into channels labeled by color representation, flavor representation, helicity, and spectroscopic term symbol ${}^{2S+1}L_J$. The pairing gap for each channel is evaluated by solving renormalization group equations that decouple at leading order in perturbation theory, allowing independent determination of the most attractive channel for each color-flavor configuration.

## Key numbers

- For color $\boldsymbol{\bar{3}}$ with antisymmetric flavor, the $^1S_0$ channel pairing gap is $\Delta_{^1S_0} \sim 5$–$50$ MeV, staying almost constant as a function of quark chemical potential $\mu$
- For color $\boldsymbol{\bar{3}}$ with symmetric flavor, the $^1P_1$ channel gap is $\Delta_{^1P_1} \sim 0.1$–$1$ MeV, with a fixed ratio $\Delta_{^1P_1}/\Delta_{^1S_0} \simeq e^{-4} \simeq 0.018$
- For color $\boldsymbol{6}$ with symmetric flavor, the $^3P_0$ channel gap is less than $10^{-9}$ eV order in the weak-coupling regime and decreases drastically as $\mu$ increases
- The crossover between $^1P_1$ and $^3S_1$ channel attractiveness occurs at coupling $g \lesssim 0.92$, but the $^1P_1$ gap always remains larger than the $^3S_1$ gap

## Context

This work extends the classification of color-superconducting pairing channels previously attempted by Alford (2002) and Alford (2005) using Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models, but performs the analysis from first-principles QCD using the OGE helicity amplitude formalism originally considered by Bailin (1983). The decoupling of renormalization group flows in different channels, building on work by Evans (1998), Schafer (1998), Son (1998), and Hsu (1999), enables independent gap calculations. The results enable mapping the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density, particularly the transition between color-flavor locked (CFL) and two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phases under neutron star conditions with Fermi momentum mismatch induced by the strange quark mass.
