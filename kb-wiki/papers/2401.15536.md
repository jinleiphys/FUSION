# Anisotropic flow, flow fluctuation and flow decorrelation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions: the roles of sub-nucleon structure and shear viscosity

> We study the transverse momentum ($p_T$) differential anisotropic flow and flow fluctuation in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02 TeV at the LHC. A (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics framework with fluctuating TRENTO (or AMPT) initial conditions is utilized to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium. The effects of shear viscosity and the sub-nucleon structure on anisotropic flow and flow fluctuation are analyzed. Our result shows that shear viscosity tends to suppress both flow coefficients (${v_2\{2}\}$, ${v_2\{4\}}$, ${\langle v_2\rangle}$) and flow fluctuation (${σ_{v_2}}$) due to its smearing effect on local density fluctuation. The flow coefficients appear to be insensitive to the sub-nucleon structure, whereas for flow fluctuation ${σ_{v_2}}$, it tends to be suppressed by the sub-nucleon structure in central collisions but enhanced in peripheral collisions. After taking into account the sub-nucleon structure effect, our numerical result can quantitatively describe the relative flow fluctuations (${v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}}$, $F({v_2})$) measured by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC. We further investigate the effects of shear viscosity, sub-nucleon structure and initial condition model on the flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelations (${A_2^f}$, ${M_2^f}$) using the four-particle correlation method. We find that the flow decorrelation effect is typically stronger in central collisions than in peripheral collisions. The flow angle decorrelation is found to be insensitive to the shear viscosity and sub-nucleon structure, whereas the flow magnitude decorrelation shows quite different behavior when using TRENTO or AMPT initial condition model. Our study sheds light on the anisotropic flow, transport properties and initial structure of the QGP created in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Shear viscosity suppresses both flow coefficients and flow fluctuation due to its smearing effect on local density fluctuation, while the sub-nucleon structure suppresses flow fluctuation in central collisions but enhances it in peripheral collisions; the flow coefficients themselves are insensitive to the sub-nucleon structure.

## Method

The authors used the (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc viscous hydrodynamics framework with fluctuating TRENTO (or AMPT) initial conditions to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. They varied the specific shear viscosity (eta_v/s = 0.08 and 0.16) and compared initial conditions with nucleon-only structure (nucleon Gaussian width 0.5 fm) versus sub-nucleon structure (three constituent partons per nucleon with Gaussian width 0.2 fm). Flow observables were constructed using two-particle and four-particle correlation methods, including the recently proposed ALICE observables A_2^f and M_2^f that separate flow angle and flow magnitude decorrelations.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta_v/s = 0.16 (default) and 0.08 (comparison)
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- Freezeout temperature T_frz = 150 MeV
- Nucleon Gaussian width: 0.5 fm; parton Gaussian width: 0.2 fm
- Number of constituent partons per nucleon: 3
- Longitudinal envelope parameters: eta_flat = 1.7, eta_gw = 2.0
- 2000 hydrodynamics events per centrality bin, each oversampled 3000 times
- Flow angle decorrelation A_2^f deviates from unity increasingly with transverse momentum p_T^a, with largest deviation in 0-5% central collisions
- Flow magnitude decorrelation M_2^f can exceed unity (e.g., in 30-40% centrality at high p_T^a), indicating strong positive correlation between p_T-differential and p_T-integrated flow magnitudes

## Context

This work extends the CLVisc hydrodynamics framework (Pang 2018, Wu 2021) to systematically study flow fluctuation and decorrelation observables, building on prior ALICE measurements of v_2{2}, v_2{4}, and the newly proposed four-particle decorrelation observables A_2^f and M_2^f (ALICE 2022). It contrasts with earlier Bayesian analyses (JETSCAPE 2020, Bernhard 2016) that constrained initial conditions and transport coefficients primarily from flow magnitudes, by showing that flow fluctuation and decorrelation observables provide additional sensitivity to sub-nucleon structure and initial condition models (TRENTO vs. AMPT). The results enable future Bayesian analyses that include these observables to better constrain the temperature dependence of shear viscosity and the initial structure of the QGP.
