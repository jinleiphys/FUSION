# Density-dependent NN-interaction from subleading chiral 3N-forces: Long-range terms

> We derive from the subleading contributions to the chiral three-nucleon force (long-range terms, published in Phys.\,Rev.\,C\,77, 064004 (2008)) a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction $V_\text{med}$ in isospin-symmetric, spin-saturated nuclear matter. Following the division of the pertinent 3N-diagrams into two-pion exchange topology, two-pion-one-pion exchange topology and ring topology, we evaluate for these all self-closings and concatenations of nucleon-lines to an in-medium loop. The momentum and $k_f$-dependent potentials associated with the isospin operators ($1$ and $\vecτ_1\!\cdot\!\vecτ_2$) and five independent spin-structures are expressed in terms of functions, which are either given in closed analytical form or require at most one numerical integration. In the same way we treat the $2π$-exchange 3N-force up to fourth order. Our results for $V_\text{med}$ are most helpful to implement the long-range subleading chiral 3N-forces into nuclear many-body calculations.

## Key claim

The authors derive a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction \(V_\text{med}\) from the long-range terms of the subleading chiral three-nucleon force, applicable specifically to on-shell scattering of two nucleons in isospin-symmetric, spin-saturated nuclear matter, with the resulting potentials expressed in closed analytical form or requiring at most one numerical integration.

## Method

The authors start from the subleading chiral three-nucleon force (long-range terms, published in Phys. Rev. C 77, 064004 (2008)), dividing the pertinent 3N diagrams into two-pion exchange, two-pion-one-pion exchange, and ring topologies. For each topology, they evaluate all self-closings and concatenations of nucleon lines by integrating over a Fermi sphere of radius \(k_f\) to produce a density-dependent NN potential \(V_\text{med}\). The momentum- and \(k_f\)-dependent potentials are expressed in terms of functions associated with the isospin operators (1 and \(\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\)) and five independent spin structures, with the same treatment applied to the \(2\pi\)-exchange 3N force at N\(^4\)LO.

## Key numbers

* The density of nuclear matter is given by \(\rho = 2k_f^3/3\pi^2\) (or \(\rho = 2k_f^3/3\pi^3\) in one instance, likely a typo in the text).
* The self-closing contribution from the two-pion exchange topology yields \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = - {g^4_A m_\pi k^3_f \over (4\pi f^2_\pi)^3} {\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\over (m^2_\pi+q^2)^2} \,\vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\, \vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\, \Big(q^2+{5m^2_\pi\over 6}\Big)\).
* The self-closing contribution from the two-pion-one-pion exchange topology gives \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = {g^4_A m_\pi k^3_f \over (4\pi f^2_\pi)^3} {\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\over m^2_\pi+q^2} \,\vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\, \vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\).
* For the ring topology, the self-closing contribution at order \(g_A^4\) is \(V^{(0)}_\text{med} = -{g^4_A k_f^3 \over 96\pi^3f_\pi^6}\,\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\bigg\{{ m_\pi(9m_\pi^2+2q^2)\over 4m_\pi^2+q^2}+{3m_\pi^2+q^2\over q}\arctan{q\over 2m_\pi}\bigg\}\).
* For the ring topology at order \(g_A^6\), the self-closing contribution includes terms such as \(\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\bigg[{23 m_\pi^3(2m_\pi^2+q^2)+3m_\pi q^4 \over (4m_\pi^2+q^2)^2}+{3 m_\pi^2+ q^2\over q}\arctan{q\over 2m_\pi}\bigg]\) and a spin-dependent term with factor \({3\over 2q^2}(\vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\,\vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q-\vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec \sigma_2 q^2 )\bigg[{m_\pi^3\over 4m^2_\pi+q^2}+{q^2-m_\pi^2\over 2q}\arctan{q \over 2m_\pi}\bigg]\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of density-dependent NN interactions from the leading chiral 3N force at N\(^2\)LO (Holt et al.) to the subleading long-range terms, following a similar approach used for the short-range terms and relativistic \(1/M\)-corrections (Kaiser and Singh, 2019). The results enable easier implementation of subleading chiral 3N forces into nuclear many-body calculations, such as studies of nuclear matter thermodynamics, neutron matter equations of state, and pairing gaps, which previously relied on numerical treatments by the Darmstadt group (e.g., Achim et al.). The authors note that future work should extend this to intermediate-range components at N\(^4\)LO and to density-dependent interactions from chiral four-nucleon forces.
