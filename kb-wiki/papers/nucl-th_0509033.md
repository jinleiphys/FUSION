# Role of the nuclear vector potential in deep inelastic scattering

> We study the influence of the strong nuclear vector potential, treated using the mean-field approximation, in deep inelastic scattering. A consistent treatment of the electromagnetic current operator, combined with the use of the operator product expansion is presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The nuclear vector potential in mean-field models does not cause a shift in the argument of the in-medium quark distribution function when the electromagnetic current operator is treated consistently, but both the shifted and unshifted approaches used in the literature are physically reasonable because the mean-field vector potential should not apply to the high-energy struck quark due to asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The authors derive the in-medium structure function for infinite nuclear matter by evaluating the current commutator in the operator product expansion, using light-cone coordinates and the Bjorken limit. They compare two approaches: one where the vector potential phase factor is cancelled by consistent use of the same quark fields throughout (Eq. reduce2), and another where the phase factor is omitted from the distribution function definition (Eq. reduce3), which is the procedure used by both Thomas et al. (Thomas:vt, Mineo:2003vc) and Smith and Miller (millersmith).

## Key numbers

- The EMC effect shows a reduction of the nuclear structure function in the Bjorken x range between 0.3 and 0.8 that cannot be explained by Fermi motion and binding effects alone.
- The vector potential shift formula used by Thomas et al. is: q(x) = (P+/(P+ - V+)) q0((P+/(P+ - V+))x - (V+/P+)), where V+ is the plus component of the vector potential and P+ is the plus component of the bound nucleon momentum.

## Context

This work addresses a technical controversy between two sets of mean-field models of the EMC effect: those of Thomas et al. (Thomas:vt, Mineo:2003vc) which include a shift in the distribution function argument from the vector potential, and those of Smith and Miller (millersmith) which do not. The paper shows that mathematically consistent treatment of the current operator yields no shift, but argues that both approaches are physically justified because the mean-field vector potential should vanish for high-energy struck quarks due to asymptotic freedom. The authors call for future work to derive a vector potential that accounts for both the high and low energy limits of the quark self-energy in nuclear matter.
