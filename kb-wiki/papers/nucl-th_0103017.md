# Shell-Model Description of Lambda Hypernuclei

> Empirical data on the spectra of light hypernuclei, especially the data from recent gamma-ray experiments, is used to constrain the parameters which govern the p(nucleon)s(Lambda) and p(nucleon)p(Lambda) two-body matrix elements that enter into shell-model calculations.

# Shell-Model Description of Lambda Hypernuclei

## Key claim

Empirical data from recent gamma-ray experiments on light hypernuclei can constrain the parameters governing the p-shell nucleon–s-shell lambda and p-shell nucleon–p-shell lambda two-body matrix elements used in shell-model calculations, but the analysis treats these matrix elements as parameters rather than deriving them from first-principles YN interactions, and the results depend on the choice of core wave functions and radial integrals.

## Method

The author parametrizes the spin-dependence of the effective Lambda-N interaction for a Lambda in a 0s orbit interacting with a p-shell core using four radial integrals: Delta (spin-spin), S_Lambda (Lambda spin-orbit), S_N (nucleon spin-orbit), and T (tensor). These parameters are fitted to reproduce gamma-ray energies and lifetimes measured in KEK E419 (7-Lambda-Li), BNL E930 (9-Lambda-Be), and BNL E929 (13-Lambda-C), using p-shell wave functions from a fit to 32 energy levels for A=6-9. For p-shell lambda states, the central interaction is parametrized separately for S=0 and S=1 using a Q.Q interaction with a space-exchange component epsilon.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state doublet splitting in 7-Lambda-Li: 692(2) keV, with the spin-spin interaction Delta = 0.50 MeV giving a calculated value of 712 keV
- Excitation energy of the 5/2+ state in 7-Lambda-Li: 2050(1) keV, requiring S_N = -0.47 MeV (changed from MGDD value of -0.08 MeV)
- 3/2+ – 5/2+ doublet splitting in 9-Lambda-Be: approximately 32 keV (updating the previous limit of < 100 keV)
- Gamma-ray energy from the first 3/2+ state of 13-Lambda-C: 4.915(33) MeV from BNL E929, in agreement with 4.89(7) MeV from KEK E336
- Separation of the two 1/2- states in 13-Lambda-C: 6.0(4) MeV from (K-,pi-) reaction
- Lowest 1/2- – 3/2- doublet separation in 13-Lambda-C at approximately 11 MeV excitation: 152(54) keV with estimated systematic error of 36 keV
- Lambda binding energy for 0s state in 7-Lambda-Li: 5.58 MeV; in 13-Lambda-C: 11.67 MeV
- For 13-Lambda-C, the 0p_Lambda separation energy is only 0.67 MeV, with an rms radius for the 0p_Lambda orbit of 4.53 fm

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological parametrization of MGDD (Millener, Gal, Dover, Dalitz, 1985) and FMZE (Fetisov, 1991) by incorporating new gamma-ray data from KEK E419, BNL E930, and BNL E929. The results are compared with G-matrix calculations from NSC97 interactions (Rijken, 1999) and with the cluster model calculations of Hiyama et al. (1999). The analysis shows that the space-exchange component of the effective Lambda-N interaction is restricted to be quite small (epsilon approximately 0.25), in agreement with the analysis of Lambda single-particle energies by Usmani and Bodmer (1999). Future measurements on 16-Lambda-O and other p-shell targets are expected to overdetermine the spin-dependent parameters and test for variations with nuclear size and the need for Lambda-NN interactions.
