# The alpha-particle based on modern nuclear forces

> The Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for the alpha-particle are solved. Accurate results are obtained for several modern NN interaction models, which include charge-symmetry breaking effects in the NN force, nucleon mass dependences as well as the Coulomb interaction. These models are augmented by three-nucleon forces of different types and adjusted to the 3N binding energy. Our results are close to the experimental binding energy with a slight overbinding. Thus there is only little room left for the contribution of possible 4N interactions to the alpha-particle binding energy. We also discuss model dependences of the binding energies and the wave functions.

## Key claim

The alpha-particle binding energy calculated with modern NN forces plus adjusted three-nucleon forces (3NFs) is close to the experimental value, with a slight overbinding, leaving only little room for a possible four-nucleon (4N) force contribution; however, the results depend on the specific 3NF model used, and the adjustment of the 3NF to the 3N binding energy removes some but not all model dependence.

## Method

The authors solved the four-nucleon Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space for the alpha-particle ground state, using several modern NN interactions (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93) augmented by three-nucleon forces of the Tucson-Melbourne (TM), modified TM (TM'), and Urbana IX (Urb-IX) types. The 3NF parameters were adjusted separately for each NN interaction to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy, and the Coulomb interaction, charge-symmetry breaking, and nucleon mass differences were included. The partial-wave expansion was truncated at total angular momentum \(j_{ij} \le 6\) and orbital angular momentum sum \(l_{12}+l_{3}+l_{4} \le 14\) (or the equivalent for the other Jacobi coordinate set), and the equations were solved iteratively using a Lanczos-type method.

## Key numbers

* Alpha-particle binding energy with CD-Bonn NN force only: \(-26.26\) MeV (underbinding by about 2 MeV relative to experimental \(-28.30\) MeV).
* Alpha-particle binding energy with AV18 NN force only: \(-24.25\) MeV (underbinding by about 4 MeV).
* With AV18 plus adjusted TM 3NF: binding energy \(-28.36\) MeV (slight overbinding relative to experimental \(-28.30\) MeV).
* With AV18 plus adjusted TM' 3NF: binding energy \(-28.28\) MeV (very close to experiment).
* With AV18 plus Urb-IX 3NF: binding energy \(-28.30\) MeV (exactly matching the experimental value within numerical precision).
* With CD-Bonn plus adjusted TM 3NF: binding energy \(-28.50\) MeV (overbinding by 0.20 MeV).
* Numerical convergence estimated to be within 50 keV for the binding energy.
* Kinetic energy expectation value for AV18 alone: 97.80 MeV; for AV18+TM: 107.21 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for the 4N system (Kamada et al., 1992; Gloeckle et al., 1993) and provides a systematic study of model dependence across several modern NN and 3N interactions. It confirms the Tjon-line correlation between the triton and alpha-particle binding energies for NN-only forces, but shows that adding adjusted 3NFs moves the predictions close to the experimental point, with only a small residual model dependence. The results enable future studies of short-range correlations in the alpha-particle wave function and provide a benchmark for testing upcoming chiral interactions and for estimating the possible size of 4N forces.
