# Thermodynamic Properties of Effective Chiral Lagrangians with BR Scaling

> We show that effective chiral Lagrangians endowed with Brown-Rho scaling can be mapped to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point theory in a way consistent with general constraints following from thermodynamics. This provides a unified scheme to treat, starting from normal nuclear matter, hadronic matter under extreme conditions that is encountered in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and in the interior of compact stars.

# Wiki Page: Thermodynamic Properties of Effective Chiral Lagrangians with BR Scaling

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that effective chiral Lagrangians with Brown-Rho (BR) scaling parameters can be made thermodynamically consistent, specifically, that density-dependent masses and coupling constants do not violate energy-momentum conservation or the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point structure, provided the density dependence is treated as arising from the expectation value of a Lorentz-invariant baryon current operator rather than as field-independent parameters.

## Method

The authors start from a mean-field effective chiral Lagrangian with BR-scaling nucleon effective mass, scalar meson mass, vector meson mass, and vector coupling constant. They replace the baryon density with a Lorentz-invariant current operator to maintain covariance, then derive the Euler-Lagrange equations including "rearrangement terms" that arise from the density dependence. Using the Noether procedure, they construct the conserved energy-momentum tensor and compute the equation of state, pressure, compression modulus, and Landau Fermi-liquid parameters (F0, F1, effective mass) in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature, verifying that the relativistic Landau Fermi-liquid relations are satisfied exactly.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus K is shown to satisfy the relativistic Landau formula K = (3k_F^2 / E_F)(1 + F0), with the accepted empirical value range given as 200–300 MeV for normal nuclear matter.
- The Landau parameter F1 is derived as F1 = -3(C_v^2 - Σ_0/ρ)ρ / [E_F + (C_v^2 - Σ_0/ρ)ρ], where C_v = g_v^⋆/m_ω^⋆, Σ_0 is the rearrangement term, and E_F = sqrt(k_F^2 + m_L^⋆^2).
- The chemical potential at zero temperature in the comoving frame is μ = C_v^2 ρ + E_F - Σ_0.
- The pressure computed from the thermodynamic relation p = ρ^2 ∂(E/ρ)/∂ρ agrees exactly with the pressure from the conserved energy-momentum tensor (1/3)⟨T_ii⟩, confirming thermodynamic consistency.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field treatment of BR-scaling chiral Lagrangians (Song, Min, Rho, 1997) by resolving the issue of thermodynamic consistency left open in that earlier paper. It builds on the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the mapping of chiral Lagrangians to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point theory (Friman and Rho, 1996). The result enables unified treatment of normal nuclear matter and extreme conditions in heavy-ion collisions and compact stars, including applications to dilepton production in CERES experiments and kaon condensation, while noting that deviations from simple BR scaling may occur at densities around 3ρ_0.
