# Multi-strange baryon production in Au+Au collisions near threshold

> The centrality dependence of Xi^- and Lambda production in Au+Au interactions at E_lab=6 AGeV is studied within a microscopic transport approach. In line with recent data, a slight enhancement of the Xi^-/(Lambda+Sigma^0) ratio toward central collisions is found. It is demonstrated that the observed production of multiple strange baryons can be traced back to multi-step meson-baryon interactions in the late stages of the collisions. Therefore, the present analysis supports an interpretation of the observed Xi abundance in terms of hadronic re-scattering.

# Multi-strange baryon production in Au+Au collisions near threshold

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ toward central collisions in Au+Au interactions at $E_{\rm lab}=6$ $A$GeV can be explained entirely by hadronic re-scattering processes, specifically multi-step meson-baryon interactions in the late stages of the collision, without requiring a quark-gluon plasma phase. However, the authors note that a mass reduction of the $\Xi$ might yield considerably larger contributions from baryon-baryon scatterings, and it is hard to distinguish between their scenario (without in-medium modifications) and a calculation with in-medium effects included solely on the basis of total multiplicities.

## Method

The Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD 1.2) was applied to Au+Au reactions from $E_{\rm lab}=2$ $A$GeV to 10 $A$GeV, with calculations performed without potential interactions and without in-medium modifications of hadron masses and widths. The production channels for $\Xi$ particles were decomposed into baryon-baryon (BB) reactions, meson-baryon (MB) reactions, and decays of baryon resonances, and the time evolution of these channels was tracked to identify the dominant production mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ ratio shows a slight enhancement toward central collisions at $E_{\rm lab}=6$ $A$GeV, with good agreement between UrQMD calculations and E895 collaboration data.
- Baryon-baryon production of $\Xi$ peaks at maximum overlap time $t_{\rm max, BB} \approx 6$ fm/c, while meson-baryon production peaks at $t_{\rm max, MB} \approx 8$ fm/c, with MB production rate remaining higher than the maximum BB rate up to 13 fm/c.
- In meson-baryon reactions leading to $\Xi$ production: ($\overline K, \overline K^*(892)$) + baryon contributes 33%; $\pi$ + baryon contributes 24%; ($\rho, \omega$) + baryon contributes 22%; ($\eta$, $\dots$, $b_1(1235)$) + baryon contributes 16%.
- Only 11% of meson-baryon interactions leading to $\Xi$ production proceed via the strangeness exchange reaction anti-Kaon + hyperon.
- At $E_{\rm lab}=6$ $A$GeV, the model predicts crossing the critical energy density of 1 GeV/fm$^3$, with a linear dependence of $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ on energy density from 2 $A$GeV to 10 $A$GeV.
- At SPS and RHIC energies, the default UrQMD simulation underpredicts the $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ ratio, requiring additional mechanisms like di-quark clustering or string fusion to match data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strange particle production in heavy ion collisions near threshold, following the E895 collaboration measurements (Chung, 2003). The authors contrast their findings with the ART transport model (Pal, 2002), which found strangeness exchange reactions ($\overline{\rm K} + \Lambda \rightarrow \Xi + \pi$) to be the most prominent $\Xi$ creation channels, whereas the present study indicates that only 11% of MB interactions proceed via that channel and that OZI forbidden channels yield a huge contribution. The results enable future searches for the onset of quark-gluon plasma formation by studying deviations from the predicted linear scaling of $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ with energy density, and by comparing in-plane and out-of-plane flow measurements to distinguish between hadronic re-scattering and in-medium modification scenarios.
