# Characterization of the inner edge of the neutron star crust

> The poorly known crustal equation of state plays a critical role in many observational phenomena associated with a neutron star. Using semi-classical Monte Carlo simulations, we explore the possible configurations of the inner edge of the neutron-star crust for a variety of baryon densities and proton fractions. Applying the Kirkwood--Buff theory to these two-component systems, we observe how the isothermal compressibility reaches a maximum when isolated non-symmetric clusters are formed in an extremely dilute neutron gas. To determine the neutron fraction, we suggest a geometrical model based on the behavior of the proton-neutron pair correlation function. Accordingly, the equation of state of the inner crust is calculated, illustrating that the nuclear energy in beta-equilibrium follows a power-law behavior with baryon density. As a possible astrophysical outcome of this study, our results could help refine the mass-radius relation. Finally, our results pave the way towards further investigations of the impact of the proton-neutron pair correlation function on transport properties within the neutron-star crust.

## Key claim

The isothermal compressibility of the inner crust reaches a maximum when isolated non-symmetric clusters form in a dilute neutron gas, suggesting a critical-point-like behavior; however, this conclusion is drawn from semi-classical Monte Carlo simulations with a modest number of nucleons (A=5000) and a simplified two-body interaction calibrated at T=1 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed semi-classical Monte Carlo simulations with 5000 nucleons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using a two-body potential with short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction between proton-neutron pairs, plus an exact Ewald summation for the Coulomb interaction in a uniform electron background. They computed pair correlation functions for all three nucleon pairs (nn, pp, pn) and applied Kirkwood-Buff theory to extract the isothermal compressibility from the angle-averaged integrals of these correlation functions. A geometrical model based on the first peak of the proton-neutron pair correlation function was used to estimate the bound neutron fraction, defining a neutron as bound if its distance to the nearest proton was less than or equal to R_pn plus the full width at half maximum of the peak.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density range simulated: 0.005 to 0.08 fm^{-3} (approximately 0.03 to 0.5 times nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3})
- Proton fractions simulated: 0 to 0.4
- Simulation box lengths: 39.69 to 100 fm
- Number of nucleons: A = 5000
- Temperature: cooled from 2 MeV to final temperature of 1 MeV, with cooling schedule of 0.1 MeV per 4000 sweeps
- Isothermal compressibility reaches maximum values of about 2500 at y_p = 0.2 and rho = 0.01 fm^{-3}, and about 3700 at y_p = 0.4 and rho = 0.008 fm^{-3}
- Average proton-neutron distance R_pn = 1.97 +/- 0.12 fm, consistent with the minimum of the two-body pn potential at 1.92 fm
- Bound neutron fraction fit parameters: f_0 = 0.6, f_1 = 25.5, f_2 = 0.52
- Two-body potential parameters: a = 110 MeV, b = -26 MeV, c = 24 MeV, Lambda = 1.25 fm^2

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical Monte Carlo simulations of neutron star crust matter by Piekarewicz and Toledo-Sanchez (2020) and Schneider et al. (2018), who studied pasta phases with up to three million nucleons. The novel contribution is the application of Kirkwood-Buff theory to compute the isothermal compressibility from pair correlation functions, which had not been previously attempted for the inner crust. The results enable future investigations of how the proton-neutron pair correlation function affects transport properties such as shear viscosity and neutrino opacity in the neutron-star crust, and could help refine the mass-radius relation through improved crustal equations of state.
