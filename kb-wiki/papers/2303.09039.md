# Classification of pairing phase transition in the hot nucleus

> The hot nucleus $^{162}\mathrm{Dy}$ is investigated using covariant density functional theory, where the shell-model-like approach treats the pairing correlation. Lee-Yang's theorem is applied to classify the pairing phase transition by analyzing the distribution of zeros of the partition function in the complex temperature plane. The distribution of zeros of the partition function converges with increasing particle numbers and illustrates the characteristics of the phase transition. In our calculations, we determine the first order of the phase transition near the critical temperature. Different seniority states show the pairing phase transition from a superfluid to a normal phase, ranging from fully paired states to completely unpaired states.

## Key claim

The pairing phase transition in the hot nucleus 162Dy is classified as first-order based on the distribution of zeros of the canonical partition function in the complex temperature plane, with the caveat that the classification scheme is applied to a finite system where the phase transition is not sharp in the thermodynamic sense.

## Method

The authors used covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a point-coupling Lagrangian to generate single-particle levels, then treated pairing correlations via the shell-model-like approach (SLAP) which diagonalizes a pairing Hamiltonian in a multi-particle configuration space with strict particle-number conservation. The canonical partition function was computed from the resulting excitation energies and level degeneracies, and its zeros were analyzed in the complex inverse-temperature plane following the Lee-Yang theorem and the classification scheme of PhysRevC.66.024322. The exponent α, derived from the spacing of the first three zeros closest to the real axis, was used to determine the order of the phase transition (α < 0 indicates first-order).

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing strength Gn = 0.29 MeV, proton pairing strength Gp = 0.32 MeV, with energy cutoff Ec = 45 MeV.
- For neutrons, a zero of the partition function appears at β ≈ 1.2 MeV⁻¹, corresponding to a critical temperature T ≈ 0.83 MeV.
- For protons, a zero appears at β ≈ 1.1 MeV⁻¹, corresponding to a critical temperature T ≈ 0.90 MeV.
- The exponent α for neutrons: −4.61 (2 pairs), −4.31 (3 pairs), −4.63 (4 pairs), −4.74 (5 pairs).
- The exponent α for protons: −5.5 (2 pairs), −4.57 (3 pairs), −4.55 (4 pairs), −4.73 (5 pairs).

## Context

This work extends the classification scheme for phase transitions in finite systems, originally applied to atomic clusters by PhysRevLett.84.3511 and PhysRevA.64.013611 and adapted for nuclear pairing by PhysRevC.66.024322, to a self-consistent nuclear structure calculation using CDFT+SLAP. The authors' previous work (PhysRevC.92.044304 and Yan 2021) studied thermodynamic properties of even-even and odd-A nuclei with the same method, establishing the S-shaped heat capacity as a signature of the pairing phase transition. The present result enables the classification of that transition as first-order, resolving ambiguity about its order in finite nuclei, and provides a framework for analyzing phase transitions in other hot nuclei with realistic interactions.
