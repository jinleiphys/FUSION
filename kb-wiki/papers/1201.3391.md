# Effects of hadronic potentials on elliptic flows in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Within the framework of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions, we show that including mean-field potentials in the hadronic phase leads to a splitting of the elliptic flows of particles and their antiparticles, providing thus a plausible explanation of the different elliptic flows between $p$ and ${\bar p}$, $K^+$ and $K^-$, and $π^+$ and $π^-$ observed in recent Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

Including mean-field potentials in the hadronic phase of the AMPT model produces a splitting of elliptic flows between particles and their antiparticles that is qualitatively consistent with BES data, but the model underestimates the relative elliptic flow difference between protons and antiprotons and between pions, while overestimating that between kaons.

## Method

The authors extended the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to include mean-field potentials for baryons, antibaryons, kaons, antikaons, and pions in the hadronic stage. The parton scattering cross section and the ending time of the partonic stage were adjusted to approximately reproduce measured elliptic flows and hadronic energy density at chemical freeze-out for Au+Au collisions at three BES energies (7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV). Hadronic potentials were taken from a relativistic mean-field model for nucleons and antinucleons, a chiral effective Lagrangian for kaons and antikaons, and chiral perturbation theory for pions.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross sections were set to 3 mb, 6 mb, and 10 mb for collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV, respectively
- Ending time of the partonic stage was set to 3.5, 2.6, and 2.9 fm/c for collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, and 39 GeV, respectively
- At normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, the nucleon potential is about -60 MeV and the antinucleon potential is about -260 MeV
- At rho_0, the kaon potential is about +20 MeV and the antikaon potential is about -120 MeV
- At rho_0 and isospin asymmetry delta = 0.2, the pi- potential is about +14 MeV and the pi+ potential is about -1 MeV
- Including hadronic potentials increases the relative v_2 difference between p and anti-p and between K+ and K- up to about 30% at 7.7 GeV and 20% at 11.5 GeV, but negligibly at 39 GeV
- Measured relative v_2 differences from experiments are about 63% (p/anti-p) and 13% (K+/K-) at 7.7 GeV, 44% and 3% at 11.5 GeV, and 12% and 1% at 39 GeV

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model (Lin 2005) by adding hadronic mean-field potentials to explain the particle-antiparticle elliptic flow splitting observed in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (Moh11). The approach builds on earlier findings from lower-energy heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI and AGS/BNL (Dan02) that nucleon elliptic flow is affected by mean-field potentials, and on theoretical work showing that particle and antiparticle potentials have opposite signs at high densities (Ko 1996, Ko 1997). The results enable future studies that could include partonic mean-field potentials (Plu10), screening effects on baryon-antibaryon annihilation (Kah93), different elliptic flows of transported and produced partons (Dun11), and the chiral magnetic effect (Bur11) to achieve more quantitative agreement with experimental data.
