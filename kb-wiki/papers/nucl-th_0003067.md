# Baryon distribution for high energy heavy ion collisions in a parton cascade model

> The baryon distribution is studied by using a parton cascade model which is based on pQCD incorporating hard partonic scattering and dynamical hadronization scheme. In order to study baryon distribution,baryonic cluster formation is newly implemented as well as hadronic higher resonance states from parton/beam cluster decay. The net baryon number and charged hadron distributions are calculated with different $K$-factors in which parameters are fixed by elementary $p\bar p$ data at $E_{c.m.}=200$ GeV. It is found that baryon stopping behavior at SPS and RHIC energies are not consequence of hard parton scattering but soft processes.

# Baryon distribution for high energy heavy ion collisions in a parton cascade model

## Key claim
Baryon stopping at SPS and RHIC energies is not a consequence of hard parton scattering but of soft processes, specifically beam cluster decay. The claim is conditional on the choice of K-factor: with K=1.0 at SPS energies, the baryon stopping behavior is fully explained by soft physics, but the K-factor cannot be uniquely fixed from rapidity and transverse momentum distributions for p-pbar collisions alone.

## Method
The author modified the VNI parton cascade model by implementing baryonic cluster formation (diquark coalescence) and allowing hadronic resonance states up to 2 GeV mass from parton/beam cluster decay, with exact flavor conservation. The model was run for p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV to fix parameters using different K-factor schemes (constant multiplicative factors K=1,2,2.5 and a running coupling scale factor eta=0.075), then applied to Pb+Pb at Elab=158 AGeV and Au+Au at Ec.m.=200 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- For p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, primordial transverse momenta of partons are generated with mean p_perp=0.44 GeV
- The confinement length scale L_c=0.8 fm and transition region L_0=0.6 fm are used for parton cluster formation probability
- For Pb+Pb at Elab=158 AGeV with K=1.0, net proton rapidity distribution is explained by beam cluster decay with negligible parton cluster contribution
- For Au+Au at Ec.m.=200 AGeV, string-based models predict proton rapidity density of 10 (HIJING/B) and 12.5 (UrQMD) at mid-rapidity, while the parton cascade model predicts almost baryon-free mid-rapidity
- Total momentum is conserved within 10% at RHIC energy for central Au+Au collision

## Context
This work extends the VNI parton cascade model of Geiger (1995) by adding baryonic cluster formation, which was absent in the original version that implicitly assumed a baryon-free region at mid-rapidity. The results contradict string-based models such as HIJING/B and UrQMD, which predict significant baryon stopping at RHIC energies through diquark breaking mechanisms. The finding that hard pQCD scattering does not produce baryon stopping enables future work on whether inverse processes (hadron-to-parton conversion) become important at higher colliding energies, and highlights the need to fix K-factor parameters using additional elementary processes beyond p-pbar data.
