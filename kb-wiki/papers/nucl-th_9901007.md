# proton-deuteron elastic scattering above the deuteron breakup

> The complex Kohn variational principle and the (correlated) hyperspherical harmonics method are applied to study the proton-deuteron elastic scattering at energies above the deuteron breakup threshold. Results for the elastic cross section and various elastic polarization observables have been obtained by fully taking into account the long-range effect of the Coulomb interaction and using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction model. Detailed comparison between the theoretical predictions and the accurate and abundant proton-deuteron experimental data can now be performed.

# Proton-Deuteron Elastic Scattering Above the Deuteron Breakup

## Key claim
The complex Kohn variational principle combined with the correlated hyperspherical harmonics method can be extended to describe proton-deuteron elastic scattering above the deuteron breakup threshold when the long-range Coulomb interaction is fully included, but the theoretical predictions for vector analyzing powers are underpredicted by about 30% compared to experimental data, indicating deficiencies in the nuclear interaction models used.

## Method
The authors applied the complex Kohn variational principle with a trial wave function expanded in pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics, decomposing the three-nucleon scattering wave function into a core part describing close interactions and an asymptotic part describing deuteron-nucleon motion. The hyperradial functions were expanded in Laguerre polynomials and matched to outgoing solutions at a matching radius of approximately 100 fm, using the Argonne AV18 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential. Elastic S-matrix elements were calculated up to total angular momentum J=11/2+ (relative angular momentum L ≤ 4), with higher partial waves up to L=8 included via the Born approximation.

## Key numbers
- Incident nucleon energies up to 10 MeV were studied for both n-d and p-d elastic scattering
- Maximum hyperangular momentum K0 = 6 was found sufficient to obtain elastic scattering observables within 1% accuracy
- Matching radius ρ0 ≈ 100 fm was used for matching the variational solutions to outgoing boundary conditions
- Vector analyzing powers Ay and iT11 are underpredicted by approximately 30% compared to experimental data
- Calculations included total angular momentum states up to J = 11/2+ and relative angular momentum L ≤ 4 (with L up to 8 in Born approximation)

## Context
This work extends the pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonic approach previously applied to n-d and p-d scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold (Kievsky, Rosati, and Viviani, 1994; 1996) and above the threshold using a semi-phenomenological s-wave potential (Kievsky, Rosati, and Viviani, 1997). The results for n-d scattering are in complete agreement with Faddeev calculations in momentum space performed by the Bochum-Cracow group (1996). The persistent discrepancy in vector analyzing powers, also observed in earlier phase-shift analyses at lower energies (Kievsky, Rosati, and Viviani, 1996), points to deficiencies in the P-wave phase-shift parameters from the AV18 potential. This work enables detailed comparison of realistic three-nucleon calculations with the accurate experimental data of Sagara (1994) and others, and the technique is intended for extension to higher energies and breakup cross sections in future work.
