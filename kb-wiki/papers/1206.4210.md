# $Σ$ Resonances from $K^- N\rightarrow πΛ$ reactions with the center of mass energy from 1550 to 1676 MeV

> For the study of the $Σ$ resonances, we analyze the differential cross sections and $Λ$ polarizations for the reactions $K^-n\toπ^-Λ$ and $K^-p\toπ^0Λ$ with an effective Lagrangian approach. Data of an early experiment and the recent Crystal Ball experiment at BNL are included in the analysis with the c.m. energy from 1550 to 1676 MeV. Our results clearly support the existence of a $Σ$ resonance with $J^P={1\over 2}^+$, mass near 1633 MeV, and width about 120 MeV, which confirms the 3-star $Σ(1660) 1/2^+$ in PDG. Meanwhile, our results do not support the existence of the 2-star $Σ(1620){1\over 2}^-$ in PDG. The analysis results for the parameters of the relevant $Σ$ resonances and couplings are presented.

# Σ Resonances from K⁻ N → πΛ Reactions with Center of Mass Energy from 1550 to 1676 MeV

## Key claim

The analysis of differential cross sections and Λ polarizations for both K⁻ n → π⁻ Λ and K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ reactions supports the existence of a Σ resonance with Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, mass near 1633 MeV, and width about 120 MeV, confirming the 3-star Σ(1660) 1/2⁺ in PDG; however, the results do not support the existence of the 2-star Σ(1620) 1/2⁻ in PDG, with the caveat that the differential cross sections alone cannot distinguish between the two solutions.

## Method

The authors performed an effective Lagrangian analysis of the reactions K⁻ n → π⁻ Λ and K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ, including t-channel K* exchange, u-channel proton exchange, and s-channel Σ resonance exchanges. They fitted the model to differential cross section and Λ polarization data from the Crystal Ball experiment at BNL (c.m. energy 1560-1676 MeV) and from an earlier experiment (c.m. energy 1550-1650 MeV), using Breit-Wigner propagators for unstable resonances and form factors with cutoff parameters constrained between 0.8 and 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Best fit includes a Σ(1/2⁺) resonance with mass 1633 ± 3 MeV and total width 121⁺⁴₋₇ MeV, with coupling √(Γ_πΛ Γ_KN)/Γ_tot = −0.064⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁵₋₀·⁰⁰³
- The fit including this resonance yields χ² = 572 for 348 data points, compared to χ² = 1680 when only the four established Σ resonances are included (Δχ² = 1008)
- For the Σ(1670) 3/2⁻ resonance, fitted mass is 1673 ± 1 MeV and total width 52⁺⁵₋₂ MeV
- When including an additional Σ(1/2⁻) instead of Σ(1633) 1/2⁺, the fitted mass goes to the preset lower limit of 1360 MeV with width 312 MeV
- Including both Σ(1633) 1/2⁺ and an additional Σ(1/2⁻) gives χ² = 548 for 348 data points, with the Σ(1/2⁻) mass at 1432 MeV and width ≥ 1000 MeV
- When fitting only the old K⁻ n → π⁻ Λ data (100 data points) with the four established Σ resonances, χ² = 116, already smaller than the χ² values of 176-180 from the original analysis of Ref. [16201]

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis of Gao, Shi, and Zou (2012) on the Crystal Ball data alone by including both K⁻ p → π⁰ Λ and K⁻ n → π⁻ Λ reactions, using data from the Crystal Ball collaboration (CryB) and the earlier experiment of Ref. [16201]. The results contradict the claims of Ref. [16208] and Ref. [16207] for a Σ(1/2⁻) resonance near 1620 MeV, and instead support the existence of Σ(1660) 1/2⁺ as suggested by Ref. [NPB94]. The analysis enables further investigation of the lowest 1/2⁻ SU(3) baryon nonet, with the data favoring a low-mass Σ(1/2⁻) around 1380-1432 MeV as predicted by unquenched quark models (pentq1, pentq2) and the meson-baryon dynamical models of Kanchan (2011) and Ramos, rather than the quenched quark model predictions near 1650 MeV.
