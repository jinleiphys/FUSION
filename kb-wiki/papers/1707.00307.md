# Semiclassical triton

> The symmetric components of the spatial part of $S$- and $D$- states' wavefunctions for triton $(^{3}H)$ are investigated utilizing semiclassical expansion (in the powers of $\hbar$). Analysis of the diagonalized Hamiltonian reveals the existence of two different mass states within the ground state of triton. We have solved the coupled differential equations for the two admixed states $^{2}S_{1/2}$ and $^{4}D_{1/2}$ owing to tensor interactions exploiting classical WKB-theory using phenomenological Feshbach-Pease potentials. The relative probability of the $D$-state is found to be in good agreement with the experimentally inferred value (4 - 5 \%).

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground state of triton contains two distinct "mass states" (effective masses differing by about 2.5 MeV at r=1 fm) that emerge from diagonalizing the Hamiltonian, and that the semiclassical WKB method yields D-state probabilities in the range 4.0-4.9% for different Feshbach-Pease potentials, consistent with the experimentally inferred range of 4-5%.

## Method

The authors start from the Derrick-Blass reduction of the three-body problem to coupled radial differential equations for the symmetric S-state (²S₁/₂) and D-state (⁴D₁/₂) components of the triton wavefunction, using phenomenological Feshbach-Pease potentials. They diagonalize the 2x2 matrix Hamiltonian via an orthogonal transformation that depends on the radial coordinate r, obtaining two potential energy surfaces v₊(r) and v₋(r). They then apply a repeated unitary transformation scheme (following Gaspard) to obtain semiclassical wave equations at successive orders in ℏ, which yields two effective one-dimensional Schrödinger equations with position-dependent effective masses M±(r). The resulting equations are solved analytically using Airy functions, with the D-state component w(r) requiring a complex turning point (r₃ = -1.1078 + 2.1338 i fm for Feshbach-Pease Potential No. 1).

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy of triton: E = -8.482 MeV (from Audi, used as input)
* Mass states separated by an energy difference of about 2.5 MeV at r = 1 fm
* D-state probability for Feshbach-Pease Potential No. 1: 4.9% (semiclassical method), compared to 2.2% (FP exponential trial), 1.9% (coupled FR), and 1.6% (modified FR)
* D-state probability for Feshbach-Pease Potential No. 2: 4.5% (semiclassical), compared to 2.8% (FP), 2.8% (coupled FR), 1.9% (modified FR)
* D-state probability for Feshbach-Pease Potential g=0: 4.4% (semiclassical), compared to 3.1% (FP), 3.1% (coupled FR), 2.4% (modified FR)
* D-state probability for Feshbach-Pease Potential No. 3: 4.0% (semiclassical), compared to 3.6% (FP), 3.6% (coupled FR), 2.4% (modified FR)
* Complex turning point for Potential No. 1: r₃ = (-1.1078 + 2.1338 i) fm
* Complex intersection point of v₊ and v₋ for Potential No. 1: (5.48242 + 11.4104 i) fm

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach previously applied to the deuteron by Jain (2004) to the three-body triton problem, where the tensor interaction couples ²S₁/₂ and ⁴D₁/₂ states. It builds on the foundational reduction of the triton problem to coupled equations by Derrick and Blatt (1958) and uses the Feshbach-Pease potentials (Pease, 1955) as the nuclear interaction. The results improve upon earlier numerical methods such as the Feshbach-Rubinow exponential trial wavefunction and the coupled Runge-Kutta approach (McMillan, 1967), achieving D-state probabilities that match the experimentally inferred 4-5% range from magnetic moment calculations (Blatt, 1952). This analytic semiclassical method opens the possibility of treating few-body systems with tensor forces, potentially extending to ⁴He.
