# An Estimate of the Thermodynamic Pressure in High-Energy Collisions

> We introduce a novel approach to estimate the thermodynamic pressure from heavy-ion collisions based on recently measured higher-order moments of particle multiplicities by the STAR experiment. We start with fitting the experimental results in the most-central collisions. Then, we integrate them back to lower ones. For example, we find that the first-order moment, the mean multiplicity, is exactly reproduced from the integral of variance, the second-order moment. Therefore, the zero-order moment, the thermodynamic pressure, can be estimated from the integral of the mean multiplicity. the possible comparison between such a kind of pressure (deduced from the integral of particle multiplicity) and the lattice pressure and the relating of Bjorken energy density to the lattice energy density are depending on lattice QCD at finite baryon chemical potential and first-principle estimation of the formation time of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP).

# An Estimate of the Thermodynamic Pressure in High-Energy Collisions

## Key claim

The paper introduces an approximative method to estimate the thermodynamic pressure from heavy-ion collisions by integrating higher-order moments of particle multiplicities measured by STAR, but explicitly states that this estimate is not exact and that direct comparison with lattice QCD pressure is not possible due to various uncertainties, including the difficulty of lattice calculations at finite baryon chemical potential and the lack of a first-principle estimation for the formation time of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author fits the first four moments (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) of net-proton multiplicity distributions measured by STAR as functions of baryon chemical potential, using analytical expressions. The variance is integrated with respect to chemical potential to reproduce the mean multiplicity, and then the mean multiplicity is integrated to obtain the thermodynamic pressure. The procedure is applied across different collision centralities, defined by the average number of participating nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicity fit: M = 37.8 * mu_b^0.6 - 2.105, with a1 = 37.8 +/- 0.11, b1 = 0.6 +/- 0.11, c1 = -2.105 +/- 1.1
- Variance fit: sigma^2 = 175.75 * mu_b^(-5.27) + 89.5, with a2 = 175.75 +/- 11.07, b2 = -5.27 +/- 1.02, c2 = 89.5 +/- 13.13
- Kurtosis is approximately constant at 0.004 +/- 0.002
- The relation between baryon chemical potential and center-of-mass energy: mu_b = 1.31 GeV / (1 + 0.27 * sqrt(s)), with uncertainties 0.03 GeV and 0.01 GeV^-1 respectively
- The integral of variance reproducing mean multiplicity uses parameters alpha1 = 25.94 +/- 3.8, beta1 = 58.6 +/- 3.7, gamma1 = -5.27 +/- 1.024, delta1 = 11
- The resulting pressure shows a sharp decrease when sqrt(s) reaches approximately 1-2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the program of using higher-order moments of conserved quantities in heavy-ion collisions, as measured by STAR at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2014), to extract thermodynamic information. It builds on lattice QCD calculations at finite baryon chemical potential (Fodor, 2012) and on statistical-thermal approaches that have been compared with lattice results (Tawfik, 2004; Karsch, 2003). The method enables a potential bridge between experimental measurements and lattice QCD simulations for the equation of state, though the author notes that direct comparison requires resolving the formation time of the quark-gluon plasma and the difficulty of lattice calculations at finite baryon chemical potential.
