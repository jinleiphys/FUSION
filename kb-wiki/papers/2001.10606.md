# Cross-conductivity: novel transport coefficients to constrain the hadronic degrees of freedom of nuclear matter

> In general, the constituents of the bulk matter produced in heavy-ion collisions carry, besides electric charge, multiple other conserved quantum numbers like baryon number and strangeness. Therefore, an electric field will not only generate an electric current but, at the same time, also currents in baryon number and strangeness. We propose that the impact of the electric field on these conserved currents should be characterized by additional transport coefficients, which we call cross-conductivities. In this paper, we introduce and present a calculation of these cross-conductivities from the Green-Kubo formalism within the transport code SMASH for different chemical compositions of hadron resonance gases. We find that the coefficients underlie an ordering in the active degrees of freedom and that thus the chemical composition of the system plays a crucial role. Further, we argue that in future comparisons of lattice QCD calculations with these findings, one could constrain which degrees of freedom and their corresponding charge properties are relevant for the QCD dynamics of the system.

## Key claim

The paper introduces two new transport coefficients, baryon-electric conductivity and strange-electric conductivity, that quantify how an electric field generates baryon-number and strangeness currents in a hadron gas, and argues that these coefficients are sensitive to the active hadronic degrees of freedom, such that future lattice QCD comparisons could constrain which hadronic states are relevant for QCD dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the Green-Kubo formalism within the SMASH transport code to compute the electric, baryon-electric, and strange-electric conductivities from equilibrium current-current correlation functions in a periodic box. They simulated three hadron resonance gases of increasing complexity: a pion-kaon-nucleon gas (with both hard-sphere and resonance interactions), a gas adding lambdas, and the full SMASH hadron gas containing all particles from the PDG 2019 recension up to masses of about 2.3 GeV. The Green-Kubo results were calibrated against semi-analytical Chapman-Enskog calculations for the simplest gas to determine systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Systematic uncertainties were established as 10% for the electric conductivity, 20% for the baryonic-electric conductivity, and 15% for the strange-electric conductivity, based on calibration calculations in a pion-kaon-nucleon gas with a constant cross-section of 30 mb.
- The electric conductivity decreases with temperature and plateaus around T = 150 MeV for all gases, with the full hadron gas showing the lowest values at every temperature.
- The baryonic-electric conductivity increases with temperature, with a clear separation between the full hadron gas and simpler gases above T = 160 MeV.
- The strange-electric conductivity rises rapidly at low temperature and appears to plateau around T = 120 MeV for all three gases, with the full hadron gas remaining lower than the simpler mixtures.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electrical conductivity in hadron gases (Greif 2016, Cassing 2013, Steinert 2013) by introducing cross-conductivities for baryon number and strangeness, for which no prior calculations existed. It builds on the Green-Kubo methodology applied to shear viscosity (Rose 2017) and electric conductivity (Hammelmann 2018), and relates the new coefficients to the diffusion coefficient matrix from Greif (2017) and Fotakis (2019). The results provide input for hydrodynamic simulations with multiple conserved charges and, if compared to future lattice QCD calculations, could help identify missing hadronic states and constrain the strength of hadronic interactions.
