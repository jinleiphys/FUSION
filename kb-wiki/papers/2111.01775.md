# Hadronic structure on the light-front II: QCD strings, Wilson lines and potentials

> This is the second paper on hadronic wave functions in the light front formulation. We first consider only confinement effects, using the classical Nambu-Gotto string with massive end points in the light cone gauge. We derive the light-front Hamiltonian and show how to solve it, using an expansion in suitable basis functions. We next discuss the correlators of Wilson lines on the light front, leading to an effective Hamiltonian that includes spin effects. At the end, we consider a separate problem of instanton-induced interactions, at the origin of the pion as a massless Goldstone mode on the light front.

## Key claim

The paper derives light-front Hamiltonians and wave functions for mesons starting from the classical Nambu-Goto string with massive endpoints, but the resulting squared mass operator is iterative and non-local, requiring the einbein trick and minimization to produce a numerically diagonalizable matrix, with the caveat that different states are not eigenstates of the same Hamiltonian unless parameters are fixed globally.

## Method

The authors first consider confinement using the Nambu-Goto string action with massive endpoints in the light-cone gauge, deriving the light-front Hamiltonian in 1+1 dimensions (leading to the 't Hooft equation) and then in 1+3 dimensions. They linearize the square-root operator using the einbein trick with auxiliary parameters a and b, represent the Hamiltonian as a matrix in an orthonormal basis of functions (transverse oscillator and sine basis in x), and diagonalize a 12x12 truncation to obtain masses and light-front wave functions. They also discuss Wilson line correlators on the light front and instanton-induced interactions for spin effects and the pion as a Goldstone mode.

## Key numbers

* String tension: σ_T = (0.42 GeV)^2, corresponding to approximately 1 GeV/fm.
* Constituent quark mass used: m_Q = 0.35 GeV.
* For the 1+1 dimensional 't Hooft equation, the semi-classical spectrum gives M_n^2 = n/α' with α' = 1/(2πσ_T) in the massless limit, and for massive quarks M_n^2 ≈ n/α' + 2m_Q^2 ln n.
* For heavy mesons on the light front, the radial excitation spectrum follows M_{H,n0}^2 ≈ (2m_Q)^2 + (6m_Q/α')^(2/3) n^(2/3).
* The lowest state light-front wave function is given explicitly with coefficients (e.g., 0.831 for the sin(πx) term, -0.0252 for sin(3πx), etc.) and oscillator parameter β = (4a/σ_T b)^(1/4) with a = 2.36 used for the numerical diagonalization.

## Context

This paper is the second in a series on hadronic wave functions in the light-front formulation, extending the authors' previous work on central and spin-dependent potentials in the center-of-mass frame (Shuryak 2021). It builds on the 't Hooft equation (1974) for 1+1 dimensional QCD and the transverse oscillator basis approach of Jia and Vary (2009, 2018), while also connecting to the soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Karch (2006) and Brodsky's light-front holography (2014). The work enables systematic derivation of light-front wave functions from first-principles nonperturbative dynamics, with future generalization to full spin and angular momentum structures planned in subsequent papers.
