# Accessing chiral-even quark generalised parton distributions in the exclusive photoproduction of a $ γπ^{\pm} $ pair with large invariant mass in both fixed-target and collider experiments

> We compute the exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,π^\pm$ pair using the collinear factorisation framework, in the kinematic regime where the pair has a large invariant mass. This exclusive channel presents a new avenue for the investigation of GPDs. It is particularly interesting as the high centre of mass energies available at future experiments will allow the study of GPDs at small skewness $ ξ$. We compute the scattering amplitude of the process, at leading twist and leading order in $α_s$, which is used to estimate its cross-section and linear polarisation asymmetries with respect to the incoming photon, for JLab~12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC and LHC (in ultra-peripheral collisions) kinematics. We find that the order of magnitude of estimates are sufficiently large for a dedicated experimental analysis to be performed, especially at JLab. We also compare the results from an asymptotic distribution amplitude (DA) to those using a recently proposed \textit{holographic} DA.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma \pi^{\pm}$ pair with large invariant mass provides a new avenue for accessing chiral-even quark GPDs at small skewness $\xi$, with cross-section estimates sufficiently large for dedicated experimental analysis, especially at JLab, provided the kinematic cuts $-u',-t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$ are enforced to ensure collinear factorisation holds.

## Method

The authors compute the scattering amplitude at leading twist and leading order in $\alpha_s$ using collinear QCD factorisation, decomposing the hard part into gauge-invariant blocks of Feynman diagrams. The amplitude is expressed in terms of Compton form factors built from GPDs (parametrised via double distributions with GRV-98 and GRSV-2000 PDFs) and pion distribution amplitudes (asymptotic and holographic forms), with the $z$ integration performed analytically and the $x$ integration performed numerically. The fully-differential cross-section and linear polarisation asymmetries are then estimated for JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC, and LHC ultra-peripheral collision kinematics.

## Key numbers

* Cross-section estimates for JLab 12-GeV kinematics are sufficiently large for a dedicated experimental analysis, with the order of magnitude of estimates being "sufficiently large" (no explicit numerical cross-section value given in the truncated text).
* The hard scale condition requires $M^2_{\gamma\pi} > 1$ GeV$^2$, enforced by cuts $-u',-t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$.
* The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 131$ MeV.
* The fixed factorisation scale is $\mu_F^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$.
* The asymptotic distribution amplitude is $\phi^{\rm as}(z) = 6z(1-z)$; the holographic DA is $\phi^{\rm hol}(z) = \frac{8}{\pi}\sqrt{z(1-z)}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Duplancic 2018) by expanding kinematics from JLab to COMPASS, EIC, and LHC ultra-peripheral collisions, adding results for the holographic distribution amplitude, and computing linear polarisation asymmetries in addition to unpolarised cross-sections. It builds on the factorisation framework established for $2\to 3$ exclusive processes (Ivanov 2002, Enberg 2006, Pire 2019) and recent proofs of collinear factorisation for similar processes (Qiu 2022). The results enable future experimental studies of GPDs at small skewness $\xi$ at multiple facilities, particularly JLab, and provide a basis for next-to-leading order computations.
