# Associated Quarkonium Hadroproduction at High-Energy Colliders

> Quarkonium production in proton-proton collision is interesting in profiling the partons inside the nucleon. Recently, the impact of double parton scatterings (DPSs) was suggested by experimental data of associated quarkonium production (J/psi+Z, J/psi+W, and J/psi+J/psi) at the LHC and Tevatron, in addition to single parton scatterings (SPSs). In this proceedings contribution, we review the extraction of the effective parameter of the DPS through the evaluation of the SPS contributions under quark-hadron duality.

# Associated Quarkonium Hadroproduction at High-Energy Colliders

## Key claim

The single parton scattering (SPS) contributions to associated quarkonium production (J/psi+Z, J/psi+W, and J/psi+J/psi) computed in the color evaporation model (CEM) are systematically smaller than the measured total cross sections, implying that double parton scatterings (DPS) fill the gap; however, the CEM predictions overshoot experimental data at high transverse momentum, so the SPS contributions can only be treated as conservative upper limits rather than precise predictions.

## Method

The authors computed the SPS contributions to J/psi+Z and J/psi+W production at next-to-leading order (NLO) in alpha_s using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, and to di-J/psi production at leading order (LO), all within the color evaporation model (CEM) where quarkonium is produced from a Q-Qbar pair with invariant mass below the open-heavy flavor threshold. The DPS cross section was parametrized using the effective parameter sigma_eff, assuming two uncorrelated parton scatterings, and sigma_eff was extracted by attributing the difference between the measured total cross section and the computed SPS contribution to DPS.

## Key numbers

- J/psi+Z production: ATLAS measured total cross section = 1.6 ± 0.4 pb; NLO CEM SPS prediction = 0.19^{+0.05}_{-0.04} pb
- J/psi+W production: ATLAS measured total cross section = 4.5^{+1.9}_{-1.5} pb; NLO CEM SPS prediction = 0.28 ± 0.07 pb
- Extracted sigma_eff from J/psi+Z: 4.7^{+2.4}_{-1.5} mb
- Extracted sigma_eff from J/psi+W: 6.1^{+3.3}_{-1.9} mb
- Extracted sigma_eff from di-J/psi (CMS, 7 TeV): 8.2 ± 2.0 (stat) ± 2.9 (sys) mb
- Extracted sigma_eff from di-J/psi (D0): 4.8 ± 0.5 (stat) ± 2.5 (sys) mb
- Extracted sigma_eff from di-J/psi (ATLAS): 6.3 ± 1.6 (stat) ± 1.0 (sys) mb
- Universal CEM parameter for prompt J/psi: P = 0.014 (LO), 0.009 (NLO)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of associated quarkonium production in NRQCD (Li 2010, Mao 2011, Gong 2012, Lansberg 2013, Shao 2016) which had difficulties explaining yields in several phase-space regions, and complements experimental measurements by ATLAS of J/psi+W (2014) and J/psi+Z (2014) and by CMS, D0, ATLAS, and LHCb of di-J/psi production. The extracted sigma_eff values from quarkonium-associated processes are consistent with each other and with extractions from other central rapidity quarkonium data, enabling future studies of multiparton interactions and nucleon structure through double parton scattering.
