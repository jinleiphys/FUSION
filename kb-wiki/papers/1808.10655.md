# Accretion heated atmospheres of X-ray bursting neutron stars

> Some thermonuclear (type I) X-ray bursts at the neutron star surfaces in low-mass X-ray binaries take place during hard persistent states of the systems. Spectral evolution of these bursts is well described by the atmosphere model of a passively cooling neutron star when the burst luminosity is high enough. The observed spectral evolution deviates from the model predictions when the burst luminosity drops below a critical value of 20-70% of the maximum luminosity. We suggest that these deviations are induced by the additional heating of the accreted particles. We present a method for computation of the neutron star atmosphere models heated by accreted particles assuming that their energy is released via Coulomb interactions with electrons. We compute the temperature structures and the emergent spectra of the atmospheres of various chemical compositions and investigate the dependence of the results on the other model parameters. We show that the heated atmosphere develops the hot (20--100 keV) corona-like surface layer cooled by Compton scattering, and the deeper, almost isothermal optically thick region with a temperature of a few keV. The emergent spectra deviate strongly from those of undisturbed neutron star atmospheres, with the main differences being the presence of a high-energy tail and a strong excess in the low-energy part of the spectrum. They also lack the iron absorption edge, which is visible in the spectra of undisturbed low-luminosity atmospheres with solar chemical composition. Using the computed spectra, we obtained the dependences of the dilution and color-correction factors as functions of relative luminosities for pure helium and solar abundance atmospheres. We show that the helium model atmosphere heated by accretion corresponding to 5% of the Eddington luminosity describes well the late stages of the X-ray bursts in 4U 1820-30.

## Key claim

The observed spectral deviations of type I X-ray bursts from passively cooling neutron star atmosphere models at low burst luminosities (below 20-70% of maximum) are caused by additional heating of accreted particles, with the critical luminosity and deviation amplitude correlating with the persistent luminosity of the system.

## Method

The authors computed steady-state neutron star atmosphere models in plane-parallel approximation with additional heating from supra-thermal accreted particles, assuming energy is released via Coulomb interactions with electrons. They solved the radiative transfer equation with Compton scattering, hydrostatic equilibrium including ram pressure, and energy balance equations iteratively for atmospheres of pure helium and solar chemical composition. The deceleration of fast particles (protons and alpha-particles) was computed using the slowing-down timescale from Coulomb interactions, with the accreted particles following a relativistic Maxwell-Juttner distribution characterized by bulk velocity, temperature, and incidence angle parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical burst luminosity where deviations begin: 20-70% of the maximum luminosity
- Persistent luminosity for 4U 1820-30: approximately 0.07 L_Edd (Eddington luminosity)
- Persistent luminosity for comparison bursts: approximately 0.01 L_Edd
- Hot corona-like surface layer temperature: 20-100 keV
- Deeper isothermal region temperature: a few keV
- Helium model atmosphere heated by accretion corresponding to 5% of the Eddington luminosity describes late stages of X-ray bursts in 4U 1820-30
- Neutron star mass used: 1.663 M_sun
- Neutron star radius used: 12 km
- Surface gravity: log g = 14.3
- Free-fall velocity at surface: 1.92 x 10^10 cm/s = 0.64 c
- Virial temperature at surface: approximately 67 MeV times average ion mass in proton mass units
- Eddington luminosity: approximately 3.8 x 10^38 (1+X)^(-1) erg/s, where X is hydrogen mass fraction

## Context

This work extends earlier models of accretion-heated neutron star atmospheres by Alme and Wilson (1973) and Deufel, Dullemond, and Spruit (2001), and the undisturbed neutron star atmosphere models by Suleimanov, Poutanen, and Werner (2011, 2012). Unlike previous work focused on pure hydrogen atmospheres heated by protons, this paper considers arbitrary chemical compositions and mixtures of protons and alpha-particles. The models are directly applicable to interpreting hard-state X-ray bursts in low-mass X-ray binaries, particularly the cooling tail method and direct spectral fitting approaches used by Kajava et al. (2014), Poutanen et al. (2014), and Nattila et al. (2017).
