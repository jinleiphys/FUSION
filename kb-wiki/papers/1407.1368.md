# Relativistic description of nuclear matrix elements in neutrinoless double-$־²$ decay

> Neutrinoless double-$־²$ ($0־½־²־²$) decay is related to many fundamental concepts in nuclear and particle physics beyond the standard model. Currently there are many experiments searching for this weak process. An accurate knowledge of the nuclear matrix element for the $0־½־²־²$ decay is essential for determining the effective neutrino mass once this process is eventually measured. We report the first full relativistic description of the $0־½־²־²$ decay matrix element based on a state-of-the-art nuclear structure model. We adopt the full relativistic transition operators which are derived with the charge-changing nucleonic currents composed of the vector coupling, axial-vector coupling, pseudoscalar coupling, and weak-magnetism coupling terms. The wave functions for the initial and final nuclei are determined by the multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) based on the point-coupling functional PC-PK1. The low-energy spectra and electric quadrupole transitions in ${}^{150}$Nd and its daughter nucleus ${}^{150}$Sm are well reproduced by the MR-CDFT calculations. The $0־½־²־²$ decay matrix elements for both the $0_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+$ and $0_1^+\rightarrow 0_2^+$ decays of ${}^{150}$Nd are evaluated. The effects of particle number projection, static and dynamic deformations, and the full relativistic structure of the transition operators on the matrix elements are studied in detail. The resulting $0־½־²־²$ decay matrix element for the $0_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+$ transition is $5.60$, which gives the most optimistic prediction for the next generation of experiments searching for the $0־½־²־²$ decay in ${}^{150}$Nd.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first full relativistic description of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element, using multireference covariant density functional theory with the point-coupling functional PC-PK1. The authors note that the resulting matrix element of 5.60 for the ground-state to ground-state transition in Nd-150 represents the "most optimistic prediction" among existing calculations, implying that other theoretical approaches yield smaller values.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations to generate intrinsic states with different quadrupole deformations for Nd-150 and Sm-150. They then applied particle number projection, one-dimensional angular momentum projection, and the generator coordinate method to construct the wave functions of the initial and final 0+ states. The full relativistic transition operators derived from charge-changing nucleonic currents (vector, axial-vector, pseudoscalar, and weak-magnetism couplings) were used directly between Dirac spinor wave functions without nonrelativistic reduction.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear matrix element for the 0_1+ to 0_1+ transition in Nd-150 is 5.60 (dimensionless, using the standard normalization with R = 1.2 * A^(1/3) fm).
* The endpoint energy Q_beta_beta for Nd-150 is 3.37 MeV, giving it the second highest endpoint energy and the largest phase-space factor G_0nu among decay candidates.
* The empirical closure energy E_d = 1.12 * A^(1/2) MeV = 13.72 MeV was used, with variations between 8 and 20 MeV producing changes of less than 10% in the matrix element.
* The pairing strength constants were set to V_n = -314.55 MeV fm^3 for neutrons and V_p = -346.5 MeV fm^3 for protons.
* The axial-vector coupling constant was set to g_A(0) = 1.254 (not renormalized).

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory framework, previously applied to ground-state properties and single-beta decay, to the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element calculation. It provides a direct comparison with nonrelativistic energy density functional calculations by Rodriguez and Martinez-Pinedo (2010) for the same nuclei Nd-150 and Sm-150, and with projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Rath et al. (2010, 2013). The relativistic approach automatically includes the tensorial part of the transition operator from high-order currents, which is treated approximately in nonrelativistic reductions. The results enable more reliable extraction of the effective neutrino mass once experimental half-lives are measured, and provide a benchmark for testing the validity of nonrelativistic approximations used in other methods.
