# Contact potentials in presence of a regular finite-range interaction using dimensional regularization and the $N/D$ method

> We solve the Lippman-Schwinger equation (LSE) with a kernel that includes a regular finite-range potential and additional contact terms with derivatives. We employ distorted wave theory and dimensional regularization, as proposed in Physics Letters B 568 (2003) 109. We analyze the spin singlet nucleon-nucleon $S-$wave as case of study, with the regular one-pion exchange (OPE) potential in this partial wave and up to ${\cal O}(Q^6)$ (six derivatives) contact interactions. We discuss in detail the renormalization of the LSE, and show that the scattering amplitude solution of the LSE fulfills exact elastic unitarity and inherits the left-hand cut of the long-distance OPE amplitude. Furthermore, we proof that the LSE amplitude coincides with that obtained from the exact $N/D$ calculation, with the appropriate number and typology of subtractions to reproduce the effective range parameters taken as input to renormalize the LSE amplitude. The generalization to higher number of derivatives is straightforward.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the scattering amplitude obtained from solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a kernel consisting of a regular finite-range potential plus contact terms with an arbitrary number of derivatives coincides exactly with the amplitude from the N/D method using the appropriate number and typology of subtractions, provided the effective range parameters used as input are the same. This equivalence holds for the spin-singlet nucleon-nucleon S-wave with one-pion exchange as the long-range potential and contact interactions up to O(Q^6).

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using distorted wave theory and dimensional regularization, following the approach of Nieves (2003). They separate the potential into a long-range one-pion exchange part and a short-range separable contact potential with terms up to six derivatives (O(Q^6)). The on-shell T-matrix is obtained algebraically by exploiting the separable nature of the short-range potential. Separately, they implement the exact N/D method with multiple subtractions, where the left-hand cut discontinuity is obtained from an integral equation that is always finite, and the right-hand cut is fixed by unitarity. The equivalence between the two approaches is proven analytically.

## Key numbers

- One-pion exchange parameters: m_pi = 138 MeV, f_pi = 93 MeV, g_A = 1.25, M_N = 938.919 MeV, hbar c = 197.327 MeV fm
- The OPE potential in the ^1S0 partial wave is given by V_pi(p',p) = -alpha_pi (M_N/2pi) (1/(p'p)) log((p_+^2+m_pi^2)/(p_-^2+m_pi^2)) with alpha_pi = g_A^2 m_pi^2/(16 pi f_pi^2)
- The effective range expansion coefficients for the OPE-only amplitude (v_i^pi) include: v_0^pi = 0.113860 x 10^1 fm^{-1}, v_1^pi = 0.618830 x 10^1 fm, v_2^pi = -0.836529 x 10^1 fm^3
- The expansion coefficients a_i and b_i (defined in Eq. defab) are tabulated for i=0 through 9, with a_0 = 0.222872 x 10^6 MeV^2 and b_0 = -0.991969 x 10^3 MeV
- The left-hand cut of the full T-matrix starts at k^2 = -m_pi^2/4

## Context

This work extends the dimensional regularization approach of Kaplan (1996) and Nieves (2003) for the nucleon-nucleon system with one-pion exchange and contact interactions, showing that the LSE solution with up to six-derivative contact terms is equivalent to the exact N/D method with multiple subtractions developed by Entem (2016) and Oller (2018). The result enables a systematic power-counting scheme that simultaneously handles the chiral order of the long-distance potential and the number of derivatives in the short-distance interaction, without requiring an ultraviolet cutoff. The generalization to higher numbers of derivatives is straightforward, and the approach connects to ongoing work on non-regular long-range interactions.
