# Connection between Distribution and Fragmentation Functions

> We show that the quark fragmentation function $D(z)$ and the quark distribution function $q(x)$ are connected in the $z \to 1$ limit by the approximate relation $D(z)/z \simeq q(2-1/z)$, where both quantities are in their physical regions. Predictions for proton production in inelastic $e^+ e^-$ annihilation, based on the new relation and standard parametrizations of quark distribution functions, are found to be compatible with the data.

## Key claim

The authors derive an approximate relation connecting the quark fragmentation function \(D(z)\) and the quark distribution function \(q(x)\) in their physical regions, specifically \(D(z)/z \simeq q(2-1/z)\) for \(z \to 1\), which is intended to be valid at a fixed and small scale \(\mu^2 < 1\) GeV\(^2\) and is presented as a more justified alternative to the traditional Gribov-Lipatov relation.

## Method

The authors start from the general definitions of the quark distribution and fragmentation functions and apply crossing symmetry to obtain the Drell-Levy-Yan relation \(D(z)/z = q(1/z)\), which connects the physical region of one process to the unphysical region of the other. They then argue that in the large-\(x\) (or large-\(z\)) limit, the quark distribution becomes approximately invariant under the substitution \(x \to 2-x\) because the matrix elements in the distribution function become \(x\)-independent when the spectator momentum is large, leading to \(q(1/z) \simeq q(2-1/z)\) and thus the new relation. The validity of the approximation is tested using a quark-diquark light-cone model with Gaussian and power-law wave functions, and the resulting predictions for the IA structure function \(z^3\bar{F}_2(z)\) are compared to DASP data at \(Q^2=13\) GeV\(^2\) using the CTEQ5L parametrization for the DIS structure functions.

## Key numbers

* The relation \(D(z)/z \simeq q(2-1/z)\) is intended to be valid at a fixed and small scale \(\mu^2 < 1\) GeV\(^2\).
* The quark-diquark model calculations use parameters \(m_q=220\) MeV, \(\beta=450\) MeV, \(m_D=800\) MeV, and \(a=3.5\) for the power-law wave function.
* The DASP data on \(z^3\bar{F}_2(z)\) used for comparison are at \(Q^2=13\) GeV\(^2\).
* The new relation is applicable for \(z \ge 0.5\), connecting the physical regions of DIS and IA.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the connection between deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and inelastic annihilation (IA) structure functions, which was previously addressed by the Drell-Levy-Yan relation (Drell, Levy, and Yan, 1970) and the Gribov-Lipatov reciprocity relation (Gribov and Lipatov, 1972). The authors show that the traditional Gribov-Lipatov relation, which assumes \(3 D(z) = q(z)\) at a nonperturbative scale, is unjustified and not supported by data, whereas their new relation provides better agreement with DASP data at large \(z\). The relation enables predictions of quark fragmentation functions from well-known quark distribution functions, with an immediate application to studying \(\Lambda\) polarization near the \(Z\) resonance in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and in polarized lepton DIS scattering, where earlier work using the Gribov-Lipatov relation (Ma, 2000) was not satisfactory.
