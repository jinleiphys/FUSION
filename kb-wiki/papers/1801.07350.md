# Pulsar glitch and nuclear EoS: Applicability of superfluid model

> In this contribution, we have made use of the contemporary equation of states (EOSs) for the complete neutron star structure, and confronted them with one particular glitch constrain for the crustal moment of inertia (MOI). We find that with these EOSs, the radii of three millisecond pulsars selected by NICER: PSR J0437-4715, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0751+1807, are all around 12.5 km. Also, a star with $M \lesssim 1.55 M_{\odot}$ would fulfill the MOI calculation for glitch constrain with the latest neutron superfluidity density, and the glitch crisis might not be present.

## Key claim
Using contemporary unified neutron star equations of state (BCPM, BSk20, BSk21), the radii of three millisecond pulsars selected by NICER are all around 12.5 km, and a star with mass less than or approximately 1.55 solar masses would satisfy the crustal moment of inertia constraint for glitches when the latest neutron superfluidity density is used, potentially resolving the "glitch crisis."

## Method
The authors selected three unified neutron star equations of state (BCPM, BSk20, BSk21) that satisfy experimental constraints from nuclear physics and astrophysics. They computed mass-radius relations using the static Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation under the slow-rotation approximation, justified because the pulsars' rotational frequencies (173.6 Hz, 317.5 Hz, 287.4 Hz) are much smaller than the Kepler frequency predictions (above 1 kHz). They then compared the resulting fractional crustal moment of inertia against glitch constraints from the Vela pulsar, incorporating updated calculations of neutron superfluid density from Watanabe and Pethick (2017).

## Key numbers
- Radii of three millisecond pulsars (PSR J0437-4715, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0751+1807) are all around 12.5 km based on the BSk21 equation of state.
- Rotational frequencies of the three pulsars: 173.6 Hz (PSR J0437-4715), 317.5 Hz (PSR J1614-2230), 287.4 Hz (PSR J0751+1807).
- The Vela pulsar glitch constraint on fractional crustal moment of inertia is I_c/I >= 1.6% from Link et al. (1999), later raised to approximately 7% by Andersson et al. (2012) and Chamel et al. (2013).
- With a typical neutron gap of Delta = 1-1.5 MeV, Watanabe and Pethick (2017) found conduction neutrons are 64%-71% of total unbound neutrons, yielding a new constraint I_c/I >= 2.5-2.3%.
- A neutron star with mass M less than or approximately 1.55 solar masses would satisfy the glitch constraint with the updated superfluid density.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate about the "glitch crisis" in the standard superfluid model for pulsar glitches, where earlier constraints from Andersson et al. (2012) and Chamel et al. (2013) raised the required fractional crustal moment of inertia to about 7%, making it difficult for nuclear equations of state to satisfy. By incorporating the updated neutron superfluid density calculations from Watanabe and Pethick (2017), which account for pairing effects and reduce the entrainment effect, the authors show that most known neutron stars with masses below 1.55 solar masses can satisfy the revised constraint. The study enables future work to examine the effects of pasta phases and strangeness phase transitions in the stellar core, as noted by Hooker et al. (2015) and Li et al. (2015), and further discussion by Chamel (2017) is needed to fully resolve the crisis.
