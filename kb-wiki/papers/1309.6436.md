# Dissipative preparation of phase- and number-squeezed states with ultracold atoms

> We develop a dissipative quantum state preparation scheme for the creation of phase- and number-squeezed states. It utilizes ultracold atoms in a double-well configuration immersed in a background Bose-Einstein condensate, with the latter consisting of an atom species different from the atoms in the double well and acting as a dissipative quantum reservoir. We derive a master equation for this system starting from microscopic physics, and show that squeezing develops on a time scale proportional to $1/N$, where $N$ is the number of particles in the double well. This scaling, caused by bosonic enhancement, allows us to make the time scale for the creation of squeezed states very short. The lifetime of squeezed states is limited by dephasing arising from the intrinsic structure of the setup. However, the dephasing can be avoided by stroboscopically switching the driving off and on. We show that this approach leads to robust stationary squeezed states. Finally, we provide the necessary ingredients for a potential experimental implementation by specifying a parameter regime for rubidium atoms that leads to squeezed states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a dissipative quantum state preparation scheme for phase- and number-squeezed states that works within a specific time window bounded by dephasing from the intrinsic energy spacing of the harmonic trap, but this dephasing can be avoided by stroboscopically switching the driving off and on to produce robust stationary squeezed states.

## Method

The authors model ultracold rubidium atoms in a double-well configuration immersed in a background Bose-Einstein condensate of a different atomic species, which acts as a zero-temperature reservoir of Bogoliubov excitations. They derive a master equation from microscopic physics using the Born-Markov approximation, then adiabatically eliminate the excited harmonic trap states to obtain an effective dissipative dynamics. The scheme uses two Raman laser configurations with antisymmetric and symmetric Rabi frequencies to couple the ground states of the wells to even- and odd-parity excited states of the harmonic trap, realizing the squeezing jump operators.

## Key numbers

* Squeezing develops on a time scale proportional to 1/N, where N is the number of particles in the double well; the effective squeezing rate is γ_eff = 4Nγ(1-ν²)
* The time window for squeezed state generation is τ_γ < t < T_ω, where τ_γ = 1/[4Nγ(1-ν²)] and T_ω = 2π/ω with ω = ε₂ - ε₁
* The steady-state squeezing measures are ξ^SS_P,N ≈ √[(1±ν)/(1∓ν)], where the upper sign corresponds to phase squeezing (ν<0) and the lower sign to number squeezing (ν>0)
* The parameter ν = (Ω₂/Ω₁)(Δ₁/Δ₂)ν̃, where ν̃ = φ_{e₂,0}(x₀)/φ_{e₁,0}(x₀)
* For the case where ε_n/ρ_bU_0 ≪ 1, η ≈ (ε₂/ε₁)³ = (1+ω/ε₁)³

## Context

This work extends the dissipative state preparation scheme of Diehl et al. (2008) by adding a second Λ-configuration that enables squeezing dynamics beyond the coherent state preparation achieved in that earlier work. The scheme builds on the squeezing jump operators proposed in a prior paper by the same authors (Caballar et al., 2013) and implements them in a physical ultracold atom setup. The approach enables rapid generation of squeezed states through bosonic enhancement and offers an alternative to unitary evolution and measurement-based methods for entanglement generation in atomic ensembles.
