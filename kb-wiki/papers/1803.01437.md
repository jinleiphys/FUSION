# The radius of a typical-mass neutron star and chiral effective field theory

> We calculate neutron star masses and radii from equations of state based on recent high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order of the chiral expansion and the leading chiral three- nucleon force. Our focus is on the radius of a 1.4 M_{Sun} neutron star, for which we report predictions that are consistent with the most recent constraints. We also show the full M(R) relations up to their respective maximum masses. Beyond the densities for which microscopic predictions are derived from chiral forces, the equations of state are obtained via polytropic continuations. However, the radius of a 1.4 M_{Sun} neutron star is nearly insensitive to the high-density extrapolation.

## Key claim

The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is predicted to lie between 10.8 and 12.8 km, based on chiral effective field theory forces up to fifth order, and this radius is nearly insensitive to the high-density polytropic extrapolation beyond about twice nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors computed equations of state for neutron-rich matter in beta equilibrium using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body calculations with chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order (N4LO) and the leading chiral three-nucleon force. For densities above approximately twice nuclear saturation density, they extended the microscopic predictions using piecewise polytropic continuations with adiabatic indices between 1.5 and 4.5, retaining only combinations that support a maximum neutron star mass of at least 1.97 solar masses. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and estimated the radius uncertainty by combining the spread from polytropic extensions with the truncation error between successive chiral orders.

## Key numbers

- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 10.8 to 12.8 km (estimated at N3LO, combining polytropic extension uncertainty and truncation error between N3LO and N4LO)
- Maximum mass constraint: at least 1.97 solar masses, based on the observed pulsar mass of 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis et al. 2013)
- Chiral NN potential at N4LO reproduces NN data with chi-squared per datum = 1.15
- Cutoff parameters used: Lambda = 450 MeV and 500 MeV
- Microscopic predictions applied up to approximately 2 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0)
- Central densities for the 1.4 solar mass star range from approximately 0.411 to 0.684 fm^{-3} (exceeding 3 rho_0 in some cases)
- For comparison: LIGO/Virgo constraint on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 11.1 to 13.4 km (90% confidence); constraint from Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown (2013): 10.4 to 12.9 km

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral EFT equation-of-state calculations by Sammarruca and collaborators (2015, 2018) using the newest N4LO potentials from Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner (2015), and applies them to neutron star structure for the first time with systematic uncertainty quantification. The results are consistent with astrophysical constraints from photospheric radius expansion measurements (Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown 2013) and from the LIGO/Virgo gravitational wave detection of the neutron star merger GW170817 (Abbott et al. 2017, 2018). The finding that the radius of a 1.4 solar mass star is determined primarily by the microscopic theory below twice saturation density, rather than by the high-density extrapolation, confirms earlier empirical correlations noted by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) and enables future work to focus on improving the low-to-moderate density equation of state from chiral forces.
