# Lightening-like interactions in nuclear collisions at CERN large hadron collider

> A simple basic model for describing proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions has been the intra-nuclear cascade model, where the interactions are simulated by a sequence of binary nucleon-nucleon (NN) collisions. This model helped to establish many scientific concepts and also creates the foundation for more modern simulation codes, especially at low and intermediate energies. In this paper, we present a new Monte Carlo model for pA and AA collisions at high CERN Large Hadron collider energies. The model implements HIJING code with a collective cascade recipe, that induces striking light-like effect in a large nucleus. A single collision (lightening) event is shown to be a complex process:A primary interacting nucleon passes its energy to the surrounding nucleons in a large nucleus. This new simulation code is shown to be good to reproduce the Large Hadron collider (LHC) data, especially the charged particle pseudorapidity density in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Monte Carlo model for proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies that supplements the HIJING code with a collective cascade recipe, inducing a "lightening-like effect" where a primary interacting nucleon passes its energy to surrounding nucleons. The model is shown to reproduce LHC charged particle pseudorapidity density data, but only when the cascade parameter C is set to 1 (full cascading) and the quark/gluon shadowing parameter is tuned to an impact-parameter-dependent form.

## Method

The authors modified the HIJING 1.0 code by replacing the old Duke-Owen parton distribution functions with modern MSTW2009 PDFs (calling this ImHIJING) and adding a collective cascade recipe. In this recipe, spectator nucleons within a mean interaction radius of 1.2 fm from a primary interacting nucleon become participants with probability φ = C exp(-r²_ij/r²_c), where C=1 for full cascading; this process repeats iteratively to include 3, 4, 5 or more nucleons in the interaction. The wounded nucleons are assigned momenta distributed according to a Gaussian law under energy-momentum conservation constraints, and the model predictions are compared to ALICE data for p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: the cut off parameter p_0 was fixed at 5.4 GeV/c; HIJING 1.0 calculations used p_0 = 2 GeV/c and parton shadowing parameter s_q(g) = 0.1.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: the cut off parameter p_0 was fixed at 4.5 GeV/c; the quark/gluon shadowing parameter was given by s_q(g)(c) = s_q(g) + 0.12c³ - 0.14c² - 0.0003c with s_q(g) = 0.1 and c = πb²/σ_in.
- The mean interaction radius for the cascade was r_c = 1.2 fm; the parameters d and ⟨p²_T⟩ were fixed at 0.5 and 0.5 (GeV/c)² respectively from analysis of p+Pb data.
- The model reproduces charged particle pseudorapidity density for p+Pb collisions and for Pb+Pb collisions across centrality classes from 0-5% to 20-30%.
- Transverse momentum distributions are described for p_T ≤ 6 GeV/c; for p_T > 6 GeV/c the model overestimates p+Pb data and slightly overestimates peripheral Pb+Pb data.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) by incorporating updated MSTW2009 parton distribution functions and a collective cascade recipe that produces a "lightening-like" effect, addressing shortcomings of previous codes that could not simultaneously describe LHC pseudorapidity density and transverse momentum data. The authors note that other models such as UrQMD, Color Glass Condensate-type models, and AMPT (which uses HIJING 2.0 as initial conditions) fail to reproduce the overall level and shape of ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions. The work enables further development of transport codes that include both initial-state parton shadowing and final-state interactions, as the authors identify that final-state parton rescattering (jet quenching) is needed to describe charged particle suppression at p_T > 6 GeV/c.
