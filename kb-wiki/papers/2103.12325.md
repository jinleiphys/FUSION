# Cross sections of solar neutrino capture by 127I nuclei and Gamow Teller resonances

> Solar neutrino capture cross-section by 127I nucleus has been studied with taking into account the influence of the resonance structure of the nuclear strength function S(E). Three types of isobaric resonances: giant Gamow-Teller, analog resonance and low-lying Gamow-Teller pigmy resonances has been investigated on the framework of self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems. The calculations have been performed considering the resonance structure of the charge-exchange strength function S(E). We analyze the effect of each resonance on the energy dependence of the cross-section. It has been shown that all high-lying resonances should be considered. Neutron emission process for high energy nuclear excitation leads to formation 126Xe isotope. We evaluate contribution from various sources of solar neutrinos to the 126Xe/127Xe isotopes ratio formed by energetic neutrinos. 126Xe/127Xe isotope ratio could be an indicator of high-energy boron neutrinos in the solar spectrum. We also discuss the uncertainties in the often used Fermi-functions calculations.

## Key claim

The authors show that all high-lying charge-exchange resonances (giant Gamow-Teller, analog, and pygmy resonances) must be included in calculations of the solar neutrino capture cross-section on 127I, and that the 126Xe/127Xe isotope ratio produced by neutron emission following capture can serve as an indicator of high-energy boron neutrinos in the solar spectrum.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge-exchange strength function S(E) for 127Xe using the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with Landau-Migdal local nucleon-nucleon interaction parameters f'0 = 1.351 and g'0 = 1.214. They fitted the resonance structure of S(E) using Breit-Wigner and Gaussian forms for the giant Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR), analog resonance (AR), and three pygmy resonances (PR1, PR2, PR3), then computed the neutrino capture cross-section sigma(E) for the reaction 127I(nu_e, e-)127Xe using the standard weak interaction formula with the Fermi function.

## Key numbers

* The threshold energy for the reaction 127I(nu_e, e-)127Xe is Q = 662.3 +/- 0.20 keV, lower than the 37Cl threshold of 813.87 +/- 0.20 keV.
* The neutron separation energy in 127Xe is S_n = 7246 +/- 5 keV; excited states above this energy decay by neutron emission to stable 126Xe.
* The total sum of B(GT) matrix elements up to 20 MeV from the 127I(p,n)127Xe experiment is 53.54 +/- 0.22, which is approximately 85% of the total GT sum rule 3(N-Z) = 63.
* The GTR peak energy is 14.9 MeV in both fits (experimental value 14.5 MeV); PR1 peak energy is 8.3 MeV (experimental value approximately 8.7 MeV).
* Disregarding GTR and PR1 reduces the cross-section by about 25% to about 80% as neutrino energy changes from 2 to 12 MeV; maximum deviation of 30% to 15% from the summed cross-section occurs at energies between 1.5 and 2.5 MeV.
* The contribution from the analog resonance to sigma(E) is less than 1%.
* The 126Xe isotope de-excitation from its low-lying 2+ state to the 0+ ground state emits a gamma ray with energy E_1 = 388.6 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of neutrino capture on 127I (Lutostansky and Shulgina, 1991; Lutostansky and Tikhonov, 2018) by including the formation of stable 126Xe via neutron emission from high-lying resonances, which was not considered in earlier studies. The calculations are compared with experimental data from the 127I(p,n)127Xe reaction by Palarczyk et al. (1999) and with prior theoretical work by Engel, Pittel, and Vogel (1991). The results enable the iodine-xenon radiochemical detector concept to distinguish the boron neutrino component of the solar spectrum through the 126Xe/127Xe isotope ratio and associated gamma-ray detection.
