# Total Cross Section of the Reaction pp \to pK^+ΛClose to Threshold

> The energy dependence of the total cross section for the pp \to pK^+Λreaction was measured in the threshold region covering the excess energy range up to 7MeV. Existing model calculations describe the slope of the measured cross sections well, but are too low by a factor of two to three in rate. The data were used for a precise determination of the beam momentum of the COSY-synchrotron.

## Key claim

The total cross section for the reaction pp → pK⁺Λ was measured in the threshold region up to 7 MeV excess energy, and while existing model calculations describe the slope of the measured cross sections well, they are too low by a factor of two to three in absolute rate.

## Method

The measurement was performed at the COSY-Jülich synchrotron using the internal target facility COSY-11. Outgoing protons and positively charged kaons were identified by particle momentum reconstruction combined with time-of-flight measurement, and the four-momentum of the unobserved Λ hyperon was calculated from conservation principles. The integrated luminosity was determined by normalizing simultaneously recorded pp elastic scattering to cross sections measured by the EDDA collaboration (1997). The geometrical acceptance was calculated using Monte-Carlo simulations based on GEANT-3, with three-body phase space folded with the proton-Λ final state interaction using the Jülich model A from Holzenkamp et al. (1989).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section at ε = 0.68 MeV: 2.1 ± 0.2 nb (statistical error only)
- Total cross section at ε = 1.68 MeV: 13.4 ± 0.7 nb
- Total cross section at ε = 2.68 MeV: 36.6 ± 2.6 nb
- Total cross section at ε = 3.68 MeV: 63.0 ± 3.1 nb (statistical) ± 14% (systematic)
- Total cross section at ε = 4.68 MeV: 92.2 ± 6.5 nb
- Total cross section at ε = 5.68 MeV: 135 ± 11 nb
- Total cross section at ε = 6.68 MeV: 164 ± 10 nb
- The ratio σ/ε² is approximately constant at 4.4 ± 0.7 nb/MeV² across the measured range
- The offset in excess energy determined from the data was Δε = 220 ± 60 keV, corresponding to a beam momentum shift of Δp = 660 ± 180 keV/c
- The proton-Λ scattering length used in the FSI correction was a = -1.6 fm with effective range r = 2.3 fm

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the pp → pK⁺Λ reaction by Balewski et al. (1996) and Fickinger et al. (1962), providing the first systematic excitation function in the threshold region. The data enable separation of the effects of the proton-Λ final state interaction and allow tests of meson exchange models of the nucleon-hyperon interaction, such as the Jülich model A from Holzenkamp et al. (1989). The results are important input for studies of hypernuclei production in nucleon-nucleus interactions (Rudy et al., 1996; Ohm et al., 1997) and for investigations of strangeness production in heavy ion collisions, which may provide information about hot, dense nuclear matter or the possible existence of a quark-gluon plasma.
