# Holographic Heavy-Light Chiral Effective Action

> We propose a variant of the $D4$-$D8$ construction to describe the low energy effective theory of heavy-light mesons, interacting with the lowest lying pseudoscalar and vector mesons. The heavy degrees of freedom are identified with the $D8_L$-$D8_H$ string low energy modes, and are approximated near the world volume of $N_f-1$ light $D8_L$ branes, by fundamental vector field valued in $U(N_f-1)$. The effective action follows from the reduction of the bulk D-brane Born-Infeld (DBI) and Chern-Simons (CS) actions, and is shown to exhibit both chiral and heavy-quark symmetry. The action interpolates continuously between the $U(N_f)$ case with massless mesons, and the $U(N_f-1)$ case with heavy-light mesons. The heavy-light meson radial spectrum is Regge-like. The one-pion and two-pion couplings to the heavy-light multiplets are evaluated. The partial widths for the charged decays $G\rightarrow H+π$ are shown to be comparable to the recently reported full widths for both the charm and bottom mesons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a holographic D4-D8 brane construction that yields a low-energy effective action for heavy-light mesons which simultaneously exhibits both chiral symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry, with the heavy-light meson radial spectrum being Regge-like. The construction interpolates continuously between the case of massless mesons (U(N_f) symmetry) and the case with heavy-light mesons (U(N_f-1) symmetry), but the heavy-light fields are approximated as local vector fields near the light brane world volume rather than treated as fully bi-local objects.

## Method

The authors construct a variant of the Sakai-Sugimoto D4-D8 holographic model using (N_f-1) light D8 probe branes and one heavy D8 probe brane in a cigar-shaped geometry that spontaneously breaks chiral symmetry. The heavy-light mesons are identified with the low-energy modes of strings stretched between the light and heavy branes, approximated near the light brane world volume by fundamental vector fields valued in U(N_f-1). The effective action is derived by expanding the bulk Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) and Chern-Simons (CS) actions, with the heavy-light meson mass arising from a Higgs-like mechanism through a vev of the dilaton moduli fields that corresponds to the brane separation.

## Key numbers

* The heavy-light meson mass spectrum follows m_n^2 = M^2 + M * (2 m_rho^2 / 0.67)^(1/2) * (n + 1/2), where M is the heavy quark mass and m_rho = sqrt(0.67) M_KK is the rho meson mass.
* The mass splitting between the odd-parity H-multiplet (n=0) and even-parity G-multiplet (n=1) is Delta m = m_G - m_H ≈ m_rho / (sqrt(2) * sqrt(0.67)) ≈ 665 MeV in the large M limit.
* The reported mean splitting is 420 MeV for charm and 396 MeV for bottom mesons (CLEOII collaboration).

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto D4-D8 holographic model (SSX) for light mesons to include heavy-light mesons, combining chiral symmetry with heavy-quark symmetry in a holographic framework. The approach is distinct from earlier holographic descriptions of heavy-light mesons (FewX, Brodsky) that did not incorporate the strictures of chiral symmetry. The results enable further calculations of heavy-light meson decay widths and chiral couplings, with the partial widths for charged decays G → H + π reported to be comparable to the full widths for both charm and bottom mesons observed by the Belle and BESIII collaborations.
