# Momentum transport properties of a hot and dense QCD matter in a weak magnetic field

> We have studied the momentum transport properties of a hot and dense QCD matter in the presence of weak magnetic field by determining the shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities in the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory. The dependence of $η$ and $ζ$ on the temperature has been explored in the presence of weak magnetic field ($B$-field) and finite chemical potential ($μ$). It is observed that both shear and bulk viscosities get decreased in the presence of a weak magnetic field, whereas the finite chemical potential increases these viscosities, specifically at low temperatures. This study is important to understand the sound attenuation through the Prandtl number (Pr), the nature of the flow through the Reynolds number (Re), the fluidity and location of transition point of the matter through the ratios $η/s$ and $ζ/s$ ($s$ is the entropy density), respectively. The Prandtl number is observed to increase in the weak magnetic field, whereas the presence of a finite chemical potential reduces its magnitude as compared to the scenario of absence of $B$-field and $μ$. However, Pr still remains larger than unity, indicating that the energy dissipation due to the sound attenuation is mostly governed by the momentum diffusion. It is noticed that the weak magnetic field makes the Reynolds number larger, whereas the chemical potential makes it smaller than that in the absence of $B$-field and $μ$. We have observed that the ratio $η/s$ decreases in the weak magnetic field regime, whereas the finite chemical potential increases its value, but the ratio $ζ/s$ is found to decrease in the presence of weak magnetic field as well as finite chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a hot and dense QCD matter, both shear and bulk viscosities decrease in the presence of a weak magnetic field (where the temperature scale dominates over the magnetic field scale, \(T^2 \gg |q_fB|\)), while a finite chemical potential increases these viscosities specifically at low temperatures; the Prandtl number remains larger than unity in all cases, indicating that sound attenuation is governed primarily by momentum diffusion.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation using an ansatz method in the weak magnetic field limit, where terms containing the cyclotron frequency \(\omega_c\) and its higher orders were neglected. They employed a quasiparticle model incorporating interactions among medium constituents through thermal masses, and considered three quark flavors (u, d, s) plus gluons. The shear and bulk viscosities were extracted by comparing the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor with its definition in terms of velocity gradients, and the Landau-Lifshitz frame condition was applied to fix the bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity \(\eta\) and bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) both decrease in the presence of a weak magnetic field (condition \(T^2 \gg |q_fB|\)).
- Finite chemical potential increases \(\eta\) and \(\zeta\) specifically at low temperatures.
- Prandtl number (Pr) increases in a weak magnetic field and decreases with finite chemical potential, but remains larger than unity in all cases.
- Reynolds number (Re) becomes larger in a weak magnetic field and smaller with finite chemical potential compared to the case without magnetic field and chemical potential.
- The ratio \(\eta/s\) decreases in the weak magnetic field regime, while finite chemical potential increases its value.
- The ratio \(\zeta/s\) decreases in the presence of both weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential.
- The magnetic field strength at RHIC is between \(m_\pi^2\) (10^18 Gauss) and 15 \(m_\pi^2\) at LHC, and may reach 50 \(m_\pi^2\) in extreme cases (from the introduction, not original numerical results of this work).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of viscosities in QCD matter by incorporating both a weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential, building on earlier calculations using the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (Danielewicz 1985, Heckmann 2012, Yasui 2017) and the Green-Kubo formalism (Kharzeev 2008, Moore 2008). It contrasts with strong magnetic field studies (Hattori 2017, Li 2018, Rath 2020) where the lowest Landau level approximation is used, and with hadronic matter calculations (Das 2019). The results enable more realistic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as at future facilities like FAIR and NICA where high baryon densities are expected.
