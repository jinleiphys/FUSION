# Triple-Gluon and Triple-Quark Elastic Scatterings and Early Thermalization

> Three-gluon to three-gluon scatterings lead to rapid thermalization of gluon matter created in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies. Thermalization of quark matter is studied from three-quark to three-quark scatterings.

# Triple-Gluon and Triple-Quark Elastic Scatterings and Early Thermalization

## Key claim

Three-gluon to three-gluon elastic scatterings, rather than the more commonly considered two-gluon scatterings, are the dominant mechanism driving rapid thermalization of gluon matter created in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, provided the gluon number density is sufficiently high (above 30 fm⁻³). For quark matter, three-quark scatterings produce a variation in the quark distribution function comparable to that from two-quark scatterings, but quark matter alone does not thermalize rapidly.

## Method

The author derived squared amplitudes for all tree-level diagrams of three-gluon to three-gluon elastic scatterings at order αₛ⁴, including interference terms, using Fortran code in the Feynman gauge. For three-quark to three-quark elastic scatterings, 42 diagrams (for same-flavor quarks) and 14 diagrams (for one quark flavor differing from the other two) were computed. A Boltzmann-type transport equation including both 2→2 and 3→3 parton scatterings was solved numerically, starting from an anisotropic momentum distribution obtained from HIJING simulation for central Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, evolving until local momentum isotropy was achieved.

## Key numbers

- Gluon number density required for triple-gluon scatterings to be important: more than 30 fm⁻³ in RHIC Au-Au collisions, potentially reaching 140 fm⁻³ in LHC Pb-Pb collisions.
- Initial time t_ini = 0.2 fm/c for the start of the evolution; thermalization time for gluon matter t_iso − t_ini = 0.45 fm/c (t_iso = 0.65 fm/c).
- Thermalized gluon matter parameters: temperature T = 0.75 GeV, fugacity λ = 0.065.
- Thermalization time for quark matter (from 2→2 and 3→3 quark scatterings): t_iso − t_ini = 1.8 fm/c, with T = 0.59 GeV and λ = 0.04.
- Ratio of three-gluon to two-gluon scattering numbers varies around 0.7 when the interaction range exceeds 0.1 fm, based on 1000 gluons generated from the HIJING distribution within a cylinder of radius R_A = 6.4 fm and rapidity |y| ≤ Y = 5.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of three-gluon scatterings (Xu, 2005) and builds on parton cascade models by Geiger, Shin, and Xu, as well as other thermalization studies by Shuryak, Eskola, Bhalerao, Nayak, Wong, Baier, and Matinya. The key advance is demonstrating that 3→3 gluon scatterings, not just the usual 2→2 processes, are essential for achieving the rapid thermalization observed in RHIC data. The results enable more complete transport treatments of the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, particularly at LHC energies where gluon densities are even higher.
