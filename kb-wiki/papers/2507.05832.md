# Revisiting $η'(958)$ nuclear states

> Observing $η'$-nuclear quasibound states requires that the $η'$-nuclear potential is both sufficiently attractive and weakly absorptive, as confirmed by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration analysis of inclusive $η'$ production experiments on nuclear targets, including liquid hydrogen (LH$_2$). Here we present an alternative derivation of the $η'$-nuclear potential, constrained by near-threshold $pp\to ppη'$ and $γp\toη' p$ production experiments on a free proton. The resulting $η'$-nuclear potential is weakly attractive and strongly absorptive, to the extent that observation of clear signals of $η'$-nuclear quasibound states is unlikely. Possible exceptions resulting from the dynamics of the nearby $I=\frac{1}{2}$ $J=({\frac{1}{2}})^-$ nucleon resonance $N^{\ast}$(1895) are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the η'(958)-nuclear potential derived from free-proton production data is weakly attractive and strongly absorptive, making the observation of clear η'-nuclear quasibound states unlikely, contrary to earlier analyses of inclusive η' production on nuclear targets that suggested a weakly absorptive potential. The authors note possible exceptions from the dynamics of the N*(1895) resonance near threshold.

## Method
The authors constructed a low-energy η'-nuclear density-dependent optical potential V_opt^η'(ρ) using the η'N scattering length a_η'N as input, constrained by near-threshold pp→ppη' and γp→η'p production experiments on free protons. The potential incorporates long-range Pauli correlations through a density-dependent scattering amplitude b_0^A(ρ), and was applied to calculate η' single-particle binding energies and widths in ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca using harmonic-oscillator and two-parameter Fermi nuclear densities.

## Key numbers
- From pp→ppη' at COSY: Re a_η'p = (0.00 ± 0.43) fm, Im a_η'p = 0.37^(+0.02+0.38)_(-0.11-0.05) fm
- From γp→η'p: |a_η'p| = (0.403 ± 0.020 ± 0.060) fm, with a more restrictive constraint |a_η'p| = (0.356 ± 0.012) fm assuming no narrow s-wave resonance near threshold
- For b_0 = 0.16 + i0.37 fm: 1s_η' in ¹²C has B = -9.73 MeV, Γ = 34.1 MeV; in ⁴⁰Ca B = -0.75 MeV, Γ = 52.9 MeV; potential depth D_η' = 11.6 MeV
- For b_0 = 0.39 + i0.25 fm: 1s_η' in ¹²C has B = 1.76 MeV, Γ = 25.1 MeV; in ⁴⁰Ca B = 10.7 MeV, Γ = 36.5 MeV; D_η' = 22.6 MeV
- Previous potential values from nuclear target fits: V_0 = -(44 ± 16 ± 15) MeV, W_0 = -(13 ± 3 ± 3) MeV
- N*(1895) resonance width Γ_1895 ≈ 120 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous methodology for constructing hadron-nucleus optical potentials (applied to Λ hypernuclei and Ξ⁻ hypernuclei) to the η' meson. It contradicts the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration analysis of inclusive η' production on nuclear targets (N12, N13, N16, F16, N18), which yielded a weakly absorptive potential (V_0 = -44 MeV, W_0 = -13 MeV) and predicted bound states with widths around 15-23 MeV. The paper enables future work on subthreshold η'N scattering amplitudes using dynamical models of N*(1895), following approaches used for ηN and N*(1535) by Friedman, Gal, and Mareš (2013) and Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, and Mareš (2014).
