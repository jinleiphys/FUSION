# Dynamics of two-cluster systems in phase space

> We present a phase-space representation of quantum state vectors for two-cluster systems. Density distributions in the Fock--Bargmann space are constructed for bound and resonance states of $^{6,7}$Li and $^{7,8}$Be, provided that all these nuclei are treated within a microscopic two-cluster model. The density distribution in the phase space is compared with those in the coordinate and momentum representations. Bound states realize themselves in a compact area of the phase space, as also do narrow resonance states. We establish the quantitative boundaries of this region in the phase space for the nuclei under consideration. Quantum trajectories are demonstrated to approach their classical limit with increasing energy.

## Key claim

Bound and narrow resonance states of two-cluster systems occupy a compact region of phase space whose quantitative boundaries can be established; quantum phase trajectories approach their classical limit with increasing energy, but the maximum of the density distribution for a 3D harmonic oscillator eigenstate corresponds to a classical energy \(E_{cl}=N+2\) rather than the naive \(E_{cl}=N+3/2\).

## Method

The authors construct density distributions in the Fock-Bargmann (coherent-state) representation for two-cluster systems, using the microscopic resonating-group method (RGM) to obtain wave functions expanded in a harmonic-oscillator basis. They integrate the six-variable density distribution over solid angles to obtain a two-dimensional density \(D_E(\xi,\eta)\) depending only on the absolute values of the relative coordinate \(\xi\) and relative momentum \(\eta\). Phase portraits are then analyzed as contours of constant \(D_E(\xi,\eta)\) for bound and resonance states of \(^{6,7}\)Li and \(^{7,8}\)Be treated as \(\alpha+d\), \(\alpha+t\), \(\alpha+^3\)He, and \(\alpha+\alpha\) clusters.

## Key numbers

- For a 3D harmonic oscillator eigenstate with total quanta \(N=2n+l\), the density distribution peaks on the circle \((\xi^2+\eta^2)/2 = 2n+l+2\), corresponding to a classical energy \(E_{cl}=N+2\).
- For a free 3D particle with \(k=1\) and zero orbital momentum, the density distribution in the momentum variable \(\eta\) peaks at \(\eta \simeq 1.25\) rather than at \(\eta=1\) as the momentum-space density does.
- For a free 3D particle with \(k=5\) and zero orbital momentum, the quantum trajectories become infinite and approach the classical trajectory \(\eta \simeq k\).

## Context

This work extends the phase-space state-vector approach of Torres-Vega and Frederic (1993, 1997) and the Fock-Bargmann representation formalism to realistic nuclear two-cluster systems, going beyond the one-dimensional analytic models studied in those earlier papers. It shares conceptual ground with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) and Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) but differs by using Slater determinants as generating functions for a complete oscillator basis rather than as independent phase-space variables. The results enable quantitative analysis of the classical-to-quantum transition in cluster dynamics and provide a method to identify the phase-space region most relevant for bound and narrow resonance states in light nuclei.
