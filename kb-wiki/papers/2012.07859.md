# The Energy-Energy Correlation in the back-to-back limit at N$^3$LO and N$^3$LL$^\prime$

> We present the analytic formula for the Energy-Energy Correlation (EEC) in electron-positron annihilation computed in perturbative QCD to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in the back-to-back limit. In particular, we consider the EEC arising from the annihilation of an electron-positron pair into a virtual photon as well as a Higgs boson and their subsequent inclusive decay into hadrons. Our computation is based on a factorization theorem of the EEC formulated within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the back-to-back limit. We obtain the last missing ingredient for our computation - the jet function - from a recent calculation of the transverse-momentum dependent fragmentation function (TMDFF) at N$^3$LO. We combine the newly obtained N$^3$LO jet function with the well known hard and soft function to predict the EEC in the back-to-back limit. The leading transcendental contribution of our analytic formula agrees with previously obtained results in $\mathcal{N} = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory. We obtain the $N=2$ Mellin moment of the bulk region of the EEC using momentum sum rules. Finally, we obtain the first resummation of the EEC in the back-to-back limit at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy, resulting in a factor of $\sim 4$ reduction of uncertainties in the peak region compared to N$^3$LL predictions.

## Key claim

The authors present the first analytic formula for the Energy-Energy Correlation (EEC) in the back-to-back limit at N$^3$LO in QCD, and achieve the first resummation at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy; the claimed factor of $\sim 4$ reduction in uncertainties in the peak region compared to N$^3$LL predictions depends on the uncertainty estimation scheme, with a $0.5\%$ uncertainty at the peak using a scheme consistent with previous literature versus a $4\%$ uncertainty using a more conservative scheme.

## Method

The computation uses a factorization theorem for the EEC in the back-to-back limit formulated within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), where the cross section factorizes into hard, jet, and soft functions. The last missing ingredient, the N$^3$LO jet function, is obtained from a recent calculation of the transverse-momentum dependent fragmentation function (TMDFF) at N$^3$LO using an exponential regulator for rapidity divergences, and is combined with the known hard and soft functions to predict the EEC. The resummation at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy is performed by solving the renormalization group equations for the hard, jet, and soft functions, incorporating the full N$^3$LO fixed-order boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

* The N$^3$LO quark jet function constant term $j_q^{(3)}$ is given analytically in terms of color factors $C_F$, $C_A$, $n_f$, and $T_F$, with numerical coefficients such as $C_F^3 (-\frac{496}{3} \zeta_3^2 -\frac{17062}{9} \zeta_6 + 600 \zeta_2 \zeta_3 +\frac{2008}{3}\zeta_5 +243 \zeta_4 +\frac{32}{3} \zeta_3 +\frac{22}{3}\zeta_2 +\frac{163}{4})$ and similar expressions for other color structures.
* The N$^3$LO gluon jet function constant term $j_g^{(3)}$ is given analytically, with the $C_A^3$ term containing $(\frac{887378}{3645} -\frac{7091}{6}\zeta_2 -\frac{52064}{45}\zeta_3 + \frac{101995}{54}\zeta_4 + \frac{8540}{9}\zeta_5 +\frac{2228}{3} \zeta_2 \zeta_3 - \frac{4853}{3}\zeta_6 -\frac{256}{3} \zeta_3^2)$.
* The resummation at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ yields a factor of $\sim 4$ reduction of uncertainties in the peak region compared to N$^3$LL predictions.
* Using a scheme consistent with previous literature, a $0.5\%$ uncertainty is obtained at the peak; using a more conservative scheme, a $4\%$ uncertainty is obtained for the N$^3$LL$^\prime$ result.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-order calculation of the EEC in the back-to-back limit from NNLO (achieved numerically by Del Duca (2016) and Tulipant (2017) using the CoLoRFulNNLO framework) to N$^3$LO, and improves the resummation from N$^3$LL (achieved by Moult (2018)) to N$^3$LL$^\prime$. The jet functions calculated here are necessary ingredients for describing the singular behavior of the Transverse Energy Energy Correlator (TEEC) in the large angle limit at $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)$ and for resummation at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ and N$^4$LL accuracy for that observable, as well as enabling the first N$^3$LL$^\prime$ resummation for any event shape sensitive to QCD radiation.
