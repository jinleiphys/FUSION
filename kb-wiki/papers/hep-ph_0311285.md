# Model Independent Results for SU(3) Violation in Light-Cone Distribution Functions

> Using chiral symmetry we investigate the leading SU(3) violation in light-cone distribution functions phi_M(x) of the pion, kaon, and eta. It is shown that terms non-analytic in the quark masses do not affect the shape, and only appear in the decay constants. Predictive power is retained including the leading analytic m_q operators. With the symmetry violating corrections we derive useful model independent relations between phi_pi, phi_eta, and phi_K. Using the soft-collinear effective theory we discuss how factorization generates the subleading chiral coefficients.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory, the authors prove that at first order in SU(3) violation, the shape of light-cone distribution functions \(\phi_M(x)\) for pion, kaon, and eta is not affected by non-analytic terms in the quark masses; chiral logarithms appear only in the decay constants \(f_M\), while analytic \(m_q\) corrections retain predictive power for the distribution functions themselves.

## Method

The authors construct hadronic chiral perturbation theory operators for the octet axial twist-2 operators \(O_k^{A,a}\) whose matrix elements give moments of the light-cone distribution functions. They show that at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, loop diagrams with derivatives acting inside the loop vanish due to \(n^2=0\), so all non-analytic corrections are contained in the decay constants. They then derive the analytic corrections from subleading operators involving quark masses, obtaining relations among moments and distribution functions for \(\pi\), \(K\), and \(\eta\) mesons.

## Key numbers

* \(f_K/f_\pi = 1.23\) and \(f_\eta/f_\pi = 1.33\) from the next-to-leading order counterterm corrections to decay constants
* The ratio of inverse moments \(\langle x_u^{-1}\rangle_{\pi^+}/\langle x_u^{-1}\rangle_{K^+} \simeq 1.12\) from recent light-cone sum rule results (Ball), correcting an earlier result of 0.80
* The leading SU(3) violation corrections are at the 10% level, while chiral logarithm corrections are sizeable at \(\sim 30\%\) in the SU(3) limit

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to light-cone distribution functions, building on earlier ChPT applications to twist-2 matrix elements by Arndt and Savage (2002) and Chen and Ji (2001). The authors provide model-independent relations that supersede earlier model-dependent estimates of SU(3) violation, where even the signs of effects were uncertain. The results enable lattice QCD extrapolations of \(\phi_M\) moment ratios using only linear \(m_q\) dependence, and provide a framework for studying SU(3) violation in \(B\) meson decays such as \(B \rightarrow \pi\ell\nu\), \(B \rightarrow D\pi\), and \(B \rightarrow \pi\pi, K\pi\) decays important for measuring CP violation and the CKM matrix element \(|V_{ub}|\).
