# Anomalous soft photon production from the induced currents in Dirac sea

> The propagation of a high energy quark disturbs the confining QCD vacuum inducing the currents in Dirac sea. Since quarks possess electric charge, these induced vacuum quark currents act as a source of soft photon radiation. This can lead to the enhancement of the soft photon production above the expectations based on the charged hadron yields and the Low theorem. We illustrate the phenomenon by using the exactly soluble 1+1 dimensional massless Abelian gauge model that shares with QCD all of the ingredients involved in this mechanism: confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, axial anomaly, and the periodic $θ$-vacuum. We show that the propagating quark throughout the process of hadronization induces in the vacuum charged transition currents that lead to a strong resonant enhancement of the soft photon yield; the Low theorem however remains accurate in the limit of very soft momenta. We then construct on the basis of our result a simple phenomenological model and apply it to the soft photon production in the fragmentation of jets produced in $Z^0$ decays. We find a qualitative agreement with the recent result from the DELPHI Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the enhancement of soft photon production above the Low theorem predictions arises from coherent oscillations of electric and axial charges in the Dirac sea, induced by a propagating high-energy quark during hadronization; however, the Low theorem remains accurate in the limit of very soft momenta (p_perp approaching zero), where the transient quark contributions cancel with the valence contributions.

## Method

The authors model the longitudinal dynamics of jet fragmentation using the exactly soluble 1+1 dimensional massless Abelian gauge model (Schwinger model), which shares confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, axial anomaly, and periodic theta-vacuum with QCD. They solve the bosonized equations of motion for the scalar field coupled to an external current representing the leading quark and antiquark, compute the induced vector current from sea quarks, and couple this 1+1 dimensional current to the 3+1 dimensional electromagnetic field to obtain the photon bremsstrahlung spectrum. To compare with DELPHI data, they introduce a Gaussian distribution for the string tension and a finite width parameter for the meson propagator.

## Key numbers

- The DELPHI Collaboration measured photons with transverse momenta p_perp < 80 MeV and total energies within 0.2 < E_gamma < 1 GeV.
- The fitted value of the effective width parameter gamma = 0.003 GeV, extracted by fitting to the DELPHI experimental photon yield.
- The mean string tension used is <kappa^2> = 0.9 GeV/fm, taken from lattice studies and Regge phenomenology.
- The branching fractions for Z0 decay are B_{2/3} = 0.331 and B_{1/3} = 0.669 from the Particle Data Group.
- The axial charge oscillates with period T = 2*pi/m, where m is the scalar meson mass.
- The potential between static fermions at short distances (r << m^{-1}) is linear: V(r) ~ (pi/2) m^2 r.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of anomalous soft photon production in hadron collisions, where experiments have found enhancements by factors of 2 to 5 above the Low theorem predictions, as seen in measurements by Chliapnikov (1984), Botterweck (1991), Banerjee (1992), and Belogianni (1997, 2002). The authors specifically target the DELPHI Collaboration result (Abdallah 2010) showing a fourfold enhancement in Z0 hadronic decays, which cannot be explained by perturbative bremsstrahlung from fragmenting quarks or final-state charged hadrons. The mechanism extends prior work by Loshaj (2011) and Kharzeev (2012) using the Schwinger model for jet fragmentation, and enables a new nonperturbative approach to soft photon production that could help resolve the background for diagnosing quark-gluon plasma.
