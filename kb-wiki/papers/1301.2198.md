# On the double-ridge effect at the LHC

## Key claim

The double-ridge structure observed in two-particle correlation functions in pPb collisions at the LHC can result from the coherent rotation of a transient quark-pion liquid state of matter, provided the collisions occur at non-zero impact parameter and the reflective scattering mode dominates at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors develop a geometric and dynamical model in which the overlap region of colliding hadrons at non-zero impact parameter generates massive virtual quarks with large orbital angular momentum, leading to coherent rotation of a strongly interacting quark-pion liquid. The rotation contributes to the x-component of transverse momentum but not the y-component, producing narrow azimuthal correlations and broad pseudorapidity correlations. The peripheral nature of inelastic collisions at LHC energies is attributed to the reflective scattering mode, which causes the inelastic overlap function to peak at non-zero impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- The analysis addresses pPb collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, as reported by the ALICE and ATLAS collaborations.
- The reflective scattering mode becomes prominent above $\sqrt{s}>2$ TeV, where the inelastic overlap function $h_{inel}(s,b)$ acquires a peripheral impact parameter dependence.
- The double-ridge structure appears as two maxima in the two-particle correlation function at $\Delta\phi=0$ and $\Delta\phi=\pi$ after subtraction of low-multiplicity events (ALICE) or dijet contributions (ATLAS).

## Context

This work extends the ridge effect first observed at RHIC in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions and later in pp collisions by CMS, and applies similar reasoning to the double-ridge structure seen in pPb collisions by ALICE and ATLAS. The mechanism contrasts with other explanations reviewed in a recent review paper (Author, year not specified in text), and the authors predict that the double-ridge effect should also be observable in pp collisions at the LHC and in peripheral AA collisions at RHIC after appropriate subtraction procedures, but not in pp or pA interactions at RHIC energies.
