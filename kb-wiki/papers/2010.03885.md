# Partial wave correlations with uncertainties in the Two Nucleon System

> The NN scattering problem is usually analyzed in terms of partial waves and the corresponding coupled channel phase-shifts and mixing angles, but the available experiments induce correlations amongst the corresponding channels with different quantum numbers. Based on the Granada-2013 database we analyze the meaning and impact of those correlations taking into account both the purely statistical ones reflecting the primary experimental data uncertainties as well as the systematic ones exhibiting the ambiguities in the form of the potential representing the unknown nuclear force for distances below 3fm. We also ponder on the implications of fixing nuclear potentials by direct fits to the phase-shifts stemming from a full fledged partial wave analysis (PWA) inferred from experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, when analyzing the Granada-2013 database, the combined statistical and systematic uncertainties in partial wave correlations are almost compatible with zero, supporting the common practice of fitting nuclear potentials separately channel by channel to phase-shifts, but only if much larger uncertainties are used than those from a full partial wave analysis (PWA).

## Method

The authors computed linear correlation coefficients between pairs of NN partial waves (total angular momentum J ≤ 3) as a function of nucleon LAB energy from 0 to 350 MeV, using 13 high-quality potential models (including 6 Granada potentials and 7 earlier phenomenological approaches). They separated the analysis into purely statistical correlations (from the covariance matrix of the Granada 2013 fit to 6713 pp+np scattering data) and systematic correlations (from the spread among different statistically equivalent potential models), assigning confidence intervals via the Fisher transformation for a sample size of N=13.

## Key numbers

- The Granada 2013 database contains 6713 pp+np scattering data below 350 MeV LAB energy, selected as 3σ-self-consistent from an initial set of 8000 data points.
- The best fit to this database used 55 fitting parameters, yielding a total χ² = 6855.50 and reduced χ² per degree of freedom = 1.025.
- Statistical uncertainties in phase-shifts are about an order of magnitude smaller than systematic uncertainties, based on comparison of 13 statistically equivalent PWA analyses.
- For the sample size N=13, empirical correlation coefficients |r| ≥ 0.5 are needed to claim ρ ≠ 0 significantly at the 95% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the Granada group's previous uncertainty quantification studies (Perez 2014, 2016; Ruiz Simo 2017) which established that statistical uncertainties in NN phase-shifts are about an order of magnitude smaller than systematic uncertainties from different potential models. The paper directly addresses the long-standing practice, dating back to Signell (1963, 1964) and Reid (1968), of fitting nuclear potentials to phase-shifts separately without a full PWA, showing that this shortcut is justified only when the much larger systematic uncertainties are used. The results enable nuclear theorists to use independent channel-by-channel fits with confidence, provided they incorporate the enlarged uncertainty bands rather than the narrow statistical ones from a single PWA.
