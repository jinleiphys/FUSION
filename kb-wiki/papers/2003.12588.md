# Gravitational form factors of hyperons in light-cone QCD

> The quark parts of the gravitational form factors of hyperons are calculated by means of the light-cone QCD sum rule. In the calculations, the distribution amplitudes (DAs) of $Σ$, $Ξ$ and $Λ$ together with the general forms of their interpolating currents as well as the quark part of the energy-momentum tensor current are used. These form factors can provide information on their mass and distributions of the angular momentum, energy and pressure inside the hyperons. It is obtained that the $Q^2$ dependencies of the hyperon gravitational form factors are nicely characterized by a multipole fit function. Using the fits of these form factors, some mechanical properties such as the mechanical radius of the hyperons and the pressure and energy distributions at the center of these particles are obtained. The obtained results can help us in better understanding of the internal structures of these baryons and the QCD as theory of the strong interaction.

## Key claim

The quark parts of the gravitational form factors of the light octet hyperons (Sigma, Xi, Lambda, and the Sigma0-Lambda transition) are calculated for the first time using light-cone QCD sum rules, with the results showing that the Q2 dependence is well described by a multipole fit function; the authors note that the method is reliable only at Q2 ≥ 1.0 GeV2 and that extrapolation to Q2 = 0 relies on this fit.

## Method

The correlation function of the quark part of the energy-momentum tensor current with interpolating currents for each hyperon state was computed in two frameworks: the hadronic side, expressed in terms of the gravitational form factors and hadron parameters, and the QCD side, expressed using the distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the Sigma, Xi, and Lambda hyperons from Liu (2008, 2009). After applying Borel transformations and continuum subtraction to suppress excited-state contributions, the two representations were matched for independent Lorentz structures to obtain sum rules for the form factors A(Q2), J(Q2), D(Q2), and c̄(Q2) for each transition.

## Key numbers

- For the Sigma-Sigma transition at Q2 = 0: A(0) = 0.86 ± 0.12, J(0) = 0.40 ± 0.06, D(0) = -2.65 ± 0.25; the c̄(0) result was not reported as reliable.
- For the Xi-Xi transition at Q2 = 0: A(0) = 0.53 ± 0.05, J(0) = 0.25 ± 0.02, D(0) = -2.30 ± 0.18, c̄(0) = -0.12 ± 0.01.
- For the Lambda-Lambda transition at Q2 = 0: A(0) = 0.58 ± 0.10, J(0) = 0.32 ± 0.06, D(0) = -2.53 ± 0.12, c̄(0) = -0.10 ± 0.01.
- The multipole fit parameter p was 3.2 ± 0.2 for most form factors, with mp values around 1.0 to 1.2 GeV-2.
- The working Borel mass windows were 2.00 GeV2 ≤ M2 ≤ 3.00 GeV2 for Sigma-Sigma, Lambda-Lambda, and Sigma0-Lambda, and 2.50 GeV2 ≤ M2 ≤ 3.50 GeV2 for Xi-Xi.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone QCD sum rule approach previously applied to nucleon gravitational form factors by Azizi (2019) and Anikin (2019) to the hyperon sector for the first time. The results for the D-term of hyperons (D(0) values around -2.3 to -2.7) are close to nucleon D-term predictions from lattice QCD (Bratt 2010, Hagler 2007), chiral perturbation theory (Dorati 2007), and JLab data (Burkert 2018), showing reasonable SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking. The Sigma0-Lambda transition form factors were found to be very small and close to zero, consistent with expectations from isospin violation.
