# QED One-loop Corrections to a Macroscopic Magnetic Dipole

> We consider the field equations of a static magnetic field including one-loop QED corrections, and calculate the corrections to the field of a magnetic dipole. PACS: 12.20.Ds, 97.60.Jd, 97.60.Gb

## Key claim

The one-loop QED corrections to the field of a static magnetic dipole generate small but nonzero higher-order multipole moments (hexapole and beyond) even when the source has no intrinsic higher moments, but these corrections are significant only for field strengths approaching the critical QED scale \(B_k \approx 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G, and the effect is generally tiny for all but the most extreme astrophysical objects.

## Method

The authors start from the Heisenberg-Euler effective Lagrangian of QED at one-loop order, take the static magnetic limit (\(K=0\)), and derive the nonlinear magnetic permeability tensor as a function of field strength. They expand the field equations order by order in the small nonlinearity, solve for the first-order correction to the magnetic scalar potential of a point dipole, and compute the resulting shifts in the multipole moments (dipole, hexapole, and higher) using spherical harmonic expansions, treating both the weak-field (constant \(\mu_1\)) and strong-field (general \(\mu_1\)) regimes analytically and numerically.

## Key numbers

- The critical QED field scale is \(B_k = m^2 c^3 / (e \hbar) \approx 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G.
- In the weak-field limit, the correction to the dipole moment between radius \(r_s\) and infinity is \(\Delta m = \frac{8}{135} \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} m \left( \frac{m}{r_s^3} \frac{1}{B_k} \right)^2\).
- In the weak-field limit, the induced hexapole moment between radius \(r_s\) and infinity is \(\Delta M_{30} = \frac{4}{165} \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} m r_s^2 \left( \frac{m}{r_s^3} \frac{1}{B_k} \right)^2\).
- The weak-field correction to the dipole moment is less than one-thousandth of the bare dipole moment for fields appreciably below \(B_k\).
- For a point dipole (e.g., an electron), the inner cutoff radius where the Heisenberg-Euler Lagrangian fails is \(r_0 \sim (m / B_k \cdot \lambda_e)^{1/4}\), where \(\lambda_e = \hbar / (m_e c)\) is the electron Compton wavelength.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop QED corrections to the electromagnetic field, previously studied by Heisenberg and Euler (1936), Weisskopf (1936), and Schwinger (1951), from uniform fields to the specific geometry of a macroscopic magnetic dipole. It builds on the analytic expression for the effective Lagrangian derived by Heyl and Hernquist (1997) and agrees numerically with the permeability results of Mielnieczuk et al. (1988). The results enable quantitative predictions for neutron star magnetospheres, particularly for magnetars (Duncan and Thompson, 1992), where QED vacuum corrections dominate over plasma contributions (Meszaros, 1992), and complicate arguments about the origin of neutron-star magnetic fields (Blandford et al., 1983; Arons, 1993).
