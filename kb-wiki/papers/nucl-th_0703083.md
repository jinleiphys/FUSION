# Beam energy dependence of Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup of 11Be

> Within the post-form distorted wave Born approximation wherein pure Coulomb, pure nuclear and their interference terms are treated consistently in a single setup we study the beam energy dependence of the Coulomb-nuclear interference terms in the breakup of 11Be on a medium mass 44Ti target. Our results suggest that the Coulomb-nuclear interference terms are dependent on the incident beam energy and can be as big as that of the individual Coulomb or nuclear terms depending on the angle and energy of the breakup fragments. We also calculate the relative energy spectra and one-neutron removal cross sections in the breakup of 11Be on a heavy 208Pb target at 69 MeV/nucleon for two different angular ranges of the projectile center of the mass scattering angle and compare them with recently available experimental data.

# Beam energy dependence of Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup of 11Be

## Key claim
The Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) terms in the breakup of 11Be on a medium-mass 44Ti target depend not only on the energies and angles of the outgoing fragments, but also on the incident beam energy; the CNI terms can be as large as the individual Coulomb or nuclear terms depending on the angle and energy of the breakup fragments, with the caveat that these effects are most visible in exclusive observables (double differential cross sections) rather than in integrated quantities like total cross sections.

## Method
The breakup reaction was calculated within the post-form distorted wave Born approximation (PFDWBA), treating pure Coulomb, pure nuclear, and their interference terms consistently in a single framework. The Coulomb and nuclear interactions between projectile fragments and the target were treated to all orders, while the fragment-fragment interaction was treated in first order; finite-range effects were handled via a local momentum approximation (LMA). The ground state of 11Be was modeled as a 2s1/2 valence neutron coupled to a 10Be core with a one-neutron separation energy of 504 keV and a spectroscopic factor of 0.74, using a Woods-Saxon potential with radius 1.236 fm and diffuseness 0.62 fm.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron separation energy of 11Be: 504 keV; spectroscopic factor: 0.74
- Woods-Saxon potential depth for valence neutron-10Be interaction: 59.08 MeV; radius parameter: 1.236 fm; diffuseness: 0.62 fm
- Root mean square radius of valence neutron in 11Be: 7.07 fm; rms radius of 11Be: 2.98 fm; rms radius of 10Be core: 2.28 fm
- Total one-neutron removal cross section for 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon, theta_at integration 0-6 degrees: 1.534 b (pure Coulomb: 1.191 b; pure nuclear: 0.367 b; incoherent sum: 1.558 b); experimental value: 1.790 +/- 0.110 (syst) +/- 0.020 (stat) b
- Total one-neutron removal cross section for 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon, theta_at integration 0-1.3 degrees: 0.489 b (pure Coulomb: 0.484 b; pure nuclear: 0.005 b; incoherent sum: 0.489 b)
- Grazing angle for 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon: 3.8 degrees
- Partial wave expansion convergence: L_i^max = 500 for Pb target; L_i^max = 400 for Ti target

## Context
This work extends earlier PFDWBA calculations by Chatterjee (2002, 2003) that treated Coulomb and nuclear breakup consistently, and addresses the previously unstudied question of beam energy dependence of CNI terms. The results contrast with prior approaches using prior-form DWBA (Shyam 1999), time-dependent Schrodinger equation models (Typel 2001, Bonaccorso 2002), and continuum discretized coupled-channels methods (CDCC) that treat Coulomb and nuclear processes consistently. The calculations for 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon are compared directly with experimental data from Nakamura (2006). The work enables future exclusive cross section measurements at low beam energies where CNI effects are predicted to be substantial, and suggests extension to (a,b gamma) reactions and to effective field theory approaches for halo nuclei at very high beam energies (Bertulani 2002).
