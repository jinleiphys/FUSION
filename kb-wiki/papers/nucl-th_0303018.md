# Building Atomic Nuclei with the Dirac Equation

> The relevance of the Dirac equation for computations of nuclear structure is motivated and discussed. Quantitatively successful results for medium- and heavy-mass nuclei are described, and modern ideas of effective field theory and density functional theory are used to justify them.

# Building Atomic Nuclei with the Dirac Equation

## Key claim
The Dirac equation provides a natural and quantitatively successful framework for describing medium- and heavy-mass nuclei, but the important relativistic effects are dynamical (from the interactions) rather than kinematical; the large scalar and vector mean fields (roughly -400 MeV and +300 MeV respectively) cancel in the binding energy but add constructively in the spin-orbit interaction, producing the nuclear shell structure automatically.

## Method
The paper constructs a relativistic mean-field theory (RMFT) based on quantum hadrodynamics (QHD-I), a model containing nucleons coupled to isoscalar scalar (φ) and vector (V^μ) mesons. The mean-field equations are solved self-consistently for spherically symmetric nuclei, treating the meson fields as classical, spatially-dependent mean fields while the nucleons obey the Dirac equation with an effective mass M* = M - g_s φ_0. The model is extended with an isovector ρ meson and Coulomb interaction, with parameters fitted to nuclear matter equilibrium properties and one nuclear length scale.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³
- Nuclear matter binding energy at equilibrium: e₀ ≈ 16 MeV
- Scalar coupling constant: C_s² = g_s² M²/m_s² = 357.4
- Vector coupling constant: C_v² = g_v² M²/m_v² = 273.8
- Nucleon effective mass at equilibrium density: M*₀ ≈ 0.6 M
- Scalar mean field at equilibrium: roughly -400 MeV
- Vector mean field at equilibrium: roughly +300 MeV
- Nuclear radius scaling: R ≈ A^(1/3) × 1.1 fm
- Surface thickness: density decreases from 90% to 10% of central value over roughly 2 fm
- EFT breakdown scale: Λ ≈ 600 MeV
- Fermi momentum at equilibrium relative to nucleon mass: k_F/M ≈ 1/3

## Context
This work extends the original Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) and the Dirac-Hartree calculations of Horowitz and Serot (1981) by interpreting the relativistic mean-field approach within modern effective field theory and density functional theory frameworks. The paper shows that the QHD lagrangian can be organized as a nonrenormalizable EFT with an expansion in powers of k_F/M, and that the mean-field energy functional should be understood as a Kohn-Sham density functional. This theoretical foundation enables systematic improvements through loop corrections (Hu, 2000) and provides a framework for predicting properties of nuclei not included in the fitting procedure (Huertas, 2002).
