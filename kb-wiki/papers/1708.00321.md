# Neutrino-nucleus cross sections in $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ar$ with KDAR neutrinos

> High intensity monoenergetic muon neutrinos of energy 236 MeV from kaon decay at rest (KDAR) at the medium energy proton accelerator facilities like J-PARC and Fermilab are proposed to be used for making precision measurements of neutrino-nucleus cross sections in $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ar$ and perform neutrino oscillation experiments in $ν_μ\to ν_μ$ and $ν_μ\to ν_e$ modes. In view of these developments, we study the theoretical uncertainties arising due to the nuclear medium effects in the neutrino-nucleus cross sections as well as in the angular and energy distributions of the charged leptons produced in the charged current (CC) induced reactions by $ν_μ$ and $ν_e$ in $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ar$ in the energy region of $E_{ν_e(ν_μ)}<$ 300 MeV. The calculations have been done in a microscopic model using the local density approximation which takes into account the nuclear effects due to the Fermi motion, binding energy and long range correlations. The results are compared with the other calculations available in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies theoretical uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross sections for monoenergetic 236 MeV KDAR neutrinos on $^{12}C$ and $^{40}Ar$, finding that nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, binding energy, and long-range RPA correlations) cause reductions of 30-50% from free-nucleon values at 236 MeV, with the exact reduction depending on the lepton flavor and the Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$ in the range 0.6-0.7.

## Method

The authors used a local Fermi gas model (LFGM) within the local density approximation, incorporating nuclear density profiles (modified harmonic oscillator for $^{12}C$, two-parameter Fermi for $^{40}Ar$) and the Lindhard function for particle-hole excitations. Long-range nucleon-nucleon correlations were treated in the random phase approximation (RPA) using $\pi$ and $\rho$ exchange potentials with a Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$ varied between 0.6 and 0.7, and $\Delta$-hole excitations were included. Coulomb distortion of the outgoing charged lepton was accounted for via an effective momentum approximation.

## Key numbers

- At $E_{\nu_e}=236$ MeV on $^{12}C$, the reduction from free-nucleon cross section is $\sim$30% without RPA and $\sim$42% with RPA; on $^{40}Ar$, the reduction is $\sim$15% without RPA and $\sim$47% with RPA.
- At $E_{\nu_\mu}=236$ MeV on $^{12}C$, the reduction is $\sim$47% without RPA and $\sim$45% with RPA; on $^{40}Ar$, the reduction is $\sim$30% without RPA and $\sim$50% with RPA.
- Varying $g'$ from 0.6 to 0.7 changes the cross section by about 10% for both $\nu_e$ and $\nu_\mu$ at 236 MeV on both targets.
- The NuWro generator prediction of $1.3\times10^{-38}$ cm$^2$ per neutron for $\nu_\mu-^{12}C$ and $\nu_\mu-^{40}Ar$ at $E_\nu=236$ MeV (used in simulation studies by Spitz et al. (2014, 2012) and Axani et al. (2015)) is compared against the present results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic model calculations for low-energy neutrino cross sections relevant to supernova, Michel, and pion decay-in-flight spectra (Sajjad Athar et al. (2004), Athar et al. (2005), Chauhan et al. (2017)). It directly addresses the systematic uncertainty from nuclear medium effects in proposed KDAR neutrino oscillation experiments at J-PARC (JSNS experiment, KPipe experiment) and Fermilab (LArTPC detector), where prior simulation studies by Spitz et al. (2014, 2012) and Axani et al. (2015) used NuWro generator cross sections that are about 25% smaller than GENIE predictions and the results of Martini et al. (2011, 2009). The results enable improved estimates of systematic errors for sterile neutrino searches via $\nu_\mu \to \nu_\mu$ disappearance and $\nu_\mu \to \nu_e$ appearance modes.
