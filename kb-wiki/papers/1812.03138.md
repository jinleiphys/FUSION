# Entanglement Suppression and Emergent Symmetries of Strong Interactions

> Entanglement suppression in the strong interaction $S$-matrix is shown to be correlated with approximate spin-flavor symmetries that are observed in low-energy baryon interactions, the Wigner $SU(4)$ symmetry for two flavors and an $SU(16)$ symmetry for three flavors. We conjecture that dynamical entanglement suppression is a property of the strong interactions in the infrared, giving rise to these emergent symmetries and providing powerful constraints on the nature of nuclear and hypernuclear forces in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the suppression of quantum entanglement in the strong-interaction S-matrix is correlated with the emergence of approximate spin-flavor symmetries (Wigner SU(4) for two flavors and SU(16) for three flavors) in low-energy baryon interactions, and conjectures that dynamical entanglement suppression is a property of QCD in the infrared that gives rise to these symmetries. The hedge is that the entanglement suppression is only partial once relativistic corrections and tensor-force mixing are included, and the SU(16) symmetry does not follow from the large-N_c expansion but does follow from entanglement suppression.

## Method

The authors compute the entanglement power of the S-matrix for low-energy baryon-baryon scattering in spin space, defined as the average ability of the S-matrix to transform a tensor-product state into an entangled state. For two-flavor QCD, they use the spin-singlet (^1S_0) and spin-triplet (^3S_1) phase shifts from phenomenological analyses to calculate the entanglement power as a function of center-of-mass momentum. For three-flavor QCD, they analyze the six independent contact operators in the leading-order effective Lagrangian for octet-baryon interactions and show that the SU(16) symmetric point (where all couplings except c_5 vanish) yields zero entanglement power across multiple two-baryon channels.

- For three-flavor QCD, lattice calculations with m_π ∼ 800 MeV show that the coefficients c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄, and c₆ are much smaller than c₅, indicating b ≪ a in the large-N_c parametrization.
- The entanglement power is averaged over momenta 0 ≤ p ≤ m_π/2 for the two-flavor effective field theory analysis.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of emergent symmetries in low-energy QCD beyond the large-N_c expansion, which predicts SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry for three flavors but cannot explain the larger SU(16) symmetry observed in lattice QCD calculations by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2012, Beane 2013, Wagman 2017). The paper builds on Wigner's SU(4) symmetry (Wigner 1936, 1937, 1939) and its connection to the large-N_c expansion (Kaplan 1995, 1996), and proposes entanglement suppression as a new dynamical principle that constrains nuclear and hypernuclear forces in dense matter. The work enables future calculations of hypernuclei and hyperon-nucleon interactions imposing SU(16) spin-flavor symmetry, and suggests that the nonperturbative mechanisms of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking together screen entanglement fluctuations beyond what is captured by the large-N_c limit.
