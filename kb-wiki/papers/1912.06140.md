# Lepton-Nucleus Cross Section Measurements for DUNE with the LDMX Detector

> We point out that the LDMX (Light Dark Matter eXperiment) detector design, conceived to search for sub-GeV dark matter, will also have very advantageous characteristics to pursue electron-nucleus scattering measurements of direct relevance to the neutrino program at DUNE and elsewhere. These characteristics include a 4-GeV electron beam, a precision tracker, electromagnetic and hadronic calorimeters with near 2$π$ azimuthal acceptance from the forward beam axis out to $\sim$40$^\circ$ angle, and low reconstruction energy threshold. LDMX thus could provide (semi)exclusive cross section measurements, with detailed information about final-state electrons, pions, protons, and neutrons. We compare the predictions of two widely used neutrino generators (GENIE, GiBUU) in the LDMX region of acceptance to illustrate the large modeling discrepancies in electron-nucleus interactions at DUNE-like kinematics. We argue that discriminating between these predictions is well within the capabilities of the LDMX detector.

## Key claim

The LDMX detector, originally designed for dark matter searches, can provide electron-nucleus scattering measurements that are directly relevant to improving neutrino interaction models for DUNE, but the paper hedges that this requires the vetoed data from the dark matter search to be repurposed, and that the actual discriminating power between generator predictions depends on the specific kinematic region and requires further study of particle identification efficiencies.

## Method

The authors simulated electron-titanium scattering at 4 GeV beam energy using the GENIE (versions 2.12.8 and 3.0.6) and GiBUU (versions 2017 and 2019) event generators, applying parametric detector smearing and acceptance cuts corresponding to the LDMX detector design. They compared the predicted inclusive cross sections as functions of electron energy transfer and scattering angle, focusing on the kinematic region with energy transfer above 1 GeV and transverse momentum above 200 MeV, corresponding to the LDMX trigger and dark matter search phase space.

## Key numbers

- LDMX electron beam energy: 4 GeV
- LDMX detector angular acceptance: approximately 40 degrees in polar angle from the forward beam axis
- LDMX electron transverse momentum resolution: less than 10 MeV
- LDMX electron kinetic energy threshold: approximately 60 MeV
- LDMX hadronic calorimeter neutron energy resolution: 5% plus 40% divided by the square root of energy in GeV
- LDMX hadronic calorimeter polar angular acceptance: 65 degrees
- LDMX data acquisition rate: approximately 5 kHz
- Assumed dataset: 1 x 10^14 electrons on target, corresponding to approximately 6 months of low-luminosity running
- Beam repetition rate: 46 MHz
- Target thickness: 0.1 radiation lengths (0.356 cm) of titanium
- Magnetic field: 1.5 T
- At scattering angles 10 to 12.5 degrees, GiBUU predicts 30% fewer events than GENIE
- At scattering angles 20 to 22.5 degrees, GiBUU predicts 50% fewer events than GENIE
- Minimum Q^2 cut applied: 0.03 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the long-recognized connection between electron and neutrino scattering, originally discussed by Smith and Moniz (1972), by proposing that the LDMX detector can provide the exclusive hadronic final-state data needed to resolve discrepancies between neutrino event generators. The paper directly compares GENIE and GiBUU predictions, noting that these generators disagree with each other and with recent high-statistics data from MINERvA, particularly in neutron production (Elkins 2019), cross-target ratios (Tice 2014), and single-pion production (Stowell 2019). The proposed measurements would complement existing inclusive electron scattering data from Jefferson Lab Hall A on argon and titanium (Dai 2018) and the CLAS12 "Electrons for Neutrinos" program, by providing forward-angle coverage and detailed hadronic final-state information that is currently unavailable.
