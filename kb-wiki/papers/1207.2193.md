# Nucleon sea and the five-quark components

> We generalize the approach of Brodsky {\it et al.} for the intrinsic charm quark distribution in the nucleons to the light-quark sector involving intrinsic $\bar u, \bar d, s$ and $\bar s$ sea quarks. We compare the calculations with the existing $\bar d - \bar u$, $s + \bar s$, and $\bar u + \bar d - s -\bar s$ data. The good agreement between the theory and the data is interpreted as evidence for the existence of the intrinsic light-quark sea in the nucleons. The probabilities for the $|uudu\bar{u}>$, $|uudd\bar{d}>$ and $|uuds\bar{s}>$ Fock states are also extracted.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the Brodsky-Hoyer-Peterson-Sakai (BHPS) model for intrinsic charm to the light-quark sector and find that the observed agreement with data on \(\bar d - \bar u\), \(s + \bar s\), and \(\bar u + \bar d - s - \bar s\) is evidence for intrinsic light-quark sea in nucleons, but the agreement depends on the choice of the initial scale \(\mu\) (0.5 GeV or 0.3 GeV) used for QCD evolution.

## Method

The authors extended the BHPS five-quark Fock state formalism, originally developed for the \(|uud c\bar c\rangle\) state, to the light-quark configurations \(|uud u\bar u\rangle\), \(|uud d\bar d\rangle\), and \(|uud s\bar s\rangle\). They used Monte-Carlo techniques to numerically solve the BHPS momentum distribution formula (Eq. 2.1) for light quarks where the heavy-quark mass approximation fails, then evolved the resulting parton distributions from a low initial scale \(\mu^2\) (0.25 or 0.09 GeV\(^2\)) to the experimental \(Q^2\) values using non-singlet DGLAP evolution. The predictions were compared with Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan data for \(\bar d - \bar u\) at \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\) and with HERMES semi-inclusive DIS data for \(s + \bar s\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The integral of \(\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)\) from the Fermilab E866 experiment is \(\int^{1}_{0} (\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)) dx = 0.118 \pm 0.012\).
- The extracted probability for the \(|uuds \bar s\rangle\) Fock state is \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.024\) for \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV and \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.029\) for \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV.
- The extracted probabilities for the five-quark states with \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV are: \({\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.122\), \({\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.240\), \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.024\).
- The extracted probabilities with \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV are: \({\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.162\), \({\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.280\), \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.029\).
- The combined probability for the proton to be in any \(|uud Q \bar Q\rangle\) state is around 40%.
- The estimated probability for the intrinsic charm \(|uud c \bar c\rangle\) state is roughly \(0.1 \times {\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 \sim 0.003\).

## Context

This work extends the BHPS model (Brodsky et al., 1980) from the charm sector to light quarks, building on earlier studies of intrinsic charm by the CTEQ collaboration (Pumplin et al., 2007). The results are compared with the meson-cloud model analysis of Szczurek et al., which found a similar probability for the three-quark bare-nucleon state. The extracted probabilities enable future searches for intrinsic charm at lower \(x\) (\(0.1 < x < 0.4\)) rather than the highest \(x\) region, and the framework can be extended to hyperons, mesons, and comparisons with lattice QCD calculations.
