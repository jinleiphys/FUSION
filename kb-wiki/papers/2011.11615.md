# Femtoscopy of the Origin of the Nucleon Mass

> We study the prospects of using femtoscopic low-momentum correlation measurements at the Large Hadron Collider to access properties of the J/psi-nucleon interaction. The QCD multipole expansion in terms of the J/psi chromopolarizability relates the forward scattering amplitude to a key matrix element to the origin of the nucleon mass problem, the average chromoelectric gluon distribution in the nucleon. We use information on the J/psi-nucleon interaction provided by lattice QCD simulations and phenomenological models to compute J/psi-nucleon correlation functions. The computed correlation functions show clear sensitivity to the interaction, in particular to the J/psi chromopolarizability.

## Key claim

Femtoscopic low-momentum correlation measurements of J/psi-nucleon pairs at the LHC can provide access to the average chromoelectric gluon distribution in the nucleon, a key matrix element for understanding the origin of the nucleon mass, but only if the J/psi chromopolarizability is larger than about 0.24 GeV^-3; otherwise the correlation signal may be too weak to extract reliably.

## Method

The authors computed J/psi-nucleon correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian emission source, employing two approaches: (1) the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formula with scattering parameters from lattice QCD simulations extrapolated to the physical pion mass using QNEFT (an effective field theory for quarkonium-nucleon interactions), and (2) full Schrodinger equation solutions for two phenomenological potentials, a spherical finite well model and a chiral soliton quark model (chiQSM) potential based on the QCD trace anomaly. The correlation functions were evaluated for source radii R = 1 fm (appropriate for pp collisions) and R = 3 fm (for pA and AA collisions), and for several values of the J/psi chromopolarizability.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length from QNEFT-extrapolated lattice data: -0.71 fm ≤ a0 ≤ -0.35 fm; effective range: 1.29 fm ≤ r0 ≤ 1.35 fm
- For a0 = -0.10 fm and R = 3 fm, C(0) ≈ 1.04; for a0 = -0.05 fm and R = 3 fm, C(0) ≈ 1.02
- For the finite well potential with alpha_J/psi = 2.00 GeV^-3: a0 = -0.68 fm, r0 = 1.59 fm; for alpha_J/psi = 0.24 GeV^-3: a0 = -0.05 fm, r0 = 9.46 fm
- For the chiQSM potential with alpha_J/psi = 2.00 GeV^-3: a0 = -0.42 fm, r0 = 1.86 fm; for alpha_J/psi = 0.24 GeV^-3: a0 = -0.03 fm, r0 = 13.05 fm
- The finite well potential with RN = 1 fm would hold an S-wave bound state only when alpha_J/psi > 4.4 GeV^-3
- The van der Waals potential range is 1/(2m_pi) ≈ 0.7 fm, with strength about 3 MeV at that range

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopy technique, previously applied to hyperon-proton and hyperon-hyperon systems (Adams 2005, Acharya 2018, 2019) and the phi-proton system (Chizali 2019), to the J/psi-nucleon system for probing nucleon mass origins. The QCD multipole expansion framework (Peskin 1979, Bhanot 1979, Voloshin 2007) relates the forward scattering amplitude to the chromoelectric gluon distribution, which connects to the nucleon mass via the QCD trace anomaly (Chanowitz 1972, Crewther 1972, Collins 1976). The study uses lattice QCD results (Yokokawa 2006, Liu 2008, Kawanai 2010) extrapolated with QNEFT (Tarrus Castella 2018) and phenomenological models (Anwar 2018, Eides 2017). The results motivate experimental femtoscopic measurements at the LHC, though the authors note that if alpha_J/psi is below 0.24 GeV^-3, extracting the chromoelectric gluon distribution will be very challenging.
