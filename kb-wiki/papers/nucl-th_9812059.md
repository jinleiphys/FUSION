# Rho Meson Broadening in Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

> The modification of the width of rho mesons due to in-medium decays and collisions is evaluated. The decay width is calculated from the imaginary part of the one-loop self-energy at finite temperature. The collision width is related to the cross sections of the $ρ$ + pion and the $ρ$ + nucleon reactions. A calculation based on an effective Lagrangian shows the importance of including the direct $ρπ\toρπ$ scattering which is dominated by the $a_1$ exchange. A large broadening of the spectral function is found, accompanied by a strength suppression at the pole.

# Rho Meson Broadening in Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter

## Key claim

The in-medium width of the rho meson increases dramatically through interactions with pions and nucleons, reaching a total width of approximately 297 MeV under conditions typical of high-energy heavy-ion collisions (T=150 MeV, zero pion chemical potential, nucleon chemical potential 0.4 GeV), but the authors note this should be taken as a lower estimate because interactions with other hadrons are expected to increase the width further.

## Method

The decay width was calculated from the imaginary part of the one-loop rho self-energy at finite temperature using thermal field theory. The collision width was computed from the thermal average of rho-pion and rho-nucleon scattering cross sections, where the rho-pion cross section was obtained from an effective Lagrangian including t-channel diagrams with form factors and the rho-nucleon cross section from a resonance model including baryonic resonances below and above the rho-nucleon threshold. The total width was then obtained by summing the decay width and the collision width from both scattering channels.

## Key numbers

- At T=150 MeV with zero pion chemical potential, the decay width increase relative to vacuum is Delta Gamma = 25 MeV; at the same temperature with mu_pi = 135 MeV, Delta Gamma = 70 MeV
- For T=100 MeV, mu_pi=0, and nucleon density rho_N = rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, the rho-pion collision rate is 12 MeV while the rho-nucleon collision rate is 180 MeV
- Under conditions typical of high-energy heavy-ion collisions (T=150 MeV, mu_pi=0, mu_N=0.4 GeV, rho_N=0.39 rho_0), the total rho width is 176 MeV (decay) + 58 MeV (rho-pion collisions) + 63 MeV (rho-nucleon collisions) = 297 MeV
- The rho meson mass is taken as 770 MeV in the free-space spectral function

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-temperature field theory calculations of rho meson properties (Gale 1991, Haglin 1995) by including both one-loop decay width and two-loop collision width contributions. The results are consistent with the small mass shift but large width increase found by Rapp (1997) and others, and the authors note their rho-nucleon scattering result is in qualitative agreement with the width increase found by Kondratyuk (1998). The large broadening and suppression of the rho peak strength is relevant for interpreting dilepton measurements from CERN/SPS experiments such as CERES, which show a suppressed rho peak in central heavy-ion collisions.
