# Electrodynamics for Nuclear Matter in Bulk

> A general approach to analyze the electrodynamics of nuclear matter in bulk is presented using the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation generalizing to the case of $N \simeq (m_{\rm Planck}/m_n)^3$ nucleons of mass $m_n$ the approach well tested in very heavy nuclei ($Z \simeq 10^6$). Particular attention is given to implement the condition of charge neutrality globally on the entire configuration, versus the one usually adopted on a microscopic scale. As the limit $N \simeq (m_{\rm Planck}/m_n)^3$ is approached the penetration of electrons inside the core increases and a relatively small tail of electrons persists leading to a significant electron density outside the core. Within a region of $10^2$ electron Compton wavelength near the core surface electric fields close to the critical value for pair creation by vacuum polarization effect develop. These results can have important consequences on the understanding of physical process in neutron stars structures as well as on the initial conditions leading to the process of gravitational collapse to a black hole.

# Electrodynamics for Nuclear Matter in Bulk

## Key claim

The paper argues that for a bulk nuclear configuration approaching the limit of \(N \simeq (m_{\rm Planck}/m_n)^3\) nucleons, global charge neutrality (total electron number equals total proton number) must replace the usual local charge neutrality condition (electron density equals proton density at every point). This leads to a positively charged baryonic core with a surface electric field close to the critical value for vacuum pair creation, a result that depends on the coordinated action of gravitational, weak, strong, and electromagnetic interactions together with Fermi-Dirac statistics.

## Method

The authors generalize the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation, previously tested for heavy nuclei up to \(Z \simeq 10^6\), to a system of \(N \simeq (m_{\rm Planck}/m_n)^3\) nucleons with a core radius of approximately 10 km. They impose global charge neutrality, the equilibrium condition from weak interactions (beta decay), and the null Fermi energy for degenerate electrons, then solve the resulting equation numerically by iterating the total number of protons and the core radius until consistency with the mass-radius relation is achieved. The eigenvalue for the first derivative of the Thomas-Fermi function at the center is determined by fulfilling the boundary conditions and the global neutrality constraint.

## Key numbers

- Core radius for the baryonic core: \(R_c \approx 10\) km, derived from \(R_c \approx (\hbar / m_\pi c) (m_{\rm Planck} / m_n)\).
- Melting density for nuclei in neutron stars: \(\rho_c = 4.34 \times 10^{13}\) g/cm\(^3\).
- Net charge of the positively charged baryonic core: \(N_{\rm net} = 0.92 (m / m_\pi)^2 (e / m_n \sqrt{G})^2 (1 / \alpha)^2\).
- Electric field at the baryonic core surface: \(E / E_c = 0.92\), where \(E_c = m^2 c^3 / (e \hbar)\) is the critical field for vacuum pair creation.
- Coulomb repulsive energy per nucleon: \(U^{\rm max}_{\rm coul} \approx 1.78 \times 10^{-6}\) MeV.
- Charge-to-mass ratio of the core: \(Q / (\sqrt{G} M) \approx 1.56 \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Thomas-Fermi approach developed for heavy nuclei by Pomeranchuk and Smorodinsky (1945), Gershtein and Zel'dovich (1970), and by Ruffini and Stella (1981) for nuclei up to \(Z \simeq 6000\), to the regime of bulk nuclear matter in neutron stars. It contrasts with the traditional treatment of neutron star matter, which imposes local charge neutrality (electron density equals proton density) as used in the work of Bethe et al. (1970) and Cameron (1970). The results enable new investigations into neutron star structure, pulsar electrodynamics, and the initial conditions for gravitational collapse to a black hole, where the presence of near-critical electric fields may significantly alter the energetics.
