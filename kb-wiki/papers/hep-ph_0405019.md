# Neutrino physics from new SNO and KamLAND data and future prospects

> We analyze the cumulative data from the latest SNO, KamLAND and other solar neutrino experiments in the standard scenario of three oscillating active neutrinos. We determine the solar neutrino oscillation parameters and obtain new bounds on $θ_x$. We also place constraints on the fraction of oscillating solar neutrinos that transform to sterile neutrinos with the $^8$B flux normalization left free. Concomitantly, we assess the sensitivity of future data from the SNO and KamLAND experiments to $θ_x$ and to the sterile neutrino content of the solar flux.

## Key claim

The paper analyzes cumulative data from SNO, KamLAND, and other solar neutrino experiments in a three-neutrino oscillation framework, finding that future SNO data will not significantly improve bounds on the mixing angle θ_x unless the atmospheric mass-squared difference δm²_a is at the lower edge of the range favored by Super-Kamiokande data, and that a nonnegligible sterile neutrino component in the solar flux will remain a possibility even after future measurements.

## Method

The authors performed a combined three-neutrino analysis of solar neutrino data (including the salt-phase SNO data), KamLAND reactor data, and CHOOZ data, treating the ⁸B flux normalization as a free parameter. They used the standard modification of the two-neutrino survival probability for the ν_e survival probability in the three-neutrino framework, and evaluated future sensitivity by simulating SNO measurements with expected 6.4% uncertainty on the neutral current (NC) flux and 5.5% uncertainty on the charged current (CC) flux, and KamLAND data with 7% uncertainty on the average ν_e survival probability P_ee.

## Key numbers

- Solar oscillation parameters from all existing data: δm²_s = 8.0^{+0.7}_{-0.6} × 10⁻⁵ eV², tan² θ_s = 0.45^{+0.17}_{-0.12} at 2σ C.L.
- Upper bound on θ_x from combined CHOOZ, KamLAND, and solar data: sin² 2θ_x ≤ 0.13 (0.20) at 90% C.L. (3σ) for δm²_a = 0.002 eV² (the Super-Kamiokande best-fit value)
- Fraction of solar neutrinos oscillating into active neutrinos: sin² α ≥ 0.91 (0.65) at 1σ (3σ) from all existing solar and KamLAND data
- Future SNO NC measurement expected uncertainty: 6.4% (statistical and systematic combined)
- Future SNO CC measurement expected uncertainty: 5.5% (statistical and systematic combined)
- Future precision on the fraction of solar neutrinos transforming to active species: σ_{sin² α}/sin² α = 16–17% at 1σ from future SNO and KamLAND data alone

## Context

This work extends the three-neutrino analysis of earlier SNO (Ahmad 2002) and KamLAND (Eguchi 2002) data that established the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution to the solar neutrino problem, incorporating the cumulative salt-phase SNO data and additional KamLAND data (2004). The analysis refines the upper bound on θ_x from the CHOOZ experiment (Apollonio 1999) and Palo Verde experiment (Boehm 2001), and assesses whether future SNO and KamLAND data can improve constraints on the sterile neutrino fraction beyond the approach of Barger (2001) that relied on the Standard Solar Model flux prediction. The results enable independent, uncorrelated tests of the sterile neutrino content of the solar flux using only SNO and KamLAND data.
