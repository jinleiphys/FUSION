# FENDL: A library for fusion research and applications

> The Fusion Evaluated Nuclear Data Library (FENDL) is a comprehensive and validated collection of nuclear cross section data coordinated by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) Nuclear Data Section (NDS). FENDL assembles the best nuclear data for fusion applications selected from available nuclear data libraries and has been under development for decades. FENDL contains sub-libraries for incident neutron, proton, and deuteron cross sections including general purpose and activation files used for particle transport and nuclide inventory calculations. We describe the history, selection of evaluations for the various sub-libraries (neutron, proton, deuteron) with the focus on transport and reactor dosimetry applications, the processing of the nuclear data for application codes, and the development of the TENDL-2017 library which is the currently recommended activation library for FENDL. We briefly describe the IAEA IRDFF library as the recommended library for dosimetry fusion applications. We also present work on validation of the neutron sub-library using a variety of fusion relevant computational and experimental benchmarks. A variety of cross section libraries are used for the validation work including FENDL-2.1, FENDL-3.1d, FENDL-3.2, ENDF/B-VIII.0, and JEFF-3.2 with the emphasis on the FENDL libraries. The results of the experimental validation showed that the performance of FENDL-3.2b is at least as good and in most cases better than FENDL-2.1. Future work will consider improved evaluations developed by the International Nuclear Data Evaluation Network (INDEN). Additional work will be needed to investigate differences in gas production in structural materials. Covariance matrices need to be updated to support the development of fusion technology. Additional validation work for high-energy neutrons, protons and deuterons, and the activation library will be needed.

# FENDL: A library for fusion research and applications

## Key claim
The Fusion Evaluated Nuclear Data Library (FENDL) version 3.2b demonstrates performance that is "at least as good and in most cases better than FENDL-2.1" (the previous ITER reference library) for fusion neutronics applications, based on experimental validation benchmarks. However, substantial differences in gas production (hydrogen and helium) in structural materials persist between libraries, and the authors note that copper evaluations remain "of similar quality as in FENDL-2.1 but not performing particularly well."

## Method
The authors assembled the FENDL library by selecting evaluations from existing nuclear data libraries (ENDF/B-VIII.0, JEFF-3.2, JENDL, TENDL-2017) for neutron, proton, and deuteron sub-libraries, processing them into ACE-formatted files using NJOY2016 with specific processing options (0.1 percent reconstruction tolerance, 293.6 K temperature, 20 probability bins in PURR). Validation was performed using both computational benchmarks (comparing neutron flux, nuclear heating, displacement damage, and tritium production across libraries) and experimental benchmarks using the MCNP transport code, comparing FENDL-3.2b against FENDL-2.1, FENDL-3.1d, ENDF/B-VIII.0, and JEFF-3.2.

## Key numbers
- Cross-section reconstruction tolerance in RECONR: 0.1 percent
- Temperature for Doppler broadening: 293.6 K
- Thinning tolerance for Doppler broadening in BROADR: 0.1 percent
- Maximum energy in BROADR: 20 MeV
- Number of probability bins in PURR: 20
- Number of resonance ladders in PURR: 100
- Bondarenko sigma_0 values used for processing: ranging from infinity (1.E+10) down to 0.001 barns, with up to ten values per material
- Neutron group structure: 211 energy groups (175 group Vitamin-J structure plus 36 groups up to 55 MeV with 1 MeV-width each)
- Gamma groups: 42 in Vitamin-J structure
- Legendre order: P-6 for transport correction to P-5
- EAF-2010 covered 816 nuclides for activation analyses
- TENDL-2017 is the currently recommended activation library for FENDL

## Context
This work extends the decades-long FENDL project coordinated by the IAEA Nuclear Data Section, replacing FENDL-2.1 as the ITER reference library. The paper describes the adoption of TENDL-2017 as the activation library, replacing the European Activation File (EAF-2010) which had been the standard for fusion activation analyses coupled with codes like FISPACT. The authors note that future work will incorporate improved evaluations from the International Nuclear Data Evaluation Network (INDEN) for materials including O, Cu, W, Li, B, and F, and that additional validation is needed for high-energy neutrons, protons, deuterons, and the activation library. Covariance matrices require updating to support fusion technology development.
