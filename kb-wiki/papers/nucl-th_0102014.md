# Utility of su(1,1)-Algebra in a Schematic Nuclear su(2)-Model

> The su(2)-algebraic model interacting with an environment is investigated from a viewpoint of treating the dissipative system. By using the time-dependent variational approach with a coherent state and with the help of the canonicity condition, the time-evolution of this quantum many-body system is described in terms of the canonical equations of motion in the classical mechanics. Then, it is shown that the su(1,1)-algebra plays an essential role to deal with this model. An exact solution with appropriate initial conditions is obtained by means of Jacobi's elliptic function. The implication to the dissipative process is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the su(1,1)-algebraic structure, revealed through the Schwinger boson representation of the su(2)-algebra, provides an exact classical solution for the time evolution of a nuclear su(2)-model coupled to a harmonic oscillator environment, but only in the zero-temperature case and under the condition that the particle-hole interaction strength χ is zero (or that the parameters satisfy χ²(1−ℏ/(2T))² < G²).

## Method
The authors start with a Hamiltonian combining an su(2) nuclear model (pairing or Lipkin type) with a harmonic oscillator representing the environment. Using the Schwinger boson representation for the su(2)-generators, they construct a coherent state as a trial state and impose canonicity conditions to parameterize it in terms of canonical variables. The time-dependent variational principle then yields classical canonical equations of motion, which for the case χ=0 reduce to a single differential equation for the variable T₀ that is solved exactly using Jacobi's elliptic function.

## Key numbers
- The Hamiltonian parameters used in a numerical example: ℏG/ε = 1/2, ω/ε = 1, γ√ℏ/ε = 0.1, E₀/ε = 0, with S/ℏ = 5 and T/ℏ = 1/2.
- The fundamental period of the solution is t₀ = 2π/ω₀, where ω₀ = (G/2)√[(τ₀−τ₂)(τ₁−τ₃)]·(π/K), with K the complete elliptic integral of the first kind.
- When the modulus k approaches 1 (specifically when τ₁ ∼ τ₂), the elliptic function reduces to sn(Zt,1) = tanh Zt, giving a monotonically increasing solution for T₀ from τ₀ at t=0 to τ₁ at t→∞.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous formalism (Tsue, Providencia, Kuriyama, Yamamura, 1999-2000) for describing thermal effects and dissipation in many-body systems using mixed-mode coherent states and su(1,1)-algebraic structures. It applies that formalism to a specific nuclear su(2)-model (the pairing model and the Lipkin model) interacting with a harmonic oscillator environment, which was previously studied in classical su(2)-models by Iachello and Yamamura. The exact elliptic-function solution enables future investigation of dissipative processes at finite temperature by replacing the coherent state with a mixed-mode coherent state.
