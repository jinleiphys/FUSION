# I=2 pi-pi Scattering from Fully-Dynamical Mixed-Action Lattice QCD

> We compute the I=2 pi-pi scattering length at pion masses of m_pi ~ 294, 348 and 484 MeV in fully-dynamical lattice QCD using Luscher's finite-volume method. The calculation is performed with domain-wall valence-quark propagators on asqtad-improved MILC configurations with staggered sea quarks at a single lattice spacing, b ~ 0.125 fm. Chiral perturbation theory is used to perform the extrapolation of the scattering length from lattice quark masses down to the physical value, and we find m_pi a_2 = -0.0426 +- 0.0006 +- 0.0003 +- 0.0018, in good agreement with experiment. The I=2 pi-pi scattering phase shift is calculated to be delta = -43 +- 10 +- 5 degrees at |p| ~ 544 MeV for m_pi ~ 484 MeV.

## Key claim

The I=2 pi-pi scattering length extrapolated to the physical pion mass using chiral perturbation theory yields m_pi a_2 = -0.0426 ± 0.0006 ± 0.0003 ± 0.0018, in good agreement with experiment, but this result depends on the assumption that lattice-spacing effects are much smaller than other uncertainties and are neglected, as the calculation was performed at a single lattice spacing.

## Method

The calculation used Luscher's finite-volume method to extract the I=2 pi-pi scattering length from fully-dynamical lattice QCD with domain-wall valence-quark propagators on asqtad-improved MILC configurations with staggered sea quarks (2+1 flavors) at a single lattice spacing b ~ 0.125 fm. The authors computed one-pion and two-pion correlation functions on three sets of configurations with pion masses of approximately 294, 348, and 484 MeV, then used chiral perturbation theory at one-loop level to extrapolate the scattering length from the lattice quark masses down to the physical value.

## Key numbers

- m_pi a_2 = -0.0426 ± 0.0006 (statistical) ± 0.0003 (fitting systematic) ± 0.0018 (theoretical from scale setting) at the physical pion mass after chiral extrapolation
- m_pi a_2 = -0.212 ± 0.024 ± 0.004 at m_pi = 293.7 ± 5.9 ± 4.4 ± 2.0 MeV (m_l = 0.007)
- m_pi a_2 = -0.222 ± 0.014 ± 0.009 at m_pi = 347.9 ± 3.3 ± 0.8 ± 3.4 MeV (m_l = 0.010)
- m_pi a_2 = -0.38 ± 0.03 ± 0.02 at m_pi = 484.4 ± 3.9 ± 4.9 ± 8.5 MeV (m_l = 0.020)
- I=2 pi-pi scattering phase shift delta = -43 ± 10 ± 5 degrees at |p| ~ 544 ± 14 ± 12 ± 10 MeV for m_pi ~ 484 MeV
- Lattice spacing b = 0.1274 ± 0.0007 ± 0.0003 fm from a two-parameter fit using the pion decay constant

## Context

This work extends previous quenched QCD calculations of I=2 pi-pi scattering (Sharpe 1992, Gupta 1993, Kuramashi 1993, Fukugita 1994, and others) and the fully-dynamical study by the CP-PACS collaboration (Yamazaki 2004) with two flavors of improved Wilson fermions at higher pion masses (0.5-1.1 GeV). The present calculation achieves lower pion masses (294-484 MeV) within the chiral regime using 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks, enabling a more reliable chiral extrapolation to the physical point. This enables future determination of the low-energy constants appearing in the chiral lagrangian, which are essential for computing more complex strong-interaction processes such as K to 2 pi decays.
