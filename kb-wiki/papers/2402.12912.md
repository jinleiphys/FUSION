# Effects of incompressibility $K_{0}$ in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Within the possible least uncertainty on the nuclear incompressibility $K_{0}$, we examine effects of $K_{0}$ in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. Based on simulations of Au + Au collision at 400 MeV/nucleon using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model, we find that the incompressibility $K_{0}$ indeed affects significantly the attainable density in central regions, and thus the particle productions and/or distributions at final states, e.g., nucleon rapidity distributions and yields of charged pions. Nevertheless, through examining the free neutron over proton ratios $n/p$, the neutron-proton differential transverse and directed flows as well as the charged pion ratio $π^{-}/π^{+}$ and its kinetic energy distribution, we find that these observables are less affected by the uncertainty of $K_{0}$, but mainly sensitive to the slope of symmetry energy at the saturation density. We also compare and discuss our results with the corresponding data.

## Key claim

Within the current tightest uncertainty on nuclear incompressibility (K0 = 230 ± 30 MeV), the incompressibility significantly affects the attainable density in central collision regions and final-state particle yields, but the free neutron-over-proton ratio n/p, neutron-proton differential flows, and the charged pion ratio π−/π+ and its kinetic energy distribution are less affected by K0 uncertainty and remain mainly sensitive to the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of central Au + Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon (impact parameter b = 0–2 fm) using an updated isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. They set K0 to three values (200, 230, and 260 MeV) while adjusting parameters x and z to keep the slope L of the symmetry energy at saturation density identical across different K0 settings, and for each K0 they also varied L among four values (33.06, 61.95, 92.66, and 123.73 MeV) to compare the relative effects.

## Key numbers

- K0 values examined: 200, 230, and 260 MeV, representing the range 230 ± 30 MeV.
- Au + Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 0–2 fm (reduced impact parameter b0 ≤ 0.15).
- For K0 = 200 MeV, the parameter σ = 1.0646; for K0 = 230 MeV, σ = 1.2652; for K0 = 260 MeV, σ = 1.4657.
- The slope L values used: 33.06, 61.95, 92.66, and 123.73 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at ρ0 is set as Esym(ρ0) = 32.5 + z MeV, with z values ranging from −1.482 to 5.8 MeV depending on K0 and L.
- The symmetry energy at ρ̃ ≈ 2ρ0/3 is constrained to 25.5 ± 1 MeV; specific values range from 24.60 to 26.06 MeV.
- Free nucleon criterion: relative distance ΔR > 3.575 fm or momentum difference Δp > 0.3 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends studies of the role of nuclear incompressibility in heavy-ion collisions, following prior investigations such as Cozma (2018) on nucleon elliptic flows with K0 from 210 to 280 MeV, Zan (2012) on fusion reactions with K0 from 195 to 225 MeV, and Bon (2018) on giant resonances in various nuclei with K0 from 210 to 240 MeV. The present study systematically examines the effect of K0 on symmetry-energy-sensitive observables in central intermediate-energy collisions, finding that ratios such as n/p, differential flows, and π−/π+ can largely suppress K0 uncertainty and thus enable cleaner extraction of the high-density symmetry energy. The results are compared with FOPI detector data from 2007, 2010, and 2012.
