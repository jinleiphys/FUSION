# Towards the critical behavior for the light nuclei by NIMROD detector

> The critical behavior for the light nuclei with A$\sim 36$ has been investigated experimentally by the NIMROD multi-detectors. The wide variety of observables indicate the critical point has been reached in the disassembly of hot nuclei at an excitation energy of 5.6$\pm$0.5 MeV/u.

## Key claim

The critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition in light nuclei with A ~ 36 is reached at an excitation energy of 5.6 ± 0.5 MeV/u, as indicated by a wide variety of observables; however, the observed minimum in the effective Fisher-law parameter is rather broad, and the claim is that the critical point "may have been reached" based on comparisons with model calculations.

## Method

The experiment used 47 MeV/u 40Ar beams from the TAMU K500 Superconducting Cyclotron incident on a 58Ni target, with the NIMROD multi-detector array (166 CsI detectors in 12 rings plus a neutron ball) to detect charged particles and neutrons. A new event-by-event reconstruction method was developed to identify quasi-projectile (QP) sources by combining three-source fits for light charged particles with a rapidity cut ( > 0.65 beam rapidity) for intermediate mass fragments (Z ≥ 4). The QP source velocity was determined from momentum conservation, and excitation energy distributions were deduced using an energy balance equation, with events selected for total charge Z_QP ≥ 12 from violent collisions.

## Key numbers

* Critical excitation energy: 5.6 ± 0.5 MeV/u, identified from the maximum in the mean normalized second moment ⟨S₂⟩, the normalized variance in Z_max/Z_QP distribution (NVZ), and the average atomic number of the second largest fragment ⟨Z₂max⟩.
* Effective Fisher-law parameter τ_eff ≈ 2.3, observed as a minimum in the excitation energy range of 5 to 6 MeV/u from power-law fits to charge distributions (Z = 2 – 7).
* Zipf's law parameter ξ ≈ 1, observed in the 5 – 6 MeV/u excitation energy range from rank-ordered fragment size distributions.
* Beam energy: 47 MeV/u for the 40Ar + 58Ni reaction.
* QP events selected with total charge number Z_QP ≥ 12.

## Context

This work extends previous Fisher Droplet Model analyses of liquid-gas phase transitions in nuclear systems (Fisher; Campi; Chomaz; Natowitz; D'Agostino) by focusing on light nuclei (A ~ 36) where Coulomb effects are reduced, as suggested by Natowitz. The results are compared with lattice gas model (LGM) and statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) calculations, which reproduce the observed behavior, while the sequential decay model fails. This enables future studies of critical phenomena in finite nuclear systems using light nuclei as a cleaner venue.
