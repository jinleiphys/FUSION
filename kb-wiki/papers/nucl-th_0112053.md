# Chiral SU(3) Symmetry and Strangeness

> In this talk we review recent progress on the systematic evaluation of the kaon and antikaon spectral functions in dense nuclear matter based on a chiral SU(3) description of the low-energy pion-, kaon- and antikaon-nucleon scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews progress on evaluating kaon and antikaon spectral functions in dense nuclear matter using a chiral SU(3) description, but the abstract glosses over the crucial condition that the antikaon spectral function requires a self-consistent treatment that simultaneously considers the in-medium modification of hyperon resonance structures, and that the results at twice nuclear saturation density should be considered cautiously because nuclear binding and correlation effects were not yet included.

## Method

The authors applied the χ-BS(3) approach, a chiral Bethe-Salpeter framework for the SU(3) flavor group, to meson-baryon scattering. They solved coupled-channel scattering equations incorporating all SU(3) channels (K̅N, πΛ, πΣ, ηΣ, ηΛ, KΞ) with correction terms from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, using large-Nc sum rules to reduce free parameters. Self-consistency was implemented by feeding back the in-medium modified antikaon spectral function into the antikaon-nucleon scattering process, using the free-space meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes from the χ-BS(3) approach.

## Key numbers

- The parameter f ≃ fπ ≃ 92.4 MeV, known from charged pion decay, appears in the Weinberg-Tomozawa term
- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the kaon quasi-particle energy increases, with the polarization function parameterized as Π_K(ω, q⃗) ≃ (1.1 m_K − ω + 0.2 q⃗²/m_K) × 46.8 MeV × ρ/ρ₀
- The parameterization is valid for kaon momenta smaller than |q⃗| < 600 MeV
- At nuclear saturation density, attractive mass shifts for the Λ(1405), Σ(1385), and Λ(1520) resonances are about 60 MeV, 60 MeV, and 100 MeV respectively
- At nuclear saturation density, the resonance widths increase to about 120 MeV, 70 MeV, and 90 MeV respectively
- The πΣ → πΣ reaction cross section is about 30 mb (including isospin two contribution)
- The χ-BS(3) results are trusted quantitatively only for √s < 1600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) approach to kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering developed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2001) and applies it to in-medium propagation. It addresses the long-standing problem that the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term fails to describe the repulsive K⁻p scattering length, as noted by Iwasaki (1996), and that the Λ(1405) resonance must be generated dynamically by coupled-channel effects. The results enable more realistic transport model calculations for heavy-ion reactions, improving upon earlier parameterizations by Brown and Lee (1997) and Cugnon (1996), and provide input for understanding kaon condensation in neutron stars as discussed by Brown and Lee (1997) and others.
