# Quasi-elastic scattering, RPA, 2p2h and neutrino--energy reconstruction

> We discuss some nuclear effects, RPA correlations and 2p2h (multinucleon) mechanisms, on charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions that do not produce a pion in the final state. We study a wide range of neutrino energies, from few hundreds of MeV up to 10 GeV. We also examine the influence of 2p2h mechanisms on the neutrino energy reconstruction.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE CCQE-like cross section data can be described with an axial mass \(M_A\) around 1.05 GeV only when both RPA correlations and 2p2h multinucleon mechanisms are included; without these nuclear effects, a fit requires a much larger \(M_A\) of 1.32–1.35 GeV, which is inconsistent with the world average of about 1.03 GeV.

## Method

The authors use a microscopic many-body model of the \(W\) gauge boson self-energy in the nuclear medium, limited to three-momentum and energy transfers below 1.2 GeV, to compute charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections on \(^{12}\)C. They separately calculate genuine quasielastic (QE) events (one nucleon ejected) and 2p2h events (two interacting nucleons ejected), including RPA (medium polarization) corrections. The model predictions are folded with the MiniBooNE neutrino and antineutrino fluxes and compared to double-differential cross section data, and also extended to higher energies for comparison with MINER\(\nu\)A data.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE CCQE-like data, when fit with a relativistic Fermi gas model without RPA and without multinucleon mechanisms, yield \(M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17\) GeV, much larger than the previous world average of about 1.03 GeV.
- The full model (QE with RPA plus 2p2h) describes the same data using \(M_A = 1.05\) GeV.
- For 3 GeV neutrinos on carbon, the 2p2h cross section is about 25% of the QE cross section; for 3 GeV antineutrinos, it is about 33%.
- The model is limited to three-momentum and energy transfers below 1.2 GeV, and results are presented for neutrino energies from a few hundred MeV up to 10 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model of Nieves (2004) and Nieves (2011) to neutrino energies up to 10 GeV, addressing the MiniBooNE \(M_A\) puzzle first reported by Aguilar-Arevalo (2010). The inclusion of both RPA correlations and 2p2h mechanisms resolves the discrepancy between the large axial mass required by simple Fermi gas fits and the world average, as also argued by Martini (2011). The results enable more reliable neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINER\(\nu\)A, and LBNE, and show that the distortion of the reconstructed energy spectrum from 2p2h events decreases steadily with increasing neutrino energy.
