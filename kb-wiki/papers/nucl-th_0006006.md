# A Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation for finite nuclei

> We develop a complete Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation to the ground state wave function and energy of finite nuclei. We apply it to spin-zero proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing within the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation (we neglect the Fock term), using a zero-range approximation to the relativistic pairing tensor. We study the effects of the pairing on the properties of the even-even nuclei of the isotopic chains of Ca, Ni and Sn (spherical) and Kr and Sr (deformed), as well as the $N$=28 isotonic chain, and compare our results with experimental data and with other recent calculations.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov (DHB) approximation for axially deformed finite nuclei, using a zero-range approximation to the meson-exchange pairing interaction, and find that the Dirac structure of the pairing field suppresses pairing in the saturated interior region relative to nonrelativistic calculations, leading to greater localization of the pairing field on the nuclear surface.

## Method

The authors derive self-consistency equations for the self-energy and pairing fields from a relativistic Lagrangian with σ, ω, ρ mesons and the photon, using a Gorkov-like factorization. They solve the Dirac-Gorkov equation in a static, axially deformed basis, truncating the sum over negative-frequency quasi-particle states to avoid renormalization, and apply the model to spin-zero proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing in even-even nuclei of the isotopic chains Ca, Ni, Sn (spherical) and Kr, Sr (deformed), plus the N=28 isotonic chain.

## Key numbers

- The wavelength of the N=16 shell in 16O is estimated as λ₁₆ ≈ 1.75 fm, using oscillator length b₀ ≈ A^(1/6) fm.
- The range of the σ meson is r_σ < 0.5 fm, and the longest-range term of the Gogny interaction has r₂ = 1.2 fm.
- The relativistic pairing gap falls to zero at a density about one-half of that at which it disappears in nonrelativistic calculations.

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (DHFB) approximation previously applied to nuclear matter by Guichon et al. (1996) and Carlson et al. (1997) to finite deformed nuclei, using a zero-range pairing interaction justified by the length-scale analysis of Meng (1998). It contrasts with the hybrid relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach using a nonrelativistic Gogny pairing interaction (e.g., Meng 1996, Lalazissis 1998), which cannot reliably extrapolate beyond the stability valley. The results enable future fully relativistic calculations of drip-line nuclei where coupling between bound and continuum states is essential.
