# Indications of a shallow potential in $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr fusion reactions

> Fusion data for $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr are analyzed by coupled-channels calculations. Puzzling features of a previous analysis are eliminated by applying a potential that has a shallow pocket in the entrance channel. Thus the observed $S$ factor for fusion, which develops a maximum at low energy, can be reproduced fairly well. The high-energy data can also be accounted for but that requires the use of a weak, short-ranged imaginary potential that absorbs the incoming flux near the location of the minimum of the potential pocket. Predictions of the fusion hindrance in other Ca+Zr systems are made and are compared with the systematics that has been developed previously.

## Key claim

The fusion hindrance observed at low energies in $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr can be reproduced by using a shallow pocket in the entrance channel potential (the M3Y+repulsion potential) rather than a Woods-Saxon potential with anomalously large diffuseness, but reproducing the high-energy data additionally requires a weak, short-ranged imaginary potential that absorbs flux near the pocket minimum.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed including one-phonon, two-phonon, and mutual excitations of the $2^+$ and $3^-$ states in both $^{48}$Ca and $^{96}$Zr (15 channels total in the rotating frame approximation). The ion-ion potential was constructed using the double-folding M3Y interaction supplemented with a repulsive contact term to produce a shallow pocket in the entrance channel. Fusion cross sections were determined by imposing ingoing-wave boundary conditions at the minimum of the pocket, and a short-ranged imaginary potential of the form $W(r) = -10\ \mathrm{MeV} / [1+\exp((r-r_p)/0.2\ \mathrm{fm})]$ was added to reproduce high-energy data.

## Key numbers

- The M3Y+repulsion potential and the Woods-Saxon potential both produce the same Coulomb barrier height, $V_{CB} = 95.4$ MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential with normal diffuseness $a = 0.677$ fm gives an overall $\chi^2/N \approx 4$ for the fusion data (based on statistical errors only).
- The pocket minimum for the M3Y+repulsion potential lies at 86.2 MeV center-of-mass energy.
- The energy $E_s$ where the $S$ factor for fusion has a maximum for $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr is $E_s = 88.1$ MeV, with a logarithmic derivative $L_s = 2.71\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}$.
- The reference energy $E_s^{\mathrm{ref}}$ for $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr is 97.4 MeV, so the measured $E_s$ is 9.3 MeV below this reference value.
- For $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr, the extrapolated $E_s = 93.2$ MeV; for $^{40}$Ca+$^{94}$Zr, $E_s = 86.4$ MeV; for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr, $E_s = 83.1$ MeV; for $^{48}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr, $E_s = 90.0$ MeV.
- The fusion cross section at $E_s$ for $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr is $\sigma_s = 0.030$ mb.
- The calculation including up to five-phonon octupole excitations in $^{96}$Zr has an average deviation of about 10% from the data, while the standard calculation deviates on average by 7%.

## Context

This work extends the M3Y+repulsion potential approach previously applied to other heavy-ion systems (Esbensen and collaborators, 2005; Misicu and Esbensen, 2006) to the $^{48}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr system, which had been analyzed by Stefanini (2008) using a Woods-Saxon potential with an anomalously large diffuseness of 0.85 fm. The paper resolves the puzzle that a large diffuseness was needed to fit both low- and high-energy data by showing that a shallow pocket potential with a short-ranged imaginary term can reproduce both regimes, and that the structure input for $^{48}$Ca (from inelastic scattering data of Fleming, 1977) was the key difference. The results enable predictions of the fusion hindrance energy $E_s$ for other Ca+Zr systems and confirm the systematics developed by Jiang (2006, 2007) relating $E_s$ to the number of valence neutrons and nuclear softness.
