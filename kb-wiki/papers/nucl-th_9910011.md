# Charge Symmetry Violation Effects in Pion Scattering off the Deuteron

> We discuss the theoretical and experimental situations for charge symmetry violation (CSV) effects in the elastic scattering of pi+ and pi- on deuterium (D) and 3He/3H. Accurate comparison of data for both types of targets provides evidence for the presence of CSV effects. While there are indications of a CSV effect in deuterium, it is much more pronounced in the case of 3He/3H. We provide a description of the CSV effect on the deuteron in terms of single- and double- scattering amplitudes. The Delta-mass splitting is taken into account. Theoretical predictions are compared with existing experimental data for pi-d scattering; a future article will speak to the pi-three nucleon case.

## Key claim

The paper argues that charge symmetry violation (CSV) effects in elastic pion-deuteron scattering are small and within current experimental uncertainties, but that double-scattering contributions can produce a modest angular dependence of the asymmetry \(A_{\pi}\) near 90 degrees, contrary to the angle-independent prediction of single-scattering approximations. The authors hedge that confirming these predictions requires data with 2-3 times better precision than currently available.

## Method

The authors calculate the \(\pi d\) elastic scattering amplitude using single- and double-scattering diagrams in the fixed-centers approximation, including the \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobar mass splitting as the primary source of CSV. They incorporate spin-flip and non-spin-flip parts of the elementary \(\pi N\) amplitude in the \(P_{33}\) wave, and add a Coulomb amplitude convoluted with the deuteron proton density. The asymmetry \(A_{\pi}\) is computed from the sum of single-scattering, double-scattering, and Coulomb contributions, linearized in the small \(\Delta\)-mass splitting parameter \(C_M\).

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta\)-multiplet mass splitting parameters are \(C_M = 4.6\ \text{MeV}\) and \(C_\Gamma = 1.7\ \text{MeV}\), derived from \(\Delta\) masses \(w_0 = 1232\ \text{MeV}\), \(a = 1231.8\ \text{MeV}\), \(b = 1.38\ \text{MeV}\), and \(c = 0.13\ \text{MeV}\).
- At \(T_\pi = 180\ \text{MeV}\), the calculated asymmetry \(A_{\pi}\) grows from 0.002 at \(\theta = 50^\circ\) to 0.015 at \(\theta = 85^\circ\).
- At \(T_\pi = 143\ \text{MeV}\), the experimental asymmetry is \(A_{\pi} \simeq 2\%\) (Masterson et al., 1982).
- For the \(^3\text{He}/^3\text{H}\) case, the ratio \(r_2 = 0.7 \pm 0.1\) at \(T_\pi = 256\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\theta = 82^\circ\) (Dhuga et al., 1996).
- The \(\Delta\) resonance full width is taken as \(\Gamma_0 = 120\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the single-scattering impulse approximation of Masterson et al. (1982) by including double-scattering terms, which had not been studied in detail for CSV effects. It addresses the discrepancy between the angle-independent CSV prediction of earlier models and the angular structure seen in some experimental data, particularly the LAMPF measurements at 143 MeV (Masterson et al., 1982) and 256 MeV (Masterson et al., 1984). The formalism developed here is intended as a foundation for a forthcoming analysis of the \(^3\text{He}/^3\text{H}\) system, where CSV effects are expected to be larger due to reduced spin-flip suppression and more numerous double-scattering diagrams.
