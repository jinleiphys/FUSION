# Potential relations for nonrelativistic and relativistic two-body Schroedinger equations

> Relations between nonrelativistic and relativistic two-body equations, also allowing for different masses, are studied and explicit expressions are given. One example is the Blankenbeclar Sugar equation. The corresponding expressions for the boosted two-body potentials are provided.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact algebraic relations that convert a relativistic two-body Schroedinger equation into a nonrelativistic form (and vice versa) for equal-mass particles, provided the relativistic energy-momentum relation \(E = 2\sqrt{m^2 + k^2}\) is retained; for unequal masses, the simple algebraic trick fails and a more complicated integral representation is required.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic two-body Schroedinger equation with kinetic energy \(2\omega = 2\sqrt{m^2 + k^2}\) and apply the operator \((2\omega + v)\) twice to obtain a nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation with a transformed potential \(V_{nr}\). They invert this relation to express the relativistic potential \(v\) in terms of the nonrelativistic T-matrix and bound-state wave function. For the general case of unequal masses, they use the completeness relation of bound and scattering states of an underlying relativistic three-dimensional equation (exemplified by the Blankenbeclar-Sugar equation) to construct the potential \(v\) that reproduces the same spectrum in the standard relativistic two-body Schroedinger form.

## Key numbers

- The bound-state eigenvalue of the nonrelativistic equation is \(M_b^2/4m - m\), which differs from the relativistic binding energy \(E_b \equiv M_b - 2m\) by the small amount \(E_b^2/4m\).
- A redefined binding energy \(\epsilon_b\) satisfies \(M_b \equiv \sqrt{4m^2 + 4\epsilon_b m}\), and in the nonrelativistic equation \(\epsilon_b = -\kappa^2/m\).
- The expansion of \(M_b\) in terms of \(\epsilon_b\) gives \(M_b = 2m + \epsilon_b - \epsilon_b^2/4m + \dots\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of relativistic kinematics for high-precision NN potentials such as CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2001), Nijmegen I/II (Stoks et al., 1994), and AV18 (Wiringa et al., 1995). It corrects the approximate analytical momentum-change transformation used in previous work by Kamada and Gloeckle (1995) for boosted potentials, and provides exact expressions that may alter the relativistic energy shifts in the triton binding energy reported there. The results enable consistent relativistic treatments of two-body systems with different masses and allow construction of boosted potentials for use in few-body calculations.
