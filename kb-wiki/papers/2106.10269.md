# Shell model analysis of the $ B(E2,2^+ \rightarrow 0^+)$'s in the A=70 T=1 triplet

> he $B(E2,2^+ \rightarrow 0^+)$ transition strengths of the T=1 isobaric triplet $^{70}$Kr, $^{70}$Br, $^{70}$Se, recently measured at RIKEN/RIBF, are discussed in terms of state of the art large scale shell model calculations using the JUN45 and JUN45+LNPS plus Coulomb interactions. In this letter we argue that, depending on the effective charges used, the calculations are either in line with the experimental data within statistical uncertainties, or the anomaly happens in $^{70}$Br, rather than $^{70}$Kr. In the latter case, we suggest that it can be due to the presence of a hitherto undetected 1$^+$ T=0 state below the yrast 2$^+$ T=1 state. Our results do not support a shape change of $^{70}$Kr with respect to the other members of the isobaric multiplet.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the measured $B(E2,2^+ \rightarrow 0^+)$ transition strengths in the A=70 T=1 isobaric triplet do not necessarily indicate a shape change of $^{70}$Kr relative to the other members; depending on the choice of effective charges, the calculations are either consistent with the data within uncertainties, or the anomaly appears in $^{70}$Br rather than $^{70}$Kr, possibly due to an undetected 1$^+$ T=0 state below the yrast 2$^+$ T=1 state.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed using the JUN45 interaction in a valence space including the 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0f5/2, and 0g9/2 orbits, and the JUN45+LNPS interaction in an extended space adding the 1d5/2 orbit, both above a $^{56}$Ni core. The calculations incorporated Coulomb interaction between protons and used two sets of effective charges: Dufour-Zuker (DZ) charges (qπ=1.31e, qν=0.46e) and standard (ST) charges (qπ=1.5e, qν=0.5e), with model-space dimensions up to O(10^9) allowing up to 10p-10h excitations.

## Key numbers

- The experimental MED (Mirror Energy Difference) for the 2+ states is -67.0 (7.5) keV, compared to the JUN45 prediction of -100 keV; the experimental TED (Triplet Energy Difference) is -45.2 (7.5) keV, compared to the JUN45 prediction of -17 keV.
- The calculated intrinsic shape parameter for the triplet members is β=0.22±0.05, with γ=32 degrees and fluctuations spanning 8 degrees to 60 degrees at the 1σ level.
- The calculated B(M1) transition probability from the 2+ T=1 state to the 1+ T=0 state in $^{70}$Br is 0.22 (2) μN^2 (with no gs quenching).
- The experimental Mp(E2) for $^{70}$Br is 38.1 (3.1) efm^2, while a linear fit from $^{70}$Kr and $^{70}$Se gives a value of 46.5 (4.6) efm^2 assuming isospin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the A=70 T=1 triplet measurements by Wimmer et al. (2021) at RIKEN/RIBF, which suggested a shape change of $^{70}$Kr based on deviations from isospin symmetry. The authors use shell model calculations with Coulomb corrections to show that standard isospin symmetry breaking effects can explain the data without invoking a shape change, and propose that the anomaly could instead be in $^{70}$Br due to an unobserved 1+ T=0 state. The work builds on prior studies of Coulomb Energy Differences and Mirror Energy Differences by Nolen (1969), Zuker (2002), and Bentley (2007), and uses the JUN45 interaction from Honma et al. (2009) and the LNPS interaction from Lenzi et al. (2010).
