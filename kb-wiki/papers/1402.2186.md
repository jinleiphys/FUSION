# Alpha-particle formation and decay rates from Skyrme-HFB wave functions

> $α$ decay is treated microscopically, where the unstable mother nucleus and residual daughter nucleus are described using HFB wave functions, obtained with the Skyrme effective interaction. From these wave functions the amplitude for forming $α$ particles in the mother nucleus is computed. Two different Skyrme parametrizations with different pairing properties are compared, and we find good agreement with experiment for relative decay rates in both cases. The absolute values of the decay rates are under-estimated.

# Alpha-particle formation and decay rates from Skyrme-HFB wave functions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that microscopic Skyrme-HFB wave functions can reproduce the relative α-decay rates across isotopic chains of Po, Rn, and Ra with good accuracy, but the absolute values of the decay rates are systematically underestimated by 3–6 orders of magnitude depending on the Skyrme parametrization and pairing interaction used.

## Method

The authors compute α-decay formation amplitudes from self-consistent spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions of mother and daughter nuclei, obtained with the Skyrme effective interactions SLy4 and UNEDF1 in the particle-hole channel and density-dependent contact pairing interactions in the particle-particle channel. The formation amplitude is evaluated by expanding the mother nucleus wave function in terms of the daughter nucleus and valence nucleons, then transforming to relative and center-of-mass coordinates using Talmi-Moshinsky oscillator brackets. The decay width is obtained via the microscopic R-matrix approach, using experimental Qα values to avoid the strong sensitivity of the Coulomb penetrability to the decay energy.

## Key numbers

- For 212Po, the experimental three-point mass differences are Δp_exp = 0.765 MeV and Δn_exp = 0.766 MeV; SLy4 with volume or surface pairing gives lowest quasiparticle energies close to these values, while UNEDF1 gives much smaller pairing: Ep_min = 0.144 MeV and En_min = 0.111 MeV.
- The mean log10 of the ratio of theoretical to experimental reduced widths at the touching radius (r0 = 1.2 fm) for 27 near-spherical Po, Rn, and Ra isotopes is −3.79 for SLy4 with volume pairing, −3.14 for SLy4 with surface pairing, and −5.71 for UNEDF1; the standard deviations are 0.32, 0.22, and 0.23, respectively.
- The α-particle wave function size parameter is chosen as bα = 1.41 fm; the δ-function approximation overestimates the formation amplitude by a factor of 10 or more compared to the full calculation.
- The oscillator basis is converged for Nmax ≥ 20 major shells; calculations use Nmax = 30 throughout.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extensive calculations for even-even near-spherical nuclei using SLy4 (Ward 2013) by introducing the UNEDF1 Skyrme parametrization (Kortelainen 2012) and investigating the δ-function approximation (Rasmussen 1963). The microscopic approach contrasts with phenomenological α-daughter potentials and semi-empirical formulas such as the Universal Decay Law (Qi 2009), which reproduce absolute decay rates well but do not capture nuclear structure effects. The results enable future work beyond the HFB level to incorporate additional correlations needed to reproduce absolute decay rates, while the relative rates already show good agreement with experiment across isotopic chains.
