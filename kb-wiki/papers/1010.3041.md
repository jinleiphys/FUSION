# Equation of state for the MCFL phase and its implications for compact star models

> Using the solutions of the gap equations of the magnetic-color-flavor-locked (MCFL) phase of paired quark matter in a magnetic field, and taking into consideration the separation between the longitudinal and transverse pressures due to the field-induced breaking of the spatial rotational symmetry, the equation of state (EoS) of the MCFL phase is self-consistently determined. This result is then used to investigate the possibility of absolute stability, which turns out to require a field-dependent bag constant to hold. That is, only if the bag constant varies with the magnetic field, there exists a window in the magnetic field vs. bag constant plane for absolute stability of strange matter. Implications for stellar models of magnetized (self-bound) strange stars and hybrid (MCFL core) stars are calculated and discussed.

## Key claim

Absolute stability of strange matter in the MCFL phase requires a field-dependent bag constant; only if the bag constant varies with the magnetic field does there exist a window in the magnetic field versus bag constant plane for absolute stability, contradicting the possibility of a constant bag constant for self-bound magnetized strange stars.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equations for the MCFL phase using a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a uniform magnetic field, obtaining the thermodynamic potential as a sum of charged and neutral quark contributions. From the thermodynamic potential, they derived the energy density, longitudinal pressure, and transverse pressure, accounting for the anisotropy induced by the magnetic field. The equations of state were then used to impose zero-pressure equilibrium conditions and the stability criterion that the energy per baryon must be below that of iron (approximately 930 MeV), both for the zero-field CFL case and the magnetized MCFL case.

## Key numbers

- The CFL gap was set to Δ_CFL = 10 MeV, with coupling constant G = 4.32 GeV^{-2} and cutoff Λ = 1 GeV.
- For the CFL phase at zero field, the maximum bag constant for absolute stability corresponds to a chemical potential μ_0 = 310 MeV at zero pressure.
- The splitting between parallel and perpendicular pressures reaches approximately 10% of the zero-field isotropic pressure at a field strength of 3 × 10^{18} G for μ = 500 MeV and B = 58 MeV/fm^3.
- The energy density shift in the ε-p_∥ relation at H̃ ∼ 10^{18} G is about 200 MeV/fm^3 relative to the zero-field value at the same pressure.
- The parallel pressure in the MCFL phase reaches zero at field strengths of order ≥ 10^{19} G for μ = 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superconducting quark matter in magnetic fields, particularly the MCFL phase introduced by Cristina-1, Cristina-2, and Cristina-3, and contrasts with earlier CFL models at nonzero field that used a single fixed gap (Aurora). The self-consistent treatment shows that the energy per baryon increases with magnetic field, contrary to those earlier claims. The results enable future calculations of mass-radius relations for magnetized strange stars and hybrid stars with MCFL cores, and provide a framework to test whether observed neutron star masses and radii can distinguish between hadronic and color-superconducting interiors, as discussed by Alford (2010) and others.
