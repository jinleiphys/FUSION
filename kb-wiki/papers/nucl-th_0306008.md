# Bertini intra-nuclear cascade implementation in Geant4

> We present here a intra-nuclear cascade model implemented in Geant4 5.0. The cascade model is based on re-engineering of INUCL code. Models included are Bertini intra-nuclear cascade model with exitons, pre-equilibrium model, nucleus explosion model, fission model, and evaporation model. Intermediate energy nuclear reactions from 100 MeV to 3 GeV energy are treated for proton, neutron, pions, photon and nuclear isotopes. We represent overview of the models, review results achieved from simulations and make comparisons with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Bertini intra-nuclear cascade model implemented in Geant4 5.0, based on re-engineering of the INUCL code, which is tested for bullet kinetic energies between 100 MeV and 3 GeV but shows approximate physical foundations below about 200 MeV and breaks down above 5-10 GeV.

## Method

The implementation solves the Boltzmann equation on average using a three-region approximation to the nuclear density distribution, with relativistic kinematics applied throughout the cascade. The cascade is stopped when all particles that can escape the nucleus have done so, after which the residual excitation energy feeds a pre-equilibrium exciton model (Griffin 1966), followed by a fission model and a Weisskopf evaporation model (Dostrowsky 1959, 1960). Path lengths are sampled using local density and free nucleon-nucleon cross-sections parameterized from Barashenkov (1972), with tabulated total reaction cross-sections from Letaw (1983, 1993) and Pearlstein (1989).

## Key numbers

- Energy range tested: 100 MeV to 3 GeV for proton-induced reactions, with detailed comparisons from 160 to 800 MeV
- Cutoff energy for cascade particle transport: 2 MeV (E_cutoff)
- Cutoff energy for evaporation chain: 0.1 MeV (E_excitation), with gamma emission cutoff at 10^-15 MeV
- Fermi break-up allowed only for light nuclei with A < 12 and 3(A-Z) < Z < 6, when E_excitation > 3 E_binding
- For targets with 1 < A < 4, a single-layer nuclear model with radius 8.0 fm is used
- For targets with A > 11, three concentric spheres are used with radii defined by C1 = 3.3836 A^(1/3) and C2 = 1.7234
- Timing benchmark: single PC with 300 MHz PII processor for Al, Fe, and Pb nuclei with proton bullet at 1-8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Bertini intra-nuclear cascade approach (Bertini 1968, 1969, 1971) originally implemented in codes such as HETC (Alsmiller 1990), by re-engineering the INUCL code (Titarenko 1999) into the Geant4 hadronic physics framework. The implementation enables simulation of intermediate-energy nuclear reactions for proton, neutron, pion, photon, and nuclear isotope projectiles within Geant4, with planned future work to extend the upper energy limit and tune performance.
