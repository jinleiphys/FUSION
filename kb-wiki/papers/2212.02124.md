# Charge-dependent anisotropic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions from relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic expansion

> We have investigated the charge-dependent anisotropic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, using relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamics (RRMHD). We consider the optical Glauber model as an initial model of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and the solution of the Maxwell equations with source term of the charged particles in two colliding nuclei as initial electromagnetic fields. The RRMHD simulation is performed with these initial conditions in Au-Au and Cu-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200$ GeV. We have calculated the charge-odd contribution to the directed flow $Δv_1$ and elliptic flow $Δv_2$ in both collisions based on electric charge distributions as a consequence of RRMHD. Our results show that the $Δv_1$ and $Δv_2$ are approximately proportional to the electrical conductivity ($σ$) of the medium. In the $σ=0.023~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ case, our result of $Δv_1$ is consistent with STAR data in Au-Au collisions. Furthermore, in Cu-Au collisions, $Δv_1$ has a non-zero value at $η= 0$. We conclude that the charge-dependent anisotropic flow is a good probe to extract the electrical conductivity of the QGP medium in high-energy heavy-ion experiments.

## Key claim

The charge-odd contributions to directed flow Δv₁ and elliptic flow Δv₂ are approximately proportional to the electrical conductivity σ of the quark-gluon plasma medium, and at σ = 0.023 fm⁻¹ the calculated Δv₁ agrees with STAR data in Au-Au collisions, but only under the specific initial conditions and model assumptions employed (optical Glauber model, ideal gas equation of state, and neglect of final-state interactions).

## Method

The authors performed relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic (RRMHD) simulations in Milne coordinates using the optical Glauber model for the initial QGP energy density profile and analytic solutions of Maxwell equations with source terms from moving charged nuclei as initial electromagnetic fields. The simulations used an ideal gas equation of state p = e/3 and Ohm's law in its simplest covariant form to close the system, with the Cooper-Frye prescription to convert freezeout hypersurface information into hadron distributions. The code was run for Au-Au and Cu-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, starting at proper time τ₀ = 0.4 fm and terminating when energy density fell below 0.15 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at (x⊥, ηs) = (0 fm, 0) set to e₀ = 55 GeV/fm³
- Electrical conductivity values used: σ = 0.0058, 0.023, and 0.1 fm⁻¹
- At σ = 0.023 fm⁻¹, the calculated Δv₁ is consistent with STAR data in Au-Au collisions
- In Cu-Au collisions, Δv₁ has a non-zero value at η = 0
- Plasma β (ratio of pressure to electromagnetic energy density) evaluated to β ∼ 10³, with magnetic field strength |B²| ∼ 0.01 GeV/fm³
- Electric charge density is very small: √(ħc) q/p ∼ 10⁻⁴
- Collision hardness parameter αH = 0.05
- Longitudinal profile parameters: ηm = 3.36, wη = 4.0, ηflat = 5.9
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.4 fm
- Freezeout energy density threshold: 0.15 GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RRMHD framework (Nakamura 2022) which showed that conduction current from Ohm's law significantly affects directed flow in asymmetric collisions, contrasting with relativistic ideal MHD studies (Inghirami 2016, 2019) that found negligible magnetic field effects on charged hadron collective flow. The results enable extraction of the QGP electrical conductivity from experimental charge-dependent flow measurements at RHIC and LHC, complementing lattice QCD estimates of σ = (5.8 ± 2.9)/ħc fm⁻¹ at T = 250 MeV (Aarts 2007, Ding 2010, Brandt 2012, Francis 2012) and addressing the need for dynamical electromagnetic response models beyond the ideal MHD limit.
