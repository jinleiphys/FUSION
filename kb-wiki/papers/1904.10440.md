# Minimally-Entangled State Preparation of Localized Wavefunctions on Quantum Computers

> Initializing a single site of a lattice scalar field theory into an arbitrary state with support throughout the quantum register requires ${\cal O}(2^{n_Q})$ entangling gates on a quantum computer with $n_Q$ qubits per site. It is conceivable that, instead, initializing to functions that are good approximations to states may have utility in reducing the number of required entangling gates. In the case of a single site of a non-interacting scalar field theory, initializing to a symmetric exponential wavefunction requires $n_Q-1$ entangling gates, compared with the $2^{n_Q-1} + n_Q-3 + δ_{n_Q,1}$ required for a symmetric Gaussian wavefunction. In this work, we explore the initialization of 1-site ($n_Q=4$), 2-site ($n_Q=3$) and 3-site ($n_Q=3$) non-interacting scalar field theories with symmetric exponential wavefunctions using IBM's quantum simulators and quantum devices (Poughkeepsie and Tokyo). With the digitizations attainable with $n_Q = 3,4$, these tensor-product wavefunctions are found to have large overlap with a Gaussian wavefunction, and provide a suitable low-noise initialization for improvement and \emph{Somma Inflation}. In performing these simulations, we have employed a workflow that interleaves calibrations to mitigate systematic errors in production. The calibrations allow tolerance cuts on gate performance including the fidelity of the symmetrizing Hadamard gate, both in vacuum ($|{\bf 0}\rangle^{\otimes n_Q}$) and in medium ($n_Q-1$ qubits initialized to an exponential function). The results obtained in this work are relevant to systems beyond scalar field theories, such as the deuteron radial wavefunction, 2- and 3-dimensional cartesian-space wavefunctions, and non-relativistic multi-nucleon systems built on a localized eigenbasis.

## Key claim

Initializing a single site of a non-interacting scalar field theory to a symmetric exponential wavefunction requires only \(n_Q-1\) entangling gates, compared to the \(2^{n_Q-1} + n_Q - 3 + \delta_{n_Q,1}\) gates needed for a symmetric Gaussian wavefunction, but the exponential wavefunction is only a suitable low-noise starting point for further improvement and Somma Inflation when the digitization is limited to \(n_Q = 3\) or \(4\) qubits per site, where its overlap with the desired Gaussian ground state is large.

## Method

The authors designed quantum circuits that prepare a symmetric exponential wavefunction on \(n_Q\) qubits by applying single-qubit \(R_y\) rotations with angles \(\theta_\ell = \arctan \exp(2^\ell \alpha)\) to \(n_Q-1\) qubits, then using a Hadamard gate and \(n_Q-1\) CNOT gates to duplicate and symmetrize the distribution. They implemented these circuits on IBM's Poughkeepsie 20-qubit superconducting device and on Qiskit simulators for 1-site (\(n_Q=4\)), 2-site (\(n_Q=3\)), and 3-site (\(n_Q=3\)) non-interacting scalar field theories, interleaving calibration workflows that included multi-qubit measurement-error correction, vacuum and in-medium Hadamard tuning, and CNOT error mitigation via extrapolation over circuits with odd numbers \(r = 1,3,5,7\) of CNOT gates per CNOT.

## Key numbers

* A symmetric exponential wavefunction on \(n_Q\) qubits requires \(n_Q-1\) entangling gates, while a symmetric Gaussian wavefunction requires \(2^{n_Q-1} + n_Q - 3 + \delta_{n_Q,1}\) entangling gates.
* For the 1-site system with \(n_Q=4\), the vacuum Hadamard effective gate application (EGA) had mean \(\mu_{\rm vac} = 0.0280(9)\) and standard deviation \(\sigma_{\rm vac} = 0.0051(7)\); the in-medium EGA had \(\mu_{\rm med} = -0.012(1)\) and \(\sigma_{\rm med} = 0.045(1)\).
* On IBM's Poughkeepsie device, the most-connected qubit has 6 nearest neighbors, allowing a localized exponential to be prepared with up to \(n_Q = 7\) using direct connectivity, or up to \(n_Q = 18\) if CNOTs are reorganized for nearest-neighbor connectivity.
* Each state preparation circuit was measured with 8000 shots for the single-site implementations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior analysis of digitized scalar field theory on NISQ-era devices (Klco 2018), which studied gate fidelity and digitization artifacts for 1-site and 2-site \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory. It builds on the Somma Inflation proposal (Somma 2016) for polynomially-efficient broadening of Gaussian wavefunctions, and on the general reflection operators discussed in a 2018 work by another group. The results enable low-noise initialization of localized wavefunctions for subsequent time evolution or adiabatic state preparation in scalar field theories, and are relevant to deuteron radial wavefunctions, cartesian-space wavefunctions in 2 and 3 dimensions, and non-relativistic multi-nucleon systems built on a localized eigenbasis.
