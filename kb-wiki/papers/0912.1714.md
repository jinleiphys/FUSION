# Low energy universality and scaling of Van der Waals forces

> At long distances interactions between neutral ground state atoms can be described by the Van der Waals potential V(r) =-C6/r^6-C8/r^8 - ... . In the ultra-cold regime atom-atom scattering is dominated by s-waves phase shifts given by an effective range expansion p cot d0 (p) = -1/a0 + r0 p^2/2 + ... in terms of the scattering length a0 and the effective range r0. We show that while for these potentials the scattering length cannot be predicted, the effective range is given by the universal low energy theorem r0 = A + B/a0+ C/a0^2 where A,B and C depend on the dispersion coefficients Cn and the reduced di-atom mass. We confront this formula to about a hundred determinations of r0 and a0 and show why the result is dominated by the leading dispersion coefficient C6. Universality and scaling extends much beyond naive dimensional analysis estimates.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for atom-atom scattering described by Van der Waals potentials, the effective range \(r_0\) follows a universal low-energy theorem \(r_0 = A + B/\alpha_0 + C/\alpha_0^2\) that depends only on the dispersion coefficients \(C_n\) and the reduced di-atom mass, with the leading \(C_6\) term dominating the result; the authors hedge that while the scattering length \(\alpha_0\) cannot be predicted, the effective range is universally correlated with it.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for two atoms interacting via the Van der Waals potential \(V(r) = -\sum_{n=6}^\infty C_n/r^n\) in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, using a superposition principle for s-wave scattering to decompose the wave function into regular and irregular solutions. They derived the effective range formula \(r_0 = A + B/\alpha_0 + C/\alpha_0^2\) from zero-energy wave functions and the effective range expansion, then compared this prediction to about a hundred determinations of \(r_0\) and \(\alpha_0\) from potential calculations for various di-atomic systems. They also analyzed the effect of adding a \(1/r^8\) correction via perturbation theory and computed universal functions for phase-shift determination.

## Key numbers

- For the pure \(1/r^6\) Van der Waals potential, the effective range in VdW units is \(r_0/R_6 = 1.39473 - 1.33333\,(R_6/\alpha_0) + 0.637318\,(R_6^2/\alpha_0^2)\) (Eq. 14).
- A \(\chi^2\) fit to 82 data points (excluding outliers) yielded coefficients \(A = 1.31\), \(B = -1.57\), and \(C = 0.66\), in good agreement with the pure \(1/r^6\) prediction.
- The Van der Waals length \(R_6\) ranges from 10.1610 a.u. (He-He) to 215.0006 a.u. (Fr-Fr), with the dimensionless correction coefficients \(g_1 \sim 10^{-2}\) and \(g_2 \sim 10^{-4}\) for most systems (Table I).
- The leading correction from a \(1/r^8\) term scales as \(\Delta r_0 \sim g_1 R_6 \log g_1 + \mathcal{O}(g_1)\), with \(g_1\) values typically around \(10^{-2}\).
- The universal functions for phase-shift determination show little dependence on \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) at momenta as large as \(k R_6 \sim 10\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum-defect theoretical approach of Gao (1998, 1999) and others (PhysRevA.48.546, PhysRevA.59.1998) by systematically demonstrating the universal correlation between effective range and scattering length for Van der Waals forces across many atomic systems. The results enable accurate low-energy atom-atom scattering descriptions using only the leading \(C_6\) coefficient and two parameters, and suggest that similar universality patterns may emerge for other power-law forces in molecular interactions. The approach also parallels nuclear physics applications where similar effective range relations have been used to check SU(4) Wigner symmetry in nucleon-nucleon interactions (Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola, 2008).
