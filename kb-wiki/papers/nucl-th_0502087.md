# Formal and Physical R-matrix Parameters

## Key claim

The relationship between formal R-matrix parameters (level energies E_λ and reduced width amplitudes γ_λc) and "physical" resonance parameters (resonance energies and observed partial widths) is not one-to-one; the conversion depends on boundary condition constants B_c and requires either iterative redefinition of B_c for each level or solution of a nonlinear eigenvalue equation, with differences between formal and observed partial widths often on the order of 30%.

## Method

The author derives the connection between the single-level R-matrix cross section formula and the Breit-Wigner formula, identifying the physical resonance energy E_R with the R-matrix pole energy E_λ when boundary conditions are set to B_c = S_c(E_λ), and defining observed partial widths via a denominator containing the energy derivative of the shift function. For multi-level cases, the author presents two approaches: (1) iterative transformation of boundary condition constants B_c using orthogonal matrix diagonalization to re-express E_λ and γ_λc for each level, and (2) solution of a nonlinear eigenvalue equation involving the matrix ℰ = e − Σ_c γ_c γ_c^T [S_c(E) − B_c] to obtain all physical resonance energies and on-resonance reduced-width amplitudes simultaneously. The reverse transformation from physical parameters back to R-matrix parameters is accomplished via a generalized eigenvalue problem involving matrices M and N constructed from the physical parameters.

## Key numbers

- The difference between formal and observed partial widths is often on the order of 30%, a large factor compared to typical experimental uncertainties.
- For the J^π = 1^- states in the ¹⁶O system (¹²C+α and ¹⁶O+γ channels), the R-matrix pole energies are E_1 = −0.0451 MeV, E_2 = 2.845 MeV, E_3 = 11.71 MeV, with reduced width amplitudes γ_1α = 0.0793 MeV^(1/2), γ_2α = 0.330 MeV^(1/2), γ_3α = 1.017 MeV^(1/2).
- The physical resonance energies derived from these parameters are Ẽ_1 = −0.0451 MeV, Ẽ_2 = 2.400 MeV, Ẽ_3 = 8.00 MeV.
- The observed partial widths for the three levels are: Γ_α^o = 0.359 MeV (level 2) and 9.041 MeV (level 3); Γ_γ^o = 55.1 meV (level 1), 8.64 meV (level 2), and 54.2 meV (level 3).
- The factor [1 + γ̃_iα² dS_α/dE(Ẽ_i)] relating formal and physical widths takes values 1.0050, 1.2034, and 1.0220 for the three levels.

## Context

This lecture extends the foundational R-matrix formalism of Lane and Thomas (1958) by clarifying the practical relationship between formal R-matrix parameters and experimentally meaningful resonance parameters, a distinction often glossed over in applications. The eigenvalue approach for extracting physical parameters builds on work by Barker (1971), Brune (2002), and Angulo and Descouvemont (2000), while the complex-pole eigenvalue method of Hale, Brown, and Jarmie (1987) is noted as a theoretically preferable but computationally more complex alternative. The example using parameters from Azuma et al. (1994) for the ¹⁶O system demonstrates the concrete numerical differences between formal and physical parameters, enabling practitioners to correctly interpret R-matrix fits and incorporate results from level compilations or shell-model calculations into R-matrix parameterizations.
