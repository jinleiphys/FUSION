# Role of spectroscopic factors in the potential-model description of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction

> In standard potential-model descriptions of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction the Be7+p spectroscopic factors S appear in the cross section. We argue that the microscopic substructure effects which are represented by S are short-ranged and cannot affect the asymptotic normalization of the wave function. We believe that the standard way of describing reactions in a potential model may be incorrect and the low-energy cross section should not depend on S in the case of external capture reactions, like Be7(p,gamma)B8

## Key claim

The conventional potential-model treatment of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction multiplies the cross section by spectroscopic factors S, but the author argues that these short-range microscopic substructure effects should not affect the asymptotic normalization of the wave function and therefore the low-energy cross section for this peripheral capture reaction should be independent of S.

## Method

The author compares the relative-motion wave function and the spectroscopic amplitude from a microscopic cluster model of Be8, using the MN effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with cluster size parameters beta=0.4 fm^-2. The spectroscopic amplitude g(r) and the relative-motion wave function chi(r) are shown to coincide beyond approximately 7 fm, demonstrating that antisymmetrization effects are confined to the internal region. The author then argues that multiplying a potential-model wave function by sqrt(S) modifies it asymptotically as well, which is incorrect for peripheral reactions where only the asymptotic parts matter.

## Key numbers

- The zero-energy astrophysical S-factor from the microscopic cluster model is S_17(0) = 37.8 (c_1^2 + c_2^2) eVb, where c_1=0.302 and c_2=0.763 are the asymptotic normalization constants for channel spins I=1 and I=2 respectively
- The spectroscopic factors from the cluster model are S^2_{3/2,2}=0.915 and S^2_{3/2,1}=0.205 for the Be7 ground state (I_7=3/2) channels, with a total S=1.12 for J=2
- The spectroscopic factor for the channel containing the excited state of Be7 (I_7=1/2) is S^2_{1/2,1}=0.25
- The binding energy of Be8 relative to the Be7+p threshold is -137 keV
- The most effective reaction energy in the sun is E_cm=20 keV, where the proton hits the Coulomb barrier at about 250 fm

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing uncertainty in S_17(0), the most uncertain input parameter in solar models according to Adelberger (1998). It extends the microscopic cluster model calculations of Csoto, Langanke, Koonin, and Shoppa (1995) and the potential-model studies of Brown, Csoto, and Sherr (1996). The paper suggests that the standard use of spectroscopic factors in potential models, as done in works by Barker (1995) and others, may be incorrect for peripheral capture reactions, and calls for a thorough study of the connection between microscopic and macroscopic approaches similar to that presented by Varga and Lovas (1991) for nuclear structure.
