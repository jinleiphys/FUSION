# Algebraic Model for scattering of three-s-cluster systems. II. Resonances in the three-cluster continuum of 6He and 6Be

> The resonance states embedded in the three-cluster continuum of 6He and 6Be are obtained in the Algebraic Version of the Resonating Group Method. The model accounts for a correct treatment of the Pauli principle. It also provides the correct three-cluster continuum boundary conditions by using a Hyperspherical Harmonics basis. The model reproduces the observed resonances well and achieves good agreement with other models. A better understanding for the process of formation and decay of the resonance states in six-nucleon systems is obtained.

## Key claim

The Algebraic Model for three-cluster scattering, using a Hyperspherical Harmonics basis and full antisymmetrization, reproduces the observed low-lying resonances in 6He and 6Be with reasonable agreement to experiment, though the calculated 2+ resonance energies in both nuclei are systematically higher than the measured values, likely due to the omission of LS forces.

## Method

The authors applied the Algebraic Version of the Resonating Group Method to the three-cluster configurations alpha+n+n (6He) and alpha+p+p (6Be), using the Volkov NN potential and including Coulomb interactions. The model space was defined by Hyperspherical Harmonics up to hypermomentum K=10 in both the internal and asymptotic regions, with a matching point at oscillator quantum number N=50. Resonance positions and widths were extracted from the eigenchannel representation of the S-matrix by scanning eigenphase shifts and applying the conditions d^2 delta/dE^2 = 0 and Gamma = 2[d delta/dE]^{-1} at the resonance energy.

## Key numbers

- 6He 2+ resonance: E = 1.490 MeV, Gamma = 0.168 MeV (experiment: E = 0.822 +/- 0.025 MeV, Gamma = 0.133 +/- 0.020 MeV)
- 6Be 0+ resonance: E = 1.172 MeV, Gamma = 0.072 MeV (experiment: E = 1.371 MeV, Gamma = 0.092 +/- 0.006 MeV)
- 6Be 2+ resonance: E = 3.100 MeV, Gamma = 0.798 MeV (experiment: E = 3.04 +/- 0.05 MeV, Gamma = 1.16 +/- 0.06 MeV)
- Oscillator radius b = 1.37 fm, fixed by optimizing the alpha-particle ground-state energy with the Volkov interaction
- Effective charge for the two-cluster configuration alpha+2p in 6Be: Z = 8/sqrt(3) e^2 approximately 6.65
- Second resonance in 6He 0+2: E = 2.1 MeV, Gamma = 4.3 MeV
- Second resonance in 6He 2+2: E = 3.7 MeV, Gamma = 5.0 MeV
- Second resonance in 6Be 0+2: E = 3.5 MeV, Gamma = 6.1 MeV
- Second resonance in 6Be 2+2: E = 5.2 MeV, Gamma = 5.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of six-nucleon systems in the three-cluster model (Vasilevsky 1996, 1997) from bound state properties to continuum resonances. The results are compared with three alternative approaches: the Complex Scaling Method (Csoto 1994), the Hyperspherical Harmonics Method (Danilin 1991), and the Continuation in the Coupling Constant Method (Suzuki and Tanaka 1997). The paper demonstrates that the Algebraic Model provides a feasible alternative to these methods for describing three-cluster continuum states, and confirms the existence of second, very broad resonance states previously predicted by CSM calculations (Aoyama 1995).
