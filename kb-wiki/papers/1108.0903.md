# Resolving the Axial Mass Anomaly in neutrino Scattering

> We present a parametrization of the observed enhancement in the transverse electron quasielastic (QE) response function for nucleons bound in carbon as a function of the square of the four momentum transfer (Q2) in terms of a correction to the magnetic form factors of bound nucleons. The parametrization should also be applicable to the transverse cross section in neutrino scattering. If the transverse enhancement originates from meson exchange currents (MEC), then it is theoretically expected that any enhancement in the longitudinal or axial contributions is small. We present the predictions of the "Transverse Enhancement" model (which is based on electron scattering data only) for the neutrino and anti-neutrino differential and total QE cross sections for nucleons bound in carbon. The 2Q2 dependence of the transverse enhancement is observed to resolve much of the long standing discrepancy ("Axial Mass Anomaly}) in the QE total cross sections and differential distributions between low energy and high energy neutrino experiments on nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing "Axial Mass Anomaly", the discrepancy between low-energy and high-energy neutrino quasielastic cross sections on nuclear targets, can be resolved by including a transverse enhancement (attributed to meson exchange currents) in the vector response, without modifying the axial form factor from its free-nucleon value of \(M_A^{free}=1.014\pm0.014\) GeV.

## Method

The authors parametrize the observed enhancement in the transverse electron quasielastic response function for nucleons bound in carbon as a function of \(Q^2\) using a fit to inclusive electron scattering data from the JUPITER collaboration (Jefferson Lab experiment E04-001) and earlier extractions by Carlson et al. The transverse enhancement ratio \(\mathcal{R}_T\) is then applied as a correction to the magnetic form factors of bound nucleons in the "Independent Nucleon" model for neutrino scattering, while leaving the axial form factor unchanged. Predictions for \(\nu_\mu\) and \(\bar{\nu}_\mu\) differential and total quasielastic cross sections on carbon are compared to MiniBooNE and NOMAD data.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon axial mass: \(M_A^{free} = 1.014 \pm 0.014\) GeV (from hydrogen and deuterium experiments).
- Effective axial mass extracted from low-energy neutrino experiments on nuclear targets: \(M_A^{eff} \approx 1.30\) GeV.
- Axial mass extracted from a global analysis of high-energy neutrino experiments on nuclear targets by Kuzmin et al.: \(0.979 \pm 0.016\) GeV.
- Transverse enhancement ratio parametrization: \(\mathcal{R}_T = 1 + A Q^2 e^{-Q^2/B}\) with \(A = 6.0\) and \(B = 0.34\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- Transverse enhancement is maximal near \(Q^2 = 0.3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and small for \(Q^2 > 1.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- For 1 GeV neutrinos, maximum accessible \(Q^2\) is 1.3 (GeV/c)\(^2\); fits yield \(M_A \approx 1.2\) GeV.
- For 3 GeV neutrinos, maximum accessible \(Q^2\) is 4.9 (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the observation from electron scattering on nuclei (Donnelly et al., Carlson et al.) that the transverse quasielastic response is enhanced relative to independent-nucleon models, while the longitudinal response is well described. The "Transverse Enhancement" model resolves the discrepancy between low-energy MiniBooNE data and high-energy NOMAD data that had previously been attributed to an ad-hoc change in the axial mass. The approach enables neutrino experiments to use free-nucleon axial form factors with a nuclear correction derived solely from electron scattering data, and is consistent with meson exchange current models such as that of Martini et al. (which predicts small axial enhancement).
