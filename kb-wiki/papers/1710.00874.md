# Exciting Nucleons in Compton Scattering and Hydrogen-Like Atoms

> This PhD thesis is devoted to the low-energy structure of the nucleon (proton and neutron) as seen through electromagnetic probes, e.g., electron and Compton scattering. The research presented here is based primarily on dispersion theory and chiral effective-field theory. The main motivation is the recent proton radius puzzle, which is the discrepancy between the classic proton charge radius determinations (based on electron-proton scattering and normal hydrogen spectroscopy) and the highly precise extraction based on first muonic-hydrogen experiments by the CREMA Collaboration. The precision of muonic-hydrogen experiments is presently limited by the knowledge of proton structure effects beyond the charge radius. A major part of this thesis is devoted to calculating these effects using everything we know about the nucleon electromagnetic structure from both theory and experiment. The thesis consists of eight chapters. The first and last are, respectively, the introduction and conclusion. The remainder of this thesis can roughly be divided into the following three topics: finite-size effects in hydrogen-like atoms, real and virtual Compton scattering, and two-photon-exchange effects.

## Key claim

This PhD thesis calculates proton structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting (HFS) and Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen using baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) at next-to-leading order, finding that the proton-polarizability contribution to the HFS is negative and disagrees with dispersive calculations by about 3.6 sigma, which the author attributes to limited empirical data on spin-dependent structure functions, especially for g2 at low momentum transfer.

## Method

The author computes two-photon-exchange (TPE) effects on the hyperfine splitting of muonic hydrogen using BChPT with chiral pion-nucleon loops and Delta(1232)-exchange contributions, combined with a large-Nc description of the Jones-Scadron form factors. The polarizability contribution is isolated by using only non-Born diagrams as input, and the results are compared to dispersive calculations based on empirical parametrizations of spin-dependent structure functions (Simula parametrization and MAID model) and elastic form factors (Bradford, Kelly, and Arrington parametrizations). The low-Q region is treated separately using empirical polarizabilities and a polarizability expansion up to second moments of the structure functions.

## Key numbers

- Combined BChPT result for the order-alpha^5 proton-polarizability contribution to the 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: E_HFS^pol(2S, muH) = -1.4 (+1.6 / -1.2) micro-eV
- Chiral loops contribution to the 2S HFS: -0.23 (+1.08 / -0.23) micro-eV
- Delta-exchange contribution to the 2S HFS: -1.15 +/- 1.15 micro-eV
- Total TPE effect on the 1S HFS in muonic hydrogen: E_HFS^TPE(1S, muH) = -1.233 (+0.030 / -0.029) meV
- Total TPE effect on the 2S HFS in muonic hydrogen: E_HFS^TPE(2S, muH) = -0.1541 (+0.004 / -0.004) meV
- Fermi energy for 1S in muonic hydrogen: E_F(1S, muH) = 182.4468 meV
- Fermi energy for 2S in muonic hydrogen: E_F(2S, muH) = 22.8058 meV
- Elastic Zemach contribution: Delta_Z(muH) = -7628 +/- 149 ppm
- Elastic recoil contribution: Delta_recoil(muH) = 929 +/- 10 ppm
- Dispersive result using Simula parametrization: E_HFS^emp.pol.(2S, muH) = 4.51 micro-eV
- Dispersive result using MAID model: E_HFS^emp.pol.(2S, muH) = 2.35 micro-eV
- HBChPT prediction from Peset et al. (2016): E_HFS^TPE(1S, muH) = -1.161 +/- 0.020 meV
- HBChPT prediction from Peset et al. (2016): E_HFS^TPE(2S, muH) = -0.1451 +/- 0.025 meV

## Context

This work extends the application of BChPT to two-photon-exchange effects in muonic hydrogen, motivated by the proton radius puzzle from the CREMA Collaboration experiments. The thesis provides the first model-independent BChPT calculation of the proton-polarizability contribution to the hyperfine splitting, complementing earlier HBChPT work by Pineda (2003) and Peset (2016). The results disagree with dispersive calculations by Carlson et al. (2008, 2011) and Faustov et al. (2006) at the 3.6 sigma level, which the author attributes to limited empirical data on the g2 spin structure function at low momentum transfer. The calculation also provides the first model-independent neutron-polarizability contributions to hyperfine splittings in muonic deuterium and muonic helium-3 ions, and extracts a new value of the proton Zemach radius. New data from Jefferson Laboratory are expected to improve the empirical situation in the important low-Q region.
