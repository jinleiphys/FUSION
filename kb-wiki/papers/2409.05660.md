# Bound state basics

> Perturbative expansions for atoms in QED are developed around interacting states, typically defined by the Schrödinger equation. Calculations are nevertheless done using the standard Feynman diagram expansion around free states. The classical $-α/r$ potential is then obtained through an infinite sum of ladder diagrams. The complexity of this approach may have contributed to bound states being omitted from QFT textbooks, restricting the field to select experts. The confinement scale 1 fm of QCD must be introduced without changing the Lagrangian. This can be done via a boundary condition on the gauge field, which affects the bound state potential. The absence of confinement in Feynman diagrams may be due to the free field boundary condition. Poincaré invariance is realized dynamically for bound states, i.e., the interactions are frame dependent. Gauge theories have instantaneous interactions, due to gauge fixing at all points of space at the same time. In bound state perturbation theory each order must have exact Poincaré invariance. This is non-trivial even for atoms at lowest order. I summarize a perturbative approach to equal time bound states in QED and QCD, using a Fock expansion in temporal ($A^0=0$) gauge. The longitudinal electric field $E_L$ is instantaneous and need not vanish at spatial infinity for the constituents of color singlet states in QCD. Poincaré covariance determines the boundary condition for $E_L$ up to a universal scale, characterised by the gluon field energy density of the vacuum. A non-vanishing density contributes a linear term to the $q\bar{q}$ potential, while $qqq,\ q\bar{q}g$ and $gg$ color singlet states get analogous confining potentials.

## Key claim

Bound states in QED and QCD can be treated perturbatively using a Fock expansion in temporal gauge, where the confinement scale of QCD (approximately 1 fm) is introduced not through the Lagrangian but via a boundary condition on the longitudinal electric field, which yields a linear confining potential for color singlet states.

## Method

The author develops a perturbative expansion for equal-time bound states in QED and QCD using temporal gauge (A^0 = 0), where canonical quantization is straightforward. Physical states are required to be invariant under time-independent gauge transformations, which associates an instantaneous longitudinal electric field with each Fock component of the bound state. The bound state condition imposes a bound state equation on the wave function, and the QCD scale is introduced through a homogeneous solution to Gauss' law, whose form is mandated by translation and rotation invariance up to a universal normalization.

## Key numbers

- The Cornell potential parameters are given as V' approximately 0.18 GeV^2 and alpha_s approximately 0.39, determined by fits to charmonia and confirmed by lattice QCD.
- Gribov's critical coupling for QCD is alpha_s^crit = pi * C_F^{-1} * (1 - sqrt(2/3)) approximately 0.43.
- For meson Fock states, the confining potential takes the form lambda^2 * |x_1 - x_2|, where the hadronic scale V' = lambda^2 is of order alpha_s^0.
- For baryon Fock states, the confining potential is (lambda^2 / sqrt(2)) * sqrt((x_1 - x_2)^2 + (x_2 - x_3)^2 + (x_3 - x_1)^2).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hoyer (2021) on bound state perturbation theory in temporal gauge, and builds on earlier work by Jarvinen (2004) on Poincaré invariance of atomic wave functions. The method contrasts with standard Feynman diagram expansions around free states, which require infinite sums of ladder diagrams to recover the classical -alpha/r potential and lack bound state poles. The approach enables a perturbative treatment of strongly bound hadrons where the confining force does not create additional quarks and gluons, and it relates to the poorly understood features of duality in hadron physics as discussed by Melnitchouk (2005).
