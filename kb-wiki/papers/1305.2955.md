# Multiple solutions for the fermion mass function in QED3

> Theories that support dynamical generation of a fermion mass gap are of widespread interest. The phenomenon is often studied via the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the fermion self energy; i.e., the gap equation. When the rainbow truncation of that equation supports dynamical mass generation, it typically also possesses a countable infinity of simultaneous solutions for the dressed-fermion mass function, solutions which may be ordered by the number of zeros they exhibit. These features can be understood via the theory of nonlinear Hammerstein integral equations. Using QED3 as an example, we demonstrate the existence of a large class of gap equation truncations that possess solutions with damped oscillations. We suggest that there is a larger class, quite probably including the exact theory, which does not. The structure of the dressed-fermion--gauge-boson vertex is an important factor in deciding the issue.

## Key claim

Using QED3 as an example, the authors demonstrate that a large class of gap equation truncations possess solutions with damped oscillations (multiple zeros), but suggest that a larger class, quite probably including the exact theory, does not; the structure of the dressed-fermion–gauge-boson vertex is the deciding factor.

## Method

The authors analyze the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the dressed fermion propagator in quenched QED3, first in rainbow truncation and then with increasingly sophisticated vertex Ansätze. They recast the chiral-limit gap equation as a nonlinear Hammerstein integral equation of the first kind, whose known mathematical properties guarantee a countable infinity of oscillatory solutions when certain conditions on the kernel and nonlinearity are satisfied. They then test whether these conditions hold for different vertex Ansätze (central Ball-Chiu, symmetric central, and more complete forms) and use homotopy continuation methods to search for nodal solutions numerically.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling for the existence of multiple solutions in the graphene gap equation is α_c = 1/2 (Gamayun 2009).
* For the rainbow-truncation QED3 gap equation with a small current-quark mass, the critical value above which only the zero-free solution survives is m_0^cr = 0.0046 (in units where e^2 = 1).
* In the linearized rainbow-ladder equation, a zero first appears at κ ≃ a/14, a second zero at κ ≃ a/36, and the pattern continues ad infinitum (where a = 16α̃/π).
* The first three nontrivial solutions of the rainbow-truncation gap equation have magnitudes at p=0 of order 10^{-1}, 10^{-4}, and 10^{-7} (in units of the ultraviolet cutoff Λ) for α = π/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Fukuda (1976), Cheng (1994), and Chang (2006) that the rainbow-truncation gap equation possesses multiple solutions, and by Fischer (2008) and Wang (2012) who found such solutions with more general truncations. The authors use the mathematical theory of Hammerstein integral equations (Pimbley, Parter, Crandall) to explain the origin of these multiple solutions and to identify conditions under which they disappear. The results suggest that realistic vertex Ansätze (e.g., the full Ball-Chiu vertex) likely eliminate all nodal solutions, which has implications for the interpretation of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in both QED3 and QCD.
