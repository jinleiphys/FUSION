# Presupernova neutrino signals as potential probes of neutrino mass hierarchy

> We assess the potential of using presupernova neutrino signals at the Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO) to probe the yet-unknown neutrino mass hierarchy. Using models for stars of 12, 15, 20, and 25 solar masses, we find that if the electron antineutrino signals from such a star can be predicted precisely and the star is within ~440-880 pc, the number of events of electron antineutrino captures on protons detected within one day of its explosion allows to determine the hierarchy at the > ~95% confidence level. For determination at this level using such signals from Betelgeuse, which is at a distance of ~222 pc, the uncertainty in the predicted number of signals needs to be < ~14-30%. In view of more realistic uncertainties, we discuss and advocate a model-independent determination using both electron neutrino and antineutrino signals from Betelgeuse. This method is feasible if the cosmogenic background for neutrino-electron scattering events can be reduced by a factor of ~2.5-10 from the current estimate. Such reduction might be achieved by using coincidence of the background events, the exploration of which for JUNO is highly desirable.

# Presupernova neutrino signals as potential probes of neutrino mass hierarchy

## Key claim
If the electron antineutrino signals from a presupernova star can be predicted precisely and the star is within approximately 440–880 pc, the number of inverse beta decay events detected within one day of its explosion at JUNO can determine the neutrino mass hierarchy at the greater than 95% confidence level; however, for Betelgeuse at approximately 222 pc, the uncertainty in the predicted number of signals must be less than 14–30% for such a determination, which is difficult to achieve with current stellar models.

## Method
The authors used stellar evolution models for stars of 12, 15, 20, and 25 solar masses to calculate the expected fluxes of presupernova electron antineutrinos from electron-positron pair annihilation, including flavor transformation via the MSW effect. They computed the expected number of inverse beta decay events (electron antineutrino capture on protons) at JUNO over the last day before core collapse, considering backgrounds from reactor and geo-neutrinos, and performed a statistical analysis to determine the limiting distance and required prediction precision for distinguishing normal from inverted hierarchy at the 95% confidence level. For a model-independent approach, they also analyzed the ratio of neutrino-electron scattering events to inverse beta decay events, which differs by a factor of approximately 4 between the two hierarchies and is insensitive to stellar models.

## Key numbers
- For a star of 12 solar masses at 1 kpc over the last day before core collapse: 6.1 IBD events for normal hierarchy, 1.9 for inverted hierarchy
- For a star of 15 solar masses at 1 kpc: 12.0 IBD events for normal hierarchy, 3.6 for inverted hierarchy
- For a star of 20 solar masses at 1 kpc: 20.5 IBD events for normal hierarchy, 5.9 for inverted hierarchy
- For a star of 25 solar masses at 1 kpc: 24.5 IBD events for normal hierarchy, 7.0 for inverted hierarchy
- Background from reactor antineutrinos: 15.7 events per day; from geo-neutrinos: 1.1 events per day
- Limiting distances for hierarchy determination at 95% CL with 10% model uncertainty: 0.44 kpc (12 solar masses), 0.6 kpc (15 solar masses), 0.8 kpc (20 solar masses), 0.88 kpc (25 solar masses)
- Betelgeuse distance: 222 pc (with uncertainty +48/-34 pc)
- Required uncertainty in predicted IBD signals for Betelgeuse: less than 14–30%
- Ratio of IBD signals between normal and inverted hierarchy: approximately 3.4
- Ratio of ES to IBD signals for normal hierarchy: approximately 0.91; for inverted hierarchy: approximately 3.8
- Predicted ES signals from Betelgeuse over last day: 117.2–479.8 for normal hierarchy, 143.5–592.1 for inverted hierarchy (depending on stellar mass)
- Current estimated ES background from cosmogenic carbon-11: approximately 2 × 10^4 events per day
- Required reduction of ES background for model-independent determination: factor of approximately 2.5–10

## Context
This work extends previous studies of presupernova neutrino detection (Odrzywolek 2004, Odrzywolek 2010, Kato 2015, KamLAND 2016, Yoshida 2016, Kato 2017, Patton 2017a, Patton 2017b) that showed presupernova antineutrinos from stars within a few kiloparsecs could provide advance warning of core collapse. The authors provide the first quantitative assessment of using these signals to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy, building on the known MSW effect dependence on hierarchy (Dighe and Smirnov 2000). The model-independent method using both IBD and ES events at JUNO is newly proposed here; the authors note that DUNE could provide complementary measurements through charged-current and neutral-current channels, though with lower event rates. The results motivate background reduction strategies at JUNO, particularly using three-fold coincidence of muon, neutron, and carbon-11 decay products as suggested by previous JUNO collaboration studies.
