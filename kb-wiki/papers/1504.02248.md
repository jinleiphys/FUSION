# The baryon mass calculation in the chiral soliton model at finite temperature and density

> In the mean-field approximation, we have studied the soliton which is embedded in a thermal medium within the chiral soliton model. The energy of the soliton or the baryon mass in the thermal medium has been carefully evaluated, in which we emphasize that the thermal effective potential in the soliton energy should be properly treated in order to derive a finite and well-defined baryon mass out of the thermal background. The result of the baryon mass at finite temperatures and densities in chiral soliton model are clearly presented.

## Key claim

The baryon mass in the chiral soliton model decreases with increasing temperature and chemical potential, but only when the thermal effective potential is properly renormalized by subtracting the thermal background energy via a temperature- and density-dependent constant B(T,μ) that forces the minimum of the thermodynamic potential to zero at all conditions.

## Method

The authors embed a single "hedgehog" soliton, representing a baryon via three valence quarks in the lowest s-wave level, into a homogeneous hot and dense quark medium. They solve the coupled nonlinear radial differential equations for the quark fields u(r), v(r) and the meson fields σ(r), π(r) in the mean-field approximation, using finite-temperature field theory to construct the thermodynamic potential Ω(σ,π;T,μ). A key step is the introduction of a subtraction term B(T,μ) that shifts the minimum of Ω to zero at each temperature and chemical potential, ensuring the soliton energy integral converges to a finite baryon mass.

## Key numbers

* At zero temperature and density, the quark eigenvalue ε is 0.216 fm⁻¹ (approximately 30.5 MeV).
* At μ = 0 MeV, the baryon mass is calculated at T = 0, 170, and 185 MeV; the corresponding ε values are 0.216, 0.289, and 0.451 fm⁻¹, and the subtraction constants B(T,μ) are 0, 0.439, and 0.746 fm⁻⁴.
* At μ = 300 MeV, the baryon mass is calculated at T = 0, 100, and 110 MeV; the corresponding ε values are 0.216, 0.257, and 0.288 fm⁻¹, and the subtraction constants B(T,μ) are 0, 0.074, and 0.202 fm⁻⁴.
* The pion decay constant f_π is 93 MeV, the pion mass m_π is 138 MeV, the constituent quark mass in vacuum M_q is 500 MeV, and the sigma mass m_σ is 1200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior chiral soliton model calculations of nucleon properties at finite temperature and density, specifically addressing a flaw in earlier treatments. Previous studies either completely neglected the thermal medium contribution to the soliton energy (references in the paper) or subtracted it improperly, leading to unphysical baryon mass growth at high temperatures. The authors' renormalization procedure yields a baryon mass that decreases with temperature and chemical potential, consistent with the Brown-Rho scaling prediction. This enables reliable calculation of hadron properties in hot, dense matter relevant to heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
