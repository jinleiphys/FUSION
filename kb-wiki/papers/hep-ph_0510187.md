# Light-front wavefunction dependence of the quark recombination

> We present an extension of the recombination formalism to analyze the effects from the variation of the hadron wavefunctions. The hadron spectra are sensitive to the shape of the wavefunctions. However, when we fit the wavefunction parameters to the physical observables, such as the average charge radius, the final spectra are very similar each other. We discuss our numerical results in comparison with the published PHENIX and STAR data at RHIC. In the hadron spectra, the recombination of thermal partons dominates at intermediate transverse momentum ($P_{T}$ = 2 $\sim$ 5 GeV), and the fragmentation dominates at high $P_{T}$ ($>$ 5 GeV). The yield ratios and the nuclear modification factors for various hadron species are also estimated and compared to the experimental data. We present a new prediction on $\bar{p}/p$ and $K^{-}/K^{+}$ ratios, including the jet quenching effects to the fragmentation mechanism.

## Key claim

The hadron spectra from quark recombination are sensitive to the shape of the light-front wavefunctions, but when wavefunction parameters are fixed by physical observables such as the average charge radius, the final spectra from Gaussian and power-law wavefunctions become very similar to each other.

## Method

The authors extended the recombination formalism of Fries et al. to explicitly include the intrinsic transverse momentum of constituent quarks inside hadrons, using non-factorizable light-front wavefunctions. They applied both Gaussian and power-law forms of the light-front wavefunctions for mesons and baryons, with parameters constrained by measured charge radii, and combined the recombination yields with pQCD fragmentation including jet quenching effects parameterized as Δp_T = ε(b)√(p_T)⟨L⟩(b)/R_A.

## Key numbers

- Recombination of thermal partons dominates at intermediate transverse momentum P_T = 2–5 GeV, while fragmentation dominates at high P_T > 5 GeV
- For pions, ⟨r²⟩ = 0.44 fm² and the corresponding β² = 0.825 GeV² for the Gaussian wavefunction
- For the power-law wavefunction, α² = 0.5 GeV² and n = 2 for pions; α² = 1.53 GeV² and n = 6 for protons
- The light quark masses used were 260 MeV for u and d quarks and 460 MeV for the s quark
- The hadronization temperature was assumed to be T = 175 MeV
- The chemical potentials used were 9, 6.7, and −3.9 MeV for u/d, s, and c quarks respectively
- The energy loss parameter for central collisions was ε₀ = 0.82 GeV^(1/2)
- The p/π⁰ and p̄/π⁰ ratios reach maximum values around P_T = 3 GeV, then decrease to about 0.1 for P_T > 6 GeV
- The p̄/p ratio is approximately 0.9 for P_T < 5 GeV, then decreases to about 0.1 for P_T > 7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the recombination formalism of Fries et al. (2003) by incorporating non-factorizable light-front wavefunctions, contrasting with the factorized ansatz used in that prior work. The authors compare their results with published PHENIX and STAR data at RHIC for √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, and note that other approaches include the soft-hard recombination model of Greco et al. (2003) and Hwa and Yang (2003), as well as the field-theoretic derivation of the recombination model by Majumder, Wang, and Wang. The new predictions for p̄/p and K⁻/K⁺ ratios at high P_T, including jet quenching effects, are distinguished from previous calculations by Fries et al. due to different treatment of sea quark contributions in the KKP fragmentation functions.
