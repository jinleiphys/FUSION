# Glasma flux tubes and the near side ridge phenomenon at RHIC

> We investigate the consequences of long range rapidity correlations in the Glasma. Particles produced locally in the transverse plane are correlated by approximately boost invariant flux tubes of longitudinal color electric and magnetic fields that are formed when two sheets of Colored Glass Condensate pass through one another, each acquiring a modified color charge density in the collision. We argue that such long range rapidity correlations persist during the evolution of the Quark Gluon Plasma formed later in the collision. When combined with transverse flow, these correlations reproduce many of the features of the recently observed ridge events in heavy ion collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The near-side ridge observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC arises from the combination of long-range rapidity correlations inherent in Glasma flux tubes (boost-invariant longitudinal color electric and magnetic fields of transverse size ~1/Q_s) and the subsequent radial flow of the produced Quark-Gluon Plasma, which collimates the otherwise isotropic azimuthal distribution of particle pairs.

## Method

The authors compute the two-particle correlation function for gluon pairs in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, averaging over color charge configurations of the two nuclei using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with a Gaussian distribution. They analytically evaluate the leading classical contribution to the correlated two-gluon spectrum for large transverse momenta (p_perp, q_perp >> Q_s), expressing the result in terms of the saturation scale Q_s and the transverse area S_perp. The effect of radial flow is then incorporated by boosting the isotropic angular distribution of emitted particles with a radial flow velocity V_r.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale Q_s is related to the color charge density by Q_s ≈ 0.57 g^2 μ_A, where μ_A^2 is the color charge squared per unit area in the MV model.
* The single inclusive gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity is ⟨dN/dy⟩ = (κ′/α_s(Q_s)) S_perp Q_s^2, with κ′ ≈ 1/13.5 for SU(3).
* The correlated pair density relative to mixed events is Δρ/√ρ_ref = K_N / α_s(Q_s), where K_N = κ κ′ ≈ 4/13.5 ∼ 0.3 from the analytic computation at large p_perp, q_perp >> Q_s.
* The ridge extends across Δη ∼ 2 units in pseudo-rapidity in STAR and up to Δη ∼ 6 units in PHOBOS, with a minimum p_perp cutoff of 150 MeV for associated particles.

## Context

This work extends the Glasma framework developed by Lappi and McLerran (2006) and Gelis et al. (2007) to explain the ridge events first reported by the STAR collaboration (2005, 2008) and corroborated by PHENIX and PHOBOS. The mechanism shares features with earlier proposals by Voloshin (2006) and Shuryak (2007) but differs in that the dominant correlation arises from particles produced independently along the length of flux tubes rather than from daughter particles splitting off a parent. The result enables quantitative predictions for the centrality dependence of the ridge height and width, which can be tested against experimental data from RHIC and eventually the LHC.
