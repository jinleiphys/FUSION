# Bulk viscosity, particle spectra and flow in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effects of bulk viscosity on pT spectra and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions. For this purpose we compute the dissipative correction df to the single particle distribution functions in leading-log QCD, and in several simplified models. We consider, in particular, the relaxation time approximation and a kinetic model for the hadron resonance gas. We implement these distribution functions in a hydrodynamic simulation of Au + Au collisions at RHIC. We find significant corrections due to bulk viscosity in hadron pT spectra and the differential elliptic flow parameter v2(pT). These corrections are dominated by viscous corrections to the distribution function. We find that the relation between df and the bulk viscosity is different in the quark gluon plasma and hadronic phases. Reliable bounds on the bulk viscosity require accurate calculations of df in a hadronic resonance gas. Based on v2 spectra at RHIC we conservatively estimate zeta/s <= 0.05 near freeze-out. We also find that effects of the bulk viscosity on the pT integrated v2 are small.

## Key claim

The authors find that bulk viscosity corrections to particle spectra and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions are dominated by viscous corrections to the distribution function (δf) rather than by modifications to the hydrodynamic flow itself, and that the relationship between δf and bulk viscosity differs between the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phases, making reliable bounds on ζ/s require accurate calculations of δf in the hadronic resonance gas.

## Method

The authors compute the dissipative correction δf to single-particle distribution functions using the Boltzmann equation in the Chapman-Enskog expansion, considering three approaches: the relaxation time approximation, a scalar field theory (λφ⁴) with 2↔2 and 2↔4 processes, and leading-log QCD treated via a Fokker-Planck equation. They implement these δf forms in a viscous hydrodynamic simulation of Au+Au collisions at RHIC using second-order Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics, with freeze-out on a constant energy density surface via the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity scales as ζ ∼ η(c_s² − 1/3)² in the relaxation time approximation, with the explicit relation ζ = 15η(1/3 − c_s²)² in the high-temperature limit
- For a speed of sound c_s² ≈ 0.2 near the phase transition, the authors estimate ζ ≈ 0.25η using either the Weinberg-type relation or the AdS/CFT-type relation
- Based on v₂ spectra at RHIC, the authors conservatively estimate ζ/s ≲ 0.05 near freeze-out
- In the high-temperature limit of scalar field theory, the bulk viscosity scales as ζ ∼ λ³T³ ln²(γλ) where λ is the coupling constant
- The drag coefficient in leading-log QCD for pure glue is μ_A = g²C_A m_D²/(8π) log(T/m_D) with m_D² = (1/3)(C_A + N_f/2)g²T²

## Context

This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic studies that focused primarily on shear viscosity (e.g., Song (2010), Romatschke (2007)) by systematically including bulk viscosity effects. It builds on prior calculations of bulk viscosity in weakly coupled QCD by Arnold (2006) and in lattice QCD by Meyer (2007), as well as on the relaxation time approximation results of Weinberg (1971) for photon gases. The study enables more precise extraction of both shear and bulk viscosities from RHIC data by identifying how bulk viscosity corrections to the distribution function affect pT spectra and v₂(pT), and it motivates future calculations of δf in hadronic resonance gases to tighten bounds on ζ/s.
