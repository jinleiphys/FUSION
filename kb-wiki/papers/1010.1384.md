# Testing collinear factorization and nuclear parton distributions with pA collisions at the LHC

> Global perturbative QCD analyses, based on large data sets from electron-proton and hadron collider experiments, provide tight constraints on the parton distribution function (PDF) in the proton. The extension of these analyses to nuclear parton distributions (nPDF) has attracted much interest in recent years. nPDFs are needed as benchmarks for the characterization of hot QCD matter in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and attract further interest since they may show novel signatures of non- linear density-dependent QCD evolution. However, it is not known from first principles whether the factorization of long-range phenomena into process-independent parton distribution, which underlies global PDF extractions for the proton, extends to nuclear effects. As a consequence, assessing the reliability of nPDFs for benchmark calculations goes beyond testing the numerical accuracy of their extraction and requires phenomenological tests of the factorization assumption. Here we argue that a proton-nucleus collision program at the LHC would provide a set of measurements allowing for unprecedented tests of the factorization assumption underlying global nPDF fits.

# Testing collinear factorization and nuclear parton distributions with pA collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The authors argue that a proton-nucleus collision program at the LHC would provide measurements allowing unprecedented tests of whether collinear factorization, the assumption that nuclear effects can be separated into process-independent nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), is valid, rather than merely testing the numerical accuracy of existing nPDF extractions. The paper does not claim that factorization holds, but that LHC pPb data can distinguish between factorized nPDF-based explanations and competing mechanisms such as transverse momentum broadening or saturation.

## Method
The authors performed leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD calculations of single inclusive neutral pion production in proton-nucleus collisions, using CTEQ6L proton PDFs with EPS09LO nuclear modifications and KKP fragmentation functions. They computed the nuclear modification factor R_pA^h for deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) and for proton-lead collisions at the LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV), scanning over transverse momentum and rapidity to map the predicted behavior onto the shadowing, anti-shadowing, and EMC regimes of nPDFs.

## Key numbers
- RHIC deuteron-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: the nuclear modification factor R_dAu^pi0 shows enhancement around p_T ~ 4 GeV at mid-rapidity, consistent with the anti-shadowing region.
- LHC proton-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV: the factorized calculation predicts suppression for p_T < 10-20 GeV at mid-rapidity (shadowing regime) and enhancement for p_T > 10-20 GeV (anti-shadowing regime), a shift of the maximum to p_T > 50 GeV compared to RHIC.
- The LHC center-of-mass energy is more than 40 times higher than RHIC, meaning final-state hadrons at the same p_T test approximately 40 times smaller momentum fractions x_i.
- Rapidity scans from y = -4 to y = +4 allow scanning the shadowing (S), anti-shadowing (AS), and EMC regimes; vertical lines in Fig. 3 indicate p_T ranges accessible with more than N_events = 1000 (or 10) per GeV-bin within one month at nominal luminosity.

## Context
This work extends global nPDF analyses such as EPS09 (Eskola 2009) and HKN07 (Hirai 2007), which have been constrained primarily by fixed-target nuclear DIS and Drell-Yan data with limited Q^2 and x coverage. The authors note that RHIC data on inclusive high-p_T hadron production (PHENIX, Adler 2006) have been included in recent nPDF fits but remain inconclusive about the dynamical origin of nuclear effects, as alternative explanations exist via initial-state parton multiple scattering (k_T broadening, Zhang 2001) and non-linear low-x QCD evolution (Albacete 2010). The paper argues that LHC pPb measurements can resolve whether nuclear modifications arise from longitudinal momentum distributions (factorizable nPDFs) or from transverse momentum broadening or saturation, and can discriminate between different nPDF parametrizations, particularly the poorly constrained gluon distribution, where EPS09 and HKN07 show qualitatively different behavior at initial scale Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.
