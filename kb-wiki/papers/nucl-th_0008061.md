# E1 transitions between spin-dipole and Gamow-Teller giant resonances

> The branching ratios for E1 transitions between the spin-dipole (SD) and Gamow-Teller (GT) giant resonances in $^{90}$Nb and $^{208}$Pb are evaluated. Assuming the main GT-state has the wave function close to that for the "ideal" GT-state, we reduced the problem to calculate the SD and GT strength functions. These strength functions are evaluated within an extended continuum-RPA approach.

## Key claim

The branching ratios for E1 transitions between the spin-dipole giant resonance and the Gamow-Teller giant resonance in \(^{90}\)Nb and \(^{208}\)Pb are evaluated under the assumption that the main GT state has a wave function close to that of the "ideal" GT state, with the correction factor \(x_g\) accounting for the difference; the accuracy of this assumption is acknowledged as "not-too-high" because the \(x_g\) values are not-too-close to unity.

## Method

The authors used an extended continuum-RPA approach with a phenomenological mean field and Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction, including partial self-consistency conditions and a phenomenological description of doorway-state coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations. They calculated the SD and GT strength functions and their Breit-Wigner parameters (strength, energy, escape width), then derived the radiative width for E1 transitions between SD and GT doorway states using the squared matrix elements of the electric dipole operator. The branching ratios were computed both schematically (assuming all J-components of the SDR have the same energy and no spreading or escape widths) and in a refined description that accounts for the distribution of spin-dipole strength and the spreading widths of both resonances.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{90}\)Nb: schematic branching ratio \(b_{g}^{schem} = 3.3 \times 10^{-4}\); refined branching ratio \(b_{g} = 4.7 \times 10^{-4}\)
- For \(^{208}\)Pb: schematic branching ratio \(b_{g}^{schem} = 0.81 \times 10^{-4}\); refined branching ratio \(b_{g} = 2.4 \times 10^{-4}\)
- Mean-squared radius \(\left<r^2\right>^{(-)} = 22.6\) fm\(^2\) for \(^{90}\)Zr and 36.4 fm\(^2\) for \(^{208}\)Pb
- Relative strength of the GT main peak \(x_g = 0.83\) for \(^{90}\)Zr and 0.69 for \(^{208}\)Pb
- Ratio \(B\) of SDR\(^{(+)}\) to SDR\(^{(-)}\) excitation strength: 0.34 for \(^{90}\)Zr and 0.06 for \(^{208}\)Pb
- Experimental GTR energy: 8.7 MeV for \(^{90}\)Nb and 15.5 MeV for \(^{208}\)Bi
- Experimental SDR\(^{(-)}\) energy: 17.9 MeV for \(^{90}\)Nb and 21.1 MeV for \(^{208}\)Bi
- Experimental total width of GTR: 4.4 MeV for \(^{90}\)Nb and 3.72 MeV for \(^{208}\)Bi
- Mean spin-dipole doorway-state spreading width: 5.0 MeV for \(^{90}\)Nb and 4.7 MeV for \(^{208}\)Bi

## Context

This work extends the continuum-RPA approach previously applied to direct proton decay of giant resonances by Mouk (1999) to the evaluation of gamma-decay branching ratios between the spin-dipole and Gamow-Teller giant resonances. It improves upon an earlier TDA calculation by Suzuki (1999) for \(^{90}\)Nb and \(^{48}\)Sc, which presented results in a form not directly comparable to experimental branching ratios. The predictions are intended for comparison with upcoming experimental data from (\(^3\)He,t\(\gamma\)) coincidence experiments, enabling quantitative tests of the microscopic particle-hole structure of these giant resonances.
