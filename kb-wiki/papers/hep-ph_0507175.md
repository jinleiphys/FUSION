# Quarkonium Production at High-Energy Hadron Colliders

> This thesis can be divided into several parts. The first one consists in a detailed historical review of quarkonium physics, centered on the different production models successively introduced. Then comes a review of the currently available data on high-energy hadronic production of J/psi, psi' and Upsilon's. In a third part, we concentrate on the extension of the Colour-Singlet Model beyond the static approximation. In this extension, we emphasise that a simple generalisation of the static case is not sufficient and new 4-point vertices are to be introduced to restore gauge invariance. Since these new vertices are not fully constrained, we propose a method to study this freedom. Finally, we apply our approach to the production at the Tevatron and RHIC, where we show that a combination of our contributions with fragmentation within the Colour-Octet Mechanism is able to reproduce the measurements both for cross sections and for polarisation.

## Key claim

The thesis argues that a simple extension of the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) beyond the static approximation is insufficient because new 4-point vertices must be introduced to restore gauge invariance; a combination of these new contributions with fragmentation within the Colour-Octet Mechanism (COM) can reproduce the Tevatron and RHIC measurements for both cross sections and polarisation.

## Method

The work first presents a detailed historical review of quarkonium production models and a review of existing data on J/psi, psi' and Upsilon hadroproduction. It then extends the Colour-Singlet Model beyond the static approximation by introducing new 4-point vertices required by gauge invariance, and proposes a method to study the freedom in these unconstrained vertices. Finally, the approach is applied to production at the Tevatron (p anti-p at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV) and RHIC (p p at sqrt(s)=200 GeV), combining the new contributions with fragmentation within the Colour-Octet Mechanism.

## Key numbers

* J/psi and psi(2S) production cross sections measured by CDF at the Tevatron (p anti-p, sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV) are used as comparison data (CDF, 1997).
* Upsilon production cross sections measured by CDF at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV are used as comparison data (CDF, 1995).
* J/psi polarisation data from CDF at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV are used as comparison data (CDF, 2000).
* RHIC p p collision data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV from PHENIX are used as comparison data (PHENIX, 2004).

## Context

This thesis extends the Colour-Singlet Model of Chang (1980) and Baier and Ruckl (1981, 1983) by going beyond the static approximation, and it builds on the Colour-Octet Mechanism formulated within NRQCD by Bodwin, Braaten and Lepage (1995). The work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between leading-order CSM predictions and Tevatron data on J/psi and psi' yields, which had been partially resolved by the COM fragmentation calculations of Braaten and Yuan (1993) and Braaten and Fleming (1995). The results enable future comparisons with LHC data and further refinements of the gauge-invariant phenomenological approach (GIPA).
