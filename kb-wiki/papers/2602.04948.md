# Towards $2+1$D quantum electrodynamics on a cold-atom quantum simulator

> Cold atoms have become a powerful platform for quantum-simulating lattice gauge theories in higher spatial dimensions. However, such realizations have been restricted to the lowest possible truncations of the gauge field, which limit the connections one can make to lattice quantum electrodynamics. Here, we propose a feasible cold-atom quantum simulator of a $(2+1)$-dimensional U$(1)$ lattice gauge theory in a spin $S=1$ truncation, featuring dynamical matter and gauge fields. We derive a mapping of this theory onto a bosonic computational basis, stabilized by an emergent gauge-protection mechanism through quantum Zeno dynamics. The implementation is based on a single-species Bose--Hubbard model realized in a tilted optical superlattice. This approach requires only moderate experimental resources already available in current ultracold-atom platforms. Using infinite matrix product state simulations, we benchmark real-time dynamics under global quenches. The results demonstrate faithful evolution of the target gauge theory and robust preservation of the gauge constraints. Our work significantly advances the experimental prospects for simulating higher-dimensional lattice gauge theories using larger gauge-field truncations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a feasible cold-atom quantum simulator of a (2+1)-dimensional U(1) lattice gauge theory with spin S=1 truncation, but the target gauge theory emerges only as an effective low-energy model at second-order perturbation theory when cold-atom lattice parameters are tuned to appropriate values, rather than being directly realized.

## Method

The authors map a spin-1 U(1) quantum link model (QLM) of scalar QED in 2+1 dimensions onto a bosonic computational basis described by an extended single-species Bose-Hubbard model (BHM) realized in a tilted optical superlattice. The implementation uses additional nearest-neighbor and next-nearest-neighbor interactions beyond the conventional BHM, such as those achievable through dipole-dipole interactions between magnetic atoms. The target gauge theory emerges as an effective low-energy description at second-order perturbation theory, stabilized by an emergent gauge-protection mechanism through quantum Zeno dynamics. The authors benchmark real-time dynamics under global quenches using infinite matrix product state (iMPS) simulations.

## Key numbers

* The gauge-field truncation corresponds to spin S=1, which is larger than the S=1/2 truncation used in previous experimental realizations of lattice gauge theories in higher spatial dimensions.
* The quantum link model energy for a configuration with two matter particles and one electric flux quantum is E_QLM = 2μ + g²/2, where μ is the matter chemical potential and g is the gauge coupling.
* The quantum link model energy for a configuration with two electric flux quanta is E_QLM = g².
* The quantum link model energy for the vacuum configuration is E_QLM = 0.

## Context

This work extends the mapping of a spin-1/2 U(1) quantum link model to a Bose-Hubbard model in 2+1D, previously proposed by Osborne et al. (2025), to the larger spin S=1 truncation. It also builds upon the extension of the Bose-Hubbard model to capture larger-spin truncations of a QLM in 1+1D by Osborne et al. (2023). Previous experimental realizations of lattice gauge theories in higher spatial dimensions have been restricted to the lowest two-level truncation of the gauge field (S=1/2), which severely limits extrapolation to the quantum field theory limit. This work enables experimental access to larger gauge-field truncations in 2+1D, bringing quantum simulations closer to the continuum limit of lattice quantum electrodynamics.
