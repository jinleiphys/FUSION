# Heavy Quark Potential at Finite Temperature Using the Holographic Correspondence

> We revisit the calculation of a heavy quark potential in N =4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature using the AdS/CFT correspondence. As is widely known, the potential calculated in the pioneering works of Rey et al. and Brandhuber et al. is zero for separation distances r between the quark and the anti-quark above a certain critical separation, at which the potential has a kink. We point out that by analytically continuing the string configurations into the complex plane, and using a slightly different renormalization subtraction, one obtains a smooth non-zero (negative definite) potential without a kink. The obtained potential also has a non-zero imaginary (absorptive) part for separations r > r_c = 0.870/πT . At large separations r the real part of the potential does not exhibit the exponential Debye falloff expected from perturbation theory and instead falls off as a power law, proportional to 1/r^4 for r > r_0 = 2.702 / πT.

## Key claim
By analytically continuing the string configurations into the complex plane and using a different renormalization subtraction than Rey et al. (1998) and Brandhuber et al. (1998), the heavy quark potential in N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature becomes smooth and non-zero for all separations, instead of vanishing above a critical distance with a kink.

## Method
The authors calculate the heavy quark potential from a temporal Wilson loop in finite-temperature N=4 SYM theory using the AdS/CFT correspondence. They extremize the Nambu-Goto action for an open string attached to a quark and anti-quark in the AdS5 black hole metric, then analytically continue the string coordinates into the complex plane for separations where the classical solution becomes complex-valued. A modified ultraviolet subtraction is used, defined by subtracting the renormalized action at infinite separation, which ensures the real part of the potential goes to zero at large distances.

## Key numbers
- The critical separation for the onset of an imaginary part is r_c = 0.870 / pi T
- The screening length scale r_0 = 2.702 / pi T, defined by an interpolating formula for the real part of the potential
- At large separations, the real part of the potential falls off as Re[V(r)] ~ - (pi^3 c_0^3 / 4) sqrt(lambda) z_h^3 / r^4, where z_h = 1/pi T and c_0 = Gamma^2(1/4)/(2 pi)^(3/2)
- At small separations, the potential recovers the zero-temperature Coulomb form V(r) = - sqrt(lambda) / (2 pi c_0^2 r)
- The imaginary part at large r scales as Im[V(r)] = - sqrt(lambda)/(2 pi z_h) [r/z_h - 1/c_0 + ...]

## Context
This work extends the pioneering calculations of the heavy quark potential in N=4 SYM at finite temperature by Rey et al. (1998) and Brandhuber et al. (1998), who found the potential vanishes for separations above r* = 0.754/pi T. The authors use complex trajectory methods similar to those applied in quasi-classical quantum mechanics and previously used by the same authors (Albacete, Kovchegov, Taliotis, 2008) for dipole scattering on a shock wave. The resulting potential develops an imaginary part, consistent with perturbative results by Laine et al. (2006) and Brambilla et al. (2008), but exhibits a power-law falloff at large distances rather than the exponential Debye screening expected from perturbation theory. This work enables further study of quarkonium melting in strongly coupled plasmas relevant to heavy ion collisions.
