# Angular asymmetries in the reactions pp \to dπ^+ηand pn \to dπ^0ηand a_0-f_0 mixing

> The reactions pp\to dπ^+ηand pn\to dπ^0ηare of special interest for investigating the a_0(980) (J^P=0^+) resonance in the process NN \to da_0 \to dπη. We study some aspects of those reactions within a general formalism and also in a concrete phenomenological model. In particular, it is shown that the presence of nonresonant (i.e. without excitation of the a_0 resonance) contributions to these reactions yields nonvanishing values for specific polarization observables, i.e. to effects like those generated by a_0-f_0 mixing. An experimental determination of these observables for the reaction pp\to dπ^+ηwould provide concrete information on the magnitude of those nonresonant contributions to πηproduction. We discuss also the possibility of extracting information about a_0-f_0 mixing from the reaction pn \to dπ^0ηwith polarized proton beam.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nonresonant (non-\(a_0\)) contributions to the reactions \(pp \to d\pi^+\eta\) and \(pn \to d\pi^0\eta\) produce a nonzero angular asymmetry that is linear in the relative momentum \(k\), mimicking the signal expected from isospin-violating \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing; therefore, a measurement of the asymmetry in the \(pp\) reaction is needed to disentangle the two effects.

## Method

The authors develop a general amplitude for \(NN \to d\pi\eta\) near threshold, allowing the \(\pi\eta\) system to be in an \(S\)-wave (via the \(a_0\) resonance) or a \(P\)-wave (nonresonant background). They derive expressions for the differential cross section and the angular-asymmetry parameter \(A(\theta,\varphi)\) in terms of scalar amplitudes. For a concrete estimate, they adopt a one-pion-exchange model with intermediate \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N^*(1535)\) resonances, evaluate the loop integral using the Bonn deuteron wave function and a monopole pion form factor, and compute the asymmetry and angular distributions at a beam energy of \(T_{lab}=2.65\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The angular asymmetry parameter \(A(90^\circ, \varphi)\) for the reaction \(pp \to d\pi^+\eta\) is calculated to be \(\le 1\%\) at \(m=980\) MeV (the \(a_0\)-meson region).
* The calculations are performed at a proton beam energy \(T_{lab}=2.65\) GeV.
* The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) for the pion form factor is varied between \(\Lambda = 1\) and \(1.3\) GeV.
* The cross section \(\sigma_{\pi^- p \to \eta N} = (21.2 \pm 1.8)\, p^{\eta}_{cm}\) [\(\mu\)b] (with \(p^{\eta}_{cm}\) in MeV/\(c\)) is taken from experiment (Binnie) to determine the \(\pi^0 N \to \eta N\) amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Kudryavtsev et al. (2002) on the reaction \(\vec{p}n \to d a_0^0\) to the \(NN \to d\pi\eta\) channels, where the \(\pi\eta\) system can be produced in higher partial waves. It contrasts with the chiral unitary approach of Oset et al., which predicts a purely isotropic \(S\)-wave \(\pi\eta\) distribution and negligible \(P\)-wave contributions near the \(a_0\) threshold. The results enable a combined analysis of the polarized reactions \(pp \to d\pi^+\eta\) and \(pn \to d\pi^0\eta\) to separate isospin-conserving background from the isospin-violating \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing signal, and to test the validity of chiral models at energies around the \(a_0(980)\) resonance.
