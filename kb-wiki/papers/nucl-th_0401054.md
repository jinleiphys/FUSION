# Theory of the Trojan-Horse Method

> The Trojan-Horse method is an indirect approach to determine the energy dependence of S factors of astrophysically relevant two-body reactions. This is accomplished by studying closely related three-body reactions under quasi-free scattering conditions. The basic theory of the Trojan-Horse method is developed starting from a post-form distorted wave Born approximation of the T-matrix element. In the surface approximation the cross section of the three-body reaction can be related to the S-matrix elements of the two-body reaction. The essential feature of the Trojan-Horse method is the effective suppression of the Coulomb barrier at low energies for the astrophysical reaction leading to finite cross sections at the threshold of the two-body reaction. In a modified plane wave approximation the relation between the two-body and three-body cross sections becomes very transparent. Applications of the Trojan Horse Method are discussed. It is of special interest that electron screening corrections are negligible due to the high projectile energy.

# Theory of the Trojan-Horse Method

## Key claim

The Trojan-Horse method can extract the energy dependence of astrophysical S-factors for two-body reactions by measuring a related three-body reaction under quasi-free conditions, because the Coulomb barrier is effectively suppressed in the three-body cross section, which remains finite even as the two-body c.m. energy approaches zero. However, the method relies on the surface approximation and a modified plane-wave treatment, and its validity must be tested against direct measurements of well-known reactions before firm conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

The authors develop the theory starting from a post-form distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) for the T-matrix element of the three-body breakup reaction A + a → C + c + b, where the Trojan horse a = (x + b). In the surface approximation, the overlap integral for the transferred particle is expressed in terms of the S-matrix elements of the two-body reaction A + x → C + c. Replacing distorted waves by plane waves yields a factorized cross section for the three-body reaction, consisting of a kinematical factor, a momentum distribution related to the Trojan-horse wavefunction, and a "Trojan-Horse" cross section that contains the S-matrix elements and special Trojan-Horse integrals.

## Key numbers

- The three-body cross section formula (Eq. d3s) includes a kinematical factor KF = (μ_Aa μ_Bb μ_Cc)/((2π)^5 ħ^6) (k_Bb k_Cc / k_Aa) (16π^2)/(k_Ax Q_Aa) (v_Cc / v_Ax).
- The quasi-free c.m. energy in the A+x system is given by E_Ax^qf = E_Aa (1 - μ_Aa/μ_Bb * μ_bx^2/m_x^2) - ε_a, where ε_a is the binding energy of the Trojan horse.
- The threshold behavior of the three-body cross section is proportional to k_Ax^(-2) v_Ax^(-1) exp(-2πη_Ax) × k_Ax^2 v_Ax exp(2πη_Ax) = constant, showing it remains finite at threshold.
- The surface approximation was checked by Kasano and Ichimura (1982) for the (d,p) reaction at E_d = 26 MeV and found to be quite good.

## Context

This work extends the earlier introduction of the Trojan-Horse method by Baur (1986) and Typel (2000, 2003), building on the observation by Fuchs (1971) of parallelism between two-body and three-body cross sections. The theory formalizes the relation between three-body breakup reactions and astrophysical two-body reactions, enabling extraction of low-energy S-factors without the problems of electron screening that affect direct measurements (Assenbaum 1987, Rolfs 2003). The method can be tested against direct measurements of well-studied reactions and may be applied to exotic nuclear beams, such as the d(56Ni,p)57Ni reaction studied in inverse kinematics by Rehm (1998).
