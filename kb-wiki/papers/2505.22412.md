# Neutron Magic Numbers in $sd$ Shell from Nuclear Charge Radii within Neutron-Proton Correction around the Fermi Surface

> Charge radii are sensitive indicators to identify the nuclear structure phenomena throughout the whole nuclide chart. In particular, the shrunken trend of changes of charge radii along a long isotopic chain is intimately associated with the shell quenching effect. In this work, the systematic evolution of charge radii along the proton numbers $Z=8$, $10$, $12$, $14$, $18$ isotopes is investigated by a relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov model. A ansatz about neutron-proton correlation around Fermi surface is considered for describing the abnormal behavior of nuclear charge radii. Our results show that the neutron-proton pairing corrections around the Fermi surface lead to a sudden strengthening of the charge radii of these isotopic chains at $N=8$, 20 and 28, reflecting the fact that this correction enhances the shell closure across $N=8$, 20 and 28. The reproduction of the $N=14$ charge radius in the Mg isotopes is affected by the way in which pairing correlations are handled, with BCS theory overestimating the shell effect of $N=14$, and the Bogoliubov quasiparticle transformation suggests a stronger pairing correlation near the proton Fermi surface, which is more consistent with experimental results. An analysis of the deviations from the theoretical and available experimental data for the charge radii of the 24 selected even-even nuclei shows that the neutron-proton pairing correction around the Fermi surface has an improved effect on the calculation of the charge {radii} using the meson-exchange effective interactions, but it does not help to significantly improve the results calculated by the density-dependent effective interactions.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton pairing correction around the Fermi surface enhances shell closure effects at N=8, 20, and 28, leading to sudden strengthening of charge radii in isotopic chains with Z=8, 10, 12, 14, and 18; however, this correction significantly improves charge radius calculations only when using meson-exchange effective interactions, not density-dependent effective interactions.

## Method

The authors employed the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model with axial and reflection symmetries, restricting calculations to quadrupole deformation β20. They incorporated a neutron-proton pairing correction term ΔD = |Dn - Dp| into the charge radius formula, where Dn,p sums the product of occupation probabilities u_k and v_k for quasiparticle orbitals within 20 MeV of the Fermi surface. Four effective interactions were tested: the meson-exchange PK1 and NL3, and the density-dependent DD-ME2 and DD-PC1.

## Key numbers

- The charge radius of 26Mg calculated with the RHB* model using NL3 effective interaction is 3.0434 fm, and with PK1 is 3.0415 fm, compared to the experimental value of 3.0340(26) fm
- For the 24 selected even-even nuclei, the average deviation χ̄² for the NL3 interaction improved from 990.43 (RHB) to 427.34 (RHB*), and for PK1 from 1625.83 to 951.87
- The root mean square deviation Δ for NL3 decreased from 0.0413 fm (RHB) to 0.0266 fm (RHB*), and for PK1 from 0.0719 fm to 0.0699 fm
- For DD-ME2, χ̄² improved from 648.80 (RHB) to 479.61 (RHB*), but Δ increased from 0.0319 fm to 0.0361 fm
- For DD-PC1, both χ̄² (952.21 to 1083.08) and Δ (0.0375 fm to 0.0482 fm) worsened after correction
- The separable pairing force used a pairing strength G = 728 MeV fm³ and effective range a = 0.644 fm

## Context

This work extends the RMF(BCS)* approach of An (2020) and An (2022), which described charge radii odd-even staggerings but overestimated the N=14 shell effect in Mg isotopes and underestimated charge radii for N>18 nuclei. The authors replace the BCS pairing treatment with Bogoliubov quasiparticle transformation, which is more suitable for nuclei far from the β-stability line as noted by Dobaczewski (1984, 1996). The results enable systematic description of charge radii from O to Ar isotopes and call for a unified theoretical approach to reconcile meson-exchange and density-dependent effective interactions.
