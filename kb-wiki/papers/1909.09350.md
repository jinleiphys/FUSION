# Precision calculation of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to T=1/2 mirror decays using multi-reference charge-dependent density functional theory

> We present systematic study of isospin impurities ($α_{\rm ISB}$) to the wave functions of $T=1/2$, $11\leq A \leq 47$ mirror nuclei and the isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections ($δ_{\rm ISB}^{\rm V}$) to their ground state vector $β$-decays using, for the first time, multi-reference charge-dependent density functional theory (MR-DFT) that includes strong-force-rooted class-III interaction adjusted to correct for the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in nuclear masses. We demonstrate that, unexpectedly, the strong-force-rooted isovector force gives rise to a large systematic increase of $α_{\rm ISB}$ and $δ_{\rm ISB}^{\rm V}$ as compared to the results obtained within MR-DFT that uses Coulomb interaction as the only source of ISB. This, in turn, increases a central value of the $V_{\rm ud}$ element of the CKM matrix extracted from the $T=1/2$ mirrors bringing it closer to the value obtained form the purely vector superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ transitions. In order to compute the value of $V_{\rm ud}$, we performed precision calculation of the Fermi matrix elements in $A=19, 21, 35$, and 37 mirror nuclei using DFT-rooted configuration-interaction model that includes all relevant axially-deformed particle-hole configurations built upon Nilsson orbitals originating from the spherical $sd$ shell. Our calculations yield $|V_{\rm ud}|=0.9736(16)$.

## Key claim
The inclusion of strong-force-rooted class-III isovector contact interactions, adjusted to correct the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, unexpectedly and systematically increases isospin impurities and ISB corrections in T=1/2 mirror nuclei compared to calculations using only the Coulomb interaction, bringing the extracted V_ud value closer to that from superallowed 0+ → 0+ transitions.

## Method
The authors used multi-reference density functional theory (MR-DFT) with isospin and angular-momentum projection, incorporating Coulomb plus short-range class-III isovector forces at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO). For the four best-measured mirror decays (A=19, 21, 35, 37), they performed DFT-rooted no-core configuration interaction (DFT-NCCI) calculations mixing axially-deformed particle-hole configurations built upon Nilsson orbitals from the spherical sd shell, using the SV_SO Skyrme force.

## Key numbers
- The LO class-III contact term increases isospin impurities relative to Coulomb-only calculations by 90% at low A, decreasing to circa 40-50% in the lower fp-shell nuclei.
- The fitted LO coupling constant is t_0^III = -6.3 ± 0.3 MeV fm^3; the NLO fit gives t_0^III = 0 ± 2 MeV fm^3, t_1^III = -2 ± 2 MeV fm^5, and t_2^III = -4 ± 1 MeV fm^5.
- For A=19, the ISB correction δ_ISB^V drops from 0.738% (single configuration) to 0.430% after admixing two excited configurations in the NLO variant.
- For A=37, δ_ISB^V drops from 1.833% to 1.042% after configuration mixing in the NLO variant.
- The final extracted V_ud = 0.9736 ± 0.0016 from the DFT-NCCI NLO calculation.
- The average nucleus-independent reduced lifetime is 6152(21) s for the NLO variant.
- The unitarity test yields 0.9983(35) for the NLO variant.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior charge-dependent DFT framework (Baczyk 2018, 2019) that corrected the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen 1969) in nuclear masses, now applied to beta-decay ISB corrections. It contrasts with the nuclear shell model results of Severijns (2008) and earlier MR-DFT calculations (Satula 2009, 2011) that used only Coulomb ISB. The results enable more precise extraction of V_ud from mirror decays, providing an independent cross-check of the CKM matrix unitarity alongside superallowed 0+ → 0+ transitions (Hardy 2015).
