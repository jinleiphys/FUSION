# Long-range azimuthal correlations for partially coherent pion emission in proton-proton collisions

> In this paper, we investigate the influence of coherent pion emission on long-range azimuthal correlations in relativistic proton-proton collisions. We study the pion momentum distribution for a coherent source with both transverse and Bjorken longitudinal expansions, and calculate the two-particle correlation function $C(Δη,Δϕ)$. A "ridge" structure is observed in the correlation function $C(Δη,Δϕ)$ of the coherent pion emission, whether the coherent source is expanding or static. The onset of this long-range azimuthal correlation can be traced back to the asymmetric initial transverse profile of the coherent source, owing to the interference in coherent emission. We further construct a partially coherent pion-emitting source by incorporating a chaotic emission component. From the experimental data of the two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}\!=\!7\,$TeV, we extract a coherent fraction of pion emission, which increases with increasing charged-particle multiplicity, as an input of the partially coherent source model. The ridge structure is observed in the correlation function $C(Δη,Δϕ)$ for the partially coherent source. The correlation becomes stronger at higher multiplicities because of the larger degrees of coherence. The results in this work are meaningful for fully understanding the collectivity in the small system of pp collisions.

## Key claim
The "ridge" structure in two-particle angular correlations can arise from coherent pion emission alone, without requiring collective expansion of the source, provided the initial transverse profile of the coherent source is asymmetric. The effect of source expansion on the correlation function is small, and the long-range azimuthal correlation becomes stronger at higher charged-particle multiplicities because the coherent fraction of pion emission increases with multiplicity.

## Method
The authors model a coherent pion-emitting source with a Gaussian transverse profile and Bjorken longitudinal expansion, then compute the single-pion momentum distribution via the coherent superposition of sub-source emission amplitudes. They construct a partially coherent source by combining this coherent component with a chaotic emission component described by a blast-wave model, and extract the coherent fraction as a function of charged-particle multiplicity from ATLAS two-pion HBT correlation data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV. Two-particle angular correlation functions C(Δη,Δϕ) are calculated by randomly generating 10^6 pions from the single-particle distributions and comparing to a background with azimuthal isotropy.

## Key numbers
- Initial transverse sizes of coherent source: R_T = sqrt(R_x R_y) = 0.12 fm, with shape S_T = R_y/R_x = 1.45.
- Source space-time rapidity range: Δη_s0 = 4.
- Initial time and lifetime of coherent source: τ_i = 0 fm/c and τ_s = 0.5 fm/c.
- Velocity parameters for anisotropic transverse expansion: a_x = 0.6, a_y = 0.56, b_{x,y} = 0.5.
- Kinematic region for pions: |η| < 2.5 and 0.5 < p_T < 5.0 GeV.
- Blast-wave model parameters for chaotic emission: T = 100 MeV, n = 2; transverse expansion velocity β_s varied from 0 to 0.66.
- Coherent fraction f_c extracted from HBT strength λ^HBT using relation f_c ≈ [1 - λ^HBT]^{1/2}; λ^HBT fitted as λ^HBT(N_ch) = γ e^{-N_ch δ}.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Ru and Zhang, 2017) on elliptic anisotropy from coherent emission, now incorporating longitudinal expansion and two-particle angular correlations. It addresses the ongoing debate on the origin of collectivity in small systems (pp and pA collisions), where ridge structures have been observed (CMS, ATLAS, ALICE, LHCb, PHENIX). The results suggest that the ridge in pp collisions may have a significant contribution from quantum interference in coherent emission rather than solely from hydrodynamic collective flow, and that the increasing coherent fraction with multiplicity extracted from HBT data (ATLAS, 2015) can explain the multiplicity dependence of the correlation strength.
