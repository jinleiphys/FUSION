# Interacting electrons and bosons in the doubly screened $G\widetilde{W}$ approximation: A time-linear scaling method for first-principles simulations

> We augment the time-linear formulation of the Kadanoff-Baym equations for systems of interacting electrons and quantized phonons or photons with the $G\widetilde{W}$ approximation, the Coulomb interaction $\widetilde{W}$ being dynamically screened by both electron-hole pairs {\em and} bosonic particles. We also show how to combine different approximations to include simultaneously multiple correlation effects in the dynamics. The final outcome is a versatile framework comprising $2^{12}$ distinct diagrammatic methods, each scaling linearly in time and preserving all fundamental conservation laws. The dramatic improvement over current state-of-the-art approximations brought about by $G\widetilde{W}$ is demonstrated in a study of the correlation-induced charge migration of the glycine molecule in an optical cavity.

## Key claim

The authors extend the time-linear formulation of the Kadanoff-Baym equations to include the $G\widetilde{W}$ approximation, where the Coulomb interaction $\widetilde{W}$ is dynamically screened by both electron-hole pairs and bosonic particles; the method scales linearly in time and preserves all fundamental conservation laws, but the demonstration is limited to a single molecule (glycine) in an optical cavity, and the authors acknowledge that achieving the full configuration interaction value for the shake-up energy would require vertex corrections beyond the current formulation.

## Method

The authors augment the Generalized Kadanoff-Baym Ansatz (GKBA) for electrons and bosons with a time-linear ordinary differential equation (ODE) scheme, deriving closed-form equations of motion for the one-electron density matrix, one-boson density matrix, and high-order Green's functions. The doubly screened $G\widetilde{W}$ method is formulated through a reducible Baym functional $\Phi_c^{(r)}$ where the effective interaction $\widetilde{v} = v + g^\dagger D g$ includes both bare Coulomb and boson-mediated interactions, leading to a system of seven first-order ODEs that can be solved with a time-stepping algorithm. The framework allows combining different diagrammatic approximations for electronic correlations (including $GW$, $T$-matrix, second-order direct and exchange) with electron-boson treatments (Ehrenfest, $GD$, $G\widetilde{W}^{(2)}$, and full $G\widetilde{W}$) through control parameters $d$, $s_1$, and $s_2$.

## Key numbers

* The energy difference between the shake-up state $\Psi_{\rm shake-up}$ and the initial state $\Psi_i$ of glycine after photoionization is $\Delta = 0.522$ a.u.
* The configuration interaction (CI) spectrogram shows a single peak at frequency $\Delta_{\rm CI} = 0.544$ a.u., approximately equal to $\Delta$
* The 2B approximation yields $\Delta_{\rm 2B} = 0.356$ a.u., while the $GW+GD$ method renormalizes this to $\Delta_{GW} = 0.503$ a.u., approximately $\Delta_{\rm 2B} + 2v^x_{4a'',5a''}$ where the exchange Coulomb integral $v^x_{4a'',5a''} = 0.08$ a.u.
* The electron-photon coupling strength is $\lambda = 0.212$ a.u., and the dipole moment between the $4a''$ and $5a''$ molecular orbitals is $d_{4a'',5a''} = 0.125$ a.u.
* The framework comprises $2^{12}$ distinct diagrammatic methods, each scaling linearly in time

## Context

This work extends the time-linear GKBA+ODE formulation developed by Pavlyukh (2021) and Karlsson (2021) for interacting electrons and bosons, which previously included $GW$, $T$-matrix, and $GD$ approximations but lacked dynamical screening of the electron-boson coupling by electron-hole pairs. The doubly screened $G\widetilde{W}$ method addresses a limitation of state-of-the-art calculations by Giustino (2007) and Marini (2015) that used only static screening of the electron-phonon coupling, and enables real-time first-principles simulations of phenomena such as carrier relaxation (Sangalli 2015, Molina-Sanchez 2017), exciton recombination (Selig 2016, Trovatello 2020), and molecular charge migration in optical or plasmonic cavities (Flick 2018, Schafer 2019, Li 2021). The demonstration on glycine in an optical cavity shows that $G\widetilde{W}$ captures the Autler-Townes doublet (Autler 1955) arising from photon-dressing of the cationic state, which is absent in the $GW+GD$ approximation, though the authors note that achieving the full CI value for the shake-up energy would require vertex corrections beyond the current GKBA+ODE formulation.
