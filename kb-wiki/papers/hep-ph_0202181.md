# Pedagogic model for Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering with quark-hadron duality

> We show how quark-hadron duality can emerge for valence spin averaged structure functions, and for the non-forward distributions of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering. Novel factorisations of the non-forward amplitudes are proposed. Some implications for large angle scattering and deviations from the quark counting rules are illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates how quark-hadron duality can emerge for valence spin-averaged structure functions and for non-forward distributions in Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS), but only under the condition that at least one complete set of resonances of each symmetry type (even and odd parity) has been summed over, causing destructive interference of higher-twist terms.

## Method

The authors construct a pedagogic model of a two-body composite system with spinless constituents confined by a linear potential described by the Klein-Gordon equation, with energy eigenvalues following a harmonic oscillator spectrum. They compute the longitudinal response function for forward and non-forward Compton scattering by summing over all intermediate resonance states, using analytic wavefunctions from the harmonic oscillator basis to derive scaling behavior and the generalized parton distribution functions for DVCS in both Radyushkin's and Ji's kinematic frames.

## Key numbers

- The energy eigenvalues are given by \(E_N = \sqrt{2\beta^2(N+3/2) + m^2}\), where \(\beta^4\) is the relativistic string constant and \(m\) is the constituent mass
- For numerical illustrations, the authors use \(\beta = 0.4\) GeV and \(m = 0.33\) GeV for the constituent mass
- The peak of the structure function occurs at \(x_{bj}^{(N)} = E_0/M \sim 1/A\), where \(A\) is the number of active constituents
- The momentum sum rule gives \(\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} F_2(x_{bj}) dx_{bj} = \frac{1}{M}(E_0 + \frac{\beta^2}{2E_0})(e_1^2 + e_2^2)\)
- The elastic form factor in the model is \(F_{00}(t) \equiv e^{-(\vec{\Delta}^2 / 4\beta^2)}\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Isgur, Jaffe, Melnitchouk, and Vanderhaeghen (1998) and Close and Isgur (2001), which analyzed duality in forward Compton scattering for spinless constituents. The paper provides a pedagogic framework connecting resonance excitations to the naive parton model, showing how the structure function collapses to a delta function \(\delta(x_{bj} - 1/A)\) in the limit \(\beta \to 0\) where constituents become free. The results enable future investigations of DVCS measurements planned at Jefferson Lab and HERA, and suggest that the commonly used factorization ansatz \(F(x,0,0) F_{el}(t)\) is not a general result in DVCS, contrary to the assumption in Vanderhaeghen (1998).
