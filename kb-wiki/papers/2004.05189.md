# Proto-Strange Quark Star Structure

> In this paper, we investigate the newborn strange quark stars with constant entropy. We also use the MIT bag model to calculate the thermodynamic properties in two cases; the density-dependent bag constant and the fixed bag constant (B = 90 MeV). We show that the equation of state becomes stiffer by using the density dependent bag constant and by increasing the entropy. Furthermore, we show that the adiabatic index of the system reaches to 4/3 at high densities. Later, we calculate the structure of a strange quark star using the equation of state and the general relativistic equations of hydrostatic equilibrium, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations. We show that the gravitational mass of the star decreases by increasing the entropy and the maximum gravitational mass is larger when we use the density-dependent bag constant at fixed central energy density. It is shown that the mass-radius relation for this system obeys M R^ 3 for different cases of the calculations. Finally, we show that for a given stellar mass considering the fixed bag constant, the maximum gravitational red shift of a strange quark star occurs at larger values of entropy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for proto-strange quark stars with constant entropy, the equation of state becomes stiffer with increasing entropy and when using a density-dependent bag constant, and that the maximum gravitational mass decreases with increasing entropy at a fixed central energy density. The abstract glosses over the condition that these results hold specifically for the MIT bag model with either a fixed bag constant of 90 MeV/fm³ or a density-dependent bag constant parameterized from CERN experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model to compute thermodynamic properties of strange quark matter (up, down, and strange quarks) at constant entropy, treating the system as a Fermi gas with the strange quark mass set to 150 MeV/c². They calculated the equation of state for two cases of the bag constant (fixed at 90 MeV/fm³ and density-dependent) at entropies of S = 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 k_B, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the mass, radius, and gravitational redshift of the proto-strange quark star.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass set to 150 MeV/c²; up and down quarks treated as massless.
- Fixed bag constant B = 90 MeV/fm³; density-dependent bag constant uses B₀ = 400 MeV/fm³ at n=0 and B_∞ = 8.99 MeV/fm³.
- Normal nuclear matter density n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ used in the density-dependent bag constant parameterization.
- For the density-dependent bag constant, the energy density shows a minimum at density 0.4 fm⁻³, indicating a stable state.
- The adiabatic index reaches 4/3 at high densities for both bag constant cases.
- Maximum gravitational redshift Z = 0.48 for the density-dependent bag constant at entropy S = 2.5 k_B; minimum Z = 0.4 for the fixed bag constant at entropy S = 1.5 k_B.
- The mass-radius relation follows M ∝ R³ for all cases considered.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange quark stars by Bordhar and collaborators (2015-2019) that used the MIT bag model at zero and finite temperatures, now incorporating constant entropy appropriate for neutrino-trapped proto-neutron star conditions. It builds on the treatment of proto-neutron star matter by Bethe et al. (2002) and the investigation of proto-quark stars using both NJL and MIT bag models by Dexheimer et al. The results enable comparison of proto-strange quark star structure with observations of candidate objects like RX-J185635-3754 (M = 1.7 M_⊙, R = 11 km) and 3C58, and provide a baseline for future calculations including magnetic fields or dynamical quark masses.
