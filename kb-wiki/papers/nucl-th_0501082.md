# Neutron capture cross sections: from theory to experiments and back

> The method for an experimental determination of the stellar enhancement factor for the cross section of the $^{151}$Sm$(n,γ)$ reaction process is proposed. This study offered the pretext for an excursus on the interconnections between capture and dissociation reactions and the interplay between theory and experiments in the determination of neutron capture cross sections.

## Key claim

The stellar enhancement factor (SEF) for the \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)\) reaction, which is normally derived from Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations with significant uncertainty, could be determined experimentally for the first time by measuring the time-reversed \(^{152}\)Sm\((\gamma,n)^{151}\)Sm dissociation reaction, because this inverse channel selectively populates only excited states in the residual \(^{151}\)Sm nucleus just above the neutron emission threshold.

## Method

The author proposes using the detailed-balance relation between the neutron capture reaction \(n + ^{151}\)Sm \(\rightarrow ^{152}\)Sm + \(\gamma\) and the photodissociation reaction \(^{152}\)Sm + \(\gamma \rightarrow ^{151}\)Sm + \(n\). Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations were performed for the \(^{152}\)Sm\((\gamma,n)^{151}\)Sm cross section using the Moldauer optical model parameters, nuclear level densities from Mengoni (1994), and experimental Giant Dipole Resonance parameters from Carlos (1974). The calculated cross section was renormalized by a factor of 1.7 to match the measured Maxwellian averaged capture cross section (MACS) of \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)\) at \(kT = 30\) keV.

## Key numbers

- MACS-30 for \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)^{152}\)Sm measured at CERN n_TOF: \(3100 \pm 160\) mb (Abbondanno, 2004).
- MACS-30 for \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)^{152}\)Sm measured at FZK, Karlsruhe: \(3031 \pm 68\) mb (Wisshak, 2004).
- Theoretical Hauser-Feshbach estimates of the MACS-30 for \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)\) are typically around 2000 mb.
- The NON-SMOKER calculation of the SEF for \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)\) at \(kT = 30\) keV is 0.87 (Rauscher, 2001); the present work obtained 0.93.
- The first excited state of \(^{151}\)Sm at \(E_x = 4.82\) keV is populated at a 30% level at stellar temperatures of \(kT = 30\) keV.
- The fifth excited state of \(^{151}\)Sm at \(E_x = 104.8\) keV is a \(J^\pi = 3/2^-\) state.
- The normalization factor applied to both the present HFSM calculations and the NON-SMOKER result to match the measured MACS-30 was 1.7.

## Context

This work extends the use of photodissociation reactions, previously applied to determine capture cross sections of unstable isotopes at \(s\)-process branching points (Utsunomiya, 2004), to the specific problem of experimentally determining the SEF for the first time. The \(^{151}\)Sm\((n,\gamma)\) reaction is a branching point in the \(s\)-process path that influences the isotopic abundance ratio of \(^{152}\)Gd and \(^{154}\)Gd, and its SEF is needed to constrain temperature conditions in AGB stars. The proposed measurement would resolve discrepancies between different Hauser-Feshbach model calculations (e.g., NON-SMOKER by Rauscher (2001) giving SEF = 0.87 versus the present calculation giving 0.93) and enable more reliable stellar reaction rates without relying solely on theoretical normalization.
