# Dibaryons with two strange quarks and total spin zero in a constituent quark model

> We investigate the symmetry property and construct the wave function of the dibaryon states containing two strange quarks with S=0 in both the flavor SU(3) symmetric and breaking cases. We discuss how the color $\otimes$ isospin $\otimes$ spin states of dibaryon in the symmetry broking case of flavor SU(3) can be extracted from the fully antisymmetric states in flavor SU(3). The stability of the dibaryon against the strong decay into two baryons are then discussed, by using the variational method within a constituent quark model with a confining and color-spin interactions. To compare our results with that from lattice QCD in flavor SU(3) limit, we search for the stable H-dibaryon in a wide range of $π$ meson mass. We find that with the given potential, there is no compact six quark dibaryon state in the SU(3) flavor symmetry broken case with realistic quark masses as well as in flavor SU(3) symmetric case in a wide range of quark masses.

## Key claim

The authors find that, within their constituent quark model using a confining and color-spin interaction Hamiltonian, there is no compact six-quark dibaryon state (including the H-dibaryon) in either the flavor SU(3) symmetric case across a wide range of quark masses or in the SU(3) flavor symmetry broken case with realistic quark masses, contrary to some lattice QCD results that suggest a bound H-dibaryon for massive pion masses.

## Method

The authors adopt a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian with confinement and hyperfine color-spin interactions, fitting parameters to reproduce experimental baryon octet and decuplet masses via a variational method. They construct the color ⊗ isospin ⊗ spin wave functions for dibaryons containing two strange quarks with total spin S=0, using Young-Yamanouchi basis states and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the permutation group, for both the flavor SU(3) symmetric and broken cases. The spatial wave function is taken as a Gaussian form with five Jacobian coordinates and three variational parameters (a, b, c), and the mass of the six-quark system is minimized variationally to test stability against strong decay into two baryons.

## Key numbers

* Fitted constituent quark masses: m_u = 0.347 GeV, m_s = 0.596 GeV.
* Fitted model parameters include κ = 0.123, a_0 = 1.049 GeV^{-3/2}, D = 0.994 GeV, κ_0 = 0.35 GeV, α = 2.105 GeV, β = 9.164, γ = 0.309 GeV^{-1}.
* Calculated baryon masses (in GeV) from the variational method: nucleon (I=1/2, S=1/2) = 0.974; Λ (I=0, S=1/2) = 1.115; Σ (I=1, S=1/2) = 1.217; Ξ (I=1/2, S=1/2) = 1.344; Δ (I=3/2, S=3/2) = 1.233; Σ* (I=1, S=3/2) = 1.398; Ξ* (I=1/2, S=3/2) = 1.554; Ω (I=0, S=3/2) = 1.7.

## Context

This work extends the systematic classification of dibaryons by Silvestre-Brac (1992) and Leandri (1995) to the case of two strange quarks with spin zero, explicitly constructing the color-isospin-spin states for the flavor SU(3) broken case. The results contradict lattice QCD findings by Beane (2010) and Inoue (2010) that suggested a bound H-dibaryon for massive pion masses, as well as the chiral constituent quark model calculation by Carames (2012) that found a bound H-dibaryon. The authors suggest that if the lattice results are valid, the H-dibaryon would have to be a hadronic molecular state rather than a compact six-quark state.
