# Thermomagnetic Effects of Quark Matter in the NJL Model: Application of Regularization Schemes

> In the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of a thermally magnetized medium, the regularization methods adopted for the thermodynamic potential and the mass gap equation are utilized to calculate the relevant thermodynamic quantities in thermomagnetic quark matter. When dealing with the thermodynamic quantities and the gap equation, three schemes can be chosen, namely magnetic field-independent regularization, soft cut-off regularization, and Pauli-Villars regularization. These three regularization schemes have different influences on the properties of thermomagnetic quark matter, and different choices of regularization schemes will also lead to different effects in the calculation of the properties of thermomagnetic quark matter.

## Key claim

The choice of regularization scheme (magnetic field-independent, soft cut-off, or Pauli-Villars) in the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model significantly alters the calculated thermodynamic properties of thermomagnetic quark matter, and no single scheme is universally applicable; the paper shows that applying regularization to the temperature-dependent part of the thermodynamic potential can lead to violations of causality and destabilize pressure at high temperatures.

## Method

The author computes the thermodynamic potential, pressure, energy density, specific heat, and speed of sound for three-flavor quark matter in a static constant magnetic field using the SU(3) NJL model under the mean-field approximation. Three regularization schemes are applied to handle ultraviolet divergences: magnetic field-independent regularization (MFIR), soft cut-off regularization using a smooth Fermi-Dirac-type function, and Pauli-Villars regularization using a sum over four mass terms. For the soft cut-off and Pauli-Villars schemes, the author compares results both with and without applying the regularization to the temperature-dependent (medium) part of the thermodynamic potential and condensate.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: current quark masses for MFIR and soft cut-off are \(m_u = m_d = 5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s = 135.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for Pauli-Villars they are \(m_u = m_d = 10.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s = 236.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Cut-off parameters: \(\Lambda = 631.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for MFIR and soft cut-off; \(\Lambda = 781.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Pauli-Villars.
- Coupling constants for MFIR and soft cut-off: \(G = 1.835 / \Lambda^2\), \(K = 9.29 / \Lambda^2\); for Pauli-Villars: \(G = 4.90 / \Lambda^2\), \(K = 129.8 / \Lambda^2\).
- Pion mass used: \(m_\pi = 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The strange quark mass decreases more at high temperatures \(T > 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) when regularization is applied to the temperature-dependent part.
- At fixed temperature \(T = 170\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the pressure under soft cut-off remains almost unchanged at low magnetic fields but shows oscillatory suppression near \(eB = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The speed of sound no longer approaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of \(1/3\) but shows a sharp increase when regularization is applied to the temperature-dependent part.

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetized quark matter within the NJL model, following prior approaches by Hatsuda (1994) for parameter sets and by Gandhi (2021), Allen (2015), and Avancini (2020) for regularization techniques. It directly compares three regularization schemes (MFIR, soft cut-off, Pauli-Villars) that have been used separately in earlier work by Chaudhuri (2022) and Carignano (2019), showing that the choice of scheme qualitatively affects predictions for pressure, phase transition temperature, and thermodynamic stability. The results enable future researchers to select the most appropriate regularization scheme for their specific physical question, such as studying anomalous magnetic moment effects where Pauli-Villars without temperature regularization is recommended, or separating magnetic and non-magnetic contributions where MFIR is preferred.
