# Transport coefficients of two-flavor superconducting quark matter

> Background: The two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase of quark matter is a possible constituent of the core of neutron stars. To assess its impact on the observable behavior of the star one must analyze transport properties, which in 2SC matter are controlled by the scattering of gapless fermionic modes by each other and possibly also by color-magnetic flux tubes. Purpose: We determine the electrical and thermal conductivities and the shear viscosity of 2SC matter. Methods: We use a variational formulation of transport theory, treating the strong and electromagnetic interactions via a weak coupling expansion. Results: We provide the leading order scaling of the transport coefficients with temperature and chemical potential as well as accurate fits to our numerical results. We also find that the scattering of fermions by color-magnetic flux tubes is insignificant for thermal conductivity, but may contribute to the electrical conductivity and shear viscosity in the limit of very low temperature or high magnetic field. We also estimate the transport coefficients in unpaired quark matter. Conclusions: Our calculation has set the stage for exploration of possible signatures of the presence of 2SC quark matter in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The transport coefficients of two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) quark matter are determined by the scattering of gapless fermionic modes (blue up quarks, blue down quarks, and electrons) via both strong and electromagnetic interactions, with the dominant transport carrier shifting from blue down quarks at low temperatures to electrons at high temperatures, depending on the relative importance of Landau-damped long-range rotated electromagnetic interactions versus short-range strong interactions.

## Method

The authors used a variational formulation of transport theory based on the linearized Boltzmann equation, treating strong and electromagnetic interactions via a weak coupling expansion. They computed the scattering matrix elements for fermion-fermion scattering mediated by the relevant gauge bosons in the 2SC phase (the longitudinal T8 and Q bosons, and the transverse X and rotated electromagnetic Q̃ bosons), incorporating Debye screening, Meissner screening, and Landau damping. The transport coefficients were obtained by solving for the relaxation times of the three ungapped fermion species (blue up quark, blue down quark, and electron) using a multicomponent relaxation time approximation, with numerical integration over energy and momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potentials in the 2SC phase (ignoring Δ²/μ_q² corrections): μ_bu ≈ 0.566 μ_q, μ_bd ≈ 1.13 μ_q, μ_e ≈ 0.566 μ_q, μ_C ≈ 0.934 μ_q
- The Cooper pair gap Δ is expected to be around 10 MeV or larger
- The fine structure constant α = e²/(4π) = 1/137
- The Debye mass for the Q̃ interaction is given by Λ² ≡ Σ_i (Q̃_i)² μ_i²/(4π)
- The maximum momentum transfer q_M = min[2μ_i, 2μ_j] for scattering between species i and j

## Context

This work extends the perturbative transport calculations of Heiselberg and Pethick (1993) from unpaired quark matter to the 2SC phase, and complements studies of transport in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase by Shovkovy (2002), Manuel and collaborators (2005, 2007), and others. The results enable modeling of thermal and magnetic evolution, rotational dynamics, and electromagnetic and gravitational wave emission from neutron stars that may contain 2SC quark matter in their cores, setting the stage for identifying possible observational signatures of this phase.
