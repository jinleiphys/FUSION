# Quantum liquids resulted from the models with four-fermion interaction

> A (nearly) perfect liquid discovered in the experements with ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is investigated by studying the quark ensembles with four-fermion interection as a fundamental theoretical approach. The comparative analysis of several quantum liquid models is performed and it results in the conclusion that the presence of gas---liquid phase transition is their characteristic feature. The problem of instability of the quark droplets of small quark number is discussed and argued to be rooted in the chiral soliton formation. An existence of mixed phase of the vacuum and baryon matter is proposed as a possible reason of the latter stability.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the (nearly) perfect liquid observed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can be understood through four-fermion interaction models, and that the presence of a gas-liquid phase transition is a characteristic feature of such quantum liquid models, with the instability of small quark-number droplets rooted in chiral soliton formation.

## Method

The authors study quark ensembles using a Hamiltonian with four-fermion interaction derived from a simplified gluon field correlator, comparing two limiting formfactor choices: the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (delta-like in coordinate space, zero correlation length) and the Keldysh (KKB) model (delta-like in momentum space, infinite correlation length). They employ the Bogolyubov–Hartree–Fock approximation to construct a statistical operator and derive the mean energy functional, which they then analyze using the framework of Landau Fermi-liquid theory to compute thermodynamic and transport properties.

## Key numbers

- NJL model parameters: cutoff momentum Λ = 631 MeV, coupling constant GΛ²/(2π²) ≈ 1.3, current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV
- Dynamical quark mass in NJL model under normal conditions (T=0, μ=0): M_q ≈ 340 MeV
- Running coupling constant at LHC energies: α_s ∼ 0.3–0.4
- In the KKB model chiral limit, quark states with momenta |p| < 2G are degenerate in energy P_0 = 2G
- The first sound velocity tends to asymptotic value C_1 = 1/√3 at baryon densities somewhat higher than normal nuclear density
- Thermal conductivity at constant volume and low temperature: C_V = (1/3)π² N_F T

## Context

This work extends the application of four-fermion interaction models (NJL model from Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) and KKB model from Keldysh) to describe the nearly perfect liquid observed in RHIC and LHC heavy ion collisions, building on the hydrodynamic framework developed by Landau (1950s) and later refined by Teaney and others. The analysis connects to the universal lower bound for η/s proposed by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) and parallels studies of strongly correlated fermionic systems in ultracold atomic gases and graphene monolayers. The results enable a thermodynamic description of quark-gluon matter that emphasizes the role of vacuum condensates and chiral symmetry restoration, suggesting that the mixed phase of vacuum and baryon matter may explain the stability of quark droplets.
