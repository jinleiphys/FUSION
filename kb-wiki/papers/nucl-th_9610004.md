# Self-Consistent Velocity Dependent Effective Interactions

> The theory of self-consistent effective interactions in nuclei is extended for a system with a velocity dependent mean potential. By means of the field coupling method, we present a general prescription to derive effective interactions which are consistent with the mean potential. For a deformed system with the conventional pairing field, the velocity dependent effective interactions are derived as the multipole pairing interactions in doubly-stretched coordinates. They are applied to the microscopic analysis of the giant dipole resonances (GDR's) of ${}^{148,154}Sm$, the first excited $2^+$ states of Sn isotopes and the first excited $3^-$ states of Mo isotopes. It is clarified that the interactions play crucial roles in describing the splitting and structure of GDR peaks, in restoring the energy weighted sum rule, and in reducing the values of $B(Eλ)$.

## Key claim

The paper extends the theory of self-consistent effective interactions to systems with velocity-dependent mean potentials, claiming that the derived doubly-stretched multipole pairing interactions are essential for correctly describing giant dipole resonance splitting and structure, restoring the energy-weighted sum rule, and reducing B(Eλ) values, but only when the interactions are constructed to satisfy both nuclear self-consistency and local Galilean invariance.

## Method

The authors apply the field coupling method of Bohr and Mottelson to derive effective interactions by requiring that the collective displacement of nucleons and the associated velocity field produce variations in the mean potential and density that satisfy self-consistency conditions. For deformed nuclei, they express the collective mode in doubly-stretched coordinates and derive both multipole-multipole interactions (from the coordinate-dependent part of the potential) and multipole pairing interactions (from the velocity-dependent part, including the monopole pairing field). The resulting interactions are then applied in random phase approximation (RPA) calculations to giant dipole resonances of samarium isotopes and low-lying states of tin and molybdenum isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Giant dipole resonance energy for a spherical nucleus: Ω_GDR = √(1+ξ) ω_0, with ξ ≈ 3 giving Ω_GDR ≈ 80A^{-1/3} MeV
- For doubly-stretched dipole interaction in deformed nuclei, the total energy splitting between K=0 and K=1 GDR components equals ε in units of Ω_GDR, independent of ξ, matching the classical geometrical relation (ω_⊥ − ω_z)/ω_0 = ε
- For ordinary (non-doubly-stretched) dipole interaction with ξ=3, the GDR splitting is too small by a factor of 4 compared to the doubly-stretched case
- The self-consistent strength for the doubly-stretched multipole interaction: χ_λ^{self} = (4π/(2λ+1)) × (Mω_0^2)/(A⟨(r'')^{2λ-2}⟩_0)
- The self-consistent strength for the multipole pairing interaction: G_λ^{self} = 1 / [Σ_{αβ} (1/4)(1/E_α + 1/E_β)|⟨α|Q''_{λμ}|β⟩|^2]

## Context

This work extends the nuclear self-consistency approach of Kammuri, Matsuo, and Sakamoto (1975) and Sakamoto and Kishimoto (1989) to include velocity-dependent potentials, building on the symmetry-restoring method developed by Pyatov and Salamov (1977) and Pyatov, Salamov, and Guseva (1977). The paper provides a unified derivation of multipole-multipole interactions in particle-hole channels and multipole pairing interactions in particle-particle channels, which had previously been treated separately. The results enable more reliable microscopic calculations of collective excitations in deformed and superconducting nuclei, particularly for giant resonances where spurious effects from Galilean invariance violation must be removed.
