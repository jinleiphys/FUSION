# Inhomogeneous quark condensate in compressed skyrmion matter

> The inhomogeneous quark condensate, responsible for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in cold nuclear matter, is studied by putting skyrmions onto the face-centered cubic crystal and treating the skyrmion matter as nuclear matter. By varying the crystal size, we explore the effect of density on the local structure of the quark-antiquark condensate. By endowing the light vector mesons $ρ$ and $ω$ with hidden local symmetry and incorporating a scalar meson as a dilaton of spontaneously broken scale symmetry, we uncover the intricate interplay of heavy mesons in the local structure of the quark condensate in dense baryonic matter described in terms of skyrmion crystal. It is found that the inhomogeneous quark density persists to as high a density as $\sim 4$ times nuclear matter density. The difference between the result from the present approach and that from the chiral density wave ansatz is also discussed.

## Key claim

The inhomogeneous quark condensate persists to densities as high as approximately 4 times nuclear matter density, but the two-quark condensate is not a true order parameter for chiral symmetry in this skyrmion crystal approach because it vanishes on average in a unit cell above the half-skyrmion transition density while chiral symmetry is not actually restored.

## Method

The authors place skyrmions (representing nucleons) onto a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice and treat the skyrmion matter as nuclear matter, varying the crystal size to simulate changes in density. They employ a generalized skyrmion model (dHLS) that incorporates pions, vector mesons ρ and ω via hidden local symmetry, and a scalar dilaton field χ associated with spontaneously broken scale symmetry. The quark condensate is extracted from the chiral field U(x) decomposed into isoscalar (φ₀) and isovector (φ₁, φ₂, φ₃) components, with the Fourier coefficients of these fields determined by minimizing the energy for each crystal size L.

## Key numbers

- The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions occurs at a density n₁/₂ ∼ 2 n₀, where n₀ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The inhomogeneous quark condensate persists to densities as high as ∼ 4 times nuclear matter density.
- The half-skyrmion phase appears at a crystal size L = 1.45 fm.
- The crystal size L = 1.5 fm corresponds to the skyrmion phase for all four models considered (HLS(π), HLS(π,ρ), HLS(π,ρ,ω), and dHLS-II(π,ρ,ω,χ)).
- The space-averaged quark condensate φ₀ is approximately 1 (its QCD vacuum value) at L = 2.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach of Klebanov (1985) and Kugler & Shtrikman (1988, 1989) by incorporating vector mesons and a dilaton field to study inhomogeneous quark condensates. It contrasts with the chiral density wave (CDW) approach of Heinz et al. (2013), where the quark-antiquark condensate serves as a bona-fide order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration, whereas in this skyrmion description it behaves more like a "pseudo-gap." The results enable future studies of parity doubling in the light meson sector, building on recent work by Suenaga et al. (2014) that showed chiral partners of heavy-light mesons become degenerate in the half-skyrmion phase.
