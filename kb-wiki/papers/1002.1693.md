# Sensitivity analysis of random two-body interactions

> The input to the configuration-interaction shell model includes many dozens or hundreds of independent two-body matrix elements. Previous studies have shown that when fitting to experimental low-lying spectra, the greatest sensitivity is to only a few linear combinations of matrix elements. Here we consider interactions drawn from the two-body random ensemble, or TBRE, and find that the low-lying spectra are also most sensitive to only a few linear combinations of two-body matrix elements, in a fashion nearly indistinguishable from an interaction empirically fit to data. We find in particular the spectra for both the random and empirical interactions are sensitive to similar matrix elements, which we analyze using monopole and contact interactions.

## Key claim

Low-lying spectra from random two-body interactions (TBRE) are sensitive to only a few linear combinations of two-body matrix elements, in a pattern nearly indistinguishable from the empirically-fit USDB interaction, at least on a statistical level.

## Method

The authors performed a sensitivity analysis using singular value decomposition (SVD) of the matrix **A** (Eq. 4), whose elements are sums over states of products of energy derivatives with respect to two-body matrix elements. They computed eigenvalues and eigenvectors of **A** for both the USDB interaction and an ensemble of 100 random two-body interactions (TBRE) in the 1s-0d valence space with an inert 16O core, considering ground-state binding energies and the first five excitation energies across all nuclides with 0 ≤ Z_valence ≤ N_valence ≤ 10. They then quantified subspace overlaps between the dominant eigenvectors and subspaces defined by monopole-monopole interactions (six terms) and contact interactions (two terms).

## Key numbers

- The leading eigenvalue for the overlap of USDB versus the six-term monopole interaction is 0.94, while that of the TBRE versus monopole is 0.66 ± 0.03.
- The leading eigenvalue for the overlap of USDB versus the two-term contact interaction is 0.60 for ground states, 0.58 for excited states, and 0.62 for all states; for TBRE the corresponding values are 0.55 ± 0.04, 0.41 ± 0.06, and 0.55 ± 0.04.
- The leading eigenvalue for the overlap of dominant eigenvectors from USDB with those from TBRE is 0.72 ± 0.06.

## Context

This work extends previous sensitivity analyses of empirical shell-model fits (Wildenthal; Brown and Richter 2006) and a study by Bogner and Johnson (2009) that found empirical fits dominated by a few linear combinations of matrix elements. It also connects to the broader TBRE literature (Johnson et al. 1998; Zelevinsky and Volya 2004; Zhao et al. 2004; Papenbrock and Weidenmueller 2007) showing that random interactions reproduce some features of real nuclear spectra. The results enable future work to understand whether the dominant linear combinations in empirical fits arise from generic properties of the many-body problem rather than specific features of the nuclear force.
