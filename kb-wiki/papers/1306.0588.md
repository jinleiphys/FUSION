# Medium induced Lorentz symmetry breaking effects in nonlocal PNJL models

> In this paper we detail the thermodynamics of two flavor nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models for different parametrizations of the quark interaction regulators. The structure of the model is upgraded in order to allow for terms in the quark selfenergy which violate Lorentz invariance due to the presence of the medium. We examine the critical properties, the phase diagram as well as the equation of state. Furthermore, some aspects of the Mott effect for pions and sigma mesons are discussed explicitly within a nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In particular, we continued the meson polarization function in the complex energy plane and under certain approximations, we were able to extract the imaginary part as a function of the meson energy. We were not able to calculate the dynamical meson mass, and therefore resorted to a technical study of the temperature dependence of the meson width by replacing the meson energy with the temperature dependent spatial meson mass. Our results show that while the temperature behavior of the meson widths is qualitatively the same for a wide class of covariant regulators, the special case where the nonlocal interactions are introduced via the instanton liquid model singles out with a drastically different behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while medium-induced Lorentz symmetry breaking (LSB) effects in nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nl-PNJL) models cause a significant splitting of the quark wave function renormalization components \(\sigma_A\) and \(\sigma_C\) around and above the chiral pseudocritical temperature, the bulk thermodynamic properties, the phase diagram, and the critical end point (CEP) remain practically unaffected by LSB.

## Method

The authors extend two-flavor nl-PNJL models to include separate scalar and vector quark selfenergy channels that break O(4) symmetry to O(3), introducing distinct dressing functions \(A(p^2)\), \(C(p^2)\), and \(B(p^2)\) in the quark propagator. They compute the thermodynamic potential at mean-field level with a Polyakov loop potential, solve the gap equations for three regulator parametrizations (sets A, B, C) and the instanton liquid model (ILM) inspired form, and then go beyond mean field by evaluating the meson polarization function in the complex energy plane to extract the imaginary part and study the Mott effect for pions and sigma mesons.

## Key numbers

- Set B parameters: \(m = 5.7\) MeV, \(\Lambda_0 = 0.814\) GeV, \(G_S \Lambda_0^2 = 32.03\), \(\Lambda_1 = 1.034\) GeV, \(\kappa_p = 4.180\) GeV.
- Set C parameters: \(m = 2.37\) MeV, \(\Lambda_0 = 0.850\) GeV, \(G_S \Lambda_0^2 = 20.818\), \(\Lambda_1 = 1.400\) GeV, \(\kappa_p = 6.034\) GeV.
- ILM parameters: \(m = 5.8\) MeV, \(\Lambda_0 = 0.902\) GeV, \(G_S \Lambda_0^2 = 15.82\).
- Polyakov loop potential parameters: \(a_0 = 3.51\), \(a_1 = -2.47\), \(a_2 = 15.22\), \(b_3 = -1.75\), with \(T_0 = 0.27\) GeV.
- Analytic estimate for the CEP temperature with LSB: \(T_{\mathrm{CEP}}^{\mathrm{LSB}} \simeq 0.37 \, v_{\mathrm{qp}}^{1/2} \, T_c^{\mathrm{LS}}(0)\), where \(v_{\mathrm{qp}} = A_0/C_0\).
- The curvature of the critical line \(\kappa = 3/\pi^2\) is unchanged by WFR or LSB in the local NJL limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlocal PNJL models by Contrera et al. (2010) and Hell et al. (2011) by incorporating medium-induced Lorentz symmetry breaking in the quark propagator, an effect well known from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies at finite temperature by Bender et al. (1996) and Blaschke et al. (1998). The results enable future investigations of mesonic correlations and the Mott effect in a framework that can be compared with lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (Borsanyi et al., 2012), and they highlight a surprising sensitivity of meson widths to the specific form of nonlocal interactions, with the ILM-inspired regulator yielding monotonically increasing widths while other covariant regulators produce widths that drop to zero at high temperature.
