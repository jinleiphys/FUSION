# Chiral Partners and their Electromagnetic Radiation -- Ingredients for a systematic in-medium calculation

> It is argued that the chiral partners of the lowest-lying hadrons are hadronic molecules and not three-quark or quark-antiquark states, respectively. As an example the case of a_1 as the chiral partner of the rho is discussed. Deconfinement -- or as a precursor large in-medium widths for hadronic states -- is proposed as a natural way to accommodate for the fact that at chiral restoration the respective in-medium spectra of chiral partners must become degenerate. Ingredients for a systematic and self-consistent in-medium calculation are presented with special emphasis on vector-meson dominance which emerges from a recently proposed systematic counting scheme for the mesonic sector including pseudoscalar and vector mesons as active degrees of freedom.

# Chiral Partners and their Electromagnetic Radiation, Ingredients for a systematic in-medium calculation

## Key claim

The chiral partners of the lowest-lying hadrons (LLH states) are not elementary quark-antiquark or three-quark states, but rather hadronic molecules dynamically generated from coupled-channel scattering of LLH states on Goldstone bosons; the paper argues that deconfinement, or at least large in-medium widths, provides a natural mechanism for the degeneracy of chiral partner spectra at chiral restoration, but this is presented as a plausible scenario rather than a proven result, with the caveat that the in-medium calculation shown is a simple model intended as a precursor to more serious calculations.

## Method

The authors study the scattering of a Goldstone boson on an LLH state (e.g., pion on rho meson) using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the Weinberg-Tomozawa point interaction as the kernel, whose strength is fixed model-independently by the pion decay constant. The renormalization point for the loop in the Bethe-Salpeter equation is fixed by requiring approximate crossing symmetry, leaving only one free parameter (the renormalization point of the entrance loop for the rescattering process) when comparing to tau decay data for the axial-vector spectrum. For the in-medium study, the authors introduce a simple model where the rho meson width is increased by a constant (50 or 100 MeV) and observe the effect on the dynamically generated a1 resonance.

## Key numbers

- The predicted width for the decay ω → 3π is Γ = 7.3 MeV, compared to the experimental value Γ_exp = (7.57 ± 0.13) MeV.
- The rho meson width is increased by 50 MeV or 100 MeV in the simple in-medium model to study the melting of the a1 resonance.
- The interaction strength for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel is fixed ∼ F_π^{-2}, where F_π is the pion decay constant.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation approach previously applied to the N*(1535) as the chiral partner of the nucleon (Kaiser and follow-up works), the sigma meson as the chiral partner of the pion (Oller and Oset), and the Delta*(1700) and N*(1520) as chiral partners of the Delta decuplet (Lutz). The paper builds on the framework of Lutz and Leupold for a systematic counting scheme in the mesonic sector including pseudoscalar and vector mesons as active degrees of freedom, and proposes that self-consistent in-medium calculations using antisymmetric tensor fields for vector mesons (as suggested by Leupold) can test the melting scenario for chiral restoration. The work enables future calculations of in-medium dilepton production and tests of whether vector-meson dominance holds for dynamically generated states.
