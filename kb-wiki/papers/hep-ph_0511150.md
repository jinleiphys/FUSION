# The $J^P=1^+$ $ud\bar s\bar s$ Tetraquark

> Using the color-magnetic interaction Hamiltonian with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking, we perform a schematic study of the masses of the $J^P=1^+$ tetraquarks in the anti-decuplet representation. After diagonalizing the mass matrix, we find the $ud\bar s\bar s$ tetraquark lies around 1347 MeV. It decays into $K^+K^0ŽĆ^0, K^+K^+ŽĆ^-, K^0K^0ŽĆ^+$ via P-wave. The dual suppression from the not-so-big three-body phase space and P-wave decay barrier may render this exotic state rather narrow. Future experimental exclusion of this state will cast doubt on the validity of applying the simple color-magnetic Hamiltonian to the multiquark system.

# The $J^P=1^+$ $ud\bar s\bar s$ Tetraquark

## Key claim
Using a color-magnetic interaction Hamiltonian with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking, the authors find the $ud\bar s\bar s$ tetraquark lies around 1347 MeV and may be rather narrow due to dual suppression from limited three-body phase space and a P-wave decay barrier. However, the authors explicitly state that future experimental exclusion of this state would cast doubt on the validity of applying the simple color-magnetic Hamiltonian to multiquark systems, indicating the prediction is conditional on the model's applicability.

## Method
The authors constructed wave functions for $J^P=1^+$ tetraquarks in the anti-decuplet representation using diquark building blocks, then calculated the color-magnetic interaction energy with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking corrections where $v_{ij}=v\,m_u^2/(m_i m_j)$. They diagonalized a $6\times 6$ mass matrix to obtain physical mass eigenstates, extracting the parameter $v$ from the experimental mass of the $a_0(980)$ meson under Jaffe's working assumption that scalar mesons below 1 GeV are tetraquarks.

## Key numbers
- $ud\bar s\bar s$ tetraquark mass: 1347 MeV
- Constituent quark masses extracted from meson inputs: $m_u = 308$ MeV, $m_s = 486$ MeV
- Color-magnetic interaction parameter for tetraquark system: $v \approx 21.1$ MeV (extracted from $a_0(980)$ mass)
- Color-magnetic interaction parameter for $q\bar q$ meson system: $v' = 29.9$ MeV
- $K^\ast + K$ threshold: 1386 MeV, which is above the predicted tetraquark mass of 1347 MeV, forbidding $K^\ast K$ decay
- Predicted masses for scalar tetraquarks under the same scheme: $m_\kappa = 663$ MeV, $m_{f_0(980)} = 980$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 316$ MeV

## Context
This work extends Jaffe's tetraquark scheme for scalar mesons (Jaffe, 1977) to the $J^P=1^+$ sector, and is motivated by the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ reported by the LEPS collaboration. It contrasts with the flux-tube model calculation by Kanada-En'yo, Morimatsu, and Nishikawa (2005), who argued the scalar tetraquark with double strangeness does not exist but found a stable $J^P=1^+$ $ud\bar s\bar s$ around 1.4 GeV with a width of 20-50 MeV decaying via S-wave to $K^\ast\pi$. The present work predicts a narrower state because the $K^\ast K$ channel is kinematically forbidden and the allowed three-body decays proceed through P-wave. The existence of this state is presented as a testable prediction of the color-magnetic interaction model for multiquark systems.
