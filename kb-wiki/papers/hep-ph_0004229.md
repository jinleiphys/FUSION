# Chiral aspects of baryon structure in the quark model

> The implications of chiral symmetry for the quark model are discussed. In particular its connection with the meson-baryon approach is outlined. It is stressed that in the closure approximation, where the intermediate baryon states are used instead of the original quark basis, it is imperative to take into account the whole infinite tower of the radially excited states in order that the crucially important effects related to the short-range part of the meson-exchange interaction be preserved. It is shown that the chiral constituent quark model is able to explain the nucleon axial vector coupling constant and automatically incorporates all the necessary loop corrections.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model can explain the nucleon axial vector coupling constant and automatically incorporates all necessary loop corrections, but only if the full infinite tower of radially excited baryon states is included in the closure approximation; truncation to only ground-state nucleon and delta intermediate states, as done by Thomas and Krein (1999, 2000), leads to incorrect results for the short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction.

## Method

The paper analyzes the Hamiltonian of a chiral constituent quark model, which includes an effective confining interaction and a short-range Goldstone boson exchange interaction between constituent quarks. It compares two approaches: direct treatment of the quark-level pion-exchange diagrams (Fig. 1a and 1b) versus the closure approximation using intermediate baryon-pion states (Eq. CL). The author demonstrates that the closure representation requires the complete infinite set of radially excited baryon states to correctly reproduce the delta-function-like short-range part of the interaction, and shows that truncation to only ground-state N and Δ states yields dramatically different mass formulas. The nucleon axial vector coupling constant is computed using the SU(6) relation g_A = 5/3 g_A^q combined with relativistic motion corrections.

## Key numbers

- The leading nonanalytic contribution to the nucleon mass from baryon chiral perturbation theory is δm_N = -3/(32π f_π²) g_A² μ³ ≈ -15 MeV, where μ is the pion mass near the chiral limit.
- The constituent quark self-energy contribution to the nucleon mass is δm_N = 3 × {-3/(32π f_π²) (g_A^q)² μ³}.
- The SU(6) relation g_A = 5/3 g_A^q gives g_A = 1.3 to 1.2 when combined with a relativistic motion correction factor of 0.8 to 0.7, assuming constituent quark velocity squared v² ≈ 0.5 to 0.7.
- The correct nucleon self-energy in the chiral limit is given by -15 P_{00} (from Eq. MN), with P_{00}^π ∼ 30 MeV.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV.

## Context

This paper extends the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska (1996) and responds to criticisms by Thomas and Krein (1999, 2000) who argued that the pion-exchange interaction in the quark model conflicts with baryon chiral perturbation theory. The author shows that the apparent contradiction arises from truncating the intermediate baryon states to only ground-state N and Δ, whereas the full infinite tower of radially excited states is required to preserve the short-range part of the interaction. The work also connects to large N_c analyses by Dashen and Manohar (1993) and to lattice results by Liu et al. (1999) on the N-Δ splitting. The paper enables a consistent framework that simultaneously respects chiral perturbation theory, large N_c counting, and the quark model description of baryon structure.
