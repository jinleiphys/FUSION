# Hadrons in Medium

> After a short motivation I outline a consistent treatment of hadronic spectral functions based on transport theory. As examples I discuss nucleon spectral functions and the observable effects of changes of the properties of vector mesons inside nuclei.

## Key claim

Transport theory can provide a consistent treatment of hadronic spectral functions in medium, and photonuclear reactions on normal nuclei can yield observable in-medium effects that are as large as those in heavy-ion collisions, despite probing densities at or below equilibrium nuclear density \(\rho_0\).

## Method

The author separates photonuclear reactions into three steps: determining shadowing for the incoming photon via a Glauber multiple scattering model, producing the primary particle (using a nucleon resonance model for invariant mass \(W < 2\) GeV and FRITIOF or PYTHIA for \(W > 2\) GeV), and propagating the produced particles through the nuclear medium using a semiclassical coupled-channel transport (BUU) equation. This transport approach propagates spectral phase space distributions of all particles, allowing production and transport of hadrons with their correct off-shell spectral functions, and includes elastic and inelastic collisions, particle production, and absorption.

## Key numbers

* The low-density expansion for selfenergies is badly violated for the \(\rho\) and \(\pi\) mesons already at densities less than \(0.5 \rho_0\), while it is a reasonable approximation for the \(\eta\) meson.
* In the NJL model, the chiral condensate drops with baryon density and temperature; at very low temperatures the only feasible method to explore this is at density at most \(\rho_0\) (equilibrium density of nuclear matter).
* For the \(\omega\) meson, theoretical predictions for its mass in nuclear matter at rest range from 640 to 765 MeV, and its width up to 50 MeV.
* A calculation for \(\omega\) photoproduction off \(^{93}\)Nb shows good agreement with TAPS/CB@ELSA data for a photon escape probability of 5% and a mass shift \(m_\omega = m_\omega^0 - 0.18 \,\rho/\rho_0\).
* In the CERES experiment at 40 AGeV, the dilepton excess in the invariant mass range from about 300 MeV to about 700 MeV is explained by in-medium modified spectral functions of vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the Delta-hole model of Ericson and Weise (Ericsson-Weise) for pions and the Delta-resonance, and the QCD sum rule approach of Leupold et al. (Leupold, LeupoldMosel) and Kaempfer et al. (Kaempfer). It provides a baseline for interpreting in-medium effects in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (such as those studied by the CERES experiment) by using photonuclear reactions on nuclei, which proceed closer to equilibrium. The method enables future experiments like the g7 experiment at JLAB (Weygand) and the planned CBM experiment at FAIR (FAIR) to extract in-medium spectral functions and potentially determine the density dependence of the chiral condensate.
