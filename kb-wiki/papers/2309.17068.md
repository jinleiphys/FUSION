# Doubly heavy tetraquark states in the constituent quark model using diffusion Monte Carlo method

> We use the diffusion Monte Carlo method to calculate the doubly heavy tetraquark $T_{cc}$ system in two kinds of constituent quark models, the pure constituent quark model AL1/AP1 and the chiral constituent quark model. When the discrete configurations are complete and no spatial clustering is preseted, the AL1/AP1 model gives an energy of $T_{cc}$ close to the $DD^*$ threshold, and the chiral constituent quark model yields a deeply bound state. We further calculate all doubly heavy tetraquark systems with $J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+$, and provide the binding energies of systems with bound states. The $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$ $bc\bar{n}\bar{n}$, $0(1^+)$ $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$, $0(1^+)$ $bc\bar{n}\bar{n}$, $\frac{1}{2}(1^+)$ $bb\bar{s}\bar{n}$ systems have bound states in all three models. Since the DMC method has almost no restriction on the spatial part, the resulting bound states have greater binding energies than those obtained in previous works.

## Key claim

The diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) method, which imposes almost no restriction on the spatial wave function, yields greater binding energies for doubly heavy tetraquark states than previous variational approaches that preselect clustering structures; the chiral constituent quark model gives a deeply bound \(T_{cc}\) state with a binding energy of \(-156\) MeV, while the pure constituent quark models AL1/AP1 give energies very close to the \(DD^*\) threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation for doubly heavy tetraquark systems using the diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) method with importance sampling, employing a trial wave function of the form \(\psi_T(\boldsymbol{R})=\prod_{i<j} e^{-a_{ij}r_{ij}}\). They used two types of non-relativistic constituent quark models: the pure constituent quark models AL1 and AP1 (containing one-gluon-exchange and confinement), and the chiral constituent quark model SLM (which additionally includes one-boson-exchange interactions between light quarks). The calculation included all spin-color configurations for \(J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+\) without presuming either diquark-antidiquark or dimeson clustering.

## Key numbers

- The \((I)J^P=(0)1^+\) \(cc\bar{n}\bar{n}\) system (the \(T_{cc}\) candidate) has a binding energy of 0 MeV in the AL1 model and \(-1\) MeV in the AP1 model, with statistical uncertainty less than 1 MeV.
- The same system in the chiral constituent quark model (SLM) has a binding energy of \(-156\) MeV.
- The \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\) \(bb\bar{n}\bar{n}\) system is bound in all three models, with binding energies of \(-113\) MeV (AL1), \(-119\) MeV (AP1), and \(-220\) MeV (SLM).
- The \(I(J^P)=0(0^+)\) \(bc\bar{n}\bar{n}\) system is bound in all three models, with binding energies of \(-6\) MeV (AL1), \(-8\) MeV (AP1), and \(-33\) MeV (SLM).
- The \(I(J^P)=\frac{1}{2}(1^+)\) \(bb\bar{s}\bar{n}\) system is bound in all three models, with binding energies of \(-61\) MeV (AL1), \(-65\) MeV (AP1), and \(-143\) MeV (SLM).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks using constituent quark models, where results depended strongly on whether the diquark-antidiquark or dimeson structure was assumed, as seen in Yang (2009) and Deng (2022). The DMC method resolves the discrepancy between these clustering assumptions by allowing a fully general spatial wave function, producing deeper binding energies than variational methods such as the Gaussian expansion method used by Meng (2020) or the Resonating Group Method used by Ortega (2022). The results enable more reliable predictions for the existence and properties of other doubly heavy tetraquark states, including those with \(bc\) and \(bb\) content, which may be searched for experimentally.
