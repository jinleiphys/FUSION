# Role of fluctuations on the pairing properties of nuclei in the random spacing model

> The influence of thermal fluctuations on fermion pairing is investigated using a semiclassical treatment of fluctuations. When the average pairing gaps along with those differing by one standard deviation are used, the characteristic discontinuity of the specific heat at the critical temperature $T_c$ in the BCS formalism with its most probable gap becomes smooth. This indicates the suppression of a second order phase transition as experimentally observed in nano-particles and several nuclei. Illustrative calculations using the constant spacing model and the recently introduced random spacing model are presented.

## Key claim

The characteristic discontinuity of the specific heat at the critical temperature predicted by mean-field BCS theory is smoothed into a shoulder-like or S-shaped curve when thermal fluctuations are included via a semiclassical treatment using the average pairing gap and gaps differing by one standard deviation, indicating the suppression of a second-order phase transition in small systems such as nuclei and nanoparticles.

## Method

The authors employ a semiclassical treatment of thermal fluctuations based on the probability distribution of the pairing gap P(Δ) ∝ exp[-Ω(T,Δ)/T], where Ω is the grand potential, and use the condition that the gap equation term G is nonzero for Δ ≠ Δ_mp. They calculate thermodynamic quantities using average values ⟨Õ⟩ = Σ Õ P(Δ) / Σ P(Δ) and compare results from the constant spacing (CS) model with those from the random spacing (RS) model, where single-particle energy levels are randomly distributed around the Fermi energy to mimic realistic nuclear level schemes.

## Key numbers

- For the CS model calculations: g = 5 MeV⁻¹, N = 144, Δ(0) = 1 MeV at T = 0, G = 0.0581 MeV, ħω ≈ 41 N^(-1/3) = 7.78 MeV, with levels uniformly distributed between ±2ħω around λ_mp(0) = -1.3471 MeV at T = 0.
- The critical temperature for the CS model is T_c ≈ 0.57 MeV, above which the probability distribution P(Δ) is peaked at Δ = 0.
- For the RS model, single-particle levels are randomly distributed within a window of 2ħω around the Fermi level for N = 144, with each level endowed with degeneracy d = 2j+1.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of pairing fluctuations first applied to nuclei by Moretto (1970) and builds on Anderson's (1959) observation that fluctuations suppress pairing in small systems. The random spacing model was recently introduced by the same authors (Mamun et al., 2018) to mimic the variation in single-particle level schemes obtained from different energy density functionals. The results enable sensitivity tests for pairing properties in astrophysical settings that harbor exotic nuclei, and the authors note that calculations of level densities and spin distributions including fluctuations in the RS model are in progress.
