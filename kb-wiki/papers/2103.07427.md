# Cold quark matter at N3LO: soft contributions

> High-order perturbative calculations for thermodynamic quantities in QCD are complicated by the physics of dynamical screening that affects the soft, long-wavelength modes of the system. Here, we provide details for the evaluation of this soft contribution to the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) pressure of high-density, zero-temperature quark matter (QM), complementing our accompanying paper in arXiv:2103.05658. Our calculation requires the determination of the pressure of the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective theory to full two-loop order at zero temperature, which we go through in considerable detail. In addition to this, we comprehensively discuss the structure of the weak-coupling expansion of the QM pressure, and lay out a roadmap towards the evaluation of the contributions missing from a full N3LO result for this quantity.

## Key claim

The paper provides the technical details for evaluating the soft contribution to the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) pressure of high-density, zero-temperature quark matter, complementing the accompanying letter (Gorda 2021). The calculation requires determining the pressure of the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective theory to full two-loop order at zero temperature, but the authors note that this represents only part of the full N3LO result, with additional contributions still missing.

## Method

The authors separate the pressure contributions into hard modes (computed via naive loop expansion in full QCD), soft modes (determined within the HTL effective theory), and mixed contributions from interactions between soft and hard modes. They classify diagrams by the loop order of the resulting HTL diagram after taking fully soft kinematics, using dimensional regularization to regulate both ultraviolet divergences from the full theory and intermediate divergences from different kinematic regions. The soft contributions are evaluated by computing the pressure of the HTL effective theory to two-loop order at zero temperature, where interactions between soft gluons remain perturbative and can be treated with a loop expansion within the HTL theory.

## Key numbers

* The effective mass scale for a single massless quark flavor is m_E^2 = (2/pi) alpha_s mu_q^2, where mu_q is the quark chemical potential.
* Soft gluons are phase-space suppressed by the integration measure int^{m_E} d^4 P -> m_E^4 ~ alpha_s^2 mu_q^4, meaning soft contributions first enter at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO).
* The leading logarithm at N3LO, containing factors of ln(alpha_s), was computed previously in Gorda (2018).
* The free Fermi pressure of the system scales as mu_q^4 for massless quarks.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of the cold quark matter equation of state beyond the N2LO results of Freedman and McLerran (1976), where screening effects were addressed through an all-loop-order diagrammatic resummation. The authors note that dimensional reduction is unavailable at low temperatures, unlike the high-temperature case where Kajantie et al. (2002, 2003) used electrostatic QCD and magnetostatic QCD to reach partial N3LO accuracy. The present calculation enables progress toward a full N3LO result for the cold quark matter pressure, with the authors laying out a roadmap for evaluating the remaining missing contributions.
