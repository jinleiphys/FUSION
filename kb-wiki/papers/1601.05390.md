# Origin of the mass splitting of elliptic anisotropy in a multiphase transport model

> The mass splitting of elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$) at low transverse momentum is considered as a hallmark of hydrodynamic collective flow. We investigate a multiphase transport (AMPT) model where the $v_2$ is mainly generated by an anisotropic escape mechanism, not of the hydrodynamic flow nature, and where mass splitting is also observed. We demonstrate that the $v_2$ mass splitting in AMPT is small right after hadronization (especially when resonance decays are included); the mass splitting mainly comes from hadronic rescatterings, even though their contribution to the overall charged hadron $v_2$ is small. These findings are qualitatively the same as those from hybrid models that combine hydrodynamics with a hadron cascade. We further show that there is no qualitative difference between heavy ion collisions and small system collisions. Our results indicate that the $v_2$ mass splitting is not a unique signature of hydrodynamic collective flow and thus cannot distinguish whether the elliptic flow is generated mainly from hydrodynamics or the anisotropic parton escape.

## Key claim

The mass splitting of elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$) at low transverse momentum is not a unique signature of hydrodynamic collective flow; it can also arise from an anisotropic escape mechanism in a multiphase transport model, and thus cannot distinguish whether elliptic flow is generated mainly from hydrodynamics or anisotropic parton escape.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model (v2.26t5) with Debye screened parton scattering cross sections (σ=3 mb, α_s=0.33, μ_D=2.265/fm), quark coalescence hadronization, and a hadron cascade including resonances. They simulated Au+Au collisions at b=6.6-8.1 fm (20-30% centrality) and d+Au collisions at b=0 fm, both at √s_NN=200 GeV, and analyzed v_2 at different evolution stages: parton final state before hadronization, primordial hadrons right after coalescence, hadrons including resonance decays but before rescatterings, and final freeze-out hadrons after hadronic rescatterings.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross section: σ=3 mb, with Debye screening mass μ_D=2.265/fm and strong coupling α_s=0.33
- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN=200 GeV, impact parameter b=6.6-8.1 fm (20-30% centrality)
- d+Au collisions at b=0 fm at √s_NN=200 GeV
- Hadronic interaction cutoff time: 30 fm/c
- v_2 analysis performed for particles within pseudo-rapidity window |η|<1
- Mass splitting examined in transverse momentum range 0.7 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies showing that azimuthal anisotropy in AMPT is mainly generated by anisotropic parton escape (He 2015, Lin 2015) rather than hydrodynamic flow. The findings are qualitatively the same as those from hybrid models that combine hydrodynamics with a hadron cascade (Hirano 2007, Song 2010, Romatschke 2015), which also found that v_2 mass splitting is small right after hadronization and strongly enhanced by hadronic rescatterings. The results contradict the common interpretation that mass splitting uniquely signals hydrodynamic collective flow, and suggest that both heavy ion and small system collisions can produce similar v_2 mass splitting through non-hydrodynamic mechanisms.
