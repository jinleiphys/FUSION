# Influence of Finite-Nuclei Constraints on High-Density Transitions and Neutron Star Properties

> We construct posterior distributions of the equation of state (EoS) for matter beyond the inner crust of neutron stars by incorporating finite nuclei (FN) constraints within relativistic mean field models. These constraints are implemented in three complementary ways: (i) through theoretical bounds on the EoS, (ii) implicitly via nuclear matter parameters, and (iii) explicitly by enforcing consistency with experimental binding energies and charge radii of selected nuclei. The resulting low-density nucleonic EoSs are subsequently matched to a model-agnostic speed-of-sound parametrization, constrained by astrophysical observations, including NICER mass-radius measurements, tidal deformability limits from GW170817, and lower bounds on the maximum neutron-star mass inferred from radio pulsar observations. We find that the admissible range of the transition density is strongly sensitive to the choice of the low-density EoS. In particular, the inclusion of explicit FN constraints significantly reduces the allowed parameter space of the nucleonic EoS at low densities, narrowing the transition-density range by nearly a factor of two. Consequently, neutron-star properties inferred from EoSs with explicit FN constraints differ substantially, with especially pronounced effects for low-mass neutron stars and their correlations with nuclear matter parameters. A quantitative comparison, using metrics based on Mahalanobis distance, shows consistency of the explicit constraints with PSRs J0740+6620, J0030+0451, and J0437-4715, but suggest a possible tension with PSR J0614-3329. These findings underscore the critical importance of a consistent treatment of finite-nuclei properties for reliably inferring the behavior of high-density matter and the presence of possible phase transitions from astrophysical observations.

## Key claim
The inclusion of explicit finite-nuclei constraints (binding energies and charge radii of selected nuclei) narrows the admissible range of the transition density between nucleonic and high-density matter by nearly a factor of two compared to using only theoretical or implicit constraints, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of low-density equation of state and the Bayesian framework employed.

## Method
The authors constructed posterior distributions of the equation of state (EoS) for neutron star matter using relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with three distinct constraint sets: theoretical (chiral EFT and perturbative QCD bounds), implicit (ranges of nuclear matter parameters from nuclear mass analyses and heavy-ion collision data), and explicit (direct fits to experimental binding energies and charge radii of 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb). The low-density nucleonic EoS was matched to a model-agnostic speed-of-sound parametrization beyond a free transition density, and the full EoS was constrained by NICER mass-radius measurements, tidal deformability from GW170817, and lower bounds on maximum neutron star mass from radio pulsar observations.

## Key numbers
- Transition density under explicit constraints: 1.92 (+2.67, -0.92) times the reference density (0.16 fm^-3), with a width ratio of 0.40 compared to 0.98 for theoretical and 0.91 for implicit constraints
- Binding energy of 208Pb under explicit constraints: -1638.17 (+7.20, -7.38) MeV, compared to experimental value of -1636.43 MeV
- Charge radius of 208Pb under explicit constraints: 5.517 (+0.016, -0.014) fm, compared to experimental value of 5.50 fm
- Effective mass ratio m*/m under explicit constraints: 0.616 (+0.048, -0.024), with a width ratio of 0.24 compared to 0.90 for theoretical constraints
- Saturation density under explicit constraints: 0.1519 (+0.0034, -0.0077) fm^-3, with a width ratio of 0.37 compared to 0.97 for theoretical constraints
- Incompressibility K0 under explicit constraints: 220.2 (+39.5, -26.4) MeV, with a width ratio of 0.44 compared to 0.91 for theoretical constraints

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Venneti 2024) demonstrating that explicit finite-nuclei constraints significantly restrict the low-density EoS and affect neutron star properties, particularly for low-mass stars. It contrasts with earlier studies by Saha (2025), Li (2025), and Huang (2025) that used NS observations to infer transition densities but often reached conflicting conclusions due to differing modeling assumptions. The results enable more reliable inference of high-density matter behavior and possible phase transitions by showing that consistent treatment of finite-nuclei properties is critical, and they suggest a possible tension with the NICER observations of PSR J0614-3329 based on Mahalanobis distance metrics.
