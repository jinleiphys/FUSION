# Cone size dependence of jet suppression in heavy-ion collisions

> The strong suppression of high-$p_T$ jets in heavy ion collisions is a result of elastic and inelastic energy loss suffered by the jet multi-prong collection of color charges that are resolved by medium interactions. Hence, quenching effects depend on the fluctuations of the jet substructure that are probed by the cone size dependence of the spectrum. In this letter, we present the first complete, analytic calculation of the inclusive $R$-dependent jet spectrum in PbPb collisions at LHC energies, including resummation of energy loss effects from hard, vacuum-like emissions occurring in the medium and modeling of soft energy flow and recovery at the jet cone. Both the geometry of the collision and the local medium properties, such as the temperature and fluid velocity, are given by a hydrodynamic evolution of the medium, leaving only the coupling constant in the medium as a free parameter. The calculation yields a good description of the centrality and $p_T$ dependence of jet suppression for $R=0.4$ together with a mild cone size dependence, which is in agreement with recent experimental results. Gauging the theoretical uncertainties, we find that the largest sensitivity resides in the leading logarithmic approximation of the phase space of resolved splittings, which can be improved systematically, while non-perturbative modeling of the soft-gluon sector is of relatively minor importance up to large cone sizes.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete analytic calculation of the inclusive jet spectrum as a function of cone size \(R\) in PbPb collisions at LHC energies, finding that resummation of energy loss from vacuum-like emissions inside the medium yields a very mild cone-size dependence of jet suppression, with the largest theoretical uncertainty residing in the leading logarithmic approximation of resolved splitting phase space rather than in non-perturbative soft-gluon modeling.

## Method

The authors compute the single-inclusive jet cross section in nucleus-nucleus collisions by convolving the vacuum jet fragmentation function (resummed to leading logarithmic accuracy for small \(R\)) with flavor-dependent quenching factors that account for medium-induced energy loss. The quenching factors are obtained by solving a non-linear evolution equation that resums contributions from multiple resolved color charges generated by early collinear splittings, using the condition \(\Theta_\text{res}(p,R) = \Theta(t_\text{form} < t_\text{decoh} < L)\) to determine which splittings are resolved by the medium. The medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum is computed using the improved opacity expansion (IOE) that unifies the BDMPS and GLV/higher-twist formalisms, and the medium geometry and local properties (temperature, fluid velocity) are provided by a hydrodynamic evolution, leaving only the medium coupling constant \(g_\text{med}\) as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

* For central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) ATeV, the calculation yields a good description of the centrality and \(p_T\) dependence of jet suppression for cone size \(R=0.4\), compared to ATLAS data (Aaboud 2018).
* The critical angle for medium resolution of splittings is \(\theta_c = (\hat q_0 L^3/12)^{-1/2}\), where \(\hat q_0\) is the bare transport coefficient.
* The Debye mass in the thermal medium is \(m_D^2 = 3 g_\text{med}^2 T^2/2\) for three active quark flavors.
* The effective transport coefficient is \(\hat q = \hat q_0 \ln(Q^2/\mu_\ast^2)\), with \(\mu_\ast^2 = m_D^2 \exp[-2 + 2\gamma_E]/4\).
* The running coupling is regularized as \(\alpha_s(k) = \min[1, 2\pi/(\beta_0 \log k/Q_0)]\) with \(\beta_0 = 23/3\) and \(Q_0 = 0.09\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the factorized picture of in-medium jet evolution developed by Mehtar-Tani (2017) and Caucal (2018), which separates vacuum-like emissions at short formation times from medium-induced energy loss, and applies it to the cone-size dependence of inclusive jet suppression. The calculation enables systematic improvement of the leading logarithmic approximation for resolved splittings and provides a controlled weak-coupling framework to probe the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, in contrast to Monte Carlo event generators (e.g., Lokhtin 2005, Zapp 2008, Armesto 2009) that rely on modeling of quantum coherence effects.
