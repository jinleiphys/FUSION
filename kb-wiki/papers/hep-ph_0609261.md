# Properties of the light scalar mesons face experimental data

> The following topics are considered. 1) Confinement, chiral dynamics, and light scalar mesons. 2) Chiral shielding of the σ(600) 3)The ϕmeson radiative decays about nature of light scalar resonances. 4) The J/ψdecays about nature of light scalar resonances. 5) The a0(980)\toγγand f0(980)\toγγdecays about nature of light scalar resonances. 6) New round in γγ\toπ^+π^-, the Belle data. 7) The a0(980)-f0(980) mixing: theory and experiment. Arguments in favor of the four-quark model of the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the light scalar mesons (σ(600), κ(700–900), f₀(980), a₀(980)) are not conventional quark-antiquark (q\bar q) P-wave states but instead have a four-quark (q²\bar q²) structure, with the caveat that the chiral shielding mechanism hides the σ(600) and κ(700–900) mesons from direct observation in scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data on radiative φ decays (φ→γπ⁰η, φ→γπ⁰π⁰), J/ψ decays, γγ collisions (γγ→π⁺π⁻), and a₀(980)–f₀(980) mixing, comparing them to predictions of the four-quark model and the K⁺K⁻ loop mechanism. The analysis uses a one-loop model φ→K⁺K⁻→γa₀(980) or γf₀(980) to describe the production of scalar mesons in φ radiative decays, and applies the linear σ model with unitarized amplitudes to account for chiral shielding of the σ(600).

## Key numbers

- BR(φ→γπ⁰η) = (0.83 ± 0.05)·10⁻⁴
- BR(φ→γπ⁰π⁰) = (1.09 ± 0.06)·10⁻⁴
- B(J/ψ→a₀(980)ρ) < 4.4·10⁻⁴ and B(J/ψ→a₂(1320)ρ) = (109 ± 22)·10⁻⁴, giving a ratio B(J/ψ→a₀(980)ρ)/B(J/ψ→a₂(1320)ρ) < 0.04 ± 0.008
- B(J/ψ→γf₀(980)→γππ) < 1.4·10⁻⁵, compared to expected B(J/ψ→γf₂(1270))/4 = (3.45 ± 0.35)·10⁻⁴ for a q\bar q state
- Γ(a₀(980)→γγ) predicted in the four-quark model as ~0.27 keV; experimental values: (0.19 ± 0.07 ⁺⁰·¹₋₀·₀₇)/B(a₀→πη) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.28 ± 0.04 ± 0.1)/B(a₀→πη) keV (JADE)
- Γ(f₀(980)→γγ) predicted in the four-quark model as ~0.27 keV; experimental values: (0.31 ± 0.14 ± 0.09) keV (Crystal Ball) and (0.24 ± 0.06 ± 0.15) keV (MARK II)
- For the a₀(980)–f₀(980) mixing amplitude between K⁺K⁻ and K⁰\bar K⁰ thresholds: |Π_{a₀f₀}(m)| ≈ 0.127 |g_{a₀K⁺K⁻} g_{f₀K⁺K⁻}|/16π ≳ 0.032 GeV²
- The averaged f₀(980)→γγ width from the K⁺K⁻ loop mechanism: ⟨Γ^{Born}_{f₀→K⁺K⁻→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.15 keV

## Context

This talk extends the author's long-standing program (Achasov, 1970s–2000s) arguing for the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons, building on Jaffe's (1977) MIT bag model prediction of light four-quark scalar states. It contradicts the naive q\bar q P-wave interpretation that would predict copious J/ψ decays and large two-photon widths for a₀(980) and f₀(980), similar to the tensor mesons a₂(1320) and f₂(1270). The work enables future experimental searches for a₀(980)–f₀(980) mixing in spin asymmetry measurements in reactions like π⁻p→ηπ⁰n, and calls for improved precision in J/ψ decay branching ratios to distinguish between the four-quark model and alternative scenarios involving gluon components or near-s\bar s states.
