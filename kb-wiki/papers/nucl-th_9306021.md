# A Quark Transport Theory to describe Nucleon--Nucleon Collisions

> On the basis of the Friedberg-Lee model we formulate a semiclassical transport theory to describe the phase-space evolution of nucleon-nucleon collisions on the quark level. The time evolution is given by a Vlasov-equation for the quark phase-space distribution and a Klein-Gordon equation for the mean-field describing the nucleon as a soliton bag. The Vlasov equation is solved numerically using an extended testparticle method. We test the confinement mechanism and mean-field effects in 1+1 dimensional simulations.

# A Quark Transport Theory to describe Nucleon–Nucleon Collisions

## Key claim

The authors formulate a semiclassical transport theory based on the Friedberg–Lee soliton bag model to describe nucleon–nucleon collisions at the quark level, but only in 1+1 dimensional simulations and only on the mean-field level without direct quark–quark collisions. The model achieves absolute confinement and transparency at high bombarding energies, but the authors caution that the fusion observed at low energies may be an artifact of the one-dimensional treatment.

## Method

The authors start from the Friedberg–Lee model with a color-dielectric effective coupling that gives quarks an infinite effective mass in the vacuum, ensuring absolute confinement. They derive a Vlasov equation for the quark phase-space distribution and a Klein–Gordon equation for the scalar sigma mean field, then solve these equations numerically using an extended testparticle method that includes both particle and antiparticle (negative-energy hole) degrees of freedom. The testparticles follow classical Hamilton equations of motion in the mean field, and the sigma field is evolved self-consistently with the scalar density. Simulations are performed in 1+1 dimensions for nucleon–nucleon collisions at bombarding energies from 1 to 25 GeV in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- The static Friedberg–Lee model in 1+1 dimensions yields a nucleon with total energy 931 MeV and RMS radius 0.56 fm for parameters a=222.91, b=-5347.05, c=38610, g0=1.
- Collisions are simulated for bombarding energies from 1 to 25 GeV in the laboratory frame.
- For boost velocities beta > 0.9, the collision time is of order 1 fm/c.
- Simulations use 20000 testparticles per nucleon in the 1-dimensional case.
- One moving nucleon is stable for times of approximately 20 fm/c, larger than typical collision times of approximately 1 fm/c.
- At bombarding energy T_lab approximately 1 GeV, fusion of the two incoming bags is observed.
- At bombarding energy E_lab = 15 GeV, the nucleons are transparent on the mean-field level.

## Context

This work extends established nucleon-based transport theories for heavy-ion collisions (Cassing and Mosel, 1990; Blattel, 1991) to a quark-level description using the Friedberg–Lee soliton bag model. It is conceptually close to the work of Zhang and Wilets, who derived transport equations from the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio model but did not perform dynamical simulations. The paper enables future extensions to 3+1 dimensions, inclusion of direct quark–quark collision terms, and particle production cross sections.
