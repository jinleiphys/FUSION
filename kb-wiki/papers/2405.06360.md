# Anatomy of critical fluctuations in hadronic matter

> Critical phenomena in phase transitions of strongly interacting matter, governed by quantum chromodynamics, are inherently encoded in the fluctuations of conserved charges. In this work, we study the net-baryon number density fluctuations, including the lowest-lying nucleon and the baryonic resonance $Δ(1232)$, based on the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation. We focus on the qualitative features of the second-order susceptibility of the net-baryon number density in dense hadronic matter and how the inclusion of $Δ(1232)$ affects it. We demonstrate that the fluctuations of the individual baryons do not necessarily reflect the total net-baryon number fluctuations at finite density, due to the non-trivial correlations between different particle species. Our results highlight the role of baryonic correlations in the interpretation of data from heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance and its chiral partner Δ(1700) introduces non-trivial correlations between baryon species that significantly affect net-baryon number fluctuations, meaning that fluctuations of individual baryons do not necessarily reflect total net-baryon number fluctuations at finite density. This qualitative conclusion is drawn from the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation, with the caveat that the results depend on the chosen values of the chirally invariant mass m₀^Δ and the repulsive coupling ratio r_Δ.

## Method
The author uses the SU(2) parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation to compute second-order susceptibilities of the net-baryon number density, including both nucleons (N(939) and N(1535)) and Δ resonances (Δ(1232) and Δ(1700)). The thermodynamic potential is evaluated with Fermi-Dirac statistics for baryons and mean-field potentials for σ and ω mesons, with parameters fixed to nuclear ground state properties (saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy -0.016 GeV, incompressibility 0.24 GeV). The susceptibilities are derived analytically from the thermodynamic potential and evaluated numerically for three representative values of the ω-meson coupling ratio r_Δ = 0.8, 1.0, 1.2 at fixed m₀^N = 0.8 GeV and m₀^Δ = 0.95 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Pseudocritical temperature at vanishing baryon chemical potential: T_c ≃ 0.17 GeV with Δ inclusion (m₀^Δ = 0.95 GeV), compared to T_c ≃ 0.21 GeV for the pure nucleonic model
- Nuclear ground state properties used: saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A - m_{N_+} = -0.016 GeV, incompressibility K = 0.24 GeV
- Vacuum masses: m_{N_+} = 0.939 GeV, m_{N_-} = 1.5 GeV, m_{Δ_+} = 1.232 GeV, m_{Δ_-} = 1.7 GeV, with chirally invariant masses m₀^N = 0.8 GeV and m₀^Δ = 0.95 GeV
- Liquid-gas crossover critical point temperature: T ≃ 0.016 GeV
- For r_Δ = 1, asymptotic high-density composition: Y_N → 0.2 (nucleon fraction) determined by degeneracy factors

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Koch (2023) and Marczenko (2023) on critical fluctuations in the parity doublet model by explicitly including the Δ(1232) resonance and its chiral partner Δ(1700), which were previously studied for neutron star applications by Marczenko (2021, 2022). The results highlight that baryonic correlations between different particle species must be accounted for when interpreting net-proton multiplicity fluctuations from heavy-ion collision experiments at BNL and CERN, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program. The findings enable future studies of higher-order fluctuations and the role of Δ matter in the QCD phase diagram at low temperature and high baryon density.
