# How to determine the shape of nuclear molecules with polarized gamma-rays

> A method has been recently proposed to establish the geometry of the alpha-cluster arrangement in $^{12}$C making use of polarized gamma-rays. The ratio of intensities of scattered radiation at 90 degrees along and perpendicular to the initial direction of the electric field vector, called depolarization ratio, is a key quantity that allows to underpin the nature of totally symmetric modes of vibrations. This allows to connect with the underlying point-group structure and therefore to the geometric shape of the nuclear molecule. This method is reviewed for $^{12}$C and extended to other configurations, such as three unequal clusters and four identical clusters (e.g. $^{16}$O).

## Key claim

The depolarization ratio of polarized gamma-rays scattered at 90 degrees can be used to determine the point-group symmetry and thus the geometric shape of nuclear molecules formed by alpha clusters, but the method is limited by the fact that many nuclear states of interest lie above particle separation thresholds and preferentially decay via particle emission rather than gamma decay.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of Raman scattering applied to nuclear cluster systems, using Placzek's theory to relate the depolarization ratio (ratio of scattered intensities perpendicular and parallel to the initial polarization direction) to the polarizability tensor invariants. For each possible spatial configuration of three identical clusters (linear centrosymmetric, linear non-centrosymmetric, equilateral triangle, isosceles triangle, scalene triangle) and for three unequal clusters, the point-group symmetry and vibrational mode characters are determined, and the expected depolarization ratio patterns are predicted: totally symmetric modes give a ratio between 0 and 3/4, while all other modes give exactly 3/4.

## Key numbers

- The depolarization ratio for non-totally symmetric modes is exactly 3/4, while totally symmetric modes satisfy 0 ≤ ρ ≤ 3/4.
- For the Hoyle state in 12C, the branching ratio for decay into the 8Be+4He channel is >99.9%, direct 3α decays amount to <0.04%, and the 2γ decay is about 0.04%.
- In 12C, only the ground state and the first excited 2+ state have energies lower than the separation into 3α particles.
- A system with N=3 particles has 3N-5=4 vibrational degrees of freedom for linear configurations and 3N-6=3 for planar configurations.

## Context

This work extends a method previously proposed for determining the alpha-cluster arrangement in 12C to other configurations including three unequal clusters and four identical clusters (e.g., 16O). The approach builds on the algebraic cluster model of Iachello and Bijker (2000s), which successfully explained the low-energy spectra of 12C and 16O in terms of equilateral and tetrahedral cluster structures respectively. The method enables future experiments using polarized gamma-rays in the 0-20 MeV range to discriminate between possible spatial arrangements of nuclear molecules, though the author notes the method loses appeal for N≥4 due to many configurations showing similar patterns.
