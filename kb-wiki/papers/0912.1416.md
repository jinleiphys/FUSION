# Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei in the Effective Chiral Model

> We systematically investigate the vacuum stability and nuclear properties in the effective chiral model with higher order terms in $σ$. We evaluate the model parameters by considering the saturation properties of nuclear matter as well as the normal vacuum to be globally stable at zero and finite baryon densities. We can find parameter sets giving moderate equations of state, and apply these models to finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors systematically investigate vacuum stability in an effective chiral model with higher-order scalar self-interactions (up to $\sigma^8$), finding that most previously published parameter sets that yield moderate nuclear incompressibility (200–400 MeV) are actually unstable against large variations of the sigma field, and they identify a stable region in the parameter space that still reproduces saturation properties.

## Method

The authors start from an SU(2) effective chiral Lagrangian with nucleons, scalar sigma, pseudoscalar pions, and vector omega and rho mesons, including sigma self-interactions up to eighth order. They fix the coupling constants $g_\omega$, $C_4$, and $C_6$ as functions of the effective mass at saturation $Y(\rho_0) = M_N^*/M_N$ and the coefficient $C_8$, by fitting the empirical saturation density and binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter. They then impose a vacuum stability condition requiring the chiral potential $V_\sigma$ to be positive for all $\sigma$ except at the vacuum minimum, and apply the resulting stable parameter sets to self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations of spherical finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.14\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$; binding energy per nucleon: $E_0/A = -(14.5\ \mathrm{to}\ 14.6)\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (adopted values used to fit parameters).
* Incompressibility of the new stable parameter sets (STO-1 through STO-5): range $K = 318.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (STO-1) to $K = 402.3\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (STO-4).
* Effective nucleon mass at saturation for STO sets: $M_N^*/M_N = 0.835$ (STO-2,3,4) and $0.850$ (STO-1,5).
* Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}$; nucleon mass: $M_N = 938\ \mathrm{MeV}$; omega meson mass: $m_\omega = 783\ \mathrm{MeV}$; rho meson mass: $m_\rho = 770\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Sigma meson mass for STO sets: range $m_\sigma = 781.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (STO-1) to $801.1\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (STO-4).
* Coupling constants for STO sets: $g_\omega$ from $6.001$ (STO-1,5) to $6.331$ (STO-2); $g_\rho$ from $3.467$ (STO-2,3,4,5) to $3.597$ (STO-1).

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Sahu et al. (2000) and Sahu et al. (2003) that introduced higher-order sigma terms ($\sigma^6$ and $\sigma^8$) to soften the equation of state in chiral models, but which did not critically examine vacuum stability at large sigma. The authors show that most of those earlier parameter sets (labeled SO, SJPP2003-I through V, and JM2009-1 through 16) are unstable, and they provide new stable sets (STO-1 through 5) that yield moderate incompressibility. This enables future applications to finite spherical nuclei and provides a foundation for testing the naturalness of the effective Lagrangian via naive dimensional analysis.
