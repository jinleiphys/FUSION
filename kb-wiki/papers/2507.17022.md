# Probing $ ϕ$N interaction through bound states of $ ϕ\textrm{N-}α$ system

> The possible bound state of the $ ϕN α$ system is explored within the framework of the three-body cluster model. The calculations are done by employing the state-of-the-art $ ϕ$N interactions obtained from the analysis of the pure elastic scattering and the coupled-channel in the $ϕp$ correlation functions. The $ ϕα$ potentials are constructed by two methods: the single-folding potential (SFP) method for the given spin-averaged $ ϕ$N potentials in coordinate space, and the optical model potential (OMP) approximation within the multiple-scattering framework for the given scattering length of the $ ϕ$N interaction. It is found that, when only the single-channel $ ϕN$ interactions are employed, the $ ϕN α$ system could be bound with a binding energy in the interval [3-26] MeV. However, the coupled-channel $ϕp$ interaction, which is most consistent with experimental measurements, does not yield any bound state, even when the spin-averaged $ϕN$ potential is employed, and this potential is found to be more attractive than the corresponding coupled-channel counterpart. It is essential to consider the contributions from the dynamics of the vector-baryon coupled channels $ϕp$ interaction. This effect is capable of playing a decisive role in the existence of mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the φNα system could be bound with a binding energy in the interval [3-26] MeV when only single-channel φN interactions are employed, but the coupled-channel φp interaction, which is most consistent with experimental measurements, does not yield any bound state. This demonstrates that the dynamics of vector-baryon coupled channels play a decisive role in the existence of φ-mesic nuclei.

## Method

The authors employed a three-body cluster model using the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion method to study the φNα system. The φα potentials were constructed through two approaches: the single-folding potential (SFP) method using spin-averaged φN potentials in coordinate space, and the optical model potential (OMP) approximation within the multiple-scattering framework using scattering lengths from φN interactions. The φN interactions were taken from two sources: single-channel potentials from HAL QCD lattice simulations at Euclidean times t/a=12 and t/a=14, and coupled-channel interactions from chiral Lagrangian models by Feijoo et al. (2025). For the Nα interaction, two potential forms were considered: the Sack-Bildenharn-Breit (SBB) potential with Gaussian form and a Woods-Saxon form.

## Key numbers

- The spin-averaged scattering length from ALICE Collaboration φp correlation function data: a₀ = (0.85 ± 0.48) + i(0.16 ± 0.19) fm
- The φN binding energy in the ²S₁/₂ channel: B(φN) = 28.93 MeV at t/a=12, and 16.64 MeV at t/a=14
- The φNα system binding energy interval when using single-channel φN interactions: [3-26] MeV
- The φp effective range and scattering length from the coupled-channel model: (a₀ = 0.272 + i 0.189 fm, r₀ = -7.20 - i 0.09 fm) and (a₀ = -0.034 + i 0.57 fm, r₀ = -8.06 + i 0.05 fm)
- The root-mean-square matter radius of ⁴He: 1.70 ± 0.14 fm
- The Gaussian parameter for the ⁴He ground-state density: b = 1.41 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of φ-mesic nuclei, including the φNN system studied by Belyaev (2008, 2009), Sofianos (2010), and others, as well as the φαα and φφα systems studied by Filikhin et al. (2024). The paper directly compares single-channel φN interactions from HAL QCD lattice simulations (Yan et al., 2022) with coupled-channel interactions from chiral Lagrangian approaches (Feijoo et al., 2025; Oset et al., 2010). The results contradict the findings of Chizzali et al. (2024), who found a φp bound state with binding energy in the range [12.8-56.1] MeV for the spin 1/2 channel using single-channel potentials. The present work enables future experimental searches for φ-mesic nuclei by demonstrating that coupled-channel dynamics must be properly accounted for in theoretical predictions.
