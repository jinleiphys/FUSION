# Probing the Ground State and Transition Densities of Halo Nuclei

> We investigate the use of elastic and inelastic scatterings with secondary beams of radioactive nuclei as a mean to obtain information on ground state properties and transition matrix elements to continuum states. An eikonal model is developed for this purpose by using the folding potential. In particular we discuss possible signatures of halo wavefunctions in elastic and inelastic scattering experiments.

## Key claim

Elastic and inelastic scattering of radioactive beams at intermediate energies (above 50 MeV/nucleon) can be used to extract ground-state densities and transition densities of halo nuclei, but only at forward scattering angles where the simple "tρρ" folding approximation is valid; at larger angles multiple-scattering corrections and dynamical polarization effects undermine a direct relationship between the data and the nuclear densities.

## Method

The authors develop an eikonal model for elastic and inelastic scattering of halo nuclei, using the "tρρ" folding approximation to construct the optical potential from ground-state densities and free nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. Ground-state densities for exotic nuclei are calculated using the Hartree-Fock approximation with Skyrme interaction (parameter set SGII), where the single-particle energy of the last neutron orbit is adjusted to reproduce the empirical neutron separation energy. Inelastic scattering cross sections are computed using both the deformed potential model and a folding model with transition densities, including Coulomb excitation.

## Key numbers

- The separation energies of halo nuclei are extremely small: for example, the one-neutron separation energy of 11Be is 0.50 MeV, and the two-neutron separation energy of 11Li is 0.30 MeV (Table 1 values cited in text)
- For the elastic scattering of 17O+208Pb at 84 MeV/nucleon, the eikonal model with the "tρρ" approximation reproduces the rainbow scattering at θ ≈ 2.5° extremely well
- For p+11Li scattering at 62 MeV/nucleon, the "tρρ" calculation agrees well with experimental data at forward angles
- For p+8He at 72 MeV/nucleon, the agreement with data is "almost perfect" at forward angles
- For 11Li+12C at Elab = 637 MeV, the quasi-elastic scattering data require inclusion of inelastic excitation of the 2+ state (β2 = 0.59) and 3- state (β3 = 0.40) in 12C to achieve good agreement
- For 12Be+12C at Elab = 679 MeV and 14Be+12C at Elab = 796 MeV, the elastic calculations agree reasonably with data, but including inelastic contributions yields cross sections too large at large angles

## Context

This work extends the model of Bertulani and Sagawa (1993) for nuclear excitations of 11Li to include Coulomb excitation processes, and applies the eikonal approximation previously developed for stable nuclei by Lenzi, Vitturi and Zardi (1988) to the study of radioactive beams. The paper enables future experiments to use elastic and inelastic scattering of radioactive beams as a probe of ground-state properties and transition densities of halo nuclei, complementing earlier studies of reaction cross sections and momentum distributions by Tanihata (1985, 1988) and elastic scattering measurements by Kobayashi (1992, 1993) and Zahar (1994).
