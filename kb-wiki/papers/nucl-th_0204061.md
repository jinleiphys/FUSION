# Shadowing in photo-production : role of in-medium hadrons

> We study the effects of in-medium hadronic properties on shadowing in photon-nucleus interactions in Glauber model as well as in the multiple scattering approach. A reasonable agreement with the experimental data is obtained in a scenario of downward spectral shift of the hadrons. Shadowing is found to be insensitive to the broadening of the spectral functions. An impact parameter dependent analysis of shadowing might shed more light on the role of in-medium properties of hadrons.

# Shadowing in photo-production: role of in-medium hadrons

## Key claim

The experimental data on shadowing in photon-nucleus interactions are reasonably well reproduced by a scenario where vector meson masses are shifted downward in the nuclear medium (universal scaling scenario), but the data are insensitive to broadening of the hadronic spectral functions; however, the authors caution that refinement of Glauber model parameters (vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitude, two-nucleon correlation) might achieve agreement even with vacuum hadronic properties, so a definitive statement about medium effects requires better estimates of these quantities.

## Method

The authors compute the shadowing ratio A_eff/A for photon-nucleus interactions using both the Glauber model (with eikonal approximation and vector meson dominance) and a multiple scattering approach (up to double scattering), incorporating in-medium modifications of hadron masses via the universal scaling scenario (USS) and a quantum hadrodynamical (QHD) model. The effective nucleon mass and vector meson masses are treated as spatially dependent through the nuclear density profile, and the coherence length for vector meson propagation is evaluated using these density-dependent masses. For photon energies below 1.2 GeV, baryonic resonance contributions are included using the prescription of Alberico (1994), while above 1.2 GeV the vector meson dominance picture is applied.

## Key numbers

- USS mass scaling: m_H*/m_H = 1 - 0.2x, where x = n(r)/n_0(r) and n_0 is normal nuclear matter density
- QHD parametrization for nucleons: a_1 = -0.351277, a_2 = 0.0766239; for rho meson: a_1 = -1.30966, a_2 = 1.78784, a_3 = -1.17524, a_4 = 0.294456; for omega meson: a_1 = -0.470454, a_2 = 0.313825, a_3 = -0.0731274
- Two-body correlation parameter q_c = 780 MeV used in the two-particle density function
- The boundary between resonance and VMD regions is set at E_gamma = 1.2 GeV
- An increase in rho mass of approximately 40 MeV (Eletsky and Ioffe, 1997) results in decreased shadowing
- The leading correction to the Glauber model due to deviation from eikonal propagation is of order (2 P_cm^A R)^{-1}, which is small for E_gamma > 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the debate between Falter, Leupold and Mosel (2000, 2001), who argued that early onset of shadowing could be explained by a negative real part of the rho-nucleon scattering amplitude (corresponding to higher effective rho mass), and Bianchi, De Sanctis, Mirazita and Muccifora (1999), who interpreted the same data as a signature of lighter rho mesons in the medium. The authors introduce spatial dependence of both nucleon and vector meson masses (neglected in prior studies) and find that the universal scaling scenario (Brown and Rho, 1991) gives better agreement than the QHD model, which overestimates shadowing due to larger mass drops. The results enable future impact-parameter-dependent analyses of shadowing that could further discriminate between medium modification scenarios, and the authors note that improved experimental statistics and better estimates of vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes are needed for definitive conclusions.
