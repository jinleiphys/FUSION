# Restoration of chiral and U(1)_A symmetries in excited hadrons in the semiclassical regime

> Restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries in excited hadrons is reviewed. Implications of the OPE as well as of the semiclassical expansion for this phenomenon are discussed. A solvable model of the 't Hooft type in 3+1 dimensions is presented, which demonstrates a fast restoration of both chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries at larger spins and radial excitations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral and U(1)_A symmetries are effectively restored in highly excited hadrons because the semiclassical approximation applies at large radial quantum number n or angular momentum J, suppressing the quantum loop effects that are responsible for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the axial anomaly. The claim is hedged by the condition that this restoration occurs only in the regime where the action S is much larger than ℏ, and that isolated hadrons must still exist at such excitation energies, which is guaranteed in the large N_c limit.

## Method

The author reviews phenomenological evidence for parity doubling in nucleon and meson spectra above 1.7 GeV, then presents a semiclassical argument based on the path integral expansion in powers of ℏ, where the classical (tree-level) contribution preserves chiral symmetries and loop contributions (starting at one-loop order) break them. A solvable generalized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with an instantaneous Lorentz-vector linear confining potential in 3+1 dimensions is used to compute meson masses from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, demonstrating the restoration pattern for light-light mesons.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon and delta spectra show parity doubling starting from the 1.7 GeV region.
- For J=0 mesons, the ground state (n=0) mass splitting between the 0^{-+} (0.00 √σ) and 0^{++} (1.49 √σ) chiral partners is 1.49 √σ; by n=6 the splitting reduces to 8.09 √σ vs 8.33 √σ (difference 0.24 √σ).
- For J=1 mesons at n=0, the 1^{+-} mass is 2.68 √σ and the 1^{--} mass is 2.78 √σ (difference 0.10 √σ); at n=6 they are 8.53 √σ and 8.68 √σ (difference 0.15 √σ).
- For J=6 mesons at n=0, all four states in the table are within 0.05 √σ of each other (6.83–6.88 √σ), indicating near-complete degeneracy.
- The mass splitting ΔM within chiral multiplets decreases asymptotically as 1/√n for fixed J, with a fitted coefficient of 0.7 √σ/√n.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological observations of parity doubling in baryons (Glozman 2001, Cohen and Glozman 2002) and mesons (Glozman 2004) by providing a theoretical semiclassical justification and a solvable model. It contrasts with the 1+1 dimensional 't Hooft model, where no spin exists and chiral multiplets never emerge, and with earlier studies of heavy-light mesons using quadratic confining potentials (KNR). The results enable a microscopic understanding of how valence quarks in high-lying hadrons decouple from the QCD vacuum and from Goldstone bosons, with implications for the quark-hadron duality and the role of condensates in the operator product expansion.
