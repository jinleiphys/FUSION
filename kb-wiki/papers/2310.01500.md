# Probing the Short-Distance Structure of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Energy Correlators

> Energy-energy-correlators (EEC's) are a promising observable to study the dynamics of jet evolution in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) through its imprint on angular scales in the energy flux of final-state particles. We carry out the first complete calculation of EEC's using realistic simulations of high-energy heavy-ion collisions, and dissect the different dynamics underlying the final distribution through analyses of jet propagation in a uniform medium. The EEC's of $γ$-jets in heavy-ion collisions are found to be enhanced by the medium response from elastic scatterings instead of induced gluon radiation at large angles. In the meantime, EEC's are suppressed at small angles due to energy loss and transverse momentum broadening of jet shower partons. These modifications are further shown to be sensitive to the angular scale of the in-medium interaction, as characterized by the Debye screening mass. Experimental verification and measurement of such modifications will shed light on this scale, and the short-distance structure of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that energy-energy correlators (EEC's) of gamma-jets in heavy-ion collisions are enhanced at large angles primarily by medium response from elastic scatterings rather than by induced gluon radiation, and suppressed at small angles due to energy loss and transverse momentum broadening; these modifications are sensitive to the Debye screening mass, which characterizes the angular scale of in-medium interactions.

## Method
The authors carried out the first complete calculation of EEC's using the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model for a uniform QGP brick and the coupled LBT (CoLBT) model, which combines LBT with CLVisc hydrodynamics to simulate event-by-event jet transport and bulk QGP evolution including jet-induced medium response. They dissected contributions from medium response, medium-induced gluon emissions, and energy loss of shower partons by analyzing single quark propagation and gamma-jet events in both a uniform medium and realistic Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The sensitivity to the short-distance structure was studied by varying a K factor that scales the Debye screening mass while keeping the jet transport coefficient fixed.

## Key numbers
- For a single quark with E=100 GeV in a QGP brick at T=0.36 GeV and L=4 fm, EEC distributions were computed for K=0.2, 1, and 4 (Fig. 3).
- For gamma-jets in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, the EEC was computed with cone-size R=0.5, pT_gamma >= 100 GeV/c, and pT_jet >= 50 GeV/c for K=0.2, 1, and 4 (Fig. 5).
- The collinear cutoff for vacuum radiation was set at mu=1 GeV in the analytic calculation (Fig. 2).
- The effective strong coupling constant was set to alpha_s=0.3 for LBT calculations and alpha_s=0.25 for CoLBT simulations.
- The Debye screening mass is defined as mu_D^2 = (3/2) K g^2 T^2, with default K=1.

## Context
This work extends prior analytic studies of energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions by Andres (2022, 2023) that focused on color-coherence and medium-induced gluon radiation but did not include medium response or jet energy loss. It also builds on the LBT model developed by Li (2010), He (2015), and Cao (2016), and the CoLBT model by Chen (2017, 2020) and Zhao (2021). The results enable experimental measurements of EEC modifications to constrain the momentum scale of parton interactions in the QGP, complementing Bayesian extractions of the jet transport coefficient by the JET (2013) and JETSCAPE (2021) collaborations.
