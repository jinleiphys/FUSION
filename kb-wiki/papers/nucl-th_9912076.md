# Positron Tunnelling through the Coulomb Barrier of Superheavy Nuclei

> We study beams of medium-energy electrons and positrons which obey the Dirac equation and scatter from nuclei with $Z > 100.$ At small distances the potential is modelled to be that of a charged sphere. A large peak is found in the probability of positron penetration to the origin for $Z \approx 184.$ This may be understood as an example of Klein tunnelling through the Coulomb barrier: it is the analogue of the Klein Paradox for the Coulomb potential.

## Key claim
For positrons scattering off superheavy nuclei with Z > 150, the exponential suppression of barrier penetration predicted by the standard formula breaks down, and a sharp peak in the penetration probability appears near Z = 184, corresponding to the supercritical 2p_{1/2} bound state rather than the first supercritical 1s_{1/2} state.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically for a modified Coulomb potential from a homogeneously charged sphere of radius b = (1.2)A^{1/3} fermi, following Pieper and Greiner (1969). They computed the ratio ρ = |ψ(0)|_{pos}^2 / |ψ(0)|_{el}^2 of positron to electron wavefunction probability at the origin for incident momenta p in the range 0.1m to 0.4m, integrating the radial Dirac equations outward from the origin and matching to asymptotic Coulomb wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- Peak in ρ occurs at Z = 184.8 for p = 0.4m and at Z = 183.8 for p = 0.1m, extrapolating to Z = 183.8 for p → 0.
- At the peak maximum for p = 0.4m, ρ ≈ 2, representing an enhancement by a factor of 10^8 between Z = 179 and Z = 184.
- Critical Z values for the modified Coulomb potential: 1s_{1/2} state reaches E = 0 at Z = 146.7 and E = -m at Z = 170.4; 2p_{1/2} state reaches E = 0 at Z = 168.1 and E = -m at Z = 183.8.
- For Z = 100, the calculation of ρ agrees with the exponential formula e^{-2πy} where y = ZαE/p.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Klein tunnelling by Calogeracos and Dombey (1999) from one-dimensional potential steps to the three-dimensional Coulomb potential of superheavy nuclei. It contradicts the expectation that the maximum transmission would occur at the first supercritical value Z = 170.4 (the 1s_{1/2} state), instead finding the peak at the 2p_{1/2} supercritical state, consistent with a prediction by Müller, Rafelski and Greiner (1972) of a resonance in the negative energy continuum at Z = 184. The result enables further study of transmission resonances in three-dimensional Dirac scattering and suggests that Klein tunnelling is a general feature of the Dirac equation in strong fields.
