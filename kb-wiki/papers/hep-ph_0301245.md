# D-Meson Production from Recombination in Hadronic Collisions

> Nonperturbative effects in $D$-meson production in pion-nucleon and proton-nucleon collisions are investigated within the recombination model. The coalescence of perturbatively created charm quarks with sea- and valence-quarks from projectile and target fragments is shown to be competitive in magnitude with standard fragmentation calculations at both central (small $x_F$) and forward rapidities. Corresponding flavor asymmetries for inclusive $D$-meson production are thus mostly generated on the (light-) parton distribution level, and turn out to be in reasonable overall agreement with available fixed-target data. Predictions for upcoming measurements at RHIC are given.

## Key claim

Nonperturbative recombination of perturbatively created charm quarks with sea- and valence-quarks from projectile and target fragments is competitive with standard fragmentation for D-meson production at both central (small x_F) and forward rapidities, with the caveat that the recombination contribution is evaluated using a simplified factorized ansatz for two-parton distribution functions rather than a fully microscopic treatment.

## Method

The authors compute D-meson production cross sections in pion-nucleon and proton-nucleon collisions using a recombination model where charm quarks from leading-order perturbative QCD (with a constant K-factor) coalesce with light quarks from the projectile or target. The recombination function is evaluated using either a parton-model wave function in momentum fractions or a Gaussian in rapidity difference, while two-parton distribution functions are taken in factorized form with phase-space corrections. Charm quarks that do not recombine are hadronized via isospin-symmetric fragmentation with delta-function approximation for the x_F dependence.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark mass fixed at m_c = 1.35 GeV/c^2
- K-factor of approximately 5-6 for typical parton distribution functions and m_c = 1.35 GeV to match experimental yields
- Recombination fraction of c and anti-c quarks reaches almost 70% when using rapidity-space wave functions
- Recombination contribution makes up 30-60% of the total cross section in the central region, dominating at forward x_F >= 0.4
- For c-quark fragmentation, a 3:1 ratio for D*:D is employed (epsilon = 3/4), giving R_{c/n} = 0.33; for recombination, epsilon = 0.6 gives R_{c/n} = 0.43
- D*(2007)^0 decays to approximately 100% in D^0(1865); D*(2010)^+ has branching ratio 68% into D^0(1865)pi^+ and 32% into D^+(1869)
- Light quark transverse masses of m_{q,t} approximately 0.3-0.5 GeV are used

## Context

This work extends the recombination model, previously applied to forward production of charmed hadrons showing leading-particle effects (Das and Hwa 1977, Kartvelishvili et al. 1981, Hwa 1995, Chang et al. 1998, Liu and Shuryak 1999), to central x_F region by incorporating sea-quark recombination. It addresses flavor asymmetries in bulk D-meson production that standard fragmentation calculations (Frixione et al. 1997) and PYTHIA event generator fits (Paige et al. 1998) cannot explain, particularly the D+/D- enhancement over D^0 total production. The results enable predictions for upcoming p-p measurements at RHIC at center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) = 200-500 GeV.
