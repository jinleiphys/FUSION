# Azimuthal Spin Asymmetries of Pion Electroproduction

> Azimuthal spin asymmetries, both for charged and neutral pion production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of unpolarized charged lepton beams on longitudinally and transversely polarized nucleon targets, are analyzed and calculated. Various assumptions and approximations in the quark distributions and fragmentation functions often used in these calculations are studied in detail. It is found that different approaches to the distribution and fragmentation functions may lead to quite different predictions on the azimuthal asymmetries measured in the HERMES experiments, thus their effects should be taken into account before using the available data as a measurement of quark transversity distributions. It is also found that the unfavored quark to pion fragmentation functions must be taken into account for $π^-$ production from a proton target, although they can be neglected for $π^+$ and $π^0$ production. Pion production from a proton target is suitable to study the $u$ quark transversity distribution, whereas a combination of pion production from both proton and neutron targets can measure the flavor structure of quark transversity distributions.

## Key claim

The azimuthal spin asymmetries measured by HERMES for pion electroproduction cannot be directly interpreted as a clean measurement of quark transversity distributions, because different assumptions about the distribution functions and fragmentation functions lead to significantly different predictions, and the contribution from unfavored fragmentation processes must be included for π⁻ production from a proton target.

## Method

The authors calculated the sin φ-weighted azimuthal spin asymmetries A_UL^sin φ for π⁺, π⁻, and π⁰ production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of an unpolarized positron beam on a longitudinally polarized proton target, using the quark-parton model formalism. They compared three different theoretical approaches to the distribution functions (Leading Approach, Approach 1, and Approach 2) and two sets of quark transversity distributions (from a quark-diquark model and a pQCD-based analysis), using the CTEQ5 parametrization for unpolarized quark distributions and the Kretzer (2001) parametrization for fragmentation functions, including both favored and unfavored fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- HERMES beam energy: E = 27.6 GeV, with s = 51.8 GeV²
- Target polarization: S = 0.86
- Average transverse momentum: ⟨P_h⊥⟩ = 0.44 GeV
- Experimental kinematic cuts: 1 < Q² < 15 GeV², W > 2 GeV, 0.2 < y < 0.85, 0.2 < z < 0.7
- Collins function parameter: M_C = 0.7 GeV
- Favored fragmentation function parametrization: D(z) = 0.689 z^{-1.039} (1-z)^{1.241}
- Unfavored fragmentation function parametrization: D̂(z) = 0.217 z^{-1.805} (1-z)^{2.037}

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of HERMES azimuthal asymmetry data (HERMES 2000, 2001) that assumed only favored fragmentation processes and neglected twist-3 contributions. The authors show that the simplifying assumptions used in earlier studies by Efremov et al. (2000) and Ma, Schmidt, and Yang (2000) are not always justified, particularly for π⁻ production. The results enable future extractions of the flavor structure of quark transversity distributions by combining pion production data from both proton and neutron targets, and they identify that π⁺ production from a neutron target would provide a cleaner test of different model predictions than π⁻ production from a proton target.
