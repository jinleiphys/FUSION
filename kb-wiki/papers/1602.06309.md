# Revised analysis of $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr fusion reactions

> Fusion data for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr are analyzed by coupled-channels calculations that are based on a standard Woods-Saxon potential and include couplings to multiphonon excitations and transfer channels. The couplings to multiphonon excitations are the same as used in a previous work. The transfer couplings are calibrated to reproduce the measured neutron transfer data. This type of calculation gives a poor fit to the fusion data. However, by multiplying the transfer couplings with a $\sqrt{2}$ one obtains an excellent fit. The scaling of the transfer strengths is supposed to simulate the combined effect of neutron and proton transfer, and the calculated one- and two-nucleon transfer cross sections are indeed in reasonable agreement with the measured cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper claims that coupled-channels calculations including multiphonon excitations and neutron transfer couplings calibrated to reproduce measured neutron transfer data still underestimate the subbarrier fusion cross sections for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr; however, multiplying the neutron transfer couplings by a factor of $\sqrt{2}$ to simulate the combined effect of neutron and proton transfer yields an excellent fit to the fusion data.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using a standard Woods-Saxon potential with parameters $R$ = 9.599 fm, $V_0$ = -73.98 MeV, and diffuseness $a$ = 0.673 fm, including couplings to multiphonon excitations (up to three-phonon) and to one-, two-, and three-neutron transfer channels. The one-neutron transfer form factor was constructed from transfer of $d_{5/2}$ and $s_{1/2}$ states in $^{96}$Zr to the $f_{7/2}$ state in $^{41}$Ca, and the coupling strengths $F_{1n}$ and $F_{2n}$ were calibrated to reproduce measured one- and two-neutron transfer probabilities at large minimum distances. The combined effect of neutron and proton transfer was then simulated by scaling the neutron transfer couplings by $\sqrt{2}$, yielding revised strengths $F_{1n}=2.25$ and $F_{2n}=0.355$ fm.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential used has parameters $R$ = 9.599 fm, $V_0$ = -73.98 MeV, and diffuseness $a$ = 0.673 fm, producing a Coulomb barrier height of 96.62 MeV and a pocket of 73.62 MeV for $L=0$.
- The ground state Q value for one-neutron transfer is 0.61 MeV; for two-neutron transfer it is $Q_{2n}=+5.52$ MeV; for three-neutron transfer it is $Q_{3n}=+5.24$ MeV.
- The calibrated one-neutron transfer strength in Ch-4 calculations is $F_{1n}=1.6$; the pair-transfer strength is $F_{2n}=0.25$ fm.
- The revised (scaled) strengths are $F_{1n}=2.25$ and $F_{2n}=0.355$ fm.
- The revised Ch-84 calculation achieves an average $\chi^2$ of 0.87 assuming a systematic error of 7%, with an optimum energy shift $\Delta E = -0.15$ MeV.
- The fusion data reach cross sections as small as 2.4 $\mu$b.
- The ground state energy of the $^{136}$Nd compound nucleus is $E_{CN}=41.089$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channels analyses of Ca+Zr fusion by Esbensen et al. (2010) that failed to reproduce the $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr data even with strong pair-transfer couplings. It directly addresses the long-standing challenge posed by the Timmers et al. (1998) data, which show strong subbarrier enhancement compared to $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr and $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr systems. The conclusion that neutron transfer alone is insufficient agrees with Scamps and Hagino (2014), while the proposed solution of including proton transfer effects via a $\sqrt{2}$ scaling provides a consistent explanation that also reproduces measured one- and two-nucleon transfer cross sections. The work enables future explicit treatment of proton transfer channels and suggests that detailed measurements of proton transfer cross sections would be valuable.
