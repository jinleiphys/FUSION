# Elliptic flow at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider: Comparing heavy-ion data to viscous hydrodynamic predictions

> I compare the first viscous hydrodynamic prediction for integrated elliptic flow in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC with the first data released by the ALICE collaboration. These new data are found to be consistent with hydrodynamic extrapolations of RHIC data with no change in medium parameters (e.g., average viscosity). I also discuss how, in general, a precise comparison of data to theoretical calculations requires an understanding of some subtleties of the measurement -- most notably the cut on transverse momentum of the particles used and the differing sensitivities to flow fluctuations and non-flow effects of the various measurement methods.

## Key claim

The first viscous hydrodynamic prediction for integrated elliptic flow at LHC energies is consistent with the ALICE data when the correct transverse momentum cut and measurement method are applied, contrary to the initial impression that predictions were too low. The implication that a change in viscosity between RHIC and LHC is required is not correct.

## Method

The author compared the viscous hydrodynamic prediction from Luzum (2009) for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC to the first ALICE data on integrated elliptic flow of charged hadrons. The hydrodynamic calculation used smooth initial conditions representing an average over many events with a fixed reaction plane, and the author recomputed the integrated elliptic flow applying the same transverse momentum cut (p_t > 200 MeV) used by ALICE. The author then analyzed how different measurement methods (two-particle cumulant, event-plane, four-particle cumulant, Lee-Yang Zeros) have differing sensitivities to flow fluctuations and non-flow correlations, and identified which methods are most appropriate for comparison to smooth hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key numbers

- The cut in transverse momentum (p_t > 200 MeV) increases the integrated elliptic flow value by 14–23%, depending on centrality.
- The hydrodynamic predictions were based on an assumed charged hadron multiplicity for a central collision at midrapidity of dN_ch/dY = 1800.
- The PHOBOS measurement at RHIC used no cut in p_t (though hadrons with p_t < 35 MeV likely did not reach the detector), while STAR used a cut of 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic framework of Luzum (2008) and Luzum (2009), which was previously fit to RHIC data from PHOBOS and STAR, to the new LHC energy regime. The paper contradicts the implication in the ALICE collaboration's data release (Aamodt, 2010) that hydrodynamic predictions are generically too low unless viscosity changes between RHIC and LHC. It enables more precise quantitative extraction of medium properties (such as shear viscosity) from future LHC data by clarifying that proper comparison requires matching the transverse momentum cut and measurement method between theory and experiment.
