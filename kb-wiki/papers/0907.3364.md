# Hydrodynamical model for $J/ψ$ suppression and elliptic flow

> In a hydrodynamic model, we have studied $J/ψ$ suppression and elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. At the initial time, $J/ψ$'s are randomly distributed in the fluid. As the fluid evolve in time, the free streaming $J/ψ$'s are dissolved if the local fluid temperature exceeds a melting temperature $T_{J/ψ}$. Sequential melting of charmonium states ($χ_c$, $ψ\prime$ and $J/ψ$), with melting temperatures $T_{χ_c}=T_{ψ\prime} \approx 1.2T_c$, $T_{J/ψ} \approx2T_c$ and feed-down fraction $F\approx 0.3$, is consistent with the PHENIX data on $J/ψ$ suppression and near zero elliptic flow for $J/ψ$'s. It is also shown that the model will require substantial regeneration of charmoniums, if the charmonium states dissolve at temperature close to the critical temperature, $T_{χ_c}=T_{ψ\prime} \leq T_c$, $T_{J/ψ}\approx1.2T_c$. The regenerated charmoniums will have positive elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a hydrodynamic model with sequential melting of charmonium states (melting temperatures T_chi_c = T_psi_prime approx 1.2 T_c, T_J/psi approx 2 T_c, feed-down fraction F approx 0.3) is consistent with PHENIX data on J/psi suppression and near-zero elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, but only if J/psi survives to high temperature; if instead J/psi dissolves near the critical temperature (T_J/psi approx 1.2 T_c), the model requires substantial regeneration of charmoniums from c-cbar recombination to match the data.

## Method

The author uses a (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model with a first-order phase transition at T_c=164 MeV and freeze-out at T_F=130 MeV. J/psi mesons are randomly distributed in the transverse plane at initial time tau_i=0.6 fm/c according to the binary collision profile from a Glauber model, with transverse momentum distributed according to a power law fit to p+p data. As the fluid evolves, free-streaming J/psi are dissolved if the local fluid temperature exceeds a melting temperature T_J/psi, using a decay width Gamma_dis(T) that is infinite above T_J/psi and proportional to (T/T_c - 1)^2 below it, with thermal width alpha=0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperatures used: T_chi_c = T_psi_prime approx 1.2 T_c, T_J/psi approx 2 T_c, with feed-down fraction F approx 0.3
- Critical temperature T_c = 164 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_F = 130 MeV
- Initial time tau_i = 0.6 fm/c; initial central entropy density S_ini = 110 fm^{-3}; initial energy density epsilon_i approx 30 GeV/fm^3
- If T_J/psi approx 1.2 T_c, approximately 98% of initially produced J/psi are dissolved in the medium
- Regenerated J/psi produced according to equilibrium distribution at T_c have approximately 15-20% v_2 at p_T = 2 GeV in 20-30% or 20-60% centrality Au+Au collisions
- PHENIX measured integrated v_2 for J/psi in 20-60% Au+Au collisions: v_2 = -0.10 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.02

## Context

This work extends the author's previous hydrodynamic model studies (Chaudhuri 2008, Chaudhuri 2008) that explained centrality dependence of J/psi suppression and p_T spectra in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions using sequential melting with high dissociation temperatures. The paper directly tests two competing theoretical predictions: potential model calculations (Satz 2006) giving T_J/psi approx 2 T_c, versus recent spectral function analyses (Mocsy 2007, Mocsy 2008) finding T_J/psi approx 1.2 T_c. The results show that if the lower dissociation temperatures are correct, the data can only be explained by substantial regeneration of charmoniums, which would produce positive elliptic flow measurable in future experiments with smaller error bars.
