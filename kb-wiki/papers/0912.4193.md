# Probing the nuclear equation of state in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energy in isospin-sensitive exclusive experiments

> We propose a few selected experimental approaches to show that new-generation instruments can give a direct access to significant observables on the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state. The form of such dependence is investigated within the Stochastic Mean Field model, coupled to a secondary-decay treatment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that new-generation instruments like FAZIA can provide direct access to observables sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, but this discrimination is only possible if the secondary decay does not completely wash out the signal; the authors show that for stable and moderately exotic systems, secondary decay can make discrimination between asy-stiff and asy-soft parametrizations difficult, while exotic systems or specific observables like the imbalance ratio preserve the sensitivity.

## Method

The authors simulated collisions of ⁵⁸Ni and ⁶⁸Ni isotopes at 15 and 40 MeV per nucleon using the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model, a semi-classical transport model based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck formalism with isospin-dependent mean field and density fluctuations. The dynamical stage was followed to 260 fm/c, then coupled to the GEMINI evaporation model to account for secondary decay. The simulated events were filtered through detector response models for FAZIA (a new-generation 4π array with mass identification) and INDRA (a former-generation detector without mass measurement), with the latter using the EPAX parametrization to deduce masses from charges.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and incompressibility modulus K∞ = 200 MeV (soft equation of state)
- Asy-stiff symmetry energy parameters: a = 12.7 MeV, b = 36 MeV; asy-soft parameters: a = 12.7 MeV, c = 481.7 MeV·fm³, d = -1638.2 MeV·fm⁶
- Incident energies studied: 15 and 40 A MeV for Ni+Ni systems
- Geometric efficiency: INDRA εgeo = 0.88, FAZIA εgeo = 0.78
- Central event selection: bred < 0.2 at 40 A MeV, bred < 0.55 at 15 A MeV; mid-peripheral: 0.25 < bred < 0.6 at 40 A MeV, 0.6 < bred < 0.75 at 15 A MeV
- 1000 transport events, each producing ten decay paths, yielding simulated statistics of 10000 events

## Context

This work extends the study of the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state, building on prior isospin-transport experiments that imposed theoretical constraints (Tsang 2004, Chen 2005, Shetty 2007, Galichet 2009, De Filippo 2009, Amorini 2009). The paper addresses the challenge that secondary decay can deform EOS-sensitive isotopic observables, as previously noted by Liu (2004), Shetty (2006), and Tian (2008). The imbalance ratio approach was introduced by Rami (2000) and exploited by the MSU group (Tsang 2004, Chen 2005, Tsang 2009). The results enable future experiments with exotic beams at facilities like FAZIA to discriminate between stiff and soft forms of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities, where complications from hadron effective mass splittings and momentum dependence in the isovector channel do not arise as they do at relativistic energies.
