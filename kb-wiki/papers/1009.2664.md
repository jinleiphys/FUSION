# Collective Flows in a Transport Approach

> We introduce a transport approach at fixed shear viscosity to entropy ratio $\etas$ to study the generation of collective flows in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Transport theory supplies a covariant approach valid also at large $\etas$ and at intermediate transverse momentum $p_T$, where deviations from equilibrium is no longer negligible. Such an approach shows that at RHIC energies a temperature dependent $\etas$ enhances significantly the $v_4/v_2^2$ respect to the case of constant $\etas$. Furthermore if NJL chiral dynamics is self-consistently implemented we show that it does not modify the relation between $v_2$ and $\etas$.

# Collective Flows in a Transport Approach

## Key claim
The paper introduces a relativistic transport approach at fixed shear viscosity to entropy ratio that remains valid at large viscosity and at intermediate transverse momentum, where viscous hydrodynamics breaks down. The approach shows that a temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy ratio significantly enhances the ratio v4/v2^2 compared to a constant value, while self-consistently implementing NJL chiral dynamics does not modify the relation between elliptic flow v2 and shear viscosity to entropy ratio.

## Method
The authors solve a Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation for (anti-)quark phase-space distribution functions, including both a collision term (associated with finite shear viscosity) and a scalar field interaction term (associated with the equation of state). For the cascade-only case, the scattering cross section is renormalized locally according to the relation eta/s * sigma_tr = <p>/(15 rho). For the full dynamics, the NJL model generates a space-time dependent mass via the mass gap equation, and the relation between cross section and viscosity is extended to massive relativistic particles. Calculations are performed for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV with a maximum initial temperature of 340 MeV and initial time 0.6 fm/c, using a Glauber model for the initial density distribution.

## Key numbers
- For constant 4*pi*eta/s = 1 in the QGP phase, the ratio v4/v2^2 is about 0.6 for Au+Au at 200 AGeV at impact parameter b = 8 fm.
- For a temperature-dependent eta/s following 4*pi*eta/s = T/T0 = (epsilon/epsilon0)^(1/4) with epsilon0 = 1.7 GeV/fm^3, the ratio v4/v2^2 increases to about 0.8.
- The presence of NJL field dynamics suppresses v2(pT) by about 20% at pT > 1 GeV compared to the cascade case.
- The hadronic phase eta/s is set to 8/(4*pi) ~ 0.65.
- The cross-over region begins when energy density falls below epsilon = 2 GeV/fm^3.

## Context
This work extends earlier parton cascade approaches (Molnar 2001) and viscous hydrodynamics calculations (Song 2009) by providing a covariant transport framework valid at large shear viscosity to entropy ratio and at intermediate transverse momentum, where the deviation from equilibrium delta f/f_eq becomes large (proportional to pT^2). The finding that v4/v2^2 is sensitive to the temperature dependence of eta/s addresses the observation at RHIC that this ratio is about 0.8-0.9, larger than ideal hydrodynamics predictions of about 0.5. The result that NJL chiral dynamics does not modify the relation between v2 and eta/s, once eta/s is fixed, validates the use of simpler cascade models for extracting the shear viscosity to entropy ratio from elliptic flow data.
