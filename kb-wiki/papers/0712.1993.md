# Comment on "Hadronic $^3$He$η$ production near threshold"

> Measurements of the differential and total cross sections for the $p d \to ^3$He $η$ reaction at five energies were recently reported [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 75}, 014004 (2007)] and comparisons with theoretical models were made. We point out that these comparisons involved a model based on ad hoc assumptions and hence the conclusions regarding the reaction mechanism as well as the role of the higher partial waves drawn in the above work are misleading. Revised conclusions based on better model calculations are presented.

## Key claim

The two-step model for the $p d \rightarrow \,^3$He $\eta$ reaction, when calculated without ad hoc restrictions on the intermediate pion propagation, cannot reproduce the forward-peaked angular distributions at high energies, contrary to claims in Phys. Rev. C 75, 014004 (2007) that relied on comparisons with a model containing unjustified assumptions.

## Method

The authors performed a two-step model calculation where the $\eta$ meson is produced via the sequential reactions $pp\rightarrow \pi d$ and $\pi N\rightarrow \eta N$, incorporating an integral over all momenta of the intermediate off-shell particles and including the $\eta\,^3$He final state interaction through a solution of few-body equations in the finite rank approximation. They compared their results with total cross section data from Mayer et al. and the COSY-ANKE experiment, and examined the role of $s$, $p$, and $d$ partial waves in the intermediate $\pi N \rightarrow \eta N$ process for angular distributions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section data for $p d \rightarrow \,^3$He $\eta$ at five energies near threshold (from Phys. Rev. C 75, 014004) are compared with two-step model calculations using $\eta N$ scattering lengths of $(0.75,0.27)$ fm and $(0.88,0.41)$ fm.
- The theoretical angular distribution at threshold (proton kinetic energy $T_p = 891$ MeV) is isotropic, but becomes backward peaked already at an excess energy of $Q = 5$ MeV.
- The $\eta\,^3$He scattering lengths from the authors' model are $a_{\eta \,3He} = (1.99, 5.99)$ fm and $(2.14,5.71)$ fm for the two $\eta N$ parameter sets, compared to $(4.2\pm 0.5, 0.4\pm1.9)$ fm reported in Phys. Rev. C 75, 014004 and $|a_{\eta \,3He}|=4.3\pm0.5$ fm from Smyrski et al.

## Context

This comment extends the earlier two-step model work of Khemchandani et al. (2003, 2005) which successfully reproduced threshold data from Mayer et al. and the COSY-ANKE experiment, but contradicts the conclusions of Adam et al. (2007) regarding the success of the two-step model and the role of higher partial waves. The authors show that the forward-peaked angular distributions at high energies cannot be reproduced without imposing an ad hoc restriction on intermediate pion propagation to angles less than 10 degrees, and caution that the scattering length deduced by Adam et al. differs significantly from their few-body calculation results.
