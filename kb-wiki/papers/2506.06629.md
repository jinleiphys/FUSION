# Charge symmetry breaking effects of $ω$-$ρ^0$ mixing in relativistic mean-field model

> We present a relativistic mean-field model that incorporates charge symmetry breaking (CSB) of nuclear force via $ ω$-$ ρ^0 $ meson mixing, along with corrections to the electromagnetic interaction including the nucleon form factors, first-order vacuum polarization, and Coulomb exchange and pairing terms. The model parameters are refitted using the mass differences of $ T = 1/2 $ mirror nuclei and ground-state properties of magic nuclei, yielding DD-ME-CSB parameter set. The DD-ME-CSB parameter set reproduces the mass differences of mirror nuclei reasonably well up to $ T = 2 $, demonstrating the importance of $ ω$-$ ρ^0 $ mixing. A connection of the present model to a Skyrme-type CSB interaction is also established through a gradient expansion of the energy density functional.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic mean-field model that incorporates charge symmetry breaking (CSB) via ω-ρ⁰ meson mixing, and demonstrates that this mixing is the dominant origin of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei mass differences, but the mixing strength Δm²ᵥ ≈ 0.07 fm⁻² is refitted from data rather than taken from first principles, and the model's agreement with experiment is best for the fitted T=1/2 systems while deviations tend to be smaller for heavier nuclei within each isospin group.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model starting from the DD-ME2 Lagrangian, adding ω-ρ⁰ mixing in the vector-meson mass term, nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and first-order vacuum polarization from electron loops. The model parameters including the mixing strength Δm²ᵥ are optimized to binding energies and charge radii of six magic nuclei and mass differences of six T=1/2 mirror pairs, yielding the DD-ME-CSB parameter set; additional parameter sets with fixed Δm²ᵥ values (0.00, 0.05, 0.10, 0.15 fm⁻²) are also generated for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Δm²ᵥ = 7.206445 × 10⁻² fm⁻² for the DD-ME-CSB parameter set, comparable to empirical estimates of 0.087 ± 0.01 fm⁻² (Coon 1977) and 0.116 ± 0.02 fm⁻² (Coon 1987)
- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.147 fm⁻³, energy per nucleon e₀,₀ = -15.88 MeV, incompressibility K_∞ = 276.92 MeV for DD-ME-CSB
- Symmetry energy parameters: J_sym = 35.9 MeV, L_sym = 84.3 MeV for DD-ME-CSB
- CSB parameters: J_CSB = -0.568 MeV, L_CSB = -1.26 MeV for DD-ME-CSB
- Neutron-proton effective mass ratio: m*_n/m_n = 0.566, m*_p/m_p = 0.565 for DD-ME-CSB
- Ratio Δm²ᵥ/m²_ω ≈ Δm²ᵥ/m²_ρ ≈ 1/220, similar to the fine-structure constant

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory approach to charge symmetry breaking, which has previously been studied mainly in Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations (Naito 2023, Baczyk 2018, 2019). The model establishes a connection to Skyrme-type CSB interactions through gradient expansion of the energy density functional. The results enable systematic calculations of mirror nuclei mass differences up to T=2 and provide a framework for studying the density dependence of the ω-ρ⁰ mixing strength, which the authors suggest may be density dependent due to many-body effects.
