# Searching for exotic states in the $N πK $ system

> We study the $N πK$ system in order to investigate the possibility of existence of strangeness +1 resonance(s). The formalism consists of solving the Faddeev equations with the $N π$, $πK $ and $K N$ $t$-matrices obtained from chiral dynamics. The same formalism, which leads to the finding of several $1/2^+$ resonances in the corresponding three-body $S$ = -1 channels in the 1400-2000 MeV energy region, results into only one broad bump around 1700 MeV with isospin 0. The amplitudes in isospin 1 and 2 configuration do not have any resonant structure.

# Searching for exotic states in the $N\pi K$ system

## Key claim
The authors find no resonant structure in the $N\pi K$ system that could correspond to the claimed $\Theta^+$ pentaquark near 1540 MeV, but they do obtain a broad bump around 1720 MeV in isospin 0 whose shape is far from a Breit-Wigner and may not correspond to a true pole in the complex plane.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the $N\pi K$ three-body system, using two-body $t$-matrices for $N\pi$, $\pi K$, and $KN$ obtained from chiral Lagrangians via unitarized chiral perturbation theory. The integral Faddeev equations are reduced to a coupled system of algebraic equations by exploiting the cancellation between off-shell two-body contributions and three-body contact terms from the same chiral Lagrangians, with all amplitudes projected to $S$-wave giving total $J^P = 1/2^+$.

## Key numbers
- No resonant structure is found near 1540 MeV, ruling out the interpretation of the $\Theta^+$ as an $N\pi K$ bound state with all interactions in $s$-wave.
- A broad bump appears around 1720 MeV in the isospin 0 amplitude, with a full width at half maximum of approximately 200 MeV.
- The calculation covers the energy range 1500 MeV $< \sqrt{s} <$ 2100 MeV and invariant mass of the $\pi K$ subsystem 600 $< \sqrt{s_{23}} <$ 1100 MeV.
- The amplitudes in isospin 1 and 2 configurations show no resonant structure.

## Context
This work extends the same Faddeev formalism previously applied to the $S = -1$ sector (Martinez Torres, 2007; Martinez Torres, 2008), which dynamically generated several $1/2^+$ resonances in the 1400-2000 MeV region identified with known $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ resonances, and to the $S = 0$ sector (Khemchandani, 2008) which produced the $N^*(1710)$. The present study finds no bound state in the $S = +1$ channel, contradicting earlier suggestions by Bicudo (2003) and Llanes-Estrada (2003) that the $\Theta^+$ could be a $K\pi N$ bound state. The broad bump around 1720 MeV may correspond to a structure seen in $K^+N$ scattering data in the $P_{01}$ partial wave (Hyslop, 1992) and in time delay analysis (Kelkar, 2003), and the authors suggest it should be observable in $KN \rightarrow \pi K N$ reactions producing $\pi K N$ in isospin 0.
