# Isospin breaking in the vector current of the nucleon

> Extraction of the nucleon's strange form factors from experimental data requires a quantitative understanding of the unavoidable contamination from isospin violation. A number of authors have addressed this issue during the past decade, and their work is reviewed here. The predictions from early models are largely consistent with recent results that rely as much as possible on input from QCD symmetries and related experimental data. The resulting bounds on isospin violation are sufficiently precise to be of value to on-going experimental and theoretical studies of the nucleon's strange form factors.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical studies of isospin violation in the nucleon's vector form factors, concluding that the resulting bounds are sufficiently precise to be of value for extracting strange form factors from experimental data, but the actual numerical predictions carry significant uncertainties, particularly from the poorly-known omega-nucleon coupling \(\kappa_\omega\).

## Method

The author reviews a chronological series of theoretical approaches: constituent quark models (Dmitrašinović and Pollock 1996; Miller 1996), a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model (Ma 1996), chiral perturbation theory (Lewis and Mobed 2000), and finally chiral perturbation theory combined with resonance saturation and dispersion analysis (Kubis and Lewis 2006). The resonance saturation approach uses \(\rho-\omega\) mixing and vector-meson-nucleon couplings extracted from dispersive analyses of nucleon electromagnetic form factors to determine the unknown low-energy constants in the chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark model (Dmitrašinović and Pollock): \(G_M^{u,d}(0) = 0\) exactly; fractional change in squared charge radius \(\delta\langle r_E^2\rangle/\langle r_E^2\rangle = 1.1\%\); fractional change in squared magnetic radius \(\delta\langle r_M^2\rangle/\langle r_M^2\rangle = 0.4\%\).
- Constituent quark model (Miller): isospin violating contributions vanish at \(q^2=0\) and are less than 0.2% in magnitude for a momentum transfer of 0.1 GeV\(^2\).
- Light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model (Ma): \(G_M^{u,d}(0) \approx 0.006\) to 0.088 (nuclear magnetons), with the range reflecting uncertainty in the Gaussian width parameter \(\alpha\).
- Chiral perturbation theory with resonance saturation (Kubis and Lewis): \(\Theta_{\rho\omega} = (-3.75 \pm 0.36) \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\); \(\rho\) couplings \(4.0 < g_\rho < 6.2\) and \(5.1 < \kappa_\rho < 6.8\); \(\omega\) couplings \(41.8 < g_\omega < 43.0\) and \(-0.16 < \kappa_\omega < 0.57\); the range of \(G_M^{u,d}(0)\) does not include zero.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on isospin violation in nucleon form factors, beginning with constituent quark models by Dmitrašinović and Pollock (1996) and Miller (1996), and a light-cone model by Ma (1996). It then incorporates chiral perturbation theory (Lewis and Mobed 2000) and resolves the unknown low-energy constants via resonance saturation and dispersion analysis (Kubis and Lewis 2006). The results enable more precise extraction of the nucleon's strange form factors from experiments such as SAMPLE, A4, HAPPEX, and G0, by providing a theoretical error bar for the isospin-violating contamination.
