# Early-Time Dynamics of Heavy-Ion Collisions through Energy Correlators: celestial blocks and the spacetime structure of out-of-equilibrium QCD matter

> Ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions provide a unique window into far-from-equilibrium states of QCD matter. The initial stages of these events are characterized by highly anisotropic, nonthermal dynamics that precede hydrodynamization, yet they remain largely inaccessible through conventional soft observables. In this work, we show that the substructure of mid-rapidity jets provides direct sensitivity to the spacetime structure of this early, anisotropic phase. Using classical Yang-Mills simulations and effective kinetic theory to model the early-time evolution of the jet quenching parameter, we compute the azimuthally differential energy-energy correlator within the BDMPS-Z framework. By decomposing the result into celestial blocks, we isolate the coefficients that encode the anisotropic geometry and dynamics of the underlying medium. We identify an observable that couples directly to spatial anisotropies in the out-of-equilibrium QCD matter and also discuss the impact of medium response on its behavior. We further extend our study to mid-rapidity jets generated with the JEWEL Monte-Carlo, adjusted to incorporate an anisotropic medium background, and find qualitative agreement with the analytical expectations. Finally, we discuss how higher-point energy correlators and generalized energy-flow operators can enhance the sensitivity to the microscopic structure of far-from-equilibrium QCD matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the azimuthally differential energy-energy correlator (EEC) inside mid-rapidity jets can directly probe the anisotropic, out-of-equilibrium spacetime structure of the earliest stages of heavy-ion collisions, provided the observable is constructed to isolate contributions from the initial-state geometry (e.g., via a "clover" subtraction) rather than relying on the full EEC which is dominated by vacuum and isotropic medium contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the azimuthally differential EEC within the BDMPS-Z framework for medium-induced radiation, using classical Yang-Mills simulations for the Glasma stage and effective kinetic theory (EKT) for the subsequent pre-equilibrium evolution to model the time-dependent, anisotropic jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\). They decompose the EEC into celestial blocks (light-ray operator product expansion coefficients) to isolate the transverse-spin contributions that encode the medium anisotropy, and define a "clover" observable as the difference between EEC integrated over vertical and horizontal azimuthal sectors relative to the beam axis. They also perform Monte-Carlo simulations with the JEWEL event generator modified to include an anisotropic medium background for qualitative comparison.

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum is fixed to \(Q_s = 2\) GeV for the Glasma simulations (corresponding to most central LHC collisions) and to \(Q_s = 1.4\) GeV for the matched EKT data.
* The transition region between Glasma and EKT descriptions is taken over \(\tau \in (0.04, 0.3)\) fm/c, connected via a cubic Hermite interpolant.
* The leading term in the clover EEC is of order \(\mathcal{O}(\xi \, \chi^3)\), where \(\xi\) is the anisotropy strength and \(\chi\) is the angular separation in the EEC.
* The expansion of the \(2n\)-th harmonic coefficient \(v_{2n}^{\rm EEC}\) in powers of anisotropy \(\xi\) starts at power \(\xi^n\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Barata et al. (2023) on using energy correlators to probe jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions, and builds on recent numerical extractions of the anisotropic jet quenching parameter in the Glasma by Ipp et al. (2020) and Avramescu et al. (2023) and in kinetic theory by Boguslavski et al. (2024). It connects to the celestial block decomposition of the EEC developed by Kologlu et al. (2019) and Chen et al. (2025), and to the light-ray OPE formalism of Chang et al. (2020). The results enable future experimental searches for initial-state anisotropy signals in jet substructure at the LHC, and motivate further theoretical work on higher-point energy correlators and generalized energy-flow operators to enhance sensitivity to the microscopic structure of far-from-equilibrium QCD matter.
