# Scaling Behavior of the Azimuthal and Centrality Dependencies of Jet Production in Heavy-ion Collisions

> For heavy-ion collisions at RHIC a scaling behavior is found in the dependencies on azimuthal angle $ϕ$ and impact parameter $b$ for pion production at high $p_T$ essentially independent of the hadronization process. The scaling variable is in terms of a dynamical path length $ξ$ that takes into account detailed properties of geometry, medium density and probability of hard scattering. It is shown in the recombination model how the nuclear modification factor depends on the average $\barξ(ϕ,b)$. The data for $π^0$ production at $p_T=$ 4-5 and 7-8 GeV/c at RHIC are shown to exhibit the same scaling behavior as found in the model calculation. Extension to back-to-back dijet production has been carried out, showing the existence of $\barξ$ scaling also in the away-side yield per trigger. At LHC the hard-parton density can be high enough to realize the likelihood of recombination of shower partons arising from neighboring jets. It is shown that such 2-jet recombination can cause strong violation of $\barξ$ scaling. Furthermore, the large value of $R_{AA}$ that exceeds 1 can become a striking signature of such a hadronization process at high energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for pion production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC exhibits a universal scaling behavior when plotted against the average dynamical path length $\bar\xi(\phi,b)$, which depends on both azimuthal angle and impact parameter, but this scaling is only approximate and holds for centralities above 0-10% and for $p_T > 4$ GeV/c, with the caveat that at LHC energies 2-jet recombination can cause strong violation of this scaling.

## Method

The authors use the recombination model to calculate pion production, separating the hadronization process into thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), and shower-shower (SS) components. They define a dynamical path length $\xi = \gamma\ell$, where $\ell$ is the integral of the local medium density along the hard parton trajectory from its creation point to the elliptical boundary of the collision zone, weighted by the nuclear overlap function $Q(x_0,y_0,b)$. The probability distribution $P(\xi,\phi,b)$ is computed from Glauber model geometry, and the momentum degradation of hard partons is modeled as $G(k,q,\xi) = q\delta(q-ke^{-\xi})$, leading to a simple exponential suppression of the parton momentum.

## Key numbers

- $\gamma = 0.11$ is the parameter relating dynamical path length $\xi$ to the density-weighted geometrical path length $\ell$, determined by fitting RHIC data
- For $p_T = 4.5$ GeV/c, $R_{AA}^\pi(\bar\xi) = 1.22(1+\bar\xi/0.69)^{-2.86}$ fits the scaling curve, with $\bar\xi$ ranging up to approximately 0.65
- For $p_T = 7.5$ GeV/c, $R_{AA}^\pi(\bar\xi) = 1.25(1+\bar\xi/0.58)^{-2.62}$ describes the scaling behavior
- The nuclear radius parameters used are $r_0 = 6.45$ fm and $\delta = 0.55$ fm for Au, with effective nuclear radius $R_A = 7$ fm
- The thermal parton distribution uses $T = 0.317$ GeV and $a = 0.47$ in Eq. (39), with $C(N_{\rm part}) = 1.1 N_{\rm part}^{0.54}$ GeV$^{-1}$
- The lower limit of the path length integral is set to $t_0 = 0.025$ (corresponding to less than 0.2 fm)

## Context

This work extends the study of the surface factor $S(\phi,b)$ from Hwa and Zhang (earlier work) that related impact parameter and azimuthal angle for low $p_T$ pion production and ridge yields. It provides a geometrical simplification of the complex dependencies of $R_{AA}$ on centrality and azimuthal angle by introducing the dynamical path length $\bar\xi$, enabling a universal description of jet quenching effects across different experimental conditions. The scaling behavior found here enables future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC, and the prediction of strong $\bar\xi$ scaling violation at LHC due to 2-jet recombination could serve as a signature of that hadronization mechanism.
