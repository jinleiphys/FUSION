# Evolution of the symmetry energy of hot neutron-rich matter formed in heavy-ion reactions

> It is shown that the experimentally observed decrease of the nuclear symmetry energy with the increasing centrality or the excitation energy in isotopic scaling analyses of heavy-ion reactions can be well understood analytically within a degenerate Fermi gas model. The evolution of the symmetry energy is found to be mainly due to the variation in the freeze-out density rather than temperature. The isoscaling analyses are useful for probing the interaction part of the nuclear symmetry energy, provided that both the freeze-out temperature and density of the fragments can be inferred simultaneously from the experiments.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed decrease of the nuclear symmetry energy with increasing centrality or excitation energy in heavy-ion reactions can be understood analytically within a degenerate Fermi gas model, and this evolution is mainly due to the variation in freeze-out density rather than temperature.

## Method

The authors used the degenerate Fermi gas limit of the Mekjian, Lee, and Zamick (MLZ) thermal model with Skyrme interactions to calculate the kinetic and interaction parts of the symmetry energy as functions of density and temperature. They compared their calculated symmetry energy values with experimental isoscaling data from the SJY group at Texas A&M University and the INDRA-ALADIN collaboration at GSI, using two different interaction parameterizations (labeled x=0 and x=-1) that are consistent with constraints from isospin diffusion and neutron-skin measurements.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy of cold nuclear matter at normal density is about 25 MeV, decreasing to values as low as 16-19 MeV in central collisions
- For the TAMU-SJY experiments (25 MeV/nucleon 86Kr and 64Ni beams), the symmetry energy decreased from about 25 MeV to 19 MeV as temperature increased from about 4.8 MeV to 5.8 MeV
- For the INDRA@GSI experiments (300 MeV/nucleon 12C on 112,124Sn targets), the symmetry energy decreased from about 26 MeV to 16 MeV as temperature increased from about 6 MeV to 9 MeV
- Using the x=0 interaction, the hottest point required an average freeze-out density of about 0.62ρ₀ for TAMU-SJY data and 0.49ρ₀ for INDRA@GSI data
- Using the x=-1 interaction, the corresponding freeze-out densities were about 0.8ρ₀ and 0.68ρ₀, respectively
- The temperature-dependent part of the symmetry energy can reach up to about 35% of the zero-temperature symmetry energy at ρ=0.1ρ₀ for the stiffer symmetry energy parameterization

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of isoscaling analyses performed by Shetty et al. (2005) and the INDRA-ALADIN collaboration (2004-2005), which observed decreasing symmetry energy with increasing centrality. The authors reconcile these observations with the degenerate Fermi gas model and show that the apparent temperature dependence is actually driven by density changes. The results enable future extraction of the interaction part of the nuclear symmetry energy from isoscaling analyses, provided that both freeze-out temperature and density can be inferred simultaneously from experiments, potentially using fragment correlation functions or source imaging techniques.
