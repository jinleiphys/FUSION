# Charm elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> Using the perturbative method for the simulation of charmed particles, the dynamical origin of charm quark elliptic flow is studied in the framework of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. Besides the expected ordering relative to that of light quarks according to quark masses, charm quark elliptic flow is seen to be sensitive to the parton scattering cross section. To describe the observed large elliptic flow of electrons from the decay of charmed mesons, a charm quark elastic scattering cross section much larger than that estimated from the perturbative QCD is required.

# Charm elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

Charm quark elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is sensitive to the parton scattering cross section, and describing the observed large elliptic flow of electrons from charmed meson decay requires a charm quark elastic scattering cross section much larger than that estimated from perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with string melting, incorporating a perturbative method to simulate charmed particles by introducing an enhancement factor for their production from initial hard scattering. Charm quarks were generated from D meson spectra measured in d+Au collisions by STAR, then propagated through the partonic system using the ZPC model with elastic scatterings, and finally recombined with light quarks into D mesons via a coordinate-space coalescence model.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark elliptic flow saturates to about the same value at larger transverse momentum compared with light quarks, with a strong mass ordering observed
- A larger charmed meson elliptic flow of about 10% at p_t = 2 GeV/c is obtained when an isotropic instead of screened Coulomb cross section is used for charm quark scattering
- Parton cross sections of 3 mb (similar to perturbative QCD) and 10 mb (needed for light quark observables) were used in the study
- Only about two charm quark pairs are produced in the mid-rapidity region of central Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energies
- The available data show charm elliptic flow of more than 10%, requiring effects beyond perturbative QCD

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charm quark interactions in the quark-gluon plasma, including the PYTHIA model and blastwave hydrodynamic model (Batsouli, 2002) which found the electron transverse momentum spectrum insensitive to charm final-state interactions, and the coalescence model (Lin, 2003; Greco, 2003) which assumed thermal equilibrium between charm and light quarks. The results are consistent with other recent studies (vanHees, 2004; Chen, 2004; Molnar, 2004; Bratkovskaya, 2004; Moore, 2004; Djordjevic, 2005; Petreczky, 2005; vanHees, 2005) showing that charm elliptic flow is sensitive to final-state interactions and that a large elliptic flow requires a charm scattering cross section larger than perturbative QCD predictions. The findings enable further investigation of heavy quark elliptic flow at high transverse momentum, where radiative energy loss and fragmentation effects become important.
