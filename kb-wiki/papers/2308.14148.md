# Production of the newly observed $\bar{T}_{c\bar{s}0}$ by kaon-induced reactions on a proton/neutron target

> Recently, a new doubly charged tetraquark $T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{++}$ and its neutral partner $T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^0$ at the invariant mass spectrum of $πD_s$ were observed by the LHCb Collaboration. According to its properties, such as the mass and decay width, the $T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{++/0}$ have been suggested to be a compact multi-quark state or a hadron molecule. In order to distinguish the various interpretations of the $T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{++/0}$, we investigate the possibility to study the $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$ [the antiparticle of $T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$] by kaon-induced reactions on a proton target in an effective Lagrangian approach. The production mechanism is characterized by the $t$-channel $D$ meson exchange. Our theoretical approach is based on the assumption that $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$ can be either a $K^{*}D^{*}-D_s^{*}ρ$ molecule or a compact tetraquark state. Using the coupling constants of the $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$ to $KD$ channel obtained from molecule or compact tetraquark picture of the $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$, we compute the cross-sections for the process $K^{-}n\to{}\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{--}Λ^{+}_c$. The $\bar{K}N$ initial state interaction mediated by Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges is also included, which reduces the production of the $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$. Our calculations show that whether $\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$ is a molecule or a compact tetraquark state, the cross-sections for the $K^{-}n\to{}\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{--}Λ^{+}_c$ reaction are of similar magnitude, ranging from approximately 0.075 nb to 0.270 nb.

## Key claim

The production cross-sections for the antiparticle of the newly observed tetraquark, \(\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)\), in kaon-induced reactions on a neutron target are of similar magnitude whether the state is a \(K^{*}D^{*}-D_s^{*}\rho\) molecule or a compact tetraquark, but a clearer distinction emerges from the subsequent decay into \(\pi^{-}D^{-}_s\Lambda^{+}_c\), where the cross-section for the compact tetraquark assignment (0.122 nb) is significantly larger than for the molecular assignment (up to \(1.83\times10^{-3}\) nb).

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the cross-section for the \(K^{-}n \to \bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{--}\Lambda^{+}_c\) reaction, assuming a \(t\)-channel \(D^{-}\) meson exchange mechanism. The coupling constant for the \(\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}KD\) vertex was derived from theoretical predictions of the partial decay width \(\Gamma(T^{++}_{c\bar{s}0} \to K^{+}D^{+})\) for both a compact tetraquark state (56.8 ± 33.4 MeV from Lian (2023)) and a \(K^{*}D^{*}-D_s^{*}\rho\) molecular state (with decay widths from Duan (2023) for two pole masses). The \(\bar{K}N\) initial state interaction was included using a parameterization with Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges from Lebiedowicz (2011).

## Key numbers

* For the compact tetraquark assignment with cutoff \(\alpha=1.5\), the cross-section for \(K^{-}n \to \bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{--}\Lambda^{+}_c\) ranges from approximately 0.134 nb to 0.127 nb for beam momenta from 23.25 GeV to 26.80 GeV.
* For the compact tetraquark assignment with cutoff \(\alpha=1.7\), the cross-section ranges from 0.169 nb to 0.160 nb over the same beam momentum range.
* For the molecular assignment with bound state mass \(m=2885\) MeV and cutoff \(\alpha=1.5\), the cross-section at beam momentum \(P_{K^{-}}=25.0\) GeV ranges from 0.156 nb to 0.175 nb.
* For the molecular assignment with bound state mass \(m=2887\) MeV and cutoff \(\alpha=1.5\), the cross-section at \(P_{K^{-}}=25.0\) GeV is about 0.034 nb, roughly 6-8 times smaller than for \(m=2885\) MeV.
* For the sequential reaction \(K^{-}n \to \bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)^{--}\Lambda^{+}_c \to \pi^{-}D^{-}_s\Lambda^{+}_c\), the cross-section for the compact tetraquark assignment reaches 0.122 nb, while for the molecular assignment it reaches up to \(1.83\times10^{-3}\) nb.

## Context

This work extends the study of the \(T^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)\) states observed by LHCb (2022) by proposing a production mechanism to distinguish between the molecular interpretation (proposed by Chen (2022) and Duan (2023)) and the compact tetraquark interpretation (supported by Liu (2022) and Lian (2023)). The results enable future experiments at facilities such as OKA@U-70, SPS@CERN, CERN/AMBER, and J-PARC to test the internal structure of the \(\bar{T}^{a}_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)\) by comparing measured cross-sections with these predictions.
