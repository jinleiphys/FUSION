# New mean field theory with the parity and charge mixing for the pion in nuclei

> We give a brief review on the important role of the pion-exchange interaction characterized by the strong tensor force. In order to illustrate the role of the pion, we have proposed a new mean field theory with the charge and parity mixing, by which the pion is treated explicitly in the mean field framework. We introduce the essence of the new mean field theory and illustrate the calculated results of the pion (tensor force) correlation by taking the alpha particle as an example.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a mean field theory can incorporate the pion-exchange interaction (tensor force) explicitly by breaking and then projecting parity and charge symmetries of single-particle orbits, but the authors note that the parity-breaking mean field is "fragile" because the critical coupling constant for a finite pion mean field lies within 0.92 to 0.97 of the free-space pion-nucleon coupling constant, meaning various neglected effects could influence its realization.

## Method

The authors formulate a charge- and parity-projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) scheme where single-particle orbits are constructed as mixtures of four components (proton positive-parity, proton negative-parity, neutron positive-parity, neutron negative-parity), and the total wave function is obtained by projecting onto good charge and parity. They apply this scheme to the alpha particle using an effective interaction that separates the tensor force contribution from the central and other components, solving the variational equation after projection.

## Key numbers

- For the alpha particle ground state (0+), the CPPHF scheme yields a total energy of -28.19 MeV, compared to -16.12 MeV for Hartree-Fock (HF) and -21.09 MeV for parity-projected Hartree-Fock (PPHF).
- The tensor force expectation value in the alpha particle is -30.59 MeV for CPPHF, versus 0.00 MeV for HF and -10.91 MeV for PPHF.
- The D-state probability (negative-parity component) in the alpha particle is 16% for CPPHF, compared to 0% for HF and 8% for PPHF.
- The root-mean-square matter radius of the alpha particle is 1.37 fm for CPPHF, versus 1.63 fm for HF and 1.50 fm for PPHF.
- The critical pion-nucleon coupling constant for various closed-shell nuclei (except 12C) lies in the range 0.92 g_pi^0 to 0.97 g_pi^0, where g_pi^0 is the free-space coupling constant.
- In the ATMS variational calculation of the alpha particle, the tensor force contributes -69.7 MeV to the potential energy, and the D-state probability is 12.8%.

## Context

This work extends the reaction matrix (G-matrix) theory developed by Brueckner and the variational calculations of light nuclei by the Argonne-Illinois Group (Wiringa, 2000) and the ATMS method (Akaishi, 1986), which demonstrated that the one-pion-exchange interaction contributes 70-80% of the two-body interaction strength in light nuclei. The CPPHF framework aims to provide a mean-field-level description of the tensor correlation that previously required explicit treatment of 2p-2h configurations, potentially enabling systematic studies of diverse nuclear structures such as shell, cluster, and halo configurations.
