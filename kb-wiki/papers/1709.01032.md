# Collective excitations of a hot anisotropic QCD medium with Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook collisional kernel within an effective description

> Collective modes of an anisotropic hot QCD medium have been studied within the semi-classical transport theory employing Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel. The modeling of the isotropic medium is primarily based on a recent quasi-particle description of hot QCD equation of state where the medium effects have been encoded in effective gluon and quark/anti-quark momentum distributions that posses non-trivial energy dispersions. The anisotropic distribution functions are obtained in a straightforward the way by stretching or squeezing the isotropic ones along one of the directions. The gluon self-energy is computed using these distribution functions in a linearized transport equation with Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel. Further, the tensor decomposition of gluon self-energy leads to the structure functions which eventually controls the dispersion relations and the collective mode structure of the medium. It has been seen that both the medium effects and collisions induce appreciable modifications to the collective modes and plasma excitations in the hot QCD medium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a hot anisotropic QCD medium described by the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel, there exist three collective modes (labeled A, G1, G2) in the small-anisotropy limit when propagation is allowed in all directions relative to the anisotropy vector, of which two modes (A and G1) can be unstable; this extends prior work by Schenke (2006) which found only two modes and only one unstable mode when restricting propagation to the anisotropy direction.

## Method

The authors employ semi-classical linearized transport theory with a BGK collisional kernel to compute the gluon self-energy for an anisotropic hot QCD medium, using the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to describe the isotropic equilibrium state with lattice and HTL-inspired equations of state. The anisotropic distribution functions are obtained by stretching or squeezing the isotropic distributions along one direction via the anisotropy parameter ξ, and the gluon self-energy is decomposed into four structure functions (α, β, γ, δ) in the weak-anisotropy limit (ξ < 1). The poles of the propagator are then analyzed to determine the dispersion relations and stability of collective modes as functions of wave vector k, collision frequency ν, anisotropy parameter ξ, and the angle θ_n between the propagation vector and anisotropy direction.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass expression: m_D^2 = 4π α_s(T) T^2 ( (2N_c/π^2) PolyLog[2, z_g] - (2N_f/π^2) PolyLog[2, -z_q] ), where z_g and z_q are effective fugacities for gluons and quarks
- Temperature parameters: T_c = 0.17 GeV, T = 0.25 GeV used in numerical calculations
- Anisotropy parameter: ξ = 0.2 used for dispersion curve calculations
- Collision frequency ν expressed in units of ω_p^LO (leading-order plasma frequency)
- Normalization constant for anisotropic distribution: C_ξ = √|ξ| / arctan(√|ξ|), with small-ξ expansion C_ξ = 1 + ξ/3 + O(ξ²)

## Context

This work extends the collisionless anisotropic QCD plasma analysis of Jamal (2017) by incorporating BGK collisional effects, and generalizes the study by Schenke (2006) which only considered modes propagating along the anisotropy direction. The results enable more realistic modeling of collective excitations in the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where both momentum anisotropy and collisions are present, and provide a framework for studying how lattice and HTL-inspired equations of state modify plasma instabilities.
