# Swelling of nuclei embedded in neutron-gas and consequences for fusion

> Fusion of very neutron rich nuclei may be important to determine the composition and heating of the crust of accreting neutron stars. We present an exploratory study of the effect of the neutron-gas environment on the structure of nuclei and the consequences for pycnonuclear fusion cross-sections in the neutron drip region. We studied the formation and properties of Oxygen and Calcium isotopes embedded in varying neutron-gas densities. We observe that the formed isotope is the drip-line nucleus for the given effective interaction. Increasing the neutron-gas density leads to the swelling of the nuclear density. We have used these densities to study the effect of this swelling on the fusion cross-sections using the São-Paulo potential. At high neutron-gas densities the cross-section is substantially increased but at lower densities the modification is minimal.

## Key claim
The presence of a dense neutron-gas background causes the bound nuclear density to swell, which lowers the ion-ion fusion barrier and can substantially increase pycnonuclear fusion cross-sections, but only at neutron-gas densities above approximately 10^13 g/cm^3; at lower densities the modification to the cross-section is minimal.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations in a 3D Cartesian box with periodic boundary conditions, using the Skyrme SLy4 effective interaction, to simulate a single nucleus (either an Oxygen or Calcium isotope) embedded in a uniform neutron gas of varying density. The resulting self-consistent density profiles for the bound nucleus were then used as input to the Sao Paulo double-folding model to compute the ion-ion potential, and fusion cross-sections were obtained via the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC) method for barrier penetration.

## Key numbers
- For the Z=8 system (28O), the free-space barrier peak is 7.87 MeV at R=10.7 fm; at the highest neutron-gas density studied, the barrier drops to 6.39 MeV and moves outward to R=13.3 fm.
- For the Z=20 system (60Ca), the free-space barrier peak is 47.4 MeV at R=11.3 fm; at the highest neutron-gas density, the barrier drops to 37.4 MeV and moves outward to R=14.3 fm.
- The peak total density of the 28O nucleus decreases from 0.16 fm^-3 in free space to 0.068 fm^-3 at the highest neutron-gas density (1020 neutron states).
- The peak total density of the 60Ca nucleus decreases from 0.165 fm^-3 in free space to 0.048 fm^-3 at the highest neutron-gas density (1040 neutron states).
- For the lowest neutron-gas density studied (2.9 x 10^12 g/cm^3), the astrophysical S-factor for 28O+28O fusion differs from the free-space value by about a factor of two at a center-of-mass energy of 2 MeV.
- The neutron drip region is estimated to occur for densities in the approximate range of a few x 10^11 to 8 x 10^13 g/cm^3.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of pycnonuclear fusion in neutron star crusts, such as the molecular dynamics simulations of Horowitz et al. (2008) which found that 24O+24O can fuse near 10^11 g/cm^3, and the barrier penetration model calculations of Beard et al. (2010) and Afanasjev et al. (2012). The key advance is that the neutron gas background is included microscopically and self-consistently, rather than by empirically adjusting the barrier height and width as was done in Afanasjev et al. (2012). The results enable more realistic modeling of the composition and heating of accreting neutron star crusts, though the authors note that future work should include pairing effects and effective interactions tailored for neutron star crusts, as in Erler et al. (2013).
