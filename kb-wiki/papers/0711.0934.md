# Time-Depentent Hartree-Fock description of heavy ions fusion

> A microscopic mean-field description of heavy ions fusion is performed in the framework of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory using a Skyrme interaction with the SLy4d parametrization. A good agreement with experiments is obtained on the position of the fusion barriers for various total masses, mass asymmetries and deformations. The excitation function of the 16O+208Pb is overestimated by about 16% above the barrier. The restriction to an independent particles state in the mean-field dynamics prevents the description of sub-barrier fusion. Effect of transfer on fusion is discussed.

## Key claim

The Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, using the SLy4d Skyrme parametrization, reproduces experimental fusion barriers for a wide range of heavy-ion systems without adjustable reaction parameters, but the restriction to a single independent-particle state prevents description of sub-barrier fusion and leads to an overestimate of the above-barrier fusion cross section for 16O+208Pb by about 16%.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations in three dimensions with a Skyrme energy functional (SLy4d parametrization) using a code with a plane of symmetry (the collision plane). For each system, they prepared the two collision partners in their Hartree-Fock ground states at an initial separation of 44.8 fm, gave them initial velocities from a Rutherford trajectory, and evolved the single Slater determinant in time. For spherical systems, the fusion barrier was determined by scanning head-on collision energies to find the threshold between non-fusion and fusion; for deformed systems, barriers were computed for extreme orientations (parallel and perpendicular to the collision axis).

## Key numbers

- Fusion barrier for 16O+208Pb: V_B^TDHF = 74.445 ± 0.005 MeV, in excellent agreement with the experimental value V_B^exp ≈ 74.5 MeV.
- The frozen-density approximation with the same interaction gives V_B^frozen = 76.55 MeV at R_B^frozen = 11.73 fm, overestimating the experimental barrier by about 2 MeV.
- The Bass barrier for 16O+208Pb is V_B^Bass = 77.10 MeV at R_B^Bass = 11.42 fm.
- Just below the barrier for 16O+208Pb, almost two protons and no neutron, on average, are transferred from 16O to 208Pb.
- The di-nuclear system formed near the barrier for 16O+208Pb persists for about 400 fm/c.
- The grid step size is 0.8 fm and the time step is 0.45 fm/c.
- The excitation function for 16O+208Pb above the barrier is overestimated by about 16% compared to experimental data from Morgenstern (1999).

## Context

This work extends the TDHF approach to heavy-ion fusion, building on earlier TDHF calculations by Kim et al. (1997), Simenel et al. (2001), and others, and using the SLy4d interaction variant of Chabanat et al. (1998). The results show that TDHF captures dynamical effects that lower the barrier by about 2 MeV compared to the frozen-density approximation and the Bass barrier (Bass, 1977, 1980). The inability to describe sub-barrier fusion is a known limitation of the mean-field approximation, and the authors note that going beyond a single Slater determinant is necessary to treat tunneling. The observed two-proton transfer just below the barrier is consistent with experimental carbon production cross sections reported by Videbaek et al. (1977) and Vulgaris et al. (1986).
