# Probing QCD critical fluctuations from the yield ratio of strange hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> By analyzing the available data on strange hadrons in central Pb+Pb collisions from the NA49 Collaboration at the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and in central Au+Au collisions from the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) in a wide collision energy range from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 6.3 GeV to 200 GeV, we find a possible non-monotonic behavior in the ratio $\mathcal{O}_\text{K-$Ξ$-$ϕ$-$Λ$}$= $\frac{N(K^+)N(Ξ^-)}{N(ϕ)N(Λ)}$ of $K^+$, $Ξ^-$, $ϕ$, and $Λ$ yields as a function of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$. Based on the quark coalescence model, which can take into account the effect of quark density fluctuations on hadron production, a possible non-monotonic behavior in the dependence of the strange quark density fluctuation on $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ is obtained. This is in contrast to the coalescence model that does not include quark density fluctuations and also to the statistical hadronization model as both fail to describe even qualitatively the collision energy dependence of the ratio $\mathcal{O}_\text{K-$Ξ$-$ϕ$-$Λ$}$. Our findings thus suggest that the signal and location of a possible critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, which is expected to result in large quark density fluctuations, can be found in the on-going Bean Energy Scan program at RHIC.

## Key claim

By analyzing published data on strange hadron yields from the NA49 and STAR Collaborations across a wide collision energy range (6.3 to 200 GeV), the authors find a possible non-monotonic behavior in the ratio O_K-Xi-phi-Lambda = N(K+)N(Xi-)/[N(phi)N(Lambda)] as a function of sqrt(s_NN), which they interpret as evidence for strange quark density fluctuations that could signal the location of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram. The claim is hedged as "possible" because the experimental uncertainties are large and the non-monotonic behavior is seen primarily at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 8 GeV.

## Method

The authors compiled published yields of K+, Xi-, phi, and Lambda hadrons from central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (NA49 Collaboration) and central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (STAR Collaboration) across sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3 to 200 GeV. They calculated the ratio O_K-Xi-phi-Lambda from these data and compared it to predictions from the quark coalescence model (COAL-SH) that includes quark density fluctuations, as well as to the standard coalescence model without fluctuations and to the statistical hadronization model (THERMUS package). By assuming uncorrelated quark density fluctuations (alpha_{q1q2}=0), they extracted the strange quark relative density fluctuation Delta s from the measured ratio.

## Key numbers

- The ratio O_K-Xi-phi-Lambda shows a peak value of 1.88 +/- 0.27 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV (full rapidity, Pb+Pb) and 1.79 +/- 0.33 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV (midrapidity, Pb+Pb).
- At the highest energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (Au+Au, midrapidity), the ratio is 1.02 +/- 0.13, consistent with the COAL-SH prediction of g = 1.1 without quark density fluctuations.
- The extracted strange quark relative density fluctuation Delta s reaches a maximum of 0.71 +/- 0.25 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV (full rapidity, Pb+Pb) and 0.63 +/- 0.30 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6 GeV (midrapidity, Pb+Pb).
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, Delta s = 0 (+0.05) (midrapidity, Au+Au), consistent with no fluctuations.
- The statistical hadronization model predicts values ranging from 1.20 to 1.40 across the energy range, failing to reproduce the non-monotonic behavior.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Sun et al. (2017) and Sun et al. (2018), who studied neutron density fluctuations via the yield ratio of light nuclei (p-d-t) and found a similar non-monotonic behavior at SPS energies. The present study uses strange hadrons instead of light nuclei because strange hadrons scatter less frequently during hadronic evolution, providing a more direct probe of quark density fluctuations at hadronization. The results contradict predictions from both the standard coalescence model without fluctuations and the statistical hadronization model (e.g., Cleymans et al. 2005), and suggest that the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram may have been reached or closely approached in heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. Future measurements from the RHIC BES phase II program, which can measure total multiplicities in full rapidity space, are needed to verify these findings.
