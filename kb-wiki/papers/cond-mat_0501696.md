# Microscopic Derivation of Causal Diffusion Equation using Projection Operator Method

> We derive a coarse-grained equation of motion of a number density by applying the projection operator method to a non-relativistic model. The derived equation is an integrodifferential equation and contains the memory effect. The equation is consistent with causality and the sum rule associated with the number conservation in the low momentum limit, in contrast to usual acausal diffusion equations given by using the Fick's law. After employing the Markov approximation, we find that the equation has the similar form to the causal diffusion equation. Our result suggests that current-current correlations are not necessarily adequate as the definition of diffusion constants.

## Key claim

The paper derives a coarse-grained equation for number density using the projection operator method that, after Markov approximation, takes the form of a causal diffusion equation (telegraph equation) rather than the usual acausal diffusion equation from Fick's law, but this result depends on not replacing the coarse-grained time-evolution operator with the ordinary time-evolution operator, an approximation that the author argues is invalid even in the low momentum limit.

## Method

The author applies the Mori projection operator to a non-relativistic fermion model with a contact interaction, projecting onto the number density fluctuations. The time-convolution (TC) equation is derived from the Heisenberg equation of motion, keeping the coarse-grained time-evolution operator in the memory term without the standard approximation of replacing it by the ordinary time-evolution operator. The memory function is evaluated at the one-loop (ring diagram) level, and the resulting integrodifferential equation is then subjected to a Markov approximation that separates the memory into a frequency function and a renormalized memory function.

## Key numbers

* The diffusion coefficient in the low momentum limit is \( D = \frac{1}{m\beta} \frac{ \int^{\Lambda}_{0} d p p^2 n^-_{\bf p} }{\int^{\Lambda}_{0}dp p^2 n^-_{\bf p} (1-n^-_{\bf p})} \), where \( n^-_{\bf p} \) is the Fermi distribution and \( \Lambda \) is a momentum cutoff.
* The relaxation time in the low momentum limit is \( 1/\tau_{\bf k} \approx \frac{2}{m\pi}|{\bf k}|\frac{\int^{\Lambda}_{0}dp p^2 n^-_{\bf p} (1-n^-_{\bf p})}{\int^{\Lambda}_{0} d p p n^-_{\bf p}(1-n^-_{\bf p})} \), showing momentum dependence.
* The causal diffusion constant is \( D^{ca}_{\bf k} \equiv D\tau_{\bf k} \), combining the two quantities above.
* The propagation speed of the causal diffusion equation is \( V = \sqrt{D/\tau} \), where \( \tau = \lim_{{\bf k}\rightarrow 0} (|{\bf k}|\tau_{\bf k}) \).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous application of the projection operator method to the chiral order parameter dynamics (Koide, 2004), where oscillatory relaxation was found instead of the overdamped time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation. It contradicts the conventional result of Fick (1983) that the projection operator method yields acausal diffusion equations, by showing that the standard approximation of replacing the coarse-grained time-evolution operator is invalid. The result enables the use of causal diffusion equations for relativistic heavy-ion collisions and for QCD critical dynamics, where acausal diffusion equations (assumed by authors such as Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993) have been standard.
