# Gamma production in neutrino interaction with nuclei

> We evaluate the cross-section for gamma production by neutrinos through a meson exchange effect which derives from the concept of axial-vector mixing. The resulting cross-section leads to some increase of the gamma production cross-section by neutrinos, especially at low neutrino energies, which may influence the understanding of the low energy excess of electron-like events seen in the MiniBooNE experiment.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new source of gamma production by neutrinos via a meson exchange effect (axial-vector mixing) that has not been considered in previous analyses of the MiniBooNE low-energy excess. The authors caution that this process is smaller than the single gamma process but significant, and that a complete evaluation of all similar meson exchange terms is needed before drawing definite conclusions about whether gamma rings simulating electron rings could affect sterile neutrino interpretations.

## Method

The authors evaluate the cross-section for gamma production by neutrinos on carbon-12 through a contact N-Delta-gamma-pion vertex derived from chiral symmetry, where the pion is dressed in the nuclear medium by particle-hole excitations and becomes invisible to the MiniBooNE detector. They use a nuclear matter description with density 0.8 times normal nuclear matter density, limiting the calculation to the axial coupling which applies to the average of neutrino and antineutrino cross-sections. The calculation includes both the contact term and a pion-in-flight term, and the in-medium pion propagator incorporates particle-hole and Delta-hole states with Landau-Migdal parameters.

## Key numbers

- For a neutrino energy of 0.3 GeV, the single gamma cross-section per nucleon on carbon-12 is approximately 0.27 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- For a neutrino energy of 0.5 GeV, the single gamma cross-section per nucleon is approximately 2.0 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- For a neutrino energy of 1.0 GeV, the single gamma cross-section per nucleon is approximately 8.0 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- The axial form factor uses a dipole form with G_A(0) = 1.26 and cutoff parameter M_A = 1.032 GeV
- The ratio of Delta and nucleon coupling constants R_{NDelta} is taken as 2
- The nuclear density used is 0.8 rho_0, where rho_0 is normal nuclear matter density
- The Landau-Migdal parameters used are g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NDelta = 0.5, g'_DeltaDelta = 0.3
- The dipole pi-NN form factor cutoff is Lambda = 0.98 GeV
- The 2p-2h absorption parameter Im C_0 = 0.13 m_pi^{-6}

## Context

This work extends the concept of axial-vector mixing introduced by Chanfray et al. (1999) to neutrino-induced gamma production, building on previous evaluations of gamma emission from Delta decay by Hill (2010, 2011), Serot (2012, 2013), and Wang (2014, 2015). The process is motivated by the MiniBooNE low-energy excess of electron-like events, where gamma rings could be mistaken for electron rings. The authors note that the Delta contribution alone has not been able to account for the observed number of electrons without sterile neutrino oscillations, as discussed by Katori (2020). The work enables future investigation of the difference between neutrino and antineutrino cross-sections, which is relevant for CP violation studies.
