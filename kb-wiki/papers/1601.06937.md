# Theoretical study on nuclear structure by the multiple Coulomb scattering and magnetic scattering of relativistic electrons

> Electron scattering is an effective method to study the nuclear structure. For the odd-$A$ nuclei with proton holes in the outmost orbits, we investigate the contributions of proton holes to the nuclear quadrupole moments $Q$ and magnetic moments $μ$ by the multiple Coulomb scattering and magnetic scattering. The deformed nuclear charge densities are constructed by the relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. Comparing the theoretical Coulomb and magnetic form factors with the experimental data, the nuclear quadrupole moments $Q$ and nuclear magnetic moments $μ$ are investigated. From the electron scattering, the wave functions of the proton holes of odd-$A$ nuclei can be tested, which can also reflect the validity of the nuclear structure model.

# Theoretical study on nuclear structure by the multiple Coulomb scattering and magnetic scattering of relativistic electrons

## Key claim

For odd-A nuclei with proton holes in the outermost orbits, the nuclear quadrupole moments Q and magnetic moments μ can be extracted by combining Coulomb and magnetic electron scattering, but only under the approximation that the deformed part of the charge density is treated in plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) while the spherical part is treated in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), and that the quadrupole moments extracted from electron scattering are smaller than twice the previously measured experimental values.

## Method

The authors constructed deformed nuclear charge densities using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with FSU and NL3* parameter sets, decomposing the density into a spherical part and a deformed part attributed to the proton hole. For three odd-A nuclei (14N, 27Al, 39K), they calculated longitudinal form factors by combining DWBA phase-shift analysis for the C0 multipole with PWBA calculations for the Cλ (λ≥2) multipoles, and magnetic form factors using PWBA with spectroscopic factors αL introduced to account for nuclear many-body effects.

## Key numbers

- For 14N, the quadrupole moment extracted from electron scattering is Q = 3.2 fm² (compared to previous experimental values)
- For 27Al, the quadrupole moment extracted from electron scattering is Q = 8.3 fm² (compared to previous experimental values)
- For 39K, the quadrupole moment extracted from electron scattering is Q = 3.9 fm² (compared to previous experimental values)
- The extracted quadrupole moments from electron scattering are smaller than twice the previous experimental values (no specific prior values given in text)
- For 27Al, the C2 multipole contribution to the longitudinal form factor is |F_{C2}^{PW}(q)|² = (10/7)I₂(q)²
- For 39K, the C2 and C4 multipole contributions are |F_{Cλ}^{PW}(q)|² = (10/7)I₂(q)² + (18/7)I₄(q)²
- For 14N, the C2 multipole contribution is |F_{C2}^{PW}(q)|² = 2I₂(q)²
- The single-nucleon factor is f_sn(q) = [1 + (q/855 MeV)²]⁻²
- The center-of-mass factor is f_cm(q) = exp(q²b²/4A)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of electron scattering from spherical nuclei (studied by Wang (2006), Roca (2008), Chu (2009), and Roca (2013) using DWBA for C0 multipoles) to deformed nuclei by incorporating Cλ multipoles. It builds on the approach of Horowitz (2014) for 27Al and provides general formulas applicable to any odd-A nucleus with a proton hole. The results enable testing of RMF model wave functions for valence nucleons and could be applied to future electron scattering experiments at RIKEN and GSI facilities for exotic nuclei.
