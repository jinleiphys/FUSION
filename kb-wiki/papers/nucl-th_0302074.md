# The role of the $Δ$(1232) on the longitudinal response in the inclusive electron scattering reaction

> We have performed a many-body calculation of the longitudinal nuclear $(e,e')$ reaction employing a Second RPA (SRPA) formalism which contains the $Δ$(1232). More explicitly, our scheme contains RPA correlations as well as Hartree-Fock and second order self-energies, where an accurate evaluation of exchange terms is achieved. Using this formalism we have evaluated the longitudinal response function for $^{40}Ca$. We give final results at momentum transfers ranging from 300 up to 500 MeV/c, obtaining a good agreement with data.

## Key claim

The Δ(1232) resonance significantly affects the longitudinal nuclear response not through direct excitation of Δ-hole pairs, but indirectly through its contribution to the many-body self-energies; the agreement with data is conditioned by the choice of the residual interaction, which remains uncertain.

## Method

The author performed a Second Random Phase Approximation (SRPA) calculation in nuclear matter for the longitudinal response function of ⁴⁰Ca, incorporating RPA correlations, Hartree-Fock self-energies, and second-order self-energies with accurate evaluation of exchange terms. The Δ(1232) was included both as a direct excitation channel (Δ-hole pairs) and through its contributions to the self-energies in the particle-hole and Δ-hole sectors. The residual interaction was based on a parameterization of the Bonn potential (π, ρ, σ, ω exchange), and the response was computed for momentum transfers from 300 to 500 MeV/c using a Fermi momentum of 235 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used for ⁴⁰Ca: k_F = 235 MeV/c
- Momentum transfer range studied: q = 300 to 500 MeV/c
- The Δ-hole direct excitation contribution (R_Δh) was found to be negligible compared to the particle-hole response (R_ph) at q = 400 MeV/c
- Good agreement with data was obtained at low q values (300–400 MeV/c); agreement at q = 450 and 500 MeV/c was less satisfactory but total intensity was satisfactory

## Context

This work extends earlier many-body calculations of the nuclear response, particularly the author's previous work (Bauer 1998) which examined the transverse channel with Δ inclusion but had no RPA terms in the longitudinal channel due to the choice of residual interaction. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between Fermi gas models and experimental data for the longitudinal response, first highlighted by Meziani (1984) and later by Williamson (1997). The results are compared with Gil et al. (1997), who studied both channels with an elaborate formalism including two-body corrections and the Δ, and Amore et al. (1999), who used the Boson Loop Expansion; both prior works agree with this paper's finding that direct Δ-hole excitation is insignificant but the many-body effect of the Δ is important. The work enables future studies with more accurate interactions and the extension to the transverse channel with proper treatment of antisymmetrization constraints.
