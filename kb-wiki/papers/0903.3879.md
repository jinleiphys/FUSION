# The Wigner Function of Produced Particles in String Fragmentation

> We show that QCD4 with transverse confinement can be approximately compactified into QCD2 with a transverse quark mass $m_{{}_T}$ that is obtained by solving a set of coupled transverse eigenvalue equations. In the limits of a strong coupling and a large number of flavors, QCD2 further admits Schwinger QED2-type bosonized solutions. We therefore examine phenomenologically the space-time dynamics of produced particles in string fragmentation by studying the Wigner function of produced bosons in Schwinger QED2, which mimics many features of string fragmentation in quantum chromodynamics. We find that particles with momenta in different regions of the rapidity plateau are produced at the initial moment of string fragmentation as a quark pulls away from an antiquark at high energies, in contrast to classical pictures of string fragmentation with longitudinal space-momentum-time ordering.

# The Wigner Function of Produced Particles in String Fragmentation

## Key claim
The paper argues that in string fragmentation, particles with different momenta across the rapidity plateau are all produced at the initial moment when the quark begins to separate from the antiquark, contrary to classical models that predict a longitudinal space-momentum-time ordering where particles with larger rapidities are produced later and farther from the origin. This conclusion is drawn from studying the Wigner function in Schwinger QED2, which the authors argue approximates transversely-confined QCD4 under conditions of strong coupling and large flavor number.

## Method
The authors first show that QCD4 with transverse confinement can be approximately compactified into QCD2 by solving coupled transverse eigenvalue equations to obtain a transverse quark mass \(m_T\), then relate the four-dimensional and two-dimensional coupling constants through transverse averaging over the flux tube profile. They then demonstrate that in the limits of strong coupling and large number of flavors, multi-flavor massless QCD2 admits Schwinger QED2-type bosonized solutions with an effective coupling constant proportional to \(\sqrt{N_f}\). Using this mapping, they evaluate the Wigner function of produced bosons in QED2 when a fermion separates from an antifermion at high energies, obtaining the space-time distribution of produced particles from the initial fermion current.

## Key numbers
- The average \(p_T^2\) of produced pions in \(e^+\)-\(e^-\) annihilation at \(\sqrt{s}=29\) GeV is 0.255 GeV\(^2\), corresponding to a flux tube radius \(R_T \sim 0.4\) fm.
- For \(\alpha_s = 0.4\) and \(R_T = 0.4\) fm, the QCD2 coupling constant is \(g_{\rm 2D}^2 = 0.40\) GeV\(^2\).
- The string tension coefficient \(b = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) = 1 GeV/fm gives \(g_{\rm 2D}^2 = 2b = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\), consistent with the compactification estimate.
- The boson mass in the bosonized QCD2 is \(M^2 = N_f g_{\rm 2D}^2 / \pi\).
- The rapidity plateau width increases with energy as \(\ln(\sqrt{s})\).
- Ridge particles detected by PHOBOS extend to pseudorapidities as large as \(|\Delta \eta| \sim 4\) with transverse momenta \(p_t > 0.035\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the QED2 string fragmentation studies of Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) and Wong (1991, 1994), which demonstrated boost-invariant rapidity distributions and plateau formation. It directly contradicts the classical space-momentum-time ordering of the Lund model (Andersson, 1983) and Bjorken hydrodynamics (Bjorken, 1983), where particles with larger rapidities are produced later and farther from the origin. The paper is motivated by the PHOBOS ridge phenomenon (Wenger, 2008) and the momentum kick model (Wong, 2007-2009), which suggest that a central rapidity jet and a parton at large absolute rapidity can be associated by a collision at early times. The results enable a quantum field theory description of the early stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions where medium partons possess a rapidity plateau, supporting the interpretation of ridge particles as medium partons present at the initial moment of string fragmentation.
