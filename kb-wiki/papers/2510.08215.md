# Recent advances in perturbative QCD at high density

> In these conference proceedings, we discuss recent progress in high-order perturbative studies of the thermodynamic and transport properties of dense quark matter. Special emphasis is placed on the introduction of a promising new computational tool, thermal Loop Tree Duality, which enables pushing the existing weak-coupling calculations to higher perturbative orders.

## Key claim

These conference proceedings review recent progress toward completing the N3LO perturbative pressure of cold and dense quark matter, with the central advance being the generalization of the Loop Tree Duality method to thermal settings, which enables pushing weak-coupling calculations to higher perturbative orders.

## Method

The authors describe the thermal Loop Tree Duality (LTD) framework, which comprises four operations on a generic Feynman graph: subtraction of infrared divergences using effective field theories, algorithmic subtraction of ultraviolet divergences via the R-operation, derivation of a locally finite three-dimensional representation for spatial momentum integrals, and numerical evaluation of the remaining phase-space integrals using Monte-Carlo methods. For the bulk viscosity of unpaired quark matter, the authors extend calculations to N2LO accuracy by combining perturbative QCD thermodynamic inputs with electroweak rates, using a mass expansion scheme that treats the strange quark mass as a perturbation.

## Key numbers

* The electric screening mass is given by m_E = (g/√3) √[(N_c + N_f/2)T² + (3N_f/(2π²)) μ_f²], and the magnetic screening mass is m_M ∼ g²T.
* For two four-loop vacuum diagrams evaluated in Karkkainen (2025), the finite (ε⁰) coefficients obtained via dense LTD were 12.36(4) and 716.32(7), agreeing with traditional semianalytic results of 12.375 and 716.38 respectively, using 110×10⁶ and 120×10⁶ Monte Carlo samples with computation times of 7.1 and 6.5 microseconds per sample per CPU core.
* The pairing contribution to the pressure is suppressed by a factor of Δ²/μ_B² relative to the unpaired part, with the pairing gap Δ ≲ 100 MeV.
* The bulk viscosity results show strong sensitivity to perturbative order even at baryon densities of order 40 times nuclear saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD program for dense matter that has reached N3LO for the pressure of cold quark matter (Gorda 2021, Gorda 2023) and N2LO for chiral perturbation theory descriptions of sub-saturation nuclear matter (Tews 2012, Drischler 2017). The thermal LTD method generalizes the vacuum Loop Tree Duality (Capatti 2019) to nonzero temperatures and chemical potentials, building on earlier approaches such as cutting rules (Ghisoiu 2016) and augmented IBP relations (Osterman 2023). The bulk viscosity results (Cruz Rojas 2024, Hernandez 2025, Sappi 2025) extend earlier work by Sad (2007) and Alford (2013). These advances enable model-independent inference of neutron-star matter equations of state (Annala 2017, Komoltsev 2021) and may allow detection of deconfined matter in neutron-star mergers (Most 2021, Chabanov 2023).
