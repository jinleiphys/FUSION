# Statistical and dynamical fluctuations of Binder ratios in heavy ion collisions

> Higher moments of net-proton Binder ratio, which is suggested to be a good experimental measurement to locate the QCD critical point, is measured in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We firstly estimate the effect of statistical fluctuations of the third and forth order Binder ratios. Then the dynamical Binder ratio is proposed and investigated in both transport and statistical models. The energy dependence of dynamical Binder ratios with different system sizes at RHIC beam scan energies are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the dynamical Binder ratio (the measured Binder ratio minus the statistical fluctuation contribution from a Skellam distribution) should be used instead of the raw Binder ratio to search for the QCD critical point, because statistical fluctuations from finite particle number give the main contribution to the measured Binder ratios at RHIC energies and must be removed to isolate critical-point-related signals.

## Method

The authors calculated the third- and fourth-order Binder ratios of net-proton distributions from the AMPT transport model (both default and string-melting versions) and the THERMINATOR statistical model for Au+Au collisions at RHIC beam energy scan energies. They derived analytic expressions for the statistical fluctuations of Binder ratios assuming independent Poisson distributions for protons and antiprotons (Skellam distribution), then subtracted these from the total Binder ratios to obtain dynamical Binder ratios. The energy dependence of dynamical Binder ratios was studied at six RHIC energies (7.7, 9.2, 11.5, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) across nine centrality bins.

## Key numbers

* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the statistical fluctuations (dashed lines in Fig. 1) give the main contribution to both \(B_3\) and \(B_4\) across all centralities in AMPT default, AMPT string melting, and THERMINATOR models.
* Dynamical \(B_3\) and \(B_4\) from THERMINATOR are zero at all centralities at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (Fig. 2).
* Dynamical \(B_3\) and \(B_4\) from AMPT models are larger than zero in peripheral collisions and tend to zero in central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (Fig. 2).
* The analysis used approximately 6 million events for each energy and centrality to ensure statistical errors under control.
* The phase space used was mid-rapidity \(|y| < 0.5\) with transverse momentum \(0.4 < p_T < 0.8\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Stephanov (2004) and others that Binder-like ratios can identify critical behavior in heavy ion collisions, and the 3D-Ising model calculations of Binder ratios by Wu et al. (2010). The paper provides a baseline for experimental searches at the RHIC beam energy scan program by showing that transport models without critical point physics (AMPT) produce no step-function behavior in dynamical Binder ratios, unlike the 3D-Ising model prediction. The dynamical Binder ratio definition enables future comparisons with experimental data from RHIC, SPS, and FAIR to isolate possible critical point signals from known statistical and thermal backgrounds.
