# Calculation of radiative corrections to the effect of parity nonconservation in heavy atoms

> We calculate the self-energy and the vertex radiative corrections to the effect of parity nonconservation in heavy atoms. The sum of the corrections is of the form ${\cal A}\ln(λ_C/r_0)+{\calB}$, where ${\cal A}$ and ${\cal B}$ are functions of $Zα$, and $λ_C$ and $r_0$ are the Compton wavelength and the nuclear radius, respectively. The function ${\cal A}$ is calculated exactly in $Zα$ and the function ${\cal B}$ is calculated in the leading order. In the leading order ${\cal A}\propto α(Zα)^2$ and ${\cal B}\propto α(Zα)$. The sum of the corrections is -0.85% for Cs and -1.48% for Tl. Using these results we have performed analysis of the experimental data on atomic parity nonconservation. The values obtained for the nuclear weak charge, $Q_W=-72.81(28)_{exp}(36)_{theor}$ for Cs and $Q_W=-116.8(1.2)_{exp}(3.4)_{theor}$ for Tl, agree with predictions of the standard model within $0.6σ$. As an application of our approach we have also calculated dependence of the Lamb shift on the finite nuclear size.

## Key claim

The sum of the self-energy and vertex radiative corrections to atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) takes the form \( \mathcal{A} \ln(\lambda_C / r_0) + \mathcal{B} \), where the logarithmic coefficient \(\mathcal{A}\) is calculated exactly in \(Z\alpha\) and the constant term \(\mathcal{B}\) is calculated only in the leading order; the total correction is \(-0.85\%\) for cesium and \(-1.48\%\) for thallium, and after applying these corrections the extracted nuclear weak charges agree with the standard model within \(0.6\sigma\).

## Method

The authors calculated the self-energy and vertex one-loop radiative corrections to the PNC matrix element between \(p_{1/2}\) and \(s_{1/2}\) states in heavy atoms. The logarithmic part \(\mathcal{A}\) was obtained by evaluating the electron self-energy operator and the vertex operator using exact electron Green's functions in the Coulomb field at distances \(r \ll \lambda_C\) in Feynman gauge, with the self-energy contribution separated into a regularized low-order part (computed in momentum space) and a higher-order part (computed via an integral representation). The non-logarithmic term \(\mathcal{B}\) was calculated in the leading order \(\alpha(Z\alpha)\) using an effective operator approach and the Fried-Yennie gauge for the low-energy forward scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Total radiative correction to PNC: \(-0.85\%\) for Cs and \(-1.48\%\) for Tl.
- Extracted nuclear weak charge for Cs: \(Q_W = -72.81(28)_{\text{exp}}(36)_{\text{theor}}\).
- Extracted nuclear weak charge for Tl: \(Q_W = -116.8(1.2)_{\text{exp}}(3.4)_{\text{theor}}\).
- Leading-order coefficient of the logarithmic term \(\mathcal{A}\) at small \(Z\alpha\): \(\mathcal{A} = -\frac{\alpha(Z\alpha)^2}{\pi} \left( \frac{15}{4} - \frac{\pi^2}{6} \right)\).
- Leading-order coefficient of the constant term \(\mathcal{B}\) at small \(Z\alpha\): \(\mathcal{B} \propto \alpha(Z\alpha)\).
- Relativistic enhancement factor \(R\): approximately 3 for Cs and approximately 9 for Tl, Pb, and Bi.
- Nuclear radius formula used: \(r_0 \approx 1.1 A^{1/3} \ \text{fm} \approx 1.5 Z^{1/3} \ \text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical calculation of the Uehling-potential (vacuum polarization) radiative correction to atomic PNC (Milstein and Sushkov, 2002) by now computing the self-energy and vertex corrections, which had been omitted in earlier many-body calculations by Dzuba et al. (1989) and Blundell et al. (1992). The results enable a refined test of the standard electroweak model at low energy using atomic PNC data, and the approach is also applied to calculate the finite-nuclear-size dependence of the Lamb shift, agreeing with previous numerical computations by Johnson and Soff (1985) and by Cheng, Johnson, and Sapirstein (1993).
