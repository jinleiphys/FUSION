# Predictions for p+Pb at 4.4A TeV to Test Initial State Nuclear Shadowing at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Collinear factorized perturbative QCD model predictions are compared for p+Pb at 4.4A TeV to test nuclear shadowing of parton distribution at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The nuclear modification factor (NMF), R_{pPb}(y=0,p_T<20 GeV/c) = dn_{p Pb} /(N_{coll}(b)dn_{pp}), is computed with electron-nucleus (e+A) global fit with different nuclear shadow distributions and compared to fixed Q^2 shadow ansatz used in Monte Carlo Heavy Ion Jet Interacting Generator (HIJING) type models. Due to rapid DGLAP reduction of shadowing with increasing Q^2 used in e+A global fit, our results confirm that no significant initial state suppression is expected (R_{pPb} (p_T) = 1 \pm 0.1) in the p_T range 5 to 20 GeV/ c. In contrast, the fixed Q^2 shadowing models assumed in HIJING type models predict in the above p_T range a sizable suppression, R_{pPb} (p_T) = 0.6-0.7 at mid-pseudorapidity that is similar to the color glass condensate (CGC) model predictions. For central (N_{coll} = 12) p+ Pb collisions and at forward pseudorapidity (η= 6) the HIJING type models predict smaller values of nuclear modification factors (R_{pPb}(p_T)) than in minimum bias events at mid-pseudorapidity (η= 0). Observation of R_{pPb}(p_T= 5-20 GeV/c) less than 0.6 for minimum bias p+A collisions would pose a serious difficulty for separating initial from final state interactions in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

# Predictions for p+Pb at 4.4A TeV to Test Initial State Nuclear Shadowing at LHC Energies

## Key claim

The paper predicts that if the nuclear modification factor R_{pPb}(p_T) for minimum bias p+Pb collisions at 4.4A TeV is measured to be less than 0.6 in the p_T range 5-20 GeV/c, this would pose a serious difficulty for separating initial from final state interactions in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on which nuclear shadowing model is correct: DGLAP-evolved nPDFs predict R_{pPb} = 1 ± 0.1 (no significant suppression), while fixed Q^2 shadowing models (HIJING type) and CGC models predict R_{pPb} = 0.6-0.7.

## Method

The authors compare collinear factorized perturbative QCD model predictions for p+Pb at 4.4A TeV using different nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) parametrizations. They compute the nuclear modification factor R_{pPb}(y=0, p_T<20 GeV/c) using electron-nucleus (e+A) global fit nPDFs with DGLAP Q^2 evolution and compare these to fixed Q^2 shadowing ansatze used in HIJING1.0, HIJING2.0, and HIJING/B-B2.0 Monte Carlo models, as well as to Color Glass Condensate (CGC) k_T factorization model predictions.

## Key numbers

- For minimum bias p+Pb at 4.4A TeV, the average number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions is N_coll^MB ≈ 7, and for central (0-20%) collisions N_coll^Cen ≈ 12, using an inelastic NN cross section σ_NN^in ≈ 65 mb.
- DGLAP-evolved nPDFs (EKS99, EPS08) predict R_{pPb}(p_T) = 1 ± 0.1 in the p_T range 5 to 20 GeV/c at mid-pseudorapidity.
- Fixed Q^2 shadowing models (HIJING type) predict R_{pPb}(p_T) = 0.6-0.7 at mid-pseudorapidity for p_T = 5-20 GeV/c.
- For central p+Pb collisions (N_coll = 12) at forward pseudorapidity η = 6, HIJING/B-B2.0 predicts R_{pPb} values of 0.35-0.40 in the p_T range 5-10 GeV/c.
- The HIJING/B-B2.0 model uses a minijet cut-off p_0 = 3.1 GeV/c and string tension κ = 2.9 GeV/fm for p+Pb at 4.4A TeV, compared to p_0 = 2 GeV/c and κ = 2.7 GeV/fm for pp at 4.4 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the methodology used for the d+Au control experiment at RHIC (2004) to LHC energies, where the much smaller fractional momenta x < 10^{-3} make initial state nuclear shadowing more uncertain. The paper directly compares predictions from DGLAP-evolved nPDFs (EKS99 from Eskola 1998, EPS08 from Eskola 2008) against fixed Q^2 shadowing models (HIJING1.0 from Wang 1991, HIJING2.0 from Deng 2010, HIJING/B-B2.0 from Topor Pop 2011) and CGC models (KKT04 from Kharzeev 2003, rcBK from Tribedy 2011 and Jalilian-Marian 2011). The results will enable discrimination between competing explanations of nuclear suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, specifically whether observed suppression is due to initial state parton flux modification or final state jet energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
