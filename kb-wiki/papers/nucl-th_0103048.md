# Comparison of Dirac and microscopic Schrodinger optical model potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering

> Detailed comparisons are made between results of calculations for intermediate energy nucleon--208Pb scattering using optical potentials obtained from Dirac phenomenology and a microscopic Schrodinger model. The two approaches yield quite similar results for all of the observables with each projectile, including spin observables, suggesting the two models are physically equivalent. A new phenomenon in the analyzing power is confirmed in the results for 100 MeV scattering.

## Key claim

The Dirac phenomenology and the microscopic Schrodinger model yield surprisingly similar results for nucleon-208Pb elastic scattering observables at 100-300 MeV, suggesting the two fundamentally different approaches are physically equivalent over this energy range. The authors hedge that the agreement worsens for neutron scattering above 20 degrees at 100 MeV and for the 300 MeV case, where the microscopic model reaches its upper energy limit.

## Method

The Dirac phenomenology uses spherically symmetric complex Lorentz scalar and vector potentials with energy-independent symmetrized Woods-Saxon form factors, reduced to a Schrodinger-equivalent equation. The microscopic Schrodinger model starts from the Bonn-B nucleon-nucleon potential, solves the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation in infinite nuclear matter, uses a local density approximation to map to the target nucleus, and folds the resulting effective interaction with a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock ground state density of 208Pb to obtain a fully nonlocal optical potential with no adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for proton and neutron scattering from 208Pb calculated at 100, 200, and 300 MeV
- The 100 MeV proton results agree with 98 MeV data from Schwandt et al. (1982)
- The 200 MeV proton results agree with data from Hutcheon et al. (1988)
- The 300 MeV case is the upper limit for the nonrelativistic microscopic formalism using a bare NN potential
- A striking phenomenon in the analyzing power at 100 MeV: proton and neutron analyzing powers are exactly out of phase, with the one nearly the inverse of the other about their mean
- At 200 and 300 MeV, proton and neutron analyzing powers are similar beyond 15 degrees
- At 300 MeV, a minimum at 8 degrees in the neutron analyzing power is missing in the proton analyzing power

## Context

This work extends the Dirac phenomenology results of Kozack and Madland (1990) for nucleon-208Pb scattering and the microscopic Schrodinger model reviewed by Amos et al. (2000). It directly compares both approaches for both proton and neutron projectiles simultaneously, which had not been done before. The results enable future experimental measurements of the predicted spin observables, especially the analyzing powers at 100 MeV, to determine whether the dramatic difference between proton and neutron analyzing powers is a real physical effect, and would also help assess the models for neutron scattering where the experimental database is currently lacking.
