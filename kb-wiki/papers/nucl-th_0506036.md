# Relation between the separable and one-boson-exchange potential for the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We investigate the relation between the rank I separable potential for the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation and the one-boson-exchange potential. After several trials of the parameter choices, it turns out that it is not always possible to reproduce the phase-shifts calculated from a single term of the one-boson-exchange potential especially of the $σ$-exchange term, separately by the rank I separable potential. Instead, it is shown that the separable potential is useful to parameterize the total nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a rank I separable potential for the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation cannot reproduce the phase shifts calculated from individual one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) terms (especially the strongly attractive sigma-exchange term) when parameters are determined in the long wavelength limit, but the separable potential can successfully parameterize the total nucleon-nucleon interaction after cancellation of strong attractive and repulsive components.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(^1S_0\) channel using a rank I separable potential with a Yukawa form factor \(g(p) = 1/(p^2 - m_b^2)\) and compared the resulting phase shifts to those calculated from individual sigma-, omega-, pi-, and rho-exchange terms of the Bonn OBEP. The separable potential parameters were first determined by matching the zero-momentum limit \(V_{\text{sep}}(0,0) = V_b(0,0)\), and then the phase shifts were compared as functions of laboratory kinetic energy \(T_{\text{lab}}\).

## Key numbers

- For the sigma channel, the OBEP potential depth reaches about 200 MeV at 0.75 fm; the separable potential could not reproduce the OBEP phase shift even in the limit \(\lambda \to -\infty\).
- For the omega channel, the OBEP phase shift could be reproduced by the separable potential using \(m_b = 630\) MeV and \(\lambda = 81.5 \times 10^6\) MeV\(^2\).
- For the pi channel, the OBEP phase shift at around \(T_{\text{lab}} \approx 200\) MeV could be reproduced using \(m_{\pi} = \Lambda_{\pi} = 1300\) MeV and \(\lambda = 135 \times 10^6\) MeV\(^2\).
- For the rho channel, reducing the coupling strength by about a factor of 8 to \(\lambda = -4.52 \times 10^6\) MeV\(^2\) gave a phase shift close to the OBEP result.
- The best fit to the total \(^1S_0\) nuclear force used \(\lambda = -0.294 \times 10^6\) MeV\(^2\) and \(m_b = 224\) MeV, reproducing experimental data up to \(T_{\text{lab}} \gtrsim 200\) MeV.
- The real part of \(h(s)\) for \(m_b = 500\) MeV starts at 0.276 GeV\(^{-2}\) and decreases monotonically.

## Context

This work extends the study of separable potentials for the Bethe-Salpeter equation previously investigated by Bondarenko et al. (2002) and contrasts with the physically more fundamental one-boson-exchange potential approach of the Bonn group (Machleidt et al.). The results show that while individual OBEP components cannot be separately represented by a rank I separable form, the cancellation between strong sigma attraction and omega repulsion yields a mild net interaction that the separable potential can describe. This enables continued use of separable potentials in covariant calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering, with the authors noting that higher-rank separable interactions are being tested in ongoing work (Manabe et al.).
