# Chiral $3π$-exchange NN-potentials: results for representation invariant classes of diagrams

> We calculate in (two-loop) chiral perturbation theory the local NN-potentials generated by certain classes of three-pion exchange diagrams. We stress that the chiral $3πNN$-contact vertex depends on the particular choice of the interpolating pion-field and therefore one has to consider representation invariant classes of diagrams by supplementing graphs involving the chiral $4π$-vertex. We find that the resulting isovector spin-spin and tensor NN-potentials are negligibly small for $r\geq 0.8$ fm. One can conclude that the effects of uncorrelated chiral $3π$-exchange in the NN-interaction are very small and therefore of no practical relevance.

## Key claim
The isovector spin-spin and tensor NN-potentials generated by uncorrelated chiral three-pion exchange are negligibly small for inter-nucleon distances r ≥ 0.8 fm, but this conclusion depends on considering complete representation-invariant classes of diagrams rather than individual graphs, because the chiral 3πNN-contact vertex depends on the arbitrary choice of the interpolating pion-field.

## Method
The author calculated in two-loop chiral perturbation theory the local NN-potentials from four classes of three-pion exchange diagrams (classes I through IV), using the heavy baryon formulation with g_A = 1.32, f_π = 92.4 MeV, and m_π = 138 MeV. The imaginary parts of the NN-amplitudes were obtained via Cutkosky cutting-rules and integrated over the Lorentz-invariant three-pion phase space, then transformed into coordinate-space potentials via continuous superpositions of Yukawa functions.

## Key numbers
- For class I at r = 0.8 fm: spin-spin potential = 11.5 keV, tensor potential = 164 keV
- For class II at r = 0.8 fm: spin-spin potential = -262 keV, tensor potential = -491 keV
- For class III at r = 0.8 fm: spin-spin potential = 596 keV, tensor potential = 596 keV
- For class IV at r = 0.8 fm: spin-spin potential = 121 keV, tensor potential = -559 keV
- Total sum of all four classes at r = 0.8 fm: spin-spin = 467 keV, tensor = -290 keV
- For r ≥ 0.8 fm, all calculated 3π-exchange potentials are less than 0.6 MeV in absolute magnitude
- In the chiral limit (m_π = 0), class I potentials follow: W_T^(I)(r) = 7 W_S^(I)(r) = [70 g_A^2]/[3(8π)^5 f_π^6] * 1/r^7
- The a_1(1260)-exchange spin-spin potential at r = 0.8 fm is -7.26 MeV, about one order of magnitude larger than the chiral 3π-exchange potentials

## Context
This work extends the calculation of Pupin and Robilotta (1999), who evaluated only a single two-loop diagram with α = 0, by showing that representation-invariant classes of diagrams produce much smaller potentials due to cancellations between diagrams. The results confirm the earlier finding of Kaiser, Brodsky, and Kivel (1997) that the 3π-continuum makes negligible contributions to nucleon form factors. The extreme smallness of uncorrelated chiral 3π-exchange means that resonant 3π-exchanges (ω(782) and a_1(1260) exchange) dominate the intermediate-range NN-interaction, and that 1π-exchange plus chiral 2π-exchange already provide an excellent description of NN-scattering data below 350 MeV laboratory kinetic energy, as shown by the Nijmegen group (1997).
