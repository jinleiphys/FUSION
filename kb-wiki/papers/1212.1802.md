# Radiative Strength Functions for Dipole Transitions in Zr-90

> Partial cross sections for the (p,gamma) reaction on the Y-89 nucleus that were measured previously at proton energies between 2.17 and 5.00 MeV and which were averaged over resonances were used to determine the absolute values and the energy distribution of the strength of dipole transitions from compound-nucleus states to low-lying levels of the Zr-90 nucleus. The data obtained in this way were compared with the predictions of various models.

# Radiative Strength Functions for Dipole Transitions in Zr-90

## Key claim

The absolute values and energy distribution of dipole transition strength in Zr-90 were extracted from previously measured (p,gamma) reaction data on Y-89, revealing an excess of strength below 11 MeV compared to a simple Lorentzian extrapolation of the giant dipole resonance, particularly at higher proton energies. The authors note that the model-dependent extraction relies on the statistical model being justifiably applicable, and that the reliability depends on the accuracy of the transmission coefficients and level densities used.

## Method

Partial cross sections for the Y-89(p,gamma)Zr-90 reaction measured at proton energies between 2.17 and 5.00 MeV were averaged over resonances and compared with Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations to extract radiative strength functions for dipole (E1+M1) transitions. The optical potential parameters were adjusted from global systematics to fit available (p,n), (p,p), and (p,p') data on neighboring nuclei, and level densities were calculated using the back-shifted Fermi gas model with parameters a=8.95(41) MeV^-1 and Delta=1.97(30) MeV. The radiative strength functions were determined by reproducing absolute experimental partial cross sections while fixing all other model parameters.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy in Zr-90: Sn = 11970(3) keV
- Proton binding energy in Zr-90: Sp = 8354.8(16) keV
- Proton energy range for the (p,gamma) measurements: 2.17 to 5.00 MeV
- Excitation energy of the compound nucleus: up to 12.5 MeV (at Ep=4.24 MeV)
- Level density parameter used: a = 8.95(41) MeV^-1 with energy shift Delta = 1.97(30) MeV
- GDR parameters used for standard Lorentzian: Er = 16.7 MeV, Gamma_r = 4.2 MeV, sigma_r = 211 mb
- Energy range of extracted dipole transitions: 5.9 to 11.1 MeV (from final state energies 0.0 to 5.2 MeV at fixed E_lambda=11.1 MeV)
- Averaging interval for cross sections at Ep=2.2-3.4 MeV: Delta_Ep = 1.2 MeV
- Averaging interval for cross sections at Ep=2.17-5.0 MeV: Delta_Ep = 0.5 MeV
- Energy gap between 2p1/2 and 1g9/2 proton orbitals: 2670(90) keV
- Proton pairing energy: 3593(8) keV
- Neutron shell-model gap: 4445(8) keV
- Neutron pairing energy: 4093(12) keV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dipole strength in Zr-90 from photoabsorption experiments by Varlamov (2003) and from bremsstrahlung measurements by Schwengner (2007), which revealed resonance-like structures at 6.5 and 9 MeV and around 11.5 MeV. The (p,gamma) reaction on Y-89 provides complementary data to photoabsorption studies, which cannot be supplemented by (n,gamma) data due to the absence of a stable target nucleus. The results enable more reliable identification of structures in the dipole strength distribution and improved modeling of photonuclear reactions relevant to p-process nucleosynthesis and nuclear technology applications involving materials in the A=90 region.
