# Peripheral, ultrarelativistic production of particles in heavy ion collisions

> The cross sections for the production of two-pions in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, calculated in the impact parameter Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA), are presented. Differential distributions in impact parameter, dipion invariant mass, single pion and dipion rapidity, pion transverse momentum and pion pseudorapidity are shown. The $γγ\to π^+ π^-$ subprocess constitutes a background to the $A A \to A ρ^0 (\to π^+ π^-) A$ process, initiated by emission of a photon by one of colliding nuclei. Only a part of the dipion invariant mass distribution associated with the $γγ$-collisions can be visible as the cross section for the $A A \to A ρ^0 A$ reaction is very large. Differential distributions for two $ρ^0$ meson production in exclusive ultraperipheral, ultrarelativistic collisions via a double scattering mechanism are presented. The cross section for $γA \to ρ^0 A$ is parametrized based on a calculation from the literature. Smearing of $ρ^0$ masses is taken into account. The results of calculations are compared to experimental data obtained at RHIC and to the contribution of the two-photon mechanism. The double scattering mechanism populates larger $ρ^0 ρ^0$ invariant masses and larger rapidity distances between the two $ρ^0$ mesons compared to the $γγ$ fusion. It gives a significant contribution to the $A A \to A A π^+ π^- π^+ π^-$ reaction. Some observables related to charged pions are presented. The results of our calculation are compared with the STAR collaboration results for four charged pion production. While the shape in invariant mass of the four-pion system is very similar to the measured one, the predicted cross section constitutes only 20 \% of the measured one. \end{abstract}

## Key claim

The double-scattering mechanism for exclusive $\rho^0 \rho^0$ production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions gives a significant contribution to the $A A \to A A \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^+ \pi^-$ reaction, but the predicted cross section constitutes only 20% of the measured STAR collaboration result, despite the shape in invariant mass of the four-pion system being very similar.

## Method

The cross sections for two-pion production via $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+ \pi^-$ and $\pi^0 \pi^0$ subprocesses were calculated using the impact parameter Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) with an absorption factor $\theta(|{\bf b}_1-{\bf b}_2|-2R_A)$ to exclude overlapping nuclei. For two $\rho^0$ meson production, both the photon-photon fusion mechanism (using a VDM-Regge parametrization of the elementary cross section) and a double-scattering mechanism (where each $\rho^0$ is produced independently via $\gamma A \to \rho^0 A$ with a constant $d\sigma/dt$ of 420 mb/GeV$^2$ for RHIC and 450 mb/GeV$^2$ for LHC) were calculated and compared. The $\rho^0$ decay into charged pions was included to apply experimental cuts.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for single $\rho^0$ production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC: 596 mb.
- The constant $d\sigma(\gamma+A \to \rho^0 A; t=0)/dt$ used: 420 mb/GeV$^2$ for RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV) and 450 mb/GeV$^2$ for LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 5.5 TeV).
- The predicted cross section for four charged pion production constitutes only 20% of the measured STAR collaboration result.
- The impact parameter distribution for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 3.5 TeV is nonzero starting from $b = 2 R_A \approx$ 14 fm.
- The $\rho^0(1450)$ resonance contribution to the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution is 5/0.27 times smaller than for $\rho^0(770)$.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of exclusive meson production in ultraperipheral collisions, particularly the photon-photon mechanism for $\rho^0 \rho^0$ production discussed by Kłusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2013) and the single $\rho^0$ production models of Klein and Nystrand (1999), Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (2003), and Goncalves and Machado (2005). The double-scattering mechanism for two vector meson production was previously discussed only by Klein and Nystrand (1999). The comparison with STAR collaboration data (2010) for four charged pion production shows that while the double-scattering mechanism reproduces the shape of the invariant mass distribution, the predicted cross section is only 20% of the measured value, indicating missing contributions or the need for refined modeling.
