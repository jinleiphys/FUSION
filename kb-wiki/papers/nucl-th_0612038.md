# Chiral phase transition and color superconductivity in an extended NJL model with higher-order multi-quark interactions

> The chiral phase transition and color superconductivity in an extended NJL model with eight-quark interactions are studied. The scalar-type nonlinear term hastens the chiral phase transition,the scalar-vector mixing term suppresses effects of the vector-type linear term and the scalar-diquark mixing term makes the coexisting phase wider.

## Key claim

The scalar-type nonlinear term (σ⁴) hastens the chiral phase transition and moves the critical endpoint to higher temperature and lower chemical potential, while the scalar-vector mixing term (σ²ω²) cancels effects of the vector-type linear term (ω²), and the scalar-diquark mixing term (σ²Δ²) widens the coexisting phase of chiral and diquark condensates, but these conclusions depend on the specific parameter choices (A, B, C) and the mean-field approximation used.

## Method

The authors extended the standard two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by including eight-quark interactions: a scalar-type nonlinear term (σ⁴), a scalar-vector mixing term (σ²ω²), and a scalar-diquark mixing term (σ²Δ²). They applied the mean-field approximation (MFA) to study the chiral phase transition and color superconductivity in the μ–T plane, with parameters g₂,₀, g₄,₀, and the cutoff Λ fixed to reproduce the pion mass (138 MeV), sigma meson mass (650 MeV), and pion decay constant (93.3 MeV), while the remaining couplings (g₀,₂, g₂,₂, d₀,₂, d₂,₂) were treated as free parameters with typical values (A, B, C) = (1.0, 0, 0), (0.8, 0.2, 0), and (0, 0, 0.2).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass fixed at 138 MeV, sigma meson mass at 650 MeV, and pion decay constant at 93.3 MeV to determine g₂,₀, g₄,₀, and Λ.
- Current quark masses of up and down quarks assumed to be 5.5 MeV.
- For the typical case, parameters are set as g₀,₂ = A g₂,₀, g₂,₂ = B g₂,₀/σ₀², d₀,₂ = 0.6 g₂,₀, d₂,₂ = C d₀,₂/σ₀², with (A, B, C) = (1.0, 0, 0), (0.8, 0.2, 0), and (0, 0, 0.2).
- The scalar density σ₀ at T = μ = 0 is used in the parameterization, with G_σ = 2g₂,₀ + 12g₄,₀σ₀².

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies that typically included only four-quark and six-quark interactions (the 't Hooft determinant), such as those by Buballa (2005) and Kashiwa et al. (2005), which predicted a critical endpoint at lower temperature and higher chemical potential than lattice QCD results (Kogut and Sinclair, 1988; Zantow et al., 2002). The inclusion of eight-quark interactions addresses the need to suppress the vector-type interaction at high density, analogous to nonlinear meson terms in relativistic meson-nucleon theory (Walecka, 1974), and enables more realistic phase diagrams for dense QCD matter where color superconductivity and chiral symmetry breaking coexist.
