# Multichannel Dyson equation: derivation and analysis

> In a recent letter [Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 216401] we presented the multichannel Dyson equation (MCDE) in which two or more many-body Green's functions are coupled. In this work we will give further details of the MCDE approach. In particular we will discuss: 1) the derivation of the MCDE and the definition of the space in which it is to be solved; 2) the rationale of the approximation to the multichannel self-energy; 3) a diagrammatic analysis of the MCDE; 4) the recasting of the MCDE on an eigenvalue problem with an effective Hamiltonian that can be solved using standard numerical techniques. This work mainly focuses on the coupling between the one-body Green's function and the three-body Green's function to describe photoemission spectra, but the MCDE method can be generalized to the coupling of other many-body Green's functions and to other spectroscopies.

## Key claim

The multichannel Dyson equation (MCDE) provides an alternative to the standard single-channel Dyson equation by coupling two or more many-body Green's functions, and when applied to the coupling of the one-body and three-body Green's functions, it naturally places quasiparticles and satellites on an equal footing; however, the method is demonstrated on the Hubbard dimer at 1/4 and 1/2 filling, where it yields exact spectral functions for all interaction strengths, whereas alternative approaches based on the single-channel Dyson equation such as GW cannot yield exact results.

## Method

The authors derive the multichannel Dyson equation by starting from the three-body Green's function (3-GF) and partitioning its non-interacting (Hartree-Fock) form into a one-body channel and a three-body channel. They construct a multichannel self-energy that couples these channels, approximating it at the RPA+exchange (RPAx) level using kernels from the electron-hole and particle-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation, which include only first-order interactions between pairs of particles. The MCDE is then recast as an eigenvalue problem with an effective Hamiltonian, solvable with standard numerical techniques.

## Key numbers

- The non-interacting three-body Green's function in its matrix representation has a satellite block with poles at energies of the form \(\omega = \epsilon^0_i - (\epsilon^0_j - \epsilon^0_l)\), where \(\epsilon^0_i\) are mean-field orbital energies (Eq. \ref{convG0:eq}).
- The non-interacting particle-particle 2-GF has poles at energies \(\omega = \epsilon^0_i + \epsilon^0_j\) with a prefactor \((1-f_i-f_j)\) (Eq. \ref{G0ppnon:eq}).
- The RPAx kernel for the electron-hole Bethe-Salpeter equation is \(\Xi^{\text{RPA}x}_{ij;mo} = -iv_{imoj} + iv_{imjo}\), where \(v_{ijom}\) are two-electron Coulomb integrals (Eq. \ref{potential:eq}).

## Context

This work extends the authors' recent letter (Riva et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 216401) by providing a detailed derivation of the multichannel Dyson equation, analysis of the multichannel self-energy approximation, and its recasting as an eigenvalue problem. The approach contrasts with standard single-channel methods such as GW, which fail to describe satellites accurately at large interaction strengths. The MCDE method can be generalized to couple other many-body Green's functions and to other spectroscopies, and it avoids self-consistency issues and unphysical solutions that plague some alternative approaches.
