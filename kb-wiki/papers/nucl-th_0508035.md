# Pairing properties of nucleonic matter employing dressed nucleons

> A survey of pairing properties of nucleonic matter is presented that includes the off-shell propagation associated with short-range and tensor correlations. For this purpose, the gap equation has been solved in its most general form employing the complete energy and momentum dependence of the normal self-energy contributions. The latter correlations include the self-consistent calculation of the nucleon self-energy that is generated by the summation of ladder diagrams. This treatment preserves the conservation of particle number unlike approaches in which the self-energy is based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. A huge reduction in the strength as well as temperature and density range of ${}^3S_1$-${}^3D_1$ pairing is obtained for nuclear matter as compared to the standard BCS treatment. Similar dramatic results pertain to ${}^1S_0$ pairing of neutrons in neutron matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the full energy and momentum dependence of the nucleon self-energy from short-range and tensor correlations, via self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations, leads to a huge reduction in the strength and temperature/density range of both \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) pairing in nuclear matter and \(^1S_0\) pairing in neutron matter compared to standard BCS treatments. The authors note that this treatment preserves particle number conservation, unlike approaches based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation.

## Method

The authors solved the generalized gap equation using dressed nucleon propagators obtained from self-consistent T-matrix calculations, which include the complete energy and momentum dependence of the normal self-energy from ladder diagram summation. The two-particle propagator was constructed from the full spectral functions of dressed nucleons rather than the delta-function quasiparticle approximation used in BCS. The SCGF calculations were performed for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at temperatures above the critical temperature for pairing, using both the CDBonn and Argonne V18 realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (\(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the BCS gap at the Fermi momentum for the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel is 8.6 MeV using CDBonn and 7.6 MeV using ArV18.
- For pure neutron matter at \(\rho = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\), the BCS gap at the Fermi momentum for the \(^1S_0\) channel is 1.4 MeV using CDBonn and 1.1 MeV using ArV18.
- The occupation probability at zero momentum in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is 0.89 for CDBonn and 0.87 for ArV18; in neutron matter at half density the corresponding values are 0.968 and 0.963.
- The imaginary part of the self-energy for nucleons at momentum 225 MeV/c in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density reaches a minimum around -100 MeV at \(\omega \approx 1.7\) GeV for ArV18, while for CDBonn the minimum is about -35 MeV at \(\omega \approx 0.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier SCGF calculations by Muther, Dickhoff, and collaborators that demonstrated the dominant influence of short-range and tensor correlations on single-particle properties in nuclear matter. It directly addresses the discrepancy between BCS calculations predicting a \(\sim\)10 MeV gap for proton-neutron pairing in infinite nuclear matter (Arseneau (1990), Vonderfecht (1991), Baldo (1992), Takatsuka (1993), and Baldo (1995)) and the absence of such strong pairing in finite nuclei. The results are consistent with earlier studies by Bozek (1999, 2003) using simplified interactions and by Dickhoff (1999) studying phase shifts of dressed nucleons, all showing substantial suppression of pairing strength. The work enables future fully self-consistent calculations that include both normal and anomalous self-energies below the critical temperature for realistic interactions.
