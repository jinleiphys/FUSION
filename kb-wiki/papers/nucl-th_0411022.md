# The compact star in the SU(3) sigma model

## Key claim

The linear chiral SU(3) × SU(3) model, when applied via relativistic mean field theory, produces a neutron star with a maximum mass close to 1.5 solar masses and radius 11 km, but the authors note that the FST equation of state gives more massive neutron stars of approximately 1.7–1.8 solar masses, and that the best fit to observational data for EXO 0748-675 prefers the more traditional FST approach.

## Method

The authors start from the linear chiral SU(3) sigma model and derive an effective relativistic mean field (RMF) theory by shifting meson fields to obtain vacuum expectation values, then fitting model parameters to observed meson masses (π, K, φ, φ*, δ) and to symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy, density, incompressibility, symmetry energy). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using the resulting equations of state to compute mass-radius relations and gravitational binding energies for protoneutron stars (hot, neutrino-trapped), cold neutron stars (with hyperons), and quark stars (using a quark mean field model).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: close to 1.5 M⊙ (solar masses), with radius 11 km
- FST equation of state gives more massive neutron stars: approximately 1.7–1.8 M⊙
- Nuclear matter saturation density n₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³ (SU(3) model), with binding energy E₀ = −16.31 MeV, incompressibility K = 194.9 MeV, symmetry energy J = 34.70 MeV
- Protoneutron star radius: approximately 20–50 km
- Cold neutron star radius: approximately 10–14 km
- Sigma meson mass from meson spectroscopy fit: mσ = 502.27 MeV
- Model parameters from meson fitting: μ = 552.274 MeV, λ = 45.11, κ = −8.917, c = 3412.25

## Context

This work extends the linear chiral SU(3) sigma model approach of Papazoglou et al. (2000) to compact star astrophysics, comparing results with the standard RMF parameterizations of Walecka (1974), the FST model of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997), and the GM3 and TM1 parameter sets of Glendenning (1985) and Sugahara and Toki (1994). The quark star treatment builds on the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and the quark mean field model of Shen and Toki (2000). The results enable comparison of chiral-model-based equations of state with observational constraints from compact stars such as SAX J1808.4-3658 and EXO 0748-675, though the authors find the SU(3) model predicts a quark star with unphysical positive binding energy.
