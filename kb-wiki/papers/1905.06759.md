# Coherent and incoherent vector meson electroproduction in the future electron-ion colliders: the hot-spot predictions

> One of the more promising observables to probe the high energy regime of the QCD dynamics in the future Electron-Ion Colliders (EIC) is the exclusive vector meson production cross section in coherent and incoherent interactions. Such processes measure the average spatial distribution of gluons in the target as well the fluctuations and correlations in the gluon density. In this paper we present a comprehensive analysis of the energy, photon virtuality, atomic number and momentum transfer dependencies of the coherent and incoherent cross sections considering two different models for the nuclear profile function. In particular, we present the predictions of the hot-spot model, which assumes the presence of subnucleonic degrees of freedom and an energy-dependent profile. Our results indicate that the analysis of the ratio between the incoherent and coherent cross sections and the momentum transfer distributions in the future EIC can be useful to constrain the description of the hadronic structure at high energies.

## Key claim

The ratio between incoherent and coherent vector meson production cross sections in future electron-ion colliders can discriminate between nuclear profile models that include subnucleonic hot-spot degrees of freedom and those that treat nucleons as smooth Gaussian distributions, provided the analysis covers a range of photon virtualities and atomic numbers.

## Method

The authors computed coherent and incoherent exclusive vector meson production cross sections using the color dipole formalism with a Glauber-Gribov dipole-nucleus cross section. They employed two nuclear profile models: a "nu" model where each nucleon is a Gaussian centered at Woods-Saxon positions, and a "hs" (hot-spot) model where each nucleon contains a fluctuating number of subnucleonic Gaussian hot spots whose mean number depends on Bjorken-x via a power-law. The dipole-proton amplitude was taken from the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff saturation model, and vector meson wave functions used the boosted-Gaussian model.

## Key numbers

- For coherent production on a Gold target at fixed Q^2, cross sections increase with energy W and decrease with Q^2; the increase with W is steeper for heavier mesons (J/Psi, Upsilon) than for light mesons (rho, phi).
- For incoherent production on Gold, the hs model predicts larger cross sections than the nu model for all Q^2 values and atomic numbers; the enhancement is quantified by the ratio of incoherent to coherent cross sections.
- The ratio of incoherent to coherent cross sections decreases at smaller photon virtuality and for larger nuclei; for rho mesons on Gold at Q^2 = 0.05 GeV^2, the ratio can differ between nu and hs models by amounts that depend on energy.
- Statistical uncertainty from 200 configurations is approximately 10% for incoherent cross sections and 3% for coherent cross sections, but for rho mesons on Gold at Q^2 = 0.05 GeV^2 the incoherent uncertainty can reach 30%.
- The t-distributions show coherent cross sections dominate at small |t| with a sharp forward diffraction peak, while incoherent cross sections dominate at large |t|; the difference between hs and nu models for incoherent cross sections increases with increasing momentum transfer t.

## Context

This work extends the hot-spot model previously applied to proton targets (Cepila 2016) and nuclear targets (Cepila 2017, 2018) to a comprehensive survey of light and heavy vector mesons across multiple nuclei (Au, Xe, Ca) and energies relevant for the future EIC (sqrt(s) ~ 100 GeV) and LHeC (sqrt(s) ~ 1000 GeV). It builds on earlier studies of coherent and incoherent vector meson production in eA collisions (e.g., Mantysaari 2017, 2018) and the Glauber-Gribov approach to nuclear structure functions (Armesto, Kowalski 2006). The results enable future experimental measurements at EIC and LHeC to constrain whether subnucleonic fluctuations are present in the nuclear gluon distribution at high energies.
