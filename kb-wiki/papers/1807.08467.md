# Nonlinear chiral transport from holography

> Nonlinear transport phenomena induced by the chiral anomaly are explored within a 4D field theory defined holographically as $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-$AdS_5$. First, in presence of external electromagnetic fields, a general form of vector and axial currents is derived. Then, within the gradient expansion up to third order, we analytically compute all (over 50) transport coefficients. A wealth of higher order (nonlinear) transport phenomena induced by chiral anomaly are found beyond the Chiral Magnetic and Chiral Separation Effects. Some of the higher order terms are relaxation time corrections to the lowest order nonlinear effects. The charge diffusion constant and dispersion relation of the Chiral Magnetic Wave are found to receive anomaly-induced non-linear corrections due to e/m background fields. Furthermore, there emerges a new gapless mode, which we refer to as {\it Chiral Hall Density Wave}, propagating along the background Poynting vector.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact, non-renormalized forms of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE) for arbitrary electromagnetic fields, but the nonlinear corrections and higher-order transport coefficients are computed only within a gradient expansion up to third order, under the probe limit where back-reaction on energy-momentum conservation is ignored.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality, specifically a 4D field theory dual to \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\), to compute constitutive relations for vector and axial currents. They perform a gradient expansion up to third order in derivatives, splitting charge densities and external fields into constant backgrounds and spacetime-dependent fluctuations, and analytically compute all transport coefficients (over 50) from the holographic model.

## Key numbers

- Charge diffusion constant: \(\mathcal{D}_0 = 1/2\) (in units where \(\pi T = 1\))
- DC electrical conductivity: \(\sigma_e^0 = 1\) (in units where \(\pi T = 1\))
- DC CME/CSE conductivity: \(\sigma_\chi^0 = 12\kappa\) (with \(\kappa\) the anomaly coefficient)
- Second-order transport coefficient \(\sigma_m^0 = 72(2\log 2 - 1) \kappa^2\)
- Second-order transport coefficient \(\mathcal{D}_H^0 = 72(3\log 2 - 2) \kappa^2\)
- Second-order transport coefficient \(\sigma_{a\chi H}^0 = 6\log 2 \, \kappa\)
- Second-order transport coefficient \(\sigma_{\chi H}^0 = 72\log 2 \, \kappa^2\)
- Relaxation time for electric field: \(\tau_e = (\log 2)/2\)
- Relaxation time for axial field: \(\tau_\chi = 12\log 2 \, \kappa\)
- Diffusion relaxation time: \(\tau_D = \pi/8\)
- Axial charge relaxation coefficient: \(\tau_{\bar\chi} = -(3\pi/2 + 3\log 2) \kappa\)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies by Bu, Demircik, and Lublinsky (2016, 2017) that computed linear and nonlinear transport in the same model, and compares with results from chiral kinetic theory (CKT) by various authors (2016, 2017). The paper enables future development of a fully self-consistent chiral magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) by providing the necessary nonlinear constitutive relations, and identifies a new gapless mode called the Chiral Hall Density Wave propagating along the background Poynting vector.
