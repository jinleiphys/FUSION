# The axial charge of the triton from lattice QCD

> The axial charge of the triton is investigated using lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Extending previous work at heavier quark masses, calculations are performed using three ensembles of gauge field configurations generated with quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 450 MeV. Finite-volume energy levels for the triton, as well as for the deuteron and diproton systems, are extracted from analysis of correlation functions computed on these ensembles, and the corresponding energies are extrapolated to infinite volume using finite-volume pionless effective field theory (FVEFT). It is found with high likelihood that there is a compact bound state with the quantum numbers of the triton at these quark masses. The axial current matrix elements are computed using background field techniques on one of the ensembles and FVEFT is again used to determine the axial charge of the proton and triton. A simple quark mass extrapolation of these results and earlier calculations at heavier quark masses leads to a value of the ratio of the triton to proton axial charges at the physical quark masses of $g_A^{^{3}{\rm H}}/g_A^p=0.91\substack{+0.07 \\ -0.09}$. This result is consistent with the ratio determined from experiment and prefers values less than unity (in which case the triton axial charge would be unmodified from that of the proton), thereby demonstrating that QCD can explain the modification of the axial charge of the triton.

## Key claim

The ratio of the triton to proton axial charges at the physical quark masses is determined to be \(g_A^{^{3}{\rm H}}/g_A^p=0.91\substack{+0.07 \\ -0.09}\) from a simple quark mass extrapolation of lattice QCD results at a pion mass of 450 MeV and earlier calculations at heavier quark masses; this result is consistent with the experimental ratio and prefers values less than unity, demonstrating that QCD can explain the modification of the axial charge of the triton, though the extrapolation is simple and the uncertainties are large.

## Method

Lattice QCD calculations were performed using three ensembles of gauge field configurations with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of clover fermions at a pion mass of 450 MeV and lattice spacings of \(a=0.1167(16)\) fm on volumes of spatial extent 2.80, 3.73, and 5.60 fm. Finite-volume energy levels for the triton, deuteron, and diproton were extracted from two-point correlation functions and extrapolated to infinite volume using finite-volume pionless effective field theory (FVEFT). The axial current matrix elements for the proton and triton were computed on the 3.73 fm ensemble using background field techniques with five values of the external axial field strength, and FVEFT was again used to determine the infinite-volume axial charges.

## Key numbers

* Triton binding energy at \(m_\pi=450\) MeV: \(\Delta E_{{}^3{\rm H}}^{\infty} = -32(23)\) MeV from infinite volume extrapolation using FVEFT.
* Deuteron binding energy at \(m_\pi=450\) MeV: \(7.2(3.2)\) MeV from FVEFT extrapolation.
* Dineutron binding energy at \(m_\pi=450\) MeV: \(11.6(3.6)\) MeV from FVEFT extrapolation.
* Infinite volume extrapolated Gamow-Teller matrix element at \(m_\pi=450\) MeV: \(\langle\mathbf{GT}\rangle_{L\to\infty}=0.938(41)\).
* Ratio of triton to proton axial charges at physical quark masses: \(g_A^{^{3}{\rm H}}/g_A^p=0.91\substack{+0.07 \\ -0.09}\).
* Experimental ratio of triton to proton axial charges: \(g_A^{{}^{3}{\rm H}}/g_A^p=0.9511(13)\) from analysis of precision tritium decay experiments (Baroni 2016).

## Context

This work extends the first lattice QCD calculation of the triton axial charge by Savage (2016), which was performed at an SU(3)-symmetric point with quark masses corresponding to \(m_\pi=806\) MeV, to significantly lighter quark masses (\(m_\pi=450\) MeV). The results demonstrate that the phenomenological reduction of the axial charge in the triton relative to the proton can be reproduced directly from QCD, supporting the understanding that two-nucleon interactions with the weak current (Gysbers 2019) are responsible for this modification. This enables future lattice QCD studies of axial matrix elements in heavier nuclei and provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting precision tritium beta-decay measurements used in neutrino mass searches and tests of physics beyond the Standard Model.
