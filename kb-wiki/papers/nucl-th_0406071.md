# FaCE: a tool for Three Body Faddeev calculations with core excitation

> FaCE is a self contained programme, with namelist input, that solves the three body Faddeev equations. It enables the inclusion of excitation of one of the three bodies, whilst the other two remain inert. It is particularly useful for obtaining the binding energies and bound state structure compositions of light exotic nuclei treated as three-body systems, given the three effective two body interactions. A large variety of forms for these interactions may be defined, and supersymmetric transformations of these potentials may be calculated whenever two body states need to be removed due to Pauli blocking.

## Key claim

FaCE solves the three-body Faddeev equations for bound and quasi-bound states of light exotic nuclei, with the specific capability to include excitation of one of the three bodies (the core) while the other two remain inert, provided the user supplies the effective two-body interactions and specifies which core states to include.

## Method

The program expands the three-body wavefunction in hyperspherical coordinates, using Jacobi polynomials for the hyper-angular part and a Laguerre basis for the hyper-radial part. It calculates the full three-body Hamiltonian matrix elements from the given two-body potentials (central, spin-orbit, tensor, spin-spin, and deformed multipole interactions) and performs diagonalization after optional reductions (isospin, orthonormal, or Feshbach) to obtain eigenenergies and eigenstates. Supersymmetric transformations of the two-body potentials are applied to remove Pauli-forbidden bound states before diagonalization.

## Key numbers

* Typical running time: 6 seconds on a 1.7 GHz Intel P4 processor.
* Memory required: 9 Mbytes of RAM and 12 MB of hard disk space.
* The code contains 13944 lines in the distributed program, including test data and outputs.
* The hyper-radial basis uses associated Laguerre polynomials \(L_n^5(z)\) with a scaling radius \(\rho_0\); the number of basis polynomials \(N_b\) and the number of Gauss-Laguerre quadrature points \(N_{lag}\) are set by the user.

## Context

This work extends the three-body Faddeev/hyperspherical formalism previously applied to Borromean systems such as \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li (reviewed in PhyRep) and to one-neutron halos like \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{19}\)C (Be11, C19), by allowing the core to have internal excited states. It enables more realistic structure calculations for light exotic nuclei and provides wavefunctions that can be used in reaction models for transfer, elastic/inelastic scattering, and breakup (Crespo 2000, Ganil 2001, Jim 2000).
