# Initial conditions and charged multiplicities in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> At ultra-relativistic energies the minijet production in heavy-ion collisions becomes sensitive to semi-hard parton rescatterings in the initial stages of the process. As a result global characteristics of the event, like the initial minijet density, become rather insensitive on the infrared cutoff that separates hard and soft interactions. This allows to define a nearly parameter-free {\it saturation cutoff} at which the initial conditions may be computed. As an application we study the centrality dependence of the charged particle multiplicity, which is compared with present RHIC data and predicted at higher energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that by including semi-hard parton rescatterings in the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions, a nearly parameter-free saturation cutoff can be defined at which the initial minijet density becomes insensitive to the infrared cutoff separating hard and soft interactions, allowing computation of initial conditions with only a weak dependence on the arbitrary saturation parameter \(c\) (chosen as 0.7 or 0.8).

## Method

The author computes the average number of minijets in nucleus-nucleus collisions using an eikonalized formula that includes semi-hard parton rescatterings, based on the nuclear parton distribution functions (GRV98LO) and a leading-order pQCD gluon-gluon cross-section with a \(k\)-factor of 2. A saturation cutoff \(p_{\text{sat}}\) is defined as the value of the infrared cutoff \(p_0\) where the minijet multiplicity reaches a fraction \(c\) of its limiting value (the maximum possible when the target is completely black). This saturation criterion is generalized to non-central collisions by using an effective nuclear distribution function that includes only partons from participating nucleons, based on the Glauber model. The charged particle multiplicity is then computed in a two-component model, where the soft part scales with the number of participants and the semi-hard part is given by the saturated minijet multiplicity converted via isentropic expansion and parton-hadron duality.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt s = 130\) GeV, the extracted soft fraction parameter \(x = 0.445\) for \(c=0.7\) and \(x = 0.453\) for \(c=0.8\), corresponding to semi-hard fractions \(F_{s.h.} = 0.805\) and \(F_{s.h.} = 0.817\), respectively.
- The saturated minijet multiplicity at a given energy shows approximately a 3% increase when the saturation parameter \(c\) is changed from 0.7 to 0.8.
- The inelastic \(pp\) cross-sections used are \(\sigma_{pp} = 39\) mbarn at \(\sqrt s = 130\) GeV, 42 mbarn at \(\sqrt s = 200\) GeV, and 75 mbarn at \(\sqrt s = 6000\) GeV.
- The saturation cutoff is required to be above approximately 0.7 GeV for the perturbative computation to be valid; below this value the parton distribution functions are extrapolated and give an unnaturally fast decrease of parton densities.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of semi-hard parton rescatterings developed by Treleani and collaborators (1990, 1991) and applies it to define a new saturation mechanism for minijet production, intermediate between initial-state saturation (based on DGLAP evolution with parton fusion) and final-state saturation (as in the EKRT model, 2000). The centrality dependence of charged multiplicities is compared with PHOBOS data at \(\sqrt s = 130\) GeV and used to predict results at \(\sqrt s = 200\) GeV and LHC energy (6 TeV), following the approach of Wang and Gyulassy (2001) and Kharzeev and Nardi (2001) who proposed using centrality dependence to distinguish between models. The results enable testing of initial-state models against future RHIC and LHC data.
