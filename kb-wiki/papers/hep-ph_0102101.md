# Muon-electron conversion in strange quark sea

> We study nuclear muon-electron (mu-e) conversion in the general framework of effective Lagrangian approach without referring to any specific realization of the physics beyond the standard model (SM) responsible for lepton flavor violation (LFV). All the possible types of short range interactions (non-photonic mechanisms), i.e. (pseudo-)scalar, (axial-)vector and tensor, are included in our formalism. We show that the mu-e conversion in the strange nucleon sea via the scalar interactions is comparable with that in the valence quarks. This provides an insight into the strange quark couplings beyond the SM. From the available experimental data on mu-e conversion and expected sensitivities of planned experiments we derived upper bounds on the generic LFV - parameters of mu-e conversion sensitive to the relevant u-,d- and s-quark couplings.

## Key claim

The contribution to nuclear muon-electron conversion from the strange quark sea via scalar interactions is comparable to that from valence quarks, providing a probe of strange quark couplings beyond the Standard Model. This result depends on the scalar nucleon form factors extracted from baryon mass data and the pion-nucleon sigma term, with uncertainties from current quark masses (which may vary by a factor of about 2).

## Method

The authors constructed a general effective Lagrangian for non-photonic (short-range) muon-electron conversion, including all possible Lorentz structures: (pseudo-)scalar, (axial-)vector, and tensor interactions. They transformed the quark-level operators to nucleon-level operators using on-mass-shell matching conditions, with nucleon form factors for vector and scalar currents derived from conservation of vector current (CVC) and from the QCD picture of baryon masses combined with experimental data on the pion-nucleon sigma term. Nuclear matrix elements for the coherent conversion process were calculated using proton and neutron densities from the literature and muon wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with Coulomb potential and vacuum polarization corrections, applied to the nuclei ²⁷Al, ⁴⁸Ti, and ¹⁹⁷Au.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on branching ratio for ⁴⁸Ti: R_μe^Ti ≤ 6.1 × 10⁻¹³ (90% C.L.) from SINDRUM II.
- Expected sensitivity for ²⁷Al: R_μe^Al ≤ 2 × 10⁻¹⁷ from the planned MECO experiment.
- Expected sensitivity for ¹⁹⁷Au: R_μe^Au ≤ 6 × 10⁻¹³ from the ongoing PSI experiment.
- Previous limit for ¹⁹⁷Au: R_μe^Au ≤ 2.0 × 10⁻¹¹.
- Expected sensitivity for ⁴⁸Ti: R_μe^Ti ≤ 10⁻¹⁸ from the planned PRIME experiment.
- Scalar nucleon form factors derived: G_S^u ≈ 5.1, G_S^d ≈ 4.3, G_S^s ≈ 2.5, using current quark masses m_u = 4.2 MeV, m_d = 7.5 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach to muon-electron conversion by explicitly including the strange quark sea contribution, which had been neglected in earlier naive quark model and MIT bag model treatments where G_S^s = 0. The formalism builds on prior nuclear structure calculations by Kosmas and collaborators (2000) and on the QCD picture of baryon masses from Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1978) and Cheng (1988). The derived constraints on generic lepton-flavor-violating parameters can be directly applied to any specific beyond-Standard-Model model (such as supersymmetric models) without repeating nucleon and nuclear structure calculations, enabling interpretation of upcoming data from SINDRUM II, MECO, and PRIME.
