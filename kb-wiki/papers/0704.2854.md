# Coupled-cluster theory for three-body Hamiltonians

> We derive coupled-cluster equations for three-body Hamiltonians. The equations for the one- and two-body cluster amplitudes are presented in a factorized form that leads to an efficient numerical implementation. We employ low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions and calculate the binding energy of He-4. The results show that the main contribution of the three-nucleon interaction stems from its density-dependent zero-, one-, and two-body terms that result from the normal ordering of the Hamiltonian in coupled-cluster theory. The residual three-body terms that remain after normal ordering can be neglected.

## Key claim

The main contribution of the three-nucleon interaction to the binding energy of He-4 arises from its density-dependent zero-, one-, and two-body terms that result from normal ordering of the Hamiltonian in coupled-cluster theory, while the residual three-body terms that remain after normal ordering can be neglected. This conclusion is based on calculations using a single cutoff value of 1.9 inverse femtometers and a restricted 3NF channel with total isospin T=1/2, total angular momentum J=1/2, and positive parity.

## Method

The authors derived coupled-cluster equations for three-body Hamiltonians at the singles and doubles (CCSD) level, presenting the equations for one- and two-body cluster amplitudes in a factorized form using a diagrammatic approach. They employed low-momentum two-nucleon interactions (V_low_k derived from the Argonne v18 potential) and corresponding low-momentum three-nucleon interactions based on the leading chiral 3NF, fitted to the binding energies of H-3 and He-4. The calculations were performed in a harmonic oscillator basis for He-4, with the 3NF matrix elements transformed from relative harmonic oscillator states to the single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of He-4 from Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation including only the dominant 3NF partial wave (T=1/2, J=1/2, positive parity): E = -28.20(5) MeV, which differs by only 100 keV from the exact energy including all partial waves (E = -28.30(5) MeV)
- Extrapolated infinite model space CCSD binding energy of He-4 with 3NFs: E_infinity = -28.09 MeV, very close to the exact result of -28.20(5) MeV
- Cutoff used for low-momentum interactions: lambda = 1.9 inverse femtometers
- In the largest calculations for He-4: n_o = 4 occupied orbitals and n_u = 220 unoccupied orbitals

## Context

This work extends coupled-cluster theory, which previously had been limited to two-nucleon interactions in nuclear structure calculations (Dean 2004, Kowalski 2004, Wloch 2005), to include three-body Hamiltonians. The approach builds on earlier work by Heisenberg and Mihaila (1999, 2000) who included selected 3NF contributions as density-dependent NN interactions in O-16, and on the development of low-momentum interactions (V_low_k) and corresponding 3NFs from chiral effective field theory (Vlowk3N). This enables ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations of nuclei beyond the lightest systems with consistent two- and three-nucleon interactions, and the method may also find applications in condensed-matter theory and quantum chemistry.
