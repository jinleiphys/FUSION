# Three-Nucleon Force and the $Δ$-Mechanism for Pion Production and Pion Absorption

> The description of the three-nucleon system in terms of nucleon and $Δ$ degrees of freedom is extended to allow for explicit pion production (absorption) from single dynamic $Δ$ de-excitation (excitation) processes. This mechanism yields an energy dependent effective three-body hamiltonean. The Faddeev equations for the trinucleon bound state are solved with a force model that has already been tested in the two-nucleon system above pion-production threshold. The binding energy and other bound state properties are calculated. The contribution to the effective three-nucleon force arising from the pionic degrees of freedom is evaluated. The validity of previous coupled-channel calculations with explicit but stable $Δ$ isobar components in the wavefunction is studied.

## Key claim

The paper extends a force model of the three-nucleon system, which already includes nucleon and Δ-isobar degrees of freedom, to also allow explicit pion production and absorption via single dynamic Δ de-excitation or excitation processes; this yields an energy-dependent effective three-body Hamiltonian, and the authors test whether previous coupled-channel calculations that treated the Δ-isobar as a stable particle remain valid.

## Method

The Faddeev equations for the trinucleon bound state were solved in momentum space using a force model that includes nucleon, Δ-isobar, and pion degrees of freedom, with the pion-nucleon-Δ vertex calibrated through P33 pion-nucleon scattering. The calculation employed two parametrizations of the baryonic interaction (labelled H and S, differing by hard or soft form factors in the transition potential), used the Paris potential as the purely nucleonic reference, and assumed no interactions in the pionic sector (QH1Q=0). The effective baryonic Hamiltonian was made diagonal in the three-baryon basis by approximating the pion-nucleon center-of-mass kinetic energy, an approximation validated by a binding-energy variation of less than 1 keV.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy variation between different approximations of the pion-nucleon c.m. kinetic energy: less than 1 keV, within numerical accuracy.
- The calculation included 18 purely nucleonic Faddeev amplitudes and 14 Faddeev amplitudes with a single Δ-isobar, in partial waves up to total pair angular momentum I=2.
- The force model was tested in the two-nucleon system above pion-production threshold, with the pion-nucleon-Δ vertex calibrated through pion-nucleon scattering up to 300 MeV pion lab energy.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel treatment of the trinucleon bound state by Sauer, Stadler, and Kortemeyer (1990s) that treated the Δ-isobar as a stable particle, by now allowing explicit pion production and absorption from dynamic Δ processes. It builds on the force model developed in Refs. [2] and [3] (cited in the paper) and tested in the two-nucleon system above pion threshold by Refs. [4] and [5]. The results enable a more complete microscopic description of three-nucleon forces and currents, and assess the validity of the simpler stable-Δ approximation used in earlier work such as Refs. [9,10] and [13].
