# Highly Sensitive Centrality Dependence of Elliptic Flow -- A Novel Signature of the Phase Transition in QCD

> Elliptic flow of the hot, dense system which has been created in nucleus-nucleus collisions develops as a response to the initial azimuthal asymmetry of the reaction region. Here it is suggested that the magnitude of this response shows a ``kinky'' dependence on the centrality of collisions for which the system passes through a first-order or rapid transition between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter. We have studied the system Pb(158AGeV) on Pb employing a recent version of the transport theoretical approach RQMD and find the conjecture confirmed. The novel phase transition signature may be observable in present and forthcoming experiments at CERN-SPS and at RHIC, the BNL collider.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of elliptic flow in Pb(158AGeV) on Pb collisions shows a "kinky" structure, a plateau followed by a second rise, when the system passes through a first-order phase transition between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter, as predicted by transport model calculations with a modified equation of state.

## Method

The author employed the transport theoretical approach RQMD (version 3.0) with a modified collision term that implements a first-order phase transition at T_c = 160 MeV with latent heat 467 MeV/fm^3, fitted to lattice QCD data. The scaled flow asymmetry A_2 = v_2 / alpha_x (where alpha_x is the spatial asymmetry of the reaction zone) was calculated as a function of impact parameter for Pb(158AGeV) on Pb collisions, using a local density approximation for the novel interaction term.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the first-order phase transition: T_c = 160 MeV
- Latent heat of the phase transition: 467 MeV/fm^3
- For Pb on Pb collisions, the spatial asymmetry parameter alpha_x varies between 0 and 0.50 for impact parameters less than 12 fm
- The scaled flow asymmetry A_2 shows a rapid increase for collisions with b >= 10 fm, corresponding to the QGP phase lifetime
- For collisions with b < 5 fm, a second rise in A_2 appears due to kinetic equilibration in the QGP phase
- The azimuthal asymmetry parameter alpha shows that essentially all observable asymmetry is created at times smaller than 4 fm/c in the QGP scenario

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier suggestion (Sorge, 1997) that elliptic flow may be sensitive to early pressure and the phase transition, improving upon the earlier RQMD (version 2.3) calculations that lacked sizable transverse pressure. The paper contrasts with hydrodynamic calculations by Ollitrault (1992, 1993) which show essentially no centrality dependence of the scaled flow asymmetry, and with NA49 experimental data (Appelshäuser et al., 1998) for semi-central Pb(158AGeV) on Pb collisions. The work enables experimental searches for the phase transition signature at CERN-SPS and future RHIC energies, and was completed concurrently with related work by Heiselberg and Levy (1998).
