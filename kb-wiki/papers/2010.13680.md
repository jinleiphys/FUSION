# QGP modification to single inclusive jets in a calibrated transport model

> We study inclusive jet suppression and modifications in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with a transport-based model. The model includes vacuum-like parton shower evolution at high-virtuality, a linearized transport for jet-medium interactions, and a simple ansatz for the jet-induced hydrodynamic response of the medium. Model parameters are calibrated to nuclear modification factors for inclusive hadron $R_{AA}^{h}$ and single inclusive jets $R_{AA}^{j}$ with cone size $R=0.4$ in 0-10% central Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions measured at the RHIC and LHC. The calibrated model consistently describes the cone-size dependent $R_{AA}^{j}(R)$, modifications to inclusive jet fragmentation functions and jet shape. We discuss the origin of these modifications by analyzing the medium-induced jet energy flow in this model and elucidate the interplay of hard parton evolution and jet-induced medium response. In particular, we demonstrate that the excess of soft hadrons at $p_T\sim 2$ GeV/$c$ in jet fragmentation function and jet shape at large $r=\sqrt{Δη^2+Δϕ^2}$ are consequences of both soft medium-induced gluon radiation and jet-induced medium excitation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a transport-based model with a single matching scale \(Q_0\) between vacuum-like parton shower evolution and in-medium transport can simultaneously describe inclusive jet and hadron suppression in central nuclear collisions at both RHIC and LHC, but only after calibrating \(Q_0\) as independent parameters for each collision system and treating the jet-induced medium response with a simplified hydrodynamic ansatz.

## Method

The model combines three stages: (1) vacuum-like parton shower evolution from the hard scale down to a matching scale \(Q_0\) using Pythia8, (2) in-medium evolution via the linearized partonic transport model LIDO, which includes elastic collisions (separated into soft diffusion and hard two-body collisions), medium-induced parton radiation (with LPM suppression corrections), and a simple ansatz for jet-induced hydrodynamic response of the medium, and (3) final vacuum evolution and hadronization outside the QGP using Pythia8. Model parameters \(Q_0\) for RHIC and LHC are calibrated to nuclear modification factors \(R_{AA}^h\) for inclusive hadrons and \(R_{AA}^j\) for single inclusive jets with cone size \(R=0.4\) in 0-10% central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^j\) for single inclusive jets with cone size \(R=0.4\) in 0-10% central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV used for calibration.
* Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^h\) for inclusive hadrons in the same collision systems used for calibration.
* Matching scale \(Q_0\) treated as independent parameters: \(Q_{\text{LHC}}\) for Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV and \(Q_{\text{RHIC}}\) for Au-Au at 200 GeV.
* Soft hadron excess observed at \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV/\(c\) in jet fragmentation functions and jet shape at large \(r = \sqrt{\Delta\eta^2 + \Delta\phi^2}\).
* Minimum energy threshold for transport equation: \(E_{\text{min}} = 4T\) in the co-moving frame.
* Debye screening mass: \(m_D = \sqrt{3/2} g(\mu_{\text{min}}) T\) for three-flavor QGP.
* Momentum transfer separation scale: \(Q_{\text{cut}} = \min\{2 m_D, \sqrt{6ET}\}\).
* Running coupling constant: \(\alpha_s(Q, T) = \frac{4\pi}{9} \frac{1}{\ln(\max\{Q^2, \mu_{\text{min}}^2\}/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2)}\) with \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 0.2\) GeV.
* Parton energy loss in 0-10% Pb-Pb collisions: elastic energy loss dominates for \(E < 10\)–\(20\) GeV; radiative energy loss grows as \(\ln E\) at high energy and dominates above that range.
* Light quark radiative energy loss without LPM effect increases faster than \(\ln(E)\) at high energy.
* Averaged fluid velocity at freeze-out: \(v_r = 0.6c\) for central nuclear collisions.

## Context

This work extends the LIDO transport model (Ke, 2018) by adding a matching procedure between vacuum-like and in-medium evolution and a simplified hydrodynamic response ansatz, building on prior approaches such as the Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model (He, 2015; Cao, 2017), MARTINI (Schenke, 2009), and the MATTER event generator (Majumder, 2013) combined within the JETSCAPE framework (Cao, 2017; Kauder, 2018; Putschke, 2019). The calibrated model enables systematic study of the interplay between medium-induced gluon radiation and jet-induced medium excitation in modifying jet fragmentation functions and jet shapes, and provides a determination of the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}\) from simultaneous description of hadron and jet suppression data.
