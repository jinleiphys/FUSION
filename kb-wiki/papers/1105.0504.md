# Theoretical study of the two-proton halo candidate $^{17}$Ne including contributions from resonant continuum and pairing correlations

> With the relativistic Coulomb wave function boundary condition, the energies, widths and wave functions of the single proton resonant orbitals for $^{17}$Ne are studied by the analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC) approach within the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Pairing correlations and contributions from the single-particle resonant orbitals in the continuum are taken into consideration by the resonant Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach, in which constant pairing strength is used. It can be seen that the fully self-consistent calculations with NL3 and NLSH effective interactions mostly agree with the latest experimental measurements, such as binding energies, matter radii, charge radii and densities. The energy of $π$2s$_{1/2}$ orbital is slightly higher than that of $\pi1d_{5/2}$ orbital, and the occupation probability of the $(π$2s$_{1/2})^2$ orbital is about 20%, which are in accordance with the shell model calculation and three-body model estimation.

# Theoretical study of the two-proton halo candidate $^{17}$Ne including contributions from resonant continuum and pairing correlations

## Key claim
The fully self-consistent RMF+ACCC+BCS calculations with NL3 and NLSH effective interactions mostly agree with experimental binding energies, matter radii, charge radii and densities for $^{17}$Ne, but the two-proton separation energy S$_{2p}$ is theoretically 0.2 MeV (NL3) or 0.14 MeV (NLSH) versus the experimental 0.93 MeV, indicating the model underestimates the binding of the valence protons.

## Method
Single-proton resonant orbital energies, widths and wave functions for $^{17}$Ne were obtained using the analytical continuation of the coupling constant (ACCC) approach within relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, with relativistic Coulomb wave function boundary conditions. Pairing correlations and continuum contributions were treated via the resonant Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approach with constant pairing strength $G = C/A$, where $C_p = 10.5$ (NL3) or 11.0 (NLSH) for protons and $C_n = 25.5$ (NL3) or 26.0 (NLSH) for neutrons, fitted to odd-even mass differences. The RMF+ACCC+BCS calculations were iterated to self-consistency, including bound proton states 1s$_{1/2}$, 1p$_{3/2}$, 1p$_{1/2}$ and resonant states 1d$_{5/2}$, 2s$_{1/2}$ in the pairing window.

## Key numbers
- Two-proton separation energy S$_{2p}$ for $^{17}$Ne: experimental 0.93 MeV; theoretical 0.2 MeV (NL3) and 0.14 MeV (NLSH)
- Experimental binding energy of $^{17}$Ne: 112.9276 MeV; theoretical values are about 1% larger
- Experimental rms matter radius of $^{17}$Ne: 2.75(7) fm; theoretical 2.86 fm (NL3) and 2.79 fm (NLSH)
- Experimental charge radius of $^{17}$Ne: 3.042(21) fm; theoretical 3.15 fm (NL3) and 3.07 fm (NLSH)
- Occupation probability of ($\pi$2s$_{1/2})^2$ orbital: about 20%; occupation probability of ($\pi$1d$_{5/2})^2$ orbital: about 40% (NLSH)
- Binding energy precision in self-consistent calculations: $10^{-3}$ MeV; density precision: $10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$
- Pairing gap for protons $\Delta_p = 0.9$ MeV; for neutrons $\Delta_n = 1.8$ MeV, extracted from three-point formula

## Context
This work extends the RMF+ACCC+BCS approach previously applied to neutron-rich Ni, Zr and Sn isotopes (Zhang 2009, Zhang 2012) to the proton-rich two-proton halo candidate $^{17}$Ne. It addresses the ongoing controversy over whether $^{17}$Ne has a (2s$_{1/2})^2$-dominant or (1d$_{5/2})^2$-dominant configuration, with the present results favoring (1d$_{5/2})^2$ dominance, consistent with Sagawa (2010) and Fortune, but in tension with Timofeyuk and Nakamura who suggest (2s$_{1/2})^2$ dominance. The calculations enable future inclusion of tensor forces and deformation effects to resolve the halo structure question.
