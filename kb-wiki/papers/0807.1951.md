# Phenomenological models of baryon

> In this paper, I present almost my works performed during my time at VNU. I was interested in the composite Skyrme model proposed by H. Y. Cheung and F. Gursey. The expanding for this model based on results obtained from the original Skyrme model given by T. H. R. Skyrme in 1961 was general and interesting.

## Key claim

The paper presents the author's work on the composite Skyrme model proposed by Cheung and Gursey, claiming that extending the original Skyrme model (Skyrme 1961) to a composite formulation with a constant SU(2) matrix P is general and interesting, but the numerical predictions for baryon static properties show significant deviations from experimental values, particularly for the iso-vector radii which diverge to infinity in the massless case.

## Method

The author formulates the composite Skyrme model by introducing a composite SU(2) configuration V_n = UPUP...PU, where U is the SU(2) matrix and P is a constant SU(2) matrix, then derives the Lagrangian density with the pion decay constant F_pi and dimensionless parameter e. The hedgehog soliton solution is used with the chiral angle F(r), and the Euler-Lagrange equation is solved numerically for the chiral angle. Quantization is performed via collective coordinates A(t) to obtain nucleon and delta masses, and static properties are computed from topological currents and vector currents. The model is extended to include a pion mass term and a supersymmetric formulation.

- For the massless composite model with n=4: iso-scalar magnetic radius sqrt(<r^2>_{M,I=0}) = 1.93 fm (experiment: 0.81 fm)

- For the massless composite model with n=4: neutron magnetic moment mu_neu = -0.66 mag (experiment: -1.91 mag)

- For the massive composite model with n=4: beta parameter = 0.0025

## Context

This work extends the composite Skyrme model proposed by Cheung and Gursey (1990), which generalizes the original Skyrme model (Skyrme 1961) and the V-T model as special cases for n=1 and n=2. The author builds on the approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) who computed baryon static properties using the Skyrme model, and on the work of Tuyen and Viet (1989) who considered the SU(2) x SU(2)/SU(2)_diag group for low-energy clustering. The paper identifies that the massless composite model gives divergent iso-vector radii, and attempts to improve predictions by adding a pion mass term, achieving better agreement for the axial coupling but worse agreement for the pion decay constant. The supersymmetric extension is proposed as a direction for future work to include contributions from meson superpartners.
