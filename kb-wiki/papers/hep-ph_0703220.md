# Phenomenological analysis of two-photon exchange effects in proton form factor measurements

> We perform a model-independent phenomenological analysis of experimental data on proton form factors ratio. We find that only one of the two photon exchange amplitudes, which we call $δG_M$, is responsible for the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization methods. The linearity of Rosenbluth plots implies that $δG_M$ is approximately linear function of $ε$. The slope of $δG_M$ is extracted from experimental data and is shown to be consistent with theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between proton form factor ratios measured via Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer can be explained by a single two-photon exchange amplitude, denoted δG_M, which is approximately linear in the virtual photon polarization parameter ε; the extracted slope of this amplitude agrees with theoretical calculations, contrary to earlier phenomenological analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent phenomenological analysis of existing experimental data on the proton form factor ratio G_E/G_M from both Rosenbluth (longitudinal-transverse separation) and polarization transfer measurements. They fitted the squared form factor ratios from both methods with third-order polynomials in Q², extracted the slope parameter b of the linearized two-photon exchange correction δG_M = (a + ε b) G_M, and compared the extracted b(Q²) with theoretical calculations of the elastic part of the two-photon exchange amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The Rosenbluth data fit polynomial: P_LT(Q²) = μ⁻²(1.0736 − 0.1864 Q² + 0.0358 Q⁴ + 0.0007 Q⁶) with χ² = 24.2 for 28 degrees of freedom (Q in GeV, μ = 2.793).
- The polarization transfer data fit polynomial: P_PT(Q²) = μ⁻²(1.1184 − 0.3256 Q² + 0.0323 Q⁴ − 0.0014 Q⁶) with χ² = 6.6 for 15 degrees of freedom.
- The extracted slope parameter b(Q²) = −0.0101/Q² + 0.0314 + 0.0008 Q² + 0.0005 Q⁴, with b varying from 0.01 to 0.05 over the Q² range considered.
- The relative size of the two-photon exchange correction δG_M/G_M = a + ε b never exceeds 0.025 if the unknown coefficient a is negative and equals about −b/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of two-photon exchange effects by Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2003) and by Tvaskis et al. (2005), but corrects their arbitrary assumptions about the ε-dependence of the two-photon exchange amplitudes. The extracted slope b is shown to be consistent with model-independent calculations of the elastic two-photon exchange contribution by Blunden et al. (2003) and by Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2006), with the remaining gap attributed to inelastic contributions. The analysis enables predictions for positron-proton scattering experiments, where the two-photon exchange corrections change sign, and suggests that averaging electron and positron Rosenbluth measurements could provide a new way to extract the true form factor ratio.
