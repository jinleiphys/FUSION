# Multiple chiral bands in $^{137}$Nd

> Two new bands have been identified in $^{137}$Nd from a high-statistics JUROGAM II gamma-ray spectroscopy experiment. Constrained density functional theory and particle rotor model calculations are used to assign configurations and investigate the band properties, which are well described and understood. It is demonstrated that these two new bands can be interpreted as chiral partners of previously known three-quasiparticle positive- and negative-parity bands. The newly observed chiral doublet bands in $^{137}$Nd represent an important support to the existence of multiple chiral bands in nuclei. The present results constitute the missing stone in the series of Nd nuclei showing multiple chiral bands, which becomes the most extended sequence of nuclei presenting multiple chiral bands in the Segré chart.

## Key claim

The two newly identified bands in \(^{137}\)Nd can be interpreted as chiral partners of previously known three-quasiparticle positive- and negative-parity bands, but the interpretation relies on constrained density functional theory and particle rotor model calculations that show the band properties are "well described and understood" rather than proven uniquely.

## Method

High-spin states in \(^{137}\)Nd were populated using the \(^{100}\)Mo(\(^{40}\)Ar,3n) reaction at a beam energy of 152 MeV, with prompt \(\gamma\)-rays detected by the JUROGAM II array of 24 clover and 15 coaxial germanium detectors. A total of \(5.1\times10^{10}\) prompt \(\gamma\)-ray coincidence events with fold \(\geq\) 3 were collected and analyzed using three-dimensional and four-dimensional coincidence matrices. Constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) and particle rotor model (PRM) calculations were performed to assign configurations and investigate band properties.

## Key numbers

* A total of \(5.1\times10^{10}\) prompt \(\gamma\)-ray coincidence events with fold \(\geq\) 3 were collected.
* The positive-parity band D3 consists of six levels with spins from \(31/2^+\) to \((41/2^+)\), interconnected by dipole transitions of 435, 482, 436, 488, and 549 keV.
* The negative-parity band D6 consists of five levels with spins from \(33/2^-\) to \(41/2^-\), interconnected by dipole transitions of 310, 382, 457, and 500 keV.
* The R\(_{ac}\) values for stretched dipole and quadrupole transitions are around 0.8 and 1.4, respectively, deduced from strong E2, E1, and M1 transitions in \(^{136}\)Nd.
* The CDFT calculations yield quadrupole deformation parameters \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.20, 28.9^\circ)\) for the configuration assigned to bands D2 and D3, and \((0.21, 29.5^\circ)\) for bands D5 and D6.

## Context

This work extends the study of multiple chiral doublet (M\(\chi\)D) bands in the \(A\approx130\) mass region, following identifications in \(^{133}\)Ce (Ayangeakaa 2013), \(^{135}\)Nd, \(^{136}\)Nd, and \(^{138}\)Nd. The results fill the "missing stone" in the sequence of Nd nuclei showing multiple chiral bands, making it the most extended such sequence in the Segré chart. The resemblance between the new negative-parity chiral doublet in \(^{137}\)Nd and those known in \(^{135}\)Nd and \(^{133}\)Ce supports the interpretation in terms of chiral vibration at low spin and a transition between chiral vibration and chiral rotation in odd-even Nd nuclei.
