# Fractionalized Quasiparticles in Dense Baryonic Matter

> I discuss a novel scenario on how baryonic matter could turn into a Fermi liquid of fractionalized baryons driven by high density inside compact stars. It involves the "hidden" local vector bosons $ρ$ and $ω$ together with the "hidden" scale symmetry dilaton $χ$, both taken to be "dual" to the quarks and gluons of QCD in the sense of quark $\leftrightarrow$ hadron duality and offers a possibility, exploiting the "Cheshire Cat Principle", to unify the "dichotomy" of baryons described in terms of skyrmions and fractional quantum Hall (FQH) droplets {\it and} to explore how the baryonic matter could behave as density goes way beyond the normal nuclear matter density to the density regime where "deconfined" quarks are presumed to figure. The question is raised as to whether the fractionalized characteristics of the "stuff" -- whatever they are -- can be "probed" in the dense star core of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that at densities several times normal nuclear matter density, baryonic matter may transition into a Fermi liquid of fractionally charged quasiparticles rather than deconfined quarks, with the caveat that this scenario relies on the "Cheshire Cat Principle" and hidden symmetries which are at best approximate in (3+1) dimensions for non-topological processes.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical framework combining hidden local symmetry (HLS) for the vector mesons ρ and ω with scale symmetry for the dilaton χ, treating them as dual to QCD's quarks and gluons. The analysis uses the Cheshire Cat Principle to unify two descriptions of baryons: skyrmions arising from the "infinite hotel" mechanism for N_f ≥ 2 flavors, and fractional quantum Hall droplets for the N_f = 1 flavor-singlet baryon B^(0). The unified baryon current is constructed from the homogeneous Wess-Zumino term in HLS theory, and the conditions for deforming between the two topological objects are examined through the behavior of the dilaton condensate and vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: n_0 ≃ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The density range of interest for compact star cores: 5 to 7 times n_0 (i.e., approximately 0.8 to 1.12 fm^{-3})
- The conformal sound velocity: v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3, which may appear precociously at a few times n_0
- The quark baryon charge is 1/N_c with N_c = 3, giving fractional charge 1/3
- The flavor-singlet axial-vector coupling g_A^0 ≈ 0.3 when the Chern-Simons term coupled to η' is included

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat Principle approach of Rho (1980s) and the hidden local symmetry framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) to dense baryonic matter, addressing the "dichotomy problem" raised by Komargodski (2018) regarding whether baryons are skyrmions or fractional quantum Hall droplets. The scenario enables a potential explanation for the "quenched g_A" puzzle in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions and the precocious onset of conformal sound velocity in massive compact stars, connecting to the work of Karasik (2019) on the deformation between skyrmions and FQH droplets via dialing quark masses.
