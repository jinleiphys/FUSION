# Eigenmodes of Decay and Discrete Fragmentation Processes

> Linear rate equations are used to describe the cascading decay of an initial heavy cluster into fragments. This representation is based upon a triangular matrix of transition rates. We expand the state vector of mass multiplicities, which describes the process, into the biorthonormal basis of eigenmodes provided by the triangular matrix. When the transition rates have a scaling property in terms of mass ratios at binary fragmentation vertices, we obtain solvable models with explicit mathematical properties for the eigenmodes. A suitable continuous limit provides an interpolation between the solvable models. It gives a general relationship between the decay products and the elementary transition rates.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytical proofs that the bra eigenvectors of the triangular transition matrix governing cascading binary fragmentation follow a power-law increase with fragment mass, specifically $Y^{\lambda}_j \propto j^{q(\lambda)}$, where the exponent $q(\lambda)$ depends on the scaling parameter $b$ of the fragmentation rates, but only for the specific case where the overall exponent $a=1$ and for a restricted family of scaling models.

## Method

The authors model cascading binary fragmentation using linear rate equations with a triangular matrix of transition rates $w_{jk}$ for a fragment of mass $k$ breaking into fragments $j$ and $k-j$. They expand the state vector of mass multiplicities into the biorthonormal basis of eigenmodes of this matrix, and derive recursion relations for the bra eigenvector components $Y^{\lambda}_j$ for models where the transition rates have a scaling property $w_{ij}=[f(i/j)+f(1-i/j)]\ j^{-a}$ with $a=1$. Analytical solutions are obtained for specific values of the splitting exponent $b$ ($b=-1,0,1$), and a continuous limit is used to interpolate between these solvable cases.

## Key numbers

* For $a=1$, $b=-1$ (or $b=0$), the bra eigenvector components follow $Y^{\lambda}_i \propto i^{2\lambda-1}$, giving exponent $q(\lambda)=2\lambda-1$.
* For $a=1$, $b=1$, the bra eigenvector components follow $Y^{\lambda}_j \propto j^{\lambda}$, giving exponent $q(\lambda)=\lambda$.
* A parabolic interpolation between these cases gives $q_{\rm int}(\lambda) = [\lambda (17-4b'^2) + 4b'^2-9] / 8$, where $b' \equiv b+1/2$.
* For $-1.2 \lesssim b \lesssim 1.2$, the continuous limit results and the parabolic interpolations do not differ by more than 4%.

## Context

This work extends the numerical evidence found in Giraud and Peschanski (1993) for power-law exponents governing bra eigenvector components in binary fragmentation processes, providing analytical proofs for specific scaling models. The models are related to QCD fragmentation as studied by Altarelli and Parisi (1977) and Gribov and Lipatov (1972), where the exponent $a=1$ is appropriate for energy-momentum fragmentation. The results enable the determination of microscopic fragmentation parameters $b$ from experimentally observable moments of multiplicity distributions, addressing the inverse problem of deriving elementary transition rates from decay products.
