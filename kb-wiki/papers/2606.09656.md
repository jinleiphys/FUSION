# Angular and Kinetic Properties of Scission Neutrons within Time-dependent Density Functional Theory

> Scission-neutron emission is investigated in $^{235}\mathrm{U}(\mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{th}},\mathrm{f})$, $^{239}\mathrm{Pu}(\mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{th}},\mathrm{f})$ and $^{252}\mathrm{Cf}(\mathrm{sf})$ within time-dependent density functional theory. Using a substantially larger simulation domain than in previous studies, the angular and energy distributions of emitted scission neutrons are extracted over a specific range of emission angles. At these angles, scission neutrons are absent below a threshold energy of roughly $1.5$--$2\,\mathrm{MeV}$, and instead contribute predominantly to the higher energy part of the prompt fission neutron spectrum. Combining the calculated scission-neutron spectrum with a Maxwellian model for the evaporated component, constrained by low-energy experimental data, reproduces the measured high-energy prompt-fission-neutron yield in both $^{239}\mathrm{Pu}(\mathrm{n}_{\mathrm{th}},\mathrm{f})$ and $^{252}\mathrm{Cf}(\mathrm{sf})$, whereas the evaporation-only model systematically underestimates it. This identifies a signature of scission neutrons already present in existing high-energy prompt fission neutron spectra and constitutes direct evidence for a non-negligible scission-neutron component in prompt fission neutron emission.

## Key claim

The paper claims that scission neutrons (SNs) emitted in the angular range 110°–150° relative to the light fission fragment direction have a finite energy threshold (1.5–2 MeV) below which no SNs appear, and that adding the TDDFT-calculated SN spectrum to a Maxwellian evaporation model reproduces the measured high-energy prompt fission neutron spectrum where the evaporation-only model systematically underestimates it. The hedge is that this signature is extracted only over a specific angular interval where the simulation domain is large enough for the SN properties to reach asymptotic limits; for other angles the computed SN yield does not saturate before reaching the boundary.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) simulations using the axially-symmetric harmonic-oscillator solver AxialHOHFB with the SkM* energy density functional and mixed surface-volume pairing, using a simulation domain (ellipsoid with semi-axes Z_max=70 fm and R_max=55 fm) about 3.2 times larger than in previous work. They identified scission-neutron regions by requiring the ratio of collective-flow to intrinsic kinetic energy density to exceed a threshold η=3, then extracted angular and kinetic distributions from the local neutron densities and currents over the time interval between scission and when neutrons reached the boundary. The evaporated-neutron component was modeled as a Maxwellian spectrum in the fragment rest frames, fitted exclusively to experimental data below the SN threshold energy (E ≤ 2 MeV for induced fission, E ≤ 1.53 MeV for spontaneous fission), and the total prompt fission neutron spectrum was compared to measured angle-energy distributions from Vorobyev (2017, 2018).

## Key numbers

- Simulation domain semi-axes: Z_max = 70 fm, R_max = 55 fm, corresponding to a volume about 3.2 times larger than in previous studies (Abdurrahman, 2024).
- SN threshold energy: E_thr ≈ 2 MeV for 235U(n_th,f) and 239Pu(n_th,f); E_thr ≈ 1.5 MeV for 252Cf(sf).
- SN spectrum peaks near 3–3.5 MeV for all three systems, with high-energy tail extending to roughly 18 MeV.
- Total SN yield for 239Pu(n_th,f): N^(SN)_tot = 0.19 ± 0.01, approximately 6.6% of the total prompt-neutron multiplicity ν̄ = 2.878 ± 0.013 (Carlson, 2018).
- Total SN yield for 252Cf(sf): N^(SN)_tot = 0.39 ± 0.02, approximately 10.4% of ν̄ = 3.7610 ± 0.0051 (Vorobyev, 2017).
- Best-fit evaporation model parameters for 239Pu(n_th,f) without SNs: T = 0.80 ± 0.03 MeV, ν̄_L/ν̄_H = 1.56 ± 0.03; with SNs: T = 0.78 ± 0.03 MeV, ν̄_L/ν̄_H = 1.39 ± 0.03.
- Best-fit evaporation model parameters for 252Cf(sf) without SNs: T = 0.84 ± 0.03 MeV, ν̄_L/ν̄_H = 1.46 ± 0.06; with SNs: T = 0.80 ± 0.03 MeV, ν̄_L/ν̄_H = 1.21 ± 0.05.

## Context

This work extends the TDDFT study of scission neutrons by Abdurrahman (2024) to a larger simulation domain, enabling the first extraction of correlated angular and kinetic distributions within a fully microscopic framework. It directly compares the TDDFT predictions to experimental angle-energy prompt fission neutron spectra for 239Pu(n_th,f) (Vorobyev, 2018) and 252Cf(sf) (Vorobyev, 2017), showing that the evaporation-only model systematically underestimates the high-energy yield while inclusion of the SN component resolves the discrepancy. The results provide a microscopic foundation for interpreting fission neutron spectra beyond the standard statistical-evaporation picture (Verbeke, 2018; Talou, 2021; Koning, 2023) and suggest that low-energy measurements below the SN threshold offer the best way to constrain the SN contribution, motivating new experimental campaigns with high precision and large angular coverage.
