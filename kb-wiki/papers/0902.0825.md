# Overview of event-by-event fluctuations

> Overview of event-by-event studies on relativistic heavy-ion collisions is given. I focus on fluctuation measurements and on theoretical ideas which appeared experimentally fruitful.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuation measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can reveal dynamical information about the system, but the interpretation is often ambiguous because the observed fluctuations are small (a few percent effect) and can arise from multiple physical mechanisms, including trivial correlations from global charge conservation, resonance decays, and geometrical fluctuations of the initial collision zone.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS (158 AGeV) and RHIC (up to 200 GeV), focusing on transverse momentum fluctuations, electric charge fluctuations, multiplicity fluctuations, balance functions, and elliptic flow fluctuations. Various fluctuation measures are discussed including Phi, sigma_dyn, Sigma, F, and nu_+-dyn, which are designed to isolate dynamical (non-statistical) correlations by comparing real events to mixed events where inter-particle correlations are removed by construction.

## Key numbers

- Phi(p_T) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 158 AGeV is less than or equal to 8 MeV, compared to the inclusive p_T dispersion of 200-250 MeV, making dynamical correlations a few percent effect
- F(p_T) in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV shows a strong dependence on the upper p_T cut-off, growing rapidly with p_T_max for N_part approximately 150
- Electric charge fluctuations quantified by Phi_q at SPS follow the trend dictated by global charge conservation, with Delta Phi_q (after subtracting GCC) agreeing with hadron gas predictions rather than QGP predictions
- The scaled variance of multiplicity distribution in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV shows a non-monotonic behavior as a function of N_part^projectile, contradicting predictions from HIJING, VENUS, UrQMD and HSD models
- Relative elliptic flow fluctuations measured by PHOBOS at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV are approximately 40 percent, but are dominated by geometrical fluctuations of the initial eccentricity rather than dynamical effects

## Context

This overview extends the earlier reviews of Heiselberg (2000) and Jeon (2003) on event-by-event physics, covering experimental results from NA49, CERES, WA98, PHENIX, STAR, and PHOBOS collaborations. The paper shows that while fluctuation measurements like the Phi measure (Gazdzicki 1992) and balance functions (Bass 2000, Jeon 2001) were proposed as clean probes of the quark-gluon plasma, the observed charge fluctuations at both SPS and RHIC do not clearly signal QGP formation, likely because fluctuations generated in the plasma phase are washed out during subsequent system evolution. The multiplicity fluctuations at SPS (Alt 2006) remain unexplained by current transport models, while elliptic flow fluctuations (Alver 2007) appear to be dominated by simple geometrical effects rather than dynamics, suggesting that hydrodynamic evolution is deterministic even at early stages.
