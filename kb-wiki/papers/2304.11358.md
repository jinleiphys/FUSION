# Neutrino-induced neutral- and charged-current reactions on $^{40}$Ar

> Neutrino-induced reactions on $^{40}$Ar are investigated by shell model for Gamow-Teller transitions and random-phase-approximation (RPA) for forbidden transitions. For the 1$^{+}$ multipole, an effective interaction in $sd$-$pf$ shell obtained by the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method from chiral interactions is used to study $B(GT)$, charged-current reaction $^{40}$Ar ($ν_e$, $e^{-}$) $^{40}$K, $B(M1)$ in $^{40}$Ar and neutral-current reaction $^{40}$Ar ($ν$, $ν$') $^{40}$Ar. A considerable quenching for spin modes is found in the analysis of $B(M1)$, and this quenching is taken into account for the evaluation of the cross sections of the neutral-current reaction. The sensitive dependence of the reaction cross sections on the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant, $g_A$, is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the neutral-current reaction cross sections on $^{40}$Ar depend sensitively on the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$, and that the quenching factor for the 1$^{+}$ multipole can be constrained by experimental $B(M1)$ data, but the authors note that more experimental $B(M1)$ data at lower excitation energies are required to determine more precise quenching factors.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid model combining shell-model calculations for Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions and random-phase-approximation (RPA) for forbidden transitions. For the 1$^{+}$ multipole, they employ a new effective interaction (EEdf2S) in the $sd$-$pf$ shell obtained by the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method from chiral interactions, using the KSHELL code with $sd^{-2}pf^{2}$+$sd^{-4}pf^{4}$ configurations. Forbidden transitions are treated with RPA using the SGII interaction, and the multipole expansion method of Walecka is used for cross section evaluations.

## Key numbers

- Experimental $B(M1)$ strength at $E_x$ = 9.757 MeV is 0.149$\pm$0.059 $\mu_{N}^2$ in $^{40}$Ar
- The quenching factor for the spin g-factor from $B(M1)$ data is $q_s^{IV}$ = 0.39$\pm$0.04 when $q_s^{IS}$ = $q_s^{IV}$ is assumed
- The difference between charged-current cross sections obtained with EEdf2S and SDPF-VMU-LS interactions is as small as within 5%
- For the spin-dipole strength with the SGII interaction, $S^{\lambda}(SD)$ = 14.87, 57.02 and 52.04 fm$^2$ for 0$^{-}$, 1$^{-}$ and 2$^{-}$, respectively
- The averaged energies for spin-dipole states with SGII are $\bar{E}^{\lambda}$ = 24.85, 21.76 and 15.71 MeV for 0$^{-}$, 1$^{-}$ and 2$^{-}$, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-induced reactions on $^{40}$Ar by Suzuki and Shimizu (2013) that used the SDPF-VMU-LS interaction, replacing the cross-shell part with a new interaction (EEdf2S) derived from chiral effective field theory via the EKK method. The new interaction was previously shown to successfully explain spectroscopic properties and driplines of isotopes with charge number Z = 9-12 by Togashi et al. (2017, 2020). The results are relevant for liquid argon detectors such as those planned for DUNE, and the authors note that the cross sections are sensitive to the quenching of $g_A$, which differs from the value used in many RPA calculations.
