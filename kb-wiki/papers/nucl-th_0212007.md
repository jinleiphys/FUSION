# A nuclear physicist on the lattice

> Nuclear and lattice physicists share several topics of common interest, like hadronic masses, electroweak form factors and structure functions of hadrons. The main physics issues that a new collaboration between a nuclear physicist and the lattice group of the SPQcdR collaboration is going to address, are summarized and preliminary results obtained for meson and baryon masses are presented. The importance of adopting non-perturbative approaches based on the fundamental theory of the strong interaction, like lattice formulations of QCD, is stressed.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD, particularly the Clover-improved action, can reproduce the experimental masses of light hadrons (including the proton and its negative-parity partner) in the quenched approximation, with the notable exception of the Lambda(1405) resonance, which is anomalously light in the quenched calculation and may be a NK molecule.

## Method

The SPQcdR collaboration performed quenched QCD simulations on a 24^3 x 64 lattice at beta = 6.0 using the Clover action, which removes O(a) discretization errors from the action. Hadronic masses were extracted from the plateau of the effective mass of two-point correlation functions at zero momentum, using standard interpolating fields for pseudoscalar and vector mesons and for positive- and negative-parity nucleons.

## Key numbers

* Inverse lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 1.90 +/- 0.08 GeV, corresponding to a = 0.104 +/- 0.004 fm, obtained by matching the K and K* meson masses.
* Light quark masses in the MS-bar scheme at mu = 2 GeV: m_n^{MS-bar}(2 GeV) = 4.1 +/- 0.1 MeV and m_s^{MS-bar}(2 GeV) = 96 +/- 4 MeV.
* Proton mass from lattice: 0.98 +/- 0.05 GeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.938 GeV.
* Mass ratio of negative-parity to positive-parity nucleon: M_{N-} / M_{N+} = 1.65 +/- 0.06, compared to the experimental M_{S11} / M_p = 1.63.
* Critical hopping parameter: K_c = 0.13526 (2).

## Context

This work extends the quenched lattice QCD results of the DESY and UKQCD collaborations (both cited in the text) at the same beta value, confirming their meson and baryon mass calculations. The collaboration plans to next compute electroweak form factors and structure functions of the baryon octet and decuplet, as well as the neutron electric dipole moment from CP violation. The anomalous lightness of the Lambda(1405) in the quenched calculation suggests it may be a NK molecule, a structure missed in the quenched approximation.
