# Optical model potential of deuteron with $1p$-shell nuclei

> A set of global optical potential parameters, DA1p, for deuterons with the $1p$-shell nuclei is obtained by simultaneously fitting 67 sets of experimental data of deuteron elastic scattering from \nuc{6}{Li}, \nuc{9}{Be}, \nuc{10}{B}, \nuc{11}{B}, \nuc{12}{C}, \nuc{13}{C}, \nuc{14}{N}, \nuc{16}{O} and \nuc{18}{O} with incident energies between 5.25 and 170 MeV. DA1p improves the description of the deuteron elastic scattering from the $1p$-shell nuclei with respect to the existing systematic deuteron potentials and can give satisfactory reproduction to the experimental data with radiative nuclei such as \nuc{9}{Li}, \nuc{10}{Be}, \nuc{14}{C} and \nuc{14}{O}.

## Key claim

A global optical potential (DA1p) for deuterons on 1p-shell nuclei is obtained by simultaneously fitting 67 sets of elastic scattering data, but the data for 6Li and 7Li at low energies (below 15 MeV) could not be described together with the other targets and were fitted separately, indicating that compound-nucleus contributions may be significant for those lightest 1p-shell nuclei.

## Method

The authors parameterized a Woods-Saxon optical model potential (real volume, volume and surface imaginary, no spin-orbit) with energy-dependent depths and radius parameters, then performed a simultaneous chi-squared minimization using the code MINOPT on 65 sets of deuteron elastic scattering angular distributions from 9Be, 10B, 11B, 12C, 13C, 14N, 16O, and 18O (plus two high-energy 6Li sets) over 5.25–170 MeV. Separate linear-parameter fits were performed for 6Li and 7Li at incident energies below 15 MeV. Parameter uncertainties were estimated via a bootstrap method with 1000 resamplings.

## Key numbers

* Real potential depth V_r = 98.9 ± 0.8 MeV for the 1p-shell systematics (DA1p), compared to 86 MeV (Daehnick), 91.85 MeV (An), and 82.18 MeV (Han) for heavy-target systematics.
* Real potential energy slope V_e = −0.278 ± 0.021 MeV⁻¹ for DA1p.
* Volume imaginary saturation parameters: W_v0 = 11.5 ± 0.7 MeV, W_ve0 = 18.1 ± 1.4 MeV, W_vew = 5.97 ± 1.33 MeV.
* Surface imaginary saturation parameters: W_s0 = 7.56 ± 0.67 MeV, W_se0 = 14.3 ± 1.4 MeV, W_sew = 4.55 ± 0.86 MeV.
* Real radius parameters: r_r = 1.11 ± 0.001 fm, r_r^(0) = −0.172 ± 0.004 fm, r_re = 0.00117 ± 0.00009 fm/MeV.
* Imaginary radius parameters: r_w = 0.561 ± 0.006 fm, r_w^(0) = 3.07 ± 0.002 fm, r_we = −0.00449 ± 0.00008 fm/MeV.
* For 6Li (E_d < 15 MeV): V_r = 47.9 ± 1.5 MeV, V_e = 2.37 ± 0.17 MeV⁻¹, r_r = 1.62 ± 0.01 fm.
* For 7Li (E_d < 15 MeV): V_r = 26.1 ± 0.7 MeV, V_e = 1.19 ± 0.02 MeV⁻¹, r_r = 1.45 ± 0.01 fm.
* DA1p chi-squared values for 10Be at 12 MeV: 20.61, versus 262.11 (Daehnick) and 163.01 (An).

## Context

This work extends the systematic deuteron optical potentials of Daehnick (1980), An (2006), and Han (2006) to the 1p-shell region (A_T ≤ 18), where those heavy-target systematics are known to require phenomenological renormalization (Faisal 2010, Chen 2016). The new DA1p potential enables more reliable predictions for deuteron elastic scattering from radiative 1p-shell nuclei (e.g., 9Li, 10Be, 14C, 14O) and can be used in CDCC calculations for reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei on deuterium targets, where spin-orbit couplings are often not implemented.
