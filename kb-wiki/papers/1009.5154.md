# Renormalized Linear Kinetic Theory as Derived from Quantum Field Theory - a novel diagrammatic method for computing transport coefficients -

> We propose a novel diagrammatic method for computing transport coefficients in relativistic quantum field theory. The self-consistent equation for summing the diagrams with pinch singularities has a form of a linearized kinetic equation as usual, but our formalism enables us to incorporate higher-order corrections of the coupling systematically for the first time. Furthermore, it is clarified that the higher-order corrections are nicely summarized into that of the vertex function, spectral function, and collision term. We identify the diagrams up to the next-to-next-leading order corrections in the weak coupling expansion of phi^4 theory, which is a difficult task in kinetic approaches and other diagrammatic methods.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel diagrammatic method for computing transport coefficients in relativistic quantum field theory that, for the first time, enables systematic incorporation of higher-order corrections of the coupling constant beyond leading order, though the explicit identification of diagrams is demonstrated only up to next-to-next-leading order in the weak coupling expansion of phi^4 theory.

## Method

The method extends and reformulates the Eliashberg resummation technique, originally developed for nonrelativistic Fermi liquids, to relativistic quantum field theory at high temperature using the real-time formalism in the R/A (retarded/advanced) basis. The self-consistent equation for summing diagrams with pinch singularities is derived, and the four-point function is decomposed into a part that contains pinch singularities and a part that does not, with the former summed via a self-consistent equation that takes the form of a linearized kinetic equation. The higher-order corrections are systematically summarized into corrections to the vertex function, spectral function, and collision term.

## Key numbers

* The damping rate from 2-to-2 scattering in phi^4 theory is of order lambda^2 T, where lambda is the coupling constant and T is the temperature.
* At weak coupling and high temperature (m << lambda T), the quasiparticle energy epsilon_n(k) is approximately +/- sqrt(k^2 + m_T^2) with thermal mass m_T, and the renormalization function z_n(k) is approximately 1/(2|epsilon_n(k)|).
* The transport coefficient from the one-loop diagram containing the product of retarded and advanced propagators scales as 1/lambda^2 at weak coupling, giving the correct coupling dependence but not the correct coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic resummation methods previously limited to leading order in the coupling constant or large-N expansion, as developed by Jeon (1995) and others using ladder diagrams, 2PI, and 3PI approaches. It enables systematic calculation of transport coefficients beyond the leading order, which was previously difficult even in scalar theories as attempted by next-leading-order works (2000s). The method provides a foundation for computing shear and bulk viscosities in relativistic systems such as the quark-gluon plasma, where the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is of interest following results from RHIC experiments and gauge/gravity correspondence calculations.
