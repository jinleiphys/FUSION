# Extensive Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation with Skyrme SIII force

> We have performed deformed Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations with the Skyrme SIII force for the ground states of even-even nuclei with 2 <= Z <= 114 and N ranging from outside the proton drip line to beyond the experimental frontier in the neutron-rich side. We obtained spatially localized solutions for 1029 nuclei, together with the second minima for 758 nuclei. The single-particle wavefunctions are expressed in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation, which is suitable to describe nucleon skins, halos, and exotic shapes as well as properties of ordinary stable nuclei. After explaining some of the practical procedures of the calculations, we compare the resulting nuclear masses with experimental data and the predictions of other models. We also discuss the quadrupole (m=0, 2) and hexadecapole (m=0, 2, 4) deformations, the skin thicknesses, the halo radii, and the energy difference between the oblate and the prolate solutions. Our results can be obtained via computer network.

# Extensive Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation with Skyrme SIII force

## Key claim

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations for the ground states of 1029 even-even nuclei across a wide range of the nuclear chart, but the method is not reliable near the neutron drip line because the HF+BCS treatment cannot correctly include coupling with the continuum, which produces unphysical neutron gas; the study therefore emphasizes proton-rich nuclei and restricts neutron-rich investigations to within a few neutrons of the experimental frontier.

## Method

The single-particle wavefunctions were expressed in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation with a mesh size of 1 fm, using the Skyrme SIII force for the mean-field part and a seniority pairing force with strengths determined from a continuous spectrum approximation combined with the empirical formula \(\bar{\Delta}_\tau = 12 A^{-1/2}\) MeV. The code EV8 employed imaginary-time evolution with a time step of \(1.5 \times 10^{-24}\) sec, and convergence was accelerated by applying an external quadrupole potential during several tens of evolution steps. The total energy was corrected for finite mesh-size effects using a five-parameter polynomial fit calibrated against spherical HF+BCS solutions with very fine radial grids.

## Key numbers

- Spatially localized solutions were obtained for **1029** even-even nuclei with \(2 \le Z \le 114\); second minima were found for **758** nuclei.
- The r.m.s. deviation of calculated ground-state masses from experimental values for **480** even-even nuclei is **2.2 MeV**.
- After applying the five-parameter mesh-size correction formula \(E_{\rm c}= E + c_1 A + c_2 (N-Z) + c_3 A^2 + c_4 A(N-Z) + c_5 (N-Z)^2\), the r.m.s. difference between corrected Cartesian-mesh energies and spherical-code energies was reduced to **114 keV** (from 6.7 MeV with a simple \(c_1 A\) correction).
- For the deformed nucleus \(^{240}_{94}\)Pu\(_{146}\), the relative errors of the quadrupole moment and total energy at 1 fm mesh are **0.4%** and **0.5%**, respectively.
- The pairing force strength \(G_\tau\) was capped at **0.6 MeV** for light nuclei when the continuous-spectrum method gave larger values.

## Context

This work extends the global nuclear-structure calculations previously done with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) by Möller et al. (1994) and the extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral method (ETFSI) by Aboussir et al. (1992), but uses a fully self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock+BCS approach with a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh rather than harmonic-oscillator basis expansions. The results enable systematic comparisons of masses, deformations, skin thicknesses, and halo radii across the nuclear chart, and the authors provide the full dataset via computer network for use in further studies of exotic nuclei and nuclear structure systematics.
