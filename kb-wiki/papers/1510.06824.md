# Testing the importance of collective correlations in neutrinoless $ββ$ decay

> We investigate the extent to which theories of collective motion can capture the physics that determines the nuclear matrix elements governing neutrinoless double-beta decay. To that end we calculate the matrix elements for a series of isotopes in the full $pf$ shell, omitting no spin-orbit partners. With the inclusion of isoscalar pairing, a separable collective Hamiltonian that is derived from the shell model effective interaction reproduces the full shell-model matrix elements with good accuracy. A version of the generator coordinate method that includes the isoscalar pairing amplitude as a coordinate also reproduces the shell model results well, an encouraging result for theories of collective motion, which can include more single-particle orbitals than the shell model. We briefly examine heavier nuclei relevant for experimental double-beta decay searches, in which shell-model calculations with all spin-orbit partners are not feasible; our estimates suggest that isoscalar pairing also plays a significant role in these nuclei, though one we are less able to quantify precisely.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a separable collective Hamiltonian, including isoscalar pairing, reproduces full shell-model neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements with good accuracy in the $pf$ shell, but the authors hedge that for heavier nuclei relevant to experiments, isoscalar pairing also plays a significant role though one they are "less able to quantify precisely."

## Method

The authors constructed a separable collective Hamiltonian from the KB3G shell-model effective interaction in the full $pf$ shell, including monopole, isovector pairing, isoscalar pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and spin-isospin terms. They compared the Gamow-Teller part of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements from this collective Hamiltonian with those from the full KB3G interaction for Ca, Ti, and Cr isotopic chains. They also performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations using the same collective interaction, with the isoscalar pairing amplitude and axial quadrupole deformation as generator coordinates, and compared those results to shell-model values.

## Key numbers

* The isoscalar pairing strength in the collective Hamiltonian is $g^{T=0} = -0.587$ MeV at $A=42$, scaling with $A^{-1/3}$.
* Removing the isoscalar pairing term from the Hamiltonian increases the matrix elements by more than a factor of two (closer to three in many isotopes), or between 1 and 2 units.
* The Gamow-Teller part accounts for about 85% of the total matrix element in the nuclei studied.
* Doubling the strength of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction shrinks the matrix elements by 30%–40%.
* Excluding the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction from parent nuclei (but not daughters) decreases matrix elements by 15%–20%.
* The matrix elements vary just about 10% when only the spin-isospin interaction is omitted from the collective Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Horoi (2010) and Menendez (2009) that found 10%–20% variations in matrix elements from different shell-model interactions, and by Rodriguez (2013) and Menendez (2014) that systematically calculated matrix elements in nuclei not usable in experiments. The results confirm that isoscalar pairing, previously shown to be important in QRPA studies by Vogel (1986) and Engel (1988) and in GCM work by Hinohara (2014), is crucial for accurate neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements. The paper enables future use of collective models (GCM, IBM, QRPA) in heavier nuclei where full shell-model calculations are not feasible, provided isoscalar pairing is included.
