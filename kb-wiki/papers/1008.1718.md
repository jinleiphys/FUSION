# Exact Path-Integral Representations for the $T$-Matrix in Nonrelativistic Potential Scattering

> Several path integral representations for the $T$-matrix in nonrelativistic potential scattering are given which produce the complete Born series when expanded to all orders and the eikonal approximation if the quantum fluctuations are suppressed. They are obtained with the help of "phantom" degrees of freedom which take away explicit phases that diverge for asymptotic times. Energy conservation is enforced by imposing a Faddeev-Popov-like constraint in the velocity path integral. An attempt is made to evaluate stochastically the real-time path integral for potential scattering and generalizations to relativistic scattering are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact path-integral representations for the nonrelativistic T-matrix that produce the complete Born series when expanded to all orders and reduce to the eikonal approximation when quantum fluctuations are suppressed, but the representations rely on "phantom" degrees of freedom and a Faddeev-Popov-like constraint to handle diverging phases and enforce energy conservation, with the stochastic evaluation remaining a work-in-progress that requires further development for practical Monte-Carlo schemes.

## Method

The author starts from the S-matrix defined as the matrix element of the time-evolution operator in the interaction picture at infinite scattering times, converts the Lagrangian path integral into a velocity path integral, and introduces "phantom" degrees of freedom (antivelocities) to cancel explicit phases that diverge as T approaches infinity. Energy conservation is enforced by inserting a Faddeev-Popov-like constraint into the velocity path integral, and the resulting representations are given in both a 3-dimensional antivelocity form and a reduced 1-dimensional longitudinal antivelocity form, with extensions to nonlocal potentials via the Wigner transform and to relativistic scattering in the Wick-Cutkosky model.

## Key numbers

- The eikonal approximation (Abarbanel-Itzykson form) is obtained as the leading term when quantum fluctuations are suppressed by inverse powers of K = k cos(theta/2) for the 3-dimensional antivelocity representation, or by inverse powers of k for the "ray" version.
- For the stochastic evaluation, the author uses a Gaussian potential V(r) = V0 exp(-r^2/R^2) with parameter 2m V0 R^2 = -4 at kR = 4, and shows results at a fixed scattering angle theta = 40 degrees.
- The relative deviation of the stochastic scattering amplitude from the exact partial wave result decreases as the damping parameter Gamma is reduced, but requires more Monte-Carlo calls for statistical validity.

## Context

This work extends prior path-integral treatments of scattering by providing representations that avoid the formal infinite differentiations or infinite time limits required by earlier approaches (e.g., the work cited as KML). The eikonal approximation derived here matches the systematic eikonal expansion due to Wallace, and the nonlocal potential representation generalizes an approximation first derived in Rowig. The variational approximations using the Feynman-Jensen principle have been studied in Carr and CaRo, showing agreement with partial-wave calculations even for larger scattering angles. The stochastic evaluation method builds on partial averaging techniques previously applied to bound systems (AlRo, KFD), and the relativistic extension uses the Wick-Cutkosky model to mimic meson exchange between nucleons.
