# Theoretical Investigation of α-decay in Heavy and Superheavy Isomers

> The heavy and superheavy elements of the periodic table predominately disintegrate by α-decay, facilitating transitions mainly between ground states and occasionally involving isomeric states. This study focuses on estimating the half-lives of α-transitions both from and to isomeric states, using a recently refined formula which shows excellent agreement with experimental data when isospin of parent nucleus as well as angular momentum taken away by the α particle are incorporated. These findings provide valuable insights for upcoming experimental investigations of isomeric states. Additionally, the study predicts potential α-decay in several yet-unobserved isomeric nuclei, contributing to a deeper understanding of nuclear structure in heavy and superheavy elements.

## Key claim

The New Modified Horoi Formula (NMHF), refitted exclusively on 165 isomeric α-transitions, achieves the lowest root-mean-square error (RMSE = 0.6061) among 18 empirical models tested, but this performance is conditional on the formula being re-parametrized using only isomeric decay data rather than ground-state data for which the formulas were originally developed.

## Method

The authors compiled 165 experimental isomeric α-transitions from NUBASE2020 and the NNDC database, covering nuclei with 67 ≤ Z ≤ 110. They re-fitted 18 existing empirical formulas (including NMHF, Royer2020, MYQZR, and others) using only this isomeric dataset, then compared their predictive performance using six statistical indicators (RMSE, χ², σ, uncertainty u, average deviation factor, and mean deviation). The angular momentum l carried by the α particle was calculated using spin-parity selection rules, and Qα values for isomeric transitions were derived from ground-state Qα values corrected by the excitation energies of parent and daughter isomeric states.

## Key numbers

- NMHF achieved RMSE = 0.6061 for 165 isomeric α-transitions, the lowest among 18 formulas tested
- Royer 2020 formula achieved RMSE = 0.6245 for the same dataset
- The linear fitting of NMHF to experimental data yielded R² = 0.9881
- NMHF fitting coefficients (re-evaluated on isomeric data): a = 136.1615, b = -263.6300, c = -172.9266, d = 334.7084, e = 11.1648, f = -18.5868, g = 0.0409
- The study predicts logarithmic half-lives for α-decays from isomeric states in 23 nuclei listed in NUBASE2020

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of α-decay in isomers by Denisov and Khudenko (2009), Dong et al. (2010), and Anjali et al. (2024) by systematically comparing 18 empirical formulas on a unified isomeric dataset. The NMHF formula, originally developed by Horoi (2004) for cluster decay and later modified by Saxena et al. (2021) to include isospin asymmetry, is shown to be the most accurate for isomeric transitions when refitted appropriately. The predictions for 23 unobserved isomeric α-decays and identification of promising decay chains involving isomeric levels can guide future experimental searches for new isomers in heavy and superheavy nuclei.
