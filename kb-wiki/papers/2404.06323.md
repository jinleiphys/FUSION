# From chiral EFT to perturbative QCD: a Bayesian model mixing approach to symmetric nuclear matter

> Constraining the equation of state (EOS) of strongly interacting, dense matter is the focus of intense experimental, observational, and theoretical effort. Chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) can describe the EOS between the typical densities of nuclei and those in the outer cores of neutron stars while perturbative QCD (pQCD) can be applied to properties of deconfined quark matter, both with quantified theoretical uncertainties. However, describing the full range of densities in between with a single EOS that has well-quantified uncertainties is a challenging problem. Bayesian multi-model inference from $χ$EFT and pQCD can help bridge the gap between the two theories. In this work, we introduce a correlated Bayesian model mixing framework that uses a Gaussian Process (GP) to assimilate different information into a single QCD EOS for symmetric nuclear matter. The present implementation uses a stationary GP to infer this mixed EOS solely from the EOSs of $χ$EFT and pQCD while accounting for the truncation errors of each theory. The GP is trained on the pressure as a function of number density in the low- and high-density regions where $χ$EFT and pQCD are, respectively, valid. We impose priors on the GP kernel hyperparameters to suppress unphysical correlations between these regimes. This, together with the assumption of stationarity, results in smooth $χ$EFT-to-pQCD curves for both the pressure and the speed of sound. We show that using uncorrelated mixing requires uncontrolled extrapolation of at least one of $χ$EFT or pQCD into regions where the perturbative series breaks down and leads to an acausal EOS. We also discuss extensions of this framework to non-stationary and less differentiable GP kernels, its future application to neutron-star matter, and the incorporation of additional constraints from nuclear theory, experiment, and multi-messenger astronomy.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a correlated Bayesian model mixing framework that uses a stationary Gaussian Process to combine chiral EFT and perturbative QCD equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter, producing a single QCD EOS across all densities with quantified uncertainties, but only under the condition that priors on the GP kernel hyperparameters are imposed to suppress unphysical correlations between the low- and high-density regimes.

## Method

The authors train a stationary Gaussian Process on the pressure as a function of number density, using chiral EFT predictions in the low-density region and perturbative QCD predictions in the high-density region, while accounting for each theory's truncation errors via the BUQEYE truncation error model. The GP kernel hyperparameters are constrained with priors to prevent unphysical correlations between the two density regimes, and the framework assimilates the two theories into a single mixed EOS without incorporating additional experimental or observational data. The method is applied to isospin symmetric matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The chiral EFT expansion is expressed as \( y_{k}(x) = y_{\textrm{ref}}(x) \sum_{n=0}^{k} c_{n}(x)Q^{n}(x) \), where \( Q(x) \) is the small expansion parameter and \( y_{\textrm{ref}}(x) \) is a dimensionful reference scale.
- The squared-exponential radial basis function kernel is used for the truncation-error model: \( r(x, x'; \ell) = \exp\big[-(x - x')^{2}/(2\ell^{2})\big] \), where \( \ell \) is the correlation length.
- The GP for the expansion coefficients is defined as \( c_n(x) \sim \mathcal{GP}[0, \bar{c}^{2}r(x, x';\ell)] \), with zero mean and marginal variance \( \bar{c}^{2} \).

## Context

This work extends the pointwise Bayesian model mixing procedure described in Phillips (2020) and Semposki (2022) by introducing correlations across density via a Gaussian Process, which avoids the uncontrolled extrapolation and acausal EOS that result from uncorrelated mixing. The framework is designed to be extended to neutron-star matter and to incorporate additional constraints from nuclear theory, experiment, and multi-messenger astronomy, building on prior work in Bayesian quantification of chiral EFT truncation uncertainties by Melendez (2019) and Drischler (2020).
