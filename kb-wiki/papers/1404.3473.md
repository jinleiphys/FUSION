# The lightest neutral hypernuclei with strangeness -1 and -2

> Our current knowledge of the baryon--baryon interaction suggests that the dineutron $(n,n)$ and its strange analogue $(n,Λ)$ are unstable. In contrast, the situation is more favorable for the strange three-body system $(n,n,Λ)$, and even better for the four-body system $T\equiv (n,n,Λ,Λ)$ with strangeness $-2$, which is likely to be stable under spontaneous dissociation. The recent models of the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions suggest that the stability of the $(n,n,Λ)$ and $T$ is possible within the uncertainties of our knowledge of the baryon-baryon interactions. This new nucleus $T$ could be produced and identified in central deuteron--deuteron collisions via reaction $d+d\to T+K^++K^+$, and the tetrabaryon $T$ could play an important role in catalyzing the formation of a strange core in neutron stars.

# The lightest neutral hypernuclei with strangeness -1 and -2

## Key claim

The four-body neutral system T = (n,n,Λ,Λ) with strangeness -2 is likely to be stable against spontaneous dissociation, but this conclusion depends on the uncertainties in the baryon-baryon interaction models; the three-body system (n,n,Λ) is marginally unbound and would become bound only if some masses are increased by about 10%.

## Method

The authors use variational methods with exponential basis functions and the stochastic variational method of Suzuki and Varga (1998) based on Gaussian wavefunctions to solve the three-body and four-body problems. They employ simple exponential and Morse potentials whose scattering lengths and effective ranges are tuned to reproduce the parameters of the Nijmegen-RIKEN ESC08 models and chiral effective field theory (CEFT) calculations for nn, nΛ, and ΛΛ pairs. The production cross section for T in deuteron-deuteron collisions is estimated using a Born-term model with π⁰ exchange and S₁₁(1535) resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of ³_ΛH = (n,p,Λ) is E₃ = -2.45 MeV, just below the deuteron-plus-Λ threshold at E₂ = -2.20 MeV
- For the Nijmegen-RIKEN ESC08 model, ⁴_ΛΛH with isospin I=0 is bound by about 3 MeV; for the CEFT model it is bound by about 9 MeV
- For the Nijmegen-RIKEN parameters, ⁴_ΛΛn misses binding by a very small amount; with CEFT parameters it becomes bound by about 1 MeV
- The adopted nn scattering length is -16.51 fm (ESC08) or -18.9 fm (CEFT); the nn effective range is 2.85 fm (ESC08) or 2.75 fm (CEFT)
- The ΛΛ scattering length is -0.88 fm with effective range 4.34 fm (ESC08), or -1.54 fm with effective range 0.31 fm (CEFT)
- The (nΛ) spin-singlet scattering length is -2.7 fm (ESC08) or -2.9 fm (CEFT); the spin-triplet scattering length is -1.65 fm (ESC08) or -1.51 fm (CEFT)

## Context

This work extends the study of light hypernuclei following the Nagara event (Takahashi, 2001) which measured ⁶_ΛΛHe and set limits on ΛΛ attraction. It revisits the ⁴_ΛΛH system previously studied by Filikhin and Gal (2002), who found it unbound, and Nemura, Akaishi and Myint (2002), who found a small amount of binding. The paper enables experimental searches for the neutral tetrabaryon T via the reaction d+d → T+K⁺+K⁺ in deuteron-deuteron collisions, and suggests T could catalyze strange core formation in neutron stars.
