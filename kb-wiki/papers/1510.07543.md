# Chemical Freeze-out Parameters via a Non-perturbative QCD Approach

> By analyzing the calculated baryon number susceptibility ratios ${χ_{1}^{B}}/{χ_{2}^{B}}$ and ${χ_{3}^{B}}/{χ_{1}^{B}}$ in two-flavor system via the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD, we determine the chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential in cases of both thermodynamic limit and finite size. We calculate the center-of-mass energy dependence of the ${χ_{4}^{B}}/{χ_{2}^{B}}\, (κσ^{2})$ at the freeze-out line and find an excellent agreement with experimental data in $\sqrt{S_{NN}^{}} \geq 19.6\,$GeV region when taking into account the finite size effect. Our calculations indicate that the $κσ^{2}$ exhibits a non-monotonic behavior in lower collision energy region.

# Chemical Freeze-out Parameters via a Non-perturbative QCD Approach

## Key claim
The Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, when incorporating finite size effects (with an effective scale L = 2.2 fm), reproduces experimental data for the kurtosis-times-variance product κσ² at the freeze-out line in the region √sNN ≥ 19.6 GeV, and predicts a non-monotonic behavior of κσ² at lower collision energies. The finite size effect is essential for agreement; the thermodynamic limit (infinite volume) fails to match the data at lower energies.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the quark propagator in a two-flavor (u,d) system using the rainbow approximation and an infrared constant gluon model (QC model). Baryon number susceptibilities χ₁, χ₂, χ₃, χ₄ are computed from the quark number density and its derivatives with respect to chemical potential. Finite size effects are incorporated via an infrared momentum cutoff |p|min = π/L with L = 2.2 fm, and surface effects are added through the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) approximation. Freeze-out parameters (Tf, μBf) are extracted by matching the calculated ratios χ₁/χ₂ and χ₃/χ₁ to experimental net-proton cumulant ratios C₁/C₂ and C₃/C₁ from RHIC Beam Energy Scan data.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature at zero chemical potential: Tc = 151.3 MeV (thermodynamic limit, L = ∞), 127.3 MeV (L = 2.2 fm), 129.4 MeV (L = 2.2 fm with MRE surface correction)
- Freeze-out temperature at √sNN = 200 GeV: Tf = 157.5 MeV (L = ∞), 127.0 MeV (L = 2.2 fm), 128.5 MeV (L = 2.2 fm + MRE)
- Freeze-out baryon chemical potential at √sNN = 200 GeV: μBf = 23.1 MeV (L = ∞), 17.6 MeV (L = 2.2 fm), 18.3 MeV (L = 2.2 fm + MRE)
- Freeze-out parameters at √sNN = 7.7 GeV (L = 2.2 fm): μBf = 283.5 ± 76.5 MeV, Tf = 118.0 ± 6.0 MeV
- Freeze-out parameters at √sNN = 5.5 GeV (extrapolated, L = 2.2 fm): μBf = 351.8 MeV, Tf = 109.5 MeV
- Fitted freeze-out line parameters for L = 2.2 fm: T⁰ = 128.3 MeV, a = 0.0122, b = 0.000990, c = 676.8 MeV, d = 0.168 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of QCD phase transitions (Qin 2011, Xin 2014, Liu 2015) to chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions. It provides a non-perturbative QCD alternative to the statistical hadronization model (Andronic 2006), hadron resonance gas model (Alba 2014), and lattice QCD simulations (Borsanyi 2014). The finite size treatment follows the approach of Bhattacharyya (2013, 2015) and the MRE surface correction follows Berger (1987) and Elze (1986). The results enable predictions for lower collision energies accessible at FAIR and NICA, and suggest that the critical end-point (CEP) shifts to higher baryon chemical potential and lower temperature when finite size is included, consistent with phenomenological model calculations (Palhares 2010, Bhattacharyya 2013).
