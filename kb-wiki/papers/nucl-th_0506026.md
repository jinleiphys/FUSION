# $Δ$ resonance contribution to two-photon exchange in electron-proton scattering

> We calculate the effects on the elastic electron-proton scattering cross section of the two-photon exchange contribution with an intermediate $Δ$ resonance. The $Δ$ two-photon exchange contribution is found to be smaller in magnitude than the previously evaluated nucleon contribution, with an opposite sign at backward scattering angles. The sum of the nucleon and $Δ$ two-photon exchange corrections has an angular dependence compatible with both the polarisation transfer and the Rosenbluth methods of measuring the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange contribution with an intermediate Δ resonance is smaller in magnitude than the previously evaluated nucleon contribution and has an opposite sign at backward scattering angles; the sum of both contributions yields an angular dependence compatible with both the polarisation transfer and Rosenbluth methods of measuring nucleon electromagnetic form factors, though the authors note that additional ingredients may be needed for complete reconciliation.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon exchange box and crossed-box loop diagrams with a Δ(1232) resonance in the intermediate state for elastic electron-proton scattering, using a quantum field theoretical approach with a γNΔ vertex containing magnetic, electric, and Coulomb coupling constants. The loop integrals were evaluated analytically in terms of Passarino-Veltman dilogarithm functions using the FeynCalc computer package, with a dipole form factor for ultraviolet regularisation using a cutoff ΛΔ = 0.84 GeV. The Δ contribution δΔ was expressed as a quadratic form in the coupling constants gM, gE, and gC, and added to the previously calculated nucleon two-photon exchange correction δN from Blunden (2003).

## Key numbers

- The Δ mass used is MΔ ≈ 1.232 GeV, compared to the proton mass MN ≈ 0.938 GeV and electron mass me ≈ 0.511 × 10⁻³ GeV.
- At Q² = 3 GeV² and ε = 0.1, the magnetic coefficient CM = 2.92 × 10⁻⁴, while at Q² = 6 GeV² and ε = 0.1, CM = 3.95 × 10⁻⁴.
- At Q² = 3 GeV² and ε = 0.9, the Coulomb coefficient CC = 1.98 × 10⁻⁴, while at Q² = 6 GeV² and ε = 0.9, CC = 6.38 × 10⁻⁴.
- The Δ contribution alters the cross section by an amount from -1% to +2%, and is largest at backward scattering angles.
- The fixed coupling constants used are gM = 7 and gE = 2, with gC varied in the range [-2, 0]; the E2/M1 ratio from the Dressed K-matrix Model is REM ≈ -3%, consistent with the PDG value of -(2.5 ± 0.5)%.
- The dipole form factor cutoff ΛΔ = 0.84 GeV was used, with a comparison value of ΛΔ = 0.68 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-photon exchange calculation of Blunden (2003), which included only nucleon intermediate states, by adding the Δ resonance contribution that had been addressed only approximately in earlier studies by Drell (1957) and Cammarata (1969). The calculation provides evidence that the two-photon exchange contribution with N and Δ intermediate states can resolve the discrepancy between the Rosenbluth (LT separation) and polarisation transfer methods of extracting nucleon form factors, though the authors note that heavier hadron resonances or quark degrees of freedom may become important at higher momentum transfers, and off-shell effects in the γNN and γNΔ vertices as suggested by Kondratyuk (2005) might be needed for complete reconciliation.
