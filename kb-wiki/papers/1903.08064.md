# Thermal Heavy Quark Self-Energy from Euclidean Correlators

> Brambilla, Escobedo, Soto, and Vairo have derived an effective description of quarkonium with two parameters; a momentum diffusion term and a real self-energy term. We point out that there is a similar real self-energy term for a single open heavy flavor and that it can be expressed directly in terms of Euclidean electric field correlators along a Polyakov line. This quantity can be directly studied on the lattice without the need for analytical continuation. We show that Minkowski-space calculations of this correlator correspond with the known NLO Euclidean value of the relevant electric field two-point function and that it differs from the real self-energy term for quarkonium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the real self-energy term for a single open heavy flavor, denoted $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$, differs from the analogous term for quarkonium, $\gamma_{\mathrm{adj}}$, at leading nontrivial order in perturbation theory; this difference arises from the distinct Wilson line structures reflecting the different time evolution of a single heavy quark versus a quark-antiquark pair.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the Minkowski-space definition of $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$ to a Euclidean correlator of color-electric fields along a Polyakov line, using the relation between the zero-frequency limit of the retarded current-current correlator and the zero-Matsubara-frequency Euclidean correlator. They then compute $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$ at leading order ($\mathcal{O}(g^4T^3)$) by integrating the known next-to-leading order Euclidean electric-field correlator from Burnier et al. (2010) over imaginary time, and verify this result by an independent real-time calculation in Coulomb gauge. They also obtain the next-to-leading order ($\mathcal{O}(g^5)$) contribution using Electrostatic QCD.

## Key numbers

* $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}^\mathrm{LO} = -2 \alpha_s^2 T^3 \zeta(3) C_F N_f$ at leading order ($\mathcal{O}(g^4T^3)$), where $C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)$, $N_f$ is the number of light quark flavors, and $\zeta(3) \approx 1.202$
* $\gamma_{\mathrm{adj}}^\mathrm{LO} = -2 \alpha_s^2 T^3 \zeta(3) C_F\left(\frac43 N_c + N_f\right)$ for the quarkonium case, showing the difference from $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$ at $\mathcal{O}(g^4T^3)$
* $\gamma_\mathrm{NLO} = \frac{\alpha_s C_F m_D^3}{3}$ at next-to-leading order ($\mathcal{O}(g^5)$), where $m_D^2 = g^2 T^2(N_c/3 + N_f/6)$ is the Debye mass; this contribution is identical for $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$ and $\gamma_{\mathrm{adj}}$

## Context

This work extends the effective description of quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma developed by Brambilla, Escobedo, Soto, and Vairo (2016, 2017), who derived two parameters: a momentum diffusion coefficient $\kappa$ and a real self-energy term $\gamma$. The authors show that a distinct real self-energy term exists for open heavy flavor and can be expressed as a Euclidean correlator amenable to lattice QCD determination without analytic continuation, unlike $\kappa$ which requires spectral function reconstruction. This enables nonperturbative lattice calculations of $\gamma_{\mathrm{fund}}$ using gradient flow techniques, following the perturbative renormalization work of Christensen and Laine (2016).
