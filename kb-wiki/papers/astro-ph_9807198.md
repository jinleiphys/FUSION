# Implications of Hyperon Pairing for Cooling of Neutron Stars

> The implications of hyperon pairing for the thermal evolution of neutron stars containing hyperons are investigated. The outcome of cooling simulations are compared for neutron star models composed only of nucleons and leptons, models including hyperons, and models including pairing of hyperons. We show that lambda and neutron pairing suppresses all possible fast neutrino emission processes in not too massive neutron stars. The inclusion of lambda pairing yields better agreement with X-ray observations of pulsars. Particularly, the surface temperatures deduced from X-ray observations within the hydrogen atmosphere model are more consistent with the thermal history of neutron stars containing hyperons, if the critical temperature for the onset of lambda and nucleon pairing is not too small.

# Implications of Hyperon Pairing for Cooling of Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The inclusion of lambda hyperon pairing, along with nucleon pairing, suppresses fast neutrino emission processes in neutron stars containing hyperons, yielding surface temperatures that are more consistent with X-ray observations of pulsars, but only if the critical temperature for the onset of lambda and nucleon pairing is not too small.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic simulations of neutron star thermal evolution using an implicit finite difference Newton-Raphson scheme, comparing models with only nucleons and leptons, models including hyperons without pairing, and models including hyperon pairing. They used two relativistic equations of state (TM1-m1 and RHF8) and incorporated lambda pairing gaps calculated by Balberg and Barnea (1998), treating the effective mass of the lambda as a parameter to examine sensitivity of the results.

## Key numbers

- The bond energy of two lambda hyperons in doubly-strange hypernuclei is Delta B_LambdaLambda = 4-5 MeV, compared to B_NN = 6-7 MeV for nucleons in nuclei.
- Lambda pairing exists from the threshold density for lambda formation up to the density where the lambda Fermi momentum exceeds 1.3 fm^-1, corresponding to a lambda fraction of 0.15-0.2.
- For the TM1-m1 EOS, the direct nucleon Urca process has a threshold mass of 0.78 solar masses; the process Sigma^- -> Lambda + e^- + anti-nu has a threshold mass of 1.30 solar masses; Lambda -> p + e^- + anti-nu has a threshold mass of 1.31 solar masses.
- For the RHF8 EOS, the direct nucleon Urca threshold mass is 1.09 solar masses; Sigma^- -> Lambda + e^- + anti-nu has a threshold mass of 1.35 solar masses; Lambda -> p + e^- + anti-nu has a threshold mass of 1.38 solar masses.
- The critical temperature for neutron 3P2 pairing is approximately 8 x 10^8 K in the TM1-m1 model.
- For a 1.4 solar mass star with TM1-m1, the critical temperature for lambda pairing at the outer boundary of the hyperon core is about 10^8 K for m*_Lambda/m_Lambda = 1 and about 10^7 K for m*_Lambda/m_Lambda = 0.7.

## Context

This work extends earlier cooling simulations by Haensel and Gnedin (1994) and Schaab et al. (1996), which found that unsuppressed hyperon-induced neutrino emission produced surface temperatures too low compared with soft X-ray and extreme UV observations. The paper uses the lambda pairing gap calculations of Balberg and Barnea (1998) and shows that hyperon pairing resolves this discrepancy, enabling future work on medium effects on the modified Urca process, meson condensation, accreted envelopes, and pairing of other hyperon species.
