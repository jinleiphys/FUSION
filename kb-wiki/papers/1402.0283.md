# Multi-Regulator Functional Renormalization Group for Many-Fermion Systems

> We propose a method of multi-regulator functional renormalization group (MR-FRG) which is a novel formulation of functional renormalization group with multiple infrared regulators. It is applied to a two-component fermionic system with an attractive contact interaction to study crossover phenomena between the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase and the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) phase. To control both the fermionic one-particle excitations and the bosonic collective excitations,IR regulators are introduced, one for the fermionic two-point function and another for the four-fermion vertex. It is shown that the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory, which is successful to capture qualitative features of the BCS-BEC crossover, can be derived from MR-FRG. Some aspects of MR-FRG to go beyond the NSR theory are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a multi-regulator functional renormalization group (MR-FRG) that introduces separate infrared regulators for fermionic two-point functions and four-fermion vertices, enabling simultaneous control of both fermionic one-particle excitations and bosonic collective excitations across the entire BCS-BEC crossover. The authors show that the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory can be derived from MR-FRG, but only under the specific approximation where self-energy corrections in loop integrals are neglected and only particle-particle correlations in the four-point vertex flow are retained.

## Method

The authors construct a two-parameter flow for the 1PI effective action by introducing a fermionic IR regulator \(R^{(f)}_{k_1}\) for the two-point function and a vertex IR regulator \(R^{(b)}_{k_2}\) for the four-fermion vertex, then set \(k = k_1 = k_2\) to obtain a single-parameter flow. They apply the vertex expansion up to fourth order in the symmetry-unbroken phase, derive coupled flow equations for the self-energy \(\Sigma_k\) and the four-point vertex \(\Gamma_k^{(4)}\), and solve these equations under the particle-particle random phase approximation (pp-RPA) by neglecting self-energy corrections in the loop integrals. The critical temperature is determined by the Thouless criterion \(1/\Gamma_0^{(4)}(p=0)=0\), and the number density is obtained from the self-energy via \(n = -2\int_p^{(T)} e^{-ip^0 0^+} / [G^{-1}(p) - \Sigma_0(p)]\).

## Key numbers

- The UV-divergent particle-particle loop \(\Pi(p) = \int_{l \le \Lambda}^{(T)} [G^{-1}(p/2+l)G^{-1}(p/2-l)]^{-1} = m\Lambda/(2\pi^2) + \mathcal{O}(1)\) provides the leading divergence that is renormalized into the scattering length \(a_s\) via \(m/(4\pi a_s) = g^{-1}(\Lambda) + m\Lambda/(2\pi^2)\).
- The Litim-type fermionic regulator is defined as \(R^{(f)}_{k_1}(\bm{p}) = Z_f[(k_1^2/(2m))\mathrm{sgn}(\xi(\bm{p})) - \xi(\bm{p})] \theta(k_1^2/(2m) - |\xi(\bm{p})|)\), which opens an energy gap of \(\pm k_1^2/(2m)\) near the Fermi surface.
- The Litim-type bosonic regulator is defined as \(R^{(b)}_{k_2} = Z_b(k_2^2/(4m) - \bm{p}^2/(4m)) \theta(k_2^2/(4m) - \bm{p}^2/(4m))\), introducing a gap of \(k_2^2/(4m)\) at low momentum.
- In the BCS side, the \(k\)-dependence of the four-point vertex decouples from fermionic excitations as \(\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{\mu} \cdot k^6/T^3)\) for small \(k\), with the wave-function renormalization \(Z_0 = \mathrm{p.v.} \int d^3\bm{\ell}/(2\pi)^3 \tanh(\beta\xi(\bm{\ell})/2) / [2\xi(\bm{\ell})]^2\).
- The chemical potential \(\mu\) is positive in the BCS region \((k_F a_s)^{-1} \lesssim -1\) and negative in the BEC region \((k_F a_s)^{-1} \gtrsim 1\), with the binding energy \(1/(2m a_s^2)\) gapping all fermions in the BEC side.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fermionic FRG studies that treated the BCS regime (Tanizaki, 2013) and the BEC regime (Tanizaki, 2013) separately using single regulators, by introducing a unified multi-regulator framework. The MR-FRG derivation of the NSR theory (Nozières and Schmitt-Rink, 1985) provides a systematic starting point for going beyond the pp-RPA approximation, enabling future calculations of the critical temperature \(T_c/\varepsilon_F\) as a function of \((k_F a_s)^{-1}\) with controlled corrections from self-energy effects and higher-order vertex contributions.
