# Search for the critical point of strongly interacting matter at the CERN SPS NA61/SHINE experiment

> The NA61/SHINE experiment performs a detailed study of the onset of deconfinement and search for critical point of hadronic matter by colliding nuclei of different sizes at various beam momenta from 13A to 158A GeV/c. Experimental setup and results on the theoretically expected signatures are discussed.

# Search for the critical point of strongly interacting matter at the CERN SPS NA61/SHINE experiment

## Key claim
The NA61/SHINE experiment searches for the critical point of hadronic matter and studies the onset of deconfinement by performing a two-dimensional scan of the hadronic phase diagram in the (T, μB) plane, varying both collision energy (13A to 158A GeV/c) and system size. The main goal is the analysis of fluctuations of various observables, particularly in the range of energies around 8 GeV in the center of mass (corresponding to 30 GeV beam energy in the fixed-target frame), where NA49 data indicate the onset of deconfinement in central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Method
The NA61/SHINE experiment uses a fixed-target setup with a large acceptance hadron spectrometer, six Time Projection Chambers, Time-of-Flight detectors, and a Projectile Spectator Detector. Data were collected for p+p, p+C, and 7Be+9Be collisions at beam momenta from 13A to 158A GeV/c, with the ion collisions programme initiated in 2009. The experiment measures hadron production, inelastic cross sections, pion spectra, rapidity distributions, inverse slope parameters, and transverse momentum fluctuations to search for signatures of the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Beam momenta range from 13A to 158A GeV/c for collisions of nuclei of different sizes.
- The critical point search focuses on energies around 8 GeV in the center of mass, corresponding to 30 GeV beam energy in the fixed-target frame.
- The inelastic cross section for 7Be+9Be collisions was measured and is well reproduced by the Glauber model.
- Inverse slope parameter T in 7Be+9Be collisions is larger than in p+p collisions, indicating possibility of transverse collective flow.
- No critical fluctuations were observed in either 7Be+9Be or p+p collisions.

## Context
This work extends the earlier NA49 experiment (Antoniou 2006, Abgrall 2008), which studied hadron production in Pb+Pb interactions and observed structures in the energy dependence of hadron production indicating the onset of deconfinement. The NA61/SHINE programme enables a systematic scan of the phase diagram by varying both collision energy and system size, complementing the fixed-system-size approach of NA49. The results provide reference data for neutrino beam experiments (T2K), cosmic-ray air shower experiments (Pierre Auger 2004, KASCADE 2003), and enable future searches for the critical point in heavier systems such as Pb+Pb collisions.
