# Molecular structures in charmonium spectrum: The $XYZ$ puzzle

> We study in the framework of a constituent quark model the possible contributions of molecular structures to the XYZ charmonium like states. We analyze simultaneously the $c\bar{c}$ structures and the possible molecular components in a formalism which allows us to treat channels below and above thresholds. The only molecular state found in the $1^{++}$ sector correspond to the X(3872). Molecular resonances also appear with other quantum numbers. So, the so called Y(3940) and the X(3915) are suggested to be $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ charmonium states. In the $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ sector we also found significant contributions of the molecular structures which can affect the phenomenology.

# Molecular structures in charmonium spectrum: The $XYZ$ puzzle

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $X(3872)$ is the only genuine molecular state in the $1^{++}$ sector, while the $Y(3940)$ and $X(3915)$ are better explained as $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ charmonium states rather than molecules, provided one accepts that the coupling to $c\bar{c}$ configurations is essential to bind the $X(3872)$ molecule and that the $X(3915)$ and $Y(3940)$ may actually be two distinct resonances with different widths.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model with chiral symmetry breaking, gluon exchange, and a screened confinement potential. They perform a coupled-channel calculation using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) that simultaneously includes $c\bar{c}$ eigenstates and two-meson molecular channels ($DD^*$, $\rho J/\psi$, $\omega J/\psi$, etc.), with the coupling between sectors driven by a $^3P_0$ quark-pair-creation model. The $T$-matrix poles are found by analytically continuing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to complex momenta, and resonance parameters are extracted from the zeros of the dressed propagator.

## Key numbers

- $X(3872)$ binding energy: $-0.60$ MeV or $-0.25$ MeV depending on the $^3P_0$ strength $\gamma$; the state is $86.61\%$ $D^0{D^*}^0$ and $8.00\%$ $c\bar{c}(2^3P_1)$ for the $-0.25$ MeV case.
- $X(3872)$ decay ratio $R_1 = \Gamma(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0 J/\psi) / \Gamma(\pi^+\pi^- J/\psi) = 0.44$ (for $-0.25$ MeV binding) compared to experimental $0.8 \pm 0.3$.
- $X(3872)$ radiative ratio $R_2 = \Gamma(\gamma\Psi(2S)) / \Gamma(\gamma J/\psi) = 1.20$ (for both binding energies), consistent with the Belle upper limit $< 2.1$ at $90\%$ C.L.
- $X(3940)$ resonance found at $M = 3941.8$ MeV with $\Gamma = 89.9$ MeV, with $61.09\%$ $c\bar{c}(2^3P_1)$ component and branching ratios $\mathcal{B.R.}(DD^*) = 0.89$, $\mathcal{B.R.}(\omega J/\psi) = 0.1$.
- $X(3915)$ candidate found at $M = 3896.5$ MeV with $\Gamma = 4.10$ MeV, composed of $34.22\%$ $c\bar{c}(2^3P_0)$ and $46.67\%$ $D^0\bar{D}^0$.
- $Y(3940)$ candidate found at $M = 3970$ MeV with $\Gamma = 189.3$ MeV, composed of $57.27\%$ $c\bar{c}(2^3P_0)$ and $35.32\%$ $D^0\bar{D}^0$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier coupled-channel calculation of Ortega et al. (2008) for the $1^{++}$ sector, which had shown that the $X(3872)$ arises from the coupling of a $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ $c\bar{c}$ state to $DD^*$ molecular channels. The present paper generalizes that formalism to treat resonances above threshold and applies it to multiple $J^{PC}$ sectors ($0^{++}$, $1^{++}$, $2^{++}$, $1^{--}$) in the 3.8â€“4.0 GeV region. It addresses the long-standing puzzle that five $XYZ$ candidates exist where only three $c\bar{c}$ states are predicted, and it provides a unified framework that can simultaneously produce bound molecules (the $X(3872)$) and broad charmonium-like resonances (the $X(3940)$, $X(3915)$, $Y(3940)$). The results enable future experimental discrimination between the one-resonance and two-resonance interpretations of the $X(3915)$/$Y(3940)$ complex, and they motivate further study of molecular admixtures in the $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ vector charmonium sector.
