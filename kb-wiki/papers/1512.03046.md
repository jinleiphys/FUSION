# Helicity Asymmetry Measurement for $π^0$ Photoproduction on the CLAS Frozen Spin Target

> The measurement was performed with circularly polarized photons incident on longitudinally polarized target in Hall B at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility. The helicity asymmetry E for $\vecγ\vec{p}\toπ^0p$ was determined at CM energies between 1450 MeV and 2050 MeV and compared to the SAID, MAID, and BnGa partial-wave analyses.

## Key claim

The helicity asymmetry \(E\) for \(\vec{\gamma}\vec{p}\to\pi^0p\) was measured at center-of-mass energies between 1450 MeV and 2050 MeV, and the preliminary results agree with predictions from the SAID, MAID, and BnGa partial-wave analyses at lower energies but begin to deviate at higher energies.

## Method

Circularly polarized photons from a bremsstrahlung radiator, produced by longitudinally polarized electrons of 1.465 GeV and 2.478 GeV, were incident on a longitudinally polarized frozen butanol (C4H9OH) target in Hall B at Jefferson Lab. The reaction \(\vec{\gamma}\vec{p}\to\pi^0p\) was identified via the missing mass technique, detecting the final-state proton in the CLAS detector. The asymmetry \(E\) was extracted from polarized yields for total helicities \(h=3/2\) and \(h=1/2\) using the average beam and target polarizations, and the results were compared to the SAID, MAID, and BnGa partial-wave analyses.

## Key numbers

- Electron-beam polarization: \(P_e = 0.84 \pm 0.04\).
- Target polarization: \(P_z = 0.82 \pm 0.05\).
- Photon energy range: \(E_\gamma = 466\) – 1825 MeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energy \(W = 1325\) – 2075 MeV.
- Asymmetry \(E\) was determined in 256 kinematic bins with \(\Delta W = 50\) MeV and \(\Delta(\cos\theta) = 0.1\).
- Electron beam energies used: \(E_e = 1.465\) GeV and 2.478 GeV.

## Context

This measurement is part of the CLAS FROST program, which aims to measure all beam-target double-polarization observables for single pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, enabling the "complete experiment" for unambiguous amplitude reconstruction as described by the work of A. M. Sandorfi (2007). The fine binning and broad coverage of these data impose tighter constraints on partial-wave analyses than previous measurements, such as the CBELSA asymmetry \(E\) for \(\vec{\gamma}\vec{p}\to\pi^0p\) which had much coarser energy binning (\(\Delta W = 300\) – 500 MeV). The results will help search for "missing" nucleon resonances predicted by constituent quark models (Capstick and Isgur, 1986) but not yet observed experimentally.
