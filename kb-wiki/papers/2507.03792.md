# $Λ$-deuteron momentum correlation functions incorporating deuteron breakup contributions in Faddeev formulation

> The effects of the deuteron breakup are estimated for the $Λ$-deuteron momentum correlation function. Faddeev amplitudes in calculating low-energy $Λ$-deuteron scattering can provide not only the elastic scattering part but also breakup wave functions in the incident and the rearrangement channels. Calculations are carried out using nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions parametrized in chiral effective field theory. The effects of the breakup in the incident channel are found to be marginally insignificant. Those of the rearrangement channel are not negligible, but not large when the source radius is larger than 2.5 fm. Nevertheless, it is worthwhile to have the information on the magnitude of these effects in analyzing the experimental data.

# Λ-deuteron momentum correlation functions incorporating deuteron breakup contributions in Faddeev formulation

## Key claim

The deuteron breakup in the rearrangement channel produces a non-negligible enhancement of the Λ-deuteron momentum correlation function, but this effect is small when the source radius is larger than 2.5 fm; the breakup in the incident channel is marginally insignificant for all source radii considered.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for low-energy Λ-deuteron scattering to obtain three-body wave functions that include both elastic scattering and deuteron breakup contributions in the incident and rearrangement channels. The correlation functions were evaluated using the formulation of Mrówczyński (2020), integrating the squared three-body wave functions over Gaussian source functions with radius parameter R_s. Calculations used chiral N⁴LO⁺ nucleon-nucleon interactions (Reinert et al., 2018) and chiral NLO13 and NLO19 hyperon-nucleon interactions (NLO13 and NLO19 collaborations).

## Key numbers

- Source radii of 1.2, 2.5, and 5.0 fm were used for the Gaussian source function D(r; R_s)
- The source radius for the Λ-deuteron pair is √(3/2) R_s, while for the pn pair it is √2 R_s, giving a 15% rescaling effect compared to the previous work by Kohno and Kamada (2024)
- For the J=3/2 channel, the correlation function magnitude with NLO19 is approximately twice as large as with NLO13, despite the phase shift difference appearing modest
- The breakup effect in the incident channel was found to be negligible for all R_s values studied (1.2, 2.5, 5.0 fm)
- For R_s > 2.5 fm, the rearrangement breakup effect is small but not negligible

## Context

This work extends the previous Faddeev calculations of Λ-deuteron elastic scattering phase shifts by Kohno and Kamada (2024), which showed that the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formula (Lednicky and Lyuboshits, 1982) using effective range parameters agrees well with the full Faddeev results for source radii larger than about 1 fm. The present study follows the theoretical framework for composite-particle correlation functions developed by Mrówczyński (2020). The results indicate that future experimental Λ-deuteron correlation data from heavy-ion collisions can help constrain the relative strength of the singlet and triplet ΛN interactions, which is not well determined by the hypertriton binding energy alone. A remaining challenge is incorporating ΛNN three-body forces, for which a method was developed by Kohno, Kamada, and Miyagawa (2022).
