# Effects of the magnetic field on the spallation reaction implemented by BUU coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner

> Based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner, spallation reaction of $p+^{197}Au$ at the incident beam energy of E$_{beam}$= 800 MeV/nucleon is studied. We find that the number of test particles per nucleon has minor effects on the neutron to proton ratio (n/p) of the produced heavier fragments while it affects much on their yields. The external strong magnetic field affects the production of heavier fragments much than the n/p of produced fragments. The n/p of free nucleons is greatly affected by the strong magnetic field, especially for the nucleons with lower energies.

# Effects of the magnetic field on the spallation reaction implemented by BUU coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner

## Key claim
The external strong magnetic field (10^15 Tesla) significantly affects the neutron-to-proton ratio (n/p) of free nucleons in the p+Au spallation reaction at 800 MeV/nucleon, especially for lower-energy nucleons, but has only minor effects on the n/p of heavier fragments. The number of test particles per nucleon in the BUU simulation has minor effects on the n/p of heavier fragments while strongly affecting their yields.

## Method
The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a Skyrme mean-field parametrization (compressibility K=201 MeV) coupled with a phase-space coalescence afterburner (coalescence parameters P0=263 MeV/c and R0=3 fm) to simulate the p+197Au spallation reaction at 800 MeV/nucleon. They varied the number of test particles per nucleon (5, 8, and 10) and applied an external magnetic field of 10^15 Tesla perpendicular to the reaction plane, implementing the Lorentz force on charged nucleons via Hamilton's equations.

## Key numbers
- Incident beam energy: 800 MeV/nucleon for p+197Au reaction
- Magnetic field strength: 10^15 Tesla (10^19 Gauss) applied perpendicular to the reaction plane
- Skyrme parameters: A = -0.356 GeV (attractive), B = 0.303 GeV (repulsive), sigma = 7/6, giving ground-state compressibility K = 201 MeV
- Coalescence parameters: P0 = 263 MeV/c, R0 = 3 fm
- The effect of test particle number (5 vs 10) on the n/p ratio of nuclear fragments is not more than 5%
- Maximum magnetic fields in the universe may reach 10^20 to 10^42 Gauss (cited from Sha06)
- Strong magnetic fields greater than 5x10^14 Tesla can be provided via energetic heavy-ion collisions (cited from Car88, Sk09, Cassing11, Cassing12)

## Context
This work extends the BUU+coalescence hybrid model approach previously used for fragment formation in proton-induced reactions (Yong 2009) and collective flows of light fragments (Yong 2009, VK, LC, FS). It contrasts with heavy-ion collision studies (Cassing 2011, Cassing 2012) where electromagnetic field effects on observables are practically absent due to mutual dynamical compensation of transverse components; such compensation is absent in a pure external magnetic field and negligible in proton-nucleus collisions. The results are relevant for applications in Accelerator-Driven Systems (ADS) and Spallation Neutron Sources, and for understanding nuclear processes in astrophysical environments such as white dwarfs, neutron stars, and accretion disks around black holes. The authors note that fragment de-excitation processes (Das 2005) affect n/p ratios more than the magnetic field does for fragments, but the magnetic field effects on free nucleon n/p are much larger than de-excitation effects.
