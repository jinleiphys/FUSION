# Parity Violation in Effective Field Theory and the Deuteron Anapole Moment

> Effective field theory, including pions, provides a consistent and systematic description of nucleon-nucleon strong interactions up to center-of-mass momentum of order 300 MeV per nucleon. We describe the inclusion of hadronic parity violation into this effective field theory and find an analytic form for the deuteron anapole moment at leading order in the expansion.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory (EFT) for hadronic parity violation in nucleon-nucleon interactions, including pions, and derives an analytic expression for the deuteron anapole moment at leading order in the expansion, but the result was later corrected in an erratum that added a previously omitted contribution from the nucleon isovector magnetic moment and fixed a factor-of-two error.

## Method

The authors embed parity-violating four-quark operators from the standard model into a chiral effective field theory with non-relativistic nucleon fields, pions, and photons, using the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme for renormalization. They classify the weak operators by isospin change (Delta I = 0, 1, 2) and construct the leading parity-violating four-nucleon contact interactions. As an example, they compute the deuteron anapole moment at leading order by evaluating Feynman diagrams involving weak one-pion exchange with coupling h_piNN^(1), minimal electromagnetic couplings, and nucleon magnetic moment interactions.

## Key numbers

* The strong interaction contact coefficients, renormalized at mu = m_pi in the PDS scheme, are: C_0^(1S0) = -3.34 fm^2, D_2^(1S0) = -0.42 fm^4, C_2^(1S0) = 3.24 fm^4; C_0^(3S1) = -5.51 fm^2, D_2^(3S1) = 1.32 fm^4, C_2^(3S1) = 9.91 fm^4.
* The corrected deuteron anapole moment is A_D = - (e g_A h_piNN^(1) M_N^2) / (12 pi f) [ kappa_1 (m_pi + gamma)/(m_pi + 2 gamma)^2 + (2 m_pi + 9 gamma) / (6 (m_pi + 2 gamma)^2) ], where gamma = sqrt(M_N B) is the deuteron binding momentum.
* The tree-level parity-violating matrix element for electron-deuteron scattering is A^(pv) = 10^(-7) (5.4 g_DeltaS - 0.21 (h_piNN^(1) / 10^(-7))) (1/M_N^2) epsilon^(abi) (e-bar gamma_i e) epsilon*_a epsilon_b.
* The estimated value of h_piNN^(1) from Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) is in the range 0 to 11 x 10^(-7); naive dimensional analysis gives h_piNN^(1) ~ 5 x 10^(-7).
* The strange axial matrix element of the nucleon g_DeltaS is approximately -0.15 +/- 0.15 from lepton scattering measurements.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon strong interactions developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) to include hadronic parity violation, providing a systematic alternative to the single-boson-exchange potential models of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and Adelberger and Haxton (1985). The deuteron anapole moment calculation enables constraints on the weak pion-nucleon coupling h_piNN^(1) from parity-violating electron-deuteron scattering experiments under consideration, complementing the treatment of the n p -> d gamma process in the same EFT framework by Kaplan, Savage, Springer, and Wise. The corrected result resolves an omission identified by Khriplovich and Korkin regarding the isovector magnetic moment contribution.
