# Coupled-channels analysis of the $^{\bf 16}$O+$^{\bf 208}$Pb fusion barrier distribution

> Analyses using simplified coupled-channels models have been unable to describe the shape of the previously measured fusion barrier distribution for the doubly magic $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb system. This problem was investigated by re-measuring the fission excitation function for $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb with improved accuracy and performing more exact coupled-channels calculations, avoiding the constant-coupling and first-order coupling approximations often used in simplified analyses. Couplings to the single- and 2-phonon states of $^{208}$Pb, correctly taking into account the excitation energy and the phonon character of these states, particle transfers, and the effects of varying the diffuseness of the nuclear potential, were all explored. However, in contrast to other recent analyses of precise fusion data, no satisfactory simultaneous description of the shape of the experimental barrier distribution and the fusion cross-sections for $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb was obtained.

## Key claim

Despite re-measuring the fission excitation function with improved accuracy and performing more exact coupled-channels calculations that avoid constant-coupling and first-order coupling approximations, no satisfactory simultaneous description of the shape of the experimental barrier distribution and the fusion cross-sections for the doubly magic 16O+208Pb system was obtained, in contrast to other recent analyses of precise fusion data.

## Method

The fission excitation function for 16O+208Pb was re-measured at the Australian National University using 16O beams from the 14UD Pelletron accelerator, with beam energies from 75 to 118 MeV in 0.6 MeV increments, using two multiwire proportional counters in coincidence for clean fission event identification. Coupled-channels calculations were performed using the codes CCFULL and FRESCO, including couplings to single- and 2-phonon states of 208Pb with exact treatment of excitation energies and phonon character, particle transfer channels (single-neutron pickup, single-proton stripping, and alpha-stripping), and variations in the diffuseness of the nuclear potential.

## Key numbers

- Fusion barrier distribution was obtained using an energy step of Delta_E_cm = 1.67 MeV
- Beam energies used in the range 75-118 MeV, in increments of 0.6 MeV up to 88 MeV
- Absolute beam energy defined to better than 0.05 MeV; relative beam energy to better than a few keV
- Target thickness was 40-45 microgram/cm2 of 208PbO on a backing of approximately 10 microgram/cm2 of C
- Isotopic purity of 208Pb was 99.0 +/- 0.1 percent
- Best-fit single-barrier parameters: V0=50 MeV, r0=1.159 fm, a=1.005 fm, giving average barrier B0=74.5 MeV at barrier radius R_B=11.3 fm with curvature hbar_omega0=3.07 MeV
- For CC calculations, potential depth increased to V0=200 MeV with r0=0.978 fm, giving B0=74.5 MeV at R_B=11.5 fm with curvature hbar_omega0=3.87 MeV
- Area under theoretical barrier distribution (single-phonon coupling) was 4227 mb, implying R_B=11.6 fm; area under experimental barrier distribution was 3981 mb, implying R_B=11.3 fm (6 percent difference)
- Single-neutron pickup transfer Q=-3.2 MeV, single-proton stripping Q=-8.3 MeV, alpha-stripping Q=-20 MeV
- 2-neutron pickup transfer (Q=-1.9 MeV) increased fusion cross-section by at most a factor of 1.11 at E_lab=78 MeV, compared to enhancement factor of approximately 2.5 from the three main transfer couplings at the same energy
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the 3-1 state in 208Pb: Q_3-1 = -0.34 eb

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by Morton (1995) of the 16O+208Pb fusion barrier distribution, which could not be adequately described by simplified coupled-channels models. The study contrasts with successful descriptions of the 16O+144Sm system by Morton (1994) using simplified CC models and by Hagino (1997) with more exact calculations. The failure to achieve a satisfactory description despite including couplings to single- and 2-phonon states of 208Pb, particle transfers, and anharmonic effects indicates that current coupled-channels models may be incomplete for this doubly magic system, and further experiments are required to address the uncertainty in the appropriate choice of the diffuseness parameter.
