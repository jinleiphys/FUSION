# Hyperon Polarization from the Vortical Fluid in Low Energy Nuclear Collisions

> In 2017, STAR Collaboration reported the measurements of hyperon global polarization in heavy ion collisions, suggesting the subatomic fireball fluid created in these collisions as the most vortical fluid. There remains the interesting question: at which beam energy the truly most vortical fluid will be located. In this work we perform a systematic study on the beam energy dependence of hyperon global polarization phenomenon, especially in the interesting $\hat{O}(1\sim 10)\ \rm GeV$ region. We find a non-monotonic trend, with the global polarization to first increase and then decrease when beam energy is lowered from $27~\rm GeV$ down to $3~\rm GeV$. The maximum polarization signal has been identified around $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7~\rm GeV$, where the heavy ion collisions presumably create the most vortical fluid. Detailed experimental measurements in the $\hat{O}(1\sim 10)\ \rm GeV$ beam energy region are expected to test the prediction very soon.

## Key claim

The hyperon global polarization in heavy ion collisions exhibits a non-monotonic beam energy dependence in the 3 to 27 GeV range, with the maximum polarization signal located around √sNN = 7.7 GeV, while the averaged thermal vorticity peaks at a lower energy around 3 to 4 GeV; this means the most vortical fluid may not coincide with the maximum polarization signal.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at multiple beam energies from 3 to 27 GeV, tracking parton and hadron motion throughout the evolution. From event-averaged simulations of 10^7 events per energy, they extracted four-velocity and energy density distributions on a space-time mesh, computed the thermal vorticity including temperature gradient terms via the energy density proxy, and then calculated the Λ hyperon spin polarization vector from the thermal vorticity at each hyperon's formation point using the standard thermal model formula.

## Key numbers

- Maximum global polarization signal identified around √sNN = 7.7 GeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameters b = 5, 7, and 8 fm
- Averaged thermal vorticity reaches a peak around 3 to 4 GeV collision energy
- Global polarization first increases then decreases when beam energy is lowered from 27 GeV down to 3 GeV
- Simulations performed at √sNN = 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.7, 9, 10, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, and 27 GeV
- 10^7 AMPT events simulated at each beam energy
- Kinematic cuts used: |yΛ| < 1.0 and 0.4 GeV < pT < 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the vorticity and polarization studies previously done with the AMPT model (Jiang 2016, Li 2017, Shi 2017, Xia 2018) to the low beam energy regime of 1 to 10 GeV, which is the focus of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (BES-II). The results contradict a simple extrapolation of the increasing trend seen from 200 GeV down to 7.7 GeV by STAR (2017), and differ from the 3FD model calculations (Ivanov 2020) which predict a polarization peak around 3 GeV with much larger signal magnitude. The findings enable experimental tests of whether the fluid-vorticity-polarization scenario established at higher energies holds in the 1 to 10 GeV regime, and could reveal alternative polarization mechanisms or changes in particle production mechanisms at very low energies.
