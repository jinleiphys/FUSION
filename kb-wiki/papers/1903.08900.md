# Embedding nuclear physics inside the unitary window

> The large values of the singlet and triplet scattering lengths locate the two-nucleon system close to the unitary limit, the limit in which these two values diverge. As a consequence, the system shows a continuous scale invariance which strongly constrains the values of the observables, a well-known fact already noticed a long time ago. The three-nucleon system shows a discrete scale invariance that can be observed by correlations of the triton binding energy with other observables as the doublet nucleon-deuteron scattering length or the alpha-particle binding energy. The low-energy dynamics of these systems is universal; it does not depend on the details of the particular way in which the nucleons interact. Instead, it depends on a few control parameters, the large values of the scattering lengths and the triton binding energy. Using a potential model with variable strength set to give values to the control parameters, we study the spectrum of $A=2,3,4,6$ nuclei in the region between the unitary limit and their physical values. In particular, we analyze how the binding energies emerge from the unitary limit forming the observed levels.

## Key claim
The two-nucleon system is close to the unitary limit (where scattering lengths diverge), and by tuning a Gaussian potential along the nuclear cut (fixed ratio of singlet to triplet scattering lengths \(a_0/a_1 \approx -4.3\)), the binding energies of \(A=2,3,4,6\) nuclei emerge from the unitary limit; however, the three-body excited state disappears into the particle-dimer continuum already at \(a_1 \approx 20\) fm, unlike the bosonic case where it persists.

## Method
The authors used a spin-isospin dependent Gaussian potential with equal ranges \(r_0 = 1.65\) fm for the \(S=0,T=1\) and \(S=1,T=0\) channels, tuning the strengths \(V_{01}\) and \(V_{10}\) to vary the scattering lengths along the nuclear cut \(a_0/a_1 = -4.3066\). They solved the Schrödinger equation for \(A=2,3,4,6\) nucleon systems using the Hyperspherical Harmonic method (for \(A=3,4\)) and the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussians (for \(A=6\) and excited states), calculating binding energies from the unitary limit to the physical point. At the physical point, a hyper-central three-body Gaussian potential was added to reproduce the triton binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Singlet scattering length at physical point: \(a_0 \approx -23.7\) fm; triplet scattering length: \(a_1 \approx 5.4\) fm; typical interaction length \(\ell \approx 1.4\) fm.
- Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d = 2.22456\) MeV; typical nuclear energy \(\hbar^2/m\ell^2 \approx 20\) MeV.
- At the unitary limit, the three-body binding energy predicted for real nuclear systems is \(E_3^0|_{\text{exp}} = 2.536\) MeV; the four-body binding energy at unitary is \(E_4^0|_{\text{exp}} = 13.474\) MeV.
- Ratio \(E_4/E_3\) varies from 5.89 at the unitary point to 3.89 at the physical point (close to the realistic value 3.67).
- At the physical point, the \(^6\)He and \(^6\)Li binding energies (without Coulomb) are \(-41.60\) MeV and \(-46.74\) MeV respectively; the energy difference between them is 5.14 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of universal Efimov physics from bosonic systems (e.g., helium droplets studied by Gattobigio, Kievsky, and Viviani in 2011-2014) to fermionic nucleons with spin-isospin degrees of freedom. It builds on earlier work by Kievsky et al. (2016, 2018) mapping the Efimov plot for three nucleons along the nuclear cut, and on studies by Epelbaum et al. (2006) and Beane et al. (2002) showing that both scattering lengths diverge at \(m_\pi \approx 200\) MeV. The results enable predictions of binding energies at the unitary limit for real nuclear systems and demonstrate that the \(A=6\) states emerge from thresholds only at finite scattering lengths, revealing a mass gap for \(A=5,6\) at unitarity.
