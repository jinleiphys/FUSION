# Background field method and nonrelativistic QED matching

> We discuss the resolution of an inconsistency between lattice background field methods and nonrelativistic QED matching conditions. In particular, we show that lack of on-shell conditions in lattice QCD with time-dependent background fields generally requires that certain operators related by equations of motion should be retained in an effective field theory to correctly describe the behavior of Green's functions. The coefficients of such operators in a nonrelativistic hadronic theory are determined by performing a robust nonrelativistic expansion of QED for relativistic scalar and spin-half hadrons including nonminimal electromagnetic couplings. Provided that nonrelativistic QED is augmented with equation-of-motion operators, we find that the background field method can be reconciled with the nonrelativistic QED matching conditions without any inconsistency. We further investigate whether nonrelativistic QED can be employed in the analysis of lattice QCD correlation function in background fields, but we are confronted with difficulties. Instead, we argue that the most desirable approach is a hybrid one which relies on a relativistic hadronic theory with operators chosen based on their relevance in the nonrelativistic limit. Using this hybrid framework, we obtain practically useful forms of correlation functions for scalar and spin-half hadrons in uniform electric and magnetic fields.

# Background field method and nonrelativistic QED matching

## Key claim
The inconsistency between lattice background field methods and standard NRQED matching conditions is resolved only when equation-of-motion (EOM) operators, which are normally removed for on-shell S-matrix matching, are retained in the effective field theory to correctly describe Green's functions in time-dependent external fields.

## Method
The authors perform a brute-force nonrelativistic expansion of relativistic QED for scalar and spin-half hadrons, including nonminimal electromagnetic couplings, to determine the coefficients of EOM operators in augmented NRQED up to O(M^{-3}). They then construct a hybrid framework using a relativistic hadronic theory with operators selected by NRQED power counting, and compute Euclidean correlation functions for hadrons in uniform electric and magnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- Energy shift for a charged spin-half hadron in a uniform electric field: ΔE(E) = -1/2 [4π α_E - (Z+κ)^2/(4M^3)] E^2, where M is hadron mass, κ is anomalous magnetic moment, α_E is electric polarizability, and Z is charge.
- For a charged scalar hadron in a uniform electric field: ΔE(E) = -1/2 [4π α_E + Z^2/(2M^3)] E^2.
- For a charged scalar in a uniform magnetic field, the nth Landau level energy: E_n = sqrt(M^2 + |ZB|(2n+1) - 4π β_M M B^2), where β_M is magnetic polarizability.
- For a charged spin-half hadron in a uniform magnetic field, the four spin/parity-projected energies: E_(n,±1,±2) = sqrt(M_B^2 ∓_1 ZB + |ZB|(2n+1)) ∓_1 (±_2 κB/(2M)), with M_B = M - 1/2 (4π β_M) B^2.
- The EOM operator coefficient for spin-half hadrons: c_{X_0} = κ - (2/3) M^2 ⟨r_E^2⟩, where ⟨r_E^2⟩ is the charge radius.
- The EOM operator coefficient for spin-half hadrons: c_{X'_0} = -(2/3) M^2 ⟨r_M^2⟩, where ⟨r_M^2⟩ is the magnetic radius.
- For a scalar hadron: c_{X_0} = -(2/3) M^2 ⟨r_E^2⟩ - Z.

## Context
This work resolves an inconsistency identified between lattice background field methods (Martinelli 1982) and standard NRQED matching (Hill 2013, Heinonen 2012) where the charge radius appeared in the energy shift for uniform electric fields. The authors extend prior work on field redefinitions (Arzt 1995) and Foldy-Wouthuysen transformations (Foldy 1950) to show that EOM operators must be retained for off-shell Green's functions. The hybrid framework enables direct extraction of magnetic moments and electromagnetic polarizabilities from lattice QCD simulations with uniform fields, while extension to spin polarizabilities in nonuniform fields remains an open challenge requiring further understanding of EOM operator contributions.
