# From Regular to Chaotic States in Atomic Nuclei

> An interesting aspect of nuclear dynamics is the co--existence, in atomic nuclei, of regular and chaotic states. In the first part of the present work, we review the state of the art of nuclear dynamics and use a schematic shell model to show how a very simple and schematic nucleon--nucleon interaction can produce an order$\to$chaos transition. The second part is devoted to a discussion of the wave function behaviour and decay of chaotic states using some simple models (to be published in Rivista Nuovo Cimento).

## Key claim

The paper argues that atomic nuclei exhibit a transition from regular (Poisson-like) to chaotic (GOE-like) spectral statistics as excitation energy increases, using a schematic shell model with SU(3) symmetry to demonstrate that a simple nucleon-nucleon interaction can produce this order-to-chaos transition. The authors hedge that the second part of the work, discussing wave function behaviour and decay of chaotic states, is to be published separately.

## Method

The authors review the shell model and collective model for regular low-energy states, then introduce random matrix theory (Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble) to describe chaotic high-energy states. They compare GOE predictions with experimental neutron and proton resonance data from the Nuclear Data Ensemble (NDE). They then present a schematic shell model with SU(3) symmetry, an extension of the two-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model proposed by Meredith, Koonin and Zirnbauer (MKZ), which has two degrees of freedom and can exhibit an order-to-chaos transition in its classical limit.

## Key numbers

- Low energy regular states are found in the range 0 to 4 MeV above the ground state
- Neutron emission threshold occurs at approximately 8 MeV excitation energy
- Neutron resonance spectroscopy on heavy even-even nuclei typically identifies 150 to 170 s-wave resonances with J^π = 1/2^+ located 8-10 MeV above ground state, with average spacings around 10 eV and average total widths around 1 eV
- Proton resonance spectroscopy yields sequences of 60 to 80 members with fixed spin and parity
- The Nuclear Data Ensemble (NDE) contains 1726 level spacings from experimental data
- The Saxon-Woods potential parameters are a ≈ 0.53 × 10^(-15) m and U_0 ≈ 50-60 MeV
- For the GOE, the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s) = (π/2)s exp(-π s^2/4) and the spectral rigidity Δ_3(L) = (1/π^2) log L for L ≫ 1

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear physics, building on the random matrix theory initiated by Wigner and developed by Mehta and others. The authors reference the experimental work of Haq, Pandey and Bohigas (1982) who compiled the NDE, and the studies by Garrett, German, Courtney and Espino (1991) and Shriner, Mitchell and Von Egidy (1991) showing that near the ground state nuclei follow Poisson or intermediate statistics rather than GOE. The paper connects to the broader field of quantum chaos in billiards studied by Bohigas, Giannoni and Schmit (1984) and the semiclassical theory of Gutzwiller (1971) and Berry (1985). The schematic SU(3) model extends the work of Lipkin, Meshkov and Glick (1965) and Meredith, Koonin and Zirnbauer (1988).
