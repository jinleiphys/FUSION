# Intermittent, Domain-Structured Coherence in the Pion Interferometry of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We investigate a domain-structured source in the pion interferometry of relativistic nuclear collisions. The source emits coherent pions intermittently with the background of chaotic pions. The coherent pions examined are either of a general nature or of disoriented chiral condensate. Two- and three-pion correlations for the source are shown to agree well with the recent NA44 experimental data.

## Key claim

A domain-structured source that emits coherent pions intermittently from a few regions (obeying a Poisson distribution) against a chaotic background can simultaneously reproduce the NA44 two-pion chaoticity λ ≈ 0.4–0.5 and three-pion weight factor ω ≈ 0.20 ± 0.19, which a simple partially coherent source cannot. The authors do not claim this proves disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) appears; they note that neutral-pion interferometry would be required to confirm DCC.

## Method

The authors model the pion source as a sum of N coherent domains (each with random phase and spatial location) plus a chaotic background current, with the number of coherent domains per event following a Poisson distribution with mean α. Using a generating functional method, they compute the normalized two- and three-pion correlation functions and extract the chaoticity λ and weight factor ω. They then apply the same formalism to a DCC-motivated source where each coherent domain carries an isospin unit vector, and compute λ and ω separately for charged and neutral pions.

## Key numbers

- NA44 experimental values: λ = 0.4–0.5 and ω = 0.20 ± 0.02 ± 0.19 (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- Best fit for the general domain-structured model: α = 0.13 (mean number of coherent domains) and ε = 0.60 (ratio of chaotic pions to total pions), yielding λ = 0.45 and ω = 0.20.
- Best fit for the DCC domain model: α = 0.18 and ε = 0.57, yielding λ₊ = 0.45 and ω₊ = 0.20 for positive pions.
- Predicted neutral-pion values for the DCC model at best fit: λ₀ = 0.35 and ω₀ = –0.12.
- Probability distribution of neutral-pion fraction f: P(f) ≈ 0.83 δ(f – 1/3) + 0.15 P₁(f), where P₁(f) is approximately 1/√f.

## Context

This work extends the standard partially coherent source model (GKW, 1980s) which could not simultaneously fit the NA44 λ and ω values. The authors introduce a Poisson-distributed number of coherent domains, a more realistic structure than a single coherent component. The DCC variant connects to theoretical proposals by Bjorken (1990s), Rajagopal (1990s), and Gavin (1990s). The results enable future neutral-pion interferometry as a definitive test for DCC formation, and the authors note that event-by-event fluctuations and particle contamination (CK, 1990s) must be carefully controlled.
