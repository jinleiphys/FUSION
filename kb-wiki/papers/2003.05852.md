# A collision geometry-based 3D initial condition for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a simple way to construct 3D initial conditions for relativistic heavy-ion collisions based on the Glauber collision geometry. Local energy and momentum conservation conditions are imposed to set non-trivial constraints on our parameterizations of longitudinal profiles for the system's initial energy density and flow velocity. After calibrating parameters with charged hadron rapidity distributions in central Au+Au collisions, we test model predictions for particle rapidity distributions in d+Au and peripheral Au+Au collisions in the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). Simulations and comparisons with measurements are also made for Pb+Pb collisions at Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) energies. We demonstrate that elliptic flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s} \sim 10$ GeV can set strong constraints on the dependence of Quark-Gluon Plasma shear viscosity on temperature and net baryon chemical potential.

# A collision geometry-based 3D initial condition for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors present a simple 3D initial condition model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions that imposes local energy and momentum conservation to constrain longitudinal profiles, claiming that elliptic flow measurements at center-of-mass energies around 10 GeV can set strong constraints on the temperature and net baryon chemical potential dependence of Quark-Gluon Plasma shear viscosity. However, the model assumes Bjorken flow for the initial flow velocity profile and ignores pre-equilibrium dynamics during the time when the two colliding nuclei pass through each other, meaning the results serve as a baseline for future comparisons with more realistic dynamical initialization frameworks.

## Method

The authors construct 3D initial conditions by extending geometry-based 2D Glauber models with parametric longitudinal profiles for energy density and net baryon density, imposing local energy and momentum conservation to eliminate two degrees of freedom in the model parameter space. They use a plateau-like longitudinal profile for energy density centered at the local center-of-mass rapidity, with the normalization determined by the local invariant mass from the nuclear thickness functions. The initial conditions are evolved using the 3D viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC with a lattice QCD-based equation of state at finite net baryon density (NEoS-BQS), transitioning to the UrQMD hadronic transport model on a constant energy density hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity parameters calibrated from elliptic flow measurements: $(\eta/s)_0 = 0.1$, $T_c = 0.165$ GeV, $T_\mathrm{low} = 0.1$ GeV, $T_\mathrm{slope} = 1.2$, $\mu_{B,\mathrm{slope}} = 0.9$, $\mu_{B, \mathrm{scale}} = 0.6$ GeV, and $\alpha = 0.8$
- Hydrodynamic to hadronic transport switching energy density: $e_\mathrm{sw} = 0.26$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Initial proper time $\tau_0$ values range from 1.0 fm/$c$ at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV to 3.6 fm/$c$ at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Energy density plateau width $\eta_0$ ranges from 2.5 at 200 GeV to 0.9 at 7.7 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- The local invariant mass at a transverse point scales as $M(x, y) \propto \sqrt{T_A(x, y) T_B(x, y)}$ for $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} > 5$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the 2D Glauber-based initial condition approach to full 3D by incorporating longitudinal profiles constrained by conservation laws, building on the plateau-like parameterization of Hirano (2005) and the net baryon density profile of Denicol (2018). The model provides a simpler alternative to dynamical initialization approaches based on classical string deceleration (Shen 2017) and transport approaches (Karpenko 2015, Du 2018), while enabling systematic studies of rapidity-dependent observables in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and SPS energies. The results can serve as a baseline for future comparisons with event-by-event simulations and more realistic dynamical initialization frameworks, and the finding that transverse energy density scales with $\sqrt{T_A T_B}$ provides a physics explanation for the scaling preferred in Bayesian extractions by Bernhard (2016, 2019).
