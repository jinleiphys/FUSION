# Phase Diagram in Quantum Chromodynamics

> It is suggested that the hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma is a first-order phase transition described by a critical curve in the temperature-(quark) density plane which terminates in a critical point. Such a critical curve is derived from the van der Waals equation and its parameters are estimated by using the theoretical approach given in M. Apostol, Roum. Reps. Phys. 59 249 (2007); Mod. Phys. Lett. B21 893 (2007). The main assumption is that quark-gluon plasma created by high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions is a gas of ultrarelativistic quarks in equilibrium with gluons (vanishing chemical potential, indefinite number of quarks). This plasma expands, gets cool and dilute and hadronizes at a certain transition temperature and transition density. The transition density is very close to the saturation density of the nuclear matter and, it is suggested that both these points are very close to the critical point n~1fm^{-3} (quark density) and T~200MeV (temperature).

# Phase Diagram in Quantum Chromodynamics

## Key claim
The paper suggests that hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma is a first-order phase transition described by a van der Waals-type critical curve in the temperature-density plane, and that the critical point lies at approximately n ≈ 1 fm⁻³ and T ≈ 200 MeV. The key hedge is that this critical point is derived under the assumption that the quark-gluon plasma is an ultrarelativistic gas of quarks with vanishing chemical potential and indefinite quark number, in equilibrium with gluons.

## Method
The author derives a critical curve T = -Bn² + An from the van der Waals equation of state under the assumption of zero pressure at the transition. Parameters A and B are estimated using a theoretical approach from Apostol (2007) that models the expanding quark-gluon plasma created in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the plasma energy and quark number scale with temperature as E ∝ VT⁴ and N ∝ VT³. The transition temperature and density are then matched to the critical curve to determine A and B.

## Key numbers
- Critical point: quark density n_c ≈ 1 fm⁻³ and temperature T_c ≈ 200 MeV
- Transition density n_t ≈ 1 fm⁻³, derived from m₀ = 4 MeV (mean mass of u and d quarks) and fraction f ≈ 2 × 10⁻²
- Transition temperature T_t ≈ 200 MeV, given by T_t = f⁻¹ T_m where T_m ≈ m₀c²
- Initial plasma temperature T₀ = 1 GeV for collision energy E/N_n = 1 TeV per nucleon
- Initial quark density n₀ ≈ 125 fm⁻³ (for N_n ≈ 100 nucleons, nucleon radius a = 2 fm)
- Transition radius R_t ≈ 8 R₀, occurring after t ≈ 5 × 10⁻²³ s from collision
- Hadronized quarks at transition: fN_t ≈ 100 N_n
- Density where critical curve crosses n-axis: A/B = 2 fm⁻³
- Parameters A = 400, B = 200 (with T in MeV, n in fm⁻³)

## Context
This work extends the van der Waals approach to the QCD phase diagram previously developed by Apostol (2007) in two papers (Roum. Reps. Phys. 59 249 and Mod. Phys. Lett. B21 893). The paper builds on lattice-gauge theory calculations and the hydrodynamical model of particle production, aiming to provide a more quantitative description of the hadronization transition. The derived critical point near nuclear saturation density enables future experimental searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collision experiments such as those at MPD-NICA JINR Dubna.
