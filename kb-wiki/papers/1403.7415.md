# Hadron-Quark Phase Transition in Quark-Hybrid Stars

> The recent discovery of the two-solar mass neutron stars $J1614-2230~(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot})$ and $J0348+0432~(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot})$ allows to consider the possible existence of deconfined quarks in the cores of neutron stars. Based on a non-local extension of the $SU(3)$ Nambu Jona-Lasinio model with vector interactions to describe the quark matter phase, and a non-linear Walecka model to represent the hadronic phase, a phase transition between these two phases can be constructed via the Gibbs conditions and imposing global electric charge neutrality condition. Depending on the strength of quark vector repulsion, we find that an extended region made of a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons may exist in high-mass neutron stars with masses up to $2.0-2.4 M_{\odot}$. The radii of these objects are between $12$ and $13$ km, as expected for neutron stars.

## Key claim
Depending on the strength of quark vector repulsion, an extended mixed phase of quarks and hadrons may exist in high-mass neutron stars with masses up to 2.0–2.4 solar masses, but only stars on the left of the mass peak are dense enough to contain quark matter, and those stars are unstable against radial oscillations.

## Method
The authors constructed a phase transition between hadronic matter (described by a non-linear Walecka model with NL3 parametrization) and quark matter (described by a non-local extension of the SU(3) Nambu Jona-Lasinio model with vector interactions) using the Gibbs conditions with global electric charge neutrality and baryon number conservation. The quark chemical potential was shifted by vector interactions in a momentum-dependent way via a Gaussian form factor. The mixed phase was characterized by the volume proportion of quark matter, χ, which varies between 0 and 1.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star masses range from 2.04 to 2.35 solar masses depending on the strength of the vector interaction (G_V/G_S = 0.00, 0.05, 0.09).
- For G_V/G_S = 0.00, the maximum mass is 2.04 M_⊙ with a quark fraction χ = 0.72 at maximum mass, and the mixed phase spans 2.71–6.87 ρ_0 (nuclear saturation density).
- For G_V/G_S = 0.05, the maximum mass is 2.21 M_⊙ with χ = 0.85, and the mixed phase spans 3.10–6.77 ρ_0.
- For G_V/G_S = 0.09, the maximum mass is 2.35 M_⊙ with χ = 0.92, and the mixed phase spans 3.25–6.53 ρ_0.
- The radii of these objects are between 12 and 13 km.
- The observed neutron stars J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙) and J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙) are cited as motivation.

## Context
This work extends the study of quark-hybrid stars by using a non-local NJL model, building on earlier work by Orsaria (2013, 2014) and the mixed-phase formalism of Glendenning (2000). It addresses the open question of whether a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons can exist in neutron stars, which depends on the surface tension between nuclear and quark matter (estimated at 5–30 MeV/fm³ by Alford (2001), Voskresensky (2002), Tatsumi (2002), and Endo (2011)). The results enable further studies of transport properties and the electric and thermal properties of neutron stars with quark cores.
