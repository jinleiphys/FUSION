# Cold uniform matter and neutron stars in the quark-mesons-coupling model

> A new density dependent effective baryon-baryon interaction has been recently derived from the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model, offering impressive results in application to finite nuclei and dense baryon matter. This self-consistent, relativistic quark-level approach is used to construct the Equation of State (EoS) and to calculate key properties of high density matter and cold, slowly rotating neutron stars. The results include predictions for the maximum mass of neutron star models, together with the corresponding radius and central density, as well the properties of neutron stars with mass of order 1.4 $M_\odot$. The cooling mechanism allowed by the QMC EoS is explored and the parameters relevant to slow rotation, namely the moment of inertia and the period of rotation investigated. The results of the calculation, which are found to be in good agreement with available observational data, are compared with the predictions of more traditional EoS. The QMC EoS provides cold neutron star models with maximum mass 1.9--2.1 M$_\odot$, with central density less than 6 times nuclear saturation density ($n_{0}= 0.16 {\rm fm}^{-3}$) and offers a consistent description of the stellar mass up to this density limit. In contrast with other models, QMC predicts no hyperon contribution at densities lower than $3n_0$, for matter in $β$-equilibrium. At higher densities, $Ξ^{-,0}$ and $Λ$ hyperons are present.

# Cold Uniform Matter and Neutron Stars in the Quark-Meson-Coupling Model

## Key claim

The quark-mesons-coupling (QMC) model, when applied to cold neutron stars, predicts maximum masses in the range 1.9–2.1 M$_\odot$ with central densities below 6 times nuclear saturation density, but only under the assumption that the critical density at which the QMC approximation breaks down is large enough to permit modeling of neutron star interiors. In contrast to other models, QMC predicts no hyperon contribution at densities lower than 3$n_0$ for matter in $\beta$-equilibrium, with $\Xi^{-,0}$ and $\Lambda$ hyperons appearing only at higher densities.

## Method

The authors constructed the Equation of State (EoS) for cold, uniform baryonic matter in $\beta$-equilibrium using the QMC model, which treats baryons as quark bags coupled to $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ meson mean fields, with the scalar polarisability of the nucleon generating many-body forces. The energy density was computed in the Hartree-Fock approximation including the full baryon octet (N, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) plus electrons and muons, with the $\sigma$ field solved self-consistently and the pion exchange contribution included via a Fock term. The EoS was then matched to the Baym-Bethe-Pethick and Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EoS for the crust region and used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for cold, slowly rotating neutron star models.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass predicted: 1.9–2.1 M$_\odot$ across the QMC model variants
- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Central density of maximum-mass stars: less than 6$n_0$
- Incompressibility modulus $K_\infty$ ranges from 256 MeV (QMC$\pi$4) to 344 MeV (QMC600)
- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: $\mathcal{E} = -15.865$ MeV (for QMC600, QMC700, QMC$\pi$1)
- Asymmetry energy at saturation: $a_s = 30$ MeV
- Free nucleon radius used: $R_N^{\text{free}} = 0.8$ fm
- $\sigma$ meson mass values tested: 600 MeV and 700 MeV
- Hyperon threshold: no hyperons below 3$n_0$; $\Xi^{-,0}$ and $\Lambda$ appear at higher densities

## Context

This work extends the QMC model, previously applied successfully to finite nuclei by Guichon et al. (2006), to the study of dense baryonic matter and neutron stars. The results are compared with traditional EoS based on the A18+$\delta$v+UIX$^*$ and modified Reid soft core potentials, the Skyrme SkM$^*$ interaction, and relativistic mean field (RMF) models for hybrid stars including quark matter. The QMC EoS provides a consistent description of stellar mass up to 6$n_0$ and enables future investigation of hyperon production rates in non-equilibrium stellar matter relevant for core-collapse supernova physics, as well as predictions for neutron star moments of inertia and rotation periods.
