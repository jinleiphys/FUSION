# Mixing of pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon in the $J^P=1/2^-$ sector and the $N^*$(1535) and $N^*$(1650) resonances

> We study the meson-baryon interaction with $J^P=1/2^-$ using the hidden-gauge Lagrangians and mixing pseudoscalar meson-baryon with the vector meson-baryon states in a coupled channels scheme with $πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$, $KΣ$, $ρN$ and $πΔ$ (d-wave). We fit the subtraction constants of each channel to the $S_{11}$ partial wave amplitude of the $πN$ scattering data extracted from experimental data. We find two poles that we associate to the $N^*(1535)$ and the $N^*(1650)$ resonances and show that the subtraction constants are all negative and of natural size. We calculate the branching ratios for the different channels of each resonance and we find a good agreement with the experimental data. The cross section for the $π^- p \to ηn$ scattering is also evaluated and compared with experiment.

## Key claim

By explicitly including vector-baryon ($\rho N$) and $\pi\Delta$ (d-wave) channels alongside the usual pseudoscalar-baryon octet channels, the authors generate both the $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ resonances dynamically, and obtain subtraction constants that are all negative and of natural size, removing the previous pathology where different channels required different cutoffs or positive subtraction constants.

## Method

The authors use hidden-gauge Lagrangians to compute meson-baryon interactions in a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter approach including $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, $\rho N$, and $\pi\Delta$ (d-wave) states with $J^P=1/2^-$. The subtraction constants for each channel, plus two parameters $\gamma_0$ and $\gamma_1$ for the $\pi\Delta$ transitions, are fitted to the $S_{11}$ partial wave amplitude of $\pi N$ scattering data from Arndt (1995). The resulting T-matrix is analytically continued to find poles in the complex plane, from which couplings and branching ratios are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Pole position for $N^*(1535)$: Re = 1508.1 MeV, 2Im = 90.3 MeV (theory); PDG range: Re = 1490–1530 MeV, 2Im = 90–250 MeV.
- Pole position for $N^*(1650)$: Re = 1672.3 MeV, 2Im = 158.2 MeV (theory); PDG range: Re = 1640–1670 MeV, 2Im = 100–170 MeV.
- Branching ratio for $N^*(1535)$ to $N\pi$: 58.6% (theory); PDG: 35–55%.
- Branching ratio for $N^*(1535)$ to $N\eta$: 37.0% (theory); PDG: 42 ± 10%.
- Branching ratio for $N^*(1650)$ to $N\pi$: 58.9% (theory); PDG: 50–90%.
- Branching ratio for $N^*(1650)$ to $N\eta$: 27.6% (theory); PDG: 5–15%.
- Subtraction constants at $\mu = 630$ MeV: $a_{N\pi} = -2.001$, $a_{N\eta} = -3.006$, $a_{\Lambda K} = -3.128$, $a_{\Sigma K} = -1.799$, $a_{N\rho} = -1.291$, $a_{\Delta\pi} = -2.720$.
- Coupling $g_i$ of $\rho N$ to $N^*(1535)$: $2.96 + i\,0.11$ (largest coupling magnitude $|g_i| = 2.96$).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Garzon and Oset (2013) from the $J^P=3/2^-$ sector to the $1/2^-$ sector, explicitly including vector-baryon and $\pi\Delta$ channels that were absent in earlier chiral unitary studies such as Kaiser (1995) and Inoue (2001). Prior work by Inoue (2001) generated only the $N^*(1535)$ and required different subtraction constants for different channels, which Hyodo (2008) interpreted as evidence for a non-negligible genuine-state component. The present results show that including $\rho N$ and $\pi\Delta$ channels fills those missing components, yielding natural subtraction constants and dynamically generating both the $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$. The improved description of the $\pi^- p \to \eta n$ cross section above 1550 MeV, where Inoue (2001) failed, demonstrates the importance of the $N^*(1650)$ generated here.
