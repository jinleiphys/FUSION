# The Tamm-Dancoff Approximation as the boson limit of the Richardson-Gaudin equations for pairing

> A connection is made between the exact eigen states of the BCS Hamiltonian and the predictions made by the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation. This connection is made by means of a parametrised algebra, which gives the exact quasi-spin algebra in one limit of the parameter and the Heisenberg-Weyl algebra in the other. Using this algebra to construct the Bethe Ansatz solution of the BCS Hamiltonian, we obtain parametrised Richardson-Gaudin equations, leading to the secular equation of the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation in the bosonic limit. An example is discussed in depth.

# The Tamm-Dancoff Approximation as the boson limit of the Richardson-Gaudin equations for pairing

## Key claim
The exact eigenstates of the reduced BCS Hamiltonian can be continuously connected to the multiphonon states of the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) by introducing a real-valued parameter ξ that interpolates between the quasi-spin su(2) algebra (ξ=1, exact fermion pairing) and the Heisenberg-Weyl hw(1) algebra (ξ=0, bosonic TDA limit), provided the parametrised Richardson-Gaudin equations are solved along the trajectory.

## Method
The author introduces a parametrised algebra that depends on a continuous parameter ξ, which yields the exact quasi-spin su(2) algebra for ξ=1 and the Heisenberg-Weyl hw(1) boson algebra for ξ=0. Using this algebra to construct the Bethe Ansatz wavefunction for the reduced BCS Hamiltonian, parametrised Richardson-Gaudin equations are derived that reduce to the standard RG equations at ξ=1 and to N/2 copies of the TDA secular equation at ξ=0. By adiabatically varying ξ from 0 to 1 and solving the parametrised RG equations numerically, each TDA multiphonon state is traced to its corresponding exact eigenstate of the BCS Hamiltonian, or shown to have no physical counterpart.

## Key numbers
- The example system has 3 single-particle levels with degeneracies Ω₁=6 (j=5/2, ε=0.0000), Ω₂=4 (j=3/2, ε=0.7796), and Ω₃=10 (j=9/2, ε=2.1024), accommodating 4 pairs (N/2=4).
- For |g|→0, the exact ground-state energy is approximately 6ε₁+2ε₂=1.5592, whereas TDA predicts 8ε₁=0.0000 for the [4,0,0] state.
- The TDA state [4,0,0] does not correspond to any exact solution for |g| < 0.190; above this value it connects to the exact ground state.
- The TDA state [3,1,0] gives rise to the exact ground state for small |g| and to the 2nd excited state for larger |g|, with a transition point near |g|≈0.190.
- The total Hilbert space dimension for the 4-pair, 3-level example is 11, while TDA predicts 15 distinct multiphonon states.

## Context
This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin exact solution of the reduced BCS Hamiltonian (Richardson and Sherman, 1963; Richardson, 1964) by connecting it to the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (Rowe, 1970), a standard many-body method for describing elementary excitations as bosonic phonons. The parametrised algebra construction is related to the contraction of u(2) to hw(1) as defined by Arecchi et al. (1972) and Gilmore (2008). The results enable a clear-cut classification of which TDA multiphonon states correspond to physical eigenstates of the BCS Hamiltonian and which are forbidden by the Pauli principle, and provide a tool to understand the collective structure of exact pairing eigenstates in terms of TDA phonon configurations.
