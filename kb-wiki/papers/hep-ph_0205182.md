# A Novel Mechanism of H^0 Di-baryon Production in Proton-Proton Interactions from Parton Based Gribov-Regge Theory

> A novel mechanism of H^0 and strangelet production in hadronic interactions within the Gribov-Regge approach is presented. In contrast to traditional distillation approaches, here the production of multiple (strange) quark bags does not require large baryon densities or a QGP. The production cross section increases with center of mass energy. Rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of the H^0 are predicted for pp collisions at E_lab = 160 AGeV (SPS) and \sqrt s = 200 AGeV (RHIC). The predicted total H^0 multiplicities are of order of the Omega-baryon yield and can be accessed by the NA49 and the STAR experiments.

# A Novel Mechanism of H^0 Di-baryon Production in Proton-Proton Interactions from Parton Based Gribov-Regge Theory

## Key claim

The paper proposes that H^0 di-baryons and strangelets can be produced in ordinary proton-proton collisions via multiple Pomeron exchanges within the parton-based Gribov-Regge framework, without requiring high baryon densities or a quark-gluon plasma. The mechanism depends on the probability parameter P_qq = 0.02 for forming di-quark string ends, which is fixed by multi-strange baryon data rather than by any assumption about deconfinement.

## Method

The authors use the Monte Carlo event generator NEXUS 3, which implements parton-based Gribov-Regge theory, to simulate proton-proton collisions. In this model, multiple Pomeron exchanges (soft parton ladders) occur in parallel; each cut Pomeron is treated as two strings with quark or di-quark ends. When a string end is a di-quark (qq) rather than a single quark, the corresponding nucleon remnant gains extra quarks, potentially forming a six-quark bag with flavor content uuddss that can bind into an H^0 di-baryon. The remnant mass distribution follows P(m^2) proportional to (m^2)^{-1.5}, and remnants with masses between 2m_Lambda - e_binding and 2m_Lambda are considered H^0 bound states.

## Key numbers

- Probability parameter for di-quark string ends: P_qq = 0.02, fixed by multi-strange baryon data at 160 GeV
- Strange quark suppression factor: f_s = 0.3
- Remnant mass distribution exponent: alpha = 1.5
- H^0 yield in pp at SPS (E_lab = 160 GeV) for e_binding = 10 MeV: 0.141 x 10^(-5) per event
- H^0 yield in pp at SPS for e_binding = 200 MeV: 2.980 x 10^(-5) per event
- H^0 yield in pp at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) for e_binding = 10 MeV: 0.313 x 10^(-5) per event
- H^0 yield in pp at RHIC for e_binding = 200 MeV: 10.32 x 10^(-5) per event
- Scaling factor gamma for yield versus binding energy: 1.35 x 10^(-7) MeV^(-1) at SPS, 4.41 x 10^(-7) MeV^(-1) at RHIC
- Statistical errors on multiplicities: 3% less than Delta N/N less than 9%

## Context

This work extends the parton-based Gribov-Regge approach (NEXUS 3) to predict H^0 di-baryon production, contrasting with traditional distillation mechanisms such as Greiner (1990s) that require high baryon densities in heavy-ion collisions. The predicted H^0 multiplicities are of the same order as Omega baryon yields, making them accessible to the NA49 experiment at CERN and the STAR experiment at RHIC. The mechanism also implies that strangelet production is not a unique signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, as previously assumed. The results extend earlier QCD-inspired predictions of di-baryons by Jaffe (1977) and Schaffner-Bielich (1999, 2000) by providing a concrete production mechanism in elementary hadronic collisions.
