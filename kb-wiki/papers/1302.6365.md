# Nonperturbative infrared enhancement of nonGaussian correlators in de Sitter space

> We compute the four-point correlation function of a light O(N) scalar field in de Sitter space in the large-N limit. For superhorizon momentum modes, infrared effects strongly enhance the size of loop contributions. We find that in the deep infrared limit, the latter are of the same order as the tree-level one. The tree-level momentum structure, characteristic of a contact term, gets renormalized by a factor of order unity. In addition loop contributions give rise to a new momentum structure, characteristic of an exchange diagram, corresponding to the exchange of an effective composite scalar degree of freedom.

## Key claim

For superhorizon momentum modes of a light O(N) scalar field in de Sitter space, loop contributions to the four-point correlation function are strongly enhanced by infrared effects and, in the deep infrared limit, become of the same order as the tree-level contribution, meaning that no perturbative expansion in the coupling exists in that regime.

## Method

The author computed the equal-time four-point correlation function for superhorizon modes in the large-N limit of an O(N)-symmetric scalar field theory with quartic self-interactions on the expanding Poincaré patch of de Sitter space. The calculation used previously obtained expressions for the field propagator and four-point vertex function, which incorporate a self-consistent dynamically generated mass from the gap equation and resum an infinite series of bubble loop diagrams via an integral equation. The resulting analytical expression was analyzed in the deep infrared regime for both the massless field case and the case of spontaneous symmetry breaking at tree-level.

## Key numbers

* For light (massless) fields with tree-level mass much smaller than the coupling, the dynamically generated mass scales as M^2 proportional to sqrt(lambda), with epsilon = sqrt(lambda_eff/d) and pi_rho = 2d epsilon, where epsilon and pi_rho are both of order sqrt(lambda).
* In the deep infrared regime for massless fields, the functions controlling the four-point correlator saturate to g_1(x) approximately -1/(4 epsilon) and g_2(x) approximately 1/(8 epsilon), giving G^(4) proportional to sqrt(lambda).
* For the symmetry breaking case with lambda much less than |m_dS^2| much less than 1, the parameters are epsilon = lambda_eff/|m_dS^2| and pi_rho = 2|m_dS^2|, and in the deep infrared regime g_1(x) approximately -1/(bar_epsilon + epsilon) and g_2(x) approximately (bar_epsilon - epsilon)/(bar_epsilon + epsilon)^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of infrared effects in de Sitter space for light scalar fields, particularly the calculation of the four-point correlator by Riotto (2008) which included loop corrections to external legs but kept a tree-level interaction vertex. The present calculation shows that loop corrections to the four-point vertex itself are also amplified by infrared effects and contribute the same order as the tree-level term, correcting the earlier result. The work builds on the nonperturbative large-N methods developed by Serreau (2011, 2013) for the propagator and vertex functions, and demonstrates that the secular logarithmic growth of perturbative contributions eventually saturates to well-defined nonperturbative expressions. This enables further investigation of possible implications for multifield inflationary models.
