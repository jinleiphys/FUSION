# Hadron production in nuclear collisions at RHIC and high density QCD

> We analyze the first results on charged particle multiplicity at RHIC in the conventional eikonal approach and in the framework of high density QCD. We extract the fraction $F$ of the hadron multiplicity originating from ``hard'' (i.e. proportional to the number of binary collisions) processes; we find a strong growth of this fraction with energy: $F(\sqrt{s}=56 {\rm GeV}) \simeq 22%$, while $F(\sqrt{s}=130 {\rm GeV}) \simeq 37 %$. This indicates a rapid increase in the density of the produced particles. We outline the predictions of high density QCD for the centrality, energy, and atomic number dependence of hadron production. Surprisingly, the predictions of the conventional eikonal approach and of high density QCD for centrality dependence of hadron multiplicity at $\sqrt{s}=130 {\rm GeV}$ appear very similar.

## Key claim

The fraction of hadron multiplicity originating from "hard" (binary-collision-scaling) processes grows rapidly with energy, from about 22% at √s = 56 GeV to about 37% at √s = 130 GeV; however, the predictions of the conventional eikonal Glauber approach and of high-density QCD for the centrality dependence of multiplicity at √s = 130 GeV appear very similar, making it difficult to distinguish between the two frameworks using only centrality-dependent multiplicity data at a single energy.

## Method

The authors analyzed charged-particle multiplicity data from the PHOBOS Collaboration at RHIC using two frameworks: (1) a conventional eikonal Glauber approach where the total multiplicity is decomposed into a "soft" component proportional to the number of participants Npart and a "hard" component proportional to the number of binary collisions Ncoll, with the fraction x of the pp multiplicity attributed to hard processes extracted from data; and (2) a high-density QCD saturation approach where the multiplicity is proportional to the number of participants times the gluon structure function evaluated at the saturation scale Qs, which itself depends on the density of participants in the transverse plane. The mean numbers of participants and binary collisions for different centrality cuts were computed using the Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions and energy-dependent inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Fraction of multiplicity from hard processes: F(√s = 56 GeV) ≃ 22%, F(√s = 130 GeV) ≃ 37%
- Extracted parameter x (fraction of pp multiplicity from hard processes): x = 0.09 ± 0.03 at √s = 130 GeV; x = 0.05 ± 0.03 at √s = 56 GeV
- Charged particle pseudo-rapidity density at mid-rapidity for 6% most central Au-Au collisions: dn/dη = 555 ± 12(stat) ± 35(syst) at √s = 130 GeV; dn/dη = 408 ± 12(stat) ± 30(syst) at √s = 56 GeV
- Mean number of participants and collisions for 6% centrality cut at √s = 130 GeV: ⟨Npart⟩ = 339 ± 2, ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 1026 ± 6
- Mean number of participants and collisions for 6% centrality cut at √s = 56 GeV: ⟨Npart⟩ = 334 ± 2, ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 921 ± 6
- Saturation scale for central Au-Au at √s = 130 GeV: Qs² ≃ 2 GeV²
- "Parton liberation" coefficient extracted from data: c = 1.23 ± 0.20
- Initial energy density estimated from saturation approach: ε ≃ 18 GeV/fm³

## Context

This paper extends the eikonal Glauber formalism previously applied to SPS energies by Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi, and Satz (1997) to the new RHIC data from the PHOBOS Collaboration (2000), and compares it with the saturation approach of high-density QCD developed by Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1983), McLerran and Venugopalan (1994), and Mueller (2000). The work enables future experimental discrimination between the two approaches through analysis of transverse momentum distributions rather than multiplicity alone, and provides predictions for the energy, centrality, and atomic number dependence of hadron production that can be tested by further RHIC measurements.
