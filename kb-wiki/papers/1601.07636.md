# Probing neutron-proton dynamics by pions

> In order to investigate the nuclear symmetry energy at high density, we study the pion production in central collisions of neutron-rich nuclei ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ at 300 MeV/nucleon using a new approach by combining the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and a hadronic cascade model (JAM). The dynamics of neutrons and protons is solved by AMD, and then pions and $Δ$ resonances in the reaction process are handled by JAM. We see the mechanism how the $Δ$ resonance and pions are produced reflecting the dynamics of neutrons and protons. We also investigate the impacts of cluster correlations as well as of the high-density symmetry energy on the nucleon dynamics and consequently on the pion ratio. We find that the $Δ^-/Δ^{++}$ production ratio agrees very well with the neutron-proton squared ratio $(N/Z)^2$ in the high-density and high-momentum region. We show quantitatively that $Δ$ production ratio, and therefore $(N/Z)^2$, are directly reflected in the $π^-/π^+$ ratio, with modification in the final stage of the reaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Delta^-/\Delta^{++}$ production ratio in central collisions of neutron-rich nuclei at 300 MeV/nucleon agrees very well with the neutron-proton squared ratio $(N/Z)^2$ in the high-density and high-momentum region, and that this ratio is directly reflected in the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio with modification only in the final stage of the reaction. The authors hedge that this relationship holds specifically when the nucleon dynamics is solved by antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and pions and $\Delta$ resonances are handled by the hadronic cascade model JAM, and that the result depends on whether cluster correlations and the density dependence of the symmetry energy are included.

## Method

The authors combined antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to solve the dynamics of neutrons and protons with the hadronic cascade model JAM to handle $\Delta$ resonances and pions, treating $\Delta$ production perturbatively since the incident energy (300 MeV/nucleon) is near threshold. They performed calculations for central collisions of ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ at 300 MeV/nucleon with five different cases: AMD with cluster correlations using soft and stiff symmetry energies, AMD without cluster correlations using soft and stiff symmetry energies, and a standalone JAM calculation without mean field. The nucleon dynamics from AMD was passed to JAM as test particles every 2 fm/$c$, and 500 JAM events were generated from each of 1000 AMD events to improve statistics for pion production.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 300 MeV/nucleon for ${}^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ central collisions (impact parameter $0<b<1$ fm)
- Maximum density reached in the central region: approximately $2\rho_0$ at $t \simeq 20$ fm/$c$
- Total system neutron-to-proton ratio: $(N/Z)_{\text{sys}} = 1.56$
- Nuclear-matter incompressibility of the Skyrme SLy4 force: $K = 230$ MeV at saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.160$ fm$^{-3}$
- Symmetry energy at $\rho_0$: $S_0 = 32.0$ MeV, with slope parameter $L = 46$ MeV (asy-soft) or $L = 108$ MeV (asy-stiff)
- In the perturbative validation using JAM+JAM, the total energy per baryon was higher than the initial value by about 2 MeV on average at $t \approx 20$ fm/$c$ at 300 MeV/nucleon
- The pion multiplicity increased by 13% in standard JAM when incident energy was raised from 300 to 310 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to a 2.3-MeV increase of total energy per baryon

## Context

This work extends the study of the nuclear symmetry energy at high density using pion ratios, a probe proposed by Li (2008) and others, by introducing a hybrid AMD+JAM approach that includes cluster correlations not present in earlier transport models such as IBUU04, ImIQMD, pBUU, and BUU. The authors note that previous calculations by ImIQMD and pBUU gave contradictory predictions for the symmetry-energy dependence of the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio, and that the FOPI Collaboration data for Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon showed a $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio larger than the squared neutron-to-proton ratio of the total system, which most transport models underestimate. The present approach enables a more precise understanding of how nucleon dynamics, cluster formation, and the high-density symmetry energy affect the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio, and is intended to support analysis of future measurements at RIBF/RIKEN.
