# QCD phase structure under rotation

> We give an introduction to the phase structure of QCD matter under rotation based on effective four-fermion models. The effects of the magnetic field on the rotating QCD matter are also explored. Recent developments along these directions are overviewed, with special emphasis on the chiral phase transition. The rotational effects on pion condensation and color superconductivity are also discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that rotation suppresses the chiral condensate and other spin-0 pairings (such as diquark condensates) in QCD matter, acting analogously to a chemical potential for angular momentum; however, this suppression is absent at zero temperature and density when a physical boundary (with no-flux or MIT bag condition) is imposed, because the effective chemical potential from rotation never exceeds the threshold set by the lowest transverse momentum mode.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as a low-energy effective theory of QCD, formulated in a rotating frame with cylindrical symmetry. They derive the thermodynamic potential under the mean-field approximation, solve the gap equations for the chiral condensate (and, in some cases, the diquark condensate), and compute the phase diagram in the temperature–rotation plane. Two types of boundary conditions (no-flux and MIT bag) are applied to enforce causality, and the local density approximation is used to handle spatially inhomogeneous condensates.

## Key numbers

- The typical vorticity in heavy-ion collisions is about \(10^{21}\)–\(10^{22}\) s\(^{-1}\) (or tens of MeV), which may strongly influence QCD matter.
- The causality condition for a uniformly rotating system is \(\Omega R \leq 1\), where \(R\) is the transverse size.
- For the no-flux boundary condition, the first zero of the Bessel function is \(\xi_{0,1} = 2.40493 > 1/2\), ensuring that \(E - \Omega j > 0\) for all modes at \(T = \mu = 0\).
- In the phase diagram from Jiang et al. (2016), the chiral condensate undergoes a first-order transition when \(\Omega\) exceeds a critical value \(\Omega_c\) at low temperature, and a smooth crossover at high temperature.
- For the diquark condensate at \(\mu = \mu_B/3 = 400\) MeV, the condensate at \(r = 0.1\) GeV\(^{-1}\) is suppressed by increasing \(\Omega\) for several fixed temperatures.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the QCD phase diagram in the temperature–baryon chemical potential plane (reviewed by Fukushima (2010), Alford et al. (2007), and Bzdak et al. (2019)) by adding rotation as a new thermodynamic axis. It builds on studies of magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis (Gusynin et al. (1994, 1995); Bali et al. (2011, 2012)) and draws an analogy between rotation and magnetic field. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations of rotating matter (Yamamoto (2013)) and guide experimental searches for rotational effects in heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
