# Measuring the Temperature of Hot Nuclear Fragments

> A new thermometer based on fragment momentum fluctuations is presented. This thermometer exhibited residual contamination from the collective motion of the fragments along the beam axis. For this reason, the transverse direction has been explored. Additionally, a mass dependence was observed for this thermometer. This mass dependence may be the result of the Fermi momentum of nucleons or the different properties of the fragments (binding energy, spin etc..) which might be more sensitive to different densities and temperatures of the exploding fragments. We expect some of these aspects to be smaller for protons (and/or neutrons); consequently, the proton transverse momentum fluctuations were used to investigate the temperature dependence of the source.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new nuclear thermometer based on fragment momentum fluctuations (quadrupole moment distributions) but acknowledges that the longitudinal (beam-axis) component is contaminated by residual collective motion from source selection cuts, requiring use of the transverse momentum components; the extracted temperature also shows a mass dependence across fragment types, which may arise from Fermi momentum or differential emission times.

## Method

The authors reconstructed quasi-projectiles from reactions of 86,78Kr + 64,58Ni at 35 MeV/nucleon using the NIMROD-ISiS array. They defined a quadrupole-moment observable Q from fragment momenta in the center-of-mass frame, derived a relation between the variance of Q and temperature assuming a Maxwellian momentum distribution, and extracted apparent temperatures from the transverse (Qxy) and longitudinal (Qz) components. Source selection cuts on total charge, longitudinal velocity relative to the largest fragment, and momentum quadrupole deformation were applied, and the excitation energy was calculated from calorimetry including neutron multiplicity corrections from the Neutron Ball.

## Key numbers

* The average contamination in the yield of a given isotope from the Gaussian-fitting method was approximately 5% across all reaction systems and all detectors.
* For 3He and 4He isotopes, 3He yields approximately 35% higher temperature than 4He across all E*/A; 4He apparent temperatures show approximately 15% increase across E*/A = 1.5–7.5 MeV/nucleon, while 3He temperatures increase by approximately 50% over the same range.
* The proton momentum fluctuation thermometer yields temperatures rising from approximately 4 MeV to 10 MeV over the excitation energy range when collective motion is removed using transverse kinetic energy.
* The level density parameter used for normalization of the reduced excitation energy was a0 = A/13.3 MeV−1.
* The Fermi energy of the nucleus ground state was taken as 36 MeV for the density analysis.

## Context

This work extends prior nuclear thermometry efforts including moving-source fitting (Wada 1989, Hirsch 1984, Odeh 2000), isotopic thermometers (Albergo 1985, Viola 2006, Tsang 1997, Hsi 1994, Trautmann 2007), and excited-state ratio thermometers (Borderie 2007). The momentum-fluctuation thermometer is compared to the caloric curve compilation of Natowitz (2002) and to Fermi-gas curves with level density parameters a = A/8 and A/13 MeV−1. The paper enables future study of the nuclear equation of state at nearly constant density and investigation of whether the system remains a quantum Fermi system at the highest measured temperatures, in contrast to assumptions of classical behavior (Bonasera 2000).
