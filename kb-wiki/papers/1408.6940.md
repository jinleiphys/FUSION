# Dynamic versus static fission paths with realistic interactions

> The properties of dynamic (least action) fission paths are analyzed and compared to the ones of the more traditional static (least energy) paths. Both the BCPM and Gogny D1M energy density functionals are used in the calculation of the HFB constrained configurations providing the potential energy and collective inertias. The action is computed as in the WKB method. A full variational search of the least-action path over the complete variational space of HFB wave functions is cumbersome and probably unnecessary if the relevant degrees of freedom are identified. In this paper, we consider the particle number fluctuation degree of freedom that explores the amount of pairing correlations in the wave function. For a given shape, the minimum action can be up a factor of three smaller than the action computed for the minimum energy state with the same shape. The impact of this reduction on the lifetimes is enormous and dramatically improves the agreement with experimental data in the few examples considered.

## Key claim

For a given nuclear shape, the minimum action can be up to a factor of three smaller than the action computed for the minimum energy state with the same shape, but this reduction only occurs when the particle number fluctuation (pairing correlations) is included as a collective degree of freedom; including shape degrees of freedom like octupole or hexadecapole moments yields essentially no reduction.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed for uranium isotopes using both the BCPM and Gogny D1M energy density functionals, with the fission path parametrized by the axial quadrupole moment Q20. The action was computed using the WKB method with collective inertias obtained from both the Adiabatic Time Dependent HFB (ATDHFB) and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) approximations, and the particle number fluctuation ΔN² was treated as an additional collective variable to be minimized for each fixed quadrupole moment.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁴U with BCPM, the minimum action path yields spontaneous fission lifetimes of 0.18 × 10²³ s (ATDHFB) and 0.21 × 10¹⁹ s (GCM), compared to the minimum energy path values of 0.81 × 10⁴³ s and 0.70 × 10³⁰ s respectively, representing reductions of 20 and 11 orders of magnitude.
- For ²³⁴U with BCPM, the minimum action integrand at a given shape can be up to a factor of three smaller than the action computed for the minimum energy state with the same shape.
- For ²³²U with Gogny D1M, the minimum action lifetimes are 5.4 × 10²⁰ s (ATDHFB) and 1.5 × 10¹⁷ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 2.5 × 10²¹ s.
- For ²³⁴U with Gogny D1M, the minimum action lifetimes are 7.3 × 10²¹ s (ATDHFB) and 2.7 × 10¹⁸ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 4.7 × 10²³ s.
- For ²³⁶U with Gogny D1M, the minimum action lifetimes are 2.8 × 10²³ s (ATDHFB) and 9.9 × 10¹⁹ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 7.8 × 10²³ s.
- For ²³⁸U with Gogny D1M, the minimum action lifetimes are 1.6 × 10²⁴ s (ATDHFB) and 6.7 × 10²⁰ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 2.6 × 10²³ s.
- For ²³²U with BCPM, the minimum action lifetimes are 8.6 × 10¹⁹ s (ATDHFB) and 8.2 × 10¹⁶ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 2.5 × 10²¹ s.
- For ²³⁴U with BCPM, the minimum action lifetimes are 1.7 × 10²² s (ATDHFB) and 2.1 × 10¹⁸ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 4.7 × 10²³ s.
- For ²³⁶U with BCPM, the minimum action lifetimes are 1.9 × 10²² s (ATDHFB) and 1.2 × 10¹⁸ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 7.8 × 10²³ s.
- For ²³⁸U with BCPM, the minimum action lifetimes are 6.1 × 10²¹ s (ATDHFB) and 6.2 × 10¹⁷ s (GCM), compared to the experimental value of 2.6 × 10²³ s.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Moretto and Babinet (1974) that pairing correlations significantly impact fission action, and builds on calculations by the Lublin school (Staszczak, 1985; Pomorski, 2007) who observed reductions of up to 8 orders of magnitude in Cm isotopes. The results contradict recent Skyrme-based calculations that found minimum energy and minimum action results to be essentially the same, but those calculations considered only shape degrees of freedom (Q20 and Q22) rather than pairing. This work enables more reliable predictions of spontaneous fission lifetimes using mean field techniques, as the minimum action approach shows much weaker dependence on the choice of energy density functional and collective mass approximation.
