# Spectral functions and in-medium properties of hadrons

> The in-medium modifications of hadron properties such as masses and decay widths have been a major focus of the scientific work of Gerry Brown and the insights gained by him and his collaborators made them major drivers of this field for several decades. Their prediction of experimental signals in di-lepton pair production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions were instrumental in initiating large experimental campaigns which continue until today. In this chapter we review recent results which elucidate the relation of hadronic spectral properties at finite temperature and density to the restoration of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the restoration of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry with increasing temperature and density leads to the degeneracy of parity-partner spectral functions, but this degeneracy occurs only at temperatures significantly higher than the (pseudo) critical temperature derived from the chiral condensate, meaning chiral correlations persist well into the restored phase.

## Method

The authors employ the Quark-Meson (QM) model, a low-energy effective theory of QCD with two light quark flavors, and compute spectral functions for pions and sigma mesons using the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) within the Local Potential Approximation (LPA). They derive flow equations for the effective potential and for the retarded two-point functions, perform an analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski space-time on the level of the flow equations, and solve these equations numerically to obtain in-medium spectral functions at finite temperature and quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Chiral crossover transition at zero chemical potential: T ≈ 170 MeV
* Chiral critical endpoint (CEP) located at T_c ≈ 9 MeV and μ ≈ 292 MeV
* Vacuum pion decay constant: σ_0 ≡ f_π = 93.5 MeV
* Vacuum pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV
* Vacuum sigma meson mass: m_σ = 509 MeV
* Vacuum quark mass: m_ψ = 299 MeV
* UV cutoff scale: Λ = 1 GeV; IR scale: k_IR = 40 MeV
* Entropy density of pions at low temperatures agrees with chiral perturbation theory result from Lang (2012)
* Total entropy density approaches Stefan-Boltzmann value s_SB/T^3 = 14π^2/15 at high temperatures

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program initiated by Gerry Brown and collaborators on in-medium modifications of hadron properties and their relation to chiral symmetry restoration, particularly the prediction of experimental signals in di-lepton pair production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The FRG approach goes beyond mean-field theory (MFT) and the Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) used by Brown and Bolsterli (1959) for nuclear collective excitations, by incorporating thermal and quantum fluctuations in a thermodynamically consistent and symmetry-conserving manner. The results enable future calculations of transport properties such as shear viscosity using the Green-Kubo formalism, and can be extended to study vector mesons (rho and its chiral partner) within a gauged Quark-Meson Model, as begun by Urban (2001) and ongoing work by C. Jung.
