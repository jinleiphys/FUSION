# Radiation of single photons from $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the CERN SPS and quark hadron phase transition

> The production of single photons in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the CERN SPS as measured by the WA98 experiment is analysed. A quark gluon plasma is assumed to be formed initially, which expands, cools, hadronizes, and undergoes freeze-out. A rich hadronic equation of state is used and the transverse expansion of the interacting system is taken into account. The recent estimates of photon production in quark-matter (at two loop level) along with the dominant reactions in the hadronic matter leading to photons are used. About 50% of the single photons are seen to have a thermal origin. An addition of the thermal and prompt photons is seen to provide a very good description of the data. Most of the thermal photons having large transverse momenta arise from the quark-matter, which contributes dominantly through the mechanism of annihilation of quarks with scattering, and which in turn is possible only in a hot and dense plasma of quarks and gluons. The results thus confirm the formation of quark gluon plasma and the existence of this mechanism of the production of single photons.

## Key claim

The WA98 single-photon data from central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS are very well described by a model assuming an initially formed quark-gluon plasma, with about 50% of the single photons having a thermal origin; the authors argue this confirms QGP formation and the existence of the annihilation-with-scattering mechanism, but the prompt-photon contribution is taken as an upper limit and the result depends on assuming a very early thermalization time of about 0.2 fm/c.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic hydrodynamic calculation with transverse expansion, assuming an initially chemically and thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma that expands, cools, undergoes a first-order phase transition at T=180 MeV to a rich hadronic equation of state (all hadrons with mass below 2.5 GeV), and freezes out at 120 MeV. Photon production rates included two-loop quark-matter rates from Aurenche et al. (with a corrected numerical factor of 4) and hadronic rates from a two-loop photon self-energy model with pi-rho and A1 resonance contributions; prompt photons were taken from NLO pQCD calculations by Wong and Wang including intrinsic parton transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 335 MeV for the favored formation time tau0 = 0.20 fm/c, derived from dN/dy = 750 for the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions
- Critical temperature for the phase transition set at T_C = 180 MeV, with freeze-out at 120 MeV
- Average number of participants estimated as 380 for impact parameters 0 to 4.5 fm, with a mass number of 190 used for the transverse area
- Initial formation time tau0 = 1/(3 T0) ≈ 0.20 fm/c, favored by the data over values of 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm/c (corresponding to T0 = 265, 232, 210, and 196 MeV respectively)
- About 50% of the single photons have a thermal origin; the sum of thermal and prompt photons provides a very good description of the WA98 data

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of the WA80 upper limit on single photons in S+Au collisions (Srivastava and Sinha, 1994) by incorporating the two-loop quark-matter photon rates of Aurenche et al. (1998) and a rich hadronic equation of state including all hadrons below 2.5 GeV, following the approach of Cleymans, Redlich, and Srivastava (1997). The results contradict the findings of Alam et al. (2001) and Peressounko and Pokrovsky (2000), who used simpler models with uniform energy-density profiles or initial transverse flow and obtained smaller QGP contributions; the authors argue their initial conditions also provide a good description of the intermediate-mass dilepton excess measured by the NA50 collaboration. The work enables future studies of photon radiation from quark matter at RHIC and LHC energies, where larger temperatures are expected.
