# A light particle solution to the cosmic lithium problem

> We point out that the cosmological abundance of ${}^7$Li can be reduced down to observed values if during its formation Big Bang Nucleosynthesis is modified by the presence of light electrically neutral particles $X$ that have substantial interactions with nucleons. We find that the lithium problem can be solved without affecting the precisely measured abundances of deuterium and helium if the following conditions are satisfied: the mass and lifetimes of such particles are bounded by $ 1.6~{\rm MeV}\leq m_X \leq 20~{\rm MeV}$ and $ {\rm few}~100~{\rm s} \lesssim τ_X \lesssim 10^4~{\rm s}$, and the abundance times the absorption cross section by either deuterium or ${}^7$Be are comparable to the Hubble rate, $n_X σ_{\rm abs} v \sim H$, at the time of ${}^7$Be formation. We include $X$-initiated reactions into the primordial nucleosynthesis framework, observe that it leads to a substantial reduction of the freeze-out abundances of ${}^7$Li+${}^7$Be, and find specific model realizations of this scenario. Concentrating on the axion-like-particle case, $X=a$, we show that all these conditions can be satisifed if the coupling to $d$-quarks is in the range of $f_d^{-1} \sim {\rm TeV}^{-1}$, which can be probed at intensity frontier experiments.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the cosmological lithium problem can be solved by introducing light, electrically neutral particles X with masses between 1.6 and 20 MeV and lifetimes between a few hundred and 10,000 seconds, provided that the product of their abundance and absorption cross section on either deuterium or beryllium-7 is comparable to the Hubble rate at the time of beryllium-7 formation. The mechanism avoids overproducing deuterium because neutrons are temporarily "borrowed" from deuterium rather than injected as extra neutrons, unlike in earlier weak-scale particle solutions.

## Method
The authors modified their BBN code to include the effects of X particles, adding parameters for mass, lifetime, initial abundance relative to baryons, and reaction cross sections for X-induced spallation of beryllium-7 and deuterium. They performed scans over reaction rates and lifetimes to identify regions where lithium-7 plus beryllium-7 abundances are reduced to observed levels while deuterium and helium-4 remain within observational bounds. For the axion-like-particle realization, they related the absorption cross sections to photo-nuclear cross sections using an E1 dipole approximation and estimated event rates at beam dump experiments.

## Key numbers
- Observed lithium-7 abundance: (1.6 ± 0.3) × 10^{-10} relative to hydrogen, from PopII star atmospheres (Sbordone 2010)
- Standard BBN prediction for lithium-7: (4.68 ± 0.67) × 10^{-10} relative to hydrogen (Cyburt 2015)
- Observed deuterium abundance: (2.53 ± 0.04) × 10^{-5} relative to hydrogen (Cooke 2013)
- X particle mass range: 1.6 MeV ≤ m_X ≤ 20 MeV
- X particle lifetime range: a few hundred seconds ≤ τ_X ≤ 10^4 seconds
- For R1 (beryllium-7 destruction): (n_X/n_b) × σ_Be v ≈ (1-2) × 10^{-31} cm^2
- For R2 (deuterium destruction): (n_X/n_b) × σ_D v ≈ (3-7) × 10^{-31} cm^2
- ALP coupling to d-quarks: f_d^{-1} ∼ TeV^{-1}
- Estimated LSND event rate: N_events ∼ 6 × (TeV/f_d)^4

## Context
This work extends the search for particle physics solutions to the cosmological lithium problem beyond the previously disfavored scenarios involving extra neutrons from decaying weak-scale particles, which were ruled out by precise deuterium measurements (Coc 2014, Kusakabe 2014). The mechanism contrasts with earlier proposals such as negatively charged weak-scale particles catalyzing BBN (Pospelov 2007, Bird 2007) and sub-GeV particle solutions (Pospelov 2010, Poulin 2015). The paper enables experimental searches for hadronic axion-like particles at intensity frontier experiments such as LSND and future beam dump facilities.
