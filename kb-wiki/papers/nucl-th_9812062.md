# Four-nucleon shell-model calculations in a Faddeev-like approach

> We use equations for Faddeev amplitudes to solve the shell-model problem for four nucleons in the model space that includes up to 14 hbar Omega harmonic-oscillator excitations above the unperturbed ground state. Two- and three-body effective interactions derived from the Reid93 and Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon potentials are used in the calculations. Binding energies, excitations energies, point-nucleon radii and electromagnetic and strangeness charge form factors for 4He are studied. The structure of the Faddeev-like equations is discussed and a formula for matrix elements of the permutation operators in a harmonic-oscillator basis is given. The dependence on harmonic-oscillator excitations allowed in the model space and on the harmonic-oscillator frequency is investigated. It is demonstrated that the use of the three-body effective interactions improves the convergence of the results.

# Four-nucleon shell-model calculations in a Faddeev-like approach

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Faddeev-like formalism combined with shell-model techniques can solve the four-nucleon problem in model spaces up to 14 ℏΩ excitations, and that including three-body effective interactions improves convergence of the results compared to using only two-body effective interactions, though exact convergence to the bare interaction limit is only approached as N_max → ∞.

## Method

The authors generalize their earlier three-nucleon Faddeev-shell-model formalism to four nucleons by introducing Faddeev amplitudes that are fully antisymmetrized for three particles, then solving the eigenvalue problem for the symmetrized Hamiltonian in a harmonic-oscillator basis truncated by total oscillator quanta N ≤ N_max. Two-body and three-body effective interactions are constructed via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method from the Reid93 and Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon potentials, with the three-body effective interaction derived from three-nucleon solutions in a larger model space (N_3max ≈ 30) and then folded into the four-nucleon calculation.

## Key numbers

- Model space includes up to 14 ℏΩ harmonic-oscillator excitations above the unperturbed ground state (N_max = 14)
- Three-nucleon basis is constructed for all N_1 ≡ 2n+l+2N+L ≤ N_max, with N_3max ≈ 30 used for three-body effective interaction construction
- Two-body effective interaction is constructed for all partial-wave channels up to j = 6
- Two-nucleon relative-coordinate Hamiltonian is solved in a full HO basis truncated by keeping states typically up to n = 152
- Results are presented for ⁴He with J=0, T=0 for both positive and negative parity states

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-nucleon Faddeev-shell-model calculations (Navratil and Barrett, 1998) to the four-nucleon system, and generalizes the approach beyond the largest previous shell-model calculations for ⁴He by Ceuleneer et al. (1988), which used a 10ℏΩ model space. The method provides an alternative to traditional Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations (Yakubovsky, 1967), Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC), and correlated hyperspherical harmonics expansion methods (Viviani et al., 1995), while enabling simultaneous study of ground-state and excited-state properties with realistic NN potentials. The formalism is designed to be extendable to systems with A > 4.
