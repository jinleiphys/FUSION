# Variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter. III. S-wave pairing

> We apply parquet-diagram summation methods for the calculation of the superfluid gap in $S$-wave pairing in neutron matter for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions such as the Argonne $v_6$ and the Reid $v_6$ potentials. It is shown that diagrammatic contributions that are outside the parquet class play an important role. These are, in variational theories, identified as so-called "commutator contributions". Moreover, using a particle-hole propagator appropriate for a superfluid system results in the suppression of the spin-channel contribution to the induced interaction. Applying these corrections to the pairing interaction, our results agree quite well with Quantum Monte Carlo data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that parquet-diagram summation methods for calculating the S-wave superfluid gap in neutron matter require corrections beyond the parquet class (identified as "commutator contributions" in variational theories), and that using a particle-hole propagator appropriate for a superfluid system suppresses the spin-channel contribution to the induced interaction; with these corrections, results agree quite well with Quantum Monte Carlo data, but the agreement depends on correctly balancing the energy numerator and interaction terms in the pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors apply parquet-diagram summation methods combined with Jastrow-Feenberg variational theory to compute the S-wave pairing gap in neutron matter, using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Argonne v6 and Reid v6 potentials). They incorporate "beyond-parquet" commutator contributions and use a BCS-appropriate Lindhard function for the particle-hole propagator in the superfluid state, rather than the normal-state Lindhard function. The gap equation is solved with the effective pairing interaction derived from the correlated BCS (CBCS) theory, including the energy numerator term that regularizes the momentum integrals.

## Key numbers

- The superfluid gap in low-density neutron matter can be of the order of half the Fermi energy, making the weak-coupling approximation invalid in that regime.
- The long-wavelength limit of the static structure function for the superfluid system is SF(0+) = 2 * sum_k u_k^2 v_k^2 / sum_k v_k^2 > 0, which prevents the Euler equation from having sensible solutions when the Landau parameter F0^s < 0.
- The pairing interaction at the Fermi surface is defined as tilde{W}_F = (1/(2 k_F^2)) integral_0^{2k_F} k dk tilde{W}(k) = N W_{k_F,k_F}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter (Krotscheck and Wang, 2020, Parts I and II) to the S-wave pairing problem, building on the correlated BCS theory developed in earlier work (CBFPairing, 2019; fullbcs, 2020). The results agree with Quantum Monte Carlo data (Gandolfi, 2009) and address the long-standing problem of treating strong correlations in superfluid neutron matter, which was first studied using Jastrow methods by Yang and Clark (1971) and later by the FHNC-EL approach. The work enables more reliable calculations of neutron star crust properties and tests the limits of parquet-diagram methods for strongly interacting fermionic systems.
