# Benchmark calculations of fully heavy compact and molecular tetraquark states

> We calculate the mass spectrum of the S-wave fully heavy tetraquark systems $ QQ\bar Q\bar Q~(Q=c,b) $ with both normal $ (J^{PC}=0^{++},1^{+-},2^{++}) $ and exotic $ (J^{PC}=0^{+-},1^{++},2^{+-}) $ C-parities using three different quark potential models (AL1, AP1, BGS). The exotic C-parity systems refer to the ones that cannot be composed of two S-wave ground heavy quarkonia. We incorporate the molecular dimeson and compact diquark-antidiquark spatial correlations simultaneously, thereby discerning the actual configurations of the states. We employ the Gaussian expansion method to solve the four-body Schrödinger equation, and the complex scaling method to identify the resonant states. The mass spectra in three different models qualitatively agree with each other. We obtain several resonant states with $ J^{PC} = 0^{++}, 1^{+-}, 2^{++}, 1^{++} $ in the mass region $(6.92,7.30)\, \mathrm{GeV}$, some of which are good candidates of the experimentally observed $X(6900)$ and $X(7200)$. We also obtain several exotic C-parity zero-width states with $ J^{PC}=0^{+-} $ and $ 2^{+-} $. These zero-width states have no corresponding S-wave diquarkonium threshold and can only decay strongly to final states with P-wave quarkonia. With the notation $T_{4Q,J(C)}(M)$, we deduce from the root mean square radii that the $ X(7200) $ candidates $ T_{4c,0(+)}(7173), T_{4c,2(+)}(7214) $ and the state $ T_{4c,1(-)}(7191) $ look like molecular states although most of the resonant and zero-width states are compact states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several fully charmed tetraquark resonant states obtained from three different quark potential models are good candidates for the experimentally observed X(6900) and X(7200), but only when considering that the quark potential models have errors up to tens of MeV and that the widths of the quarkonia were neglected in the calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation for S-wave fully heavy tetraquark systems QQ̄QQ̄ (Q=c,b) using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with both compact diquark-antidiquark and molecular dimeson spatial configurations simultaneously. They employed the complex scaling method (CSM) to identify resonant states and distinguish them from scattering states, using three different quark potential models (AL1, AP1, BGS) without introducing any new free parameters. The spatial structures of the states were analyzed through root-mean-square radii calculated from a non-antisymmetric component of the wave function to eliminate ambiguity from antisymmetrization of identical particles.

## Key numbers

- Resonant states with JPC = 0++, 1+-, 2++, 1++ found in the mass region (6.92, 7.30) GeV
- Lower 0++ state at approximately 6978 MeV (AP1) with width Γ ≈ 72 MeV; higher 0++ state at approximately 7173 MeV (AP1) with width Γ ≈ 40 MeV
- Lower 2++ state at approximately 7017 MeV (AP1) with width Γ ≈ 78 MeV; higher 2++ state at approximately 7214 MeV (AP1) with width Γ ≈ 60 MeV
- X(7200) candidates T4c,0(+)(7173), T4c,2(+)(7214), and state T4c,1(-)(7191) identified as molecular states based on rms radii analysis
- No resonant states found in the mass region (6.2, 6.6) GeV, meaning no candidates for X(6400) or X(6600)
- Several exotic C-parity zero-width states with JPC = 0+- and 2+- obtained, which have no corresponding S-wave diquarkonium threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Wang et al. (2022, 2023) that used GEM and CSM for fully charmed tetraquark systems but did not include fully bottomed tetraquark states or systems with exotic C-parity (JPC = 0+-, 1++, 2+-). The authors also build on their own prior work (Chen et al., 2023) that developed the approach for analyzing spatial structures of tetraquark states. The calculations provide benchmark results that can guide experimental searches for fully heavy tetraquark states beyond the already observed X(6900) and X(7200), particularly for the 1+- states that cannot decay into J/ψJ/ψ or ψ(2S)J/ψ channels.
