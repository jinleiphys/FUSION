# Consideration of Nuclear Forces from the Viewpoint of the Renormalization Group - Relation of Nuclear Forces between Phenomenological Approach and Nuclear Effective Field Theory -

> A relation between nuclear forces derived using a phenomenological approach and nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) is proposed from a renormalization group point of view. A phenomenological nuclear force (V_ph) and an NEFT-based NN-potential (V_EFT) are satisfactorily related to each other through the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) method. It is clearly shown that use of the simple contact interactions in NEFT is adequate to simulate small scale phenomena, and that an NEFT-based NN-potential (V_EFT) is free from dependence on the model used to describe small scale phenomena. We study the characteristics of V_EFT from a WRG point of view, emphasizing points that have not previously been fully recognized. We also use the V_{low k} method and a unitary transformation method to relate V_ph to V_EFT. It is found that they are not appropriate for this purpose.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that phenomenological nuclear forces (V_ph) and nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) based potentials (V_EFT) can be satisfactorily related through the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) method, but only when the model-space reduction is performed in a way that preserves full off-shell T-matrix invariance; the V_low_k method and unitary transformation method are found to be inappropriate for this purpose.

## Method

The author starts with three high-precision phenomenological NN-potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Reid93) and reduces their model spaces using the Wilsonian renormalization group equation, which integrates out high-momentum nucleon states while preserving full off-shell T-matrix invariance. The resulting model-space potentials are then fitted to NEFT-based potentials consisting of one-pion-exchange plus contact interactions with zero, two, and four derivatives. The same procedure is also attempted using the V_low_k method and a unitary transformation method, and the results are compared for consistency with NEFT assumptions of naturalness and systematicness.

## Key numbers

- The model-space reduction is performed to a cutoff of Lambda = 200 MeV for the np ^1S_0 partial wave, with on-shell momentum p = 10 MeV
- The ratio |C_2 Lambda^2 / C_0| for the ^1S_0 channel at Lambda = 200 MeV and p = 10 MeV is 0.217 for V_piI and 0.289 for V_piII, demonstrating naturalness
- For Lambda = 70 MeV and p = 10 MeV, the ratio |C_2 Lambda^2 / C_0| is 7.56 x 10^(-2) for V_pilessI and 8.93 x 10^(-2) for V_pilessII
- The C_0 LEC for ^1S_0 at Lambda = 200 MeV and p = 10 MeV is -5.44 fm^2 for V_piI and -5.52 fm^2 for V_piII
- The C_2 LEC for ^1S_0 at Lambda = 200 MeV and p = 10 MeV is 1.15 fm^4 for V_piI and 1.55 fm^4 for V_piII

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the relationship between phenomenological NN-potentials (such as the CD-Bonn potential by Machleidt (2001) and the Nijmegen potential by Stoks et al. (1994)) and nuclear effective field theory as originally proposed by Weinberg (1990). The paper provides a quantitative top-down test of NEFT assumptions (naturalness, systematicness, and integrability) that complements previous bottom-up studies where LECs were determined from experimental data. The results enable a deeper understanding of why NEFT-based potentials are model-independent and why simple contact interactions suffice to simulate small-scale physics, while also establishing that the WRG method is the proper reduction scheme for connecting phenomenological and EFT approaches.
