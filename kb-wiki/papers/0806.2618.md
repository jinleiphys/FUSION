# A Fresh Look into the Neutron EDM and Magnetic Susceptibility

> We reexamine the estimate of the neutron Electric Dipole Moment (NEDM) from chiral and QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) approaches. In the former, we evaluate the pion mass corrections which are about 5% of the leading Log. results. However, the chiral estimate can be affected by the unknown value of the renormalizaton scale nu. For QSSR, we analyze the effect of the nucleon interpolating currents on the existing predictions. We conclude that previous QSSR results are not obtained within the optimal choice of these operators, which lead to an overestimate of these results by about a factor 4. The weakest upper bound |theta|< 2 10^-9 for the strong CP-violating angle is obtained from QSSR, while the strongest upper bound |theta|< 1.3 10^-10 comes from the chiral approach evaluated at the scale ν=M_N. We also re-estimate the proton magnetic susceptibility, which is an important input in the QSSR estimate of the NEDM.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines the neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) from chiral and QCD spectral sum rule (QSSR) approaches, finding that previous QSSR results overestimate the NEDM by about a factor of 4 due to a non-optimal choice of nucleon interpolating currents, while the chiral estimate is affected by the unknown renormalization scale nu.

## Method

The chiral approach evaluates pion mass corrections to the NEDM using an effective Lagrangian with pseudoscalar meson and baryon fields, computing Feynman diagrams for the CP-violating pi NN coupling. The QSSR approach analyzes the nucleon two-point correlator in a background with nonzero theta and electromagnetic field, using Borel/Laplace sum rules (LSR) to extract the NEDM and the proton magnetic susceptibility chi, testing the dependence on the nucleon operator mixing parameter b.

## Key numbers

- Chiral approach NEDM: D_N = (40 +/- 10) x 10^{-17} theta cm, corresponding to the value at scale nu = M_N
- Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) NEDM range: D_N(nu) = (5.4 to 47.6) x 10^{-17} theta cm, depending on the unknown renormalization scale nu
- Strongest upper bound on CP-violating angle: |theta| < 1.3 x 10^{-10} from the chiral approach at nu = M_N
- Weakest upper bound on CP-violating angle: |theta| < 2 x 10^{-9} from QSSR
- QSSR NEDM: D_N = (4.24 +/- 1.40) x 10^{-17} theta cm, using optimal nucleon current b ~ -0.5
- Proton magnetic susceptibility: chi = -(8.5 +/- 1.0) GeV^{-2} from LSR analysis
- Constituent quark model NEDM: D_N = (8.7 +/- 4.4) x 10^{-17} theta cm
- CP-violating pi NN coupling: gbar_{pi NN} = (0.0282 +/- 0.0071) theta

## Context

This work extends the chiral estimate of the NEDM from Crewther et al. (1979) by evaluating pion mass corrections (about 5% of the leading-log result) and clarifies the scale dependence issue raised by Pich (1991) in Chiral Perturbation Theory. The QSSR analysis revisits the results of Ritz (1994) and shows that the choice b=1 for the nucleon interpolating current used there is not optimal; the preferred range -1 <= b <= 0 includes the Ioffe (1981) choice b=-1 and the Dosch et al. (1989) optimal value b=-1/5. The re-estimated magnetic susceptibility chi agrees with Ioffe and Smilga (1984) and the triangle anomaly result from Vainshtein (2003). The results enable improved constraints on the strong CP-violating angle theta and provide inputs for future lattice QCD calculations of the NEDM.
