# Medium-induced bosonic clusters in a Bose-Fermi mixture: Towards simulating cluster formations in neutron-rich matter

> Considering bosonic atoms immersed in a dilute Fermi gas, we theoretically investigate medium-induced bosonic clusters associated with fermion-mediated two- and three-body interactions. Using the variational approach combined with the fermion-mediated interactions, we numerically calculate the binding energies of two- and three-body bosonic clusters in a one-dimensional system. It is found that the bosonic clusters can be formed even with a repulsive boson-boson interaction due to the fermion-mediated interactions. Our results would be relevant for ultracold atomic systems as well as analogue quantum simulations of alpha clusters in neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim

Bosonic clusters can form even with repulsive boson-boson interactions when immersed in a dilute Fermi gas, due to fermion-mediated two- and three-body interactions, but this result is derived in a one-dimensional system at low-energy and long-wavelength limits with contact-type interactions, and the authors note that nonlocal properties and higher-dimensional effects may be important for more quantitative studies.

## Method

The authors used a variational approach combined with diagrammatically derived fermion-mediated two- and three-body interaction strengths at the low-energy and long-wavelength limits in one dimension. They solved the resulting Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian-type equation for three-body bound states at zero center-of-mass momentum, considering bosons with bare mass \(m_b\) immersed in a dilute Fermi gas of fermions with bare mass \(m_f\) at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

* Three-body ground-state energy \(E_3\) in vacuum agrees with the McGuire trimer result \(E_3 = -4/(m_b a_{bb}^2)\), where \(a_{bb}\) is the boson-boson scattering length.
* For a \(^6\)Li-\(^{133}\)Cs mixture at \(1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bf}) = 1.0\), the three-body energy \(E_3\) becomes nonzero even at \(1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bb}) = 0\) and reaches zero at \(1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bb}) \simeq -1.8\).
* The in-medium two-body energy is given by \(E_2 = -1/(m_b {a_{bb}^{\rm eff}}^2)\) with effective scattering length \(1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bb}^{\rm eff}) = 1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bb}) + (m_f m_b)/(4\pi m_r^2) (1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bf}))^2\).
* At \(1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bf}) = 1/(k_{\rm F} a_{bb}) = 1.0\), \(|E_3|\) is larger than \(|E_2|\) for all mass ratios explored, including the alpha-neutron relevant case \(m_b/m_f = 4\).

## Context

This work extends the study of medium-induced cluster formation from three-dimensional alpha particles in cold neutron matter (Moriya 2021) to a one-dimensional Bose-Fermi mixture, using ultracold atoms as a quantum simulator. The fermion-mediated interactions are analogous to the RKKY interaction (Ruderman 1954, Kasuya 1956, Yosida 1957) and have been experimentally studied in Bose-Fermi mixtures (DeSalvo 2019, Chen 2022). The results enable experimental observation of in-medium cluster formation via three-body recombination processes in ultracold atomic systems and propose a pathway to simulate alpha clustering in neutron-rich matter.
