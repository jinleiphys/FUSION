# Multipole decomposition of the nucleon transverse phase space

> We present a complete study of the leading-twist quark Wigner distributions in the nucleon, discussing both the $\mathsf T$-even and $\mathsf T$-odd sector, along with all the possible configurations of the quark and nucleon polarizations. We identify the basic multipole structures associated with each distribution in the transverse phase space, providing a transparent interpretation of the spin-spin and spin-orbit correlations of quarks and nucleon encoded in these functions. Projecting the multipole parametrization of the Wigner functions onto the transverse-position and the transverse-momentum spaces, we find a natural link with the corresponding multipole parametrizations for the generalized parton distributions and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions, respectively. Finally, we show results for all the distributions in the transverse phase space, introducing a representation that allows one to visualize simultaneously the multipole structures in both the transverse-position and transverse-momentum spaces.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete classification of all 32 leading-twist quark Wigner distributions in the nucleon transverse phase space via multipole decomposition, but the specific numerical results shown are model-dependent (computed within a light-front constituent quark model) and the naive T-odd contributions are constructed from the T-even coefficient functions with arbitrarily chosen global signs, not from a full calculation including initial- and final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors start from the quark GTMD correlator at leading twist and perform a Fourier transform to obtain five-dimensional phase-space distributions in longitudinal momentum fraction x, transverse momentum k_T, and impact parameter b_T. They decompose each of the 32 distributions (16 polarization configurations each with T-even and T-odd parts) into basic multipoles in both k_T-space and b_T-space, where the multipole orders (m_k, m_b) are constrained by parity and time-reversal symmetries. The T-even contributions are computed explicitly using the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) of Lorce and Pasquini (2011), while the T-odd contributions are obtained by extracting coefficient functions from the T-even part and multiplying by the appropriate basic multipoles.

## Key numbers

- 32 total leading-twist quark phase-space distributions (16 polarization configurations, each with T-even and T-odd parts)
- 8 points in impact-parameter space are shown, lying on a circle with radius |b_T| = 0.4 fm
- The k_T-distributions are normalized to the absolute maximal value over the circle: max_{|b_T|=0.4 fm} |rho_X(k_T|b_T)| = 1
- For the spin-independent contribution rho_UU, there are exactly 2 basic multipoles: B^{(0,0)}_{UU} = 1 (monopole) and B^{(1,1)}_{UU} = eta (k_T-hat dot b_T-hat) (dipole)
- For rho_UL (longitudinally polarized quark in unpolarized target), there are 2 basic multipoles: B^{(1,1)}_{UL} proportional to (b_T-hat x k_T-hat)_L and B^{(2,2)}_{UL} proportional to (b_T-hat x k_T-hat)_L (k_T-hat dot b_T-hat)
- For rho_UT (transversely polarized quark in unpolarized target), there are 4 basic multipoles: B^{(0,1)}_{UT}, B^{(2,1)}_{UT}, B^{(1,0)}_{UT}, and B^{(1,2)}_{UT}

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Lorce and Pasquini (2011) which analyzed only the four naive T-even distributions associated with longitudinal polarization, now providing a complete treatment of all 32 distributions including the T-odd sector. The multipole decomposition connects naturally to the parametrizations of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), as the T-even multipoles with m_b=0 reduce to TMD amplitudes and those with m_k=0 reduce to impact-parameter distributions related to GPDs. The T-odd multipoles represent new information not accessible via GPDs or TMDs at leading twist, and the analysis enables a transparent interpretation of all possible spin-spin and spin-orbit correlations between quarks and the nucleon in phase space, including the identification of which distributions encode canonical quark orbital angular momentum correlations.
