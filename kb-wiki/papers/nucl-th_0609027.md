# Half-lives of $α$-emitters approaching the N=Z line

> The half-lives of newly observed $α$-emitters $^{105}$Te and $^{109}$Xe [Seweryniak \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{73}, 061301(R) (2006); Liddick \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{97}, 082501 (2006)] are investigated by the density-dependent cluster model. The half-lives of $α$-emitters close to the N=Z line are also studied in a unified framework where the influence of the nuclear deformation is properly taken into account. Good agreement between model and data is obtained and some possible $α$-emitters are suggested for future experiments.

## Key claim

The density-dependent cluster model (DDCM), when including an isospin-dependent preformation factor, reproduces the experimental partial half-lives of light alpha-emitters near the N=Z line within a factor of 3 for most cases, with the largest deviation (a factor of 5) occurring for \({}^{114}\)Ba, possibly due to underestimated deformation parameters.

## Method

The authors applied the deformed version of the density-dependent cluster model (DDCM), which uses a microscopic double-folding potential with the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction and accounts for axially symmetric core deformation via multipole expansion. The penetration probability through the deformed Coulomb barrier was obtained by averaging over orientation angles, and the preformation factor was the only free parameter, chosen to fit experimental half-lives. For improved agreement, an isospin-dependent preformation factor \(P_{\alpha}=c_1+c_2(N-Z)\) was used instead of a constant one, with a linear dependence \(P_{\alpha}^{ee}=0.73-0.09\times(N-Z)\) for even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- \({}^{105}\)Te: experimental partial half-life \(T_{\alpha}(\text{Exp}) = 0.70\ \mu\text{s}\) (with uncertainty \(-0.17/+0.25\ \mu\text{s}\)); DDCM calculation (Cal1) gives \(0.54\ \mu\text{s}\), and the isospin-dependent calculation (Cal2) gives \(0.76\ \mu\text{s}\).
- \({}^{109}\)Xe: experimental partial half-life \(T_{\alpha}(\text{Exp}) = 5.0\times10^{-2}\ \text{s}\); DDCM (Cal1) gives \(2.9\times10^{-2}\ \text{s}\), and Cal2 gives \(4.1\times10^{-2}\ \text{s}\).
- \({}^{114}\)Ba: experimental partial half-life \(T_{\alpha}(\text{Exp}) = 4.8\times10^{1}\ \text{s}\); DDCM (Cal1) gives \(2.6\times10^{2}\ \text{s}\) (largest deviation, factor of 5).
- The root-mean-square deviation between experiment and theory for all available emitters reduces from 0.319 (constant preformation factor) to 0.242 (isospin-dependent preformation factor).
- The quadrupole deformation parameter for \({}^{114}\)Ba is \(\beta_2 = 0.169\) and hexadecapole deformation \(\beta_4 = 0.052\).
- Experimental partial half-lives across the isotopic chains range from \(7.0\times10^{-7}\) to \(2.0\times10^{7}\) seconds.

## Context

This work extends the application of the density-dependent cluster model, previously used for heavy and superheavy nuclei, to the light mass region just above \({}^{100}\)Sn, specifically the "light island" of alpha-emitters (Te, I, Xe, Cs, Ba isotopes) near the N=Z line. It builds on the experimental discovery of \({}^{105}\)Te by Seweryniak et al. (2006) and \({}^{109}\)Xe by Liddick et al. (2006), and follows the analysis of alpha-clustering effects by Delion and co-workers (2004, 2006), who noted the suppression of clusterization with increasing proton-neutron asymmetry. The predictions of five possible alpha-emitters with equal proton and neutron numbers (including \({}^{104}\)Te) provide targets for future experiments.
