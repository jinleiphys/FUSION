# A model for two-proton emission induced by electron scattering

> A model to study two-proton emission processes induced by electron scattering is developed. The process is induced by one-body electromagnetic operators acting together with short-range correlations, and by two-body $Δ$ currents. The model includes all the diagrams containing a single correlation function. A test of the sensitivity of the model to the various theoretical inputs is done. An investigation of the relevance of the $Δ$ currents is done by changing the final state angular momentum, excitation energy and momentum transfer. The sensitivity of the cross section to the details of the correlation function is studied by using realistic and schematic correlations. Results for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca nuclei are presented.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model for (e,e'2p) reactions in which two-proton emission is induced by one-body electromagnetic operators acting together with short-range correlations (SRC) and by two-body Δ currents, but the model truncates the cluster expansion to include only diagrams containing a single correlation function, and the authors explicitly state that a comparison with available data is outside the scope of the article.

## Method

The authors calculate (e,e'2p) cross sections using a correlated basis function approach where the nuclear ground state is a Slater determinant modified by a scalar correlation function f(r). The transition matrix elements are evaluated by expanding in powers of the correlation function and retaining only terms where the function h(r)=f²(r)-1 appears once (Meyer-like diagrams). The final state consists of two continuum protons and two holes, described using single-particle wave functions from a Woods-Saxon well for holes and an optical potential for particles, with the no-recoil approximation (NRA) applied to simplify kinematics.

- Incoming electron energy ε = 800 MeV and excitation energy ω = 100 MeV for the standard kinematics

- The Δ electromagnetic form factor uses Λ = 855 MeV
- The Δ mass is m_Δ = 1232 MeV and the nucleon mass is m_n = 938.9 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of SRC effects in electromagnetic reactions on finite nuclei, including (e,e'p) and (γ,p) reactions (Co' 2001, Mokeev 2001, Anguiano 2002), where SRC effects were difficult to disentangle due to large one-body contributions. The model is inspired by nuclear matter calculations of Fantoni (1987) and Fabrocini (1989), and the truncation scheme was validated by comparing nuclear matter charge responses with full expansion results (Amado 1998). The approach differs from the Pavia group's two-nucleon spectral function method (Giusti 1991, 1997) and the Gent group's effective two-body operator approach (Ryckebusch 1995-1997). The paper enables future comparison with experimental (e,e'2p) data from measurements at NIKHEF (Onderwater 1997, Starink 2000) and other facilities.
