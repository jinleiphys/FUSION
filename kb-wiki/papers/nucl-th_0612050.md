# Baryonic Effect on chi_cJ Suppression in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC Energies

> We predict that initially produced chi_cJ mesons at low transverse momentum in the central rapidity region are almost dissociated by nucleons and antinucleons in hadronic matter produced in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies sqrt {s_{NN}}= 130 and 200 GeV. In calculations the nucleon and antinucleon distributions in hadronic matter are results of evolution from their freeze-out distributions which well fit the experimental p_T spectra of proton and antiproton. Any measured chi_cJ mesons at low p_T are generated from deconfined matter and give an explicit proof of regeneration mechanism (recombination mechanism).

# Baryonic Effect on χ_cJ Suppression in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC Energies

## Key claim

The authors predict that initially produced χ_cJ mesons at low transverse momentum (below approximately 6 GeV/c) in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies are almost completely dissociated by collisions with nucleons and antinucleons in hadronic matter, such that any measured χ_cJ at low p_T must originate from deconfined matter via a regeneration (recombination) mechanism rather than from initial production.

## Method

The authors first fit freeze-out distributions of protons and antiprotons to PHENIX experimental data up to p_T = 4.5 GeV/c at √s_NN = 130 and 200 GeV using a Cooper-Frye formalism with Boltzmann distributions, obtaining two parameter sets for fugacity and transverse velocity. They then evolve these distributions backward in time using hydrodynamic equations and number conservation to obtain nucleon and antinucleon distributions in hadronic matter from freeze-out temperature T_fh = 0.13 GeV up to the phase transition temperature T_c = 0.175 GeV. Finally, they compute the survival probability of χ_cJ mesons by integrating the thermal average of baryon-χ_cJ dissociation cross sections over the hadronic matter evolution, assuming neutron and antineutron distributions identical to those of protons and antiprotons.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 130 GeV and 200 GeV for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fh = 0.13 GeV; phase transition temperature: T_c = 0.175 GeV
- Hadronization time τ_f = 4.0 fm/c at √s_NN = 130 GeV and 4.3 fm/c at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Freeze-out time τ_fh = 9.6 fm/c at √s_NN = 130 GeV and 10.3 fm/c at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Freeze-out transverse radius R(τ_fh) = 12.2 fm at √s_NN = 130 GeV and 12.6 fm at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Flow velocity of lateral face of hadronic matter: v_sf = 0.6c
- Survival probability at low p_T approaches zero; survival probability increases rapidly when χ_cJ transverse momentum exceeds 6 GeV/c
- χ_cJ contribution to prompt J/ψ production occupies about 30%

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic modeling of hadron spectra at RHIC (Xu, 2004) by updating freeze-out distributions to fit PHENIX data up to higher p_T, and applies these to the previously unstudied problem of χ_cJ dissociation by baryons in hadronic matter. The prediction that initial χ_cJ are almost completely suppressed at low p_T complements studies of J/ψ suppression and provides a specific signature for regeneration from deconfined matter. The authors note that future PHENIX upgrade measurements of χ_cJ could test this prediction, and that a finite survival probability at low p_T would indicate χ_cJ production from the partonic system in prethermal and thermal stages, as suggested by earlier work (Xu, 2004).
