# Charge Independence and Charge Symmetry

> Charge independence and charge symmetry are approximate symmetries of nature, violated by the perturbing effects of the mass difference between up and down quarks and by electromagnetic interactions. The observations of the symmetry breaking effects in nuclear and particle physics and the implications of those effects are reviewed.

## Key claim

Charge independence and charge symmetry are approximate symmetries of nature, violated by the perturbing effects of the mass difference between up and down quarks and by electromagnetic interactions; the review argues that the term "accidental symmetry" for isospin is a misnomer because dynamical symmetry breaking in QCD naturally explains why constituent quark masses are nearly equal despite the large ratio of current quark masses (m_d/m_u ≈ 1.76).

## Method

The paper reviews the quark-level origins of charge independence and charge symmetry breaking (CSB), focusing on the mass difference between up and down current quarks and electromagnetic interactions. It examines how these effects manifest in hadronic mass splittings, meson mixing (particularly ρ⁰-ω mixing), and baryon mixing (Λ-Σ⁰), and then connects these to nucleon-nucleon scattering observables via meson exchange models. The review evaluates experimental data on scattering lengths, phase shifts, and electron-positron annihilation to quantify symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses: m_u = 5.1 ± 1.5 MeV, m_d = 8.9 ± 2.6 MeV (evaluated at a momentum transfer scale of 1 GeV), with ratio m_d/m_u = 1.76 ± 0.13 (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1982)
- ρ⁰-ω mixing matrix element: ⟨ρ⁰|H|ω⟩ = -4520 ± 60 MeV², with strong contribution ⟨ρ⁰|H_str|ω⟩ = -5160 ± 150 MeV² after subtracting electromagnetic part 640 ± 140 MeV²
- Nuclear scattering lengths (after electromagnetic corrections): a_pp^N = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm, a_nn^N = -18.8 ± 0.3 fm, a_np^N = -23.75 ± 0.09 fm
- Charge independence breaking measure: Δa_CD = 5.7 ± 0.3 fm
- Charge symmetry breaking measure: Δa_CSB = a_pp^N - a_nn^N = 1.5 ± 0.5 fm
- Λ-Σ⁰ mixing parameter α ≈ -0.013 (from SU(3) symmetry) or -0.011 (from quark model), with mixing matrix element about 1 MeV
- ρ⁰-ω exchange contribution to Δa_CSB: 1.4 fm (rescaled from Coon and Barrett, 1987)
- Pion mass difference: (m_π± - m_π⁰)/m_π⁰ ≈ 0.04

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews (Refs. 3-7 in the paper) on charge independence and charge symmetry breaking, providing an updated synthesis of quark-level mechanisms and experimental observations from systems of baryon number 0 to 208. It builds on the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1982) on quark masses, the ρ⁰-ω mixing analysis of Coon and Barrett (1987), and the nucleon-nucleon scattering analyses of the Nijmegen group and Wiringa et al. (1994). The paper enables future work by identifying open questions, particularly the q² dependence of the ρ⁰-ω mixing matrix element (debated by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas, 1992; Krein, Thomas and Williams, 1993; and Coon and Scadron, 1994), and by suggesting improved experiments on Σ beta decays at the 1% level to observe baryon mixing effects.
