# Hyperon-Quark Mixed Phase in Compact Stars

> We investigate the properties of the hadron-quark mixed phase in compact stars using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model for quark matter. We find that the equation of state of the mixed phase is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction. The composition of the mixed phase, however, is very different from that of the Maxwell construction; in particular, hyperons are completely suppressed.

# Hyperon-Quark Mixed Phase in Compact Stars

## Key claim
The equation of state of the hadron-quark mixed phase is similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, but the composition differs dramatically: hyperons are completely suppressed in the mixed phase when full Gibbs conditions with geometrical structures are taken into account, contrary to the Maxwell construction where a relevant hyperon (Σ⁻) fraction appears.

## Method
The authors employed a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework for hadronic matter with microscopic NN and NY potentials fitted to scattering phase shifts, including nucleonic three-body forces. For quark matter they used the MIT bag model with massless u and d quarks, a massive s quark (m_s = 150 MeV), bag constant B = 100 MeV/fm³, and QCD fine structure constant α_s = 0. The mixed phase was treated using a Wigner-Seitz approximation with spherical (3D), cylindrical (2D), and slab (1D) geometries, a sharp hadron-quark boundary, surface tension parameter σ = 40 MeV/fm², and Coulomb potential solved via the Poisson equation.

## Key numbers
- Surface tension parameter: σ = 40 MeV/fm²
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- Bag constant: B = 100 MeV/fm³
- Wigner-Seitz cell radius for a 3D (quark droplet) cell at ρ_B = 0.4 fm⁻³: R_W = 26.7 fm, with droplet radius R = 17.3 fm
- Mixed phase density range: from approximately ρ_B = 0.326 fm⁻³ (quark droplet onset) to ρ_B = 0.666 fm⁻³ (bubble structure end)
- Pure hadron phase exists below ρ_B < 0.44 fm⁻³; pure quark phase above ρ_B > 0.44 fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of hadron-quark mixed phase with pasta structures by Maruyama and collaborators (2005), and contrasts with the bulk Gibbs calculation of Glendenning (1992) which predicted a broad mixed phase region. The suppression of hyperons in the mixed phase has implications for neutrino transport and cooling of pulsars, and addresses the tension between BHF predictions of low maximum neutron star masses (below ~1.5 M_⊙) and observational constraints.
