# Strong Isospin Breaking in Heavy-Meson Decay Constants: Employing Borelized QCD Sum Rules in Local-Duality Limit

> Applying the QCD sum-rule machinery in the so-called local-duality shape to heavy-light mesons reveals that, as a consequence of the non-zero mass gap between up and down quarks, the leptonic decay constants of the neutral and charged versions of the $D,$ $D^\ast,$ $B$ and $B^\ast$ mesons differ by approximately 1 MeV.

## Key claim

Applying Borelized QCD sum rules in the local-duality limit (τ→0 with exponent N=0) to heavy-light mesons shows that the leptonic decay constants of neutral and charged D, D*, B, and B* mesons differ by approximately 1 MeV as a consequence of the up-down quark mass gap, with the exact values depending on modeling of the effective threshold matched to lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors use Borel-transformed QCD sum rules for two-point correlation functions of interpolating currents for heavy-light pseudoscalar and vector mesons, taking the local-duality limit τ→0 with exponent N=0 to eliminate all power corrections and concentrate nonperturbative effects in the effective threshold s_eff. They compute the perturbative spectral density up to O(α_s m_q) and O(α_s^2 m_q^0), and model the light-quark mass dependence of the effective threshold by matching to lattice QCD results at three neighboring points within the range m_q ∈ [0, m_s]. The isospin-breaking difference f_{H_d} - f_{H_u} is extracted from the difference of the integrated spectral density function, with strange sea quark contributions canceling in the difference.

## Key numbers

- f_{D±} - f_{D0} = (0.96 ± 0.09) MeV
- f_{D*±} - f_{D*0} = (1.18 ± 0.35) MeV
- f_{B0} - f_{B±} = (1.01 ± 0.10) MeV
- f_{B*0} - f_{B*±} = (0.89 ± 0.30) MeV
- The up-down quark mass difference used is (m_d - m_u)(2 GeV) ≈ 2.5 MeV, taken from the Particle Data Group

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous local-duality QCD sum rule studies (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, several papers from 2015-2018) by focusing specifically on strong isospin breaking in heavy-light meson decay constants. The results are compared to earlier findings (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2016) shown as blue squares in the figures, and the method relies on perturbative spectral densities computed by earlier authors (referenced as d1-d4). The approach enables precise determination of isospin-breaking effects in leptonic decay constants, which are important inputs for extracting Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements from experimental measurements of D and B meson decays.
