# Few-Body Systems and the Pionless Effective Field Theory

> The pionless effective field theory (EFT) is the appropriate low-energy EFT for short-range interactions that display a large scattering length. It has been successfully applied in atomic, nuclear and particle physics. We give an overview over recent calculations employing the pionless effective field theory and lay emphasis on applications in the three- and four-body sector where the most exciting developments have occurred.

## Key claim

The pionless effective field theory (EFT) provides a model-independent framework for calculating low-energy observables in systems with short-range interactions and a large scattering length, but its predictive power in the three-body sector requires one additional three-body datum for renormalization due to a limit-cycle behavior of the three-body force.

## Method

The authors used the pionless EFT Lagrangian with an auxiliary dimer field to reformulate the three-body problem as an effective two-body problem, deriving an integral equation for particle-dimer scattering after S-wave projection. For the four-body system, they solved the corresponding integral equations and analyzed the dependence of four-body observables on two-body and three-body inputs, comparing results to experimental data from ultracold cesium atoms and to calculations for halo nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the four-boson system near the unitary limit (gamma ~ 0), the deeper tetramer binding energy E_{4,0} is approximately 5 times the trimer binding energy E_T, and the shallower tetramer binding energy E_{4,1} is approximately 1.01 E_T (Eq. 4).
- At exact unitarity (a -> infinity), E_{4,0} ≈ 4.57 E_T and E_{4,1} ≈ 1.01 E_T (Eq. 5).
- The scattering lengths at which tetramer binding energies vanish are a*_{4,0} ≈ 0.43 a_* and a*_{4,1} ≈ 0.92 a_*, where a_* is the three-body Efimov resonance scattering length (Eq. 6).
- Experimental measurements with ultracold 133Cs atoms at 50 nK gave ratios a*_{4,0}/a_* ≈ 0.47 and a*_{4,1}/a_* ≈ 0.84, with the triatomic Efimov resonance at -870 a_0 and four-body loss features at -730 a_0 and -410 a_0.

## Context

This review extends the work of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998, 1999) who first applied the pionless EFT to the three-body system of identical bosons, and of Hammer and Platter (2004, 2006) who showed that only one two-body and one three-body input are needed for model-independent four-body predictions, explaining the Tjon line correlation between triton and alpha-particle binding energies. The results enable future calculations of electroweak observables such as triton beta-decay and electroweak reactions in the four-body sector, and provide a framework for analyzing whether halo nuclei like 20C exhibit excited Efimov states.
