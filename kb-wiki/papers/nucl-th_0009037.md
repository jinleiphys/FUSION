# Medium corrections in the formation of light charged particles in heavy ion reactions

> Within a microscopic statistical description of heavy ion collisions, we investigate the effect of the medium on the formation of light clusters. The dominant medium effects are self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking that produce the Mott effect for composite particles and enhanced reaction rates in the collision integrals. Microscopic description of composites in the medium follows the Dyson equation approach combined with the cluster mean-field expansion. The resulting effective few-body problem is solved within a properly modified Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism. The results are incorporated in a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck simulation for heavy ion collisions. The number and spectra of light charged particles emerging from a heavy ion collision changes in a significant manner in effect of the medium modification of production and absorption processes.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that incorporating medium-dependent cross sections (via self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking) into a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck simulation significantly changes the number and spectra of light charged particles produced in heavy ion collisions, but the authors caution that a complete treatment of heavier clusters (A ≥ 4) is required before a decisive comparison with experiment can be made.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled set of semiclassical Boltzmann equations for nucleon, deuteron, triton, and helion Wigner functions within a BUU approach. The in-medium three-body transition operators are calculated using a modified Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism derived from the Dyson equation approach and cluster mean-field expansion, with rank-one Yamaguchi potentials for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The medium-dependent deuteron break-up cross sections and the Mott effect (parameterized by a cut-off value F_cut on the overlap of bound-state wave functions with the Fermi sphere) are incorporated into the collision integrals, and the results are applied to central collisions of 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/A.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron break-up cross section is strongly enhanced near threshold in the medium, leading to an enhancement of about 30% in the deuteron number in the energy range E_cm ≤ 50 MeV when comparing medium-dependent rates to isolated cross sections.
- The Mott effect parameterization uses a cut-off value F_cut = 0.15 for deuterons at temperature T = 10 MeV, with slight variation (F_cut ≈ 0.17 for T = 5 MeV).
- The Mott momenta are calculated for temperature T = 10 MeV over a wide density range, with no bound states possible below the respective curves in Fig. 1.
- The beam energy of the chosen reaction is 50 MeV/A for 129Xe+119Sn.

## Context
This work extends the BUU approach of Danielewicz (1991, 1992) by incorporating microscopic in-medium few-body calculations from Beyer et al. (1996, 1997) and Schadow et al. into the collision integrals, rather than using free-space experimental cross sections. The results are compared to experimental data from the INDRA Collaboration for 129Xe+119Sn at 50 MeV/A. The approach enables future inclusion of medium effects for three- and four-nucleon clusters (alpha particles), with first calculations for the alpha Mott effect given in Kuhrts et al.
