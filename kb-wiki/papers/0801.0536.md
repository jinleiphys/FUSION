# Chromodynamic Fluctuations in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Fluctuations of chromodynamic fields in the collisionless quark-gluon plasma are found as a solution of the initial value linearized problem. The plasma initial state is on average colorless, stationary and homogeneous. When the state is stable, the initial fluctuations decay exponentially and in the long-time limit a stationary spectrum of fluctuations is established. For the equilibrium plasma it reproduces the spectrum which is provided by the fluctuation-dissipation relation. Fluctuations in the unstable plasma, where the memory of initial fluctuations is not lost, are also discussed.

## Key claim

Chromodynamic field fluctuations in a collisionless quark-gluon plasma are obtained as solutions of the linearized initial-value problem; the method reproduces the fluctuation-dissipation relation for equilibrium plasmas, but for unstable plasmas the memory of initial fluctuations is not lost and the fluctuation spectrum depends on the initial state.

## Method

The author solves the linearized transport equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons together with the linearized Maxwell equations using a one-sided Fourier transformation in time and space, treating the plasma as initially colorless, stationary, and homogeneous. The initial fluctuations are identified with those of a system of free quarks and gluons, and the correlation functions of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields are expressed through these initial fluctuations and the chromodielectric tensor of the plasma.

## Key numbers

- The correlation function of the initial distribution function \(\delta N_0^a\) is given by \(\langle \delta N_0^a({\bf k}_1,{\bf p}_1) \delta N_0^b({\bf k}_2,{\bf p}_2) \rangle = \frac{1}{2} \delta^{ab} (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}({\bf p}_1 - {\bf p}_2) (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}({\bf k}_1 + {\bf k}_2) f({\bf p}_1)\), where \(f({\bf p}) \equiv n({\bf p}) + \bar n({\bf p}) + 2N_c n_g({\bf p})\).
- The initial chromoelectric field correlation is \(\langle E_{a0}^i({\bf k}_1) E_{b0}^j({\bf k}_2) \rangle = -\frac{g^2}{2} \delta^{ab} (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}({\bf k}_1 + {\bf k}_2) \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} f({\bf p}) \frac{(({\bf k}_1 \cdot {\bf v})v^i - k_1^i)(({\bf k}_2 \cdot {\bf v})v^j - k_2^j)}{(({\bf k}_1 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_1^2)(({\bf k}_2 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_2^2)}\).
- The initial chromomagnetic field correlation is \(\langle B_{a0}^i({\bf k}_1) B_{b0}^j({\bf k}_2) \rangle = -\frac{g^2}{2} \delta^{ab} (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}({\bf k}_1 + {\bf k}_2) \epsilon^{ikl} \epsilon^{jmn} k_1^k k_2^m \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} f({\bf p}) \frac{v^l v^n}{(({\bf k}_1 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_1^2)(({\bf k}_2 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_2^2)}\).
- The initial cross-correlation is \(\langle E_{a0}^i({\bf k}_1) B_{b0}^j({\bf k}_2) \rangle = -\frac{g^2}{2} \delta^{ab} (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}({\bf k}_1 + {\bf k}_2) \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} f({\bf p}) \frac{(({\bf k}_1 \cdot {\bf v})v^i - k_1^i) \epsilon^{jkl} k_2^k v^l}{(({\bf k}_1 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_1^2)(({\bf k}_2 \cdot {\bf v})^2 - {\bf k}_2^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends the Klimontovich method, previously applied to electromagnetic fluctuations in electron-ion plasmas by Mrowczynski (2007), to the non-Abelian case of chromodynamic fluctuations in quark-gluon plasma. It provides an analytical framework that complements numerical simulations of unstable QGP performed by groups such as Arnold (2005), Rebhan (2005), Dumitru (2006), Romatschke (2006), Bodeker (2007), and Berges (2007). The results enable analytical computation of fluctuation spectra in both stable and unstable QGP, which are needed to determine transport coefficients that are strongly modified by chromomagnetic field fluctuations as discussed by Asakawa (2006).
