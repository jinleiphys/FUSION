# Particle-unstable light nuclei with a Sturmian approach that preserves the Pauli principle

> Sturmian theory for nucleon-nucleus scattering is discussed in the presence of all the phenomenological ingredients necessary for the description of weakly-bound (or particle-unstable) light nuclear systems. Currently, we use a macroscopic potential model of collective nature. The analysis shows that the couplings to low-energy collective-core excitations are fundamental but they are physically meaningful only if the constraints introduced by the Pauli principle are taken into account. The formalism leads one to discuss a new concept, Pauli hindrance, which appears to be important to understand the structure of weakly-bound and unbound systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a Sturmian-based coupled-channel (CC) approach with a macroscopic collective potential can describe weakly-bound and particle-unstable light nuclei, but only if the Pauli principle is enforced via an Orthogonalizing Pseudo-Potential (OPP); the authors introduce the new concept of "Pauli hindrance" for states that are neither fully forbidden nor fully allowed, which they claim is important for understanding such systems.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation using Sturmian (Weinberg) states, which provide a discrete eigenvalue spectrum for a given energy and allow algebraic determination of the S-matrix, bound states, and resonances. They couple a nucleon to low-lying collective core excitations (quadrupole, octupole) using a macroscopic potential, and enforce the Pauli principle by adding a highly non-local Orthogonalizing Pseudo-Potential (OPP) term that eliminates spurious deeply-bound states. For weakly-bound or unbound systems, they introduce intermediate OPP strength values (a few MeV) to model "Pauli-hindered" shells rather than fully blocking or allowing them.

## Key numbers

- For n-12C scattering, stability of the 5/2- resonance at 0.1 MeV is reached using lambda = 1000 MeV in the OPP term.
- For 15F, three narrow negative-parity resonances (1/2-, 5/2-, 3/2-) are predicted in the range 5-8 MeV.
- For 7Li, the ground state (3/2-) is at -9.975 MeV (experiment) and -9.975 MeV (theory); the 1/2- state is at -9.497 MeV (experiment) and -9.497 MeV (theory).
- For 7He, the ground state (3/2-) is at 0.445 MeV with width 0.15 MeV (experiment) and 0.43 MeV with width 0.1 MeV (theory).
- For 7B, the ground state (3/2-) is at 2.21 MeV with width 1.4 MeV (experiment) and 2.10 MeV with width 0.19 MeV (theory).
- For 7Li, the 7/2- state at -5.323 MeV has width 0.069 MeV (experiment); theory gives -5.323 MeV.
- For 7Be, the ground state (3/2-) is at -10.676 MeV (experiment) and -11.046 MeV (theory).

## Context

This work extends the Sturmian approach to coupled-channel problems (previously applied by the authors to n-12C scattering) to unstable nuclei like 15F and the mass-7 isobars (7He, 7Li, 7Be, 7B). It contrasts with cluster models (e.g., 7Li as 3H+alpha dicluster) and microscopic models (no-core shell model, Green Function Monte Carlo calculations) by using a single macroscopic collective potential with Pauli blocking via OPP. The concept of Pauli hindrance builds on earlier observations in Resonating Group Method (RGM) approaches (Langanke) and enables predictions for new resonances in 15F and mass-7 systems that could stimulate experimental searches.
