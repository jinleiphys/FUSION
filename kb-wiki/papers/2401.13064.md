# Can we measure Double DVCS at JLab and the EIC?

> Double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS) is a very precise tool for the nucleon tomography. Its measurement requires high luminosity electron beams and precise dedicated detectors, since its amplitude is quite small in the interesting kinematical domain where collinear QCD factorization allows the extraction of quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs). We analyze the prospects for its study in the JLab energy domain as well as in higher energy electron-ion colliders. Our results are very encouraging for various observables both with an unpolarized and polarized lepton beam. Using various realistic models for GPDs, we demonstrate that DDVCS measurements are indeed very sensitive to their behaviour. Implementing our lowest order cross-section formulae in the EpIC Monte Carlo generator, we estimate the expected number of interesting events.

## Key claim

Double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS) can be measured at JLab and the EIC, with asymmetries at leading order of order 15-20% for JLab and 3-7% for EIC, provided that high-luminosity beams and dedicated detectors are available; the authors qualify that their results do not include any acceptances or detector response.

## Method

The authors implemented lowest-order cross-section formulae for DDVCS, including the Bethe-Heitler (BH) background, using the Kleiss-Stirling helicity amplitude technique and the PARTONS software framework. They computed unpolarized differential cross-sections and lepton beam helicity asymmetries for JLab12, JLab20+, and two EIC beam configurations (5x41 and 10x100 GeV), using the GK, VGG, and MMS GPD models. The cross-section formulae were then implemented in the EpIC Monte Carlo generator to estimate event yields and phase-space coverage.

## Key numbers

- Total cross-section for electroproduction of a muon pair at JLab12 (E_e = 10.6 GeV, E_p = M): 0.14 pb, requiring 70 fb^-1 integrated luminosity for 10000 events
- Total cross-section at JLab20+ (E_e = 22 GeV, E_p = M): 0.46 pb, requiring 22 fb^-1 for 10000 events
- Total cross-section at EIC (E_e = 5 GeV, E_p = 41 GeV): 3.9 pb, requiring 2.6 fb^-1 for 10000 events
- Total cross-section at EIC (E_e = 10 GeV, E_p = 100 GeV): 4.7 pb, requiring 2.1 fb^-1 for 10000 events
- Fraction of events retained after y_min cut at EIC 5x41: 0.73 (y_min = 0.05); at EIC 10x100: 0.32 (y_min = 0.05)
- Asymmetries A_LU at leading order: 15-20% for JLab, 3-7% for EIC
- Kinematic point used for predictions: Q^2 = 0.6 GeV^2, Q'^2 = 2.5 GeV^2, t = -0.2 GeV^2 for JLab, t = -0.1 GeV^2 for EIC

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for DDVCS originally developed by Belitsky, Mueller, and others (Belitsky 2002, Guidal 2003, Belitsky 2003) by providing realistic feasibility estimates for current and future experimental facilities. It enables experimental planning for DDVCS measurements at JLab (Chen 2014, Camsonne 2017, Zhao 2021) and the EIC (Abdul Khalek 2021, Anderle 2021), and the authors note that future work should include NLO corrections (Pire 2011) and kinematic higher-twist corrections (Braun 2014, Braun 2022) based on the conformal operator-product expansion (Braun 2020).
