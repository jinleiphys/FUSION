# States of $ρD^* \bar D^*$ with $J=3$ within the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations

> We study the interaction of the a $ρ$ and $D^*$, $\bar D^*$ with spins aligned using the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations. We select a cluster of $D^* \bar D^*$, which is found to be bound in $I=0$ and can be associated to the X(3915), and let the $ρ$ meson orbit around the $D^*$ and $\bar D^*$. In this case we find an $I=1$ state with mass around 4340 MeV and narrow width of about 50 MeV. We also investigate the case with a cluster of $ρD^*$ and let the $\bar D^*$ orbit around the system of the two states. The $ρD^*$ cluster is also found to bind and leads to the $D^*_2(2460)$ state. The addition of the extra $\bar D^*$ produces further binding and we find, with admitted uncertainties, a state of $I=0$ around 4000 MeV, and a less bound narrow state with $I=1$ around 4200 MeV.

# States of ρD*D̅* with J=3 within the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev Equations

## Key claim

The paper claims the existence of a narrow I=1 three-body state with mass around 4340 MeV and width about 50 MeV from ρ meson orbiting a D*D̅* cluster (associated with X(3915)), but this result is conditional on the Fixed Center Approximation being reliable when the orbiting particle (ρ) is lighter than the cluster constituents. The I=0 and I=1 states found from a D̅* orbiting a ρD* cluster (associated with D*₂(2460)) are presented as more uncertain and only indicative, because the FCA is less reliable when the external particle is heavier than the cluster constituents.

## Method

The authors apply the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations for the ρD*D̅* system with all spins aligned (J=3). They consider two configurations: (1) a ρ meson scattering from a D*D̅* cluster (the X(3915) state, I=0, J=2) and (2) a D̅* meson scattering from a ρD* cluster (the D*₂(2460) state, I=1/2, J=2). The two-body ρD*, ρD̅*, and D*D̅* scattering amplitudes are taken from prior coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter calculations using the hidden gauge formalism, and the three-body T-matrix is obtained by solving the FCA coupled equations with a form factor derived from the cluster wave function.

## Key numbers

- I=1 state from ρ + (D*D̅*) cluster: mass around 4340 MeV, width about 50 MeV, about 360 MeV below the X(3915)-ρ threshold
- I=0 state from D̅* + (ρD*) cluster: mass around 4000 MeV, width about 250 MeV, about 470 MeV below the D*₂(2460)-D̅* threshold
- I=1 state from D̅* + (ρD*) cluster: mass around 4195 MeV, width about 60 MeV, about 270 MeV below the D*₂(2460)-D̅* threshold
- Cutoff used for form factor regularization: k_max = 1200 MeV, same as used to generate D*₂(2460) in prior work
- Subtraction constants for two-body loops: α = -1.74 (μ = 1500 MeV) for ρD* to reproduce D*₂(2460); α = -2.07 (μ = 1000 MeV) for channels with heavy mesons and α = -1.65 for light meson channels in D*D̅* to reproduce X(3915)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of multi-vector-meson bound states with aligned spins, which were previously investigated for multi-ρ states (Roca 2010), multi-K* states (Yamagata-Sekihara 2010), and multi-D* states (Xiao 2012). It builds directly on the two-body results for D*D̅* interaction (which produces X(3915) as a molecule in I=0, J=2) from the work of Geng (2008) and Molina (2008), and on the ρD* interaction (which produces D*₂(2460) in I=1/2, J=2) from the work of the same group. The results are intended to motivate more accurate three-body calculations using full Faddeev equations with chiral dynamics, as previously applied to two-meson-one-baryon and three-meson systems by Martinez Torres (2007-2010), and to stimulate experimental searches for high-spin states in the hidden charm sector.
