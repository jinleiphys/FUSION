# Hall Droplet Sheets in Holographic QCD

> In single-flavor QCD, the low energy description of baryons as Skyrmions is not available. In this case, it has been proposed by Komargodski that baryons can be viewed as kinds of charged quantum Hall droplets, or "sheets". In this paper we propose a string theory description of the sheets in single-flavor holographic QCD, focusing on the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. The sheets have a "hard" gluonic core, described by D6-branes, and a "soft" mesonic shell, dual to non-trivial D8-brane gauge field configurations. We first provide the description of an infinitely extended sheet with massless or moderately massive quarks. Then, we construct a semi-infinite sheet ending on a one-dimensional boundary, a "vortex string". The holographic description allows for the precise calculation of sheet observables. In particular, we compute the tension and thickness of the sheet and the vortex string, and provide their four dimensional effective actions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a holographic string theory description of baryons as quantum Hall droplet sheets in single-flavor QCD, using the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, but explicitly states that the construction only provides a semi-infinite sheet with a straight boundary (a "vortex string") rather than the finite-size disk-shaped baryon, leaving the full baryon solution for future work.

## Method

The authors introduce a D6-brane wrapped on the four-sphere of the holographic background, extended along (2+1) Minkowski directions, to realize the hard gluonic core of the sheet, while the soft mesonic shell arises from non-trivial configurations of the Abelian gauge field on the D8-brane, which corresponds to the η' meson. They solve the equations of motion derived from the five-dimensional Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory on the curved D8-brane background, first for an infinitely extended sheet with massless and massive quarks, then for a semi-infinite sheet ending on a straight boundary, using a combination of analytical series expansions and numerical methods.

## Key numbers

* Tension of the hard core: T_hard = (1/(π^3 3^6)) λ^2 N M_KK^3, scaling as λ^2 N
* Thickness of the hard core: δ_hard ~ 1/(λ^{1/2} M_KK)
* Tension of the soft shell (massless quarks): T_soft = (1/(3^{9/2} 2^3 π^3)) λ^2 N^{1/2} M_KK^3, scaling as λ^2 N^{1/2}
* Thickness of the soft shell (massless quarks): δ_soft ~ 1/m_WV ~ (√N)/(λ M_KK), where m_WV = λ^2/(27π^2 N)
* Witten-Veneziano mass: m_WV^2 = χ_g π/(2κ) = λ^2/(27π^2 N)
* Topological susceptibility: χ_g = λ^3/(4(3π)^6) in units of M_KK
* The integrated flux condition from the η' profile: ∫ F_{x3 z} dx_3 dz = -2√2 π, providing a unit of anti-D6-brane charge

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Komargodski (2018) that one-flavored baryons can be viewed as charged quantum Hall droplets or sheets, providing a concrete holographic realization in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Witten 1998, Sakai and Sugimoto 2004). The construction builds on earlier studies of domain walls in holographic QCD at θ=π by Argurio et al. (2018) and on axion monodromy by Dubovsky et al. (2011). The paper enables future calculation of the full finite-size disk-shaped baryon solution, which would require imposing the Abelian instanton number condition ∫ (F∧F) = 48π^2 to stabilize the sheet size, and could allow study of the chiral edge modes on the boundary of the Hall droplet.
