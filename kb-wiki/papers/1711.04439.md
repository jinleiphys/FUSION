# 2$α$+$t$ cluster structure in $^{11}$B

> The 2$α+t$ cluster structure in $^{11}$B is investigated by the microscopic generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Brink cluster wave functions. With a proper choice of the parameters of the effective interaction, the calculated energy spectrum shows reasonable agreement with the observed low-lying spectra of both parities. On the basis of the calculated radii, monopole and $B(E2)$ transition strengths, several developed cluster states of $^{11}$B are suggested. For the negative-parity states, in addition to the well-known $3/2_3^-$ cluster state, the $1/2_2^-$ and $5/2_3^-$ states are also proposed as the well-developed cluster states. For the positive-parity states, it is found that many states around the 2$α+t$ threshold show the feature of developed clusters. In particular, the $1/2_2^+$ state is found to have a linear-chain-like structure, which is consistent with the previous antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculation, but contradicts to the orthogonality condition model calculation. It is also found that many of these positive-parity cluster candidates have the non-negligible isoscalar dipole transition strengths, which require the experimental confirmation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several positive-parity states around the 2α+t threshold show developed cluster structure, and specifically that the 1/2₂⁺ state has a linear-chain-like structure, but this conclusion is hedged by noting that the result is consistent with previous antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations while contradicting orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculations, and that many of these positive-parity cluster candidates require experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation using Brink cluster wave functions for the 2α+t system in ¹¹B, with the Volkov No.2 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction combined with the spin-orbit part of the G3RS interaction. They generated 186 Brink wave functions as basis states by varying the inter-cluster distances from 1 to 6 fm in 1 fm steps, then applied parity and angular momentum projections and solved the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the GCM wave functions. From these wave functions, they calculated root-mean-square radii, isoscalar monopole (ISM) transition strengths, isoscalar dipole (ISD) transition strengths, and electric quadrupole (E2) transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ground state (3/2₁⁻) matter radius is 2.38 fm, compared to the experimental value of 2.09 ± 0.12 fm
- The 3/2₃⁻ state has a matter radius of 2.99 fm, comparable to that of the Hoyle state
- The ISM transition strength from the ground state to the 3/2₃⁻ state is 147 fm⁴, consistent with the experimental value of 96 ± 16 fm⁴
- The ISM transition strength from the ground state to the 3/2₂⁻ state is 8.42 fm⁴, consistent with the experimental upper limit of less than 9 fm⁴
- The ISM transition strength between the 1/2₁⁺ and 1/2₂⁺ states is 210 fm⁴, much stronger than the ISM strength between the ground and 3/2₃⁻ states
- The optimum inter-cluster distance Rαα for the ground state is 2.4 fm with |Rt| = 2.2 fm and maximum squared overlap of 0.93
- The optimum inter-cluster distance Rαα for the 3/2₃⁻ state is 4.2 fm with |Rt| = 4.0 fm and maximum squared overlap of 0.57
- The calculated electric quadrupole moment of the ground state is 4.07 e²fm², agreeing with the observed value of 4.07(3) e²fm²
- The B(E2) strength for 1/2₁⁻ → 3/2₁⁻ is 4.80 e²fm⁴ (experiment: 5.2 ± 0.8 e²fm⁴)
- The B(E2) strength for 3/2₁⁻ → 5/2₁⁻ is 14.3 e²fm⁴ (experiment: 13.3 ± 4.8 e²fm⁴)

## Context

This work extends the study of 2α+t cluster structure in ¹¹B, following earlier orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculations by Nishioka (1979) and Yamada (2010), and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations by Suhara (2012) and Kanada-Enyo (2015). The paper addresses the contradicting claims about which state is the Hoyle analog in ¹¹B: the AMD calculations suggested the 3/2₃⁻ state, while the OCM calculations proposed the 1/2₂⁺ state with a very large radius of 5.93 fm. The present GCM calculation supports the AMD conclusion that the 3/2₃⁻ state is a developed cluster state, but finds the 1/2₂⁺ state has a radius of only 2.88 fm, contradicting the OCM result. The work enables future experimental searches for the predicted positive-parity cluster states using isoscalar dipole transition measurements.
