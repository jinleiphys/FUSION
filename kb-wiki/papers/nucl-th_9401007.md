# Resistance to Collapse: The Tensor Interaction

> We consider negative-parity excitations in $^4\mbox{He}$, $J^π$=$0^-$ and $1^-$ with isospin $T$=$0$ and $T$=$1$. We show tha t, in contrast to one-particle one-hole results, in a shell model diagonalization none of the above collapses to zero energy as the strength of the tensor interaction is increased.

# Resistance to Collapse: The Tensor Interaction

## Key claim

In a full shell model diagonalization of negative-parity excitations in 4He, none of the 0- or 1- states (with isospin T=0 or T=1) collapse to zero energy as the tensor interaction strength is increased, contrary to what one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) calculations predict. The excitation energies initially decrease with increasing tensor strength but then increase again beyond a certain point (e.g., y approximately 3 for the 0- T=0 state).

## Method

The authors performed shell model matrix diagonalizations using the OXBASH code for 4He, allowing four nucleons to occupy the first three major shells (0s, 0p, and 1s-0d). They used a schematic interaction V_sche = V_c + x V_so + y V_t, where the tensor strength parameter y was varied while keeping x=1 (spin-orbit strength at its physical value). The excitation energies of the lowest 0- and 1- states with T=0 and T=1 were computed as functions of y, and the ground state binding energy and shell occupancies were tracked.

## Key numbers

- For the 0- T=0 state in the 1p-1h approximation, the particle-hole matrix element is V_ph(T=0) = 2.575 - 3.820y MeV, giving a slope (coefficient of y) of -3.820 MeV per unit y.
- For the 0- T=1 state in the 1p-1h approximation, the particle-hole matrix element is V_ph(T=1) = 3.445 - 1.270y MeV, with a slope of -1.270 MeV per unit y, exactly one-third of the T=0 slope.
- The change in ground state binding energy relative to y=0 is: 0.00 MeV at y=0, 1.83 MeV at y=1, 7.01 MeV at y=2, 14.68 MeV at y=3, 23.99 MeV at y=4, and 34.42 MeV at y=5.
- The 0s shell occupancy decreases from 3.53 at y=0 to 2.76 at y=5, while the 0p_1/2 occupancy increases from 0.08 at y=0 to 0.95 at y=5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collapse in nuclear systems, particularly those related to pion condensation, which typically used RPA or TDA calculations with particle-hole states having quantum numbers S=1, T=1 and negative parity. The authors contrast their full shell model results with the simpler 1p-1h approach used in prior work by Zheng and Zamick (1993) on schematic interactions. The finding that higher-order corrections (renormalization of single-particle energies and particle-hole interactions via phonon exchange) prevent collapse suggests that more complete treatments beyond 1p-1h TDA or RPA are necessary for reliable predictions of collective behavior in light nuclei.
