# Neutron-Mirror Neutron oscillations in Matter

> The possibility that a neutron can be transformed to a hidden sector particle remains intriguingly open. Proposed theoretical models conjecture that the hidden sector can be represented by a mirror sector, and the neutron n can oscillate into its sterile mirror twin n', exactly or nearly degenerate in mass with n. Oscillations n - n' can take place in vacuum and in the environment of the regular matter and the magnetic field where only neutron will be subject of interaction with the environment. We describe the propagation of the oscillating n - n' system as a particle of the cold neutron beam passing through the dense absorbing materials in connection with the possible regeneration type of experiments where the effect of n -> n' -> n transformation can be observed.

## Key claim
Neutron-mirror neutron (n-n') oscillations, if they exist, could be detected via a regeneration-type experiment where a cold neutron beam passes through dense absorbing materials; the observable signal is a neutron that disappears into a mirror neutron and later reappears as a regular neutron after traversing an absorber. The paper describes the propagation formalism for this system, but does not claim to have observed the effect.

## Method
The authors model the n-n' system as a two-state quantum system propagating through matter, where the neutron component experiences absorption and interactions with the environment (including magnetic fields) while the mirror neutron component is sterile and does not interact. They derive the evolution equations for a cold neutron beam passing through dense absorbing materials, specifically to describe the regeneration scenario where an initial neutron converts to n', traverses an absorber invisibly, and then converts back to a detectable neutron.

- No specific numerical results (oscillation probabilities, cross sections, or limits) are reported in the provided text; the paper is a theoretical description of the formalism.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for n-n' oscillations originally proposed in mirror-matter models, where the hidden sector is a mirror copy of ordinary matter. It connects to prior experimental searches for neutron disappearance and regeneration, such as those conducted at neutron sources, by providing a quantitative description of how such oscillations would behave in the presence of absorbing materials. The formalism enables future regeneration-type experiments to be designed with optimized absorber thicknesses and magnetic field configurations to search for n-n' mixing.
