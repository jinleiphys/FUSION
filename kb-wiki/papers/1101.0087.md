# Update on the status of Hadronic Squeezed Correlations at RHIC Energies

> In high energy heavy ion collisions a hot and dense medium is formed, where the hadronic masses may be shifted from their asymptotic values. If this mass modification occurs, squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs are predicted to appear, both in the femionic (fBBC) and in the bosonic (bBBC) sectors. Although they have unlimited intensity even for finite-size expanding systems, these hadronic squeezed correlations are very sensitive to their time emission distribution. Here we discuss results in case this time emission is parameterized by a Levy-type distribution, showing that it reduces the signal even more dramatically than a Lorentzian distribution, which already reduces the intensity of the effect by orders of magnitude, as compared to the sudden emission. However, we show that the signal could still survive if the duration of the process is short, and if the effect is searched for lighter mesons, such as kaons. We compare some of our results to recent PHENIX preliminary data on squeezed correlations of K^+K^- pairs.

## Key claim

Hadronic squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs, predicted to arise from in-medium mass shifts in high-energy heavy ion collisions, are extremely sensitive to the time emission distribution; a Lévy-type parameterization reduces the signal even more dramatically than a Lorentzian distribution, but the signal could still survive if the emission duration is short and the effect is searched for lighter mesons such as kaons.

## Method

The authors model a finite, expanding system with non-relativistic radial flow and a constant, homogeneous mass shift linearly related to the asymptotic mass. They compute the squeezed correlation function for charged kaon pairs using a Bogolyubov-Valatin transformation, incorporating a time emission factor described by a symmetric α-stable Lévy distribution, and compare the results to preliminary PHENIX data on squeezed K⁺K⁻ correlations.

## Key numbers

- For instantaneous emission (δ-function at freeze-out), the squeezed correlation for K⁺K⁻ pairs reaches values as high as C(k,-k) ~ 330 at maximal mass shift m* = 350 MeV, for expanding systems with radial flow velocity <u> = 0.5.
- For a Lorentzian time emission with Δt = 2 fm/c, the maximum intensity of the squeezed correlation is roughly a factor of 20 smaller than in the instantaneous emission case.
- The Lévy distribution index used is α ~ 1 for 0.2 < m_T < 0.3 GeV, and α ~ 1.35 for 0.3 < p_T < 0.45 GeV/c, with Δt = 1 fm/c.
- A simulation with a small system radius R = 1.5 fm and modified mass m* = 460 MeV describes the trend of PHENIX data reasonably well.
- Two-volume scenarios with R = 7.0 fm, R_s = 5 fm and R = 3.2 fm, R_s = 2 fm (with m* = 350 MeV) describe the data qualitatively well, showing parameter ambiguity.

## Context

This work extends the squeezed correlation formalism originally proposed by Asakawa et al. (1999) and Panda et al. (2001) for static infinite media, and later developed for finite expanding systems by Panda et al. (2006) and Padula et al. (2010). The authors show that the Lévy-type time emission distribution, used by the PHENIX Collaboration to fit Bose-Einstein correlations, suppresses the squeezed correlation signal even more than the previously studied Lorentzian distribution. The comparison with preliminary PHENIX data on squeezed hadronic correlations does not allow an unambiguous conclusion about in-medium mass modification at RHIC energies, but does not discard it either; higher statistics data and analysis of the correlation function in the (K₁₂, q₁₂) plane could resolve the ambiguity.
