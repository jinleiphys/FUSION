# New theoretical results in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions

> We study dilepton, proton-antiproton and diphoton production in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 5.02 and 5.5 TeV. The nuclear calculations are based on equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. For correct description of the $γγ\to p\bar{p} $ Belle data we include the proton-exchange, the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2(1950)$ s-channel exchanges, as well as the handbag mechanism. For four muon production, we take into account electromagnetic (two-photon) double-scattering production and direct $γγ$ production of four muons in one scattering. The cross sections for elementary $γγ\to γγ$ subprocess are calculated including three different mechanisms: box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops, a VDM-Regge contribution with virtual intermediate hadronic excitations of the photons and the two-gluon exchange contribution. We find that the cross section for elastic $γγ$ scattering could be measured in the lead-lead collisions for the diphoton invariant mass up to $W_{γγ} \approx$ 15 - 20 GeV. Our Standard Model predictions are compared with a recent ATLAS experimental result. We discuss results for PbPb$\to$PbPb$μ^+μ^-μ^+μ^-$, PbPb$\to$PbPb$e^+e^-e^+e^-$, PbPb$\to$PbPb$p\bar{p}$ and PbPb$\to$PbPb$γγ$ reactions at LHC energy.

## Key claim

The authors predict that elastic photon-photon scattering in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at LHC energies can be measured for diphoton invariant masses up to approximately 15–20 GeV, and their Standard Model predictions for the fiducial cross section are compatible with the ATLAS experimental result of 70 ± 20 (stat.) ± 17 (syst.) nb, though the predicted value of 49 ± 10 nb lies slightly below the central measured value.

## Method

The nuclear cross sections are calculated using the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space, with realistic nuclear charge form factors and an absorption factor to ensure only ultraperipheral collisions are considered. For each final state, the elementary two-photon subprocess cross sections are computed separately: for proton-antiproton production, the authors include proton-exchange, s-channel exchanges of the f2(1270) and f2(1950) resonances, and the handbag mechanism; for four-muon production, both electromagnetic double-scattering and direct single-scattering mechanisms are considered; for diphoton production, the calculation includes box diagrams with leptons and quarks, a VDM-Regge contribution, and a two-gluon exchange mechanism.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb→PbPb e+e- at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with ATLAS detector angular coverage and a transverse momentum cut pt > 0.5 GeV on each electron/positron, the predicted number of events is 235 for an integrated luminosity of 1 nb-1.
- For PbPb→PbPb p pbar, the integrated cross section for the full phase space is a factor of 5 larger than that corresponding to the Belle angular coverage.
- For PbPb→PbPb p pbar, the predicted cross sections are 35 μb for ALICE cuts (|y| < 0.9, pt > 1 GeV) and 155 μb for ATLAS or CMS cuts (|y| < 2.5, pt > 0.5 GeV).
- For PbPb→PbPb γγ at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the ATLAS measured fiducial cross section is 70 ± 20 (stat.) ± 17 (syst.) nb, compared to the authors' predicted value of 49 ± 10 nb.
- The diphoton invariant mass range where elastic γγ scattering could be measured is up to Wγγ ≈ 15–20 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical studies of ultraperipheral collisions (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2016; Klusek-Gawenda et al., 2016) by providing comprehensive predictions for multiple final states and comparing with recent ATLAS data on light-by-light scattering (ATLAS Collaboration, 2017). The results enable future experimental searches for elastic photon-photon scattering and provide benchmarks for testing the Standard Model in the two-photon sector at LHC energies, particularly in the region of intermediate transverse momenta (1 GeV < pt < 2–5 GeV) where the two-gluon exchange contribution may be enhanced relative to the box diagram contribution.
