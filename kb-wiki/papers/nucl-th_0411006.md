# Angular distribution and azimuthal asymmetry for pentaquark production in proton-proton collisions

> Angular distributions for production of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark are calculated for the collisions of polarized protons with polarized target protons. We compare calculations based on different assumptions concerning spin and parity ($J=1/2^\pm,3/2^\pm$) of the $Θ^+$ state. For a wide class of interactions the spin correlation parameters describing the asymmetric angular distributions are calculated up to 250 MeV above production threshold. The deviations from the near threshold behavior are investigated.

## Key claim

The azimuthal asymmetry and spin correlation parameters for $\Theta^+$ pentaquark production in polarized proton-proton collisions can distinguish between spin-parity assignments ($J=1/2^\pm,3/2^\pm$) up to about 50 MeV above threshold, but the characteristic threshold signals may break down at higher energies if destructive interference from $K^*$ exchange is present.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section and spin correlation parameters $A_{ij}$ for the reaction $pp \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+$ using a combination of kaon ($K$) and vector kaon ($K^*$) exchange Lagrangians in a tree-level Born approximation. They consider four possible spin-parity assignments for the $\Theta^+$ ($1/2^+$, $1/2^-$, $3/2^+$, $3/2^-$) and constrain the coupling constants and cut-off parameters to reproduce a total cross section of about 0.4 $\mu$b at an excess energy of 0.13 GeV, as measured by the COSY experiment. The energy dependence of the correlation parameters is computed from threshold up to 250 MeV above threshold.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_{KN\Theta} = 3.27$ for $J^\pi = 1/2^+$ assuming $\Gamma_\Theta = 10$ MeV and $m_\Theta = 1.53$ GeV (from Eq. 4).
- For $\Gamma_\Theta = 1$ MeV, the coupling constant for $1/2^+$ is $g = 1.03$ with cut-off $\Lambda = 1.03$ GeV; for $1/2^-$, $g = 0.14$ with $\Lambda = 1.55$ GeV; for $3/2^+$, $f = 0.46$ with $\Lambda = 0.90$ GeV; for $3/2^-$, $f = 3.44$ with $\Lambda = 0.69$ GeV (Table 1).
- For $\Gamma_\Theta = 10$ MeV, the coupling constants are: $1/2^+$: $g = 3.27$, $\Lambda = 0.76$ GeV; $1/2^-$: $g = 0.44$, $\Lambda = 0.94$ GeV; $3/2^+$: $f = 1.47$, $\Lambda = 0.70$ GeV; $3/2^-$: $f = 10.9$, $\Lambda = 0.60$ GeV (Table 1).
- The $K^*$ exchange coupling constants (for $\Gamma_\Theta = 10$ MeV) range from $g^* = 0.3$ to $-0.6$ for $1/2^+$, $g^* = 0.6$ to $-0.6$ for $1/2^-$, $f^* = 2.0$ to $-0.9$ for $3/2^+$, and $f^* = 3.1$ to $-3.5$ for $3/2^-$ (Table 1).
- At threshold, for positive parity of $\Theta^+$, the sum rule $A_{xx}+A_{yy}+A_{zz} = -3$ holds; for negative parity, $A_{xx}+A_{yy}+A_{zz} = +1$ (from parity and Pauli principle arguments).
- The characteristic threshold signals survive up to about 50 MeV above threshold; beyond that, destructive interference from $K^*$ exchange can cause $A_{xx}$ and $A_{yy}$ for the $1/2^+$ state to change sign at 100 MeV excess energy.

## Context

This work extends the near-threshold analysis of azimuthal angular distributions for $\Theta^+$ production in proton-proton collisions, previously studied by Hanhart (2004), Nam (2004), and Thomas (2004), to higher energies using a model-dependent interaction with both $K$ and $K^*$ exchange. The calculations are constrained by the COSY measurement of the production cross section. The results show that the threshold behavior of spin correlation parameters can distinguish parity up to about 50 MeV above threshold, but at higher energies the $K^*$ exchange contributions can alter the pattern, particularly through destructive interference. This enables experimental design for parity determination at facilities like COSY, but warns that model-independent parity identification may be limited to the near-threshold region.
