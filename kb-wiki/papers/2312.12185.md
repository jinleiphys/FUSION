# On the spin period distribution of millisecond pulsars

> Spin period distribution provides important clues to understand the formation of millisecond pulsars (MSPs). To uncover the intrinsic period distribution, we analyze three samples of radio MSPs in the Galactic field and in globular clusters. The selection bias due to pulse broadening has been corrected but turns out to be negligible. We find that all the samples can be well described by a Weibull distribution of spin frequencies. Considering MSPs in the Galactic field or in globular clusters, and in isolation or in binary systems, we find no significant difference in the spin distribution among these subpopulations. Based on the current known population of MSPs, we find that sub-millisecond pulsars are unlikely to be discovered by the Square Kilometer Array, although up to $\sim10$ discoveries of pulsars that spin faster than the current record holder of $P=1.4$~ms are expected.

## Key claim

The intrinsic spin period distribution of millisecond pulsars (MSPs) is well described by a Weibull distribution in spin frequency, and no significant difference is found between MSPs in the Galactic field versus globular clusters, or between isolated and binary systems. Sub-millisecond pulsars are unlikely to be discovered by the Square Kilometer Array, although up to approximately 10 pulsars spinning faster than the current record holder (P = 1.4 ms) are expected.

## Method

The authors compiled three samples of radio MSPs: Sample A (260 MSPs from five well-defined L-band surveys with selection-effect modelling), Sample B (473 Galactic field MSPs), and Sample C (273 globular cluster MSPs). They applied a Bayesian framework with ten candidate distribution models (single-component, lower cut-off, and two-component models) and used the ptemcee sampler to obtain posterior distributions and Bayes factors for model comparison. Selection bias due to pulse broadening was modelled via a two-parameter function of period and dispersion measure, but was found to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- Sample A contains 260 MSPs, more than four times the sample size of LEP+15 (58 MSPs).
- The best-fitting Weibull (frequency-space) model for Sample A has shape parameter k = 1.98 ± 0.12 and scale θ = 0.29 ± 0.01 ms⁻¹ (290 Hz).
- The fraction of sub-millisecond pulsars (f_sub) from the Weibull model is 9.5 × 10⁻⁶ for Sample A with selection-effect modelling.
- The fraction of pulsars with P ≤ 1.396 ms (f_≤1.396) from the Weibull model is 2.6 × 10⁻³ for Sample A with selection-effect modelling.
- Expected discoveries by SKA: fewer than 0.1 sub-millisecond pulsars (for both low yield of 1500 and high yield of 6000 MSPs), and between approximately 1 and 16 pulsars faster than 1.396 ms.
- The log Bayes factor for the Weibull (frequency-space) model relative to the normal model is 40.5 for Sample A with selection-effect modelling, and 110.0 for Sample B without selection-effect modelling.

## Context

This work extends the study of MSP period distributions by Cordes & Chernoff (1997), who used a power-law model with 22 MSPs, and by Levin et al. (2015), who favoured a log-normal distribution with 56 MSPs. It also builds on the finding of Parthasarathy et al. (2017) that a Weibull model in spin frequency performed better than log-normal for 337 MSPs. The results enable predictions for SKA pulsar surveys and provide constraints on the minimum spin period, which is relevant for neutron star equation-of-state studies. The lack of sub-millisecond pulsars is consistent with the puzzle noted by Chakrabarty et al. (2003) and Patruno (2010) regarding the absence of fast pulsars near the break-up limit.
