# Study of heavy-flavor jets in a transport approach

> Measurements at the RHIC and the LHC have observed flavor dependence of single-hadron suppression, which reveal the role played by quark masses in the parton interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium. In this study, we explore the manifestation of quark mass effect and flavor dependence in jet observables. We approach this study using the LIDO transport model. Both elastic and medium-induced radiative processes are implemented for hard parton evolution in the medium. To guarantee energy-momentum conservation in the model for the study of full jet observables, we also include a component that mimics the energy-momentum transported by medium excitation. We first predict the heavy-jet (B-jet, D-jet) and inclusive-jet nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ in central nuclear collisions at both the RHIC and the LHC beam energies. We observe a flavor-dependent jet suppression as a function of jet transverse momentum, which can be tested by future precision measurements of heavy jets. We further investigate a novel observable that considers the angular correlation between two hard objects: a D-meson and a jet, which provides model constraints in addition to those imposed by inclusive measurements.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a flavor-dependent jet suppression (B-jet, D-jet, inclusive jet) in central nuclear collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, but notes that this flavor dependence is not entirely due to the quark mass effect in single parton interactions; qualitatively similar ordering persists even when the dead-cone factor for gluon radiation from heavy quarks is removed, indicating that heavy-flavor triggering selects a different jet sample than inclusive jets.

## Method

The authors used the LIDO transport model, which implements both elastic and medium-induced radiative processes for hard parton evolution in the quark-gluon plasma, including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. Initial hard processes and parton shower evolution down to a scale Q0 were generated by Pythia8, with Q0 = 0.8 GeV at RHIC and Q0 = 1.3 GeV at LHC. For heavy quarks, the dead-cone approximation was used to suppress gluon radiation relative to light quarks by a factor [1 + (theta_D/theta)^2]^{-2}, where theta_D = M/E. To ensure energy-momentum conservation for full jet observables, a hydrodynamic-motivated ansatz for medium excitation was included, with energy and momentum density perturbations propagated according to a conformal speed-of-sound c_s^2 = 1/3.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, Q0 = 0.8 GeV was used for jets with pT below 30 GeV; at LHC, Q0 = 1.3 GeV was used for measurements up to 100 GeV and 1 TeV jets.
- The dead-cone size for heavy quarks is defined as theta_D = M/E, where M is the quark mass and E its energy.
- The in-medium coupling strength parameter mu_min was varied from 1.5 pi T to 2 pi T, producing the bands shown in the results.
- For D-meson-jet angular correlations, D-meson momentum ranges were 4 < pT^D < 20 GeV and pT^D > 20 GeV, with jets having pT > 60 GeV and |eta| < 2.1.
- The medium excitation model uses a conformal speed-of-sound c_s^2 = 1/3 and an average radial flow profile v_r = 0.6 hat{k} in central nuclear collisions.

## Context

This work extends the study of flavor-dependent parton energy loss from single-hadron suppression (observed at RHIC and LHC) to jet observables, using the LIDO transport model developed by Ke et al. (2018). The predictions for heavy-jet R_AA can be tested by future precision measurements, and the D-meson-jet angular correlation study extends prior work by Wang et al. (2019) using a different transport-based model. The calculation underestimates the width of the D-meson-jet correlation in Pb-Pb compared to CMS data (Sirunyan et al., 2019), suggesting insufficient drifting of the jet axis from medium effects in the model, which motivates further study of jet axis diffusion.
