# Operator evolution from the similarity renormalization group and the Magnus expansion

> The Magnus expansion is an efficient alternative to solving similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow equations with high-order, memory-intensive ordinary differential equation solvers. The numerical simplifications it offers for operator evolution are particularly valuable for in-medium SRG calculations, though challenges remain for difficult problems involving intruder states. Here we test the Magnus approach in an analogous but more accessible situation, which is the free-space SRG treatment of the spurious bound-states arising from a leading-order chiral effective field theory (EFT) potential with very high cutoffs. We show that the Magnus expansion passes these tests and then use the investigations as a springboard to address various aspects of operator evolution that have renewed relevance in the context of the scale and scheme dependence of nuclear processes. These aspects include SRG operator flow with band- versus block-diagonal generators, universality for chiral EFT Hamiltonians and associated operators with different regularization schemes, and the impact of factorization arising from scale separation. Implications for short-range correlations physics and the possibilities for reconciling high- and low-resolution treatments of nuclear structure and reactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The Magnus expansion reproduces the generator-dependent SRG behavior for high-cutoff chiral potentials with spurious bound states, but the choice of SRG generator (Wegner versus relative kinetic energy) plays a significant role in overcoming convergence issues analogous to the intruder state problem in IMSRG calculations, though the authors leave a full intruder-state study to future work.

## Method

The authors solve the SRG flow equations using both the conventional high-order ODE approach and the Magnus expansion, where the unitary transformation is parameterized as \(U(s)=e^{\Omega(s)}\) and the flow equation for \(\Omega(s)\) is integrated with a first-order Euler step method. They test both band-diagonal generators (Wegner \(G=H_D\) and relative kinetic energy \(G=T_{\text{rel}}\)) and block-diagonal generators on leading-order chiral potentials with cutoffs of 4, 9, and 20 fm\(^{-1}\) in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)-\(^3\)D\(_1\) coupled channel, and extend the analysis to higher-order chiral potentials (EMN N\(^4\)LO 500 MeV, RKE N\(^4\)LO 450 MeV, Gezerlis N\(^2\)LO 1 fm).

## Key numbers

- The spurious bound state in the 9 fm\(^{-1}\) LO potential has energy \(\varepsilon \approx -2000\) MeV in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)-\(^3\)D\(_1\) coupled channel.
- The block-diagonal generator decouples the spurious bound state at \(\Lambda_{\text{BD}} \approx 4.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), a value found to be scheme-independent across different momentum meshes.
- For the Wegner generator with the 20 fm\(^{-1}\) cutoff potential, the Frobenius norms of \(\eta(s)\) and \(\Omega(s)\) jump several orders of magnitude at \(s \sim 10^{-4}\), indicating a convergence issue.
- Block-diagonal decoupling at \(\lambda = 1\) fm\(^{-1}\) reproduces eigenvalues to better than 0.1% accuracy for both low- and high-momentum sub-blocks.

## Context

This work extends the study of SRG evolution of high-cutoff chiral potentials by Wendt et al. (2011), who showed that band-diagonal decoupling with the \(G=T_{\text{rel}}\) generator fails for potentials with spurious bound states. The authors test the Magnus expansion approach of Morris et al. (2015) on these difficult systems, finding that the generator choice matters for convergence. The results inform ongoing work on high-cutoff chiral EFT interactions (Tews et al., 2018) and the intruder state problem in IMSRG calculations (Stroberg et al., 2019), and enable future studies of universality in evolved operators and short-range correlation physics.
