# Two-Photon-Exchange effects in $ep\rightarrow enÏ€^+$ and their corrections to separated cross sections $Ïƒ_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$ at small $-t$

> In this work, the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effects in $ep\rightarrow enÏ€^+$ at small $-t$ are discussed within a hadronic model. Under the pion dominance approximation the TPE contribution to the amplitude can be described by a scalar function in the limit $m_e\rightarrow 0$. The TPE contributions to the amplitude and the unpolarized differential cross section are both estimated when only the elastic intermediate state is considered. We find that the TPE corrections to the unpolarized differential cross section are about from $-4\%$ to $-20\%$ at $Q^2=1$--$ 1.6$ GeV$^2$. After considering the TPE corrections to the experimental data sets of unpolarized differential cross section, we analyze the TPE corrections to the separated cross sections $Ïƒ_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$. We find that the TPE corrections (at $Q^2=1$--$1.6$ GeV$^2$) to $Ïƒ_{\textrm{L}}$ are about from $-10\%$ to $-30\%$, to $Ïƒ_{\textrm{T}}$ are about $20\%$, and to $Ïƒ_{\textrm{LT,TT}}$ are much larger. By these analysis, we conclude that the TPE contributions in $ep\rightarrow enÏ€^+$ at small $-t$ are important to extract the separated cross sections $Ïƒ_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$ and the electromagnetic magnetic form factor of $Ï€^+$ in the experimental analysis.

## Key claim

The two-photon-exchange (TPE) corrections to the unpolarized differential cross section in $ep\rightarrow en\pi^+$ at small $-t$ range from about $-4\%$ to $-20\%$ at $Q^2=1$--$1.6$ GeV$^2$, and these corrections are important for extracting the separated cross sections $\sigma_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$ and the electromagnetic form factor of $\pi^+$, but the calculation is limited to the elastic intermediate state only and uses a hadronic model under the pion dominance approximation.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic model with the pion dominance approximation, considering only the elastic intermediate state between the two exchanged photons, and took the Feynman gauge to calculate the TPE contributions to the amplitude and unpolarized differential cross section. They employed a monopole form for the pion electromagnetic form factor $F_\pi(q^2)$ with $\Lambda=0.77$ GeV, and used the packages FEYNCALC and LOOPTOOLS for analytical and numerical calculations. The TPE corrections to the separated cross sections $\sigma_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$ were then analyzed by applying the calculated TPE corrections to experimental data sets from JLab $F_\pi$ measurements.

## Key numbers

- TPE corrections to the unpolarized differential cross section are about from $-4\%$ to $-20\%$ at $Q^2=1$--$1.6$ GeV$^2$.
- TPE corrections to $\sigma_{\textrm{L}}$ are about from $-10\%$ to $-30\%$ at $Q^2=1$--$1.6$ GeV$^2$.
- TPE corrections to $\sigma_{\textrm{T}}$ are about $20\%$ at $Q^2=1$--$1.6$ GeV$^2$.
- The imaginary part of the TPE correction to the amplitude coefficient $c_1$ is almost about $7\%$ and not sensitive to $Q^2$, $\phi_\pi$, $-t$, or $\epsilon$ at $W=1.95$ GeV.
- At $\epsilon=0.1$, the real part of the TPE correction $\textrm{Re}[c_{1,\text{Tsai}}^{(2\gamma)}/c_1^{(1\gamma)}]$ reaches about $-9\%$ at $Q^2=1$ GeV$^2$ and about $-12\%$ at $Q^2=1.6$ GeV$^2$.
- The input pion form factor uses a monopole form with $\Lambda=0.77$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon-exchange effects, previously investigated in $ep\rightarrow ep$ (hadronic model, GPD method, pQCD, dispersion-relation approach by various authors), to the process $ep\rightarrow en\pi^+$ at small $-t$. It builds on prior studies of TPE in $e\pi\rightarrow e\pi$ (Blunden 2010) and $ep\rightarrow e\Delta\rightarrow ep\pi^0$ (Afanasev 2013), and is motivated by the experimental extraction of the pion electromagnetic form factor from $ep\rightarrow en\pi^+$ data at JLab (electro-pion-production-JLab-2). The results enable more accurate extraction of the separated cross sections $\sigma_{\textrm{L,T,LT,TT}}$ and the pion electromagnetic form factor by accounting for TPE corrections that are larger than in related processes.
