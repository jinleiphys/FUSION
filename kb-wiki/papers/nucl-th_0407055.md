# Chiral and magnetic rotation in atomic nuclei studied within self-consistent mean-field methods

> Currently, one application of the mean-field methods in nuclear physics is the investigation of exotic nuclear symmetries. This is related, in particular, to the study of nuclear rotation about an axis tilted with respect to the principal axes of the mass distribution in the Tilted-Axis Cranking (TAC) model. The present work presents one of the first TAC calculations performed within fully self-consistent methods. The Hartree-Fock method with the Skyrme effective two-body interaction has been used. A computer code has been developed that allows for the breaking of all spatial symmetries of the solution. As a first application, calculations for the magnetic bands in 142Gd and for the chiral bands in 130Cs, 132La, 134Pr, and 136Pm have been carried out. The appearance of those bands is due to a new mechanism of breaking the spherical symmetry and to the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry, respectively. The self-consistent solutions for 142Gd confirm the important role of the shears mechanism in generating the total angular momentum. However, the agreement with experimental data is not satisfactory, probably due to the lack of the pairing correlations in the calculations or to the possibly overestimated deformation. The results obtained for 132La constitute the first fully self-consistent proof that the nuclear rotation can attain a chiral character. It has been shown that the chiral rotation can only exist above a certain critical angular frequency. It has also been checked that the terms of the Skyrme mean field odd under the time reversal have no qualitative influence on the results.

## Key claim

The work presents one of the first fully self-consistent Tilted-Axis Cranking (TAC) calculations using the Hartree-Fock method with the Skyrme effective interaction, but the agreement with experimental data for the magnetic band in 142Gd is not satisfactory, likely due to the omission of pairing correlations or possibly overestimated deformation.

## Method

The author developed a computer code that performs TAC calculations within the fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) method using the Skyrme effective two-body interaction, allowing for the breaking of all spatial symmetries of the solution. The code was applied to study magnetic bands in 142Gd and chiral bands in the N=75 isotones 130Cs, 132La, 134Pr, and 136Pm. The calculations examined the shears mechanism for magnetic rotation and the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry for chiral rotation, including an analysis of the critical angular frequency above which chiral rotation can exist.

## Key numbers

* For 142Gd, the self-consistent HF solutions confirmed the important role of the shears mechanism in generating total angular momentum, but the agreement with experimental data was not satisfactory.
* For the N=75 isotones studied (130Cs, 132La, 134Pr, 136Pm), chiral solutions were found in 132La, constituting the first fully self-consistent proof that nuclear rotation can attain a chiral character.
* Chiral rotation was shown to exist only above a certain critical angular frequency, whose origin was explained using a classical model that provided surprising agreement with the HF results.
* For magnetic bands, the reduced E2 transition probabilities B(E2) typically do not exceed about 0.1 e^2b^2, while B(M1) values are in the range of about 2-10 mu_N, exhibiting a characteristic fall with increasing spin.
* The ratio J^(2)/B(E2) for magnetic bands assumes values of about 100 hbar^2 MeV^-1 e^-2 b^-2, roughly an order of magnitude larger than for normal or superdeformed bands.
* From TAC calculations, beta deformations as small as about 0.05 and rarely approaching about 0.19 are assigned to the shears bands.
* The shears mechanism is expected to dominate for epsilon less than about 0.12, with core rotation contributing about 15% of the total spin for known magnetic bands.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological TAC model developed by Frauendorf (1993), which used a phenomenological mean potential and the liquid-drop model, by implementing fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme interaction. Prior to this work, only one magnetic band in 84Rb had been studied within the Relativistic-Mean-Field method by Madokoro et al. (2000). The self-consistent approach provides a stronger test of the stability of shears and chiral configurations with respect to core degrees of freedom, and enables study of core polarization effects and full energy minimization with respect to all deformation variables, including current and spin distributions. The code developed has already been used to calculate the time-reversal violating Schiff moment of 225Ra in connection with searches for fundamental T or CP symmetry violation beyond the Standard Model.
