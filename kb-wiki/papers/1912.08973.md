# Microscopic calculations of nuclear level densities with the Lanczos method

> A new method for computing the density of states in nuclei making use of an extrapolated form of the tri-diagonal matrix obtained from the Lanczos method is presented. It will be shown that the global, average properties of the entire Lanczos matrix can be predicted from just four Lanczos iterations. The extrapolated Lanczos matrix (ELM) approach provides for an accurate computation of the density of states described within the configuration space, which, in some cases, is sufficient to accurately calculate the density of states at, or near, the neutron separation energy. Comparisons between theory and experiment are shown for $^{57}$Fe, $^{74}$Ge, and $^{76}$Ge. In addition, we show results for the $J$-dependence of moments and the level density for these three nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors present a new method (extrapolated Lanczos matrix, or ELM) that can predict the global, average properties of the entire Lanczos tri-diagonal matrix from just four Lanczos iterations, enabling accurate computation of nuclear level densities within the configuration space. The method is claimed to be sufficient in some cases to calculate the density of states at or near the neutron separation energy, though the accuracy depends on the system and the number of moments used.

## Method
The authors use the Lanczos algorithm to compute the first eight moments of the nuclear Hamiltonian from just four Lanczos iterations, using a random-phase initial pivot vector. They then model the entire tri-diagonal Lanczos matrix using polynomial forms for the diagonal elements α_i and off-diagonal elements β_i as functions of z_i = ln(i/N), with parameters constrained to reproduce the computed moments. Shell-model calculations were performed using angular momentum projected basis states with the NuShellX code framework, using the GXPF1A interaction for Fe isotopes and the jj44b and jun45 interactions for Ge isotopes.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴⁸Cr (J^π = 0^+), the exact first moment H₁ = -55.004 MeV, while the Lanczos-inferred value is -54.996 MeV; the exact second moment M₂ = 111.121 MeV², Lanczos gives 111.828 MeV².
- For ⁵⁷Fe (J^π = 1/2^-), the model space dimension N = 13,434,903; individual random pivots agree with the exact H₁ to within 10 keV and σ to within 5 keV.
- For systems in the 1p0f shell, typical scaled moments are R₄ ≈ 2.8, R₆ ≈ 12, and R₈ ≈ 65–75; for comparison, a Gaussian distribution has R₄ = 3, R₆ = 15, and R₈ = 105.
- For ⁷⁴Ge, the scaled eighth moment R₈ decreases from about 150 at low angular momentum to about 100 at high angular momentum, indicating a transition toward a more Gaussian-like distribution.

## Context
This work extends moments methods for nuclear level densities, which were previously limited by the ability to compute higher moments of the Hamiltonian and by the need to match to exact low-lying energies (Mon75; Horoi). It provides an alternative to the Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach, which is limited by the sign problem for realistic interactions (AFMC-2), and to the stochastic estimation method (shi), which has computational cost similar to the Lanczos method but requires special code for shifted Krylov-subspace methods. The ELM approach enables microscopic level density calculations for Hauser-Feshbach reaction models, particularly for neutron capture on short-lived nuclei relevant to the r-process, where experimental data are unavailable.
