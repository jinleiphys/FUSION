# The Proton Spin Problem in the Chiral Bag Model

> The flavor singlet axial charge has been a source of study in the last years due to its relation to the so called {\it Proton Spin Problem}. The relevant flavor singlet axial current is anomalous, i.e., its divergence contains a piece which is the celebrated $U_A(1)$ anomaly. This anomaly is intimately associated with the $η^\prime$ meson, which gets its mass from it. When the gauge degrees of freedom of QCD are confined within a volume as is presently understood, the $U_A(1)$ anomaly is known to induce color anomaly leading to "leakage" of the color out of the confined volume (or bag). For consistency of the theory, this anomaly should be cancelled by a boundary term. This ``color boundary term" inherits part or most of the dynamics of the volume (i.e., QCD). In this thesis, we exploit this mapping of the volume to the surface via the color boundary condition to perform a complete analysis of the flavor singlet axial charge in the chiral bag model using the Cheshire Cat Principle. This enables us to obtain the hitherto missing piece in the axial charge associated with the gluon Casimir effect. The result is that the flavor singlet axial charge is small independent of the confinement (bag) size ranging from the skyrmion picture to the MIT bag picture, thereby confirming the (albeit approximate) Cheshire Cat phenomenon.

## Key claim

The flavor singlet axial charge (FSAC) is found to be small and independent of the confinement (bag) size across the entire range from the skyrmion picture to the MIT bag picture, thereby confirming the approximate Cheshire Cat phenomenon; this result includes the previously missing gluon Casimir effect contribution.

## Method

The author performs a complete analysis of the flavor singlet axial charge within the chiral bag model by exploiting the mapping of the QCD volume dynamics to the bag surface via the color boundary condition required to cancel the color anomaly induced by the UA(1) anomaly. The Cheshire Cat Principle is used to connect the skyrmion and MIT bag limits, and the gluon Casimir effect contribution to the axial charge is computed analytically, including a proof that the sum over electric (E) modes vanishes.

## Key numbers

* The flavor singlet axial charge is small independent of the confinement (bag) size, ranging from the skyrmion picture to the MIT bag picture (no specific numerical value given for the charge itself).
* The sum over E-modes to the Casimir effect of the FSAC is shown to vanish analytically: I(X_n) = 0, where X_n = ω_n R are the zeros of the spherical Bessel function j_J(X_n)=0.

## Context

This work extends the chiral bag model approach to the proton spin problem, which was triggered by polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments by the European Muon Collaboration (Ashman 1988) and earlier experiments by Alguard (1978) and Baum (1983). The analysis builds on the Cheshire Cat Principle developed by Nielsen, Rho, Wirzba, and Zahed (1991) and earlier work on the chiral bag model by Vento, Rho, Nyman, Jun, and Brown (1980) and by Brown, Jackson, Rho, and Vento (1984). The result enables a unified description of the flavor singlet axial charge that reconciles the skyrmion and MIT bag pictures, and provides a framework for understanding the role of the UA(1) anomaly and gluon degrees of freedom in nucleon spin structure.
