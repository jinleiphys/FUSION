# A nucleon-pair and boson coexistent description of nuclei

> We study a mixture of s-bosons and like-nucleon pairs with the standard pairing interaction outside a inert core. Competition between the nucleon-pairs and s-bosons is investigated in this scenario. The robustness of the BCS-BEC coexistence and crossover phenomena is examined through an analysis of pf-shell nuclei with realistic single-particle energies in which two configurations with Pauli blocking of nucleon-pair orbits due to the formation of the s-bosons is taken into account. When the nucleon-pair orbits are considered to be independent of the s-bosons, the BCS-BEC crossover becomes smooth with the number of the s-bosons noticeably more than that of the nucleonpairs near the half-shell point, a feature that is demonstrated in the pf-shell for several values of the standard pairing interaction strength. As a further test of the robustness of the BCS-BEC coexistence and crossover phenomena in nuclei, results are given for B(E2; 0^{+}_{g}->2^{+}_1) values of even-even 102-130Sn with 100Sn taken as a core and valence neutron pairs confined within the 1d5/2, 0g7/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 1h11/2 orbits in the nucleon-pair orbit and the s-boson independent approximation. The results indicate that the B(E2) values are well reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that BCS-BEC coexistence and crossover phenomena are robust in nuclei when a mixture of s-bosons and like-nucleon pairs is considered with the standard pairing interaction, but the detailed crossover behavior depends on the specific Pauli blocking configuration of nucleon-pair orbits due to s-boson formation; the nucleon-pair orbit and s-boson independent approximation yields a smooth crossover with s-boson content noticeably exceeding nucleon-pair content near the half-shell point.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian that mixes s-bosons and like-nucleon pairs with a standard pairing interaction outside an inert core, including a term coupling s-bosons to nucleon pairs. They solve the resulting eigenvalue equation using the Heine-Stieltjes polynomial approach for pf-shell nuclei with realistic single-particle energies, considering two distinct Pauli blocking configurations (lowest orbits blocked or highest orbits blocked) as well as a nucleon-pair orbit and s-boson independent approximation. As an application, they fit the model to experimental 2+1 level energies of even-even 102-130Sn and calculate B(E2; 0+1 → 2+1) values.

## Key numbers

- For the pf-shell with G = 1.4 MeV and r = 1, the ground-state s-boson occupation probability ρs increases from 40% to 93% for n = 6 to n = 10 in the first blocking configuration, and from 50% to 60% over the same n range in the second blocking configuration.
- In the nucleon-pair orbit and s-boson independent approximation for the pf-shell, the maximal ρs is about 62% when G = 2.0 MeV.
- For the Sn isotope application, the pairing strength G = 0.18 MeV and β(1) − β(0) = −10.617 MeV are fixed for all nuclei, while the scale factor r decreases from 3.885 (102Sn, n = 1) to 0.367 (130Sn, n = 15).
- The effective quadrupole parameters are χ = 0.44 and q2 = 0.0827 eb from the best fit for all Sn nuclei considered.

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover phenomena from continuum Fermi gas models (as in the work of Eagles, Leggett, Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, and others) to finite nuclear systems with realistic single-particle energies, building on the interacting boson model (IBM) framework of Arima and Iachello. The analysis of 102-130Sn provides a test of the model against experimental B(E2) values and large-scale shell model calculations by Brown et al. (2000), showing that the model reproduces the data well, particularly for lighter Sn isotopes. The results suggest that a mixed description incorporating both s- and d-bosons from the IBM with a few interacting valence nucleons in a shell-model mean field may offer a better description of low-energy nuclear structure.
