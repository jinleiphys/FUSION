# Mapping Deconfinement with a Compact Star Phase Diagram

> We have found correlations between properties of the equation of state for stellar matter with a phase transition at supernuclear densities and two characteristic features of a "phase diagram" for rotating compact stars in the angular velocity - baryon number plane: 1) the critical dividing line between mono- and two-phase star configurations and 2) the maximum mass line. The second line corresponds to the minimum mass function for black hole candidates whereas the first one is observable by a population statistics, e.g. for Z-sources in low-mass X-ray binaries. The observation of a population gap in the mass distribution for the latter is suggested as an astrophysical verification of the existence of a first order phase transition in QCD at high densities such as the deconfinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutrino trapping in hyperonic matter significantly alters the composition and equation of state of protoneutron stars, and that the presence of hyperons reverses the effect of deleptonization on maximum mass compared to purely nucleonic matter, creating a window of metastable stars that collapse into low-mass black holes after neutrinos diffuse out.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner–Bethe–Goldstone many-body theory at zero temperature using the BHF approximation, extended to include the complete baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ−, Σ0, Σ+, Ξ−, Ξ0) with the Nijmegen baryon-baryon interaction from Stoks and Rijken (1999). Nucleonic three-body forces were incorporated via the Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (1999) parametrization of the Argonne V18 interaction with relativistic boost corrections. The composition of β-stable matter was determined by solving charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions with trapped neutrinos (YLe = 0.4, YLμ = 0), and the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkov equations were solved for non-rotating stellar configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for neutrino-trapped hyperon stars: 1.595 M⊙ (central energy density 2.30 × 10^15 g/cm³, central baryon density 1.066 fm⁻³)
- Maximum gravitational mass for neutrino-free hyperon stars: 1.283 M⊙ (central energy density 3.19 × 10^15 g/cm³, central baryon density 1.537 fm⁻³)
- Radius of maximum-mass configuration: 11.14 km (trapped) vs 9.86 km (free)
- Hyperonic core radius for maximum-mass configuration: 6.32 km (trapped) vs 7.60 km (free)
- Metastable gravitational mass window: 1.28 – 1.59 M⊙ (baryonic mass 1.40 – 1.72 M⊙)
- Onset density for Σ− hyperons in neutrino-trapped matter: ρb ≈ 0.50 fm⁻³
- Onset density for Λ hyperons in neutrino-trapped matter: ρb ≈ 0.57 fm⁻³
- Energy carried away by neutrinos for a star with MB = 1.34 M⊙: about 9 × 10^52 erg

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic studies of cold deleptonized hyperon stars by Baldo et al. (2000) and Vidaña et al. (2000a) to the protoneutron star regime with trapped neutrinos, which had previously been studied only in relativistic mean-field models by Keil and Janka (1994) and Prakash et al. (1997). The finding that neutrino trapping increases the maximum mass for hyperonic matter (opposite to the purely nucleonic case) enables a new mechanism for delayed black hole formation, similar to the scenario proposed for kaon condensates by Brown and Bethe (1994) and Prakash et al. (1997).
