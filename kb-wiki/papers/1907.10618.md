# (Not as) Big as a Barn: Upper Bounds on Dark Matter-Nucleus Cross Sections

> Critical probes of dark matter come from tests of its elastic scattering with nuclei. The results are typically assumed to be model-independent, meaning that the form of the potential need not be specified and that the cross sections on different nuclear targets can be simply related to the cross section on nucleons. For point-like spin-independent scattering, the assumed scaling relation is $σ_{χA} \propto A^2 μ_A^2 σ_{χN}\propto A^4 σ_{χN}$, where the $A^2$ comes from coherence and the $μ_A^2\simeq A^2 m_N^2$ from kinematics for $m_χ\gg m_A$. Here we calculate where model independence ends, i.e., where the cross section becomes so large that it violates its defining assumptions. We show that the assumed scaling relations generically fail for dark matter-nucleus cross sections $σ_{χA} \sim 10^{-32}-10^{-27}\;\text{cm}^2$, significantly below the geometric sizes of nuclei, and well within the regime probed by underground detectors. Last, we show on theoretical grounds, and in light of existing limits on light mediators, that point-like dark matter cannot have $σ_{χN}\gtrsim10^{-25}\;\text{cm}^2$, above which many claimed constraints originate from cosmology and astrophysics. The most viable way to have such large cross sections is composite dark matter, which introduces significant additional model dependence through the choice of form factor. All prior limits on dark matter with cross sections $σ_{χN}>10^{-32}\;\text{cm}^2$ with $m_χ\gtrsim 1\;\text{GeV}$ must therefore be re-evaluated and reinterpreted.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard model-independent scaling relation $\sigma_{\chi A} \propto A^4 \sigma_{\chi N}$ for spin-independent dark matter-nucleus scattering generically fails for dark matter-nucleus cross sections $\sigma_{\chi A} \sim 10^{-32}-10^{-27}\;\text{cm}^2$, which are significantly below the geometric sizes of nuclei and well within the regime probed by underground detectors. The authors further show on theoretical grounds, and in light of existing limits on light mediators, that pointlike dark matter cannot have $\sigma_{\chi N}\gtrsim10^{-25}\;\text{cm}^2$, above which many claimed constraints originate from cosmology and astrophysics.

## Method

The authors use nonrelativistic potential scattering theory, employing both the Born approximation and partial wave expansion methods to analyze spin-independent elastic scattering of dark matter with nuclei. They model the nuclear interaction as a contact interaction with a top-hat potential of radius $r_A \approx A^{1/3}r_N$ where $r_N \simeq 1.2\;\text{fm}$, and compute the elastic scattering cross section as a function of coupling strength, nuclear mass number $A$, and incident dark matter momentum $k$. They also examine the case of light mediators and composite dark matter as alternative mechanisms for achieving large cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The model-independent scaling relation fails for dark matter-nucleus cross sections $\sigma_{\chi A} \sim 10^{-32}-10^{-27}\;\text{cm}^2$, well below the geometric sizes of nuclei
- Pointlike dark matter cannot have $\sigma_{\chi N}\gtrsim10^{-25}\;\text{cm}^2$ on theoretical grounds
- The first Born approximation is only valid for elastic scattering cross sections much smaller than the geometric cross section: $\sigma^{(1)}_{\chi A}\ll \frac{16}{9}\pi r_A^2$, which for nucleons gives $\sigma^{(1)}_{\chi N}\ll 10^{-25}\;\text{cm}^2$ using $r_N\approx 1.2\;\text{fm}$
- For a repulsive contact interaction in the strong coupling limit, the s-wave cross section saturates at $\sigma^{l=0}_{\chi A}=4\pi r_A^2$, which is 4 times the geometric cross section
- Direct-detection constraints for underground detectors are affected by the breakdown of scaling at the $\mathcal{O}(1)$ level for $\sigma_{\chi N}\simeq10^{-32}\;\text{cm}^2$
- All prior limits on dark matter with cross sections $\sigma_{\chi N}>10^{-32}\;\text{cm}^2$ with $m_\chi\gtrsim 1\;\text{GeV}$ must be re-evaluated and reinterpreted

## Context

This work systematically explores the theoretical upper limits on dark matter-nucleon cross sections, extending prior considerations of strongly interacting dark matter by Starkman (1990), Wandelt (2000), and others. The paper shows that most claimed constraints in the literature, including those from underground detectors, XQC experiments, and atmospheric/space-based detectors as summarized by Kavanagh (2017) and Mahdawi (2018), occupy parameter space where the standard scaling relations are invalid or where pointlike dark matter is not even allowed. The results require reinterpretation of a large body of prior work and enable future analyses to properly account for model dependence when setting limits on dark matter with cross sections above $10^{-32}\;\text{cm}^2$.
