# $η_c'$ Hadroproduction at Next-to-Leading Order and its Relevance to $ψ'$ Production

> We proceed to the first study of $η_c'$ prompt hadroproduction at next-to-leading order in $α_s$. Based on heavy-quark-spin symmetry, which is systematically used in quarkonium-production phenomenology, we demonstrate that prompt $η_c'$ can be studied at the LHC with the existing data. We emphasize its relevance to constrain $ψ'$ production, in the same way as the first prompt $η_c'$ data at the LHC lately strongly impacted the phenomenology of $J/ψ$ studies.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that prompt $\eta_c'$ hadroproduction can be studied at the LHC with existing data, and that such a measurement would severely constrain the long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for $\psi'$ production via heavy-quark-spin symmetry, but only if the cross section is measured in a $P_T$ range where the color-octet channel dominates (above $P_T = 10$ GeV).

## Method

The authors performed a complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation in $\alpha_s$ of prompt $\eta_c'$ hadroproduction within the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization framework, using the FDC automated computation tool to generate Born, real-emission, and virtual contributions. They used LDMEs for $\eta_c'$ obtained from three existing NLO fits to $\psi'$ data (Shao et al., Gong et al., Bodwin et al.) by applying heavy-quark-spin symmetry to relate the $\eta_c'$ color-octet LDME $\langle\mathcal{O}^{\eta_c'}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle$ to the $\psi'$ LDME $\langle\mathcal{O}^{\psi'}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle$. The cross sections were computed at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV within the LHCb rapidity acceptance, with the charm quark mass set to 1.5 GeV and scale variations around $\mu_0^2 = 4m_c^2 + P_T^2$.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio $\mathcal{B}(\eta_c' \to p\bar{p})$ is derived as $(1.0 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}$, using LHCb measurements of $B^+ \to \eta_c' K^+$ decays and known branching ratios.
- The ratio $\mathcal{B}(\eta_c' \to p\bar{p}) / \mathcal{B}(\eta_c \to p\bar{p})$ is estimated to be about 10\%.
- The $\eta_c'$ color-octet LDME $\langle\mathcal{O}^{\eta_c'}(^3S_1^{[8]})\rangle$ ranges are: $[0, 3.82] \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ (Shao et al.), $[-0.881, 0.857] \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ (Gong et al.), and $[2.35, 3.93] \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ (Bodwin et al.).
- The radial wave function at the origin for the color-singlet channel is taken as $|R(0)|^2 = 0.53$ GeV$^3$.
- Projected statistical uncertainties at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV with 1.5 fb$^{-1}$ luminosity and 2\% detection efficiency show the $\eta_c'$ cross section should be measurable up to $P_T = 20$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to $\eta_c$ production at the LHC (LHCb, 2014), where the measured cross section was compatible with negligible color-octet contributions and set stringent constraints on $J/\psi$ production via heavy-quark-spin symmetry. The authors note that unlike the $J/\psi$ case, no $e^+e^-$ or $ep$ data exist to constrain $\psi'$ NRQCD fits, making $\eta_c'$ measurements crucial to lift the degeneracy between the three existing NLO fits (Shao et al., 2014; Gong et al., 2012; Bodwin et al., 2015). A measurement compatible with the color-singlet contribution alone would directly set an upper limit on $\langle\mathcal{O}^{\psi'}(^1S_0^{[8]})\rangle$, while a larger cross section would discriminate between the competing fits.
