# Three-Nucleon Force Effects in the FSI configuration of the d(n, nn)p Breakup Reaction

> We investigated three-nucleon (3N) force effects in the final state interaction (FSI) configuration of the d(n, nn)p breakup reaction at the incoming nucleon energy E n = 200 MeV. Although 3N force effects for the elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering cross section at comparable energies are located predominantly in the region of intermediate and backward angles, the corresponding 3N force effects for the integrated FSI configuration breakup cross section are found also at forward scattering angles.

# Three-Nucleon Force Effects in the FSI configuration of the d(n, nn)p Breakup Reaction

## Key claim
Three-nucleon force (3NF) effects in the integrated final state interaction (FSI) configuration breakup cross section for the d(n, nn)p reaction at 200 MeV appear at forward scattering angles, whereas in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering at comparable energies such effects are predominantly located at intermediate and backward angles. The claim is based on theoretical calculations using the CD Bonn two-nucleon potential plus the Tucson-Melbourne 3NF, not on experimental data.

## Method
The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum space using the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential alone and with the addition of the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force. They computed an integrated breakup cross section around the FSI condition (where the two outgoing neutrons have equal momenta) by integrating the differential cross section over the arc-length variable S in an interval of width 20 MeV centered at the FSI point S0, with the two neutrons detected at the same laboratory scattering angle.

## Key numbers
- Incident neutron laboratory energy: E_n = 200 MeV
- Integration width parameter: ΔS = 20 MeV
- The FSI condition occurs at S = S0, where the two neutron energies are equal (E1 = E2)

## Context
This work extends the authors' long-standing program of three-nucleon continuum studies using exact Faddeev equation solutions, which began in the 1980s with realistic two-nucleon forces (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijmegen potentials). Previous work by Witala (1998) showed that including the Tucson-Melbourne or Urbana IX 3NF models restored agreement with elastic scattering data below approximately 140 MeV, but discrepancies remained at higher energies. The present study examines a specific kinematic configuration of the breakup channel where 3NF effects appear at forward angles, unlike the elastic channel. The authors hope these predictions can be confronted with future experimental data, though they note the experimental challenge of detecting two neutrons from a neutron beam.
