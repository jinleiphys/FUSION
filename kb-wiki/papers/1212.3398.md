# Three-particle scattering amplitudes from a finite volume formalism

> We present a quantization condition for the spectrum of a system composed of three identical bosons in a finite volume with periodic boundary conditions. This condition gives a relation between the finite volume spectrum and infinite volume scattering amplitudes. The quantization condition presented is an integral equation that in general must be solved numerically. However, for systems with an attractive two-body force that supports a two-body bound-state, a diboson, and for energies below the diboson breakup, the quantization condition reduces to the well-known Luscher formula with exponential corrections in volume that scale with the diboson binding momentum. To accurately determine infinite volume phase shifts, it is necessary to extrapolate the phase shifts obtained from the Luscher formula for the boson-diboson system to the infinite volume limit. For energies above the breakup threshold, or for systems with no two-body bound-state (with only scattering states and resonances) the Luscher formula gets power-law volume corrections and consequently fails to describe the three-particle system. These corrections are nonperturbatively included in the quantization condition presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantization condition for three identical bosons in a finite volume that relates the finite-volume spectrum to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes, but this condition is an integral equation that must be solved numerically in general, except for the special case of energies below the diboson breakup threshold where it reduces to the Luscher formula with exponential volume corrections.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic field-theoretical approach with an auxiliary S-wave dimer field that non-perturbatively sums all two-body interactions, then compute the finite-volume three-body correlation function from its poles. The quantization condition is obtained by summing the diagrammatic expansion of the three-body correlation function and requiring the determinant of (1 + scattering amplitude times finite-volume loop function) to vanish, with the determinant taken over both open channels and partial waves.

## Key numbers

- For reliable use of the boson-diboson Luscher formula, one must be in the regime where gamma_d * L is at least 4, giving phase shifts with a few percent uncertainty.
- For deuteron-neutron scattering at physical parameters, one would naively need L greater than or approximately 17 fm, but by canceling leading exponential corrections using different boost momenta, the required volume can be reduced to L greater than or approximately 12 fm.
- The next-to-leading exponential correction due to bound-state size scales as e^{-sqrt(2)*gamma_d*L}/L.

## Context

This work extends the Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986, 1991) from two-body systems to three identical bosons, addressing a major obstacle in finite-volume scattering theory. It corrects and improves upon prior three-body finite-volume approaches by Kreuzer et al. (2008-2012), which did not account for D-wave partial-wave mixing from the boosted two-body subsystem, and by Guo et al. (2012), whose isobar approximation fails for resonant two-body subsystems due to power-law volume corrections. The formalism enables lattice QCD calculations of three-particle scattering amplitudes and nuclear reaction cross sections.
