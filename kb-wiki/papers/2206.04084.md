# Pion distribution amplitude at the physical point using the leading-twist expansion of the quasi-distribution-amplitude matrix element

> We present a lattice QCD determination of the distribution amplitude (DA) of the pion and the first few Mellin moments from an analysis of the quasi-DA matrix element within the leading-twist framework. We perform our study on a HISQ ensemble with $a=0.076$ fm lattice spacing with the Wilson-Clover valence quark mass tuned to the physical point. We analyze the ratios of pion quasi-DA matrix elements at short distances using the leading-twist Mellin operator product expansion (OPE) at the next-to-leading order and the conformal OPE at the leading-logarithmic order. We find a robust result for the first non-vanishing Mellin moment $\langle x^2 \rangle = 0.287(6)(6)$ at a factorization scale $μ=2$ GeV. We also present different Ansätze-based reconstructions of the $x$-dependent DA, from which we determine the perturbative leading-twist expectations for the pion electromagnetic and gravitational form-factors at large momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The authors determine the pion distribution amplitude (DA) and its first few Mellin moments from lattice QCD using the leading-twist expansion of the quasi-DA matrix element, finding a robust result for the first non-vanishing Mellin moment ⟨x²⟩ = 0.287(6)(6) at μ = 2 GeV, but note that the extraction relies on modeling higher-twist and lattice corrections and on truncating the operator product expansion.

## Method

The authors compute the bare quasi-DA matrix element for a boosted pion using a mixed-action lattice setup with HISQ sea quarks and Wilson-clover valence quarks on a 64³ × 64 ensemble at a = 0.076 fm with physical pion mass. They construct renormalization-group-invariant ratios of the matrix element at different momenta and analyze the short-distance behavior using both the Mellin operator product expansion at next-to-leading order and the conformal OPE at leading-logarithmic order to extract the Mellin and Gegenbauer moments of the pion DA.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.076 fm on a 64³ × 64 ensemble with physical pion mass of 140 MeV
- First non-vanishing Mellin moment ⟨x²⟩ = 0.287(6)(6) at factorization scale μ = 2 GeV
- Eight spatial momenta used: P₃ = 0.254 × n₃ GeV for n₃ = 0 to 7, with highest momentum 1.78 GeV
- 350 statistically independent configurations used; for n₃ ≤ 3, (N_ex, N_sl) = (4, 80) and for n₃ > 3, (8, 160) all-mode averaging samples

## Context

This work extends the leading-twist expansion approach previously applied to current-current correlators by Braun (2007) and later studies by Bali (2017, 2018) to the quasi-DA matrix element framework based on LaMET and pseudodistribution methods. It complements recent lattice calculations of the pion PDF by Izubuchi (2019), Gao (2020), and Lin (2020), and provides a pathway to reconstruct the x-dependent pion DA and compute perturbative expectations for pion electromagnetic and gravitational form factors at large momentum transfers.
