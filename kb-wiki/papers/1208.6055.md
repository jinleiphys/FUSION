# The no-core shell model with general radial bases

> Calculations in the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) have conventionally been carried out using the harmonic-oscillator many-body basis. However, the rapid falloff (Gaussian asymptotics) of the oscillator functions at large radius makes them poorly suited for the description of the asymptotic properties of the nuclear wavefunction. We establish the foundations for carrying out no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations using a basis built from general radial functions and discuss some of the considerations which enter into using such a basis. In particular, we consider the Coulomb-Sturmian basis, which provides a complete set of functions with a realistic (exponential) radial falloff.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the formal and computational foundations for performing no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations using a general radial basis, specifically the Coulomb-Sturmian basis, as an alternative to the conventional harmonic oscillator basis. The authors demonstrate that this basis provides realistic exponential asymptotics but note that the exploratory results have not yet probed several variational freedoms available with the Coulomb-Sturmian basis, including the length parameters and the many-body truncation scheme.

## Method

The authors transform two-body matrix elements of the Hamiltonian from the harmonic oscillator basis to the Coulomb-Sturmian basis using overlap integrals between the radial wavefunctions of the two bases, with the transformation sum truncated at a maximum oscillator quantum number Ncut. Kinetic energy matrix elements are instead evaluated directly in the Coulomb-Sturmian basis using its momentum-space representation in terms of Jacobi polynomials. Many-body calculations are then performed using the MFDn code with the JISP16 interaction for 6Li, applying the Nmax truncation scheme by formally defining N = 2n + l for each Coulomb-Sturmian single-particle state.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 6Li 1+ state: variational minimum occurs at approximately 20 MeV for the harmonic oscillator basis parameter hbar-Omega
- Coulomb-Sturmian basis calculations performed with Nmax = 4, 6, 8, and 10
- Change-of-basis transformation truncation tested at Ncut = 9, 11, and 13
- Center-of-mass expectation value <Ncm[Omega-tilde]> minimum obtained at hbar-Omega-tilde approximately 17.5 MeV for Coulomb-Sturmian basis calculations with basis hbar-Omega = 30 MeV
- Minimum value of <Ncm[Omega-tilde]> appears to converge towards a nonzero value of approximately 0.2 with increasing Nmax
- Ground state energy results stable for hbar-Omega greater than or approximately 20 MeV with respect to Ncut cutoff

## Context

This work extends the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) approach, which has conventionally used the harmonic oscillator many-body basis as established by Navratil et al. (2000). The harmonic oscillator basis provides exact center-of-mass factorization and simple Moshinsky transformation for two-body matrix elements, but its Gaussian asymptotics are poorly suited for describing asymptotic properties of nuclear wavefunctions. The Coulomb-Sturmian basis has previously been applied to few-body problems in atomic physics by Hylleraas (1928) and Rotenberg (1962) and in hadronic physics by Jacobs (1986) and Keister (1997). This work enables improved convergence for observables sensitive to large-distance properties, such as RMS radii and E2 matrix elements, and may be particularly appropriate for halo nuclei where the mismatch with oscillator functions at large distances is severe.
