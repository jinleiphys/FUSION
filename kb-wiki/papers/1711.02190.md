# Anomalous effects of dense matter under rotation

> We study the anomaly induced effects of dense baryonic matter under rotation. We derive the anomalous terms that account for the chiral vortical effect in the low-energy effective theory for light Nambu-Goldstone modes. The anomalous terms lead to new physical consequences, such as the anomalous Hall energy current and spontaneous generation of angular momentum in a magnetic field (or spontaneous magnetization by rotation). In particular, we show that, due to the presence of such anomalous terms, the ground state of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) under sufficiently fast rotation becomes the "chiral soliton lattice" of neutral pions that has lower energy than the QCD vacuum and nuclear matter. We briefly discuss the possible realization of the chiral soliton lattice induced by a fast rotation in noncentral heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that under sufficiently fast rotation, the ground state of QCD with both baryon and isospin chemical potentials becomes a "chiral soliton lattice" of neutral pions, which has lower energy than both the QCD vacuum and nuclear matter; this result depends on the condition that the rotation speed exceeds a threshold value \(\Omega_{\rm CSL}\) that scales inversely with the product of the chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors derive new anomalous terms in the low-energy effective chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) that account for the chiral vortical effect (CVE) by requiring anomaly matching between QCD and its low-energy effective theory for Nambu-Goldstone modes. They then apply this formalism to two-flavor QCD at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials under rotation, solving the resulting Hamiltonian for the neutral pion field \(\pi_0\) both in the chiral limit and with finite quark masses, where the ground state is found analytically using Jacobi elliptic functions.

## Key numbers

* The threshold rotation for the chiral soliton lattice is \(\Omega_{\rm CSL} = 8\pi m_{\pi} f_{\pi}^2 / (\mu_{\rm B} |\mu_{\rm I}|)\), with \(f_{\pi} \approx 93\) MeV and \(m_{\pi} \approx 140\) MeV.
* For a rotating nuclear matter made of \(^{197}_{\ 79}\)Au with \(\mu_{\rm B}^0 \approx 1000\) MeV and \(\mu_{\rm I}^0 \approx 10\) MeV, \(\Omega_{\rm CSL}^0 \approx 6 \times 10^3\) MeV.
* With chemical potentials several times larger than \(\mu_{\rm B}^0\) and \(\mu_{\rm I}^0\), \(\Omega_{\rm CSL} \sim 10^2\) MeV, which may be within experimental reach.
* The angular momentum density per unit area in the CSL state is \(J_z/A = \mu_{\rm B} \mu_{\rm I} / \pi\).
* The baryon number per unit area is \(N_{\rm B}/A = \mu_{\rm I} \Omega / \pi\) and the isospin number per unit area is \(N_{\rm I}/A = \mu_{\rm B} \Omega / \pi\).
* The total energy density of the CSL is \({\cal E}_{\rm tot}/V = 2 m_{\pi}^2 f_{\pi}^2 (1 - 1/k^2) < 0\).
* The cross-correlated susceptibility between rotation and magnetic field is \(\chi_{jB} = \chi_{m\Omega} = \mu_{\rm B}^2 \mu_{\rm I} / (8\pi^4 f_{\pi}^2)\) in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of anomaly-induced effects in dense matter under rotation, building on prior results for the chiral vortical effect by Vilenkin (1979, 1980), Erdmenger (2008), Banerjee (2008), Son (2009), and Landsteiner (2011, 2012, 2016). It parallels the earlier finding by Son (2007) and Brauner (2016) that a chiral soliton lattice forms in QCD under a strong magnetic field, here replacing the magnetic field with rotation. The results enable future experimental searches for such exotic states in low-energy heavy ion collisions at facilities like RHIC, NICA, and FAIR, and open the door to studying axion electrodynamics in rotating QCD matter.
