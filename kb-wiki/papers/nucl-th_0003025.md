# Nuclear Multifragmentation in the Non-extensive Statistics - Canonical Formulation

> We apply the canonical quantum statistical model of nuclear multifragmentation generalized in the framework of recently proposed Tsallis non-extensive thermostatistics for the description of nuclear multifragmentation process. The test calculation in the system with A=197 nucleons show strong modification of the 'critical' behaviour associated with the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition for small deviations from the conventional Boltzmann-Gibbs statistical mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even tiny deviations from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (entropic index q = 1.0005 or 1.001) strongly modify the 'critical' behavior associated with the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in a finite system of A=197 nucleons, but only within a specific 'critical zone' of excitation energies (E*/A between 2.5 and 10 MeV).

## Method

The authors generalized the canonical quantum statistical model of nuclear multifragmentation using Tsallis non-extensive thermostatistics, replacing the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs fragment partition function with a Tsallis form. They applied the recurrence equation method to solve the canonical ensemble exactly without Monte Carlo techniques, calculating the caloric curve, specific heat, multiplicity fluctuations, and fragment-size distributions for a system with A=197 nucleons (Z=79, N=118) at freeze-out volume V_f = A/ρ_f with ρ_0 = 0.168 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- System: A=197 nucleons (Z=79, N=118); free volume V_f = 3A/ρ_0 with ρ_0 = 0.168 fm⁻³
- Critical zone of excitation energies: E*/A ∈ (2.5 – 10) MeV where strong sensitivity to q is observed
- Entropic indices studied: q = 1 (Boltzmann-Gibbs limit), q = 1.0005, and q = 1.001
- For q = 1.001, the maximum of the normalized second factorial cumulant moment γ₂ is comparable to values found in 2D and 3D percolation systems of comparable size
- Constraint verification: for T > 1 MeV and 1 ≤ q ≤ 1.001, the condition U > -T/(q-1) is always satisfied

## Context

This work extends the canonical multifragmentation model of Parvan, Gudima, and Toneev (1999) by incorporating Tsallis non-extensive thermostatistics (Tsallis, 1988) to account for off-equilibrium correlations in nuclear fragmentation. The results show that signals of 'criticality' become stronger for q > 1, resembling features of the shattering phase transition in off-equilibrium systems described by Botet and Ploszajczak (1996), and the authors argue this demonstrates the robustness of the critical phenomenon while cautioning that finite-size scaling analysis of nuclear phase transitions may be hazardous.
