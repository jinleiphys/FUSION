# Quark exchange effects in single flavored dibaryons

> We reveal the quark exchange effects related to both the kinetic energy and various interactions in the single flavored dibaryon bound states with $^1S_0$ in the quark models. The hadron covalent bond can be established by the shared identical quarks due to the quark exchange effect between two colorless baryons. Such hadron covalent bond plays a decisive role in the deuteronlike di-$Ω_{ccc}$ and di-$Ω_{bbb}$ covalent molecule states. The $σ$-meson exchange is indispensable in the deuteronlike di-$Δ^{++}$ and compact di-$Ω$ states. The hadron covalent bond clearly appears in the di-$Δ^{++}$ state but is hidden in the di-$Ω$ state. The chromomagnetic interaction is always repulsive in the di-$Δ^{++}$, di-$Ω$, di-$Ω_{ccc}$, and di-$Ω_{bbb}$ states. The color-electric interaction is strongly attractive in the di-$Ω$ state but weakly attractive or repulsive in the di-$Δ^{++}$, di-$Ω_{ccc}$, and di-$Ω_{bbb}$ states.

# Quark exchange effects in single flavored dibaryons

## Key claim

The hadron covalent bond, established by shared identical quarks due to quark exchange between two colorless baryons, plays a decisive role in the deuteronlike di-Ωccc and di-Ωbbb covalent molecule states, while the σ-meson exchange is indispensable in the deuteronlike di-Δ++ and compact di-Ω states; however, the chromomagnetic interaction is always repulsive in all four systems studied, and the color-electric interaction is strongly attractive only in the di-Ω state.

## Method

The author solved the six-body Schrödinger equation for single-flavored dibaryons (di-Δ++, di-Ω, di-Ωccc, di-Ωbbb) in the ¹S₀ channel using both the naive quark model (NQM) and the chiral quark model (ChQM) of the Salamanca group. The Gaussian expansion method was employed for high-precision numerical solution, and the binding energy was obtained by subtracting the mass of two isolated baryons from the dibaryon eigenvalue. Contributions from kinetic energy, confinement, Coulomb, chromomagnetic, and meson-exchange terms were decomposed to identify binding mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- di-Δ++: binding energy −7.57 MeV in ChQM (unbound in NQM); average distance between baryons 2.48 fm; baryon size 0.64 fm
- di-Ω: binding energy −61.66 MeV in ChQM (unbound in NQM); average distance between baryons 1.03 fm; baryon size 0.55 fm
- di-Ωccc: binding energy −0.54 MeV in NQM, −1.16 MeV in ChQM; average distance between baryons 3.71 fm (NQM) and 2.34 fm (ChQM); baryon size 0.32 fm (NQM) and 0.39 fm (ChQM)
- di-Ωbbb: binding energy −1.07 MeV in NQM, −1.08 MeV in ChQM; average distance between baryons 1.96 fm (NQM) and 1.80 fm (ChQM); baryon size 0.21 fm (NQM) and 0.25 fm (ChQM)
- σ-meson exchange contribution to binding: −27.42 MeV in di-Δ++, −116.97 MeV in di-Ω
- Chromomagnetic repulsion: 12.79 MeV in di-Δ++, 33.99 MeV in di-Ω, less than 3 MeV in di-Ωccc and di-Ωbbb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dibaryon systems including the deuteron and the d*(2380) resonance, and builds on earlier quark model investigations of di-Δ++ by Oka (1980), di-Ω by Zhang (2000) and Huang (2019), and fully heavy dibaryons by Huang (2020) and Lyu (2021). The results contrast with lattice QCD predictions by Gongyo (2017) that found a di-Ω binding energy of about 1.6 MeV, and with Mathur (2022) who found a deep di-Ωbbb bound state. The identification of hadron covalent bond as the dominant binding mechanism in di-Ωccc and di-Ωbbb enables future work on hidden-color components and the role of vector meson exchanges in reducing the strong σ-meson attraction seen in chiral quark models.
