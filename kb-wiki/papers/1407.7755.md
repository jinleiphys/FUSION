# Extension of the Liège Intranuclear-Cascade model to reactions induced by light nuclei

> The purpose of this paper is twofold. First, we present the extension of the Liège Intranuclear Cascade model to reactions induced by light ions. Second, we describe the C++ version of the code, which it is physics-wise equivalent to the legacy version, is available in Geant4 and will serve as the basis for all future development of the model. We describe the ideas upon which we built our treatment of nucleus-nucleus reactions and we compare the model predictions against a vast set of heterogeneous experimental data. In spite of the discussed limitations of the intranuclear-cascade scheme, we find that our model yields valid predictions for a number of observables and positions itself as one of the most attractive alternatives available to Geant4 users for the simulation of light-ion-induced reactions.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Liège Intranuclear Cascade model to reactions induced by light ions (projectiles with A ≤ 18), but acknowledge that the intranuclear-cascade scheme has inherent limitations for nucleus-nucleus reactions, particularly regarding the neglect of mean-field interaction between projectile constituents and the inability to describe collective rearrangement of the mean field.

## Method

The model prepares projectile nucleons in their center-of-mass frame using Gaussian or Paris-potential momentum distributions, then applies a Lorentz boost to the laboratory frame while accounting for projectile binding by putting nucleons off their mass shell via a dynamical pseudopotential. The intranuclear cascade phase treats binary nucleon-nucleon collisions with Pauli blocking within a single mean-field potential (that of the target nucleus), and includes a coalescence algorithm for light cluster production. Low-energy reactions are handled through a fusion model, and the cascade stopping time is determined self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The model is valid for projectile masses up to A = 18, with incident energies from a few tens of MeV to several GeV per nucleon
- The nucleon mass is set to m = 938.2796 MeV for all nucleons during the intranuclear cascade phase
- For projectiles with Z > 2, the Coulomb barrier is calculated using Shen's parametrization: B_Shen = e²Z_pZ_t/(R_p+R_t+3.2 fm) - a(R_pR_t)/(R_p+R_t), with R_i = (1.12A_i^{1/3} - 0.94A_i^{-1/3}) fm and a = 1 MeV/fm
- The dynamical pseudopotential distributions have mean values and standard deviations of (8 ± 18) MeV for deuteron, (25 ± 10) MeV for triton, (29 ± 9) MeV for ⁴He, and (30 ± 5) MeV for ¹²C
- The model uses a hard Fermi sphere momentum distribution for target nuclei with p_F = 270 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the INCL model (Boudard et al., 2013), which was recognized as one of the most accurate models for nucleon-induced reactions in the 60–3000 MeV range by the IAEA Benchmark of Spallation Models (Leray et al., 2011). The extension enables simulation of light-ion-induced reactions relevant to hadrontherapy, space radiation shielding, and radioactive beam production, and is implemented as INCL++ in the Geant4 toolkit. The model positions itself as an alternative to other Geant4 options for light-ion reactions, though it cannot handle symmetric reactions between heavy nuclei.
