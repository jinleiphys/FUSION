# Finite temperature properties of QCD with two flavors and three, four and five colors

> I present a numerical study of the crossover between the low temperature chirally broken phase and the high temperature chirally restored phase in $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory with $N_c=3-5$ colors and $N_f=2$ degenerate fermion flavors. Fermion masses span a range of intermediate values (represented by the squared ratio of pseudoscalar to vector meson masses $(m_{PS}/m_V)^2\sim 0.25$ to 0.63). Observables include the temperature dependent chiral condensate and screening masses. At each fermion mass these quantities show nearly identical temperature dependence across $N_c$.

## Key claim

The crossover temperature for the chiral transition in SU(N_c) gauge theory with N_f=2 fermions shows nearly identical temperature dependence across N_c=3, 4, and 5 at common fermion masses, contradicting the naive large-N_c expectation that T_c should scale as sqrt(N_c) from chiral symmetry breaking arguments.

## Method

The author performed lattice simulations with two degenerate flavors of Wilson-clover fermions and the Wilson plaquette gauge action, using normalized hypercubic smeared links. For each N_c value, zero-temperature data at selected bare parameters were used to fix the lattice spacing via the Wilson flow parameter t_0 and the fermion mass proxy (m_PS/m_V)^2; temperature was then varied by changing the temporal lattice extent N_t while holding all other bare parameters fixed, generating approximately 120 datasets across three values of (m_PS/m_V)^2 (0.63, 0.5, and 0.25) and five values of N_t per bare parameter set.

## Key numbers

- The peak in the temperature derivative of the chiral condensate occurs at sqrt(t_0) T ~ 0.18, corresponding to a temperature of about 225 MeV, independent of (m_PS/m_V)^2 across the range 0.25 to 0.63
- Pure gauge transition temperatures (for comparison) are sqrt(t_0) T_c = 0.265, 0.261, and 0.278 for N_c = 3, 4, and 5 respectively
- The physical point (2+1 flavors) gives sqrt(t_0) T_c = 0.11, corresponding to T_c = 150 MeV with sqrt(t_0) = 0.147 fm
- Zero-temperature condensate values are (3/N_c) t_0^(3/2) Sigma ~ 0.03-0.04 at (m_PS/m_V)^2 = 0.63, ~0.02 at 0.5, and ~0.015 at 0.25

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of large-N_c QCD (reviewed by Lucini (2012), Garcia-Perez (2020), and Hernandez (2020)) by testing three competing scenarios for the crossover temperature: the naive large-N_c gluonic dominance prediction, the Pisarski-Wilczek (1983) linear sigma model prediction of T_c proportional to sqrt(N_c), and the Hagedorn spectrum prediction of N_c-independent behavior. The results support the Hagedorn scenario and contradict the Pisarski-Wilczek scaling expectation, while also ruling out the first-order deconfinement scenario (already unlikely given Cuteri (2020) found the critical point at pseudoscalar mass ~4 GeV for N_c=3). The data enable future combined fits to chiral perturbation theory including lattice artifacts, requiring more zero-temperature datasets than used here.
