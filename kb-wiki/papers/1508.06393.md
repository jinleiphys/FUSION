# Calculation of Spin Observables for Proton-Proton Elastic Scattering in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> Bethe-Salpeter equation is applied to $p$-$p$ elastic scattering. The observables of spin are calculated in the framework of the M matrix using the two-body interaction potential. The parameter of the pseudovector coupling constant is adjusted so as to reproduce the spin singlet part. It is shown that the spin rotation $R(θ)$ and $A(θ)$ are improved by the resonance effect for ${}^{\rm 1}S_{\rm 0}$.

# Calculation of Spin Observables for Proton-Proton Elastic Scattering in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation, when applied to proton-proton elastic scattering with an adjusted pseudovector coupling constant, improves the spin rotation parameters R(θ) and A(θ) by including a resonance effect from the π⁺+p+n quasi-stationary state in the ¹S₀ channel, but only the zeroth axial-vector (av) component of the BS amplitude, not the pseudoscalar (ps) component, reproduces the spin singlet part at intermediate energy.

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved for p-p elastic scattering using an M-matrix formalism with nuclear bar phase shift parametrization, excluding the Coulomb force. The wave function was approximated under the leading-order inverse-square potential derived from the BS equation, with regularization of divergences from the pseudovector coupling interaction applied only to the Born term. The pseudovector coupling constant f was adjusted to reproduce the spin singlet part, and a resonant correction was applied to the S-wave phase shift to model the π⁺+p+n intermediate state near the pion production threshold.

## Key numbers
- The experimental value of I₀(1−Cₙₙ) at θ=90° and 310 MeV laboratory energy is 3.7×(1−0.6) ≈ 1.48; the av equation reproduces this value, while the ps equation gives about threefold the experimental value.
- The optimum value of the pseudovector coupling constant f is around 0.065 from the differential cross section at θ=90° and 310 MeV laboratory energy, with the av equation giving a tentative value of f at ∼0.065.
- At 400 MeV laboratory energy, the experimental I₀Cₖₚ value is 3.7×(−0.32) ≈ −1.18; the optimum f for the S=1 part is around 0.05, a little smaller than the S=0 case.
- The resonance effect reverses the sign of R(θ) at θ=90° for the av equation, making it possible to explain the experimental trend from θ=10° to 50° at 400 MeV laboratory energy.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous application of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kinpara, year not specified), which had shown that the BS equation improves differential cross section and polarization beyond the Born approximation. The study contrasts the zeroth axial-vector and pseudoscalar components of the BS amplitude for the spin singlet state, finding that only the av component reproduces experimental spin-correlation data. The charge independence of the two-nucleon system is broken because the pion-nucleon coupling constant is reduced to ∼1/20 of its standard value. The results enable future inclusion of higher partial waves beyond the P-wave and suggest that the resonance effect from the π⁺+p+n quasi-stationary state is essential for understanding triple-scattering spin observables at intermediate energies near the pion production threshold.
