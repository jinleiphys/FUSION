# R-Matrix description of particle energy spectra produced by low-energy T+T reactions

> An R-matrix model for three-body final states is presented and applied to a recent measurement of the neutron energy spectrum from the T+T->2n+alpha reaction. The calculation includes the n-alpha and n-n interactions in the final state, angular momentum conservation, antisymmetrization, and the interference between different channels. A good fit to the measured spectrum is obtained, where clear evidence for the 5He ground state is observed. The model is also used to predict the alpha-particle spectrum from T+T as well as particle spectra from 3He+3He. The R-matrix approach presented here is very general, and can be adapted to a wide variety of problems with three-body final states.

# R-Matrix description of particle energy spectra produced by low-energy T+T reactions

## Key claim

The R-matrix model for three-body final states provides a good fit to the measured neutron energy spectrum from T+T -> 2n+alpha at an effective center-of-mass energy of 16 keV, but the authors caution that the improvement in fit quality from adding additional channels (beyond the 1/2- and 3/2- n-alpha channels used in prior work) may be comparable to neutron-energy-dependent systematic errors in the scattering and attenuation corrections.

## Method

The authors constructed an R-matrix model for three-body final states that includes n-alpha and n-n interactions, angular momentum conservation, antisymmetrization via direct and exchange terms, and interference between different channels. They applied this model to fit raw time-of-flight neutron data from the T+T reaction measured at the National Ignition Facility, incorporating thermal broadening from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, neutron attenuation and scattering, detector light output and time response, and background from the T(d,n) reaction. The model was also used to predict alpha-particle spectra from T+T and particle spectra from 3He+3He.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for T+T -> 2n+alpha: 11.332 MeV
- Effective center-of-mass energy for the NIF measurement: 16 keV
- Channel radius for n+alpha parameters: 3.0 fm
- Channel radius for n+5He: 4.0 fm
- Di-neutron channel parameters: E_c = 3.119 MeV, gamma^2_c = 31.95 MeV, channel radius 2.0 fm, reproducing Argonne V18 potential scattering length -18.487 fm and effective range 2.840 fm
- (nn)+alpha channel radius: 3.5 fm
- Best fit (fit 16) achieved chi^2_min = 632 with 6 variable parameters fitting 812 data points
- Fit considering only 1/2- and 3/2- n-alpha channels (fit 9) achieved chi^2_min = 1085

## Context

This work extends the sequential-decay R-matrix model first presented in Sayre et al. (2013) for the T+T reaction at NIF, providing a more complete treatment including additional channels (1/2+ n-alpha and di-neutron emission) and interference effects. The approach builds on the R-matrix formalism for three-body final states described by Barker (1988) and earlier work by Balashov et al. (1974), Gee et al. (1977), and Fynbo et al. (2003, 2009). The model can be adapted to mirror reactions such as 3He+3He -> 2p+alpha and T+3He -> n+p+alpha currently under study at inertial confinement fusion facilities, and is general enough for application to a wide variety of problems with three-body final states.
