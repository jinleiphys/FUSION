# Predictions for photon-jet correlations at forward rapidities in heavy-ion collisions

> In this work, we study for the first time jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions with introduction of saturation and Sudakov effects with parameters tuned for upcoming forward calorimeter acceptances in experiments, in particular the ALICE FoCal detector. We focus on $γ+$jet correlations by taking into account in-medium parton evolution using the BDIM equation that describes jet interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) combined with vacuum-like emissions (VLE). We systematically introduce the early time gluon saturation dynamics through the small-$x$ Improved Transverse Momentum Dependent factorization (ITMD). For our purpose, we use Monte Carlo programs KATIE and TMDICE to generate hard events and in-medium parton evolution, respectively. We present results of azimuthal correlations and nuclear modification ratios to gauge the impact of the gluon saturation effects at early time for the in-medium jet energy loss.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that gluon saturation effects at early times in heavy-ion collisions will remain visible in photon-jet correlations at forward rapidities even after the jet has undergone significant in-medium energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, provided the saturation and Sudakov parameters are tuned for the ALICE FoCal detector acceptance (pseudorapidity range 3.4 < η < 5.8).

## Method

The authors combine the Improved Transverse Momentum Dependent (ITMD) factorization framework to describe early-time gluon saturation with the BDIM evolution equation for in-medium parton interactions in the quark-gluon plasma. Hard scattering events are generated using the KATIE Monte Carlo program, while in-medium parton evolution including vacuum-like emissions (VLE) and medium-induced radiation is simulated using TMDICE. The VLE stage follows DGLAP splitting functions until the decoherence time becomes shorter than the branching time, after which coherent BDMPS-Z medium-induced emissions take over.

## Key numbers

- The FoCal detector covers pseudorapidities 3.4 < η < 5.8, while ATLAS FCal covers 3.1 < η < 4.9 and CMS HF covers 3.0 < η < 5.2
- The nuclear modification factor for isolated photons in PbPb collisions was measured as R_AA = 0.99 ± 0.31 (stat.) ± 0.26 (syst.)
- The decoherence time is estimated as t_decoh ≈ (12/(q̂ θ_ij^2))^(1/3), where θ_ij is the branching angle and q̂ is the average transverse momentum transfer per unit length
- The branching time is t_br ≈ 2 E_i / Q_i^2, where E_i and Q_i are the energy and virtuality of the jet particle
- The critical angle for resolved splittings inside the medium is θ_c = √(12/(q̂ L^3))
- The medium energy scale from multiple scattering is ω_c = q̂ L^2 / 2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of gluon saturation in pA collisions (Marquet 2007, Kutak 2012, van Hameren 2023) to the AA collision system, where jet-medium interactions in the quark-gluon plasma must be accounted for. It builds on the BDIM evolution framework (Blaizot 2012, 2013; Blanco 2021) and the ITMD formalism (Kotko 2015, Altinoluk 2019) to provide predictions specifically for the upcoming ALICE FoCal detector. The results enable experimental searches for saturation effects in heavy-ion collisions at forward rapidities, where previous studies at mid-rapidity found saturation effects to be small.
