# REAL-TIME DESCRIPTION OF PARTON-HADRON CONVERSION AND CONFINEMENT DYNAMICS

> We propose a new and universal approach to the hadronization problem that incorporates both partonic and hadronic degrees of freedom in their respective domains of relevance, and that describes the conversion between them within a kinetic field theory formulation in real time and full 7-dimensional phase space. We construct a scale-dependent effective theory that reduces to perturbative QCD with its scale and chiral symmetry properties at short space-time distances, but at large distances (r > 1 fm) yields symmetry breaking gluon and quark condensates plus hadronic excitations. The approach is applied to the evolution of fragmenting qq~ and gg jet pairs as the system evolves from the initial 2-jet configuration, via parton showering and cluster formation, to the final yield of hadrons. The phenomenological implications for e+e- -> hadrons are investigated, such as the time scale of the transition, and its energy dependence, cluster size and mass distributions. We compare our results for particle production and Bose-Einstein correlations with experimental data, and find an interesting possibility of extracting the basic parameters of the space-time evolution of the system from Bose enhancement measurements.

## Key claim

The authors propose a universal dynamical approach to hadronization that treats parton-hadron conversion as a real-time process governed by a scale-dependent effective field theory, rather than relying on ad hoc fragmentation prescriptions. The key hedge is that the approach assumes the QCD vacuum can be modeled as a "color dielectric medium" characterized by collective scalar gluon condensate and pseudoscalar quark condensate fields, with the confinement transition occurring when a local energy density condition is no longer satisfied.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-dependent effective Lagrangian that interpolates between perturbative QCD at short distances (r << 1 fm) and a non-perturbative regime with gluon and quark condensates plus hadronic excitations at large distances (r > 1 fm), using a color-dielectric function κ(χ) that vanishes in the long-distance limit to enforce confinement. They derive transport equations from the Dyson-Schwinger equations of the effective field theory and apply them to simulate the real-time evolution of fragmenting qq̅ and gg jet pairs from e⁺e⁻ annihilation, following the system through parton showering, cluster formation, and hadronic cluster decay.

- The quark condensate Q₀ is given by ⟨0| q̅q |0⟩ = c (χ/χ₀)³ U₀.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Ellis and Geiger, 1994) and the color-dielectric model of Fai and collaborators (1988) and Krein and collaborators (1991) by providing a fully dynamical, real-time formulation of confinement and hadron formation. It contrasts with static approaches such as perturbative QCD (limited to short distances), effective chiral models, QCD sum rules (Shifman, 1979), and the parton fragmentation model of Webber (1986), while noting that lattice QCD (Karsch, 1989) cannot yet perform dynamical calculations of parton-hadron conversion. The approach enables future study of the QCD phase transition in the early Universe and in high-energy heavy-ion collisions where a deconfined quark-gluon plasma is expected.
