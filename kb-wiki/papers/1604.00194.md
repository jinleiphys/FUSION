# Effect of particle fluctuation on isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio of nuclear multifragmentation

> Isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio parameters are compared from canonical and grand canonical ensembles when applied to multifragmentation of finite nuclei. Source dependence of isoscaling parameters and source and isospin dependence of isobaric yield ratio parameters are examined in the framework of the canonical and the grand canonical models. It is found that as the nucleus fragments more, results from both the ensembles converge and observables calculated from the canonical ensemble coincide more with those obtained from the formulae derived using the grand canonical ensemble.

# Effect of particle fluctuation on isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio of nuclear multifragmentation

## Key claim

The isoscaling and isobaric yield ratio formulae derived from the grand canonical ensemble do not hold exactly for canonical ensemble calculations of finite nuclei; the deviation is largest for small, highly asymmetric fragmenting sources and diminishes as source size increases or asymmetry decreases, because particle-number fluctuations in the grand canonical ensemble become negligible when fragmentation is more extensive.

## Method

The authors performed canonical and grand canonical statistical model calculations of nuclear multifragmentation for finite sources at fixed temperature (5 MeV) and freeze-out volume (3V₀). Fragment multiplicities were computed using the canonical recursion relation of Das et al. and the grand canonical chemical-potential method of Chaudhuri et al., with the same liquid-drop input (volume energy 15.8 MeV, surface energy σ₀=18.0 MeV, symmetry energy 23.5 MeV, Coulomb parameter a_c=0.72 MeV). Isoscaling parameters α and β were extracted by linear fits of ln(R₂₁) versus N (at constant Z) and versus Z (at constant N), and isobaric yield ratio parameters c and m were obtained from linear fits of ln R[1,-1,A] versus A^(2/3); these were compared between ensembles and against the grand-canonical formulae.

## Key numbers

- Temperature fixed at 5 MeV; freeze-out volume fixed at 3V₀.
- Source size A₀ varied from 44 to 264 (Z₀ from 16 to 96); asymmetry parameter y = (N₀−Z₀)/(N₀+Z₀) varied from 0 to 0.33.
- For isoscaling, source pairs had y = 0.27 (neutron-rich) and y = 0.11 (less neutron-rich); α and β were averaged over fragment Z=1 to 8 and N=1 to 8.
- For isobaric yield ratio, the slope m = a*_c/T and intercept c = Δμ/T were compared; a*_c = a_c[1−(V₀/V_f)^(1/3)] with a_c = 0.72 MeV.
- Chemical potentials from canonical and grand canonical models were "almost equal" for all source sizes and asymmetries, with slight differences only for very small sources and high y.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of statistical ensembles for nuclear multifragmentation previously studied for mass distributions by Mallik et al. (2014) to the specific observables of isoscaling and isobaric yield ratios. It directly tests the validity of the grand-canonical-derived formulae of Tsang et al. (2001) and Huang et al. (2010) when applied to finite, conserved systems described by the canonical model of Das et al. (2005). The results enable experimentalists to quantify systematic uncertainties when extracting symmetry-energy coefficients from multifragmentation data using these methods, particularly for small or highly asymmetric sources.
