# Mass Spectrum of Non-Charmed and Charmed Meson States in Extended Linear-Sigma Model

> The mass spectrum of different meson particles is generated using an effective Lagrangian of the extended linear-sigma model (eLSM) for scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields and quark flavors, up, down, strange, and charm. Analytical formulas for the masses of scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector meson states are derived assuming global chiral symmetry. The various eLSM parameters are analytically deduced and numerically computed. This enables accurate estimations of the masses of sixteen non-charmed and thirteen charmed meson states at vanishing temperature. The comparison of these results to recent compilation of the particle data group (PDG) allows to draw the conclusion that the masses of sixteen non-charmed and thirteen charmed meson states calculated in the eLSM are in good agreement with the PDG. This shows that the eLSM, with its configurations and parameters, is an effective theoretical framework for determining the mass spectra of various non-charmed and charmed meson states, particularly at vanishing temperature.

## Key claim

The extended linear-sigma model (eLSM) with four quark flavors and global chiral symmetry can accurately reproduce the masses of sixteen non-charmed and thirteen charmed meson states at vanishing temperature, with the results showing good agreement with Particle Data Group (PDG) compilations, though the paper acknowledges that some mesons, particularly pions, exhibit large standard deviations.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian for the mesonic sector of the eLSM with SU(4) flavor symmetry, including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector meson fields along with scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs, anomaly terms, and explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms. Analytical mass formulas were derived for each meson type from the Lagrangian, and the model parameters were determined by fitting to known meson masses, allowing calculation of the mass spectra for both non-charmed and charmed meson states at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Sixteen non-charmed meson states and thirteen charmed meson states were calculated and compared to PDG data
- The pion mass formula includes wavefunction renormalization factor Z_π and depends on condensates σ_x, σ_y, σ_c with parameters m_0, λ_1, λ_2
- The charmed scalar meson χ_c0 mass is given by m²_χ_c0 = m_0² + λ_1[σ_x² + σ_y²] + 3(λ_1 + λ_2)σ_c² + 2ε_c, where ε_c = m_c² = (1/2)[m²_χ_c0 - m_0² - λ_1(σ_x² + σ_y²) - 3σ_c²(λ_1 + λ_2)]
- The D meson mass formula includes an explicit charm quark mass term ε_c and depends on condensates σ_x, σ_y, σ_c with wavefunction renormalization factor Z_D

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the linear-sigma model framework, including the SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model studies by Tawfik (2014, 2016, 2019, 2021) that analyzed thermodynamics, phase structure, and meson masses in thermal and dense medium, and the SU(4) linear-sigma model work by Diab (2016) on quark-hadron phase structure. The results enable future calculations of meson masses in thermal and dense medium using the eLSM parameters determined at vanishing temperature, and provide a systematic comparison with the PDG compilation by Particle Data Group (2022).
