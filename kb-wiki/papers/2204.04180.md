# Transport coefficients of heavy quarkonia comparing with heavy quark coefficients

> We revisit the transport coefficients of heavy quarkonia moving in high-temperature QCD plasmas. The thermal width and mass shift for heavy quarkonia are closely related to the momentum diffusion coefficient and its dispersive counterpart for heavy quarks, respectively. For quarkonium at rest in plasmas the longitudinal gluon part of the color-singlet self-energy diagram is sufficient to determine the leading-order thermal width, whereas the momentum dependence is obtained from the transverse gluon channel. Using the quarkonium-gluon effective vertex based on the dipole interaction of color charges, we discuss the damping rate, the effective rest and kinetic mass shifts of slowly moving quarkonia and compare with the corresponding coefficients of heavy quarks.

# Transport coefficients of heavy quarkonia comparing with heavy quark coefficients

## Key claim

The thermal width and mass shift of heavy quarkonia moving in high-temperature QCD plasmas are related to the momentum diffusion coefficient and its dispersive counterpart for heavy quarks, respectively, but the transverse gluon contribution makes the velocity dependence of quarkonium transport coefficients differ from those of heavy quarks beyond leading order in velocity.

## Method

The authors compute the thermal width of heavy quarkonia at leading order in a weak-coupling expansion using the quarkonium-gluon effective vertex derived from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude via the dipole interaction of color charges, with hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagators in Coulomb gauge. The thermal mass shift is evaluated at leading order (LO) from hard momentum scales and at next-to-leading order (NLO) from soft momentum transfer using dimensional regularization. The damping rate is obtained from the spectral density derived from a Schrödinger-type equation with a complex potential.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order thermal width for a Coulombic 1S bound state at rest: $\Gamma^{\rm 1S}(v=0) \sim g^4 T^3 a_0^2$, where $a_0$ is the Bohr radius
- Leading-log thermal width for moving quarkonium: $\Gamma^{\rm 1S}(v) \simeq \frac{(N_c^2-1)g^4T^3a_0^2}{8\pi d_\Upsilon v} \left(1+\frac{N_f}{2N_c}\right) \ln\frac{1+v}{1-v} \, \ln\frac{T}{m_D}$
- Leading-order thermal mass shift: $\delta m_{\rm LO}^{\rm 1S} = -\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\zeta(3)\left(N_f+\frac{4}{3}N_c\right)g^4T^3a_0^2$
- Next-to-leading order mass shift for small velocity: $\delta m_{\rm NLO}^{\rm 1S}(v) = \frac{3(N_c^2-1)g^2m_D^3a_0^2}{16\pi N_c d_\Upsilon}\left[1 - \frac{1}{2}\left(1+\frac{\pi^2}{16}\right)v^2\right] + \mathcal{O}(v^4)$
- For small $v$: $\frac{\Gamma(v)}{\Gamma(v=0)} = \frac{\kappa(v)}{\kappa(v=0)} = 1+\frac{v^2}{3}+\mathcal{O}(v^4)$, where $\kappa$ is the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient
- Damping rate: $\frac{\Gamma}{2} \simeq \frac{g^2a_0^2}{3\pi}\left(1+\frac{v^2}{3}\right) m_D^2T\ln\frac{T}{m_D}$
- Heavy quark mass shift: $\delta m_{HQ} = -\frac{C_Fg^2m_D}{8\pi} + \frac{1}{2}\left(1-\frac{\pi^2}{16}\right)\frac{C_Fg^2m_D}{24\pi}v^2$

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of quarkonium thermal width and mass shift by Brambilla et al. (2008, 2013, 2016) and the heavy quark diffusion coefficient by Moore and Teaney (2004) to include the velocity dependence of quarkonium transport coefficients. The authors compare their results for moving quarkonia with the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient and damping rate computed by Pisarski (1993) and Thoma (1990). The analysis enables a systematic understanding of how quarkonium transport coefficients differ from heavy quark coefficients in different kinematical regimes, and can supplement lattice QCD computations of the heavy quark-antiquark potential.
