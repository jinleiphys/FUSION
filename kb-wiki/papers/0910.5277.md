# Effect of Tensor Correlations on Single-particle and Collective States

> We study the effect of tensor correlations on single-particle and collective states within Skyrme Hartree-Fock and RPA model. Firstly, We study the role of tensor interactions in Skyrme effective interaction on the spin-orbit splittings of N=82 isotones and Z=50 isotope. The isospin dependence of the shell structure is well described as the results of the tensor interactions without destroying good properties of the binding energy and the rms charge radii of the heavy nuclei. Secondly, We performed self-consistent HF+RPA calculations for charge exchange 1$^+$ states in $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb to elucidate the role of tensor interactions on spin dependent excitations. It is pointed out that Gamow-Teller(GT) states can couple strongly with the spin-quadrupole (SQ) 1$^+$ states in the high energy region above E$_x$=30 MeV due to the tensor interactions. As the result of this coupling, more than 10% of the GT strength is shifted to the energy region above 30 MeV, and the main GT peak is moved 2 MeV downward.

## Key claim

Tensor correlations within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus RPA model can shift more than 10% of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength to excitation energies above 30 MeV and move the main GT peak downward by about 2 MeV, but only when the tensor terms are included in both the HF mean field and the RPA residual interaction; including them only in HF does not produce this effect.

## Method

The author performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) plus BCS calculations for single-particle states in N=82 isotones and Z=50 isotopes, and charge-exchange RPA calculations for 1+ states in 90Zr and 208Pb. The tensor terms (triplet-even and triplet-odd) were added to the SLy5 Skyrme parameterization, with coupling constants T and U fitted to reproduce experimental spin-orbit splittings. Three calculation types were compared: no tensor terms (00), tensor terms in HF only (10), and tensor terms in both HF and RPA (11).

## Key numbers

- Optimal tensor parameters: α_T = -170 MeV·fm^5 and β_T = 100 MeV·fm^5, fitted to N=82 isotone and Z=50 isotope data.
- For 90Zr with tensor terms in both HF and RPA (case 11): the non-energy weighted sum rule (NEWSR) in the 0-30 MeV region is 27.00, and in the 30-60 MeV region is 2.89; the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in 0-30 MeV is 366.9 MeV, and in 30-60 MeV is 122 MeV.
- For 208Pb with tensor terms in both HF and RPA (case 11): the NEWSR in 0-30 MeV is 114.10, and in 30-60 MeV is 16.58; the EWSR in 0-30 MeV is 1658 MeV, and in 30-60 MeV is 694 MeV.
- In 90Zr, the centroid of GT strength below 30 MeV moves downward by about 1 MeV when tensor terms are included in both HF and RPA, compared to the no-tensor case.
- In 208Pb, the centroid of GT strength below 30 MeV moves downward by about 1.5 MeV, and the higher energy peak at Ex ~18 MeV moves downward by about 3.3 MeV, when tensor terms are included in both HF and RPA.
- About 10% of the NEWSR is shifted from the 0-30 MeV region to the 30-60 MeV region by tensor correlations in the 1p-1h RPA.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Stancu et al. (1977) on tensor interactions in Hartree-Fock calculations, and by Otsuka (2005) on shell evolution in exotic nuclei. It provides the first self-consistent HF+RPA treatment of tensor terms for spin-isospin excitations, following the introduction of tensor terms in Skyrme parameterizations by Brown (2006), Colo (2007), and Brink (2007). The results show that tensor correlations can shift GT strength to high energy within the 1p-1h model space, a mechanism previously attributed only to 2p-2h couplings as studied by Bertsch (1980). This enables future studies of tensor effects on spin-dipole and other spin-dependent excitation modes.
