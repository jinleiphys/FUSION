# Nuclear Structure Uncertainties in Parity-Violating Electron Scattering from Carbon 12

> High precision measurements of the parity-violating asymmetry in polarized electron scattering from nuclei can be used to extract information on nuclear and nucleon structure or to determine Standard Model couplings and higher-order radiative corrections. To this end, low uncertainties are also required in the effects that inevitably arise from modeling the underlying nuclear structure. An experimental precision of a few tenths of a percent may be attainable for the asymmetry if the appropriate kinematic range is chosen, as will be discussed here for the case of $^{12}$C. And given this, the dual goal of ascertaining both the sizes of various nuclear structure related effects and of providing estimates of their uncertainties for this particular target will be discussed.

## Key claim

For elastic parity-violating electron scattering from \(^{12}\)C, the theoretical uncertainty from nuclear structure effects can be kept below the target goal of 0.3% in the asymmetry, provided the appropriate kinematic range is chosen; however, the current experimental uncertainty on the strangeness content of the nucleon introduces a larger spread of 0.5–1.5% and must be reduced by restricting measurements to low momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors computed the parity-violating asymmetry within the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) for elastic scattering of polarized electrons from \(^{12}\)C at incident energies of 150, 300, and 500 MeV. They modeled Coulomb distortion effects using a three-parameter Fermi charge distribution, and estimated isospin mixing in the nuclear ground state via Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing, varying the Skyrme parametrization, pairing strength, and axial deformation. The impact of nucleon strangeness form factors was evaluated using parametrized electric and magnetic strangeness content parameters \(\rho_s\) and \(\mu_s\) taken from the experimental range.

## Key numbers

* The reference parity-violating asymmetry for \(^{12}\)C in plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) is \(A^0 \cong 3.22 \times 10^{-6} |Q^2|\) when the momentum transfer is given in fm\(^{-1}\).
* The Coulomb distortion effect on the asymmetry at 150 MeV incident energy is about 3%, with a theoretical spread of only 0.01% from varying the charge distribution parameters.
* The isospin-mixing deviation of the asymmetry is 0.3–0.5% in the region of interest, with a theoretical spread below 0.1% across different Skyrme forces, pairing strengths, and axial deformations.
* The strangeness-induced deviation of the asymmetry ranges from 0.2% to 1% in the region of interest, with a spread of 0.5–1.5% from the current experimental range of \(\rho_s\) and \(\mu_s\).
* The target goal for both statistical and theoretical uncertainty in the asymmetry is 0.3% (i.e., \(\Delta A/A < 0.003\)).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Donnelly (1989) and Feil (1975) for parity-violating electron scattering to quantify nuclear structure uncertainties for \(^{12}\)C, motivated by proposed low-energy, high-luminosity facilities such as MESA at Mainz (Aulenbacher, 2011) and an upgraded FEL at Jefferson Lab (Neil, 2000). The study shows that Coulomb distortion and isospin-mixing uncertainties are manageable, but that the dominant theoretical uncertainty comes from the poorly known strangeness content of the nucleon, as constrained by experiments such as HAPPEX-He (Acha, 2007) and reviewed by Gonzalez-Jimenez (2013). The results enable a path forward for extracting Standard Model couplings or higher-order radiative corrections from a precision measurement of the asymmetry on \(^{12}\)C, provided the kinematic region is chosen to keep the strangeness uncertainty below 0.3%.
