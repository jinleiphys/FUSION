# Analytic Formulae for the Matrix Elements of the Transition Operators in the Symplectic Extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model

> The tensor properties of all the generators of Sp(12,R) - the group of dynamical symmetry of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), are given with respect to the reduction chain Sp(12,R) $\supset $ U(6) $\supset $ U(3) x U(2) $\supset $ O(3) x U(1). Matrix elements of the basic building blocks of the model are evaluated in symmetry adapted basis along the considered chain. As a result of this, the analytic form of the matrix elements of any operator in the enveloping algebra of the Sp(12,R), defining a certain transition operator, can be calculated. The procedure allows further applications of the symplectic IVBM for the description of transition probabilities between nuclear collective states.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytic formulae for the matrix elements of transition operators in the symplectic extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), but only for the specific rotational limit defined by the reduction chain Sp(12,R) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ U(3) × U(2) ⊃ O(3) × U(1), and only for basis states with even total boson number N.

## Method

The authors classified all generators of Sp(12,R) as irreducible tensor operators with respect to the subgroup chain Sp(12,R) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ U(3) × U(2) ⊃ O(3) × U(1), expressing them in terms of creation and annihilation operators of two types of vector bosons (p and n bosons). They constructed symmetry-adapted basis states as symmetric tensor products of raising operators applied to the boson vacuum, and used the Wigner-Eckart theorem in two steps (SU(3) → O(3) and SU(2) → U(1)) to factor the matrix elements. The triple-barred reduced matrix elements were then evaluated using explicit actions of the boson creation and annihilation operators on the SU(3) basis states labeled by the quantum numbers (λ, μ) and total boson number N.

## Key numbers

- The total number of bosons operator is given by N̂ = (1/√2) Σ_m C^{00}_{1m 1-m} (p_m⁺ p_{-m} + n_m⁺ n_{-m})
- The SU(3) quantum numbers are related to boson numbers by λ = N_p - N_n, μ = N_n, N = λ + 2μ
- The dimension of an SU(3) irrep (λ, μ) is dim(λ, μ) = (1/2)(λ + μ + 2)(λ + 1)(μ + 1)
- The action of p_i⁺ on a state |[N];(λ,μ)⟩ gives a factor √[(2μ+λ+1)(λ+μ+2)(λ+1)/((λ+μ+3)(λ+2))] times |[N+1];(λ+1,μ)⟩
- The action of n_i⁺ on a state |[N];(λ,μ)⟩ gives a factor √[(2μ+λ+1)(λ+μ+2)(λ+1)(μ+1)/(λ(λ+μ+1)(μ+2))] times |[N+1];(λ-1,μ+1)⟩
- The action of p_i on a state |[N];(λ,μ)⟩ gives a factor √[(2μ+λ)(λ+μ+2)(λ+1)/(λ(λ+μ+1))] times |[N-1];(λ-1,μ)⟩
- The action of n_i on a state |[N];(λ,μ)⟩ gives a factor √[(2μ+λ)(λ+1)(μ+1)/((λ+2)μ)] times |[N-1];(λ+1,μ-1)⟩

## Context

This work extends the algebraic IVBM previously developed for low-lying bands of well-deformed even-even nuclei (Ganev et al., earlier work) and the symplectic extension Sp(12,R) that was applied to describe positive and negative parity bands (Ganev et al., 2004). The paper provides the mathematical tools needed to calculate transition probabilities, which were not previously available for this model, enabling future comparison with experimental data on collective nuclear states. The approach differs from the SU(3) construction in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and the Sp(6,R) symplectic model (Rowe) by using two kinds of vector bosons to build the SU(3) representations.
