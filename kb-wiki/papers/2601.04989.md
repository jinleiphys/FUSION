# Effective Range Expansion with the Left-Hand Cut: Higher Order Improvements

> A model-independent parameterization of the low-energy scattering amplitude that incorporates the left-hand cut from one-particle exchange, an extension of the conventional effective-range expansion (ERE), was recently proposed and successfully applied to the low-energy $DD^*$ system [Phys. Rev. Lett. 135, 011903 (2025)]. While the original formulation is based on a nonrelativistic approximation and is thus limited to a [1,1] approximant for self-consistency, we extend the framework by explicitly including the higher-order terms up to $\mathcal{O}(k^6)$. We systematically investigate the reliability and robustness of the generalized ERE by incorporating relativistic kinematic effects. In addition, we develop a relativistic version of the ERE that accounts for lhc contributions. These results affirm the generalized ERE as a robust and systematically improvable framework for near-threshold scattering processes, providing both analytical and numerical reliability for applications in two-body scattering problems with a particle exchange.

## Key claim
The paper extends the generalized effective-range expansion (ERE) that incorporates left-hand cut (lhc) contributions from one-particle exchange to higher orders up to \(\mathcal{O}(k^6)\), showing that the framework is systematically improvable and robust for near-threshold scattering, but only when the higher-order terms in the lhc functions are explicitly included and relativistic kinematic effects are accounted for.

## Method
The authors derived explicit expressions for the u-channel lhc functions \(L_u(k^2)\) and \(d_u^R(k^2)\) up to \(\mathcal{O}(k^6)\) by expanding the Mandelstam variable \(u\) in powers of \(k^2\) and retaining only the near-threshold branch points. They then used the \(N/D\) method to construct a generalized ERE parameterization with polynomials \(\tilde{n}(k^2)\), \(\tilde{d}(k^2)\), and a constant \(\tilde{g}\) encoding the lhc coupling strength, and tested this parameterization against numerical solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) for both t-channel and u-channel Yukawa potentials. A relativistic version of the ERE with lhc contributions was also developed using the full relativistic expressions for \(L_t(k^2)\) and \(L_u(k^2)\).

## Key numbers
- For the t-channel Yukawa potential with \(m_1 = m_2 = 1\ \text{GeV}\) and \(m_{\mathrm{ex}} = 0.6\ \text{GeV}\), the [0,1] approximant with three parameters (\(\tilde{d}_0\), \(\tilde{d}_1\), \(\tilde{g}\)) reproduced LSE phase shifts for couplings \(g = -20, -10, 5, 13, 20, 40\) over the range \(k^2 \in [-0.3, 0.5]\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For \(g = 40\), the bound state binding energy was approximately \(1\ \text{GeV}\); for \(g = 20\), the binding energy was \(71\ \text{MeV}\); for \(g = 13\), the binding energy was \(0.1\ \text{MeV}\).
- For the u-channel case with \(m_1 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_2 = 0.6\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_{\mathrm{ex}} = 0.5\ \text{GeV}\), and \(g = -50\), the deviation at the lhc branch point decreased from \(\mathcal{O}(1\ \text{MeV})\) at \(\mathcal{O}(k^2)\) to nearly undetectable levels at \(\mathcal{O}(k^6)\).
- The \(\mathcal{O}(k^4)\) u-channel parameterization with five parameters (\(\tilde{d}_0\), \(\tilde{d}_1\), \(\tilde{n}_1\), \(\tilde{n}_2\), \(\tilde{g}_0\)) showed significantly improved accuracy over the \(\mathcal{O}(k^2)\) version.

## Context
This work extends the generalized ERE framework proposed by Du et al. (2024) and Du et al. (2025), which was limited to a [1,1] approximant due to a nonrelativistic approximation for u-channel exchanges. The higher-order expansion enables more precise descriptions applicable to high-quality data over a broader energy range, and the relativistic version provides a kinematically complete treatment. The framework is relevant for analyzing lattice QCD data and experimental scattering data for systems such as \(DD^*\), baryon-baryon, \(B^*B^{(*)}\), \(ND^*\), and \(\Sigma_{(c)}^{(*)} D^*\) scattering, where one-particle exchange generates near-threshold left-hand cuts.
