# Leading order, next-to-leading order, and non-perturbative parton collision kernels: effects in static and evolving media

> Energetic partons traveling in a strongly interacting medium lose energy by emitting radiation and through collisions with medium constituents. Non-perturbative, next-to-leading order and leading order collision kernels are implemented within AMY-McGill formalism. The resulting gluon emission rates are then evaluated and compared by considering scattering occurring in a brick of quark-gluon plasma, as well as in a realistic simulation of Pb-Pb, collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ ATeV using MARTINI. We find that the variations in quenching of hard partons resulting from using different kernels can be important, depending on the overall value of the strong coupling constant $α_s$.

## Key claim

The variations in hard parton quenching resulting from using leading-order (LO), next-to-leading-order (NLO), and non-perturbative (NP) collision kernels can be large when evaluated at the same value of the strong coupling constant αs, but these differences are largely absorbed by rescaling αs as a fitting parameter in phenomenological analyses.

## Method

The authors implemented LO, NLO, and NP parton collision kernels within the AMY-McGill formalism to compute gluon emission rates, solving the integral equation for the function F(h,p,k) that appears in the radiative energy-loss rates. They compared the resulting rates using a "brick test" (an idealized static QGP medium of infinite extent at constant temperature) and then embedded the rates in the MARTINI event generator coupled with MUSIC (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for realistic simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at √s=2.76 ATeV. The initial hard parton distributions were generated by PYTHIA 8.209 with CTEQ 6L1 proton PDFs and nCTEQ15-np nuclear PDFs, with initial conditions from IP-GLASMA.

## Key numbers

- For gluon emission from a gluon at p ~ 10 T, the NP curve is approximately 60% above the LO result while the NLO curve is nearly double the LO rate when using the same αs value
- With scaled coupling, NLO and NP results at p ~ 10 T are approximately 40% above the LO curve for g → g+g
- In the brick test with αs = 0.3, the NLO curve for charged hadron RAA is approximately 75% of the LO result (averaged over the pT range), while the NP curve stands at approximately 88% of the LO result
- Fitted αs values from χ² minimization: LO = 0.280, NLO = 0.242, NP = 0.260
- Ratio of fitted couplings: αs^NLO/αs^LO = 0.86, αs^NP/αs^LO = 0.93
- The scaling factors from the brick test were αs^NLO = √0.66 × αs^LO and αs^NP = √0.85 × αs^LO
- The pp baseline calculation agrees with experimental data within 25%

## Context

This work extends the AMY formalism for jet-medium interactions by implementing the NLO scattering kernel computed by Caron-Huot (2008) and the NP scattering kernel computed on the lattice by Moore (2019) for EQCD and matched to (3+1)D QCD by Moore (2021). It builds on prior studies by Schlichting (2021) that found significant differences between NP and LO rates in infinite medium calculations. The results enable quantitative comparison of different scattering kernels in realistic hydrodynamic simulations, showing that while intrinsic differences between kernels can be large (up to 100% for some moment distributions), they can be largely absorbed by rescaling αs in phenomenological fits, though some residual differences remain at low pT where elastic scattering channels dominate.
