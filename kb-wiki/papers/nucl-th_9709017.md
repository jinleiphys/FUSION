# A relativistic dynamical model for pi-N scattering

> We present a unitary relativistic quasi-potential model for describing the low-energy pion-nucleon interaction, based on the equal time Bethe-Salpeter equation. It preserves the covariant structure of a relativistic spin 1/2 particle for the nucleon propagator, to be contrasted to other quasi-potential approximations.

## Key claim

The equal-time (instantaneous) approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation preserves the covariant structure of a relativistic spin-1/2 particle for the nucleon propagator, unlike other quasi-potential approximations which violate the symmetry between positive and negative energy-state renormalization and thus break relativistic covariance.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the equal-time (instantaneous) approximation for low-energy pion-nucleon scattering. The potential includes tree-level exchanges of sigma, rho(770), nucleon(938), Delta(1232), and Roper-resonance, with bare vertices and propagators derived from an effective Lagrangian. The model parameters are fitted to reproduce elastic pion-nucleon phase shifts, and the Delta and Roper widths are generated dynamically through the scattering equation rather than being input as widths.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_piNN^2/4pi = 13.5
* Delta(1232) mass: m_Delta = 1.24 GeV, with cutoff Lambda_Delta = 1.4 GeV
* Roper resonance mass: m_N* = 1.54 GeV, with coupling g_piNN*^2/4pi = 0.9
* Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 0.55 GeV, with coupling g_sigmaNN g_sigmapipi/4pi = 0.1
* Rho meson mass: m_rho = 0.77 GeV, with coupling g_rhoNN g_rhopipi/4pi = 2.5 and kappa_rho = 3.7
* Nucleon cutoff: Lambda_N = 1.5 GeV; pion cutoff: Lambda_pi = 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter approach to pion-nucleon scattering, contrasting with other quasi-potential approximations such as the spectator approximation (Gross and Surya, 1993) and the Pearce-Jennings (1991) scheme, which the authors show violate the symmetry between positive and negative energy-state renormalization. The model enables a consistent relativistic treatment of pion-nucleon dynamics that preserves covariance and charge conjugation symmetry, and can be used as a foundation for studying higher-energy processes or including additional channels.
