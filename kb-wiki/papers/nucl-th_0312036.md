# Polarization phenomena in hyperon-nucleon scattering

> We investigate polarization observables in hyperon-nucleon scattering by decomposing scattering amplitudes into spin-space tensors, where each component describes scattering by corresponding spin-dependent interactions, so that contributions of the interactions in the observables are individually identified. In this way, for elastic scattering we find some linear combinations of the observables sensitive to particular spin-dependent interactions such as symmetric spin-orbit (LS) interactions and antisymmetric LS ones. These will be useful to criticize theoretical predictions of the interactions when the relevant observables are measured. We treat vector analyzing powers, depolarizations, and coefficients of polarization transfers and spin correlations, a part of which is numerically examined in $Σ^{+} p$ scattering as an example. Total cross sections are studied for polarized beams and targets as well as for unpolarized ones to investigate spin dependence of imaginary parts of forward scattering amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that linear combinations of polarization observables in hyperon-nucleon elastic scattering can isolate contributions from specific spin-dependent interactions (symmetric spin-orbit, antisymmetric spin-orbit, spin-spin, and tensor), but this sensitivity is conditional on the scalar amplitudes being dominant over vector and tensor amplitudes in certain combinations.

## Method

The authors decompose the T-matrix for two spin-1/2 particle scattering into spin-space tensors of rank 0 (scalar), rank 1 (vector), and rank 2 (tensor), relating these to conventional interaction forms: central, spin-spin, symmetric spin-orbit (SLS), antisymmetric spin-orbit (ALS), and tensor. They then derive analytic expressions for vector analyzing powers, depolarizations, polarization transfer coefficients, spin correlation coefficients, and spin-dependent total cross sections in terms of these decomposed amplitudes, and test the predictions numerically for Sigma+ p scattering using the Nijmegen NSC97 potential models at 170 and 450 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- At p_Sigma+ = 170 MeV/c, the magnitude of the spin-spin scalar amplitude U_beta is much larger than that of the spin-independent scalar amplitude U_alpha and of the vector and tensor amplitudes, except near cos(theta) ~ 1 where Coulomb scattering dominates U_alpha.
- At p_Sigma+ = 450 MeV/c, the magnitudes of U_alpha, U_beta, and the tensor amplitudes are comparable.
- The "total" cross section for Sigma+ p scattering is defined with cos(theta_min) = -0.5 and cos(theta_max) unspecified, integrated over that angular range.
- The longitudinal asymmetry Delta_sigma_L and transverse asymmetry Delta_sigma_T determine the imaginary parts of U_beta and T_1 at forward angles via: Im(U_beta(theta=0)) = -(k/(6 pi)) (Delta_sigma_L + 2 Delta_sigma_T) and Im(T_1(theta=0)) = -(sqrt(2) k/(4 sqrt(3) pi)) (Delta_sigma_L - Delta_sigma_T).

## Context

This work extends the spin-tensor decomposition method previously applied to nucleon-deuteron scattering by Ishikawa et al. (2001, 2002, 2003) to the hyperon-nucleon system, where the antisymmetric spin-orbit interaction (absent in nucleon-nucleon scattering due to particle identity) becomes active. The approach enables critical tests of boson-exchange models (e.g., Holzenkamp et al. 1989, Rijken et al. 1999) and quark-cluster models (e.g., Fujiwara et al. 1996, Takeuchi et al. 2000) once the relevant polarization observables are measured experimentally, building on recent asymmetry measurements for polarized Sigma+ and Lambda on protons by Kadyk et al. (2002).
