# X(1835): A Possible Baryonium?

> We point out that (1) the large $p\bar p$ coupling and suppressed mesonic coupling of X(1835) and (2) the suppression of the three-body strange final states strongly indicate that X(1835) may be a $p\bar p$ baryonium. We also point out that the branching ratio of $X(1835)\toηππ$ should be bigger than that of $X(1835)\toη^\prime ππ$. If BES further confirms the non-observation of X(1835) in the $ηππ$ channel, that will be very puzzling. Finally, X(1835) may be used a tetraquark generator if X(1835) is really established as a baryonium state.

# X(1835): A Possible Baryonium?

## Key claim

The large $p\bar p$ coupling and suppressed mesonic coupling of X(1835), together with the suppression of three-body strange final states, strongly indicate that X(1835) may be a $p\bar p$ baryonium; however, the non-observation of X(1835) in the $\eta\pi\pi$ channel is puzzling because symmetry arguments predict the branching ratio of $X(1835)\to\eta\pi\pi$ should be larger than that of $X(1835)\to\eta^\prime\pi\pi$.

## Method

The authors analyzed the effective coupling constants of X(1835) to $p\bar p$ and to $\eta^\prime\pi\pi$ using the measured branching ratios from BES, extracting $\alpha_{X p\bar p}$ from a Breit-Wigner integral and $\alpha_{X \eta^\prime \pi\pi}$ from an S-wave decay formula. They then applied SU(3) flavor symmetry and $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing with mixing angle $\theta\approx -\pi/9$ to derive the expected ratio of $X(1835)\to\eta\pi\pi$ to $X(1835)\to\eta^\prime\pi\pi$ decay widths, and compared this prediction with the preliminary BES observation of X(1835) in $\eta^\prime\pi\pi$ but not in $\eta\pi\pi$.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(1835): $(1833.7\pm 6.2\pm 2.7)$ MeV, width $(67.7\pm 20.3\pm 7.7)$ MeV, from BES in the $J/\psi \to \gamma \eta^\prime \pi^+\pi^-$ channel with $264\pm 54$ events and $7.7\sigma$ significance.
- $BR(J/\psi \to \gamma X)\cdot BR(X\to p\bar p) \sim 7\times 10^{-5}$ from BES measurement.
- Extracted coupling $\alpha_{X p\bar p} = g_{X p\bar p}^2/(4\pi) \approx 1.0$, assuming $BR(X\to p\bar p)\sim 10\%$.
- Extracted mesonic coupling $\alpha_{X \eta^\prime \pi\pi} \approx 0.05$, assuming $\Gamma(X\to \eta^\prime \pi^+\pi^-)\approx 20$ MeV.
- Predicted ratio: $\eta\pi\pi$ modes are a factor of two larger than $\eta^\prime\pi\pi$ modes from SU(3) symmetry, ignoring phase space; even in the extreme anomaly-dominated case, the $\eta\pi\pi$ to $\eta^\prime\pi^+\pi^-$ branching ratio should exceed $\tan^2\theta = 13\%$.
- $BR(J/\psi \to \gamma\eta \pi\pi) = (6.1\pm 1.0)\times 10^{-3}$, while $J/\psi \to \gamma\eta^\prime$ has branching ratio $(4.31\pm 0.30)\times 10^{-3}$ (from PDG).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of the $p\bar p$ threshold enhancement observed by BES in $J/\psi\to\gamma p\bar p$, following earlier speculation about a possible resonance or bound state by Yan (2005), Rosner (2005), and others. The authors had previously argued (Zhu and Gao, 2005) that the quantum numbers of the signal were likely $J^{PC}=0^{-+}, I^G=0^+$ based on non-observation in mesonic channels. The present paper uses the new BES observation of X(1835) in $\eta^\prime\pi\pi$ to test the baryonium hypothesis, and identifies a puzzle that could motivate further experimental searches in $\eta\pi\pi$ and searches for tetraquark signals in the $\eta^\prime\pi$ invariant mass spectrum.
