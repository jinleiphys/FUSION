# Properties of nuclear matter in cut-off field theory and renormalization group methods

> The properties of nuclear matter are studied in the cut-off field theory. It is found that, under the Hartree approximation, the small cut-off makes the equations of state hard, especially at higher densities. The theory is modified in the framework of the renormalization group methods with arbitrary cut-off $Λ^\prime$. It is found that the expansion in terms of the $σ$ meson field is more favorable than the naive expansion of the inverse of $Λ^\prime$, when we do not use very large $Λ^\prime$.

## Key claim

Under the Hartree approximation, a small momentum cut-off Λ makes the nuclear matter equation of state harder, especially at higher densities; the expansion of the effective potential in powers of the σ meson field Φ is more favorable than the naive expansion in inverse powers of the new cut-off Λ′ when Λ′ is not very large.

## Method

The authors study nuclear matter in the σ-ω model (Walecka model) with a four-dimensional momentum cut-off Λ, using the relativistic Hartree approximation. They determine the coupling constants g_s and g_v by fitting the saturation density (k_F0 = 1.42 fm⁻¹) and binding energy (−15.75 MeV) at normal density ρ₀. They then modify the theory by introducing a second, arbitrary cut-off Λ′ (smaller than Λ) and construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian via renormalization group methods, truncating the Taylor expansion of the one-loop vacuum potential either in powers of (M*/Λ′)² or in powers of Φ.

## Key numbers

- Normal density ρ₀ corresponds to Fermi momentum k_F0 = 1.42 fm⁻¹ and binding energy E_b0 = −15.75 MeV.
- At large Λ (> 5 GeV), the effective nucleon mass M* approaches 0.718 M (the renormalized result at Λ = ∞).
- At Λ ≈ 2 GeV, M*/M becomes very small (≈ 0.5), making the equation of state very hard.
- The incompressibility K and skewness coefficient K′ both increase as Λ decreases in the available region (Λ ≳ 2 GeV).
- The expansion in Φ with l_max = 6 shows stable behavior for Λ′ values down to small cut-offs, whereas the naive 1/Λ′ expansion (truncated at m_max = 3 or 4) diverges for Λ′ < 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the cut-off field theory approach of Cohen (1995) for nuclear matter, who introduced a four-dimensional cut-off into the relativistic Hartree calculation and found that the vacuum energy contribution differs from the renormalization procedure when the cut-off is not large. The authors also build on the renormalization group methods of Lepage (1989) and others to construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian. The results suggest that the Φ-expansion is more suitable than the naive 1/Λ′ expansion for calculations in more complicated models (e.g., Hartree-Fock or random phase approximation), especially when the cut-off is not extremely large.
