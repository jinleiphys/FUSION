# Nature of yrast excitations near N=40: Level structure of Ni-67

> Excited states in Ni-67 were populated in deep-inelastic reactions of a Ni-64 beam at 430 MeV on a thick U-238 target. A level scheme built on the previously known 13 micro-s isomer has been delineated up to an excitation energy of ~5.3 MeV and a tentative spin and parity of (21/2-). Shell model calculations have been carried out using two effective interactions in the f5/2pg9/2 model space with a Ni-56 core. Satisfactory agreement between experiment and theory is achieved for the measured transition energies and branching ratios. The calculations indicate that the yrast states are associated with rather complex configurations, herewith demonstrating the relative weakness of the N=40 subshell gap and the importance of multi particle-hole excitations involving the g9/2 neutron orbital.

## Key claim

The yrast excitations in Ni-67, while appearing to have single-particle character, are actually associated with complex configurations involving multi particle-hole excitations of the g9/2 neutron orbital, demonstrating that the N=40 subshell gap is relatively weak even in a nucleus only one neutron removed from N=40.

## Method

Excited states in Ni-67 were populated using deep-inelastic reactions of a 430-MeV Ni-64 beam on a thick U-238 target, with gamma-ray coincidence events collected by Gammasphere. Prompt-delayed coincidence techniques with a pulsed beam (412-ns repetition rate) were used to identify transitions feeding the known 13-microsecond isomer, and angular-correlation analysis was performed to determine spin and parity assignments. Shell-model calculations using the jj44b and JUN45 effective interactions in the f5/2pg9/2 model space with a Ni-56 core were compared with the experimental level energies and branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- The previously known isomer in Ni-67 has a half-life of 13.3(2) microseconds, with spin and parity 9/2+
- A level scheme was delineated up to an excitation energy of approximately 5.3 MeV and a tentative spin and parity of (21/2-)
- The 9/2+ isomer decays via 313.8(2)-keV and 694.3(2)-keV transitions in a 9/2+ -> 5/2- -> 1/2- cascade
- The jj44b interaction predicts the 9/2+ state within 192 keV of the data, while JUN45 predicts it 498 keV lower than the measured value
- An upper limit of approximately 15 ns was placed on the lifetime of the 4565-keV level
- The magnetic dipole moment of the 1/2- ground state was measured as +0.601(5) micro_N in prior work
- The isomer g-factor was reported as |g| = 0.125(6) in prior work

## Context

This work extends the limited prior knowledge of Ni-67, where only the 9/2+ isomer and its decay had been identified by Pawlat et al. (1994) and Grzywacz et al. (1998). The results contradict a simple doubly-magic picture of Ni-68 at N=40, supporting instead the view from Sorlin et al. (2002) and others that the N=40 gap is only a subshell closure of about 2 MeV. The findings enable future investigations of collectivity evolution in neutron-rich nuclei near N=40 and provide benchmarks for shell-model calculations using the jj44b and JUN45 interactions, with the JUN45 interaction showing better agreement with measured branching ratios.
