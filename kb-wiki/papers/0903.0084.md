# Rotational energy term in the empirical formula for the yrast energies in even-even nuclei

> We show that part of the empirical formula describing the gross features of the measured yrast energies of the natural parity even multipole states for even-even nuclei can be related to the rotational energy of nuclei. When the first term of the empirical formula, $αA^{-γ}$, is regarded as the otational energy, we can better understand the results of the previous analyses of the excitation energies. We show that the values of the parameters $α$ and $γ$ newly obtained by considering the $αA^{-γ}$ term as the rotational energy of a rigid rotor are remarkably consistent with those values extracted from the earlier `modified' $χ^2$ analyses, in which we use the logarithms of the excitation energies in defining the `modified' $χ^2$ values.

## Key claim

The first term (αA⁻γ) of an empirical formula for yrast energies in even-even nuclei can be identified as the rotational energy of a deformed rigid rotor, but this interpretation is valid only for nuclei near the doubly mid-shell region and not for closed-shell nuclei or light nuclei with A ≲ 30.

## Method

The authors fitted the empirical formula E_x = αA⁻γ + β_p exp(-λ_p N_p) + β_n exp(-λ_n N_n) to measured yrast energies of 2⁺ through 10⁺ states in even-even nuclei using a modified χ² analysis that uses logarithms of excitation energies. They fixed γ = 5/3 and α = α₀ J(J+1) with α₀ = 65.96 MeV (derived from the rigid-rotor moment of inertia with k=1/2 and δ=0.3) and re-extracted the remaining four parameters β_p, β_n, λ_p, λ_n. They compared these results with earlier unconstrained fits from Kim (2008) and with fits using γ fixed to 1.40.

## Key numbers

- For the rigid-rotor interpretation, γ' = 5/3 (1.67) and α₀ = 65.96 MeV, derived using k = 1/2 and distortion parameter δ = 0.3 for ¹⁷⁴Yb.
- The modified χ² values with fixed α' and γ' were larger than those from Kim (2008) by: 25% for 2₁⁺, 32% for 4₁⁺, 25% for 6₁⁺, 13% for 8₁⁺, and 18% for 10₁⁺.
- The average of the previously extracted γ values from Kim (2008) was 1.40, close to γ' = 5/3.
- The ratio A⁻¹·⁴⁰ / A⁻¹·⁶⁷ = A⁰·²⁷ is about 4 for ¹⁷⁴Yb, explaining the factor-of-4 difference in α between the γ=1.40 and γ=1.67 fits.
- The number of data points N₀ ranged from 557 (2₁⁺) to 265 (10₁⁺).

## Context

This work extends the empirical formula approach of Ha and Cha (2007) and Kim (2008) by providing a physical interpretation for the first term as rotational energy, which had been suggested but not explained by Jin (2008). The results are consistent with the Bohr and Mottelson (1975) rigid-rotor model and show that the exponential terms remain robust and separable from the rotational term. This enables future work to treat the rotational and shell-effect contributions to yrast energies separately across the nuclear chart.
