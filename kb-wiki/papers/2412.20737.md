# Possible scenario of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the instanton liquid

> Based on simulations of the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) with three-flavor quarks, we compute the free energy density of the QCD vacuum as a function of the quark condensate. We then evaluate the second derivative of the free energy density with respect to the quark condensate at the origin. This evaluation allows us to investigate whether chiral symmetry breaking in the IILM occurs in an anomaly-driven way. Such a breaking pattern of chiral symmetry has been proposed in a previous study to connect the QCD vacuum structure with meson properties, such as the mass of the sigma meson. We also perform the quenched simulations, in which no dynamical quarks interact with instantons. Comparing these results with the full calculations provides a better understanding of the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking in the IILM. We find that in the full IILM, chiral symmetry is dynamically broken in anomaly-driven way, whereas in the quenched IILM, it is broken through the ordinary mechanism. Based on these results, we suggest that chiral symmetry breaking in real QCD could also occur in an anomaly-driven way. Consequently, in phenomena where chiral symmetry breaking plays a crucial role, the anomaly effect may also have significant influence.

## Key claim

Based on simulations of the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) with three-flavor quarks, the authors find that chiral symmetry is dynamically broken in an anomaly-driven way in the full IILM, whereas in the quenched IILM it is broken through the ordinary mechanism; they suggest that chiral symmetry breaking in real QCD could also occur in an anomaly-driven way.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy density of the QCD vacuum as a function of the quark condensate using Monte Carlo simulations of the IILM partition function with fixed instanton number. They evaluate the second derivative (curvature) of the free energy density with respect to the quark condensate at the origin by fitting the data to polynomials of degree 2, 3, and 4. Full simulations include both inter-instanton and instanton-quark interactions, while quenched simulations omit the quark determinant, thereby excluding instanton-quark interactions.

## Key numbers

- In full calculations, quark masses of \(m_f = 37.0, 54.0\) and \(70.0~\mathrm{MeV}\) are used in the flavor SU(3) limit.
- In quenched calculations, smaller quark masses of \(m_f = 2.8, 14\) and \(28~\mathrm{MeV}\) are used.
- The curvature \(C_2\) is found to be positive for the full IILM across the range of quark masses considered, indicating anomaly-driven breaking.
- The curvature \(C_2\) is found to be negative for the quenched IILM across a wide range of quark masses, indicating ordinary breaking.

## Context

This work extends a previous study by Kono (2021) that proposed anomaly-driven chiral symmetry breaking in the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, where the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) term provides sufficient strength even when the attractive quark interaction is below its critical coupling. The present study tests this scenario in the IILM, which naturally incorporates the 't Hooft vertex through the quark determinant. The results suggest that the anomaly effect may have significant influence in phenomena where chiral symmetry breaking plays a crucial role, and open questions include the pattern in intermediate flavor regimes (e.g., \(N_f=2\)) and whether the sigma meson mass becomes lighter than about \(800~\mathrm{MeV}\) as predicted in the previous work.
