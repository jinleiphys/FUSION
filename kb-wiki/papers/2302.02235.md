# Viscous QCD medium effects on the bottom quark transport coefficients

> The bottom quark transport coefficients, i.e., drag and diffusion coefficients, have been studied for the collisional and soft gluon radiative processes within the viscous QCD medium. The thermal medium effects are incorporated using the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM). Both the shear and bulk viscous effects at leading order are embedded through the near-equilibrium distribution functions of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) constituent quasiparticles. The transport coefficients' dependence on the bottom quark's initial momentum and QGP temperature has been investigated. The relative dominance of the radiative over the collisional process for the bottom quark seems to occur at a higher initial momentum compared to that of the charm quark. In contrast, the effect of the viscous corrections seems to be more for the charm quark.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shear and bulk viscous corrections to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium significantly modify the bottom quark drag and diffusion coefficients for both collisional and radiative processes, with the effect being most prominent at low initial momenta and near the transition temperature; however, the relative dominance of radiative over collisional energy loss for the bottom quark occurs at a much higher initial momentum (around 30 GeV) compared to the charm quark (around 3 GeV), and the viscous corrections affect the charm quark more than the bottom quark.

## Method

The authors treat the bottom quark as a Brownian particle moving through a viscous QGP medium, using the Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann transport equation under the soft-scattering approximation. The thermal medium effects are incorporated via the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM), which uses temperature-dependent effective fugacities for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons to encode QCD interactions. Shear and bulk viscous corrections at leading order are embedded into the near-equilibrium distribution functions of the QGP constituents by solving the effective Boltzmann equation with the relaxation time approximation (RTA) and the Chapman-Enskog iterative expansion method, and these modified distributions are then used to compute the drag and diffusion coefficients for both elastic (2 to 2) and inelastic soft-gluon radiative (2 to 3) processes.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass set to \(m_b = 4.2\) GeV; charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.3\) GeV.
- Quark-hadron transition temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV; proper time \(\tau = 0.25\) fm.
- For the bottom quark, the ratio of radiative to collisional differential energy loss exceeds one at around \(p \approx 30\) GeV at \(T = 3 T_c = 510\) MeV, whereas for the charm quark this crossover occurs at around \(p \approx 3\) GeV.
- The shear viscous correction reduces the scaled drag coefficient \(A(\eta)/A(\eta=0)\) at low momenta (approximately \(p = 1-8\) GeV for collision and \(p = 1-14\) GeV for radiation) at \(T = 3 T_c\).
- The bulk viscous correction reduces the drag coefficient ratio \(A(\zeta)/A(\zeta=0)\) with almost constant dependence across the momentum range, with the effect most prominent near \(T \approx 1.5 T_c\) and approaching the conformal limit at high temperatures \(T \gtrsim 4 T_c\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of viscous corrections to heavy quark transport coefficients for collisional processes (Das 2012, Song 2019, Singh 2019, Kurian 2020) and the authors' own prior work on charm quark transport coefficients (Shaikh 2021) by now including the radiative process for bottom quarks. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark energy loss in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for understanding the different roles of collisional versus radiative energy loss for charm and bottom quarks in a viscous QGP medium described by relativistic viscous hydrodynamics.
