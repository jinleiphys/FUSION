# Constraining the relativistic mean-field model equations of state with gravitational wave observations

> The first detection of gravitational waves from the binary neutron star merger event GW170817 has started to provide important new constraints on the nuclear equation of state at high density. The tidal deformability bound of GW170817 combined with the observed two solar mass neutron star poses a serious challenge to theoretical formulations of realistic equations of state. We analyze a fully comprehensive set of relativistic nuclear mean-field theories by confronting them with the observational bounds and the measured neutron-skin thickness. We find that only a few models can withstand these bounds which predict a stiff overall equation of state but with a soft neutron-proton symmetry energy. Two possible indications are proposed: Circumstantial evidence of hadron-quark phase transition inside the star and new parametrizations that are consistent with ground state properties of finite nuclei and observational bounds. Based on extensive analysis of these sets, an upper limit on the radius of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star of $R_{1.4}\lesssim 12.9$ km is deduced.

## Key claim

The paper finds that only three existing relativistic mean-field (RMF) models survive the combined constraints from the GW170817 tidal deformability upper limit (Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580) and the requirement of supporting a maximum neutron star mass of at least 1.97 M⊙, and these models all require a soft symmetry energy at around twice saturation density; the authors also propose that hadron-quark phase transitions could reconcile more models with the data.

## Method

The authors analyzed 269 RMF equations of state (EOS) from five families (NL-type, NL3-type, FSU-type, BSR-type, and density-dependent), first filtering to 67 models that satisfy experimental nuclear matter saturation properties (incompressibility K∞ between 210 and 280 MeV, symmetry energy Esym(ρ₀) between 28 and 35 MeV, and slope parameter L between 30 and 87 MeV). They then computed neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for each EOS and compared them against the GW170817 tidal deformability bounds (Λ₁.₄ ≤ 800 and the tighter Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580) and the maximum mass constraint (Mmax ≥ 1.97 M⊙ from PSR J0348+0432). For the phase transition study, they combined representative hadronic EOSs with a bag model for quark matter, varying the bag parameter continuously.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: R₁.₄ ≤ 12.87 km from the Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 bound; R₁.₄ ≤ 13.49 km from the Λ₁.₄ ≤ 800 bound
- Only three existing RMF models satisfy both Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 and Mmax ≥ 1.97 M⊙: NLρ, HC, and TW99; TW99 gives Λ₁.₄ ≈ 400
- The three surviving models predict neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb in the range R_skin²⁰⁸ = 0.18–0.20 fm
- The pressure bound at twice saturation density from GW170817 is P(2ρ₀) = 3.5⁺²·⁷₋₁.₇ × 10³⁴ dyn/cm² (90% confidence); at six times saturation density it is P(6ρ₀) = 9.0⁺⁷·⁹₋₂.₆ × 10³⁵ dyn/cm²
- The correlation between Λ₁.₄ and R₁.₄ for EOSs with Mmax ≥ 1.97 M⊙ follows Λ₁.₄ = 1.53 × 10⁻⁵ (R₁.₄/km)⁶·⁸³
- For EOSs with hadron-quark phase transition, the bag parameter range is B_eff¹/⁴ ≈ 145–200 MeV, and these predict R₁.₄ ≤ 12.94 km
- The new IU-FSU1 and IU-FSU2 parameter sets give Mmax > 1.97 M⊙ and Λ₁.₄ < 580, with IU-FSU1 giving R₁.₄ ≤ 12.81 km

## Context

This work extends the analysis of GW170817 constraints on nuclear EOSs, following earlier studies by Margalit (2017), Radice (2017), Fattoyev (2017), Annala (2017), Most (2018), and Nandi (2017) that used the initial Λ₁.₄ ≤ 800 bound. The paper incorporates the tighter Λ₁.₄ ≤ 580 bound from Abbott (2018) and combines it with the maximum mass constraint from Antoniadis (2013) and the PREX neutron-skin measurement from Abrahamyan (2012). The results enable future comparisons with microscopic many-body calculations such as those by Hebeler (2010) and Rrapaj (2015), and motivate the upcoming PREX-II experiment to further constrain the symmetry energy slope.
