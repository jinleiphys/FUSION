# Determination of matter radius and neutron-skin thickness of $^{60,62,64}$Ni from reaction cross section of proton scattering on $^{60,62,64}$Ni targets (published in Results in Physics)

> In our previous work, we determined matter radii $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp})$ and neutron-skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$ from reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ of proton scattering on $^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{12}$C targets, using the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model with the densities calculated with Gogny-D1S-HFB (D1S-GHFB) with angular momentum projection (AMP). The resultant $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})$ agree with the PREX2 and CREX values. As for $^{58}$Ni, our value is consistent with one determined from the differential cross section for $^{58}$Ni+$^{4}$He scattering. As for p+$^{60,62,64}$N scattering, $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ are available as a function of incident energies $E_{\rm in}$, where $E_{\rm in}=22.8 \sim 65.5$~MeV for $^{60}$Ni, $E_{\rm in}=40,60.8$~MeV for $^{62}$Ni, $E_{\rm in}=40, 60.8$~MeV for $^{64}$Ni. Our aim is to determine matter radii $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp})$ for $^{60,62,64}$Ni from the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$. Our method is the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the densities scaled from D1S-GHFB+AMP densities, Our skin values are $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp})=0.076 \pm 0.019,~0.106 \pm 0.192,~0.162 \pm 0.176$~fm, and $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp})=3.759 \pm 0.011,~3.811 \pm 0.107,~3.864 \pm 0.101$~fm for $^{60,62,64}$Ni, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper determines matter radii and neutron-skin thicknesses for $^{60,62,64}$Ni from existing reaction cross section data using the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model, with the caveat that the extracted values depend on scaling the theoretical densities from Gogny-D1S-HFB with angular momentum projection to match experimental proton radii from electron scattering.

## Method

The authors apply the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model to proton-nucleus scattering on $^{60,62,64}$Ni targets, using proton and neutron densities scaled from D1S-GHFB+AMP densities. For each isotope, the neutron density is scaled so that the product of a scaling factor $f$ and the theoretical reaction cross section $\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm D1S})$ reproduces the experimental reaction cross section $\sigma_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$, while the proton density is constrained to match the experimental charge radius from electron scattering. The matter radius $r_{\rm m}(E_{\rm in})$ is determined at each incident energy, and the final $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp})$ is the average over incident energies.

## Key numbers

* For $^{60}$Ni: matter radius $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) = 3.759 \pm 0.011$ fm; neutron-skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp}) = 0.076 \pm 0.019$ fm
* For $^{62}$Ni: matter radius $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) = 3.811 \pm 0.107$ fm; neutron-skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp}) = 0.106 \pm 0.192$ fm
* For $^{64}$Ni: matter radius $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) = 3.864 \pm 0.101$ fm; neutron-skin thickness $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp}) = 0.162 \pm 0.176$ fm
* The scaling factors $f$ are 0.97488 for $^{60}$Ni, 0.96213 for $^{62}$Ni, and 0.98232 for $^{64}$Ni
* The product $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp})/A^{1/3} \times E_{\rm B}/A$ is nearly constant at $8.437385$ MeV$\cdot$fm with a relative error of 0.2847%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of matter radii and neutron-skin thicknesses for $^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni, $^{40,48}$Ca, and $^{12}$C (Tagami et al., 2023) to the nickel isotopes $^{60,62,64}$Ni. The method is consistent with the earlier result for $^{58}$Ni, which agreed with the matter radius determined from $^{58}$Ni+$^{4}$He scattering at the GSI storage ring (Zamora, 2017). The paper also reports an empirical inverse proportionality between the matter radius per nucleon and the total binding energy per nucleon for the nickel isotopic chain, which enables estimation of matter radii from binding energies alone.
