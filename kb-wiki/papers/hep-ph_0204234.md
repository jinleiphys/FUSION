# Quark and Nucleon Self-Energy in Dense Matter

> In a recent work we introduced a nonlocal version of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio(NJL) model that was designed to generate a quark self-energy in Euclidean space that was similar to that obtained in lattice simulations of QCD. In the present work we carry out related calculations in Minkowski space, so that we can study the effects of the significant vector and axial-vector interactions that appear in extended NJL models and which play an important role in the study of the $ρ$, $ω$ and $a_1$ mesons. We study the modification of the quark self-energy in the presence of matter and find that our model reproduces the behavior of the quark condensate predicted by the model-independent relation $<\bar qq>_ρ = <\bar qq>_0(1-σ_Nρ_N/f_π^2m_π^2 +...)$, where $σ_N$ is the pion-nucleon sigma term and $ρ_N$ is the density of nuclear matter. (Since we do not include a model of confinement, our study is restricted to the analysis of quark matter. We provide some discussion of the modification of the above formula for quark matter.) The inclusion of a quark current mass leads to a second-order phase transition for the restoration of chiral symmetry. That restoration is about 80% at twice nuclear matter density for the model considered in this work. We also find that the part of the quark self-energy that is explicitly dependent upon density has a strong negative Lorentz-scalar term and a strong positive Lorentz-vector term, which is analogous to the self-energy found for the nucleon in nuclear matter when one makes use of the Dirac equation for the nucleon. In this work we calculate the nucleon self -energy in nuclear matter using our model of the quark self-energy and obtain satisfactory results.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model from Euclidean space to Minkowski space, enabling the study of vector and axial-vector interactions that are essential for describing rho, omega, and a1 mesons. The model reproduces the model-independent relation for the quark condensate density dependence and finds that chiral symmetry restoration is about 80% complete at twice nuclear matter density, but only for the specific parameter set considered and with the caveat that confinement is not included.

## Method

The authors solve coupled nonlinear equations for the scalar (A), vector (B), and time-like vector (C) components of the quark self-energy in Minkowski space using a nonlocal version of the NJL model with momentum-dependent quark-antiquark interactions. They introduce a Gaussian regulator with parameter alpha = 0.60 GeV and compute the self-energy components both in vacuum and in quark matter at finite density, where the matter contribution arises from Fermi seas of up and down quarks with Fermi momentum k_F. The nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter is then calculated using a simple quark-diquark model of the nucleon, integrating the quark self-energy weighted by the quark-diquark wave function.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonlocal NJL model (Celenza et al., 2002) from Euclidean to Minkowski space, enabling the inclusion of vector and axial-vector interactions that are crucial for meson phenomenology. The results contrast with those from Schwinger-Dyson equation approaches (reviewed by Roberts and Schmidt, 2000) which find that the quark condensate increases with chemical potential, a difference the authors attribute to those calculations not representing the physical situation of nucleons in nuclear matter. The calculated nucleon scalar and vector potentials (V_S ≈ -400 MeV, V_V ≈ 300 MeV) agree with values from relativistic nuclear physics (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Horowitz and Serot, 1981), enabling future studies of dense matter properties relevant to neutron stars.
