# From super-charged nuclei to massive nuclear density cores

> Due to $e^+e^-$-pair production in the field of supercritical $(Z \gg Z_{cr}\approx 170 $) nucleus an electron shell, created out of the vacuum, is formed. The distribution of the vacuum charge in this shell has been determined for super-charged nuclei $Ze^3 \ga 1$ within the framework of the Thomas-Fermi equation generalized to the relativistic case. For $Ze^3 \gg 1$ the electron shell penetrates inside the nucleus and almost completely screens its charge. Inside such nucleus the potential takes a constant value equal to $V_0=-(3π^2 n_p)^{1/3} \sim -2m_πc^2$, and super-charged nucleus represents an electrically neutral plasma consisting of $e,p$ and $n$. Near the edge of the nucleus a transition layer exists with a width $λ\approx α^{-1/2} \hbar/m_π c\sim 15$ fm, which is independent of $Z (\hbar/m_π c \ll λ\ll \hbar/m_e c)$. The electric field and surface charge are concentrated in this layer. These results, obtained earlier for hypothetical superheavy nuclei with $Z \sim A/2\la 10^4 ÷10^6$, are extrapolated to massive nuclear density cores having a mass number $A \approx (m_{Planck}/m_n)\sim 10^{57}$. The problem of the gravitational and electrodynamical stability of such objects is considered. It is shown that for $A \ga 0.04 (Z/A)^{1/2}(m_{Planck}/m_n)^3$ the Coulomb repulsion of protons, screened by relativistic electrons, can be balanced by gravitational forces. The overcritical electric fields $E\sim m^2_π c^3/e\hbar$ are present in the narrow transition layer near the core surface.

## Key claim
For super-charged nuclei with \(Ze^3 \gg 1\), the electron shell penetrates inside the nucleus and almost completely screens its charge, creating an electrically neutral plasma; these results, previously obtained for hypothetical superheavy nuclei with \(Z \sim A/2 \lesssim 10^4 \div 10^6\), are extrapolated to massive nuclear density cores with mass number \(A \approx (m_{\text{Planck}}/m_n) \sim 10^{57}\), where gravitational forces can balance the screened Coulomb repulsion of protons.

## Method
The distribution of vacuum charge in the electron shell formed by \(e^+e^-\)-pair production in the field of a supercritical nucleus (\(Z \gg Z_{\text{cr}} \approx 170\)) was determined using the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation. For \(Ze^3 \gg 1\), the problem reduces to a plane geometry with a transition layer of thickness \(\lambda \approx \alpha^{-1/2} \hbar/m_\pi c \sim 15\) fm, and the resulting differential equation was solved analytically. The Coulomb and gravitational energies of the thin proton shell were then computed from this analytic solution to assess the stability of massive nuclear density cores.

## Key numbers
- Critical nuclear charge for the \(1s_{1/2}\) state: \(Z_{\text{cr}} = 172\) (with screening by outward electrons, except the K-shell), corresponding to \(\zeta_{\text{cr}} = 1.255\) and nuclear radius \(r_N = 9.14\) fm.
- Critical nuclear charge for the \(2p_{1/2}\) state: \(Z_{\text{cr}} = 185\) (\(\zeta_{\text{cr}} = 1.350\), \(r_N = 9.33\) fm).
- Critical nuclear charge for the \(2s_{1/2}\) state: \(Z_{\text{cr}} = 239\) (\(\zeta_{\text{cr}} = 1.745\), \(r_N = 10.1\) fm).
- Transition layer thickness: \(\lambda \approx \alpha^{-1/2} \hbar/m_\pi c \sim 15\) fm, independent of \(Z\).
- Maximum electric field at the edge of a super-charged nucleus: \(E_{\text{max}} \approx 0.95 \sqrt{\alpha} \, m_\pi^2/e \approx 6000 \, E_{\text{cr}}\), where \(E_{\text{cr}} = m_e^2 c^3/e\hbar = 1.32 \times 10^{16}\) V/cm.
- Constant potential inside the super-charged nucleus: \(V(0) = -(9\pi/4)^{1/3} m_\pi \approx -1.92 \, m_\pi\).
- Stability condition for massive nuclear density cores: \(A > A_R \approx 0.039 \, (N_p/A)^{1/2} (m_{\text{Planck}}/m_n)^3\), with \(m_{\text{Planck}} = \sqrt{\hbar c/G} \sim 10^{-5}\) g and \(m_n \sim 10^{-24}\) g.

## Context
This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach for super-charged nuclei (Popov, 1970s; Migdal, 1970s) to the regime of massive nuclear density cores with mass numbers up to \(10^{57}\), typical of neutron stars. It addresses the stability problem left open in earlier work (Popov, 1970s), where nuclei with \(A \sim 10^4 - 10^6\) were found unstable due to Coulomb repulsion. The results enable a consistent model of stable massive cores incorporating gravitational, strong, electromagnetic, and weak interactions, though many aspects of stability remain unsolved and require further investigation.
