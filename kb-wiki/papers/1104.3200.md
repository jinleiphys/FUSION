# Impurity effect of Lambda hyperon on collective excitations of atomic nuclei

> Taking the ground state rotational band in $^{24}$Mg as an example, we investigate the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon on collective excitations of atomic nuclei in the framework of non-relativistic energy density functional theory. To this end, we take into account correlations related to the restoration of broken symmetries and fluctuations of collective variables by solving the eigenvalue problem of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom. The parameters of the collective Hamiltonian are determined with constrained mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes using the SGII Skyrme force. We compare the low-spin spectrum for $^{24}$Mg with the spectrum for the same nucleus inside $^{25}_Λ$Mg. It is found that the $Λ$ hyperon stretches the ground state band and reduces the $B(E2:2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value by $\sim 9%$, mainly by softening the potential energy surface towards the spherical shape, even though the shrinkage effect on the average proton radius is only $\sim0.5%$.

## Key claim

The addition of a Lambda hyperon to the nucleus 24Mg stretches the ground state rotational band and reduces the B(E2: 2+1 -> 0+1) value by about 9%, primarily by softening the potential energy surface toward spherical shape, even though the shrinkage effect on the average proton radius is only about 0.5%.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations for triaxial shapes using the SGII Skyrme force for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the No.1 set of Yamamoto (1988) for the Lambda-nucleon interaction. They determined the seven collective quantities (collective potential, three mass parameters, and three moments of inertia) as functions of quadrupole deformation variables beta and gamma, then solved the eigenvalue problem of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom. The low-spin spectra for 24Mg in free space and for the same nucleus inside 25_LambdaMg were calculated and compared.

## Key numbers

* The Lambda hyperon increases the excitation energy of the 2+1 state by about 7% in the nuclear core of 25_LambdaMg compared to free 24Mg.
* The B(E2: 2+1 -> 0+1) value is reduced by about 9% due to the presence of the Lambda hyperon.
* The shrinkage effect on the average proton radius is only about 0.5%.
* The average values of beta and gamma for the 0+1 state are 0.54 (20.0 degrees) for 24Mg, 0.53 (20.7 degrees) for 25_LambdaMg (core, without Lambda-nucleon interaction energy), and 0.52 (20.8 degrees) for 25_LambdaMg (core, with Lambda-nucleon interaction energy).

## Context

This work extends the framework of nuclear energy-density functionals, previously applied to study hypernuclei by Rayet (1976), Rufa (1990), and others, to include collective correlations from symmetry restoration and fluctuations. It builds on recent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies of Lambda hyperon effects on nuclear deformation by Zhou (2007), Schulze (2010), and Win (2011), and on the development of five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian methods by Libert (1999), Prochniak (2004), Niksic (2009), and Li (2009). The results enable future calculations of gamma-ray spectra for single-Lambda hypernuclei and motivate extension to relativistic mean-field approaches and to odd-mass or odd-odd hypernuclei.
