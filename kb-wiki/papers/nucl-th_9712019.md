# Photodisintegration of three- and four- nucleon systems

> Three- and four-nucleon photodisintegration processes are quite efficiently treated by means of effective two-body integral equations in momentum space. We recall some aspects of their derivation, present previous and most recent results obtained within this framework, and discuss general features, trends and effects observed in these investigations: At low energies final-state interaction plays an important role. Even more pronounced is the effect of meson exchange currents. A considerable potential dependence shows up in the low-energy peak region. The different peak heights are found to be closely correlated with the corresponding binding energies. Above the peak region only the difference between potentials with or without p-wave contributions remains relevant. In the differential cross sections the electric quadrupole contributions have to be taken into account. The remarkable agreement between theory and experiment in $p$-$d$ radiative capture is achieved only when incorporating this contribution, together with most of the above-mentioned effects. In the final part of this report we briefly review also methods developed, and results achieved in three- and four- nucleon electrodisintegration. We, in particular, compare them with a recent access to this problem, based on the construction of nucleon-nucleus potentials via Marchenko inversion theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective two-body integral equations in momentum space provide an efficient framework for treating three- and four-nucleon photodisintegration, but the abstract's claim of "quite efficiently treated" glosses over the fact that achieving agreement with experiment requires incorporating final-state interaction, meson exchange currents via Siegert's theorem, electric quadrupole contributions, and p-wave components in the nuclear potentials, with the validity of Siegert's theorem in three- and four-body cases being an assumption that "would need a similar verification as in the two-body case."

## Method

The authors derive photodisintegration amplitudes from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-body systems and from Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three- and four-body systems, reducing them to effective two-body integral equations in momentum space via separable expansions of the subsystem T-matrices using the W-matrix method or Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) expansion. For three-nucleon photodisintegration (gamma + t -> n + d) and four-nucleon photodisintegration (gamma + He-4 -> n + He-3 or p + H-3), they solve these equations with the electromagnetic operator in electric dipole (E1) and electric quadrupole (E2) approximations, including meson exchange currents via Siegert's theorem. The calculations employ the Yamaguchi, Malfliet-Tjon, Paris, Bonn A, and Bonn B potentials, with the triton wave function determined from the homogeneous counterpart of the effective two-body equation.

## Key numbers

* For the Paris potential in three-nucleon photodisintegration, final-state interaction enhances the low-energy peak cross section by about 25% compared to the Born approximation, independent of the potential employed.
* Meson exchange currents in three-nucleon photodisintegration enhance the cross section by about a factor of 1.7 relative to calculations without MEC, which is much stronger than in the two-body case.
* At E_gamma = 60 MeV for deuteron photodisintegration, the differential cross section requires inclusion of both E1 and E2 contributions to match experimental data from Depas (1982) and Krause (1988).
* For p-d radiative capture at E = 12.1 MeV, the full calculation including E2 contributions achieves remarkable agreement with experimental data from Belt (1970), while the plane-wave (Born) approximation lies clearly below the data.
* The peak heights of three-nucleon photodisintegration cross sections show a linear correlation with triton binding energies, with s-wave potentials (Yamaguchi, Malfliet-Tjon) falling on one line and realistic potentials (Paris, Bonn A, Bonn B) on a separate parallel line.

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space integral equation approach to photodisintegration pioneered by Barbour and Phillips (1967) and Gibson and Lehman (1975), who first derived three-body photonuclear equations within the Faddeev framework and AGS equations respectively. The paper contrasts its results with earlier calculations using simple separable Yamaguchi potentials and demonstrates that realistic potentials with p-wave components produce systematically different cross sections above the peak region compared to s-wave potentials. The findings enable future tests of nuclear potentials through more accurate photodisintegration measurements, which the authors note are needed because current experimental errors in the sensitive peak region do not allow for definitive judgment between potentials. The four-body formalism extends the three-body approach to gamma + He-4 processes, building on the work of Casel (1980) and using the W-matrix method and energy dependent pole expansion for subsystem operators.
