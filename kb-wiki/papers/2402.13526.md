# Pion-nucleus elastic scatterings incorporating medium effects within the Eikonal-Glauber mode

> In this present investigation, we explore the elastic scattering of pions with nuclei ($π$-$A$), primarily influenced by the $Δ$(1232) resonance, within the Eikonal-Glauber model. The medium effects are incorporated by considering nuclear-density ($ρ_A$) dependent masses of baryons and strong coupling constants. These dependencies are computed and parameterized up to $\mathcal{O}(ρ_A^2)$ based on the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The Wood-Saxon type density profile is utilized for the bound nucleons within finite nuclei. The element $π^+$-$N$ scattering cross section for the Glauber approach is determined using the conventional effective Lagrangian method. Subsequently, we analyze the total cross sections for elastic scattering with $^4$He and $^{12}$C targets. Our numerical results demonstrate a favorable agreement with JINR data for the $^4$He target, accurately reproducing the total cross-section. However, when considering the $^{12}$C target, deviations of approximately $\lesssim10\%$. We also consider the multiple-scattering effects inside the nucleus approximately, using the single-channel meson-baryon Bethe-Salpeter equation, resulting in the effective width broadening of the $Δ$ resonance to reproduce the data better.

## Key claim

The Eikonal-Glauber model with medium effects parameterized up to $\mathcal{O}(\rho_A^2)$ from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model reproduces the JINR data for $^4$He targets accurately, but for $^{12}$C targets the results overestimate the SINR data by approximately $\lesssim 10\%$, with the deviation attributed to oversimplification of the Glauber model for heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors computed elastic $\pi^+$-$A$ total cross sections within the Eikonal-Glauber model using elementary $\pi^+$-$N$ cross sections as input, calculated via an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Bonn approximation with $\Delta$(1232) resonance dominance. Medium effects were incorporated through nuclear-density-dependent baryon masses and strong coupling constants, parameterized up to $\mathcal{O}(\rho_A^2)$ from the QMC model, using a Wood-Saxon density profile for bound nucleons. Multiple-scattering effects inside the nucleus were approximated using a single-channel meson-baryon Bethe-Salpeter equation, resulting in effective broadening of the $\Delta$ resonance decay width.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass $M^*_N$ at the nucleus center is approximately 800 MeV, converging to the vacuum mass of $\sim 940$ MeV at larger radial distances.
- The parameterization coefficients for effective baryon mass are $C_1 = -1.543\ \mathrm{GeV\cdot fm}^3$ and $C_2 = 2.036\ \mathrm{MeV\cdot fm}^3$.
- The in-medium coupling constants are given by $f^*_{\pi NN}(\rho_A) = f_{\pi NN} - 0.633\,\rho_A$ and $f^*_{\pi N\Delta}(\rho_A) = f_{\pi N\Delta} - 1.360\,\rho_A$, with vacuum values $f_{\pi NN} = 0.989$, $f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.127$, and $f_\pi = 93.2$ MeV.
- The spreading width parameter for the $\Delta$ decay is $\Gamma_{\mathrm{sp}} = 80$ MeV, with cutoff parameter $\beta_0 = 200$ MeV.
- The resonance peak for $^4$He appears at $E_{\mathrm{cm}} \sim 1222$ MeV.
- The free parameter for multiple-scattering effects is $\xi = -15.8 \times A$ MeV, where $A$ is the mass number.
- Wood-Saxon parameters for $^4$He: $r_A = 1.01$ fm, $d = 0.327$ fm, $\rho^c_A = 0.2381$ fm$^{-3}$, $c = 0.445$.
- Wood-Saxon parameters for $^{12}$C: $r_A = 2.36$ fm, $d = 0.522$ fm, $\rho^c_A = 0.1823$ fm$^{-3}$, $c = -0.149$.

## Context

This work extends the Eikonal-Glauber model approach to pion-nucleus elastic scattering by incorporating medium effects from the QMC model, building on prior calculations of $\pi^+$-$N$ scattering by Han (2021). The study addresses discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental data from JINR (Shcherbakov, 1975) for $^4$He and SINR (Ashery, 1981) for $^{12}$C, finding that multiple-scattering effects via the Bethe-Salpeter equation improve agreement for heavier nuclei. The results enable predictions for $^{16}$O targets and motivate future experimental measurements at intermediate energies to test the model further.
