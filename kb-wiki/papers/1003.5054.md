# Nature of the a_0(980) Meson in the Light of Photon-Photon Collisions

> New high-statistics Belle data on the reaction γγ\toπ^0ηare analyzed to clarify the two-photon production mechanisms and the nature of the a_0(980) meson. The obtained solution for the amplitude γγ\toπ^0ηis consistent with the chiral theory expectation for the πηscattering length, with the strong coupling of the a_0(980) to the πη, K\bar K, and πη' channels, and with a key role of the rescattering mechanisms a_0(980)\to(K\bar K+ π^0η+π^0η')\toγγin the a_0(980)\toγγdecay. Such a picture argues in favor of the q^2\bar q^2 nature of the a_0(980) meson and is in agreement with the properties of its partners, the σ_0(600) and f_0(980) mesons, in particular, with those that manifest themselves in γγ\toππ. An important role of the vector meson exchanges in the formation of the nonresonant background in γγ\toπ^0ηis also revealed. The preliminary information on the reaction π^0η\toπ^0ηis obtained.

## Key claim

The Belle data on γγ→π⁰η are consistent with the a₀(980) meson being a four-quark (q²q̄²) state, provided that the dominant decay mechanism is rescattering through intermediate K̄K, πη, and πη' channels rather than direct coupling to photons. The analysis requires a specific combination of rescattering contributions, vector-meson exchange backgrounds, and a heavy a₀' resonance to reproduce the observed cross section.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for the helicity amplitudes of γγ→π⁰η that includes Born contributions from ρ, ω, K*, and K exchanges with Regge-type form factors, rescattering amplitudes through πη, πη', and K̄K intermediate states, and direct resonance-photon couplings for the a₀(980) and a₀' resonances. The S-wave hadronic amplitudes were parametrized with a coupled-channel formalism including elastic background phase shifts and a mixed a₀-a₀' resonance complex. The model was fitted to Belle cross-section data for |cosθ|≤0.8 in the invariant mass region below 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the four-quark model for scalar mesons developed by Achasov and collaborators (Achasov and Shestakov, 1988; Achasov et al., 1980) by applying it to high-statistics Belle data on γγ→π⁰η (Uehara, 2009). The analysis contradicts the naive qq̄ model prediction Γ_{f₂→γγ}/Γ_{a₂→γγ}=25/9 for the tensor mesons and excludes the corresponding ratio for the scalar mesons f₀(980) and a₀(980). The results enable future studies of γγ* collisions to distinguish four-quark from conventional qq̄ states, as the four-quark contributions should decrease more rapidly with photon virtuality Q², analogous to the behavior observed by the L3 Collaboration for the exotic 2⁺(2⁺⁺) state in γγ*→ρ⁰ρ⁰.
