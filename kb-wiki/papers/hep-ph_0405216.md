# Quantum Anomalies in Dense Matter

> We consider the effects of quantum anomalies involving the baryon current for high-density matter. In the effective Lagrangian, the anomaly terms describe the interaction of three light fields: the electromagnetic photons A_mu, neutral light Nambu-Goldstone bosons (pi, eta, eta'), and the superfluid phonon. The anomaly induced interactions lead to a number of interesting phenomena which may have phenomenological consequences observable in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Quantum anomalies involving the baryon current in dense matter produce observable effects only when the Nambu-Goldstone fields \(\varphi_\A\) and \(\varphi_\B\) make full \(2\pi\) rotations, i.e., in the presence of topological defects such as vortices or domain walls; otherwise the anomalous terms vanish identically or are total derivatives.

## Method

The authors derive an effective Lagrangian for high-density QCD by coupling the system to external U(1) gauge fields (electromagnetic \(A_\mu\) and a spurion baryon field \(B_\mu\)), imposing anomaly matching between the microscopic theory and the low-energy effective theory. They then replace \(B_\mu\) with the chemical potential \(\mu n_\mu\) and incorporate the superfluid phonon \(\varphi_\B\) and neutral Nambu-Goldstone bosons (\(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), \(\eta'\)) via covariant derivatives. The resulting anomalous terms are evaluated for topological configurations (vortices and domain walls) in the 2SC phase, CFL phase, and nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- In the CFL phase, the electric current running along an axial vortex core is \(j^{\rm em} = e C_{\A\B\gamma} \mu / (2\pi)\); taking \(\mu \sim 1.5\ \text{GeV}\) gives approximately \(40\ \text{kA}\).
- The magnetic moment per unit area of a U(1)\(_\A\) domain wall is \(e C_{\A\B\gamma} \mu / (4\pi)\); for the 2SC phase this is \(e\mu/(12\pi)\) and for the CFL phase \(e\mu/(6\pi)\).
- The axial current on a superfluid (baryon) vortex in the CFL phase has magnitude \(\mu/(12\pi)\).
- The pion decay constant in vacuum is given as \(f_\pi \approx 93\ \text{MeV}\) for normalization of the \(\pi^0\) field.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of anomalies in dense matter by Nowak (2000) and Forbes (2001), who considered specific processes like \(\pi^0 \to 2\gamma\) and \(\eta' \to 2\gamma\) at finite density, by systematically including anomalies involving the baryon current. The results reveal that baryon vortices carry weak neutral currents that may affect neutrino interactions and neutron star cooling, and that axial vortices carry electric currents reminiscent of Witten's superconducting strings (Witten, 1985) and zero fermion modes on vortices (Jackiw and Rossi, 1981). The findings enable further exploration of topological defect phenomenology in compact objects and can be extended to other dense phases such as those with kaon or eta condensation.
