# Enforced Electrical Neutrality of the Color-Flavor Locked Phase

> We demonstrate that quark matter in the color-flavor locked phase of QCD is rigorously electrically neutral, despite the unequal quark masses, and even in the presence of an electron chemical potential. As long as the strange quark mass and the electron chemical potential do not preclude the color-flavor locked phase, quark matter is automatically neutral. No electrons are required and none are admitted.

# Enforced Electrical Neutrality of the Color-Flavor Locked Phase

## Key claim

The color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD is rigorously electrically neutral with equal numbers of up, down, and strange quarks and no electrons present, even when the strange quark mass and electron chemical potential are nonzero, provided that neither the strange quark mass nor the electron chemical potential is too large, specifically when the condition |m_s²/(4μ̄) − δμ| < Δ₀/√2 is satisfied.

## Method

The authors analyze a BCS-type gap equation for pairing between quarks of different flavors in the CFL phase, using a four-fermion interaction model with the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange. They construct the free energy density of the paired state and compare it to the unpaired (normal) state, treating the common Fermi momentum as a variational parameter. By shifting the integration variable p₀ → p₀ + iδμ in the gap equation, they show the gap equation is independent of δμ, and by differentiating the free energy with respect to chemical potentials they obtain the number densities.

## Key numbers

- The condition for the CFL phase to be the global minimum of the free energy is |m_s²/(4μ̄) − δμ| < Δ₀/√2, where Δ₀ is the superconducting gap, m_s is the strange quark mass, μ̄ is the average chemical potential, and δμ = μ_e/2 with μ_e the electron chemical potential.
- For an example with m_s = 200 MeV, μ̄ = 400 MeV, and μ_e = 150 MeV, the CFL phase is favored as long as Δ₀/√2 > 50 MeV, which is plausible since Δ₀ ∼ 100 MeV is not unreasonable.
- The free energy difference between the BCS state and the normal state is Ω_BCS − Ω_normal = (μ̄²/π²)[(m_s²/(4μ̄) − δμ)² − Δ₀²/2].
- The common Fermi momentum for paired quarks is ν₁ = ν₂ = μ̄ − m_s²/(4μ̄).
- The number densities of paired quarks are N₁ = N₂ = (μ̄ − m_s²/(4μ̄))³/(3π²) + (1/(4π²))(∂/∂μ̄)[μ̄²Δ₀(μ̄)²].

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the CFL phase first described by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and others, which had previously been assumed to require a neutralizing electron fluid when m_s ≠ 0, as in noninteracting quark matter described by Freedman and McLerran (1977). The result contradicts the tacit assumption in earlier literature that CFL quark matter with nonzero strange quark mass would be electrically positive. The enforced neutrality enables new predictions for neutron star phenomenology: CFL quark matter cores would be transparent to the modified photon of the unbroken U(1)̃_Q gauge symmetry, have much lower specific heat than previously thought (dominated by the neutral superfluid rather than electrons), and could form a direct interface with nuclear matter without requiring a mixed phase region as described by Glendenning (1992). The analysis also relates to the work of Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1999) on unlocking color and flavor, and to the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) state analysis by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000).
