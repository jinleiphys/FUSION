# Bottomonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma -- From effective field theories to non-unitary quantum evolution

> In this proceedings contribution I review recent work which computes the suppression of bottomonium production in heavy-ion collisions using open quantum systems methods applied within the potential non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics (pNRQCD) effective field theory. I discuss how the computation of bottomonium suppression can be reduced to solving a Gorini-Kossakowski-Sudarshan-Lindblad (GKSL) quantum master equation for the evolution of the b-bbar reduced density matrix. The open quantum systems approach used allows one to take into account the non-equilibrium dynamics and decoherence of bottomonium in the quark-gluon plasma. Finally, I present comparisons of phenomenological predictions obtained using a recently obtained next-to-leading-order GKSL equation with ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS experimental data for bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow.

# Bottomonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma, From effective field theories to non-unitary quantum evolution

## Key claim
The next-to-leading-order (NLO) open quantum systems framework within pNRQCD effective field theory, when solved via a Gorini-Kossakowski-Sudarshan-Lindblad (GKSL) quantum master equation without dynamical quantum jumps, reproduces experimental data for $\Upsilon(1S)$ and $\Upsilon(3S)$ suppression and elliptic flow, but systematically overestimates the suppression of the $\Upsilon(2S)$ excited state regardless of hydrodynamic initial conditions used.

## Method
The authors coupled a Markovian GKSL quantum master equation for the $b\bar{b}$ reduced density matrix, derived from pNRQCD effective field theory at NLO in binding energy over temperature, to a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code. They used both smooth Glauber initial conditions and fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions for the hydrodynamic background, and solved for the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ of bottomonium states as functions of centrality, number of participants, and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- The NLO predictions for $R_{AA}[\Upsilon(1S)]$ and $R_{AA}[\Upsilon(3S)]$ show good agreement with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS.
- The $\Upsilon(2S)$ suppression is somewhat lower than experimental results, particularly for the most central collisions.
- The elliptic flow coefficient $v_2[1S]$ is predicted to have a maximum on the order of 1%, with a rather flat dependence on centrality.
- The parameter $\hat\gamma$ was fixed to -2.6 to best reproduce $R_{AA}[\Upsilon(1S)]$, with variations explored in the range $-3.5 \leq \hat\gamma \leq 0$.
- The parameter $\hat\kappa$ was varied over three values $\hat\kappa_L(T)$, $\hat\kappa_C(T)$, and $\hat\kappa_U(T)$ extracted from lattice QCD calculations.

## Context
This work extends the open quantum systems approach to heavy-quarkonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma, building on the pNRQCD effective field theory framework developed by Brambilla et al. (2016, 2017, 2019) and implemented in the QTraj code by Omar et al. (2021). The NLO formalism improves upon the leading-order treatment by allowing application at lower temperatures. The results are compared with experimental data from ALICE (2019, 2020), ATLAS (2021), and CMS (2018, 2020, 2022). The finding that fluctuating initial conditions have negligible impact on $R_{AA}$ but a small effect on $v_2$ suggests that future work should focus on improving the treatment of the $\Upsilon(2S)$ state, possibly by including dynamical quantum jumps.
