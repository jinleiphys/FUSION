# Green's function relativistic mean field theory for $Λ$ hypernuclei

> The relativistic mean-field theory with Green's function method is extended to study $Λ$ hypernuclei. Taking hypernucleus $^{61}_Λ$Ca as an example, the single-particle resonant states for $Λ$ hyperons are investigated by analyzing density of states and the corresponding energies and widths are given. Different behaviors are observed for the resonant states, i.e., the distributions of the very narrow $1f_{5/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ states are very similar as bound states while that of the wide $1g_{7/2}$ and $1g_{9/2}$ states are like scattering states. Besides, the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperons on the single-neutron resonant states are investigated. For most of the resonant states, both the energies and widths decrease with adding more $Λ$ hyperons due to the attractive $ΛN$ interaction. Finally, the energy level structure of $Λ$ hyperons in the Ca hypernucleus isotopes with mass number $A=53-73$ are studied, obvious shell structure and small spin-orbit splitting are found for the single-$Λ$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field theory with Green's function method is extended to study Λ hypernuclei, and the authors demonstrate that the method can extract both energies and widths of single-particle resonant states for Λ hyperons, with the caveat that the results are shown only for the specific case of ⁶¹_ΛCa and Ca isotopes with mass numbers A=53-73.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for Λ hyperons coupled to σ- and ω-meson fields in coordinate space using the Green's function method, constructing the Green's function from two linearly independent Dirac spinors regular at the origin and at infinity. The densities for Λ hyperons are obtained by contour integration of the Green's function on the complex energy plane, and the coupled RMF equations are solved iteratively to self-consistency. Single-particle resonant state energies and widths are extracted from the density of states by comparing with free-particle spectra.

## Context

This work extends the RMF-GF method previously developed for ordinary nuclei (Sun 2014, 2016) to include Λ hyperons, building on earlier RMF treatments of hypernuclei by Brockmann and Weise (1977), Mares (1994), and Vretenar (1998). The method enables study of single-particle resonant states in hypernuclei, which is relevant for understanding hyperon halo structures that may become accessible with radioactive ion beam facilities, and complements other resonant-state techniques such as RMF-S, RMF-ACCC, RMF-RSM, and RMF-CSM.
