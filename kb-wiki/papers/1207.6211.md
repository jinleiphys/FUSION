# Pseudospin symmetry in supersymmetric quantum mechanics: Schrödinger equations

> The origin of pseudospin symmetry (PSS) and its breaking mechanism are explored by combining supersymmetry (SUSY) quantum mechanics, perturbation theory, and the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method. The Schrödinger equation is taken as an example, corresponding to the lowest-order approximation in transforming a Dirac equation into a diagonal form by using the SRG. It is shown that while the spin-symmetry-conserving term appears in the single-particle Hamiltonian $H$, the PSS-conserving term appears naturally in its SUSY partner Hamiltonian $\tilde{H}$. The eigenstates of Hamiltonians $H$ and $\tilde{H}$ are exactly one-to-one identical except for the so-called intruder states. In such a way, the origin of PSS deeply hidden in $H$ can be traced in its SUSY partner Hamiltonian $\tilde{H}$. The perturbative nature of PSS in the present potential without spin-orbit term is demonstrated by the perturbation calculations, and the PSS-breaking term can be regarded as a very small perturbation on the exact PSS limits. A general tendency that the pseudospin-orbit splittings become smaller with increasing single-particle energies can also be interpreted in an explicit way.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pseudospin symmetry (PSS) breaking term can be treated as a small perturbation on the exact PSS limit, contrary to previous suggestions of a non-perturbative nature, by using the supersymmetric (SUSY) partner Hamiltonian where the PSS-conserving term appears naturally.

## Method

The authors combine supersymmetric (SUSY) quantum mechanics, the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, and Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory to analyze the Schrodinger equation, which corresponds to the lowest-order approximation of the Dirac equation transformed by SRG. They solve the radial Schrodinger equation for a Woods-Saxon potential in coordinate space using the shooting method, construct the SUSY partner Hamiltonian, and compute perturbation corrections up to third order to evaluate the PSS breaking.

## Key numbers

- The reduced pseudospin-orbit (PSO) splittings $(E_{j_<}-E_{j_>})/(2\tilde{l}+1)$ are less than 1 MeV for the Woods-Saxon potential with parameters $V_0=-63.297$ MeV, $R=6.278$ fm, and $a=0.615$ fm, corresponding to neutrons in $^{132}$Sn.
- The largest perturbation correction $|W_{mk}/(E_m-E_k)|$ is roughly 0.13 for the pseudospin doublets $2\tilde{p}$ and $1\tilde{f}$, fulfilling the perturbative criterion.
- First-, second-, and third-order perturbation corrections achieve 0.001 MeV accuracy when the energy cut-off for $E_m$ is taken as 150 MeV.
- The PSO splitting for the $2\tilde{p}$ doublet ($3s_{1/2}$ and $2d_{3/2}$) is 1.552 MeV, and for the $1\tilde{f}$ doublet ($2d_{5/2}$ and $1g_{7/2}$) is 4.365 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbation theory approach of Liang (2011) and Li (2011) for investigating Dirac Hamiltonian symmetries, and fills the gap between perturbation calculations and SUSY descriptions identified by Guo (2012) using the SRG technique. It contradicts the non-perturbative nature of PSS suggested by Alberto (2002) and Ginocchio (2011), and provides a quantitative framework for understanding the energy-dependent behavior of PSO splittings observed in self-consistent relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Meng (1998, 1999). The approach enables future quantitative studies of PSS in realistic nuclei and can be extended to the full Dirac equation.
