# Ab initio calculation of muon capture on $^{24}$Mg

> In this work we study ordinary muon capture (OMC) on $^{24}$Mg from a first principles perspective. Starting from a particular two- and three-nucleon interaction derived from chiral effective field theory, we use the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) framework to construct effective Hamiltonians and muon-capture operators which nonperturbatively account for many-body physics outside the valence space. The obtained nuclear matrix elements are compared against those from the phenomenological shell model. The impact of including the correlations from the nuclear shell model (NSM) as well as including the induced two-body part is studied in detail. Furthermore, the effects of realistic bound-muon wave function on the operators is studied. Finally, predictions for capture rates to the lowest excited states in $^{24}$Na are given and compared with available data. It is found that the spectroscopic properties of $^{24}$Mg and its OMC daughter $^{24}$Na are fairly well described by both the NSM and VS-IMSRG, and that the effect of the hadronic two-body currents significantly reduces the OMC rates. Both models have some difficulties in matching the measured OMC rates, especially for the $2^+$ final states. This calls for further studies in other light nuclei with available OMC data.

## Key claim

The authors find that ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations and phenomenological nuclear shell model (NSM) calculations both have difficulties matching measured ordinary muon capture (OMC) rates on $^{24}$Mg, especially for $2^+$ final states, despite fairly well describing spectroscopic properties of the parent and daughter nuclei; the inclusion of hadronic two-body currents significantly reduces the OMC rates.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral effective field theory Hamiltonian (1.8/2.0 EM interaction) and use the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) framework to construct effective Hamiltonians and muon-capture operators that nonperturbatively account for many-body physics outside the valence space. They compute OMC nuclear matrix elements using the Morita-Fujii formalism with realistic bound-muon wave functions obtained by solving the Dirac equation with a finite-size nuclear charge distribution, and compare results against phenomenological shell-model calculations using the USDB interaction in the $sd$-shell. The effect of hadronic two-body currents is included via normal-ordered one-body corrections to the axial-vector and pseudoscalar couplings using a Fermi-gas reference state.

## Key numbers

- The small component of the bound-muon wave function is only about 0.1% of the large component at $r < R_c$ for $^{24}$Mg.
- The Goldberger-Treiman PCAC value $g_{\rm P}/g_{\rm A} \approx 6.8$ is used for the ratio of pseudoscalar to axial-vector coupling strengths.
- The free-nucleon value $g_{\rm A} = 1.27$ is used for the axial-vector coupling.
- At zero-momentum transfer, the axial-vector two-body current corresponds to quenching of $g_{\rm A}$ by a factor of approximately 0.73 to 0.70.
- The density range used for the two-body current corrections is $\rho = 0.09\dots 0.11~{\rm fm}^{-3}$.
- The muonic $^{24}$Mg atom has a lifetime of 1067 ns (from prior work cited as Suzuki 1987).
- $^{24}$Mg has a natural abundance of 79%.

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of muon capture from very light nuclei ($A \leq 6$) studied with quantum Monte Carlo methods (Lovato 2019, King 2021) to the $sd$-shell nucleus $^{24}$Mg, using VS-IMSRG for the first time with realistic bound-muon wave functions. It compares against phenomenological shell-model calculations (using the USDB interaction from Brown 2006) and prior NSM-based OMC studies (Siiskonen 1998, 1999, 2001; Kortelainen 2000, 2002, 2004; Suzuki 2018), as well as pnQRPA frameworks (Giannaka 2015, Jokiniemi 2019b, Simkovic 2020, Ciccarelli 2020). The work serves as a benchmark for future OMC measurements at PSI (Belov 2020) and enables further studies of the $g_{\rm A}$ quenching puzzle at high momentum exchange relevant to neutrinoless double-beta decay.
