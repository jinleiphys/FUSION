# Flavor structure of $Λ$ baryons from lattice QCD: From strange to charm quarks

> We study $Λ$ baryons of spin-parity $\frac{1}{2}^{\pm}$ with either a strange or charm valence quark in full 2+1 flavor lattice QCD. Multiple $SU(3)$ singlet and octet operators are employed to generate the desired single baryon states on the lattice. Via the variational method, the couplings of these states to the different operators provide information about the flavor structure of the $Λ$ baryons. We make use of the gauge configurations of the PACS-CS Collaboration and chirally extrapolate the results for the masses and $SU(3)$ flavor components to the physical point. We furthermore gradually change the hopping parameter of the heaviest quark from strange to charm to study how the properties of the $Λ$ baryons evolve as a function of the heavy quark mass. It is found that the baryon energy levels increase almost linearly with the quark mass. Meanwhile, the flavor structure of most of the states remains stable, with the exception of the lowest $\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ state, which changes from a flavor singlet $Λ$ to a $Λ_c$ state with singlet and octet components of comparable size. Finally, we discuss whether our findings can be interpreted with the help of a simple quark model and find that the negative-parity $Λ_c$ states can be naturally explained as diquark excitations of the light $u$ and $d$ quarks. On the other hand, the quark-model picture does not appear to be adequate for the negative-parity $Λ$ states, suggesting the importance of other degrees of freedom to describe them.

## Key claim

The flavor structure of most Lambda baryon states remains stable as the heavy valence quark mass is varied from strange to charm, except for the lowest negative-parity state, which transitions from a flavor singlet in the strange sector to a state with comparable singlet and octet components in the charm sector. The authors find that a simple quark model can explain the negative-parity Lambda_c states as diquark excitations but is inadequate for the negative-parity Lambda states.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations using PACS-CS gauge configurations with a lattice spacing of 0.0907 fm and a 32^3 x 64 lattice. They constructed 4x4 cross-correlation matrices using three flavor-octet and one flavor-singlet interpolating operators for spin-1/2 Lambda baryons, then applied the variational method to extract eigenenergies and coupling strengths. The heavy quark hopping parameter was varied across five values from strange (kappa_s = 0.13640) to charm (kappa_c = 0.1224), with light quark masses corresponding to pion masses from approximately 700 MeV to 290 MeV, and results were chirally extrapolated to the physical point.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907 fm, lattice size: 32^3 x 64
- Light quark hopping parameters: 0.13700, 0.13727, 0.13754, and 0.13770, giving pion masses from approximately 700 MeV to 290 MeV
- Strange quark hopping parameter: kappa_s = 0.13640; charm quark hopping parameter: kappa_c = 0.1224
- For the Lambda(1/2^-) state at the physical point: the state lies between the pi Sigma and anti-K N thresholds, reproducing the experimental level ordering
- The third excited negative-parity state lies about 500 MeV above the lowest three states for both Lambda and Lambda_c
- For positive parity Lambda_c, the first excited state is found about 500 MeV above the ground state
- The negative-parity Lambda_c baryon lies below both the pi Sigma_c and D N thresholds for all pion masses studied

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of the Lambda(1405) by Menadue (2011) and Takahashi (2009), confirming that the lowest negative-parity Lambda state lies between the pi Sigma and anti-K N thresholds at the physical point. The study also builds on quark model investigations by Yoshida that suggested diquark degrees of freedom emerge in Lambda_c baryons. The results enable future experimental searches for predicted excited Lambda_c states and provide lattice QCD input for understanding the transition from flavor SU(3) symmetry to heavy quark symmetry as the heavy quark mass increases.
