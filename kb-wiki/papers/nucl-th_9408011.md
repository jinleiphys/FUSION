# Muon to Electron Conversion; A Symbiosis of Particle and Nuclear Physics

> $μ-e$ conversion is the experimentally most interesting lepton flavor violating process. From a theoretical point of view it is an interesting interplay of particle and nuclear physics. The effective transition operator, depending on the gauge model, is in general described in terms of a combination of four terms of transition operators (isoscalar and isovector, Fermi-like as well as axial vector-like). The experimentally most interesting ground state to ground state transition is adequately described in terms of the usual proton and neutron form factors. These were computed in both the shell model and RPA. Since it is of interest to know the portion of the strength exhausted by the coherent (ground state to ground state) transition, the total transition rate to all final states must also be computed. This was done i) in RPA by explicitly summing over all final states ii) in the context of the closure approximation (using shell model and RPA for constructing the initial state) and iii) in the context of nuclear matter mapped into nuclei via a local density approximation. We found that, apart from small local oscillations, the conversion rate keeps increasing from light to heavy nuclear elements. We also find that the coherent mode is dominant (it exhausts more than 90\% of the sum rule). Various gauge models are discussed. In general the predicted branching ratio is much smaller compared to the present experimental limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, across the periodic table, the coherent (ground state to ground state) muon-to-electron conversion mode dominates the total conversion rate, exhausting more than 90% of the sum rule, and that the conversion rate increases monotonically from light to heavy nuclei, contrary to earlier beliefs of a maximum near A ≈ 60. These conclusions are hedged by the caveat that the predicted branching ratios in all gauge models considered are much smaller than the present experimental limits.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear matrix elements for muon-to-electron conversion using three approaches: (i) the shell model and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) for constructing initial and final nuclear states; (ii) the closure approximation with a mean excitation energy, applied in both shell model and RPA; and (iii) a nuclear matter approach mapped into finite nuclei via a local density approximation (LDA) using the relativistic Lindhard function. The effective transition operator was expressed as a combination of isoscalar and isovector, Fermi-like and axial-vector-like terms, and the coherent ground-state-to-ground-state transition was described in terms of proton and neutron form factors.

## Key numbers

- The coherent mode exhausts more than 90% of the sum rule (η ≥ 90%) throughout the periodic table.
- For the photonic mechanism, the nuclear parameter κ (which combines proton and neutron form factor contributions) lies in the range 1.67 ≤ κ ≤ 1.89.
- The nuclear factor γ_ph varies from 1.6 to 26 across nuclei, with the largest value for ¹³²Sn and the smallest for ¹²C.
- The ratio of the muon-to-electron conversion branching ratio to the μ → eγ branching ratio ranges from 1.5 × 10⁻³ (for ¹²C) to 3 × 10⁻² (for ¹³²Sn).
- In the supersymmetric model considered, the predicted branching ratio R_{e⁻N} lies between 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁸ and 2.4 × 10⁻¹⁶.
- The experimental upper limit for μ⁻Ti → e⁻Ti is 4.6 × 10⁻¹² (90% CL, TRIUMF, Ahmad et al., 1987).
- The experimental upper limit for μ⁻Pb → e⁻Pb is 4.9 × 10⁻¹⁰ (90% CL, Ahmad et al., 1988).
- Ground state correlations reduce both coherent and total matrix elements by about 30% (for ⁴⁸Ti).
- Including the muon binding energy in heavy nuclei increases the coherent matrix element by about 40% compared to calculations that ignore it.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of muon-to-electron conversion nuclear matrix elements by Kosmas, Vergados, and collaborators, and contradicts the earlier claim by Weinberg and Feinberg that the conversion rate peaks near A ≈ 60. The paper provides a systematic comparison of shell model, RPA, and nuclear-matter-mapped-into-nuclei (LDA) methods, and shows that the coherent mode dominates across all nuclei. The results enable more reliable predictions for upcoming experimental searches at facilities such as SINDRUM II and future muon-to-electron conversion experiments, and they clarify that the nuclear dependence is not strong enough to overcome the small predicted branching ratios from gauge models.
