# The Lamb Shift in Dimensional Regularization

> We present a simple derivation of the Lamb shift using effective field theory techniques and dimensional regularisation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a derivation of the Lamb shift that is simpler and more transparent than conventional textbook treatments by using two sequential effective field theories (NRQED and pNRQED) together with dimensional regularization, which handles both ultraviolet and infrared divergences in a manifestly gauge-invariant way. The derivation is limited to the Lamb shift at order \(m\alpha^5\) for hydrogen-like atoms with a static nucleus.

## Method

The authors first match QED to NRQED at one loop, integrating out the hard scale (the electron mass), then match NRQED to pNRQED by integrating out the soft scale (the typical relative momentum \(m\alpha\)), producing potential terms that are non-local in space. The pNRQED Lagrangian is then projected onto the one-electron one-nucleus subspace, and the ultrasoft photon contribution to the bound-state energy is calculated from a single diagram in dimensional regularization, with the divergent part absorbed by a counterterm in the pNRQED Lagrangian using the \(\overline{MS}\) subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- The matching coefficient for the spin-orbit term is \(c_S = 1 + \alpha/\pi\).
- The matching coefficient for the Darwin term is \(c_D = 1 + (\alpha/\pi)(8/3)\log(m/\mu)\).
- The matching coefficient for the photon kinetic term is \(d_2 = \alpha/(60\pi)\).
- The ultrasoft contribution to the energy shift is given by \(\delta^{US} E_n = (2/3)(\alpha/\pi)\left[(\log(\mu/m) + 5/6 - \log 2)(Ze^2/2)|\phi_n(0)|^2/m^2 - \sum_{m\neq n} |\langle n| \mathbf{v} |m\rangle|^2 (E_n - E_m)\log(m/|E_n - E_m|)\right]\).
- The total width from the imaginary part is \(\Gamma_n = \sum_{m<n} (4/3)\alpha |\langle n| \mathbf{v} |m\rangle|^2 (E_n - E_m)\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to the Lamb shift pioneered by Labelle (1996), who used NRQED with a cutoff regulator and photon mass, by introducing pNRQED as a second effective theory for ultrasoft photons and using dimensional regularization. The derivation enables a systematic and gauge-invariant calculation of bound-state energy shifts in hydrogen-like atoms and is directly applicable to heavy quarkonium bound states in QCD, as outlined by Pineda and Soto (1997) for pNRQCD.
