# Strong decays of the low-lying doubly bottom baryons

> In this work, we adopt the $^3P_0$ model to investigate the strong decays of the low-lying doubly bottom baryons in the $j-j$ coupling scheme systematically. In this scheme, we construct the formulism of $^3P_0$ model under the spectator assumption, and then the heavy diquark symmetry is preserved automatically. Our results show that some of the $λ$-mode $Ξ_{bb}(1P)$ and $Ω_{bb}(1P)$ states are narrow, which have good potentials to be observed by future experiments. For the low-lying $ρ$-mode and $ρ$-$λ$ hybrid states, the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka-allowed strong decays are highly suppressed and they should be extremely narrow. Future experiments can test our phenomenological predictions at the quark level.

## Key claim
The authors use the \(^3P_0\) model in the \(j-j\) coupling scheme to predict that some \(\lambda\)-mode \(\Xi_{bb}(1P)\) and \(\Omega_{bb}(1P)\) states are narrow and potentially observable, while low-lying \(\rho\)-mode and \(\rho\)-\(\lambda\) hybrid states have highly suppressed strong decays; these predictions are phenomenological and depend on the assumption that the heavy diquark symmetry is approximately preserved for doubly bottom baryons.

## Method
The authors adopt the \(^3P_0\) model under the spectator assumption to calculate Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka-allowed two-body strong decays of low-lying doubly bottom baryons (\(\Xi_{bb}\) and \(\Omega_{bb}\)) in the \(j-j\) coupling scheme, which automatically preserves heavy diquark symmetry. They use simple harmonic oscillator wave functions with a harmonic oscillator parameter \(\alpha_\lambda = 0.4~\text{GeV}\) and a constant quark pair creation strength \(\gamma = 0.4\), taken from meson decay studies, and employ masses from a relativistic quark model (Ebert 2002) with estimated masses for \(1D\) states from Regge trajectories and heavy diquark symmetry.

## Key numbers
- The average mass of \(\Xi_{bb}(1S)\) states is 10225 MeV; the average mass of \(\Xi_{bb}(1P)\) states is 10664 MeV.
- The average mass of \(\Omega_{bb}(1S)\) states is 10379 MeV; the average mass of \(\Omega_{bb}(1P)\) states is 10798 MeV.
- For the \(\Xi_{bb}(1P)\) states, the \(j=1/2\) doublet has total decay widths of about 196 MeV (\(\Xi_{bb}(\frac{1}{2}^-, \frac{1}{2})\)) and 206 MeV (\(\Xi_{bb}(\frac{3}{2}^-, \frac{1}{2})\)), while the \(j=3/2\) triplet has narrower total widths: 27.21 MeV (\(\Xi_{bb}(\frac{1}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2})\)), 37.81 MeV (\(\Xi_{bb}(\frac{3}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2})\)), and 53.15 MeV (\(\Xi_{bb}(\frac{5}{2}^-, \frac{3}{2})\)).
- For the \(\Xi_{bb}(2S)\) state, the total decay width is about 93 MeV, with branching ratios \(Br(\Xi_{bb} \pi) = 9.9\%\) and \(Br(\Xi_{bb}^{*} \pi) = 90.1\%\); for the \(\Xi_{bb}^{*}(2S)\) state, the total width is about 79 MeV, with branching ratios \(Br(\Xi_{bb} \pi) = 39.4\%\), \(Br(\Xi_{bb}^{*} \pi) = 59.8\%\), and \(Br(\Omega_{bb} K) = 0.8\%\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of strong decays for doubly heavy baryons, which had focused mainly on doubly charmed baryons (e.g., Xiao 2017, Eakins 2012) and used the \(L-S\) coupling scheme; here the \(j-j\) coupling scheme is applied to doubly bottom baryons to better preserve heavy diquark symmetry due to the larger bottom quark mass. The results show similar decay patterns to bottom and bottom-strange mesons (Lu 2016), supporting the idea that the \(bb\) diquark acts like a static color source, and provide narrow states and specific branching ratios that can guide future experimental searches at LHCb or other facilities.
