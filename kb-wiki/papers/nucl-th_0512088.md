# Strangeness dynamics in heavy-ion collisions: the K/pi ratios and the lifetime of a fireball

> We propose a non-equilibrium, hadronic kinetic model for describing the relative abundance of strange particles in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The energy dependence of the multiplicity ratios of charged kaons and lambdas to pions are studied in detail. The pronounced peak in positively charged K/pi is conjectured to be due to the decreasing lifetime of a fireball with an increase in beam energy above 30 AGeV. Such a behaviour may be the consequence of a stronger stopping at lower beam energies. We study the dependence of the multiplicity ratios on energy and baryon density of the fireball and its total lifetime.

## Key claim

The pronounced peak in the positively charged kaon-to-pion ratio (the "horn") at a beam energy of 30 AGeV can be explained by a decreasing fireball lifetime with increasing beam energy above that point, without invoking a phase transition to deconfined matter, provided that a non-equilibrium hadronic kinetic model with specific expansion scenarios is used.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-equilibrium hadronic kinetic model that evolves the densities of \(K^+\), \(K^0\), \(K^{*+}\), and \(K^{*0}\) via a master equation incorporating production and annihilation reactions, expansion dilution, and thermal averaging of cross sections. The fireball expansion is parameterized with a quadratic initial phase matched to a power-law late phase, tuned by the initial energy density, total lifetime, and final freeze-out conditions taken from the statistical hadronization model of Becattini et al. (2004). All non-strange hadrons are assumed chemically equilibrated, and strange species with \(S<0\) are kept in relative chemical equilibrium with each other, while kaon densities are evolved explicitly.

## Key numbers

* The peak in the \(\langle K^+ \rangle / \langle \pi^+ \rangle\) ratio occurs at a beam energy of 30 \(A\)GeV, corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.6\) GeV.
* Kaon scattering rates are typically of order 0.1 fm\(^{-1}\), leading to a mean free path of about 7 fm for thermal kaons (velocity \(\approx 0.7c\)) and about 10 fm for light-speed kaons.
* At the beginning of the fireball evolution, at most 6% of the total energy is contained in kaons.
* The chemical freeze-out temperature for 30 \(A\)GeV collisions is 139.0 MeV, with a baryon chemical potential of 423.1 MeV (from Becattini et al. (2004)).
* The chemical freeze-out temperature for 158 \(A\)GeV collisions is 157.8 MeV, with a baryon chemical potential of 243.7 MeV (from Becattini et al. (2004)).

## Context

This work extends the debate on the origin of the "horn" in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) excitation function, previously interpreted by the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES) as evidence for a first-order phase transition to deconfined matter at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.6\) GeV. The authors challenge the necessity of deconfinement by showing that a purely hadronic, non-equilibrium scenario with a decreasing fireball lifetime above 30 \(A\)GeV can reproduce the data, contradicting the broader maximum predicted by the statistical model of Cleymans et al. (2004) and the modest bump from transport models UrQMD and HSD. This work enables future studies to test whether hadronic scenarios can be ruled out by additional observables such as multi-strange baryon ratios or femtoscopic data.
