# Probing the shape of the quark-gluon plasma droplet via event-by-event QGP tomography

> This study investigates Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions through two avenues: high-$p_{\perp}$ frameworks and hydrodynamic modeling. Using the T$_{\text{R}}$ENTo model, we find that IP-Glasma mimicking $p=0$ value aligns well with high-$p_{\perp}$ data, in agreement with Bayesian analysis of the low-$p_{\perp}$ regime. While adjusting $p$ values may improve a fit to a particular high-$p_{\perp}$ observable, it does not permit an earlier onset of transverse expansion.

## Key claim

The high-\(p_{\perp}\) data (nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and flow harmonics \(v_2\), \(v_3\), \(v_4\)) favors the T\(_{\text{R}}\)ENTo parameter value \(p \approx 0\) for the initial state of the quark-gluon plasma droplet, in agreement with Bayesian analyses of low-\(p_{\perp}\) data; however, even a highly anisotropic initial state (very negative \(p\)) does not permit an earlier onset of transverse expansion while still reproducing the high-\(p_{\perp}\) observables.

## Method

The authors generated event-by-event fluctuating initial entropy profiles using the T\(_{\text{R}}\)ENTo model for \(p\) values in \(\{1/3, 0, -1/3, -2/3, -1, -3\}\), with no pre-equilibrium free-streaming and fluid-dynamical evolution starting at \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm (and later at 0.6 fm and 0.2 fm) using the VISHNew code with shear viscosity adjusted per \(p\) to reproduce low-\(p_{\perp}\) charged particle multiplicities and elliptic flow \(v_2\{4\}\). High-\(p_{\perp}\) observables (\(R_{AA}\), \(v_2\), \(v_3\), \(v_4\)) for charged hadrons, D mesons, and B mesons were computed using the generalized DREENA-A energy loss framework, which incorporates both radiative and collisional energy loss without free parameters, and compared to experimental data from CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- The T\(_{\text{R}}\)ENTo parameter \(p\) was varied over the set \(\{1/3, 0, -1/3, -2/3, -1, -3\}\) to modulate initial state shape.
- Shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratios \(\eta/s\) used for each \(p\) at \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm ranged from 0.03 (for \(p = 1/3\)) to 0.29 (for \(p = -3\)); for \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm, \(\eta/s = 0.17\) (\(p = 0\)) and 0.33 (\(p = -3\)); for \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm, \(\eta/s = 0.20\) (\(p = 0\)) and 0.60 (\(p = -3\)).
- The initial time for fluid-dynamical evolution was set to \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm, 0.6 fm, and 0.2 fm in different parts of the study.
- The fluid was particlized on an isothermal hypersurface at \(T_{\mathrm{sw}} = 151\) MeV.
- The specific bulk viscosity maximum was \((\zeta/s)_{\text{max}} = 0.03\).
- The charm quark mass was taken as 1.2 GeV and bottom quark mass as 4.75 GeV; the magnetic to electric mass ratio was \(\mu_M/\mu_E = 0.6\); \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.2\) GeV; effective number of light quark flavors \(n_f = 3\).
- The analysis covered centrality classes 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50% for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analyses of Bernhard (2019) and Nijs (2020), which used low-\(p_{\perp}\) data to constrain the T\(_{\text{R}}\)ENTo parameter to \(p \approx 0\), by testing whether high-\(p_{\perp}\) observables (QGP tomography) favor the same initial shape. It also builds on the authors' prior studies (Stojku 2020, 2023) which found that high-\(p_{\perp}\) observables require transverse expansion to be delayed until \(\tau \approx 1\) fm; here they test whether a more anisotropic initial state (very negative \(p\)) could allow earlier expansion. The results confirm the \(p \approx 0\) preference and rule out earlier transverse expansion even with extreme anisotropy, while also highlighting a persistent "high-\(p_{\perp}\) \(v_4\) puzzle" where the framework underpredicts experimental \(v_4\) data, suggesting that alternative initial state or energy loss models may be needed.
