# Pion productions by proton and Helium-3 on Au197 target at beam energies of 2.8, 5, 10 and 16.587 GeV/nucleon

> Based on a Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling- Uhlenbeck transport model, proton and $^{3}$He induced reactions on $^{197}$Au target at beam energies of 2.8, 5, 10 and 16.587 GeV/nucleon are studied. It is found that compared with proton induced reactions, $^{3}$He induced reactions give larger cross sections of pion production, about 5 times those of the proton induced reactions. And more importantly, pion production from $^{3}$He induced reaction is more inclined to low-angle emission. Neutrino production via positively charged pion is also discussed accordingly.

## Key claim
Using a Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model, the authors find that Helium-3 induced reactions on a gold target produce about 5 times more pions than proton induced reactions at the same beam energy per nucleon, and that these pions are emitted more strongly at forward angles (low-angle emission), making Helium-3 beams potentially more suitable for neutrino production in accelerator-based experiments.

## Method
The authors used the ART (A Relativistic Transport) model, a relativistic form of the BUU model, to simulate proton and Helium-3 induced reactions on a Au-197 target at beam energies of 2.8, 5, 10, and 16.587 GeV/nucleon. The model includes baryons (N, Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Lambda, Sigma), mesons (pi, rho, omega, eta, K), and their elastic and inelastic scatterings, with a Skyrme-type mean field parametrization (compressibility K=201 MeV). The simulations were validated by comparing pion production cross sections from p+Au at 17.5 GeV/c against E910 experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For p+Au and He-3+Au at 17.5 GeV/c (16.587 GeV/nucleon), differential cross sections of pion production from He-3 induced reactions are about 5 times those of proton induced reactions.
- At low-angle (cos theta between 0.9 and 1) and high momentum, cross sections of pion production from He-3+Au are 5 to 10 times those of p+Au across the beam energy range 2.8 to 16.587 GeV/nucleon.
- At the highest beam momentum (17.5 GeV/c), the relative emitting number at low-angle (cos theta between 0.9 and 1) reaches about 50% for He-3+Au, compared to about 25% at 2.8 GeV/nucleon.
- The low-angle relative pion emission for He-3+Au is about 5% to 10% larger than for p+Au at the studied beam energies.
- Cross sections of charged pion production for both p+Au and He-3+Au increase by about a factor of 3 over the studied beam energy range (2.8 to 16.587 GeV/nucleon).
- The ratio of cross sections of charged pion production from He-3 induced reactions to proton induced reactions is about 5, larger than the ratio of projectile mass numbers (A_He3 / A_H = 3).
- At 2.8 GeV/nucleon, the ratio of mean pion production per nucleon of projectile with mass number A to that of a proton (A=1) is always larger than 1, indicating non-independent nucleon contributions.
- The most probable neutrino energy from pion decay is about 30 to 70 MeV for several GeV incident beam energy.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of pion production in proton-nucleus reactions, such as those by the HARP Collaboration (2009) and the E910 experiment (Chemtob, 2002), by systematically comparing proton and Helium-3 projectiles on a gold target using the ART transport model. The results suggest that Helium-3 beams could provide higher forward-directed pion fluxes for neutrino beam facilities, complementing simulations using Monte Carlo generators like GEANT4 (Agostinelli, 2003) and MARS (Mokhov, 2008). The findings enable further studies of neutrino production via pion decay, with implications for the design of target/capture systems in muon storage rings and neutrino oscillation experiments.
