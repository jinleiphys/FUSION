# Finite size effect on the thermodynamics of a hot and magnetized hadron resonance gas

> The thermodynamic properties of a non-interacting ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) of finite volume have been studied in the presence of an external magnetic field. The inclusion of background magnetic field in the calculation of thermodynamic potential is done by the modification of the dispersion relations of the charged hadrons in terms of Landau quantization. The generalized Matsubara prescription has been employed to take into account the finite size effects in which a periodic (anti-periodic) boundary conditions is considered for the mesons (baryons). We find significant effects of the magnetic field as well as system size on the temperature dependence of energy density, longitudinal and transverse pressure especially in low temperature regions. The HRG is found to exhibit diamagnetism (paramagnetism) in the low (high) temperature region whereas the finite size effect is seen to strengthen the diamagnetic behavior of the medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that finite system size significantly modifies the thermodynamic properties of a hot hadron resonance gas in an external magnetic field, with the effect being most pronounced at low temperatures; however, this conclusion is conditional on the use of periodic (anti-periodic) boundary conditions for mesons (baryons) and the subtraction of vacuum contributions via normalization at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential of a non-interacting ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) by modifying the dispersion relations of charged hadrons through Landau quantization in a constant external magnetic field along the z-direction. Finite volume effects are incorporated using a generalized Matsubara prescription that replaces momentum integrals with discrete sums over modes, applying periodic boundary conditions for mesons and anti-periodic boundary conditions for baryons in a cubic box of side length L. All thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, magnetization) are normalized by subtracting their zero-temperature values at the same magnetic field to remove divergent vacuum contributions.

## Key numbers

* At zero magnetic field, the HRG model results for scaled pressure P/T^4 and energy density ε/T^4 for infinite system size (L = ∞) agree with Lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014) up to temperatures near the transition region.
* For finite system sizes, the scaled pressure and energy density increase substantially as L decreases; the effect is most prominent at low temperatures where Boltzmann suppression is strongest.
* At non-zero magnetic field, longitudinal pressure P_∥/T^4 shows non-monotonic behavior with magnetic field: it decreases with increasing eB at low temperatures but increases at high temperatures, for eB = 0.10 GeV^2 and eB = 0.15 GeV^2.
* Transverse pressure P_⊥/T^4 shows almost monotonic increase with magnetic field due to an extra factor of eB in the numerator of its expression.
* The energy density is the least affected by finite system size compared to longitudinal and transverse pressure.

## Context

This work extends previous HRG model studies at zero magnetic field (Bhattacharyya 2015) and magnetized HRG studies (Endrodi 2013, Tawfik 2016) by simultaneously incorporating finite volume effects. It also connects to broader efforts using effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Abreu 2019) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Shi 2018) to study finite-size effects in QCD matter. The results are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC, where the fireball volume is finite (estimated freeze-out volumes of 50 to 250 fm^3 from Graef 2012), and for understanding the early universe where strong magnetic fields may have existed.
