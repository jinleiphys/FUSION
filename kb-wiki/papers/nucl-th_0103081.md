# a0(980)-f0(980) mixing and isospin violation in the reactions pN -> d a0, pd -> 3He/3H a0 and dd -> 4He a0

> It is demonstrated that f_0-a_0 mixing can lead to a comparatively large isospin violation in the reactions pN -> da_0, pd -> ^3He/^3H a_0 and dd -> ^4He a_0 close to the corresponding production thresholds. The observation of such mixing effects is possible, e.g., by measuring the forward-backward asymmetry in the reaction pn -> d a_0^0 -> d eta pi^0.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that \(f_0(980)\)-\(a_0(980)\) mixing can produce large isospin-violating effects in several near-threshold nuclear reactions, with the magnitude depending on the mixing intensity of \(|\xi|^2 = (8 \pm 3)\%\) as suggested by WA102 data, but also on unknown relative phases between the \(f_0\) and \(a_0\) production amplitudes.

## Method

The authors use a two-step model (TSM) previously applied to meson production in \(pN \to dX\) reactions, where the dominant mechanism is \(u\)-channel nucleon exchange. They calculate differential and total cross sections for \(a_0\) and \(f_0\) production in \(pN \to da_0\), \(pd \to {}^{3}\mathrm{He}/{}^{3}\mathrm{H}\, a_0\), and \(dd \to {}^{4}\mathrm{He}\, a_0\) reactions, incorporating finite resonance widths via a Flatte mass distribution for the \(a_0\) and a Breit-Wigner distribution for the \(f_0\). The mixing effect is introduced by adding an \(S\)-wave \(f_0\) production amplitude to the \(P\)-wave \(a_0\) production amplitude, and the resulting forward-backward asymmetry and cross-section ratios are evaluated.

## Key numbers

- Mixing intensity from WA102 data: \(|\xi|^2 = 8 \pm 3\%\)
- Ratio of coupling constants: \(g_{f_0NN}/g_{a_0NN} = 2.3\) (using \(g_{a_0NN} = 3.7\) and \(g_{f_0NN} = 8.5\)); alternatively \(R(f_0/a_0) = 1.46\) from Mull and Holinde (37% lower)
- Forward-backward asymmetry at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} = 180^\circ\) for \(pn \to d a_0^0\): \(A_a(180^\circ) = 0.86\)–\(0.96\) for \(R(f_0/a_0) = 1.46\), or \(0.9\)–\(0.98\) for \(R(f_0/a_0) = 2.3\)
- Nonresonant background cross section for \(pn \to d \pi^0 \eta\): \(\sigma_{\mathrm{BG}} \simeq 0.8\ \mu\)b (cut-off \(\Lambda_\pi = 1\) GeV)
- Resonant \(a_0\) cross section and background for different \(M_{\eta\pi^0}\) cuts: \(\sigma_{a_0} (\sigma_{\mathrm{BG}}) = 0.3 (0.4),\ 0.27 (0.29),\ 0.19 (0.15)\ \mu\)b for \(M_{\eta\pi^0} \geq 0.85,\ 0.9,\ 0.95\) GeV respectively
- Cross section for \(pd \to {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\, K^+K^-\) from MOMO collaboration: \(\sigma = 9.6 \pm 1.0\) nb at \(Q = 40\) MeV and \(17.5 \pm 1.8\) nb at \(Q = 56\) MeV
- Expected cross section for \(pd \to {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\, a_0^0 \to {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\, \pi^0 \eta\): upper limit about 80–150 nb at \(Q = 40\)–60 MeV
- \(a_0\) and \(f_0\) widths: about 40–100 MeV; for calculations: \(\Gamma_{a_0 \to \pi\eta} = 70\) MeV, \(\Gamma(K\bar K)/\Gamma(\pi\eta) = 0.23\), \(f_0\) width \(\Gamma_R \simeq 70\) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Achasov (1979) and others that \(a_0(980)\)-\(f_0(980)\) mixing arises from their coupling to the \(K\bar K\) threshold, and follows the observation by Close and Kirk (2000) that WA102 data indicate significant mixing intensity. The proposed reactions provide experimental tests analogous to charge-symmetry breaking studies via \(\pi^0\)-\(\eta\) mixing in reactions such as \(pn \to d\pi^0\) (Tippens, 1998) and \(pd \to {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\,\pi^0\) (Magiera, ongoing at COSY-Julich). The calculations enable dedicated searches with the ANKE spectrometer at COSY-Julich, where corresponding measurements are in preparation.
