# Effect of Flow on Caloric Curve for Finite Nuclei

> In a finite temperature Thomas-Fermi theory, we construct caloric curves for finite nuclei enclosed in a freeze-out volume few times the normal nuclear volume, with and without inclusion of flow. Without flow, the caloric curve indicates a smooth liquid-gas phase transition whereas with flow, the transition may be very sharp. We discuss these results in the context of two recent experiments, one for heavy symmetric system (Au + Au at 600A MeV) and the other for highly asymmetric system (Au + C at 1A GeV) where different behaviours in the caloric curves are seen.

## Key claim

In a finite temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) theory, the caloric curve for finite nuclei enclosed in a freeze-out volume shows a smooth liquid-gas phase transition without flow, but with inclusion of collective radial flow the transition may become very sharp, potentially explaining the different behaviors observed in Au+Au at 600A MeV and Au+C at 1A GeV experiments.

## Method

The authors used a finite temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) theory with a Seyler-Blanchard type momentum and density dependent finite range two-body effective interaction, including Coulomb terms. They modeled the effect of collective radial flow by introducing a negative external pressure \(P_0\) in the thermodynamic potential at freeze-out, with the system enclosed in a freeze-out volume \(V = 8 V_0\) (where \(V_0\) is the normal nuclear volume at zero temperature). Calculations were performed for the system \(^{150}\)Sm at three constant pressures (\(P_0 = 0, -0.05, -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)) and also with an excitation-energy-dependent flow that saturates at \(T \simeq 5\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the system \(^{150}\)Sm without flow (\(P_0 = 0\)), the caloric curve shows a kink at \(T \simeq 10\) MeV.
- With flow pressure \(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), the caloric curve shows a plateau at \(T \simeq 5\) MeV in the excitation energy range of 5–10 MeV per particle.
- The average flow energy per nucleon for \(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) is \(\simeq 1.3\) MeV.
- Without flow, the phase transition temperature is \(T \simeq 9.5\) MeV; with flow (\(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\)), the transition occurs at \(T \simeq 5\) MeV.
- With flow, the density distribution undergoes a shape transition to a bubble shape within an interval of \(\Delta T \sim 0.1\) MeV near the transition temperature; without flow, the transition occurs over \(\Delta T \sim 0.5\) MeV.
- Transition temperatures differ by at most \(\approx 0.2\) MeV across the mass range of experimental interest (A \(\approx 40\) to A \(\approx 200\)) for \(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier FTTF calculations by Samaddar, De, and Shlomo (1997) that showed a broad-based peak in specific heat at \(T \sim 9-10\) MeV, and addresses the discrepancy with the experimental caloric curve from the Au+Au experiment at 600A MeV by Pochodzalla et al. (1995), which showed a constant temperature of \(T \sim 5\) MeV. The results also help explain the different behavior seen in the Au+C experiment at 1A GeV by Hubele et al. (1995), where the temperature rises slowly but steadily with excitation energy. The work enables a unified understanding of how modest radial flow energy (\(\sim 1.3\) MeV per nucleon) can shift the apparent phase transition from continuous to first-order, and suggests that bubble-shaped configurations may appear during the transition.
