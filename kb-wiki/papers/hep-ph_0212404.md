# Thermalization of fermionic quantum fields

> We solve the nonequilibrium dynamics of a 3+1 dimensional theory with Dirac fermions coupled to scalars via a chirally invariant Yukawa interaction. The results are obtained from a systematic coupling expansion of the 2PI effective action to lowest non-trivial order, which includes scattering as well as memory and off-shell effects. The dynamics is solved numerically without further approximation, for different far-from-equilibrium initial conditions. The late-time behavior is demonstrated to be insensitive to the details of the initial conditions and to be uniquely determined by the initial energy density. Moreover, we show that at late time the system is very well characterized by a thermal ensemble. In particular, we are able to observe the emergence of Fermi--Dirac and Bose--Einstein distributions from the nonequilibrium dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that far-from-equilibrium fermionic quantum fields coupled to scalars via a chirally invariant Yukawa interaction approach a universal late-time thermal state characterized by Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions, with the late-time behavior uniquely determined by the initial energy density and insensitive to the details of the initial conditions, provided one goes beyond Gaussian (mean-field) approximations to include scattering, memory, and off-shell effects.

## Method

The authors solve the nonequilibrium dynamics of a 3+1 dimensional quantum field theory with Dirac fermions coupled to scalar fields via a chirally invariant Yukawa interaction, using a systematic coupling expansion of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to lowest nontrivial (two-loop) order. They derive and numerically solve the exact evolution equations for the spectral function and statistical two-point function for both fermions and scalars, decomposed into Lorentz components under the symmetries of spatial homogeneity, isotropy, parity, CP-invariance, and chiral symmetry, without further approximations beyond the truncation of the 2PI effective action.

## Key numbers

- The system is solved in 3+1 dimensions with two fermion flavors (quarks) coupled to a scalar sigma field and a triplet of pseudoscalar pions via a Yukawa coupling \(g\), with a purely quadratic scalar potential \(V = \frac{1}{2} m^2_0 (\sigma^2 + \pi^2)\).
- The initial condition for the spectral function is fixed by the fermion anticommutation relation: \(\gamma^0 \rho_{ij}(x,y)|_{x^0=y^0} = i \delta(\bx-\by)\,\delta_{ij}\).
- The late-time behavior is demonstrated to be universal and characterized by Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions, with the system losing dependence on the details of the initial condition and being uniquely determined by the initial energy density.

## Context

This work extends the study of thermalization from scalar field theories (Berges 2000, Berges 2001, Aarts 2002) to include fermionic fields, going beyond previous Gaussian (mean-field, Hartree) approximations that exhibit spurious conserved quantities and non-universal late-time behavior (Cooper 1996, Aarts 2001). It enables first-principle calculations of thermalization in fermionic quantum field theories relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions and early-universe cosmology, without recourse to coarse-graining or effective kinetic descriptions.
