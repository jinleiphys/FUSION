# Hyperon threshold and stellar radii

> We study how the $Λ$ hyperon threshold influences the radius of the canonical $1.4 ~M_\odot$ neutron star in the light of the measurements found in the recent literature. We show that the onset of a new degree of freedom not only causes the well known reduction of the maximum mass, but also compacts the neutron stars with high central density. With the help of the strange mesons $ϕ$ and $σ^*$, we show that it is possible to simulate very compact neutron stars keeping realistic hyperon potentials, $U_Λ(n_0)= -28$ MeV and $ U_Λ^Λ(n_0/5)$ in agreement with recents measurements. In the end we generalize these results showing that the onset of a yet not known dark matter particle with mass of 1.04 GeV is able to produce simultaneously a 2 $M_\odot$ neutron star and a canonical one with a radius of only 11.62 km.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the onset of a new degree of freedom (such as the Λ hyperon or a hypothetical dark matter particle) not only reduces the maximum mass of neutron stars, but also compresses the radii of stars with central densities above the threshold density, provided the new particle appears at sufficiently low densities. The authors demonstrate that by tuning the coupling constants of strange mesons φ and σ*, they can simultaneously reproduce a 2 M⊙ neutron star and a canonical 1.4 M⊙ star with a radius as low as 12.45 km (for hyperons) or 11.62 km (for a dark matter particle), while keeping the Λ potential depth at the realistic value of UΛ = −28 MeV.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model based on a slightly modified GM1 parametrization, solving the mean-field approximation to obtain the equation of state (EoS) for beta-equilibrium matter. They introduce Λ hyperons as a new degree of freedom, fixing the Λ potential depth to UΛ = −28 MeV, and systematically vary the Λ-meson coupling constants for the ω, σ, φ, and σ* mesons. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the results are compared with the observed mass of PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙) and recent radius constraints for the canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star.

## Key numbers

- Λ potential depth fixed to UΛ = −28 MeV, consistent with experimental measurements.
- For the best hyperon model (set D-VI), maximum mass = 1.97 M⊙, radius of the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star = 12.45 km, central density at maximum mass = 8.11 n0.
- For the dark matter particle (mass 1.04 GeV) with potential depth −33 MeV (set H), maximum mass = 2.00 M⊙, radius of the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star = 11.62 km.
- Hyperon threshold density for set D (lowest Λ-ω coupling) occurs at 1.83 n0; for set A (highest Λ-ω coupling) at 2.40 n0.
- Hyperon-hyperon potential UΛΛ(n0/5) calculated to be approximately −2 MeV, lying between the experimental range of −4 to −8 MeV from Fortin (1995) and the value −0.67 MeV from Ahn (2013).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon effects in neutron stars (e.g., Glen (1991), Vidana (2012), Miyatsu (2013)) by focusing specifically on how the hyperon threshold affects the radius of the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star, not just the maximum mass. The authors show that adding the strange scalar meson σ* (which softens the EoS at low densities) combined with the strange vector meson φ (which stiffens it at high densities) can simultaneously satisfy the mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013) and the radius constraints from Hebbeler (2013), Lim (2015), Ozel (2016), and Steiner (2016). The results also reconcile the EoS with heavy-ion collision data from Daniel (2015) for symmetric matter up to five times saturation density. This approach enables future studies of exotic degrees of freedom (including dark matter) as a mechanism to produce compact neutron stars without violating the 2 M⊙ mass limit.
