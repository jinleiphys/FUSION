# Matching Chiral Perturbation Theory and the Dispersive Representation of the Scalar Kpi Form Factor

> We perform a matching of the two loop-chiral perturbation theory representation of the scalar Kpi form factor to a dispersive one. Knowing the value of F_K/F_pi and f_+(0) in the Standard Model (SM) allows to determine two O(p^6) LECs, the slope of the scalar form factor and the deviation of the Callan-Treiman theorem. Going beyond the SM and assuming the knowledge of the slope of the scalar form factor from experiment, the matching allows us to determine the ratio of F_K/F_pi, f_+(0), a certain combination of non-standard couplings, the deviation of the Callan-Treiman theorem and the two O(p^6) LECs.

## Key claim

The matching of two-loop chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to a dispersive representation of the scalar Kπ form factor allows determination of the O(p⁶) low-energy constants (LECs) C₁₂ and C₃₄, the slope λ₀, and the deviation from the Callan-Treiman theorem, but the results depend sensitively on the input value of Δ(0) and on whether one assumes Standard Model couplings or allows for non-standard right-handed current contributions parametrized by εₛ − εₙₛ and δ + εₙₛ.

## Method

The authors match the two-loop ChPT expression for the scalar Kπ form factor from Bijnens and Talavera (2003) to a twice-subtracted dispersive representation introduced by Bernard et al. (2006). The matching uses the derivative and curvature of the form factor at t=0, together with the ChPT expression for f₊(0) and the dispersive relation linking the slope λ₀ to ln C (the form factor at the Callan-Treiman point). The resulting second-order equation for λ₀ is solved as a function of the non-standard parameters εₛ − εₙₛ and y = 2(δ+εₙₛ)/sin²θ̂, and then all other quantities (F_K/F_π, f₊(0), C₁₂, C₃₄, Δ_CT) are derived.

## Key numbers

- In the Standard Model (εₛ = εₙₛ = δ = 0) with Δ(0) = −0.008: λ₀ = 15.20 × 10⁻³, Δ_CT = −0.118 × 10⁻², C₁₂ = −0.421 × 10⁻⁶, C₃₄ = 6.480 × 10⁻⁶
- For a deviation from unitarity Δ_unitarity = −1.5 × 10⁻³ (εₛ − εₙₛ = 0): λ₀ = 15.03 × 10⁻³, f₊(0) = 0.972, F_K/F_π = 1.210
- For λ₀ = 9.0 × 10⁻³ (matching NA48 result) with Δ(0) = −0.008 and εₛ − εₙₛ = −0.032: f₊(0) = 0.987, F_K/F_π = 1.152, C₁₂ = 1.107 × 10⁻⁶, C₃₄ = −0.216 × 10⁻⁶
- The hat values (SM-extracted) are: F̂_π = (92.3 ± 0.1) MeV, F̂_K/F̂_π = 1.192 ± 0.007, f̂₊(0) = 0.9574(52)
- The dispersive input G(0) = 0.0398 ± 0.0036 ± 0.0020, and the curvature relation gives λ′₀ = λ₀² + (4.16 ± 0.50) × 10⁻⁴
- The NLO ChPT prediction for the Callan-Treiman deviation is Δ_CT^{NLO} = (−3.5 ± 8) × 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the dispersive representation of the scalar Kπ form factor developed by Bernard et al. (2006) and the two-loop ChPT calculation of Bijnens and Talavera (2003). It builds on earlier determinations of the O(p⁶) LECs C₁₂ and C₃₄ by Jamin et al. (2004) and Cirigliano et al. (2005) that used experimental knowledge of decay constants. The matching enables simultaneous extraction of F_K/F_π, f₊(0), the LECs, and the Callan-Treiman deviation, and allows testing for right-handed currents beyond the Standard Model as discussed by Bernard et al. (2007). The results are relevant for the NA48, KLOE, KTEV, and ISTRA experiments measuring Kℓ3 decays, and for lattice QCD results from MILC (2007) and UKQCD (2007). The main limitation is the large uncertainty in the ChPT input Δ(0), which prevents precise determinations until updated Kℓ4 fits become available.
