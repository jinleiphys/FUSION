# Softening of the Hypertriton Transverse Momentum Spectrum in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Understanding the properties of hypernuclei helps to constrain the interaction between hyperon and nucleon, which is known to play an essential role in determining the properties of neutron stars. Experimental measurements have suggested that the hypertriton ($^3_Λ\text{H}$), the lightest hypernucleus, exhibits a halo structure with a deuteron core encircled by a $Λ$ hyperon at a distance of about 10 fm. This large $Λ-d$ distance in $^3_Λ\text{H}$ wave function is found to cause a suppressed $^3_Λ\text{H}$ yield and a softening of its transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectrum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Within the coalescence model based on nucleons and $Λ$ hyperons from a microscopic hybrid hydro model with a hadronic afterburner for nuclear cluster production in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 5.02 TeV, we show how this softening of the hypertriton $p_T$ spectrum appears and leads to a smaller mean $p_T$ for $^3_Λ\text{H}$ than for helium-3 ($^3$He). The latter is opposite to the predictions from the blast-wave model which assumes that $^3_Λ\text{H}$ and $^3$He are thermally produced at the kinetic freeze-out of heavy-ion collisions. The discovered quantum mechanical softening of the (anti-)hypertriton spectrum can be experimentally tested in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at different collision energies and centralities and used to obtain valuable insights into the mechanisms for light (hyper-)nuclei production in these collisions.

## Key claim

The large Lambda-deuteron distance (about 10 fm) in the hypertriton wave function causes not only a suppression of its yield but also a softening of its transverse momentum spectrum, leading to a smaller mean pT for hypertriton than for helium-3; this contradicts the blast-wave model prediction that hypertriton should have a larger mean pT due to its larger mass and radial flow.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model (MUSIC+UrQMD+COAL) to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. The quark-gluon plasma evolution was modeled with (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC), hadronization via the Cooper-Frye formula, and subsequent hadronic rescatterings and decays with UrQMD. Hypertriton and helium-3 yields were then computed using a coalescence model that incorporates the Wigner function of the hypertriton internal wave function, with size parameters sigma_1 (deuteron core) and sigma_2 (Lambda-d distance) determined from the Lambda separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Lambda-d distance sqrt(<r^2_Lambda d>) = 9.5 fm for the world average B_Lambda = 0.164 +/- 0.043 MeV; 6.8 fm for B_Lambda = 0.42 MeV (STAR); 11.9 fm for B_Lambda = 0.102 MeV (ALICE)
- Size parameter sigma_2 = 6.52 fm for world average B_Lambda; 5.45 fm for STAR B_Lambda; 7.96 fm for ALICE B_Lambda
- The yield ratio hypertriton/helium-3 varies by a factor of 2-3 among the three B_Lambda values across 0-10%, 10-30%, and 30-50% centralities
- The mean pT ratio <pT>_hypertriton / <pT>_helium-3 is less than 1 in the coalescence model, versus about 1.03-1.04 in the blast-wave model
- Blast-wave model parameters for 0-10% centrality: T_kin = 0.090 GeV, beta_s = 0.907, n = 0.735; for 30-50%: T_kin = 0.105 GeV, beta_s = 0.88, n = 0.828

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model studies of Sun (2018) on hypertriton suppression in small systems to the transverse momentum dependence in large systems (Pb-Pb). It directly contradicts the blast-wave model (a simplified version of the hydrodynamic model used in heavy-ion collisions) and the statistical hadronization model, which both predict a larger mean pT for hypertriton than for helium-3. The results enable experimental tests at different collision energies and centralities to distinguish between thermal and coalescence production mechanisms for light hypernuclei, and can provide constraints on the Lambda-nucleon interaction relevant for neutron star properties.
