# Microscopic justification of the Equal Filling approximation

> The Equal Filling Approximation, a procedure widely used in mean field calculations to treat the dynamics of odd nuclei in a time reversal invariant way, is justified as the consequence of a variational principle over an average energy functional. The ideas of Statistical Quantum Mechanics are employed in the justification. As an illustration of the method, the ground and lowest lying states of some octupole deformed Radium isotopes are computed.

## Key claim

The Equal Filling Approximation (EFA) for treating odd nuclei in mean-field calculations is justified as a statistical quantum mechanics mixed state, where the energy is the statistical average of the Hamiltonian over a density operator that populates the blocked orbital and its time-reversed partner with equal probability 1/2, rather than arising from any single product wave function.

## Method

The authors reformulate the EFA using the finite-temperature HFB formalism but with fixed statistical occupancies of 1/2 for the blocked quasiparticle state and its time-reversed partner (and zero for all others). They show that the EFA energy corresponds to the statistical mean value of the Hamiltonian under this density operator, and that the variational principle applied to this statistical energy yields the standard HFB equations for the EFA, justifying the previously ad-hoc assumptions. The gradient method is then adapted to solve the resulting equations, including treatment of constraints and density-dependent interactions.

## Key numbers

- The EFA density operator assigns probability \( p_\mu = 1 \) for the blocked state \(\mu_B\) and its time-reversed partner \(\overline{\mu}_B\), and \( p_\mu = 0 \) for all other states, yielding occupancies \( f_\mu = 1/2 \) for those two states.
- The EFA energy is an average over four states with equal weights: \( E_{EFA} = \frac{1}{4} \left( \langle \phi | \hat{H} | \phi \rangle + \langle \phi | \beta_{\mu_B} \hat{H} \beta_{\mu_B}^\dagger | \phi \rangle + \langle \phi | \beta_{\overline{\mu}_B} \hat{H} \beta_{\overline{\mu}_B}^\dagger | \phi \rangle + \langle \phi | \beta_{\mu_B} \beta_{\overline{\mu}_B} \hat{H} \beta_{\overline{\mu}_B}^\dagger \beta_{\mu_B}^\dagger | \phi \rangle \right) \).
- The gradient method uses a step size \(\eta\) with a Padé approximation of order \(p=1\) for the exponential: \( e^{i\mathbb{Z}} \approx (\mathbb{1} + \frac{i}{2}\mathbb{Z})(\mathbb{1} - \frac{i}{2}\mathbb{Z})^{-1} \).

## Context

This work provides a formal foundation for the EFA, which had previously been used as an intuitive but unjustified phenomenological procedure in mean-field calculations of odd nuclei (Berger 2003, Sara 2005, Baldini 2006, Bonneau 2007). The justification enables the use of gradient methods and beyond-mean-field techniques like the Generator Coordinate Method within the EFA framework. The approach extends ideas from statistical quantum mechanics and finite-temperature HFB (Blaizot 1986) to the fixed-probability case, and the authors note that Duguet (2001) had mentioned the mixed-state interpretation in the zero-pairing limit but did not develop it further.
