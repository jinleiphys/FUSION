# Nucleon Momentum Decomposition in QCD

> ~Based on the gauge invariant quark canonical momentum we construct two theoretically possible decompositions of nucleon momentum to those of quarks and gluons. We predict that either 6% or 21% of nucleon momentum is carried by gluons, depending on what type of gluons are in nucleons. We clarify the existing confusions on this problem and discuss the physical implications of our result on the proton spin crisis problem.

# Nucleon Momentum Decomposition in QCD

## Key claim
The authors argue that there are multiple gauge-invariant ways to decompose nucleon momentum into quark and gluon contributions, and that the correct decomposition depends on whether nucleons contain only "binding" gluons or all eight types of gluons. They predict that gluons carry either 6% or 21% of nucleon momentum, depending on which type of gluon is present and which momentum operator (canonical or kinematic) is measurable.

## Method
The authors construct gauge-invariant decompositions of the QCD momentum operator by separating the gluon field into a pure-gauge vacuum part and a physical part, and further applying an Abelian decomposition that separates gluons into "binding" (color-neutral) and "valence" (colored) types. They then derive four distinct gauge-invariant expressions for the total nucleon momentum, corresponding to different choices of quark momentum operator (canonical or kinematic) and different gluon content (binding only or all gluons). The gluon momentum fraction is estimated using asymptotic formulas from perturbative QCD, with the number of active gluons set to either 2 (binding only) or 8 (all gluons).

## Key numbers
- For the kinematic momentum with all gluons (n_g = 8) and n_f = 5, the standard prediction is that about 51% of nucleon momentum is carried by gluons (based on Pol (year) asymptotic formula).
- For the kinematic momentum with only binding gluons (n_g = 2) and n_f = 5, the prediction is that about 21% of nucleon momentum is carried by gluons.
- For the canonical momentum with all gluons (n_g = 8), the prediction is that about 21% of nucleon momentum is carried by gluons (based on Chen (year) formula).
- For the canonical momentum with only binding gluons (n_g = 2), the prediction is that about 6% of nucleon momentum is carried by gluons.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing debate on the nucleon momentum decomposition initiated by Pol (year), who argued that gluons carry about half the nucleon momentum, and by Chen (year), who claimed only about one-fifth. The authors introduce the Abelian decomposition of QCD (Cho 1980, 1981) to distinguish binding gluons from valence gluons, a distinction previously ignored in momentum decomposition discussions. The results directly impact the proton spin crisis problem, as the same ambiguity applies to quark orbital angular momentum decompositions (Cho 2011, Ji 1997, Chen 2009). The paper calls for experimental measurement of the gluon momentum fraction to determine which gluon types actually reside in nucleons.
