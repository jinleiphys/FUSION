# Particle-Hole Symmetry in Generalized Seniority, Microscopic Interacting Boson (Fermion) Model, Nucleon-Pair Approximation, and Others

> The particle-hole symmetry (equivalence) of the full shell-model Hilbert space is straightforward and routinely used in practical calculations. In this work we show that this symmetry is preserved in the subspace truncated at a certain generalized seniority, and give the explicit transformation between the states in the two types (particle and hole) of representations. Based on the results, we study the particle-hole symmetry in popular theories that could be regarded as further truncations on top of the generalized seniority, including the microscopic interacting boson (fermion) model, the nucleon-pair approximation, and others.

# Particle-Hole Symmetry in Generalized Seniority, Microscopic Interacting Boson (Fermion) Model, Nucleon-Pair Approximation, and Others

## Key claim

The particle-hole symmetry of the full shell-model Hilbert space is preserved in subspaces truncated at arbitrary generalized seniority $S$, provided the truncation is based on generalized seniority itself; however, further truncations on top of generalized seniority, such as the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), can break this symmetry, meaning that near half-filled shells the choice between particle and hole representations matters for NPA calculations.

## Method

The author proves the particle-hole symmetry for arbitrary generalized seniority $S$ by using the identity relating the particle-pair condensate $(P^\dagger)^N|0\rangle$ to the hole-pair condensate $(\bar{P})^{\bar{N}}|\bar{0}\rangle$ (originally shown by Talmi for $S=0$ and $S=2$) and applying it in Pauli-blocked subspaces. The proof proceeds in M-scheme for even systems (generalizing to arbitrary $s = S/2$) and odd systems, then couples the M-scheme results into J-scheme expressions using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The author then examines whether the particle-hole symmetry holds in theories that further truncate the generalized seniority subspace, including the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM), the interacting boson fermion model (IBFM), the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), and other truncation schemes such as restricting unpaired nucleon numbers per $j$-level or cutting by mean energy.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle space degeneracy is $2\Omega = \sum_j (2j+1)$, where $2N$ particles correspond to $2\bar{N} \equiv 2(\Omega - N)$ holes.
- The generalized seniority $S = 2s$ ranges $0 \le s \le \min(N, \bar{N})$ for even systems; for odd systems $S = 2s+1$ ranges $0 \le s \le \min(N, \bar{N}-1)$.
- The transformation for maximal generalized seniority even states is given by Eq. (J_tran): $(a_{j_1}^\dagger a_{j_2}^\dagger ... a_{j_{2s}}^\dagger)^{\tau,J} (P^\dagger)^{N-s} |0\rangle = \eta^s_s (\tilde{a}_{j_1} \tilde{a}_{j_2} ... \tilde{a}_{j_{2s}})^{\tau,J} (\bar{P})^{\bar{N}-s} |\bar{0}\rangle / (v_{j_1} v_{j_2} ... v_{j_{2s}}) + O(s-1)$, where $\eta^s_s$ is a normalization factor involving factorials and products of $v_\alpha$.
- For odd maximal seniority states, Eq. (J_tran_odd): $(a_{j_1}^\dagger ... a_{j_{2s+1}}^\dagger)^{\tau,J} (P^\dagger)^{N-s} |0\rangle = -\eta^s_{s+1} (\tilde{a}_{j_1} ... \tilde{a}_{j_{2s+1}})^{\tau,J} (\bar{P})^{\bar{N}-1-s} |\bar{0}\rangle / (v_{j_1} ... v_{j_{2s+1}}) + O(s-1)$.
- For the NPA counterexample with two $D^\dagger$ pairs coupled to $J=4$, the transformation yields a new hexadecapole pair $\bar{G}$ of multipolarity $\lambda=4$ in the hole representation, demonstrating broken symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proof by Talmi (1982) for generalized seniority $S=0$ and $S=2$ to arbitrary $S$, and corrects a misprint in the treatment by Allaart et al. (1988) who claimed the symmetry for arbitrary $S$ without proof. The results clarify the particle-hole symmetry in the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM) and interacting boson fermion model (IBFM), confirming earlier work by Talmi (1982) and Johnson and Vincent (1985) for the $\mathcal{S}$-$\mathcal{D}$ subspace and generalizing to larger boson spaces. The finding that the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) breaks particle-hole symmetry is new and guides practical calculations near half-filled shells, where the choice between particle and hole representations can affect results. The transformation formulas enable future work on truncation schemes that preserve or break the symmetry, such as restricting unpaired nucleon numbers per $j$-level (which preserves symmetry for upper bounds but breaks it for lower bounds) and energy-cutoff truncation (which generally breaks symmetry).
