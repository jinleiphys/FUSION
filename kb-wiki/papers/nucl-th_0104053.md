# Final state interactions in the eta-d system

> The eta-deuteron scattering amplitude is calculated with a recent K-matrix model of eta-N interactions. The existence of a narrow virtual state in the eta-deuteron system is inferred. This state couples strongly to the eta-d channel but its effect on final state interactions in the pn --> eta-d reaction is shown to be rather weak. Fairly large eta-N scattering lengths are allowed by the experimental data.

# Final state interactions in the eta-d system

## Key claim

The paper infers the existence of a narrow virtual state in the eta-deuteron system, but this state couples only weakly to the pn → eta-d reaction and produces limited effects at energies below deuteron breakup, contrary to earlier predictions of a broad and prominent peak above threshold.

## Method

The authors calculate the eta-deuteron scattering amplitude at low energies using a partial summation of the multiple scattering series, extending a previous method used for the eta-deuteron scattering length to the continuum case. They employ a recent K-matrix model of eta-N interactions with separable form factors and a Yamaguchi potential for the NN interaction, and evaluate the multiple scattering integrals in momentum space to obtain the on-shell and half-off-shell T-matrices.

## Key numbers

- The eta-N scattering length from the K-matrix model is a_etaN = 0.75(4) + i0.27(3) fm, with effective range r_etaN = -1.50(13) - i0.24(4) fm
- A second solution from the same model gives a_etaN ≈ 0.3 + i0.2 fm with a very large effective range
- The inverse range parameter for the eta-N form factor is taken as kappa_eta = 3.3 fm^{-1}
- The inverse deuteron radius <1/r> = 0.448 fm^{-1} for the Paris potential and 0.463 fm^{-1} for the Bonn potential
- The static approximation gives <<1/r>> = 0.83 fm^{-1} while the correct three-body value is <<1/r>>_{3B} = 0.32 fm^{-1} (Hulthen-Yamaguchi I wave function)
- The width of the virtual state is in the 1 MeV range

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Green and Wycech (1996) on the eta-deuteron scattering length and by Ueda, who predicted a broad peak above the eta-d threshold that the present authors do not reproduce. The results are compared with Faddeev equation solutions by Shevchenko (1998), Deloff (1999), and Garcilazo (2000). The paper uses the K-matrix model of the eta-N interaction from a four-coupled-channel analysis, and discusses constraints on eta-N scattering matrices from eta-NN data, with implications for the existence of eta-nuclear quasi-bound states predicted by Haider and Liu and by Li et al.
