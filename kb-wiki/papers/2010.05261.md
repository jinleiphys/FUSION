# Azimuthal anisotropy and multiplicities of hard photons and free nucleons in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Anisotropic flow can offer significant information of evolution dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. A systematic study of the directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ of hard photons and free nucleons is performed for $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions in a framework of isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The study firstly reveals that thermal photons emitted in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions have the behaviors of directed and elliptic flows. The interesting phenomena of incident energy dependence of $v_1$ and $v_2$ for thermal photons in central collisions also confirmed that it can be regarded as a good probe of evolution dynamics. Moreover, the multiplicities of hard photons and free nucleons and their correlation are also investigated. We find that direct photon emission is positively related to free nucleons emission, however, there exists an anti-correlation for thermal photons with free nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal photons emitted in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions exhibit directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) for the first time, based on IQMD model simulations of $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions. However, this claim is qualified by the model-dependent framework and the specific definition of thermal photons as those emitted after the first minimum of the system's average density.

## Method

The authors embedded an incoherent neutron-proton bremsstrahlung photon production channel into the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, using an in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and a soft nuclear equation of state. They simulated $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions at incident energies from 40 to 120 MeV/nucleon, separating hard photons into direct photons (emitted during the early compression phase) and thermal photons (emitted after the first minimum of the average density). Directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ were calculated from the azimuthal Fourier expansion of particle distributions relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow $v_1$ for direct photons is positive (repulsive mean field), while $v_1$ for thermal photons and free nucleons is negative (attractive mean field) for $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca at 60, 90, and 120 MeV/nucleon in centrality 40%-60%.
- Elliptic flow $v_2$ for thermal photons in central collisions (0%-20% centrality) fluctuates around a constant value of approximately 1.1% across incident energies.
- The separation time $t_s$ between direct and thermal photons for $^{40}$Ca+$40$Ca at 60 MeV/nucleon at impact parameter $b=0.0$ fm is approximately 105 fm/c (from average density) or 107.5 fm/c (from photon production probability).
- A scaling analysis of transverse flow for charged particles ($Z=1$ and $Z=2$) shows a drastic change of flow sign around 140 MeV/nucleon, indicating a transition from attractive-dominated to repulsive-dominated interaction.
- The multiplicity correlation factor $1+R_{\gamma-n}$ for direct photons is slightly larger than 1.0 (weak positive correlation with free nucleons), while for thermal photons it is less than 1.0 (anti-correlation with free nucleons).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hard photon azimuthal anisotropy in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, which had demonstrated directed and elliptic flow for direct photons using the BUU model (Ma 2012, Guo 2008, Deng 2018). The novel contribution is the demonstration that thermal photons also exhibit anisotropic flow, which was previously unknown. The study also builds on the IQMD model framework (Aichelin, Hartnack) and the Bonasera and Csenai scaling analysis of transverse flow (Bonasera 1997). The results enable using thermal photon flow as a probe of the later-stage evolution dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, complementing direct photon probes of the early compression phase.
