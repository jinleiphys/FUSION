# Highlights from Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and the Acoustics of the Little Bangs

> At the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory, heavy nuclei are collided at high energies to create matter that is hot enough and dense enough to dissolve hadrons into a quark-gluon-plasma (QGP). In this lecture, dedicated to the memory of Aditya Sambamurti, I present an introduction to heavy-ion collisions and highlights from the first decade of RHIC results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a pedagogical overview of the first decade of RHIC results, arguing that the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions behaves as a nearly perfect liquid with extremely low viscosity, and that the detailed study of higher-order flow harmonics ($v_n$) provides an analogy to cosmic microwave background power spectrum analysis, though viscous effects in the QGP suppress higher harmonics much more strongly than in cosmology.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200$ GeV at RHIC, focusing on particle ratio measurements from the STAR and PHENIX detectors, elliptic flow ($v_2$) measurements as a function of transverse momentum and particle species, and two-particle correlation analyses. The paper also presents Monte Carlo Glauber model calculations of participant eccentricity fluctuations and compares them to hydrodynamic model calculations (NEXSPHERIO) and experimental correlation data to interpret the origin of long-range ridge correlations.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200$ GeV, the maximum center-of-mass energy for gold ions at RHIC
- The QGP fireball size is approximately 10 fm (10$^{-14}$ meters) and exists for $t \approx 20$ fm/$c$ ($\approx 10^{-22}$ seconds)
- In semi-central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, $v_2=0.25$ corresponds to nearly twice as many 1.5 GeV particles emitted along the short axis compared to the long axis
- Pion and kaon $v_2$ reach a maximum of $v_2 = 0.14$ at $p_T$ near 2.5 GeV/$c$; baryon $v_2$ reaches a maximum of $v_2 = 0.20$ at $p_T$ near 4.0 GeV/$c$
- At $p_T$ near 7 GeV/$c$, $v_2$ for all particles is consistent with $v_2 = 0.10$
- The Gaussian width for $v_n\{2\}^2$ vs. $n$ from all pairs independent of charge is $\sigma_{n}=1.63\pm 0.02$
- The acoustic horizon grows to approximately 7 fm (estimated from lattice data and hydrodynamics)
- The average emission radius for all particles is 8 fm (consistent with blast-wave model fits); for particles near $p_T=2$ GeV/$c$ the average emission radius is closer to 12 fm
- Woods-Saxon diffuseness parameter for gold nucleus: $a=0.535$ fm (from electron scattering data)

## Context

This lecture-style review summarizes the first decade of RHIC results (2000-2010) and extends the long-standing theoretical program of QGP studies dating back to Hagedorn's work on the limiting temperature of hadronic matter and early Lattice QCD calculations of the QCD equation of state. The paper connects RHIC findings to the broader field by drawing analogies between heavy-ion collisions and cosmology, specifically comparing $v_n$ spectra to CMB power spectrum measurements as proposed by Mishra et al. (2007). The work builds on prior hydrodynamic modeling by Kolb and Heinz, the participant eccentricity framework generalized by Alver and Roland (2010) and Broniowski, and the IPsat Color Glass Condensate model of Kowalski and Teaney. The results enable future precision measurements of QGP transport properties, particularly the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, and motivate continued study of initial-state fluctuations and their evolution through the QGP medium.
