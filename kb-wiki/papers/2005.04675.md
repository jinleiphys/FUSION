# Smooth flux-sheets with topological winding modes

> The inclusion of the Gaussian-curvature term in the bulk of Polyakov-Kleinert string action renders new boundary terms and conditions by Gauss-Bonnet theorem. Within a leading approximation, the eigenmodes of smooth worldsheets and the free-energy of a gas of open rigid strings appears to be altered at second order in the coupling by the topological term . In analogy to the topological $θ$ term, the Gauss-Bonnet term is introduced into the effective action with a complex coupling to implement signed energy shifts. We investigate the rigid color flux-sheets between two static color sources near the critical point in the light of the topologically induced shifts. The Yang-Mills lattice data of the potential of static quark-antiquark $Q\bar{Q}$ in a heatbath is compared to the string potential. The Monte-Carlo data correspond to link-integrated Polyakov-loop correlators averaged over SU(3) gauge configurations at $β=6.0$. Substantial improvement in the fit behavior is displayed over the nonperturbative source separation distance $0.2$ fm to $1.0$ fm. Remarkably, the returned coupling parameter of the topological term from the fit exhibits a proportionality to a quantum number. These findings suggest that the manifested modes are the winding number of a topological particle on the string's worldsheet.

## Key claim
The inclusion of the Gaussian-curvature (topological) term in the Polyakov-Kleinert rigid string action yields a substantial improvement in fits to lattice QCD static quark-antiquark potential data over source separations from 0.2 fm to 1.0 fm, but only when the topological coupling parameter is allowed to take nonzero values that exhibit quantization consistent with integer winding numbers.

## Method
The authors fit the static potential derived from the Polyakov-Kleinert string action, augmented by the Gaussian-curvature term with a complex coupling, to SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice data for the quark-antiquark potential at temperature T/Tc = 0.9. The lattice data were obtained from link-integrated Polyakov-loop correlators averaged over gauge configurations at beta = 6.0 on a 3.6^3 x 8 fm^4 lattice with spacing a = 0.1 fm. The fits were performed over various source separation intervals within the range 0.2 fm to 1.0 fm, comparing models with and without the topological term, and also including next-to-leading order Nambu-Goto self-interactions.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1 fm, corresponding to coupling beta = 6.00
- Temperature: T/Tc = 0.9
- Lattice volume: 3.6^3 x 8 fm^4
- Fit interval: source separation distances from 0.2 fm to 1.0 fm
- For the model without topological term (theta^2 = 0) over interval [2,6]: chi^2 = 176.1, string tension sigma_0 = 4.185(4) fm^{-2}, rigidity alpha/a^2 = 5.22(3)
- For the model with topological term (theta^2 = 4.3(2)) over interval [2,6]: chi^2 = 9.5, sigma_0 = 4.02(2) fm^{-2}, alpha/a^2 = 27(3)
- For the model with Nambu-Goto self-interactions and topological term over interval [2,6]: chi^2 = 1.8, sigma_0 = 4.28(1) fm^{-2}, theta^2 = 22.4(5)
- The returned coupling parameter 2*theta^2 exhibits integer multiples of pi, with quantum numbers n = 1, 2, or 5 in the simpler model, and n = 8 to 23 in the model with self-interactions

## Context
This work extends the Polyakov-Kleinert (PK) rigid string model (Polyakov and Kleinert, 1980s) and the Luscher-Weisz (LW) string action framework by incorporating the Gaussian-curvature topological term, which had previously been considered negligible for long strings. The study builds on lattice investigations of the confining potential in compact U(1) (Caselle et al., 2014) and SU(N) gauge theories in 3D (Brandt et al., 2017), as well as recent work identifying rigid-like structure in the gluonic string condensate (Bakry et al., 2020). The results enable future studies of topological winding modes on QCD flux-tube worldsheets and suggest that the Gaussian-curvature coupling may serve as a probe of worldsheet self-intersection numbers in the non-perturbative regime of hadronic forces.
