# Composite Vector Particles in External Electromagnetic Fields

> Lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) studies of electromagnetic properties of hadrons and light nuclei, such as magnetic moments and polarizabilities, have proven successful with the use of background field methods. With an implementation of nonuniform background electromagnetic fields, properties such as charge radii and higher electromagnetic multipole moments (for states of higher spin) can be additionally obtained. This can be achieved by matching lattice QCD calculations to a corresponding low-energy effective theory that describes the static and quasi-static response of hadrons and nuclei to weak external fields. With particular interest in the case of vector mesons and spin-1 nuclei such as the deuteron, we present an effective field theory of spin-1 particles coupled to external electromagnetic fields. To constrain the charge radius and the electric quadrupole moment of the composite spin-1 field, the single-particle Green's functions in a linearly varying electric field in space are obtained within the effective theory, providing explicit expressions that can be used to match directly onto lattice QCD correlation functions. The viability of an extraction of the charge radius and the electric quadrupole moment of the deuteron from the upcoming lattice QCD calculations of this nucleus is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory framework for extracting the charge radius and electric quadrupole moment of composite spin-1 particles (such as vector mesons and the deuteron) from lattice QCD calculations using nonuniform background electromagnetic fields, but the viability of such extractions depends on the specific system and field configuration, and the method requires matching lattice correlation functions to the derived Green's functions in a linearly varying electric field.

## Method

The authors construct a semi-relativistic effective field theory for massive spin-1 particles coupled to external electromagnetic fields, using a first-order formalism based on the Sakata-Taketani equations with a 10-component representation of the vector field. They include the most general nonminimal couplings up to order 1/M², which encode the electric charge radius and electric quadrupole moment, and match the coefficients to on-shell form-factor decompositions. The single-particle Green's functions are then derived for a linearly varying electric field in space, both in the relativistic and nonrelativistic frameworks, providing explicit expressions that can be matched to lattice QCD correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The electric quadrupole moment is defined as \(\overline{Q}_2 \equiv G_Q(0) = F_1(0) - F_2(0) + F_3(0)\), in units of \(e/M^2\).
- The magnetic dipole moment is defined as \(\overline{\mu}_1 \equiv G_M(0) = F_2(0)\), in units of \(e/(2M)\).
- The mean-squared electric charge radius is given by \(\langle r^2 \rangle_E = -6e \, dG_C(Q^2)/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0} = -6e \, dF_1(Q^2)/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0} - e\overline{Q}_2/M^2\).
- The mean-squared magnetic charge radius is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_M = -6e \, dG_M(Q^2)/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0} = -6e \, dF_2(Q^2)/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0}\).
- The form-factor decomposition at low momentum transfer yields \(F_1(Q^2) = Q_0 - C^{(2)}_1 Q^2/M^2 + \mathcal{O}(Q^4/M^4)\), where \(C^{(2)}_1\) is a coefficient in the effective Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the background field method previously applied to magnetic moments and polarizabilities of hadrons and light nuclei (Beane 2014, Chang 2015) to the case of nonuniform electromagnetic fields, enabling access to charge radii and electric quadrupole moments. The formalism builds on earlier effective field theory treatments for spin-0 and spin-1/2 particles (Lee 2013, Lee 2014) and on the Sakata-Taketani and Young-Bludman first-order formulations for spin-1 fields. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron's quadrupole moment and charge radius, complementing studies of S/D-wave mixing in the two-nucleon channel (Briceno 2013, Orginos 2015) that probe the tensor component of nuclear forces.
