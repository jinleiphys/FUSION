# Electromagnetic form factors of the $Ω^-$ baryon in the spacelike and timelike regions

> We present complete calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of the $Ω^-$ in the spacelike region and in the timelike region. The four elastic form factors: electric charge ($G_{E0}$), magnetic dipole ($G_{M1}$), electric quadrupole ($G_{E2}$) and magnetic octupole ($G_{M3}$), are estimated within the covariant spectator quark model, in terms of the square momentum transfer $q^2$. The free parameters of the $Ω^-$ wave function, including a $S$-wave state and two independent $D$-wave states radial wave functions and the admixture coefficients are fixed by the comparison with the lattice QCD data in the spacelike region ($Q^2=-q^2 \le 0$) and with the recent $e^+ e^- \to Ω^- \bar Ω^+$ data from CLEO in the timelike region ($q^2 > 0$). The estimates in the timelike region for square momentum transfer $q^2 \ge 4 M_Ω^2$ are based on large-$q^2$ asymptotic relations ($M_Ω$ is the $Ω^-$ mass). We examine also the impact of the large-$Q^2$ correlations between different form factors and analyze the possible solutions. The electric quadrupole and the magnetic octupole moments of the $Ω^-$, and the $e^+ e^- \to Ω^- \bar Ω^+$ integrated cross sections for very large $q^2$ are estimated based on the model results.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model can simultaneously describe the $\Omega^-$ electromagnetic form factors in both spacelike and timelike regions, but only when the free parameters of the wave function (including S-wave and two independent D-wave components) are constrained by lattice QCD data, the experimental magnetic moment, CLEO timelike data, and asymptotic perturbative QCD relations at large momentum transfer.

## Method

The author employed the covariant spectator quark model, representing the $\Omega^-$ as a quark-diquark system with a wave function composed of a dominant S-wave state and two D-wave states (D3 with core-spin 3/2 and D1 with core-spin 1/2). The four elastic electromagnetic form factors (electric charge $G_{E0}$, magnetic dipole $G_{M1}$, electric quadrupole $G_{E2}$, and magnetic octupole $G_{M3}$) were calculated in the spacelike region ($Q^2 \ge 0$) and extended to the timelike region ($q^2 > 0$) using large-$q^2$ asymptotic relations. The model parameters were fixed by fitting to lattice QCD data from Alexandrou et al. (2010) in the spacelike region and to CLEO $e^+ e^- \to \Omega^- \bar \Omega^+$ data in the timelike region, with additional constraints from perturbative QCD asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment: $\mu_{\Omega} = (-2.02 \pm 0.05) \mu_N$, corresponding to $G_{M1}(0) = -3.60 \pm 0.09$ (from PDG 2020)
- Lattice QCD data from Alexandrou et al. (2010) provide form factors $G_{E0}$, $G_{M1}$, and $G_{E2}$ for $Q^2$ up to 4 GeV$^2$, with reliable precision only for $Q^2 \le 2$ GeV$^2$
- The only lattice QCD estimate for $G_{M3}$ from Boinepalli et al. (2009) at $Q^2 = 0.23$ GeV$^2$ gives $G_{M3} = 1.25 \pm 7.50$
- Perturbative QCD predicts $G_{E0}, G_{M1} \propto 1/Q^4$ and $G_{E2}, G_{M3} \propto 1/Q^6$ for very large $Q^2$, with the additional asymptotic relation $G_{M1} = \frac{4}{5} \tau G_{M3}$ where $\tau = Q^2/(4M_\Omega^2)$

## Context

This work extends previous covariant spectator quark model calculations for the $\Omega^-$ (Ramalho, 2018) by incorporating both spacelike lattice QCD data from Alexandrou et al. (2010) and timelike data from CLEO (Dobbs et al., 2014, 2017), along with perturbative QCD constraints. The study enables predictions for the electric quadrupole and magnetic octupole moments of the $\Omega^-$, as well as the $e^+ e^- \to \Omega^- \bar \Omega^+$ cross sections at large $q^2$, which can be tested at facilities like BABAR, BES III, and PANDA. The work also highlights the need for more accurate lattice QCD calculations of $G_{M3}$ and additional timelike measurements to reduce uncertainties in the form factors.
