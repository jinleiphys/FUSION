# Role of deformation in the nonmesonic decay of light hypernuclei

> We discuss the nonmesonic decay of deformed p-shell hypernuclei. The Nilsson model with angular momentum projection is employed in order to take into account the deformation effects. The nonmesonic decay rate and the intrinsic $Λ$ asymmetry parameter decrease as a function of the deformation parameter, while the ratio of the neutron- to proton-induced decay rates increases. We find that the deformation effects change these observables by about 10 % for $^9_Λ$Be from the spherical limit.

# Role of deformation in the nonmesonic decay of light hypernuclei

## Key claim
Deformation effects change the nonmesonic decay observables (total decay rate, neutron-to-proton ratio, and intrinsic Λ asymmetry parameter) by about 10% for ⁹ΛBe from the spherical limit, but this is smaller than current experimental uncertainties and other theoretical uncertainties, so the spherical approximation remains adequate for p-shell hypernuclei at present precision.

## Method
The authors employ the Nilsson model with angular momentum projection to describe the deformed core nucleus ⁸Be, using a weak coupling scheme where the Λ hyperon couples to the ground state of the deformed core. The nonmesonic decay transition amplitudes are computed within a one-meson-exchange (OME) model including π, ρ, η, ω, K, and K* mesons, with monopole form factors and the Nijmegen potential for the final-state NN interaction. Coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP) for the deformed core are derived analytically for the four-valence-nucleon system of ⁸Be.

## Key numbers
- The total nonmesonic decay rate Γ_nm/Γ_Λ (in units of the free Λ decay rate Γ_Λ = 3.8 × 10⁹ s⁻¹) decreases as a function of the deformation parameter β₂, with about 10% change from the spherical limit at β₂ = 1.0 for ⁹ΛBe.
- The neutron-to-proton ratio Γ_n/Γ_p increases with deformation, changing by about 10% from the spherical limit at β₂ = 1.0 for ⁹ΛBe.
- The intrinsic Λ asymmetry parameter a_Λ increases with deformation, changing by about 10% from the spherical limit at β₂ = 1.0 for ⁹ΛBe.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter extracted from the experimental quadrupole moment of ⁹Be is β₂ = 1.00 (using radius parameter r₀ = 1.2 fm).
- The experimental binding energy of the Λ in ⁹ΛBe is 6.71 ± 0.04 MeV.
- Using Cohen-Kurath shell-model CFP for ¹²ΛC yields Γ_nm/Γ_Λ = 0.76 and Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.22, compared to spherical limit values of 0.74 and 0.25 respectively.
- Experimental uncertainties for p-shell hypernuclei: 7%–17.5% for total decay rate, 46.2%–84.2% for neutron-to-proton ratio, and 50%–1000% for asymmetry.

## Context
This work extends previous spherical shell-model calculations of nonmesonic decay for p-shell hypernuclei (Parreno et al. 1997, Goudsmit 1997) by incorporating deformation effects via the Nilsson model. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical and experimental values of the neutron-to-proton ratio, which had resisted resolution through other mechanisms such as ΔI=1/2 violations (Parreno et al. 1998) or three-nucleon emission channels (Alberico and Parreno 1991, Ramos et al. 1997). The results show that deformation alone cannot resolve this discrepancy, motivating future work on heavy deformed hypernuclei such as ²³⁸ΛU using the projected shell model (Hara and Sun 1995, Sun and Hara 1997).
