# How to extract physics from HBT radius parameters

> I review recent progress in the understanding of the connection between the space-time structure of the particle emitting source and the form of the two-particle correlation function in momentum space. Based on a new scheme for calculating the HBT radius parameters from the emission function, strategies are suggested to separate for rapidly expanding sources the information on the spatial and temporal structure of the source. To this end a new fitting function for the two-particle correlation function is proposed. Its usefulness is demonstrated for a typical expanding model source, and it is shown how the dependence of the resulting fit parameters on the momentum of the particle pair can be used to measure the longitudinal and transverse expansion of the source.

# How to extract physics from HBT radius parameters

## Key claim

The width of the two-particle correlation function does not directly measure the geometric size of the source, as was naively expected from stellar interferometry; instead, for rapidly expanding sources with inhomogeneous temperature profiles and collective dynamical expansion, the HBT radius parameters measure only "space-time regions of homogeneity" whose size varies with the momentum of the emitted particles, and a new Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrisation can separate longitudinal and transverse dynamics from temporal information.

## Method

The author derives model-independent expressions for HBT radius parameters in terms of space-time variances of the emission function, using a Gaussian expansion of the emission function around its mean space-time point for a given pair momentum. The standard Cartesian parametrisation and the new Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrisation of the two-particle correlation function are compared analytically, and their relations are derived. The momentum dependence of the HBT parameters is then computed numerically for a simple model emission function describing a finite expanding thermalized source with boost-invariant longitudinal flow and linear transverse flow, using fixed source parameters (R=3 fm, τ0=3 fm/c, Δτ=1 fm/c, Δη=1.2, T=140 MeV) for pion and kaon pairs.

## Key numbers

- Source model parameters: R=3 fm (transverse Gaussian radius), τ0=3 fm/c (average freeze-out proper time), Δτ=1 fm/c (mean proper time duration of emission), Δη=1.2 (longitudinal extension), T=140 MeV (freeze-out temperature)
- For longitudinal expansion without transverse flow, R_l shows an inverse power law dependence on M⊥ with power approximately 0.55, in rough agreement with the approximate √(T/M⊥)-scaling law
- The YKP parameter R_0 at small M⊥ is approximately 2.5 fm/c (effective source lifetime) in the YK frame for the chosen parameters
- For transverse flow strength ηf=0.6, the breaking of M⊥-scaling between pions and kaons is visible in the YKP radii, with opposite sign effects for R∥ compared to R⊥ and R0

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of HBT interferometry developed by the Regensburg group in a series of publications (Csörgő et al. 1995, Chapman et al. 1995, Wiedemann et al. 1996, Heiselberg et al. 1996, Wiedemann et al. 1996), particularly the discovery of a new cross-term in the correlation function mixing outward and longitudinal components and the development of the YKP parametrisation. The paper corrects earlier naive expectations that HBT radii directly measure geometric source sizes, and provides a framework for extracting collective flow parameters from experimental data. The analytical approximations from Csörgő and Lörstad (1995) are shown to be insufficiently accurate for quantitative analysis, especially for the outward radius and the cross-term. The work enables experimental determination of longitudinal source velocity, separation of spatial and temporal source structure, and measurement of transverse and longitudinal expansion from the momentum dependence of HBT parameters, though resonance decay contributions (studied by the Marburg group) require further systematic analysis.
