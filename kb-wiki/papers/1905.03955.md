# Dense Baryonic Matter and Strangeness in Neutron Stars

> Recent developments of chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) applications to nuclear and neutron matter are summarized, with special emphasis on a (non-perturbative) extension using functional renormalisation group methods. Topics include: nuclear thermodynamics, extrapolations to dense baryonic matter and constraints from neutron star observables. Hyperon-nuclear interactions derived from SU(3) will be discussed with reference to the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star matter.

# Dense Baryonic Matter and Strangeness in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory extended with functional renormalization group methods suggests that neutron star matter remains in the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry phase up to densities as high as 5-6 times nuclear saturation density, with no first-order chiral phase transition occurring at densities relevant for neutron star cores, provided that fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation are properly included.

## Method

The author applies functional renormalization group (FRG) flow equations to a chiral nucleon-meson field theory, starting from the chiral symmetry breaking scale (approximately 1 GeV) and evolving down to infrared scales characteristic of nuclear many-body problems at Fermi momenta much smaller than the chiral scale. The FRG framework incorporates resummations of multi-pion fluctuations, nucleon-hole excitations around the Fermi surface, and many-body correlations to all orders, enabling extrapolation to baryon densities beyond the perturbative regime. For the hyperon puzzle, chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective field theory at next-to-leading order is used to construct hyperon-nucleon interactions, with Brueckner calculations of the Lambda single-particle potential in neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Average distance between nucleons at n0: d_NN approximately 1.8 fm; at 5 n0: d_NN approximately 1.1 fm
- Fermi momentum at n0: pF = 1.33 fm^-1, approximately 1.9 m_pi
- Ratio pF/Lambda_chi < 0.3 at n0 and < 0.4 at 3 n0, where Lambda_chi = 4 pi f_pi approximately 1 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi approximately 0.09 GeV
- Chiral order parameter at 5-6 n0 remains at about 40% of its vacuum value in FRG calculations
- Symmetry energy at n = n0: [E(Z=0) - E(Z=N)]/A = 32 MeV
- Predicted neutron star radius: R approximately 11.5 km
- Squared sound velocity exceeds c_s^2 = 1/3 at baryon density around n approximately 4 n0
- Baryon density in center of two-solar-mass neutron star does not exceed n approximately 5 n0
- Nucleons begin losing identity at n greater than approximately 8 n0

## Context

This work extends perturbative chiral effective field theory calculations of nuclear and neutron matter (H. K. and L. H., 2017, 2018) to higher densities using non-perturbative FRG methods, building on earlier FRG developments (D. W. and collaborators, 2013, 2015, 2017). The results contradict mean-field predictions of a first-order chiral phase transition at approximately 3 n0 from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and chiral quark-meson models (Fukushima, 2008; Schaefer, 2007; Herbst, 2013). The equation-of-state satisfies constraints from two-solar-mass neutron star observations (Demorest, 2010; Fonseca, 2016; Antoniadis, 2013) and gravitational wave tidal deformability limits from LIGO and Virgo (Abbott, 2017; Most, 2018). The hyperon puzzle discussion extends SU(3) chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon interactions (Haidenbauer, 2013) with Lambda NN three-body forces (Petschauer, 2016, 2017) to suggest that repulsive three-body effects may prevent hyperon appearance at neutron star core densities.
