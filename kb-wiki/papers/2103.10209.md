# Quarkyonic stars with isospin-flavor asymmetry

> We suggest an extension to isospin asymmetric matter of the quarkyonic model from McLerran and Reddy. This extension allows us to construct the $β$-equilibrium between quarks, nucleons and leptons. The concept of the quarkyonic matter originates from the large number of color limit for which nucleons are the correct degrees of freedom near the Fermi surface -- reflecting the confining forces -- while deep inside the Fermi sea quarks naturally appear. In isospin asymmetric matter, we suggest that this new concept can be implemented within a global isoscalar relation between the shell gaps differentiating the nucleon and the quark sectors. In addition, we impose the conservation of the isospin-flavor asymmetry in the nucleon and the quark phases. Within this model, several quarkyonic stars are constructed on top of the SLy4 model for the nucleon sector, producing a bump in the sound speed, which implies that quarkyonic stars are systematically bigger and have a larger maximum mass than the associated neutron stars. They also predict lower proton fraction at $β$-equilibrium, which potentially quenches fast cooling in massive compact stars.

## Key claim

The authors extend the quarkyonic model to isospin-asymmetric matter by imposing a global isoscalar relation between nucleon and quark Fermi seas and conserving isospin-flavor asymmetry between the two phases, rather than treating nucleons and quarks as independent particles in chemical equilibrium as done by Zhao and Lattimer (2020).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- SLy4 model parameters: E_sat = -15.97 MeV, E_sym = 32.01 MeV, K_sat = 230 MeV, n_sat = 0.1595 fm^{-3}
- Quarkyonic scale: Λ_Qyc ≈ 250-300 MeV; κ_Qyc ≈ 0.3
- Sound speed peak occurs at n_B ≈ 0.4 fm^{-3}, nearly independent of isospin asymmetry
- For Λ_Qyc = 250 MeV and κ_Qyc = 0.3, the sound speed at high density approaches the conformal limit of 1/3
- Increasing Λ_Qyc from 250 MeV to 270 MeV raises the proton fraction above the dURCA threshold at higher density

## Context

This work extends the original quarkyonic model of McLerran and Reddy (2019) from symmetric and neutron matter to isospin-asymmetric matter, and differs from the approach of Zhao and Lattimer (2020) by not treating nucleons and quarks as independent particles in chemical equilibrium. The model predicts a bump in sound speed at 2-3 times saturation density, similar to findings by Jeong, McLerran and Sen (2020), and suggests that quarkyonic stars are systematically larger and have higher maximum masses than pure neutron stars, while reducing the proton fraction and potentially quenching fast cooling via the direct URCA process.
