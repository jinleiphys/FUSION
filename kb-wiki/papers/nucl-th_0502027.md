# Multi-level and two-level models of the decay out of superdeformed bands

> We compare a multi-level statistical model with a two-level model for the decay out of superdeformed rotational bands in atomic nuclei. We conclude that while the models depend on different dimensionless combinations of the input parameters and differ in certain limits, they essentially agree in the cases where experimental data is currently available. The implications of this conclusion are discussed.

## Key claim

The multi-level statistical model and the two-level model for decay out of superdeformed bands depend on different dimensionless combinations of input parameters and differ in certain limits, but they essentially agree in the parameter regimes where experimental data are currently available, particularly when the normal-deformed level spacing is much larger than the normal-deformed electromagnetic width.

## Method

The authors compared two phenomenological models for the decay out of superdeformed rotational bands: a multi-level statistical model where the superdeformed state couples to a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) of normal-deformed states, and a two-level mixing model where the superdeformed state couples to a single normal-deformed state. For the statistical model, they used both the ensemble-averaged result derived by Gu and Weidenmuller (1999) and an energy-averaging result from Sargeant et al. (2002). For the two-level model, they used the exact analytic expression for the decay-out intensity and considered both a fixed energy splitting and an average over the Wigner nearest-neighbor spacing distribution. They performed numerical comparisons of the decay-out intensity as a function of the coupling strength for various values of the dimensionless parameters.

## Key numbers

- The statistical model depends on the dimensionless variables \(\Gamma/\Gamma_S = 2\pi(V/D)^2(D/\Gamma_S)\) and \(\Gamma_N/D\), where \(V\) is the coupling, \(D\) is the normal-deformed level density, \(\Gamma_S\) is the superdeformed electromagnetic width, and \(\Gamma_N\) is the normal-deformed electromagnetic width.
- The two-level model depends on \(V/D\), \(\bar\Gamma/D = (\Gamma_N/D)(1+\Gamma_S/\Gamma_N)/2\), and \(\Gamma_S/\Gamma_N\).
- For the mass 190 region, the representative parameters are \(\bar\Gamma/D = 10^{-4}\) and \(\Gamma_N/\Gamma_S = 1000\).
- When \(\Gamma_N/D \ll 1\) (the physically realized regime), the variance of the intra-band decay intensity in the statistical model is large, but is suppressed when \((\Gamma+\Gamma_S)/\Gamma_N \gg 1\).
- The average decay-out intensity for a uniform distribution of the energy splitting \(\Delta\) reduces to \(\Gamma/\Gamma_S\) in the limit \(V \ll \Gamma_N, \Gamma_S\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the statistical model of Gu and Weidenmuller (1999) and Sargeant et al. (2002) and the two-level model of Stafford (1999), Cardamone (2002), and Cardamone (2003). The authors show that the agreement between models in the mass 190 region explains why similar values of the coupling \(V\) were extracted from experimental data using both approaches, as reported by Krucken (2001). The paper enables a more informed choice of model for analyzing superdeformed band decay data and highlights the need for an analytical calculation of the variance of the decay-out intensity valid in the physically realized regime where \(\Gamma_N/D \ll 1\).
