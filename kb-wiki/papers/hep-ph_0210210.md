# Leading Chiral Logarithms to the Hyperfine Splitting of the Hydrogen and Muonic Hydrogen

> We study the hydrogen and muonic hydrogen within an effective field theory framework. We perform the matching between heavy baryon effective theory coupled to photons and leptons and the relevant effective field theory at atomic scales. This matching can be performed in a perturbative expansion in alpha, 1/m_p and the chiral counting. We then compute the O(m_{l_i}^3 alpha^5/m_p^2 x logarithms) contribution (including the leading chiral logarithms) to the Hyperfine splitting and compare with experiment. They can explain about 2/3 of the difference between experiment and the pure QED prediction when setting the renormalization scale at the rho mass. We give an estimate of the matching coefficient of the spin-dependent proton-lepton operator in heavy baryon effective theory.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading chiral-logarithm contributions to the hyperfine splitting of hydrogen and muonic hydrogen at order \(O(m_{l_i}^3 \alpha^5 / m_p^2 \times \text{logarithms})\), finding that these logarithms can explain about 2/3 of the discrepancy between the pure QED prediction and experiment when the renormalization scale is set to the rho mass, but the result is scale-dependent, and the remaining discrepancy is attributed to a finite counterterm of expected size.

## Method

The matching between heavy baryon effective theory (HBET) coupled to photons and leptons and the relevant effective field theory at atomic scales (pNRQED) was performed in a perturbative expansion in \(\alpha\), \(1/m_p\), and the chiral counting. The spin-dependent proton-lepton operator matching coefficient \(c_{4,NR}^{pl_i}\) was computed by evaluating chiral and electromagnetic loops, splitting the hadronic contributions into point-like, Zemach, and polarizability corrections, and extracting only the logarithmic pieces. The resulting coefficient was then used to compute the hyperfine splitting energy via the pNRQED potential.

## Key numbers

- The total leading-logarithm hadronic contribution to the hyperfine splitting at scale \(\nu = m_\rho\) is \(E_{\rm HF,logarithms}(m_\rho) = -0.031\;{\rm MHz}\).
- The Zemach contribution from u and d quarks gives \(E_{\rm HF,Zemach-u,d}(m_\rho) = -0.022\;{\rm MHz}\).
- The Zemach contribution from the \(\Delta\) baryon gives \(E_{\rm HF,Zemach-\Delta}(m_\rho) = -0.004\;{\rm MHz}\).
- The point-like contribution gives \(E_{\rm HF,point-like}(m_\rho) = -0.003\;{\rm MHz}\).
- The difference between the pure QED prediction and experiment is \(E_{\rm HF}(QED)-E_{\rm HF}(exp) = -0.046\;{\rm MHz}\).
- The extracted counterterm at scale \(m_\rho\) is \(c_{4,R}(m_\rho) \simeq -16\alpha^2\), while the full experimental value is \(c_{4,NR} = -48\alpha^2\).
- Including the strange quark Zemach contribution gives \(E_{\rm HF,Zemach-kaon}(m_\rho) = 0.003\;{\rm MHz}\), bringing the total to \(-0.027\;{\rm MHz}\) and \(c_{4,R}(m_\rho)\) to \(-20\alpha^2\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to hydrogen and muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting by incorporating chiral logarithms from HBET, building on prior calculations of hadronic corrections using dispersion relations (Zemach, 1956; Iddings, 1965; De Rafael, 1966) and form factor fits (Bodwin and Yennie, 1988). The results enable a model-independent separation of long-distance chiral logarithms from short-distance counterterms, and the universality of the matching coefficient \(c_{4,R}^{p}\) between electron and muon systems suggests that a future measurement of muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting could provide additional constraints on hadronic structure.
