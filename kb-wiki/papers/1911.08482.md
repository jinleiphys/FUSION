# Exact T=0 Eigenstates of the Isovector Pairing Hamiltonian

> We derive the exact $T=0$ seniority-zero eigenstates of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian for an even number of protons and neutrons. Nucleons are supposed to be distributed over a set of non-degenerate levels and to interact through a pairing force with constant strength. We show that these eigenstates (and among them, in particular, the ground state) are linear superpositions of products of $T=1$ collective pairs arranged into $T=0$ quartets. This grouping of protons and neutrons first into $T=1$ collective pairs and then into $T=0$ quartets represents the distinctive feature of these eigenstates. This work highlights, for the first time on the grounds of the analytic expression of its eigenstates, the key role played by the isovector pairing force in the phenomenon of nuclear quarteting.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact $T=0$ seniority-zero eigenstates of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian for even numbers of protons and neutrons, showing that these eigenstates are linear superpositions of products of $T=1$ collective pairs arranged into $T=0$ quartets; this result holds for a system with non-degenerate single-particle levels and a constant pairing strength, but the formalism requires solving an overdetermined system of nonlinear equations whose solution is found by minimizing a sum-of-squares function rather than by direct analytic inversion.

## Method

The authors construct eigenstates as linear superpositions of products of collective isovector pairs (Richardson-type pairs) coupled into $T=0$ quartets, where each collective pair is defined as $B^{\dag}_{\nu M_T}=\sum_k (1/(2\epsilon_k-E_{\nu})) P^{\dag}_{kM_T}$. For a system of $2N$ nucleons, the ansatz wavefunction is $|\Psi^{(N)}\rangle = \sum_{s} d_s |s\rangle$, where each $|s\rangle$ is a product of $N/2$ quartets $[B^\dagger_{\nu_1} B^\dagger_{\nu_2}]^0$. The pair energies $E_\nu$ and amplitudes $d_s$ are determined by requiring that the action of the Hamiltonian produces no unwanted terms, leading to a system of $N_s \cdot N$ coupled nonlinear equations in $N_s + N - 1$ unknowns, which is solved by minimizing the sum of squares of these equations.

## Key numbers

* For a 4-proton-4-neutron system (two-quartet ground state), two critical pairing strengths are observed: $g^{(1)}_{cr} \approx 0.88$ and $g^{(2)}_{cr} \approx 1.78$ (arbitrary units), where pair energies transition from real to complex-conjugate values.
* For a 6-proton-6-neutron system (three-quartet ground state), the expansion contains $N_s = 15$ components; four of the six pair energies become complex-conjugate at critical points while two remain real.
* For an 8-proton-8-neutron system (four-quartet ground state), the expansion contains $N_s = 105$ components; the eight pair energies split into two groups of four, each group behaving similarly to the two-quartet case.
* For the first excited state of a 4-proton-4-neutron system, a single critical point $g_{cr} \approx 1.15$ is observed.
* Single-particle energies are set as $\epsilon_i = -16 + 2(i-1)$, giving a spacing $\Delta\epsilon = 2$ (arbitrary units), with half-filled systems of $4\mathcal{N}$ nucleons over $2\mathcal{N}$ levels.

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution for like-particle pairing (Richardson, 1960s) to the isovector pairing Hamiltonian for $T=0$ seniority-zero states, a problem previously treated by Richardson and Chen (1970s), Pan and Draayer (1990s), Links et al. (1990s), and Dukelsky et al. (2006). Unlike those earlier exact treatments, which expressed eigenstates in terms of isospin and special raising operators with additional spectral parameters lacking clear physical interpretation, the present formulation reveals that the eigenstates are built from $T=1$ collective pairs grouped into $T=0$ quartets. This provides analytic evidence for the role of isovector pairing in nuclear quarteting, previously studied in approximate treatments (Sambataro, 2000s) and in the degenerate-level SO(5) model (Dobes, 1990s). The result enables a transparent physical interpretation of correlations induced by isovector pairing in $N=Z$ nuclei.
