# Symmetry Reduction and Semiclassical Analysis of Axially Symmetric Systems

> We derive a semiclassical trace formula for a symmetry reduced part of the spectrum in axially symmetric systems. The classical orbits that contribute are closed in (ρ,z,p_ρ,p_z) and have p_ϕ= m\hbar where m is the azimuthal quantum number. For m > 0, these orbits vary with energy and almost never lie on periodic trajectories in the full phase space in contrast to the case of discrete symmetries. The transition from m=0 to m > 0 is however not dramatic as our numerical results indicate, suggesting that contributing orbits occur in topologically equivalent families within which p_ϕvaries smoothly.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semiclassical trace formula for the symmetry-reduced part of the spectrum in axially symmetric systems, showing that the contributing classical orbits are closed in the reduced coordinates \((\rho,z,p_\rho,p_z)\) and have quantized angular momentum \(p_\varphi = m\hbar\), but for \(m \neq 0\) these orbits vary with energy and almost never lie on periodic trajectories in the full phase space, in contrast to the case of discrete symmetries.

## Method

The authors start from the full Green's function for an axially symmetric system and use the eigenfunction form \(\psi(\varphi,\eta) = (2\pi)^{-1/2}e^{im\varphi}\phi(\eta)\) to obtain a symmetry-reduced Green's function for a given azimuthal quantum number \(\mu\). They insert the semiclassical approximation to the full Green's function, perform a stationary phase integration over the azimuthal angle difference \(\varphi\), which restricts trajectories to those with \(p_\varphi = \mu\hbar\), and then evaluate the trace by further stationary phase integration over the reduced coordinates \(\eta\), yielding a sum over quasi-periodic trajectories (reduced periodic orbits) with a stability determinant factor.

## Key numbers

* For a circular billiard of radius \(R\), the stationary phase condition gives \(p_\varphi = \sqrt{E}R\cos(\varphi/2) = \mu\hbar\), leading to \(\varphi^* = 2\cos^{-1}(\mu\hbar/R\sqrt{E})\) and reduced action \(\overline{S}_j = 2R\sqrt{E}\sqrt{1-\mu^2\hbar^2/(R^2E)} - 2\mu\hbar\cos^{-1}(\mu\hbar/R\sqrt{E})\).
* The average density of states for a cavity is \(d_{av}^\mu(E) = A/(4\pi\hbar^2) - |\mu| Z_0/(4\pi\sqrt{E}\hbar)\) for small \(|\mu|/\sqrt{E}\), where \(A\) is the area of the \(\rho Z\) plane and \(Z_0\) is the extent of the cavity along the \(Z\)-axis.
* The average integrated density of states is \(N_{av}^\mu(E) = AE/(4\pi) - |\mu| Z_0\sqrt{E}/(2\pi)\) with \(\hbar = 1\).
* For the \(P_2\)-deformed cavity studied (\(R_0 = 1\), \(\lambda = 1.008087\), \(\alpha_2 = 0.2\)), the mean separation between curves of \(k_N^{(\mu)} - k_N^{(0)}\) for successive \(\mu\) is approximately \(1.38|\mu|\).
* For \(\mu = 26\), the power spectrum computed over two different energy windows shows peaks shifting to the right at higher energy, indicating that contributing orbit lengths increase with energy.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical trace formula approach of Gutzwiller (1990) to systems with continuous symmetry, specifically axial symmetry, and contrasts with the case of discrete symmetries treated by Robbins (1989) and Creagh and Edmonds (1995) where contributing orbits are eventually periodic in the full phase space. The derivation provides an alternative viewpoint to the group-theoretical method of Creagh (1996) and enables the semiclassical analysis of spectra for individual azimuthal quantum number subspaces in nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics, with the numerical results for a deformed cavity confirming that contributing orbits occur in topologically equivalent families within which \(p_\varphi\) varies smoothly.
