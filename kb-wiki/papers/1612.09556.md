# Generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to the equation of state for quark-hadron matter

> A unified equation of state for quark-hadron matter is presented in the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck form. It follows from a $Φ-$derivable approach to the thermodynamic potential where the ansatz for the $Φ$ functional contains all 2PI diagrams at two-loop order formed with quark cluster Green's functions for quark, diquark, meson and baryon propagators. We present numerical results using an effective model for the generic behaviour of hadron masses and phase shifts at finite temperature which shares basic features with recent developments within the PNJL model for correlations in quark matter. We obtain the transition between a hadron resonance gas phase and the quark gluon plasma where the Mott dissociation of hadrons is encoded in the hadronic phase shifts. The resulting thermodynamics is in very good agreement with recent lattice QCD simulations.

# Generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to the equation of state for quark-hadron matter

## Key claim

The authors present a unified equation of state for quark-hadron matter that reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics across the crossover region, but the model is schematic and relies on a postulated generic behavior of hadron phase shifts rather than a first-principles derivation; the agreement with lattice data depends on including both collisional broadening from quark-hadron scattering and perturbative virial corrections simultaneously.

## Method

The thermodynamic potential is constructed from a Phi-derivable approach using all two-loop 2PI diagrams formed with quark, diquark, meson, and baryon propagators. The total pressure is decomposed into a Mott hadron resonance gas (MHRG) term with temperature-dependent hadron masses and phase shifts, a Polyakov-loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model term for nonperturbative quark-gluon contributions, and a perturbative QCD term at two-loop order. The temperature dependence of quark masses is taken from lattice QCD data for the chiral condensate, and hadron phase shifts are modeled with a generic function that satisfies the Levinson theorem and encodes Mott dissociation when the hadron mass equals the continuum threshold.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from hadron dominance to quark-gluon dominance: T_trans = 156 MeV, where both partial pressures are equal.
- Scaled pion and kaon pressure components both peak at T = 153 MeV, related to Mott dissociation of these states.
- Light quark mass at zero temperature: m_0 = 5.5 MeV; strange quark mass: m_s = 100 MeV.
- Parameters for hadron width temperature dependence: a = 2.5 GeV, b = 8.0 GeV.
- Universal cross section for collisional broadening: sigma = 35 mb.
- Pion gas limit applies at T < 100 MeV; quark-gluon plasma limit applies above T ~ 250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Ropke 2012, Typel 2009) and quark matter (Blaschke 2015) to a unified description of the QCD equation of state. It improves upon earlier PNJL model treatments by incorporating hadronic phase shifts that satisfy the Levinson theorem, and it resolves discrepancies found in a previous version of the model (Blaschke 2015). The approach enables modeling of the full QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potentials currently inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem, and provides a basis for interpreting lattice QCD results (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014) in terms of hadronic Mott dissociation.
