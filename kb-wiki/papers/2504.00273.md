# A weak entanglement approximation for nuclear structure: a progress report

> We report on a recently proposed approach, inspired by quantum informationtheory, for calculating low-energy nuclear structure in the framework of the configuration-interaction shell-model. Empirical evidence has demonstrated that the many-proton and many-neutron partitions of nuclear configuration-interaction wave functions are weakly entangled, especially away from $N=Z$. This has been developed into a practical methodology, the Proton And Neutron Approximate Shell-model (PANASh). We review the basic ideas and present recent results. We also discuss some technical developments in calculations.

# A weak entanglement approximation for nuclear structure: a progress report

## Key claim

The proton-neutron bipartite partition of configuration-interaction shell-model wave functions exhibits low entanglement entropy (weak entanglement), especially for neutron-rich systems with N not equal to Z, and this property can be exploited as a practical truncation scheme (PANASh) that approximates full configuration-interaction results using far smaller basis dimensions, though the approximation works best for even-even nuclei and requires careful selection of basis components.

## Method

The authors decompose the nuclear Hamiltonian into proton, neutron, and proton-neutron parts, then solve for eigenstates of the proton and neutron Hamiltonians separately using the BIGSTICK M-scheme code. They construct a J-coupled basis from these proton and neutron eigenstates, truncate by selecting only the lowest-energy proton and neutron components, and compute the full Hamiltonian matrix elements including the proton-neutron coupling via one-body transition density matrices. Technical improvements include a post-processing code (RHODIUM) that computes density matrices between states in different bases and a more efficient Lanczos convergence check using LAPACK routines.

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Zn in the pf valence space with M=0, the full M-scheme basis dimension is 2.2 billion, while the basis dimension for 10 protons alone is 17,276 and for 10 neutrons alone is 17,276
- The PANASh calculation using only nine proton components and nine neutron components outperforms angular-momentum projected Hartree-Fock (PHF) for the $^{60}$Zn ground state energy
- At 1% of the proton and neutron components, PANASh reproduces the $^{60}$Zn excitation spectrum very well compared to full configuration-interaction
- For $^{132}$Ba with a $^{100}$Sn core, the full configuration-interaction dimension is 20 billion; PANASh with 1.6% of proton and neutron components gives a very good approximation to experimental excitation energies
- For $^{129}$Cs, the full configuration-interaction dimension is 50 billion; PANASh used 1000 proton components (6.8%) and 1000 neutron components (0.15%)

## Context

This work extends the weak entanglement approach introduced by Johnson (2023) and Phys. Rev. C 110 (2024) 034305, which builds on quantum information theory concepts applied to nuclear structure. It contrasts with earlier iterative basis optimization methods by Papenbrock (2003-2005) by using a fixed "good enough" basis rather than iterative refinement. The PANASh method enables calculations for systems with dimensions of 20-50 billion basis states that are at or beyond current computational limits, such as $^{132}$Ba and $^{129}$Cs in the 50-82 valence space, and opens the possibility of extending configuration-interaction shell-model calculations to heavier neutron-rich nuclei that are currently inaccessible.
