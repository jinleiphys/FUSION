# Neural-network excited states of $A=4$ nuclei and hypernuclei

> We present the first variational Monte Carlo study of nuclear and hypernuclear excited states within the neural-network quantum states (NQS) framework. We implement both the overlap penalty (OP) and natural excited state (NES) methods to compute low-lying excitation spectra. To address the spin contamination in hypernuclear calculations, we propose a quantum number targeting (QNT) technique for the OP method. Both the OP-QNT and NES methods can reproduce diagonal observables, such as energies and spatial structures, in excellent agreement with rigorous benchmarks. We further provide, to our knowledge, the first \textit{ab initio} calculation of the $M1$ transition strength for $^{4}_Λ\mathrm{H}$. The calculated transition strength is consistent with the weak-coupling limit, exhibiting a $\sim$1.3\% suppression. This work demonstrates that NQS can be elevated from ground-state solvers to practical tools for nuclear and hypernuclear spectroscopy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neural-network quantum states (NQS) can be extended from ground-state solvers to practical tools for nuclear and hypernuclear spectroscopy, achieving excitation energies and transition strengths in agreement with rigorous benchmarks, though with a small variational trade-off of approximately 0.1 MeV underbinding when a shared network architecture is used for multiple states.

## Method

The authors implement two variational Monte Carlo methods within a message-passing neural-network architecture: the overlap penalty (OP) method, which adds a penalty term proportional to the squared overlap between states, and the natural excited state (NES) method, which represents K states as a Slater determinant of single-state ansatzes to enforce orthogonality via Pauli exclusion. For hypernuclear calculations, they introduce a quantum number targeting (QNT) technique that adds an explicit penalty for deviations from target spin values to the loss function. The interactions are derived from leading-order pionless effective field theory (LO-slashed-pi EFT) with two-body forces only.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 4He: -39.77(0) MeV (NES method), compared to HH benchmark of -39.843 MeV
- Excitation energy of 4He 0+2 state: 28.66(0) MeV (NES method), compared to HH benchmark of 28.650 MeV
- Ground state point-proton radius of 4He: 1.21(0) fm (NES method)
- First excited state point-proton radius of 4He: 3.18(1) fm (NES method)
- Lambda separation energy for 4-Lambda-H ground state (0+): 2.43(0) MeV (OP-QNT method)
- Lambda separation energy for 4-Lambda-H first excited state (1+): 1.25(1) MeV (OP-QNT method)
- Excitation energy of 4-Lambda-H: 1.18(1) MeV (OP-QNT method), consistent with QNP reference of 1.18(0) MeV
- B(M1; 1+ -> 0+) for 4-Lambda-H: 2.634(5) mu_N^2 (OP-QNT method), compared to weak-coupling limit of 2.668(0) mu_N^2, showing approximately 1.3% suppression

## Context

This work extends the variational Monte Carlo with neural-network quantum states framework, previously applied to ground states of nuclei and hypernuclei by Zhang et al. (2025), to excited-state spectroscopy for the first time. The methods are benchmarked against hyperspherical harmonics calculations by PhysRevC.100.034004 for 4He and against the quantum number projection method from the authors' prior work for 4-Lambda-H. The calculation of the M1 transition strength for 4-Lambda-H provides the first ab initio prediction for this quantity, where previously only the weak-coupling limit of Dalitz (1978) was available as reference. This enables systematic application of NQS to nuclear and hypernuclear spectroscopy, including off-diagonal observables like gamma transitions.
