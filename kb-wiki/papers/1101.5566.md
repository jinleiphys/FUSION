# On the suppression of the $n\bar{n}$ transitions in medium

> The new model of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in the medium based on unitary $S$-matrix is considered. The time-dependence and corrections to the model are studied. The lower limit on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time $τ$ is in the range $10^{16}\; {\rm yr}>τ>1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$

# On the suppression of the $n\bar{n}$ transitions in medium

## Key claim
The free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time $\tau$ is bounded between $1.2 \times 10^9$ s (from a model with dressed antineutron propagator) and $10^{16}$ yr (from a model with bare propagator), but the author expresses caution about both extremes, noting that the model with bare propagator gives a quadratic time-dependence that seems "very unusual" and that the process is "unstable" to small changes in the antineutron self-energy.

## Method
The author constructs two models for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclear matter followed by annihilation, based on a unitary $S$-matrix approach rather than the optical potential model. Model **a** uses a bare antineutron propagator with zero self-energy, while model **b** dresses the propagator with a scalar field self-energy $\Sigma = V$. The process amplitude is derived from the interaction Hamiltonian containing the $n\bar{n}$ conversion term and the annihilation Hamiltonian, with the antineutron annihilation amplitude defined through the $S$-matrix. For model **a**, the infrared divergence from zero momentum transfer in the $n\bar{n}$ vertex is handled using a finite-time-interval field-theoretical approach.

## Key numbers
- Free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time lower limit from model **b**: $\tau^b = 1.2 \times 10^9$ s, derived using $\Gamma = 100$ MeV, $\Sigma = 10$ MeV, and $\tau_{\text{pot}} = 2.36 \times 10^8$ s from Super-Kamiokande data on oxygen.
- Free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time lower limit from model **a**: $\tau^a = 10^{16}$ yr, obtained from the quadratic time-dependence $W_a(t) \approx \epsilon_{n\bar{n}}^2 t^2$ for $\Gamma t \gg 1$.
- Ratio of model **b** to potential model probability: $r = W_b / W_{\text{pot}} = 25$, using $\Gamma = 100$ MeV and $\Sigma = 10$ MeV.
- Potential model lower limit from Super-Kamiokande: $\tau_{\text{pot}} = 2.36 \times 10^8$ s, based on experimental bound $T_{n\bar{n}} > 1.77 \times 10^{32}$ yr in oxygen.
- Antineutron annihilation width in medium: $\Gamma \sim 100$ MeV.
- Antineutron self-energy estimate: $\Sigma \approx 10$ MeV.
- Free-space $n\bar{n}$ conversion time scale: $\tau_c \sim 1/m_H < 10^{-29}$ s, from Higgs boson mass $m_H > 10^5$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous critique of the optical potential model for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in medium (Nazaruk, 2010, 2011), which showed that the potential model gives the wrong $\Gamma$-dependence ($W \sim 1/\Gamma$) because it uses a non-unitary $S$-matrix. The author had earlier proposed a diagram technique model (Nazaruk, 2006) but later abandoned it (Nazaruk, 2010) because it incorrectly placed the $n\bar{n}$ transition in a propagator rather than using a neutron wave function. The present paper refines the unitary model and provides the wide range $10^{16}$ yr $> \tau > 1.2 \times 10^9$ s as the current theoretical uncertainty. The lower bound exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14 and the potential model limit by a factor of 5. The work calls for further theoretical and experimental investigation to narrow this range.
