# HBT signature for clustered substructures probing primordial inhomogeneity in hot and dense QCD matter

> We propose a novel approach to probe primordial inhomogeneity in hot and dense matter which could be realized in non-central heavy-ion collisions. Although the Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) interferometry is commonly used to infer the system size, the cluster size should be detected if substructures emerge in space. We demonstrate that a signal peak in the HBT two-particle correlation stands at the relative momentum corresponding to the spatial scale of pseudo one-dimensional modulation. We assess detectability using the data prepared by an event generator (AMPT model) with clustering implemented in the particle distribution.

## Key claim

The authors propose that Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) interferometry can detect clustered substructures (primordial inhomogeneity) in hot and dense QCD matter produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions, provided that the spatial modulation is pseudo one-dimensional and the experimental alignment with the magnetic field axis is better than about 20 degrees.

## Method

The authors first derive an analytical Gaussian model for the two-particle correlation function with a cosine modulation term representing one-dimensional spatial inhomogeneity. They then implement a spatial modulation by hand into the phase-space distribution of pions generated by the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) event generator for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV with impact parameters between 3.0 fm and 4.0 fm. The two-particle correlation function for pi+ pairs is computed from the modulated particle distribution, applying a momentum filter to select pairs with small transverse relative momentum components.

## Key numbers

- The analytical Gaussian model uses a system size r0 = 6 fm, modulation amplitude alpha = 0.6, and wave number k = 0.4 fm^-1 (approximately 0.08 GeV), producing a signal peak at q_y approximately equal to k.
- The wave number k = 0.4 fm^-1 corresponds to a baryon chemical potential mu_B approximately 120 MeV, i.e., sqrt(s_NN) approximately 30 GeV, using the Chiral Spirals relation k approximately 2 mu_B/3.
- In the AMPT simulation, 1000 events of Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV produce 416824 pi+ particles (with p_z less than 1 GeV), yielding approximately 400 pi+ per event.
- The modulation amplitude a is set to 5 fm in the AMPT simulation; the signal peak in the normalized correlation ratio remains visible by a few percent even with a momentum filter of Delta q = 50 MeV.
- The signal is detectable when the tilting angle theta_n is approximately 20 degrees, but becomes marginal at theta_n = 30 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of inhomogeneous QCD phases, particularly the Chiral Soliton Lattice (CSL) state predicted by Brauner (2016) and the Quarkyonic Chiral Spirals of Kojo (2009, 2011), which are expected under strong magnetic fields in non-central heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that inhomogeneous states in three dimensions are fragile against fluctuations as argued by Landau (1969) and Peierls (1934), but strong magnetic fields can effectively reduce the system to a pseudo one-dimensional state where genuine inhomogeneity can develop. The proposed HBT method differs from conventional femtoscopic correlation measurements (used by STAR in 2014 and ALICE in 2020 for hadronic interactions) by focusing on the cluster size rather than the overall system size. This work enables experimental searches for inhomogeneous QCD matter using existing heavy-ion collision data, with the next step being systematic investigations of the modulation amplitude a and its relation to magnetic field strength.
