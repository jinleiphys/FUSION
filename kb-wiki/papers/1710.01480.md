# Effects of tensor and $T=0$ pairing interactions on nuclear $β^+$/EC decay

> The Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) plus proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) approach based on Skyrme interaction is applied to study the nuclear $β^+$/EC decay for nuclei near the proton magic numbers $Z=$20, 28, and 50. With properly selected Skyrme interactions that include the tensor terms, and the $T=0$ pairing interaction, the experimental $β^+$/EC decay half-lives of these nuclei can be systematically reproduced quite well. It is shown that the tensor and $T=0$ pairing interactions play different roles in different nuclei. The specific effect is relevant to the configurations that contribute to the decay. The attractive and repulsive properties of tensor interaction for the GT state is also studied. The present results indicate that it works repulsively on the GT states dominated by the configurations from $πj_\gtrless$ to $νj_\gtrless$ orbits, while working attractively for those mainly composed of the configurations from $πj_\gtrless$ to $νj_\lessgtr$ orbits.

# Effects of tensor and T=0 pairing interactions on nuclear β+/EC decay

## Key claim
The tensor and T=0 pairing interactions affect β+/EC decay half-lives differently depending on the dominant configuration type: tensor interaction works repulsively for GT states dominated by πj> to νj> transitions (raising excitation energies) and attractively for πj> to νj< transitions (lowering excitation energies), while T=0 pairing universally reduces half-lives by shifting states downward.

## Method
The authors applied the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) plus proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) approach using Skyrme-type interactions from the TIJ family (T21, T32, T43, T54). They included zero-range density-dependent surface pairing in the T=1 channel and a similar T=0 pairing interaction with strength f=1.1 relative to the T=1 channel. Calculations were performed in spherical approximation for nuclei near proton magic numbers Z=20, 28, and 50, comparing results with and without tensor terms and T=0 pairing.

## Key numbers
- T=0 pairing strength f = 1.1 times the T=1 channel strength (V0 = -470.0 MeV·fm³)
- D = 6163.4 s in the half-life formula; (gA/gV)eff = 1 adopted
- ΔnH = 0.78227 MeV (mass difference between neutron and hydrogen atom)
- For ³²Ar: GT state at Ex = -11.04 MeV with B(GT) = 0.94, dominated by π2s1/2 → ν2s1/2 configuration (R = 0.636)
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn: GT state at Ex = -5.33 MeV with B(GT) = 18.13, dominated by π1g9/2 → ν1g7/2 configuration (R = 0.949)
- For ⁵⁰Ni: GT state at Ex = -12.26 MeV with B(GT) = 5.03, dominated by π1f7/2 → ν1f7/2 configuration (R = 1.557)
- Tensor interaction shifts GT states: e.g., ³⁶Ca from Ex = -10.95 MeV (no tensor) to -9.03 MeV (with tensor); ¹⁰⁰Sn from Ex = -4.32 MeV to -5.33 MeV

## Context
This work extends the HFB+pnQRPA framework previously applied to β- decay (Mustonen 2016, Niu 2013) to β+/EC decay, specifically for nuclei near proton magic numbers. It builds on studies showing T=0 pairing shifts low-energy GT states downward (Engel 1999, Niu 2013) and tensor interaction effects on GT states (Bai 2009, Minato 2013, Mustonen 2014). The results confirm the tensor interaction property reported by Otsuka (2005) for single-particle states and by Colo (2007) and Brink (2007) in mean-field calculations, now extended to GT states in QRPA. The work enables systematic predictions of β+/EC half-lives for astrophysical applications and clarifies which interaction dominates for different configuration types.
