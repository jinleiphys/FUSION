# Microscopic study of the asymptotic behavior of the reduced width amplitude in $^7$Li and $^7$Be

> We investigate the effects of different basis model spaces on the calculation of reduced width amplitude (RWA) and asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the $^{7}$Li and $^{7}$Be nuclei. The two-cluster model ($α+t/^3$He) and three-cluster model ($α+d+n/p$) with the generator coordinates method (GCM) are applied to calculate the wave function of $^7$Li and $^7$Be. Specifically, the model space for the three-cluster model is constructed upon a sufficiently broad space of two-cluster configuration bases by further including three-cluster configuration bases. We compare the impact on the results from two basis sets for these added three-cluster bases: one with a compact and one with a broad spatial distribution. The final results reveal that the two-cluster model cannot accurately reproduce the binding energies of $^7$Li and $^7$Be, and tends to overestimate their ANCs. Regarding the calculations with the three-cluster model, while the two basis sets do not give significant differences in energy or energy spectrum, the basis set with a compact model space fails to describe the asymptotic behavior of the RWA adequately. This introduces excessive uncertainty into the ANC calculation. In the end, we conclude that when calculating ANC via a microscopic framework, particular attention must be paid to ensuring sufficient model space, especially for the components describing the breakup channels. This approach provides ANC values for $^7$Li and $^7$Be that agree well with experimental results.

# Microscopic study of the asymptotic behavior of the reduced width amplitude in $^7$Li and $^7$Be

## Key claim

The three-cluster model with a sufficiently broad spatial distribution of basis states (Set2) yields asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for $^7$Li and $^7$Be that agree well with experimental data, whereas the two-cluster model overestimates ANCs and a three-cluster model with only compact basis states (Set1) introduces excessive uncertainty into the ANC calculation despite giving nearly identical energies and wave function overlaps (99.7%) to the broad-basis calculation.

## Method

The authors applied the generator coordinates method (GCM) within a microscopic cluster model framework, using the Volkov No.2 NN interaction and G3RS spin-orbit potential. They constructed wave functions for $^7$Li and $^7$Be using a two-cluster model ($\alpha + t/^3$He) and a three-cluster model ($\alpha + d + n/p$), where the three-cluster model space augmented the full two-cluster basis with additional three-cluster configuration basis states. Two sets of three-cluster basis states were compared: Set1 with a compact spatial distribution and Set2 with a broader spatial distribution, both optimized using a control neural network (Ctrl.NN) that minimized the ground state energy. The reduced width amplitude (RWA) was computed via the Laplace expansion method, and ANCs were extracted by matching the logarithmic derivative of the RWA to that of the Whittaker function in the asymptotic region from the first contact point $a_0$ to 10 fm.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy of $^7$Li: experimental -39.25 MeV; two-cluster -37.13 MeV; three-cluster Set1 -38.68 MeV; three-cluster Set2 -38.77 MeV.
* Ground state energy of $^7$Be: experimental -37.60 MeV; two-cluster -35.48 MeV; three-cluster Set1 -37.40 MeV; three-cluster Set2 -37.31 MeV.
* Charge radius of $^7$Li: experimental 2.44 fm; two-cluster 2.55 fm; three-cluster Set1 2.53 fm; three-cluster Set2 2.55 fm.
* Charge radius of $^7$Be: experimental 2.65 fm; two-cluster 2.75 fm; three-cluster Set1 2.73 fm; three-cluster Set2 2.76 fm.
* Overlap between ground-state wave functions of Set1 and Set2: 99.7%.
* ANC for $^7$Li(g.s.)$\rightarrow \alpha + t$: two-cluster $4.58^{+0.35}_{-0.20}$ fm$^{-1/2}$; three-cluster Set1 $3.50^{+0.59}_{-0.59}$ fm$^{-1/2}$; three-cluster Set2 $4.03^{+0.13}_{-0.11}$ fm$^{-1/2}$.
* ANC for $^7$Be(g.s.)$\rightarrow \alpha + {}^3$He: two-cluster $5.15^{+0.18}_{-0.12}$ fm$^{-1/2}$; three-cluster Set1 $3.88^{+0.68}_{-0.88}$ fm$^{-1/2}$; three-cluster Set2 $4.29^{+0.21}_{-0.29}$ fm$^{-1/2}$.

## Context

This work extends microscopic cluster model studies of $^7$Li and $^7$Be beyond the single-channel approximation, motivated by findings from Mertelmeier and Hofmann (1986) and Csoto (2000) that incorporating the ($^6$Li + n/p) channel alters both the absolute value and low-energy slope of the S-factor. It also follows studies by Timofeyuk (2008, 2014) showing that valence nucleon correlations in three-body cluster systems induce non-standard asymptotic behavior. The results enable more reliable theoretical determination of the ${}^3$H(${}^4$He,$\gamma$)${}^7$Li and ${}^3$He(${}^4$He,$\gamma$)${}^7$Be radiative capture reaction rates relevant to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino production, and demonstrate that a wave function component constituting less than 0.3% of the total norm can be decisive for accurate ANC calculation even when its impact on total energy is negligible.
