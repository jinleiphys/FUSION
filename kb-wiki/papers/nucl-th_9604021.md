# On the asymptotical equations in the harmonic oscillator representation

> In the harmonic oscillator representation, the Schrodinger equation has a form of a set of infinite number of algebraical equations which are labeled by the radial quantum number "n". It is shown that at n>>1 these equations are significantly simplified and become an algebraical analogue of the original differential Schrodinger equation. This result is generalized to the case of multi-channel systems being studied in the framework of the resonating group method.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for large radial quantum numbers (n ≫ 1), the infinite set of algebraic equations in the harmonic oscillator representation simplifies to a three-term recurrence relation that is a discrete analogue of the original differential Schrödinger equation, but this simplification holds only for potentials that are not singular at the origin and requires n ≥ 20 for practical convergence.

## Method

The authors start from the Schrödinger equation for a particle in a spherical-symmetric potential and expand the wave function in the harmonic oscillator basis, obtaining an infinite set of algebraic equations for the Fourier coefficients C_{nl}. They then analyze the asymptotic behavior at large n, using the property that the basis functions Φ_{nl}(r) behave like a delta function at the turning point r_n = √(4n+2l+3), which allows them to replace the infinite sum over potential matrix elements with a local potential term U(r_n)C_{nl}. The resulting three-term recurrence relation is shown to be equivalent to the original differential equation at large r.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic simplification becomes valid for n ≥ 20, based on numerical tests of convergence for the sum of matrix elements.
- For the potential U(r) = (1 − exp(−γr²))/r with γ = 0.25, the sums of matrix elements converge to the asymptotic limit at n = 20.
- The turning point relation is r_n = √(4n+2l+3), which connects the discrete quantum number n to the continuous radial coordinate r.
- For the Coulomb interaction between channels, the asymptotic matrix elements decrease as U_{αβ} ≈ A_{αβ}/√(4n+2l+3).
- In the hyperspherical function method generalization, √(4n+2l+3) is replaced by √(4n+2K+3(N−1)) where N is the number of particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier quasi-classical approximations for truncating the infinite harmonic oscillator basis, as developed in Refs. [1-3] of the paper, by providing a rigorous asymptotic treatment of the potential matrix elements. The method is particularly relevant for the algebraic version of the resonating group method and the hyperspherical function method, where the potential is not known explicitly but only through its matrix elements. The results enable the determination of asymptotic wave functions including long-range effects such as Coulomb repulsion, which is important for complex scaling calculations in nuclear reactions.
