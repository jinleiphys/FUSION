# Mass of Y(3940) in Bethe-Salpeter equation for quarks

> The general form of the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for the bound states composed of two vector fields of arbitrary spin and definite parity is corrected. Using the revised general formalism, we investigate the observed \emph{Y}(3940) state which is considered as a molecule state consisting of $D^{*0}\bar{D}^{*0}$. Though the attractive potential between $D^{*0}$ and $\bar{D}^{*0}$ including one light meson ($σ$, $π$, $ω$, $ρ$) exchange is considered, we find that in our approach the contribution from one-$π$ exchange is equal to zero and consider SU(3) symmetry breaking. The obtained mass of \emph{Y}(3940) is consistent with the experimental value.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the mass of the Y(3940) state, interpreted as a D*0D̅*0 molecule, can be obtained from a corrected Bethe-Salpeter formalism and is consistent with the experimental value, but only after finding that one-pion exchange contributes zero to the potential and after incorporating SU(3) symmetry breaking in the vector-meson exchange kernel.

## Method

The authors first corrected the general form of the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for bound states of two vector fields of arbitrary spin and definite parity. They then applied this revised formalism to the Y(3940) assumed to be a D*0D̅*0 molecule with J^P = 0^+ or 2^+, constructing the interaction kernel from one-light-meson exchange (σ, π, ω, ρ) where the heavy mesons are treated as bound states of u and c quarks. The interaction potential was derived via form factors without an extra cutoff parameter, and the resulting relativistic Schrödinger-like equation was solved numerically in the instantaneous approximation.

## Key numbers

- The mass of Y(3940) obtained from the calculation is consistent with the experimental value (no explicit numerical result for the mass is given in the truncated text).
- The mixing angle between SU(3) octet and singlet vector mesons is taken as θ = 38.58°, obtained by the KLOE collaboration.
- The ω-meson-quark coupling constant squared is g_ω^2 = 2.42.
- The φ-meson-quark coupling constant squared is g_φ^2 = 13.0.
- The π-meson-quark coupling constant is g_π = 340/97.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule interpretation of Y(3940) proposed by Liu (no year given) by using a Bethe-Salpeter approach rather than treating the heavy mesons as pointlike objects with a cutoff-dependent potential. The authors correct a previous general formalism for BS wave functions of two-vector-field bound states from their own prior work (Chen and Lü, no year given). The result enables a determination of the definite quantum numbers (0^+ or 2^+) of Y(3940) from the mass calculation, which previous work could not deduce.
