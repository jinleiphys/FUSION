# Localization of shadow poles by complex scaling

> Through numerical examples we show that the complex scaling method is suited to explore the pole structure in multichannel scattering problems. All poles lying on the multisheeted Riemann energy surface, including shadow poles, can be revealed and the Riemann sheets on which they reside can be identified.

## Key claim

Through numerical examples, the paper shows that the complex scaling method can be extended to explore the pole structure of multichannel scattering problems, including shadow poles on all Riemann sheets, provided that the rotation angles in different channels can be chosen independently when necessary.

## Method

The author constructs a two-channel model using one-term separable potentials with harmonic oscillator basis functions, where one channel has zero diagonal interaction so that all poles originate from the other channel. The standard complex scaling transformation is applied with a single rotation angle theta, and then generalized to allow different rotation angles theta_alpha in different channels, with matrix elements computed via back-rotated channel states. Pole positions are extracted by solving the eigenvalue problem of the transformed Hamiltonian for various rotation angles and identifying which continua have swept over each pole.

## Key numbers

- Uncoupled single-channel resonance pole at E = 3.049 - i2.153 (atomic units, hbar = m = 1)
- With coupling (lambda_12 = lambda_21 = 1.0), pole on (--) sheet at E = 4.742 - i1.810
- With coupling, shadow pole on (-+) sheet at E = 2.395 - i1.467
- Second example: uncoupled single-channel pole at E = 2.430 - i3.704 (lambda_22 = 0.2)
- With coupling (lambda_12 = lambda_21 = 0.42), pole on (--) sheet at E = 3.381 - i3.228
- With coupling, shadow pole on (+-) sheet at E = 2.373 - i3.357

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously well-understood only for single-channel problems and applied only to ordinary poles in multichannel cases, to systematically locate shadow poles on all Riemann sheets. The method builds on the sheet-labeling scheme of Pearce and Gibson (1989) and addresses unexplained phenomena noted by Brandas, Rittby, and Elander (1989) where varying the rotation angle caused resonances to be absorbed by continua. The generalized complex scaling with channel-dependent rotation angles enables access to Riemann sheets that cannot be reached from the physical sheet when degenerate thresholds are present.
