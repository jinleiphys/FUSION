# Transparent Nuclei and Deuteron-Gold Collisions at RHIC

> The current normalization of the cross section of inclusive high-pT particle production in deuteron-gold collisions measured RHIC relies on Glauber calculations for the inelastic d-Au cross section. These calculations should be corrected for diffraction. Moreover, they miss the Gribov's inelastic shadowing which makes nuclei more transparent (color transparency). The magnitude of this effect rises with energy and it may dramatically affect the normalization of the RHIC data. We evaluate these corrections employing the light-cone dipole formalism and found a rather modest corrections for the current normalization of the d-Au data. The results of experiments insensitive to diffraction (PHENIX, PHOBOS) should be renormalized by about 20% down, while those which include diffraction (STAR), by only 10%. Such a correction completely eliminates the Cronin enhancement in the PHENIX data for pions. The largest theoretical uncertainty comes from the part of the inelastic shadowing which is related to diffractive gluon radiation, or gluon shadowing. Our estimate is adjusted to data for the triple-Pomeron coupling, however, other models do not have such a restrictions and predict much stronger gluon shadowing. Therefore, the current data for high-pT hadron production in d-Au collisions at RHIC cannot exclude in a model independent way the possibility if initial state suppression proposed by Kharzeev-Levin-McLerran. Probably the only way to settle this uncertainty is a direct measurement of the inelastic d-Au cross sections at RHIC. Also d-Au collisions with a tagged spectator nucleon may serve as a sensitive probe for nuclear transparency and inelastic shadowing. We found an illuminating quantum-mechanical effect: the nucleus acts like a lens focusing spectators into a very narrow cone.

## Key claim

The normalization of inclusive high-\(p_T\) particle production in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, which currently relies on Glauber model calculations for the inelastic \(d\)-Au cross section, must be corrected for both diffraction effects and Gribov's inelastic shadowing (color transparency); these corrections are modest (about 10–20% reduction) but sufficient to eliminate the Cronin enhancement in PHENIX pion data, yet the theoretical uncertainty from gluon shadowing is large enough that the data cannot model-independently rule out initial-state suppression as proposed by Kharzeev-Levin-McLerran.

## Method

The author employed the light-cone dipole formalism to sum multiple interactions to all orders and evaluate inelastic shadowing corrections to the inelastic \(d\)-Au cross section. The Glauber model was extended to treat the deuteron as a two-nucleon system, and the inelastic \(NN\) cross section was corrected for diffractive channels (single and double diffraction) to match the event selection of each experiment (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS). The valence-quark and gluonic contributions to inelastic shadowing were estimated separately, with the gluonic part constrained by data on the triple-Pomeron coupling.

## Key numbers

- The correction factor \(K\) for experiments insensitive to diffraction (PHENIX, PHOBOS) is about 0.83–0.87, corresponding to a renormalization downward by about 20%.
- The correction factor \(K\) for experiments that include diffraction (STAR) is about 0.91–0.96, corresponding to a renormalization downward by about 10%.
- The inelastic \(d\)-Au cross section in the Glauber model (including diffraction, STAR trigger) is 2422.7 mb; after valence-quark fluctuations it becomes 2226.6 mb (or 2335.8 mb with an alternative treatment), and after including gluonic excitations it becomes 2118.3 mb (or 2228.3 mb).
- The non-diffractive inelastic \(d\)-Au cross section (PHENIX/PHOBOS trigger) in the Glauber model is 2146.0 mb; after valence-quark fluctuations it becomes 1998.3 mb (or 2100.1 mb), and after including gluonic excitations it becomes 1930.3 mb (or 2033.7 mb).
- The mean number of collisions \(N_{coll}\) for minimum-bias inelastic events (STAR) increases from 6.9 (Glauber) to 7.9 (including gluon shadowing).
- The mean number of collisions \(N_{coll}\) for minimum-bias non-diffractive events (PHENIX) increases from 5.5 (Glauber) to 6.1 (including gluon shadowing).
- The total \(d\)-Au cross section is 4110.1 mb in the Glauber model, 3701.0 mb after valence-quark fluctuations, and 3466.2 mb after including gluonic excitations.
- The cross section for tagged spectator events (non-diffractive) is 324.3 mb in the Glauber model and 498.4 mb after including gluon shadowing.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections (Gribov 1969) to deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC energies, correcting the Glauber model calculations used by the PHENIX, STAR, and PHOBOS collaborations. The author shows that the published nuclear modification factors \(R_{dA}(p_T)\) are subject to significant renormalization (10–20%) and that the Cronin enhancement in PHENIX pion data disappears after correction. The paper highlights that the theoretical uncertainty from gluon shadowing (with some models predicting a correction factor as low as \(K=0.65\)) prevents a model-independent conclusion about initial-state suppression proposed by Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran. The author recommends direct measurement of the inelastic \(d\)-Au cross section at RHIC and notes that tagged spectator events can serve as a sensitive probe of nuclear transparency, revealing a quantum-mechanical focusing effect where the nucleus acts like a lens concentrating spectators into a narrow cone.
