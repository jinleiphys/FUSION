# A novel nuclear dependence of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations

> A linear correlation is found between the magnitude of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and the nuclear binding energy per nucleon with pairing energy removed. By using this relation, the strengths of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations of some unmeasured nuclei are predicted. Discussions on nucleon-nucleon pairing energy and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations are made. The found nuclear dependence of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations may shed some lights on the short-range structure of nucleus.

# A novel nuclear dependence of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations

## Key claim

A linear correlation exists between the magnitude of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) and the nuclear binding energy per nucleon, but only after the pairing energy is subtracted from the binding energy; without this subtraction, the correlation is poor (chi-squared per degree of freedom of 8.9 for a2 vs. B, compared to 1.4 for a2 vs. B^Mod).

## Method

The authors take measured SRC ratios (a2 and R2N) from inclusive electron scattering experiments at SLAC and JLab for nuclei from 3He to 197Au, and compare them to nuclear binding energies from atomic mass evaluations. They define a modified binding energy B^Mod by subtracting the pairing energy delta (taken from the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula with a_p = 12 MeV) from the experimental binding energy B. Linear fits are performed between SRC ratios and B^Mod/A, and the resulting formulas are used to predict SRC ratios for unmeasured nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For a2 vs. B^Mod/A: slope = 0.496 ± 0.017 (MeV^-1), intercept = 0.849 ± 0.072, chi-squared/ndf = 1.4
- For R2N vs. B^Mod/A: slope = 0.389 ± 0.027 (MeV^-1), intercept = 0.90 ± 0.15, chi-squared/ndf = 0.55
- Measured a2 values range from 2.13 ± 0.04 (3He) to 5.30 ± 0.60 (27Al)
- Measured R2N values range from 1.92 ± 0.09 (3He) to 4.40 ± 0.60 (27Al)
- Modified binding energies per nucleon B^Mod/A range from 2.573 MeV (3He) to 8.761 MeV (56Fe)
- For the EMC slope vs. (B - delta - B^Coul)/A: chi-squared/ndf = 0.505 (7 data points), compared to 1.01 for the correlation without pairing removal

## Context

This work extends the known linear correlation between N-N SRC and the EMC effect (NuclDepenRev, 2014; Hen et al., 2012; Weinstein et al., 2011) by finding a new nuclear dependence on modified binding energy. It builds on the approach of Wang (2015) who used nuclear binding energy to study the EMC effect. The authors note that average nuclear density fails to predict SRC strength accurately, and that the local density approximation (Benhar et al., 1989, 1994, 2008) is used for spectral function calculations. The found correlation enables predictions of SRC ratios for unmeasured nuclei such as 3H, 6Li, 10B, 14N, 40Ca, and 48Ca, and suggests experimental measurements of odd-odd nuclei to further test the dependence.
