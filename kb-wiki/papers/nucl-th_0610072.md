# Complex coupled-cluster approach to an ab-initio description of open quantum systems

> We develop ab-initio coupled-cluster theory to describe resonant and weakly bound states along the neutron drip line. We compute the ground states of the helium chain 3-10He within coupled-cluster theory in singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation. We employ a spherical Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis generated from the low-momentum N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction. This basis treats bound, resonant, and continuum states on equal footing, and is therefore optimal for the description of properties of drip line nuclei where continuum features play an essential role. Within this formalism, we present an ab-initio calculation of energies and decay widths of unstable nuclei starting from realistic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab-initio coupled-cluster calculation of both energies and decay widths of unstable nuclei starting from a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the computed widths and lifetimes are only in semi-quantitative agreement with experiment, and the perturbative triples correction (CCSD(T)) fails for the truly open-shell nucleus ⁶He.

## Method

The authors employ coupled-cluster theory in the singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation, using a spherical Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis generated from a low-momentum N³LO nucleon-nucleon interaction (with momentum cutoff Λ = 1.9 fm⁻¹). The Berggren basis treats bound, resonant, and continuum single-particle states on equal footing, and the Hamiltonian becomes complex symmetric rather than Hermitian. For neutrons in s and p orbits, a complex Woods-Saxon basis with discretized continuum states is used, while harmonic oscillator states (ħω = 20 MeV) are used for protons and higher partial waves (d-g). The ground states of the helium chain ³–¹⁰He are computed, and the accuracy is benchmarked against exact diagonalizations in small model spaces.

## Key numbers

- CCSD ground state energy of ⁴He: -27.35 MeV (s-g basis); experimental value is -28.30 MeV.
- CCSD ground state energy of ⁵He: -24.87 MeV with width Γ = 0.32 MeV (from Im[E] = -0.16 MeV); experimental energy is -27.41 MeV with width Γ = 0.66(4) MeV.
- CCSD ground state energy of ⁶He: -25.16 MeV with width Γ = 0.12 MeV (from Im[E] = -0.06 MeV); experimental energy is -29.27 MeV (bound).
- CCSD ground state energy of ⁷He: -24.83 MeV with width Γ = 0.18 MeV (from Im[E] = -0.09 MeV); experimental energy is -28.83 MeV with width Γ = 0.16(4) MeV.
- CCSD ground state energy of ⁸He: -26.26 MeV (bound, Im[E] = 0.00); experimental energy is -31.41 MeV.
- For ⁷He in a small model space (nine p₃/₂ orbitals above ⁴He core), CCSD reproduces the exact diagonalization result (E = 2.37 MeV, Γ = 0.23 MeV) to within less than 10 keV.
- In benchmark comparisons with exact diagonalization, CCSD results for ³–⁵He differ by not more than 500 keV; CCSD(T) corrections reduce this to 200 keV or less.
- For ⁶He in the benchmark, CCSD differs from exact by 1.7 MeV; CCSD(T) reduces the error to 200 keV.
- Extrapolation of ⁵He CCSD results to infinite basis yields E∞ = -24.89 ± 0.01 MeV; for ⁸He, E∞ = -26.90 ± 0.03 MeV.

## Context

This work extends coupled-cluster theory, previously applied to closed quantum systems, to open quantum systems by employing a Gamow (Berggren) basis that includes resonant and continuum states. It builds on prior Gamow-Hartree-Fock diagonalization methods (Michel et al., Rotureau et al.) and on low-momentum interactions (Bogner et al., Nogga et al.). The results are compared with other ab-initio methods such as Green's function Monte Carlo (Pudliner et al., 1997) and the no-core shell model (Caurier et al., 2006). The paper enables future ab-initio studies of neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, but notes that three-body forces and non-perturbative treatment of triples corrections will be needed for quantitative accuracy.
