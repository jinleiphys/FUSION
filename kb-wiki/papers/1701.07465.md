# Relating the finite-volume spectrum and the two-and-three-particle $S$ matrix for relativistic systems of identical scalar particles

> Working in relativistic quantum field theory, we derive the quantization condition satisfied by coupled two- and three-particle systems of identical scalar particles confined to a cubic spatial volume with periodicity $L$. This gives the relation between the finite-volume spectrum and the infinite-volume $\textbf 2 \to \textbf 2$, $\textbf 2 \to \textbf 3$ and $\textbf 3 \to \textbf 3$ scattering amplitudes for such theories. The result holds for relativistic systems composed of scalar particles with nonzero mass $m$, whose center of mass energy lies below the four-particle threshold, and for which the two-particle $K$ matrix has no singularities below the three-particle threshold. The quantization condition is exact up to corrections of the order $\mathcal{O}(e^{-mL})$ and holds for any choice of total momenta satisfying the boundary conditions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantization condition that relates the finite-volume energy spectrum to the coupled two-to-two, two-to-three, and three-to-three scattering amplitudes for relativistic systems of identical scalar particles, removing the previous restriction that two-to-three transitions are forbidden. The result holds for center-of-mass energies below the four-particle threshold and requires that the two-particle K matrix has no singularities below the three-particle threshold, with corrections of order e^{-mL}.

## Method

The authors construct a matrix of finite-volume correlators M_L containing two-to-two, two-to-three, three-to-two, and three-to-three entries, defined as sums of amputated, on-shell, connected Feynman diagrams with spatial loop momenta summed over the finite-volume set (2πn/L). They use time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) to identify all two- and three-particle poles explicitly, employing modified propagators built from two-particle-irreducible (2PI) and three-particle-irreducible (3PI) self-energies to keep intermediate states explicit. Smooth cutoff functions H_2 and H_3 are introduced to remove disconnected two-to-three transitions, and the sums over finite-volume momenta are expressed as products of infinite-volume quantities and finite-volume functions using extensions of results from Luscher (1986, 1990) and Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015).

## Key numbers

- The quantization condition is valid for center-of-mass energies in the range m < E* < min[4m, m + M_p], where M_p is the lowest-lying pole in the two-particle K matrix
- The result is exact up to corrections of order O(e^{-mL}), where L is the linear extent of the cubic spatial volume and m is the particle mass
- The kinematic window for three-particle physics is 3m < E* < 4m, below the four-particle production threshold
- The two-particle on-shell momentum in the CM frame is q* = sqrt(E*^2/4 - m^2)
- The two-particle CM energy for a spectator with momentum k is E*_{2,k} = sqrt((E - ω_k)^2 - (P - k)^2)

## Context

This work extends the three-particle quantization condition derived by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) by removing the Z_2 symmetry restriction that forbade two-to-three transitions, enabling the study of resonances that decay into both two- and three-particle final states such as the Roper resonance N(1440) and XYZ states. The result generalizes the two-particle quantization condition of Luscher (1986, 1990) and its extensions by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995), Kim, Sachrajda, and Sharpe (2005), and Hansen and Sharpe (2012) to coupled two- and three-particle systems, providing a necessary step toward using lattice QCD to extract resonance properties from finite-volume spectra when multiple decay channels are open.
