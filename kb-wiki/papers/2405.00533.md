# Damping of spin waves

> We show that, in ideal-spin hydrodynamics, the components of the spin tensor follow damped wave equations. The damping rate is related to nonlocal collisions of the particles in the fluid, which enter at first order in $\hbar$ in a semi-classical expansion. This rate provides an estimate for the timescale of spin equilibration and is computed by considering a system of spin-1/2 fermions interacting via a quartic self-interaction as well as via (screened) one-gluon exchange. It is found that the relaxation times of the components of the spin tensor can become very large compared to the usual dissipative timescales of the system. Our results suggest that the spin degrees of freedom in a heavy-ion collision may not be in equilibrium by the time of freeze-out, and thus should be treated dynamically.

## Key claim

In ideal-spin hydrodynamics, the components of the spin tensor follow damped wave equations, where the damping originates from nonlocal collisions at first order in ℏ; the relaxation time for the magnetic component of the spin potential can become orders of magnitude larger than typical dissipative timescales, implying that spin degrees of freedom in heavy-ion collisions may not equilibrate before freeze-out and must be treated dynamically.

## Method

The authors derive damped wave equations for the electric and magnetic components of the spin potential from the conservation of total angular momentum in a static fluid background, using a semi-classical expansion of quantum kinetic theory. They compute the damping coefficients (relaxation times τκ and τω) by evaluating the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor arising from nonlocal collision terms, considering spin-1/2 fermions with a scalar four-fermion interaction and with screened one-gluon exchange. The relaxation times are expressed as integrals over the transition amplitude and the Lorentz-covariant spacetime shift, and are compared to the shear-stress relaxation time τπ computed in the 14-moment approximation.

## Key numbers

- For a scalar four-fermion interaction, the ratio τκ/τπ stays approximately constant as a function of z = m/T, while τω/τπ ∼ 2 at small z and grows like z², reaching τω/τπ ∼ 2 × 10³ at z ≈ 100.
- For strange quarks in the deconfined phase (z ≈ 0.5), τω is about a factor of four larger than τκ and a factor of two larger than τπ.
- For hyperons in the hadronic phase (z ≈ 10), the relaxation time τω is considerably larger than the typical dissipative timescales of the system.
- In the limit of small z for a vector interaction (one-gluon exchange), τκ and τω assume the same value.

## Context

This work extends the undamped spin-wave equations found by Ambrus (2022) and Hu (2022) by including damping from nonlocal collisions, which were previously treated only in a relaxation-time approximation by Hu (2022). It complements the calculations of Weickgenannt (2022) on dissipative spin-tensor components by computing relaxation times for the ideal components of the spin tensor. The results agree qualitatively with earlier estimates by Kapusta (2019) and Hidaka (2023) that spin relaxation can be slow, and they explain why the equilibrium approach of Becattini (2013) works for high-energy collisions but fails at lower energies.
