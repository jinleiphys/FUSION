# Deformation and orientation effects in the driving potential of the dinuclear model

> A double-folding method is used to calculate the nuclear and Coulomb interaction between two deformed nuclei with arbitrary orientations. A simplified Skryme-type interaction is adopted. The contributions of nuclear interaction and Coulomb interaction due to the deformation and orientation of the nuclei are evaluated for the driving potential used in the description of heavy-ion fusion reaction. So far there is no satisfactory theory to describe the evolution of the dynamical nuclear deformation and orientations during the heavy-ion fusion process. Our results estimated the magnitude of above effects.

## Key claim
The paper estimates the magnitude of deformation and orientation effects on the nuclear and Coulomb parts of the driving potential in the dinuclear system (DNS) model, but acknowledges that there is no satisfactory theory to describe the dynamical evolution of nuclear deformation and orientations during heavy-ion fusion.

## Method
A double-folding method with a simplified Skyrme-type interaction was used to calculate the nuclear and Coulomb potentials between two deformed nuclei with arbitrary orientations. The nuclear densities were described by two-parameter Woods-Saxon distributions with quadrupole deformations, and the six- and three-dimensional integrals in the folding formulas were computed numerically. The driving potential for the DNS model was obtained by taking the sum of Coulomb and nuclear potentials at the minimum in the internuclear distance R that is shorter than the Coulomb-barrier saddle point.

## Key numbers
- For the system $^{28}Na+^{244}Es$ forming $^{272}Ds$, the prolate deformation parameters are $\beta_2=0.257$ for $^{28}Na$ and $\beta_2=0.234$ for $^{244}Es$.
- For the system $^{74}Zn+^{198}Hg$ forming $^{272}Ds$, $^{74}Zn$ is prolate with $\beta_2=0.125$ and $^{198}Hg$ is oblate with $\beta_2=-0.112$.
- The diffuseness parameters were set to $a_{\rho_1}=a_{\rho_2}=0.6$ fm, and the radial cutoff for nuclear potential integration was $r_{cut}=25$ fm.
- The parameters used in the nuclear potential calculation include $C_0=300$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$, $f_{in}=0.09$, $f_{ex}=-2.59$, $f'_{in}=0.42$, $f'_{ex}=0.54$, and $\rho_{00}=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For the fragmentation $\eta=-0.243$, the configuration is $^{103}Mo+^{169}Er$ with deformations $(\beta_2^1, \beta_2^2)=(0.358, 0.304)$.
- For $\eta=-0.169$, the configuration $^{113}Pd+^{159}Gd$ consists of $^{113}Pd$ with $\beta_2=-0.25$ and $^{159}Gd$ with $\beta_2=0.28$.

## Context
This work extends the DNS model calculations of Li et al. (2003) by including deformation in both the nuclear and Coulomb interactions, whereas previous work had only considered deformation in the Coulomb part and used spherical nuclei for the nuclear interaction. The results show that the driving potential is sensitive to orientation, with the tip-tip configuration giving the lowest interaction energy, and that deviations from spherical treatments appear in regions of large deformation (around $|\eta|\sim0.2$ and $|\eta|\sim0.8$). The findings enable future calculations of fusion probabilities for various projectile-target combinations and motivate the development of a two-dimensional potential as a function of mass asymmetry and internuclear distance to study quasi-fission effects.
