# Tri-axial deformation in nuclei with realistic NN interactions

> The structure of finite nuclei is investigated by employing an interaction model which is based on the low-momentum interaction $V_{lowk}$. It is supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction fitted to reproduce the saturation properties of infinite nuclear matter within the Hartree-Fock approach. The calculations of finite nuclei are performed in a basis of plane waves discretized in a cartesian box of appropriate size. As a first example the structure of Ne isotopes is considered ranging from $^{18}$Ne to the neutron drip line. Rather good agreement is obtained for the bulk properties of these nuclei without any free parameter. The basis is also appropriate to describe other deformed nuclei and the transition from discrete nuclei to homogeneous matter which is supposed to occur in the crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Hartree-Fock approach using a low-momentum NN interaction (\(V_{lowk}\)) supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction with only three adjustable parameters, fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties, can reproduce bulk properties of open-shell neon isotopes from \(^{18}\)Ne to the neutron drip line, including tri-axial deformations, without any free parameters for finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock equations using a basis of plane-wave states with periodic boundary conditions in a cubic cartesian box of side length \(L = 18\) fm, restricting basis momenta to \(|\vec{k}| \leq 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) (739 momenta, 1478 basis states per nucleon type). The interaction model combines the \(V_{lowk}\) potential derived from the CD Bonn potential via the unitary-model-operator approach with a cut-off \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\), plus a contact interaction of Skyrme-like form whose three parameters (\(t_0 = 584.1\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 8330.7\) MeV fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\), \(x_3 = -0.5\), with \(\alpha=0.5\) and \(x_0=0.0\) fixed) are fitted to reproduce the empirical saturation point and symmetry energy of symmetric nuclear matter. The HF iteration is initiated with tri-axial deformed Woods-Saxon eigenstates, and only parity symmetry is imposed.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{18}\)Ne: calculated \(-7.20\) MeV, experimental \(-7.34\) MeV (difference 0.14 MeV).
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{20}\)Ne: calculated \(-7.96\) MeV, experimental \(-8.03\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{22}\)Ne: calculated \(-8.03\) MeV, experimental \(-8.08\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{24}\)Ne: calculated \(-8.02\) MeV, experimental \(-7.99\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{26}\)Ne: calculated \(-7.63\) MeV, experimental \(-7.75\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{28}\)Ne: calculated \(-7.31\) MeV, experimental \(-7.39\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{30}\)Ne: calculated \(-7.01\) MeV, experimental \(-7.07\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{32}\)Ne: calculated \(-6.72\) MeV, experimental \(-6.67\) MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{34}\)Ne: calculated \(-6.17\) MeV (no experimental value; total binding energy smaller than for \(^{32}\)Ne, indicating drip line).
* Energy gain from tri-axial deformation for \(^{24}\)Ne: 0.8 MeV per nucleon (19.2 MeV total), the largest among isotopes studied.
* Energy gap between highest occupied and lowest unoccupied state for \(^{20}\)Ne: larger than 6 MeV for both protons and neutrons.
* Box size used: \(L = 18\) fm; basis cutoff: \(|\vec{k}| \leq 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) yielding 739 momenta (1478 basis states per nucleon type).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (van Dalen and Müther, 2009) from closed-shell nuclei (\(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca) to open-shell, weakly bound neon isotopes, using a tri-axial plane-wave basis in a cartesian box rather than the spherical basis used earlier. It contrasts with phenomenological density functional theory calculations (e.g., Ebran et al., 2011; Martini et al., 2011) that employ axially-symmetric cylindrical coordinates and phenomenological interactions, by using a realistic NN interaction (\(V_{lowk}\)) and allowing fully tri-axial deformations. The method enables future studies of the transition from discrete nuclei to homogeneous matter in neutron star crusts, as demonstrated by a preliminary calculation of a system with 10 protons and 32 neutrons showing a quasi-nucleus with a neutron gas extending in one direction.
