# Effects of Jets in the Flow Observables

> The transverse momentum anisotropy of the particles produced in heavy ion collisions is one of the most important experimental observable to investigate the collective behavior of the systems created in such collisions. Recent studies show that the complex nature of the system evolution, such as initial condition fluctuations and jets, may lead to important effects in the flow coefficients and, therefore, to misinterpretation of the results obtained. In this study, we used simulated events produced with a hydrodynamic model which allows inhomogeneous initial condition combined with proton-proton collisions produced with the Pythia event generator to create a final set of particles to be analyzed with the usual experimental flow calculation techniques. Although this simplified approach is somehow unrealistic, since it does not include the interaction of the jet with the medium, our results have shown a good agreement of the behavior of the elliptic flow coefficient as a function of the transverse momentum up to 6 GeV/c for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. Although each model alone is not able to describe the full range, the combination of both sets of particles as seen by the flow calculation techniques may be the key to explain the behavior observed in experimental data.

# Effects of Jets in the Flow Observables

## Key claim
The combination of a hydrodynamic model (NeXSPheRIO) with embedded Pythia jet events can reproduce the elliptic flow coefficient as a function of transverse momentum up to 6 GeV/c for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, even though the model does not include jet-medium interactions and each component alone fails to describe the full range.

## Method
The authors generated Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using the NeXSPheRIO ideal hydrodynamic code and embedded an integer number alpha of proton-proton collisions from the Pythia event generator into each event. The number of Pythia events was chosen so that the combined charged pion spectra matched experimental data from STAR (Abelev 2006). The best configuration used Pythia QCDJets with CKIN(3) = 3.5 GeV/c, with alpha ranging from approximately 1 to 9 depending on centrality. The resulting events were analyzed with standard experimental flow calculation techniques including the event plane method and cumulant method.

## Key numbers
- Elliptic flow coefficient v2 was reproduced up to pT = 6 GeV/c for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The best Pythia configuration used CKIN(3) = 3.5 GeV/c (minimum transverse momentum of the main scattering process)
- The number of embedded Pythia events alpha ranged from approximately 1 to 9, being higher for more central collisions
- Two-particle correlations were calculated using charged particles with 0.5 < pT^a < 1.5 GeV/c (associated) and pT^b > 2.0 GeV/c (trigger)

## Context
This work extends studies of flow observables in heavy ion collisions by addressing how jets from hard parton-parton interactions affect flow coefficients, building on prior work by Hama (2004) and the NeXSPheRIO collaboration. The results suggest that non-flow contributions from jets are negligible for integrated flow estimates, with the observed behavior dominated by initial condition fluctuations as studied by Takahashi (2009). The approach enables future investigations where jet-medium interactions are included, which the authors note would likely improve agreement with experimental data in the intermediate pT region around 5 GeV/c.
