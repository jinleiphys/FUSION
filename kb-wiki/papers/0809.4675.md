# Abstract composition rule for relativistic kinetic energy in the thermodynamical limit

> We demonstrate by simple mathematical considerations that a power-law tailed distribution in the kinetic energy of relativistic particles can be a limiting distribution seen in relativistic heavy ion experiments. We prove that the infinite repetition of an arbitrary composition rule on an infinitesimal amount leads to a rule with a formal logarithm. As a consequence the stationary distribution of energy in the thermodynamical limit follows the composed function of the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential with this formal logarithm. In particular, interactions described as solely functions of the relative four-momentum squared lead to kinetic energy distributions of the Tsallis-Pareto (cut power-law) form in the high energy limit.

# Abstract composition rule for relativistic kinetic energy in the thermodynamical limit

## Key claim

Power-law tailed kinetic energy distributions observed in relativistic heavy ion experiments can arise as the thermodynamical limit of an arbitrary composition rule for energy, provided the interaction energy depends on the relative four-momentum squared; the stationary distribution in this limit follows the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential composed with a formal logarithm derived from the composition rule, and in the extreme relativistic limit this yields a Tsallis-Pareto (cut power-law) form.

## Method

The author proves that infinitely repeating an arbitrary pairwise composition rule on infinitesimal amounts leads to an associative asymptotic composition rule expressible via a formal logarithm function L(x). This formal logarithm is constructed from the partial derivative of the composition rule with respect to its second argument evaluated at zero. The stationary distribution in the thermodynamical limit is then given by the exponential of the formal logarithm, f(x) = (1/Z) exp(-β L(x)). The author applies this formalism to derive composition rules from relativistic kinematics by assuming the interaction energy between two particles depends solely on the square of their relative four-momentum, then averaging over isotropic directions.

## Key numbers

- For the Tsallis-Pareto rule h(x,y) = x + y + a x y, the formal logarithm is L(x) = (1/a) ln(1 + a x), leading to the stationary distribution f(E) = (1/Z) (1 + a E)^(-β/a).
- For the Kaniadakis rule based on hyperbolic functions, the formal logarithm is L(x) = (1/κ) arsinh(κ x), giving f_eq(p) = (1/Z) (κ p + √(1+κ²p²))^(-β/κ).
- For the non-associative rule h(x,y) = x + y + a x y/(x+y), the fiducial derivative is h₂'(x,0⁺) = 1 + a, leading to L(x) = x/(1+a) and asymptotic addition φ(x,y) = x+y.
- For Einstein's velocity addition h(x,y) = (x+y)/(1+xy/c²), the fiducial derivative is h₂'(x,0⁺) = 1 - x²/c² and the formal logarithm is L(x) = c atanh(x/c), which equals the rapidity.
- In the extreme relativistic limit (m=0), the fiducial derivative becomes h₂'(x,0) = 1 + 2x U'(0), leading to a Tsallis-Pareto distribution in kinetic energy.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies (Biro, NEBE; Biro, PLAnoneq) which showed that power-law spectra can arise from altered energy composition rules in two-particle collisions. It provides a general mathematical framework showing that associative composition rules are attractors in the thermodynamical limit, unifying several previously proposed non-extensive formalisms including those of Tsallis (GE5) and Kaniadakis (KANIADAKIS). The result enables a thermodynamic interpretation of power-law tailed transverse momentum spectra observed by PHENIX and STAR collaborations in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and suggests that such spectra may be understood as stationary distributions rather than requiring case-by-case non-equilibrium dynamics.
