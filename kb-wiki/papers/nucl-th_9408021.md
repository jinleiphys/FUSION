# Comparison of $K^+$ and $e^-$ Quasielastic Scattering

> We formulate $K^+$-nucleus quasielastic scattering in a manner which closely parallels standard treatments of $e^-$-nucleus quasielastic scattering. For $K^+$ scattering, new responses involving scalar contributions appear in addition to the Coulomb (or longitudinal) and transverse $(e,e')$ responses which are of vector character. We compute these responses using both nuclear matter and finite nucleus versions of the Relativistic Hartree Approximation to Quantum Hadrodynamics including RPA correlations. Overall agreement with measured $(e,e')$ responses and new $K^+$ quasielastic scattering data for $^{40}$Ca at $|\qs|=500$ MeV/c is good. Strong RPA quenching is essential for agreement with the Coulomb response. This quenching is notably less for the $K^+$ cross section even though the new scalar contributions are even more strongly quenched than the vector contributions. We show that this ``differential quenching'' alters sensitive cancellations in the expression for the $K^+$ cross section so that it is reduced much less than the individual responses. We emphasize the role of the purely relativistic distinction between vector and scalar contributions in obtaining an accurate and consistent description of the $(e,e')$ and $K^+$ data within the framework of our nuclear structure model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the strong quenching observed in the Coulomb (longitudinal) response for electron-nucleus quasielastic scattering does **not** appear for the \(K^+\) quasielastic cross section, even though the \(K^+\) process is dominated by a contribution superficially very similar to the Coulomb response; this occurs because "differential quenching" alters sensitive cancellations in the expression for the \(K^+\) cross section, reducing it much less than the individual responses.

## Method

The authors formulate \(K^+\)-nucleus quasielastic scattering in a manner closely paralleling standard treatments of electron-nucleus quasielastic scattering, deriving new hadronic responses involving scalar contributions (\(W^{ss}\) and \(W^{0s}\)) in addition to the Coulomb (longitudinal) and transverse responses familiar from \((e,e')\). They compute these responses using both nuclear matter and finite nucleus versions of the Relativistic Hartree Approximation to Quantum Hadrodynamics, including RPA correlations, and compare with measured \((e,e')\) responses and \(K^+\) quasielastic scattering data for \(^{40}\)Ca at momentum transfer \(|\mathbf{q}| = 500\) MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- \(K^+\) laboratory momentum: 705 MeV/c, with data taken at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 300\) and 500 MeV/c for \(^{12}\)C and \(^{40}\)Ca.
- For \(^{40}\)Ca at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 500\) MeV/c, overall agreement with measured \((e,e')\) responses and new \(K^+\) quasielastic scattering data is good.
- At a laboratory momentum of 700 MeV/c, the proton and \(\pi^+\) have mean free paths of about 2 fm, while the \(K^+\) mean free path is roughly twice that value (approximately 4 fm).
- The \(K^+\)-nucleus data were taken at a laboratory \(K^+\) momentum of 705 MeV/c and \(\theta_{lab} = 43\) degrees for the \(|\mathbf{q}| = 500\) MeV/c kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the study of quasielastic scattering beyond electron probes, motivated by the limitations of \((e,e')\) which only provides two independent observables (Coulomb and transverse responses). It builds on prior hadronic probe experiments such as \((\vec{p},\vec{p}')\) and \((\vec{p},\vec{n})\) measurements (LAMPF and TRIUMF collaborations) and addresses the persistent discrepancy between measured \(K^+\)-nucleus elastic and total cross sections and theoretical multiple scattering model predictions. The results enable complementary extraction of nuclear structure information by combining electron and kaon scattering data within a consistent relativistic nuclear structure framework.
