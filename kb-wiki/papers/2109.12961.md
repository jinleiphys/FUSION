# New insights into the nucleon's electromagnetic structure

> We present a combined analysis of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the space- and timelike regions using dispersion theory. Our framework provides a consistent description of the experimental data over the full range of momentum transfer, in line with the strictures from analyticity and unitarity. The statistical uncertainties of the extracted form factors are estimated using the bootstrap method, while systematic errors are determined from variations of the spectral functions. We also perform a high-precision extraction of the nucleon radii and find good agreement with previous analyses of spacelike data alone. For the proton charge radius, we find $r_E^p = 0.840^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.002}_{-0.002}$~fm, where the first error is statistical and the second one is systematic. The Zemach radius and third moment are in agreement with Lamb shift measurements and hyperfine splittings. The combined data set of space- and timelike data disfavors a zero crossing of $μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ in the spacelike region. Finally, we discuss the status and perspectives of modulus and phase of the form factors in the timelike region in the context of future experiments as well as the onset of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a combined dispersion-theoretical analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in both spacelike and timelike regions, achieving a consistent description across the full momentum transfer range for the first time. The analysis disfavors a zero crossing of the proton form factor ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p in the spacelike region, a possibility that had been suggested by some earlier measurements.

## Method

The authors use dispersion theory to relate the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the spacelike and timelike regions through analytic continuation, exploiting the connection between elastic electron scattering and electron-positron annihilation to nucleon-antinucleon pairs. The spectral functions are constructed from two-pion, kaon-antikaon, and rho-pion continuum contributions, supplemented by narrow and broad effective vector meson poles fitted to 1753 data points from both spacelike and timelike experiments. Statistical uncertainties are estimated using the bootstrap method, while systematic errors are determined from variations in the spectral function parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: r_E^p = 0.840^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.002}_{-0.002} fm (first error statistical, second systematic)
- Proton magnetic radius: r_M^p = 0.849^{+0.003}_{-0.003}{}^{+0.001}_{-0.004} fm
- Neutron magnetic radius: r_M^n = 0.864^{+0.004}_{-0.004}{}^{+0.006}_{-0.001} fm
- Zemach radius: r_z = 1.054^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.000}_{-0.001} fm
- Third Zemach moment: ⟨r^3⟩_(2) = 2.310^{+0.022}_{-0.018}{}^{+0.014}_{-0.015} fm^3
- Neutron charge radius squared (fixed from chiral EFT analysis of electron-deuteron scattering): ⟨r_n^2⟩ = -0.105^{+0.005}_{-0.006} fm^2
- Fit quality: χ^2/d.o.f = 1.223 for full data set, 1.063 for timelike data, 1.297 for spacelike data
- Data included: 1753 total data points spanning |t| from 0.000215 to 20.25 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersion-theoretical analyses of spacelike data only (Lin 2021) by incorporating timelike data from electron-positron annihilation experiments including BaBar, BESIII, and earlier measurements. The extracted proton charge radius of 0.840 fm is consistent with the small radius from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Antognini 2013) and with recent electron scattering determinations, contributing to the resolution of the "proton radius puzzle." The framework enables future comparisons with planned measurements at PANDA at FAIR and provides predictions for the phase of the form factor ratio G_E/G_M in the timelike region, which remains experimentally unconstrained.
