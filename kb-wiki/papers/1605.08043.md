# Analysis strategies for general spin-independent WIMP-nucleus scattering

> We propose a formalism for the analysis of direct-detection dark-matter searches that covers all coherent responses for scalar and vector interactions and incorporates QCD constraints imposed by chiral symmetry, including all one- and two-body WIMP-nucleon interactions up to third order in chiral effective field theory. One of the free parameters in the WIMP-nucleus cross section corresponds to standard spin-independent searches, but in general different combinations of new-physics couplings are probed. We identify the interference with the isovector counterpart of the standard spin-independent response and two-body currents as the dominant corrections to the leading spin-independent structure factor, and discuss the general consequences for the interpretation of direct-detection experiments, including minimal extensions of the standard spin-independent analysis. Fits for all structure factors required for the scattering off xenon targets are provided based on state-of-the-art nuclear shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized formalism for spin-independent (SI) WIMP-nucleus scattering that includes all coherent responses up to third order in chiral effective field theory (ChEFT), but the dominant corrections to the standard SI structure factor come specifically from interference with the isovector counterpart of the standard SI response and from two-body currents, while the hierarchy of nuclear structure factors motivates a minimal extension with only four independent parameters.

## Method

The authors decompose the WIMP-nucleus cross section into generalized structure factors that include the standard isoscalar and isovector one-body responses, two-body meson-exchange currents coupling to the pion via a scalar current and via the trace anomaly of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, momentum-dependent corrections to nucleon form factors, and the quasi-coherent response from the nucleon spin-orbit operator. They calculate all nuclear structure factors for stable xenon isotopes using state-of-the-art nuclear shell-model calculations, and provide fit functions for the one-body and two-body responses.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma-term slope from dispersive analysis: \(\dot\sigma = 0.27(1)\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\)
- The strangeness form factor slope: \(\dot\sigma_s = 0.3(2)\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\)
- The scalar u and d couplings of the nucleon from phenomenology: \(f_u^p = 20.8(1.5) \times 10^{-3}\), \(f_u^n = 18.9(1.4) \times 10^{-3}\), \(f_d^p = 41.1(2.8) \times 10^{-3}\), \(f_d^n = 45.1(2.7) \times 10^{-3}\)
- The scalar s coupling of the nucleon from lattice QCD: \(f_s^N = 43(11) \times 10^{-3}\) (average from Junnarkar and Walker-Loud, 2013)
- The leading-loop ChPT prediction for the slope of the scalar form factor: \(\dot\sigma|_\text{ChPT} = 0.17\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\)
- The leading-loop SU(3) ChPT prediction for the strangeness radius slope: \(\dot\sigma_s|_\text{ChPT} = 0.24\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the standard spin-independent analysis of direct-detection dark-matter experiments, which previously relied on a single structure factor proportional to \(A^2\) and the Helm form factor (Lewin and Smith, 1996), by incorporating chiral EFT constraints from Hoferichter et al. (2015) and the non-relativistic EFT operator classification of Fitzpatrick et al. (2012) and Anand et al. (2013). The formalism enables future global correlated analyses of direct-detection experiments with multiple target nuclei, and provides the necessary structure factors for xenon targets used in experiments such as XENON1T, LZ, and DARWIN.
