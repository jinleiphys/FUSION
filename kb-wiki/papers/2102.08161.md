# Semiclassical propagator approach for emission processes. I. Two body non-relativistic case

> We compare the coupled channels procedure to the semiclassical approach to describe two-body emission processes, in particular $α$-decay, from deformed nuclei within the propagator method. We express the scattering amplitudes in terms of a propagator matrix, describing the effect of the deformed field, multiplied by the ratio between internal wave function components and irregular Coulomb waves. In the spherical case the propagator becomes diagonal and scattering amplitudes acquire the well-known form. We describe a more rigorous formulation of the 3D semiclassical approach, corresponding to deformed potentials, which leads to the exact results and we also compare them with the much simpler expressions given by the Angular Wentzel-Krames-Brillouin (AWKB) and Linearized WKB (LWKB) with its approximation, known as Fröman WKB (FWKB) method. We will show that LWKB approach is closer than AWKB to the exact coupled-channels formalism. An analysis of alpha-emission from ground states of even-even nuclei evidences the important role played by deformation upon the channel decay widths.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Linearized WKB (LWKB) semiclassical approach provides a more accurate approximation to the exact coupled-channels formalism for alpha decay from deformed nuclei than the Angular WKB (AWKB) method, with the caveat that this holds for small to reasonable deformations and when the centrifugal barrier is properly included in the deformed correction term.

## Method

The authors formulated a 3D semiclassical propagator approach for two-body non-relativistic emission from deformed nuclei by expanding the wavefunction in powers of Planck's constant and solving the resulting eikonal and transport equations. They compared two semiclassical approximations (LWKB and AWKB) against the exact coupled-channels (CC) fundamental matrix of solutions, using the propagator matrix formalism to express scattering amplitudes in terms of internal wave function components and irregular Coulomb waves. The comparison was performed for alpha decay from even-even nuclei using a realistic double-folding alpha-daughter potential with quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

- For the system $^{238}U \rightarrow ^{234}Th + \alpha$ with Q = 4.270 MeV and quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.215$, the wave function amplitudes at matching radius $R_m = 12.1$ fm for L=0 were +0.83 (LWKB) and +0.85 (AWKB), compared to +0.83 from Stewart et al.
- The relative error of the LWKB fundamental matrix at the barrier radius was $\sigma_{LWKB}(r_B) \sim 3\%$ for $\beta_2 = 0.4$, while AWKB gave $\sigma_{AWKB}(r_B) \sim 6\%$ for the same deformation.
- The harmonic oscillator frequency parameter of the inverted parabola approximation was $\hbar\omega \sim 9$ MeV, with the kinetic alpha-particle parameter $d_{\alpha} = 0.192$ MeV$^{-1}$fm$^{-2}$.
- The barrier maximum relation was $V_B = 0.94 V_C(r_B)$, where $V_C(r) = 2Z_D/r$ is the external Coulomb potential.

## Context

This work extends the propagator method previously developed for coupled channels by Delion (2010) and provides a rigorous 3D formulation of the semiclassical WKB approach for deformed potentials, building on the earlier intuitive treatment by Froman (1957). The LWKB approach improves upon the Angular WKB method used by Delion (2015) and Stewart (1996) by properly accounting for the angular momentum dependence of the deformed correction term. The results enable systematic analysis of alpha decay widths from deformed even-even nuclei and can be extended to other two-body emission processes such as proton and heavy cluster decays.
