# Casimir Energies in the Light of Renormalizable Quantum Field Theories

> Effective hadron models commonly require the computation of functional determinants. In the static case these are one--loop vacuum polarization energies, known as Casimir energies. In this talk I will present general methods to efficiently compute renormalized one--loop vacuum polarization energies and energy densities and apply these methods to construct soliton solutions within a variational approach. This calculational method is particularly useful to study singular limits that emerge in the discussion of the {\it classical} Casimir problem which is usually posed as the response of a fluctuating quantum field to externally imposed boundary conditions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Casimir energies computed by imposing boundary conditions *a priori* are not internally consistent and that the only physically meaningful way to treat divergences in these energies is through renormalization in continuum quantum field theory, where the boundary conditions are realized as singular limits of smooth background potentials.

## Method

The author presents a method to compute renormalized one-loop vacuum polarization energies and energy densities by expressing the matrix element of the energy density operator in terms of a Green's function, then deforming momentum integrals along a cut on the positive imaginary axis. Leading Born approximations to the Green's function are subtracted to regulate ultraviolet divergences and later added back as Feynman diagrams, which are regularized and renormalized using ordinary perturbation theory. The method is applied to construct soliton solutions within a variational approach in a D=1+1 chiral model and to study singular delta-function backgrounds that mimic Dirichlet boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- For two Dirichlet points in one space dimension with a massive scalar field, the force in the massless limit is \(\widetilde{F}(a)=-\pi/96a^2\), obtained from the boundary condition calculation.
- The renormalized Casimir energy for two delta-function backgrounds in one dimension diverges like \(\lambda\log\lambda\) as \(\lambda\to\infty\) (the Dirichlet limit).
- For a scalar field in two space dimensions constrained to vanish on a circle of radius \(a\), the renormalized two-point Feynman diagram in the sharp delta-function limit contributes \(-\frac{\lambda^2 a^2}{8}\int_0^M dp\,J_0^2(ap)\,\arctan(p/2m)\), which diverges logarithmically as \(M\to\infty\).
- For a delta-function background in three space dimensions, the renormalized two-point function is proportional to \(\lambda^2 a^4\int_0^M dp\,f(p)\) with \(f(p)=p^2 j_0^2(pa)\ln p\) for large \(p\), diverging like \(M\ln(M)\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Graham, Jaffe, and collaborators (2002) for computing renormalized Casimir energies using spectral methods and applies it to both smooth soliton backgrounds and singular delta-function potentials. The method enables the study of soliton stabilization by quantum corrections in models like the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu, 1961; Ebert and Reinhardt, 1986) and resolves inconsistencies in the standard boundary-condition approach to the Casimir problem (Milton, 2001). The results show that while Casimir forces and energy densities away from boundaries remain finite in the Dirichlet limit, the total Casimir energy diverges due to singular contributions at the boundary, contradicting claims by Milton (2001) about higher-dimensional cases.
