# Jet softening and decollimation in heavy-ion collisions

> Two suggested models of jet fragmentation in a quark-gluon plasma have been tested, combined and further developed. This has been done by generating hard processes in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ GeV in PYTHIA 8 with a modified parton shower algorithm. Subsequently, a jet analysis of the final state hadrons have been performed with the FastJet recombination package. The results have been compared to unmodified jets in proton-proton collisions at the same collision energy, and to experimental data obtained by ATLAS. It has been shown that modifications of the parton splitting kernels alone is an insufficient modification to reproduce experimental data on dijet energy asymmetry and azimuthal decorrelation. Additional jet decollimation qualitatively reproduces the main features of the data provided certain variables are chosen aptly. We also study other jet characteristics with the model, such as the jet substructure, that may be observable experimentally in a near future.

## Key claim

Modifications of the parton splitting kernels alone are insufficient to reproduce experimental data on dijet energy asymmetry and azimuthal decorrelation; additional jet decollimation qualitatively reproduces the main features of the data provided certain variables are chosen aptly.

## Method

Hard processes in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV were generated using PYTHIA 8 with a modified parton shower algorithm. Final-state hadrons were analyzed with the FastJet recombination package, and results were compared to unmodified proton-proton jets and to experimental data from ATLAS. Two models of jet fragmentation in a quark-gluon plasma were tested, combined, and further developed.

## Key numbers

- Proton-proton collisions were generated at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV with a hard transverse momentum scale \(\hat{p}_t=70\) GeV/c.
- A minimum cut of 20 GeV was placed on all measured jets to exclude fake jets.
- The hump-backed plateau in the \(\xi\) distribution shows a mean of \(\sim 4.3\), corresponding to a momentum fraction of \(\sim 1.4\%\).
- Approximately 75% of the jet energy is concentrated in the innermost half of the jet (for \(R=0.7\)).
- For the hardest jet, \(E_{T1}>100\) GeV and for the second jet \(E_{T2}>25\) GeV, with both jets at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta| < 1\)).
- The cone size was limited to \(0.4 < R < 0.7\) for further reference.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, where experiments at RHIC established strong suppression of high-\(p_t\) hadrons (PHENIX, STAR) and medium-induced gluon radiation was suggested as the underlying mechanism (Baier). The model builds on the concept that multiple small-angle scatterings cause Brownian motion of parton shower components (Casalderrey), and the results enable future experimental observation of jet substructure characteristics in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
