# Renormalization group analysis of nuclear current operators

> We review our study of the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) analysis for nuclear current operators. We apply WRG method to axial-current operators derived from various approaches and obtain the unique effective low-energy operator.

# Renormalization group analysis of nuclear current operators

## Key claim
The Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) analysis, when applied to axial-current operators from different approaches (models and chiral effective field theory with pions), yields a unique effective low-energy operator after reducing the model space to a sufficiently low cutoff, but only at a resolution where the one-pion range mechanism itself ceases to be model dependent.

## Method
The authors derive a WRG equation for nuclear current operators and apply it to reduce the model space of exchange axial-current operators relevant to solar neutrino-induced deuteron breakup. They evolve bare two-body operators from various models and from chiral effective field theory with pions (EFT(π)) down to low-momentum cutoffs, and then simulate the resulting effective operator using a parameterization based on EFT without explicit pions (EFT(⧸π)).

## Key numbers
- Cutoff Λ = 200 MeV: effective two-body current operators from different approaches still show model dependence (left panel of Fig. 1).
- Cutoff Λ = 70 MeV: model dependence among the effective currents is no longer visible, yielding a unique effective operator (right panel of Fig. 1).
- A two-parameter fit using the EFT(⧸π)-based parameterization yields an almost perfect simulation of the effective two-body current at Λ = 70 MeV, except for "jump-up" structures from the bare one-body current contribution.

## Context
This work extends the WRG approach previously applied to nuclear potentials (Ref. 1 in the paper), where all potentials converge to a single effective low-momentum potential. It addresses the observation that different current operators give essentially the same reaction rates for low-energy reactions such as solar-neutrino reactions on the deuteron (Ref. 2 in the paper). The result enables a systematic derivation of low-energy current operators from either phenomenological models or chiral EFT with pions, parameterized in the simpler EFT(⧸π) framework.
