# Di-electron production from vector mesons with medium modifications in heavy ion collisions

> We reproduce the di-electron spectra in the low and intermediate mass regions in most central Au+Au collisions by the STAR Collaboration incorporation of the STAR detector acceptance. We also compare our results with PHENIX data constrained by the PHENIX acceptance. We include the medium modifications of vector mesons from scatterings of vector mesons by mesons and baryons in the thermal medium. The freezeout contributions from vector mesons are also taken into account. The space-time evolution is described by a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model. The backgrounds from semi-leptonic decays of charm hadrons are simulated by the PYTHIA event generator and corrected by the nuclear modification factor of electrons from charm decays. It is difficult to extract the thermal contributions from those from charm decays in the invariant mass spectra alone and in the current detector acceptances. Other observables such as transverse momenta and collective flows may provide additional tools to tag these sources.

## Key claim

The authors reproduce the di-electron spectra in low and intermediate mass regions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but conclude that with current detector acceptances and parameter sets, it is impossible to extract thermal dilepton sources from the invariant mass spectra alone because backgrounds from charm hadron decays dominate in both regions.

## Method

The space-time evolution of the fireball is described by a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with two equations of state (S95P-CE and S95P-PCE). Medium modifications of vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) are included via hadronic many-body effective theory, with rho meson spectral functions broadened by scattering with mesons and baryons in the thermal medium. Backgrounds from semi-leptonic decays of charm hadrons (D0, D+, Ds, Lambda_c) are simulated using the PYTHIA event generator and corrected by the nuclear modification factor of electrons from charm decays. The results are compared with STAR and PHENIX data incorporating each detector's acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions: equilibration time tau_0 = 0.4 fm, initial energy density e_0 = 45 GeV/fm^3 (initial temperature T_0 = 395 MeV) for most central collisions with impact parameter b = 2.4 fm
- Kinetic freeze-out temperatures: T_f = 136 MeV for S95P-CE (set S1); T_f = 136 MeV and T_f = 106 MeV for S95P-PCE (sets S2 and S3)
- Rapidity densities for set S3: dN_pi+/dy = 261.7, dN_p/dy = 24.6, rho/pi ratio = 5.50 x 10^-2, omega/pi ratio = 9.82 x 10^-2, phi/pi ratio = 3.13 x 10^-2
- Collision width for phi meson in medium: Gamma_phi_coll = (22 MeV) x (T/T_0)^6 with T_0 = 150 MeV
- Cross section of c-cbar pairs from PYTHIA: sigma_c-cbar = 0.5 mb
- Number of binary collisions for most central Au+Au: N_coll = 950
- Integrated di-electron yield from heavy-flavor decays in PHENIX acceptance in mass range [1.1, 2.5] GeV/c^2: (4.21 +/- 0.28 +/- 1.02) x 10^-8 per event

## Context

This work extends the approach used for NA60 di-muon data at SPS energies (vanHees 2006, Ruppert 2007) to RHIC energies, where charm backgrounds become significant. The authors use a hydrodynamic model similar to Deng (2010) and compare with preliminary STAR data (Zhao 2011) and PHENIX data (Adare 2009). The paper shows that unlike at SPS energies, the thermal QGP signal in the intermediate mass region at RHIC is overwhelmed by correlated charm decays, motivating the need for additional observables such as transverse momentum spectra and collective flows to disentangle these sources.
