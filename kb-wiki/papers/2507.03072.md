# Soft background fields at next-to-leading power in transverse momentum dependent SIDIS with jets

> We present a factorization formula for the $e + h \rightarrow e + \text{jet} + X$ cross section at small transverse momenta up to next-to-leading power (NLP), derived using the background field method (BFM) with explicit inclusion of soft modes. We discuss the relation between soft modes and overlap subtractions at NLP, showing that the inclusion of soft modes enables a definition of twist-3 operators with properly subtracted rapidity and endpoint divergences. We also derive the effective current operator at NLP and identify additional structures compared to previous approaches based on soft-collinear effective theory and the BFM without soft modes. Nevertheless, for the hadronic tensor, we find agreement at the perturbative order that we are working at, and identify a condition that needs to be satisfied for this agreement to extend to higher orders. Furthermore, we construct the most general form factors for this process, taking into account the polarization of the initial states. These involve perturbatively-calculable jet functions, opening a path to precise determinations of twist-3 hadronic distributions. Finally, our formalism is illustrated with phenomenological results for a specific azimuthal asymmetry, whose leading-logarithmic contribution depends solely on a twist-3 hadronic distribution and a twist-2 jet function.

## Key claim

The paper derives a factorization formula for the $e + h \rightarrow e + \text{jet} + X$ cross section at small transverse momenta up to next-to-leading power (NLP) using a modified background field method that explicitly includes soft modes, finding agreement with previous SCET-based approaches at the perturbative order considered but identifying a condition that must hold for this agreement to extend to higher orders.

## Method

The authors introduce a third approach to TMD factorization at NLP, using a background field method (BFM) with explicit inclusion of soft background fields alongside collinear and anti-collinear fields, then integrate out hard and hard-collinear modes to derive an effective current operator. They match the resulting operator onto gauge-invariant SCET building blocks, use symmetry arguments to generalize to all orders in perturbation theory, and subtract overlap between soft and collinear sectors to define twist-3 operators free of rapidity and endpoint divergences. The formalism is applied to jet production in SIDIS, deriving factorized cross sections and form factors for the $e+H\rightarrow e+\text{jet}+X$ process.

## Key numbers

- The power counting parameter $\lambda$ is defined as $\lambda \sim q_T/Q$, where $q_T$ is the transverse momentum of the intermediate photon and $Q$ is the center-of-mass energy
- Collinear momentum scaling: $n$-collinear $\sim (\lambda^2, 1, \lambda)$, $\bar{n}$-collinear $\sim (1, \lambda^2, \lambda)$, soft $\sim (\lambda, \lambda, \lambda)$
- Collinear fermion background fields scale as $\xi \sim \lambda$ and $\eta \sim \lambda^2$ for both $n$ and $\bar{n}$ sectors
- Soft fermion background field scales as $\psi \sim \lambda^{3/2}$
- The effective current operator is derived to $\mathcal{O}(g^2, \lambda^{7/2})$

## Context

This work bridges two existing approaches to TMD factorization at NLP: the background field method of Vladimirov (2021) which does not include soft modes as separate degrees of freedom, and the SCET-based approach of Ebert (2021) which does include a soft sector. The paper extends TMD factorization to jet production in SIDIS at NLP, enabling precise determinations of twist-3 hadronic distributions from azimuthal and spin asymmetry observables at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). The formalism is illustrated with phenomenological predictions for a $\sin\phi_J$ asymmetry whose leading-logarithmic contribution depends solely on a twist-3 hadronic distribution and a twist-2 jet function.
