# Pre-equilibrium Longitudinal Flow in the IP-Glasma Framework for Pb+Pb Collisions at the LHC

> In this work, we debut a new implementation of IP-Glasma and quantify the pre-equilibrium longitudinal flow in the IP-Glasma framework. The saturation physics based IP-Glasma model naturally provides a non-zero initial longitudinal flow through its pre-equilibrium Yang-Mills evolution. A hybrid IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD frame- work is employed to test this new implementation against experimental data and to make further predictions about hadronic flow observables in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Finally, the non-zero pre-equilibrium longitudinal flow of the IP-Glasma model is quantified, and its origin is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma model naturally generates a non-zero longitudinal flow velocity \(u^{\eta}\) during its pre-equilibrium Yang-Mills evolution, even in the boost-invariant case, contradicting the common assumption of vanishing initial longitudinal flow in hydrodynamic simulations. The authors quantify that this longitudinal flow is of the same order of magnitude as the transverse flow, with \(\langle \tau u^{\eta} \rangle \approx 0.5\langle u^{\perp}\rangle\).

## Method

The authors implemented a new numerical version of the IP-Glasma model, which uses the IP-Sat model to determine saturation scales and the McLerran-Venugopalan model for color charge fluctuations, then solves classical Yang-Mills equations on a lattice up to a switching time of \(\tau_{sw} = 0.4\) fm. The resulting stress-energy tensor is diagonalized to extract the local flow velocity and energy density, which are then fed into the MUSIC 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code followed by the UrQMD hadronic cascade, using fixed parameters from prior work (Shen et al., 2015). The model was tested against experimental data for Pb+Pb collisions at both 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

* The switching time from Yang-Mills evolution to hydrodynamics was \(\tau_{sw} = 0.4\) fm.
* The relationship between initial energy density and final charged hadron multiplicity was fitted as \(\frac{dN_\mathrm{ch}}{d\eta}\big\vert_{\vert \eta \vert < 0.5}=0.839\left( \frac{dE}{\tau d\eta_s}\big\vert_{\tau=0.4 \, {\rm fm}} \right)^{0.833}\).
* The study used 1500 events per 10% centrality class at each collision energy (2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV).
* The shear viscosity was set to a constant \(\eta/s = 0.095\) and the switching temperature to hadronic cascade was \(T_{sw}=145\) MeV.
* The RMS longitudinal flow was found to be \(\langle \tau u^{\eta} \rangle \approx 0.5\langle u^{\perp}\rangle\), based on 100 events at 2.76 TeV in the 0-5% centrality bin.

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke et al., 2012) by providing a new numerical implementation and explicitly quantifying the pre-equilibrium longitudinal flow that had been neglected in previous 2+1D hydrodynamic simulations. The hybrid IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework reproduces experimental flow observables at both LHC energies, and the authors suggest that retaining the non-zero initial longitudinal flow will be important for future 3+1D simulations, particularly for observables sensitive to early times such as thermal photon production.
