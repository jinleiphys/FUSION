# A link of information entropy and kinetic energy for quantum many-body systems

> A direct connection of information entropy $S$ and kinetic energy $T$ is obtained for nuclei and atomic clusters, which establishes $T$ as a measure of the information in a distribution. It is conjectured that this is a universal property for fermionic many-body systems. We also check rigorous inequalities previously found to hold between S and T for atoms and verify that they hold for nuclei and atomic clusters as well. These inequalities give a relationship of Shannon's information entropy in position-space with an experimental quantity i.e. the rms radius of nuclei and clusters.

# A link of information entropy and kinetic energy for quantum many-body systems

## Key claim
The paper establishes a direct empirical connection between Shannon information entropy \(S\) and total kinetic energy \(T\) for the total density distributions of nuclei and atomic clusters, and conjectures that this may be a universal property of fermionic many-body systems. The claim is hedged by the fact that the relationship is demonstrated only for these two specific systems and the authors explicitly state that further testing on other systems (e.g., atoms) is needed before universality can be confirmed.

## Method
The authors employed Hartree-Fock wave functions with the SKIII interaction for nuclei and a Woods-Saxon potential for neutral sodium atomic clusters to compute total density distributions. They calculated Shannon information entropy in position-space \(S_r\) and momentum-space \(S_k\) for these systems, then fitted the total entropy \(S = S_r + S_k\) as a function of kinetic energy \(T\) to the functional form \(S = \alpha T + \beta T \ln(\gamma T)\). They also verified that previously derived rigorous inequalities relating \(S_r\), \(S_k\), and \(S\) to \(T\) and the rms radius, established for atoms by Gadre (1987), hold for nuclei and atomic clusters as well.

## Key numbers
- For nuclei, the fitted parameters for the relation \(S = \alpha T + \beta T \ln(\gamma T)\) are: \(\alpha = 0.4461\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), \(\beta = -0.0586\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 1\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\).
- For atomic clusters, the fitted parameters are: \(\alpha = 3.4722\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}\), \(\beta = -0.5970\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 1\ \mathrm{eV}^{-1}\).
- The entropic uncertainty relation lower bound for three-dimensional systems is \(S = S_r + S_k \ge 3(1+\ln\pi) \cong 6.434\) (for densities normalized to one, with \(\hbar = 1\)).
- For systems normalized to particle number \(N\), the lower bound becomes \(S \ge 3N(1+\ln\pi) - 2N\ln N\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous finding (Massen and Panos, 1998) that Shannon entropy for total density distributions follows \(S = a + b\ln N\) for nuclei, atoms, and atomic clusters, and their later connection of single-particle entropy to single-particle energy (Panos et al., 2000). It also verifies for nuclei and clusters the rigorous inequalities between entropy and kinetic energy originally derived for atoms by Gadre (1987). The paper enables future work testing whether the \(S\)-\(T\) link holds for other fermionic systems such as atoms, and suggests that kinetic energy can be interpreted as a measure of information content in a quantum many-body system, consistent with the information-theoretic view of kinetic energy as a randomizing force discussed by Sears (1980).
