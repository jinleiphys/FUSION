# Two-Boson Exchange Physics: A Brief Review

> Current status of the two-boson exchange contributions to elastic electron-proton scattering, both for parity conserving and parity-violating, is briefly reviewed. How the discrepancy in the extraction of elastic nucleon form factors between unpolarized Rosenbluth and polarization transfer experiments can be understood, in large part, by the two-photon exchange corrections is discussed. We also illustrate how the measurement of the ratio between positron-proton and electron-proton scattering can be used to differentiate different models of two-photon exchange. For the parity-violating electron-proton scattering, the interest is on how the two-boson exchange (TBE), γZ-exchange in particular, could affect the extraction of the long-sought strangeness form factors. Various calculations all indicate that the magnitudes of effect of TBE on the extraction of strangeness form factors is small, though can be large percentage-wise in certain kinematics.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections can account for more than half of the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer extractions of the proton form factor ratio, but theoretical models still suffer from uncertainties such as the on-shell approximation for vertices in hadronic calculations and extrapolations beyond the region of validity in partonic calculations.

## Method

The paper reviews hadronic model calculations (Blunden et al., 2003) that treat TPE effects rigorously using FeynCalc and LoopTools, including finite proton size and elastic nucleon intermediate states, with later extensions to include the Delta(1232) resonance. It also reviews partonic calculations (Chen et al., 2004) that use generalized parton distributions to evaluate the handbag diagram where the incident electron scatters from quarks in the proton. For parity-violating scattering, the review covers hadronic model calculations of gamma-Z exchange interference with the one-photon-exchange diagram, including nucleon and Delta intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The Rosenbluth separation experiments over the last half century indicate that R = mu_p G_E/G_M is approximately 1 for Q^2 less than 6 GeV^2
- A measurement of the positron-proton to electron-proton cross section ratio at Q^2 = 1.6 GeV^2 and epsilon = 0.4 gives R^(+/-) = 1.056 +/- 0.011 (Nikolenko et al., 2010)
- The parity-violating asymmetry A_PV in elastic electron-proton scattering ranges from 0.1 to 100 ppm
- The OLYMPUS experiment plans to measure the e^+p/e^-p ratio for 0.5 less than Q^2 less than 2.2 GeV^2 with epsilon greater than 0.2

## Context

This review addresses the discrepancy between unpolarized Rosenbluth separation experiments and polarization transfer experiments (Jones et al., 2000) in extracting the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio, which prompted intensive theoretical efforts to evaluate TPE corrections. The work extends earlier radiative correction treatments by Mo and Tsai (1969) that used the soft-photon approximation, and builds on the hadronic model framework of Blunden et al. (2003) and partonic calculations of Chen et al. (2004). For parity-violating scattering, it updates the leading-order radiative corrections of Marciano and Sirlin (1983) that were evaluated at zero momentum transfer, showing that more rigorous calculations are needed for the extraction of strangeness form factors from experiments such as SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, and G0.
