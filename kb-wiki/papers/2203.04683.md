# Signatures of shape phase transitions in krypton isotopes based on relativistic energy density functionals

> Spectroscopic properties that characterize the shape phase transitions in krypton isotopes with the mass $A\approx80$ region are investigated within the framework of the nuclear density functional theory. Triaxial quadrupole constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations that employ relativistic energy density functionals and a pairing interaction are carried out for the even-even nuclei $^{76-86}$Kr. The spectroscopic properties are computed by solving the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, with the ingredients, i.e., the deformation-dependent moments of inertia and mass parameters, and the collective potential, determined by using the SCMF solutions as microscopic inputs. Systematic behaviors of the SCMF potential energy surfaces, the corresponding low-energy spectra, electric quadrupole and monopole transition probabilities, and the fluctuations in the triaxial quadrupole deformations indicate evolution of the underlying nuclear structure as functions of the neutron number, that is characterized by a considerable degree of shape mixing. A special attention is paid to the transitional nucleus $^{82}$Kr, which has been recently identified experimentally as an empirical realization of the E(5) critical-point symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the even-even krypton isotopes $^{76-86}$Kr exhibit shape phase transitions characterized by considerable shape mixing, with the transitional nucleus $^{82}$Kr showing signatures consistent with the E(5) critical-point symmetry, though the agreement with E(5) predictions is approximate rather than exact and depends on which observable is considered.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial quadrupole constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework using two relativistic energy density functionals (DD-ME2 and DD-PC1) and a separable pairing force of finite range. They then solved the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, with deformation-dependent moments of inertia, mass parameters, and collective potential determined from the self-consistent mean-field solutions, to compute excitation spectra, electric quadrupole and monopole transition probabilities, and deformation fluctuations for the even-even nuclei $^{76-86}$Kr.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength used was $V_0=728$ MeV fm$^3$ with parameter $a=0.644$ fm for both proton and neutron pairings, based on the D1S force parametrization
- For $^{82}$Kr, the calculated energy ratio $R_{4/2}=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ was close to the E(5) limit of 2.20
- For $^{82}$Kr, the calculated ratio $R_{0/0}=E(0^+_2)/E(0^+_3)$ was 0.704 (DD-ME2) or 0.721 (DD-PC1), compared to the E(5) limit of 0.84 and the experimental value of 0.9
- For $^{82}$Kr, the calculated ratio $R_{2/2}=E(2^+_2)/E(2^+_1)$ was 2.00 (DD-ME2) or 1.97 (DD-PC1), compared to the experimental value of 1.90 and the E(5) limit of 2.20
- The calculated $\rho^2(E0;0^+_2\to0^+_1)$ values for nuclei with $N=40-44$ were considerably larger than experimental values from Kibedi (2005), with a sudden decrease from $N=44$ to 46

## Context

This work extends the study of shape phase transitions in the $A\approx80$ mass region, following the experimental identification of $^{82}$Kr as a candidate E(5) critical-point symmetry nucleus by Rajbanshi (2021). The relativistic density functional approach used here builds on previous theoretical investigations of Kr isotopes using the RHB+QCH method (Niksic 2009, Li 2010, Fu 2013) and earlier studies of neutron-deficient Kr isotopes around the $N=40$ sub-shell gap (Bender 2006, Girod 2009, Nomura 2017). The results enable comparison with the E(5) critical-point symmetry predictions of Iachello (2000) and the dynamical symmetries of the interacting boson model, and may inform calculations of nuclear matrix elements for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{82}$Se.
