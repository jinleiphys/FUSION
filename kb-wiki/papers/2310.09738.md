# Hadron-quark transition and chiral symmetry restoration at high density

> A simple phenomenological hybrid hadron-quark model with effective volume effects of baryons and chiral dynamics is investigated. The hybrid EoS naturally connects the low density baryonic matter with the high density quark matter. In the intermediate region, the phase which can not be regarded as pure hadron matter or pure quark matter appears. In this model, there is a possibility that the abrupt first -order like transition to pure quark matter induces the strong chiral symmetry restoration and the speed of sound has a large peak at considerable large density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that an abrupt first-order-like hadron-to-quark transition, driven by a rapid change in the effective baryon volume, can induce a strong chiral symmetry restoration and produce a large peak in the speed of sound at high baryon density, but this outcome depends on the free parameter \(a\) that controls how abruptly the baryon volume approaches the quark-based value.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state (EoS) by combining a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with excluded volume effects (EVE) and a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter. The baryon volume \(v_{\rm B}\) is interpolated between a constant vacuum value \(v_{\rm B0}\) and \(3/n_{\rm q}\) (where \(n_{\rm q}\) is the quark number density from the NJL model) using a smooth function with a free parameter \(a\); the baryon density \(n_{\rm B}\) is then derived from the EVE relation and the thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to the chiral condensates \(\sigma_f\) to determine the equilibrium state.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum baryon volume is set to \(v_{\rm B0} = 4\pi r_{\rm B0}^3/3\) with nucleon radius \(r_{\rm B0} = 0.8\) fm.
* The NJL model parameters are: current quark masses \(m_{u,d}=5.5\) MeV, \(m_s=140.7\) MeV, coupling \(G_{\rm s}\Lambda^2=1.835\), \(G_{\rm d}\Lambda^5=12.36\), and cutoff \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV.
* For the crossover setup (\(a=0.1\)), the intermediate phase exists in the energy density range \(\varepsilon = 0.2\) to \(2.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
* For the first-order-like setup (\(a=0.8\)), the abrupt transition to pure quark matter occurs at quark chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm q} = 0.52\) GeV.
* The speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) shows a double peak structure in the crossover setup, with the left peak induced by excluded volume effects and the right peak related to the hadron-quark transition.

## Context

This work extends hybrid hadron-quark models that use excluded volume effects, such as those by Cleymans (1986) and Rischke (1991), and contrasts with approaches that employ explicit interpolation functions for the EoS, e.g., Masuda (2012). It also relates to studies of the trace anomaly and speed of sound in dense matter by Fujimoto (2022) and Kojo (2020). The model enables future investigations of the relation between macroscopic hybrid descriptions and microscopic quarkyonic matter models, such as that of Jeong (2019).
