# Shell model results for $T=1$ and $T=0$ bands in $^{66}$As

> Results of a comprehensive shell model (SM) analyses, within the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space, of the recently available experimental data [P. Ruotsalainen et al., Phy. Rec. C {88}, 024320 (2013)] with four $T=0$ bands and one $T=1$ band in the odd-odd $N=Z$ nucleus $^{66}$As are presented. The calculations are performed using jj44b effective interaction developed recently by B.A. Brown and A.F. Lisetskiy for this model space. For the lowest two $T=0$ bands and the $T=1$ band, the results are in reasonable agreement with experimental data and deformed shell model is used to identify their intrinsic structure. For the $T=1$ band, structural change at $8^+$ is predicted. For the third $9^+$ band with $T=0$, the shell model $B(E2)$ values and quadrupole moments (in addition to energies) are consistent with the interpretation in terms of aligned isoscalar $np$ pair in $g_{9/2}$ orbit coupled to the $^{64}$Ge ground band. Similarly, the $9^+$ level of band 4 and a close lying $5^+$ level are found to be isomeric states in the analysis. Finally, energies of the band 5 members calculated using shell model with both positive and negative parity show that the observed levels are most likely negative parity levels. The SM results with jj44b are also compared with the results obtained using JUN45 interaction.

## Key claim

The shell model calculations using the jj44b interaction provide a reasonable description of the observed T=0 and T=1 bands in 66As, but the agreement is not as good as previously achieved for 62Ga, and the jj44b interaction is not able to give as good a description of the spectroscopy as seen in the lighter N=Z nucleus.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed within the full f5/2pg9/2 model space using the jj44b effective interaction, with 56Ni as the inert core. Deformed shell model (DSM) calculations were also performed using the same single-particle orbitals and two-body matrix elements, solving the Hartree-Fock single-particle equation self-consistently assuming axial symmetry, followed by angular momentum projection, isospin projection, and band mixing calculations. The shell model results with jj44b were compared with results obtained using the JUN45 interaction and with experimental data from Ruotsalainen et al. (2013).

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of 66As by Honma et al. using JUN45 and Hasegawa et al. using an extended pairing plus quadrupole interaction, and follows the authors' successful application of the jj44b interaction to 62Ga (Srivastava et al., 2016). The results enable future experimental tests of predicted B(E2) values and quadrupole moments, particularly for band 4 which shows evidence for a spin-aligned isoscalar np pair in the g9/2 orbit coupled to a 64Ge core, similar to the structure reported in 92Pd by Cederwall et al. The paper also identifies the need for better effective interactions for describing N=Z nuclei starting from 66As.
