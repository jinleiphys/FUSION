# Pairing dynamics in low energy nuclear collisions

> Superfluidity is a generic feature of various quantum systems at low temperatures and it is in particular important for the description of dynamics of low energy nuclear reactions. The time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) is, to date, the only microscopic method which takes into account in a consistent way far from equilibrium dynamics of pairing field and single-particle degrees of freedom. The local version of TDDFT, so called TDSLDA, is particularly useful for the description of nuclear reactions and is well suited for leadership class computers of hybrid (CPU+GPU) architecture. The preliminary results obtained for collisions involving both medium-mass and heavy nuclei at the energies around the Coulomb barrier are presented.

# Pairing dynamics in low energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) reveals that a nonzero phase difference in the pairing field between colliding superfluid nuclei creates a solitonic excitation that raises the fusion barrier, but the magnitude of this barrier enhancement depends on the chosen energy density functional; the SLy4 functional produces a weaker enhancement than previously reported with the Fayans functional.

## Method
The authors performed TDSLDA simulations of head-on collisions for symmetric systems (⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr) and asymmetric systems (⁴⁸Ca+²⁵²Cf and ⁵⁰Ti+²⁵²Cf) at energies around the Coulomb barrier. Ground states were prepared using the conjugate-orthogonal conjugate-gradient method on a grid with 1.25 fm spacing in a box of 80 × 25 × 25 fm³, then evolved using the full Skyrme functional SLy4 including the spin-orbit term. The evolution time for checking whether nuclei split was approximately 10⁴ fm/c.

## Key numbers
- For ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr collisions, creating a π phase difference between the two superfluid nuclei increased the fusion threshold by 5 MeV compared to no phase difference; no increase was found for ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr (which has zero pairing gap).
- The ⁴⁸Ca+²⁵²Cf collisions were studied in the center-of-mass energy range 200–300 MeV; the ⁵⁰Ti+²⁵²Cf collisions were studied in the range 220–300 MeV.
- Contact times for TDSLDA simulations were approximately 2–3 times longer than comparable TDHF calculations.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+²⁵²Cf, the kinetic energy carried away by the heavy fragment was 5–10 MeV smaller than for ⁵⁰Ti+²⁵²Cf.
- The initial separation of nuclei in the simulation box was 40 fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier TDSLDA studies of solitonic excitations in nuclear collisions reported in PhysRevLett.119.042501 (2017), which used the Fayans functional without spin-orbit coupling. The present results with the SLy4 functional show a weaker barrier enhancement, consistent with empirical analysis from PhysRevC.97.044611 (2018). The asymmetric collisions investigate quasi-fission relevant to superheavy element formation, building on prior TDSLDA fission studies by Stetcu (2011, 2015), Bulgac (2016), and Grineviciute (2017). The observation of pairing transfer to an initially-normal projectile resembles the "pairing reentrance" effect predicted in hot rotating nuclei by Kammuri and by Belyaev, Fomenko, and Vdovin.
