# Chiral hydrodynamics of expanding systems

> We obtain equations of motion for the boost-invariant expansion of a system of chiral particles. Our analysis is based on the Boltzmann equation for left- and right-handed massless particles in the relaxation time approximation. We assume Bjorken symmetry, but allow for parity breaking. We generalize the relaxation time approximation to take into account the so-called side-jump effect, but we show that the ensuing correction happens to vanish for Bjorken symmetry. After expressing the conserved currents in terms of chiral moments, we derive equations of motion for these moments from the Boltzmann equation. After a suitable truncation, these equations allow us to study the transition from the early-time collisionless regime to the hydrodynamic regime at late time, where the parity-violating chiral moments decay exponentially. The truncation that we use for the parity-violating moments is shown to be identical to Israel-Stewart's 14-moment approximation. Our final set of equations can be used to calculate the energy-momentum tensor, vector-, and axial-vector currents with chiral degrees of freedom for possible applications in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed equations of motion for chiral hydrodynamics under Bjorken symmetry that are valid both in the early-time collisionless regime and the late-time hydrodynamic regime, but the authors show that the side-jump correction to the relaxation time approximation vanishes for this specific symmetry, meaning the claimed generality of the covariant RTA is not actually tested in the Bjorken flow scenario studied.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation for left- and right-handed massless particles in the relaxation time approximation, generalized to preserve covariance under Lorentz transformations by fixing the fluid rest frame as the reference frame. They impose Bjorken symmetry (boost invariance along the collision axis and translational invariance in the transverse plane) while allowing parity breaking, then express the conserved currents (vector current, axial-vector current, and energy-momentum tensor) in terms of chiral moments defined by spherical harmonic integrals of the distribution function. Equations of motion for these moments are derived from the Boltzmann equation, and a truncation scheme is applied to close the system, which is shown to be equivalent to the Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation for the parity-violating moments.

## Key numbers

* The vector and axial-vector currents have spatial components given by \(J_V^x = -\sum_{\lambda=\pm1} \text{Re} \mathcal{J}^\lambda_{11} = J_V^y\) and \(J_A^x = -\sum_{\lambda=\pm1} \lambda \text{Re} \mathcal{J}^\lambda_{11} = J_A^y\), with \(J_V^z = J_A^z = 0\).
* The off-diagonal energy-momentum tensor component is \(T^{xz} = -\frac{1}{15}\frac{\hbar}{\tau}\sum_{\lambda=\pm1} \lambda \text{Re} (4\mathcal{J}^\lambda_{11} + \mathcal{J}^\lambda_{31}) = -T^{yz}\).
* The coefficients in the moment equations satisfy \(a_{n\ell} + b_{n\ell} + c_{n\ell} = 1 + \ell\) for \(\mathcal{J}\) moments and \(\bar{a}_{n\ell} + \bar{b}_{n\ell} + \bar{c}_{n\ell} = 2 + \ell\) for \(\mathcal{T}\) moments.
* In the collisionless limit, moments with \(\ell=0\) diverge as \(\tau_0/\tau\) for \(\mathcal{J}\) and as \((\tau_0/\tau)^2\) for \(\mathcal{T}\) when \(\tau \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Blaizot (2021) on moment methods for Bjorken flow to include chiral degrees of freedom and parity breaking, which was not considered in that prior work. The results enable calculation of the energy-momentum tensor, vector current, and axial-vector current for systems with chiral imbalance in heavy-ion collisions, where the axial-vector current is directly related to polarization phenomena such as the chiral vortical effect studied by Vilenkin (1979) and Son (2009). The paper also connects to the Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation by showing that the truncation used for parity-violating moments is identical to that approach.
