# Exclusive tensor meson photoproduction

> We study tensor meson photoproduction outside of the resonance region, at beam energies of few GeVs. We build a model based on Regge theory that includes the leading vector and axial exchanges. We consider two determinations of the unknown helicity couplings, and fit to the recent a2 photoproduction data from CLAS. Both choices give a similar description of the a2 cross section, but result in different predictions for the parity asymmetries and the f2 photoproduction cross section. We conclude that new measurements of f2 photoproduction in the forward region are needed to pin down the correct production mechanism. We also extend our predictions to the 8.5 GeV beam energy, where current experiments are running.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Regge model for tensor meson photoproduction that can describe the recent CLAS data on a2(1320) production, but the two considered models for the vector helicity couplings (minimal and Tensor Meson Dominance) give similar descriptions of the a2 cross section while yielding different predictions for f2(1270) photoproduction and parity asymmetries; the authors conclude that new measurements of f2 photoproduction in the forward region are needed to determine the correct production mechanism.

## Method

The authors built a Regge pole model for gamma p -> T p (T = a2(1320), f2(1270)) at beam energies of 3-10 GeV, including leading vector exchanges (rho, omega) and axial exchanges (b1, h1). They considered two determinations of the unknown helicity couplings for the photon-tensor-vector vertex (a "minimal" model and a Tensor Meson Dominance model) and fitted the overall normalizations of both vector and axial exchanges to the CLAS a2 photoproduction data at 4 and 5 GeV beam energies. The f2 cross section predictions were then compared to data extracted from a CLAS partial wave analysis of pi+ pi- photoproduction.

## Key numbers

* The wrong-signature zero in the vector exchange amplitude occurs at t = -0.55 GeV^2, corresponding to the point where the Regge trajectory passes through J=0.
* The a2(1320) photoproduction data from CLAS were measured in the 4-5 GeV beam energy range, with differential cross sections shown for -t bins including [0.4, 0.6] GeV^2.
* The fitted vector coupling beta^V for the minimal model is 0.251 +/- 0.053, and for the TMD model is 1.060 +/- 0.073.
* The fitted axial coupling beta^A for the minimal model is 0.821 +/- 0.023, and for the TMD model is 0.581 +/- 0.053.
* The Regge trajectory slope for vector exchanges is alpha' = 0.9 GeV^{-2}, and for axial exchanges is alpha' = 0.7 GeV^{-2}.
* The a2(1320) -> omega pi pi decay width used for normalization is 11.1 +/- 3.4 MeV.
* The a2(1320) -> gamma gamma decay width used for normalization is 1.00 +/- 0.06 keV.
* Predictions are extended to beam energy E_gamma = 8.5 GeV, where GlueX and CLAS12 experiments are operating.

## Context

This work extends Regge-based analyses of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (pi0, eta) by Nys (2016, 2017) and Mathieu (2015, 2018) to tensor mesons, using the same framework of vector and axial exchanges. The model is fitted to the recent CLAS a2 photoproduction data by Celentano (2020) and compared to f2 data extracted from the CLAS partial wave analysis of Battaglieri (2009). The results enable future measurements by GlueX and CLAS12 at 8.5 GeV to discriminate between the two production mechanisms, which is important for understanding hybrid meson production where tensor mesons serve as a benchmark.
