# Strangeness thermodynamic instabilities in hot and dense nuclear matter

> We explore the presence of thermodynamic instabilities and, con\-se\-quen\-tly, the realization of a pure hadronic phase transition in the hot and finite baryon density nuclear matter. The analysis is performed by means of an effective relativistic mean-field model with the inclusion of hyperons, $Δ$-isobars, and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector meson degrees of freedom. The Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number and zero net strangeness in symmetric nuclear matter are required. Similarly to the liquid-gas phase transition, we show that a phase transition, characterized by mechanical instabilities (due to fluctuations on the baryon number) and chemical-diffusive instabilities (due to fluctuations on the strangeness number), can take place for a finite range of $Δ$-meson coupling constants, compatible with different experimental constraints. The hadronic phase transition, which presents similar features to the quark-hadron phase transition, is characterized by different strangeness content during the mixed phase and, consequently, by a sensible variation of the strange anti-particle to particle ratios.

## Key claim

The authors show that a pure hadronic phase transition, driven by thermodynamic instabilities, can occur in hot and dense symmetric nuclear matter when $\Delta$-isobar degrees of freedom are included with a sufficiently attractive scalar coupling, but only for a finite range of the $\Delta$-meson coupling constants compatible with experimental constraints; the transition is not guaranteed for all coupling choices and disappears for repulsive or absent $\Delta$-interactions.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set, including the full baryon octet (p, n, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$), $\Delta(1232)$-isobars, and the lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons as a quasi-particle gas. They impose Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number and zero net strangeness in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, and analyze mechanical and chemical-diffusive stability conditions to map the binodal coexistence surface.

## Key numbers

- Mechanical instabilities appear in the temperature range from about 125 MeV to 145 MeV, over a finite range of baryon densities.
- At T = 140 MeV with $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.2$, the mixed phase begins at baryon density $\rho_B(A) \approx 0.3\,\rho_0$ and ends at $\rho_B(C) \approx 1.1\,\rho_0$, with a baryon chemical potential $\mu_B \simeq 320$ MeV.
- At T = 130 MeV with $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.2$, the phase transition occurs at $\mu_B \simeq 600$ MeV, with phase I at about $1.5\,\rho_0$ and phase II at about $2.2\,\rho_0$.
- At T = 140 MeV with $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.2$, the nucleon effective mass ratio $M^*/M_N$ is reduced to 0.08 and the $\Delta$ isobar ratio $M^*/M_\Delta \simeq 0.13$ at the end of the phase transition ($\rho_B \approx 1.1\,\rho_0$).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in nuclear matter with two conserved charges (Muller and Serot, 1995) and of $\Delta$-isobar driven phase transitions in warm asymmetric matter (Lavagno and Pigato, 2012) to the high-temperature, high-density regime with hyperons and $\Delta$-isobars. The results show analogies to the quark-hadron phase transition and the strangeness distillation mechanism (Greiner, 1987; Greiner, 1991), and the phase diagrams with isentropic trajectories are relevant for future compressed baryonic matter experiments such as CBM, J-PARC, and SHINE.
