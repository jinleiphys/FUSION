# Non-Gaussian Effects on Domain Growth

> The vacuum two-point function is calculated beyond the Gaussian approximation during the second order phase transition. It is found that the correlation function is dominated by the Gaussian term immediately after the phase transition but later is taken over by non-Gaussian terms as the spinodal instability continues. The non-Gaussian effects lead to larger size of domains and may imply a smaller density of topological defects than that predicted by the Hartree-Fock approximation.

## Key claim

The vacuum two-point correlation function during a second-order phase transition is initially dominated by the Gaussian term, but non-Gaussian terms take over as spinodal instability continues, leading to a domain size larger by a factor of sqrt(3) than predicted by the Hartree-Fock (Gaussian) approximation; this may imply a smaller density of topological defects.

## Method

The authors study a real scalar field with a quartic self-interaction undergoing an instantaneous quench from positive to negative mass squared. They use a time-dependent Fock basis constructed via the Liouville-von Neumann approach, treat the quartic interaction as a perturbation to the Gaussian Hamiltonian, and compute the two-point correlation function to second order in the coupling constant. The dominant non-Gaussian contribution is extracted from the time-ordered exponential of the perturbing Hamiltonian, using approximate soft-mode solutions that grow exponentially during spinodal instability.

## Key numbers

* The Gaussian correlation length scales as ξ_G(t) = sqrt(8 t / m_f), where m_f is the final mass parameter after the quench.
* The non-Gaussian correlation length scales as ξ_NG(t) = sqrt(8 * 3 t / m_f) = sqrt(3) ξ_G(t), giving a domain size larger by a factor of sqrt(3).
* Before the quench, the non-Gaussian contribution to the correlation function is of order (λ ħ)^2 / (2^11 Ω_{i,k}^4), which is negligible for both weak coupling (λ ≪ m_i^2) and strong coupling (λ ≈ m_i^2).
* The soft-mode solutions after the quench have the approximate form φ_{f,k}(t) ≈ (1/(2 sqrt(2 Ω_{i,k}))) [(1 - i Ω_{i,k}/Ω̃_{f,k}) exp(Ω̃_{f,k} t) + (1 + i Ω_{i,k}/Ω̃_{f,k}) exp(-Ω̃_{f,k} t)], where Ω̃_{f,k} ≈ sqrt(m_f^2 - k^2).

## Context

This work extends quantum field theory treatments of phase transitions beyond the Hartree-Fock (Gaussian) approximation used in earlier studies by Boyanovsky, de Vega, and Holman (1994) and by Gill and Rivers (1995). It provides a systematic perturbative method for including non-Gaussian corrections, in contrast to the improved Gaussian state corrected by the second excited state used by Cheetham and Copeland (1996). The results suggest that non-Gaussian effects could resolve the discrepancy between the small (1-2 fm) disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) regions predicted by Hartree-Fock simulations and the larger (>10 fm) regions anticipated by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), and may reduce the density of topological defects in condensed matter, heavy-ion collisions, and early Universe scenarios.
