# Nuclear shadowing and in-medium properties of the rho^0

> We explain the early onset of shadowing in nuclear photoabsorption within a multiple scattering approach and discuss its relation to in-medium modifications of the rho^0.

## Key claim
The early onset of nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption at photon energies around 1 GeV can be explained by the negative real part of the rho-nucleon forward scattering amplitude, which corresponds to an increase of the effective rho^0 mass in medium, contrary to interpretations that require a decreasing rho^0 mass.

## Method
The authors used a multiple scattering approach based on the Glauber model to calculate the nuclear photoabsorption cross section, relating it via the optical theorem to the nuclear forward scattering amplitude. They included contributions from vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) and, in an improved model, also from intermediate pi^0 mesons, accounting for two-body nucleon correlations and the widths of the vector mesons. The rho-nucleon forward scattering amplitude was taken from dispersion theoretical analyses, including its negative real part.

## Key numbers
- The onset of shadowing in nuclear photoabsorption occurs at photon energy E_gamma approximately 1 GeV.
- For the simple Glauber model neglecting the real part of f_rho, anti-shadowing appears below 2 GeV for Pb and below 1 GeV for C.
- In the improved model, intermediate pi^0 mesons contribute 30% of the total shadowing effect for carbon and 10% for lead in the onset region.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model approach to nuclear shadowing (Bauer 1978) and addresses the interpretation of early shadowing data (Bianchi 1996, Mucchietto 1999). It contradicts the interpretation by Bialas (1999) that the low-energy onset of shadowing signals a decreasing rho^0 mass in medium, instead showing consistency with dispersion theoretical analyses (Eletsky 1997, Kondratyuk 1998) that predict an increase of the effective rho^0 mass. The results enable a more complete understanding of in-medium vector meson properties and their role in nuclear photoabsorption.
