# Theoretical study of the $Δ^{++}-Δ^-$ configuration in the deuteron using antiproton beam

> We study the manifestation of the $Δ^{++}-Δ^-$ component of the deuteron wave function in the exclusive reaction $\bar p d \to π^- π^- Δ^{++}$. Due to the large binding energy the internal motion in the $Δ-Δ$ system is relativistic. We take this into account within the light-cone (LC) wave function formalism and, indeed, found large differences between calculations based on the LC and non-relativistic (NR) wave functions. We demonstrate, that the consistent LC treatment of the $Δ-Δ$ system plays the key role in the separation of the signal and background. Within the LC approach, the characteristic shape of the momentum distribution of the $Δ-Δ$ bound system predicted by the meson-exchange model is well visible on the background of usual annihilations at beam momenta between 10 and 15 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The $\Delta^{++}-\Delta^-$ component of the deuteron wave function can be observed in the exclusive reaction $\bar p d \to \pi^- \pi^- \Delta^{++}$ at beam momenta between 10 and 15 GeV/c, but only when the internal motion of the $\Delta-\Delta$ system is treated relativistically using the light-cone (LC) wave function formalism rather than the non-relativistic (NR) approach, as the large binding energy makes relativistic effects crucial for separating signal from background.

## Method

The authors calculated the signal cross section for $\bar p$ annihilation on the $\Delta^-$ within a $\Delta^{++}-\Delta^-$ deuteron component using both non-relativistic (NR) and light-cone (LC) wave function formalisms, with the $\Delta-\Delta$ momentum distribution taken from the coupled-channel folded-diagram (CCF) potential model of Haidenbauer (1993). They computed two background channels: (i) $\bar p n \to \pi^- \pi^0$ followed by charge exchange $\pi^0 p \to \pi^- \Delta^{++}$, and (ii) $\bar p n \to \pi^- \pi^- \pi^+$ followed by $\pi^+ p \to \Delta^{++}$, using reggeized $\rho$ exchange for the CEX amplitude and a nucleon- and $\Delta$-exchange model for the elementary two-pion annihilation amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Total probability of the $\Delta-\Delta$ configuration in the deuteron in the CCF model: $P_{\Delta-\Delta} = 1.35\%$, with individual components $P_{\Delta-\Delta}^{01} = 0.33\%$ ($^3S_1$), $P_{\Delta-\Delta}^{21} = 0.03\%$ ($^3D_1$), $P_{\Delta-\Delta}^{23} = 0.97\%$ ($^7D_1$), and $P_{\Delta-\Delta}^{43} = 0.02\%$ ($^7G_1$).
- The $\Delta-\Delta$ momentum distribution peaks at $k \simeq 0.4-0.6$ GeV, corresponding to inter-$\Delta$ distances $\sim 0.6-0.8$ fm.
- The signal is predicted to be visible at beam momenta between 10 and 15 GeV/c.
- The OBELIX experiment gave an upper limit on the $\Delta-\Delta$ configuration probability $\leq 1\%$ from stopped antiproton data, corresponding to $Y_{\bar p (\Delta^- \Delta^{++}) \to 2\pi^- \pi^+ p} \leq 6.5 \times 10^{-5}$.
- Previous analyses deduced $\sim 3\%$ $\Delta-\Delta$ admixture from DESY photon data (Benz 1974) and $\sim 16\%$ from antiproton data (Braun 1974), while neutrino studies gave an upper limit of 0.4% (Allasia 1986).

## Context

This work extends meson-exchange model calculations of the deuteron's $\Delta-\Delta$ component (Haapakoski 1974, Arenhovel 1975, Dymarz 1990, Haidenbauer 1993) and constituent quark model predictions (Glozman 1993, 1994) by providing a concrete experimental signature for the PANDA experiment. The key advance is demonstrating that the light-cone treatment of the strongly bound $\Delta-\Delta$ system is essential for separating the signal from annihilation background, resolving ambiguities in earlier experimental searches such as the OBELIX data (Bertin 1997) and the high $\Delta-\Delta$ probability claimed by Braun (1974).
