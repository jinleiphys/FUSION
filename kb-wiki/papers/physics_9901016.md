# How harmonic is dipole resonance of metal clusters?

> We discuss the degree of anharmonicity of dipole plasmon resonances in metal clusters. We employ the time-dependent variational principle and show that the relative shift of the second phonon scales as $N^{-4/3}$ in energy, $N$ being the number of particles. This scaling property coincides with that for nuclear giant resonances. Contrary to the previous study based on the boson-expansion method, the deviation from the harmonic limit is found to be almost negligible for Na clusters, the result being consistent with the recent experimental observation.

## Key claim

The anharmonic energy shift of the double phonon state in dipole plasmon resonances of metal clusters scales as \(N^{-4/3}\) and is almost negligible for Na clusters, contrary to the earlier prediction by Catara et al. (1993) based on the boson-expansion method, and consistent with the experimental observation by Schlipper et al. (1998).

## Method

The author applied the time-dependent variational principle to the dipole plasmon resonance of a system of N electrons interacting with a spherical ionic background. The collective potential was expanded to fourth order in the collective coordinate \(\beta\), and the coefficients \(k\) and \(k_4\) were evaluated analytically using the jellium approximation for the ionic density and an error-function approximation for the electronic density. The resulting collective Hamiltonian was requantized to obtain the phonon frequencies and the energy shift of the double phonon state.

## Key numbers

- The Mie frequency for Na clusters (Wigner-Seitz radius \(r_s = 4\) a.u.) is \(\omega_0 = 3.39\) eV.
- The energy shift of the double phonon state is given by \(\Delta^{(2)}E = -0.45 \times N^{-4/3}\) eV.
- For \(N = 92\), the energy shift is as small as \(-1.1 \times 10^{-3}\) eV.
- The surface diffuseness parameter for the electronic density was taken as \(a = 2.14\) a.u. for \(N = 198\) from Bertsch and Eckardt (1985).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent variational approach previously applied to nuclear giant resonances by Bertsch and Feldmeier (1997) to the case of metal clusters, showing that the same \(N^{-4/3}\) scaling law holds for both systems. It contradicts the earlier claim of large anharmonic effects by Catara et al. (1993) based on the boson-expansion method, and is consistent with the TDLDA calculation by Yabana and Bertsch (1996) and the experimental observation by Schlipper et al. (1998). The result enables further theoretical and experimental study of multiple plasmon excitations in metal clusters.
