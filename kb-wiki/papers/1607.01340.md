# Electrical conductivity of hadronic matter from different possible mesonic and baryonic thermal fluctuations

> Electromagnetic current-current correlators in pionic and nucleonic medium have been evaluated in the static limit to obtain electrical conductivities for pion and nucleon components respectively, where former decreases and latter one increases with the variation of temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_N$. Therefore, total electrical conductivity of pion and nucleon system exhibits a valley structure in the $T$-$μ_N$ plane. To get non-divergent and finite values of correlators, finite thermal widths of medium constituents, pion and nucleon have been considered, where these thermal widths have been determined from the in-medium scattering probabilities of pion and nucleon with other mesonic and baryonic resonances, based on effective hadronic model. At $μ_N=0$, the results of present work are more or less agrees with the results of earlier works and its finite $μ_N$ extension show a decreasing nature of electrical conductivity for hadronic medium during spanning from freeze out line to quark-hadron transition line in $T$-$μ_N$ plane.

# Electrical conductivity of hadronic matter from different possible mesonic and baryonic thermal fluctuations

## Key claim
The total electrical conductivity of hadronic matter (pion plus nucleon components) exhibits a valley structure in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane, where the pion component decreases and the nucleon component increases with both temperature and baryon chemical potential; this valley structure arises purely from phase space effects of the hadronic medium and is unrelated to the quark-hadron transition.

## Method
The electromagnetic current-current correlators for pionic and nucleonic media were evaluated in the static limit using the Kubo formula within real-time thermal field theory. Finite thermal widths of pions and nucleons, obtained from in-medium scattering probabilities via one-loop self-energy diagrams with mesonic (sigma, rho) and baryonic (N, Delta, and other 4-star baryon resonances) loops based on effective hadronic Lagrangian densities, were used to regulate the otherwise divergent correlators. The electrical conductivities for pion and nucleon components were calculated separately and summed to obtain the total electrical conductivity.

## Key numbers
- At zero baryon chemical potential (μ_N=0), the present results for σ/T in the hadronic temperature domain 0.110 GeV < T < 0.175 GeV agree with the results of Fraile et al. and Lee et al., but are smaller than the results of Marty et al. and Cassing et al.
- The minimum of total electrical conductivity shifts toward lower temperature as μ_N increases; for example, at μ_N=0.400, 0.500, and 0.600 GeV the valley positions move to lower T.
- The freeze-out line spans from (T_f ≈ 0.166 GeV, μ_f ≈ 0) to (T_f ≈ 0.140 GeV, μ_f ≈ 0.420 GeV), within which the valley structure may be observable from RHIC energies (√s=200 GeV) down to √s ≈ 8 GeV.
- The numerical values of σ/T from earlier works lie in the range 10^{-3} to 10^{-2} for the hadronic phase and 10^{-3} to 10^{-1} for the quark phase.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of electrical conductivity in hadronic matter by Lee et al., Fraile et al. (Nicola), Marty et al., and Cassing et al., which showed conflicting temperature dependence at zero baryon chemical potential. The present calculation provides the first estimation of σ in the T-μ_N domain of hadronic matter, showing that the decreasing σ(T) at μ_N=0 becomes a valley structure at finite μ_N. This enables phenomenological studies of electromagnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions across different beam energies, and the results can be compared with lattice QCD calculations by Ding et al., Aarts et al., Buividovich et al., Burnier et al., Gupta et al., Barndt et al., and Amato et al., which cover a large numerical band.
