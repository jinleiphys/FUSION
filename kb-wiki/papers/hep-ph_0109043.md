# Partonic Scattering Cross Sections in the QCD Medium

> A medium modified gluon propagator is used to evaluate the scattering cross section for the process gg-->gg in the QCD medium by performing an explicit sum over the polarizations of the gluons. We incorporate a magnetic sreening mass from a non-perturbative study. It is shown that the medium modified cross section is finite, divergence free, and is independent of any ad-hoc momentum transfer cut-off parameters. The medium modified finite cross sections are necessary for a realistic investigation of the production and equilibration of the minijet plasma expected at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using a medium-modified gluon propagator (including both electric and magnetic screening) yields finite, divergence-free cross sections for the process \(gg \rightarrow gg\) without any ad-hoc momentum transfer cut-off, but the practical utility of the result depends on the specific non-perturbative magnetic screening mass adopted from prior work.

## Method

The authors compute the matrix element squared for the t-channel \(gg \rightarrow gg\) scattering using the medium-modified gluon propagator in covariant gauge, split into longitudinal and transverse parts with self-energies \(\Pi_L\) and \(\Pi_T\). They perform an explicit sum over physical gluon polarizations using projection operators, then integrate the differential cross section to obtain total and transport cross sections. A non-perturbative magnetic screening mass \(m_{\rm mag}^2 = \frac{3}{2} (0.255 g^2 T)^2\) from Biro and Heiselberg is introduced to remove the logarithmic singularity in the transverse part, while the longitudinal part uses the perturbative Debye mass \(m_D^2 = g^2 T^2\).

## Key numbers

* The energy density of jets and minijets at RHIC is estimated to be larger than about \(50~\mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\) and at LHC larger than about \(1000~\mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\).
* The lattice QCD deconfinement temperature is cited as \(\sim 200~\mathrm{MeV}\) and energy density \(\sim 2~\mathrm{GeV/fm^3}\).
* The non-perturbative magnetic screening mass used is \(m_{\rm mag}^2 = \frac{3}{2} (0.255 g^2 T)^2\).
* The total cross section in the leading-order medium expression is given by \(\sigma_{\rm tot} = \frac{9 \pi \alpha^2}{8} \left( \frac{s}{\Pi_L (s+2\Pi_L)} + \frac{1}{\Pi_T} + 2\frac{\ln(s/\Pi_L+2)-\ln(s/\Pi_T+2)}{\Pi_T-\Pi_L} - \frac{2}{s+2\Pi_T} \right)\).
* The transport cross section in the leading-order medium expression is given by a lengthy formula (Eq. transmedium) involving \(\Pi_L\), \(\Pi_T\), and \(s\).
* The figures use \(T = \sqrt{s}/5.4\) and \(\alpha = 0.3\) for numerical comparisons.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to regularize partonic cross sections in the QCD medium, which previously relied on introducing a Debye screening mass by hand (e.g., Zhang and Nayak) or using a momentum cut-off in parton cascade models (e.g., Geiger). The key advance is the inclusion of magnetic screening via a non-perturbative mass, which the authors show leads to enhanced total and transport cross sections compared to the Debye-only screened vacuum formula, especially at lower \(s\). This enables more realistic transport studies of minijet plasma evolution at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that a self-consistent transport calculation incorporating these medium-modified cross sections remains to be performed.
