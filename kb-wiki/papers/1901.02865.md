# Spectral functions of nucleon form factors: Three-pion continua at low energies

> We study the imaginary parts of the isoscalar electromagnetic and isovector axial form factors of the nucleon close to the $3π$-threshold in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. At the two-loop level, the contributions arising from leading and next-to-leading order chiral $πN$-vertices, as well as pion-induced excitations of virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobars, are calculated. It is found that the heavy baryon treatment overestimates substantially these $3π$-continua. From a phenomenological analysis, that includes the narrow $ω(783)$-resonance or the broad $a_1$-resonance, one can recognize small windows near threshold, where chiral $3π$-dynamics prevails. However, in the case of the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors $G_{E,M}^s(t)$, the radiative correction provided by the $π^0γ$-intermediate state turns out to be of similar size.

# Spectral functions of nucleon form factors: Three-pion continua at low energies

## Key claim

The heavy baryon treatment overestimates the three-pion continua for nucleon form factors by about a factor of 2 to 3 near threshold, and the chiral $3\pi$-dynamics prevails only in small windows near threshold that are further reduced by radiative corrections from the $\pi^0\gamma$-intermediate state, which are of similar size for the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors performed a two-loop calculation in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, applying the Cutkosky cutting rule to diagrams with a $3\pi$-intermediate state to obtain the imaginary parts of the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors $G_{E,M}^s(t)$ and the isovector axial form factor $G_A(t)$. They evaluated contributions from leading-order chiral $\pi N$-vertices, next-to-leading order vertices (involving low-energy constants $c_1, c_2, c_3, c_4$), and pion-induced excitations of virtual $\Delta(1232)$-isobars described by Rarita-Schwinger spinors, performing the four-dimensional phase space integration numerically.

## Key numbers

- The heavy baryon treatment overestimates the $3\pi$-continua near threshold by about a factor of 2 to 3, with relativistic corrections of magnitude $\sqrt{t}/M > 3m_\pi/M = 0.44$.
- The $\omega(783)$-meson has mass $m_\omega = 783$ MeV and decay width $\Gamma_\omega = 8.5$ MeV, decomposed as $(7.6 + 0.7 + 0.2)$ MeV for $\omega \to \pi^+\pi^0\pi^-$, $\omega \to \pi^0\gamma$, and other modes.
- The $\omega$-meson pole is found at $\sqrt{t} = (782.8 - 4.14 i)$ MeV, corresponding to $t = (32.17 - 0.340 i) m_\pi^2$.
- The $a_1$-meson parameters from COMPASS are $m_{a_1} = 1.3$ GeV and $\Gamma_{a_1} = 0.38$ GeV; from $\tau$-decay analyses $m_{a_1} = 1.2$ GeV and $\Gamma_{a_1} = 0.48$ GeV.
- The $\pi^0\gamma$-intermediate state contribution to Im$G_{E,M}^s(t)$ exceeds the leading-order chiral $3\pi$-continua in the region $3m_\pi < \sqrt{t} < 4m_\pi$, with threshold behavior Im$G_{E,M}^s(t) \sim (t - m_\pi^2)^2$.
- The $\Delta$-isobar suppression factor is about 2 to 3 for Im$G_E^s(t)$ and Im$G_A(t)$, and about 7 to 8 for Im$G_M^s(t)$.
- The chiral $3\pi$-dynamics prevails in an energy window $3m_\pi < \sqrt{t} < 5m_\pi$ for the axial spectral function.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of three-pion continua for nucleon form factors from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (3piHB, 2003) to covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, motivated by the expected size of relativistic corrections. It builds on previous two-loop calculations of isovector electromagnetic spectral functions (2loop) and dispersion analyses of $\pi N$-scattering using Roy-Steiner equations (Roy and Steiner; rpuzzle). The results enable improved dispersion-theoretic treatments of nucleon form factors by providing more accurate chiral inputs near the $3\pi$-threshold, though the authors conclude these continua are too weak to significantly influence physical observables.
