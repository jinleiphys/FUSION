# Spin polarization evolution in a boost invariant hydrodynamical background

> Relativistic hydrodynamic equations for particles with spin one-half are used to determine the space-time evolution of the spin polarization in a boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous background. The hydrodynamic approach uses the forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors based on de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert formalism. Our calculations illustrate how the formalism of hydrodynamics with spin can be used to determine physical observables related to the spin polarization and how the latter can be compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous hydrodynamic background, the spin polarization tensor evolves independently from the thermal vorticity, with its space-time changes following solely from angular momentum conservation; this independence is presented as one extreme case, with the opposite extreme being the assumption that the polarization tensor equals the thermal vorticity (as in Becattini (2013)).

## Method

The authors solve standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations (charge, energy, and linear momentum conservation) for a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion to obtain the temperature and chemical potential as functions of proper time. Using the de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert (GLW) forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors, expanded to linear order in the spin polarization tensor ωμν, they then solve the angular momentum conservation equations to determine the proper-time evolution of the six independent coefficients defining ωμν. The spin evolution is decoupled from the hydrodynamic background, as the linear terms in ωμν appear only in the spin tensor.

## Key numbers

* Initial baryon chemical potential μ0 = 800 MeV and initial temperature T0 = 155 MeV, with particle mass m = 1116 MeV (Λ-hyperon mass).
* Initial proper time τ0 = 1 fm; hydrodynamic evolution continued to final time τf = 10 fm.
* The coefficient CκZ increases by about 0.1 within 1 fm of evolution, showing the strongest time dependence among the spin polarization coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of hydrodynamics with spin developed by Florkowski (2017) and Florkowski (2018) by implementing the GLW forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors, which connect to underlying kinetic theory as shown by Florkowski (2018). It contrasts with the approach of Becattini (2013) and Becattini (2017), where the spin polarization tensor is constrained to equal the thermal vorticity; the authors note that the STAR collaboration's measurement of a quadrupole dependence of longitudinal Λ polarization cannot be reproduced by that assumption. The calculations set the stage for more realistic simulations that could be compared with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions.
