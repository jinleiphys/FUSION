# Searching for Solitons in Heavy-Ion Reactions near the Fermi Energy

> Solitons are special shape-conserving hydrodynamical solutions that appear in many areas of physics. Here, we explore the existence of such solutions in microscopic descriptions of the heavy ion reaction $^{12}$C + $^{28}$Si $\rightarrow$ $^{12}$C$^*$ + 7$α$ in the range E/A=10-65 MeV/u. After recognizing the centrality of the collision and time-reversibility as fundamental requirements for the presence of solitonic $^{12}$C, we utilize the Hybrid $α$-Cluster model for our analysis with a novel methodology. Our results suggest soliton production for E/A=25-45 MeV/u in the forward direction, with a total cross section being at least in the order of a few $μ$b. This, apart from being encouraging for possible experimental studies, maybe connected to possible toroid states of $^{28}$Si at low angular momenta.

# Searching for Solitons in Heavy-Ion Reactions near the Fermi Energy

## Key claim

The authors suggest that solitonic carbon-12 nuclei may be produced in the reaction ¹²C + ²⁸Si → ¹²C* + 7α at beam energies of 25–45 MeV/u in the forward direction, but only under the restrictive conditions of central collisions (zero impact parameter), time-reversible dynamics, and a no-particle-exchange channel that makes the reported cross sections lower limits rather than absolute predictions.

## Method

The authors used the Hybrid α-Cluster (HαC) model, a molecular dynamics framework where α particles interact via Coulomb, Bass, and effective Fermi energy potentials, with time evolution given by Hamiltonian equations of motion. They simulated 10⁶ events at each of eight beam energies (10–65 MeV/u) with initial relative angular momentum l=0, randomly rotated initial conditions, and identified final fragments via a proximity algorithm. The appearance probability density of ¹²C* as a function of exit time from the ²⁸Si target was fitted to a standard soliton density profile (cosh⁻² form), and angular distributions in the center-of-mass frame were calculated to distinguish solitonic from thermalized or transparent regimes.

## Key numbers

- The soliton region is identified for beam energies E/A = 25–45 MeV/u, based on fits of the ¹²C* appearance probability density to the soliton profile.
- Total cross section for the l=0, no-particle-exchange ¹²C* + 7α channel is in the order of a few μb (lower limit).
- Beam energies studied: 10, 15, 20, 25, 35, 45, 55, and 65 MeV/u.
- The characteristic crossing distance used is 2R_Si = 6.98 fm.
- For the soliton region at E/A = 35 and 45 MeV/u, the 7α excitation energy peaks at approximately 105 MeV.
- At the high energy extreme (65 MeV/u), the 7α excitation energy peaks at approximately 76 MeV.
- The ratio τ_Max/τ_cross is 2–3 for the lowest energies, indicating compound nucleus behavior, and approaches 1 at the highest energies, indicating transparency.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Fowler et al. (1982), who derived solitonic solutions from the hydrodynamic equations of motion for nuclear fluids near the speed of sound, and applies it to microscopic molecular dynamics simulations. The study also connects to prior work on the ²⁸Si + ¹²C reaction at 35 MeV/u by Depastas et al. (2025), where the 7α excitation energy tail was reproduced. The results enable experimental searches for nuclear solitons using spectrometers such as VAMOS++ at GANIL, with the Hoyle state and 2⁺₁ (4.43 MeV) state of ¹²C as additional signatures, and may provide evidence for toroidal states of ²⁸Si at low angular momenta, a contested topic in the current literature.
