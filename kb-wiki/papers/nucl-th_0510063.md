# A description of the yrast states in 24-Mg by the self-consistent 3D-cranking model

> With the self-consistent 3D-cranking model, the ground-state rotational band in 24-Mg is analysed. A role of triaxial deformation is discussed, in particular, in a description of the observed two I=8+ states.

## Key claim
The self-consistent 3D-cranking model explains the two observed Iπ=8+ states in 24Mg on the same footing: the yrast 8+ is an axially-symmetric high-K state built from d5/2 orbitals, while the second 8+ is a rotational member of the ground-state band with substantial triaxial deformation, resolving a disagreement in prior axial cranking calculations.

## Method
The author performed self-consistent 1D- and 3D-cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a spherical Nilsson Hamiltonian plus pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+QQ) force, with a valence space of two major shells (N=2,3). The variational state was constrained for angular momentum components (J1, J2, J3), particle numbers, and orientation of the angular momentum vector via Lagrange multipliers. The method of steepest descent was used to solve the variational equations, starting from a deformed Nilsson+BCS trial state with initial deformation parameters (β,γ)=(0.347,0.0°) and pairing gaps (Δp,Δn)=(1.840 MeV,1.962 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Initial deformation parameters for the ground state: β=0.347, γ=0.0°; pairing gap energies: Δp=1.840 MeV, Δn=1.962 MeV.
- In the 1D-cranking calculation, gap energies disappear for both protons and neutrons before J=6ℏ.
- At high spin (J=8,10ℏ), substantial triaxial deformation forms with γ ≳ -10° (Hill-Wheeler convention).
- The yrast 8+ state from the 3D-cranking calculation is about 2.5 MeV lower in energy than the 1D-cranking solution (which corresponds to the second 8+ state).
- The yrast 8+ state has β≃0.24 and γ≃0° (axial symmetry); its total spin is constructed almost purely from d5/2 orbits: 3.97ℏ each from protons and neutrons.
- The two observed 8+ states are energetically separated by about 2 MeV experimentally.

## Context
This work extends the cranked Skyrme HF+BCS and generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations of Valor et al. (2000), which assumed axial symmetry and could not reproduce the two 8+ states in 24Mg. The results are consistent with the shell model calculation of Wiedenhover et al. (2001), which identified the first 8+ state as a (d5/2)8 configuration not belonging to the ground-state band. The study enables a unified microscopic description of low-K and high-K rotational states in light deformed nuclei using the 3D-cranking framework.
