# Pygmy resonances and nucleosynthesis

> A microscopic theoretical approach based on a self-consistent density functional theory for the nuclear ground state and QRPA formalism extended with multi-phonon degrees of freedom for the nuclear excited states is implemented in investigations of new low-energy modes called pygmy resonances. Advantage of the method is the unified description of low-energy multiphonon excitations, pygmy resonances and core polarization effects. This is found of crucial importance for the understanding of the fine structure of nuclear response functions at low energies. Aspects of the precise knowledge of nuclear response functions around the neutron threshold are discussed in a connection to nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a microscopic approach combining density functional theory with a three-phonon quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) is necessary to accurately describe pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) and their impact on nucleosynthesis, because standard QRPA calculations alone are insufficient to reproduce the fine structure of nuclear response functions around the neutron threshold.

## Method
The authors used a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field theory for the nuclear ground state, extended with a QRPA formalism that includes multi-phonon degrees of freedom (up to three-phonon configurations) via the QPM. The model Hamiltonian includes mean-field, pairing, and residual interactions (isoscalar and isovector multipole and spin-multipole terms in the particle-hole channel, and multipole pairing in the particle-particle channel). The resulting microscopic strength functions were then implemented into the statistical reaction code TALYS to calculate neutron-capture cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The n-capture cross section for the reaction $^{85}$Kr(n,$\gamma$)$^{86}$Kr calculated with the three-phonon QPM is in very good agreement with experimental data, while the QRPA gives a reduced value of about ≈35%.
- The estimated pure PDR contribution to the QRPA-calculated n-capture cross section for $^{85}$Kr(n,$\gamma$)$^{86}$Kr is of the order of ≈30%.
- In N=50 isotones, the excitation energy region below E ≤ 9 MeV is related to the PDR, with total strength smoothly decreasing with increasing proton number Z.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Tsoneva et al. (2004, 2008) and Schwengner et al. (2008, 2013) that correlated PDR strength with neutron skin thickness, and by Goriely (2002) that suggested PDR could impact neutron-capture reaction cross sections. The authors show that earlier QRPA-based studies could not explain experimental data in detail, and that the three-phonon QPM is needed to account for core polarization effects and multiphonon coupling. This enables more accurate predictions of photonuclear reaction cross sections and neutron-capture rates for nucleosynthesis, particularly in experimentally inaccessible mass regions.
