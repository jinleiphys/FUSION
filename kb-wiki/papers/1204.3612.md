# Evolution of shell structure in neutron-rich calcium isotopes

> We employ interactions from chiral effective field theory and compute the binding energies and low-lying excitations of calcium isotopes with the coupled-cluster method. Effects of three-nucleon forces are included phenomenologically as in-medium two-nucleon interactions, and the coupling to the particle continuum is taken into account using a Berggren basis. The computed ground-state energies and the low-lying 2+ states for the isotopes 42,48,50,52Ca are in good agreement with data, and we predict the excitation energy of the first 2+ state in 54Ca at 1.9 MeV, displaying only a weak sub-shell closure. In the odd-mass nuclei 53,55,61Ca we find that the positive parity states deviate strongly from the naive shell model.

# Evolution of shell structure in neutron-rich calcium isotopes

## Key claim
Three-nucleon forces (3NFs) are essential for producing the shell closure in 48Ca, while 54Ca exhibits only a weak sub-shell closure (not a full shell closure) with a predicted first 2+ state at 1.9 MeV, contrary to some earlier predictions of a strong shell gap at N=34.

## Method
The authors employed chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N3LO) from Entem and Machleidt, together with a schematic in-medium effective three-nucleon force (3NF) derived from chiral EFT, parameterized by fitting binding energies of 48,52Ca. Ground states of closed-(sub)shell calcium isotopes were computed using the coupled-cluster method in the singles-doubles (CCSD) approximation with perturbative triples (Lambda-CCSD(T)), in model spaces of up to Nmax=19 major spherical oscillator shells; excited states and open-shell nuclei were treated with equation-of-motion (EOM) methods, and coupling to the particle continuum was included via a Gamow-Hartree-Fock (Berggren) basis.

## Key numbers
- Predicted excitation energy of the first 2+ state in 54Ca: 1.9 MeV (from EOM with 2p excitations of 52Ca ground state; also 1.80 MeV from 1p-1h excitation of 54Ca ground state in 17 shells)
- Computed 2+ excitation energies (coupled-cluster vs. experiment): 48Ca 3.58 MeV (exp. 3.83 MeV); 52Ca 2.19 MeV (exp. 2.56 MeV); 54Ca 1.89 MeV (no data available)
- Neutron separation energies (coupled-cluster vs. experiment): 48Ca 9.45 MeV (exp. 9.95 MeV); 52Ca 6.59 MeV (exp. 6.0 MeV from recent measurement); 54Ca 4.59 MeV (exp. 4.0 MeV from mass table evaluation)
- Resonant state energies (real part) relative to one-neutron emission threshold in 53Ca: 5/2+ at 1.99 MeV (width 1.97 MeV), 9/2+ at 4.75 MeV (width 0.28 MeV); in 55Ca: 5/2+ at 1.63 MeV (width 1.33 MeV), 9/2+ at 4.43 MeV (width 0.23 MeV); in 61Ca: 5/2+ at 1.14 MeV (width 0.62 MeV), 9/2+ at 2.19 MeV (width 0.02 MeV)
- 62Ca ground state unbound by about 0.2 MeV with respect to 60Ca

## Context
This work extends coupled-cluster calculations with chiral EFT interactions from neutron-rich oxygen isotopes (Hagen 2012) to the calcium isotopic chain, addressing the open question of shell evolution near the neutron dripline. The results contradict the GXPF1 empirical interaction prediction of a strong shell gap in 54Ca, and instead support the KB3 interaction and the low-momentum shell-model interaction (Luigi 2009) which yield no shell gap or a weak sub-shell closure. The findings also refine earlier shell-model calculations including 3NFs (Holt 2011) that predicted a shell closure in 54Ca within the fp model space but a sub-shell closure when including the g9/2 orbital. The predictions for level ordering in 53,55,61Ca deviate from the naive shell model and provide testable targets for future experiments on neutron-rich calcium isotopes.
