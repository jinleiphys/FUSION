# Pseudorapidity dependence of parton energy loss in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We analyze the recent data from the BRAHMS Collaboration on the pseudorapidity dependence of nuclear modification factors in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV by using the full three dimensional hydrodynamic simulations for the density effects on parton energy loss. We first compute the transverse spectra at $η=0$ and 2.2, and next take a ratio $R_η=R_{AA}(η=2.2)/R_{AA}(η=0)$, where $R_{AA}$ is a nuclear modification factor. It is shown that hydrodynamic components account for $R_η\simeq 1$ at low $p_\mathrm{T}$ and that quenched pQCD components lead $R_η < 1$ at high $p_\mathrm{T}$ which are consistent with the data. Strong suppression at $η=2.2$ is compatible with the parton energy loss in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed strong suppression of high-\(p_\mathrm{T}\) hadrons at pseudorapidity \(\eta=2.2\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV is consistent with final-state parton energy loss in a dense medium, but the ratio \(R_\eta = R_{AA}(\eta=2.2)/R_{AA}(\eta=0)\) being less than unity at high \(p_\mathrm{T}\) is primarily due to the steeper slope of the \(p_\mathrm{T}\) spectrum at forward rapidity from pQCD components, not necessarily from a larger parton density at \(\eta=2.2\).

## Method

The authors used a hybrid "hydro+jet" model combining full three-dimensional (3D) ideal hydrodynamics for the soft (thermalized) component and PYTHIA 6.2 with independent fragmentation for the hard (pQCD) component. Parton energy loss in the medium was implemented via the approximate first-order GLV formula in opacity expansion, with the thermalized parton density taken from the hydrodynamic simulation. The initial energy density distribution in space-time rapidity \(\eta_{\mathrm{s}}\) was parametrized with a flat region of width \(\eta_{\mathrm{flat}}/2 = 2\) and a Gaussian tail of width \(\eta_{\mathrm{Gauss}} = 0.8\), tuned to reproduce BRAHMS pseudorapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- The initial time for hydrodynamics was \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- The GLV energy loss formula used parameters \(L = 3\) fm, \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV, and an adjustable parameter \(C = 0.45\).
- The thermal freeze-out temperature was \(T^{\mathrm{th}} = 100\) MeV; the chemical freeze-out temperature was \(T^{\mathrm{ch}} = 170\) MeV.
- The nuclear shadowing effect (EKS98) on \(R_{AA}(\eta=2.2) < R_{AA}(\eta=0)\) was found to be \(\sim 15\%\) around \(p_\mathrm{T} = 2\)-3 GeV/\(c\).
- The thermalized parton density at \(\eta_{\mathrm{s}} = 3.25\) was about 40% smaller than at midrapidity.
- The \(K\)-factor used in the pQCD parton model was \(K = 2.5\), with scale \(Q = p_{\mathrm{T,jet}}/2\) and primordial transverse momentum \(\langle k_{\mathrm{T}}^2\rangle_{NN} = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/\(c^2\).

## Context

This work extends the hydro+jet model previously applied to midrapidity observables (Hirano and Nara, 2002-2003) to the forward rapidity region, directly confronting the first BRAHMS data on pseudorapidity dependence of nuclear modification factors. It shows that the observed \(R_\eta < 1\) at high \(p_\mathrm{T}\) can be explained without invoking a rapidity-dependent density effect, because the transverse dynamics at \(\eta=0\) and \(\eta=2.2\) are nearly identical in the hydrodynamic simulation. The paper also predicts a strong rapidity dependence of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) at high \(p_\mathrm{T}\), which could serve as a clearer test of the density effect on parton energy loss.
