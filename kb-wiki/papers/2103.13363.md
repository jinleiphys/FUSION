# On-shell transition of SRG and nuclear systems

> We make variational estimates for the binding energies of $^2H,~^3H,~^4He,~^{16}O,~^{40}Ca$, showing their running with the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) cutoff towards the infrared region and show the generalized Tjon lines that emerge from the calculations. We infer the SRG evolution of the three-body contributions for the $^3H$ and $^4He$ binding energies by computing the two-body contributions with a variational approach assuming that four-body forces are negligible. At any given SRG cutoff, the three-body contributions may then be inferred as being the experimental value minus the two-body contributions. The off-shell / on-shell transition at a critical SRG cutoff $λ_c$ drives the behavior of all binding energies and this scale is the turning point of the generalized Tjon lines. Also, at $λ_c$ the ratios between three-body and two-body contributions to the binding energies, $B_{λ_c}(3) ~/~ B_{λ_c}(2)$, are the same in both $^3H$ and $^4He$ systems and equal to $1/4$, so that $B_{λ_c}(2) ~=~ 4~B_{λ_c}(3)$. All calculations are carried out with the Idaho-Salamanca N3LO potential, which is evolved with the SRG up to the infrared fixed point ($λ\to 0$) in all S, P, D, F and G partial-wave channels in order to compute the variational binding energies at several SRG cutoff scales.

## Key claim

The off-shell to on-shell transition of the SRG-evolved nucleon-nucleon interaction, occurring at a critical cutoff \(\lambda_c = 0.9~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), drives the behavior of binding energies for light nuclei and causes the back-bending of generalized Tjon lines; at this scale the ratio of three-body to two-body contributions to the binding energy is exactly \(1/4\) for both \(^3H\) and \(^4He\).

## Method

The authors start from the Idaho-Salamanca N3LO chiral potential and evolve it via the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) flow equation with the Wilson generator down to the infrared fixed point (\(\lambda \to 0\)) in all S, P, D, F and G partial-wave channels. They compute variational binding energies for \(^2H\), \(^3H\), \(^4He\), \(^{16}O\) and \(^{40}Ca\) using harmonic-oscillator wave functions, obtaining the two-body contribution \(B_\lambda(2)\) at each SRG cutoff \(\lambda\). The three-body contribution \(B_\lambda(3)\) is then inferred as the experimental binding energy minus the two-body contribution, assuming four-body forces are negligible for \(^4He\). The off-shell/on-shell transition is characterized by the Frobenius norm \(\phi_\lambda\) of the potential and its derivatives, defining an order parameter \(\beta_\lambda = \partial\phi_\lambda/\partial\lambda\) and a similarity susceptibility \(\eta_\lambda = \partial\beta_\lambda/\partial\lambda\).

## Key numbers

- Critical SRG cutoff for the on-shell transition: \(\lambda_c = 0.9~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (for \(N=30\) grid points with \(p_{\mathrm{max}} = 4~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)).
- At \(\lambda_c\), the ratio \(B_{\lambda_c}(3) / B_{\lambda_c}(2) = 1/4\) for both \(^3H\) and \(^4He\), so that \(B_{\lambda_c}(2) = 4 B_{\lambda_c}(3)\).
- Phenomenological SRG cutoffs where two-body forces alone best reproduce experimental binding: \(\lambda_{\mathrm{ph}} = 0.6~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(^2H\), \(1.0~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(^3H\), \(1.4~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(^4He\), \(0.7~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(^{16}O\), and \(0.5~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for \(^{40}Ca\).
- SRG cutoff where two-body contribution alone best describes triton: \(\lambda_{\mathrm{2B}} = 1.05~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\); for helium: \(\lambda_{\mathrm{2B}} = 1.40~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- SRG cutoff where two-body and three-body contributions are equal (\(B(3)=B(2)\)): \(\lambda \sim 0.5~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for both \(^3H\) and \(^4He\).
- SRG cutoff where binding is described only by three-body forces (\(B(2)=0\)): \(\lambda \sim 0.3~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- Deuteron S-wave probability at \(\lambda = 2.0~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\): about 96%; D-wave probability: about 4%.
- SRG cutoff range explored: \(0.1 \leq \lambda \leq 2.0~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- Momentum grid parameters: \(p_{\mathrm{max}} = 4~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) with \(N=20\) or \(N=30\) grid points.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the infrared limit of SRG evolution and its connection to Levinson's theorem (Arriola 2014, 2016) and the on-shell transition (Timoteo 2016). It builds on the SRG framework developed by Wegner (1994) and Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994) and applied to nuclear systems by Bogner, Furnstahl and Perry (2006). The results show that the SRG cutoff, traditionally viewed as a mathematical tool to soften interactions, has a physical interpretation tied to the on-shell transition, enabling a description of nuclear binding with nearly on-shell interactions and moderate three-body forces. The approach is complementary to computationally expensive ab initio calculations and suggests that simple variational methods can capture qualitative features of the SRG flow, particularly in the infrared limit.
