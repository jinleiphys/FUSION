# Extended Two--Particle Green's Functions and Optical Potentials for Two--Particle Scattering by Many--Body Targets

> An extension of the fermionic particle--particle propagator is presented, that possesses similar algebraic properties to the single--particle Green's function. In particular, this extended two--particle Green's function satisfies Dyson's equation and its self energy has the same analytic structure as the the self energy of the single--particle Green's function. For the case of a system interacting by one--particle potentials only, the two--particle self energy takes on a particularly simple form, just like the common self energy does. The new two--particle self energy also serves as a well behaved optical potential for the elastic scattering of a two--particle projectile by a many--body target. Due to its analytic structure, the two--particle self energy avoids divergences that appear with effective potentials derived by other means.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an extension of the fermionic particle-particle propagator that satisfies a Dyson equation and whose self energy has the same analytic structure as the single-particle Green's function self energy, thereby providing a well-behaved optical potential for elastic two-particle scattering that avoids the high-energy divergences present in effective potentials derived by other means.

## Method

The authors construct extended composite states in a direct-sum space that includes the N+2 particle space, the dual of the N-2 particle space, and a tensor product space of one-particle and N-1 particle states, using an indefinite metric to enforce an orthogonality relation. They define an extended two-particle Green's function as the resolvent of an operator in this composite space, then apply matrix partitioning to derive a Dyson equation and define a two-particle self energy. The formalism is developed for fermionic systems with a correlated N-particle ground state, and the special case of systems interacting only by one-particle potentials is examined explicitly.

## Key numbers

* The extended two-particle Green's function contains the standard particle-particle propagator as an additive component, with additional terms that are analytic in the lower half of the complex plane and feature single poles at energies that are sums of single-particle energies and quasi-hole energies.
* For a system interacting only by one-particle potentials, the two-particle self energy becomes energy independent and exact already in first order, taking the form Self_{rs,r's'} = h_{rr'} delta_{ss'} + h_{ss'} delta_{rr'} - h_{rs'} delta_{sr'} - h_{sr'} delta_{rs'}.
* The zeroth order extended propagator has the inverse [mcG^0(omega)]^{-1} = omega * 1 - epsilon, where epsilon contains the zeroth order single-particle energies epsilon_r + epsilon_s.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic properties of the single-particle Green's function, which satisfies a Dyson equation and provides an optical potential for one-particle scattering (Bell and Squires, Namiki), to the two-particle case. The standard particle-particle propagator (Fetter and Walecka, Gross and Runge) does not satisfy a Dyson equation, and previous approaches to two-particle scattering via optical potentials (Junkin) suffered from divergences at high energies. The new formalism enables well-behaved effective two-particle scattering equations for projectiles such as deuterons scattering off atomic nuclei or positronium scattering off molecules, and it provides a framework for balanced treatment of correlations in both target and scattering states that is difficult to achieve in wavefunction approaches.
