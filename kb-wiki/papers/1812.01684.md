# QCD matter with a crossover and a first-order phase transition

> We present a phenomenological parametrization of the phase diagram of QCD as a function of temperature $T$ and baryochemical potential $μ$. The parametrization is constructed by introducing a switching function which controls the nature of the transition between the Hadron-Resonance Gas (HRG) and Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) phases of nuclear matter, such that the equation of state (EOS) possesses a rapid crossover at large $T$ and small $μ$, a critical point placed anywhere along the phase transition line, and a first-order transition at small $T$ and large $μ$. This EOS offers a convenient phenomenological tool for assessing the possible effects of the conjectured QCD critical point on heavy-ion observables.

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological parametrization of the QCD phase diagram that can simultaneously describe a rapid crossover at high temperature and low baryochemical potential, a critical point placed anywhere along the phase transition line, and a first-order phase transition at low temperature and high baryochemical potential; however, the authors find that including a first-order transition and critical point worsens the global fit to available lattice data compared to earlier crossover-only models.

## Method
The authors construct a switching function S(T,μ) that interpolates between the Hadron-Resonance Gas (HRG) and Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) equations of state, with S=0 corresponding to pure HRG and S=1 to pure QGP. The switching function is designed to be infinitely differentiable everywhere except at the critical point and along the first-order transition line, using a combination of hyperbolic tangent and arctangent functions controlled by parameters that separate the phase diagram into a crossover region and a critical region. The model is fitted to lattice data for the normalized pressure P/T^4 and trace anomaly (ε-3P)/T^4 at both zero and finite baryochemical potential, using three different hadronic models for the HRG phase.

## Key numbers
- The best-fit parameters for the excluded-volume HRG model exI give T₀ = 211.8876 MeV, k₂ = 0.000015, k₄ = 0.386452, r = 4, with χ²/d.o.f. = 4.302
- The best-fit parameters for the excluded-volume HRG model exII give T₀ = 212.3557 MeV, k₂ = 0.000093, k₄ = 0.387725, r = 4, with χ²/d.o.f. = 4.574
- The best-fit parameters for the point-particle HRG model pt give T₀ = 197.423 MeV, k₂ = 0.341508, k₄ = 0.000010, r = 8, with χ²/d.o.f. = 9.980
- The critical point temperature is fixed at T_c = 130 MeV for the fit results presented
- The switching function parameters a and b are fixed to a = 1 and b = 0.8 for the results shown

## Context
This work extends the switching function approach of Albright (2014) and Albright (2015), which only modeled a rapid crossover, by adding a critical point and first-order phase transition. The authors find that the optimal parameters from those earlier studies are excluded by the requirement of a coexistence line between the HRG and QGP phases, and that including the critical point worsens agreement with lattice data from Borsanyi (2012) compared to the crossover-only models. The model enables heavy-ion phenomenologists to study the effects of a conjectured QCD critical point on observables using hydrodynamic simulations, and the authors note that improvements could come from using more reliable equations of state for the small-T, large-μ region as explored by Kurkela (2016).
