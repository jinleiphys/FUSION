# Beta decay of r-process waiting-point nuclei in a self-consistent approach

> Beta-decay rates for spherical neutron-rich r-process waiting-point nuclei are calculated within a fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation, formulated in the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov canonical single-particle basis. The same Skyrme force is used everywhere in the calculation except in the proton-neutron particle-particle channel, where a finite-range force is consistently employed. In all but the heaviest nuclei, the resulting half-lives are usually shorter by factors of 2 to 5 than those of calculations that ignore the proton-neutron particle-particle interaction. The shorter half-lives alter predictions for the abundance distribution of r-process elements and for the time it takes to synthesize them.

## Key claim

Beta-decay half-lives for spherical neutron-rich r-process waiting-point nuclei are calculated using a fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) based on Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) theory, where the same Skyrme force is used in the mean-field and QRPA except in the proton-neutron particle-particle channel. The resulting half-lives are usually shorter by factors of 2 to 5 than calculations that ignore the proton-neutron particle-particle interaction, but this result is conditional on the choice of Skyrme force (SkO') and the adjustment of one parameter (the strength of the T=0 pairing interaction) to reproduce measured decay rates near closed neutron shells.

## Method

The authors formulated a fully self-consistent QRPA in the HFB canonical single-particle basis, using the Skyrme energy-density functional SkO' for the particle-hole channel and a finite-range force for the proton-neutron particle-particle (T=0 pairing) channel. They solved the coordinate-space HFB equations to obtain the canonical basis and occupation amplitudes, then constructed and diagonalized the QRPA matrices to compute Gamow-Teller transition strengths and beta-decay half-lives for spherical even-even semimagic nuclei along the r-process path.

## Key numbers

- Half-lives are shorter by factors of 2 to 5 compared to calculations that ignore the proton-neutron particle-particle interaction, for all but the heaviest nuclei
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g0' for the SkO' force is 0.792, compared to the empirical value of approximately 1.80
- The neutron-hydrogen mass difference used in Q-value calculations is Delta M_{n-H} = 0.78227 MeV
- The weak axial nucleon coupling constant g_A is set to 1 (rather than 1.26) to account for quenching of isovector spin matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent approach of Borzov et al. (1995, 1996), who used an energy-density functional with a phenomenological spin-dependent residual interaction but did not achieve full self-consistency between the mean-field and QRPA. It contrasts with global but non-self-consistent calculations by Moller et al. (1997), Homma et al. (1996), and Hirata et al. (1997) that used the Strutinsky method with BCS and a schematic interaction. The shorter half-lives alter predictions for the r-process abundance distribution and synthesis timescale, enabling more reliable estimates for the crucial waiting-point nuclei with closed neutron shells (N=50, 82, 126).
