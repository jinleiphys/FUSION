# First observation of multi-phonon $γ$-vibrations in an odd-odd nuclear system

> The identification of the first multi-phonon $γ$-vibrational bands in an odd-odd neutron-rich nucleus of the nuclear chart is presented. These high spin structures of hard to access $^{104}_{41}$Nb$_{63}$, produced in fission, were studied by combining a spectrometer with isotopic resolution coupled to a $γ$-ray tracking array and independently high-fold $γ$ coincidence measurements. Triaxial Projected Shell Model calculations for the high-spin states are in good agreement with the measured observables for the yrast, one-phonon and two-phonon $γ$ bands. The possibility of an oblate shape of an isomeric state and coexistence of triaxial and oblate configurations are investigated from the decay of the 141 keV isomer. The present work illustrates the robustness of vibration excitations in the presence of odd valence proton and neutron as well as the possibly coexisting shapes beyond the $N=60$ transitional region.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first identification of multi-phonon gamma-vibrational bands in an odd-odd nucleus, specifically in neutron-rich $^{104}$Nb, with the caveat that the assignment relies on the bands having band-head spins (K=7 and K=9) too high to be generated by two-quasiparticle configurations and on the good agreement with Triaxial Projected Shell Model calculations, rather than on direct measurement of the phonon character.

## Method

The $^{104}$Nb nuclei were produced via spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf and via fission using a 6.2 MeV/u $^{238}$U beam on a $^{9}$Be target. The first measurement used the Gammasphere array with 101 HPGe detectors at LBNL, sorting data into three- and four-fold gamma-ray coincidence matrices. The second measurement at GANIL combined the VAMOS++ spectrometer for A and Z identification of fission fragments with the AGATA gamma-ray tracking array for prompt gamma detection and the EXOGAM array for delayed gamma rays (100 ns to 200 microsecond range). Angular correlations from high-statistics triple and four-fold coincidences were used to assign spins and parities, and Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) calculations were performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

* The gamma-phonon energy ratio $E_{2\gamma}/E_{1\gamma}$ in $^{104}$Nb is 1.81, compared to 1.79 in $^{103}$Nb and 1.97 in $^{105}$Nb.
* Inertia parameters extracted from level energies: A = 13.7 for the 0-gamma band, 13.9 for the 1-gamma band, and 14.5 for the 2-gamma band in $^{104}$Nb.
* Angular correlation coefficients for the 656-532 keV cascade (band 2-3) are $A_2 = 0.09(3)$ and $A_4 = 0.02(5)$, matching calculated Q-Q values of $A_2 = 0.10$ and $A_4 = 0.01$.
* The half-life of the 141 keV isomer was measured as 66(6) ns.
* The deduced B(M1) for the 141 keV transition is $1.2 \times 10^{-4}$ W.u. and B(E1) is $1.7 \times 10^{-6}$ W.u., at least an order of magnitude lower than interband transitions in $^{101,103,105}$Nb and $^{105}$Mo.
* The average $|(g_K - g_R)/Q_0|$ values for bands (1), (2), and (3) are 0.079(3), 0.079(9), and 0.083(9) b$^{-1}$, respectively.
* The mixing ratio for the 185 keV transition is $\delta(E2/M1) = 0.34(7)$, yielding $(g_K - g_R)/Q_0 = 0.074(16)$.

## Context

This work extends the study of multi-phonon gamma-vibrational bands, previously observed only in even-even and odd-mass nuclei (e.g., $^{168}$Er by Borggreen et al. (1991), $^{106}$Mo by Gueorguieva et al. (1995), and in odd-mass Nb isotopes by Fry et al. (2017)), to the odd-odd system $^{104}$Nb. The identification was enabled by combining complementary spectroscopic techniques (Gammasphere and VAMOS++/AGATA/EXOGAM) and is supported by TPSM calculations. The results enable future searches for multi-phonon bands in odd-odd nuclei in the A~130 and A~230 mass regions, and the proposed oblate shape isomer at 141 keV suggests shape coexistence beyond the N=60 transitional region, motivating further studies of valence nucleon effects on collective vibrations.
