# Bremsstrahlung in $α$ Decay

> A quantum mechanical analysis of the bremsstrahlung in $α$ decay of $^{210}$Po is performed in close reference to a semiclassical theory. We clarify the contribution from the tunneling, mixed, outside barrier regions and from the wall of the inner potential well to the final spectral distribution, and discuss their interplay. We also comment on the validity of semiclassical calculations, and the possibility to eliminate the ambiguity in the nuclear potential between the alpha particle and daughter nucleus using the bremsstrahlung spectrum.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung spectrum in alpha decay results from a subtle interference among contributions from the tunneling, mixed, and outside-barrier regions plus the wall of the inner potential well, each of comparable magnitude; semiclassical and classical theories are not reliable for describing these interference effects quantitatively, though they provide physical insight.

## Method

The authors performed a quantum mechanical analysis of bremsstrahlung in the alpha decay of 210Po using Fermi's golden rule in the dipole approximation, with a simple square-well plus Coulomb potential for the alpha-daughter interaction. They divided the spatial integration into tunneling, mixed, and outside regions defined by the classical turning points of the initial and final states, and compared the exact quantum mechanical results with semiclassical approximations derived from WKB wave functions. The potential parameters were determined by matching wave functions at the nuclear surface and reproducing the decay width.

## Key numbers

- The alpha decay energy of 210Po is E_alpha = 5.3 MeV.
- One potential parameter set used was V_0 = 21.37 MeV and r_0 = 8.055 fm, giving 5 nodes in the radial wave function.
- Another potential set was V_0 = 107.3 MeV and r_0 = 7.947 fm, corresponding to n = 11 nodes.
- The coefficient C_alpha gradually decreases from 0.041 to -0.055 for the shallow potential, and from -0.25 to -0.28 for the deep potential, as E_gamma changes from 0 to 600 keV.
- The spectral distribution is almost indistinguishable for potentials with n <= 7 nodes, but for larger n the soft photon emission (below 100 keV) becomes recognizably larger than the classical prediction.

## Context

This work extends the quantum mechanical calculation of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1996) for bremsstrahlung in alpha decay, and addresses the discrepancy between their monotonic spectrum and the semiclassical calculations of Kasagi et al. (1997) which showed a hump. The analysis clarifies that the wall contribution, overlooked by Kasagi et al., is a key issue in resolving this discrepancy. The results suggest that extending bremsstrahlung measurements to lower photon energies could provide a stringent test of the nuclear potential between the alpha particle and daughter nucleus, and potentially discriminate between shallow and deep potential wells.
