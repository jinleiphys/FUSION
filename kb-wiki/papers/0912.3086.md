# Symbolic computation of the Hartree-Fock energy from a chiral EFT three-nucleon interaction at N$^2$LO

> We present the first of a two-part Mathematica notebook collection that implements a symbolic approach for the application of the density matrix expansion (DME) to the Hartree-Fock (HF) energy from a chiral effective field theory (EFT) three-nucleon interaction at N$^2$LO. The final output from the notebooks is a Skyrme-like energy density functional that provides a quasi-local approximation to the nonlocal HF energy. In this paper, we discuss the derivation of the HF energy and its simplification in terms of the scalar/vector-isoscalar/isovector parts of the one-body density matrix. Furthermore, a set of steps is described and illustrated on how to extend the approach to other three-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a symbolic Mathematica implementation for computing the Hartree-Fock energy from a chiral effective field theory three-nucleon interaction at N$^2$LO, but only for the first part of a two-part project: the derivation and simplification of the HF energy in terms of scalar/vector-isoscalar/isovector parts of the one-body density matrix, with the actual density matrix expansion to produce a Skyrme-like functional deferred to a subsequent publication. The authors note that the regulator function can be neglected only under the condition that the local Fermi momentum is much smaller than the cutoff parameter employed in the interaction.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral EFT three-nucleon interaction at N$^2$LO, which has three components (E-term, D-term, and C-term), and express the HF energy in terms of one of the three $\hat{V}_{ij}$ operators using exchange operators. They perform an inverse-Fourier transformation to express the matrix elements in coordinate space, then rewrite the direct, single-exchange, and double-exchange parts of the HF energy in terms of auxiliary density matrices. The Mathematica implementation performs automated spin-isospin tracing operations by expressing the auxiliary density matrices in terms of their scalar/vector-isoscalar/isovector components and grouping spin-isospin operators by particle identity.

## Key numbers

- The Mathematica notebooks require 256 Mb of RAM and run for several hours on any computer running Mathematica 6.0 or later versions
- The three-nucleon interaction is treated at N$^2$LO (next-to-next-to-leading order) in chiral effective field theory
- The E-term coupling constant is defined as $E \equiv C_E/(f^4_\pi \Lambda_\chi)$, where $f_\pi$ is the pion decay constant and $\Lambda_\chi$ is the chiral symmetry breaking scale
- The D-term coupling involves the factor $-g_A/(4 f^2_\pi) \cdot C_D/(f^2_\pi \Lambda_\chi)$ where $g_A$ is the axial coupling constant
- The C-term coupling involves the factor $(g_A/(2 f_\pi))^2$ and the low-energy constants $c_1$, $c_3$, and $c_4$

## Context

This work extends the program of constructing microscopically-based nuclear energy density functionals from chiral effective field theory interactions, following the density matrix expansion method of Negele and Vautherin (1972) and earlier work by the same authors (Gebremariam, Bogner, and Duguet, 2009). The approach is motivated by the need to go beyond phenomenologically adjusted Skyrme and Gogny functionals (Bender, 2003) which lack predictive power away from fitted regions. The symbolic computation enables the handling of the tremendous spin-isospin algebra that makes manual calculation impractical for three-nucleon interactions, and the resulting notebooks can be extended to other three-nucleon interactions and to relax the isospin symmetry imposed in the present work.
