# Transverse Momentum Distributions from Lattice QCD without Wilson Lines

> The transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs), which are defined by gauge-invariant 3D parton correlators with staple-shaped lightlike Wilson lines, can be calculated from quark and gluon correlators fixed in the Coulomb gauge on a Euclidean lattice. These quantities can be expressed gauge-invariantly as the correlators of Coulomb-gauge-dressed fields, which reduce to the standard TMD correlators under principal-value prescription in the infinite boost limit. In the framework of Large-Momentum Effective Theory, a quasi-TMD defined from such correlators in a large-momentum hadron state can be matched to the TMD via a factorization formula, whose exact form is derived using Soft Collinear Effective Theory and verified at one-loop order. Compared to the currently used gauge-invariant correlators, this new method can substantially improve statistical precision and simplify renormalization for the time-reversal-even TMDs, which will greatly enhance the predicative power of lattice QCD in the non-perturbative region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) can be calculated from quark and gluon correlators fixed in the Coulomb gauge on a Euclidean lattice, without using staple-shaped Wilson lines, and that this method can substantially improve statistical precision and simplify renormalization for time-reversal-even TMDs, though it does not yet solve the problem for time-reversal-odd TMDs.

## Method

The author defines a quasi-TMD using equal-time correlators of quark fields in a large-momentum hadron state, with the Coulomb gauge condition fixed to eliminate Wilson lines. Using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), a factorization formula is derived that relates this Coulomb-gauge quasi-TMD to the standard TMD, and the matching coefficient is calculated at one-loop order in perturbation theory. The quasi soft function needed for the factorization is extracted from a meson form factor and the Coulomb-gauge quasi-TMD wave function.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop matching coefficient for the quasi-TMD is: \( C(p^z/\mu) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi}\left[-\frac{L_p^2}{2} - 3L_p - 12 + \frac{7\pi^2}{12}\right] \), where \( L_p = \ln(\mu^2/(4p_z^2)) \), \( \alpha_s = g^2/(4\pi) \), and \( C_F = 4/3 \).
- The one-loop quasi soft function is: \( \tilde S_C^{(1)}(b_\perp, \mu, y_n) = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} (1-2y_n) L_b \), where \( L_b = \ln(\mu^2 b_\perp^2 e^{2\gamma_E}/4) \).
- The one-loop quasi-TMD for a free massless quark target is: \( \tilde B^{(1)}(x, b_\perp,\mu, p^z,\eps) = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left[-\frac{1+x^2}{1-x}\left(\frac{1}{\eps}+ L_b\right) + (1-x) \right]_+^{(0,1)} + \delta(1-x)\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi}\left[ \frac{5}{2}L_b - 3L_p - \frac{(L_b-L_p)^2}{2} - \frac{23}{2} + \frac{\pi^2}{2}\right] \).

## Context

This work extends the Large-Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET) approach to TMDs, which was pioneered by Ji (2013) and developed further by Ebert, Ji, and others (2018-2022), by replacing the staple-shaped Wilson lines with Coulomb gauge fixing. The method is inspired by the author's previous proposal for PDFs (Gao, 2023) and aims to overcome the statistical precision limitations encountered in recent lattice calculations of the TMD evolution kernel (Avkhadiev, 2024), where errors became too large at transverse momenta below approximately (0.8 fm)^{-1}. This approach enables access to the deep non-perturbative region for time-reversal-even TMDs and simplifies renormalization by eliminating linear power divergences, operator mixings, and power corrections associated with Wilson lines.
