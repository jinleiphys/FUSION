# Application of Coulomb energy density functional for atomic nuclei: Case studies of local density approximation and generalized gradient approximation

> We test the Coulomb exchange and correlation energy density functionals of electron systems for atomic nuclei in the local density approximation (LDA) and the generalized gradient approximation (GGA). For the exchange Coulomb energies, it is found that the deviation between the LDA and GGA ranges from around $ 11 \, \% $ in $ {}^{4} \mathrm{He} $ to around $ 2.2 \, \% $ in $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $, by taking the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) functional as an example of the GGA\@. For the correlation Coulomb energies, it is shown that those functionals of electron systems are not suitable for atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether Coulomb exchange and correlation energy density functionals developed for electron systems can be applied to atomic nuclei, finding that the exchange functionals (especially GGA) give reasonable results but the correlation functionals are fundamentally unsuitable because the nuclear force dominates the correlation effects in nuclei.

## Method

The authors used experimentally measured charge-density distributions from sum-of-Gaussian analysis (Vries 1987) as inputs for selected nuclei, then computed the Coulomb exchange and correlation energies using LDA and several GGA functionals (B88, PW91, PBE, PBEsol). For the exchange energy, they compared the LDA and GGA results and calculated the deviation between them; for the correlation energy, they evaluated the LDA correlation functional and compared the ratio of correlation to exchange energy against theoretical expectations for nuclear systems.

## Key numbers

- Deviation between LDA and GGA exchange energies (PBE functional): 11% in 4He, 6.0% in 16O, and 2.2% in 208Pb
- GGA exchange energy enhancements relative to LDA for 208Pb: PW91 gives 2.3%, PBE gives 2.2%, PBEsol gives 1.4%
- Ratio of correlation energy to exchange energy in LDA: 1.996% for 4He, 1.823% for 208Pb, ranging from about 2.0% in light nuclei to about 1.8% in heavy nuclei
- Direct Coulomb energy for 208Pb: 808.5 MeV; exchange energy (LDA): -30.31 MeV; correlation energy (LDA): -0.5524 MeV
- Exchange energy for 4He: LDA gives -0.6494 MeV, PBE gives -0.7281 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of electron-system density functionals to nuclear Coulomb energies, following the Hohenberg-Kohn-Sham DFT framework (Hohenberg 1964, Kohn 1965). The GGA exchange results show similar behavior to the deviation between Hartree-Fock-Slater and exact Hartree-Fock calculations reported by Le Bloas (2011), suggesting the GGA exchange functionals are valid for nuclei. The correlation functionals are found unsuitable because, as argued by Bulgac and Shaginyan (1996, 1999), the nuclear force dominates the correlation response, making the electron-system correlation functionals inapplicable. This work enables future self-consistent DFT calculations with local GGA Coulomb potentials at O(N^3) cost instead of the O(N^4) cost of exact Hartree-Fock, though issues of double-counting correlation and proton finite-size effects remain open.
