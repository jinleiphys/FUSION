# Skyrme forces with extended density dependence

> A generalized parameterization of the Skyrme effective force is discussed. Preliminary results are presented for infinite symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. In particular, it is shown that an enlarged density dependence based on two terms allows to choose independently the incompressibility and the isoscalar effective mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that extending the Skyrme effective force to include two density-dependent terms (rather than the standard single term) allows the incompressibility and the isoscalar effective mass in infinite nuclear matter to be chosen independently, removing a constraint that previously forced a specific relationship between these two quantities.

## Method

The authors generalized the Skyrme parametrization by adding a second density-dependent term of the form ρ^(i/3) to the standard single-term form, then adjusted the parameters to reproduce fixed saturation properties (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, E/A = -16 MeV) while independently setting K_∞ = 230 MeV and m*/m = 0.8. They fitted the remaining parameters to the neutron matter equation of state from Akmal et al. (1998) and computed the energy per particle for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter over a range of densities up to ~3ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and energy per particle E/A = -16 MeV were used as fixed constraints for infinite symmetric nuclear matter.
- Incompressibility K_∞ = 230 MeV and isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.8 were chosen independently for the generalized forces [0,1,2], [0,1,3], and [0,2,3].
- The equations of state were compared to the variational results of Akmal et al. (1998) for densities from 0 to approximately 3ρ₀ (i.e., up to ~0.48 fm⁻³).
- Standard Skyrme forces with α ≈ 1 (like SIII) cannot reach a correct incompressibility; only α values from 1/6 to 1/3 allow an acceptable set {m*/m, K_∞}.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme parametrization (Vautherin and Brink, 1972) which used a single density-dependent term, and addresses the known correlation between incompressibility and effective mass discussed by Chabanat et al. (1998) for the Sly4 family. The motivation comes from beyond-mean-field calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method and Projected Mean Field Method (Duguet, 2002; Duguet, 2003), where two distinct density dependences arise from three-body force effects and Brueckner correlations respectively. The new parametrization enables construction of Skyrme-like forces that avoid collapse at relevant densities and can be extrapolated reliably to beyond-mean-field calculations.
