# Gorkov algebraic diagrammatic construction for infinite nuclear matter

> We propose a novel many-body truncation for Gorkov self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory where pairing correlations are handled at first order, while dynamical correlations are described using the particle-number-conserving Dyson-SCGF scheme up to third order in the algebraic diagrammatic construction. The new method is enabled by the introduction of a scheme that allows to approximate the Gorkov propagator in terms of a particle-number-conserving optimized reference state. The approach provides state-of-the-art predictions of the equation of state and spectral properties of infinite nuclear matter at zero temperature and in the presence of pairing. We find satisfactory results using modern saturating Hamiltonians at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid many-body truncation for Gorkov self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory where pairing correlations are handled only at first order (static mean-field level), while dynamical correlations are described using the particle-number-conserving Dyson-SCGF scheme up to third order in the algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)], with the caveat that the dynamical dependence of the anomalous self-energies is neglected entirely.

## Method

The authors simulate infinite nuclear matter as a finite system of nucleons in a cubic box with twisted-angle boundary conditions, using a momentum basis truncated by a maximum momentum cutoff. They construct a hybrid Gorkov-Dyson self-energy where the static self-energy (including the anomalous pairing field) is computed in full Gorkov formalism, but the dynamical self-energy is restricted to the normal (particle-number-conserving) Dyson-ADC(3) expansion. Three-body interactions are incorporated via the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation, and the method is further improved by combining ADC amplitudes with amplitudes determined from coupled-cluster computations to include higher-order correlations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (used as reference value from literature)
- Finite-temperature SCGF calculations are performed at temperatures \(\ge 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to avoid pairing instability (contrasted with the present method's stability)
- Particle numbers used for periodic boundary condition simulations: \(N = 66\) neutrons for pure neutron matter and \(A = 132\) nucleons for symmetric nuclear matter
- Momentum basis truncation condition: \(|\mathbf{k}|^2 \le (4\pi^2/L^2) N_{max}\)
- Shell closures in momentum space occur at particle numbers \(A/g = 1, 7, 19, 27, 33\) etc., where \(g\) is the spin-isospin degeneracy (2 for spin-saturated PNM, 4 for spin-saturated SNM)

## Context

This work extends the Gorkov-ADC formalism previously developed for finite open-shell nuclei (Soma 2011, Soma 2014, Barbieri 2022) to infinite nuclear matter, where earlier exploratory ADC(3) computations based on Dyson theory were unstable to pairing effects at low densities. The approach avoids the pairing instability that forces finite-temperature SCGF calculations (Carbone 2013, Rios 2020) to use temperatures above 5 MeV, and it overcomes convergence failures of coupled-cluster theory in low-density neutron matter. The method enables state-of-the-art predictions of both the equation of state and single-particle properties (momentum distributions, spectral functions) for nuclear matter with modern chiral effective field theory interactions.
