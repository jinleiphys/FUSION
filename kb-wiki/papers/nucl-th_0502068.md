# A Shell Model Description of the Decay Out of the Super-Deformed Band of 36Ar

> Large scale shell model calculations in two major oscillator shells (sd and pf) describe simultaneously the super-deformed excited band of 36Ar and its low-lying states of dominant sd character. In addition, several two particle two hole states and a side band of negative parity are also well reproduced. We explain the appearance of the super-deformed band at such low excitation energy as a consequence of the very large correlation energy of the configurations with many particles and many holes (np-nh) relative to the normal filling of the spherical mean field orbits (0p-0h). We study the mechanism of mixing between these different configurations, to understand why the super-deformed band survives and how it finally decays into the low-lying sd-dominated states via the indirect mixing of the 0p-0h and 4p-4h configurations.

## Key claim

The super-deformed band in 36Ar appears at low excitation energy because the 4p-4h configuration gains roughly 15 MeV more correlation energy than the spherical 0p-0h ground state, but the band survives and decays out only because mixing between the spherical 2p-2h states and the super-deformed 4p-4h states is severely hindered, forcing the decay to proceed via small 0p-0h and 2p-2h components.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations in a valence space spanning the sd and pf major oscillator shells (excluding the 1d5/2 orbit to control center-of-mass contamination), using the SDPF.SM effective interaction with modifications to account for the truncated space. They first diagonalized the Hamiltonian in fixed np-nh spaces (0p-0h, 2p-2h, 4p-4h, 6p-6h) to determine the relative energies and correlation energies of each configuration, then performed a fully mixed diagonalization in the full valence space using the shell model code ANTOINE.

## Key numbers

- The 4p-4h band-head is calculated at 4319 keV excitation energy, compared to the experimental value of 4329 keV.
- The 4p-4h configuration lies only 12 MeV above the 0p-0h configuration at the uncorrelated Slater determinant level, rather than the naive estimate of 28 MeV (4 times the ~7 MeV quasi-particle gap).
- The correlation energy of the 4p-4h super-deformed band exceeds that of the spherical ground state by 15 MeV, representing 5% of the total binding energy.
- The 0p-0h dominated ground state gains 5 MeV from mixing, while the 4p-4h dominated band-head gains 2.5 MeV.
- The calculated B(E2) for the 2+_SD → 0+_1 transition is 4.0 e²fm⁴ (experiment: 4.6(23) e²fm⁴); for the 4+_SD → 2+_2 transition it is 18.4 e²fm⁴ (experiment: 19.2(30) e²fm⁴).
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment corresponds to a deformation β = 0.5, justifying the term "super-deformed."
- Opening the 1d5/2 orbit increases the B(E2) values of the 4p-4h band by 12%.

## Context

This work extends the shell model description of super-deformed bands in medium-light nuclei, following experimental discoveries in 36Ar (Svensson et al., 2001) and similar bands in 56Ni and 40Ca. The authors compare their results to mean-field calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method (Bender et al., 2003) and the Angular Momentum Projected Generator Coordinate Method (Rayner et al., 2003), noting that those approaches struggle with proton-neutron pairing and produce moments of inertia that are either too large or too small for the wrong reasons. The calculation simultaneously reproduces the spherical ground state, the super-deformed band, and a negative parity side band with a 3- band-head near 4 MeV, demonstrating that multiple coexisting nuclear structures can be described within a single shell model framework.
