# Parity projection of QCD sum rules for the nucleon

> The nucleon and its negative-parity excited states are examined in a maximum entropy method analysis of QCD sum rules. First, we rederive the parity projected sum rules for baryons using forward correlation functions. Doing this, the method is generalized so that higher order operator product expansion (OPE) terms can be calculated unambiguously. We then apply this approach to the nucleon channel taking into account all known first order alpha_s corrections to the Wilson coefficients of the OPE. As these corrections have turned out to be large, we suppress them by using a phase-rotated Gaussian kernel. Simultaneously, this phase rotation strongly suppresses the continuum contribution and improves the convergence of the OPE. The resulting sum rule has the interesting feature that it is dominated by the term containing the chiral condensate of dimension 3. Analyzing this sum rule by the maximum entropy method, we are able to extract information of both the positive and negative parity states.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by using a phase-rotated Gaussian kernel with a specific rotation angle (θ = 0.108π), they can suppress the large first-order α_s corrections to the perturbative term and the continuum contribution in parity-projected QCD sum rules for the nucleon, making the sum rule dominated by the chiral condensate term and thus suitable for extracting both positive and negative parity states via the maximum entropy method.

## Method

The authors rederive parity-projected sum rules for baryons starting from the "old-fashioned" (forward) correlation function rather than the time-ordered correlator, resolving ambiguities in higher-order operator product expansion (OPE) terms pointed out by Kondo et al. (2006). They then construct the parity-projected nucleon sum rule including all known first-order α_s corrections to the Wilson coefficients, using a phase-rotated Gaussian kernel W(q_0,θ) with θ = 0.108π to suppress the α_s corrections and continuum contributions. The resulting sum rule is analyzed using the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract spectral functions for both positive and negative parity states without assuming a "pole + continuum" form.

## Key numbers

- The phase rotation angle is set to θ = 0.108π, chosen so that the term (71/12) and 2θ tan(5θ) in the α_s correction coefficient C_{0α_s} approximately cancel.
- At θ = 0.108π and τ = 0.5 GeV^4, the α_s correction to the dimension-0 term is reduced to about 5% of the leading-order term (compared to ~90% at θ = 0 for α_s = 0.5).
- The vacuum condensate values used are: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.24 ± 0.01)^3 GeV^3, ⟨(α_s/π)G^2⟩ = 0.012 ± 0.0036 GeV^4, and ⟨q̄gσ·Gq⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ = 0.8 ± 0.2 GeV^2, taken from Colangelo (2000).
- The renormalization scale μ is set to 1 GeV, with α_s = 0.5 at that scale, following Bethke (2007).

## Context

This work extends the parity-projected sum rule approach of Jido et al. (2001) by including first-order α_s corrections and resolving technical ambiguities in the treatment of the old-fashioned correlator that were pointed out by Kondo et al. (2006). It also builds on the authors' previous MEM analysis of the nucleon channel (Ohtani, 2011) and on the phase-rotated kernel method introduced by Ioffe and Zyablyuk (2003) for suppressing large α_s corrections. The resulting sum rule, dominated by the chiral condensate term, is expected to enable future studies of the relation between chiral symmetry breaking and the mass splitting of positive and negative parity nucleon states.
