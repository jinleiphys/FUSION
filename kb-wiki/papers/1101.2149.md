# Neutrinoless double beta decay of deformed nuclei within QRPA with realistic interaction

> In this paper a microscopic state-of-the-art approach to calculation of the nuclear matrix element $M^{0ν}$ for neutrinoless double beta decay with an account for nuclear deformation is presented in length and applied for $^{76}$Ge, $^{150}$Nd and $^{160}$Gd. The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a realistic residual interaction [the Brueckner $G$ matrix derived from the charge-depending Bonn (Bonn-CD) nucleon-nucleon potential] is used as the underlying nuclear structure model. The effects of the short range correlations and the quenching of the axial vector coupling constant $g_A$ are analyzed. The results show that neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{150}$Nd, to be measured soon by the SNO+ collaboration, provides one of the best probes of the Majorana neutrino mass. This confirms our preliminary conclusion in Ref. {Fang10}.

## Key claim

The paper presents a detailed QRPA calculation of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element for deformed nuclei, finding that the previously reported suppression of the matrix element for 150Nd by about 40% relative to spherical calculations is confirmed, but the final result depends significantly on the BCS overlap factor between initial and final ground states and on the quenching of the axial coupling constant gA.

## Method

The authors solve the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in the intrinsic coordinate system of axially deformed nuclei, using the adiabatic Bohr-Mottelson approximation. The residual interaction is the Brueckner G matrix derived from the charge-dependent Bonn (Bonn-CD) nucleon-nucleon potential, and the single-particle basis is obtained from a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field with quadrupole deformation parameters fitted to experimental quadrupole moments. The nuclear matrix element M0ν is constructed by summing partial contributions from all intermediate Kπ states, with the overlap between initial and final QRPA states and BCS vacua included.

## Key numbers

- For 150Nd → 150Sm with deformation and gA = 1.25, the total NME M'0ν = 4.00 (with quenched gA = 0.94) and 4.69 (with bare gA = 1.25), compared to the spherical limit value of 5.30 (gA = 1.25)
- The BCS overlap factor between initial and final vacua for 150Nd (150Sm) is 0.52 with deformation, versus 0.85 in the spherical limit
- For 76Ge → 76Se with deformation and gA = 1.25, M'0ν = 4.69, with a corresponding half-life T0ν1/2 = 7.15 × 1026 years assuming mββ = 50 meV
- The deformation parameters used are β2 = 0.240 for 150Nd and β2 = 0.153 for 150Sm; for 76Ge β2 = 0.10 and for 76Se β2 = 0.16
- The particle-particle strength parameter gpp fitted to reproduce the experimental 2νββ NME is 1.05 for 150Nd (case I, gA = 1.25) and 1.00 (case II, gA = 0.94)

## Context

This work extends the preliminary QRPA calculation of Fang (2010) for 150Nd to include 76Ge and 160Gd, providing full details of the deformed QRPA formalism with realistic interactions. The results contradict the earlier spherical QRPA calculation of Rodin (2005) which gave M0ν = 4.74 for 150Nd without deformation, and show a suppression consistent with more phenomenological approaches like the pseudo-SU(3) model (Hirsch, 1995), the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach (Hirsch, 2008), and the interacting boson model (Barea, 2009). The calculation enables more reliable interpretation of the upcoming SNO+ experiment, which is expected to achieve sensitivity to the effective Majorana neutrino mass in the inverse hierarchy region.
