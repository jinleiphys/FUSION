# Spectral ratio: an observable to determine $K^{+}$ nucleus potential and $K^{+}$ N scattering cross section

> Here we aim to show that the ratio of the momentum spectra of $K^{+}$ at small transverse momentum measured for symmetric systems of different sizes can be such an observable.

## Key claim

The ratio of transverse momentum spectra of \(K^{+}\) mesons at small transverse momentum, measured for symmetric systems of different sizes, can separately determine the \(K^{+}\) nucleus potential (using light systems like C+C vs. Ar+Ar) and the in-medium \(K^{+}N\) scattering cross section (using heavier systems like Ar+Ar vs. Au+Au), provided the systems are chosen to exploit the different density and rescattering regimes.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate symmetric heavy-ion collisions at 1.25 AGeV incident energy for systems of varying mass (C+C, Ar+Ar, Au+Au). They computed the ratio of transverse momentum spectra near midrapidity for pairs of systems, varying the rescattering cross section by a factor between 0.5 and 2 and the strength of the \(K^{+}\) nucleus potential by a factor \(\alpha\), to isolate the sensitivity of each ratio to these two quantities.

## Key numbers

- The maximum central density in symmetric \(A+A\) reactions at 1.25 AGeV rises strongly up to about \(A=40\) and then only slightly for higher masses.
- In the Ar+Ar system, the central density exceeds twice normal nuclear matter density, whereas in C+C the central density does not exceed much the normal nuclear matter density.
- The mean number of collisions a \(K^{+}\) suffers before leaving the system rises moderately with \(A\) up to \(A=40\) and then increases much stronger for heavier systems.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing theoretical effort to determine meson properties in dense hadronic matter, where predictions from different groups differ substantially due to poorly known in-medium coupling constants and dressing effects (Aichelin 1986, Hartnack physics reports). The proposed spectral ratio observable enables experimental extraction of the \(K^{+}\) nucleus potential from light-system comparisons and the \(K^{+}N\) scattering cross section from heavy-system comparisons, using present-day experimental precision. This approach avoids the need for absolute yield measurements and can help resolve theoretical uncertainties in coupled-channels calculations of kaon in-medium properties.
