# Effect of a magnetic field on the thermodynamic properties of a high-temperature hadron resonance gas with van der Waals interactions

> We study the behavior of a hadronic matter in the presence of an external magnetic field within the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model, considering both attractive and repulsive interactions among the hadrons. Various thermodynamic quantities like pressure ($P$), energy density ($\varepsilon$), magnetization ($\mathcal{M}$), entropy density ($s$), squared speed of sound ($c_{\rm s}^{2}$), and specific-heat capacity at constant volume ($c_{v}$) are calculated as functions of temperature ($T$) and static finite magnetic field ($eB$). We also consider the effect of baryochemical potential ($μ_{B}$) on the above-mentioned thermodynamic observables in the presence of a magnetic field. Further, we estimate the magnetic susceptibility ($χ_{\rm M}^{2}$), relative permeability ($μ_{\rm r}$), and electrical susceptibility ($χ_{\rm Q}^{2}$) which can help us to understand the system better. Through this model, we quantify a liquid-gas phase transition in the T-eB-$μ_B$ phase space.

## Key claim

The van der Waals hadron resonance gas model, which includes both attractive and repulsive interactions among hadrons, predicts a liquid-gas phase transition in the three-dimensional phase space of temperature, magnetic field strength, and baryochemical potential, extending the known critical point behavior beyond the T-μB plane.

## Method

The authors extended the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model to include an external static magnetic field by modifying the single-particle energy for charged hadrons to include Landau level quantization, replacing the three-dimensional momentum integral with a one-dimensional integral summed over Landau levels and spin projections. They calculated thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, entropy density, magnetization, squared speed of sound, and specific heat capacity as functions of temperature, magnetic field strength, and baryochemical potential, using van der Waals parameters a = 0.926 GeV fm³ and hard-core radii rM = 0.2 fm for mesons and rB = 0.62 fm for baryons and antibaryons. The vacuum contribution to pressure in the magnetic field was regularized using dimensional regularization and renormalization of the electric charge and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- The van der Waals parameters used are a = 0.926 GeV fm³ for the attractive interaction and b = (16/3)πr³ with hard-core radii rM = 0.2 fm for mesons and rB = 0.62 fm for baryons and antibaryons.
- The magnetic field strength considered reaches up to 15 mπ² at LHC energies, with mπ² ≈ 10¹⁸ G.
- The ideal hadron resonance gas model agrees with lattice QCD results for temperatures up to T ≈ 140-150 MeV at zero baryochemical potential.
- The van der Waals hadron resonance gas model explains lattice QCD data up to T ≈ 180 MeV.
- A critical point in the T-μB plane is expected at T ≈ 67 MeV and μB ≈ 700 MeV from prior van der Waals hadron resonance gas studies.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hadron resonance gas models in magnetic fields, which used ideal or excluded-volume formulations (Endrodi 2013, Kadam 2019), by incorporating van der Waals interactions that include both attractive and repulsive components. The van der Waals hadron resonance gas model was previously shown to explain lattice QCD data up to higher temperatures than the ideal model (Samanta 2017, Vovchenko 2015, Sarkar 2018), and this study adds the magnetic field dimension to explore the liquid-gas phase transition in the T-eB-μB phase space, which is relevant for understanding matter in peripheral heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
