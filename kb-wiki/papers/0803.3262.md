# QCD sum rules for rho mesons in vacuum and in-medium, re-examined

> An updated investigation of QCD sum rules for the first two moments of rho meson spectral functions, both in vacuum and in-medium, is performed with emphasis on the role of the scale related to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. It is demonstrated that these lowest moments of vector current spectral distributions do permit an accurate sum rule analysis with controlled input including QCD condensates of the lowest dimensions, whereas higher moments are subject to uncertainties from higher dimensional condensates. Possible connections with Brown-Rho scaling are discussed. The factorization approximation for four-quark condensates is shown not to be applicable in any of the cases studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first two moments of the rho meson spectral function permit an accurate QCD sum rule analysis with controlled input from low-dimensional condensates, but only if one abandons the debate over "mass shift versus broadening" and instead focuses on spectral moments; the factorization approximation for four-quark condensates is shown to be inapplicable in all cases studied.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule analysis of the first two moments (zeroth and first) of the rho meson spectral function, both in vacuum and at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}), using finite energy sum rules with a Borel transformation. They test two complementary in-medium spectral functions: one from a chiral effective Lagrangian with vector meson couplings (KKW) and one emphasizing particle-hole excitations including baryon resonances (RW). The continuum threshold scale s_0 is determined self-consistently from the sum rules and identified with the chiral symmetry breaking scale 4πf_π.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum continuum threshold: sqrt(s_0) = 1.13 ± 0.02 GeV from zeroth moment, 1.14 ± 0.01 GeV from first moment, consistent with 4πf_π ≈ 1.16 GeV using f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Vacuum average rho mass from moment ratio: m_bar_ρ = 0.78 ± 0.01 GeV (m_bar_ρ^2 = 0.611 ± 0.013 GeV^2)
- In-medium continuum threshold for KKW spectral function at ρ_0: sqrt(s_0^*) = 1.00 ± 0.02 GeV
- In-medium average mass for KKW at ρ_0: m_bar^* = 0.67 ± 0.02 GeV, giving m_bar^*/m_bar_ρ(vac) = 0.85 ± 0.02
- In-medium continuum threshold for RW spectral function at ρ_0: sqrt(s_0^*) = 1.09 ± 0.01 GeV, giving m_bar^*/m_bar_ρ(vac) = 0.96 ± 0.02
- Running coupling used: α_s(s_0 ~ 1 GeV^2) = 0.50 ± 0.03
- Quark condensate from GOR relation: m_q⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.11 GeV)^4
- Gluon condensate upper limit: ⟨(α_s/π)G^2⟩^{1/4} ≲ 0.31 GeV
- Nucleon sigma term: σ_N = 45 ± 8 MeV

## Context

This work updates previous QCD sum rule studies of the rho meson by Kwon and Weise (1999) and extends the finite energy sum rule approach of Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise (1997). It directly addresses the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (1991) by identifying the continuum threshold with the chiral symmetry breaking scale 4πf_π. The analysis enables a refined test of whether in-medium spectral changes follow chiral scaling, showing that the KKW spectral function exhibits BR scaling tendency while the RW spectral function shows predominantly broadening with almost no shift. The paper also provides an updated discussion of the in-medium pion decay constant using recent work by Jido, Hatsuda, and Kunihiro (2007) and in-medium chiral perturbation theory results.
