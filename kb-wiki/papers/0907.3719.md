# Thermodynamics of the QCD plasma and the large-N limit

> The equilibrium thermodynamic properties of the SU(N) plasma at finite temperature are studied non-perturbatively in the large-N limit, via lattice simulations. We present high-precision numerical results for the pressure, trace of the energy-momentum tensor, energy density and entropy density of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories with N=3, 4, 5, 6 and 8 colors, in a temperature range from 0.8T_c to 3.4T_c (where T_c denotes the critical deconfinement temperature). The results, normalized according to the number of gluons, show a very mild dependence on N, supporting the idea that the dynamics of the strongly-interacting QCD plasma could admit a description based on large-N models. We compare our numerical data with general expectations about the thermal behavior of the deconfined gluon plasma and with various theoretical descriptions, including, in particular, the improved holographic QCD model recently proposed by Kiritsis and collaborators. We also comment on the relevance of an AdS/CFT description for the QCD plasma in a phenomenologically interesting temperature range where the system, while still strongly-coupled, approaches a `quasi-conformal' regime characterized by approximate scale invariance. Finally, we perform an extrapolation of our results to the N to $\infty$ limit.

## Key claim

The equilibrium thermodynamic properties of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories show a very mild dependence on the number of colors N when normalized per gluon degree of freedom, supporting the idea that the strongly-interacting QCD plasma could admit a description based on large-N models, but the paper explicitly notes that this assumption is "not guaranteed to be true a priori" and requires numerical verification.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories with N = 3, 4, 5, 6 and 8 colors on isotropic hypercubic lattices using the standard Wilson gauge action, with a combination of heat-bath and overrelaxation update algorithms. The pressure was computed via the integral method from plaquette expectation values at finite and zero temperature, while the trace of the energy-momentum tensor was obtained from the plaquette difference multiplied by the lattice beta function. The physical scale was set using Sommer's parameter r0 for SU(3) and string tension measurements for the larger gauge groups.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: from 0.8 T_c to 3.4 T_c, where T_c denotes the critical deconfinement temperature
- Number of colors simulated: N = 3, 4, 5, 6 and 8
- Lattice size: N_t = 5 time slices for all ensembles, with N_s = 20 for N=3 and N_s = 16 for N=4,5,6,8
- For SU(3): 181 beta values from beta = 5.7 to 6.6, with statistics ranging from 3000 to 6400 configurations at zero temperature and 12000 to 33600 at finite temperature
- For SU(4): 131 beta values from beta = 10.5 to 11.8, with statistics of 4200 to 16000 at zero temperature and 12300 to 24000 at finite temperature
- For SU(5): 35 beta values from beta = 16.75 to 18.45, with 2500 zero-temperature and 7500 finite-temperature configurations
- For SU(6): 26 beta values from beta = 24.4 to 26.9, with 5200 to 10000 zero-temperature and 8400 to 20000 finite-temperature configurations
- For SU(8): 43 beta values from beta = 43.9 to 48.1, with 3200 zero-temperature and 9200 finite-temperature configurations

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of SU(N) thermodynamics by Bringoltz and Teper (2005) and by Datta and Gupta (2009) to higher precision and a wider range of N values. The results are compared with the improved holographic QCD model proposed by Kiritsis and collaborators (2007-2009), which reproduces the main non-perturbative features of the SU(3) Yang-Mills spectrum and thermodynamics. The study enables extrapolation of thermodynamic observables to the N to infinity limit and provides a benchmark for testing whether AdS/CFT descriptions of the QCD plasma are relevant in the phenomenologically interesting temperature range where the system approaches a quasi-conformal regime.
