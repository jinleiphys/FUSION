# The Last Neutrino Mixing angle theta13

> Among the still unmeasured neutrino properties, the third neutrino mixing angle, theta13, is likely to be the one we will next find out. In this contribution, first a brief summary of the limits and the preliminary measurements of this angle is given. Second a critical assessment of a widely used formula connecting two- and three-flavor evolution is provided.

# The Last Neutrino Mixing angle theta13

## Key claim
The third neutrino mixing angle θ₁₃ is likely the next neutrino property to be measured, with strong hints of a non-zero value emerging from global fits and the T2K experiment; however, the widely used two-flavor reduction formula connecting solar neutrino data to θ₁₃ is accurate only to leading order, with corrections proportional to a small parameter α ∼ 3 × 10⁻² that yield less than one percent correction even for the largest T2K-allowed values.

## Method
The author derives the three-flavor MSW evolution equation in matter, rotates away the atmospheric angle θ₂₃, and then treats θ₁₃ as a small perturbation to the Hamiltonian. By solving the evolution operator perturbatively to first order in sin 2θ₁₃ and applying the Riemann-Lebesgue lemma to average over rapid oscillations from the large Δ₃₁+Δ₃₂ phase, the author obtains a corrected version of the standard two-flavor reduction formula for the electron neutrino survival probability.

## Key numbers
- Current limit from Particle Data Group: sin²(2θ₁₃) < 0.15 at 90% C.L.
- SNO low-threshold analysis: θ₁₃ = 7.2⁺²·⁰₋₂·₈ degrees (non-Gaussian errors)
- KamLAND global analysis: sin²θ₁₃ = 0.009⁺⁰·⁰¹³₋₀·₀₀₇ at 79% C.L.
- T2K 2.5σ result: 0.03 < sin²2θ₁₃ < 0.28 for normal hierarchy and 0.04 < sin²2θ₁₃ < 0.34 for inverted hierarchy, both for δCP=0
- Correction parameter α = (1/(Δ₃₂+Δ₃₁)) Vμe(r=0) ∼ 3 × 10⁻² for a 10 MeV neutrino with δm₃₂² ∼ 2 × 10⁻³ eV² and electron density 100 NA/cm³
- First-order correction to survival probability is less than one percent for a 10 MeV neutrino

## Context
This paper extends the line of work on extracting θ₁₃ from solar neutrino data, which previously showed a slight discrepancy between solar experiments and KamLAND (Balantekin, 2008) and was addressed in global fits (Fogli, 2008). The author provides a critical assessment of the standard two-flavor reduction formula originally used in such fits, deriving a corrected version (Eq. a18) that includes first-order corrections in the small parameter α. This work enables more precise extraction of θ₁₃ from ongoing reactor experiments (Double Chooz, Daya Bay, RENO) and the T2K accelerator experiment, and clarifies that reactor disappearance experiments can quote θ₁₃ independently of the CP-violating phase δCP, while accelerator appearance experiments cannot.
