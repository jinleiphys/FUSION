# Damping of giant dipole resonance in hot rotating nuclei

> The phonon damping model (PDM) is extended to include the effect of angular momentum at finite temperature. The model is applied to the study of damping of giant dipole resonance (GDR) in hot and noncollectively rotating spherical nuclei. The numerical results obtained for Mo88 and Sn106 show that the GDR width increases with both temperature T and angular momentum M. At T > 4 MeV and M<= 60 hbar the increase in the GDR width slows down for Sn106, whereas at M<= 80 hbar the GDR widths in both nuclei nearly saturate. By adopting the nuclear shear viscosity extracted from fission data at T= 0, it is shown that the maximal value of the angular momentum for Mo88 and Sn106 should be around 46 and 55 hbar, respectively, so that the universal conjecture for the lower bound of the specific shear viscosity for all fluids is not violated up to T= 5 MeV.

## Key claim

The phonon damping model (PDM) is extended to include angular momentum effects, showing that the GDR width increases with both temperature and angular momentum but saturates at high values; the paper uses the KSS lower bound conjecture for specific shear viscosity to argue that the maximal realistic angular momenta for Mo88 and Sn106 are around 46 and 55 hbar, respectively, so that the viscosity bound is not violated up to T=5 MeV.

## Method

The PDM Hamiltonian is modified by adding a term -γM̂ to incorporate noncollective rotation about the symmetry axis, where the rotation frequency γ and chemical potential λ are determined by solving coupled equations for particle number and angular momentum conservation at each temperature. The GDR strength function and width are derived from double-time retarded Green functions with a decoupling approximation, and the specific shear viscosity η/s is computed via the Green-Kubo formula using the GDR photoabsorption cross section and entropy from the grand-canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers

- For Mo88 at T=3 MeV and M=41 hbar, the theoretical GDR width is approximately 11 MeV, in fair agreement with experiment
- For Mo88 at T=4 MeV and M=44 hbar, the experimental GDR width is approximately 7.5 MeV, with error bars giving 6-8.5 MeV
- The maximal angular momentum for Mo88 is around 46 hbar and for Sn106 around 55 hbar, so that η/s does not violate the KSS lower bound up to T=5 MeV
- The smoothing parameter ε in the width calculation is 0.5 MeV, with results insensitive in the range 0.1-1 MeV
- The GDR energy is taken as 15 MeV for Mo88 and 15.5 MeV for Sn106, independent of T and M
- Pairing gaps at T=0 and M=0 are: Δ_Z(0)≈1.25 MeV and Δ_N(0)≈1.39 MeV for Mo88; Δ_N(0)≈1.25 MeV for Sn106
- Two values of η(0) are used: 1u and 0.6u, where u=10^{-23} MeV s fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the phonon damping model (PDM) previously developed by the same author (Dang, 2000s) to include angular momentum, which was a known shortcoming of the model. The PDM had successfully described GDR width temperature dependence and saturation, as well as the specific shear viscosity ratio η/s approaching 1.3-4 KSS units at T=5 MeV (Dang, 2011). The present extension allows comparison with experimental data from Maj (2011) for Mo88 and Mattiuzzi (2000) and Bra (2004) for Sn isotopes, and enables testing of the KSS conjecture (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005) for the lower bound of specific shear viscosity in nuclear systems.
