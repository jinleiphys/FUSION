# Directed flow in asymmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions and the inverse Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect

> It is proposed to identify a strong electric field - created during relativistic collisions of asymmetric nuclei - via the observation of pseudorapidity and transverse momentum distributions of hadrons with the same mass but opposite charge. The results of detailed calculations within the Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) approach for the charge-dependent directed flow $v_1$ are presented for semi-central Cu+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV incorporating the inverse Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (iLPM) effect, which accounts for a delay in the electromagnetic interaction with the charged degree of freedom. Including the iLPM effect we achieve a reasonable agreement of the PHSD results for the charge splitting in $v_1(p_T)$ in line with the recent measurements of the STAR Collaboration for Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV while an instant appearance and coupling of electric charges at the hard collision vertex overestimates the splitting by about a factor of 10. We predict that the iLPM effect should practically disappear at energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx$9 GeV, which should lead to a significantly larger charge splitting of $v_1$ at the future FAIR/NICA facilities.

# Directed flow in asymmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions and the inverse Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect

## Key claim

The charge-dependent splitting of directed flow $v_1$ in semi-central Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV can be reconciled with STAR data only when the inverse Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (iLPM) effect is included, which delays the electromagnetic interaction with preformed charged particles by a formation time $\tau_f^{\rm em} = \tau_f/10$; an instantaneous coupling of electric charges at the hard collision vertex overestimates the splitting by about a factor of 10.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic transport calculations using the Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) approach for semi-central Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=9$ GeV, incorporating the retarded electromagnetic field generated by spectator charges via Liénard-Wiechert potentials. The inverse LPM effect was implemented by allowing the electromagnetic field to act on preformed charged particles only after a delay of $\tau_f^{\rm em} = \tau_f/10$, where $\tau_f$ is the hadronic formation time, rather than acting instantaneously on both formed and preformed charges.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the dominant electric field component reaches $\langle eE_x\rangle \approx 1.0 \, m_\pi^2 c^3/\hbar$ (in natural units GeV$^2$) for a time of approximately 0.15 fm/c at impact parameter $b=5$ fm.
- The instantaneous coupling of electric charges at the hard collision vertex overestimates the charge splitting $\Delta v_1 = v_1(h^+) - v_1(h^-)$ by about a factor of 10 compared to STAR data.
- With the iLPM effect using $\tau_f^{\rm em} = \tau_f/10$, the PHSD results for $\Delta v_1(p_T)$ show reasonable agreement with the STAR measurements for Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=9$ GeV, the maximal average energy density in a central cylinder (radius $R=2$ fm, length $|z|<2.5/\gamma$ fm) is about 1.6 GeV/fm$^3$.
- The iLPM effect is predicted to practically disappear at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 9$ GeV, leading to a significantly larger charge splitting of $v_1$ at lower energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Toneev et al. (2014) which predicted a charge-dependent directed flow in asymmetric Cu+Au collisions but overestimated the effect when assuming instantaneous electromagnetic coupling. It builds on the finding by Toneev et al. (2011) that electric and magnetic forces compensate in symmetric systems, motivating the use of asymmetric collisions. The inverse LPM effect is conceptually related to the formation-time concept in the Lund string model and to the original Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect for photon radiation. The results enable experimental tests at the RHIC BESII program and at future FAIR and NICA facilities, where the predicted larger charge splitting at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 9$ GeV could provide evidence for the early creation of strong electromagnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
