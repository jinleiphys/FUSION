# An $X(3872)$-like peak in $B\to (J/ψπ^+π^-) Kπ$ due to triangle singularity

> Triangle mechanisms for $B^0\to (J/ψπ^+π^-) K^+π^-$ are studied. Experimentally, an $X(3872)$ peak has been observed in this process. When the final $(J/ψπ^+π^-)π$ invariant mass is around the $D^*\bar D^*$ threshold, one of the triangle mechanisms causes a triangle singularity and generates a sharp $X(3872)$-like peak in the $J/ψπ^+π^-$ invariant mass distribution. The Breit-Wigner mass and width fitted to the spectrum are 3871.68 MeV (a few keV above the $D^{*0}\bar{D}^0$ threshold) and $\sim$0.4 MeV, respectively.These Breit-Wigner parameters hardly depends on a choice of the model parameters. Comparing with the precisely measured $X(3872)$ mass and width, $3871.69\pm 0.17$ MeV and $< 1.2$ MeV, the agreement is remarkable. When studying the $X(3872)$ signal from this process, this non-resonant contribution has to be understood in advance. We also study a charge analogous process $B^0\to (J/ψπ^0π^-) K^+π^0$. A similar triangle singularity exists and generates an $X^-(3876)$-like peak.

## Key claim

Triangle mechanisms in $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-) K^+\pi^-$ can produce a sharp peak in the $J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution that mimics the $X(3872)$ resonance, with fitted Breit-Wigner parameters that agree remarkably with the precisely measured $X(3872)$ mass and width without any parameter adjustment; this non-resonant contribution must be understood and subtracted before studying the true $X(3872)$ signal from this process.

## Method

The author calculates triangle diagrams for $B^0\to J/\psi\rho^0 K^+\pi^-$ where the loop contains $D^{*-}$, $D^{*0}$, and $\bar{D}^0$ mesons, with the amplitude integrated over loop momentum. The double differential decay width $d\Gamma_{B^0\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-K^+\pi^-} / dW dM_{J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-}$ is computed as a function of the $J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass and the $J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-$ invariant mass $W$, then integrated over $W$ in the triangle singularity region near the $D^{*-}D^{*0}$ threshold. A Breit-Wigner plus background fit quantifies the peak position and width.

## Key numbers

- Breit-Wigner mass fitted to the triangle singularity peak: $m_{\rm BW}=3871.68$ MeV, which is a few keV above the $D^{*0}\bar{D}^0$ threshold
- Breit-Wigner width fitted to the triangle singularity peak: $\Gamma_{\rm BW}=0.42\pm 0.01$ MeV
- The fitted mass agrees with the PDG $X(3872)$ mass of $3871.69\pm 0.17$ MeV
- The fitted width is below the PDG upper limit of $\Gamma_{\rm PDG}< 1.2$ MeV
- The triangle singularity occurs in the kinematical region $0 < M_{J/\psi\rho} - (m_a+m_b) \leq 0.2$ MeV and $0 < W - (m_{D^{*-}}+m_{D^{*0}}) \lesssim 1.0$ MeV
- The $D^{*\pm}$ decay width is $\Gamma_{D^{*\pm}}=83.4\pm 1.8$ keV; the $D^{*0}$ width is taken as 55 keV from isospin symmetry

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities as possible explanations for peaks attributed to exotic hadrons, following prior work on $XYZ$ states (Nakamura 2020). The finding that a triangle mechanism can produce a peak indistinguishable from $X(3872)$ in $B^0\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-K^+\pi^-$ means that the $X(3872)$ mass determination method proposed by Guo (2020) must account for this non-resonant contribution. The author proposes studying the charge analogous process $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^0\pi^-) K^+\pi^0$, where triangle diagrams C and D produce an $X^-(3876)$-like peak, as a way to identify and quantify the triangle singularity contribution in data.
