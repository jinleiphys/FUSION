# Triaxial quadrupole deformation dynamics in sd-shell nuclei around 26Mg

> Large-amplitude dynamics of axial and triaxial quadrupole deformation in 24,26Mg, 24Ne, and 28Si is investigated on the basis of the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian constructed with use of the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus the local quasiparticle random phase approximation method. The calculation reproduces well properties of the ground rotational bands, and beta and gamma vibrations in 24Mg and 28Si. The gamma-softness in the collective states of 26Mg and 24Ne are discussed. Contributions of the neutrons and protons to the transition properties are also analyzed in connection with the large-amplitude quadrupole dynamics.

## Key claim

The CHFB+LQRPA method reproduces the ground rotational bands and beta/gamma vibrations in 24Mg and 28Si, but the key finding is that 26Mg and 24Ne exhibit gamma-softness and anharmonic collective dynamics due to competing neutron and proton shell effects, unlike the rigid prolate/oblate minima in the N=Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian using the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) method to obtain the collective potential, combined with the local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (LQRPA) to compute vibrational masses and rotational moments of inertia on the (beta, gamma) deformation plane. The pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) model including quadrupole-pairing interaction was used as the microscopic Hamiltonian within a two-major-shell harmonic oscillator model space (p-shell and sd-shell). The collective Schrodinger equation was solved for states up to I=6, and electric transition properties were calculated using effective charges (e_eff^(n), e_eff^(p)) = (0.5, 1.5).

## Key numbers

* 24Mg: collective potential shows a prolate minimum at beta ~ 0.41; the calculation reproduces the yrast band (0_1+, 2_1+, 4_1+, 6_1+) and an excited K=2 band (2_2+, 3_1+, 4_2+, 5_1+, 6_2+) in good agreement with experimental energy levels.
* 28Si: collective potential shows an oblate minimum at beta ~ 0.26; no prolate local minimum appears in the present calculation.
* 26Mg: potential minimum at beta = 0.16 with small oblate deformation; the potential is soft against both axial and triaxial quadrupole deformations.
* 24Ne: potential minimum at beta = 0.20 with small oblate deformation; similarly soft against quadrupole deformations.
* Monopole pairing gaps: proton pairing gaps in 24Mg, 26Mg, and 24Ne become zero around beta ~ 0.4 in the prolate region; neutron pairing gaps in 28Si, 26Mg, and 24Ne become zero in the oblate region.
* 24Mg: the lowest LQRPA mode at gamma=0° (mode A) has eigenfrequency around 1-2 MeV in the region where pairing gaps vanish (0° < gamma < 8°); the beta vibrational mode is found at around 7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CHFB+LQRPA method previously applied to shape coexistence in proton-rich Se and Kr isotopes (Hinohara et al., 2010; Sato et al., 2011) to sd-shell nuclei around 26Mg. It contrasts with earlier axial-symmetry-restricted mean-field calculations for 26Mg (Rodriguez-Guzman et al., 2002; Peru et al., 2005; Terasaki et al., 1996) that found oblate-prolate coexistence, and with symmetry-unrestricted calculations (Inakura, private communication; Yao et al., 2011) showing triaxially soft potential surfaces. The results enable systematic analysis of triaxial quadrupole dynamics and neutron-proton contributions to transition properties in nuclei where neutrons and protons favor different shapes.
