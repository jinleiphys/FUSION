# Adiabatic internuclear potentials obtained by energy variation with the internuclear-distance constraint

> We propose a method to obtain adiabatic internuclear potentials via energy variation with the intercluster-distance constraint. The adiabatic $^{16}$O + $^{16,18}$O potentials obtained by the proposed method are applied to investigate the effects of valence neutrons in $^{16}$O + $^{18}$O sub-barrier fusions. Sub-barrier fusion cross sections of $^{16}$O + $^{18}$O are enhanced more compared to those of $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O because of distortion of valence neutrons in $^{18}$O.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to obtain adiabatic internuclear potentials via energy variation with an intercluster-distance constraint in the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework, and applies it to show that the enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections in 16O+18O relative to 16O+16O is primarily due to the alignment of deformed 18O rather than dipole polarization, though the agreement with experiment is only qualitative and depends on the choice of effective interaction.

## Method

The authors perform energy variation of a deformed-basis AMD wave function while constraining the internuclear distance between two nuclei, using a parabolic constraint potential. Adiabatic potentials are obtained by minimizing the energy at each distance, allowing the internal structures of the colliding nuclei to relax, while sudden potentials are constructed by shifting frozen ground-state wave functions of the separated nuclei to the same distance and antisymmetrizing. Fusion cross sections are then calculated using the potential model code potfus3 with incoming-wave boundary conditions at 6 fm, using the Modified Volkov No.1 case 1 (MV1') and Gogny D1S effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy term (T1(cl) + T2(cl)) is less than 5 x 10-2 MeV for internuclear distances R >= 4.5 fm.
- Adiabatic 16O+18O potentials are lower than the sudden potentials, with barrier tops occurring at larger internuclear distances than for 16O+16O.
- For incident energies E_c.m. <= 9 MeV, the 16O+18O sub-barrier fusion cross section obtained with adiabatic potentials is a few times larger than the 16O+16O sub-barrier fusion cross sections.
- The deviation of the distance between centers of mass of protons in 16O and 18O from the internuclear distance is less than 0.04 fm in the R >= 5 fm region.
- For the MV1' interaction, theoretical cross sections agree qualitatively with experimental data from PhysRevC.31.1980; for the D1S interaction, theoretical sub-barrier fusion cross sections are less than the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the method of obtaining adiabatic potentials via the Hartree-Fock method with an internuclear-distance constraint (Zint 1975, PhysRevC.14.1488) to asymmetric systems using the deformed-basis AMD framework. It contrasts with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock method with density constraint (PhysRevC.74.021601), which may insufficiently capture distortion effects at sub-barrier energies. The results enable microscopic interpretation of the enhancement of 16O+18O sub-barrier fusion observed experimentally (PhysRevC.31.1980) and previously attributed to the low 2+ state in 18O via channel-coupling calculations (PhysRevC.32.1432), showing that alignment of deformed 18O is the dominant mechanism rather than dipole polarization.
