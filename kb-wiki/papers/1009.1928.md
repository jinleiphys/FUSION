# The Influence of in-medium NN cross-sections, symmetry potential and impact parameter on the isospin observables

> We explore the influence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, symmetry potential and impact parameter on isospin sensitive observables in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with the ImQMD05 code, a modified version of Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. At incident velocities above the Fermi velocity, we find that the density dependence of symmetry potential plays a more important role on the double neutron to proton ratio $DR(n/p)$ and the isospin transport ratio $R_i$ than the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, provided that the latter are constrained to a fixed total NN collision rate. We also explore both $DR(n/p)$ and $R_i$ as a function of the impact parameter. Since the copious production of intermediate mass fragments is a distinguishing feature of intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, we examine the isospin transport ratios constructed from different groups of fragments. We find that the values of the isospin transport ratios for projectile rapidity fragments with $Z\ge20$ are greater than those constructed from the entire projectile rapidity source. We believe experimental investigations of this phenomenon can be performed. These may provide significant tests of fragmentation time scales predicted by ImQMD calculations.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry potential has a more important influence on the double neutron-to-proton ratio and the isospin transport ratio than the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, but only when the latter are constrained to a fixed total NN collision rate; the isospin transport ratios for projectile rapidity fragments with Z ≥ 20 are greater than those constructed from the entire projectile rapidity source.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD05 code, a modified Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, to simulate collisions of 112,124Sn + 112,124Sn at E/A = 50 MeV. They varied the symmetry potential parameter γi (0.5 for soft, 2.0 for stiff), the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections (free, isospin-dependent reduced, and isospin-independent reduced), and the impact parameter (b = 0, 2, 6, 8 fm). Asymptotic clusters were constructed using a minimum spanning tree method with coalescence parameters R0 = 3.5 fm and P0 = 250 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry potential parameters: γi = 0.5 (soft asy-EOS) and γi = 2.0 (stiff asy-EOS), with symmetry coefficient Cs = 35.19 MeV
- In-medium NN cross section reduction factor: ξ(Ebeam) = 0.2 for Ebeam = 50 A MeV, using σ*_nn/np = (1 − 0.2ρ/ρ0)σ^free_nn/np
- Impact parameters studied: b = 0, 2, 6, 8 fm for 112,124Sn + 112,124Sn at E/A = 50 MeV
- The heaviest fragments lost about 35% of their initial velocity for central collisions, and about 25% (10%) for b = 6 fm (b = 8 fm)
- Isospin diffusion for 124Sn + 112Sn at b = 6 fm stops around 150 fm/c
- Coalescence parameters: R0 = 3.5 fm, P0 = 250 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the ImQMD05 calculations published in Tsang (2009) by systematically exploring the sensitivity of isospin observables to in-medium NN cross sections, symmetry potential, and impact parameter. It addresses discrepancies between QMD and BUU approaches (such as IBUU04 from Li (2005), SMF from Baran (2002), and pBUU from Danielewicz (2000)) that have produced different constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The finding that DR(n/p) is robust against variations in in-medium cross sections at E/A = 50 MeV enables more confident use of this observable to constrain the symmetry energy, while the proposed fragment-based isospin transport ratios may provide tests of fragmentation time scales predicted by ImQMD calculations.
