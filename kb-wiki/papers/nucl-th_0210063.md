# $π$NN coupling and two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon

> Effects of non-resonant photoproductions arising from two different $πNN$ couplings are investigated in the $γN\toππN$ reaction. We find that the pseudoscalar (PS) $πNN$ coupling is generally preferable to the pseudovector (PV) $πNN$ coupling and particularly the total cross sections are successfully described by the model with the PS $πNN$ coupling. In order to see the difference between the two couplings, we also show the results of invariant mass spectra and helicity-dependent cross sections in various isospin channels calculated with the PS and PV couplings.

## Key claim

The pseudoscalar (PS) πNN coupling is generally preferable to the pseudovector (PV) πNN coupling for describing two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon, particularly for reactions involving neutral pions, because the PS coupling produces larger non-resonant contributions that can account for cross sections previously attributed to a strong ρ Kroll-Ruderman term with an artificially soft form factor.

## Method

The authors computed total cross sections, invariant mass spectra, and helicity-dependent cross sections for the γN → ππN reaction using both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) πNN couplings in a relativistic framework, including Born terms, contact interactions from an effective Lagrangian, vector meson contributions (ρ and ω), and resonant processes (Δ(1232), N*(1520), and ρ meson) treated with an isobar model. The calculations were performed for three isospin channels: γp → π⁺π⁻p, γp → π⁺π⁰n, and γp → π⁰π⁰p, and compared with experimental data from MAMI up to photon energies around 0.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- πNN coupling constant: g²_πNN/4π = 14.4
- ρ meson mass: m_ρ = 775 MeV; width: Γ_ρ(m_ρ) = 150 MeV
- ρ meson bare mass in isobar model: m⁰_ρ = 910 MeV; ρππ coupling: f_ρππ = 7.8; range parameter: q_ρππ = 800 MeV/c
- ω meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV; γπω coupling: g_γπω = 0.374 e
- Form factor range parameters: Λ = 1.25 GeV for π, Λ = 1.4 GeV for ρ and ω
- Relativistic calculations of non-resonant contributions with PV coupling are 30-60% larger than non-relativistic calculations around the N*(1520) resonance energy
- In the γp → π⁺π⁰n channel, the PS calculation is remarkably larger than the PV calculation and roughly consistent with the size of the ρ Kroll-Ruderman term from Ochi et al. (2000) that used a range parameter of 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the models of Ochi, Hirata, and Takaki (2000) and Nacher et al. (2001) for two-pion photoproduction, which had difficulty simultaneously describing all isospin channels. The authors address the speculation by Ochi et al. that their strong ρ Kroll-Ruderman term (requiring a soft ρππ form factor of 200 MeV/c that overestimated low-energy ππ p-wave phase shifts) might effectively simulate a background process from the PS πNN coupling. The results enable improved modeling of neutral-pion photoproduction channels and suggest that the PS coupling captures non-resonant background contributions in the I=J=1 ππ channel that were previously attributed to an artificially enhanced ρ meson mechanism.
