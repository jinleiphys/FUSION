# Hyperonic equation of state at finite temperature for neutron stars

> We review the composition and the equation of state of the hyperonic core of neutron stars at finite temperature within a relativistic mean-field approach. We make use of the new FSU2H* model, which is built upon the FSU2H scheme by improving on the Xi potential according to the recent analysis on the Xi atoms, and we extend it to include finite temperature corrections. The calculations are done for a wide range of densities, temperatures and charge fractions, thus exploring the different conditions that can be found in protoneutron stars, binary mergers remnants and supernovae explosions. The inclusion of hyperons has a strong effect on the composition and the equation of state at finite temperature, which consequently would lead to significant changes in the properties and evolution of hot neutron stars.

# Hyperonic equation of state at finite temperature for neutron stars

## Key claim
The FSU2H* model, which improves the Xi potential based on recent Xi atom data, predicts that hyperons from the full baryon octet appear in significant abundances in hot neutron star cores under various temperature and charge fraction conditions, and that the correct finite-temperature treatment of the hyperonic equation of state is essential for accurate simulations of proto-neutron stars, binary merger remnants, and supernovae, contrary to the common use of approximated models which the authors previously showed to be particularly inaccurate when hyperons are present.

## Method
The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with baryons (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) interacting via σ, ω, ρ, φ, and σ* mesons, using the FSU2H* model parameterized to reproduce nuclear saturation properties, heavy-ion collision constraints, 2 M⊙ neutron star observations, and radii below 13 km. They solve the Euler-Lagrange equations in the RMF approximation for fixed baryon density, temperature, and charge fraction Y_Q, obtaining the composition and thermodynamic quantities from the energy-momentum tensor across a wide range of densities, temperatures (including 25 MeV and 75 MeV), and charge fractions (including 0.01 and 0.5).

## Key numbers
- The nucleon mass is set to m_N = 939 MeV in the model.
- The σ, ω, ρ, σ*, and φ meson masses are 498 MeV, 783 MeV, 763 MeV, 980 MeV, and 1020 MeV respectively.
- The Λ hyperon coupling ratios are R_σΛ = 0.6613, R_ωΛ = 2/3, R_ρΛ = 0, R_σ*Λ = 0.2812, and R_φΛ = -√2/3.
- The Σ hyperon coupling ratios are R_σΣ = 0.4673, R_ωΣ = 2/3, R_ρΣ = 1, R_σ*Σ = 0.2812, and R_φΣ = -√2/3.
- The Ξ hyperon coupling ratios are R_σΞ = 0.3305, R_ωΞ = 1/3, R_ρΞ = 1, R_σ*Ξ = 0.5624, and R_φΞ = -2√2/3.
- At high charge fraction Y_Q = 0.5, the pressure-density curves for different temperatures become similar near ρ_B = 0.5 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends the FSU2H model by improving the Xi potential based on recent Xi atom analysis (Kochankovski 2022) and adds finite-temperature corrections, making the equation of state available for relativistic neutron star simulations. The authors previously showed (Kochankovski 2022) that approximated finite-temperature models used in simulations (Hotokezaka 2013) are particularly inaccurate when hyperons are present in the core. This work enables more accurate numerical simulations of proto-neutron stars, binary neutron star mergers, and supernova explosions by providing a hyperonic EoS that covers a wide range of densities, temperatures, and charge fractions without further approximations.
