# Scales in nuclear matter: Chiral dynamics with pion nucleon form factors

> A systematic calculation of nuclear matter is performed which includes the long-range correlations between nucleons arising from one- and two-pion exchange. Three-body effects from $2π$-exchange with excitations of virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobars are also taken into account in our diagrammatic calculation of the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f)$. In order to eliminate possible high-momentum components from the interactions we introduce at each pion-baryon vertex a form factor of monopole type. The empirical nuclear matter saturation point, $ρ_0 \simeq 0.16 $fm$^{-3}$, $\bar E_0\simeq -16 $MeV, is well reproduced with a monopole mass of $Λ\simeq 4πf_π\simeq 1.16 $GeV. As in the recent approach based on the universal low-momentum $NN$-potential $V_{\rm low-k}$, the inclusion of three-body effects is crucial in order to achieve saturation of nuclear matter. We demonstrate that the dependence of the pion-exchange contributions to $\bar E(k_f)$ on the ''resolution'' scale $Λ$ can be compensated over a wide range of $Λ$ by counterterms with two ''running'' contact-couplings. As a further application we study the in-medium chiral condensate $<\bar q q >(ρ)$ beyond the linear density approximation. For $ρ\leq 1.5 ρ_0$ we find small corrections from the derivative $d \bar E(k_f)/d m_π$, which are stable against variations of the monopole regulator mass $Λ$.

## Key claim

The empirical nuclear matter saturation point (density ~0.16 fm⁻³, energy per particle ~ -16 MeV) is well reproduced using a monopole form factor mass Λ ≈ 4πfπ ≈ 1.16 GeV, but only when three-body effects from 2π-exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation are included; without these three-body terms, nuclear matter does not saturate.

## Method

The authors performed a diagrammatic calculation of the energy per particle Ē(kf) for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, including one- and two-pion exchange contributions with monopole form factors at each pion-baryon vertex to eliminate high-momentum components. They incorporated two-loop and three-loop diagrams for iterated one-pion exchange, irreducible two-pion exchange via spectral functions, and three-body Hartree and Fock diagrams involving virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitations. The dependence on the monopole mass Λ was compensated by introducing two "running" short-distance contact-couplings B₃(Λ) and B₅(Λ) that scale with kf³ and kf⁵.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.158 fm⁻³ (corresponding to Fermi momentum kf0 = 262 MeV)
- Saturation energy per particle: Ē₀ = -16.7 MeV
- Monopole mass reproducing saturation: Λ₀ = 1.145 GeV (close to chiral symmetry breaking scale 4πfπ ≈ 1.16 GeV)
- Nuclear matter compressibility: K = 292 MeV
- Decomposition of Ē₀ (in MeV): kinetic energy 21.64, 1π-exchange 13.63, 2π-exchange -72.86, irreducible 2π-exchange -6.41, iterated 1π-exchange Hartree 19.06, iterated 1π-exchange Fock -1.46, three-body Hartree 4.63, three-body Δ-Hartree 7.72, three-body Δ-Fock -2.67
- Δ(1232)-nucleon mass splitting: Δ = 293 MeV
- Nucleon sigma-term: σN = (45 ± 8) MeV
- For ρ ≤ 1.5 ρ₀, corrections to the in-medium chiral condensate from dĒ(kf)/dmπ are small and stable against variations of Λ

## Context

This work extends the chiral approach to nuclear matter previously developed by Lutz (2000) and Kaiser (2002), which computed pion-exchange contributions up to three-loop order, and by Kaiser (2003) which included Δ(1232)-isobar effects. It directly connects to the Vlow-k approach of Bogner (2003), which demonstrated that perturbative treatment of nuclear matter is justified when high-momentum components are removed and that repulsive three-nucleon interactions are crucial for saturation. The paper enables a stable calculation of the in-medium chiral condensate beyond linear density approximation, showing that nuclear correlations produce only small corrections up to saturation density, consistent with the earlier chiral approach of Lutz (2000) and the relativistic mean-field study of Rolf (2004).
