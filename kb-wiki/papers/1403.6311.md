# Strangeness enhancement at the hadronic chemical freeze-out

> The chemical freeze-out of hadrons created in the high energy nuclear collisions is studied within the realistic version of the hadron resonance gas model. The chemical non-equilibrium of strange particles is accounted via the usual $γ_{s}$ factor which gives us an opportunity to perform a high quality fit with $χ^2/dof \simeq 63.5/55 \simeq 1.15$ of the hadronic multiplicity ratios measured from the low AGS to the highest RHIC energies. In contrast to previous findings, at low energies we observe the strangeness enhancement instead of a suppression. In addition, the performed $γ_{s}$ fit allows us to achieve the highest quality of the Strangeness Horn description with $χ^2/dof=3.3/14$. For the first time the top point of the Strangeness Horn is perfectly reproduced, which makes our theoretical horn as sharp as an experimental one. However, the $γ_{s}$ fit approach does not sizably improve the description of the multi-strange baryons and antibaryons. Therefore, an apparent deviation of multi-strange baryons and antibaryons from chemical equilibrium requires further explanation.

## Key claim
The authors find that, contrary to earlier claims of strangeness suppression in heavy-ion collisions, a sizable strangeness enhancement (γ_s > 1) occurs at low collision energies below √s_NN = 8.8 GeV, while at higher energies γ_s ≈ 1. This conclusion is hedged by the observation that the γ_s fit does not sizably improve the description of multi-strange baryons and antibaryons, and that at the lowest AGS energies (2.7–4.3 GeV) two local minima with close χ² values exist, making the γ_s concept ambiguous.

## Method
The authors employ a multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with different hard-core repulsion radii for pions (R_π = 0.1 fm), kaons (R_K = 0.38 fm), other mesons (R_m = 0.4 fm), and baryons (R_b = 0.2 fm). They introduce a γ_s factor multiplying the thermal particle density for each strange valence quark/antiquark to account for chemical non-equilibrium of strange particles, and fit the temperature T, baryonic chemical potential μ_B, isospin chemical potential μ_I3, and γ_s to 111 independent hadron yield ratios measured at 14 center-of-mass energies from √s_NN = 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Global fit quality: χ²/dof = 63.5/55 ≈ 1.15 for the γ_s fit, compared to χ²/dof = 80.5/69 ≈ 1.16 for the model with γ_s = 1 (from prior work by the same group).
- Strangeness Horn description: χ²/dof = 3.3/14 for the γ_s fit, improved from χ²/dof = 7.5/14 in the prior γ_s = 1 fit (Bugaev et al., 2013).
- At low collision energies (√s_NN = 2.7–4.3 GeV), γ_s values are found in the range 1.2–1.6; for example, at √s_NN = 3.8 GeV the deepest minimum gives γ_s ≈ 1.6, while a secondary minimum gives γ_s ≈ 0.8.
- Hard-core radii used: R_b = 0.2 fm, R_m = 0.4 fm, R_π = 0.1 fm, R_K = 0.38 fm (from Bugaev et al., 2013).

## Context
This work extends the multicomponent HRGM with hard-core repulsion developed by Bugaev et al. (2013) by introducing a γ_s factor to account for strange particle chemical non-equilibrium. It directly contradicts the findings of Becattini et al. (2004–2006), who reported strangeness suppression (γ_s < 1) in heavy-ion collisions based on fits with χ²/dof between 2 and 5 per energy point, which the authors argue are of low statistical quality. The results are consistent with the γ_s ≈ 1 findings of PBM (2006) at high energies. The paper enables future work to refine the γ_s concept at low energies and to develop alternative approaches for multi-strange baryons, as suggested by Bugaev et al. (2013) and Bugaev et al. (2013, SFO).
