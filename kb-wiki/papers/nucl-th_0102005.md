# Scaling of single photon production in hadronic collisions

> Scaling of single photon production in $pp$ and $p\overline{p}$ collisions is studied. It is empirically observed the available data scales $\sim \sqrt{s}/p_T^5$ for $x_T=2p_T/\sqrt{s} \leq 0.1$ and $\sim (\sqrt{s})^{3.3}/p_T^9$ for larger $x_T$. The NLO pQCD predictions for $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ of 200 and 5500 GeV, relevant for RHIC and LHC energies are seen to closely follow this scaling behaviour. Implications for single photon production in heavy ion collisions are discussed.

# Scaling of single photon production in hadronic collisions

## Key claim

The available data for single photon production in pp and pbar-p collisions empirically scale as sqrt(s)/p_T^5 for x_T <= 0.1 and as (sqrt(s))^3.3/p_T^9 for x_T > 0.1, and NLO pQCD predictions for RHIC and LHC energies closely follow this scaling behavior, but the E704 data at sqrt(s)=19.4 GeV are excluded from the fit because they destabilize the procedure and appear inconsistent with the scaling by a factor of about 3.5.

## Method

The author compiled existing single photon production data from pp and pbar-p collisions across multiple experiments and performed empirical fits to the invariant cross-section Ed^3sigma/d^3p at y=0 as a function of p_T and sqrt(s), separately for the kinematic regions x_T < 0.1 and x_T > 0.1. The scaling relations were then tested against NLO pQCD predictions for pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and 5500 GeV, and applied to estimate prompt photon yields in heavy ion collisions by comparing the WA80 S+Au and WA98 Pb+Pb systems.

## Key numbers

- For x_T < 0.1: (Ed^3sigma/d^3p)_{y=0} = 6495 x sqrt(s)/p_T^5 pb/GeV^2, corresponding to n=4 in Owens' notation
- For x_T > 0.1: (Ed^3sigma/d^3p)_{y=0} = 574.6 x (sqrt(s))^3.3/p_T^9.14 pb/GeV^2, corresponding to n=5.8 in Owens' notation
- The E704 data at sqrt(s)=19.4 GeV are about 50% larger at p_T approx 3.2 GeV compared to NA24 data at sqrt(s)=23.75 GeV
- The scaling predicts that production at 19.4 GeV should be a factor of 2 smaller than at 23.75 GeV for the same p_T, but the E704 data are a factor of 3.5 larger than expected from the scaling
- The prompt photon yield for WA98 Pb+Pb can be obtained from WA80 S+Au by a numerical factor of about 3.43, close to the geometrical factor of 3.5 for thermal photon yield

## Context

This work extends the scaling arguments for single photon production originally discussed by Owens (1987), who proposed a leading-order scaling of F(x_T,theta)/p_T^4, and provides an empirical update using the comprehensive NLO pQCD data compilation of Vogelsang and Whalley (1997) and Aurenche et al (1999). The scaling relations enable estimation of prompt photon backgrounds in heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS energies, and the author applies them to compare the WA80 S+Au and WA98 Pb+Pb experiments, suggesting that the sum of thermal and prompt photon yields should differ by a factor of about 3.5 between the two systems. The results also highlight the inconsistency of the E704 data, which if corrected would bring NLO pQCD into perfect agreement without requiring intrinsic k_T effects as discussed by Aurenche et al (1999).
