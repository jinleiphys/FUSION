# Dissipative currents and transport coefficients in relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> We determine the form of dissipative currents at the first order in relativistic spin hydrodynamics with finite chemical potential including gradients of the spin potential. Taking advantage of isotropy in the hydrodynamic local rest frame, using a suitable matching condition for the flow velocity and enforcing the semi-positivity of entropy production, we find 23 dissipative transport coefficients relating dissipative currents to gradients of the thermo-hydrodynamic fields: 4 for the symmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor, 5 for the antisymmetric part, 3 for the conserved vector current, and 11 for the spin tensor. We compare our finding with previous results in literature.

## Key claim

The paper derives the most general form of first-order dissipative currents in relativistic spin hydrodynamics with finite chemical potential, finding 23 independent transport coefficients under the assumptions of isotropy in the local rest frame, a specific matching condition for the flow velocity, and semi-positive entropy production, a reduction from 98 unconstrained coefficients.

## Method

The authors express dissipative currents (symmetric and antisymmetric parts of the energy-momentum tensor, conserved vector current, and spin tensor) as linear combinations of thermo-hydrodynamic gradients (thermal shear, gradient of reduced chemical potential, difference between spin potential and thermal vorticity, and gradient of spin potential) with tensor-valued coefficients. They decompose these coefficients into irreducible representations of the rotation group SO(3,R) in the local co-moving frame, then impose matching conditions (vanishing projections of dissipative currents onto the four-velocity) and the semi-positivity of entropy production to eliminate redundant coefficients.

## Key numbers

- 23 dissipative transport coefficients are found in total: 4 for the symmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor, 5 for the antisymmetric part, 3 for the conserved vector current, and 11 for the spin tensor.
- The initial unconstrained decomposition yields 98 undetermined factors before imposing matching conditions and entropy production constraints.
- Matching conditions eliminate 34 of the 98 coefficients, leaving 64 undetermined coefficients before the semi-positivity constraint is applied.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Becattini (2023) and others, providing a systematic derivation of first-order dissipative currents that includes gradients of the spin potential, an aspect not fully treated in prior formulations such as those by Hongo (2021) or Gallegos (2020, 2021). The results enable future microscopic calculations of the 23 transport coefficients from quantum kinetic theory or field theory, and clarify the structure of dissipative corrections needed for phenomenological modeling of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions.
