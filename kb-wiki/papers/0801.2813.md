# A consistent thermodynamic treatment for quark mass density-dependent model

> The ambiguities and inconsistencies in previous thermodynamic treatments for the quark mass density-dependent model are addressed. A new treatment is suggested to obtain the self-consistent results. A new independent variable of effective mass is introduced to make the traditional thermodynamic calculation with partial derivative still practicable. The contribution from physical vacuum has been discussed. We find that the properties of strange quark matter given by quark mass density-dependent model are nearly the same as those obtained by MIT bag model after considering the contribution of the physical vacuum.

# A consistent thermodynamic treatment for quark mass density-dependent model

## Key claim
The authors show that previous thermodynamic treatments of the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model are inconsistent because they incorrectly include extra partial-derivative terms from the density-dependent effective mass. They propose that by introducing the effective mass \(m^*\) as an independent thermodynamic degree of freedom, the standard partial-derivative relations become self-consistent, and the negative pressure in the QMDD model arises from physical vacuum contributions rather than from spurious derivative terms.

## Method
The authors first demonstrate the inconsistency of prior treatments by showing that partial derivatives of the thermodynamic potential \(\Omega(T,V,\mu,m^*(\rho))\) with respect to \(T\), \(V\), or \(\mu\) produce extra terms involving \(\partial m^*/\partial\rho\) that violate standard thermodynamic identities such as \(\Omega = -pV\). They then propose treating \(m^*\) as an independent intensive variable, rewriting the differential of the thermodynamic potential as \(d\Omega = -S dT - p dV - \overline{N} d\mu + X dm^*\), so that all partial derivatives are taken with \(m^*\) held fixed. For the QMDD model at zero temperature, they compute the energy per baryon and equation of state for strange quark matter using this consistent formalism, and separately introduce a vacuum energy term \(\Omega_0\) (following the method of Gorenstein and Yang (1995) and Wang (2000)) to recover negative pressure and stability.

## Key numbers
- Saturation point of energy per baryon in the consistent treatment: \(\varepsilon/\rho_B = 906.3\) MeV at baryon density \(\rho_B = 0.433\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Saturation point from the treatment of Benvenuto et al. (1995a, 1995b): \(\varepsilon/\rho_B = 1042.8\) MeV at \(\rho_B = 0.692\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- At \(\rho_B \to 0\), the consistent treatment gives \(\{\varepsilon = 170\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3},\ p = 0\}\); the treatment of Benvenuto et al. gives \(\{\varepsilon = 340\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3},\ p = -170\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3}\}\); the treatment of Peng et al. (2000) gives \(\{\varepsilon = 170\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3},\ p = -170\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3}\}\).
- Model parameters used: \(B = 170\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), \(m_{s0} = 150\) MeV.

## Context
This work resolves long-standing ambiguities in the thermodynamic treatment of the QMDD model, originally proposed by Fowler, Raha and Weiner (1981). Prior inconsistent formulations appeared in Chakrabarty et al. (1989, 1991, 1993), Benvenuto et al. (1995a, 1995b), and Peng et al. (2000), each giving different expressions for pressure and energy density. The authors show that the correct negative pressure and stability of strange quark matter come from physical vacuum contributions, analogous to the MIT bag model (Farhi 1984), rather than from derivative terms that violate thermodynamic consistency. The method is applicable to other quasiparticle models with density- or temperature-dependent effective masses, such as the quark mass density and temperature dependent (QMDTD) model used by Zhang et al. (2001, 2002, 2003), Wen et al. (2005), and others.
