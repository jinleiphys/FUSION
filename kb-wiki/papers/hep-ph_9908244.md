# Charmonium suppression by gluon bremsstrahlung in p-A and A-B collisions

> Prompt gluons are an additional source for charmonium suppression in nuclear collisions, in particular for nucleus-nucleus collisions. These gluons are radiated as bremsstrahlung in N-N collisions and interact inelastically with the charmonium states while the nuclei still overlap. The spectra and mean number <n_g> of the prompt gluons are calculated perturbatively and the gluon-Psi inelastic cross section is estimated. The integrated cross sections for AB --> J/Psi (Psi')X for p-A and A-B collisions and the dependence on transverse energy for S-U and Pb-Pb can be described quantitatively with some adjustment of one parameter <n_g>σ(gPsi).

## Key claim

Prompt gluons radiated as bremsstrahlung in N-N collisions provide an additional source of charmonium suppression in nuclear collisions, particularly for nucleus-nucleus collisions, and can quantitatively describe the integrated cross sections and transverse energy dependence for p-A, S-U, and Pb-Pb systems with adjustment of a single parameter \(\langle n_g\rangle \sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}^{\Psi g}\); however, the authors acknowledge that due to uncertainties in the input variables, other mechanisms (plasma, hadronic comovers) cannot be excluded.

## Method

The authors derive a closed expression within a classical multiple scattering model with straight-line trajectories, calculating charmonium suppression caused by nucleons and prompt gluons. The spectra and mean number \(\langle n_g\rangle\) of prompt gluons are calculated perturbatively using QCD bremsstrahlung formulas, with a time-dependent mean number of gluons approximated by a step function rising to a constant value \(n_g^0\) after a fixed time \(t_0 = 0.6\) fm/c. The inelastic gluon-Psi cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}^{\Psi g}\) is estimated from the Psi-N cross section via a color factor relation \(\sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}^{\Psi g} \simeq (3/4) \sigma_{\mathrm{abs}}^{\Psi N}\), and formation time effects are included for both the charmonium premeson and the gluon production.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic Psi-N cross section used: \(\sigma_{\mathrm{in}}^{J/\psi N} = 6.7\) mb (including contributions from \(\chi_c\) and \(\psi'\)); \(\sigma_{\mathrm{in}}^{\psi' N} = 12\) mb
- Initial premeson-nucleon cross section (for \(\tau \rightarrow 0\)): \(\sigma^{\mathrm{pre}}_{\mathrm{in}} = 3\) mb, taken the same for \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi'\) channels
- Fixed gluon production time: \(t_0 = 0.6\) fm/c
- Range of \(n_g^0\) values used: \(0.5 \leq n_g^0 \leq 1\) for \(J/\psi\) (corresponding to \(\omega_{\mathrm{min}}\) between 0.5 and 1 GeV); \(1 \leq n_g^0 \leq 2\) for \(\psi'\) (due to smaller binding energy)
- Minimum gluon energy for \(J/\psi\) destruction: \(\omega_{\mathrm{min}} = 0.7\) GeV; for \(\psi'\) destruction: \(\omega_{\mathrm{min}} = 0.1\) GeV
- Minimum gluon transverse momentum used in calculation: \(k \geq 0.5\) GeV/c
- Lorentz factor for SPS 200 GeV/A energy: \(\gamma = 10\)
- Best-fit value for \(J/\psi\): \(n_g^0 = 0.5\) (corresponding to \(\omega_{\mathrm{min}} = 0.75\) GeV) fits absolute \(J/\psi\) production cross sections well

## Context

This work extends the preliminary calculations presented in Huefner et al. (1998) by introducing a closed expression within a classical multiple scattering model, including formation time effects, and calculating charmonium suppression as a function of transverse energy. The paper contrasts with three existing mechanisms: (1) inelastic collisions of charmonium premesons with nucleons (Brodsky 1988, Kopeliovich 1991, Aichelin 1999, He 1999), which reproduces p-A and light A-B data but fails for central Pb-Pb; (2) charmonium dissociation in quark-gluon plasma (Blaizot 1996, Wong 1997, Kharzeev 1997, Vogt 1998); and (3) interaction with comoving hadrons (Gavin 1997, Capella 1997, Armesto 1999, Cassing 1997). The prompt gluon mechanism enables quantitative description of the "anomalous" suppression in central Pb-Pb collisions without invoking QGP or comovers, though the authors note that the relative importance of various contributions cannot be quantitatively determined at present.
