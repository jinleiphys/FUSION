# Resolved photoproduction in {\tt MadGraph5\_aMC@NLO}

> The upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), with its high luminosity, will offer an unprecedented opportunity to explore the internal structure of atomic nucleus over an extended energy range from $\sqrt{s_{ep}} =$ 45 GeV to $\sqrt{s_{ep}} =$ 140 GeV. A particularly promising aspect of this collider is the study of the partonic structure with quasi-real photons which can also be studied in inclusive ultra-peripheral collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In this work, we present our validation of resolved photoproduction at fixed order for (next-to-)leading order using \texttt{MadGraph5\_aMC@NLO}, a widely adopted framework at the LHC.

# Resolved photoproduction in MadGraph5_aMC@NLO

## Key claim
The authors validate that resolved photoproduction at next-to-leading order (NLO) can be reliably computed using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, achieving approximately 2% agreement with the FMNR code for b-quark production at HERA kinematics, but this agreement is conditional on the specific choice of photon PDF set (GRV-HO), proton PDF set (CTEQ6M), and kinematic cuts applied.

## Method
The authors extended MadGraph5_aMC@NLO to handle asymmetric collisions by enabling simultaneous invocation of two distinct LHAPDF sets, then applied this to resolved photoproduction by substituting one hadron PDF with a resolved photon PDF and convoluting with the photon flux under the equivalent photon approximation. They validated the implementation by computing the transverse momentum distribution of b-quark resolved photoproduction at HERA kinematics (sqrt(s_ep) = 319 GeV) and comparing with FMNR predictions and H1 experimental data, then produced predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider at two center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers
- Agreement between MadGraph5_aMC@NLO and FMNR is approximately 2% for b-quark resolved photoproduction in the range 1 GeV < ⟨P_T(b)⟩ < 10 GeV at sqrt(s_ep) = 319 GeV
- b-quark mass set to m_b = 4.75 GeV for all calculations
- At sqrt(s_ep) = 45 GeV for the EIC, direct photon contribution dominates for P_T < 12 GeV, while resolved-photon contributions become more significant for P_T > 12 GeV
- At sqrt(s_ep) = 140 GeV for the EIC, direct photon processes dominate throughout the entire P_T spectrum
- Photon flux range for HERA validation: 16 GeV < W_γp < 207 GeV
- Photon flux range for EIC at sqrt(s_ep) = 45 GeV: 5 GeV < W_γp < 41 GeV
- Photon flux range for EIC at sqrt(s_ep) = 140 GeV: 14 GeV < W_γp < 126 GeV

## Context
This work extends the capabilities of MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, a widely used framework at the LHC, to handle resolved photoproduction processes relevant for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider and ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC. It builds on prior theoretical work on photon structure from 1971 and earlier implementations such as FMNR (Frixione, 1993), HELAC-Onia (Shao, 2015), and Pythia (Helenius, 2017), while providing a more flexible automated NLO framework. The validation against HERA H1 data (2012) and FMNR predictions establishes the tool for future studies of photon parton distribution functions at the EIC and for interpreting data from inclusive ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC.
