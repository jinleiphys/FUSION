# Soft Pion Emission in DVCS

> We examine soft-pion emission in deeply virtual Compton scattering. Contrary to previous claims, we find that the amplitude for soft-pion emission is not directly related to the generalized parton distributions in the nucleon.

## Key claim

Contrary to the claim of Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaeghen (2003) that chiral symmetry alone relates soft-pion emission in DVCS directly to the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs), the authors find that at leading order in the chiral expansion the amplitude is controlled by the parton distribution functions (PDFs) and by additional strong-interaction parameters that are not related to the elastic GPDs.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order contribution to the process γ* p → γ n π+ using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) with nucleons, Δ-resonances, and pions. They construct the most general set of leading-order operators consistent with the chiral transformation properties of the twist-2 operators that dominate deeply virtual Compton scattering, and evaluate tree-level diagrams for n ≥ 2 twist-2 operator insertions. The matrix elements are then compared with the results obtained by Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaeghen (2003) using current-algebra methods.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order matrix elements are O(𝒫⁰) in the chiral power-counting, where 𝒫 ∼ k, q, Δ, mπ are the small expansion parameters.
- For the n=1 isovector-vector current, current conservation forces a(1) = −2 gA, which agrees with the known result from heavy-baryon χPT and with Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaeghen (2003).
- For n ≥ 2, the coefficients a(n), b(n), and c(n) cannot be related to the PDF moments ⟨xⁿ⁻¹⟩_qV(1) or ⟨xⁿ⁻¹⟩_qA(1) by chiral symmetry alone; the specific relation a(n) = −2 ⟨xⁿ⁻¹⟩_qA(1) claimed by Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaeghen (2003) is shown to be false for n=2 because the operator θV,μν(2),b is not conserved.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of twist-2 operator matrix elements developed by Arndt and Savage (2002) and Chen and Ji (2001) to the case of soft-pion emission in DVCS. It directly contradicts the earlier calculation by Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaeghen (2003), who used current-algebra techniques and claimed that no additional unknown strong-interaction parameters beyond the GPDs appear. The result implies that soft-pion production constitutes a presently incalculable background to extracting GPDs from DVCS experiments, though naive dimensional analysis can provide rough estimates of the unknown coefficients a(n), b(n), and c(n).
