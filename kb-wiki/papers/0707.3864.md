# The equation of state for the nuclear matter and the properties of the neutron star

> The equation of state for the beta stable nuclear matter is calculated numerically, and then the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for the structure of the neutron star is solved in the fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm. It shows the mass and radius of the neutron star are functions of the central density of the neutron star and a maximum mass of 1.932 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 9.340km is obtained. Considering the equation of state of the nuclear matter must obey the causality, a new factor c is added in the nuclear potential energy formula. Therefore, with a new equation of state for the beta stable nuclear matter when c=0.15, a new maximum mass of 1.440 solar masses with a radius of 9.765km for the neutron star is obtained. Finally, the contribution of the cosmological constant to the structure of the neutron star is discussed, and we find the cosmological constant has minimal or negligible influence on the properties of the neutron star.

## Key claim
Using a simple nuclear potential energy model with a causality-preserving correction factor c=0.15, the maximum mass of a neutron star is reduced from 1.932 solar masses to 1.440 solar masses, with the latter value being the physically acceptable result because the uncorrected equation of state violates causality at high density.

## Method
The authors constructed an equation of state for beta-stable nuclear matter using a phenomenological potential energy formula for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, with parameters fixed by saturation properties (binding energy -16 MeV, compressibility 360 MeV). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations, then introduced a corrective factor c in the potential to enforce causality (speed of sound less than speed of light). Finally, they examined the effect of a cosmological constant on the TOV equation.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass without causality correction: 1.932 solar masses with radius 9.370 km
- Maximum mass with causality correction (c=0.15): 1.440 solar masses with radius 9.765 km
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16 MeV
- Nuclear compressibility used: k0 = 360 MeV
- Bulk symmetry energy parameter: S0 = 30 MeV
- Cosmological constant limits considered: upper limit 4 x 10^-35 s^-2, lower limit -2 x 10^-35 s^-2

## Context
This work extends the asymmetric nuclear matter model of Prakash (1996) and Sierk (2003) to neutron star structure calculations, similar to the approach of Psonis et al. (2007) who studied the symmetry energy within the same model. The results contrast with Ozel (2006), who argued that soft equations of state are ruled out by observations of EXO 0748-676, but align with Alford et al. (2007) who showed quark matter can be stiff enough to reach 2.0 solar masses. The finding that the cosmological constant has negligible effect on neutron star properties enables future studies to safely ignore it in standard neutron star modeling.
