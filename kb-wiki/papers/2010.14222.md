# Roton instabilities in the superfluid outer core of neutron stars

> We study the superfluid dynamics of the outer core of neutron stars by means of a hydrodynamic model made of a neutronic superfluid and a protonic superconductor, coupled by both the dynamic entrainment and the Skyrme SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interactions. The resulting nonlinear equations of motion are probed in the search for dynamical instabilities triggered by the relative motion of the superfluids that could be related to observed timing anomalies in pulsars. Through linear analysis, the origin and expected growth of the instabilities is explored for varying nuclear-matter density. Differently from previous findings, the dispersion of linear excitations in our model shows rotonic structures below the pair-breaking energy threshold, which lies at the origin of the dynamical instabilities, and could eventually lead to emergent vorticity along with modulations of the superfluid density.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, contrary to previous studies focusing on long-wavelength phonon excitations, the dispersion of linear excitations in the superfluid outer core of neutron stars exhibits rotonic structures below the pair-breaking energy threshold when relative motion between the neutron superfluid and proton superconductor is present; these rotonic modes are the origin of dynamical instabilities that could eventually lead to emergent vorticity and modulations of superfluid density, though the model deliberately omits mutual friction, viscosity, and magnetic-field effects that could damp or suppress these instabilities.

## Method

The authors constructed a hydrodynamic model of the neutron-star outer core consisting of a neutron superfluid and a proton superconductor coupled by both dynamic entrainment (following Andreev and Bashkin (1976) and Chamel (2006)) and the Skyrme SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the equation of state provided by Douchin and Haensel (2001). They linearized the resulting nonlinear Hamilton's equations of motion around stationary states with constant relative velocity between the two superfluids, derived the dispersion relations for plane-wave perturbations along the direction of relative motion, and analyzed the spectrum of excitation modes as a function of nuclear-matter density and relative velocity.

## Key numbers

* At total density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the speed of sound in the neutron superfluid alone is c_n = 0.22 c, and in the proton superfluid alone is c_p = 0.1 c; with electronic contributions the effective proton speed becomes c_p* = 0.23 c.
* The entrainment coefficient for the SLy4 interaction is α ≈ −1.566 fm³.
* The neutron ³PF₂ pairing gap increases with a linear slope of 0.23 MeV × fm³ up to about 0.8 MeV at ρ = 0.24 fm⁻³; the proton ¹S₀ pairing gap reaches a maximum of about 0.8 MeV near nuclear saturation density.
* For a relative velocity V = 0.125 c between the superfluids at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the kinetic-energy shift amounts to 4.9 MeV in neutrons and 9.2 MeV in protons.
* The Skyrme SLy4 parameters used include t₀ = −2488.91 MeV fm³, t₁ = 486.82 MeV fm⁵, t₂ = −546.39 MeV fm⁵, t₃ = 13777.0 MeV fm⁴, with x₀ = 0.834, x₁ = −0.344, x₂ = −1.0, and x₃ = 1.354.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on hydrodynamical instabilities in coupled superfluids with relative motion by Andersson (2004) and Kobyakov (2017), which focused on the longest wavelength excitations (essentially phonons), by demonstrating the relevance of rotonic modes at shorter wavelengths. The model uses the SLy4 interaction, which is compatible with a maximum neutron star mass of about 2 M⊙ in agreement with observations by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), and calculates entrainment quantities following Chamel (2006). The findings suggest that the superfluid outer core may contribute to observed timing anomalies in pulsars, such as glitches, complementing the standard Anderson and Itoh (1975) theory of vortex unpinning in the inner crust.
