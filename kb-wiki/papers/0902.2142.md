# Hypernuclear Gamma-Ray Spectroscopy and the Structure of p-shell Nuclei and Hypernuclei

> Information on Lambda-7Li, Lambda-9Be, Lambda-10B, Lambda-11B, Lambda-12C, Lambda-15N, and Lambda-16O from the Ge detector array Hyperball is interpreted in terms of shell-model calculations that include both Lambda and Sigma configurations with p-shell cores. It is shown that the data puts strong constraints on the effective Lambda-N interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that gamma-ray spectroscopy of p-shell hypernuclei, interpreted through shell-model calculations that explicitly include both Lambda and Sigma configurations, places strong constraints on the spin dependence of the effective Lambda-N interaction, but the parameter extraction depends on the specific model used for the p-shell core wave functions and the treatment of Lambda-Sigma coupling.

## Method

The author interprets gamma-ray transition energies measured by the Hyperball Ge detector array for hypernuclei from A=7 to A=16 using shell-model calculations in a weak-coupling basis, where a Lambda or Sigma hyperon in an s orbit is coupled to p-shell nuclear core states. The effective Lambda-N interaction is parametrized in terms of five radial integrals (central, spin-spin, Lambda spin-orbit, nucleon spin-orbit, and tensor) derived from the Nijmegen NSC97 meson-exchange potentials via G-matrix calculations, with explicit inclusion of both Lambda and Sigma configurations in the basis states.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state doublet splitting in Lambda-7Li gives a spin-spin parameter Delta = 0.430 MeV, with the 3/2+ to 1/2+ transition energy calculated as 693 keV
- The excited-state doublet splitting in Lambda-7Li (7/2+ to 5/2+) is 494 keV calculated versus 471 keV experimental, with contributions from Delta (557 keV), S_Lambda (-32 keV), S_N (-8 keV), and T (-71 keV)
- The 1/2+;1 state in Lambda-7Li lies at 3.88 MeV excitation energy, just below the Lambda-5He + d threshold at 3.94(4) MeV
- For the s-shell hypernuclei, the 1+ to 0+ splitting is 1.04(5) MeV in Lambda-4H and 1.15(4) MeV in Lambda-4He, with Lambda-Sigma coupling contributing approximately 0.74 MeV to the 0+ state binding via a matrix element v(0) = 7.7 MeV
- The Lambda separation energies from emulsion data are: Lambda-3H 0.13(5) MeV, Lambda-4H 2.04(4) MeV, Lambda-4He 2.39(3) MeV, and Lambda-5He 3.12(2) MeV

## Context

This work extends the shell-model analysis of Gal, Soper, and Dalitz (1978) and Millener, Gal, Dover, and Dalitz (1985) by incorporating explicit Lambda-Sigma coupling and using high-resolution gamma-ray data from the Hyperball array at KEK and BNL. The results enable tighter constraints on the spin-dependent components of the effective Lambda-N interaction than were possible from ground-state binding energies alone, and provide a framework for interpreting future gamma-ray measurements on heavier p-shell hypernuclei up to Lambda-16O.
