# Two-component model for the axial form factor of the nucleon

> The axial form factor of the nucleon is studied in a two-component model consisting of a three-quark intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud. The experimental data in the space-like region are well reproduced with a minimal number of parameters. The results are similar to those obtained from a dipole fit up to 1 GeV$^2$, but they surprisingly show a zero between 2 and 3 GeV$^2$. Such behavior, predicted by the present model completely disagrees with the dipole parametrization. The theoretical expression for the form factor is then analytically extrapolated to the time-like region, giving the first predictions in this regime. This kinematical region is especially interesting for present and future colliders.

# Two-component model for the axial form factor of the nucleon

## Key claim

The two-component model, which describes the nucleon as a three-quark intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud, reproduces the axial form factor data in the space-like region comparably to a dipole fit up to 1 GeV², but predicts a zero between 2 and 3 GeV² for one parametrization (IJL), a behavior that completely disagrees with the dipole parametrization. The model provides the first predictions for the axial form factor in the time-like region via analytic continuation.

## Method

The authors fit the axial form factor data from pion electroproduction experiments using a two-component model with a single free parameter α, while fixing the intrinsic structure parameter γ from previous fits to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (either γ = 0.25 GeV⁻² from Iachello-Jackson-Landé (1973) or γ = 0.515 GeV⁻² from Bijker-Iachello (2004)). The model is then analytically continued to the time-like region by replacing Q² with -t, introducing a complex phase e^(iδ) into the intrinsic form factor, and substituting the vector-meson dominance term with a Breit-Wigner formula for the a₁(1260) axial meson with width Γ_A = 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Global fit with γ = 0.25 GeV⁻² (IJL parametrization) gives α = 1.57 ± 0.04 with χ²/n.d.f. = 85.36/48 = 1.78
- Global fit with γ = 0.515 GeV⁻² (BI parametrization) gives α = 0.95 ± 0.05 with χ²/n.d.f. = 69.60/48 = 1.45
- The IJL parametrization predicts a zero in the axial form factor at Q² = 2.65 GeV² in the space-like region
- Axial radius √⟨r²⟩_A = 0.60 fm for IJL, 0.62 fm for BI, and 0.64 fm for the dipole parametrization
- The axial meson mass m_A = 1.230 GeV (mass of a₁(1260)) is used as a fixed parameter, not fitted
- The dipole axial mass M_A = 1.069 ± 0.018 GeV from charged pion electroproduction is used for comparison
- The axial form factor at Q² = 0 is G_A(0) = 1.2695 ± 0.0029 from neutron β decay

## Context

This work extends the two-component model previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Iachello-Jackson-Landé (1973) and Bijker-Iachello (2004) to the axial form factor. The model provides an alternative to the standard dipole parametrization (Bernard et al., 2002) and offers predictions for the time-like region, which is relevant for experiments at FAIR (Germany), BES3 (China), and DANAE (Italy). The results also impact the extraction of strange form factors from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as G0 and SAMPLE.
