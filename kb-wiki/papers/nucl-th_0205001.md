# Selection rules in the double beta decay of deformed nuclei

> The 2 nu double beta decay half-lives of six nuclei, whose decays were previously reported as theoretically forbidden, are calculated by including the pairing interaction, which mixes different occupations and opens up the possibility of the decay. All allowed channels for the 0 nu double beta decay are also computed. The estimated 2 nu double beta half-lives suggest that measurements in 244Pu may find positive signals, and that planned experiments would succeed in detecting the 2 nu double beta decay in 160Gd. Limits for the zero neutrino mode, in the analyzed deformed emitters, are predicted.

## Key claim

The two-neutrino double beta decay of six deformed nuclei previously reported as theoretically forbidden is shown to be possible when pairing interaction mixes configurations with different occupation numbers, but the resulting half-lives are suppressed by a factor of about 10 compared to allowed decays, except for 244Pu where near-degenerate configurations produce maximal mixing and a shorter half-life.

## Method

The authors used the pseudo SU(3) shell model to describe the ground states of six deformed nuclei (154Sm, 160Gd, 170Er, 176Yb, 232Th, 244Pu) and their daughters, representing each ground state as a linear combination of two configurations: the dominant configuration with the most probable occupations and an excited configuration with different occupation numbers. The pairing interaction matrix element h_pair between these configurations was calculated, and the mixing coefficients x_1 and x_2 were obtained by diagonalizing a 2x2 Hamiltonian matrix. The two-neutrino matrix elements were evaluated using the summation method with the Gamow-Teller operator, and the zero-neutrino matrix elements were computed using the neutrino potential in the closure approximation.

## Key numbers

- 154Sm: 2ν half-life = 1.04 × 10^23 yr (Q = 1.251 MeV)
- 160Gd: 2ν half-life = 6.02 × 10^21 yr (Q = 1.730 MeV)
- 170Er: 2ν half-life = 1.10 × 10^25 yr (Q = 0.654 MeV)
- 176Yb: 2ν half-life = 2.77 × 10^23 yr (Q = 1.086 MeV)
- 232Th: 2ν half-life = 5.30 × 10^22 yr (Q = 0.858 MeV)
- 244Pu: 2ν half-life = 6.43 × 10^20 yr (Q = 1.352 MeV)
- 244Pu: mixing coefficient x_2 = 0.673, with h_pair = 0.419 MeV and ΔE = 0.080 MeV (near-degenerate configurations)
- 160Gd: 0ν half-life × ⟨m_ν⟩^2 = 2.09 × 10^25 yr eV^2 (M_0ν = 0.919)
- 244Pu: 0ν half-life × ⟨m_ν⟩^2 = 1.30 × 10^24 yr eV^2 (M_0ν = 1.171)

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) calculations of Cas94, Hir95a, Hir95b, Hir95c, Hir95d, which predicted complete suppression of 2ν decay for these six nuclei, by including pairing-induced mixing between different occupation configurations. The predicted 2ν half-life for 160Gd (6.02 × 10^21 yr) suggests that planned experiments using GSO crystals (Dan00) could detect this decay, while the 0ν half-life for 160Gd is at least three orders of magnitude larger than the 2ν half-life, making background suppression less effective than optimistically envisioned in Dan00. The results for 244Pu, with a 2ν half-life of 6.43 × 10^20 yr, are not far from the limits reported in the Livermore experiment (Moo92).
