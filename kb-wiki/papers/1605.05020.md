# Productions of J/psi mesons in p-Pb collisions at 5 TeV

> The rapidity distributions of $J/ψ$ mesons produced in proton-lead ($p$-Pb) collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5$ TeV are studied by using a multisource thermal model and compared with the experimental data of the LHCb and ALICE Collaborations. Correspondingly, the pseudorapidity distributions are accurately obtained from the parameters extracted from the rapidity distributions. At the same time, the transverse momentum distributions in the same experiments are described by the simplest Erlang distribution which is the folding result of two exponential distributions which are contributed by the target and projectile partons respectively.

## Key claim

The multisource thermal model, using an Erlang distribution for transverse momentum and a three-source picture for rapidity, can describe the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of J/psi mesons produced in p-Pb collisions at 5 TeV, but the simplest single-component Erlang distribution systematically underestimates the tail of the transverse momentum distribution, requiring a two-component Erlang distribution for a better description.

## Method

The authors applied a multisource thermal model where J/psi production is treated as a hard process from parton-parton collisions between a target parton and a projectile parton. Each parton contributes an exponential transverse momentum distribution, and the folding of two such exponentials yields an Erlang distribution for the J/psi transverse momentum. In rapidity space, sources are divided into three groups (central, target fragmentation, projectile fragmentation) with different rapidity shifts, and the model uses Monte Carlo sampling to generate the rapidity, pseudorapidity, and transverse momentum distributions, which are then fitted to LHCb and ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For prompt J/psi in the full rapidity range: total production cross-section sigma_0 = (5099.8 +/- 350.0) microbarn, with mean parton transverse momentum <p_Ti> = 1.50 +/- 0.10 GeV/c, backward rapidity shift y_T = -4.41 +/- 0.40, forward rapidity shift y_P = 3.78 +/- 0.40, and chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.394.
* For J/psi from b-hadron decays in the full rapidity range: total production cross-section sigma_0 = (629.2 +/- 41.0) microbarn, with <p_Ti> = 1.50 +/- 0.10 GeV/c, y_T = -3.86 +/- 0.40, y_P = 3.67 +/- 0.35, and chi-squared/dof = 2.299.
* For inclusive J/psi to mu+mu-: total production cross-section sigma_0 = (6135.5 +/- 510.0) microbarn, with <p_Ti> = 1.40 +/- 0.08 GeV/c, y_T = -4.20 +/- 0.40, y_P = 3.67 +/- 0.40, and chi-squared/dof = 0.454.
* The mean number of p-nucleon collisions in p-Pb collisions is 2.7, with mean rapidity shifts in the backward and forward regions calculated as -4.06 and 3.73 respectively, consistent with the fitted y_T and y_P values.
* For the two-component Erlang fit to prompt J/psi in the rapidity range 1.5 < y < 4.0 (Fig. 2a): first component <p_Ti>_1st = 1.31 +/- 0.07 GeV/c, second component <p_Ti>_2nd = 1.80 +/- 0.16 GeV/c, with k_1st = 0.80 +/- 0.10 and chi-squared/dof = 0.917.

## Context

This work extends the multisource thermal model, previously applied to light hadrons in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions by Liu et al. (2015), to heavy quarkonium production (J/psi) in asymmetric p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The model describes experimental data from the LHCb Collaboration (2014) and ALICE Collaboration (2014, 2015), and the two-component Erlang distribution is presented as a universal statistical law applicable across particle and nuclear physics, as well as other fields. The results enable further studies of cold nuclear matter effects and parton energy loss in proton-nucleus collisions, and the authors note that the physics underlying the Erlang distribution remains an open question.
