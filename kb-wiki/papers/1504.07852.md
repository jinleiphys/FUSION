# Low-energy theorems for nucleon-nucleon scattering at unphysical pion masses

> The longest-range part of the nuclear force due to the one-pion exchange governs the energy dependence of the scattering amplitude in the near-threshold region and imposes correlations between the coefficients in the effective range expansion. These correlations may be regarded as low-energy theorems and are known to hold to a high accuracy in the neutron-proton 3S1 partial wave. We generalize the low-energy theorems to the case of unphysical pion masses and provide results for the correlations between the coefficients in the effective range expansion in this partial wave for pion masses up to Mpi ~ 400 MeV. We discuss the implications of our findings for the available and upcoming lattice-QCD simulations of two-nucleon observables.

## Key claim

The correlations between coefficients in the effective range expansion (ERE) for neutron-proton scattering in the \(^3\)S\(_1\) partial wave, which are determined by the long-range one-pion exchange potential, can be generalized to unphysical pion masses up to \(M_\pi \sim 400\) MeV, providing model-independent low-energy theorems (LETs) that hold to high accuracy even when the pion mass is varied.

## Method

The authors employed a modified version of Weinberg's chiral effective field theory (EFT) based on the Kadyshevsky equation, which is a three-dimensional integral equation satisfying relativistic elastic unitarity. The leading-order (LO) amplitude was obtained by solving this equation numerically in the partial wave basis, using the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) plus a momentum-independent contact interaction whose strength was adjusted to reproduce the S-wave scattering length. The integral equation was regularized with an ultraviolet momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\), and the limit \(\Lambda \to \infty\) was taken when calculating phase shifts, after which the effective range and shape parameters were extracted by matching the truncated ERE to the effective range function.

## Key numbers

- The physical pion mass is taken as \(M_\pi^{\rm phys} \equiv (2 M_{\pi^\pm} + M_{\pi^0})/3 = 138.03\) MeV.
- The pion decay constant and axial vector coupling are set to \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and \(g_A = 1.267\) at the physical pion mass.
- The low-energy theorems are generalized for pion masses up to \(M_\pi \sim 400\) MeV.
- The convergence radius of the ERE for NN scattering is bounded by \(|E_{\rm lab}| \sim M_\pi^2/(2 m_N) = 10.5\) MeV at the physical pion mass.
- For the \(^3\)S\(_1\) partial wave, when \(1/(a M_\pi) \simeq 1.4\), a pole in the effective range function appears at \(k \simeq 60\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorems previously established for the physical pion mass in the neutron-proton \(^3\)S\(_1\) partial wave (Epelbaum 2009, Epelbaum 2010) to unphysical pion masses, providing a framework for consistency checks of lattice-QCD simulations. The results enable nontrivial tests of calculations by groups such as NPLQCD (Beane 2013) and Yamazaki (2015), and can be used to predict scattering length, effective range, and shape parameters from deuteron binding energies computed on the lattice, complementing earlier chiral EFT studies of quark mass dependence (Berengut 2013, Epelbaum 2013).
