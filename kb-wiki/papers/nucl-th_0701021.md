# The role of $Δ^{++*}(1620)$ resonances in $pp \to nK^+Σ^+$ reaction and its important implications

> The $pp \to nK^+Σ^+$ reaction is a very good isospin 3/2 filter for studying $Δ^{++*}$ decaying to $K^+Σ^+$. With an effective Lagrangian approach, contributions from a previous ignored sub-$K^+Σ^+$-threshold resonance $Δ^{++*}(1620)1/2^-$ are fully included in addition to those already considered in previous calculations. It is found that the $Δ^{++*}(1620)1/2^-$ resonance gives an overwhelmingly dominant contribution for energies very close to threshold, with a very important contribution from the t-channel $ρ$ exchange. This solves the problem that all previous calculations seriously underestimate the near-threshold cross section by order(s) of magnitude. Many important implications of the results are discussed.

# Wiki Page: Role of Δ⁺⁺*(1620) Resonances in pp → nK⁺Σ⁺ Reaction

## Key claim
The sub-K⁺Σ⁺-threshold resonance Δ⁺⁺*(1620)1/2⁻, previously ignored in calculations, gives an overwhelmingly dominant contribution to the pp → nK⁺Σ⁺ cross section at energies very close to threshold, solving the problem that all previous calculations seriously underestimated the near-threshold cross section by orders of magnitude. The t-channel ρ exchange also provides a very important contribution.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with a Lorentz covariant orbital-spin scheme to compute Feynman diagrams for pp → nK⁺Σ⁺. They included contributions from Δ⁺⁺*(1620)1/2⁻ production via both π⁺ and ρ⁺ exchange, and Δ⁺⁺*(1920)3/2⁺ production via π⁺ exchange, with coupling constants determined from experimental partial decay widths and SU(3) symmetry. The n-Σ⁺ final state interaction was included using the Watson-Migdal approach with effective-range parameters taken as similar to those for p-Λ.

## Key numbers
- The Δ⁺⁺*(1620) resonance has mass 1620 MeV and full width 150 MeV, with branching ratios 25% to πN, 14% to ρN, and coupling g²/4π = 0.06 to KΣ from SU(3) symmetry.
- The Δ⁺⁺*(1920) resonance has mass 1920 MeV and full width 200 MeV, with branching ratio 12.5% to πN and 2.1% to KΣ.
- The n-Σ⁺ final state interaction enhances the total cross section by a factor of about 3 for the two lowest data points at excess energies Q = 13 MeV and Q = 60 MeV.
- The calculation reproduces experimental data for proton beam kinetic energies T_P between 1.5 and 6.0 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous resonance model calculations by Tsushima et al. (2000), Sibirtsev et al. (2006), and Shyam et al. (2006), which only considered Δ⁺⁺*(1920) and failed to reproduce the near-threshold cross section measured by the COSY-11 collaboration (2006). The Jülich meson exchange model by Gasparyan et al. (2004) also underestimated the data. The authors show that including the sub-threshold Δ⁺⁺*(1620) resonance and ρ exchange resolves this discrepancy. The results enable improved modeling of strangeness production in heavy ion collisions and suggest the existence of a 1/2⁻ SU(3) decuplet of vector-meson-baryon S-wave states, including a predicted Ω*1/2⁻ resonance around 2160 MeV to be searched for at BES3.
