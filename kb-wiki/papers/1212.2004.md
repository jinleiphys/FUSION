# One-pion exchange in the X(3872) revisited

> We re-examine one-pion exchange as a possible binding mechanism in the X(3872) charmonium-like state and argue it to be not sufficiently binding for this purpose. We conclude therefore that other short-range dynamics are responsible for the X formation.

## Key claim

One-pion exchange (OPE) alone is not sufficiently binding to produce the X(3872) as a bound state; the authors conclude that other short-range dynamics must be responsible for its formation, contradicting the claim of Thomas and Close (2012) that OPE can bind the system with a cut-off as low as 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the D0D̅*0 and D+D̅*- systems, including both neutral and charged pion exchange with proper isospin factors. They went beyond the static approximation by using relativistic kinematics for the pion, including the imaginary parts of the OPE potential from the D* → Dπ decay widths, and incorporating the momentum-dependent coefficients α in the three-body propagator. The minimal cut-off parameter Λmin required to produce a bound state exactly at the D0D̅*0 threshold was computed for different treatments (static vs. full, with and without the second ordering term V2).

## Key numbers

- For the phenomenologically adequate coupling V0 = 1.3 MeV (derived from D*+ pionic decay data), no bound state exists for Λ = 1 GeV; a bound state at threshold requires Λmin = 3800 MeV with the full first-ordering potential alone, and Λmin = 2100 MeV when the second ordering term is added in static form.
- In the static limit with V0 = 1.3 MeV, the minimal cut-off is Λminstat = 2750 MeV for the first ordering alone, and Λminstat = 1650 MeV with both orderings.
- For V0 = 2.6 MeV (the effective coupling used by Thomas and Close (2012)), the full treatment with both orderings gives Λmin = 1050 MeV, while the static approximation with both orderings gives Λminstat ≈ 1 GeV, reproducing the result of Thomas and Close (2012).
- The D*+ total width is Γc = (96 ± 22) keV, and the D*0 total width is estimated as Γ0 = (65 ± 15) keV.
- The mass difference between charged and neutral D̅D* thresholds is δ = 8.08 MeV.

## Context

This work revisits the long-standing question of whether one-pion exchange can bind the X(3872) as a deuteron-like D̅D* molecule, previously studied by Thomas and Close (2012) (who claimed OPE is sufficient) and by Chen et al. (2012) (who found OPE insufficient for the neutral channel alone). The authors extend these calculations by including both neutral and charged channels, going beyond the static approximation, and incorporating the imaginary parts of the OPE potential from D* decays. The result supports the conclusion of Chen et al. (2012) that OPE alone cannot bind the X(3872), and points to the need for short-range dynamics such as s-channel coupling to charmonium as the binding mechanism.
