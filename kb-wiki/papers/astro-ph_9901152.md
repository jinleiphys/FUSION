# Neutron stars and quark phases in the NJL model

> We study the possible existence of deconfined quark matter in the interior of neutron stars using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to describe the quark phase. We find that typical neutron stars with masses around 1.4 solar masses do not possess any deconfined quark matter in their center. This can be traced back to the property of the NJL model which suggests a large constituent strange quark mass over a wide range of densities.

## Key claim

For typical neutron stars with masses around 1.4 solar masses, the NJL model predicts no deconfined quark matter in their center, but this result depends on the NJL model's property of predicting a large constituent strange quark mass over a wide range of densities, which delays the appearance of strange quarks and thus the onset of a quark phase.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in Hartree (mean field) approximation to describe the quark phase, with a sharp three-momentum cut-off regularization. They constructed the phase transition between hadronic matter (described by four different relativistic mean field models including hyperonic degrees of freedom, matched to the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland EOS at subnuclear densities) and quark matter using a Gibbs construction for chemical and mechanical equilibrium, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark masses: \( m^*_{q,\rm vac} = 367.7\,\)MeV for light quarks, \( m^*_{s,\rm vac} = 549.5\,\)MeV for strange quarks
- NJL model parameters: cut-off \(\Lambda = 602.3\,\)MeV, \(G \Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(K \Lambda^5 = 12.36\), current quark masses \(m_q = 5.5\,\)MeV and \(m_s = 140.7\,\)MeV
- Vacuum bag constant: \(B_0 = (217.6 \,\mbox{MeV})^{4}\)
- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\,\)fm\(^{-3}\), energy density \(\epsilon_0 = 140\,\)MeV/fm\(^3\)
- The plateau value of the bag pressure in the NJL model is \(B_{\rm MIT}^{1/4} = 170\,\)MeV, and the plateau value of the strange quark mass is \(m_s^{\rm MIT} = 465\,\)MeV
- Typical neutron stars with masses around 1.4 solar masses do not possess deconfined quark matter in their center

## Context

This work extends the study of Buballa and Oertel (1998), who found that strange quark matter is not absolutely stable in the NJL model, by performing a detailed phase transition calculation to determine whether quark phases could still exist inside neutron stars. The authors compare their NJL-based results with the MIT bag model approach of Farhi and Jaffe (1984) and with relativistic mean field models including hyperons (Schaffner and Mishustin, 1996; Gosh, Phatak and Sahu, 1995). The finding that typical neutron stars lack deconfined quark matter in their center suggests that future observations of neutron star masses and radii could help discriminate between different models of dense matter.
