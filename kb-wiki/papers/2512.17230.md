# Dynamical equation for quark spin polarization in the rotating medium

> In non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the produced quark-gluon plasma (QGP) behaves approximately as a rotating fluid due to the system's initial angular momentum. In this rotating fluid, the spins of quarks become polarized due to the coupling between spin and angular momentum, as well as random spin-spin interactions. Since the Landau-Lifshitz (LL) equation effectively describes the spin polarization of fermions in a medium with a magnetic field, we derive a phenomenological equation analogous to the LL equation for heavy quark spin dynamics in the rotating medium. The spin-angular momentum coupling and random spin-spin interactions are incorporated, leading to a detailed balance of heavy quark spin distributions. This equation provides insight into the spin dynamics of heavy quarks and quarkonium in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive a phenomenological Landau-Lifshitz-like equation for heavy quark spin dynamics in a rotating medium, incorporating both spin-angular momentum coupling and random spin-spin interactions, but the derivation relies on a non-relativistic approximation of the heavy quark Hamiltonian and the introduction of a noise term with a quantum correction factor of 1/3 to mimic discrete spin distributions.

## Method

Starting from the Dirac equation in a rotating reference frame, the authors apply the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to obtain a non-relativistic Hamiltonian for heavy quarks. They then construct a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian to describe spin polarization and derive a quantum Landau-Lifshitz-like equation for the time evolution of the heavy quark spin vector, adding a Gaussian white noise term to account for random spin-spin interactions. The noise strength is determined via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, with a quantum correction factor \(C_Q = 1/3\) introduced to match the equilibrium spin distribution for spin-1/2 fermions.

## Key numbers

* Medium vorticity \(\omega = 20\) MeV used as a constant value for numerical simulations, with variations at \(\omega = 10\) MeV and \(\omega = 30\) MeV.
* Medium temperature \(T = 0.2\) GeV used as a constant value, typical for semi-central collisions at RHIC energies.
* Damping factor values explored: \(\lambda = 0.03, 0.05, 0.1\).
* In the equilibrium limit, the spin polarization \(\langle S_z \rangle / |S|\) is proportional to \(\omega / (2T)\), consistent with equilibrium theories.
* The ratio of classical to quantum \(\langle S_z \rangle\) values for spin-1/2 fermions is calculated to be \(1/3\), leading to the quantum correction factor \(C_Q = 1/3\) in the noise term.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Lifshitz equation framework, previously applied to heavy quark spin polarization in magnetic fields by Liu (2024) and Li (2025), to the case of rotating QCD medium without magnetic fields. It builds on experimental observations of \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin polarization by STAR (2017) and CMS (2025), and the measurement of \(J/\psi\) spin alignment by ALICE (2022). The derived equation enables future phenomenological studies of heavy quark spin polarization and charmonium spin alignment in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly addressing how the spin-angular momentum coupling (which polarizes quarks and antiquarks similarly, unlike magnetic fields) can alter vector meson spin alignment.
