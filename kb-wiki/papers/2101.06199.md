# Coulomb corrections to Fermi beta decay in nuclei

> We study the influence of the Coulomb force on the Fermi beta-decays in nuclei. This work is composed of two main parts. In the first part, we calculate the Coulomb corrections to super-allowed beta decay. We use the notion of the isovector monopole state and the self-consistent charge-exchange Random Phase Approximation to compute the correction. In the second part of this work, we examine the influence of the anti-analog state on isospin mixing in the isobaric analog state and the correction to the beta-decay Fermi transition.

## Key claim

The Coulomb correction δ_C for super-allowed Fermi beta decays is smaller by a factor of 1.5–2 compared to the widely used Hardy-Towner values, because the collective nature of the isovector monopole state shifts its energy upward to about 3ħω, reducing the isospin mixing that enters δ_C.

## Method

The authors used self-consistent charge-exchange Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF-RPA) with five different Skyrme interactions (SIII, SKM*, SLy4, BSK17, SAMi0) to compute the isovector monopole (IVM) strength distribution and isospin admixture in the ground states of N=Z closed-shell nuclei (⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Ni, ⁸⁰Zr, ¹⁰⁰Sn). From these results they extracted the IVM energy Ē₀ and the symmetry energy parameter V₁, then applied a closed-form expression δ_C = 8(T+1)(V₁/ξħωA)ε₁² to obtain the Coulomb correction for the neighboring T=1 super-allowed emitters (⁴²Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁸²Zr, ¹⁰²Sn). In the second part, they evaluated the contribution of the anti-analog state to isospin mixing in the isobaric analog state using both a harmonic-oscillator estimate and self-consistent HF calculations for ⁸⁸Sr.

## Key numbers

* For ⁴²Ca, δ_C ranges from 0.32% (SAMi0) to 0.46% (SLy4) across the five Skyrme interactions.
* For ⁵⁸Ni, δ_C ranges from 0.52% (SAMi0) to 0.68% (SLy4).
* For ⁸²Zr, δ_C ranges from 0.68% (SAMi0) to 0.98% (SLy4).
* For ¹⁰²Sn, δ_C ranges from 0.98% (SIII, SAMi0) to 1.30% (SLy4).
* The isospin admixture ε̄² in the N=Z nucleus ⁴⁰Ca is 0.68% (SIII) to 0.78% (SKM*); in ¹⁰⁰Sn it is 4.54% (SIII) to 5.34% (SKM*).
* The IVM energy Ē₀ in ⁴⁰Ca is 31.13 MeV (SLy4) to 35.08 MeV (SIII); in ¹⁰⁰Sn it is 30.83 MeV (SLy4) to 34.35 MeV (SIII).
* For the anti-analog mixing in ⁸⁸Sr, the Coulomb matrix element ⟨Ā|V_C|A⟩ is 0.257 MeV (SKM*) to 0.331 MeV (SAMi0), yielding δ_C = 0.14% to 0.23%.
* The harmonic-oscillator estimate for the anti-analog contribution in A=32 (T=2) gives δ_C = 0.25%.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Auerbach (2009) by replacing schematic sum-rule estimates with fully microscopic HF-RPA calculations of isospin mixing from Loc (2019). The resulting δ_C values are about a factor of two smaller than those of Hardy and Towner (2020) and are closer to the results of Liang et al. (2009) and Rodin (2013), which also incorporate some collective effects. The paper does not attempt to compute V_ud or test CKM unitarity; it focuses solely on the nuclear-structure aspect of the Coulomb correction. The second part on the anti-analog state addresses isospin mixing in the isobaric analog state, which is relevant for understanding isospin-forbidden decays and the spreading width of analog resonances, particularly in proton-rich exotic nuclei with low isospin where the anti-analog mechanism can become significant.
