# To bin or not to bin: does binning in multiplicity reliably suppress unwanted volume fluctuations?

> In this study, we examine the effect of the so-called Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) on the measurement of (net-)proton number cumulants in nucleus-nucleus collisions. We present an analytically tractable model, which includes correlations between multiplicity and proton number similar to those generated by the decay of baryon resonances. Within this model, we analyze the circumstances under which the CBWC method correctly removes the undesired effects of volume or impact parameter fluctuations. Additionally, we explore situations where the method fails and produces misleading results.

## Key claim

The Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method, which bins events by charged-particle multiplicity to suppress volume fluctuations, can fail when there are strong dynamical correlations between multiplicity and proton number, such as those generated by baryon resonance decays, leading to an underestimation of the true cumulants; the method works best when such correlations are weak.

## Method

The authors construct an analytically tractable model using a bi-variate (and later tri-variate) Poisson distribution to represent the joint probability of proton number and charged-particle multiplicity, with correlations introduced via a parameter О» that mimics the decay of О” resonances into protons and pions. Volume fluctuations are modeled by a Gamma distribution for the reduced volume, and the joint distribution is obtained by folding the Poisson model with this volume distribution. Cumulants of the (net-)proton number are then computed analytically for the full distribution including volume fluctuations, for the true fixed-volume distribution, and for the CBWC-corrected distribution, allowing direct comparison of the three cases.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of volume fluctuation corrections in heavy-ion collisions, building on the CBWC method introduced by Luo (2013) and used by the STAR collaboration, and on the general treatment of volume fluctuations by Skokov, Koch, and others (2012). The model specifically addresses the concern that resonance decays (e.g., О” вҶ’ p + ПҖ) create correlations between multiplicity and proton number that the CBWC may misinterpret as volume fluctuations, leading to overcompensation. The results enable a more critical assessment of CBWC-corrected cumulant measurements from experiments such as STAR at RHIC, and suggest that alternative methods, such as the Edgeworth expansion approach proposed by Wang (2025) or the use of multiplicity moments by Rustamov (2022) and Holzmann (2024), may be needed when resonance decay correlations are strong.
