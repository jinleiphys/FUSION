# Spin alignment of vector mesons in local equilibrium by Zubarev's approach

> We compute the $00$ element of the spin density matrix, denoted as $ρ_{00}$ and called the spin alignment, up to the second order of the gradient expansion in local equilibrium by Zubarev's approach. In the first order, we obtain $ρ_{00}=1/3$, meaning that the contributions from thermal vorticity and shear stress tensor are vanishing. The non-vanishing contributions to $ρ_{00}-1/3$ appear in the second order of gradients in the Belinfante and canonical cases. We also discuss the properties of the spin density matrix under the time reversal transformation. The effective transport coefficient for the spin alignment induced by the thermal shear stress tensor is T-odd in the first order, implying that the first order effect is dissipative.

## Key claim

The spin alignment of vector mesons, quantified by the deviation of the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ from 1/3, vanishes at first order in the gradient expansion in local equilibrium; non-vanishing contributions to ρ₀₀ − 1/3 appear only at second order in gradients, and the first-order effect from the thermal shear stress tensor is T-odd and therefore dissipative.

## Method

The authors use Zubarev's approach in quantum statistical field theory to compute the spin density matrix for vector mesons in local equilibrium. They expand the local equilibrium density operators in the Belinfante and canonical forms to second order in gradients of the inverse temperature four-vector βᵘ = uᵘ/T, and evaluate the matrix-valued spin dependent distribution function (MVSD) for a Proca field using the isochronous hypersurface. The spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ is then obtained from the normalized MVSD up to second order in the gradient expansion.

## Key numbers

* ρ₀₀ = 1/3 at first order in the gradient expansion, meaning contributions from thermal vorticity and shear stress tensor vanish at this order.
* Non-vanishing contributions to ρ₀₀ − 1/3 appear only at second order in gradients for both the Belinfante and canonical cases.
* The effective transport coefficient for spin alignment induced by the thermal shear stress tensor is T-odd at first order, implying the first order effect is dissipative.

## Context

This work extends the application of Zubarev's approach, previously used to study spin polarization of Λ hyperons in global equilibrium (Becattini, 2013) and local equilibrium (Becattini, 2021), to the spin alignment of vector mesons. It addresses the discrepancy between the large ρ₀₀ − 1/3 observed for φ mesons by STAR (2022) and the small values expected from first-order hydrodynamic effects. The results enable systematic computation of spin alignment from second-order gradient effects in heavy-ion collisions, complementing studies based on the relativistic quark coalescence model (Sheng, 2019) and shear viscous tensor contributions (Li, 2022).
