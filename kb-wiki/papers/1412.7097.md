# Flavor instabilities in the neutrino line model

> A dense neutrino medium can experience collective flavor oscillations through nonlinear neutrino-neutrino refraction. To make this multi-dimensional flavor transport problem more tractable, all existing studies have assumed certain symmetries (e.g., the spatial homogeneity and directional isotropy in the early universe) to reduce the dimensionality of the problem. In this work we show that, if both the directional and spatial symmetries are not enforced in the neutrino line model, collective oscillations can develop in the physical regimes where the symmetry-preserving oscillation modes are stable. Our results suggest that collective neutrino oscillations in real astrophysical environments (such as core-collapse supernovae and black-hole accretion discs) can be qualitatively different from the predictions based on existing models in which spatial and directional symmetries are artificially imposed.

## Key claim

Collective neutrino oscillations can develop in the neutrino line model even in physical regimes where symmetry-preserving oscillation modes are stable, provided that both directional and spatial symmetries are not artificially enforced; this suggests that collective oscillations in real astrophysical environments may be qualitatively different from predictions based on models with imposed symmetries.

## Method

The authors studied the neutrino line model, a two-dimensional (x-z) model with two spatial dimensions and two momentum dimensions (single energy E, two propagation directions L and R), where neutrinos are emitted from the x-axis. They performed a linearized stability analysis by expanding the equations of motion in Fourier modes along the x-direction and computing the eigenvalues of the stability matrix to identify unstable collective oscillation modes with exponential growth rates.

## Key numbers

- The size of the periodic box for neutrino sources on the x-axis was set to L = 20π/ω, where ω is the vacuum neutrino oscillation frequency.
- Neutrino propagation directions were given by unit vectors [v_x, v_z] = [±√3/2, 1/2], making a 60° angle with the z axis.
- For the bipolar model (both symmetries enforced), collective oscillations occur only in inverted mass hierarchy and within the regime 2ω/(1+√α)² < μ < 2ω/(1-√α)², where α is the antineutrino-to-neutrino flux ratio.
- The ratio of antineutrino to neutrino number flux was α = 0.8 in one case and α = 0.5 in another.
- The Fermi coupling constant is G_F ≈ (293 GeV)⁻².

## Context

This work extends the bipolar model (Kostelecky 1994, Duan 2005, Hannestad 2006, Duan 2007) and the two-beam model (Raffelt 2013) by removing both spatial translation symmetry and directional left-right symmetry simultaneously. It also generalizes the toy model of Mangano (2014) which had α=1 and only symmetric modes. The results suggest that collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae and black-hole accretion discs may occur at higher neutrino fluxes than predicted by symmetry-preserving models, potentially affecting nucleosynthesis more strongly than previously expected (Pastor 2002, Balantekin 2004, Chakraborty 2009, Duan 2010).
