# Invariants of Collective Neutrino Oscillations

> We consider the flavor evolution of a dense neutrino gas by taking into account both vacuum oscillations and self interactions of neutrinos. We examine the system from a many-body perspective as well as from the point of view of an effective one-body description formulated in terms of the neutrino polarization vectors. We show that, in the single angle approximation, both the many-body picture and the effective one-particle picture possess several constants of motion. We write down these constants of motion explicitly in terms of the neutrino isospin operators for the many-body case and in terms of the polarization vectors for the effective one-body case. The existence of these constants of motion is a direct consequence of the fact that the collective neutrino oscillation Hamiltonian belongs to the class of Gaudin Hamiltonians. This class of Hamiltonians also includes the (reduced) BCS pairing Hamiltonian describing superconductivity. We point out the similarity between the collective neutrino oscillation Hamiltonian and the BCS pairing Hamiltonian. The constants of motion manifest the exact solvability of the system. Borrowing the well established techniques of calculating the exact BCS spectrum, we present exact eigenstates and eigenvalues of both the many-body and the effective one-particle Hamiltonians describing the collective neutrino oscillations. For the effective one-body case, we show that spectral splits of neutrinos can be understood in terms of the adiabatic evolution of some quasi-particle degrees of freedom from a high density region where they coincide with flavor eigenstates to the vacuum where they coincide with mass eigenstates. We write down the most general consistency equations which should be satisfied by the effective one-body eigenstates and show that they reduce to the spectral split consistency equations for the appropriate initial conditions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the collective neutrino oscillation Hamiltonian, under the single angle approximation, belongs to the class of Gaudin Hamiltonians and therefore possesses multiple constants of motion that manifest its exact solvability, with the caveat that this result holds specifically within the single angle approximation where the angular factor (1 - cos θ_pq) averages to zero.

## Method

The authors formulate the many-body neutrino Hamiltonian in terms of SU(2) flavor isospin operators and the effective one-body Hamiltonian in terms of neutrino polarization vectors, then identify the Hamiltonian as belonging to the Gaudin class which also includes the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian. They apply the Bethe ansatz method to obtain exact eigenstates and eigenvalues for both the many-body and effective one-particle Hamiltonians, and use Bogoliubov transformations to diagonalize the random phase approximation (RPA) Hamiltonian in terms of noninteracting quasi-particle states.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum oscillation frequency ω is defined as ω = δm²/(2p) where δm² = m₂² - m₁² and p is the neutrino energy
- The neutrino-neutrino coupling constant μ is defined as μ = √2 G_F / V, where G_F is the Fermi constant and V is the quantization volume
- The system with Ω different energy modes has 2Ω independent constants of motion: Ω invariants from the spin length conservation (L_ω = J_ω · J_ω) and Ω invariants from the Gaudin magnet Hamiltonians (h_ω)
- The conserved quantity C₀ = B · J equals -J^z = (N₂ - N₁)/2, where N₁ and N₂ are the total particle numbers for mass eigenstates ν₁ and ν₂

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach of Balantekin (2006) by identifying the collective neutrino oscillation Hamiltonian as a Gaudin Hamiltonian, establishing a formal connection to the exactly solvable BCS pairing Hamiltonian of Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957). The paper builds on earlier observations of conserved quantities by Raffelt (2007) and Duan (2007), and provides a complementary explanation to the spectral split phenomenon described by Raffelt (2007) in terms of adiabatic quasi-particle evolution. The results enable exact analytical solutions for collective neutrino oscillations in the single angle approximation, borrowing techniques from the Richardson-Gaudin integrable models.
