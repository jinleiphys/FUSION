# String-based axial and helicity-flip GPDs: a comparison to lattice QCD

> We construct an analytic, string based representation of the nucleon's axial and helicity flip conformal moments of generalized parton distributions that holds for any skewness and for both the quark and gluon channels. The starting point is the Mellin Barnes resummation of the conformal partial wave expansion, where the moments are parametrized by open (Reggeon) and closed string (Pomeron) trajectories with slopes determined by experimental form factors and meson/glueball spectroscopy. The forward limits are fixed by the empirical unpolarized and polarized parton distributions. Polynomiality, crossing symmetry and support are satisfied by construction. After NLO DGLAP ERBL evolution to $μ=2$ GeV our analytic framework (i) reproduces some of the currently available lattice moments of $\mathbb{E}$ and $\widetilde{\mathbb{H}}$ in the non singlet sector, (ii) predicts sea quark and gluon polarized moments that will be testable by forthcoming simulations and experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future EIC, and (iii) yields axial and helicity flip GPDs in $x$ space in reasonable agreement with lattice QCD.

## Key claim
The paper constructs an analytic, string-based representation of nucleon axial and helicity-flip generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that, after NLO DGLAP-ERBL evolution to 2 GeV, reproduces some available lattice moments of E and H̃ in the non-singlet sector, but the agreement is qualified by noting that the second moment of the E_{u-d} GPD shows discrepancy due to angular momentum conservation constraints not captured by the string model.

## Method
The authors start from a Mellin-Barnes resummation of the conformal partial wave expansion, parametrizing the conformal moments by open (Reggeon) and closed string (Pomeron) trajectories with slopes determined by experimental form factors and meson/glueball spectroscopy. The forward limits are fixed by empirical unpolarized and polarized parton distributions from the AAC (2006) and MSTW 2009 global fits at an initial scale of 1 GeV. The conformal moments are then evolved to 2 GeV using NLO DGLAP-ERBL kernels, and the inverse Mellin-Barnes transform is evaluated numerically to recover the x-space GPDs.

## Key numbers
- Regge slopes for the non-singlet helicity moments: α'_{u-d} = 0.3140 GeV^{-2} and α'_{u+d} = 0.2975 GeV^{-2} for H̃; α'_{u-d} = 1.3929 GeV^{-2} and α'_{u+d} = 1.1368 GeV^{-2} for E
- Regge slope for the polarized gluon singlet: α'_g = 1.179 GeV^{-2} for H̃
- The initial resolution scale is μ₀ = 1 GeV, evolved to μ = 2 GeV in the MS-bar scheme
- Lattice data comparisons use ensembles with pion masses ranging from 130 MeV to 352 MeV and lattice spacings from 0.042 fm to 0.124 fm
- The string-based predictions reproduce lattice moments for j = 1-3 in the non-singlet sector up to |t| ≈ 3 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier string-based formalism for unpolarized GPDs (Mamo 2024) to the axial and helicity-flip sectors, generalizing the approach to arbitrary skewness. It provides a framework that satisfies polynomiality, crossing symmetry, and support by construction, and enables predictions for sea-quark and gluon polarized moments that can be tested by forthcoming lattice simulations and experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider. The approach contrasts with purely phenomenological parametrizations by using gauge/string duality to fix the skewness dependence through a hypergeometric kernel derived from cubic string field theory, building on the conformal partial-wave formalism of Mueller (2005) and the Regge pole approach of Nishio (2014).
