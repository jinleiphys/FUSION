# Microscopic approach to the proton asymmetry in the non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei

> The non--mesonic weak decay of polarized $Λ$-hypernuclei is studied with a microscopic diagrammatic formalism in which one- and two-nucleon induced decay mechanisms, $\vecΛ N \to NN$ and $\vecΛ NN \to NNN$, are considered together with (and on the same ground of) nucleon final state interactions. We adopt a nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation. Our approach adopts different one-meson-exchange weak transition potentials, while the strong interaction effects are accounted for by a Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction. We also consider the two-pion-exchange effect in the weak transition potential. Both the two-nucleon induced decay mechanism and the final state interactions reduce the magnitude of the asymmetry. The quantum interference terms considered in the present microscopic approach give rise to an opposite behavior of the asymmetry with increasing energy cuts to that observed in models describing the nucleon final state interactions semi-classically via the intranuclear cascade code. Our results for the asymmetry parameter in $^{12}_Λ$C obtained with different potential models are consistent with the asymmetry measured at KEK.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully quantum-mechanical, microscopic treatment of nucleon final state interactions (FSI) and two-nucleon induced decay mechanisms reduces the magnitude of the proton asymmetry parameter in the non-mesonic weak decay of polarized Λ-hypernuclei, and that quantum interference terms produce an opposite behavior of the asymmetry with increasing energy cuts compared to semi-classical intranuclear cascade models.

## Method

The authors employ a nuclear matter diagrammatic formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, computing the Λ self-energy from Feynman diagrams that include one-nucleon induced (ΛN → NN) and two-nucleon induced (ΛNN → NNN) decay mechanisms on equal footing with nucleon final state interactions. The weak transition potential incorporates one-meson-exchange contributions plus two-pion-exchange effects, while the strong interaction is described by a Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential; the spin-dependent observables are obtained by evaluating Goldstone diagrams with the Λ spin fixed to the polarization axis.

## Key numbers

- The two-nucleon induced decay contributions represent almost 30% of the decay width, yet they affect the asymmetry in a very moderate way.
- The intrinsic asymmetry a^{1N}_Λ predicted by one-meson-exchange models falls in the range from -0.7 up to -0.4.
- Results for the asymmetry parameter in ¹²_ΛC obtained with different potential models are consistent with the asymmetry measured at KEK.

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid approaches that combined shell-model weak decay descriptions with semi-classical intranuclear cascade codes for FSI, such as those by Alberico et al. (2005) and Chumillas et al. (2007). By treating both the weak decay and nucleon rescattering within a unified quantum-mechanical framework, the authors provide a self-consistent alternative that enables more reliable calculations of the observable asymmetry and nucleon emission spectra, and for the first time includes two-nucleon induced decays in the asymmetry calculation.
