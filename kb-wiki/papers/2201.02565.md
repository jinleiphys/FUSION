# On the definition of local spatial densities in hadrons

> We show that the matrix element of a local operator between hadronic states gives rise to an unambiguous definition of the associated spatial density. As an explicit example, we consider the charge density of a spinless particle in the rest and moving frames and clarify its relationship to the electric form factor. Our results suggest that the interpretation of the spatial densities of local operators and their moments such as the mean square charge radius needs to be revised.

## Key claim

The matrix element of a local operator between hadronic states yields an unambiguous definition of the associated spatial density, provided one uses spherically symmetric wave packets in the rest frame and takes the sharp-localization limit; this definition does not require the static approximation and remains valid even when the system's intrinsic size is comparable to or smaller than its Compton wavelength, contrary to claims by Jaffe (2021).

## Method

The authors start from the matrix element of the charge density operator between momentum eigenstates of a spin-0 system, expressed in terms of the electric form factor. They construct normalizable wave-packet states with characteristic size R, evaluate the charge density in the sharp-localization limit (R → 0) using dimensional counting, and impose spherical symmetry of the wave packet in the rest frame to obtain a wave-packet-independent result. The procedure is then generalized to boosted frames using Lorentz transformation properties of the wave packet, and the infinite-momentum-frame limit is recovered.

## Key numbers

* For the proton, using the dipole form factor with Λ² = 0.71 GeV², the mean square charge radius from the new definition is ⟨r²⟩ = 4 F'(0), yielding a proton charge radius of √⟨r_p²⟩ = 0.6866(3) fm, compared to the conventional value √⟨r_p²⟩_naive = 0.8409(4) fm from the Particle Data Group (2020).
* The second radial moment in three spatial dimensions is given by ⟨r²⟩ = 4 F'(0), in contrast to the naive Breit-frame result ⟨r²⟩_naive = 6 F'(0).
* In one spatial dimension, the second moment of the charge distribution vanishes identically, ⟨x²⟩ = 0, independent of the form factor slope Δ².

## Context

This work extends the debate on the definition of spatial densities in hadrons initiated by Hofstadter (1958), Ernst, Sachs and Wali (1960), and Sachs (1962), and later criticized by Burkardt (2000), Miller (2007, 2009, 2010, 2018, 2019), Jaffe (2021), and Freese and Miller (2021). The authors resolve the ambiguity raised by Jaffe (2021) by showing that a wave-packet-independent definition exists without requiring the static limit, and they demonstrate that the conventional Breit-frame density does not emerge from the exact result in the m → ∞ limit. The new relation ⟨r²⟩ = 4 F'(0) revises the interpretation of the mean square charge radius and enables consistent definitions of spatial densities for light hadrons and for gravitational form factors as introduced by Polyakov (2003) and Polyakov and Schweitzer (2018).
