# Azimthally sensitive femtoscopy and v2

> I investigate the correlation between spatial and flow anisotropy in determining the elliptic flow and azimuthal dependence of the HBT correlation radii in non-central nuclear collisions. It is shown that the correlation radii are in most cases dominantly sensitive to the anisotropy in space. In case of v2, the correlation depends strongly on particle species. A procedure for disentangling the spatial and the flow anisotropy is proposed.

## Key claim

The azimuthal dependence of HBT correlation radii is in most cases dominantly sensitive to spatial anisotropy, while the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) alone cannot disentangle spatial and flow anisotropy; a combined analysis of both observables for multiple particle species can uniquely determine the two anisotropy parameters.

## Method

The author uses two variants of an azimuthally anisotropic blast-wave model (Model 1, where velocity is perpendicular to the fireball surface, and Model 2, where velocity is directed radially) to calculate \(v_2\) and the azimuthal oscillation amplitudes of HBT radii \(R_o^2\) and \(R_s^2\) as functions of the spatial anisotropy parameter \(a\) and the flow anisotropy parameter \(\rho_2\). The models are then compared to STAR data for Au+Au collisions at 200 \(A\)GeV, centrality 20–30%.

## Key numbers

* For pions at \(p_T = 0.2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(p_T = 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) with \(T = 100\) MeV and \(\rho_0 = 0.88\), the correlation between \(a\) and \(\rho_2\) in \(v_2\) is shown in contour plots with steps of 0.02.
* For HBT calculations, parameters used are \(T = 0.1\) GeV, \(\rho_0 = 0.88\), \(R = 9.41\) fm, \(\tau_0 = 9\) fm/\(c\), \(\Delta\tau = 1\) fm/\(c\), with \(K_T = 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) and \(K_T = 0.9\) GeV/\(c\); oscillation amplitudes are shown in contour steps of 0.1.
* In the fit to STAR data, Model 1 uses \(a = 0.946\) and Model 2 uses \(a = 1.057\), with common parameters \(T = 0.12\) GeV, \(\rho_0 = 0.99\), \(\rho_2 = 0.035\), \(R = 9.41\) fm, \(\tau_0 = 5.02\) fm/\(c\), \(\Delta\tau = 2.9\) fm/\(c\); data cover \(K_T = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, 0.52\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion by Heinz and Kolb (2002) on distinguishing fireball states via HBT interferometry and addresses the STAR collaboration’s conclusion (2003) that spatial anisotropy cannot be determined from \(v_2\) alone. The proposed combined analysis of \(v_2\) and azimuthally sensitive HBT radii, building on the blast-wave model of Retiere and Lisa (2004), enables disentangling spatial and flow anisotropy in non-central collisions; the author notes that Model 1 matches RHIC data while Model 2 may be relevant for LHC conditions.
