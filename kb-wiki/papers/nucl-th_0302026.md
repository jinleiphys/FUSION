# Recent Advances in Dyson-Schwinger Studies

> There have been many demonstrations of the utility of the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD as a systematic, phenomenological framework for describing the perturbative and non-perturbative dynamics of hadrons in terms of Euclidean Green functions of quarks and gluons. Still, there remain some unanswered questions regarding the theoretical underpinnings of the approach. I review several studies that are shedding light on how these questions might be resolved and review predictions for some exotic meson states.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger framework provides a systematic, phenomenologically successful approach to hadron physics, but its theoretical underpinnings still have unresolved questions regarding truncation schemes, connection to lattice QCD, and analytic continuation to Minkowski space. The paper reviews recent studies that address these questions while noting that the robustness of truncation schemes varies by channel, with pseudoscalar and vector channels being particularly stable.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical studies of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) of QCD, focusing on three areas: convergence of truncation schemes for the quark-gluon vertex and Bethe-Salpeter kernel, comparison of DSE results for the quark propagator with lattice QCD simulations, and analytic continuation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation from Euclidean to Minkowski space. The author also presents a model calculation of exotic meson spectra using a separable approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses used in lattice comparison: 27, 50, and 102 MeV
- Gluon propagator scale parameter: Lambda_g = 1020 +/- 100 MeV, with renormalization scale zeta = 19 GeV
- Exotic pseudoscalar meson mass prediction: 1319 MeV
- Exotic vector meson mass predictions: 1440 and 1499 MeV
- Ground-state vector meson mass obtained from analytic continuation of the Bethe-Salpeter eigenvalue equation lambda(P^2 = -m_rho^2) = 1

## Context

This review extends the long line of DSE studies dating back to the 1950s-1960s, with particular focus on developments since the mid-1990s that have applied the framework to chiral physics, electromagnetic form factors, and scattering processes. The work connects to lattice QCD studies by Fischer, Maris, and Jarecke, and to specific calculations by Bender, Detmold, and others on truncation scheme convergence. The exotic meson predictions build on the separable model of Burden and Pichowsky (1996) and BQRTT. The paper enables future systematic improvements to DSE truncation schemes and extends the reach of DSE calculations to heavier meson masses through new analytic continuation methods.
