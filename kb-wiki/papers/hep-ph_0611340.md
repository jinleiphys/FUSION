# Associated Charmonium Production in Low Energy p-pbar Annihilation

> The QCD mechanisms underlying the exclusive strong decays and hadronic production amplitudes of charmonium remain poorly understood, despite decades of study and an increasingly detaled body of experimental information. One set of hadronic channels of special interest are those that include baryon-antibaryon states. These are being investigated experimentally at BES and CLEO-c in terms of their baryon resonance content, and are also of interest for the future PANDA experiment, in which charmonium and charmonium hybrids will be produced in p-pbar annihilation in association with light mesons. In this paper we develop a simple initial-state light meson emission model of the near-threshold associated charmonium production processes p pbar -> pi0 ccbar, and evaluate the differential and total cross sections for these reactions in this model. (Here we consider the ccbar states eta_c, J/psi, psi', chi_0 and chi_1.) The predicted near-threshold cross section for p pbar -> pi0 J/psi is found to be numerically similar to two previous theoretical estimates, and is roughly comparable to the (sparse) existing data for this process. The theoretical charmonium angular distributions predicted by this model are far from isotropic, which may be of interest for PANDA detector design studies.

# Associated Charmonium Production in Low Energy p-pbar Annihilation

## Key claim

The authors develop a simple hadronic "pole" model for associated charmonium production in proton-antiproton annihilation with an associated pion, and find that the predicted near-threshold cross section for p pbar -> pi0 J/psi is numerically similar to two previous theoretical estimates and roughly comparable to the sparse existing data, though the model appears to overestimate the cross section at center-of-mass energies near 3.5 GeV by about a factor of 2 when compared to the two published data points from E760.

## Method

The model treats the reaction p pbar -> pi0 Psi as a two-step process: initial pion emission from an incident proton or antiproton (using the known g_{pppi} coupling constant of 13.5 with a gamma_5 vertex), followed by direct annihilation of the remaining proton-antiproton pair into a charmonium state Psi via a pointlike p pbar Psi vertex. The authors derive analytic expressions for the unpolarized differential and total cross sections from the two tree-level Feynman diagrams, first in a massless pion limit and then for massive pions, and evaluate them numerically for five charmonium states (eta_c, J/psi, psi', chi_0, chi_1) using coupling constants extracted from experimental partial widths to p pbar.

## Key numbers

- The ppi0 coupling constant is taken as g_pi = 13.5.
- The predicted total cross section for p pbar -> pi0 eta_c is roughly a factor of 30 larger than that for p pbar -> pi0 J/psi in the plateau region above E_cm ~ 4 GeV.
- The predicted total cross section for p pbar -> pi0 chi_0 is roughly 5 times larger than that for p pbar -> pi0 J/psi.
- The predicted total cross sections for p pbar -> pi0 chi_1 and p pbar -> pi0 psi' are smaller than that for p pbar -> pi0 J/psi by about a factor of 5-10.
- The extracted coupling constants (10^3 * g_{p pbar Psi}) are: eta_c = 19.0 +/- 3.2, J/psi = 1.62 +/- 0.03, psi' = 0.97 +/- 0.04, chi_0 = 5.42 +/- 0.37, chi_1 = 1.03 +/- 0.07.
- The masses used are: m_pi0 = 0.1350 GeV, m_p = 0.9383 GeV, M_eta_c = 2.9804 GeV, M_J/psi = 3.0969 GeV, M_chi_0 = 3.4148 GeV, M_chi_1 = 3.5107 GeV, M_psi' = 3.6861 GeV.
- The chi_0 has a radiative branching fraction to gamma J/psi of 35.6 +/- 1.9 percent, implying that J/psi production through the radiative process p pbar -> pi0 chi_0, chi_0 -> gamma J/psi is comparable to the direct process p pbar -> pi0 J/psi.

## Context

This work extends the earlier pole-model approach of Gaillard, Maiani and Petronzio (1982) and the constant amplitude approximation of Lundborg, Barnes and Wiedner (2005) by providing extensive analytic results for differential and total cross sections for five charmonium states. The predictions are directly relevant to the PANDA experiment at GSI, which will produce charmonium and charmonium hybrids in p-pbar annihilation with associated light mesons, and the strongly non-isotropic angular distributions predicted may inform PANDA detector design studies. The model also connects to ongoing experimental work at BES and CLEO-c on baryon-antibaryon final states and N* spectroscopy.
