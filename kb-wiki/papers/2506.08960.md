# Entanglement Suppression, Quantum Statistics and Symmetries in Spin-3/2 Baryon Scatterings

> We explore the interplay among entanglement suppression, quantum statistics and enhanced symmetries in the non-relativistic $S$-wave scattering involving the lowest-lying spin-3/2 baryons, which can be considered as four-dimensional qudits. These baryons form a ten-dimensional representation (decuplet) under the $\text{SU}(3)$ light-flavor symmetry and, in this limit, are considered indistinguishable under strong interactions. Treating the $S$-matrix in the spin-3/2 baryon-baryon scattering as a quantum logic gate in the spin space, we study the consequence of entanglement suppression and compute the entanglement power of the $S$-matrix. When the entanglement power vanishes, the $S$-matrix is either an Identity or a SWAP gate and spin-flavor symmetries and/or non-relativistic conformal invariance emerge, as previously observed in spin-1/2 baryons. In the case of scattering identical particles, the entanglement power never vanishes due to constraints from spin statistics, which we interpret as projection-valued measurements onto symmetric or antisymmetric Hilbert space and define the entanglement power accordingly. When the entanglement power is non-vanishing but sits at a global or local minimum, enhanced symmetries still emerge and the $S$-matrix can be interpreted as an Identity or a SWAP gate acting on the restricted Hilbert space allowed by quantum statistics. In general, when scattering identical spin-$s$ particles, we identify an enhanced $\text{SU}(2s+1)_{\text{spin}}$ symmetry for the Identity gate.

## Key claim

Entanglement suppression in the non-relativistic S-wave scattering of spin-3/2 decuplet baryons leads to the S-matrix being either an Identity or a SWAP gate in spin space, with enhanced spin-flavor symmetries or non-relativistic conformal invariance emerging, but only when the phase shifts for even total spin channels are equal to each other and those for odd total spin channels are equal to each other, with the two sets differing by either 0 or π/2; for identical particles, the entanglement power never vanishes due to spin-statistics constraints, but enhanced symmetries still emerge when the entanglement power sits at a global or local minimum.

## Method

The authors constructed the S-matrix for non-relativistic S-wave scattering of two spin-3/2 decuplet baryons using projection operators onto irreducible representations of the spin group SU(2) and the flavor group SU(3), with phase shifts δ_JF as parameters. They computed the entanglement power of the S-matrix by averaging the linear entropy of output states over all possible product input states using the Fubini-Study measure on the spin Hilbert space. For identical particles, they redefined the entanglement power by projecting onto the symmetric or antisymmetric Hilbert subspace allowed by Fermi-Dirac statistics, and identified enhanced symmetries through group-theoretic analysis and non-relativistic effective field theory.

* For distinguishable spin-3/2 particles, the entanglement power vanishes when δ_0 = δ_2 = δ_even and δ_1 = δ_3 = δ_odd, with |δ_even − δ_odd| = 0 or π/2 (Eq. 3/2delta).
* For distinguishable spin-1/2 particles, the entanglement power is E(Ŝ) = (1/6) sin²[2(δ_0 − δ_1)], vanishing when |δ_0 − δ_1| = 0 or π/2 (Eq. 1/2delta).
* The deuteron is a ³S₁ bound state with a binding energy of 2.2 MeV, while the ¹S₀ nn pair has a virtual state about 66 keV below threshold.
* Lattice calculation for the ΩΩ system in the ¹S₀ channel gives a large scattering length a = 4.6 fm (Gongyo 2017).

## Context

This work extends the entanglement-suppression hypothesis previously applied to spin-1/2 octet baryons (Beane 2018, Low 2021, Liu 2022) to the spin-3/2 decuplet baryons, which form a ten-dimensional representation under SU(3) flavor symmetry. The results show that the same pattern of emergent symmetries, spin-flavor symmetry for the Identity gate and non-relativistic conformal invariance for the SWAP gate, applies to higher-spin systems, with the additional complication that quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac for baryons) prevents the entanglement power from ever vanishing for identical particles. The analysis enables future comparisons with experimental data on ΔΔ dibaryons such as d*(2380) observed by WASA-at-COSY, and with lattice QCD studies of systems like ΩΩ.
