# Clusters, Halos, And S-Factors In Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> In Fermionic Molecular Dynamics antisymmetrized products of Gaussian wave packets are projected on angular momentum, linear momentum, and parity. An appropriately chosen set of these states span the many-body Hilbert space in which the Hamiltonian is diagonalized. The wave packet parameters - position, momentum, width and spin - are obtained by variation under constraints. The great flexibility of this basis allows to describe not only shell-model like states but also exotic states like halos, e.g. the two-proton halo in 17Ne, or cluster states as they appear for example in 12C close to the α-breakup threshold where the Hoyle state is located. Even a fully microscopic calculation of the 3He(α,γ)7Be capture reaction is possible and yields an astrophysical S-factor that compares very well with newer data. As representatives of numerous results these cases will be discussed in this contribution, some of them not published so far. The Hamiltonian is based on the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) can describe both shell-model-like states and exotic cluster or halo states (such as the two-proton halo in 17Ne and the Hoyle state in 12C) using a flexible basis of antisymmetrized Gaussian wave packets, and can also perform fully microscopic calculations of capture reactions like 3He(α,γ)7Be that yield astrophysical S-factors agreeing well with newer data, though some results (such as the 3H(α,γ)7Li S-factor) show a 15% discrepancy in absolute normalization.

## Method

The authors construct many-body basis states as Slater determinants of Gaussian wave packets with complex parameters for position, momentum, width, and spin, then project onto parity, angular momentum, and total linear momentum. The Hamiltonian, based on the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction with a phase-shift-equivalent low-momentum interaction derived via the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), is diagonalized in a set of non-orthogonal projected intrinsic basis states. For the capture reaction, the Hilbert space is divided into an external region (antisymmetrized products of 3He and 4He clusters at various distances) and an interaction region (FMD configurations varied after projection on spin-parity), with boundary conditions implemented via the microscopic R-matrix method matched to Whittaker and Coulomb functions at a channel radius of 12 fm.

## Key numbers

- 12C Hoyle state (0+2) excitation energy relative to 3α threshold: 0.38 MeV (experiment) vs. 0.43 MeV (FMD)
- 12C 2+2 state excitation energy relative to 3α threshold: 2.76(11) MeV (experiment) vs. 2.77 MeV (FMD)
- 12C ground state charge radius: 2.47(2) fm (experiment) vs. 2.53 fm (FMD)
- 12C Hoyle state point matter radius: 3.38 fm (FMD)
- 12C 2+2 state point matter radius: 4.43 fm (FMD)
- 12C B(E2, 2+2 → 0+1): 0.73(13) e2fm4 (experiment) vs. 0.46 e2fm4 (FMD)
- 12C M(E0, 0+1 → 0+2): 5.4(2) fm2 (experiment) vs. 6.53 fm2 (FMD)
- 17Ne ground state: s2 component of about 40% (FMD)
- 3H(α,γ)7Li S-factor: FMD absolute cross section is larger than data by Brune et al. by about 15%

## Context

This work extends the FMD approach previously applied to light nuclei (Neff 2012) to describe cluster states in 12C (including the Hoyle state), halo nuclei like 17Ne, and radiative capture reactions. The 12C results are compared with ab initio nuclear lattice calculations (Epelbaum 2011, 2012) and earlier three-α cluster models (Kamimura 1981, Funaki 2003). The capture reaction calculations provide a fully microscopic alternative to potential models (Kim 1981), microscopic cluster models (Langanke 1986, Kajino 1986), and ab initio approaches that rely on potential models for scattering phase shifts (Nollett 2001, Navratil 2007). The results enable future comparisons with more refined nuclear lattice calculations and provide microscopic S-factors for the 3He(α,γ)7Be reaction important for solar neutrino production.
