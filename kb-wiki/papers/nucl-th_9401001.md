# Relativistically generated asymmetry in the missing momentum distribution from the $(e,e'p)$ reaction

> We calculate the asymmetry in the missing-momentum distribution from the $(e,e'p)$ reaction in a relativistic formalism. Longitudinal and transverse response functions are evaluated in parallel kinematics as a function of the three-momentum transfer to the nucleus. Analytic expressions for the responses are obtained in a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation. These expressions reveal a large asymmetry in the momentum distribution, at the plane-wave level, induced by the lower components of the bound-state wave functions. These relativistic effects contaminate any attempt to infer color transparency from a measurement of the asymmetry in the $(e,e'p)$ reaction.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects, specifically the lower components of the bound-state Dirac spinors, generate a large asymmetry in the missing-momentum distribution from the (e,e'p) reaction even at the plane-wave impulse approximation level, contradicting the assumption that such an asymmetry is absent in conventional calculations at large momentum transfers and thereby contaminating any attempt to infer color transparency from asymmetry measurements.

## Method

The authors calculate longitudinal and transverse response functions for the (e,e'p) reaction in parallel kinematics using a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA). Analytic expressions for the hadronic tensor are derived via Feynman trace techniques, with the bound-state propagator expressed in closed form using Dirac gamma matrices. The bound-state wave functions for 16O are obtained from a relativistic mean-field approximation to the Walecka model, and the electromagnetic current includes single-nucleon form factors parameterized from on-shell eN scattering.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous examination of relativity's role on the spectral function (Piekarewicz 1992), where relativistic effects driven by particle-antiparticle mixing generated features associated nonrelativistically with physics beyond the mean-field level. The paper directly challenges the claim by Bianconi, Boffi, and Kharzeev (1993) that the missing-momentum asymmetry can serve as a clean signal of color transparency, as well as the Glauber theory baseline calculations suggesting asymmetry should be small at large momentum transfers. The results establish that relativistic corrections increase with momentum transfer and can become large, limiting the utility of the asymmetry as a color transparency signal and necessitating a robust relativistic baseline calculation against which novel effects may be inferred.
