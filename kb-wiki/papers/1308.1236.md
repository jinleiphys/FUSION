# Nucleon spin structure at very high-x

> Dyson-Schwinger equation treatments of the strong interaction show that the presence and importance of nonpointlike diquark correlations within the nucleon are a natural consequence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Using this foundation, we deduce a collection of simple formulae, expressed in terms of diquark appearance and mixing probabilities, from which one may compute ratios of longitudinal-spin-dependent u- and d-quark parton distribution functions on the domain x =1. A comparison with predictions from other approaches plus a consideration of extant and planned experiments shows that the measurement of nucleon longitudinal spin asymmetries on x =1 can add considerably to our capacity for discriminating between contemporary pictures of nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ratios of longitudinal-spin-dependent u- and d-quark parton distribution functions at x ≈ 1, computed from simple formulae based on diquark appearance and mixing probabilities derived from Dyson-Schwinger equation treatments, can discriminate between contemporary pictures of nucleon structure; the hedge is that this discrimination requires future measurements of nucleon longitudinal spin asymmetries at x ≈ 1, as existing data do not yet distinguish between models.

## Method

The authors use a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation framework, which reduces the three-body problem to a quark-diquark system by approximating the quark+quark scattering matrix with nonpointlike scalar (0⁺) and axial-vector (1⁺) diquark correlations. They derive formulae for the x=1 values of spin-dependent PDF ratios in terms of diquark appearance probabilities (P₁^{p,s}, P₁^{p,a}, P₁^{p,m}) and angular momentum components (ψ_{L=0}, ψ_{L=1}) from the Faddeev amplitude, then compute these probabilities using two distinct DSE kernels: one with a momentum-dependent dressed-quark mass function (DSE-1, "realistic") and one with a contact interaction yielding a momentum-independent mass (DSE-2, "contact").

## Key numbers

- DSE-1 (realistic): d_v/u_v at x=1 = 0.28; Δd/Δu at x=1 = -0.11; Δu/u at x=1 = 0.65; Δd/d at x=1 = -0.26; A₁ⁿ at x=1 = 0.17; A₁ᵖ at x=1 = 0.59
- DSE-2 (contact): d_v/u_v at x=1 = 0.18; Δd/Δu at x=1 = -0.07; Δu/u at x=1 = 0.88; Δd/d at x=1 = -0.33; A₁ⁿ at x=1 = 0.34; A₁ᵖ at x=1 = 0.88
- Scalar-diquark-only model: d_v/u_v at x=1 = 0; Δd/Δu at x=1 = 0; A₁ⁿ at x=1 = 1; A₁ᵖ at x=1 = 1
- SU(6) symmetry: d_v/u_v at x=1 = 1/2; Δd/Δu at x=1 = -1/4; A₁ⁿ at x=1 = 0; A₁ᵖ at x=1 = 5/9
- pQCD model: d_v/u_v at x=1 = 1/5; Δd/Δu at x=1 = 1/5; A₁ⁿ at x=1 = 1; A₁ᵖ at x=1 = 1
- DSE-1 Faddeev amplitude components: ψ_{L=0}=0.55, ψ_{L=1}=0.22; DSE-2: ψ_{L=0}=0.88, ψ_{L=1}=0
- DSE-1 probabilities: P₁^{p,a}=0.25, P₁^{p,m}=0.14; DSE-2: P₁^{p,a}=0.22, P₁^{p,m}=0

## Context

This work extends the DSE/Faddeev equation approach to nucleon structure previously applied to unpolarized PDF ratios (Holt 2010, Wilson 2011) by deriving analogous formulae for spin-dependent distributions at x=1. It contrasts with scalar-diquark-only models (Close 1988), SU(6) symmetry predictions, constituent quark models, and pQCD-based predictions (Farrar 1975, Brodsky 1994), showing that the DSE results occupy a distinct region of parameter space. The paper enables future experiments at JLab (planned up to x≈0.8 for A₁ᵖ and x≈0.75 for A₁ⁿ), BoNuS, and potential EIC facilities to discriminate between these competing pictures of nucleon structure.
