# Are eta- and omega-nuclear states bound ?

> We investigate theoretically whether it is feasible to detect $η$- and $ω$-nucleus bound states. As well as the closed shell nuclei, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb, we also investigate $^6$He, $^{11}$B and $^{26}$Mg, which are the final nuclei in the proposed experiment involving the (d,$^3$He) reaction at GSI. Potentials for the $η$ and $ω$ mesons in these nuclei are calculated in local density approximation, embedding the mesons in the nucleus described by solving the mean-field equations of motion in the QMC model. Our results suggest that one should expect to find $η$- and $ω$-nucleus bound states in all these nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors predict that both η- and ω-nucleus bound states should exist across a wide range of nuclei, but the actual binding energies depend on the choice of QMC model version (QMC-I vs QMC-II) and on the assumed in-medium meson widths, which are treated as phenomenological parameters rather than calculated self-consistently.

## Method

The authors embed MIT bag-model η and ω mesons in finite nuclei described by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, solving the mean-field equations of motion self-consistently in local density approximation. The effective meson masses are calculated as functions of position in the nucleus, and the resulting potentials are used in Klein-Gordon equations to obtain single-particle energies. The in-medium meson widths are included phenomenologically using a parameter γ that scales with the mass shift, with values chosen to cover the estimated ranges from prior work.

## Key numbers

- η meson 1s binding energy in ¹⁶O: -33.1 MeV (γ_η=0, no width)
- η meson 1s binding energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: -56.6 MeV (γ_η=0)
- ω meson 1s binding energy in ¹⁶O: -93.5 MeV (γ_ω=0)
- ω meson 1s binding energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: -118 MeV (γ_ω=0)
- ω meson 1s binding energy in ⁶He: -55.7 MeV (γ_ω=0)
- η meson 1s binding energy in ⁶He: -11.4 MeV (γ_η=0)
- Potential depth at center of nuclei: approximately 60 MeV for η and 130 MeV for ω
- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³): η effective mass = 483.9 MeV, ω effective mass = 658.7 MeV
- Free space η width: 1.18 keV; free space ω width: 8.43 MeV

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Hayano et al. (1997) to use the (d,³He) reaction at GSI to produce η and ω mesons with nearly zero recoil, and improves upon their first-order optical potential approach by using the self-consistent QMC model. The results are compared with two different parameter sets from Hayano et al. (1997) and with an alternative QMC-II version. The study enables experimental searches for meson-nucleus bound states at GSI and TJNAF, though the authors note that future work should calculate the in-medium widths self-consistently within QMC and include σ-ω mixing effects.
