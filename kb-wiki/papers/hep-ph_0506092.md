# Pion Velocity near the Chiral Phase Transition Point in the Vector Manifestation

> We study the pion velocity near the critical temperature $T_c$ of chiral symmetry restoration in QCD. Using chiral perturbation theory based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) as an effective field theory, where the chiral symmetry restoration is realized as the vector manifestation (VM), we show that the pion velocity for $T \to T_c$ is close to the speed of light, $v_π(T) = 0.83 - 0.99$ which is at variance with the sigma model result which predicts $v_π(T_c) = 0$. Our prediction on $v_π(T_c)$ is compared with the value extracted from the recent RHIC data.

## Key claim

Using hidden local symmetry (HLS) with vector manifestation (VM), the pion velocity near the critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration is predicted to be close to the speed of light, \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\), in stark contrast to sigma model predictions of \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0\). The result depends on the specific choice of matching scale, QCD scale, and critical temperature, and applies in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory based on hidden local symmetry (HLS), which includes both pions and vector mesons as dynamical degrees of freedom, with chiral symmetry restoration realized as the vector manifestation (VM). They performed Wilsonian matching between the HLS effective theory and QCD operator product expansion (OPE) at a matching scale \(\Lambda\) to determine the bare pion velocity, then exploited a non-renormalization property (protection by the VM) to equate the bare velocity with the physical pion velocity at \(T_c\). Thermal matrix elements were evaluated using a dilute gas approximation extended to include both pions and longitudinal vector mesons near \(T_c\).

## Key numbers

- Bare pion velocity at the critical temperature: \(V_{\pi,\text{bare}}(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\), corresponding to a deviation \(\delta_{\text{bare}}(T_c) = 1 - V_{\pi,\text{bare}}^2 = 0.0061 - 0.29\).
- The matching scale range used: \(\Lambda = 0.8 - 1.1\ \text{GeV}\).
- QCD scale range used: \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.30 - 0.45\ \text{GeV}\).
- Critical temperature range used: \(T_c = 0.15 - 0.20\ \text{GeV}\).
- Physical pion velocity at chiral restoration: \(v_\pi(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99\).
- For comparison, the value extracted from RHIC/STAR data by Cramer et al. is \(v_\pi(T) = 0.65\) (no error bars assigned).

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2001, 2003) and the Brown-Rho scaling (1991) to predict the pion velocity near the chiral phase transition, contradicting the sigma model result of Son and Stephanov (2002) which gives \(v_\pi(T_c)=0\). The prediction is compared to the value \(v_\pi = 0.65\) extracted by Cramer et al. (2005) from STAR/RHIC data. The result enables future calculations incorporating explicit chiral symmetry breaking from quark masses, which would likely lower the predicted velocity toward the observed value.
