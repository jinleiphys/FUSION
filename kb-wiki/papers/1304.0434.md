# Role of three-nucleon forces and many-body processes in nuclear pairing

> We present microscopic valence-shell calculations of pairing gaps in the calcium isotopes, focusing on the role of three-nucleon (3N) forces and many-body processes. In most cases, we find a reduction in pairing strength when the leading chiral 3N forces are included, compared to results with low-momentum two-nucleon (NN) interactions only. This is in agreement with a recent energy density functional study. At the NN level, calculations that include particle-particle and hole-hole ladder contributions lead to smaller pairing gaps compared with experiment. When particle-hole contributions as well as the normal-ordered one- and two-body parts of 3N forces are consistently included to third order, we find reasonable agreement with experimental three-point mass differences. This highlights the important role of 3N forces and many-body processes for pairing in nuclei. Finally, we relate pairing gaps to the evolution of nuclear structure in neutron-rich calcium isotopes and study the predictions for the 2+ excitation energies, in particular for 54Ca.

## Key claim

When three-nucleon (3N) forces and many-body processes (particle-hole contributions) are consistently included to third order in many-body perturbation theory, the calculated three-point mass differences for calcium isotopes show reasonable agreement with experiment, whereas calculations restricted to particle-particle and hole-hole ladder contributions (which approximately correspond to energy density functional methods) systematically underpredict pairing gaps and exhibit an inverted odd-even staggering.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic valence-shell calculations for calcium isotopes using chiral effective field theory two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, with the NN potential evolved via renormalization group to a low-momentum cutoff of 2.0 fm^-1. Valence-space Hamiltonians were constructed to third order in many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) in a harmonic-oscillator basis of 13 major shells, including normal-ordered one- and two-body parts of 3N forces. Exact diagonalizations were then performed in both the pf-shell and an extended pf+g9/2 valence space using the shell-model codes ANTOINE and NATHAN.

## Key numbers

- The reduction in pairing gaps from 3N forces at the ladder level ranges from 0.2 to 0.5 MeV, similar to the ~30% reduction seen in energy density functional calculations.
- From first to second order in ladder contributions, the three-point mass differences increase by approximately 0.4 MeV; from second to third order, the change is only about 0.1 MeV.
- The 2+ excitation energy in 54Ca is reduced to 1.7 - 2.2 MeV when 3N forces are included consistently to third order.
- For 52Ca, the TITAN precision mass measurement deviates from the earlier AME2003 value by 1.74 MeV.
- The calculated single-particle energies in 41Ca range from -8.05 MeV (f7/2) to -1.23 MeV (g9/2), with 3N contributions of 1-6 MeV depending on the orbital.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic valence-shell calculations (Holt 2012, 2013) that demonstrated the key role of 3N forces for neutron-rich nuclei, and directly addresses the open question raised by the energy density functional study of Lesinski (2012), which found that 3N forces reduce pairing gaps by ~30% but could not account for the missing pairing strength. By including particle-hole many-body processes (core-polarization effects) to third order, the present calculations show that these contributions can provide the missing pairing strength, improving upon the ladder-only results that correspond approximately to the EDF level. The improved treatment of 3N forces also refines predictions for shell closures, particularly reducing the 2+ energy in 54Ca compared to the first-order 3N calculations of Holt (2012), and enables future extensions to heavier isotopic chains such as nickel and tin.
