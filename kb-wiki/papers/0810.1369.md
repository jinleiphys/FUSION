# Roles of Direct Break-up Reaction on Neutrino Scattering off $^{12}$C near Nucleon Threshold Region

> Neutrino (antineutrino) scattering off $^{12}$C is one of various important key reactions for $ν$-process in the nucleosysnthesis of light nuclei. Most of neutrino-nucleus scattering are considered through indirect processes within the energy range from a few to tens of MeV. Target nuclei are excited by incident neutrino (antineutrino) through various transitions, and subsequently decay into other nuclei with emitting particles. But, direct processes are also feasible, in which incident neutrino (antineutrino) strips directly one nucleon from target nuclei. Consequently, direct processes may affect abundances of $^{11}$C and $^{11}$B additionally to indirect processes. We investigate direct neutrino (antineutrino) quasi-elastic scattering off $^{12}$C around the energy region liberating one nucleon and discuss implications of direct processes in the nucleosynthesis. The direct processes might be comparable to the indirect processes if the final state interaction is taken into account.

## Key claim

Direct one-nucleon knockout processes in neutrino and antineutrino scattering off 12C may contribute to the abundances of 11C and 11B in the nu-process of nucleosynthesis, and these direct processes could be comparable to the traditionally considered indirect processes if final-state interactions (FSI) of the outgoing nucleon are taken into account in the indirect calculations.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) formalism, previously applied to quasi-elastic electron scattering, to calculate total cross sections for direct charge-current (CC) reactions 12C(nu_e, e-p) and 12C(anti-nu_e, e+n) and neutral-current (NC) reactions 12C(nu, nu'N) and 12C(anti-nu, anti-nu'N). Bound and continuum nucleon wave functions were obtained from the Dirac equation using the sigma-omega model for bound states and a relativistic phenomenological optical potential for continuum states, with the optical potential also used to incorporate FSI.

## Key numbers

- Direct process cross sections are smaller than indirect process cross sections by a factor of 2 to 3 for 12C(nu, nu') and 12C(anti-nu_e, e+), and by a factor of 3 to 4 for 12C(nu_e, e-).
- Including the optical potential for FSI reduces the direct process cross sections by a factor of 2 compared to calculations without FSI.
- The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.16639 x 10^-11 MeV^-2.
- The Cabibbo angle squared is cos^2(theta_C) = 0.9749.
- Experimental inclusive cross sections for 12C(nu_e, e-)12N* are in the range 4.3 to 5.7 x 10^-42 cm^2, and exclusive cross sections for 12C(nu_e, e-)12N_g.s. are in the range 8.9 to 10.5 x 10^-42 cm^2.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of neutrino-12C scattering, which had focused exclusively on indirect processes through excited compound nuclei, by calculating direct knockout contributions using DWBA methods previously applied to quasi-elastic electron scattering (Kim et al., 2006) and neutrino scattering (Kim et al., 2007). The results are compared to shell model calculations of indirect processes by Suzuki et al. (2006) and Yoshida et al. (2008), and to RPA and QRPA calculations by Kolbe et al. (1996), Volpe et al. (2000), and Kosmas et al. (2007), which overestimated exclusive reaction data by a factor of 4 to 5. The work enables more complete network calculations for nu-process nucleosynthesis and motivates future experimental measurements at proposed beta-beam neutrino facilities.
