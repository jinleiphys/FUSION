# New Critical Point Induced by the Axial Anomaly in Dense QCD

> We study the interplay between chiral and diquark condensates within the framework of the Ginzburg-Landau free energy, and classify possible phase structures of two and three-flavor massless QCD. The QCD axial anomaly acts as an external field applied to the chiral condensate in a color superconductor and leads to a crossover between the broken chiral symmetry and the color superconducting phase, and, in particular, to a new critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The QCD axial anomaly, via a cubic coupling term between the chiral and diquark condensates in the Ginzburg-Landau free energy, acts as an external field on the chiral condensate in a color superconductor, turning the first-order chiral transition into a crossover and producing a new critical point in the QCD phase diagram, but only under the assumption that the phase transitions are not strongly first order and that the condensed phases are spatially homogeneous.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy for massless three-flavor and two-flavor QCD, including chiral condensate fields Φ and diquark condensate fields d up to fourth order, constrained by chiral, color, and baryon number symmetries. They classified possible phases (normal, Nambu-Goldstone, color-superconducting, and coexistence) by comparing free energies and locating phase boundaries in the (a, α) plane, where a and α are the coefficients that change sign at the chiral and diquark transitions respectively. For three flavors, they reduced the system to an effective Ising-like free energy in an external field to identify the critical point.

## Key numbers

* For three flavors with γ > 0 and λ = 0, the critical point A is located at a = c²/(3b) + 2γ²/β and α = -β c³/(27 γ b²).
* For three flavors, the critical end point B is at a = 2c²/(9b) + 2γ²/β and α = 0.
* For three flavors, the triple point C is at a = 2c²/(9b) and α = √(β/b) [c/(3√b) + γ/√β]².
* For three flavors, the tri-critical point D appears when γ > (√(β/b)) c/3.
* For two flavors with b < 0 and λ > 0, the critical end point E is at a = 3b²/(16f) and α = 0.
* For two flavors with b < 0 and λ > 0, the critical end point F is at a = 3b'(b' + 16λ²/β)/(16f) and α = 3λ b'/(2f), where b' ≡ b - 4λ²/β.

## Context

This work extends earlier Ginzburg-Landau treatments of the chiral-diquark interplay (Schäfer and Wilczek, 1999) and random matrix model studies (Rischke, 2000) by providing a model-independent classification of phase structures for both two and three flavors. The results enable future phenomenological model calculations and lattice QCD simulations to search for the predicted new critical point, and raise open questions about whether it survives in inhomogeneous Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov states and how the coexistence phase at low temperature and chemical potential is affected by quark confinement.
