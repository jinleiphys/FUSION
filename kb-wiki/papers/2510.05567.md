# Factorial cumulants of proton multiplicity near a critical point using maximum entropy freeze-out prescription

> We present the first application of the maximum-entropy freeze-out prescription to calculate factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities near the conjectured QCD critical point in thermal equilibrium. We map the Gibbs free energy of the 3D Ising model to a parameterized class of possible EoS near QCD critical point. This equilibrium baseline highlights how factorial cumulants isolate critical fluctuations by subtracting trivial self-correlations, setting the stage for future out-of-equilibrium analyses. We identify the key non-universal aspects of the mapping to the Ising model that strongly control the characteristic properties, such as magnitude and location of the peaks of the factorial cumulants along the freeze-out curve.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first application of the maximum-entropy freeze-out prescription to calculate factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities near the conjectured QCD critical point, but only under the simplifying assumption of thermal equilibrium; the authors explicitly note that finite-time out-of-equilibrium effects are expected to become important near the critical point, making this an equilibrium baseline rather than a complete description of heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors map the Gibbs free energy of the 3D Ising model to a parameterized class of possible QCD equations of state near the critical point using six non-universal mapping parameters (critical temperature T_c, critical chemical potential mu_c, angles alpha_1 and alpha_2, and scale factors rho and w). They then apply the maximum-entropy freeze-out prescription, which maximizes the relative entropy of the hadron resonance gas while matching local hydrodynamic densities at freeze-out on an event-by-event basis, to compute factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities along freeze-out curves displaced below the crossover curve by Delta T_f = 4, 6, and 9 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential is fixed at mu_c = 600 MeV and alpha_2 = 0 degrees; the critical temperature T_c = 90 MeV and alpha_1 follow from the pseudo-critical boundary from lattice calculations (Kahangirwe 2023).
- The magnitude of the maximum of the k-th factorial cumulant of proton multiplicity scales as Delta T_f^(1+1/delta - k), where delta is approximately 4.8 (the 3D Ising critical exponent).
- The peak height scales as w^(-1-1/delta), approximately w^(-6/5) given delta approximately 4.8; the peak location is controlled by the combination bar_rho = rho w^(1-1/(beta*delta)) = rho w^(2/5), where beta is approximately 0.326.
- The second and third factorial cumulants are shown for (w, rho) values of (1,1), (5,1), and (5,0.56) along freeze-out curves with Delta T_f = 4, 6, and 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the maximum-entropy freeze-out prescription developed by Pradeep (2022) and applies it to the family of candidate QCD equations of state constructed via mapping to the 3D Ising model by Parotto (2018). The calculations provide an equilibrium baseline for interpreting STAR collaboration measurements of proton factorial cumulants (STAR 2025), which show statistically significant deviations from non-critical baselines computed by Vovchenko (2025). The results enable future Bayesian comparisons between experimental data and theoretical calculations to constrain the non-universal mapping parameters and the location of the QCD critical point, with out-of-equilibrium effects as the necessary next step.
