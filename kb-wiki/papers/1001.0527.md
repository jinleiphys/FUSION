# Squeezed correlations of strange particle-antiparticles

> Squeezed correlations of hadron-antihadron pairs are predicted to appear if their masses are modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions. If discovered experimentally, they would be an unequivocal evidence of in-medium mass shift found by means of hadronic probes. We discuss a method proposed to search for this novel type of correlation, illustrating it by means of D_s-mesons with in-medium shifted masses. These particles are expected to be more easily detected and identified in future upgrades at RHIC.

## Key claim

Squeezed correlations of hadron-antihadron pairs, if discovered experimentally, would provide unequivocal evidence of in-medium mass shifts probed by hadronic probes; however, the signal strength depends critically on finite emission time, which can reduce the correlation by about three orders of magnitude compared to instantaneous emission.

## Method

The authors apply a non-relativistic analytical model of squeezed correlations derived from a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation, assuming a Gaussian-shaped finite system with radial flow and a Lorentzian emission time distribution. They compute the squeezed correlation function \(C_s(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k}, m_*)\) for back-to-back \(D_s^+ D_s^-\) pairs as a function of momentum magnitude \(|\mathbf{k}|\) and in-medium mass \(m_*\), and also study the correlation in terms of the sum and difference momenta \(\mathbf{K}_{12}\) and \(\mathbf{q}_{12}\) for two system radii (4 fm and 7 fm) and two flow velocities (\(\langle u \rangle = 0\) and 0.23).

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic mass of the \(D_s\) meson is \(m_{D_s} \sim 1969\) MeV; at \(m_* = m_{D_s}\) the squeezed correlation \(C_s(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k}, m_*) \equiv 1\) (no squeezing).
- The maxima of \(C_s(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k}, m_*)\) for \(D_s^+ D_s^-\) pairs are shifted about \(2-2.5\%\) from the asymptotic mass (above or below).
- A Lorentzian emission distribution with \(\Delta t = 2\) fm/c reduces the squeezed correlation signal by about three orders of magnitude compared to instantaneous emission (\(\Delta t = 0\)).
- For \(R=4\) fm and \(m_* = 1930\) MeV, radial flow (\(\langle u \rangle = 0.23\)) enhances \(C_s(2\mathbf{K}_{12},\mathbf{q}_{12})\) by about \(40\%\) at low \(|\mathbf{q}_{12}|\) and roughly \(15\%\) at high \(|\mathbf{q}_{12}|\), compared to the static case (\(\langle u \rangle = 0\)).
- Two freeze-out temperatures are considered: \(T = 220\) MeV and \(T = 140\) MeV; the correlation intensity increases for lower temperature.

## Context

This work extends the squeezed correlation formalism originally developed for bosons by Asakawa, Csörgő, and Gyulassy (1999) and for fermions by Panda et al. (2001), and later applied to \(\phi\phi\) and \(K^+K^-\) pairs by the same authors. The present study applies the model to \(D_s^+ D_s^-\) pairs, motivated by the possibility of detecting them in future RHIC upgrades, and shows that the signal strength grows with the asymptotic mass of the particles, making heavier mesons more promising for experimental searches. The results enable experimental strategies to search for back-to-back correlations by measuring the correlation function in the \((2\mathbf{K}_{12}, \mathbf{q}_{12})\) plane rather than requiring exactly opposite momenta.
