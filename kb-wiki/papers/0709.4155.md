# Photonuclear reactions of actinide and pre-actinide nuclei at intermediate energies

> Photonuclear reaction is described with an approach based on the quasideuteron nuclear photoabsorption model followed by the process of competition between light particle evaporation and fission for the excited nucleus. Thus fission process is considered as a decay mode. The evaporation-fission process of the compound nucleus is simulated in a Monte-Carlo framework. Photofission reaction cross sections are analysed in a systematic manner in the energy range $\sim$~50-70 MeV for the actinides $^{232}$Th, $^{233}$U, $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U and $^{237}$Np and the pre-actinide nuclei $^{208}$Pb and $^{209}$Bi. The study reproduces satisfactorily well the available experimental data of photofission cross sections at energies $\sim$~50-70 MeV and the increasing trend of nuclear fissility with the fissility parameter $Z^2/A$ for the actinides and pre-actinides at intermediate energies [$\sim$~20-140 MeV].

## Key claim

The paper claims that a quasideuteron photoabsorption model combined with a Monte-Carlo simulation of evaporation-fission competition reproduces photofission cross sections for actinides and pre-actinides at intermediate energies (50-70 MeV), but the agreement is qualified as "excellent" for actinides except for thorium where the calculation "somewhat overestimates" the data, and only "reasonable" for pre-actinide and medium-mass nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the quasideuteron model to calculate total photoabsorption cross sections, with Levinger's constant derived from modern root-mean-square radius data and a Pauli-blocking factor. They then simulated the decay of the excited compound nucleus using a Monte-Carlo framework that treats fission as a decay mode competing with light particle evaporation, employing Weisskopf evaporation widths and Bohr-Wheeler fission widths with Sierk's diffuse-surface fission barriers. Each calculation used 40000 incident photon events to obtain computational statistics.

## Context

This work extends the quasideuteron photoabsorption model of Levinger (1951) and the statistical evaporation-fission framework of Weisskopf (1937) and Bohr and Wheeler (1939) to photofission at intermediate energies (20-140 MeV). It uses Sierk's (1979) diffuse-surface fission barriers and compares with experimental data from Ivanov (1989), Lepretre (1987), and others. The calculations confirm the finding of Berman (1993) and Cetina (2000) that nuclear fissility does not saturate at 100% for thorium and some uranium isotopes even at high photon energies. The work enables predictions for photofission as a method to produce neutron-rich radioactive ion beams, particularly near 78Ni.
