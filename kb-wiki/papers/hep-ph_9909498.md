# Derivation of O(q^4) Effective Pion-Nucleon Lagrangian Within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We construct a complete list of O(q^4) terms directly within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) in the absence of external fields and assuming isospin symmetry. In addition to a phase rule recently developed (NPA646, 343, '99), a variety of algebraic identities and reparameterization invariance, are used to ensure linear independence of the terms and their low energy coupling constants. We first construct O(q^4) terms terms for off-shell nucleons, and then perform the on-shell reduction, again within HBChPT. We discuss an application of the O(q^4) terms to the evaluation of the O(q^4) operator insertions in the "contact graph" of pion double charge exchange.

## Key claim

This paper constructs a complete list of O(q⁴) terms in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) directly within the nonrelativistic framework, rather than by reducing the relativistic baryon chiral Lagrangian. The construction assumes no external fields and exact isospin symmetry, and the author notes that the techniques can be extended to include external fields and isospin violation.

## Method

The author generates all possible O(q⁴) terms from products of building blocks (covariant derivatives D_μ, pion-field derivatives u_μ, chiral symmetry breaking fields χ_±, the nucleon velocity v_μ, and the Pauli-Lubanski spin operator S_ν) using a phase rule for charge conjugation invariance developed in earlier work (Misra, 1999). Algebraic identities including Jacobi-like identities, the curvature relation [D_μ,D_ν] = (1/4)[u_μ,u_ν], and reparameterization invariance are applied to reduce the number of linearly independent terms. The construction is first performed for off-shell nucleons, then the on-shell reduction is carried out within HBChPT.

## Key numbers

- The paper constructs O(q⁴) terms, where q denotes a small momentum or pion mass scale in chiral perturbation theory.
- The curvature relation [D_μ,D_ν] = (1/4)[u_μ,u_ν] is used extensively for reduction of (4,0,0,0), (0,4,0,0), and (2,2,0,0) type terms.
- For (0,4,0,0)-type terms with u_μ = u_μ^a τ^a, the isospin algebra yields specific non-zero combinations: [τ^a,[τ^b,[τ^c,τ^d]]]_+ and [[τ^a,τ^b],[τ^c,τ^d]]_+ are non-zero, while [τ^a,[τ^b,[τ^c,τ^d]_+]] = 0 and [[τ^a,τ^b],[τ^c,τ^d]_+] = 0.

## Context

This work extends the direct HBChPT construction method previously developed up to O(q³) by Misra (1999, Nuclear Physics A646, 343) to the next order O(q⁴). It provides an alternative to the standard approach of Bernard et al. (1995) and Ecker and Mojžiš (1995) which starts from the relativistic baryon chiral Lagrangian and performs a nonrelativistic reduction. The complete O(q⁴) Lagrangian enables one-loop calculations in the single-nucleon sector, such as pion production off a single nucleon where convergence problems require going to O(q⁴) as noted by Bernard et al. (1995) and others. The author also discusses an application to pion double charge exchange in the multi-nucleon sector.
