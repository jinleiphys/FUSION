# Fusion barrier distributions in systems with finite excitation energy

> Eigen-channel approach to heavy-ion fusion reactions is exact only when the excitation energy of the intrinsic motion is zero. In order to take into account effects of finite excitation energy, we introduce an energy dependence to weight factors in the eigen-channel approximation. Using two channel problem, we show that the weight factors are slowly changing functions of incident energy. This suggests that the concept of the fusion barrier distribution still holds to a good approximation even when the excitation energy of the intrinsic motion is finite. A transition to the adiabatic tunneling, where the coupling leads to a static potential renormalization, is also discussed.

# Fusion barrier distributions in systems with finite excitation energy

## Key claim

The eigen-channel approximation for heavy-ion fusion reactions, which is exact only when the intrinsic excitation energy is zero, remains applicable to a good approximation even for finite excitation energies because the weight factors in the eigen-channel representation are slowly varying functions of incident energy. The paper demonstrates this explicitly for a two-channel problem and also discusses the transition to the adiabatic tunneling limit where coupling produces a static potential renormalization.

## Method

The authors perform exact coupled-channels calculations for a schematic two-level model of heavy-ion fusion, using a Gaussian bare potential and coupling form factor that mimic the fusion reaction between two $^{58}$Ni nuclei. They parametrize the penetrability as a weighted sum of eigen-channel penetrabilities, then extract the optimum weight factors at each incident energy by solving the exact coupled-channels equations and applying the unitarity condition. The energy dependence of these weight factors is examined across a range of excitation energies from zero to 8 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The coupling strength $F_0$ is set to 3 MeV, the bare potential height $U_0$ to 100 MeV, and both Gaussian widths $s = s_f = 3$ fm, mimicking the $^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni system.
- The reduced mass is taken as 29 $m_N$, where $m_N$ is the nucleon mass, and the excitation energy $\epsilon$ is set to 2 MeV for the main calculation.
- The optimum weight factors change by barely 2.6% from 10 MeV below the barrier to 10 MeV above the barrier for the $\epsilon = 2$ MeV case.
- For the adiabatic limit study, the excitation energy is increased to 8 MeV, which significantly exceeds the bare barrier curvature of about 4 MeV.
- The first derivative of the penetrability is computed using a point difference formula with $\Delta E = 2$ MeV, as is standard in heavy-ion fusion analyses.

## Context

This work extends the eigen-channel approach of Dasso, Landowne, and Winther (1983) and the barrier distribution extraction method of Rowley, Satchler, and Stelson (1991), which had been successfully applied to systems with finite excitation energies such as $^{16}$O + $^{144}$Sm (octupole phonon at 1.8 MeV) and $^{58}$Ni + $^{60}$Ni (quadrupole phonons at 1.45 and 1.33 MeV) despite lacking a rigorous theoretical foundation. The paper resolves the question of whether the second derivative of $E\sigma$ genuinely represents a barrier distribution when excitation energies are nonzero, and it enables the continued use of barrier distribution analysis for a wide range of heavy-ion fusion systems. The transition from sudden to adiabatic tunneling limits is discussed in relation to the work of Takigawa, Hagino, and Balantekin (1995, 1994) and Hagino et al. (1997).
