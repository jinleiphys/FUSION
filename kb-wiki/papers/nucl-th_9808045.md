# Semiclassical description of multiple giant dipole resonance excitation and decay

> A semiclassical description of multiple giant resonance excitation and decay that incorporates incoherent fluctuation contributions of the Brink-Axel type is developed. Numerical calculations show that the incoherent contributions are important at low to intermediate bombarding energies.

## Key claim

The paper develops a semiclassical description of multiple giant dipole resonance excitation and decay that incorporates incoherent fluctuation contributions of the Brink-Axel type, and numerical calculations show these incoherent contributions are important at low to intermediate bombarding energies. The key hedge is that the model is "schematic" and assumes harmonic phonons with no spin degeneracies or magnetic multiplicities, and neglects escape widths as extremely small compared to spreading widths.

## Method

The authors extend a semiclassical density-matrix formalism from Ko (1978) and Canto et al. (1994) to include both coherent collective excitation and incoherent statistical decay of giant dipole resonances. States are labeled by a collective index n (number of dipole phonons) and a statistical index s (number of phonons that have decayed into the incoherent background), with the time evolution governed by a master equation containing coherent coupling terms, loss terms from statistical decay, and gain terms from decay of higher states. The model assumes harmonic n-phonon giant dipole states with straight-line projectile-target relative motion, and the decay widths are taken as Γ_ns = nΓ_d where Γ_d is the spreading width of the giant dipole resonance.

## Key numbers

- For the system 208Pb + 208Pb, the giant dipole resonance centroid is ε_d = 13.8 MeV and spreading width Γ_d = 4.1 MeV, obtained from the global systematics ε_d = 43.4 A^(-0.215) MeV and Γ_d = 0.3 ε_d
- Calculations are performed for projectile energies from 100 to 1000 MeV/nucleon
- The strong-interaction radius is R = 1.23(A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) fm with diffusivity a = 0.75 fm
- The differential excitation cross section is shown for 208Pb + 208Pb at 640 MeV/nucleon
- Coherent and total n-phonon cross sections decrease by about an order of magnitude for each additional phonon of excitation

## Context

This work extends the collective-statistical theory of multiple giant resonance excitation and decay developed by Carlson, Canto, Cruz-Barrios, Hussein, and de Toledo Piza (1997, 1998) and the semiclassical description of collective multiple giant resonance excitation from Canto et al. (1994). The model provides a theoretical basis for understanding the energy-dependent enhancement of multiple excitation cross sections and energy-dependent effective widths observed experimentally in heavy-ion Coulomb excitation of the Double Giant Dipole Resonance reported by Emling (1994) and Boretsky et al. (1996). The results explain the apparent discrepancy in experimental double giant dipole resonance cross sections by arguing that what is observed is the total 2-phonon cross section rather than just the coherent cross section.
