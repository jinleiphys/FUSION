# Breaking and restoration of rotational symmetry for irreducible tensor operators on the lattice

> We study the breaking of rotational symmetry on the lattice for irreducible tensor operators and practical methods for suppressing this breaking. We illustrate the features of the general problem using an $α$ cluster model for $^{8}$Be. We focus on the lowest states with non-zero angular momentum and examine the matrix elements of multipole moment operators. We show that the physical reduced matrix element is well reproduced by averaging over all possible orientations of the quantum state, and this is expressed as a sum of matrix elements weighted by the corresponding Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. For our $α$ cluster model we find that the effects of rotational symmetry breaking can be largely eliminated for lattice spacings of $a\leq 1.7$ fm, and we expect similar improvement for actual lattice Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the breaking of rotational symmetry on the lattice for irreducible tensor operators can be largely suppressed by averaging matrix elements over all possible orientations of the quantum state, expressed as a sum weighted by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. This method is shown to work well for lattice spacings up to 1.7 fm in an alpha cluster model, but the authors note this is a model-dependent result and expect similar improvement for actual lattice Monte Carlo calculations.

## Method
The authors used an alpha cluster model for Be-8 with an Ali-Bodmer type potential, where the alpha-alpha interaction includes nuclear and Coulomb parts. They constructed lattice wave functions by simultaneously diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and the rotation operator R_z(pi/2), then applied unitary transformations to obtain angular momentum eigenstates. The matrix elements of multipole moment operators (r^l' Y_lm) were computed on the lattice and compared to continuum values, with isotropic averaging performed by integrating over all SO(3) rotation angles.

## Key numbers
- The Be-8 ground state energy is E(0+) = -10.8 MeV and the first excited state is E(2+) = -3.3 MeV, measured relative to the alpha-alpha threshold
- The alpha particle mass is m_alpha = 3727.0 MeV
- The nuclear potential parameters are V_0 = -216.0 MeV, V_1 = 354.0 MeV, eta_0 = 0.436 fm^-1, eta_1 = 0.529 fm^-1
- The alpha particle radius is R_alpha = 1.44 fm
- Rotational symmetry breaking effects can be largely eliminated for lattice spacings a <= 1.7 fm
- The box size used was L >= 16 fm to remove finite volume effects
- The kinetic energy was computed using an N=4 finite difference formula

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Lu et al. (2014) which studied rotational symmetry breaking for bound state energies using the same alpha cluster model, now extending to other observables like radii and quadrupole moments. The method builds on earlier work by Dudek et al. (2009) on identifying continuum spin in lattice QCD and by Davoudi et al. (2012) on quantifying rotational invariance breaking. The results enable future ab initio lattice simulations for nuclear systems where alpha cluster structures are important, particularly for computing nuclear radii, quadrupole moments, and transition probabilities.
