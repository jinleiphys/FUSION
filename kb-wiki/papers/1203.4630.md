# Quadrupole collectivity beyond N=28: Intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation of 47,48Ar

> We report on the first experimental study of quadrupole collectivity in the very neutron-rich nuclei \nuc{47,48}{Ar} using intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation. These nuclei are located along the path from doubly-magic Ca to collective S and Si isotopes, a critical region of shell evolution and structural change. The deduced $B(E2)$ transition strengths are confronted with large-scale shell-model calculations in the $sdpf$ shell using the state-of-the-art SDPF-U and EPQQM effective interactions. The comparison between experiment and theory indicates that a shell-model description of Ar isotopes around N=28 remains a challenge.

## Key claim

The first experimental study of quadrupole collectivity in 47,48Ar via intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation shows that while shell-model calculations with the SDPF-U and EPQQM effective interactions reproduce the B(E2) value for 48Ar within 2 sigma, they significantly overpredict the low-lying quadrupole collectivity in 47Ar, indicating that a shell-model description of Ar isotopes around N=28 remains a challenge.

## Method

Secondary beams of 47,48Ar were produced by fragmenting a 140 MeV/u 48Ca primary beam on a 9Be target at the NSCL Coupled Cyclotron Facility, separated in the A1900 fragment separator, and delivered to the S800 spectrograph. The projectiles were scattered off a 518 mg/cm2 gold target, and de-excitation gamma rays were detected in coincidence using the Segmented Germanium Array (SeGA) with event-by-event Doppler reconstruction. Very peripheral collisions were selected by restricting the analysis to forward scattering angles corresponding to minimum impact parameters above 13.3 fm, and the angle-integrated Coulomb excitation cross sections were translated into absolute B(E2) excitation strengths using Winther-Alder theory.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ar: B(E2; 0+1 → 2+1) = 346(55) e2fm4, derived from a Coulomb excitation cross section of σ = 74(11) mb at a mid-target energy of 96 MeV/u
- For 47Ar: B(E2; 3/2− → 5/2−) = 135(17) e2fm4, derived from a cross section of σ = 28(3) mb at a mid-target energy of 100 MeV/u
- The 2+ state in 48Ar decays via a 1040 keV gamma-ray transition; the first excited 5/2− state in 47Ar decays via a 1227 keV gamma-ray transition
- Upper limits for other members of the 47Ar multiplet: B(E2; 3/2− → 7/2−) < 74(37) e2fm4 and B(E2; 3/2− → 3/2−) < 20(10) e2fm4

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quadrupole collectivity along the N=28 isotonic chain, where conflicting results exist for 46Ar between early Coulomb excitation measurements (Scheit 1996, Gade 2003) reporting low B(E2) values and a later lifetime measurement (Mengoni 2010) reporting a higher value. The results for 47,48Ar are compared to large-scale shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U interaction (Nowacki and Poves 2009) and the EPQQM interaction (Kaneko 2011), and demonstrate that odd-A nuclei provide sensitive benchmarks for nuclear structure calculations in this region of rapid structural change between doubly-magic 48Ca and the collective S and Si isotopes.
