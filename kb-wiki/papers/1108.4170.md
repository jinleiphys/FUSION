# Relativistic complex separable potential for describing the neutron-proton system in $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial-wave state

> Within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach the relativistic complex separable kernel of the neutron-proton interaction for the coupled $^3S_1^+$-$^3D_1^+$ partial-wave state is constructed. The rank-six separable potential elaborated earlier is real-valued, and therefore makes it possible to describe only the elastic part (phase shifts, low-energy parameters, deuteron properties, etc.) of the elastic neutron-proton scattering. The description of the inelasticity parameter comes out of the imaginary part introduced intthe potential. The complex potential parameters are obtained using the available elastic neutron-proton scattering experimental data up to 1.1 GeV.

## Key claim
Within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, a complex separable kernel for the neutron-proton interaction in the coupled \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) partial-wave state is constructed by adding an imaginary part to a previously developed real-valued rank-six separable potential, enabling description of the inelasticity parameter in elastic neutron-proton scattering up to 1.1 GeV.

## Method
The authors start from the real-valued rank-six separable interaction kernel (MY6) from prior work (Bondarenko et al., 2009), which describes phase shifts, low-energy parameters, and deuteron properties. They introduce an imaginary part to the potential with new parameters \(\bar\lambda^i_{mn}\), which are energy-dependent above an inelasticity threshold \(s_{th}\) via a factor \(\theta(s-s_{th})(1-s_{th}/s)\). The parameters are fitted to experimental inelasticity parameters \(\rho_<\) and \(\rho_>\) from the SAID program using a \(\chi^2\) minimization procedure, while keeping the real part of the potential fixed.

## Key numbers
- The inelasticity threshold kinetic energy is \(T_{\text{Lab, th}} = 0.4\) GeV.
- The potential is constrained by experimental data up to 1.1 GeV laboratory energy.
- The imaginary part parameters \(\bar\lambda^i_{mn}\) are listed in Table 1, with values ranging from \(-366.6091\) GeV\(^2\) (\(\bar\lambda^i_{11}\)) to \(5685.375\) GeV\(^2\) (\(\bar\lambda^i_{55}\)).
- The model (MYI6) describes phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for the \(^3S_1^+\) and \(^3D_1^+\) states up to 1.1 GeV, with deviations appearing at laboratory energies \(\gtrsim 2\) GeV for the \(^3S_1^+\) state.

## Context
This work extends the real-valued separable potential approach of Bondarenko et al. (2009) (MY6) by adding an imaginary part to account for inelasticity, following the method previously applied to uncoupled partial-wave states by Bondarenko et al. (2011). It enables a consistent description of both phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in the coupled \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel, and the results are compared with the SP07 solution from Arndt et al. (2007) and the optical potential FGA from Funk et al. (2001). The authors note that experimental data above 1.1 GeV are needed to determine the correct high-energy behavior of the potential.
