# The Nuclear response in the isoscalar channel

> The nuclear response is evaluated in the frame of the bosonic loop expansion in a purely nucleonic dynamical scheme, which seems to be reliable in handling those channels where a direct excitation of a $Δ$-resonance is not allowed. It is shown that the response strongly depends upon the effective interaction in the spin-transverse isovector channel. New experiments at CEBAF on parity-violating electron scattering could further constrain the form of the effective interaction.

# The Nuclear Response in the Isoscalar Channel

## Key claim

The nuclear response in the isoscalar channel depends strongly on the effective interaction in the spin-transverse isovector channel, but different values of the cutoff parameter q_{c_T} (ranging from 1.1 GeV/c to 1.4 GeV/c) can all satisfactorily reproduce the charge-longitudinal response, meaning that the separated isoscalar and isovector responses are needed to discriminate between models.

## Method

The authors evaluate the nuclear response using the bosonic loop expansion at the 1-loop level, considering only nucleonic degrees of freedom and neglecting Delta resonances in the ground state. They include exchange, self-energy, and correlation diagrams (fig. 1) with pi, rho, and transverse omega exchange, while neglecting longitudinal omega and sigma meson propagation. The effective interaction in the rho channel is parametrized with a momentum-dependent Landau parameter g'_T(q) and a cutoff q_{c_T} that controls the repulsive-attractive balance.

## Key numbers

- The charge-longitudinal response is evaluated at q = 350 MeV/c with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.2 fm^{-1} for different q_{c_T} values: 1100, 1200, 1300, and 1400 MeV/c.
- The cutoff for the rho dipole form factor is set at 2500 MeV/c.
- The 1-loop corrections are approximately 20% of the total result.
- The isoscalar exchange diagram (fig. 1a) has a coefficient of 3/2, while the isovector exchange diagram has a coefficient of -1/2.
- Preliminary SATURNE data at q = 500 MeV/c show the ratio R_L/R_T for the isoscalar response is significantly larger than 1 inside the quasielastic peak.

## Context

This work extends the bosonic loop expansion approach previously applied to the charge-longitudinal response (Cenni et al., 1990; Cenni and Saracco, 1994) and to the S=1, T=1 channels (Cenni and Saracco, 1990). The results agree with calculations by Fabrocini (1994) using the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain expansion and Correlated Basis Function perturbation theory. The paper enables future experiments at CEBAF on parity-violating electron scattering to separate isoscalar and isovector contributions, which could constrain the effective interaction in the spin-transverse channel.
