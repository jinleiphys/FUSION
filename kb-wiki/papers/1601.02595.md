# Jets in d(p)-A Collisions: Color Transparency or Energy Conservation

> The production of jets, and high momentum hadrons from jets, produced in deuteron ($d$)-$Au$ collisions at the relativistic heavy-ion collider (RHIC) and proton ($p$)-$Pb$ collisions at the large hadron collider (LHC) are studied as a function of centrality, a measure of the impact parameter of the collision. A modified version of the event generator PYTHIA, widely used to simulate $p$-$p$ collisions, is used in conjunction with a nuclear Monte-Carlo event generator which simulates the locations of the nucleons within a large nucleus. We demonstrate how events with a hard jet may be simulated, in such a way that the parton distribution function of the projectile is frozen during its interaction with the extended nucleus. Using our approach, we demonstrate that the puzzling enhancement seen in peripheral events at RHIC and the LHC, as well as the suppression seen in central events at the LHC are mainly due to mis-binning of central and semi-central events, containing a jet, as peripheral events. This occurs due to the suppression of soft particle production away from the jet, caused by the depletion of energy available in a nucleon of the deuteron (in $d$-$Au$ at RHIC) or in the proton (in $p$-$Pb$ at LHC), after the production of a hard jet. We conclude that partonic correlations built out of simple energy conservation are mostly responsible for such an effect.

## Key claim

The puzzling enhancement of jet yields in peripheral events and suppression in central events at both RHIC and LHC are mainly due to mis-binning of central and semi-central events containing a jet as peripheral events, caused by the depletion of soft particle production away from the jet due to energy conservation within a single nucleon, rather than color transparency effects.

## Method

A modified version of the PYTHIA event generator was used in conjunction with a nuclear Monte-Carlo event generator that simulates nucleon positions within the large nucleus using a Woods-Saxon distribution. The parton distribution function of the projectile nucleon was frozen during its passage through the extended nucleus, and the collision of a projectile nucleon with multiple target nucleons was treated as a single collision with a "super-nucleon" having a modified PDF without energy enhancement, using a shadowing function that depends on the number of collisions encountered.

## Key numbers

* For the Woods-Saxon distribution of gold (Au): radius R = 6.38 fm, skin depth a = 0.535 fm
* For the Woods-Saxon distribution of lead (Pb): radius R = 6.62 fm, skin depth a = 0.546 fm
* The Hulthén wave-function parameters for the deuteron: a = 0.228/fm, b = 1.18/fm
* The relativistic gamma factor is about 100 at RHIC and close to 2750 at the LHC
* Events were divided into 4 centrality bins: 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-88%

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to understand centrality-dependent nuclear modification factors in asymmetric collisions, including approaches by Bzdak (2014) who proposed similar mechanisms without an event generator framework, Alvioli (2014) who suggested proton wave-function modification in the presence of a hard parton, and Armesto (2015) who used the HIJING event generator. The authors abandoned HIJING because it resamples the parton distribution function between collisions, which changes the distribution of soft partons after a hard parton has been extracted. This work enables future dedicated event generators for studying hard-soft correlations in asymmetric collisions.
