# Correlations between nuclear incompressibility, liquid-gas critical point, and quarkyonic transition

> We systematically probe different parametrizations of the attractive nuclear force based on real gas models to construct the nuclear matter equation of state. In each of the cases, the repulsion between nucleons is treated in the framework of excluded volume, and interaction parameters are fitted to the empirical properties of the nuclear ground state. We calculate the critical temperature $T_c$ and critical particle number density $n_c$, and find that they are strongly correlated. Both are also correlated with the incompressibility $K_0$ in the nuclear ground state. We also include a quarkyonic matter phase in the quasiparticle description and investigate the relationships among $K_0$, transition density to the quarkyonic phase, $n_{tr}$, and corresponding peak in the speed of sound, $v_{s, {\rm max}}^2$. At each density, the quark fraction is found by minimizing the energy density. We find that both $n_{tr}$ and $v_{s, {\rm max}}^2$ are negatively correlated with $K_0$, $n_c$, and $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in real-gas-based nuclear matter equations of state, the nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) is positively correlated with both the critical temperature \(T_c\) and critical density \(n_c\) of the liquid-gas phase transition, and negatively correlated with the transition density to quarkyonic matter \(n_{tr}\) and the peak speed of sound \(v_{s,\text{max}}^2\); these correlations hold across five different parametrizations of the attractive nuclear force when parameters are fitted to the empirical nuclear ground state.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear matter equations of state using five real gas models (van der Waals, Redlich-Kwong-Soave, Peng-Robinson, Clausius, and Dieterici) with repulsion treated via excluded volume and attraction via density-dependent mean fields. For each model, the interaction parameters \(a\) and \(b\) (and third parameters \(\alpha\) or \(c\) for Dieterici and Clausius) are uniquely fitted to reproduce the empirical nuclear ground state saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy \(W_0 = -16\ \text{MeV}\). The critical point is found by solving \((\partial P/\partial n)_T = 0\) and \((\partial^2 P/\partial n^2)_T = 0\) with quantum Fermi statistics included. For the quarkyonic matter extension, nucleons and quarks coexist with a sharp momentum-space separation; at each baryon density the quark fraction is determined by minimizing the total energy density, and the speed of sound is computed from \(v_s^2 = (n/\mu_B) d\mu_B/dn\).

## Key numbers

*   Nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) ranges from 250 MeV (Dieterici with \(\alpha=5/3\)) to 763 MeV (van der Waals).
*   Critical temperature \(T_c\) ranges from 13.0 MeV (Dieterici, \(\alpha=5/3\)) to 19.7 MeV (van der Waals).
*   Critical density \(n_c\) ranges from 0.058 fm\(^{-3}\) (Dieterici, \(\alpha=5/3\)) to 0.072 fm\(^{-3}\) (van der Waals).
*   For the Dieterici model with \(\alpha=5/3\) (\(K_0=250\) MeV), the quarkyonic transition density \(n_{tr} = 2.13\,n_0\) and peak speed of sound \(v_{s,\text{max}}^2 = 0.686\).
*   For the Clausius model with \(c=4.74\ \text{fm}^3\) (\(K_0=250\) MeV), \(n_{tr} = 2.79\,n_0\) and \(v_{s,\text{max}}^2 = 0.742\).
*   For the van der Waals model (\(K_0=763\) MeV), \(n_{tr} = 1.43\,n_0\) and \(v_{s,\text{max}}^2 = 0.596\).
*   Experimental estimates cited: \(K_0 \in [250, 315]\ \text{MeV}\), \(T_c = 17.9 \pm 0.4\ \text{MeV}\), \(n_c = 0.06 \pm 0.01\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (Stone 2014, PhysRevC.87.054622).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear matter correlations within relativistic mean-field models (Lourenco 2016) and real-gas-based equations of state (Vovchenko 2015, 2017) by systematically comparing five different attraction parametrizations. The quarkyonic matter analysis builds on the van der Waals quarkyonic model of Poberezhnyuk (2023) and Moss (2024), extending it to softer equations of state with lower incompressibility. The results enable future exploration of how softer nuclear matter affects neutron-star properties and the behavior of quarkyonic matter at non-zero isospin asymmetry.
