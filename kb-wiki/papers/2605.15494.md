# Forward hadron production in pp collisions at LHC energies from an event generator based on the color glass condensate framework

> We investigate inclusive forward single-hadron production in high-energy proton--proton collisions using a CGC-inspired Monte Carlo event generator, MC-CGC. We carried out a systematic study of the sensitivity of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation to its initial conditions by comparing three parameterizations: the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and its two HERA DIS-constrained variants, MV$^γ$ and MV$^e$. Our results indicate that the current LHCb data favor the MV$^γ$ and MV$^e$ models, while the differences from the original MV model become more pronounced at higher transverse momentum and at mid-rapidity. As a complementary analysis, we also compared the dilute-dense (DHJ factorization) and dense-dense ($k_T$ factorization) frameworks. We found that the $k_T$ factorization framework provides a better description of the particle production spectra at mid-rapidity than the DHJ framework, where both the projectile and target are in the dense regime at LHC energies. Predictions for the FoCal measurements at ALICE, including the production of identified neutral mesons and jets, are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the current LHCb forward hadron production data favor the MV$^\gamma$ and MV$^e$ initial conditions for the rcBK evolution equation over the original MV model, but this preference is conditional on the kinematic region: the differences between models become more pronounced at higher transverse momentum and at mid-rapidity, while the dilute-dense DHJ framework and the dense-dense $k_T$ factorization framework each perform better in different rapidity regimes.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo event generator (MC-CGC) that implements the DHJ dilute-dense factorization formula and the $k_T$ factorization dense-dense formula within the PYTHIA framework, using rcBK-evolved dipole amplitudes with three different initial conditions (MV, MV$^\gamma$, MV$^e$). They simulated inclusive forward single-hadron production in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, incorporating multiple hard scatterings via a negative binomial distribution, initial- and final-state radiation, beam remnant handling, and Lund string fragmentation, then compared the results with LHCb data and made predictions for the ALICE FoCal detector.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse area of the proton is set to $\sigma_0/2 = 16.5$ mb, constrained by DIS fits from HERA
- The rcBK evolution is initialized at $x_0 = 10^{-2}$ for all three initial condition parameterizations
- The Gaussian width parameter for the impact parameter dependent thickness function is $B = 0.325$ fm$^2$, tuned to reproduce LHCb multiplicity data
- The shape parameter for the negative binomial distribution of hard scatterings is $k = 0.9$
- The minimum transverse momentum cut for scattering and radiation processes is set to $p_T = 1.5$ GeV
- The infrared running coupling is frozen at $\alpha_{\text{fr}} = 0.5$ (MV), $0.8$ (MV$^\gamma$), or $0.7$ (MV$^e$), with $\Lambda = 0.241$ GeV (MV, MV$^\gamma$) or $\Lambda = 0.24$ GeV (MV$^e$)
- The initial saturation scales are $Q_{s0}^2 = 0.200$ GeV$^2$ (MV), $0.157$ GeV$^2$ (MV$^\gamma$), and $0.060$ GeV$^2$ (MV$^e$)

## Context

This work extends the previous MC-CGC event generator by Deng et al. (2014) by updating the model to achieve a consistent description of recent LHCb data and by investigating observables not discussed in that earlier work. It builds on global analyses of DIS data at small $x$ based on the rcBK equation by Albacete et al. (2010, 2012) and Lappi et al. (2013), and on the DHJ hybrid formalism by Dumitru et al. (2005) for dilute-dense collisions. The results enable predictions for the upcoming ALICE FoCal measurements, including identified neutral meson and jet production at forward rapidities, and provide a framework for testing saturation physics against future experimental data.
