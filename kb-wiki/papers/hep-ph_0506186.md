# Natural Nuclear Reactor Oklo and Variation of Fundamental Constants Part 1: Computation of Neutronics of Fresh Core

> Using modern methods of reactor physics we have performed full-scale calculations of the natural reactor Oklo. For reliability we have used recent version of two Monte Carlo codes: Russian code MCU REA and world wide known code MCNP (USA). Both codes produce similar results. We have constructed a computer model of the reactor Oklo zone RZ2 which takes into account all details of design and composition. The calculations were performed for three fresh cores with different uranium contents. Multiplication factors, reactivities and neutron fluxes were calculated. We have estimated also the temperature and void effects for the fresh core. As would be expected, we have found for the fresh core a significant difference between reactor and Maxwell spectra, which was used before for averaging cross sections in the Oklo reactor. The averaged cross section of Sm-149 and its dependence on the shift of resonance position (due to variation of fundamental constants) are significantly different from previous results. Contrary to results of some previous papers we find no evidence for the change of the fine structure constant in the past and obtain new, most accurate limits on its variation with time: -4 10^{-17}year^{-1} < d alpha/dt/alpha < 3 10^{-17} year^{-1} A further improvement in the accuracy of the limits can be achieved by taking account of the core burnup. These calculations are in progress.

## Key claim

Using full-scale Monte Carlo simulations of the fresh Oklo reactor core RZ2, the authors find that the reactor neutron spectrum differs significantly from the Maxwell spectrum used in prior work, and they obtain new limits on the variation of the fine structure constant that are consistent with zero change, contrary to some previous claims.

## Method

The authors constructed a computer model of the Oklo reactor zone RZ2 with detailed composition and geometry, using three variants of initial uranium content (38.4%, 49.4%, and 59.6% uranium in dry ore). They performed neutronics calculations with two independent Monte Carlo codes (Russian MCU-REA and US MCNP4C) to compute multiplication factors, reactivities, neutron fluxes, and temperature and void effects for the fresh core, then used the calculated reactor spectrum to obtain the averaged neutron capture cross section of Sm-149 and its dependence on resonance shift.

## Key numbers

- The age of the Oklo reactor is T0 = 1.8 × 10^9 years, with a duration of operation d ≈ 0.6 million years
- The total energy yield of the reactor was 1.5 × 10^4 MWa, corresponding to a mean power output of 25 kW
- The initial enrichment of U-235 1.8 billion years ago was ζ5(0) = 3.1%
- The effective multiplication factor Keff for the fresh core with 49.4% uranium content is 1.1271(1) (MCU-REA) and 1.1238(2) (MCNP4C)
- The mean thermal neutron flux (E < 0.625 eV) for the fresh core with 49.4% uranium is 0.63 × 10^8 n/cm^2·s
- The limit on the variation of the fine structure constant is: -4 × 10^{-17} year^{-1} ≤ dα/dt/α ≤ 3 × 10^{-17} year^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the approach of Damour and Dyson (1996), who first calculated the temperature-dependent capture cross section of Sm-149 using a Maxwell spectrum, and of Fujii et al. (2000), who reduced the experimental error on that cross section. The authors improve upon these prior studies by using the actual reactor neutron spectrum (which includes the Fermi slowing-down tail) rather than a Maxwell distribution, and by using full-scale Monte Carlo simulations of the Oklo core. The results contradict some previous papers that claimed evidence for a change in the fine structure constant, and the authors note that further improvement in accuracy can be achieved by accounting for core burnup, which they report as work in progress.
