# Isospin composition of the high-momentum fluctuations in nuclei from asymptotic momentum distributions

> The variations of short-range correlations (SRC) across nuclei can be quantified in an approximately model-independent fashion in terms of the so-called SRC scaling factors. We provide predictions for the SRC scaling factors for both bound protons and bound neutrons across the nuclear periodic table and determine the contribution of the different nucleon pair combinations to this quantity. Thereby we employ the low-order correlation operator approximation (LCA) to compute the SRC contribution to the single-nucleon momentum distribution. We do this for a sample of fifteen nuclei from He to Pb thereby gaining access to the evolution of the SRC scaling factor with the nuclear mass and the neutron-to-proton ratio. For the studied sample of fifteen nuclei, the total SRC scaling factor is in the range 4.05-5.14. The computed SRC scaling factors in the LCA are in line with those of ab-initio calculations and with measured quantities from SRC-sensitive inclusive electron-scattering data. In asymmetric nuclei there are sizeable differences between the SRC scaling factors for protons and neutrons. It is suggested that this phenomenon may impact the variations of the magnitude of the European muon collaboration (EMC) effect across nuclei. Our results corroborate the finding that SRC physics can be qualitatively understood by universal principles that build on local modifications of mean-field wave functions of nucleon pairs.

## Key claim
The paper provides predictions for SRC scaling factors across fifteen nuclei from He to Pb using the low-order correlation operator approximation (LCA), showing that the total SRC scaling factor ranges from 4.05 to 5.14, with approximately 3 attributable to proton-neutron correlations, and that in asymmetric nuclei the minority species (protons) becomes increasingly more short-range correlated with increasing neutron-to-proton ratio.

## Method
The authors employ the low-order correlation operator approximation (LCA) to compute the SRC contribution to single-nucleon momentum distributions for fifteen nuclei spanning mass numbers A=4 to A=208 and neutron-to-proton ratios from 1.0 to 1.54. The LCA uses a Slater determinant wave function modified by a correlation operator that accounts for central (Jastrow), tensor, and spin-isospin SRC correlations, with all terms included up to order O(G^2). The SRC scaling factors are extracted from the asymptotic high-momentum behavior of the computed single-nucleon momentum distributions by evaluating A-to-deuteron ratios at momenta exceeding about 4 fm^-1.

## Key numbers
- Total SRC scaling factor range across fifteen nuclei: 4.05 to 5.14
- Approximately 3 of the total SRC scaling factor is attributable to proton-neutron (pn) correlations
- The study covers mass numbers 4 ≤ A ≤ 208 and neutron-to-proton ratios 1.0 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1.54
- The four symmetric nuclei studied: 4He, 12C, 16O, 40Ca
- The eleven asymmetric nuclei studied: 9Be, 27Al, 40Ar, 48Ca, 56Fe, 63Cu, 84Kr, 108Ag, 124Xe, 197Au, 208Pb
- The Fermi momentum used as reference: p_F = 1.25 fm^-1
- The high-momentum tail region studied: p ≳ 2 fm^-1
- The asymptotic scaling region: momenta exceeding about 4 fm^-1
- Harmonic oscillator parameter used: ℏω = 45A^(-1/3) - 25A^(-2/3)

## Context
This work extends the study of short-range correlations to medium-heavy and heavy nuclei (A>40) where ab-initio calculations are not available, providing systematic predictions for SRC scaling factors across the nuclear table. The LCA results are compared with ab-initio quantum Monte Carlo calculations for light nuclei (Lonardoni 2017) and with measured a2 scaling factors from inclusive electron-scattering data (Fomin 2011, Schmookler 2019). The paper also connects to the observed linear correlation between SRC scaling factors and the magnitude of the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect (Hen 2012, Weinstein 2010), suggesting that the isospin dependence of SRC may impact variations in the EMC effect across nuclei.
