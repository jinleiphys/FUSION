# Density-functional theory for fermions in the unitary regime

> In the unitary regime, fermions interact strongly via two-body potentials that exhibit a zero range and a (negative) infinite scattering length. The energy density is proportional to the free Fermi gas with a proportionality constant $ξ$. We use a simple density functional parametrized by an effective mass and the universal constant $ξ$, and employ Kohn-Sham density-functional theory to obtain the parameters from fit to one exactly solvable two-body problem. This yields $ξ=0.42$ and a rather large effective mass. Our approach is checked by similar Kohn-Sham calculations for the exactly solvable Calogero model.

## Key claim
The paper uses a simple two-parameter density functional (universal constant ξ and effective mass) fitted to the exactly solvable two-fermion harmonic trap problem to obtain ξ = 0.42, which agrees with more elaborate Monte Carlo results; the abstract does not mention that this value depends on simultaneously fitting the effective mass and that the result is checked against the Calogero model.

## Method
Kohn-Sham density-functional theory was applied to fermions in the unitary regime using a density functional parametrized by the universal constant ξ and an effective mass m_eff, with the kinetic energy term modified by m/m_eff and the density-dependent term proportional to (ξ − m/m_eff) ρ^(5/3). The parameters were determined by fitting the Kohn-Sham density and energy to the exact analytical density and energy of two spin-1/2 fermions in a harmonic trap interacting via a zero-range potential with infinite scattering length. The approach was validated by applying the same functional form to the exactly solvable Calogero model in one dimension.

## Key numbers
- ξ = 0.42 from best fit to the two-fermion harmonic trap problem, with m/m_eff = 0.69.
- The Kohn-Sham energy for the two-fermion system deviates about 0.1% from the exact value E_ex = 2 ħω.
- For the Calogero model with β = 4, the effective mass follows m/m_eff ≈ 6.3 + 8.0/N² for particle numbers N = 2, 4, 16, 32.
- The Thomas-Fermi estimate from equating Eq. (E_ex) with Eq. (TF) for N = 2 yields ξ = 64/6^(8/3) ≈ 0.54.
- Fixing ξ = 0.44 (Carlson et al., 2003) gives m/m_eff = 0.52 with energy deviation less than 1%.
- Fixing m/m_eff = 1 gives ξ = 0.40 with energy deviation less than 1%.

## Context
This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach for the unitary regime by incorporating gradient corrections through an effective mass, improving upon the naive Thomas-Fermi result (ξ ≈ 0.54) which deviates about 20% from Monte Carlo values. The result ξ = 0.42 agrees with Monte Carlo simulations by Carlson et al. (2003) (ξ ≈ 0.44 ± 0.01) and Astrakharchik et al. (2004) (ξ = 0.42 ± 0.01), while simpler approaches by Heiselberg (2001) (ξ = 0.326) and Engelbrecht et al. (1997) (ξ = 0.59) gave different values. The method enables a simple DFT-based determination of the universal constant without requiring full Monte Carlo simulations, and the functional form can be tested on other scale-invariant systems like the Calogero model.
