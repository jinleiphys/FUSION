# Low Energy Tests of the Standard Model from Beta-Decay and Muon Capture

## Key claim
Two recent low energy precision experiments, one in nuclear beta-decay and one in nuclear muon capture on 3He, set limits on couplings beyond the Standard Model that are complementary to high energy constraints, probing regions of parameter space inaccessible to collider experiments, particularly for left-right symmetric models, contact interactions, and leptoquarks.

## Method
The beta-decay experiment measured the ratio of longitudinal polarizations of beta particles emitted parallel and antiparallel to the nuclear polarization of oriented 12N and 107In nuclei, extracting the parameter Delta which vanishes in the Standard Model. The muon capture experiment measured the statistical muon capture rate on 3He to the triton channel with 0.3% precision, and the result was compared to theoretical predictions based on CVC and PCAC to extract values for the pseudoscalar form factor and second-class current form factors.

## Key numbers
- Delta = 0.0004 ± 0.0026 from combined 12N and 107In beta-decay polarization-asymmetry measurements
- Experimental muon capture rate on 3He: lambda_exp = 1496 ± 4 s^-1
- Theoretical muon capture rate on 3He: lambda_theor = 1497 ± 12 s^-1
- Ratio of extracted pseudoscalar form factor to PCAC prediction at nuclear level: 1.004 ± 0.076
- Ratio of extracted pseudoscalar form factor to PCAC prediction at nucleon level: 1.05 ± 0.19
- Second-class scalar form factor: F_S = -0.062 ± 1.18
- Second-class tensor form factor: F_T = 0.075 ± 1.43
- Limit on charged vector contact interaction scale from beta-decay: Lambda^V_Reta2 > 2.5 TeV (90% CL)
- Limit on charged vector contact interaction from muon capture: Lambda^V_LR > 4.9 TeV (90% CL)
- Limit on tensor contact interaction scale from muon capture: Lambda^T_RL > 9.3 TeV (90% CL) for G_T = 1
- Limit on S0 and V0 leptoquark couplings from beta-decay: |lambda^R_S0 lambda^nuR_S0 / M^2(S0)| and 2|lambda^R_V0 lambda^nuR_V0 / M^2(V0)| each less than 4.1 TeV^-2 (90% CL)
- Limit on tensor leptoquark couplings from muon capture: |lambda^L_S0 lambda^R_S0 / M^2(S0)| |G_T| and |lambda^L_S1/2 lambda^R_S1/2 / M^2(S1/2)| |G_T| both less than 0.29 TeV^-2 (90% CL)

## Context
This work extends the program of low energy precision tests of the Standard Model, complementing high energy constraints from collider experiments such as those at FERMILAB and HERA. The beta-decay result probes regions in left-right symmetric model parameter space inaccessible to collider experiments (Thomas, 1997), particularly for the ratio V^R_ud/V^L_ud when the heavier charged gauge boson mass M_2 is close to the W mass. The muon capture result provides the most precise test of nuclear PCAC available, contrasting with radiative muon capture on the proton (Jonk, 1997) which deviates by more than a factor of 1.5 from the PCAC prediction. These measurements enable new constraints on leptoquark couplings (Buch, 1997; Davi, 1997) and contact interaction scales that are not directly accessible to unpolarized high energy measurements.
