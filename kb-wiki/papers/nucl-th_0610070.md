# Tensor polarization of $ω$ produced at threshold in $p-p$ collisions

> It is shown that the dominant decay mode of $\vecω\to π^+ π^- π^0$ can be employed to determine the Fano statistical tensor $t^2_0$ of $\vec ω$ with respect to the quantization axis normal to the decay plane. In $pp \to pp \vec ω$ one can choose decay planes with different orientations for a given $ω$ direction, $θ$. By choosing three different experimentally convenient orientations of the decay planes for the same $θ$, one may determine empirically the $t^2_0$ and $t^2_{\pm 2}$ characterising the tensor polarization of $ω$ in the Transverse Frame for $pp \to pp \vec ω$.

# Tensor polarization of ω produced at threshold in p-p collisions

## Key claim
The dominant decay mode ω → π⁺π⁻π⁰ can be used to determine the Fano statistical tensors t²₀ and t²±₂ characterizing the tensor polarization of ω in the Transverse Frame for pp → ppω, provided one chooses three different experimentally convenient orientations of the decay planes for the same ω direction θ.

## Method
The authors derive the angular distribution of the three pions from ω decay using irreducible tensor operators and Racah techniques, showing that the distribution depends only on the tensor analyzing power A²₀(DF) and the t²₀(DF) component in the decay frame. They relate t²₀(DF) to the Fano statistical tensors t²₀, t²₂, and t²₋₂ in the Transverse Frame via Wigner rotation matrices, using Euler angles (α, β, γ) that describe the orientation of the decay plane relative to the Transverse Frame. By measuring the number of decay events N(DF) in three different decay plane orientations for the same ω production angle θ, one can solve the resulting linear equations for the three real unknowns: t²₀, Re(t²₂), and Im(t²₂).

## Key numbers
- The multiplicative factor (1 − √2 t²₀(DF)) in the decay distribution can vary between 0 and 3, since −√2 ≤ t²₀ ≤ 1/√2.
- The distance probed is approximately 0.21 fm for ω production, compared to 0.53 fm for pion production.
- The OZI rule estimate for the φ/ω production ratio is R_OZI = 4.2 × 10⁻³, while the latest experimental estimate is R_φ/ω ≈ 8 × R_OZI.
- Total cross sections for pp → ppω have been measured at five different c.m. energies in the range 3.8 MeV to 30 MeV above threshold by Hibou et al., and at excess energies of 92 MeV and 173 MeV by other groups.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent irreducible tensor approach previously applied to pion production (Ramachandran et al., 2005) and to ω production using the ω → π⁰γ decay mode (Ramachandran et al., 2004). The authors note that the Jülich meson exchange model (Hanhart et al., 2000) deviates significantly from empirically extracted estimates for certain transitions in pp → ppπ⁰. The measurement of ω tensor polarization enables empirical determination of threshold partial wave amplitudes for pp → ppω, and is motivated by the possibility of fully reconstructing ω → π⁺π⁻π⁰ decays to identify the decay plane orientation relative to the beam direction (Brinkmann, private communication).
