# Mesons in Nuclei and Partial Restoration of Chiral Symmetry

> Recent topics on mesons in nuclei are discussed by especially emphasizing the role of the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the nuclear medium. The spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in vacuum is considered to be incompletely restored in finite nuclear density systems with moderate reduction of the magnitude of the quark condensate. On the partial restoration of chiral symmetry, the wave function renormalization is important to be taken into account for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons. We also discuss the possible change of the meson properties in the nuclear medium and meson-nucleus systems for the $\bar K$, $η$, $K^{+}$ and $η^{\prime}$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter (with approximately 30% reduction of the quark condensate) leads to observable modifications of meson properties in nuclei, but emphasizes that for the $\bar K$ meson, the dynamics of the $\bar KN$ interaction and the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance are much more significant than the effects of partial chiral restoration.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical frameworks including chiral perturbation theory, coupled-channel analyses, and the linear sigma model to describe in-medium modifications of pseudoscalar mesons ($\bar K$, $K^{+}$, $\eta$, $\eta^{\prime}$) in nuclear matter. For each meson system, the in-medium self-energy is calculated using density-dependent interactions, with particular attention to the wave function renormalization of Nambu-Goldstone bosons and the coupling of the $\eta$ meson to the $N^{*}(1535)$-nucleon hole mode.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate in nuclear matter is reduced by approximately 30% compared to vacuum, as suggested by pionic atom data and pion-nucleus elastic scattering
- The $K^{+}$-nucleus scattering amplitude is repulsively enhanced by about 15% compared to the free $K^{+}N$ scattering amplitude
- The wave function renormalization for $K^{+}$ in nuclear matter gives a factor $Z \approx 1 + 0.082 \rho/\rho_c$, where $\rho_c = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- The in-medium $\pi^{0} \to \gamma\gamma$ decay width is enhanced by a factor $Z \simeq 1 + 0.4 \rho/\rho_0$
- The mass difference between $N$ and $N^{*}(1535)$ is estimated to be reduced by about 150 MeV at saturation density in a chiral doublet model
- The $\eta^{\prime}$ mass reduction is estimated at 100 MeV at saturation density (with 35% reduction of the quark condensate), with alternative models predicting 80 MeV (linear sigma model) or 150 MeV (NJL model)
- A two-body $\eta^{\prime}N$ bound state is found with 6 MeV binding energy, and with coupled-channel effects including $\eta N$, the binding energy becomes 12 MeV with 6 MeV width
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is described by a two-pole structure: one state at around 1420 MeV with a small width (bound state of $\bar KN$) and another at around 1390 MeV with a large width ($s$-wave resonance of $\pi\Sigma$)
- The two-body absorption $\bar KNN \to YN$ contributes 30% of total absorption at saturation density

## Context

This review extends prior theoretical studies on partial chiral restoration in nuclear matter by Cohen (1991), Drukarev (1991), Brown (1996), Kaiser (2008), and Meissner (2002), and builds on experimental evidence from pionic atoms by Suzuki (2002) and pion-nucleus elastic scattering by Friedman (2004). The work connects to ongoing experimental efforts such as the J-PARC E31 experiment investigating the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance via $K^{-}d$ reactions, and enables future searches for $\eta$ and $\eta^{\prime}$ mesonic nuclei through reactions such as $(\gamma, p)$, $(d, ^{3}{\rm He})$, and $(p,d)$ on nuclear targets.
