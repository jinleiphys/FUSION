# Equation of State for Strange Quark Matter in a Separable Model

> We present the thermodynamics of a nonlocal chiral quark model with separable 4-fermion interaction for the case of U(3) flavor symmetry within a functional integral approach. The four free parameters of the model are fixed by the chiral condensate, and by the pseudoscalar meson properties (pion mass, kaon mass, pion decay constant). We discuss the T=0 equation of state (EoS) which describes quark confinement (zero quark matter pressure) below the critical chemical potential mu_c=333 MeV. The new result of the present approach is that the strange quark deconfinement is separated from the light quark one and occurs only at a higher chemical potential of mu_{c,s}=492 MeV. We compare the resulting EoS to bag model ones for two and three quark flavors, which have the phase transition to the vacuum with zero pressure also at mu_c. We study quark matter stars in general relativity theory assuming beta-equilibrium with electrons and show that for configurations with masses close to the maximum of stability at M=1.62 ... 1.64 M_{solar} strange quark matter can occur.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a nonlocal chiral quark model with a separable interaction, the deconfinement of strange quarks is separated from that of light quarks, occurring only at a higher chemical potential of μ_{c,s}=492 MeV, whereas light quark deconfinement occurs at μ_c=333 MeV. This separation is a new result that differs from bag models or NJL models, and it implies that strange quark matter appears in compact stars only near the maximum stable mass.

## Method

The authors use a nonlocal chiral quark model with a separable 4-fermion interaction and U(3) flavor symmetry, treated via a functional integral approach with bosonisation and mean-field approximation. The four free parameters (coupling constant D0, interaction range Λ, light quark mass m_q, and strange quark mass m_s) are fixed by the chiral condensate, pion mass (140 MeV), kaon mass (494 MeV), and pion decay constant (93 MeV). The T=0 equation of state is derived from the grand canonical thermodynamic potential, and the gap equations for the flavor-dependent mass gaps Δ_f are solved separately. The resulting EoS is then used to compute compact star configurations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation under beta-equilibrium with electrons.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for light quark deconfinement: μ_c = 333 MeV.
- Critical chemical potential for strange quark deconfinement: μ_{c,s} = 492 MeV.
- Strange quark mass gap in the confining phase: Δ_s = 682 MeV.
- Bag constant for a two-flavor bag model describing the separable model in the region 333 MeV ≤ μ ≤ 492 MeV: B = 81.3 MeV/fm³.
- Bag constant for a three-flavor bag model: B = 100.7 MeV/fm³.
- Critical energy density for quark confinement: ε_c = 350 MeV/fm³.
- Critical energy density for three-flavor deconfinement: ε_{c,s} = 930 MeV/fm³.
- Maximum mass for three-flavor quark stars in the separable model: M = 1.64 M_⊙.
- Maximum mass for two-flavor quark stars in the separable model: M = 1.71 M_⊙.
- Maximum radius for quark stars in the separable model: 11 km.
- Maximum mass for bag model strange stars: about 1.5 M_⊙.
- Maximum radius for bag model strange stars: about 8 km.
- Electron fraction maximum in beta-equilibrium: x_e ≃ 0.002.
- Central energy density at maximum mass: about 1350 MeV/fm³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of separable quark models (Birse 1994, Golli 1998, Blaschke et al. 2000) from two flavors to three flavors, and contrasts with the non-confining NJL model (Klevansky 1992, Buballa 1996) which predicts free quarks below the chiral phase transition. The key advance is that the separable model exhibits confinement (zero pressure below μ_c) and a delayed strange quark deconfinement, which differs from bag model predictions (Witten 1984, Alcock et al. 1986). The results enable more realistic calculations of compact star structure, showing that strange quark matter appears only near the maximum mass of 1.64 M_⊙, with masses below 1.62 M_⊙ containing only two-flavor quark matter.
