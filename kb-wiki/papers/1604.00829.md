# Application of the triaxial quadrupole-octupole rotor to the ground and negative-parity levels of actinide nuclei

> In this work we examine the possibility to describe yrast positive- and negative-parity excitations of deformed even-even nuclei through a collective rotation model in which the nuclear surface is characterized by triaxial quadrupole and octupole deformations. The nuclear moments of inertia are expressed as sums of quadrupole and octupole parts. By assuming an adiabatic separation of rotation and vibration degrees of freedom we suppose that the structure of the positive- and negative- parity bands may be determined by the triaxial-rigid-rotor motion of the nucleus. By diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a symmetrized rotor basis with embedded parity we obtain a model description for the yrast positive- and negative-parity bands in several actinide nuclei. We show that the energy displacement between the opposite-parity sequences can be explained as the result of the quadrupole-octupole triaxiality.

## Key claim

The energy displacement between opposite-parity sequences in actinide nuclei can be explained as the result of quadrupole-octupole triaxiality and the associated K-mixing effect, rather than solely by octupole vibration-rotation dynamics. The model assumes an adiabatic separation of rotation and vibration, treating the deformation parameters as constants that effectively determine a rigid triaxial rotor.

## Method

The nuclear surface is parametrized with triaxial quadrupole deformations (parameters β₂, γ) and triaxial octupole deformations (parameters β₃, η). The moments of inertia are expressed as sums of quadrupole and octupole contributions, and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a symmetrized rotor basis with built-in parity, where K takes only even values and the parity of the rotation state is given by (-1)^I. The four adjustable parameters (two effective mass parameters and two triaxiality angles) are fitted to experimental data through least-square minimization.

## Context

This work extends the triaxial rotor concept of Davydov and Chaban (1960) and earlier treatments of non-axial quadrupole-octupole deformations (Nadirbekov et al., 2015; Minkov et al., 2006) by applying the adiabatic approximation to formulate a pure rotor problem. The results are comparable to descriptions from the quadrupole-octupole vibration-rotation concept (Minkov et al., 2006; Nomura et al., 2012) but invoke a different mechanism based on K-mixing from triaxiality. The model enables future work beyond the adiabatic approximation that would include vibration modes to connect different approaches to alternating-parity spectra.
