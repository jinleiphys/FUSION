# Neutron-${}^3$H scattering above the four-nucleon breakup threshold

> The four-body equations of Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas are solved for the neutron-${}^3$H scattering at energies above the four-nucleon breakup threshold. The accuracy and practical applicability of the employed complex energy method is significantly improved by the use of integration with the special weights. This allows to obtain fully converged results with realistic nuclear interactions. A satisfactory description of the existing neutron-${}^3$H elastic scattering data is obtained.

## Key claim

The authors solve the four-body Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for neutron-${}^3$H scattering above the four-nucleon breakup threshold using a complex energy method with special integration weights, obtaining fully converged results with realistic nuclear interactions that provide a satisfactory description of existing elastic scattering data, though the agreement depends on the specific energy and angular region.

## Method

The symmetrized AGS equations for four-particle transition operators are solved in momentum-space partial-wave framework using the complex energy method, where calculations are performed for finite positive imaginary energy values $\varepsilon$ and then extrapolated to the $\varepsilon \to +0$ limit via analytic continuation using continued fractions. The key technical improvement is the use of special integration weights that absorb quasi-singular factors of the pole type, allowing accurate numerical integration with modest grid sizes (30-40 points for Jacobi momenta, 10 points for angular integrations). The method is tested using the INOY04 and CD Bonn realistic two-nucleon potentials, with partial waves up to $l_x,l_y,l_z,j_x,j_y,J_y \le 4$ and total angular momentum $\mathcal{J} \le 5$.

## Key numbers

- At 22.1 MeV neutron energy, the ${}^1S_0$ phase shift is 62.63 degrees and inelasticity is 0.990 (INOY04 potential, $[\varepsilon_{\mathrm{min}},\varepsilon_{\mathrm{max}}] = [1.0,2.0]$ MeV)
- At 22.1 MeV, the ${}^3P_0$ phase shift is 43.03 degrees with inelasticity 0.959, and the ${}^3P_2$ phase shift is 65.27 degrees with inelasticity 0.950 (INOY04 potential)
- The total cross section $\sigma_t$ for $n$-${}^3$H scattering is 947 mb at 14.1 MeV, 738 mb at 18.0 MeV, and 597 mb at 22.1 MeV (INOY04 potential)
- The breakup cross section $\sigma_b$ is 19 mb at 14.1 MeV, 41 mb at 18.0 MeV, and 61 mb at 22.1 MeV (INOY04 potential)
- The INOY04 potential reproduces experimental binding energies of ${}^3$H (8.48 MeV) and ${}^3$He (7.72 MeV) without an irreducible three-nucleon force
- Test calculations at 3.5 and 6.0 MeV neutron energy show agreement better than 0.05% between the complex energy method and previous real-energy subtraction technique results

## Context

This work extends previous four-nucleon scattering calculations that were limited to energies below the three-cluster breakup threshold, including the AGS framework results by Deltuva (2007) and benchmark comparisons between hyperspherical harmonics, Faddeev-Yakubovsky, and AGS techniques by Viviani (2011). The method enables fully converged calculations with realistic interactions above the four-body breakup threshold, where previous attempts by Uzu (2003) and Lazauskas were limited to simple model potentials, and the no-core shell model RGM by Quaglioni (2008) was insufficient as it included only the ground state of the three-nucleon system. The authors note that extension to other four-nucleon reactions is in progress.
