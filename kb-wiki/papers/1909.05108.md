# Supernarrow Dibaryons

> An analysis of the experimental search for supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs) have been performed. The sum rules for SND masses have been constructed. The calculated values of the SND masses are in good agreement with the existing experimental values. It has been shown that the SND decay leads to the formation of N* with small masses. Experimental observations of N* is an additional confirmation of the possibility of the SND existence.

# Supernarrow Dibaryons

## Key claim
Supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs), six-quark states whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli principle, exist with masses below the pion production threshold (M < 2m_N + m_π), and their decay proceeds via photon emission through the virtual singlet ³¹S₀ state, producing narrow peaks in missing-mass spectra; however, the evidence remains inconclusive because some experiments (e.g., RCNP) found no such states, and the data from supporting experiments (INR, LEGS, SPES3) suffer from limited statistics or resolution.

## Method
The author constructs sum rules for SND masses using dispersion relations with two subtractions, calculating the self-energy in one-loop approximation through pion interactions with the six-quark state of the deuteron. The coupling constant for the vertex D(0,1⁺) + π → D(1,1⁻) is fixed by requiring reproduction of the 1904 MeV mass, and subsequent masses are computed iteratively by taking the previous SND as the intermediate state. The decay widths into γNN are calculated assuming the dominant contribution comes from the ³¹S₀ intermediate state, with the probability η of a six-quark state in the deuteron taken as 0.01.

## Key numbers
- Three narrow peaks observed in the missing mass M_{pX₁} spectra of the reaction pd → p + pX₁ at 1904 ± 2 MeV, 1926 ± 2 MeV, and 1942 ± 2 MeV, each with width equal to the experimental resolution (~5 MeV) and statistical significances of 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations, respectively.
- The corresponding missing mass M_{X₁} peaks from the same reaction appear at 966 ± 2 MeV, 986 ± 2 MeV, and 1003 ± 2 MeV, consistent with simulation of SND decay D → γ + ³¹S₀ → γ pn.
- Calculated SND masses from the sum rules: 1904 MeV (1⁻), 1925 MeV (1⁺), 1945 MeV (1⁻), 1965 MeV (1⁺), 1985 MeV (1⁻), and 2006 MeV (1⁺); these agree with experimental values 1904 ± 2, 1926 ± 2, 1942 ± 2, and 1956 ± 6 MeV (the latter from the d₁* candidate in pp → ppγγ at JINR).
- Decay widths for D(1,1⁻) range from 0.0514 eV at M = 1904 MeV to 5.073 eV at M = 2006 MeV; for D(1,1⁺) from 0.206 eV to 23.49 eV over the same mass range, calculated at η = 0.01.
- The product η g₁²/4π = 1.4 × 10⁻⁴ and η g₂²/4π = 3 × 10⁻⁴ extracted from LEGS data on the reaction γ⃗ d → π⁺ D → γ' π⁺ nn.
- The coupling constant ḡ₁²/4π = 26.5888 fixed by requiring reproduction of the 1904 MeV mass; ḡ₂²/4π = 3/4 of that value by isotopic invariance.
- Predicted N* masses from SND decay: 965 ± 2, 986 ± 2, 1005 ± 2, 1025 ± 1, 1044 ± 1, and 1063 ± 1 MeV, compared with experimental N* candidates at 966 ± 2, 986 ± 2, 1003 ± 2 MeV (INR) and 1044 ± 2 MeV (SPES3 at Saturne).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for supernarrow dibaryons originally proposed by Fil'kov and collaborators (2000s) and compares with the MIT bag model calculations of Mulders et al. (1980), which predicted NN-decoupled dibaryons but with masses above the pion threshold, and the chiral soliton model of Kopeliovich (1990s), which gave masses below threshold. The analysis addresses the experimental controversy between positive results from INR (Konobeevsky et al., 2000s) and LEGS (Norum et al., 2000s) and the null result from RCNP (Tamii et al., 2000s). The paper enables future searches using linearly polarized photons on deuterium, where the cross-section ratio σ(α=90°)/σ(α=0°) for π⁺ emission is predicted to show large variation, providing a sensitive test for vector SNDs.
