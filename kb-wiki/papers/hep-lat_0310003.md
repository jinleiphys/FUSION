# Extraction of parton distributions from lattice QCD

> We review the calculation of moments of both the polarized and unpolarized parton distribution functions of the nucleon in lattice QCD, and in particular their extrapolation to the physical region. We also discuss the reconstruction of the x dependence of the valence quark distributions in the nucleon from a finite number of lattice moments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that lattice QCD calculations of parton distribution function (PDF) moments, when combined with chiral perturbation theory extrapolations that incorporate the correct leading non-analytic behavior in the quark mass, can yield results for unpolarized isovector moments that agree with experiment, but for polarized distributions the agreement is conditional on understanding finite-volume effects and the role of the Delta isobar, with residual discrepancies of 10-15% remaining for the axial charge g_A.

## Method

The authors review the calculation of moments of unpolarized, helicity, and transversity parton distribution functions from lattice QCD, focusing on the extrapolation of these moments from the unphysically large quark masses used in simulations (typically above 30 MeV) to the physical quark mass (approximately 5 MeV) using chiral perturbation theory constraints. They then reconstruct the x-dependence of valence quark distributions by fitting a parametric form x f(x) = A x^b (1-x)^c (1 + epsilon sqrt(x) + gamma x) to the extrapolated moments, fixing epsilon and gamma to phenomenological values and determining A, b, and c from the lattice data.

## Key numbers

* Unpolarized isovector moment <x^1>_{u-d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.18(3) compared to experimental value 0.145(4)
* Unpolarized isovector moment <x^2>_{u-d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.05(2) compared to experimental value 0.054(1)
* Unpolarized isovector moment <x^3>_{u-d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.02(1) compared to experimental value 0.022(1)
* Axial charge g_A = <x^0>_{Delta u - Delta d}: extrapolated lattice value 1.12(6) compared to experimental value 1.267(4)
* Helicity moment <x^1>_{Delta u - Delta d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.27(2) compared to experimental value 0.210(25)
* Helicity moment <x^2>_{Delta u - Delta d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.14(4) compared to experimental value 0.070(11)
* Transversity moment <1>_{delta u - delta d}: extrapolated lattice value 1.23(7) with no experimental comparison available
* Transversity moment <x>_{delta u - delta d}: extrapolated lattice value 0.51(9) with no experimental comparison available
* Lattice simulations are performed with quark masses typically above 30 MeV, while physical u and d quark masses are approximately 5 MeV
* The chiral coefficient for unpolarized moments is c_LNA = -(1 + 3 g_A^2)/(4 pi f_pi)^2
* The chiral coefficient for helicity moments is Delta c_LNA = -(1 + 2 g_A^2)/(4 pi f_pi)^2
* The chiral coefficient for transversity moments is delta c_LNA = -(1 + 4 g_A^2)/2(4 pi f_pi)^2
* A lattice box size of approximately 4 fm on a side may be needed to see substantial chiral curvature in the extrapolation

## Context

This review extends the work of Detmold et al. (2001) on chiral extrapolation of PDF moments and the earlier demonstration by Thomas et al. (2000) of the leading non-analytic behavior m_pi^2 log m_pi arising from pi N intermediate states. The paper builds on lattice simulations from the QCDSF, KEK, and RBCK Collaborations (2002) and provides a framework for reconstructing x-dependent PDFs from a finite number of lattice moments. The work enables future lattice calculations at lower pion masses and larger volumes to test the predicted chiral curvature, and it identifies the resolution of the g_A discrepancy as essential for reliable predictions of transversity distributions, for which no experimental data yet exist.
