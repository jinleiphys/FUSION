# Heavy meson flow harmonics in event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamics

> Event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics is combined with heavy quark energy loss models to compute heavy flavor flow cumulants $v_2\{2\}$, $v_3\{2\}$, and $v_2\{4\}$ as well as the nuclear modification factors of $D^0$ and $B^0$ mesons in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV. Our results indicate that bottom quarks can flow as much as charm quarks in the $p_T$ range 8--30 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within their model, bottom quarks can flow as much as charm quarks in the $p_T$ range 8–30 GeV, but this result is conditional on the specific energy loss models and hadronization parameters used, and the authors note that the observables are not yet sensitive enough to distinguish between the two energy loss models tested.

## Method

Event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro) with MCKLN initial conditions was combined with two simple heavy quark energy loss models (one with $dE/dx \sim T^2$ and one with $dE/dx \sim \alpha$) to compute heavy flavor flow cumulants and nuclear modification factors. Heavy quarks were sampled with FONLL initial momentum distributions, propagated through the hydrodynamic backgrounds, and hadronized via fragmentation when the local temperature fell below a decoupling temperature $T_d$ in the range 120–160 MeV. Multi-particle cumulants $v_2\{2\}$, $v_3\{2\}$, and $v_2\{4\}$ were computed by oversampling each hydro event with millions of heavy quarks.

## Key numbers

* $D^0$ and $B^0$ meson $R_{AA}$ were computed in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, with parameters fixed by matching $D^0$ $R_{AA}$ to data at $p_T \sim 10$ GeV in the 0–10% centrality class.
* $v_2\{2\}$ for $D^0$ and $B^0$ mesons was computed in the 30–50% centrality class and found to be nearly equal in the $p_T$ range 10–30 GeV.
* $v_3\{2\}$ for heavy mesons was found to be approximately an order of magnitude smaller than $v_2\{2\}$.
* The ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ was found to be very close to unity for both $D^0$ and $B^0$ mesons, nearly constant in $p_T$, regardless of energy loss model and $T_d$ value.
* The decoupling temperature $T_d$ was varied in the range 120–160 MeV, producing bands in the results.

## Context

This work extends event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics calculations previously applied to light hadrons and jet energy loss (Noronha-Hostler 2016, Betz 2016) into the heavy flavor sector. It provides predictions for $B^0$ meson flow harmonics at LHC energies and introduces the computation of $v_2\{4\}$ for heavy mesons, following the cumulant methodology of Luzum (2012). The results enable future experimental tests of flavor-dependent flow at LHC run 2 energies (5.02 TeV) and motivate more sensitive observables such as event engineering correlations (Prado 2016) to discriminate between energy loss models.
