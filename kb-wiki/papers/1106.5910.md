# Stability Peninsulas on the Neutron Drip Line

> Using HF+BCS method with Skyrme forces we analyze the neutron drip line. It is shown that around magic and new magic numbers the drip line may form stability peninsulas. It is shown the location of these peninsulas does not depend on the choice of Skyrme forces. It is found that the size of the peninsulas is sensitive to the choice of Skyrme forces and the most extended peninsulas appear with the SkI2 set.

## Key claim

The neutron drip line may form "stability peninsulas" around magic and new magic neutron numbers, where nuclei that become unstable against neutron emission can regain stability after adding more neutrons; the location of these peninsulas is independent of the choice of Skyrme force, but their size (how many additional proton numbers remain stable) is sensitive to the force, with the SkI2 set producing the most extended peninsulas.

## Method

The authors used the Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) method with deformed harmonic oscillator basis (DEF HF) and a spherical HF code (SPH HF) that includes localized quasibound continuum states confined under the centrifugal barrier. They applied four Skyrme force parameter sets (Ska, SkM*, SLy4, and SkI2) and defined the one-neutron drip line by the condition that the one-neutron separation energy (approximated via Koopmans theorem) equals zero. The pairing constant was set to G = (19.5/A)[1 ± 0.51(N-Z)/A], with plus for protons and minus for neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The nucleus ²³⁶Te at N=184 with SkI2 forces has Z=52 and a neutron-to-proton ratio N/Z=3.54
- ⁴⁰O has a neutron-to-proton ratio N/Z=4
- For ²⁴⁰Ba on the edge of the N=184 stability peninsula, the one-neutron separation energy is S_n = 0.024 MeV (DEF HF) and S_n = 0.064 MeV (SPH HF)
- The last filled one-particle level in ²⁴⁰Ba is 1j₁₅/₂, producing a centrifugal barrier height of 8.58 MeV
- In the Gd isotope chain, the first loss of stability against two-neutron emission occurs at A=222 (DEF HF), close to A=220 from HFB calculations
- ²⁴⁸Gd (N=184) is stable against both one- and two-neutron emission
- ²²⁴-²³⁰Gd isotopes are strongly deformed with β ≃ 0.45

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Tarasov et al., 2009-2011) on stability peninsulas in isotopes of O, Ar, Ni, Zr, Kr, Pb, Rn and other elements, and is consistent with the stability restoration by additional neutrons observed using the D1S Gogny force (reference 16.2 in the paper). The results indicate that the neutron drip line may have a more complicated structure than the simple monotonic boundary assumed in standard theoretical procedures, and the peninsulas at N=32, 58, 82, 126, and 184 contain spherical nuclei that may have long lifetimes due to high centrifugal barriers, enabling future experimental searches for these extremely neutron-rich isotopes.
