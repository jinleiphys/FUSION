# Electromagnetic field of ultrarelativistic charge in topologically random nuclear matter

> Electromagnetic field of a fast electric charge in nuclear matter with spatially uniform but random topological charge density is derived. A useful approximation is developed for the relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the electromagnetic field of an ultrarelativistic charge in nuclear matter where the topological charge density is a spatially uniform but random (stochastic) process, specifically for time intervals much longer than the sphaleron transition time \(\tau_c\), whereas prior work had only treated the case of a nearly constant topological charge density at early times \(t \ll \tau_c\).

## Method

The Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) equations are expanded in the helicity basis, yielding an ordinary differential equation for the magnetic field amplitude \(\Phi_{\lambda \mathbf{k}}(t)\). For late times \(t \gg \tau_c\), the chiral conductivity \(\sigma_\chi(t)\) is treated as a stationary stochastic process with zero mean and dispersion \(\Sigma_\chi\), and the equation is ensemble-averaged using the method of Van Kampen (1975) under the assumption \(\alpha \ll 1\). The resulting averaged field expressions are then simplified using the kinematics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where \(\sigma \ll k_\perp \ll k_z \ll \sigma\gamma\) and \(\gamma \gg 1\).

## Key numbers

*   Plasma conductivity at the critical temperature is \(\sigma^{-1} = 34\ \text{fm}\) (Aarts 2007, Ding 2010, Amato 2013).
*   The sphaleron transition time is taken as \(\tau_c = 2\ \text{fm}\) for the numerical example.
*   The magnetic field oscillates when the dispersion of the chiral conductivity \(\Sigma_\chi\) is sufficiently large compared to the electrical conductivity \(\sigma\); the numerical example uses \(\gamma = 100\), \(b = 7.4\ \text{fm}\), and \(z = 0\).
*   The anomalous-free solution yields the magnetic field \(B_\phi = \frac{q\sigma b}{8\pi\zeta^2} \exp\left(-\frac{b^2 \sigma}{4\zeta}\right) \theta(\zeta)\) in the limit \(Y \ll 1\) (poor conductor, high energy).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of the electromagnetic field in a chiral medium with constant topological charge density by Tuchin (2014) and Li (2016) to the regime where the topological charge density fluctuates randomly on timescales longer than the sphaleron transition time. It builds on the stochastic approach developed by Tuchin (2019) for the late-time behavior of the chiral instability. The results enable a more realistic assessment of the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) and related phenomena in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the challenge of observing these effects if the chiral conductivity is large.
