# New calculation schemes for proton-deuteron scattering including the Coulomb interaction

> The Coulomb interaction between the protons is included in the description of proton-deuteron scattering using the screening and renormalization approach in the framework of momentum-space integral equations. Two new calculational schemes are presented that confirm the reliability of the perturbative approach for treating the screened Coulomb interaction in high partial waves, used by us in earlier works.

# New calculation schemes for proton-deuteron scattering including the Coulomb interaction

## Key claim

The two new calculational schemes confirm the reliability of the perturbative approach for treating the screened Coulomb interaction in high partial waves used in earlier works (Deltuva 2005a, Deltuva 2005d), but only for the purely nucleonic charge-dependent Bonn potential and at energies where Delta-isobar effects are insignificant.

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space for proton-deuteron scattering, including the Coulomb interaction via a screened Coulomb potential with screening radius R and renormalization to recover the unscreened limit. Two alternative schemes are implemented: one using the isospin formalism with symmetrized AGS equations, and another treating protons and neutrons as nonidentical particles with properly antisymmetrized channel states; both solve the equations without any perturbative treatment of high partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Elastic pd scattering at 3 MeV proton lab energy: nucleon analyzing power A_y(N) and deuteron analyzing power T_21 are shown as functions of c.m. scattering angle; new scheme results are graphically indistinguishable from the perturbative approach.
- pd breakup at 130 MeV deuteron lab energy: differential cross section and deuteron analyzing power A_xx are shown for the kinematical configuration (15°, 15°, 160°) as functions of arclength S along the kinematical curve.
- The dividing line between partial waves included exactly and perturbatively is set at total angular momentum I_N = 4 to 6; screened Coulomb partial waves up to I = 12 or 14 must be included.
- Computing time increases only by a factor of 2 to 4 compared to previous perturbative calculations.

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach for including the Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon scattering, previously developed by Deltuva (2005a, 2005d) and based on the perturbative method of Deltuva (2003b). The new schemes directly demonstrate the reliability of that perturbative treatment without recourse to the approximation itself. The calculations use the purely nucleonic charge-dependent Bonn potential (Machleidt 2001) and could be extended to include Delta-isobar excitation as an effective three-nucleon force, though the authors refrain from doing so here as those effects are insignificant at the energies considered.
