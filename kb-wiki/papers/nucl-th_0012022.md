# Short-range correlations in nuclear matter using Green's functions within a discrete pole approximation

> We treat short-range correlations in nuclear matter, induced by the repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon potential, within the framework of a self-consistent Green's function theory. The effective in-medium interaction sums the ladder diagrams of both the particle-particle and hole-hole type. The demand of self-consistency results in a set of nonlinear equations which must be solved by iteration. We explore the possibility of approximating the single-particle Green's function by a limited number of poles and residues.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether the single-particle Green's function in nuclear matter can be approximated by a small number of discrete poles (rather than a continuous spectral function) while still capturing the effects of short-range correlations induced by the repulsive core of the nucleon-nucleon potential. The authors find that the BAGEL discretization scheme, while computationally tractable, produces a central pole that carries more than 97% of the strength (far more than the 65-75% typical of the quasi-particle pole) and is located about 20 MeV above the on-shell energy, indicating it does not faithfully represent the quasi-particle behavior.

## Method

The authors solve the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) equations for infinite nuclear matter at zero temperature, where the effective in-medium interaction sums ladder diagrams of both particle-particle and hole-hole type. The single-particle Green's function is approximated by a discrete set of poles and residues generated using the BAGEL scheme, which reproduces the lowest-order energy-weighted moments of the continuous spectral function. The coupled nonlinear equations for the self-energy and effective interaction are solved by iteration, using a modified Reid93 interaction (with the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) partial waves multiplied by 0.75 and 0.5 respectively) to avoid pairing instabilities.

## Key numbers

- The central BAGEL pole carries more than 97% of the strength for all momenta, compared to the quasi-particle pole which typically carries 65-75% for hole states (as cited from Muther and Polls).
- The central BAGEL pole is located about 20 MeV above the on-shell energy for all momenta.
- The BAGEL approximation yields a particle excess of 4% using the sum of discrete poles and an excess of 8% using the integrated hole spectral function.
- A quasi-particle calculation using the soft-core Reid interaction and a CBF calculation using the Urbana V\(_{14}\) potential both predict an occupation probability of \(n(0)=0.82\) (cited from Vonderfecht and Benhar respectively).
- The empirical saturation density is given as \(k_F = 1.36\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle scheme (used by Ramos (1990) and Vonderfecht (1993)) by incorporating off-shell propagation of nucleons through a discrete pole approximation, aiming to capture the deformation of the Fermi sea caused by short-range correlations. The approach contrasts with the parametrization method of Gearhart (1995) and Roth (1998) and the mixed approach of De Jong (1998). The results suggest that including the correlated Fermi sea reduces the depletion of hole states and may allow a normal (non-superfluid) ground state at saturation density, as proposed by Bozek (1999), but the BAGEL scheme itself is inadequate and an improved discretization scheme is needed.
