# Au+Au Collisions: the Suppression of High Transverse Momentum in Neutral pion Spectra

> Au+Au, $\sqrt{s} = 200$ A GeV measurements at RHIC, obtained with the PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS and BRAHMS detectors, have all indicated a suppression of production, relative to an appropriately normalized NN level. For central collisions and vanishing pseudo-rapidity these experiments indicate a considerable reduction, relative to that nominally expected from equivalent PP measurements, in charged and particle production, especially at mid- to large transverse momenta. In the PHENIX experiment similar behavior has been reported for $π^0$ spectra. In a recent work \cite{luc4brahms} on the presumably simpler D+Au interaction, to be considered perhaps as a tune-up for Au+Au, we reported on a hadronic cascade mechanism which can explain the observed moderatereduced $p_\perp$ suppression at higher pseudorapidity as well as the Cronin enhancement at mid- rapidity. Here we present the extension of this work to the more massive ion-ion collisions.

# Au+Au Collisions: the Suppression of High Transverse Momentum in Neutral pion Spectra

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high transverse momentum π⁰ production in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 A GeV can be explained by a hadronic cascade mechanism (stage II of the LUCIFER simulation) acting on prehadronic degrees of freedom, without requiring the formation of a quark-gluon plasma. The authors explicitly hedge that a pQCD-based explanation remains viable and more fundamental, but argue that the suppression may arise from final-state interactions in a prehadron-dominated medium rather than from partonic energy loss.

## Method

The authors used the LUCIFER Monte Carlo simulation, which divides the collision into two stages: stage I treats initial nucleon-nucleon interactions in parallel (not as a cascade) using a group structure based on spatial contiguity, assigning transverse momentum via a collision-driven random walk; stage II is a hadronic cascade where prehadrons (colorless q-qbar mesonic and baryonic objects with hadron-sized cross-sections) interact and decay, imposing energy-momentum conservation at the two-body level. The NN input spectra were fitted to UA1 and PHENIX pp data, and the same prehadronic interaction cross-sections (meson-meson scaled to 4/9 of NN cross-sections) were used without additional free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 A GeV; suppression observed relative to normalized NN level
- Effective production time for stage II collisions: 0.25–0.35 fm/c in the center-of-momentum frame
- Prehadron mass distribution centered at m₀ ≈ 800 MeV for non-strange mesons, m₀ ≈ 950 MeV for strange mesons, with width w = m₀/4
- Prehadron decay (hadronisation) time: τ_f ≈ 1 fm/c
- Ambient transverse energy densities at initialization of stage II: ≤ 1.8 GeV/fm³ for the shortest initial time τ_p
- Approximately 90% of all final mesonic yields occur for p⊥ ≤ 0.7 GeV/c
- Stage II collisions involve energies less than √s ∼ 15 GeV
- The ratio R_{AuAu/NN} shows a dip at p⊥ = 1.25 GeV/c in earlier PHENIX data, which later data (Shimamura) modified to a flatter shape
- Each prehadron in stage I decays into approximately 2.5 stable hadrons
- Some 5%–10% of the integrated meson spectrum comes from p⊥ ≥ 1 GeV in an average NN event

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadronic cascade analysis of D+Au collisions (Kahana and Kahana, 2005) to the heavier Au+Au system, where suppression is considerably greater. The LUCIFER approach is closest in spirit to RQMD and work by Gallmeister, Greiner, and Xu, as well as Cassing, and contrasts with purely or partly partonic models (Boal, Eskola, Wang, Geiger, Bass). The simulation reproduces the small (13%) increase in dN^{ch}/dη between √s = 130 and 200 A GeV seen in PHOBOS data, which was predicted by the authors. The results suggest that final-state cascading cannot be ignored and that the observed suppression may not be a definitive sign of quark-gluon plasma production, instead motivating further search for more definite signatures of QCD plasma from the earliest times in ion-ion collisions.
