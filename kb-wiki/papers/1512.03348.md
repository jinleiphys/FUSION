# Calculation of expectation values of operators in the Complex Scaling method

> The complex scaling method (CSM) provides with a way to obtain resonance parameters of particle unstable states by rotating the coordinates and momenta of the original Hamiltonian. It is convenient to use an L$^2$ integrable basis to resolve the complex rotated or complex scaled Hamiltonian H$_θ$, with $θ$ being the angle of rotation in the complex energy plane. Within the CSM, resonance and scattering solutions do not exhibit an outgoing or scattering wave asymptotic behavior, but rather have decaying asymptotics. One of the consequences is that, expectation values of operators in a resonance or scattering complex scaled solution are calculated by complex rotating the operators. In this work we are exploring applications of the CSM on calculations of expectation values of quantum mechanical operators by retrieving the Gamow asymptotic character of the decaying state and calculating hence the expectation value using the unrotated operator. The test cases involve a schematic two-body Gaussian model and also applications using realistic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Tikhonov regularization technique can stably retrieve the Gamow character of complex-scaled wavefunctions, enabling calculation of expectation values of operators using the unrotated operator rather than the complex-rotated one, provided the regularization parameter is chosen within a plateau region where results converge.

## Method

The author applies the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) to a two-body Hamiltonian with a Gaussian potential, expanding solutions in a Harmonic Oscillator basis and solving the resulting complex symmetric eigenvalue problem. The backrotation of CSM wavefunctions to recover Gamow states is regularized via the Tikhonov method, which involves mapping the wavefunction to a logarithmic coordinate, Fourier transforming, and applying a smoothing parameter kappa. Expectation values of the radius-squared operator and dipole strength functions are then computed using the regularized backrotated states with the bare (unrotated) operators and compared to benchmark CSM results using the complex-rotated operators.

## Key numbers

* For the 1₃⁻ resonant state of the Gaussian potential, the rms radius from the complex-scaled operator converges to (3.220, 1.393) fm (real and imaginary parts) for θ ≥ 0.4 rad with N=30 HO basis states.
* Using Tikhonov regularization with κ = 3×10⁻⁵ to 3×10⁻¹⁰, the rms radius from backrotated wavefunctions agrees with the CSM benchmark value (3.22008, 1.39351) fm to the fourth significant digit.
* The 1₃⁻ resonant state has complex energy E = 2.017 − i(0.972/2) MeV as calculated by integrating the Schrödinger equation with outgoing boundary conditions.
* For κ = 3×10⁻⁷ and κ = 3×10⁻⁸, the overlaps of regularized backrotated 1⁻ states are mostly zero but the matrix is not strictly unity, indicating the backrotated states are not orthogonal.

## Context

This work extends the Tikhonov regularization approach first applied to CSM backrotation by Kruppa and collaborators, building on the ABC theorem foundations of Aguilar, Balslev, and Combes (1971) and the complex scaling formalism of Moiseyev and Yaris. The method enables calculation of observables in resonant states without the divergence problems that plague direct Gamow state calculations, which typically require techniques like exterior complex scaling (Gyarmati, 1971) or Zel'dovich regularization. The approach is tested on a schematic Gaussian potential previously used by Gyarmati and others for CSM benchmark studies, and the author notes potential applications to realistic nuclear interactions and the possibility of generating a basis from backrotated CSM solutions, though the non-orthogonality of such a basis would require generalized diagonalization.
