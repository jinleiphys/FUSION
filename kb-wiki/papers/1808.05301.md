# Proton spectral functions in finite nuclei based on the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> We have calculated the proton spectral functions in finite nuclei based on the local density approximation where the properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter are calculated by the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method and the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, respectively. The scaled spectral function from our calculation is in good agreement with experimental results at small momenta while the difference between them becomes apparent at high momenta. Besides, a target dependence of the scaled proton spectral function is also obtained in our calculation as was observed in experiment. A further investigation indicates that the proportion of the high density region of the proton has a significant contribution to this target-dependent behavior since the spectral function in asymmetric nuclear matter increases significantly as a function of density.

## Key claim
The scaled proton spectral functions calculated via the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with local density approximation agree well with experimental (e,e'p) data at low momenta (around 330 MeV), but the agreement degrades at high momenta (above 490 MeV) because final-state interactions and Delta-resonance contributions are not included in the calculation.

## Method
The authors used the local density approximation (LDA) to compute proton spectral functions for finite nuclei (12C, 27Al, 56Fe, 197Au). The nuclear density and asymmetry profiles were obtained from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method with the LNS1 parameter set, while the nuclear-matter spectral functions were calculated from the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach using the Argonne V18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force. The proton spectral function for each finite nucleus was obtained by radially integrating the nuclear-matter hole spectral function weighted by the local density and asymmetry.

## Key numbers
- Proton spectral functions were calculated at four momenta: 330 MeV, 410 MeV, 490 MeV, and 570 MeV for four nuclei: 12C, 27Al, 56Fe, and 197Au.
- The proportion of the high-density region of the proton (chi) increases from 0.45 for 12C to 0.76 for 197Au; the scaled proportion (chi_C, relative to 12C) rises from 1.00 (12C) to 1.67 (197Au).
- The spectral function in asymmetric nuclear matter was shown at five densities: 0.169, 0.162, 0.135, 0.096, and 0.048 fm^-3 at a fixed asymmetry beta=0.038.
- The spectral function decreases with asymmetry at saturation density for three asymmetries: beta=0.035, 0.087, and 0.22.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous extended BHF calculations of spectral functions in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter (Wang 2013, Wang 2014) to finite nuclei via LDA. It directly addresses the target dependence of scaled proton spectral functions observed experimentally by Rohe (2003) for 12C, 27Al, 56Fe, and 197Au. The results contrast with the self-consistent T-matrix approach of Bozek (2004), which used the CD-Bonn interaction; the present AV18-based calculation yields larger spectral functions and attributes the target dependence primarily to the proportion of the high-density proton region rather than to the (N+Z)/Z factor emphasized by Bozek (2004). The work enables future inclusion of Delta-resonance and final-state interaction effects to improve agreement with experiment at high momenta and missing energies.
