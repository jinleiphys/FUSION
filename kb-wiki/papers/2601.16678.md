# Tribute to Tullio Bressani, Bogdan Povh and Toshimitsu Yamazaki

> In this HYP2025 talk I pay tribute to Tullio Bressani (1940-2024), Bogdan Povh (1932-2024) and Toshimitsu Yamazaki (1934-2025), all of whom made lasting contributions to shaping up Strangeness Nuclear Physics. Yoshinori Akaishi's (1941-2025) record is also noted.

# Tribute to Tullio Bressani, Bogdan Povh and Toshimitsu Yamazaki

## Key claim

This tribute talk at HYP2025 summarizes the lasting contributions of Bressani, Povh, and Yamazaki to shaping Strangeness Nuclear Physics, while noting that the early (K-,pi-) experiments by Bressani's group suffered from energy resolution of about 6 MeV that was insufficient to draw strong conclusions, and that Povh subsequently left hypernuclear physics for other topics after the realization that the Sigma-nuclear interaction is largely repulsive.

## Method

The talk reviews the historical development of hypernuclear spectroscopy from the early 1970s using in-flight (K-,pi-) production reactions at CERN by Bressani's and Povh's groups, followed by the (pi+,K+) production reaction at BNL in 1985 and at KEK from 1995 led by Yamazaki. The author then summarizes major physics contributions of each scientist, including Bressani's leadership of FINUDA at DAPhiNE, Povh's systematic observation of bound-state and continuum spectra from hypernuclei Be-9 to Ca-40, and Yamazaki's work on deeply bound pionic atoms and kaonic proton matter.

## Key numbers

- Lambda-hypernuclear ground state binding energies from Li-7 to Pb-208 suggest a Lambda-nucleus potential depth D_Lambda approximately -30 MeV
- Bressani's group used K- momentum 390 MeV/c with energy resolution of about 6 MeV; Povh's group used K- momentum 900 MeV/c and later 715 MeV/c reaching 2 MeV energy resolution
- For deeply bound pionic atoms, the extracted pion-nucleon sigma term is sigma_piN = 57 +/- 7 MeV from global fitting of pionic atom energy shifts and widths
- For K-pp, J-PARC E15 reported binding energy B_K-pp = 42 +/- 3 (+3/-4) MeV and width Gamma_K-pp = 100 +/- 7 (+19/-9) MeV
- For Lambda* nuclei, binding energy per particle B/A saturates at values well below 100 MeV for mass number A >= 120, with central density at roughly twice nuclear-matter density
- Two-body binding energy B(Lambda*Lambda*) approximately 40 MeV was deduced from B(K-K-pp) = 93 MeV

## Context

This tribute extends the historical record of hypernuclear physics from the early (K-,pi-) experiments at CERN by Bressani and Povh in the 1970s through Yamazaki's leadership at KEK and J-PARC. It connects to prior work by Friedman and Soff (1985) and Toki and Yamazaki (1988) on deeply bound pionic atoms, and to Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) on kaonic nuclei. The talk enables future work by documenting the experimental and theoretical foundations of strangeness nuclear physics, particularly the ongoing J-PARC program and the open question of whether Kaonic Proton Matter can exist as absolutely stable matter, which was questioned by the Jerusalem-Prague collaboration using Relativistic Mean Field calculations.
