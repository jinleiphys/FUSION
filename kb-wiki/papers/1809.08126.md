# Dense matter equation of state for neutron star mergers

> In simulations of binary neutron star mergers, the dense matter equation of state (EOS) is required over wide ranges of density and temperature as well as under conditions in which neutrinos are trapped, and the effects of magnetic fields and rotation prevail. Here we assess the status of dense matter theory and point out the successes and limitations of approaches currently in use. A comparative study of the excluded volume (EV) and virial approaches for the $npα$ system using the equation of state of Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall for interacting nucleons is presented in the sub-nuclear density regime. Owing to the excluded volume of the $α$-particles, their mass fraction vanishes in the EV approach below the baryon density 0.1 fm$^{-3}$, whereas it continues to rise due to the predominantly attractive interactions in the virial approach. The EV approach of Lattimer et al. is extended here to include clusters of light nuclei such as d, $^3$H and $^3$He in addition to $α$-particles. Results of the relevant state variables from this development are presented and enable comparisons with related but slightly different approaches in the literature. We also comment on some of the sweet and sour aspects of the supra-nuclear EOS. The extent to which the neutron star gravitational and baryon masses vary due to thermal effects, neutrino trapping, magnetic fields and rotation are summarized from earlier studies in which the effects from each of these sources were considered separately. Increases of about $20\% (\gtrsim 50\%)$ occur for rigid (differential) rotation with comparable increases occurring in the presence of magnetic fields only for fields in excess of $10^{18}$ Gauss. Comparatively smaller changes occur due to thermal effects and neutrino trapping. Some future studies to gain further insight into the outcome of dynamical simulations are suggested.

## Key claim

The paper assesses the status of dense matter theory for neutron star mergers, finding that the excluded volume (EV) approach for the npα system causes the α-particle mass fraction to vanish below a baryon density of 0.1 fm⁻³ due to repulsive excluded volume effects, whereas the virial approach shows it continuing to rise due to predominantly attractive interactions, a key qualitative difference that the abstract presents as a comparative finding rather than a resolved conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed a comparative study of the excluded volume (EV) and virial approaches for the npα system using the equation of state of Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (APR) for interacting nucleons in the sub-nuclear density regime. They extended the EV approach of Lattimer et al. to include clusters of light nuclei (d, ³H, and ³He) in addition to α-particles, and computed relevant state variables. The paper also reviews earlier studies on how neutron star gravitational and baryon masses vary due to thermal effects, neutrino trapping, magnetic fields, and rotation.

## Key numbers

- The α-particle mass fraction vanishes in the EV approach below baryon density 0.1 fm⁻³, whereas it continues to rise in the virial approach.
- For rigid rotation, increases of about 20% occur for neutron star masses; for differential rotation, increases of greater than 50% occur.
- Comparable mass increases occur in the presence of magnetic fields only for fields in excess of 10¹⁸ Gauss.
- The critical density for spinodal instability in symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) is n_sp = (2/3)n_s ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³, independent of the compression modulus K_S.
- For the APR EOS, the spinodal density n_sp ≈ 0.10 fm⁻³ for SNM, and n_sp(n,0.1) ≈ 0.05 fm⁻³ for proton fraction x = 0.1.
- The critical density n_c ≈ 0.06 fm⁻³ and critical temperature T_c ≈ 17.9 MeV for SNM using the APR EOS.
- The α-particle excluded volume v_α ≈ 24 fm³ (using the proton-α scattering length) or 19.9 fm³ (from Table 1); other excluded volumes: d = 40.5 fm³, ³H = 23.2 fm³, ³He = 32.0 fm³.
- Binding energies: d = 2.22 MeV, ³H = 8.48 MeV, ³He = 7.72 MeV, α = 28.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the excluded volume approach of Lattimer et al. (1991) and Lattimer et al. (1985) by including additional light nuclear clusters (d, ³H, ³He) beyond α-particles, enabling comparisons with the virial approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006). The paper critically assesses the sweet and sour aspects of current EOS approaches for neutron star mergers, building on the APR EOS (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) and earlier studies of thermal effects, neutrino trapping, magnetic fields, and rotation. The findings enable future dynamical simulations of binary neutron star mergers to incorporate more complete treatments of light clusters and to understand the limitations of both EV and virial methods in the sub-nuclear density regime.
