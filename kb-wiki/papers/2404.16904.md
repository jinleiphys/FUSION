# Charged pion vortices in rotating systems

> Possibilities for formation of the charged pion field vortices in a rotating empty vessel (in vacuum) and in the rotating pion gas with a dynamically fixed particle number at zero temperature are studied within the $λ|ϕ|^4$ model. It is shown that in the former case at a rapid rotation a supervortex of a charged pion field can be formed. Important role played by the electric field is demonstrated. Field configurations in presence and absence of the pion self-interaction are found. Conditions for formation of the vortex lattice at the rotation of the charged pion gas at zero temperature are studied. Observational effects are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charged pion field vortices can form in a rapidly rotating empty vessel (vacuum) and in a rotating pion gas with dynamically fixed particle number at zero temperature within the λ|φ|^4 model, but only under specific conditions: the vacuum case requires an external electric potential V0 to reach zero energy levels, while the pion gas case requires rotation frequencies exceeding a much lower critical threshold.

## Method

The author studies the Klein-Gordon equation for a charged scalar field in a rigidly rotating cylindrical coordinate system, incorporating the λ|φ|^4 self-interaction term and an external electric potential. Solutions are sought in the form of individual vortices with winding number ν, using Bessel functions for the non-interacting case (λ=0) and asymptotic solutions for the self-interacting case (λ≠0). The energy balance is evaluated both in the rotating frame and the laboratory frame, considering two scenarios: fixed rotation frequency (case i) and fixed angular momentum (case ii).

## Key numbers

- Rotation frequency observed in heavy-ion collisions: Ω ≃ (9 ± 1) × 10^21 Hz ≃ 0.05 m_π, where m_π ≃ 140 MeV is the pion mass
- For the ideal pion gas with fixed particle number, the critical rotation frequency for vortex formation with ν=1 is Ω_{c1}^{id}(1) ≃ (j^2_{1,1} - j^2_{1,0})/(2 R^2 m_π), with j_{1,0} ≃ 2.403 and j_{1,1} ≃ 3.832
- For the self-interacting pion gas, the critical rotation frequency for the first vortex filament is Ω_{c1}^λ(ν) = ν ln(R/r_0)/(R^2 \bar{μ})
- For the rotating vacuum with self-interaction, the critical rotation frequency is Ω_c^π = (m_π - V_0)/ν, requiring Ων + V_0 > m_π for vortex formation
- For the rotating vacuum without self-interaction and V_0 ≠ 0, the critical frequency is Ω_c = (√(m^2 + j_{1,ν}^2/R^2) - V_0)/ν

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion condensation in rotating systems, particularly the work of Guo (2020) which suggested a giant pion supervortex within the λ|φ|^4 model but did not allow the H→0 limit and disregarded electric field effects. It also builds on the earlier work of Voskresensky (2023) which studied pion-σ supervortices in rotating nuclear systems using the σ model. The paper enables future investigation of vortex lattice formation in heavy-ion collision fireballs, where the ALICE Collaboration observed suppression of Bose-Einstein correlations suggesting coherent pion emission, with about 5% of pions possibly stemming from a Bose-Einstein condensate as indicated by Begun (2015).
