# Off-Shell Behavior of Nucleon Self-Energy in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The off-shell behavior of the nucleon self-energy in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is investigated within the framework of relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach based on projection techniques. The dependence of the Dirac components of the self-energy on momentum as well as energy is evaluated for symmetric as well as asymmetric nuclear matter. Special attention is paid to the various contributions to the momentum dependence of the real and imaginary part of the optical potential. The consequences to the different definitions of the effective nucleon mass and particle spectral functions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the off-shell behavior of the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter can be characterized within the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, but the energy and momentum dependence of the self-energy components is weak enough that the "reference spectrum approximation" used in the iteration procedure remains valid, although small effects become magnified when solving the Dirac equation due to large cancellations between scalar and vector components.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation using the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential, employing the subtracted T-matrix representation scheme to minimize ambiguities in the covariant representation. The self-energy components (scalar, time-like vector, and space-like vector) are computed as functions of both momentum and energy for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at a fixed density of n_B = 0.181 fm^{-3}, with an asymmetry parameter beta = (n_n - n_p)/n_B = 0.5 for the asymmetric case. The coupled set of equations for the T-matrix and self-energy is iterated to self-consistency, and the resulting self-energy components are used to calculate optical potentials, spectral functions, single-particle energies, and effective masses.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density used in calculations: n_B = 0.181 fm^{-3} for both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter
- Asymmetry parameter for asymmetric matter: beta = (n_n - n_p)/n_B = 0.5
- Fermi energy in symmetric nuclear matter at this density: -26.5 MeV
- Neutron occupation numbers in asymmetric matter range from 0.95 (at k approx 0.5 k_Fn) to 0.87 (at k approx k_Fn)
- Proton occupation numbers in asymmetric matter range from 0.87 (at k approx 0.5 k_Fp) to 0.8 (at k approx k_Fp)

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic DBHF studies of asymmetric nuclear matter by van Dalen and Muther (2004, 2007) and others (Ulrych 1997, de Jong 1998, Alonso 2003) that were restricted to on-shell behavior, incorporating the off-shell dependence that had previously been studied only in non-relativistic frameworks by Zuo (1999) and Hassaneen (2004). The results enable more accurate predictions for nucleon-nucleus scattering and reactions at radioactive beam facilities such as FAIR and SPIRAL2, and provide a basis for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic treatments of the effective mass decomposition into k-mass and E-mass components.
