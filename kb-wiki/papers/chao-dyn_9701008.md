# Scars of Invariant Manifolds in Interacting Chaotic Few-Body Systems

> We present a novel extension of the concept of scars for the wave functions of classically chaotic few-body systems of identical particles with rotation and permutation symmetry. Generically there exist manifolds in classical phase space which are invariant under the action of a common subgroup of these two symmetries. Such manifolds are associated with highly symmetric configurations. If sufficiently stable, the quantum motion on such manifolds displays a notable enhancement of the revival in the autocorrelation function which is not directly associated with individual periodic orbits. Rather, it indicates some degree of localization around an invariant manifold which has collective characteristics that should be experimentally observable.

## Key claim

The paper extends the concept of quantum scars to interacting few-body systems of identical particles, showing that wave functions can localize around invariant manifolds in classical phase space that are stabilized by the combination of rotational and permutation symmetries, rather than around individual periodic orbits. The effect is conditional on the manifold having sufficiently small transverse stability exponents; a collinear manifold with large transverse Lyapunov exponents shows no such scarring.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian for four identical particles in two dimensions with rotational and permutation symmetry, and identified a low-dimensional invariant manifold corresponding to a rectangular configuration. They computed the full phase-space monodromy matrices for all periodic orbits up to code length three (12 orbits) within the manifold, extracting stability exponents both parallel and perpendicular to the manifold. Quantum dynamics were studied by propagating Gaussian wave packets using Heller's cellular dynamics within the manifold and linearized dynamics in the transverse directions, computing the autocorrelation function and its Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian uses units where m = alpha = 1, hbar = 0.01; coordinates are in units of hbar^(1/3) alpha^(-1/6) m^(-1/6) and momenta in units of hbar^(2/3) alpha^(1/6) m^(1/6).
* The initial wave packet energy was centered around E = 1, corresponding to approximately the 10^10th energy level.
* The Fourier transform of the autocorrelation function shows an equidistant structure with spacing Delta E = 0.034, corresponding to the period of the orbit on which the wave packet was launched.
* For the periodic orbit with code "2", the period T = 2.622, the stability exponent inside the manifold u_parallel = 4.86, and the sum of real positive transverse stability exponents is 3.29 (n = 2 transverse directions).
* For the periodic orbit with code "122", the period T = 13.259, u_parallel = 23.03, and the sum of transverse exponents is 21.41 (n = 5 transverse directions).
* The energy of all tabulated periodic orbits is E = 4.

## Context

This work extends the concept of scars introduced by Heller (1984) and Bogomolny (1988) from individual periodic orbits to invariant manifolds in systems with identical particles, building on the authors' prior analysis of periodic-orbit structure in symmetric chaotic systems (Papenbrock and Seligman, 1996). The results are similar to scars found by Prosen (1997) in billiards and are expected to be relevant for finite many-body systems such as atoms, molecules, and atomic nuclei, where the collinear manifold in Helium (Wintgen, Richter, and Tanner, 1992) already supports doubly excited states. The work enables the study of collective motion in chaotic few-body systems through a generalized scarring mechanism that should be experimentally observable.
