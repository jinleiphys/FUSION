# Unique first-forbidden $β$-decay transitions in odd-odd and even-even heavy nuclei

> The allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions are the most common weak nuclear processes of spin-isospin $(στ)$ type. These transitions play a key role in numerous processes in the domain of nuclear physics. Equally important is their contribution in astrophysics, particularly in nuclear synthesis and supernova-explosions. In situations where allowed GT transitions are not favored, first-forbidden transitions become significant, specifically in medium heavy and heavy nuclei. For neutron-rich nuclei, first-forbidden transitions are favored mainly due to the phase-space amplification for these transitions. In this work we calculate the allowed GT as well as unique first-forbidden (U1F) $|Δ$J$|$ = 2 transitions strength in odd-odd and even-even nuclei in mass range $70\leq A \leq214$. Two different pn-QRPA models were used with a schematic separable interaction to calculate GT and U1F transitions. The inclusion of U1F strength improved the overall comparison of calculated terrestrial $β$-decay half-lives in both models. The \textit{ft} values and reduced transition probabilities for the $2^-\longleftrightarrow 0^+$ transitions were also calculated. We compared our calculations with the previously reported correlated RPA calculation and experimental results. Our calculations are in better agreement with measured data. For stellar applications we further calculated the allowed GT and U1F weak rates. These include $β^{\pm}$-decay rates and electron/positron capture rates of heavy nuclei in stellar matter. Our study shows that positron and electron capture rates command the total weak rates of these heavy nuclei at high stellar temperatures.

## Key claim

The authors calculate allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and unique first-forbidden (U1F) $|\Delta J| = 2$ transition strengths for 26 odd-odd and even-even nuclei in the mass range $70 \leq A \leq 214$ using two different pn-QRPA models, finding that including U1F strength improves the comparison of calculated terrestrial beta-decay half-lives with experimental data, and that at high stellar temperatures positron and electron capture rates dominate the total weak rates.

## Method

Two proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) models were employed: the pn-QRPA(WS) model used a Woods-Saxon potential with Chepurnov parametrization and considered only spherical nuclei, while the pn-QRPA(N) model used a deformed Nilsson potential and included both particle-hole and particle-particle channels. Both models used schematic separable interactions, with a quenching factor of 0.7 applied to all charge-changing transitions. The pn-QRPA(N) model was further used to calculate stellar weak rates including beta-decay, beta-plus decay, electron capture, and positron capture rates over a stellar density range of 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3 and temperature range of 0.01 to 30 in units of 10^9 K.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviations for reduced matrix elements: 0.1876 for pn-QRPA(WS) and 0.0966 for pn-QRPA(N) models, compared to experimental data
- Strength parameter for U1F effective interaction in pn-QRPA(WS) model: $\chi_{U1F} = 350 A^{-5/3}$ MeV fm$^{-2}$
- Pairing correlation constants in pn-QRPA(WS): $C_n = C_p = 12/\sqrt{A}$ for open shell nuclei
- Quenching factor: $(g_A/g_V)^2_{eff} = 0.7 (g_A/g_V)^2_{bare}$ with $(g_A/g_V)^2_{bare} = -1.254$
- Particle-hole interaction constant in pn-QRPA(N): $\chi = 4.2/A$ MeV for allowed transitions and $56.16/A$ MeV fm$^{-2}$ for U1F transitions
- Particle-particle interaction constant in pn-QRPA(N): $\kappa = 0.01$
- Main contributions to U1F strength situated at energies of order 21-25 MeV, corresponding to the giant first-forbidden resonance (FFR) with $I^\pi = 2^-$
- Stellar weak rates calculated at densities 10, 10^3, 10^7, and 10^11 g/cm$^3$ and temperatures 1.5, 5, 10, and 30 GK (T$_9$ units)

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of allowed GT transitions by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999, 2004) and U1F stellar rates by Nabi and Stoica (2014), and improves upon the correlated RPA calculations of Civitarese et al. (1986) which assumed proportionality between relativistic and non-relativistic beta-moments. The authors compare their results with experimental data from Lederer and Shirley (1978) and with the earlier RPA calculations, finding better agreement. The calculated U1F transition strengths and stellar rates are intended for use in supernova simulations and r-process nucleosynthesis studies, with the authors noting that non-unique forbidden transitions remain to be studied in future work.
