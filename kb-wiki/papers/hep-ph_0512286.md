# The spin dependent structure functions of the nucleon

> We calculate the spin dependent structure functions g_{1}(x) and g_{2}(x) of the proton and neutron. Our calculation uses the meson cloud model of nucleon structure, which has previously given a good description of the HERMES data on polarized sea quark distributions, and includes all the leading contributions to spin dependent effects in this model. We find good agreement between our calculations and the current experimental data for the structure functions. We include in our calculations kinematic terms, which mix transverse and longitudinal spin components, for hadrons of spin 1/2, 1 and 3/2, and which can give considerable contributions to the g_{2} structure functions. We also consider the possible interference terms between baryons or mesons in different final states with the same quantum numbers, and show that most of these terms do not give leading contributions to the spin dependent structure functions.

## Key claim
The meson cloud model (MCM) provides a good description of the spin dependent structure functions g1(x) and g2(x) for both proton and neutron, but the authors show that most interference terms between different baryon or meson states in the cloud do not give leading contributions to these structure functions, contrary to some earlier claims in the literature.

## Method
The authors calculate the spin dependent structure functions g1(x) and g2(x) using the meson cloud model, where the physical nucleon is treated as a superposition of a bare nucleon plus baryon-meson Fock states resulting from fluctuations N → M B. They include kinematic terms that mix transverse and longitudinal spin components for hadrons of spin 1/2, 1, and 3/2, and evaluate contributions from spin 1/2 baryons, spin 1 mesons, and spin 3/2 baryons in the cloud using time ordered perturbation theory. The structure functions of the cloud hadrons themselves are calculated using the MIT bag model.

## Key numbers
- The Bjorken sum rule has been tested at the five per cent level by measurements on deuteron and neutron targets.
- The maximum transverse momentum squared for baryon contributions is given by (k⊥²)_max = (m_N²/γ²)(1 + √(1+γ²))(1 - 2y + √(1+γ²)) - m_B², which at small x becomes Q²(1-y)/x² - m_B² ≫ m_N².
- For spin 3/2 baryons, the parameter ω² = 1 + (s·q)²/(λ²ν²) goes to 2 in the Bjorken limit.
- The spin 3/2 structure functions are expressed as g_i^(3/2,3/2) = (3/(2√5))^(3/2)_1g_i + (1/(2√5))^(3/2)_3g_i and g_i^(3/2,1/2) = (1/(2√5))^(3/2)_1g_i - (3/(2√5))^(3/2)_3g_i.

## Context
This work extends earlier MCM calculations of g1(x) by Holtmann et al. and Boros and Thomas, and incorporates vector mesons (particularly the ρ) which were shown by Kumano and Miyama (1997) to play a role in spin structure. The model had previously given good agreement with HERMES data on polarized sea quark distributions (Cao and Signal, 2003) and with the observed violation of the Gottfried sum rule. The authors find that interference contributions between π and ρ mesons, K and K* mesons, and N and Δ baryons are mostly zero or very small, contradicting earlier claims by Szczurek and Trawinski (1997), Boros and Thomas, and others who had calculated these terms without consistent phase conventions.
