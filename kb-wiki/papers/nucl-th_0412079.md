# A microscopic investigation of the transition form factor in the region of collective multipole excitations of stable and unstable nuclei

> We have used a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus Continuum-RPA model to study the low-multipole response of stable and neutron/proton-rich Ni and Sn isotopes. We focus on the momentum-transfer dependence of the strength distribution, as it provides information on the structure of excited nuclear states and in particular on the variations of the transition form factor (TFF) with the energy. Our results show, among other things, that the TFF may show significant energy dependence in the region of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance and that the TFF corresponding to the threshold strength in the case of neutron-rich nuclei is different compared to the one corresponding to the respective giant resonance. Perspectives are given for more detailed future investigations.

## Key claim

The transition form factor (TFF) can show significant energy dependence in the region of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance, and the TFF corresponding to threshold strength in neutron-rich nuclei is different from that of the respective giant resonance; these variations are demonstrated through self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus Continuum-RPA calculations for selected Ni and Sn isotopes.

## Method

The authors employed a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) plus Continuum-RPA (CRPA) model in coordinate space to calculate the isoscalar and isovector monopole, dipole, and quadrupole response of spherical, closed-shell Ni and Sn isotopes. The strength distribution S(E,q) was computed for external fields of the form j_L(qr) at momentum transfers q = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm^{-1}, using the Skyrme parametrization SkM* and, for qualitative comparison, MSk7. The radial part of the transition density was extracted to examine variations with excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers used: q = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm^{-1} for all six isotopes studied (56,78,110Ni and 100,120,132Sn)
- For 56Ni, the ISM strength distribution shows two distinct energy regions separated at approximately 23 MeV (region P_ below and P_ above this value)
- The node of the IS GMR transition density in 56Ni occurs at radius R ≈ 1.2 A^{1/3} = 4.6 fm (or 4.3 fm using SHF result), giving q_{01} = π/R = 0.68 (or 0.73) fm^{-1}
- The IV GMR in neutron-rich nuclei lies above 25 MeV, while the IS GMR lies at lower energy
- The IV GQR lies above 20 MeV
- 132Sn has a half-life of 39.7 s and no excited states detected below 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus Continuum-RPA approach previously used by Hamamoto and collaborators (Hamamoto et al., 1996-1999) for systematic studies of nuclei far from stability, and by the authors' own group (Papakonstantinou et al., 2004; Papakonstantinou et al., 2004). The results are relevant to the analysis of inelastic electron and hadron scattering experiments, particularly for determining the energy-weighted sum rule and centroid energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in light and medium-heavy nuclei. The findings also provide theoretical guidance for future experimental studies of unstable nuclei at radioactive ion beam facilities, and the threshold strength behavior in neutron-rich nuclei is consistent with earlier predictions for 60Ca (Hamamoto et al., 1997) and 28O (Hamamoto and Sagawa, 1996).
