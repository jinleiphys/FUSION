# RPA-Approach to the Excitations of the Nucleon, Part II: Phenomenology

> The tensor-RPA approach developed previously in part I is applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. As a first step we investigate the structure of Dirac-Hartree-Fock solutions for a rotationally and isospin invariant ground-state density. Whereas vacuum properties can be reproduced, no solitonic configuration for a system with unit baryon number is found. We then solve the tensor-RPA equation employing simple models of the nucleon ground state. In general the ph interaction effects a decrease of the excited states to lower energies. Due to an enhanced level density at low energies the obtained spectra cannot be matched with the experimental data when a standard MIT-bag configuration is used. However, when the size of the nucleon quark core is reduced to approximately 0.3 fm a fair description of the baryon spectrum in the positive-parity channel is achieved. For this purpose the residual interaction turns out to be crucial and leads to a significant improvement compared with the mean-field spectra.

## Key claim

The tensor-RPA approach, when applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a rotationally and isospin invariant ground-state density, fails to produce a bound solitonic configuration for baryon number B=1; however, by using a phenomenological MIT-bag ground state with a reduced quark-core size of approximately 0.3 fm and including the residual interaction, a fair description of the positive-parity baryon spectrum is achieved.

## Method

The authors first solved the Dirac-Hartree-Fock equations for the NJL model in a basis of MIT-bag states, using both Woods-Saxon and proper-time regularization schemes, to investigate vacuum and B=1 configurations. They then applied the tensor-RPA formalism developed in Part I, constructing particle-hole (ph) configurations on the nucleon ground state with good angular momentum (J) and isospin (T) quantum numbers, using phenomenological bag-model wavefunctions together with a residual NJL-type interaction.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate from the gap equation was reproduced with high accuracy: 1.39 x 10^7 MeV^3 (Woods-Saxon cut-off) and 1.33 x 10^7 MeV^3 (proper-time cut-off).
- The masses of the B=1 configurations (with G_v=0) were 3769 MeV, 4586 MeV, and 5440 MeV for constituent-quark masses of 400 MeV, 600 MeV, and 800 MeV, respectively.
- The total energy of the B=1 configuration (m=600 MeV) converged to 4586 MeV for a cavity radius R_c=8 fm, compared to 4587 MeV at R_c=10 fm.
- The ratio of the pionic to scalar-isoscalar interaction matrix elements in the nucleon ground state was approximately 2.4.
- A fair description of the baryon spectrum in the positive-parity channel was achieved when the size of the nucleon quark core was reduced to approximately 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the tensor-RPA formalism developed in Part I (Hardt et al., 1996) to the NJL model, contrasting with the NJL soliton model approaches of Alkofer et al. (1995) and Christov et al. (1995) which use hedgehog configurations and perturbative cranking for J/T projection. The authors find that a rotationally and isospin invariant mean-field configuration cannot produce solitonic solutions, unlike the hedgehog ansatz which allows a nonvanishing pion field. The results enable future application of the tensor-RPA method to models with dynamical confinement, such as the chromodielectric model of Fiolhais et al. (1988), and suggest that the residual interaction is crucial for improving upon mean-field spectra.
