# Multi-neutron emission of Cd isotopes

> An influence of the phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) on the $β$-decay half-lives and multi-neutron emission probabilities is analysed within the microscopic model based on the Skyrme interaction with tensor components included. The finite-rank separable approximation is used in order to handle large two-quasiparticle spaces. The even-even nuclei near the r-process pathes at $N=82$ are studied. The characteristics of ground states, $2^+$ excitations and $β$-decay strength of the neutron-rich Cd isotopes are treated in detail. It is shown that a strong redistribution of the Gamow-Teller strength due to the PPC is mostly sensitive to the multi-neutron emission probability of the Cd isotopes.

## Key claim

The phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) redistributes the Gamow-Teller strength in neutron-rich Cd isotopes, and this redistribution is "mostly sensitive" to the multi-neutron emission probability, particularly the $P_{2n}/P_{1n}$ ratio, rather than to the half-lives alone. The abstract's claim of "strong redistribution" is hedged by the paper's finding that the effect is most pronounced for the multi-neutron emission probabilities, while half-life changes are more modest.

## Method

The authors use the finite-rank separable approximation (FRSA) to the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with the Skyrme energy density functional T43 (including tensor terms) and a surface-peaked zero-range pairing force. They construct wave functions for $1^+$ states in the daughter nuclei as a mixture of one-phonon QRPA configurations and two-phonon $[1^+_i \otimes 2^+_{i'}]_{QRPA}$ configurations, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in this space. The $\beta$-decay half-lives and multi-neutron emission probabilities $P_{xn}$ are then calculated from the resulting Gamow-Teller strength distribution within the $Q_\beta$ window, using the statistical model for neutron emission.

## Key numbers

*   For $^{130}$Cd, the QRPA main GT peak is shifted by +120 keV when PPC is included, and its amplitude increases by 5%.
*   The calculated half-life of $^{130}$Cd is 121 ms with PPC, compared to the experimental value of $127 \pm 2$ ms (L15).
*   For $^{132}$Cd, the calculated $P_{2n}$ is 25.6% and $P_{1n}$ is 74.4% with PPC, yielding a $P_{2n}/P_{1n}$ ratio of 0.34.
*   The QRPA $P_{2n}/P_{1n}$ ratio for $^{132}$Cd is 0.22; inclusion of PPC increases this ratio by 55% to 0.34.
*   For $^{130}$Cd, the calculated total neutron-emission probability $P_{tot}$ is 13.7%, compared to the experimental value of $3.5 \pm 1.0$% (D03).
*   The calculated half-lives for the Cd isotopes range from 263 ms ($^{126}$Cd) to 32 ms ($^{134}$Cd) with PPC; experimental values range from $513 \pm 6$ ms to $65 \pm 15$ ms (L15).

## Context

This work extends the FRSA+QRPA approach previously developed by the same group (Severyukhin et al., 2014) to include phonon-phonon coupling for the study of $\beta$-delayed multi-neutron emission, a process important for $r$-process nucleosynthesis. It contrasts with earlier one-phonon approximations used in the DF3+cQRPA approach (Borzov, 2008) and the finite-amplitude method (Mustonen and Engel, 2016), finding that the two-phonon configurations significantly increase the $P_{2n}/P_{1n}$ ratio for $^{132}$Cd compared to those predictions. The results enable future microscopic calculations of $\beta$-delayed neutron emission rates for $r$-process modeling, and the authors note that extension to include first-forbidden transitions and higher-multipolarity phonons is underway.
