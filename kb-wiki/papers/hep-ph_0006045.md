# S-wave Kpi scattering in chiral perturbation theory with resonances

> We present a detailed analysis of S-wave Kpi scattering up to 2 GeV, making use of the resonance chiral Lagrangian predictions together with a suitable unitarisation method. Our approach incorporates known theoretical constraints at low and high energies. The present experimental status, with partly conflicting data from different experiments, is discussed. Our analysis allows to resolve some experimental ambiguities, but better data are needed in order to determine the cross-section in the higher-energy range. Our best fits are used to determine the masses and widths of the relevant scalar resonances in this energy region.

## Key claim

The authors present a detailed analysis of S-wave Kpi scattering up to 2 GeV using resonance chiral Lagrangian predictions combined with a unitarisation method, but they note that the experimental status is unsatisfactory with partly conflicting data, and that better data are needed to determine the cross-section in the higher-energy range; the analysis allows to resolve some experimental ambiguities but cannot fully resolve the scalar sector due to large unitarity corrections that tend to mask resonance behaviour.

## Method

The authors constructed the Kpi scattering amplitudes at one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory including explicit scalar and vector resonance fields, then unitarised the partial-wave amplitudes using an N/D-based method that matches the chiral expansion up to O(p^4) and satisfies elastic unitarity. For the elastic region below 1.3 GeV, they fitted the I=1/2 and I=3/2 phase shifts and amplitudes to data from Estabrooks et al. (1978), Aston et al. (1988), and older experiments, and above 1.3 GeV they extended the analysis to a coupled-channel treatment including the Keta and Keta' channels.

## Key numbers

- The best fit with free c_d and c_m yields M_{K*_0} = 1.26 GeV, c_d = 24.8 MeV, c_m = 76.7 MeV, with chi^2 = 201.1/115 d.o.f. = 1.75.
- The fit imposing c_m = c_d gives M_{K*_0} = 1.19 GeV, c_d = c_m = 45.4 MeV, with chi^2 = 210.6/116 d.o.f. = 1.82.
- The fit fixing c_d and c_m to central estimates (|c_d| = 30 +/- 10 MeV, |c_m| = 43 +/- 14 MeV) gives M_{K*_0} = 1.29 GeV, with chi^2 = 223.5/117 d.o.f. = 1.91.
- Using a K-matrix ansatz for I=1/2 with a background parametrisation for I=3/2, the fit yields M_R = 1.32 GeV, g_R = 0.628, with chi^2 = 86.9/116 d.o.f. = 0.75.
- The I=1/2 scattering length from the chiral fit is alpha_0^{1/2} = 0.18 M_pi^{-1}, while the K-matrix fit gives 0.31 M_pi^{-1}; the I=3/2 scattering lengths are -0.05 M_pi^{-1} (chiral) and -0.12 M_pi^{-1} (background).
- The large-N_c estimate G_V = f_pi/sqrt(2) = 65.3 MeV was used, consistent with K* decay width giving G_V = 65 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) for Kpi scattering by incorporating explicit resonance fields and a unitarisation method based on the N/D approach developed by Oller and Oset (1999) and Oller, Oset, and Ramos (2000). It addresses the unsatisfactory experimental situation in the scalar sector where masses and widths of scalar resonances are not clearly established due to large unitarity corrections, and it enables future determination of the strange quark mass from QCD sum rules via the Kpi scalar form factor, which the authors plan to discuss in a subsequent publication.
