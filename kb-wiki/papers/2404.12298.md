# Dirac Spectral Density in N$_f$=2+1 QCD at T=230 MeV

> We compute the renormalized Dirac spectral density in $N_f = 2+1$ QCD at physical quark masses, temperature $T = 230$ MeV and system size $L_s = 3.4$ fm. To that end, we perform a point-wise continuum limit of the staggered density in lattice QCD with staggered quarks. We find, for the first time, that a clear infrared structure (IR peak) emerges in the density of Dirac operator describing dynamical quarks. We also provide numerical evidence that a component of this peak, which becomes dominant in the thermodynamic limit, is due to a non-trivial accumulation of near-zero modes. Features of this structure are consistent with those previously attributed to the recently-proposed IR phase of thermal QCD. Our results (i) provide the only complete first-principles evidence that these IR features exist and are physical; (ii) improve the upper bound for IR-phase transition temperature $T_{\mathrm{IR}}$ so that the new window is $200 < T_{\mathrm{IR}} < 230\,$MeV; (iii) are consistent with non-restoration of anomalous U$_{\mathrm A}$(1) symmetry (chiral limit) below $T = 230$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors provide the first complete first-principles evidence that an infrared (IR) peak structure exists in the spectral density of the dynamical staggered Dirac operator describing physical quarks in QCD, after performing a point-wise continuum limit at T = 230 MeV. The IR peak survives the removal of would-be zero modes, indicating a non-trivial accumulation of near-zero modes, and the results are consistent with the system being in the proposed IR phase with inverse power-law accumulation of IR modes (p < 0).

## Method

The authors compute the renormalized Dirac spectral density in N_f = 2+1 lattice QCD with physical quark masses using the staggered Dirac operator with stout smearing and tree-level Symanzik-improved glue action, performing a point-wise continuum extrapolation (linear in a^2) across lattice spacings from 0.1073 fm down to 0.0536 fm at fixed spatial size L_s = 3.43 fm and temperature T = 230 MeV. They obtain 150 lowest positive eigenvalues per configuration using the PARPACK library, renormalize the spectral density using the strange quark mass with its FLAG value m_s^MS-bar(2 GeV) = 92.2(1.0) MeV, and compare results before and after removing would-be zero modes identified via the prescription of Borsanyi et al. (2016) using gluonic topological charge determinations from the same ensembles.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 230 MeV, approximately 1.48 T_c where T_c ≈ 155 MeV
- System spatial size L_s = 3.43 fm; IR cutoff 1/L_s ≈ 57 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.1073 fm (N_t = 8), 0.0857 fm (N_t = 10), 0.0715 fm (N_t = 12), 0.0613 fm (N_t = 14), 0.0536 fm (N_t = 16)
- Renormalized strange quark mass m_s^MS-bar(2 GeV) = 92.2(1.0) MeV used for renormalization
- Anderson-like mobility edge λ_A ≈ 166(8) MeV from the same ensembles (Bonanno et al., 2023)
- Power-law exponent near peak top: p ≈ -1.3(2) at a = 0.0536 fm
- New window for IR-phase transition temperature: 200 < T_IR < 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the line of research on the IR phase of thermal QCD initiated by Alexandru et al. (2015, 2019), who proposed that high-temperature QCD exhibits an IR-symmetric phase with inverse power-law accumulation of Dirac modes (p < 0) based on overlap operator studies. It directly addresses doubts raised by Aoki et al. (2020) about the existence of the IR peak in dynamical-quark spectral densities and the suggestion by Ding et al. (2020) that the peak might only be observable in derivatives of ρ(λ). The results enable future studies of the IR phase transition temperature and provide the necessary condition for non-restoration of anomalous U_A(1) symmetry in the chiral limit below T = 230 MeV.
