# $^{14}$N(p,$γ)^{15}$O $S$ factor and the puzzling solar composition problem

> In stellar hydrogen burning, the CNO cycle dominates, with the $^{14}$N(p,$γ)^{15}$O reaction being the slowest process. Consequently, this reaction critically influences the solar composition, CNO neutrino fluxes, and the evolution of star clusters and galaxies. Recent direct measurements of $^{14}$N(p,$γ)^{15}$O have reported an enhanced astrophysical $S$-factor. This work presents a microscopic theoretical study of the $^{14}$N(p,$γ)^{15}$O reaction using the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC). The calculations achieve good agreement with experimental data for both the total $S$-factors and the separate contributions from transitions to the ground state and excited states of $^{15}\mathrm{O}$. However, the predicted $S$-factor at zero energy exceeds the experimental value. Based on the computed $S$-factors, the derived carbon and nitrogen abundances align closely with predictions from recent $^{14}$N(p,$γ)^{15}$O cross-section measurements, yet remain significantly lower than the latest solar neutrino observation values.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) achieves good agreement with experimental data for the total and partial astrophysical S-factors of the $^{14}$N(p,$\gamma)^{15}$O reaction, but the predicted S-factor at zero energy exceeds the experimental value recommended by the Solar Fusion III Compilation, and the derived carbon and nitrogen abundances remain significantly lower than values from the latest solar neutrino observations.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of the $^{14}$N(p,$\gamma)^{15}$O radiative capture reaction using the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC), which provides a unified framework for nuclear structure and reaction studies. Two variants of the Hamiltonian were implemented: GSM-CC(1) fitted to the energies of low-lying states of $^{14}$N and $^{15}$O, and GSM-CC(2) with the Woods-Saxon potential depth for the $sd$ shells shifted downward by 2 MeV and corrective factors slightly readjusted. The model spaces included $psd$ partial waves for protons and five shells for neutrons, with resonant and non-resonant continuum states represented by Berggren contours, and the calculations included $E1$, $M1$, and $E2$ transitions with effective charges from center-of-mass recoil correction.

## Key numbers

* Total astrophysical S-factor at zero energy S(0) = 2.263 keV b for GSM-CC(1) and S(0) = 1.996 keV b for GSM-CC(2), compared to the SF-III recommended value of 1.68 $\pm$ 0.14 keV b and the recent HINEG measurement of 1.92 $\pm$ 0.08 keV b.
* Partial S-factors at zero energy for GSM-CC(1) and GSM-CC(2) respectively: capture to $3/2^+_1$ state (6.79 MeV) gives 1.033 keV b and 0.897 keV b; capture to $1/2^+_1$ state gives 0.673 keV b and 0.622 keV b; capture to $1/2^-_1$ ground state gives 0.447 keV b and 0.365 keV b.
* Derived carbon and nitrogen abundances relative to hydrogen $N_{\mathrm{CN}}$ = 3.61 $\times$ 10$^{-4}$ for GSM-CC(1) and $N_{\mathrm{CN}}$ = 4.15 $\times$ 10$^{-4}$ for GSM-CC(2), compared to $N_{\mathrm{CN}}$ = 5.81$_{-0.94}^{+1.22}$ $\times$ 10$^{-4}$ from solar neutrino measurements (Basilico 2023) and $N_{\mathrm{CN}}$ = 4.45$_{-0.61}^{+0.69}$ $\times$ 10$^{-4}$ from the HINEG measurement (Chen 2024).
* Calculated proton decay width of the $1/2^+_2$ resonance: $\Gamma$ = 0.14 keV in GSM-CC(1) and 0.07 keV in GSM-CC(2), compared to the experimental value of 0.99 keV.
* Calculated proton decay width of the $3/2^+_2$ resonance: $\Gamma$ = 6.6 keV in GSM-CC(1) and 5.89 keV in GSM-CC(2), compared to the experimental value of 3.6 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $^{14}$N(p,$\gamma)^{15}$O reaction, which were done mainly using the R-matrix method (Angulo 2001, Imbriani 2005, Bemmerer 2006, Runkle 2005, Li 2016, Wagner 2018, Frentz 2022, Chen 2024) or the modified potential cluster model (Dubovichenko), by providing the first microscopic study using the GSM-CC approach. The results address the "solar composition problem" -- the 30%-40% reduction in solar metallicity found by Asplund (2009, 2021) and Caffau (2011) compared to the traditional Grevesse and Sauval (1998) modeling -- and the discrepancy between CNO neutrino fluxes measured by Borexino (2020, 2022) and predictions from standard solar models. The calculations enable improved constraints on the solar metallicity through the connection between the $^{14}$N(p,$\gamma)^{15}$O S-factor and the $^{15}$O decay neutrino flux, though the predicted carbon and nitrogen abundances remain significantly lower than those derived from solar neutrino measurements.
