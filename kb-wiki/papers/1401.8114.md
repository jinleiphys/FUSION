# Realistic shell-model calculations for isotopic chains "north-east'' of 48Ca in the (N,Z) plane

> We perform realistic shell-model calculations for nuclei with valence nucleons outside 48Ca, employing two different model spaces. The matrix elements of the effective two-body interaction and electromagnetic multipole operators have been calculated within the framework of the many-body perturbation theory, starting from a low-momentum potential derived from the high-precision CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential. The role played by the neutron orbital 1d5/2 has been investigated by comparing experimental data on yrast quadrupole excitations of isotopic chains north-east of 48Ca with the results of calculations including or not including this single-particle state in the model space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that realistic shell-model calculations using a two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential can reproduce the onset of collectivity in neutron-rich chromium and iron isotopes, but only when the neutron \(1d_{5/2}\) orbital is included in the model space; the authors claim this challenges the prevailing view that three-nucleon forces are necessary for such descriptions.

## Method

The authors performed realistic shell-model calculations for nuclei with valence nucleons outside a \(^{48}\)Ca core, using two model spaces: model space (I) spanned by proton \(0f_{7/2},1p_{3/2}\) and neutron \(1p_{3/2},1p_{1/2},0f_{5/2},0g_{9/2}\) orbitals, and model space (II) which additionally includes the neutron \(1d_{5/2}\) orbital. The effective two-body interaction and electromagnetic multipole operators were derived via many-body perturbation theory (Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion) from a \(V_{\rm low-k}\) potential (cutoff \(\Lambda=2.6\) fm\(^{-1}\)) obtained from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential, with single-particle energies taken from experiment for \(^{49}\)Sc and \(^{49}\)Ca.

## Key numbers

- Proton single-particle energy spacing: \(\epsilon_{p_{3/2}}-\epsilon_{f_{7/2}} = 3.1\) MeV, taken from the yrast \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) and \(\frac{7}{2}^-\) states in \(^{49}\)Sc.
- Neutron single-particle energy spacings for model space (I): \(\epsilon_{p_{1/2}}-\epsilon_{p_{3/2}} = 2.0\) MeV, \(\epsilon_{f_{5/2}}-\epsilon_{p_{3/2}} = 4.0\) MeV, \(\epsilon_{g_{9/2}}-\epsilon_{p_{3/2}} = 4.1\) MeV.
- Neutron single-particle energy spacings for model space (II): \(\epsilon_{g_{9/2}}-\epsilon_{p_{3/2}} = 3.8\) MeV, \(\epsilon_{d_{5/2}}-\epsilon_{p_{3/2}} = 8.0\) MeV.
- The neutron effective single-particle energy gap between \(0g_{9/2}\) and \(0f_{5/2}\) is about 2.5 MeV in chromium isotopes at \(N=40\), compared to about 4.0 MeV in nickel isotopes at \(N=40\).
- The calculated yrast \(2^+\) excitation energy in \(^{70}\)Ca drops to 0.7 MeV when the \(0g_{9/2}\) orbital is blocked out.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous realistic shell-model calculations for calcium isotopes (Coraggio 2009) and addresses the disappearance of the \(N=40\) shell closure and onset of collectivity in neutron-rich chromium and iron isotopes, a phenomenon previously studied by Caurier, Nowacki, and Poves (2002) and Lenzi (2010) using empirical interactions. The results enable a microscopic understanding of shell evolution in the region north-east of \(^{48}\)Ca, particularly the role of the neutron \(1d_{5/2}\) orbital in producing quadrupole collectivity, and challenge the necessity of three-nucleon forces as argued by Schwenk (2006) and Holt (2013) for describing these isotopic chains.
