# Anomalous propagators and the particle-particle channel: Bethe-Salpeter equation

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation has been extensively employed to compute the two-body electron-hole propagator and its poles which correspond to the neutral excitation energies of the system. Through a different time-ordering, the two-body Green's function can also describe the propagation of two electrons or two holes. The corresponding poles are the double ionization potentials and double electron affinities of the system. In this work, a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-body particle-particle propagator is derived within the linear-response formalism using a pairing field and anomalous propagators. This framework allows us to compute kernels corresponding to different self-energy approximations ($GW$, $T$-matrix, and second-Born) as in the usual electron-hole case. The performance of these various kernels is gauged for singlet and triplet valence double ionization potentials using a set of 23 small molecules. The description of double core hole states is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-body particle-particle propagator using pairing fields and anomalous propagators, which yields kernels that are fully analogous to the electron-hole BSE kernels; the derivation relies on the linear-response formalism and the approximation that the dynamic kernel can be linearized by substituting the full propagator \(K\) with the non-interacting propagator \(K_0\), an approximation that has proven successful in the electron-hole case.

## Method

The authors start from the Schwinger relation for the particle-particle propagator \(K\) as the functional derivative of an anomalous Green's function with respect to a pairing field, then use the Gorkov (Nambu) propagator formalism and the Gorkov-Dyson equation to derive a Dyson equation for \(K\). The resulting pp-BSE kernel is defined as the derivative of the anomalous self-energy \(\Sigma^\ee\) with respect to the anomalous propagator \(G^\ee\). The authors then construct approximate kernels from the Bogoliubov self-energy (giving pp-RPA), from a second-order self-energy (GF(2)-like), from the \(GW\) self-energy, and from the \(T\)-matrix self-energy, and solve the resulting eigenvalue problem in a finite spinorbital basis, optionally including a perturbative treatment of dynamical effects within the Tamm-Dancoff approximation.

## Key numbers

- The test set consists of 23 small molecules for which singlet and triplet valence double ionization potentials are computed.
- The pp-RPA kernel uses matrix elements \(C_{ab,cd}^{\RPA} = (\epsilon_a + \epsilon_b)\delta_{ac}\delta_{bd} + \mel{ab}{}{cd}\), \(B_{ab,ij}^{\RPA} = \mel{ab}{}{ij}\), and \(D_{ij,kl}^{\RPA} = -(\epsilon_i + \epsilon_j)\delta_{ik}\delta_{jl} + \mel{ij}{}{kl}\).
- The second-order kernel involves the effective interaction \(W^{(2)}_{pqrs}(\omega)\) with terms such as \(\frac{\mel{pm}{}{re} \mel{eq}{}{ms}}{\omega - (\epsilon_e - \epsilon_m - \ii\eta)}\).
- The perturbative dynamical correction uses a renormalization factor \(Z_{n} = \left[1 - (\boldsymbol{Y}^{(0)}_{n})^\dag \cdot \eval{\pdv{\bD^{(1)}(-\omega)}{\omega}}_{\omega = \Omega^{(0)}_{n}} \cdot \boldsymbol{Y}^{(0)}_{n} \right]^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter equation formalism from the electron-hole channel (used for neutral excitation energies) to the particle-particle channel, which yields double ionization potentials and double electron affinities. It builds on the pp-RPA approach developed by Yang and co-workers (2013-2024) and on the dynamic kernel linearization technique of Sangalli (2011) used in the electron-hole BSE. The derived pp-BSE kernels (\(GW\), \(T\)-matrix, second-Born) are direct analogs of well-known electron-hole BSE kernels, enabling systematic improvement beyond pp-RPA for double ionization and double electron affinity calculations.
