# The detailed mechanism of the eta production in pp scattering up to the Tlab = 4.5 GeV

> Contrary to very early beliefs, the experimental cross section data for the eta production in proton-proton scattering are well described if pi and only eta meson exchange diagrams are used to calculate the Born term. The inclusion of initial and final state interactions is done in the factorization approximation by using the inverse square of the Jost function. The two body Jost functions are obtained from the S matrices in the low energy effective range approximation. The danger of double counting in the p-eta final state interaction is discussed. It is shown that higher partial waves in meson-nucleon amplitudes do not contribute significantly bellow excess energy of Q=100 MeV. Known difficulties of reducing the multi resonance model to a single resonance one are illustrated.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier beliefs that heavier mesons (rho, omega, sigma) were required, the experimental cross section data for eta production in proton-proton scattering are well described using only pi and eta meson exchange diagrams for the Born term, provided that initial and final state interactions are included in the factorization approximation using Jost functions. The authors note that the danger of double counting in the p-eta final state interaction is present when full T-matrices are used instead of individual N* resonances, and they offer two versions of the p-eta final state interaction to address this.

## Method

The authors calculate the Born term using S11 meson-nucleon amplitudes from a multi-resonance, coupled-channel unitary model for pi, eta, and an "effective" two-body channel, with the three-particle vertex determined by Bonn potential parameters. Initial and final state interactions are included in the factorization approximation using the inverse square of the Jost function, with two-body Jost functions obtained from S matrices in the low-energy effective range approximation using scattering lengths and effective ranges for the pp 31S0 state, the pp 33P1 state, and the eta-p S11 subsystem. The model is assembled in four steps: Born term only (model A), adding pp final state interaction (model B), and two versions of p-eta final state interaction with full Jost function (model C, n=1) and an effective reduction to avoid double counting (model D, n=1/2).

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_piNN^2/4pi = 13.6, g_etaNN^2/4pi = 3, with cut-off parameters Lambda_pi = 1.3 GeV and Lambda_eta = 1.5 GeV
- pp scattering length and effective range for the 31S0 state: a_0^pp = 7.8 fm, r_0^pp = 2.79 fm
- eta-p S11 s-wave scattering length: a_0^p-eta = (0.717 + i 0.265) fm
- eta-p S11 effective range: r_0^p-eta = (-1.574 - i 0.020) fm
- pp 33P1 state p-wave scattering length: a_10^pp = -3.3 fm^3, effective range: r_10^pp = 4.22 fm^-1
- Higher partial waves in meson-nucleon amplitudes do not contribute significantly below excess energy Q = 100 MeV
- The model describes total cross section from near threshold up to T_lab = 4.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Batinić et al. (1997) which first showed that only pi and eta meson exchange contributions were sufficient for the Born term, and extends the examined energy range up to T_lab = 4.5 GeV. The authors confirm that a single resonance model using only N(1535) drastically fails, and that the next S11 resonance N(1650) must be included, with the controversial N(2090) providing further improvement. The model enables more detailed calculations of differential cross sections (angular distributions and invariant mass distributions) to compare with new low-energy experiments at Jülich from Smyrski (1999) and Moskal (2003), though the authors note that quantitative comparison awaits experimental accord between conflicting data sets from Calén (1999) and Abdul-Bary (2002).
