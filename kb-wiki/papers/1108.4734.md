# Next-to leading order analysis of target mass corrections to structure functions and asymmetries

> We perform a comprehensive analysis of target mass corrections (TMCs) to spin-averaged structure functions and asymmetries at next-to-leading order. Several different prescriptions for TMCs are considered, including the operator product expansion, and various approximations to it, collinear factorization, and xi-scaling. We assess the impact of each of these on a number of observables, such as the neutron to proton F_2 structure function ratio, and parity-violating electron scattering asymmetries for protons and deuterons which are sensitive to gamma-Z interference effects. The corrections from higher order radiative and nuclear effects on the parity-violating deuteron asymmetry are also quantified.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares several prescriptions for target mass corrections (TMCs) to spin-averaged structure functions and asymmetries at next-to-leading order, finding that the model dependence among prescriptions can reach 50-80% for the longitudinal structure function at moderate x, but that the uncertainty in the neutron-to-proton F2 ratio from TMCs is less than 3% at the kinematics of planned experiments, which is smaller than the current uncertainty from nuclear corrections in deuterium.

## Method

The authors computed target mass corrections to the F1, F2, F3, and FL structure functions using four different prescriptions: the operator product expansion (OPE) of Georgi and Politzer (1976), the collinear factorization approach of Ellis, Furmanski, and Petronzio (1983), the xi-scaling prescription of Aivazis et al. (1994), and the Accardi-Qiu (2008) prescription. They evaluated these using the CTEQ-Jefferson Lab (CJ) global PDF fits at Q2 = 2 GeV2 and 10 GeV2, and applied them to observables including the neutron-to-proton F2 ratio, longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratios, and parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering asymmetries on protons and deuterons.

## Key numbers

- At Q2 = 2 GeV2, target mass corrections to the neutron-to-proton F2 ratio reach approximately 25-30% at x = 0.7 for the OPE and EFP prescriptions, and approximately 12% for the xi-scaling and AQ prescriptions
- At Q2 = 10 GeV2, TMCs to the neutron-to-proton F2 ratio decrease to approximately 5% for OPE/EFP and approximately 2% for xi-scaling/AQ calculations
- For the F1 structure function at Q2 = 2 GeV2, the deviation from unity of the TMC ratio ranges from approximately 10% at x = 0.4 to more than a factor of 2 for x greater than 0.7
- The OPE and EFP results for F1 agree to within a few percent for all x, while the xi-scaling and AQ prescriptions differ from OPE by less than 5% and 15% respectively for x less than 0.8
- For the FL structure function, the OPE and EFP corrected functions exceed uncorrected ones by more than a factor of 5 by x = 0.5, while the AQ and xi-scaling ratios differ from OPE/EFP by more than 50% by x = 0.3 and by more than 80% for x greater than 0.8
- The phenomenological OPE approximation for F2 stays within 5% of the full OPE result for all x values

## Context

This work extends previous studies of target mass corrections by systematically comparing multiple prescriptions at next-to-leading order, which had not been done before. The analysis builds on the OPE framework of Georgi and Politzer (1976) and the collinear factorization approach of Ellis, Furmanski, and Petronzio (1983), as well as more recent formulations by Accardi and Qiu (2008) and the xi-scaling approach of Aivazis et al. (1994) and Kretzer and Reno (2002). The results enable more reliable extraction of parton distribution functions at large x from upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments such as BONUS12, MARATHON, and SOLID, and provide quantitative guidance on which TMC prescription introduces the least model dependence for different observables.
