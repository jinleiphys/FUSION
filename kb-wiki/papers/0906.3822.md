# Regularization, renormalization and "peratization" in effective field theory for two nucleons

> We discuss conceptual aspects of renormalization in the context of effective field theories for the two-nucleon system. It is shown that, contrary to widespread belief, renormalization scheme dependence of the scattering amplitude can only be eliminated up to the order the calculations are performed. We further consider an effective theory for an exactly solvable quantum mechanical model which possesses a long- and short-range interaction to simulate pionful effective field theory. We discuss the meaning of low-energy theorems in this model and demonstrate their validity in calculations with a finite cutoff $Λ$ as long as it is chosen of the order of the hard scale in the problem. Removing the cutoff by taking the limit $Λ\to \infty$ yields a finite result for the scattering amplitude but violates the low-energy theorems and is, therefore, not compatible with the effective field theory framework.

## Key claim

Contrary to widespread belief, renormalization scheme dependence of the scattering amplitude in effective field theories for two nucleons can only be eliminated up to the order the calculations are performed, meaning that the KSW framework does not offer any conceptual advantage over Weinberg's approach in this regard. Additionally, removing the cutoff by taking the limit Λ → ∞ yields a finite result for the scattering amplitude but violates low-energy theorems and is therefore not compatible with the effective field theory framework.

## Method

The authors analyze renormalization scheme dependence in both the KSW and Weinberg approaches using a pionless effective field theory for S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with cutoff-regularized loop integrals. They then construct an effective theory for an exactly solvable quantum mechanical model with separable long-range (scale m_l) and short-range (scale m_s) interactions to simulate pionful effective field theory, computing the effective range expansion coefficients and comparing subtractive renormalization (KSW-like) with cutoff regularization (Weinberg-like).

## Key numbers

- The cutoff range Λ = 450...600 MeV used in Refs. Entem (2003) and Epelbaum (2004) was criticized by Nogga et al. (2005) who employed a much larger range of cutoffs with Λ < 4 GeV
- The pion decay constant is normalized to f = 132 MeV
- The leading-order renormalized coupling constant in the ^1S_0 partial wave is given by C_0^(^1S_0)(μ) = -(4π)/(μm) * (1/(1 - [μ(a + 1/Λ_NN)]^{-1}) + μ/Λ_NN) with Λ_NN = (8π f^2)/(g_A^2 m)
- The renormalized low-energy constants in the cutoff scheme are C_R = 4πa/m and C_2^R = πa^2 r/m
- The hard scale of the problem is m_s ~ 1/√(ra) for the case r ≪ a
- The scattering length expansion coefficients are α_a^(0) = α_l, α_a^(1) = (α_l - 1)^2 α_s, α_a^(2) = (α_l - 1)^2 α_l α_s^2

## Context

This paper extends the ongoing debate about renormalization consistency in chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon interactions, addressing criticisms by Kaplan et al. (1996) regarding Weinberg's approach and the alternative KSW power counting scheme proposed by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1996, 1998). The work enables a clearer understanding of how cutoff values should be chosen in practical calculations, arguing that the cutoff should be taken of the order of the hard scale rather than removed entirely, which contradicts the approach of groups such as Frederico (1999), Pavon Valderrama (2003), and others who explore the Λ → ∞ limit.
