# Pseudospin, supersymmetry and the shell structure of atomic nuclei

> The evolution of single-particle energies with varying isospin asymmetry in the shell model is an important issue when predicting changes in the shell structure for exotic nuclei. In many cases pseudospin partner levels, that are almost degenerate in energy for stable nuclei, are relevant in extracting the size of the shell gaps. A breaking of the pseudospin symmetry can affect the size of these gaps and change the magic numbers accordingly. The strength of the pseudospin splitting is expected to depend in particular on isovector-dependent and tensor contributions to the effective nuclear interaction. A description employing supersymmetric quantum mechanics allows to derive a pseudospin symmetry breaking potential that is regular in contrast to the pseudospin-orbit potential in the conventional relativistic treatment. The derived perturbation potential provides a measure to quantify the symmetry breaking and it can be employed to improve mean-field calculations in order to better reproduce the experimentally observed shell evolution. General potentials with exact pseudospin symmetry are obtained that can be used in relativistic mean-field Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper argues that supersymmetric quantum mechanics provides a way to derive a pseudospin symmetry breaking potential that is regular (has no pole) for all radii, in contrast to the conventional relativistic pseudospin-orbit potential which exhibits a pole at finite radii and is therefore not a good quantitative measure of symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author starts from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Dirac equation with scalar, vector, and tensor potentials for spherical nuclei, writing the coupled first-order differential equations for the upper and lower radial wave functions. The Hamiltonian for the upper component is then factorized into supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians using first-order operators (supercharges) containing a supermomentum function. By comparing the supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians for the two pseudospin partner states (with quantum numbers κ and κ' = -κ-1), a regular pseudospin symmetry breaking potential is extracted, and general potentials that yield exact pseudospin symmetry are derived.

## Key numbers

- For the example of the {2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂} pseudospin pair in a Woods-Saxon potential with radius R = 3.8 fm, diffuseness a = 0.65 fm, scalar strength S₀ = 450 MeV, and vector strength V₀ = 370 MeV (representing neutrons in ⁴⁰Ca), the single-particle energies are E(2s₁/₂) - m = -15.604 MeV and E(1d₃/₂) - m = -15.424 MeV, with a splitting of only 0.180 MeV.
- The 1s₁/₂ state in the same potential lies at E(1s₁/₂) - m = -56.848 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic description of pseudospin symmetry pioneered by Ginocchio (1997, 1998, 1999) and others, which identified approximate pseudospin degeneracy in RMF models with large scalar and vector potentials of nearly equal magnitude. The conventional pseudospin-orbit potential derived from the lower-component wave equation has a pole at finite radii (noted by Marcos et al., 2000), making it unsuitable as a quantitative measure. The supersymmetric approach provides a regular perturbation potential that can be used to improve mean-field calculations and to test how different interaction contributions (isovector and tensor terms) affect pseudospin symmetry breaking and shell evolution in exotic nuclei.
