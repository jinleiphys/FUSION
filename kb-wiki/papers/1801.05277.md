# Neutron Anomalous Magnetic Moment in Dense Magnetized Systems

> In this work, we calculate the neutron anomalous magnetic moment supposing that this value can depend on the density and magnetic field of system. We employ the lowest order constraint variation (LOCV) method and $AV_{18}$ nuclear potential to calculate the medium dependency of the neutron anomalous magnetic moment. It is confirmed that the neutron anomalous magnetic moment increases by increasing the density, while it decreases as the magnetic field grows. The energy and equation of state for the system have also been investigated.

## Key claim

The neutron anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) in dense magnetized neutron matter is not constant but depends on both the density and the magnetic field of the system; the AMM increases with density and decreases with magnetic field strength, and the equilibrium value of the AMM at which the system is stable differs from the free-neutron value.

## Method

The author used the lowest order constraint variation (LOCV) method with the AV18 two-body nuclear potential to calculate the energy per particle of a pure homogeneous system of spin-polarized neutrons in a uniform magnetic field. The trial many-body wave function employed a Jastrow correlation function, and the energy functional was expanded up to the two-body cluster term. The two-body energy was minimized with respect to the correlation functions under a normalization constraint, yielding Euler-Lagrange differential equations whose solutions gave the correlation functions, two-body energy, and total energy per particle for various densities, magnetic fields, and values of the dimensionless AMM parameter r_mu = mu_dep / mu_n.

## Key numbers

- The free neutron AMM is given as mu_n = -1.9130427(5) (dimensionless, in nuclear magnetons).
- At a magnetic field of B = 10^18 G, the energy per particle versus spin polarization parameter was calculated for dimensionless AMM values r_mu at densities including rho = 0.5 fm^{-3}.
- The equilibrium value of the dimensionless AMM increases with density; for example, at a given magnetic field, the value at higher density is larger than at lower density (specific numerical values for r_mu at each density and field are not given in the truncated text).
- The equilibrium value of the dimensionless AMM decreases as the magnetic field grows; at higher magnetic fields the equilibrium r_mu is smaller than at lower magnetic fields (specific numerical values not given in the truncated text).
- The energy of neutron matter with medium-dependent AMM has a minimum at a specific density for each magnetic field; the system is more bound when the magnetic field increases.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron matter properties using the LOCV method (Bordhar 2011) by now allowing the neutron AMM to depend on the medium, rather than assuming it is constant. It builds on a body of theoretical work showing that nucleon AMMs increase with density in various models, including chiral non-linear meson theory (Meissner), cloudy bag model (Cheon), quark-meson coupling models (Saito, Ryu9), and shell-model calculations (Lu), and that strong magnetic fields can decrease the neutron radius (Mandal) and thus the AMM (Ferrer). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors, where both high density and strong magnetic fields are present, and suggest that the equation of state with medium-dependent AMM is softer than with constant AMM, which has implications for neutron star structure.
