# Theory of Multiphonon Excitation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the effects of channel coupling in the excitation dynamics of giant resonances in relativistic heavy ions collisions. For this purpose, we use a semiclassical approximation to the Coupled-Channels problem and separate the Coulomb and the nuclear parts of the coupling into their main multipole components. In order to assess the importance of multi-step processes, we neglect the resonance widths and solve the set of coupled equations exactly. Finite widths are then considered. In this case, we handle the coupling of the ground state with the dominant Giant Dipole Resonance exactly and study the excitation of the remaining resonances within the Coupled-Channels Born Approximation. A comparison with recent experimental data is made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multi-step processes (channel coupling) significantly affect the excitation of giant resonances in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and that a Coupled-Channels treatment, rather than first-order perturbation theory or the harmonic oscillator model, is necessary to reproduce experimental cross sections for multiphonon states such as the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) and double giant quadrupole resonance (DGQR).

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approximation in which the relative motion of projectile and target follows a straight-line classical trajectory, while the intrinsic nuclear excitation is treated quantum mechanically via time-dependent Coupled-Channels equations. They separate the Coulomb and nuclear coupling interactions into their main multipole components (E1, E2, M1) and derive explicit time-dependent matrix elements from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials and the Bohr-Mottelson particle-vibrator model. For the case of finite resonance widths, they treat the coupling between the ground state and the dominant Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) exactly, and evaluate excitation of the remaining resonances (including the double-phonon states) within the Coupled-Channels Born Approximation (CCBA).

## Key numbers

- The collisional time scale for the Coulomb interaction is given by Δt ≈ b/γv, with the fields peaking around τ = 0 and decreasing within Δτ ≈ 1.
- For E1 excitations, the first-order amplitude is expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions K₁(ξ) and K₀(ξ), where ξ = ωb/γv.
- The energy-weighted sum rule for E1 transitions yields B(E1; I_i → I_f) = (1/(2I_f+1)) × (9/4π) × (ℏ²/(2m_N)) × (NZ/(A E_x)) e².
- The energy-weighted sum rule for E2 transitions yields B(E2; I_i → I_f) = (1/(2I_f+1)) × (ℏ²/m_N) × (15R²/(4π E_x)) e² × (Z²/A for isoscalar, NZ/A for isovector).
- For isoscalar excitations, the deformation length squared is δ_0² = 2π (ℏ²/(m_N ⟨r²⟩)) (1/(A E_x)) for monopole, and δ_{λ≥2}² = (2π/3) (ℏ²/m_N) λ(2λ+1) (1/(A E_x)) for higher multipoles.
- For isovector dipole excitations, the deformation length is δ₁ = (π/2) (ℏ²/m_N) (A/(NZ)) (1/E_x).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Coupled-Channels formalism of Alder and Winther (1965) and the relativistic Coulomb excitation treatment of Winther and Alder (1979) to include both Coulomb and nuclear coupling in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. It addresses discrepancies between experimental data from GSI (SIS accelerator) and calculations based on first-order perturbation theory or the harmonic oscillator model, as noted by Baur (1990), Hans (1994), and Chomaz (1994). The method enables more accurate predictions for multiphonon giant resonance excitation cross sections and provides a framework for testing the boson-enhancement factor and the matrix elements for transitions between giant resonance states, which are relevant for studies of nuclei far from stability.
