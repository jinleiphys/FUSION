# Why nuclear forces favor the highest weight irreducible representations of the fermionic SU(3) symmetry

> The consequences of the attractive, short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction on the wave functions of the Elliott SU(3) and the proxy-SU(3) symmetry are discussed. The NN interaction favors the most symmetric spatial SU(3) irreducible representation, which corresponds to the maximal spatial overlap among the fermions. The percentage of the symmetric components out of the total in an SU(3) wave function is introduced, through which it is found, that no SU(3) irrep is more symmetric than the highest weight irrep for a certain number of valence particles in a three dimensional, isotropic, harmonic oscillator shell. The consideration of the highest weight irreps in nuclei and in alkali metal clusters, leads to the prediction of a prolate to oblate shape transition beyond the mid-shell region.

## Key claim

The attractive, short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction favors the most symmetric spatial SU(3) irreducible representation, which corresponds to maximal spatial overlap among fermions; no SU(3) irrep is more symmetric than the highest weight irrep for a given number of valence particles in a three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator shell.

## Method

The authors analyze the spatial symmetry of SU(3) wave functions by introducing the percentage of symmetric components out of the total number of quanta, defined as r = (lambda+mu)/(lambda+2mu) * 100%. They compare the highest weight irreducible representation with the most deformed irrep (highest second-order Casimir operator) for configurations of identical nucleons in a given harmonic oscillator shell, using Gelfand-Zeitlin patterns and the reduction U(Omega) superset U(3) superset SU(3) to determine particle distributions and their corresponding SU(3) irreps (lambda,mu).

## Key numbers

* For 10 protons in the pf shell (N=3), the highest weight irrep is (10,4) with weight vector w=(2,2,2,2,2,0,0,0,0,0), corresponding to the first five orbitals of Eq. (order3) each occupied by two protons.
* For 12 protons in the pf shell, the highest weight irrep is (12,0) which is totally symmetric, while the most deformed irrep is (4,10) containing mu=10 non-symmetric quanta.
* The percentage of symmetric quanta is defined as r(lambda,mu) = (lambda+mu)/(lambda+2mu) * 100%, where lambda+mu is the number of symmetric quanta and lambda+2mu is the total number of quanta.
* For the example of three protons in the p shell (N=1) with highest weight vector w=(2,1,0), the SU(3) irrep is (1,1) with lambda+mu=2 symmetric quanta out of lambda+2mu=3 total quanta.

## Context

This work extends the Elliott SU(3) symmetry (Elliott 1958) and the proxy-SU(3) model (Bonatsos 2017) by providing a physical justification for why the highest weight irrep is favored over the most deformed irrep. It builds on the Wigner SU(4) symmetry (Wigner 1937) and the Majorana exchange force formalism (Talmi 1993) to connect the short-range attractive NN interaction to spatial symmetry. The results enable parameter-independent predictions of prolate-to-oblate shape transitions beyond mid-shell in both atomic nuclei and alkali metal clusters, addressing the long-standing puzzle of prolate dominance in even-even nuclei (Hamamoto 2005).
