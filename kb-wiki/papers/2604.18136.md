# Equation of State for warm Neutron Star outer crusts

> We describe the equation of state (EoS) of a warm ion plasma as obtained by performing microscopic many-body simulations using Molecular Dynamics computational techniques. Using the cold one-component plasma (OCP) composition in the Neutron Star (NS) outer crust assumed in Murarka et al. (2022) with a representative heavy nucleus for each density, we refine previous calculations. We include electron screening and modeling of ions as finite-size Gaussian distributions in the interaction potential, together with an efficient Ewald energy summation procedure. From this, the EoS relation $P(n_B,T)$ is obtained as a function of baryonic density and temperature in the NS outer crust under conditions $n_B\in[7.48\times 10^{-10},2.09\times10^{-4}]$ $ \rm fm^{-3}$ , $k_{B}T\in[1,5]$ MeV. In order to improve the usability of our results we provide tabulated data values along with a neural network parametrization available in the Zenodo repository, see https://zenodo.org/records/15348712. We find that even at moderate temperatures, thermal effects of ions are key in the higher density region closer to the inner crust, when described using a thermal effective parametrization based on the thermal adiabatic index $Γ_{th}$. We compare our results with other EoS in the literature performing a critical discussion.

## Key claim

The authors use Molecular Dynamics simulations of a warm one-component plasma (OCP) with finite-size Gaussian ions and electron screening to compute the equation of state for the neutron star outer crust, finding that thermal ionic effects are significant even at moderate temperatures (up to 5 MeV) in the higher-density region near the inner crust, contrary to simpler models that treat thermal effects as negligible in that regime.

## Method

The authors performed Molecular Dynamics simulations of N=500 ions per sample, using the cold OCP composition from Murarka (2022) with a single representative heavy nucleus at each baryonic density. Ions were modeled as finite-size Gaussian charge distributions interacting via a screened Yukawa potential, with electron screening treated through a Thomas-Fermi dielectric function and an Ewald summation procedure for the electrostatic energy and pressure. The system was evolved using a Velocity Verlet algorithm for at least 5×10⁴ time steps to ensure thermalization, and the total pressure was obtained from the stress tensor including kinetic, short-range, long-range, and volume contributions, with the electron and photon contributions added separately.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic density range: n_B ∈ [7.48×10⁻¹⁰, 2.09×10⁻⁴] fm⁻³
- Temperature range: k_B T ∈ [1, 5] MeV
- Simulations performed at temperatures k_B T = 1, 2, 3.5, and 5 MeV
- Number of ions per simulation: N = 500
- For ions with mass number A ∈ [60,100] at k_B T = 1 MeV, the thermal de Broglie wavelength λ_dB ∼ [1.6, 2.1] pm, and the ratio λ_dB/a ∼ 10⁻⁵–10⁻³, confirming the semiclassical regime
- The electron relativistic parameter x_e,r ∼ 1.008 (ρ_6 Z/A)^(1/3) where ρ_6 = ρ/10⁶ g cm⁻³
- The electron degeneracy temperature T_r ∼ 5.930×10⁹ K

## Context

This work extends previous cold OCP calculations for the neutron star outer crust by Murarka (2022) and Pearson (2018) to finite temperatures using microscopic many-body simulations, improving upon point-like ion approximations by including finite-size Gaussian charge distributions and electron screening. The results enable more accurate modeling of warm neutron star crusts in core-collapse supernova simulations, protoneutron star evolution, and binary neutron star merger scenarios, where thermal effects from ions are often approximated using simple Γ-law parametrizations (typically Γ_th ∈ [1.5, 2]) that may not capture the actual behavior. The authors provide tabulated data and a neural network parametrization to facilitate use in numerical simulations.
