# Langevin dynamics of heavy flavors in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the stochastic dynamics of c and b quarks, produced in hard initial processes, in the hot medium created after the collision of two relativistic heavy ions. This is done through the numerical solution of the relativistic Langevin equation. The latter requires the knowledge of the friction and diffusion coefficients, whose microscopic evaluation is performed treating separately the contribution of soft and hard collisions. The evolution of the background medium is described by ideal/viscous hydrodynamics. Below the critical temperature the heavy quarks are converted into hadrons, whose semileptonic decays provide single-electron spectra to be compared with the current experimental data measured at RHIC. We focus on the nuclear modification factor R_AA and on the elliptic-flow coefficient v_2, getting, for sufficiently large p_T, a reasonable agreement.

# Langevin dynamics of heavy flavors in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The stochastic dynamics of charm and bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, modeled via the relativistic Langevin equation with transport coefficients computed from a combination of perturbative QCD and Hard Thermal Loop resummation, yields a reasonable description of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for non-photonic electrons at RHIC at sufficiently high p_T, but the elliptic-flow coefficient v_2 is systematically underestimated, suggesting that additional hadronization mechanisms such as coalescence may be needed.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic Langevin equation for c and b quarks propagating in an expanding fireball described by ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, using a time step of 0.02 fm/c in the local fluid rest frame. The momentum-diffusion coefficients kappa_T and kappa_L were computed by separating soft collisions (treated with Hard Thermal Loop resummation) from hard collisions (treated with kinetic pQCD for 2-to-2 processes), with an intermediate cutoff |t|* ~ m_D^2 separating the two regimes. Heavy quarks were initialized using POWHEG with CTEQ6M PDFs and EPS09 nuclear effects, then evolved until hadronization via Peterson fragmentation functions, with final semileptonic decays simulated using PYTHIA.

## Key numbers
- The c\bar{c} production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s)_NN = 200 GeV is 254.14 microbarns; in AA collisions it is 236.11 microbarns.
- The b\bar{b} production cross section in pp collisions is 1.769 microbarns; in AA collisions it is 2.033 microbarns.
- The ideal hydro scenario uses tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, initial entropy density s_0 = 110 fm^{-3}, and initial temperature T_0 = 357 MeV.
- The viscous hydro scenario uses tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c, s_0 = 83.8 fm^{-3}, and T_0 = 333 MeV.
- The high-momentum region (p_T greater than or approximately 4 GeV/c) of the electron R_AA is best reproduced when alpha_s is evaluated at the scale 1.5 pi T.
- The initial sample contained 45 x 10^6 c\bar{c} and b\bar{b} pairs for each explored case.

## Context
This work extends the Langevin-based approach of Alberico et al. (2009) from static media to the dynamical expanding fireball created in heavy-ion collisions, using hydrodynamical backgrounds from Kolb et al. and Romatschke et al. The transport coefficient evaluation follows the method of Peigne and Peshier (2008) combining pQCD and HTL resummation. The results are compared to PHENIX data for non-photonic electrons, and the authors note that the v_2 underprediction may be addressed by incorporating coalescence hadronization as studied by Rapp et al. (2009). The approach enables systematic extraction of heavy-quark transport coefficients from experimental data.
