# Probing the QCD phase diagram with dileptons - a study using coarse-grained transport dynamics

> Dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at various energies is studied using coarse-grained transport simulations. Microscopic output from the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model is hereby put on a grid of space-time cells which allows to extract the local temperature and chemical potential in each cell via an equation of state. The dilepton emission is then calculated applying in-medium spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory and partonic production rates based on lattice calculations. The comparison of the resulting spectra with experimental data shows that the dilepton excess beyond the decay contributions from a hadronic cocktail reflects the trajectory of the fireball in the $T-μ_{\mathrm{B}}$ plane of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The measured dilepton excess over the hadronic decay cocktail can be interpreted as an effect of medium-modified spectral distributions that reflect the emission conditions, and the trajectory of the fireball in the \(T-\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\) plane of the QCD phase diagram can be extracted from the dilepton spectra, though the identification of signals for a phase transition requires systematic and precise studies that remain a challenge for future experiments.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to generate an ensemble of heavy-ion collision events, then coarse-grained the microscopic output onto a space-time grid of small cells. For each cell, the local energy density and baryon density were extracted, and an equation of state (hadron-gas EoS for \(T \leq 170\) MeV, lattice EoS for higher temperatures) was used to obtain local temperature \(T\) and baryochemical potential \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\). Thermal dilepton emission was then calculated for each cell using in-medium spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory for the hadronic phase and partonic production rates from lattice calculations for the quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at \(E_{\mathrm{lab}}=2\,A\)GeV, the thermal dilepton emission peaks at \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}=0.7-0.9\) GeV, with a maximum temperature of \(T \approx 100\) MeV.
- For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\)GeV (top RHIC energy), most lepton pairs stem from cells with nearly vanishing baryochemical potential, and temperatures reach \(400-500\) MeV.
- The hadronic emission (for \(T < 170\) MeV) was considered separately from partonic emission in the analysis of the \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\) dependence.
- The thermal dilepton invariant mass spectra were calculated for central Au+Au collisions at mid-rapidity (\(|y_{\mathrm{ee}}| < 0.5\)) for energies from SIS 18 up to LHC.

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach previously developed by Huovinen and collaborators (2002) and detailed further by Endres et al. (2014), connecting microscopic transport dynamics to macroscopic thermodynamic descriptions. The results show that thermal emission rates from hadronic and partonic matter are dual to a large extent, producing conformable spectral shapes, which makes identifying phase transition signals challenging. This enables future systematic studies at FAIR and the beam-energy scan program at RHIC to search for signatures of the QCD phase transition in dilepton spectra.
