# Cherenkov Radiation of Gluon Currents

## Key claim
The paper presents a full quantum calculation of in-medium Cherenkov gluon emission by a gluon current in transparent media without dispersion, but the calculation is restricted to the quasiabelian case where the chromopermittivity tensor reduces to a scalar function \(\epsilon(\omega)\) and is valid only for real \(\epsilon(\omega)\).

## Method
The authors generalize the QED calculation of Cherenkov radiation (R57) to the three-wave interaction in in-medium QCD, corresponding to the decay of a free vacuum gluon into a free in-medium gluon and a free vacuum gluon. They adopt a step-function model for the chromopermittivity \(\epsilon(\omega) = \epsilon_0 \cdot \theta(\omega_0 - \omega) + 1 \cdot \theta(\omega - \omega_0)\) based on RHIC data, and compute the energy spectrum of the emitted Cherenkov gluons from the decay kinematics and the three-gluon vertex.

## Key numbers
- The chromopermittivity model parameters from RHIC data: \(\epsilon_0 \simeq 5\) and \(\omega_0 \simeq 3\) GeV.
- The Cherenkov decay is possible only when the initial gluon energy \(E\) satisfies \(E > \omega_0\) and the emitted gluon energy \(\omega < \omega_0\), with the restriction \(\frac{\omega}{E} \leq \frac{2}{\sqrt{\epsilon(\omega)} + 1}\).
- The full energy spectrum of Cherenkov gluons is given by \(P(\omega) = 4 \pi \alpha_s N_c \omega \left(1-\frac{1}{\epsilon}\right) \left(1-\frac{\omega}{E}-\frac{\epsilon-1}{4} \frac{\omega^2}{E^2}\right) \times \left[ 1+\frac{1}{2}\left( \epsilon+\frac{\epsilon+1}{1-\frac{\omega}{E}}+\frac{\epsilon}{\left( 1-\frac{\omega}{E} \right)^2} \right) \frac{\omega^2}{E^2}+\frac{(\epsilon+1)^2}{8\left( 1-\frac{\omega}{E} \right)^2}\frac{\omega^4}{E^4} \right]\).
- In the soft limit \(\omega \to 0\), the spectrum reduces to the abelian Cherenkov form: \(P(\omega) \simeq 4 \pi \alpha_s N_c \omega \left(1-\frac{1}{\epsilon(\omega)}\right)\).

## Context
This work extends the QED Cherenkov radiation calculation of R57 to non-abelian gluon currents in QCD, motivated by experimental observations from RHIC (STAR 2005, 2010; PHENIX 2006, 2008) of double-humped away-side angular correlations. The Cherenkov gluon radiation mechanism was previously proposed as an explanation for these correlations by KMW06, MR05, NMR08, and DKLV09. The calculation enables quantitative comparison with RHIC data using the chromopermittivity model extracted from experiment, and the authors note that further details are available in their companion paper AL10.
