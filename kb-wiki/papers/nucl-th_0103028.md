# Coulomb and nuclear breakup effects in the single neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X

> We analyze the recently obtained new data on the partial cross sections and parallel momentum distributions for transitions to ground as well as excited states of the 16C core, in the one-neutron removal reaction 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X at the beam energy of 61 MeV/nucleon. The Coulomb and nuclear breakup components of the one-neutron removal cross sections have been calculated within a finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory and an eikonal model, respectively. The nuclear contributions dominate the partial cross sections for the core excited states. By adding the nuclear and Coulomb cross sections together, a reasonable agreement is obtained with the data for these states. The shapes of the experimental parallel momentum distributions of the core states are described well by the theory.

## Key claim

The sum of nuclear and Coulomb breakup cross sections, calculated separately with an eikonal model and a finite-range DWBA theory respectively, provides a reasonable description of the partial cross sections for transitions to excited states of the 16C core in the 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X reaction at 61 MeV/nucleon, but the theory underpredicts the ground-state transition by more than an order of magnitude, a discrepancy that cannot be resolved by changing the spin-parity assignment of the 17C ground state.

## Method

The nuclear breakup partial cross sections were calculated within an eikonal model using the black disc approximation for core-target and neutron-target interactions in the optical limit of Glauber theory, including both diffraction dissociation and stripping contributions. The Coulomb breakup partial cross sections were calculated using a post-form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) theory that treats the core-valence neutron interaction to all orders. The two cross sections were added assuming negligible Coulomb-nuclear interference, and the projectile ground state was described by coupling a valence neutron with specific (n,l,j) quantum numbers and spectroscopic factors from Warburton and Brown (1992) to the corresponding 16C core states.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 61 MeV/nucleon for the 197Au(17C,16C gamma)X reaction
- One-neutron separation energy of 17C ground state: 0.729 MeV
- For the 16C ground state (0+, Ex=0.0 MeV, l=2): Coulomb cross section = 148 mb, nuclear cross section = 113 mb, theoretical total = 261 mb, experimental = 113 +/- 26 mb
- For the 16C 2+ state at 1.77 MeV (sum of l=0 and l=2 configurations): Coulomb cross section = 142 mb, nuclear cross section = 237 mb, theoretical total = 379 mb, experimental = 162 +/- 56 mb
- For the 16C states near 4.1 MeV (2,3(+),4+, sum of l=0 and l=2): Coulomb cross section = 42 mb, nuclear cross section = 155 mb, theoretical total = 197 mb, experimental = 75 +/- 25 mb
- Spectroscopic factor for the ground state transition (l=2): C^2S = 0.03
- Spectroscopic factor for the 2+ state at 1.77 MeV with l=0 configuration: C^2S = 0.16; with l=2 configuration: C^2S = 1.44
- Excitation energies of 16C core states: 0.0 MeV (0+), 1.77 MeV (2+), 4.1 MeV (2,3(+),4+)

## Context

This work extends the technique of one-nucleon removal reactions with gamma-ray coincidence (a,b gamma type) from light 9Be targets, where nuclear breakup dominates, to heavy 197Au targets where Coulomb breakup also contributes significantly. The results contrast with earlier measurements on the same heavy target using the one-neutron halo nucleus 14B (Guimaraes et al., 2000), where no transitions to excited core states were observed, whereas substantial partial cross sections to excited states of 16C are found here. The analysis enables future investigations of non-halo neutron-rich nuclei and highlights the need for a unified quantum mechanical treatment of Coulomb and nuclear breakup including their interference, as well as possible coupled-channel effects for the 17C ground state structure.
