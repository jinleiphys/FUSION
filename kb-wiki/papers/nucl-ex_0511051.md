# Mass measurements and superallowed beta decay

> A recent Penning-trap measurement of the masses of 46V and 46Ti leads to a Qec value that disagrees significantly with the previously accepted value, and destroys overall consistency among the nine most precisely characterized T=1 superallowed beta emitters. This raises the possibility of a systematic discrepancy between Penning-trap measurements and the reaction-based measurements upon which the Qec values depended in the past. We carefully re-analyze (n,gamma) and (p,gamma) reaction measurements in the 24 \leq A \leq 28 mass region, and compare the results to very precise Penning-trap measurements of the stable nuclei 24Mg, 26Mg and 28Si. We thus determine upper limits to possible systematic effects in the reaction results, and go on to establish limits for the mass of radioactive 26Al, to which future on-line Penning-trap measurements can be compared. We stress the urgency of identifying or ruling-out possible systematic effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a recent Penning-trap measurement of the $^{46}$V $Q_{EC}$ value, which disagrees with the previously accepted value from a $(^3$He$,t)$ reaction measurement, may indicate a systematic discrepancy between Penning-trap and reaction-based $Q$-value measurements; however, the authors hedge that the anomaly could also be an isolated occurrence or could point to a deficiency in nuclear-structure-dependent corrections for $^{46}$V.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed ($n$,$\gamma$) and ($p$,$\gamma$) reaction $Q$-value measurements in the $24 \leq A \leq 28$ mass region, updating calibration standards where possible, and compared the resulting mass excesses to very precise Penning-trap measurements of the stable nuclei $^{24}$Mg, $^{26}$Mg, and $^{28}$Si. They then used the validated reaction methodology to derive a conservative range for the mass excess of radioactive $^{26}$Al, which can serve as a test case for future on-line Penning-trap measurements.

## Key numbers

- The new Penning-trap $Q_{EC}$ value for $^{46}$V is 7052.90(40) keV, which disagrees with the $(^3$He$,t)$ result of 7050.41(60) keV from Vonach et al. (1977) by nearly three standard deviations.
- For ($n$,$\gamma$) reactions, systematic differences between Penning-trap and reaction results are less than 100 eV, based on comparisons of $^{26}$Mg mass excesses.
- For ($p$,$\gamma$) reactions, systematic differences are less than 200 eV, based on comparisons of $^{28}$Si mass excesses.
- The corrected $^{26}$Mg($p$,$\gamma$)$^{27}$Al $Q$ value is revised to 8271.2(3) keV from the original 8271.0(5) keV.
- The corrected $^{27}$Al($p$,$\gamma$)$^{28}$Si $Q$ value is revised to 11584.8(2) keV from the original 11584.5(4) keV.
- The $^{26}$Al mass excess derived from reaction measurements, adjusted for possible systematics, is -12210.21(22) keV.

## Context

This work extends the survey of superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ decays by Hardy and Towner (2005), which established corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ values consistent with the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis for nine transitions. The new Penning-trap result for $^{46}$V by Savard et al. (2005) destroys that consistency, raising the possibility that the $(^3$He$,t)$ measurements of Vonach et al. (1977) may have systematic errors. The paper enables future on-line Penning-trap measurements of $^{26}$Al to serve as a critical test for systematic effects in reaction-based $Q$-value measurements, which are essential for determining the $V_{ud}$ element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix from superallowed beta decay.
