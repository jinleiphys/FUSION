# Pion-less effective field theory for atomic nuclei and lattice nuclei

> We compute the medium-mass nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca using pionless effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-leading order (NLO). The low-energy coefficients of the EFT Hamiltonian are adjusted to experimantal data for nuclei with mass numbers $A=2$ and $3$, or alternatively to results from lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at an unphysical pion mass of 806 MeV. The EFT is implemented through a discrete variable representation in the harmonic oscillator basis. This approach ensures rapid convergence with respect to the size of the model space and facilitates the computation of medium-mass nuclei. At NLO the nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are bound with respect to decay into alpha particles. Binding energies per nucleon are 9-10 MeV and 30-40 MeV at pion masses of 140 MeV and 806 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that pionless effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-leading order (NLO), implemented through a discrete variable representation in the harmonic oscillator basis, can produce bound medium-mass nuclei (16O and 40Ca) when the low-energy coefficients are adjusted to either experimental data for A=2 and A=3 nuclei or to lattice QCD results at an unphysical pion mass of 806 MeV, but only when the UV cutoff is limited to about 700 MeV due to the Wigner bound.

## Method
The authors formulated pionless EFT interactions directly in a harmonic oscillator basis using a discrete variable representation (DVR), where the finite oscillator space itself serves as the regulator without external regulator functions. They implemented IR-improved contact interactions for nucleon-nucleon (NN) S-waves at LO and NLO, and for three-nucleon (NNN) forces, using both a square cutoff in Jacobi momenta and a hyperspherical cutoff. The low-energy coefficients were fitted to either experimental data for A=2 and A=3 nuclei or to lattice QCD results from Beane (2013), and the NLO potentials were solved non-perturbatively to compute 16O and 40Ca.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies per nucleon for 16O and 40Ca at physical pion mass (140 MeV): 9-10 MeV
- Binding energies per nucleon for 16O and 40Ca at unphysical pion mass (806 MeV): 30-40 MeV
- The breakdown scale of pionless EFT is set by the pion mass m_pi ≈ 140 MeV
- The Fermi momentum of heavy nuclei is k_F ≈ 270 MeV, which exceeds the breakdown scale
- The Fermi energy is about 40 MeV, which is below the pion mass
- The UV cutoff range is limited to about 700 MeV due to the Wigner bound
- The three-nucleon force uses constants Lambda_chi = 700 MeV and F_pi = 92.4 MeV to make the LEC c_E dimensionless

## Context
This work extends pionless EFT beyond previous calculations of light nuclei (up to 4He by Platter (2005) and Kirscher (2010), and 6Li by Stetcu (2007)) to medium-mass nuclei 16O and 40Ca, which had previously only been accessible to chiral EFT calculations (Binder 2013, Lahde 2014, Hagen 2014, Hergert 2016, Morris 2017). The authors follow the approach of Barnea (2015) and Contessi (2017) in using lattice QCD results from Beane (2013) as input, and build upon the DVR formulation in the oscillator basis developed by Binder (2016). This work enables future studies that may eventually connect nuclear structure directly to QCD as lattice QCD calculations improve.
