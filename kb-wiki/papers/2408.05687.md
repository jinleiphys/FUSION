# Constraining the competition between the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions in light of the multimessenger era

> We extend the parity doublet model for hadronic matter and study the possible presence of quark matter inside the cores of neutron stars with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. Considering the uncertainties of the QCD phase diagram and the location of the critical endpoint, we aim to explore the competition between the chiral phase transition and the deconfinement phase transition systematically, regulated by the vacuum pressure $-B$ in the NJL model. Employing a Maxwell construction, a sharp first-order deconfinement phase transition is implemented combining the parity doublet model for the hadronic phase and the NJL model for the high-energy quark phase. The position of the chiral phase transition is obtained from the NJL model self-consistently. We find stable neutron stars with a quark core within a specific parameter space that satisfies current astronomical observations. The observations suggest a relatively large chiral invariant mass $m_0=600$ MeV in the parity doublet model and a larger split between the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions while assuming the first-order deconfinement phase transition. The maximum mass of the hybrid star that we obtain is $\sim 2.2 M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that stable hybrid neutron stars with quark cores can exist within a specific parameter space satisfying current multimessenger observations, but only when the chiral invariant mass in the parity doublet model is relatively large (m0 = 600 MeV) and the split between the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions is also large, assuming a first-order deconfinement transition. The authors treat the vacuum pressure -B in the NJL model as a free parameter to systematically vary the deconfinement point while keeping chiral dynamics fixed, rather than calculating it from first principles.

## Method

The authors combine the parity doublet model (PDM) for hadronic matter with a modified (2+1)-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, implementing a sharp first-order deconfinement phase transition via Maxwell construction at the chemical potential where pressures of both phases are equal. The NJL model includes Fierz-transformed interactions weighted by a parameter α to incorporate vector repulsion effects, and the chiral phase transition point μχ is determined from the maximum of the chiral susceptibility. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved for spherically symmetric static stars using the BPS equation of state for the crust, PDM for the outer core, and NJL for the possible quark core.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the hybrid star obtained: ∼ 2.2 M⊙
- Chiral invariant mass in PDM suggested by observations: m0 = 600 MeV
- Chiral phase transition positions (μχ) for different α values: 301.0 MeV (α = 0.00), 307.6 MeV (α = 0.60), 385.0 MeV (α = 0.80), and 318.7 MeV (α = 0.95)
- For PDM500 with α = 0.6: Δ = 0.086 GeV for B^(1/4) = 130 MeV and Δ = 0.208 GeV for B^(1/4) = 145 MeV
- For α = 0.6: Δ = 0.168 GeV; for α = 0.8: Δ = 0.171 GeV (with fixed other parameters)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using parity doublet models (Marczenko et al., 2015-2024; Gao et al., 2022, 2024) and NJL-type models (Wang et al., 2019; Li et al., 2020; Yuan et al., 2022, 2023, 2024) by systematically exploring the competition between deconfinement and chiral phase transitions as regulated by the vacuum pressure parameter B. The approach enables investigation of a possible quarkyonic phase (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007; Hidaka et al., 2011) where chiral symmetry is restored while quarks remain confined, and the results are constrained by multimessenger observations of massive pulsars including PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013), and PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie et al., 2020). The methodology permits future studies to further constrain the QCD phase diagram using neutron star observations.
