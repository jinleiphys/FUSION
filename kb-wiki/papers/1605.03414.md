# Degeneracies with the Q.Q interaction in a single j shell

> Previously [1] it was shown that for a configuration of 2 protons and 2 neutrons in the g_{9/2}shell there is a certain degeneracy that occurs when the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (Q.Q) is used to to obtain the wave functions.. We here show 3 other examples of such degenerate pairs, all, as before, involving T=0 and T=2 states. . More important we discuss an unusual peculiarity of the original example. Also we point out that degeneracies can be confusing and steps can be taken to remove them.

## Key claim

The paper reports four examples of degenerate T=0 and T=2 states in a single-j shell for 2 protons and 2 neutrons using the quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q) interaction, but emphasizes that such degeneracies can be confusing because any linear combination of the two states loses isospin information; the most interesting case involves a J=4+ pair in the g9/2 shell where both members have pure seniority v=4, which is expected for the T=2 member but surprising for the T=0 member.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for 2 protons and 2 neutrons in a single j shell using the quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q) interaction, with the two-body matrix elements scaled so that the J=0 matrix element equals -1.0 MeV. They systematically searched for degenerate pairs of states with total angular momentum J and isospin T=0 and T=2 across the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells, and analyzed the wave functions in terms of proton-neutron coupling amplitudes D^Jα(J_P,J_N). For the g9/2 J=4+ case, they used the J=0 T=1 pairing interaction of Flowers and Edmonds to identify seniority quantum numbers and computed overlaps with the unique v=2 state to confirm seniority purity.

## Key numbers

- f7/2 shell: J=2+ degenerate pair at energy E = 3.604 MeV
- g9/2 shell: J=4+ degenerate pair at energy E = 3.5284 MeV
- g9/2 shell: J=5+ degenerate pair at energy E = 4.5890 MeV
- h11/2 shell: J=5+ degenerate pair at energy E = 4.1458 MeV
- For the g9/2 J=4+ T=0 state, the overlap with the unique v=2 wave function is zero, confirming it has seniority v=4
- The J=0 T=1 pairing interaction yields 6 degenerate T=0 v=4 states at excitation energy 2.2 MeV and one v=2 state at 1.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Escuderos and Zamick (2015) of a degenerate T=0, T=2 pair in the g9/2 shell, and systematically identifies three additional examples in the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells. The paper connects to the concept of partial dynamical symmetry and the unique seniority v=4 state for J=4+ in the g9/2 shell previously discussed by Escuderos and Zamick and later proven by Qi and others. The results enable further study of seniority conservation in mixed proton-neutron systems and highlight the need for careful handling of degeneracies when extracting isospin information from shell-model calculations.
