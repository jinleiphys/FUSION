# Hyperfine Level Splitting for Hydrogen-Like Ions due to Rotation-Spin Coupling

> The theoretical aspects of spin-rotation coupling are presented. The approach is based on the general covariance principle. It is shown that the gyrogravitational ratio of the bare spin-1/2 and the spin-1 particles is equal unity. That is why spin couples with rotation as an ordinary angular momentum. This result is the rigorous substantiation of the cranking model. To observe the phenomenon, the experiment with hydrogen-like ions in a storage ring is suggested. It is found that the splitting of the $1 ^2!S_{1/2}, F=1/2$ hyperfine state of the $^{140}{\rm Pr}^{58+}$ and $^{142}{\rm Pm}^{60+}$ ions circulating in the storage ring ESR in Darmstadt along a helical trajectory is about 4.5 MHz. We argue that such splitting can be experimentally determined by means of the ionic interferometry.

# Hyperfine Level Splitting for Hydrogen-Like Ions due to Rotation-Spin Coupling

## Key claim
The spin-rotation coupling splits the degenerate magnetic sub-states of the hyperfine structure in hydrogen-like ions circulating in a storage ring, with the splitting arising primarily from the spin-rotation term in the Hamiltonian; however, the splitting of about 4.5 MHz cannot be resolved by precision laser spectroscopy due to the large Doppler width (~100 MHz), and instead requires a novel ion interferometry method that exploits the forbidden electron-capture decay from one of the sub-states.

## Method
The author derives the relativistic Dirac equation for the electron and the Proca equation for the spin-1 nucleus in a rotating reference frame using the general covariance principle, obtaining Hamiltonians that include spin-rotation coupling terms with gyrogravitational ratio unity. The two-particle Hamiltonian is separated into center-of-mass and relative-motion parts in the adiabatic approximation, and the energy eigenvalues are computed to first order in the rotation-induced perturbations. The predicted splitting is then related to the betatron oscillation frequencies of ions in the ESR storage ring, and an ion interferometry scheme using two magnetic "flipper" regions and the electron-capture decay as a state-selective probe is proposed to measure the splitting.

## Key numbers
- Splitting of the $1^2S_{1/2}, F=1/2$ hyperfine state: $\Delta = \Omega/(2\pi) \approx 4.5$ MHz, derived from the transverse betatron frequencies $\nu_x = 2.27$ and $\nu_y = 2.23$ times the revolution frequency $\nu \approx 2$ MHz.
- Doppler width of the hyperfine transition line: $\delta\nu = \nu_0 \delta\beta/(1-\beta^2) \sim 100$ MHz, for relative velocity spread $\delta\beta/\beta \sim 10^{-7}$ and transition frequency $\nu_0 \sim 10^3$ THz.
- Mean lifetime of the magnetic dipole transition between $M_F = \pm 1/2$ sub-states: $1.4 \times 10^{25}$ s.
- Period of spatial oscillations in the interferometer: $L_0 = V/(\Delta \sqrt{1-\beta^2}) \approx 68$ m.
- Magnetic field strength needed to mix the sub-states: $H \sim \hbar\Omega/\mu_0 = 3.2$ G, where $\mu_0$ is the Bohr magneton.
- Ion velocity parameter: $\beta = V/c = 0.71$ for the ESR.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of spin-rotation coupling, previously predicted by Mashhoon (2000) and Silverman (1991) using simplified cranking-model approaches, by providing a rigorous derivation from general covariance for both spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles. The proposed experiment builds on time-resolved electron-capture decay measurements of hydrogen-like $^{140}$Pr and $^{142}$Pm ions at the GSI ESR storage ring (Litvinov, 2007; Litvinov, 2008), and adapts the atomic interferometry technique used for Lamb shift measurements in hydrogen (Sokolov, 1976). The work enables a direct test of whether the gyrogravitational ratio of elementary particles differs from unity, complementing neutron interferometry proposals by Mashhoon (2000) that have not yet been performed.
