# The Nuclear Tensor Interaction in Covariant Energy Density Functional

> The origin of the nuclear tensor interaction in the covariant energy density functional (EDF) is presented in this work, associated with the Fock diagrams of Lorentz scalar and vector couplings. With this newly obtained relativistic formalism of the nuclear tensor interaction, more distinct tensor effects are found in the Fock diagrams of the Lorentz scalar and vector couplings, as compared to the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor channels. A unified and self-consistent treatment on both the nuclear tensor and spin-orbit interactions, which dominate the spin-dependent features of the nuclear force, is then achieved by the relativistic models. Moreover, careful analysis on the tensor strengths indicates the reliability of the nuclear tensor interaction in the covariant EDF for exploring the nuclear structure, excitation and decay modes.

## Key claim
The nuclear tensor interaction in covariant energy density functionals originates not only from the Fock diagrams of isovector meson couplings (π and ρ), as previously thought, but also from the Fock diagrams of isoscalar Lorentz scalar (σ) and vector (ω) couplings, with the latter producing tensor effects almost one order of magnitude larger than the former.

## Method
The authors derived a relativistic formalism (Eqs. 2-5) to extract the tensor force components hidden in the Fock diagrams of σ-scalar, ω-vector, π-pseudo-vector, and ρ-tensor couplings within the density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory. They tested this formalism by calculating contributions to spin-orbit splittings of neutron orbits in ⁴⁸Ca using the DDRHF functional PKA1, and verified the tensor sum rule. The tensor strength factors α and β from the relativistic formalism were compared with those from non-relativistic Skyrme forces SGII+Te3 and Skxta.

## Key numbers
- Tensor effects from isoscalar σ-S and ω-V couplings are "almost one order of magnitude larger" than those from isovector π-PV, ρ-V, and ρ-T channels, as shown in Fig. 2 of the paper.
- Interaction matrix elements V^T for σ-S coupling range from -1.72 × 10⁻¹ MeV to 3.43 × 10⁻¹ MeV for neutron orbits in ⁴⁸Ca (Table I).
- Interaction matrix elements V^T for ω-V coupling range from -1.08 × 10⁻¹ MeV to 0.57 × 10⁻¹ MeV for neutron orbits in ⁴⁸Ca (Table I).
- The tensor sum rule (2j_>+1)V^T_{j_>j'} + (2j_<+1)V^T_{j_<j'} = 0 is exactly fulfilled under the limit of identical radial wave functions for spin partner states.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of nuclear tensor interactions beyond the conventional view that they arise only from π and ρ meson exchanges (Otsuka 2005, Long 2008). It provides a relativistic formalism that unifies the treatment of tensor and spin-orbit interactions within covariant density functional theory, enabling self-consistent descriptions of nuclear structure, excitations, and decay modes. The results explain the success of the DDRHF+RPA model (Liang 2008, Liang 2012) in describing spin-isospin resonances, and offer a path to constrain tensor strengths directly from parametrization of DDRHF functionals, in contrast to the empirical tuning required in Skyrme models like SGII+Te3 (Bai 2011) and Skxta (Brown 2006).
