# Pairing-correlations and particle-number projection methods

> A systematic study of the pairing-correlations derived from various particle-number projection methods is performed in an exactly soluble cranked-deformed shell model Hamiltonian. It is shown that most of the approximate particle-number projection methods including the method of Lipkin-Nogami, which is used quite extensively in nuclear structure studies, breakdown in the weak pairing limit. The results obtained from the recently formulated number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) equations, on the other hand, are in complete agreement with the exact solutions of the model Hamiltonian. The pairing-energy calculated from PHFB method is shown to be finite in all the studied limits. More importantly, the numerical work involved in the solution of the PHFB equations appears to be comparable to the solution of the bare HFB equations.

## Key claim

Most approximate particle-number projection methods, including the widely used Lipkin-Nogami method, break down in the weak pairing limit; only the recently formulated number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) equations yield results in complete agreement with exact solutions of the model Hamiltonian across all studied regimes.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic comparison of several particle-number projection methods (HFB, projection after variation PAV, Lipkin-Nogami LN, and the new PHFB) using an exactly soluble cranked-deformed shell model Hamiltonian with six particles in a j=11/2 shell. They tested three different pairing interactions (monopole, monopole plus quadrupole, and full delta-interaction) and examined the total energy, pairing energy, aligned angular momentum, and dynamic moment of inertia as functions of pairing strength, rotational frequency, and deformation.

## Key numbers

- HFB pairing-energy collapses to zero for pairing strength G less than or equal to 0.4 (in the paper's energy units), while PHFB pairing-energy remains non-zero for all values of G.
- For the monopole interaction, HFB pairing-energy vanishes for rotational frequency hbar-omega greater than or equal to 0.45 G, while PHFB pairing-energy remains non-zero for all rotational frequencies.
- The LN aligned angular momentum crossing frequency is lower than PHFB by hbar-omega = 0.1 G for the monopole interaction.
- HFB pairing-energy collapses at deformation kappa = 5 G for monopole and (monopole plus quadrupole) interactions, and at kappa = 5.5 G for the full delta-interaction; PHFB pairing-energy remains finite for all studied deformation values.
- For superdeformed deformation kappa = 5.5, HFB dynamic moment of inertia J^(2) is quite large compared to PHFB J^(2) for all three pairing interactions.

## Context

This work extends the study of particle-number projection methods in nuclear structure physics, building on the earlier formulation of projected HFB equations by Sheikh and Ring (2000). It directly tests the Lipkin-Nogami method (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964) and projection after variation against exact solutions, showing that these approximate methods fail in the weak pairing limit where fluctuations become important. The results enable the replacement of the Lipkin-Nogami method with the PHFB approach in realistic nuclear structure calculations, as the numerical work involved in solving the PHFB equations is comparable to that of the bare HFB equations.
