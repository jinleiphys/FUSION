# Density correlations under global and local charge conservation

> This work presents a formalism to compute spatial $n$-point correlations of a conserved charge density in a large thermal system in the canonical ensemble, with explicit results presented up to 4th order. The resulting correlators contain local and balancing terms expressed through the grand-canonical susceptibilities for any equation of state. The new formalism is used to introduce a Gaussian local baryon number conservation in (spatial) rapidity in heavy-ion collisions at LHC conditions through a modulation of the balancing term. Connection to the conservation volume $V_c$ approach is established, indicating that the latter is appropriate for observables within a limited range around midrapidity. Quantitative analysis of the experimental data of the ALICE Collaboration on net proton cumulants in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV shows significant evidence for local baryon conservation.

## Key claim
The paper presents a formalism for computing spatial n-point correlations of a conserved charge density in the canonical ensemble, showing that experimental net proton variance data from ALICE at √sNN = 5.02 TeV provides "significant evidence for local baryon conservation" rather than just global conservation, with the caveat that the analysis neglects possible baryon interactions and baryon annihilation.

## Method
The author derives explicit expressions for 2-, 3-, and 4-point spatial density correlators by partitioning the total volume into subvolumes and using the maximum term method in the thermodynamic limit to express joint cumulants in terms of grand-canonical susceptibilities. The formalism is then applied to heavy-ion collisions at LHC conditions by introducing a Gaussian modulation of the balancing term in the 2-point correlator to model local baryon number conservation in spatial rapidity. The net proton variance is computed by integrating the 2-point correlator over spatial rapidity with a Bernoulli filter based on blast-wave model acceptance probabilities, and the results are fitted to ALICE data on net proton cumulants in central Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers
- Total fireball volume V_tot = 10.2 dV/dy, corresponding to η_max = 5.1, based on Gaussian volume distribution with width σ_V = 4.12 ± 0.10 from charged multiplicity measurements
- Fitting the largest acceptance point (0.6 < p < 2.0 GeV/c, |η̃| < 0.8) yields σ_y = 0.78^{+0.19}_{-0.16} for the Gaussian width of local baryon conservation
- This corresponds to conservation volume V_c = (1.95^{+0.48}_{-0.39}) dV/dy, about five times smaller than the total fireball volume V_tot ≈ 10.2 dV/dy
- The efficiency factor q ≈ 0.33 is used to distinguish protons from all baryons based on HRG model estimation at freeze-out

## Context
This work extends the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) developed by Vovchenko (2020) for correcting cumulants for global charge conservation effects, generalizing it to compute n-point spatial density correlators. It establishes a connection to the conservation volume V_c approach used by ALICE (2022, 2024) to constrain local conservation of QCD charges, showing that V_c estimates should be regarded as an upper bound on the correlation range. The formalism enables future applications to lower-energy collisions at RHIC, SPS, HADES, and CBM, and can be extended to incorporate lattice QCD susceptibilities, excluded volume effects, baryon clustering, and multiple conserved charges.
