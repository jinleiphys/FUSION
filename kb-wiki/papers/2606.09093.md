# Hyperon polarization in isobaric Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: TRENRo3D + CLVisc with an initial longitudinal flow gradient

> We present a theoretical study of global and azimuthal-angle-dependent $Λ$ hyperon polarization in isobaric $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV using the TRENTo3D initial condition model coupled to the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc. A longitudinal flow velocity gradient, controlled by $f_v$, is introduced into TRENTo3D for the first time, providing an essential source of initial vorticity in this symmetric isobaric system. Within the isothermal polarization framework, the model provides a simultaneous description of STAR measurements of the global polarization $-P^{y}$ (centrality, $p_T$, and $η$ dependences) and the azimuthal modulation coefficients $P_{y,\mathrm{c0}}$ and $P_{y,\mathrm{c2}}$. The $p_T$ dependence reflects the competition between thermal vorticity and shear contributions: the thermal term decreases with $p_T$, while the shear term rises and increasingly shapes the curvature of the total polarization. In this decomposition, $P_{y,\mathrm{c2}}$ is dominantly shear-driven and serves as a clean probe of shear-induced polarization. Scans of $f_v$, $k_T$, and nuclear structure provide complementary constraints on the initial state, while the bulk-viscosity dependence is also examined; the five nuclear structure configurations from the STAR isobar blind analysis yield nearly indistinguishable polarization. For $P_z$, the isothermal scenario captures the azimuthal modulation but overpredicts the high-$p_T$ modulation amplitude, and comparison with the standard thermal treatment shows that neither scenario achieves a unified description of all observables.

## Key claim

The TRENTo3D initial condition model, extended with an initial longitudinal flow velocity gradient controlled by parameter $f_v$, can simultaneously describe STAR measurements of global and azimuthal-angle-dependent Lambda hyperon polarization in isobaric Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV, but only within the isothermal polarization framework; neither the isothermal nor the standard thermal treatment achieves a unified description of all polarization observables, particularly for the longitudinal polarization component P_z.

## Method

The authors coupled the TRENTo3D initial condition model to the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic code CLVisc, introducing a longitudinal flow velocity gradient parameterized by $f_v$ into the initial energy-momentum tensor to generate initial vorticity in this symmetric isobaric system. They computed Lambda hyperon polarization on an approximately isothermal freeze-out hypersurface using the decomposition into thermal vorticity and thermal shear contributions, evaluating both the isothermal limit (where temperature-gradient terms vanish) and the standard thermal treatment for comparison. Systematic scans were performed over the longitudinal flow gradient parameter $f_v$, the fragmentation-induced geometric tilt parameter $k_T$, five nuclear structure configurations from the STAR isobar blind analysis, and bulk viscosity values.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal flow gradient parameter $f_v = 0.10$ and fragmentation tilt parameter $k_T = 0.33$ GeV provide simultaneous description of STAR measurements
- Nuclear deformation parameters for Zr-96: $R_0 = 5.02$ fm, $a_0 = 0.52$ fm, quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.06$, octupole deformation $\beta_3 = 0.20$
- Specific shear viscosity fixed at $\eta/s = 0.08$, consistent with Bayesian extractions from RHIC
- Freeze-out energy density $e_{\text{frz}} = 0.4$ GeV/fm$^3$, corresponding to hadronization temperature approximately $T_H \simeq 155$ MeV
- Kinematic region for Lambda hyperons: $p_T \in [0.5, 3.0]$ GeV and $y \in [-1, 1]$
- Thermal vorticity term decreases with $p_T$ while shear term overtakes it near $p_T \approx 2.5$ GeV in 20-60% centrality
- Five nuclear structure configurations from STAR isobar blind analysis yield nearly indistinguishable polarization results

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of hyperon polarization in Au+Au and Pb+Pb systems (STAR Collaboration, 2018; ALICE Collaboration, 2021) to isobaric Zr+Zr collisions, addressing the challenge that three-dimensional initial fireball geometry and longitudinal flow profiles remain poorly constrained by existing data (Shen, 2020; Ryu, 2021; Jiang, 2023). The framework enables systematic testing of whether hyperon polarization is sensitive to isobaric nuclear-structure variations and to the three-dimensional initial condition, while the comparison between isothermal and standard thermal treatments highlights the ongoing "sign puzzle" in longitudinal polarization P_z (Florkowski, 2019; Becattini, 2021; Palermo, 2024). The finding that P_{y,c2} is dominantly shear-driven provides a clean probe of shear-induced polarization for future experimental and theoretical studies.
