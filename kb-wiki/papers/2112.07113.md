# Structure of two- and three-alpha systems in cold neutron matter

> We present stability and structure of two- and three-alpha systems embedded in dilute cold neutron matter. By solving a few-alpha Schrödinger equation with quasiparticle properties, i.e., effective mass and induced two- and three-alpha interactions, which are evaluated in terms of Fermi polarons, it is shown that $^8 \mathrm{Be}$ and the Hoyle state become bound at densities of about $10^{-4}$ and $10^{-3}$ of the saturation density, respectively. It is also seen that, under cold neutron matter environment, both systems become smaller than the corresponding systems in vacuum. Our results would affect astrophysical models for stellar collapse and neutron star mergers, as well as relevant reaction rates for nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^8$Be and the Hoyle state become bound in dilute cold neutron matter at densities of about $10^{-4}$ and $10^{-3}$ of the saturation density, respectively, but only when the Fermi momentum of the background neutrons exceeds thresholds of approximately $0.08$ fm$^{-1}$ and $0.16$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Method

The authors solved a few-alpha Schrödinger equation using a multi-alpha cluster model with a Hamiltonian that includes the effective mass of the alpha particle and medium-induced two- and three-alpha interactions, all evaluated from a Fermi polaron description of an alpha impurity in neutron matter. The wave functions were expanded in fully symmetrized correlated Gaussian basis functions, with variational parameters optimized by the stochastic variational method, and forbidden Pauli states were eliminated via a projection method.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of $^8$Be becomes bound for Fermi momentum $k_F \gtrsim 0.08$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to approximately $10^{-4}$ of the saturation density ($0.16$ fm$^{-3}$).
- The Hoyle state becomes bound for $k_F \gtrsim 0.16$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to approximately $10^{-3}$ of the saturation density.
- Both the $^8$Be ground state and the Hoyle state shrink monotonically in size (root-mean-square pair distance) for larger $k_F$, due to the attractive induced two-alpha interaction.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi polaron treatment of a single alpha particle in neutron matter by Nakano (2020) to two- and three-alpha systems, introducing medium-induced few-body interactions. The results suggest that alpha clustering and the triple-alpha reaction could be significantly modified in astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, and they motivate future studies of finite $2\alpha + Xn$ and $3\alpha + Xn$ systems to bridge the gap between few-neutron and infinite-neutron-matter descriptions.
