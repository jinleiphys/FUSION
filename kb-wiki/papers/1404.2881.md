# Higher-order corrections to electron-scattering multipoles

> A procedure is suggested for calculating electro-excitation multipoles to order $1/M^{2}_{N}$ with only the operators required in the calculations to order $1/M_{N}$. It is also shown that calculations to order $1/M^{2}_{N}$ cannot account for the contributions of a fully relativistic calculation of the transverse response.

## Key claim
The paper suggests a procedure for calculating electro-excitation multipoles to order \(1/M^{2}_{N}\) using only the operators required for calculations to order \(1/M_{N}\), but demonstrates that such calculations to order \(1/M^{2}_{N}\) cannot account for the contributions of a fully relativistic calculation of the transverse response, particularly for bound states at low energy transfer.

## Method
The authors start from a covariant electromagnetic current density and apply a "direct Pauli reduction," approximating the lower component of the Dirac wave function by \(\tilde{G} = (\vec{\sigma}\cdot\vec{p})/(E+M)\eta_U\). They then use an algebraic manipulation to express the lower component in terms of the upper component and its radial derivative, allowing second-order terms to be included without calculating new operators beyond those in the first-order multipoles. The procedure is tested by comparing transverse and longitudinal form factors from the full relativistic continuum shell model (in the Tamm-Dancoff approximation) with those from the approximate reduction, for electro-excitation of states in \(^{16}\)O.

## Key numbers
- Transverse form factor for the \(2^{-}\) state in \(^{16}\)O: the full relativistic calculation (solid line) and the approximation of Eq. (16) (dashed line) show a "very large difference," with the difference almost entirely in the first-order terms.
- For the \(3^{-}\) state in \(^{16}\)O, the transverse form factor again shows a large difference between the full calculation and the approximation, while the longitudinal form factor calculations are "nearly indistinguishable" because the substitution contributes only to \(1/M^{2}_{N}\) terms.
- The lower component of the \(d_{5/2^{-}}p^{-1}_{1/2}\) proton configuration for the \(2^{-}\), \(T=0\) state is plotted, showing the full lower component and the approximation of Eq. (16) are "very different."
- For the \(d_{5/2}\) proton in the continuum, the difference between the lower component and the approximation decreases as energy increases; by 120 MeV the difference is small.

## Context
This work extends prior investigations of relativity in \((e,e^{\prime}p)\) reactions by authors such as Udias (1995, 1998, 1999) and Hedayati-Poor and Johansson (1994), who used Dirac equations with vector and scalar potentials. It contrasts with the Foldy-Wouthuysen approach of McVoy and Van Hove (1962) and the direct Pauli reduction discussed by Friar (1994). The paper enables future non-relativistic shell model calculations to include second-order relativistic corrections for transverse responses, but warns that such corrections are insufficient for bound states at low energy transfer, where fully relativistic treatments are needed.
