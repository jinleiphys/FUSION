# Orbifold equivalence for finite density QCD and effective field theory

> In the large N_c limit, some apparently different gauge theories turn out to be equivalent due to large N_c orbifold equivalence. We use effective field theory techniques to explore orbifold equivalence, focusing on the specific case of a recently discovered relation between an SO(2N_c) gauge theory and QCD. The equivalence to QCD has been argued to hold at finite baryon chemical potential, μ_B, so long as one deforms the SO(2N_c) theory by certain "double-trace" terms. The deformed SO(2N_c) theory can be studied without a sign problem in the chiral limit, in contrast to SU(N_c) QCD at finite μ_B. The purpose of the double-trace deformation in the SO(2N_c) theory is to prevent baryon number symmetry from breaking spontaneously at finite density, which is necessary for the equivalence to large N_c QCD to be valid. The effective field theory analysis presented here clarifies the physical significance of double-trace deformations, and strongly supports the proposed equivalence between the deformed SO(2N_c) theory and large N_c QCD at finite density.

## Key claim
The paper uses effective field theory (EFT) to argue that a deformed SO(2N_c) gauge theory remains large-N_c equivalent to QCD at finite baryon chemical potential, provided the double-trace deformation prevents spontaneous breaking of U(1)_B symmetry; the equivalence holds non-perturbatively within the regime where the low-energy EFT is valid, i.e., for chemical potentials μ ∼ m_π and quark masses small compared to Λ_SO(2N_c).

## Method
The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory for the deformed SO(2N_c) gauge theory based on the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking, and analyze the vacuum structure in the presence of two types of double-trace deformations: a chirally symmetric deformation V_+ and a chirally non-symmetric deformation V_-. They determine the orientation of the vacuum and the conditions for U(1)_B symmetry breaking by studying the effective potential for the meson and bmeson fields, using the EFT to compute the effects of the deformations non-perturbatively in the 't Hooft coupling λ = g_YM^2 N_c.

## Key numbers
- The U(1)_B symmetry of the undeformed SO(2N_c) theory breaks when the chemical potential exceeds half the pion mass, μ ≥ m_π/2, where m_π ∼ √(m_q) at μ=0.
- The three-meson interaction vertex scales as N_c^{-1/2}, a four-meson vertex scales as 1/N_c, and the matrix element for a current to create a meson from the vacuum scales as N_c^{1/2}.
- The deformation parameter C has dimensions of inverse mass; the deformation potential is V = C^2 Σ_{a,b} S_ab^† S_ab for the simplest case, where S_ab = ψ_a^T C γ_5 ψ_b.

## Context
This work extends the orbifold equivalence framework of Kovtun, Ünsal, and Yaffe (2003, 2004) to finite-density QCD, building on the proposal by Cherman and Tiburzi (2010) that an SO(2N_c) gauge theory can be used to circumvent the fermion sign problem at finite baryon chemical potential. The double-trace deformation technique generalizes the approach of Ünsal and Yaffe (2008) for protecting center symmetry in large-N_c volume independence, here applied to protect U(1)_B symmetry. The results support the validity of the equivalence at the non-perturbative level and enable future lattice Monte Carlo studies of large-N_c QCD at finite density using the deformed SO(2N_c) theory.
