# Relativistic Effect on Low-Energy Nucleon-Deuteron Scattering

> The relativistic effect on differential cross sections, nucleon-to-nucleon and nucleon-to-deuteron polarization transfer coefficients, and the spin correlation function, of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering is investigated employing several three-dimensional relativistic three-body equations and several nucleon-nucleon potentials. The polarization transfer coefficients are found to be sensitive to the details of the nucleon-nucleon potentials and the relativistic dynamics employed, and prefer trinucleon models with the correct triton binding energy. (To appear in Phys. Rev. C)

## Key claim

The polarization transfer coefficients of nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering are sensitive to both the nucleon-nucleon potential model and the relativistic dynamics employed, but this sensitivity largely reflects the triton binding energy produced by each model rather than providing independent information about the tensor force or off-shell behavior.

## Method

The authors solved three-dimensional relativistic three-body equations (propagators labeled A, B, C, and D) and the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering, using separable Yamaguchi and Tabakin nucleon-nucleon potentials in the S-wave spin-triplet and spin-singlet channels. They calculated differential cross sections, spin correlation functions, and nucleon-to-nucleon and nucleon-to-deuteron polarization transfer coefficients at laboratory energies of 10, 22.7, and 100 MeV, and compared results across five representative models with varying triton binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy for Model A (nonrelativistic, triplet Y singlet Y): 10.65 MeV
- Triton binding energy for Model B (relativistic propagator B, triplet T-2 singlet Y): 8.34 MeV
- Triton binding energy for Model C (relativistic propagator A, triplet T-2 singlet Y): 8.14 MeV
- Triton binding energy for Model D (relativistic propagator C, triplet T-2 singlet Y): 7.91 MeV
- Triton binding energy for Model E (nonrelativistic, triplet Y singlet T-2): 7.69 MeV
- Relativistic correction to triton binding energy ranges from -0.3 MeV to +0.7 MeV across different propagators and potentials
- 48 mesh points needed for converged relativistic scattering results with a rotation angle of 5 degrees, versus 24 mesh points for nonrelativistic results
- The minimum of the nucleon-to-nucleon polarization transfer coefficient K^{y'}_y occurs at approximately theta_cm = 110 degrees at 10 MeV and 22.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous nonrelativistic benchmark calculations of the three-nucleon system (referred to as "bench" and "bench2" in the text) and earlier relativistic studies of the trinucleon bound state and S-wave nd scattering length by Adhikari and Tomio (1994). It contradicts the conclusion of Clajus et al. (cited as "glo") that the nucleon-to-nucleon polarization transfer coefficient K^{y'}_y at 22.7 MeV favors the weak tensor force of the Bonn A potential over the Paris potential, arguing instead that the better agreement is due to the Bonn A potential producing a triton binding energy (8.38 MeV) closer to the experimental value (8.48 MeV) than the Paris potential (7.47 MeV). The results enable future studies to recognize that relativistic dynamics alone cannot be distinguished from variations in two- and three-nucleon potentials in nonrelativistic calculations, and that accurate experimental data for the noncentrality measures Delta K and Delta K' are needed to extract information about tensor forces and higher partial waves.
