# 1/N_c- expansion of the quark condensate at finite temperature

> Previously the quark and meson properties in a many quark system at finite temperature have been studied within effective QCD approaches in the Hartree approximation. In the present paper we consider the influence of the mesonic correlations on the quark self-energy and on the quark propagator within a systematic $1/N_c$- expansion. Using a general separable ansatz for the nonlocal interaction, we derive a selfconsistent equation for the $1/N_c$ correction to the quark propagator. For a separable model with cut-off formfactor, we obtain a decrease of the condensate of the order of 20\% at zero temperature. A lowering the critical temperature for the onset of the chiral restoration transition due to the inclusion of mesonic correlations is obtained what seems to be closer to the results from lattice calculations.

# 1/N_c Expansion of the Quark Condensate at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The inclusion of mesonic correlations beyond the Hartree approximation, via a systematic 1/N_c expansion, reduces the absolute value of the quark condensate by about 20% at zero temperature and lowers the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration, bringing it closer to lattice QCD results; however, the calculation is restricted to a separable model with a cut-off formfactor and uses a pole approximation for the meson propagators, and higher orders in 1/N_c may become important near the phase transition.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral quark model with a nonlocal separable four-point interaction and compute the quark self-energy and propagator to next-to-leading order in a 1/N_c expansion. They derive a self-consistent equation for the 1/N_c correction to the quark propagator, including both the correction to the Hartree self-energy and the dynamical mesonic self-energy contributions from scalar and pseudoscalar channels. The mesonic correlations are treated in the pole approximation, where the RPA-resummed quark-antiquark T-matrix is expanded around the meson bound-state poles, and the resulting two-loop Matsubara sums are evaluated numerically at finite temperature using a cut-off formfactor.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate in the Hartree approximation is <ūu>^H = -(250 MeV)^3, with a dynamical quark mass m^H = 300 MeV at zero temperature.
- At zero temperature, the inclusion of 1/N_c mesonic correlations decreases the absolute value of the condensate by 20% compared to the Hartree approximation.
- The critical temperature for chiral restoration in the Hartree approximation is T_c ~ 200 MeV; including mesonic correlations shifts this to lower temperatures, closer to lattice results where T ≈ 150 MeV for N_f = 2.
- The prefactor Z = 1/(1 - J^σ(0)) rescales the 1/N_c correction and is approximately Z ~ 4 for the NJL model at zero temperature.
- The model parameters are fixed to reproduce M_π = 140 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, and the quark condensate at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter at finite temperature within effective QCD approaches, which were limited to the Hartree (mean-field) approximation (e.g., Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994; Klevansky, 1992). It addresses the incomplete treatment of mesonic fluctuations in earlier 1/N_c expansions, as noted by Volkov (1993) and Snyderman (1991), and provides a consistent regularization of the two-loop diagrams that removes the ambiguity in the choice of the second momentum cut-off present in earlier NJL model calculations (Zhuang et al., 1994; Lemmer, 1995). The result that mesonic correlations lower the critical temperature brings the model closer to lattice QCD results for N_f = 2 (1995). This work enables a more complete description of the quark-meson plasma at low temperatures, where mesonic degrees of freedom are expected to dominate, and provides a foundation for including scattering states beyond the pole approximation in future studies.
