# Enhanced Magnetic Quadrupole Moments in Nuclei with Octupole Deformation and their CP-violating effects in molecules

> Nuclei with an octupole deformation have a non-zero electric octupole moment, electric dipole moment (EDM), Schiff moment and magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) in the intrinsic frame which rotates with the nucleus. In a state with definite angular momentum in the laboratory frame, these moments are forbidden by parity (P) and time reversal invariance (T) conservation, meaning their expectation values vanish due to nuclear rotation. However, nuclei with an octupole deformation have doublets of close opposite parity rotational states with the same spin, which are mixed by T,P-odd nuclear forces. This mixing produces the orientation of the nuclear axis along nuclear spin and all moments existing in the intrinsic frame appear in the laboratory frame (provided the nuclear spin I is sufficiently large to allow such moment). Such a mechanism produces enhanced T,P-violating nuclear moments. This enhancement also takes place in nuclei with a soft octupole vibration mode. Schiff moments in such nuclei have been calculated in previous works. In the present paper we consider the magnetic quadrupole moment which appears in isotopes with nuclear spin $I \geq 1$. Magnetic interaction between the nuclear MQM and electrons produces an atomic EDM and T,P-violating nuclear spin - molecular axis interaction constants for molecules in electronic states with non-zero electron angular momentum. Measurements of these constants may be used to test CP-violation theories and search for axion dark matter in atomic, molecular and solid state experiments. Potential candidate nuclei include 153Eu, 161Dy, 221Fr, 223Fr, 223Ra, 223Rn, 225Ac, 227Ac, 229Th, 229Pa, 233U and 235U. We subsequently consider molecules containing these nuclei (EuO, EuN+, RaF, AcO, AcN+, AcF+, ThO and ThF+).

## Key claim

Nuclei with octupole deformation or a soft octupole vibration mode can produce enhanced time-reversal and parity-violating magnetic quadrupole moments (MQMs) in the laboratory frame, provided the nuclear spin I is at least 1, due to the mixing of close opposite-parity rotational doublet states by T,P-odd nuclear forces.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the nuclear MQM in the laboratory frame by combining the intrinsic-frame MQM expectation value (proportional to the octupole deformation parameter β₃ and the nucleon magnetic moment projection) with the mixing coefficient α between opposite-parity rotational doublet states. They then applied this formalism to 13 candidate nuclei with known octupole deformation parameters, level splittings, and magnetic moments, expressing the resulting MQMs in terms of the CP-violating π-meson-nucleon interaction constants (ḡ₀, ḡ₁, ḡ₂), the QCD θ-parameter, and quark chromo-EDMs.

## Key numbers

- For ²²⁹Pa, the MQM expressed in terms of the QCD θ-parameter is M(θ) ≈ 1200 × 10⁻²⁸ θ̄ e cm², the largest value in the table, due to its extremely small opposite-parity level splitting of 0.220 keV.
- For ²²⁷Ac, the MQM is M(η) ≈ 13 × 10⁻³⁴ η e cm², with a level splitting ΔE = 23.4 keV and β₃ = 0.12.
- For ²³⁷Np, the MQM is M(θ) ≈ 1.5 × 10⁻²⁷ θ̄ e cm², with a level splitting ΔE = 59.5 keV and β₃ = 0.12.
- For ¹⁵³Eu, the MQM is M(η) ≈ 18 × 10⁻³⁴ η e cm², with a level splitting ΔE = 97.4 keV and β₃ = 0.15.
- For ²²⁵Ac, the MQM is M(η) ≈ 9.9 × 10⁻³⁴ η e cm², with a level splitting ΔE = 40.1 keV and β₃ = 0.10.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of enhanced Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei by Auerbach, Spevak, and Engel (2000) to the magnetic quadrupole moment, which is not subject to the electrostatic screening that suppresses the Schiff moment. The authors note that the MQM enhancement (1-2 orders of magnitude over spherical nuclei) is smaller than the Schiff moment enhancement (2-3 orders of magnitude) but that the MQM interaction with electrons is not screened, making it a competitive probe. The results enable searches for CP-violation and axion dark matter through measurements of atomic EDMs and T,P-violating molecular interaction constants in molecules such as ThO, RaF, and AcF⁺, building on experimental efforts with ²²⁵Ra by the RaEDM collaboration and theoretical work on collective MQMs by Flambaum (1994) and Lackenby (2018).
