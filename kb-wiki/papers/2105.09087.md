# Heavy baryon spectrum with chiral multiplets of scalar and vector diquarks

> Chiral effective theory of scalar and vector diquarks is formulated according to the linear sigma model. The main application is to describe the ground and excited states of singly heavy baryons with a charm or bottom quark. Applying the potential quark model between the diquark and the heavy quark ($Q=c, b$), we construct a heavy-quark--diquark model. The spectra of the positive- and negative-parity states of $Λ_Q$, $Σ_Q$, $Ξ^{(')}_Q$ and $Ω_Q$ are obtained. The masses and interaction parameters of the effective theory are fixed partly from the lattice QCD data and also from fitting low-lying heavy baryon masses. We find that the negative parity excited states of $Ξ_Q$ (flavor $\bar{\bf 3}$) are different from those of $Λ_Q$, because of the inverse hierarchy of the pseudoscalar diquark. On the other hand, $Σ_Q, Ξ'_Q$ and $Ω_Q$ (flavor ${\bf 6}$) baryons have similar spectra. We compare our results of the heavy-quark--diquark model with experimental data as well as the quark model.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a chiral effective theory for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector diquarks and uses a heavy-quark–diquark potential model to predict the spectra of singly heavy baryons, finding that the negative-parity excited states of Ξ_Q (flavor anti-triplet) differ from those of Λ_Q due to the inverse mass hierarchy of the pseudoscalar diquark, while the flavor-sextet baryons Σ_Q, Ξ'_Q, and Ω_Q have similar spectra.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian for diquarks based on SU(3)_R × SU(3)_L symmetry and the linear sigma model, including scalar/pseudoscalar (0^±) and vector/axial-vector (1^±) diquarks in color anti-triplet representation. They then build a nonrelativistic heavy-quark–diquark potential model with spin-independent (Coulomb + linear + constant) and spin-dependent (spin-spin, spin-orbit, tensor) terms, solving the Schrödinger equation via the Gaussian expansion method. Diquark masses and potential parameters are fixed partly from lattice QCD data and partly by fitting low-lying heavy baryon masses from experiment, using three different quark-model parameter sets (Yoshida, Silvestre-Brac, and Barnes).

## Key numbers

- The ud scalar diquark mass M_qq(0^+) is taken as 725 MeV from lattice QCD input.
- The spin-averaged masses used as inputs for vector/axial-vector diquarks: M(Σ_c, 1/2^+, 3/2^+) = 2496.60 MeV, M(Ξ'_c, 1/2^+, 3/2^+) = 2623.52 MeV, and M_ρ(Λ_c, 1/2^-, 3/2^-) = 2927.65 MeV.
- The chiral invariant mass for vector/axial-vector diquarks m_V0 is about 714 MeV (S-pot. and B-pot.) or 708 MeV (Y-pot.).
- The mass of the ud axial-vector diquark M_qq(1^+) is 973 MeV (Y-pot.), 1013 MeV (S-pot.), or 1019 MeV (B-pot.).
- The mass of the ud vector diquark M_qq(1^-) is 1447 MeV (Y-pot.), 1527 MeV (S-pot.), or 1540 MeV (B-pot.).
- The spin-orbit/tensor coupling strength η is set to 0.2494 for all potentials.
- The spin-spin coefficient for charm κ_c is 0.8586 (Y-pot.), 1.1570 (S-pot.), or 2.7258 (B-pot.); for bottom κ_b is 0.6635 (Y-pot.) or 0.8145 (S-pot.).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral effective theory for scalar and pseudoscalar diquarks (Kim et al., 2021) to include vector and axial-vector diquarks, enabling the description of flavor-sextet baryons (Σ_Q, Ξ'_Q, Ω_Q) in addition to the anti-triplet baryons (Λ_Q, Ξ_Q). It builds on the diquark chiral effective theory of CETdiquark (2015) and the heavy-quark–diquark potential model of scalar (2021), and it compares results with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2020) and with quark-model calculations by Yoshida et al. (2015), Silvestre-Brac (1996), and Barnes et al. (2005). The work enables future studies of diquark behavior under chiral symmetry restoration by varying the chiral condensate.
