# Heavy baryon spectrum with chiral multiplets of scalar and vector diquarks

> Chiral effective theory of scalar and vector diquarks is formulated according to the linear sigma model. The main application is to describe the ground and excited states of singly heavy baryons with a charm or bottom quark. Applying the potential quark model between the diquark and the heavy quark ($Q=c, b$), we construct a heavy-quark--diquark model. The spectra of the positive- and negative-parity states of $Œõ_Q$, $Œ£_Q$, $Œû^{(')}_Q$ and $Œ©_Q$ are obtained. The masses and interaction parameters of the effective theory are fixed partly from the lattice QCD data and also from fitting low-lying heavy baryon masses. We find that the negative parity excited states of $Œû_Q$ (flavor $\bar{\bf 3}$) are different from those of $Œõ_Q$, because of the inverse hierarchy of the pseudoscalar diquark. On the other hand, $Œ£_Q, Œû'_Q$ and $Œ©_Q$ (flavor ${\bf 6}$) baryons have similar spectra. We compare our results of the heavy-quark--diquark model with experimental data as well as the quark model.

## Method

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral effective theory for scalar and pseudoscalar diquarks (Kim et al., 2021) to include vector and axial-vector diquarks, enabling the description of flavor-sextet baryons (Œ£_Q, Œû'_Q, Œ©_Q) in addition to the anti-triplet baryons (Œõ_Q, Œû_Q). It builds on the diquark chiral effective theory of CETdiquark (2015) and the heavy-quark‚Äìdiquark potential model of scalar (2021), and it compares results with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2020) and with quark-model calculations by Yoshida et al. (2015), Silvestre-Brac (1996), and Barnes et al. (2005). The work enables future studies of diquark behavior under chiral symmetry restoration by varying the chiral condensate.
