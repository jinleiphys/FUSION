# Inclusive spectra and Bose-Einstein correlations in small thermal quantum systems

> The spectra and correlation of identical particles emitted from small local-equilibrium sources are considered. The size of the system is defined by the negative part of the parabolic falling chemical potential. The analytical solution of the problem is found for the case of inclusive measurements. It is shown that in the case where the size of the system is comparable to the thermal wavelength of the particles, the spectra and correlation functions are far from the quasiclassical approximation expected for large systems, and observed femtoscopy scales (interferometry radii) will be essentially smaller than the Gaussian radii of the source. If the maximum value of the chemical potential approaches the critical one, specific for the system, one can consider the possibility of the Bose-Einstein condensation. In such a case the reduction of the intercept of the correlation function for inclusive measurements takes place. The results can be used for the searching of femtoscopy homogeneity lengths in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies.

# Inclusive spectra and Bose-Einstein correlations in small thermal quantum systems

## Key claim
The paper shows that when the size of a thermal boson source is comparable to the thermal wavelength of the emitted particles, the observed femtoscopy (interferometry) radii become significantly smaller than the Gaussian radii of the source, and the correlation function intercept is reduced. This effect is demonstrated analytically for inclusive measurements in a model where the system size is defined by a parabolic falling chemical potential, and the results are relevant for interpreting femtoscopy data in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors construct a local-equilibrium statistical operator for a free scalar field in (d+1)-dimensional space-time, using a parabolic chemical potential profile to define the system size. They solve the resulting integral equation for the two-operator averages analytically by introducing Gaudin-type transformed creation operators and applying Mehler's formula for Hermite functions. The inclusive single-particle spectrum and two-particle correlation function are then computed from these averages, and the interferometry radii are extracted by Gaussian fitting of the correlation function in the low relative-momentum region. For systems with high ground-state occupancy, a partially coherent ensemble is introduced where the ground state becomes a Glauber coherent state while excited states follow grand canonical statistics.

## Key numbers
- Thermal wavelength for kaons at T=155 MeV: approximately 0.75 fm
- Thermal wavelength for pions at T=155 MeV: approximately 1.35 fm
- System sizes considered for correlation function comparison: R=0.75 fm, R=1.25 fm, and R=3 fm
- Temperature at freeze-out: T=155 MeV (approximately 10^12 K)
- Half-momentum used in correlation function calculations: k=0.15 GeV/c
- Critical ground-state occupancy for coherence onset: N_c=2 (assumed fixed value)
- Minimum total particle number for coherence description at R=1.5 fm: <N>=5

## Context
This work extends the approach of Sinyukov (2015) on correlation femtoscopy for small systems with fixed multiplicity to the case of inclusive measurements in thermal systems. It provides an analytical solution that complements numerical hydrokinetic model (HKM) descriptions of proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV and 13 TeV by the CMS, ATLAS, ALICE, and LHCb collaborations. The results enable the extraction of femtoscopy homogeneity lengths in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, and the formalism shares mathematical features with trapped Bose gas descriptions by Naraschewski (1998) and Wong (2015), though the physical interpretation differs in the treatment of coherent condensates.
