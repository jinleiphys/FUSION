# Deconfinement and Hadron Properties at Extremes of Temperature and Density

> After introducing essential, qualitative concepts and results, we discuss the application of Dyson-Schwinger equations to QCD at finite T and mu. We summarise the calculation of the critical exponents of two-light-flavour QCD using the chiral and thermal susceptibilities; and an algebraic model that elucidates the origin of an anticorrelation between the mu- and T-dependence of a range of meson properties. That model also provides an algebraic understanding of why the finite-T behaviour of bulk thermodynamic properties is mirrored in their mu-dependence, and why meson masses decrease with mu even though f_pi and -<q-bar q> increase. The possibility of diquark condensation is canvassed. Its realisation is uncertain because it is contingent upon an assumption about the quark-quark scattering kernel that is demonstrably false in some applications; e.g., it predicts the existence of coloured diquarks in the strong interaction spectrum, which are not observed.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential provides a nonperturbative, continuum framework that can simultaneously study confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, but the possibility of diquark condensation is uncertain because it depends on an assumption about the quark-quark scattering kernel that is demonstrably false in some applications (e.g., it predicts coloured diquarks not observed in the strong interaction spectrum).

## Method

The authors apply Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to QCD at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ, using a simple infrared-dominant Ansatz for the dressed-gluon propagator that reduces the quark DSE from an integral equation to an algebraic equation. They solve the quark DSE in the rainbow truncation, then calculate bound state properties using the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons. The chiral and thermal susceptibilities are used to extract critical exponents of two-light-flavour QCD, and a separable model with meson-diquark bosonisation is employed to study the possibility of diquark condensation.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at μ=0: T_c^X = 152 MeV (from chiral susceptibility analysis)
- Critical exponents take mean-field values: β=0.5, δ=3.0 (z_h=0.67, z_t=0.33)
- For the algebraic model with η=1.06 GeV: T_c^0 = 0.170 GeV at μ=0; critical chemical potential at T=0: μ_c^0 = 0.3 GeV
- At nuclear matter density (μ_0=260 MeV): M_ρ(μ_0) ≈ 0.75 M_ρ(μ=0), M_φ(μ_0) ≈ 0.85 M_φ(μ=0)
- At twice nuclear matter density (2ρ_0): M_ω = M_ρ ≈ 0.72 M_ρ(μ=0), M_φ ≈ 0.85 M_φ(μ=0)
- At the T=0 critical chemical potential (~3ρ_0): M_ω = M_ρ ≈ 0.65 M_ρ(μ=0), M_φ ≈ 0.80 M_φ(μ=0)
- Diquark bound state masses from ladder truncation: m_{0+}^{ud} ≈ 740 MeV, m_{1+}^{ud} ≈ 950 MeV, m_{1-}^{ud} ≈ m_{0-}^{ud} ≈ 1500 MeV
- For the separable model: Λ=0.71 GeV yields f_π = 93 MeV and -⟨q̄q⟩ = (238 MeV)^3 at T=0=μ

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, building on earlier DSE studies of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (Roberts and Williams, 1994; and the review by Roberts and Schmidt). The algebraic model elucidates the anticorrelation between T- and μ-dependence of meson properties previously observed in more sophisticated Ansätze (such as those by Bender et al., 1996). The results enable exploration of the QCD phase diagram in regimes inaccessible to lattice simulations, particularly at finite chemical potential, and provide a framework for understanding the relationship between deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions.
