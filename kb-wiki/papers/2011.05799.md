# Wavefunction structure in quantum many-fermion systems with $k$-body interactions: conditional $q$-normal form of strength functions

> For finite quantum many-particle systems modeled with say $m$ fermions in $N$ single particle states and interacting with $k$-body interactions ($k \leq m$), the wavefunction structure is studied using random matrix theory. Hamiltonian for the system is chosen to be $H=H_0(t) + λV(k)$ with the unperturbed $H_0(t)$ Hamiltonian being a $t$-body operator and $V(k)$ a $k$-body operator with interaction strength $λ$. Representing $H_0(t)$ and $V(k)$ by independent Gaussian orthogonal ensembles (GOE) of random matrices in $t$ and $k$ fermion spaces respectively, first four moments, in $m$-fermion spaces, of the strength functions $F_κ(E)$ are derived; strength functions contain all the information about wavefunction structure. With $E$ denoting the $H$ energies or eigenvalues and $κ$ denoting unperturbed basis states with energy $E_κ$, the $F_κ(E)$ give the spreading of the $κ$ states over the eigenstates $E$. It is shown that the first four moments of $F_κ(E)$ are essentially same as that of the conditional $q$-normal distribution given in: P.J. Szabowski, Electronic Journal of Probability {\bf 15}, 1296 (2010). This naturally gives asymmetry in $F_κ(E)$ with respect to $E$ as $E_κ$ increases and also the peak value changes with $E_κ$. Thus, the wavefunction structure in quantum many-fermion systems with $k$-body interactions follows in general the conditional $q$-normal distribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that strength functions (local density of states) in quantum many-fermion systems with \(k\)-body interactions follow a conditional \(q\)-normal distribution, which naturally produces asymmetry in the strength functions as the unperturbed basis state energy \(E_\kappa\) moves toward the spectrum edges, a feature that the simpler \(q\)-normal distribution cannot capture.

## Method

The authors model the Hamiltonian as \(H = H_0(t) + \lambda V(k)\) with \(H_0(t)\) and \(V(k)\) represented by independent Gaussian orthogonal ensembles (GOE) in \(t\)-fermion and \(k\)-fermion spaces respectively, using the embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (EGOE) formalism. They derive the first four moments of the strength functions \(F_\kappa(E)\) in \(m\)-fermion spaces using the binary correlation approximation (BCA) in the dilute limit (\(N \to \infty\), \(m/N \to 0\), \(k \ll m\), \(t \ll m\)), and compare these moments to those of the conditional \(q\)-normal distribution \(f_{CqN}\).

## Key numbers

- Variance of the strength function: \(\sigma^2(y) = 1 - \xi^2\), independent of \(E_\kappa\).
- Skewness of the strength function: \(\gamma_1(y) = -\xi (1-q) y / \sqrt{1-\xi^2}\), where \(y = \hat{E}_\kappa\) is the standardized unperturbed energy.
- Excess (kurtosis) of the strength function: \(\gamma_2(y) = (q-1) + [(1-q)^2 \xi^2 y^2 + \xi^2 (1-q^2)]/(1-\xi^2)\).
- Correlation coefficient: \(\xi = \sigma_{H_0} / \sigma_H = \sqrt{ \binom{m}{t} / [ \binom{m}{t} + \lambda^2 \binom{N}{t}^{-1} \binom{N}{k} \binom{m}{k} ] }\).
- \(q\)-parameter for \(H_0\): \(q^h = \binom{m}{t}^{-1} \binom{m-t}{t}\).
- \(q\)-parameter for \(V\): \(q^v = \binom{m-k}{k} / \binom{m}{k}\).
- Cross-term \(q\)-parameter: \(q^{hv} = \binom{m-t}{k} / \binom{m}{k}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by Kota and collaborators (2019) showing that strength functions for states at the center of the spectrum follow a \(q\)-normal distribution, and by Vyas and Kota (2021) showing that the conditional \(q\)-normal distribution describes numerical results for all \(E_\kappa\). The paper provides the first analytical derivation of the moments confirming the conditional \(q\)-normal form, contradicting the possibility that the bivariate \(q\)-normal distribution (which requires \(\mu_{PQ} = \mu_{QP}\)) could represent the joint density \(\rho(E_\kappa, E)\). This enables analytical formulas for the number of principal components (NPC) and information entropy (\(S^{info}\)) in wavefunctions, and has implications for understanding thermalization, fidelity decay, and out-of-time order correlators (OTOC) in finite quantum many-body systems.
