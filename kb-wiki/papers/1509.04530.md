# Heavy-flavor transport

> The formation of a hot deconfined medium (Quark-Gluon Plasma) in high-energy nuclear collisions affects heavy-flavor observables. In the low/moderate-pT range transport calculations allow one to simulate the propagation of heavy quarks in the plasma and to evaluate the effect of the medium on the final hadronic spectra: results obtained with transport coefficients arising from different theoretical approaches can be compared to experimental data. Finally, a discussion of possible effects on heavy-flavor observables due to the possible formation of a hot-medium in small systems (like in p-A collisions) is presented.

# Heavy-flavor transport

## Key claim

Transport calculations can describe heavy-quark propagation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma and allow comparison of results obtained with transport coefficients from different theoretical approaches to experimental data, but the extraction of transport coefficients from charm measurements is limited if the low-pT region cannot be accessed; for beauty, the momentum-diffusion coefficients stay close to each other and quite constant over an extended momentum range, potentially enabling tighter constraints with the ALICE upgrade.

## Method

The author reviews the Boltzmann equation for heavy-quark phase-space evolution and its approximation via the relativistic Langevin equation, which combines a friction term and a noise term set by momentum-diffusion coefficients kappa_L and kappa_T. Transport coefficients from weak-coupling (HTL+pQCD) calculations and lattice-QCD simulations are compared. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, heavy-quark propagation is simulated with the POWLANG setup, where heavy quarks below a decoupling temperature are combined with light thermal partons into strings fragmented via PYTHIA's Lund model. For small systems (p-Pb), a smooth hydrodynamic background is constructed from Glauber-MC fluctuating initial conditions and evolved with the ECHO-QGP viscous hydrodynamics code, then used as a background for heavy-quark transport.

## Key numbers

- Lattice-QCD estimate of the momentum-diffusion coefficient at zero momentum is "much larger" than the perturbative (weak-coupling) value (no explicit numerical value given for either)
- For beauty quarks, kappa_L and kappa_T values stay close to each other and "quite constant" for an extended momentum range (no explicit numerical values given)
- D-meson elliptic flow from recombination with light thermal partons in p-Pb collisions can reach up to approximately 5% (from Fig. 19 caption: "v2 up to ~5%")
- Heavy-flavor electron nuclear modification factor in central d-Au collisions at RHIC was found to "slightly exceed 1" (no explicit numerical value given)
- D-meson RpA in minimum-bias p-Pb collisions at LHC is "compatible with 1 within the large systematic error-bars" (no explicit numerical value given)

## Context

This contribution reviews the status of heavy-flavor transport in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, extending the work of Gossiaux (2008) and Uphoff (2011) on direct Boltzmann equation solutions, and comparing weak-coupling results from Alberico (2013) with lattice-QCD estimates from Francis (2015). The POWLANG setup (Beraudo, 2014) implements in-medium hadronization via recombination with thermal partons, an alternative to the coalescence approach of vanHees (2007). The discussion of small systems extends the observation of collective effects in p-Pb and d-Au collisions (ridge correlations, flow harmonics) to heavy-flavor observables, following the blast-wave analysis of Sickles (2013) and the muon-hadron correlation measurements of Adam (2015). Future work should focus on heavy-flavor correlations and beauty at low pT to enable more solid extraction of transport coefficients.
