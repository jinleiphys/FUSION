# Determination of the Collins-Soper kernel from Lattice QCD

> This work presents a determination of the quark Collins-Soper kernel, which relates transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) at different rapidity scales, using lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). This is the first such determination with systematic control of quark mass, operator mixing, and discretization effects. Next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic matching is used to match lattice-calculable distributions to the corresponding TMDs. The continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results are consistent with several recent phenomenological parameterizations of the Collins-Soper kernel and are precise enough to disfavor other parameterizations.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD determination of the Collins-Soper kernel with systematic control of quark mass, operator mixing, and discretization effects, using next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic matching. The continuum-extrapolated results are consistent with several recent phenomenological parameterizations and precise enough to disfavor other parameterizations, but the lattice data are not sufficient to establish a clear preference between functional forms for the nonperturbative part of the kernel.

## Method

The Collins-Soper kernel is extracted from lattice QCD calculations on three ensembles of gauge fields with lattice spacings a = 0.15, 0.12, and 0.09 fm, using the Large-Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET) framework. Bare quasi-TMD wavefunctions are computed from matrix elements of staple-shaped Wilson line operators in pion states at multiple boost momenta, then renormalized using the RI/xMOM scheme and converted to MSbar. The kernel is obtained from ratios of Fourier-transformed renormalized quasi-TMD wavefunctions at different momenta, with uNNLL perturbative matching corrections, and results are averaged over Dirac structures, momentum combinations, and x values in [0.3, 0.7] before fitting to a parameterization that includes discretization effects proportional to a/b_T.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.15 fm (32^3 x 48 volume), a = 0.12 fm (48^3 x 64 volume), a = 0.09 fm (64^3 x 96 volume)
- Pion boost momenta up to P^z = 2.32 GeV (on the a = 0.15 fm ensemble)
- Best-fit parameters for the AIC-preferred model: c_0 = 0.032(12), k_1 = 0.22(8), with chi^2/dof = 0.39
- The BLNY parameterization fit gives g_2 = 0.085(26) with chi^2/dof = 0.58, consistent with the phenomenological result g_2 = 0.053(24) from Isaacson et al. (2023)
- The SV19 phenomenological result c_0^SV19 = 0.043(11) with B_NP^SV19 = 1.9(2) GeV is consistent with the lattice result
- The ART23 global fit gives c_0^ART23 = 0.037(6)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the Collins-Soper kernel by Shanahan et al. (2019, 2020, 2021) and others (LatticeParton 2020, Li 2021, Schlemmer 2021, LPC 2022, Shu 2023, Chu 2023, Avkhadiev 2023) by adding systematic control of discretization effects through multiple lattice spacings. The results are compared with phenomenological parameterizations from BLNY (Landry 2002), SV19 (Scimemi 2019), Pavia19 (Bacchetta 2019), MAP22 (Bacchetta 2022), ART23 (Moos 2023), and IFY23 (Isaacson 2023), and enable future joint fits of lattice QCD results with experimental data from Drell-Yan and SIDIS processes.
