# Advances in perturbative thermal field theory

> The progress of the last decade in perturbative quantum field theory at high temperature and density made possible by the use of effective field theories and hard-thermal/dense-loop resummations in ultrarelativistic gauge theories is reviewed. The relevant methods are discussed in field theoretical models from simple scalar theories to non-Abelian gauge theories including gravity. In the simpler models, the aim is to give a pedagogical account of some of the relevant problems and their resolution, while in the more complicated but also more interesting models such as quantum chromodynamics, a summary of the results obtained so far are given together with references to a few most recent developments and open problems.

## Key claim

This review argues that the apparent breakdown of perturbative QCD at finite temperature below ridiculously high temperatures (~10⁵ GeV) is much too pessimistic, and that weak-coupling methods can still be useful even in strongly coupled QCD if proper use is made of effective field theories and the fact that nontrivial spectral data of quasiparticles can absorb much of the strong interactions.

## Method

The authors review the progress of the last decade in perturbative quantum field theory at high temperature and density, focusing on effective field theories that exploit dimensional reduction for static quantities and hard-thermal-loop resummation for dynamical quantities. They discuss these methods across a range of models from simple scalar φ⁴ theory to non-Abelian gauge theories including gravity, providing pedagogical accounts of the problems and their resolution in simpler models while summarizing results and open problems for more complex theories like QCD.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop thermal mass in scalar φ⁴ theory is m_th^(1) = gT, where g is the coupling and T the temperature
* In the large-N limit of scalar O(N) theory, the tachyon (Landau pole) mass is m_tachyon² = -μ̄² exp(8π²/3g² + 2), where μ̄ is a renormalization scale
* Perturbative QCD at finite temperature was thought to lose predictive power below temperatures ~10⁵ GeV, where the coupling becomes small enough for an ideal gas description

## Context

This review extends the material presented in textbooks by Kapusta (1989), Das (1997), and LeBellac (1996), with particular overlap with the latter which already covers hard thermal loop resummations. The work addresses the technical difficulties in deriving analytical weak-coupling results for equilibrium and near-equilibrium properties of ultrarelativistic matter, relevant to early-universe cosmology, compact star astrophysics, and heavy-ion collider experiments at RHIC and LHC. The review enables further progress by showing how effective field theories and hard-thermal-loop resummation can improve perturbative calculations, and identifies open problems such as the nonperturbative nature of nonabelian magnetostatic fields and collinear physics requiring infinite-loop resummations.
