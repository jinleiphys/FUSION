# High energy scattering in Quantum Chromodynamics

> In this series of three lectures, we discuss several aspects of high energy scattering among hadrons in Quantum Chromodynamics. The first lecture is devoted to a description of the parton model, Bjorken scaling and the scaling violations due to the evolution of parton distributions with the transverse resolution scale. The second lecture describes parton evolution at small momentum fraction x, the phenomenon of gluon saturation and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). In the third lecture, we present the application of the CGC to the study of high energy hadronic collisions, with emphasis on nucleus-nucleus collisions. In particular, we provide the outline of a proof of high energy factorization for inclusive gluon production.

## Key claim

These lectures argue that high-energy hadronic scattering in QCD can be treated perturbatively even in the regime of moderate momentum transfers, where gluon saturation and multiple scatterings dominate, by using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory; the claim is conditional on the existence of a high-energy factorization theorem for inclusive gluon production, which the authors outline but do not fully prove.

## Method

The first lecture derives the parton model and DGLAP evolution equations using the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) in the Bjorken limit of Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS). The second lecture derives the BFKL equation and its non-linear extension, the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, in the eikonal approximation, and presents the Color Glass Condensate effective theory as a framework for gluon saturation. The third lecture applies the CGC to nucleus-nucleus collisions, resumming infinite sets of diagrams by solving classical equations of motion for gauge fields to compute inclusive gluon and quark spectra at leading and next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC, center-of-mass energies reach up to sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon; the LHC will collide nuclei at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV per nucleon.
* In a collision at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV between gold nuclei at RHIC, 99% of the multiplicity comes from hadrons with transverse momentum p_perp below 2 GeV.
* The kinematical variable x in DIS is defined as x = Q^2 / (2 nu), where Q^2 = -q^2 > 0 and nu = P dot q, with 0 <= x <= 1.

## Context

These lectures extend the parton model and the DGLAP evolution equations (Dokshitzer, Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, Parisi, 1970s) to small-x physics, where the BFKL equation (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov, 1970s) and its non-linear generalization (Balitsky, Kovchegov, 1990s) become relevant. The CGC framework builds on earlier work on gluon saturation and provides a systematic approach to high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions; the authors note that understanding the high-energy factorization they outline may hold the key to understanding early thermalization in heavy ion collisions.
