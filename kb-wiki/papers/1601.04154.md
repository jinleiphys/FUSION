# Towards a microscopic understanding of nucleon polarizabilities

> We outline a microscopic framework to calculate nucleon Compton scattering from the level of quarks and gluons within the covariant Faddeev approach. We explain the connection with hadronic expansions of the Compton scattering amplitude and discuss the obstacles in maintaining electromagnetic gauge invariance. Finally we give preliminary results for the nucleon polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a microscopic framework for calculating nucleon Compton scattering from quarks and gluons using the covariant Faddeev approach, but the presented results for nucleon polarizabilities are only preliminary ballpark estimates because the quark Compton vertex calculation is extremely sensitive to numerics and the extraction of the momentum dependence is still too noisy.

## Method

The author constructs the Compton scattering amplitude from quark-level diagrams within a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev equations, calculating the quark propagator, nucleon Faddeev amplitude, and quark Compton vertex (with 128 tensor structures) from their respective equations. To make the calculation tractable, diagram (a) containing the three-quark scattering matrix is approximated by summing the leading hadronic contributions (the Delta resonance), diagram (c) is neglected, and only diagram (b) is calculated without further approximations. The Compton form factors are extracted using a transverse tensor basis that separates gauge parts, ensuring the results remain free of kinematic singularities even when gauge invariance is broken by the incomplete calculation.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability PDG value: α = 11.2(4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton magnetic polarizability PDG value: β = 2.5(4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- The rainbow-ladder Faddeev approach reproduces octet and decuplet ground state masses within 5-10%
- The nucleon polarizabilities α and β are related to Compton form factors f₁ and f₂ by {α+β, β} = {f₁, f₂} × α_QED/m³ in the limit where all kinematic variables are zero

## Context

This work extends the covariant three-body Faddeev approach established by Eichmann (2009) from mass spectra and form factors to the more challenging Compton scattering process. It aims to connect hadronic descriptions such as chiral perturbation theory (Griesshammer, 2012) and dispersion relations (Drechsel, 2002; Schumacher, 2013) with the handbag picture of deeply virtual Compton scattering, providing a common quark-level framework valid in all kinematic regions. The approach enables future calculations of spin polarizabilities, two-photon corrections to nucleon form factors relevant to the proton radius puzzle, and can be adapted to other processes like pion electroproduction.
