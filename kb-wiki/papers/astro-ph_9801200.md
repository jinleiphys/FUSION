# Medium effects and the structure of neutron stars in the effective mass bag model

> One of the most intriguing consequence of the extreme conditions inside neutron stars is the possibility of the natural existence of a deconfined strange quark matter phase in the high density interior of the star. The equation of state (EOS) of strange quark matter (SQM) was recently improved in the framework of the MIT bag model by including medium effects. It was found that medium effects increase the energy per baryon of SQM and therefore lower the stability of this phase. In this work we investigate the influence of medium effects on the structure of hybrid stars within this model. We found that the medium effects reduce the extent of a pure SQM phase in the interior of an hybrid star significantly in favor of a mixed phase of quark and hadronic matter.

## Key claim

Medium effects in the effective mass bag model, parameterized by the strong coupling constant \(g\), reduce the extent of a pure strange quark matter phase in hybrid star interiors significantly in favor of a mixed phase of quark and hadronic matter, but for a wide range of the coupling constant (\(g \lessapprox 3\), \(\alpha_s \lessapprox 0.72\)) strange quark matter is still present at least as a mixed phase.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to calculate mass-radius relations for pure strange quark matter stars and hybrid stars. For the quark matter equation of state they used the effective mass bag model, treating quarks as quasi-particles with medium-dependent effective masses from the hard dense loop quark self energy, with a bag constant of \(B^{1/4}=165\) MeV and current quark masses \(m_u=m_d=0\), \(m_s=150\) MeV. For the hadronic phase they used an equation of state from the nonlinear Walecka model including nucleons, hyperons (\(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^-\)), electrons, muons, and \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\) mesons with a compression modulus of \(K=300\) MeV, and for subnuclear densities the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland equation of state. The deconfinement phase transition was constructed using the weaker condition of global charge neutrality, allowing a mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: \(B^{1/4}=165\) MeV (\(B\approx 96\) MeV/fm\(^3\))
- Current strange quark mass: \(m_s=150\) MeV
- Compression modulus of hadronic EOS: \(K=300\) MeV
- For a canonical star of mass \(M=1.4 M_\odot\) with coupling constant \(g=1.5\) (\(\alpha_s\approx0.18\)), the pure quark phase radius shrinks from \(R \approx 6\) km (with no medium effects) to \(R \approx 3\) km
- At \(g=2\) (\(\alpha_s\approx0.32\)), the pure strange quark matter core vanishes completely
- For \(g \gapprox 3.5\), a phase transition to strange quark matter is completely suppressed

## Context

This work extends the effective mass bag model of Schertler (1997) by applying it to the structure of hybrid stars, following the phase transition construction of Glendenning (1992). The results contradict the expectation that medium effects would simply eliminate quark matter; instead, they transform pure quark cores into mixed phases. This enables future studies of how the mixed phase, expected to form a crystalline lattice of various geometries as discussed by Glendenning (1992) and others, affects observable neutron star properties such as mass and radius.
