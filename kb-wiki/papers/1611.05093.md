# Condensate of excitations in moving superfluids

> A possibility of the condensation of excitations with a non-zero momentum in rectilinearly moving and rotating superfluid bosonic and fermionic (with Cooper pairing) media is considered in terms of a phenomenological order-parameter functional at zero and non-zero temperature. The results might be applicable to the description of bosonic systems like superfluid $^4$He, ultracold atomic Bose gases, charged pion and kaon condensates in rotating neutron stars, and various superconducting fermionic systems with pairing, like proton and color-superconducting components in compact stars, metallic superconductors, and neutral fermionic systems with pairing, like the neutron component in compact stars and ultracold atomic Fermi gases. Order parameters of the "mother" condensate in the superfluid and the new condensate of excitations, corresponding energy gains, critical temperatures and critical velocities are found.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that in superfluids moving with a velocity exceeding the Landau critical velocity, a condensate of excitations (CoE) with non-zero momentum can form as a second-order phase transition, provided the back-reaction on the "mother" condensate is accounted for and the parameter χ_T remains less than unity. The authors hedge that this occurs under specific conditions where the normal component's friction timescale is much longer than the CoE formation rate, and where the relative velocity between superfluid and normal components remains below the critical value for mother condensate destruction.

## Method
The authors constructed a phenomenological order-parameter functional based on the Ginzburg-Landau (Ginzburg-Pitaevskii) formalism, coupling the mother superfluid condensate to a new condensate of excitations with momentum k₀. They imposed momentum conservation between the moving fluid and the CoE, minimized the free-energy density functional at zero and finite temperature, and derived expressions for the CoE amplitude, final flow velocity, and energy gain. For fermionic systems with Cooper pairing, they compared the CoE formation to Cooper pair breaking and the Rogers-Bardeen gap reduction effect.

## Key numbers
- Landau critical velocity for fermionic systems: v_c^L ≈ Δ/p_F, where Δ is the pairing gap and p_F the Fermi momentum.
- For fermionic systems in the BCS weak-coupling limit, the parameter χ_T = 3b_T ρ̄/k₀² ≪ 1, reducing to the inequality Δ ≪ ε_F.
- The new critical temperature for fermionic BCS systems: T̃_c/T_c ≈ 1 - (v - v_c^L)/v_F, for v > v_c^L.
- For superfluid ⁴He at t = 0.5 (T/T_c = 0.5), v_c^L = 59 m/s; at t = 0.1, v_c^L = 55 m/s.
- The maximal second critical velocity for BCS fermionic systems: v_c2^max ≈ v_c^L + v_F.
- For cold fermionic systems, the energy gain from CoE formation compared to full destruction of pairing is δĒ_gap = -3ρ̄(v_c^L)²/4 at v > v_c2,f^L = (e/2)v_c^L.

## Context
This work extends the earlier proposal by Pitaevskii (1984) for roton condensation in superfluid helium in a capillary at T=0, and the study by Voskresensky (1993) for various relativistic and non-relativistic media. It generalizes these to finite temperatures, both bosonic and fermionic superfluids with Cooper pairing, and includes back-reaction on the mother condensate. The results enable predictions for superfluid ⁴He in bulk, ultracold atomic Bose and Fermi gases, and astrophysical applications such as pion and kaon condensates in rotating neutron stars and r-mode stability in rapidly rotating pulsars, building on work by Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky (2015).
