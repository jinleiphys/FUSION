# Strangeness fluctuations from $K-π$ interactions

> Motivated by recent lattice QCD studies, we explore the effects of interactions on strangeness fluctuations in strongly interacting matter at finite temperature. We focus on S-wave $Kπ$ scattering and discuss the role of the $K_0^*(800)$ and $K^*(1430)$ resonances. The interaction contribution is obtained within the S-matrix formulation of thermodynamics, using the empirical $Kπ$ phase shifts as input. We find that the simplified treatment of the interactions in this channel, employed in the hadron resonance gas approach, leads to an overestimate of the strangeness fluctuations. Thus, our calculation indicates that broad resonances must be treated with caution, when modeling QCD thermodynamics in the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The S-matrix treatment of S-wave Kπ interactions shows that the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model systematically overestimates strangeness fluctuations when including the κ (K₀*(800)) resonance as a zero-width particle, and that the κ channel alone cannot resolve the discrepancy between lattice QCD and HRG results for the strangeness susceptibility.

## Method

The authors parametrize empirical Kπ S-wave phase shifts in the isospin I=1/2 and I=3/2 channels using an energy-dependent Breit-Wigner form for the κ and K₀*(1430) resonances plus a repulsive hard-sphere background, with parameters from Ishida et al. (1997). They then apply the S-matrix formulation of thermodynamics (Beth-Uhlenbeck form) to compute the interaction contribution to the thermodynamic potential and strangeness susceptibility, using the derivative of the phase shift with respect to invariant mass as an effective spectral weight. The scattering length is varied between 0.18 mπ⁻¹ and 0.4 mπ⁻¹ to cover the range of uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The κ resonance mass used in the HRG treatment is mκ = 0.682 GeV with degeneracy four.
- The fitted parameters for the I=1/2 channel are: ακ = 3.0098 GeV², Mκ = 0.905 GeV, αK₀* = 1.437 GeV², MK₀* = 1.41 GeV, rcI=1/2 = 3.57 GeV⁻¹.
- The I=3/2 channel uses rcI=3/2 = 0.81 GeV⁻¹, yielding a scattering length of 0.112 mπ⁻¹.
- The S-matrix result for the strangeness susceptibility lies between the Breit-Wigner results with and without the κ resonance, and the cancellation from the I=3/2 channel reduces the enhancement from S-wave Kπ scattering by 70%.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies by Bazavov et al. (2014) and Borsanyi et al. (2011, 2013) that showed HRG results for strangeness susceptibilities fall below lattice data, suggesting missing interaction strength in the strange sector. The authors apply the S-matrix approach of Dashen (1969) and Beth-Uhlenbeck (1937), previously used for πN interactions by Weinhold et al. (1997), to the κ channel. The results contradict the naive expectation that adding the κ resonance as a zero-width particle would resolve the lattice-HRG discrepancy, and instead demonstrate that broad resonances require careful treatment beyond the HRG approximation. This enables future systematic studies of other strangeness-carrying channels to identify the missing interaction strength.
