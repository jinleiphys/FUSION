# Non-classical transport with angular-dependent path-length distributions. 1: Theory

> This paper extends a recently introduced theory describing particle transport for random statistically homogeneous systems in which the distribution function p(s) for chord lengths between scattering centers is non-exponential. Here, we relax the previous assumption that p(s) does not depend on the direction of flight Ω; this leads to an extended generalized linear Boltzmann equation that includes angular-dependent cross sections, and to an extended generalized diffusion equation that accounts for anisotropic behavior resulting from the statistics of the system.

## Key claim

This paper extends the previously developed generalized linear Boltzmann equation (GLBE) for non-classical particle transport by relaxing the assumption that the chord-length distribution function \(p(s)\) is independent of the direction of flight \(\bm\Omega\); the resulting theory includes angular-dependent cross sections \(\Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s)\) and yields a generalized diffusion equation that accounts for anisotropic behavior arising from the statistics of the random medium.

## Method

The authors derive a new GLBE for the angular flux \(\psi(\bm x, \bm\Omega, s)\) by formulating a conservation equation in phase space that includes the path-length \(s\) as an independent variable, with the total cross section \(\Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s)\) depending on both direction and path-length. They then derive integral equation formulations that eliminate the explicit \(s\) dependence, and perform an asymptotic diffusion limit using a scaling where \(\Sigma_t = O(\varepsilon^{-1})\), \(1-c = O(\varepsilon^2)\), and \(Q = O(\varepsilon)\), with \(\varepsilon \ll 1\), to obtain a generalized diffusion equation.

## Key numbers

- The mean distance-to-collision (mean free path) in a given direction \(\bm\Omega\) is given by \(s_{\bm\Omega}(\bm\Omega) = \int_0^{\infty} e^{- \int_0^s \Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s') ds'} \, ds\) (Equation 3.16).
- The overall mean free path \(\langle s \rangle\) is \(\langle s \rangle = \int_{4\pi} \xi(\bm\Omega) s_{\bm\Omega}(\bm\Omega) d\Omega\) (Equation 3.18), where \(\xi(\bm\Omega)\) is the probability distribution for direction of flight.
- The conditional distribution function for distance-to-collision in a given direction is \(q(\bm\Omega,s) = \Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s) e^{-\int_0^s \Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s') \; ds'}\) (Equation 3.7).

## Context

This work extends the non-classical transport theory introduced by Larsen (2007) and Larsen (2011), which assumed the chord-length distribution was independent of direction, and which was applied to 2-D pebble bed reactor cores. The new angular-dependent formulation captures anisotropic transport effects observed in random systems such as pebble beds near boundaries (Vasques, 2009; Vasques, 2013), where particles travel longer distances in directions parallel to walls. The theory enables modeling of systems where the geometry of the random medium (e.g., gravitational arrangement of pebbles) produces direction-dependent diffusion coefficients, and the authors note that a companion paper will discuss how \(\Sigma_t(\bm\Omega,s)\) might be numerically derived from hypothesized correlations between scattering centers.
