# Improving the cold quark-matter pressure via soft interactions at N3LO

> The propagation of long-wavelength gluons through a dense QCD medium at high baryon chemical potential mu_B is qualitatively modified by the effects of screening, arising from scatterings off the high-momentum quarks in the medium. This same screening phenomenon also impacts gluons occurring in loop corrections to the pressure of cold quark matter, leading to contributions from the parametric scale Sqrt(alpha_s)*mu_B, starting at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in the strong coupling constant alpha_s. At next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), interactions between these long-wavelength gluonic modes contribute to the pressure. These interaction corrections have recently been computed in arXiv:2103.07427 and arXiv:2103.05658, and the inclusion of these interactions slightly improves the convergence of the equation of state of cold quark matter. In these proceedings, we present these results and provide details summarizing how this lengthy calculation was performed.

## Key claim

Including the recently computed N3LO corrections from interactions between long-wavelength screened gluonic modes slightly improves the convergence of the cold quark-matter equation of state, though the result still retains a dependence on the UV renormalization scale and the factorization scale, with the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS) value for the factorization scale producing the most conservative (largest) renormalization-scale dependence.

## Method

The calculation separates the pressure into hard, soft, and mixed contributions from different momentum scales, with the soft contribution at N3LO arising from two-loop diagrams in the hard-dense-loop (HDL) effective theory. The authors computed these diagrams in dimensional regularization using the MSbar scheme in Feynman gauge, employing a strategic decomposition of the resummed gluon propagator to isolate ultraviolet-divergent terms, then performing angular integrals analytically and evaluating the remaining finite contributions via Monte Carlo integration using the CUBA library.

## Key numbers

- The soft contribution to the pressure at N3LO is expressed as coefficients from the expansion: p_{-2} = 11π²/24, p_{-1} ≈ 11.6840(15), p_0 = 17.150(7)
- The PMS value for the factorization scale is Λ_h^PMS = exp[-p_{-1}/(2p_{-2})] m_E, where m_E is the screening mass
- The cold quark-matter equation of state from pQCD is considered reliable at baryon densities n_B ≳ 40 n_0, with n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ (nuclear saturation density)
- The renormalization scale is chosen as Λ̅ = X μ_B with central value X = 2 and range X ∈ [1,4]

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of the cold quark-matter equation of state from N2LO (Kurkela 2009) to partial N3LO by including interactions between soft screened gluonic modes, which were computed in Gorda (2021) and Gorda (2021). The result contrasts with the high-temperature case where soft modes limit convergence (Blaizot 2003), and the p_{-2} coefficient appears related to the one-loop beta function of pure gauge theory, a connection further investigated in Fernandez (2021) for all-orders resummation. These improvements may have implications for neutron-star matter equations of state, as astrophysical constraints from Annala (2019) suggest the neutron-star matter equation of state follows the pQCD cold quark-matter behavior to densities lower than where pQCD calculations are currently converged.
