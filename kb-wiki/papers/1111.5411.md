# Extended nonlocal chiral-quark model for the D- and B-meson weak-decay constants

> In this work, we construct a phenomenological effective model for the heavy-light quark systems, which consist of (u,d,c,b) quarks, i.e. extended nonlocal chiral-quark model (ExNLChQM) to compute the heavy-meson weak-decay constants f_D and f_B. ExNLChQM is based on the heavy-quark effective field theory as well as the dilute instanton-vacuum configuration. In ExNLChQM, a certain portion of the heavy-meson mass is considered to be generated from the nontrivial QCD vacuum contribution, being similar to that for the light quarks in usual instanton approaches. Hence, the effective heavy- and light-quark masses become momentum-dependent and play the role of a smooth UV regulator. Employing a generic external-field method applied to the effective action from ExNLChQM, we obtain f_D = (169.28 ~ 234.57) MeV and f_B = (165.41 ~ 229.21) MeV from the numerical results, depending on different model parameters. These values are in relatively good agreement with experimental data and various theoretical estimations. We also discuss the heavy-quark effects on the QCD vacuum, and the decay constants f_D^* and f_B^* in terms of the heavy-quark spin symmetry.

# Extended nonlocal chiral-quark model for the D- and B-meson weak-decay constants

## Key claim

The paper constructs a phenomenological effective model (ExNLChQM) that combines heavy-quark effective field theory with instanton-inspired nonlocal chiral-quark model to compute heavy-meson weak-decay constants, obtaining values that depend on different choices for the momentum-dependence parameterization of the effective quark masses (Models I, II, and III). The claimed ranges f_D = (169.28–234.57) MeV and f_B = (165.41–229.21) MeV are not single predictions but rather span the results across these model parameter choices.

## Method

The author extends the SU(2) nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM), derived from the dilute instanton vacuum, to SU(4) flavor including (u,d,c,b) quarks, incorporating heavy-quark effective field theory (HQEFT) to treat the heavy quarks. The effective heavy- and light-quark masses are made momentum-dependent using a parameterized form with a renormalization scale Λ ≈ 591 MeV (from the average instanton size), and the effective heavy-quark mass at zero momentum is determined phenomenologically from the observed D- and B-meson masses. The weak-decay constants are then computed via an external-field method applied to the effective action, with the integration rendered finite by the momentum-dependent masses acting as UV regulators.

## Key numbers

* f_D = 234.57 MeV for Model I (n_q = n_Q = 1) and f_D = 169.28 MeV for Model II (n_q = n_Q = 2), with the overall range (169.28–234.57) MeV across all parameter choices.
* f_B = 229.21 MeV for Model I (n_q = n_Q = 1) and f_B = 165.41 MeV for Model II (n_q = n_Q = 2), with the overall range (165.41–229.21) MeV across all parameter choices.
* The effective light-quark mass at zero momentum is taken as M_{q,0} ≈ 345 MeV, and the renormalization scale is Λ ≈ 591 MeV from the average instanton size ρ ≈ 1/3 fm.
* The effective heavy-quark mass at zero momentum is estimated as M_{Q,0} ≈ 244.57 MeV from the D-meson mass (1869.57 MeV) and M_{Q,0} ≈ 254.17 MeV from the B-meson mass (5279.17 MeV), using m_c = 1290.0 MeV and m_b = 4670.0 MeV.
* The ratios f_{D*}/f_D and f_{B*}/f_B from heavy-quark spin symmetry are in the range 1.01–1.03.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM) previously developed for light quarks (Praszalowicz 2001, Nam 2006) to include heavy-light systems by incorporating HQEFT (Georgi 1990, Neubert 1993). It provides an alternative to other approaches for computing heavy-meson decay constants such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Ebert 1994), Bethe-Salpeter approach (Cvetic 2004, Wang 2005), relativistic quark model (Ebert 2006), field-correlator method (Badalian 2007), light-cone formalism (Hwang 2010), QCD sum rules (Penin 2001), and lattice QCD simulations (Becirevic 1998, Gray 2005). The results are compared with experimental estimates from CLEO (f_D = 206.7 ± 8.9 MeV) and from Belle and BaBar data (f_B ≈ 204 ± 31 MeV). The model enables further study of heavy-quark effects on the QCD vacuum and can be extended to vector meson decay constants via heavy-quark spin symmetry.
