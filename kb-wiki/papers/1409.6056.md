# Inclusive and Exclusive Scatterings from Tensor Polarized Deuteron

> The possibility of using a tensor polarized deuteron target in electroproduction reactions creates new opportunities for studying different phenomena related to the short-range hadronic and nuclear physics. The use of tensor polarized deuteron allows to isolate smaller than average inter-nucleon distances for the bound two-nucleon system. In this report we consider several of high $Q^2$ reactions which are particularly sensitive to the short-range two-nucleon configurations in the deuteron. The one is the relativistic dynamics of electron-bound-nucleon scattering which can be studied in both inclusive and exclusive reactions, other is the strong final state interaction in close proximity of two nucleons that can be used as a sensitive probe for color-transparency phenomena.

# Inclusive and Exclusive Scatterings from Tensor Polarized Deuteron

## Key claim

Using a tensor polarized deuteron target in high $Q^2$ electroproduction reactions can isolate smaller-than-average internucleon distances in the bound two-nucleon system, enabling studies of relativistic dynamics of electron-bound-nucleon scattering and strong final state interactions that may serve as a sensitive probe of color transparency phenomena, but the sensitivity is contingent on the ability to cancel the dominant $S$-wave contribution through specific combinations of polarization components.

## Method

The authors analyze the deuteron polarized density matrix, showing that tensor polarization (specifically the combination $\rho^{20} = \frac{1}{3}(\rho^{11} + \rho^{1-1} - 2\rho^{10})$) cancels the $u(k_1)u(k_2)$ term and retains only terms proportional to $u(k)w(k)$ and $w(k)^2$, where $u(k)$ and $w(k)$ are the $S$- and $D$-wave partial amplitudes. They then apply this formalism to calculate the tensor asymmetry $A_{zz}$ for inclusive scattering and $A_d$ for exclusive $d(e,e'p)n$ reactions, comparing predictions from the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) and light-cone (LC) approaches using AV18 and cdBonn NN potentials.

## Key numbers

- The charge-rms radius of the deuteron is about 2.1 fm.
- The overall probability of the $D$-wave in the deuteron is predicted to be in the range of 4.87% (cdBonn potential) to 5.76% (AV18 potential).
- For inclusive scattering at $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$, measurable relativistic effects are expected already at $x \sim 1.2$.
- For exclusive $d(e,e'p)n$ reactions, significant relativistic effects appear at spectator nucleon momenta of 250–300 MeV/c.
- Color transparency effects can change the asymmetry $A_d$ by as much as a factor of two for $Q^2 \sim 10$ GeV$^2$, compared to only 10–15% effects for unpolarized nuclear targets.
- The $k^{-4}$ scaling of momentum distributions in deuteron and nuclei is observed in the momentum range of 300–600 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of studying short-range correlations in nuclei, building on earlier theoretical frameworks for relativistic deuteron description developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1977, 1981, 1988) and Buck and Gross (1979). The authors connect to recent experimental observations of isospin-dependent short-range correlations by Subedi et al. (2008) and the scaling behavior of momentum distributions related to contact formalism in atomic systems. The proposed measurements are part of a broader program at Jefferson Lab, enabling discrimination between different NN potentials (AV18 vs. cdBonn) and between relativistic approaches (VNA vs. LC), as well as providing a sensitive testbed for color transparency models such as the quantum diffusion model and the three-state resonance model.
