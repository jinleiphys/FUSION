# Dimensional Reduction and Screening Masses in Pure Gauge Theories at Finite Temperature

> We studied screening masses in the equilibrium thermodynamics of SU(2) and SU(3) pure gauge theories on the lattice. At a temperature of 2 Tc we found strong evidence for dimensional reduction in the non-perturbative spectrum of screening masses. Mass ratios in the high temperature SU(3) theory are consistent with those in the pure gauge theory in three dimensions. At the first order SU(3) phase transition we report the first measurement of the true scalar screening mass.

## Key claim

At temperatures of approximately 2 Tc, the degeneracy pattern of screening masses in SU(2) and SU(3) pure gauge theories is consistent with the symmetry group of two-dimensional rotations (C⁴_v), providing strong evidence for dimensional reduction in the non-perturbative spectrum even though the coupling is too large for perturbative decoupling arguments to apply.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(2) and SU(3) pure gauge theories with Wilson action at multiple temperatures (Tc, 3Tc/2, 2Tc, and 4Tc), using Cabbibo-Marinari pseudo heat-bath updates for SU(3) and over-relaxation plus heat-bath for SU(2). They measured correlation functions of loop operators projected onto irreducible representations of the lattice symmetry groups D⁴_h and C⁴_v, extracting screening masses via local mass plateaus and two-mass fits with variational ground state projection.

## Key numbers

- SU(3) at 2Tc: A₁⁺⁺ screening mass = 0.65 ± 0.01 (in lattice units) on the 4×8²×16 lattice
- SU(3) at 2Tc: A₂⁻⁺ screening mass = 0.69 ± 0.04 (fitted mass), nearly degenerate with A₁⁺⁺
- SU(3) at 2Tc: B₁⁺⁺ screening mass = 1.2 ± 0.1, B₁⁻⁺ = 1.34 ± 0.08, B₂⁺⁺ = 1.4 ± 0.1 (all consistent within errors)
- SU(3) at Tc: physical A₁⁺⁺ mass = 0.86_{-0.10}^{+0.11} (lattice units, Nτ=4, β=5.7)
- SU(3) mass ratio at Tc: m(Tc, A₁⁺⁺)/m(T=0, A₁⁺⁺) = 0.89 ± 0.10
- SU(2) at 2Tc: A₁⁺ screening mass = 0.71 ± 0.05 (small lattice, set A)
- SU(2) at 2Tc: A₁⁻ screening mass = 1.14 ± 0.02, B₁⁺ = 1.18 ± 0.03, B₁⁻ = 1.9 ± 0.2, B₂⁺ = 1.8 ± 0.1

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of screening masses by the authors of "old" (who first classified glueball-like screening masses and measured several of them) and by Yaffe and collaborators (who used symmetry representations to define Debye mass operators). The results confirm the dimensional reduction picture proposed by Braaten and collaborators for high temperatures, but demonstrate that the reduction to a three-dimensional pure gauge theory works even at temperatures as low as 2Tc where the coupling is large. The measured non-perturbative spectrum matches that of three-dimensional pure gauge theory studied by "2plus1", enabling future work to use the screening mass spectrum as a probe of the effective theory governing the magnetic sector at finite temperature.
