# Geometry of particle emission in UrQMD Ar+Sc collisions at SPS energies

> Over the past few decades, progress in femtoscopy has been driven by the interplay between experimental measurements and theoretical calculations. Measurements provide data to support the theory, while theoretical predictions guide new measurements. In the recent decade, several experiments have confirmed that the two-particle pion-emitting source is well described by Lévy alpha-stable distributions. To enable theoretical interpretation, phenomenological simulations have been done at RHIC and LHC energies, in large systems such as Au+Au or Pb+Pb, using various available heavy-ion collision models. However, such simulations have not been done in intermediate systems. In this paper, we investigate three-dimensional two-pion pair source distributions from $^{40}$Ar+$^{45}$Sc central collisions at SPS energies, generated with the Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics Monte-Carlo event generator. Supplemented by a validation of the simulated hadronic spectra, we find that the pair source can be described with Lévy-stable distributions. We subsequently interpret the physical meaning of the extracted Lévy parameters corresponding to the spatial scale, shape, and strength of the source. Our results form a baseline for future experimental measurements in intermediate systems.

## Key claim

The three-dimensional two-pion pair source distributions from UrQMD-simulated Ar+Sc collisions at SPS energies can be described by Lévy-stable distributions, but the extracted Lévy index α shows a decreasing trend with collision energy that is opposite to preliminary NA61/SHINE experimental data, suggesting that the purely hadronic UrQMD model lacks hydrodynamic or critical phenomena present in real collisions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD event generator (v3.4) in cascade mode, modified to include η meson decays, to simulate 10,000 events per energy for central (0-10% centrality) ⁴⁰Ar+⁴⁵Sc collisions at six beam momenta from 13 to 150A GeV/c. They reconstructed three-dimensional two-pion pair source distributions in the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS) using the Bertsch-Pratt coordinate system, aggregated events into blocks of 2500-5000 events for statistical convergence, and fitted one-dimensional projections of the distributions with three-dimensional Lévy-stable distributions to extract the Lévy index α, scale parameters Rout, Rside, Rlong, and the correlation strength parameter λ*.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta simulated: 13, 19, 30, 40, 75, and 150A GeV/c, corresponding to center-of-mass energies √sNN ≈ 5-17 GeV
- 10,000 events generated per energy in the 0-10% centrality range with time evolution set to 10,000 fm/c
- Pion selection: single-track rapidity y ± 1 (1.5 for 150A GeV/c) around midrapidity, transverse momentum pT < 1.5 GeV/c
- Pairs selected with momentum difference qLCMS < √(mT · 150 MeV/c²)
- Eight KT classes from 0 to 1 GeV/c were investigated
- Average relative systematic uncertainty on α across all KT intervals: 1.90% at 150A GeV/c, 0.98% at 40A GeV/c, 0.78% at 13A GeV/c
- Average relative systematic uncertainty on Rlong across all KT intervals: 1.88% at 150A GeV/c, 1.03% at 40A GeV/c, 1.18% at 13A GeV/c
- The "true" λ would be about 92-93% of the obtained λ* value if weak decays were incorporated

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological simulations of Lévy-stable source distributions, which had been performed at RHIC and LHC energies in large systems such as Au+Au or Pb+Pb (Kincses 2025, Korodi 2022), to intermediate systems (Ar+Sc) at SPS energies for the first time. The results form a baseline for comparison with experimental measurements from the NA61/SHINE experiment (Porfy 2024) and with simulations from other models such as EPOS3 (Kincses 2025) and EPOS4 (Huang 2025). The observed discrepancy between the decreasing α trend in UrQMD and the opposite trend in preliminary NA61/SHINE data suggests that the purely hadronic UrQMD model, which lacks critical dynamics near the QCD critical point predicted by Csorgo (2005) and hydrodynamic phenomena, cannot fully capture the physics of the collision system.
