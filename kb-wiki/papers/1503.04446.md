# Enhancement of the CP-odd effect in the nuclear electric dipole moment of $^6$Li

> We calculate for the first time the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the $^6$Li nucleus within the alpha + p + n three-body cluster model using the Gaussian expansion method, assuming the one meson exchange P, CP-odd nuclear forces. It is found that the EDM of the $^6$Li is 2 times more sensitive on the isovector pion exchange P, CP-odd nuclear force than the deuteron EDM, due to the CP-odd interaction between the nucleons and the alpha cluster. The $^9$Be EDM is also calculated in the same framework as an alpha + alpha + n three-body system. We also test the ab initio calculation of the EDM of the deuteron, $^3$H and $^3$He nuclei using the realistic Argonne $v18$ nuclear force. In the ab initio calculations, good agreements with previous studies are obtained. We finally discuss the prospects for the new physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The electric dipole moment (EDM) of the $^6$Li nucleus is approximately 2 times more sensitive to the isovector pion exchange P, CP-odd nuclear force than the deuteron EDM, due to the additional CP-odd interaction between nucleons and the alpha cluster; this enhancement is conditional on the cluster approximation and may be overestimated for heavy meson exchange contributions due to the folding procedure.

## Method

The authors calculated the EDM of $^6$Li within an $\alpha + p + n$ three-body cluster model using the Gaussian expansion method, assuming one-meson exchange P, CP-odd nuclear forces. They also calculated the $^9$Be EDM as an $\alpha + \alpha + n$ three-body system and performed ab initio calculations of the deuteron, $^3$H, and $^3$He EDMs using the realistic Argonne $v18$ nuclear force. The CP-odd $\alpha$-nucleon potential was obtained by folding the CP-odd nucleon-nucleon force with the nucleon density of the $\alpha$ cluster.

## Key numbers

* $^6$Li EDM linear coefficient for isovector pion exchange: $a_\pi^{(1)} = 2.8 \times 10^{-2} e$ fm, compared to the deuteron value of $1.45 \times 10^{-2} e$ fm (a factor of approximately 2 enhancement).
* $^6$Li EDM linear coefficient for isovector $\omega$ exchange: $a_\omega^{(1)} = -0.41 \times 10^{-2} e$ fm, compared to the deuteron value of $-5.90 \times 10^{-2} \times 10^{-2} e$ fm.
* $^6$Li spin expectation values: $\langle \sigma_p \rangle = 0.88$ and $\langle \sigma_n \rangle = 0.88$.
* $^9$Be EDM linear coefficient for isovector pion exchange: $a_\pi^{(1)} = 1.4 \times 10^{-2} e$ fm, comparable to the deuteron value.
* Binding energies from Argonne $v18$: $^3$He at 6.93 MeV and $^3$H at 7.63 MeV (experimental values are 7.7 MeV and 8.4 MeV, respectively).
* Prospective experimental sensitivity for $^6$Li EDM: $O(10^{-29}) e$ cm, which could probe new physics at the PeV scale.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of nuclear EDMs in the deuteron (Liu 2004, Song 2013) and three-nucleon systems (Stetcu 2008, Bsaisou 2015) to the $^6$Li nucleus, the lightest stable system with non-zero angular momentum beyond three nucleons. The results enable experimental searches for CP violation beyond the standard model using storage ring measurements of light nuclear EDMs planned at Brookhaven National Laboratory, and the authors recommend measurement of the $^6$Li EDM specifically.
