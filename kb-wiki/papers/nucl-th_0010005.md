# Solution of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations using realistic NN and 3N interaction

> We solve the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for 3N and 4N bound states based on the most modern realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. We include different realistic 3N forces. It is shown that all 3N force models can remove the underbinding of the triton and alpha-particle which one obtains with existing NN interactions. The agreement of theoretical predictions and the experimental binding energy is quite good and there is little room left for the action of four-nucleon forces in the alpha-particle. The effect of 3N forces on the wave function is investigated.

## Key claim

All realistic three-nucleon force (3NF) models can remove the underbinding of the triton and alpha-particle that results from using only modern realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions, but the agreement leaves little room for four-nucleon forces in the alpha-particle. The authors adjust the cutoff parameter in the 3NF individually for each NN interaction to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy, meaning the results are not parameter-free predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space for 3N and 4N bound states using parallel computers, employing realistic NN potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93) combined with various 3NF models (Tucson-Melbourne TM, a chirally modified version TM', and Urbana-IX). For each NN+3NF combination, the cutoff parameter in the 3NF was adjusted so that the resulting Hamiltonian reproduces the experimental triton binding energy, and the resulting alpha-particle binding energy and wave function properties were then computed.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with CD-Bonn alone: -8.012 MeV (underbinding of ~0.47 MeV relative to experimental -8.48 MeV)
- Triton binding energy with AV18 alone: -7.623 MeV (underbinding of ~0.86 MeV)
- Alpha-particle binding energy with CD-Bonn alone: -26.26 MeV (underbinding of ~2.04 MeV relative to experimental -28.30 MeV)
- Alpha-particle binding energy with AV18 alone: -24.28 MeV (underbinding of ~4.02 MeV)
- CD-Bonn+TM gives triton binding energy -8.480 MeV and alpha-particle binding energy -29.15 MeV (overbinding of 0.85 MeV)
- AV18+TM gives triton binding energy -8.476 MeV and alpha-particle binding energy -28.84 MeV (overbinding of 0.54 MeV)
- AV18+Urb-IX gives triton binding energy -8.478 MeV and alpha-particle binding energy -28.50 MeV (overbinding of 0.20 MeV)
- Cutoff parameter for CD-Bonn+TM: 4.784 m_pi; for AV18+TM: 5.156 m_pi; for AV18+TM': 4.756 m_pi

## Context

This work extends the rigorous solution of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for 4N bound states (Kamada 1992, Nogga 2000) to include modern realistic NN interactions and multiple 3NF models. It confirms the earlier finding (Tjon 1975) of a linear correlation between binding energy and kinetic energy for modern forces including charge independence breaking. The results show that while all 3NF models can resolve the underbinding problem, the different 3NF operators act very differently on the wave functions, with the TM force being strongly attractive in S-S matrix elements while Urbana-IX attraction comes mainly from S-D transitions; this opens the question of how to pin down these differences and visualize the distinct 3N correlations.
