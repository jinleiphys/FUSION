# Superallowed Fermi Beta Decay -- a Status Report

> Data on superallowed Fermi beta decay in nuclei are compiled and electromagnetic corrections discussed. Recommended values for the weak vector coupling constant and the leading element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa mixing matrix are given. Comments on neutron decay and limits extracted on extensions to the Standard Model are made.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the nine best-measured superallowed Fermi beta decays are consistent with the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis at the level of a few parts in 10^4, but the resulting Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix unitarity test yields a sum of first-row elements of 0.9972(13), which deviates from unity by more than twice the estimated error, a result that may indicate either new physics or inadequacies in the theoretical corrections.

## Method

The authors compiled world data on Q-values, lifetimes, and branching ratios for nine superallowed 0+ to 0+ Fermi beta transitions (10C, 14O, 26mAl, 34Cl, 38mK, 42Sc, 46V, 50Mn, 54Co). They applied nucleus-dependent radiative corrections (delta_R) and Coulomb corrections (delta_C), the latter taken as the unweighted average of two independent calculations by Towner-Hardy-Harvey and by Ormand-Brown, to obtain corrected Ft values. The corrected Ft values were then used to extract the weak vector coupling constant and, via comparison with muon decay, the CKM matrix element V_ud.

## Key numbers

*   Weighted average corrected Ft value for nine transitions: 3072.3 +/- 2.0 s (including both statistical and systematic uncertainties).
*   Effective vector coupling constant from nuclear decays: G_V'/(hbar c)^3 = (1.14959 +/- 0.00038) x 10^-5 GeV^-2.
*   V_ud from nuclear decays: 0.9740 +/- 0.0005.
*   CKM unitarity sum from nuclear decays: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 0.9972 +/- 0.0013.
*   Neutron mean lifetime (average of eleven measurements): tau_n = 887.0 +/- 2.0 s.
*   Neutron beta-asymmetry parameter (weighted average of three experiments): lambda = G_A'/G_V' = -1.2599 +/- 0.0025.
*   Effective vector coupling constant from neutron decay: G_V'/(hbar c)^3 = (1.1561 +/- 0.0023) x 10^-5 GeV^-2.
*   Nucleus-independent radiative correction: Delta_R^V = (2.40 +/- 0.08)%.
*   Left-right mixing angle limit from superallowed decays: zeta = 0.0014 +/- 0.0006.

## Context

This work updates the 1989 survey by Hardy et al. (1989) and the 1995 update by Towner and Hardy (1995), incorporating new Q-value measurements by Barker et al. (1994) and new lifetime and branching-ratio measurements from Chalk River. The paper extends the CVC test to four parts in 10^4 and provides the most precise determination of V_ud from nuclear decays at that time. The discrepancy between the nuclear and neutron results for G_V' (serious disagreement) and the marginal unitarity violation motivate further theoretical work on Coulomb corrections (e.g., the empirical approach of Wilkinson (1990) and the R-matrix calculations of Barker (1994)) and open the possibility of constraining extensions to the Standard Model such as left-right symmetric models or additional Z' bosons.
