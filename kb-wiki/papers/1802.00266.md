# Quark mass functions and pion structure in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> The Covariant Spectator Theory is applied to the description of quarks and the pion. The dressed quark mass function is calculated dynamically in Minkowski space and used in the calculation of the pion electromagnetic form factor. The effects of the mass function on the pion form factor and the different quark-pole contributions to the triangle diagram are analyzed.

## Key claim

The Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) can dynamically generate a dressed quark mass function in Minkowski space that, when used in pion form factor calculations, shows that the active quark-pole contributions are as important as spectator contributions for small pion masses, but the overall pion form factor is surprisingly insensitive to the specific functional form of the quark mass function in the timelike region.

## Method

The authors solved the CST Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator using a kernel consisting of a mixed pseudoscalar-scalar linear confining potential plus a constant term, which satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity and ensures dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The resulting quark mass function was fitted to lattice QCD data in the spacelike region and extended by an Ansatz into the timelike region, then used in the impulse-approximation calculation of the pion electromagnetic form factor from triangle diagrams, taking all six quark propagator-pole contributions into account.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-limit constituent quark mass \(m\) and a mass cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) were determined by fitting the mass function \(M(p^2)=m h^2(p^2)\) to lattice QCD data extrapolated to the chiral limit (Bowman 2005).
- For large pion mass \(\mu\) and large \(Q^2\), the active quark contribution \(F_{\rm act}\) is suppressed relative to the spectator contribution \(F_{\rm spect}\) by about 30%.
- The ratio \(F_{\rm spect}/F_{\rm act}\) was calculated for pion masses \(\mu = 0.6\), \(0.42\), and \(0.14\) GeV, with the pairs of curves shown from top to bottom in that order.
- The pion form factor was calculated in the spacelike region of momentum-transfer squared \(Q^2 > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory (Gross 1969) to dynamical quark mass generation and pion form factor calculations, complementing lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approaches (DSBS) that typically work in Euclidean space. The CST framework allows calculations directly in Minkowski space, enabling straightforward extension to the timelike \(Q^2\) region relevant for medium effects. This first calculation serves as a reference for future form factor calculations within CST, particularly for applications to the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
