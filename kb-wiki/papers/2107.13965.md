# Time reversal-odd effects in QCD and beyond

> In this paper we will describe a parallel between phenomenological and symmetry-based descriptions of the process dependence of the time-reversal-odd phenomena in QCD, such as the Sivers effect, with the goal of defining the essential elements that lead to such process dependence in QCD. This will then serve as a starting point to explore possible generalizations in a different gauge theory.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the process dependence of time-reversal-odd (T-odd) phenomena in QCD, such as the Sivers effect, arises from the interplay between time-reversal symmetry and the gauge symmetry encoded in Wilson lines, and that this mechanism can be generalized to other gauge theories like QED, potentially enabling experimental tests in atomic physics.

## Method
The authors review the historical development of T-odd transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions, contrasting phenomenological approaches (Sivers 1990; Brodsky, Hwang, Schmidt 2002) with symmetry-based arguments. They analyze the quark correlation function including Wilson lines, apply time-reversal transformations to derive relations between correlators with future-pointing and past-pointing staple-like Wilson lines, and show that the Sivers function changes sign between semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan processes. They then draw an analogy between QCD Wilson lines and the QED Aharonov-Bohm effect.

## Key numbers
* The Sivers function satisfies \(f_{1T}^{\perp [+]}(x,k_\st^2) = -f_{1T}^{\perp [-]}(x,k_\st^2)\), where \([+]\) and \([-]\) denote future-pointing and past-pointing Wilson line configurations, respectively, establishing the process-dependent sign change between SIDIS and Drell-Yan.
* In collinear factorization, the single spin asymmetry from partonic-level calculations is proportional to \(\alpha_s \, m_q / \sqrt{s}\), where \(\alpha_s\) is the QCD coupling constant, \(m_q\) the quark mass, and \(\sqrt{s}\) the center-of-mass energy, leading to suppression of the effect.
* The Aharonov-Bohm phase shift in QED is proportional to \(\exp \left\{ -i e \oint ds \cdot A(s) \right\}\), where \(A(s)\) is the electromagnetic potential, providing a formal analogy to the non-abelian Wilson lines in QCD.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of T-odd effects from the original Sivers proposal (Sivers 1990) and the subsequent model calculation by Brodsky, Hwang, and Schmidt (2002), which demonstrated non-zero single spin asymmetries via gluon exchange interference. It formalizes the correction by Collins (2002) that Wilson lines are essential for non-zero T-odd distributions, contradicting his earlier argument (Collins 1993) that such functions must vanish. The paper enables future searches for analogous T-odd effects in QED systems, such as atomic physics experiments, where measurements can be more precise than in QCD and where non-perturbative complications are absent.
