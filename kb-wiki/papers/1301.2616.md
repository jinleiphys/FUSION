# Review of low-energy interaction of strange and charm hadrons with nucleons and nuclei

> The properties of strange and charm mesons in nuclear matter and nuclei are reviewed. Different frameworks are presented and discussed paying a special attention to unitarized coupled-channel approaches. Possible experimental signatures of the in-medium properties of these mesons are also addressed, in particular in connection with the future FAIR facility at GSI.

## Key claim

This review argues that unitarized coupled-channel approaches, which respect chiral symmetry in the light sector and heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) in the charm sector, provide a consistent framework for describing the in-medium properties of strange and charm mesons, but the experimental confirmation of these modifications remains incomplete and model-dependent.

## Method

The authors review multiple theoretical frameworks for computing the self-energies and spectral functions of strange mesons (K, anti-K, anti-K*) and open-charm mesons (D, anti-D, D*) in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. They focus on unitarized coupled-channel approaches that incorporate the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction for light quarks and HQSS for heavy quarks, solving the in-medium Dyson equation self-consistently to obtain meson spectral functions. For anti-K* mesons, the self-energy includes contributions from the decay mode anti-K pi modified by the medium and from unitarized coupled-channel interactions with nucleons that generate the Lambda(1783) and Sigma(1830) resonances. For D and D* mesons, the self-energy is obtained by summing transition amplitudes over the Fermi sea of nucleons across different spin and isospin channels, incorporating Pauli blocking and open-charm meson self-energies in intermediate propagators.

## Key numbers

* Anti-K meson optical potential: attraction of approximately -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} from unitarized coupled-channel approaches based on chiral dynamics and meson-exchange models.
* Anti-K* meson optical potential: moderate real part of -50 MeV at rho_0, with the spectral function width at normal nuclear matter density becoming five times larger than in free space.
* D meson mass shift: approximately -50 MeV at rho_0 from QCD sum-rule approaches, with a splitting of D+ and D- masses of 60 MeV at rho_0 from a second analysis.
* D+ meson-nucleus potential: approximately -140 MeV at rho_0 from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model.
* D0-nucleus bound states: weakly bound with significant widths, in contrast to earlier QMC predictions for 208Pb.
* Anti-K* transparency ratio: calculated for photon energies of 4.3 GeV and 6 GeV in the lab frame (center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) = 3 GeV and 3.5 GeV), showing strong attenuation with increasing nuclear mass number A.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on in-medium hadron properties that began with kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan, early work) and charmonium suppression as a probe for quark-gluon plasma formation (Satz, early work). It contrasts earlier relativistic mean-field calculations (Schaffner, 1996) that obtained anti-K potentials of several hundred MeV at saturation density with later unitarized coupled-channel approaches (Lutz; Ramos, 1999; Tolos, 2001; Tolos, 2002) that find much weaker attraction. The review connects to experimental programs at SIS/GSI, SPS/CERN, RHIC/BNL, LHC/CERN, and the forthcoming PANDA and CBM experiments at FAIR/GSI, and it enables future searches for D-mesic nuclei and measurements of transparency ratios for photoproduction reactions.
