# Phase Transition to Hyperon Matter in Neutron Stars

> Recent progress in the understanding of the high density phase of neutron stars advances the view that a substantial fraction of the matter consists of hyperons. The possible impacts of a highly attractive interaction between hyperons on the properties of compact stars are investigated. We find that a hadronic equation of state with hyperons allows for a first order phase transition to hyperonic matter. The corresponding hyperon stars can have rather small radii of R=8 km.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first order phase transition to hyperonic matter can occur in neutron stars if the hyperon-hyperon interaction is sufficiently attractive, producing compact stars with radii as small as R=8 km, but this result depends on the strength of the coupling to the hidden strange scalar meson (g_σ*/g_σN) being above a threshold of about 0.71.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field model of baryons (nucleons and the full baryon octet) interacting via scalar (σ, σ*) and vector (ω, ρ, φ) meson exchange, with hyperon coupling constants fixed by hypernuclear data and SU(6) symmetry. They vary the coupling of hyperons to the hidden strange scalar meson σ* to explore the effects of enhanced hyperon-hyperon interactions, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for charge-neutral β-equilibrated matter to obtain mass-radius relations for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass without hidden strange meson exchange: M_max = 1.8 M_⊙, with minimum radius R_min = 12.9 km and central density ρ_c = 0.78 fm⁻³ (about five times normal nuclear density)
- For the third family of compact stars (0.71 < g_σ*/g_σN < 0.78): radii range from 8.6 km < R < 11.6 km, with central baryonic densities between 1.1 fm⁻³ < ρ_c < 2.0 fm⁻³
- For self-bound hyperon stars (g_σ*/g_σN = 1.0): radii range from 4.2 km < R < 7.3 km, with masses as low as M ≈ 0.05 M_⊙
- Mass difference between an ordinary neutron star and its twin star: about 0.03 M_⊙, corresponding to a conversion energy of about 0.5 × 10⁵³ erg
- The hyperon potential in nuclear matter is U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strange hadronic matter (Schaffner-Bielich 1993) and hyperon-rich neutron stars (Glendenning 1991) by incorporating a highly attractive hyperon-hyperon interaction motivated by double Λ hypernuclear data and the Nijmegen soft-core potential (Stoks 1999). The predicted third family of compact stars (Gerlach 1968) and neutron star twins provides a potential observational signature distinct from ordinary neutron stars and strange quark stars (Haensel 1986), and the conversion process could produce gravitational wave signals detectable by LIGO, VIRGO, and GEO600.
