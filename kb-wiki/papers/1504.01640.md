# Beta-decay properties of neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Ru, and Pd isotopes from deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation

> Beta-decay properties of even and odd-A neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Ru, and Pd isotopes involved in the astrophysical rapid neutron capture process are studied within a deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The underlying mean field is described self-consistently from deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with pairing correlations. Residual interactions in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels are also included in the formalism. The isotopic evolution of the various nuclear equilibrium shapes and the corresponding charge radii are investigated in all the isotopic chains. The energy distributions of the Gamow-Teller strength as well as the beta-decay half-lives are discussed and compared with the available experimental information. It is shown that nuclear deformation plays a significant role in the description of the decay properties in this mass region. Reliable predictions of the strength distributions are essential to evaluate decay rates in astrophysical scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing and residual interactions can reliably describe beta-decay half-lives and Gamow-Teller strength distributions for neutron-rich Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Ru, and Pd isotopes, but only when nuclear deformation is properly included as a degree of freedom; the method is tested over a wide range of nuclear shapes (spherical, prolate, oblate, and shape-coexisting) and the same coupling strengths are used for all nuclei without case-by-case fitting.

## Method

The author performed deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 interaction and BCS pairing to generate the mean field for each nucleus, then applied a deformed proton-neutron QRPA with separable spin-isospin residual interactions in the particle-hole (ph) and particle-particle (pp) channels using fixed coupling strengths (chi_ph = 0.15 MeV, kappa_pp = 0.03 MeV). Potential energy curves were constructed from constrained HF calculations to identify equilibrium deformations, and Gamow-Teller strength distributions were computed for each equilibrium shape. Beta-decay half-lives were obtained by summing the GT strength below the Q-beta energy window weighted by phase space factors, with a standard quenching factor of 0.77 applied to the axial-vector coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Coupling strengths used for all nuclei: chi_ph = 0.15 MeV and kappa_pp = 0.03 MeV for the residual interaction in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, respectively.
- Quenching factor: (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77 (g_A/g_V)_free, applied to all GT strength distributions and half-life calculations.
- D = 6200 s in the half-life formula: T_{1/2}^{-1} = (g_A/g_V)_eff^2 / D * sum f(Z, Q_beta - E_ex) B(GT, E_ex).
- Q_beta values in odd-A isotopes are about 2-3 MeV larger than in neighboring even-even isotopes.
- Deformation parameters: prolate shapes with beta approximately 0.2 to 0.4 and oblate shapes with beta approximately -0.2 to -0.25 are found across the isotopic chains; Sr isotopes show a transition from oblate to strong prolate (beta approximately 0.4) at A = 96-98 (N = 58-60).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous deformed QRPA studies of Zr and Mo isotopes (Sarriguren, 2013, 2014) to the neighboring isotopic chains of Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Ru, and Pd, which are involved in the astrophysical r-process. The calculations are compared with available experimental half-lives from Audi (2012) and Nishimura (2011), and with other theoretical approaches including deformed Woods-Saxon QRPA with realistic CD-Bonn forces (Fang, 2013) and self-consistent Skyrme QRPA (Yoshida, 2013). The results enable predictions of beta-decay half-lives for more exotic neutron-rich nuclei not yet measured, which are needed for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations.
