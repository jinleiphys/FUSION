# Delta isobar resonance effects studied by summed isospin spin strengths and nuclear matrix elements for double and single beta decays

> Nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for double and single beta decays are crucial for studying neutrino properties beyond and within the standard model. The NMEs consist mainly of the spin isospin components. The delta isobar resonance, which is strongly excited by the qualk spin isospin excitation, is shown to quench the spin isospin NMEs by a coefficient around 0.7. Impact of the delta resonance on neutrino studies in nuclei is discused.

## Key claim

The summed Gamow-Teller (GT) and spin-dipole (SD) strengths measured in the nucleonic excitation region (0-30 MeV) for double-beta decay nuclei are reduced to approximately 47% and 50% of their respective nucleon-based sum-rule limits, and this reduction is attributed to the non-nucleonic Delta-isobar resonance effect rather than to nucleonic correlations alone.

## Method

The author reanalyzed charge-exchange reaction (CER) data from (³He,t) experiments on medium-heavy double-beta decay nuclei (⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ⁹⁶Zr, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹³⁰Te, ¹³⁶Xe, ¹⁵⁰Nd) to extract summed GT and SD strengths in the 0-30 MeV excitation region. A schematic quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) model including both nucleon-nucleon (NN) and nucleon-Delta (NΔ) tau-sigma interactions was used to evaluate the Delta-isobar quenching effect on the summed strengths and on individual nuclear matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Summed GT strength S⁻(GT) for double-beta decay and Sn nuclei is 47 ± 5% of the Ikeda sum-rule limit S_N(GT) = 3(N-Z), based on (³He,t) CER data.
- Summed SD strength S⁻(SD) for double-beta decay nuclei is 50 ± 7% of the nucleon-based summed strength S⁻_N(SD).
- The quenching coefficient K_Δ derived from the summed GT and SD strengths is 0.7 ± 0.07, corresponding to a susceptibility κ_Δ ≈ 0.43.
- The effective tau-sigma coupling including the Delta effect is g^{Δ}_{τσ} ≈ 0.7 g_{τσ}, where g_{τσ} is the coupling for a free nucleon.
- The GR_N(GT) energies for double-beta decay nuclei are given by E_CA(GT) ≈ 0.22(N-Z) + 9.3 MeV, reproduced with g'_NN = 0.62 and g'_NΔ = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of Delta-isobar effects on tau-sigma nuclear matrix elements by Oset (1979), Bohr and Mottelson (1981), Ejiri (1982, 1983), and Towner (1987), and is contrasted with claims by Wakasa (1997) that 90% of the GT sum rule is found below 50 MeV. The experimental quenching coefficient of 0.7 is consistent with theoretical two-body current calculations by Gysbers (2019) and with the NΔ coupling parameter g'_NΔ ≈ 0.5 from the Julich-Tokyo potential. The results enable incorporation of the Delta quenching effect into nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay and astro-neutrino studies.
