# Nuclear break-up of 11Be

> The break-up of 11Be was studied at 41AMeV using a secondary beam of 11Be from the GANIL facility on a 48Ti target by measuring correlations between the 10Be core, the emitted neutrons and gamma rays. The nuclear break-up leading to the emission of a neutron at large angle in the laboratory frame is identified with the towing mode through its characteristic n-fragment correlation. The experimental spectra are compared with a model where the time dependent Schrodinger equation (TDSE) is solved for the neutron initially in the 11 Be. A good agreement is found between experiment and theory for the shapes of neutron experimental energies and angular distributions. The spectroscopic factor of the 2s orbital is tentatively extracted to be 0.46+-0.15. The neutron emission from the 1p and 1d orbitals is also studied.

## Key claim

The nuclear break-up of 11Be at 41 AMeV on a 48Ti target is identified with the "towing mode" mechanism through characteristic neutron-fragment angular correlations, and the spectroscopic factor of the 2s orbital is tentatively extracted to be 0.46 ± 0.15, though this value is sensitive to the choice of minimum impact parameter and target potential depth in the time-dependent Schrodinger equation calculation.

## Method

The experiment used a secondary 11Be beam at 41 AMeV from GANIL incident on a 48Ti target, detecting 10Be fragments in the SPEG spectrometer in coincidence with neutrons from the NordBall and Uppsala arrays and gamma rays from the Chateau de Cristal BaF2 detectors. The experimental neutron angular and energy distributions were compared with time-dependent Schrodinger equation (TDSE) calculations for the halo neutron initially in 2s, 1p, and 1d orbitals, with spectroscopic factors extracted by fitting calculated absolute cross sections to the data.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the 2s orbital: 0.46 ± 0.15 (using sharp cut-off at b_min = 8.5 fm); 0.42 with smooth cut-off prescription from Bonaccorso (1999)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 1p orbital: 3.9 ± 1.5 (purely statistical error)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 1d orbital: 0.50 ± 0.20 (purely statistical error)
- 11Be beam energy: 41 AMeV
- 2s neutron binding energy in the calculation: 0.503 MeV
- Maximum impact parameter for cross section integration: 110 fm
- Enhancement factor of about 4 for neutron cross section when detected on opposite side of beam relative to 10Be compared to same side

## Context

This work extends previous break-up studies of 11Be by Anne (1994) by measuring the scattering angle of the 10Be core, enabling the first experimental observation of the characteristic angular anti-correlation predicted for the "towing mode" mechanism. The TDSE approach follows calculations by Lacroix (1999) and Esbensen (2001) and provides an alternative to Eikonal approximation methods for extracting spectroscopic information from nuclear break-up reactions. The extracted spectroscopic factors can be compared with results from Coulomb dissociation experiments by Palit (2003) and transfer reactions by Fortier (1999), and the sensitivity analysis highlights that, similar to knock-out reactions reviewed by Hansen (2003) and transfer reactions by Lee (2006), model parameters must be carefully optimized to infer reliable spectroscopic factors.
