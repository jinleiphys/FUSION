# Hyperon and nuclear symmetry energy in the neutron star

> In this work, masses and radii of neutron stars are considered to investigate the effect of nuclear symmetry energy to the astrophysical observables. A relativistic mean field model with density-dependent meson-baryon coupling constants is employed in describing the equation of state of dense nuclear matter, and the density dependencies of the symmetry energies are quoted from the recent phenomenological formulae obtained from the heavy ion data at subnuclear saturation densities. Since hyperons can take part in the $β$-equilibrium of the dense matter inside neutron stars, we include hyperons in our estimation and their roles are discussed in combination with that of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how different density-dependent forms of the nuclear symmetry energy, constrained by heavy-ion data at subnuclear densities, affect neutron star properties when hyperons are included in beta-equilibrium; the authors find that a super-soft symmetry energy (MDI model) extrapolated to high densities prevents the formation of a stable neutron star, suggesting such extrapolations may be unrealistic.

## Method

The authors employ a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model with octet baryons and sigma, omega, and rho mesons, using the Typel and Wolter (1999) parameterization for the isoscalar couplings. Four empirical forms of the symmetry energy are used: MDI (momentum-dependent interaction) from Chen et al. (2005), FSU from Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005), NL3 from Lalazissis et al. (1997), and TW99 from Typel and Wolter (1999); these are inserted into the rho-meson coupling to control the isovector channel. The equation of state is computed for matter with nucleons only (np) and with the full baryon octet including hyperons (npH), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for the TW99 model: rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon 16.247 MeV and compression modulus K_0 = 240 MeV.
- For the FSU model: saturation density rho_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}, symmetry energy J = 32.59 MeV, slope L = 60.5 MeV, curvature K_sym = -51.3 MeV.
- For the NL3 model: saturation density rho_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}, symmetry energy J = 37.29 MeV, slope L = 118.2 MeV, curvature K_sym = 100.9 MeV.
- Maximum neutron star mass with nucleons only (np) for NL3: approximately 2.2 solar masses.
- Maximum neutron star mass with full octet (npH) for NL3, FSU, and TW99: approximately 1.6 solar masses.
- Radius at maximum mass for FSU and TW99 models (npH): 10.5 - 10.9 km; for NL3 model (npH): approximately 13.1 km.
- In the MDI model, the potential symmetry energy V_sym becomes negative for densities above n = 2.1 (in units of saturation density), and the pressure decreases above rho = 6.5 rho_0, signaling instability.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy effects on neutron stars, such as the free gas model analysis by Khoa and Khan (2009), by using a relativistic mean-field framework with density-dependent couplings. It also builds on the authors' earlier work (Hyun et al., 2005) showing that density-dependent meson masses and couplings critically affect neutron star properties. The results are compared to the recent observation of the neutron star J1614-2230 with mass (1.97 +/- 0.04) solar masses (Demorest et al., 2010), which can rule out soft equations of state; the paper also notes that kaon condensation (work in preparation) may compete with hyperons at high densities.
