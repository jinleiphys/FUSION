# Constructing chiral effective field theory around unnatural leading-order interactions

> A momentum-dependent formulation based on a stationary spin-0 and isospin-1 dibaryon field is proposed to improve convergence of chiral effective field theory in the $\cs{1}{0}$ channel of $NN$ scattering. Although the two-parameter leading-order interaction appears to be unnatural, it nevertheless has the necessary features of an effective field theory. A rapid order-by-order convergence is found in $\cs{1}{0}$. As an application beyond the two-body level, the triton binding energy is studied and compared to standard chiral effective field theory with partly perturbative pions. The consistency of the chiral Lagrangian for the new formulation is examined by working out the pionic radiative corrections, and consequences of nontrivial chiral-connection terms are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a momentum-dependent, separable potential to replace the energy-dependent dibaryon-exchange potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel of nucleon-nucleon scattering, claiming that this new formulation improves the order-by-order convergence of chiral effective field theory while remaining consistent with renormalization-group invariance and chiral symmetry, despite the leading-order interaction appearing unnatural.

## Method

The authors introduce a stationary spin-0, isospin-1 dibaryon field without a kinetic term, whose coupling to nucleons is constructed by summing an infinite series of derivative operators to produce a separable, momentum-dependent potential \(V_\text{spr}(p', p) = -\frac{4\pi}{m_N} \frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{p^{\prime 2} + m_N \Delta}\sqrt{p^2 + m_N \Delta}}\). The leading-order amplitude is obtained by resumming this potential together with the Yukawa part of one-pion exchange via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, with higher-order corrections built perturbatively. Pionic radiative corrections to the dibaryon vertex are computed at N\(^2\)LO, and the triton binding energy is calculated using Faddeev equations up to NLO.

## Key numbers

- The generalized effective range in \(^1S_0\) is approximately 130 MeV, identified as the light mass scale that motivates promoting a momentum-dependent contact term to leading order (from Steele (1998)).
- The fitted leading-order parameters yield \(\sqrt{m_N \Delta} \simeq 150\) MeV and \(\lambda \simeq 200\) MeV, confirming the expected scaling \(\sqrt{m_N \Delta} \sim \lambda \sim M_\text{lo}\).
- The running of \(\Delta^{(0)}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda\): \(\Delta^{(0)} = 27.1\) MeV at \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV, 24.6 MeV at \(\Lambda = 1200\) MeV, and 24.1 MeV at \(\Lambda = 2400\) MeV.
- The running of \(\lambda^{(0)}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda\): \(\lambda^{(0)} = 270\) MeV at \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV, 223 MeV at \(\Lambda = 1200\) MeV, and 209 MeV at \(\Lambda = 2400\) MeV.
- For the \(^3S_1\) channel, the radiative corrections renormalize the counterterms by up to 50% compared to the minimally modified Weinberg scheme, but the phase shift differences are no more than about one degree.

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon formalism of Long (2013) and Sanchez Sanchez (2017) by replacing the energy-dependent dibaryon potential with a momentum-dependent separable form to facilitate many-body calculations, while retaining the rapid convergence in the \(^1S_0\) channel. It contrasts with the minimally modified Weinberg scheme of Long (2012), which converges slowly in \(^1S_0\), and with the proposal by Mishra (2021) to promote two-pion exchange to leading order. The results enable future applications to nuclear structure calculations, as already explored by Yang (2020, 2021) and Sanchez Sanchez (2020), and raise open questions about the role of three-body forces at leading order.
