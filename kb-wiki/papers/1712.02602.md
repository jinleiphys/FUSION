# Towards the full realization of the RIBLL2 beam line at the HIRFL-CSR complex

> The RIBLL2 in-flight separator at IMP, the secondary beam line between two storage rings at the \blue{\uwave{Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL-CSR)}}, has been commissioned to study the rare-isotope beam (RIB) physics at around 300 MeV/nucleon for the first time, in combination of the external target facility (ETF). The unambiguous particle identification in mass and charge states for $^{18}$O and $^{40}$Ar fragments has been achieved in recent experiments. A full realization of RIBLL2 will open many potentials to address important RIB physics problems at around 300 MeV/nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the first half of the RIBLL2 in-flight separator, combined with the external target facility (ETF), has achieved unambiguous particle identification for rare isotopes produced by fragmentation of 18O and 40Ar beams at around 300 MeV/nucleon, but notes that full realization of the entire RIBLL2 beam line (F0 to F4) with a complete detector system is still required to reach its full potential as a separator-spectrometer.

## Method

The first half of RIBLL2 (F0 to F2) was used as a fragment separator, with heavy ions extracted from the CSRm synchrotron to bombard a primary beryllium target at the entrance. Rare isotopes were separated in flight using magnetic rigidity (Bρ) selection via slits at the dispersive focal plane F1, then delivered to the ETF. Particle identification was achieved by coincidence measurements of energy loss (ΔE) at ETF, Bρ analysis at F1, and time-of-flight (TOF) between plastic scintillators at F1 and ETF.

## Key numbers

- 77 isotopes were produced and identified via fragmentations of 18O and 40Ar beams.
- The separator achieved a charge resolution of 0.12 (σ) using a multi-sampling ionization chamber (MUSIC), demonstrated for 40Ar fragments at 320 MeV/nucleon on a 10 mm-thick Be target.
- The intrinsic TOF resolution from detectors was determined as 80 ps (σ), with the TOF-stop detector at ETF achieving about 30 ps (σ).
- A maximum dispersion of 1.169 m/% can be reached at the dispersive planes F1 and F3.
- The momentum resolution power of 1200 can be expected for a beam size at half width of 1 mm.
- The total TOF length from F1 to F4 is 42 meters, which is 16 meters longer than that of the RIBLL2-ETF configuration.

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of the RIBLL2 separator at the HIRFL-CSR complex in Lanzhou, which was previously used primarily to deliver radioactive isotopes into the CSRe storage ring for mass measurements (CSR, 2016). The demonstrated particle identification for isotopes with Z < 30 at around 300 MeV/nucleon opens opportunities for studying nuclear spin and isospin excitations (Osterfeld, 1992) and nucleon density distributions of exotic nuclei (Tanihata, 2013; Meng, 2006). Full realization of RIBLL2 in separator-spectrometer mode will enable a new direct method to weigh masses of neutron-rich nuclei (Sun, 2015) and provide experience for designing the high-energy fragment separator at the future High Intensity heavy ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF, 2013).
