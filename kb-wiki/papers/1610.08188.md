# Effects of the Lorentz invariance violation in Coulomb interaction in nuclei and atoms

> Anisotropy in the speed of light that has been constrained by Michelson-Morley-type experiments also generates anisotropy in the Coulomb interactions. This anisotropy can manifest itself as an energy anisotropy in nuclear and atomic experiments. Here the experimental limits on Lorentz violation in 21Ne are used to improve the limits on the Lorentz symmetry in the photon sector, namely the anisotropy of the speed of light and the Coulomb interactions, by 7 orders of magnitude in comparison with previous experiments: the speed of light is isotropic to a part in E-28.

## Key claim

The paper shows that existing experimental limits on Lorentz violation in the 21Ne nucleus can be reinterpreted to constrain the anisotropy of the speed of light and Coulomb interactions to a part in 10^28, improving previous laboratory limits by 7 orders of magnitude, but only under the assumption that Lorentz violation in the strong interaction sector is zero or has been removed by a coordinate transformation.

## Method

The authors calculated the energy shift due to Lorentz invariance violation (LLIV) in the Coulomb potential of a deformed nucleus by integrating the anisotropic Coulomb potential from the Standard Model Extension (SME) with a spheroidal nuclear charge distribution. They derived a proportionality relation between the nuclear tensor M_ij and the nuclear electric quadrupole moment tensor Q_ij, obtaining both an analytical estimate and a numerical result from integration. They then combined this with existing experimental limits on neutron LLIV coefficients from the 21Ne NMR experiment of Romalis (2013) to extract limits on the photon-sector coefficients kappa_DE.

## Key numbers

- For the 21Ne nucleus, the nuclear quadrupole moment Q = 10.3 fm^2, and the calculated nuclear tensor M = 0.25 MeV.
- The numerical proportionality factor between M and Q is M = 0.06 (Z-1)e^2 Q / R^3 = 0.055 (Z-1)/A (Q/fm^2) MeV, with nuclear radius R = 1.15 A^(1/3) fm.
- The extracted limits on the photon-sector coefficients (assuming zero nucleon LLIV) are: kappa_DE^X = -(13 ± 12) × 10^(-29), kappa_DE^Y = (7 ± 9) × 10^(-29), kappa_DE^Z = -(3.2 ± 3.7) × 10^(-29), kappa_DE^- = -(3.7 ± 4.5) × 10^(-29).
- These limits are 7 to 11 orders of magnitude better than previous laboratory measurements from Kostelecky (2010), Eisele (2009), Hermann (2009), Hohensee (2013), and Pruttivarasin (2014).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the 21Ne NMR experiment by Romalis (2013), which originally set limits on neutron LLIV coefficients c_ij, by showing that the same data also constrain photon-sector LLIV coefficients kappa_DE through the anisotropic Coulomb interaction in deformed nuclei. The authors note that in atoms, the virial theorem forces the photon and electron LLIV coefficients to appear only as a sum, making them inseparable, but in nuclei the strong interaction breaks this relationship, allowing independent constraints. This enables future nuclear experiments to probe Lorentz violation in the photon sector at levels competitive with or exceeding Michelson-Morley-type experiments.
