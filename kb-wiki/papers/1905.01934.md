# Operator Relations for Gravitational Form Factors

> The form factors for the hadron matrix element of the QCD energy-momentum tensor not only describe the coupling of the hadron with a graviton as the ``gravitational form factors'', but also serve as unique quantities for describing the shape inside the hadron reflecting dynamics of quarks and gluons, such as the internal shear forces acting on the quarks/gluons and their pressure distributions. We consider the gravitational form factors for a hadron, in particular, for a (pseudo)scalar hadron and for the nucleon. We derive and clarify the relations satisfied by the gravitational form factors as direct consequences of the symmetries and the equations of motion in QCD, and connections to the generalized parton distributions. Our results reveal that the gravitational form factors are related to the higher-twist quark-gluon correlation effects inside the hadrons and also to QCD trace anomaly.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factors of hadrons are related to higher-twist quark-gluon correlation effects and the QCD trace anomaly, with the D-term form factor corresponding to a twist-three quark-gluon correlation rather than being directly accessible from the generalized parton distribution sum rules alone.

## Method

The author derives exact operator identities from the QCD equations of motion and symmetries, relating the gravitational form factors to matrix elements of quark-gluon interaction operators. Using light-cone gauge fixing and light-cone quantization techniques, the quark part of the energy-momentum tensor is decomposed to reveal the operator content of the D-term form factor. The analysis is performed for both nucleon and spin-0 hadron (pion) cases, and one-loop results for the QCD trace anomaly are used to constrain the forward-limit form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleon, the sum rules give A_q(0)+A_g(0)=1 from the total energy-momentum, and (A_q(0)+B_q(0)+A_g(0)+B_g(0))/2=1/2 from the total angular momentum
- For large momentum transfer t, the nucleon gravitational form factors behave as A_q(t) ~ 1/t^2 and D_q(t) ~ 1/t^3, based on dimensional counting where F_1^q(t→∞) ~ 1/t^2
- For the pion, the large t behavior gives Θ_{2q}(t) ~ 1/t and Θ_{1q}(t) ~ 1/t^2, based on the vector form factor scaling F_v(t→∞) ~ 1/t
- The asymptotic value of the renormalized form factor C̅_q^R in the chiral limit as μ→∞ is -0.146 for n_f=3 flavors and -0.103 for n_f=2 flavors
- The gluon condensate at μ=1 GeV gives ⟨p|F^2|p⟩ ≃ -8.61 m_N^2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting gravitational form factors to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as described by Burkert (2018) and Polyakov (2018), and provides the operator basis for interpreting experimental extractions from deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production. The results enable quantitative connections between the D-term form factor and twist-three quark-gluon correlations, and provide renormalization scale evolution equations for the form factors C̅_{q,g} that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations. The pion results are relevant for extracting gravitational form factors from Belle data on γ*γ → π^0π^0 as proposed by Kumano (2017).
