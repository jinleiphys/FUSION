# Global spin alignment of vector mesons and strong force fields in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed large positive deviation of the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ for φ mesons from 1/3 in STAR data can be explained by local correlations or fluctuations of strong force fields (φ meson fields) on the hadronization hyper-surface, rather than by conventional mechanisms such as vorticity or electromagnetic fields which predict effects orders of magnitude smaller.

## Method

The authors review the quark coalescence model (QCM) formalism for calculating the spin density matrix of vector mesons from the spin polarization vectors of constituent quarks and antiquarks. They apply both a non-relativistic QCM and a more rigorous relativistic QCM based on the closed-time-path (Schwinger-Keldysh) method in quantum field theory, where the spin polarization vector for strange quarks and antiquarks includes contributions from thermal vorticity fields and φ meson fields. The resulting ρ₀₀ depends on local correlations of φ fields squared, with parameters F_T² and F_z² representing averaged field fluctuations in transverse and longitudinal directions relative to the beam axis, determined by fitting to STAR data across collision energies.

## Key numbers

- ρ₀₀^φ = 0.3512 ± 0.0017 (stat.) ± 0.0017 (syst.), averaged over Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 11.5 to 62.4 GeV for centrality 20%–60%
- ρ₀₀^K*0 = 0.3356 ± 0.0034 (stat.) ± 0.0043 (syst.), averaged over Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 11.5 to 54.4 GeV for centrality 20%–60%
- The deviation of ρ₀₀^φ from 1/3 has a significance of 7.4 σ
- STAR data cover beam energies from √s_NN = 11.5 to 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions
- Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV from ALICE (Acharya 2019) show ρ₀₀ consistent with 1/3 within large uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework first proposed by Liang and Wang (2004) for spin alignment of vector mesons along the orbital angular momentum direction in heavy-ion collisions, following the breakthrough measurement of global Λ hyperon polarization by STAR (2017). The interpretation builds on Sheng, Oliva, and Wang (2019) who proposed that local φ meson field fluctuations could produce large positive deviations of ρ₀₀ from 1/3. The results open a new avenue to investigate non-perturbative properties of quantum chromodynamics and strongly interacting quark matter, with the expectation that average field fluctuations ⟨E_φ²⟩ and ⟨B_φ²⟩ could be calculable using lattice QCD. The explanation remains subject to debate and requires further systematic experimental and theoretical studies.
