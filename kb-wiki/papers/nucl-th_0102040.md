# How a quark-gluon plasma phase modifies the bounds on extra dimensions from SN 1987a

> The shape of the neutrino pulse from the supernova SN1987a provides one of the most stringent constraints on the size of large, compact, "gravity-only" extra dimensions. Previously, calculations have been carried out for a newly-born proto-neutron star with a temperature of about 50 MeV at nuclear matter density. It is arguable that, due to the extreme conditions in the interior of the star, matter might be a quark-gluon plasma, where the relevant degrees of freedom are quarks and gluons rather than nucleons. We consider an energy-loss scenario where seconds after rebounce the core of the star consists of a hot and dense quark-gluon plasma. Adopting a simplified model of the plasma we derive the necessary energy-loss formulae in the soft-radiation limit. The emissivity is found to be comparable to the one for nuclear matter and bounds on the radius of extra dimensions are similar to those found previously from nuclear matter calculations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that even if the core of SN 1987a seconds after bounce consists of a quark-gluon plasma rather than nuclear matter, the bounds on the size of extra dimensions remain similar to those previously derived from nuclear matter calculations, because the emissivity from quark-quark scattering is comparable to that from nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The author calculates the energy loss from soft graviton, KK-graviton, and KK-dilaton radiation emitted during quark-quark scattering in a degenerate QCD plasma, using the soft-radiation limit where the emitted particle's momentum is small compared to the quark momenta. A simplified plasma model is adopted consisting of three massless light quark flavors (u, d, s) at high density, and the QCD coupling constant is evaluated using the leading-log running coupling formula. To handle divergences from colinear scattering, a Debye screening mass is introduced into the gluon propagator, and the emissivity is computed for unphysically high densities (up to 10^7 times nuclear matter density) and then extrapolated down to the physical regime.

## Key numbers

- The maximum possible emissivity for a new energy loss process that does not conflict with the SN 1987a neutrino signal is 10^19 ergs/g/s (from Raffelt).
- Nuclear matter density n_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}; the star's core density may reach up to ~15 times n_0, with temperatures up to ~100 MeV.
- For quark matter at n_q = 10-20 n_0 and T = 50 MeV, the quark chemical potential is approximately 500-600 MeV.
- The Debye mass for cold quark matter of three flavors is given by q_D^2 = 6 α_s μ_q^2 / π^2, and for densities n_q = 100-10^7 n_0, the running coupling α_s ranges from about 0.48 to 0.13.
- The ratio of nucleons to quarks participating in scattering at n_B = 10 n_0 is N_N/N_q ≈ 0.3.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by BHK99, CuP99, and HPR00 that derived bounds on extra dimensions from SN 1987a assuming nuclear matter at ~50 MeV, and it complements the more rigorous approach of Hanhart et al. (2001) who performed detailed simulations of exotic radiation effects on the neutrino signal. The result enables future work to refine bounds on extra dimensions using more realistic models of the quark-gluon plasma phase, including color superconducting phases that may occur below the critical temperature T_c ≈ 50 MeV.
