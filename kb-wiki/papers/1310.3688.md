# Self-consistent Green's functions formalism with three-body interactions

> We extend the self-consistent Green's functions formalism to take into account three-body interactions. We analyze the perturbative expansion in terms of Feynman diagrams and define effective one- and two-body interactions, which allows for a substantial reduction of the number of diagrams. The procedure can be taken as a generalization of the normal ordering of the Hamiltonian to fully correlated density matrices. We give examples up to third order in perturbation theory. To define nonperturbative approximations, we extend the equation of motion method in the presence of three-body interactions. We propose schemes that can provide nonperturbative resummation of three-body interactions. We also discuss two different extensions of the Koltun sum rule to compute the ground state of a many-body system.

## Key claim

The paper extends the self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) formalism to include three-body interactions (3BFs) by defining effective one- and two-body interactions that subsume interaction-reducible diagrams, but the approach is formal and does not present numerical implementations; the authors explicitly state they "will not comment on the actual numerical implementation of the approach."

## Method

The authors analyze the perturbative expansion of the one-body Green's function in terms of Feynman diagrams for a Hamiltonian containing two-body and three-body interactions. They define effective one-body and two-body interaction operators by contracting three-body interaction vertices with fully correlated one-body and two-body propagators, which eliminates articulation vertices and reduces the set of diagrams to interaction-irreducible skeletons. They extend the equation of motion method to include three-body interactions and propose nonperturbative resummation schemes, including improved ladder and ring summations. Two extensions of the Koltun sum rule are derived to compute the ground-state energy in the presence of three-body forces.

## Key numbers

* The paper reports that for nuclear interactions, three-body forces are typically weaker than two-body forces, with the ratio approximately `<\widehat{W}> ≈ 1/10 <\widehat{V}>` (from the full text, citing grange1989 and Epel09).
* At third order in perturbation theory, the effective interaction approach reduces the number of interaction-irreducible self-energy diagrams from 53 to 17 (stated explicitly in the text).
* At second order, the effective interaction approach reduces the number of skeleton diagrams from 5 to 2 (stated explicitly in the text).

## Context

This work extends the SCGF formalism, originally developed for Hamiltonians with only two-body interactions (dating to the 1960s work of Martin and Schwinger, Fetter and Walecka, and Abrikosov et al.), to include three-body forces that are essential for nuclear saturation and ab-initio nuclear structure calculations. The effective interaction approach generalizes the normal ordering procedure previously applied to three-body forces in nuclear physics by Hagen (2007), Bogner (2010), and Roth (2012), but uses fully correlated density matrices rather than reference-state averages. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of three-body interactions in nonperturbative many-body calculations for nuclear systems and other fermionic systems where induced many-body forces arise from model-space truncation or renormalization group techniques.
