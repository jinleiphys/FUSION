# Theoretical investigation of charge-changing cross section and interaction cross section for Be, B, C, N, O, and F isotopes on $^{12}$C at 200-1050 MeV/nucleon

> To establish credibility for the use of the Slater determinant harmonic oscillator (SDHO) density in predicting root-mean-square proton and neutron radii for Be, B, C, N, O, and F isotopes [M. Imran et al., {\color{blue}Phys. Rev. C {\bf 110}, 014623 (2024)}], in this work we propose to study charge-changing and interaction cross sections for the said isotopes on $^{12}$C at a wider range of incident energies (200-1050 MeV/nucleon), involving different density distributions; the calculations also assess the importance of nuclear medium effects. Working within the framework of the Glauber model, we involve two-parameter Fermi (2pF) and three-parameter Fermi (3pF) shapes of density distributions, and use the in-medium as well as free behavior of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) amplitude. The results provide enough basis to support the matter radii of exotic isotopes obtained using SDHO densities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charge-changing and interaction cross sections for Be, B, C, N, O, and F isotopes on a carbon-12 target are insensitive to the choice of projectile density distribution (Slater determinant harmonic oscillator, two-parameter Fermi, or three-parameter Fermi) as long as the densities describe the nuclear surface region similarly, thereby supporting the matter radii previously obtained using SDHO densities in Imran et al. (2024).

## Method

The authors performed Glauber model calculations for charge-changing and interaction cross sections of Be, B, C, N, O, and F isotopes on a carbon-12 target at incident energies from 200 to 1050 MeV per nucleon. They employed three different density distributions for the projectile nuclei: Slater determinant harmonic oscillator (SDHO), two-parameter Fermi (2pF), and three-parameter Fermi (3pF), and used both in-medium and free nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. For charge-changing cross sections, a phenomenological correction parameter epsilon was introduced to account for the contribution of projectile neutrons, and the parameters of the 2pF and 3pF densities were adjusted to reproduce experimental charge radii and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Correction parameter epsilon_avg for beryllium isotopes using 2pF density: 1.075 (average across Be isotopes)
- Correction parameter epsilon_avg for carbon isotopes using SDHO density: 0.993 (average across C isotopes)
- Proton radius for beryllium-7 extracted using 2pF density: 2.6402 fm (at 772 MeV/nucleon on carbon-12)
- Proton radius for beryllium-14 extracted using SDHO density: 2.5612 fm (at 833 MeV/nucleon on carbon-12)
- Neutron radius for beryllium-11 extracted using 2pF density: 2.8839 fm (at 790 MeV/nucleon on carbon-12)
- Neutron radius for beryllium-14 extracted using SDHO density: 3.1937 fm (at 800 MeV/nucleon on carbon-12)
- Experimental charge-changing cross section for beryllium-7 on carbon-12 at 772 MeV/nucleon: 706 plus or minus 8 mb
- Experimental interaction cross section for beryllium-14 on carbon-12 at 800 MeV/nucleon: 1082 plus or minus 34 mb

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model analysis of Imran et al. (2024), which used SDHO densities to extract proton and neutron radii for the same isotopic chains, by testing the sensitivity of cross sections to different density parameterizations (2pF and 3pF) over a wider energy range (200-1050 MeV/nucleon). The results confirm that charge-changing and interaction cross sections are density-independent for predicting radii, supporting the reliability of the SDHO-based radii for exotic nuclei. This enables future studies to use simpler density parameterizations for extracting nuclear sizes from cross section measurements, and validates the approach of Bhagwat and Gambhir, Yamaguchi et al., and Li et al. for using a correction parameter in charge-changing cross section calculations.
