# The chiral magnetic effect and the chiral spin symmetry in QCD above Tc

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is an exact statement that connects via the axial anomaly the electric current in a system consisting of interacting fermions and gauge field with chirality imbalance that is put into a strong external magnetic field. Experimental search of the magnetically induced current in QCD in heavy ion collisions above a pseudocritical temperature hints, though not yet conclusive, that the induced current is either small or vanishing. This would imply that the chirality imbalance in QCD above $T_c$ that could be generated via topological fluctuations is at most very small. Here we present the most general reason for absence (smallness) of the chirality imbalance in QCD above Tc. It was recently found on the lattice that QCD above Tc is approximately chiral spin (CS) symmetric with the symmetry breaking at the level of a few percent. The CS transformations mix the right- and left-handed components of quarks. Then an exact CS symmetry would require absence of any chirality imbalance. Consequently an approximate CS symmetry admits at most a very small chirality imbalance in QCD above Tc. Hence the absence or smallness of an magnetically induced current observed in heavy ion collisions could be considered as experimental evidence for emergence of the CS symmetry above Tc.

# The chiral magnetic effect and the chiral spin symmetry in QCD above Tc

## Key claim
The smallness or absence of a magnetically induced electric current in heavy ion collisions above the pseudocritical temperature, as hinted by experimental data, can be explained by the emergence of approximate chiral spin (CS) symmetry in QCD above Tc, because exact CS symmetry would forbid any chirality imbalance and the observed symmetry breaking at the few-percent level allows only a very small imbalance.

## Method
The author reviews the definition of chiral spin SU(2)CS symmetry, which mixes right- and left-handed quarks and is a symmetry of the chromoelectric interaction but not of the chromomagnetic interaction or quark kinetic term. He then examines the transformation properties of the axial chemical potential term under SU(2)CS, showing that it transforms as a triplet and is therefore forbidden in a CS-symmetric theory, which implies that the chiral magnetic conductivity σ5 must vanish if the symmetry were exact.

## Key numbers
- The pseudocritical temperature Tc determined from chiral susceptibility is around 155 MeV in Nf=2+1 QCD.
- Lattice results at T = 1.2 Tc show CS symmetry breaking at the level of less than 5%.
- The CS symmetry is observed on the lattice in the temperature range Tc – 3 Tc.

## Context
This work extends lattice observations by Glozman (2019) and collaborators (R1, R2, R3) that QCD above Tc exhibits approximate chiral spin and SU(4) symmetries, which are not symmetries of the Dirac Lagrangian but of the chromoelectric interaction. It contradicts the expectation that topological fluctuations should produce a chirality imbalance and a measurable chiral magnetic effect (CME) as predicted by Kharzeev (2008) and others. The result enables interpreting the null or small CME signal from RHIC and LHC experiments as experimental evidence for the CS symmetry regime, which the author terms a "stringy fluid" of chirally symmetric quarks bound by chromoelectric interactions.
