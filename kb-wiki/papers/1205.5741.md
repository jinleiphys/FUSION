# Parton Transport via Transverse and Longitudinal Scattering in Dense Media

> The effect of multiple scatterings on the propagation of hard partonic jets in a dense nuclear medium is studied in the framework of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus. Power counting arguments based on the Glauber improved Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory are used to identify the class of leading power corrections to the process of a single parton traversing the extended medium without emission. It turns out that the effect of longitudinal drag and diffusion (often referred to as straggling) is as important as transverse scattering, when relying solely on power counting arguments. With the inclusion of momentum exchanges in both transverse and longitudinal directions between the traversing hard parton and the constituents of the medium, we derive a differential equation for the time (or distance) evolution of the hard parton momentum distribution. Keeping up to the second order in a momentum gradient expansion, this equation describes in-medium evolution of hard jets which experience longitudinal drag and diffusion plus the transverse broadening caused by multiple scatterings from the medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, based on power counting in the Glauber-improved Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory, longitudinal momentum exchange (drag and diffusion, or straggling) is as important as transverse scattering for a hard parton traversing dense nuclear matter without radiation, contrary to prior assumptions that medium gluons have vanishingly small light-cone components in both directions.

## Method

The authors study semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus and compute higher-twist contributions to the hadronic tensor from multiple scatterings of the struck quark with gluon fields in the nuclear medium, using power counting rules from GiSCET. They retain corrections up to order λ (where λ is a small dimensionless parameter) in the propagators, allowing both transverse and longitudinal momentum exchanges (k⊥ ∼ λQ, k⁻ ∼ λQ, k⁺ ∼ λ²Q), and resum multiple scatterings via a momentum gradient expansion up to second order to derive a differential equation for the time evolution of the hard parton momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The hard parton momentum is assumed to scale as q = (q⁺, q⁻, q⊥) ∼ (λ²Q, Q, λQ), where Q is a hard scale and λ is a small dimensionless number.
- The medium gluon momentum components are taken as k ∼ (λ², λ², λ)Q in prior work, but the paper allows k⁻ ∼ λQ instead of λ²Q.
- The Bjorken variable is defined as x_B = Q² / (2p⁺q⁻) in the Breit frame of DIS.
- The nuclear density ρ appears in the factor C_{p₀, p₁ … p_n} = A C_p^A (ρ/(2p⁺))ⁿ for non-interacting nucleons in a uniform-density nucleus.
- The longitudinal extent of the nucleus is denoted L⁻, and the resummation yields a factor 1/n! from the product of θ-functions over Y_i⁻ integrals from 0 to L⁻.

## Context

This work extends the theory of parton energy loss based on the Glauber-improved Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory (GiSCET) approach of Idilbi and D'Eramo (2010) and is closely linked with the multiple-scattering version of the Higher Twist (msHT) scheme of Majumder (2007, 2009). It contradicts prior calculations that assumed medium gluons have vanishingly small light-cone components in both the direction of the jet's momentum and opposite to it. The derived transport coefficients D_{L1}, D_{L2}, and D_{T2 are related to the elastic energy loss rate ê, longitudinal diffusion ê₂, and transverse diffusion ˆq, respectively, and the formalism enables future inclusion of radiation effects and the study of jet shower evolution, energy loss distribution within and outside the jet cone, and energy-momentum deposition into the medium.
