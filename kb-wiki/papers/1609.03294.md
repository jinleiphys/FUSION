# Gamma Matrix Expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter Equation for Nucleon-Nucleon System

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-nucleon system can be reduced to a set of simultaneous equations for the coefficients of a Gamma-matrix expansion of the BS amplitude, which under auxiliary conditions (specifically, imposing the constraint \(p_0\,\chi_B(p)=0\) and assuming the relative-time dependence is neglected at \(t=0\)) become equivalent to nonrelativistic equations suitable for investigating the two-nucleon system.

## Method
The author expands the modified Bethe-Salpeter amplitude \(\chi(p)_c\) in a complete set of 16 Gamma matrices (scalar, vector, pseudoscalar, axial-vector, and tensor components) and substitutes this expansion into the BS equation with a meson-exchange kernel in the ladder approximation, using \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\pi\), and \(\rho\) mesons. By imposing the auxiliary condition \(p\cdot\chi_V(p)=0\) (which leads to \(\chi(p)\sim\delta(p_0)\) and thus neglects relative-time dependence) and setting the axial tensor components \(\chi_{T ij}(p)=0\), the author derives coupled integral equations for the pseudoscalar (\(\chi_P\)), axial-vector (\(\chi_a\)), and polar tensor (\(\chi_i\)) components in momentum space, with the interaction strengths modulated by coefficients \(\lambda^{P,A,T}_j\) listed in Table 1.

## Key numbers
- The meson masses used are \(m_\sigma\), \(m_\omega\), \(m_\pi\), and \(m_\rho\) (specific numerical values not given in the text).
- The isospin factor \(\vec{\tau}_1\cdot\vec{\tau}_2 = -3\) for the isospin singlet state (\(T=0\)) and \(+1\) for the triplet state (\(T=1\)).
- The tensor coupling mixing parameter is \(a \equiv \kappa/(2M)\) with \(\kappa \equiv f_\rho/g_\rho\).
- Table 1 gives the coefficients \(\lambda^{P,A,T}_{\sigma,\omega,\pi,\rho}\): for the pseudoscalar equation, \(\lambda_\sigma^P=1\), \(\lambda_\omega^P=-4\), \(\lambda_\pi^P=1\), \(\lambda_\rho^P=-4+3a^2m_\rho^2\); for the axial-vector equation, \(\lambda_\sigma^A=1\), \(\lambda_\omega^A=2\), \(\lambda_\pi^A=1\), \(\lambda_\rho^A=2+a^2m_\rho^2\); for the tensor equation, \(\lambda_\sigma^T=1\), \(\lambda_\omega^T=0\), \(\lambda_\pi^T=1\), \(\lambda_\rho^T=-a^2m_\rho^2\).
- The \(\omega\) meson mass is noted to be \(5\sim6\) times as heavy as the pion mass.

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the two-nucleon system, following the standard ladder approximation and the approach of Nakanishi (no year given in text) for the center-of-mass frame. The derived equations with auxiliary conditions enable a connection to nonrelativistic potential models and are intended for studying the nuclear force, including the role of pion pseudovector coupling and \(\rho\) meson tensor coupling. The author notes that the \(\omega\) meson does not contribute to the tensor equation (since \(f_\omega\sim0\)), and that the derivative terms in the vector boson propagator are negligible for finite nuclei such as \({}^{16}\)O because the momentum transfer is much smaller than the \(\omega\) mass. The work enables future calculations of elastic scattering and spin correlation parameters, as referenced in Kinpara (no year given in text).
