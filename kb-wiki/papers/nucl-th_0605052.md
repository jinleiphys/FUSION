# The order, shape and critical point for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition

> The order, shape and critical point for the phase transition between the hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma are considered in a thermodynamical consistent approach. The hadronic phase is taken as Van der Waals gas of all the known hadronic mass spectrum particles $m_H\le 2.0$ GeV as well as Hagedorn bubbles which correspond hadronic states with mass spectrum $m_H> 2.0$ GeV. The density of states for Hagedorn bubbles is derived by calculating the microcanonical ensemble for a bag of quarks and gluons with specific internal color-flavor symmetry. The mixed-grand and microcanonical ensembles are derived for massless and massive flavors. We find Hagedorn bubbles are strongly suppressed in the dilute hadronic matter and they appear just below the line of the phase transition. The order of the phase transition depends on Hagedorn bubble's internal color-flavor structure and the volume fluctuation as well. On the other hand, the highly compressed hadronic matter undergoes a smooth phase transition from the gas of known mass spectrum hadrons to another one dominated by Hagedorn bubbles with specific internal color-flavor structure before the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma takes place at last. The phase transition is found a first order for the intermediate and large chemical potentials. The existence of the tri-critical point depends on the modification of the bubble's internal structure specified by a phenomenological parameter $γ\proptoμ_B$ in the medium.

## Key claim

The order of the phase transition between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma depends on the Hagedorn bubble's internal color-flavor structure and volume fluctuation, with the existence of a tri-critical point determined by a phenomenological parameter gamma proportional to baryon chemical potential in the medium.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase as a Van der Waals gas of known hadronic mass spectrum particles up to 2.0 GeV plus Hagedorn bubbles for states above 2.0 GeV, deriving the density of states for these bubbles from the microcanonical ensemble of a confined quark-gluon bag with specific internal color-flavor symmetry. They compute mixed-grand and microcanonical ensembles for both massless and massive quark flavors, incorporating volume fluctuations by going beyond the standard MIT bag model with a sharp surface to bag models with extended surfaces and volume distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic mass spectrum cutoff: particles with mass m_H less than or equal to 2.0 GeV are treated as known hadrons; states with m_H greater than 2.0 GeV are treated as Hagedorn bubbles
- The phase transition is found to be first order for intermediate and large chemical potentials
- The tri-critical point existence depends on a phenomenological parameter gamma proportional to mu_B (baryon chemical potential)

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model with internal symmetry developed by Redlich (1979) and the gas of bags approach studied by Gorenstein et al. (1983), who measured volume fluctuation for hadronic bags using the isobaric partition function but failed to predict the phase transition to deconfined quark-gluon plasma. The authors build on Auberson et al. (1986), who showed that deconfined QGP phase transition is possible when asymptotic volume fluctuation is correctly accounted for, and demonstrate that Hagedorn bubbles are strongly suppressed in dilute hadronic matter but appear just below the phase transition line, enabling a scenario where highly compressed hadronic matter undergoes a smooth transition to a Hagedorn bubble-dominated phase before the final transition to quark-gluon plasma.
