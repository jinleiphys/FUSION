# Ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density in multifragmentation of Au + Au

> The ratio of the shear viscosity ($η$) to entropy density ($s$) for the intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions has been calculated by using the Green-Kubo method in the framework of the quantum molecular dynamics model. The theoretical curve of $η/s$ as a function of the incident energy for the head-on Au+Au collisions displays that a minimum region of $η/s$ has been approached at higher incident energies, where the minimum $η/s$ value is about 7 times Kovtun-Son- Starinets (KSS) bound (1/4$π$). We argue that the onset of minimum $η/s$ region at higher incident energies corresponds to the nuclear liquid gas phase transition in nuclear multifragmentation.

# Ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density in multifragmentation of Au + Au

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) for head-on Au+Au collisions, calculated using the Green-Kubo method within the IQMD model, exhibits a minimum region at higher incident energies around 120 MeV/nucleon, with a minimum value approximately 7 times the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of 1/4π. The authors argue that this minimum region corresponds to the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in multifragmentation, though they note that the location of the minimum in η/s (around 120 MeV/nucleon) does not exactly coincide with the turning point in intermediate mass fragment multiplicity (around 90 MeV/nucleon).

## Method

The authors simulated head-on Au+Au collisions using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for beam energies from 70 to 200 MeV/nucleon. Thermodynamic properties (temperature and entropy density) were extracted from nuclear matter in a central sphere of radius 3.5 fm using the generalized hot Thomas-Fermi formalism (GHTFF). Shear viscosity was calculated via the Green-Kubo relation, where the shear component of the energy-momentum tensor correlation function was fitted to an exponential decay to extract the relaxation time, and the post-equilibration starting time was determined from the stopping parameter approaching unity.

## Key numbers

- Minimum η/s value is approximately 0.6, which is about 7 times the KSS bound (1/4π ≈ 0.08), for head-on Au+Au collisions.
- The minimum η/s region occurs at an incident energy around 120 MeV/nucleon for beam energies from 70 to 200 MeV/nucleon.
- The turning point (maximum) of intermediate mass fragment multiplicity (M_IMFs) occurs around 90 MeV/nucleon for head-on Au+Au collisions.
- The Gaussian width parameter L = 2.16 fm² was used for the Au+Au system.
- The central sphere radius used for thermodynamic extraction was R = 3.5 fm.
- The Skyrme potential parameters used were α = -356 MeV, β = 303 MeV, and γ = 7/6, corresponding to a soft equation of state, with ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of η/s in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions, which had been limited compared to ultra-relativistic collisions studying the quark-gluon plasma phase. The authors' prior work using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model (Li 2011) showed a gradual decrease of η/s at lower energies with saturation around 100 MeV/nucleon but no minimum, which they attribute to the absence of dynamical fluctuation and cluster formation in the BUU model. The present QMD calculation, which includes cluster formation, finds a minimum η/s region that may serve as a probe of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, complementing traditional signals such as intermediate mass fragment multiplicity. The results support the AdS/CFT conjecture that η/s is always larger than 1/4π.
