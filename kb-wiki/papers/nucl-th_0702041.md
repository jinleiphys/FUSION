# Microscopic calculation of symmetry projected nuclear level densities

> We present a quantum Monte Carlo method with exact projection on parity and angular momentum that is free of sign-problems for seniority-conserving nuclear interactions. This technique allows a microscopic calculation of angular momentum and parity projected nuclear level densities. We present results for the $^{55}$Fe, $^{56}$Fe and $^{57}$Fe isotopes. Signatures of the pairing phase transition are observed in the angular momentum distribution of the nuclear level density.

## Key claim
The paper presents a quantum Monte Carlo method with exact projection on parity and angular momentum that is free of sign problems for seniority-conserving nuclear interactions, enabling microscopic calculation of symmetry-projected nuclear level densities. The method is restricted to isovector J=0 pairing models, and including higher-order interaction multipoles would reintroduce a sign problem.

## Method
The authors formulate the nuclear pairing problem in the quasi-spin formalism and use a non-local loop update quantum Monte Carlo scheme based on a perturbative expansion of the partition function. They employ worm operators to sample configurations at fixed particle number and seniority, constraining moves to preserve angular momentum J and parity. Level densities are calculated in the saddle point approximation for the iron isotopes ⁵⁵Fe, ⁵⁶Fe, and ⁵⁷Fe within the complete 0f+1p+0g₉/₂ model space, with an extended 1s+0d+0f+1p+0g₉/₂+2s+1d space used to study truncation effects.

## Key numbers
- Inverse temperature β ranges from 0 to 2.5 MeV⁻¹ for the QMC calculations.
- Calculations are free of truncation effects for excitation energies up to E_x ≲ 20 MeV for the pf+g₉/₂ valence space and E_x ≲ 50 MeV for the sd+pf+g₉/₂+sd valence space.
- For ⁵⁶Fe, the backshifted Bethe formula fit yields Fermi gas parameter a = 5.741 ± 0.034 MeV⁻¹ and backshift Δ = 1.591 ± 0.057 MeV (fitted for 4.5 MeV < E_x < 40 MeV).
- The SMMC level density parameters from Nakada (1997) for comparison are a = 5.780 ± 0.055 MeV⁻¹ and Δ = 1.560 ± 0.161 MeV.
- A strong suppression of the J=1 level density relative to J=2 is observed for ⁵⁶Fe at low excitation energies, with ρ₁ ≈ ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method of Koonin (1997) and Nakada (1997) by introducing exact angular momentum and parity projection without sign problems for seniority-conserving interactions, overcoming the sign problem that limited earlier angular momentum projection work by Alhassid (2006). The results enable microscopic tests of the spin-cutoff model of Bloch (1954) and Ericson (1960) and the backshifted Bethe formula, revealing signatures of the pairing phase transition in the angular momentum distribution of nuclear level densities. The calculations show strong parity dependence in angular momentum projected level densities for excitation energies up to ~20 MeV, which could be compared with experimental data from Kalmykov (2006).
