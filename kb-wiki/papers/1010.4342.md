# Distribution of Resonance Widths and Dynamics of Continuum Coupling

> We analyze the statistics of resonance widths in a many-body Fermi system with open decay channels. Depending on the strength of continuum coupling, such a system reveals growing deviations from the standard chi-square (Porter-Thomas) width distribution. The deviations emerge from the process of increasing interaction of intrinsic states through common decay channels; in the limit of perfect coupling this process leads to the super-radiance phase transition. The width distribution depends also on the intrinsic dynamics (chaotic vs regular). The results presented here are important for understanding the recent experimental data concerning the width distribution for neutron resonances in nuclei.

# Distribution of Resonance Widths and Dynamics of Continuum Coupling

## Key claim
The standard Porter-Thomas (chi-squared with ν=1) distribution for resonance widths is valid only for extremely weak continuum coupling; as the coupling strength increases, the best-fit ν value decreases below the number of open channels M, and the quality of the chi-squared fit degrades, with deviations appearing already at the weak-coupling regime relevant for low-energy neutron resonances in heavy nuclei.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian H - (i/2)W, where H is the Hermitian Hamiltonian of the closed system (modeled either by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) or by the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) for 6 fermions in 12 single-particle states) and W is a factorized matrix built from real decay amplitudes coupling intrinsic states to M open channels. They computed complex eigenvalues for 10^3 random realizations, selected states at the center of the energy band, and fitted the resulting width distributions (normalized to their average) with a chi-squared distribution with ν degrees of freedom, using the reduced chi-square criterion χ²_r ≈ 1 as a measure of fit quality.

## Key numbers
- For the GOE intrinsic Hamiltonian with M=1 open channel, at the super-radiance transition κ=1, the tail of the width distribution follows a power law P(Γ/⟨Γ⟩) ∝ (Γ/⟨Γ⟩)^{-2}.
- For the TBRE model, the critical interaction for onset of strong chaos is v_cr/d_0 ≈ 1/20, where d_0 is the mean single-particle level spacing and v_0 is the variance of two-body random matrix elements.
- The TBRE simulations used n=6 fermions distributed over m=12 single-particle states, giving N=924 many-body states.
- At κ=0.5 for the GOE case, the level spacing distribution P(0) ≈ 0.2, indicating partial disappearance of level repulsion.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of super-radiance in open quantum systems (Dicke 1954) and the non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian formalism (Mahaux and Weidenmüller 1969) to explain recent experimental deviations from the Porter-Thomas distribution observed in neutron resonance data (Koehler 2010). The results contradict the interpretation that such deviations indicate a breakdown of nuclear theory, and instead show they arise naturally from continuum coupling effects previously studied in random matrix theory (Verbaarschot 1985, Sokolov and Zelevinsky 1989) and numerical simulations (Kleinwachter 1985, Mizutori 1993, Izrailev 1994). The work enables a proper statistical description of resonance widths that accounts for the collectivization of widths and the super-radiant phase transition, with implications for signal transmission through any complicated quantum system.
