# Evidence of a higher nodal band $α$+$^{44}$Ca cluster state in fusion reactions and $α$ clustering in $^{48}$Ti

> In the nucleus $^{48}$Ti, whose structure is essential in evaluating the half-life of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay ($0νββ$) of $^{48}$Ca, the existence of the $α$ cluster structure is shown for the first time. A unified description of scattering and structure is performed for the $α$+$^{44}$Ca system. By using a global potential, which reproduces experimental $α$+$^{44}$Ca scattering over a wide range of incident energies, $E_α$=18 - 100 MeV, it is shown that the observed $α$+$^{44}$Ca fusion excitation function at $E_α$=9 -18 MeV is described well. The bump at $E_α$=10.2 MeV is found to be due to a resonance which is a $7^-$ state of the higher nodal band with the $α$+$^{44}$Ca cluster structure in $^{48}$Ti. The local potential $α$+$^{44}$Ca cluster model locates the ground band of $^{48}$Ti in agreement with experiment and reproduces the enhanced $B(E2)$ values in the ground band well. This shows that collectivity due to $α$ clustering in $^{48}$Ti should be taken into account in the evaluation of the nuclear matrix element in the $0νββ$ double-$β$ decay of $^{48}$Ca.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the bump observed in the α+⁴⁴Ca fusion excitation function at Eα=10.2 MeV is due to a 7⁻ resonance of the higher nodal N=15 band with α+⁴⁴Ca cluster structure in ⁴⁸Ti, but this claim is conditional on using a global Luneburg lens-like potential that reproduces scattering data from 18–100 MeV and is then extrapolated to lower energies (9–18 MeV) with an energy-dependent imaginary strength adjusted for the threshold anomaly.

## Method

The author performed a unified description of α+⁴⁴Ca scattering and structure using a global optical potential with a Luneburg lens-like real part. Fusion cross sections were calculated using the short-ranged imaginary potential prescription, with the energy dependence of the imaginary strength modified linearly below 18 MeV according to the dispersion relation of the threshold anomaly. The phase shifts were analyzed with the real potential alone (imaginary potentials switched off) to identify resonances, and the bound and quasi-bound states were classified by the Wildermuth condition for Pauli-forbidden states.

## Key numbers

- The bump in the α+⁴⁴Ca fusion excitation function at Eα=10.2 MeV is reproduced and attributed to a 7⁻ resonance with width Γc.m.=0.18 MeV
- The 7⁻ state of the N=15 band lies at excitation energy Ex=18.8 MeV in ⁴⁸Ti, with the α threshold at 9.45 MeV
- The D181 potential (V=181 MeV) gives volume integral Jv=388 MeV fm³ at Eα=18 MeV and Jv=356 MeV fm³ at Eα=24.1 MeV
- The D171 potential (V=171 MeV, Jv=367 MeV fm³) locates the ground band: calculated B(E2: 2⁺→0⁺)=13.6 W.u. (experiment 15.0 W.u.) and B(E2: 4⁺→2⁺)=17.9 W.u. (experiment 18.4 W.u.)
- The calculated rms intercluster radius for the 0⁺ ground state is 4.41 fm
- The ground state wave function has 78.0% Nho=12 component and 22% higher Nho≥14 components

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of α clustering in the fp-shell region, following previous successful descriptions of α+⁴⁰Ca cluster structure in ⁴⁴Ti by Michel (1986, 1988) and Ohkubo (1988), and the recent discovery of higher nodal band states in ⁵²Ti by Ohkubo (2020). The finding contradicts earlier conclusions by Gaul (1969) that α+⁴⁴Ca scattering shows no backward enhancement due to strong absorption and thus no cluster structure. The result enables future evaluation of the nuclear matrix element for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of ⁴⁸Ca, as the α clustering collectivity in ⁴⁸Ti must be taken into account beyond the shell model within the Nho=12 model space.
