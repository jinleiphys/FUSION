# Electromagnetic transitions in an effective chiral Lagrangian with the eta-prime and light vector mesons

> We consider the chiral Lagrangian with a nonet of Goldstone bosons and a nonet of light vector mesons. The mixing between the pseudoscalar mesons eta and eta-prime is taken into account. A novel counting scheme is suggested that is based on hadrogenesis, which conjectures a mass gap in the meson spectrum of QCD in the limit of a large number of colors. Such a mass gap would justify to consider the vector mesons and the eta-prime meson as light degrees of freedom. The complete leading order Lagrangian is constructed and discussed. As a first application it is tested against electromagnetic transitions of light vector mesons to pseudoscalar mesons. Our parameters are determined by the experimental data on photon decays of the omega, phi and eta-prime meson. In terms of such parameters we predict the corresponding decays into virtual photons with either dielectrons or dimuons in the final state.

## Key claim

The authors construct a leading-order chiral Lagrangian that includes both a nonet of Goldstone bosons and a nonet of light vector mesons, incorporating eta-eta-prime mixing, based on a novel counting scheme derived from the hadrogenesis conjecture which assumes a mass gap in the large-Nc meson spectrum; the scheme is tested against electromagnetic decays of omega, phi, and eta-prime mesons, but the authors note that the mixing angle for eta-eta-prime has a large uncertainty and is treated as a free parameter to be determined from radiative vector-meson decays.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral U(3) Lagrangian with pseudoscalar meson fields (Phi) and vector meson fields in the antisymmetric tensor representation (Phi_mu_nu), using a power counting scheme based on the hadrogenesis conjecture that treats both vector meson masses and pseudoscalar meson masses as soft scales Q, with a hard scale Lambda_hard of at least 2-3 GeV. They determine the Lagrangian parameters by fitting to experimental data on photon decays of omega, phi, and eta-prime mesons, then use those parameters to predict decays into virtual photons with dielectron or dimuon final states.

## Key numbers

- The vector meson mass parameter m_V is set to approximately 0.764 GeV, with b_D = 0.92 plus or minus 0.05, to recover empirical vector meson masses
- The vector meson decay constant f_V is determined as (140 plus or minus 14) MeV from electromagnetic decays rho_0 to e+ e-, omega to e+ e-, and phi to e+ e-
- The eta-eta-prime mixing angle theta is approximately -11 degrees from the empirical eta and eta-prime masses using equation (dettheta), but the authors note that a 3 percent deviation in meson masses makes the mixing angle compatible with zero degrees
- The hard scale Lambda_hard is estimated to be at least 2-3 GeV based on the hadrogenesis conjecture

## Context

This work extends the counting scheme proposed by Lutz (2008) for a chiral Lagrangian with vector mesons, which had been applied at tree level by Leupold (2008) and Terschluesen (2010) and in a coupled-channel framework by Danilkin (2011). The authors aim to generalize and systematize those counting rules to incorporate the eta-prime meson, building on the hadrogenesis conjecture from Lutz (2001, 2003, 2004, 2005, 2007) and the summation scheme of Gasparyan (2010). The work enables predictions for electromagnetic transition form factors of vector to pseudoscalar mesons involving virtual photons, specifically processes such as omega to pi0 l+ l-, omega to eta l+ l-, and phi to eta l+ l- with l = mu, e.
