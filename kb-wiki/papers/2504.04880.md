# Investigation of the baryon time-like electromagnetic form factors in the electron-positron annihilation reactions

> Based on the experimental measurements of the electron-positron annihilation reactions into a baryon ($B$) and anti-baryon ($\bar{B}$) pair, the electromagnetic form factors of hyperons in the time-like region can be investigated within the vector meson dominance model. The theoretical model parameters are determined by fitting them to the total cross sections of the process $e^+e^-\to B \bar{B}$, and it is found that the current experimental data on the baryon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region can be well reproduced. In addition to the total cross sections, the electromagnetic form factors $G_E$ and $G_M$, and the charge radii of those baryons are also estimated, which are in agreement with the experimental data. On the other hand, we have also investigated the nonmonotonic behavior of the time-like baryon electromagnetic form factors, and it is found that the previously proposed periodic behaviour of the nucleon time-like electromagnetic form factor is not confirmed. However, we do observe the nonmonotonic structures in the line shape of the baryon effective form factors or the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ for the charmed baryon $Λ^+_c$. These features can be naturally explained by incorporating contributions from excited vector states. More precise measurements of the $e^+e^-\to B \bar{B}$ reaction offer a valuable opportunity to probe the properties of excited vector states, which are at present poorly known. Additionally, comprehensive theoretical and experimental studies of baryon timelike electromagnetic form factors can provide critical insights into the underlying mechanisms of electron-positron annihilation processes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nonmonotonic structures observed in baryon time-like electromagnetic form factors can be naturally explained within the vector meson dominance model by including contributions from excited vector states, but the previously proposed periodic behaviour of the nucleon time-like electromagnetic form factor is not confirmed.

## Method

The authors employed an extended vector meson dominance (VMD) model to parameterize the Dirac and Pauli form factors of baryons, incorporating contributions from ground and excited vector mesons. Model parameters were determined by fitting to experimental total cross section data for the electron-positron annihilation reactions \(e^+e^- \to B\bar{B}\) for nucleons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\), and \(\Lambda^+_c\). The effective form factor \(|G_{\rm eff}|\) and the ratio \(|G_E/G_M|\) were then calculated from the fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the \(e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) reaction, a narrow vector meson \(X(2232)\) with a mass of about 2231.8 MeV was required to reproduce the near-threshold enhancement.
- For the \(e^+e^- \to \Xi\bar{\Xi}\) reaction, two resonances were crucial: \(V_1\) with mass 2.742 GeV and width 71 MeV, and \(V_2\) with mass 2.993 GeV and width 88 MeV.
- For the \(e^+e^- \to \Lambda^+_c \bar{\Lambda}^-_c\) reaction, the model described data in the energy region 4.57 GeV \(< \sqrt{s} <\) 4.96 GeV, with contributions from \(\psi(4500)\), \(\psi(4660)\), \(\psi(4790)\), and \(\psi(4900)\).
- The ratios of total cross sections for \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Sigma^0\), and \(\Sigma^-\) were found to be \(9.7 \pm 1.3 : 1 : 3.3 \pm 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends previous VMD model studies of baryon electromagnetic form factors by Iachello (1972, 2004) and Bijker (2004) to include excited vector states for multiple baryon species. It addresses discrepancies between earlier theoretical predictions and recent experimental data from BESIII, BABAR, and Belle collaborations, particularly the large threshold enhancement in \(e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) that previous calculations underestimated. The results enable future studies of poorly known excited vector states through more precise measurements of \(e^+e^- \to B\bar{B}\) reactions, especially around the \(V_1\) peak near 2.74 GeV and the \(\psi(4900)\) region near 4.9 GeV.
