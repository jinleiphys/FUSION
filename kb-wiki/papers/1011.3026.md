# Lattice calculation for unitary fermions in a finite box

> A fundamental constant in systems of unitary fermions is the so-called Bertsch parameter, the ratio of the ground state energy for spin paired unitary fermions to that for free fermions at the same density. I discuss how we computed this parameter as well as the pairing gap using a recently developed lattice construction for unitary fermions, by measuring correlation functions for up to 38 fermions in a finite box. Our calculation illustrates interesting issues facing the study of many-body states on the lattice, which may eventually be confronted in QCD calculations as well.

## Key claim

The Bertsch parameter (the ratio of ground-state energy of unitary fermions to that of free fermions at the same density) is extracted from lattice calculations for up to 38 fermions in a finite box, but the quoted preliminary value of ξ = 0.412(4) is obtained only from a fit to the second momentum shell and does not include systematic errors from finite volume effects or discretization errors.

## Method

The authors use a lattice action for non-relativistic two-component fermions with a four-Fermi contact interaction, where the fermion determinant is quenched by employing open boundary conditions and stochastic Z₂ auxiliary fields. They tune the coupling constants by matching low-lying two-particle eigenvalues from the lattice transfer matrix to continuum finite-volume energies for p cot δ₀ = 0 using Lüscher's formula, and construct N-body correlation functions from Slater determinants of single-fermion propagators. Improved sinks are built from correlated two-particle states with a 1/r-like wavefunction to achieve better overlap with the unitary fermion ground state, and the Multi-Correlation Prony Method is used to extend plateaus in effective mass plots.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter ξ = 0.412(4) (preliminary), obtained from a fit to the second momentum shell for N ≤ 38 fermions in a V = 12³ lattice.
- Pairing gap Δ = 0.52(1) (preliminary), in units of the free Fermi energy, extracted from the second momentum shell for N ≤ 37 fermions.
- For N ≥ 14, the Bertsch parameter on a V = 8³ lattice is slightly greater than on V = 12³ and V = 16³ lattices, indicating that V = 8³ is too small for correct results at those particle numbers.

## Context

This work extends prior Quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Gezerlis 2010) which gave ξ = 0.40(1) and ultra-cold atomic experiments (Luo 2009) which reported ξ = 0.39(2) and ξ = 0.41(2) using two extraction methods. The lattice approach enables study of up to 38 fermions in a finite box, and the authors note that the same techniques for handling many-body states may eventually be applied to QCD calculations. The pairing gap result Δ = 0.52(1) is compared to a QMC calculation (quasi 2008) giving Δ = 0.50(3) and an experiment (Kettler 2008) giving Δ = 0.43(3).
