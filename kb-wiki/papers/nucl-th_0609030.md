# Slow proton production in semi-inclusive DIS off nuclei: the role of final state interaction

> The effects of the final state interaction on the production of slow protons in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic lepton scattering off nuclei is considered within the spectator mechanism and a realistic approach in which the rescattering in the medium of both the recoiling proton and the hadronizing nucleon debris are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper shows that final state interactions (FSI) significantly reduce the cross section for slow proton production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off nuclei, with the largest effect coming from rescattering of produced pre-hadrons on the spectator (A-2) system, but the authors present this as preliminary results from a realistic approach with a full account to be given elsewhere.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for the process A(e,e'p)(A-2) using a two-nucleon correlation spectral function that includes the center-of-mass motion of the correlated pair (extended 2NC model). They model FSI through two mechanisms: an eikonal distorted wave with a complex optical potential for the slow recoiling proton, and a time-dependent effective cross section for the interaction of the hadronizing nucleon debris with the spectator (A-2) system, where the effective cross section includes contributions from baryon-nucleon scattering and pre-hadron production via string decay and gluon radiation.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for 12C(e,e'p)10B is calculated for detected proton kinetic energy T2 at forward angle theta2 = 25 degrees and backward angle theta2 = 140 degrees, for various values of the Bjorken variable x.
- Backward emission (theta2 = 140 degrees) is noted to be experimentally accessible and sensitive to nuclear effects, with the 2NC model exhibiting kinematical restrictions not present in the extended 2NC model.
- At x > 1, nucleons from the spectator mechanism can only be emitted forward.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the spectator mechanism in semi-inclusive DIS that either completely disregarded FSI or treated them within simple models, such as the two-nucleon correlation (2NC) model of Frankfurt and Strikman and the extended 2NC model of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators. The approach enables quantitative comparisons with experimental data from the CLAS collaboration and may help determine whether reliable information on bound nucleon structure functions and hadronization mechanisms can be obtained from SIDIS off complex nuclei with slow nucleon production.
