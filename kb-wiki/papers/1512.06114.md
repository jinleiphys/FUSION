# Three-body systems in physics of cold atoms and halo nuclei

> Few-body systems, such as cold atoms and halo nuclei, share universal features at low energies, which are insensitive to the underlying inter-particle interactions at short ranges. These low-energy properties can be investigated in the framework of effective field theory with two-body and three-body contact interactions. I review the effective-field-theory studies of universal physics in three-body systems, focusing on the application in cold atoms and halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions provides a systematic framework for describing universal three-body physics in both cold atomic gases and halo nuclei, but emphasizes that the universality is conditional on a separation of scales where the scattering length $|a|$ is much larger than the range of interactions $\ell$, and that higher-order corrections (such as effective range effects) break the discrete scaling symmetry logarithmically.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of a short-range EFT (also called pionless EFT in nuclear physics or halo EFT for nuclear systems) where an effective Lagrangian is built from two-body and three-body contact interactions. The three-body problem is solved via the modified Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation for atom-dimer scattering, which is equivalent to a non-relativistic Faddeev equation. Higher-order corrections from finite effective range $r_0$ are included perturbatively in an expansion in $r_0/a$, with the three-body coupling renormalized by fitting to one (LO) or two (NLO) three-body observables.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling constant for three identical bosons is $\lambda = 22.694$, with $s_0 = \pi/\ln\lambda = 1.00624$.
- Universal relations for Efimov features: $a_{-}\kappa_* = -1.50763$, $a_{+}\kappa_* = 0.316473$, $a_{*}\kappa_* = 0.0707645$, where $\kappa_*$ is the trimer binding momentum in the unitary limit.
- The three-body parameter $\Lambda_*$ relates to $\kappa_*$ by $\Lambda_* = 0.548 \kappa_*$.
- For $^4$He atoms, the atom-atom scattering length is $\sim 100$ Å, about 20 times the range of the van der Waals potential.
- In the $^6$Li-$^{133}$Cs mixture, the measured geometric scaling factor between consecutive Efimov trimers is $4.9$ (Pires et al. found $5.8$ between two $a_{-}$ values), consistent with the zero-range theory prediction $\lambda=4.88$.
- For $^4$He trimers, NLO corrections to the excited-state binding energy are $\sim 1$-$2\%$, while N$^2$LO corrections are $\sim 1$-$3\permil$; for the ground state, NLO corrections are $\sim 10\%$ and N$^2$LO corrections are $\sim 20\%$.

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive reviews of Efimov physics in cold atoms by Braaten (2004) and in nuclear/particle physics by Hammer (2010), focusing specifically on the EFT formulation. It connects the universal physics of cold atomic gases (where Efimov trimers have been observed in $^{133}$Cs, $^7$Li, $^{39}$K, $^{85}$Rb, and $^6$Li) with halo nuclear systems (such as $^{11}$Li, $^{18,20}$C, $^{12,14}$Be) where the same EFT framework applies. The work enables systematic inclusion of finite-range corrections beyond the zero-range limit, and opens the question of how the van der Waals universality observed by Berninger (2011) connects to the EFT description.
