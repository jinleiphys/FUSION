# Asymmetric Bethe-Salpeter equation for pairing and condensation

> The Martin-Schwinger hierarchy of correlations are reexamined and the three-particle correlations are investigated under various partial summations. Besides the known approximations of screened, ladder and maximally crossed diagrams the pair-pair correlations are considered. It is shown that the recently proposed asymmetric Bethe-Salpeter equation to avoid unphysical repeated collisions is derived as a result of the hierarchical dependencies of correlations. Exceeding the parquet approximation we show that an asymmetry appears in the selfconsistent propagators. This form is superior over the symmetric selfconsistent one since it provides the Nambu-Gorkov equations and gap equation for fermions and the Beliaev equations for bosons while from the symmetric form no gap equation results. The selfenergy diagrams which account for the subtraction of unphysical repeated collisions are derived from the pair-pair correlation in the three-particle Greenfunction. It is suggested to distinguish between two types of selfconsistency, the channel-dressed propagators and the completely dressed propagators, with the help of which the asymmetric expansion completes the Ward identity and is $Φ$-derivable.

## Key claim

The paper derives the asymmetric Bethe-Salpeter equation from the Martin-Schwinger hierarchy of correlations, showing that the asymmetry, previously introduced as an ad-hoc assumption to avoid unphysical repeated collisions, emerges naturally from the hierarchical structure of correlation functions, and that this asymmetric form is necessary to obtain the gap equation for pairing and condensation while the symmetric selfconsistent form fails to produce a gap.

## Method

The author reexamines the Martin-Schwinger hierarchy using a generating functional approach with cumulant expansion of n-particle causal Green's functions, deriving integral equations for correlated Green's functions. The two-particle correlation is first treated in binary collision approximation (neglecting three-particle correlations), yielding the ladder approximation with asymmetric propagators (Hartree-Fock combined with full Green's function). Then three-particle correlations are investigated by selecting specific diagrammatic sets, screened ladder, maximally crossed, and ladder diagrams, and introducing channel-dressed propagators, followed by analysis of pair-pair correlations that provide the selfenergy contributions accounting for subtraction of unphysical repeated collisions.

## Key numbers

* The symmetric selfconsistent Dyson equation yields a propagator of the form \( G(\omega,{\bf k}) = 1/(\omega - \epsilon_{\bf k} \pm \Delta^2 G(-\omega,-{\bf k})) \) which shows no pole and no gap equation.
* The asymmetric Kadanoff-Martin approximation yields \( G(\omega,{\bf k}) = 1/(\omega - \epsilon_{\bf k} \mp \Delta^2/(\omega + \epsilon_{-{\bf k}})) \), possessing the typical two-pole structure of the BCS gap equation for fermions.
* The full propagator from the subtraction scheme is given by \( G(p) = (\omega + \epsilon + \bar\Sigma_{11}) / [(\omega + \epsilon + \bar\Sigma_{11})(\omega - \epsilon - \Sigma_{11}) \mp \Sigma_{12}^2] \), where the upper/lower sign stands for fermions/bosons, which is the Beliaev form for bosons or the Nambu-Gorkov form for fermions.

## Context

This work extends the foundational analysis of Kadanoff and Martin (1961), who first observed the need for asymmetric propagators to obtain the gap equation, and the more recent proposal by L. (2008) to avoid unphysical repeated collisions using a subtraction scheme. The paper provides a first-principles derivation showing that the hierarchical structure of correlation functions naturally produces the asymmetric selfconsistency, going beyond the parquet approximation. This enables a systematic justification for T-matrix approximations used across nuclear physics, condensed matter, and ultracold gases, and clarifies that the T-matrix schemes should be revised when singular channels appear (as in condensation or pairing) to incorporate the asymmetry in selfconsistency.
