# Neutrino Interactions in Hot and Dense Matter

> We study the charged and neutral current weak interaction rates relevant for the determination of neutrino opacities in dense matter found in supernovae and neutron stars. We establish an efficient formalism for calculating differential cross sections and mean free paths for interacting, asymmetric nuclear matter at arbitrary degeneracy. The formalism is valid for both charged and neutral current reactions. Strong interaction corrections are incorporated through the in-medium single particle energies at the relevant density and temperature. The effects of strong interactions on the weak interaction rates are investigated using both potential and effective field-theoretical models of matter. We investigate the relative importance of charged and neutral currents for different astrophysical situations, and also examine the influence of strangeness-bearing hyperons. Our findings show that the mean free paths are significantly altered by the effects of strong interactions and the multi-component nature of dense matter. The opacities are then discussed in the context of the evolution of the core of a protoneutron star.

# Neutrino Interactions in Hot and Dense Matter

## Key claim

The authors establish that neutrino mean free paths in dense matter are significantly altered by strong interactions and the multi-component nature of matter, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of potential or field-theoretical model used to incorporate in-medium single-particle energies, and on the assumption that the formalism applies to both charged and neutral current reactions for interacting, asymmetric nuclear matter at arbitrary degeneracy.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism for calculating differential cross sections and mean free paths for neutrino interactions in hot, dense, asymmetric nuclear matter using Fermi's golden rule with current-current weak interaction Lagrangians from Weinberg-Salam theory. Strong interaction corrections are incorporated through in-medium single-particle energies derived from both nonrelativistic Skyrme-like potential models and relativistic Walecka-type mean-field models, with the dynamic form factor (structure function) expressed analytically in terms of Polylogarithmic functions for noninteracting matter and generalized to interacting cases. The formalism is applied to compute opacities for thermodynamic conditions relevant to protoneutron star evolution, considering both neutrino-trapped (fixed lepton fraction Y_L=0.4) and neutrino-free (Y_ν=0) regimes, including the effects of strangeness-bearing hyperons.

- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Fermi weak coupling constant: G_F ≃ 1.436 × 10^{-49} erg cm^{-3}
- For nondegenerate matter, the total cross section per unit volume for charged current reactions is σ(E_1)/V = (G_F^2/π)(𝒱² + 3𝒜²) E_1² n_2, where n_2 is the neutron number density
- For highly degenerate baryons (μ_i/T ≫ 1), the cross section per unit volume is σ(E_1)/V = (G_F²/4π³)(𝒱² + 3𝒜²) M² T² Ξ (E_1 + μ̂) [π² + ((E_1 - μ_1)/kT)²] [1 + exp((μ_1 - E_1)/T)]^{-1}
- Previous nonrelativistic calculations by Sawyer and by Iwamoto & Pethick predicted increases in mean free paths by factors of ~2-3 for densities in the range of 2-4 times nuclear saturation density
- Relativistic calculations by Horowitz and Wehrberger found mean free paths factors of 1.5-2 times that for noninteracting nucleons
- The Cabibbo factor C = cos θ_c for ΔS=0 and C = sin θ_c for ΔS=1, with sin θ_c = 0.231
- SU(3) symmetry breaking corrections to couplings are about 10-30%

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Sawyer (nondegenerate matter), Iwamoto & Pethick (degenerate neutron matter), and Horowitz & Wehrberger (relativistic effective Lagrangian models) by providing a unified formalism valid for arbitrary degeneracy and including both potential and field-theoretical models of strong interactions. The authors generalize Schinder's exact results for noninteracting nucleons (expressed as lengthy series of Fermi integrals) into simpler Polylogarithmic functions that can be extended to interacting matter, and incorporate the multi-component nature of dense matter including hyperons as emphasized by Prakash et al. and Reddy & Prakash. The results enable more accurate neutrino transport calculations for simulations of gravitational collapse, supernovae, protoneutron star evolution, and binary neutron star mergers, where previous simulations employed limiting expressions with simple scaling factors that may be inconsistent with the underlying nuclear matter equation of state.
