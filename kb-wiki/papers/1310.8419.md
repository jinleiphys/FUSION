# Valence double parton distributions of the nucleon in a simple model

> Valence double parton distribution functions of the nucleon are evaluated in the framework of a simple model, where the conservation of the longitudinal momentum is taken into account. The leading-order DGLAP QCD evolution from the low quark-model scale to higher renormalization scales is carried out via the Mellin moments of the distributions. Results of the valence quark correlation function show that in general the double distributions cannot be approximated as a product of the single-particle distributions.

## Key claim

Valence double parton distribution functions of the nucleon cannot be approximated as a product of single-parton distributions, even in a simple model where correlations arise solely from longitudinal momentum conservation; this non-factorization persists and becomes more pronounced after leading-order DGLAP evolution to experimentally accessible scales.

## Method

The authors construct a three-quark probability distribution as a product of single-quark wave-function squares multiplied by a delta function enforcing longitudinal momentum conservation, then integrate over one quark to obtain the valence double parton distribution. They perform leading-order DGLAP evolution via Mellin moments from a low quark-model scale (where valence quarks carry all momentum) to higher scales, solving the evolution equations analytically in moment space and inverting via contour integration.

## Key numbers

- The quark-model scale is determined to be μ₀ = 285 MeV, where valence quarks carry 100% of the nucleon momentum; at μ = 2 GeV the valence quarks carry 41.6% of the momentum (matching the NNPDF2.3 fit).
- The valence single-parton distribution for model II with a=2 is D₁(x) = (168/145)(1-x)³(1+6x+16x²+6x³+x⁴).
- The double parton distribution for model II with a=2 is D₂(x₁,x₂) = (1008/29)(1-x₁)²(1-x₂)²(x₁+x₂)².
- Evolution is carried out to μ = 2 GeV and μ = 1 TeV, showing the correlation function ρ(x₁,x₂) becomes large and positive along edges where x₁ or x₂ is small, and negative in the region where x₁ and x₂ are similar and relatively large.

## Context

This work extends the limited set of nonperturbative calculations of valence double parton distributions, previously carried out only in the MIT bag model (Chang, 2012) and the constituent quark model (Rinaldi, 2013), both of which produced distributions with unphysical support outside the region 0 ≤ x₁, x₂, x₁+x₂ ≤ 1. The simple model presented here satisfies exact momentum conservation and Lorentz invariance, yielding correct support and sum rules (Gaunt and Stirling, 2009). The results enable future studies of double parton scattering at the LHC and demonstrate that approximating double parton distributions as products of single-parton distributions is unjustified, particularly after QCD evolution.
