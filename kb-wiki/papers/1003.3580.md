# Constraints on the symmetry energy and on neutron skins from the pygmy resonances in 68Ni and 132Sn

> Correlations between the behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, the neutron skins, and the percentage of energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in 68Ni and 132Sn have been investigated by using different Random Phase Approximation (RPA) models for the dipole response, based on a representative set of Skyrme effective forces plus meson-exchange effective Lagrangians. A comparison with the experimental data has allowed us to constrain the value of the derivative of the symmetry energy at saturation. The neutron skin radius is deduced under this constraint.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy at saturation is constrained to \(64.8 \pm 15.7\) MeV by combining pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) data in \({}^{68}\)Ni and \({}^{132}\)Sn across multiple nonrelativistic and relativistic mean-field models, resolving a previous tension where the PDR-only result from \({}^{132}\)Sn did not overlap significantly with heavy-ion collision analyses.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) calculations of the dipole strength for \({}^{68}\)Ni and \({}^{132}\)Sn using 19 Skyrme effective forces (nonrelativistic) and 7 meson-exchange effective Lagrangians (relativistic). They correlated the percentage of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the PDR with the slope parameter \(L\) for each model, then used experimental EWSR percentages to extract a constrained range for \(L\) and, subsequently, the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R\).

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter \(L\) constrained to \(64.8 \pm 15.7\) MeV (weighted average from both nuclei).
- Symmetry energy at saturation \(J = 32.3 \pm 1.3\) MeV.
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R = 0.200 \pm 0.015\) fm for \({}^{68}\)Ni.
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R = 0.258 \pm 0.024\) fm for \({}^{132}\)Sn.
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R = 0.194 \pm 0.024\) fm for \({}^{208}\)Pb.
- Experimental EWSR percentage for the PDR in \({}^{68}\)Ni is \(5\% \pm 1.5\%\).
- Experimental PDR peak energy is 11 MeV for \({}^{68}\)Ni and 9.8 MeV for \({}^{132}\)Sn.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Klimkiewicz et al. (2007) on the PDR in \({}^{132}\)Sn by adding the \({}^{68}\)Ni PDR datum from Wieland et al. (2009) and employing a broader set of theoretical models (both Skyrme and relativistic). The resulting \(L\) value overlaps with results from heavy-ion collision analyses by Chen et al. (2005) and Tsang et al. (2009), as well as with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana et al. (2009), resolving a previous discrepancy. The neutron skin thickness for \({}^{68}\)Ni is determined for the first time, and the results enable more precise predictions for neutron star structure and the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
