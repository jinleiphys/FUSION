# One plus two-body random matrix ensembles with parity: Density of states and parity ratios

> One plus two-body embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrices with parity [EGOE(1+2)-$π$] generated by a random two-body interaction (modeled by GOE in two particle spaces) in the presence of a mean-field, for spinless identical fermion systems, is defined, generalizing the two-body ensemble with parity analyzed by Papenbrock and Weidenmüller [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 78}, 054305 (2008)], in terms of two mixing parameters and a gap between the positive $(π=+)$ and negative $(π=-)$ parity single particle (sp) states. Numerical calculations are used to demonstrate, using realistic values of the mixing parameters appropriate for some nuclei, that the EGOE(1+2)-$π$ ensemble generates Gaussian form (with corrections) for fixed parity eigenvalue densities (i.e. state densities). The random matrix model also generates many features in parity ratios of state densities that are similar to those predicted by a method based on the Fermi-gas model for nuclei. We have also obtained, by applying the formulation due to Chang et al [Ann. Phys. (N.Y.) {\bf 66}, 137 (1971)], a simple formula for the spectral variances defined over fixed-$(m_1,m_2)$ spaces, where $m_1$ is the number of fermions in the +ve parity sp states and $m_2$ is the number of fermions in the -ve parity sp states. Similarly, using the binary correlation approximation, in the dilute limit, we have derived expressions for the lowest two shape parameters. The smoothed densities generated by the sum of fixed-$(m_1,m_2)$ Gaussians with lowest two shape corrections describe the numerical results in many situations. The model also generates preponderance of +ve parity ground states for small values of the mixing parameters and this is a feature seen in nuclear shell model results.

## Key claim

The EGOE(1+2)-$\pi$ ensemble, which generalizes the parity-conserving two-body ensemble of Papenbrock and Weidenmüller (2008) by adding a mean-field one-body term and two mixing parameters, generates fixed-parity eigenvalue densities that are approximately Gaussian (with corrections) and reproduces features of parity ratios seen in Fermi-gas model calculations, but only for specific ranges of the mixing parameters and the gap between positive and negative parity single-particle states.

## Method

The authors define an embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble with one-plus-two-body interactions and parity [EGOE(1+2)-$\pi$] for spinless identical fermions, where the two-body interaction is modeled by independent GOE matrices in the three two-particle parity blocks (both positive, both negative, and mixed) with variances $\tau^2$ for diagonal blocks and $\alpha^2$ for the mixing block, and the one-body part consists of degenerate positive parity states and degenerate negative parity states separated by an energy gap $\Delta$ (set to 1). They numerically construct 100-200 ensemble members for various $(N_+, N_-, m)$ values and compute fixed-parity state densities, parity ratios, and ground-state parity probabilities, while also deriving analytic propagation formulas for centroids, variances, skewness, and excess of fixed-$(m_1,m_2)$ partial densities using group theory and the binary correlation approximation in the dilute limit.

## Key numbers

- For realistic nuclear interactions in $sdfp$ and $fpg_{9/2}$ spaces with $\Delta = 3$ MeV and $5$ MeV, the deduced parameter ranges are $\tau \sim 0.09-0.24$ and $\alpha \sim (0.9-1.3) \times \tau$.
- The ratio $N_+/N_-$ for these nuclear spaces is approximately $0.5-2.0$.
- For $(N_+,N_-,m) = (8,8,6)$ with $(\tau,\alpha/\tau) = (0.1,0.5)$, the ensemble-averaged total spectral width $\sigma_t$ is $2.29$ (exact) vs $2.32$ (asymptotic formula); for $(0.2,1.5)$ it is $5.08$ vs $5.13$.
- For $(N_+,N_-,m) = (10,10,6)$ with $(\tau,\alpha/\tau) = (0.1,0.5)$, $\sigma_t$ is $2.95$ (exact) vs $2.99$ (asymptotic); for $(0.2,1.5)$ it is $6.83$ vs $6.91$.
- In the dilute limit with $\alpha \ll \tau$ or $\tau \ll \alpha$, the excess parameter $\gamma_2 \sim -4/m$ for $m_1 = m_2 = m/2$ and $N_1 = N_2 = N$.

## Context

This work extends the parity-only two-body random matrix ensemble of Papenbrock and Weidenmüller (2008) by including a mean-field one-body term and two independent mixing parameters, making it more realistic for nuclear applications. It provides the first random matrix theory for parity ratios of nuclear level densities, complementing the Fermi-gas model approach of Mocelj et al. (2007) and addressing the preponderance of positive parity ground states seen in nuclear shell model calculations. The analytic formulas for variances and shape parameters enable construction of smoothed fixed-parity densities without full diagonalization, and the ensemble can be extended in future work to include angular momentum and proton-neutron degrees of freedom.
