# Asymmetry of the neutrino mean free path in partially spin polarized neutron matter within the Skyrme model

> The asymmetry in the neutrino mean free path for the absorption reaction $ν+ n \rightarrow e^{-} + p$, is evaluated within hot neutron matter under a strong magnetic field. We consider densities in the range $0.05 \leq ρ\leq 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$, several temperatures up to 30 MeV and magnetic field strengths from $B=0$ up to $B=10^{18}$~G. Polarized neutron matter is described within the non--relativistic Hartree--Fock model using the LNS Skyrme interaction. The neutrino mean free path has a weak dependence with the temperature and in the strong magnetic field region, it decreases for growing values of it. This contrast with the scattering reaction $ν+ n \rightarrow ν' + n'$, where the average mean free path is almost independent of the magnetic field and has a strong dependence with the temperature. We have evaluated the asymmetry from both the absorption and scattering reactions. Our results shows that the total asymmetry depends on the magnetic field intensity, the density and the temperature. For a density of $0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ and for a magnetic field strength of B=$10^{17}$~G, the asymmetry in the mean free path is found to be, $\sim 9\%$ and $\sim 3.4\%$ for temperatures of T$=15$ and $30$ MeV, respectively. While the same set of asymmetries for B=$10^{18}$~G, is $\sim 58\%$ and $\sim 48\%$.

## Key claim

The asymmetry in the neutrino mean free path for the absorption reaction in hot neutron matter under a strong magnetic field depends on the magnetic field intensity, density, and temperature, with the absorption mean free path showing a weak temperature dependence and decreasing with increasing magnetic field strength, in contrast to the scattering reaction where the mean free path is almost independent of the magnetic field but strongly temperature-dependent.

## Method

The authors evaluated the neutrino mean free path for the absorption reaction ν + n → e⁻ + p in hot neutron matter using a non-relativistic Hartree-Fock model with the LNS Skyrme interaction. The equation of state was solved self-consistently to obtain the spin polarization, single-particle energies, and chemical potential for given density, temperature, and magnetic field strength. The absorption cross section per unit volume was calculated using the Fermi Golden Rule with the weak interaction Hamiltonian, incorporating Landau level quantization for the charged proton and electron final states and a mixed spin wave function for the neutron.

## Key numbers

- Density range considered: 0.05 ≤ ρ ≤ 0.4 fm⁻³
- Temperature range: up to 30 MeV
- Magnetic field range: from B = 0 up to B = 10¹⁸ G
- For density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and B = 10¹⁷ G: asymmetry in mean free path is ~9% at T = 15 MeV and ~3.4% at T = 30 MeV
- For density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and B = 10¹⁸ G: asymmetry in mean free path is ~58% at T = 15 MeV and ~48% at T = 30 MeV
- The spin asymmetry A follows log₁₀(|A|) ≈ a log₁₀(B) + b, where a ≈ 1 and b is approximately constant for fixed density, within the range B = 10¹⁴ G to 2.5 × 10¹⁸ G

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the scattering reaction ν + n → ν' + n' (Torres Patiño and Bauer, 2019) to the absorption reaction, using the same Skyrme model approach. The asymmetry in neutrino emission from both reactions is relevant to the "pulsar kick problem," where an asymmetry of ~1% could explain the observed high velocities of pulsars (Lai and Qian, 1998). The results enable more complete modeling of asymmetric neutrino emission from neutron stars, combining both the differential cross section asymmetry (from scattering) and the mean free path asymmetry (from both absorption and scattering), which are independent mechanisms that should be considered simultaneously.
