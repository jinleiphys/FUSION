# Net-proton number cumulant ratios as function of beam energy from an expanding nonequilibrium chiral fluid

> The beam energy scan program at RHIC provides data on net-proton number fluctuations with the goal to detect the QCD critical end point and first-order phase transition. Interpreting these experimental signals requires a vital understanding of the interplay of critical phenomena and the nonequilibrium dynamics of the rapidly expanding fireball. We study these aspects with a fluid dynamic expansion coupled to the explicit propagation of the chiral order parameter sigma via a Langevin equation. Assuming a sigma-proton coupling through an effective proton mass, we relate cumulants of the order parameter and the net-proton number at freeze-out and obtain observable cumulant ratios as a function of beam energy. We emphasize the role of the nonequilibrium first-order phase transition where a mixed phase with gradual freeze-out can significantly alter the cumulants. We find that the presence of a critical end point is clearly visible in the cumulant ratios for a relatively wide range of center-of-mass energies.

## Key claim

The presence of a QCD critical end point (CEP) is clearly visible in net-proton number cumulant ratios for a relatively wide range of center-of-mass energies, but only if the CEP exists in the low-energy range around √s ≈ 5 GeV; the authors emphasize that their model's CEP location is not a prediction for the physical CEP but a tool to estimate its impact on observables in a fully dynamical nonequilibrium setup.

## Method

The authors couple a longitudinal Bjorken expansion of an ideal quark fluid to a Langevin equation for the volume-averaged chiral order parameter σ (the sigma field) from the quark-meson model, with a CEP at (T, μ) = (100, 200) MeV. They evolve 10⁷ events from initial conditions matched to beam energies via a parametrized freeze-out curve, extract event-by-event cumulants of σ_V at freeze-out, and relate them to net-proton number cumulants assuming a sigma-proton coupling through an effective proton mass plus a Poisson baseline, with volume fluctuation corrections applied.

## Key numbers

- CEP location in the quark-meson model: (T_CEP, μ_CEP) = (100, 200) MeV.
- Freeze-out curve parametrization: T_f.o.(μ_B) = 0.166 GeV − 0.139 GeV⁻¹ μ_B² − 0.053 GeV⁻³ μ_B⁴.
- Beam energy mapping: μ_B(√s) = 1.308 GeV / (1 + 0.273 GeV⁻¹ √s).
- Crossover temperature at μ=0: T_crossover(μ=0) = 145 MeV.
- Initial thermalization time: τ₀ = 1 fm.
- Gold nucleus radius: R = 7.3 fm.
- Number of events simulated: 10⁷.
- The ratio C₂/C₁ reaches a maximum around √s ≈ 5 GeV for evolutions passing through or close to the CEP.
- At the lowest beam energy (√s = 2.4 GeV), the second-hit C₄/C₂ ratio lies in the range ~1–10, encompassing the HADES data point.

## Context

This work extends the nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics model of Nahrgang (2011) to a longitudinal Bjorken expansion, building on earlier studies of critical dynamics by Nahrgang (2011), Herold (2013, 2016, 2017, 2018), Stephanov (2011, 2017), and others. It directly addresses the interpretation of net-proton fluctuation data from the STAR beam energy scan (STAR, 2021) and HADES (2020) by providing a dynamical, nonequilibrium calculation of cumulant ratios that includes both a CEP and a first-order phase transition. The results enable future comparisons with data from FAIR and NICA, which will explore the high-μ_B region, and suggest that the current gap in beam energies between 2.4 and 7.7 GeV must be filled experimentally.
