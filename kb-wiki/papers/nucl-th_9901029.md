# About QCD sum rules for pion-nucleon coupling

> We point out subtleties in the previous sum rule calculations of the $πNN$ coupling associated with using either the PV or the PS coupling schemes in modeling the phenomenological side. We propose a sum rule which is independent of the coupling schemes used and has less uncertainty in the OPE. The obtained value is $g_{πN}=9.76 \pm 2.04$ where the uncertainty mainly comes from the quark-gluon mixed condensate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that previous QCD sum rule calculations of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N}\) suffered from scheme dependence because they used either the pseudoscalar (PS) or pseudovector (PV) coupling Lagrangian to model the phenomenological side, and proposes a new sum rule for the \(\gamma_5 \sigma^{\mu\nu} p_\mu q_\nu\) Dirac structure that is independent of the coupling scheme and yields \(g_{\pi N} = 9.76 \pm 2.04\), with the uncertainty dominated by the quark-gluon mixed condensate parameter \(m_0^2\).

## Method

The author constructs a two-point correlation function for the proton and neutron interpolating fields with an external pion, Eq. (2), and evaluates the operator product expansion (OPE) beyond the soft-pion limit, keeping only the diquark component of the pion wave function and using vacuum saturation. The phenomenological side is built for the \(\gamma_5 \sigma^{\mu\nu} p_\mu q_\nu\) structure, which contains only a double pole from the nucleon and a simple pole from \(N \rightarrow N^*\) transitions, making it independent of the PS or PV coupling scheme. The unknown nucleon coupling \(\lambda_N\) is eliminated using the nucleon chiral odd sum rule, and the final sum rule, Eq. (15), is fitted with a straight line in the Borel window to extract \(g_{\pi N}\).

## Key numbers

- \(g_{\pi N} = 9.76 \pm 2.04\), where the uncertainty mainly comes from the quark-gluon mixed condensate parameter \(m_0^2\).
- The range for \(m_0^2\) is taken as \(0.6 \le m_0^2 \le 1.4\) GeV\(^2\), following Ovc (year not given in text).
- The empirical value of \(g_{\pi N}\) is noted as around 13.4.
- In the PV scheme, the additional term in the sum rule is \(M^2 / (2 m^2)\), which is about 0.5 for \(M \sim m \sim 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to the pion-nucleon coupling by addressing the scheme dependence identified in earlier calculations by Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (year not given) and Shiomi and Hatsuda (year not given), who used the PS coupling and the soft-pion limit. It also builds on the method of Birse and Krippa (year not given), who went beyond the soft-pion limit but still used the PS scheme, by proposing a new Dirac structure that avoids the coupling-scheme ambiguity. The result enables a more reliable extraction of \(g_{\pi N}\) from QCD sum rules, with reduced theoretical uncertainty, and can be refined by improved determinations of the quark-gluon mixed condensate.
