# Electron and positron pair production of compact stars

> Neutral stellar core at or over nuclear densities is described by a positive charged baryon core and negative charged electron fluid since they possess different masses and interactions. Based on a simplified model of a gravitationally collapsing or pulsating baryon core, we approximately integrate the Einstein-Maxwell equations and the equations for the number and energy-momentum conservation of complete degenerate electron fluid. We show possible electric processes that lead to the production of electron-positron pairs in the boundary of a baryon core and calculate the number and energy of electron-positron pairs. This can be relevant for understanding the energetic sources of supernovae and gamma-ray bursts.

## Key claim

The authors show that in a gravitationally collapsing or pulsating neutral stellar core at nuclear densities, the different dynamics of baryons and electrons produce an oscillating overcritical electric field in a thin surface layer, which can convert gravitational energy into electron-positron pairs via the Sauter-Heisenberg-Euler-Schwinger mechanism, provided the pair-production rate exceeds the oscillation frequency.

## Method

The authors model the baryon core as a rigid sphere with a sharp boundary (width of order the pion Compton length) at nuclear density, undergoing either gravitational collapse or pulsation with an inward boundary velocity. They numerically integrate the coupled Maxwell equations, electron number conservation, and energy-momentum conservation for a completely degenerate electron fluid in the Schwarzschild metric, using the static equilibrium configuration as the initial condition. The dynamical evolution of the electron fluid and electric field is computed in a thin layer of a few electron Compton lengths around the baryon core boundary, and the pair-production rate is estimated using the Sauter-field formula for the oscillating electric field component.

## Key numbers

* The baryon core density is at or above nuclear density \(n_{\rm nucl} \approx 1.4 \times 10^{38} \, {\rm cm}^{-3}\).
* For a core of mass \(M = 10 M_\odot\), radius \(R_c \sim 10^7 \, {\rm cm}\), and boundary velocity \(v_p = 0.2c\), the oscillation frequency of the electric field is \(\omega = \tau^{-1}_{\rm osci} \sim 1.5 m_e\) (in natural units, \(m_e\) is the electron mass).
* The relaxation rate of the oscillating electric field is \(\tau^{-1}_{\rm relax} \sim 0.05 m_e\).
* For the same parameters, the oscillating electric field energy density is \(\epsilon_{\rm osci} \approx 4.3 \times 10^{28} \, {\rm ergs/cm}^3\), the pair number density is \(n_{\rm pair} \approx 1.1 \times 10^{33} \, {\rm cm}^{-3}\), and the mean pair energy is \(\bar \epsilon_{\rm pair} \approx 24.5 \, {\rm MeV}\).
* The pair-production rate is estimated as \(\tau_{\rm pair}^{-1} \sim \alpha m_e (\ell/\lambda_e) (E/E_c) \simeq 6.6 m_e\), which exceeds the oscillation frequency \(\tau^{-1}_{\rm osci}\).

## Context

This work extends the static equilibrium analysis of charge separation and overcritical electric fields on the surface of compact stars at nuclear density, previously studied by Usov (1998) and by Obukhov (1975). It introduces a dynamical model for how gravitational collapse or pulsation of the baryon core drives oscillations of the electron fluid and electric field, enabling electron-positron pair production. The results provide a possible mechanism for converting gravitational energy into observable radiation, relevant for understanding the energetic sources of supernovae and gamma-ray bursts, and motivate future full numerical integration of the coupled Einstein-Maxwell-fluid equations at macroscopic scales.
