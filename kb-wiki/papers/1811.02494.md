# QCD phase diagram for finite imaginary chemical potential with HISQ fermions

> We present results from an ongoing study of the phase diagram of (2+1)-flavor QCD using the HISQ action with smaller than physical light quark masses in the Roberge-Weiss (RW) plane on lattices with temporal extent $N_τ=4$. We find that the endpoint of the $1^{st}$ order RW transition line remains $2^{nd}$ order at least down to light quark masses corresponding to Goldstone pion masses of $55$ MeV. Furthermore, we show that the chiral condensate is sensitive to the RW transition and may serve as a energy-like operator characterizing universal behavior close to the RW endpoint.

## Key claim

Using the HISQ action on coarse lattices with temporal extent Nτ=4, the endpoint of the first-order Roberge-Weiss transition line remains second order at least down to light quark masses corresponding to Goldstone pion masses of 55 MeV, contradicting results from unimproved staggered fermions that found a first-order triple point at much larger pion masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD using the HISQ action with a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action at imaginary chemical potential μ/T=π/3 (the Roberge-Weiss plane) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=4 and spatial extents Nσ=8, 12, 16, and 24. They varied the light quark mass ratio ml/ms from the physical value 1/27 down to 1/160, corresponding to Goldstone pion masses from 135 MeV down to 55 MeV, while keeping the strange quark mass fixed at its physical value, and performed finite-size scaling analyses of the order parameter M=⟨|Im L|⟩ and its susceptibility χh using 3-d Z(2) universal scaling functions.

## Key numbers

- The smallest light quark mass used corresponds to a Goldstone pion mass of 55 MeV (ml/ms=1/160 on Nσ=24 lattices)
- Physical light quark mass corresponds to ml/ms=1/27 and a pion mass of 135 MeV
- Intermediate light quark masses studied: ml/ms=1/40 (mπ≈110 MeV) and ml/ms=1/60 (mπ≈90 MeV)
- Simulations used 20,000 trajectories per temperature away from Tc and 80,000 trajectories near Tc
- Temperature range studied: T∼Tc±0.1Tc
- The peak of the susceptibility at Tc rises significantly slower than the Nσ3 divergence expected for a first-order transition, consistent with Z(2) scaling

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD phase diagram in the Roberge-Weiss plane using improved fermion actions, following calculations with the stout action by Bonati et al. (2016, 2018) that found no first-order triple point for pion masses above 50 MeV. It contradicts earlier results with unimproved staggered fermions by de Forcrand et al. (2010), Philipsen et al. (2016), and Cuteri et al. (2015), which found a first-order triple point for pion masses below approximately 400 MeV on Nτ=4 lattices. The results enable further exploration of the chiral limit in (2+1)-flavor QCD and suggest that the chiral condensate can serve as an energy-like operator for characterizing universal Z(2) behavior near the Roberge-Weiss endpoint.
