# Dynamic fermion flavor mixing through transition dipole moments

> We show that Majorana neutrino flavor mixing can be driven by transition dipole moments in the presence of external electromagnetic fields. We demonstrate the sensitivity of the rotation mixing matrix to strong fields obtaining dynamical mass eigenstates in the two-flavor model. The three-flavor case and extensions to the quark sector are introduced.

## Key claim

The authors show that Majorana neutrino flavor mixing can be driven by transition dipole moments in external electromagnetic fields, but the effect only becomes significant when the free-particle masses are nearly degenerate or the fields are extremely strong (e.g., magnetar-strength fields of order 10^11 Tesla), because the transition dipole coupling is bounded to be very small.

## Method

The authors introduce an anomalous transition magnetic dipole moment into the Majorana neutrino Lagrangian via a Pauli term, combining it with the standard Majorana mass term into an effective mass-dipole matrix. They diagonalize this matrix using a biunitary transformation, separating the rotation into a standard free-particle mixing matrix and an additional field-dependent rotation, and solve explicitly for the two-flavor case to obtain field-dependent mass eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The effective neutrino magnetic moment is bounded between approximately 10^-20 μ_B (lower bound from Standard Model with m_ν ~ 0.1 eV) and 10^-10 μ_B (upper bound from experimental observations), where μ_B = eℏ/(2m_e) is the Bohr magneton.
- The sum of neutrino masses from cosmological studies is Σ m_i < 0.12 eV; the electron (anti)neutrino mass is bounded by m_e^ν < 0.8 eV (KATRIN).
- To generate an electromagnetic mass splitting of 10^-3 eV using the upper bound μ_ν^eff ~ 10^-10 μ_B requires a magnetic field of approximately 1.7 × 10^11 T (magnetar-scale fields).
- In the strong-field degenerate-mass limit (C_s >> |Δm|^2), the effective mass splitting between the two EM-mass eigenstates is given by C_s, which depends on the EM field energy density and momentum density.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutrino electromagnetic properties (Schechter 1981, Giunti 2014, Popov 2019) and neutrino oscillation in magnetic fields (Lim 1987, Akhmedov 1988, Pal 1991, Elizalde 2004, Akhmedov 2022) by incorporating the full electromagnetic field tensor (both electric and magnetic fields) in a covariant formalism. The effect is distinct from the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein matter effect (Wolfenstein 1977, Mikheyev 1985, Smirnov 2003). The results enable exploration of neutrino flavor mixing in extreme astrophysical environments such as magnetars and the Early Universe, and suggest extensions to quark sector mixing via transition dipole moments.
