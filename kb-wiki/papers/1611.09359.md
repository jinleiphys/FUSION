# The branching ratio $ω\to π^+π^-$ revisited

> We analyze the most recent data for the pion vector form factor in the timelike region, employing a model-independent approach based on dispersion theory. We confirm earlier observations about the inconsistency of different modern high-precision data sets. Excluding the BaBar data, we find an updated value for the isospin-violating branching ratio $\mathcal{B}(ω\to π^+π^-) = (1.46\pm 0.08) \times 10^{-2}$. As a side result, we also extract an improved value for the pion vector or charge radius, $\sqrt{\langle r_V^2\rangle} = 0.6603(5)(4)\text{fm}$, where the first uncertainty is statistical as derived from the fit, while the second estimates the possible size of nonuniversal radiative corrections. In addition, we demonstrate that modern high-quality data for the decay $η' \to π^+π^-γ$ will allow for an even improved determination of the transition strength $ω\toπ^+π^-$.

## Key claim
The authors find that the BaBar data for the pion vector form factor are inconsistent with other modern high-precision data sets; excluding BaBar, they extract an updated branching ratio for the isospin-violating decay ω → π⁺π⁻ using a dispersive analysis that relies on the universality of pion-pion final-state interactions.

## Method
The authors employ a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor and the η′ → π⁺π⁻γ decay amplitude, using the Omnès function constructed from pion-pion P-wave phase shifts (Madrid and Bern analyses). They fit the parameters of polynomial functions that multiply the Omnès function, including a Breit-Wigner term for the ω-meson, to data from e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ experiments (SND, CMD-2, BaBar, KLOE10, KLOE12, BESIII) up to s = 1 GeV², and also analyze pseudo-data for η′ → π⁺π⁻γ generated according to preliminary BESIII results.

## Key numbers
- Branching ratio B(ω → π⁺π⁻) = (1.46 ± 0.08) × 10⁻², obtained from the combined fit excluding BaBar data
- Pion vector (charge) radius √⟨r_V²⟩ = 0.6603(5)(4) fm, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second estimates nonuniversal radiative corrections
- ω mass fixed to 782.65 MeV and total width to 8.49 MeV from the Particle Data Group
- ω → e⁺e⁻ decay width Γ(ω → e⁺e⁻) = (0.60 ± 0.02) keV, used to extract g_ωγ = (5.9 ± 0.1) × 10⁻²
- η′ → ωγ decay width Γ(η′ → ωγ) = (5.17 ± 0.35) keV, used to extract g_η′ωγ = -(0.127 ± 0.004) GeV⁻¹
- The Omnès function at the ω mass squared gives |Ω(m_ω²)|² ≈ 30, enhancing the ω → π⁺π⁻ transition rate

## Context
This work extends the dispersive formalism developed by Stollenwerk et al. (2011) and Hanhart et al. (2013) for the pion vector form factor and η → π⁺π⁻γ decay, and by Kubis et al. (2015) for η′ → π⁺π⁻γ. It confirms earlier observations about inconsistencies between modern e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data sets, particularly that BaBar data yield a significantly larger ω → π⁺π⁻ branching ratio than other experiments. The improved determination of the pion vector radius and the ω → π⁺π⁻ branching ratio provides crucial input for the Standard Model prediction of the hadronic contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and the authors demonstrate that upcoming high-quality η′ → π⁺π⁻γ data from BESIII will enable an independent, even more precise extraction of the ω → π⁺π⁻ transition strength.
