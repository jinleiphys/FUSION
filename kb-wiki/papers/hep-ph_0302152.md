# rho propagation and dilepton production at finite pion density and temperature

> We study the propagation properties of the rho vector in a dense and hot pion medium. We introduce a finite value of the chemical potential associated to a conserved pion number and argue that such description is valid during the hadronic phase of a relativistic heavy-ion collision, between chemical and thermal freeze-out, where the strong interaction drives pion number to a fixed value. By invoking vector dominance and rho saturation, we also study the finite pion density effects into the low mass dilepton production rate. We find that the distribution moderately widens and the position of the peak shifts toward larger values of the pair invariant mass, at the same time that the height of the peak decreases when the value of the chemical potential grows. We conclude by arguing that for the description of the dilepton spectra at ultra-relativistic energies, such as those of RHIC and LHC, the proper treatment of the large pion density might be a more important effect to consider than the influence of a finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The authors argue that at ultra-relativistic energies (RHIC and LHC), the large pion density in the hadronic phase between chemical and thermal freeze-out may be a more important effect for dilepton spectra than finite baryon density, but the observed modifications (widening, peak shift, height decrease) are described as "moderate" rather than dramatic.

## Method

The authors gauge an effective Lagrangian derived from the linear sigma model to introduce the rho meson, then compute the rho self-energy at one-loop order in the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory. A finite pion chemical potential is introduced to describe conserved pion number between chemical and thermal freeze-out, and the pion self-energy is resummed via the superdaisy (tadpole) approximation to obtain temperature- and density-dependent pion masses. The dilepton production rate is then computed under the assumptions of vector dominance and rho saturation.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Effective quartic coupling: alpha = 6(m_pi^2 / 2f_pi^2)
- Rho-pion coupling: g^2/4pi = 2.93 (determined from the rho width in vacuum)
- Chemical potential values studied: mu = 0 to 130 MeV
- Temperature values studied: T = 50 to 150 MeV
- For k=0, the rho half-width at finite mu is given by: Im F(k_0,k=0) = -(g^2/48pi) k_0^2 (1 - 4 m_tilde_pi^2/k_0^2)^(3/2) [1 + 2n(k_0/2 + mu)]
- The effective coupling in the resummation: alpha/(4pi^2) is of order 0.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the linear sigma model to describe pion propagation in a pion medium (Ayala 2002, Ayala2 2002) by adding the rho meson via gauging. It contrasts with the "dropping rho mass" scenario (Brown 1991) and the "melting of resonances" scenario (Dominguez 1992, Asakawa 1992), which rely on finite baryon density effects. The paper enables future work incorporating these results into dynamical models of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and possibly including other vector or axial vector mesons.
