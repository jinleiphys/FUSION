# Antideuteron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

> The experimental data of the antideuteron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions are analyzed within a simple model based on the diagrammatic approach to the coalescence model. This model is shown to be able to reproduce most of existing data without any additional parameter.

## Key claim

The diagrammatic approach to the coalescence model, originally developed for deuteron production, can successfully describe antideuteron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions without any free parameter, provided that threshold effects and the strong anisotropy of antiproton angular distributions are taken into account.

## Method

The authors generalized the diagrammatic coalescence model of Kolybasov by introducing a threshold correction factor based on the four-nucleon phase space and by incorporating anisotropic angular distributions of antiproton production. They then applied this model to existing experimental data on antideuteron production, using measured or parameterized antiproton inclusive cross-sections and the Paris potential deuteron wave function as inputs, with the total inelastic cross-section taken from PDG data or standard parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- For ISR data at √s = 53 GeV, the exponential parameterization of antiproton production gave A = 0.195 mbarn/(GeV/c)², α = 0.310, B = 2.49 (GeV/c)⁻¹.
- The deuteron binding energy ε ≈ -2.2 MeV was used in the model.
- The coalescence momentum p₀ was shown to be independent of the nucleon-nucleon potential for slope parameter Q up to approximately 300 MeV/c.
- For the IHEP pp data at √s = 11.5 GeV, the threshold correction factor R improved agreement with experimental data significantly.
- For p-Al collisions, the Gaussian parameterization gave a slope parameter Q of about 1.3 GeV/c, where the deuteron wave function is not well known.
- For FNAL data at √s = 23.7 GeV on Be, Ti, and W targets, the model showed quite good agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic coalescence model of Kolybasov (1990s) to antideuteron production, improving upon the earlier coalescence model used by Salati (1990s) which treated p₀ as a free parameter. The approach differs from other models by Mrowczynski, Dover, and Heinz which required additional parameters and did not describe the full ensemble of experimental data. The results enable more reliable predictions of antideuteron fluxes for cosmic-ray experiments such as AMS and PAMELA, and support the feasibility of antideuteron beam experiments.
