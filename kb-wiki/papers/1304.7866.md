# Parity doubling structure of nucleon at non-zero density in the holographic mean field theory

> We develope the holographic mean field theory approach in a bottom-up holographic QCD model including baryons and scalar mesons in addition to vector mesons and pions. We study the effect of parity doubling structure of baryons at non-zero density to the equation of state between the chemical potential and the baryon number density. We first show that we can adjust the amount of nucleon mass coming from the chiral symmetry breaking by changing the boundary value of the five-dimensional baryon fields. Then, introducing the mean field for the baryon fields, we calculate the equation of state between the baryon number density and its corresponding chemical potential. Then, comparing the predicted equation of state with the one obtained in a Walecka type model, we extract the density dependence of the effective nucleon mass. The result shows that the effective mass decreases with increasing density, and that the rate of decreasing is more rapid for the larger percentage of the mass coming from the chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper shows that by adjusting the boundary value of the five-dimensional baryon fields, one can control the percentage of the nucleon mass coming from chiral symmetry breaking versus the chiral invariant mass; the effective nucleon mass extracted from the equation of state decreases more rapidly with increasing baryon density when a larger fraction of the nucleon mass originates from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors develop a holographic mean field theory approach in a bottom-up holographic QCD model that includes five-dimensional baryon fields (N1 and N2), scalar mesons (X), vector mesons, and pions. They introduce a parameter c1, the IR boundary value of the f+2 baryon field component, to control the chiral invariant mass fraction of the nucleon. They then solve the coupled equations of motion for the mean fields at finite baryon density, treating the chemical potential as an eigenvalue, and compute the equation of state relating chemical potential to baryon number density. Finally, they extract the density-dependent effective nucleon mass by comparing their predicted equation of state with a Walecka-type model.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass is fixed to 0.94 GeV for all calculations.
- The IR cutoff is set at z_m = 1/0.3236 (GeV)^{-1}.
- For c1 = 0, all of the nucleon mass comes from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking; for c1 > 0.25, more than half of the nucleon mass is chiral invariant mass.
- The critical value of c1 where the parity ordering of excited states changes is c1* ≈ 0.12.
- The vacuum quark condensate is σ_0 = (318 MeV)^3.
- The omega meson masses used are m_ω^(n) ~ 780, 1794, ... MeV.
- The omega-nucleon couplings in vacuum are g_ω^(n)NN = 15.5-15.8, 8.9-10.9, ... depending on c1.
- Normal nuclear matter density is ρ_0 = 0.16 (fm)^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the holographic mean field theory approach of Harada (2011) from a top-down Sakai-Sugimoto model to a bottom-up holographic QCD model based on the work of Erlich (2005) and DaRold (2005), as formulated by Hong (2006) to include baryons. The parity doubling structure studied here connects to parity doublet models by Detar (1988), Jido (2001), Sasaki (2010), and Paeng (2011). The results enable future studies of fluctuations around the mean field and investigations of isospin chemical potential and symmetry energy in dense matter.
