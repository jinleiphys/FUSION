# Dinucleon correlation of $^9$Li, $^{10}$Be, and $^{9,10}$C

> We study the dinucleon (dineutron and diproton) correlation of the ground states of $^9$Li, $^{10}$Be, and $^{9,10}$C. We assume an $α+t$ core for $^9$Li, an $α+α$ core for $^{10}$Be and $^{10}$C, and an $α+^3$He core for $^9$C, and investigate the effect of core structure changes on the degree of dineutron formation and spatial expansion from the core. For $^9$Li, $t$ cluster breaking in the core significantly enhances the dineutron component inside the nuclei. Moreover, its component markedly depends on the strength of the spin-orbit interaction since a dineutron is fragile and dissociates readily due to the spin-orbit interaction. Compared with $^9$Li, the dineutron of $^{10}$Be dissociates largely due to the stronger spin-orbit attraction from the $α+α$ core than the $α+t$ core. We also investigate diproton features in $^{9,10}$C, the mirror nuclei of $^9$Li and $^{10}$Be, respectively, and compare them with the dineutron features of $^9$Li and $^{10}$Be. No qualitative difference is observed between diprotons and dineutrons in the degree of formation at the surface, but a quantitative difference may exist in the degree of dinucleon-size change far from the core.

## Key claim

The degree of dineutron formation in \(^9\)Li is significantly enhanced by \(t\) cluster breaking in the \(\alpha+t\) core, but the dineutron component depends markedly on the strength of the spin-orbit interaction because a dineutron is fragile and dissociates readily due to spin-orbit effects. For diprotons in the mirror nuclei \(^{9,10}\)C, no qualitative difference from dineutrons is observed at the surface, though a quantitative difference may exist in the degree of dinucleon-size change far from the core.

## Method

The authors constructed a dineutron condensate (DC) wave function superposed with a \(^6\)He\(+t\) cluster wave function to describe the \(^9\)Li ground state, with the \(\alpha+t\) core allowed to undergo \(t\) cluster breaking by assigning imaginary momentum components to nucleons in the \(t\) cluster. For \(^{10}\)Be, a similar superposition was used with an \(\alpha+\alpha\) core and \(\alpha\) cluster breaking, while for the mirror nuclei \(^{9,10}\)C, protons and neutrons were exchanged in the same framework. The wave functions were projected to the appropriate total spin-parity (\(3/2^-\) for \(^9\)Li and \(^9\)C, \(0^+\) for \(^{10}\)Be and \(^{10}\)C) and the coefficients were determined by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian with Volkov No.2 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

- \(^9\)Li ground state total energy: \(-42.85\) MeV with \(t\) cluster breaking (experimental: \(-45.34\) MeV); \(2n\) separation energy \(S_{2n} = 5.62\) MeV with \(t\) breaking (experimental: \(6.09\) MeV)
- \(^9\)Li matter radius: \(2.37\) fm with \(t\) breaking (experimental: \(2.32 \pm 0.02\) fm)
- \(^{10}\)Be ground state total energy: \(-65.14\) MeV with \(\alpha\) breaking (experimental: \(-64.98\) MeV); \(S_{2n} = 9.43\) MeV with \(\alpha\) breaking (experimental: \(8.38\) MeV)
- \(^{10}\)Be matter radius: \(2.41\) fm with \(\alpha\) breaking (experimental: \(2.30 \pm 0.02\) fm)
- \(^9\)C ground state total energy: \(-36.73\) MeV with \(h\) (\({}^{3}\)He) cluster breaking (experimental: \(-39.04\) MeV); \(S_{2p} = 1.04\) MeV with \(h\) breaking (experimental: \(1.44\) MeV)
- \(^{10}\)C ground state total energy: \(-60.76\) MeV with \(\alpha\) breaking (experimental: \(-60.32\) MeV); \(S_{2p} = 5.05\) MeV with \(\alpha\) breaking (experimental: \(3.73\) MeV)
- Gaussian width parameter \(\nu = 0.235\) fm\(^{-2}\) used for all nucleons
- For the \(^9\)Li DC wave function, \(\beta\) values from 2 to 6 fm and \(b_n\) values from approximately 1.5 fm upward were superposed
- The \(\alpha\)-\(t\) threshold energy in free \(^7\)Li is 2.47 MeV; the \(^6\)He\(+t\) threshold energy in \(^9\)Li is 7.59 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of dineutron correlation in \(^{10}\)Be using a DC wave function (Kobayashi, 2011) to the \(^9\)Li system, where the \(\alpha+t\) core allows \(t\) cluster breaking unlike the rigid \(\alpha+\alpha\) core in \(^{10}\)Be. The method for cluster breaking follows the approach of Itagaki (2005) to gain spin-orbit energy. The comparison of dineutron in \(^9\)Li and \(^{10}\)Be with diproton in their mirror nuclei \(^{9,10}\)C addresses whether Coulomb repulsion between protons qualitatively alters dinucleon correlation, building on earlier suggestions by Goldansky (1960) and Grigorenko (2000, 2001, 2003) that diproton correlation is as likely as dineutron correlation. The results enable systematic studies of how core structure changes affect dinucleon formation and expansion in neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei.
