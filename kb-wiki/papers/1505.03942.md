# Structures in 9Be, 10Be and 10B studied with tensor-optimized shell model

> We investigate the structures of $^{9,10}$Be and $^{10}$B with the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) using the effective interaction based on the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8$^\prime$. The tensor correlation is treated in TOSM with the full optimization of 2p2h configurations including high-momentum components. The short-range correlation is described in the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). It is found that the level orders of the low-lying states of $^{9,10}$Be and $^{10}$B are entirely reproduced. For $^9$Be, ground band states are located relatively in higher energy than the experiments, which indicates the missing $α$ clustering correlation in these states as seen in the case of $^8$Be with TOSM. In addition, the tensor force gives the larger attraction for $T$=1/2 states than for $T$=3/2 ones for $^9$Be. For $^{10}$Be, the tensor contribution of $0^+_2$ shows the largest value among the $0^+$ states. This can be related to the $α$ clustering correlation in this state. It is also found that the level order of three nuclei depends on the tensor force in comparison with the results obtained with the Minnesota interaction without the tensor force.

## Key claim
The tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) with the AV8$^\prime_{\rm eff}$ interaction reproduces the level orders of low-lying states in $^{9,10}$Be and $^{10}$B, but for $^9$Be the ground band states are located at higher energy than experiment, indicating missing $\alpha$ clustering correlation in these states as previously seen for $^8$Be with TOSM.

## Method
The authors used the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) with an effective interaction based on the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8$^\prime$, where the tensor matrix elements were enhanced by 10% and the spin-orbit matrix elements by 40% to reproduce the $^4$He results of the tensor-optimized few-body model (TOFM). Short-range correlations were treated with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). The TOSM wave function superposes 0p0h, 1p1h, and 2p2h configurations with particle states up to orbital angular momentum $L_{\rm max}=10$ using Gaussian basis functions, and the variational parameters include both the amplitudes of configurations and the length parameters of the basis functions.

## Key numbers
- Total binding energy of $^9$Be ground state in TOSM: 27.71 MeV (experimental value: 58.17 MeV)
- Total binding energy of $^8$Be with AV8$^\prime_{\rm eff}$: 30.19 MeV (experimental value: 56.50 MeV)
- Matter radius of $^9$Be ground state in TOSM: 2.32 fm (experimental value: 2.38(1) fm)
- Matter radius of $^{10}$Be in TOSM: 2.31 fm (experimental value: 2.30(2) fm)
- Matter radius of $^{10}$B in TOSM: 2.20 fm
- Tensor contribution to $^9$Be $\frac32^-_1$ ground state ($T=1/2$): -102.07 MeV
- Tensor contribution to $^9$Be $\frac32^-$ state ($T=3/2$): -84.25 MeV
- Relative energy between $4^+_1$ and $2^+_2$ in $^8$Be from TOSM: about 2 MeV (experimental value: 6 MeV)
- Binding energy of $^9$Be ground state with Minnesota interaction: 66.50 MeV (experimental value: 58.17 MeV)
- Radius of $^9$Be with Minnesota interaction: 1.96 fm

## Context
This work extends the previous TOSM study of $^8$Be (Myo et al., 2014) to neighboring nuclei $^{9,10}$Be and $^{10}$B, investigating how the tensor force affects the energy spectra and isospin dependence. The results are compared with calculations using the Minnesota interaction without tensor force (Tang et al., 1978) to isolate tensor effects. The missing $\alpha$ clustering correlation in the ground band states of $^9$Be, analogous to the $^8$Be case, suggests that extending TOSM to include explicit $\alpha$ clustering components could improve the description of these states. The study also enables systematic investigation of tensor correlation in $p$-shell nuclei using the AV8$^\prime_{\rm eff}$ interaction.
