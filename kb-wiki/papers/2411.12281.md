# Preponderance of triaxial shapes in atomic nuclei predicted by the proxy-SU(3) symmetry

> The proxy-SU(3) symmetry predicts, in a parameter-free way, based only on the Pauli principle and the short-range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, non-vanishing values of the collective variable gamma almost everywhere across the nuclear chart. Substantial triaxiality with gamma between 15 and 45 degrees is proved to be expected along horizontal and vertical stripes on the nuclear chart, covering the nucleon numbers 22-26, 34-48, 74-80, 116-124, 172-182. Empirical support for these stripes is found by collecting all even-even nuclei for which the first two excited 2+ states are known, along with the B(E2)s connecting them, as well as the second 2+ state to the ground state. The stripes are related to regions in which oblate SU(3) irreducible representations appear, bearing similarity to the appearance of triaxiality within the SU(3)* dynamical symmetry of the interacting boson model-2. Detailed comparisons of the proxy-SU(3) predictions to the data and to predictions by state-of-the-art Monte Carlo shell model calculations for deformed N=94, 96, 98 isotones in the rare earth region show good overall agreement, with the exception of Z=70 and N=94, which correspond to fully symmetric proxy-SU(3) irreps, suggesting that the latter are an artifact of the method which can be amended by considering the influence of the neighboring irreps.

# Preponderance of triaxial shapes in atomic nuclei predicted by the proxy-SU(3) symmetry

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) symmetry predicts, in a parameter-free way, that substantial triaxiality (gamma between 15 and 45 degrees) occurs along specific horizontal and vertical stripes on the nuclear chart covering nucleon numbers 22-26, 34-48, 74-80, 116-124, and 172-182, but the predictions fail for nuclei where the highest-weight irreducible representation has mu=0 (fully symmetric irreps), which the authors identify as an artifact amendable by including next-higher-weight irreps.

## Method

The authors used the proxy-SU(3) approximation to the shell model, which restores SU(3) symmetry broken by the spin-orbit interaction in heavy nuclei, to compute the collective variable gamma from the Elliott quantum numbers (lambda, mu) via the mapping of Castanos (1988). They compared these parameter-free predictions to experimental gamma values extracted from energy ratios R = E(2_2+)/E(2_1+) and branching ratios R_2 = B(E2; 2_2+ to 2_1+)/B(E2; 2_2+ to 0_1+) using the Davydov model formalism, and to state-of-the-art Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) calculations for N=94, 96, 98 isotones in the rare earth region.

## Key numbers

- Triaxiality stripes cover nucleon numbers: 22-26, 34-48, 74-80, 116-124, 172-182
- Substantial triaxiality defined as gamma between 15 degrees and 45 degrees (plus or minus 15 degrees around maximal triaxiality at 30 degrees)
- At gamma = 15 degrees, the energy ratio R = 6.85 and the branching ratio R_2 = 2.71
- The energy ratio R starts from 2 at gamma = 30 degrees and rises toward infinity at gamma = 0
- The branching ratio R_2 starts from 1.43 at gamma = 0 and rises toward infinity at gamma = 30 degrees
- For N=94, 96, 98 isotones: proxy-SU(3) predictions are close to empirical R values, MCSM predictions are close to empirical R_2 values, with the former pair systematically higher than the latter
- The problematic Z=70 and N=94 cases correspond to highest-weight irreps (20,0) and (36,0) respectively, both with mu=0
- After including next-higher-weight irreps, the Z=70 proton irrep changes from (20,0) to (10,14) and the N=94 neutron irrep changes from (36,0) to (28,10)
- The collective variable beta changes by only a few percent when replacing highest-weight with next-higher-weight irreps

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry approach (Bonatsos 2017) to systematically predict regions of triaxiality across the nuclear chart, complementing earlier studies of shape coexistence (Martinou 2021). The predicted stripes are compatible with the SU(3)* dynamical symmetry of the interacting boson model-2 (Dieperink 1982, Walet 1987), where triaxiality appears when one type of valence nucleon corresponds to particles and the other to holes. The comparison with MCSM calculations (Otsuka 2024) for rare-earth isotones provides guidance for amending the proxy-SU(3) scheme when fully symmetric irreps appear, and the results enable future extension to superheavy nuclei where triaxiality has been predicted by symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing calculations (Egido 2020).
