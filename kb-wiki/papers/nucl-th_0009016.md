# Symmetry Violation in Heavy Nuclei

> The advantage of searching for violation of Time Reversal Invariance in neutron induced reactions using relative measurements of Time and Parity violating effects is discussed. This approach gives the enhancement of $T$-violating effects by many orders of magnitude and, at the same time, decrees theoretical uncertainties and experimental errors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that simultaneous measurement of parity-violating (P) and time-reversal-violating (PT) effects in neutron-induced reactions on heavy nuclei allows extraction of the ratio of PT- to P-violating nucleon coupling constants, eliminating both nuclear structure uncertainties and experimental normalization errors. This approach is presented as a "pure test of T violation with no reference to the CPT theorem" that can achieve sensitivity to many CP-violating models.

## Method

The author derives expressions for the difference in total cross sections for polarized neutron transmission through polarized targets (PT-odd effect, proportional to the T-odd correlation (σ·[k×I])) and through unpolarized targets (P-odd effect, proportional to (σ·k)). Using a two-resonance approximation for compound nuclear states, the ratio of these cross-section differences is shown to equal a calculable spin-dependent coefficient times the ratio w/v of the imaginary (PT-violating) to real (P-violating) parts of the mixing matrix element between s-wave and p-wave compound resonances. This ratio is then related to the fundamental nucleon-nucleon coupling constant ratio λ = g_PT/g_P through a nuclear-model-dependent factor ξ.

## Key numbers

- Parity violating effects in heavy nuclei can be as large as ~10⁻¹ compared to the "natural" scale of ~10⁻⁷ for few-nucleon systems.
- Typical neutron energy for resonance reactions is about 1 eV to 100 eV; compound nuclear excitation energy is about 6 MeV.
- Energy resolution in neutron resonance experiments is about 2×10⁻⁷, and can be improved to ~10⁻¹¹ in real experiments.
- The PT-violating effect has nuclear enhancement factors of ~10⁶, similar to the P-violating effect.
- The expected experimental sensitivity for the parameter λ_exp is ~10⁻⁴ at the Spallation Neutron Source.
- Model predictions for λ = g_PT/g_P include: Kobayashi-Maskawa model ≤ 10⁻¹⁰; Right-Left model ≤ 4×10⁻³; Horizontal symmetry ≤ 10⁻⁵; Weinberg model (charged Higgs) ≤ 2×10⁻⁶; Weinberg model (neutral Higgs) ≤ 3×10⁻⁴; θ-term in QCD ≤ 5×10⁻⁵; neutron EDM (one π-loop) ≤ 4×10⁻³; atomic EDM (¹⁹⁹Hg) ≤ 2×10⁻³.
- The nuclear correction factor ξ is in the range 1 to 7.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of using heavy nuclei for symmetry violation studies, building on the nuclear enhancement mechanisms described by Sushkov and Flambaum (1980) and Bunakov and Gudkov (1981). The proposed method addresses the difficulty that P-violating effects in heavy nuclei cannot provide information about weak interactions due to the complexity of nuclear wave functions, as noted by Auerbach (1980) and others. The approach enables a test of T-violation in the ΔS = 0 sector, which is complementary to CP-violation measurements in K⁰-meson decays (CPLEAR experiment, 1998) and to electric dipole moment searches in neutrons and atoms (¹⁹⁹Hg).
