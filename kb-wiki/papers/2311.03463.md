# Universal features of $ 2\to N$ scattering in QCD and gravity from shockwave collisions

> A remarkable double copy relation of Einstein gravity to QCD in Regge asymptotics is $Γ^{μν}= \frac12C^μC^ν- \frac12N^μN^ν$, where $Γ^{μν}$ is the gravitational Lipatov vertex in the $2\to 3$ graviton scattering amplitude, $C^μ$ its Yang-Mills counterpart, and $N^μ$ the QED bremssstrahlung vertex. In QCD, the Lipatov vertex is a fundamental building block of the BFKL equation describing $2\to N$ scattering of gluons at high energies. Likewise, the gravitational Lipatov vertex is a key ingredient in a 2-D effective field theory framework describing trans-Planckian $2\to N$ graviton scattering. We construct a quantitative correspondence between a semi-classical Yang-Mills framework for radiation in gluon shockwave collisions and its counterpart in general relativity. In particular, we demonstrate the Lipatov double copy in a dilute-dilute approximation corresponding to $R_{S,L}$, $R_{S,H}$ $ \ll b$, with $R_{S,L}$, $R_{S,H}$ the respective emergent Schwarzchild radii generated in shockwave collisions and $b$ is the impact parameter. We outline extensions of the correspondence developed here to the dilute-dense computation of gravitational wave radiation in close vicinity of one of the black holes, the construction of graviton propagators in the shockwave background, and a renormalization group approach to compute $2\rightarrow N$ amplitudes that incorporates graviton reggeization and coherent graviton multiple scattering.

# Universal features of $2\to N$ scattering in QCD and gravity from shockwave collisions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Lipatov double copy relation $\Gamma^{\mu\nu}= \frac12C^\mu C^\nu- \frac12N^\mu N^\nu$ (where $\Gamma^{\mu\nu}$ is the gravitational Lipatov vertex, $C^\mu$ its Yang-Mills counterpart, and $N^\mu$ the QED bremsstrahlung vertex) holds in a semi-classical shockwave collision framework, but only in the dilute-dilute approximation where the emergent Schwarzschild radii $R_{S,L}$ and $R_{S,H}$ are both much smaller than the impact parameter $b$.

## Method

The authors construct a quantitative correspondence between semi-classical Yang-Mills shockwave collisions (as formulated in the Color Glass Condensate EFT) and gravitational shockwave collisions in Einstein-Hilbert gravity. For the gravitational case, they solve the linearized Einstein equations in the Aichelburg-Sexl shockwave metric for mass distributions with transverse extent, deriving the geodesic equations for the energy-momentum tensor and extracting the gravitational Lipatov vertex from the solution. The derivation is restricted to the dilute-dilute regime where $R_{S,L}/b \ll 1$ and $R_{S,H}/b \ll 1$, corresponding to impact parameters large enough that multiple scattering and nonlinear tidal effects are sub-dominant.

## Key numbers

- The double copy relation is demonstrated in the regime $R_{S,L}, R_{S,H} \ll b$, where $R_{S,L}$ and $R_{S,H}$ are the emergent Schwarzschild radii generated in shockwave collisions and $b$ is the impact parameter.
- In QCD, the BFKL exponent for the growth of the inclusive DIS cross-section is $\omega = 4N_c\alpha_s(\log 2)/\pi$, where $N_c$ is the number of colors.
- The gravitational analog of the QCD saturation scale condition is $\alpha N \sim 1$, where $\alpha = Q^2/M_{\rm Pl}^2$ for a probe with momentum resolution $Q \sim 1/R_S$, and the microstate entropy in black hole scattering is $S = 1/\alpha$, saturating the Bekenstein-Hawking area law.
- In QCD, gluon distributions saturate at maximal occupancy $N = 1/\alpha_S$ in a region of size $1/Q_S$, where $Q_S \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ is the emergent saturation scale.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative double copy relations of Lipatov (1982) between QCD and gravity in Regge asymptotics, and connects them to the semi-classical strong-field frameworks of the Color Glass Condensate EFT (McLerran, Venugopalan 1994) for QCD and the Amati-Ciafaloni-Veneziano approach (1993) for trans-Planckian graviton scattering. The authors aim to develop a renormalization group framework for gravitational $2\to N$ scattering analogous to the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation in QCD, which would describe black hole formation as a possible fixed point at a critical impact parameter $b_c \sim R_S$. The work is complementary to the BCJ double copy program (Bern, Carrasco, Johansson 2008) and to post-Minkowskian expansions for binary black hole mergers, and is motivated by the universal features of high-occupancy scattering discussed by Dvali and collaborators (2011-2021).
