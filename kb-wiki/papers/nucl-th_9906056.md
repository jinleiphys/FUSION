# The NN scattering 3S1-3D1 mixing angle at NNLO

> The 3S1-3D1 mixing angle for nucleon-nucleon scattering, epsilon_1, is calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order in an effective field theory with perturbative pions. Without pions, the low energy theory fits the observed epsilon_1 well for momenta less than $\sim 50$ MeV. Including pions perturbatively significantly improves the agreement with data for momenta up to $\sim 150$ MeV with one less parameter. Furthermore, for these momenta the accuracy of our calculation is similar to an effective field theory calculation in which the pion is treated non-perturbatively. This gives phenomenological support for a perturbative treatment of pions in low energy two-nucleon processes. We explain why it is necessary to perform spin and isospin traces in d dimensions when regulating divergences with dimensional regularization in higher partial wave amplitudes.

## Key claim

The NNLO calculation of the $^3S_1-{}^3D_1$ mixing angle $\epsilon_1$ in an effective field theory with perturbative pions achieves accuracy comparable to non-perturbative pion treatments for momenta up to $\sim 150$ MeV, but only when the unknown parameter $C_2^{(SD)}$ is fitted to low-energy data; the paper notes in a note added that subsequent NNLO calculations in other channels suggest a breakdown of the perturbative treatment of pions.

## Method

The authors computed the $^3S_1-{}^3D_1$ mixing angle $\epsilon_1$ to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, order $Q^2$) in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power counting, using dimensional regularization with the PDS renormalization scheme. They evaluated Feynman diagrams with potential pions and contact interactions, summing bubble graphs with $C_0$ to all orders, and determined the unknown parameter $C_2^{(SD)}$ by fitting to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis at low momentum. Spin and isospin traces were performed in $n$ dimensions to preserve rotational symmetry and Wigner symmetry when regulating divergences.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: $B = 2.22$ MeV, giving $\gamma = \sqrt{M B} = 45.7$ MeV
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 137$ MeV; pion decay constant: $f = 131$ MeV; nucleon axial-vector coupling: $g_A = 1.25$
- Fitted parameters: $\zeta_1 = 0.2345$, $\zeta_2 = -0.1038$, $\zeta_6 = 0.385$, corresponding to $C_2^{(SD)}(m_\pi) = -4.56$ fm$^4$
- At $p = m_\pi$, the NNLO prediction for $\bar\epsilon_1$ differs from data by $\sim 0.5^\circ$, with error $\sim 20\%$
- For $p \le 180$ MeV, $| \text{Im}(\bar\epsilon_1) / (\bar\epsilon_1^{(1)} + \bar\epsilon_1^{(2)}) | \le 0.2$
- For $p \le m_\pi$, $| \mathcal{A}^{SD(1)} / \mathcal{A}^{SD(0)} | \le 0.6$
- Effective range expansion coefficients from fit to Nijmegen data: $g_1 = 0.30 \pm 0.01$ fm$^2$, $g_2 = -2.0 \pm 0.2$ fm$^4$, $g_3 = 8.7 \pm 4.3$ fm$^6$; NNLO predictions: $g_1 = 0.22$ fm$^2$, $g_2 = -1.5$ fm$^4$, $g_3 = 9.5$ fm$^6$

## Context

This work extends the KSW effective field theory calculations (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998a, 1998b) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to NNLO for the mixing angle, following earlier NLO calculations of $\epsilon_1$ in the same framework. The results improve upon the theory without pions (which breaks down around $m_\pi/2 \sim 68$ MeV) and achieve accuracy comparable to the non-perturbative pion approach of Ordonez et al. (1996), which uses Weinberg's power counting and solves the Schroedinger equation with one-pion exchange. The calculation enables future work including $\Delta$ isobars or going to one higher order in $Q$ to improve agreement at momenta above $m_\pi$, though a note added reports that NNLO calculations in other channels show large corrections suggesting a breakdown of perturbative pions.
