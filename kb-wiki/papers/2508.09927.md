# Thermodynamics of fermionic excitations in heavy-quark QCD

> We investigate the thermodynamic properties of fermionic excitations in heavy-quark QCD on the lattice with Wilson fermions. The grand potential is calculated analytically in the hopping parameter expansion (HPE) on the basis of the cumulant expansion. Using the grand potential, we compute the quark number susceptibilities and their ratios up to next-to-leading order in the HPE. The ratio of fourth- to second-order susceptibilities is shown to be unity (nine) in the deconfined (confined) phase at the leading order. Excitation properties of baryonic and quark modes in each phase are also investigated utilizing the Boltzmann statistics. We obtain an analytic formula for the quark excitation energy in the deconfined phase, while that for baryonic excitations in the confined phase is decomposed into flavor multiplets.

## Key claim

The ratio of fourth- to second-order quark-number susceptibilities is exactly unity in the deconfined phase and exactly nine in the confined phase at leading order in the hopping parameter expansion, but this result holds only up to order κ^{2N_t−1} above T_c and up to order κ^{6N_t−1} below T_c, with the next-to-leading order terms being significantly suppressed by cancellations.

## Method

The authors perform a hopping parameter expansion (HPE) of the fermion determinant for heavy-quark QCD with Wilson fermions on the lattice, reorganizing the grand potential via a cumulant expansion to separate terms by their chemical-potential dependence. They compute quark-number susceptibilities and their ratios up to next-to-leading order in the HPE, and extract excitation energies by matching the grand potential to that of a lattice Boltzmann gas of heavy fermions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio χ₄/χ₂ equals 1 (deconfined phase) and 9 (confined phase) at leading order in the HPE, up to order κ^{2N_t−1} and κ^{6N_t−1} respectively.
- The leading-order term for X₁ (the w=1 contribution to the grand potential) is 2 N_c N_f (2κ)^{N_t} ⟨L₁⟩, where N_c=3.
- The quark excitation energy in the deconfined phase is E = m_pole − T ln[ (2κ)^{N_t} (1/(N_c N_x³)) ∑_s ⟨tr_c ℓ_P(s)⟩ ].
- The leading-order term for Λ₀ is −(2κ)⁴ N_f ∑_s ∑_{μ<ν} Re tr_c P_{μν}(s) + O(κ⁶).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of quark-number susceptibilities in heavy-quark QCD, building on lattice studies by Ejiri (2005) and Asakawa (2015) which showed that even-order baryon-number susceptibilities are equivalent below T_pc in QCD with physical quark masses. The authors provide an analytic derivation showing that the ratio χ₄/χ₂ jumps discontinuously between 1 and 9 at T_c due to Z₃ symmetry breaking, and they connect the excitation energies to Polyakov-loop expectation values. This enables future lattice simulations of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory to compute the NLO corrections to these ratios using the analytic formulas provided.
