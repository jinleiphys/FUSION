# Caloric Curves for small systems in the Nuclear Lattice Gas Model

> For pedagogical reasons we compute the caloric curve for 11 particles in a $3^3$ lattice. Monte-Carlo simulation can be avoided and exact results are obtained. There is no back-bending in the caloric curve and negative specific heat does not appear. We point out that the introduction of kinetic energy in the nuclear Lattice Gas Model modifies the results of the standard Lattice Gas Model in a profound way.

# Caloric Curves for small systems in the Nuclear Lattice Gas Model

## Key claim

For 11 particles in a 3^3 lattice, the nuclear Lattice Gas Model (LGM) produces no backbending in the caloric curve and no negative specific heat, in contrast to the standard Lattice Gas Model (SLGM) where backbending appears due to the finite upper bound on excitation energy. The introduction of kinetic energy in the nuclear LGM removes this upper bound and eliminates the negative temperature zone entirely.

## Method

The authors computed exact degeneracy factors g(N_nn) for all possible nearest-neighbor bond configurations of 11 particles on a 3^3 lattice. For the standard Lattice Gas Model (SLGM), they used these degeneracies directly to construct the density of states as a function of excitation energy. For the nuclear LGM, they convolved the discrete potential-energy degeneracies with the continuous kinetic-energy density of states for 11 non-interacting particles, using the integral form of the momentum-space volume. Temperature was extracted both from the standard microcanonical derivative of the total density of states and from the average kinetic energy via the relation T = <E_kin>/(1.5N-1).

## Key numbers

- System: 11 particles on a 3^3 lattice; freeze-out density is 0.41 rho_0
- Nearest-neighbor bond energy: epsilon = -5.33 MeV, chosen to reproduce nuclear matter binding energy
- Degeneracy factors range from g=462 for 0 bonds to g=96 for 17 bonds (the ground state)
- The microcanonical temperature in SLGM diverges to +infinity at E*/|epsilon| approximately 8, corresponding to about 4 MeV excitation per particle for 11 particles
- The saddle-point approximation for the nuclear LGM density of states is excellent except at low excitation energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microcanonical Lattice Gas Model calculations (Das, Das Gupta, Samaddar, 2000) which found no backbending for systems as small as 84Kr. It directly contradicts findings of backbending in small systems reported by Carmona et al. (2000), Gross and Votyakov (2000), and Chomaz, Gulminelli, and Duflot (in press) using other models. The exact treatment of 11 particles demonstrates that the presence or absence of backbending is model-dependent, and the nuclear LGM with kinetic energy avoids the finite-energy bound that causes backbending in the standard LGM. The work also validates the saddle-point approximation for connecting canonical and microcanonical treatments in this model.
