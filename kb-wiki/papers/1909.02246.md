# Strangeness production in the new version of the Liège Intra-Nuclear Cascade model

> The capabilities of the new version of the Liège Intra-Nuclear Cascade model (INCL++6) are presented in detail. This new version INCL is able to handle strange particles, such as kaons and the $Λ$ particle, and the associated reactions and also allows extending nucleon-nucleon collisions up to about $15-20$ GeV incident energy. Compared to the previous version, new observables can be studied, e.g., kaon, hyperon, and hypernuclei production cross sections (with the use of a suitable de-excitation code) as well as aspects of kaon-induced spallation reactions. The main purpose of this paper is to present the specific ingredients of the new INCL version and its new features, notably the new variance reduction scheme. We also compare for some illustrative strangeness production cases calculated using this version of INCL with experimental data.

## Key claim

The new version of the Liège Intra-Nuclear Cascade model (INCL++6) extends the model's energy range up to approximately 15-20 GeV and adds the capability to handle strange particles (kaons, antikaons, Σ baryons, and Λ hyperons), but the comparison with experimental data is limited to "some illustrative strangeness production cases" and the observables depending on both the cascade and de-excitation model are deferred to a future publication.

## Method

The authors implemented strangeness production in INCL++6 by adding kaons (K⁰, K⁺), antikaons (K⁻, K̄⁰), Σ baryons (Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺), and Λ hyperons as new particle types, each with defined masses, charges, isospin, decay modes, and constant average nuclear potentials. Production, scattering, and absorption cross sections for these particles were determined using experimental measurements and isospin symmetry, supplemented by model-based cross sections from Tsushima et al. for ΔN reactions and inclusive strangeness production channels. A variance reduction scheme (VRS) was implemented that artificially increases the probability of strangeness-producing binary collisions while preserving total interaction cross sections, with the bias corrected by weighting each final particle by its importance factor (the ratio of unbiased to biased probability).

## Key numbers

- The model extends nucleon-nucleon collisions up to about 15-20 GeV incident energy, compared to the previous upper limit of 2-3 GeV.
- Strangeness production represents 0.014% of the total cross section in proton-proton collision at 2 GeV kinetic energy, and 0.15% at 3 GeV.
- The Λ hyperon uses an attractive nuclear potential of 28 MeV for symmetric nuclei, increasing to 41 MeV for the highest asymmetries ((A-2Z)/A = 0.25).
- The K⁺ potential is set to a repulsive 25 MeV; the K⁻ potential is an attractive 60 MeV.
- K⁰ and K̄⁰ potentials are set to 15 MeV and -50 MeV respectively (10 MeV correction from K⁺/K⁻ due to Coulomb repulsion).
- Σ baryons use a repulsive potential of 16 MeV for all Σ particles.

## Context

This work extends the Liège Intra-Nuclear Cascade model (INCL) series, which was recognized by the IAEA benchmark in 2010 as one of the best spallation models when combined with the ABLA07 de-excitation code. The high-energy extension builds on prior work by Pedoux (multipion production) and by Cugnon (η and ω particle implementation), and is based on a previous study by the same group that reunified the main ingredients for strange particle implementation. The new version enables studies of kaon, hyperon, and hypernuclei production cross sections, and aims to improve the transition between intra-nuclear cascade models and string models like the FTF model at the few-GeV scale, which is crucial for cosmic ray interactions.
