# Strange Exotic States and Compact Stars

> We discuss the possible appearance of strange exotic multi-quark states in the interior of neutron stars and signals for the existence of strange quark matter in the core of compact stars. We show how the in-medium properties of possible pentaquark states are constrained by pulsar mass measurements. The possibility of generating the observed large pulsar kick velocities by asymmetric emission of neutrinos from strange quark matter in magnetic fields is outlined.

# Strange Exotic States and Compact Stars

## Key claim

The presence of pentaquark states in neutron star cores reduces the maximum mass of compact stars, allowing constraints on their in-medium potential depths from pulsar mass measurements; however, present limits give only loose bounds, with the constraint becoming much tighter only with a slightly increased pulsar mass limit.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field model with nucleons, leptons, and hyperons, where interactions are mediated by scalar (σ, σ*) and vector (ω, ρ, φ) mesons with SU(6) symmetry relations for vector meson coupling constants. The scalar coupling constant for pentaquarks is determined by choosing a potential depth at normal nuclear matter density and varied accordingly, while vector coupling strength is fixed by naive quark counting rules with a negative sign as advocated by QCD sum rules. The model is applied to beta-equilibrated neutron star matter to compute the maximum mass and composition for various pentaquark species.

## Key numbers

- The light pentaquark Θ⁺ has a mass of 1.54 GeV and a width of less than 1 MeV.
- For a pulsar mass of M > 1.6 M⊙, the potential depth of the Θ⁺ pentaquark must be U(Θ⁺) > -190 MeV (for parameter set GM1).
- For a hypothetical negatively charged pentaquark Θ⁻, pulsar mass constraints give U(Θ⁻) > -55 to -125 MeV for M > 1.44 M⊙ and U(Θ⁻) > 0 to -85 MeV for M > 1.6 M⊙.
- Assuming U(Θ⁺) = -100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, the Θ⁺ appears around 4n₀ in beta-equilibrated dense matter, with a maximum fraction of about five percent.
- The negatively charged Θ⁻ appears already at 2n₀ (for U(Θ⁻) = -100 MeV and set GM1), and the maximum density for stable configurations is reduced to only 3.3n₀.
- The highest directly measured pulsar kick velocity is 1080 ± 100 km/s.
- For a neutrino rocket to produce a 1000 km/s kick, an initial temperature of about T = 5 MeV is needed.
- Production rates for multiply charmed multiquark states at RHIC amount to 10⁻³ to 10⁻² per event within a simple coalescence model.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of multiquark states, beginning with Gell-Mann (1964) and Jaffe (1977) on tetraquarks and the H-dibaryon, and the chiral soliton model prediction of the Θ⁺ pentaquark by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). The authors apply the same reasoning used for hyperon effects on neutron star maximum mass (Glendenning and Moszkowski, 1991) to pentaquarks, and compare with recent pulsar mass measurements from binary pulsars with white dwarfs. The work enables future constraints from improved pulsar mass measurements and motivates searches for charmed multiquark states at RHIC using PHENIX and STAR detectors with micro-vertex detectors.
