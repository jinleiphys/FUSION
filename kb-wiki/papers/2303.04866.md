# Multi-scale Imaging of Nuclear Deformation at the Electron Ion Collider

> We show within the Color Glass Condensate framework that exclusive vector meson production at high energy is sensitive to the geometric deformation of the target nucleus at multiple length scales. Studying $e+$U collisions and varying the deformation of the uranium target, we demonstrate that larger deformations result in enhanced incoherent vector meson production cross sections. Further, different multipole deformation parameters affect different regions of transverse momentum transfer. Employing JIMWLK evolution to study the Bjorken-$x$ dependence of our results, we find that the ratio of incoherent to coherent cross sections decreases with decreasing $x$, largely independently of the quadrupole deformation of the target. Comparing results for the same process using ${\rm {^{20}Ne}}$ targets with ${\rm {^{16}O}}$ targets, we find that differences in deformation are clearly visible in the incoherent cross section. These findings show that certain observables at the Electron-Ion Collider are very sensitive to nuclear structure. Consequently, deformations need to be taken into account when interpreting experimental results. More importantly, this also means that $\vert t\vert$-differential diffractive vector meson production could become a powerful tool, enabling the most direct measurements of nuclear structure at different length scales, ranging from nuclear deformation at low $\vert t\vert$ to nucleon- and subnucleon-size scales at higher $\vert t\vert$.

## Key claim

Within the Color Glass Condensate framework, exclusive vector meson production at high energy is sensitive to the geometric deformation of the target nucleus at multiple length scales, with the caveat that the ratio of incoherent to coherent cross sections decreases with decreasing Bjorken-\(x\) largely independently of the quadrupole deformation, meaning deformations are not washed out by JIMWLK evolution.

## Method

The authors model nuclear geometry by sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution with multipole deformation parameters \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\), and \(\beta_4\), and include sub-nucleonic fluctuations via gluonic hot spots with parameters constrained by a Bayesian analysis of HERA exclusive J/\(\psi\) data. They compute coherent and incoherent J/\(\psi\) photoproduction cross sections in the Color Glass Condensate framework using the dipole amplitude obtained from Wilson lines, and apply JIMWLK evolution to study the Bjorken-\(x\) dependence of the results for uranium targets. For light nuclei, they compare \({}^{20}\)Ne from ab initio Projected Generator Coordinate Method calculations with spherical \({}^{20}\)Ne and \({}^{16}\)O from variational Monte Carlo methods.

## Key numbers

- For uranium with realistic \(\beta_2 = 0.28\), the incoherent cross section at \(|t| \sim 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) is increased by a factor \(\sim 5\) compared to the spherical case with \(\beta_2 = 0.0\).
- The \(\beta_2\) deformation modifies the incoherent cross section in the region \(|t| \lesssim 0.015\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\); \(\beta_3\) is important for \(0.005\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \lesssim |t| \lesssim 0.025\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\); \(\beta_4\) for \(0.015\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \lesssim |t| \lesssim 0.035\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- For the PGCM \({}^{20}\)Ne target compared to \({}^{16}\)O, the incoherent cross section ratio shows an enhancement of up to a factor 2.7, compared to 1.5 when comparing spherical neon to oxygen.
- At \(|t| \approx 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), the incoherent cross section of \({}^{20}\)Ne is almost a factor of 2 larger than that of \({}^{16}\)O.
- The initial Bjorken-\(x\) for the uranium configurations is \(\xpom = 1.7 \times 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis of proton geometry from HERA J/\(\psi\) data (Mantysaari et al., 2022) to electron-nucleus collisions, and for the first time explores the effects of nuclear deformation on diffractive cross sections for large nuclei. It builds on hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions that revealed how nuclear structural properties are visible in final state particle correlations (Filip, 2009; Masui, 2009; Hirano, 2012; Shen, 2014; Schenke, 2014, 2020; Xu, 2021; Giacalone, 2021; Zhang, 2021; Nijs, 2021; Cheng, 2023; Ryssens, 2023). The results enable the EIC to provide the most direct measurements of nuclear structure at different length scales, from nuclear deformation at low \(|t|\) to nucleon- and subnucleon-size scales at higher \(|t|\), complementing low-energy nuclear structure experiments and constraining the initial state of heavy ion collisions.
