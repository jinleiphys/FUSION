# Baryon magnetic moments: Symmetries and relations

> Magnetic moments of the octet baryons are computed using lattice QCD in background magnetic fields, including the first treatment of the magnetically coupled Sigma-Lambda system. Although the computations are performed for relatively large values of the up and down quark masses, we gain new insight into the symmetries and relations between magnetic moments by working at a three-flavor mass-symmetric point. While the spin-flavor symmetry in the large Nc limit of QCD is shared by the naive constituent quark model, we find instances where quark model predictions are considerably favored over those emerging in the large Nc limit. We suggest further calculations that would shed light on the curious patterns of baryon magnetic moments.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD computation of octet baryon magnetic moments at a three-flavor mass-symmetric point (where up, down, and strange quarks have equal masses) reveals that naive constituent quark model predictions are often favored over those from the large \(N_c\) limit of QCD, particularly for relations that depend on the level of SU(3)\(_F\) breaking.

## Method

Magnetic moments of the octet baryons were computed using lattice QCD with background magnetic fields, employing tadpole-improved clover fermions on two ensembles: one with exact SU(3)\(_F\) symmetry at a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, and another with physical strange quark mass and a pion mass of approximately 450 MeV. Classical U(1) gauge links were post-multiplied to the color links to introduce a uniform magnetic field, and the Zeeman splitting between spin states was extracted from double ratios of two-point correlation functions. For the magnetically coupled \(\Sigma^0\)-\(\Lambda\) system, a coupled-channels analysis using the matrix of correlation functions was performed to determine the energy eigenvalues and transition polarizability.

## Key numbers

* Proton magnetic moment extraction was limited by systematics of fitting the linear magnetic field-strength term, not by statistics from approximately 400k measurements across roughly 1500 configurations.
* On the SU(3)\(_F\)-symmetric ensemble, the pion mass was \(m_\pi \approx 800\) MeV; on the ensemble with physical strange quark mass, the pion mass was \(m_\pi \approx 450\) MeV.
* The transition polarizability in the \(\Sigma^0\)-\(\Lambda\) system was determined as \(\beta_{\Lambda \Sigma} = -1.82(06)(12)(02) \, \text{fm}^3\), compared to the neutron magnetic polarizability \(\beta_n = 3.48(12)(26)(04) \, \text{fm}^3\).
* The lattice spacing was determined as \(a = 0.1453(16) \, \text{fm}\) from the quarkonium hyperfine splitting.
* For the SU(3)\(_F\)-symmetric ensemble, the spatial lattice size was \(N = 32\) sites in each direction.

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD collaboration's program of computing electromagnetic properties of hadrons and light nuclei in background magnetic fields, following earlier determinations of magnetic moments and polarizabilities of light nuclei by Beane et al. (2014) and Chang et al. (2015). The study provides the first lattice QCD treatment of the magnetically coupled \(\Sigma^0\)-\(\Lambda\) system and enables future investigations of the curious pattern of baryon magnetic moments, particularly through further SU(3)\(_F\)-symmetric computations at lighter quark masses and calculations with larger numbers of colors (\(N_c = 5, \dots\)) to distinguish between quark model and large \(N_c\) predictions.
