# 3-dimensional QCD phase diagram with pion condensate in the NJL model

> With the isovector coupling constants adjusted to reproduce the physical pion mass and lattice QCD results in baryon-free quark matter, we have carried out rigourous calculations for the pion condensate in the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and studied the 3-dimensional QCD phase diagram. With the increasing isospin chemical potential $μ_I$, we have observed two nonzero solutions of the pion condensate at finite baryon chemical potentials $μ_B$, representing respectively the pion superfluid phase and the Sarma phase, and their appearance and disappearance correspond to a second-order (first-order) phase transition at higher (lower) temperatures $T$ and lower (higher) $μ_B$. Calculations by assuming equal constituent mass of $u$ and $d$ quarks would lead to large errors of the QCD phase diagram within $μ_B \in (500, 900)$ MeV, and affect the position of the critical end point.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a rigorous calculation allowing unequal constituent masses for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks reveals two distinct nonzero solutions of the pion condensate (the pion superfluid phase and the Sarma phase) in the 3-dimensional QCD phase diagram, and that the common assumption of equal \(u\) and \(d\) constituent masses leads to large errors in the phase diagram within \(\mu_B \in (500, 900)\) MeV and shifts the position of the critical end point.

## Method

The authors employed a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, including scalar-isoscalar, vector-isoscalar, scalar-isovector, vector-isovector, and Kobayashi-Maskawa-t' Hooft (KMT) interactions. They adjusted the scalar-isovector coupling constant \(R_{IS} = G_{IS}/G_S = -0.002\) to reproduce the physical pion mass \(m_\pi = 140.9\) MeV and the vector-isovector coupling constant \(R_{IV} = G_{IV}/G_S = 0.25\) to fit lattice QCD results for the isospin density in baryon-free quark matter. The thermodynamic potential and gap equations for the chiral condensates, net-quark densities, and pion condensate were solved self-consistently without imposing the assumption \(M_u = M_d\), allowing the exploration of the full 3-dimensional phase diagram in the space of temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), and isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I\).

## Key numbers

* The scalar-isovector coupling constant was set to \(R_{IS} = -0.002\) to fit the physical pion mass to \(m_\pi = 140.9\) MeV.
* The vector-isovector coupling constant was set to \(R_{IV} = 0.25\) to reproduce lattice QCD results for the isospin density in baryon-free quark matter.
* The cutoff in the momentum integral was \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV/c, with current quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 3.6\) MeV and \(m_s = 87\) MeV.
* The reduced energy density \(\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{SB}\) as a function of \(\mu_I/m_\pi\) in cold baryon-free quark matter shows a peak at \(\mu_I/m_\pi \sim 1.3\), consistent with lattice QCD results.
* The assumption of equal \(u\) and \(d\) constituent masses leads to large errors in the QCD phase diagram within \(\mu_B \in (500, 900)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite isospin chemical potential, which were often limited to baryon-free conditions or the 2-flavor NJL model (e.g., He et al. (2005), Xia et al. (2013), Lop et al. (2021), Cao et al. (2021)). By performing rigorous calculations without the common approximation \(M_u = M_d\), the authors reveal the existence of a Sarma phase (unstable pion condensate solution) at finite baryon chemical potential and quantify the errors introduced by that approximation. The results enable more accurate predictions for the 3-dimensional QCD phase diagram, which is relevant for interpreting experiments at RHIC-BES, FAIR-CBM, NICA, and HIAF, and for understanding the role of isovector interactions in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions and compact star properties.
