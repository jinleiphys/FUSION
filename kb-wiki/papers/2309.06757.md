# Novel relations for twist-3 tensor-polarized fragmentation functions in spin-1 hadrons

> There are three types of fragmentation functions (FFs) which are used to describe the twist-3 cross sections of the hard semi-inclusive processes under QCD collinear factorization, and they are called intrinsic, kinematical, and dynamical FFs. In this work, we investigate the theoretical relations among these FFs for a tensor-polarized spin-1 hadron. Three Lorentz-invariance relations are derived by using the identities between the nonlocal quark-quark and quark-gluon-quark operators, which guarantee the frame independence of the twist-3 spin observables. The QCD equation of motion relations are also presented for the tensor-polarized FFs. In addition, we show that the intrinsic and kinematical twist-3 FFs can be decomposed into the contributions of twist-2 FFs and twist-3 three-parton FFs, and the latter are also called dynamical FFs. If one neglects the dynamical FFs, we can obtain relations which are analogous to the Wandzura-Wilczek relation. Then, the intrinsic and kinematical twist-3 FFs are expressed in terms of the leading-twist ones. Since the FFs of a spin-1 hadron can be measured at various experimental facilities in the near future, these theoretical relations will play an important role in the analysis of the collinear tensor-polarized FFs.

## Key claim

The paper derives three Lorentz-invariance relations (LIRs) and QCD equation-of-motion (e.o.m.) relations for twist-3 tensor-polarized fragmentation functions (FFs) in spin-1 hadrons, showing that intrinsic and kinematical twist-3 FFs can be decomposed into twist-2 FFs and twist-3 three-parton (dynamical) FFs, with Wandzura-Wilczek-type relations obtained if the dynamical FFs are neglected.

## Method

The author defines three classes of twist-3 tensor-polarized FFs (intrinsic, kinematical, and dynamical) for spin-1 hadrons using the collinear fragmentation correlator, the k_T-weighted TMD fragmentation correlator, and the three-parton fragmentation correlator. QCD e.o.m. relations are derived from the Dirac equation for quarks, and LIRs are obtained by taking derivatives of nonlocal quark-quark operator identities and matching them to quark-gluon-quark operator matrix elements in the lightcone limit.

## Key numbers

- The violation of the Wandzura-Wilczek relation for spin-1/2 PDFs can be as large as 15%-40% of the size of g_2 (from prior work cited as Accardi (2009))
- The support region for the three-parton fragmentation correlator is 0 ≤ z ≤ 1 and 0 < z/z_1 < 1
- The intrinsic twist-3 FF F_{LT}(z) is expressed in terms of the twist-2 FF F_{1LL}(z) and dynamical FFs via Eq. (ww1a), with the WW-type relation obtained by dropping dynamical FF contributions
- The kinematical twist-3 FF H_{1LL}^{(1)}(z) is expressed in terms of the twist-2 FF H_{1LT}(z) and the dynamical FF via Eq. (ww2b)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Lorentz-invariance relations and Wandzura-Wilczek-type relations, previously studied for spin-1/2 hadron PDFs and FFs by Kanazawa (2015) and Koike (2019), and for tensor-polarized PDFs by Kumano (2021), to the case of tensor-polarized fragmentation functions for spin-1 hadrons. The derived relations will provide constraints for future experimental measurements of tensor-polarized FFs at facilities such as BESIII and Belle II, where measurements of FFs for phi mesons are already in progress.
