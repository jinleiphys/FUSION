# Electroproduction of the Roper resonance and the constituent quark model

> A parameter-free evaluation of the $N - P_{11}(1440)$ electromagnetic transition form factors is performed within a light-front constituent quark model, using for the first time the eigenfunctions of a mass operator which generates a large amount of configuration mixing in baryon wave functions. A one-body electromagnetic current, which includes the phenomenological constituent quark form factors already determined from an analysis of pion and nucleon experimental data, is adopted. For $Q^2$ up to few $(GeV/c)^2$, at variance with the enhancement found in the elastic channel, the effect of configuration mixing results in a significant suppression of the calculated helicity amplitudes with respect to both relativistic and non-relativistic calculations, based on a simple gaussian-like ansatz for the baryon wave functions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that configuration mixing from the one-gluon-exchange interaction suppresses the calculated helicity amplitudes for the N-P11(1440) transition, at variance with the enhancement found in the elastic channel, for Q^2 up to a few (GeV/c)^2. This suppression brings the predictions closer to phenomenological analyses, but the authors note that those analyses may contain significant model-dependent uncertainties.

## Method
The authors solved the Capstick-Isgur mass operator using a Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle with a harmonic oscillator basis up to 20 quanta, obtaining eigenfunctions for the nucleon and Roper resonance. They then computed the electromagnetic transition form factors within the light-front formalism using a one-body current that includes phenomenological constituent quark form factors previously determined from pion and nucleon data. The helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} were evaluated for both proton and neutron targets.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon wave function composition: P_S^N = 98.1%, P_{S'}^N = 1.7%, P_D^N = 0.2%
- Roper resonance wave function composition: P_S^{Roper} = 90.6%, P_{S'}^{Roper} = 9.3%, P_D^{Roper} = 0.1%
- Constituent quark mass: m_u = m_d = 0.220 GeV
- For Q^2 ~ 1-4 (GeV/c)^2, the magnetic form factor G_M^{*p}(Q^2) is suppressed by a factor ~3-4 relative to the gaussian-based prediction of Capstick and Keister (1995)
- At the photon point, the ratio A_{1/2}^n / A_{1/2}^p is approximately -4/3 with S' components and approximately -1.1 without S' components
- For Q^2 ~ 0.2-0.6 (GeV/c)^2, the transverse helicity amplitudes change sign

## Context
This work extends the light-front constituent quark model previously applied to nucleon form factors and the N-Delta(1232) transition by Cardarelli, Pace, Salme, and Simula (1995-1996). It contradicts the simple gaussian-like wave function approach used by Capstick and Keister (1995) and by Weber (1990), showing that configuration mixing from the one-gluon-exchange potential of Capstick and Isgur (1986) significantly alters predictions for the Roper resonance. The results enable comparison with planned experiments at TJNAF and suggest that hybrid q^3G model calculations, such as those by Barnes and Close (1983) and others, must include both relativistic effects and configuration mixing before meaningful comparison with the constituent quark picture can be made.
