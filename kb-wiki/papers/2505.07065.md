# $ϕ$ meson properties in dense resonance matter at finite temperature

> The effective mass and decay width of the $ϕ$ meson in the isospin asymmetric hot and dense resonance matter are studied using the effective Lagrangian framework considering the $ϕK \bar K $ interactions at one-loop level. In addition to spin$-1/2$ octet baryons, we consider the effect of resonances $Δ^{++,+,0,-}, Σ^{*\pm,0},Ξ^{*0,-}, Ω^{-}$, on the properties of $ϕ$ meson. The in-medium effects on the $ϕ$ meson properties are simulated through the effective masses of kaons and antikaons computed using the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean field model in the presence of resonance baryons. The loop integral appearing in the computation of $ϕ$ meson self energies is regularized using the dipole form factor with a cutoff parameter. The presence of resonance baryons within the medium at finite temperature is observed to significantly modify the effective mass and decay width of $ϕ$ mesons. Examining the $ϕ$ meson masses and decay width within a dense medium is anticipated to be essential for understanding experimental results from heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The effective mass and decay width of the phi meson in isospin asymmetric hot and dense matter are significantly modified by the inclusion of resonance baryons (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega) beyond the standard octet baryons, with the modification depending on the baryon density, temperature, isospin asymmetry, and strangeness fraction of the medium.

## Method

The authors use the chiral SU(3) hadronic mean field model to compute the in-medium scalar and vector fields and the effective masses of kaons and antikaons in dense resonance matter containing octet baryons and decuplet resonances (Delta++,+,0,-, Sigma*+-,0, Xi*0,-, Omega-). The phi meson self-energy is then calculated at one-loop level from the phi K anti-K interaction Lagrangian, with the loop integral regularized using a dipole form factor with a cutoff parameter, and the in-medium phi meson mass and decay width are obtained from the self-energy.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation density is taken as rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3, with binding energy B/A = -16 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
* The spin degeneracy factor is 2 for octet baryons and 4 for decuplet baryons.
* The isospin asymmetry I_a is defined as -sum_i I_3i rho_i^v / rho_B, and the strangeness fraction f_s as sum_i |S_i| rho_i^v / rho_B.
* The effective baryon mass is given by m_i* = -(g_sigma_i sigma + g_zeta_i zeta + g_delta_i tau_3i delta) + m_i0, where tau_3i takes values +1, -1, 0, +3, or -3 depending on the baryon species.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson properties in nuclear matter using the chiral SU(3) model (Kumar 2020, Chahal 2024) by incorporating decuplet resonance baryons (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega) in addition to the standard octet baryons. It builds on earlier theoretical approaches such as QCD sum rules (Hatsuda 1992, Asakawa 1994), effective Lagrangian methods (Klingl 1997, Oset 2001), and coupled-channel approaches (Ramos 2000, Lutz 1998) that examined phi meson modifications in dense matter. The results are relevant for interpreting experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and upcoming FAIR experiments, and for understanding the possible formation of phi-nucleus bound states.
