# Identified charged hadron production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 54.4 GeV with the STAR detector

> We present results on the production of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 54.4~GeV using the STAR detector at RHIC, at midrapidity ($|y| <$ 0.1). Invariant yields of these particles as a function of transverse momentum are shown. We determine bulk properties such as integrated particle yields ($dN/dy$), mean transverse momentum ($\langle p_{T} \rangle$), particle ratios, which provide insight into the particle production mechanisms. Additionally, the kinetic freezeout parameters ($T_\text{kin}$ and $\langle β_{T} \rangle$), which provide information about the dynamics of the system at the time of freezeout, are obtained. The Bjorken energy density ($ε_{\rm{BJ}}$), which gives an estimate of the energy density in the central rapidity region of the collision zone at the formation time $τ$, is calculated and presented as a function of multiplicity for various energies. The results are compared with those from the models such as A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) and Heavy Ion Jet INteraction Generator (HIJING) for further insights.

## Key claim

The paper presents identified charged hadron production in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 54.4 GeV, but the results are limited to midrapidity (\(|y| < 0.1\)) and do not include strange hadron yields, which are noted as unavailable for this energy; the kinetic freezeout parameters are extracted using a blast-wave model fit that assumes the same functional form and fit ranges as used in prior STAR analyses at lower energies.

## Method

The STAR detector's Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time-of-Flight (TOF) detector were used to identify \(\pi^{\pm}\), \(K^{\pm}\), \(p\), and \(\bar{p}\) via ionization energy loss (\(dE/dx\)) and mass-squared distributions. Raw yields were extracted using \(z\)-variable distributions from Bichsel energy-loss functions and TOF mass-squared fits, then corrected for tracking efficiency, acceptance, and energy loss using Monte Carlo simulations (GEANT3) and data-driven techniques. The corrected transverse momentum spectra were fitted with Bose-Einstein (pions), Levy-Tsallis (kaons), and double-exponential (protons/antiprotons) functions to obtain integrated yields (\(dN/dy\)) and mean transverse momentum (\(\langle p_T \rangle\)), and the blast-wave model was used to extract kinetic freezeout temperature (\(T_\text{kin}\)) and average radial flow velocity (\(\langle \beta_T \rangle\)).

## Key numbers

- Approximately 500 \(\times\) 10\(^6\) minimum-bias events were analyzed after event selection cuts.
- The total systematic uncertainties on particle yields are 9–10% for \(\pi^{\pm}\), 8–10% for \(K^{\pm}\), and 9–11% for \(p\) and \(\bar{p}\).
- For the most central (0–5%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 54.4 GeV, the antiparticle-to-particle ratios are: \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) approximately 1, \(K^{-}/K^{+}\) close to 0.84, and \(\bar{p}/p\) approximately 0.40.
- The percentage of extrapolation in the unmeasured \(p_T\) regions was 36–40% for pions, 15–20% for kaons, and 16–29% for protons and antiprotons across centralities.
- The power-law fit parameter \(p_1\) for \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{BJ}} \times \tau\) versus \(\langle N_{\mathrm{part}} \rangle\) at 54.4 GeV is 0.48 (0.04), consistent with values at other energies (e.g., 0.49 at 200 GeV, 0.52 at 5020 GeV).
- The Bjorken energy density times formation time (\(\epsilon_{\mathrm{BJ}} \times \tau\)) at 54.4 GeV exceeds the lattice QCD phase transition value of 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) for all centralities when using formation times from the literature.

## Context

This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan (BES) program results by providing identified hadron spectra at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\) = 54.4 GeV, a previously unreported energy between the BES-I range (7.7–39 GeV) and the top RHIC energy (200 GeV). The results are compared with published STAR data from BES (2014) and 200 GeV (STAR:2008med), as well as world data from AGS, SPS, and LHC experiments. The measured particle yields, ratios, and freezeout parameters enable further testing of models such as AMPT and HIJING, and the observed scaling of \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{BJ}} \times \tau\) with \(\langle N_{\mathrm{part}} \rangle\) across a wide energy range (7.7–5020 GeV) suggests a universal initial energy density distribution, which can inform future studies of the QCD phase diagram and the search for the critical point.
