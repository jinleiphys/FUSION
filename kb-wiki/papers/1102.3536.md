# More kaonic bound states and a comprehensive interpretation of the D_{sJ} states

> The leading order interaction between a Goldstone boson and a matter field is universally dominated by the Weinberg-Tomozawa term. Based on this observation, we predict a rich spectrum of bound states of a kaon and a heavy meson. We argue that if the lifetime of an excited heavy meson is significantly longer than the range of forces, then the finite width of that state can be neglected in a first approximation. Then, the D_{s0}^*(2317), D_{s1}(2460), D_{sJ}(2860) and D_{sJ}(3040) are generated as DK, D^*K, D_1(2420)K and D^*(2600)K bound states, respectively. In addition to the remarkable agreement with the measured masses, the decay patterns of the D_{sJ}(2860) and D_{sJ}(3040) can also be understood. Two more D_{sJ} states, and kaonic bound states with the bottom mesons as well as the doubly charmed baryon are also predicted.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the \(D_{s0}^{*}(2317)\), \(D_{s1}(2460)\), \(D_{sJ}(2860)\), and \(D_{sJ}(3040)\) can all be interpreted as kaonic bound states of specific heavy mesons, provided the finite width of the excited heavy meson constituent is much smaller than the range of forces (set by \(M_{\rho}\) or \(2M_{\pi}\)), so that the width can be neglected in a first approximation.

## Method

The leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction between a kaon (Goldstone boson) and a heavy matter field is used to compute \(S\)-wave \(I=0\) scattering amplitudes via a resummed amplitude \(T(s) = V(s)[1-G(s)V(s)]^{-1}\), where \(V(s)\) is the potential and \(G(s)\) is a loop function for a nonrelativistic heavy meson and a relativistic light meson. The subtraction constant \(a(\mu)\) in the loop function is fixed by reproducing the mass of the \(D_{s0}^{*}(2317)\), and the same constant is used for all heavy hadrons. Bound-state masses are found by searching for poles in the amplitude, and decay patterns are analyzed using heavy quark spin symmetry and heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

*   \(D_{s1}(2460)\) predicted mass: \(2458 \pm 3\) MeV (experimental: \(2459.5 \pm 0.6\) MeV).
*   \(D_{sJ}(2860)\) predicted mass: \(2870 \pm 9\) MeV (experimental: \(2862 \pm 2^{+5}_{-2}\) MeV).
*   \(D_{sJ}(3040)\) predicted mass: \(3052 \pm 11\) MeV (experimental: \(3044 \pm 8^{+30}_{-5}\) MeV).
*   Ratio of decay widths for \(D_{sJ}(2860)\): \(R_{D_{sJ}(2860)} = \Gamma(D_{sJ}(2860) \to D^{*}K) / \Gamma(D_{sJ}(2860) \to DK) = 1.23\) (predicted), compared to the measured value \(1.10 \pm 0.24\).
*   Predicted mass of \(\bar{B}K\) bound state: \(5705 \pm 31\) MeV.
*   Predicted mass of \(\bar{\Xi}_{cc}(3520)K\) bound state: \(3956 \pm 20\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the \(D_{s0}^{*}(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) as \(DK\) and \(D^{*}K\) bound states, previously studied by Kolomeitsev and Lutz (2003), Guo et al. (2006, 2008, 2009), Gamermann et al. (2006), and Cleven et al. (2010), to the higher-mass \(D_{sJ}(2860)\) and \(D_{sJ}(3040)\) states. It contradicts a simple \(c\bar{s}\) quark-model assignment for these states, as the \(D_{sJ}(2860)\) mass and decay ratio are inconsistent with a \((2S, 1^{-})\) assignment (already taken by the \(D_{s1}^{*}(2700)\)) and a \((2P, 2^{+})\) assignment would predict a mass near 3.1 GeV. The results enable predictions of new kaonic bound states in the bottom sector and with doubly charmed baryons, and suggest that the decay \(D_{sJ}(3040) \to D_s \omega\) can distinguish the molecular interpretation from a \(c\bar{s}\) picture.
