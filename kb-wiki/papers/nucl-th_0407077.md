# Relativistic nature of the EMC effect

> The deep inelastic scattering of leptons off nuclei is considered within the the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. It is shown that nuclear short-range structure can be expressed in terms of the nucleon structure functions and four-dimensional Fermi motion of the nucleons. The four-dimensional Fermi motion broadens the bound nucleon localization area, what lead to the observation of the nucleon structure change in nuclei -- EMC effect. The $\rm ^4He$ to deuteron structure functions ratio is found in good agreement with experimental data. It is shown that the pattern of the ratio is defined by dynamical properties of the nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The EMC effect arises not from an actual change in the bound nucleon's internal partonic structure, but from the four-dimensional Fermi motion of nucleons inside the nucleus, which broadens the bound nucleon localization area and introduces a measurement uncertainty that mimics a structure change.

## Method

The nuclear deep inelastic scattering amplitude was derived within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, expressing the nuclear hadron tensor as an integral over the four-momentum of the struck nucleon. By contour integration around the struck nucleon pole, the nuclear structure function was rewritten in terms of the on-shell nucleon structure function and its derivative, with the energy shift parameter Δ^N_A characterizing the contribution from the time-like component of the Fermi motion (the τ-shift). Numerical calculations for ^4He used nuclear momentum distributions from Ciofi degli Atti and nucleon structure functions from the parameterization of Plett and collaborators.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R^4He = 2σ^4He/(Aσ^D) = F2^4He/F2^D falls below unity in the middle x region, with the full calculation (sum of 3D Fermi motion and τ-shift contributions) agreeing with experimental data within error bars.
- The τ-shift contribution to the nuclear structure function is proportional to Δ^N_A = M_A - E_N - E_{A-1}, where the mean value ⟨Δ^N_A⟩ = ε_A - ⟨T⟩_A · A/(A-1), with ε_A the binding energy per nucleon and ⟨T⟩_A the mean kinetic energy.
- The accuracy of the nuclear structure function expression is up to terms of order (Δ^N_A/M_A)^2 ∝ O(10^{-4}).
- The mean kinetic energy of bound nucleons follows ⟨T⟩_A ≈ ⟨T_c⟩ (1 - 0.98 N_s(A)/A), where N_s(A) is the number of surface nucleons and ⟨T_c⟩ is the mean kinetic energy in the central part of the nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the binding model (which uses the nucleon mass shift m → m* = m - ε) and the Q^2-rescaling model (which posits a change in quark confinement radius) by showing that both can be understood as consequences of four-dimensional Fermi motion rather than actual nucleon structure modification. It contradicts the conclusion of quasi-potential approaches (reviewed by various authors) that the τ-shift is unphysical and unobservable. The result enables a unified explanation of the EMC effect, antishadowing, and shadowing across all x regions, and provides a direct link between sub-nucleon structure dynamics and space-time geometry at microscopic distances.
