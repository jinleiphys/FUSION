# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations in Electromagnetically Induced Knockout Reactions

> The attempts to investigate correlations in electromagnetically induced one- and two-nucleon knockout are reviewed. The theoretical framework for cross section calculations is outlined and some results are presented for the exclusive $^{16}$O($e,e'p)^{15}$N and $^{16}$O($e,e'pp)^{14}$C reactions. For the ($e,e'p$) reaction attention is focussed on extracting the spectroscopic factors. For the ($e,e'pp$) reaction the possibility of obtaining direct and clear information on short-range correlations is discussed.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations in Electromagnetically Induced Knockout Reactions

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) can be directly and clearly investigated through two-nucleon knockout reactions like $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C, but only in specific final states and kinematics where the one-body knockout mechanism dominates over two-body meson-exchange currents; the $0^+$ ground state transition in $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C is particularly suited because the $^1S_0$ $pp$ knockout channel suppresses the competing isobar current contribution.

## Method

The theoretical framework uses a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) for one-nucleon knockout and an analogous model for two-nucleon knockout, where transition matrix elements of the nuclear current operator are computed between initial and final nuclear states. For ($e,e'p$) reactions, the overlap functions and spectroscopic factors are extracted from one-body density matrices calculated within various correlation methods (Jastrow, Correlated Basis Function, Green's Function Method, Generator Coordinate Method) and compared to NIKHEF data using the DWEEPY code. For ($e,e'pp$) reactions, two-nucleon overlap functions are constructed from a two-step procedure treating long-range correlations in a shell-model space and short-range correlations via defect functions from a G-matrix, with final-state interactions described by phenomenological optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors extracted from DWIA analyses of ($e,e'p$) data indicate removal of single-particle strength for quasi-hole states near the Fermi energy is about 60-70%
- In RDWIA calculations for $^{16}$O($e,e'p$), the reduction factor applied to reproduce experimental cross sections is 0.7 for RDWIA and 0.65 for DWIA for both transitions
- Relativistic DWIA is necessary above 300 MeV outgoing proton energy; at JLab kinematics with $Q^2 = 0.8$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and $T'_1 = 433$ MeV, the RDWIA model describes data with the same spectroscopic factor of 0.7
- For the $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C reaction, cross sections are presented for transitions to the $0^+$ ground state and the $1^+$ state at 11.31 MeV at two kinematical settings from NIKHEF and MAMI experiments
- The $^{16}$O($\gamma,p$)$^{15}$N$_{\text{g.s.}}$ reaction calculations are shown at $E_\gamma = 60$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the long-standing effort to extract nuclear correlation information from electromagnetic knockout reactions, building on the extensive ($e,e'p$) program at NIKHEF (Oxford, 1990s) and the first SRC evidence from $^{16}$O($e,e'pp$)$^{14}$C experiments (Ronald, 1990s). The work contrasts with earlier mean-field and independent-particle shell model descriptions that fail to account for correlation effects, and it directly compares multiple correlation methods including Jastrow (Stoitsov, 1996), Correlated Basis Function (Vignati, 1997; Salcedo, 1996), Green's Function Method (Pollock, 1996), and Generator Coordinate Method (Christensen, 1986; Ivanov, 2000) for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca. The results enable future combined studies of $pp$ and $np$ knockout with real and virtual photons to disentangle short-range correlations from tensor correlations and two-body meson-exchange currents, with the goal of distinguishing different models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction at short distance.
