# An effective field theory for warm QCD

> Using only global symmetries of QCD, we set up an effective model of quarks at finite temperature near the cross over, including all possible terms up to dimension 6. We first treat this in mean field theory. Then we investigate low-energy fluctuations around it up to one-loop order in fermions below the cross over. Static correlation functions of pions and the cross over temperature, both measured on the lattice, completely suffice to fix all parameters of the theory. We examine predictions of this theory, including those for thermodynamic quantities. The results are encouraging.

## Key claim

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) for QCD near the finite-temperature crossover using only global symmetries, with all parameters fixed by lattice measurements of static pion correlation functions and the crossover temperature; the hedge is that the model is treated in mean field theory with one-loop fermionic fluctuations, and the results are "encouraging" rather than definitive, with the caveat that lattice inputs have not been corrected for power-law lattice spacing artifacts.

## Method

The authors build a Euclidean EFT of quarks at finite temperature, including all operators up to mass dimension six that are invariant under SU_V(N_f) × SU_A(N_f) chiral symmetry and the O(3)×Z_2 symmetry of finite-temperature Euclidean space. They treat the model first in mean field theory (MFT), then integrate out fermions at one-loop order to obtain an effective pion action, and fix the three free parameters (d^3, d^4, and the critical temperature T_0) by fitting to lattice data for the pion screening mass M_π, the coefficient c^4, and the crossover temperature T_co from Brandt et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature from lattice set C1: T_co = 211 ± 5 MeV (Brandt et al. 2017)
- Input temperature for fits: T/T_co = 0.84 ± 0.02, corresponding to T = 177 ± 2 MeV
- Best-fit ratio T_co/T_0 = 1.24 ± 0.03, yielding T_0 = 170 ± 6 MeV (the critical temperature in the chiral limit)
- Mean field prediction for the curvature of the critical line in the chiral limit: T_c(0)κ = 3/π² (parameter-free)
- Lattice estimates for the curvature parameter: T_c(0)κ ≈ 0.01–0.05 (from prior lattice work cited as "kappa")
- The coefficient c^4 vanishes at T_c in the chiral limit, with leading behavior c^4 ∝ (1 − T/T_c) log(1 − T/T_c)

## Context

This work extends the approach of effective field theories for QCD at very low temperatures (Weinberg 1979, chiral perturbation theory) and at very high temperatures (Karsch 1989, weak-coupling expansion), which break down near the crossover region where g ∼ 1. The model differs from the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio 1961) by including all symmetry-allowed dimension-6 terms and using a temperature scale T_0 to organize the expansion, rather than assuming Lorentz invariance. The results enable predictions for thermodynamic quantities and other low-energy constants (such as f_π and the quartic coupling c^{41}) that can be tested against future lattice computations with improved continuum extrapolation.
