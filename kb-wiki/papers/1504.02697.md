# Chiral Mirror-Baryon-Meson Model and Nuclear Matter beyond Mean-Field

> We consider a chiral baryon-meson model for nucleons and their parity partners in mirror assignment interacting with pions, sigma and omega mesons to describe the liquid-gas transition of nuclear matter together with chiral symmetry restoration in the high density phase. Within the mean-field approximation the model is known to provide a phenomenologically successful description of the nuclear-matter transition. Here, we go beyond this approximation and include mesonic fluctuations by means of the functional renormalization group. While these fluctuations do not lead to major qualitative changes in the phase diagram of the model, beyond mean-field, one is no-longer free to adjust the parameters so as to reproduce the binding energy per nucleon, the nuclear saturation density, and the nucleon sigma term all at the same time. However, the prediction of a clear first-order chiral transition at low temperatures inside the high baryon-density phase appears to be robust.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including mesonic fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG) does not qualitatively change the phase diagram of the chiral mirror-baryon-meson model, but it removes the freedom to simultaneously reproduce the binding energy per nucleon, nuclear saturation density, and nucleon sigma term that was available in mean-field treatments.

## Method

The authors construct a parity-doublet model with two baryon species (nucleons and their parity partners) in mirror assignment, coupled to pions, sigma mesons, and omega mesons. They go beyond the mean-field approximation by including mesonic fluctuations using the functional renormalization group in the local potential approximation (LPA), while treating the omega meson at mean-field level due to its large mass (782 MeV). The flow equations for the effective potential are solved starting from a UV cutoff scale of 1 GeV, with parameters fixed to reproduce vacuum properties including the pion mass (138 MeV), pion decay constant (93 MeV), and nucleon mass (939 MeV) together with its parity partner mass (1535 MeV).

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density reproduced at n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 with binding energy Eb = 16 MeV per nucleon and nucleon sigma term sigma_N = 36 MeV in extended mean-field (eMF) with m0 = 820 MeV and vector coupling hv = 4.10
* In-medium chiral condensate at saturation density: sigma(n0) = 69 MeV
* First-order chiral transition at low temperatures inside the high baryon-density phase occurs at mu_B = 1472 MeV, corresponding to a density of approximately 13.7 times saturation density
* Baryon masses in the chirally symmetric phase (sigma = 0) become degenerate at m0 = 820 MeV

## Context

This work extends parity-doublet models previously studied at mean-field level by authors such as Jido (2001), Sasaki (2010, 2011), and Gallas (2009, 2011) by including mesonic fluctuations through the FRG. The approach contrasts with the chiral Walecka model, which produces massless baryons in the chirally restored phase, whereas the mirror assignment ensures a finite nucleon mass (m0) even when chiral symmetry is restored. The results enable more reliable predictions for the nuclear matter equation of state at high densities relevant for neutron star physics, though the authors note that the LPA introduces a Silver-Blaze problem that could be resolved by including wave-function renormalization beyond the LPA.
