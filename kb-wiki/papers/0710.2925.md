# Bridging over p-wave pi-production and weak processes in few-nucleon systems with chiral perturbation theory

> We focus on a powerful aspect of chiral perturbation theory (chi PT) which provides us with a ``bridge'' over different reactions. For this purpose, we study a contact operator which contributes p-wave pi-production and several weak processes. We fix the unknown coupling of the contact operator using a matrix element of a low-energy weak process (pp\to de^+ν_e), and then calculate the partial wave amplitude (a_0) for the p-wave pi-production (pp\to pnπ^+). We find that the chiral operator including the contact term does not reproduce a_0 extracted from data, showing that the bridging over reactions with significantly different kinematics is not necessarily successful. We argue the importance of a specific higher order calculation. In order to gain an insight into a higher order calculation, we consider a higher order counter term, and find that the energy dependence of a_0 is then consistent with the data.

# Bridging over p-wave pi-production and weak processes in few-nucleon systems with chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The chiral perturbation theory bridging program, fixing the low-energy constant (LEC) of a contact operator using a low-energy weak process and then predicting a strong reaction, fails for the p-wave pion production amplitude \(a_0\) in \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) when only next-to-leading order (NLO) operators are used, showing that bridging over reactions with significantly different kinematics is not necessarily successful. However, including a higher-order counter term (\(\tilde{e}\)) and fitting both LECs to data from both reactions yields an energy dependence of \(a_0\) consistent with experiment.

## Method

The author fixed the unknown coupling \(\tilde{d}\) of the contact operator \(\mathcal{L} = \tilde{d} N^\dagger S \cdot u N N^\dagger N\) by fitting the Gamow-Teller matrix element for the weak process \(pp \to de^+\nu_e\) to a benchmark calculation based on the tritium \(\beta\)-decay rate from Park et al. (2003). Using this \(\tilde{d}\) value, the partial wave amplitude \(a_0\) for the p-wave pion production reaction \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) was then calculated with a chiral NLO operator that included one-body direct production, \(\Delta\)-excitation via pion exchange or contact interaction, recoil corrections, pion rescattering through \(c_3\) and \(c_4\) vertices or the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, and the \(\tilde{d}\) contact term. In a second step, a higher-order counter term \(\mathcal{L}_{\rm CT}^{(2)} = \tilde{e} N^{\dagger}{\boldsymbol\tau}\vec{\sigma}\cdot\vec{\nabla}{\boldsymbol\pi} N\, (N^{\dagger} \vec{\nabla}^2N + {\rm h.c.})\) was added, and both \(\tilde{d}\) and \(\tilde{e}\) were fitted to reproduce the benchmark weak-process matrix element and the experimental \(a_0\) at \(\eta = 0.5\).

## Key numbers

- The partial wave amplitude \(a_0\) for \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) was calculated as a function of \(\eta \equiv q_\pi^{\rm max}/m_\pi\), where \(q_\pi^{\rm max}\) is the maximum pion momentum; the NLO prediction with \(\tilde{d}\) fixed from the weak process disagreed with data from Flammang et al. (1998).
- The inclusion of the \(\tilde{d}\) term made the disagreement worse than the case with \(\tilde{d} = 0\); even the sign of \(\tilde{d}\) fixed by the low-energy weak process was inconsistent with the data.
- With the extended operator including the \(\tilde{e}\) term, \(a_0\) was fitted to the central value of the data at \(\eta = 0.5\), and the \(\eta\) dependence of \(a_0\) was then consistent with the data for \(\Lambda = 500\), 600, and 800 MeV and \(h_A = 2.10\).
- The CD-Bonn \(NN\) potential was used; cutoff values \(\Lambda = 500\), 600, and 800 MeV and \(\pi N\Delta\) couplings \(h_A = 2.10\) and 2.68 were tested.

## Context

This work tests the chiral perturbation theory bridging program previously applied by Park et al. (2003), who fixed \(\tilde{d}\) using tritium \(\beta\)-decay and predicted weak proton capture, and by Hanhart et al. (2001), who showed that \(\tilde{d}\) fixed by three-nucleon observables reproduced the p-wave pion production amplitude. The present study finds that bridging between the weak process \(pp \to de^+\nu_e\) and the strong reaction \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) fails at NLO due to the significantly different kinematics of the two reactions, and argues that higher-order terms (such as the \(\tilde{e}\) counter term) and data from both energy regions are necessary for accurate predictions. This work enables future systematic studies of higher-order chiral operators for few-nucleon reactions spanning different energy scales.
