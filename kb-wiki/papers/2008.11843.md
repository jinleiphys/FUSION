# Learning about the QCD medium using electromagnetic and weak probes

> Recent theoretical developments concerning radiation of electromagnetic and weak probes in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is overviewed. These proceedings focus on electromagnetic probes and briefly cover weak probes. An outlook regarding the future use of electromagnetic probes is formulated whereby a quantitative Bayesian comparison, simultaneously employing electromagnetic and hadronic calculations of experimental observables against data, is highlighted as a path towards a better understanding of the properties of the QCD medium.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic and weak probes can separate initial-state information from later dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) due to their small coupling, but the paper hedges that a quantitative Bayesian comparison simultaneously employing electromagnetic and hadronic calculations against data is needed as a path toward better understanding of QCD medium properties, rather than claiming such understanding has been achieved.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments for electromagnetic (real photons and dileptons) and weak (W± and Z0) probe production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It describes in-equilibrium electromagnetic rates calculated using perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order and non-perturbative lattice QCD, as well as hadronic rates from many-body effective Lagrangians with vector meson dominance. Dissipative corrections to these rates are obtained via Chapman-Enskog/14-moment approximations applied to Boltzmann kinetic theory, and the resulting rates are used in dynamical models (hydrodynamics, effective kinetic theory, hadronic transport) compared against experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The low invariant mass region for dileptons is defined as M ≲ 1 GeV, dominated by hadronic medium radiation from in-medium decays of ρ, ω, and φ vector mesons.
- The intermediate invariant mass region for dileptons is defined as 1 ≲ M ≲ 2.5 GeV, where radiation directly from the QGP is an important source.
- Using hadronic Lagrangians, the ρ and a1 spectral functions start to overlap at temperature T = 170 MeV, indicating chiral symmetry restoration.
- Using the functional renormalization group (FRG), the ρ and a1 spectral functions overlap at T = 300 MeV, though this calculation lacks baryonic degrees of freedom.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for QCD is T = 156 ± 1.5 MeV (Bazavov 2018).
- Direct photon yield and v2 are shown for √sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC within 20-40% centrality (Gale 2020).
- Dilepton v2(M) within 20-40% centrality at top RHIC collision energy is shown, including sources from hadronic medium and QGP (Vujanovic 2017).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of the QGP that includes perturbative QCD calculations by Ghiglieri (2013, 2016), Laine (2013), and Ghisoiu (2014), as well as lattice QCD results by Brandt (2017, 2019). It also builds on hadronic rate calculations by Rapp (1999, 2009), Turbide (2003), and Eletsky (2001). The paper enables future Bayesian model-to-data comparisons that simultaneously use electromagnetic and hadronic observables to better constrain transport coefficients such as specific shear viscosity η/s(T) and the momentum diffusion coefficient q̂, and to test models of chiral symmetry restoration against experimental data.
