# Quantum free-streaming: out of equilibrium expansion for the free scalar fields

> The collisionless Boltzmann equation, also called free-streaming, is a convenient approximation. It is rather simple to implement numerically, and and it is effective at reducing the irregularities of rough initial conditions. It can be obtained as a small $\hbar$ limit from a free scalar quantum field. Namely, by neglecting the $\hbar^2$ term in the dynamical evolution of the Wigner distribution, the quantum precursor of the distribution function. In this work it is presented the general form for the exact solutions of the Wigner distribution, for a scalar field undergoing a $(1+1)$-dimensional expansion. Namely, with the symmetry constraint of rotation and translation invariance in the transverse plane. It is very different from the (on-shell) free-streaming of classical particles. It is shown how to recognize the classical, $\hbar\to 0$, limit from the general form. The numerical analysis for a specific example shows very large quantum corrections to the classical free-streaming.

## Key claim

The exact solutions of the Wigner distribution for a free scalar field undergoing a (1+1)-dimensional expansion (with rotation and translation invariance in the transverse plane) are very different from the classical on-shell free-streaming of particles, and the numerical analysis for a specific example shows very large quantum corrections to the classical free-streaming that do not vanish even in the long time limit.

## Method

The author derives the general form of exact solutions for the Wigner distribution of a free scalar field under the symmetry constraint of homogeneity in the transverse plane (ISO(2) invariance), without requiring boost invariance or local equilibrium initial conditions. The equations of motion for the Wigner distribution are separated into a real and imaginary part, yielding a Klein-Gordon-like equation and a transport equation, which are solved using the method of characteristics and Fourier decomposition. The semiclassical limit is then extracted by identifying a class of functions that produce a Dirac delta in the dimensionless variable chi when the Planck constant goes to zero, recovering the classical on-shell measure.

## Key numbers

- The Planck constant is given as hbar c approximately 200 MeV·fm, providing the context for why quantum corrections may be significant at initial stages of heavy-ion collisions where densities change over a small fraction of a fm.
- The general solution for the Wigner distribution in (1+1)-dimensional expansion contains trigonometric functions of the argument 2w*sqrt((k^2-m^2)/((k^0)^2-(k^z)^2)), where w = zk^0 - tk^z, with the prefactor sqrt(((k^0)^2-(k^z)^2)/(k^2-m^2)) for the sine term to ensure smooth transition when the radical becomes imaginary for space-like k^mu.
- The residual term W_d satisfies a Klein-Gordon equation with mass 2*sqrt(m^2 + k_T^2), where k_T is the transverse momentum modulus, and its general solution involves exponentials with argument (t*sqrt(4m_T^2 + xi^2) - z*xi)/hbar.

## Context

This work extends the exact (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken-symmetric solutions for scalar field expansion previously computed by Akkelin (2018, 2020) and Rindori (2021), which already showed non-trivial vacuum effects and significant deviations from classical free-streaming that do not vanish in the long time limit. The present paper relaxes the requirement of boost invariance and local equilibrium initial conditions, providing a novel class of exact solutions for the (1+1)-dimensional expansion. These results enable quantitative estimation of quantum corrections to the classical free-streaming approximation used to connect initial stages of heavy-ion collisions to the hydrodynamic phase, as employed by Kurkela (2018), Schlichting (2019), and others.
