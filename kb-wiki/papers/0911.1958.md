# New Charmonium States in QCD Sum Rules: a Concise Review

> In the past years there has been a revival of hadron spectroscopy. Many interesting new hadron states were discovered experimentally, some of which do not fit easily into the quark model. This situation motivated a vigorous theoretical activity. This is a rapidly evolving field with enormous amount of new experimental information. In the present report we include and discuss data which were released very recently. The present review is the first one written from the perspective of QCD sum rules (QCDSR), where we present the main steps of concrete calculations and compare the results with other approaches and with experimental data.

## Key claim
This review argues that QCD sum rules (QCDSR) can discriminate between tetraquark and molecular structures of newly discovered charmonium states by comparing the calculated mass from a chosen current with the physical mass; the key hedge is that the discrimination relies on the assumption that a current with maximum overlap with the physical state will produce a sum rule with better convergence and a mass closer to the experimental value, but the method cannot definitively rule out alternative configurations because the Fierz transformation shows that tetraquark and molecular currents are related by suppression factors.

## Method
The authors apply the QCD sum rule formalism to two-point correlation functions of hadronic currents with the quantum numbers of the observed states, expanding the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-eight condensates including the four-quark condensate and the mixed condensate. They construct both tetraquark (diquark-antidiquark) and molecular (meson-meson) currents for states such as X(3872) and Z+(4430), perform Borel transformations to suppress continuum contributions, and extract the mass from the ratio of moments of the spectral density. The Fierz transformation is used to relate tetraquark currents to sums of molecular-type currents, revealing suppression factors (e.g., 1/3 for the overlap of a tetraquark current with a molecular component) that are compared with the coupling strengths extracted from the sum rules.

## Key numbers
- X(3872) world average mass: M_X = (3871.20 ± 0.39) MeV; most precise measurement: M_X = (3871.61 ± 0.16 ± 0.19) MeV.
- X(3872) width: less than 2.3 MeV at 90% confidence level.
- Ratio of branching fractions for X(3872): Γ(X → J/ψ γ) / Γ(X → J/ψ π+π-) = 0.14 ± 0.05.
- For Z+(4430) with J^P = 0^-, the ratio of couplings λ_{Z0-}/λ_{D*D1} = 0.66, compared to the molecular suppression factor of 1/3 from Fierz transformation.
- Charm quark mass: m_c(m_c) = (1.23 ± 0.05) GeV; bottom quark mass: m_b(m_b) = (4.24 ± 0.06) GeV.
- Quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.23 ± 0.03)^3 GeV^3; gluon condensate: ⟨g^2 G^2⟩ = 0.88 GeV^4; mixed condensate parameter: m_0^2 = 0.8 GeV^2.

## Context
This review is the first comprehensive survey of new charmonium states written from the perspective of QCD sum rules, extending earlier work by Tornqvist (1994) who predicted molecular states near the D*0D0 threshold, and by Liu and Stancu who constructed molecular currents for the X(3872). The paper systematically applies the QCDSR method to states including X(3872), Y(1--) family, Z+(4430), Z1+(4050), Z2+(4250), Y(3930), Y(4140), X(3915), X(4350), X(3940), Z(3930), X(4160), and Y(4008), comparing results with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., predictions for the 2^3P1 charmonium state ranging from 3853 to 4067 MeV) and with experimental data. The work enables future studies to use the Fierz transformation analysis as a diagnostic tool for inferring the internal structure (tetraquark vs. molecular) of newly discovered exotic hadrons.
