# Alpha decay of nuclei interesting for synthesis of $Z=119, 120$ isotopes

> Four groups (even-even, even-odd, odd-even and odd-odd) of heavy and super-heavy nuclei are of interest for the synthesis of the isotopes with $Z=119, 120$. We report calculations of $α$~decay half-lives using four models: AKRA (Akrawy); ASAF (Analytical Super-Asymmetric Fission); UNIV (Universal Formula), and semFIS (Semi-empirical formula based on Fission Theory). We compare the experimental $Q_α$ values either with AME16 atomic mass evaluation (whenever available) and with the theoretical model WS4, able to give masses of not yet measured nuclides. For $^{92,94}$Sr cluster radioactivity of $^{300,302}$120 we predict a branching ratio relative to $α$~decay of -0.10 and 0.49, respectively, meaning that it is worth trying to detect such kind of decay modes in competition with $α$~decay.

# Alpha decay of nuclei interesting for synthesis of Z=119, 120 isotopes

## Key claim

The authors report that for the superheavy nuclei 300,302120, the predicted branching ratio for 92,94Sr cluster radioactivity relative to alpha decay is -0.10 and 0.49 respectively, suggesting that cluster decay modes may be detectable in competition with alpha decay, but only for 302120 does the branching ratio exceed unity. The overall accuracy of the four models tested depends strongly on the quality and size of the experimental data set used for fitting.

## Method

Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated for 21 even-even, 21 even-odd, 4 odd-even, and 5 odd-odd superheavy nuclei (plus additional nuclides from decay chains of 297,299,300,301,302120 and 297,300119) using four models: AKRA (Akrawy), ASAF (Analytical Super-Asymmetric Fission), UNIV (Universal Formula), and semFIS (Semi-empirical formula based on Fission Theory). Experimental Q_alpha values were taken from the AME16 atomic mass evaluation where available, and from the theoretical WS4 mass model for nuclides not in AME16; for 297,299119 the KTUY05 model was used instead. The authors compared the rms standard deviations of log10 half-life for each model across the four parity groups.

## Key numbers

- For 302120, the predicted branching ratio of 92Sr cluster radioactivity relative to alpha decay is 0.49; for 300120, the branching ratio of 94Sr cluster radioactivity is -0.10 (i.e., log10 of the ratio).
- The weighted mean rms standard deviation sigma for the AKRA model over 64 nuclei (21 e-e, 21 e-o, 13 o-e, 9 o-o) is 4.6065; for ASAF it is 4.9477; for UNIV it is 4.6476; for semFIS it is 5.4519.
- For even-even nuclei, the rms standard deviation sigma_ee is 2.9892 (AKRA), 3.397 (ASAF), 2.952 (UNIV), and 3.178 (semFIS).
- For even-odd nuclei, sigma_eo is 8.0620 (AKRA), 8.458 (ASAF), 8.146 (UNIV), and 9.453 (semFIS).
- For odd-even nuclei, sigma_oe is 3.8810 (AKRA), 4.056 (ASAF), 3.989 (UNIV), and 4.439 (semFIS).
- For odd-odd nuclei, sigma_oo is 1.366 (AKRA), 1.663 (ASAF), 1.548 (UNIV), and 2.885 (semFIS).
- The difference DQ between the theoretical Q_alpha from WS4 (or KTUY05) and experimental Q_alpha reaches -4.721 MeV for 288Fl114 and -5.339 MeV for 288Fl114 among even-even nuclides; for 279Ds110 the difference is -4.948 MeV among even-odd nuclides.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of alpha-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei, comparing four models on a set of nuclides relevant to the synthesis of Z=119 and 120 isotopes. The authors note that Wang et al. (2015) found semFIS2 to be the best formula for predicting alpha-decay half-lives in superheavy nuclei, but in the present study AKRA performed best on the selected set. The predictions for cluster radioactivity in 300,302120 follow earlier work by Poenaru and collaborators (2011, 2012) suggesting that for nuclei with Z>121, cluster decay may compete with alpha decay. The results are intended to guide future experiments attempting to synthesize elements 119 and 120, particularly at GSI, RIKEN, and JINR.
