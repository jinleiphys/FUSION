# Nucleus - Air Interactions for Extensive Air Showers Experiments at Ultrahigh Energies

> The HIJING and VENUS models of relativistic hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions are used to study interactions of proton and nuclei with nitrogen, specific for the extensive air shower developments initiated by cosmic rays in the atmosphere. The transverse energy,transverse momenta and secondary particles produced spectra as well as their energy and mass dependence were investigated in detail. Results are presented with particular emphasis on the contributions of minijets in HIJING model and validity of superposition models in this energy range .

## Key claim

The HIJING and VENUS models can describe particle production in proton-air and nucleus-air collisions relevant for extensive air shower development, but the validity of the superposition model as an approximation for nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultrahigh energies remains contested, with the present work finding it a rather good approximation for the air shower cascade while other approaches (Ranft, 1994) conclude it is a rather rough approximation.

## Method

The HIJING Monte Carlo model, which combines soft string fragmentation (LUND-FRITIOF and Dual Parton Model phenomenology) with perturbative QCD minijet production via the PYTHIA algorithm, was used to generate 10^4 minimum-bias events for proton-air and nucleus-air collisions at various energies. The VENUS model, which includes final-state interactions and "double strings" mediating multinucleon interactions, was used for comparison at the same energies. Both models were first tested against accelerator data for proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies (200 GeV to 160 AGeV) before extrapolating to ultrahigh energies.

## Key numbers

- For proton-air interactions, the fraction of transverse energy carried by gluons increases from 2.8% at 1 TeV to 17.3% at 1000 TeV
- The percentage of events containing gluons increases from 7.20% at 17.86 TeV to 10.71% at 1000 TeV in proton-air collisions
- Nucleus-air interactions were studied at 17.86 TeV/nucleon, corresponding to 1 PeV laboratory energy for iron (Fe) nuclei
- For proton-proton interactions at 200 GeV laboratory energy, calculated average multiplicities were compared to experimental data using 10^5 generated events
- For antiproton-proton interactions at 160 TeV laboratory energy (collider energy), mini-jet production plays a much more important role
- The low transverse momentum cutoff for jet production in HIJING is P0 approximately 2 GeV/c, with a constant soft cross section sigma_soft = 57 mb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic interaction models for cosmic ray air shower simulations, specifically comparing the HIJING and VENUS approaches that had been applied separately to various collision systems. The paper builds on earlier work by Schatz et al. (1995) using VENUS with the CORSIKA code to test the superposition hypothesis, and by Ranft (1994) using DPMJET-II who concluded superposition is a rough approximation. The results enable future implementation of HIJING as a Monte Carlo generator in air shower simulation codes, particularly for studying prompt muon and neutrino components from charmed meson production, though the authors note that 10^4 events are insufficient statistics for charmed meson distributions.
