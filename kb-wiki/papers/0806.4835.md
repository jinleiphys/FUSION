# Photo- and Electroproduction of the Hypertriton on 3He

> Differential cross sections of the photo- and electroproduction of the hypertriton have been calculated by utilizing modern nuclear wave functions and the elementary operator of KAON-MAID. It is found that a proper treatment of Fermi motion is essential for the two processes. While the average momentum approximation can partly simulate the Fermi motion in the process, the ``frozen nucleon'' assumption yields very different results, especially at lower energies. The Coulomb effect induced by the interaction between the positively charged kaon and the hypertriton is found to be negligible. The influence of higher partial waves is also found to be relatively small, in contrast to the finding of the previous work. The off-shell assumption is found to be very sensitive in the case of electroproduction rather than in photoproduction. It is shown that the few available experimental data favor the assumption that the initial nucleon is off-shell and the final hyperon is on-shell. This seems to be reasonable, since the hyperon in the hypertriton is less bound than the nucleon in the initial 3He nucleus. The effect of the missing resonance D_{13}(1895) is more profound in the longitudinal cross sections. Excluding this resonance reduces the longitudinal cross sections by one order of magnitude, but does not change the effects of various off-shell assumptions on the cross sections.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections for hypertriton photo- and electroproduction on 3He are calculated using modern nuclear wave functions and the KAON-MAID elementary operator, with the finding that a proper treatment of Fermi motion is essential; the average momentum approximation can partly simulate Fermi motion, but the "frozen nucleon" assumption yields very different results, especially at lower energies.

## Method

The authors used the impulse approximation to compute the nuclear transition matrix element for the reactions gamma + 3He -> K+ + 3LambdaH and e + 3He -> e' + K+ + 3LambdaH, employing the KAON-MAID isobar model for the elementary kaon production operator and modern Faddeev-type wave functions for 3He (Nijmegen93) and the hypertriton (Miyagawa et al.). The nuclear wave functions were expanded in partial waves (34 components for 3He, 14 for the hypertriton), and the elementary operator was decomposed into a non-relativistic form in Pauli space to construct the nuclear current. The cross sections were obtained by summing and averaging over nuclear spins to build the spin-averaged Lorentz tensor and then transforming to the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

* The hypertriton separation energy into a Lambda and a deuteron is 0.13 +/- 0.05 MeV, with a total binding energy of 2.35 MeV.
* For photoproduction, the cross sections are predicted to be on the order of 1 nb and drop quickly as a function of the kaon scattering angle.
* Excluding the D13(1895) resonance reduces the longitudinal cross sections by one order of magnitude at k^2 = -0.5 GeV^2, where the cross section is about four times smaller without this resonance.
* The electroproduction data from Dohrmann et al. (2004) show an almost flat angular distribution at theta_K^c.m. = 2.7, 9.5, and 18.9 degrees, with the photoproduction extrapolation underpredicting the last data point by one order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the earlier photoproduction calculation by Mart (1998) using the Reid soft core potential and a simple hypertriton wave function, now employing modern Faddeev wave functions (Nijmegen93 for 3He, Miyagawa et al. for hypertriton) and the KAON-MAID elementary operator. The study is motivated by the surprising flat angular distribution observed in the electroproduction data of Dohrmann et al. (2004), which contrasts with the smoothly decreasing cross section seen in 4He(e,e'K+)4LambdaH from the same experiment. The results enable refined comparisons with the few available experimental data and provide a framework for investigating off-shell effects, Fermi motion, and the role of the D13(1895) resonance in hypernuclear production.
