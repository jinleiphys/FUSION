# Distinctive features of hadronizing heavy quarks

> The color field of a quark, stripped off in a hard reaction, is regenerated via gluon radiation. The space-time development of a jet is controlled by the coherence time of gluon radiation, which for heavy quarks is subject to the dead-cone effect, suppressing gluons with small transverse momenta. As a result, heavy quarks can radiate only a small fraction of the initial energy. This explains the peculiar shape of the measured heavy quark fragmentation function, which strongly peaks at large fractional momenta z. The fragmentation length distribution, related to the fragmentation function in a model independent way, turns out to be concentrated at distances much shorter than the confinement radius. This implies that the mechanisms of heavy quark fragmentation is pure perturbative.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks radiate only a small fraction of their initial energy due to the dead-cone effect, which suppresses gluons with small transverse momenta; this leads to fragmentation functions that peak at large fractional momenta and implies that the fragmentation length distribution is concentrated at distances much shorter than the confinement radius, meaning the mechanism of heavy quark fragmentation is purely perturbative.

## Method

The authors calculate the radiative energy loss of heavy quarks in vacuum using the coherence length of gluon radiation, given by the inverse longitudinal momentum transfer, and the gluon radiation spectrum which includes the dead-cone effect. They relate the production length distribution to the measured heavy quark fragmentation function via a model-independent relation based on the fractional light-cone momentum loss. The analysis is performed for heavy quarks produced in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and high-\(p_T\) production in \(pp\) collisions.

## Key numbers

- For \(b \to B\) fragmentation, the distribution peaks at \(z \sim 0.85\).
- For \(c \to D\) fragmentation, the distribution peaks at \(z \sim 0.60 \div 0.65\).
- The fractional light-cone momentum \(\alpha\) of the light quark in the formed meson is about \(15\%\) for \(c\) quarks and \(5\%\) for \(b\) quarks.
- The full formation path length for a \(B\) meson is \(L_f = 0.06\ \text{fm} \times [p_T / 1\ \text{GeV}]\), using a mass splitting \(m_{B^\prime} - m_B = 0.6\ \text{GeV}\).
- The lower limit of the \(k_{\perp}^2\) integration is \(\lambda \approx 0.7\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of heavy quark hadronization by applying the dead-cone effect, previously described by Troyan (1980s), to explain the measured fragmentation functions for \(c \to D\) and \(b \to B\) from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation data (charm and bottom collaborations). It contrasts with light quark fragmentation, which falls steadily with \(z\) as known from KKP parameterizations. The results enable a perturbative QCD description of heavy meson production, with the production length distribution extracted directly from data, and suggest that the subsequent formation of the meson wave function is controlled by the binding potential.
