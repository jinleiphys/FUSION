# Baryon spectroscopy on the lattice: recent results

> 5 pages, 4 figures, talk presented at the Conference on the Intersections of Particle and Nuclear Physics, New York, NY, May 19-24, 2003, submitted to American Institute of Physics Conference Proceedings. After publication, it will be found at http://proceedings.aip.org/proceedings/

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the baryon spectrum are still embryonic, with systematic errors from discretization, finite volume, and the quenched approximation not yet under control; the identification of the Roper resonance as a radial excitation of the nucleon depends on using sufficiently light quark masses and large volumes, and remains contested by results from different actions and volumes.

## Method

The paper reviews quenched and unquenched lattice QCD calculations of baryon masses using various gauge actions (Iwasaki, improved, Wilson) and fermion actions (clover tadpole-improved, fat-link irrelevant clover, overlap, domain wall, Wilson, D234, NRQCD). Results are extracted from correlation functions using empirical Bayesian constrained curve fitting or maximum entropy methods, with some studies employing multiple lattice volumes to check finite volume effects. The Lattice Hadron Physics Collaboration (LHPC) plans to use large sets of extended three-quark operators with smeared fields, covariant Laplacians, and displacement operators in correlation matrix techniques to extract excited states without maximum entropy methods.

## Key numbers

- Quenched spectrum from CP-PACS collaboration deviates from experiment by under ten per cent.
- CSSM Lattice collaboration used a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice with spacing \(a = 0.12\) fm, set by the string tension.
- Overlap fermion calculations (Lee et al.) used \(16^3 \times 28\) lattices at spacing \(a \approx 0.2\) fm, with pion masses below 300 MeV showing dramatic changes in baryon masses.
- Finite volume checks for overlap fermion calculations used lattice extents of 3.2 fm and 2.4 fm.
- Sasaki et al. used volumes with spatial extent \(La \approx 2.2 - 3.0\) fm extrapolated to infinite volume using \(1/L^3\), with Wilson action at \(\beta = 6.0\) and spacing \(a \sim 0.1\) fm, and pion masses in the range \(m_\pi = 0.6 - 1.0\) GeV.
- Doubly charmed baryon studies used two lattice spacings \(a \sim 0.15, 0.22\) fm and four light quark masses.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing effort to resolve the "missing resonances" problem in baryon spectroscopy, where the quark model (Isgur and Karl, Capstick) predicts many more states than observed experimentally, while quark-diquark models predict a sparser spectrum. The paper contrasts quenched results from CP-PACS (2000) and CSSM/Adelaide groups with newer overlap fermion calculations (Lee et al., 2003) that reach realistically light quark masses, and highlights discrepancies with Sasaki et al. (2003) regarding the Roper resonance mass. The formation of the Lattice Hadron Physics Collaboration (LHPC), spearheaded by Nathan Isgur and funded by the DOE SciDAC initiative, promises systematic calculations using correlation matrix techniques to capture a significant portion of the baryon spectrum, with methods adaptable to mesons and pentaquark systems.
