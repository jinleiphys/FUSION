# Observation of Thermodynamical Properties in the $^{162}$Dy, $^{166}$Er and $^{172}$Yb Nuclei

> The density of accessible levels in the ($^3$He,$αγ$) reaction has been extracted for the $^{162}$Dy, $^{166}$Er and $^{172}$Yb nuclei. The nuclear temperature is measured as a function of excitation energy in the region of 0 -- 6 MeV. The temperature curves reveal structures indicating new degrees of freedom. The heat capacity of the nuclear system is discussed within the framework of a canonical ensemble.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental extraction of nuclear temperature and heat capacity from gamma-ray spectra for rare earth nuclei, but the heat capacity could only be obtained by using the canonical ensemble because the microcanonical temperature data were too scattered to allow a second differentiation; the temperature curves from the microcanonical ensemble reveal broad structures in the 1-5 MeV region that are not explained by the Fermi gas model and are interpreted as pair breaking and quenching of pair correlations.

## Method

Level densities for 162Dy, 166Er, and 172Yb were extracted from primary gamma-ray spectra measured with the CACTUS multidetector array using the (3He,alpha-gamma) reaction on 163Dy, 167Er, and 173Yb targets at 45 MeV beam energy. The first-generation gamma-ray matrix was factorized according to the Brink-Axel hypothesis to obtain the level density rho(E) and the gamma-strength function, with data below E_gamma = 1 MeV excluded to reduce methodical uncertainties. The microcanonical temperature was obtained by differentiating the entropy S(E) = ln rho(E) using a least-squares fit to five adjacent data points, and the canonical heat capacity was derived from the temperature dependence of the thermal average excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Level density parameter a = 17.8, 19.0, and 18.7 MeV^{-1} for 162Dy, 166Er, and 172Yb, respectively, from Fermi gas fits in the 3.5-6.0 MeV excitation region
- Temperature bumps observed at 1.8 MeV and 3.2 MeV in 162Dy, at 2.5 MeV and 3.7 MeV in 166Er, and at 1.8 MeV and 3.0 MeV in 172Yb
- Energy bin widths of 120, 120, and 96 keV for 162Dy, 166Er, and 172Yb, respectively
- Standard deviation sigma_E = 3 MeV at E = 7 MeV for the canonical thermal average
- The sum in the canonical partition function was tested up to E = 30 MeV and found to be a sufficiently high upper limit

## Context

This work extends the Oslo group's method for extracting level densities from gamma-ray spectra (Tveter et al., 1996) to the extraction of thermodynamical quantities, providing the first experimental temperature and heat capacity data for rare earth nuclei in the 0-6 MeV excitation energy region. The results enable comparison with microscopic model calculations such as the shell model Monte Carlo method (Koonin, Dean, and Langanke, 1997; Nakada and Alhassid, 1997) and with the phenomenological back-shifted Fermi gas model (Gilbert and Cameron, 1965), while the observed fine structures in the microcanonical temperature curves call for more realistic theoretical descriptions of pair correlations and their quenching.
