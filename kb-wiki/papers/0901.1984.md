# Space-time Evolution of J/psi Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The space-time evolution of J/psi production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is investigated in a transport model. Both gluon dissociation and continuous regeneration of J/psis inside deconfined state are considered.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed J/ψ yield in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC results from a competition between initial production (suppressed by gluon dissociation) and continuous regeneration from uncorrelated charm quarks inside the quark-gluon plasma, with the total yield remaining nearly constant over time. The key qualifier is that this balance depends on the dissociation temperature of J/ψ being set to 1.92 times the critical temperature (T_c = 165 MeV) to fit experimental data, and that the model assumes no suppression or regeneration in the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors solve a classical Boltzmann transport equation for the J/ψ distribution in transverse phase space, coupled to 2+1 dimensional Bjorken hydrodynamic equations that describe the QGP evolution from thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c. The loss term includes gluon dissociation with a temperature-dependent cross section modified by a step function at the dissociation temperature T_d, and the gain term is obtained by detailed balance. Initial charm quark and charmonium production cross sections are taken from experimental data at RHIC, with feed-down from χ_c and ψ' in a 6:3:1 ratio.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm for the QGP at RHIC
- J/ψ binding energy ε_J/ψ = 150 MeV used in the model
- J/ψ dissociation temperature T_d^{J/ψ} = 1.92 T_c, with T_c = 165 MeV from lattice simulations
- Charm quark production cross section at mid-rapidity: dσ_{NN}^{c\bar c}/dy|_{y=0} = 120 μb
- B_ll dσ_{NN}^Ψ/dy|_{y=0} = 26.4 nb for J/ψ, 4.4 nb for ψ', and 13.2 nb for χ_c
- Saturation of suppression and regeneration occurs at τ ∼ 4 fm/c, corresponding to the phase transition temperature T_c
- For Cu+Cu collisions, the maximum participant number N_p = 110, and R_AA and ⟨p_t²⟩ agree with Au+Au results for N_p < 110

## Context

This work extends the transport model approach of Zhuang (2008) by incorporating both gluon dissociation and continuous regeneration of J/ψ inside the QGP, building on earlier discussions of sudden generation on the hadronization surface by Braun-Munzinger (2001) and Gorenstein (2001) and continuous regeneration by Rapp (2003) and Thews (2001). The model explains the plateau structure in R_AA for semi-central collisions and the further suppression in central collisions observed by the PHENIX collaboration (2007, 2008), which could not be explained by the flat tail from the gluon dissociation cross section alone. The results enable future studies of geometry fluctuations at RHIC and comparisons with SPS energy data from the NA50 collaboration.
