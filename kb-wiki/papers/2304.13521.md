# Determination of the moments of the proton charge density

> A global analysis of proton electric form factor experimental data from Rosenbluth separation and low squared four-momentum transfer experiments is discussed for the evaluation of the spatial moments of the proton charge density based on the recently published integral method. Specific attention is paid to the evaluation of the systematic errors of the method, particularly the sensitivity to the choice of the mathematical expression of the form factor fitting function. Within this comprehensive analysis of proton electric form factor data, the moments of the proton charge density are determined for integer order moments, particularly: $\langle r^2 \rangle$=0.694(09)$_{Sta.}$(16)$_{Sys.}$~fm$^2$, $\langle r^3 \rangle$=0.870(46)$_{Sta.}$(80)$_{Sys.}$~fm$^3$, and $\langle r^4 \rangle$=1.47(25)$_{Sta.}$(45)$_{Sys.}$~fm$^4$.

## Key claim

The proton charge density moments are determined from a global fit of electric form factor data using an integral method, but the results carry significant systematic uncertainties that depend on the choice of the fitting function and the truncation of the integral; the method is demonstrated to be equivalent to the derivative method for positive even moments, yet the statistical precision limits reliable moment extraction to orders below \(\lambda < 6\).

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit of 19 proton electric form factor data sets from Rosenbluth separation and low-\(k^2\) experiments, using a polynomial ratio function \(G_E(k^2) = (1 + a_1 k^2)/(1 + b_1 k^2 + b_2 k^4 + b_3 k^6)\) with separate normalization parameters for each data set. Spatial moments of the charge density were then computed via the integral method, which evaluates \(\langle r^\lambda \rangle\) from a Fourier-transform integral of \(G_E(k^2)\) regularized by a weak limit, truncated at \(Q^2 = 51.4\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) (2 GeV\(^2\)). Systematic errors were assessed from five sources: experimental data systematics, integral truncation, fit function bias, normalization parameter uncertainty, and model dependence (tested by requiring \(\chi^2_r \le 1.97\) for alternative functional forms).

## Key numbers

* \(\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.694(09)_{\text{Sta.}}(16)_{\text{Sys.}}\ \text{fm}^2\) (proton charge radius squared, from integral method)
* \(\langle r^3 \rangle = 0.870(46)_{\text{Sta.}}(80)_{\text{Sys.}}\ \text{fm}^3\) (third moment of proton charge density)
* \(\langle r^4 \rangle = 1.47(25)_{\text{Sta.}}(45)_{\text{Sys.}}\ \text{fm}^4\) (fourth moment of proton charge density)
* Reduced chi-squared of the global fit: \(\chi^2_r = 1.97\) for 19 data sets up to \(k^2 = 226\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) (8.8 GeV\(^2\))
* Truncated moment \(\langle r^{-2} \rangle_Q = 6.5245\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) at \(Q^2 = 52\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), with infinite limit \(8.7226\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) (showing truncation sensitivity for negative moments)

## Context

This work extends the integral method previously introduced by Hoballah et al. (2020) to a comprehensive global data set, addressing the proton radius puzzle that arose from discrepancies between muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl et al., 2010; Antognini et al., 2013) and earlier scattering measurements (Bernauer et al., 2014; Xiong et al., 2019). The analysis enables determination of odd-order and negative-order moments inaccessible to the traditional derivative method, and provides systematic error budgets that are critical for comparing with spectroscopy results; the findings enable future high-precision scattering experiments to target the low-\(k^2\) region needed for higher-order moments.
