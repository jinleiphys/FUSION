# Parton Fragmentation Functions

> The field of fragmentation functions of light quarks and gluons is reviewed. In addition to integrated fragmentation functions, attention is paid to the dependence of fragmentation functions on transverse momenta and on polarization degrees of freedom. Higher-twist and di-hadron fragmentation functions are considered as well. Moreover, the review covers both theoretical and experimental developments in single-inclusive hadron production in electron-positron annihilation, deep-inelastic lepton-nucleon scattering, and proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

This review paper systematically catalogs the known types of fragmentation functions (FFs) for light quarks and gluons, including integrated, transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD), higher-twist, and di-hadron FFs, but does not present any new experimental measurements or theoretical calculations; it is a comprehensive summary of the field as of 2016.

## Method

The authors survey the theoretical definitions and properties of all major classes of fragmentation functions, including their operator definitions via light-cone correlators, their classification by twist and polarization dependence, and their evolution equations. They then review the experimental observables from single-inclusive annihilation (SIA), semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and proton-proton collisions that are used to extract these functions, followed by a summary of global fitting efforts and model calculations.

## Key numbers

* The unpolarized integrated FF \(D_1^{h/i}(z)\) describes the number of hadrons \(h\) inside parton \(i\) in the momentum fraction range \([z, z+dz]\), with support \(0 \le z \le 1\).
* Six twist-3 quark-quark FFs are identified: \(E\), \(E_L\), \(D_T\), \(G_T\), \(H\), and \(H_L\), each suppressed by a factor \(M_h / P_h^-\) relative to leading-twist FFs.
* Three twist-4 FFs are listed: \(D_3\), \(G_3\), and \(H_3\), suppressed by \(M_h^2 / (P_h^-)^2\).
* For gluons, the unpolarized FF is \(D_1^{h/g}(z)\) and the longitudinally polarized FF is \(G_1^{h/g}(z)\); there is no integrated gluon FF for linearly polarized gluons fragmenting into spin-1/2 hadrons.

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the classification of fragmentation functions originally developed in the Amsterdam notation by Mulders (1995) and Bacchetta (2006), and builds on the foundational work of Collins (1981, 1992) on factorization and the Collins function. It enables future global analyses of FFs from combined SIA, SIDIS, and proton-proton data, and provides the theoretical framework for interpreting spin-dependent measurements such as the transversity PDF via the Collins function \(H_1^{\perp h/q}\) or di-hadron FFs \(H_1^{\sphericalangle h_1 h_2 / q}\).
