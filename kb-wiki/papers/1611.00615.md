# Determination of the number of participating nucleons in p+p interactions at SPS energies

> In the past the analyses of inelastic p+p collision for example in terms of the hadron gas model have assumed that always both protons participate in the interaction. In this article we show that (at sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV ) on average only 1,89 protons are interacting. Measurements of the mean multiplicities of protons, neutrons, charged pions, charged kaons and Λ hyperons allow to compute the number of initial state nucleons using conservation of baryon number, strangeness, and isospin. We further argue that once the number of initial state nucleons is given and the mean multiplicities of protons and charged pions are known in p+p interactions at center-of-mass energies below a few tens of GeV, the yields of net neutrons, kaons, and hyperons can be estimated with a precision of a few percent using again baryon and isospin conservation.

## Key claim

At a center-of-mass energy of 17.3 GeV, on average only 1.89 protons participate in inelastic p+p interactions, contrary to the common assumption that both protons always interact; the difference is attributed to single diffractive events, which constitute about 16% of the total inelastic cross section.

## Method

The mean number of interacting nucleons was computed from the net baryon multiplicity in the final state, using experimental measurements of protons, neutrons, charged pions, charged kaons, and Λ hyperons from the NA49 and NA61 collaborations at a beam momentum of 158 GeV/c. A second independent calculation was performed using isospin conservation, summing the third isospin components of all final-state hadrons, with corrections for neutral kaon and charged Σ hyperon contributions estimated from a Hadron Gas Model (HGM) scaled to the measured Λ + Σ⁰ multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Mean number of interacting nucleons from baryon conservation: ⟨N⟩ = 1.87
- Mean number of interacting nucleons from isospin conservation: ⟨N⟩ = 1.904
- Final averaged result: ⟨N⟩ = 1.89 ± 0.04
- Net proton multiplicity: 1.12
- Net neutron multiplicity: 0.60 (measured), 0.58 (computed)
- Charged pion multiplicities: π⁺ = 3.02, π⁻ = 2.36
- Λ multiplicity (including Σ⁰): 0.12
- Net charged kaon multiplicity (K⁺ – K⁻): 0.097
- Uncertainty on the result dominated by 6% uncertainty on neutron multiplicity, corresponding to 0.04 in magnitude

## Context

This work corrects the long-standing assumption in hadron gas model analyses (e.g., Begun) that both protons always participate in inelastic p+p collisions. The finding that only about 1.89 nucleons interact, consistent with the single diffractive cross section of roughly 5 mb (about 16% of the total inelastic cross section), enables estimation of neutron, kaon, and hyperon yields from proton and charged pion measurements alone at center-of-mass energies below a few tens of GeV. The method could in principle be extended to nucleus-nucleus collisions, though the smaller net isospin per nucleon pair and large pion multiplicity may introduce excessive statistical errors.
