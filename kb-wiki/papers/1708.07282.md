# Chiral geometry in symmetry restored states: Chiral doublet bands in 128Cs

> The pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a symmetry restored basis, i.e., the triaxial quasiparticle-states with angular momentum and particle number projections, and applied for chiral doublet bands in 128Cs. The observed energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities are reproduced well without introducing any parameter. The orientation of the angular momentum in the intrinsic frame is investigated by the distributions of its components on the three principle axes as well as those of its tilted angles. The evolution of the chirality with spin is illustrated and the chiral geometry is demonstrated in the angular momentum projected model for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time the chiral geometry of angular momentum in symmetry-restored states using the angular momentum projection (AMP) approach, showing that the evolution from chiral vibration near the band head to static chirality at higher spins can be extracted from the K-distributions and the profile of tilted angles in the intrinsic frame, with the caveat that the calculated trend of B(E2) deviates from data at higher spins due to the frozen nuclear shape.

## Method

The pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a symmetry-restored basis consisting of triaxial quasiparticle states with three-dimensional angular momentum projection and particle number projection. The intrinsic states for the odd-odd nucleus 128Cs were obtained by constrained variation with blocking of specific neutron and proton orbitals, and the resulting Hill-Wheeler equation was solved to obtain the eigenstates. The orientation of the angular momentum in the intrinsic frame was analyzed via the K-distributions on the three principal axes and the profile of the tilted angles (θ, φ) derived from the collective wave functions in the Euler angle representation.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters (β, γ) constrained to (0.20, 30.0°) for 128Cs, close to the ground state deformation (0.23, 23.8°) from covariant density functional theory with interaction PC-PK1
- At I = 11 ħ, the angular momentum for band A mainly orients at (θ ∼ 60°, φ = 90°), a planar rotation within the s-l plane; band B orients at (θ ∼ 85°, φ ∼ 45°) and (θ ∼ 85°, φ ∼ 135°)
- At I = 14 ħ, band A shows maxima at (θ ∼ 80°, φ ∼ 40°) and (θ ∼ 80°, φ ∼ 140°); band B shows maxima at (θ ∼ 65°, φ ∼ 60°) and (θ ∼ 65°, φ ∼ 120°), demonstrating static chirality
- At I = 18 ħ, band A orients at (θ = 90°, φ = 30°) and (θ = 90°, φ = 150°); band B orients at (θ = 75°, φ = 45°) and (θ = 75°, φ = 135°)

## Context

This work extends the angular momentum projection approach to the study of chiral doublet bands, which were first predicted by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and experimentally observed in 2001 by Starosta et al. The method combines advantages of the tilted axis cranking model and the particle rotor model, and goes beyond previous projected shell model studies by Bhat et al. (2012, 2014) that could not examine the chiral geometry of angular momentum. The approach can be generalized to other exotic rotational modes such as wobbling and combined with state-of-the-art mean field approaches.
