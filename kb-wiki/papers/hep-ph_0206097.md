# Weak magnetism effects in the direct Urca processes in cooling neutron stars

> In the mean field approximation, we study the effects of weak magnetism and pseudoscalar interaction in the neutrino energy losses caused by the direct Urca processes on relativistic nucleons in the degenerate baryon matter. Our formula for the neutrino energy losses incorporates the effects of nucleon recoil, parity violation, weak magnetism, and pseudoscalar interaction. For numerical testing of our formula, we use a self-consistent relativistic model of the multicomponent baryon matter. We found that, due to weak magnetism effects, relativistic emissivities increase by approximately 40-50%, while the pseudoscalar interaction only slightly suppresses the energy losses, approximately by 5%.

## Key claim

The paper claims that weak magnetism effects increase relativistic neutrino emissivities from direct Urca processes in neutron stars by approximately 40-50%, while the pseudoscalar interaction slightly suppresses the energy losses by approximately 5%, based on a mean-field approximation using a Walecka-type relativistic model of multicomponent baryon matter.

## Method

The author derived a relativistic expression for neutrino energy losses from direct Urca processes (n → p + l + ν̄_l and p + l → n + ν_l) using the mean-field approximation with a Walecka-type relativistic model where baryons interact via exchange of σ, ω, and ρ mesons. The matrix element incorporated nucleon recoil, parity violation, weak magnetism (via the CVC theory with anomalous magnetic moments λ_p = 1.7928 and λ_n = -1.9132), and pseudoscalar interaction (via PCAC with pion mass m_π = 139 MeV). The emissivity was calculated by integrating over phase space with degenerate Fermi distributions for nucleons and leptons, fixing momenta at Fermi surfaces and using the approximation that neutrino momentum is negligible compared to other particles.

## Key numbers

* Weak magnetism increases relativistic emissivities by approximately 40-50% for direct Urca processes in degenerate baryon matter.
* Pseudoscalar interaction suppresses energy losses by approximately 5%.
* The Cabibbo factor used is C = cos θ_C = 0.973.
* The axial-vector coupling constant is C_A = 1.26, and the weak magnetism coupling constant is C_M = λ_p - λ_n = 3.7.
* The pion mass is m_π = 139 MeV, and the nuclear saturation density is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
* The temperature-dependent integral factor yields (457/5040)π^6 T^6 for the neutrino energy integration.
* Emissivities are given in units of 10^27 T_9^6 erg cm^{-3} s^{-1}, where T_9 = T/10^9 K.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic formula for direct Urca neutrino energy losses from Lattimer (1991) by incorporating relativistic effects including weak magnetism and pseudoscalar interaction, building on earlier work by Leinson and Perez (2001) who studied baryon recoil and parity violation. The results are relevant for neutron star cooling models, particularly at central densities up to eight times nuclear saturation density where relativistic nucleon motion becomes important, and complement studies by Burrows (2000) and Horowitz (2002) showing weak magnetism increases antineutrino mean free paths in core-collapse supernovae.
