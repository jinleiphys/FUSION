# The QCD equation of state in background magnetic fields

> We determine the equation of state of 2+1-flavor QCD with physical quark masses, in the presence of a constant (electro)magnetic background field on the lattice. To determine the free energy at nonzero magnetic fields we develop a new method, which is based on an integral over the quark masses up to asymptotically large values where the effect of the magnetic field can be neglected. The method is compared to other approaches in the literature and found to be advantageous for the determination of the equation of state up to large magnetic fields. Thermodynamic observables including the longitudinal and transverse pressure, magnetization, energy density, entropy density and interaction measure are presented for a wide range of temperatures and magnetic fields, and provided in ancillary files. The behavior of these observables confirms our previous result that the transition temperature is reduced by the magnetic field. We calculate the magnetic susceptibility and permeability, verifying that the thermal QCD medium is paramagnetic around and above the transition temperature, while we also find evidence for weak diamagnetism at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of stout-improved staggered quarks at physical quark masses, coupled to a constant external magnetic field. They developed a generalized integral method that expresses the change in free energy due to the magnetic field as an integral of the quark condensate differences over the quark masses from their physical values up to infinity, where the magnetic field has no effect; this avoids the flux quantization problem that prevents direct differentiation with respect to the magnetic field. Thermodynamic observables including longitudinal and transverse pressure, magnetization, energy density, entropy density, and interaction measure were computed for a range of temperatures (110 MeV to 300 MeV) and magnetic fields up to eB = 0.7 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of QCD in magnetic fields (Bali 2011, Bruckmann 2013) by providing the full equation of state rather than just the transition temperature. The new integral method is compared to three existing approaches: the anisotropy method (Bali 2013), the half-half method (Levkova 2013), and the finite difference method (Bonati 2013), and is found advantageous for large magnetic fields. The results enable improved modeling of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors, and allow comparison with the Hadron Resonance Gas model at low temperatures and perturbation theory at high temperatures.
