# Recent status of the understanding of neutrino-nucleus cross section

> In this work we have presented current understanding of neutrino-nucleon/nucleus cross sections in the few GeV energy region relevant for a precise determination of neutrino oscillation parameters and CP violation in the leptonic sector. In this energy region various processes like quasielastic and inelastic production of single and multipion production, coherent pion production, kaon, eta, hyperon production, associated particle production as well as deep inelastic scattering processes contribute to the neutrino event rates.

# Recent status of the understanding of neutrino-nucleus cross section

## Key claim
The current understanding of neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the few GeV region still carries an overall uncertainty of 20-25% due to nuclear medium effects, and existing Monte Carlo generators fail to reproduce experimental data, particularly in the low $Q^2$ region for quasielastic events and in single pion production, indicating that better theoretical models are needed.

## Method
The authors review neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus cross sections for quasielastic, single and multipion production, coherent pion production, kaon, eta, hyperon, and associated particle production, as well as deep inelastic scattering processes. They compare experimental data from MiniBooNE, K2K, MINOS, MINERvA, ANL, and BNL with theoretical models including the relativistic Fermi gas model, local Fermi gas model with random phase approximation correlations, two particle-two hole contributions, nucleon spectral functions, and mesonic cloud contributions.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear medium effects lead to an overall uncertainty of 20-25% in cross sections
- The world average value of the axial mass $M_A$ is 1.026 GeV; MiniBooNE data required a much larger value, but inclusion of 2p-2h contributions allows $M_A=1.03$ GeV consistent with NOMAD and MINERvA
- For single pion production, the best fit to reanalyzed ANL and BNL data gives $C_5^A(0)=1.0$ and $M_A=1.026$ GeV for the N-$\Delta$ axial vector transition form factor
- Valencia group fit to the same data gives $C_5^A(0)=1.12\pm0.11$ (old data) and $C_5^A(0)=1.14\pm0.07$ (reanalyzed data) with $M_A=0.954\pm 0.063$ GeV
- Coherent pion production contributes only 2-3% of total pion production due to nuclear medium effects
- The deep inelastic scattering region is defined as $W \ge 2.0$ GeV and $Q^2 \ge 1.0$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This review extends the work of the Aligarh group (Singh 1993, Athar 1999, Haider 2011, 2016) and the Valencia group (Nieves 2004, Alvarez-Ruso 2007, 2015) on nuclear medium effects in neutrino-nucleus scattering. It addresses the discrepancy between experimental data from MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arévalo 2007), K2K (Gran 2006), and MINERvA (Wilkinson 2016, McGivern 2016) and standard Monte Carlo generators like NEUT and NuWro. The work enables improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions needed for precise determination of neutrino oscillation parameters and CP violation searches, as well as for estimating backgrounds in nucleon decay experiments.
