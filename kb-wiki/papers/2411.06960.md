# Collective modes in relativistic cold asymmetric nuclear matter within the covariant Vlasov approach

> A covariant relativistic approach based on the Vlasov equation is applied to the study of infinite asymmetric nuclear matter. We use several Walecka-type hadronic models and obtain the dispersion relations for the longitudinal modes. The isovector and isoscalar collective modes are determined for a wide range of densities as a function of isospin asymmetry and momentum transfer within a set of eleven relativistic mean field models with different nuclear matter properties. Special attention is given to beta-equilibrium matter. It is shown that the possible propagation of isoscalar and isovector-like modes depends directly on the density dependence of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state and of the symmetry energy, with a stiff equation of state favouring the propagation of isoscalar like collective modes at high densities, and a stiff symmetry energy defining the behavior of the isovector like modes which propagate for densities below two times saturation density. The coupling of the nuclear modes to the electron plasmon is also discussed.

## Key claim

The propagation of isoscalar and isovector-like collective modes in cold asymmetric nuclear matter depends directly on the density dependence of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state and of the symmetry energy, with a stiff equation of state favoring isoscalar-like modes at high densities and a stiff symmetry energy defining the behavior of isovector-like modes which propagate only for densities below two times saturation density.

## Method

The authors apply a covariant relativistic Vlasov equation formalism based on the Wigner function to study small oscillations around equilibrium in infinite asymmetric nuclear matter. They use eleven different relativistic mean field (RMF) models of the Walecka type (including NL3, NL3ωρ, TM1, TM1e, TM1-2, TM1-2ωρ, BigApple, FSU, FSU2, FSU2R, and FSU2H) to obtain dispersion relations for longitudinal collective modes. The analysis covers both neutron-proton (np) matter and neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter in beta-equilibrium at zero temperature, including the coupling of nuclear modes to the electron plasmon.

## Key numbers

- Eleven RMF models were used: NL3, NL3ωρ, TM1, TM1e, TM1-2, TM1-2ωρ, BigApple, FSU, FSU2, FSU2R, and FSU2H, with saturation densities ranging from 0.145 fm⁻³ to 0.155 fm⁻³.
- The isovector-like modes propagate for densities below two times saturation density (2ρ₀).
- The effective scalar mass is given by ṁ_s² = m_s² + κφ⁰ + (λ/2)(φ⁰)² - g_s² Σⱼ dρ_s⁰⁽ʲ⁾, with meson masses m_σ ≈ 492-511 MeV, m_ω ≈ 782-783 MeV, and m_ρ ≈ 763-770 MeV across the models.
- The Lindhard function L(sⱼ) = 2 - sⱼ log((sⱼ+1)/(sⱼ-1)) is used, where sⱼ = ω/ω₀ⱼ and ω₀ⱼ = q p_Fⱼ / E_Fⱼ.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective modes in nuclear matter using the covariant Vlasov approach (Avancini 2018) and Landau-Fermi liquid theory (Matsui 1981), which had primarily focused on symmetric matter or neutron matter. It builds on earlier RMF studies of isoscalar and isovector modes (Greco 2003, Avancini 2005) by incorporating a wider set of eleven models with different symmetry energy behaviors and including beta-equilibrium npe matter with electron plasmon coupling. The results enable improved predictions for the crust-core transition in neutron stars and neutrino opacity calculations in supernova matter.
