# Fluctuations from dissipation in a hot non-Abelian plasma

> We consider a transport equation of the Boltzmann-Langevin type for non-Abelian plasmas close to equilibrium to derive the spectral functions of the underlying microscopic fluctuations from the entropy. The correlator of the stochastic source is obtained from the dissipative processes in the plasma. This approach, based on classical transport theory, exploits the well-known link between a linearized collision integral, the entropy and the spectral functions. Applied to the ultra-soft modes of a hot non-Abelian (classical or quantum) plasma, the resulting spectral functions agree with earlier findings obtained from the microscopic theory. As a by-product, it follows that Bödeker's effective theory is consistent with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spectral functions of microscopic fluctuations in a hot non-Abelian plasma can be derived solely from the entropy and the dissipative collision term in a Boltzmann-Langevin transport equation, without requiring detailed knowledge of the underlying microscopic dynamics, provided the system remains close to equilibrium.

## Method

The authors start from a generic Boltzmann-Langevin transport equation for the one-particle distribution function of coloured point particles in a non-Abelian plasma. They expand the entropy density to quadratic order in the deviation from equilibrium to identify the thermodynamic force, then linearize the collision integral in terms of that force. Using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, they derive the noise correlator and the equal-time correlator for fluctuations directly from the dissipative term and the entropy, without recourse to a microscopic phase-space average.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass is given by \( m_D^2 = - 8 \pi g^2 C_2 \int dp\, p^2 \, \frac{d f_{\rm eq}}{d p} \), where \( C_2 \) is the colour-charge normalization constant.
- The collision integral for ultra-soft modes uses the kernel \( {\cal I}({\bf v},{\bf v}') = \delta^{(2)}({\bf v}-{\bf v}') - \frac{4}{\pi} \frac{({\bf v}\cdot{\bf v}')^2}{\sqrt{1-({\bf v}\cdot {\bf v}')^2}} \).
- The damping coefficient in the collision integral is \( \gamma = g^2 N T \ln{(1/g)} / 4 \pi \).
- The noise correlator for the current density is \( \langle \zeta^\mu_a (x,{\bf v})\, \zeta^\nu_b (y,{\bf v}') \rangle = 2\,\gamma\,T\,m^2_D \, v^\mu v'^\nu\, {\cal I}({\bf v},{\bf v}') \, \delta_{ab}\, \delta^{(4)} (x-y) \).

## Context

This work extends the classical transport approach of Heinz (1980s) and the microscopic phase-space formalism of Litim and Manuel (1999) by showing that the fluctuation-dissipation theorem alone suffices to determine the noise correlator for ultra-soft modes. It confirms that Bodeker's effective theory (1998) is consistent with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, and it provides a general prescription that can be applied to other kinetic approaches, such as those of Arnold (1999), Blaizot and Iancu (1999), and Valle (1999). The results enable future extensions to out-of-equilibrium and non-linear regimes.
