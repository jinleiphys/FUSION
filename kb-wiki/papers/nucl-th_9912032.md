# On the Delta-Delta component of the deuteron in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

## Key claim

The probability of the Delta-Delta component of the deuteron is predicted to be 0.08% in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD), which agrees with the experimental upper limit of less than 0.4% at 90% confidence level. This prediction applies when the Delta(1232) resonance is treated on-mass-shell, where the off-shell parameter Z does not contribute.

## Method

The authors calculate the effective Delta-Delta-deuteron interaction Lagrangian using the RFMD, where the deuteron is treated as a neutron-proton Cooper pair induced by a local four-nucleon interaction. They evaluate the coupling constant g_DeltaDeltaD through one-nucleon loop exchanges in the large N_C expansion, using the pi N Delta coupling constant (g_piNDelta = 2.12 g_piNN) and a cutoff Lambda_D = 115.729 MeV that restricts the 3-momenta of fluctuating nucleon fields forming the deuteron. The probability P(Delta-Delta) is then obtained as 2 times the squared ratio of the effective Delta-Delta-deuteron coupling to the nucleon-deuteron coupling.

## Key numbers

- P(Delta-Delta) = 0.08% for the probability of the Delta-Delta component of the deuteron, calculated in the RFMD with the Delta(1232) resonance on-mass-shell
- Experimental upper limit: P(Delta-Delta) < 0.4% at 90% confidence level, from Allasia et al. (1986)
- Cutoff parameter Lambda_D = 115.729 MeV, restricting 3-momenta of fluctuating nucleon fields in the deuteron
- Coupling constant ratio: g_piNDelta = 2.12 g_piNN, where g_piNN is the pion-nucleon coupling constant
- Masses used: M_N = 940 MeV (nucleon), M_Delta = 1232 MeV (Delta resonance), deuteron quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm

## Context

This work extends the RFMD previously developed by Ivanov and collaborators (1990s) to include non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the deuteron via the Delta(1232) resonance. The result is consistent with the potential model approach of Dymarz and Khanna, who also found probabilities compatible with the experimental upper limit from Allasia et al. (1986). The effective Delta-Delta-deuteron interaction derived here enables calculation of the Delta-Delta component's contribution to any low-energy process involving the deuteron in initial or final states, though such calculations are noted to be complicated and beyond the scope of this letter.
