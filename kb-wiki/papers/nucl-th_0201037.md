# Overview of Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

> An overview of neutrino-nucleus interactions is presented. After a brief discussion of theory and applications of detector response three examples are discussed: i) Recent work on neutrino-deuteron reactions, ii) Probing strangeness content of the nucleon with neutrinos, and iii) The role of neutrino reactions in supernova dynamics and r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

Neutrino-nucleus interactions are essential for interpreting neutrino detector data and for understanding core-collapse supernova dynamics and r-process nucleosynthesis, but the theoretical accuracy for key reactions such as neutrino-deuteron charged-current breakup is currently limited to about 5%, with the dominant uncertainty coming from an unknown axial two-body counter-term that must be fixed experimentally.

## Method

The paper reviews three specific areas of neutrino-nucleus physics: (i) effective field theory calculations of neutrino-deuteron scattering, where next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order expansions are compared to potential model results; (ii) the use of neutrino elastic scattering ratios (proton-to-neutron knockout) to extract the strange quark contribution to the nucleon axial form factor, Δs; and (iii) coupled neutrino evolution and nuclear statistical equilibrium calculations in supernova wind models, where the electron fraction Y_e is tracked self-consistently with weak capture rates for active-sterile neutrino mixing scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The only measurement of the total cross section for charged-current breakup of deuterons by electron neutrinos has about 35% precision (Willis, year not given in text).
- Current theoretical accuracy for neutrino-deuteron cross sections is about 5%, sufficient for Sudbury Neutrino Observatory results but needing improvement.
- In supernova models, ν_τ and ν̄_τ decouple at temperatures about 8 MeV; ν̄_e decouple at about 5 MeV; ν_e decouple at about 3.5 to 4 MeV.
- The electron fraction in the nucleosynthesis region is approximately Y_e ≈ 1/(1 + T_{ν̄_e}/T_{ν_e}), so if T_{ν̄_e} > T_{ν_e} the medium is neutron-rich.
- Expansion timescales of τ = 0.3 seconds and τ = 0.9 seconds are used in the supernova wind model calculations.
- For Y_e > 1/3 only electron neutrinos, and for Y_e < 1/3 only electron antineutrinos can undergo an MSW resonance in active-sterile mixing scenarios.

## Context

This overview extends the theoretical framework for neutrino-nucleus interactions discussed in earlier reviews by Vogel (1998) and Haxton (1999), and applies it to three active research areas. The neutrino-deuteron work builds on effective field theory calculations by Kaplan (1998) and Butler (1999, 2000). The supernova nucleosynthesis analysis extends the neutrino-driven wind models of Woosley (year not given) and Janka (year not given), and the MSW resonance treatment follows Qian (year not given) and McLaughlin (1999). The paper enables future experimental programs at spallation neutron sources such as ORLaND and MiniBooNE to probe neutrino parameters relevant to both Big Bang and supernova nucleosynthesis.
