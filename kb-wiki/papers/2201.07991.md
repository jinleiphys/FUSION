# Chiral anomaly in non-relativistic systems: Berry curvature and chiral kinetic theory

> Chiral anomaly and the novel quantum phenomena it induces have been widely studied for Dirac and Weyl fermions. In most typical cases, the Lorentz covariance is assumed and thus the linear dispersion relations are maintained. However, in realistic materials, such as Dirac and Weyl semimetals, the non-linear dispersion relations appear naturally. We develop a kinetic framework to study the chiral anomaly for Weyl fermions with non-linear dispersions by using the methods of Wigner function and semi-classical equations of motion. In this framework, the chiral anomaly is sourced by Berry monopoles in momentum space and could be enhanced or suppressed due to the windings around the Berry monopoles. Our results can help understand the chiral anomaly-induced transport phenomena in non-relativistic systems.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly for Weyl fermions with non-linear dispersion relations is sourced by Berry monopoles in momentum space and can be enhanced or suppressed due to the windings around those monopoles; the anomaly relation formally remains unchanged from the relativistic case only when the Fermi surface encloses the Berry monopole, with the distribution function at the monopole location determining the anomaly strength.

## Method

The authors develop a chiral kinetic theory using two complementary approaches: (1) a Wigner function method starting from the quantum field theory for a simple Weyl Hamiltonian with an added quadratic momentum term (H = λ σ·p + α p²), expanding in ℏ to derive the kinetic equation and single-particle equations of motion; (2) a semi-classical expansion method for the more general Hamiltonian H = K(p)·σ + K₀(p), using spectral projections and Berry curvature to obtain the equations of motion and the anomaly relation. Both methods yield the same form of the chiral anomaly expressed in terms of the Berry monopole charge.

## Key numbers

- Berry curvature for the simple Hamiltonian: Ω_λ = λ p/(2|p|³), with divergence ∇_p·Ω_λ = 2πλ δ⁽³⁾(p) (Berry monopole charge λ at p=0)
- The chiral anomaly relation for the general Hamiltonian: ∂_μ j^μ = ±(ℏ/4π²) E·B Σ_{p*} N_{p*} f(t,x,p*), where N_{p*} is the winding number of the map p → K(p) around each Berry monopole at p*
- The phase-space measure correction: √G = 1 + ℏ Ω_λ·B
- The CME current expression: j_CME = ∓(ℏ/4π²) B Σ_{p*} N_{p*} F(ε_{p*}), where F'(ε_p)=f(ε_p) with boundary condition F(ε_p)→0 for p→∞

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework, previously developed for relativistic Dirac and Weyl fermions (Gao 2012, Chen 2012, Liu 2018, Huang 2018), to non-relativistic systems with non-linear dispersions that appear naturally in realistic Weyl and Dirac semimetals. It generalizes earlier studies of tilted and warped Weyl nodes (Fang 2012, Soluyanov 2015, Sun 2015) and connects to the index theorem approach (Yee 2019). The results enable understanding of chiral anomaly-induced transport phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect and anomalous Hall effect in condensed matter systems where the linear dispersion approximation breaks down.
