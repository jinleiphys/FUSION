# Centrality and transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow of multi-strange hadrons and $ϕ$ meson in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV

> We present high precision measurements of elliptic flow near midrapidity ($|y|<1.0$) for multi-strange hadrons and $ϕ$ meson as a function of centrality and transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV. We observe that the transverse momentum dependence of $ϕ$ and $Ω$ $v_{2}$ is similar to that of $π$ and $p$, respectively, which may indicate that the heavier strange quark flows as strongly as the lighter up and down quarks. This observation constitutes a clear piece of evidence for the development of partonic collectivity in heavy-ion collisions at the top RHIC energy. Number of constituent quark scaling is found to hold within statistical uncertainty for both 0-30$\%$ and 30-80$\%$ collision centrality. There is an indication of the breakdown of previously observed mass ordering between $ϕ$ and proton $v_{2}$ at low transverse momentum in the 0-30$\%$ centrality range, possibly indicating late hadronic interactions affecting the proton $v_{2}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow for phi mesons and Omega baryons is similar to that of pions and protons, respectively, which may indicate that heavier strange quarks flow as strongly as lighter up and down quarks; this is presented as evidence for partonic collectivity, but the claim is hedged by noting that the similarity is observed "within statistical uncertainty" and that the mass ordering breakdown between phi and proton at low transverse momentum is only an "indication" that "possibly" indicates late hadronic interactions.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed 730 million minimum-bias Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV recorded in 2010 and 2011, using the Time Projection Chamber and Time of Flight detectors for particle identification. Elliptic flow was measured using the eta sub-event plane method with an eta gap of |Delta eta| > 0.1 to suppress non-flow effects, and the observed v2 values were corrected for finite event plane resolution. Short-lived hadrons (K0_S, phi, Lambda, Xi, Omega) were reconstructed through their decay channels, with v2 calculated as a function of invariant mass for each pT and centrality bin to account for signal-to-background ratio.

## Key numbers

- 730 million minimum-bias Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV were analyzed
- The ratio v2(phi)/v2(p) at pT = 0.52 GeV/c for 0-30% centrality is 4.35 +/- 0.98 (stat.) +0.66/-0.45 (sys.)
- The ratio v2(phi)/v2(p) at pT = 0.52 GeV/c for 30-80% centrality is 1.12 +/- 0.10 +0.047/-0.053 (sys.)
- Deviation from NCQ scaling for phi in 0-30% centrality: 2.7 +/- 2.6 (stat.) +/- 1.8 (sys.)%
- Deviation from NCQ scaling for Omega in 0-30% centrality: 10.1 +/- 8.4 (stat.) +/- 5.3 (sys.)%
- Deviation from NCQ scaling for Xi in 0-30% centrality: 11.3 +/- 2.3 (stat.) +/- 1.4 (sys.)%
- Event bias correction factors for 0-30% centrality: 1.002 for phi, 1.019 for Xi, 1.068 for Omega
- Event bias correction factors for 30-80% centrality: 1.053 for phi, 1.054 for Xi, 1.067 for Omega

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of phi and Omega v2 that were statistically limited (STAR Collaboration, 2007), providing high-precision results as a function of both transverse momentum and centrality. The observation that NCQ scaling holds within approximately 10% for both 0-30% and 30-80% centrality at RHIC energy contrasts with the ALICE experiment's observation of up to ~20% deviation at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV at the LHC. The breakdown of mass ordering between phi and proton at low pT supports hydrodynamic model calculations with hadron cascade (JAM) that predict this effect due to late-stage hadronic re-scattering on the proton v2, while the phi meson remains unaffected due to its small hadronic interaction cross-section.
