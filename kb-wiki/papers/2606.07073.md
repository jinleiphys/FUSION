# Mechanical distribution of the pseudoscalar charmonium and bottomonium on the light-front

> We investigate the energy-momentum tensor of pseudoscalar charmonium and bottomonium within the framework of the light-front quark model. The gravitational form factors (GFFs), namely the $A$ and $D$-terms, are evaluated in terms of the light-front wave functions. The corresponding spatial mechanical distributions in the transverse plane are obtained through the Fourier transform of these GFFs. To examine the sensitivity of the results to the internal quark-antiquark distribution inside the meson, two distinct Gaussian forms are employed for the spatial part of the wave function. We analyze several mechanical properties in the transverse plane, including the momentum density, pressure distribution, shear stress, force density, and internal energy density. The pressure distribution exhibits a node where it changes sign from positive (repulsive) to negative (attractive) with increasing transverse distance. The force distribution remains positive throughout the transverse plane, supporting the stability condition proposed in earlier studies. Most of the spatial distributions, except for the shear stress, are found to be sensitive to the choice of the spatial wave function near the center of the meson, while they become nearly insensitive toward the periphery. In contrast, the shear stress distribution exhibits noticeable sensitivity to the choice of wave function in the intermediate transverse region.

## Key claim

The gravitational form factors (GFFs) and mechanical distributions of pseudoscalar charmonium and bottomonium are computed within the light-front quark model, but the results depend on the choice of spatial wave function: the pressure, momentum density, force density, and energy density are sensitive to the wave function near the meson center, while the shear stress distribution is sensitive in the intermediate transverse region.

## Method

The authors evaluate the matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) operator for pseudoscalar quarkonia using the light-front constituent quark model (LFQM) in the valence Fock-state approximation. The gravitational form factors A and D are extracted from the EMT matrix elements using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction with an asymmetric frame at zero skewness. Two distinct Gaussian spatial wave functions (SET-I from Chung-Coester-Polyzou and SET-II from Terentev-Karmanov) are employed to test sensitivity to the internal quark-antiquark distribution, and the transverse-plane mechanical distributions are obtained via two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the GFFs.

## Key numbers

- D-term at zero momentum transfer for charmonium: D^CJ_ηc = -0.291 (SET-I wave function), D^TK_ηc = -0.268 (SET-II wave function)
- D-term at zero momentum transfer for bottomonium: D^CJ_ηb = -0.134 (SET-I), D^TK_ηb = -0.127 (SET-II)
- Quark masses used: m_c = 1.68 GeV, m_b = 5.10 GeV
- Harmonic scale parameters: β_ηc = 0.699 GeV, β_ηb = 1.376 GeV
- The A-term satisfies the momentum sum rule A(0) = 1 for both mesons

## Context

This work extends studies of gravitational form factors from nucleons (Burkert 2018) and pions (Choi 2025) to the heavy-quark sector of pseudoscalar quarkonia, where prior calculations exist using a contact-interaction approach (Sultan 2024) and other methods (Xu 2024, Hu 2024). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD data for charmonium electromagnetic form factors (Li 2020, Chen 2011, Delaney 2023) and provide predictions for mechanical properties such as pressure and shear stress that could be tested at the Electron-Ion Collider.
