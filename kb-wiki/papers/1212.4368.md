# Bottomonium from lattice QCD as a probe of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the temperature dependence of bottomonium for temperatures in the range 0.4 Tc < T < 2.1 Tc, using non-relativistic dynamics for the bottom quark and full relativistic lattice QCD simulations for Nf=2 light flavors. We consider the behaviour of the correlators in Euclidean space, we analyze the associated spectral functions and we study the dependence on the momentum. Our results are amenable to a successful comparison with effective field theories. They help build a coherent picture of the behaviour of bottomonium in the plasma, consistent which the current LHC results.

## Key claim
The paper claims that bottomonium ground states (Υ and η_b) survive in the quark-gluon plasma up to temperatures of about 2.1 T_c, while excited states melt above approximately 1.4 T_c, but the authors note that the apparent width at zero temperature is likely a lattice/MEM artifact and that systematic effects require further control.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid lattice QCD approach: full relativistic dynamics for two degenerate light quark flavors (N_f=2) on highly anisotropic lattices, combined with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the bottom quark, including terms up to O(v^4). They computed Euclidean correlators for bottomonium states (Υ, η_b, χ_b, h_b) at temperatures from 0.4 T_c to 2.1 T_c, extracted spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), and studied the momentum dependence of ground state peaks for velocities up to v ≈ 0.2.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: 0.4 T_c < T < 2.1 T_c, with T_c ≈ 220 MeV
- Ground state Υ and η_b peaks remain visible up to T ≈ 2.1 T_c; excited states are no longer discernible at T/T_c ≈ 1.68
- For the vector (Υ) channel, the upper limit on the width gives Γ/T ∼ 1, corresponding to α_s ∼ 0.4 in the weak-coupling formula Γ/T = (1156/81)α_s^3 ≈ 14.27 α_s^3
- The thermal mass shift prediction δE_thermal/M = 5.93 α_s (T_c/M)^2 (T/T_c)^2 ≈ 0.0046 (T/T_c)^2, using M ≈ 5 GeV and α_s ≈ 0.4
- Largest momentum studied: |p| ≈ 1.73 GeV, corresponding to v ≈ 0.183 for the Υ(1S) (mass 9.460 GeV)
- The velocity dependence of the width follows Γ_v/Γ_0 = 1 − (2/3)v^2 + O(v^4), giving corrections at the percent level for v^2 ≲ 0.04

## Context
This work extends the FASTSUM collaboration's earlier studies (Aarts 2010, Aarts 2011, Aarts 2012) on bottomonium in the quark-gluon plasma, using a hybrid NRQCD+lattice approach. The results are compared with effective field theory predictions from Brambilla (2010) for thermal widths and mass shifts, and from Escobedo (2011) for velocity dependence. The observed survival of the Υ(1S) and suppression of excited states is consistent with LHC PbPb collision results from CMS (2012). The authors plan to extend this work to N_f=2+1 flavors, finer lattices, and larger momenta to improve systematic control and enable more quantitative comparisons with effective theories.
