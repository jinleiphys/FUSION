# Critical slowing down and bulk viscosity in binary neutron star mergers

> Hydrodynamic simulations of neutron star mergers rely on the clear separation between the strong-interaction, weak-interaction, and hydrodynamic timescales. In this effective framework, weak Urca interactions are typically the slowest microscopic processes, and therefore the Urca rate determines the bulk-viscous dissipation. This assumed hierarchy of dissipative mechanisms can be decisively altered, without invalidating hydrodynamics, if the trajectory of the matter in a neutron star merger passes through the vicinity of a possible low temperature QCD critical point. The enhanced density fluctuations lead to critical slowing down and rapid growth of transport coefficients including bulk viscosity. While this growth is regulated by finite-time effects, finite-size effects, and the breakdown of hydrodynamic scale separation, which bound the correlation length, we demonstrate that the QCD contribution to bulk viscosity can rival the electroweak contribution in realistic conditions. Thus, critical dynamics could leave observable imprints on the hydrodynamic evolution of neutron star mergers.

# Critical slowing down and bulk viscosity in binary neutron star mergers

## Key claim
If the trajectory of matter in a binary neutron star merger passes near a possible low-temperature QCD critical point, the critical contribution to bulk viscosity from critical slowing down can rival or exceed the electroweak contribution, even though finite-time effects, finite-size effects, and the breakdown of hydrodynamic scale separation bound the correlation length growth.

## Method
The authors estimate the maximum growth of the correlation length near a QCD critical point under two constraints: finite equilibration time (approximately 1 ms based on merger dynamics) and the spatial variation of thermodynamic fields (using a temperature gradient of 5 MeV/km). They then compute the critical contribution to bulk viscosity using the Kawasaki approximation for the dynamic scaling of Model H universality class, with the non-critical part of the equation of state modeled by an excluded volume Hadron Resonance Gas (EV-HRG) using the PDG21+ hadronic list. The critical bulk viscosity is compared to electroweak baselines from prior literature.

## Key numbers
- Maximum correlation length from finite-time constraint: approximately 10 nm (assuming 1 ms near the critical point)
- Maximum correlation length from spatial gradient constraint: approximately 100 nm (assuming temperature gradient of 5 MeV/km and critical chemical potential of 1.2 GeV)
- Estimated maximum achievable bulk viscosity from critical slowing down: range of 10^25 to 10^33 g/cm·s across scanned temperatures and chemical potentials
- Critical bulk viscosity scales approximately as T_c^(10/3) at low temperatures in EV-HRG
- For a critical point at mu_B,c = 1.8 GeV and T_c = 50 MeV, critical effects dominate electroweak contributions for the non-universal mapping parameter rho*w greater than approximately 1
- Hydrodynamic description remains valid for coarse-graining scales down to 10^(-4) m even for rho*w = 10^2

## Context
This work extends the study of bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers beyond the standard electroweak processes (reviewed by Harris (2024) and Alford (2022)) to include QCD critical dynamics. Prior work by Most (2022) showed that bulk viscosity from weak interactions can affect gravitational wave emission, and by Chabanov (2023) further explored these effects. The authors build on the critical slowing down framework of Stephanov (1999) and the critical bulk viscosity calculation method of Martinez (2019), applying it for the first time to binary neutron star merger conditions. This opens the possibility of searching for QCD critical point signatures in compact astrophysical objects, a prospect not previously seriously considered.
