# Kaon and Nucleon States with Hidden Charm

> In this talk we discuss the formation of exotic hadrons with hidden charm arising from three-body interactions. To be more specific, in the strangeness sector, we predict the existence of a mesonic state, $K^*(4307)$, which is dynamically generated from the three-body interactions of the $KD\bar{D}^*$ system, has mass around $4307$ MeV and quantum numbers $I(J^P) = 1/2\,(1^-)$. In the baryonic sector, we predict the existence of $N^*$ states, which are generated from the three-body interactions of the $ND\bar{D}^*$ system, with masses around $4400\sim 4600$ MeV, widths of $2\sim 20$ MeV and positive parity.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of a mesonic state K*(4307) and several N* baryonic states as dynamically generated three-body systems, but these predictions depend on the validity of the fixed center approximation (FCA) and on the specific models used for the two-body interactions (SU(4) heavy-quark spin symmetry or SU(8) spin flavor symmetry), with the results showing some dependence on the cut-off parameter.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations using the fixed center approximation (FCA) for the three-body systems KD*D* and ND*D*, treating the D*D* subsystem as a cluster (either X(3872) or Zc(3900)). The two-body t-matrices for the KD, K*D*, ND, and N*D* subsystems were obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with effective Lagrangians based on heavy-quark spin symmetry and SU(4) or SU(8) spin flavor symmetries, with the loop functions regularized using a cut-off around 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- K*(4307) mesonic state: mass around 4307 MeV, quantum numbers I(JP) = 1/2(1-), generated from the KD*D* system
- N* states from ND*D* system: masses in the range 4400-4600 MeV, widths 2-20 MeV, positive parity
- For the N* states with JP = 1/2+: mass range 4404-4410 MeV with width 2 MeV; and mass range 4556-4560 MeV with width approximately 4-20 MeV
- For the N* states with JP = 3/2+: mass range 4467-4513 MeV with width approximately 3-6 MeV; and mass range 4558-4565 MeV with width approximately 5-14 MeV
- Varying the cut-off in the form factor from 700 to 770 MeV caused a small shift of about 3-5 MeV on the masses of the N* states
- The Zc(3900) width used in the calculation was approximately 28 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of exotic hadrons with hidden charm beyond the known X, Y, and Z families (Hosaka 2016, Eberhard, Eulogio 2016, Stephen, Richard) and the recently observed hidden charm pentaquarks with non-zero strangeness at around 4459 MeV reported by the LHCb collaboration (Mwang). The predictions are based on the known attractive interactions in the D*D* subsystem (generating X(3872) and Zc(3900)), the KD and KD* subsystems (generating Ds0(2317) and Ds1(2460)), and the ND and ND* subsystems (generating Lambda_c(2595)). The results suggest that experimental searches for a K* meson around 4307 MeV and N* states in the 4400-4600 MeV region could reveal new three-body molecular states in the charmonium sector.
