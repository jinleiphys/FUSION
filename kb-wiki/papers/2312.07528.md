# Continuum extrapolated high order baryon fluctuations

> Fluctuations play a key role in the study of QCD phases. Lattice QCD is a valuable tool to calculate them, but going to high orders is challenging. Up to the fourth order, continuum results are available since 2015. We present the first continuum results for sixth order baryon fluctuations for temperatures between $T=130 - 200$ MeV, and eighth order at $T=145$ MeV in a fixed volume. We show that for $T \leq 145$ MeV, relevant for criticality search, finite volume effects are under control. Our results are in sharp contrast with well known results in the literature obtained at finite lattice spacing.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated results for sixth-order baryon number fluctuations (over 130-200 MeV) and eighth-order fluctuations (at 145 MeV) in lattice QCD, showing that previous finite-lattice-spacing results at low temperatures (below 145 MeV) suffer from large cutoff effects that had been mistaken for physical deviations from the hadron resonance gas model.

## Method
The authors used a novel 4HEX staggered fermion action with DBW2 gauge action, which strongly suppresses taste-breaking lattice artifacts compared to earlier actions (4stout, HISQ). They performed direct determinations of baryon number fluctuations at zero baryochemical potential using the exponential definition of the chemical potential on three lattice volumes (16^3x8, 20^3x10, 24^3x12) with aspect ratio LT=2, then carried out a continuum extrapolation using a combined spline fit with systematic error estimation from two scale settings (f_pi and w_1) and three different spline node choices.

## Key numbers
- Sixth-order baryon fluctuations (chi_6^B) continuum-extrapolated over temperature range T = 130 - 200 MeV
- Eighth-order baryon fluctuations (chi_8^B) continuum-extrapolated at a single temperature T = 145 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.261 - 0.072 fm, corresponding to beta = 0.46 - 0.83
- Number of configurations analyzed: up to 336,975 on the 16^3x8 lattice at T=145 MeV; 188,571 on 20^3x10 at T=145 MeV; 111,435 on 24^3x12 at T=145 MeV
- Taste breaking in the pion sector reduced by up to more than one order of magnitude compared to the 4stout action for finer lattices
- Continuum value of the Wilson flow ratio w_1/w_0 = 1.515(1)

## Context
This work extends continuum results for baryon fluctuations beyond fourth order (available since Bellwied (2015)) to sixth and eighth order, resolving a discrepancy between earlier finite-lattice-spacing calculations: the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2018) using imaginary chemical potential with 4stout action, and the HotQCD collaboration (2022) using direct determination with HISQ action at N_tau=8. The results show that previous N_tau=8 HISQ results had large cutoff effects that produced deviations from the hadron resonance gas model, while the continuum-extrapolated results agree well with HRG predictions for T below 145 MeV. This enables reliable determinations of the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion for the QCD equation of state, Padé approximant poles for critical endpoint searches, and fluctuation observables at nonzero baryochemical potential for freeze-out studies.
