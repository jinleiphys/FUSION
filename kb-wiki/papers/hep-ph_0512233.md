# Finite volume effects in a quenched lattice-QCD quark propagator

> We investigate finite volume effects in the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking. To this end we employ a formulation of the Schwinger-Dyson equations on a torus which reproduces results from the corresponding lattice simulations of staggered quarks and from the overlap action. Studying the volume dependence of the quark propagator we find quantitative differences with the infinite volume result at small momenta and small quark masses. We estimate the minimal box length L below which chiral perturbation theory cannot be applied to be L \simeq 1.6 fm. In the infinite volume limit we find a chiral condensate of |<\bar{q}q>|_{\bar{MS}}^{2 GeV} = (253 \pm 5.0 MeV)^3, an up/down quark mass of m_{\bar{MS}}^{2 GeV} = 4.1 \pm 0.3 MeV and a pion decay constant which is only ten percent smaller than the experimental value.

## Key claim

The paper claims that finite volume effects in the quenched quark propagator are quantitatively significant at small momenta and small quark masses, and that the minimal box length below which chiral perturbation theory cannot be safely applied is L ≃ 1.6 fm, a condition that depends on the specific volume and quark mass regime studied.

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs) for the ghost, gluon, and quark propagators on a torus with periodic boundary conditions for gluons and ghosts and antiperiodic boundary conditions for quarks, using a box of equal length L in all four directions. The quark-gluon vertex was parametrized with a simple ansatz depending only on gluon momentum, fitted to reproduce lattice data from staggered quarks (Bowman 2002) and overlap quarks (Zhang 2004) on their respective manifolds. The Yang-Mills sector input came from numerical SDE solutions on the torus that matched lattice gluon and ghost propagator data from Bowman (2004) and Sternbeck (2005).

## Key numbers

* Minimal box length below which chiral perturbation theory cannot be applied: L ≃ 1.6 fm, estimated from the quark SDE on a torus.
* Chiral condensate in the infinite volume limit: |⟨q̄q⟩|_MS̅^(2 GeV) = (253 ± 5.0 MeV)^3.
* Up/down quark mass in the infinite volume limit: m_MS̅^(2 GeV) = 4.1 ± 0.3 MeV.
* Pion decay constant in the infinite volume limit: only ten percent smaller than the experimental value.
* For staggered quarks, the bare lattice quark masses (0.028, 0.057, 0.114 GeV) correspond to renormalized SDE quark masses at ζ=2.9 GeV of 0.044, 0.080, and 0.151 GeV, respectively.
* For overlap quarks, the bare lattice quark masses (0.090, 0.140, 0.210, 0.300 GeV) correspond to renormalized SDE quark masses at ζ=2.9 GeV of 0.076, 0.112, 0.162, and 0.225 GeV, respectively.
* The infrared exponent κ for the gluon propagator is κ ≈ 0.5 (IR-finite) on a compact manifold, versus κ ≈ 0.596 (IR-vanishing) on R^4.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume analysis of Fischer and Pennington (2005) on gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory to the quark propagator, using the same SDE-on-torus framework. It differs from the earlier approach of Bhagwat (2003) by solving the coupled ghost, gluon, and quark SDEs and by fitting the interaction to reproduce lattice results on their respective manifolds. The results enable systematic study of chiral symmetry restoration at small volumes and provide a direct SDE-based estimate of the minimal volume for chiral perturbation theory, complementing the hadron-level analysis of Colangelo (2005).
