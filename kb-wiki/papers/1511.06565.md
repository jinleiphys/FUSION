# Distributions of the $S$-matrix poles in Woods-Saxon and cut-off Woods-Saxon potentials

> The positions of the $l=0$ $S$-matrix poles are calculated in generalized Woods-Saxon (GWS) potential and in cut-off generalized Woods-Saxon (CGWS) potential. The solutions of the radial equations are calculated numerically for the CGWS potential and analytically for GWS using the formalism of Gy. Bencze \cite{[Be66]}. We calculate CGWS and GWS cases at small non-zero values of the diffuseness in order to approach the square well potential and to be able to separate effects of the radius parameter and the cut-off radius parameter. In the case of the GWS potential the wave functions are reflected at the nuclear radius therefore the distances of the resonant poles depend on the radius parameter of the potential. In CGWS potential the wave function can be reflected at larger distance where the potential is cut to zero and the derivative of the potential does not exist. The positions of most of the resonant poles do depend strongly on the cut-off radius of the potential, which is an unphysical parameter. Only the positions of the few narrow resonances in potentials with barrier are not sensitive to the cut-off distance. For the broad resonances the effect of the cut-off can not be corrected by using a suggested analytical form of the first order perturbation correction.

## Key claim

The positions of most resonant S-matrix poles in a cut-off Woods-Saxon potential depend strongly on the unphysical cut-off radius parameter, and only the few narrow resonances in potentials with a barrier are insensitive to this cut-off distance; the effect of the cut-off on broad resonances cannot be corrected using the suggested first-order perturbation correction.

## Method

The authors calculated the l=0 S-matrix pole positions for generalized Woods-Saxon (GWS) potentials analytically using the Bencze formalism and for cut-off generalized Woods-Saxon (CGWS) potentials by numerical integration of the radial Schroedinger equation. They used the program BENCZE in Wolfram Mathematica to find zeros of the denominator of the analytical S-matrix for GWS potentials, and the program JOZSO, which minimizes the difference of logarithmic derivatives over a wide radial region, for CGWS potentials. The pole positions were analyzed by fitting first-order polynomials to the |k_m| values as a function of the pole sequence number m, and the resulting slopes were used to extract an effective range parameter.

## Key numbers

* For the WS potential with V1=44.4 MeV, a=0.7 fm, R=7.52 fm (208Pb+n system), the slope of the fitted line to |k_m| values approached π/R ≈ 0.418 fm^-1, with deviations within 5% for m_s > 20 even at a=0.7 fm.
* For the CWS potential with the same parameters and Rmax=12 fm, the slope of the fitted line approached π/Rmax ≈ 0.262 fm^-1.
* For the 56Fe+n system with V1=47.78 MeV, V2=-200 MeV, R=4.92 fm, a=0.6 fm, the narrow resonance at m=1 had k = (1.24873 - 0.07323i) fm^-1, and this value was identical for Rmax values of 12, 15, 18, and 21 fm.
* For the same system, the m=2 narrow resonance had k = (1.89306 - 0.29392i) fm^-1 at Rmax=12 fm, changing only in the last three decimal places when Rmax was increased to 21 fm.
* For the CGWS potential with V2=0 (no barrier), the resonance at k=(1.18982 - 0.73207i) fm^-1 in GWS shifted to k=(1.15854 - 0.61148i) fm^-1 in CGWS with Rmax=12 fm; the perturbation correction gave k_corr=(1.16310 - 0.65090i) fm^-1, which did not fully restore the GWS value.
* For the CGWS potential with V2=-200 MeV, the ranges extracted from the three pole groups were: R_B ≈ 15.0 fm (for Rmax=15 fm), R_A ≈ 4.84 fm, and R_C ≈ 10.19 fm (close to Rmax-R = 10.08 fm).

## Context

This work extends the study of S-matrix pole distributions from square well potentials (Nussenzveig, 1959) and strictly finite range potentials (Salamon, 2014) to the more realistic Woods-Saxon and generalized Woods-Saxon potentials used in nuclear reaction theory and the Gamow shell model (Michel, 2009). The analytical solution for l=0 GWS potentials follows the formalism of Bencze (1966) and recent work by Bayrak and Aciksoz (2015) on bound states in 56Fe+n. The results show that cut-off potentials introduce unphysical poles whose positions depend on the cut-off radius, which is problematic for applications like the Gamow shell model where the Berggren ensemble must avoid pole positions; the narrow resonances in potentials with a barrier are the only reliable physical poles, while broad resonances and the extra poles from the cut-off cannot be corrected perturbatively.
