# A reciprocal formulation of non-exponential radiative transfer. 1: Sketch and motivation

> Previous proposals to permit non-exponential free-path statistics in radiative transfer have not included support for volume and boundary sources that are spatially uncorrelated from the scattering events in the medium. Birth-collision free paths are treated identically to collision-collision free paths and application of this to general, bounded scenes with inclusions leads to non-reciprocal transport. Beginning with reciprocity as a desired property, we propose a new way to integrate non-exponential transport theory into general scenes. We distinguish between the free-path-length statistics between correlated medium particles and the free-path-length statistics beginning at locations not correlated to medium particles, such as boundary surfaces, inclusions and uncorrelated sources. Reciprocity requires that the uncorrelated free-path distributions are simply the normalized transmittance of the correlated free-path distributions. The combination leads to an equilibrium imbedding of a previously derived generalized transport equation into bounded domains. We compare predictions of this approach to Monte Carlo simulation of multiple scattering from negatively-correlated suspensions of monodispersive hard spheres in bounded two-dimensional domains and demonstrate improved performance relative to previous work. We also derive new, exact, reciprocal, single-scattering solutions for plane-parallel half-spaces over a variety of non-exponential media types.

# A reciprocal formulation of non-exponential radiative transfer. 1: Sketch and motivation

## Key claim

Non-exponential radiative transfer in bounded media requires two distinct free-path distributions, one for paths beginning at medium-correlated events (collisions) and one for paths beginning at medium-uncorrelated origins (boundaries, inclusions, uncorrelated sources), and the uncorrelated distribution must equal the normalized correlated transmittance to preserve a weak form of reciprocity. The paper proposes this equilibrium imbedding of the generalized linear Boltzmann equation but notes it cannot be applied to fractal media where the mean correlated free path is unbounded.

## Method

The author derives a weak reciprocity condition for correlated-media transport by comparing forward and adjoint probability densities for single-scattering paths in a half-space, requiring equal total path throughput for both directions when endpoints are medium-uncorrelated. This yields the uncorrelated free-path distribution as the normalized correlated transmittance, $p_u(s) = X_c(s) / \mfp_c$. The formalism is then validated by Monte Carlo simulation of multiple scattering from negatively-correlated suspensions of monodispersive hard spheres in bounded two-dimensional domains, using minimum-distance Poisson-disk sampling (dart-throwing) to generate blue-noise random media with a specified minimum separation distance $\smin$.

## Key numbers

- For power-law transmittance (Davis and Xu, 2014) with shape parameter $a$, the correlated free-path distribution is $p_c(s) = a(a+1) \ell (a \ell)^a / (a \ell + s)^{a+2}$ with correlated mean $\mfp_c = \ell$, and the uncorrelated transmittance is $X_u(s) = (a \ell / (a \ell + s))^a$.
- For $a = 1/2$ (half-integer), the single-scattering from a half-space is $f_1(\mu_i,\mu_o) = (c / 4\pi) \cdot 1 / (2 \sqrt{\mu_i \mu_o} + \mu_i + \mu_o)$.
- For Gamma-2 steps in 3D, the correlated free-path distribution is $p_c(s) = s e^{-s}$, the uncorrelated free-path distribution is $p_u(s) = (1/2) e^{-s} (s+1)$, and the single-scattering from a half-space is $f_1(\mu_i,\mu_o) = (c / 4\pi) \cdot (\mu_i^2 + 3 \mu_i \mu_o + \mu_o^2) / (\mu_i + \mu_o)^3$.
- For classical exponential transport, $p_u(s) = p_c(s) = \Sigma_t e^{-\Sigma_t s}$, the unique case where the same free-path statistics produce reciprocal transport.

## Context

This work extends the generalized linear Boltzmann equation (GLBE) of Larsen and Vasques (2011), originally formulated for infinite media with correlated emission, to bounded domains with uncorrelated sources, boundaries, and inclusions. It contradicts the approach of Davis et al. (2014, 2018) and others who used the correlated free-path distribution $p_c(s)$ for boundary interactions, which abandons Helmholtz reciprocity. The relationship $p_c(s) = \mfp_c \, \partial^2 X_u(s) / \partial s^2$ maps to known results in statistical mechanics for two-phase random media (Torquato, 1993) and to renewal-process treatments of binary mixtures (Levermore, 1988; Sanchez, 1991). Concurrent work by Jarabo et al. (2018) proposed a similar extension including path-length-dependent albedo and phase functions but did not study reciprocity. The formalism enables predictive condensed-history applications for light transport in discrete random media (Moon, 2007; Meng, 2015; Muller, 2016) and motivates further work on heterogeneous densities, reflective boundaries, and angular memory effects.
