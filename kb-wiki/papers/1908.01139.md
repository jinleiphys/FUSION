# Nucleon resonances in $γp \to ωp$ reaction

> The most recent high-precision data on spin observables $Σ$, $T$, $P'$, $E$, $F$ and $H$ reported by the CLAS Collaboration together with the previous data on differential cross sections and spin-density-matrix elements reported by the CLAS, A2, GRAAL, SAPHIR and CBELSA/TAPS Collaborations for the reaction $γp \to ωp$ are analyzed within an effective Lagrangian approach. The reaction amplitude is constructed by considering the $t$-channel $π$ and $η$ exchanges, the $s$-channel nucleon and nucleon resonances exchanges, the $u$-channel nucleon exchange and the generalized contact current. The latter accounts effectively for the interaction current and ensures that the full photoproduction amplitude is gauge invariant. It is shown that all the available CLAS data can be satisfactorily described by considering the $N(1520)3/2^-$, $N(1700)3/2^-$, $N(1720)3/2^+$, $N(1860)5/2^+$, $N(1875)3/2^-$, $N(1895)1/2^-$ and $N(2060)5/2^-$ resonances in the $s$-channel. The parameters of these resonances are extracted and compared with those quoted by PDG.

## Key claim

The authors claim that all available CLAS data for the reaction γp → ωp, including the most recent high-precision spin observables, can be satisfactorily described by considering seven specific nucleon resonances in the s-channel within a tree-level effective Lagrangian approach, but this description is conditional on fitting the resonance parameters to the data rather than using quark-model predictions.

## Method

The reaction amplitude was constructed using an effective Lagrangian approach including t-channel π and η exchanges, s-channel nucleon and nucleon resonance exchanges, u-channel nucleon exchange, and a generalized contact current to ensure gauge invariance. The authors fitted the resonance parameters (masses, widths, and coupling constants) of seven nucleon resonances to the available experimental data on differential cross sections, spin-density-matrix elements, and six spin observables (Σ, T, P', E, F, H) from the CLAS, A2, GRAAL, SAPHIR, and CBELSA/TAPS Collaborations. The analysis was performed in the center-of-mass energy range from threshold up to W ~ 2.5 GeV, where Pomeron exchange contributions were safely ignored.

## Key numbers

- Seven nucleon resonances were included in the s-channel: N(1520)3/2⁻, N(1700)3/2⁻, N(1720)3/2⁺, N(1860)5/2⁺, N(1875)3/2⁻, N(1895)1/2⁻, and N(2060)5/2⁻
- The coupling constants used for non-resonant contributions include g_NNπ = 13.46, g_NNη = 4.76, g_NNω = 11.76, κ_ω = 0, g_ωπγ = 0.32, and g_ωηγ = 0.25
- The analysis covers center-of-mass energies from the ω-production threshold up to W ~ 2.5 GeV
- The ω meson mass is 782.7 MeV with a width of about 8.5 MeV, compared to the ρ meson mass of about 775 MeV with a width of about 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of ω photoproduction by Zhao (2000), Oh et al. (2000), Titov and Lee (2002), and the Giessen group (2002, 2005), which showed discrepancies on which resonances dominate the reaction. Unlike those earlier approaches that used quark-model predictions for resonance couplings, the present analysis lets the recent high-precision CLAS data determine the relevant resonances by fitting parameters directly. The results enable more stringent constraints on nucleon resonance properties in the 2 GeV mass region and can be compared with the partial-wave analyses of Williams et al. (2009) and Denisenko et al. (2016) within the Bonn-Gatchina approach.
