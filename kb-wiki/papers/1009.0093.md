# Color Glass Condensate and Glasma

> 11 pages, 14 figures, to appear in the proceedings of the RIKEN BNL Research Center Workshop on "Saturation, the Color Glass Condensate and Glasma: What Have we Learned from RHIC?", May 10-12, 2010

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) provides a first-principles QCD framework for describing the early stages of high-energy nuclear collisions in the gluon saturation regime, but the leading-order energy-momentum tensor from the glasma has negative longitudinal pressure, requiring resummation of fluctuations to achieve a form compatible with hydrodynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews the CGC effective theory, which separates fast partons (treated as static color currents with a stochastic distribution \(W[\rho]\)) from slow partons (described as gauge fields). The JIMWLK equation governs the evolution of the source distribution \(W_{\Lambda^+}[\rho]\) with the cutoff \(\Lambda^+\), resumming powers of \(\alpha_s \ln(1/x)\). For heavy ion collisions, the energy-momentum tensor is computed at leading order from classical Yang-Mills solutions, and at next-to-leading order by including a slice of slow modes, leading to a factorization formula that absorbs logarithms into JIMWLK-evolved distributions. A scalar \(\phi^4\) toy model with longitudinal expansion is used to study the effect of initial-field fluctuations on the approach to hydrodynamical behavior.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy, the typical momentum fraction for bulk particle production is \(x \sim 10^{-2}\); at the LHC, it is \(x \sim 4 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The glasma flux tubes have a typical transverse size of order \(Q_s^{-1}\) and are correlated over \(\alpha_s^{-1}\) units of rapidity in the longitudinal direction.
- The strength of the 2-particle ridge correlation is controlled by \((Q_s R)^{-2}\), the area of one flux tube relative to the total transverse area.
- In the scalar toy model with initial fluctuations, the energy density decreases as \(\tau^{-4/3}\), and the pressure relaxes toward \(\epsilon/3\) (the scale-invariant equation of state in four dimensions).

## Context

This review extends the CGC framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the JIMWLK evolution equation, applying it to heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. It connects the glasma flux-tube picture to the ridge correlation observed by STAR, and addresses the long-standing problem of matching the CGC initial state to hydrodynamics, as previously studied by Lappi and McLerran (2006) and others. The work enables future first-principles calculations of thermalization and the initial conditions for relativistic fluid dynamics.
