# Resurrecting the Strong KSS Conjecture

> Many counterexamples to the proposed KSS bound $\fracηs \ge \frac 1 {4π}$ depend on constructing systems with large numbers of species. As a result, the entropy density grows large and $\frac ηs$ can be made arbitrarily small. However, these constructions do not affect the dimensionless shear viscosity $\frac {ηT}{ε+ P}$, which agrees with the traditional $\frac ηs$ only for vanishing chemical potential. This raises the possibility that a KSS-like bound holds for all systems, not just UV-complete quantum field theories, contrary to the previous understanding.

# Resurrecting the Strong KSS Conjecture

## Key claim
The dimensionless shear viscosity $\hat\eta \equiv \eta T/(\epsilon + P)$ may obey a universal lower bound for all systems described by hydrodynamics, not just UV-complete quantum field theories, because the large-number-of-species counterexamples to the original KSS bound rely on nonvanishing chemical potential where $\eta/s$ and $\hat\eta$ diverge.

## Method
The paper examines the large-$N_s$ dilute gas construction used to violate the original KSS bound $\eta/s \ge 1/(4\pi)$, showing that while the entropy of mixing term $\log N_s$ makes $\eta/s$ arbitrarily small, the shear wave decay rate $\exp[-\eta/(\epsilon+P) k^2 t]$ remains unchanged. The author then demonstrates that the dimensionless shear viscosity $\hat\eta = \eta T/(\epsilon+P)$ is the physically relevant quantity at nonvanishing chemical potential, and that the large-$N_s$ construction leaves $\hat\eta$ unaffected.

## Key numbers
- The original KSS bound is $\eta/s \ge 1/(4\pi)$, proposed by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
- For a dilute gas with $N_s$ species, the entropy density is $s \approx n[\log N_s + 5/2 + \log(\bar v^{3/2}/n)]$, where the first term is the entropy of mixing.
- The shear wave decay rate is given by $\exp[-\eta/(\epsilon+P) k^2 t]$, which depends on $\hat\eta = \eta T/(\epsilon+P)$ rather than $\eta/s$.

## Context
This work extends the original KSS conjecture of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) and addresses the counterexamples constructed by Cohen (2007) and Cherman (2007), which used large numbers of species to violate $\eta/s \ge 1/(4\pi)$. Cherman (2007) had proposed that a generalized KSS bound could only hold for UV-complete quantum field theories at vanishing chemical potential. The present paper argues that the dimensionless viscosity $\hat\eta$ is the correct quantity to bound, and that a prototype argument from Kovtun (2011) shows that when shear and sound waves decay slowly, they contribute to the dimensionless viscosity themselves, providing a mechanism for a universal lower bound. This enables future work to search for counterexamples to $\hat\eta \ge C_{\mathrm{KSS}}$ rather than to $\eta/s \ge 1/(4\pi)$.
