# Lattice Generalized Parton Distributions and Form Factors of the Nucleon

> In these proceedings we discuss recent progress in nucleon structure using lattice QCD simulations at or near the physical value of the pion mass. Main focus will be given in observables such as the nucleon axial charge and the first moments of parton distributions, for both the valence and sea quark contributions, and discuss their implications on the spin content of the nucleon. We will will also report developments on the evaluation of the gluon momentum fraction, which contributes significantly to the nucleon spin.

# Lattice Generalized Parton Distributions and Form Factors of the Nucleon

## Key claim
Lattice QCD simulations at or near the physical pion mass can now provide ab initio estimates of the quark and gluon contributions to the nucleon spin, but the data for the axial charge at the physical point show an upward trend toward the experimental value only with current statistics, volume, and lattice spacing, statistical and systematic uncertainties are not yet well controlled, and final conclusions require increased statistics and volume dependence studies.

## Method
The authors compute two- and three-point correlation functions on the lattice for nucleon matrix elements, forming optimized dimensionless ratios to extract ground-state observables via plateau, two-state fit, and summation methods. Connected and disconnected quark-loop diagrams are evaluated for the axial charge, axial form factor, and quark momentum fraction, while gluon contributions require a separate disconnected insertion with operator mixing eliminated through a renormalization prescription. Simulations use multiple fermion formulations (Clover, Domain Wall, HISQ, Twisted Mass) at various lattice spacings and volumes, with results renormalized to the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV where applicable.

## Key numbers
- Experimental axial charge: g_A^exp = 1.2701(25)
- Axial dipole mass from Twisted Mass fermions at m_pi ~ 130 MeV: M_A = 1.24(8) GeV
- Axial dipole mass from Domain Wall fermions at m_pi = 172 MeV: M_A = 1.24(14) GeV
- Axial dipole mass from HISQ formulation at m_pi = 130 MeV (preliminary): M_A = 1.02(4) GeV
- Disconnected light quark contribution to the axial charge: approximately 7-10% of the connected part (negative)
- Disconnected light quark momentum fraction at physical point: <x>^{DI}_{u+d} = 0.223(99)
- Disconnected strange quark momentum fraction at physical point: <x>^{DI}_s = 0.092(41)
- Ratio of strange to light quark momentum fraction (ETMC at m_pi = 130 MeV): <x>_s / <x>_{u/d} = 0.76(30)
- Ratio of strange to light quark momentum fraction (chiQCD chirally extrapolated): <x>_s / <x>_{u/d} = 0.78(03)
- Gluon momentum fraction at physical point: <x>_g = 0.273(23)(24)
- Momentum sum rule satisfied: sum_q <x>_q + <x>_g = 1.01(10)(2)

## Context
This proceedings paper reviews lattice QCD progress on nucleon structure following the European Muon Collaboration discovery (Ashman 1987) that quarks contribute only about half of the proton spin, known as the proton spin crisis. It extends earlier quenched and heavier-pion-mass calculations by presenting results at or near the physical pion mass, including disconnected quark-loop contributions that were historically neglected due to poor signal-to-noise. The work enables direct comparison with experimental determinations of the axial charge and form factors, tests of the Ji spin decomposition (Ji 1996), and first lattice tests of the momentum sum rule using gluon contributions that eliminate operator mixing (Alexandrou 2016).
