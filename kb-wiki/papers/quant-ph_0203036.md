# Wavepackets falling under a mirror

> We depict and analyze a new effect for wavepackets falling freely under a barrier or well. The effect appears for wavepackets whose initial spread is smaller than the combination $\ds \sqrt{\frac{l_g^3}{|z_0|}}$, between the gravitational length scale $\ds l_g = \frac{1}{(2 m^2 g)^{1/3}}$ and the initial location of the packet $z_0$. It consists of a diffractive structure that is generated by the falling and spreading wavepacket and the waves reflected from the obstacle. The effect is enhanced when the Gross-Pitaevskii interaction for positive scattering length is included. The theoretical analysis reproduces the essential features of the effect. Experiments emanating from the findings are proposed.

# Wavepackets falling under a mirror

## Key claim

A freely falling quantum wavepacket that is blocked by an obstacle (barrier or well) from above develops a persistent diffractive structure only when its initial spread is smaller than the combination sqrt(l_g^3 / |z_0|), where l_g is the gravitational length scale and z_0 is the initial packet location; for wider packets the pattern merges into a single peak. The effect is enhanced when the Gross-Pitaevskii interaction for positive scattering length is included.

## Method

The author numerically integrates the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for Gaussian wavepackets falling under a repulsive barrier or attractive well in a uniform gravitational field, using Sodium atom parameters. The analysis is supplemented by two analytical approaches: a single-image-packet approximation using plane-wave solutions transformed to an accelerating frame, and an expansion in terms of Airy function solutions that satisfy the boundary condition of a vanishing wavefunction at the mirror surface. The Gross-Pitaevskii nonlinear interaction is included in some runs to model Bose-Einstein condensate behavior.

## Key numbers

- Gravitational length scale for Sodium: l_g = 0.73 microns
- Gravitational length scale for Hydrogen: l_g = 5.86 microns
- Initial packet location used in simulations: z_0 = -7 microns
- Barrier strength used: U = 10^6 sec^-1
- Well strength used: U = -10^2 sec^-1
- Gross-Pitaevskii interaction strength: g approximately 25 (for wavefunction normalized to one, using Sodium scattering length and 250 atoms/micron^3)
- Initial packet width for thin packets: 4 sigma approximately 0.4 microns
- Numerical accuracy: flux conservation around 0.2%, energy conservation around 2%
- Time reached in numerical integration: 30 msec, with packet center at approximately 4.5 mm below the mirror

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of "wavepacket diffraction in space and time" (Kalbermann, earlier papers k1-k5) to the case of a gravitational field, motivated by the experimental realization of Bose-Einstein condensation in alkali atoms (Anderson 1995, Davis 1995) and the need to include gravity in theoretical calculations with condensates. The effect is distinct from the "quantum bouncer" problem (Bana 1999) and from standard diffraction in time (Moshinsky 1952), as the diffractive structure persists to infinite times rather than fading. The findings suggest a possible alternative method for continuous output coupling in an atom laser, using a pencil-like thin jet of atoms falling under a mirror.
