# Analysis of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering with global optimization in the cluster effective field theory

> We analyze the elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering including the contribution of resonance states below the $p$-$^{15}$N breakup threshold energy. We use the cluster effective field theory in which scattering amplitude is expanded in terms of the effective range expansion parameters for the angular momentum states from $l=0$ to $l=6$. The amplitude contains 37 parameters, which are determined by fitting to 11 392 differential cross section data points of the elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering. To optimize the fitting process, we implement the differential evolution (DE) algorithm, which performs a global search over the high-dimensional parameter space and consistently converges to the same minimum $χ^{2}$ value across independent runs, suggesting proximity to the global minimum within the explored domain. In parallel, the Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method is used to crosscheck the DE results and to estimate the parameter uncertainties. The best fit yields $χ^{2}/N\!\simeq\!6.2$ for the elastic scattering data. Using the determined 37 parameters, we calculate the differential cross sections and the phase shifts of the elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering and compare the results with experimental data and those of an $R$-matrix analysis. Our result of the cross section agrees with the experimental data as accurately as an $R$-matrix analysis. The results demonstrate that the cluster effective field theory, combined with global optimization and uncertainty quantification based on DE-MCMC methods, provides a reliable and systematic framework for applications to low energy phenomena relevant to stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis.

# Analysis of elastic α-¹²C scattering with global optimization in the cluster effective field theory

## Key claim

The cluster effective field theory, combined with differential evolution and Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, reproduces experimental elastic α-¹²C differential cross section data as accurately as an R-matrix analysis, with a best-fit χ²/N ≃ 6.2 for 11,392 data points, but the fit quality remains substantially above unity, indicating that the model does not capture all details of the data within experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors construct scattering amplitudes for elastic α-¹²C scattering using cluster effective field theory, expanding the amplitude in effective range expansion parameters for angular momentum states from l=0 to l=6. The 37 free parameters are determined by fitting to 11,392 differential cross section data points from Tischhauser et al. (2009) covering α-particle energies from 2.6 to 6.7 MeV and 32 laboratory angles from 24.0° to 165.9°. The differential evolution algorithm performs global optimization over the 37-dimensional parameter space, followed by Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling with 200 walkers and 1.2×10⁵ steps each to crosscheck results and estimate parameter uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit χ²/N ≃ 6.2 for N = 11,392 differential cross section data points of elastic α-¹²C scattering
- Mean χ²/N from MCMC analysis = 6.1876 with standard deviation 7.5×10⁻⁴ and 95% credible interval [6.1861, 6.1891]
- MCMC sampling used 200 walkers evolved for 1.2×10⁵ steps each, with 2×10⁴ steps discarded as burn-in and thinning factor of 10, yielding approximately 2×10⁶ independent samples
- Mean integrated autocorrelation time over all parameters = 1,434.1 steps (minimum 963.7, maximum 2,206.8)
- DE convergence from χ² ≃ 3.19×10⁵ at start to 7.10×10⁴ at end of run
- Energy range of experimental data: 2.6–6.7 MeV (α-particle energy) over 32 angles from 24.0° to 165.9° (laboratory frame)
- Binding energies of subthreshold 0₂⁺, 1₁⁻, 2₁⁺, 3₁⁻ states of ¹⁶O used as constraints, with α-¹²C breakup threshold at 7.162 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous cluster effective field theory analyses by Mun, Park, Hyun, and Ando (2018–2023) that fitted parameters to phase shift data rather than directly to differential cross sections. It contrasts with R-matrix analyses by Tischhauser et al. (2009) and by other authors (1987), achieving comparable accuracy for cross sections but showing discrepancies for l=5 and l=6 phase shifts between the two R-matrix analyses. The methodology enables systematic uncertainty quantification for extrapolation to astrophysical energies relevant to the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O capture process at Gamow energy EG ≃ 0.3 MeV, where current theoretical estimates have uncertainties of 20–30% (Tang et al. reporting S_E1(EG) = 80±18 keV b and Oulebsir et al. reporting 100±28 keV b).
