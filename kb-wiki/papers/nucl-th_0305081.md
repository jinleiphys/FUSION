# Total Nuclear Reaction Cross Section Induced by Halo Nuclei and Stable Nuclei

> We develop the method for the calculation of the total reaction cross sections induced by the halo nuclei and stable nuclei. This approach is based on the Glauber theory, which is valid for nuclear reactions at high energy. It is extended for nuclear reactions at low energy and intermediate energy by including both the quantum correction and Coulomb correction under the assumption of the effective nuclear density distribution. The calculated results of the total reaction cross section induced by stable nuclei agree well with the 30 experimental data within 10 percent accuracy.The comparison between the numerical results and the 20 experimental data for the total nuclear reaction cross section induced by the neutron halo nuclei and the proton halo nuclei indicates a satisfactory agreement after considering the halo structure of these nuclei, which implies the quite different mean fields for the nuclear reactions induced by halo nuclei and stable nuclei. The halo nucleon distributions and the root mean square radii of these nuclei can be extracted from above comparison based on the improved Glauber model, which indicate clearly the halo structures of these nuclei. Especially, it is clear to see that the medium correction of the nucleon-nucleon collision has little effect on the total reaction cross sections induced by the halo nuclei due to the very weak binding and the very extended density distribution.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Glauber model, which is normally valid only at high energies, to low and intermediate energies by adding quantum and Coulomb corrections under an effective nuclear density assumption. The method reproduces 30 experimental total reaction cross sections for stable nuclei within 10 percent accuracy, and also agrees with 20 data points for halo nuclei, but the medium correction of nucleon-nucleon collisions has little effect on halo-induced reactions due to their very weak binding and extended density distribution.

## Method

The approach is based on the Glauber theory, which is valid for nuclear reactions at high energy. The authors extend it to low and intermediate energies by including both quantum correction and Coulomb correction under the assumption of an effective nuclear density distribution. The halo nucleon distributions and root mean square radii are extracted by comparing numerical results with experimental data using this improved Glauber model.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model approach previously applied to high-energy reactions, making it applicable to low and intermediate energies where quantum and Coulomb effects become important. The method enables extraction of halo nucleon distributions and root mean square radii for halo nuclei, which clearly indicate halo structures. The finding that medium corrections have little effect on halo-induced reactions contrasts with their importance for stable nuclei, suggesting quite different mean fields for the two cases.
