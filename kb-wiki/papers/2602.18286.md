# On self-dualities for scalar $ϕ^4$ theory

> Scalar field theory is studied by constructing interacting saddle point expansions in the symmetric and broken phase, respectively. Focusing on analytically tractable saddle expansions, it is found that broken and symmetric phases are related by sign flip of the quartic coupling. Applications to dimensions $d<4$ recover previous results for the phase diagram, whereas $d=4$ is possibly new.

## Key claim

The symmetric and broken phases of scalar φ⁴ theory are related by a sign flip of the quartic coupling across dimensions, but this relation is derived within the R1-level resummation scheme, which the author cautions is quantitatively unreliable and may disagree with leading-order weak-coupling perturbation theory.

## Method

The author constructs two interacting saddle-point expansions for scalar φ⁴ theory: a symmetric saddle expansion using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and a broken-phase saddle expansion around a nonzero field expectation value φ₀. Both expansions are evaluated at the R1 resummation level, which corresponds to a mean-field-like approximation that resums a specific class of Feynman diagrams. The free energies of the two saddle expansions are compared to determine which phase is thermodynamically preferred as a function of the bare coupling.

## Key numbers

- In d=2, the critical coupling where symmetric and broken phases have equal free energy is λ_c ≡ λ_B/Λ²_MS̅ ≃ 1.69371, which exceeds the threshold eπ/6 ≈ 1.423 for real-valued broken-phase pole masses.
- In d=2 at the critical coupling, the symmetric-phase pole mass satisfies λ_B/M² ≃ 2.5527, compared to lattice results of λ_B/M² ≃ 2.7 ± 0.025.
- In d=3, the critical coupling where the free energies cross is λ_c ≡ λ_B/m_B ≃ 1.55, compared to a literature value of λ_c ≃ 1.07 from high-order methods.
- In d=4, the critical mass parameter where the broken-phase free energy equals the symmetric-phase free energy is m²_crit ≃ 0.0626 Λ²_MS̅, under one specific interpretation of the broken-phase parameters.
- In d=1 (quantum mechanics), the method wrongly predicts a phase transition at m²_B/λ_B^{2/3} ≃ −5.4, where no such transition exists.

## Context

This work extends the study of self-dualities in scalar field theory, specifically Chang duality in d=2 (Chang 1976, Serone 2018, 2019) and Magruder duality in d=3 (Magruder 1976, Sberveglieri 2020), by analyzing them within an R1-level resummation framework previously developed for large-N field theory (Romatschke 2023, 2024). The results qualitatively reproduce known phase diagrams from lattice field theory (Schaich 2009) and high-order perturbation theory, but the numerical values of critical couplings differ due to the low-order approximation. The d=4 case is presented as possibly new, with implications for triviality theorems (Aizenman 2019, Romatschke 2023) and for interpreting negative-coupling scalar field theory as a symmetry-broken phase with positive coupling.
