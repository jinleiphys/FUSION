# Quark polarization from parton scatterings in heavy ion collisions

> Based on the result in our previous work, we calculate the polarization of quarks from parton scatterings in high energy heavy ion collisions. The result is compared with the STAR data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key claim
The authors calculate quark polarization from parton scatterings in heavy ion collisions using a microscopic model that does not assume local equilibrium for the spin degree of freedom, and they find that the resulting polarization magnitude of about 0.3% agrees with STAR data for Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, but only under specific assumed values for the average vorticity, time interval, and coherence length.

## Method
The authors apply their previously derived formalism (Zhang et al., 2019) for spin asymmetry from 2-to-2 parton scatterings, considering all processes with at least one quark or anti-quark in the final state. They evaluate the polarization rate tensor numerically using Gaussian wave packets for incoming partons, with parameters including quark mass 0.2 GeV, gluon Debye screening mass 0.2 GeV, wave packet width 0.28 GeV, and temperature 0.3 GeV. They then estimate the quark polarization by assuming an average vorticity of 0.04, a polarization time interval of 5 fm, and an impact parameter cutoff of 2.5 fm.

## Key numbers
- Quark polarization estimate: approximately 0.3% for the y-component, compared to STAR data of 0.277 +/- 0.040 +/- 0.039 [%] for Lambda and 0.240 +/- 0.045 +/- 0.061 [%] for anti-Lambda at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Quark mass used in calculation: m_q = 0.2 GeV for all flavors (u, d, s, anti-u, anti-d, anti-s)
- Gluon Debye screening mass: m_D = 0.2 GeV
- Gaussian wave packet width: alpha = 0.28 GeV
- Temperature: T = 0.3 GeV
- Assumed average vorticity: (1/2) times the y-component of the gradient of beta times fluid velocity equals approximately 0.04
- Assumed polarization time interval: 5 fm
- Impact parameter cutoff: b_0 = 2.5 fm

## Context
This work extends the microscopic model for global polarization from parton scatterings proposed by Zhang et al. (2019) and provides the first numerical comparison with STAR data (Adam et al., 2018) for Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperon polarization at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The approach differs from earlier models by Liang and Wang (2004), Voloshin (2004), Becattini et al. (2007, 2013), and others that assume local equilibrium for spin degrees of freedom; instead, the polarization emerges naturally from spin-orbit coupling in scattering amplitudes. The results enable future calculations of momentum-dependent polarization and predictions for other collision energies.
