# $<m_T>$ excitation function: Freeze-out and equation of state dependence

> An integrated Boltzmann+hydrodynamics transport approach is applied to investigate the dependence of the mean transverse mass on the freeze-out and the equation of state over the energy range from $E_{\rm lab}=2-160A $GeV. This transport approach based on the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage allows for a systematic comparison without adjusting parameters. We find that the multiplicities of pions and protons are rather insensitive to different freeze-out prescriptions and changes in the equation of state, but the yields are slightly reduced in the hybrid model calculation compared to a pure transport calculation while the (anti)kaon multiplicities are increased. The mean transverse mass excitation functions of all three particle species are found to be sensitive to the different freeze-out treatments as well as to the equation of state. We find that the bag model equation of state with a strong first order phase transition is in qualitative agreement with the experimentally observed step-like behaviour in the < m_T > excitation function. The hybrid model with a hadron gas equation of state leads to a overestimation of the < m_T >, especially at higher energies. However, non-equilibrium effects seem also to be substantial as is suggested by the comparison with standard UrQMD results.

## Key claim

The mean transverse mass excitation function's step-like behavior, often attributed to a first-order phase transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma, can also be reproduced by non-equilibrium effects in a purely hadronic transport calculation, meaning the feature is not a unique signature of deconfinement.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid Boltzmann+hydrodynamics approach based on the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model with an intermediate (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic stage. They compared two freeze-out prescriptions (isochronous freeze-out and gradual freeze-out) and three equations of state (hadron gas, bag model with a strong first-order phase transition, and a chiral model) for central Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions from 2 to 160 AGeV, without adjusting parameters across energies.

## Key numbers

- Pion multiplicity is insensitive to freeze-out procedure; mean transverse mass at higher energies is decreased when gradual freeze-out is applied.
- Kaon production is enhanced by roughly 10% in the gradual freeze-out scenario due to higher average temperatures at the transition point.
- The bag model equation of state decreases the mean transverse mass of pions at high energies compared to the hadron gas equation of state.
- The hybrid model with a hadron gas equation of state overestimates the mean transverse mass, especially at higher energies.
- The pure transport calculation (UrQMD-2.3) reproduces the flattening in the intermediate energy regime best for pions and protons.
- The gradual freeze-out at an energy density of 5 times the ground state energy density (5 epsilon_0) provides the best description of the data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Van Hove (1982) and others who proposed the mean transverse momentum excitation function as a signal for the deconfinement phase transition, and directly addresses a suggestion by Ivanov (2006) that the step-like behavior might be a relic of freeze-out treatment rather than a unique QGP signal. The hybrid approach enables systematic comparison of different equations of state within the same framework, and the results suggest that distinguishing between a first-order phase transition and non-equilibrium effects requires additional observables such as elliptic flow or the R_O/R_S ratio.
