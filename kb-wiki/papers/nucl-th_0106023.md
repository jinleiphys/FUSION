> To appear in the proceedings of Quark Matter 2001, the 15th International Conference on Ultra-Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions, January 15-20, 2001, Stony Brook, NY

# Diffractive Dijet Production and Nuclear Shadowing in pA Interactions

## Key claim

The breakdown of factorization observed between diffractive events in ep (HERA) and pp̄ (Tevatron) implies that nuclear shadowing in pA collisions is dramatically reduced when the diffractive cross section is normalized using a scheme that reproduces the pp̄ data, rather than using the naive triple Pomeron form that describes ep diffraction.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear shadowing correction term δσ_pA using the Gribov (1969) formalism for elastic and inelastic shadowing, which integrates the single diffractive pp cross section over the coherence length and nuclear density. They then derive a formula for the ratio R(M²) of diffractive dijet production in pA relative to pp, based on an expansion of the hadronic state into diffractive eigenstates (Grassberger 1977, Miettinen 1978), and evaluate this ratio for both proton and virtual photon probes at √s = 100 GeV.

- The Pomeron intercept is parameterized as α_P(t) = 1 + ε + 0.25t, where ε ~ 0.08–0.10, and s_0 = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear shadowing from γ*A interactions (reviewed by Piller and Weise) to pA collisions, using the breakdown of factorization observed between ep diffraction at HERA and pp̄ diffraction at the Tevatron (Collins 1998, Abramowicz 2000). The authors apply the normalization scheme of Goulianos (1999) for the diffractive flux, which makes the cross section constant at high energies, to show that nuclear shadowing in pA is much weaker than naive triple Pomeron expectations. The results motivate future measurements of diffractive dijet production and nuclear shadowing in pA interactions at RHIC energies to better understand diffraction and the quark-gluon wave function of the nucleus.
