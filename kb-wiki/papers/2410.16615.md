# Double $Ď•$ Production in $\bar{p}p$ Reactions Near Threshold

> We employ an effective Lagrangian approach to investigate double $Ď•$ production in the $\bar{p}p$ reaction near the threshold and describe a notable violation of the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule in this reaction process through hadronic degrees of freedom, using the currently available theoretical and experimental information. The ground-state nucleon and its $s$-wave resonances, i.e., $N^*(1535,1650,1895)$, are taken into account in the $t$- and $u$-channel tree-level diagrams. The pentaquark-like nucleon resonance $P_s(2071,3/2^-)$ is also considered. In the $s$-channel diagrams, scalar and tensor mesons are incorporated, specifically $f_0(2020,2100,2200)$ and $f_2(1950,2010,2150)$, respectively, along with the pseudoscalar meson $Î·(2225)$. Our calculations suggest that contributions from the $N$ and $N^*$ resonances significantly enhance the cross-section near the threshold. At the same time, those from the $f_0$ and $f_2$ mesons produce distinctive peak structures around $W=2.2$ GeV, qualitatively reproducing the JETSET experimental data. The openings of the $\barÎ›Î›$ and $\barÎŁÎŁ$ channels lead to the cusp structures, which can explain the nontrivial structures in the cross-section at $W\approx2M_Î›$ and $2M_ÎŁ$ observed in the data. It turns out that contributions from the $Î·$ and $P_s$ resonances are almost negligible. Additionally, polarization observables such as the spin density matrix element (SDME) provide crucial insights into the individual hadronic processes involved in this reaction.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the large violation of the OZI rule observed in the JETSET experiment for $\bar{p}p\to\phi\phi$ can be described using only hadronic degrees of freedom (nucleon and nucleon-resonance exchanges in $t$- and $u$-channels, plus scalar and tensor meson exchanges in the $s$-channel), without invoking explicit quark-gluon or glueball contributions, provided that the cusp effects from the opening of $\bar{\Lambda}\Lambda$ and $\bar{\Sigma}\Sigma$ channels are included.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation, computing $s$-, $t$-, and $u$-channel Feynman diagrams for $\bar{p}p\to\phi\phi$. The $t$- and $u$-channel amplitudes included ground-state nucleon ($N$) and $s$-wave nucleon resonances $N^*(1535,1650,1895)$ plus a pentaquark-like resonance $P_s(2071,3/2^-)$; the $s$-channel included scalar mesons $f_0(2020,2100,2200)$, tensor mesons $f_2(1950,2010,2150)$, and the pseudoscalar $\eta(2225)$. Cusp structures from $\bar{\Lambda}\Lambda$ and $\bar{\Sigma}\Sigma$ loop diagrams were added to describe nontrivial structures in the cross-section. Phenomenological form factors with cutoff masses were used, and coupling constants were taken from chiral unitary model calculations and the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers
- The measured JETSET cross-section for $\bar{p}p\to\phi\phi$ is $(2-4)\,\mu$b for incoming antiproton momenta from 1.1 to 2.0 GeV/$c$, which is two orders of magnitude larger than the 10 nb expected from $\phi$-$\omega$ mixing.
- The $\phi NN$ coupling constant used is $g_{\phi NN} = -1.47$, about 10% of the strangeless coupling $g_{\omega NN} = 10.4$ from Nijmegen potential models.
- The $\phi NN^*$ coupling constants for the three $s$-wave resonances are: $N^*(1535)$: $1.4+i2.2$; $N^*(1650)$: $4.1-i2.7$ and $4.5+i5.2$ (two states); $N^*(1895)$: $2.1+i1.8$ and $0.9-i0.2$.
- The reduced coupling constants for scalar and tensor mesons are $g_{(S,P,T)} = 0.115$ for the $f_0$ mesons and $-0.1$ for the $f_2$ mesons.
- The cutoff masses for hadronic form factors are $\Lambda_{h,\mathrm{loop}} = (550,300)$ MeV, with reduced couplings $g_{\bar{\Lambda}\Lambda,\bar{\Sigma}\Sigma} = (2,1.2)$.
- The $\eta(2225)$ couplings are $g_{\eta(\phi\phi, NN)} = (-4.062, 0.5)$.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Shi et al. (2010) and Xie et al. (2014, 2007), which included only the $N^*(1535)$ exchange in the $t$-channel and either $f_0$ or $f_2$ in the $s$-channel. The present paper adds additional nucleon resonances ($N^*(1650,1895)$ and $P_s(2071)$) and multiple scalar and tensor mesons, plus cusp effects from $\bar{\Lambda}\Lambda$ and $\bar{\Sigma}\Sigma$ loops. The results qualitatively reproduce the JETSET experimental data and provide predictions for spin-density matrix elements (SDMEs) that could be tested in future experiments to validate the reaction mechanisms.
