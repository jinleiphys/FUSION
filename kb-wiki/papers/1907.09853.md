# Asymmetry for tensor $t_{2j}$ and vector $t_{1i}$ polarizations with taking into account the deuteron wave function in coordinate space

> The analytic forms of deuteron wave function in coordinate space were applied for theoretical calculations of full set asymmetries of tensor $t_{2j}$ and vector $t_{1i}$ polarizations. Nucleon-nucleon realistic phenomenological potentials of Nijmegen group (Nijm1, Nijm2, Nijm93, Reid93) and Argonne group (Argonne v18) as well as other widely used and popular potentials (OBEPC, MT, Paris) are used for numerical calculations. The angular asymmetry is calculated in the range of momentums 0-7 fm$^{ - 1}$. The values of the asymmetry $t_{ij,}$ by polarization type and with each other, are analysed. This is the angular-momentum dependence of values vector $t_{1i} (p,θ_e )$ and tensor $t_{2i} (p,θ_e )$ polarizations in 3D format at momentums 0-7 fm$^{-1}$ and scattering angles 1-180 degrees were calculated and compared for Reid93 potential. The perspectives of further application of the obtained results for calculating the values of the cross-sections, asymmetries and other characteristics of processes with the participation of a deuteron are discussed.

# Asymmetry for tensor \(t_{2j}\) and vector \(t_{1i}\) polarizations with taking into account the deuteron wave function in coordinate space

## Key claim
The paper presents a complete theoretical calculation of the full set of angular and angular-momentum asymmetries for tensor \(t_{2j}\) and vector \(t_{1i}\) polarizations using analytic forms of the deuteron wave function in coordinate space, but the results are not compared with experimental data, the author explicitly states that theory and experiment are not compared in this work, and the novelty lies in the complete presentation of asymmetries across multiple nucleon-nucleon potentials rather than validation against measurements.

## Method
The author used analytic parameterizations of the deuteron wave function in coordinate space (Eqs. 1-4) for eight nucleon-nucleon potentials: Nijm1, Nijm2, Nijm93, Reid93, Argonne v18, OBEPC, MT, and Paris. The tensor \(t_{2j}\) and vector \(t_{1i}\) polarizations were calculated from the deuteron electromagnetic form factors (charge \(G_C\), quadrupole \(G_Q\), and magnetic \(G_M\)) using standard formulas (Eqs. 14-18) that relate these polarizations to the form factors and scattering angle \(\theta_e\). The angular asymmetry was computed over a momentum range of 0-7 fm\(^{-1}\) and scattering angles of 1-180 degrees, with 3D format results presented specifically for the Reid93 potential.

## Key numbers
- Momentum range for calculations: 0-7 fm\(^{-1}\)
- Scattering angle range: 1-180 degrees
- Minimum values for vector polarization \(t_{10}\) and \(t_{11}\) are located in the momentum region \(p = 4\) to 6 fm\(^{-1}\), respectively
- Minimum value for tensor polarization \(t_{20}\) is in the momentum region \(p = 3-4\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Maximum and minimum for tensor polarizations \(t_{21}\) and \(t_{22}\) occur at \(p \approx 4\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- The value of \(t_{20}\) has a constant limit value of 0.3-0.7 in the absence of angle asymmetry
- Tensor polarization \(t_{22}\) is an order of magnitude smaller than \(t_{20}\) and \(t_{21}\) in contribution to the cross-section

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of deuteron polarization asymmetries by Darwish (2009, 2017) and the author's own prior studies for Argonne v18 (IJARPS 2018) and Reid93 (Zhaba 2017) potentials, which had only partially studied the angle and impulse asymmetry. The paper provides the first complete angular and angular-momentum asymmetry results for all eight potentials simultaneously, and the 3D format presentation of polarizations in the \(p\theta_e\) plane is original for these nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results can be used to calculate cross-sections, asymmetries, and other characteristics of processes involving deuterons, and could be systematized together with the angular-momentum dependence for vector and tensor asymmetries in elastic electron-deuteron scattering studied by Gakh (2012) and spin correlation coefficients in lepton-deuteron elastic scattering by Gakh (2014).
