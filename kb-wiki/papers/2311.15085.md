# Corrections to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation in nonlinear bosonized theory: Application to $g_A^L$ in nuclei

> We calculated in nonlinear bosonized theory $1/\bar{N}$ corrections to the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point (FLFP) axial-vector coupling constant in nuclear matter $g_A^L\approx 1$ to which the Landau parameter $F_1^ω$ predominantly contributes. We obtain the correction to $F_1^ω$ to calculate the correction $δg_A^L$ to the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A^L$ at the nuclear saturation density. It comes out to be extremely small, $δg_A^L\sim O(10^{-4})$. We discuss how the "dilaton-limit fixed-point (DLFP)" result $g_A=1$ can be preserved from finite nuclei to high densities relevant to massive neutron stars and its possible impact on $0νββ$ decay processes involved in going beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates $1/\bar{N}$ corrections to the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point (FLFP) axial-vector coupling constant $g_A^L \approx 1$ in nuclear matter, finding that the correction $\delta g_A^L$ is extremely small ($\sim O(10^{-4})$), meaning the FLFP prediction remains essentially unchanged at normal nuclear matter density despite corrections beyond the fixed-point approximation.

## Method

The authors apply nonlinear bosonization of Fermi surfaces using the coadjoint orbit method, originally developed for condensed matter systems, to nuclear interactions described by a generalized chiral effective field theory (G$n$EFT) that incorporates hidden local symmetry (vector mesons $\rho$, $\omega$) and hidden scale symmetry (dilaton $\sigma_d$). They compute $1/\bar{N}$ corrections to the $\omega$ meson propagator (where $\bar{N} = p_F/\Lambda$ with $\Lambda$ the cutoff relative to the Fermi surface), which modifies the Landau parameter $F_1^\omega$ and consequently the nucleon mass scaling, then propagate this correction through the relation connecting $g_A^L$ to the Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

- The $1/\bar{N}$ correction to the axial-vector coupling constant at nuclear saturation density $n_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is $\delta g_A^L = 1.8 \times 10^{-4}$, obtained with cutoff $\Lambda \sim 10$ MeV and coupling $g_\omega = 10.15$.
- The leading-order polarization tensor $\Pi_{ii} = n/m_N^\sigma \sim 10^3$ MeV$^2$, while the correction $\delta\Pi_{ii}$ is of order a tenth of MeV$^2$, explaining the smallness of $\delta g_A^L$.
- The correction $\delta g_A^L$ is nearly linear in the cutoff $\Lambda$ over the range shown (approximately 0 to 30 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the FLFP prediction $g_A^L \approx 1$ from Friman et al. (1996) and Rho (2021) by computing corrections beyond the fixed-point approximation using the nonlinear bosonization technique of Delacretaz et al. (2022). The result confirms that the "quenched" $g_A^* \approx 1$ observed in nuclei (Wilkinson, 1973) remains robust against $1/\bar{N}$ corrections, and the authors discuss implications for the dilaton-limit fixed-point (DLFP) at high densities relevant to neutron stars and for $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay searches beyond the Standard Model.
