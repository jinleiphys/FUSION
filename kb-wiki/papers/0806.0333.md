# Ab initio coupled-cluster and configuration interaction calculations for 16-O using V_UCOM

> Using the ground-state energy of 16-O obtained with the realistic V_UCOM interaction as a test case, we present a comprehensive comparison of different configuration interaction (CI) and coupled-cluster (CC) methods, analyzing the intrinsic advantages and limitations of each of the approaches. In particular, we use the importance-truncated (IT) CI and no-core shell model (NCSM) schemes with up to 4-particle-4-hole (4p4h) excitations as well as the size extensive CC methods with a complete treatment of one- and two-body clusters (CCSD) and a non-iterative treatment of connected three-body clusters via the completely renormalized correction to the CCSD energy defining the CR-CC(2,3) approach. We discuss the impact of the center-of-mass contaminations, the choice of the single-particle basis, and size-extensivity on the resulting energies. When the IT-CI and IT-NCSM methods include the 4p4h excitations and when the CC calculations include the 1p1h, 2p2h, and 3p3h clusters, as in the CR-CC(2,3) approach, we observe an excellent agreement among the different methodologies. This shows that despite their individual limitations, the IT-CI, IT-NCSM, and CC methods can provide precise and consistent ab initio nuclear structure predictions. Furthermore, the IT-CI, IT-NCSM, and CC ground-state energy values obtained with 16-O are in good agreement with the experimental value, proving that the V_UCOM two-body interaction allows for a realistic description of binding energies for heavier nuclei and that all of the methods used in this study account for most of the relevant particle correlation effects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that importance-truncated configuration interaction (IT-CI), importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM), and coupled-cluster (CC) methods yield consistent ground-state energies for 16-O when the IT-CI and IT-NCSM include up to 4p4h excitations and the CC calculations include up to 3p3h clusters via the CR-CC(2,3) approach, but this agreement depends on adding Davidson extensivity corrections to the IT-CI energies and accounting for connected three-body clusters in the CC calculations.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio calculations for the 16-O ground state using the V_UCOM two-body interaction, comparing three many-body approaches: importance-truncated configuration interaction (IT-CI) and importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) with up to 4p4h excitations, and coupled-cluster methods including CCSD (singles and doubles) and CR-CC(2,3) (with non-iterative triples corrections). The IT-CI and IT-NCSM calculations used an iterative importance-truncation scheme with thresholds between 2x10^-5 and 15x10^-5, followed by extrapolation to zero threshold, with multi-reference Davidson corrections added to approximately restore size extensivity. The CC calculations employed the exponential ansatz with truncation of the cluster operator at the T_2 level (CCSD) or with a completely renormalized correction for connected T_3 clusters (CR-CC(2,3)).

## Key numbers
- The ground-state energy of 16-O obtained with IT-CI(4p4h)+MRD, IT-NCSM(4p4h)+MRD, and CR-CC(2,3) methods agree with each other within uncertainties below 0.5 MeV after extrapolation to zero importance threshold
- The IT-CI and IT-NCSM calculations used importance thresholds in the range kappa_min = 2x10^-5 to 15x10^-5
- The largest dimensions of the importance-truncated model spaces for 16-O reached a factor of a few times 10^7 basis states
- The extrapolation uncertainties for the IT-CI and IT-NCSM energies are reported as always below 0.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the comparison of many-body methods for nuclear structure beyond the A=12 region, where Green's function Monte Carlo and no-core shell model calculations have been successful, into the A=16 mass region. The study directly compares importance-truncated CI and NCSM methods (Roth and collaborators, 2007-2008) with coupled-cluster approaches (Dean and collaborators, 2004-2006; Kowalski and collaborators, 2004) using the same V_UCOM interaction, showing that these complementary methods can provide consistent results. This enables future applications of these methods to heavier nuclei such as those in the A=40 mass region, where few other ab initio approaches are computationally tractable.
