# Utilizing high-$p_\perp$ theory and data to constrain the initial stages of quark-gluon plasma

> The scarce knowledge of the initial stages of quark-gluon plasma before the thermalization is mostly inferred through the low-$p_\perp$ sector. We propose a complementary approach in this report - the use of high-$p_\perp$ probes' energy loss. We study the effects of four commonly assumed initial stages, whose temperature profiles differ only before the thermalization, on high-$p_\perp$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ predictions. The predictions are based on our Dynamical Radiative and Elastic ENergy-loss Approach (DREENA) framework. We report insensitivity of $v_2$ to the initial stages, making it unable to distinguish between different cases. $R_{AA}$ displays sensitivity to the presumed initial stages, but current experimental precision does not allow resolution between these cases. We further revise the commonly accepted procedure of fitting the energy loss parameters, for each individual initial stage, to the measured $R_{AA}$. We show that the sensitivity of $v_2$ to various initial stages obtained through such procedure is mostly a consequence of fitting procedure, which may obscure the physical interpretations. Overall, the simultaneous study of high-$p_\perp$ observables, with unchanged energy loss parametrization and restrained temperature profiles, is crucial for future constraints on initial stages.

## Key claim

High-pT v2 is insensitive to the assumed initial-stage temperature profiles before thermalization, contrary to previous claims; the sensitivity reported in earlier studies is mostly an artifact of separately fitting energy loss parameters for each initial stage, not a genuine physical effect.

## Method

The authors used the DREENA-B framework (Dynamical Radiative and Elastic ENergy-loss Approach with 1D Bjorken expansion) to compute high-pT RAA and v2 for charged hadrons, D mesons, and B mesons in 30-40% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. They imposed four different temperature profiles (free-streaming, linear, constant, divergent) that are identical after thermalization at tau0=0.6 fm/c but differ before thermalization, then compared the resulting observables without any fitting parameters, and separately tested the common procedure of fitting a multiplicative factor in the energy loss to reproduce the free-streaming RAA.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time set at tau0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Initial QGP temperature T0 = 391 MeV for 30-40% centrality at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- QGP transition temperature assumed to be TC ≈ 160 MeV
- Chromomagnetic to chromoelectric mass ratio mu_M/mu_E = 0.5
- Heavy quark masses: Mc = 1.2 GeV, Mb = 4.75 GeV
- Fitted multiplicative factors C_fit_i: 1 (free-streaming), 0.87 (linear), 0.74 (constant), 0.67 (divergent)
- Predictions valid for pT >= 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends the DREENA framework (Djordjevic et al., 2016) to systematically test initial-stage sensitivity, contradicting the conclusion of IS3 (2015) that v2 can differentiate initial-stage scenarios. The results are consistent with earlier findings (Djordjevic et al., 2018; Renk, 2012; Molnar, 2014) that high-pT RAA is sensitive only to averaged medium properties like average temperature. The study enables future constraints on initial stages by requiring simultaneous analysis of high-pT RAA and v2 with fixed energy loss parametrization and restrained temperature profiles, rather than separate fitting per scenario.
