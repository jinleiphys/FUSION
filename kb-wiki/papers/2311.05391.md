# Three-body unitary coupled-channel approach to radiative $J/ψ$ decays and $η(1405/1475)$

> Recent BESIII data on radiative $J/ψ$ decays from $\sim 10^{10}$ $J/ψ$ samples should significantly advance our understanding of the controversial nature of $η(1405/1475)$. This motivates us to develop a three-body unitary coupled-channel model for radiative $J/ψ$ decays to three-meson final states of any partial wave ($J^{PC}$). Basic building blocks of the model are bare resonance states such as $η(1405/1475)$ and $f_1(1420)$, and $πK$, $K\bar{K}$, and $πη$ two-body interactions that generate resonances such as $K^*(892)$, $K^*_0(700)$, and $a_0(980)$. This model reasonably fits $K_SK_Sπ^0$ Dalitz plot pseudo data generated from the BESIII's $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$ amplitude for $J/ψ\toγK_SK_Sπ^0$. The experimental branching ratios of $η(1405/1475)\toηππ$ and $η(1405/1475)\toγρ$ relative to that of $η(1405/1475)\to K\bar{K}π$ are simultaneously fitted. Our $0^{-+}$ amplitude is analytically continued to find three poles, two of which correspond to $η(1405)$ on different Riemann sheets of the $K^*\bar{K}$ channel, and the third one for $η(1475)$. This is the first pole determination of $η(1405/1475)$ and, furthermore, the first-ever pole determination from analyzing experimental Dalitz plot distributions with a manifestly three-body unitary coupled-channel framework. Process-dependent $ηππ$, $γπ^+π^-$, and $πππ$ lineshapes of $J/ψ\toγ(0^{-+})\to γ(ηππ)$, $γ(γρ)$, and $γ(πππ)$ are predicted, and are in reasonable agreement with data. A triangle singularity is shown to play a crucial role to cause the large isospin violation of $J/ψ\toγ(πππ)$.

## Key claim
The paper develops a three-body unitary coupled-channel model for radiative $J/\psi$ decays that simultaneously fits $K_SK_S\pi^0$ Dalitz plot pseudodata and branching ratio data, finding three poles in the $0^{-+}$ amplitude, two corresponding to $\eta(1405)$ on different Riemann sheets of the $K^*\bar{K}$ channel and one for $\eta(1475)$, but the analysis is performed on pseudodata generated from the BESIII amplitude rather than actual experimental data, and the results depend on the specific model choices for the $a_0(980)$ resonance.

## Method
The authors constructed a three-body unitary coupled-channel model for radiative $J/\psi$ decays to three-meson final states, incorporating bare resonance states ($\eta(1405/1475)$, $f_1(1420)$) and two-body interactions ($\pi K$, $K\bar{K}$, $\pi\eta$) that generate resonances such as $K^*(892)$, $K^*_0(700)$, and $a_0(980)$. The model was fitted to $K_SK_S\pi^0$ Dalitz plot pseudodata generated from the BESIII $0^{-+}$ amplitude for $J/\psi\to\gamma K_SK_S\pi^0$, along with experimental branching ratios of $\eta(1405/1475)\to\eta\pi\pi$ and $\eta(1405/1475)\to\gamma\rho$ relative to $\eta(1405/1475)\to K\bar{K}\pi$, using a $\chi^2$-minimization procedure with bootstrap resampling for statistical uncertainty estimation.

## Key numbers
- The pseudodata contains approximately $1.23 \times 10^5$ events, consistent with BESIII data statistics
- The $E$ range of 1300 to 1600 MeV is divided into 30 bins of 10 MeV width each
- The $K_SK_S\pi^0$ Dalitz plot is binned into $50 \times 50$ bins in the ranges $(0.95\ {\rm GeV})^2 \le m^2_{K_SK_S} \le (1.50\ {\rm GeV})^2$ and $(0.60\ {\rm GeV})^2 \le m^2_{K_S\pi^0} \le (1.15\ {\rm GeV})^2$
- The experimental ratio $\Gamma[J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405/1475)\to\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)] / \Gamma[J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405/1475)\to\gamma(\eta\pi^+\pi^-)] = 6.8$ to $11.9$, derived from $(2.8\pm 0.6)\times 10^{-3}$ and $(3.0\pm 0.5)\times 10^{-4}$
- The experimental ratio $\Gamma[J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405/1475)\to\gamma(\rho^0\gamma)] / \Gamma[J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405/1475)\to\gamma(K\bar{K}\pi)] = 0.015$ to $0.043$
- The $f_0(980)$ width in $\pi\pi$ invariant mass distributions from BESIII data is approximately 10 MeV, narrower than the approximately 50 MeV seen in other processes
- The $\rho$ width used in the model is $\Gamma_\rho = 150$ MeV (constant)
- The vector-meson dominance coupling uses $g_\rho^2/4\pi = 2.2$ and $e^2/4\pi \simeq 1/137$

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Wu et al. (2012, 2013), Aceti et al. (2012), and Du et al. (2019), who proposed that triangle singularities from $K^*\bar{K}K$ loops could explain the large isospin violation in $\eta(1405/1475)\to\pi\pi\pi$ decays and process-dependent lineshapes with a single state. The present analysis improves upon the BESIII amplitude analysis of $J/\psi\to\gamma(K_SK_S\pi^0)$ (2023), which used Breit-Wigner amplitudes that do not respect unitarity and cannot properly handle resonances near thresholds or overlapping states. The pole extraction method builds on the rigorous three-body unitary approach used for $a_1(1260)$ pole extraction from $\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau$ by the GWU group (2018), but extends it to include coupled channels and fit Dalitz plot distributions rather than projected invariant mass distributions. This work enables future analyses of high-statistics Dalitz plot data from BESIII and other facilities using manifestly three-body unitary frameworks, which will be increasingly important for extracting resonance poles from inelastic kinematical regions.
