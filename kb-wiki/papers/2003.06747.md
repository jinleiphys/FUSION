# Collision system size scan of collective flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Initial geometrical distribution and fluctuation can affect the collective expansion in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This effect may be more evident in small system (such as B + B) than in large one (Pb + Pb). This work presents the collision system dependence of collective flows and discusses about effects on collective flows from initial fluctuations in a framework of a multiphase transport model. The results shed light on system scan on experimental efforts to small system physics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the response of collective flow to initial geometry asymmetry (e.g., \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\), \(v_3/\varepsilon_3\)) increases with collision system size, indicating higher transfer efficiency from initial geometry to final momentum in larger systems, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that it is drawn from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model simulation at a single center-of-mass energy (6.73 TeV) and for most central collisions only, and the extracted viscosity-related parameter \(\beta\) differs between linear and nonlinear modes.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (version 2.26t7b) to simulate the most central collisions (\(b=0\)) of seven systems from \(^{10}\text{B}+^{10}\text{B}\) to \(^{208}\text{Pb}+^{208}\text{Pb}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.73\) TeV. They calculated harmonic flow coefficients \(v_n\) (\(n=2,3,4\)) via two-particle correlation with a \(\Delta\eta\) gap and two-particle cumulants, and computed initial geometry eccentricity coefficients \(\varepsilon_n\) from the participant distribution. The response ratios \(v_n/\varepsilon_n\) were then fitted to a hydrodynamic acoustic scaling formula to extract the parameter \(\beta\), which is related to the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\).

## Key numbers

- Seven collision systems were simulated: \(^{10}\text{B}+^{10}\text{B}\), \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{16}\text{O}+^{16}\text{O}\), \(^{20}\text{Ne}+^{20}\text{Ne}\), \(^{40}\text{Ca}+^{40}\text{Ca}\), \(^{96}\text{Zr}+^{96}\text{Zr}\), and \(^{208}\text{Pb}+^{208}\text{Pb}\), all at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.73\) TeV.
- Event numbers ranged from \(2.3\times10^5\) for \(^{10}\text{B}+^{10}\text{B}\) to \(3.5\times10^3\) for \(^{208}\text{Pb}+^{208}\text{Pb}\).
- The extracted \(\beta\) from fitting \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\), \(v_3/\varepsilon_3\), and \(v_4^L/\varepsilon_4^L\) was \(0.641\pm0.002\) (two-particle correlation) and \(0.626\pm0.004\) (two-particle cumulant).
- The extracted \(\beta\) from fitting \(v_{4,22}/\varepsilon_{4,22}\) was \(0.281\pm0.031\).
- For comparison, the STAR experiment at RHIC reported \(\beta=0.82\pm0.02\) from fitting \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\) in small and large systems at top RHIC energies.
- The screening mass \(\mu\) in the partonic matter was set to \(2.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) in this work, versus \(3.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) in a prior AMPT study at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective flow from large systems (Au+Au, Pb+Pb) to a systematic scan of small systems (B+B, C+C, O+O, Ne+Ne, Ca+Ca, Zr+Zr) using the AMPT model, building on prior theoretical work on initial geometry fluctuations and flow (e.g., the AMPT initial fluctuation studies by the same group, and hydrodynamic calculations by others). It is motivated by experimental programs at RHIC-STAR and LHC-ALICE investigating small-system physics, and by the open question of whether matter created in different-sized systems undergoes similar dynamical processes with similar viscosity. The results suggest that a system scan experiment could probe the transformation efficiency from initial geometry to final momentum and the viscous properties of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly for higher-order nonlinear modes.
