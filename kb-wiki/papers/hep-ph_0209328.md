# Leading particles and diffractive spectra in the Interacting Gluon Model

> We discuss the leading particle spectra and diffractive mass spectra from the novel point of view, namely by treating them as particular examples of the general energy flow phenomena taking place in the multiparticle production processes. We argue that they show a high degree of universality what allows for their simple description in terms of the Interacting Gluon Model developed by us some time ago.

## Key claim

The Interacting Gluon Model (IGM) can describe leading particle spectra and diffractive mass spectra as universal energy-flow phenomena, provided that the diffractive component is properly incorporated and that the gluonic energy-momentum fraction is adjusted to the specific hadron (e.g., \(p \simeq 0.5\) for nucleons but \(p_{I\!P} \simeq 0.05\) for the Pomeron and \(p^{J/\Psi} \simeq 0.033\) for charmonium).

## Method

The IGM treats high-energy hadron collisions as an incoherent sum of independent binary gluon-gluon collisions, with valence quarks passing through essentially undisturbed. The probability distribution \(\chi(x,y)\) for forming a central fireball carrying momentum fractions \(x\) and \(y\) from the two projectiles is computed from a spectral function \(\omega(x',y')\) that includes soft and semihard gluonic cross sections and gluon structure functions \(G(x) \sim 1/x\). For single diffractive (SD) and double Pomeron exchange (DPE) processes, the phase space is restricted (e.g., \(y_{\text{max}} = M_X^2/s\) for SD) and the gluonic energy-momentum fraction \(p\) is reduced to \(p_{I\!P} \simeq 0.05\); the leading particle spectrum \(F(x_L)\) is then obtained by summing non-diffractive and diffractive contributions weighted by the diffractive fraction \(\alpha\).

## Key numbers

*   For the nucleon gluonic structure function, the default value used is \(m=5\) in \(G(x) = p(m+1)(1-x)^m/x\) with \(p=0.5\).
*   For single diffractive processes, the Pomeron energy-momentum fraction is \(p_{I\!P} \simeq 0.05\) (compared to \(p \simeq 0.5\) for all gluons in a nucleon).
*   For leading \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction, the best-fit gluonic energy-momentum fraction is \(p^{J/\Psi} = 0.033\), consistent with the non-relativistic estimate \(p^{J/\Psi} = (M_{J/\Psi} - 2 m_c)/M_{J/\Psi} \simeq 0.033\) using \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV and \(M_{J/\Psi} = 3.1\) GeV.
*   For DPE processes, the data from Brandt (2002) are fitted with a constant Pomeron-Pomeron cross section \(\sigma_{I\!P-I\!P} = 0.5\) mb (or 1 mb as an alternative).
*   The typical correlation length of soft gluon fields from lattice QCD is about 0.3 fm, and the typical instanton size in the QCD vacuum is also about 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the IGM, previously applied to inelasticity (Duraes 1993), multiparticle production in hydrodynamical models (Duraes 1994), leading charm production (Duraes 1995), and single diffraction in hadronic and photoproduction reactions (Duraes 1997a, 1997b), to new data from the ZEUS collaboration on leading proton spectra in \(ep\) collisions and to DPE mass spectra from Brandt (2002). The IGM provides a unified description of these processes without invoking glueball formation, using the same basic parameters and only adjusting kinematical limits and the gluonic energy-momentum fraction. The approach enables systematic predictions for leading particle spectra and diffractive mass distributions across hadronic, photoproduction, and DPE reactions, and suggests future tests in double diffraction dissociation and nuclear collisions.
