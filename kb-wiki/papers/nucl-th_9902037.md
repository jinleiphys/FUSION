# The low-lying quadrupole collective excitations of Ru and Pd isotopes

> Quadrupole excitations of even-even Ru and Pd isotopes are described within microscopic approach based on the general collective Bohr model which includes the effect of coupling with the pairing vibrations. The excitation energies and E2 transition probabilities observed in 104-114Ru and 106-110Pd are reproduced in the frame of the calculation containing no free parameters.

## Key claim
The authors reproduce the excitation energies and E2 transition probabilities of neutron-rich Ru and Pd isotopes using a microscopic Bohr model with no free parameters, but only after incorporating the average effect of coupling to pairing vibrations by shifting the pairing gap from its BCS equilibrium value to a smaller "most probable" value (typically about 0.7 times the equilibrium gap).

## Method
The authors start from the general collective Bohr hamiltonian for quadrupole excitations, which includes vibrational and rotational kinetic terms plus a collective potential calculated via the Strutinsky macroscopic-microscopic method with a Nilsson single-particle potential. They then add a separate collective pairing hamiltonian for protons and neutrons, solve for the pairing vibrational ground state at each deformation point, and extract the most probable pairing gap values (where the probability density is maximal). Finally, they diagonalize the quadrupole hamiltonian using these most probable gaps rather than the equilibrium BCS gaps, with all inertial functions and the collective potential evaluated at those shifted gap values; no additional free parameters are introduced beyond the standard Nilsson potential parameters and pairing strength fixed for all nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the most probable pairing gap Δ_vib to the equilibrium BCS gap Δ_eq is typically about 0.7.
- For the pairing strength, the authors use G = g₀ / N^(2/3) with g₀ = 0.26 ℏω₀, where N is the proton or neutron number.
- The calculations cover isotopes ¹⁰⁴–¹¹⁴Ru and ¹⁰⁶–¹¹⁰Pd.
- For ¹⁰⁴Ru, the calculated energies of positive-parity states agree well with experiment; the 8⁺₂ level discrepancy is attributed to a mixture of two-particle mode.
- For ¹⁰⁶–¹¹⁴Ru, the calculated ground-state band E2 transition probabilities tend to lie below the few available experimental data points.
- For ¹⁰⁶–¹¹⁰Pd, the ground-state bands and γ bands with their characteristic staggering are reproduced very well; the 0⁺₂ bands in ¹⁰⁸Pd and ¹¹⁰Pd show larger discrepancies attributed to intruder states.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic approach (Zajac et al., 1998) from neutron-deficient Te, Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd isotopes to the neutron-rich A≈100 region. It directly addresses the long-standing failure of the microscopic Bohr model to reproduce experimental level densities (Rohozinski, 1977), which was attributed by Piasecki (1993) to the neglect of pairing vibrations. The results are compared with phenomenological fits using the rigid triaxial rotor model (Sharma, 1994; Troltenier, 1996), the rotation-vibration model (Lunardi, 1995), and the generalized collective model (Troltenier, 1996) which require 8 or more fitted parameters per nucleus, as well as with IBA-2 calculations (Isacker, 1980; Svensson, 1989) using 15 or more free parameters. The work enables future improvements by including explicit mixing terms between quadrupole and pairing variables in the collective hamiltonian.
