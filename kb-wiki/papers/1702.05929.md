# Decay angular distributions of $K^*$ and $D^*$ vector mesons in pion-nucleon scattering

> The production mechanisms of open strangeness ($K^*$) and open charm ($D^*$) vector mesons in $π^- p$ scattering, namely, $π^- +p \to K^{*0} + Λ$ and $π^- + p \to D^{*-} + Λ_c^+$, are investigated within the modified quark-gluon string model. In order to identify the major reaction mechanisms, we consider the subsequent decays of the produced vector mesons into two pseudoscalar mesons, i.e., $K^* \to K + π$ and $D^* \to D + π$. We found that the decay distributions and density matrix elements are sensitive to the production mechanisms and can be used to disentangle the vector trajectory and pseudoscalar trajectory exchange models. Our results for $K^*$ production are compared with the currently available experimental data and the predictions for $D^*$ production processes are presented as well. Our predictions can be tested at the present or planned experimental facilities.

# Decay angular distributions of $K^*$ and $D^*$ vector mesons in pion-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

The decay angular distributions and spin-density matrix elements of $K^* \to K\pi$ and $D^* \to D\pi$ are sensitive to the production mechanism and can distinguish vector-trajectory exchange from pseudoscalar-trajectory exchange, even though the unpolarized differential cross sections for the two mechanisms have similar $t$-dependence. The available data for $K^*$ production suggest that vector-trajectory exchange alone cannot fully explain the observations, implying a noticeable contribution from pseudoscalar-type exchange.

## Method

The authors employ the modified quark-gluon string model (QGSM) of Kaidalov and collaborators, representing the binary reaction amplitude as an effective Regge amplitude with trajectories and energy-scale parameters determined from planar quark diagram decomposition and unitarity. They compute spin-density matrix elements and decay angular distributions for the two-step reactions $\pi^- + p \to K^{*0} + \Lambda \to (K^+ \pi^-) + \Lambda$ and $\pi^- + p \to D^{*-} + \Lambda_c^+ \to (D^- \pi^0) + \Lambda_c^+$, comparing vector-meson trajectory exchange against pseudoscalar-meson trajectory exchange. The spin structure of the amplitudes is specified by "dressing" the spin-independent amplitude with spin factors carrying the symmetry of the exchanged Reggeon.

## Key numbers

- For $\pi^- + p \to K^{*0} + \Lambda$: vector trajectory $\alpha^V_{{\cal R}_{p\Lambda}}(t) = 0.414 + 0.707\,t$, energy scale $s^V_{0{\cal R}_{p\Lambda}} = 1.66$ GeV$^2$, $\bar s_{p\Lambda} = 1$ GeV$^2$, and coupling $g_0^2/4\pi \simeq 0.796$ determined from experimental data.
- For $\pi^- + p \to D^{*-} + \Lambda_c^+$: vector trajectory $\alpha^V_{{\cal R}_{p\Lambda_c}}(t) = -1.02 + 0.467\,t$, energy scale $s^V_{0{\cal R}_{p\Lambda_c}} = 4.75$ GeV$^2$, $\bar s_{p\Lambda_c}=1$ GeV$^2$.
- Pseudoscalar trajectories: $\alpha^{\rm PS}_{{\cal R}_{p\Lambda}}(t) = -0.151 + 0.617\,t$ for $K^*$ production; $\alpha^{\rm PS}_{{\cal R}_{p\Lambda_c}}(t) = -1.611 + 0.439\,t$ for $D^*$ production.
- Tensor coupling constant $\kappa_{K^*p\Lambda} = 2.79$ from the Nijmegen soft-core potential, assumed equal for charm sector.
- Initial pion laboratory momentum: $p_\pi = 6$ GeV/$c$ for strangeness production, $p_\pi = 15$ GeV/$c$ for charm production.
- Pseudoscalar coupling adjusted to match vector-exchange cross section at $t = t_{\rm max}$: $(g^{\rm PS}_0)^2/4\pi = 1.1$ for $K^*$ and 13.5 for $D^*$ production.

## Context

This work extends the quark-gluon string model (Kaidalov 1980, 1982) previously applied to exclusive $\Lambda_c$ production in $pp$, $\bar{p}p$, and $\pi p$ collisions (Kiselev and Vasilevskaya 1994; Titov and Kampfer 2008; Kim et al. 2015, 2016) to the study of spin observables in $K^*$ and $D^*$ production. It directly compares with experimental data from the 1970s (Chung et al. 1972) for $K^{*0}$ production and finds that vector-exchange dominance is insufficient, requiring pseudoscalar contributions. The predictions for $D^*$ production can be tested at J-PARC and FAIR facilities, and the complementary study of strangeness and charm production is expected to illuminate the dependence of production mechanisms on quark mass scale.
