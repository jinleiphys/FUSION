# Elliptic flow in U+U collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76 TeV: Prediction from a hybrid approach

> We predict the elliptic flow parameter v_2 in U+U collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76 TeV using a hybrid model in which the evolution of the quark gluon plasma is described by ideal hydrodynamics with a state-of-the-art lattice QCD equation of state, and the subsequent hadronic stage by a hadron cascade model.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC will be significantly larger than in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and that the ratio \(v_2/\varepsilon_{\mathrm{part}}\) will reach \(\sim 0.26\)–\(0.30\) at LHC, exceeding the value of \(\sim 0.22\) at RHIC. This breaks the scaling behavior observed at RHIC energies, but the prediction is conditional on the use of ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice QCD equation of state and a hadron cascade, and the result depends on the assumed final charged particle multiplicity.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid model combining ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase with the JAM hadron cascade for the subsequent hadronic stage. Initial entropy distributions are generated using both Monte-Carlo Glauber (MC-Glauber) and Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) models, with parameters tuned to reproduce transverse momentum spectra in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. For U+U collisions, the prolate deformation of uranium nuclei is included via a Woods-Saxon distribution with quadrupole (\(\beta_2 = 0.28\)) and hexadecapole (\(\beta_4 = 0.093\)) deformation parameters. For Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, the MC-KLN model is used with the same parameters as at RHIC, but with three different assumptions for the final charged particle multiplicity in central collisions.

## Key numbers

- In 0-5% central U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the initial eccentricity \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{part}}\) reaches 0.146 (MC-Glauber) and 0.148 (MC-KLN).
- The maximum temperature in 0-5% central U+U collisions is \(T_0 = 367\) MeV (energy density \(e_0 = 33.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\)), compared to \(T_0 = 361\) MeV (\(e_0 = 31.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\)) in Au+Au collisions at the same energy and centrality.
- The transverse charged particle density in 0-5% central U+U collisions is 25.4 fm\(^{-2}\), only \(\sim 6\%\) larger than the 24.1 fm\(^{-2}\) in Au+Au collisions.
- In 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the maximum temperature is \(T_0 = 474\), 456, and 436 MeV (energy densities \(e_0 = 96.2\), 81.7, and 67.8 GeV/fm\(^3\)) for multiplicities \(dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/d\eta \sim 1600\), 1400, and 1200, respectively.
- The ratio \(v_2/\varepsilon_{\mathrm{part}}\) in central Pb+Pb collisions reaches \(\sim 0.26\)–\(0.30\), which is \(\sim 20\)–\(35\%\) larger than the value at RHIC (\(\sim 0.22\)).
- At LHC, the contribution of the hadronic cascade to the total \(v_2\) is less than 5% at most centralities, compared to 10-20% in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow scaling with transverse density at RHIC (Heinz 2004, Kolb 2000) by predicting \(v_2\) for U+U collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. The authors find that the scaling of \(v_2/\varepsilon_{\mathrm{part}}\) with transverse density observed at RHIC breaks at LHC energies, contradicting the expectation of a universal hydrodynamic limit. The results enable experimental tests at LHC and provide a baseline for future viscous hydrodynamic calculations, such as those by Luzum (2009), which used a different method to estimate \(v_2\).
