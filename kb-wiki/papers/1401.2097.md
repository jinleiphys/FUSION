# Order, Chaos and Quasi Symmetries in a First-Order Quantum Phase Transition

> We study the competing order and chaos in a first-order quantum phase transition with a high barrier. The boson model Hamiltonian employed, interpolates between its U(5) (spherical) and SU(3) (deformed) limits. A classical analysis reveals regular (chaotic) dynamics at low (higher) energy in the spherical region, coexisting with a robustly regular dynamics in the deformed region. A quantum analysis discloses, amidst a complicated environment, persisting regular multiplets of states associated with partial U(5) and quasi SU(3) dynamical symmetries.

## Key claim

In a first-order quantum phase transition with a high barrier, regular dynamics persists robustly in the deformed region even at energies far above the barrier, while the spherical region exhibits a transition from regular to chaotic dynamics with increasing energy; this coexistence of order and chaos is associated with partial U(5) and quasi SU(3) dynamical symmetries that survive despite strong symmetry-breaking interactions.

## Method

The authors study an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian that interpolates between the U(5) (spherical vibrator) and SU(3) (axially-deformed rotor) dynamical symmetry limits, with control parameters ρ and ξ driving a first-order quantum phase transition. They perform a classical analysis using Glauber coherent states to obtain a classical Hamiltonian constrained to L=0 vibrations, examining Poincaré surfaces of section to characterize regular and chaotic dynamics. The quantum analysis uses Peres spectral lattices (plotting expectation values of the d-boson number operator versus energy) for eigenstates with N=80 and N=50 bosons, and computes Shannon entropies in the U(5) and SU(3) bases to quantify the purity of eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- Barrier height at the critical point: V_bar/h_2 = 0.268, compared to V_bar/h_2 = 0.0018 in previous works
- Spinodal point: ρ* = 1/2; critical point: (ρ_c = 1/√2, ξ_c = 0); anti-spinodal point: ξ** = 1/3
- For large N, normal mode frequencies in the spherical phase: ε = 4h̄₂N; in the deformed phase: ε_β = 4h̄₂N(2ξ + 1) and ε_γ = 12h̄₂N
- Quantum analysis uses N=80 bosons for L=0 eigenstates and N=50 bosons for L=0,2,3,4 eigenstates at the critical point
- The U(5)-PDS solvable states include |N, n_d=τ=L=0⟩ with E_PDS = 0 and |N, n_d=τ=L=3⟩ with E_PDS = 12(N - 2 + 3ρ²)h̄₂
- The SU(3)-PDS solvable states include the ground band |N,(2N,0)K=0,L⟩ with E_PDS = 0 and γ^k bands |N,(2N-4k,2k)K=2k,L⟩ with E_PDS = 6k(2N - 2k + 1)h̄₂

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum chaos in first-order quantum phase transitions within the IBM framework (MacLev11, LevMac12, MacLev13) by introducing a high barrier at the critical point, which reveals a richer pattern of mixed regular and chaotic dynamics than earlier studies with extremely low barriers (AW93). The results demonstrate that partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) and quasi-dynamical symmetries (QDS), as previously discussed by Leviatan (2011) and Rowe (2004), can persist across a first-order QPT and provide a symmetry-based explanation for the survival of regular multiplets. The findings enable future studies of how these persisting regular structures manifest in experimental nuclear spectra and in other mesoscopic systems exhibiting shape coexistence, such as those discussed by Cejnar (2010).
