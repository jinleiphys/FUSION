# The $πρ$ Cloud Contribution to the $ω$ Width in Nuclear Matter

> The width of the $ω$ meson in cold nuclear matter is computed in a hadronic many-body approach, focusing on a detailed treatment of the medium modifications of intermediate $πρ$ states. The $π$ and $ρ$ propagators are dressed by their selfenergies in nuclear matter taken from previously constrained many-body calculations. The pion selfenergy includes $Nh$ and $Δh$ excitations with short-range correlations, while the $ρ$ selfenergy incorporates the same dressing of its $2π$ cloud with a full 3-momentum dependence and vertex corrections, as well as direct resonance-hole excitations; both contributions were quantitatively fit to total photo-absorption spectra and $πN\toρN$ scattering. Our calculations account for in-medium decays of type $ωN\to πN^{(*)}, ππN(Δ)$, and 2-body absorptions $ωNN\to NN^{(*)},πNN$. This causes deviations of the in-medium $ω$ width from a linear behavior in density, with important contributions from spacelike $ρ$ propagators. The $ω$ width from the $ρπ$ cloud may reach up to 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, with a moderate 3-momentum dependence. This largely resolves the discrepancy of linear $T$-$\varrho$ approximations with the values deduced from nuclear photoproduction measurements.

# The πρ Cloud Contribution to the ω Width in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The in-medium ω meson width from the πρ cloud may reach up to 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, with a moderate 3-momentum dependence, but the dominant uncertainty is due to the ρNN vertex form factor, which could not be accurately constrained from ρ properties alone. This largely resolves the discrepancy of linear T-ρ approximations with values deduced from nuclear photoproduction measurements, though the calculation does not include direct 3π couplings or ωN resonances (ω-sobars).

## Method

The ω selfenergy from the πρ decay channel is computed using a hadronic many-body approach with dressed π and ρ propagators in nuclear matter. The pion selfenergy includes P-wave nucleon-hole (NN⁻¹) and Delta-hole (ΔN⁻¹) excitations with short-range correlations parameterized by Migdal parameters, while the ρ selfenergy incorporates the same dressing of its 2π cloud with full 3-momentum dependence and vertex corrections, plus direct resonance-hole excitations (ρ-sobars). The ω width is obtained from the imaginary part of the selfenergy via the Lehmann representation, using spectral functions for both mesons.

## Key numbers

- Free ω → ρπ partial width: 3.6 MeV (about 1/2 of the total 3π width), obtained with coupling g_{ωρπ} = 1.9/f_π (f_π = 92 MeV)
- In-medium ω width at normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀) and zero 3-momentum: 130–200 MeV, with the range reflecting uncertainty in the ρNN form factor (Λ_{ρNN} = 0.3–0.6 GeV)
- Approximately 40% of the in-medium ω width at ρ₀ is generated by spacelike ρ modes (for Λ_{ρNN} = 0.3 GeV)
- Estimated direct 3π in-medium contribution at ρ₀: less than 20 MeV (for Λ_{πNN(Δ)} = 0.3 GeV)
- Free ρ → ππ width: approximately 150 MeV (with g_{ρππ} ≈ 6)
- Pion form factor cutoff: Λ_π = 0.3 GeV, constrained by πN → ρN scattering and nuclear photo-absorption
- Migdal parameters: g'₁₁ = 0.6, g'₁₂ = g'₂₂ = 0.2
- ρΔN coupling: f_{ρΔN}²/4π = 16.2, Λ_{ρΔN} = 0.7 GeV
- ρNN coupling: f_{ρNN}²/4π = 6.0, Λ_{ρNN} = 0.6 GeV (from γp → ρp analysis), varied down to 0.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends beyond the linear T-ρ approximation used in earlier calculations by Klingl (1997), Post (2000), Lutz (2001), and others, which could not reproduce the large in-medium widths (130–150 MeV from CBELSA/TAPS, above 200 MeV from CLAS) deduced from nuclear photoproduction measurements. The approach uses the same constrained many-body techniques previously applied to the ρ meson by Rapp (1997) and Urban (1998), which successfully described dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions and ρ broadening in nuclear photoproduction. The results are consistent with the very recent work of Ramos (2013), who reported Γ_ω^{med} = 129 ± 10 MeV at ρ₀ using similar methods but with a ρ spectral function exhibiting about a factor of 2 less broadening. This work enables future systematic analyses of vacuum ω scattering data to reduce uncertainties, and corroborates the hadronic many-body approach as a suitable tool for assessing in-medium hadron properties.
