# Charm production from photo-nucleon reaction in a hadronic model

> We study the total cross section for photo production of open charm hadrons near threshold using a hadronic Lagrangian. Both two-body final states involving $Λ_c$ and a charmed meson as well as three-body final states involving nucleon and a charm-anticharm meson pair are included. With appropriate cut-off parameters in the form factors at interaction vertices, the model gives a total cross section that is consistent with the measured data at center-of-mass energy of 6 GeV. The result is compared with the prediction from the leading-order perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Using a hadronic Lagrangian with appropriate cutoff parameters in the form factors, the model gives a total cross section for photoproduction of open charm hadrons that is consistent with the measured data at center-of-mass energy of 6 GeV, but at higher energies its value is much smaller than that measured experimentally or given by leading-order perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors used a gauged SU(4) flavor symmetric Lagrangian with empirical masses and coupling constants, introducing the photon via U_em(1) gauging. They calculated cross sections for both two-body final states (gamma N -> D-bar Lambda_c and gamma N -> D-bar* Lambda_c) and three-body final states (gamma N -> N D-bar D, gamma N -> N D-bar D*, and gamma N -> N D-bar* D*) using pion and rho meson exchange diagrams. Monopole and dipole form factors were introduced at interaction vertices, with cutoff parameters taken from previous studies of J/psi absorption by hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The reaction gamma N -> D-bar D* N (D-bar* D N) has the largest three-body cross section with a peak value of about 40 nb.
- The reaction gamma N -> D-bar D N has the smallest three-body cross section of only about 1 nb.
- For two-body final states, the peak cross section is about 30 nb for gamma N -> D-bar Lambda_c and about 50 nb for gamma N -> D-bar* Lambda_c.
- Two-body and three-body final states have comparable magnitudes around center-of-mass energy of about 5.7 GeV.
- At 6 GeV, the final state with a Lambda_c constitutes about 35% of the total cross section, consistent with experimental data from SLAC.
- The cutoff parameters used are Lambda_pi NN = 1.3 GeV, Lambda_rho NN = 1.4 GeV, and a universal dipole cutoff Lambda = 1.45 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective hadronic Lagrangian approach previously used by Liu et al. (2002) to study charmed meson scattering by hadrons and J/psi absorption by nucleons. The model provides another independent test of the hadronic model for determining cross sections involving heavy flavors at low energies, where perturbative QCD calculations (such as those by Peskin (1979) and others) become unreliable due to large higher-order corrections. The results will be useful for evaluating other low energy cross sections involving heavy flavored hadrons that can be studied at the Japanese Hadron Facility (JPARC) and the GSI future accelerator.
