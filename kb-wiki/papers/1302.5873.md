# Towards detailed tomography of high energy heavy-ion collisions by $γ$-jet

> Within a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with string melting scenario, the transverse momentum imbalance between prompt photon and jet is studied in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV. Jet loses more energy in more central collisions due to strong partonic interactions between jet parton shower and partonic matter, which is more significant than due to hadronic interactions only. The hadronization and final-state hadronic interactions have little influences on the imbalance. The imbalance ratio $x_{jγ}$ is sensitive to both production position and passing direction of $γ$-jet, which provides an opportunity to do detail $γ$-jet tomography on the formed partonic matter by selecting different $x_{jγ}$ ranges. It is also proposed that $γ$-hadron azimuthal correlation associated with photon+jet is a probe to see the medium responses to different $γ$-jet production configurations.

# Towards detailed tomography of high energy heavy-ion collisions by γ-jet

## Key claim

The transverse momentum imbalance ratio \(x_{j\gamma}\) between a prompt photon and its associated jet is sensitive to both the production position and the passing direction of the γ-jet pair within the AMPT model, which could enable detailed tomography of the partonic matter formed in heavy-ion collisions, but this sensitivity is model-dependent and may be weakened or lost in other theoretical frameworks.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string melting scenario to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}\) = 2.76 TeV, with a triggered γ-jet of \(p_{T}^{\gamma}\sim\) 60 GeV/c. Two sets of partonic interaction cross sections (0 mb and 1.5 mb) are applied to compare scenarios with hadronic interactions only versus partonic plus hadronic interactions. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-\(k_{t}\) algorithm with cone size \(R=0.3\), and the imbalance ratio \(x_{j\gamma}=p_{T}^{jet}/p_{T}^{\gamma}\) is analyzed as a function of production radius \(r\) and angle \(\theta\) relative to the collision center.

## Key numbers

- The averaged energy loss fraction of jets in central collisions is \(\sim\) 15% with partonic interactions (1.5 mb), decreasing to \(\sim\) 5% in peripheral collisions; with hadronic interactions only (0 mb), the loss is 4%–2%.
- The analysis uses a photon transverse momentum threshold of \(p_{T}^{\gamma} >\) 60 GeV/c, jet \(p_{T}^{jet} >\) 30 GeV/c, and pseudorapidity ranges \(|\eta^{\gamma}|<1.44\) and \(|\eta^{jet}|<1.6\).
- The imbalance ratio distributions are shown for four centrality bins in Pb+Pb collisions and compared to p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}\) = 2.76 TeV.
- The fraction \(R_{j\gamma}\) of photons with an associated jet above 30 GeV/c is reproduced only when partonic interactions (1.5 mb) are included.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of γ-jet tomography proposed by Qin (2012) and builds on experimental measurements from CMS (2012) and ATLAS (2012) showing a decreasing jet-to-photon momentum imbalance ratio from peripheral to central collisions. The AMPT model results suggest that selecting different \(x_{j\gamma}\) ranges can classify γ-jet events into punch-through, escaped, and tangential jet configurations, and that combining γ-hadron azimuthal correlations with these selections could probe medium excitations such as Mach-cone-like structures. The author notes that the geometry sensitivity of \(x_{j\gamma}\) may be model-dependent, referencing Renk (2009).
