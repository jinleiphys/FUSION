# Modification of mass formula by considering isospin effects

> We propose a semi-empirical nuclear mass formula based on the macroscopic-microscopic method in which the isospin and mass dependence of model parameters are investigated with the Skyrme energy density functional. The number of model parameters is considerably reduced compared with the finite range droplet model. The rms deviation with respect to 2149 measured nuclear masses is reduced by 21%, falls to 0.516 MeV. The new magic number N=16 in light neutron-rich nuclei and the shape coexistence phenomena for some nuclei have been examined with the model. The shell corrections of super-heavy nuclei are also predicted.

## Key claim

The authors propose a semi-empirical nuclear mass formula that reduces the root-mean-square (rms) deviation for 2149 measured nuclear masses to 0.516 MeV, a 21% improvement over the finite-range droplet model (FRDM), but this result depends on self-consistently incorporating isospin and mass dependence of model parameters derived from the Skyrme energy density functional.

## Method

The authors use a macroscopic-microscopic method where the macroscopic liquid-drop energy is described by a modified Bethe-Weizsacker formula with an isospin-dependent symmetry energy coefficient, and the microscopic shell correction is calculated via the Strutinsky procedure using an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential. The isospin and mass dependence of parameters such as the symmetry energy coefficient, deformation curvatures, and spin-orbit strength are determined from the Skyrme energy density functional with the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. The 13 independent model parameters are fitted to 2149 measured nuclear masses from the AME2003 database using downhill searching and simulated annealing algorithms.

## Key numbers

- Rms deviation for 2149 measured nuclear masses: 0.516 MeV (21% reduction compared to FRDM's 0.656 MeV)
- Rms deviation for 1988 measured neutron separation energies: 0.346 MeV (compared to FRDM's 0.399 MeV)
- Number of model parameters: 13 independent parameters (plus 2 for the Strutinsky procedure), compared to about 31 in FRDM
- Symmetry energy coefficient parameter c_sym: 29.2876 MeV (close to nuclear matter symmetry energy at saturation density)
- Shell correction of 24O: -4.6 MeV (supporting N=16 as a magic number in neutron-rich nuclei)
- Shell corrections for super-heavy nuclei around 292114: about -6 MeV (compared to about -8.9 MeV from FRDM)

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach of the finite-range droplet model (Moller 1995) by improving consistency between macroscopic and microscopic parts through isospin-dependent parameters derived from the Skyrme energy density functional. The model reduces the number of free parameters compared to FRDM while achieving better mass predictions, and it enables more reliable extrapolations to neutron-rich nuclei and super-heavy nuclei. The predicted shell corrections suggest a flat super-heavy island centered near Z=114 and N=178 rather than N=184, consistent with earlier work by Fiset and by Möller (1986), and the model reproduces the new magic number N=16 in light neutron-rich nuclei observed experimentally by Hoffman and others.
