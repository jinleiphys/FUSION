# Statistical measure of complexity of hard-sphere gas: applications to nuclear matter

> We apply the statistical measure of complexity, introduced by López-Ruiz, Mancini and Calbet to a hard-sphere dilute Fermi gas whose particles interact via a repulsive hard-core potential. We employ the momentum distribution of this system to calculate the information entropy, the disequilibrium and the statistical complexity. We examine possible connections between the particle correlations and energy of the system with those information and complexity measures. The hard-sphere model serves as a test bed for concepts about complexity.

## Key claim

The statistical complexity measure C_LMC, applied to the momentum distribution of a hard-sphere dilute Fermi gas, increases monotonically with the strength of particle correlations (parameterized by k_F c) and exhibits an approximately linear dependence on the ground-state energy per particle, suggesting that complexity can serve as a quantitative proxy for correlation strength in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employed the analytical momentum distribution n(k) for a hard-sphere Fermi gas, derived by Czyz and Gottfried (1961) and Sartor and Mahaux (1980), which is expressed as a power series in the dimensionless wound parameter y = k_F c. They computed the Shannon information entropy S_k, the Onicescu disequilibrium D_k, and the LMC complexity C = exp(S_k) * D_k in momentum space for nuclear matter (ν = 4 spin-isospin components). The results were fitted as functions of y and of the Fermi-surface discontinuity Z, and compared with the ground-state energy per particle from the low-density expansion of Baker (1982) and with the Low Order Approximation (LOA) method of Flynn (1984).

## Key numbers

- For the ideal Fermi gas (no correlations), the complexity C takes its minimal value C_0 = 1.
- The fitted complexity as a function of the correlation parameter y = k_F c is C(y) = 1 + 1.68358 y^1.67566.
- The fitted complexity as a function of the discontinuity Z is C(Z) = 1 + 1.94305 (1-Z)^0.83784.
- The energy per particle E/E_0 (in units of the ideal gas energy E_0 = (3/5)(ħ² k_F²)/(2m)) is approximately linear in C: e = -0.5847 + 1.6358 C.
- The maximum complexity considered for the interacting gas is not bounded; for the ideal gas the upper limit is C_max ≈ 1.5845.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of complexity in uniform Fermi systems (Moustakidis 2005) by applying the LMC measure to the hard-sphere model, which serves as a simple test bed for connecting information-theoretic quantities to nuclear-matter correlations. The results confirm and generalize the recent finding of an empirical connection between complexity and the specific heat of an ideal electron gas (Moustakidis 2010). The approach enables future quantitative studies linking statistical complexity to experimentally measurable quantities in nuclear matter and other fermionic systems, and complements the LOA method of Flynn (1984) for assessing correlation strength.
