# Limits on the neutrino mass from neutrinoless double-$β$ decay

> Neutrinoless double-$β$ decay is of fundamental importance for the determining neutrino mass. By combining a calculation of nuclear matrix elements within the framework of the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) with an improved calculation of phase space factors, we set limits on the average light neutrino mass and on the average inverse heavy neutrino mass (flavor violating parameter).

## Key claim

By combining nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) calculated with the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) and improved phase space factors, the authors set limits on the average light neutrino mass and the average inverse heavy neutrino mass (flavor violating parameter), but note that these limits depend on the free value of the axial vector coupling constant \(g_A = 1.269\) and that renormalization of \(g_A\) in nuclei could multiply the limits by a factor of 2-4.

## Method

The authors calculated NMEs for neutrinoless double-beta decay in all nuclei of interest using the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2), assuming the closure approximation and including finite nucleon size and short-range correlations via a Jastrow function. They combined these NMEs with improved phase space factors obtained by solving the Dirac equation for outgoing electrons in the presence of a charge distribution and including electron screening. The resulting half-lives were compared with current experimental limits to extract constraints on the average light neutrino mass \(\langle m_\nu \rangle\) and, for heavy neutrino exchange, on the flavor violating parameter \(\eta\) and the average heavy neutrino mass \(\langle m_{\nu_h} \rangle\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{76}\)Ge decay, the calculated half-life for \(\langle m_\nu \rangle = 1\) eV is \(1.19 \times 10^{24}\) yr; the experimental limit \(\tau_{1/2,\text{exp}}^{0\nu} > 1.9 \times 10^{25}\) yr (IGEX) yields \(\langle m_\nu \rangle < 0.25\) eV.
- For \(^{136}\)Xe decay, the calculated half-life for \(\langle m_\nu \rangle = 1\) eV is \(0.512 \times 10^{24}\) yr; the KamLAND-Zen limit \(\tau_{1/2,\text{exp}}^{0\nu} > 5.7 \times 10^{24}\) yr yields \(\langle m_\nu \rangle < 0.30\) eV, and the EXO limit \(\tau_{1/2,\text{exp}}^{0\nu} > 1.6 \times 10^{25}\) yr yields \(\langle m_\nu \rangle < 0.18\) eV.
- For heavy neutrino exchange in \(^{76}\)Ge, the calculated half-life for \(\eta = 2.75 \times 10^{-7}\) is \(0.190 \times 10^{24}\) yr; the IGEX limit gives \(|\eta| < 0.300 \times 10^{-7}\) and \(\langle m_{\nu_h} \rangle > 8.6\) GeV (assuming \(M_{WR} = 3.5\) TeV).
- For heavy neutrino exchange in \(^{136}\)Xe, the EXO limit gives \(|\eta| < 0.177 \times 10^{-7}\) and \(\langle m_{\nu_h} \rangle > 14.6\) GeV.
- The IBM-2 light-neutrino NME for \(^{76}\)Ge is 5.98, compared to 5.81 in QRPA (Simkovic et al.) and 2.81 in ISM (Menendez et al.), all with similar short-range correlation treatments.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the IBM-2 method for NMEs (Barea et al.) and improved phase space factors (Kotila et al.) to produce consistent half-life predictions and neutrino mass limits across 17 candidate nuclei. The IBM-2 results show close correspondence with QRPA calculations (Simkovic et al.) but are approximately a factor of 2 larger than ISM results (Menendez et al.), a difference the authors attribute primarily to model space size. The derived limits enable comparison with experimental programs including KamLAND-Zen, EXO, and IGEX, and indicate that only the degenerate neutrino mass region can be tested in the near future, with exploration of the inverted hierarchy requiring ton-scale experiments.
