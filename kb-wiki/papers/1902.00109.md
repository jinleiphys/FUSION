# Lévy imaging of elastic hadron-hadron scattering: Odderon and inner structure of the proton

> A novel model-independent Lévy imaging method is employed for reconstruction of the elastic $pp$ and $p\bar p$ scattering amplitudes at low and high energies. The four-momentum transfer $t$ dependent elastic slope $B(t)$, the nuclear phase $ϕ(t)$ as well as the excitation function of the shadow profile $P(b)$ have been extracted from data at ISR, Tevatron and LHC energies. We found qualitative differences in properties of $B(t)$ and $ϕ(t)$ between $pp$ and for $p\bar p$ collisions, that indicate an Odderon effect. A proton substructure has also been identified and found to have two different sizes, comparable to that of a dressed quark at the ISR and of a dressed diquark at the LHC energies, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that model-independent Lévy imaging of elastic hadron scattering data reveals qualitative differences in the elastic slope \(B(t)\) and nuclear phase \(\phi(t)\) between \(pp\) and \(p\bar p\) collisions, which indicate an Odderon effect; however, the claim is hedged by noting that the 7 TeV \(pp\) fit had a marginal confidence level of about 0.02%, and the authors suggest that analysis including the full covariance matrix or separate low- and large-\(t\) fits may be needed.

## Method

The authors applied a Lévy expansion method (with complex coefficients) to the elastic scattering amplitude, fitting a fourth-order series to published differential cross-section data for \(pp\) collisions at ISR (23.5–62.5 GeV) and LHC (7 and 13 TeV) energies, and for \(p\bar p\) collisions at Tevatron (1.96 TeV). From the fitted amplitude, they extracted the \(t\)-dependent elastic slope \(B(t)\), the nuclear phase \(\phi(t)\), and the shadow profile \(P(b)\) in impact-parameter space, using the eikonal formalism. The zeroth-order Lévy fits (\(d\sigma/dt = A \exp(-(R^2 |t|)^\alpha)\)) with fixed \(\alpha = 0.9\) were used to characterize the diffractive cone and tail regions separately.

## Key numbers

* The elastic slope in the tail region for \(pp\) at ISR energies (23.5–62.5 GeV) is \(B_{\rm tail}(pp|{\rm ISR}) \approx 2\) GeV\(^{-2}\), indicating a small proton substructure.
* The elastic slope in the tail region for \(pp\) at LHC energies (7 and 13 TeV) is \(B_{\rm tail}(pp|{\rm LHC}) \approx 5\) GeV\(^{-2}\), indicating a larger proton substructure.
* The non-exponentiality of the diffractive cone at \(\sqrt{s}=8\) TeV was confirmed by TOTEM as a more than \(7\sigma\) effect.
* The shadow profile \(P(b)\) at TeV energies reaches a nearly black region where \(P(b) \approx 1\), and this region increases with energy.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent Lévy imaging technique previously developed by Csörgő et al. (2018) for elastic scattering, building on earlier Lévy and Edgeworth expansions used in femtoscopy (Csörgő, 2000; De Kock, 2012). The results contradict a purely exponential description of the diffractive cone and support the existence of the Odderon, a C-odd three-gluon state proposed by Łukaszuk and Serbo (1973). The finding of two distinct proton substructure sizes (dressed-quark-like at ISR and dressed-diquark-like at LHC) enables further theoretical study of the energy evolution of hadron structure and motivates new measurements at intermediate energies between 0.5 and 1 TeV.
