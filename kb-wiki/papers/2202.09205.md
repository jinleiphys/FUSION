# Chiral plasma instability and inverse cascade from nonequilibrium left-handed neutrinos in core-collapse supernovae

> We show that the backreaction of left-handed neutrinos out of equilibrium on the matter sector induces an electric current proportional to a magnetic field even without a chiral imbalance for electrons in core-collapse supernovae. We derive the transport coefficient of this effect based on the recently formulated chiral radiation transport theory for neutrinos. This chiral electric current generates a strong magnetic field via the so-called chiral plasma instability, which could provide a new mechanism for the strong and stable magnetic field of magnetars. We also numerically study the physical origin of the inverse cascade of the magnetic energy in the magnetohydrodynamics including this current. Our results indicate that incorporating the chiral effects of neutrinos would drastically modify the hydrodynamic evolutions of supernovae, which may also be relevant to the explosion dynamics.

# Chiral plasma instability and inverse cascade from nonequilibrium left-handed neutrinos in core-collapse supernovae

## Key claim
The backreaction of nonequilibrium left-handed neutrinos on the matter sector induces an electric current proportional to the magnetic field even without a chiral imbalance for electrons, potentially providing a new mechanism for magnetar magnetic fields. The authors hedge that this mechanism operates at least qualitatively and perhaps even quantitatively for neutrinos near equilibrium, but that whether it operates at a more quantitative level should be tested by future numerical applications of chiral radiation transport theory.

## Method
The authors derive the transport coefficient for the chiral electric current using the recently formulated chiral radiation transport theory for neutrinos, based on the Standard Model. They then perform numerical magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) simulations including this current, using the HLLD Riemann solver with MUSCL-type interpolation and Runge-Kutta time integration, with the constrained transport method to enforce the divergence-free magnetic field condition. The simulations cover initial values of the transport coefficient $\xi_{B, {\rm ini}}$ from $10^{-5}$ to $10^{-1}$ (in units of $100\,{\rm MeV} = 1$).

## Key numbers
- Transport coefficient estimate: $\xi_{B} \sim 10\,{\rm MeV}$ for typical supernova parameters ($n_{\rm n} - n_{\rm p} \sim 0.1\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$, $\mu_{\rm n} - \mu_{\rm p} \sim 100\,{\rm MeV}$, $\mu_{\nu} \sim \mu _{\rm e} \sim 100\,{\rm MeV}$, $T \sim 10\,{\rm MeV}$, $|{\bm v}| \sim 0.01$, $\ell \sim 10\,{\rm km}$), regarded as an upper bound.
- Effective chiral chemical potential: $\mu_{5, {\rm eff}} = 2\pi^2 \xi_B \sim 100\,{\rm MeV}$.
- Maximum magnetic field estimate from CPI: $B_{\rm CPI} \sim \mu_{5, {\rm eff}}^2 \sim 10^{18}\,{\rm Gauss}$.
- Critical wavenumber for CPI: $k_{\rm crit} \equiv \xi_B$, with critical length $\lambda_{\rm crit} = 2\pi/\xi_B$.
- CPI growth timescale: $\tau_{\rm CPI}=4/(\eta \xi_B^2)$, with typical wavelength $\lambda_{\rm CPI} = 4\pi/\xi_B$.
- Simulation parameters: viscosity $\nu=0.01$, resistivity $\eta=1$, grid size $N^3=128^3$, with $\lambda_{\rm crit}$ resolved by 10 grid points.
- Simulation domain sizes $L$ range from $8 \times 10^{2}$ (model 1, $\xi_{B,{\rm ini}}=10^{-1}$) to $8 \times 10^{6}$ (model 5, $\xi_{B,{\rm ini}}=10^{-5}$), with corresponding $\tau_{\rm CPI}$ from $4 \times 10^{2}$ to $4 \times 10^{10}$.

## Context
This work extends the chiral radiation transport theory for neutrinos developed by Yamamoto (2020) and Yamamoto (2021) to derive a new chiral electric current in core-collapse supernovae. It differs from the chiral magnetic effect (CME) studied by Vilenkin (1980), Nielsen (1983), and Fukushima (2008) in that it occurs without electron chirality imbalance, avoiding the damping issue from finite electron mass that affected the scenarios of Ohnishi (2014) and Grabowska (2014). The mechanism provides a new origin for the chiral plasma instability (CPI) previously studied by Joyce (1997) and Akamatsu (2013), and extends the chiral MHD simulations of Masada (2018) to larger values of $\xi_B$. The results enable future self-consistent simulations incorporating the chiral effects of neutrinos on supernova explosion dynamics and magnetar magnetic field generation.
