# Collective states in nuclei and many-body random interactions

> Low-lying collective states in nuclei are investigated in the framework of the interacting boson model using an ensemble of random many-body interactions. It is shown that whenever the number of bosons is sufficiently large compared to the rank of the interactions, the spectral properties are characterized by a dominance of L(P)=0(+) ground states and the occurrence of both vibrational and rotational band structures. This indicates that these features represent a general and robust property of the collective model space.

# Collective states in nuclei and many-body random interactions

## Key claim

The dominance of L^P=0^+ ground states (approximately 60% of cases) and the appearance of vibrational and rotational band structures in the interacting boson model are robust properties that emerge whenever the number of bosons N is sufficiently large compared to the rank k of the random interactions, independent of the specific values of the interaction matrix elements.

## Method

The authors used the interacting boson model (IBM) with ensembles of random one-body, two-body, and three-body interactions, where the matrix elements were drawn independently from Gaussian distributions with zero mean. For each set of randomly generated interactions, they diagonalized the Hamiltonian for boson numbers N ranging from 2 to 16 and computed the entire energy spectrum and B(E2) values between yrast states, repeating the procedure for 1000 runs per parameter set. They analyzed the percentage of L^P=0^+ ground states and the probability distribution of the energy ratio R = E(4^+_1)-E(0^+_1) / E(2^+_1)-E(0^+_1) as functions of N and the rank k of the interactions.

## Key numbers

- For N=16 with random two-body interactions, the ground state has L^P=0^+ in 60.5% of cases (Table I), compared to approximately 12.9% for L=2 and 18.6% for L=32.
- The probability distribution P(R) for N=16 with random two-body interactions shows two peaks: one at R ~ 1.95 (close to the harmonic vibrator value R=2) and one at R ~ 3.35 (close to the rotor value R=10/3 ~ 3.33).
- For N=3 with random two-body interactions, P(R) shows little structure; for N=6 a maximum develops at R ~ 1.9; for N=10 a second maximum appears at R ~ 3.3.
- The percentage of L^P=0^+ ground states for 3 ≤ N ≤ 16 with random two-body interactions shows oscillations with maxima at N=3n (multiples of 3), attributed to cases where the energy ratio R < 1.
- For random three-body interactions with N=16, P(R) shows two maxima at approximately the same vibrator and rotor values, though the peaks are less pronounced than for two-body interactions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) and Bijker, Frank, and Pittel (1999) that found dominance of 0^+ ground states in the nuclear shell model and the IBM with random interactions. It also builds on the authors' previous work (Bijker and Frank, 2000) which showed vibrational and rotational band structures in the IBM with random one- and two-body interactions for N=16. The paper systematically investigates how these features depend on boson number and interaction rank, showing that the results saturate when N is sufficiently large compared to k, independent of whether two- or three-body interactions are used. This enables future work on whether similar collective behavior can be observed in the shell model with appropriate Hilbert space truncation, and on the form of the lowest-eigenvalue distributions in exactly solvable schematic models.
