# The spin parity of $Z_c^-$(4100), $Z_1^+$(4050) and $Z_2^+$(4250)

> We conjecture that $Z_c^-$(4100) found by LHCb group from a Dalitz plot analysis of $B^0\to η_c K^+π^-$ decay is the charge conjugate of $Z_1^+$(4050) observed in $χ_{c1}π^+$ distribution from Belle collaboration. Some interesting conclusions are inferred from this assumption. The $Z_2$(4250) would be assigned to be a $J^P = 1^+$ or $1^-$ state because of its absence in $η_{c}π^-$ invariant mass distribution, while $Z_1^+$(4050)/$Z_c^-$(4100) could be a $0^+$ or $1^-$ state but $2^+$ is unfavored because it would be coupled to $η_{c}π$ in $D$-wave. The null observation of $Z_1 Z_2$, $Z_1 Z_1$ and $Z_2 Z_2$ production in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation and $Υ(1S,2S)$ decay by Belle collaboration would further allocate the spin parity combination of $Z_1^+$(4050)/$Z_c^-$(4100) and $Z_2$(4250). Our deductions can be used to exclude a set of proposed models and could be further tested by future experiment, e.g. in $γγ$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper conjectures that the LHCb state Zc-(4100) is the charge conjugate of the Belle state Z1+(4050), and uses this assumption together with observed decay patterns and null production results to narrow the possible spin-parity assignments: Z1+(4050)/Zc-(4100) is likely 1- or 0+, while Z2+(4250) is likely 1+ or 1-; the authors hedge that the 1- assignment for Z1+/Zc- is slightly favored by branching ratio data but that large uncertainties prevent a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors compare the Breit-Wigner masses and widths of Zc-(4100) from LHCb's B0 → ηc K+ π- Dalitz plot analysis with those of Z1+(4050) and Z2+(4250) from Belle's χc1 π+ distribution in B0 → K- χc1 π+ decays. They analyze the possible JPC assignments from the relative orbital angular momenta L of the χc1 π and ηc π systems, then use the measured branching fraction ratios and the null observation of Z1 Z2, Z1 Z1, and Z2 Z2 production in e+e- annihilation and Υ(1S,2S) decays to further constrain the spin-parity combinations.

## Key numbers

- Z1+(4050) mass: 4051 ± 14 +20/-41 MeV, width: 82 +21+47/-17-22 MeV (Belle, from χc1 π+ distribution)
- Z2+(4250) mass: 4248 +44+180/-29-35 MeV, width: 177 +54+316/-39-61 MeV (Belle, from χc1 π+ distribution)
- Zc-(4100) mass: 4096 ± 20 +18/-22 MeV, width: 152 ± 58 +60/-35 MeV (LHCb, from ηc π- spectrum)
- Branching fraction product for Z1+(4050): B(B0 → K- Z1+) × B(Z1+ → χc1 π+) = 3.0 +1.5+3.7/-0.8-1.6 × 10-5 (Belle); upper limit < 1.8 × 10-5 at 90% C.L. (BABAR)
- Branching fraction product for Zc-(4100): B(B0 → K+ Zc-) × B(Zc- → ηc π-) = 1.89 ± 0.64 ± 0.04 +0.69/-0.63 ± 0.22 × 10-5 (LHCb)
- Ratio of branching fractions B(Zc → ηc π)/B(Z1 → χc1 π) = 0.63 +0.50/-0.89, with upper limit < 1.05 +0.54/-0.51
- Ratio of coupling constants |gηcπ/gχc1π|: for 1- Zc: 2.20 +1.75/-3.11, upper limit < 2.85 +1.47/-1.38; for 0+ Zc: 0.66 +0.52/-0.93, upper limit < 0.85 +0.44/-0.41

## Context

This work extends the discussion of charged charmonium-like states by proposing that Zc-(4100) from LHCb (Aaij 2018) and Z1+(4050) from Belle (Mizuk 2008) are the same particle, a possibility also mentioned by Zhao (2018). It contradicts the BABAR (Lees 2011) and LHCb thesis (Sbordone 2013) conclusions that Z1+(4050) and Z2+(4250) are not genuine resonances, attributing the discrepancy to different background treatments. The paper enables future experimental tests, particularly in γγ collisions at Belle-II or LHC, to distinguish between the 0+ and 1- assignments for Z1+/Zc- and to probe the exotic nature of these states via two-photon decay widths.
