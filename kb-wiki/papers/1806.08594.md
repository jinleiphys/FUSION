# Asymmetric Relativistic Fermi Gas model for quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering

> We develop an asymmetric relativistic Fermi gas model for the study of the electroweak nuclear response in the quasielastic region. The model takes into account the differences between neutron and proton densities in asymmetric (N > Z) nuclei, as well as differences in the neutron and proton separation energies. We present numerical results for both neutral and charged current processes, focusing on nuclei of interest for ongoing and future neutrino oscillation experiments. We point out some important differences with respect to the commonly employed symmetric Fermi gas model.

## Key claim

The Asymmetric Relativistic Fermi Gas (ARFG) model improves upon the standard symmetric relativistic Fermi gas by incorporating different Fermi momenta for neutrons and protons in asymmetric (N > Z) nuclei and, for charged-current reactions, also including energy offsets from measured neutron and proton separation energies; the authors show that these effects are small for neutral-current processes but can be significantly larger for charged-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering.

## Method

The authors define separate Fermi spheres for protons and neutrons with Fermi momenta \(k_F^p = (3\pi^2 Z/V)^{1/3}\) and \(k_F^n = (3\pi^2 N/V)^{1/3}\), and shift nucleon energies by offsets \(D = E_F + S\) using measured separation energies \(S_p\) and \(S_n\) from the ENSDF database. They compute the nuclear hadronic tensor and response functions for electron scattering, neutral-current (anti)neutrino scattering, and charged-current (anti)neutrino scattering on \(^{12}\)C, \(^{40}\)Ar, and \(^{208}\)Pb, comparing results with the symmetric RFG model at momentum transfers of 300 and 800 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C: average Fermi momentum \(k_F^0 = 228\) MeV/c; neutron separation energy \(S_n = 18.72\) MeV; proton separation energy \(S_p = 15.96\) MeV.
- For \(^{40}\)Ar: average Fermi momentum \(k_F^0 = 241\) MeV/c; neutron Fermi momentum \(k_F^n = 248.23\) MeV/c; proton Fermi momentum \(k_F^p = 232.17\) MeV/c; neutron separation energy \(S_n = 9.87\) MeV; proton separation energy \(S_p = 12.53\) MeV.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb: average Fermi momentum \(k_F^0 = 248\) MeV/c; neutron Fermi momentum \(k_F^n = 261.77\) MeV/c; proton Fermi momentum \(k_F^p = 226.85\) MeV/c; neutron separation energy \(S_n = 7.37\) MeV; proton separation energy \(S_p = 8.00\) MeV.
- At \(q = 800\) MeV/c for \(^{40}\)Ar electromagnetic responses: ARFG/SRFG ratio at the quasielastic peak is approximately 1.03 for longitudinal and 1.01 for transverse.
- At \(q = 800\) MeV/c for \(^{208}\)Pb electromagnetic responses: ARFG/SRFG ratio at the quasielastic peak is approximately 1.07 for longitudinal and 1.03 for transverse.
- At \(q = 800\) MeV/c for \(^{40}\)Ar weak neutral-current responses: ARFG/SRFG ratio at the quasielastic peak is approximately 0.98 for longitudinal and 1.01 for transverse; for \(^{208}\)Pb the ratios are approximately 0.96 for longitudinal and 1.02 for transverse.
- Energy offset for CC neutrino scattering on \(^{12}\)C: \(D^{n\to p} = 15.21\) MeV; for \(^{40}\)Ar: \(D^{n\to p} = 5.25\) MeV; for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(D^{n\to p} = 12.24\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic asymmetric Fermi gas models by Alberico (1989) and Davesne (2014) to a fully relativistic framework, and builds upon the superscaling analysis of electron scattering data by Donnelly (2002) to determine average Fermi momenta. The model is directly applicable to neutrino oscillation experiments using \(^{40}\)Ar targets (as in the DUNE experiment) and \(^{12}\)C targets (as in T2K and NOvA), and the authors note that the formalism can be extended to include 1p1h inelastic processes following the approach of PhysRevC.69.035502, with 2p2h excitations left for future work.
