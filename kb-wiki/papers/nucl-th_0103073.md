# Plasma production and thermalisation in a strong field

> Aspects of the formation and equilibration of a quark-gluon plasma are explored using a quantum kinetic equation, which involves a non-Markovian, Abelian source term for quark and antiquark production and, for the collision term, a relaxation time approximation that defines a time-dependent quasi-equilibrium temperature and collective velocity. The strong Abelian field is determined via the simultaneous solution of Maxwell's equation. A particular feature of this approach is the appearance of plasma oscillations in all thermodynamic observables. Their presence can lead to a sharp increase in the time-integrated dilepton yield.

## Key claim

The non-Markovian aspects of the source term do not generate observable effects, but the field-current feedback characteristic of strongly coupled charge production manifests as plasma oscillations in thermodynamic observables, which can significantly enhance the time-integrated dilepton yield by up to a factor of 2 at t=5 fm under RHIC-like conditions.

## Method

The authors solve a quantum Vlasov equation with a non-Markovian, Abelian source term for quark-antiquark production, coupled self-consistently to Maxwell's equation for the electric field. Collisions are treated via a relaxation time approximation with a time-dependent relaxation time, defining a quasi-equilibrium temperature and collective velocity at each time step through energy and momentum conservation constraints.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium energy densities for the four parameter sets: 1.0, 2.5, 12, and 24 GeV/fm^3 (Sets 1-4 respectively)
- Equilibrium temperatures: 0.20, 0.26, 0.38, and 0.44 GeV (Sets 1-4)
- Equilibrium number densities: 1.4, 3.0, 10, and 17 fm^{-3} (Sets 1-4)
- Plasma oscillations produce an enhancement in the time-integrated dilepton yield as large as a factor of 2 at t=5 fm for Set 4 initial conditions (A_0 = 1.0 GeV^2)
- The plasma period satisfies m * tau_pl ≈ 2.5, comparable to the relaxation time parameter tau_c = 1.0
- The external field profile uses A_0 = 1.0 GeV^2 and b = 0.2 fm for RHIC-like conditions

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model of particle production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier studies of non-Markovian source terms by the same group (KME, 1998; PRD, 1999) and previous work using constant relaxation times (Bloch, 1999; Memory, 2000). The approach enables exploration of whether plasma oscillations from the pre-equilibrium stage can leave observable signatures in dilepton spectra, which had been proposed as a signal by McLerran and Toimela (1985). The results suggest that for sufficiently energetic collisions (energy densities above ~10 GeV/fm^3), plasma oscillations may produce measurable enhancements in dilepton yields despite collisional damping.
