# Precursor of Color Superconductivity

> We investigate possible precursory phenomena of color superconductivity at finite temperature $T$ with an effective theory of QCD. It is found that the fluctuation of the diquark pair field exists with a prominent strength even well above the critical temperature $T_c$. We show that such a fluctuaiton forms a collective mode, the corresponding pole of which approaches the origin as $T$ is lowered to $T_c$ in the complex energy plane. We discuss the possible relevance of the precursor to the observables to be detected in heavy-ion collisions.

# Precursor of Color Superconductivity

## Key claim
The fluctuation of the diquark pair field in the Wigner phase above the critical temperature \(T_c\) forms a collective mode (a soft mode) whose pole approaches the origin in the complex energy plane as \(T\) is lowered toward \(T_c\); this precritical region extends to temperatures well above \(T_c\) (up to \(\epsilon \equiv (T-T_c)/T_c \sim 0.2\)), which is one to two orders larger in units of \(T_c\) than in ordinary metallic superconductors.

## Method
The authors use the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit with a diquark coupling term \(G_C\). They first determine the phase diagram in the \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane in the mean-field approximation. Then, in the Wigner phase above \(T_c\), they calculate the linear response of the diquark pair field to an external field, obtaining the retarded response function \(D^R(\mathbf{k},\omega)\) via analytic continuation of the particle–particle correlation function \(\mathcal{Q}(\mathbf{k},\omega_n)\). The spectral function \(\rho(\mathbf{k},\omega)\) and the pole structure of the response function are examined as functions of temperature at fixed chemical potentials.

## Key numbers
- The tricritical point where the chiral transition changes from second to first order is located at approximately \((T,\mu) = (70, 280)\) MeV.
- At \(\mu = 400\) MeV, a prominent peak in the spectral function survives at temperatures well above \(T_c\) with \(\epsilon \equiv (T-T_c)/T_c \sim 0.2\).
- The precritical region of color superconductivity is one to two orders larger in units of \(T_c\) than that in ordinary electric superconductors of metals.
- At \(\mu = 500\) MeV, the growth of the peak in the spectral function becomes relatively moderate, but the qualitative features do not change.

## Context
This work extends the study of fluctuation effects in color superconductivity, analogous to the paraconductivity and precursor phenomena known in ordinary metallic superconductivity (Tsuneto) and in high-\(T_c\) superconductivity. The authors use the NJL model, following their own prior work (Kitazawa, Koide, Kunihiro, Nemoto). The results suggest that heavy-ion collisions on Earth might be used to study color superconductivity through precursory signals such as anomalous electric conductivity (paraconductivity) via Aslamazov-Larkin and Maki-Tompson diagrams, and possibly pseudo-gap phenomena similar to those in copper-oxide superconductors. The next step is to compute the effect of these fluctuations on dilepton production and other observables.
