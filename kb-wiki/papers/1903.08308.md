# Publicising Lattice Field Theory through Visualisation

> The gluon field configurations that form the foundation of every lattice QCD calculation contain a rich diversity of emergent nonperturbative structure. Visualisations of these phenomena not only serve to explain the concept of a nontrivial vacuum but also entertain a diverse audience from research funding panels to the next generation of science enthusiasts. In this brief review, a collection of QCD-vacuum visualisations is presented including the structure of chromo-electromagnetic fields, centre-cluster evolution at finite temperature, the structure of projected centre vortices, and novel correlations between the electromagnetic fields of QED and the chromo-electromagnetic fields of QCD in QED+QCD dynamical-fermion simulations from the QCDSF collaboration.

## Key claim

This brief review presents a collection of QCD-vacuum visualisations developed by the Centre for the Subatomic Structure of Matter (CSSM) over 20 years, illustrating that visualisations of genuine lattice calculations can reveal emergent nonperturbative structure and serve both explanatory and outreach purposes for audiences ranging from funding panels to science enthusiasts.

## Method

The authors present four categories of visualisation based on lattice QCD calculations: (1) chromo-electromagnetic field structure using the O(a^4)-five-loop improved field-strength tensor on pure SU(3) gluon configurations with the Wilson action at beta=6.0 on a 24^3 x 36 lattice; (2) centre-cluster evolution in local Polyakov loops using an anisotropic gauge action with stout-link smearing; (3) projected centre-vortex structure in SU(3) gauge theory correlated with topological charge density; and (4) correlations between QED and QCD fields in QED+QCD dynamical-fermion simulations from the QCDSF collaboration. Each visualisation is accompanied by animations available via YouTube and HD-streaming formats.

## Key numbers

- Pure SU(3) gluon dynamics used beta=6.0 on a 24^3 x 36 lattice with lattice spacing 0.1 fm, creating a 24^2 x 36 spatial volume
- Centre-cluster correlations persist for approximately 5 seconds corresponding to 25 Hybrid Monte Carlo trajectories
- Centre clusters visualised at T = 0.89(1) T_C on a cubic volume with side length 2.4 fm
- QED+QCD simulations used a 24^3 x 48 lattice at the flavor-symmetric point with lattice spacing 0.068 fm, with electric charge enhanced by a factor of 10 in the simulations
- Short-distance fluctuations suppressed via 25 sweeps of cooling with the mean-field-improved O(a^4)-three-loop improved action
- Topological charge determined using eight sweeps of O(a^4)-three-loop improved cooling

## Context

This work extends the CSSM's two-decade program of visualising the nontrivial QCD vacuum, which began with animations of Euclidean action density and topological charge density that captured the attention of Frank Wilczek for his 2004 Nobel Prize lecture (where the "QCD Lava Lamp" animation appeared). The visualisations build on prior lattice QCD calculations including the QCDSF collaboration's QED+QCD dynamical-fermion simulations (Horsley 2015) and centre-vortex studies showing that removal of SU(3) centre vortices removes confinement (Langfeld 2003) while consideration of vortices alone provides confinement. The work enables broader public engagement with lattice field theory and provides new insights into the correlation between centre-vortex structure, topological charge density, and the interplay of QED and QCD fields in the vacuum.
