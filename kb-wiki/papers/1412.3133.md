# Double parton correlations in Light-Front constituent quark model

> Double parton distribution functions (dPDF) represent a tool to explore the 3D proton structure. They can be measured in high energy proton-proton and proton nucleus collisions and encode information on how partons inside a proton are correlated among each other. dPFDs are studied here in the valence quark region, by means of a constituent quark model, where two particle correlations are present without any additional prescription. This framework allows to understand the dynamical origin of the correlations and to clarify which, among the features of the results, are model independent. Use will be made of a relativistic light-front scheme, able to overcome some drawbacks of the previous calculation. Transverse momentum correlations, due to the exact treatment of the boosts, are predicted and analyzed. The role of spin correlations is also shown. Due to the covariance of the approach, some symmetries of the dPDFs are seen unambigously. For the valence sector, the study of the QCD evolution of the model results, which can be performed safely thanks to the property of good support, has been also completed.

# Double parton correlations in Light-Front constituent quark model

## Key claim
Double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) calculated in a Light-Front constituent quark model show that the commonly used factorized ansatz for dPDFs is violated in the valence region at the hadronic scale, but after perturbative QCD evolution to high energy scales, unpolarized dynamical correlations become suppressed at small x, while double spin correlations remain significant even at low x.

## Method
The authors compute double parton distribution functions using a relativistic Light-Front approach applied to a hyper-central constituent quark model, which provides a fully Poincare-covariant description with a fixed number of on-shell constituents. The Light-Front wave function is obtained from the canonical proton wave function via Melosh rotations, allowing the calculation of dPDFs for unpolarized and longitudinally polarized u quarks at the hadronic scale (mu_0^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2). The dPDFs are then evolved to higher energy scales using leading-order perturbative QCD evolution equations for the valence non-singlet sector, solving the homogeneous part via Mellin transformations.

## Key numbers
- The hadronic energy scale used for the initial model calculation is mu_0^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2.
- The high energy scale to which dPDFs are evolved is Q^2 = 10 GeV^2.
- At the hadronic scale, the ratio r_5 (comparing 2*uu(x1,x2,k_perp=0) to u(x1)*u(x2)) deviates significantly from 1, indicating violation of the factorized ansatz.
- After pQCD evolution to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, the ratio r_5 approaches approximately 1 for small x values, indicating suppression of unpolarized dynamical correlations.
- The ratio r_6 (for polarized distributions) remains different from 1 even after evolution to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, showing persistent double spin correlations.

## Context
This work extends previous non-relativistic constituent quark model calculations of dPDFs by Chang et al. (2013) and the MIT bag model calculations by Manohar and Waalewijn (2012), which both found violations of the factorized ansatz but suffered from incorrect support (dPDFs non-zero for x1+x2 > 1). The Light-Front approach fixes this support problem and restores expected symmetries such as x1-x2 exchange invariance at k_perp=0. The results enable future comparisons with double parton scattering data from the LHC and provide guidance for whether the commonly used factorized approximation in Eq. (app) is valid for DPS simulations.
