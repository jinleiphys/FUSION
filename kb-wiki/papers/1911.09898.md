# Quark fluctuations and anisotropic confinement in magnetic fields

> We study the string tension $σ$ of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) in external magnetic fields $B$ by combining the quasi-particle treatments for quarks with the collective treatments for gluons. We utilize the 1/Nc-expansion (Nc: number of colors) and the framework of Wilson's strong coupling expansion at long distance, and compare the resulting string tensions with the lattice data. The effects of magnetic fields are taken into account through the dielectric functions, which are evaluated from the one-loop polarization due to light flavor quarks with $B$-dependent effective masses $M_{\rm f}(B)\ ({\rm f}=u,d,s)$. All Landau levels are adopted in the calculations so that we can explore the string tensions from weak to strong magnetic fields. For the effective quark masses, we use a simple parameterization: $M_{\rm f}(B) = M_{\rm f}(0) + C_B |q_{\rm f}B|^{1/2}$ with $q_{\rm f}$ the electric charge of a given flavor and $C_B$ an adaptive parameter. We find that the parameter $C_B$ needs to be small, $C_B \lesssim 0.05$, to qualitatively reproduce the trends of lattice QCD results for $0 \leq |eB| \lesssim 1.5\, {\rm GeV}^2$. The quantitative agreement is found for $C_B \simeq 0$ and the strong coupling $α_S \simeq 5$ in the infrared.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anisotropic string tensions of QCD in external magnetic fields can be reproduced by a simple framework combining quasi-particle quark treatments with collective gluon dynamics, provided the effective quark masses do not grow rapidly with the magnetic field; specifically, the parameter \(C_B\) in the mass parameterization \(M_{\rm f}(B) = M_{\rm f}(0) + C_B |q_{\rm f}B|^{1/2}\) must be small (\(C_B \lesssim 0.05\)) to qualitatively match lattice QCD trends for \(0 \leq |eB| \lesssim 1.5\, {\rm GeV}^2\).

## Method

The authors used the \(1/N_c\) expansion (with \(N_c=3\)) and Wilson's strong coupling expansion to compute string tensions, incorporating magnetic field effects through dielectric functions evaluated from one-loop quark polarization. All Landau levels were included in the polarization calculations, using a parameterized effective quark mass that depends on the magnetic field. The string tension ratios \(\sigma_i^B / \sigma_0\) were estimated from the infrared limit of the dielectric functions via the formula \(\sigma_i^B / \sigma_0 = \ln(\lambda / \bar{\xi}_i^B) / \ln \lambda\), and compared to lattice QCD data from Bonati (2016).

## Key numbers

* The parameter \(C_B\) must satisfy \(C_B \lesssim 0.05\) to qualitatively reproduce lattice QCD trends for \(0 \leq |eB| \lesssim 1.5\, {\rm GeV}^2\).
* Quantitative agreement with lattice data is found for \(C_B \simeq 0\) and the strong coupling \(\alpha_S \simeq 5\) in the infrared.
* The effective quark masses in vacuum are taken as \(M_u(0) = 336\,{\rm MeV}\), \(M_d(0) = 340\,{\rm MeV}\), and \(M_s(0) = 486\,{\rm MeV}\).
* The magnetic field range studied is \(0 \leq eB \leq 1.5\, {\rm GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of Bonati (2014, 2016) on the static potential between heavy quarks in magnetic fields, which found that the confining force perpendicular to the field becomes slightly stronger while the parallel direction becomes considerably weakened. The paper provides a theoretical framework to understand these anisotropic confinement effects and their connection to chiral magnetic catalysis, using a quasi-particle approach that could be applied to other extreme conditions such as high baryon density or rotating systems.
