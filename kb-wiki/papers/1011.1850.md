# Projecting the Bethe-Salpeter Equation onto the Light-Front and back: A Short Review

> The technique of projecting the four-dimensional two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation onto the three-dimensional Light-Front hypersurface, combined with the quasi-potential approach, is briefly illustrated, by placing a particular emphasis on the relation between the projection method and the effective dynamics of the valence component of the Light-Front wave function. Some details on how to construct the Fock expansion of both i) the Light-Front effective interaction and ii) the electromagnetic current operator, satisfying the proper Ward-Takahashi identity, will be presented, addressing the relevance of the Fock content in the operators living onto the Light-Front hypersurface. Finally, the generalization of the formalism to the three-particle case will be outlined.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation can be projected onto the three-dimensional Light-Front hypersurface using a quasi-potential approach, establishing a one-to-one correspondence between the full Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the valence component of the Light-Front wave function, but only under the condition that the expansion of the effective interaction in terms of Fock components converges rapidly enough to justify truncation.

## Method

The authors apply the quasi-potential reduction technique to the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation, introducing an auxiliary Green's function that eliminates the relative Light-Front time by integrating over the minus components of the constituent momenta. This yields a three-dimensional effective interaction for the valence component, whose Fock-space content is then analyzed through a hierarchy of coupled equations for Light-Front Green's functions, with explicit construction for both bosonic and fermionic Yukawa models. The formalism is extended to three-particle systems via a Faddeev decomposition of the quasi-potential.

## Key numbers

- The free two-body Light-Front propagator is given by \( g_0(K) = \frac{\widehat P}{\widehat{k}_{1}^{+}(K^{+}-\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})\left( K^{-}-\widehat{k}_{1on}^{-}-\widehat{k}_{2on}^{-}+i\varepsilon\right) } \), where \(\widehat P = i\theta (K^{+}-\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})\theta (\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})\) for two bosons and \(\widehat P = i\theta (K^{+}-\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})\theta (\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})(2m_{1})(2m_{2})\Lambda _{+}(\widehat{k}_{1on})\Lambda _{+}(\widehat{k}_{2on})\) for two fermions.
- The interaction vertex for bosons in the Yukawa model has matrix element \(\langle q k_\sigma |v|k\rangle = -2(2\pi)^3\delta^3 (\tilde q+\tilde k_\sigma-\tilde k) \frac{g_S}{\sqrt{q^+k^+_\sigma k^+}} \theta (k^+_\sigma)\).
- For fermions, the vertex matrix element is \(\langle (q,s^\prime) k_\sigma |v|(k,s)\rangle = -2m(2\pi)^3\delta^3 (\tilde q+\tilde k_\sigma-\tilde k) \frac{g_S}{\sqrt{q^+k^+_\sigma k^+}} \theta (k^+_\sigma){\overline u}(q,s^\prime)u(k,s)\).

## Context

This review extends the quasi-potential approach to Light-Front dynamics previously developed by the same authors (Sales et al., 2000; Sales et al., 2001) and connects it to the "iterated resolvents" method of Pauli and collaborators (Pauli, 1998; Pauli, 1999). The formalism enables systematic treatment of higher Fock-state contributions to few-body systems, with potential applications to nucleon electromagnetic form factors where valence and nonvalence contributions must be distinguished, as studied by the authors (Adnei et al., 2007; Adnei et al., 2008) and compared with the Covariant Spectator Theory of Gross (2010).
