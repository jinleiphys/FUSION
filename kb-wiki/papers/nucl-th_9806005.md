# QCD bound states and their response to extremes of temperature and density

> We describe the application of Dyson-Schwinger equations to the calculation of hadron observables. The studies at zero temperature (T) and quark chemical potential (mu) provide a springboard for the extension to finite-(T,mu). Our exemplars highlight that much of hadronic physics can be understood as simply a manifestation of the nonperturbative, momentum-dependent dressing of the elementary Schwinger functions in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a Poincaré-invariant, continuum framework for calculating hadron observables at both zero and finite temperature and chemical potential, with the hedge that truncation schemes must preserve global symmetries to yield reliable results, and that the approach is complementary to lattice-QCD rather than a replacement.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using an infrared-enhanced dressed-gluon propagator in Landau gauge, combined with a rainbow truncation of the quark-gluon vertex. For meson bound states, they solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a ladder truncation of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, ensuring consistency with axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. At finite temperature and chemical potential, they extend the formalism by introducing Matsubara frequencies and a quark chemical potential, solving the quark DSE with model-dependent Ansätze for the dressed-gluon propagator.

## Key numbers

- Current-quark masses at 1 GeV: m_{u/d} = 6.6 MeV, m_s = 140 MeV, m_c = 1.0 GeV, m_b = 3.4 GeV
- Chiral limit vacuum quark condensate at mu = 1 GeV: <q_bar q>^0 = -(0.241 GeV)^3
- Ratio M^E/m_{mu~20 GeV} for u/d quarks: 150; for s quarks: 10; for c quarks: 2.3; for b quarks: 1.4; for t quarks: approaches 1
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in two-light-flavour QCD: T_c ≈ 150 MeV, with critical exponent beta = 0.33 ± 0.03
- Critical chemical potential for first-order phase transition: mu_c = 375 MeV
- Baryon number density at mu ≈ 2 mu_c: rho_B^{u+d} ≈ 3 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Baryon number density at mu ≈ 5 mu_c: rho_B^{u_F+d_F} ≈ 350 rho_0
- Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation for pion: accurate to 7%; for kaon: receives 43% corrections from O(m_q^2) and higher terms

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach developed by Bender (1996) and others for preserving chiral symmetry in truncations, and complements finite-temperature lattice-QCD simulations by Karsch (1995). The results enable simultaneous study of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and density, and suggest that a quark-gluon plasma may be present in the core of dense neutron stars, with implications for understanding the QCD phase diagram in regimes inaccessible to lattice simulations due to the sign problem at nonzero chemical potential.
