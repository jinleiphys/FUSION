# Isospin dependence of liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> By using the Furnstahl, Serot and Tang's model, the effect of density dependence of the effective nucleon-nucleon-rho-meson (NN-rho) coupling on the liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter is investigated. A limit pressure p_lim has been found. We found that the liquid-gas phase transition cannot take place if p>p_lim. The binodal surface for density dependent NN-rho coupling situation is addressed.

# Isospin dependence of liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

Using the Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (FST) model with a density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon-rho-meson (NN-rho) coupling, a limit pressure p_lim exists such that the liquid-gas phase transition cannot take place if the pressure exceeds p_lim. This conclusion depends on the specific ansatz that the effective coupling g_rho decreases with increasing density, and the limit pressure varies with the parameters A and B that control the density dependence.

## Method

The authors employ the FST model, an extension of quantum hadrodynamics, in the mean-field approximation to describe hot asymmetric nuclear matter. They introduce an ansatz g_rho' = g_rho [1 - A rho + B rho^2] to model the density dependence of the effective NN-rho coupling, with adjustable parameters A and B. The two-phase coexistence equations (equal chemical potentials and pressures for liquid and vapor phases) are solved numerically using the FST parameter set T1, and the binodal surface is constructed from the solutions.

## Key numbers

- For constant g_rho (g_rho^2 = 70.2) at T = 10 MeV, the critical pressure p_crit = 0.165 MeV (fm)^{-3}.
- For the density-dependent case with A = 1, B = 0 at T = 10 MeV, the limit pressure p_lim = 0.130 MeV (fm)^{-3}.
- For A = 1, B = 0 at T = 10 MeV and p = 0.145 MeV (fm)^{-3} (above p_lim), the neutron chemical potential becomes monotoneous and no phase transition occurs.
- The limit pressure decreases with increasing A and B: for A = 2, B = 1, p_lim = 0.115 MeV (fm)^{-3}; for A = 3, B = 1, p_lim = 0.105 MeV (fm)^{-3}; for A = 5, B = 2, p_lim = 0.90 MeV (fm)^{-3}.
- For negative A (A = -2, B = 1 or B = -1), p_lim = 0.125 MeV (fm)^{-3}; for A = -5, B = -2, p_lim = 0.115 MeV (fm)^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter by Muller and Serot (1995), who showed that the two-component system can undergo a continuous second-order phase transition. The authors had previously demonstrated that isospin-vector rho-meson interactions are essential to avoid Coulomb instability and negative asymmetry parameters in the vapor phase (Song, Qian and Su, 1993, 1994; Wang et al., 1999). The present paper introduces density-dependent effective NN-rho couplings, building on earlier calculations of effective couplings using Thermo Field Dynamics (Gao, Zhang and Su, 1995; Qian, Su and Su, 1993; Su, Zheng and Siu, 1993). The finding of a limit pressure is analogous to Coulomb instability in finite nuclei but applies to infinite asymmetric nuclear matter. The results enable future studies of the temperature dependence (limit temperature) and more realistic three-line vertex calculations of the effective coupling.
