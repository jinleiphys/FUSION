# Jet quenching parameter of quark-gluon plasma in strong magnetic field: Perturbative QCD and AdS/CFT correspondence

> We compute the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ of QCD plasma in the presence of strong magnetic field in both weakly and strongly coupled regimes. In weakly coupled regime, we compute $\hat q$ in perturbative QCD at complete leading order (that is, leading log as well as the constant under the log) in QCD coupling constant $α_s$, assuming the hierarchy of scales $α_s eB\ll T^2\ll eB$. We consider two cases of jet orientations with respect to the magnetic field: 1) the case of jet moving parallel to the magnetic field, 2) the case jet moving perpendicular to the magnetic field. In the former case, we find $\hat q\sim α_s^2 (eB)T\log(1/α_s)$, while in the latter we have $\hat q\sim α_s^2 (eB)T\log(T^2/α_seB)$. In both cases, this leading order result arises from the scatterings with thermally populated lowest Landau level quarks. In strongly coupled regime described by AdS/CFT correspondence, we find $\hat q\sim \sqrtλ(eB)T$ or $\hat q\sim\sqrtλ\sqrt{eB}T^2$ in the same hierarchy of $T^2\ll eB$ depending on whether the jet is moving parallel or perpendicular to the magnetic field, respectively, which indicates a universal dependence of $\hat q$ on $(eB)T$ in both regimes for the parallel case, the origin of which should be the transverse density of lowest Landau level states proportional to $eB$. Finally, the asymmetric transverse momentum diffusion in the case of jet moving perpendicular to the magnetic field may give an interesting azimuthal asymmetry of the gluon Bremsstrahlung spectrum in the BDMPS-Z formalism.

## Key claim

The paper computes the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ of QCD plasma in a strong magnetic field ($eB \gg T^2$) under the additional assumption $\alpha_s eB \ll T^2$, finding that the leading-order result arises from scatterings with thermally populated lowest Landau level quarks rather than from thermal gluons, with a universal dependence on $(eB)T$ in the parallel jet case across both weakly and strongly coupled regimes.

## Method

The authors compute $\hat q$ in perturbative QCD at complete leading order (including both the leading logarithm and the constant under the log) by evaluating the t-channel gluon exchange scattering rate between the jet and hard thermal particles, using the 1-loop retarded jet self-energy formalism with Debye screening from lowest Landau level quark states. For the strongly coupled regime, they employ the AdS/CFT correspondence to obtain $\hat q$ in the same hierarchy $T^2 \ll eB$. Two jet orientations relative to the magnetic field are considered: parallel and perpendicular.

## Key numbers

- For a jet moving parallel to the magnetic field in weakly coupled regime: $\hat q \sim \alpha_s^2 (eB)T \log(1/\alpha_s)$
- For a jet moving perpendicular to the magnetic field in weakly coupled regime: $\hat q \sim \alpha_s^2 (eB)T \log(T^2/\alpha_s eB)$
- For a jet moving parallel to the magnetic field in strongly coupled regime (AdS/CFT): $\hat q \sim \sqrt{\lambda}(eB)T$
- For a jet moving perpendicular to the magnetic field in strongly coupled regime (AdS/CFT): $\hat q \sim \sqrt{\lambda}\sqrt{eB}T^2$
- The complete leading order result for the parallel case includes the constant term: $\hat q = (1/\pi)(1+1/v)C_2^J T_R N_F \alpha_s^2 (eB)T (\log(1/\alpha_s) - 1 - \gamma_E - \log(T_R N_F/\pi))$, where $\gamma_E \approx 0.577$
- The hierarchy of scales assumed: $\alpha_s eB \ll T^2 \ll eB$

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD framework for heavy-quark diffusion in strong magnetic fields recently introduced by Fukushima (2015) to the jet quenching parameter, and provides the first AdS/CFT computation of $\hat q$ under the same magnetic field hierarchy. The results enable future studies of azimuthal asymmetry in gluon Bremsstrahlung spectra within the BDMPS-Z formalism, as the asymmetric transverse momentum diffusion for perpendicular jet motion breaks rotational symmetry around the jet direction.
