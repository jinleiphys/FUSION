# Baryon-baryon interactions in the SU6 quark model and their applications to light nuclear systems

> Interactions between the octet-baryons (B8) in the spin-flavor SU6 quark model are investigated in a unified coupled-channels framework of the resonating-group method (RGM). The interaction Hamiltonian for quarks consists of the phenomenological confinement potential, the color Fermi-Breit interaction with explicit flavor-symmetry breaking (FSB), and effective-meson exchange potentials of scalar-, pseudoscalar- and vector-meson types. The model parameters are determined to reproduce the properties of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) system and the low-energy cross section data for the hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions. The NN phase shifts and many observables for the NN and YN interactions are nicely reproduced. Properties of these B8 B8 interactions are analyzed through the G-matrix calculations. The B8 B8 interactions are then applied to some of few-baryon systems and light Lambda-hypernuclei in a three-cluster Faddeev formalism using two-cluster RGM kernels. An application to the three-nucleon system shows that the quark-model NN interaction can give a sufficient triton binding energy with little room for the three-nucleon force. The hypertriton Faddeev calculation indicates that the attraction of the Lambda N interaction in the 1S0 state is only slightly more attractive than that in the 3S1 state. In the application to the alpha alpha Lambda system, the energy spectrum of 9 Lambda Be is well reproduced using the alpha alpha RGM kernel. The very small spin-orbit splitting of the 9 Lambda Be excited states is also discussed. In the Lambda Lambda alpha Faddeev calculation, the NAGARA event for 6 Lambda Lambda He is found to be consistent with the quark-model Lambda Lambda interaction.

## Key claim

The SU6 quark model with explicit flavor-symmetry breaking and effective-meson exchange potentials can simultaneously reproduce nucleon-nucleon phase shifts up to 350 MeV laboratory energy and low-energy hyperon-nucleon cross section data, while the triton binding energy is obtained with little room for a three-nucleon force.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic resonating-group method (RGM) for the (3q)-(3q) system, using harmonic-oscillator wave functions with a common size parameter for all octet baryons. The quark-quark interaction Hamiltonian included a quadratic confinement potential, the full Fermi-Breit interaction with explicit flavor-symmetry breaking, and effective-meson exchange potentials of scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector types at the quark level. The RGM equation was solved in momentum representation via a Lippmann-Schwinger formalism, and the resulting baryon-baryon interactions were applied to few-baryon systems and light Lambda-hypernuclei using a three-cluster Faddeev formalism with two-cluster RGM kernels.

* NN phase shifts were reproduced up to T_lab = 350 MeV with accuracy comparable to one-boson exchange potentials.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier quark-model approaches RGM-F, FSS, RGM-H, and fss2 by incorporating vector-meson exchange potentials and solving the RGM equation in momentum representation. The model builds on the early quark-model studies of Oka and Yazaki (1980), Harvey (1980), and the Tubingen group (1988, 1990), and provides a unified framework for octet-baryon interactions that can be applied to few-body systems and hypernuclei. The results enable systematic G-matrix calculations of single-particle potentials in nuclear matter and three-cluster Faddeev calculations for light hypernuclei such as hypertriton, 9_Lambda Be, and 6_Lambda_Lambda He.
