# Can one identify the intrinsic structure of the yrast states in $^{48}$Cr after the backbending?

> The backbending phenomenon in $^{48}$Cr has been investigated using the recently developed Projected Configuration Interaction (PCI) method, in which the deformed intrinsic states are directly associated with shell model (SM) wavefunctions. Two previous explanations, (i) $K=0$ band crossing, and (ii) $K=2$ band crossing have been reinvestigated using PCI, and it was found that both explanations can successfully reproduce the experimental backbending. The PCI wavefunctions in the pictures of $K=0$ band crossing and $K=2$ band crossing are highly overlapped. We conclude that there are no unique intrinsic states associated with the yrast states after backbending in $^{48}$Cr.

## Key claim
Both the previously proposed $K=0$ band crossing (associated with CHFB/GCM) and $K=2$ band crossing (associated with PSM) explanations can successfully reproduce the experimental backbending in $^{48}$Cr, but the PCI wavefunctions from these two pictures are highly overlapped, meaning there are no unique intrinsic states associated with the yrast states after backbending.

## Method
The authors used the Projected Configuration Interaction (PCI) method, which builds a basis from Slater determinants with different deformations and associated particle-hole excitations, then projects onto good angular momentum. They constructed four PCI bases: a "g.s." basis with a $K=0$ deformed configuration ($\epsilon_2=0.19$, $\epsilon_4=-0.05$), and three candidate bases for the high-spin states: a spherical $K=0$ basis (A), a spherical $K=2$ basis (B), and a deformed $K=2$ basis (C) with the same deformation as the g.s. basis. The KB3 interaction was used for all calculations, and overlaps between PCI wavefunctions from different bases were computed.

## Key numbers
- The g.s. basis $|\kappa_1,0\rangle$ has deformation $\epsilon_2=0.19$ and $\epsilon_4=-0.05$, with a minimum energy of $\langle\kappa_1,0|H_\text{KB3}|\kappa_1,0\rangle = -28.296$ MeV (close to the HF energy $-28.423$ MeV).
- The number of selected Slater determinants for each basis: g.s. = 143, A = 192, B = 186, C = 149.
- Overlaps $|\langle\Psi_A(I)|\Psi_B(I)\rangle| \approx 1$ for $I > 10$, despite bases A and B being strictly orthogonal before angular momentum projection due to different $K$ values.
- Overlaps $|\langle\Psi_{g.s.+A}(I)|\Psi_{g.s.+C}(I)\rangle|$ are at least 92% for all spins.
- The band termination state at $I=16$ has a projected energy of $-18.342$ MeV with the KB3 interaction.
- PCI energies lie 500 keV on average above the exact full shell model values, using fewer than 400 SDs compared to 1,963,461 SDs in full SM.

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing controversy between CHFB analyses (Caurier 1995; Tanaka 1998) which suggested shape change to spherical without band crossing, and the PSM analysis (Hara 1999) which proposed a $K=2$ band crossing. The PCI method, developed by Gao (2009), shows these apparently contradictory explanations are qualitatively equivalent. This enables future studies to recognize that for $^{48}$Cr above spin $I=10$, intrinsic states cannot be uniquely identified, and that multiple different intrinsic bases can produce nearly identical wavefunctions after angular momentum projection.
