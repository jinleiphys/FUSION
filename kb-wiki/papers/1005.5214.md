# Analytical parametrization of fusion barriers using proximity potentials

> Using the three versions of proximity potentials, namely proximity 1977, proximity 1988, and proximity 2000, we present a pocket formula for fusion barrier heights and positions. This was achieved by analyzing as many as 400 reactions with mass between 15 and 296. Our parametrized formula can reproduced the exact barrier heights and positions within an accuracy of $\pm1%$. A comparison with the experimental data is also in good agreement.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical parametrization (pocket formula) for fusion barrier heights and positions that reproduces the exact barrier values calculated from three proximity potentials (Prox 77, Prox 88, Prox 00) within ±1%, but this accuracy claim applies to the fit to the model calculations, not directly to experimental data; the comparison with experimental data is described only as "good agreement" without a quantified error.

## Method

The authors calculated the total ion-ion potential by adding the Coulomb potential to the nuclear part from each of the three proximity potentials (Prox 77, Prox 88, Prox 00) for 400 reactions spanning mass numbers 15 to 296. They extracted fusion barrier heights (V_B) and positions (R_B) by solving the conditions dV_T/dr = 0 and d²V_T/dr² ≤ 0 at the barrier. The reduced barrier position s_B = R_B - C_1 - C_2 was then parametrized as an exponential function of x = Z_1 Z_2 / (A_1^{1/3} + A_2^{1/3}), and the barrier height was parametrized as a linear function of (1.44 Z_1 Z_2 / R_B^{par}) × (1 - 0.75 / R_B^{par}).

## Key numbers

- The parametrized formula reproduces exact barrier positions within ±1% for all three proximity potentials; average deviations are -0.01% (Prox 77), -0.02% (Prox 88), and 0% (Prox 00) over 400 reactions.
- The parametrized formula reproduces exact barrier heights within ±1%; average deviations are -0.10% (Prox 77), -0.12% (Prox 88), and 0.07% (Prox 00).
- For the reduced barrier position parametrization s_B^{par} = α exp[-β (x - 2)^{1/4}], the constants (α, β) are: (5.18419, 0.33979) for Prox 77; (5.37457, 0.31326) for Prox 88; (5.08758, 0.29518) for Prox 00.
- For the barrier height parametrization V_B^{par} = δ [1.44 Z_1 Z_2 / R_B^{par} (1 - 0.75 / R_B^{par})], the constant δ is 0.99903 (Prox 77), 0.99868 (Prox 88), and 1.002 (Prox 00).
- The lightest reaction studied was ⁶Li + ⁹Be; the heaviest was ⁴⁸Ca + ²⁴⁸Cm.

## Context

This work extends previous parametrization attempts for fusion barriers, such as those by Ngo (1980) and by Broglia and Winther (1981), by providing a simpler exponential form that achieves ±1% accuracy for three proximity potential versions. The authors note that Prox 77 overestimates experimental data by about 4% as reported by Myers and Swiatecki (2000), and that the newer versions (Prox 88, Prox 00) improve agreement. The resulting pocket formulas enable instantaneous estimation of fusion barriers for systems where measurements do not exist, and can guide studies of new, unexplored nuclei.
