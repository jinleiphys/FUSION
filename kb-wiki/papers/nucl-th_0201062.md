# Neutrino-deuteron reactions at solar neutrino energies

> In interpreting the SNO experiments, very accurate estimates of the νd reaction cross sections are of great importance. We improve the previous estimates of our group by updating some of its inputs and by taking into account the results of a recent effective-field-theoretical calculation. The new cross sections are slightly (\sim 1%) larger than the previously reported values. We present arguments that lead to the conclusion that it is reasonable to assign 1% uncertainty to the νd cross sections reported here.

## Key claim

The new cross sections are approximately 1% larger than the previously reported values from the same group, and the authors argue it is reasonable to assign 1% uncertainty to the νd cross sections reported here, though this error estimate does not include radiative corrections.

## Method

The authors improved upon the previous standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) calculation of Nakamura et al. (NSGK) by updating the axial coupling constant gA from 1.254 to 1.267, adopting the weak coupling constant GF′ = 1.1803×10−5 GeV−2 (which includes inner radiative corrections) instead of GF, and using a full set of exchange-current diagrams (Model I) adjusted to reproduce the tritium β-decay rate rather than the np→γd cross section. They also compared their SNPA results with a parameter-free effective field theory calculation (EFT*) by Ando et al. that determines the low-energy constant d̂R directly from the tritium β-decay rate.

## Key numbers

- The new cross sections are ∼1% larger than the previously reported NSGK values; specifically, σνdCC is ∼1.3% larger at Eν ∼ 5 MeV, ∼0.8% larger at Eν ∼ 10 MeV, and ∼0.4% larger at Eν ∼ 20 MeV
- Changing the weak coupling constant from GF to GF′ scales σνd by an overall factor of (GF′/GF)2 ∼ 1.02
- Changing gA from 1.254 to 1.267 enhances σνd by another factor of (1.267/1.254)2 ∼ 1.02
- The contribution of the exchange current (AEXC) to σνd has an assigned uncertainty of 0.2% in the solar neutrino energy range (Eν < 20 MeV)
- The EFT* calculation by Ando et al. shows cut-off dependence of only 0.02% in σνd, with the dominant uncertainty being 0.5% from the experimental value of the tritium β-decay rate
- SNPA and EFT give identical results at the 1% level
- The CD-Bonn potential (PD = 4.2%) and AV18 potential (PD = 5.8%) give practically negligible differences in σνdNC

## Context

This work extends the previous SNPA calculation by Nakamura et al. (NSGK) for neutrino-deuteron reactions at solar neutrino energies, updating input parameters and incorporating a more complete set of exchange-current diagrams based on the work of Schiavilla et al. The results are compared with an effective field theory calculation by Ando et al. (EFT*) that uses a cut-off scheme à la Weinberg, and the good agreement between SNPA and EFT supports the robustness of the calculated cross sections. These improved cross sections are directly relevant for interpreting SNO experiments measuring solar neutrino fluxes and studying neutrino oscillations.
