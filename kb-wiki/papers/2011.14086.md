# Fusion reaction of a weakly-bound nucleus with a deformed target

> We discuss the role of deformation of the target nucleus in the fusion reaction of the $^{15}$C + $^{232}$Th system at energies around the Coulomb barrier, for which $^{15}$C is a well-known one-neutron halo nucleus. To this end, we construct the potential between $^{15}$C and $^{232}$Th with the double folding procedure, assuming that the projectile nucleus is composed of the core nucleus, $^{14}$C, and a valance neutron. By taking into account the halo nature of the projectile nucleus as well as the deformation of the target nucleus, we simultaneously reproduce the fusion cross sections for the $^{14}$C + $^{232}$Th and the $^{15}$C + $^{232}$Th systems. Our calculation indicates that the net effect of the breakup and the transfer channels is small for this system.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the fusion cross sections for the weakly-bound halo nucleus \(^{15}\)C with the deformed target \(^{232}\)Th can be simultaneously reproduced with those for the core \(^{14}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th system only when the halo structure, target deformation, and coupling to the one-neutron transfer channel are all included; the net effect of breakup and transfer channels is small for this system, but the transfer coupling is essential to match the data across the full energy range.

## Method
The authors constructed the internuclear potential for the \(^{15}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th system using a double folding procedure, treating \(^{15}\)C as a \(^{14}\)C core plus a valence neutron with a \(2s_{1/2}\) wave function reproducing the empirical neutron separation energy of \(-1.21\) MeV. The target deformation was included via a deformed Woods-Saxon density with quadrupole \(\beta_{2T}=0.233\) and hexadecapole \(\beta_{4T}=0.0946\) parameters. Fusion cross sections were calculated with the incoming wave boundary condition, and a coupled-channels calculation was performed that included coupling to the one-neutron transfer channel \(^{14}\)C + \(^{233}\)Th using a derivative Woods-Saxon form factor with parameters fitted to the experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb barrier height for the \(^{15}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th system (with deformation parameters set to zero) is \(V_b = 58.81\) MeV, which is 1.53 MeV lower than the barrier for the \(^{14}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th system (60.66 MeV).
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters for the \(^{14}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th system are: depth \(V_0 = 68.174\) MeV, radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.231\) fm, diffuseness \(a = 0.548\) fm, yielding a barrier position \(R_b = 12.22\) fm and barrier curvature \(\hbar\Omega = 4.96\) MeV.
- The fitted neutron-target potential parameters for the monopole component are \(V_0 = 105.923\) MeV, \(R_0 = 7.981\) fm, \(a = 1.572\) fm; for the quadrupole component \(V_0 = 28.756\) MeV, \(R_0 = 7.080\) fm, \(a = 1.890\) fm; for the hexadecapole component \(V_0 = 4.398\) MeV, \(R_0 = 8.131\) fm, \(a = 1.944\) fm.
- The transfer coupling form factor parameters are \(F_t = 27.5\) MeV fm, \(R_{\rm coup} = 14.638\) fm, \(a_{\rm coup} = 0.69\) fm, with the transfer \(Q\)-value set to zero.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of fusion with halo nuclei, particularly the \(^{15}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th experiment reported by the authors of PhysRevLett.106.172701, which used a simple spectator model without accounting for the halo structure. The results contrast with the \(^{6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U system (Raabe 2004), which showed no significant halo effect, possibly due to the fissile nature of \(^{238}\)U. The calculation enables systematic investigations of fusion of halo nuclei with deformed targets, such as comparing \(^{15}\)C + \(^{232}\)Th with \(^{15}\)C + \(^{238}\)U and \(^{15}\)C + \(^{208}\)Pb systems.
