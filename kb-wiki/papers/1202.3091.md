# Nonextensive statistical effects in the quark-gluon plasma formation at relativistic heavy-ion collisions energies

> We investigate the relativistic equation of state of hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density in the framework of the non-extensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions. We impose the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number, electric charge and strangeness number. For the hadronic phase, we study an extended relativistic mean-field theoretical model with the inclusion of strange particles (hyperons and mesons). For the quark sector, we employ an extended MIT-Bag model. In this context we focus on the relevance of non-extensive effects in the presence of strange matter.

## Key claim

Non-extensive statistical mechanics (Tsallis statistics with q > 1) significantly modifies the equation of state of hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density, producing a strong enhancement of strange hadronic particle production and a softening of the pressure in the mixed phase, but only for small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (q ≈ 1).

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic equation of state for hadronic matter using an extended relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the GM3 parameter set, including the full baryon octet (p, n, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) and mesons treated as an ideal Bose gas. For the quark-gluon plasma phase, they employed an extended MIT Bag model with a baryon-chemical-potential-dependent effective bag constant. They imposed Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium with global conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and zero net strangeness, solving for the mixed phase region at fixed temperature T = 120 MeV and electric charge fraction Z/A = 0.4.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Effective bag constant parameters: B₀¹/⁴ = 250 MeV (at vanishing μ_B), B_∞¹/⁴ = 160 MeV (at large μ_B), μ₀ = 600 MeV, a = 320 MeV
- Critical temperature at low μ_B for q = 1: ≈ 170 MeV
- Mixed phase starts forming at density slightly exceeding 3ρ₀ for T ≈ 90–100 MeV (for q = 1)
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 150 MeV
- At the beginning of the mixed phase (ρ_B = 2ρ₀) and T = 120 MeV, the net strangeness density of baryons and mesons for q = 1.1 is about 4 times the values obtained for q = 1
- Temperature fixed at T = 120 MeV for the main phase diagram and particle concentration calculations

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same group (Lavagno et al., 2011) to include strange particles (hyperons and mesons) in the hadronic phase and strange quarks in the quark phase, applying non-extensive statistics to heavy-ion collision conditions. The results contradict standard Boltzmann-Gibbs predictions for particle yields and phase boundaries, and suggest that enhanced strange-to-non-strange particle ratios could serve as a signature of non-extensive effects in future compressed baryonic matter experiments at facilities such as AGS, where indirect indications of EOS softening have been reported by Ivanov et al. (2005), Bonanno et al. (2007), and others. The work enables future investigations with more sophisticated SU(3) chiral quark models to replace the simple MIT bag model used here.
