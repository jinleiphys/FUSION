# Protoneutron stars in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach and finite-temperature kaon condensation

> We study the properties of hot neutrino-trapped beta-stable stellar matter using an equation of state of nuclear matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach including three-body forces, combined with a standard chiral model for kaon condensation at finite temperature. The properties of (proto)neutron stars are then investigated within this framework.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the impact of kaon condensation on protoneutron star (PNS) properties at finite temperature, finding that temperature effects are minor compared to neutrino trapping, and that a delayed collapse scenario is facilitated by kaon condensation, but only for certain combinations of nuclear equation of state stiffness and kaon-nucleon interaction strength.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach at finite temperature for asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Argonne V18 two-body force supplemented by either a microscopic or phenomenological three-body force (TBF). They combined this with a standard chiral model for kaon condensation at finite temperature, solving the coupled equations for chemical equilibrium, charge neutrality, and lepton number conservation to determine the composition and equation of state of hot, neutrino-trapped beta-stable matter, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain protoneutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for kaon condensation in untrapped matter: 0.489, 0.490, 0.492, 0.497 fm⁻³ at T = 0, 10, 30, 50 MeV respectively (for a₃mₛ = -222 MeV with micro TBF)
- Critical density for kaon condensation in trapped matter (Yₑ = 0.4): 0.580, 0.583, 0.589, 0.629 fm⁻³ at T = 0, 10, 30, 50 MeV respectively (for a₃mₛ = -222 MeV with micro TBF)
- Maximum mass of cold (T=0) neutron star without kaon condensation: 2.34 M☉ (micro TBF) and 1.92 M☉ (pheno TBF)
- Maximum mass of hot (T=30 MeV) trapped PNS with kaon condensation (a₃mₛ = -222 MeV): 2.05 M☉ (micro TBF) and 1.61 M☉ (pheno TBF)
- Maximum mass of hot (T=30 MeV) untrapped PNS with kaon condensation (a₃mₛ = -222 MeV): 2.02 M☉ (micro TBF) and 1.67 M☉ (pheno TBF)
- Kaon condensation onset density ranges from approximately 0.4 fm⁻³ to 0.6 fm⁻³ in untrapped matter and 0.45 fm⁻³ to 0.75 fm⁻³ in trapped matter, depending on the a₃ parameter

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature calculations by Li et al. (2006, 2008) to finite temperature, and complements studies of hyperon effects in hot PNS matter by Burgio et al. (2003). The results are consistent with earlier findings by Thorsson et al. (1994), Tatsumi (2000), and Pons et al. (2000) regarding delayed collapse scenarios, but show smaller temperature effects than claimed by Pons et al. (2000). The paper provides analytical fits for the finite-temperature equation of state that enable future dynamical simulations of PNS evolution and supernova core collapse.
