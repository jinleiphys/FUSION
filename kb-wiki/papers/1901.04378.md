# Multi-particle and charge-dependent azimuthal correlations in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider

> We study multi-particle azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at a center of mass energy of 200 GeV. We use the IP-Glasma model to initialize the viscous hydrodynamic simulation MUSIC and employ the UrQMD transport model for the low temperature region of the collisions. In addition, we study effects of local charge and global momentum conservation among the sampled particles. With the exception of the lowest order three particle correlator $C_{112}$, our framework provides a good description of the existing charge inclusive azimuthal correlation data for Au+Au and U+U collisions at RHIC. We also present results for charge dependent two and three particle correlators in Au+Au and U+U collisions, and make predictions for isobar (Ru+Ru & Zr+Zr) collisions to provide a much needed baseline for the search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect at RHIC.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD hybrid framework provides a good description of charge-inclusive multi-particle azimuthal correlation data for Au+Au and U+U collisions at 200 GeV, with the exception of the lowest-order three-particle correlator \(C_{112}\), where the calculated sign is opposite to the experimental result even after including global momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors initialize viscous hydrodynamic simulations (MUSIC) with IP-Glasma initial conditions at \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c, using a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity (\(\eta/s = 0.12\)) and a broad-peak specific bulk viscosity (\(\zeta/s\) peaking at \(T_\mathrm{peak} = 165\) MeV with maximum \(B_\mathrm{norm} = 0.24\)). The hydrodynamic evolution is matched to the UrQMD hadronic transport model at a switching energy density \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{sw} = 0.18\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (corresponding to \(T \approx 145\) MeV), and global momentum conservation and local charge conservation are imposed during particle sampling.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity is fixed to a constant \(\eta/s = 0.12\) by matching to measured anisotropic flow coefficients of charged hadrons.
- The specific bulk viscosity peaks at \(T_\mathrm{peak} = 165\) MeV with maximum value \(B_\mathrm{norm} = 0.24\); the width parameters are \(B_\mathrm{width} = 1.5\) and \(T_\mathrm{width} = 50\) MeV.
- The hydrodynamic simulation uses a lattice spacing \(dx = dy = 0.067\) fm and time step \(d\tau = 0.005\) fm/c.
- For Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the model reproduces \(v_2\{4\}\) across all centralities, while \(v_2\{2\}\) is underestimated for centralities beyond 40% (i.e., \(N_\mathrm{part} \lesssim 100\)).
- In 20-30% Au+Au collisions, the symmetric cumulant \(10^7 SC\{2,3\}\) changes from \(-0.3(4)\) (decay only) to \(-1.1(4)\) (with UrQMD), and \(10^7 SC\{2,4\}\) changes from \(0.9(1)\) to \(1.7(2)\).
- For isobar collisions, the model predicts a 5-10% larger \(v_2\) in Ru+Ru compared to Zr+Zr for the most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of anisotropic flow (e.g., Ryu (2015), McDonald (2016)) by using a different lattice QCD equation of state (HotQCD Collaboration, 2014) and a broader peak in \(\zeta/s(T)\), which improves the description of mean transverse momentum in peripheral collisions. The framework provides a baseline for charge-dependent multi-particle correlators relevant to the Chiral Magnetic Effect search at RHIC, including predictions for the isobar run (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) that will be compared with upcoming experimental data from the STAR Collaboration. The failure to reproduce the sign of \(C_{112}\) suggests that local momentum conservation during particle sampling may need to be incorporated in future work.
