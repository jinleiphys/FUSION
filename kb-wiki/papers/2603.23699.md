# Semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on a polarized spin-1 target. I. Cross section and spin observables

> We develop the theoretical framework for semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on a polarized spin-1 target and apply it to scattering on the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging. In Part I (this article) we present the general form of the semi-inclusive cross section and polarization observables for the spin-1 target. A relativistically covariant formulation in terms of 4-vectors and invariant polarization parameters is employed. The target polarization is described by a spin density matrix with vector and tensor polarization. The spin and azimuthal angle dependence of the semi-inclusive cross section is derived and parametrized in terms of invariant structure functions. To validate the result, the structure functions are expressed as photon-target helicity amplitudes with known symmetry properties. The expressions presented here are kinematic (no assumptions about particle production dynamics) and valid in all regions of the deep-inelastic final state (current and target fragmentation regions). In Part II (following article), we consider deep-inelastic scattering on the polarized deuteron with spectator nucleon tagging as a special case of target fragmentation. The semi-inclusive structure functions are computed by separating nuclear and hadronic structure, and the polarization observables are explored as functions of the tagged nucleon momentum.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general form of the semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross section and polarization observables for a spin-1 target, using a relativistically covariant formulation that is purely kinematic and valid in all regions of the deep-inelastic final state, without assumptions about particle production dynamics.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistically covariant formulation using 4-vectors and invariant polarization parameters, describing the target polarization via a spin density matrix with both vector and tensor polarization components. The spin and azimuthal angle dependence of the semi-inclusive cross section is derived and parametrized in terms of invariant structure functions, which are then validated by expressing them as photon-target helicity amplitudes with known symmetry properties.

## Key numbers

* The spin-1/2 target SIDIS cross section involves 18 independent structures; the spin-1 case exhibits 23 additional structures, some with new azimuthal dependences not found in the spin-1/2 case.
* The target polarization is described by 3 vector polarization parameters {S_L, S_T, phi_S} and 5 tensor polarization parameters {T_LL, T_LT, T_TT, phi_TL, phi_TT}.
* Four of the semi-inclusive tensor-polarized structures reduce to the well-known inclusive tensor-polarized structures (denoted b_1 to b_4) when integrated over the identified hadron momentum; the others integrate to zero.
* The momentum transfer satisfies Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 and the energy transfer nu >> 1 GeV for the deep-inelastic regime.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for SIDIS from spin-1/2 targets (as reviewed by Kotzinian (1994), Diehl (2005), Bacchetta (2006), and others) to spin-1 targets, building on earlier work on inclusive DIS on tensor-polarized deuterons by Frankfurt (1983) and Hoodbhoy (1988). The formalism enables future quantitative analysis of SIDIS experiments on polarized deuterons with spectator nucleon tagging at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provides the foundation for Part II of this study which computes the specific case of deuteron DIS with spectator nucleon tagging.
