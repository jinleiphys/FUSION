# The Phase Diagram of Two Color QCD

> I present recent results from lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theory with Nf=2 Wilson quark flavors, at non-zero quark chemical potential. The thermodynamic equation of state is discussed along with the nature of the high density matter which forms. It is conjectured that deconfinement may mean different things for bulk and Fermi surface phenomena.

# The Phase Diagram of Two Color QCD

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theory with two quark flavors at non-zero chemical potential, but the authors now conclude that previously reported signals of Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of tightly bound diquarks are most likely infrared lattice artifacts rather than physical phenomena. The key hedge is that deconfinement may mean different things for bulk thermodynamic quantities versus Fermi surface phenomena.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations using the Wilson plaquette action with unimproved Wilson fermions and a hopping parameter κ, introducing chemical potential via weighting forward/backward temporal hops by e^(±μa). A scalar, isoscalar, gauge-invariant diquark source term jqq was introduced to mitigate infrared fluctuations from Goldstone modes associated with superfluidity. Matched ensembles were accumulated on a coarse 8³×16 lattice with a=0.229(3) fm and a finer 12³×24 lattice with a=0.178(6) fm, with scale set by assuming the two-color string tension is (440 MeV)², and temperatures ranging from 47 to 141 MeV by varying Nτ from 24 to 8.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a=0.229(3) fm on the coarse 8³×16 lattice and a=0.178(6) fm on the finer 12³×24 lattice
- Temperature T≈50 MeV for the cold lattices, with simulations at T=47, 141 MeV
- Quark mass corresponds to mπ/mρ≈0.8
- Onset threshold at μo=mπ/2≈0.32a⁻¹≈360 MeV
- Crossover to quarkyonic regime at μQ≈0.5a⁻¹≈530 MeV
- Deconfinement transition at μd≈0.8a⁻¹≈850 MeV
- Quark density at deconfinement is 16–32 fm⁻³, some 30–60 times that of nuclear matter
- Diquark source term values ja=0.04, 0.02 were used

## Context
This work extends lattice studies of two-color QCD (QC₂D) at non-zero chemical potential, which offers a tractable model for dense baryonic matter because the path integral measure is positive definite for even Nf, avoiding the sign problem of real QCD. The authors reference their prior work (QC2D1, QC2D2, QC2D3) and contrast their findings with mean-field theory predictions (KSTVZ) and large-Nc arguments for quarkyonic matter (quarkyonic). The results challenge earlier interpretations of BEC signals and suggest that simulations closer to the chiral limit are needed to expose BEC behavior. The work enables future studies of the relationship between confinement, superfluidity, and Fermi surface phenomena in dense gauge theories.
