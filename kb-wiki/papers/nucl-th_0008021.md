# Final State Interaction in Exclusive $(e,e'NN)$ Reactions

> Contributions of nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, meson exchange currents and the residual final state interactions (FSI) on exclusive two-nucleon knock-out reactions induced by electron scattering are investigated. All contributions are derived from the same realistic meson exchange model for the NN interaction. Effects of correlations and FSI are determined in a consistent way by solving the NN scattering equation, the Bethe-Goldstone equation, for two nucleons in nuclear matter. One finds that the FSI re-scattering terms are non-negligible even if the two nucleons are emitted back to back.

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) re-scattering terms are non-negligible in exclusive two-nucleon knock-out reactions even when the two nucleons are emitted back to back, contradicting the common assumption that such effects are minimal in that kinematics.

## Method

The authors extended their previous nuclear matter approach (Knödler, Müther, and Czerski (2000)) to include FSI effects alongside nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, meson exchange currents (MEC), and isobar currents (IC). All contributions were derived consistently from the same realistic meson exchange model (the Bonn A potential) by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for two nucleons in nuclear matter at saturation density, using the G-matrix for bound-state correlations and the T-matrix for above-Fermi-energy re-scattering.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.35 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\)
- Effective mass parameter: \(m^* = 623 \, \text{MeV}\); single-particle potential: \(U = -86.8 \, \text{MeV}\)
- Photon energy used in all super-parallel kinematics: \(\omega = 215 \, \text{MeV}\)
- For asymmetric energy splitting in super-parallel \(pp\) knock-out (proton kinetic energies \(T_{p,1}=156 \, \text{MeV}\) and \(T_{p,2}=33 \, \text{MeV}\)), FSI contribution is almost as large as the correlation contribution
- For symmetric energy splitting in super-parallel \(pp\) knock-out (\(T_{p,1}=116 \, \text{MeV}\) and \(T_{p,2}=73 \, \text{MeV}\)), structure functions are reduced by almost one order of magnitude compared to the asymmetric case
- For \(pn\) knock-out, structure functions are almost one order of magnitude larger than for \(pp\) knock-out
- For the symmetric \(30^\circ\) kinematics (\(T_p = T_n = 70 \, \text{MeV}\), \(\omega = 230 \, \text{MeV}\)), FSI contribution in the longitudinal channel of \((e,e'pp)\) is the dominating one
- FSI contributions in \((e,e'pn)\) are about twice as large as in \((e,e'pp)\) for comparable kinematics

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter approach of Knödler, Müther, and Czerski (2000) by adding FSI re-scattering effects to the previously studied correlations, MEC, and IC contributions. It provides a consistent framework for comparing the relative importance of these mechanisms across different kinematics, complementing studies on finite nuclei such as those by Giusti, Pacati, and collaborators (1998, 1999) on \(^{16}\text{O}\) targets. The results enable systematic interpretation of exclusive \((e,e'NN)\) experiments by clarifying when FSI must be included alongside correlation and MEC effects in theoretical analyses.
