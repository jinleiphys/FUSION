# Nonlocal structure of the leading order \textit{ab initio} effective potentials for proton elastic scattering from light nuclei

> Based on the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series we employ a chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) nucleon-nucleon interaction on the same footing in the structure as well as in the reaction calculation to obtain an in leading-order consistent effective potential for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering, which includes the spin of the struck target nucleon. As an example we present proton scattering off $^{12}$C.

## Key claim

The leading-order *ab initio* effective potential for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering, derived from the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory using a chiral NNLO nucleon-nucleon interaction consistently in both structure and reaction calculations, produces differential cross sections for proton-12C scattering that are well described up to momentum transfer q ~ 2 fm^-1, though the analyzing power at 122 MeV is slightly over-predicted at smaller q.

## Method

The authors employ the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory, where the leading order term involves two-body interactions between the projectile and one target nucleon, requiring a convolution of fully off-shell NN scattering amplitudes with nonlocal one-body nuclear densities from the no-core shell model (NCSM). The chiral NNLO_opt nucleon-nucleon interaction is used on the same footing for both the NCSM structure calculation (with N_max=10 and hbar-omega=20 MeV) and the reaction calculation. The effective potential is expressed in the Wolfenstein representation, including spin-orbit terms from the projectile and the spin of the struck target nucleon, with separate treatment of proton and neutron contributions summed over target nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for proton-12C elastic scattering are well described up to momentum transfer q ~ 2 fm^-1 at both 122 MeV and 200 MeV projectile energy
- The NCSM structure calculation uses N_max=10 and hbar-omega=20 MeV, with a variation band shown for hbar-omega=16 and 24 MeV
- The radius obtained from the NCSM ground state calculation of 12C is about 2 fm, consistent with the effective potential having largest contributions for r=r' less than or equal to 2 fm
- The effective potential is shown for 100 MeV projectile energy as a function of momenta p and p' at fixed angle theta=10 degrees

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* approach to nucleon-nucleus scattering by using the same chiral NN interaction for both structure and reaction calculations, building on the spectator expansion framework. The nonlocal structure of the real central s-wave effective potential for 12C is quite similar to that described by Arellano (2018) for the heavier nucleus 40Ca using a folding with a two-nucleon g-matrix, and also shows similar characteristics to a real s-wave potential calculated from a Green's function approach combined with the coupled cluster method for 16O by Rotureau (2016). This enables consistent *ab initio* calculations of elastic scattering observables for light nuclei, with the potential for extension to higher-order terms in the spectator expansion.
