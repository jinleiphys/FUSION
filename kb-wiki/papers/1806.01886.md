# The Fullerene-like Structure of Superheavy Element Z=120 (Greinerium)-a tribute to Walter Greiner

> We review the basic ideas and some theoretical models behind the concept of fullerene-like structures made of $α$-particles. The possibility of such a peculiar nuclear shape developing in a double magic superheavy nucleus with $Z$=120 was mentioned for the first time in the literature by Walter Greiner and then constantly defended by him. We provide estimates of the energy of such metastable states within the liquid-drop model. In the second part of this paper we discuss a simple model rooted in the nontopological soliton model consisting of a complex scalar field describing a finite system of $α$ bosons with non-linear self-interactions coupled to the electromagnetic field. We demonstrate that this model predicts density depletion in the central region of a soliton-like structure for a range of model parameters.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a superheavy nucleus with Z=120 (Greinerium) could exist in a metastable fullerene-like configuration composed of 60 alpha-particles arranged on a buckminsterfullerene lattice, but only if the outer shell is doped with additional neutrons and the central region is nearly hollow; the liquid-drop model shows that a perfect bubble with a completely hollow center is the most favourable configuration for the nucleus 292120.

## Method

The authors first estimate the stability of a semi-bubble nuclear shape using a liquid-drop model that includes Coulomb and surface energies (via a Yukawa-plus-exponential interaction) for the superheavy nucleus 292120, varying the ratio of inner to outer radius (breathing deformation) and the number of excess neutrons in the outer shell. They then construct a nontopological soliton model (charged Q-ball) where a complex scalar field describes alpha-particles with quartic and sextic self-interactions, coupled to the electromagnetic field, and solve the coupled nonlinear differential equations for the scalar field profile and the electrostatic potential using a Runge-Kutta method of order 5 and 6.

## Key numbers

* For the nucleus 292120, a bubble-like state with R1 ≈ 0.6 R2 is unstable when the outer shell contains equal numbers of protons and neutrons (ΔN_shell = 0); adding 24, 40, or 52 neutrons to the outer shell produces a metastable state with decreasing breathing deformation.
* The squared charge of the alpha-particle is taken as q² = 4 × 1.44/(ℏc) = 0.029.
* The scalar potential parameters used for the Q-ball profile calculation are g₂ = -30000 and g₃ = 4786193.6 fm², with the sextic strength λ² set to approximately 0.47 (the value where the nontrivial minimum of the potential disappears).
* The Q-ball radius for total alpha-particle number Q is given by R = [3Q/(4π φ₀ √(2U(φ₀)))]^(1/3) (1 + C^(2/3)/45), where C = 3e³Q √(2π φ₀⁴/U(φ₀)).

## Context

This work extends the earlier conjecture by Walter Greiner (Greiner, 2001) that superheavy nuclei near Z=120 could form hollow, fullerene-like structures of alpha-particles, based on relativistic mean-field calculations (Misicu, 2002) that showed central density depletion. The paper contrasts with standard liquid-drop treatments of uniform nuclear matter and with earlier alpha-cluster models for light N=Z nuclei (Oertzen, 2006). It enables future studies of collective excitations (giant resonances) and alpha-decay channels in such exotic shapes, and suggests that experimental searches for superheavy elements should consider these hollow configurations as a possibility.
