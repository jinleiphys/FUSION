# Isospin breaking in pion-deuteron scattering and the pion-nucleon scattering lengths

> In recent years, high-accuracy data for pionic hydrogen and deuterium have become the primary source of information on the pion-nucleon scattering lengths. Matching the experimental precision requires, in particular, the study of isospin-breaking corrections both in pion-nucleon and pion-deuteron scattering. We review the mechanisms that lead to the cancellation of potentially enhanced virtual-photon corrections in the pion-deuteron system, and discuss the subtleties regarding the definition of the pion-nucleon scattering lengths in the presence of electromagnetic interactions by comparing to nucleon-nucleon scattering. Based on the pi^{+/-} p channels we find for the virtual-photon-subtracted scattering lengths in the isospin basis a^{1/2}=(170.5 +/- 2.0) x 10^{-3} mpi^{-1} and a^{3/2}=(-86.5 +/- 1.8) x 10^{-3} mpi^{-1}.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that potentially enhanced virtual-photon corrections in pion-deuteron scattering cancel due to the Pauli principle and wave-function orthogonality, so that the dominant isospin-breaking effects for extracting pion-nucleon scattering lengths come from next-to-leading order corrections in the pion-nucleon amplitude rather than from three-body dynamics in the deuteron.

## Method

The authors analyze isospin-breaking corrections in threshold pion-deuteron scattering using chiral effective field theory, separating mass-difference insertions from virtual-photon contributions. They evaluate the infrared-enhanced momentum scales in both isovector and isoscalar pion-nucleon interactions, showing that the leading cancellations arise from the Pauli principle forcing intermediate nucleon-nucleon pairs into P-wave (isovector) and from the orthogonality of bound-state and continuum wave functions (isoscalar). The results are combined with data from pionic hydrogen and deuterium to extract the pion-nucleon scattering lengths in the isospin basis after subtracting virtual-photon effects.

## Key numbers

- Virtual-photon contribution to the pion-deuteron scattering length: a^EM = (0.95 ± 0.01) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Isoscalar scattering length: a^+ = (7.6 ± 3.1) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Isovector scattering length: a^- = (86.1 ± 0.9) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Virtual-photon-subtracted scattering length in the isospin 1/2 channel: a^{1/2}_{slashed gamma} = (170.5 ± 2.0) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Virtual-photon-subtracted scattering length in the isospin 3/2 channel: a^{3/2}_{slashed gamma} = (-86.5 ± 1.8) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant from the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule: g_c^2/4π = 13.7 ± 0.2
- Next-to-leading order isospin-breaking shift: Δ a^+ = (-3.3 ± 0.3) × 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pionic atom data from the Juelich-Osaka-Bonn collaboration (JOB, 2011) by systematically evaluating isospin-breaking corrections in pion-deuteron scattering. The results validate the chiral power counting for the three-body contributions and provide the virtual-photon-subtracted scattering lengths needed as input for dispersive analyses of pion-nucleon scattering, such as the work by Ditsche, Hoferichter, Kubis, and Meissner (DHKM, 2012). The paper also clarifies the scale dependence of virtual-photon subtraction by comparing to the well-studied case of proton-proton scattering, where the Coulomb-subtracted scattering length a_pp = (-17.3 ± 0.4) fm differs dramatically from the Coulomb-included value a_pp^C = (-7.8063 ± 0.0026) fm.
