# Vortex Rings in Event-by-Event Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present event-by-event simulations for central asymmetric light+heavy and Au+Au collisions to investigate the formation and evolution of vortex-ring structures in the longitudinal flow velocity profile. The production-plane polarization of $Λ$ hyperons, defined w.r.t. the $Λ$ momentum and the beam, can track the "vortex-ring" feature in the event, a characteristic vortical structure generated by longitudinal flow gradients. We make comprehensive model predictions for the rapidity-dependent vortex-ring observables for different collision system sizes at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ and 72 GeV. Our predictions at the latter energy can be explored in the future LHCb fixed-target experiment at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim
Event-by-event simulations with lumpy initial conditions and impact parameter fluctuations confirm that the production-plane polarization of Λ hyperons can track vortex-ring structures in the longitudinal flow velocity profile, but the observable shows strong sensitivity to the amount of initial-state longitudinal flow (parameterized by f between 0 and 1) and to the transverse hotspot size w, with the slope of the pT-differential observable potentially disentangling these two effects.

## Method
The authors performed (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-MUSIC framework with geometric-based 3D initial conditions that map nuclear thickness functions to the energy-momentum tensor at τ0 = 1 fm/c, conserving orbital angular momentum. The initial longitudinal flow is parameterized by a factor f ∈ [0,1] controlling how much net longitudinal momentum is attributed to flow velocity, with f=0 recovering Bjorken flow. The Λ hyperon spin polarization is computed via the Cooper-Frye-like formula from the thermal vorticity tensor at freeze-out on a constant energy density hypersurface of e_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm³, using a constant QGP specific shear viscosity ηT/(e+P) = 0.08.

## Key numbers
- Λ polarization observable RΛ^ẑ shows values about a factor of 10 different between f=0 and f=1 simulations for central p+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- RΛ^ẑ(pT) scales linearly with pT for pT > 1 GeV at mid-rapidity
- For central p+Pb and ⁴He+Pb collisions at √sNN = 72 GeV with f=0, the maximum RΛ^ẑ(η) could be about twice the values in similar small systems at 200 GeV
- Hydrodynamic starting time τ0 = 1 fm/c, freeze-out energy density e_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Model parameters: η0 = 5.0 (σ_η = 0.55) at √sNN = 200 GeV; η0 = 4.5 (σ_η = 0.5) at √sNN = 72 GeV

## Context
This work extends the vortex-ring studies first discussed in Lisa (2021) from smooth initial conditions to event-by-event simulations with lumpy initial conditions and impact parameter fluctuations, confirming the robustness of the observable. The results build on the 3D initial model developed by Shen (2020), Ryu (2021), and Alzhrani (2022) and connect to experimental measurements from PHENIX (2018) and PHOBOS (2005) for charged hadron distributions. The predictions at √sNN = 72 GeV are designed for future measurements in the LHCb fixed-target SMOG experiment and could also be accessed by the STAR forward spectrometer, enabling quantitative comparison with earlier experimental results interpreted via QCD spin-orbit couplings from Heller (1977), Hauenstein (2016), Bunce (1976), Agakishiev (2014), and Abt (2006).
