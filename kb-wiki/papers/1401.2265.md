# Elliptic Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions at Energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2.7--39 GeV

> The transverse-momentum integrated elliptic flow of charged particles at midrapidity, $v_2$(charged), and that of identified hadrons from Au+Au collisions are computed in a wide range of incident energies 2.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$ 39 GeV. The simulations are performed within a three-fluid model employing three different equations of state (EoS's): a purely hadronic EoS and two versions of the EoS involving the deconfinement transition---a first-order phase transition and a smooth crossover one. The present simulations demonstrate low sensitivity of $v_2$(charged) to the EoS. All considered scenarios equally well reproduce recent STAR data on $v_2$(charged) for mid-central Au+Au collisions and properly describe its change of sign at the incident energy decrease below $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx$ 3.5 GeV. The predicted integrated elliptic flow of various species exhibits a stronger dependence on the EoS. A noticeable sensitivity to the EoS is found for anti-baryons and, to a lesser extent, for $K^-$ mesons. In particular, the $v_2$ excitation functions of anti-baryons exhibit a non-monotonicity within the deconfinement scenarios that was predicted by Kolb, Sollfrank and Heinz. However, low multiplicities of anti-baryons at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq$ 10 GeV result in large fluctuations of their $v_2$ which may wash out this non-monotonicity.

## Key claim

The transverse-momentum integrated elliptic flow of charged particles shows low sensitivity to the equation of state (EoS) across all three scenarios tested (purely hadronic, first-order phase transition, and smooth crossover), with all scenarios equally reproducing STAR data; however, the integrated elliptic flow of identified hadrons, particularly anti-baryons and K⁻ mesons, exhibits a stronger dependence on the EoS, though low multiplicities of anti-baryons at collision energies below √sNN = 10 GeV may wash out the predicted non-monotonicity in their v₂ excitation functions.

## Method

The authors performed simulations within a three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, employing three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a two-phase EoS with a first-order deconfinement transition, and a crossover EoS with a smooth deconfinement transition. The model was applied to Au+Au collisions across incident energies from √sNN = 2.7 GeV to 39 GeV, computing the transverse-momentum integrated elliptic flow (v₂) at midrapidity for charged particles and identified hadrons, with a transverse momentum cutoff of pₜ < 2 GeV/c. The calculated v₂ values were rescaled using a factor based on the ratio of eccentricities calculated with realistic nuclear surface diffuseness (d = 0.6 fm) versus zero diffuseness (d = 0) to correct for the sharp-edged nuclear spheres used in the 3FD model.

## Key numbers

- The energy range studied is √sNN = 2.7 GeV to 39 GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The freeze-out energy density used in the model is ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³.
- The formation time for the fireball fluid is τ = 2 fm/c for the hadronic scenario and τ = 0.17 fm/c for scenarios involving deconfinement transition.
- The calculated v₂ values have an estimated accuracy not worse than 15%.
- A weak non-monotonicity (peak) in the charged particle v₂ is observed at √sNN ≈ 8 GeV for the first-order phase transition scenario.
- The elliptic flow changes sign at incident energies below √sNN ≈ 3.5 GeV.
- At impact parameter b = 2 fm, the calculated v₂ is approximately twice as low compared with STAR data.
- The eccentricity ε_BN(d = 0.6 fm) for mid-central collisions (b = 5-9 fm) is close to ε_part{2} from the Glauber model.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow within the 3FD model (Ivanov et al., 2013) and addresses the controversial question of whether elliptic flow can signal the onset of deconfinement, as originally predicted by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (2000). The results contradict earlier claims by Kestin and Heinz (2008) that phase transition signatures would be washed out by viscous effects, while supporting findings by Petersen et al. (2010) that elliptic flow at SPS energies is more sensitive to viscosity than to the EoS. The study enables future comparisons with data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, as well as from facilities such as FAIR and NICA, and provides a consistent framework for analyzing correlations among multiple observables without observable-dependent parameter tuning.
