# Impact of the neutron matter equation of state on neutron skin and neutron drip lines in chiral effective field theory

> We present predictions of the binding energy per nucleon and the neutron skin thickness in highly neutron-rich isotopes of Oxygen, Magnesium, and Aluminum. The calculations are carried out at and below the neutron drip line as predicted by our model. The nuclear properties are obtained via an energy functional whose input is the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric infinite matter. The latter is based on a microscopic derivation of the energy per particle in neutron matter applying chiral few-nucleon forces together with a phenomenological model for the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter. We highlight the impact of the neutron matter equation of state at different orders of chiral effective field theory on neutron skins and the binding energy per particle and quantify the uncertainty carried by our predictions.

## Key claim

The neutron matter equation of state (EoS) at different orders of chiral effective field theory directly impacts the predicted binding energy per nucleon and neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich isotopes of Oxygen, Magnesium, and Aluminum, but the authors limit themselves to exploring this impact without making claims about convergence of the EFT predictions from LO to N3LO, since the symmetric nuclear matter EoS is taken from phenomenology rather than calculated microscopically.

## Method

The authors compute the neutron matter EoS microscopically using chiral few-nucleon forces at LO, NLO, N2LO, and N3LO in the chiral power counting, employing a nonperturbative particle-particle ladder approximation. They combine this with a phenomenological Skyrme-type EoS for symmetric nuclear matter having a saturation point at 0.16 fm^-3 with -16.0 MeV energy per particle. Nuclear properties for finite nuclei are obtained via an energy functional based on the semi-empirical mass formula, using Thomas-Fermi distributions for proton and neutron densities that are optimized by energy minimization.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon in 20O at N3LO: 7.709 ± 0.01 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in 20O at N3LO: 0.227 ± 0.005 fm
- Binding energy per nucleon in 28O at N3LO: 6.142 ± 0.040 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in 28O at N3LO: 0.649 ± 0.019 fm
- Binding energy per nucleon in 40Mg at N3LO: 6.797 ± 0.045 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in 40Mg at N3LO: 0.574 ± 0.020 fm
- Binding energy per nucleon in 44Al at N3LO: 6.709 ± 0.027 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in 44Al at N3LO: 0.598 ± 0.022 fm
- Energy per neutron in neutron matter at N3LO at k_F^n = 1.39 fm^-1: 10.063 ± 0.58 MeV
- The phenomenological symmetric nuclear matter EoS has saturation density 0.16 fm^-3 with energy per particle -16.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' systematic efforts (Sammarruca 2014) to explore the nuclear equation of state through applications ranging from neutron skins to neutron stars. It uses chiral EFT interactions from Refs. (NLO, EM03, ME11) with cutoff 450 MeV, and the density-dependent effective 3NF from Holt et al. (2009, 2010). The results are relevant for interpreting upcoming Jefferson Laboratory measurements of neutron skin thickness in 208Pb (recently measured as 0.33 +0.16 -0.18 fm) and potentially 48Ca, and for guiding radioactive beam experiments exploring neutron drip lines, where 26O is found to be just unbound (Kondo) and 40Mg and 42Al are predicted as drip line nuclei (Bauger, Heenen).
