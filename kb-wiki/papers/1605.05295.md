# Shear Viscosity of Turbulent Chiral Plasma

> It is well known that the difference between the chemical potentials of left-handed and right-handed particles in a parity violating (chiral) plasma can lead to an instability. We show that the chiral instability may drive turbulent transport. Further we estimate the anomalous viscosity of chiral plasma arising from the enhanced collisionality due to turbulence.

## Key claim

The authors show that the chiral plasma instability (CPI), arising from a difference between left- and right-handed chemical potentials, can drive turbulent transport; they estimate the resulting anomalous shear viscosity from the enhanced effective collisionality due to turbulence, but the calculation is performed in the quasi-static limit (|ω| ≪ k) and assumes strong turbulence where the decorrelation rate dominates over the wave-particle resonance width.

## Method

The authors start from the Berry-curvature modified collisionless Vlasov equation for chiral fermions and perform a linear response analysis to derive the polarization tensor, obtaining the dispersion relation for transverse waves in the quasi-static limit. They then apply Resonance Broadening theory (following Dupree and Diamond) to incorporate nonlinear particle-wave interactions, computing the diffusion coefficient and decorrelation time from the turbulent magnetic field fluctuations at saturation, and finally relate these to the anomalous shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

- The instability growth rate in the quasi-static limit is given by ρ(k) = (4αμ₅ / π²m²_D) k² [1 − πk / (μ₅α_e)], where α = e²/(4π) is the electromagnetic coupling and μ₅ = μ_R − μ_L is the chiral chemical potential.
- The wavenumber of maximum instability is k_max = 2μ₅α / (3π).
- The Debye mass squared is m²_D = e²[(μ_R² + μ_L²)/(2π²) + T²/3].
- The parity-odd coefficient is C_E = e²μ₅ / (4π²).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chiral plasma instabilities studied by Akamatsu (2013) and others, who showed that chiral imbalance can lead to growth of magnetic helicity, and by Hong (2014), who demonstrated that Weibel instability can lower the shear viscosity in heavy-ion collision contexts. The present paper applies a similar methodology to chiral plasmas, relevant for compact star interiors (where magnetic fields of order 10¹⁶ G may be generated, as discussed by Akira (2014) and Dvornikov (2015)) and for the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable estimation of transport coefficients in regimes where binary collision frequencies become very small (e.g., low-temperature neutron star matter).
