# The $^3$He$(e,e^\prime p)^2$H and $^4$He$(e,e^\prime p)^3$H reactions at high momentum transfer

> We present updated calculations for observables in the processes $^3$He($e,e^\prime p$)$^2$H, $^4$He($e,e^\prime p$)$^3$H, and $^4$He($\vec{e},e^\prime \vec{p}\,$)$^3$H. This update entails the implementation of improved nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) amplitudes to describe final state interactions (FSI) within a Glauber approximation and includes full spin-isospin dependence in the profile operator. In addition, an optical potential, which has also been updated since previous work, is utilized to treat FSI for the $^4$He($e,e^\prime p$)$^3$H and $^4$He($\vec{e},e^\prime \vec{p}\,$)$^3$H reactions. The calculations are compared with experimental data and show good agreement between theory and experiment. Comparisons are made between the various approximations in the Glauber treatment, including model dependence due to the $NN$ scattering amplitudes, rescattering contributions, and spin dependence. We also analyze the validity of the Glauber approximation at the kinematics the data is available, by comparing to the results obtained with the optical potential.

## Key claim

The updated calculations, which use improved nucleon-nucleon (NN) amplitudes from the SAID analysis and an updated optical potential, show good agreement with experimental data for the 3He(e,e'p)2H and 4He(e,e'p)3H reactions at high momentum transfer, but the validity of the Glauber approximation is explicitly tested by comparing it to optical-potential results at the available kinematics.

## Method

The authors computed observables for two-body electrodisintegration of 3He and 4He using non-relativistic wave functions from the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian. Final-state interactions (FSI) were treated either with a Glauber approximation (including full spin-isospin dependence in the profile operator derived from SAID NN amplitudes) or, for the 4He reactions, with an optical potential whose parameters were adjusted to reproduce elastic, charge-exchange, and induced polarization data.

## Key numbers

- For the 3He(e,e'p)2H reaction, the Glauber approximation with single and double rescattering and meson-exchange currents (MEC) shows that double rescattering becomes significant for missing momentum \(p_m > 200\) MeV/c.
- For the 4He(e,e'p)3H reaction, three kinematic setups were used: CQ2 with \(q \simeq 1.43\) GeV and \(\omega \simeq 0.52\) GeV in quasi-perpendicular kinematics; PY1 with beam energy about 2.4 GeV and scattering angle 16.9 degrees; PY2 with beam energy about 3.2 GeV and scattering angle 18.9 degrees, both covering \(0 \lesssim p_m \lesssim 500\) MeV/c.
- The optical potential parameters for the charge-exchange central term have a real part given by \([7.60 - 0.033\, T_{\rm lab}({\rm MeV})]\) MeV with radius 1.2 fm and diffuseness 0.15 fm, and an imaginary part given by \([0.893 - 0.0025\, T_{\rm lab}({\rm MeV})]\) MeV with radius 1.8 fm and diffuseness 0.2 fm.
- For the 4He(e,e'p)3H reaction at the highest Q2 values, the proton electric and magnetic form factors were taken from the parameterization of Brash (2002) at Q2 = 1.6 (GeV/c)2 and 2.6 (GeV/c)2.

## Context

This work updates and extends the calculations of Schiavilla (2005a, 2005b) by implementing improved NN amplitudes from the SAID analysis (Arndt 2000, 2007) instead of the earlier forward-scattering parametrization of Wallace (1981), and by adjusting the optical potential to fit new induced polarization data from Malace (2011). The results enable more reliable interpretation of Jefferson Lab experiments E89-044, E97-111, and the polarization measurements of Strauch (2003) and Paolone (2010), and provide a benchmark for testing the Glauber approximation against optical-potential methods in few-body knockout reactions.
