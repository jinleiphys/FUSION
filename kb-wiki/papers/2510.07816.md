# Probing the Neutron Skin with Extreme Collision Geometries in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Understanding how protons and neutrons are located differently in an atomic nucleus can provide fundamental information on nuclear structure and have far-reaching implications for astrophysics. A precise determination of this important difference, often quantified by the so-called neutron skin thickness, is challenging both theoretically and experimentally. Here we show how one can use a new category of observables in heavy ion collisions to probe the neutron skin thickness of nuclei like $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca, by utilizing the asymmetry between neutrons and protons of spectator nucleons in super-central collisions as well as that of participant nucleons in peripheral collisions. Using quantitative simulations, we demonstrate their sensitivity and great potential in constraining neutron skin thickness for both $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca nuclei in these extreme event geometries. Furthermore, we propose the asymmetric collisions between $^{48}$Ca and $^{40}$Ca nuclei as a unique and powerful way to nail down the neutron skin thickness.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that new observables in heavy-ion collisions, specifically spectator neutron counts in super-central collisions and participant neutron/proton asymmetry in peripheral collisions, can provide independent constraints on the neutron skin thickness of nuclei like 208Pb and 48Ca, with the potential to improve upon current constraints from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as PREX and CREX.

## Method

The authors used Monte Carlo Glauber simulations to model PbPb and CaCa collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, sampling nucleon positions from Woods-Saxon distributions with fixed proton parameters and tunable neutron radius parameters. For peripheral collisions, they employed the AVFD hydrodynamic simulation framework to translate initial participant neutron/proton asymmetries into final-state hadron yield ratios. They also simulated asymmetric 40Ca48Ca collisions to exploit forward-backward spectator neutron asymmetry as a probe of the 48Ca neutron skin.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, PREX reports delta_np = 0.283 +/- 0.071 fm; for 48Ca, CREX reports delta_np = 0.121 +/- 0.035 fm.
- For PbPb super-central collisions (0-0.1% centrality) at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the average number of spectator neutrons increases from approximately 2 (for delta_np = 0 fm) to approximately 4 (for delta_np = 0.3 fm), as shown in Figure 2.
- For 48Ca48Ca super-central collisions (0-0.1% centrality), the average spectator neutron number increases from approximately 1.5 (for delta_np = 0 fm) to approximately 2.5 (for delta_np = 0.2 fm).
- For 40Ca48Ca asymmetric collisions in 0-0.1% centrality, the ratio of forward-to-backward spectator neutrons reaches approximately 2.0 for delta_np = 0.2 fm, compared to approximately 1.0 for delta_np = 0 fm.
- The projected uncertainty on the spectator neutron observable based on current PREX results is at the 25% level; for the asymmetric Ca collision observable based on CREX results, the uncertainty is close to 30%.

## Context

This work extends the use of heavy-ion collisions to probe nuclear structure beyond the established approach of collective flow observables (e.g., STAR 2024, Jia 2022, Giacalone 2019). It directly addresses the neutron skin thickness problem previously constrained by parity-violating electron scattering experiments PREX (2021) and CREX (2022), which reported differing values for 208Pb and 48Ca. The proposed observables enable future measurements at RHIC and LHC to provide independent constraints on neutron skin thickness, and the asymmetric 40Ca48Ca collision scheme offers a unique way to isolate the 48Ca neutron skin effect.
