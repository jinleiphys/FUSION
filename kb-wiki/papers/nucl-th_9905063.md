# Effects of Nonlocal One-Pion-Exchange Potential in Deuteron

> The off-shell aspects of the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) are discussed. Relativistic Hamiltonians containing relativistic kinetic energy, relativistic OPEP with various off-shell behaviors and Argonne $v_{18}$ short-range parameterization are used to study the deuteron properties. The OPEP off-shell behaviors depend on whether a pseudovector or pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling is used and are characterized by a parameter $μ$. We study potentials having $μ$=-1, 0 and +1 and we find that they are nearly unitarily equivalent. We also find that a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian containing local potentials and nonrelativistic kinetic energy provides a good approximation to a Hamiltonian containing a relativistic OPEP based on pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling and relativistic kinetic energy.

## Key claim

The off-shell nonlocalities in the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP), characterized by the parameter μ, produce noticeably different deuteron D-state probabilities (4.98% for μ=-1, 5.73% for μ=0, 6.26% for μ=+1), but these potentials are nearly unitarily equivalent; a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian with local potentials and nonrelativistic kinetic energy provides a good approximation to a Hamiltonian containing relativistic OPEP based on pseudovector (μ=+1) pion-nucleon coupling and relativistic kinetic energy.

## Method

Relativistic Hamiltonians were constructed containing relativistic kinetic energy, relativistic OPEP with off-shell behaviors corresponding to μ=-1 (pseudoscalar coupling), μ=0 (minimal nonlocality), and μ=+1 (pseudovector coupling), plus the Argonne v18 short-range parameterization. The fifteen free parameters in the short-range part were adjusted to fit the NN phase shifts in the S=1, T=0 channel (³S₁, E₁, ³D₁, ³D₂, ³D₃, E₃, ³G₃) and the deuteron binding energy. Unitary transformations relating the different μ potentials were tested by comparing exact solutions of the Schrödinger equation with unitarily transformed wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron D-state probability P_D = 4.98% for v18^{μ=-1}, 5.73% for v18^{μ=0}, and 6.26% for v18^{μ=+1} (compared to 5.76% for nonrelativistic AV18 and 4.83% for CD-Bonn)
- Deuteron binding energy fixed to -2.242 MeV by construction for all v18^μ potentials (experimental value -2.224 MeV from full AV18 with electromagnetic interactions)
- Using Λ_π = 1.7 GeV instead of 1.2 GeV in v18^{μ=-1} gave nearly identical results: P_D = 4.97%, D/S ratio = 0.0240, quadrupole moment Q_d = 0.0267 fm²
- Deuteron quadrupole moment difference between v18^{μ=-1} and CD-Bonn is approximately 1.5%
- Triton binding energies from local potentials: -7.63 MeV (Nijmegen II), -7.62 MeV (Reid93), -7.61 MeV (AV18); from nonlocal CD-Bonn: -8.00 MeV; experimental value: -8.48 MeV
- Deuteron D-state probabilities from local potentials: 5.70% (Nijmegen II), 5.64% (Reid93), 5.76% (AV18); from CD-Bonn: 4.83%

## Context

This work extends Friar's (1999) categorization of off-shell ambiguities in two-body potentials and tests the unitary equivalence theorem relating pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion-nucleon couplings, which Friar (1977, 1980) suggested two decades earlier. The study was motivated by the observation that CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 1996) gives markedly different deuteron P_D (4.83%) and triton energy (-8.00 MeV) compared to local potentials like AV18 (Wiringa, 1995), Nijmegen II, and Reid93 (Stoks et al., 1994). The results enable the use of simpler local nonrelativistic Hamiltonians for nuclei with A>3, where momentum-space techniques face computational difficulties, while confirming that the different deuteron P_D values are associated with OPEP off-shell behaviors rather than short-range potential differences.
