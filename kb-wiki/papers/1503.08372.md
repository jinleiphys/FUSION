# Precise determination of $^{12}_Λ$C level structure by $γ$-ray spectroscopy

> Level structure of the $^{12}_Λ$C hypernucleus was precisely determined by means of $γ$-ray spectroscopy. We identified four $γ$-ray transitions via the $^{12}$C$(π^{+},K^{+}γ)$ reaction using a germanium detector array, Hyperball2. The spacing of the ground-state doublet $(2^{-}_{1},1^{-}_{1})$ was measured to be $161.5\pm0.3\text{(stat)}\pm0.3\text{(syst)}$\,keV from the direct $M1$ transition. Excitation energies of the $1^{-}_{2}$ and $1^{-}_{3}$ states were measured to be $2832\pm3\pm4$\,keV and $6050\pm8\pm7$\,keV, respectively. The obtained level energies provide definitive references for the reaction spectroscopy of $Λ$ hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first precise determination of the level structure of the $^{12}_{\Lambda}$C hypernucleus via $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy, providing definitive reference energies for reaction spectroscopy experiments; however, the results are based on a specific reaction channel ($^{12}$C$(\pi^{+},K^{+}\gamma)$ at 1.05 GeV/$c$) and rely on event-by-event Doppler-shift correction with a systematic uncertainty of 1% on measured $\gamma$-ray energies.

## Method

The experiment used the $(\pi^{+}, K^{+})$ reaction at 1.05 GeV/$c$ on a $^{12}$C target at the KEK-PS K6 beam line, with outgoing kaons detected by the Superconducting Kaon Spectrometer (SKS). Gamma rays from the produced $^{12}_{\Lambda}$C hypernucleus were detected by the Hyperball2 germanium detector array (24 detectors, 30% of $4\pi$ solid angle, 4% photo-peak efficiency at 1 MeV). An event-by-event Doppler-shift correction was applied using reconstructed recoil momenta, reaction vertices, and detector positions to compensate for the hypernucleus recoil velocity ($\beta=0.028$--$0.038$).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state doublet ($2^{-}_{1},1^{-}_{1}$) spacing: $161.5 \pm 0.3\text{(stat)} \pm 0.3\text{(syst)}$ keV from the direct $M1$ transition
- Excitation energy of the $1^{-}_{2}$ state: $2832 \pm 3 \pm 4$ keV
- Excitation energy of the $1^{-}_{3}$ state: $6050 \pm 8 \pm 7$ keV
- Gamma-ray energies from the $1^{-}_{2}$ state: $2671 \pm 3 \pm 3$ keV (lower peak, $56 \pm 10$ counts) and $2838 \pm 4 \pm 3$ keV (upper peak, $24 \pm 7$ counts)
- Missing mass resolution: 6 MeV (FWHM) for the $^{12}_{\Lambda}$C excitation spectrum
- In-beam energy resolution of Hyperball2: 5.4 keV (FWHM) at 1.33 MeV
- In-beam live time of Ge detectors: $(59.3 \pm 0.3)\%$ on average

## Context

This work extends previous reaction spectroscopy measurements of $^{12}_{\Lambda}$C from KEK-PS E369 (Hotchi et al., 2001), FINUDA (Agnello et al., 2005), and JLab Hall A (Iodice et al., 2007) and Hall C (Tang et al., 2014), which showed significant discrepancies in excitation energies despite agreeing on global spectral features. The precise $\gamma$-ray data provide definitive reference energies for calibrating and cross-checking all reaction spectroscopy methods, and enable systematic studies of spin-dependent $\Lambda$N interaction strengths, particularly the $\Lambda$N-$\Sigma$N coupling effect predicted by Millener (2005).
