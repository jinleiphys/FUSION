# Double-folding potentials from chiral effective field theory

> The determination of nucleus-nucleus potentials is important not only to describe the properties of the colliding system, but also to extract nuclear-structure information and for modelling nuclear reactions for astrophysics. We present the first determination of double-folding potentials based on chiral effective field theory at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order. To this end, we construct new soft local chiral effective field theory interactions. We benchmark this approach in the $^{16}$O-$^{16}$O system, and present results for cross sections computed for elastic scattering up to 700 MeV in energy, as well as for the astrophysical $S$-factor of the fusion reaction.

## Key claim

The first determination of double-folding potentials for nucleus-nucleus reactions using chiral effective field theory interactions at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order, benchmarked on the 16O-16O system; the approach works only for sufficiently soft interactions (cutoff R0 greater than or approximately 1.4 fm) because harder interactions produce repulsive double-folding potentials at the Hartree-Fock level used.

## Method

The authors constructed new soft local chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions with coordinate-space cutoffs of 1.4 fm and 1.6 fm, fitting the low-energy constants to np phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis up to 50 MeV laboratory energy at leading order and up to 150 MeV at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order. They then applied the double-folding formalism to compute the real nucleus-nucleus potential for 16O-16O by folding these chiral interactions over two-parameter Fermi densities from Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, using a density-matrix expansion for the exchange term. For elastic scattering, they added an imaginary part proportional to the real double-folding potential with a coefficient NW between 0.6 and 0.8, and for fusion they used the real potential with incoming-wave boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering cross sections computed for laboratory energies from 124 to 704 MeV, compared to data from multiple experiments
- Double-folding potentials constructed with cutoffs R0 = 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6 fm; only R0 greater than or approximately 1.4 fm yields attractive potentials at the Hartree-Fock level
- Imaginary part coefficient NW taken in the range 0.6-0.8 for elastic scattering calculations
- Fusion S-factor computed for center-of-mass energies around 12 MeV, with the exchange potential evaluated at Ecm = 12 MeV
- Decrease of exchange contribution attractive strength with increasing energy, shown for laboratory energies from 124 to 704 MeV
- Deuteron binding energies from the new interactions: 1.886 MeV (LO, R0=1.4 fm), 2.151 MeV (NLO, R0=1.4 fm), 2.193 MeV (N2LO, R0=1.4 fm); experimental value is 2.224 MeV

## Context

This work extends the double-folding approach of Satchler (1979) and the Sao Paulo group (Chamon 2002, Pereira 2009) by replacing phenomenological or G-matrix nucleon-nucleon interactions with chiral effective field theory interactions, providing systematic order-by-order improvement and uncertainty estimates. It builds on local chiral interactions developed by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) and Lynn (2014, 2016, 2017) and on previous work deriving nucleon-nucleus optical potentials from chiral EFT by Holt (2013) and Vorabbi (2015). The results enable future improvements including more realistic densities, beyond-Hartree-Fock many-body contributions, three-nucleon forces, and a microscopic imaginary part, aiming toward first-principles optical potentials with controlled uncertainties for nuclear reactions in astrophysics.
