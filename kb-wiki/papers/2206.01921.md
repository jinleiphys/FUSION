# Open charm and bottom meson-nucleon potentials à la nuclear force

> We discuss the interaction of an open heavy meson ($\bar{D}$ and $\bar{D}^{\ast}$ for charm or $B$ and $B^{\ast}$ for bottom) and a nucleon ($N$) by considering the $π$, $σ$, $ρ$, and $ω$ exchange potentials. We construct a potential model by respecting chiral symmetry for light quarks and spin symmetry for heavy quarks. Model parameters are adjusted by referring the phenomenological nuclear (CD-Bonn) potentials reproducing the low-energy $NN$ scatterings. We show that the resulting interaction may accommodate $\bar{D}N$ and $BN$ bound states with quantum number $I(J^{P})=0(1/2^{-})$, and a $BN$ bound state with $I(J^{P})=1(1/2^{-})$. We find that, in the present potential model, the $σ$ exchange potential plays an important role.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a potential model for the interaction between an open heavy meson (anti-D or B) and a nucleon using pi, sigma, rho, and omega exchange, with parameters adjusted to reproduce low-energy NN scattering from the CD-Bonn potential; the model may accommodate bound states in certain channels, but the existence of these states depends on the specific choice of cutoff parameters and the sigma exchange potential plays a crucial role.

## Method

The authors constructed meson-exchange potentials for the coupled PN-P*N systems (where P is anti-D or B meson and P* is the corresponding vector meson) by respecting heavy-quark spin symmetry for the heavy quarks and chiral symmetry for the light quarks. They included one-pion exchange (OPEP), sigma exchange, and rho and omega exchange potentials, with coupling constants and cutoff parameters adjusted by reference to the CD-Bonn nuclear potential. The sigma exchange potential was introduced with channel-dependent masses and coupling constants for isosinglet and isotriplet channels, and the cutoff parameters were treated as free parameters within a range around previously determined values.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 94 MeV
- Pion coupling constant for heavy mesons: g_pi = 0.59
- Pion-nucleon coupling squared: g_piNN^2/4pi = 13.6
- Universal vector-meson coupling: g_V = 5.8
- Vector meson coupling constants: beta = 0.9, lambda = 0.56 GeV^{-1}
- Rho-nucleon coupling squared: g_rhoNN^2/4pi = 0.84
- Omega-nucleon coupling squared: g_omegaNN^2/4pi = 20.0
- Tensor-to-vector ratio for rho: f_rhoNN/g_rhoNN = 6.1
- Tensor-to-vector ratio for omega: f_omegaNN/g_omegaNN = 0.0
- Cutoff ratio for anti-DN system: kappa_{DN} = 1.35
- Cutoff ratio for BN system: kappa_{BN} = 1.29
- Nucleon cutoff scaling parameters: kappa_0 = 0.804 (I=0), kappa_1 = 0.773 (I=1)
- Pion mass: 138.04 MeV
- Rho meson mass: 769.68 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of PN-P*N interactions that considered only one-pion exchange (Yasui 2009, Yamaguchi 2011) by adding sigma, rho, and omega exchange potentials modeled after the CD-Bonn nuclear potential (Machleidt 2000). The approach enables investigation of possible bound states in the anti-DN and BN systems with quantum numbers I(J^P)=0(1/2^-) and I(J^P)=1(1/2^-), and is relevant for understanding pentaquark formation and heavy-flavored exotic nuclei as studied by Hosaka (2016).
