# Operator Approach to Isospin Violation in Pion Photoproduction

> Unambiguous isospin violation in the strong interaction sector is a key issue in low energy hadronic physics, both experimentally and theoretically. Bernstein has employed the Fermi-Watson theorem to demonstrate that pion photoproduction is a process where isospin violation in the pi N system can be revealed, an approach we review here. Here we propose a general operator approach to the phenomenon in pion photoproduction, thereby providing an analogue for the framework that was proposed for pi N scattering by Kaufmann and Gibbs. The resulting set of amplitudes could form the basis for determining the multipole amplitudes for photoproduction. Thus, the so resulting phase shift determination from photoproduction can then be used via the Fermi-Watson theorem to resolve discrepancies in pi N phase shift analyses. We point out that casting effective Lagrangian results in terms of our framework would be beneficial. The upcoming polarization experiments are an ideal setting to test our approach, and also to constrain better the isotensor currents which strictly are not forbidden.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a general operator framework for isospin violation in pion photoproduction, analogous to the Kaufmann and Gibbs framework for πN scattering, which can be used to determine multipole amplitudes without relying on input from πN phase shift analyses or the Fermi-Watson theorem, unlike the approach of Bernstein.

## Method

The author constructs all possible isospin-violating operators for the γN → πN system by classifying the nine combinations τ_i Φ_j (i,j = 0,1,2) into a scalar O_S, a vector O_V, and a traceless symmetric tensor O_T, where O_S is isospin-conserving and O_V, O_T are isospin-violating. Transition amplitudes for the four physical reactions (γn → π⁰n, γp → π⁰p, γn → π⁻p, γp → π⁺n) are then expressed in terms of amplitudes A_R^(±) and A_R^(0) for each operator type R = S, V, T. The framework is compared with Bernstein's Fermi-Watson approach, which uses πN scattering lengths or phase shifts as input and is valid only below the 2π threshold.

## Key numbers

- At a pion kinetic energy of about 40 MeV, Bernstein's analysis gives ψ ≃ 0.010 ± 0.004 for the mixing parameter between I=1/2 and I=3/2 channels in the πN system.
- The ratio σ_n/σ_p for cross sections on neutron and proton targets at the Δ(1232) resonance from recent SLAC experiments NE11 and E133 is 0.72 ± 0.09, compared to 0.91 ± 0.03 from older data of Köbberling.
- The triangle relation for pion photoproduction in the isospin-conserving limit is: T(γn → π⁻p) + T(γp → π⁺n) = -√2 [T(γn → π⁰n) - T(γp → π⁰p)].

## Context

This work extends the operator approach to isospin violation developed by Kaufmann and Gibbs (1990) for πN scattering to the process of pion photoproduction, which Bernstein (2000-2005) had identified as an ideal probe using the Fermi-Watson theorem. The framework enables multipole analyses independent of πN input, and the author notes that upcoming polarization experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (LOI) could test the approach and constrain isotensor contributions to the electromagnetic current, which were previously investigated by Sanda and Shaw (1971) but yielded null results in later experiments.
