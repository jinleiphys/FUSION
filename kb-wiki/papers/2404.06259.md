# Stress out of charmonia

> We investigate the gravitational form factors of charmonium. Our method is based on a Hamiltonian formalism on the light front known as basis light-front quantization. The charmonium mass spectrum and light-front wave functions were obtained from diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian that incorporates confinement from holographic QCD and one-gluon exchange interaction from light-front QCD. We proposed a quantum many-body approach to construct the hadronic matrix elements of the energy momentum tensor $T^{++}$ and $T^{+-}$, which are used to extract the gravitational form factors $A(Q^2)$ and $D(Q^2)$. The obtained form factors satisfy the known constraints, e.g. von Laue condition. From these quantities, we also extract the energy, pressure and light-front energy distributions of the system. We find that hadrons are multi-layer systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of charmonium using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), finding that hadrons are multi-layer systems with distinct radial distributions for matter density, energy density, invariant mass squared density, and scalar density; the results are obtained within a valence approximation (only the \(c\bar c\) Fock sector) and rely on an effective Hamiltonian with parameters fit to the charmonium mass spectrum below the \(D\overline D\) threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the light-front Schrödinger equation by diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian in a finite basis space (BLFQ) with \(N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 8\) (and up to 16 for uncertainty estimates), using 2D harmonic oscillator functions in the transverse direction and Jacobi polynomials in the longitudinal direction. The effective Hamiltonian combines confinement from holographic light-front QCD with a one-gluon exchange interaction from light-front QCD, and the resulting light-front wave functions (LFWFs) were used to construct hadronic matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor components \(T^{++}\) and \(T^{+-}\) in the Drell-Yan frame (\(q^+ = 0\)) to extract the gravitational form factors \(A(Q^2)\) and \(D(Q^2)\). From these form factors, the authors derived the transverse-plane distributions of energy, pressure, invariant mass squared, and scalar density.

## Key numbers

- The charmonium mass spectrum computed from the effective Hamiltonian deviates from the PDG compilation by within 40 MeV; for the ground state \(\eta_c\), the mass deviation is about \(\sim 15\%\) of the total binding energy.
- The basis truncation parameters are \(N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 8\) for primary results, corresponding to a UV resolution \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} \approx M_{c\bar c}\); uncertainty is estimated from the difference between \(N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 8\) and \(N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 16\) results.
- The \(D\)-term for charmonium is \(D \sim -5\) (conjectured negative for mechanically stable systems).
- The 3D r.m.s. radius of the matter distribution is \(r_A^2 = -6A'(0)\); the 3D r.m.s. radius of the energy distribution is \(r_E^2 = r_A^2 - \frac{3}{2} \lambda_C^2 (1 + D)\), where \(\lambda_C = 1/M\) is the Compton wavelength.
- The von Laue condition \(\lim_{q_\perp \to 0} q_\perp^2 D(q_\perp^2) = 0\) is satisfied, implying mechanical equilibrium.

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ calculations of charmonium observables (mass spectra, radiative transitions, parton distributions, GPDs) by Li et al. (2015, 2017, 2021) to the gravitational form factors, which describe the stress and energy distributions inside the hadron. It complements lattice QCD results for the nucleon and pion EMT by Hackett et al. (2023) and phenomenological extractions from experimental data by Kumano et al. (2017) and Burkert et al. (2018, 2021). The multi-layer picture of hadrons (valence quarks at the core, wee partons and glueballs at larger radii) provides a framework for interpreting future measurements at electron-ion colliders.
