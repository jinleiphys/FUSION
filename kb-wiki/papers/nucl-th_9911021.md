# Double electron capture in 156Dy, 162Er and 168Yb

> The double electron capture half-lives of 156Dy, 162Er and 168Yb are evaluated using the pseudo SU(3) model, which describes ground and excited bands as well as their B(E2) and B(M1) transition strengths in remarkable agreement with experiment. The best candidate for experimental detection is the decay 156Dy -> 156Gd, with tau{1/2} (0+{gs} --> 0+{gs}) = 2.74 E22 yrs and tau{1/2} (0+{gs} --> 0+{1}) = 8.31 E24 yrs.

## Key claim
The pseudo SU(3) model predicts that the double electron capture decay of 156Dy to the first excited 0+ state in 156Gd has a half-life of 8.31 × 10^24 years, making it the best candidate for experimental detection among the three nuclei studied, but only if experiments can reach sensitivities around 10^24 years.

## Method
The authors used the pseudo SU(3) shell model to calculate nuclear matrix elements for two-neutrino double electron capture (ECEC2ν) decays in 156Dy, 162Er, and 168Yb. The model describes ground and excited bands using symmetry-truncated shell model wavefunctions with Hamiltonians including single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole, and pairing interactions, while restricting normal parity configurations to pseudo spin zero and abnormal parity configurations to seniority zero. Half-lives were computed from the product of kinematical factors G2ν(σ) and squared nuclear matrix elements |M2ν(σ)|² for transitions to ground and excited 0+ states.

## Key numbers
- 156Dy → 156Gd: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+gs) = 2.74 × 10^22 yr, with G2ν = 9.79 × 10^-21 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.061
- 156Dy → 156Gd: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+1) = 8.31 × 10^24 yr, with G2ν = 1.65 × 10^-22 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.027
- 156Dy → 156Gd: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+2) = 1.08 × 10^25 yr, with G2ν = 7.58 × 10^-23 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.035
- 162Er → 162Dy: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+gs) = 2.85 × 10^22 yr, with G2ν = 8.06 × 10^-21 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.066
- 162Er → 162Dy: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+1) = 3.70 × 10^27 yr, with G2ν = 1.60 × 10^-24 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.013
- 168Yb → 168Er: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+gs) = 2.00 × 10^23 yr, with G2ν = 2.47 × 10^-21 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.045
- 168Yb → 168Er: τ1/2(0+gs → 0+1) = 5.36 × 10^33 yr, with G2ν = 5.18 × 10^-28 yr^-1 and |M2ν| = 0.0006
- Nuclear matrix elements to ground states are 0.05-0.06, a factor of 5 smaller than the assumption of Barabash (1994)

## Context
This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model, previously applied to β⁻β⁻ decay by Castanos et al. (1994) and to even-even and odd-even nuclei by Beuschel et al. (1998) and Vargas et al. (1999), to double electron capture in heavy deformed nuclei. The calculated nuclear matrix elements are significantly smaller than the rough estimates used by Barabash (1994), and the results confirm that deformed nuclei have smaller matrix elements than spherical ones as noted by Castanos et al. (1994). The paper identifies 156Dy as the only viable candidate among these three nuclei for experimental detection, with the decay to the first excited 0+ state providing a detectable gamma-ray signature through two coincident gamma rays (960.55 keV or 1079.24 keV followed by 88.97 keV).
