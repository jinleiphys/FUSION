# Quantum effects in the quadrupole rotor picture of ultra-relativistic ion-ion collisions

> The azimuthal hadronic flow observed in ultra-relativistic ion-ion collisions provides a sensitive probe of many-body ground-state correlations in the colliding nuclei. In particular, collective correlations associated with nuclear "intrinsic deformation" are expected to leave pronounced fingerprints on specific final-state observables. However, such effects are commonly interpreted within a classical rigid-rotor picture, despite the intrinsically quantum nature of nuclei. In this Letter, the validity of this interpretation is assessed systematically across the nuclear chart by comparing the quantum quadrupole rotor with its classical rigid-rotor limit. Quantum contributions associated with the fermionic nature of the nucleons are shown to be largely independent of shell effects, and hence of the intrinsic deformation. While they account for nearly all of the quantum rotor effective quadrupole deformation in light and/or spherical nuclei, they drop below 10% in intrinsically well deformed heavy nuclei. The present letter demonstrates that a quantitative interpretation of nuclear-structure effects in final-state observables requires going beyond the classical rigid-rotor paradigm. Beyond the quantum contributions quantified presently, correlations associated with collective vibrations and with the non-collective nucleonic motion must be further included and characterized.

# Quantum effects in the quadrupole rotor picture of ultra-relativistic ion-ion collisions

## Key claim

The classical rigid-rotor (CRR) picture commonly used to interpret nuclear deformation effects in ultra-relativistic ion-ion collisions is validated within a 10% error on the extracted effective deformation for most nuclei, but the justification does not rely on the (incorrect) time-scale argument previously put forward. Quantum contributions from the fermionic nature of nucleons are largely independent of shell effects and intrinsic deformation, accounting for nearly all of the quantum rotor effective quadrupole deformation in light and/or spherical nuclei while dropping below 10% in intrinsically well-deformed heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) calculations for five representative even-even nuclei (20Ne, 28Si, 40Ca, 76Ge, 136Xe) using the EM1.8/2.0 chiral effective field theory Hamiltonian, constraining the intrinsic quadrupole deformation over a large range. They also performed systematic PHFB calculations within the multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) framework based on the PC-F1 covariant EDF parameterization for isotonic chains with Z in [4,100]. The quantum rotor (QR) results were compared to the quantum rigid rotor (QRR) obtained via the needle approximation to angular-momentum projection, and to the classical rigid rotor (CRR) obtained by further removing exchange and pairing contributions.

## Key numbers

- Quantum contributions to the QRR effective quadrupole deformation drop below 10% of the classical contribution in relatively heavy (A >= 100) well-deformed nuclei with beta^2_20[dHFB] >= 0.15 (beta_20[dHFB] >= 0.4)
- The equality B^2_2(HE)_CRR = beta^2_20[dHFB] holds within 5% as soon as beta^2_20[dHFB] >= 0.03 (beta_20[dHFB] >= 0.17)
- The exchange contribution to the effective deformation has a maximum absolute value around 0.06 in light nuclei and decreases monotonically with A
- The exchange contribution drops below 10% when B^2_2(HE)_QR becomes larger than about 0.14
- The impact of the needle approximation on the effective quadrupole deformation remains smaller than 10% for the large majority of nuclei, except for a few light systems where it reaches 30%
- A previous study found a 6%-8% reduction in the second-order fireball eccentricity for central Ne-Ne and Ne-Pb collisions due to angular-momentum restoration effects (Ke, 2025)

## Context

This work extends the quantum mechanical formulation of the link between nuclear structure and final-state observables in ultra-relativistic collisions developed by Duguet (2025) and Blaizot (2025). It systematically assesses the validity of the classical rigid-rotor approximation that has been traditionally used in analyses following Giacalone (2018, 2021) and the STAR collaboration (2024). The results contradict the improper time-scale argument previously used to justify the classical approximation, as shown by Dobaczewski (2025). The next step will be to include dynamical correlations beyond the quantum rotor description, such as collective vibrations and non-collective nucleonic motion, to enable fully quantitative extraction of intrinsic nuclear deformation from ultra-central symmetric collisions.
