# Branch-cut in the shear-stress response function of massless $λφ^4$ with Boltzmann statistics

> Using an analytical result for the eigensystem of the linearized collision term for a classical system of massless scalar particles with quartic self-interactions, we show that the shear-stress linear response function possesses a branch-cut singularity that covers the whole positive imaginary semi-axis. This is demonstrated in two ways: (1) by truncating the exact, infinite linear system of linear equations for the rank-two tensor modes, which reveals the cut touching the origin; and (2) by employing the Trotterization techniques to invert the linear response problem. The former shows that the first pole tends towards the origin and the average separation between consecutive poles tends towards zero as power laws in the dimension of the basis. The latter allows one to obtain the response function in closed form in terms of Tricomi hypergeometrical functions, which possess a branch-cut on the above-mentioned semi-axis. This suggests that the presence of a cut along the imaginary frequency axis of the shear stress correlator, inferred from previous numerical analyses of weakly coupled scalar $λφ^4$ theories, does not arise due to quantum statistics but instead emerges from the fundamental properties of this system's interactions.

## Key claim

The shear-stress linear response function for a classical (Boltzmann) system of massless scalar particles with quartic self-interactions possesses a branch-cut singularity covering the whole positive imaginary frequency semi-axis, demonstrating that this feature previously inferred from numerical analyses of quantum scalar theories does not arise from Bose statistics but instead emerges from the fundamental properties of the system's interactions.

## Method

The authors used the exact eigensystem of the linearized collision term for massless lambda-phi-4 theory with Boltzmann statistics, recently derived by Denicol (2022), to construct the infinite tridiagonal matrix system for the rank-two tensor eigenmoments. They analyzed the shear-stress response function in two complementary ways: (1) by truncating the infinite linear system at increasing dimension N and studying the pole structure of the resulting finite-dimensional inverse matrix, and (2) by employing Trotterization techniques to invert the linear response problem analytically, obtaining the response function in closed form in terms of Tricomi hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers

* The smallest eigenvalue of the linearized collision operator for rank-two modes is chi_02 = -g*M/6, while the asymptotic value for large n is -g*M/2, a factor of 3 different.
* For a truncation of the basis at N=1000, the pole closest to the origin lies at approximately Omega_hat approximately 10^-4.
* The distance of the nearest pole to the origin scales as min Omega_hat approximately 1/N^0.996 as the truncation order N increases up to N=750.
* The total cross section for the interaction is sigma_T(s) = lambda^2/(32*pi*s) = g/s, where g = lambda^2/(32*pi).

## Context

This work extends the numerical analyses of Moore (2018) and Ochsenfeld (2023), who studied the shear-stress two-point function in bosonic scalar lambda-phi-4 theory and found evidence for a branch-cut singularity. The authors show analytically that this branch cut persists even when Boltzmann statistics replace Bose statistics, contradicting the interpretation that the cut arises from quantum statistical effects. The result enables a deeper understanding of the non-hydrodynamic modes in this system: modes with large energies relative to temperature relax slowly because the cross-section vanishes at high energies, and these long-lived modes remain free-streaming while decoupling from the part of the system that hydrodynamizes. The hydrodynamic transport coefficients derived by Rocha (2023) are recovered in the asymptotic low-frequency regime.
