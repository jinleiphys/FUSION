# Invariant amplitudes for coherent electromagnetic pseudoscalar production from a spin-one target (II): crossing, multipoles and observables

> The formal properties of the recently derived set of linearly independent invariant amplitudes for the electromagnetic production of a pseudoscalar particle from a spin-one particle have been further exploited. The crossing properties are discussed in detail. Since not all of the amplitudes have simple crossing behaviour, we introduce an alternative set of basic amplitudes which are either symmetric or antisymmetric under crossing. The multipole decomposition is given, and the representation of the multipoles as integrals over the invariant functions weighted with Legendre polynomials is derived. Furthermore, differential cross section and polarization observables are expressed in terms of the corresponding invariant functions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the formal framework for coherent pseudoscalar photoproduction and electroproduction from a spin-one target by deriving crossing properties, multipole decompositions, and observable expressions for the 13 linearly independent invariant amplitudes previously introduced, noting that the alternative set of amplitudes that separate transverse and longitudinal contributions has complicated crossing behavior, while a newly introduced set of amplitudes with simple crossing symmetry does not separate into transverse and longitudinal components.

## Method

The author first reviews the 13 gauge-invariant invariant amplitudes for the reaction γ(*) + D → D + Π, then derives their crossing properties under interchange of initial and final spin-one particle (s ↔ u) using charge conjugation invariance. An alternative set of 13 amplitudes is introduced that are either symmetric or antisymmetric under crossing, and their explicit forms are given. The multipole decomposition is performed by expanding the helicity t-matrix into charge, electric, and magnetic multipoles, and the multipoles are expressed as integrals over the invariant functions weighted with Legendre polynomials. Finally, differential cross section and polarization observables (beam, target, and recoil asymmetries) are expressed in terms of structure functions built from bilinear combinations of the invariant functions.

## Key numbers

- 13 linearly independent invariant amplitudes are used to represent the T-matrix for the reaction
- 9 of the 13 amplitudes are purely transverse (describing photoproduction); 4 additional amplitudes appear in electroproduction describing charge and longitudinal current contributions
- The multipole expansion uses Clebsch-Gordan coefficients with angular momentum quantum numbers j, l, and L (electromagnetic multipolarity)
- The kinematic factor for virtual photons is c = α/(6π²) · k₂^(lab)/(k₁^(lab) q_ν⁴)
- For real photons, the kinematic factor is c = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Arenhoevel (1998) which first derived the 13 invariant amplitudes for coherent pseudoscalar production from a spin-one target. It parallels the treatment of electromagnetic pion production on the nucleon by Donnachie (1972) and follows the framework used for deuteron photo- and electrodisintegration in Arenhoevel (1988), Arenhoevel and Sanzone (1990), and Arenhoevel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak (1993). The results enable calculation of differential cross sections and all polarization observables for reactions such as coherent π⁰ photoproduction or electroproduction on deuterium, providing a complete formal basis for experimental analysis.
