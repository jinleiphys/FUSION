# Monte Carlo Models: Quo Vadimus?

> Coherence, multiple scattering and the interplay between soft and hard processes are discussed. These physics phenomena are essential for understanding the nuclear dependences of rapidity density and $p_T$ spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The RHIC data have shown the onset of hard processes and indications of high $p_T$ spectra suppression due to parton energy loss. Within the pQCD parton model, the combination of azimuthal anisotropy ($v_2$) and hadron spectra suppression at large $p_T$ can help one to determine the initial gluon density in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The interplay between coherent soft processes and incoherent hard processes in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be understood through a two-component model, and the combination of azimuthal anisotropy (v2) and hadron spectra suppression at large pT can help determine the initial gluon density at RHIC, but this determination depends on the specific modeling of parton energy loss and its energy dependence.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares Monte Carlo models for heavy-ion collisions, focusing on the two-component model implemented in HIJING that separates soft processes (modeled by Lund strings proportional to the number of participant nucleons) from hard processes (incoherent parton scatterings proportional to the number of binary collisions, with a pT cut-off p0 around 2 GeV/c). The author also presents a pQCD parton model calculation of high pT hadron spectra that incorporates parton energy loss via modified fragmentation functions, and compares these calculations to RHIC data on charged multiplicity, nuclear modification factor R_AB(pT), and azimuthal anisotropy v2(pT).

## Key numbers

- The transition scale between soft and hard processes is around p0 = 1.5-2 GeV/c, determined from hadron spectra in pp and pbar-p collisions
- In the two-component model fit to pp(pbar) data, the soft component contributes <n>_soft ≈ 1.6 and the hard component contributes <n>_hard ≈ 2.2 to the charged multiplicity per unit rapidity
- The pQCD parton model calculation for central Au+Au at RHIC gives an average parton energy loss dE/dx ≈ 0.25 GeV/fm, consistent with preliminary PHENIX pi0 spectra suppression
- Without parton energy loss, nuclear modification of parton distributions and pT broadening give about 10-30% increase in pT spectra in central Au+Au relative to pp

## Context

This paper extends the two-component model approach of HIJING (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and compares it with the EKRT saturation model (Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, Tuominen, 2000) for describing charged multiplicity in heavy-ion collisions. The RHIC data favor the HIJING two-component model over the EKRT model for centrality dependence. The work enables future determination of initial gluon density through combined measurements of high pT hadron suppression and azimuthal anisotropy v2, and calls for further study of the energy and centrality dependence of parton energy loss to understand the transition from SPS to RHIC energies.
