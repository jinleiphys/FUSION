# Strong decays of the newly observed narrow $Ω_b$ structures

> Motivated by the newly observed narrow structures $Ω_b(6316)^-$, $Ω_b(6330)^-$, $Ω_b(6340)^-$, and $Ω_b(6350)^-$ in the $Ξ_b^0 K^-$ mass spectrum, we investigate the strong decays of the low-lying $Ω_b$ states within the $^3P_0$ model systematically. According to their masses and decay widths, the observed $Ω_b(6316)^-$, $Ω_b(6330)^-$, $Ω_b(6340)^-$, and $Ω_b(6350)^-$ resonances can be reasonably assigned as the $λ-$mode $Ω_b(1P)$ states with $J^P=1/2^-, 3/2^-, 3/2^-$, and $5/2^-$. Meanwhile, the remaining $P-$wave state with $J^P=1/2^-$ should have a rather broad width, which can hardly be observed by experiments. For the $Ω_b(2S)$ and $Ω_b(1D)$ states, our predictions show that these states have relatively narrow total widths and mainly decay into the $Ξ_b \bar K$, $Ξ_b^\prime \bar K$ and $Ξ_b^{\prime*} \bar K$ final states. These abundant theoretical predictions may be valuable for searching more excited $Ω_b$ states in future experiments.

## Key claim

The four narrow Omega_b resonances observed by LHCb can be assigned as the lambda-mode Omega_b(1P) states with J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-, 3/2^-, and 5/2^-, but only if a small mixing angle (between -3.3 and 3.3 degrees for the two J^P = 1/2^- states) is introduced; without mixing, the pure j-j coupling scheme cannot simultaneously explain all four narrow states.

## Method

The authors used the nonrelativistic 3P0 model (quark-pair creation model) to calculate OZI-allowed two-body strong decay widths of low-lying Omega_b baryons. They employed simple harmonic oscillator wave functions, a constant pair-production strength gamma = 9.83 calibrated to the Sigma_c(2520)++ decay, and considered mixing between quark-model states with identical J^P via a mixing angle theta. For the Omega_b(1P) states, experimental masses from LHCb were used; for the Omega_b(2S) and Omega_b(1D) states, masses from the relativistic quark model of Ebert (2011) were adopted.

## Key numbers

- Omega_b(6316) mass: 6315.64 +/- 0.31 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.50 MeV; width < 2.8 MeV
- Omega_b(6330) mass: 6330.30 +/- 0.28 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.50 MeV; width < 3.1 MeV
- Omega_b(6340) mass: 6339.71 +/- 0.26 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.50 MeV; width < 1.5 MeV
- Omega_b(6350) mass: 6349.88 +/- 0.35 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.50 MeV; width = 1.4 +1.0 -0.8 +/- 0.1 MeV
- For the pure j=0 Omega_b0(1/2^-,1P) assignment, predicted widths range from 870.52 MeV (at Omega_b(6316) mass) to 1224.29 MeV (at Omega_b(6350) mass)
- For the pure j=2 Omega_b2(3/2^-,1P) or Omega_b2(5/2^-,1P) assignments, predicted widths range from 0.35 MeV (at Omega_b(6316) mass) to 2.98 MeV (at Omega_b(6350) mass)
- Mixing angle for the two J^P = 1/2^- states constrained to -3.3 degrees to 3.3 degrees (excluding zero) by the Omega_b(6316) width
- Predicted total widths: Omega_b(2S) J^P=1/2^+ = 49.67 MeV; Omega_b*(2S) J^P=3/2^+ = 53.32 MeV
- Predicted total widths for Omega_b(1D) states: 105.99 MeV (J^P=1/2^+), 108.30 MeV (J^P=3/2^+), 26.80 MeV (J^P=3/2^+), 20.58 MeV (J^P=5/2^+), 2.98 MeV (J^P=5/2^+), 2.80 MeV (J^P=7/2^+)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the LHCb discovery of four narrow Omega_b resonances (Aaij 2020) by providing a systematic strong-decay calculation within the 3P0 model, using parameters previously successful for Lambda_c(b) and Sigma_c(b) states (Lu 2018, Liang 2019, Lu 2019). The results favor the j-j coupling scheme over the L-S coupling scheme, contradicting predictions of five narrow Omega_b(1P) states from Santopinto (2018) and three narrow states from Wang (2017) under L-S coupling. The predictions for Omega_b(2S) and Omega_b(1D) states provide guidance for future experimental searches in the Xi_b K-bar, Xi_b' K-bar, and Xi_b'* K-bar final states.
