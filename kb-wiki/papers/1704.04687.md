# Bulk viscous effects on flow and dilepton radiation in a hybrid approach

> Starting from IP-Glasma initial conditions, we investigate the effects of bulk pressure on low mass dilepton production at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. Though thermal dilepton $v_2$ is affected by the presence of both bulk and shear viscosity, whether or not these effects can be measured depends on the dilepton "cocktail" contribution to the the low mass dilepton $v_2$. Combining the thermal and "cocktail" dileptons, the effects of bulk viscosity on total dilepton $v_2$ is investigated.

## Key claim
Bulk viscosity increases the elliptic flow ($v_2$) of thermal dileptons while suppressing the $v_2$ of cocktail dileptons, but whether these effects are observable in the total dilepton $v_2$ depends on the relative contribution of the dilepton cocktail, which is more significant at RHIC than at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid approach combining IP-Glasma initial conditions with viscous hydrodynamics (including both shear and bulk viscosity) and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD). Thermal dilepton rates were computed from the hydrodynamical evolution using hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom with viscous corrections, while cocktail dileptons were obtained by decaying the final hadron spectra from either Cooper-Frye freeze-out or UrQMD evolution. The specific shear viscosity $\eta/s$ was taken as nearly temperature-independent, while the specific bulk viscosity $\zeta/s$ was temperature-dependent, with switching temperatures of 165 MeV at RHIC and 145 MeV at LHC.

## Key numbers
- Switching temperature at top RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV): $T^{RHIC}_{sw}=165$ MeV
- Switching temperature at LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV): $T^{LHC}_{sw}=145$ MeV
- Cocktail dileptons dominate the total dilepton $v_2$ only for invariant masses $M<0.65$ GeV at LHC
- Bulk viscosity effects on total $v_2$ show ordering changes in invariant mass windows $0.85<M<0.95$ GeV and $M>1.1$ GeV at RHIC

## Context
This work extends prior studies of dissipative hydrodynamics and electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions, particularly building on the Bayesian analyses of transport coefficients by Pratt (2015) and Bernhard (2016, 2017). It complements earlier findings by Vujanovic (2016) that hadrons are less sensitive to shear relaxation time than to shear viscosity, while electromagnetic probes show increased sensitivity. The results enable future studies that will include dynamical generation of cocktail dileptons during hadronic transport evolution, and further investigation of the mechanisms by which bulk viscosity increases thermal dilepton flow anisotropy.
