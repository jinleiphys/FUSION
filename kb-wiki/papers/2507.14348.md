# Goldstone bosons across thermal phase transitions

> Temperature has a significant effect on the properties of quantum field theories (QFTs) with a spontaneously broken symmetry, in particular on the massless Goldstone bosons that exist in the vacuum state. It has recently been shown using lattice calculations for a $\mathrm{U}(1)$ complex scalar field theory that the Goldstone mode persists even when the symmetry is restored above the critical temperature $T_{c}$, and has the properties of a screened excitation, a so-called thermoparticle. In this work, we continue the investigation of this theory by determining explicitly how the Goldstone mode evolves as the temperature is increased both below and above $T_{c}$. We find that the two phases of the theory are entirely characterised by the thermal dissipative effects experienced by the Goldstone mode, with the broken and symmetry-restored phases associated with weak and strong damping, respectively. These findings are consistent with the non-perturbative constraints imposed by spontaneous symmetry breaking, and provide a new way in which to characterise thermal phase transitions in QFTs.

## Key claim

The two phases of the U(1) complex scalar field theory are entirely characterised by the thermal dissipative effects experienced by the Goldstone mode, with the broken phase associated with weak damping and the symmetry-restored phase associated with strong damping; this characterisation holds even though the Goldstone mode persists above the critical temperature as a screened excitation (thermoparticle).

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of a U(1) complex scalar field theory on a spatially symmetric volume L^3 x L_tau, using a fixed lattice spacing a and bare parameters (am_0, g_0) = (0.297i, 0.85) that yield a symmetry-broken vacuum. They computed the Euclidean two-point function C(tau, x) = <phi(tau, x) phi^dagger(0)>_beta for multiple temperatures corresponding to N_tau = 2, 4, 6, 8, 16, 32, and extracted the infinite-volume vacuum expectation value |v|^2 by extrapolating C_L(0, z = L/2) as a function of L. The spatial correlator data were fitted to functional forms derived from the thermal Goldstone mode contribution, including a massless thermoparticle ansatz with a damping factor D_beta^G(x).

## Key numbers

* The UV cutoff satisfies Lambda/|v| = pi/(a|v|) approximately 36, where |v| is extrapolated from the coldest lattice with N_tau = 32.
* For N_tau = 32 (coldest broken phase), the infinite-volume extrapolation gives a^2|v|^2 = 0.00782(4).
* For N_tau = 16 (broken phase), the infinite-volume extrapolation gives a^2|v|^2 = 0.00683(8).
* For N_tau = 8 (broken phase), the infinite-volume extrapolation gives a^2|v|^2 = 0.0029(1).
* The system is in the symmetry-broken phase for N_tau = 8, 16, 32 and in the symmetry-restored phase for N_tau = 2, 4, 6, as determined by the infinite-volume limit of C_L(0, z = L/2).

## Context

This work extends the lattice investigation of Lowdon (2025) on the U(1) complex scalar theory, which previously demonstrated the existence of a thermal Goldstone mode above T_c at a single temperature, by now tracking the mode across multiple temperatures spanning the phase transition. The analysis is guided by the non-perturbative spectral representation framework of Bros (1996), which established that spontaneous symmetry breaking at finite temperature is controlled by the asymptotic damping of the Goldstone mode. These findings enable a new characterisation of thermal phase transitions in quantum field theories based on dissipative effects rather than correlation length behaviour, and suggest that symmetry restoration can occur without requiring exponential decay of correlation functions.
