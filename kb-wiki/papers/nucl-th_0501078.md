# Influence of the Dirac sea on proton electromagnetic knockout

> We use the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) to study the effects of negative-energy components of Dirac wave functions on the left-right asymmetry for (e,e'p) reactions on 16-O with 0.2 < Q^2 < 0.8 and 12-C with 0.6 < Q^2 < 1.8 (GeV/c)^2. Spinor distortion is more important for the bound state than for the ejectile and the net effect decreases with Q^2. Spinor distortion breaks Godon equivalence and the data favor the CC2 operator with intermediate coupling to the sea. The left-right asymmetry for Q^2 < 1.2 (GeV/c)^2 is described well by RDWIA calcuations, but at Q^2 = 1.8 (GeV/c)^2 the observed variation with missing momentum is flatter than predicted.

## Key claim

The left-right asymmetry in (e,e'p) reactions is sensitive to negative-energy (Dirac sea) components of the bound-state wave function, with the effect decreasing as Q^2 increases; the data favor the CC2 current operator with intermediate coupling to the sea, but at Q^2 = 1.8 (GeV/c)^2 the observed variation with missing momentum is flatter than RDWIA predictions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for 16-O(e,e'p) at Q^2 values between 0.2 and 0.8 (GeV/c)^2 and for 12-C(e,e'p) at Q^2 values between 0.6 and 1.8 (GeV/c)^2, using Dirac-Hartree wave functions from Horowitz and Serot (1986) and NLSH wave functions from Sharma et al. (1993). They compared full RDWIA calculations with approximations that project onto positive-energy states (removing Dirac sea contributions) and with the noSV approximation, and tested three Gordon-equivalent current operators (CC1, CC2, CC3) to evaluate sensitivity to spinor distortion.

## Key numbers

- Q^2 range studied: 0.2 to 0.8 (GeV/c)^2 for 16-O; 0.6 to 1.8 (GeV/c)^2 for 12-C
- For 16-O at Q^2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)^2, the left-right asymmetry A_LT for the 1p_{1/2} state shows sensitivity to Dirac sea contributions, with data supporting the full RDWIA over positive-energy projection
- For 12-C at Q^2 = 1.8 (GeV/c)^2, the observed a_LT variation with missing momentum is flatter than RDWIA predictions
- The sensitivity to Gordon-equivalent operators decreases as Q^2 increases and has nearly disappeared by Q^2 = 1.8 (GeV/c)^2
- For 12-C, the left-right asymmetry for p_m less than about 250 MeV/c is rather insensitive to the choice of optical potential

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinor distortion in (e,e'p) reactions by Caballero (1998), Kelly (1999), Udias (1999, 2001), Vignote (2004), and Martinez (2004), and uses data from Gao (2000) and Fissum (2004) for 16-O and from Dutta (2003) for 12-C. The results enable improved understanding of relativistic effects in nucleon knockout reactions and provide constraints on the choice of current operator and the treatment of negative-energy states in nuclear structure calculations, though the flattening at high Q^2 suggests either that spin-orbit potentials from Dirac phenomenology may be too strong above 600 MeV or that two-body currents become important.
