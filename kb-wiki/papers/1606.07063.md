# Jet substructure using semi-inclusive jet functions within SCET

> We propose a new method to evaluate jet substructure observables in inclusive jet measurements, based upon semi-inclusive jet functions in the framework of Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET). As a first example, we consider the jet fragmentation function, where a hadron $h$ is identified inside a fully reconstructed jet. We introduce a new semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function ${\mathcal G}^h_i(z= ω_J/ω,z_h=ω_h/ω_J,ω_J, R,μ)$, which depends on the jet radius $R$ and the large light-cone momenta of the parton `$i$' initiating the jet ($ω$), the jet ($ω_J$), and the hadron $h$ ($ω_h$). The jet fragmentation function can then be expressed as a semi-inclusive observable, in the spirit of actual experimental measurements, rather than as an exclusive one. We demonstrate the consistency of the effective field theory treatment and standard perturbative QCD calculations of this observable at next-to-leading order (NLO). The renormalization group (RG) equation for the semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function ${\mathcal G}_i^h(z,z_h, ω_J, R,μ)$ are also derived and shown to follow exactly the usual timelike DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions. The newly obtained RG equations can be used to perform the resummation of single logarithms of the jet radius parameter $R$ up to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL$_R$) accuracy. In combination with the fixed NLO calculation, we obtain NLO+NLL$_R$ results for the hadron distribution inside the jet. We present numerical results for $pp\to(\mathrm{jet}\,h)X$ in the new framework, and find excellent agreement with existing LHC experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for evaluating jet substructure observables in inclusive jet measurements using semi-inclusive jet functions within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), where the jet fragmentation function is expressed as a semi-inclusive observable rather than an exclusive one, with the key qualifier that the renormalization group equations for the new semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function follow standard timelike DGLAP evolution (resumming single logarithms of R) rather than the multiplicative RG equations with double logarithms found in the exclusive approach.

## Method

The authors introduce a semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function G_i^h(z, z_h, omega_J, R, mu) that depends on the jet radius R and the large light-cone momenta of the initiating parton, the jet, and the identified hadron. They compute this function at next-to-leading order (NLO) for both cone and anti-k_T jet algorithms using pure dimensional regularization, derive the renormalization group equations which follow timelike DGLAP evolution, and solve these equations in Mellin moment space to resum single logarithms of the jet radius parameter R up to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL_R) accuracy, combining this with the fixed NLO calculation to obtain NLO+NLL_R results.

## Key numbers

- The semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function at NLO contains single logarithms L = ln(mu^2 / (omega_J^2 tan^2(R/2))) and single 1/epsilon poles, in contrast to the exclusive case which has double logarithms and 1/epsilon^2 poles
- The renormalization group equation for the semi-inclusive fragmenting jet function has anomalous dimension gamma_{ij}^G(z, mu) = (alpha_s(mu)/pi) P_{ji}(z), matching the usual timelike DGLAP evolution
- For the anti-k_T algorithm, the jet algorithm-dependent function is I_{ij}^{anti-k_T}(z_h) = 2 P_{ji}(z_h) ln(z_h); for the cone algorithm, I_{ij}^{cone}(z_h) = 2 P_{ji}(z_h) ln(z_h/(1-z_h)) theta(1/2 - z_h)
- The matching coefficients J_{ij}(z, z_h, omega_J, mu) are given explicitly at O(alpha_s) for all parton channels (qq, gg, qg, gq) with the logarithm L = ln(mu^2 / (omega_J^2 tan^2(R/2)))

## Context

This work extends the semi-inclusive jet function formalism introduced by Kang, Ringer, and Vitev (2016) for inclusive jet production to jet substructure observables, specifically the jet fragmentation function. It contrasts with the exclusive SCET treatment of the jet fragmentation function by Jain, Procura, and Waalewijn (2011) and Chien, Hornig, and Lee (2015), which required an upper energy cut Lambda and produced double logarithmic dependence on R; the new semi-inclusive approach eliminates power corrections of order O(Lambda/Q) and enables resummation of single logarithms of R. The results are consistent with fixed NLO calculations by Arleo (2013) and Kaufmann, Mukherjee, and Vogelsang (2015, 2016), and the numerical NLO+NLL_R predictions show excellent agreement with existing LHC experimental data, enabling improved theoretical descriptions of jet substructure measurements at small jet radii relevant for heavy-ion collisions.
