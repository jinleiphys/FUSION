# Impact of dark matter distribution on neutron star properties

> We investigate the structural and observable impacts of dark matter (DM) on neutron stars using a combined equation of state that integrates the relativistic mean field (RMF) model for baryonic matter with a variable density profile for DM, incorporating DM-baryon interactions mediated by the Higgs field. Employing three RMF parameter sets (NL3, BigApple, and IOPB-I) for baryonic matter, we analyze mass-radius relations, maximum mass, and tidal deformability, focusing on DM density scaling ($α$) and steepness ($β$) parameters. Our findings reveal that increased DM concentration significantly enhances NS compactness, shifting mass-radius profiles and reducing tidal deformability. The DM influence strongly depends on the steepness of the DM density profile, where high $β$ values lead to strongly confined DM within the NS core, resulting in more compact and less deformable configurations. Observational constraints from PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817 impose consistent structural limits on DM fractions across different equations of state models, narrowing the allowable parameter space for DM and linking specific combinations of $αM_χ$ ($M_χ$ being the mass of dark matter particle) and $β$ values to viable NS structures. This study highlights the interplay among DM concentration, nuclear stiffness, and observational data in shaping NS structure, offering insights into future constraints on DM in high-density astrophysical environments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the impact of dark matter on neutron star structure is governed primarily by the product of the DM density scaling factor and DM particle mass (αMχ), rather than by either parameter individually, and that this effect depends strongly on the steepness parameter β of the DM density profile.

## Method

The authors construct a single-fluid equation of state combining the relativistic mean field (RMF) model for baryonic matter (using three parameter sets: NL3, BigApple, and IOPB-I) with a variable DM density profile that depends on baryon density via two free parameters: a scaling factor α and a steepness parameter β. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations, maximum masses, and tidal deformabilities for DM-admixed neutron stars, then compare these results against observational constraints from PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817.

## Key numbers

- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star with the IOPB-I parameter set and β=2, the DM number density peaks at approximately 0.33% of the baryonic number density without DM, while the central baryon number density increases by up to 20% for α=10⁻³ relative to the no-DM case.
- The core-crust transition densities used for DM confinement are 4.512×10⁻² fm⁻³ (NL3), 6.113×10⁻² fm⁻³ (BigApple), and 3.646×10⁻² fm⁻³ (IOPB-I).
- The DM particle mass Mχ is fixed at 100 GeV for most calculations, with α values ranging from 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻² for β=1 and from 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻³ for β=2 and 4.
- The product αMχ is varied from 10⁻⁴ to 10⁰ GeV across the models.
- The observed mass of PSR J0740+6620 is M=2.08±0.07 M⊙, with radius constraints of 12.39⁺¹·³⁰₋₀·₉₈ km (Riley 2021) and 13.7⁺²·⁶₋₁·₅ km (Miller 2021).

## Context

This work extends the DM-admixed neutron star model introduced by Panotopoulos and Lopes (2017) by replacing the previously assumed constant DM density with a variable density profile that depends on baryon density, addressing a physical implausibility noted in prior studies. The results enable future constraints on DM properties from gravitational wave observations (such as GW170817) and NICER pulsar measurements, and suggest that theoretical models should prioritize the combined parameter αMχ rather than treating α and Mχ independently.
