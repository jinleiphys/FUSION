# Signature of intermittency in hybrid UrQMD-hydro data at 10 AGeV Au+Au collisions

> An attempt has been made, in the light of scaled factorial moment (SFM) analysis, to investigate hybrid UrQMD hydro generated events of Au+Au collisions at 10 AGev to realize the role of hydrodynamic evolution on observed intermittency, if any. ln <F_q> values for q = 2 - 6 are found to increase with increasing values of ln M^2 indicating unambiguously the presence of intermittency in our data sample generated with both chiral and hadronic equation of states (EOS). Although various late processes like meson-meson (MM) and meson-baryon (MB) hadronic rescattering and/or resonance decays are to influence the intermittency index significantly, these processes could not be held responsible for the observed intermittency in hybrid UrQMD hydro data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intermittency, observed via scaled factorial moment analysis in hybrid UrQMD-hydro generated Au+Au collisions at 10 AGeV, is not caused by late-stage hadronic rescattering or resonance decays, but rather arises from the hydrodynamic evolution of the medium itself, regardless of whether a chiral or hadronic equation of state is used.

## Method

The authors generated 3.02 x 10^4 central (0-5%) Au+Au collision events at 10 AGeV using the hybrid UrQMD-hydro model, which combines microscopic transport with intermediate hydrodynamic calculations. They performed two-dimensional scaled factorial moment (SFM) analysis in pseudorapidity-azimuthal space, converting to cumulative variables chi(eta) and chi(phi) to remove kinematic shape dependence, and computed ln<F_q> versus ln M^2 for moment orders q=2-6. To isolate the role of late-stage processes, they generated additional event sets with meson-meson (MM) and meson-baryon (MB) scattering turned off, resonance decays turned off, and both turned off, comparing results between chiral and hadronic equations of state.

## Key numbers

* Intermittency index alpha_2 for UrQMD-hydro with chiral EoS: (1.20 +/- 0.70) x 10^-3; with hadronic EoS: (0.20 +/- 0.10) x 10^-3
* Intermittency index alpha_6 for UrQMD-hydro with chiral EoS: (20.80 +/- 1.10) x 10^-3; with hadronic EoS: (11.38 +/- 0.99) x 10^-3
* Intermittency index alpha_6 for UrQMD-hydro with both MM, MB scattering and resonance decays off: (31.50 +/- 2.54) x 10^-3
* Intermittency index alpha_6 for UrQMD-hydro with resonance decays off: (24.56 +/- 1.93) x 10^-3
* Intermittency index alpha_6 for UrQMD-hydro with MM and MB scattering off: (27.9 +/- 2.46) x 10^-3
* Number of events generated: 3.02 x 10^4 for each data set

## Context

This work extends prior SFM analyses of UrQMD transport model data, which found no intermittency signature (as reported by other workers), by incorporating hydrodynamic evolution via the hybrid UrQMD-hydro model. The study is motivated by the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, where 10 AGeV is the highest achievable energy for A-A collisions at SIS100 and where deconfinement is first reached around this energy according to hydrodynamic calculations. The results enable future experimental searches for intermittency as a signature of hydrodynamic evolution in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at FAIR energies, and suggest that final-state hadronic interactions and resonance decays only weaken, rather than cause, the observed intermittent behavior.
