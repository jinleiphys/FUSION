# Ground State Masses and Biding Energies of the Nucleon, Hyperon and Heavy Baryons in a Light-Front Model

> The ground state masses and binding energies of the nucleon, $Λ^0$, $Λ^+_c$, $Λ^0_b$ are studied within a constituent quark QCD-inspired light-front model. The light-front Faddeev equations for the $Qqq$ composite spin 1/2 baryons, are derived and solved numerically. The experimental data for the masses are qualitatively described by a flavor independent effective interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a light-front model with a flavor-independent contact interaction between constituent quarks can qualitatively describe the ground state masses of the nucleon, Lambda0, Lambda_c+, and Lambda_b0, but only after the physical scale of the nucleon mass is used as input and the spin degrees of freedom are averaged out.

## Method

The authors derived and solved numerically the light-front Faddeev equations for a Qqq (heavy-light-light) three-quark system with spin 1/2, using a contact interaction between constituent quarks in the ladder approximation. The two-quark scattering amplitudes were obtained from a geometrical series of bubble diagrams, with the light-quark pair bound state pole used to define the interaction strength. The constituent quark masses were extracted from experimental vector meson masses (rho, K*, D*, B*), and the diquark mass in the light sector was fixed using the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark masses derived from vector meson masses: m_u = 0.384 GeV, m_s = 0.508 GeV, m_c = 1.623 GeV, m_b = 4.941 GeV
* Experimental binding energy of the proton: B_p^exp = 0.214 GeV (defined as 3/2 M_rho minus M_p)
* Experimental binding energy of Lambda0: B_Lambda0^exp = 0.161 GeV
* Experimental binding energy of Lambda_c+: B_Lambdac+^exp = 0.106 GeV
* Experimental binding energy of Lambda_b0: B_Lambdab0^exp = 0.085 GeV
* The model uses m_u = 0.386 GeV and nucleon mass 0.938 GeV, giving diquark mass M_d = 0.695 GeV
* For baryon mass above 2.3 GeV, the bound Qqq system saturates at a binding energy of 0.077 GeV

## Context

This work extends a QCD-inspired light-front model previously applied to mesons by Pauli and Frederico (2001) and to the proton by Araujo, de Melo, and Frederico (1995). The model had successfully described the pion and rho meson masses and the proton mass, charge radius, and electric form factor. The present study generalizes the approach to heavy-light baryons (Lambda0, Lambda_c+, Lambda_b0) and demonstrates that a flavor-independent contact interaction can reproduce the trend of baryon binding energies as a function of the heavy quark mass. This enables future extension of the model to more realistic baryon descriptions and supports the application of the QCD-inspired meson model to baryonic systems.
