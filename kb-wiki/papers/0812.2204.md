# A Poincaré invariant treatment of the three-nucleon problem

## Key claim
Poincaré-invariant three-nucleon models can provide a realistic description of observables at intermediate energies (up to 2 GeV) while satisfying exact Poincaré symmetry, cluster properties, and a bounded energy spectrum, but the practical implementation requires careful treatment of scattering equivalences to restore cluster properties when computing electroweak observables or extending to four-body systems.

## Method
The authors construct dynamical representations of the Poincaré group on three-nucleon Hilbert spaces by adding interactions to the non-interacting mass operator, using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn, Argonne V18) as input via a relativistic embedding where the two-body mass operator is defined as M = M₀(k² + 2μV). The Faddeev equations are solved in a partial-wave expansion up to 250 MeV and by direct integration without partial waves up to 2 GeV, with relativistic effects isolated by comparing against non-relativistic calculations using identical two-body input.

## Key numbers
- Faddeev equations solved for energies up to 250 MeV using partial-wave expansion with realistic interactions
- Converged results obtained by direct integration without partial waves for energies up to 2 GeV
- Triton binding energy corrections from relativistic effects are typically less than 0.1 MeV, depending on the two-body interaction
- Relativistic spin rotations contribute nontrivially to the observable A_y at energies of 5-13 MeV
- Unphysical dependence of the charge form factor on deuteron momentum in the non-clustered representation can be as large as 6%
- For soft interactions (N3LO, JISP16), exchange current contributions become significant in observable A for momentum transfers between 0.5 and 1 GeV², in B above 0.5 GeV², and in tensor polarization above 0.15 GeV²
- Elastic electron-deuteron scattering observables A, B, and T₂₀ calculated up to Q² = 2.5 (GeV)²

## Context
This work extends the successful low-energy few-nucleon program (Witała, 2005; Witała, 2006) to intermediate energies by incorporating exact Poincaré invariance, building on prior formulations by Coester (1982) and Polyzou (1985). The relativistic spin-rotation effects on A_y confirm the scale of an effect observed by Miller and Schwenk (2007) in a simpler model, though with opposite sign, indicating these effects must be accounted for in resolving the A_y puzzle. The calculations enable future treatment of three-nucleon problems at energy scales up to a few GeV while respecting all fundamental principles, and demonstrate that soft interactions designed for low-energy many-body calculations require significant exchange current contributions when used in relativistic intermediate-energy models.
