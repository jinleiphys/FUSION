# DRAGON: Monte Carlo generator of particle production from a fragmented fireball in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> A Monte Carlo generator of the final state of hadrons emitted from an ultrarelativistic nuclear collision is introduced. An important feature of the generator is a possible fragmentation of the fireball and emission of the hadrons from fragments. Phase space distribution of the fragments is based on the blast wave model extended to azimuthally non-symmetric fireballs. Parameters of the model can be tuned and this allows to generate final states from various kinds of fireballs. A facultative output in the OSCAR1999A format allows for a comprehensive analysis of phase-space distributions and/or use as an input for an afterburner.

## Key claim

DRAGON is a Monte Carlo event generator that simulates hadron production from a fragmented fireball in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, where the fireball can break into droplets either via spinodal decomposition during a first-order phase transition or due to a sudden rise of bulk viscosity at the crossover transition. The generator unifies the blast-wave model for the bulk fireball with fragment emission, but the abstract does not emphasize that the model assumes hadrons are emitted from fragments without any further scattering after hadronization.

## Method

The generator places spherical fragments within an ellipsoidal fireball according to the blast-wave model with zero temperature, assigning each fragment the local fluid velocity at its position. Hadrons are then generated from the bulk fireball using the blast-wave emission function and from fragments using thermal Boltzmann distributions in the fragment rest frames, with fragment volumes either fixed or drawn from a gamma-distribution with k=2. All directly produced hadrons and resonance decay products are tracked, with resonances decayed according to their branching ratios and exponential lifetime distributions, and the final state is output in OSCAR1999A format for further analysis or afterburner input.

## Key numbers

- Fragment volumes follow a gamma-distribution with parameter k=2, where the scale parameter b (in fm³) controls the characteristic fragment size
- The program requires approximately 100 Mbytes of RAM to execute with typical data
- Generation of 10⁴ events can take between 2 hours and several days, depending on fragment size and density
- The code includes all baryons with masses up to 2.0 GeV/c² and mesons up to 1.5 GeV/c²
- The initial phase calculating thermal weights can take up to 1 hour when many species are included
- The distributed package contains 6368 lines of code and 153,939 bytes total

## Context

This work extends the THERMINATOR generator (Kisiel et al., 2006) by adding the crucial feature of fragment emission, and builds on the blast-wave parametrizations developed by Retiere and Lisa (2004) and Csanad et al. (2003). The model is motivated by theoretical arguments for fireball fragmentation at hadronization from Mishustin (1999) and Torrieri et al. (2007), and enables systematic studies of event-by-event fluctuations and angular correlations that could reveal cluster formation in momentum distributions, as observed experimentally by Adcox et al. (2002) and Adams et al. (2003).
