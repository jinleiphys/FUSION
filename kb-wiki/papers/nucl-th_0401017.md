# Analysis of Hadronic Properties at SPS energies from a Statistical Model

> Using a statistical model of multiparticle production by Chou, Yang and Yen, which looks similar to the thermal model and is known to account for the single particle distributions in e$^+$ e$^-$ collisions, we fit the rapidity and transverse mass spectra of pions and kaons measured in Pb+Pb collisions by NA49 collaboration. This model nicely fits both the rapidity and the transverse mass spectra of each particle species with a few parameters. However, fitting all the particles with a single value of $T_p$ is not possible. This analysis shows that the success of the thermal models does not necessarily mean the thermalization of the system in collision.

# Analysis of Hadronic Properties at SPS energies from a Statistical Model

## Key claim

The Chou-Yang-Yen statistical model, which does not require thermalization or a freeze-out concept, can fit both the rapidity and transverse mass spectra of pions and kaons in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but fitting all particle species with a single value of the partition temperature T_p is not possible, contrary to the case in e+e- collisions. The success of thermal models for hadron multiplicities and transverse momentum spectra does not necessarily imply thermalization of the system.

## Method

The authors applied the statistical model of multiparticle production developed by Chou, Yang and Yen (1985), which uses a canonical ensemble single-particle distribution proportional to g(p_perp) exp(-E/T_p) where T_p is the partition temperature and g(p_perp) = exp(-alpha p_perp) is a transverse momentum cutoff factor. They performed separate least-square fits of the rapidity distributions dN/dy and transverse mass spectra dN/(m_T dm_T) for pi+, pi-, K+ and K- measured by the NA49 collaboration in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, treating K, T_p, and alpha as free parameters for each species. They also attempted simultaneous fits using charge and strange chemical potentials to relate the normalization constants for opposite-charge pairs.

## Key numbers

* For pi+: K = 599.9, T_p = 1.27 GeV, alpha = 4.23 c/GeV, <p_perp> = 0.47 GeV/c, chi^2/n = 5.4
* For pi-: K = 649.8, T_p = 1.19 GeV, alpha = 4.15 c/GeV, <p_perp> = 0.48 GeV/c, chi^2/n = 15.0
* For K+: K = 66.86, T_p = 1.57 GeV, alpha = 2.69 c/GeV, <p_perp> = 0.74 GeV/c, chi^2/n = 2.5
* For K-: K = 37.82, T_p = 1.35 GeV, alpha = 2.79 c/GeV, <p_perp> = 0.72 GeV/c, chi^2/n = 2.7
* Simultaneous fit of pi+ and pi- with a common T_p and alpha plus charge chemical potential gave K = 625.0, T_p = 1.21 GeV, alpha = 4.17 c/GeV, mu_c = 0.043 GeV
* Simultaneous fit of K+ and K- with a common T_p plus strange chemical potential gave K = 50.7, T_p = 1.43 GeV, alpha_K+ = 2.6 c/GeV, alpha_K- = 2.9 c/GeV, mu_s = -0.415 GeV
* Fitting only rapidity distributions of all species with a single T_p = 0.98 GeV gave alpha values (pi+: 5.98 c/GeV, pi-: 5.67 c/GeV, K+: 11.4 c/GeV, K-: 17.1 c/GeV) that are inconsistent with the experimental average transverse momenta

## Context

This work extends the statistical model of Chou, Yang and Yen (1985), previously applied to e+e- collisions by the same authors and by Hoang (1991), to relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies. It contrasts with thermal models that assume chemical equilibrium (Braun-Munzinger, 1999; Becattini, 1996; Rafelski, 2002) and fireball models with radial flow (Lee, 1990; Heinz, 1993; Broniowski, 2002), which require thermalization and freeze-out concepts. The finding that different particle species require different T_p values challenges the interpretation of thermal model success as evidence for thermalization, and suggests that further studies with Lorentz-boosted exponentials are needed to check whether a single T_p can describe all species in heavy-ion collisions.
