# The partition function of an interacting many body system: beyond the perturbed static path approximation

> Based on the path integral representation of the partition function of a many body system with separable two body interaction we propose a systematic extension of the perturbed static path approximation (PSPA) to lower temperatures. Thereby, special attention must be paid to instabilities of the classical mean field solution in functional space that cause divergencies within the conventional PSPA. As a result we develop an approximation applicable from high to very low temperatures. These findings are tested against exact results for the archetypical cases of a particle moving in a one dimensional double well and the exactly solvable Lipkin model. In particular, we obtain a very good approximation to the level density of the Lipkin model even at low thermal excitations. Our results may have potential applications in low temperature nuclear physics and mesoscopic systems, e.g. for gap fluctuations in nanoscale superconducting devices previously studied within a PSPA type of approximation. PACS: 5.30.-d, 24.60.-k, 21.10.Ma, 74.25.Bt

## Key claim

The paper proposes a systematic extension of the perturbed static path approximation (PSPA) that remains valid across the crossover temperature where the conventional PSPA breaks down due to instabilities of the classical mean field solution, provided that the quartic coefficient B(β,q₀) and λ₂(β,q₀) remain positive and sufficiently large for all q₀.

## Method

The authors start from the path integral representation of the partition function for a many-body system with separable two-body interaction, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary field. They expand the logarithm of the thermal expectation value of the evolution operator to fourth order in the Fourier modes qᵣ of the auxiliary field, keeping only the q₁ and q₋₁ modes beyond Gaussian order while treating higher modes (|r|>1) within the Gaussian approximation. The resulting integrals over q₂ and q₋₂ are performed analytically, leaving a one-dimensional integral over the q₁ amplitude that is evaluated either numerically or by stationary phase approximation for temperatures below T₀.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature T₀ where the conventional PSPA breaks down is given by T₀ = max_{q₀} [ħ |ω_ν^{inst}(q₀)| / (2π)], where ω_ν^{inst}(q₀) are imaginary local RPA frequencies.
- The ePSPA definitely fails at T = T₀/2, where λ₂ vanishes, corresponding to instability in the q₂ direction in functional space.
- For the one-dimensional double well potential V(q) = -Λq² + (1-Λ)q⁴ + Λ²/[4(1-Λ)] with Λ = 0.9, the inverse crossover temperature is β₀ = π/(ħ√Λ).
- The Gaussian width of the q₁ mode is Δᵣ = √[|k|T/(2λᵣ(β,q₀))], which grows as λ₁ → 0 when temperature approaches T₀ from above.

## Context

This work extends the perturbed static path approximation (PSPA) developed by Attias and Alhassid (1997) and the SPA+RPA approach by Puddu, Bortignon, and Broglia (1991), which break down at a crossover temperature due to instabilities of the classical mean field solution. The method is analogous to techniques used in dissipative tunneling (Grabert) to regularize the decay rate at T₀, and is tested against exact results for a one-dimensional double well and the exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick, 1965). The results enable improved calculations of thermodynamic properties and level densities in finite systems at low temperatures, with potential applications to gap fluctuations in nanoscale superconducting devices previously studied within PSPA-type approximations.
