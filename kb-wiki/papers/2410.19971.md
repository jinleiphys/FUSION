# Resonant shattering flares as asteroseismic tests of chiral effective field theory

> Chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) has proved to be a powerful microscopic framework for predicting the properties of neutron-rich nuclear matter with quantified theoretical uncertainties up to about twice the nuclear saturation density. Tests of $χ$EFT predictions are typically performed at low densities using nuclear experiments, with neutron star (NS) constraints only being considered at high densities. In this work, we discuss how asteroseismic quasi-normal modes within NSs could be used to constrain specific matter properties at particular densities, not just the integrated quantities to which bulk NS observables are sensitive. We focus on the crust-core interface mode, showing that measuring this mode's frequency would provide a meaningful test of $χ$EFT at densities around half the saturation density. Conversely, we use nuclear matter properties predicted by $χ$EFT to estimate that this mode's frequency is around 185 $\pm$ 50 Hz. Asteroseismic observables such as resonant phase shifts in gravitational-wave signals and multimessenger resonant shattering flare timings, therefore, have the potential to provide useful tests of $χ$EFT.

# Resonant shattering flares as asteroseismic tests of chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

Measuring the crust-core interface mode frequency in neutron stars would provide a meaningful test of chiral effective field theory predictions at densities around half the nuclear saturation density, but the reliability of this test depends on improvements to neutron star crust modeling, particularly the inclusion of nuclear pasta phases and their shear moduli.

## Method

The authors constructed a neutron star meta-model using an extended Skyrme interaction with variable symmetry energy parameters (J, L, K_sym) and a two-piece polytropic core, then calculated the crust-core interface mode frequency by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and relativistic pulsation equations under the Cowling approximation. They injected simulated i-mode frequency measurements with 15 Hz uncertainty and compared the resulting posterior constraints on the symmetry energy to conservative and optimistic chiral effective field theory predictions from Lim and Holt (2018) and Drischler et al. (2020).

## Key numbers

- The predicted crust-core interface mode frequency from chiral effective field theory is 185 ± 50 Hz, where the uncertainty comes primarily from the standard deviation of the i-mode frequency in symmetry energy bins rather than from the chiral EFT uncertainty.
- The i-mode frequency measurement uncertainty of 15 Hz is reasonable for frequencies around 200 Hz based on the rate at which gravitational wave frequency changes during resonance.
- The symmetry energy is most constrained by the i-mode frequency at a density of approximately 0.068 fm^-3, while nuclear mass measurements primarily constrain the symmetry energy around 0.105 fm^-3.
- Injected i-mode frequency measurements at 140 Hz and 410 Hz produce posterior symmetry energy constraints that bracket the chiral EFT predictions below half saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of resonant shattering flares by Neill et al. (2021, 2023) and the connection between the i-mode frequency and nuclear matter properties, while directly comparing to chiral effective field theory predictions from Lim and Holt (2018) and Drischler et al. (2020). The results show that i-mode frequency measurements are complementary to terrestrial nuclear experimental constraints from binding energies and charge radii of doubly magic nuclei, and that combining both types of data could provide more stringent tests of chiral EFT than either alone. Future work requires improved modeling of the neutron star crust, particularly nuclear pasta phases and their shear moduli as discussed by Pethick et al. (1998) and Caplan et al. (2018), before this asteroseismic test can be considered reliable.
