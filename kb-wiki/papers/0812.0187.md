# Nucleon and gamma N -> Delta lattice form factors in a constituent quark model

> A covariant quark model, based both on the spectator formalism and on vector meson dominance, and previously calibrated by the physical data, is here extended to the unphysical region of the lattice data by means of one single extra adjustable parameter - the constituent quark mass in the chiral limit. We calculated the Nucleon (N) and the gamma N -> Delta form factors in the universe of values for that parameter described by quenched lattice QCD. A qualitative description of the Nucleon and gamma N -> Delta form factors lattice data is achieved for light pions.

# Nucleon and gamma N -> Delta lattice form factors in a constituent quark model

## Key claim
A covariant spectator quark model with vector meson dominance, previously calibrated to physical data, can be extended to describe quenched lattice QCD data for nucleon and gamma N -> Delta form factors using a single extra parameter (the constituent quark mass in the chiral limit, M_chi), achieving a qualitative description for light pions but with the caveat that the optimal value of M_chi differs between the nucleon and the gamma N -> Delta datasets.

## Method
The authors start from a covariant spectator quark model for the nucleon and Delta as quark-diquark systems, with the electromagnetic current described by vector meson dominance (VMD) and wavefunctions parametrized by monopole factors of Hulthen form. They extend the model to the lattice region by introducing a dependence of the constituent quark anomalous magnetic moment on the quark mass, which scales as 1/M_q, and parametrizing the quark mass as M_q = M_chi + c m_q, where M_chi is the only free parameter. The nucleon, Delta, and rho meson masses are taken from lattice simulations, and the model is compared to quenched lattice QCD data for the nucleon isovector form factors (from Gockeler et al., 2005) and the gamma N -> Delta magnetic form factor (from Alexandrou et al., 2008) for three values of M_chi: 0.42 GeV, 0.80 GeV, and infinity.

## Key numbers
- The constituent quark mass in the chiral limit M_chi is varied among three values: 0.42 GeV, 0.80 GeV, and +infinity (the limit of no pion mass dependence).
- For the nucleon isovector form factors, lattice data are considered at pion masses m_pi = 504 MeV, 615 MeV, and 649 MeV, with lattice spacings a = 0.059 fm and 0.078 fm.
- For the gamma N -> Delta magnetic form factor, lattice data are considered at pion masses m_pi = 0.411 GeV, 0.490 GeV, and 0.563 GeV, with lattice spacing a = 0.092 fm.
- With M_chi = 0.42 GeV, the model underestimates the gamma N -> Delta magnetic form factor data by less than 9% at low Q^2 for m_pi < 500 MeV.
- The rho meson mass is parametrized as m_rho = 0.776 GeV + 0.427 GeV^{-1} * m_pi^2.
- The heavy vector meson mass in the VMD current is set to M_h = 2 M_N, where M_N is the nucleon mass from lattice calculations.
- For the quadrupole form factors G_E* and G_C*, D-state admixtures of approximately 0.9% in the Delta wavefunction provide an excellent description of lattice data, with valence quark contributions estimated as only about 20% of the total quadrupole at the physical point.

## Context
This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously developed by the same authors (Ramalho and Pena, 2008; references NDelta and Nucleon) from the physical region to the unphysical region of quenched lattice QCD data. It follows the approach of Cloet et al. (2002) for incorporating chiral symmetry into quark models via an analytic continuation of the chiral expansion. The model provides an alternative to chiral perturbation theory methods (Pascalutsa, 2006) for extrapolating lattice QCD results, particularly at higher Q^2 where chiral methods are not adequate. The results enable future refinements by introducing pion mass dependence in additional parameters (such as the long-range wavefunction parameter alpha_1 or the heavy meson mass M_h), and by extending the comparison to unquenched lattice data and higher pion masses.
