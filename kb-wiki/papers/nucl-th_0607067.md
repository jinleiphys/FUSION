# On the scattering length of the K^- d system

> Multiple-scattering approximations to Faddeev calculations of the K^- d scattering length are reviewed and compared with published Kbar-N-N <--> pi-Y-N fully reactive Faddeev calculations. A new multiple-scattering approximation which goes beyond the `fixed-center' assumption for the nucleons is proposed, aiming at accuracies of 5-10%. A precise value of the K^- d scattering length from the measurement of the K^- d 1s atomic level shift and width, planned by the DEAR/SIDDHARTA collaboration, plus a precise value for the K^- p scattering length by improving the K^- p atom measurements, are essential for extracting the K^- n scattering length, for resolving persistent puzzles in low-energy Kbar-N phenomenology and for extrapolating into Kbar-nuclear systems.

# On the scattering length of the K⁻ d system

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new multiple-scattering (MS) approximation for the K⁻ d scattering length that goes beyond the "fixed-center" assumption for the nucleons, aiming at accuracies of 5-10% for approximating fully-reactive Faddeev calculations. The existing "fixed-center" MS approximations provide accuracy only in the range of 10-25%.

## Method
The author reviews multiple-scattering approximations to Faddeev calculations of the K⁻ d scattering length and compares them with published Kbar-N-N to pi-Y-N fully reactive Faddeev calculations. A new MS approximation is derived by renormalizing the input Kbar-N scattering amplitudes and replacing the free Green's function G₀ with G₃ (which accounts for the NN interaction) in the coupled Faddeev equations, while keeping the formal structure of the "fixed-center" MS formulae. Charge-exchange degrees of freedom are included using the formalism of Ref. KOR01.

## Key numbers
- The DEAR measurement gives a K⁻ p scattering length a_{K⁻p} = (-0.45 ± 0.090) + i(0.27 ± 0.12) fm
- The relevant expansion parameter for the MS series is <1/r>_d ≈ 0.5 fm⁻¹ for the deuteron wavefunction
- The "fixed-center" MS approximation reproduces the real part of a_{K⁻d} to better than about 15% and the imaginary part to within 5-10% when compared to Faddeev calculations
- For the TGE Faddeev calculation (isospin basis): Faddeev a_{K⁻d} = (-1.67 + i 1.00) fm versus MS a_{K⁻d} = (-1.46 + i 1.09) fm
- For the TDD Faddeev calculation (isospin basis): Faddeev a_{K⁻d} = (-1.34 + i 1.04) fm versus MS a_{K⁻d} = (-1.42 + i 1.09) fm
- For the BFMS Faddeev calculation (particle basis): Faddeev a_{K⁻d} = (-1.80 + i 1.55) fm versus MS a_{K⁻d} = (-1.73 + i 1.06) fm

## Context
This work extends the multiple-scattering approach to K⁻ d scattering originally developed by Brueckner (1953) for pion-deuteron systems and later generalized by Deloff (2001) for π⁻ d scattering. It addresses persistent puzzles in low-energy Kbar-N phenomenology, particularly the inconsistency between the DEAR measurement of a_{K⁻p} (Beer et al., 2005) and comprehensive fits to low-energy K⁻ p scattering data discussed by Borasoy et al. (2005) and Baca et al. (2006). The proposed approximation aims to enable extraction of the K⁻ n scattering length from joint measurements of K⁻ p and K⁻ d atomic 1s level shifts and widths planned by the DEAR/SIDDHARTA collaboration, without requiring new time-consuming Faddeev calculations.
