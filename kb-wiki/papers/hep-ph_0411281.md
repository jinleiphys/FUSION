# Chiral and U(1)_A restorations high in the hadron spectrum, semiclassical approximation and large N_c

> In quantum systems with large $n$ (radial quantum number) or large angular momentum the semiclassical (WKB) approximation is valid. A physical content of the semiclassical approximation is that the quantum fluctuations effects are suppressed and vanish asymptotically. The chiral as well as $U(1)_A$ breakings in QCD result from quantum fluctuations. Hence these breakings must be suppressed high in the spectrum and the spectrum of high-lying hadrons must exhibit $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ symmetry of the classical QCD Lagrangian. This argument can be made stronger for mesons in the large $N_c$ limit. In this limit all mesons are stable against strong decays and the spectrum is infinite. Hence, one can excite mesons of arbitrary large size with arbitrary large action, in which case the semiclassical limit is manifest. Actually we do not need the exact $N_c=\infty$ limit. For any large action there always exist such $N_c$ that the isolated mesons with such an action do exist and can be described semiclassically. From the empirical fact that we observe multiplets of chiral and $U(1)_A$ groups high in the hadron spectrum it follows that $N_c=3$ is large enough for this purpose.

## Key claim

Chiral and U(1)_A symmetries of the classical QCD Lagrangian should be approximately restored in highly excited hadrons and manifest asymptotically high, because in such hadrons the semiclassical (WKB) approximation becomes valid and quantum fluctuation effects are suppressed. The argument is strengthened in the large N_c limit where mesons are stable and the spectrum is infinite, but the empirical observation of chiral multiplets high in the hadron spectrum with N_c=3 suggests that three colors are already large enough for this semiclassical regime to be realized.

## Method

The paper uses a semiclassical (WKB) argument based on the path integral formulation: when the action S of a hadron is much larger than hbar, the functional integral is dominated by the classical path and quantum fluctuations are suppressed to order (hbar/S)^1 and higher. The author connects this to the operator product expansion (OPE) of two-point correlators at large space-like momenta Q^2, where condensate contributions are suppressed by inverse powers of Q^2, and uses dispersion relations to argue that the large-s spectral density becomes chirally symmetric. The large N_c limit of QCD is invoked to demonstrate that isolated mesons with arbitrarily large action can exist, making the semiclassical description manifest.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological observation of effective chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, first reported by Glozman (2001) for baryons and by Glozman (2002, 2003) for mesons, by providing a theoretical justification based on semiclassical dynamics. The argument contrasts with the standard OPE-based reasoning of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), which shows asymptotic symmetry restoration in spectral functions but cannot guarantee it for isolated bound states. The paper enables a connection to the Nambu string picture (Nambu, 1974) and suggests that highly excited hadrons can be described as strings with quarks of definite chirality at the ends, explaining both Regge trajectories and the absence of spin-orbit force in the u,d sector.
