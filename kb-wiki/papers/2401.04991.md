# Light Hypernuclei Production in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV within Thermodynamic Approach

> Simulations of the $Λ$-hyperon and light-hypernuclei production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV were performed within updated Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) final State interactions (THESEUS). The light (hyper)nuclei are treated thermodynamically, i.e. they are considered on the equal basis with hadrons. The only additional parameter is related to the late freeze-out that imitates the afterburner stage for the light (hyper)nuclei because the UrQMD is not able to dynamically treat them. The calculation of hypernuclei production is completely similar to that of light nuclei. The hypernuclei results are compared with recent STAR data. It is found that the calculated midrapidity $_Λ^3$H/$Λ$ ratio falls within the error bars of the experimental point. It is remarkable that large difference between the $t/p$ and $_Λ^3$H/$Λ$ ratios is reproduced without any additional parameters. Rapidity distributions of $_Λ^3$H/$Λ$ and $_Λ^4$He/$Λ$ ratios are predicted. Midrapidity mean transverse momenta of protons, $Λ$s and light (hyper)nuclei in central collisions well agree with the data. The calculated directed flow also reasonably well reproduces of the data.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic treatment of light hypernuclei within the updated THESEUS generator reproduces the measured midrapidity $_\Lambda^3$H/$\Lambda$ ratio within experimental error bars and captures the large difference between $t/p$ and $_\Lambda^3$H/$\Lambda$ ratios without any additional parameters, though the approach relies on a late freeze-out parameter to imitate the afterburner stage that UrQMD cannot handle dynamically.

## Method

The authors extended the Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD final State interactions (THESEUS) to include light hypernuclei ($_\Lambda^3$H and $_\Lambda^4$He) on equal thermodynamic footing with hadrons, using the same freeze-out procedure previously applied to light nuclei. The baryon chemical potentials were recalculated to conserve local baryon number in a system that includes both hadrons and light (hyper)nuclei species, and the hypernuclei were sampled from the freeze-out hypersurface with a late freeze-out energy density of 0.2 GeV/fm$^3$ for $_\Lambda^3$H and either 0.2 or 0.4 GeV/fm$^3$ for $_\Lambda^4$He, using three different equations of state (hadronic, first-order phase transition, and crossover).

## Key numbers

- Midrapidity $_\Lambda^3$H/$\Lambda$ ratio falls within the error bars of the experimental point from STAR at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV
- Late freeze-out energy density $\varepsilon_{\rm frz}=$ 0.2 GeV/fm$^3$ was used for deuterons, tritons, $^3$He, and $_\Lambda^3$H
- Standard 3FD freeze-out energy density $\varepsilon_{\rm frz}=$ 0.4 GeV/fm$^3$ was used for $^4$He and $_\Lambda^4$He
- Binding energy of $_\Lambda^4$He is $B_\Lambda\simeq$ 2.4 MeV, similar to $^3$He with $B_N=$ 2.6 MeV
- Central collisions simulated at impact parameter $b=$ 3 fm; semicentral at $b=$ 6 fm

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic treatment of light nuclei production from Kozhevnikova et al. (2021, 2023, 2024) to hypernuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 GeV, comparing with recent STAR data from Ji (2023) and STAR (2022, 2023). The approach contrasts with coalescence models used by Steinheimer et al. (2012), Reichert et al. (2023), and the Parton-Hadron Quantum Molecular Dynamics approach of Aichelin et al. (2020) and Glassel et al. (2022), as well as the hybrid dynamical-statistical approach of Buyukcizmeci et al. (2023). The results enable predictions of rapidity distributions for $_\Lambda^3$H/$\Lambda$ and $_\Lambda^4$He/$\Lambda$ ratios and demonstrate that a thermodynamic treatment can describe hypernuclei bulk properties without the need for coalescence parameters.
