# Elliptic flow from partially thermalized heavy-ion collisions

> We study to what extent the measured elliptic flow at RHIC constrains viscous deviations from ideal hydrodynamics. We solve a toy model where only transverse momenta are thermalized while the system undergoes longitudinal free-streaming. We show that RHIC data exclude such a model and thus require fast 3-dimensional thermalization.

## Key claim
The paper shows that even if only transverse momenta are thermalized while longitudinal motion remains free-streaming (a "transversally thermalized" toy model), the resulting elliptic flow is only about half the hydrodynamic limit, so the measured RHIC elliptic flow data still require fast three-dimensional thermalization within about 1 fm/c.

## Method
The authors constructed an analytic toy model (TTHM) with boost-invariant initial conditions where the phase-space distribution function enforces complete local thermalization of transverse momenta but collisionless free-streaming in the longitudinal direction, yielding zero longitudinal pressure. They derived the resulting energy-momentum tensor and macroscopic equations of motion, then solved them numerically for transverse expansion using standard hydrodynamic codes, retuning initial conditions to match measured central collision spectra before comparing elliptic flow predictions to RHIC data.

## Key numbers
- The TTHM model required starting transverse expansion about 10 times later than ideal hydrodynamics (HDM), with a 15-20 times lower initial energy density, to reproduce the same final spectra and radial flow as HDM tuned to RHIC data.
- The TTHM model produced only about half as much elliptic flow as HDM, both for the pT-integrated v2 and for the pT-slope of differential v2.
- The thermalization time used in the successful HDM description was tau_th = 0.6 fm/c, yielding an initial energy density at least an order of magnitude above the critical hadronization energy density of epsilon ≈ 1 GeV/fm^3.
- For massless bosons in TTHM, v2(pT -> 0) approaches a non-zero value, reflecting the singular nature of the Bose distribution at pT = 0.

## Context
This work extends the argument by Ollitrault (1992) that elliptic flow is sensitive to the equation of state, testing whether a stiffer EOS from partial thermalization could trade off against the need for early full thermalization. The paper contradicts the possibility that longitudinal thermalization could be delayed while still reproducing RHIC v2 data, and it enables the conclusion that the thermalized state formed at RHIC must be a quark-gluon plasma. The authors suggest using the non-zero v2 at pT=0 as a test for thermalization of small-pT gluons in parton cascades and of direct photons in experiment.
