# Stoner ferromagnetism of a strongly interacting Fermi gas in the quasirepulsive regime

> Recent advances in rapidly quenched ultracold atomic Fermi gases near a Feshbach resonance have brought about a number of interesting problems, in the context of observing the long-sought Stoner ferromagnetic phase transition. The possibility of experimentally obtaining a "quasirepulsive" regime in the upper branch of the energy spectrum due to the rapid quench is currently being debated, and the Stoner transition has mainly been investigated theoretically by using perturbation theory or at high polarization, due to the limited theoretical approaches in the strongly repulsive regime. In this work, we present a nonperturbative theoretical approach to the quasirepulsive upper branch of a Fermi gas near a broad Feshbach resonance, and we determine the finite-temperature phase diagram for the Stoner instability. Our results agree well with the known quantum Monte-Carlo simulations at zero temperature, and we recover the known virial expansion prediction at high temperature for arbitrary interaction strengths. At resonance, we find that the Stoner transition temperature becomes of the order of the Fermi temperature, around which the molecule formation rate becomes vanishingly small. This suggests a feasible way to observe Stoner ferromagnetism in the nondegenerate temperature regime.

## Key claim

The authors present a nonperturbative theoretical framework for the quasirepulsive upper branch of a Fermi gas near a broad Feshbach resonance, finding that the Stoner ferromagnetic transition occurs at finite temperatures of order the Fermi temperature, where molecule formation rates become vanishingly small, suggesting a feasible experimental observation route in the nondegenerate temperature regime.

## Method

The authors employed a large-N expansion (with N fermionic flavors for each spin) and a dimensional epsilon expansion (around four or two spatial dimensions) to treat the strongly interacting Fermi gas nonperturbatively. They defined a many-body phase shift for the quasirepulsive upper branch by subtracting the bound-state contribution (a π-shift) from the attractive-branch phase shift above the scattering threshold, which was justified by resumming two-particle virial contributions from the scattering continuum to all orders in fugacity. The pressure was then computed from a non-interacting mixture of fermions and bosonic pairs using the repulsive phase shift, and the Stoner instability was determined from the spin susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the Stoner transition occurs at \(k_F a_s \simeq 0.8-0.9\) from quantum Monte Carlo simulations, consistent with the authors' nonperturbative approach.
- At resonance, the Stoner transition temperature becomes of the order of the Fermi temperature \(T_F\), around which the molecule formation rate becomes vanishingly small.
- For a unitary Fermi gas in the attractive branch, the large-N expansion predicts a Bertsch parameter \(\xi_N = 0.279\) at zero temperature, compared to the experimental value \(\xi = 0.376 \pm 0.005\).
- The large-N prediction for Tan's contact at unitarity is \(C_N = 0.0789 k_F^4\), which is 1.4% below the bold diagrammatic Monte Carlo result \(C = 0.080 \pm 0.005 k_F^4\).
- The dimensional epsilon expansion at next-to-leading order predicts a Bertsch parameter \(\xi_{\rm{NLO}} = 0.377 \pm 0.014\) and a superfluid transition temperature \((T_c/T_F)_{\rm{NLO}} = 0.183 \pm 0.014\) for the attractive unitary Fermi gas.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Stoner ferromagnetism in Fermi gases, which were limited to perturbation theory (predicting transitions at \(k_F a_s = \pi/2\) or \(k_F a_s \simeq 1.054\)) or high polarization, and addresses the controversy between experimental reports by Jo (2009) and Sanner (2012). The nonperturbative approach builds on the large-N expansion method previously applied to Bose gases by Nikolic and Veillette (2007) and the dimensional epsilon expansion by Nishida and Son (2006-2007), and it improves upon the excluded-molecular-pole approximation by Shenoy (2011) by avoiding a forbidden low-temperature region and enabling access to the repulsive unitary limit. The results enable future experimental searches for Stoner ferromagnetism at finite temperatures where atom loss is suppressed.
