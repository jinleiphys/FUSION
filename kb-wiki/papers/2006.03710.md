# Estimating Compressibility from Maximal-mass Compact Star Observations

> We investigated recent observation data of pulsar masses of PSR J0740$+$6620, PSR J0348$+$0432, and PSR J1614$-$2230 based on the extended $σ$-$ω$ model. We assumed that these pulsars are maximal mass compact star, which suggest that the core approximation can be applied. Using the linear relations between the microscopic and macroscopic parameters of neutron stars suggested by this model, we estimated the values of the nucleon Landau mass and nuclear compressibility $m_{L} = 776.0^{+38.5}_{-84.9}$~MeV and $K=242.7^{+57.2 }_{-28.0}$, respectively.

## Key claim
Using the extended sigma-omega model and assuming that three observed pulsars (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J1614-2230) are maximal-mass compact stars, the authors estimate the nucleon Landau mass and nuclear compressibility from the pulsar mass data, finding values consistent with saturated nuclear matter parameters. The key qualifier is that the "core approximation" is applied, meaning the crust is neglected and only the core of the star is considered, which is justified only for maximal-mass stars.

## Method
The authors used the extended sigma-omega mean-field model at zero temperature and finite chemical potential to describe dense nuclear matter in beta-equilibrium. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with a core approximation stopping condition (p(r)=p0 from the BPS crust equation of state) to compute mass-radius diagrams for maximal-mass stars. Linear and two-parameter phenomenological formulae connecting the microscopic parameters (Landau mass mL and compressibility K) to macroscopic observables (maximal mass MmaxM and corresponding radius RmaxM) were fitted from the model calculations, then applied to the observed pulsar masses to extract mL and K values.

## Key numbers
- Landau mass average: mL = 776.0 (+38.5 / -84.9) MeV, estimated from the three pulsar mass data assuming they are maximal-mass stars.
- Nuclear compressibility average: K = 242.7 (+57.2 / -28.0) MeV, estimated from the same pulsar data.
- For PSR J0740+6620 (mass 2.17 +0.11 / -0.10 solar masses): mL = 748.39 (+63.3 / -57.2) MeV, K = 351.8 (+115 / -84.5) MeV.
- For PSR J0348+0432 (mass 2.01 +0.04 / -0.04 solar masses): mL = 785.25 (+20.0 / -20.3) MeV, K = 206.4 (+42.7 / -20.5) MeV.
- For PSR J1614-2230 (mass 1.97 +0.04 / -0.04 solar masses): mL = 794.47 (+20.1 / -20.4) MeV, K = 170.0 (+15.5 / -20.9) MeV.
- Linear fit for maximal mass as function of compressibility: MmaxM (solar masses) = 1.766 + 0.00110 K (MeV), with theoretical uncertainty less than 2%.
- Linear fit for radius as function of compressibility: RmaxM (km) = 8.878 + 0.00767 K (MeV), with theoretical uncertainty less than 14%.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Posfay 2020) which established linear relations between macroscopic observables and the Landau mass for maximal-mass stars, and found that the Landau mass variation has about 10 times stronger effect on maximal mass than compressibility variation, which in turn is about 10 times stronger than asymmetry energy variation. The results are consistent with a more complex Bayesian analysis by the same group (Alvarez 2020) that used a set of 10^3 equations of state and included gravitational wave and radius constraints, yielding mL = 727.4 +/- 15 MeV and K = 232 +/- 20 MeV. The estimated compressibility also agrees with laboratory measurements of K approximately 240 +/- 20 MeV (Shlomo 2006, Piekarewicz 2010) and with the range of 203-275 MeV from various equation of states summarized by Klahn (2006). The work enables future studies to use the simple linear formulae for rapid estimation of nuclear parameters from new pulsar mass measurements, though the authors note the "masquerade problem" where different nuclear parameter combinations can produce similar macroscopic observables.
