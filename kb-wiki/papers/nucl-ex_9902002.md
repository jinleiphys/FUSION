# Study of supernarrow dibaryon production in process pd-->pX

> The reactions p+d-> p+pX and p+d-> p+dX at 305 MeV are studied with the aim to search for supernarrow dibaryons, decay of which into two nucleons is suppressed by the Pauli principle. The experiment was carried out at the Moscow Meson Factory using a spectrometer TAMS, which detected the scattered proton in coincidence with the second charged particle (the proton or deuteron) at various angles. Narrow peaks at 1905 and 1924 MeV with widths less than the experimental resolution (3 MeV) have been observed in missing- mass spectra of the process pd->pX. An analysis of the angular dependence of the experimental data shows that the resonance at $M$=1905 MeV most likely corresponds to the production of the isovector supernarrow dibaryon.

# Study of supernarrow dibaryon production in process pd→pX

## Key claim
Two narrow structures at missing masses of 1905 MeV and 1924 MeV, each with width less than the experimental resolution of 3 MeV, are observed in the reactions p+d→p+X at 305 MeV; the peak at 1905 MeV most likely corresponds to an isovector supernarrow dibaryon whose decay into two nucleons is suppressed by the Pauli principle, based on angular dependence analysis of experimental and simulated yields.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the Moscow Meson Factory using a 305 MeV proton beam on CD₂ and ¹²C targets, with the pd-reaction contribution isolated by subtracting the carbon spectrum. The two-arm spectrometer TAMS detected the scattered proton in the left movable arm at θ_L = 70° or 72.5° in coincidence with a second charged particle (proton or deuteron) in the right fixed arm at θ_R = 33°, 35°, and 37°, using ΔE−ΔE−E telescopes for energy and time-of-flight measurement. Missing-mass spectra were constructed from the energy spectra of the scattered proton, and Monte Carlo simulations were performed to estimate expected contributions from supernarrow dibaryons with various quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- Incident proton energy: 305 MeV
- Peak at 1905 ± 2 MeV containing 58 ± 13 events, statistical significance 4.5 standard deviations
- Peak at 1924 ± 2 MeV containing 79 ± 16 events, statistical significance 4.7 standard deviations
- Widths of both peaks: less than experimental resolution of 3 MeV
- Expected ratio of calculated contributions for isovector dibaryon interpretation: 0.3 : 1 : 0.7 across the three angular configurations

## Context
This work extends earlier measurements by Fil'kov et al. (1998) which observed a narrow structure at 1905 MeV with 7 MeV resolution using a double-arm spectrometer at the same energy. The improved facility (TAMS spectrometer) achieved 3 MeV resolution and confirmed the narrow structures. The results support theoretical predictions of supernarrow dibaryons by Fil'kov et al. (1992), Akhmedov et al. (1993), Gerasyuta et al. (1993), and Alimov et al. (1994), whose decay into two nucleons is suppressed by the Pauli exclusion principle. The angular dependence analysis enables discrimination between isovector and isoscalar dibaryon assignments, with the isovector interpretation favored. Further measurements at additional angles would be needed to fully characterize the 1924 MeV structure.
