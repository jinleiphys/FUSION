# Proof of Factorization of Fragmentation Function in Non-Equilibrium QCD

> In this paper we prove factorization of fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD by using Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism. We use the background field method of QCD in a pure gauge in path integral approach to prove factorization of fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD. Our proof is valid in any arbitrary gauge fixing parameter $α$. This may be relevant to study hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at high energy heavy-ion colliders at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD factorizes into a non-perturbative correlation function multiplied by Wilson lines, using the background field method in a pure gauge combined with the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism, with the proof valid for any arbitrary gauge fixing parameter α.

## Method

The author extends the background field method of QCD to non-equilibrium systems by combining it with the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism. A gauge fixing identity relating the generating functional in QCD to that in the background field method in a pure gauge is used to factor soft and collinear divergences into path-ordered exponentials (Wilson lines). The proof is carried out for non-equilibrium QCD with an arbitrary initial state |in> representing the medium, using the frozen ghost formalism for the medium part at initial time.

## Key numbers

- The fragmentation function definition includes a prefactor 1/(1+f_q(k)) where f_q(k) is the arbitrary non-equilibrium distribution function of the quark, replacing the vacuum expectation value ⟨0|...|0⟩ with ⟨in|...|in⟩.
- The Wilson line is expressed as P exp[ig ∫_{-∞}^{0} dλ h·A^a(x^μ + h^μ λ) T^a], with h^μ a free four-vector that can be chosen as a lightlike vector n^μ = (1,0,0) or n^μ = (0,1,0) for soft gluons, or as a non-lightlike vector n^μ_B = (n^+_B, n^-_B, 0) for collinear divergences.
- The factorization result in non-equilibrium QCD is given by eq. (fact1fn): ⟨in|ψ^i_r(x_2) ψ̄^j_s(x_1)|in⟩^A_background QCD = [P exp(ig∫_{-∞}^{0} dλ h·A_r^a(x_2^μ + h^μ λ)T^a)] × [⟨in|ψ^i_r(x_2) ψ̄^j_s(x_1)|in⟩^{A=0}_QCD] × [P̄ exp(ig∫_0^{-∞} dλ h·A_s^b(x_1^ν + h^ν λ)T^b)].

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proof of factorization in non-equilibrium QED (Nayak, 2009) to non-equilibrium QCD, which is not straightforward because the gauge fixing and ghost terms in the background field method depend on the background field. It builds on the Collins-Soper-Sterman factorization theorem for fragmentation functions in vacuum (Collins, Soper, Sterman) and on the author's earlier derivation of the non-equilibrium fragmentation function (Nayak, 2009) and DGLAP evolution in non-equilibrium QCD (Nayak, 2009). The result enables the study of hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC using factorized non-equilibrium fragmentation functions.
