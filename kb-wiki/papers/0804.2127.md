# Conservation Properties in the Time-Dependent Hartree Fock Theory

> We discuss the conservation of angular momentum in nuclear time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for a numerical representation of wave functions and potentials on a three-dimensional cartesian grid. Free rotation of a deformed nucleus performs extremely well even for relatively coarse spatial grids. Heavy ion collisions produce a highly excited compound system associated with substantial nucleon emission. These emitted nucleons reach the bounds of the numerical box which leads to a decrease of angular momentum. We discuss strategies to distinguish the physically justified loss from numerical artifacts.

## Key claim

The conservation of angular momentum in time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid is excellent for free rotation of a deformed nucleus, but heavy-ion collisions produce substantial nucleon emission that reaches the numerical box boundary and causes a decrease in angular momentum; the paper works to distinguish this physically justified loss from numerical artifacts.

## Method

The authors solved the static and time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations on a Cartesian three-dimensional mesh with 1 fm grid spacing using the Skyrme energy functional with the SLy6 parametrization, including the minimum set of time-odd terms to assure Galilei invariance. Spatial derivatives were calculated using the Fast Fourier Transform with periodic boundary conditions (except for the Coulomb potential, which used boundary conditions at infinity), and time stepping employed a sixth-order Taylor expansion of the time evolution operator. To disentangle boundary effects, three setups were compared: a small grid of 24×32×32 fm³, a doubled grid of 48×64×64 fm³, and the small grid with an absorbing boundary layer 6 cells wide.

## Key numbers

- For a cranked ²⁴Mg nucleus rotating at ω = 2 MeV/ℏ, the observed angular momentum was 7.54 ℏ, corresponding to a moment of inertia of about 98% of the rigid-body value.
- For free rotation of a deformed nucleus on a 1 fm grid, variations in angular momentum and moments of inertia were of the order of 10⁻⁴.
- For ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at E_cm = 125 MeV with impact parameter b = 4.8 fm, the initial angular momentum was J_x ≈ 33 ℏ; the total reduction in angular momentum was about 30%, with only 1.7 nucleons absorbed.
- For ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at E_cm = 25 MeV with impact parameter b = 2.8 fm, the initial angular momentum was J_x ≈ 8.6 ℏ; the total change in angular momentum exceeded 10%, with 0.4 nucleons emitted.
- Energy and total momentum were conserved with relative fluctuations at the order of 10⁻⁴.
- The time step was Δt ≈ 0.2 fm/c, and the calculation was stable for several thousand time steps with particle number changing by less than 0.1%.

## Context

This work extends the long line of nuclear TDHF applications dating back to Bonche (1976) and the three-dimensional TDHF calculations without symmetry restrictions by Simenel (2003, 2004), Nakatsukasa (2005), Umar (2005, 2006), Maruhn (2005, 2006), and Guo (2007). While conservation of energy and momentum had been tested for all existing codes, conservation of angular momentum had not been studied prior to this paper. The results enable future TDHF calculations to properly account for angular momentum loss in heavy-ion collisions, either by using larger grids, absorbing boundary conditions, or confining analysis to early time evolution of the compound system.
