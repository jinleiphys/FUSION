# Calculable Microscopic Theory for $^{12}$C($־±$, $־³$)$^{16}$O Cross Section near Gamow Window II

> A microscopic approach to the $^{12}$C$(־±, ־³)^{16}$O radiative-capture reaction near the Gamow window has been proposed by Y. Suzuki, Few-Body Syst. {\bf 62}, 2 (2021). The important ingredients of the approach include the following: (1) The states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O relevant to the reaction are described by fully microscopic 3\,$־±$-particle and 4\,$־±$-particle configurations. (2) The isovector electric dipole transition is accounted for through the isospin impurity of the constituent $־±$-particles. (3) The relative motion among the $־±$-particles is expanded in terms of correlated-Gaussian basis functions. A calculation of the radiative-capture cross section demands double angular-momentum projections, that is, the angular momentum of $^{12}$C consisting of 3 $־±$-particles and the orbital angular momentum for $^{12}$C$-־±$ relative motion. Advancing the previous formulation based on the single angular-momentum projection, I carry out the double projection and present all the formulas needed for the cross section calculation.

## Key claim

The paper provides the complete mathematical framework for a microscopic calculation of the $^{12}$C($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{16}$O radiative-capture cross section near the Gamow window, advancing a previous single-projection formulation to include double angular-momentum projections of both the $^{12}$C internal state and the $^{12}$C-$\alpha$ relative motion, with the isospin impurity of $\alpha$-particles treated via a mixing coefficient $\epsilon \approx -4.2 \times 10^{-3}$ determined independently of $^{12}$C $E1$ transition data.

## Method

The author constructs $^{16}$O states from fully antisymmetrized $3\alpha$ (for $^{12}$C) and $4\alpha$ (for $^{16}$O) microscopic cluster-model wave functions, using correlated-Gaussian basis functions to describe the relative motion among $\alpha$-particles. The isovector $E1$ transition is introduced through a small isospin-impurity component in the $\alpha$-particle wave function, with the mixing amplitude estimated by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian for the $\alpha$-particle. The basis functions are expressed in terms of generator-coordinate-method kernels via integral transforms, and all formulas for double angular-momentum projections are derived analytically, enabling coupled-channel calculations including $^{12}$C($0^+$)$+\alpha$ and $^{12}$C($2^+$)$+\alpha$ configurations.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-mixing coefficient $\epsilon$ for the $\alpha$-particle is approximately $-4.2 \times 10^{-3}$, corresponding to a $T=1$ component probability on the order of $2 \times 10^{-3}\%$.
- The Gamow window for the $^{12}$C($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction is at $E_{\rm c.m.} \approx 0.3$ MeV, while experimental measurements have only reached $E_{\rm c.m.} \approx 1$ MeV.
- The $^{12}$C$+\alpha$ threshold in $^{16}$O is at 7.16 MeV, with subthreshold states including $0_1^+$ (0 MeV), $0_2^+$ (6.05 MeV), $2_1^+$ (6.92 MeV), $3_1^-$ (6.13 MeV), and $1_1^-$ (7.12 MeV), and states above threshold including $1_2^-$ (9.59 MeV) and $2_2^+$ (9.84 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach proposed by Suzuki (2021) for the $^{12}$C($\alpha$, $\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction by upgrading from single to double angular-momentum projections, enabling coupled-channel calculations with $^{12}$C($0^+$)$+\alpha$ and $^{12}$C($2^+$)$+\alpha$ configurations. It builds upon earlier semi-microscopic orthogonality-condition model calculations by Suzuki (1976a, 1976b, 1978) and microscopic two-cluster model calculations by Descouvemont and Baye (1987a, 1987b) that included isospin mixing via coupling to nucleon channels, but uses fully microscopic $3\alpha$ cluster-model wave functions for $^{12}$C rather than simple shell-model configurations. The isospin-mixing treatment has been successfully applied to predict the $E1$ decay width of the 9.64 MeV $3^-$ state of $^{12}$C by Suzuki (2024).
