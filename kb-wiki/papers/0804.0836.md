# Light nuclei without a core

> We introduce the no-core full configuration (NCFC) approach and present results for $^4$He, $^{12}$C and $^{14}$F with the realistic NN interaction, JISP16. We obtain ground state energies and their uncertainties through exponential extrapolations that we demonstrate are reliable in $^4$He where fully converged results are obtained. We find $^{12}$C is overbound by 1.7 MeV and we predict the yet-to-be-measured binding energy of $^{14}$F to be $68.1 \pm 2.7$ MeV. The extrapolated spectrum of $^{14}$F is in reasonable agreement with known features of the $^{14}$B spectrum.

## Key claim

The no-core full configuration (NCFC) approach, using the bare JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction without renormalization, can produce converged or extrapolated ground state energies for light nuclei, but the method's reliability depends on the availability of sufficient basis-space data and the exponential extrapolation procedure contains several arbitrary aspects that are cross-checked against solvable cases.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the many-body Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by the maximum total oscillator quanta above the minimum configuration (Nmax), using the code MFDn. They calculate ground state energies at fixed oscillator energy (h-bar Omega) for a sequence of Nmax values, then extrapolate to the infinite-basis limit by fitting an exponential plus constant to the calculated points, excluding Nmax=0. Two extrapolation variants are used: method A fits four successive Nmax points with a common asymptote constraint across multiple h-bar Omega values, while method B fits three successive points at the most stable h-bar Omega value.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground state energy: 28.299(1) MeV (both extrapolation A and B), overbound by 3 +/- 1 keV compared to the experimental value of 28.296 MeV
- 12C ground state energy: 93.9(1.1) MeV (extrapolation A) and 95.1(2.7) MeV (extrapolation B), overbound by about 1.7 MeV relative to the experimental value of 92.162 MeV
- 14F ground state energy prediction: 68.1 +/- 2.7 MeV (extrapolation A) and 69.4 MeV (extrapolation B); the experimental value is not yet measured
- 6He ground state energy: 28.68(12) MeV (extrapolation A) and 28.69(5) MeV (extrapolation B), compared to experimental 29.269 MeV
- 6Li ground state energy: 31.43(12) MeV (extrapolation A) and 31.45(5) MeV (extrapolation B), compared to experimental 31.995 MeV
- 8He ground state energy: 29.74(34) MeV (extrapolation A) and 30.05(60) MeV (extrapolation B), compared to experimental 31.408 MeV
- 16O ground state energy: 143.5(1.0) MeV (extrapolation A) and 150(14) MeV (extrapolation B), compared to experimental 127.619 MeV

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach of Navratil (2007) by using the bare JISP16 interaction without Lee-Suzuki-Okamoto renormalization, which the authors argue produces more uniform convergence trends suitable for exponential extrapolation. The method enables predictions for neutron-deficient nuclei such as 14F, where planned experiments at Texas A&M University may test the predicted binding energy of 68.1 +/- 2.7 MeV, and the extrapolated spectrum shows reasonable agreement with the known features of the mirror nucleus 14B.
