# The production of K+K- pairs in proton-proton collisions at 2.83 GeV

> Differential and total cross sections for the pp -> ppK+K- reaction have been measured at a proton beam energy of 2.83 GeV using the COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer. Detailed model descriptions fitted to a variety of one-dimensional distributions permit the separation of the pp -> pp phi cross section from that of non-phi production. The differential spectra show that higher partial waves represent the majority of the pp -> pp phi total cross section at an excess energy of 76 MeV, whose energy dependence would then seem to require some s-wave phi-p enhancement near threshold. The non-phi data can be described in terms of the combined effects of two-body final state interactions using the same effective scattering parameters determined from lower energy data.

## Key claim

The total cross section for $pp \to pp\phi$ at an excess energy of 76 MeV is dominated by higher partial waves (not pure S-wave), and the energy dependence of this cross section requires some s-wave $\phi p$ enhancement near threshold, though the differential spectra themselves show no direct evidence of such an enhancement.

## Method

The $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ reaction was measured at a proton beam energy of 2.83 GeV using the COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer. Phenomenological parametrizations for both $\phi$ and non-$\phi$ production were fitted to one-dimensional distributions of the experimental data, with the $\phi$ contribution modeled by a spin-averaged squared transition matrix element containing Ss, Sp, Ps, and Pp partial waves, and the non-$\phi$ contribution described by factorized final state interactions in the $pp$, $K^-p$, and $K^+K^-$ systems. The fitted parametrizations were used to perform acceptance corrections via Monte Carlo simulations based on GEANT4.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $pp \to pp\phi$ at $T_p = 2.83$ GeV: $142.2 \pm 2.1 \pm 17.9$ nb (corrected for $\phi \to K^+K^-$ branching ratio of 0.491)
- Total cross section for non-$\phi$ $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ at $T_p = 2.83$ GeV: $91.0 \pm 3.0 \pm 11.4$ nb
- Total cross section for $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ at $T_p = 2.83$ GeV: $160.8 \pm 3.2 \pm 14.4$ nb
- Spin density matrix element $\rho_{00} = 0.30 \pm 0.01$ for the $\phi$ meson at $\varepsilon_\phi = 76$ MeV, extracted from the decay angular distribution parameter $b_K = -0.11 \pm 0.04$
- Fit parameters for the angular distribution $d\sigma/d\Omega = a[1 + b P_2(\cos\theta)]$ for the $\phi$ c.m. production angle: $a = 10.71 \pm 0.21$ nb/sr, $b = 0.21 \pm 0.04$
- Missing-mass resolution for the $K^+K^-p$ system: $\sigma = 4.7$ MeV/$c^2$

## Context

This work extends previous ANKE measurements of $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ at excess energies of 18.5, 34.5, and 76 MeV (Hartmann 2006, Maeda 2008) with much higher statistics at the highest energy, and confirms the importance of higher partial waves suggested by the DISTO collaboration (Balon 2001) at $\varepsilon_\phi = 83$ MeV. The non-$\phi$ data are described using the same effective scattering parameters determined from lower energy COSY-11 data (Winter 2006), and the results provide detailed differential spectra that challenge theoretical models such as those of Tsushima and Nakayama (2003), Kaptari and Kampfer (2005), and Xie et al. (2008). The measured $pp \to pp\phi$ cross section serves as an important ingredient for understanding proton-induced nuclear transparency measurements (Polyanskiy 2011).
