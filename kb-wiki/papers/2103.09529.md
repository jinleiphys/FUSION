# Parton physics from a heavy-quark operator product expansion: Formalism and Wilson coefficients

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) and light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) are central non-perturbative objects of interest in high-energy inelastic and elastic scattering, respectively. As a result, an ab-initio determination of these objects is highly desirable. In this paper we present theoretical details for the calculation of the PDFs and LCDAs using a heavy-quark operator product expansion method. This strategy was proposed in a previous paper [Phys. Rev. D 73, 014501 (2006)] for computing higher moments of the PDFs using lattice QCD. Its central feature is the introduction of a fictitious, valence heavy quark. In the current article, we show that the operator product expansion (OPE) of the hadronic matrix element we study can also be expressed as the convolution of a perturbative matching kernel and the corresponding light-cone distribution, which in principle can be inverted to determine the parton momentum fraction dependence. Regarding the extraction of higher moments, this work also provides the one-loop Wilson coefficients in the OPE formulas for the unpolarized PDF, helicity PDF and pseudo-scalar meson LCDAs. Although these Wilson coefficients for the PDFs can be inferred from existing results in the literature, those for the LCDAs are new.

## Key claim

The heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method, which introduces a fictitious valence heavy quark to suppress higher-twist effects, can be extended beyond the extraction of Mellin moments to directly determine the parton momentum fraction (ξ) dependence of parton distribution functions (PDFs) and light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs), provided the expansion parameter satisfies |ω̃| < ω̃_pole = 1 − (m_π² + m_Ψ² − m_HL²)/Q̃².

## Method

The authors perform a one-loop perturbative calculation of the hadronic amplitude for the heavy-light axial-vector current correlator in the HOPE framework, decomposing the amplitude into box, vertex, and self-energy diagrams for the unpolarized PDF, helicity PDF, and pseudoscalar meson LCDA cases. They regulate ultraviolet and infrared divergences using dimensional regularization in both the MS and on-shell renormalization schemes, and verify consistency by showing that the collinear divergences in the amplitude reproduce those of the Mellin moments in the standard OPE. The finite non-logarithmic parts of the amplitudes are provided in supplementary material, and their Taylor expansion in ω̃ yields the one-loop Wilson coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The convergence radius of the HOPE expansion is determined by the pole at ω̃_pole = 1 − (m_π² + m_Ψ² − m_HL²)/Q̃², with the first branch point at ω̃_b.p. = 1 + ((m_HL + m_π)² − m_Ψ² − m_π²)/Q̃².
- In the massless heavy-quark limit (τ → 1), the hadronic amplitude satisfies the chiral Ward identity q_μ T_q^{μν} = q_ν T_q^{μν} = 0, leading to the relations H₁ = ω̃ H₂ and H₁ = H₃ + H₄.
- The on-shell scheme result for the unpolarized PDF Wilson coefficients differs from the MS scheme by (1−τ)[4 + 3 ln(μ²/m_Ψ²)] × [4ω̃/(1−ω̃²)²] for H₁ and H₃, and by (1−τ)[4 + 3 ln(μ²/m_Ψ²)] × [2(1+ω̃²)/(1−ω̃²)²] for H₂.
- For the helicity PDF, the on-shell scheme result differs from the MS scheme by (1−τ)[4 + 3 ln(μ²/m_Ψ²)] × [2(1+ω̃²)/(1−ω̃²)²].
- For the LCDA, the on-shell scheme result differs from the MS scheme by (1−τ)[4 + 3 ln(μ²/m_Ψ²)] × [8(4 + x₀²ω̃²)/(4 − x₀²ω̃²)²].

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method originally proposed by Detmold (2006) for computing higher moments of PDFs via lattice QCD, now enabling direct determination of the ξ-dependence of PDFs and LCDAs through a perturbative matching kernel. The one-loop Wilson coefficients for the unpolarized and helicity PDFs confirm existing results from Gottschalk (1980) and Gluck (1996) for heavy-quark production in deep inelastic scattering, while the one-loop Wilson coefficients for the LCDAs are new to this work. The approach is related to the quasi-PDF method of Ji (2013), the lattice cross section approach of Ma (2017), and the pseudo-PDF method of Radyushkin (2017), but uses a fictitious heavy quark to provide additional higher-twist suppression beyond the large momentum transfer Q².
