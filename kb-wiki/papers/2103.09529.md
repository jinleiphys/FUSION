# Parton physics from a heavy-quark operator product expansion: Formalism and Wilson coefficients

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) and light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) are central non-perturbative objects of interest in high-energy inelastic and elastic scattering, respectively. As a result, an ab-initio determination of these objects is highly desirable. In this paper we present theoretical details for the calculation of the PDFs and LCDAs using a heavy-quark operator product expansion method. This strategy was proposed in a previous paper [Phys. Rev. D 73, 014501 (2006)] for computing higher moments of the PDFs using lattice QCD. Its central feature is the introduction of a fictitious, valence heavy quark. In the current article, we show that the operator product expansion (OPE) of the hadronic matrix element we study can also be expressed as the convolution of a perturbative matching kernel and the corresponding light-cone distribution, which in principle can be inverted to determine the parton momentum fraction dependence. Regarding the extraction of higher moments, this work also provides the one-loop Wilson coefficients in the OPE formulas for the unpolarized PDF, helicity PDF and pseudo-scalar meson LCDAs. Although these Wilson coefficients for the PDFs can be inferred from existing results in the literature, those for the LCDAs are new.

## Method

The authors perform a one-loop perturbative calculation of the hadronic amplitude for the heavy-light axial-vector current correlator in the HOPE framework, decomposing the amplitude into box, vertex, and self-energy diagrams for the unpolarized PDF, helicity PDF, and pseudoscalar meson LCDA cases. They regulate ultraviolet and infrared divergences using dimensional regularization in both the MS and on-shell renormalization schemes, and verify consistency by showing that the collinear divergences in the amplitude reproduce those of the Mellin moments in the standard OPE. The finite non-logarithmic parts of the amplitudes are provided in supplementary material, and their Taylor expansion in П‰Мѓ yields the one-loop Wilson coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method originally proposed by Detmold (2006) for computing higher moments of PDFs via lattice QCD, now enabling direct determination of the Оѕ-dependence of PDFs and LCDAs through a perturbative matching kernel. The one-loop Wilson coefficients for the unpolarized and helicity PDFs confirm existing results from Gottschalk (1980) and Gluck (1996) for heavy-quark production in deep inelastic scattering, while the one-loop Wilson coefficients for the LCDAs are new to this work. The approach is related to the quasi-PDF method of Ji (2013), the lattice cross section approach of Ma (2017), and the pseudo-PDF method of Radyushkin (2017), but uses a fictitious heavy quark to provide additional higher-twist suppression beyond the large momentum transfer QВІ.
