# Role of Hyperons in Neutron Stars

> The latest observation of a Shapiro delay of the binary millisecond pulsar J1614-2230 by Demorest et al. Nature 467 1081 (2010) yielded the pulsar gravitational mass to be 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar mass, the heaviest observed pulsar to-date. This result produces a stringent constraint on Equation(s) of State (EoS) of high density neutron star matter. One of the main conclusions of Demorest et al. was that their result makes the presence of non-nucleonic components in the neutron star matter unlikely. We compare the result with our recent work and conclude that hyperons in high-density matter are fully consistent with the observation and that their presence is a necessary consequence of general physical laws.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the observation of a 1.97 ± 0.04 solar mass pulsar by Demorest et al. (2010) does not rule out hyperons in neutron star interiors, contrary to Demorest et al.'s conclusion; hyperons are fully consistent with this mass when using the Quark-Meson-Coupling model, and their presence is a necessary consequence of the Pauli principle and weak interaction equilibrium at high density.

## Method

The authors compare the observed pulsar mass of J1614-2230 with predictions from the Quark-Meson-Coupling (QMC) model, which incorporates the full baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) and accounts for medium modifications of quark and gluon structure. They compute mass-radius relations by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation using five closely related versions of the QMC model with hyperons and five with nucleons only, and compare the resulting maximum masses and radii to the observational constraint.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar J1614-2230 gravitational mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (Demorest et al., 2010)
- Upper limit on maximum density of observable cold matter from Demorest et al.: 3.74 ± 0.15 × 10^15 g cm^-3 (~10 times nuclear saturation density n_s)
- QMC model predicts cold neutron star masses in the range (1.99 – 1.90) solar masses for matter containing the full baryon octet
- Corresponding radii: (12.45 – 11.93) km
- Central densities: (1.66 – 1.74) × 10^15 g cm^-3, equivalent to (6.61 – 6.93) n_s
- Central pressures: (182 – 209) MeV fm^-3
- Gravitational red shift: 0.38
- Speed of sound: 0.66 c
- Radii of stars compatible with the observation cluster near 12.35 km
- Hyperons become energetically favorable at densities of (2 – 3) n_s

## Context

This work directly responds to Demorest et al. (2010), who argued that their 1.97 solar mass pulsar measurement made non-nucleonic components unlikely based on a limited selection of typical equations of state. The authors extend their own prior work (Stone et al., 2007) using the QMC model, which differs from relativistic field models by avoiding uncertainties in hyperon-nucleon coupling constants and correctly reproducing binding energies of known Λ hypernuclei while not binding Σ hypernuclei (Guichon et al., 2008). The result challenges theorists to develop equations of state, like those from QMC, that are consistent with both hypernuclear data and the new observational constraint.
