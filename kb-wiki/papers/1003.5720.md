# Shell structure in neutron-rich Ca and Ni nuclei under semi-realistic mean fields

> Shell structure in the neutron-rich Ca and Ni nuclei is investigated by the spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with the semi-realistic $NN$ interactions. Specific ingredients of the effective interaction, particularly the tensor force, often play a key role in the $Z$ dependence of the neutron shell structure. Such examples are found in N=32 and N=40; N=32 becomes magic or submagic in $^{52}$Ca while its magicity is broken in $^{60}$Ni, and N=40 is submagic (though not magic) in $^{68}$Ni but not in $^{60}$Ca. Comments are given on the doubly magic nature of $^{78}$Ni. We point out that the loose binding can lead to a submagic number N=58 in $^{86}$Ni, assisted by the weak pair coupling.

## Key claim

The tensor force and the central part of the one-pion exchange potential together account for the Z-dependence of neutron shell structure, producing the N=32 magicity in 52Ca and its breakdown in 60Ni, while the N=40 submagic nature in 68Ni is likely destroyed in 60Ca due to tensor-force-driven Z dependence. The paper does not support N=34 as a magic number even when a realistically strong tensor force is included.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations were performed for neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes using the semi-realistic M3Y-P5' interaction, which includes explicit tensor channels and the central part of the one-pion exchange potential. For comparison, calculations were also carried out with M3Y-P4' (no tensor force), the Gogny D1S interaction, and the Skyrme SkM* functional. Single-particle energies, shell gaps, and pair energies from HF and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were analyzed to identify magic and submagic numbers.

## Key numbers

- N=32 shell gap in 52Ca: comparable among D1S, M3Y-P4', and M3Y-P5' interactions, consistent with measured Ex(2+1) value
- Z=28 shell gap in 78Ni: 5.8 MeV with M3Y-P5', sufficient to maintain magicity
- Ex(2+1) in 78Ni predicted by HF+RPA: 3.15 MeV (D1S), 3.00 MeV (D1M), 3.28 MeV (M3Y-P4'), 3.25 MeV (M3Y-P5')
- B(E2;2+1 -> 0+1) in 78Ni predicted by HF+RPA: 84.4 e2fm4 (D1S), 83.4 e2fm4 (D1M), 87.6 e2fm4 (M3Y-P4'), 84.4 e2fm4 (M3Y-P5')
- Pairing coupling matrix element between n2s1/2 and n1d3/2 in 86Ni: approximately 0.3 MeV (M3Y-P5')
- Energy difference 2[epsilon_n(1d3/2)-epsilon_n(2s1/2)] in 86Ni: approximately 3 MeV
- Ex(2+1) in 86Ni from HF+RPA: approximately 1 MeV
- B(E2;2+1 -> 0+1) in 86Ni from HF+RPA: 10-20 e2fm4

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of semi-realistic M3Y-type interactions (Nakada 2003, 2008, 2010) to systematically investigate shell evolution in neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes. It builds on experimental indications of N=32 magicity in 52Ca (references N32, Ca52_Ex2) and N=40 submagicity in 68Ni (reference Ni68), and on theoretical work by Otsuka et al. (reference Vtn) emphasizing the tensor force role in shell evolution. The results contradict predictions of N=34 magicity from shell model calculations (reference GXPF1A) and show that the tensor force alone does not guarantee N=34 as a magic number. The prediction of N=58 submagicity in 86Ni from loose binding and weak pair coupling provides a testable hypothesis for future experiments on drip-line nuclei.
