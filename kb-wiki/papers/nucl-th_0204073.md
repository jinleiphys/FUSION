# Learning from Dispersive Effects in the Nucleon Polarisabilities

## Key claim
Dynamical (energy-dependent) nucleon polarisabilities do not contain more or less information than the corresponding Compton scattering amplitudes, but they make the underlying physics more readily accessible and easier to interpret. The analysis provides stringent constraints for models and model-independent power countings of low-energy effective field theories of the nucleon, though the agreement with dispersion theory is limited to the regime where the chiral effective field theory is supposed to work.

## Method
The paper defines dynamical nucleon polarisabilities as the energy-dependent generalization of static polarisabilities extracted from real Compton scattering on the nucleon. The non-Born part of the Compton amplitude is expanded in multipoles to extract the first four electric and magnetic dipole and quadrupole polarisabilities (α_E1(ω), β_M1(ω), α_E2(ω), β_M2(ω)). The results from a dispersion theory analysis are compared with a chiral effective field theory called Modified Small Scale Expansion (MSSE), which includes the nucleon, the Δ(1232) resonance, and their respective pion clouds as explicit low-energy degrees of freedom at leading one-loop order.

## Key numbers
- Static magnetic dipole polarisability: β_M1(0) = 1.5 (experimentally measured value, in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory prediction for static magnetic dipole polarisability: β_M1^HBχPT(0) = 1.2 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Counter term strengths fitted to reproduce static dipole polarisabilities: δα_E1 ≈ -5, δβ_M1 ≈ -10 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), much larger than the naive dimensional analysis estimate of |δα_E1, δβ_M1| ~ 1-2
- Discrepancy between dispersion theory and chiral calculations appears at photon energies ω > 250 MeV, suggesting a possible nucleon resonance

## Context
This work extends the concept of static nucleon polarisabilities to energy-dependent dynamical polarisabilities, providing a bridge between dispersion theory and chiral effective field theory approaches to low-energy QCD. The analysis builds on prior work by Griesshammer and collaborators (2002) on Compton scattering and polarisabilities, and compares the MSSE approach with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (which includes only pions and nucleons). The results enable parameter-free predictions of the energy dependence of polarisabilities once the zero-energy values are fixed, and demonstrate that the Δ(1232) resonance must be included as an explicit degree of freedom to correctly describe the magnetic dipole polarisability, even below the pion production threshold.
