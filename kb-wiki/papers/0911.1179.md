# The $Λ(1405)$ as a Hybrid

> Using the QCD Sum Rule Method, we estimate the mass of the lowest strange hybrid baryon with $IJ^P=0(1/2)^-$. We find the mass is approximately that of the $Λ(1405)$, whose nature has been a puzzle for many decades. Possible tests of this result are discussed.

## Key claim

Using QCD Sum Rules, the authors find that the mass of the lowest strange hybrid baryon with quantum numbers \(IJ^P = 0(1/2)^-\) is approximately 1407 MeV, consistent with the \(\Lambda(1405)\) mass, but they note that the calculation has an estimated error of about 15% and that the result depends on the choice of parameters \(s_0\) and \(c_i\) to achieve a stable sum rule solution.

## Method

The authors constructed a QCD Sum Rule calculation using a current for a strange hybrid baryon with \(IJ^P = 0(1/2)^-\) composed of three quarks (u, d, s) in a color octet plus a valence octet gluon. They computed the operator product expansion (OPE) for the correlator up to dimension-six operators, including the perturbative term, gluon condensate, three-gluon condensate, and strange quark condensate contributions, then applied a Borel transform to extract the mass. They also investigated a mixed hybrid/three-quark current to estimate the three-quark admixture, finding it contributes only about 20% of the second-order term in the hybrid correlator and does not change the mass result within errors.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the lowest strange hybrid baryon with \(IJ^P = 0(1/2)^-\) is found to be 1407 MeV, approximately matching the \(\Lambda(1405)\) mass.
- The continuum threshold parameter \(s_0\) is set to 2.5 GeV\(^2\) in the sum rule solution.
- The gluon condensate used is \(\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = 0.0377\) GeV\(^2\).
- The three-gluon condensate used is \(\langle g^3 f G^3 \rangle = 0.0422\) GeV\(^6\).
- The estimated error in the mass calculation is about 15%.
- The mixed hybrid/three-quark correlator contribution from the three-quark component is approximately 20% of the second-order term in the hybrid correlator.

## Context

This work extends prior QCD Sum Rule studies of hybrid baryons, specifically the calculation of the Roper resonance as a hybrid by Kisslinger and collaborators (1995), and the study of the \(\Lambda(1600)\) as a strange hybrid by Kisslinger (2004). It contrasts with bag model studies by Barnes and Close (1983) which found the lightest hybrid baryon at 1710 MeV and ruled out a mass of 1405 MeV, and with chiral SU(3) meson-baryon potential models that treat the \(\Lambda(1405)\) as a \(\bar{K}-N\) resonance. The result enables future experimental tests of the hybrid nature of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) via sigma decay into \(\Lambda + 2\pi\) and polarization measurements in photoproduction, which are being studied at JLAB with the CLAS detector.
