# Efficient creation of maximally entangled states by modulation of tunneling rates

> For systems described by the two-site Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian, we show that a sinusoidal modulation of the tunneling matrix element assists higher-order co-tunneling processes. Using this mechanism, we propose an efficient new scheme for creating a coherent superposition of states in which all particles are either on one site or all on the other site, the so-called NOON state. This scheme yields an almost perfect NOON state periodically. For larger numbers of particles, further reduction of the time to create the state is possible if more than one modulation frequency is employed. With this scheme, NOON states with a larger number of particles could be realized with state-of-the-art techniques for cold Bose gases in a double-well potential.

# Efficient creation of maximally entangled states by modulation of tunneling rates

## Key claim

By sinusoidally modulating the tunneling matrix element in a two-site Bose-Hubbard system, higher-order co-tunneling processes are assisted, enabling the periodic creation of almost perfect NOON states in a time much shorter than the exponentially long tunneling period without modulation. The scheme works provided the modulation amplitude is large enough to couple the states with \(|\Delta N|=N\) and \(|\Delta N|=N-2\), and the modulation frequency satisfies a resonance condition \(\hbar\omega \simeq U(N-1)\).

## Method

The authors consider the two-site Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian with a time-dependent tunneling matrix element \(J(t) = J(1 + A \sin \omega t)\). Starting from the initial state \(|0,N\rangle\) (all particles in one well), they numerically evolve the system and analyze the resulting dynamics of \(\langle \Delta N \rangle\) and its fluctuation \(\sigma_{\Delta N}\). Floquet theory is used to identify resonance conditions by examining the eigenvalues of the Floquet operator as functions of modulation frequency.

## Key numbers

- For \(N=5\), \(U/J=4\), \(A=0.1\), and \(\hbar\omega/J=15\), the tunneling period is \(T \simeq 88\, T_0\), where \(T_0 = \pi\hbar/J\). Without modulation, the period would be \(T = 1228.8\, T_0\).
- At \(t = 285.27\, T_0\) for the same parameters, the fluctuation \(\sigma_{\Delta N} = 4.994\), indicating an almost perfect NOON state (maximum possible is \(N=5\)).
- The reduction ratio between the unmodulated and modulated tunneling periods for \(N=5\) and \(U/J=4\) is \(T_N/T_{N-2} = 460.8\).
- The resonance condition for coupling \(|\Delta N|=N\) and \(|\Delta N|=N-2\) is \(\hbar\omega \simeq U(N-1)\); for \(N=5\) and \(U/J=4\), this gives \(\hbar\omega/J = 16\).
- The width \(\Gamma\) of the wide resonance scales as \(\Gamma \simeq 0.49\, A\, U\, (N-1)(N-2)\) for \(N \lesssim 6\).
- For \(N=4\) and \(U/J=5\), the decoherence time due to three-body losses in a \({}^{87}\)Rb experiment is \(\tau_3 \simeq 16\) ms, while the tunneling period can be reduced to \(\lesssim 20\, T_0\) (corresponding to \(\lesssim 14.4\) ms in that experiment).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Watanabe, 2008) which showed that NOON states can be formed by co-tunneling in a double-well potential under strong interactions, but with a formation time that grows exponentially with particle number \(N\). The present scheme overcomes this drawback by using a sinusoidal modulation of the tunneling rate, achieving a dramatic reduction in formation time. The mechanism is related to protocols for creating NOON states in double-well potentials (Gordon and Savage, 1999; Micheli et al., 2003; Mahmud et al., 2005) and is also relevant to Josephson qubits. The results enable the realization of NOON states with \(N\) comparable to the largest values achieved for trapped ions (Leibfried et al., 2005) using current cold-atom experiments (Folling et al., 2007).
