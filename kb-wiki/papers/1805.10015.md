# Universal aspects of the phase diagram of QCD with heavy quarks

> The flavor dependence of the QCD phase diagram presents universal properties in the heavy quark limit. For the wide class of models where the quarks are treated at the one-loop level, we show, for arbitrary chemical potential, that the flavor dependence of the critical quark masses-for which the confinement-deconfinement transition is second order-is insensitive to the details of the (confining) gluon dynamics and that the critical temperature is constant along the corresponding critical line. We illustrate this with explicit results in various such one-loop models studied in the literature: effective matrix models for the Polyakov loop, the Curci-Ferrari model, and a recently proposed Gribov-Zwanziger-type model. We further observe that the predictions which follow from this one-loop universality property are well satisfied by different calculations beyond one-loop order, including lattice simulations. For degenerate quarks, we propose a simple universal law for the flavor dependence of the critical mass, satisfied by all approaches.

# Universal aspects of the phase diagram of QCD with heavy quarks

## Key claim
For a wide class of models where quarks are treated at the one-loop level, the flavor dependence of the critical quark masses (where the confinement-deconfinement transition becomes second order) is insensitive to the details of the confining gluon dynamics, and the critical temperature is constant along the corresponding critical line, these properties hold for arbitrary chemical potential and extend beyond one-loop order, including to lattice simulations.

## Method
The authors analyze the effective potential for QCD with heavy quarks, which takes the form V = v_glue(ℓ,β) − 2N_f f(βM)ℓ at zero chemical potential, where v_glue is a confining glue potential and f(x) = (3x²/π²)K₂(x) with K₂ the modified Bessel function. The critical point is determined by solving ∂_ℓV = ∂_ℓ²V = ∂_ℓ³V = 0; the last two equations involve only v_glue, making the critical ℓ and β independent of quark content. This analysis is extended to nonzero chemical potential and to nondegenerate quark masses, and tested against explicit calculations in the Curci-Ferrari model at one- and two-loop order, a Gribov-Zwanziger-type model (with three scenarios GZ1, GZ2, GZ3), a matrix model, and Dyson-Schwinger equation results.

## Key numbers
- For N_f = 1, 2, 3 degenerate flavors at μ = 0, the critical mass parameter R_N_f = βM_c takes values: lattice gives R₁ = 7.23, R₂ = 7.92, R₃ = 8.33; GZ1 gives R₁ = 7.09, R₂ = 7.92, R₃ = 8.40; CF1 (one-loop) gives R₁ = 6.74, R₂ = 7.59, R₃ = 8.07; CF2 (two-loop) gives R₁ = 7.53, R₂ = 8.40, R₃ = 8.90.
- The ratio Y₃ = (R₃ − R₁)/(R₂ − R₁) is approximately 1.58–1.59 across all approaches (lattice, GZ1, GZ2, matrix model, CF1, CF2, DSE), consistent with the universal asymptotic formula Y_N_f ≈ ln N_f / ln 2.
- At imaginary chemical potential βμ = iπ/3, lattice gives R₁ = 5.56, R₂ = 6.25, R₃ = 6.66; GZ1 gives R₁ = 5.02, R₂ = 5.91, R₃ = 6.42; CF2 gives R₁ = 5.47, R₂ = 6.41, R₃ = 6.94.
- The lattice scaling relation at large mass is N_f exp(−R_N_f) cosh(β_c μ) ≈ 0.00075, valid for all μ.

## Context
This work extends and unifies observations from lattice simulations (Saito 2011, Fromm 2011) and matrix models (Kashiwa 2012, 2013) that the critical temperature is approximately independent of quark content in the heavy quark limit. It provides a theoretical explanation for this universality within one-loop treatments and demonstrates it across multiple continuum approaches: the Curci-Ferrari model (Reinosa 2015, Maelger 2017), a Gribov-Zwanziger-type model (Canfora 2015, Kroff 2018), and Dyson-Schwinger equation results (Fischer 2014). The universal scaling law Y_N_f ≈ ln N_f / ln 2 enables prediction of critical masses for larger N_f and provides a robust test for future continuum and lattice calculations.
