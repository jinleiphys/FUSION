# Comment on "Entropy of Classical Systems with Long-Range Interactions" by T.M. Rocha Filho et al, PRL 95, 190601 (2005)

> In a recent letter (PRL 95, 190601 (2005)), T.M. Rocha Filho and coworkers address the very interesting issue of the entropic form to be used for Hamiltonians with long-range interactions. In our opinion the letter misses several points quite debated in the recent literature which are of fundamental importance for a complete discussion of this problem. Moreover it contains several statements which are not true or not corroborated by any evidence. In this comment we discuss these arguments and severely question the generality of the conclusions of this letter.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the conclusions of Rocha Filho et al. (2005) are not general because they assume homogeneity and equiprobability of microstates in quasi-stationary states of long-range Hamiltonian systems, whereas numerical evidence shows strong hierarchical microscopic structure that invalidates the claim that Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy is always the correct form to use.

## Method

The comment re-examines the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, a paradigmatic long-range interacting system, and contrasts the assumptions of Rocha Filho et al. (2005) with published numerical evidence from multiple groups. The authors identify three specific incorrect statements from the target paper: that interparticle correlations are negligible in the N→∞ limit, that all microstates are equally probable in the quasi-stationary state, and that Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy is always the correct form. They argue that the order of limits (t→∞ after N→∞ versus N→∞ after t→∞) is crucial and that the target paper ignores relevant results on entropy extensivity and nonextensive statistics.

## Key numbers

- The prediction γ = 2/(3-q) for the anomalous diffusion coefficient has been successfully verified in the HMF model, where γ is the anomalous diffusion exponent and q is the Tsallis entropic parameter.
- The comment references system sizes and initial conditions studied in the HMF model, but no specific numerical values for system sizes, energies, or precision are given in the text.

## Context

This comment extends the debate on whether Boltzmann-Gibbs or nonextensive (Tsallis) statistics is appropriate for long-range interacting Hamiltonian systems, directly challenging Rocha Filho et al. (2005). It builds on prior work by Tsallis (1988) proposing generalized statistics, and on numerical studies of the HMF model by Rapisarda, Pluchino, Latora, and Tsallis (2001-2006) showing q-exponential decay of velocity correlations and anomalous diffusion. The comment also references Morita and Kaneko (2006) who observed metastable collective oscillations beyond Vlasov analysis, and notes that criticisms cited in Rocha Filho et al. (2005) were already replied to by Tsallis (2004). The work enables further investigation into whether a new kinetic theory beyond standard statistical mechanics is needed for long-range systems.
