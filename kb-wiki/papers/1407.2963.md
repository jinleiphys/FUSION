# Jet quenching from the lattice

> We present a lattice study of the momentum broadening experienced by a hard parton in the quark-gluon plasma. In particular, the contributions to this real-time phenomenon from soft modes are extracted from a set of gauge-invariant operators in a dimensionally reduced effective theory (electrostatic QCD), which can be simulated on a Euclidean lattice. At the temperatures accessible to present experiments, the soft contributions to the jet quenching parameter are found to be quite large. We compare our results to phenomenological models and to holographic computations.

## Key claim

The soft (non-perturbative) contributions to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) are found to be quite large at temperatures accessible to present experiments, around 6 GeV\(^2\)/fm at RHIC temperatures, with a total uncertainty of about 15–20%.

## Method

The authors separate the contributions to momentum broadening from hard thermal excitations (evaluated analytically in weak-coupling) from those due to soft modes up to the scale \(gT\), which are extracted non-perturbatively from Monte Carlo simulations of a dimensionally reduced effective theory, electrostatic QCD (EQCD). The soft contribution is obtained by measuring the expectation value of a gauge-invariant "decorated Wilson loop" operator on a Euclidean lattice, whose exponential decay defines the coordinate-space collision kernel \(V(r)\), and the curvature of \(V(r)\) near the origin yields \(\hat{q}\). The EQCD parameters are matched to QCD with \(n_f=2\) light quarks at temperatures \(T \simeq 398\) MeV and \(T \simeq 2\) GeV, including subleading corrections.

## Key numbers

- The final estimate for \(\hat{q}\) at \(T \simeq 398\) MeV (RHIC temperatures) is around 6 GeV\(^2\)/fm, with a total uncertainty of 15–20%.
- The study uses two temperatures: \(T \simeq 398\) MeV (roughly twice the deconfinement temperature) and \(T \simeq 2\) GeV (roughly ten times the deconfinement temperature).
- The perturbative NLO expression for \(\hat{q}\) is given as \(g^4 T^2 m_D C_f C_a (3\pi^2 + 10 - 4\ln 2)/(32\pi^2)\), with \(C_f = 4/3\) and \(C_a = 3\) for SU(3).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Caron-Huot (2008) and Ghiglieri (2013), who showed that soft contributions to light-cone physics can be computed exactly in the dimensionally reduced effective theory EQCD. The results contradict naive perturbative expectations that soft contributions would be small, and instead yield values close to holographic estimates from Liu (2006), Armesto (2006), and Gursoy (2009), as well as phenomenological models by Dainese (2004) and Eskola (2004). The method enables first-principles lattice studies of real-time phenomena in the quark-gluon plasma that were previously inaccessible, and could be extended to investigate the temperature dependence of \(\hat{q}\) and its dependence on the number of colors.
