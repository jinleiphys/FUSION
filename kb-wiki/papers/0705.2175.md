# Progress in Lattice QCD at finite temperature

## Key claim
Lattice data suggest that at temperatures above 1.5 T_c the quark gluon plasma can be considered as a gas of quarks and gluons, but this conclusion is qualified by the need for more precise lattice data and further perturbative calculations to establish the nature of the plasma in that temperature regime.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD calculations of the transition temperature, equation of state, and static screening lengths using improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, and stout) on lattices with temporal extents N_tau = 4, 6, 8, and 10. The transition temperature is determined from peaks in the chiral susceptibility and Polyakov loop susceptibility, with the lattice spacing set using the Sommer scale r_0. The equation of state is computed via the interaction measure epsilon - 3p, and screening lengths are extracted from spatial correlation functions of Wilson lines and spatial Wilson loops.

## Key numbers
- Transition temperature for 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses: T_c = 192(7)(4) MeV using the p4 action and r_0 = 0.469(7) fm
- Continuum extrapolated value: T_c r_0 = 0.457(7)[+8][-2] for physical pion mass m_pi r_0 = 0.321
- Debye screening mass coefficient: A approximately 1.4 for both N_f = 0 and N_f = 2, representing about 40% correction over leading-order perturbation theory
- Transition temperature from stout action calculations: 176(3)(4) MeV
- Chiral transition temperature from stout action: 151(3)(3) MeV
- Spatial string tension follows sqrt(sigma_s(T)) = c_M g^2(T) T, with c_M agreeing with dimensional reduction results
- String tension from Sommer scale: sqrt(sigma) = 468 MeV, more than 10% larger than the value of 420 MeV used in earlier work by Karsch (2001)

## Context
This review extends lattice QCD calculations of finite-temperature properties beyond earlier work by Boyd (1996) on pure gauge theory and Karsch (2001) on the transition temperature. The results contradict the earlier lower value of T_c = 173(8) MeV obtained using a different string tension value. The work enables comparison with resummed perturbative approaches by Arnold (2003), Braaten, and Blaizot, and tests dimensional reduction as formulated by Kajantie (1997). Open questions include the discrepancy between p4 and stout action results for T_c, the need for calculations on N_tau = 8 lattices with p4 action, and the challenge of calculating transport coefficients on the lattice.
