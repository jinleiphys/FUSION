# Exploration of Resonant Continuum and Giant Resonance in the Relativistic Approach

> Single-particle resonant-states in the continuum are determined by solving scattering states of the Dirac equation with proper asymptotic conditions in the relativistic mean field theory (RMF). The regular and irregular solutions of the Dirac equation at a large radius where the nuclear potentials vanish are relativistic Coulomb wave functions, which are calculated numerically. Energies, widths and wave functions of single-particle resonance states in the continuum for ^{120}Sn are studied in the RMF with the parameter set of NL3. The isoscalar giant octupole resonance of ^{120}Sn is investigated in a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation. Comparing the results with including full continuum states and only those single-particle resonances we find that the contributions from those resonant-states dominate in the nuclear giant resonant processes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the contributions from single-particle resonant-states in the continuum dominate the nuclear giant resonance processes, but this is demonstrated only for the isoscalar giant octupole resonance of \(^{120}\)Sn and the authors note that some strength from non-resonant continuum states is missing in the simplified resonant-state approach.

## Method

Single-particle resonant-states in the continuum were determined by solving scattering states of the Dirac equation with proper asymptotic conditions in the relativistic mean field theory (RMF), using the S-matrix method where a resonance is identified when the phase shift reaches \(\pi/2\). The regular and irregular solutions of the Dirac equation at large radius were relativistic Coulomb wave functions calculated numerically. The isoscalar giant octupole resonance of \(^{120}\)Sn was then investigated in a fully consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) where the continuum was replaced by only the single-particle resonant-states, and results were compared with calculations including all discretized continuum states.

## Key numbers

- Neutron 1h\(_{9/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 0.229 MeV, width \(4.4 \times 10^{-8}\) MeV (very narrow)
- Proton 2f\(_{7/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 6.210 MeV, width 0.043 MeV
- Proton 3p\(_{3/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 7.513 MeV, width 0.924 MeV
- Proton 1i\(_{13/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 10.110 MeV, width 0.012 MeV
- Proton 2g\(_{9/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 14.772 MeV, width 3.502 MeV
- Neutron 1i\(_{11/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 9.751 MeV, width 1.384 MeV
- Neutron 1j\(_{15/2}\) resonance in \(^{120}\)Sn: energy 12.658 MeV, width 1.051 MeV
- Bound-state particle-hole pairs contribute approximately 54% to the energy-integrated unperturbed strength (M\(_0\)) for the isoscalar octupole response
- Neutron particle-hole configurations contribute approximately 67% of M\(_0\)
- Low-lying octupole strength at excitation energy \(\leq 7.5\) MeV is formed entirely from neutron bound-level excitations
- The low-energy octupole resonance (LEOR) lies at 6 to 20 MeV excitation energy; the high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) peaks around 27 to 38 MeV

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic resonant random phase approximation approach of Curutchet et al. (1989) to the relativistic framework, using the S-matrix method in the RMF to identify single-particle resonances. The authors build on prior self-consistent RRPA calculations by Ma et al. (2001) and Ring et al. (2001) that treated the continuum via discretization with harmonic oscillator basis expansion. The results enable a computationally efficient method for describing giant resonances that captures both the Landau width and single-particle escaping width, and the authors note that a complete treatment of the continuum would require the continuum RRPA with a Green function method as described by Wehrberger (1993).
