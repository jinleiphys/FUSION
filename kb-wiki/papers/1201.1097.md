# Evolution of the N=50 gap from Z=30 to Z=38 and extrapolation towards 78Ni

> The evolution of the N=50 gap is analyzed as a function of the occupation of the proton f5/2 and p3/2 orbits. It is based on experimental atomic masses, using three different methods of one or two-neutron separation energies of ground or isomeric states. We show that the effect of correlations, which is maximized at Z=32 could be misleading with respect to the determination of the size of the shell gap, especially when using the method with two-neutron separation energies. From the methods that are the least perturbed by correlations, we estimate the N=50 spherical shell gap in 78Ni. Whether 78Ni would be a rigid spherical or deformed nucleus is discussed in comparison with other nuclei in which similar nucleon-nucleon forces are at play.

## Key claim

The N=50 shell gap in 78Ni is estimated to be about 4.2 MeV when accounting for a "doubly-magic effect" similar to that seen in 48Ca, but the actual structure of 78Ni is uncertain and could be spherical, deformed, or exhibit shape coexistence depending on a delicate balance between the sizes of the Z=28 and N=50 gaps and the amount of quadrupole correlations.

## Method

The authors analyzed the evolution of the N=50 shell gap from Z=30 to Z=38 using three methods based on experimental atomic masses: one-neutron separation energies of N=51 and N=50 isotones, two-neutron separation energies of N=52 and N=50 isotones, and a novel method using the binding energy of the 8+ isomeric state in N=48 isotones to remove correlation effects. Linear fits to the data at Z=30, 34, and 36 were used to extrapolate the gap to Z=28, and the doubly-magic effect was estimated by analogy with the 48Ca system.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron binding energy method gives an extrapolated correlated N=50 gap at Z=28 of 3.44 MeV.
- The slope of the binding energy of the last neutron in N=50 isotones is |V̄^{pn}_{f5/p3-g9}| = 0.60 MeV/Z.
- The slope of the binding energy of the last neutron in N=51 isotones is |V̄^{pn}_{f5/p3-d5}| = 0.48 MeV/Z.
- The BE*_{2n} values from the 8+ isomer method display a slope of -1.19(2) MeV/Z, matching the -1.20(1) MeV/Z slope from the 2BE_{1n} values.
- The reduction of correlation energy at the Z=38 shell closure is 0.17 MeV.
- The averaged doubly-magic effect from the 48Ca system is 0.78 MeV, leading to an estimated N=50 spherical gap in 78Ni of 4.2 MeV.
- The global reduction of the N=50 gap between Z=38 and Z=28 is about 0.55 MeV.
- The 2+_1 excitation energy of N=50 nuclei lies between 1.35 MeV and 1.84 MeV.
- The E(4+_1)/E(2+_1) ratio in N=48 isotones reaches a maximum of about 2.7 at Z=32-34.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of shell gap evolution from Sorlin (2008) by incorporating new high-precision atomic masses from Hakala (2008) and Bachelet (2008) for Ge and Zn isotopes. The authors contradict the interpretation of Hakala (2008) that the N=50 gap is reinforced towards 78Ni, showing instead that the two-neutron separation energy method is strongly perturbed by correlation effects, particularly at Z=32. The results enable a more reliable extrapolation of the N=50 gap to 78Ni and inform predictions about whether this nucleus will resemble the spherical doubly-magic 132Sn or the deformed 42Si, with shape coexistence being a possibility as recently observed in 44S at N=28 by Force (2010).
