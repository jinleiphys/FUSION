# Constraining baryon annihilation in the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions via event-by-event fluctuations

> We point out that the variance of net-baryon distribution normalized by the Skellam distribution baseline, $κ_2[B-\bar{B}]/\langle B+\bar{B}\rangle$, is sensitive to the possible modification of (anti)baryon yields due to $B\bar{B}$ annihilation in the hadronic phase. The corresponding measurements can thus place stringent limits on the magnitude of the $B\bar{B}$ annihilation and its inverse reaction. We perform Monte Carlo simulations of the hadronic phase in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC via the recently developed subensemble sampler + UrQMD afterburner and show that the effect survives in net-proton fluctuations, which are directly accessible experimentally. The available experimental data of the ALICE Collaboration on net-proton fluctuations disfavors a notable suppression of (anti)baryon yields in $B\bar{B}$ annihilations predicted by the present version of UrQMD if only global baryon conservation is incorporated. On the other hand, the annihilations improve the data description when local baryon conservation is imposed. The two effects can be disentangled by measuring $κ_2[B+\bar{B}]/\langle B+\bar{B}\rangle$, which at the LHC is notably suppressed by annihilations but virtually unaffected by baryon number conservation.

## Key claim

The normalized variance of the net-baryon distribution, $\kappa_2[B-\bar{B}]/\langle B+\bar{B}\rangle$, is sensitive to baryon-antibaryon annihilation in the hadronic phase, but the effect can only be disentangled from baryon number conservation if both the net and total (baryon+antibaryon) scaled variances are measured simultaneously; the available ALICE data on net-proton fluctuations are consistent with either global baryon conservation without annihilation or local baryon conservation with annihilation.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the hadronic phase in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV using the subensemble sampler for particlization at T=160 MeV and $\mu_B=0$, followed by the UrQMD afterburner. They ran UrQMD in two configurations: one with standard baryon-antibaryon annihilations and one with these reactions switched off, and additionally considered a "decays only" scenario where resonances decay immediately without rescattering. The simulations incorporated a blast-wave hypersurface with parameters $\beta_s=0.8$, $r_{\rm max}=10$ fm, and $\tau_0=11.6$ fm/c, and included exact global baryon conservation; local baryon conservation was modeled by reducing the space-time rapidity cut-off to $\Delta Y_{\rm cons}=3$.

## Key numbers

- Mean baryon yield suppression factor from annihilation: $\gamma_{B(\bar{B})} = 0.84$ in central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC
- Mean transverse momentum of protons: $\langle p_T \rangle = 1.14$ GeV/$c$ (decays only), $1.32$ GeV/$c$ (afterburner with annihilation), $1.36$ GeV/$c$ (afterburner without annihilation); ALICE data: $\langle p_T \rangle = 1.33 \pm 0.03$ GeV/$c$
- Corrected scaled variance $\kappa_2[B-\bar{B}]/\langle B+\bar{B}\rangle$ at large rapidity acceptance: approximately 0.94 (no annihilation) and 1.12 (with annihilation)
- ALICE net-proton scaled variance: $0.972 \pm 0.015$ in 0-5% central collisions, decreasing to $0.935 \pm 0.011$ in 60-70% central collisions
- Baryon-antibaryon annihilation cross section: $>$60 mb

## Context

This work extends the analysis of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously used primarily to probe the QCD phase structure and critical point (Stephanov 1999, Bzdak 2019), to constrain baryon annihilation in the hadronic phase. It builds on the subensemble sampler approach of Vovchenko (2020) and addresses the thermal proton yield anomaly at the LHC (Abelev 2013) that hadronic transport models like UrQMD (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999) and SMASH (Weil 2016) attribute to baryon annihilation. The results enable experimental discrimination between scenarios with and without baryon regeneration in hadronic afterburners, and suggest that measurements of the scaled variance of total proton+antiproton number can disentangle annihilation effects from baryon number conservation.
