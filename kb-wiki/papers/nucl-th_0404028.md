# Effective Interactions for the Three-Body Problem

> The three-body energy-dependent effective interaction given by the Bloch-Horowitz (BH) equation is evaluated for various shell-model oscillator spaces. The results are applied to the test case of the three-body problem (triton and He3), where it is shown that the interaction reproduces the exact binding energy, regardless of the parameterization (number of oscillator quanta or value of the oscillator parameter b) of the low-energy included space. We demonstrate a non-perturbative technique for summing the excluded-space three-body ladder diagrams, but also show that accurate results can be obtained perturbatively by iterating the two-body ladders. We examine the evolution of the effective two-body and induced three-body terms as b and the size of the included space Lambda are varied, including the case of a single included shell, Lambda hw=0 hw. For typical ranges of b, the induced effective three-body interaction, essential for giving the exact three-body binding, is found to contribute ~10% to the binding energy.

## Key claim

The Bloch-Horowitz (BH) equation for the three-body effective interaction reproduces the exact binding energy of triton and He3 regardless of the oscillator parameterization of the low-energy included space, but only when the two-body ladder diagrams are summed to all orders first; a direct perturbative expansion without this summation fails to converge.

## Method

The authors evaluated the energy-dependent Bloch-Horowitz effective interaction for the three-nucleon system (triton and He3) using the Av18 potential with no bare three-body interaction, working in Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator bases. They developed a Faddeev decomposition that first sums two-body ladder interactions to all orders via a (2+1)-body effective interaction V^{(2+1)}_{12,eff}, then treats the induced three-body interaction perturbatively by iterating this softened interaction. They also developed a non-perturbative method that sums the three-body ladder diagrams exactly through numerical solution of an integral equation.

## Key numbers

- The induced effective three-body interaction contributes approximately 10% to the three-body binding energy for typical ranges of the oscillator parameter b.
- Exact binding energies from Faddeev calculations (Nogga 2002) are accurate to within approximately 20 keV for the Av18 potential with Lambda=60.
- The zeroth-order A=3 results using endshell-corrected states underbind by approximately 3 MeV compared to exact answers.
- For the deuteron, third-order perturbation theory reproduced the binding energy to within approximately one keV for suitably chosen oscillator parameters b (0.4-0.5 fm).

## Context

This work extends earlier two-body effective interaction calculations by Haxton (2002) and the no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations of Navratil (1998, 1999, 2003) to the three-body level, demonstrating that a perturbative expansion for the three-body binding energy becomes viable only after summing two-body ladder interactions to all orders. The method enables future tests of the cluster expansion assumption by applying the technique to He4, and provides a framework for building effective interactions that could be used in shell-model calculations for heavier nuclei where the Hilbert space growth is prohibitive.
