# Conformal Invariance for Non-Relativistic Field Theory

> Momentum space Ward identities are derived for the amputated n-point Green's functions in 3+1 dimensional non-relativistic conformal field theory. For n=4 and 6 the implications for scattering amplitudes (i.e. on-shell amputated Green's functions) are considered. Any scale invariant 2-to-2 scattering amplitude is also conformally invariant. However, conformal invariance imposes constraints on off-shell Green's functions and the three particle scattering amplitude which are not automatically satisfied if they are scale invariant. As an explicit example of a conformally invariant theory we consider non-relativistic particles in the infinite scattering length limit.

## Key claim

Any scale-invariant 2-to-2 scattering amplitude in non-relativistic conformal field theory is automatically conformally invariant, but conformal invariance imposes additional constraints on off-shell Green's functions and on the 3-to-3 scattering amplitude that are not automatically satisfied by scale invariance alone.

## Method

The authors derive momentum-space Ward identities for amputated n-point Green's functions from invariance under the Schrodinger group (Galilean boosts, scale transformations, and conformal transformations) in 3+1 dimensions. They apply these identities to the 4-point and 6-point functions, both off-shell and on-shell, and construct an explicit conformally invariant field theory by taking the infinite scattering length limit of a non-relativistic effective field theory with a four-fermion contact interaction.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave scattering lengths in nuclear physics are \(a^{(^1S_0)} = -23.7 \,{\rm fm}\) and \(a^{(^3S_1)} = 5.4 \,{\rm fm}\), which are large compared to typical nuclear length scales.
- In the infinite scattering length limit, the 2-to-2 scattering amplitude takes the form \({\cal A}^{(4)} = { 8 \pi \over M}{1 \over \sqrt{-4M(E_1+E_2) +(\vec p_1 + \vec p_2)^2 - i\epsilon}}\).
- The fixed point condition for the rescaled coupling is \({\hat C_0} = -1\), corresponding to the limit \(a \rightarrow \pm\infty\).
- For the \(\ell\)th partial wave with \(\ell>0\), the threshold behavior of the phase shift is \(\delta_\ell \sim p^{2\ell+1}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of non-relativistic conformal (Schrodinger) invariance, previously investigated by Hagen (1972), Niederer (1972), and Jackiw and Pi (1990), to momentum-space Ward identities for scattering amplitudes. The authors are motivated by effective field theory approaches to nucleon-nucleon scattering where the large scattering lengths make the system approximately scale invariant, as developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). The results enable systematic study of conformal constraints on few-body nuclear systems, particularly for three-body scattering in channels where no three-body contact operator is needed.
