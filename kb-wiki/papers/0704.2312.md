# Low energy proton-proton scattering in effective field theory

> Low energy proton-proton scattering is studied in pionless effective field theory. Employing the dimensional regularization and MS-bar and power divergence subtraction schemes for loop calculation, we calculate the scattering amplitude in 1S0 channel up to next-to-next-to leading order and fix low-energy constants that appear in the amplitude by effective range parameters. We study regularization scheme and scale dependence in separation of Coulomb interaction from the scattering length and effective range for the S-wave proton-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the strong proton-proton scattering length and effective range up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory, finding that NNLO corrections are small but that the results for the scattering length depend significantly on the renormalization scheme and scale parameter, contrary to the expectation that these quantities should be scheme-independent.

## Method

The authors employed dimensional regularization with both MS-bar and power divergence subtraction (PDS) schemes to calculate the S-wave proton-proton scattering amplitude in the 1S0 channel up to NNLO, including the Coulomb interaction non-perturbatively. Low-energy constants (LECs) appearing in the strong NN interaction were fixed using effective range parameters (a_C, r_0, P) from experimental data. Three different constraints on the NNLO LECs C_4 and tilde-C_4 were considered to check the dependence of results on these choices.

## Key numbers

- Strong scattering length at mu = m_pi: a(mu = m_pi) = -14.9 +/- 0.3 fm (NNLO result with error bars from constraints on C_4 and tilde-C_4)
- Strong effective range at mu = m_pi: r(mu = m_pi) = 2.78 +/- 0.05 fm (NNLO result)
- LO scattering length in MS-bar scheme at mu = m_pi: a_LO^{MS-bar}(mu = m_pi) = -30.72 fm
- LO scattering length in MS scheme at mu = m_pi: a_LO^{MS}(mu = m_pi) = 738.62 fm
- NLO scattering length at mu = m_pi: a(mu) = -14.98 fm (before NNLO corrections)
- NNLO scattering lengths at mu = m_pi for three constraints: -15.11 fm (case 1, tilde-C_4=0), -15.18 fm (case 2, C_4=tilde-C_4), -14.62 fm (case 3, C_4=0)
- Effective range parameters used: a_C = -7.82 fm, r_0 = 2.78 fm, P = 0.022
- Convergence scale from PDS scheme: Lambda_20(mu = m_pi) = 147 MeV (with PDS terms), 30.6 MeV (without PDS terms)

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory treatment of low-energy proton-proton scattering by Kong and Ravndal (1999, 2000), who calculated up to NLO and obtained a(mu = m_pi) = -29.9 fm. The present NNLO calculation shows that higher-order corrections are small but reveals significant renormalization scheme dependence in the LO and NLO results, with the MS scheme giving a_LO = 738.62 fm versus -30.72 fm in the MS-bar scheme. The results are relevant for understanding isospin breaking in S-wave nucleon-nucleon interactions and for determining the strong scattering length a_pp, which has been extracted from potential models (giving -17.1 +/- 0.2 fm) and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (giving -17.51 to -16.96 fm). The authors note that similar scheme-dependence issues have been discussed by Gegelia et al. (2003) and Gegelia (2004).
