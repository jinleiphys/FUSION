# The ratios between the moments of inertia of triaxial nuclei: Comment on the Physical Review C 95, 064315 (2017)

## Key claim
The claim by Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe (2017) that transverse wobbling does not exist is incorrect; stable transverse wobbling does exist, and the assumption of rigid-body ratios between the moments of inertia of the triaxial rotor core contradicts basic concepts of quantal rotation. The authors' conclusion of instability applies only to weakly deformed nuclei such as ¹³⁵Pr, not to strongly deformed systems like ¹⁶³Lu.

## Method
The comment compares three sets of moment-of-inertia ratios for triaxial nuclei: rigid-body values (Eq. 1), irrotational-flow values (Eq. 2), and microscopic values obtained from cranking calculations using the modified oscillator potential (with Woods-Saxon potential giving essentially the same results). The author examines the ordering of the three principal-axis moments of inertia (short, medium, long) as functions of the triaxiality parameter γ, and evaluates the stability of transverse wobbling in the particle-triaxial-rotor model under different moment-of-inertia assumptions.

## Key numbers
- For ¹³⁵Pr, Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe find instability of transverse wobbling for I > 13/2 using irrotational-flow moments of inertia; Frauendorf and Dönau (2014) found instability only for I > 17/2 in the exact particle-rotor solution.
- When the moment of inertia of the short axis is increased to J_s = 0.6 J_m, the instability moves up to I = 29/2, where it is observed in experiment.
- Microscopic cranking calculations are shown for Z=68, N=96 at deformation ε=0.25 (panels a,b); Z=38, N=76 at ε=0.2 (panel c); and Z=68, N=96 at ε=0.4 (panel d), with pairing strengths ranging from Δ_p=1.1 MeV, Δ_n=1.0 MeV to Δ_p=0.3 MeV, Δ_n=0.6 MeV.

## Context
This comment disputes the conclusions of Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe (2017) regarding the nonexistence of transverse wobbling, which was originally suggested by Frauendorf and Dönau (2014). The work extends the experimental evidence for wobbling in ¹⁶³Lu (Od01) and ¹³⁵Pr (Matta15), and references the systematic study of 2⁺₂ states in even-even nuclei by Allmond and Wood (2017) as providing experimental evidence for a γ dependence of moment-of-inertia ratios close to the irrotational-flow values. The comment enables continued use of the transverse wobbling classification for interpreting rotational bands in triaxial nuclei, particularly for strongly deformed systems.
