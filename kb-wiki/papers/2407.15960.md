# The CSS Hamiltonian: high energy evolution of rapidity dependent observables

> We consider evolution of observables which depend on a small but fixed value of longitudinal momentum fraction $x$, to high rapidity, such that $η>\ln 1/x$. We show that this evolution is not given by the JIMWLK (or BK) equation. We derive the evolution Hamiltonian - $H_{CSS-JIMWLK}$ which generates this evolution in the cases of dilute and dense projectile wave function. The two limits yield identical results for $H_{CSS-JIMWLK}$. We show that the resulting evolution for the gluon TMD is identical to the (double logarithmic) perturbative Collins-Soper-Sterman evolution equation in the longitudinal resolution parameter at a fixed and very large transverse resolution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for observables depending on a fixed small longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) when evolved to rapidities \(\eta > \ln 1/x\), the evolution is not governed by the standard JIMWLK (or BK) equation; instead, the authors derive a new Hamiltonian \(H_{CSS-JIMWLK}\) that generates this evolution and is identical in both the dilute and dense projectile limits.

## Method

The authors modify the standard JIMWLK derivation by treating the observable as unchanged during evolution (integrating out soft gluons completely in the Wilsonian sense) rather than allowing the soft gluon charge to contribute to the observable. They expand the resulting master Hamiltonian to second order in \(\tau\) for the dense projectile case and to lowest order in \(j\) for the dilute projectile case, finding both limits yield the same Hamiltonian. The evolution equation is then expressed in operator form as a Kossakowski-Lindblad-type equation in rapidity.

## Key numbers

* The CSS-JIMWLK Hamiltonian is given by \(H_{CSS-JIMWLK} = \frac{g^2}{2\pi} \int_{\mathbf{x}_\perp,\mathbf{y}_\perp} \mathcal{I}^V(\mathbf{x}_\perp) \frac{1}{\partial^2}(\mathbf{x}_\perp,\mathbf{y}_\perp) \mathcal{I}^V(\mathbf{y}_\perp)\), where \(\mathcal{I}^V\) is the difference of left and right color rotation operators.
* The evolution equation for generic operators is \(\frac{d}{d\eta}\langle\eta| \hat{\mathcal{O}} |\eta\rangle = -\frac{g^2}{2\pi} \langle\eta| \int d^2\mathbf{u}_\perp d^2\mathbf{v}_\perp \frac{1}{\partial^2}(\mathbf{u}_\perp,\mathbf{v}_\perp) [\hat{j}^a(\mathbf{u}_\perp),[\hat{j}^a(\mathbf{v}_\perp),\hat{\mathcal{O}}]] |\eta\rangle\).
* The resulting evolution for the gluon TMD is identical to the double logarithmic perturbative Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) evolution equation in the longitudinal resolution parameter at fixed and very large transverse resolution.

## Context

This work extends the JIMWLK/Balitsky-Kovchegov framework (Balitsky 1995, JIMWLK) to observables that are not dominated by the softest gluons in the evolved wave function, such as gluon TMDs at fixed \(x\) and dijet production with small momentum imbalance (dijet). The derivation unifies the dilute and dense projectile limits and shows that the resulting evolution matches the perturbative CSS equation (Collins, Soper, Sterman) for TMD evolution, enabling the study of Sudakov suppression and multi-gluon production with large rapidity differences (Jalilian-Marian 2004, Kovner 2006).
