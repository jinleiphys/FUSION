# Effects of the centrality determination method for the equation of state and nucleonic observables from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.4 GeV

> Centrality determination remains one of the major sources of systematic uncertainty in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collision analyses, especially for probing the nuclear equation of state (EoS) at supra-saturation densities. To quantitatively assess the uncertainties associated with different centrality determination methods and to investigate their effects on final-state EoS-sensitive observables. Within the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.4 GeV are performed within a soft and a hard EoS. Event centrality is determined using the multiplicity of all charged particles ($M_\mathrm{ch}$) and two impact parameter-based centrality filters, one based on a geometrical interpretation and the other based on the Glauber Monte Carlo (MC) model, denoted as $b_{f}$ and $b_{r}$, respectively. It is shown that there exist significant differences between the real impact parameter distributions of event samples selected by $M_\mathrm{ch}$, $b_{f}$, and $b_{r}$, particularly between $M_\mathrm{ch}$ and $b_{r}$. When the $b_{f}$ is employed, uncertainties associated with centrality selection have a weaker influence on observables than the effects induced by the EoS. In contrast, when the $b_{r}$ is used, the influence of centrality-related uncertainties becomes more pronounced than that of the EoS. These results demonstrate that a rigorous and consistent mapping between $M_\mathrm{ch}$ and impact parameter is essential to impose quantitative constraints on the high-density nuclear EoS. Furthermore, our study indicates that the geometrical interpretation of centrality remains valid and consistent with dynamical multiplicity selection, whereas the Glauber MC-based centrality determination becomes unreliable at the investigated energy.

## Key claim

The choice of centrality determination method can introduce systematic uncertainties that either are smaller than or exceed the effects of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) on final-state observables, depending on which method is used. Specifically, the geometrical centrality filter ($b_f$) yields uncertainties weaker than EoS effects, while the Glauber Monte Carlo-based method ($b_r$) produces centrality-related uncertainties that become more pronounced than EoS effects at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.4 GeV.

## Method

Within the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, 5 million Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.4 GeV were simulated using both a soft EoS (incompressibility $K_0$ = 200 MeV) and a hard EoS ($K_0$ = 380 MeV), each with momentum dependence. Event centrality was determined using three methods: charged-particle multiplicity ($M_\mathrm{ch}$), a geometrical impact parameter filter ($b_f$), and a Glauber Monte Carlo-based impact parameter filter ($b_r$). The real impact parameter distributions of event samples selected by each method were compared, and the effects on free proton yield, rapidity distributions, directed flow ($v_1$), elliptic flow ($v_2$), transverse momentum distributions, and mean transverse momentum were analyzed across centrality classes from 0-10% to 30-40%.

## Key numbers

- The soft EoS (SM) uses incompressibility $K_0$ = 200 MeV; the hard EoS (HM) uses $K_0$ = 380 MeV.
- For 0-10% centrality, the $b_f$ filter uses impact parameter $\leq$ 4.26 fm; the $b_r$ filter uses $\leq$ 4.70 fm; $M_\mathrm{ch}$ uses $\geq$ 174 charged particles.
- For 10-20% centrality, approximately 8% of events selected by $M_\mathrm{ch}$ have impact parameters smaller than the $b_f$ lower bound, while about 13% have impact parameters larger than the $b_f$ upper bound.
- For 10-20% centrality, differences between $b_r$ and $M_\mathrm{ch}$ event samples amount to approximately 20% (events with larger $b$) and 0.8% (events with smaller $b$); for 20-30%, these are 41% and 0.1%; for 30-40%, 60% and 0.06%.
- The free proton yield ratio between HM and SM ($b_f$: HM/SM) decreases slowly with increasing centrality; the ratio between $M_\mathrm{ch}$ and $b_r$ (SM: $M_\mathrm{ch}/b_r$) increases significantly with centrality.
- Directed flow slope $v_{11}$ values from HM are larger than those from SM across all centrality classes; $v_{11}$ is insensitive to centrality determination method but sensitive to EoS.
- The $y$-dependent mean transverse momentum $\langle p_\mathrm{T} \rangle$ of free protons is insensitive to both EoS and centrality determination method.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on centrality determination uncertainties in heavy-ion collisions, particularly following Li (2020) which found that true impact parameter distributions from impact parameter filters have considerable width at beam energies below 0.15$A$ GeV. The study directly compares three centrality methods at SIS18 energies, where the Glauber MC model's assumption of proportionality between multiplicity and participant number is questionable, as noted by Omana Kuttan (2023). The results enable more reliable constraints on the high-density nuclear EoS by demonstrating that geometrical centrality determination remains valid at 2.4 GeV, while Glauber MC-based methods become unreliable at this energy, informing future experimental analyses at facilities like HADES and S$\pi$RIT.
