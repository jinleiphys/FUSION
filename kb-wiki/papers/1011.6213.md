# Highly anisotropic hydrodynamics -- discussion of the model assumptions and forms of the initial conditions

> The model assumptions of the recently formulated framework of highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics (ADHYDRO) are analyzed. In particular, we study dependence of numerical results on different forms of the entropy source and compare our approach with other frameworks describing locally anisotropic fluids. We also discuss the effects of different forms of the initial conditions on the process of isotropization.

## Key claim

The ADHYDRO framework's predictions for the isotropization of matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are robust against the specific choice of the entropy source term, with different forms (including those from the Martinez-Strickland model) yielding very similar time evolution of the anisotropy parameter, provided the relaxation times are matched to give the same near-equilibrium behavior.

## Method

The authors solved the ADHYDRO hydrodynamic equations for one-dimensional boost-invariant and non-boost-invariant flows, using three different forms of the entropy source: one based on the difference of the transverse and longitudinal temperature scales (Eq. 1), one based on the difference of the transverse and longitudinal pressures (Eq. 2), and one derived from the Martinez-Strickland kinetic-theory model (Eq. 3). The relaxation times for the two alternative entropy sources were fixed by requiring that their expansions near equilibrium match that of the standard ADHYDRO source. The initial conditions were set at proper time τ₀ = 0.2 fm with an initial anisotropy parameter x₀ = 100, and the time evolution of x, the pressure ratio P∥/P⊥, and the entropy density were computed numerically.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.2 fm; initial anisotropy parameter x₀ = 100.
- Standard relaxation time τ_eq = 0.25 fm, chosen so that the system equilibrates at proper time about 1 fm.
- The relation between relaxation times for the two entropy sources: τ̃_eq = (64/25) τ_eq.
- The relation between the inverse relaxation time Γ in the Martinez-Strickland model and the standard ADHYDRO relaxation time: Γ = 15/(2 τ_eq).
- For non-boost-invariant calculations, initial central energy density ε₀ = 100 GeV/fm³.
- For non-boost-invariant calculations, initial entropy profile parameters: plateau half-width Δη = 1, Gaussian tail half-width δη = 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the ADHYDRO framework introduced by Florkowski and Ryblewski (2010) for modeling the early, highly anisotropic stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where standard viscous hydrodynamics may break down. The authors compare their entropy source choices with the kinetic-theory-based model of Martinez and Strickland (2010), showing that all three forms produce nearly identical evolution for the anisotropy parameter, with only minor differences in the pressure equilibration rate and entropy production. The results enable the use of ADHYDRO as a unified description from the pre-equilibrium stage through to the perfect-fluid stage, and the insensitivity to the entropy source form suggests the framework is robust for phenomenological applications.
