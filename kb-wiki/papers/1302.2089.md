# Properties of trapped neutrons interacting with realistic nuclear Hamiltonians

> We calculate properties of neutron drops in external potentials using both quantum Monte Carlo and no-core full configuration techniques. The properties of the external wells are varied to examine different density profiles. We compare neutron drop results given by a selection of nuclear Hamiltonians, including realistic two-body interactions as well as several three-body forces. We compute a range of properties for the neutron drops: ground-state energies, spin-orbit splittings, excitation energies, radial densities and rms radii. We compare the equations of state for neutron matter for several of these Hamiltonians. Our results can be used as benchmarks to test other many-body techniques, and to constrain properties of energy-density functionals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that *ab initio* calculations of neutron drops using realistic nuclear Hamiltonians can serve as benchmarks for testing other many-body techniques and for constraining energy-density functionals, but the accuracy of the results depends on the specific Hamiltonian and method used, with systematic uncertainties of a few percent between different quantum Monte Carlo methods.

## Method

The authors used Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) for the Argonne AV8' interaction with and without three-nucleon forces (UIX and IL7), and No-Core Full Configuration (NCFC) calculations for the nonlocal JISP16 interaction. Neutron drops were confined in either harmonic oscillator wells with varying strengths (5 MeV and 10 MeV) or a Woods-Saxon potential, and the calculations produced ground-state energies, spin-orbit splittings, excitation energies, radial densities, and rms radii for systems of up to 14 neutrons.

## Key numbers

- For 8 neutrons in a 5 MeV HO well with AV8'+UIX: GFMC total energy = 67.00(1) MeV, AFDMC total energy = 67.1(1) MeV, difference of 0.1%
- For 14 neutrons in a 5 MeV HO well with AV8'+UIX: GFMC total energy = 142.2(2) MeV, AFDMC total energy = 146.7(1) MeV, difference of 3.1%
- For 8 neutrons in a 10 MeV HO well with AV8'+UIX: GFMC total energy = 135.8(0) MeV, AFDMC total energy = 134.7(1) MeV, difference of -0.8%
- For 14 neutrons in a 10 MeV HO well with AV8'+UIX: GFMC total energy = 291.9(2) MeV, AFDMC total energy = 291.2(2) MeV, difference of -0.2%
- The Illinois-7 three-nucleon force reproduces nuclear energies up to A=12 with an rms error of 600 keV
- The difference between AV8' and AV18 binding energies in pure neutron systems is about 0.06 MeV per neutron

## Context

This work extends previous neutron drop calculations by Chang (2004), Pieper (2005), Gandolfi (2006, 2008) and provides systematic comparisons across multiple Hamiltonians and many-body methods. The results can be used to calibrate model effective Hamiltonians and Energy Density Functionals (EDFs) as discussed by Bogner (2011) and Gandolfi (2010), particularly for neutron-rich systems relevant to neutron star crust physics. The paper enables future work with improved microscopic Hamiltonians and many-body methods, and provides benchmarks for testing other many-body techniques.
