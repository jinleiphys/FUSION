# Estimation of attractive and repulsive interactions from the fluctuation observables at RHIC using van der Waals hadron resonance gas Model

> Experimental data on the moments of net-proton distribution in central Au-Au collisions for various center of mass energies ($\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$) measured by the STAR collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are compared to the corresponding results from a van der Waals type interacting hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model. For $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 11.5 and 7.7 GeV there is drop in the strength of both attractive and repulsive interactions relative to $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 19.6 GeV. ## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model studies by Vovchenko (2015) and Samanta (2017), who previously fixed the van der Waals parameters by reproducing nuclear matter properties or fitting lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential. Here, for the first time, the attractive and repulsive parameters are extracted directly from experimental fluctuation data at RHIC beam energies, enabling an estimate of how the relative contributions of these interactions change with collision energy. The results suggest that the higher-order fluctuation observables (SÏƒ and ÎºÏƒÂ²) are more sensitive to attractive interactions, and the enhanced attractive strength near âˆšsNN = 19.6 GeV may be related to critical point physics, motivating further experimental and theoretical investigation.
