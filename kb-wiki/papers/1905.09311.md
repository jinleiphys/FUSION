# Estimation of attractive and repulsive interactions from the fluctuation observables at RHIC using van der Waals hadron resonance gas Model

> Experimental data on the moments of net-proton distribution in central Au-Au collisions for various center of mass energies ($\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$) measured by the STAR collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) are compared to the corresponding results from a van der Waals type interacting hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model. The parameters representing the attractive and repulsive interactions in the VDWHRG model have been extracted by fitting the $σ^{2}$/$M$, $\it{S}σ$ and $κσ^{2}$, where $M$ is the mean, $σ$ is the standard deviation, $\it{S}$ is the skewness and $κ$ the kurtosis of the net-proton distribution. Considering all the three moment products we observe that the strength of the repulsive interactions increases with decrease in $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 200 to 19.6 GeV while the strength of the attractive interaction is of the similar magnitude. For $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 11.5 and 7.7 GeV there is drop in the strength of both attractive and repulsive interactions relative to $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 19.6 GeV. On the other hand, if we consider only the higher order moment products, $\it{S}σ$ and $κσ^{2}$, which are more sensitive to critical point physics, a segregation with respect to the strength of the attractive parameter is observed. The data for $\sqrt{\mathrm {s_{NN}}}$ = 19.6 and 27 GeV supports a larger attractive strength compared to other energies. For this latter case, the repulsive interaction values are of similar order for most of the beam energies studied, except for 7.7 GeV where the parameter value is not well constrained due to large uncertainties.

## Key claim

The van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model can describe the measured net-proton fluctuation observables from the STAR collaboration, but the extracted attractive and repulsive interaction strengths depend on which moment products are used in the fit; when only the higher-order moments (Sσ and κσ²) are used, a larger attractive strength is observed at √sNN = 19.6 and 27 GeV, which may indicate a change in equation of state near those energies.

## Method

The authors fitted the VDWHRG model parameters (attractive parameter a and repulsive hard-sphere radius r) to STAR data on net-proton fluctuation observables (σ²/M, Sσ, κσ²) from central Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. Two parameter sets were extracted: Set-1 used all three moment products, while Set-2 used only Sσ and κσ², which are more sensitive to critical point physics. The chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential at each energy were fixed using parametrized functions from Cleymans (2005), and the experimental acceptance cuts (|y| < 0.5, 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV) were incorporated into the model calculations.

## Key numbers

- For Set-1 (all three moment products), the repulsive interaction strength increases as √sNN decreases from 200 to 19.6 GeV, while the attractive strength is of similar magnitude across those energies; at √sNN = 11.5 and 7.7 GeV, both a and r decrease relative to 19.6 GeV.
- For Set-2 (only Sσ and κσ²), the attractive parameter a at √sNN = 19.6 GeV is double the value obtained from Set-1, indicating higher-order moments are more sensitive to attractive interactions.
- The repulsive parameter r from Set-2 varies approximately within 0.4 to 0.75 fm at all √sNN except 7.7 GeV, where the value is not well constrained due to large uncertainties.
- The VDWHRG model with Set-1 parameters describes σ²/M and Sσ at all energies within uncertainties, and κσ² qualitatively; with Set-2 parameters, it describes Sσ and κσ² at all energies within error bars, and σ²/M at all energies except 200 GeV where the model underestimates the data.

## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model studies by Vovchenko (2015) and Samanta (2017), who previously fixed the van der Waals parameters by reproducing nuclear matter properties or fitting lattice QCD data at zero chemical potential. Here, for the first time, the attractive and repulsive parameters are extracted directly from experimental fluctuation data at RHIC beam energies, enabling an estimate of how the relative contributions of these interactions change with collision energy. The results suggest that the higher-order fluctuation observables (Sσ and κσ²) are more sensitive to attractive interactions, and the enhanced attractive strength near √sNN = 19.6 GeV may be related to critical point physics, motivating further experimental and theoretical investigation.
