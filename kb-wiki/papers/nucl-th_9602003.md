# Exchange Current Corrections to Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering. I. Nuclear Matter

> Relativistic exchange current corrections to the impulse approximation in low and intermediate energy neutrino--nucleus scattering are presented assuming non--vanishing strange quark form factors for constituent nucleons. Two--body exchange current operators which treat {\em all} $SU(3)$ vector and axial currents on an equal footing are constructed by generalizing the soft--pion dominance method of Chemtob and Rho. For charged current reactions, exchange current corrections can reduce the impulse approximation results by 5 to 10 \% depending on the nuclear density. A finite strange quark form factor may change the total cross section for neutral current scattering by 20\% while exchange current corrections are found to be sensitive to the nuclear density. Implications on the current LSND experiment to extract the strange quark axial form factor of the nucleon are discussed.

## Key claim

Exchange current corrections to the impulse approximation in neutrino-nucleus scattering are constructed using a generalized soft-pion dominance method that treats all SU(3) vector and axial currents on an equal footing, but the results are limited to a simple Relativistic Fermi Gas model without binding energy corrections, final state interactions, or other density-dependent nuclear medium effects, in order to isolate the effects of soft-pion meson exchange currents.

## Method

The authors generalized the Chemtob and Rho soft-pion dominance method to construct two-body meson exchange current operators for all SU(3) vector and axial currents, using the soft-pion limit of pion production amplitudes and SU(3) × SU(3) current algebra. These operators were then applied to quasi-elastic electron scattering and neutrino-nucleus scattering in nuclear matter using a Relativistic Fermi Gas model with on-shell nucleons, zero binding energy, and only 1p1h final states, with kinematics relevant to the LSND experiment.

## Key numbers

- For charged current reactions, exchange current corrections can reduce the impulse approximation results by 5 to 10% depending on the nuclear density.
- A finite strange quark form factor may change the total cross section for neutral current scattering by 20%.
- For transverse response in quasi-elastic electron scattering on 12C at |k| = 400 MeV and kF = 225 MeV, soft-pion MEC correction increases the quasi-elastic peak magnitude by about 20% relative to impulse approximation and shifts the peak position to a lower energy transfer by about 20 MeV.
- The vector and axial masses used in form factor parametrizations are MV = 840 MeV and MA = 1030 MeV.
- Fermi momentum of kF = 225 MeV and fixed three momentum transfer of |k| = 400 MeV were used in the RFG model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the soft-pion dominance method of Chemtob and Rho (1971) and the chiral filter hypothesis of Kubodera, Delorme and Rho (1978) to neutrino-nucleus scattering with finite strange quark form factors, building on earlier work by Garvey et al. (1992, 1993) who calculated the proton-to-neutron yield ratio in quasi-elastic neutral current scattering within a non-relativistic RPA framework using impulse approximation. The results are relevant to the LSND experiment at Los Alamos for extracting the strange quark axial form factor of the nucleon, and the authors note that additional nuclear effects (finite nucleus effects, final state interactions, density-dependent medium effects) must be incorporated in a forthcoming paper to make realistic comparisons with experiment.
