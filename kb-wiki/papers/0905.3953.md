# Resonant Spectrum Analysis of the Conductance of Open Quantum System and Three Types of Fano Parameter

> We explain the Fano peak (an asymmetric resonance peak) as an interference effect involving resonant states. We reveal that there are three types of Fano asymmetry according to their origins: the interference between a resonant state and an anti-resonant state, that between a resonant state and a bound state, and that between two resonant states. We show that the last two show the asymmetric energy dependence given by Fano, but the first one shows a slightly different form. In order to show the above, we analytically and microscopically derive a formula where we express the conductance purely in terms of the summation over all discrete eigenstates including resonant states and anti-resonant states, without any background integrals. We thereby obtain microscopic expressions of the Fano parameters that describe the three types of the Fano asymmetry. One of the expressions indicate that the corresponding Fano parameter becomes complex under an external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Fano asymmetry in conductance peaks arises from three distinct types of interference between discrete eigenstates of an open quantum system: interference between a resonant state and an anti-resonant state, between a resonant state and a bound state, and between two resonant states. The last two types follow the standard Fano form, but the first type yields a slightly different squared-Lorentzian form.

## Method

The authors analytically derive a conductance formula for an N-site tight-binding quantum dot coupled to two semi-infinite leads, using the Landauer formula and the Fisher-Lee relation. They express the conductance purely in terms of the sum of retarded and advanced Green's functions, which they expand as a summation over all discrete eigenstates (bound states, anti-bound states, resonant states, and anti-resonant states) without any background integrals. The interference terms in the squared modulus of this sum are then classified into three types based on which pairs of discrete states contribute.

## Key numbers

* The conductance is given by the Landauer formula: G = (2e^2/h) T, where e is the charge unit and h is the Planck constant.
* The lead dispersion relation is E_k = -2t cos k, with band width 4t, where t > 0 is the hopping parameter.
* The matrix Gamma for the contact sites is diagonal with elements (t_alpha^2 / t^2) sqrt(4t^2 - E^2), where t_alpha is the dot-lead coupling for lead alpha.
* The total number of discrete eigenstates for an N-site dot is 2N.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Fano argument (Fano, 1961) by providing a microscopic, analytic derivation of the Fano asymmetry in open quantum systems, using a resonant-state expansion that eliminates background integrals common in prior approaches such as Berggren (1968, 1970). The results enable detection of broad, short-lived resonant states through their influence on the asymmetry of nearby resonant peaks, and provide microscopic expressions for the Fano parameter that can become complex under an external magnetic field (Aharonov-Bohm effect).
