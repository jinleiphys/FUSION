# From Quark Stars to Hybrid Stars

> We show the possible existence of compact stars having a surface composed of a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons. This scenario can be realized both for self-bound stars, satisfying the so-called Witten-Bodmer hypothesis, and for gravitationally bound stars. This class of solutions of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation can be obtained in all the models we discuss, within a physically acceptable range of values of the model parameters.

# From Quark Stars to Hybrid Stars

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that compact stars can have a surface composed of a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons, and that this configuration can exist for both self-bound stars (satisfying the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis) and gravitationally bound stars, depending on the model parameters. The key hedge is that this result depends on using Gibbs conditions rather than the Maxwell construction for the phase transition, and that it only occurs within a specific range of quark model parameters (e.g., bag constant values between approximately 158 and 162 MeV for the MIT bag model with certain parameters).

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using equations of state that incorporate a mixed quark-hadron phase via the Gibbs conditions for chemical and mechanical equilibrium, following the technique developed by Glendenning for systems with two conserved charges (baryon number and electric charge). They combine several hadronic models (GM1, GM2, GM3 from Glendenning-Moszkowsky and B91 from Bodmer) with two quark models (MIT bag model at first order in the strong coupling constant and the Color Dielectric Model), and compute the energy per baryon number, pressure as a function of density, and mass-radius relations for the resulting compact stars.

## Key numbers

- For the GM3 hadronic model with MIT bag model (alpha_s=0, m_s=100 MeV): the second zero of pressure occurs at baryon density rho=0.274 fm^{-3} with quark fraction chi=0.55 for B^{1/4}=160 MeV
- The critical bag constant K_W (boundary between pure quark stars and mixed-phase stars) ranges from 143.9 MeV to 159.2 MeV across all model combinations shown
- The critical bag constant K_H (boundary between mixed-phase stars and normal hybrid stars) ranges from 136.7 MeV to 162.5 MeV across all model combinations shown
- At K_H, the energy per baryon number E/N is consistently 942-944 MeV across all models, with quark fraction chi ranging from 0.13 to 0.33
- For the CDM model with GM1 hadronic model: K_W=21.9 MeV (E/N=932 MeV), K_H=23.2 MeV (E/N=944 MeV, chi=0.19)
- Stars with radius larger than 4 km (or mass greater than approximately 0.1 solar masses) are stable against evaporation into iron nuclei

## Context

This work extends the study of compact stars beyond the traditional dichotomy of pure neutron stars and pure quark stars, showing that a mixed-phase surface is possible for a range of physically acceptable parameters. It builds on the earlier work of Glendenning (1992, 1995) on Gibbs conditions for phase transitions in neutron stars, and on the Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) hypothesis about absolutely stable strange quark matter. The authors note that Schaffner et al. (1997) found a similar second minimum in the energy per baryon for hyperonic matter, and the present work obtains analogous results for the quark-hadron phase transition. The existence of a mixed-phase surface could be relevant for explaining glitches (which require a crystallized phase) and for understanding r-mode instability and cooling of quark stars as discussed by Madsen (1998) and Blaschke et al. (2000).
