# What the collective flow excitation function can tell about the quark-gluon plasma

> Recent STAR data from the RHIC beam energy scan (BES) show that the midrapidity slope dv1/dy of the directed flow v1 of net-protons changes sign twice within the collision energy range 7.7 - 39 GeV. To investigate this phenomenon, we study the collision energy dependence of v1 utilizing a Boltzmann + hydrodynamics hybrid model. Calculations with dynamically evolved initial and final state show no qualitative difference between an equation of state with a cross-over and one with a first-order phase transition, in contrast to earlier pure fluid predictions. Furthermore, our analysis of the elliptic flow v2 shows that pre-equilibrium transport dynamics are partially compensating for the diminished elliptic flow production in the hydrodynamical phase at lower energies, which leads to a qualitative agreement with STAR BES results in midcentral collisions. No compensation from transport is found in our model for integrated v3, which decreases from approx 0.02 at sqrt(sNN) = 27 GeV to approx 0.005 at sqrt(sNN) = 7.7 GeV in midcentral collisions.

## Key claim

The midrapidity slope of directed flow (dv1/dy) of net-protons cannot currently be considered a good signal for a first-order phase transition, because hybrid model calculations with dynamically evolved initial and final states show no qualitative difference between a crossover equation of state and one with a first-order phase transition, contrary to earlier pure fluid dynamical predictions.

## Method

The authors used a (3+1)-D Boltzmann + hydrodynamics hybrid model (UrQMD transport for initial and final stages, SHASTA algorithm for ideal hydrodynamics in the intermediate stage) to simulate Au+Au collisions across the RHIC beam energy scan range (sqrt(sNN) = 5-200 GeV). They compared two equations of state: a chiral model with a crossover transition and a Bag model with a first-order phase transition, using both isochronous and iso-energy density (epsilon_C = 2epsilon_0 or 4epsilon_0) switching criteria between hydrodynamics and the transport afterburner.

## Key numbers

- Integrated v3 in midcentral collisions (b = 6.7-8.2 fm) decreases from approximately 0.02 at sqrt(sNN) = 27 GeV to approximately 0.005 at sqrt(sNN) = 7.7 GeV.
- Integrated v3 in most central collisions (b = 0-3.4 fm) increases from approximately 0.01 to above 0.015 with increasing collision energy.
- In midcentral collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 5 GeV, integrated v3 reaches approximately zero.
- The critical energy density for particlization was set to epsilon_C = n*epsilon_0, where epsilon_0 = 146 MeV/fm^3, with n=2 or n=4 used in different calculations.
- The hybrid model overestimates the experimental dv1/dy data across the entire examined collision energy range (7.7-39 GeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier pure fluid dynamical predictions (Rischke 1995, Csernai 1999, Brachmann 1999, Stoecker 2004) that directed flow slope sign changes would signal a first-order phase transition, and directly addresses the STAR beam energy scan results (Adamczyk 2014) showing double sign changes in dv1/dy of net-protons. The hybrid model approach (Petersen 2008) provides a more realistic framework than pure hydrodynamics by including pre-equilibrium transport dynamics, which the authors show partially compensate for diminished elliptic flow production at lower energies. The finding that v3 is a clearer signal of low-viscous QGP formation than v1 or v2 enables future experimental focus on triangular flow measurements at lower beam energies.
