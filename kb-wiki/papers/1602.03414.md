# Charm production in high multiplicity pp events

> Recent experimental studies of the multiplicity dependence of heavy quark (HQ) production in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV showed a strong non-linear increase of the HQ multiplicity as a function of the charged particle multiplicity. We try to understand this behavior using the EPOS3 approach. Two issues play an important role: multiple scattering, in particular its impact on multiplicity fluctuations, and the collective hydrodynamic expansion. The results are very robust with respect to many details of the modeling, which means that these data contain valuable information about very basic features of the reaction mechanism in proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

The strong non-linear increase of D meson multiplicity relative to charged particle multiplicity in high-multiplicity pp collisions at 7 TeV can be explained by the combination of multiple scattering (Pomeron number fluctuations) and collective hydrodynamic expansion, where the charged particle multiplicity is reduced by flow rather than charm production being enhanced.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS3 model, which combines a Gribov-Regge multiple scattering approach (Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Theory) with a core-corona separation, viscous hydrodynamic expansion (eta/s = 0.08), statistical hadronization via Cooper-Frye, and a final-state hadronic cascade (UrQMD). Heavy quarks are produced during the initial stage in the parton ladders using masses m_c = 1.3 GeV and m_b = 4.2 GeV, with no interactions between heavy quarks and the fluid. The authors compared EPOS basic (without hydro) to full EPOS (with hydro) and to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- D meson multiplicity versus charged particle multiplicity shows a more-than-linear increase, with the effect larger for higher p_t ranges: 1-2, 2-4, 4-8, and 8-12 GeV/c (the N_D1, N_D2, N_D4, N_D8 bins).
- The charm quark mass used is m_c = 1.3 GeV; the bottom quark mass used is m_b = 4.2 GeV.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used in the hydrodynamic evolution is eta/s = 0.08.
- The D meson and charged particle multiplicities are measured at central rapidities (|y| <= 0.5 for D mesons, |eta| <= 1 for charged particles), with Vzero multiplicity covering 2.8 < eta < 5.1 and -3.7 < eta < -1.7.
- The non-linear increase is considerably reduced when using Vzero multiplicity (large rapidities) compared to central multiplicity, in both data and simulation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of ALICE (2015) on heavy quark production in pp collisions, which showed that PYTHIA 8.157 simulations (even with multiple scattering) produced results close to the diagonal (linear) and far from the observed non-linear data. The authors also reference a percolation approach that predicts faster-than-linear increase but without p_t dependence. The EPOS3 results demonstrate that the combination of Pomeron number fluctuations and hydrodynamic flow (which reduces charged particle multiplicity) can reproduce the observed trend, and that the effect is robust to many modeling details, providing insight into the basic reaction mechanism of pp collisions.
