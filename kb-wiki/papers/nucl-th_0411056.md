# Collective excitations in the inner crust of neutron stars : supergiant resonances

> We investigate the nuclear collective excitations of Wigner-Seitz cells containing nuclear clusters immersed in a gas of neutrons. This baryonic non-uniform system is specific to the structure of inner crust matter of neutron stars. The collective excitations are studied in the framework of a spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov + Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation, formulated in coordinate representation. The calculations are done for two representative Wigner-Seitz cells with baryonic density equal to 0.02 fm$^{-3}$ and 0.08 fm$^{-3}$. It is shown that the excitations with low multipolarities are concentrated almost entirely in one strongly collective mode which exhausts a very large fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule. Since these collective modes are located at very low energies compared to the giant resonances in standard nuclei, they may affect significantly the specific heat of baryonic inner crust matter of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the residual interaction between quasiparticles in Wigner-Seitz cells of neutron star inner crust matter generates "supergiant resonances", extremely collective, low-energy modes that can significantly affect the specific heat of baryonic inner crust matter, but this claim is conditional on the need for future finite-temperature HFB+QRPA calculations to provide a quantitative estimation of these effects.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the ground states of two representative Wigner-Seitz cells, using the Skyrme interaction SLy4 in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent contact force for pairing. They then constructed the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) Green function in coordinate representation, including all quasiparticle states up to 60 MeV, and calculated the collective response for quadrupole, dipole, and monopole excitations. The residual interaction between quasiparticles was derived from the second derivative of the HFB energy functional, with the Landau-Migdal approximation applied for velocity-dependent terms of the Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the cell \(^{1800}\)Sn (50 protons, 1750 neutrons; cell radius 27.6 fm; neutron gas density 0.018 fm\(^{-3}\)): the quadrupole mode is located at about 3 MeV, exhausts about 70% of the energy-weighted sum rule, collects more than 99% of the total quadrupole strength, and has a reduced transition probability B(E2) equal to 25 × 10\(^3\) Weisskopf units.
- For the cell \(^{982}\)Ge (32 protons, 950 neutrons; cell radius 14.4 fm; neutron gas density 0.074 fm\(^{-3}\)): the lowest quadrupole mode appears at 1.8 MeV.
- The baryonic densities of the two representative cells are 0.02 fm\(^{-3}\) and 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The coherence length of the neutron gas superfluid in the \(^{1800}\)Sn cell is about 3 fm.
- The energy smoothing factor used in the QRPA Green function calculation is 150 keV.
- The radial mesh step for solving HFB and QRPA equations is 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superfluid properties and specific heat in neutron star inner crust matter, which had considered only non-interacting quasiparticle states (e.g., Barranco et al. 1998, Pizzochero et al. 2002, Sandulescu et al. 2004). The authors note that no microscopic study of nuclear collective modes in inner crust matter existed prior to this work. The results enable future finite-temperature HFB+QRPA calculations to quantitatively assess the impact of these supergiant resonances on the entropy and specific heat of baryonic inner crust matter, which could significantly affect models of neutron star cooling.
