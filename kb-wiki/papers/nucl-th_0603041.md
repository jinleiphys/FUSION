# Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon Model I. Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering (ESC04) Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon Model I. Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering (ESC04)

> The NN results are presented from the extended-soft-core (ESC) interactions. They cosist of local- and non-local-potentials due to (i) one-boson-exchange (OBE), pseudo-scalar-, vector-, scalar-, and axial-vector-nonets, (ii) diffractive exchanges, (iii) two-pseudoscalar-exchange, and (iv) meson-pair-exchange (MPE). Fits to the pp- and np-data for 0 < T(lab) < 350 MeV have typical χ^2/Ndata= 1.15. Used are less than 20 parameters, which are coupling constants and cut-off masses. The couplings follow rather closely the pattern predicted by the quark-pair-creation model. As a result the 11 OBE-couplings are rather constrained, i.e. quasi-free. The deuteron binding energy and several NN scattering lengths are fitted at the same time.

## Key claim

The ESC04 model achieves a fit to pp- and np-data with a typical χ²/Ndata = 1.155 using fewer than 20 quasi-free parameters, where the 11 OBE-coupling constants are constrained to follow the pattern predicted by the ³P₀ quark-pair-creation model rather than being completely free.

## Method

The authors constructed local and non-local nucleon-nucleon potentials from one-boson-exchange (OBE) of pseudoscalar, vector, scalar, and axial-vector nonets, diffractive exchanges, two-pseudoscalar-exchange, and meson-pair-exchange (MPE). They solved the Thompson equation derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, using Gaussian form factors with cut-off masses around 1 GeV, and introduced a zero in the scalar meson form factor and axial-vector meson exchange as innovations over earlier soft-core models.

## Key numbers

- χ²/Ndata = 1.155 for fits to pp- and np-data in the laboratory energy range 0 < Tlab ≤ 350 MeV
- Fewer than 20 quasi-free physical parameters (coupling constants and cut-off masses) were used
- 11 OBE-coupling constants were constrained by the ³P₀ quark-pair-creation model
- Pseudoscalar mixing angle θP = -23.0° from the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula
- Vector meson ideal mixing angle θV = 37.56°
- Axial-vector mixing angle θA = 47.3°
- Cut-off masses Λ ≈ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier Nijmegen soft-core OBE models (NRS78, MRS89) and the previous ESC-model (Rij93, RS96a, RS96b, SR97, RPN02) by introducing a zero in the scalar meson form factor and incorporating axial-vector meson exchange for the first time. The ESC04 model enables simultaneous fitting of NN and YN data (reported in companion papers II and III) and tests flavor SU(3)-symmetry in baryon-baryon interactions, with the constrained couplings providing a bridge to the quark-pair-creation model of Mic69, Yao73, and Yao75.
