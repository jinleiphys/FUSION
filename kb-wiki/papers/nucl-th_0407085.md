# Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics with Coherent State Pion and Its Application to Excited Spectrum of 12C

> We have introduced coherent state neutral pion into Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics. With the aid of coherent state technique, it becomes possible to calculate transition matrix elements of the pion field operator and to study excited states containing pions. For large pion-nucleon coupling fpiN > 1.6, pions have a finite expectation value and bring large energy gain in 12C. We discuss two aspects of pionic effects in spectroscopy; the LS interaction like effect and the mixing of different nucleon parity states, which would modify low energy nuclear levels.

# Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics with Coherent State Pion and Its Application to Excited Spectrum of 12C

## Key claim

For pion-nucleon coupling constants fπN ≳ 1.6, neutral pions acquire a finite expectation value in 12C and produce large energy gains, but the authors note that this threshold is obtained without isospin projection (which would enhance tensor effects by roughly a factor of three), so the effective coupling used is larger than the physical value to compensate.

## Method

The authors introduce a coherent state neutral pion field into the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) framework, representing the total wave function as a product of a nucleon Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets and a pion coherent state. The Hamiltonian includes the pion kinetic energy, mass term, and the axial-vector P-wave pion-nucleon coupling; matrix elements are evaluated using the coherent state technique, and the intrinsic state is obtained by cooling variation, followed by angular momentum and parity projection after variation (PAV). The pion eigenfunction is expanded in local Gaussians, and the nucleon-nucleon interaction is a modified Brink-Boeker type (BBOπ) chosen to reproduce the ground state and first excited state energies of 12C.

## Key numbers

- For fπN ≳ 1.6, pions have a finite expectation value and bring large energy gain in 12C; the specific value used in calculations is fπN = 1.63 (with a scaled coupling fπN(A) = √((A-1)/A) fπN to approximately include Fock exchange effects).
- The total pionic energy amounts to around -90 MeV for fπN = 1.63 in the intrinsic state.
- At the projected energy minima, the nucleonic abnormal parity probability reaches about 5% for the 0⁻ state and about 3% for the 1⁺ state.
- The ground state (0⁺) total energy is -92.4 MeV with BBOπ interaction, with a pionic energy component of -58.2 MeV.
- The 2₁⁺ excitation energy is 4.6 MeV with pions included (BBOπ), compared to 4.2 MeV with Volkov interaction and strong LS force.
- The 0⁻ state excitation energy is 27.0 MeV, and the 1⁺ state is at 17.0 MeV, with pionic energy components of -89.1 MeV and -89.6 MeV respectively.
- The pion number expectation value nπ at the energy minima is around 0.05 to 0.1 for natural parity states, and larger for unnatural parity states.

## Context

This work extends the study of explicit pionic degrees of freedom in nuclei beyond mean-field treatments such as the relativistic mean field framework of Toki (2002), which demonstrated neutral pion condensation in nuclear surfaces, and the Charge and Parity Projected Hartree-Fock (CPPHF) method of Sugimoto (2004), which showed that charge projection enhances tensor contribution by about three times in 4He. The AMD with coherent state pion enables, for the first time, calculation of excitation energies for specified Jπ with explicit pion degrees of freedom through parity and angular momentum projection, allowing comparison with cluster model descriptions of 12C where the 2₁⁺ state is typically too low. The results suggest that pionic effects act as an LS-like interaction and mix different nucleon parity states, which could be tested experimentally via pion knock-out reactions leaving the nucleus in unnatural parity states.
