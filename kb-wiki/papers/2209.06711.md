# Probing $ŒûN$ interaction through inversion of spin-doublets in $ŒûN Œ±Œ±$ nuclei

> A new way to study the spin-isospin dependence of the $ŒûN$ interaction is explored through the energy levels of $ŒûNŒ±$ and $ŒûNŒ±Œ±$ systems with $Œ±$ being a spectator to attract the $ ŒûN$ pair without changing its spin-isospin structure. By using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with the state-of-the-art $ŒûN$ potential obtained from lattice QCD calculations, it is found that $ŒûNŒ±Œ±$ has spin-doublet bound states with $J^œÄ={1^-}$ and $2^-$ in both isospin triplet and singlet channels. The inversion of the $1^-$-$2^-$ spin-doublet between the iso-triplet and the iso-singlet is found to be strongly correlated with the relative strengths of the $ŒûN$ interaction in the $^{11}{\rm S}_0, ^{13}{\rm S}_1,^{31}{\rm S}_0$ and $^{33}{\rm S}_1$ channels. The $(K^-, K^+)$ and $(K^-,K^0)$ reactions on the $^{10}$B target are proposed to produce those bound states.

## Method

The authors performed three- and four-body cluster model calculations using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for the ŒûNŒ± and ŒûNŒ±Œ± systems, with Œ± clusters treated as spin-isospin saturated spectators. They employed the HAL QCD ŒûN potential from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses, using the single-channel effective potential for each spin-isospin channel, and solved the Schr√∂dinger equation via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with Gaussian basis functions arranged in geometric progressions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light Œû hypernuclei by Hiyama (2008) using Nijmegen potentials and Hiyama (2020) on ŒûNN and ŒûNNN systems with the HAL QCD potential, where a shallow bound state was found in the ŒûNNN system. The proposed method using Œ± clusters as spectators enables direct access to the spin-isospin decomposition of the ŒûN interaction, which has been difficult to extract from femtoscopic analyses by the ALICE Collaboration or from emulsion data.
