# Probing $ΞN$ interaction through inversion of spin-doublets in $ΞN αα$ nuclei

> A new way to study the spin-isospin dependence of the $ΞN$ interaction is explored through the energy levels of $ΞNα$ and $ΞNαα$ systems with $α$ being a spectator to attract the $ ΞN$ pair without changing its spin-isospin structure. By using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with the state-of-the-art $ΞN$ potential obtained from lattice QCD calculations, it is found that $ΞNαα$ has spin-doublet bound states with $J^π={1^-}$ and $2^-$ in both isospin triplet and singlet channels. The inversion of the $1^-$-$2^-$ spin-doublet between the iso-triplet and the iso-singlet is found to be strongly correlated with the relative strengths of the $ΞN$ interaction in the $^{11}{\rm S}_0, ^{13}{\rm S}_1,^{31}{\rm S}_0$ and $^{33}{\rm S}_1$ channels. The $(K^-, K^+)$ and $(K^-,K^0)$ reactions on the $^{10}$B target are proposed to produce those bound states.

## Key claim

The inversion of the 1^- and 2^- spin-doublet ordering between the T=1 and T=0 isospin channels of the ΞNαα system is strongly correlated with the relative strengths of the ΞN interaction in the ¹¹S₀, ¹³S₁, ³¹S₀, and ³³S₁ channels, providing a new way to probe the spin-isospin dependence of the ΞN interaction. The authors find bound states using the HAL QCD potential, but note that the existence and ordering depend on the specific lattice QCD time slice (t/a=11,12,13) used to extract the potential.

## Method

The authors performed three- and four-body cluster model calculations using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for the ΞNα and ΞNαα systems, with α clusters treated as spin-isospin saturated spectators. They employed the HAL QCD ΞN potential from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses, using the single-channel effective potential for each spin-isospin channel, and solved the Schrödinger equation via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with Gaussian basis functions arranged in geometric progressions.

- Binding energy of Ξα (⁵ΞH) with respect to breakup: 0.64, 0.45, 0.25 MeV for t/a=11,12,13 respectively, with width Γ ~ 5 keV
- Binding energy of Ξ⁻αα (T=1/2, J^π=1/2^+): 2.08^{+0.77}_{-0.63} MeV
- For ΞNαα T=1, J^π=1^-: E = -4.50^{+1.04}_{-0.80} MeV relative to four-body breakup, Γ = 0.02^{+0.01}_{-0.01} MeV
- For ΞNαα T=1, J^π=2^-: E = -4.70^{+1.09}_{-0.83} MeV, Γ = 0.02^{+0.01}_{-0.00} MeV
- For ΞNαα T=1, J^π=3^-: E = -2.47^{+1.09}_{-0.84} MeV, Γ = 0.02^{+0.01}_{-0.00} MeV
- For ΞNαα T=0, J^π=1^-: E = -4.31^{+1.28}_{-1.04} MeV, Γ = 0.04^{+0.01}_{-0.01} MeV
- For ΞNαα T=0, J^π=2^-: E = -3.26^{+1.10}_{-0.90} MeV, Γ = 0.03^{+0.00}_{-0.01} MeV

- Binding energy of ⁹Be (T=1/2, J^π=3/2^-): 1.57 MeV (experimental)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light Ξ hypernuclei by Hiyama (2008) using Nijmegen potentials and Hiyama (2020) on ΞNN and ΞNNN systems with the HAL QCD potential, where a shallow bound state was found in the ΞNNN system. The proposed method using α clusters as spectators enables direct access to the spin-isospin decomposition of the ΞN interaction, which has been difficult to extract from femtoscopic analyses by the ALICE Collaboration or from emulsion data. The results enable future experiments at J-PARC using (K⁻,K⁺) and (K⁻,K⁰) reactions on ¹⁰B targets to produce these bound states and extract the relative strengths of the ΞN interaction in different spin-isospin channels.
