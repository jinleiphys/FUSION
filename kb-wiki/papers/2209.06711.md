# Probing $őěN$ interaction through inversion of spin-doublets in $őěN őĪőĪ$ nuclei

> A new way to study the spin-isospin dependence of the $őěN$ interaction is explored through the energy levels of $őěNőĪ$ and $őěNőĪőĪ$ systems with $őĪ$ being a spectator to attract the $ őěN$ pair without changing its spin-isospin structure. By using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with the state-of-the-art $őěN$ potential obtained from lattice QCD calculations, it is found that $őěNőĪőĪ$ has spin-doublet bound states with $J^ŌÄ={1^-}$ and $2^-$ in both isospin triplet and singlet channels. The inversion of the $1^-$-$2^-$ spin-doublet between the iso-triplet and the iso-singlet is found to be strongly correlated with the relative strengths of the $őěN$ interaction in the $^{11}{\rm S}_0, ^{13}{\rm S}_1,^{31}{\rm S}_0$ and $^{33}{\rm S}_1$ channels. The $(K^-, K^+)$ and $(K^-,K^0)$ reactions on the $^{10}$B target are proposed to produce those bound states.

## Key claim

The inversion of the 1^- and 2^- spin-doublet ordering between the T=1 and T=0 isospin channels of the őěNőĪőĪ system is strongly correlated with the relative strengths of the őěN interaction in the ¬Ļ¬ĻS‚āÄ, ¬Ļ¬≥S‚āĀ, ¬≥¬ĻS‚āÄ, and ¬≥¬≥S‚āĀ channels, providing a new way to probe the spin-isospin dependence of the őěN interaction. The authors find bound states using the HAL QCD potential, but note that the existence and ordering depend on the specific lattice QCD time slice (t/a=11,12,13) used to extract the potential.

## Method

The authors performed three- and four-body cluster model calculations using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for the őěNőĪ and őěNőĪőĪ systems, with őĪ clusters treated as spin-isospin saturated spectators. They employed the HAL QCD őěN potential from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical quark masses, using the single-channel effective potential for each spin-isospin channel, and solved the Schr√∂dinger equation via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with Gaussian basis functions arranged in geometric progressions.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light őě hypernuclei by Hiyama (2008) using Nijmegen potentials and Hiyama (2020) on őěNN and őěNNN systems with the HAL QCD potential, where a shallow bound state was found in the őěNNN system. The proposed method using őĪ clusters as spectators enables direct access to the spin-isospin decomposition of the őěN interaction, which has been difficult to extract from femtoscopic analyses by the ALICE Collaboration or from emulsion data. The results enable future experiments at J-PARC using (K‚ĀĽ,K‚Āļ) and (K‚ĀĽ,K‚Āį) reactions on ¬Ļ‚ĀįB targets to produce these bound states and extract the relative strengths of the őěN interaction in different spin-isospin channels.
