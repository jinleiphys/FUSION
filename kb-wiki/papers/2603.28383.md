# Study of radiative proton capture by the 7Be nucleus with the use of ab initio approaches

> A theoretical study of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction in the astrophysical energy range with the use of ab initio methods is presented. The used approaches are No-Core Shell Model and Cluster Channels Orthogonal Functions Method. The scheme also contains elements of R-matrix theory and procedures for extrapolating various data obtained in ab initio computations. The developed approach as a whole allows one not only to calculate the astrophysical S-factor and all nuclear characteristics that determine its value, but also to evaluate the reliability of the obtained results and to identify the dominant reaction mechanisms against a background of insignificant ones.The high accuracy of the obtained results and has been demonstrated.

# Study of radiative proton capture by the 7Be nucleus with the use of ab initio approaches

## Key claim

The authors present a combined ab initio approach (No-Core Shell Model plus Cluster Channels Orthogonal Functions Method) that can calculate the astrophysical S-factor for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction and evaluate the reliability of the results, but the accuracy is limited by the Daejeon16 NN-potential imperfections and the need to use experimental resonance energies because NCSM calculations converge only to approximately 100 keV even after extrapolation.

## Method

The authors performed No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations of 7Be and 8B wave functions using the Daejeon16 NN-potential derived from Chiral Effective Field Theory at N3LO, with basis cut-off parameters N*max from 4 to 12 and oscillator parameters hbar-omega from 10 to 25 MeV. They then applied the Cluster Channels Orthogonal Functions Method (CCOFM) to compute cluster form factors, asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs), and reduced partial width amplitudes for the 7Be + p decay channels of 8B states. These quantities were combined with NCSM-computed electromagnetic transition probabilities and used as input to the AZURE2 R-matrix code to calculate the cross-section and astrophysical S-factor, with experimental resonance energies substituted for theoretical ones to improve accuracy.

## Key numbers

* Total binding energy of 8B ground state (2+): extrapolated value -38.116 ± 0.118 MeV, with experimental value -37.737 MeV
* Total binding energy of 7Be ground state (3/2-): theoretical -37.965 MeV, experimental -37.600 MeV
* Width of M1 transition 1_1+ → 2_1+: (2.86 ± 0.006) × 10^-2 eV, in agreement with experimental (2.52 ± 0.11) × 10^-2 eV
* Width of M1 transition 3_1+ → 2_1+: 0.10 ± 0.0036 eV, in agreement with experimental 0.1 ± 0.05 eV
* Total squared ANC of 2_1+ state: 0.602 fm^-1, compared to literature values 0.711 ± 0.092 fm^-1 and 0.452 fm^-1
* Total width of 1_1+ resonance: 43.6 keV (theoretical) vs 35.6 ± 0.6 keV (experimental)
* Width of 3_1+ resonance: 850 keV (theoretical) vs 350 ± 30 keV (experimental)
* Reduced probability B(1_1+ → 2_1+): 3.7675 ± 0.0025 μN^2

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio studies of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, particularly the NCSMC calculations by the authors of NCSMC (2019) which gave S17(0) = 19.8 ± 0.3 eV·Bn for N4LO and 21.0 eV·Bn for N3LO interactions, and the GSM-CC calculations using effective interactions with inert 4He core which gave S17(0) = 23.2 eV·Bn. The present approach differs by using CCOFM to compute ANCs and reduced partial width amplitudes directly from NCSM wave functions, enabling systematic evaluation of convergence and reliability. The method enables future application to other radiative capture reactions of astrophysical interest where experimental data at low energies are unavailable.
