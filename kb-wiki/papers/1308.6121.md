# A new equation of state for neutron star matter with nuclei in the crust and hyperons in the core

> The equation of state for neutron stars in a wide-density range at zero temperature is constructed. The chiral quark-meson coupling model within relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation is adopted for uniform nuclear matter. The coupling constants are determined so as to reproduce the experimental data of atomic nuclei and hypernuclei. In the crust region, nuclei are taken into account within the Thomas-Fermi calculation. All octet baryons are considered in the core region, while only $Ξ^{-}$ appears in neutron stars. The resultant maximum mass of neutron stars is $1.95M_\odot$, which is consistent with the constraint from the recently observed massive pulsar, PSR J1614-2230.

# A new equation of state for neutron star matter with nuclei in the crust and hyperons in the core

## Key claim

The chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) model within relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, with coupling constants calibrated to reproduce nuclear mass data via Thomas-Fermi calculations, yields a neutron star maximum mass of 1.95 solar masses that lies within the 1-sigma limit of the PSR J1614-2230 mass constraint, but only when hyperons are included and the crust is treated with nonuniform matter distributions.

## Method

The authors adopt the chiral quark-meson coupling model within relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation for uniform nuclear matter, including all octet baryons in the core. Coupling constants for nucleons are determined by fitting to the binding energies of 2226 nuclei using Thomas-Fermi calculations, while hyperon coupling constants are fixed from experimental hyperon potential depths in symmetric nuclear matter. For the crust region, nonuniform matter is treated with a Thomas-Fermi calculation in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells, assuming a bcc lattice and parameterized nucleon density profiles.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: 1.95 solar masses, consistent with the 1-sigma limit of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses)
- Neutron drip density: 2.70 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (4.48 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³ in baryon mass density)
- Transition density from nonuniform to uniform matter: 6.85 × 10⁻² fm⁻³ (1.14 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ in baryon mass density)
- Saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter: w₀ = -16.1 MeV
- Root-mean-square deviation of calculated nuclear masses from experimental data: 2.93 MeV for 2226 nuclei
- Symmetry energy at saturation: consistent with the range 30 MeV to 34 MeV suggested by intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions
- Slope of symmetry energy L: 77.1 MeV
- Hyperon potential depths used: U_Λ = -30 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -15 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density

## Context

This work extends the KMS12 model (Katayama, Miyatsu, and Saito, 2012) by recalibrating coupling constants against nuclear mass data using Thomas-Fermi calculations, which the original KMS12 model failed to reproduce (root-mean-square deviation of 36.33 MeV). The resulting equation of state addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the softening of the EOS when hyperons are included, by using the exchange (Fock) term in RHF theory to stiffen the EOS, unlike earlier RMF models by Shen (1998) and Ishizuka (2008) that could not reach two-solar-mass neutron stars with hyperons. The EOS is intended for use in astrophysical simulations of neutron stars, supernovae, and binary neutron star mergers, with future extension to finite temperatures planned.
