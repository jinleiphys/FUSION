# Microscopic Dynamics of Shape Coexistence Phenomena around 68Se and 72Kr

> The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method is applied to the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P + Q) model Hamiltonian including the quadrupole pairing, and the oblate-prolate shape coexistence phenomena in proton-rich nuclei, 68Se and 72Kr, are investigated. It is shown that the collective path connecting the oblate and prolate local minima runs along a triaxial valley in the beta-gamma plane. Quantum collective Hamiltonian is constructed and low-lying energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities are calculated for the first time using the ASCC method. Basic properties of the shape coexistence/mixing are well reproduced. We also clarify the effects of the time-odd pair field on the collective mass (inertial function) for the large-amplitude vibration and on the rotational moments of inertia about three principal axes.

# Microscopic Dynamics of Shape Coexistence Phenomena around 68Se and 72Kr

## Key claim
The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, applied to a pairing-plus-quadrupole model Hamiltonian including quadrupole pairing, reproduces the basic patterns of oblate-prolate shape coexistence in 68Se and 72Kr, but the quantitative agreement for E2 transition probabilities is limited because the theoretical B(E2) values are normalized to a single experimental datum using a polarization charge.

## Method
The ASCC method was used to extract a one-dimensional collective path connecting the oblate and prolate local minima in the (β,γ) plane from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) manifold. A quantum collective Hamiltonian was constructed including vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom, and the collective Schrödinger equation was solved to obtain excitation energies and wavefunctions. Three rotational moments of inertia were evaluated by extending the QRPA equations for rotation to general HFB states on the collective path.

## Key numbers
- For 72Kr, the experimental B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) value is 1000 e² fm⁴, which was used to set the polarization charge e_pol = 0.881
- The collective mass M(q) and three rotational moments of inertia J_i(q) are enhanced by the time-odd pair field generated by the quadrupole pairing interaction
- The calculation yields an excited prolate rotational band and an oblate ground-state band for both 68Se and 72Kr
- The oblate-prolate shape mixing decreases as angular momentum increases for both nuclei

## Context
This work extends the previous application of the ASCC method to 68Se and 72Kr by Kobayashi et al. (2005), which successfully extracted the one-dimensional collective path but did not calculate energy spectra or transition probabilities. The present calculation is the first to compute low-lying energy spectra and E2 transition probabilities using the ASCC method. The time-odd pair field effect on the collective mass, which is ignored in the cranking mass of Ring and Schuck (1980), was previously investigated for particle-hole residual interactions by Dobaczewski (1981) but not for pairing interactions until this work. The results enable future microscopic studies of shape coexistence dynamics in other proton-rich nuclei and suggest the existence of an excited 0⁺ state in 68Se not yet found experimentally.
