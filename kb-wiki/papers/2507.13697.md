# The equation of state and surface tension of QCD in the first order phase transition region

> We build up a complete description of QCD phase structure by applying the parametrization of the chiral and deconfinement order parameters upon the calculations from functional QCD approaches. In particular in the first order phase transition region at high chemical potential, both the phase transition line using Maxwell construction and the coexistence boundary lines from the spinodal decompostion are determined. We compute the thermodynamic quantities including the number density, the energy density, the pressure and also the free energy for both stable and unstable phases of QCD. Additionally, after applying a phenomenological description of the inhomogeneity of the QCD free energy, we obtain the surface tension of the first order phase transition of QCD.

# The equation of state and surface tension of QCD in the first order phase transition region

## Key claim
The authors provide a complete description of QCD thermodynamics in the first order phase transition region at high chemical potential by parametrizing the chiral and deconfinement order parameters based on functional QCD calculations, but the surface tension results depend on a phenomenological description of inhomogeneity and a chosen interface thickness parameter of 0.33 fm, rather than being derived from first principles alone.

## Method
The authors parametrize the chiral condensate (quark mass M_q) using a 3D Ising-type potential with a phi^6 term and the Polyakov loop using a fit to functional Renormalization Group results at zero chemical potential, then insert these parametrized order parameters into a simplified quark propagator to compute the number density analytically. The pressure is obtained by integrating the number density along the trajectory of the multi-valued function, and the Maxwell construction is applied to determine the phase transition line. The surface tension is computed by introducing a phenomenological inhomogeneous free energy density with a gradient term, using a chosen interface thickness parameter a = 0.33 fm and the baryon number density and energy density at the critical end point.

## Key numbers
- Critical end point location: (T^CEP, mu_B^CEP) = (106, 600) MeV
- Zero-temperature chiral phase transition occurs at mu_B = 930 MeV
- Zero-temperature chiral crossover parameters: T_c(0) = 155 MeV, kappa = 0.016, kappa_2 = 3.5 x 10^{-4}
- Quark mass scale M_0 = 350 MeV chosen as the typical mass scale for light-flavor quarks
- Baryon number density at CEP: n_B^CEP = 0.19 fm^{-3} (derived from n_q^CEP/3)
- Energy density at CEP: E_g = 0.572 GeV x fm^{-3}
- Interface thickness parameter: a = 0.33 fm
- Polyakov loop fit parameters: l_1 = 2.732, m_1 = 0.5495, m_2 = 1.831
- Ising potential parameter lambda = 1 adopted for the coexistence region calculation

## Context
This work extends the Ising parametrization approach of Parotto (2018) and Karthein (2024) by incorporating the parametrized order parameters into the quark propagator to compute thermodynamic quantities, rather than mapping thermodynamic quantities directly. It builds on functional QCD calculations from Fu (2019) and Gao (2020) that provided the chiral phase transition line and CEP location, and uses lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2020) and the HotQCD collaboration (2018) for the zero-chemical-potential pressure. The surface tension results enable phenomenological studies of QCD first order phase transitions in heavy-ion collision hydrodynamics simulations and cosmological studies of gravitational wave signals from the cosmic QCD transition.
