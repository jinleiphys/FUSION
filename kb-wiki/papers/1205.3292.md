# Relation between baryon number fluctuations and experimentally observed proton number fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We explore the relation between proton and nucleon number fluctuations in the final state in relativistic heavy ion collisions. It is shown that the correlations between the isospins of nucleons in the final state are almost negligible over a wide range of collision energy. This leads to a factorization of the distribution function of the proton, neutron, and their antiparticles in the final state with binomial distribution functions. Using the factorization, we derive formulas to determine nucleon number cumulants, which are not direct experimental observables, from proton number fluctuations, which are experimentally observable in event-by-event analyses. With a simple treatment for strange baryons, the nucleon number cumulants are further promoted to the baryon number ones. Experimental determination of the baryon number cumulants makes it possible to compare various theoretical studies on them directly with experiments. Effects of nonzero isospin density on this formula are addressed quantitatively. It is shown that the effects are well suppressed over a wide energy range.

## Key claim

The authors show that, for collision energies √sNN ≳ 10 GeV, the isospins of nucleons in the final state are randomized by Nπ reactions via Δ(1232) resonances, leading to a factorization of the joint proton-neutron distribution into a nucleon distribution times binomial functions. This factorization allows baryon number cumulants (not directly observable) to be reconstructed from experimentally measured proton number cumulants, with the caveat that the approximation breaks down at lower beam energies where pion production is insufficient.

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas relating baryon number cumulants to proton number cumulants using the factorization property that, given N nucleons in a phase space, the number of protons follows a binomial distribution with probability r = ⟨Np⟩/⟨NN⟩. They estimated the mean time for a nucleon to undergo charge-exchange reactions via Δ resonances using the Breit-Wigner form for the cross section (mΔ = 1232 MeV, Γ = 110 MeV, σΔ = 20 fm²) and the Bose distribution for thermal pions. The analysis was extended to include nonzero isospin density and the contribution from strange baryons (Λ, Σ) decaying into protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Mean time τΔ for a nucleon at rest to form Δ⁺ or Δ⁰ in the hadronic medium: τΔ = 3–4 fm for T = 150–170 MeV
- Lifetime of Δ resonances: τΓ = 1/Γ ≈ 1.8 fm
- Ratio of probabilities that a proton in isospin-symmetric medium forms Δ⁺ or Δ⁰ and decays into a proton versus a neutron: Pp→p : Pp→n = 5:4
- Ratio of probabilities that a neutron in isospin-symmetric medium forms Δ⁺ or Δ⁰ and decays into a neutron versus a proton: Pn→n : Pn→p = 5:4
- Branching ratio for Λ decay: PΛ→p : PΛ→n ≈ 16:9
- Branching ratio for Σ⁺ decay: PΣ⁺→p : PΣ⁺→n ≈ 13:12
- Production ratio of protons to neutrons from Λ and Σ decays: PΛ,Σ→p : PΛ,Σ→n ≈ 9:11
- Beam energy threshold for validity of factorization: √sNN ≳ 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier discussion in Kitazawa (2011) from isospin-symmetric media to cases with nonzero isospin density, and provides detailed derivations omitted in that prior paper. The results enable direct comparison of experimental proton number fluctuation measurements from the RHIC energy scan program (Aggarwal 2010, Mohanty 2011) with theoretical predictions for baryon number cumulants from lattice QCD (Gavai 2010, Schmidt 2010, Mukherjee 2011, Borsanyi 2011, Bazavov 2012) and other theoretical studies (Asakawa 2000, Jeon 2000, Ejiri 2005, Stephanov 2011). The approach relies on the large pion density relative to nucleons at RHIC energies and the rapid charge-exchange reactions via Δ resonances, conditions that break down at lower beam energies where Tchem ≲ mπ.
