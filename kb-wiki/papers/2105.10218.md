# Sensitivity study of \emph{r}-process abundances to nuclear masses

> The impact of nuclear mass uncertainties on the \emph{r}-process abundances has been systematically studied with the classical \emph{r}-process model by varying the mass of every individual nucleus in the range of $\pm0.1$ to $\pm3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}$ based on six different mass models. A new quantitative relation between the uncertainties of \emph{r}-process abundances and those of the nuclear masses is extracted, i.e., a mass uncertainty of $\pm0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$ would lead to an abundance uncertainty of a factor around 2.5. It is found that this conclusion holds true for various mass models.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a quantitative relation between nuclear mass uncertainties and r-process abundance uncertainties, finding that a mass uncertainty of ±0.5 MeV leads to an abundance uncertainty of a factor around 2.5, and this relation holds across six different mass models. The hedge is that this conclusion is derived from a classical (site-independent) r-process model that neglects neutron freeze-out dynamics and fission recycling.

## Method

The authors used a classical r-process model with 16 neutron flows (neutron densities from 10^20 to 10^27.5 cm^-3) at temperature T = 1.5 GK, fitting astrophysical parameters to reproduce Solar r-process abundances. They performed sensitivity studies by varying every individual nuclear mass by ±0.1 to ±3.0 MeV across six mass models (WS4, ETFSI-Q, FRDM2012, RMF, HFB31, and DZ31), using the relation 1/T_1/2 ∝ Q^5 to adjust β-decay half-lives consistently with mass variations.

## Key numbers

- A mass uncertainty of ±0.5 MeV leads to an abundance uncertainty factor of about 2.5 (range 10^0.38 ≈ 2.40 to 10^0.45 ≈ 2.82 across different mass models)
- For each 0.5 MeV increase in mass uncertainty, the average abundance variance W increases by 0.38 to 0.45
- Mass variations were studied from ±0.1 MeV to ±3.0 MeV
- The rms deviations between theoretical mass models and experimental data reach about 0.5 MeV, reduced to about 0.2 MeV by machine learning approaches
- Abundance deviations ΔY for non-RMF mass models have similar values around 0.3, despite rms mass deviations ranging from 0.30 MeV to 0.81 MeV

## Context

This work extends sensitivity studies by Mumpower (2015) that examined mass variations of ±0.1 and ±0.5 MeV using HFB17, DZ33, FRDM1995, and FRDM2012 mass models, finding that ±0.5 MeV uncertainties significantly impact abundances while ±0.1 MeV may resolve details. The present study systematically covers larger mass variations (up to ±3.0 MeV) across six mass models, finding that the rare-earth peak around A = 160 is entirely covered by the uncertainty band at ±0.5 MeV, meaning this feature cannot be used to constrain the r-process site at current mass uncertainties. The results enable future experimental and theoretical work to identify which mass measurements would most reduce abundance uncertainties.
