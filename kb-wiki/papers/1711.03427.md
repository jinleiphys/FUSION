# Formation of $Σπ$ pairs in nuclear captures of K$^-$ mesons

> The capture of K$^-$ mesons on nucleons bound in nuclei offer a chance to study the $Σπ$ pairs below the kinematic threshold of the $\bar{\mathrm{K}}$N systems. Various hyperon-pion charged combination are presently under investigation by AMADEUS. These data allow to test both isospin 0 and 1 amplitudes giving the possibility to detect the structure of resonant $Λ$(1405) state. Contrasted against similar electro-production data, it is possible to detect changes of $Λ$(1405) in nuclear media. Expected spectra and their uncertainties are calculated.

# Formation of Σπ pairs in nuclear captures of K⁻ mesons

## Key claim
The invariant mass distributions of Σπ pairs produced in K⁻ captures on nuclei can reveal the structure of the Λ(1405) resonance below the K̅N threshold, but the extraction is complicated by a sharp phase-space cutoff at 1416 MeV for atomic captures; the ratio R±(MΣπ) shows an anomaly 35 MeV below the K⁻p threshold that appears to be nucleus-related, as electro-production data on free protons show a smooth energy dependence.

## Method
The authors model K⁻ captures on protons bound in ¹²C using a zero-range transition operator, factorizing the amplitude into an isospin-dependent transition amplitude T(MΣπ) and a nuclear form-factor Fᵖ(PΣπ) that incorporates the initial proton and kaon wave functions and final-state hyperon interactions. The form-factor is averaged over atomic and nuclear magnetic orientations and summed over protons, using an oscillator model for the nucleus and Coulomb atomic wave functions for the kaon, with the dominant capture occurring from atomic l=2 states. The invariant mass distribution P(MΣπ) is then calculated as |T(MΣπ)|² times the squared form-factor times the phase-space factor ρ.

## Key numbers
- Kinematic limit on achievable invariant mass for atomic capture: 1416 MeV
- For in-flight captures with kaon momenta of ~120 MeV, the upper limit is pushed up by ~14 MeV
- Binding energy of 2p proton orbital: 16 MeV; 1s nucleon orbital separation energy: ~30-50 MeV
- Valence protons contribute 90% of the capture rate
- Absolute rate of radial l=3 → l=2 atomic transition in carbon: 0.36(6); rate of subsequent radial transition: 0.028(8)
- The anomaly in R±(MΣπ) appears 35 MeV below the K⁻p threshold

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of Σπ emission following K⁻ captures in nuclear emulsion and bubble chambers (e.g., emulsion work by various authors, FINUDA collaboration measurements on light nuclei) and is part of the AMADEUS program investigating K⁻ captures on ¹²C and ⁴He. The ratio R±(MΣπ) was previously studied by Keane (thesis) and analyzed by Staro (data reported there), with a suggested three-quark component in Λ(1405) by Dalitz. Recent electro-production experiments on protons (Jefferson Lab) show R± to be a smooth function of energy, indicating the anomaly is nucleus-related. The authors also note a low-momentum peak in Σ⁺ momentum distributions observed by AMADEUS in carbon but not in beryllium, interpreted elsewhere as a Gamov state formation. The present work provides the "gross structure" of the invariant mass spectra, while the hyperon momentum distribution is treated in a parallel work.
