# Low-density neutron matter

> The properties of low-density neutron matter are important for the understanding of neutron star crusts and the exterior of large neutron-rich nuclei. We examine various properties of dilute neutron matter using quantum Monte Carlo methods, with s- and p-wave terms in the interaction. Our results provide a smooth evolution of the equation of state and pairing gap from extremely small densities, where analytic expressions are available, up to the strongly interacting regime probed experimentally and described theoretically in cold atomic systems, where the Fermi momentum is approximately half an inverse fermi and the pairing gap becomes of the order of magnitude of the Fermi energy. We also present results for the momentum distribution and pair distributions, displaying the same evolution from weak to strong coupling. Combined with previous quantum Monte Carlo and other calculations at moderate densities, these results provide strong constraints on the neutron matter equation of state up to saturation densities.

# Low-density neutron matter

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that quantum Monte Carlo calculations of low-density neutron matter, including both s-wave and p-wave interactions, provide a smooth evolution of the equation of state and pairing gap from extremely dilute regimes where analytic expressions hold up to the strongly interacting regime at k_F ≈ 0.5 fm⁻¹, but the pairing gap results are significantly larger than most diagrammatic and renormalization group approaches, suggesting those methods may not describe the strongly paired regime accurately.

## Method
The authors used Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods with a Jastrow-BCS trial wave function for 66-68 neutrons in periodic boundary conditions. They employed the AV4 potential including both s-wave and p-wave interactions, with the s-wave part used in the propagator for opposite-spin pairs and p-wave contributions added perturbatively for same-spin pairs. The pairing gap was extracted from odd-even staggering of ground-state energies, and the quasiparticle excitation spectrum, momentum distribution, and pair distribution functions were also calculated.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length a ≈ -18.5 fm, effective range r_e ≈ 2.7 fm, giving |r_e / a| ≈ 0.15
- At the highest density examined (k_F a = -10, k_F ≈ 0.54 fm⁻¹), the p-wave interaction increases the energy by approximately 7% compared to s-wave only; at k_F a = -5 the increase is only 1%
- The pairing gap at k_F a = -1 is suppressed by approximately a factor of two from the BCS value, roughly consistent with the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov polarization suppression factor of 1/(4e)^(1/3) ≈ 0.45
- At k_F a = -10, the AV4 pairing gap is 1.05 (0.11) MeV, with a perturbative increase of 0.16 (0.06) MeV from the s-wave result
- At k_F a = -5 (k_F = 0.27 fm⁻¹), the AV4 pairing gap is 0.48 (0.04) MeV, with a perturbative increase of 0.012 (0.008) MeV from the s-wave result
- At k_F a = -7.5 (k_F = 0.40 fm⁻¹), the AV4 pairing gap is 0.77 (0.08) MeV, with a perturbative increase of 0.11 (0.03) MeV from the s-wave result
- QMC AV4 energy at k_F a = -10 with optimized wave function: 0.5866(6) MeV; with CBF input wave function: 0.6254(9) MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous comparison of cold atoms with neutron matter (Gezerlis and Carlson, 2008) by including p-wave interactions and calculating additional properties like quasiparticle spectra and pair distributions. It provides a bridge between analytic Lee-Yang results (Lee and Yang, 1957) at extremely low densities and previous quantum Monte Carlo calculations at moderate densities (Carlson and Morales, 2003; Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998). The results enable improved constraints on density-functional theories of neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star crusts, and the comparison with cold-atom experiments (where the effective range is nearly zero) helps isolate the impact of finite effective range in neutron matter. The findings disagree with several diagrammatic approaches (Chen et al., 1993; Wambach et al., 1993; Schulze et al., 1996) and renormalization group calculations (Schwenk et al., 2003), while showing better agreement with Brueckner calculations including self-energy corrections (Cao et al., 2006).
