# Existence of One-Body Barrier Revealed in Deep Sub-Barrier Fusion

> Based on the adiabatic picture for heavy-ion reactions, in which the neck formation in the one-body system is taken into account, we propose a two-step model for fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies. This model consists of the capture process in the two-body potential pocket, which is followed by the penetration of the adiabatic one-body potential to reach a compound state after the touching configuration. We describe the former process with the coupled-channels framework, while the latter with the WKB approximation by taking into account the coordinate dependent inertia mass. The effect of the one-body barrier is important at incident energies below the potential energy at the touching configuration. We show that this model well accounts for the steep fall-off phenomenon of fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni and $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni reactions.

# Existence of One-Body Barrier Revealed in Deep Sub-Barrier Fusion

## Key claim

The steep fall-off of fusion cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies for medium-heavy systems such as 64Ni+64Ni and 58Ni+58Ni can be explained by an adiabatic two-step model in which, after the two nuclei touch, the system must penetrate an inner one-body barrier formed by the adiabatic potential with neck formation, but this one-body barrier only becomes important at incident energies below the potential energy at the touching configuration.

## Method

The authors propose a two-step model that separates the fusion process into capture through the two-body potential (described with coupled-channels calculations including inelastic excitations) followed by penetration of the adiabatic one-body potential (described with the WKB approximation using coordinate-dependent inertia mass). The one-body potential is constructed using the Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS) model with Lemniscatoids shape parametrization for the neck configuration after touching, and the inertia mass is taken as a linear combination of the irrotational-flow mass and the reduced mass with a normalization factor k=46.

## Key numbers

- The potential energy at the touching configuration V_touch for 64Ni+64Ni is 88.61 MeV, which coincides with the energy E_s where experimental fusion cross sections begin to fall off abruptly.
- The KNS model parameters used are a_0=0.68 fm, a_s=21.33 MeV, kappa_s=2.378 from FRLDM2002, with radius parameter r_0=1.204 fm fine-tuned to fit experimental fusion cross sections at high incident energies.
- The normalization factor for the inertia mass is k=46, which leads to a vibrational energy of 0.2 hbar*omega_0.
- The IWBC is imposed at R_IWBC=8.0 fm with a potential depth V_0=70 MeV for the sharp-cut KNS potential.
- For the 16O+197Au system, the estimated V_touch is 68.23 MeV using r_0=1.2 fm in the KNS potential.

## Context

This work extends the debate between sudden and adiabatic pictures for heavy-ion fusion at deep sub-barrier energies, directly contrasting with the sudden model of Misicu and Esbensen (2006, 2007) which uses a shallow potential based on the frozen density approximation with a phenomenological repulsive core. The authors note that while both models reproduce the steep fall-off for 64Ni+64Ni, they predict different average angular momenta of the compound nucleus, suggesting that experimental measurement of this quantity could discriminate between the approaches. The paper also references the coupled-channels approach of Das and collaborators (1998) which succeeded at energies near the Coulomb barrier, and notes that the exponential energy dependence observed for 16O+208Pb by Dasgupta and collaborators (2006) contrasts with the medium-heavy systems studied here, motivating future application of the two-step model to mass-asymmetric systems.
