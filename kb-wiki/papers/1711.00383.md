# Heavy quarkonia in a contact interaction and an algebraic model: mass spectrum, decay constants, charge radii and elastic and transition form factors

> For the flavor-singlet heavy quark system of bottomonia, we compute the masses of the ground state mesons in four different channels, namely, pseudo-scalar ($η_{b}(1S)$), vector ($Υ(1S)$), scalar ($χ_{b_0}(1P)$) and axial vector ($χ_{b_{1}}(1P)$). We also calculate the weak decay constants of the $η_{b}(1S)$ and $Υ(1S)$ as well as the charge radius of $η_{b}(1S)$. It complements our previous study of the corresponding charmonia systems: $η_c(1S)$, $J/Ψ(1S)$, $χ_{c_0}(1P)$) and ($χ_{c_{1}}(1P)$). The unified formalism for this analysis is provided by a symmetry-preserving Schwinger-Dyson equations treatment of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction. Whenever a comparison is possible, our results are in fairly good agreement with experimental data and model calculations based upon Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations involving sophisticated interaction kernels. Within the same framework, we also report the elastic and transition form factors to two photons for the pseudo-scalar channels $η_{c}(1S)$ and $η_{b}(1S)$ in addition to the elastic form factors for the vector mesons $J/Ψ$ and $Υ$ for a wide range of photon momentum transfer squared ($Q^2$). For $η_{c}(1S)$ and $η_{b}(1S)$, we also provide predictions of an algebraic model which correlates remarkably well between the known infrared and ultraviolet limits of these form factors.

## Key claim

The paper extends a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (CI) model, previously applied to charmonia, to bottomonia and reports mass spectra, decay constants, charge radii, and form factors; the authors note that while the CI model reproduces mass spectra within 3% of experiment, the decay constants for vector mesons show deviations up to approximately 50% compared to sophisticated SDE calculations, and the form factors are "harder than we expect from the proper treatment of full QCD with a running quark mass function."

## Method

The authors employ a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations with a momentum-independent vector×vector contact interaction, regularized via proper-time regularization to preserve the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. They solve the gap equation for dressed quark propagators and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound-state amplitudes, then compute decay constants, elastic form factors, and transition form factors using the canonically normalized amplitudes. For the pseudoscalar channels, they also construct an algebraic model (AM) that interpolates between known infrared and ultraviolet limits of the form factors.

## Key numbers

- Bottomonia masses from the CI model: m_ηb(1S) = 9.345 GeV, m_Υ(1S) = 9.460 GeV, m_χb0(1P) = 9.594 GeV, m_χb1(1P) = 9.603 GeV; experimental values are 9.32 (9.4), 9.46, 9.860, and 9.892 GeV respectively.
- CI model decay constant for ηb(1S): f_ηb = 0.553 GeV; for Υ(1S): f_Υ = 0.219 GeV (experimental f_Υ = 0.506 GeV).
- CI model parameters for bottomonia: α_IR = 0.023, Λ_UV = 6.4 GeV, current b-quark mass m_b = 3.8 GeV, dynamically generated mass M_b = 4.7 GeV.
- The dimensionless coupling α (defined as α_IR Λ_UV^2 / m_g^2) decreases from 3.739 for light quarks to 1.496 for bottom quarks, a reduction factor of about 0.400 relative to the light sector.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CI model study of charmonia (Bedolla 2015, Bedolla 2016) to the bottomonium sector, complementing calculations by Jain (1993), Blank (2011), Fischer (2014), and Ding (2015) that used more sophisticated SDE kernels. The results enable comparison of a simple contact interaction against both experimental data and lattice QCD results (e.g., McNeile 2012, Colquhoun 2014) for heavy quarkonia, while the algebraic model provides a computationally efficient alternative for form factor calculations that mimics full QCD behavior.
