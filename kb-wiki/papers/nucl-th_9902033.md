# Phases of dense matter in neutron stars

> Recent equations of state for dense nuclear matter are discussed with possible phase transitions arising in neutron stars such as pion, kaon and hyperon kondensation, superfluidity and quark matter. Specifically, we treat the nuclear to quark matter phase transition, the possible mixed phase and its structure. A number of numerical calculations of rotating neutron stars with and without phase transitions are given and compared to observed masses, radii, temperatures and glitches.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how various phase transitions (pion, kaon, hyperon condensation, superfluidity, and quark matter) may occur in neutron star interiors, but emphasizes that the existence and structure of a mixed phase of nuclear and quark matter depend on surface and Coulomb energies, and that such mixed phases are energetically favorable only for reasonable bulk and interface properties.

## Method

The authors survey multiple theoretical approaches to the equation of state (EoS) for dense neutron star matter, including Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions (Nijmegen, Argonne V18, CD-Bonn), relativistic mean-field models, and the Bag model for quark matter. They impose conditions of beta-equilibrium, charge neutrality, and local neutrality for quark matter, then compute global neutron star properties (mass, radius, moment of inertia) for both non-rotating and rotating stars using general relativistic stellar structure equations.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 ± 0.02 fm⁻³, with binding energy B/A = -15.6 ± 0.2 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
* Neutron drip density: ρd ≈ 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³
* Observed binary neutron star masses lie in the narrow interval 1.3–1.5 M⊙
* Mass of Vela X-1: M = (1.9 ± 0.1) M⊙; mass of Cygnus X-2: M = (1.8 ± 0.2) M⊙
* Symmetry energy at saturation density: range 27–38 MeV
* Radio pulsar periods: 0.033 s ≤ P ≤ 4.0 s; millisecond pulsar periods: 1.56 ms ≤ P ≤ 100 ms
* Magnetic fields of radio pulsars: B ∼ 10¹¹–10¹³ G; millisecond pulsars: B ∼ 10⁸–10¹⁰ G
* Surface temperature of a thermally radiating neutron star: T ≈ 60 eV, with deduced radius R ≤ 14 km

## Context

This review extends earlier work on neutron star structure by Tolman, Oppenheimer, Volkoff (1939) and Wheeler (1960), and builds on the mixed-phase concept introduced by Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg, Pethick, and Staubo (1993). It connects microscopic EoS calculations to observables such as masses, radii, glitches, and cooling rates, and discusses how future observations of quasi-periodic oscillations and gravitational microlensing may constrain the EoS and possible phase transitions in neutron star cores.
