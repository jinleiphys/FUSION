# Isotropization by QCD Plasma Instabilities

> Numerical solutions of the Wong-Yang-Mills equations with anisotropic particle momentum distributions are presented. Their isotropization by collective effects due to the classical Yang-Mills field is shown.

# Isotropization by QCD Plasma Instabilities

## Key claim
Numerical solutions of the Wong-Yang-Mills equations show that anisotropic particle momentum distributions can be isotropized by collective effects from classical Yang-Mills fields, but this isotropization occurs via two distinct regimes: rapid exponential growth of longitudinal pressure from plasma instabilities when the separation between hard and soft modes is large, or direct isotropization by strong fields without pronounced instabilities when that separation is small.

## Method
The authors solve Wong's equations for test particles coupled to classical SU(2) Yang-Mills fields in 1+1 dimensions (fields depend on time and one spatial coordinate, while particles propagate in three spatial dimensions), using an update algorithm closely related to Hu and Muller (1997). The initial anisotropic phase-space distribution of hard gluons is taken as a quasi-thermal distribution in two transverse dimensions with a delta function in longitudinal momentum, representing free-streaming after nuclear collision. Initial field amplitudes are sampled from a Gaussian distribution with width tuned to a given initial energy density, with zero gauge links and electric field polarized transverse to the x-axis at t=0.

## Key numbers
- For the weak-field case: initial hard scale p_hard = 10 GeV, initial field energy density ≈ 10^{-2} GeV/fm^3/g^2, producing rapid exponential growth of longitudinal pressure and approach to isotropy via plasma instabilities.
- For the strong-field case: initial hard scale p_hard = 1 GeV, initial field energy density ≈ 10^{-1} GeV/fm^3/g^2, with lattice size L = 10 fm setting the time scale, where strong instabilities are not seen but the system still approaches isotropy.
- For the weak-field case at t/L/N_c = 0.5, the initial delta-function in longitudinal momentum becomes a thermal distribution extending over several orders of magnitude, though the longitudinal direction remains much colder than transverse directions.

## Context
This work extends the numerical approach of Dumitru and Nara (2005) by presenting additional results including modification of particle spectra, and builds on the hard-loop effective theory simulations of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2005) which showed that instabilities grow more slowly once field strength becomes large. The results are relevant to the "bottom-up scenario" for thermalization of the Color Glass Condensate proposed by Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which would be modified significantly by QCD plasma instabilities as argued by Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003). Future work requires full 3D simulations, inclusion of longitudinal expansion, and addition of collisions with momentum transfer above that mediated by the classical field.
