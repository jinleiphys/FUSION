# Center vortices in the novel phase of staggered fermions

> The geometry of center vortices is studied in the novel lattice-artefact phase that appears with staggered fermions to elucidate any insight provided by the center-vortex degrees of freedom. For various numbers of fermion flavors, the single-site shift symmetry of the staggered-fermion action is broken in a finite region of the $(β, m)$ phase space. Simulations are performed with six degenerate fermion flavors and a range of $β$ values that span the phase boundary. Center vortices are demonstrated to capture the broken shift symmetry that manifests in the unphysical phase. This persists at the level of each individual plaquette orientation, where it is revealed that only the plaquettes that span the broken dimension are affected. Several bulk center-vortex quantities, including the vortex and branching point densities, are considered to highlight other aspects of vortex geometry sensitive to the unphysical phase. A slight preference for the plaquettes affected by the broken shift symmetry to be pierced by a vortex is observed. This translates also to a greater branching point density in three-dimensional slices that span the broken dimension. Combined, these findings provide a novel characterization of the unphysical phase in terms of the fundamental center degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Center vortices are shown to capture the broken single-site shift symmetry that characterizes the unphysical lattice-artefact phase of staggered fermions, with the effect persisting at the level of individual plaquette orientations where only plaquettes spanning the broken dimension are affected. The authors note that the asymmetry between even and odd plaquettes is only about 10%, making it unlikely to be discernible in visualizations.

## Method

The authors generate lattice ensembles with six degenerate flavors of staggered fermions at bare mass m = 0.015 and beta values from 2.5 to 2.9, using symmetric 16^4 and 24^4 volumes with two steps of stout smearing and the tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action. They fix to maximal center gauge and project links onto Z_3 to extract thin center vortices, then compute the order parameter Delta_mu P (the difference between adjacent plaquettes in a given dimension) on both untouched and vortex-only configurations. They further decompose the order parameter by individual plaquette orientation and compute vortex densities, branching point densities, and chain length distributions to characterize the vortex geometry.

## Key numbers

- The order parameter (Delta_mu P)^2 on vortex-only fields is approximately an order of magnitude larger than on untouched configurations across the beta range studied.
- At beta = 2.55, the ratio R_z of odd to even plaquettes pierced by vortices reaches a maximum of approximately 1.1 within a 30-configuration window, indicating a 10% asymmetry.
- The maximum ratio on any single configuration was approximately 1.15.
- Bootstrap statistical uncertainties on vortex density are of order 10^(-4), too small to resolve at the plotted scale.
- The vortex density shows a gentle increase toward lower beta values, with no rapid change at the phase boundary.

## Context

This work extends studies of the unphysical lattice-artefact phase in staggered fermion simulations first noted by Lee (1999) and Aubin (2004), with concrete demonstrations of the phase by Cheng (2011), Hasenfratz (2021), and Kotov (2021). The authors build on extensive prior work showing that center vortices underpin confinement and chiral symmetry breaking (Del Debbio 1996, Langfeld 1997, Engelhardt 1998, Greensite 2003, among many others), and on their own recent analysis of flavor dependence in the conformal window (Mickley 2025). The results enable a novel characterization of the unphysical phase in terms of fundamental center degrees of freedom, though the small magnitude of the even-odd asymmetry (approximately 10%) suggests visual identification of the broken symmetry in vortex visualizations is unlikely.
