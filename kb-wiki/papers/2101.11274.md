# Nucleon-pair coupling scheme in Elliott's SU(3) model

> Elliott's SU(3) model is at the basis of the shell-model description of rotational motion in atomic nuclei. We demonstrate that SU(3) symmetry can be realized in a truncated shell-model space if constructed in terms of a sufficient number of collective $S$, $D$, $G$, $\dots$ pairs (i.e., with angular momentum zero, two, four, $\dots$) and if the structure of the pairs is optimally determined either by a conjugate-gradient minimization method or from a Hartree-Fock intrinsic state. We illustrate the procedure for 6 protons and 6 neutrons in the $pf$ ($sdg$) shell and exactly reproduce the level energies and electric quadrupole properties of the ground-state rotational band with $SDG$ ($SDGI$) pairs. The $SD$-pair approximation without significant renormalization, on the other hand, cannot describe the full SU(3) collectivity. A mapping from Elliott's fermionic SU(3) model to systems with $s$, $d$, $g$, $\dots$ bosons provides insight into the existence of a decoupled collective subspace in terms of $S$, $D$, $G$, $\dots$ pairs.

# Nucleon-pair coupling scheme in Elliott's SU(3) model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Elliott's SU(3) symmetry can be exactly reproduced in a truncated shell-model space using collective nucleon pairs (S, D, G, ...) only if a sufficient number of pair types is included and their structure is optimally determined; the SD-pair approximation without significant renormalization cannot describe the full SU(3) collectivity, contrary to earlier expectations.

## Method

The authors perform nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) calculations for systems of 6 protons and 6 neutrons in the pf and sdg harmonic-oscillator shells, interacting through a quadrupole-quadrupole force. They optimize the structure coefficients of collective pairs using either a conjugate-gradient minimization method or by projecting pairs from a Hartree-Fock intrinsic state, then diagonalize the Hamiltonian in the truncated space spanned by these pairs. The results are compared to exact Elliott SU(3) solutions and to calculations using generalized seniority (GS) pairs.

## Key numbers

- For 6p-6n in the pf shell, the SDG-pair approximation with conjugate-gradient optimization (SDG_CG) reproduces the exact binding energy of 810/pi MeV to a precision of eight digits.
- The SDG_CG calculation reproduces 98% of the exact binding energy, 99% of the exact moment of inertia, and 97% of the exact B(E2) values when using SU(3) tensor pairs.
- The SDG_GS (generalized seniority) approach reproduces only 76% of the exact binding energy and the predicted moment of inertia is approximately 43% of the exact value.
- For 6p-6n in the pf shell, the SD_GS, SD_CG, and SDS'D'_CG spaces reproduce only 76%, 83%, and 84% of the exact binding energy, respectively.
- For 6p-6n in the sdg shell, the SDGI_CG-pair approximation reproduces exactly the SU(3) results; the SDGI-pair approximation with SU(3) tensor pairs reproduces 99% of the exact binding energy, 97% of the moment of inertia, and 99% of the B(E2) values.
- The SDG_CG-pair approximation in the sdg shell yields 96% of the binding energy and 57% of the moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), previously successful for nearly spherical nuclei (as in the generalized seniority approach of Xu (2009) and others), to well-deformed rotational nuclei described by Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958). The results contradict earlier attempts such as the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) which constructed an SU(3) limit with Sp(6) symmetry using S and D pairs but found their structure far from realistic pairs (Halse, 1989), and earlier SD-pair approximations that gave much smaller binding energies, moments of inertia, and E2 transitions than the exact SU(3) limit (Zhao, 2000). The work enables the construction of viable collective subspaces for realistic shell-model interactions and clarifies the derivation of boson Hamiltonians for quadrupole-deformed nuclei, including the mapping to the interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello.
