# Why does the Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC behave as a nearly ideal fluid ?

> The lecture is a brief review of the following topics: (i) collective flow phenomena in heavy ion collisions. The data from RHIC indicate robust collective flows, well described by hydrodynamics with expected Equation of State. The transport properties turned out to be unexpected, with very small viscosity; (ii) physics of highly excited matter produced in heavy ion collisions at T_c<T<4T_c is different from weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma because of relatively strong coupling generating bound states of quasiparticles; (iii) wider discussion of other ``strongly coupled systems'' including strongly coupled supersymmetric theories studied via Maldacena duality, as well as recent progress in trapped atoms with very large scattering length.

## Key claim

The Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) produced at RHIC behaves as a nearly ideal fluid because the effective interaction in QGP is much stronger than expected from perturbation theory, with the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density \(\eta/s \sim 1/10\) being surprisingly small; this arises from relatively strong coupling generating bound states of quasiparticles in the temperature range \(T_c < T < 4T_c\), rather than from a weakly coupled gas.

## Method

The paper reviews collective flow phenomena in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, comparing experimental data for transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow to predictions from ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice-based Equation of State. It also examines transport model calculations by Gyulassy and Molnar (2002) that vary the matter opacity (density times cross section) to reproduce the observed elliptic flow, and uses the sound attenuation length \(\Gamma_s\) derived from viscous hydrodynamics to quantify dissipative corrections to the ideal flow.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density for QGP is \(\eta/s \sim 1/10\), making it "the most perfect fluid known" since the same ratio is greater than 1 for most liquids.
- The latent heat of the QCD phase transition that best describes the data is about \(0.8\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\), in good agreement with lattice predictions.
- The transverse flow velocity at RHIC is \(v_t \sim 0.7\).
- The sound attenuation length extracted from deviations of elliptic flow from ideal hydro at \(p_\perp \approx 1.6\ \text{GeV}\) is \(\Gamma_s \sim 0.15\ \text{fm}\) (using \(\tau \sim 3\ \text{fm/c}\)), compared to a weak coupling prediction of \(\Gamma_s \sim 2\ \text{fm}\).
- The freezeout temperature from blast-wave fits to RHIC spectra is \(T_{kin} \sim 30\ \text{MeV}\) for BEVALAC data, while at RHIC the chemical freezeout temperature \(T_{ch}\) appears independent of centrality and is identified with the QCD critical temperature.

## Context

This lecture review extends the earlier work of Shuryak and Zhirov (1978) on radial flow in pp collisions and the hydrodynamical model of Landau (1953), and contrasts with perturbative QCD parton cascade calculations that predicted negligible collective effects. The paper connects RHIC findings to strongly coupled supersymmetric theories studied via Maldacena duality and to recent experiments with trapped fermionic \(Li^6\) atoms at large scattering length, enabling a broader understanding of strongly coupled systems across different fields of physics.
