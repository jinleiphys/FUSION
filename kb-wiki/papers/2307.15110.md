# Seeing Beauty in the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Energy Correlators

> Heavy quarks created in heavy-ion collisions serve as an excellent probe of the produced quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The radiation pattern of jets formed from heavy quarks as they traverse the QGP exhibits a particularly interesting structure due to the interplay of two competing effects: the suppression of small-angle radiation, also known as the ``dead-cone'' effect, and the enhancement of emitted gluons by medium-induced radiation. In this Letter, we propose a new observable, based on the energy correlator approach to jet substructure, which will allow us to disentangle the two scales associated to these two phenomena and to determine under which conditions the dead-cone is filled by medium-induced radiation. Combined with the forthcoming high-statistics measurements of heavy-flavor jets, this work provides a novel tool to unravel the dynamics of the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the two-point energy correlator for heavy-flavor jets in heavy-ion collisions can simultaneously resolve the dead-cone angle (vacuum suppression of small-angle radiation) and the onset angle (medium-induced enhancement), and that when these two angular scales overlap, the correlator displays new structure that cannot be reduced to the sum of the two phenomena, enabling a determination of under which conditions the dead-cone is filled by medium-induced radiation.

## Method

The authors compute the medium-modified two-point energy correlator for heavy-quark jets using a simplified "brick" model of a static quark-gluon plasma of fixed length with a constant linear density of scatterings and a screened Coulomb-like (Yukawa) interaction. The calculation incorporates the leading-log mass dependence through the massive O(alpha_s) collinear splitting function for q -> qg and through massive quark propagators in the semi-hard approximation of the multiple scattering BDMPS-Z formalism, with the vacuum formation time replaced by its massive version. The results are compared with an alternative single scattering approximation for consistency.

## Key numbers

- The dead-cone angle is defined as theta_0 ~ m_Q / E, where m_Q is the quark mass and E its energy.
- The screening mass mu is set to 1 GeV for numerical calculations.
- Three parameter sets are shown: (E=500 GeV, L=15 GeV^-1, n_0=0.6), (E=200 GeV, L=30 GeV^-1, n_0=0.2), and (E=50 GeV, L=40 GeV^-1, n_0=0.2).
- The onset angle for light-quark jets scales as theta_L = 1 / sqrt(L E).
- The colored bands in the ratio plots use a threshold of 15% deviation (where the A-A to p-p ratio drops to 0.85) as a proxy for the onset of medium enhancement, and 85% of the saturated value for the boundary of the filled dead-cone region.
- The maximum angle at which the dead-cone effect is visible is approximately 4 m_b / E for bottom quarks.

## Context

This work extends the energy correlator approach first proposed for massless quark jets in heavy-ion collisions by Andres et al. (2022, 2023) to the case of heavy-flavor jets, building on the demonstration by Craft et al. (2022) that the dead-cone effect produces a depletion in the correlator spectra for heavy-quark p-p jets. It addresses the theoretical question raised by Armesto (2003) of whether medium-induced radiation fills the dead-cone region, and provides a new observable that avoids the difficulties of iterative declustering techniques in heavy-ion environments, where medium-induced emissions do not follow angular ordering as shown by Mehtar-Tani (2010) and grooming techniques are prone to misidentify splittings as discussed by Mulligan (2020). The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and the LHC to disentangle the competing scales of vacuum suppression and medium enhancement in heavy-flavor jet substructure.
