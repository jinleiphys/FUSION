# Extended Goldberger-Treiman relation obtained in a three-flavor parity doublet model

> We study masses and decay widths of positive and negative parity nucleons using a three-flavor parity doublet model, in which we introduce three representations, $\left[({\bf 3} , \bar{\bf 3})\oplus (\bar{\bf 3} , {\bf 3})\right]$, $\left[({\bf 3} , {\bf 6}) \oplus ({\bf 6}, {\bf 3})\right]$, and $\left[({\bf 8} , {\bf 1}) \oplus ({\bf 1} , {\bf 8})\right]$ of the chiral U$(3)_{\rm L}\times$U$(3)_{\rm R}$ symmetry. We find an extended version of the Goldberger-Treiman relation among the mass differences and the coupling constants for pionic transitions. This relation leads to an upper bound for the decay width of $N(1440) \rightarrow N(939) + π$ independently of the model parameters. We perform the numerical fitting of the model parameters and derive several predictions, which can be tested in future experiments or lattice QCD analysis. Furthermore, when we use the axial coupling of the excited nucleons obtained from lattice QCD analyses as inputs, we find that the ground state nucleon $N(939)$ consists of about 80% of $\left[({\bf 3} , {\bf 6}) \oplus ({\bf 6}, {\bf 3})\right]$ component, and that the chiral invariant mass of $N(939)$ is roughly $500$ -- $800$MeV.

## Key claim

The authors derive an extended Goldberger-Treiman relation that holds for a three-flavor parity doublet model with three chiral representations, and this relation yields an upper bound for the decay width of \(N(1440) \rightarrow N(939) + \pi\) that is independent of the model parameters, provided the experimental values for other excited nucleon decays are used as inputs.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian for baryons in three representations of U(3)\(_{\rm L}\times\)U(3)\(_{\rm R}\): \([({\bf 3},\bar{\bf 3})\oplus(\bar{\bf 3},{\bf 3})]\), \([({\bf 3},{\bf 6})\oplus({\bf 6},{\bf 3})]\), and \([({\bf 8},{\bf 1})\oplus({\bf 1},{\bf 8})]\), with mirror assignment for parity partners. They derive the mass matrix for the six nucleon states (three positive-parity and three negative-parity) and the pionic coupling matrix on the chiral representation basis, then diagonalize to obtain physical masses and couplings. The extended Goldberger-Treiman relation is obtained by relating the coupling matrix to the commutator of the axial charge matrix with the mass matrix.

## Key numbers

- The experimental axial coupling of the nucleon \(g_A = 1.268 \pm 0.006\) yields \(\frac{1}{4}g_A^2 = 0.402 \pm 0.004\).
- The sum \(\frac{1}{4}g_A^2 + \sum_{N^*} \tilde{\Gamma}(N^*)\) from experimental data equals \(0.49 \pm 0.02\), which is below the upper bound of \(25/36 \approx 0.69\).
- The upper bound on \(\tilde{\Gamma}(N(1440))\) is \(0.269 \pm 0.007\), which translates to an allowed region on the mass versus decay width plane for \(N(1440)\).
- The experimental mass of \(N(1440)\) is \(1420\)–\(1450\) MeV and its decay width to \(N(939)+\pi\) is \(210 \pm 70\) MeV, which is consistent with the model-independent upper bound.
- The ground state nucleon \(N(939)\) consists of about 80% of the \([({\bf 3},{\bf 6})\oplus({\bf 6},{\bf 3})]\) component.
- The chiral invariant mass of \(N(939)\) is roughly \(500\)–\(800\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor parity doublet model of Detar and Kunihiro (1988) to three flavors with three chiral representations, following the earlier three-flavor construction by Chen et al. (2009, 2010, 2011). The analysis reconciles the tension between two-flavor studies that prefer a small chiral invariant mass \(m_0 \lesssim 500\) MeV (Jido et al., 2001; Gallas et al., 2013) and nuclear matter studies that prefer larger \(m_0\) values (Zschiesche et al., 2006; Dexheimer et al., 2007; Motohiro et al., 2015), by finding \(m_0 \approx 500\)–\(800\) MeV. The results can be tested in future experiments or lattice QCD analyses of excited nucleon properties.
