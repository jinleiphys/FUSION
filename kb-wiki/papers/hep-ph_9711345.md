# Strange Particle Abundance in QGP Formed in 200 GeV A Nuclear Collisions

> We investigate here the relative abundance of strange particles produced in nuclear collisions at the SPS energies (9 GeV A in CM frame) assuming that the central reaction fireball consists of quark-gluon plasma. We show that the total strangeness yield in S-S, S-Ag and S-W reactions is compatible with this picture.

# Strange Particle Abundance in QGP Formed in 200 GeV A Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The total strangeness yield observed in Sulphur-induced reactions at SPS energies (approximately 9 GeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame) is compatible with the assumption that the central reaction fireball consists of quark-gluon plasma, provided one allows for a strangeness occupancy factor between 0.5 and 1 that depends on the size and lifetime of the QGP region.

## Method

The authors model the central fireball formed in 200 GeV per nucleon S-S, S-Ag, and S-W collisions as a thermalized quark-gluon plasma with QGP equations of state including thermal masses, reduced degrees of freedom from perturbative QCD corrections, and a strangeness occupancy factor gamma_s. They compute the expected ratio of strange quark abundance to entropy content in the QGP phase and compare this theoretical ratio to experimental data on strange particle multiplicities and negative hadron multiplicities, using the relation S/B approximately equals 4h/B to connect entropy to hadronic multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for S-W collisions: T = 232 +/- 3 MeV (from WA85 data)
- Initial temperature for S-S collisions: T = 194 +/- 15 MeV (from NA35 data)
- Quark fugacity for S-W collisions: lambda_q = 1.49 +/- 0.05
- Quark fugacity for S-S collisions: lambda_q = 1.36
- Strange quark fugacity: lambda_s = 1.0 +/- 0.1 (from Omega-bar/Omega ratio)
- Strangeness occupancy for S-S/Ag: gamma_s = 0.56 +/- 0.07 +/- 0.15
- Strangeness occupancy for S-W/Pb: gamma_s = 0.8 +/- 0.1
- Strangeness occupancy for N-N reference: gamma_s = 0.37 +/- 0.05
- Theoretical strange-to-entropy ratio in QGP: s_h = 0.25 * (1 + 0.47(alpha_s - 0.6)) * gamma_s
- N-N background strange-to-entropy ratio: s_h^{NN} = 0.092 +/- 0.014
- S-S/Ag experimental strange-to-entropy ratio: s_h^{S} = 0.14
- Predicted strange-to-entropy ratio for Pb-Pb: s_h^{PbPb} = 0.25
- Center-of-mass energies: S-S at 9.7 GeV, S-Ag at 9.4 GeV, S-W/Pb at 8.8 GeV, Pb-Pb at 8.6 GeV
- Negative hadron yield per baryon for S-S/Ag: h^-/B = 1.8 +/- 0.2

## Context

This work extends the thermal fireball model developed by Rafelski (1991) and Letessier et al. (1993, 1994) to explain strange particle abundances in SPS nuclear collisions as evidence for QGP formation. It contradicts the hadronic gas (HG) phase interpretation by showing that the high entropy content and unique quark fugacities observed are incompatible with confined hadronic matter, consistent with earlier findings by Letessier et al. (1994). The analysis enables quantitative predictions for the upcoming Pb-Pb collisions at the SPS, including full strangeness saturation (gamma_s = 1) and a prediction that one in four produced particles will carry strangeness. The work builds on experimental data from the WA85, NA35, and NA36 collaborations and uses the N-N reference values from Gazdzicki and Hansen (1991).
