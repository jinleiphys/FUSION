# Combined method to extract spectroscopic information

> Spectroscopic factors (SF) play an important role in nuclear physics and astrophysics. The traditional method of extracting SF from direct transfer reactions suffers from serious ambiguities. We discuss a modified method which is based on including the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the overlap functions into the transfer analysis. In the modified method the contribution of the external part of the reaction amplitude, typically dominant, is fixed and the SF is determined from fitting the internal part. We illustrate the modified method with $(d,p)$ reactions on ${}^{208}{\rm Pb}, {}^{12}{\rm C}$, and ${}^{84}{\rm Se}$ targets at different energies. The modified method allows one to extract the SF, which do not depend on the shape of the single-particle nucleon-target interaction, and has the potential of improving the reliability and accuracy of the structure information. This is specially important for nuclei on dripline, where not much is known.

# Combined method to extract spectroscopic information

## Key claim

The modified method eliminates the dependence of extracted spectroscopic factors on the shape of the single-particle nucleon-target interaction potential, but only works when the transfer reaction has a significant contribution from the nuclear interior and is well described by one-step DWBA; the balance between these two conditions is not trivial.

## Method

The authors combine a measured asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) with a (d,p) transfer reaction cross section measured at an energy where the interior contribution is significant. They split the DWBA reaction amplitude into an interior part (which depends on the single-particle ANC b) and an exterior part (fixed by the true ANC), then impose equality between the experimental ratio R_exp = dσ_exp/dΩ / C² and the calculated ratio R_DW(b) to determine the correct b and thus the spectroscopic factor S = C²/b².

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb(d,p)²⁰⁹Pb(g.s.) at E_d = 22 MeV: ANC C²_{g9/2} = 2.15(0.16) fm⁻¹; extracted b = 1.82 fm⁻¹/² and S = 0.74; the standard approach with (r₀,a) = (1.2,0.6) fm gives S = 0.866 and C² = 1.56 fm⁻¹, outside the experimental range.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb(d,p)²⁰⁹Pb at 22 MeV, the interior contribution is about 10%; the uncertainty in b ∈ [1.1, 3.1] fm⁻¹/² propagates to S ∈ [0.3, 2.2].
- For ¹²C(d,p)¹³C(g.s.) at 51 MeV: R_exp = σ(2.5°)/C²_{1,1/2} = 2.92(0.35) fm mb/srad; a shell-model S = 0.66 would require b = 1.89 fm⁻¹/².
- ANCs can be determined with 5% accuracy from sub-Coulomb reactions.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Goncharov (various years) by explicitly incorporating the ANC into transfer reaction analysis to fix the external contribution, addressing the long-standing ambiguity in spectroscopic factor extraction criticized by Brown (various years) and others. The method enables more reliable spectroscopic information for exotic nuclei near the dripline, where transfer reactions are the primary tool, and is relevant to programs at rare isotope facilities such as Oak Ridge, NSCL-MSU, GANIL, and RIKEN. The authors note that the method can rule out inadequate optical potential parameterizations, as demonstrated by comparing adiabatic and standard deuteron potentials for the ¹²C case.
