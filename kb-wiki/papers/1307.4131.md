# Pairing and rotational properties of actinides and superheavy nuclei in covariant density functional theory

> The cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory has been applied for a systematic study of pairing and rotational properties of actinides and light superheavy nuclei. Pairing correlations are taken into account by the Brink-Booker part of finite range Gogny D1S force. For the first time in the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) framework the pairing properties are studied via the quantities (such as three-point $Δ^{(3)}$ indicators) related to odd-even mass staggerings. The investigation of the moments of inertia at low spin and the $Δ^{(3)}$ indicators shows the need for an attenuation of the strength of the Brink-Booker part of the Gogny D1S force in pairing channel. The investigation of rotational properties of even-even and odd-mass nuclei at normal deformation, performed in the density functional theory framework in such a systematic way for the first time, reveals that in the majority of the cases the experimental data are well described. These include the evolution of the moments of inertia with spin, band crossings in the $A\geq 242$ nuclei, the impact of the particle in specific orbital on the moments of inertia in odd-mass nuclei. The analysis of the discrepancies between theory and experiment in the band crossing region of $A\leq 240$ nuclei suggests the stabilization of octupole deformation at high spin, not included in the present calculations. The evolution of pairing with deformation, which is important for the fission barriers, has been investigated via the analysis of the moments of inertia in the superdeformed minimum. The dependence of the results on the CDFT parametrization has been studied by comparing the results of the calculations obtained with the NL1 and NL3* parametrizations.

## Key claim

The paper systematically demonstrates that the strength of the Brink-Booker part of the Gogny D1S force in the pairing channel must be attenuated (by average scaling factors of 0.9147 for NL1 and 0.899 for NL3*) to simultaneously reproduce experimental moments of inertia at low spin and three-point odd-even mass staggerings in actinides, whereas the original unattenuated force overestimates pairing correlations in this mass region.

## Method

The cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method (CRHB+LN) was applied to even-even and odd-mass actinide and light superheavy nuclei. Pairing correlations were treated using the Brink-Booker part of the finite-range Gogny D1S force, with calculations performed in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis (fermionic shells up to N_F=14 for normal deformation, N_F=16 for superdeformation) using the NL1 and NL3* parametrizations of the covariant density functional. The scaling factor for the pairing force was determined by fitting to experimental moments of inertia extracted from the 2^+ states of ground-state rotational bands across all available even-even actinides.

## Key numbers

- Average scaling factor f_av = 0.9147 for the NL1 parametrization and f_av = 0.899 for the NL3* parametrization, determined from fits to moments of inertia at rotational frequency Omega_x ~ 0.02 MeV
- For the NL1 parametrization, the previously obtained scaling factor f = 0.893 for ^254No (from Ref. A250) is reasonably close to the new average f_av = 0.9147
- Basis truncation: fermionic states up to N_F=14 and bosonic states up to N_B=20 for normal-deformed minimum; N_F increased to 16 for superdeformed minimum
- Calculations performed in rotational frequency range Omega_x = 0.01-0.45 MeV with steps of 0.02 MeV outside band crossing regions and 0.01 MeV in band crossing regions
- Oscillator frequency hbar omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV used in the basis

## Context

This work extends the limited previous CDFT studies of rotating actinides (such as the investigation of ^254No in Afanasjev (2013) and the rare-earth studies in Afanasjev (2005)) to a systematic survey covering both even-even and odd-mass nuclei. It directly confronts the CRHB+LN approach with experimental moments of inertia and three-point Delta^(3) indicators, enabling future assessments of pairing strength for fission barrier calculations and providing a benchmark for configuration assignments in odd-mass superheavy nuclei where alternative methods are unreliable. The results also highlight the need to include octupole deformation at high spin for A <= 240 nuclei, which is not present in the current reflection-symmetric calculations.
