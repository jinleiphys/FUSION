# Neutron spin rotation in \vec{n}-d scattering

> The neutron spin rotation induced by parity-violating (PV) components in the nucleon-nucleon potential is studied in \vec{n}-d scattering at zero energy. Results are obtained corresponding to the Argonne v_{18} two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon strong-interaction potentials in combination with either the DDH or pionless EFT model for the weak-interaction potential. We find that this observable is dominated by the contribution of the long-range part of the PV potential associated with pion exchange. Thus its measurement could provide a further constraint, complementary to that coming from measurements of the photon asymmetry in \vec{n}-p radiative capture, on the strength of this component of the hadronic weak interaction.

# Neutron spin rotation in \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) scattering

## Key claim
The neutron spin rotation in \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) scattering at zero energy is dominated by the long-range pion-exchange component of the parity-violating potential, with short-range vector-meson contributions being roughly two orders of magnitude smaller. This observable could provide a constraint on the hadronic weak interaction that is complementary to measurements of the photon asymmetry in \(\vec{n}\)-\(p\) radiative capture, but only if the strong-interaction Hamiltonian reproduces the experimental doublet and quartet scattering lengths.

## Method
The neutron spin rotation per unit length was calculated perturbatively to first order in the parity-violating potential using \(n\)-\(d\) continuum wave functions generated by the hyperspherical-harmonics method. The strong-interaction Hamiltonians employed were the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon potential alone and combined with the Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential. Two models for the weak-interaction potential were used: the DDH meson-exchange model (with \(\pi\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) exchanges) and a pionless effective field theory model with five independent four-nucleon contact terms. Matrix elements were evaluated via a hybrid Monte Carlo and Gaussian quadrature technique, keeping only S- and P-wave channels in the partial wave expansion.

## Key numbers
- Predicted spin rotation per unit length with the AV18/UIX strong potential and DDH-best weak couplings: \(0.53 \times 10^{-7}\) rad-cm\(^{-1}\) (assuming liquid deuterium density \(\rho = 0.4 \times 10^{23}\) atoms cm\(^{-3}\)).
- Predicted spin rotation with the AV18/UIX strong potential and DDH-adj weak couplings: \(0.56 \times 10^{-7}\) rad-cm\(^{-1}\).
- With the complete DDH potential (best values): \(0.52 \times 10^{-7}\) rad-cm\(^{-1}\); with only the pion-exchange term retained: \(0.46 \times 10^{-7}\) rad-cm\(^{-1}\).
- The coefficient \(I_1^{\rm DDH}\) for the pion-exchange component is approximately \(0.256 \times 10^3\) fm (AV18) and \(0.270 \times 10^3\) fm (AV18/UIX) for the DDH-I cutoff set, roughly two orders of magnitude larger than the vector-meson coefficients.
- The doublet S-wave channel contribution to \(I_1^{\rm DDH}\) (DDH-I) is 45.0 fm (AV18) and 74.2 fm (AV18/UIX); the quartet S-wave channel contribution is 210.6 fm (AV18) and 196.6 fm (AV18/UIX).

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of parity-violating observables in few-nucleon systems by the same group, specifically the longitudinal asymmetry in \(\vec{p}\)-\(p\) scattering (Carlson, 2002) and the neutron spin rotation, longitudinal asymmetry, and photon asymmetry in \(\vec{n}\)-\(p\) systems (Schiavilla, 2004). The DDH model used is that of Desplanques (1980), and the pionless EFT model is that of Zhu (2005). The authors note that no measurements of the \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) spin rotation have been performed, and the study aims to motivate such experiments. The results enable future experimental constraints on the long-range pion-exchange component of the hadronic weak interaction, complementary to ongoing measurements of the photon asymmetry in \(\vec{n}\)-\(p\) radiative capture.
