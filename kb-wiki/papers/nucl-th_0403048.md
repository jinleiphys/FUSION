# Theory Highlights of Quark Matter 2004

## Key claim
The combination of RHIC data with theoretical analyses indicates that the matter created in central Au-Au collisions is very dense, thermalized, and probably nonperturbative, leading to the conclusion that the Quark-Gluon Plasma has been produced; however, a deeper understanding of the relevant microscopic degrees of freedom and their interactions is still required before claiming a definitive discovery.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical developments reported at the 2004 Quark Matter conference, covering lattice QCD results at finite temperature and density, color-glass condensate initial-state effects, hydrodynamic and parton energy-loss descriptions of bulk matter, quark coalescence models for hadronization, and microscopic probes including resonance spectroscopy, electromagnetic radiation, and charmonium production. The review synthesizes multiple theoretical approaches and compares them to experimental data from RHIC runs 2 and 3.

## Key numbers
- Energy density inferred from parton energy-loss calculations in 200 AGeV central Au-Au collisions: epsilon approximately 20 GeV/fm^3 in the early stages
- Thermalization time required by hydrodynamic descriptions: tau_0 approximately 0.5 fm/c
- Transition momentum from collective to perturbative production in central Au-Au: 2 GeV for pions, 2.6 GeV for kaons, 3.5 GeV for protons
- Saturation momentum estimate for forward rapidity (eta=3) at RHIC: Q_s approximately 2 GeV
- Chemical freezeout parameters: (mu_N, mu_pi, T)_fo = (370, 90, 110) MeV, with pion density 0.65 rho_0 and baryon+antibaryon density 0.25 rho_0 (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
- Rho-mass shift inferred from STAR pi+pi- invariant-mass spectra: approximately -50 MeV
- Number of c-cbar pairs in central 200 AGeV Au-Au: 10-20 pairs
- Lattice QCD indicates charmonia (eta_c, J/psi) survive up to approximately 2 T_c
- Critical endpoint estimate from lattice expansions: mu_B = 3 mu_q approximately 500 MeV

## Context
This theory summary extends the line of work on QGP characterization at RHIC, building on prior lattice QCD results by Karsch (2002) and spectral function analyses by Asakawa and Hatsuda (2004) and Datta (2003). The review discusses how color-glass condensate calculations by Jalilian-Marian, Venugopalan (2003) and Kovchegov address forward-rapidity suppression in d-Au collisions, and how quark coalescence models by Fries et al. (2003), Greco et al. (2003), and Hwa et al. (2003) explain baryon-to-meson ratios at intermediate p_t. The paper identifies open questions including the HBT puzzle, the nature of the nonperturbative QGP phase, and the need for quantitative comparisons of CGC predictions with data incorporating more mundane mechanisms as stressed by Accardi (2004).
