# Away-side distribution in a parton multiple scattering model and background-suppressed measures

> A model of parton multiple scattering in a dense and expanding medium is described. The simulated results reproduce the general features of the data. In particular, in the intermediate trigger momentum region there is a dip-bump structure, while at higher trigger momentum the double bumps merge into a central peak. Also, a new measure is proposed to quantify the azimuthal distribution with the virtue that it suppresses the statistical fluctuations event-by-event, while enhancing the even-structure of the signal.

## Key claim
A parton multiple scattering model, rather than a Mach cone or collective medium response, can reproduce the observed dip-bump structure in the away-side azimuthal distribution at intermediate trigger momentum (2.5-4 GeV/c) and the merging into a central peak at higher trigger momentum (8-15 GeV/c), with the important consequence that only one jet on the away side accompanies a near-side jet, not two.

## Method
The authors constructed a Monte Carlo event generator where a recoil parton from a hard scattering near the surface undergoes multiple scatterings in a dense, expanding medium with Hubble-like expansion. The scattering angles are drawn from a Gaussian distribution whose width depends on local density and parton energy, and energy loss follows the BDMPS form (dE/dx proportional to sqrt(E/E_s)). The model uses four adjustable parameters (rho_s=0.63, sigma_1=0.88 rad, Delta_s=1.9 fm, kappa=0.17) to reproduce the PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at 0-5% centrality, with the effective trigger momentum shifted by 0.3 GeV/c to account for recombination of exit partons with thermal partons.

## Key numbers
- Trigger momentum range for dip-bump structure: 2.5-4 GeV/c; associated particle momentum range: 1-2.5 GeV/c (PHENIX data, Au+Au 0-5% centrality)
- Double bumps appear at phi approximately +/- 1 rad from the away-side direction
- Higher trigger momentum range: 8-15 GeV/c; associated particle momenta: 4-6 GeV/c and >6 GeV/c (STAR data)
- Default parameters: R_0=6 fm, xi=xi_1=0.8, H=0.07, E_s=5 GeV, E_cut=0.3 GeV/c
- Exit parton momentum range: 0.7 to 2.2 GeV/c for associated hadrons with 1 < p_T(assoc) < 2.5 GeV/c
- Exit parton tracks multiplied by factor 1.7 to account for average number of hadrons produced per exit parton via recombination

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier multiple scattering model (Chiu 2006) and provides an alternative to Mach cone interpretations (Casalderrey-Solana 2006, Koch 2005, Dremin 2006, Shuryak 2006) and Cherenkov gluon radiation explanations for the dip-bump structure observed by PHENIX (2006). The paper also introduces a factorial moment (FM) method for analyzing azimuthal distributions that suppresses statistical fluctuations event-by-event and eliminates the need for background subtraction, building on prior FM techniques (Chiu 2006a). This method could enable direct comparison of results from different experiments and subtraction schemes.
