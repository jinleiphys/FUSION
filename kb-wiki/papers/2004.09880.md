# Probing the electromagnetic fields in ultrarelativistic collisions with leptons from $Z^0$ decay and charmed mesons

> Ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are expected to generate a huge electromagnetic field, $eB \approx 10^{18}\, Gauss$, that induces a splitting of the directed flow, $v_1=\left\langle p_x/p_T \right\rangle $, of charged particles and anti-particles. Such a splitting for charmed meson manifests even for neutral particle/anti-particles pairs ($D^0, \overline{D}^0$), hence being also a unique probe of the formation of the quark-gluon plasma phase. For the first time here we show that electromagnetic field may generate a $v_1(D^0) - v_1( \overline{D}^0)$ as huge as the one recently observed at LHC against early expectations. Within this new research topic we point out a novel measurement: $v_1$ of leptons from $Z^0$ decay and its correlation to that of $D$ and $B$ mesons. The correlation for $Δv_1$ of $l^{+} - l^{-}$, $D^0-\overline{D}^0$ and $B^0-\overline{B}^0$ would provide a strong probe of the electromagnetic origin of the splitting and hence of the formation of a quark-gluon plasma phase with heavy quarks as degrees of freedom. The case of the $v_1({l^{\pm}})$ presents features due to the peculiar form of the $p_T$ spectrum never appreciated before in the study of heavy ion collisions. We specifically predict a sudden change of the $Δv_1(p_T)$ of leptons at $p_T=45$ GeV$/c$, that can be traced back to a universal relation of $Δv_1$ with the slope of the $p_T$ particle distribution and the integrated effect of the Lorentz force.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observed splitting of directed flow between $D^0$ and $\overline{D}^0$ mesons at LHC can be generated by the electromagnetic field, but only if the magnetic field has a slow time evolution (case C); contrary to early expectations, the sign and magnitude of the splitting are determined not by the initial field strength but by the time derivative of the field. The authors propose that measuring the directed flow of leptons from $Z^0$ decay and correlating it with that of $D$ and $B$ mesons would provide a strong probe of the electromagnetic origin of the splitting.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of the electromagnetic fields in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at 20-30% centrality using three different parametrizations (cases A, B, C) that differ in the assumed conductivity and damping rate of the magnetic field. They simulate the propagation of charm quarks and leptons from $Z^0$ decay through these fields using Langevin equations that include the Lorentz force, with charm quarks also experiencing drag and diffusion from the quark-gluon plasma. The directed flow $v_1 = \langle p_x/p_T \rangle$ is computed as a function of pseudorapidity and transverse momentum for each particle species.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field at formation time is $eB_0 = 73\, m_\pi^2$ for cases B and C, corresponding to $eB \approx 10^{18}$ Gauss.
- The decay proper time of the $Z^0$ boson is $\tau_{Z^0} = \hbar/m_{Z^0} = 0.0022$ fm/c.
- The charm quark formation time is $\tau_{form} = \hbar/(2 m_c) \simeq 0.08$ fm/c.
- For case C with $\tau_B = 0.4 \pm 0.1$ fm/c, the slope $d\Delta v_1/d\eta$ for $D^0$ minus $\overline{D}^0$ is $0.44 \pm 0.15$, compared to the ALICE measurement of $0.49 \pm 0.18$.
- For case B with $\tau_B = 0.4$ fm/c, $d\Delta v_1/d\eta = -0.42$ for $D^0$ minus $\overline{D}^0$.
- For case A, $d\Delta v_1/d\eta = -0.004$ for $D^0$ minus $\overline{D}^0$.
- The lepton spectrum from $Z^0$ decay peaks at $p_T = 45$ GeV/c, where a sudden change in $\Delta v_1(p_T)$ is predicted.
- The average momentum shift $\overline{\Delta p}_x$ is about 0.36 GeV/c for charm quarks and 0.7 GeV/c for leptons at $y_z = 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic field effects in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, following earlier work by Das (2016) and Chatterjee (2018) who predicted directed flow splitting for charmed mesons, and responds to the first measurements by ALICE (2019) that showed a larger splitting than initially expected. The paper introduces a novel observable -- the directed flow of leptons from $Z^0$ decay -- and derives a universal relation connecting $\Delta v_1$ to the slope of the $p_T$ particle distribution and the integrated Lorentz force. This enables future experimental tests of the electromagnetic origin of flow splitting and provides a new probe of quark-gluon plasma formation with heavy quarks as degrees of freedom, with implications for studies of the chiral magnetic effect, chiral magnetic wave, and spin polarization.
