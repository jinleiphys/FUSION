# Medium modifications of the bound nucleon GPDs and incoherent DVCS on nuclear targets

> We study incoherent DVCS on $^4$He in the $^4{\rm He}(e,e^{\prime}γp)X$ reaction, which probes medium-modifications of the bound nucleon GPDs and elastic form factors. Assuming that the bound nucleon GPDs are modified in proportion to the corresponding bound nucleon elastic form factors, as predicted in the Quark-Meson Coupling model, we develop an approach to calculate various incoherent nuclear DVCS observables. As an example, we compute the beam-spin DVCS asymmetry, and predict the $x_B$- and $t$-dependence of the ratio of the bound to free proton asymmetries, $A_{\rm LU}^{p^{\ast}}(ϕ)/A_{\rm LU}^{p}(ϕ)$. We find that the deviation of $A_{\rm LU}^{p^{\ast}}(ϕ)/A_{\rm LU}^{p}(ϕ)$ from unity is as much as $\sim$ 6%.

# Medium modifications of the bound nucleon GPDs and incoherent DVCS on nuclear targets

## Key claim

The beam-spin DVCS asymmetry ratio of bound to free protons, $A_{\rm LU}^{p^{\ast}}(\phi)/A_{\rm LU}^{p}(\phi)$, deviates from unity by up to approximately 6%, under the assumption that bound nucleon GPDs are modified in proportion to the corresponding bound nucleon elastic form factors as predicted by the Quark-Meson Coupling model. The sign of the deviation depends on kinematics: the ratio is less than unity at small $t$ and $x_B$, and greater than unity as $t$ and $x_B$ increase.

## Method

The authors derived a relation connecting the squared amplitude of incoherent DVCS on a nuclear target to the squared amplitude of DVCS on bound nucleons using nuclear light-cone wave functions, neglecting Fermi motion by evaluating the nucleon light-cone distribution at $\alpha = 1/A$. They modeled bound proton GPDs by scaling free proton GPDs (from a double distribution model using CTEQ5L and GRSV2000 PDFs) with the ratios of bound-to-free elastic form factors, where the bound form factors were calculated in the Quark-Meson Coupling model and averaged over the $^4$He nuclear density distribution. The beam-spin asymmetry $A_{\rm LU}$ was then computed using standard DVCS formalism for the $^4{\rm He}(e,e^{\prime}\gamma p)X$ reaction.

## Key numbers

- The deviation of $A_{\rm LU}^{p^{\ast}}(\phi)/A_{\rm LU}^{p}(\phi)$ from unity is as much as $\sim$ 6% for $^4$He at $E=6$ GeV, $Q^2=2$ GeV$^2$, and $\phi=\pi/2$.
- The ratio $A_{\rm LU}^{p^{\ast}}(\phi)/A_{\rm LU}^{p}(\phi)$ is less than 1 at small $t$ and $x_B$, and greater than 1 as $t$ and $x_B$ increase, for kinematics with $E=6$ GeV and $Q^2=2$ GeV$^2$.
- The bound-to-free proton elastic form factor ratios $F_1^{p^{\ast}}(t)/F_1^{p}(t)$, $F_2^{p^{\ast}}(t)/F_2^{p}(t)$, and $G_1^{\ast}(t)/G_1(t)$ are shown as functions of $-t$ in Figure 2 of the paper, with $F_2^{p^{\ast}} > F_2^{p}$ in $^4$He.
- The slope parameter for the $t$-dependence of GPDs is $\alpha^{\prime}=1.105$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium modifications of nucleon structure from inclusive DIS (the EMC effect, nuclear shadowing, and antishadowing) and elastic form factors (measured in $^4{\rm He}(\vec{e},e^{\prime}\vec{p})^3{\rm H}$ at Jefferson Lab) to generalized parton distributions probed in DVCS. It contrasts with the approach of Liuti and Taneja (2005), who modeled bound nucleon GPDs through kinematic off-shell effects; the present predictions are significantly smaller in magnitude and differ in the $x_B$-dependence of the asymmetry ratio. The results enable experimental discrimination between models using the upcoming Jefferson Lab measurement of DVCS on $^4$He.
