# Inverse magnetic catalysis for the chiral transition induced by thermo-magnetic effects on the coupling constant

> We compute the one-loop thermo-magnetic corrections to the self-coupling in a model where charged scalars interact also with a constant magnetic field. The calculation is motivated by the possibility that the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition in a magnetic background can be influenced by the dependence of the coupling constant on the magnetic field. We show that the coupling decreases as a function of the field strength. This functional dependence introduces in turn a correction to the boson masses which causes the critical temperature to decrease as a function of the field strength.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the chiral phase transition in a magnetic background becomes a decreasing function of the magnetic field strength (inverse magnetic catalysis) when the one-loop thermo-magnetic corrections to the boson self-coupling are computed consistently within the Abelian Higgs model, rather than assumed or taken as constant.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop correction to the self-coupling λ in the Abelian Higgs model with charged scalars in a constant external magnetic field, using Schwinger's proper-time method for the boson propagator. They separated the zero Matsubara mode from the nonzero modes, worked in the infrared limit (zero external momentum), and included plasma screening effects via ring diagram resummation (Π = λT²/12). The effective coupling λ_eff was then evaluated at v=0 and inserted into the thermo-magnetic effective potential to determine the critical temperature from the vanishing of the second derivative at v=0.

## Key numbers

- The effective coupling λ_eff(v=0) decreases as a function of b = qB/μ² for all three temperatures shown (t = T/μ = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5) in Figure 2.
- The critical temperature normalized to its zero-field value becomes a decreasing function of b = qB/μ² when the full thermo-magnetic dependence of λ_eff is included (Figure 4), in contrast to the increasing behavior seen with constant λ = 0.9 or with only thermal effects on the coupling (Figure 3).
- The calculation is valid in the regime where qB/T² < 1 and −μ² + Π > qB, with Π = λT²/12.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Ayala et al., 2014) of the effective potential in the Abelian Higgs model with constant couplings, now including the one-loop thermo-magnetic correction to the self-coupling. It contradicts earlier lattice results (Braguta, D'Elia, 2011) that found an increasing critical temperature with magnetic field, and aligns with the more recent lattice consensus (Fodor, Bali, 2012) showing inverse magnetic catalysis. The approach differs from earlier works (Andersen, Fukushima, Skokov) by including plasma screening via ring diagram resummation rather than renormalization group analysis, and by computing the magnetic field dependence of the boson self-coupling rather than assuming a running coupling as in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies (Farias, Ferreira, 2014). The results suggest that confinement may not be necessary to understand inverse magnetic catalysis, and the authors plan to extend the analysis by including fermions.
