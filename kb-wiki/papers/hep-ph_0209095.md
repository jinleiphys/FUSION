# Does A Narrow Tetraquark $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ State Exist?

> The existence of a shallow or virtual tetraquark state, $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$, is discussed. Using the putative masses for the doubly charmed baryons ($ccu/ccd$) from SELEX, the mass of the $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ state is estimated to be about $3.9 GeV$, only slightly above the $DD^*$ threshold. The experimental signatures for various $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ masses are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that a doubly charmed tetraquark state \(cc\bar{u}\bar{d}\) with \(J^P=1^{+}\) likely exists near the \(D D^*\) threshold, but the sign and magnitude of the binding energy depend critically on which SELEX peak for the doubly charmed baryon is taken as the true value, yielding a mass either about 25 MeV below or about 35 MeV above the threshold.

## Method

The authors use a phenomenological double-difference relation that cancels quark masses and most pairwise interactions, equating the mass difference between the tetraquark and the doubly charmed baryon \(\Xi_{ccu}\) to the difference between \(\Lambda_c\) and \(D^0\) masses, with a hyperfine correction term. They then insert the SELEX candidate masses for the doubly charmed baryons (3460 MeV for \(ccu\) and 3520 MeV for \(ccd\)) to obtain two possible tetraquark masses. The analysis also considers the possibility of a "deuson" (molecular) component from one-pion exchange between \(D\) and \(D^*\) mesons, and argues that mixing between the compact tetraquark and the deuson will lower the ground state energy.

## Key numbers

- Tetraquark mass estimate using the \(ccu\) SELEX peak at 3460 MeV: \(m_{T(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})} \approx 3.845\) GeV, which is about 25 MeV below the \(D D^*\) threshold.
- Tetraquark mass estimate using the \(ccd\) SELEX peak at 3.52 GeV: \(m_{T(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})} \approx 3.905\) GeV, which is about 35 MeV above the \(D D^*\) threshold.
- Expected binding energy magnitude: \(|\epsilon| \leq 30 \div 60\) MeV, where \(\epsilon = m_{T(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})} - m_{D^*} - m_D\).
- Estimated production rate ratio: \(R_{(cc\bar{u}\bar{d})} \approx 1/10 \times R_{(ccu)}\), based on the analogy with \(\Lambda_c / D\) production ratios.
- Estimated decay width for the \(S\)-wave decay \(T(cc\bar{u}\bar{d}) \to D^* D\): reduced from a naive \(\Gamma \approx 300\) MeV to \(\Gamma \approx 13-19\) MeV by a phase-space factor \(\beta_D \approx 0.13 \times (\epsilon / 30\text{ MeV})^{1/2}\) and a deuson component suppression factor \(|\alpha|^2 \leq 1/3 - 1/4\).

## Context

This work extends the search for multiquark exotic hadrons, following earlier theoretical studies by Jaffe, Lipkin, and Richard, and is motivated by the SELEX (2002) candidate observation of doubly charmed baryons \(ccu/ccd\). The paper directly addresses the disagreement between Tornqvist (who finds \(D^* D\) bound states via one-pion exchange) and Manohar and Wise (who find only \(B^* B\) bound states), attributing the discrepancy to different choices of the cutoff radius in the OPEP. The results enable experimental searches for a narrow tetraquark at hadron colliders (e.g., FNAL) and at \(e^+ e^-\) colliders near the \(\Upsilon(4s)\) energy, using the distinctive signature of two aligned weak decay vertices from the \(D\) mesons.
