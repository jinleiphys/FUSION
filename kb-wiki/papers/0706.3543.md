# Study of π^{-}p \to ηn at low energies in a chiral constituent quark model

> A chiral quark model approach is extended to the study of the $πN$ scattering at low energies. The process of $π^{-}p \to ηn$ near threshold is investigated. The model is successful in describing the differential cross sections and total cross section near the $η$ production threshold. The roles of the resonances in $n\leq 2$ shells are clarified. Near threshold, the $S_{11}(1535)$ dominates the reactions, while the interferences from the $S_{11}(1650)$ turn out to be destructive around $W\lesssim 1.6$ GeV. The $D_{13}(1520)$ is crucial to give correct shapes of the differential cross sections. The nucleon pole term contributions are significant. The $P_{11}(1710)$ plays an important role around the c.m. energy $W=1.7$ GeV, it is crucial to produce an enhancement in the region of $W>1.6$ GeV as suggested by the data for total cross section. The $t$-channel is negligible in the reactions.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model, extended to low-energy πN scattering, successfully describes differential and total cross sections for π⁻p → ηn near threshold, but the predictions for differential cross sections become worse with increasing center-of-mass energy W above about 1.6 GeV, and the sign of the P₁₁(1710) amplitude must be adjusted to reproduce the total cross section enhancement in that region.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic chiral quark model with a harmonic oscillator confinement potential (Isgur-Karl model) to compute s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel transition amplitudes for π⁻p → ηn. The quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling was treated via the leading-order pseudovector interaction from a chiral Lagrangian, and resonance contributions were separated by expanding the propagator in the harmonic oscillator basis and substituting Breit-Wigner forms for s-channel resonances. The model includes resonances up to the n=2 shell (S, P, D, F waves) and the t-channel via a₀ meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The reaction was studied from the η production threshold up to center-of-mass energy W ≃ 1.7 GeV.
- The incident π beam momenta covered from threshold to p_π = 747 MeV/c, based on BNL Crystal Ball data.
- The S₁₁(1535) dominates near threshold; S₁₁(1650) provides destructive interference for W ≲ 1.6 GeV.
- The P₁₁(1710) is crucial for producing an enhancement in the total cross section for W > 1.6 GeV.
- The axial vector coupling constants used are g_A^π⁻ = 5/3 for the π⁻pn vertex and g_A^η = 1 for the ηNN vertex.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach, previously applied to meson photoproduction (Li 1997, and others), to the low-energy πN scattering process π⁻p → ηn. It addresses the need for improved theoretical descriptions noted by Penner (2002) and Shklyar (2004), and confronts recent high-quality data from the BNL Crystal Ball experiment. The results clarify the roles of specific resonances (S₁₁(1535), S₁₁(1650), D₁₃(1520), P₁₁(1710)) and enable future extraction of the ηN interaction, relevant for the possible existence of η-mesic nuclei as discussed by Baru (2006).
