# The pion-cloud contribution to the electromagnetic nucleon structure

> The present contribution continues and extends foregoing work on the calculation of electroweak form factors of hadrons using the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics. Here we are particularly interested in studying pionic effects on the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon. To this aim we employ a hybrid constituent-quark model that comprises, in addition to the $3q$ valence component, also a $3q$+$π$ non-valence component. With a simple wave function for the $3q$ component we get reasonable results for the nucleon form factors. In accordance with other authors we find that the pionic effect is significant only below $Q^2\lesssim 0.5$~GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The pion-cloud (3q+π non-valence component) contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors is significant only below momentum transfers Q² ≲ 0.5 GeV², a finding that agrees with other authors such as Pasquini (2007) but is obtained using a fully microscopic calculation of both the electromagnetic and strong vertex form factors from the same 3q bound-state wave function.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid constituent-quark model within the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to maintain Poincaré invariance. They calculate the 1γ-exchange amplitude for elastic electron scattering off a nucleon that contains both a 3q valence component and a 3q+π non-valence component, reducing the multichannel mass-eigenvalue equation via Feshbach projection to obtain an optical potential. The bare nucleon form factors and the strong πÑÑ vertex form factor are both computed from the same simple 3q bound-state wave function Φ_Ñ, parameterized as Φ_Ñ ∝ [(∑ ω̃_i)² + β²]⁻ᵞ, with parameters β and γ taken from the front-form calculation of Pasquini (2007).

## Key numbers

- The pionic effect on nucleon form factors is significant only for momentum transfers Q² ≲ 0.5 GeV².
- The 3q bound-state wave function uses the functional form Φ_Ñ = 𝒩 [(∑ ω̃_i)² + β²]⁻ᵞ, with parameters β and γ taken from Pasquini (2007).
- The normalization 𝒩 is fixed such that the full nucleon wave function (including the 3q+π component) is normalized to one.

## Context

This work extends the point-form approach previously developed by Biernat (2009, 2010, 2014) and Gomez Rocha (2012) for calculating electroweak form factors of few-body bound states. It directly parallels the front-form calculation of Pasquini (2007) but improves upon it by computing both the electromagnetic and strong πÑÑ vertex form factors from the same microscopic 3q wave function rather than using a phenomenological πÑÑ vertex. The results enable future work toward a consistent description of the baryon spectrum and baryon structure, where the confinement potential and πqq coupling strength would be fitted to the baryon mass spectrum rather than using a model wave function.
