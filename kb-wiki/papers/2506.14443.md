# Study of the beyond standard model interaction using Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering process

> We have conducted an extensive study that highlights the potential of the Indian Coherent Neutrino-nucleus Scattering Experiment (ICNSE) detector in constraining neutrino-quark interactions that go beyond the standard model. By utilizing reactors with varied core configurations and power outputs as sources for electron antineutrinos, and operating with a target mass of 10 kg over a year, our findings reveal that the ICNSE detector is remarkably effective in narrowing down the vast majority of the Non-standard Interaction (NSI) parameter space. Moreover, incorporating results from two distinct detectors, like sapphire and high-purity germanium, markedly enhances sensitivity by reducing the degeneracies between some pairs of NSI parameters to a smaller region.This research highlights its role in enabling future developments and investigations.

## Key claim

The ICNSE detector, using a 10 kg target mass for one year, can effectively constrain the majority of the Non-standard Interaction (NSI) parameter space for electron antineutrino-quark vector couplings, but the sensitivity is almost independent of reactor core configuration and power output; combining results from sapphire and high-purity germanium detectors markedly enhances sensitivity by reducing degeneracies between some pairs of NSI parameters.

## Method

The study simulated CEνNS event rates for the proposed ICNSE detector using electron antineutrinos from four Indian reactor types (Apsara-U, Dhruva, PFBR, VVER) with different core configurations and thermal powers. A rate-only χ² analysis was performed assuming the measured event rate matches the Standard Model prediction, with systematic uncertainties of 5.5% and background uncertainties of 5%, to derive 90% CL exclusion contours for vector-type NSI parameters ε_{ee}^{uV}, ε_{ee}^{dV}, ε_{eτ}^{uV}, and ε_{eτ}^{dV}, as well as for scalar, vector, and tensor NGI couplings.

## Key numbers

*   Sensitivity to NSI parameters was evaluated for a 10 kg target mass with one year exposure, assuming 80% detection efficiency, 90% fiducial volume, and 70% reactor duty cycle.
*   Energy thresholds of 100 eV for sapphire (Al₂O₃) and 150 eV for high-purity germanium detectors were used in the event rate estimation.
*   The allowed region in the ε_{ee}^{uV}-ε_{ee}^{dV} plane at 90% CL was obtained for four reactors: Apsara-U (2.0 MW_th), Dhruva (100 MW_th), PFBR (1250 MW_th), and VVER (3000 MW_th).
*   An overall systematic uncertainty of 5.5% was considered, comprising normalization uncertainty (5%), nonlinear energy response uncertainty (1.0%), and energy calibration uncertainty (1.0%).
*   The slope of the linear band in the ε_{ee}^{uV}-ε_{ee}^{dV} plane is given by the ratio m = (A + N)/(A + Z), where A is mass number, N is neutron number, and Z is proton number.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity studies for the proposed ICNSE experiment (Behera 2023) by specifically constraining NSI parameters using reactor antineutrinos, complementing measurements from the COHERENT experiment (COHERENT 2017, 2021, 2024) which used accelerator neutrinos with CsI, LAr, and germanium detectors. The analysis follows the approach of using multiple target materials to break degeneracies, as suggested by Barranco (2005), and enables future experimental designs for the ICNSE setup to probe BSM physics, including neutrino generalized interactions with scalar, vector, and tensor couplings.
