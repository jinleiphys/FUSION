# Exploring the role of $d^*$ hexaquarks on quark deconfinement and hybrid stars

> We investigate the impact of the $d^*$(2380) hexaquark on the equation of state (EoS) of dense matter within hybrid stars (HSs) using the Chiral Mean-Field model (CMF). The hexaquark is included as a new degree of freedom in the hadronic phase, and its influence on the deconfinement transition to quark matter is explored. We re-parametrize the CMF model to ensure compatibility with recent astrophysical constraints, including the observation of massive pulsars and gravitational wave events. Our results show that the presence of $d^*$ significantly modifies the EoS, leading to a softening at high densities and a consequent reduction in the predicted maximum stellar masses. Furthermore, we examine the possibility of a first-order deconfinement phase transition within the context of the extended stability branch of slow stable HSs (SSHSs). We find that the presence of hexaquarks can delay the deconfinement phase transition and reduce the associated energy density gap, affecting the structure and stability of HSs. Our results suggest that, as the hexaquark appearance tends to destabilize stellar configurations, fine tuning of model parameters is required to obtain both the presence of hexaquarks and quark deconfinement in these systems. In this scenario, the SSHS branch plays a crucial role in obtaining HSs with hexaquarks that satisfy current astrophysical constraints. Our work provides new insights into the role of exotic particles like $d^*$ in dense matter and the complex interplay between hadronic and quark degrees of freedom inside compact stellar objects.

## Key claim

The presence of the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark in hybrid stars softens the equation of state at high densities and reduces the maximum stellar mass, but fine tuning of model parameters is required to simultaneously obtain hexaquarks and quark deconfinement while satisfying current astrophysical constraints; the slow stable hybrid star branch is crucial for achieving such configurations.

## Method

The authors included the $d^*(2380)$ hexaquark as a new degree of freedom in the hadronic phase of the Chiral Mean-Field (CMF) model, re-parametrizing the quark sector by increasing the coupling $g_{\Phi q}$ from 470 MeV to 1000 MeV and adjusting the Polyakov loop potential parameter $a_1$ from $-1.81 \times 10^{-3}$ to $-1.1 \times 10^{-3}$ to shift the deconfinement phase transition to higher densities. They solved the coupled equations of motion for the meson fields and the Polyakov loop field $\Phi$ under charge neutrality and $\beta$ equilibrium at zero temperature, then constructed hybrid star sequences using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations under the assumption of slow hadron-quark conversion.

## Key numbers

- The re-parametrized CMF model produces a first-order deconfinement phase transition at baryon density $n_b = 4.6\, n_0$ with transition pressure $P^{\rm{t}} = 237.23$ MeV/fm$^3$ and energy density gap $\Delta\varepsilon = 2303.58$ MeV/fm$^3$
- The maximum mass of purely hadronic configurations increases from $M = 1.871 M_\odot$ (original RC4 parametrization) to $M = 2.118 M_\odot$ (re-parametrized CMF), satisfying the $M_{\rm{max}} \geq 2.01 M_\odot$ constraint from pulsar J0740+6620
- The original RC4 parametrization had a phase transition at $n_b \sim 3\,n_0$ with $P^{\rm{t}}_{RC4} = 97.85$ MeV/fm$^3$ and $\Delta \varepsilon_{RC4} = 1370.87$ MeV/fm$^3$
- The deconfinement phase transition in the re-parametrized model occurs at baryon chemical potential $\mu_B \simeq 1500$ MeV (compared to $\mu_B \simeq 1250$ MeV for RC4)
- The $d^*$ hexaquark vacuum mass is set to $m_{d^*}^* = 2380$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $d^*(2380)$ hexaquarks in neutron stars using density-dependent relativistic mean-field models (Celi 2024) and earlier RMF treatments (Faessler 1998, Mantziris 2020), now incorporating the hexaquark into the unified CMF framework that describes both hadronic and quark phases. The re-parametrization enables compatibility with modern astrophysical constraints from massive pulsars (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432) and gravitational wave events (GW170817, GW190425), while the slow hadron-quark conversion hypothesis (Pereira 2018, Mariani 2019) allows for extended stability branches of hybrid stars. The results enable future experimental guidance: astrophysical constraints on the hexaquark-nucleon coupling constants can inform ongoing photoproduction experiments at A2@Mainz searching for $d^*$ in nuclear targets.
