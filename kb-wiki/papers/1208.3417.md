# Pygmies, Giants, and Skins

> Understanding the equation of state (EOS) of neutron-rich matter is a central goal of nuclear physics that cuts across a variety of disciplines. Indeed, the limits of nuclear existence, the collision of energetic heavy ions, the structure of neutron stars, and the dynamics of core-collapse supernova all depend critically on the nuclear-matter EOS. In this contribution I focus on the EOS of cold baryonic matter with special emphasis on its impact on the structure, dynamics, and composition of neutron stars. In particular, I discuss how laboratory experiments on neutron skins as well as on Pygmy and Giant resonances can help us elucidate the structure of these fascinating objects.

## Key claim

The neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei and the electric dipole polarizability are strongly correlated, so combined measurements of these laboratory observables can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy and thereby the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, which in turn determines neutron-star radii. However, the correlation between the pygmy dipole resonance and the neutron skin appears to be model dependent, with some studies finding a weak correlation.

## Method

The author uses relativistic mean-field models (NL3, FSU, IU-FSU) supplemented by nonlinear meson interactions (with coupling constants κ, λ, ζ, and Λ_v) to compute both nuclear structure and neutron-star structure. The models are calibrated to ground-state observables of finite nuclei and then used without further adjustment to predict neutron-skin thicknesses, electric dipole polarizability, and dipole strength distributions via random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations. Correlation coefficients between observables are obtained from covariance analysis based on the FSU interaction.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear-matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 2.48 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Neutron-drip density marking transition from outer to inner crust: 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³
- Outer crust density range: from about 10⁴ g/cm³ to 4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³
- Maximum neutron star mass increased from 1.72 M⊙ (FSU model) to 1.94 M⊙ (IU-FSU model) by tuning the ζ parameter
- Demorest et al. observation of a (1.97 ± 0.04) M⊙ neutron star
- PREX measurement of neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: R_n − R_p = 0.33⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀·₁₈ fm
- Correlation coefficient between neutron-skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and slope of symmetry energy L: 0.979
- Correlation coefficient between dipole polarizability and neutron-skin thickness across 48 models: 0.77
- Correlation coefficient between neutron skin and α_D in ²⁰⁸Pb within FSU model: about 0.9
- Pygmy dipole resonance in ⁶⁸Ni accounts for 20-25% of total dipole polarizability
- Pygmy dipole resonance exhausts 5-8% of the energy-weighted sum rule

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the neutron-star equation of state through laboratory measurements, building on the parity-violating electron scattering experiment PREX (Abrahamyan 2012, Horowitz 2012) and high-resolution measurements of electric dipole polarizability in ²⁰⁸Pb (Tamii 2011, Poltoratska 2012). The author discusses how the symmetry energy slope L, which controls neutron-star radii, can be constrained through neutron-skin measurements, and examines correlations between the dipole polarizability and neutron skin that were previously studied by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010). The paper enables future multi-prong experimental approaches combining neutron-skin measurements and dipole polarizability measurements on multiple nuclei to constrain the isovector sector of nuclear energy density functionals.
