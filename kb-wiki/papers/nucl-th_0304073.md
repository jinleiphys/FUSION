# Comparison of Nuclear Suppression Effects on Meson Production at High p_T and p_L

> The medium effect on the pion distribution at high $p_T$ in $AA$ collisions is compared to that of the pion distribution at high $p_L$ in $pA$ collisions. Both the suppression of the spectra and the energy losses of the measured pions are studied. Although the medium effect on $p_T$ is larger than on $p_L$, the difference is found surprisingly to be not as big as one would naively expect.

## Key claim

The nuclear suppression effect on high-\(p_T\) pion production in \(AA\) collisions is larger than on high-\(p_L\) pion production in \(pA\) collisions, but the difference is surprisingly not as large as one would naively expect given the very different media (dense and hot versus cold and uncompressed) and momentum regimes involved.

## Method

The authors compare the suppression factor \(S\) for pions as a function of scaled transverse momentum \(x_T = p_T / K_0\) in \(Au+Au\) collisions at RHIC (using PHENIX \(\pi^0\) data up to \(p_T = 8\) GeV/c) with the suppression factor for pions as a function of scaled longitudinal momentum \(x_L = 2p_L/\sqrt{s}\) in \(p+Pb\) collisions at SPS (using a model calculation based on NA49 proton data). For the \(p_T\) case, they extract \(S(x_T, N)\) from a scaling function \(\Phi(z)\) that fits all centralities, then convert the number of participants \(N\) to an average path length \(L\). For the \(p_L\) case, they parameterize the pion inclusive distribution \(H(x_L, \bar{\nu})\) from the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2002) and convert the average number of collisions \(\bar{\nu}\) to path length \(L\). They also study energy loss via a shift in \(x_T\) or \(x_L\) required to equate in-medium and minimal-medium distributions.

## Key numbers

- For \(AA\) collisions, the suppression factor obeys \(\ln S(x_T, L) = -\Lambda(x_T)(L - 0.71)\) with \(\Lambda(0.6) = 0.25\) and \(\Lambda(0.8) = 0.26\) (path length \(L\) in fm).
- For \(pA\) collisions, the suppression factor obeys \(\ln S(x_L, L) = -\lambda(x_L)(L - 2.1)\) with \(\lambda(0.6) = 0.16\) and \(\lambda(0.8) = 0.18\).
- The fractional shift in \(p_T\) for \(AA\) collisions is \(\xi'_N = 5.26 \times 10^{-4}\) per unit \(N\) (number of participants), or \(\xi'_T = 0.0327\) per fm of path length.
- The fractional shift in \(p_L\) for \(pA\) collisions is \(\xi'_{\bar{\nu}} = 0.0385\) per unit \(\bar{\nu}\), or \(\xi'_L = 0.0185\) per fm of path length.
- The ratio of the two fractional shift coefficients is \(\xi'_T / \xi'_L \approx 1.77\), comparable to the ratio of \(\Lambda\) to \(\lambda\) near \(x_{T,L} \approx 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of nuclear suppression from the transverse to the longitudinal direction, comparing the dense medium of \(AA\) collisions at RHIC with the cold nuclear medium of \(pA\) collisions at SPS. It uses the scaling function approach of Hwa and Yang (2002, 2003) for the \(p_T\) distributions and the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2002) for the \(p_L\) distributions. The exponential form of the suppression factor in path length is similar to the Gerschel-Hufner formula for \(J/\psi\) suppression. The finding that the suppression strengths are comparable suggests that gluon depletion may be the main mechanism for both directions, and it sets the stage for future comparisons when \(p_L\) data in \(AA\) collisions become available.
