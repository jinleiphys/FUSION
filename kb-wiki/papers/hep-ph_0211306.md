# Radiative corrections to low energy neutrino reactions

> We show that the radiative corrections to charged current (CC) nuclear reactions with an electron(positron) in the final state are described by a universal function. The consistency of our treatment of the radiative corrections with the procedure used to extract the value of the axial coupling constant $g_A$ is discussed. To illustrate we apply our results to (anti)neutrino deuterium disintegration and to $pp$ fusion in the sun. The limit of vanishing electron mass is considered, and a simple formula valid for $E_{obs}\gsim 1$ MeV is obtained. The size of the nuclear structure-dependent effects is also discussed. Finally, we consider CC transitions with an electron(positron) in the initial state and discuss some applications to electron capture reactions.

## Key claim

The radiative corrections to charged current nuclear reactions with an electron or positron in the final state are described by a universal function \(g(E_{obs})\) that depends only on the observed energy, but the universality holds only in the one-body approximation and neglects nuclear structure-dependent many-body corrections estimated to enter at the 0.1% level.

## Method

The authors computed the complete one-loop Standard Model radiative corrections to charged current reactions, separating virtual photon exchanges into high-momentum (inner) and low-momentum (outer) contributions, and combined them with bremsstrahlung emission from the charged lepton. They demonstrated that the correction function \(g(E_{obs})\) is identical for neutrino-deuteron disintegration, antineutrino-deuteron disintegration, and the \(pp\) fusion reaction by using time reversal and crossing symmetry arguments, and by showing that the bremsstrahlung matrix element factorizes into a universal leptonic part times a process-dependent hadronic part.

## Key numbers

- The radiative correction to the total cross section for neutrino-deuteron disintegration with a \(pp\) final state at \(E_\nu = 4\) MeV is 3.28% from the exact one-loop calculation, while the truncated formula using the average energy gives 3.27% with \(\langle E \rangle = 2.35\) MeV.
- The radiative correction to the total cross section for the \(pp\) fusion reaction is 3.87% from exact evaluation, while the truncated formula gives 3.86% for an average electron energy of 0.67 MeV.
- The truncation error for neutrino-deuteron disintegration at \(E_\nu \gtrsim 5\) MeV is \(\le 0.01\%\).
- The truncation error for the \(pp\) reaction is estimated at 0.05%.
- The proper treatment of radiative corrections to \(S_{pp}\) lowers the Standard Solar Model prediction for the \({}^{8}\)B neutrino flux \(\Phi(^{8}{\rm B})\) by about 0.6% relative to the currently accepted value, using the relation \(\Phi(^{8}{\rm B}) \sim S_{pp}^{-2.6}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach to radiative corrections previously used in nuclear beta decay by Sirlin (1967) and applied to deuteron disintegration by Kurylov et al. (2001) and Towner (1999), generalizing it to a wide class of low-energy neutrino reactions including antineutrino capture and nuclear fusion. The universal function \(g(E)\) enables simplified calculation of radiative corrections for reactions with narrow electron spectra, and the results are directly applicable to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) measurements and solar model calibrations. The authors note that a complete first-principles computation of the non-perturbative QCD matching constant has yet to be performed, and the uncertainty in \(g_v(E)\) associated with this constant is estimated at about 0.08%.
