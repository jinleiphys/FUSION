# Determination of Asymptotic Normalization Coefficients Based on the Dispersive Optical Model

> A method for determining asymptotic normalization coefficients for removing nucleons from nuclei is proposed. It is based on the use of the dispersive optical model potential. Within the method, the strength parameter of the Hartree- Fock type potential at the Fermi energy is the only one fitting parameter. It was found that adjusting this parameter allows us to achieve a coincidence of the calculated binding energies with the experimental ones accurately enough. Specific calculations were carried out for 17O, 17F, 41Ca, and 41Sc nuclei. An acceptable agreement with the results of other works, which are characterized by a noticeable spread, was achieved.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method for determining asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for nucleon removal from nuclei using the dispersive optical model (DOM) potential, where the strength parameter of the Hartree-Fock type potential at the Fermi energy is the only fitting parameter; the method achieves acceptable agreement with other works that show a noticeable spread in ANC values, but the accuracy depends critically on reproducing experimental binding energies, especially for weakly bound states.

## Method

The radial wave function for a nucleon bound state is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the real part of the dispersive optical model potential, which includes a Hartree-Fock type component and dispersive components derived from the imaginary part via dispersion relations. The single-particle ANC \(b_j\) is extracted from the asymptotic form of the corrected radial wave function \(\bar{u}_j(r)\) at \(r = 15\) fm, and the true ANC \(C_j\) is then calculated as \(C_j = S_j^{1/2} b_j\), where the spectroscopic factor \(S_j\) is computed from the same DOM wave function using the energy-dependent effective mass. The only adjustable parameter is the strength \(V_{\text{HF}}(E_F)\), which is tuned to reproduce the experimental nucleon separation energy within 0.5% for each bound state individually.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{41}\text{Ca}(7/2^-)\) ground state: \(b_{7/2} = 2.77\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\), \(S_{7/2} = 0.806\), \(C_{7/2} = 2.49\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\) (binding energy \(\varepsilon_{7/2} = 8.36\) MeV).

* For \(^{17}\text{O}(5/2^+)\) ground state: \(b_{5/2} = 0.929\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\), \(S_{5/2} = 0.842\), \(C_{5/2} = 0.853\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\) (binding energy \(\varepsilon_{5/2} = 4.14\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the application of the dispersive optical model, previously used by Mahaux and Sartor (1991) and by Nguyen and collaborators for (d,p) transfer reactions on closed-shell nuclei, to the direct calculation of ANCs for removing nucleons from \(^{17}\text{O}\), \(^{17}\text{F}\), \(^{41}\text{Ca}\), and \(^{41}\text{Sc}\). The results agree well with the empirical ANCs extracted from radiative capture data by Huang and collaborators, and with the continuum-shell-model calculations of Okolowicz and collaborators, while systematically exceeding the source-term approach values of Timofeyuk and collaborators. The method enables determination of ANCs for peripheral astrophysical reactions such as \(^{16}\text{O}(p,\gamma)^{17}\text{F}\) in the CNO cycle, and the authors plan to apply it to other nuclei.
