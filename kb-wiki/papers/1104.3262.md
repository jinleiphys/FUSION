# Feeding of the elliptic flow by hard partons

> We propose that in nuclear collisions at the LHC the elliptic flow may get a contribution from leading hard and semihard partons which deposit energy and momentum into the hydrodynamic bulk medium. The crucial effect is that these partons induce wakes which interact and merge if they come together. The contribution to the integrated elliptic flow is estimated with the help of a toy model to about 25% of the observed value and shows strong event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key claim
The elliptic flow observed in nuclear collisions at the LHC may receive a contribution of about 25% from the energy and momentum deposited by leading hard and semihard partons into the hydrodynamic bulk medium, but this estimate comes from a toy model and shows strong event-by-event fluctuations.

## Method
The authors constructed a toy model where fluid streams induced by hard partons are represented as blobs with momenta generated from parametrized gluon production cross sections in proton-proton collisions. Blobs were distributed according to the nuclear overlap function for non-central Pb+Pb collisions, allowed to propagate, and merged when they approached within a distance of 2R_b in their pair center-of-mass frame. After all possible mergers, blobs evaporated pions thermally at T=160 MeV, and the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 was computed from the azimuthal distribution of these pions.

## Key numbers
- The parametrized gluon production cross section used B = 14.7 mbarn/GeV^2, p_0 = 6 GeV, and n = 9.5 for LHC energies.
- The formation time for blobs was set to τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- The kinetic temperature for pion evaporation was T = 160 MeV.
- The integrated v_2 from the simulation reached up to about 0.02.
- This v_2 amounts to up to 25% of the integrated v_2 measured in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV.
- Simulations used stream radii R_b = 1.5 fm and R_b = 2.5 fm as model parameters.

## Context
This work extends the standard interpretation of elliptic flow as arising from anisotropic pressure gradients in the hydrodynamic bulk, and is related in spirit to event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations with hot spots in initial conditions (Andrade 2006, Schenke 2010, Holopainen 2010). The authors note that while simulations with a single jet depositing momentum into bulk matter have been performed (Betz 2008), no hydrodynamic simulations exist that investigate the effect of energy and momentum feeding from many hard partons simultaneously. The proposed mechanism could influence conclusions about fast thermalisation and low viscosity extracted from elliptic flow data.
