# Equilibrium statistical mechanics for incomplete nonextensive statistics

> The incomplete nonextensive statistics in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles is explored in the general case and in a particular case for the ideal gas. By exact analytical results for the ideal gas it is shown that taking the thermodynamic limit, with $z=q/(1-q)$ being an extensive variable of state, the incomplete nonextensive statistics satisfies the requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics. The thermodynamical potential of the statistical ensemble is a homogeneous function of the first degree of the extensive variables of state. In this case, the incomplete nonextensive statistics is equivalent to the usual Tsallis statistics. If $z$ is an intensive variable of state, i.e. the entropic index $q$ is a universal constant, the requirements of the equilibrium thermodynamics are violated.

# Equilibrium statistical mechanics for incomplete nonextensive statistics

## Key claim
The incomplete nonextensive statistics satisfies the requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics (zeroth law, additivity, Euler theorem, Gibbs-Duhem relation) only in the thermodynamic limit and only when the variable $z = q/(1-q)$ is treated as an extensive variable of state; if $z$ is treated as intensive (i.e., $q$ a universal constant), the requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics are violated. In the thermodynamically consistent case, the incomplete nonextensive statistics becomes equivalent to the usual Tsallis statistics.

## Method
The authors derived exact analytical results for the classical ideal gas in both the microcanonical and canonical ensembles, for finite systems and in the thermodynamic limit. They tested the homogeneity of the thermodynamic potential (entropy in the microcanonical ensemble, free energy in the canonical ensemble) under two treatments of the variable $z = q/(1-q)$: as an extensive variable and as an intensive variable. The zeroth law, additivity, Euler theorem, and Gibbs-Duhem relation were checked for each case.

## Key numbers
- For the microcanonical ideal gas with $z$ extensive in the thermodynamic limit, the entropy is $S = k \tilde{z} N \left(1- w^{-1/\tilde{z}}\right)$ where $\tilde{z}=z/N$; the temperature is $T = T_{G} w^{1/\tilde{z}}$; the heat capacity is $C_{E} = k \tilde{z}N w^{-1/\tilde{z}} \left(1+\frac{2\tilde{z}}{3}\right)^{-1}$.
- For the microcanonical ideal gas with $z$ intensive in the thermodynamic limit, the entropy is $S = k (z+1) \left(1- w^{-N/z}\right)$; the temperature is $T = T_{G}\frac{z w^{N/z}}{z+1}$; the heat capacity is $C_{E} = k (z+1) w^{-N/z} \left(1+\frac{2z}{3N}\right)^{-1}$.
- For the canonical ideal gas with $z$ extensive in the thermodynamic limit, the free energy is $F = kT\tilde{z}N (B-1) = \Lambda$; the energy is $\langle H\rangle = \frac{3}{2} NkT \frac{B}{1+\frac{3}{2\tilde{z}}}$; the entropy is $S = k N\tilde{z} \left(1- \frac{B}{1+\frac{3}{2\tilde{z}}}\right)$.
- For the canonical ideal gas with $z$ intensive in the thermodynamic limit, the entropy becomes $S = \mathcal{O}(N^{0})$ (intensive, of order $N^0$), specifically $S = k (z+1) \left(1- \frac{z}{z+1}\tilde{Z}_{G}^{-2/3} e^{-1}\right)$; the energy is $\langle H\rangle = \Lambda$; the heat capacity is $C_{VzN} = \mathcal{O}(N^{0}) = k \tilde{Z}_{G}^{-2/3} e^{-1}$.
- In the Gibbs limit ($z \to \pm\infty$), all incomplete nonextensive quantities recover their Gibbs values: $S|_{z\to\pm\infty} = S_{G}$, $T|_{z\to\pm\infty} = T_{G}$, $C_{E}|_{z\to\pm\infty} = C_{E,G} = 3Nk/2$ for the microcanonical ensemble.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of thermodynamic consistency for nonextensive statistics previously applied to Tsallis statistics by Parvan and Biro (2006) and to Rényi statistics by Parvan and Biro (2009). It directly addresses the incomplete nonextensive statistics introduced by Wang (2001, 2002), which differs from Tsallis statistics only in the norm equation and ensemble averages. The paper shows that the incomplete nonextensive statistics, when thermodynamically consistent, duplicates the Tsallis statistics and leads to no new results, contradicting earlier claims by Wang and collaborators (2004, 2006, 2008) about the zeroth law. The results enable a unified understanding: the parameter $q$ must be treated as related to an extensive variable of state ($z = q/(1-q)$) for thermodynamic consistency, resolving the debate about whether incomplete nonextensive statistics constitutes a distinct framework.
