# Finite size effects on pion spectra in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the pion inclusive transverse momentum distribution assuming thermal equilibrium together with transverse flow and accounting for finite size effects and energy loss at the time of decoupling. We compare to data on mid-rapidity pions produced in central collisions in RHIC at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV. We find that a finite size for the system of emitting particles results in a power-like fall-off of the spectra that follows the data up to larger p_t values, as compared to a simple thermal model.

## Key claim

The authors show that including finite size effects (via a spherical confining boundary with Dirichlet conditions on the wave functions) in a thermal model with transverse flow allows the thermal component of the pion transverse momentum distribution to describe RHIC data up to larger \(p_t\) values than a simple exponential thermal model, but only for \(p_t \lesssim 3\) GeV; at higher \(p_t\) the model deviates from data because fragmentation of fast partons outside the fireball dominates.

## Method

The pion system is treated as a thermal gas of bosons confined within a sphere of radius \(R\) at freeze-out, with wave functions vanishing at the boundary. The invariant transverse momentum distribution is computed by summing the Wigner transforms of the stationary states weighted by a Maxwell-Boltzmann occupation factor, including a radial transverse flow profile with surface velocity \(\beta\) and a constant average Lorentz factor. Energy loss is incorporated via a parametrization of the measured nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) for neutral pions from PHENIX, fitted to a sigmoid-plus-constant form. The model is compared to mid-rapidity charged and neutral pion data from central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* Temperature \(T = 120\) MeV and surface transverse expansion velocity \(\beta = 0.6\) (corresponding to average transverse expansion velocity \(\langle\beta\rangle \simeq 0.5\)) give good description of charged pion data for \(0 < p_t \lesssim 3\) GeV.
* Confining radius \(R\) in the range 8 fm \(\lesssim R \lesssim\) 10 fm yields the best description of data up to \(p_t \sim 3\) GeV.
* For neutral pions, the model describes data for \(R\) between 8 and 10 fm up to \(p_t \sim 3\) GeV, but deviates for \(3 \lesssim p_t < 10\) GeV.
* The parametrization of \(R_{AA}\) uses fit parameters \(A=0.18\), \(B=3.17\), and \(C=0.20\).
* The calculation without finite size effects (plane-wave states) shows a steeper fall-off and does not describe the data over the same range with the same \(T\) and \(\beta\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of finite size (boundary) effects on particle spectra by Ayala et al. (2004, 2005) and Mostafa (2004), which treated pions in a mean-field reflecting boundary scenario. It contrasts with simple thermal models that ignore the finite volume at freeze-out and with the fragmentation-dominated picture at high \(p_t\). The results enable future extension to fermions (protons) and to finite size effects during the partonic phase, including possible influence on the recombination scenario (Wong, 2004).
