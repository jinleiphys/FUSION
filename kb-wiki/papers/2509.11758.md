# Investigating the correlations of bulk properties of hyperon star with dark matter

> The strong gravitational pull of the neutron star leads to the accretion of dark matter (DM) inside the core of the neutron star. The accretion of DM affects the bulk properties of the neutron star. Here, we study how the accretion of WIMP (Weakly Interacting Massive Particles) dark matter particles affects the $Δ-$admixed hyperon star's bulk properties specifically mass, radius, tidal deformability, $f-$mode frequency and moment of inertia. The inclusion of dark matter softens the EOS (equation of state) and reduces the maximum possible mass, canonical radius, canonical tidal deformability, and moment of inertia of canonical star. However, the $f-$mode frequency of the canonical star increases. We find a cubical correlation between the dark matter fermi momenta $k_f^{DM}$ and bulk properties of canonical star.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the accretion of WIMP dark matter into the core of a hyperon star containing Delta-baryons softens the equation of state and reduces maximum mass, canonical radius, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia, while increasing the f-mode frequency. The key hedge is that these results depend on the specific choice of RMF parameter set (NLD, IOPB, G3) and the dark matter Fermi momentum range (0-0.05 GeV), with the NLD parameter set being the only one that satisfies all NICER and GW170817 constraints for the full range studied.

## Method
The authors used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with NLD, IOPB, and G3 parameter sets to model nuclear matter, including hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and Delta-baryons (Delta-, Delta0, Delta+, Delta++) in beta-equilibrium. Dark matter was modeled as neutralino WIMPs of mass 200 GeV interacting with baryons through the Standard Model Higgs boson (mass 125 GeV, coupling y=0.07), with the dark matter content varied by changing the Fermi momentum from 0 to 0.05 GeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the Cowling approximation was used to compute f-mode oscillation frequencies.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass for NLD parameter set with dark matter Fermi momentum k_f^DM = 0.05 GeV: not explicitly given in the truncated text, but the canonical radius R_1.4 decreases by 3.774 km for hyperon stars and 3.888 km for Delta-admixed hyperon stars at this Fermi momentum
- Canonical tidal deformability for NLD parameter set at k_f^DM = 0.04 GeV: 371.858
- Canonical tidal deformability for NLD parameter set at k_f^DM = 0.05 GeV: 192.538
- Dark matter Fermi momentum range studied: 0 to 0.05 GeV
- Neutralino dark matter mass: 200 GeV
- Higgs boson mass: 125 GeV
- Higgs-dark matter coupling constant y: 0.07
- Proton-Higgs form factor f: 0.35
- Delta-baryon mass: 1232 MeV
- Hyperon coupling potentials: U_Lambda = -28 MeV, U_Sigma = +30 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Jena, 2025) on the effects of Delta-baryons on neutron star bulk properties, now incorporating dark matter accretion. It builds on prior work by Sandin (2009), Ciarcelluti (2011), Leung (2011), and Das (2021) who found that dark matter decreases mass, radius, and tidal deformability of neutron stars, and by Das (2021) who found that dark matter increases f-mode frequency. The results are constrained by NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0952-0607, and PSR J1810+1714, as well as the GW170817 event tidal deformability constraint (Lambda_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120}). The cubical correlation found between dark matter Fermi momentum and bulk properties enables future predictions of dark matter content in neutron stars from observable quantities.
