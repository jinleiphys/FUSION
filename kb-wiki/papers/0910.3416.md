# A model for multifragmentation in heavy-ion reactions

> From an experimental point of view, clear signatures of multifragmentation have been detected by different experiments. On the other hand, from a theoretical point of view, many different models, built on the basis of totally different and often even contrasting assumptions, have been provided to explain them. In this contribution we show the capabilities and the shortcomings of one of this models, a QMD code developed by us and coupled to the nuclear de-excitation module taken from the multipurpose transport and interaction code FLUKA, in reproducing the multifragmentation observations recently reported by the INDRA collaboration for the reaction Nb + Mg at a 30 MeV/A projectile bombarding energy. As far as fragment production is concerned, we also briefly discuss the isoscaling technique by considering reactions characterized by a different isospin asymmetry, and we explain how the QMD + FLUKA model can be applied to obtain information on the slope of isotopic yield ratios, which is crucially related to the symmetry energy of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The QMD+FLUKA model can reproduce multifragmentation observations from the INDRA collaboration for the Nb + Mg reaction at 30 MeV/A projectile bombarding energy, but only with the caveat that the comparison is limited by experimental selection cuts and that the model overestimates the size of the reconstructed source for the most central collisions when using the same a-posteriori reconstruction procedure as the experiment.

## Method

The authors coupled their own Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code, which simulates the fast dynamical stage of heavy-ion collisions up to 200-300 fm/c, with the statistical de-excitation module from the FLUKA Monte Carlo code to describe the subsequent decay of excited primary fragments. They applied this QMD+FLUKA model to the reaction Nb + Mg at 30 MeV/A and compared the simulated event distributions and source properties to experimental data from the INDRA detector, using the same event selection cuts on transverse energy and charged-particle multiplicity. They also applied the isoscaling technique to reactions Ar + Ni, Ar + Fe, and Ca + Ni at 45 MeV/A to extract the isoscaling parameter A_coeff related to the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- For the Nb + Mg reaction at 30 MeV/A, the average transverse energy <E_transv> in region T1 (peripheral) was 74.8 (simulation) vs 72.9 +/- 10.1 (experiment); in region T3 (central) it was 155.1 (simulation) vs 176.4 +/- 13.8 (experiment).
- The average charge of the largest residual <Z_maxres> in region T3 was 31.1 (simulation) vs 30.5 +/- 2.8 (experiment).
- The isoscaling parameter A_coeff from simulation was 0.18 for Ar + Ni / Ca + Ni and 0.31 for Ar + Fe / Ca + Ni at 45 MeV/A.
- For central collisions of Ca + Ni, the yield of emitted protons was larger than the yield of emitted neutrons by 20%; for Ar + Ni and Ar + Fe, the proton yield was lower than the neutron yield by 10-15%.
- The source charge <Z_s> in region T3 reconstructed from simulation was 48.5 vs 45.1 +/- 2 from experiment; the source mass <A_s> was 109.5 vs 101.3 +/- 4.6.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical description of multifragmentation using QMD approaches, which preserve nucleon-nucleon correlations, in contrast to statistical models like the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) of Botvina, Bondorf, and Gupta that assume thermal equilibrium at freeze-out. The results are compared directly to experimental data from the INDRA collaboration (Manduci, 2009) for Nb + Mg reactions and to isoscaling studies by Shetty (2007) and Tsang (2001) for asymmetric systems. The model enables further studies of the nuclear Equation of State at subnormal densities, particularly the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and can be applied to investigate isospin distillation effects and bimodality as signatures of a phase transition.
