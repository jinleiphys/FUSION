# Isoscaling and the nuclear EOS

> Experiments with rare isotopes are shedding light on the role isospin plays in the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter, and isoscaling -an straight-forward comparison of reactions with different isospin- could deliver valuable information about it. In this work we test this assertion pragmatically by comparing molecular dynamics simulations of isoscaling reactions using different equations of state and looking for changes in the isoscaling parameters; to explore the possibility of isoscaling carrying information from the hot-and-dense stage of the reaction, we perform our study in confined and expanding systems. Our results indicate that indeed isoscaling can help us learn about the nuclear EoS, but only in some range of excitation energies.

## Key claim

Isoscaling can help learn about the nuclear equation of state (EoS), but only in a limited range of excitation energies; at high excitation energies the isoscaling parameters converge to a purely probabilistic "symmentropic" limit and carry no information about the EoS.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations of disassembling nuclear systems corresponding to merged Ca+Ca collisions, using two parameterizations of the Illinois potential that yield different nuclear compressibilities (250 MeV and 535 MeV). They constructed fragment yield matrices for systems with (N,Z) = (40,40) and (56,40) at various densities and excitation energies, both in confined (spherical container) and free-expanding conditions, and extracted the isoscaling parameter α by fitting the ratio of yields to the power law Y₂(N,Z) ∝ Y₁(N,Z) exp(αN + βZ).

## Key numbers

- The medium model yields a symmetry energy coefficient C_sym = -25.08 MeV, while the stiff model yields C_sym = -34.07 MeV (experimental value: -23.7 MeV).
- The compressibility values used are ~250 MeV for the medium model and ~535 MeV for the stiff model.
- At a fixed density of ρ = 0.007 fm⁻³, the medium model produces α values exceeding those of the stiff model by as much as ~15% at low excitation energies.
- The symmentropic limit for the isoscaling parameter α between the two systems is α = ln(1.16) = 0.154.
- Excitation energies studied range from -5 to 8 MeV/A; number densities range from 0.001 fm⁻³ to 0.007 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isoscaling using classical molecular dynamics (Dorso et al., various years) and nuclear percolation models, which showed that isoscaling can arise from purely probabilistic effects. It directly tests the interpretation proposed by Tsang et al. (2001) and Ono et al. that α is connected to the symmetry energy via α = 4C_sym[(Z₁/A₁)² - (Z₂/A₂)²]/T. The results enable experimentalists to focus isoscaling studies of the symmetry energy on low-energy collisions, and motivate theoretical work to decouple the symmentropic and nuclear contributions to the isoscaling parameters.
