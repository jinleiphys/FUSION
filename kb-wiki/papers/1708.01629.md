# The s-process nucleosynthesis: impact of the uncertainties in the nuclear physics determined by Monte Carlo variations

> We investigated the impact of uncertainties in neutron-capture and weak reactions (on heavy elements) on the s-process nucleosynthesis in low-mass stars and massive stars using a Monte-Carlo based approach. We performed extensive nuclear reaction network calculations that include newly evaluated temperature-dependent upper and lower limits for the individual reaction rates. We found beta-decay rate uncertainties affect only a few nuclei near s-process branchings, whereas most of the uncertainty in the final abundances is caused by uncertainties in the neutron capture rates. We suggest a list of uncertain rates as candidates for improved measurement by future experiments.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo simulations show that uncertainties in neutron-capture rates dominate the uncertainty in final s-process abundances for both low-mass AGB stars and massive stars, while beta-decay rate uncertainties affect only a few nuclei near s-process branchings; the authors identify specific reaction rates with Pearson correlation coefficients above 0.65 as candidates for improved measurement.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo nuclear reaction network calculations using simplified 1D stellar evolution trajectories for a 3 solar mass AGB star (main s-process) and a 25 solar mass massive star (weak s-process), both at solar metallicity. They applied newly evaluated temperature-dependent upper and lower limits for individual neutron-capture and beta-decay reaction rates, using experimental uncertainties for ground-state contributions and a factor of 2 for excited-state uncertainties, with a uniform random distribution between limits for each variation. The Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient was calculated between rate variations and final abundance changes to identify key reactions, using a threshold of 0.65.

## Key numbers

- Main s-process: abundance uncertainties were evaluated for 116 stable s-process isotopes up to bismuth, selecting isotopes contributing at least 10% to total elemental abundance
- Weak s-process: abundance uncertainties were evaluated for stable s-process isotopes up to mass number approximately 90
- Correlation threshold: 0.65 (chosen to account for numerical uncertainties, based on literature suggesting 0.7 indicates strong correlation)
- Key rate correlations for main s-process: Ge-72(n,gamma)Ge-73 with Ge-72 at r = -0.93; Ge-74(n,gamma)Ge-75 with Ge-74 at r = -0.97; Se-78(n,gamma)Se-79 with Se-78 at r = -0.96; Kr-84(n,gamma)Kr-85 with Kr-84 at r = -0.99; Kr-85(n,gamma)Kr-86 with Kr-86 at r = 0.88
- Key rate correlations for weak s-process: Ge-72(n,gamma)Ge-73 with Ge-72 at r = -0.85; Se-78(n,gamma)Se-79 with Se-78 at r = -0.71; Kr-85(n,gamma)Kr-86 with Kr-86 at r = 0.84
- Beta-decay uncertainty: factor 10 maximum at high temperature, approximately factor 2 at stellar burning temperatures

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo and statistical analysis approach applied to s-process nucleosynthesis by previous studies such as Iliadis et al. (2015) and Rauscher et al. (2016), moving beyond the already-studied neutron source and poison reactions to systematically evaluate the impact of neutron-capture and beta-decay rate uncertainties. The identified key reaction rates, particularly Ge-72(n,gamma)Ge-73, Se-78(n,gamma)Se-79, and Kr-85(n,gamma)Kr-86, provide experimental targets for improved measurements that could reduce abundance uncertainties in both the main and weak s-process, with more detailed analysis presented in Nishimura (2017) for massive stars and upcoming work by Cescutti (2017) for the main s-process.
