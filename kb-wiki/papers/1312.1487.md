# Explanation of hadron transverse-momentum spectra in heavy-ion collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV within chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model

> Chemical non-equilibrium version of the statistical hadronization model combined with a single-freeze-out scenario is used to analyze the transverse-momentum spectra of pions, kaons and protons produced in heavy-ion collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV. With the statistical parameters found in earlier studies of hadronic ratios and two new geometric parameters determined by the absolute normalization and slopes of the spectra, we obtain a very good agreement between the data and the model predictions. In particular, the enhancement of pions observed at the very low transverse-momenta is very well reproduced. In the chemical non-equilibrium approach, this effect may be naturally interpreted as the onset of pion condensation in heavy-ion collisions. We further stress that the chemical non-equilibrium explains not only the proton yield but the proton spectra as well.

## Key claim

The chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (NEQ SHM) combined with the Cracow single-freeze-out scenario simultaneously explains both the proton yield anomaly and the low-transverse-momentum enhancement of pions in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV, where the low-pT pion enhancement is interpreted as a signature of the onset of pion Bose-Einstein condensation.

## Method

The authors used the chemical non-equilibrium version of the statistical hadronization model (NEQ SHM) with thermodynamic parameters (temperature T, quark phase-space occupancies gamma_q and gamma_s) taken from earlier fits to hadronic ratios by Petran et al. (2013). They combined this with the Cracow single-freeze-out model, which assumes a cylindrically symmetric, boost-invariant freeze-out hypersurface with Hubble-like flow, implemented via the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo code. Two geometric parameters, the transverse size r_max and the invariant time tau_f, were fitted to the measured transverse-momentum spectra of pions and kaons, while proton spectra were treated as predictions.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions (c=0%–5%): T = 138.0 MeV, gamma_q = 1.63, gamma_s = 2.05; geometric parameters tau_f = 7.68 fm, r_max = 11.7 fm.
- For semi-peripheral collisions (c=30%–40%): T = 139.85 MeV, gamma_q = 1.62, gamma_s = 2.0; geometric parameters tau_f = 4.83 fm, r_max = 7.5 fm.
- The non-equilibrium chemical potential of pions, mu_pi = 2T ln(gamma_q) ≈ 134.9 MeV, is very close to the neutral pion mass m_pi0 ≈ 134.98 MeV.
- For the equilibrium model (EQ SHM) comparison: T = 165.6 MeV; geometric parameters for c=0%–5%: tau_f = 8.5 fm, r_max = 11.0 fm; for c=30%–40%: tau_f = 5.5 fm, r_max = 7.0 fm.
- The low-transverse-momentum pion spectra show enhancement by about 25%–50% with respect to predictions of various thermal and hydrodynamic models.

## Context

This work extends the chemical non-equilibrium approach previously advocated by Rafelski and collaborators (2011) for hadronic abundances, and by Petran et al. (2013) for hadronic yields at LHC energies, to the description of transverse-momentum spectra. It contrasts with the equilibrium thermal model (EQ SHM) used by Kalweit (2012) and Stachel (2013), which fails to reproduce the proton yield at LHC. The results suggest that the low-pT pion enhancement may be connected to a coherent contribution to pion production reported by ALICE (Abelev 2013), and open the question of whether gluon condensation, as suggested by Blaizot (2011, 2013) in the context of QGP thermalization, may also be related.
