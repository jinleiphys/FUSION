# Relativistic Causal Hydrodynamics Derived from Boltzmann Equation: a novel reduction theoretical approach

> We derive the second-order hydrodynamic equation and the microscopic formulae of the relaxation times as well as the transport coefficients systematically from the relativistic Boltzmann equation. Our derivation is based on a novel development of the renormalization-group method, a powerful reduction theory of dynamical systems, which has been applied successfully to derive the non-relativistic second-order hydrodynamic equation Our theory nicely gives a compact expression of the deviation of the distribution function in terms of the linearized collision operator, which is different from those used as an ansatz in the conventional fourteen-moment method. It is confirmed that the resultant microscopic expressions of the transport coefficients coincide with those derived in the Chapman-Enskog expansion method. Furthermore, we show that the microscopic expressions of the relaxation times have natural and physically plausible forms. We prove that the propagating velocities of the fluctuations of the hydrodynamical variables do not exceed the light velocity, and hence our seconder-order equation ensures the desired causality. It is also confirmed that the equilibrium state is stable for any perturbation described by our equation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equation from the Boltzmann equation using a renormalization-group (RG) method, claiming that the resulting equation ensures causality (propagation speeds do not exceed light velocity) and that the equilibrium state is stable, but the derivation relies on the assumption that the macroscopic frame vector has no time dependence and that the spatial inhomogeneity serves as the sole perturbation parameter.

## Method

The authors apply the doublet scheme of the renormalization-group method to the relativistic Boltzmann equation, expanding the distribution function perturbatively in the Knudsen number (the ratio of mean free path to macroscopic length) around a local equilibrium solution. They introduce a time-like Lorentz vector (macroscopic frame vector) to define a covariant coarse-grained coordinate system, then construct the mesoscopic dynamics by incorporating excited modes as additional components of the invariant manifold, using the linearized collision operator to derive the deviation of the distribution function without the ad-hoc ansatz of the fourteen-moment method.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficients derived microscopically coincide with those from the Chapman-Enskog expansion method (no specific numerical values given in the text).
- The particle-number density, internal energy, and pressure are expressed in terms of modified Bessel functions K_2 and K_3, with sums over k from 1 to infinity involving terms like (km/T)^{-1} K_2(km/T) and (km/T)^{-2} K_2(km/T) (Eqs. for n, e, P).
- The natural unit system is used: hbar = c = k_B = 1.
- The Minkowski metric is g^{mu nu} = diag(+1, -1, -1, -1).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic doublet scheme developed by Tsumura and Kunihiro (2013) to the relativistic case with full quantum statistics (bosons and fermions) as well as classical statistics. It corrects an earlier attempt by Tsumura and Kunihiro (2009) that used the Israel-Stewart fourteen-moment ansatz, which did not solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation. The resulting equation is intended to replace the Israel-Stewart equation (Israel, 1979) and other second-order formulations, providing a microscopically consistent causal hydrodynamic description for applications to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and high-energy astrophysics.
