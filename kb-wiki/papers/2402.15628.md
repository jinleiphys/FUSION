# Toward extracting the scattering phase shift from integrated correlation functions II: a relativistic lattice field theory model

> In present work, a relativistic relation that connects the difference of interacting and non-interacting integrated two-particle correlation functions in finite volume to infinite volume scattering phase shift through an integral is derived. We show that the difference of integrated finite volume correlation functions converge rapidly to its infinite volume limit as the size of periodic box is increased. The fast convergence of our proposed formalism is illustrated by analytic solutions of a contact interaction model, the perturbation theory calculation, and also Monte Carlo simulation of a complex $ϕ^4$ lattice field theory model.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic relation connecting the difference of interacting and non-interacting integrated two-particle correlation functions in finite volume to the infinite-volume scattering phase shift through an integral, and demonstrates that this difference converges rapidly to its infinite-volume limit as the periodic box size increases, even at short Euclidean times where the conventional Luscher formula approach suffers from signal-to-noise problems.

## Method

The author extends a previously proposed nonrelativistic formalism (Guo, 2023) to a relativistic framework in 1+1 dimensions, deriving the infinite-volume limit of the integrated two-particle correlation function difference and its relation to the scattering phase shift. The fast convergence is illustrated through three approaches: analytic solutions of a contact interaction model (complex phi^4 theory), leading-order perturbation theory calculations, and Monte Carlo simulations of the complex phi^4 lattice field theory model using the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The signal-to-noise ratio for two-nucleon correlation functions at large Euclidean times behaves as exp[-(m_N - 3/2 m_pi) tau], where m_N and m_pi are nucleon and pion masses
- The energy level splitting in large volumes scales as Delta E approximately equal to (1/m_N)(2pi/L)^2, where L is the spatial extension of the lattice
- For the non-interacting Monte Carlo test with kappa = 0.1213, lambda = 0, T = 100, and L = 80, the single particle mass was measured as m = 0.3502 +/- 0.0032
- The contact interaction model uses parameters m = 1 and V_0 = 5 with box sizes L = 3, 5, and 10

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic formalism proposed by Guo (2023) to a relativistic framework, offering an alternative to the conventional two-step Luscher formula approach that has faced challenges in two-nucleon lattice QCD calculations due to signal-to-noise problems and dense energy spectra at large volumes. The approach is also distinct from the HAL QCD collaboration potential method (Ishii et al., 2007; Aoki et al., 2013), which has shown disagreement with Luscher formula results in the so-called two-nucleon controversy. The formalism enables extraction of scattering phase shifts from integrated correlation functions at short Euclidean times, potentially overcoming the ground-state saturation requirements that have prevented Luscher formula calculations with pion masses below 300 MeV.
