# Dileptons from a Quark Gluon Plasma with Finite Baryon Density

> We investigate the effects of a baryon-antibaryon asymmetry on the spectrum of dileptons radiating from a quark gluon plasma. We demonstrate the existence of a new set of processes in this regime. The dilepton production rate from the corresponding diagrams is shown to be as important as that obtained from the usual quark-antiquark annihilation.

## Key claim

At finite baryon chemical potential, a new set of diagrams for dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma becomes nonzero due to broken charge conjugation invariance; the gluon-gluon fusion channel \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) can dominate the differential production rate over the standard \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation at low invariant mass (0.5–1.5 GeV), but the calculation is restricted to back-to-back dileptons (\(\vec{p}=0\)) and uses a conservative temperature of 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors used equilibrium thermal field theory in the imaginary-time formalism with a finite quark chemical potential \(\mu\) (assumed equal for three massless flavors \(u,d,s\)) and zero gluon chemical potential. They derived the three-loop photon self-energy from the two-gluon–photon vertex, applied the Braaten–Pisarski–Yuan (BPY) identity to extract the imaginary part corresponding to the \(gg \rightarrow \gamma^*\) cut, and numerically integrated the resulting expression for back-to-back dileptons (\(\vec{p}=0\)) over the internal momenta \(q\) and \(q'\).

## Key numbers

- Temperature \(T = 400\ \text{MeV}\) used for the numerical evaluation.
- Two values of quark chemical potential considered: \(\mu = 0.1T\) and \(\mu = 0.5T\).
- The strong coupling constant is \(\alpha_s(T) = 6\pi / [27 \ln(T/50\ \text{MeV})]\).
- The differential production rate from \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) is compared with the tree-level \(q\bar{q}\) rate for dilepton invariant masses from \(0.5\ \text{GeV}\) to \(2.5\ \text{GeV}\).
- The new process dominates by "orders of magnitude" between 0.5 and 1.5 GeV (no exact numerical ratio given in the text).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma that considered only \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation (Shuryak 1980, Kajantie 1986, Dumitru 1993) and chemical non-equilibrium with gluon excess (Lin 2000). It introduces the effect of a finite baryon chemical potential, which breaks charge conjugation invariance and enables processes forbidden by Furry's theorem at zero density. The results suggest that the new \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) channel could be a significant signal for baryon-rich plasma at RHIC and LHC, but the authors note that the calculation should be extended to arbitrary pair momentum and that other cuts (with infrared-divergent internal gluon lines) require hard-thermal-loop resummation.
