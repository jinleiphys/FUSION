# Deconfinement and Hadron Resonance Gas for Heavy Quarks

> 11 pages, LaTeX, 4 figures, Invited talk at Workshop on Criticality in QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas, July 29-31, 2020, Wroclaw, Poland, to be published in the proceedings, typos corrected

## Key claim

The deconfinement transition in 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses coincides with the chiral crossover, as indicated by the peak in the entropy of a static quark around 156-160 MeV, but the renormalized Polyakov loop itself behaves smoothly and cannot define a deconfinement temperature in QCD unlike in pure SU(N) gauge theories.

## Method

The author analyzes lattice QCD results for the renormalized Polyakov loop and its susceptibilities using both conventional renormalization and gradient flow methods, with calculations performed using the HISQ action for 2+1 flavor QCD with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 161 MeV in the continuum limit. The entropy of a static quark is obtained from the temperature derivative of the static quark free energy, and charm-baryon number correlations up to fourth order are studied through derivatives of the QCD pressure with respect to charm and baryon chemical potentials. These lattice results are compared to predictions from the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, which includes static-light and static-strange hadrons as well as charm hadrons from the Particle Data Group and quark model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass used in the lattice calculations: 161 MeV (light quark masses corresponding to this value in the continuum limit)
- Chiral crossover temperature for physical quark masses: 156.5 MeV (used for rescaling HRG results)
- Chiral crossover temperature for the lattice setup with m_l = m_s/20: 159.5 MeV (3 MeV upward shift from physical value)
- Chiral phase transition temperature in the limit m_l → 0: 132 MeV with +6/-3 MeV uncertainties
- The HRG model can describe the static quark free energy only for temperatures below 140 MeV
- Casimir scaling for Polyakov loops in higher representations holds for temperatures above 300 MeV
- Polyakov loop susceptibilities show a peak around 180-200 MeV for flow time f = 3f_0
- The ratio chi_13^{BC}/chi_22^{BC} for charm-baryon correlations equals 1 in the HRG regime (below crossover) and approaches 3 at high temperatures where charm quarks are the relevant degrees of freedom
- Charm quarks become the dominant degrees of freedom only for temperatures above 200 MeV

## Context

This invited talk extends the author's prior lattice QCD work on Polyakov loops and deconfinement (Bazavov 2016) and compares results with pure SU(2) and SU(3) gauge theories (Kaczmarek 2002, 2004; Digal 2003). The analysis of static quark entropy builds on earlier studies of deconfinement in 3-flavor and 2-flavor QCD (Petreczky 2004; Kaczmarek 2005) and shows that the chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide for physical quark masses, contrary to the behavior in pure gauge theories. The charm-baryon correlation analysis (Bazavov 2014) enables future work on whether charm hadron-like excitations persist above the crossover temperature, as proposed by Mukherjee (2015), and whether the chiral phase transition at vanishing light quark masses leaves an imprint on the Polyakov loop, as suggested by recent calculations (Clarke 2020).
