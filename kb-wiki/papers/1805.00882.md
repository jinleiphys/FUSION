# Holographic Bjorken flow of a hot and dense fluid in the vicinity of a critical point

> In this paper we use the gauge/gravity duality to perform the first systematic study of the onset of hydrodynamic behavior in a hot and dense far-from-equilibrium strongly coupled relativistic fluid with a critical point. By employing a top-down holographic construction that stems from string theory, we numerically obtain the full nonlinear evolution of the far-from-equilibrium system undergoing a Bjorken expansion and address the following question: how does hydrodynamic behavior emerge in the vicinity of a critical point in the phase diagram? For the top-down holographic system analyzed in the present work, we find that the approach to hydrodynamics is strongly affected by the presence of the critical point: the closer the ratio between the chemical potential and the temperature is to its critical value, the longer it takes for the system to be well described by the equations of viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the onset of hydrodynamic behavior in a strongly coupled relativistic fluid undergoing Bjorken expansion is significantly delayed as the ratio of chemical potential to temperature approaches its critical value at the critical point in the phase diagram, with the delay quantified by the relative increase in hydrodynamization time compared to the zero-chemical-potential case.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the full nonlinear Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton equations of motion in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter spacetime, using the 1RCBH (one-charged black hole) top-down holographic model that stems from string theory. They impose Bjorken boost-invariant flow symmetry and evolve the system from far-from-equilibrium initial conditions spanning different values of the chemical potential to temperature ratio, extracting the energy density, pressure anisotropy, charge density, and scalar condensate as functions of proper time.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in the 1RCBH model occurs at \((\mu/T)_c = \pi/\sqrt{2}\).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) for this holographic system.
- Hydrodynamization times are defined using a tolerance of \(tol = 0.01\) (1%) and \(tol = 0.1\) (10%) in the relative difference between numerical and Navier-Stokes pressure anisotropy.
- The relative increase in hydrodynamization time \(\Delta w_{\text{hydro}}^{(\varepsilon/\Lambda)}\) is plotted as a function of \(\mu/T\) up to the critical value, showing monotonic growth as the critical point is approached.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of Bjorken flow in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma at zero chemical potential by Chesler (2009), Heller (2011), and Jankowski (2014) to finite chemical potential with a critical point. It also builds on prior analyses of the 1RCBH model's thermodynamics and hydrodynamics by DeWolfe (2011), quasinormal modes by Finazzo (2016), and homogeneous isotropization by Critelli (2017). The results enable future investigations of hydrodynamic attractor behavior in the presence of a critical point and motivate more realistic bottom-up holographic models for the QCD critical point search in heavy ion collisions.
