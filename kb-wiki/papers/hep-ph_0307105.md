# Nuclear modification of structure functions in lepton scattering

> We discuss nuclear structure functions in lepton scattering including neutrino reactions. First, the determination of nuclear parton distribution functions is explained by using the data of electron and muon deep inelastic scattering and those of Drell-Yan processes. Second, NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly is discussed by focusing on nuclear corrections in the iron target. Third, we show that the HERMES effect, which indicates nuclear modification of the longitudinal-transverse structure function ratio, should exist at large x with small Q^2 in spite of recent experimental denials at small x.

## Key claim
Nuclear corrections to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, specifically the difference between up- and down-valence quark nuclear modifications (ε_v), could partially explain the NuTeV sin²θ_W anomaly, but the contribution is not a major mechanism when NuTeV kinematics are taken into account; the HERMES effect (nuclear modification of the longitudinal-transverse structure function ratio) should exist at large x with small Q² despite recent experimental denials at small x.

## Method
The author performed a χ² analysis of electron and muon deep inelastic scattering data and Drell-Yan data to determine nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) using a parametrization with weight functions w_i(x,A,Z) at Q₀²=1 GeV². For the NuTeV anomaly, the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation was expanded in terms of small correction factors ε_n, ε_v, ε_s, and ε_c, with ε_v estimated from charge and baryon-number conservation constraints and from the χ² analysis results. The HERMES effect was investigated using a convolution model with a spectral function and projection operators to derive the transverse-longitudinal admixture from nucleon Fermi motion perpendicular to the virtual photon direction.

## Key numbers
- NuTeV measured sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), compared to the global analysis value 0.2227 ± 0.0004 (on-shell)
- The ε_v contribution to sin²θ_W was estimated to be of order 0.005 (the NuTeV deviation) for a simple x average, but significantly reduced when NuTeV kinematics were taken into account
- Nuclear modification of R(x,Q²) was calculated for nitrogen-14 at Q² = 0.5, 1, 10, and 100 GeV², with the largest effects at small Q² (0.5–1 GeV²) due to the admixture term proportional to p_{N⊥}²/Q²
- The NPDF analysis used data for nuclei in the range 2 ≤ A ≤ 208 at Q₀² = 1 GeV²

## Context
This paper extends the work of Eskola et al. (2001) and HKM (2001) on nuclear parton distribution functions, and addresses the NuTeV sin²θ_W anomaly reported by the NuTeV collaboration (2002). It investigates nuclear corrections to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation as an alternative to new physics explanations, following studies by McFarland et al. (2002) and Kulagin (2002). The HERMES effect analysis contradicts the CCFR/NuTeV (2001) and later HERMES (2002) results at small x, proposing instead that the effect should be observable at large x with small Q², potentially testable by JLab experiments.
