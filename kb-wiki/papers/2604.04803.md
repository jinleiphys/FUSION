# Glueballs, Constituent Gluons and Instantons

> We present a constituent two-gluon description of the lowest-lying glueball states in pure Yang--Mills theory, calibrated against quenched lattice results. The framework incorporates an instanton-induced dynamical gluon mass, Casimir-scaled adjoint confinement, the short-distance adjoint Coulomb interaction, and instanton-induced central and tensor forces. The scalar $0^{++}$ glueball is found to be exceptionally compact, with a radius of order the instanton size, $ρ\sim \frac 13\,\mathrm{fm}$, consistent with lattice indications. By contrast, the tensor $2^{++}$ state remains spatially extended due to the centrifugal barrier. We also discuss the role of $S$-$D$ mixing. A semiclassical analysis further supports Regge behavior for excited states, in agreement with lattice results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a constituent two-gluon description of glueball states in pure Yang-Mills theory, claiming that most glueball states (except the lowest scalar and pseudoscalar) can be described by a nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with a screened adjoint confining potential and a heavy constituent gluon mass of about 900 MeV, while the lowest scalar glueball requires a separate relativistic Bethe-Salpeter treatment due to its exceptionally compact size.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for two effective constituent gluons in their center-of-mass frame, incorporating an adjoint Coulomb interaction (9/4 times stronger than quark-antiquark), a Casimir-scaled screened confining potential with string tension sigma_8 = (9/4) sigma_3 where sigma_3 = 0.18 GeV^2, and instanton-induced central and spin-dependent forces parameterized by an enhancement factor eta relative to the dilute instanton liquid model. They solve the resulting Schrodinger equation for various J^PC channels and compare the spectrum to quenched lattice results from Meyer (2004) and Morningstar (1999), fitting the constituent gluon mass to reproduce the higher scalar states before adding short-range interactions for the lowest states.

## Key numbers

- Constituent gluon mass fitted to m_g = 0.90 GeV, with m_g0 = 0.36 GeV at eta = 1 and eta = 6.25 for the full model
- Scalar glueball ground state mass from lattice: M(0^{++}) = 1.475 GeV with errors (30)(65)
- Tensor glueball mass from lattice: M(2^{++}) = 2.150 GeV with errors (30)(100)
- Pseudoscalar glueball masses from lattice: M(0^{-+})^0 = 2.250 GeV (60)(100) and M(0^{-+})^1 = 3.370 GeV (150)(150)
- Instanton size rho ~ 1/3 fm, with scalar glueball radius predicted to be about 0.2 fm from instanton liquid model
- Scalar glueball r.m.s. radii from Schrodinger equation: 2.03 GeV^{-1} (n=0), 5.0 GeV^{-1} (n=1), 6.26 GeV^{-1} (n=2), 6.25 GeV^{-1} (n=3)
- Adjoint string tension sigma_8 = (9/4) * 0.18 GeV^2
- Local interaction strength needed to reproduce scalar ground state: G ~ 38 GeV^{-2}
- Pseudoscalar WKB baseline mass at eta=1: M^{WKB}_{0^{-+}} = 3.33 GeV with turning point r_+ = 1.27 fm

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model calculations of Schafer (1994) and Shuryak (2021) to a full constituent-gluon Hamiltonian framework for glueball spectroscopy, calibrated against quenched lattice results from Morningstar (1999) and Meyer (2004). It contrasts with holographic QCD approaches (Gursoy 2007) and functional methods (Alkofer 2004, Fischer 2012) by providing a nonrelativistic potential model that reproduces the "normal" excited glueball states while treating the exceptionally compact scalar ground state separately via a relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation. The results enable future inclusion of glueballs in light-front formulations of hadron structure and provide a framework for understanding the interplay between confinement-driven Regge behavior and channel-dependent instanton dynamics across different J^PC sectors.
