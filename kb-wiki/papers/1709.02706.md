# Strangeness Production and Color Deconfinement

> The relative multiplicities for hadron production in different high energy collisions are in general well described by an ideal gas of all hadronic resonances, except that under certain conditions, strange particle rates are systematically reduced. We show that the suppression factor gamma_s, accounting for reduced strange particle rates in pp, pA and AA collisions at different collision energies, becomes a universal function when expressed in terms of the initial entropy density s_0 or the initial temperature T of the produced thermal medium. It is found that gamma_s increases from about 0.5 to 1.0 in a narrow temperature range around the quark-hadron transition temperature T_c = 160 MeV. Strangeness suppression thus disappears with the onset of color deconfinement; subsequently, full equilibrium resonance gas behavior is attained.

# Strangeness Production and Color Deconfinement

## Key claim

The strangeness suppression factor γ_s becomes a universal function of initial entropy density or initial temperature across pp, pA, and AA collisions, with suppression disappearing in a narrow temperature range around T_c ≈ 160 MeV; the abstract's claim of "universal function" depends on using the Bjorken relation to convert collision energy into thermal variables, and the AA data at lowest temperatures (AGS) are considered estimates only due to finite baryochemical potential effects.

## Method

The authors compile measured values of the strangeness suppression factor γ_s and hadronization temperatures from pp, pA, and AA collisions at various center-of-mass energies. They use the Bjorken relation to convert measured charged-particle multiplicities per unit rapidity into initial entropy density s_0, then employ finite-temperature lattice QCD results to map s_0 to initial temperature T, producing γ_s as a function of T.

## Key numbers

- γ_s increases from about 0.5 to 1.0 in a narrow temperature range around T_c ≈ 160 MeV
- The transition in entropy density occurs around s_0 ≈ 3 fm⁻³
- For pp collisions, γ_s(s) = 1 − 0.5595 exp(−0.0242 s^{1/4})
- For AA collisions, γ_s(s) = 1 − 0.606 exp(−0.0209 √(A√s))
- Hadronization temperature is T_c ≈ 160 ± 10 MeV for all high collision energies and configurations
- Multiplicity fits: (dN/dy)^{AA} = 0.7613 (√s)^{0.3} + 0.0534; (dN/dy)^{pp} = 0.797 (√s)^{0.22} + 0.04123
- Transverse radii used: R_pp = 0.8 fm, R_AA = 1.25 A^{1/3} fm

## Context

This work extends the long-standing observation by Rafelski and Muller (1982) that quark-gluon plasma formation would enhance strangeness production, and the subsequent statistical hadronization model of Letessier, Rafelski, and Tounsi (1994) that introduced γ_s as a suppression parameter. The authors show that earlier separate treatments of strangeness suppression in pp and AA collisions (as functions of collision energy) can be unified into a single curve when expressed in terms of thermal variables, confirming theoretical predictions by Castorina and Satz (2013, 2016) that strangeness equilibration should occur with increasing medium lifetime. This enables using γ_s as a direct probe of whether the initial medium was in a deconfined state, with potential applications to multiplicity-dependent studies at LHC energies.
