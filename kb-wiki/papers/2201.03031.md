# Biexciton-like quartet condensates in an electron-hole liquid

> We theoretically study the ground-state properties and the condensations of exciton-like Cooper pairs and biexciton-like Cooper quartets in an electron-hole system. Applying the variational approach associated based on the quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) model to the four-component fermionic system consisting of spin-1/2 electrons and spin-1/2 holes, we show how Cooper pairs and quartet correlations appear in the equation of state at the thermodynamic limit. The biexciton-like four-body correlations survive even at the high-density regime as a many-body BCS-like state of Cooper quartets. Our results are useful for further understanding of exotic matter in the interdisciplinary context of quantum many-body physics with multiple degrees of freedom.

# Biexciton-like quartet condensates in an electron-hole liquid

## Key claim

The authors show that biexciton-like four-body correlations (Cooper quartets) survive even at high density as a many-body BCS-like state, not merely as isolated bound states; however, this result depends on using a contact-type attractive interaction with a large momentum cutoff, and the absence of a droplet phase in their model is an artifact of neglecting finite-range interactions.

## Method

The authors apply a quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) variational approach to a three-dimensional four-component fermionic system of spin-1/2 electrons and spin-1/2 holes with short-range attractive interactions. They solve self-consistent variational equations for the ground-state wave function that includes exciton-like Cooper pair amplitudes and biexciton-like Cooper quartet amplitudes, then compute the ground-state energy density and excitation gap as functions of fermion number density at zero temperature in the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- The four-body (biexciton) energy B_XX is taken as 500 meV, close to the value 434 meV employed in Florez (2020)
- The two-body (exciton) energy B_X is approximately 225 meV in the low-density limit (rho ~ 10^-6 nm^-3), close to 193 meV from Florez (2020)
- The biexciton binding energy measured from the two-exciton threshold is E_XX = -50 meV, close to -43 meV from Florez (2020)
- At density rho = 0.5 nm^-3, the chemical potential mu reaches 100 meV
- At rho = 0.5 nm^-3, the excitation gap with quartet correlations is larger by about 5.5% compared to the case without quartets
- At rho = 0.5 nm^-3, the relative momentum giving the minimum of the energy dispersion becomes larger by about 55.6% when quartet correlations are included
- The momentum cutoff is Lambda = 100 k_F, where k_F = (3 pi^2 rho / 2)^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the quartet BCS framework previously applied to nuclear systems by Baran (2020), Senkov (2011), and Guo (2021) to electron-hole systems in semiconductors. It builds on studies of biexciton condensation in CuSe by Chase (1979) and Peyghambarian (1983), in transition metal dichalcogenides by You (2015) and Stevens (2018), and in the electron-hole Hubbard model by Huang (2020). The results enable future quantitative studies of the electron-hole droplet phase using more realistic long-range Coulomb interactions and two-dimensional models relevant to layered transition metal dichalcogenides.
