# The SuSA model for neutrino oscillation experiments: from quasielastic scattering to the resonance region

> High precision studies of Beyond-Standard-Model physics through accelerator-based neutrino oscillation experiments require a very accurate description of neutrino-nucleus cross sections in a broad energy region, going from quasielastic scattering up to deep inelastic scattering. In this work we focus on the following processes: quasielastic scattering, two-particle-two-hole excitations, and the excitation of the first (Delta) and second (Roper) resonances of the nucleon. The nuclear model is fully relativistic and includes both one- and two-body currents. We compare our results with recent T2K and MicroBooNE data on carbon and argon targets, and present predictions for DUNE kinematics.

## Key claim

The SuSA (SuperScaling Approximation) model, extended for the first time to include the Delta and Roper resonance regions alongside quasielastic and 2p2h contributions, provides a good description of T2K and MicroBooNE neutrino-nucleus cross section data, but the authors note the comparison is qualitative (no chi-squared calculated) and that higher inelasticities and pion re-absorption are not yet included.

## Method

The authors embed one-body (elastic, Delta, Roper) and two-body (meson-exchange current, 2p2h) elementary tensors into the SuSA nuclear model, which replaces the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) scaling function with a phenomenological function fitted to world inclusive electron scattering data. The 2p2h contributions are computed within the RFG framework using a parametrization of numerical results, and the full model is flux-averaged and compared to T2K (carbon target) and MicroBooNE (argon target) double differential cross sections as a function of muon momentum and angle.

## Key numbers

* Fermi momenta used: 228 MeV/c for carbon and 241 MeV/c for argon, with an energy shift of 20 MeV, fitted to inclusive electron scattering data.
* The SuSA scaling function parameters are alpha=2.9883, beta=1.9438, gamma=0.6731, and delta=3.8538, fitted to the world quasielastic electron scattering data analyzed by Jourdan (1996) and Donnelly (1999).
* The Fermi weak constant is G = 1.166 x 10^{-11} MeV^{-2} and the Cabibbo angle cosine is 0.975.
* The parametrization of the 2p2h numerical results is valid for momentum transfers up to 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA model, previously applied to quasielastic and 2p2h regions (Amaro 2019, Megias 2016), to include the Delta and Roper resonance regions for the first time. It builds on the RFG model and the observation that electron scattering data exhibit superscaling (Donnelly 1998, 1999), and it enables future comparisons with DUNE data, where the Delta resonance contribution is predicted to be comparable to or larger than the quasielastic one. The authors note that heavier resonances and interference effects must be added for a better quantitative description of the inelastic region.
