# Colour Confinement and Deformed Baryons in Quantum Chromodynamics

> The confinement of coloured entities in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is traced to colour singletness of the observed entities. This is believed to arise from colour singlet state of quark-antiquark for mesons and a fully colour antisymmetric state for baryons. This demands a spherically symmetric baryon in the ground state. However it is pointed out that a deformed baryon in the ground state has been found to be extremely successful phenomenology. There are convincing experimental supports for a deformed nucleon as well. This means that something has been missed in the fundamental theory. In this paper this problem is traced to a new colour singlet state for baryons which has been missed hitherto and incorporation of which provides a consistent justification of a deformed baryon in the ground state. Interestingly this new colour singlet state is global in nature.

# Colour Confinement and Deformed Baryons in Quantum Chromodynamics

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard colour-singlet confinement mechanism for baryons, which demands a spherically symmetric ground state, is incomplete because it misses a second colour-singlet state that is global in nature; incorporating this missed state provides a fundamental QCD justification for the empirically successful but theoretically problematic 25% D-state admixture (deformation) in the nucleon ground state.

## Method
The author identifies a previously overlooked colour-singlet state for baryons by separating the colour-singlet sum over colour fields into terms where the colour and anticolour sit at distinct positions (the known meson and baryon confinement state) and a term where they coincide at the same spatial point. Using the SU(3) identity that relates an anticolour to an antisymmetric pair of colours, this second term is rewritten as a three-quark colour-antisymmetric state in which one quark is rigidly fixed at the centre of mass of the other two, producing even-power orbital terms (L=2,4,6...) that naturally yield the D-state admixture. The resulting wavefunction is shown to match the phenomenological mixed-symmetry states (ρ and λ) of the harmonic-oscillator quark model after removing spurious centre-of-mass excitations.

## Key numbers
- The D-state probability required to fit experimental data is P_D ~ 0.25 (25% deformation), as originally found by Glashow for the axial vector coupling constant g_A and the D/F ratio.
- The canonical quark model prediction for g_A is 5/3, while the experimental value is 1.24.
- The same P_D ~ 0.25 successfully fits: allowed semileptonic decays of all 1/2+ baryon octet baryons; the πNΔ to πNN coupling constant ratio; the E2/M1 ratio; double delta coupling constants; constituent quark masses; nucleon spin structure; and magnetic dipole and Gamow-Teller strengths in nuclei.
- One-gluon exchange tensor terms can induce at most a few percent D-state admixture, not the 25% required by phenomenology.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological deformed-baryon program initiated by Glashow (who proposed a D-state admixture to resolve the g_A and D/F discrepancies in the Naive Quark Model) and later developed by Vento et al. (who applied the same P_D ~ 0.25 to πNΔ coupling). It contradicts the standard assumption that colour-singlet confinement forces baryons into a spherically symmetric s-state, as codified in the canonical quark model. The new colour-singlet state also provides a natural explanation for baryon Regge trajectories. The paper resolves a long-standing tension between the spherical confinement picture and the extensive experimental evidence for deformed nucleons (e.g., the p→Δ+ transition quadrupole moment).
