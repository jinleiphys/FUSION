# Extracting the equation of state from a microscopic non-equilibrium model

> We study the thermodynamic properties of infinite nuclear matter with the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD), a semiclassical transport model, running in a box with periodic boundary conditions. It appears that the energy density rises faster than $T^4$ at high temperatures of $T\approx 200-300$~MeV. This indicates an increase in the number of degrees of freedom. Moreover, We have calculated direct photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at 160~GeV/u within this model. The direct photon slope from the microscopic calculation equals that from a hydrodynamical calculation without a phase transition in the equation of state of the photon source.

## Key claim

The energy density of infinite nuclear matter, as simulated by the non-equilibrium transport model URQMD in a box with periodic boundary conditions, rises faster than T^4 at high temperatures (T ≈ 200–300 MeV), indicating an increase in the number of degrees of freedom; however, the authors note that the statistics are poor and the results are preliminary.

## Method

The authors ran the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD) transport model in a box of volume 250 fm³ with periodic boundary conditions, initialized with 40 nucleons at ground-state nuclear density (ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³) and varying total energy density. After equilibration, the temperature was extracted by fitting the particles' momentum spectra (least square fit to the baryon momentum spectrum), and cross-checked using the Δ/N ratio. For the photon production study, direct photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 GeV/u was calculated within URQMD, considering the processes πρ→πγ, πη→πγ, and ππ→ργ with fixed meson-meson cross sections of 15 mb.

## Key numbers

- Box volume: 250 fm³ with 40 nucleons initialized at net-baryon density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (ground-state nuclear density)
- Temperature range where energy density rises faster than T⁴: T ≈ 200–300 MeV
- Pb+Pb collision energy for direct photon study: 160 GeV/u
- Fixed meson-meson cross sections for photon study: 15 mb (independent of √s)

## Context

This work extends the use of semiclassical cascade models (specifically URQMD) to extract the equation of state of nuclear matter, which is not an explicit input to the transport approach unlike in hydrodynamics. The direct photon slope from URQMD equals that from a hydrodynamical calculation without a phase transition (referenced as ADRIAN), while a phase transition would produce a steeper slope. The study enables future comparisons with experimental direct photon measurements from CERN (WA98) and motivates improved statistics and investigations of equilibration times and particle abundances.
