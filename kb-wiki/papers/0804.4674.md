# Center-of-mass motion and cross-channel coupling in time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> We provide a discussion of issues related to the center-of-mass motion and cross-channel coupling in applications of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to heavy-ion collisions. We find that the entrance channel dynamics of a heavy-ion collision as described by TDHF does not seem to be significantly influenced by these effects, whereas the long-time evolution may be less reliable.

# Center-of-mass motion and cross-channel coupling in time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory

## Key claim
The entrance channel dynamics of a heavy-ion collision as described by time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory does not appear to be significantly influenced by center-of-mass corrections or cross-channel coupling effects, whereas the long-time evolution (e.g., deep-inelastic collisions producing well-separated fragments) may be less reliable.

## Method
The authors analyze the theoretical foundations of center-of-mass motion in TDHF, including the wave-packet nature of boosted Slater determinants and the condition for classical wave-packet behavior. They test the validity of omitting center-of-mass corrections by using the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method to extract heavy-ion potentials for head-on collisions of ¹⁶O+¹⁶O at E_cm = 34 MeV and ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰,⁹⁶Zr systems, comparing the resulting potentials to empirical double-folding potentials and point Coulomb potentials.

## Key numbers
- The classical spreading time for a nuclear wave packet is approximately τ_classical ≈ 19 A^(5/3) fm/c, where A is the mass number.
- For light ions, the spread in beam energy ΔE/E can be as much as 10%, but reduces for heavier systems.
- In deep-inelastic collisions, the expected spread in energy is as much as 10 MeV for light systems, and the spread in impact parameter is of order 1 fm.
- For the ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰,⁹⁶Zr systems, comparison of the DC-TDHF potential with the point Coulomb potential shows differences on the order of 50 keV until nuclear effects become significant.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of center-of-mass corrections in mean-field studies previously examined by Bender et al. (2000) and by Jiang et al. (2007) in the context of fission barriers. The paper also relates to the density-constrained TDHF method introduced by Cusson et al. (1985) and Umar et al. (1985), and to recent investigations by Guo et al. (2008) on angular momentum conservation in TDHF. The findings support the reliability of TDHF for entrance-channel-dominated processes such as fusion barrier calculations using the DC-TDHF method, while identifying uncertainties for long-time evolution processes like deep-inelastic scattering.
