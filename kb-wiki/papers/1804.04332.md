# Screening and anti-screening of the pairing interaction in low-density neutron matter

> We study pairing in low-density neutron matter including the screening interaction due to the exchange of particle-hole and RPA excitations. As bare force we employ the effective low-momentum interaction $V_{low\,k}$, while the Fermi-liquid parameters are taken from a phenomenological energy density functional (SLy4) which correctly reproduces the equation of state of neutron matter. At low density, we find screening, i.e., pairing is reduced, while at higher densities, we find anti-screening, i.e., pairing is enhanced. This enhancement is mostly due to the strongly attractive Landau parameter $f_0$. We discuss in detail the critical temperature $T_c$ in the limit of low densities and show that the suppression of $T_c$ predicted by Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov can only be reproduced if the cutoff of the $V_{low\,k}$ interaction is scaled with the Fermi momentum. We also discuss the effect of non-condensed pairs on the density dependence of $T_c$ in the framework of the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in low-density neutron matter, the screening of the pairing interaction (reduction of pairing) occurs at low densities, while anti-screening (enhancement of pairing) occurs at higher densities, contrary to earlier studies by Cao et al. (2006) which predicted screening for all densities. This anti-screening is mostly due to the strongly attractive Landau parameter \(f_0\), and the suppression of the critical temperature predicted by Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov can only be reproduced if the cutoff of the \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) interaction is scaled with the Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors used the effective low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) (evolved from the AV\(_{18}\) two-body potential) as the bare force, and determined the Fermi-liquid parameters (Landau parameters and effective mass) from the phenomenological Skyrme SLy4 energy density functional, which reproduces the neutron-matter equation of state. They computed the induced interaction by summing the particle-hole bubble series (random-phase approximation, RPA) within the Landau approximation, keeping only the lowest-order (\(L=0\)) Landau parameters, and then solved the BCS gap equation for the critical temperature \(T_c\) using the bare plus induced interaction. They also studied the effect of non-condensed pairs on \(T_c\) using the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory.

## Key numbers

*   At low densities, the screening reduces the critical temperature; the Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov result predicts a reduction factor of \((4e)^{-1/3}\) for \(T_c\).
*   The Fermi momentum range where neutron matter is in a strong-coupling situation is approximately between \(1/|a| \sim 0.05\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(1/r_e \sim 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\), where \(a\) is the neutron-neutron scattering length and \(r_e\) the effective range.
*   In the unitary limit, the NSR theory predicts a reduction of \(T_c\) from the BCS result of \(\sim 0.5\,E_F\) to \(\sim 0.22\,E_F\).
*   The neutron-drip density in the inner crust is \(\sim 2.5\times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
*   The cutoff of the \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) interaction used is \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
*   The effective mass \(m^*/m\) and Landau parameters \(F_0\) and \(G_0\) from SLy4 and D1N are shown as functions of \(k_F\) up to \(k_F \sim 1\) fm\(^{-1}\).
*   For the example \(k_F = 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\), convergence of the induced interaction is reached for maximum angular momentum \(j_{\text{max}} = 3\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of medium corrections to the pairing interaction in neutron matter, such as those by Wambach (1993), Schulze et al. (1996), Shen et al. (2005), and Cao et al. (2006), which used many-body methods analogous to screening in an electron gas. The authors note that Cao et al. (2006) predicted screening for all densities, whereas the present results show anti-screening at higher densities, compatible with Quantum Monte-Carlo results by Abe et al. (2009), Gandolfi et al. (2008), and Gezerlis et al. (2010) which rule out extremely strong screening. The work also builds on the NSR theory of pair correlations in the normal phase (Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, 1985) and its application to neutron matter by Ramanan et al. (2013), and it enables more reliable predictions of neutron superfluidity in the inner crust of neutron stars, relevant for pulsar glitches and neutron-star cooling.
