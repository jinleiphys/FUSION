# MURCA driven Bulk viscosity in neutrino trapped baryonic matter

> We examine bulk viscosity, taking into account trapped neutrinos in baryonic matter, in the context of binary neutron star mergers. Following the merging event, the binary star can yield a remnant compact object with densities up to $5$ nuclear saturation density and temperature upto $50$ MeV resulting in the retention of neutrinos. We employ two relativistic mean field models, NL3 and DDME2, to describe the neutrino-trapped baryonic matter. The dissipation coefficient is determined by evaluating the Modified URCA interaction rate in the dense baryonic medium, and accounting for perturbations caused by density oscillations. We observe the resonant behavior of bulk viscosity as it varies with the temperature of the medium. The bulk viscosity peak remains within the temperature range of $\sim 13-50$ MeV, depending upon the underlying equation of states and lepton fractions. This temperature range corresponds to the relevant domain of binary neutron star mergers. We also note that in presence of neutrinos in the medium the bulk viscosity peak shifts towards higher temperature and the peak value of bulk viscosity also changes. The time scale of viscous dissipation is dictated by the beta-off-equilibrium susceptibilities derived from the nuclear equation of state. The resulting viscous decay time scale ranges from $32-100$ milliseconds, which aligns with the order of magnitude of the post-merger object's survival time in some specific scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in neutrino-trapped baryonic matter relevant to binary neutron star mergers, the bulk viscosity peak shifts to higher temperatures (13-50 MeV) and its magnitude changes compared to neutrino-transparent matter, with the specific peak temperature and viscosity enhancement depending on the equation of state (NL3 or DDME2) and lepton fraction.

## Method

The authors compute bulk viscosity by solving an integro-differential equation for the chemical potential perturbation \(\mu_{\Delta}\) arising from density oscillations, using the Modified URCA (MURCA) interaction rate in neutrino-trapped baryonic matter. They employ two relativistic mean-field models (NL3 and DDME2) to obtain the beta-off-equilibrium susceptibilities \(\mathcal{B}\) and \(\mathcal{C}\) from the nuclear equation of state, and numerically integrate the MURCA rate without assuming \(\mu_{\Delta}/T \ll 1\).

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity peak temperature for neutrino-trapped matter: \(T_{\zeta_{max}} = 14.1\) MeV at \(n_B = n_0\), 21.1 MeV at \(n_B = 2n_0\), and 28.6 MeV at \(n_B = 3n_0\) (NL3 EoS, lepton fraction \(Y_l = 0.2\))
- Peak bulk viscosity values for neutrino-transparent matter (free EoS): \(1.45 \times 10^{27}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) at \(n_B = n_0\), \(8.62 \times 10^{27}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) at \(n_B = 2n_0\), and \(2.57 \times 10^{28}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) at \(n_B = 3n_0\)
- Peak value increase with trapped neutrinos relative to transparent case: factor of 8.25 at \(n_B = n_0\), factor of 3.23 at \(n_B = 2n_0\), factor of 1.33 at \(n_B = 3n_0\)
- Viscous decay time scale: 32-100 milliseconds
- Temperature range for bulk viscosity peak: \(\sim 13-50\) MeV depending on equation of state and lepton fraction
- Densities considered: up to \(5 n_0\) (nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\))
- Temperatures considered: up to 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity in neutron star mergers by Alford et al. (2017-2023) and others, which addressed neutrino-transparent matter and various microphysical aspects including weak interaction rates and beta equilibration. The authors specifically incorporate trapped neutrinos into the MURCA interaction rate calculation, building on the framework of Alford et al. (2010) for large-amplitude oscillations and the recent work by Chabanov et al. (2023) showing that bulk viscosity can damp post-merger oscillations and affect gravitational wave signals. The results enable more realistic simulations of binary neutron star merger remnants by providing bulk viscosity coefficients valid in the neutrino-trapped regime relevant immediately after merger.
