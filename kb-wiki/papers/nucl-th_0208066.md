# Off-shell effects and consistency of many-body treatments of dense matter

> Effective field theory requires all observables to be independent of the representation used for the quantum field operators. It means that off-shell properties of the interactions should not lead to any observable effects. We analyse this issue in the context of many-body approaches to nuclear matter, where it should be possible to shift the contributions of lowest order in purely off-shell two-body interactions into three-body forces. We show that none of the commonly used truncations of the two-body scattering amplitude such as the ladder, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock or parquet approximations respect this requirement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the requirement of reparametrization invariance (independence of observables from off-shell properties of interactions) is violated by all commonly used many-body truncation schemes for nuclear matter, including ladder, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), and parquet approximations, even though exact calculations would allow off-shell two-body effects to be cancelled by three-body forces.

## Method

The authors analyze an effective field theory (EFT) for nucleon-nucleon interactions with an unnaturally large scattering length, using a spin- and isospin-independent potential expanded to next-to-leading order (NLO). They solve the Feynman-Galitskii equation for the in-medium T-matrix in nuclear matter, treating the off-shell coupling \(C_2\) as a perturbation, and examine the cancellation of off-shell dependence against a contact three-body force by comparing the degeneracy factors of the resulting integrals \(I_0\), \(I_1\), and \(I_2\) from Hugenholtz diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The LO coupling constant \(C_0\) is of order \(Q^{-1}\) in Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) counting, where \(Q\) is a generic low-energy scale.
- The NLO coupling \(C_2^\prime\) (proportional to the effective range) is of order \(Q^0\).
- The three-body force strength required for cancellation at lowest order is \(D_0 = 12 M C_0 C_2\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass.
- For symmetric nuclear matter, the spin-isospin degeneracy factor is \(g = 4\); for neutron matter, \(g = 2\).
- The sum of contributions proportional to the integral \(I_1\) from Figs. I1(a-c) is \(-2g(g-1)(2g-3) M C_0 C_2 I_1\), while the three-body force gives \(g(g-1)(g-2) D_0 I_1 / 2\); these do not cancel for \(g=2\) (neutron matter) where the Pauli principle forbids a contact three-body force.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Furnstahl et al. (2001), who demonstrated cancellation of off-shell effects by three-body forces for dilute Fermi systems with a natural scattering length, to dense systems with an unnatural scattering length such as nuclear matter. The paper shows that the ladder, BHF, and parquet approximations all violate reparametrization invariance, and that achieving consistency requires including diagrams with iteration in the particle-hole (ph) channel, which are absent from these standard truncations. The results highlight the need for a consistent expansion scheme for applying EFTs to dense, strongly interacting matter.
