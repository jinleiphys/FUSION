# Dilepton production from moaton quasiparticles

> The phase diagram of QCD may contain a moat regime in a large region of temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ\neq0$. A moat regime is characterized by quasiparticle moatons (pions) whose energy is minimal at nonzero spatial momentum. At $μ\neq 0$, higher mass dimension operators play a critical role in a moat regime. At dimension six, there are nine possible gauge invariant couplings between scalars and photons. For back-to-back dilepton production, only one operator contributes, which significantly enhances production near a moat threshold. This enhancement is an experimental signature of moatons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "moat regime" in the QCD phase diagram, where pion quasiparticles ("moatons") have minimum energy at nonzero spatial momentum, produces a significant enhancement in back-to-back dilepton production near threshold, providing an experimental signature of this regime. The authors hedge that the exact enhancement depends on effective model parameters (such as the moat momentum scale and effective mass) that cannot be predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for the moat regime including dimension-six gauge-invariant operators coupling scalar fields (pions) to photons, then compute the photon self-energy from moaton-anti-moaton production at one-loop order in the thermal medium. They evaluate the imaginary part of the photon self-energy for back-to-back dilepton pairs (zero total spatial momentum) using bosonic Matsubara frequency sums, and compare the resulting dilepton production rate to that from pions with a normal dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature at zero chemical potential is approximately $T \approx 156.5 \pm 1.5$ MeV, from lattice QCD (HotQCD 2018, Borsanyi 2020).
- The critical endpoint is estimated to occur at $(T_\chi, \mu_\chi) \approx (100, 200)$ MeV from FRG and Schwinger-Dyson analyses (Fu 2019, Gao 2020, Gunkel 2021).
- The moaton threshold for dilepton production occurs at $\omega \approx 2 m_{\rm eff}$, where $m_{\rm eff}$ is the effective moaton mass; the authors expect $m_{\rm eff}$ to be moderately less than $m_\pi$ and the scale $M$ to be a few times $m_\pi$.
- For normal pions, the imaginary part of the photon self-energy scales as $(\omega^2 - 4m^2)^{3/2}/\omega$; for moatons, the rate is proportional to $\sqrt{\omega^2 - 4 m_{\rm eff}^2} \sum k_\pm^3 / (M \omega)$, where $k_\pm^2 = k_M^2 \pm M\sqrt{\omega^2 - 4 m_{\rm eff}^2}/2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of moat regimes in QCD using the Functional Renormalization Group (Fu 2019, Fu 2024) and earlier proposals that moat regimes affect particle production through two-particle correlations (Pisarski 2020, Pisarski 2021) and Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry (Rennecke 2023, Fukushima 2023). The analysis is related to dilepton production studies in color superconductors (Nishimura 2022), near critical endpoints (Nishimura 2023, Nishimura 2024), and in chiral spirals (Hayashi 2024), but uniquely focuses on the moat regime's enhancement from modified photon-moaton vertices. The results enable experimental searches for moat regimes at the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and the CBM experiment at FAIR.
