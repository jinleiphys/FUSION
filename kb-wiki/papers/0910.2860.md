# A minimal length versus the Unruh effect

> In this Letter we study the radiation measured by an accelerated detector, coupled to a scalar field, in the presence of a fundamental minimal length. The latter is implemented by means of a modified momentum space Green's function. After calibrating the detector, we find that the net flux of field quanta is negligible, and that there is no Planckian spectrum. We discuss possible interpretations of this result, and we comment on experimental implications in heavy ion collisions and atomic systems.

## Key claim

The net Unruh radiation flux measured by an accelerated detector coupled to a scalar field vanishes when a Lorentz-invariant minimal length is implemented via a Gaussian-damped momentum-space propagator, and the usual Planckian spectrum disappears entirely; this result holds at first order in perturbation theory and requires calibrating the detector by subtracting a dissipation term that does not depend on acceleration.

## Method

The authors modify the Euclidean momentum-space propagator for a scalar field by including a Gaussian damping factor exp(-ℓ²p²/2), where ℓ is the fundamental minimal length, and compute the Fourier transform to obtain the regularized Euclidean Wightman-Green function. They then evaluate the detector response rate for a uniformly accelerated (Rindler) observer by Wick-rotating to Euclidean space, using the saddle-point approximation expanded to fourth order around Δτ=0, and subtracting the acceleration-independent leading-order term as a calibration procedure.

## Key numbers

- The regularized Euclidean Wightman-Green function at coincident points is G_ℓ^E → -1/(8π²ℓ²) in the massless case.
- The net Unruh response rate after calibration is approximately ℓ exp(-ΔE²ℓ²)/(16π^(3/2)α²), where 1/α is the detector acceleration.
- The effective Unruh temperature after calibration is T_ℓ = ℓ/(16π^(3/2)α²).
- For the most optimistic case with 1/ℓ ~ 1 TeV (extra dimensions), the resulting Unruh temperature is T_ℓ ~ 10 keV.
- Parton accelerations in heavy ion collisions of 1/α ~ 1 GeV correspond to a standard Unruh temperature T ~ 10^(-1) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of minimal-length effects on acceleration radiation using modified dispersion relations (Corley, Unruh) and path-integral duality (Paddy, Pepe1, Pepe2), which found negligible impact on the Unruh effect. The result contrasts sharply with Pepe1, Pepe2 and Paddy, where similar covariant minimal-length propagators without Gaussian damping led to large deviations from thermal spectra. The findings suggest that Unruh-driven thermalization of Color Glass Condensates into Quark-Gluon Plasma (Kharzeev 2005) may be too weak to drive the phase transition, and that the usual thermal bath could survive at most at order γ⁴ in perturbation theory.
