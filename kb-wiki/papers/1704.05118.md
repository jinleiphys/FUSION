# Jet modifications in event-by-event hydrodynamically evolving media

> The nearly perfect fluid-like nature of the Quark Gluon Plasma may be understood through two key experimental signatures: collective flow and jet suppression. Event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (with an extremely small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio) has been very successful at describing collective flow observables for the last 7 years. More recently, the effects of event-by-event fluctuations have been studied in the context of high $p_T$ particles that lose energy as they pass through the dense Quark Gluon Plasma liquid. In this summary of the corresponding plenary talk at Quark Matter 2017, the recent developments on the effects of event-by-event fluctuations on jet suppression are summarized.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions are essential for describing high-\(p_T\) flow harmonics, particularly triangular flow \(v_3\), which vanishes when averaged initial conditions are used; the combination of event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics with an energy loss model can resolve the long-standing \(R_{AA}\otimes v_2\) puzzle.

## Method

The author combines event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro) with the BBMG energy loss model, using fluctuating initial conditions from MCKLN and MCGlauber, to compute the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and flow harmonics \(v_2\{SP\}(p_T)\) and \(v_3\{SP\}(p_T)\) for high-\(p_T\) particles in Pb+Pb collisions. The scalar product method is used to correlate one soft particle with one hard particle, and calculations include centrality rebinning and multiplicity weighting. Predictions are compared to LHC data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV and 5.02 TeV, and Soft-Hard Event Engineering (SHEE) is introduced to study the scaling of high-\(p_T\) flow with the underlying soft event shape.

## Key numbers

- Roughly a 30% difference between the eccentricities \(\varepsilon_2\) of MCKLN and MCGlauber initial conditions, corresponding to a roughly 30% larger \(v_2\) in the calculations.
- At LHC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, v-USPhydro+BBMG with \(dE/dL\sim L\) shows good agreement with CMS data for \(v_2\{SP\}(p_T)\) and \(v_3\{SP\}(p_T)\) up to \(p_T \sim 40\) GeV.
- ALICE data show \(v_2\{SP\}(p_T)\) to \(v_4\{SP\}(p_T)\) up to \(p_T = 40\) GeV; ATLAS data show \(v_2\{SP\}(p_T)\) to \(v_7\{SP\}(p_T)\) up to \(p_T = 25\) GeV, with higher-order harmonics \(n \geq 4\) consistent with zero above \(p_T \geq 10\) GeV.
- The ratio \(v_2\{4\}(p_T)/v_2\{SP\}(p_T) \sim 1\) for \(p_T \geq 10\) GeV at LHC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, indicating that soft-hard fluctuations become dominant around \(p_T \sim 10\) GeV.
- SHEE measurements from ATLAS at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV show a clear scaling of \(v_n\{SP\}\) with the underlying soft event shape up to \(p_T \sim 15\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions revolution in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, which began with triangular flow studies by Alver and Roland (2010) and was advanced by Schenke, Jeon, and Gale (2010). It directly addresses the \(R_{AA}\otimes v_2\) puzzle, where theoretical calculations using averaged initial conditions consistently under-predicted experimental data for about 10 years (Xu and Liao, 2014). The results enable new observables such as Soft-Hard Event Engineering (SHEE), which can probe the path-length dependence of energy loss, and provide a path toward coupling Monte Carlo event generators (e.g., JETSCAPE, THOR) with event-by-event hydrodynamics for full jet reconstruction.
