# What the inflaton might tell us about RHIC/LHC

> Topical phenomena in high-energy physics related to collision experiments of heavy nuclei ("Little Bang") and early universe cosmology ("Big Bang") involve far-from-equilibrium dynamics described by quantum field theory. One example concerns the role of plasma instabilities for the process of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions. The reheating of the early universe after inflation may exhibit rather similar phenomena following a tachyonic or parametric resonance instability. Certain universal aspects associated to nonthermal fixed points even quantitatively agree, and considering these phenomena from a common perspective can be fruitful.

## Key claim

The paper argues that far-from-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions ("Little Bang") and early-universe reheating after inflation ("Big Bang") exhibit quantitatively similar universal scaling behavior, particularly in the turbulent regime at higher momenta, but notes that research concerning these quantitative agreements is still in its infancy and that important open questions remain, especially regarding the existence of nonthermal infrared fixed points in gauge theories.

## Method

The author compares classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) gauge theory in three spatial dimensions (for QCD plasma instabilities) with (2PI) resummed 1/N expansion calculations for N-component scalar inflaton models (for parametric-resonance and tachyonic preheating). Both approaches track the time evolution of occupation numbers and field correlators from initial instabilities through exponential growth, secondary growth stages, and subsequent power-law scaling regimes.

## Key numbers

- Inverse maximum primary growth rate for |A(t,p)|² in heavy-ion collisions: γ_max⁻¹ ≃ 1.2–1.8 fm/c (RHIC) and γ_max⁻¹ ≃ 1.0–1.5 fm/c (LHC), based on initial energy densities of 5–25 GeV/fm³ for RHIC and about twice that for LHC
- Effective isotropization occurs up to a characteristic momentum of |p| ≲ 1 GeV, similar to the range where hydrodynamic descriptions work (deviations above about 2 GeV)
- For classical-statistical gauge theory, the power-law exponent κ = 3/2 describes the turbulent regime at higher momenta (⟨|A(t,p)|²⟩ ∼ |p|⁻⁽¹⁺κ⁾)
- For inflaton dynamics, the same exponent κ = 3/2 appears at higher momenta (n(t,p) ∼ |p|⁻κ), while the infrared regime shows κ = 4 with occupation numbers n(t,p) ∼ 24π/λ for λ ≪ 1
- The initially fastest growing inflaton mode with momentum p₀ and primary growth rate 2γ₀ is followed by a higher momentum mode at 2p₀ with secondary growth rate 6γ₀
- Exponential growth in inflaton dynamics stops when ⟨|φ(t,p)|²⟩ ∼ O(N⁰ λ⁻¹)
- Conservative limit requires thermal equilibrium at a reheating temperature of order 10 MeV before Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

## Context

This work extends the comparison between heavy-ion collision thermalization and early-universe reheating dynamics, building on prior studies of plasma instabilities (ALMY05, Romatschke 2006, Berges 2007) and inflaton preheating (preheat1, preheat2, Berges 2002, Arrizabalaga 2004). The paper highlights that the same high-momentum scaling exponent κ = 3/2 found in inflaton dynamics (Berges 2008) has also been found in classical-statistical lattice QCD simulations (BSS2), while noting that the value κ = 2 reported by Arnold (2005) based on Vlasov equations seems excluded by the classical-statistical simulations. The work enables future investigation of whether nonthermal infrared fixed points exist in QCD, which could dramatically affect thermalization timescales and the applicability of hydrodynamic descriptions.
