# KN Scattering and the Nucleon Strangeness Radius

> The leading non-zero electric moment of the nucleon strange-quark vector current is the mean square strangeness radius, $<r_s^2>$. We evaluate the lightest OZI-allowed contribution to $<r_s^2>$, arising from the kaon cloud, using dispersion relations. Drawing upon unitarity constraints as well as $K^{+}N$ scattering and $e^+e^-\to K\bar{K}$ cross section data, we find the structure of this contribution differs significantly from that suggested by a variety of QCD-inspired model calculations. In particular, we find evidence for a strong $ϕ$-meson resonance which may enhance the scale of kaon cloud contribution to an observable level.

## Key claim

The kaon cloud contribution to the nucleon strangeness radius is enhanced by roughly a factor of three compared to second-order perturbative calculations when effects beyond O(g²), particularly resonant and non-resonant kaon rescattering, are included via dispersion relations and analytic continuation of experimental K⁺N scattering amplitudes, bringing it to a potentially observable level in parity-violating electron scattering experiments.

## Method

The authors used backward dispersion relations to analytically continue experimental K⁺N scattering amplitudes from the physical region into the unphysical t-channel region (4m_K² ≤ t ≤ 4m_N²), where the K̅K → N̅N partial wave amplitudes are needed. They employed a conformal mapping technique and Legendre polynomial expansion of the discrepancy function, with high-energy behavior modeled by Regge exchange. The kaon strangeness form factor was parameterized using a Gounaris-Sakurai form fitted to e⁺e⁻ → K̅K cross section data, and the unitarity bound on the partial wave amplitudes was imposed for t ≥ 4m_N².

## Key numbers

- The O(g²) (Born approximation) kaon cloud prediction for the strangeness radius ⟨r_s²⟩ is -0.024 fm² (total from unphysical region -0.017 fm² and physical region -0.007 fm²)
- The analytic continuation with Gounaris-Sakurai form factor (AC/GS) gives |⟨r_s²⟩| = 0.066 fm² (unphysical region 0.065 fm², physical region 0.001 fm²)
- The O(g²) Born approximation violates the unitarity bound on the K̅K → N̅N partial wave by a factor of four or more at the physical N̅N threshold (t = 4m_N²)
- Results for ⟨r_s²⟩ change by ≤ 15% when the high-energy parameter α and Legendre truncation order n are varied over reasonable ranges

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation analysis of Musolf, Hammer, and Drechsel (1997) for the strangeness form factors and resolves a long-standing issue between kaon cloud model predictions (which typically give ⟨r_s²⟩ too small to observe) and vector meson dominance predictions of Jaffe (1989) (which give larger values from a ϕNN coupling). The analysis demonstrates that the ϕ-meson resonance emerges naturally from the K̅K continuum when all orders in the strong coupling are included, rather than requiring an a priori pole dominance assumption. The results suggest that model calculations such as those of Geiger and Isgur (1997) using a tower of OZI-allowed intermediate states will be significantly modified when rescattering effects beyond O(g²) are included.
