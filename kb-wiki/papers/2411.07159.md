# Probing nuclear structure and the equation of state through pre-equilibrium dipole emission in charge-asymmetric reactions

> We investigate the pre-equilibrium dipole response in the charge-asymmetric reaction $^{40}$Ca+$^{152}$Sm, of recent experimental interest, at several beam energies within the range $[5, 11]$ AMeV and different collision centralities. By employing Skyrme-like effective interactions for the nuclear mean field, we probe the role of the different ingredients performing theoretical calculations based on the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach or a semi-classical transport model that also includes two-body correlations. A comparative analysis between these approaches allowed us to disentangle the role of deformation effects in the entrance channel from the ones associated with structure details of genuine quantal nature on the dipole emission. Moreover, we also investigate the impact of the occurrence of residual two-body collisions on the reaction dynamics. This study contributes to the understanding of the microscopic processes that determine the complex dynamics of low-energy heavy-ion collisions along the fusion-fission path, which is relevant to super-heavy element synthesis, unraveling interesting connections with the characteristics of the nuclear effective interaction and the associated equation of state.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium dipole emission in the charge-asymmetric reaction ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁵²Sm is sensitive not only to the symmetry energy but also to momentum-dependent terms, surface terms, and ground-state deformation of the target nucleus; the authors show that using momentum-dependent Skyrme interactions (SAMi-J and SAMi-m) resolves the discrepancy where previous momentum-independent calculations underestimated the experimental centroid energy of the dipole gamma spectrum.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations and semi-classical Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport simulations for the reaction ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁵²Sm at beam energies from 5 to 11 AMeV and various impact parameters. They employed several Skyrme-like energy density functionals (SAMi-J27, SAMi-J31, SAMi-m060, SAMi-m085, and momentum-independent interactions) to probe the role of the symmetry energy, momentum dependence, surface terms, and two-body collisions on the dynamical dipole emission, and introduced a scaling technique to incorporate prolate deformation of ¹⁵²Sm into the semi-classical model.

## Key numbers

- The initial dipole moment at the touching point for ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁵²Sm is |D₀| = 30.65 fm.
- The experimental centroid energy of the dynamical dipole gamma spectrum is E_DD^exp = (11.7 ± 0.3) MeV with width Γ_DD^exp = (2.0 ± 0.5) MeV, extracted from a Lorentzian fit.
- The experimental average impact parameter is ⟨b_exp⟩ ∼ 5 fm.
- The beam energy used for the main analysis is E_lab/A = 11 MeV.
- The deformation parameter β₂ for ¹⁵²Sm varies within the range [0.220, 0.287] from experimental data.
- The isoscalar effective mass at saturation density for SAMi-J interactions is m_S^*(ρ₀)/m = 0.67.
- The saturation density used is ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with a crossing density ρ_c = 0.6ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the dynamical dipole mode in charge-asymmetric reactions, particularly the experimental work on ⁴⁰Ca+¹⁵²Sm by Parascandolo (2016, 2022) which found that BNV calculations with momentum-independent interactions underestimated the centroid energy and overestimated the gamma multiplicity. The study also builds on the findings of Zheng (2017) for the reaction ¹³²Sn+⁵⁸Ni, which showed differences in dipole power spectra between momentum-dependent and momentum-independent interactions. The results enable more reliable predictions for pre-equilibrium dipole emission as a probe of the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy, relevant to super-heavy element synthesis and the cooling of compound nuclei.
