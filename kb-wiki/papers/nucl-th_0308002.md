# Determination of S17 from Systematic Analysis of 8B Coulomb Dissociation

> Systematic analysis of 8B Coulomb dissociation with the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) method is proposed to determine the astrophysical factor S17 accurately. An important advantage of the analysis is that uncertainties of the extracted S17 coming from the use of the ANC method can quantitatively be evaluated, in contrast to previous analyses using the Virtual Photon Theory (VPT). Calculation of measured spectra in dissociation experiments is done by means of the method of Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC). From the analysis of 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 25.8 MeV, S17=22.83 +/- 0.51(theo) +/- 2.28(expt) (eVb) is obtained; the ANC method turned out to work in this case within 1% of error. Preceding systematic analysis of experimental data at intermediate energies, we propose hybrid (HY) Coupled-Channels (CC) calculation of 8B Coulomb dissociation, which makes numerical calculation much simple, retaining its accuracy. The validity of the HY calculation is tested for 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 240 MeV. The ANC method combined with the HY CC calculation is shown to be a powerful technique to obtain a reliable S17.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the astrophysical factor S17 can be determined from 8B Coulomb dissociation using the Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC) method instead of the Virtual Photon Theory (VPT), with the specific hedge that the ANC method works within 1% error for the Notre Dame data at 25.8 MeV, but the experimental uncertainty of 10% prevents achieving the required 5% accuracy.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations to describe the 8B breakup process on a 58Ni target, treating the projectile as a three-body system of 7Be, proton, and target. They extracted the ANC from the angular distribution of the 7Be fragment by fitting the CDCC results to experimental data, then converted the ANC to S17 using the relationship between the ANC and the astrophysical factor. For intermediate energies, they developed a hybrid (HY) Coupled-Channels method combining quantum-mechanical CDCC for low partial waves with Eikonal-CDCC for high partial waves to reduce computational complexity.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 22.83 ± 0.51(theo) ± 2.28(expt) eVb from analysis of 58Ni(8B,7Be+p)58Ni at 25.8 MeV
- ANC value C = 0.59 ± 0.004 fm^{-1/2}, showing the ANC method works within 1% error
- Spectroscopic factor S_exp = 0.96 for the Kim 8B wave function and 1.20 for the Esbensen-Bertsch wave function
- For the hybrid calculation at 240 MeV, the appropriate connecting angular momentum L_C = 200 for elastic cross section and L_C = 400 for breakup cross section, with error less than 1% compared to full quantum-mechanical calculation
- Maximum angular momentum required for CDCC at intermediate energies is approximately 15,000 partial waves

## Context

This work extends the ANC method, previously applied to p-transfer reactions by Azhari (2001) and Trache (2002) and to one-nucleon breakup reactions, to Coulomb dissociation of 8B. It contradicts previous analyses using Virtual Photon Theory (VPT) by Esbensen and Bertsch, which could not quantitatively evaluate uncertainties from nuclear interaction effects and E2 contributions. The proposed hybrid CDCC+ANC method enables systematic analysis of 8B Coulomb dissociation data from RIKEN, GSI, MSU, and Notre Dame experiments across a wide energy range, potentially providing a more reliable S17 value for solar neutrino flux predictions.
