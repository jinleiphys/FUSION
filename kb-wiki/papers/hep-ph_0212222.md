# Evidence for Majorana Neutrinos: Dawn of a new era in spacetime structure

> We show that Majorana particles belong to the Wigner class of fermions in which the charge conjugation and the parity operators commute, rather than anticommute. Rigorously speaking, Majorana spinors do not satisfy the Dirac equation [a result originally due to M. Kirchbach, which we re-render here]. Instead, they satisfy a different wave equation, which we derive. This allows us to reconcile Stückelberg-Feynman interpretation with the Majorana construct. We present several new properties of neutral particle spinors and argue that discovery of Majorana particles constitutes dawn of a new era in spacetime structure.

## Key claim

Majorana particles belong to a Wigner class of fermions where charge conjugation and parity operators commute (rather than anticommute), and their spinors do not satisfy the Dirac equation but instead obey a different wave equation; the author argues that the discovery of Majorana particles would constitute a new era in spacetime structure because the underlying representation spaces exploit additional relative phases between the two SU(2) building blocks of the Lorentz algebra.

## Method

The author constructs neutral particle spinors as eigenspinors of the charge conjugation operator C in the (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) representation space, deriving explicit self-conjugate and anti-self-conjugate spinors using Wigner's time reversal operator and boost operators. The paper then examines the action of parity and charge conjugation operators on these spinors, demonstrating that C and P commute for neutral particle spinors (while they anticommute for charged Dirac spinors), and derives the wave equation satisfied by Majorana spinors, showing it differs from the Dirac equation.

## Key numbers

- The bi-orthonormal norms of the self-conjugate neutral spinors are ±2im (e.g., λ̄^S_{-,+}(p) λ^S_{+,-}(p) = +2im, λ̄^S_{+,-}(p) λ^S_{-,+}(p) = -2im)
- The bi-orthonormal norms of the anti-self-conjugate neutral spinors are ∓2im (e.g., λ̄^A_{-,+}(p) λ^A_{+,-}(p) = -2im, λ̄^A_{+,-}(p) λ^A_{-,+}(p) = +2im)
- For charged particle spinors, P^2 = +1_4; for neutral particle spinors, P^2 = -1_4 (and P^4 = +1_4)
- In the massless limit, the λ^S_{-,+}(p) and λ^A_{-,+}(p) spinors identically vanish, while λ^S_{+,-}(p) and λ^A_{+,-}(p) do not

## Context

This work extends and formalizes the original Majorana construct (1937) and later treatments by McLennan (1957) and Case (1957), building on the author's prior studies of spacetime structure (Ahluwalia 1996, Ahluwalia and collaborators 1993, 1994). The paper reconciles the Stückelberg-Feynman interpretation of antiparticles with the Majorana framework and claims that the commutativity of C and P for neutral particle spinors places them in a new Wigner block that is necessary for implementing supersymmetry, potentially enabling a deeper understanding of spacetime structure beyond the standard SU(2)_R ⊗ SU(2)_L realization of the Lorentz algebra.
