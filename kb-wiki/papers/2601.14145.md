# On the Realization of Quantum State Teleportation in Proton Systems

> We discuss how to generate entangled Bell states of two nucleons using unpolarized nucleon-nucleon scattering or the exclusive deuteron breakup reaction. We follow the the approach of Z. X. Shen et al., arXiv:2510.24325v1 [nucl-th], where Bell states were identified in unpolarized proton-proton elastic scattering. We confirm these results and show that, in the unpolarized proton-deuteron breakup reaction, it is also possible to generate proton-proton entangled Bell states in kinematically complete proton-proton quasi-free scattering (QFS) and final-state interaction (FSI) configurations. We also discuss an experimental setup that, by exploiting such entangled states, could enable the teleportation of quantum mechanical states in a three-proton system. Such an experiment requires triple coincidences among the outgoing nucleons, which precludes the use of entangled Bell states generated with extremely polarized incoming particles. Since counting rates for unpolarized reactions are much higher than for polarized ones, the present results open a pathway toward searching for signatures of quantum state teleportation in hadronic systems.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that entangled proton-proton Bell states can be generated in unpolarized proton-proton elastic scattering and in the unpolarized proton-deuteron breakup reaction, and argues that such states could enable quantum state teleportation in a three-proton system, provided that the transition matrix has a specific single-term structure in the Bell-state basis.

## Method

The author solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential (including the pp Coulomb force for proton-proton scattering) to compute the transition matrix elements for nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering, then expanded these in the Bell-state basis. For the exclusive deuteron breakup reaction, solutions of the three-nucleon Faddeev equations with the CD Bonn potential were used to compute the breakup transition operator, and the spin density matrix of the outgoing nucleon pair was obtained by tracing over the third nucleon's spin states. The analysis focused on kinematically complete quasi-free scattering (QFS) and final-state interaction (FSI) configurations at laboratory energies of 13 MeV and 156 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At Elab = 10 MeV in pp elastic scattering, the coefficient C44 dominates the transition matrix by more than one order of magnitude over C32 and C23 for center-of-mass angles in the range (55 degrees, 125 degrees), yielding the Bell state |psi->
- At Elab = 151 MeV in pp elastic scattering, the coefficient C32 dominates the transition matrix by about one order of magnitude for center-of-mass angles in the range (80 degrees, 100 degrees), yielding the Bell state |psi+>
- In the FSI(nn) configuration of the nd breakup at Elab = 156 MeV, the only nonvanishing coefficient is C44br, giving a pure Bell state |psi-> for all laboratory angles of the first outgoing neutron
- In the QFS(nn) configuration at Elab = 156 MeV, the coefficient C33br exceeds the remaining coefficients by more than three orders of magnitude in a narrow angular interval around Theta1lab approximately 45 degrees, yielding the Bell state |psi+>
- In the FSI(np) configuration at Elab = 13 MeV, the leading coefficient C44br is about three orders of magnitude larger than the next-largest coefficients
- In the QFS(pp) configuration at Elab = 13 MeV, the coefficient C44br exceeds the remaining coefficients by more than four orders of magnitude around Theta1lab approximately 40 degrees
- The pp Coulomb force reduces the FSI(nn) cross section by at least a factor of approximately 10 at both 13 MeV and 156 MeV

## Context

This work extends the findings of Shen et al. (2025, arXiv:2510.24325), who identified Bell states in unpolarized proton-proton elastic scattering, by confirming those results and demonstrating that entangled proton-proton pairs can also be generated in the unpolarized proton-deuteron breakup reaction. It contrasts with earlier studies by Witala (2024) and Witala (2025), which required extreme polarization of incoming particles to generate entangled nucleon pairs, a condition that is impractical due to the lack of suitable polarization sources and the much lower counting rates of polarized reactions. The results enable a proposed teleportation experiment (described in the paper as Ref. teleport) that would require triple coincidences among outgoing nucleons and could search for signatures of quantum state teleportation in hadronic systems.
