# The Cheshire Cat Bag Model: Color Anomaly and $η'$ Properties

> We show that color can leak from a QCD bag if we allow for pseudoscalar isoscalar singlet ($η'$) coupling at the surface. To enforce total confinement of color an additional boundary term is suggested. New relations between the $η'$ mass and decay constant and the QCD gluon condensates are derived and compared with the empirical parameters.

## Key claim
Color can leak from a QCD bag when pseudoscalar isoscalar singlet (η') coupling is allowed at the bag surface, contradicting the classical expectation that the chiral boundary condition ensures color confinement. To restore total confinement of color at the quantum level, an additional gauge-dependent boundary term must be introduced.

## Method
The author first demonstrates the color anomaly in a 1+1 dimensional toy model (the Schwinger model) where exact bosonization is possible, showing that a time-dependent boundary field induces a fermion number anomaly that becomes a charge anomaly when a U(1) gauge field is coupled. This is then generalized to 3+1 dimensions using a heuristic argument based on Landau levels of quarks near the bag surface, where the lowest levels behave analogously to the 1+1 dimensional case, leading to a non-abelian color anomaly. The Cheshire cat principle is then applied to derive relations between the η' mass and decay constant and the QCD gluon condensates by treating the bag as a test volume that can be moved arbitrarily.

## Key numbers
- The η' decay constant derived from the Cheshire cat principle is f_{η'} = sqrt(2/N_F) f = 100 MeV.
- The gluon condensate value borrowed from QCD sum rule calculations is ⟨0| (α(μ)/π) G(μ)^2 |0⟩ ∼ (330 MeV)^4.
- The derived η' mass ranges from 0.33 GeV to 1.6 GeV, compared to the empirical value of 0.96 GeV.
- In the 1+1 dimensional Schwinger model, the Cheshire cat derivation yields the exact result m^2 = e^2/π.

## Context
This work extends the Cheshire cat bag model approach developed by Nielsen (1987) and Nielsen and Wirzba (1987), which treats the bag surface as a formal separation with no physical significance. The paper contradicts the traditional MIT bag model assumption that color is perfectly confined by the standard boundary conditions (color electric fields parallel to the surface, color magnetic fields orthogonal). The derived relations for the η' mass are analogous to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations for octet Goldstone bosons (Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner, 1968). The results enable a consistent treatment of the η' meson within the bag model framework and provide testable predictions for the η' mass and decay constant that can be compared with empirical data.
