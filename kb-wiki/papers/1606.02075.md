# Quark deconfinement in the proto-magnetar model of Long Gamma-Ray Bursts

> We investigate the possible implications of quark deconfinement on the phenomenology of Long Gamma-Ray Bursts focusing, in particular, on the possibility to describe multiple prompt emission phases in the context of the proto-magnetar model. Starting from numerical models of rotating Hadron Stars and Quark Stars in full general relativity we track the electromagnetic spin-down evolution in both the hadronic and quark phase, linking the two families through conservation of baryon number and angular momentum. We give estimates of the timescales and the energetics involved in the spin-down process deriving, in the relevant spin range, the relation between the initial and the final masses and rotational energies, whenever hadron-quark conversion is possible. We show how the results can be used in relevant astrophysical cases such as the double burst GRB 110709B.

## Key claim

Quark deconfinement in a proto-magnetar can produce a second episode of gamma-ray emission, separated from the initial prompt phase by timescales of minutes to hours, provided the hadronic star has a baryonic mass between 1.666 and 1.820 solar masses and spins down to a critical central density of 1.34 x 10^15 g/cm^3.

## Method

The authors constructed numerical models of rigidly rotating hadron stars and quark stars in full general relativity using the XNS code with a conformally-flat metric approximation. They linked hadronic and quark configurations through conservation of baryon number and angular momentum, then tracked the electromagnetic spin-down evolution using the aligned dipole rotator formula assuming constant magnetic flux, for a set of equilibrium sequences at constant baryonic mass.

## Key numbers

- Critical central baryon density for deconfinement: 1.34 x 10^15 g/cm^3
- Limiting non-rotating hadronic configuration A: gravitational mass 1.45 solar masses, baryonic mass 1.67 solar masses, radius 10.9 km
- Limiting 1 ms rotating hadronic configuration B: gravitational mass 1.58 solar masses, baryonic mass 1.82 solar masses, radius 12 km
- Energy released in the hadron-to-quark transition: approximately 0.1 solar masses (about 10^53 erg) for the gravitational mass change, with rotational energy change Delta K_d ranging from 0.00 to 2.80 x 10^52 erg across the labeled configurations
- Spin-down timescale to start deconfinement Delta t_sd: ranges from 0 (for the highest mass configuration B) to 2.7 hours (for the lowest mass configuration with initial period 1 ms)
- For GRB 110709B: first burst isotropic energy 2.6 x 10^53 erg, second burst isotropic energy 4.4 x 10^52 erg, delay between bursts about 600 s, with assumed beaming correction of about 10^-2

## Context

This work extends the proto-magnetar model of Metzger and Giannios (2011) by incorporating the two-families scenario of Drago, Lavagno, and Pagliara (2014, 2015), where both hadronic stars and quark stars exist and a delayed conversion can occur. It enables a new interpretation of multi-episode prompt emission in long gamma-ray bursts, such as the double burst GRB 110709B studied by Zhang and Burrows (2012), and provides testable predictions linking the initial magnetic field, rotation period, and stellar mass to observable timing and energetics.
