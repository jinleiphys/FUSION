# Strange tribaryons as Kbar-mediated dense nuclear systems

> We discuss the implications of recently discovered strange tribaryons in 4He(stopped-K-, p)S0 (3115) and 4He(stopped-K, n) S1 (3140) within the framework of deeply bound kbar states formed on shrunk nuclear cores. S1 (3140) corresponds to T=0 ppnK-, whereas S0 (3115) to T=1 pnnK-, which is an isobaric analog state of pppK-, predicted previously. The observed binding energies can be accounted for by including the relativistic effect and by invoking a medium-enhanced kbar-N interaction by 15%. We propose various experimental methods to further study these and related bound systems.

# Strange Tribaryons as \(\bar{K}\)-Mediated Dense Nuclear Systems

## Key claim

The observed strange tribaryons S\(^0\)(3115) and S\(^1\)(3140) can be understood as deeply bound \(\bar{K}\) nuclear states with shrunk cores, but the observed binding energies (194 and 169 MeV, respectively) are substantially larger than the original non-relativistic predictions (97 and 118 MeV), requiring both a relativistic correction and a medium-enhanced \(\bar{K}N\) interaction by 15% to be simultaneously accounted for.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic calculations of \(\bar{K}\)-nuclear bound states using bare \(\bar{K}N\) interactions derived from empirical \(\bar{K}N\) scattering and kaonic hydrogen data, with the \(\Lambda_{1405}\) treated as a \(\bar{K}N\) bound state. They then applied a relativistic correction by mapping the Schrodinger energy to a Klein-Gordon energy via \(\varepsilon_{\rm KG} = m_K c^2 (\sqrt{1 + 2\varepsilon_{\rm S}/(m_K c^2)} - 1)\), and tuned the bare \(\bar{K}N\) interaction strength by a factor of 1.15 to match the observed binding energies. Spin-orbit contributions from both one-body Thomas-type and two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions were also evaluated for the \(T=1\) state.

## Key numbers

- S\(^0\)(3115) mass: \(M = 3117 \pm 5\) MeV/\(c^2\) with total binding energy \(S_K = 194 \pm 5\) MeV and width less than 25 MeV, from the \(^4\)He(stopped-\(K^-, p\)) reaction
- S\(^1\)(3140) mass: \(M = 3141 \pm 6\) MeV/\(c^2\) with total binding energy \(S_K + BE(^3{\rm He}) = 169 \pm 6\) MeV and width less than 25 MeV, from the \(^4\)He(stopped-\(K^-, n\)) reaction
- Original non-relativistic predictions: \(ppnK^-\) (\(T=0\)) with \(-E_K = 118\) MeV; \(pppK^-\) (\(T=1\)) with \(-E_K = 97\) MeV; \(ppK^-\) with \(-E_K = 48\) MeV
- Calculated rms distances in \(ppnK^-\): \(R_{NN} = 1.50\) fm, \(R_{\bar{K}-N} = 1.19\) fm; average nucleon density \(\rho^{\rm ave}_N = 3.1 \times \rho_0\) where \(\rho_0 \sim 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Relativistic correction: \(-23\) MeV for \(T=0\) state, \(-46\) MeV for \(T=1\) state
- Enhanced \(\bar{K}N\) interaction strength: \(\bar{K}N / \bar{K}N^{\rm bare} \sim 1.15\)
- Recalculated \(ppK^-\) with enhanced interaction: mass \(M = 2284\) MeV/\(c^2\) (\(S_K = 86\) MeV), width \(\Gamma = 58\) MeV (compared to original non-relativistic \(M = 2322\) MeV/\(c^2\), \(S_K = 48\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 61\) MeV)
- Predicted spin-orbit splitting in \(T=1\) state: \(E(1/2^+) - E(3/2^+) \sim 60\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions of deeply bound \(\bar{K}\) nuclear states (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, Dote 2002, 2003) by addressing the surprising experimental discovery of S\(^0\)(3115) and S\(^1\)(3140) by the E471 group (Iwasaki et al.) at KEK. The observed binding energies are much larger than the original predictions, and the \(T=1\) state lies below the \(T=0\) state, contrary to naive expectation. The analysis invokes a medium-enhanced \(\bar{K}N\) interaction that may indicate partial chiral symmetry restoration, contrasting with claims for a shallow \(\bar{K}\) potential (Ramos 2000, Cieply 2001). The results enable future experimental searches for the \(pppK^-\) isobaric analog state via \(^3\)He(\(K^-,\pi^-\)) and \(^3\)He(\(\pi^+,K^+\)) reactions, and for the spin-orbit partner \(J^\pi=1/2^+\) state with a predicted splitting of 60 MeV.
