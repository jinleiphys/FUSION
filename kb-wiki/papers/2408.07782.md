# Collective behaviour in proton number fluctuations seen in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2.4 GeV Au+Au collisions

> At energies of a few GeV per nucleon, nuclear collisions exhibit phenomena more complex than the individualistic nucleon interactions observed at much higher energies. From recent results on proton number fluctuations in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$~GeV obtained by the HADES experiment at GSI, we suggest that measuring the multiplicity distributions heavy-ion collisions can be used to probe density fluctuations associated with correlation phenomena. By using the combinant analysis, one can obtain new information contained in them and otherwise unavailable, which may broaden our knowledge of the particle interactions mechanism.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the oscillatory pattern of combinants extracted from proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at 2.4 GeV can be explained by a compound distribution (CBD) where a binomial distribution describes the number of proton bunches and a Poisson distribution describes the particles within each bunch, rather than by critical point phenomena or other dynamical effects.

## Method

The authors analyzed HADES experimental data on proton number fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV, extracting the multiplicity distribution P(N) within rapidity windows Delta y = +/- 0.5 and +/- 0.2 around midrapidity. They computed the recurrence relation r(N) and combinants C_j from the measured distributions, then fitted the data using a compound distribution (CBD) combining a binomial distribution (BD) for cluster production with a Poisson distribution (PD) for particle decay within clusters. The oscillation period of the combinants was used to extract the mean number of proton bunches <M> = <N>/lambda.

## Key numbers

* Mean proton number <N> = 16.44 with scaled variance omega = 1.40 for 0-5% centrality in Delta y = 0.5 window
* Mean proton number <N> = 7.60 with scaled variance omega = 1.20 for 0-5% centrality in Delta y = 0.2 window
* Oscillation half-period lambda = 3.6 for 0-5% centrality in Delta y = 0.5 window, decreasing to lambda = 1.6 for 25-30% centrality
* Number of proton bunches <M> = <N>/lambda is roughly constant at 4.7 with standard deviation sigma = 0.4 across all centralities and rapidity windows
* Two-particle correlation function <nu_2> = 0.025 for most central collisions, consistent across both Delta y = 0.5 and Delta y = 0.2 intervals
* CBD fit parameters for 0-5% centrality: K = 4, p = 0.911, lambda = 4.335

## Context

This work extends the combinant analysis method previously developed by Rybczynski (2018) to HADES data on proton number fluctuations reported by the HADES Collaboration (2020). The approach provides an alternative to standard fluctuation measures like factorial moments and cumulants used by Broniowski (2017) and Bzdak (2012), and addresses the acceptance-dependence issues discussed by Savchuk (2022). The results suggest that the observed fluctuations can be interpreted in terms of a fire-streak classical matter dynamical model, where the number of proton bunches corresponds to the number of overlapping nuclear tubes, with a transverse bunch radius roughly 3 times the nucleon radius. Further dynamical modeling calculations are needed to confirm whether this simple scenario applies to the broader spectrum of experimental data.
