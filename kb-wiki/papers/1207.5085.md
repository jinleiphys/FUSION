# Single-Œõ Hypernuclei in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory with parameter set FSU

> In this work, we conduct a study on the properties of single-Œõ hypernuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory with the parameter set FSU, where the isoscalar-isovector coupling has been included to soften the symmetry energy. In our model, a tensor coupling between œâ and Œõ is employed, which is found to be essential in obtaining the small spin-orbit interaction in single-Œõ hypernuclei. Our calculated values of Œõ single-particle energies are in good agreement with the known experimental data. As a comparison to other existing parameter sets, calculations are also carried out by using parameterizaions such as NL3 and NL3*. The results show that the parameter set FSU is as successful as those of NL3 and NL3* in terms of reproducing the properties of single-Œõ hypernuclei.

# Single-Œõ Hypernuclei in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory with parameter set FSU

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field parameter set FSU, which includes an isoscalar-isovector coupling to soften the symmetry energy, reproduces Œõ single-particle energies in single-Œõ hypernuclei as successfully as the established NL3 and NL3* parameter sets, provided that a tensor coupling between the œâ meson and the Œõ hyperon is included to obtain the small spin-orbit splitting.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Dirac equations for nucleons and Œõ hyperons self-consistently with the Klein-Gordon equations for œÉ, œâ, and œÅ mesons and the photon, using the FSU, NL3, and NL3* parameter sets for the nucleonic sector. The Œõ-meson coupling ratios were fixed as R_œâ = 2/3 (from the naive quark model) and R_œÉ adjusted to 0.619 (FSU), 0.620 (NL3*), and 0.621 (NL3) to reproduce experimental Œõ binding energies, with the tensor coupling strength set to f_œâ^Œõ = -g_œâ^Œõ. Calculations were performed for spherical hypernuclei using BCS pairing for open-shell cores.

## Context

This work extends the application of the FSU parameter set, originally developed by Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) for finite nuclei and nuclear matter, to single-Œõ hypernuclei, following prior RMF studies of hypernuclei by Rufa, Mares, Vretenar, and others. The tensor coupling between œâ and Œõ, first emphasized by Jennings, is shown to be essential for reproducing the small spin-orbit splitting observed experimentally (Hashimoto, 2006; Usmani). The results enable systematic comparisons of FSU with the widely used NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997) and NL3* (Lalazissis, 2009) parameter sets for strangeness physics, and provide a foundation for future studies of multi-Œõ hypernuclei and neutron star matter where the softened symmetry energy of FSU may be important.
