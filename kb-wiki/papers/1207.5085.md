# Single-Λ Hypernuclei in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory with parameter set FSU

> In this work, we conduct a study on the properties of single-Λ hypernuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory with the parameter set FSU, where the isoscalar-isovector coupling has been included to soften the symmetry energy. In our model, a tensor coupling between ω and Λ is employed, which is found to be essential in obtaining the small spin-orbit interaction in single-Λ hypernuclei. Our calculated values of Λ single-particle energies are in good agreement with the known experimental data. As a comparison to other existing parameter sets, calculations are also carried out by using parameterizaions such as NL3 and NL3*. The results show that the parameter set FSU is as successful as those of NL3 and NL3* in terms of reproducing the properties of single-Λ hypernuclei.

# Single-Λ Hypernuclei in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory with parameter set FSU

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field parameter set FSU, which includes an isoscalar-isovector coupling to soften the symmetry energy, reproduces Λ single-particle energies in single-Λ hypernuclei as successfully as the established NL3 and NL3* parameter sets, provided that a tensor coupling between the ω meson and the Λ hyperon is included to obtain the small spin-orbit splitting.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Dirac equations for nucleons and Λ hyperons self-consistently with the Klein-Gordon equations for σ, ω, and ρ mesons and the photon, using the FSU, NL3, and NL3* parameter sets for the nucleonic sector. The Λ-meson coupling ratios were fixed as R_ω = 2/3 (from the naive quark model) and R_σ adjusted to 0.619 (FSU), 0.620 (NL3*), and 0.621 (NL3) to reproduce experimental Λ binding energies, with the tensor coupling strength set to f_ω^Λ = -g_ω^Λ. Calculations were performed for spherical hypernuclei using BCS pairing for open-shell cores.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle energy for the 1s_{1/2} state in ¹⁶_ΛO: -12.88 MeV (FSU), compared to experimental -12.5 ± 0.35 MeV
- Λ single-particle energy for the 1s_{1/2} state in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: -26.26 MeV (FSU), compared to experimental -26.3 ± 0.8 MeV
- Spin-orbit splitting δ_Λ of the 1p orbit in ¹⁶_ΛO: -0.23 MeV with tensor coupling (FSU, NL3, NL3* all give this value), versus -1.41 MeV without tensor coupling (FSU)
- Compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter predicted by FSU: K = 230 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb predicted by FSU: R_n - R_p = 0.21 fm
- Λ binding energy for the 1s_{1/2} state approaches the nuclear matter limit ε_Λ = -28 MeV as mass number increases
- Proton spin-orbit splitting δ_P of 1p state in ¹⁶_ΛO: -6.99 MeV (FSU)
- Proton spin-orbit splitting δ_P of 1p state in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: -0.61 MeV (FSU)

## Context

This work extends the application of the FSU parameter set, originally developed by Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) for finite nuclei and nuclear matter, to single-Λ hypernuclei, following prior RMF studies of hypernuclei by Rufa, Mares, Vretenar, and others. The tensor coupling between ω and Λ, first emphasized by Jennings, is shown to be essential for reproducing the small spin-orbit splitting observed experimentally (Hashimoto, 2006; Usmani). The results enable systematic comparisons of FSU with the widely used NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997) and NL3* (Lalazissis, 2009) parameter sets for strangeness physics, and provide a foundation for future studies of multi-Λ hypernuclei and neutron star matter where the softened symmetry energy of FSU may be important.
