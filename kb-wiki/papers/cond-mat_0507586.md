# Phase diagram of a cold polarized Fermi gas

> We propose a phase diagram for a cold polarized atomic Fermi gas with zero-range interaction. We identify four main phases in the plane of density and polarization: the superfluid phase, the normal phase, the gapless superfluid phase, and the modulated phase. We argue that there exists a Lifshitz point at the junction of the normal, the gapless superfluid, and the modulated phases, and a splitting point where the superfluid, the gapless superfluid, and the modulated phases meet. We show that the physics near the splitting point is universal and derive an effective field theory describing it. We also show that subregions with one and two Fermi surfaces exist within the normal and the gapless superfluid phases.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a phase diagram for a cold polarized atomic Fermi gas with zero-range interaction, but the authors explicitly state that their proposal is an "educated guess" anchored on known phase structure in the BEC and BCS limits, the existence of the splitting point as at least a local minimum of the free energy, and the structure of the phase diagram around that point. The global stability of the splitting point depends on the condition that the crossover parameter κ₀ is less than κc, which the authors argue is supported by quantum Monte Carlo data but not by mean-field theory.

## Method
The authors construct a phase diagram in the plane of the diluteness parameter κ = -1/(na³) and the scaled polarization chemical potential η = H/Δ(μ,a), where Δ is the gap in the unpolarized gas. They identify the splitting point S at κ = κ₀ and η = 1, where the minimum of the fermion quasiparticle dispersion shifts from p=0 to p≠0, and derive an effective field theory for the low-energy quasiparticles and superfluid phonons near this point. The free energy is minimized as a function of superfluid velocity v_s to determine the phase boundaries, with the function f_h(x) capturing the shape of the free energy at a given polarization chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The splitting point S is located at κ = κ₀ and η = 1, where κ₀ < 0 (on the BEC side of the phase diagram).
- The first-order phase transitions occur at η₁ = 1 + h₁ α³ / [2(15π²)² ρ² β⁴ Δ] and η₂ = 1 + h₂ α³ / [2(15π²)² ρ² β⁴ Δ], with h₁ ≈ -0.067 and h₂ ≈ 0.502.
- The transition from a spherical shell to a solid ball of occupied fermion modes occurs at η₃ = 1 + α²/(4βΔ).
- At unitarity (κ=0), quantum Monte Carlo gives ξ ≈ 0.42 and Δ/μ ≈ 1.2, making the pressure ratio P_SF/P_N consistent with 1 within errors.
- In the BCS limit, the normal phase wins for η > 0.754, and the FFLO phase exists for η₁ < η < 0.754 with η₁ ≈ 1/√2.

## Context
This work extends the study of polarized Fermi gases from the BEC and BCS limits into the intermediate regime, building on prior work by Eagles, Leggett, and Nozieres on the unpolarized case, and by Bedaque, Pao, Wu, and Yip on polarized systems. The paper identifies a splitting point where the onset transition from the unpolarized superfluid to the gapless superfluid splits into two first-order transitions, and argues that mean-field calculations (such as those by Bedaque, Pao, and Wu) likely miss this point by overestimating the normal phase region. The results enable future investigation of asymmetric fermion pairing in neutron star cores and QCD at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials.
