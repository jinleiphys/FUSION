# Collective model for cluster motion in $^8$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O systems

> A microscopic $nα$ cluster model was applied to $^{8}$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O systems to investigate cluster motion in the ground state and radial excitation. In the microscopic calculation of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O using the generator coordinate method with the coordinate $D$ of the $α$-$α$ distance, excited states were obtained as the large-amplitude mode built on the ground state. A collective model was constructed to describe the cluster motion of these states by utilizing inputs from the microscopic cluster model such as the norm kernel and energy expectation values. Furthermore, the cluster model was extended by introducing the imaginary part of the coordinate $D$ to incorporate the dynamical effects on the collective mass. The collective wave function obtained with the collective model was found to be in reasonable agreement with the results of the generator coordinate method for energies, root-mean-square radii, and amplitude functions.

## Key claim

The authors construct a collective model for cluster motion in $^8$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O that can approximately reproduce the microscopic generator coordinate method (GCM) results for energies, root-mean-square radii, and amplitude functions, but only when the collective mass is derived from the imaginary part of the coordinate $D$ and the metric from the norm kernel; the model does not claim exact equivalence to the full microscopic calculation, particularly for the excited states where the GCM describes large-amplitude motion rather than small-amplitude vibrations.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic $n\alpha$ cluster model calculations using the Brink-Bloch wave functions for $^8$Be (2$\alpha$), $^{12}$C (3$\alpha$), and $^{16}$O (4$\alpha$) with the generator coordinate method (GCM) using the $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance $D$ as the generator coordinate, with 16 mesh points from 0.5 to 8.0 fm. They then constructed a collective Hamiltonian with a potential term taken from the diagonal energy expectation values $E^\pm(D)$ of the microscopic wave functions and a kinetic term that incorporates a $D$-dependent metric $\gamma_N(D)$ derived from the norm kernel and a $D$-dependent collective mass $M_P(D)$ obtained by introducing an imaginary part to the coordinate $D$ ($D \to D + iP/(2\hbar\nu)$) and evaluating the energy difference $\Delta E_P(D) = \hbar^2 P^2 / [2M_P(D)]$.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic $n\alpha$ cluster model approach of Brink (1966) and the generator coordinate method of Hill and Wheeler (1953) and Griffin (1957) to construct a practical collective Hamiltonian for cluster motion. The authors compare their collective model results with the full GCM calculations and with the small-amplitude approximation using two-basis diagonalization, finding that the GCM states in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are large-amplitude motions rather than small-amplitude vibrations. The approach enables future studies of cluster dynamics without performing the full GCM diagonalization, and the authors note that the theoretical radial excitation modes ($+$)$_2$ in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O may couple with other degrees of freedom including $\alpha$-cluster motion and single-particle excitation, potentially contributing to the giant monopole resonance.
