# Hard Probe of Soft Matter Geometry and Fluctuations from RHIC to LHC

> We report results on event-by-event hard probe of soft matter geometry and fluctuations in heavy ion collisions. Geometric data ($v_2$ of high $p_t$ hadrons) from RHIC plus LHC clearly favors jet "monography" model with strong near-Tc enhancement of jet-medium interaction strength which also implies a less opaque medium at LHC. We also quantify the jet responses to all harmonic anisotropy $v_n$($n=1,2,3,4,5,6$) and their manifestation in hard-soft azimuthal correlations.

## Key claim

Geometric data (v2 of high pT hadrons) from RHIC and LHC favors the jet "monography" model with strong near-Tc enhancement of jet-medium interaction strength, which also implies a less opaque medium at LHC compared to RHIC, but only the NTcE model is consistent with all data while the L2 and L3 models each fail at one of the two colliders.

## Method

The study used a Monte-Carlo Glauber simulation to compute RAA(phi) and its Fourier harmonics vn event-by-event for three geometric models of jet quenching: the NTcE model (strong near-Tc enhancement of jet-medium interaction strength with quadratic path-length dependence), the L2 model (constant interaction strength with quadratic path-length dependence), and the L3 model (constant interaction strength with cubic path-length dependence). The computed v2 values for high pT hadrons were compared with experimental data from RHIC at 0.2 TeV and LHC at 2.76 TeV. The jet response to harmonic fluctuations up to n=6 was quantified, and hard-soft azimuthal correlations were constructed from the computed jet response vn^h combined with hydro response vn^s from literature.

## Key numbers

- RHIC collision energy: 0.2 TeV; LHC collision energy: 2.76 TeV
- Jet response to harmonic fluctuations quantified for n=1 through n=6
- Central RAA at LHC is close to that at RHIC despite multiplicity being doubled at LHC

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of jet quenching anisotropy by Gyulassy (2001), Shuryak (2002), and Drees (2005), and specifically builds on the proposal by Liao (2009) that jet-medium interaction is strongly enhanced in the near-Tc plasma. It contrasts with the L2 model (jet "tomography") and the L3 model (AdS/CFT-motivated "holography") previously studied by Jia (2010) and Jia (2011). The quantification of jet response to harmonic fluctuations up to n=6, first attempted by Zhang (2012), enables new constraints on initial condition fluctuations independent of bulk collective expansion studies. The finding of a less opaque medium at LHC is consistent with multiple independent studies including GLV/WHDG/CUJET, polytrope models, geometric models, data scaling analysis, and lattice attempts.
