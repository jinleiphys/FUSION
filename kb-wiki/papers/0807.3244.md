# Open and Hidden Strangeness Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions

> We present an overview of the description of K and eta meson productions in nucleon-nucleon collisions within an effective Lagrangian model where meson production proceeds via excitation, propagation and subsequent decay of intermediate baryonic resonant states. The $K$ meson contains a strange quark ($s$) or antiquark ($\bar s$) while the $η$ meson has hidden strangeness as it contains some component of the $s{\bar s}$ pair. Strange meson production is expected to provide information on the manifestation of quantum chromodynamics in the non-perturbative regime of energies larger than that of the low energy pion physics. We discuss specific examples where proper understanding of the experimental data for these reactions is still lacking.

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian model, using the same set of vertex parameters, can describe most data on open and hidden strangeness production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, but it underpredicts the total cross section for the \(pp \to pp\eta\) reaction at excess energies below 15 MeV, and it is incompatible with the published data for the \(pp \to n\Sigma^+K^+\) reaction.

## Method

The author uses a tree-level effective Lagrangian model where meson production proceeds via excitation, propagation, and decay of intermediate baryonic resonant states (\(N^*(1535)\), \(N^*(1650)\), \(N^*(1710)\), \(N^*(1720)\)). The initial nucleon-nucleon interaction is modeled by exchange of \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\sigma\) mesons, with coupling constants and form factors fitted to elastic \(NN\) scattering data from 400 MeV to 4.0 GeV. Final state interactions (FSI) among the three outgoing particles are included via a Watson-Migdal factorization approach using Jost functions and scattering length parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the \(pp \to pp\eta\) reaction, the model reproduces data for excess energies \(\varepsilon\) in the range of 15 – 130 MeV, but underpredicts data for \(\varepsilon < 15\) MeV.
- The best description of the \(pp \to pp\eta\) data is provided by an \(\eta N\) scattering amplitude corresponding to \(a_{\eta N} = 0.51 + i 0.26\).
- For the \(pp \to n\Sigma^+K^+\) reaction, the lower energy data points at excess energies of 13 MeV and 60 MeV (COSY-11) are at least an order of magnitude larger than the model calculations, while the data point at 129 MeV (COSY-ANKE) is smaller than the theoretical results by a factor of about 3.
- The model describes the total cross sections for \(pp \to p\Lambda K^+\) and \(pp \to p\Sigma^0K^+\) reactions well across the entire range of beam energies studied.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Shyam (1999) to describe recent data from COSY-11 and COSY-ANKE collaborations (Mos02, Roz06, Val07) on \(\eta\) and kaon production. The model is used to extract the \(\eta N\) scattering length, which is compared to values from coupled-channels analyses (Feu98, Gar02, Luz02, Gas03, Lee08) and is about half the real part of the "preferred" set of Green (2005). The unresolved discrepancy in the \(pp \to pp\eta\) reaction at very low excess energies and the contrasting data for \(pp \to n\Sigma^+K^+\) motivate future measurements by the COSY-ANKE group and further theoretical work, including possible inclusion of the \(D_{13}(1520)\) resonance with coupling constants consistent with coupled-channels analyses (Shk05, Uso06).
