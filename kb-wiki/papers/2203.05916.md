# Revisiting the chiral effective action in holographic models

> We obtain the pion decay constant and coefficients of fourth derivative terms in the chiral Lagrangian for massless quarks in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. We extract these quantities from the two-pion scattering amplitude, which we compute directly in the holographic dual through tree-level Witten diagrams. Identification of the low energy coefficients in the chiral action is subtle as their values will be shifted when the tower of massive vector bosons are integrated out. Indeed, by a direct comparison with the existing standard procedure of constructing the chiral action with radial modes in the gravity dual, we explicitly show that there are finite 't Hooft coupling corrections that have been missed. This suggests that past derivations of effective actions from holographic models may have to be revisited and future derivations more carefully considered.

## Key claim

The authors show that the low-energy coefficients (LECs) of the chiral Lagrangian extracted from the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model receive finite 't Hooft coupling corrections that were missed in previous derivations, meaning that past identifications of LECs from holographic effective actions must be revisited because the naive contact terms in the action are not the full story once vector meson exchange contributions are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors compute the two-pion scattering amplitude directly in the holographic dual through tree-level Witten diagrams in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with two flavors of massless quarks, using the D8-brane action expanded to fourth order in the field strength. They extract the pion decay constant and the coefficients of fourth-derivative terms in the chiral Lagrangian by matching the low-energy expansion of the holographic scattering amplitude to the Weinberg amplitude, carefully distinguishing between contributions from contact interactions and those from vector meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) is obtained from the two-point function of the axial current in the holographic model.
- The combination \(e_S^2 f_\pi^2 \simeq 0.51\) appears in the original WSS action, where \(e_S\) is a coupling constant in the quartic pion term.
- The shift in LECs from integrating out a vector meson is \(\Delta L_2^V = -\Delta L_1^V = \frac{f_\pi^4}{4} \frac{g_{V\pi\pi}^2}{m_V^4}\), where \(g_{V\pi\pi}\) is the vector-pion coupling and \(m_V\) the vector meson mass.
- The 't Hooft coupling enters through the expansion parameter \(\frac{\pi \alpha'}{L^2} = \frac{27\pi}{4\lambda_{\text{YM}}}\), where \(\lambda_{\text{YM}} = g_{\text{YM}}^2 N_c\).
- The Kaluza-Klein mass scale is \(M_{\text{KK}} = (3/2) U_{\text{KK}}^{1/2}/R^{3/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the original derivation of the chiral effective action in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by Sakai and Sugimoto (2005), showing that the LECs originally quoted arise from vector meson exchange diagrams rather than from contact interactions, and that additional higher-derivative corrections from the DBI action produce finite 't Hooft coupling corrections that were previously neglected. The method follows the approach of Hoyos, Jokela, and collaborators (2019, 2020) for computing scattering amplitudes directly from Witten diagrams. This enables future holographic calculations of low-energy constants to correctly account for both contact and exchange contributions when extracting the chiral Lagrangian from holographic models.
