# A Simultaneous Description of Hadron and Jet Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a global fit to all data on the suppression of high energy jets and high energy hadrons in the most central heavy ion collisions at the LHC for two different collision energies, within a hybrid strong/weak coupling quenching model. Even though the measured suppression factors for hadrons and jets differ significantly from one another and appear to asymptote to different values in the high energy limit, we obtain a simultaneous description of all these data after constraining the value of a single model parameter. We use our model to investigate the origin of the difference between the observed suppression of jets and hadrons and relate it, quantitatively, to the observed modification of the jet fragmentation function in jets that have been modified by passage through the medium produced in heavy ion collisions. In particular, the observed increase in the fraction of hard fragments in medium-modified jets, which indicates that jets with the fewest hardest fragments lose the least energy, corresponds quantitatively to the observed difference between the suppression of hadrons and jets. We argue that a harder fragmentation pattern for jets with a given energy after quenching is a generic feature of any mechanism for the interaction between jets and the medium that they traverse that yields a larger suppression for wider jets. We also compare the results of our global fit to LHC data to measurements of the suppression of high energy hadrons in RHIC collisions, and find that with its parameter chosen to fit the LHC data our model is inconsistent with the RHIC data at the $3σ$ level, suggesting that hard probes interact more strongly with the less hot quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC.

## Key claim

The hybrid strong/weak coupling model can simultaneously describe the suppression of both high-energy hadrons and jets in LHC heavy ion collisions using a single free parameter, even though the measured suppression factors for hadrons and jets differ significantly and appear to asymptote to different values in the high energy limit; the model is inconsistent with RHIC data at the 3 sigma level when its parameter is fixed to LHC data.

## Method

The authors employed a hybrid model combining perturbative QCD parton showering via Pythia 8.230 with holographic energy loss rates from strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, where the energy loss of partons traversing the quark-gluon plasma is governed by a single free parameter kappa_sc that controls the stopping distance. They performed a global chi-squared fit to nine LHC data sets on hadron and jet suppression in central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 and 5.02 TeV, using hydrodynamic profiles for the expanding QGP and testing two values of the plasma resolution length (L_res=0 and L_res=2/(pi T)) and two values of the critical temperature (T_c=145 and 170 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid strong/weak coupling model developed in Casalderrey-Solana (2014), Casalderrey-Solana (2015), Casalderrey-Solana (2016), and Hulcher (2017) by performing the first global fit to both hadron and jet suppression data simultaneously. The finding that narrow jets lose less energy than wide jets provides a generic explanation for the observed difference between hadron and jet suppression, and the same physics predicts an enhancement in the fragmentation function ratio at high z that agrees with ATLAS measurements. The tension with RHIC data suggests that hard probes interact more strongly with the lower temperature QGP produced at RHIC, motivating future higher statistics measurements at both RHIC and LHC to constrain the plasma resolution length and the temperature dependence of energy loss.
