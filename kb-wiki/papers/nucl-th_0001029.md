# Sensitivities of the Proton-Nucleus Elastical Scattering Observables of 6He and 8He at Intermediate Energies

> We investigate the use of proton-nucleus elastic scattering experiments using secondary beams of 6He and 8He to determine the physical structure of these nuclei. The sensitivity of these experiments to nuclear structure is examined by using four different nuclear structure models with different spatial features using a full-folding optical potential model. The results show that elastic scattering at intermediate energies (<100 MeV per nucleon) is not a good constraint to be used to determine features of structure. Therefore researchers should look elsewhere to put constraints on the ground state wave function of the 6He and 8He nuclei.

## Key claim

Proton-nucleus elastic scattering at intermediate energies (below 100 MeV per nucleon) is not a good constraint for determining the ground state structure of ⁶He and ⁸He, because four highly disparate nuclear structure models all produce differential cross sections that agree with the limited existing data, making it impossible to distinguish between models with or without a halo.

## Method

The authors constructed first-order full-folding optical potentials for proton-⁶He and proton-⁸He elastic scattering using four different nuclear structure models as input: a boot-strap model (BS), a chiral effective field theory model (FZ4), a Dirac-Hartree model (DH), and a cluster orbit shell model approximation (COSMA). All calculations used the Nijmegen I nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the resulting elastic scattering observables (differential cross section, analyzing power, and spin rotation function) were computed at laboratory energies between 66 MeV and 100 MeV and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- ⁶He root-mean-square matter radii from the four models: BS 2.90 fm, FZ4 2.54 fm, DH 3.75 fm, COSMA 2.57 fm; experimental value 2.39 fm (Tanihata)
- ⁸He root-mean-square matter radii: BS 2.84 fm, FZ4 2.57 fm, DH 2.75 fm, COSMA 2.52 fm; experimental value 2.49 fm (Tanihata)
- Only the COSMA model of ⁶He produced a positive separation distance S_d = +0.02 fm, indicating a halo; all other models gave negative S_d values
- The two-neutron separation energy for ⁶He: BS 0.98 MeV, FZ4 1.80 MeV, DH 0.19 MeV; experimental value 0.97 MeV
- The four-neutron separation energy for ⁸He: BS 3.12 MeV, FZ4 3.47 MeV, DH 2.68 MeV; experimental value 3.1 MeV
- At 72.5 MeV for ⁸He, experimental data extend to approximately 65 degrees center-of-mass angle; distinguishing between models would require measurements with less than 20% error at angles above 65 degrees

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Korsheninnikov et al. (1990s) and Chulkov (1990s), who also concluded that elastic scattering is not a promising tool for determining valence neutron structure, and contrasts with claims by Karataglidis et al. (1990s) that ⁸He is not a halo nucleus based on elastic scattering data. The authors recommend that researchers pursue inelastic hadron reactions, transfer reactions, Coulomb breakup, or electron scattering to constrain the ground state wave functions of ⁶He and ⁸He.
