# Partonic effects on the elliptic flow at relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at RHIC is studied in a multiphase transport model. By converting the strings in the high energy density regions into partons, we find that the final elliptic flow is sensitive to the parton scattering cross section. To reproduce the large elliptic flow observed in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s=130A$ GeV requires a parton scattering cross section of about 6 mb. We also study the dependence of the elliptic flow on the particle multiplicity, transverse momentum, and particle mass.

## Key claim

To reproduce the large elliptic flow observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC requires a parton scattering cross section of about 6 mb, but only if the initial strings in high energy density regions are first converted into partons; without this string melting, the default AMPT model gives a much smaller elliptic flow that is insensitive to the parton cross section.

## Method

The authors extended the multiphase transport model (AMPT) by taking initial conditions from HIJING, letting strings fragment to hadrons via LUND fragmentation, then converting those hadrons into partons (mesons to quark-antiquark pairs, baryons to three quarks) with quark masses from PYTHIA. These partons underwent elastic scatterings via the ZPC parton cascade with a universal cross section parameterized by an effective screening mass, and were later hadronized by coalescence of nearest partons into mesons and baryons, after which the hadrons were fed into the ART hadronic transport model for final-state rescatterings.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross section of about 6 mb is required to reproduce the observed elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV.
- Quark masses used in the conversion: m_u=5.6 MeV, m_d=9.9 MeV, m_s=199 MeV.
- Hadron formation time tau_0^H=0.7 fm/c in the hadron rest frame.
- Parton formation time tau_0^P=1/Q, where Q is the transverse mass of the parent hadron.
- The strong coupling constant alpha_s was taken as 0.47.
- The elliptic flow develops mostly within the first 5 fm/c for both 3 mb and 6 mb parton cross sections.
- The transverse energy at midrapidity decreases by about 30% within a couple of fm/c.
- The average transverse energy per parton saturates at about 20% below its initial value.
- For the centrality dependence, charged particles were selected within pseudorapidity eta in (-1.3, 1.3) and transverse momentum p_t in (0.1, 2.1) GeV/c.
- Changing the parton formation time by a factor of 2 or setting hadron formation time to 0.01 fm/c changed the final charged particle v_2 by less than 10% for p_perp < 2 GeV/c.
- For less central events (N_ch/N_max < 0.6), the elliptic flow at 200A GeV increased by no more than 0.01 in magnitude compared to 130A GeV for the same parton cross section.

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model of Zhang, Lin, and Ko (2000) by adding a schematic string-melting mechanism to convert soft strings into partons, addressing the fact that the initial energy density at RHIC is more than an order of magnitude above the critical QCD energy density (~1 GeV/fm^3). The results contradict purely hadronic transport models (RQMD, UrQMD) which give smaller elliptic flow than observed, and are consistent with hydrodynamic models (Huovinen 2001) in showing a linear rise of v_2 with p_t at low transverse momentum. The study enables determination of the partonic interaction strength from elliptic flow data and motivates future work including inelastic parton scatterings and particle subdivision in the parton cascade.
