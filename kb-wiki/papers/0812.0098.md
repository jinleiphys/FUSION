# Coupled-channel effects in kaon pair production

> Two coupled channel effects connected with kaon pair production in proton-proton collisions are discussed. (1) Although there is ample evidence that the antikaon is strongly attracted to the recoil protons in pp -> K+pK-p, residual effects of the K+K- interaction are seen, including a possible cusp at the K0\bar{K}0 threshold. This is investigated within a simple K-matrix approach. (2) The production rates and invariant mass distributions for pp -> K+p{K-p} and pp -> K+p{pi0 Sigma0} are related using a separable potential description of the coupled K-p/pi0 Sigma0 channels. It can be plausibly argued that this pair of reactions is driven through the production of the Lambda(1405).

# Coupled-channel effects in kaon pair production

## Key claim
Residual effects of the K+K- interaction, including a possible cusp at the K0K̅0 threshold, are seen in pp → K+pK-p data even though the antikaon is strongly attracted to the recoil protons; the production rates and invariant mass distributions for pp → K+p{K-p} and pp → K+p{π0Σ0} can be related through a separable potential description of the coupled K-p/π0Σ0 channels, plausibly driven by the Λ(1405), though the results are preliminary and sensitive to the choice of the preparation vector.

## Method
The K+K- final state interaction was investigated using a K-matrix approach with s-wave scattering lengths in isospin-0 and isospin-1 channels, fitting the ratio of bare production amplitudes to ANKE data at 2.65, 2.70, and 2.83 GeV. For the coupled K-p/π0Σ0 system, a separable potential model with Yamaguchi form factors was solved algebraically to obtain the T-matrix, then coupled perturbatively to an initial pp channel to predict invariant mass spectra and cross section ratios at a beam momentum of 3.65 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The K-p scattering length used in the final state interaction approximation is |a| ≈ 1.5 fm.
- The K0K̅0 threshold is at 995.3 MeV/c² and the K+K- threshold at 987.4 MeV/c².
- Best fit to the K+K- invariant mass data gives |B1/B0|² = 0.38 (+0.24, -0.14), indicating dominant isospin-zero kaon pair production.
- The fitted s-wave scattering lengths are A1 = (0.1 ± 0.1) + i(0.7 ± 0.1) fm and A0 = (-0.45 ± 0.2) + i(1.63 ± 0.2) fm.
- The separable potential model yields a Λ(1405) pole at W = (1406.5 - 25i) MeV.
- The predicted cross section ratio R(Kπ) = σ(pp → pK+{pK-}) / σ(pp → pK+{Σπ}0) = 9.4 × 10⁻³, compared to the experimental value of (22 ± 8) × 10⁻³.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Maeda et al. (2008) on pp → ppK+K- by adding a K+K- final state interaction to the existing pp and K-p final state interactions, and relates this to the pp → pK+Σ0π0 data of the ANKE collaboration. The separable potential description follows the coupled-channel formalism of Gal (2007) for the I=0 πΣ/K̅N system. The results suggest that both reactions are driven by Λ(1405) production, and that K̅K scalar resonances are unlikely to contribute significantly to pp → pK+pK- except through final state interactions. The approach enables future combined dynamical modeling of these two reactions and addresses the question of whether the Λ(1405) is a single resonance or two closely spaced states, as discussed by Geng and Oset (2008).
