## Key claim

The authors calculate that the production cross section of the hypertriton ground state in the $^3$He($\pi^-$, $K^0$) reaction is approximately 3 $\mu$b/sr at incident pion momenta of 1.05â€“1.20 GeV/$c$ and a kaon scattering angle of 3Â°, but this result depends critically on including recoil effects (a factor of $M_C/M_A = 2/3$ for the $^3$He target), without which the cross section is reduced by an order of magnitude or more.

## Method

The authors use the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) combined with the continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) method to describe the $NN$ continuum states above the $N+N+\Lambda$ breakup threshold in $^3$He($\pi$, $K$) reactions. They calculate inclusive differential cross sections using a Green's function method, with the $\pi N \to \Lambda K$ amplitude in nuclear medium obtained by optimal Fermi averaging, and the $\Lambda N$ potential taken as a single Gaussian form fitted to the NSC97f scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda$ binding energy of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) is $B_\Lambda = 130 \pm 50$ keV
- The integrated production cross section of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) in the $^3$He($\pi^-$, $K^0$) reaction at $p_\pi = 1.20$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab} = 3^\circ$ is $d\sigma/d\Omega = 3.13$ $\mu$b/sr
- At $p_\pi = 1.05$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab} = 3^\circ$, the production cross section of $^3_\Lambda$H(1/2$^+$) is $d\sigma/d\Omega = 3.07$ $\mu$b/sr
- The momentum transfer $q_\Lambda$ for the ($\pi$, $K$) reaction on $^3$He at $p_\pi = 1.20$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab} = 3^\circ$ is 326 MeV/$c$
- The effective momentum transfer $q_\Lambda^{\rm eff}$ (including the recoil factor of 2/3) at $p_\pi = 1.20$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab} = 3^\circ$ is 217 MeV/$c$
- The elementary $\pi N \to \Lambda K$ cross section in medium at $p_\pi = 1.05$ GeV/$c$ and $\theta_{\rm lab} = 3^\circ$ is 344 $\mu$b/sr, compared to 463 $\mu$b/sr for the free amplitude

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDCC approach (Harada 2015) to the specific case of $^3$He($\pi$, $K$) reactions, addressing the hypertriton lifetime puzzle raised by the HypHI Collaboration (Rappold 2013) and the ALICE Collaboration (2018). The calculations provide predictions for planned experiments at J-PARC using $^3$He($K^-$, $\pi^0$) and $^3$He($\pi^-$, $K^0$) reactions, and enable comparison with three-body calculations that suggest a possible $pp\Lambda$ resonance or virtual state near the $p+p+\Lambda$ threshold.
