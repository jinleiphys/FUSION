# $D \bar{D}^*$ and $πψ$ interactions in a unitary coupled-channel approximation

> The $D \bar{D}^*$ interaction via a $πψ$ intermediate state is studied carefully in the isospin $I=1$ sector. By solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the unitary coupled-channel approximation, we obtain the S-wave amplitude as a function of the total energy of the system in the center of mass frame. A resonance state is generated dynamically in the 3900MeV region, which might correspond to the $Zc(3900)$ particle. Moreover, the loop function of a vector meson and a pseudoscalar meson is deduced explicitly in the dimensional regularization scheme and the contribution of the longitudinal part of the vector meson propagator is taken into account. The initial and final polarization vectors in the vertex of the vector meson and the pseudoscalar meson are eliminated when the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved, and it is certified that the amplitude is still unitary in the calculation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a resonance state dynamically generated near 3900 MeV in the isospin I=1 sector of the coupled D D* and pi psi system can be associated with the Zc(3900) particle, but the authors hedge that the influence of the longitudinal part of the vector meson propagator is not important and that the results are not sensitive to the chosen coupling constants.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for D D* contact interactions and D D* psi pi vertices based on heavy quark symmetry and chiral perturbation theory. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the unitary coupled-channel approximation for the S-wave amplitude, using a loop function for a vector meson and a pseudoscalar meson derived in dimensional regularization that includes the longitudinal part of the vector meson propagator. The polarization vectors of the initial and final vector mesons are eliminated from the kernel, and the unitarity of the amplitude is verified.

## Key numbers

- The resonance pole is found at 3876 - i9 MeV in the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane when the longitudinal part of the vector meson propagator is included.
- Without the longitudinal part, the pole appears at 3876 - i33 MeV.
- The coupling of the resonance to D D* is g = 2.4 + i1.0 (|g| = 2.6), and to pi psi is g = 0.1 - i0.4 (|g| = 0.4).
- The subtraction constant in the loop function is fixed to a = -2, and the regularization scale is mu = 500 MeV.
- The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Zc(3900) as a D D* molecule state, following the approach of Gong (2016) for the effective Lagrangian. It differs from earlier treatments by explicitly including the longitudinal part of the vector meson propagator in the loop function, which was neglected in works such as Nagahiro (2008), Molina (2008), and Geng (2008). The calculation enables a more complete unitary treatment of the D D* and pi psi coupled channels and provides a dynamically generated resonance that can be compared with experimental data from BESIII (2013) and Belle (2013).
