# Impact of the neutron-star deformability on equation of state parameters

> We use a Bayesian inference analysis to explore the sensitivity of Taylor expansion parameters of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) to the neutron star dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$) on 1 to 2 solar masses neutron stars. A global power law dependence between tidal deformability and compactness parameter (M/R) is verified over this mass region. To avoid superfluous correlations between the expansion parameters, we use a correlation-free EOS model based on a recently published meta-modeling approach. We find that assumptions in the prior distribution strongly influence the constraints on $Λ$. The $Λ$ constraints obtained from the neutron star merger event GW170817 prefer low values of $L_\text{sym}$ and $K_\text{sym}$, for a canonical neutron star with 1.4 solar mass. For neutron star with mass $<1.6$ solar mass, $L_\text{sym}$ and $K_\text{sym}$ are highly correlated with the tidal deformability. For more massive neutron stars, the tidal deformability is more strongly correlated with higher order Taylor expansion parameters.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 prefer low values of the symmetry energy slope parameter $L_\text{sym}$ and the symmetry energy curvature parameter $K_\text{sym}$ for a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star, but this conclusion is strongly influenced by the choice of prior distributions for the equation of state parameters.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using 1,500,000 sampled equations of state from a correlation-free meta-modeling approach (ELFc) to explore the sensitivity of Taylor expansion parameters of the nuclear equation of state to the neutron star dimensionless tidal deformability. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to calculate tidal deformabilities for neutron stars with masses from 1 to 2 solar masses, then applied a likelihood function based on the GW170817 constraint of $\Lambda = 190^{+390}_{-120}$ along with filter conditions for stability, causality, and a maximum mass of at least 2.04 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: $\Lambda = 190^{+390}_{-120}$ (asymmetric Gaussian used for likelihood)
- Saturation density fixed at $\rho_0 = 0.155\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Saturation energy fixed at $E_{\text{sat}} = -15.8\ \text{MeV}$
- Maximum mass condition: EOS must produce a neutron star of at least 2.04 solar masses
- 682,652 of 1,500,000 sampled EOS satisfied all constraints; only 11,711 EOS applied without switching to the stiffest EOS
- Posterior average for $L_\text{sym}$: $71.6 \pm 16.5\ \text{MeV}$ (prior weighted average: $69.0 \pm 20.1\ \text{MeV}$)
- Posterior average for $K_\text{sym}$: $-76.9 \pm 66.0\ \text{MeV}$ (prior weighted average: $-45.3 \pm 70.8\ \text{MeV}$)
- Posterior average for $K_\text{sat}$: $245 \pm 23\ \text{MeV}$ (prior weighted average: $248 \pm 18.3\ \text{MeV}$)
- Transition density formula: $\rho_T = (-3.75\times10^{-4} L_{\text{sym}} + 0.0963)\ \text{fm}^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies that placed tidal deformability constraints on Taylor expansion parameters in two-dimensional parameter planes (Zhang 2013, Tsang 2019), on diverse sets of models (Malik 2018, Gil 2019, Carson 2019), and with Bayesian analysis on equations of state from chiral effective field theory (Lim 2018). The authors use a less restrictive meta-modeling approach (Margueron 2018) that avoids the superfluous correlations present in Skyrme-type or relativistic mean field parameterizations, and explore a larger parameter space including higher order terms. The results enable improved understanding of the mass dependence of the relation between tidal deformability and compactness parameter, and show that for neutron stars with mass below 1.6 solar masses, $L_\text{sym}$ and $K_\text{sym}$ are highly correlated with tidal deformability, while for more massive neutron stars the tidal deformability correlates more strongly with higher order Taylor expansion parameters.
