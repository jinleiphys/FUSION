# Neutrinos and their interactions

> We present a short overview of the importance of the study of "Neutrino Interactions in the Intermediate and High Energy Region", with an introduction to the neutrinos and a very brief description about the collection of invited articles.

## Key claim

This editorial introduces a topical volume on neutrino interactions in the intermediate and high energy region, but it is a review-type overview of the field rather than presenting a single new experimental or theoretical result. The paper's main purpose is to summarize the scope of seventeen invited articles covering quasielastic, inelastic, and deep inelastic scattering processes.

## Method

The paper is an editorial that provides a historical overview of neutrino physics from Pauli's 1930 hypothesis through the Standard Model, describes the current experimental landscape (including MINERvA, NOvA, MicroBooNE, ArgoNeuT, and IceCube), and outlines the theoretical challenges in modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions. It summarizes the contents of seventeen contributed articles arranged into experimental, theoretical, and phenomenological categories, covering topics from quasielastic scattering to deep inelastic processes and Monte Carlo generators (GENIE and NEUT).

## Key numbers

- The MINERvA experiment took data from 2009 to 2019 using neutrino beams that peak at 3 GeV (Low-Energy range) and 6 GeV (Medium-Energy range)
- For precision neutrino oscillation experiments, an accuracy of 2-3% in systematics is desired, but current uncertainties in neutrino-nucleus cross sections are 25-30%
- The shallow inelastic scattering (SIS) region is defined for invariant mass W between M + m_pi and 2 GeV, while the DIS region requires Q^2 >= 1 GeV^2 and W >= 2 GeV
- The NEUT Monte Carlo generator simulates interactions for neutrinos between 100 MeV and a few TeV of energy

## Context

This editorial introduces a topical volume of the European Physical Journal - Special Topics, extending the discussions from recent community meetings such as Snowmass and NUSTEC (2019). It provides context for the seventeen invited articles that address the ongoing challenge of understanding neutrino-nucleus interactions with sufficient precision for next-generation oscillation experiments like DUNE. The paper references prior work by Lipari (1994) on cross section parameterizations, and notes that the field lacks consensus on nuclear models as highlighted by NuSTEC (2017) and Katori (2016).
