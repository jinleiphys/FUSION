# The Linear Sigma-Model in the 1/N-Expansion via Dynamical Boson Mappings and Applications to $ππ$-Scattering

> We present a non-perturbative method for the study of the O(N+1)-version of the linear sigma-model. Using boson-mapping techniques, in close analogy to those well-known for fermionic systems, we obtain a systematic 1/N-expansion for the Hamiltonian which is symmetry-conserving order by order. The leading order for the Hamiltonian is evaluated explicitly and we apply the method to $ππ$-scattering, in deriving the T-matrix to leading order.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative method for the O(N+1) linear sigma-model using boson-mapping techniques that yields a systematic 1/N-expansion of the Hamiltonian which is symmetry-conserving order by order, but only for a cut-off version of the theory since the renormalized coupling constant vanishes when the cut-off is removed, making the theory trivial in the infinite cut-off limit.

## Method

The authors apply boson-mapping techniques, analogous to those used for fermionic systems in nuclear physics, to bosonize pion-pair operators using a Holstein-Primakoff-type mapping. They expand the Hamiltonian of the O(N+1) linear sigma-model in powers of 1/N by introducing ideal boson operators for pion pairs and sigma bosons, then determine the Hartree-Bogoliubov basis variationally and diagonalize the quadratic part of the Hamiltonian via a Bogoliubov rotation that mixes quasisigma bosons with bosonized pion pairs. The T-matrix for pion-pion scattering is derived to leading order from this diagonalized Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass in the large-N limit is given by m_pi^2 = c/s, where c is the explicit symmetry-breaking parameter and s is the condensate, with the pion becoming a Goldstone boson (m_pi = 0) in the chiral limit c = 0.
- The sigma mass in the large-N limit is m_sigma^2 = c/s + 2 lambda^2 s^2 / [1 - lambda^2 Sigma_bar_pipi(m_sigma^2)], where lambda is the renormalized coupling and Sigma_bar_pipi is the regularized pipi bubble.
- The Ward identity for the sigmapipi vertex is obeyed: m_sigma^2 - m_pi^2 = [2 lambda^2 s / (1 - lambda^2 Sigma_bar_pipi(m_sigma^2))] * s.
- The quasipion mass at the Hartree-Bogoliubov level is E_pi^2 = mu_0^2 + lambda_0^2 [I_pi + s^2], where I_pi is the quasipion tadpole, and the condensate satisfies c/s = mu_0^2 + lambda_0^2 [I_pi + s^2].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB-RPA treatment of the linear sigma-model (Aouissat, Schuck, and Wambach, 1995) by identifying the origin of spurious pion mass generation at the mean-field level and developing a systematic 1/N-expansion that avoids this problem. The approach builds on boson expansion methods developed in nuclear physics by Marshalek (1974, 1993) and by Curutchet, Dukelsky, Dussel, and Fendrik (1989) and Bijker, Pittel, and Dukelsky (1994) for interacting Bose systems. The method enables the derivation of a unitary pion-pion scattering equation consistent with chiral symmetry constraints, with the possibility of systematic 1/N corrections for quantitative studies at N=3.
