# Nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay within mechanisms involving light Majorana neutrino mass and right-handed current

> Employing the projected-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model in conjunction with four different parametrizations of pairing plus multipolar effective two body interaction and three different parametrizations of Jastrow short range correlations, nuclear transition matrix elements for the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes are calculated within mechanisms involving light Majorana neutrino mass and right handed current. Statistically, model specific uncertainties in sets of twelve nuclear transition matrix elements are estimated by calculating the averages along with the standard deviations. For the considered nuclei, \ the most stringent extracted on-axis limits on the effective light Majorana neutrino mass $<m_{ν}>$, the effective weak coupling of right-handed leptonic current with right-handed hadronic current $<λ>$, and the effective weak coupling of right-handed leptonic current with left-handed hadronic current $<η>$ \ from the observed limit on half-life $T_{1/2}^{0ν}$ of $^{130}$Te isotope are $0.33$ eV, $4.57\times 10^{-7}$ and $4.72\times 10^{-9}$, respectively.

## Key claim

Using the projected-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model with four different parametrizations of the effective two-body interaction and three different parametrizations of Jastrow short-range correlations, the authors calculate nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay of seven isotopes under both light Majorana neutrino mass and right-handed current mechanisms, but they neglect induced pseudoscalar terms in the nonrelativistic reduction of the right-handed V+A current, which they state will not apparently change the final conclusions.

## Method

The PHFB model was employed with four different parametrizations of pairing plus multipolar effective two-body interaction (PQQ1, PQQ2, PQQHH1, PQQHH2) and three different parametrizations of Jastrow short-range correlations (SRC1, SRC2, SRC3 corresponding to Miller-Spencer, Argonne NN, and CD-Bonn potentials). Sets of twelve nuclear transition matrix elements (NTMEs) were calculated for the 0+ to 0+ transition of neutrinoless double-beta decay in 94,96Zr, 100Mo, 110Pd, 128,130Te, and 150Nd isotopes, including the effects of finite nucleon size and short-range correlations. Averages and standard deviations were computed statistically to estimate model-specific uncertainties in the NTMEs.

## Key numbers

- For 130Te, the most stringent extracted on-axis limits are: effective light Majorana neutrino mass <m_nu> = 0.33 eV, effective weak coupling <lambda> = 4.57 x 10^-7, and effective weak coupling <eta> = 4.72 x 10^-9.
- For 100Mo, the extracted limits are: <m_nu> = 0.38 eV, <lambda> = 4.39 x 10^-7, and <eta> = 5.23 x 10^-9.
- The effective NTME M^(0nu) for 130Te is 4.05, M_eff^(0lambda) is 5.71, and M_eff^(0eta) is 567.26.
- The effective NTME M^(0nu) for 100Mo is 6.25, M_eff^(0lambda) is 10.71, and M_eff^(0eta) is 920.40.
- The maximum uncertainty in average NTMEs M_omegaF,qF, M_omegaGT,qGT, and M_P is about 15%, except for 150Nd where the standard deviation of M_P is about 40%.
- The maximum uncertainty in average M_R is about 30%.
- NTMEs M_eff^(0lambda) are about twice M^(0nu), and M_eff^(0eta) are larger by two orders of magnitude than M^(0nu).
- In the mass range A=90-150, NTMEs M_alpha are suppressed by a factor of about 2-7 due to deformation effects, with D_qT and D_P suppressed by factors of about 25 and 14 respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous PHFB studies of neutrinoless double-beta decay within light Majorana neutrino mass, classical Majorons, and sterile neutrinos (Rath 2013), heavy Majorana neutrino mass (Rath 2012), and new Majoron models (Rath 2016) by now including right-handed current mechanisms. The calculated NTMEs are compared with those from QRPA (Muto 1989) and QRPA with partial isospin restoration (Simkovic 2017), and the results enable extraction of limits on effective neutrino mass and right-handed current couplings from experimental half-life limits, particularly for 130Te and 100Mo. The authors also demonstrate the distinguishability of the lambda-mechanism and the mechanism involving both left and right handed currents for the studied isotopes.
