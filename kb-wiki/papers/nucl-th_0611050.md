# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> Elastic electromagnetic nucleon form factors have long provided vital information about the structure and composition of these most basic elements of nuclear physics. The form factors are a measurable and physical manifestation of the nature of the nucleons' constituents and the dynamics that binds them together. Accurate form factor data obtained in recent years using modern experimental facilities has spurred a significant reevaluation of the nucleon and pictures of its structure; e.g., the role of quark orbital angular momentum, the scale at which perturbative QCD effects should become evident, the strangeness content, and meson-cloud effects. We provide a succinct survey of the experimental studies and theoretical interpretation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim
The paper surveys how modern, high-precision experimental data on nucleon electromagnetic form factors have forced a significant reevaluation of nucleon structure, particularly regarding the role of quark orbital angular momentum and the onset of perturbative QCD, but it notes that the interpretation of these data is complicated by a persistent discrepancy between results from Rosenbluth separation and polarisation transfer measurements, which is currently believed to be explained by two-photon exchange corrections.

## Method
The authors provide a succinct survey of experimental studies and theoretical interpretations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors. They review unpolarised elastic and quasi-elastic scattering (Rosenbluth separation), polarised elastic scattering and ratio measurements (including polarisation transfer and beam-target asymmetry), and future experimental programs. The theoretical discussion covers the formalism of Dirac and Pauli form factors, Sachs form factors, and the flavour decomposition of form factors via parity-violating scattering.

## Key numbers
- The proton magnetic form factor, $\gmp$, has been extracted with high precision for $Q^2 \in [0.1, 30]\,{\rm GeV}^2$, and the proton electric form factor, $\gep$, from $0.01$ to $2\,{\rm GeV}^2$.
- The ratio $\mu_p G_E^p / G_M^p$ from polarisation transfer measurements shows a significant decrease with $Q^2$, fitted as $\mu_p G_E^p / G_M^p = 1 - 0.13 (Q^2 - 0.04)$ (with $Q^2$ in GeV$^2$), in contrast to Rosenbluth extractions which were roughly consistent with unity up to $Q^2 \sim 6\,{\rm GeV}^2$.
- Measurements of $\gmn$ have achieved $\sim 3\%$ uncertainties for $Q^2 \lesssim 0.5\,{\rm GeV}^2$, $\gtrsim 5\%$ uncertainty for $0.5$ to $5\,{\rm GeV}^2$, and $10$–$15\%$ uncertainty for $5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 10\,{\rm GeV}^2$.
- Future measurements are planned to extend $\mu_p G_E^p / G_M^p$ data to $Q^2 = 8.5\,{\rm GeV}^2$ (in 2007) and precision neutron and proton data to $Q^2 \approx 14\,{\rm GeV}^2$ (mid-next decade).

## Context
This review extends the discussion of nucleon form factors from earlier work, such as the Galster parametrisation (Galster, 1971) and the global analysis of cross-section data by Arrington (2004). It highlights the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarisation transfer results, which has spurred theoretical work on two-photon exchange corrections (e.g., Blunden, 2005). The paper enables future combined analyses of parity-conserving and parity-violating elastic scattering to separate up, down, and strange quark contributions to the nucleon’s electromagnetic structure.
