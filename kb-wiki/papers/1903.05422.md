# Extensivity, entropy current, area law and Unruh effect

> We present a general method to determine the entropy current of relativistic matter at local thermodynamic equilibrium in quantum statistical mechanics. Provided that the local equilibrium operator is bounded from below and its lowest lying eigenvector is non-degenerate, it is proved that, in general, the logarithm of the partition function is extensive, meaning that it can be expressed as the integral over a 3D space-like hypersurface of a vector current, and that an entropy current exists. We work out a specific calculation for a non-trivial case of global thermodynamic equilibrium, namely a system with constant comoving acceleration, whose limiting temperature is the Unruh temperature. We show that the integral of the entropy current in the right Rindler wedge is the entanglement entropy.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the logarithm of the partition function for a system at local thermodynamic equilibrium is extensive (expressible as an integral of a vector current over a 3D hypersurface) provided the local equilibrium operator is bounded from below and its lowest eigenvector is non-degenerate; this proof is the first general demonstration of a property usually tacitly assumed in relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors define a local equilibrium density operator following Zubarev and van Weert, then introduce a dimensionless parameter λ that rescales the exponent. By integrating the derivative of log Z(λ) from λ₀ to 1 and showing that log Z(λ→∞)=0 when the lowest eigenvalue of the local equilibrium operator is subtracted, they derive an explicit integral expression for the thermodynamic potential current φ^μ. They then apply this formalism to a free real scalar field in the right Rindler wedge at global thermodynamic equilibrium with constant comoving acceleration, computing the stress-energy tensor expectation values using Rindler-mode expansions and normal ordering with respect to the Rindler vacuum.

## Key numbers

- The energy density of a free real scalar field in the right Rindler wedge is ρ = π²/(30β⁴) − α²/(12β⁴), where β is the four-temperature vector and α is the four-acceleration divided by comoving temperature.
- The entropy current in the right Rindler wedge is s^μ = (2π²/(45β⁴) − α²/(6β⁴)) β^μ.
- For a massless field, the normally ordered field square is ⟨:ψ²:⟩_R = T₀²/(12 a² (z'²−t²)) = 1/(12β²), with T₀ the constant parameter in the pure acceleration Killing vector.
- The normally ordered quantity ⟨:u·∂ψ u·∂ψ:⟩_R = π²/(30β⁴).

## Context

This work extends the Zubarev-van Weert approach to local thermodynamic equilibrium (Zubarev, 1970s; van Weert, 1970s) by providing the first general proof of extensivity of the partition function, a property previously assumed in formulations of relativistic hydrodynamics such as Israel's (Israel, 1970s). The specific calculation for accelerated equilibrium reproduces and generalizes perturbative results of Becattini and Grossi (2015) and Buzzegoli (2017), and shows that the integrated entropy current in the right Rindler wedge equals the entanglement entropy, connecting the formalism to the Unruh effect (Unruh, 1976) and Fulling's Rindler quantization (Fulling, 1973). The method enables rigorous derivation of entropy currents in relativistic hydrodynamics and can be extended to systems with rotation or curved spacetime.
