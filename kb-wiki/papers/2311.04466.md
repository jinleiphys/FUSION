# Dynamics of causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in an expanding system

> We develop a framework of causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in one-dimensional expanding system performing linearisation of the hydrodynamic equations around the boost invariant solution. Through the description of space-time evolution of thermodynamic variables and flow velocity, we find a novel phenomenon that the structure of thermodynamic variables is almost frozen. We also show that two-particle correlation functions of final hadrons after freezeout are closely related with the mass of hadrons and properties of the medium such as viscosity, relaxation time and equation of state.

# Dynamics of causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in an expanding system

## Key claim

The structure of thermodynamic variables (energy density fluctuations) becomes almost frozen during the one-dimensional expansion of the system, and two-particle correlation functions of final hadrons after freezeout are sensitive to hadron mass, viscosity, relaxation time, and equation of state, but the positions of dips and bumps in the correlations do not depend on transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors linearise the energy-momentum conservation equations and causal Israel-Stewart constitutive equations (including noise terms via fluctuation-dissipation relations) around the boost-invariant (Bjorken) solution in (1+1)-dimensional Milne coordinates. They solve the resulting stochastic differential equations numerically on an event-by-event basis, then compute two-particle correlation functions of final hadrons via the Cooper-Frye formula assuming Boltzmann distribution with viscous correction.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time: τ_ini = 1 fm; initial background energy density: e₀ = 10 GeV/fm³
- Specific shear viscosity: η/s = 1/4π (default setting)
- Relaxation time for shear pressure: τ_π = (2 − ln 2)/(2πT)
- Final time of evolution: τ = 11 fm
- Correlation functions computed from 10,000 events
- Freezeout performed at τ = 10.0 fm (isochronous) with lattice equation of state
- Degrees of freedom for conformal equation of state: d = 47.5 (massless N_f = 3 QCD)

## Context

This work extends the causal framework of hydrodynamic fluctuations developed by Chattopadhyay (2021) and the earlier first-order approach by Kapusta (2012), both of which linearised around the Bjorken (1983) boost-invariant solution. Unlike prior work that focused on pion yield correlations, the authors solve the stochastic equations numerically on an event-by-event basis and analyse identified hadron spectra, showing that heavier hadrons (protons, kaons) are better probes of two-particle correlations and that the magnitude of correlations is sensitive to transport coefficients while the positions of dips and bumps are not. This enables multidimensional analysis of QGP properties in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
