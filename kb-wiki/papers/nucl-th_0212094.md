# Ion induced quark-gluon implosion

> We investigate nuclear fragmentation in the central proton-nucleus and nucleus - nucleus collisions at the energies of LHC. We argue that within the semi-classical approximation because of fast increase with energy of cross sections of soft and hard interactions each nucleon is stripped in the average process off ``soft'' partons and fragments into a collection of leading quarks and gluons with large $p_t$. Valence quarks and gluons are streaming in the opposite directions when viewed in the c.m. of the produced system. The resulting pattern of the fragmentation of the colliding nuclei leads to an implosion of the quark and gluon constituents of the nuclei. The matter density produced at the initial stage in the nucleus fragmentation region is estimated to be $\geq$ 50 GeV/fm$^3$ at the LHC energies and probably $\geq$ 10 GeV/fm$^3$ at RHIC.

# Ion induced quark-gluon implosion

## Key claim

Within the semiclassical approximation, the black body limit (BBL) reached in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies will strip nucleons of their soft partons, producing a compressed, high-density state of leading quarks and gluons with large transverse momenta in the nucleus fragmentation region; the authors estimate energy densities of at least 50 GeV/fm³ at LHC and probably at least 10 GeV/fm³ at RHIC, but note that their reasoning is insufficient to demonstrate whether thermalization will occur.

## Method

The authors apply the semiclassical approximation and the black body limit formalism to central proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, using the condition that for most quark-gluon components of the nucleon wave function the interaction cross section satisfies σ·2ρR_A ≫ 1, where ρ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear density. They calculate emission angles, Lorentz factors, and volume compression factors for the produced parton system in the rest frame of the fragmenting nucleus, then estimate energy densities from the number of partons, their transverse momenta, and their light-cone momentum fractions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear density: ρ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Energy density in the nucleus fragmentation region at LHC: ≥ 50 GeV/fm³
- Energy density at RHIC: probably ≥ 10 GeV/fm³
- Enhancement factor for hard interaction cross section in central nucleus-nucleus collisions relative to nucleon-nucleon: ≈ 6 for A ≈ 200
- Black body limit for p_t at LHC: up to 2–3 GeV/c for x ≥ 0.01; p_t^{b.b.l.} may reach 4 GeV/c at minimal x_A
- Number of partons at LHC for x ≥ 0.01 and relevant virtualities: ∼ 8–10
- Ratio of energy density to nuclear matter energy density (R_E): > 250 at LHC
- Parton density in the compressed state: ≥ 25 partons per fm³
- Small principal axis of the ellipsoidal compressed state: ∼ 1 fm
- Average invariant energy for parton reinteractions: s ≈ 2p_t² ∼ 8 GeV²
- Leading-order estimate for gluon-gluon cross section: ≥ 1 mb

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of nuclear fragmentation in the framework of soft dynamics (referred to as Larry and references therein) by applying the black body limit regime to predict much higher densities. The authors build on the QCD factorization theorem for q\bar{q} dipole interactions with nuclei and on prior numerical studies (FGSrev) showing that the perturbative cross section reaches the black body limit for x ∼ 10⁻⁴. The paper enables experimental searches for this superdense quark-gluon state at LHC and RHIC, particularly using forward detectors, and suggests that the production of leading hadrons in AA collisions may be stronger than in central pA collisions, a paradoxical result that could be tested.
