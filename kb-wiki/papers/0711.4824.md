# Bulk Viscosity of a Gas of Massless Pions

> In the hadronic phase, the dominant configuration of QCD with two flavors of massless quarks is a gas of massless pions. We calculate the bulk viscosity (zeta) using the Boltzmann equation with the kinetic theory generalized to incorporate the trace anomaly. We find that the dimensionless ratio zeta/s, s being the entropy density, is monotonic increasing below T=120 MeV, where chiral perturbation theory is applicable. This, combined with previous results, shows that zeta/s reaches its maximum near the phase transition temperature Tc, while eta/s, eta being the shear viscosity, reaches its minimum near Tc in QCD with massless quarks.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (zeta/s) for a gas of massless pions increases monotonically below T = 120 MeV, where chiral perturbation theory is applicable, and combined with previous results for gluon plasma and quark-gluon plasma, zeta/s reaches its maximum near the phase transition temperature Tc, while eta/s reaches its minimum near Tc.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a gas of massless pions using a variational method, with the kinetic theory generalized to incorporate the trace anomaly from QCD. The scattering amplitude was taken from leading-order chiral perturbation theory, and the trace anomaly was included by modifying the energy-momentum tensor to account for loop corrections from pion interactions. The calculation used a polynomial ansatz for the deviation from equilibrium distribution and assumed that 2-to-2 scattering is the dominant process.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless constant h in the bulk viscosity expression is approximately 65.
- The bulk viscosity is given by zeta approximately equals 0.15 times (l/2.5) times (ln(Lambda_p/T) - 1/4) times (ln(Lambda_p/T) - 3/8) times T^7 / f_pi^4, where Lambda_p is approximately 275 MeV and f_pi = 88.3 MeV.
- The leading-order entropy density for a free pion gas is s = (2 pi^2 g_pi / 45) T^3, with g_pi = 3.
- The ratio zeta/eta is approximately 180 times (l/2.5) times (1/3 - P/epsilon) times (1/3 - v_s^2).
- The error on the zeta/s curve is estimated to be 30% to 40%.
- For massless pion gas, zeta/s is proportional to 1/(N_c^2 N_f^2) in the large N_c limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients in QCD, following earlier calculations of shear viscosity for pion gas by Chen (2006) and bulk viscosity calculations for gluon plasma by Meyer (2007) and for quark-gluon plasma by Kharzeev (2007). The results show that zeta/s rises sharply near Tc from below, complementing the known behavior that eta/s reaches its minimum near Tc. A recent massive pion gas calculation by Fernandez-Fraile (2008) found two peaks in zeta, one near 10 MeV from pion mass effects and one near Tc from the anomaly, with the higher-temperature peak behavior similar to this massless pion result.
