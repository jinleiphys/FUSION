# Gravitational Tensor-Monopole Moment of Hydrogen Atom To Order ${\cal O}(α)$

> We calculate the gravitational tensor-monopole moment of the momentum-current density $T^{ij}$ in the ground state of the hydrogen atom to order ${\cal O}(α)$ in quantum electrodynamics (QED). The result is \begin{align} τ_H/τ_0 - 1 = \frac{4α}{3π}\left(\lnα^2-0.028\right) \nonumber \end{align} where $τ_0= \hbar^2/4m_e$ is the leading-order moment. The physics of the next-to-leading-order correction is similar to that of the famous Lamb shift for energy levels.

## Key claim

The gravitational tensor-monopole moment of the hydrogen atom ground state receives a next-to-leading-order QED correction of order \({\cal O}(\alpha)\), with the result \(\tau_H/\tau_0 - 1 = \frac{4\alpha}{3\pi}(\ln\alpha^2-0.028)\), where the leading-order moment \(\tau_0 = \hbar^2/4m_e\) is positive, opposite in sign to what the "mechanical stability" condition from fluid-like interpretations would require.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic QED (NRQED) in the Coulomb gauge, treating the proton as an infinitely heavy static source and the electron via the Schrödinger equation with the Coulomb potential. They compute the gravitational tensor-monopole moment \(\tau_H\) of the momentum-current density \(T^{ij}\) by matching the NRQED energy-momentum tensor to full QED at one-loop order, including radiative corrections from soft and ultra-soft photons, and regulating ultraviolet divergences with dimensional regularization. The bound-state calculation uses the complete set of Schrödinger wave functions (discrete and continuum) and exploits scale separation \(m_e \gg \alpha m_e \gg \alpha^2 m_e\) to isolate the \({\cal O}(\alpha)\) contribution.

## Key numbers

* \(\tau_H/\tau_0 - 1 = \frac{4\alpha}{3\pi}(\ln\alpha^2-0.028)\) for the hydrogen atom ground state, where \(\tau_0 = \hbar^2/4m_e\) is the leading-order tensor-monopole moment and \(\alpha\) is the fine-structure constant.
* The leading-order moment \(\tau_0 = \hbar^2/4m_e\) is positive, with \(g_H = 1\) at leading order, opposite in sign to a point-like boson.
* The binding energy scale is \(\alpha^2 m_e\) and the inverse Bohr radius scale is \(\alpha m_e\), with \(\alpha \approx 1/137\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Ji, 2021) to interpret gravitational form factors as multipole moments, specifically the tensor-monopole moment related to the \(C(q^2=0)\) form factor (Ji, 1996). It contradicts the "mechanical stability" condition of Polyakov (2002, 2018) and Burkert (2018), which assumes a negative \(D\)-term for stable systems, by showing that the hydrogen atom's tensor-monopole moment is positive at leading order. The calculation parallels the simplified Lamb shift computation using NRQED (Labelle, 1996; Pineda, 1997) and enables future studies of gravitational multipole moments in more complex quantum systems.
