# Justification of Landau Hydrodynamic-Tube Model in Central Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The statistical event-by-event analysis of inelastic interactions of 16O and 32S nuclei in emulsion at 60 A GeV/c and 200 A GeV/c reveals the existence of groups of high multiplicity events belonging to very central nuclear interactions with Gaussian pseudorapidity distributions for produced particles as suggested by the original hydrodynamic-tube model. Characteristics of these events are presented. The experimental observations are interpreted as a result of quark-gluon plasma formation in the course of central nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a small subset of very high-multiplicity events in central collisions of light ions (oxygen and sulfur) at CERN SPS energies exhibit Gaussian pseudorapidity distributions consistent with the original Landau hydrodynamic-tube model, but this behavior is not observed in gold-induced collisions at lower BNL AGS energy, and the probability of such Gaussian distributions arising by chance is negligibly small.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical event-by-event analysis of inelastic interactions of 16O and 32S nuclei in nuclear emulsion at 60 A GeV/c and 200 A GeV/c, and of 197Au nuclei at 11.6 A GeV/c. For each event, they computed the coefficients of skewness (g1) and excess (g2) of the pseudorapidity distribution of relativistic shower particles, then constructed normalized quantities d1 and d2 whose expected values are zero with unit variance if the pseudorapidity distribution is Gaussian. They tested whether the sums of these normalized quantities over event ensembles fell within the 95% confidence interval (absolute value less than 2) for different multiplicity thresholds.

## Key numbers

- For 16O at 60 A GeV/c: 4 events identified with Gaussian pseudorapidity distributions, with average shower multiplicity ns = 185.8.
- For 16O at 200 A GeV/c: 9 events identified, with average ns = 247.0.
- For 32S at 200 A GeV/c: 6 events identified, with average ns = 440.0.
- For 197Au at 11.6 A GeV/c: zero events identified with Gaussian pseudorapidity distributions.
- Estimated energy density for the selected events ranges from 1 GeV/fm3 to 16 GeV/fm3, depending on the assumed radius of the interaction volume (4 fm to 1 fm).
- The average pseudorapidity values for the 9 selected 16O events at 200 A GeV/c range from 2.74 to 3.10, with standard deviations from 1.17 to 1.48.
- The average pseudorapidity values for the 6 selected 32S events at 200 A GeV/c range from 2.88 to 3.31, with standard deviations from 1.34 to 1.48.

## Context

This work extends the original Landau hydrodynamic-tube model (Landau, 1953; Belenkij and Landau, 1956) to central heavy-ion collisions, where the model predicts Gaussian pseudorapidity distributions for produced particles. The analysis uses a statistical method based on parametrically invariant quantities from mathematical statistics (Cramer, 1946) to test the Gaussian hypothesis on an event-by-event basis, which differs from the inclusive and semi-inclusive analyses previously applied to Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions (reviewed by example in a 2006 reference). The findings suggest that the formation of quark-gluon plasma in central collisions is a rare, random phenomenon that can be identified through the Gaussian shape of pseudorapidity distributions, and the authors recommend confirming this possibility in other experiments.
