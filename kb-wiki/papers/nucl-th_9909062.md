# Droplet formation in cold asymmetric nuclear matter in the quark-meson-coupling model

> The quark-meson-coupling model is used to study droplet formation from the liquid-gas phase transition in cold asymmetric nuclear matter. The critical density and proton fraction for the phase transition are determined in the mean field approximation. Droplet properties are calculated in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. The electromagnetic field is explicitly included and its effects on droplet properties are studied. The results are compared with the ones obtained with the NL1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model.

## Key claim

The quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model predicts a higher critical pressure and proton fraction for the liquid-gas phase transition in cold asymmetric nuclear matter than the NL1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model, and the density dependence of the effective sigma-nucleon coupling in the QMC model leads to significantly different droplet properties such as higher surface energy density and smaller proton radius.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson-coupling model in the mean field approximation to determine the binodal section and critical point for the liquid-gas phase transition in cold asymmetric nuclear matter. Droplet properties were calculated using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for finite spherical systems, solving the coupled non-linear differential equations for the sigma, omega, rho, and Coulomb fields with the electromagnetic field explicitly included. The results were compared with those obtained from the NL1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model.

## Key numbers

- Critical point for QMC model: pressure approximately 0.43 MeV/fm^3 (from text: "for P less than or approximately 0.43 MeV fm^{-3}, the gas phase always has Y_p=0")
- Free bag radius fixed at R_B = 0.8 fm; bag constant B^{1/4} = 170.0 MeV; z = 3.295 (for m_q = 5 MeV)
- QMC model parameters: g_sigma^2/4pi = 3.12, g_omega^2/4pi = 5.31, g_rho^2/4pi = 6.93, m_sigma = 418 MeV; m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV
- Central density in QMC model is approximately 0.01-0.02 fm^{-3} higher than in NL1 for droplets with A=40 and N_p/A=0.5
- Surface thickness in QMC model is approximately 0.4-0.5 fm lower than in NL1 across all solutions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of droplet formation from the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter using the non-linear Walecka model (Menezes and Providencia, 1998, 1999) by applying the quark-meson-coupling model originally proposed by Guichon (1988). The results enable comparison between models with different density-dependent couplings and incompressibilities, and the authors note that the QMC model predicts higher surface energy density, smaller proton radius, and smaller neutron skin thickness than the NL1 parametrization. The work also sets the stage for future studies including finite temperature effects and exchange terms.
