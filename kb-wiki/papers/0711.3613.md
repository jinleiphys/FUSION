# Neutron matter at finite temperature

> We calculate the neutron matter equation of state at finite temperature based on low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions. The free energy is obtained from a loop expansion around the Hartree-Fock energy, including contributions from normal and anomalous diagrams. We focus on densities below saturation density with temperatures T <= 10 MeV and compare our results to the model-independent virial equation of state and to variational calculations. Good agreement with the virial equation of state is found at low density. We provide simple estimates for the theoretical error, important for extrapolations to astrophysical conditions.

# Neutron matter at finite temperature

## Key claim

The neutron matter equation of state at subsaturation densities and temperatures up to 10 MeV can be computed perturbatively using low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions, with the free energy obtained from a loop expansion around the Hartree-Fock energy; however, the theoretical uncertainties from three-nucleon interactions (particularly the low-energy constant c3) overwhelm the error due to the approximate many-body treatment at these densities.

## Method

The authors compute the grand-canonical potential using a loop expansion around the Hartree-Fock free energy, including first-order nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) contributions, plus second-order anomalous and normal diagrams with NN interactions. Low-momentum interactions V_low_k are derived from the Argonne v18 potential with cutoff Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1, and the free energy is obtained via Legendre transformation with the chemical potential expanded order by order following Kohn-Luttinger-Ward theory. The pressure, entropy, and energy are then calculated using standard thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures considered: T = 3 MeV, 6 MeV, and 10 MeV
- Density range: below saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Cutoff range for error estimates: Lambda = 1.9 fm^-1 to 2.5 fm^-1
- Low-energy constants used: c1 = -0.76 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.78 GeV^-1 (from Nijmegen partial wave analysis)
- For error estimates including c_i uncertainties: c1 = -0.9^{+0.2}_{-0.5} GeV^-1, c3 = -4.7^{+1.2}_{-1.0} GeV^-1 (from Ulf)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138.04 MeV, axial coupling g_A = 1.29
- Good agreement with virial equation of state found at low densities where fugacity z = e^{mu/T} = 0.5
- At T = 1.5 MeV, the energy per particle closely follows the T = 0 results except at low densities where E/N approaches 3/2 T

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature perturbative approach of Vlowknucmatt (2007) to finite temperature for neutron matter, generalizing the loop expansion around Hartree-Fock energy to include thermal effects. The results are compared to the model-independent virial equation of state (vEOSneut, 2005) and to variational calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981). The paper enables systematic error quantification for astrophysical applications such as neutron star structure and core-collapse supernovae, and establishes a path to match the virial equation of state to microscopic calculations at higher densities.
