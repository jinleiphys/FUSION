# Neutron scattering off spherical nuclei with global nonlocal dispersive optical model

> We present a global nonlocal and dispersive optical model potential for neutron scattering off spherical nuclei with incident energies up to 250 MeV. This optical model is an extension of the non-dispersive Perey-Buck potential. The imaginary components are chosen energy-dependent and the dispersive constraints are taken into account. The surface imaginary part is nonlocal, whereas the volume imaginary part above 10 MeV is local, allowing to reproduce total cross sections and scattering data for high energies. We obtain a good description of scattering observables for target-nuclei ranging from $A=16$, up to $209$. The inclusion of nonlocal spin-orbit term enables a better description of the analyzing power data relative to the local dispersive model.

## Key claim

The authors present the first global nonlocal dispersive optical model potential for neutron scattering off spherical nuclei, extending the non-dispersive Perey-Buck potential by including energy-dependent imaginary components and dispersive constraints, with the surface imaginary part kept nonlocal while the volume imaginary part above 10 MeV is treated as local to reproduce high-energy data.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological optical model potential with a Gaussian nonlocality form factor of Perey-Buck type for both central and spin-orbit terms, using energy-dependent imaginary strengths with Brown-Rho shapes and dispersion relations to relate real and imaginary components. They calibrated the parameters against experimental elastic scattering observables and bound state energies for twenty-two spherical target nuclei with masses from A=16 to A=209, using the EXFOR database and solving the integro-differential Schrodinger equation via Chebyshev polynomial expansion. For neutron energies below about 15 MeV, compound nucleus emission was treated with Hauser-Feshbach formalism using TALYS code.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: up to 250 MeV
- Target mass range: A=16 to A=209 (twenty-two nuclei: C, O, Mg, Al, Si, S, Ca, Ti, Cr, Fe, Ni, Cu, Y, Zr, Nb, Mo, Sn, Ce, Au, Hg, Pb, Bi)
- Nonlocality range beta: 0.915 fm
- Real volume depth: V_V^NL = -69.71 - 1.140 x 10^-2 A (MeV)
- Real surface depth: V_S^NL = -8.600 - 8.000 x 10^-3 A (MeV)
- Real spin-orbit depth: V_so^NL = -9.787 - 1.140 x 10^-2 A (MeV)
- Surface imaginary depth parameter A_S^+: -19.62 - 1.500 x 10^-2 A (MeV) for E > E_F
- Surface imaginary depth parameter A_S^-: -16.00 (MeV) for E < E_F
- Volume imaginary depth parameter B_V: 135.0 MeV
- Diffuseness a: 6.1600 x 10^-1 - 1.8200 x 10^-4 A (fm)
- Reduced radius r_0 for A > 70: 1.1446 + 2.4200 x 10^-4 A (fm)
- Relative difference with data for total cross section: approximately 3% for medium-mass nuclei
- For light nuclei, NLD model yields roughly 1% better agreement than LD approach

## Context

This work extends the local dispersive optical model of Morillon and Romain (2007) by incorporating explicit nonlocality, and builds upon the Perey-Buck (1962) potential which was limited to neutron energies below 28 MeV. The nonlocal spin-orbit term, motivated by microscopic studies of Arellano (2022), enables better description of analyzing power data compared to the local dispersive model. The global nonlocal dispersive potential enables simultaneous description of scattering states and bound states for neutron-nucleus systems across the nuclear chart, with potential applications in nuclear data evaluation and reaction modeling.
