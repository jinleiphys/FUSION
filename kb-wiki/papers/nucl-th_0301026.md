# Effects of resonant single-particle states on pairing correlations

> Effects of resonant single-particle (s.p.) states on the pairing correlations are investigated by an exact treatment of the pairing Hamiltonian on the Gamow shell model basis. We introduce the s.p. states with complex energies into the Richardson equations. The solution shows the property that the resonant s.p. states with large widths are less occupied. The importance of many-body correlations between bound and resonant prticle pairs is shown.

## Key claim

Resonant single-particle states with large widths are less occupied in pairing-correlated systems, and such states with too large widths should be excluded from the pairing correlation space to maintain physical meaning (positive occupation probabilities).

## Method

The authors extended the Richardson equations, which exactly solve the pairing Hamiltonian, to include single-particle states with complex energies representing resonant states from a Woods-Saxon potential. They solved these equations for model systems with a dozen equally spaced levels (eight bound, four resonant) and for the realistic case of 16 neutrons in ²⁴O, calculating total energies and occupation probabilities as functions of pairing strength G and resonant width γ.

## Key numbers

- For the N=8 model system with γ/2=0.5, the ground state shows increasing imaginary energy (width) as pairing strength G increases; at a critical G the ground-state width exceeds that of the second pair-correlated state
- For N=8 with G=0.3, Re(v²₉) of the lowest resonant level becomes negative at γ/2 ≈ 1.15, marking the onset of unphysical behavior
- For ²⁴O with G=0.61 MeV: pairing gap Δ = 2.45 MeV when neglecting widths, Δ = 2.09 MeV when including widths, Δ = 1.27 MeV when excluding 2p₃/₂ and 2p₁/₂ orbits, and Δ = 0.73 MeV when also excluding 1f₇/₂
- Ground-state energy of ²⁴O: -189.95 MeV (neglecting widths), -189.29 MeV (including widths), -187.77 MeV (excluding 2p₃/₂ and 2p₁/₂), -186.91 MeV (excluding all resonant orbits)
- Imaginary part of ²⁴O ground-state energy changes from -1.12 MeV to -0.09 MeV when excluding 2p₃/₂ and 2p₁/₂

## Context

This work extends the Richardson exact-pairing method to complex-energy resonant states, building on earlier studies by Kruppa et al. (2000) and Sandulescu et al. (2000) who used HF+BCS approximations with resonant continua, and by Betan et al. (2002) and Michel et al. (2002) who studied one-pair systems outside a core. The results confirm the suggestion of Kruppa et al. (2000) that only narrow-width resonant states should be included, and show that many-body correlations between bound and resonant pairs are important for binding energies. The approach cannot treat continuum states properly, requiring future work with different pairing interactions.
