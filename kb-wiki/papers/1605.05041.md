# Interplay between tensor force and deformation in even-even nuclei

> In this work we study the effect of the nuclear tensor force on properties related with deformation. We focus on isotopes in the Mg, Si, S, Ar, Sr and Zr chains within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using the D1ST2a Gogny interaction. Contributions to the tensor energy in terms of saturated and unsaturated subshells are analyzed. Like-particle and proton-neutron parts of the tensor term are independently examinated. We found that the tensor term may considerably modify the potential energy landscapes and change the ground state shape. We analyze too how the pairing characteristics of the ground state change when the tensor force is included.

## Key claim

The tensor term in the D1ST2a Gogny interaction can considerably modify potential energy landscapes and change the ground state shape of even-even nuclei, but the effect is highly dependent on whether the proton and neutron subshells are spin-saturated or spin-unsaturated; the tensor force tends to stretch local minima (making oblate minima more oblate and prolate minima more prolate) and can invert the ground state location when minima are close in energy for the D1S interaction.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1ST2a Gogny interaction, which includes a finite-range tensor term added to the D1S Gogny interaction, with the D1S parameters kept unchanged. They computed potential energy curves (PEC) as a function of the total quadrupole moment for isotopes in the Mg, Si, S, Ar, Sr, and Zr chains, assuming axial and time-reversal symmetry, using a harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells and a fixed oscillator length of 2.1 fm. The tensor energy was decomposed into like-particle (proton-proton and neutron-neutron) and proton-neutron contributions by independently modifying the tensor parameters V_T1 and V_T2.

## Key numbers

* The tensor parameters in D1ST2a are V_T1 = -135 MeV and V_T2 = +115 MeV, with a range mu_T = 1.2 fm.
* For the like-particle decomposition, V_T1^lp + V_T2^lp = -20 MeV with V_T2^lp = 0 MeV; for the proton-neutron decomposition, V_T1^pn + V_T2^pn = 0 MeV with V_T2^pn = +115 MeV.
* At sphericity, the total tensor energy E_TS(tot) ranges from +1.637 MeV for 36Ar (SU/SU) to -1.205 MeV for 90Zr (SS/SU), with 40Ca (SS/SS) giving +0.027 MeV and 80Zr (SS/SS) giving +0.052 MeV.
* For the Zr chain (A=100 to 116), the D1ST2a interaction gives a spherical ground state (Q_0 = 0.00 eb) for all isotopes, while D1S gives oblate or prolate ground states for A=100 to 110, with Q_0 values ranging from -1.82 eb to +4.46 eb.
* The intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0 for the first isomeric state with D1ST2a ranges from -1.83 eb (A=100) to -2.01 eb (A=112) in the Zr chain.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor force effects in mean-field theories, following the development of the D1ST2a Gogny interaction by Anguiano et al. (2012), which was adjusted to reproduce neutron single-particle energies in 48Ca. It builds on prior studies of tensor effects on deformation by Zalewski et al. (2008, 2009) using Skyrme functionals and by Bender et al. (2009) who analyzed Skyrme parametrizations from Lesinski et al. (2007) with respect to deformation properties of magic and semi-magic nuclei. The results enable identification of specific regions and observables most sensitive to the tensor force, which can guide a future consistent refit of the Gogny interaction including tensor terms.
