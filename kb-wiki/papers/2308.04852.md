# A novel approach to light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions

> The issue of cluster production in heavy-ion collisions is addressed in a new manner, by implementing cluster correlation into the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model. We demonstrate for the first time, the good potentialities of this popular transport approach in the description of light cluster production including the deuteron, triton, $^{3}$He and $α$ particle in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. Both the INDRA and FOPI experimental data of the total multiplicities of light clusters and the charge distributions of heavier fragments are reasonably reproduced by the unified approach. The effects of both the cluster binding energies and the pauli repulsion are also shown to play crucial roles in the production of clusters.

## Key claim
The authors implement cluster correlation into the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model for the first time, achieving a unified description of light cluster production (deuteron, triton, \(^{3}\)He, and \(\alpha\)) alongside heavier fragments in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, but note that the inner-working of their model in describing cluster correlation differs from that of AMD-cluster, particularly for \(\alpha\) formation which is partly mimicked through scatterings rather than aggregation.

## Method
The authors follow the method of Ono in AMD-cluster, including quantum transitions to clustered states as possible final states of nucleon-nucleon scattering, with a projection operator to consider all scattering channels between nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-cluster, and cluster-cluster. They employ the LQMD mean-field Hamiltonian with modifications: the surface interaction term between nucleons within a cluster is turned off, a zero-point potential energy is added for each cluster, and a correction term is added to reproduce the deuteron binding energy. A continuous phase-space constraint method using frictional cooling is devised to act like a Pauli potential, with density-dependent bounds on phase-space occupation to facilitate cluster formation in dilute regions.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon of deuteron: 1.15 MeV, close to the experimental value.
- Density cut for cluster correlation: \(\rho_{cut} = 0.170\) fm\(^{-3}\) (higher than the 0.125 fm\(^{-3}\) used in AMD-cluster).
- Phase-space constraint bounds: upper bound \(f_{boun,u} = 0.95\) and lower bound \(f_{boun,l} = 0.65\), with corresponding marginalities \(M_u = 17\) and \(M_l = 8\).
- Tolerance parameters: \(\epsilon_{boun,l} = 0.10\) and \(\epsilon_{boun,u} = 0.05\).
- MST parameters for cluster dissolution: \(k_{ij} \le 1.75\) between nucleons within a cluster.
- MST parameters for fragment formation: \(r_0 = 3.5\) fm and \(p_0 = 200\) MeV/c.
- Time step for dynamical evolution and frictional cooling: \(dt = 0.1\) fm/c.
- For \(^{129}\)Xe+\(^{118}\)Sn@50A MeV, cluster multiplicities reach an approximate plateau above 120 fm/c.
- For \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au@250A MeV, the model results are very similar to those of AMD-cluster (Ono 2016), with yields of deuterons, tritons, and \(^{3}\)He underestimated.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic approach to cluster production, previously implemented in the Anti-symmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) model by Ono (2013, 2016) and in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) models by Danielewicz (1991, 1992) and others, to the QMD transport model for the first time. It also builds on the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) model by Coci (2023), which incorporated deuteron production but not heavier clusters. The approach enables future studies of hypercluster formation in spectator fragmentation reactions, where QMD has special advantages over BUU-type models.
