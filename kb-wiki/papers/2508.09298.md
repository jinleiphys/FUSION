# Charge dependence of mesons with flavored contact-interaction couplings

> Effective interaction models of quantum chromodynamics, based on quark degrees of freedom, have been successfully employed to compute the properties of a large array of ground and excited meson and baryon states, along with their electromagnetic form factors, distribution functions and thermal behavior. Amongst them, the contact-interaction model, while non-renormalizable, implements confinement, satisfies Lorentz covariance and correctly describes chiral symmetry and its dynamical breaking pattern. Original studies focused on the light hadron sector in the isospin limit and were thereafter extended to heavy mesons and baryons. The strong effective couplings, as well as infrared and ultraviolet regulators, are flavor-dependent model parameters adjusted to reproduce hadronic observables. In contrast, in this study we combine SU(4) flavor-symmetry breaking couplings, obtained from one-loop vacuum polarization amplitudes in the presence of background constituent quark currents, with the contact-interaction model. This allows us to reduce the number of mass-dimensioned parameters and to consistently relate all flavored couplings to a single mass scale, while the masses and weak decay constants of the pions, kaons, $D$ and $D_s$ mesons are in good agreement with average reference values. Allowing for realistic isospin breaking, $m_d/m_u = 1.7$, in conjunction with the effect of the flavored couplings, leads to a mass splitting, $m_{π^+}- m_{π^0} \approx 0.3$ MeV, that agrees with lattice QCD values. For the kaons, the mass difference is $m_{K^0}- m_{K^\pm} \approx 2.3$ MeV, whereas $m_{D^\pm} - m_{D^0} \approx 0.5$ MeV and the $η_c$ is 6\% lighter than the experimental mass.

## Key claim

By combining SU(4) flavor-symmetry breaking couplings derived from one-loop vacuum polarization amplitudes with the contact-interaction model, the authors reduce the number of free mass-dimensioned parameters to a single mass scale while still obtaining meson masses and decay constants in good agreement with reference values; however, the η_c mass is 6% lighter than the experimental value and its decay constant is 23.7% below the lattice QCD prediction.

## Method

The authors incorporate flavor-dependent couplings obtained from one-loop vacuum polarization integrals in the presence of background constituent quark currents into the contact-interaction model's gap equation (Dyson-Schwinger equation) and Bethe-Salpeter equation kernel. They employ a common proper-time regularization scheme with a single set of infrared and ultraviolet regulators for all flavors, and solve the resulting self-consistent equations for quark masses and meson bound-state amplitudes. The model parameters (quark masses, coupling strength g₀, and regulators) are adjusted in a least-square fit to reproduce worldwide averaged masses and weak decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting m_{π⁺} - m_{π⁰} ≈ 0.3 MeV, obtained with isospin breaking ratio m_d/m_u = 1.7, in agreement with lattice QCD values
- Kaon mass difference m_{K⁰} - m_{K±} ≈ 2.3 MeV; ratio (m_{K⁰}² - m_{K±}²)/(m_d - m_u) = 2.54 GeV, in excellent agreement with Giusti (2017)
- D meson mass difference m_{D±} - m_{D⁰} ≈ 0.5 MeV; m_{D±}² - m_{D⁰}² ≈ 0.002 GeV²
- η_c mass = 2798.0 MeV, which is 6.2% below the experimental reference of 2984.1 MeV; η_c decay constant = 293.6 MeV, 23.7% below the lattice QCD prediction of 385.0 MeV from Davies (2010)
- Light-quark constituent masses: M_u = 244.9 MeV, M_d = 254.0 MeV, M_s = 480.8 MeV, M_c = 1555.5 MeV, with current quark masses m_u = 1.3 MeV, m_d = 2.2 MeV, m_s = 52.0 MeV, m_c = 970.0 MeV
- Model parameters: g₀ = 0.195042 GeV⁻², Λ_ir = 0.240 GeV, Λ_uv = 2.325 GeV

## Context

This work extends the contact-interaction model, previously applied in the isospin limit by Serna (2017) and others, by incorporating flavor-dependent couplings derived from vacuum polarization processes originally developed in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Braghin (2021). The approach enables charge-dependent meson masses that earlier CI model versions could not distinguish, and relates all flavored couplings to a single mass scale rather than treating them as independent parameters. The results enable future studies of vector mesons (J/ψ, D*, D_s*) and electromagnetic form factors with charge dependence, though the η_c remains a challenge that may require inclusion of η_c-η-η' mixing, which contributes approximately 11 MeV to the η_c mass according to Bali (2011).
