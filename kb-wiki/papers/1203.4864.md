# Theoretical study of the synthesis of superheavy nuclei with Z= 119 and 120 in heavy-ion reactions with trans-uranium targets

> By using a newly developed di-nuclear system model with a dynamical potential energy surface---the DNS-DyPES model, hot fusion reactions for synthesizing superheavy nuclei (SHN) with the charge number Z = 112-120 are studied. The calculated evaporation residue cross sections are in good agreement with available data. In the reaction 50Ti+249Bk -> (299-x)119 + xn, the maximal evaporation residue (ER) cross section is found to be about 0.11 pb for the 4n-emission channel. For projectile-target combinations producing SHN with Z=120, the ER cross section increases with the mass asymmetry in the incident channel increasing. The maximal ER cross sections for 58Fe+244Pu and 54Cr + 248Cm are relatively small (less than 0.01 pb) and those for 50Ti+249Cf and 50Ti+251Cf are about 0.05 and 0.25 pb, respectively.

## Key claim

The DNS-DyPES model predicts that the maximal evaporation residue cross section for synthesizing element 119 via 50Ti + 249Bk is about 0.11 pb for the 4n-emission channel, and for element 120 the cross section depends strongly on the target choice, ranging from less than 0.01 pb for 58Fe + 244Pu and 54Cr + 248Cm up to about 0.25 pb for 50Ti + 251Cf, with the caveat that these are theoretical predictions that have not yet been experimentally confirmed.

## Method

The authors developed a di-nuclear system model with a dynamical potential energy surface (DNS-DyPES) that incorporates time-dependent dynamical deformations of the projectile and target nuclei during nucleon transfer. The evaporation residue cross section is calculated as a sum over partial waves of the capture cross section (from an empirical coupled channel approach), the compound nucleus formation probability (from solving a master equation for nucleon transfer along the mass asymmetry coordinate), and the survival probability (from a statistical model). The dynamical deformations evolve in an overdamped motion with a relaxation time of 40 × 10^(-22) s, and the maximal dynamical deformation is determined by minimizing the total intrinsic energy of the di-nuclear system.

## Key numbers

- For 50Ti + 249Bk → 299-x119 + xn, the maximal evaporation residue cross section is about 0.11 pb for the 4n-emission channel.
- For 50Ti + 249Bk, the maximal cross section for the 3n channel is about 0.06 pb.
- For 50Ti + 249Cf producing Z=120, the maximal evaporation residue cross section is about 0.05 pb.
- For 50Ti + 251Cf producing 298120, the maximal evaporation residue cross section is about 0.25 pb.
- For 58Fe + 244Pu, the maximal evaporation residue cross section is only about 4 fb (0.004 pb) in the 4n channel.
- For 54Cr + 248Cm, the maximal cross section is about 6 fb (0.006 pb) in the 4n channel.
- The experimental upper limit from the null result for 58Fe + 244Pu is 0.4 pb.
- The relaxation time for dynamical deformations is τ_def = 40 × 10^(-22) s.
- For 50Ti + 249Cf, the maximal dynamical deformation δβ_max is about 0.36.

## Context

This work extends the di-nuclear system model approach previously applied by Feng et al. (2007) and others to the synthesis of superheavy nuclei with Z=119 and 120, specifically addressing hot fusion reactions with trans-uranium targets. The inclusion of a dynamical potential energy surface reduces the fusion probability and evaporation residue cross section by about an order of magnitude compared to static treatments. The predictions for 50Ti + 251Cf producing element 120 (about 0.25 pb) are close to current experimental sensitivity limits, suggesting that this reaction may be feasible for future experiments, while the much smaller cross sections for 58Fe + 244Pu and 54Cr + 248Cm (below 10 fb) explain the null results from Oganessian et al. (2009).
