# Adler function and hadronic vacuum polarization from lattice vector correlators

> We study a representation of the hadronic vacuum polarization based on the time-momentum representation of the vector correlator. This representation suggests a way to compute the hadronic vacuum polarization and the associated Adler function for any value of virtuality, irrespective of the flavor structure of the current. We present results on both of these phenomenologically important functions, derived from local-conserved two-point lattice vector correlation functions, computed on a subset of light two-flavor ensembles made available to us through the CLS effort.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the hadronic vacuum polarization and the Adler function can be computed directly from the time-momentum representation of the lattice vector correlator at any virtuality, without requiring an extrapolation to zero momentum transfer, though the method relies on extending the lattice correlator to all time separations via an exponential fit dominated by the lowest-lying vector meson mass.

## Method

The authors compute the subtracted hadronic vacuum polarization \(\widehat\Pi(Q^2)\) and the Adler function \(D(Q^2)\) using the time-momentum representation of the local-conserved isovector vector correlation function on a \(96 \times 48^3\) lattice with two mass-degenerate flavors of O(\(a\)) improved Wilson fermions. The correlator is extended to all time separations by fitting the lattice data with a two-exponential ansatz, where the lowest mass is fixed from a separate smeared-smeared correlation function, and the amplitude is fitted to data around \(x_0 \simeq T/4\). The integral representation \(\widehat\Pi(Q^2) = \int_0^\infty dx_0 G(x_0) K_{\Pi}(Q_0^2,x_0^2)\) is then evaluated using the extended correlator.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0631(21)\) fm on the F6 ensemble.
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 324\) MeV, with \(m_\pi L = 5.0\).
* Renormalization constant: \(Z_V = 0.750(5)\) used to renormalize the vector correlator.
* Slope of the Adler function at the origin: \(D'(0) = 5.8(5)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) from the lattice data.
* Ground-state vector meson mass on the lattice: \(m_1 = 894(2)\) MeV.
* Comparison phenomenological model value: \(D'(0) = 9.81(30)\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) from Bernecker and Meyer (2011).

## Context

This work extends the time-momentum representation approach previously presented by Francis et al. (2013) and Bernecker and Meyer (2011), and is similar in spirit to work by the ETM Collaboration. The method avoids the extrapolation to zero virtuality required in standard lattice calculations of \(\widehat\Pi(Q^2)\) (e.g., Della Morte et al. 2012), and enables direct computation of the Adler function and the slope of the vacuum polarization. The results enable systematic study of finite-size and discretization effects in lattice QCD calculations of the leading hadronic contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon \(a_\mu^{\rm HLO}\) and the running of the QED coupling \(\Delta\alpha(M_Z^2)\).
