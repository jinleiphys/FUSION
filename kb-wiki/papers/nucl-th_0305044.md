# Total Reaction Cross Section in an Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) Model

> The isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model is used to study the total reaction cross section $σ_R$. The energy-dependent Pauli volumes of neutrons and protons have been discussed and introduced into the IDQMD calculation to replace the widely used energy-independent Pauli volumes. The modified IDQMD calculation can reproduce the experimental $σ_R$ well for both stable and exotic nuclei induced reactions. Comparisons of the calculated $σ_R$ induced by $^{11}Li$ with different initial density distributions have been performed. It is shown that the calculation by using the experimentally deduced density distribution with a long tail can fit the experimental excitation function better than that by using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculated density without long tails. It is also found that $σ_R$ at high energy is sensitive to the long tail of density distribution.

# Total Reaction Cross Section in an Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) Model

## Key claim

The IDQMD model with an energy-dependent Pauli volume (replacing the previously used constant Pauli volume) can reproduce experimental total reaction cross sections for both stable and exotic nuclei, but only when the correct density distribution is used: for halo nuclei like 11Li, the experimentally deduced density distribution with a long tail gives better agreement than the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock density without long tails, especially at high energy.

## Method

The authors modified the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model by introducing an energy-dependent Pauli volume for neutrons and protons, calibrated by fitting the excitation function of the 12C + 12C system across a wide energy range. They then calculated the total reaction cross section sigma_R using the transport function T(b) derived from the average nucleon-nucleon collision number N as a function of impact parameter b, with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections that depend on incident energy and nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C + 12C system, the Pauli radius R increases at intermediate energy and becomes saturated above 400 MeV/u.
- For 11Li + 12C, calculations using the experimental density distribution with a long tail fit the experimental sigma_R better than those using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock density; the SHF results are lower than experimental values at high incident energy by "a few percent."
- For Li isotopes on a 12C target at 790 MeV/u, sigma_R varies smoothly with mass number up to 9Li, with a sudden increase between 9Li and 11Li corresponding to the halo structure in 11Li.
- The average collision number N becomes constant after 50 fm/c at high energy and after 100 fm/c at intermediate energy when using the energy-dependent Pauli volume.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ma et al. (2000), who originally used the BUU transport model to study total reaction cross sections, and later applications of the QMD model in the same spirit. The authors address the limitation of previous IDQMD calculations that used a constant Pauli volume (hbar^3/2), which could not reproduce experimental data for 12C + 12C. The modified model enables simultaneous study of total reaction cross sections, fragmentation cross sections, and momentum distributions of fragments for halo nuclei, providing a more comprehensive criterion for halo structure identification.
