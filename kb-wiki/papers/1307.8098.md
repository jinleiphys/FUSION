# Equation of State of hot and dense QCD: Resummed perturbation theory confronts lattice data

> We perform a detailed analysis of the predictions of resummed perturbation theory for the pressure and the second-, fourth-, and sixth-order diagonal quark number susceptibilities in a hot and dense quark-gluon plasma. First, we present an exact one-loop calculation of the equation of state within hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) and compare it to a previous one-loop HTLpt calculation that employed an expansion in the ratios of thermal masses and the temperature. We find that this expansion converges reasonably fast. We then perform a resummation of the existing four-loop weak coupling expression for the pressure, motivated by dimensional reduction. Finally, we compare the exact one-loop HTLpt and resummed dimensional reduction results with state-of-the-art lattice calculations and a recent mass-expanded three-loop HTLpt calculation.

## Key claim

Resummed perturbation theory, specifically exact one-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) and a resummation of the four-loop weak coupling expression motivated by dimensional reduction (DR), can reproduce lattice data for the pressure and diagonal quark number susceptibilities of hot and dense QCD, but only when the HTLpt mass expansion converges reasonably fast and the DR resummation dramatically reduces renormalization scale dependence.

## Method

The authors performed an exact one-loop HTLpt calculation of the pressure and second-, fourth-, and sixth-order diagonal quark number susceptibilities without the previously used expansion in the ratio of thermal masses to temperature (m/T-expansion), and compared it to the expanded one-loop HTLpt results. They also performed a resummation of the existing four-loop weak coupling expression for the pressure using dimensional reduction to Electrostatic QCD (EQCD), keeping the three-dimensional gauge coupling and electric screening mass unexpanded in the four-dimensional coupling. The results were compared to state-of-the-art lattice calculations and to a recent mass-expanded three-loop HTLpt calculation.

## Key numbers

* For Nf=3 at zero density, the exact one-loop HTLpt pressure shows that the m/T-expansion truncated at orders g^2, g^3, and g^4 converges reasonably fast, with the g^4 truncation (brown dashed line) approaching the exact result (red solid line) as the coupling g increases.
* The second-order diagonal quark number susceptibility normalized to its Stefan-Boltzmann value, chi_u2/chi_u2_SB, is given to order g^4 as: 1 - (3 d_A)/(8 N_c pi^2) g^2 + d_A (1 + N_f/(2 N_c))^(1/2) sqrt(3/N_c)/(8 pi^3) g^3 + (d_A)/(32 pi^4) (1 + N_f/(2 N_c)) [pi^2/3 - 7/2 + gamma_E + log(Lambda_bar/(4 pi T)) + d_A (6 - pi^2)/(4 N_c (2 N_c + N_f))] g^4.
* The fourth-order diagonal quark number susceptibility normalized to its Stefan-Boltzmann value, chi_u4/chi_u4_SB, is given to order g^4 as: 1 - (3 d_A)/(8 N_c pi^2) g^2 + 3 d_A (N_f/N_c) (1 + N_f/(2 N_c))^(-1/2) sqrt(3/N_c)/(32 pi^3) g^3 + (3 d_A)/(32 pi^4) (N_f/(2 N_c)) [pi^2/3 - 7/2 + gamma_E + log(Lambda_bar/(4 pi T)) + d_A (6 - pi^2)/(12 N_c N_f)] g^4.
* The Debye mass parameter is defined as m_D^2 = (g^2/3) [(N_c + N_f/2) T^2 + (3/(2 pi^2)) sum_g mu_g^2], and the quark thermal mass as m_q_f^2 = (g^2/16) ((N_c^2 - 1)/N_c) (T^2 + mu_f^2/pi^2).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop HTLpt calculations of Mogliacci et al. (2013) by providing an exact (non-expanded) one-loop result and generalizing to sixth-order susceptibilities and the full chemical potential dependence of the pressure, covering Nf=2 as well. It also extends the DR-motivated resummation of Blaizot et al. (2003) and Mikkel and York (2010) to nonzero chemical potentials. The results enable a direct comparison with lattice data and with the three-loop HTLpt calculation of Haque et al. (2013), testing the convergence of the m/T-expansion that is essential for higher-order HTLpt computations.
