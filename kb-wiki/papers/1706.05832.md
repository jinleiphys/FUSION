# Parameter-independent predictions for shape variables of heavy deformed nuclei in the proxy-SU(3) model

> Using a new approximate analytic parameter-free proxy-SU(3) scheme, we make predictions of shape observables for deformed nuclei, namely beta and gamma deformation variables, and compare these with empirical data and with predictions by relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field theories.

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) model provides parameter-free predictions for the beta and gamma deformation variables of heavy deformed nuclei that agree well with both relativistic mean-field (RMF) predictions and empirical data, though the predictions rely on a rescaling factor A/(S_p+S_n) to account for shell size and show a sudden minimum at N=116 that may indicate a prolate-to-oblate shape transition.

## Method

The authors compute beta deformation variables from the SU(3) irreducible representation quantum numbers (lambda, mu) using the established relation beta^2 = (4pi/5)(1/(A*r_bar^2)^2)(lambda^2+lambda*mu+mu^2+3lambda+3mu+3), with a rescaling factor A/(S_p+S_n) applied to account for shell size (S_p=32, S_n=44 for rare earths). Gamma deformation variables are computed from gamma = arctan(sqrt(3)(mu+1)/(2lambda+mu+3)). The (lambda,mu) values for ground state bands are taken from a prior table, and predictions are compared to RMF calculations, Gogny D1S calculations, and empirical values from B(E2) transition rates and gamma vibrational bandhead ratios.

## Key numbers

- Rescaling factor for rare earths: A/76, where S_p = 32 (shell size 82-50) and S_n = 44 (shell size 126-82)
- Constant r_0 = 0.87 used in the mean square radius relation sqrt(r_bar^2) = r_0 * A^(1/6)
- Gamma variable shows a sharp jump from values close to 0 degrees to values close to 60 degrees near N=116 in both proxy-SU(3) and Gogny D1S predictions
- In some isotopic chains, gamma rises only up to 30 degrees at large neutron number N, indicating possible triaxial shapes

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry scheme (Bonatsos et al., prior papers) to make quantitative predictions for shape variables of deformed rare earth nuclei, connecting SU(3) quantum numbers to the collective model variables of Bohr and Mottelson. The predictions are compared to relativistic mean-field calculations (Lalazissis et al.) and Gogny D1S calculations (Robledo et al.), as well as empirical values from Raman et al. for beta and from Casten and Esser for gamma. The results enable systematic parameter-free predictions of nuclear shapes across isotopic chains and identify regions near N=116 where prolate-to-oblate shape transitions may occur.
