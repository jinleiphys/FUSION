# Renormalization of Singular Potentials and Power Counting

> We use a toy model to illustrate how to build effective theories for singular potentials. We consider a central attractive 1/r^2 potential perturbed by a 1/r^4 correction. The power-counting rule, an important ingredient of effective theory, is established by seeking the minimum set of short-range counterterms that renormalize the scattering amplitude. We show that leading-order counterterms are needed in all partial waves where the potential overcomes the centrifugal barrier, and that the additional counterterms at next-to-leading order are the ones expected on the basis of dimensional analysis.

## Key claim

The authors show that for an attractive singular potential (here a toy model with a leading-order $-1/r^2$ potential and a next-to-leading-order $\pm 1/r^4$ correction), leading-order counterterms are required in all partial waves where the potential overcomes the centrifugal barrier, and the number of next-to-leading-order counterterms follows naive dimensional analysis, but only if the NLO correction is treated in perturbation theory rather than iterated to all orders.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in partial-wave projection for a central attractive $-1/r^2$ potential with a perturbative $\pm 1/r^4$ correction, using a momentum-space cutoff $\Lambda$. They determine the minimum set of short-range counterterms needed to achieve renormalization-group invariance (cutoff independence) of the scattering amplitude at leading order and next-to-leading order, treating the NLO potential in first-order distorted-wave Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular momentum for the singularity of the $-1/r^2$ potential is $l_c = \sqrt{\lambda} - 1/2$, where $\lambda > 0$ is the dimensionless strength parameter of the inverse-square potential.
- The perturbative expansion in $\lambda$ breaks down when $l \lesssim l_p \equiv \lambda \pi/4 - 1/2$, requiring non-perturbative treatment.
- For the $P$-wave example, $\lambda = 4.25$ is chosen, which gives $1 < l_c < l_p$, meaning the singularity is present in the $P$ wave.
- The running coupling constant $C_l^{(0)}(\Lambda)$ satisfies the RG equation $\Lambda d C_l^{(0)}/d\Lambda = (1/(2l+1)\Lambda^{2l+1}) (\lambda - \Lambda^{2l+1} C_l^{(0)})^2$, with solution exhibiting log-periodic behavior with period set by $\nu_l = \sqrt{\lambda - (l+1/2)^2}$.
- The boundary condition is set by $\Lambda_{*l}$, defined such that $\Lambda_{*l}^{2l+1} C_l^{(0)}(\Lambda_{*l}) = -\lambda$, with $\Lambda_{*1}/m = 0.2$ used in the $P$-wave numerical example.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group treatment of singular potentials from the $S$-wave case studied by Beane et al. (2001), Coon et al. (2002), and Hammer and Higa (2004) to all partial waves, and from a pure $-1/r^2$ potential to include a perturbative $-1/r^4$ correction. The authors address the problem raised by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) that counterterms appear necessary in all partial waves where the tensor force is attractive, which could threaten predictive power in few-body systems. The toy model demonstrates that a perturbative treatment of subleading singular corrections requires only the counterterms expected from dimensional analysis, providing a framework for systematic improvement in nuclear effective field theories where two-pion exchange gives a $1/r^5$ correction to the one-pion-exchange tensor force.
