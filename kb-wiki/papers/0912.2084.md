# Molecule model for kaonic nuclear cluster anti-KNN

> We analyse the properties of the kaonic nuclear cluster (KNC) anti-KNN with the structure Nx(anti-KN)_(I = 0), having the quantum numbers I(J^P) = 1/2(0^-), and treated as a quasi-bound hadronic molecule state. We describe the properties of the hadronic molecule, or the KNC Nx(anti-KN)_(I = 0), in terms of vibrational degrees of freedom with oscillator wave functions and chiral dynamics. These wave functions, having the meaning of trial wave functions of variational calculations, are parameterised by the frequency of oscillations of the (anti-KN)_(I = 0) pair, which is fixed in terms of the binding energy of the strange baryon resonance Lambda(1405), treated as a quasi-bound (anti-KN)_(I = 0) state. The binding energies B_X and widths Gamma_X of the states X = (anti-KN)_(I = 0) and X = anti-KNN, respectively, are calculated in the heavy-baryon approximation by using chiral Lagrangians with meson-baryon derivative couplings invariant under chiral SU(3)xSU(3) symmetry at the tree-level approximation. The results are B_(anti-KNN) = 40.2 MeV and Gamma_(anti-KNN) = Gamma^(non-pionic)_(anti-KNN) + Gamma^(pionic)_(anti-KNN) ~ (85 - 106) MeV and, where Gamma^(non-pionic)_(anti-KNN) ~ 21 MeV and Gamma^(pionic)_(anti-KNN) ~ (64 - 86) MeV are the widths of non-pionic anti-KNN -> N Lambda^0, N Sigma and pionic anti-KNN -> N Sigma pi decay modes, calculated for B_(anti-KN) = 29 MeV and Gamma_(anti-KN) = (30 - 40) MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the kaonic nuclear cluster (KNC) anti-KNN, treated as a quasi-bound hadronic molecule state with structure N⊗(anti-KN)_(I=0), has a binding energy of 40.2 MeV and a total width of 85–106 MeV, but these results depend on the assumed mass and width of the Lambda(1405) resonance (taken as 1405 MeV with width either 30 or 40 MeV) and are calculated at tree-level approximation using chiral Lagrangians with heavy-baryon approximation.

## Method

The authors model the anti-KNN system as a hadronic molecule with vibrational degrees of freedom described by harmonic oscillator wave functions, parameterized by oscillation frequencies fixed by the binding energy of the Lambda(1405) resonance treated as a quasi-bound (anti-KN)_(I=0) state. They calculate binding energies and widths using chiral Lagrangians with meson-baryon derivative couplings invariant under chiral SU(3)×SU(3) symmetry at tree-level in the heavy-baryon approximation, with the binding energies induced by Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions only.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of anti-KNN: B_(anti-KNN) = 40.2 MeV
- Total width of anti-KNN: Gamma_(anti-KNN) ~ (85 – 106) MeV, depending on input width of Lambda(1405)
- Non-pionic width of anti-KNN (decays to N Lambda^0, N Sigma): Gamma^(non-pionic)_(anti-KNN) ~ 21 MeV
- Pionic width of anti-KNN (decay to N Sigma pi): Gamma^(pionic)_(anti-KNN) ~ (64 – 86) MeV
- Binding energy of (anti-KN)_(I=0) state: B_(anti-KN) = 29.0 MeV
- Width of (anti-KN)_(I=0) state: Gamma_(anti-KN) = (30 – 40) MeV
- Oscillation frequency of (anti-KN)_(I=0) pair: Omega_(Lambda*) = 46.3 MeV
- Oscillation frequency of N relative to (anti-KN)_(I=0) pair: Omega_(Lambda*N) = 35.0 MeV
- Reduced mass of anti-KN pair: mu_(Lambda*) = 324 MeV
- Reduced mass of N(anti-KN) pair: mu_(Lambda*N) = 568 MeV
- Partial width for anti-KNN → N Lambda^0: 14.6 MeV
- Partial width for anti-KNN → N Sigma^0: 2.0 – 2.2 MeV
- Partial width for anti-KNN → N Sigma^+: 4.0 – 4.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002–2005), who predicted a binding energy of 48 MeV and pionic width of 61 MeV for the same system, by providing a molecule model that also calculates non-pionic decay widths. The results agree well with the coupled-channel Faddeev equation approach (KNC5, KNC6) and with the width of the unbound Lambda*N system calculated in KNC8 (22 MeV), but only qualitatively with other approaches such as relativistic mean field models (KNC1, KNC2) and variational analyses (KNC4, KNC7). The model enables calculation of all decay modes including two-body non-pionic channels relevant for experimental observation by FINUDA and DISTO collaborations.
