# Holographic Heavy Quark Symmetry

> We investigate the heavy quark spin symmetry, i.e. the mass degeneracy of pseudo-scalar and vector quarkonia at heavy quark limit, by using the gauge/gravity correspondence. We allow generic D3-like geometry with a flavor D7-brane, to avoid supersymmetric mass degeneracy. For geometries admitting physical QCD-like properties, we find that the mass degeneracy is generically achieved in a good accuracy, up to a few percent mass splitting. We also compute spectra of excited quarkonia states, and discuss comparisons with experiments and quark-model calculations.

## Key claim

The heavy quark spin symmetry (mass degeneracy of pseudo-scalar and vector quarkonia in the heavy quark limit) is generically approximate but not exact in holographic QCD models, with a slight mass splitting of at most about 1.5% even in the limit of infinite quark mass.

## Method

The authors construct a semi-bottom-up deformed D3-D7 model in type IIB superstring theory, where the background geometry is deformed from AdS5 x S5 by four parameters (alpha, beta, gamma, delta) and holds no supersymmetry or conformal symmetry. They impose physical constraints including the null energy condition, area law of holographic entanglement entropy, and conditions for finite quark masses, which restrict the parameter space to a triangular region. The mass spectra of pseudo-scalar and vector quarkonia are computed as fluctuations of the scalar and vector fields on a probe D7-brane, solving the equations of motion derived from the DBI action.

## Key numbers

- The mass splitting between pseudo-scalar and vector quarkonia is at most about 1.5% in the heavy quark limit, based on the numerical analysis of the allowed parameter region.
- The parameter alpha - beta is constrained to be less than or equal to sqrt(3) (approximately 1.732) from the null energy condition.
- The parameter alpha - beta is further constrained to be less than or equal to 1 from the requirement of weakly coupled gravity (gamma >= 0).
- The meson mass M scales as M proportional to r0^(alpha+beta), where r0 is the turning point of the D7-brane, while the constituent quark mass scales as m_Q proportional to r0^(alpha-beta).

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark spin symmetry from heavy quark effective theory (HQET) by Isgur (1989) and Neubert (1993) and from potential nonrelativistic QCD by Brambilla (2004) into the holographic framework using gauge/gravity duality. The authors note that in supersymmetric D3/D7 models (Karch 2002, Kruczenski 2003), pseudo-scalar and vector mesons are exactly degenerate regardless of quark mass, so a non-supersymmetric deformed geometry is necessary to test whether the symmetry emerges. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD simulations (Aoki 2013) and quark-model calculations (Godfrey 1985) for heavy quarkonium spectra, and can be applied to study heavy hadron decays and productions where heavy quark spin symmetry provides relations among decay ratios.
