# Equilibrium distributions in entropy driven balanced processes

> For entropy driven balanced processes we obtain final states with Poisson, Bernoulli, negative binomial and Pólya distributions. We apply this both for complex networks and particle production. For random networks we follow the evolution of the degree distribution, $P_n$, in a system where a node can activate $k$ fixed connections from $K$ possible partnerships among all nodes. The total number of connections, $N$, is also fixed. For particle physics problems $P_n$ is the probability of having $n$ particles (or other quanta) distributed among $k$ states (phase space cells) while altogether a fixed number of $N$ particles reside on $K$ states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified master-equation framework, based on entropy-driven balanced processes with symmetric decay and growth rates, yields stationary distributions of the Poisson, Bernoulli, negative binomial, and Pólya types for both random network degree distributions and particle multiplicity distributions, but only under the specific condition that the rates respect detailed balance and the system is closed.

## Method

The authors model the dynamics of a subsystem (k cells) exchanging "stones" (connections or particles) with an environment (K-k cells), using a master equation for the probability P_n(t) of having n stones in the subsystem. They derive the stationary (detailed balance) distribution Q_n from the ratio of growth rates μ_n and decay rates λ_n, and then compute Q_n explicitly for several physically motivated rate choices: constant rates (Poisson), rates proportional to the remaining capacity of the environment (Bernoulli), rates derived from random repositioning of distinguishable stones (Pólya for fermions), and rates derived from random repositioning of indistinguishable stones (Pólya for bosons, leading to the negative binomial in the small-subsystem limit).

## Key numbers

* For the fermionic Pólya distribution (single-occupancy cells), the stationary probability is Q_n = [binom(k,n) binom(K-k, N-n)] / binom(K,N), with expectation value ⟨n⟩ = kN/K.
* For the bosonic Pólya distribution (multiple-occupancy cells), the stationary probability is Q_n = [binom(n+k-1,n) binom((N-n)+(K-k)-1, N-n)] / binom(N+K-1,N), with expectation value ⟨n⟩ = kN/K.
* For the negative binomial distribution (bosonic, small-subsystem limit), the variance satisfies δn² = ⟨n⟩ (1 + ⟨n⟩/k).
* For the negative binomial distribution, the resulting single-particle energy spectrum is ρ^NBD(ω) = (1 + (⟨n⟩/k) (ω/E))^{-k}, which is a Tsallis–Pareto distribution with Tsallis parameter q = 1 + 1/k.
* In the dilute limit (K ≫ N, k ≫ n), the Poisson distribution emerges with spectrum ρ^POISSON(ω) = e^{-ω/T}, where the kinetic temperature is T = E/⟨n⟩.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between quantum statistics and random-network degree distributions previously explored by Krapivsky (2000) and Dorogovtsev (2000), and it unifies the statistical counting approach of Boltzmann with a master-equation dynamics. The results enable a classification scheme for equilibrium distributions in closed systems with random rewiring (networks) or random particle production (high-energy collisions), and they show that the observed Tsallis–Pareto particle spectra in experiments follow naturally from a negative binomial multiplicity distribution. The authors note that power-law tailed degree distributions, as in Barabási (1999), require far-from-equilibrium open systems rather than the balanced closed-system dynamics considered here.
