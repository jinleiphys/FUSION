# Instabilities driven equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma

> Due to anisotropic momentum distributions the parton system produced at the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions is unstable with respect to the magnetic plasma modes. The instabilities isotropize the system and thus speed up the process of its equilibration. The whole scenario of the instabilities driven isotropization is reviewed.

## Key claim

Due to anisotropic momentum distributions, the parton system produced at the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions is unstable with respect to magnetic plasma modes (Weibel/filamentation instabilities), and these instabilities isotropize the system and thus speed up its equilibration; however, the instabilities-driven isotropization is a mean-field reversible phenomenon not accompanied by entropy production, so collisions are still needed to reach full thermodynamic equilibrium.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of plasma instabilities in the quark-gluon plasma, analyzing the dispersion equation for unstable gluonic modes derived from the polarization tensor (gluon self-energy) in an anisotropic parton system. The analysis uses the Hard Loop effective action and the color permittivity tensor to derive the dispersion relation, applying the Penrose criterion to prove the existence of unstable solutions when the momentum distribution is anisotropic. Numerical solutions of the dispersion equation are presented for specific anisotropic momentum distributions parameterized by longitudinal and transverse momentum scales.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time from hydrodynamic analysis of experimental data: 0.6 fm/c (Heinz, 2004)
- Equilibration time from the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario with binary and 2↔3 processes: at least 2.6 fm/c (Baier, 2002)
- Inverse equilibration time from binary collisions: of order g⁴ ln(1/g) T, where g is the QCD coupling constant and T the characteristic momentum/temperature
- Characteristic inverse time of instability development: roughly of order gT for a sufficiently anisotropic momentum distribution
- Numerical example: coupling α_s = 0.3, σ_⟂ = 0.3 GeV, effective parton density 6 fm⁻³, showing unstable modes exist in a finite interval of wave vectors

## Context

This review extends the line of work on quark-gluon plasma equilibration that began in the 1980s with studies by Baym (1984), Chakraborty (1985), and Kajantie (1985), and continues through the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000). The paper contrasts the collisional equilibration approach (which gives times ≥ 2.6 fm/c) with the instability-driven isotropization mechanism, which is much faster (∼ 1/gT). It builds on the demonstration by Mrowczynski (1993) that the magnetic Weibel instability is relevant for heavy-ion collisions, and on numerical simulations by Dumitru (2005) showing efficient isotropization. The review enables future work on incorporating instabilities into the "bottom-up" scenario, as discussed by Bodeker (2005), and on distinguishing isotropization from full equilibration.
