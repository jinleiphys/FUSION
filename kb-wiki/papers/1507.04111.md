# Small-angle scattering and quasiclassical approximation beyond leading order

> In the present paper we examine the accuracy of the quasiclassical approach on the example of small-angle electron elastic scattering. Using the quasiclassical approach, we derive the differential cross section and the Sherman function for arbitrary localized potential at high energy. These results are exact in the atomic charge number and correspond to the leading and the next-to-leading high-energy small-angle asymptotics for the scattering amplitude. Using the small-angle expansion of the exact amplitude of electron elastic scattering in the Coulomb field, we derive the cross section and the Sherman function with a relative accuracy $θ^2$ and $θ^1$, respectively ($θ$ is the scattering angle). We show that the correction of relative order $θ^2$ to the cross section, as well as that of relative order $θ^1$ to the Sherman function, originates not only from the contribution of large angular momenta $l\gg 1$, but also from that of $l\sim 1$. This means that, in general, it is not possible to go beyond the accuracy of the next-to-leading quasiclassical approximation without taking into account the non-quasiclassical terms.

## Key claim

The quasiclassical approximation for small-angle electron elastic scattering can be extended to next-to-leading order in the scattering angle, but going beyond that accuracy is impossible without including non-quasiclassical contributions from angular momenta \(l\sim 1\), because the corrections of relative order \(\theta^2\) to the cross section and \(\theta^1\) to the Sherman function receive contributions from both large and small angular momenta.

## Method

The authors used the quasiclassical wave function formalism to derive the differential cross section and Sherman function for high-energy small-angle electron elastic scattering in an arbitrary localized potential, keeping the leading and next-to-leading terms in the scattering angle \(\theta\). They then performed a small-angle expansion of the exact partial-wave Coulomb scattering amplitude, treating the parameters \(\eta = Z\alpha\) and \(\nu = Z\alpha/\beta\) as independent, to obtain the cross section and Sherman function with relative accuracy \(\theta^2\) and \(\theta^1\) respectively. The contributions from large angular momenta (\(l\gg 1\)) and small angular momenta (\(l\sim 1\)) were separated analytically.

## Key numbers

- The leading quasiclassical Sherman function for a Coulomb field scales as \(\theta^2\): \(S = \frac{\pi m \eta \theta^2}{4\varepsilon} \Im \frac{h(\eta)}{1+2i\eta}\), where \(\eta = Z\alpha\).
- The Mott result for the Sherman function scales as \(\theta^3 \ln \theta\) and is proportional to \(\eta\), while the quasiclassical result starts at \(\eta^2\); the Mott result is not applicable when \(\theta \lesssim \eta\).
- The differential cross section for a Coulomb field in the quasiclassical approximation is \(\frac{d\sigma_0}{d\Omega} = \frac{4\eta^2}{\varepsilon^2\theta^4}\left[1 + \frac{\pi\theta\eta}{2} \Re h(\eta)\right]\).
- The charge asymmetry \(A\) and Sherman function \(S\) for a finite-size nucleus (radius \(R\)) depend on the parameter \(b = \theta \varepsilon R\) and change sign at \(b \sim 1\).
- The small-angle expansion of the exact Coulomb amplitude yields the cross section \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} = \frac{\nu^2}{4p^2 s^4}\left[1 + \pi \eta\beta \Re h(\nu) s - 2\nu^{-1} \Im [s^{2i\nu} C^*(\eta,\nu)] s^2\right]\) with \(s = \sin(\theta/2)\).

## Context

This work extends the quasiclassical approach previously applied to bremsstrahlung, pair photoproduction, Delbrück scattering, and photon splitting (Beth and Maxwell 1954; Davies, Bethe, and Maximon 1954; Olsen, Maximon, and Wergeland 1957; Olsen and Maximon 1959; Milstein 1994; Lee, Milstein, and Strakhovenko 2000; Krachkov, Lee, and Milstein 2014, 2015). It directly addresses the question of how far the quasiclassical framework can be pushed in accuracy, using electron elastic scattering as a test case. The results enable precise calculations of spin-dependent scattering observables for high-energy electrons in atomic fields, including nuclear size effects, and clarify the domain of validity of the quasiclassical approximation relative to the Born expansion (Mott 1929; McKinley and Feshbach 1948; Dalitz 1951; Johnson, Weber, and Mullin 1961; Gluckstern and Lin 1964).
