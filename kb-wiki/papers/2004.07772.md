# QZE and IZE in a simple approach and the neutron decay

> We discuss a simple and analytically solvable measurement model which describes the famous Quantum Zeno Effect (QZE) and Inverse Zeno Effect (IZE), that correspond to the slow down and to the increase of the decay rate caused by measurements (or, more in general, by the interaction of an unstable state with the detector and the environment). Within this model one can understand quite general features of the QZE and IZE: by considering an unstable quantum state, such as an unstable particle, whose decay width as function of energy is $Γ(ω)=g^{2}ω^α,$ then -- under quite general assumptions -- the QZE occurs for $α\in(0,1)$, while the IZE for $α\in(-\infty,0)\cup(1,\infty).$ This result is valid also for more realistic measurement models than the one described in this work. We then apply these considerations to the decay of the neutron, for which $α=5.$ Hence, the realization of the IZE for the neutron decay (and for the majority of weak decays) is in principle possible. Indeed, trap experiments find a lifetime that is $8.7\pm2.1$ s shorter than beam experiments, suggesting that the IZE could have taken place.

# Quantum Zeno Effect and Inverse Zeno Effect in a Simple Approach and the Neutron Decay

## Key claim
The paper shows that for an unstable quantum state with a decay width scaling as Γ(ω) = g²ω^α, the Quantum Zeno Effect (QZE) occurs for α ∈ (0,1) while the Inverse Zeno Effect (IZE) occurs for the much broader range α ∈ (-∞,0) ∪ (1,∞), making the IZE generically easier to realize than the QZE. The neutron decay (α = 5) is therefore a candidate for IZE, and the 8.7 ± 2.1 s discrepancy between trap and beam experiments could potentially be explained by the IZE rather than by beyond-standard-model physics.

## Method
The author constructs a simple analytically solvable measurement model using a rectangular response function f_rect(τ,ω) that satisfies exact normalization and limiting conditions, then expands the measured decay width Γ^measured(τ) as a Taylor series in λ/ω_n (where λ = 1/τ is the measurement rate parameter). The result is generalized to any symmetric response function expressible as a sum of such rectangular functions, and applied to the neutron decay using the known phase-space factor α = 5 for its weak decay width.

## Key numbers
- Neutron on-shell decay energy: ω_onshell = 0.782333 MeV
- Neutron on-shell decay width: Γ_onshell = 7.41146 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV (corresponding to τ_n^beam = 888.1 s)
- Neutron coupling constant: g_n = 1.59028 × 10⁻¹² MeV⁻²
- Beam experiment neutron lifetime: τ_n^beam = 888.1 ± 2.0 s
- Trap experiment neutron lifetime: τ_n^trap = 879.37 ± 0.58 s
- For λ = 0.0424 MeV in the rectangular model, Γ^measured/Γ_onshell = 1.0098 (a 0.98% increase)
- Muon on-shell energy: ω_on-shell ≈ 105.658 MeV; to achieve a 1% IZE increase would require λ = 5.787 MeV

## Context
This work extends the general framework for QZE and IZE developed in earlier works by Koshino and collaborators, and by Facchi and Pascazio, by providing a simple exactly solvable model that clarifies the conditions for each effect. The specific application to the neutron lifetime anomaly builds on the proposal by Giacosa and Pagliara (2020) that the 8.7 s discrepancy between beam experiments (which measure emitted protons) and trap experiments (which monitor surviving neutrons) could be due to IZE rather than systematic errors or beyond-standard-model dark decays as proposed by Fornal and Grinstein (2018). The result enables future experimental searches for IZE in cold neutron systems and clarifies why weak decays generically favor the IZE over the QZE.
