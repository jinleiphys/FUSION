# On the lepton-nucleon neutral and charged current deep inelastic scattering cross sections

> Based on the requirement in the simulation of lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS), we construct a fortran program LDCS 1.0 calculating the differential and total cross sections for the unpolarized charged lepton-unpolarized nucleon and neutrino-unpolarized nucleon neutral current (charged current) DIS at leading order. Any set of the experimentally fitted parton distribution functions could be employed directly. The mass of incident and scattered leptons is taken into account and the boundary conditions calculating the single differential and total cross section are studied. The calculated results well agree with the corresponding experimental data which indicating the LDCS 1.0 program is good. It is also turned out that the effect of tauon mass is not negligible in the GeV energy level.

## Key claim

The authors present the FORTRAN program LDCS 1.0 for calculating lepton-nucleon deep inelastic scattering cross sections at leading order, claiming good agreement with experimental data. The abstract glosses over the condition that the agreement is good only at high incident lepton energy (above approximately 100 GeV) for neutrino-iron charged current scattering, while agreement is poor at low energy because the theory only covers DIS and not quasi-elastic scattering or resonance production.

## Method

The authors derived the double differential cross section formula for unpolarized lepton-unpolarized nucleon neutral current and charged current DIS at leading order, including the masses of incident and scattered leptons. They implemented this formula in a FORTRAN 90 program called LDCS 1.0, which uses the LHAPDF 5.9.1 library to access experimentally fitted parton distribution functions. The program calculates double differential, single differential, and total cross sections by integrating over kinematic variables with boundary conditions that account for lepton masses and DIS kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- Electron-proton DIS total cross section calculations used kinematic cuts of Q²_min = 1.0 GeV² and W²_min = 1.96 GeV²
- Unpolarized electron-unpolarized proton neutral current DIS total cross section at √s = 318.7 GeV calculated with HERAPDF1.5 LO PDF set took 3.4 seconds of running time
- Neutrino-unpolarized iron charged current DIS total cross section at √s = 20.0 GeV calculated with HKNlo PDF set took 0.46 seconds of running time
- For neutrino-iron charged current scattering, the theory agrees with experiment at high incident lepton energy (E_i ≥ 100 GeV) but disagrees at low energy
- The tauon mass effect is not negligible at GeV energy levels, as demonstrated by the reduced x and y integral region for tauon-induced DIS compared to electron or muon-induced DIS at √s = 3.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of existing PDF fitting packages like HERAfitter (Alekhin et al., 2014; Aaron et al., 2010) by providing a standalone program specifically for lepton-nucleus DIS simulations, such as the PACIAE 2.2 model (Zhou et al., 2014). The inclusion of lepton masses addresses a point raised by Hayato (2009) that tauon mass cannot be neglected at GeV energies, which is typically ignored in standard calculations (Beringer et al., 2012). The program enables future lepton-nucleus DIS Monte Carlo simulations by providing a simple, self-consistent cross section calculator that can use any experimentally fitted PDF set.
