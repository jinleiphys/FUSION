# Tensor interaction contributions to single-particle energies

> We calculate the contribution of the nucleon-nucleon tensor interaction to single-particle energies with finite-range $ G $ matrix potentials and with zero-range Skyrme potentials. The Skx Skyrme parameters including the zero-range tensor terms with strengths calibrated to the finite-range results are refitted to nuclear properties. The fit allows the zero-range proton-neutron tensor interaction as calibrated to the finite-range potential results and that gives the observed change in the single-particle gap $ε$(h$_{11/2}$)-$ε$(g$_{7/2}$) going from $^{114}$Sn to $^{132}$Sn. However, the experimental $\ell$ dependence of the spin-orbit splittings in $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb is not well described when the tensor is added, due to a change in the radial dependence of the total spin-orbit potential. The gap shift and a good fit to the $\ell$-dependence can be recovered when the like-particle tensor interaction is opposite in sign to that required for the $ G $ matrix.

# Tensor interaction contributions to single-particle energies

## Key claim

The finite-range nucleon-nucleon tensor interaction can explain the observed 1.76 MeV shift of the 0g7/2–0h11/2 single-particle gap between 114Sn and 132Sn, but a zero-range tensor implementation in Skyrme Hartree-Fock fails to reproduce the ℓ-dependence of spin-orbit splittings in 132Sn and 208Pb unless the like-particle (T=1) tensor strength is taken opposite in sign to the G-matrix prediction.

## Method

The authors calculated tensor contributions to proton single-particle energies in 132Sn using the finite-range Hosaka–Kubo–Toki (HKT) G-matrix interaction, then calibrated the strengths αt and βt of a zero-range tensor interaction to match those finite-range results. They refitted the Skx Skyrme parameters (t0, t1, t2, t3, x0, x1, x2, x3, and spin-orbit strength W) with the tensor terms fixed, producing parameter sets Skxta (αt=60, βt=110) and Skxtb (βt=110, αt=−118), and compared the resulting single-particle energies to experimental centroid energies from 114Sn and 132Sn.

## Key numbers

- The experimental 0g7/2–0h11/2 gap changes by 1.76 MeV: from 2.79 MeV in 132Sn to 1.03 MeV in 114Sn.
- The finite-range tensor interaction alone produces a gap shift of 1.64 MeV between 114Sn and 132Sn.
- The Skx interaction (no tensor) gives a χ² of 0.60; Skxta (αt=60, βt=110) gives χ²=1.50; Skxtb (αt=−118, βt=110) recovers χ²=0.63.
- The zero-range tensor strengths calibrated to the finite-range G-matrix are αt=60 MeV·fm⁵ and βt=110 MeV·fm⁵.
- The Skxtb fit requires αt ≈ −βt, with βt=110 MeV·fm⁵ and αt=−118 MeV·fm⁵, opposite in sign to the G-matrix value of αt≈60 MeV·fm⁵.
- The one-pion exchange range parameter is rπ=1.414 fm; the short-range potential has rs=0.25 fm with strengths Ws,T=0=3105 MeV and Ws,T=1=−1382 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tensor effects on shell structure by Otsuka et al. (2005) and Stancu et al. (1977), and complements finite-range tensor calculations in the Gogny model by Goriely et al. (2005). It provides the first systematic global fit of a Skyrme interaction including zero-range tensor terms to nuclear single-particle energies, but reveals that the zero-range approximation distorts the radial shape of the spin-orbit potential, motivating future work with improved density functionals, three-body forces, and coupling to vibrations as discussed by Lesinski et al. (2006).
