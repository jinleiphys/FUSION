# Gauge-invariant theory of pion photoproduction with dressed hadrons

> Based on an effective field theory of hadrons in which quantum chromodynamics is assumed to provide the necessary bare cutoff functions, a gauge-invariant theory of pion photoproduction with fully dressed nucleons is described. The formalism provides consistent dynamical descriptions of pi-N --> pi-N scattering and Gamma-N --> pi-N production mechanisms in terms of nonlinear integral equations for fully dressed hadrons. The dressed hadron currents and the pion photoproduction current satisfy gauge invariance in a self-consistent manner. Approximations are discussed that make the nonlinear formalism manageable in practice and yet preserve gauge invariance.

# Gauge-invariant theory of pion photoproduction with dressed hadrons

## Key claim
The paper presents a comprehensive, gauge-invariant formalism for pion photoproduction (γN → πN) using fully dressed hadrons, where the gauge invariance of all physical currents is maintained self-consistently through a "gauge derivative" operation that goes beyond minimal substitution for nonlocal vertices. The formalism is highly nonlinear, but the author claims that gauge invariance can be preserved even under practical approximations by replacing the full interaction current with a sum of single-particle leg currents that require only physical values of vertex functions.

## Method
The formalism derives physical currents for all processes contributing to pion photoproduction from their corresponding hadronic n-point Green's functions using a mathematical operation called a "gauge derivative." The interaction current for the fully dressed case is decomposed into a bare Kroll-Ruderman contact term, an exchange current contribution U^μ, and an auxiliary current b^μ dressed by hadronic final-state interactions X. The underlying effective hadronic field theory for πN scattering provides coupled nonlinear integral equations for the dressed nucleon propagator, the dressed N-Nπ vertex, and the nonpolar πN amplitude X, with the full πN Bethe-Salpeter amplitude expressed as the sum of a polar s-channel term and the solution X.

## Context
This work extends a long history of pion photoproduction descriptions dating back to the 1950s, including foundational work by Ward (1950), Kroll and Ruderman (1954), Takahashi (1957), and Kazes (1959). It adds to more recent approaches by Gross, Afnan, Ohta, Friar, Coester, and Banerjee that attempt to account for the internal structure of hadrons. The formalism enables consistent dynamical descriptions of both πN scattering and γN photoproduction within a single gauge-invariant framework, and the author proposes an approximation method that avoids theoretical problems identified by Banerjee with Ohta's method, which requires vertex functions at unphysical values due to an underlying non-Hermitian effective Lagrangian.
