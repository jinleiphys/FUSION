# Evidence for Gluon Energy Loss as the Mechanism for Heavy Quarkonium Suppression in $pA$ Collisions

> We study the energy and nuclear A dependence of the hadronic production of heavy quarkonia. We review theoretical ideas which have been put forward, seeking a consistent global picture reconciling the large effects in quarkonia with the small nuclear effects observed in continuum Drell Yan production. The data indicates that shadowing or leading twist modifications of parton distributions can be ruled out as explanations, leaving higher twist energy loss. {}From general principles the maximum allowed energy loss of partons traversing the nuclear medium can be related to the parton transverse momenta. We then show that the experimental data on nuclear suppression of charm- and bottom- onium for large $x_F$ is consistent with this effect: using the observed transverse momenta to bound the $x_F$ dependence in an almost model independent manner generates a relation that practically reproduces the data. Several prediction are discussed; the dependence on $x_F$ as $x_F\to 1$, and large and small $k_T^2$ cuts, can be used to discriminate between quark and gluon induced effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of heavy quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions is consistent with gluon energy loss via a higher-twist mechanism, and that the data rule out leading-twist explanations such as gluon shadowing or EMC-type modifications of parton distributions. The authors claim this conclusion is "almost model independent" because the bound on energy loss derived from the uncertainty principle, when combined with the experimentally measured transverse momentum distributions, reproduces the observed x_F dependence of the suppression.

## Method

The authors derive an upper bound on parton energy loss from the uncertainty principle, relating the maximum allowed energy loss Δx₁ to the nuclear length L_A and the parton transverse momentum k_T via Δx₁ ≤ L_A k_T²/(2E_p). They then apply the Gavin-Milana mechanism, where a small shift in the x_F value of incoming gluons produces a large suppression due to the steep (1-x)⁵ falloff of the gluon distribution function. Using experimentally measured values of ⟨k_T²⟩_A for bottomonium and Drell-Yan production, they compute the resulting x_F-dependent suppression ratios for nuclear targets relative to deuterium.

## Key numbers

- For tungsten (A=184) and x_F=0.5, the energy loss mechanism leads to approximately 60% of the events compared to a calculation neglecting energy loss
- The average transverse momentum squared for bottomonium on tungsten is ⟨k_T²⟩_A = [0.16 (A^{1/3} - 2^{1/3}) + 2.59] GeV²
- The A-dependent part of ⟨k_T²⟩_A for bottomonium increases as 0.16 A^{1/3} GeV²
- The Drell-Yan continuum ⟨k_T²⟩_A has an A-dependent part about 10 times smaller than that of bottomonium
- The p₀ parameter for the k_T distribution fit is 3.22 for Υ production on deuterium and 2.78 for Drell-Yan on deuterium
- The intrinsic transverse momentum ⟨k_T²⟩_int is taken as 0.91 GeV², in analogy to Drell-Yan

## Context

This paper extends the energy loss mechanism proposed by Gavin and Milana (1992) for quarkonium suppression, while addressing the bound derived by Brodsky and Hoyer (1993) that claimed such energy loss was insufficient. The authors argue that the Brodsky-Hoyer bound, when applied with the actual measured transverse momenta (which are much larger than the 300 MeV assumed by Brodsky and Hoyer), is consistent with the data. The work contradicts the shadowing explanation of Banesh, Qiu and Vary (1993), who claimed quark shadowing accounts for the suppression. The paper enables future experimental tests by predicting that the x_F dependence and k_T² cuts can discriminate between quark and gluon induced effects, and that the charmonium k_T distribution should be similar to that of bottomonium.
