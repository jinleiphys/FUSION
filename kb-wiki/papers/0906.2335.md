# Internal stucture of clusters in $^{112-122}$Ba nuclei within relativistic mean field theory

> We study the clustering structure and the internal or sub-structure of clusters in $^{112-122}$Ba nuclei within the framework of relativistic mean field theory in an axially deformed cylindrical co-ordinate. We calculate the total density distribution, and the individual neutrons and protons density distributions. From the analysis of the clustering confugurations of the density distributions of various shapes, we find different sub-structures inside the Ba nuclei considered here. The important step, carried out for the first time, is the counting of number of protons and neutrons present in the clustering region(s). $^{12}$C is shown to consitute the cluster configuration of Ba nuclei in most cases, with $^{2,3}$H and $^4$He constituting the neck between two fissioning symmetrical fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework, the internal cluster structure of \(^{112-122}\)Ba nuclei can be identified and quantified for the first time by counting the number of protons and neutrons in each clustering region, with \(^{12}\)C constituting the most common cluster configuration in most cases, but only for specific deformation minima and not for all isotopes or all shapes.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 parameter set in a cylindrical coordinate system, solving the coupled field equations by expanding Dirac spinors and boson fields in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis. They computed potential energy surfaces (PES) via constrained calculations, obtained total and individual neutron/proton density distributions for each deformation minimum, and then integrated the density over selected spatial regions to count the number of nucleons, protons, and neutrons in each identified cluster.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy for \(^{112}\)Ba: 897.33 MeV (RMF) at \(\beta_2 = 10.71\) (hyper-deformed solution), with the largest binding energy minimum at \(\beta_2 = 0.24\) giving 895.38 MeV.
- Ground-state binding energy for \(^{122}\)Ba: 1015.52 MeV (RMF) at \(\beta_2 = 0.32\), compared to the experimental value of 1015.499 MeV.
- Quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) for the ground state of \(^{116}\)Ba: 0.36 (RMF), compared to the FRDM value of 0.28.
- Matter rms radius \(r_m\) for the hyper-deformed state of \(^{112}\)Ba at \(\beta_2 = 10.71\): 12.17 fm.
- For the oblate state of \(^{112}\)Ba at \(\beta_2 = -0.39\), the cluster identified contains 11.6 nucleons (5.6 protons, 6.0 neutrons), assigned as \(^{12}\)C.
- For the hyper-deformed state of \(^{120}\)Ba at \(\beta_2 = 10.97\), the neck region contains 3.8 nucleons (1.6 protons, 2.1 neutrons), assigned as \(^{4}\)He.

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of clustering in light nuclei such as \(^{8}\)Be (Arumugam 2005) and in heavy actinides (Patra 2007), where cluster structures were identified but the internal composition was not determined. It also connects to the preformed cluster model (PCM) of Gupta and collaborators (Gupta 1993, Kumar 1994-1996) which predicted \(^{12}\)C decay from \(^{112-120}\)Ba, and to the dynamical cluster-decay model (DCM) used to describe intermediate mass fragment emission from \(^{116}\)Ba\(^*\) compound nuclei (Gupta 2006). The ability to count nucleons in clusters enables future comparisons with measured fusion-evaporation residues and fission fragment data from reactions such as \(^{58}\)Ni+\(^{58}\)Ni (Campo 1988, 1991) and \(^{78,82}\)Kr+\(^{40}\)Ca (Bonnet 2008).
