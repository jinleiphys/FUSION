# The Rest-Frame Darwin Potential from the Lienard-Wiechert Solution in the Radiation Gauge

> In the semiclassical approximation in which the electric charges of scalar particles are described by Grassmann variables ($Q_i^2=0, Q_iQ_j\ne 0$), it is possible to re-express the Lienard-Wiechert potentials and electric fields in the radiation gauge as phase space functions, because the difference among retarded, advanced, and symmetric Green functions is of order Q_i^2. By working in the rest-frame instant form of dynamics, the elimination of the electromagnetic degrees of freedom by means of suitable second classs contraints leads to the identification of the Lienard-Wiechert reduced phase space containing only N charged particles with mutual action-at-a-distance vector and scalar potentials. A Darboux canonical basis of the reduced phase space is found. This allows one to re-express the potentials for arbitrary N as a unique effective scalar potential containing the Coulomb potential and the complete Darwin one, whose 1/c^2 component agrees for with the known expression. The effective potential gives the classical analogue of all static and non-static effects of the one-photon exchange Feynman diagram of scalar electrodynamics.

## Key claim

By using Grassmann-valued electric charges (satisfying \(Q_i^2=0, Q_iQ_j\neq0\)) in the semiclassical approximation, the retarded, advanced, and symmetric Lienard-Wiechert solutions coincide on the particle equations of motion, allowing the elimination of electromagnetic degrees of freedom and the extraction of an instantaneous action-at-a-distance effective scalar potential that contains the Coulomb potential and the complete Darwin potential to all orders in \(1/c^2\).

## Method

The authors start from the rest-frame instant form of dynamics for \(N\) scalar charged particles plus the electromagnetic field on Wigner hyperplanes, with charges described by Grassmann variables to regularize Coulomb self-energies. After canonical reduction to the radiation gauge, they evaluate the Lienard-Wiechert potentials as phase space functions and impose second-class constraints that force the electromagnetic field to coincide with the Lienard-Wiechert solution. They then introduce Dirac brackets, find a Darboux canonical basis for the reduced phase space, and derive the physical Hamiltonian containing an effective scalar potential that includes both the Coulomb and complete Darwin interactions.

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical regularization \(Q_i^2=0\) eliminates the radiation from a single particle and the causal problems of the Abraham-Lorentz-Dirac equation, since the radiation reaction coefficient \(\tau_o = 2Q^2/3mc^2\) vanishes if \(Q^2=0\).
* The fine structure constant \(\alpha = e^2/4\pi \approx 1/137\) is retained because it gets contributions from \(Q_iQ_j\) terms, not from \(Q_i^2\).
* The lowest-order \(1/c^2\) component of the Darwin potential obtained agrees with the known expression from the literature.
* For the two-body case in the center-of-mass rest frame, the generalized Darwin interaction can be written in closed form.

## Context

This work extends the rest-frame instant form of dynamics developed by Lusanna and collaborators (1990s) for \(N\) charged scalar particles plus the electromagnetic field, building on the parametrized Minkowski theory and the canonical reduction to the radiation gauge. It provides the classical analogue of all static and non-static effects of the one-photon exchange Feynman diagram of scalar electrodynamics, and the resulting Darwin potential connects to the two-body constraint models of Todorov (1970s), Crater, and others that approximate the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The approach enables a Hamiltonian description of charged particles with instantaneous action-at-a-distance interactions, avoiding the integro-differential delay equations of the standard Lienard-Wiechert formalism.
