# Thermalization, evolution and LHC observables in an integrated hydrokinetic model of A+A collisions

> A further development of the evolutionary picture of A+A collisions, which we call the integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKM), is proposed. The model comprises a generator of the initial state GLISSANDO, pre-thermal dynamics of A+A collisions leading to thermalization, subsequent relativistic viscous hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon and hadron medium (vHLLE), its particlization, and finally hadronic cascade ultrarelativistic QMD. We calculate mid-rapidity charged-particle multiplicities, pion, kaon, and antiproton spectra, charged-particle elliptic flows, and pion interferometry radii for Pb+Pb collisions at the energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider, $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV, at different centralities. We find that the best description of the experimental data is reached when the initial states are attributed to the very small initial time 0.1 fm/c, the pre-thermal stage (thermalization process) lasts at least until 1 fm/c, and the shear viscosity at the hydrodynamic stage of the matter evolution has its minimal value, $η/s = \frac{1}{4π}$. At the same time it is observed that the various momentum anisotropies of the initial states, different initial and relaxation times, as well as even a treatment of the pre-thermal stage within just viscous or ideal hydrodynamic approach, leads sometimes to worse but nevertheless similar results, if the normalization of maximal initial energy density in most central events is adjusted to reproduce the final hadron multiplicity in each scenario. This can explain a good enough data description in numerous variants of hybrid models without a prethermal stage when the initial energy densities are defined up to a common factor.

## Key claim

The integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKM) provides the best description of LHC observables when the initial state is set at 0.1 fm/c, the pre-thermal stage lasts at least until 1 fm/c, and the shear viscosity at the hydrodynamic stage takes its minimal value η/s = 1/(4π); however, the authors observe that various momentum anisotropies, different initial and relaxation times, and even treating the pre-thermal stage within viscous or ideal hydrodynamics can yield similar results if the normalization of maximal initial energy density is adjusted to reproduce final hadron multiplicity in each scenario.

## Method

The iHKM combines five components: the GLISSANDO 2 Monte Carlo Glauber generator for initial-state energy density distributions; a relaxation-model pre-thermal stage from proper time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c to τ_th = 1.0 fm/c that smoothly converts a free-streaming component into a hydrodynamic one; 2+1-dimensional boost-invariant relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (vHLLE code) using the Israel-Stewart framework with the Laine-Schroeder equation of state; a sudden Cooper-Frye particlization at the T = 165 MeV isotherm with viscous corrections via Grad's 14-moment ansatz; and finally the UrQMD hadronic cascade. The authors compute mid-rapidity charged-particle multiplicities, pion, kaon, and antiproton spectra, charged-particle elliptic flow v₂, and pion interferometry radii for Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV across seven centrality classes, using averaged initial conditions from 50000 GLISSANDO events per centrality and 20000 UrQMD events per hydrodynamic run.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c (default) and 0.5 fm/c (for comparison); thermalization time τ_th = 1.0 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s = 0.08 ≈ 1/(4π) (minimal value) and η/s = 0.2 (for comparison)
- Relative weight of binary collisions α = 0.24 gives best fit for charged-hadron multiplicities at √s = 2.76 TeV
- Maximal initial energy density ε₀ = 799.5 GeV/fm³ for the basic scenario (Λ=100, τ_rel=0.25 fm/c, η/s=0.08, τ₀=0.1 fm/c)
- Mean χ²/ndf = 3.68 for the basic scenario over all observables; χ²/ndf = 0.59 for soft pions (p_T < 0.5 GeV/c) in the basic scenario
- χ²_pion/ndf = 2.48 for η/s = 0.2 scenario; χ²_v₂/ndf = 27.92 for η/s = 0.2 scenario
- χ²_kaon/ndf = 1.9 for the basic scenario
- Grid spacing δx = δy = 0.2 fm/c and time step δτ = 0.05 fm/c for hydrodynamic calculations
- Particlization temperature T = 165 MeV, corresponding to energy density ε = 0.5 GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the HydroKinetic Model (HKM) developed by Sinyukov and collaborators (2006-2014) by integrating it with the GLISSANDO initial-state generator, the vHLLE viscous hydrodynamic code, and the UrQMD hadronic cascade into a single framework called iHKM. The pre-thermal relaxation model used here was previously introduced by Naboka et al. (2014) as an alternative to anisotropic hydrodynamics approaches such as those by Martinez and Strickland (2010-2011). The results help explain why many hybrid models without an explicit pre-thermal stage can still describe data well, since adjusting the initial energy density normalization compensates for the missing thermalization dynamics. The model enables future studies of event-by-event fluctuations and 4π-geometry observables where continuous particlization may become important.
