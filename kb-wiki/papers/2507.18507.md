# Probing Spin and Lifetime Correlations in Entangled Hyperon-AntiHyperon Pairs

> Quantum entanglement has now been demonstrated in several hadronic systems, revealing that non-classical spin correlations survive even through the strong-interaction hadronization process. To date, however, all studies have focused exclusively on angular observables, leaving the possibility untouched that quantum coherence might also influence the decay times of entangled partners. In this work we propose data-driven tests of spin-lifetime and lifetime-lifetime correlations for Lambda-antiLambda pairs produced in high-energy collisions. By examining the opening-angle distribution in slices of Delta t, constructing a pair-wise spin-lifetime correlator, and testing a simple lifetime-lifetime covariance, we search for deviations from independent exponential decay that align with known spin correlations. Observation of nonzero lifetime correlations would compel a reassessment of how entanglement manifests in decaying systems, revealing hitherto unexplored temporal coherence.

# Probing Spin and Lifetime Correlations in Entangled Hyperon-AntiHyperon Pairs

## Key claim
The paper proposes that quantum coherence in entangled Lambda–antiLambda pairs may manifest not only in angular spin correlations (already observed) but also in the decay times of the two hyperons, producing nonzero lifetime-lifetime or spin-lifetime correlations. The authors explicitly state that no known symmetry principle forbids such temporal correlations, and they propose data-driven tests using existing experimental datasets, with the null hypothesis being independent exponential decay.

## Method
The authors develop three complementary tests using reconstructed proper decay times and opening angles of Lambda–antiLambda pairs. First, they bin events by the time difference Delta-t and extract the spin-correlation coefficient P(Delta-t) from the acceptance-corrected angular distribution in each slice, testing for a non-flat dependence. Second, they construct a per-pair spin-lifetime correlator by standardizing a spin-weight variable w_i = alpha1*alpha2*cos(theta*_i) and computing its covariance with the lifetime product (t1 - tbar)*(t2 - tbar), subtracting a mixed-event baseline and assessing significance via permutation tests. Third, they propose the simplest lifetime-lifetime covariance Cov(t1, t2) as a model-independent first test.

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, STAR observes O(10^2) potentially entangled Lambda–antiLambda pairings per event and collects ~10^9 events per year, yielding ~10^11 pairs for analysis.
- With N_Lambda-antiLambda ~ 10^11, fractional lifetime correlations as small as epsilon ~ 10^-5 are within reach at the 1-sigma level (or ~3 x 10^-5 for a 3-sigma observation), where epsilon quantifies the fractional deviation from independent exponential decay via Cov(t1,t2) = epsilon * sigma_t^2.
- The Delta-t-binned test resolves modulations in P(Delta-t) at the percent level when deviations delta-P become measurable once delta-P is greater than or approximately equal to O(1)/sqrt(N_Lambda-antiLambda/K).
- The opening-angle distribution is fitted to (1/N) dN/d(cos theta*) = 1/2 [1 + (alpha1*alpha2*P) cos theta*], where alpha1,2 are weak-decay parameters and P quantifies the spin correlation.

## Context
This work extends the lineage of entanglement studies in hadronic systems, which began with neutral-meson Delta-t oscillations in K0–antiK0 and B0–antiB0 pairs (ARGUS, 1987; CPLEAR, 1998; BaBar, 2001; Belle, 2002; KLOE, 2006) and continued with spin-entanglement measurements in Lambda–antiLambda pairs at BESIII (2019) and in top–antitop pairs at ATLAS (2023) and CMS (2024). The STAR Collaboration (2025) recently demonstrated that quark-level entanglement survives nonperturbative hadronization via spin correlations in Lambda–antiLambda pairs. The proposed time-domain tests complement these angular measurements and could provide empirical constraints on decoherence models (Alicki and Lendi, 2007; Rivas, 2012; Gardiner and Zoller, 2004; Benatti, 1998; Benatti, 2017) and wavefunction-collapse models (Bassi, 2012; Ghirardi, 1985; Pearle, 1988). A positive signal would compel reassessment of how entanglement manifests in decaying systems, while a null result would place upper bounds on hidden time-domain correlations.
