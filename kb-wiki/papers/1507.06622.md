# The resonant $π^+γ\toπ^+π^0$ amplitude from Quantum Chromodynamics

> We present the first ab initio calculation of a radiative transition of a hadronic resonance within quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We compute the amplitude for $ππ\to πγ^\star$, as a function of the energy of the $ππ$ pair and the virtuality of the photon, in the kinematic regime where $ππ$ couples strongly to the unstable $ρ$ resonance. This exploratory calculation is performed using a lattice discretization of QCD with quark masses corresponding to $m_π\approx 400$~MeV. We obtain a description of the energy dependence of the transition amplitude, constrained at 48 kinematic points, that we can analytically continue to the $ρ$ pole and identify from its residue the $ρ\to πγ^\star$ form factor.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first *ab initio* calculation of a radiative transition of a hadronic resonance within QCD, specifically the amplitude for ππ → πγ* in the region where the ππ pair couples to the unstable ρ resonance. The calculation is exploratory, performed at unphysically heavy quark masses corresponding to mπ ≈ 400 MeV, and the authors note that the ρ resonance width at these masses is approximately 12 times smaller than the experimental width, meaning the cross section results are not directly comparable to physical phenomenology.

## Method
The authors use lattice QCD with Nf=2+1 dynamical fermions on an anisotropic 20³ × 128 lattice with spatial spacing as ≈ 0.12 fm and mπ ≈ 400 MeV. They compute three-point correlation functions with variationally optimized π and isospin-1 ππ operators, inserting a tree-level improved Euclidean vector current renormalized by ZV = 0.833(9). The finite-volume matrix elements are related to the infinite-volume transition amplitude using the Lellouch-Lüscher formalism extended to external currents, and the ρ → πγ* form factor is extracted by analytically continuing the amplitude to the ρ pole in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers
- mπ ≈ 400 MeV: the pion mass used in the lattice calculation
- Lattice volume: (L/as)³ × (T/at) = 20³ × 128, with as/at = 3.444(6) and as ≈ 0.12 fm
- ZV = 0.833(9): multiplicative renormalization factor for the vector current, determined by requiring the π form factor to be 1 at Q² = 0 GeV²
- 48 kinematic points: number of distinct kinematic points at which the matrix element was determined
- 8 discrete Eππ* values: number of different ππ center-of-mass energies sampled
- Q² range: -0.4 ≤ (Q/GeV)² ≤ 1, covering both spacelike and timelike kinematics
- ρ pole position: Eππ* = [2.1805(32) - i·0.0151(5)] mπ
- ρ width at mπ ≈ 400 MeV: 12.4(6) MeV
- Δt = 32 at: Euclidean time separation used for correlation function extraction

## Context
This work extends the Lellouch-Lüscher formalism (Lellouch and Lüscher, 2000) for K → ππ decays to the case of radiative transitions of resonances, building on the theoretical framework developed by Briceno et al. (2015, 2014). Previous lattice calculations of the ρ → πγ* transition (Shultz et al., 2015; Owen et al., 2015) treated the ρ as a stable hadron incapable of decay to ππ, an uncontrolled approximation that this work removes. The results enable future calculations of more complex processes including γ*N → N* → Nπ in the baryon sector, B → Kπ ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ decays, and transitions involving resonances that decay to multiple hadronic channels such as Kγ* → K* → Kπ/Kη.
