# COVARIANT SINGLE-TIME BOUND-STATE EQUATION

> We derive a system of covariant single-time equations for a two-body bound state in a model of scalar fields $ϕ_1$ and $ϕ_2$ interacting via exchange of another scalar field $χ$. The derivation of the system of equations follows from the Haag expansion. The equations are linear integral equations that are explicitly symmetric in the masses, $m_1$ and $m_2$, of the scalar fields, $ϕ_1$ and $ϕ_2$. We present an approximate analytic formula for the mass eigenvalue of the ground state and give numerical results for the amplitudes for a choice of constituent and exchanged particle masses.

# Covariant Single-Time Bound-State Equation

## Key claim

The authors derive a system of covariant single-time equations for a two-body bound state that is explicitly symmetric in the masses of the two constituents, extending prior work by Raychaudhuri that was only valid for equal masses. The equations use retarded boundary conditions rather than Feynman boundary conditions, which the authors argue avoids spurious unphysical solutions including those of negative norm that appear in the Bethe-Salpeter method.

## Method

The derivation uses the Haag expansion, expanding Lagrangian fields in terms of in-fields including those for stable bound states, and truncates to one-loop approximation to obtain coupled linear integral equations for two bound-state amplitudes: one with particle 1 off-shell and particle 2 on-shell (f1), and the other with roles interchanged (f2). The equations are solved numerically by approximating the integrals with finite sums using Gauss integration with 18 mesh points, smoothing logarithmic singularities by keeping a finite epsilon. The model consists of two scalar fields φ1 and φ2 interacting via exchange of a third scalar field χ with coupling g.

## Key numbers

- The numerical solution requires 18 mesh points to obtain g² to an accuracy of 4%.
- The empirical mass eigenvalue formula is M = (m1 + m2) cos((λ - a)/b), where λ = g²/(32π m1 m2), a = 0.9 √(μ/m_red), and b = 0.8 - 1.1 ln(m_</m_>), with m_< the smaller mass and m_> the larger mass.
- The range of validity of the empirical formula is 0 ≤ μ ≤ m_<, 0.01 ≤ m_</m_> ≤ 1, 0.5 ≤ η ≤ 1 for m_</m_> = 1, and 0.9 ≤ η ≤ 1 for m_</m_> = 0.1, where η = M/(m1 + m2).
- For m_</m_> = 0.1 and μ = 0, the coupling parameter λ is plotted as a function of η, with the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Zlatev et al., 1969) giving consistently smaller binding.

## Context

This work extends the Haag expansion method for bound states previously applied by Raychaudhuri (1990s) to the equal-mass case, now treating unequal-mass constituents symmetrically. The approach is presented as an alternative to the Bethe-Salpeter method, which the authors note has difficulties including spurious unphysical solutions and dependence on an unphysical relative-time coordinate. The equations most similar to this work are the "spectator equations" of Gross (1980s), but the present derivation differs in using the Haag expansion and operator field equations rather than summing Feynman graphs, and in using retarded rather than Feynman boundary conditions. The authors plan to apply this method to bound states of spin-1/2 particles such as hydrogen and positronium for comparison with experiment, and hope to eventually extend the method to confining theories such as QCD.
