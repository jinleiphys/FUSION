# Quasielastic and inelastic neutrino reactions in $^{12}C$ at K2K energies

> In this paper, we present the results of a study made for the effect of nuclear medium in the charged current induced quasielastic lepton production(CCQE) and the incoherent and coherent one pion production (CC1$π^+$)processes from $^{12}C$ in the $ν_μ$ energy region of 0.4-3GeV. The theoretical results are compared with the recent experimental results for the ratio of charged current $ν_μ$ induced one pion production cross section to the quasielastic lepton production cross section reported by K2K collaboration. We also present the results for the angular and momentum distributions of leptons and pions produced in these processes.

## Key claim
Nuclear medium effects (RPA correlations, modified Δ properties, and pion final-state interactions) reduce the ratio of charged-current single-pion production to quasielastic cross sections on carbon by about 30% near 1 GeV neutrino energy, bringing the theoretical ratio into better agreement with K2K data than calculations without such medium modifications.

## Method
The authors compute charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections on ¹²C using a local Fermi gas model with Lindhard functions, including RPA correlations via a nucleon-nucleon potential with pion and rho exchanges. For incoherent and coherent single-pion production (CC1π⁺), they use a Δ-resonance dominance model in local density approximation, with in-medium modifications to the Δ mass and width (Pauli blocking, two- and three-body absorption channels), and treat pion final-state interactions via a Monte Carlo code (incoherent) or Eikonal approximation (coherent). All cross sections are averaged over the K2K neutrino flux in the 0.4–3 GeV energy range.

## Key numbers
- Reduction in CCQE total cross section due to RPA correlations: 12–15% in the energy region 0.4–3 GeV.
- Contribution of coherent pion production to total pion production: 5–6% in the energy region 0.4–3 GeV.
- Reduction in the ratio R(E) = σ(CC1π⁺)/σ(CCQE) due to nuclear medium effects: about 30% near 1 GeV, becoming 20–25% at 2–3 GeV.
- Variation in R(E) from uncertainties in vector form factors and axial dipole mass (M_A = 1.05–1.21 GeV): about 4–6%.
- Reduction in Q² distribution peak for CCQE with RPA effects: about 30%.
- Reduction in muon momentum distribution peak for CCQE with RPA effects: about 12%.
- Reduction in Q² distribution peak for incoherent pion production from nuclear medium effects: about 30%; further reduction of about 14% from pion absorption.
- Reduction in pion momentum distribution peak from nuclear medium effects: about 40%; further reduction of about 15% from pion absorption.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear medium effects in neutrino-nucleus reactions, such as the Smith-Moniz model (1972) for quasielastic scattering and the Rein-Sehgal model (1981) for pion production, which omitted in-medium modifications of Δ properties and RPA correlations. It directly compares with K2K collaboration data (Rodriguez, 2007) and earlier ANL measurements (Radecky, 1982; Barish, 1979), showing that including medium effects improves agreement. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for oscillation experiments like T2K and NOνA, and provide distributions (Q², lepton momentum, pion angular/momentum) useful for Monte Carlo event generators.
