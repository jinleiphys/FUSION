# Axial anomaly and the delta_{LT} puzzle

> The axial anomaly contribution to generalized longitudinal-transverse polarizability $δ_{LT}^{}$ is calculated within Regge approach. It is shown that the contribution from the exchange of the $a_1^{}(1260)$ Regge trajectory is nontrivial and might have the key role to explain the large difference between the predictions of chiral perturbation theory and the experimental data for the neutron $δ_{LT}^{}$. We also present the prediction for the proton $δ_{LT}^{}$, which will be measured at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility in near future.

## Key claim

The axial anomaly contribution to the generalized longitudinal-transverse polarizability δLT, arising from t-channel exchange of the a1(1260) Regge trajectory, is large and negative for the neutron and might resolve the discrepancy between chiral perturbation theory predictions and experimental data, though the authors note that at Q2 = 0.26 GeV2 the combined result still overestimates the data.

## Method

The authors calculate the axial anomaly contribution to δLT by evaluating the a1(1260) Regge trajectory exchange contribution to the spin-dependent forward Compton scattering amplitude for γ*N → γ*N. They use the Reggeized a1 propagator with intercept αA(0) ≈ -0.36 and slope α'A ≈ 0.9 GeV-2, and incorporate the triangle diagram form factor F(Q2,mq2) that vanishes in the real photon limit. The coupling constant ga1pp = 7.15 ± 1.92 is obtained from axial-vector dominance, and the constituent quark mass mq = 0.27 GeV is used in the form factor.

## Key numbers

- a1(1260) Regge trajectory intercept: αA(0) ≈ -0.36, with slope α'A ≈ 0.9 GeV-2, estimated from a1 meson mass MA = 1.23 GeV
- a1-nucleon coupling constant: ga1pp = 7.15 ± 1.92, derived from axial-vector dominance using gA/gV = 1.2694 ± 0.0028 and fa1 = (0.19 ± 0.03) GeV2
- Constituent quark mass used in triangle diagram: mq = 0.27 GeV
- The a1-exchange contribution to δLTn is large and negative, bringing the χPT prediction of Kochelev and Oh (2002) closer to the Jefferson Lab E94010 experimental data at low Q2, though the combined result still overestimates the data at Q2 = 0.26 GeV2
- The a1-exchange contribution to δLTp has the opposite sign (positive) compared to the neutron case, due to the isovector nature of the a1 meson

## Context

This work addresses the "δLT puzzle", the large discrepancy between chiral perturbation theory predictions (Kochelev and Oh 2002; Bernard et al. 2003) and the Jefferson Lab E94010 experimental data for the neutron generalized longitudinal-transverse polarizability at low Q2. The authors extend the Regge approach to include axial anomaly contributions through a1 exchange, which had not been considered in previous χPT calculations. The prediction for the proton δLTp provides a testable hypothesis for the planned experiment at TJNAF (Slifer 2009), and the approach could be refined by considering running quark masses or non-local a1-quark vertices at higher Q2.
