# First implementation of transverse spherocity analysis for heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies

> Transverse spherocity, an event shape observable, has a very unique capability to separate the events based on their geometrical shape, i.e. jetty and isotropic. In this work, we use transverse spherocity for the first time in heavy-ion collisions using A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT). We obtain the transverse momentum spectra, integrated yield, mean transverse momentum and azimuthal anisotropy for identified particles in Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.44$ TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV. The indication of collectivity in heavy-ion collisions can be clearly seen while comparing the transverse momentum spectra from jetty and isotropic events. The elliptic flow as a function of transverse spherocity shows that the isotropic events have nearly zero elliptic flow and the elliptic flow is mostly dominated by the jetty events. This study will pave a way to focus on jetty events in heavy-ion collisions in order to investigate jet medium modification and jet hadro-chemistry in a sophisticated manner.

# First implementation of transverse spherocity analysis for heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies

## Key claim

Transverse spherocity can successfully separate heavy-ion collision events into jetty and isotropic topological classes in AMPT simulations, with isotropic events showing nearly zero elliptic flow and jetty events dominating the measured elliptic flow; this separation is demonstrated for the first time in heavy-ion systems (Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb) rather than only in small collision systems like pp or p-Pb.

## Method

The authors used the String Melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT-SM, version 2.26t7) to generate Xe-Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. Transverse spherocity was calculated for each event in the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 0.8 with a minimum of 5 charged particles having pT > 0.15 GeV/c, and events were classified as jetty (lowest 20% of spherocity distribution) or isotropic (highest 20% of spherocity distribution). Transverse momentum spectra, integrated yields, mean transverse momentum, and elliptic flow (v2) were computed for identified pions, kaons, and protons across different centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Xe-Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV were simulated
- Spherocity cuts applied: lowest 20% of distribution for jetty events, highest 20% for isotropic events
- For Pb-Pb 20-30% centrality: jetty cut at spherocity = 0.75955, isotropic cut at 0.88355
- For Xe-Xe 20-30% centrality: jetty cut at spherocity = 0.77385, isotropic cut at 0.90365
- For Pb-Pb 70-100% centrality: jetty cut at spherocity = 0.53425, isotropic cut at 0.82325
- For Xe-Xe 70-100% centrality: jetty cut at spherocity = 0.51535, isotropic cut at 0.81455
- A mass-dependent "crossing point" was observed in pT spectra where jetty and isotropic contributions cross, shifting to higher pT with increasing particle mass (pion < kaon < proton)
- Elliptic flow for isotropic events was found to be nearly zero across all centralities
- Pb-Pb collisions showed higher elliptic flow than Xe-Xe collisions at the same (50-60)% centrality class

## Context

This work extends the transverse spherocity methodology previously applied to small collision systems (pp and p-Pb) by Cuautle et al. (2014, 2015) and Tripathy et al. (2019) to heavy-ion collisions where QGP formation is established. The results provide a baseline for future experimental studies at the LHC, enabling investigation of jet medium modification and jet hadro-chemistry by isolating jetty events in heavy-ion collisions. The observation that isotropic events have nearly zero elliptic flow while jetty events dominate v2 suggests that event shape selection can help disentangle collective flow from hard-process contributions to azimuthal anisotropy.
