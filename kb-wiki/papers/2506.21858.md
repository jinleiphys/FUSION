# Nature of the $P_c$ states from compositeness criteria

> Based on a coupled-channel approach, we investigate the structures of four $P_c$ states through compositeness criteria. Toward a more precise description of the states, we have obtained refined fit results of the LHCb data on the $J/ψp$ invariant mass distribution of the $Λ_b^0\to J/ψp K^-$ decay. Allowing for the fact that each of the four $P_c$ states couples strongly to a nearby $S$-wave channel, three criteria on the compositeness/elementariness are adopted in this study: the pole-counting rule, the spectral density function, and the Gamow wave function. Compositeness information is extracted from the scattering amplitudes and the pole parameters (pole positions and residues), without any preconceived assumptions on the nature of the $P_c$ states, and without any dependence on the model parametrization. Consistently within the framework of all the three methods, it has been found that the $P_c(4312)\,1/2^-$ is mainly composed by $\bar{D}Σ_c$, $P_c(4380)\,3/2^-$ by $\bar{D}Σ_c^*$, while the $P_c(4440)\,1/2^-$ and $P_c(4457)\,3/2^-$ states both turn out as composite states of $\bar{D}^*Σ_c$. The upper limits of the values of their elementariness are estimated to be rather small. This paper provides an additional confirmation of the molecular interpretation for the $P_c$ states in the literature.

## Key claim

The four \(P_c\) states are consistently found to be predominantly hadronic molecules (composite states) of nearby \(S\)-wave meson-baryon channels, with very small upper limits on their elementariness, based on three model-independent compositeness criteria applied to refined fits of LHCb data.

## Method

The authors performed refined fits of the Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel model to LHCb data on the \(J/\psi p\) invariant mass distribution of \(\Lambda_b^0\to J/\psi p K^-\) decay, extracting pole positions and residues for the four \(P_c\) states. They then applied three compositeness criteria, the pole-counting rule, the spectral density function method, and the Gamow wave function approach, using only the pole parameters as inputs, without any dependence on the model parametrization or preconceived assumptions about the states' nature.

## Key numbers

- \(P_c(4312)\,1/2^-\) pole positions: \(4313.4-2.2i\) MeV (Fit A), \(4314.0-1.1i\) MeV (Fit B), \(4312.6-2.9i\) MeV (Fit C).
- \(P_c(4380)\,3/2^-\) pole positions: \(4378.2-4.5i\) MeV (Fit A), \(4378.8-3.8i\) MeV (Fit B), \(4378.1-4.3i\) MeV (Fit C).
- \(P_c(4440)\,1/2^-\) pole positions: \(4435.7-9.2i\) MeV (Fit A), \(4440.3-4.7i\) MeV (Fit B), \(4438.3-9.9i\) MeV (Fit C).
- \(P_c(4457)\,3/2^-\) pole positions: \(4463.6-12i\) MeV (Fit A), \(4465.4-11i\) MeV (Fit B), \(4466.3-9.0i\) MeV (Fit C).
- The refined fits used 175 data points and 76 free parameters.
- The \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) threshold is at 4462 MeV; \(P_c(4457)\) lies slightly above this threshold in all three fits.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Shen 2024) using the Jülich-Bonn model, which had three fit solutions with unstable residues, by performing refined fits that yield more precise pole parameters. It provides a systematic, model-independent confirmation of the molecular interpretation of the \(P_c\) states, complementing earlier studies that applied the pole-counting rule (Kuang 2020, Santos 2024) or explored complex compositeness (Meißner 2015, Guo 2019). The results enable future investigations into the nature of other exotic hadrons using the same compositeness criteria.
