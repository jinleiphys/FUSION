# Prospective study on microscopic potential with Gogny interaction

> We present our current studies and our future plans on microscopic potential based on effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and many-body theory. This framework treats in an unified way nuclear structure and reaction. It offers the opportunity to link the underlying effective interaction to nucleon scattering observables. The more consistently connected to a variety of reaction and structure experimental data the framework will be, the more constrained effective interaction will be. As a proof of concept, we present some recent results for both neutron and proton scattered from spherical target nucleus, namely 40 Ca, using the Gogny D1S interaction. Possible fruitful crosstalks between microscopic potential, phenomenological potential and effective interaction are exposed. We then draw some prospective plans for the forthcoming years including scattering from spherical nuclei experiencing pairing correlations, scattering from axially deformed nuclei, and new effective interaction with reaction constraints.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic optical potential built from the Gogny D1S effective interaction, using the nuclear structure method (NSM) that treats structure and reactions in a unified framework, can reproduce elastic scattering cross sections for neutrons and protons on $^{40}$Ca up to about 30 MeV incident energy, but with the caveat that beyond 30 MeV backward-angle cross sections are overestimated and that a phenomenological width must be assigned to RPA states to simulate missing escape, damping, and compound-elastic widths.

## Method

The authors construct a microscopic optical potential for spherical targets using the nuclear structure method (NSM), which combines a Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field potential with a polarization potential built from target nucleus excitations described within the random-phase approximation (RPA). Both the HF and RPA calculations use the Gogny D1S effective interaction consistently, and the resulting nonlocal, complex, energy-dependent potential is used to solve the integro-differential Schrodinger equation without localization procedures, including both discrete and continuum spectra of intermediate single-particle states in the HF field.

## Key numbers

* HF single-particle resonance energies for neutron scattering from $^{40}$Ca: 12.18 MeV (j=7/2, l=4), 3.15 MeV (j=9/2, l=4), 14.87 MeV (j=11/2, l=5), 31.09 MeV (j=13/2, l=6)
* HF single-particle resonance energies for proton scattering from $^{40}$Ca: 3.70 MeV (j=1/2, l=1), 2.15 MeV (j=3/2, l=1), 5.65 MeV (j=5/2, l=3), 20.69 MeV (j=7/2, l=4), 9.55 MeV (j=9/2, l=4), 22.08 MeV (j=11/2, l=5), 39.78 MeV (j=13/2, l=6)
* Phenomenological widths assigned to RPA states: 2 MeV at 20 MeV excitation energy, 5 MeV at 50 MeV, 15 MeV at 100 MeV, and 50 MeV at 200 MeV
* The D1M parametrization of Gogny interaction reaches a root-mean-square deviation of 798 keV with respect to 2149 measured nuclear masses

## Context

This work extends the nuclear structure method for scattering previously applied with Skyrme interactions (Mizuyama et al., 2012) and with Gogny interaction (Blanchon et al., 2015) by emphasizing the exact treatment of intermediate single-particle resonances and the consistent use of the same effective interaction for mean-field, excited states, and couplings. The framework enables future applications to spherical nuclei with pairing correlations, axially deformed nuclei, and the development of new effective interactions constrained by reaction data, complementing phenomenological approaches such as the Koning-Delaroche potential and semi-microscopic methods based on the Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux model.
