# Generalized Density Matrix Revisited: Microscopic Approach to Collective Dynamics in Soft Spherical Nuclei

> The generalized density matrix (GDM) method is used to calculate microscopically the parameters of the collective Hamiltonian. Higher order anharmonicities are obtained consistently with the lowest order results, the mean field [Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation] and the harmonic potential [quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA)]. The method is applied to soft spherical nuclei, where the anharmonicities are essential for restoring the stability of the system, as the harmonic potential becomes small or negative. The approach is tested in three models of increasing complexity: the Lipkin model, model with factorizable forces, and the quadrupole plus pairing model.

## Key claim

The generalized density matrix (GDM) method provides a consistent microscopic derivation of the collective Hamiltonian parameters, including higher-order anharmonicities, but the cubic and quartic anharmonicities are not fully determined in the general case; only one relation constraining them emerges from the solvability condition, and the cubic and quartic potential terms can be fixed only near the critical point where the harmonic frequency squared approaches zero.

## Method

The GDM method maps the exact operator equations of motion for the density matrix operator \(R_{12}=a_2^\dagger a_1\) onto the collective subspace by taking matrix elements between collective states. The fermionic Hamiltonian is approximated within the collective band by a boson expansion in collective coordinate \(\alpha\) and momentum \(\pi\) up to quartic anharmonicities, and the density matrix operator is similarly expanded. Equating coefficients of the same phonon operator structure order by order yields equations for the generalized density matrix components, with the zero order giving the Hartree-Fock (HF) mean field, the first order giving the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA), and higher orders determining anharmonicities through solvability conditions.

## Context

This work extends the GDM formalism originally developed by Kerman and Klein (GDM_Klein) and further elaborated by Zelevinsky and collaborators (BZ1, BZ2, BZ3, Zele_ptps), which was previously applied to collective rotation and large-amplitude collective motion. The approach addresses the long-standing problem of deriving the geometric Bohr Hamiltonian (Bohr, Bohr_Hamil) and the interacting boson model (IBM) from microscopic principles, particularly for soft spherical nuclei where the QRPA harmonic approximation fails because the harmonic potential becomes small or negative. The method enables a systematic calculation of anharmonicities that restore stability near critical points, with applications to mode-mode coupling relevant to the search for mechanisms of many-body enhancement of the nuclear Schiff moment and atomic electric dipole moment (Mul_Mode).
