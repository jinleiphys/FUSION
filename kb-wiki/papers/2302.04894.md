# Two-loop hard thermal loops for any model

> Hard thermal loops describe how soft gauge fields are screened and damped in hot plasmas. As such they are used to calculate transport coefficients, Sphaleron rates, equations of state, and particle production. However, most calculations are done using one-loop self-energies. And two-loop contributions can be large. To that end this paper provides vector two-loop self-energies for generic models: Any scalar, fermion, or vector representation; and all possible renormalizable terms. Several examples are given to showcase the results. Two-loop results for higher-point functions are also given.

## Key claim

This paper provides two-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) self-energies for vector bosons in any generic renormalizable quantum field theory, including arbitrary scalar, fermion, and vector representations, using a kinetic theory approach rather than direct Feynman diagram evaluation; the results are valid for soft external fields (momenta of order \(eT\)) and hold only when scattering processes at timescales \(t \sim (e^4 T)^{-1}\) are not yet dominant.

## Method

The author uses the real-time formalism in the retarded/advanced (r/a) basis and employs kinetic (Vlasov) transport equations for hard quasiparticles with momenta \(p \sim T\) to derive two-loop corrections to the vector self-energy. Instead of evaluating two-loop Feynman diagrams directly, the method computes corrections to the fermion, scalar, ghost, and vector currents by expanding the quasiparticle distributions around equilibrium and integrating over hard loop momenta, using dimensional regularization in \(D = 4 - 2\epsilon\) dimensions. The calculation is performed in Feynman gauge, where several diagrams (scalar four-point, vector four-point, and ghost-current diagrams) vanish at two loops, and the results are expressed in terms of two universal Lorentz structures \(\Pi_1^{\mu\nu}\) and \(\Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\) that automatically satisfy transversality.

## Key numbers

* Pure vector contribution: \(\Pi^{\mu\nu,ab}_\text{V} = \frac{11 T^2}{36 \pi^2} \left( \log\frac{\mu e^\gamma}{4\pi T} - \frac{1}{22} \right) g_V^{adc} g_V^{cef} g_V^{dfn} g_V^{enb} \Pi_1^{\mu\nu} - \frac{T^2}{12 \pi^2} g_V^{adc} g_V^{cef} g_V^{dfn} g_V^{enb} \Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\)
* Scalar-vector contribution includes a term \(\frac{T^2}{192 \pi^2} \text{Tr}[g_S^a g_S^b]_{jl} \lambda^{jlnn} \Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\) and a term \(\frac{1}{8 \pi^2} \text{Tr}[g_S^a g_S^b]_{ij} \mu^{ij} \Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\)
* Fermion-vector contribution includes a term \(\frac{T^2}{16 \pi^2} \text{Tr}[g_F^c g_F^c g_F^a g_F^b] \Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\) and a term \(\frac{1}{8 \pi^2} \text{Tr}[g_F^a M_F M_F^\dagger g_F^b] \Pi_2^{\mu\nu}\)
* One-loop Debye mass contributions: scalar loop gives \(g_{\mu\nu}\Pi^{\mu\nu,ab}_S(K) = \text{Tr}[g_S^a g_S^b] \frac{K^2}{16\pi^2} \left( -\frac{2}{3} + k^0 L(K) \right)\); fermion loop gives \(g_{\mu\nu}\Pi^{\mu\nu,ab}_F(K) = \text{Tr}[g_F^a g_F^b] \frac{K^2}{16\pi^2} \left( \frac{4}{3} + 4 k^0 L(K) \right)\); vector loop gives \(g_{\mu\nu}\Pi^{\mu\nu,ab}_V(K) = \text{Tr}[g_V^a g_V^b] \frac{K^2}{16\pi^2} \left( \frac{4}{3} + 10 k^0 L(K) \right)\)

## Context

This work extends previous two-loop HTL calculations, which were limited to quantum electrodynamics (Carignano 2017, Carignano 2019, Gorda 2022), to arbitrary non-abelian gauge theories with scalars and fermions. The kinetic theory approach used here is a generalization of the leading-order HTL method developed by Blaizot and Iancu (1993, 1994) and Litim and Manuel (2001). The results enable next-to-leading-order calculations of transport coefficients, sphaleron rates, equations of state, and particle production in generic models, including the Standard Model, and are accompanied by the Mathematica file HTLGen.m for practical implementation.
