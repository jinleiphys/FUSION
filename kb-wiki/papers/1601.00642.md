# Calculating Masses of Pentaquarks Composed of Baryons and Mesons

> In this paper, we consider an exotic baryon (pentaquark) as a bound state of two-particle systems composed of a baryon (nucleon) and a meson.We used a baryon - meson picture to reduce a complicated five-body problem to a simple two-body problems. The homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation is solved in configuration space by using one pion exchange potential. We calculate the masses of pentaquarks $θ_{c}(uudd\bar{c})$, $θ_{b}(uudd\bar{b})$.

# Calculating Masses of Pentaquarks Composed of Baryons and Mesons

## Key claim
The authors claim that pentaquarks can be modeled as bound two-body systems of a baryon and a meson, and that solving the homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a one-pion exchange potential yields pentaquark masses in agreement with prior calculations. However, the calculation ignores the tensor term of the potential and considers only the central part of the Yukawa potential, with the authors stating that rho and omega meson exchange will be elaborated in subsequent work.

## Method
The authors reduce the five-body pentaquark problem to a two-body system by treating the pentaquark as a bound state of a nucleon (baryon) and a heavy meson (D or B meson). They solve the homogeneous Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation in configuration space using a one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) with a central Yukawa form, ignoring the tensor term. The integral equation is discretized using the Gauss-Legendre method with 200 grid points for radial coordinates and 100 points for angular integration, and the kernel is diagonalized to find the eigenvalue corresponding to the binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $\theta_{c}$ (uudd$\bar{c}$) with $V_0 = -276$ MeV and $r_0 = 1$ fm: 113.32 MeV
- Binding energy of $\theta_{c}$ with $V_0 = -62.79$ MeV and $r_0 = 1.5$ fm: 8.21 MeV
- Binding energy of $\theta_{b}$ (uudd$\bar{b}$) with $V_0 = -276$ MeV and $r_0 = 1$ fm: 140 MeV
- Binding energy of $\theta_{b}$ with $V_0 = -62.79$ MeV and $r_0 = 1.5$ fm: 15.1 MeV
- Mass of $\theta_{c}$ with parameter set A: 2665.92 MeV
- Mass of $\theta_{c}$ with parameter set B: 2790.82 MeV
- Mass of $\theta_{b}$ with parameter set A: 6104.6 MeV
- Mass of $\theta_{b}$ with parameter set B: 6209.71 MeV
- Potential parameters used: $g_A = 1.27$, $f_\pi = 131$ MeV, $g_H = -0.59$, $m_\pi = 138$ MeV, $m_{Ni} = 938.92$ MeV, $m_B = 5279$ MeV, $m_D = 1867$ MeV
- Two values of the constant potential $V_0$ for $r < r_0$: -62.79 MeV or -276 MeV

## Context
This work extends the baryon-meson molecular picture for pentaquarks previously studied by Lipkin (1990s) and Gignoux et al. (1990s), who predicted that anticharmed and antibeauty pentaquarks are the most bound. The authors compare their calculated masses to prior results from other groups (referenced as [25,26,27] in the paper), finding agreement with the predicted mass of $\theta_{c}$ at 2650 MeV and $\theta_{b}$ at 6391 MeV from those sources. The method is presented as applicable to tetraquark systems as well, building on the authors' previous work on diquark-antidiquark bound states using the same Lippmann-Schwinger approach. The calculation neglects tensor contributions and heavier meson exchanges (rho and omega), which the authors indicate will be addressed in future work.
