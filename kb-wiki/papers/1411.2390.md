# Universal description of radially excited heavy and light vector mesons

> A new qualitative stringlike picture for mesons is proposed which leads to a simple and intuitively clear generalization of linear radial Regge trajectories to the case of massive quarks. The obtained universal relation is successfully tested in the sector of unflavored vector mesons, where many radial excitations are known. Some new predictions are given. Our results suggest that the quark masses can be easily estimated from the spectra of radially excited mesons.

# Universal description of radially excited heavy and light vector mesons

## Key claim
The authors propose that the mass spectrum of radially excited unflavored vector mesons (ω, φ, ψ, Υ) follows a universal linear relation (M_n - 2m)^2 = 2πσ(n + b) where only the quark mass m varies, provided one treats the quarks as static rather than as free on-shell particles. The claim is hedged by the caveat that this relation is derived in the large-N_c limit of QCD and is tested only within the accuracy of that approximation, not as an exact description of the real N_c=3 world.

## Method
The authors derive a mass formula by modeling a meson as two static quarks at fixed relative distance bound by exchange of a massless mediator particle, with a linearly confining string potential. They apply Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization to the exchanged particle's momentum, obtaining (M_n - 2m)^2 = 2πσ(n + b). They then perform global fits of this relation to the known S-wave radial spectra of ω, φ, ψ, and Υ mesons from the Particle Data Group, treating the quark masses, string tension σ, and intercept b as free parameters in two different fitting scenarios (Fit A and Fit B).

## Key numbers
- Fit A yields slope a = 1.06(3) GeV^2 and intercept b = 0.63(7) in the relation (M_n - 2m)^2 = a(n + b)
- Fit A quark masses: m_{u,d} = 0 (fixed), m_s = 0.12(8) GeV, m_c = 1.20(7) GeV, m_b = 4.32(6) GeV
- Fit B yields slope a = 0.67(7) GeV^2 and intercept b = 0.00(0)
- Fit B quark masses: m_{u,d} = 0.28(4) GeV, m_s = 0.40(3) GeV, m_c = 1.48(5) GeV, m_b = 4.59(5) GeV
- For ψ mesons, Fit A gives χ^2 = 6 × 10^{-3} and Fit B gives χ^2 = 12 × 10^{-3} across n = 0–4
- For Υ mesons, Fit A gives χ^2 = 0.4 × 10^{-3} and Fit B gives χ^2 = 3.5 × 10^{-3} across n = 0–5
- The linear radial Regge fit for ψ mesons alone gives M_n^2 = 3.26 (n + 3.03) GeV^2
- The linear radial Regge fit for Υ mesons alone gives M_n^2 = 6.90 (n + 13.28) GeV^2

## Context
This work extends the linear radial Regge trajectory phenomenology observed in light nonstrange mesons by the Crystal Barrel Collaboration (Anisovich et al., 2000) and Bugg (2004) to heavy quarkonia, proposing a universal stringlike description. It contrasts with standard semirelativistic potential models (Kang and Schnitzer, 1975; Isgur and Godfrey, 1985) and Bethe-Salpeter approaches that introduce strong nonlinearity for heavy quarks via the substitution p → √(p^2 + m^2). The authors' static-quark picture is motivated by the large degeneracy M^2 ∼ L + n observed in light mesons (Glozman et al., 2001; Klempt, 2002; Shifman and Vainshtein, 2005). The results enable predictions for unobserved radial excitations of φ, ψ, and Υ mesons, and suggest that quark masses can be estimated from meson spectra, with the caveat that the relation is limited to the first few excitations before string breaking sets in (estimated at k ≈ 4–9 observable states).
