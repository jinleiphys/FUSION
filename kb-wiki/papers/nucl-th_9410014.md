# Photon-Photon Luminosities in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC Energies

> Effective photon-photon luminosities are calculated for various realistic hadron collider scenarios. The main characteristics of photon-photon processes at relativistic heavy-ion colliders are established and compared to the corresponding photon-photon luminosities at electron-positron and future Photon Linear Colliders (PLC). Higher order corrections as well as inelastic processes are discussed. It is concluded that feasible high luminosity Ca-Ca collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are an interesting option for photon-photon physics up to about 100 GeV photon-photon CM energy.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that feasible high-luminosity Ca-Ca collisions at the LHC are an interesting option for photon-photon physics up to about 100 GeV photon-photon center-of-mass energy, but note that for very high invariant masses above approximately 200 GeV the proton-proton option becomes better due to the harder form factor of the proton.

## Method

The authors compute effective photon-photon luminosities using the semiclassical impact-parameter-dependent equivalent photon approximation, with a cutoff at impact parameter b = 2R to exclude central collisions where strong interactions occur. They compare unrestricted and restricted (in rapidity Y) luminosities for Ca-Ca and Pb-Pb beams at the LHC against those for proton-proton, electron-positron, and future Photon Linear Collider scenarios. They also evaluate higher-order corrections from nuclear excitation, particularly giant dipole resonance (GDR) excitation, and inelastic photon emission processes using a generalized double equivalent photon approximation.

## Key numbers

- Ca-Ca collisions at LHC can reach photon-photon center-of-mass energies up to about 100 GeV
- For Pb-Pb collisions at b=2R, the GDR excitation probability is about 40% per nucleus, giving a total probability of about 65% for excitation of at least one nucleus
- For Ca-Ca collisions at b=2R, the GDR excitation probability is about 1% per nucleus, giving a total probability of about 2% for excitation of at least one nucleus
- The GDR resonance energy is given by E_GDR = 80 MeV / A^(1/3)
- Elastic-inelastic processes contribute about 1% to the total luminosity for heavy-ion collisions
- For small invariant masses W, the luminosity scales as dL/dW = (Z^2 alpha/pi)^2 * (32/(3W)) * ln^3(2 gamma/(W R))

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Baron (1994) and Vidovic (1993) on photon-photon interactions in heavy-ion collisions, using beam luminosity parameters from a study by Eggert and Morsch (1994). The authors compare their results to photon-photon luminosities at electron-positron colliders (as described in the ECFA 1986 report and Budnev 1975) and to future Photon Linear Collider designs (Ginzburg 1994, Barden 1994). The paper enables experimental planning for photon-photon physics at the LHC, particularly for Ca-Ca collisions, and provides a framework for calculating higher-order nuclear excitation effects that must be accounted for in detector design and luminosity monitoring.
