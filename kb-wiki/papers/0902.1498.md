# Nonperturbative Yukawa theory at finite density and temperature

> In-medium Yukawa theory is part of the thermodynamics of the Standard Model of particle physics and is one of the main building blocks of most effective field theories of fermionic systems. By computing its pressure we investigate the nonperturbative thermodynamics at finite temperature and density using the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) framework. Our calculations are valid for arbitrary fermion and scalar masses, temperature, chemical potential, and not restricted to weak coupling. The model is considered in the presence as well as in the absence of condensates. Comparison with nonperturbative results shows that second order perturbation theory (PT) fails in the first case but performs rather well when condensates are absent, even at high-temperature regimes.

## Key claim

Second-order perturbation theory (PT) performs surprisingly well in the symmetric phase (no condensates) up to couplings of order one, even at high temperature and density, but fails badly when condensates are present because it cannot resum the direct tadpole contributions associated with symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic pressure of Yukawa theory at finite temperature and chemical potential using the optimized perturbation theory (OPT) framework, evaluating contributions up to two loops including both direct (Hartree-like) and exchange (Fock-like) terms. They compared OPT results with ordinary second-order perturbation theory (PT) and mean-field theory (MFT), considering the model both with and without scalar condensates, for arbitrary fermion and scalar masses, temperature, chemical potential, and coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and in the presence of condensates, PT predicts "very high values for the pressure as mu increases, in disagreement with MFT and OPT results" (no explicit numerical value given for the discrepancy)
- The coupling values considered in the numerical analysis cover the range 0 to pi
- The temperature and chemical potential ranges considered cover 0 to 10m, where m = 0.1 Lambda_MS (the renormalization scale)
- The scalar boson mass is set to m_phi = m/2 in the numerical routines (except for the special case m_phi = 0)
- For the cold and dense case with m_phi = 0, the in-medium contribution to the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature is given by: Omega_Y^med(T=0,mu) = -N_F * (1/(24 pi^2)) * [2 mu p_f^3 - 3 m^2 u] - N_F * (g^2/(64 pi^4)) * {3 u^2 - 4 p_f^4 + m^2 u [7 - 3 log(m^2/Lambda^2)]}, where p_f^2 = mu^2 - m^2 and u = mu p_f - m^2 log((mu + p_f)/m)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative two-loop results for cold and dense Yukawa theory previously obtained by some of the same authors (Palhares, 2008) to finite temperature and to nonperturbative couplings. The OPT framework had been successfully applied to other models such as the Gross-Neveu model (Kneur, 2007) and Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC). The results enable reliable nonperturbative calculations for effective field theories in nuclear and particle physics, particularly for the linear sigma model (Gell-Mann, 1960) and pion-nucleon models (Weinberg, 1978; van Kolck, 1999), where Yukawa couplings can be much larger than one.
