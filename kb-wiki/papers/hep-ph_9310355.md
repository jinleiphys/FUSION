# Anomalies, VMD and the Enjl Model

> We show how the effective action of the Extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (ENJL) can be defined in the presence of anomalies in a way that reproduces the flavour anomaly. This necessarily breaks Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) in the usual sense. The same method can be used to construct other chiral effective theories involving constituent quarks and spin-1 mesons that give the correct flavour anomaly. We also comment on how standard VMD is the root of this problem and those observed by previous authors in implementing VMD in the anomalous sector.

## Key claim

The Extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model can be defined in the presence of anomalies to reproduce the correct QCD flavour anomaly, but this necessarily breaks Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) in the usual sense; the violation of anomalous Ward identities reported by Ruiz-Arriola and Salcedo (1993) can be avoided by subtracting local polynomials that depend only on the left-handed and right-handed auxiliary fields and external fields.

## Method

The authors start from the ENJL generating functional with four-fermion interactions and introduce auxiliary fields (M, L_mu, R_mu) to remove the four-quark couplings. They then integrate out the fermions and show that the standard procedure defines the fermion measure using a Dirac operator that depends on shifted fields l'_mu = l_mu + L_mu and r'_mu = r_mu + R_mu, which is the root of the anomaly problem. The anomalous part of the left and right currents is separated into a chirally invariant piece and a local polynomial of O(p^3); the terms that spoil the anomalous Ward identities are identified as local functions of l, L, r, R and their derivatives, and these are subtracted consistently without affecting the chirally covariant part of the effective action.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous left current J^{L mu} contains a factor N_c/(48 pi^2) multiplying an epsilon tensor structure with terms involving the chiral connection L_nu and the field strength l_{nu alpha}.
- The local polynomial j^{L mu} in the anomalous current also carries the factor N_c/(48 pi^2) and involves terms like i l_nu l_alpha l_beta and {l_{nu alpha}, l_beta}.
- The four-fermion coupling constants are defined as g_0 = 8 pi^2 G_S/(N_c Lambda_chi^2) and g_1 = 8 pi^2 G_V/(N_c Lambda_chi^2), where N_c is the number of colours.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ruiz-Arriola and Salcedo (1993), who found that the ENJL model deviates from the anomalous Ward identity prediction for the gamma pi^0 pi^+ pi^- vertex while giving the correct result for pi^0 -> gamma gamma. The authors also relate their solution to the subtraction method used by Brihaye (1984) for constructing anomalous effective Lagrangians with full VMD. The prescription enables consistent construction of chiral effective theories involving constituent quarks and spin-1 mesons that give the correct flavour anomaly, and it clarifies that the value of the axial coupling g_A is not constrained by the chiral anomaly, contrary to the conclusion of Ruiz-Arriola and Salcedo (1993) and in agreement with Manohar and Moore (1988).
