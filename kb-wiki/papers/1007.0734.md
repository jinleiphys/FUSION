# On Parity-Violating Three-Nucleon Interactions and the Predictive Power of Few-Nucleon EFT at Very Low Energies

> We address the typical strengths of hadronic parity-violating three-nucleon interactions in "pion-less" Effective Field Theory in the nucleon-deuteron (iso-doublet) system. By analysing the superficial degree of divergence of loop diagrams, we conclude that no such interactions are needed at leading order. The only two linearly independent parity-violating three-nucleon structures with one derivative mix two-S and two-P-half waves with iso-spin transitions Delta I = 0 or 1. Due to their structure, they cannot absorb any divergence ostensibly appearing at next-to-leading order. This observation is based on the approximate realisation of Wigner's combined SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry in the two-nucleon system, even when effective-range corrections are included. Parity-violating three-nucleon interactions thus only appear beyond next-to-leading order. This guarantees renormalisability of the theory to that order without introducing new, unknown coupling constants and allows the direct extraction of parity-violating two-nucleon interactions from three-nucleon experiments.

## Key claim

No parity-violating three-nucleon interactions are needed at leading order (LO) or next-to-leading order (NLO) in pion-less Effective Field Theory for the nucleon-deuteron system, because the only two possible PV three-nucleon structures with one derivative cannot absorb any divergences that appear at those orders, based on the approximate realization of Wigner's SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry in the two-nucleon system even when effective-range corrections are included.

## Method

The authors analyzed the superficial degree of divergence of loop diagrams in pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT($\pi\!\!/$)) for the nucleon-deuteron (iso-doublet) system, constructing all parity-violating three-nucleon interactions with one derivative that mix the $^2S_{1/2}$ and $^2P_{1/2}$ waves with isospin transitions $\Delta I = 0$ or $1$. They examined the UV limit of the Faddeev equations for half-offshell amplitudes, using the Wigner-basis transformation to decouple the spin-doublet channels and studying the asymptotic behavior of the integral equations at large momenta.

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter for low momenta is typically $Q \approx 1/[3\dots5]$, with the breakdown scale set by the pion mass $m_\pi$ and typical low momenta like $\gamma \approx 45$ MeV
* The parity-violating strength relative to parity-conserving is $\epsilon \approx 10^{-6}$
* The asymptotic coefficient for the $^2S_{1/2}$ wave ($\lambda=1$) is $s_0(\lambda=1) = \pm 1.00624\dots i$, giving a logarithmic divergence that vanishes upon angular integration
* The asymptotic coefficient for the spin-quartet ($\lambda=-1/2$) $^2S_{1/2}$ wave is $s_0(\lambda=-1/2) = 2.16622\dots$
* The degree of divergence for LO one-loop S-to-P wave amplitudes is $\Delta(\lambda=1) = -1$ and $\Delta(\lambda=-1/2) = -3.16\dots$
* The effective range parameters are $\rho_{0s} = 2.73$ fm and $\rho_{0t} = 1.76$ fm, with $(c_{0t}-c_{0s})/(c_{0t}+c_{0s}) \approx -0.22 \sim Q$

## Context

This work extends the parity-violating sector of pion-less Effective Field Theory, first developed for two-nucleon systems by Savage (1998) and comprehensively described by Zhu (2004), to the three-nucleon system. It builds on the analysis of divergence structure in three-nucleon amplitudes from the authors' prior work (effrange1, effrange2) and the understanding that parity-conserving three-nucleon interactions are promoted to leading order due to anomalously large NN scattering lengths (Bedaque 1999, Hammer 2001). The result enables the direct extraction of parity-violating two-nucleon interactions from three-nucleon experiments without needing additional unknown coupling constants up to NLO, which is essential for interpreting experiments at slow-neutron facilities such as SNS (Oak Ridge), NIST (Gaithersburg), ILL (Grenoble), PSI (Villingen), and FRM-II (Munich).
