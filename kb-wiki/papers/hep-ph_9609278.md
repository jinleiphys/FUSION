# Baryon Structure and the Chiral Symmetry of QCD

> Beyond the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale light and strange baryons should be considered as systems of three constituent quarks with an effective confining interaction and a chiral interaction that is mediated by the octet of Goldstone bosons (pseudoscalar mesons) between the constituent quarks.

## Key claim

Light and strange baryons beyond the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking scale should be described as systems of three constituent quarks with an effective confining interaction and a chiral interaction mediated by the octet of Goldstone bosons (pseudoscalar mesons), rather than by perturbative gluon exchange, because the chiral interaction correctly predicts the ordering of positive and negative parity states in the baryon spectrum while the color-magnetic gluon exchange interaction cannot explain this ordering.

## Method

The author constructs a constituent quark model where the quark-quark interaction consists of a harmonic oscillator confining potential plus a chiral boson exchange interaction of the form \(H_\chi \sim -\sum_{i<j} V(\vec r_{ij}) \vec \lambda^{\rm F}_i \cdot \vec \lambda^{\rm F}_j \, \vec \sigma_i \cdot \vec \sigma_j\), representing pseudoscalar meson exchange. The matrix elements of this interaction are computed for different flavor-spin symmetry configurations of the three-quark states, and the resulting energy shifts are compared to the empirical baryon spectrum up to the \(N=2\) harmonic oscillator shell. The two free parameters (the chiral interaction strength \(C_\chi\) and the oscillator parameter \(\hbar\omega\)) are fixed from the N-\(\Delta\) splitting and the mass difference between ground states and first radial excitations.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate value: \( \langle {\rm vacuum} | \bar{q}q | {\rm vacuum} \rangle \approx -(240-250\ {\rm MeV})^3\) (order parameter for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking)
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \(\Lambda_{\chi {\rm SB}} \simeq 4\pi f_\pi \simeq 1\ {\rm GeV}\), corresponding to a distance of \(\frac{1}{\Lambda_{\chi {\rm SB}}} \simeq 0.2\ {\rm fm}\)
* The chiral interaction constant \(C_\chi\) is determined from the N-\(\Delta\) splitting to be 29.3 MeV
* The oscillator parameter \(\hbar\omega\) is determined from the mass difference between first excited \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) states and ground states (e.g., N(1440) - N) to be \(\hbar\omega \simeq 250\ {\rm MeV}\)
* In the N and \(\Sigma\) sectors, the predicted mass difference between the lowest excited \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) states and the \(\frac{1}{2}^- - \frac{3}{2}^-\) negative parity pairs is \(m(\frac{1}{2}^+) - m(\frac{1}{2}^- - \frac{3}{2}^-) = -102\ {\rm MeV}\) (negative, meaning positive parity lies below negative parity)
* In the \(\Lambda\) system, the corresponding mass difference is \(m(\frac{1}{2}^+) - m(\frac{1}{2}^- - \frac{3}{2}^-) = 74\ {\rm MeV}\) (positive, meaning negative parity lies below positive parity)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach by replacing the commonly used one-gluon exchange interaction (as in DeRujula-Georgi-Glashow (1975) and Isgur-Karl (1978)) with a chiral boson exchange interaction, motivated by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD. The author shows that the color-magnetic gluon exchange interaction cannot explain why the positive parity N(1440) and \(\Delta\)(1600) lie below the negative parity N(1520)-N(1535) and \(\Delta\)(1620)-\(\Delta\)(1700) states, nor why the \(\Lambda\) spectrum shows a different ordering with \(\Lambda\)(1405)-\(\Lambda\)(1520) as the lowest excited states. The chiral interaction framework builds on the effective Lagrangian ideas of Manohar and Georgi (1984) and the instanton liquid picture of Shuryak (1982) and Diakonov-Petrov (1986), and it enables systematic three-body calculations of the full baryon spectrum including both positive and negative parity states.
