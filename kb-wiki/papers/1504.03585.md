# The Vacuum Structure of Vector Mesons in QCD

> We study the chiral dynamics of vector mesons in two-flavor QCD in vacuum by utilizing a functional renormalization group approach. This allows us to capture the dynamical transition from the quark-gluon phase at high energies to the hadronic phase at low energies without the necessity of model parameter tuning. We use this to analyze the scaling of vector meson masses towards the chiral symmetry breaking scale, the decoupling of the mesons at high energies and the validity of vector meson dominance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the vacuum properties of vector mesons (rho and a1) in two-flavor QCD are uniquely determined by the underlying quark-gluon dynamics without model parameter tuning, but only within a truncation that neglects irrelevant operators and focuses on qualitative behavior rather than quantitative precision.

## Method

The author uses a functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to QCD, extended by dynamical hadronization to smoothly transition from the quark-gluon phase at high energies to the hadronic phase at low energies. The scale-dependent effective action includes scalar-pseudoscalar and vector-axialvector four-quark interactions, Yukawa couplings, and meson self-interactions with running wave function renormalizations, while the pi-a1 mixing is eliminated by a field redefinition that introduces additional scale dependence into the a1 field.

## Key numbers

- The critical scale k_chi separates the phases with broken and restored chiral symmetry, and the running of hadronic parameters is governed by an infrared-attractive fixed point as long as the gauge coupling is small.
- The rho meson mass is given by m_rho,k^2 = m_V,k^2 + g_3,k sigma_0,k^2, and the a1 meson mass by m_a1,k^2 = m_V,k^2 + (g_2,k + g_3,k) sigma_0,k^2, where sigma_0,k is the chiral order parameter.
- The mass-splitting between rho and a1 is determined by the coupling g_2,k, while the mass-splitting between pion and sigma is determined by the quartic scalar meson coupling lambda_4,k.
- The vector meson dominance (VMD) relations g_1,k^2 = g_2,k = g_4,k^2 = g_5,k and g_3,k = 0 are shown to be an oversimplification of the dynamics, though VMD is a good approximation at low energies.

## Context

This work extends the FRG approach to QCD developed by Braun (2014) by incorporating vector mesons (rho and a1) as chiral partners, building on earlier studies of four-quark interactions by Mitter (2014) in the quenched limit. The analysis enables future in-medium studies of vector meson modifications at finite temperature and density, which are relevant for interpreting dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions and understanding chiral symmetry restoration. The paper contradicts the assumption of vector meson dominance as a fundamental principle, showing instead that it emerges only as a low-energy approximation.
