# Thermal and transport properties in central heavy-ion reactions around a few hundred MeV/nucleon

> Thermalization process of nuclear matter in central fireball region of heavy-ion collisions is investigated by employing an extension model of Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck, namely the Van der Waals Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VdWBUU) model. Temperature ($T$) is extracted by the quantum Fermion fluctuation approach and other thermodynamic quantities, such as density ($ρ$), entropy density ($s$), shear viscosity ($η$), isospin diffusivity ($D_{I}$) and heat conductivity ($κ$), are also deduced. The liquid-like and gas-like phase signs are discussed through the behavior of shear viscosity during heavy-ion collisions process with the VdWBUU model.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity as a function of temperature exhibits a liquid-like phase (viscosity decreases with increasing temperature) during the compression stage and a gas-like phase (viscosity decreases with decreasing temperature) during the expansion stage of central Au+Au collisions at 100-300 MeV/nucleon, with an inflection point suggesting a mixed phase. The ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density at the maximum temperature approaches an asymptotic value of approximately 6 times the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of ℏ/(4π), but only for the soft equation of state and the specific central region studied.

## Method
Central collisions (b=0 fm) of 197Au+197Au at beam energies of 100-300 MeV/nucleon were simulated using the Van der Waals Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VdWBUU) model, which incorporates both isospin-dependent mean-field potentials and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections correlated via a Van der Waals equation of state. Temperature was extracted from the momentum distribution of nucleons using the quantum Fermion fluctuation method based on the variance of the quadrupole moment Qxy = px² - py². Transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, isospin diffusivity DI, and heat conductivity κ) were calculated from density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry using parameterized formulas from Li and Shen (2003), then scaled by the ratio of the in-medium to free nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Key numbers
- Average density in the central region reaches 1.2-1.6 times normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³) at maximum compression.
- Maximum temperature extracted ranges from 13 to 48 MeV across beam energies of 100-300 MeV/nucleon.
- Average entropy density reaches maximum values of 0.1-0.7 fm⁻³.
- The ratio η/s at the maximum temperature for different beam energies tends to an asymptotic value of approximately 6 times the KSS bound (ℏ/(4π)).
- Shear viscosity, isospin diffusivity, and heat conductivity are scaled by the ratio σ/σ_free, where σ_free = 40 mb is the free nucleon-nucleon cross section.
- Isospin asymmetry reaches a minimum around 60 fm/c after the start of the collision.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear matter transport properties from relativistic energies (where quark-gluon plasma studies focus on the KSS bound) down to intermediate energies of a few hundred MeV/nucleon, where liquid-gas phase transition signatures are expected. The VdWBUU model builds on earlier BUU approaches by Veselsky and Ma (2013) and incorporates a Van der Waals equation of state to correlate mean-field and collision terms. The temperature extraction using the quantum fluctuation method follows the approach of He and Zhou (2011), and the transport coefficient formulas are taken from Li and Shen (2003). The results are compared with Zhou et al. (2013), who used the Thomas-Fermi method with the IQMD model for similar systems. The liquid-like and gas-like phase signatures via shear viscosity behavior are analogous to classical fluids as described by Enskog (1934) and Chapman and Cowling (1946). This work enables future studies of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition using transport coefficients as observables, and provides a framework for comparing transport properties across different beam energies and equations of state.
