# Bayesian Analyses of Proton Multiple Flow Components in Intermediate Heavy Ion Collisions with Momentum-Dependent Interactions

> We perform a comprehensive Bayesian analyses of Au + Au collision data at 1.23 GeV/nucleon using an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model that incorporates a momentum-dependent mean field and medium-modified baryon-baryon cross sections. The model parameters are calibrated to empirical properties of nuclear matter at saturation density, with particular attention to variations in the incompressibility $K_0$. Within a Bayesian statistical framework and using a Gaussian Process emulator, we simultaneously extract constraints on the incompressibility $K_0$ and the in-medium baryon-baryon scattering modification factor $X$ by systematically comparing model predictions with HADES measurements of proton collective flow, including the slopes ($F_1$ and $F_3$) of directed and triangular flow, as well as elliptic ($v_2$) and quadrupole ($v_4$) flow observables. We find that the extracted incompressibility favors relatively small values, indicating a soft nuclear equation of state, while the inferred average $X$ values fall at $0.9$-$1.0$, suggesting mild suppression of baryon-baryon cross sections in the medium. Furthermore, we demonstrate that transport models employing momentum-independent mean fields require stiffer equations of state and stronger in-medium corrections to reproduce the same observables. These results highlight the critical role of momentum dependence in the mean field and its interplay with in-medium scattering in constraining the properties of dense nuclear matter from heavy-ion collisions.

# Bayesian Analyses of Proton Multiple Flow Components in Intermediate Heavy Ion Collisions with Momentum-Dependent Interactions

## Key claim
The extracted incompressibility $K_0$ favors relatively small values (indicating a soft nuclear equation of state) and the inferred average in-medium baryon-baryon scattering modification factor $X$ falls at 0.9–1.0, but only when a momentum-dependent mean field is used; transport models employing momentum-independent mean fields require stiffer equations of state ($K_0 > 240$ MeV) and stronger in-medium corrections ($X \sim 1.3$–$1.6$) to reproduce the same HADES flow data.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian analyses of Au + Au collision data at 1.23 GeV/nucleon using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean field (ImMDI) and medium-modified baryon-baryon cross sections. They employed a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 240 parameter sets (varying $K_0$ from 100 to 380 MeV and $X$ from 0.5 to 2.0) and used Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to simultaneously extract constraints on $K_0$ and $X$ by comparing model predictions with HADES measurements of proton collective flow observables $F_1$, $v_2$, $F_3$, and $v_4$ at two centralities (10–20% and 20–30%).

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian inference studies in heavy-ion collisions (Li et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023) by simultaneously constraining the nuclear incompressibility $K_0$ and the in-medium cross-section modification factor $X$ using multiple flow observables, rather than varying one parameter at a time. The results highlight the critical interplay between momentum dependence in the mean field and in-medium scattering, showing that momentum-independent mean-field models (such as those used in earlier transport studies by Danielewicz et al., 2002) require systematically stiffer equations of state and stronger medium corrections. This enables more reliable extraction of dense nuclear matter properties from heavy-ion collision data and provides constraints relevant for neutron star physics.
