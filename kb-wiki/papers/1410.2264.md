# Non extensive thermodynamics and neutron star properties

> In the present work we apply non extensive statistics to obtain equations of state suitable to describe stellar matter and verify its effects on microscopic and macroscopic quantities. Two snapshots of the star evolution are considered and the direct Urca process is investigated with two different parameter sets. $q$-values are chosen as 1.05 and 1.14. The equations of state are only slightly modified, but the effects are enough to produce stars with slightly higher maximum masses. The onsets of the constituents are more strongly affected and the internal stellar temperature decreases with the increase of the $q$-value, with consequences on the strangeness and cooling rates of the stars.

## Key claim

Applying non-extensive (Tsallis) statistics with q-values of 1.05 and 1.14 to neutron star equations of state produces only slightly modified EOS, but these modifications are sufficient to yield stars with slightly higher maximum masses; the internal stellar temperature decreases with increasing q-value, contrary to results reported in prior work by Lavagno and Pigato.

## Method

The authors used the non-linear Walecka model with two parameter sets (GM1 and IU-FSU) to describe hadronic matter, replacing the standard Fermi-Dirac distributions with q-deformed distribution functions derived from non-extensive statistics. They solved the mean-field equations for stellar matter under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, considering two snapshots of protoneutron star evolution: one with fixed entropy per baryon S/nB=1 and lepton fraction Yl=0.3, and another with S/nB=2 and zero neutrino chemical potential. Calculations were performed for matter containing only nucleons and for matter including the eight lightest baryons (n, p, Lambda, Sigma-, Sigma0, Sigma+, Xi-, Xi0).

## Key numbers

- q-values chosen: 1.05 and 1.14 (with q=1.14 taken from prior analysis of pT-distributions in high-energy physics by Cleymans and Worku)
- For the case S/nB=2 at nB/n0 approximately 3, the strangeness content decreases by 2.5% when q goes from 1 to 1.05, by 4.7% when q goes from 1.05 to 1.14, and by 7.1% from q=1 to q=1.14
- At densities of about 5 times nuclear saturation density, the temperature decreases by approximately 25% in average with increasing q
- The IU-FSU parameter set has saturation density n0=0.155 fm^-3, while GM1 has n0=0.153 fm^-3
- The IU-FSU symmetry energy slope L=47.2 MeV, compared to GM1 with L=94 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics (NESCT) from high-energy physics, where it was developed by Deppman and collaborators, to neutron star physics. It contrasts with the approach of Lavagno and Pigato, who used a different partition function and found that temperature increases with q, whereas the present authors find the opposite behavior. The results enable further investigation of how non-extensive statistics affects neutrino diffusion during the Kelvin-Helmholtz epoch and the cooling rates of protoneutron stars, particularly in light of the confirmed existence of 2-solar-mass neutron stars reported by Demorest and Antoniadis.
