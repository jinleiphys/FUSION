# Quantum Dynamical Microscopic Approach to Stellar Carbon Burning

> The process of carbon burning is vital to understanding late stage stellar evolution of massive stars and the conditions of certain supernovae. Carbon burning is a complex problem, involving quantum tunnelling and nuclear molecular states. Quantum dynamical calculations of carbon burning are presented, combining the time-dependent wave-packet method and the density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) approach. By limiting the contribution of triaxial molecular configurations to fusion, we demonstrate that the DC-TDHF interaction potential successfully explains the appearance of some resonant structures in the sub-barrier fusion cross-section. This result shows the critical role of nucleon-nucleon interactions in the 12C + 12C fusion resonances observed at astrophysical energies.

## Key claim

By limiting the contribution of triaxial molecular configurations to fusion through a shifted absorptive potential, the DC-TDHF interaction potential can explain some resonant structures in the sub-barrier fusion cross-section, but the model underpredicts the experimentally observed S*-factor at low energies due to the relatively small oblate deformation of 12C produced by the Skyrme force parameter set.

## Method

The authors combine the time-dependent wave-packet (TDWP) method with density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) theory to compute 12C + 12C fusion cross-sections. The DC-TDHF method extracts microscopic ion-ion interaction potentials for different nuclear orientations using the Sky3D code with the SkI3 Skyrme force, while the TDWP method solves the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the collective dynamics including a Woods-Saxon absorptive potential with an orientation-dependent term to simulate fusion.

## Key numbers

- The static HF calculation for 12C gave quadrupole deformation beta_2 = 0.219 and gamma = 60 degrees, compared to the experimental value of approximately beta_2 = -0.5
- The absorptive potential shifting parameter r_iw = 0.70 fm produced a resonance peak in the transmission coefficient for head-on collisions
- Total angular momentum values contributing to the low-energy region are J = 0, 2, 4, 6
- The numerical error associated with both the norm of the wave packet and conservation of energy is approximately 10^{-12}
- The DC-TDHF simulations used a grid spacing of 0.5 fm in all directions, with fragments initially separated by 25 fm
- The absorptive potential strength V_i = 50 fm and diffuseness a_i = 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends the TDWP model of Diaz-Torres (2018) by replacing the macroscopic finite-range liquid-drop potential with microscopic DC-TDHF interaction potentials, and by using the full Hamiltonian in the window operator method for calculating transmission coefficients. The calculations are compared with experimental data from Spillane (2007), Fruet (2020), and others, as well as with the Trojan Horse Method results of Tumino (2018). The approach enables future investigation of both cluster effects in the nuclear molecule and compound nucleus resonances through giant resonance analysis, and could be improved by using a different Skyrme force parameter set that predicts better binding energies and quadrupole deformation for 12C.
