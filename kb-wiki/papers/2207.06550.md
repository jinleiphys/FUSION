# Pseudo-scalar mesons: light front wave functions, GPDs and PDFs

> We develop a unified algebraic model which satisfactorily describes the internal structure of pion and kaon as well as heavy quarkonia ($η_c$ and $η_b$). For each of these mesons, we compute their generalized parton distributions (GPDs), built through the overlap representation of their light-front wave function, tightly constrained by the modern and precise knowledge of their quark distribution amplitudes. From this three-dimensional knowledge of mesons, we deduce parton distribution functions (PDFs) as well as electromagnetic form factors and construct the impact parameter space GPDs. The PDFs for mesons formed with light quarks are then evolved from the hadronic scale of around 0.3 GeV to 5.2 GeV, probed in experiments. We make explicit comparisons with experimental results available and with earlier theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified algebraic model for pseudo-scalar mesons (pion, kaon, ηc, ηb) that connects their light-front wave functions to their parton distribution amplitudes, but the model's validity is explicitly conditional on the hadronic scale (around 0.3 GeV) where valence degrees of freedom fully express hadron properties, and the PDFs for light mesons must be evolved to 5.2 GeV for comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors construct an algebraic model for the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using a form-invariant spectral density parameterized by the leading-twist parton distribution amplitude (PDA). They derive the light-front wave function (LFWF) from the Bethe-Salpeter wave function via Mellin moments, obtaining a closed algebraic relation between the LFWF and the PDA. Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are computed through the overlap representation of the LFWF in the DGLAP kinematic region, from which parton distribution functions (PDFs), electromagnetic form factors, and impact parameter space GPDs are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic scale ζ_H = 0.33 (1 ± 0.1) GeV, used as the initial scale for PDF evolution
- PDFs evolved from ζ_H to ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV for comparison with experimental data
- For ν = 1, the model recovers results from prior work by Chouika (2017), Mezrag (2016), and Chavez (2021)
- The PDF exhibits large-x behavior q_M(x; ζ_H) ∼ (1-x)^2 when the input PDA behaves as φ(x→1) ∼ (1-x)
- The charge radius benchmark is varied by 5% to produce the uncertainty band for pion and kaon form factors

## Context

This work extends the algebraic modeling approach previously applied to GPD-related distributions by Chavez (2021), Chouika (2017), Mezrag (2016), and Raya (2022) by introducing a w-dependent definition of the cutoff parameter Λ_w that enables closed algebraic expressions relating different distributions. The model uses PDA inputs from SDE calculations by Cui (2020) and Ding (2015) to determine parameters, and the PDF evolution scheme follows the all-orders approach developed by Rodriguez-Quintero (2019), Ding (2019), and Cui (2020). The results enable comparisons with experimental pion and kaon form factor data from Amendolia (1986) and the Jefferson Lab Fπ collaborations (2000, 2006), as well as with pion PDF data from Conway (1989).
