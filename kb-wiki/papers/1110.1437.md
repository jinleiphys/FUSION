# Parity-violating asymmetry in $γd \to \vec{n}p$ with a pionless effective theory

> Nuclear parity violation is studied with polarized neutrons in the photodisintegration of the deuteron at low energies. A pionless effective field theory with di-baryon fields is used for the investigation. Hadronic weak interactions are treated by parity-violating di-baryon-nucleon-nucleon vertices, which have undetermined coupling contants. A parity-violating asymmetry in the process is calculated for the incident photon energy up to 30 MeV. If experimental data for the parity-violating asymmetry become available in the future, we will be able to determine the unknown coupling contants in the parity-violating vertices.

## Key claim

The parity-violating neutron spin asymmetry \(P_{z'}\) in \(\gamma d \to \vec{n}p\) is calculated using a pionless effective field theory with di-baryon fields, but the numerical value cannot be determined because the low-energy constants \(h_d^{0t}\), \(h_d^{0s}\), and \(h_d^{1}\) are unknown; instead, the coefficients \(a_{0t}\), \(a_{0s}\), and \(a_1\) that multiply these constants are presented as functions of photon energy up to 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors employ a pionless effective field theory with di-baryon fields representing two-nucleon \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) states. Parity-violating interactions are introduced via di-baryon-nucleon-nucleon vertices with dimensionless low-energy constants \(h_d^{\Delta I}\) for isospin transfers \(\Delta I = 0\) and \(\Delta I = 1\), which mix S-wave and P-wave states. The leading-order (\(Q^0\)) diagrams for \(\gamma d \to \vec{n}p\) are computed, and the neutron spin asymmetry \(P_{z'}\) along the \(\hat{z}'\) direction (parallel to the relative momentum of the outgoing nucleons) is expressed as a linear combination of the unknown coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The calculation covers incident photon energies up to 30 MeV.
- The asymmetry \(P_{z'}\) is expressed as \(P_{z'} \equiv a_{0t} h^{0t}_d + a_{0s} h^{0s}_d + a_1 h^1_d\), where the coefficients \(a_{0t}\), \(a_{0s}\), and \(a_1\) are plotted as functions of \(E_\gamma^{lab}\) at colatitude angles \(\theta = 30^\circ\), \(60^\circ\), and \(90^\circ\).
- The coefficient \(a_{0t}\) shows no angle dependence, attributed to cancellations between E1 and M1 contributions.
- The magnitudes of \(a_{0t}\), \(a_{0s}\), and \(a_1\) are similar across the energy range considered.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity violation in the two-nucleon system using pionless effective field theory, following the initial treatment by Savage (2001) and the authors' own prior calculations of PV observables in \(np \to d\gamma\) at threshold (Hyun 2009, Ando 2010, Shin 2010). The results are framed in terms of the DDH potential (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein), where \(\rho\)- and \(\omega\)-meson exchange correspond to \(h_d^{0t}\) and \(h_d^{0s}\), and \(\pi\)-exchange corresponds to \(h_d^1\); the similar magnitudes of the coefficients suggest that future measurements of \(P_{z'}\) could determine the unknown PV coupling constants and provide a cross-check with DDH potential calculations.
