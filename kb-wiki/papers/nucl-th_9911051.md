# Photo- and Electro-Disintegration of 3He at Threshold and pd Radiative Capture

> The present work reports results for: pd radiative capture observables measured at center-of-mass (c.m.) energies in the range 0--100 keV and at 2 MeV by the TUNL and Wisconsin groups, respectively; contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) integral in 3He from the two- up to the three-body breakup thresholds, compared to experimental determinations by the TUNL group in this threshold region; longitudinal, transverse, and interference response functions measured in inclusive polarized electron scattering off polarized 3He at excitation energies below the threshold for breakup into ppn, compared to unpolarized longitudinal and transverse data from the Saskatoon group. The calculations are based on a realistic Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon interactions and a realistic current operator, including one- and two-body components. The theoretical predictions obtained by including only one-body currents are in violent disagreement with data. These differences between theory and experiment are, to a large extent, removed when two-body currents are taken into account, although some rather large discrepancies remain in the c.m. energy range 0--100 keV, particularly for the pd differential cross section and tensor analyzing power at small angles, and contributions to the GDH integral. A rather detailed analysis indicates that these discrepancies have, in large part, a common origin, and can be traced back to an excess strength obtained in the theoretical calculation of the E1 reduced matrix element associated with the pd channel having L,S,J=1,1/2,3/2. It is suggested that this lack of E1 strength observed experimentally might have implications for the nuclear interaction at very low energies. Finally, the validity of the long-wavelength approximation for electric dipole transitions is discussed.

## Key claim

The theoretical predictions for pd radiative capture, photo- and electro-disintegration of 3He, based on a realistic Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon interactions and a current operator including one- and two-body components, remove most of the violent disagreement with data seen when using only one-body currents, yet some rather large discrepancies remain in the c.m. energy range 0–100 keV, particularly for the pd differential cross section and tensor analyzing power at small angles, and contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral; these discrepancies are traced to an excess strength in the theoretical calculation of the E1 reduced matrix element associated with the pd channel having L,S,J=1,1/2,3/2.

## Method

The authors used pair-correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (PHH) bound and continuum wave functions obtained from a Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The electromagnetic current operator included one- and two-body components, with leading terms constructed from the charge-independent part of the Argonne v18 interaction, and two-body currents associated with Delta-isobar degrees of freedom treated non-perturbatively via the transition-correlation-operator method. They calculated pd radiative capture observables at c.m. energies 0–100 keV and at 2 MeV, contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral in 3He from the two- up to the three-body breakup thresholds, and longitudinal, transverse, and interference response functions in inclusive polarized electron scattering off polarized 3He at excitation energies below the threshold for breakup into ppn.

## Key numbers

- S-factor value in S-wave capture at zero energy: 0.110 eV-b, compared to experimental value 0.109 ± 0.010 eV-b (TUNL group)
- S-factor value in P-wave capture at zero energy: 0.109 eV-b, compared to experimental value 0.074 ± 0.01 eV-b (TUNL group)
- At Ep=40 keV, two-body currents and Delta degrees of freedom increase the doublet M1 reduced matrix element in magnitude by 87%
- At Ep=40 keV, two-body currents reduce the quartet M1 reduced matrix element by 10%
- At Ep=40 keV, two-body currents increase the E1(1,1/2,1/2) and E1(1,1/2,3/2) reduced matrix elements by 7% and 10%, respectively
- Three-nucleon binding energies from AV18/UIX PHH wave functions: B(3H)=8.48 MeV, B(3He)=7.73 MeV
- Matter radii: <r2(3H)>1/2 = 1.725 fm, <r2(3He)>1/2 = 1.928 fm
- Momentum transfers for electrodisintegration calculations: q=0.88, 1.64, and 2.74 fm−1 (compared to Bates data from Retzlaff et al. (1994))
- Cutoff values at meson-baryon vertices: Lambda_pi=3.8 fm−1, Lambda_rho=Lambda_omega=6.3 fm−1
- Gamma N Delta coupling: mu_gamma N Delta=3 n.m.; gamma Delta Delta coupling: mu_gamma Delta Delta=4.35 n.m.

## Context

This work extends and updates previous calculations by Viviani et al. (1996) that only dealt with pd radiative capture in the c.m. energy range 0–100 keV and nd radiative capture at thermal neutron energies, incorporating an improved treatment of the pd continuum within the PHH scheme. The results are compared to experimental data from the TUNL group (Sealey et al. 1996, Meachum et al. 1997) for pd capture below 100 keV, the Wisconsin group (Schmidt and Kline 1999) at 2 MeV, and the Saskatoon group (Retzlaff et al. 1994) for electrodisintegration response functions. The discrepancies identified, particularly the excess E1 strength in the L,S,J=1,1/2,3/2 channel, suggest implications for the nuclear interaction at very low energies and motivate further refinement of two- and three-nucleon interaction models.
