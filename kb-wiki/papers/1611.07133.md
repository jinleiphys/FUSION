# Symmetry Parameter Constraints From A Lower Bound On The Neutron-Matter Energy

> We propose the existence of a lower bound on the energy of pure neutron matter (PNM) on the basis of unitary-gas considerations. We discuss its justification from experimental studies of cold atoms as well as from theoretical studies of neutron matter. We demonstrate that this bound results in limits to the density-dependent symmetry energy, which is the difference between the energies of symmetric nuclear matter and PNM. In particular, this bound leads to a lower limit to the volume symmetry energy parameter $S_0$. In addition, for assumed values of $S_0$ above this minimum, this bound implies both upper and lower limits to the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$, which describes the lowest-order density dependence of the symmetry energy. A lower bound on the neutron-matter incompressibility is also obtained. These bounds are found to be consistent with both recent calculations of the energies of PNM and constraints from nuclear experiments. Our results are significant because several equations of state that are currently used in astrophysical simulations of supernovae and neutron star mergers, as well as in nuclear physics simulations of heavy-ion collisions, have symmetry energy parameters that violate these bounds. Furthermore, below the nuclear saturation density, the bound on neutron-matter energies leads to a lower limit to the density-dependent symmetry energy, which leads to upper limits to the nuclear surface symmetry parameter and the neutron-star crust-core boundary. We also obtain a lower limit to the neutron-skin thicknesses of neutron-rich nuclei. Above the nuclear saturation density, the bound on neutron-matter energies also leads to an upper limit to the symmetry energy, with implications for neutron-star cooling via the direct Urca process.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the energy of pure neutron matter (PNM) has a lower bound given by the unitary gas (UG) energy, based on the conjecture that realistic nuclear interactions (including repulsive three-body forces) always produce higher energy than the unitary limit. This bound is not rigorously proven but is justified by the combination of effective-range effects, small p-wave and tensor interactions, and repulsive three-body forces in neutron matter.

## Method
The authors use the unitary gas energy per particle \(E_{UG} = \frac{3}{5} \xi_0 E_F\) with the experimentally measured Bertsch parameter \(\xi_0 \approx 0.37\) as a lower bound for the PNM energy at densities \(n \lesssim 1.5 n_0\). They then derive constraints on the symmetry energy parameters \(S_0\) (volume symmetry energy) and \(L\) (slope parameter) by requiring that the symmetry energy \(S(u) = E_{PNM}(u) - E_{SNM}(u)\) remains above the lower bound \(S^{LB}(u) = E_{UG}(u) - E_{SNM}(u)\), using a Taylor expansion of the symmetric nuclear matter energy around saturation density.

## Key numbers
- The unitary gas energy at saturation density is \(E_{UG}^0 \approx 12.6\) MeV.
- The lower bound on the volume symmetry energy parameter is \(S_0^{LB} = E_{UG}^0 - E_0 \approx 28.14\) MeV, using \(E_0 = -15.5\) MeV.
- The lower bound on the symmetry energy slope parameter is \(L_0 = 2 E_{UG}^0 \approx 25.28\) MeV.
- The lower bound on the neutron-matter incompressibility is \(K_{n,0} = -2 E_{UG}^0 \approx -25.28\) MeV.
- The Bertsch parameter is \(\xi_0 = 0.376 \pm 0.004\) (Ku 2012) or \(\xi_0 = 0.370 \pm 0.005 \pm 0.008\) (Zurn 2013).
- The saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter is \(E_0 = -15.9 \pm 0.4\) MeV, and the saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.164 \pm 0.007\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Drischler 2016).

## Context
This work extends the idea that the unitary gas provides a lower bound for neutron matter, previously suggested by PTEP (2012) and ARNPS (2015), by applying it to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy parameters \(S_0\) and \(L\). The results are consistent with ab initio calculations of PNM by SCGF (2016), Lynn et al. (2015), Hebeler et al. (2010), Gandolfi et al. (2012), and others, and with experimental constraints from nuclear experiments. The bounds rule out several equations of state currently used in supernova and neutron-star merger simulations, and enable improved predictions for neutron-star crust-core boundaries, neutron-skin thicknesses, and direct Urca cooling thresholds.
