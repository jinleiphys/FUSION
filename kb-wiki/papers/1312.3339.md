# Density of Saturated Nuclear Matter at Large $N_{c}$ and Heavy Quark Mass Limits

> We exhibit the existence of stable, saturated nuclear matter in the large $N_{c}$ and heavy quark mass limits of QCD. In this limit, baryons (with the same spin flavor structure) interact at leading order in $N_{c}$ via a repulsive interaction due to the Pauli exclusion principle and at subleading order in $1/N_c$ via the exchange of glueballs. Assuming that the lightest glueball is a scalar, which implies that the subleading baryon interaction is attractive, we find that nuclear matter saturates since the subleading attractive interaction is longer ranged than the leading order repulsive one. We find that the saturated matter is in the form of a crystal with either a face-centered cubic or a hexagonal-close-packed symmetry with baryon densities of $\mathcal{O}((\, \tildeα_{s} m_q (\ln (N_{c}m_{q}Λ_{\textrm{QCD}}^{-1}))^{-1})^3 )$. Remarkably, the leading order expression for the density of saturated nuclear matter is independent of the lighest glueball mass and scalar-glueball-baryon coupling in the extreme large $N_{c}$ limit or heavy quark limit (or both), which we define precisely in this work.

# Density of Saturated Nuclear Matter at Large \(N_{c}\) and Heavy Quark Mass Limits

## Key claim
Stable, saturated nuclear matter exists in the combined large \(N_{c}\) and heavy quark mass limits of QCD, but only when subleading-order glueball exchange (attractive and longer-ranged) is included alongside leading-order Pauli repulsion; at leading order alone, nuclear matter does not saturate and requires external pressure to stabilize. The saturation density is independent of the lightest glueball mass and scalar-glueball-baryon coupling in the extreme large \(N_{c}\) or heavy quark limits.

## Method
The authors construct a toy model where all baryons share the same spin-flavor structure, then extend to the full problem with \(2N_f\) possible spin-flavor states. They treat baryons as heavy, non-relativistic particles with kinetic energy suppressed, and write the energy per baryon as a sum of a leading-order Pauli repulsion term (of order \(N_c\)) and a subleading scalar-glueball-exchange Yukawa attraction term (of order \(N_c^0\)). The saturation density is found by minimizing the energy per baryon with respect to the dimensionless nearest-neighbor separation \(\tilde{d} = \tilde{\alpha}_s m_q d\), assuming the baryons form a crystal with either face-centered cubic or hexagonal-close-packed symmetry (maximum packing factor \(P_{\text{max}} = \pi/\sqrt{18}\)).

## Key numbers
- The nearest-neighbor separation at saturation in the toy problem: \(\tilde{d}_{\text{SNM}}^{\text{toy}} \approx \frac{1}{c_2} \ln\left(\frac{N_c m_q}{\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}}\right)\), where \(c_2 \approx 3.62275\).
- The saturation density in the toy problem: \(\rho_{\text{SNM}}^{\text{toy}} = \sqrt{2} \left( \frac{c_2 \tilde{\alpha}_s m_q}{\ln(N_c m_q / \Lambda_{\text{QCD}})} \right)^3\), with \(c_2 \approx 3.62275\).
- The saturation density for the full problem (including spin-flavor degeneracy): \(\rho_{\text{SNM}} = \sqrt{2} \left( \frac{c_2 \tilde{\alpha}_s m_q}{\ln(N_c m_q / \Lambda_{\text{QCD}})} \right)^3 \left(1 + \mathcal{O}\left( \frac{\ln(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} c_1 c_2 \tilde{\alpha}_s^2 / (2N_f g_{\text{gb}} m_{\text{gb}}))}{\ln(N_c m_q / \Lambda_{\text{QCD}})} \right) + \mathcal{O}\left( \frac{\tilde{m}_{\text{gb}}}{c_2} \right) \right)\), with \(c_2 \approx 3.62275\).
- The Pauli repulsion energy per baryon has the form: \(\frac{E_{\text{Pauli}}}{B} = c_1 N_c m_q \tilde{\alpha}_s^2 \tilde{d}^p \exp(-c_2 \tilde{d})\), with numerical constants \(c_1 = 0.0245881\), \(c_2 = 3.62275\), and \(p = 7.0107\).
- The maximum packing factor for hard spheres: \(P_{\text{max}} = \pi/\sqrt{18}\), assumed by both face-centered cubic and hexagonal-close-packed structures.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical study of nuclear matter saturation beyond the regime of real QCD, where lattice methods are hindered by the fermion sign problem (as noted by Stephanov and others) and effective field theory approaches (Weinberg, 1990) face difficulties with many-body forces. The authors build on the 't Hooft large \(N_c\) limit and the Witten heavy quark mass limit, where baryons are coherent states of \(N_c\) quarks (as studied by Witten, 1979, and others). The result that saturation occurs via competition between short-range repulsion and longer-range attraction parallels the Skyrme crystal picture (Skyrme, 1961), but here the attraction arises from scalar glueball exchange rather than pion exchange. The work enables future lattice QCD studies at large \(N_c\) to determine the glueball mass and glueball-baryon coupling (as in Teper, 1999, and Dalley, 2005), which would make the saturation density prediction fully quantitative.
