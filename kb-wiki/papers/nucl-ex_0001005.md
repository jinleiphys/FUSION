# Experimental search for molecular-nuclear transitions in water

> Experimental search for molecular-nuclear transitions H_2O\to^{18}Ne^*(4.522,1^-)\to^{18}F\to^{18}O in water molecules was carried out. The measurements were performed in a low-background laboratory at the Baksan Neutrino Observatory. Under the assumption that the above transitions take place, the estimate for the half-life time of water molecule was found to be about 10^{18} years.

## Key claim
The experiment reports a tentative estimate for the half-life of a water molecule with respect to a proposed molecular-nuclear transition chain (H₂O → ¹⁸Ne* → ¹⁸F → ¹⁸O) of about 10¹⁸ years, but the authors emphasize that the total statistics were insufficient to make a decisive conclusion and that the observed excess was at the level of only 1–2 RMS deviations.

## Method
The experiment used a cycling procedure: water was heated to the critical point (about 647 K) in sealed chambers to ensure all water was in vapor phase, then cooled and placed between two NaI(Tl) scintillators operating in gamma-gamma coincidence mode to detect 511 keV annihilation radiation from positron decays. Measurements were performed first in a surface laboratory at the Institute of Physics and Technology Problems (Dubna) and then in a deep underground laboratory at the Baksan Neutrino Observatory (600 m water equivalent rock shielding). Background was assessed using control measurements with heavy water (D₂O, 99.0% enrichment) and by comparing counting rates in two time periods within each measurement cycle.

## Key numbers
- For condensed-phase water, a lower limit on the half-life with respect to the decay chain H₂O → ¹⁸Ne(β⁺; 1.7 s) → ¹⁸F(β⁺; 109 min) was estimated as T₁/₂ ≥ 4 × 10²¹ years (99% confidence level).
- Under the assumption that the observed non-stationary component corresponds to accumulation and decay of ¹⁸F, the estimate for the efficient half-life of the water molecule is T₁/₂ ∼ 10¹⁸ years.
- The ¹⁸Ne* resonance used has energy 4.522 MeV, spin-parity 1⁻, and width < 9 keV.
- The accumulation interval was about three half-lives of ¹⁸F (5.5 hours); measuring periods of about four half-lives of ¹⁸F (∼440 min each) were used for analysis.

## Context
This work extends theoretical predictions by Belyaev, Motovilov, and Smirnov (1999) that the overlap integral between H₂O molecular wave functions and the ¹⁸Ne resonant state (4.522, 1⁻) should be enhanced due to long-range effective interactions, analogous to the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) and Zeldovich's work (Zeldovich, 1960) on short-range interactions generating extended states. The results are preliminary but encourage further experiments with more sensitive approaches and additional theoretical work on fusion probability estimates for this and other molecular systems.
