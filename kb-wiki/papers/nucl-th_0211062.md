# Comment on "Interaction induced deformation of the momentum distribution of spin polarized nuclear matter" by Frick, Muether, and Sedrakian

> The paper does not take into account previous work where the effect they discuss is explored in detail. It is known from this previous work that the approach is incomplete.

# Comment on "Interaction induced deformation of the momentum distribution of spin polarized nuclear matter" by Frick, Muether, and Sedrakian

## Key claim

The paper by Frick, Müther, and Sedrakian (2002) claims that the exchange part of the tensor component of the one-pion exchange potential produces an anisotropic single-nucleon momentum distribution in spin polarized nuclear matter, but this claim is not novel and the approach is known to be incomplete based on work from approximately 1975 onward.

## Method

The comment reviews prior literature showing that Dabrowski and Haensel (1974) and Haensel (1975) first observed that the exchange part of the tensor force produces a spin-dependent single-nucleon potential varying with nucleonic momentum direction, leading to a non-spherical Fermi surface. The authors further note that their own work (Frauendorf and Neergård, 1996) demonstrated that for some momentum directions the spin-dependent field is quantized in a direction different from the bulk polarization, making the earlier approach incomplete. The comment also identifies a missing factor in the equations of Frauendorf and Neergård (1996).

## Key numbers

- The observation that the exchange part of the tensor force produces a spin-dependent single-nucleon potential was first made around 1975 in papers by Dabrowski and Haensel (1974) and Haensel (1975).
- The distortion of the Fermi surface to first order in the polarization is given entirely by the sum of a monopole and a quadrupole term (Frauendorf and Neergård, 1996).
- A factor k̂(m_t) is missing in the denominator in the expressions for A(m_t, m̃_t) and B(m_t, m̃_t) in Eqs. (57) of Frauendorf and Neergård (1996).

## Context

This comment extends the work of Dabrowski and Haensel (1974, 1976) and Haensel (1975), who first identified the anisotropic Fermi surface in spin polarized nuclear matter within an extended Landau liquid model. It also corrects the incomplete approach taken in those earlier works, as previously pointed out in Frauendorf and Neergård (1981, 1996), and notes that Haensel and Jerzak (1982) and Dabrowski (1984) subsequently published complete analyses. The comment enables proper treatment of spin polarization effects in rotating infinite nuclear matter and neutron matter in magnetic fields.
