# Description of the baryon mass spectrum by open strings and diquarks

> We analyze the mass spectra of hadrons and demonstrate that the physical spectra of mesons and baryons are well described by the exponential spectrum of open strings. The open-string spectrum, derived from string theory, is characterized by a unique Hagedorn temperature $T_{\rm H}$ and free from any other parameters. Notably, our fitting to the physical spectra yields consistent values for both mesons and baryons, $T_{\rm H} \simeq 0.34\,\text{GeV}$, which contrasts with previous phenomenological analyses that suggested different values. This obtained value aligns well with typical string tension derived from lattice-QCD calculations and the Regge slope. In the baryonic sector, our results indicate that diquarks play a crucial role in describing the mass spectrum, implying that baryons can be understood as a quark-diquark system, as anticipated by Regge phenomenology. These findings have significant implications for our understanding of quark deconfinement, especially in the possibly existing regime at high temperature and small baryon chemical potential within the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the baryon mass spectrum is well described by the open-string exponential spectrum with a single Hagedorn temperature, provided baryons are modeled as a quark-diquark system; the key hedge is that the diquark is not a gauge-invariant asymptotic state but is supported by phenomenological success.

## Method

The author fits the cumulative mass spectrum of established baryons from the 2020 PDG tables (three- and four-star ratings) to the asymptotic open bosonic string density of states in four spacetime dimensions, which has a single free parameter: the Hagedorn temperature T_H. Baryons are modeled as an open string connecting a quark at one end and a color-antitriplet diquark at the other end, with degeneracy factors and mass thresholds assigned based on the quark-diquark composition (good diquark [qq] versus bad diquark (qq)) and the isospin and strangeness quantum numbers of the lightest baryon in each channel.

## Key numbers

* Fitted Hagedorn temperature from the baryon spectrum: T_H ≈ 0.341 GeV, obtained by fitting in the mass range m_p < m < 2.0 GeV.
* Fitted Hagedorn temperature from the meson spectrum: T_H ≈ 0.340 GeV, obtained by fitting in the mass range m_ρ(770) < m < 1.5 GeV.
* The Hagedorn temperature derived from lattice-QCD string tension √σ = 0.485(6) GeV gives T_H = 0.335(4) GeV; from √σ = 0.4817(97) GeV gives T_H = 0.3329(67) GeV.
* Using the typical Regge slope α' ≈ 1 GeV^{-2} yields T_H ≈ 0.28 GeV; using σ ≈ 1 GeV/fm yields T_H ≈ 0.31 GeV.
* The deconfinement temperature in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is T_d ≈ 0.285 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the open-string description of hadron mass spectra, previously applied only to mesons by Fujimoto (2025) and Marczenko (2025), to the baryon sector by incorporating diquark correlations. The resulting common Hagedorn temperature T_H ≈ 0.34 GeV contrasts with the lower values (0.14–0.16 GeV) obtained from the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn (1965) and Frautschi (1971), and supports the existence of a single deconfinement scale in QCD. The result enables string-model-based studies of QCD thermodynamics at finite baryon chemical potential, particularly in the high-temperature, small-chemical-potential regime of the QCD phase diagram.
