# Collective quadrupole excitations in the 50<Z,N<82 nuclei with the generalized Bohr Hamiltonian

> The generalized Bohr Hamiltonian is applied to a description of low-lying collective excitations in even-even isotopes of Te, Xe, Ba, Ce, Nd and Sm. The collective potential and inertial functions are determined by means of the Strutinsky method and the cranking model, respectively. A shell-dependent parametrization of the Nilsson potential is used. An approximate particle-number projection is performed in treatment of pairing correlations. The effect of coupling with the pairing vibrations is taken into account approximately when determining the inertial functions. The calculation does not contain any free parameter.

## Key claim

The generalized Bohr Hamiltonian (GBH), constructed entirely from microscopic input with no free parameters, can qualitatively reproduce low-lying collective quadrupole excitations in even-even nuclei of the 50<Z,N<82 region, but it cannot simultaneously reproduce all collective characteristics in detail; the coupling of quadrupole and pairing vibrations brings energy levels down to a scale comparable with experiment, yet the model works better for elements with larger Z (Ce, Nd, Sm) than for Xe, Ba, and especially Te.

## Method

The collective potential was calculated using the Strutinsky macroscopic-microscopic method, and the inertial functions (vibrational mass parameters and moments of inertia) were determined via the cranking model, both based on a Nilsson single-particle potential with a shell-dependent parametrization (Seo 1986) and monopole pairing forces. An approximate particle-number projection using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with Gaussian overlap approximation was applied to the BCS treatment of pairing, and the effect of coupling with pairing vibrations was approximated by replacing the BCS equilibrium pairing gap with the most probable dynamical gap from the zero-point pairing vibration at each deformation point. The resulting collective Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a symmetrized, orthonormalized basis of five-dimensional oscillator wavefunctions with no adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength parameter was set to g₀ = 0.285 ℏω₀, with G_t = g₀/(A−Z)^(2/3) for protons and G_t = g₀/Z^(2/3) for neutrons, using A = 130 as the average nuclear mass.
- The maximal order of the polynomial in β (deformation) in the basis was n = 16, determined by the plateau condition.
- The neutron energy gap at deformation β = 0.15, γ = 60° for N = 74 had a BCS equilibrium value Δ_BCS ≈ 0.20 ℏω₀ and a most probable dynamical value Δ₀ ≈ 0.12 ℏω₀.
- The calculation covered 48 even-even nuclei across six isotope chains: Te (116-130), Xe (118-132), Ba (114-136), Ce (124-138), Nd (126-140), and Sm (132-142).

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of the generalized Bohr Hamiltonian to transitional nuclei and builds on the microscopic derivation framework of Kumar (1967), Rohozinski (1977), and Góźdź (1985). It directly addresses the gamma-soft and triaxial character of the 50<Z,N<82 region first studied by Arseniev et al. (1969) and later interpreted with the Wilets-Jean model (Rohozinski 1973) and the Davydov-Filippov model (Srebrny 1993). The results enable systematic, parameter-free comparisons with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1 SO(6) limit by Casten 1985, IBM-2 by Puddu 1980), the dynamical deformation model (Subber for Te), the Frankfurt general collective model (Petkov for Ba), and microscopic MONSTER calculations (Hara 1986). The approximate treatment of pairing-vibration coupling improves upon earlier attempts that artificially reduced the pairing strength (Rohozinski 1977) and suggests that a full nine-dimensional "quadrupole + pairing" collective model may be needed for detailed agreement.
