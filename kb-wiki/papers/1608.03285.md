# Properties of an $α$ particle in a Bohrium $270$ Nucleus under the Generalized Symmetric Woods-Saxon Potential

> The energy eigenvalues and the wave functions of an $α$ particle in a Bohrium $270$ nucleus were calculated by solving Schrödinger equation for Generalized Symmetric Woods-Saxon potential. Using the energy spectrum by excluding and including the quasi-bound eigenvalues, entropy, internal energy, Helmholtz energy, and specific heat, as functions of reduced temperature were calculated. Stability and emission characteristics are interpreted in terms of the wave and thermodynamic functions. The kinetic energy of a decayed $α$ particle was calculated using the quasi-bound states, which is found close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The kinetic energy of a decayed alpha particle from Bohrium 270, calculated as the mean value of the quasi-bound energy spectrum (12.079 MeV), is found to be in reasonable agreement with experimental data, with the difference attributed to ignoring spin-orbit coupling and orbital contributions that reduce the problem to one dimension.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation for an alpha particle in a Generalized Symmetric Woods-Saxon Potential (GSWSP), which adds a surface barrier term to the standard Woods-Saxon potential. They classified the resulting energy eigenvalues into tight-bound states (negative energies, real) and quasi-bound states (positive energies, complex with imaginary parts indicating decay probability). Using these energy spectra, they calculated the partition function and derived thermodynamic functions (entropy, internal energy, Helmholtz energy, specific heat) as functions of reduced temperature, both including and excluding the quasi-bound states.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius of Bohrium 270: L = 8.068 fm
- Potential depth: V0 = 75.617 MeV (calculated from V0 = 40.5 + 0.13A with A=270)
- Surface barrier parameter: W0 = 215.523 MeV
- Diffusion parameter: a = 1.538 fm^-1
- Tight-bound energy eigenvalues range from -75.166 MeV (n=0) to -4.402 MeV (n=16)
- Quasi-bound energy eigenvalues: n=17 at (2.263 - 0.537×10^-3 i) MeV, n=18 at (8.929 - 0.146×10^-1 i) MeV, n=19 at (15.439 - 0.133 i) MeV, n=20 at (21.688 - 0.650 i) MeV
- Upper bound for quasi-bound energies: 22.705 MeV
- Kinetic energy of decayed alpha particle (mean of quasi-bound spectrum): 12.079 MeV
- Entropy saturation with only bound states: 2.33×10^-4 eV/K; with quasi-bound states included: 2.96×10^-4 eV/K
- Internal energy at 0 K: -74.995 MeV; saturation without quasi-bound states: -46.739 MeV; with quasi-bound states: -35.535 MeV
- Specific heat in linear regime: approximately 5.35×10^-5 eV/K

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Lütfüoğlu and Akdeniz (2016) for solving the Schrödinger equation with the Generalized Symmetric Woods-Saxon Potential, applying it specifically to an alpha particle in Bohrium 270. The study builds on the standard Woods-Saxon potential introduced by Woods and Saxon (1954) and its various generalized forms used in nuclear physics (Candemir and Bayrak 2014; Bayrak, Sahin, and Aciksoz 2015). The thermodynamic analysis follows approaches used by Pacheco et al. (2003, 2014) for the Dirac oscillator and by Boumali (2015) for relativistic harmonic oscillators. The results enable future studies of alpha decay and nuclear stability using thermodynamic functions, and the method can be applied to other heavy nuclei where surface interactions are important.
