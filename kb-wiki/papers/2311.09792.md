# PSR J$0952-0607$ and GW170817: Direct Multimessenger Constraints on Neutron Star Equation of State through a Novel Wide-ranging Correlation

> Our knowledge about neutron star (NS) masses is renewed once again due to the recognition of the heaviest NS PSR J$ 0952-0607 $. By taking advantage of both mass observations of super massive neutron stars and the tidal deformability derived from event GW170817, a joint constraint on tidal deformability is obtained. A wide-ranging correlation between NS pressure and tidal deformability within the density range from saturation density $ρ_0$ to $5.6ρ_0$ is discovered, which directly yields a constrained NS EoS. The newly constrained EoS has a small uncertainty and a softer behavior at high densities without the inclusion of extra degrees of freedom, which shows its potential to be used as an indicator for the component of NS core.

# PSR J0952-0607 and GW170817: Direct Multimessenger Constraints on Neutron Star Equation of State through a Novel Wide-ranging Correlation

## Key claim
The authors claim that a newly discovered wide-ranging linear correlation between neutron star pressure and tidal deformability across densities from saturation density to 5.6 times saturation density allows direct multimessenger constraints on the neutron star equation of state, but this correlation is established using only 16 nucleonic effective interactions assuming a pure npeμ core, and the resulting EoS shows a softer behavior at high densities without requiring extra degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors computed 16 neutron star EoSs using both non-relativistic (MSL0, SGI, SIV, SV, SkA, SkM, SLy0, KDE0, SAMi) and relativistic (DD-ME2, TW99, PKA1, PKDD, PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) effective interactions, assuming a pure npeμ core and a BPS+BBP crust model. They solved the TOV equations and tidal deformability equations to obtain a dataset of pressure, maximum mass, and tidal deformability for a 1.4-solar-mass neutron star. They then derived probability distribution functions for the tidal deformability from both the mass limit of supermassive neutron stars (including PSR J0952-0607) and from GW170817, combined these into a joint distribution, and used the pressure-tidal deformability correlation to convert the joint distribution into a constrained EoS.

## Key numbers
- PSR J0952-0607 mass: M = 2.35 ± 0.17 solar masses, the heaviest neutron star known
- Tidal deformability from GW170817: Λ₁.₄ = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ (90% confidence)
- EM-derived tidal deformability from supermassive neutron stars: Λ₁.₄ = 675⁺⁵⁴³₋₁₈₈ (90% confidence)
- Joint constraint from both messengers: Λ₁.₄ = 576⁺²⁶²₋₁₃₅ (90% confidence)
- Coefficient of determination for the power-law correlation between maximum mass and Λ₁.₄: R² = 0.91, with fitted relation Λ₁.₄ = 11.5346 · M_max^4.84693
- Coefficient of determination for the linear correlation between pressure and Λ₁.₄ at density ρ = 1.6ρ₀: R² = 0.992, the highest among all densities
- All correlations between pressure and Λ₁.₄ in the density range ρ₀ to 5.6ρ₀ have R² > 0.81
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 2.8 × 10¹⁴ g·cm⁻³

## Context
This work extends the previously discovered correlation between neutron star pressure at twice saturation density and tidal deformability (Lim 2018, Tsang 2019) to a wide density range from saturation density to 5.6 times saturation density. It directly incorporates the updated mass limit from the heaviest known neutron star PSR J0952-0607 (Romani 2022) and combines it with the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (Abbott 2018). The resulting EoS has significantly smaller uncertainties than the spectral parametrization approach used by Abbott (2018), and the method provides a framework for converting future neutron star observations directly into EoS constraints.
